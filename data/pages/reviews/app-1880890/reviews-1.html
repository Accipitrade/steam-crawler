Then Hellblade: Senua's Sacrifice VR. Incredible. Wildly atmospheric and intense. Wow.

Don't let Lost Ember VR be your first VR experience with a Steam Index. Hell, don't let it be your first experience with Lost Ember. Listen to the other reviewers. It deserves so much better than this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a VR developer from 2013 on I can not recommend the VR port of this game to anyone. It's a poorly slapped on VR experience that has put no time into any of the best practices for VR.

Let's get specific. From the beginning the 2d cinematics are way too close to the viewer and anchored to your face. We have all this space in VR, it could have been a movie-theater-like display, but instead it's right on the viewers face and you can barely see the controller prompt to skip as it's tucked in the corner of your peripheral vision. Off-putting and hard to watch. Best practice here would be to anchor the 2d cinematic and give the player a basic or blank environment to be in while watching it like a large movie screen at a nice distance.

Second is keeping the original implementation of 3d cutscenes. It is very much motion sick inducing to move the camera around out of control of the player. The camera movements in the cutscenes should have been given to the player to control or held still, and let the player look around to find the focus details. Instead it's almost sick inducing even for someone who has logged thousands of hours in VR.

The third and probably worst point (since you have some opportunity to skip the rest though not well implemented) is that the camera on the player character itself is off-center from the character. This also induces motion sickness in many if not most players. The VR camera here should be centered on the player character similar to games like Lucky's Tale. As it stands as you try to look around the environments the camera does an oddly placed swivel and is very awkward.

I truly hope the developers take these criticisms to heart and maybe hire someone that has a lot of VR experience to fix this version of the game. They clearly did not take any real time to change the existing mechanics to fit any of the best practices for VR development, all of which have been well known for at least 5 years. They just changed their existing 2d camera to a VR camera, and it just does not do the game justice in any way.
