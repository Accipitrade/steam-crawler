just like the developer's solitaire offering, mahjong wouldn't need a lot more to get a recommendation, even if not at full or even half price, but in its current (and most likely final) state you'd better stay away.

starting up in french is a one-time annoyance, and while settings work better this time, no loading screen for every single option, only one to get to the options menu and another to get back, you'll still see the loading screen all over the game way more than you should.

rules are explained in the game, including combos for a higher score, controls are rebindable (who plays mahjong with the keyboard?), windowed mode and resolutions are available, just like local, friends-only and global leaderbords, but still no volume settings.

70 layouts, only one unlocked at a time, which isn't ideal even if some other mahjong games do it too, and while the graphics and music are fine (there are 4 backgrounds and 4 tilesets to choose from), the animation after every single match is beyond annoying even without the just as irritating sound effect and despite being able to click around while it's playing.

no timer, move limit or star rating, you can use hints 3 times per level (costs 200 points each and it's available even if you don't have enough points), but there are further issues that break the deal: no way to highlight matchable tiles and it's sometimes hard to figure out what's not blocked just by looking at the screen, lots of unnecessary clicking required. and the worst offender: no shuffle or undo, instant game over if you run out of moves. no next level button on the level complete screen either, more unnecessary loading screens just to get to the next one.

while at 90% off I didn't find it as much of a waste as solitaire mentioned above, I still recommend checking out other mahjong titles instead of this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat relaxing game with good, smooth animation and no forced time constraints. Alternative tile-sets add some variation and arguably increase repeat play-through value if you so wish.

Only ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ I have is that it's woefully unoptimised - literally 'loading' on every single option that you pick in the main menu. I'm pretty sure the entire game could be loaded to memory with 98% remaining free. There's a bizarre insistence to check if you want to upload your level-completing score to an online leaderboard, yet the worst issue is with the proximity of identical tiles.

The randomness of tile placement at the start of the level really does need shaking up. It's not uncommon to see four of the same tile lined up in a row - meaning that you have to clear both sides simultaneously to have any chance of clearing these sibling tiles. I get that the game relies on a lot of luck and random tile placement at the beginning - but this issue comes up far too often.

I'll give it a 7/10 for now - but would put it higher if this issue with the tiles was resolved.\t\t\t\t\t
