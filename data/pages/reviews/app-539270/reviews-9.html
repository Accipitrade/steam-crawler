\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat I liked about this game is it's a standard mah jong solitaire pyramid game that follows the generally accepted rules of play.
What I didnt like about it is there is no shuffle of tiles. The menus are slow to respond and hyper-excessively animated. The menus pop out at you which is fine once, but hour after hour of play it becomes a real headache, plus the excessive animation slows down game play. The game is designed around playing online where each score is shared with your friends. If you chose to just play yourself you still have to deal with the same screens about posting your score over and over and over and over. Click on not posting your score and it asks for your name again and again and again.
This game needs to scale down the animation and work on speeding menus and overall performance over purposeless animation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a Mahjong game. If you've ever played it on previous versions of Windows (when it was also known as Taipei way back when) then you know what this game is. It's a simplistic game and yet I'll probably get a couple paragraphs out of this review because that's how I tend to do things. It's rather annoying but here I go.

Mahjong is played by choosing a tile layout and then clicking similar tiles away until every tile is off the board and you win. There are four different styles of tiles to choose from as well as four different animated backgrounds to choose from. The backgrounds are all very nicely drawn and this game is a rather relaxing time waster as a result. About the only negative is that there is only one music track in the entire game so it can get a bit repetitive after a while, particularly if you're going after all the achievements which you earn simply by beating each of the seventy layouts.

If you're looking for a fun little time waster or an easy way to pad up that perfect game count on your Steam profile, pick this up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompetent, no-frills version of Mahjong Solitaire. 70 advertised layouts but several feel like very close duplicates of each other, and many don't feature the full count of tiles so the difficulty (i.e. how often you have to guess) varies quite a bit. Everything works as advertised, I experienced no real slowdown or crashes. But it has a lot of the hallmarks of a contracted port of a mobile game. The graphics get the job done, the backgrounds are nicely animated and the tiles are attractively rendered. No serious problems, just a long list of relatively minor nitpicks that could be resolved to make this game really shine

Loading times feel longer than they should. From main menu to actually playing a game requires ~12 seconds and this is not a graphically demanding game, nor should the algorithm that places tiles be all that computationally expensive. The options menu can only be accessed by completely exiting to the main menu, some of the sound effects are not properly normalized to the rest of the sfx (the tile clear sound in particular is entirely too loud), there are two music tracks, the menu music and the game music. For some reason no music plays during loading screens. Music and sound effects have on/off toggles but no volume control, so either they're muted or they're on at full volume.

The largest complaints come down to the design and responsiveness of the UI. I just want a 'quick play' button that gives me the traditional layout from a random seed so I can begin playing immediately instead of having to sit through multiple loading screens. There's a short period between matching tiles and the animation of the tiles clearing where you can't click on tiles that are now free and that's kind of frustrating. You have to sit through the leaderboard prompts animating in and out every time you clear a stage; the game should just remember an 'automatically upload my scores to leaderboards' setting. These little details separate what could be a casual relaxing session of solitaire to one that introduces brief periods of irritation while you try to chill.\t\t\t\t\t
