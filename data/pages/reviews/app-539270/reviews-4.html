\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a thoroughly mediocre Mahjong game. The only thing that recommends it is that it is mercifully free of advertising and other BS, which puts it slightly over the Mahjongg that you can get directly from Microsoft.

Otherwise, it's full of problems. The tilesets are poor and unexplained -- it isn't clear what's dragons, what's flowers, and what's seasons. Nowhere does it explain which of those will match. There's nowhere -- even in the help -- where the full tileset is displayed. The help text is ONLY text. Anyone who has played Mahjongg before isn't going to miss it, but a newbie would be completely lost.

The tiles are displayed in a sort of straight-on front-plan view, with little or no shadows, which makes it very difficult to tell what level (altitude?) a tile is on. A 3/4 view would be more appreciated.

There's no shuffle option. I know it is non-traditional and makes scoring complicated, but I'm just trying to play a casual game, here. There IS a naggy online leaderboard system that I wish I could just turn off. I don't care about a score, and care even less about what scores the rest of the world is getting.

The music can be turned off, which is a mercy. The sound effects are clangy and annoying, but I feel there's SOMETHING there required, so I left them on and cringed.

The standard layout is the only one unlocked, and you appear to unlock other layouts one by one, in a misguided attempt to gamify a complicate a casual game. The UI is almost entirely obtuse symbols instead of clear text, and each single transition is separated by an animated loading screen. Load times aren't terrible, but why the hell are there load times in the first place??

It's not all bad. The graphics are nice and subtle. They don't light up your GPU and they don't get in the way of the game. They're in the background and stay there.

So, look: I'd probably recommend that Microsoft Mahjong over this one. There might even be pervy hentai anime ones that are better games. But I got this one cheap on a sale (1USD), and in the end it's worth a buck.
