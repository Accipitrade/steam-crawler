\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI struggle to recommend this because if you deconstruct it, it's just a thinly-veiled card game RPG where every battle is a pong match that can drag on for stupidly long, and it's grindy af.

The pong gameplay itself has the least amount of polish out of anything, and I'm specifically talking about the confusing hitboxes--the ball can just pass right through your head, or it can register a hit but go behind you anyway, or the ball can be going fast enough that it clips through your paddle completely because its "pixels moved per frame on the X axis" is greater than the width of the paddle. Somtimes, the game even serves the ball to you at such an angle that you don't have time to reach it, and you score against yourself through no fault of your own. All of these things have happened to me on multiple occasions.

Do you like long battles where the enemy keeps spamming the heal powerup? Well the final dungeons feature a Mary Poppins looking ass enemy called the druid which has ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ 10 healing cards and 60 health, and she will make sure that the battle lasts as long as possible. In an RPG, enemies that spam heal are extremely annoying. Anyone who's fought Yu Yevon after acquiring the Magus Sisters aeon in Final Fantasy X knows exactly what I'm talking about. The only good thing about this enemy is that defeating them gives you a ♥♥♥♥ load of healing cards. And I will keep calling them cards instead of balls, because that's what they are.

The game deletes your inventory if you enter the options screen at all. This happened to me twice, meaning I had to go back to the first dungeon to grind for an hour to rebuild my stockpile of healing and damage cards. Second time was after I beat the game so I didn't care as much.

Finally, the netcode is completely busted. I can't actually play a pvp match in this game, because if I try to use remote play together, it just kills my internet and my friend sees a black screen before my status goes to offline and I get disconnected from the friends network. Ok, so I try matchmaking. Invite friend, matchmaking error. Friend invites me, matchmaking error. Try to matchmake with a random, wait for 5 minutes, nothing.

So do I recommend this game? Not really. The two major selling points of this game (multiplayer and pong) are either busted or scuffed. When you have a game called "pong quest", I don't think it's unreasonable to expect the actual pong parts to be highly polished and well-designed. The clunky hit detection and obnoxious cards that some enemies have serve to undermine the thing that is in the game's name.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPong Quest est un jeu très sympatique, au concept très accrocheur. C'est d'ailleurs pour cela que je l'ai acheté. Quoi de mieux que de retourner aux premières gloires du jeu vidéo avec Pong, mais avec une dose RPG pour le moderniser et lui donner plus d'intérêt ! Et quoi de mieux encore qu'Atari pour éditer ce jeu !

=======================================

# Points positifs :

+ Super mélange de genres : le plaisir basique de Pong avec la "modernité" du RPG façon Dungeon Crawler.
+ Graphismes, musiques, ergonomie et interface.
+ Plein de références aux premières gloires d'Atari : Breakout, Centipede ou encore Asteroids.
+ Variété des balles et des effets pour diversifier le gameplay basique et simple de Pong.
+ Mode multijoueurs pour s'affronter sur Pong comme jamais !

# Points négatifs :

- Redondant à la longue. Même avec du RPG, cela reste Pong. Le Level Design n'apporte rien de différent au fil des donjons. On les arpente de la même façon, sans cesse, pour arriver au boss. D'ailleurs, je déconseille de vouloir nettoyer toutes les salles des ennemis, car si au début cela se fait avec plaisir (nécessaire même pour Level Up), à la longue, cela peut rendre le jeu pénible.

- Pas vraiment d'Histoire prenante ou épique --> C'est Pong, encore une fois !

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has so much potential.

The new version of pong they created is actually a ton of fun and the healthbar system is pretty cool.

The few boss fights there are are really cool as they are what actually resemble old Atari games the most.

The problem with the game is how it fills in the gaps between the boss fights. The entire game feels like filler and you can actually just skip most of it to get to the good parts(the boss fights).

This game needed to be a roguelite game or even a bossrush type of game but instead it made the entire game feel like filler.

If you have actual friends to play the new version of pong with I could see this game being worth it on sale but otherwise I can't recommend it.
