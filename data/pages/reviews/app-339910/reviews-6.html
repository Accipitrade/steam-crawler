\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE: In 2019 I tried to play the game again and saw I was missing 1 skin. Went to the forums to learn where it is unlocked and instead learned that the developers of this game have given up on development. Not in so many words, but that is the long and short of it. So it's going to be in early access forever.

DO NOT BUY THE GAME.

_________________________________________________________________________

Ok so after "finishing" the last 2 levels I'm happy to say that I would not recommend this game anymore.

The old review is still below if you want to read it.

The No to recommend all boils down to the developers.
1) A game of which extreme precision is expected from the player, yet the coding and collision detection are not on the same level.
2) The "openness" of how you can do levels is supposed to be a feature, but it found me (and the main reason this game gets a No) doing one of the last and most extremely hard levels with no savepoint to do the last "chest". So navigating to where the last chest is, is not easy and normally you are left with a checkpoint so you can try the chest.
However this culmination of all your hard effort is awarded with no check point (which means getting that last chest is a huge ammount of effeort just to get to it's "cave" let alone the chest itself) and a "joke" from the developers.

It's as if they thought that for all your effort, frustration and anger you deserve to be pissed off one last time. To save you the effort and spoil the ending, the last two levels show a anime girl icon, but when you finish the level one is a womans body with a fish head and the very last and most difficult level is a female body with a scruffy man's face.

So what the dev's are saying is "thanks for your money and all your effort is a waste."

Very dissapointing game with a very dissapointing ending.

----------------------------------------

/!\\ WARNING! /!\\
This recommendation is 0.1% away from not being recommended.
----------------------

This game is a excersize in frustration. To begin with it's early access and that means it's not "finished". As of today 31/12/2015 it is still in Early access and I'm very close to NOT recommending the game.
The problem comes in with the games level of precision needed in later levels. You have to be super accurate while the game itself is not 100% coded. This is where the frustrations come in.
As an example. Eirene's level at the end is a great example of how frustrating this game can be. At the end you need to navigate a small, laval filled hallway with the odd rocket that can kill you thrown into the mix. The rockets seek you out and the small blocks you can land on are way too small. I began by saying that it's probably just me getting frustrated at the game and I'm seeing things. But fact was that the collision detection is a bit wonkey. Take-off's from standstill sometimes work great. Other times you land and do a quick take off and you shoot to the left or right faster than you are used to. Hitting the edge of the blocks pinball you sometimes into the lava.

However the worst problem with the game is the "jitter".

The "jitter" is when the game seems to skip a frame. It's a very small problem and a miniscule problem starting out with the game, but in later levels that 1 frame skip is super deadly.

Ok ok... so all that sounds bad, but I'd still say give it a shot. The game is very fun in the beginning and if it stayed like that there would not be a challenge. But the end game does step over that line very quickly.

Ah yes... the topic of the "artwork". Look there are dime a dosen games like this. Take your character from point A to B through a difficult maze. Or if it's the trying over and over again thing you can play Darksouls. From a marketing standpoint this is a great idea. Skandily clad female anime characters are the next best thing to the obvious other that sells very well. However then this game would not make it to steam. (NOTE: you can make your own levels and swop out artworks.) If you just want the pictures you can google them I'm sure. Trust me, after about 5 levels the picture is the last thing you're thinking about or looking at. If the pictures are the reason you are not buying the game then don't be a prude.

I had alot of fun in the beginning, but the later levels just seriously ruin the experiance with the level of precision needed to finish them. However I'm just happy enough to recommend it. For now...\t\t\t\t\t
