\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, let me say that yes, this game is mostly about the background art and the ability to replace each level's 'art' with other images using Steam Workshop. Thankfully the gameplay is pretty good for what it is. The default images for the main levels are really nice and the art is clean. The 'paid' (with in-game money) level images are not as good, but still decent. As other's have mentioned, you can access the art through the install folder.

That being said, I feel that I definitely got my monies worth (when half-off). The platforming is rather tight considering the random hitches that occur and it can be challenging. Controls are simple as you just have movement and a jump along with a limited rocket boost that refreshes when you land on a platform. As with these types of platformers 1-hit-kills are plenty with spinning blades, homing rockets of various speeds, lava, timed lasers, etc.

As for the 'extreme' difficulty that some reviewers mentiond, don't let that be a turn-off, I really only had issues with one treasure near the end of the game, it took me maybe 1-2hrs to get it (only time I yelled at this game). The levels provide a solid challenge and some areas require a few tries, but as a whole the levels are not generally unfair. There are numerous checkpoints throughout each level that helps even out the difficulty at times. It does feel rewarding to complete a level and unlock a nice piece of art afterward. If the gameplay was crap then I could see no real value in this game other than the cheesecake, but at least the game is rather fun.

There is an annoying issue that I get when I try to download different workshop images one after another, the game will throw up an error message and it will cause me to force-quit the game and restart. Other than that the game seems rather bug free. As this is early access I'd love to see more levels added with more high quality art, but it doesn't seem like the developer has had the time to update. I have not tried the multiplayer so I can't comment on that portion.

Overall, I'd say if you like more difficult platformers like Super Meat Boy or Celeste and you like anime/manga styled babe art, give this a go when it is on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun and difficult platformer with interesting level designs. You can really see how much fun they had in making this game.
The difficulty can vary greatly from level to level and the background art can also vary in quality. To remedy the art problem, you can upload your own artwork or download it from the workshop. The level editor, on the other hand, has a lot of problems, so I wouldn't hope on it getting fixed in the near future.
Overall, this is a good game that has some replayability and the option to play with your friends. Just play it at your own pace.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat's good to say here? Well, it's fun for a couple of hours. A few of the stages are rather clever, like the one themed after The Matrix. Also, the soundtrack is great, though it consists entirely of old FantomenK tracks you can find elsewhere.

The bad definitely outweighs the good, though. The controls aren't exactly precise, but later stages demand an obscene amount of precision, making them way too hard and frustrating to be any fun. The pinup CGs are passable, but not great -- though they're helpfully provided in the game files as loose PNGs for later 'perusal' if that's your sort of thing. Also, the "rage voice" is incredibly obnoxious and I recommend switching it off in the options immediately if you do decide to play this.

Not really worth either your time or money. Everything done here has been done better elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd pass on this one.

Uncraft World has some novel ideas, but the actual gameplay is lackluster. I don't feel at all compelled to return to it. See, the thing is, if you are going to try to build a competent/competitve hard-as-nails platformer, you need super tight controls and great level design. The adult content alone will not carry it. You also need to understand you are in an overcrowded genre. If you took away the adult content, this wouldn't have any legs to stand on.

Essentially, this is a difficult platforming game where you try to get to the end to open up the background picture. The neat part is that you can choose the picture from user-submitted images. That's great and makes content limited only by the community submissions. The level design is pretty ho-hum in my opinion, and the controls feel floaty. I hear that late game becomes nearly impossible because of the controls.

Pros:
+Unique game idea, for sure
+Great use of community-produced content

Cons:
-Price is too high
-Controls feel floaty, and in the hard-as-nails platformer genre - this is the kiss of death
-I wasn't all that impressed with the artwork (the game or the girls)

Eh, I just didn't have a good time.\t\t\t\t\t
