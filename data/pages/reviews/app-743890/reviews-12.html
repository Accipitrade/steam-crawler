\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI watched TAS videos of Mega Man X4,
I loved the soundtrack to Mega Man X4,
I wanted to play Mega Man X4,
6 Years later, I buy this for Mega Man X4,
I play Mega Man X4,
I beat Mega Man X4,
I love Mega Man X4.


Mega Man X4 is such an awesome game. I played X1 and beat it years before X4, so I can say that X4 is definitely where new players can start. X4 is one of my favorite games nowadays because it's such a great game all around: Great Soundtrack, Great Bosses, Great levels, fair and good challenge (Except for General, screw him), and Zero is such a fantastic character to control. So powerful and rewarding to use. It's sad since when I beat the X4, and was sent back to the title screen, a smile visibly faded. That's how amazing X4 is.

Oh, and it even includes Mega Man X1, X2, and X3 as bonuses, that's kinda cool.

Jokes aside, I feel like X4 should be your starting point if you want to get into Mega Man X. It does "getting newcomers into a series" right. Not jokingly easy or ridiculously hard, just right.

Current issues addressed; I didn't run into input lag like many others do. Or even those odd crashes, perhaps I got lucky with the settings and build. Maybe input lag DOES exist and I couldn't notice at all, but perhaps a person that played on OG hardware would know.

At least I can remap controls in the in game options, but I wished my layout would be saved so I wouldn't have to constantly visit the in game options menu every time.

I got the good end of the stick, but apparently the bad side of it is rampant. Best to buy it on sale, test if there's input lag, try to fix, if not possible, then refund. Its NOT a bad collection, but I want to address the elephants in the room for safety.

Beginners should start here, all things considered. There's even a "Rookie" mode if you're struggling too much.\t\t\t\t\t
