PROS

+ Contains the more consistent half of the MMX franchise (I do know that X5 and X8 have their fans but I'd rather have X1 through X4.)
+ Seems to use the actual direct data of the games rather than rebuild them from the ground up. I can somewhat confirm this thanks to a fanmade ROM extraction script which functions on X1, X2, and X3 but not X4.
+ THE SAVE SYSTEM!!! The main reason why I got this collection was to have a more consistent HDD saving system rather than to flip-flop between the password systems for X1~X3 and the memstick saves for X4. Now, it's all in one tight package, akin to the similarly titled Mega Man X Collection for the Nintendo Gamecube and the Sony PlayStation 2.
+ The bonuses. It's interesting to see how a franchise like MMX evolved over the years and how it was publicly shown before the internet saw widespread usage. This also includes The Day of Sigma, a 24-minute OVA which serves to explain some backstory about MMX's plot. You can also listen to the soundtrack of all of the games, including some tunes original to MMXLC 1!
+ For me, anyway, there was no real slowdown or input lag. The games functioned pretty well for me with 1:1 input. As soon as I bind the Dash key to R1/RB, I'm rollin', and there haven't been any hiccups to break my flow. Maybe this is a case-by-case thing and I just got extremely lucky? Who knows. It works for me and I'm not going to complain.

CONS

- There are no uncompressed ROMs, the aforementioned fanmade extraction script must be used if you want to retrieve X1, X2, and X3's ROMs. Currently no way of doing the same for X4, which might be a dealbreaker if you wanted to pay for just the games and extract them for use on emulators if MMXLC 1 didn't end up working for you.
- X Challenge Mode seems like a last-minute inclusion, even the idea is somewhat lackluster on paper. I feel like Capcom would've been able to do something even crazier with how versatile the MMX style already is, like parkour levels, harder versions of existing stages, maybe even some sorta level editor? I dunno.
- Inconsistent optimization. I myself never encountered any problems with slowdown or input lag per se, but the amount of other peeps complaining about this was worrying. Now, I'm not saying you should blindly buy this regardless of how it runs on your end, far from it. If the input lag gets on your nerves, refund that sumb*tch. I'm saying that your mileage may vary wildly. What's funny is that this complaint is nonexistant for the Mega Man Classic collections on Steam and I've heard from a friend of mine that you can limit the NES slowdown on those games by using the collections' own enhancements. Not to say X1-2-3-4 were inherently "slow" unless the action got *too* hectic, but the point still stands.
- The emulation bells and whistles leave a lot to be desired. A LOT. The first "HD" filter is dogsh*t, the CRT filter is... alright but I would prefer CRT filters to be as faithful as possible on if I do want one, and this is more like subtle scanlines. Wallpapers are also lacking. The artwork itself is good but there's not a lot of variety, so to say. Most of the time, I don't use wallpapers if I'm playing a game that was made with 4:3 displays in mind, but I leave 'em on while playing MMXLC, mostly to appreciate the admittedly cool design and drawings they proudly display. Not to mention (and this ties back to the previous con), there are no options to "lower the quality", you can only adjust the resolution. Granted, X1-2-3-4 did not need any visual settings and MMXLC doesn't exactly do anything that would push a GTX to its limits, but I find it odd that MMXLC doesn't have many visual tweaks. No rewind feature, no save states, no.. probably whatever else that the MMLC already had. That collection came out before, so what exactly is this collection's excuse?
- 7 GiB download size. Not the most egregious download, granted, but X1-2-3-4 are frickin' lightweight by today's standards and I feel that Capcom would've been able to compress the Legacy Collection goodies well enough so it would clock in at... I dunno, 4 or 5 gigs?

All in all, this is a bit of a tough sell. X1-2-3-4 are great games and, in a way, I prefer X over Classic. So, I don't really regret this purchase, but the lack of bells and whistles are going to be a bit of a concern, so will the possibility of having performance issues. Think twice before putting the 20 buckaroos down.

But hey. This has the "WHAT AM I FIGHTING FOOOOOOOOOOOOOORRRRR?????!!!!" scene untouched in all it's 1997 voice acting glory, and that's all that matters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMegaman X was my entry point to the franchise as a whole. I loved it so much that I retroactively sought out the NES Megaman titles, which was way easier to do in the mid nineties than it is today if you went yard-sale hopping.

While I will start by saying this collection is not bad, sadly, it is disappointing.

1. Performance issues. There's weird miniscule lag that was not present on the Snes or PS2 collections. It may not seem like much but anything that messes with timing in a MMX game is bad news. It also runs my CPU really hot for no good reason. Sometimes the game crashes, but rarely for me.

2. Graphics aren't carried over super well. There is no softening/pixel-interpolation filter at all, so every single pixel is sharp. Now, for some of you that may not sound too bad, but for me I want the games to look the way they did when I played them as a kid. Super sharp pixels was not it. Plus, Snes games in general look far better when the image is softened like they would have been on a real CRT set. PLUS, the megaman Legacy collections 1 and 2 had pixel softening, so why not this collection? Nit-pick to some, problem for me (The Zsnes looks and plays these games better btw).

3. The overall presentation seems rushed and unpolished. There are a lot of cool little features from the Megaman classic legacy volumes that were not implemented here. You can't fight Mavericks from the databases. The save/load menu is set up strangely. The menus in general are just not as intuitive as the MMLC volume 1. Plus, there's even lag issues in the menus at times.

It kinda feels like Capcom did not care about MMX as much, and it shows.

Knowing that, it is still a way to play the best MMX games on PC. So I recommend it for that alone. Even though I like MMX5, I do not think I will be buying the second volume, however. Those games are not worth this rushed mess.\t\t\t\t\t
