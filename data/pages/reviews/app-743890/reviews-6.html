what else is there to say. I might be a little biased, yeah, but its still the best representation of what X is and why its so great.
10/10

-basically an expansion pack for x1, introduces new mechanics and ideas
-music is still good, level design is still good
-puzzle solving with weapons is a thing now
-crystal snail stage. dang it looks nice
-shoryuken!
-mavericks are fun and cool.

pros:
-upwards air dashing. the armor and its upgrades are helpful
-playable zero ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•
-its an x game on the snes, which means its playable.
-tri thunder is nice

-gravity well stands out as being the worst weapon in any videogame, like ever. most of the sub weapons are meh at best
-the soundtrack is bad. it made me want to finish the game as quickly as possible, and the worst part is that you cant switch to the remaster ost (though I don't love that one either).
-level design and mavericks are forgettable at best, annoying at worst. this game is hard but not really in a good way. the whole game feel is generally more sloppy than it should be for a megaman x game on the snes
-getting some of the items is weird thanks to the ride armor system
-sigma returns despite the introduction of a new villain. The doppler fight is also lame
-zero is barely playable, but is basically necessary because x takes 4x as much damage for most of the game. I don't know why this is, but it's like x is made of paper mache until he gets the body armor. also, if zero dies once you lose him for the rest of your playthrough.

-the soundtrack is amazing. among the best in the series

-the fourth armor is one of, if not the best armor, in the entire series. Everything works great

cons:
-voice acting.
-a couple of the x weapons are useless except for puzzles or a boss
-story could be better. I want to know more about iris, the colonel, repliforce. but you dont get too much

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot much to say here that hasn't been said many times already, really. If you're simply looking for a way to play the first four Mega Man X games in an easy and accessible way, this collection's pretty great for that. Though it does suck that the first three games don't remember any custom control configurations you put in, so you'll have to redo them each time you boot them back up. That, and quite frankly X3 is a sour point in this collection, but it's nice it was included regardless.

Day of Sigma's a pretty nice bonus along with all the other cool promotional content and even merchandise preserved in this collection, that's a huge bonus I'd say! (RIP Maverick Hunter X though..) I do question the decision to have the museum content be identical between both this and XLC2. As for X Challenge, very cool idea that left me hyped for the game's launch, but in execution I just found it "meh". Some bosses really paired well here for some fun fights, while others genuinely were infuriating and..yeah. You can go buster only and even without the armor X is usually given for the challenges, but that's practically suicide with how hard these bosses hit for on Normal and especially Hard.

So..yeah. Unless you already own the original games or the Mega Man X Collection on Gamecube/PS2, or just aren't interested in the X series (let alone Mega Man), I'd say this is a must-buy.\t\t\t\t\t
