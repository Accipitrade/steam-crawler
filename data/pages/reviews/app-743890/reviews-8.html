\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played these games to death long before a Legacy Collection was a glint in Capcom's eye. I'll try and keep the game to game reviews short here because there's not much to say that hasn't been said already:

X1 will probably remain one of the most highly regarded platformers ever for good reason. It's infinitely replayable and well-crafted; I'd go as far as saying I often play it three or four times in a year, often not even changing up my route.

X2 definitely iterates on a lot of the ideas in X1, and adds tons of little gimmicks to stages to make them interesting. It's definitely a respectable sequel, even if I personally find the X-Hunter mechanic a little frustrating.

X3 has a solid foundation building off of the prior SNES games but kind of fumbles the landing. Stages can feel sluggish and enemies are designed to exacerbate that further. It can often feel crammed with secrets purely for the sake of secrets and you'll become very intimate with the overdriven guitar sample used by the music team. I have a soft spot for it but it's a bit of a slog and sometimes unfair.

X4 is the bigger budget beast that caps off this collection. The addition of Zero with his own story, FMV cutscenes, many of which expand on the lore of the series. It looks great, the music is nice, and although I have some minor gripes with the perspective of the 2D levels and the final Sigma fight I think this is a solid entry that confidently brings the X series to the PS1.

Personally I think there's no reason to put all 8 X games into one collection, hell, even put Command Mission on there! But alas we get them split up into two for some strange reason. Even the additional X Challenge mode uses bosses from 5 and 6, games not in this collection. Speaking of which, the mode is fine, it's basically 3 rounds of 1v2 boss fights where you can bring 3 weapons ahead of time, but it feels kind of piddly when there's a lot of other things we could get out of this. The PS2 collection with the first six X games had Battle & Chase as a bonus, as well as touching up X6. I like having the Day of Sigma OVA, and a quite comprehensive gallery of neat stuff like trailers, toys and art.

So as far as being a collection goes, it joins the steadily growing pile of collections that kinda lack that extra oomph to really appeal to superfans, in my opinion at least. However, it is a collection of 4 really rock solid platformers, so if you've never tried the games before, this is a great intro.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll 4 of the games in this collection are totally worth your time.
Each one of them are an absolute joy to play (For me, the exception is X3, but I know a lot of people love it, so it could also be a game for you!)

X4 and X2 are easily some of my favorite games ever made.
Zero's campaign in X4 is straight up THERAPY for me.

While I can only recommend the Legacy Collection #2 to the hardcore Megaman X fans; Collection #1 is easily a must buy for anyone remotely interested in action platformers. These are some of the best 2D platformers ever made, and they haven't aged a day.

You will fall in love with the gameplay, music, action, tone, exploration, and replayability of these games; I guarantee it.

=======================================================================
However; I HIGHLY HIGHLY HIGHLY recommend to go through the guides by user "NightCurse" on modding the game to enhance the performance. These ports are not very performance-accurate. as the random input lag and frame drops KILL the whole experience, as this game requires very precise inputs.

The mods are extremely easy to apply, simply tweaking some text files and launch options (no downloads), and it will absolutely make a night-day difference with your play experience, making the games resemble their original counterparts more accurately.

Also, trust me on this, you are going to want to follow the guide by user "Kaed✩Wolf" on switching all the English Dub to Japanese Dub for Megaman X4. If you have played X4 before, I do not even have to explain why. If not, look up any X4 cutscene with spoken English dialogue on youtube, and you will be rushing to that guide. Don't believe me? \r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonal ranking of games in this collection

X1- The Classic Megaman X game and arguably the best of the 16 bit games (imo)
X2- Improves several aspects from the first game such as having the dash by default however the level designs are not as good as the original.
X3- The weakest of the games in this collection. Little is improved over X2 and Zero while being playable is also limited in where he can be played plus nothing that happens in this game is really referenced in future games.
X4- The best of the PS1 era of Megaman X games. Pick X or Zero at the start of your game and then play through the entire game only as them (X being the easier of the two while more story events tied to future games are mentioned in Zeros story)

Pros and Cons of the collection itself:

Pros:
Challenge mode where 2 bosses can be fought at once
several concept art and wallpaper unlockables for beating the games
can switch between the English or Japanese versions of each game

Cons: Controller input delay is commonly reported issue with the 16 bit games in the collection (experienced myself as well)
No save state options (Not as big of a deal in later in the PS1 entries but the 16 bit games will make you redo entire stages if you game over instead of checkpoints

Verdict: Grab this collection on a sale if you want or have played any of the Megaman-X games listed
