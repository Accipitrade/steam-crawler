\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've only recently became a Mega Man nerd thanks to all the Legacy Collections Capcom made and MM11. And the X series, so far, is my favorite Mega Man sub-franchise, due to the lovable characters and their amazing designs, great controls and top-tier music. And I think MMXLC1 is definitely worth your time:

Pros:
- 4 amazing games. Don't listen to some people saying X3 sucks. It's not as good as X1 or X4 but it's definitely not X7 or even X6
- I'm quite nitpicky about input lag in my games, especially platformers, so I'm happy to say I didn't encounter any lag in MMXLC2 (played with an XBO controller)
- Top-tier UI. I know it sounds dumb but I just LOVE the XLC1/2 UI, and I'm glad Capcom realized this and that's why Z/ZXLC has the same exact one, just more burgundy
- A lot of side-content. Gallery that features a ton of concept art (alongside character biographies), music player that features HQ tracks of all games and an OVA from MMMHX (a failed reboot of the X series)

Nitpicks:
- X Challenge. An amazing idea on paper, but in reality is really lackluster. There's only a couple of bosses that actually compliment each other movesets (Chill Penguin/Frost Walrus) while others are just plain unfair (Vile MK-3/High Max)
- X Challenge's White Ultimate Armor. It's looks absolutely gorgeous in various illustrations, but the actual in-game sprite for it is just so lazily done. Literally looks like it was made in MS Paint

Cons:
- Unlike Z/ZXLC (which consists of ports), XLC1/2 mostly consists of ROM's. That means X1-X3 are here in their pure, sometimes slow glory, just like on SNES (even though X Collection on PS2 did not have any slowdowns)
- If you're planning to buy this or MMXLC2 on PC, there's a 50/50 chance you'll encounter a "black screen on start-up" bug. It's really annoying since it exits the game and your only option is to launch the collection again and hope to god it starts. I rarely get the black screen now since (I guess) I fixed it by "Verifying In-Game Cache". Just a little tip for you
- Another "bug" that I personally can't stand (also appears in MMXLC2) is when you enter the X Challenge menu, quit it and then return to the main menu, the collection's UI speed will be slower than usual. Granted, you can always fix this by closing the game and then launching it again, but I wanted to let you know this just in case

Overall, a good collection that's worth your time. Buy it to support the X series, so we can finally get X9 (the collection's physical Japanese OST release basically confirms it)

My score - 10/10

Also, it be really awesome if Capcom tries exploring the Command Mission timeline (timeline where the MMX cast lives happily ever after). The timeline we all familiar with is basically finished (all we need is ZX Extremus and Legends 3), so getting a Command Mission 2 would honestly be quite something\t\t\t\t\t
