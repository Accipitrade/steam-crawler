\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSince this is a collection of 4 games, I'm going to split this review into a review of each one, plus the X Challenge.

X1: As the game that placed the X series on the map, the first game in the series is, quite simply, a masterpiece. While rough around some edges, the never truly felt unfair or made me feel like I was "cheated" in any way.

X2: A fantastic sequel to a fantastic game. The armor upgrades are fun, the weapons are useful, and the final boss, while easier than the first game's, is still a solid challenge.

X3: This is when the series started to suffer in the quality department. The levels here suffer from "Surprise " moments when it comes to enemy placement, and the chip system I believe could have been handled much better. And of course there's Zero, who played just like X with no real significant changes.

X4: This is the game that X3 should have been. At least in how Zero is played. An awesome game with fun levels and even more fun weapons. Zero's techniques are by themselves worth the replay, just to play around with combos.

X Challenge: The new thing in the collection. A dual boss rush. It's as simple as that. Three rounds of dual boss fights. You can either fight against them with just your X-Buster or fight them holding 3 boss weapons from a list. The fights range from simple, to challenging, to frustrating. And while that is true, I never felt like the challenges were unfair.\t\t\t\t\t

But X2, X3 and X4 are considerably harder to find, and with that comes a heftier price tag. I have been playing MegaMan X since I was about five years old, and it remains one of my favorite games of all time. X2 and X3 only expand on the concepts introduced in their predecessors, while also ramping up the challenging difficulty the MegaMan series is known for. X4 allows you to play through the campaign fully as Zero, which was absolutely mindblowing upon first playthough when the game originally released on PlayStation in the 90's, and is still just as satisfying to play today. The addition of the X Challenge mode - wherein you face two Mavericks together as a suped-up version of X, is brutally difficult, especially in later stages, but retains the "hard but fair" aspect that the series as a whole is known for.

Overall, a $20 price tag is a steal, especially for fans of the series that would not otherwise be able to experince these games due to barriers in price or availability.\t\t\t\t\t
