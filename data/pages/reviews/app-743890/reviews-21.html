\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI still don't get why they bothered making two separate collections for the X games, especially because XLC2 is the definition of sloppy seconds, but as for XLC1, it's the most convenient way to have the best of the X series on demand whenever you want. Reviewing the things that it adds... Basically nothing of value. Being able to play the great OSTs is nice, being able to swap over to the Japanese versions for whatever reason is neato, X Challenge is a hit-or-miss addition, but X's armor in X Challenge also looks ghetto as can be; it literally looks like someone went ham with the paint bucket tool in MS Paint. The balancing is also... questionable at times. Anyway, that's all I can muster for the additions. Moving on to the actual games, they're all here and accounted for, but with some major and minor issues. The biggest one, and I have no idea what genius thought this was a stellar idea, is that when playing X1, X2 and X3, you have to rebind your controls every time you boot up the games, which is insanely annoying, especially since there's no way to actually rebind them in-game, meaning you have to restart the game and rebind them from the main menu. Yeah, it's not that big of a deal and only takes a few seconds, but why is it here to begin with? The save function is also janky, since the people working on this collection either couldn't be bothered to make a save feature or were just plain incompetent, so instead of an actual save feature, it instead just reloads a corresponding password. Even the checkpoint save system in MMLC2 would have been superior. At least then, you wouldn't have to remap your controls every single time you want to play the games. As for the input lag that people keep reporting, I personally haven't experience it, so I can't really say much on that front. I also don't get why the PS1/Sega Saturn versions of X3 and X4 respectively aren't available. You're telling me I can play the original Rock Man versions of the games, but not the other ports, that in some aspects are superior to the SNES/PS1 counterparts? Why? I also don't remember if the slowdown from the SNES games is present in XLC1, mainly because I'm too lazy to go and check it. If it hasn't been fixed, then you're basically just getting a worse experience than emulating. As for the actual games themselves, as I've stated before they're the best in the X series by far, however; they've still got a few flaws as far as game design goes.

X1 Section:

«Pros»
- 'Tis a Mega Man game, therefore; it controls well, except for the dash sometimes. This applies to all the games in the X series. (Except for X6 and X7.)
- The soundtrack is godly.
- The boss fights can be pretty enjoyable once you know what you're doing.
- Gives X the gift from the gods of not getting screwed from knockback while in midair over pits.
- Generally fun just to mindlessly mash the shoot, dash and jump buttons.
- A fair number of unlockables and collectibles.
- Replay value that's through the roof.

«Cons»
- Intro Stage is slow and boring on repeat playthroughs due to lack of being able to dash.
- Literally every collectible requires the dash, either directly or indirectly, in order to be obtained.
- Not having the dash makes beating the bosses a pipe dream.
- Dash can be finicky compared to X2/X3.
- Level design's a bit lackluster.
- Weapons break the bosses hardcore.
- Literally just pick Chill Penguin as your starting Maverick; anyone will just result in a brutal difficulty spike.

X2 Section:
«Pros»
- Generally the most well-designed out of the SNES games as far as game design theory is considered.
- Doesn't suffer from the issue of 7/8 bosses and basically all of the unlockables requiring the dash.
- Bosses are generally fun, enjoyable and more well-balanced, albeit a bit easy.
- Level design is much cleaner with a stronger emphasis on platforming than X1.
- Dash controls better here than in X1.
- Backtracking for stuff isn't that common.

«Cons»
- OST is pretty meh for Mega Man standards.

Note: There's not really much to say regarding X2; it's just a solid game and sequel. Pretty much all of the pros mentioned in X1 also apply for X2, X3 and X4.

X3 Section:
«Pros»
- Probably the hardest game out of the collection, if that's your kink.
- The largest amount of collectibles in the collection, which by extension gives it the most replay value.
- Weapons are the best in the collection and are much more well-balanced than in X1, X2 and X4. X2's and X4's weapons were meh. Not terrible, but not really something most players would be actively trying to experiment with, and X1's were outshined by the Storm Tornado and Fire Wave.
- Music is better than X2, though it might be a bit of an acquired taste. Might be a con if you're not into gritty grunge rock.

«Cons»
- My god, that early game difficulty spike will spear your rectum hard if you're not prepared. X3 is easily the most unforgiving out of the collection, and probably the entire series, save for like, X6.
- The bosses feel a bit weaker in terms of balancing and game design. Certainly not in power, as they can kill default X in like 3 hits, have patterns that are hard to get the hang of, or are just complete dumpster fires without abusing their AI with their weakness.
- Zero's implementation sucks hard and is basically useless; just play X4 if you want Zero action.
- For some reason, they fixed the issue of enhancement chips not being interchangeable in the PS1 version of X3, but not in the SNES version, so... sucks to suck, I guess.
- The backtracking in this game is aids if you pick a bad order, which is very likely, since there's only, like, one good order to playthrough the game.
- Starting from X3 is when Mega Man games start to... struggle a bit with visibility, especially when verticality is a factor, so have fun occasionally facehumping spikes, bottomless pits or enemies that shave off a clean 3rd of your health in the early game.

X4 Section:
«Pros»
- Easily the most competent level design, boss design and general game design theory of the collection.
- Slowdown doesn't exist natively; experience may vary if you're running it on a literal potato.
- OST is pretty nice with a wide array of tunes with great instrumentation and composition, however; since the PS1 version doesn't loop tracks seamlessly like the Saturn port, the music just starts and ends abruptly. I don't remember if this is an issue in XLC1, because I'm too lazy to go check.
- Controls are the best in the collection.
- Zero enjoys his promotion to fully playable character with a unique and customized playstyle that's pretty fun.
- Voice acting is so bad that's good.
- Sound design is great.
- The animation and pixel art in general hold up really nicely and look amazing.
- Bosses are insanely fun to run through as Default X and Zero, though problems will be discussed in the cons section.
- Has innate functionality for saving. Thank the lord.

«Cons»
- Probably the easiest X Game.
- Level Design is nice to burn through and speedrun, however; the levels feel short and don't offer much in the way of challenge.
- Checkpoint system could be seen as too generous, allowing for mindless brute forcing. It's so bad in some stages that you can literally walk the level, accept all of the hits and still progress because of the checkpoint. When you die, everything gets refilled, and game overs send you back to the last checkpoint anyway. I really don't get why lives are still a thing in this game.
- The bosses with the weaknesses turn into absolute ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.
- Story sucks, even by Mega Man standards.
- Collectibles are lowered from X3.

Tl;dr: The only real pro is accessibility.\t\t\t\t\t
