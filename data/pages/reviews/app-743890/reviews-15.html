\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, if you are already a fan of Megaman X and just want a way to play them, this collection will probably be to your liking. It's a solid way to play the games. However, I really can't recommend this to newcomers at all.

These games suffer from terrible design flaws that really reflect the times in which they were made, when game design theory was still in its fledging state, and developers were much more prone to making unbalanced, unfair games as a result.

The X games really haven't aged well at all. You start with far too little health and without enough abilities to deal with how unforgiving the games are, and you need to find upgrades to even stand a chance against anything. The idea of getting upgrades to improve your abilities is good in theory, but the fact that you're so weak in the beginning is really discouraging. Finding upgrades just feels more like you're making the game less unbearable, rather than making you feel a sense of achievement and progress. On top of that, some of the things you need to do in order to get upgrades are absolutely insane by modern gaming standards, like needing to purposefully SKIP certain upgrades in X3 in order to get the best upgrade. And good luck finding where it's hidden to begin with.

The enemy placement can also be incredibly annoying, an issue accentuated by the fact that enemies respawn if you walk as much as one inch away from where you originally saw them and then come back. This is a particularly big issue in X2, where there are many situations in which you'll instinctively want to back away from enemies, but you can't because they'll just respawn after you defeat them.

You only get a few chances to beat a boss before you have to redo the entire stage, and for some reason this collection doesn't let you save the game right before bosses, unlike the classic Megaman Legacy Collections. This is particularly bad in the beginning of the game, where you have far too little health to deal with the bosses, and nothing but the standard weapon.

All in all, Megaman X is a true exercise in patience for today's standards. They have far too many design flaws to be enjoyable in a time where we have much better alternatives for 2D platformers. If you're looking for a retro-style 2D platformer with a level of challenge that's actually fair, I'd really recommend Kaze and the Wild Masks instead.\t\t\t\t\t

I wish all my favorite retro collections got this kind of treatment.
This package feels really well done and is oozing with nostalgia and extras. The challenge mode that pits you against 2 bosses simultaneously is A+. The Rookie Hunter mode (50% dmg reduction) is a great addition for when I feel like facerolling content.

If the Zero collection is any bit as good as this, I'll pick it up too.

Gameplay Notes
  • X = Has random perma-slowdown issue requiring restart. Controls don't feel responsive.

  • X2 = Has same slowdown issue as X but happens way less often. Controls don't feel responsive (you will want to rip your hair out when trying to get Shoryuken).

  • X3 = Excellent. No complaints. Plays like the original.

  • X4 = Excellent. No complaints. This is my first time playing this title.

Music Notes
This music is AWESOME but holy heck are they asking for an insane amount of money for the soundtracks. Even at 50% off, you'd be paying $29 to get all 5 soundtracks. The base game itself only costs $10 on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
