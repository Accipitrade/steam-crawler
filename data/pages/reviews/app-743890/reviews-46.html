\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMega Man X Legacy Collection is an excellent blast from the past, allowing me to revisit X1-3 from the SNES and X4 from the PSX. That was also the last Mega Man game I played.

To briefly recap, the X series takes place a hundred years in the future beyond the original Mega Man series. X was made by the Dr. Light of the original series, and the good doctor makes several appearances from beyond the grave to give X upgrades, including some Street Fighter moves. Like the original series, X hunts down rogue robots. This time he gets occasional help in the form of Zero, a light saber wielding creation of Dr. Wily, the main antagonist of the original.

Running through it all again I remember why I loved some of these games more than others. X1 was just fun. Meanwhile X2-3 were designed for increased difficulty, making them more frustration than fun. X4 then opened a can of awesome.

X4 lets you finally play as Zero. Most fans of the series were probably as eager as me to play him, which is probably the wrong way to approach the game since Zero is both hard mode and much more fun. This makes playing X after both incredibly easier and kind of a let down. Still fun, just not Zero fun.

Overall, well worth the cost.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think that the tl;dr below speaks volumes…

➕ X1, X2, X3 and X4 are still great games.
➕ Menus look pretty… I guess?
➖ Poor resolution options. Pixels aren’t scaled correctly. 21:9 monitors are problematic.
➖ Horrible control settings. Barely configurable. Settings not saving in X1-3.
➖ Laughing stock for a save system. No save states like in MMLC1. No checkpoint saves like in MMLC2.
➖ It feels like there is an input lag in X1-X3 games.
➖ Original games in emulation only. No slowdown and audio fixes etc.
➖ X-Challenge mode is an interesting addition, but it replaces Challenge modes from previous collections.

This collection is a disgrace to the Mega Man X legacy.
This collection is an atrocious piece of software that surprisingly is far worse than Capcom previous compilations.
This collection is an barebones effort that imploded upon itself and is an example of how bad packaging can ruin the pack of great classics.

Most of the complains about Mega Man X Legacy Collection are true. I fortunately wasn’t hit by black screens, crashes or FPS problems, but it took me close to 2 hours to get this thing running in the correct fashion. I had incredible problems with the resolution. Selection of ratios is abysmal. None of the options worked on my 21:9 monitor and games ended up unnaturally stretched. Only an intervention in GPU drives helped the matter and I finally could play the game full screen. This was enough to sour the experience of one of the most memorable platforming games created, but it wasn’t the end of the problems!

Control settings and saving systems are a horrible step down from the previous collections. It is so stupid, that X1 to X3 need a control reconfiguration every time you turn on the game and are limited in what buttons you can use. This is absolutely unacceptable.

Save states? What save states? You are forced to use an override of the original password system. Saving at any moment? Nada. Automatic saves at checkpoints? Non. Saving between Sigma Fortress stages? You wish. Your E-Tanks not being drained at each game reload? Hahaha… Mega Man Classic Legacy Collections did this correctly, so why cut those features in the subsequent releases?

There is also a lot of fuss about the input lag. I cannot confirm this 100%, but it feels like original three games are less responsive than Mega Man X4 included in the pack. All games are also bare emulations. There are no improvements or enhancements over the original releases are present here. You need to experience those slowdowns, just like kids playing those games on the SNES in the 90s.

They also removed the Challenge mode from the previous Classic Legacy Collections and put in the X-Challenge mode where you fight a series of boss fights with two enemies at once. While the idea is interesting and mode has some semblance of effort but into it, it’s far too unbalanced and boss oriented that it feels like a downgrade over the Level Remixes and timed Boss Rushes from previous compilations.

This is a second game that gets a music clip below as a representation of developers' effort and competence levels. Good job Capcom… you earned it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo people are giving really long reviews talking about problems, while I do note that there is a input lag since I used to speedrun these games I must tell, they are not as bad as people make it seem. I also tested launching the game without an internet connection and I was able to play it just fine... So maybe they fixed it.

As for the games:

X1: Great pacing, great design, great power ups and sub weapons (With the exception of the helmet) and great soundtrack. If you buy this collection this is THE GAME you want to play.

X2: A good follow-up for the last game, great stage design, one of my personal favorite armors because of the double shot, does not have as a memorable soundtrack but it is still good. (Also in this game starts the trend of sub weapons being useless for most situations, with some of them only strictly used to get power ups)

X3: Ehhh, let us get this outta the way first, this is the SNES that is why there is the lack of animated cutscenes which gave some charm to this entry. Now... I personally hate X3 because of the little things like the charged shot, how obscure it is with its power ups...
The hyper chips are also a issue, the lack of sense of how some stages "interact" with each other and FORCED BACKTRACKING IF YOU WANT TO GET ALL THE ITENS.

The game sets you up for failure on more than one situation (Like the chips you get from the capsules or how it doesn't even explain you can use Zero and if he dies you lost him for good, did I mention that he gives you another power up for your "buster" if he fights the ONLY boss IN THE ENDGAME THAT HE CAN ENTER THE ROOM in the ENTIRE GAME). Is it good? Kind of. Would you recommend this to a new player in the franchise? No.

X4: For me this game is also the reason why anyone should consider buying this, this game is perfect with the soundtrack, you can finally play as Zero through the entire game. X4 is basically Megaman X, but better.\t\t\t\t\t

If you enjoyed the X series, I wholehearrtedlly recommend you get this. But if you only like a couple of the games, or are trying the series for the first time, definitely wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

“The Original Mega Man series is one of the best series of games ever” Hold on, I’m going to have to pull a Kanye here. “Mega Man X is one of the best Mega Man games of all time.” Thank you.

The fact is when the original Mega Man Legacy Collection came out, I picked it up and played it, and it was a solid game. However, even with save states and more, the entire time I played it, I kept thinking “I really miss Mega Man X”. So Mega Man Legacy Collection 2 came out and admittedly I haven’t played it yet, but I still wanted the Mega Man X versions. Finally, Capcom figured it out and released Mega Man X Legacy Collection. This is a collection I’ve been dying to play, even playing it as one of the first games I tried at E3 this year. So was I wearing nostalgia goggles or is Mega Man X Legacy Collection live up to the hype?

Well, the good news is that yes, Mega Man X Legacy Collection has the best games of the series in it. Since I’m reviewing the games on Steam, I am lucky enough to actually have gotten Mega Man X Legacy Collection and Mega Man X Legacy Collection 2 as separate games. The good news here is that I can review them at different times. The better news is I’m reviewing ONLY the Mega Man Legacy Collection here. Which means this review only concerns itself with Mega Man X, Mega Man X2, Mega Man X3, Mega Man X4 as well as the all-new Mega Man X Challenge.

Why is that good? I get to talk about four amazing titles and not a certain other title starring a new character that is called X7. With that foreshadowing out of the way, we can talk about the Mega Man X Legacy Collection.

The fact is I really wish I could come in here, gush about amazing games and call this game perfect, just call it a 5/5 and be done with it. Honestly, it should have been that easy. After seeing the amazing Mega Man Legacy Collection the bar was raised, and ultimately Mega Man X Legacy shouldn’t get judged just on games in its collection, but how well it showcases the series. Sadly Mega Man X Legacy Collection has a significant number of issues I have to talk about.

The biggest problem is that there’s a lot missing from the game. The original collection had save states, remixes of each game, and more. It was a huge improvement for the series. However Mega Man X Legacy only adds a Rookie Hunter mode, no save states, and has the Mega Man X Challenge, I’ll treat as its own game.

The remixed games from the original collection would be something I looked forward to due to my love of the series, but sadly that’s not here. In addition, there are no save states. It isn’t as big a loss as it would be in the original pack as the X series are easier overall, and rarely that difficult. However, the Rookie Hunter mode that seems to replace it is a disappointment.

As I said, the X Series is relatively easy. There are not many huge difficulty spikes or impossible challenges, and when there are, the rewards are usually worth it, such as a special ability, that wasn’t required to beat the game but did make it easier. However there are still difficult bosses, usually the final stages (When the game should be getting harder) are challenging, so triggering Rookie Hunter might make sense at that point in the game.

The problem is Rookie Hunter in Mega Man X Legacy Collection makes the player take less damage. The game claims that the damage is reduced to 1/4th of the original value, but I believe it’s closer to 1/8th or less of the damage. I was having trouble in X4, and finally activated it, but where I died to almost every boss during a boss rush mode on normal difficulty. In the Rookie Hunter mode, I was rarely taking more than an energy capsule of damage a boss, and beat each boss on the first attempt.

It also eliminates spike and pit death in X4-X8 (according to sources, I have not needed it for that). And gives you a massive amount of sub-weapons uses (far more than you need).

Rookie Hunter mode basically is a “Win” button for the game in my opinion. There’s very little challenge once you’ve turned it on, and rather than a helping hand that makes the game easier, it basically lets you beat the game with no challenge.

I like the idea behind the feature, I just think it makes the game far too easy so that I felt that it wasn’t just holding my hand but practically playing the game for me. This is just an opinion on game design but it does limit my ability to use the feature.

There are other issues as well, sadly.

If you turn on Full-Screen mode for the game, at least Mega Man X runs very slowly, and likely all games do. You can use Borderless window mode and it runs correctly, but not being able to use full screen makes very little sense, it seems like someone didn’t test that part of the game.

There’s also no 4k support for the game. Granted these are old games, but the system supports 1080p, 1440p so why not support 2160p/4k? That’s a rather odd omission.

In addition, since I play games on Steam, I use the Steam Overlay and often use that to set up my controller. Some games like Undertale do not support the Steam Overlay, and while I dislike that, it’s fine due to running in GameMaker, or for other reasons. Mega Man X Legacy still allows the overlay to come up but doesn’t show it for some reason (likely not calling the right function in the Steam library when rendering a scene). You can hear the overlay active, you can use it and select functionality in it, but you can’t see it.

Really those three issues are rather bad, and they reflect negatively on the game. The thing is I wanted to enjoy every piece of the Mega Man Legacy Collection but those issues did hold me back quite a bit, especially the overlay. Still, even if the experience isn’t perfect, it should come down to the games, and as long as you’re ok with a borderless windowed mode of 1080p, they work, so how are they?

Well, we might just have the four best Mega Man X games ever, along with Mega Man X Challenge. The fact that there are two collections is odd, but it does mean the first collection is extremely tight. Let’s go through them. I won’t give them full reviews, but give them a couple of paragraphs to discuss what I enjoyed and what was made them special.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis isn't a review of the games themselves, so much as it is a report of the handful of technical issues I've had. I will say that I've thoroughly enjoyed my time with these games and would recommend them to anyone remotely interested in the Mega Man series, or action platformers in general.

Besides a few instances of slowdown in Mega Man X4, the games all run fine (said slowdown is probably due to my rather basic laptop, rather than the fault of the game itself). I have some realy odd issues with this collection, though; for some reason, I can't change the volume of the game while playing. I have to exit full-screen, and click away from the collection in order to change the volume. Sometimes, this causes the game to be stuck on a black screen, and I have to restart my computer.

That's not the only oddity I've had with this collection. I can't even access the Steam home menu (I've used both a DS4 and a Switch Pro controller for this game, and neither of their home buttons function like they should). Instead, whenever I press the home button, my game just freezes for about five seconds before giving me control again.

I've never had these issues with any other Steam games. The first two Mega Man Legacy Collections did not have these problems, so I assume this was ported by a different company or something. If anyone else has experienced these issues, or knows of a means of fixing them, please let me know. I might opt for getting the X collections on my PS4 instead, if my issues with the Steam version can't be remedied.

Thank you for reading! I'd still recommend this game, if you don't mind the possibility of certain technical issues. For the time being, I'd say get them on something that isn't Steam, if you can.
