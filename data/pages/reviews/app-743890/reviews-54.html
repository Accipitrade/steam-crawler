In short – If you’re a Megaman X veteran like me, Just get it! It’s a no brainer! Highly recommend!

Ok, so Megaman X was actually my first game that I got on the Snes and I played the ♥♥♥♥ outta that game! I then found out there were other Megaman Titles before this, and so that’s how I got hooked!

So I’ve played X-3 and X6 but not the others (due to parental issues but we won’t go into that!), so it’s great that for me I have “new games” to explore. But knowing what I know on the games that I’ve played, it really is a trip down memory lane noting down the passwords on pieces of paper where I can continue where I left off! This is also the case for X-3 where the passwords are needed, but instead of putting pen to paper, I’ve been taking pictures of it using my smart phone 😉

Anyways Love the series, if you’re new to this it’s worth playing in Rookie mode, as you will find some of the stages challenging! (back then we did not have that!)
Love the extras they have in these and abit of info on each boss and characters. I will definitely spend more hours on this nostalgic trip 😊
All in all 100/10!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJUMP'N SHOOT'N MAN

Mega Man X is finally on Steam, working on modern systems. But beware, appearently the emulated games (X1-3) have a slight input lag. And there we also have another issue: X1 & 3 are emulated, dispite the fact that PC versions of both games exists. This is a huge drawback for X3 since the PC/PSX version of it featured animated cut scenes and improved music (they even included 2 music tracks of the PSX version in the musuem), which I personally prefer for the most part (especially Dr.Lights theme, the SNES version of it is just horrible). The whole collection feels like a low effort project that seems nice due to its interesting main menu but under the hood nothing have been really improved. There are so many little issues that should have been addressed but up to this point, they never did. Capcom should fix these issues via a patch but it looks like they wont put in any effort in it. A real shame!

Also many people complained that you can't change the key settings but that's not true for the most part, you can change the non-movement keys in the ingame settings of every game. Altough the settings are not saved and you have to reassign them everytime you "unload" the game (doesn't happen if you just restart the game via the tab menu).

Also the PSP remake of MMX1 and Command Mission, which I never had the oppertunity to play, are missing. It would be great if they were added for free (maybe one for each bundle?) or later as buyable DLCs for both collections.

I'd also like to mention that I didn't bought the second collection, since I won't play X7 and I already own X8 which should still work fine on modern systems. So 20 bucks for 2 games are a bit to much for me.

So, can I still recommend the Collection? Not really, maybe... I don't know. However, it's still a must buy for every true Mega Man X fan (this is why i gave it a positive review). The games are still play/beatable with the delay (altough it's VERY annoying) and they never lost their charme due to their great design.

I really hope the collection is succesfull enough, so that Capcom knows there's a market for a new MMX game. It's great that they're developing MM11 but personally I never liked the original series due to it's way less serious "appearence".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this because it is pretty much exactly what I expected it to be. However, I can see where they could have improved some. For example, each game lets you pick what version you would like to play. However, the versions you pick are mainly languages. Now see, this is unfortunate since the original SNES Megaman X games had slowdown whereas the legacy collection made for the PS2 managed to fix them up some and take away the slowdown. I think it would have been cool to pick between the PS2 and the SNES versions as well. Also, there are no save states. Instead the game will save after a boss, after armor/health upgrades, or after a continue. This bothers some but I am okay with it. I feel like having the option to save state (example right before the final stage of stigma) is too tempting and would take away from the overall experience. Overall, these are basically just the games as they were. Not a remaster or anything special. What is special is the insane amount of extras and the challenge stages which are pretty cool actually. I really am having a blast with it and though I wish they did a little more I am not upset about the product that we got overall.

Feel free to ask me anything about it if there was something I didn't address. I tried multiple set ups to see how the implemented the password save feature. Chances are I explored most routes so far and will continue exploring more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
The four best Mega Man X games, as they were released, all on your PC
Access to tons of images, concept art, and music from the games
Ability to play the English and Japanese versions of the games
X Challenge provides a fun (albeit extremely frustrating at times) new way to experience the maverick fights for veteran players
Rookie Hunter mode (which can be switched on and off during play) allows newcomers to fully experience the games without the handicap of not being able to collect items that the traditional password system normally abided by.

Cons:
Can't map actions to LT and RT for X1~X3
No ability to switch between the original and arranged versions of the X3 soundtrack
No options to reconfigure keyboard controls (not even in a config file from what I can tell)
Framerate issues when multitasking (lower end computers / streaming on the same system)
Only one save slot for X1~X3
Japanese version of X4 does not contain the Japanese intro song in the US release of the collection (Most likely due to licensing issues, not a big deal in the first place)
Aspect ratio for X1~X3 is in 4:3 instead of their original 8:7 aspect ratio (with no option to change)

Conclusion:
The accessibility of the legacy collection already makes it far superior to the original Mega Man X Collection, which only ran at 240p and couldn't be played on modern televisions. Despite the issues I have with this collection, I'm finding it difficult to not recommend it for Mega Man fans or just fans of platformers in general. If you can't get any of your own controllers working on your PC though, I would suggest getting it on console instead.\t\t\t\t\t
