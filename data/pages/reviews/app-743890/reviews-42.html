\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good collection for anyone willing to jump into the Megaman X franchise, with lots of replayability among the games such as varying routes to take and what order to fight the bosses in. If you've never played a megaman game before, i'd recommend these, as they're traditionally easier than their classic 8-bit counterparts.

Megaman X - Great music, Great weapons, Great gameplay, just an overall GREAT game. i would recommend this game alone, it's that enjoyable to play.

Megaman X2 - The music takes a step down, but a lot of the weapons are still really useful and the bosses are really fun to fight against, even with their weaknesses.

Megaman X3 - i would not recommend this to new players without the new rookie hunter mode (easy mode) turned on. High amounts of damage gets thrown at you a lot, combined with loads of enemies in really annoying spots, and a ton of backtracking to boot. Not very enjoyable but has some small moments of worthwhile fun. the music is kind of hit or miss.

Megaman X4 - A massive step up from the previous game, with 2 characters to enjoy, cheesy animated cutscenes, and beautiful graphics to go alongside it. i don't remember much of the music, but the gameplay is solid.

X Challenge - The new mode that makes you fight 2 bosses at the same time. Includes bosses from X5 and X6, which are from games on the second collection, meaning if you don't want to shelf money out for the lesser collection of games you will have to look up those bosses weaknesses online.

In conclusion, a solid compilation of fast-paced platformers for people to enjoy, minus X3.\t\t\t\t\t
