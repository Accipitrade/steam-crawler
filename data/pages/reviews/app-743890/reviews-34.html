\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a huge fan of Mega Man X there is a chance my views are a bit biased, but I will do my best to avoid that.

While I have heard some people complain about input lag (at least near launch), I myself have not seen anything of the sort. Then again, I am no speedrunner so if the lag is minuscule then there is a chance I just have not noticed it even if it is still there. It was nothing that ruined my experience and I played using an Xbox controller.

The fact that you no longer have to rely on passwords for the older games is neat. You can instead just save the game.

The X Challenge is an interesting mode for those who would like to fight two bosses at a time, but to me it wasn't really anything too special. I myself liked the gallery for the remixes and artwork since I love that stuff.

I would say that out of the two collections, this one offers the most in terms of quality regarding the games. X1-X4 are probably seen as a better choice than X5-X8 (although I still like X5).

It depends on what you are looking for, but if you just want some classic Mega Man X goodness then I would say that this collection serves that purpose just fine. But if you want something with a lot of new features then you can probably skip this one without missing out on anything special.\t\t\t\t\t
