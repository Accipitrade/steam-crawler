\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, if you liked the old school Mega Man X series games, you'll love this game as well. The game on normal is as challenging as it was originally released. The game on Rookie Hunter makes everything "X"-treamly easy. In X 2, 3, & 4, you have to get hit multiple times in order to recieve any damage. I really like this mode for the fact that if you have young ones that want to get into Mega Man games like this, putting it on Rookie Hunter and letting them have fun with the game while taking little to no damage should be a ball for them. Rookie Hunter mode is also great for back tracking for achievement hunting.

In terms of difficulty, the games are the same as when you once played them when they released. X to X3 if you die in a stage or even at a boss fight, you'll have to redo the entire stage over. Which isn't too bad, on the count of how short the stages are depending on how you pace your gameplay. X4 however, even if you lose ALL your lives, if you continue playing, you'll just wind up back at the beginning of either the first or second stage of a level. This feature in X4 made it EXTREMELY easy for the arm upgrade in Storm Owl's stage.

In terms of annoyance, X2 is probably the most annoying game on this volume collection, due to the fact that you have to collect Zero's parts in order to complete an achievement.

For Sigma fights, X3 is the most annoying Sigma fight in my opinion, due to how tiny the hitbox is for Sigma's head.

X4 you'll have a ball with the stages because how forgiving the game is to the player. Armor upgrades is a must in the game, because without it, the bosses hit pretty hard.

If you like Mega Man games, or you want to get into the X series, I recommend this volume. In comparision of the ENTIRE X series, I believe these first 4 games are the best ones. Though, that shouldn't discourage you from buying Volume 2, I know I will. X5 & 6 are alright.

7 & 8.....................Let's not talk about those.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis collection is pretty dang fun. As far as the games Mega Man X1-X4 are all great games and excellent examples 90's platforming perfection. I feel like more work was put into this collection than the classic mega man collections and all the bonuses are nice. All of the artwork and pictures of past porducts is neat and I love seeing the history of this franchise. The X challenge mode is fun if you like difficult games and plays fairly well considering the mix of 16 and 32 bit sprites. I also personally had a blast with all the acheivements and am glad it wasn't just simple acheivements like beat every game.

I do have some problems with the game as Capcom did seem to screw up a few things. FIrst off you NEED A CONTROLLER in order to play this game. The keyboard support for this game is laughable, as the only customization you have is with a select few keys. I played through both classic legacy collections with wasd and you can not do the same here. I hope they add more support in the future because I do not want to use the arrow keys for movement. Another big issue is that the controls do not save after exiting a game. This is a big problem especially in the snes games, where if I forget to change the controls and enter the game I am forced to restart because there is no way to change controls outside the start screen. The game does manage to save your password in case you want to leave but it would be nice if it could do the same for the controls. A small thing I have seen others complain about is lag and slow down, but it is only in the snes games and did not really hinder play or bother me. A lot of my issues could easily be fixed in a small patch and should not overshadow the res of the game. I do wish it would have just been one big collection of all 8 games, but if you are looking to try out the good mega man x games, this is the collection to get.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI purchased the game as a means to conveniently practice some runs on my pc when I'm not able to hook up my ps1 or snes to speed run the games. And to show my support for my favorite childhood games, plus a possible mmx9 in the future! After the download I eagerly booted the collection up. I fiddled with all the new settings and features available to me at this point, some are cool but mosly nothing to write home about. However I do appreciate the menu layout. Sadly for me, the excitement didnt last much longer.

I set up all the options for what I assume would offer the "best performance" then started mmx and immediately noticed the input lag as soon I held right and shot a few lemons. I quit game, checked my settings and tried the others and was appalled by the delay in all the titles, especially x3, while x4 didn't seem too bad yet still very noticable. Enough to hinder the enjoyability of each game. The delay is much worse than the ps2 mmx collection if memory serves. Since my first couple tries with this collection, Ive only dabbled with some settings in hopes to mitigate the input latency but to no avail.

Unfortunately I may be out of 20$ unless I can somehow get a refund. A part of me honestly feels ripped off, if no go on a refund, my fingers are crossed for an update to address this poor performance, of which I cant think of an excuse for it to be there anyway. What a shame too, I was actually really excited for this :(﻿

Be that as it may, I cant say Id recommend this if you enjoy playing these games, whether its just for fun or if youre interested in blasting through them quickly. Although some of the extra content such as the artwork and action figure galleries are pretty neat, and the dual maverick fights from what Ive seen but thats about it. Unless if this is your first mmx experience then maybe Id recommend it with a fair warning. But other than that, if you dont own a snes, ps1, ps2 or backwards compatible ps3 Id look towards emulation or nintendos virtual console on any of their systems.

If you arent familiar with Input latency, Id encourage you to google or even better search on youtube for videos explaining and giving examples. Theres a few uploaded showcasing the difference between this collection vs the past collections and original titles.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI admit that I was skeptical about getting this one, given what people were reporting, but I had to see for myself. To recap, the most common issues people are reporting are framerate drops, sound buffering issues, and control lag. Enough people are reporting all three that I have no reason to doubt that they're experiencing those things on their given rigs. On mine, however, I'm running into none of that. So, for those with similar setups to mine, you're in luck. Here's what I've got that'd be most likely relevant:

-Intel i7 2600K (not overclocked)
-24GB DDR3 RAM, PC3-12800
-nVidia GeForce GTX 1060 (6GB)
-Windows 10

Perhaps the issues are with AMD CPU and/or GPU? In any case, with this setup, it works perfectly fine. Framerate is locked steady at 60FPS (I have VSync enabled, as I'm not a fan of screen tearing--if you want higher FPS, I'm sure it can deliver), sound isn't stuttering or distorted at any point, and perhaps most importantly, my XBox One gamepad is working just fine with no control lag. Again, I have no doubt that the people reporting those issues are actually experiencing them, but with my rig, I'm not. Hopefully Capcom will take note of which systems it is working for and which ones it isn't, and we'll see a patch or two for those having no joy so far.

All of that said...the games are as you'd expect, but ported to the PC. You have some options on screen size and whether or not to apply a smoothing filter, as well as what window size/fullscreen/windowed fullscreen to use. There are several additional features, like a Rookie Hunter mode for those new to Mega Man X, and some bonus features in the form of a mixed boss rush mode. Overall, it's not bad at all--a good mix of a straight-up port with enough additional materials to make sure it stayed true to the source material while offering something new as well.\t\t\t\t\t
