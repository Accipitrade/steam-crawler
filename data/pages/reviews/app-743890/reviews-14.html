\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're going to get either of these collections, this is the one you want.

MMX1: An extremely solid action platformer in all ways. If you like Megaman games, you'll like Megaman X, unless you don't like the tone. The MMX series is more serious than the regular Megaman games, but it's not so serious that it involves blood and dismemberment. That comes later. The premise of the story is Dr. Light's last creation is Megaman X. Dr. Cain creates new robots based off of him who have free will. Sigma, one of the reploids, decides humanity is inferior, and we're off to the races. One of the staples of MMX is X has a dash instead of a slide. This makes these games play faster. In addition to charging your regular X-Buster, you can also gain the ability to charge special weapons. Sometimes these are pretty cool effects. These games tend to be moderately challenging at first, but once you know where all the upgrades are and you know all the boss weaknesses, these games are a cakewalk. Nothing much more to say about MMX1.

MMX2: X's dash is now something you don't need an upgrade for; you start out with it. The leg upgrade is replaced with an air dash. The game plays out basically the same way X1 does, but you can fight secret bosses. If you beat all of them, the end plays out differently. If you don't... I'd say this game is a bit harder than X1. If you're like me, you'll feel as though Magna Centipede's stage seems a little out of place. You're right. Originally, his level was intended to be in the chain of last levels, but something something the development didn't allow for that to happen. Another oddity is now you can jump on water. It's useful for getting one of the upgrades, but not necessary. I'm mentioning it here because I don't think it's in the manual. The Megaman 25th Anniversary book has an anecdote that mentions Inafune (the director) got mad at his team for that being added in without his consent.

MMX3: Somewhat controversial. Some people don't like this game. Generally it's because it doesn't have quite the same soul as the previous games. That's because it was farmed out to a third party. I'd say the most legitimate criticism overall is that the levels aren't as interesting. It does however include something very interesting: this is the first time in a Megaman game where you can play as a second character. However, Zero is limited. The stages all include a mid-level boss door where you can fight these bosses that, if you kill them, change how the final stages go...slightly. The real use of this is being able to unlock Zero's sword as X (X3's equivalent of the hadoken/shoryuken secrets), and a secret armor capsule. While you can choose to play as Zero once you get to the main 8 stages, you're forced to switch characters when you hit the mid-level boss doors. They're not secret anymore. They're just on the main path. So you have to decide which half of the stage you'd like to use Zero in. In the beginning, he's stronger than X because he has more life and has a 3-tier charge ending with a sword strike. It's pretty strong, but the sword move can get interrupted which is kind of annoying. Once you're fully upgraded, using him doesn't serve any practical purpose because he can't use any boss weapons, and he can't take upgrades. The most interesting change in X3 is in addition to X's regular upgrades, you can choose one special chip that gives you an additional upgrade on top of it all. This adds replay value because you can play it again choosing different chips and see how it goes.

Another note: This game was also on Saturn and PSX. Those versions of the game have some anime cutscenes instead of the usual jingle you hear before you start a stage. The music is arranged instead of 16-bit chiptune music, and sometimes this sounds better, sometimes it doesn't. The sound is also higher quality. This version of the game was not included in this collection, and that's a huge shame.

MMX4: This was originally released on PSX and Saturn. Obviously, the graphics are a lot nicer, and they added in some anime cutscenes, which are pretty cool. They're also legendary for their god awful voice acting. X4 is more MMX by the numbers--no limitations on using Zero, no special upgrade chips, not even optional bosses that alter the ending. However you can choose between two different flavors of X-Buster upgrades. Capcom didn't bother to do any kind of upscaling on the cutscenes, so you'll see them in all their nasty PSX glory. Additionally, they did not include the Japanese version of this game. The reason that's significant is because the voice acting is better, especially for X. X does not sound like you'd expect in the English version. He actually sounds like regular Megaman, a la Megaman 8. In other words, he sounds like a little kid. This is extremely out of place, and I don't know why Capcom is okay with re-releasing this game this way. Well, we all know why, but...

Aside from that, it's an extremely solid entry into the series. From a consumer's perspective, it's a great package. The game as X alone is really cool, but you can also play as Zero--and it's a completely different gameplay style. In MMX3, he played exactly the same as X, but he had one sword move if you charged long enough. In X4, he has all melee moves. When you beat bosses, he learns new moves. It doesn't feel at all out of place. It works much better than you might expect. Plus, Zero's story plays out differently, including the legendary "What am I fighting for?" cutscene.

As far as extras, this package sucks, other than the Day of Sigma short. Day of Sigma is really well done. I highly recommend watching it. But that's it. The other extras aren't interesting. It's not an interesting challenge to fight two bosses at once. There's a reason you don't do this. It didn't work in Dark Souls 2, and it sure as ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• doesn't work in Megaman.

Purely in terms of gameplay, this collection is fine, but please don't buy it for its extras. There's a lot of stuff Capcom did not include in this collection and they easily could have. The MMX collection on PS2/Gamecube is much better, so if you can get your hands on that, do so. Watch Day of Sigma on YouTube. And explain to me why Maverick Hunter X isn't here?

Also, these are the only good Megaman X games. Do not, I repeat, do not get the other collection. Most people agree all of those games are at least lackluster. Capcom dropped the ball after X4. The later games don't make good on what happens at the end of X4. Instead, they take the story in a different, weirder direction that adds a bunch of nonsense. Aside from that, the gameplay isn't fun. If you need more Megaman, perhaps consider the Megaman Zero collection instead.\t\t\t\t\t
