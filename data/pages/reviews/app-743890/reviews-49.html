\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I came into this with high hopes. Finally X on a modern platform!

This is an insultingly bad port of a 25 year old series...

For graphics options you have different resolutions (standard), Borderless / windowed / fullscreen (standard) and a neat option to use the original aspect ratios of the games, as well as enable a CRT filter.

Controls: First off, there's input lag. And it's awful. Tried messing with graphics settings to see if that helps, but it doesn't. For a fast paced action game, this is absolutely unacceptable. The only way I am able to not have input lag is playing with the keyboard (LMAO)....Why would anyone do that?

Control options: Not really there. You can remap the buttons (kind of), but you can't use the triggers at all. This is 2018 and it's a pc port, with no real customization options? You're better off just using a SNES emulator and playing the roms.

Overall, I cannot reccomend this product to anyone. The input lag makes gameplay sluggish, and the lack of controller remapping options is unacceptable for a modern game. This feels like a really bad emulator instead of an actual port.

3/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMegaman has went through many titles from the classic to the zx series and a few spin offs. Majority of the x games was released for the X collection back in the PS2 era but the question is whether or not this collection is good enough or just not worth a buy. I will be reviewing the game in a pro/ con style and end with my conclusion.

Pro:
The games run very well regardless of what all other reviewers say. Now there is slow down in the the games but that was due to a variable being set too low in the config file. It is an easy fix but this caused alot of fans of the series to jump ship too soon. There are guides on steam to fix this issue.

There is an easy mode to help newcommers get into the series. The X series is pretty challenging, after years of not playing myself jumping in again was hard since my skills were really rusty. The devs were kind enough to offer a rookie hunter mode that halfs the ammount of damage recieved from attacks. New players will be able to experience the game at an easier pace.

There is a pretty expansive gallery for the game. There are alot of artwork for each of the main games, a complete soundtrack of each of the games supported in this collection, trailers for the games, and other merchandise images. I mainly found the soundtracks the most appealing of all the items in the gallery but everything else is always welcomed since others might find it nice to look at the concept art of the game with info attached.

There is a challenge mode. This is not super amazing since it just has 2 bosses fighting X at the same time and it ranges from easy to just a pain. Sometimes you will be fighting against bosses from X5 and X6 that you can not normally fight unless you played the 2nd X legacy collection. It seems a bit forced and a strong armed tactic to buy the other collection to fight against the X5 and X6 bosses more effencently.

Con:
There are 2 Separate legacy collections for the X series. I understand why the classic series was separated into 2 collections but with the X series both collections were released on the same day and seems really greedy to have to pay $20 for each when lets be honest each version has one or 2 games that are really worth it and the other range from meh to bad. One must pay $40 to have all of the X series which is kind of sad that this is the price.

The soundtrack DLC are so expensive. Now the X series is no stranger to good music. Unfortunantly all of the soundtracks are $10 each which is expensive for just music. If someone wanted atll the music and the games that would be about $140. That is a crazy ammount of money to be milking from fans of the series.

The controls can not be fully changed on a controller specifically on the classic games. Playing on a controller can be uncomfortable sometimes since dashing, jumping, and shooting at the same time is very tricky and unkind to fingers.

Conclusion:
Megaman X has to be one of my if not my favorite series in the megaman franchise. I was very happy to hear that the entire X series was released on steam allowing me to revist an old game I played way back when. Now I can go through my favorite games like x4 and x5 and finally get to experience x7 and x8 for the first time. I was very disappointed to see the price to own the entires series since the classic games (x-x3) as good as they are, are not really worth $20 total for the first bundle of games. The games do run very well, let me make that clear. So if people are interested in playing the X series I can reccomend this version at least not sure about the 2nd bundle though you should wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter a long hiatus, the X series makes a comeback to modern platforms packed with additional content, all 8 main games, and a Challenge mode that adds a lot of replayability and enjoyment both for seasoned players and newcomer alike.

There are a lot of things to consider, so I will be dividing my opinions on the game in two separate sections.

CONS: Let's get the bad things out of the way first.
(Many people keep mentioning that the game constantly crashes, boots up to a black screen, doesn't register inputs and is full of glitches and errors here and there. I've played for more than 10 hours and this has not happened to me even once. Game has worked perfectly fine and smooth, so I can't complain about that)

- As expected, the games are emulations. Many of the performance issues that were present on the original games (both for the SNES and PlayStation consoles) are still present. You will experience lag and slowdowns on specific sections where a lot of enemies congregate, for example. This affects the pacing of the game and is something that should have been revised.
- To try and create the illusion of high definition and remastered character sprites, the game offers the player the use of filters. The HD filter, however, makes spirtes look unpolished and ugly. They could have done a better job with this. A missed opportunity to refresh the look of the classic X games.
- Mega Man X4 features animated cutscenes that look awful by today standards, not because of the animation, but the resolution. They could have remastered these cutscenes to look way better than what they do.
- The SNES games have limited controller customization options. To keep it as close as possible to the SNES controller (which only has 2 shoulder buttons instead of the modern 4 button standard) they take away the option of using trigger buttons, which is a real problem.
- The 4 games featured in this collection do not have additional content. All the additional content was poured in the collection itself, so you will only be able to access to it on the main menu. They didn't even include remastered tracks or remixes for stages or boss battles. The rest of the music is accessible through the Music Player option, but it automatically pauses when you switch windows, making it useless as a background tune while you work on something else.

PROS: Now let's talk about all the things this collection gets right.

- You can freely change between the US and JP versions freely, and have two separate save-files for you to play without compromising your current one. Having this option available for Mega Man X4 is great because you can enjoy the cutscenes with japanese voice acting which is infinitely better than the american dub.
- Additional content includes character scans, concept art, early designs of characters, cover arts, alternative covers, wallpapers, a music player with music from the entire collection, The Day of Sigma OVA, collectables, and tons of information regarding the Maverick Hunters, Mavericks, character traits and motivations, lore and much more. Valuable stuff if you are a Mega Man X fanatic and want to have an entire database all together in the same place.
- Achievements add a ton of replayability. You probably have a favorite X game from the bunch (mine being Mega Man X4) that you can probably beat in less than an hour and that you have memorized from top to bottom. The game now rewards you for that, but also motivates you to try less usual paths and boss strategies which helps you enjoy the games you have played for countless times in a new, completely different way.
- Challenge mode tests your knowledge of the games and your abilities as a Maverick Hunter. This is the best way of giving seasoned Mega Man X players their money's worth. Now that you have beaten all the Mavericks from X to X6, you will have to beat two of them at the same time, while only being able to bring 3 weapons from a pre-selected bunch with you. Your knowledge of enemy patterns, weaknesses and resistances will be very valuable to be successful in this mode. Also, it comes with Online Leaderboards to check how good you are against other players globally.

To conclude, this Collection will allow you to enjoy what is, debatable, the better half of the Mega Man X franchise. If you have never experienced the games before, this is a great way of enjoying them for the first time. If you have beaten all the games before, Achievements and the X Challenge mode will keep you busy and entertained enough that you will feel like you actually bought the X Challenge mode with the 4 games bundled as a gift for your purchase.

This collection has everything for everyone, wether you are a newcomer or a veteran. I recommend it all the way.\t\t\t\t\t
