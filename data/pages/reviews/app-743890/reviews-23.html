Then, I came across Megaman X being on sale and couldn't resist returning back to the original action platformer. Especially, Megaman X4 was one of the first few
games I started on and ended up playing up to X8.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood games, definitely the stronger of the two collections no doubt.

X: The game that started the series still kicks it to this day. Everything that could be said about this game has already been said, but this game just bleeds fantastic. Very few flaws here.

9.1 / 10 (Outstanding)


X2: A worthy successor to the original, this game definitely emphasizes speed over platforming. Still very fun, with every stage being memorable in its own right, just like the first (Magna Centipede being my favorite). Somewhat underwhelming bosses let it down somewhat, but it's still a very fun, action-packed experience.

8.5 / 10 (Great)


X3: While a good game, X3 just feels more bland than the first two. The ride armor collectibles, while an interesting idea, just don't hold up as much, and the stages just seem more bland with empty space, as well as repetitive music. But it still has the gameplay of the first two, and you will still enjoy this game and the secrets it does have, and the upgrades are still quite fun to play with.

7.3 / 10 (Good)


X4:
This game was awesome when I first played it a while ago, but the main issue with X4 is that it lacks depth. Most of the stages, while fun, are beaten too quickly, to the point where you wonder if there even was a stage. This is worst in the final stages, which last basically 15 seconds each. But it still has great visuals, and very fun gameplay when it is there.

7.5 / 10 (Good)\t\t\t\t\t
