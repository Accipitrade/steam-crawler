\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a bit miffed that they only included X - X4 and sold the rest in a second pack, but honestly I don't want anything besides X - X4. It's a nostalgia blast. For X, The soundtracks are great, the bosses are tough but fair (once you figure out their weaknesses), taking advantage of weaknesses is extremely satisfying, and the secrets are cleverly hidden but not impossible to find without a guide. The Legacy version also has achievements, so you can always try and nab those too.

I've seen complaints about inputs dropping but I've been playing with a keyboard and haven't had any trouble at all. I guess they're mostly controller complaints, so if you absolutely HAVE to play it on controller, you may experience issues.

Also, Mega Man X is not an easy game. Period. You have to have extremely good timing and pattern recognition to tackle these games. Even looking up a review guide will not guarantee a victory. The standard mode still uses the limited lives system, so you'll have to hustle back through the whole level if you die three times, and enemies respawn if they get offscreen. Buckle up, buttercup. Mega Man X is unforgiving and will absolutely kick your head in if you're not careful.

tl;dr It's worth it in my opinion. I've only played X and already don't regret the price. If $20 is too steep, just wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t
