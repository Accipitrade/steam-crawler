\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is how you make a compilation! I was nervous to try this, as ports can be hit or miss, but this is done flawlessly to the point that this is absolutely the definitive best way to play these amazing classics.

The games run perfectly, including allowing you to apply or adjust filters, aspect ratio, screen size, and bezels to taste (OG all the way). On top of that, this has some of the most polished use of Steam achievements I've ever seen. It has a full soundtrack for every game and the collection itself built it. It has both NA and JP versions of all game along with all localisations/languages. It includes the full gamut of artwork for all games and the collection itself. It even includes full-angle images of all the applicable Mega Man X merchandise that was ever available for these games. This is, quite simply, a fan/collector's wet dream. Lastly, the game includes an "X Challenge" mode where you get to face off against three waves of two bosses at a time at varying difficulties. Everything about this collection, right down to the menu's animations and sound effects, had an incredible amount of love poured into it.

As for the games themselves, they're all great:

Mega Man X

The masterpiece that started it all. I won't waste your time here, as you should instead check out Egoraptor's entertaining and informative video explaining not only why Mega Man X is incredible but why it's one of the most important games ever made: https://youtu.be/8FpigqfcvlM

Mega Man X2

A direct continuation of Mega Man X that feels almost like an expansion. To me, X2 is a tad awkward in places and lacks the unprecedented polish of the original but it's still an excellent game that's well worth your time. Primary detractor is the optional sub-boss system with unclear restrictions that may cause you to have to restart your game if you go too far without beating them.

Mega Man X3

X3 has more polish than X2 but still slightly less than the original. Good story in its own right and a fun and challenging lb help this entry stand on its own. It also blends what worked from X and X2 well.

Mega Man X4

A phenomenal gameplay experience with well done anime cutscenes. Truly, the only detractor in X4 is the atrocious voice acting... but once you get past that this is one of the best titles in the series and a fast paced joy to play.

X Challenge

A blend of skill and strategy make this an engaging and interesting mode, especially on harder difficulties. If you beat this on Hard, you get an ultra-secret cutscene and a fight against ultimate X & Zero. You can satisfy your achievement requirements by completing this on any mode, however, so it will never have you pulling your hair out.

Seriously though... if you've never played Mega Man X I strongly encourage you to do so. If you already have, this is the definitive compilation and its well worth our money, sale or not. No regrets with this whatsoever. Play this with a controller.\t\t\t\t\t
