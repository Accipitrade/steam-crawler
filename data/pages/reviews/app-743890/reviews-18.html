The game has realy solid level design, althought Sigma stage 1 as a hole is a pain in the ass, I still think the game is mostly great, but, I can't realy say I like it that much, I simply don't find myself having fun with it, but, the soundtrack is no doubt the best thing about this game, and it is pretty much the thing that made me begin to play the MMX games, so it has a special place in my heart.

Megaman X2
My favorite one from the snes trilogy, one day I had decided I was going to play just one level to see how it was, and I ended up playing the hole thing throught in one afternoon. Soundtrack dosen't compare to the first one, but it is still mostly amazing, and that zero theme, oh boy, that got me into listening to the megaman X soundtracks.

Megaman X3
The only one in the collection I don't realy like, as much as I try to, this game "suffers" not realy from bad level design, but that enemies take way too long to kill, be it for X buster beeing ♥♥♥♥ in this game, be it for they just beeing damage sponges, it just gives a false impression that the game is hard, specialy when most of them can pretty much take from 1/4 to 1/3 of your health with one hit. The bosses are definitely the worst part, beeing the easiest in the snes if you abuse of there ♥♥♥♥♥ AI, or beeing the most hard if you just let them go at it, remenber what I said about the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ buster with the low health of X ? The bosses make it worse, much worse. The thing that bothers me the most is the fact that if you get the torso upgrade for X that reduces the damage he takes, all the bosses become actualy managable without becoming too easy. Every time I try to play this game, I just rush to the torso upgrade so I can finish the bosses buster only, and I can safely say that the torso part just makes the game way more fun to play. X3 has many more issues, but overall, I think it can be fun if you know what to do, but that doesn't make the game better.

Megaman X4
This used to be my favorite one of the series, considering how good level design was, and the new mechanic of holding the dash buttom to perform a "dash jump" is the best thing ever introduced in the series to me, specialy considering how "clunky" the normal dash is. The reason X4 isn't my favorite one anymore is mostly because of the gameplay, because after I played X6, with no doubt the best playable zero in the MMX series, coming back to X4 was realy... interesting. Starting the game, zero just sucks, plain out sucks, there are some bosses in the beggining of the game that just suck to fight not because of there design, but because zero sucks to play as, only becoming better to play after you kill the mushroom lad, of which can luckly be killed as early as the second boss. Why not kill him first you ask ?... if you've played the game, you know damn well why. The game after the last fight with colonel is just... mhe, not that it sucks, but I always stop playing after that, the fight with Iris is kind cool, The fight agains double is realy good, and the one with general as X is stupidly easy, and with zero is stupidly boring,and the final level is just your standard kill the bosses again to extend play time and the fight with sigma is extremely hard on your first time, untill you discover how to abuse his AI and he becomes extremely easy. MMX4 is still the gem of this collection, but I can't lie that I do not love it as much as I used too.

Completing it
From the 52 challenges in the game, none realy bothered me, they were a nice way of getting an excuse to play the games again, and the extra mode with the boss fights is a realy nice addition.

Conclusion
Worth it, to some, this first part of the collection is the "one with the good games", although I some what agree with that, even with the inumerous problems X3 has, I still think that the collection is more than worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
