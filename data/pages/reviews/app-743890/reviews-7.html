\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed on Steam Deck using GE-Proton7-41. There will be no audio in the animated cutscenes for X4, but otherwise the Collection works great on Deck.

First thing, technical quality.
People aren't wrong when they say there's some input delay in these ports. But I personally did not find it insurmountable. It was very annoying in X1 because I know that game very well and it just threw me off the whole time. X2 and X3 I don't know so well, so I adjusted to it better. X4 I'd never played before so idk if it's the playstation emulator or just the game, but it really felt like ass to play. Dash jumps felt way off, and I just had to give up on dash-jumping from walls. Just couldn't do it with any consistency.

This collection really is the games just as they were when they released. You still get slowdown in the SNES games. There are no save states. You still can't save progress in the Sigma stages. The only nod to QOL you'll find here is not having to write down the passwords on the SNES games as at least that gets saved for you.

I did not run into any crashes, glitches, or bugs in the game itself. No issues other than no sound in the X4 cutscenes, a very common problem on Linux. I doubt you'll have any issues if you play on Windows.

And now the games themselves. None of them are outright bad, but each entry is worse than the last.
X1 is basically peak Mega Man. One of the best games ever in my opinion.
X2 is nearly as good. I could even see people liking it better, but this is my review.
X3 is also excellent in most respects, but dragged down by artificial difficulty. Everything generally just does more damage and takes longer to kill, making it pretty frustrating at times.
X4 takes a turn for the worse. It isn't to the point I would call it "bad" but is definitely a big disappointment. As I said above, it controls pretty poorly relative to the other games. I also don't like the level design, don't like the healing at checkpoints, don't like the extremely forgiving continue system, and don't like that subweapons don't have enough ammo to kill the boss that is weak to them. Zero is finally a fully playable character, but he's clearly unfinished. He controls even worse than X, doesn't get armor upgrades, at least half his skills are useless, and the bosses were pretty obviously designed around X, not Zero's melee.

So do I recommend it? Yes, but probably on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
