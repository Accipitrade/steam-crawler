\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to recommend this game. I really do. The presentation is very slick, it looks nice and easy to navigate. It mostly includes the US and Japanese versions of the games*. There's some fun extras, achievements, art galleries, etc. However, there are some flaws that add up that prevents me from being able to give it a complete recommendation.

The first, is that the control schemes are limited. You aren't allowed full keyboard customization. This is something that is an automatic fail for me. Being able to adjust controls is very important, especially on PC games. Even beyond that, there is no sound control - though this game is fortunately not as loud as the original Mega Man Legacy Collection pair. The volume is reasonable, but it would still be nice to adjust it. There are limited video resolutions, and 1360x768 - a fairly common native resolution in smaller LCD screens, is not one of them. And, honestly, I'd like to see the different versions of some of the games available. For example, Mega Man X3 was released on 32 bit systems as well as the SNES, but we only get the SNES version here (as opposed to the previous collection that had the 32-bit version.) (It also lacks the Battle & Chase game that set had... though some may consider that a positive.)

But the biggest issue I have by far, and the one that means I cannot recommend this set, is that the games run slow for me. At least the SNES ones. There are several suggested fixes, running it in full screen windowed mode instead of full screen, turning off vsync - which has to be done by editing the .ini file - but they didn't fix the problem. The game IS measurably, noticeably slow compared to running it on an emulator.

I also don't understand why they decided to break this into two games, instead of including all 8 under one banner in order to merge the special features even better.

So many collections lately are so close, yet so far away, and unfortunately this set from Capcom is one of those. It had every opportunity to be perfect. But they didn't spend enough time on the actual functionality of the collection, and the performance issues make it a disappointment. If Capcom fixes these issues, I would be more than happy to change my opinion. The games are fun, and worth playing. Just not in this state.

*Mega Man X4 does not actually have the proper, full Japanese release. Apparently there are licensing issues with some of the songs, so the Japanese version contains the US intro and exit songs still.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played Megaman X just fine and it is awesome. I feel it flowing better than when I played it emulated on my PC after I fixed the slowdown issue by changing some game configurations. There definetly could have been better control settings, since you can't change keys normally via an ingame menu, but even using A, Z and X to dash, jump and shoot I played pretty well. I'm beggining to think that the steam community has become overly sensitive, the control settings is a real smaller issue but not as big as the community wants it to make it. The slowdown is annoying but not gamebreaking nor something you would call as an "unbearable" flaw AND there's a workaround to it.

The devs should definetly fix these issues but being overly loud and vocal is not the best way to get it noticed.

To workaround the slowdown issue go to the config.ini of the game and change the following section.

[CPU]
JobThread=15
RenderingThread=ON

For some idiotic unknown reason the default job thread is 7 but if you change it to 15 it will work just fine.

The default path to find config.ini is usually the following:

C:\\Program Files (x86)\\Steam\\steamapps\\common\\Mega Man X Legacy Collection

Or where ever you have your steam library, just go to ...\\steamapps\\common\\Mega Man X Legacy Collection just in case you dont know where to look.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far, so good! Solid entry in the Mega Man PC release line-up.

Good:
-UI's simple, clean and efficient. Jump in and out of games quickly, they fire up instantly. Huge A+, probably my favorite UI in the Legacy Collections so far
-Games play fairly identically to original hardware; MMX4 is super snappy, almost no loadtimes
-Low-end frequency tweaks to MMX1-3. More bass, more brutal bangers, great move
-No input lag! Even with a wireless controller, games feel SUPER responsive, only took six dashing jumps to get arm upgrade in MMX1, tying my previous record on a SuFami
-1440p scaling is good! Everything looks great
-Museum includes promotional trailers for each game; check out the Rockman X promo for some beta footage!
-MMX ON PC
-No option for MMX3 Saturn soundtrack. Yes, this is absolutely plus for me. Those arrangements were atrocious.

Eh:
-MMX 1-3 do suffer from very occasional frame jitters/skips. Very infrequent however.
-No save states, but they do save passwords
-MMX4 screen options 1 and 2 are identical, no option for original scaling
-Full screen veritcal aspect stretch isn't using integer scaling so you'll see some inconsistent pixel allignment in the best available video output option
-No options to try out bosses in Museum mode like you could in MMLC2
-Capcom USA must not have kept their trailers on file; most of the early entries only have Japanese promos available

Poor form:
-No customizable controls, probably frustrates keyboard enthusiasts

Final notes: It's 2018, frankly it's time for people to spend $15 on a used wired 360 controller (You've been complaining since Castlevania!) so the only huge negative for me's fairly inconsequential, but I wouldn't argue if it came up at some point. Plays great with an Xbox One wireless controller, 1440p looks sharp as heck. Love the interface. Love the games! Mega Man X4 is all hu-ha-hoh, it's not over yet (as it should be). I think the lack of save states may have been an artistic call; save-scumming is a cop-out. Git gud!

Maybe this is a "My Rig Versus Their Rig" issue (My PC's pretty buff) but... I'm thinking people are mistaking the "lag" with how the game performs normally possibly? Everything I tried felt fairly identical to actual hardware performance (maybe even a bit better in spots, but that's possibly my mind playing tricks on me). MMX2 and 3 chug often, and yes, even MMX lags at times, even on a SNES. Emulation performance is about a step below Higan, but honestly I even get frame hitches in that so it's not MUCH worse. Capcom has released much worse.

I'm somewhat of a snob when it comes to MMX emulation, and honestly, these perform admirably across the board, and a huge nod to boosting the bass in the original three titles, really helps drive the awesome tunes. Speaking of which, while people hoist the MMX PS2 collection in their arguments in performance (much of the lag inherent in the original titles is greatly reduced), they fall strangely silent on how NONE OF THE MUSIC in the SNES titles actually loops properly; they have a fade-out and repeat, it's absolutely gross. And comparatively they look much, much... MUCH worse than this assembly. Either they didn't play the old collection and are just repeating things they've read, or they play with the sound off and have beer goggles on and can't see how *bad* they look in general.

Couldn't have asked for much more in this release. Looking forward to spending time going through everything again! Welcome to PC, X. Good to have you!

Windows 10
GTX 1070 Ti
Intel Core i5 8600K
16GB DDR4
Stock clocks across the board\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not sure what all of the negative reviews are about. I'm a long time Mega Man X fan, and was looking forward to this collection for a while. Here was my experience:

Loading up the game
0 issues, loaded up, no crashing. As soon as I got into the menus, I changed the resolution to 1920x1080, turned the smoothing filter off, and made the aspect ratio to the original (I believe the option was type 2). All of the options worked fine, no issues here.

Gameplay
I read people talking about slowdown, frame rate issues, sound issues, etc. I experience NONE OF THIS. If you have played Mega Man X before, you know that the original has some slow down in certain areas (I'm looking at you Armored Armadillo stage). This is to be expected, and I honestly want it there. That same slow down is present in this collection, but nothing beyond the normal extent of the original game. I feel like half of the people writing the slow down comments hadn't played the original. Gameplay for me so far (beat all 8 bosses in X1 as of writing this), is fantastic.

Overall 10/10
Overall this collection for me is a 10/10. I have about 2 hours in so far, and will spend tons more. As a long time fan of the series, I've been wanting the X collection on PC - and I'm not disappointed at all.

I'll say the ONLY issue I have so far, which I'm sure will get fixed, is when I click on the achievements under my library, it goes to my steam profile, not the achievement list.

I also got the wallpapers (which are amazing) - they're in the folder where the game is stored.

If you're a fan of the series, do yourself a favor and buy this collection.\t\t\t\t\t
