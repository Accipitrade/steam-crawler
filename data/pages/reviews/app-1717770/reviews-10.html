What I liked: The gameplay is incredibly fun for just being a little guy running around and throwing boxes. It's rewarding in later levels when an enemy that was the focus of an older level becomes shown often because you really feel like you've learned. In my opinion, the game has just the right amount of "learning what to do for yourself". Figuring things out in this game was a very nice experience. I like the character designs, while simplistic they're very fun and endearing. MOST of the levels felt fresh and fun, and I especially enjoyed both the fake outdoors one with the western theme and the arcade.

It's both a pro and a con to me that you can skip levels that you find too difficult.

What I didn't like: The couple levels in the last part of the game didn't have the aforementioned fresh and fun feeling. Except the bird flight one and the little guys that hide in the dark. Otherwise it just felt like I was kindve just slugging through this to get to the ending. Speaking of the ending, while I enjoyed the final dialogue with Ik and the reveal to why everything in the hotel is.. like that, the horse thing RUINED my enjoyment of it. Your character just evaporating as a random horseman steps into the road isn't fun or rewarding. I know the tone of the game is consistently goofy, but you can tell a comedy story while having a fulfilling ending. \t\t\t\t\t

There was one in particular where you are on a floor inhabited by spiders that move very quickly and sound like children, and the only way to avoid them is to use a light. That got under my skin, which not many games have been able to do.
  • You defeat enemies by throwing furniture at them, and it never becomes boring.
  • Wacky storyline where you don‚Äôt know what‚Äôs going to happen next.
  • Good length (5+ hours) at a very fair price.
