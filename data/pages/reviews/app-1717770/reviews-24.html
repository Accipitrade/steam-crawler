\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLiterally one of the best games I've played all year. The game is about 7-8 hours, about what you'd expect for a $10 dollar game and an overall fair price if not a bit low... On to the game itself; it's amazing. It's not easy to join comedy and horror but this game does it perfectly. I'll try to brief you a little bit about it, but I won't spoil anything past the first ten minutes.
tl;dr You're a dead guy trying to find your room in the undead hotel.

Gameplay:
The gameplay is based around funny and chaotic throwing physics, which is (uncommonly) spiced up with some short puzzles. The horror aspect of this game is the absolute chaos. One minute you'll be having fun smashing every piece of furniture in the room to bits, and the next you'll be chased by a horrifying horde of enemies, frantically picking up and throwing any item in sight to stop them.

Difficulty:
This game hits right at home with the difficulty imo. It's hard enough so that the chaotic moments are truly chaotic and scary. It's also not too hard, which would ruin the vibe of the game and it's humor. You will die perhaps 15-35 times throughout the game depending on your skill level. This may not sound like a lot, but for a seven hour playthrough it's about what I'd hope for. I never felt frustrated throughout this game, which is partly because of how much fun I was having throughout it.

Humor:
The dialogue is funny, the levels are funny, there's few levels in which I didn't laugh at least once. Even when the jokes do run stale (they don't often), the physics and chaotic nature of the game is enough to laugh at in and of itself. SPOILER WARNING: As an example, there is a part where you're trying to do platforming while being chased. Mid chase, one of the characters calls you and asks if you want to hear his "banger beats". If you say yes, he will blast it on the loud speaker, as the base of the song makes the entire building shake and makes the platforming way harder. It's a hilarious moment.

Price:
As I said, I think $10 is fair if not underpriced for this game. I'm pretty stingy when it comes to money, but this game was well worth it. Since it's only about seven hours long, I can't price range this any higher than $20 if it killed me. That being said, it's definitely worth the price, even if you're cheap.

Story:
The story isn't that serious, but enough to keep you interested. You want to find out how you died, and the future of humanity including your own life is slowly unveiled to you each floor you ascend. Admittedly, the ending left me a bit confused, but maybe I just missed something. Nevertheless, it felt like a proper ending, and overall the story was good, but not great. I wouldn't consider it by any means as a flaw, but it is probably the only weakpoint of this game, if any.

Replayability:
Well... it's about what you would expect. Considering it's part comedy, a lot of that comedic effect will have worn off if you tried replaying it. There are secret collectibles that allow you to play bonus levels, which does help boost the replayability (for the record, I have not tried any yet,) That being said, they most likely won't add more than an 1-3 hours (depending on how many collectibles you missed). That being said, I don't think anyone expected great replayability. Horror games generally don't have great replayability due to the loss of the fear factor, and comedy games suffer the same issue. The replayability isn't bad, just okay.

10/10 game and the best $10 spent in a very long time... and that's coming from someone pretty short on cash. If you don't believe me, check the reviews lol (at the time of writing this, there are 158 reviews, and zero negative reviews). I RARELY give out a 10/10 rating, because I consider that as stating "this game has no flaws"... But in this case, I can't think of one thing I disliked about this game. And on the off chance that the dev is reading this, thank you for making this! I look forward to seeing your future works (if you plan on continuing this route).\t\t\t\t\t
