The story feels only at the start and the end because all the questions you have at the start are only answer at the end and doesn't feel that you are advancing for the lower number that the floors have before the sudden increase of the numbers floor at the end.

The mayority of bonus levels doesn't feel bonus levels, just experimental levels, with this a refer like the glass pane bonus level. You just have to press the levels until appears the item you want, its boring and doesn't feel a level. Other example is the magnet and ball with light level, is creative and has potential but the puzzle is to short and isn't well timed, the door closes before you can arrive with the ball.
There are good levels like the it steals and survive level, but there are only the minory. The other bonus levels only feel like normal or experimental and short levels.

Also i think that the system for obtainig the bonus levels is bad, becuase you depend of collecting all chocolates on 1 especific level, so there are many levels that you can't ignore completing it 100%. I think that it will better to implement a system which depending of how chocolates, or levels with all chocolates, unlock you new levels for giving more reward for completing levels with chocolates.

Also all levels that has chocolates need to have a mark in the selecting menu and when you have all the chocolates of that level, its have to change. It feels like the game doesn't wanna be completed at 100% or at least indicate you that you have completed all.

There are some objects like the fan that aren't explored and are just used like 1 or 2 puzzle and a chocolate. The idea has potential, imagine a levle where you have to go in a moving giant fan and you have to dodge obstacles going up and down or the bird level but with a portable fan and you have to handle it and change direction for avoiding obstacles.

Or the water idea, it just feels annoying at the mayority of the time, only is good on the cocodrile and tide level, becuase it slows you and nothing more, it doesn't affect enemies or has other interaction.

It feels like these elements just appears for no reason in the mayority of its levels with no real use.

It's good that is basic at the start but its never grows in complexity or difficult. I know that isn't the main of the gameplay but it's the more revelant seccondary aspect of the gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpturned is one of the most genuinely just fun and enjoyable experiences I've had in a video game over the past few years. There was genuinely never a dull moment, not a single level that I did not enjoy playing through. Additionally, it manages to seamlessly blend the Horror and 3D Comedy Platformer genres together so surprising success. I was not left in pure terror, but I was consistently scared, and I think that's exactly what Zeekerss was aiming for.

While it may be going to sing the praises of Upturned too highly for many, personally this game came at a really good point in my life. I was at pretty low point mentally, and the message Upturned brings to the table, and the overall enjoyment of it's characters, game-play, and really every part of the game; it really helped to pull me out of a bad spot. So, if you find yourself in a bad spot- I especially recommend it for that.\t\t\t\t\t
