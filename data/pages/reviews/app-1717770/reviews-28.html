\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe humour and animation in The Upturned make it one of the few games I've played that nails the mix of slapstick comedy with genuine horror.
Timing is so important to both suspense and humour, and this game doesn't miss a beat.

The main story is easy to follow with the help of great and funny writing, while the grand picture may lend itself to an open interpretation, culminating in what I found to be an satisfying conclusion.

The bite-sized nature of each level lets you forgive some of the more brutal ones, and while there's a level select option if a level is being rather nasty, using it may come at the expense of missing a bit of story.

I do wish the gamepad controls were a bit more tight.
Steam does help with the lack of button remapping.
I found it much more comfortable moving Jump and Flashlight to the triggers, while changing the Grab button to a toggle instead of a hold.
But the lock-on system fails to target enemies more than it should.
Combat can be frantic enough that even if it would auto-target the closest enemy, and then allow for switching to other targets with the right analogue, encounters would still remain challenging.

One thing that could be better explained is the use of the Chocolate Bars.
Collecting all Chocolate Bars in one level is supposed to unlock the bonus version of that level, but you're only told this at the end of the game, and you cannot access those bonus levels before that.
Although it would be great that, after completing the game, the level select menu would show how many Chocolate Bars are missing from each of the regular levels.

At the time of this review, those bonus levels are still being added, but don't think the game feels incomplete because of that.
This is an enjoyable experience that I'll be glad to come back later when all of those extra levels have landed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRefreshing.

It's becoming increasingly rare for a story game to actually provide a sense of closure and to tie up loose ends. This game does that.
This game doesn't end on a cliff hanger to set up the next game in the series, it wraps the whole story up into a nice little bow and that's it. "Thanks for playing."

Often the pacing in story games don't feel like they've reached the climax yet or that they're still building up to something before having the credits roll.
This game's pacing was perfect. I knew exactly where I was in the story until the very end.

It's very difficult for me to get into games nowadays since they often have glaring issues even years after launch.
I bought this game at launch and then played it a few months later, I ran into a few bugs but overall I'd say the game's design was effectively perfect.

Good job, Zeekerss.
I look forward to your next masterpiece.\t\t\t\t\t
