I kept playing because I wanted to get the "Completed the journal" achievment, but after almost 15 hours of gameplay, I'm still far away from that. I would still recommend this game though, but only at a steep discount.\t\t\t\t\t

For me the price was certainly worth it - I've payed way more than 25 bucks for way less than 6 hours of this kind of fun, and I haven't finished the game yet.

If you want a challenge, high stakes, a deep, dark plot, if you hate collectibles or side quests - this game is not for you. It's a little bit like a visual novel, with the added bonus of getting to pet dogs periodically.

If the devs ever release a DLC with new mushing routs or dogs, I will 10,000% be buying it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo... it's cute. Extremely cute. But the actual gameplay is.... severely lacking.

You are playing a young woman who has decided on impulse to go to Alaska, adopt some dogs, and sled out to a cabin in the wilderness to live. Your goal is to get there alive. You have to find resources as you go. These resources are presented to you as random encounters--and they are not well optimized. I made three runs and only was presented the chance to collect firewood twice, which resulted in spending most of the game eating raw meat (and suffering a status effect for it)... despite being in a forest, filled with trees.

Among these random encounters include opportunities to bond with your dogs, or fulfill their "quest". This is the bulk of the game, since once you reach the cabin, all you can really do is go on more runs to complete each dogs quest. After that... that's the game. So essentially you are repeating a bunch of random encounters over and over again, hoping you get the ones you need for some doggy questing, while eating raw meat because the game won't give you any firewood to cook with.

It took me about 100 minutes to get to the cabin (three runs). By then I was so bored with the gameplay that I really didn't want to bother trying to unlock each dog's story, as cute as the dogs themselves are.

I was really expecting something more fully fleshed out given the price tag this game comes with. Sure it's pretty, but even walking simulators have more content in them.\t\t\t\t\t
