\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I really enjoyed this game! Beautiful scenery, very simple UI and easy to make choices that can result in some very very interesting encounters/stories. Having the journal to log special moments is a wonderful idea, I adore it. The animation is cute even though it is relatively simple. Voice acting is amazing. The atmosphere is divine.

Pros:
+ very adorable doggos with different appearances and personalities
+ dogs have their own story lines and eventually accessories
+ first person POV + voice acting really immerses you
+ entertaining even as an adult player (and I could see it being very riveting for younger audiences, too).
+ encounters/events are a mix of choice and RNG, meaning you can have multiple outcomes to the same 'scene'
+ survival system is simple to understand but mastering resources takes multiple runs
+ beautiful story; hopeful, emotional, and heartfelt without having to resort to tragedy

Cons:
- very short story. Once you reach the cabin that is pretty much it-- I was expecting to have at least a few other maps or even some trouble at the cabin to manage. Really get settled into the MC's new life as a musher, you know?
- repeat runs are pretty fun for dogs you haven't yet gotten on your team, but their personal stories are very short and only need one good complete run to finish their part.
- cost. I happily bought the game at full price (think it was about $27 USD for me), but for almost $30 I was expecting more content. Not a whole lot, but I think I finished a full run within 1-2 hrs of the game. The other hours I have spent running with other dogs to unlock their stories but it is getting repetitive.


Hopefuls:
~ being able to have different maps for different runs
~ tasks to do at home (supply runs, working with the dogs, dealing with encounters around the cabin or at home)
~ some dog sled racing story to continue on with the MC's dream. Nothing crazy long, but a mix of cooperation and antagonistic encounters for that kind of content would be so wonderful.
- if other content is added, perhaps looking into some more systems for 'training/working' to become better? I love the way progression works in the game; doing practice runs or learning from races to learn how to build a good team (maybe include personalities/clashing dynamics, balancing resources for owning more dogs, etc).


This game is beautiful and I have enjoyed the first 3 hrs immensely. If the price drops even to about $20 USD, I would be encouraging friends and family to purchase and try it out. It is the kind of game that sticks in your mind-- with some more content, I think this game could become very very memorable and even more emotionally connected with the players.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is cute. It managed to take the roguelike formula and cleverly use it to make an ambient narrative experience. It does this by letting the experiences you have out on the trail turn into prep for future runs. This prep gradually reduces the boundary to fail, until you are a resource rich enough that most threats are a non issue. This way, it keeps even the least rigorous players moving through new content at a decent pace.

There are only three downsides of this little gem:
-Its pretty short? Both time wise, and in terms of content I got to see. I wanted more narrative, more character development. I really loved the parts of this character that were in the trailer, and the parts you see in game. But there just wasn't enough to make me feel like it was actually there. This was made better/worse by the fact that Ashley Burch did an incredible job, as always, with the voice acting. Got excited as soon as I heard her voice in the trailer.
-You're not actually racing in the Iditarod. That was one of the things that made me want to play the game, was the idea of going on this super cool endurance race with my dog bugs. Instead you're going on like a three day training run to a cabin that was left to you. When you finish your 'training run', the credits roll, and its not like you can then race in an actual dog race. You can just replay the training run repeatedly.
-♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ bears. Because the mechanics aren't really deep at all, and all of the progression elements you find SIGNIFICANTLY declaw said mechanics, whats left is to just punish the player by rolling dice. This takes the form of you just sometimes randomly getting mauled by a bear/gored by a muskox. In these events, you can sometimes walk away with some food, but mostly you just get injuries. Injuries are important because if you get 2, you die. You can fix them with medkits, but you only have 1 medkit until more than halfway through the progression ladder, and you don't really find more. This means that sometimes you lose your run, even though you have tons of food, extra bullets, and lots of other supplies, because you roll three unavoidable damage encounters in the same game, which is very common. I almost stopped playing because I lost two consecutive runs this way when I otherwise would have cruised through to the end. The first run I won was the run where I started with 2 medkits. It *really* feels like they did this just to prevent players from winning on their first try and refunding the game if I'm being honest, which is pretty frustrating. My last run wasn't really fun, and I was reading something on the other monitor while listening to repeated dialogue, and just clicking whatever. I finished with like 18 meat, 4 bullets, a wounded dog, and my character was also injured, meaning that I was one injury from losing, again. Major points lost for this sloppy design.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a bit mixed on this game. Because it's definitely overpriced, and even if it wasn't, it's definitely too short for my tastes. After just 3 hours, it seems as though you've already seen the majority of what the game has to offer, barring teeny tiny (and grindy) pet stories that require you to go through the same content over and over again just to get 3-4 or so events related to a given pet. But the 3 hours that I played were really fun and relaxing. I really wish there were more maps to delve into once you beat the game once, as that would more than have motivated me to keep going. But once you've reached the cabin, it seems as though that's just kind of it. You can go out onto the same trail or adopt a new dog, only to go out into the same content again. Which is too bad.

Sooo, this is a very tentative "recommended", or rather a "I recommend this game if it goes on a 50% or more sale".\t\t\t\t\t

+ It won't make you feel depressed like other games that "lure you in" with cute animal characters to push someone's nightmares on you. It's quite relaxing.
+ You have an option to toggle dog death off. Thanks for that.
+ The game gets even more enjoyable after the main storyline. Cudos for that!
+ No stress gameplay. Fair, out to teach folks not to give up. Well done!
+ Dogs have different personalities, you get to choose your team that fits you the best. Like!

- Is there any? Probably the limited gameplay, but that's how the game was made, so no complaints here just wishes for some more adventure.
- I really hate the graphics. With nowadays technology put yourself out there and animate correctly. Please.

Overall it was totally worth the purchase and I give my heartfelt recommendation to anyone who loves dogs.
I will keep playing it and hoping for a musher game with much better quality graphics and animations, some open-world adventure, maybe a bit of competition in the end to show off the great team I put together. I'd totally keep the gameplay elements, it is fun and surprising. One can dream. Anyways, thanks for the game, I really enjoy it.\t\t\t\t\t
