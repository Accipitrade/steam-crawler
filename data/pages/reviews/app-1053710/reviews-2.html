\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very atmospheric game, gives me SO much nostalgia for my childhood in a cold part of the world. I really appreciated that it balanced the reality of wilderness survival without going over the top edgy about it; you have to respect the wildlife and the environment because it has genuine dangers, but at the same time, the vast majority of wildlife has no desire to go out of their way to hurt you unless you disturb them first. It's really sweet getting to know the different dogs' personalities and I love the little details that show up in the journal as you discover more about the dogs and the wildlife. It's obvious that a lot of care went into it.

It's cool that I could totally imagine myself as a kid being obsessed with this game, and I still enjoy it a lot now. There's something refreshing about it just being a way to experience a journey through the wilderness with your dogs; a lot of different people can enjoy it for different reasons. I do think it would be most fun for people who like to play games through the eyes of the character and imagine their own story alongside what the game gives you. People who prefer more structure, or who like super strategic survival games, might not feel like it's the right kind of challenge for them. I absolutely love the more sentimental/creative/poetic vibe of this game, personally, and I would recommend it to anyone who also tends to like that kind of atmosphere.

I think it'd be great to see a sequel or additional content where you either get to repair/maintain the cabin using supplies foraged during your runs, or where you can participate in long-distance races along different routes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a bit mixed on this game. Because it's definitely overpriced, and even if it wasn't, it's definitely too short for my taste. After just 4 hours, it seems as though you've already seen the majority of what the game has to offer, barring teeny tiny (and grindy) pet stories that require you to go through the same content over and over again just to get 3-4 or so events related to a given pet. But the 4 hours that I played were really fun and relaxing. I really wish there were more maps to delve into once you beat the game once, as that would more than have motivated me to keep going. New events, new dangers, perhaps a sort of resource management game with the cabin where you actually have to live in the cabin, I would've loved that. But once you've reached the cabin, it seems as though that's just kind of it. You can go out onto the same trail or adopt a new dog, only to go out into the same content again. Which is too bad. A great concept, definitely. But I personally thought there'd be more to it. If you want something to chill out with for a few hours, fine, go for it. But I wouldn't pay full price for it, wait for it to go on sale!\t\t\t\t\t
