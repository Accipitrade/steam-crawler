\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRecommended with *huge* caveats.

**Do Not** buy this game if you are expecting an action-adventure game similar to The Long Dark or Green Hell or another other Survival genre game out there where you actively explore, loot, hunt, fish and craft. This isn't that kind of game.

Roguelite- expect to die often. Food does not go far, and you will die of starvation over and over, as your hunting opportunities are all scripted encounters. The hunting mini-game kinda sucks too- you will have few bullets, and some big critters like a caribou may turn out to be bullet sponges- leaving you with no food and no bullets. You'll die, and you'll start again.

The game is more of a Visual Novel with a "Survive in the wilds" flavor to it.
"Choices Matter"- well, yes and no. Some situations and encounters seem to have scripted encounters or results, no matter what choice you make, while others have differing results.You only get to choose what direction you "explore" in at forks in the road- you choose left or right. You do not get to explore the entire world- which is a shame, since it is a beautiful world.

The audio is quite good, as is the voice acting.
You can pet the doggos.
The music is quite nice.
I really like the stylized art style of the game.

But it *is not* a survival game, It is a guided story, linear, with some differences in what you will find and where you will find the,m based on "Gee" and "Haw" choices at forks in the road. You are not going to be fist-fighting a wolf, or killing every yummy-looking animal along the way. You are going to find new things, meet new critters, learn new things each time, then die, and start again with the new items and knowledge.

There are no good guides for the game, and it looks like the developers may have abandoned the game, or run into development interruptions. They were active on the forums when the game first released, but there has been no response to player questions in some time.

It's still a beautiful game that runs well pn my older machine with an 19 9900K/ 2070 RTX Super/16 GB DDR4 RAM- though it does not run at a steady 144 fps (setting it to a max 61 fps via the NIVIDIA control panel does make it run smoothly at 60 fps). It is a chill game- not high action.

Knowing what you are buying and not expecting it to be a TheLogDarkDogSleddingSim is key. I recommend it only at a deep discount, since any bugs or technical issues may or may not be dealt with if the devs have ghosted. But I can see sinking 10-20 hours into the game trying to complete the story and the journal, in short-ish sessions- so 50% off or more, maybe, if hours/$ matter to you.

I don't love it all (only parts of it), but I don't hate it either- just a bit frustrated with the hunting minigame and lack of food availability for you and your dog team if no hunting situations come up in your travels. Expect to die and restart a good number of times.\t\t\t\t\t

wonderfully immersive and cosy

all the dogs are adorable and interesting in their own ways, and i ended up loving even the ones i didn't care much for at the start. the journey to the home (which can be considered the "meat" of the gameplay) gets a little repetitive after you've done it many times; you need to complete it 9 times to unlock all the dogs' accessories, and unless you're very efficient, several times more to check off everything in the journal. neither of these are really needed however and the dog-specific events at least give the runs some variety.

apart from that i found the survival aspect somewhat difficult at first but after a few (possibly failed) runs you start to get the micromanagement between food, bullets, etc. and never really have to worry about it again. after that it's just about exploring the different choices you're presented during the events, and trying to get all the outcomes to fill the journal.

overall it's a very fun, chill game that i was able to complete (apart from a few remaining journal notes) in about 10 hours, although i did also take the time to pet each dog every time we set up camp and every time we got back to the house, which probably accounts for at least an hour. the only bugs that ever showed up were minor visual glitches that didn't affect anything.

screw the ball toss minigame though\t\t\t\t\t
