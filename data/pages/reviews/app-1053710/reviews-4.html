\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had heard of this game briefly before I saw it on the Steam sale yesterday, but decided to take a leap of faith with it without much research. I can say that I'm glad I did.

The Red Lantern has a bit of a learning curve that can be frustrating. Until you reach your destination at the end of your first true run, you can feel trapped repeating the same choices. The key is to let loose and take as many different routes as possible. Once you start gaining tools and learning how to ration your supplies, the game gets a LOT more fun.

I love the art style. The clear Alaskan night sky is one of my favorite parts of the game. The dogs are distinctly different from each other and each bring something new to the table. You have the choice in the options menu to keep all the dogs alive if pet death is triggering for you, which I thought was a very nice touch. The tutorials are VERY fast, maybe a little too fast at times (it took me four runs and a guide on how to play this game on the switch for me to figure out the controls on my laptop with an xbox controller.)

That being said, this game is controller compatible. I finished the main run in about two and a half hours of gameplay, but I still have many things in the journal to discover as well as the dogs' stories. I understand not wanting to spend the full price on this game just because it is so short, but if you are like me and happen to catch it on sale, it is absolutely worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
