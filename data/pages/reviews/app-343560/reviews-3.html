\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYea, I only have 3 hours on record...
But... this... thing...
It scares me.

You think Getting Over it will drive you insane..? HA!
The deceptively inviting facade of this cute little ball of... I don't know... is it a meatball of everything it eats that's just really really stale? Whatever it is, don't mistake it to be a creature worth aiding in its neigh pointless quest to retrieve its foods stores on its ship and go home. This game is a rage game to rival all rage games. 3 hours in and I was just barely short of the thousand deaths achievement. Perhaps that's a statement of how bad I am at the game, but this isn't the first platform game I've dove into. Seriously, read the achievement statistics. Past the second world, you WILL have a thousand deaths to your name. I can say that with educated certainty.

There is a certain element of trial and error in a game that's supposed to be rewarding. Its not here. This game hardly knows what trial is. It's the backbreaking strain of figuring out how on earth the ice-like platforming (yea, before you get to the ice levels) is fair, let alone possible. The countless saws, drills and spikes you going to make sweet love to wont even be all that long before you set foot in the level. It's almost like a shooter game. Holding still gets you killed, moving gets you killed, being stupid gets you killed, and getting killed GETS YOU KILLED.

The completionist doesn't know about this game and shouldn't. I don't wish this game on any poor bastard that dares pay for this game with the intent to play it rather than give the creators a chance to redeem themselves.

Unless your a sadist... than hate yourself all you want. I'm not doing it. You can't make me.
