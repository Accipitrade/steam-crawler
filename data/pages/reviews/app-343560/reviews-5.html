\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like this game. I really do. But the game suffers from a serious fault: the three-dimensional graphics don't fit this game. The entire gameplay is two-dimensional, and the 3-D environment leads to fake difficulty. (I tried the very first hard level, but first, the camera didn't catch enough of the playing area - not even after I waited a couple of seconds for it to zoom out; second, your view was blocked by a tree in the foreground. As a result, you were forced to blindly jump and hope that you won't hit the moving enemy that you can't see.)

Give me a way to switch to 2-D, and I'll gladly recommend it. The way it is, the game stands as a example that less is sometimes better.\t\t\t\t\t
