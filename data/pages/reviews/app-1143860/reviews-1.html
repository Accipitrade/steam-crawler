\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun, satirical little Hotline Miami-esque Topdown Shooter. The gimmick of the game is to run around randomly generated floors, completing tasks and trying not to die. Every task you complete, however, eats at your health bar and it will drain until it's at 25% or lower. You can drink random cups of coffee scattered around the office to restore health, but this drains your "bathroom" meter. You, of course, refill the bathroom meter by taking bathroom breaks. More on this system later.

The music is very fitting for a satirical office setting, sounding less like the zombie apocalypse is raging on and more like you're taking a trip to an actual office with easy listening playing from speakers one could vaguely hear through the noises of said office. The graphics are fine and get the job done, and the ragdolls of zombies hitting the ground and becoming spaghetti are way better than any amount of dismemberment can provide.

Now, to survive, you have a very small arsenal of 5 weapons (6, counting your fists, which aren't useful,) to get through 8 levels.
- The Pistol, which is only slightly better than your fists and takes 2-3 shots to kill even the weakest zombies. Good for the first and second level, but is better replaced by....
- The Shotgun, a staple of zombie shooters. A notably kickass weapon that fires several projectiles, and can easily one-shot a group of zombies. Easily the best weapon in the whole game.
- The SMG. A weapon that's fast, but inaccurate and takes 2-3 shots to kill all but the weakest zombies. More on inaccuracy later.
- The Assault Rifle. Unfortunately, the third least useful option, as it fires in bursts of 3, meaning it's a waste against single targets 9 times out of 10. It does good damage, but it's inefficient ammo usage makes it a liabilty.
- The Minigun. Basically, it's an SMG, but consistently 1-shots most threats.

Now, in addition to completing all tasks and leaving, there are side objectives in every level you have to do if you want 100% completion. But, they're all the same. Namely....
- Finish all tasks
- Don't go into overtime (complete the level before time runs out)
- No Coffee Breaks
- No Bathroom Breaks.

Given that doing jobs eats up your health as mentioned earlier, 100% completion requires one to complete all levels at critical health levels. This wouldn't be so bad if it wasn't for one particularly pesky zombie type. Namely, The Charger, distinguished by his Red Shirt. He will bolt at the player and tackle them for huge amounts of damage. This wouldn't be so bad if he couldn't charge while he's outside of the player's line of sight, but he absolutely can. The Later Levels make him harder to distinguish because every zombie other than The Spitter is coated in yellow-y wounds.

I've had several cracks at perfect running The Last Level ruined by no less than three charging out the darkness at once. Look, I understand making a challenge centered around "no healing" but can we at least not have doing objectives drain health? This is compounded with how zombie spawns work, which seem to operate off of Killing Floor 2 rules. I.E.- Zombies can spawn anywhere you can't see them. Given a good chunk of your screen will be coated in a darkness simulating line of sight, this can get frustrating.

Also, it may just be me, but the hit detection feels very strict for the player, especially with the fists. It can be very annoying to "miss" a zombie if it looks like the barrel of my gun is touching the zombie's skull. However, zombies seem not to have this problem and can hit the player even if it looks like you can skirt around it's attack.

But despite this, you can definitely do a lot worse for less than $5 USD. This is a perfectly good game and I hope to see more top-down shooters from the dev in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn the simplest of levels, this isn't an awful top down shooter. At the price point, you could do way worse, but here are my primary frustrations with this game. 1. The hit detection is awful. 2. The use of firearms, especially assault weapons is asking to suddenly be swarmed by about 25 zombies. 3. There are no melee or silenced weapons and the punch feature is just sad. 4. The games difficulty levels don't appear to have any difference at all, if they do, it's truly unnoticeable. The difficulty settings also don't seem to impact the achievements at all. In conclusion, I would probably rate this game somewhere in the middle of the road at its best and at its worst, just poor. I'd recommend sticking with games like Hotline Miami if you're into top down shooters. I'm not planning to return it, but I probably won't play it beyond wrapping up my final achievements.\t\t\t\t\t
