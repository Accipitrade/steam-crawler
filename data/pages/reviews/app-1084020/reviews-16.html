\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first time I encountered TheoTown I thought it was a port of SimCity2000. While it is so much more, I get the impression it's working hard to be a spiritual successor, so it's not a bad way to think of this game. The art style in particular harkens back to that pixel isometric style of the late 90s. But while on the surface it feels and looks like one of those older games, the mechanics are anything but old.

The scope and tools available to simulate a full city are on par with something modern like Cities: Skylines. The sheer amount of available assets are a bit overwhelming if you start in sandbox mode. You have a few data overlays I've never seen before, such as religion and sports, which need to be fulfilled to see your city grow to its fullest potential. Some things like traffic are simulated accurately enough that you can spot congestion and gridlock before you ever need to refer to the data overlay. And city beautification...holy cow, you can paint your city to look just about any conceivable way you can imagine. Artists will have a ball with this game, fitting together the picture perfect city with all the right accents and touches to really make it pop beyond a simple set of street grids and high rises.

At the start, there's a tiered progression system that allows you to focus on each new system one at a time so that you're not overwhelmed. With higher population densities and achievements you're able to unlock gradually more impressive features to build up from a small town into a sprawling metropolitan area (a feature severely missing from city simulation since SimCity 4). Make sure you leave plenty of open space in your early layout to facilitate these new features, otherwise you'll have to demolish subdivisions to fit in services you didn't have before.

And you can cooperatively build cities with your friends online. I have sorely missed this.

A worthwhile contender in the city simulation genre, go ahead and buy this one at full price. You'll enjoy every minute.\t\t\t\t\t
