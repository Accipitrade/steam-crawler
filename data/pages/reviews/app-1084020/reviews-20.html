\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI simply cannot recommend TheoTown as it stands right now.

The game, whilst visually and mechanically reminiscent of SimCity 2000 (with elements of 3000 & 4) is mostly shallow, unexciting and really rather dull.

The systems are half baked at best. Whilst they work to a degree, the do not have any major impact on the long term gameplay. Once you create a small enough city, the funds just keep pouring in. The challenge therefore is non existent even on hard. You'd have to do extremely badly on purpose to fail at this game.

TheoTown therefore is great as a city sandbox. With plug-ins for extra buildings and structures, you can create a visual masterpiece. But if you're looking to be a mayor, plan a city and deal with all the challenges that face you as a result? You won't find it here.

Another issue is this game has the instability of a North Korean Dictator. Even if you update your graphics drivers and Java to the latest version, this game will crash often. You may be lucky and get away with stable gameplay for an hour or more. But most of the time the game will crash in 3-20 minute increments, which very quickly becomes infuriating.

I purchased this game on sale, and only after 2 days worth 7 hours game time, I've already uninstalled it. I may return to it one day, but it will need an awful lot of work at every level to make it worthwhile.

If you want a game that's actually enjoyable, go play Cities Skylines. If you want something old school, SimCity 4 Deluxe often goes for about 79% the base price of TheoTown and has everything this game has and more. SimCity 4 also works mechanically and has stability.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I say 8/10.

tl;dr: Get the game on sale.

I got TheoTown during its first launch on the Google Play Store, sometime back around ~2017. It was free. It was a sweet, humble game, and I spent endless hours as a young teen playing it. When I saw it on steam recently, I was hyped. However, the biggest turn-off was that $5.99 price tag. Since I played for free, I may have a bit of a bias, but I was hesitant with spending that much money on this game. However recently (5.19.2020), it went on sale for 40% off and I decided to get it for $3.59, which I would say is a fair price. I just got it a few hours ago, and of course some things have changed over a couple years. Okay, a lot has changed. But, it's still that good game that I spent my time building cities on, and it still has all the same controls I remember.
As you may see some comments talking about the interface, I've had the same problem. It's not as bad as it seems, but I guess it can get pretty annoying if you need to quickly swap over to another tab.
As a returning player, I was happy to hear the calming piano play in the background as I laid out foundations for homes. The $6 price was a bit of a turn off, and I honestly wouldn't pay that much for this game. Nevertheless, it is still a great game and runs ((mostly)) smoothly. Thank you for bringing this to Steam,\t\t\t\t\t
