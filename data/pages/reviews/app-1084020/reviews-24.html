\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wasn't sure if to mark this as a positive or a negative review, because as flawed as it is, I'm still playing it, but I eventually decided to go with negative for one simple reason: The thing this game does best is make me want to play Simcity 4.

If you take into consideration that this is a former mobile game, it's impressive as hell, for sure, and honestly, you wouldn't guess it just from playing it. If you want to get this as a mobile game? GET IT.
But as a PC game it just feels like anything it tries to do, Simcity already does better to the point where this game feels incredibly lacking at times, and every feature simcity has that this game doesn't jsut feels so much more obivous because of this.

Cons:
-Not as much information is available to you as with Simcity, even when it's information that could be pretty useful. Especially when it comes to transportation. I can't know how much usage I'm getting out of my train station unless it's overloaded.
-The demand for the different kinds of zones is erratic as hell. You could have an intensely high demand for residential or commercial or industrial one second, and no demand for a different kind of zone, and moments later, without you having done anything, it could be completely flipped. This makes my decisions of how to expand my city feel completely pointless.
-The game's sense of scale feels off, A small elementary school only affects the block next to it, and at 9x9 city blocks size, the game had already classified my city as having gone past "City", "Big city", "Metropolis", "Megalopolis", "Small Country", "Big country", "Continent", and given my city the title of "Solar system".
Again, my city was 9 city blocks big. Smaller than a neighborhood in most real cities. This is despite the fact the the game map was definitely big enough to support a full sized, massive city.
-The construction mechanic is clunky. Not so clunky that you can't get a grip on it, but annoyingly inconvenient.
-You don't usually get abandoned buildings in your city but if you do, they're not going anywhere until you demolish them, no matter what happens with RCI demand.
-Even though when you place a bus station it placces them on both sides of the road, you can only have the bus go to one of them. It won't let you reselect it on the same route. Which means a bus can't go back the other way on the same street it came from.
-The transportation mechanic feels off and weird, sometimes when creating a bus route I would see it loop around a bunch of city blocks to reach the next station for no apparant reason, even if there is a far shorter, direct route to the next station.
-When I place a subway station it soemtimes tells me it's being used by a ton of people, to which I wonder "how. I haven't even connected it to the subway lines yet." Which makes me wonder if I even ened to be wasting all that money building subway lines.
-Not enough zoning options!!! You have regular zoning and high desnity zoning. That's it. If you want to create quiet little suburbs with lots of low to the ground houses and scenery? Tough luck, as soon as demand hits, those are all going to be brownstones and densely packed houses.
-Road and transportation infrastructure lacks realism. Highways can have hairpin turns and onramps/offramps are just one way streets that are connected tot he highway at 90 degree angles.
-Lack of save slots. I wanted to play around with the "Disasters" feature but relaized I can't save a city, blow it to bits, then load it like nothing happened. At best I can try to do it really quickly, hope the game doesn't autosave, and exit the ame entirely without saving. Why have disasters in a game you can't save/load?
-Speaking of which, the disasters are really disappointing. There's lots of different kinds but buildings are just suddenly blocks of rubble once they get hit. No fun destruction sequences. Even in simcity 2000 there was at least a somewhat satisfying puff of dust when a building gets demolished. Plus, it usually stays in the disasters overlay for a second, which doesn't even let you witness the destruction until you switch back.
-The weird way it handles Graveyards, landfills, and bus depots. They behave more like a minigame than like a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ service. Why does it keep expecting me to "clear room" in the graveyard when it gets full? How about you just STAY FULL because that's how a graveyard works?! Why do I need to wiggle around my mouse over it and watch the little cremation smoke come up every time?! And why does the button that do it autmaotically cost money?
-The city lacks life. If you click a building, all it really says is what zoning and economic level it is, and what is it the least happy about. Nothing has a name, or interesting details about it, and there are no pedestrians.
-Buses and trains seem to function by a different logic entirely. You need to decide on your bus's routes, but trains will jsut automaticlaly take you to whichever station you need to go.
-SOME building repetition. It's not TERRIBLE like in other games. But I did end up having a LOT of the same high density residential spires splattered across my city.
-I have yet to encounter a game that handles Landmarks correctly and this is no exception. You can mostly only build real world landmarks, and with the exception of generating a certain amount of income and possibly acting as a park for the nearby area, they don't usually serve any purpose, they don't provide workplaces or stuff like that. At least not noticably so.
I want my city to have it's own landmarks, not London or Sydney's! Sure, building Landmarks is optional, but still.
-Nothing generates noise pollution besides industrial buildings and power plants. You could have a fully jammed highway, it'll be nice and quiet according to the overlay.
-The pixel art doesn't hold up to quality when you zoom in too close (but that's natural)
-Buildings change rotation so they are always facing the camera
-You'd probably find more mods for Simcity, just because it's a more well known game. If this even has any. And let's face it, Simcity 4 lives on mods.
-Doesn't unlock certain buildigns like a highschool until your city is pretty big. Which means you're not going to get any wealthy residents during this time because they won't be happy with your level of education.

Pros:
-PIXEL ART :D
-2D graphics means buildings can actually look detailed and good rather than have to worry about being large smooth geometric shapes that don't have too many polygons. I'm looking at you, Cities Skylines.
-Honestly, anything that wasn't explicity mentioned in the cons section is done exceptionally well.
-The ability to turn on/off traffic lights at any intersection. (Though an arguable con would be the fact that this works SO well that it begs the question, why does a city need traffic lights to begin with?)
-Lovely architecture. Buildings actually have different styles, and they're pretty ones.
-The same "style" of building often comes in various different heights! So even if buildings are pretty similar, one cna be short, one can be tall. This adds to the variety.
-Cars seem to care where they're going. That's always a plus.
-While completely janky, the way cosntruction is handled does let you build multi-level overpasses and really tlal roadways if you want. Which is fairly impressive for a grid based game.
-There's not a lot but there ARE some features that Simcity doesn't have. Like religion, or treating sports facilities and parks as two seperate things.
-You can choose a difficulty setting.

Overall, a good yet flawed game, but it tries so hard to be Simcity that at some point the question is asked - why not just play simcity, then?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTheotown.

A fun little classy town-building simulation game developed by a small-time indie company.

This game is very slow-paced and the music reminds me of early 2000's .MIDI files. It is a basic town-simulation game that allows you build cities, often from scratch. Here are the pros and cons:

Pros:
- Very classy music that resemble the early 2000s and its .MIDI.
- Slow-paced city-building game that resembles Simcity and has inspiration from it.
- Lots of small mods to choose from with the in-game mod manager.
- Beautiful retro graphics and design, often simplistic at times.
- Even has two modes known as Free and Sandbox, both allow you construct the city of your dreams without any usage of ingame money.

Cons:
- None.
- Or it could become boring at times.

Other words, GOOD RELAXING CITY-SIMULATOR GAME OF 2019.

also don't buy android version, it forces you to buy random in-app purchases just to get some basic buildings.
PC + Good price = heaven.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is, in my opinion, the best city simulator since Simcity 4. Simcity 4 with the Network Area Mod is still more complex. There are NO microtransactions or paywalls in this game. This is the full version.

They have REGIONS JUST LIKE SC4. THIS IS THE ONLY GAME OUTSIDE OF SC4 I KNOW OF TO HAVE REGIONS . If you build a lone city without the region feature it can be HUGE! probably 10x bigger than SC4's biggest spaces.

I know that, as a mobile port it doesn't look like it, but it really is complex. It has all of the simcity 4 systems that I can remember: religion, education, garbage collection, electricity, water, fire, police, health, sports and environment. All of these things contribute to the happiness of the sims and their potential for wealth increase. It has regular pollution and noise pollution (nobody wants to live near factories or other loud things).

There are nuclear power plants with can apparently have explosive meltdowns if not cooled properly.

And it has traffic, oh boy does it have traffic. I have played 14 hours right now and still cannot manage stop the traffic jams in my city. I LOVE IT. I love micromanaging the roads and traffic to optimize transport. To make this even harder, sims do not like living near traffic because it is noisy, so if traffic exists around residences they are NOT pleased.

There is a "plugin" system that is stupid easy to use and built-in to the engine. Now that the game is on PC, it will surely see a resurgence of modders.

There is a mod that is being merged with the official game that adds a space program to your city!


CON
Disasters can be turned to auto-occur (instead of player created), but occur a bit too frequently for my tastes. They leave a trail of destruction that is devastating to pay for cleanup. The frequency of disasters mixed with the cost of cleaning up the debri, makes me not like them.

DO NOT TURN ON AUTO NUKES!!! THEY ARE DEVASTATING AND FREQUENT. Unless you want to pretend your city is in a war-torn country. Which could be interesting........ (might RP it some time).

The UI could do with being optimized for pc's, but I can live with it.

TLDR

I cannot recommend this game enough to fans of SC4. It is not exactly the same, but it is comfortably close and I love it!\t\t\t\t\t
