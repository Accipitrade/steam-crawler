\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis one didn't have a good start - this game doesn't scale properly on the UI. In 4K the fonts are so small I thought I'd been pulled back 1000 yards from my screen. Even once I'd quit, setting the desktop to 1080P so I could see the settings menus to set things up, the fonts still do not scale properly, the text isn't properly visible on the icons and even if you meddle about with the font size, it's using a font that isn't very readable. I hope you're not dyslexic or you'll have serious problems.

Those issues aside, this feels like an early access game (it isn't) and it's kind of a tribute to old-school Sim City games (even the zoning is the same colour). There is one oddity here though, there are different levels of zoning, but I couldn't find a way of changing the levels (poor/medium/rich) - when you do zone areas there's a pop-up with a tick and cross you have to click - unfortunately, half the time this doesn't appear on the screen and so you'll need to scroll about or zoom out to find it.

As you grow your city, it'll gain in levels and this will then unlock more buildings, but there doesn't really appear to be much logic to this. I'm not quite sure why you'd need a lighthouse when there's no sea. Or why public transport wouldn't be available before or at the same time as a taxi service, but that's how it is.

The game didn't crash though and it's quite cheap, so there is that, but I still can't really recommend it due to the UI issues. Overall I give this game 3 build more or betters roads out of 10.\t\t\t\t\t
