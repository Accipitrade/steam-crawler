\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not a hardcore city builder. I'm more of a dabbler. This game is good for both. It probably has more longevity for a hardcore player than a dabbler like me. There are a lot of options and one can get as detailed and in-depth as they want.

What I liked:

1. The huge variety of map types and sizes.
2. That there were easy, normal, and hard modes. (I only played easy.)
3. That I could alter the terrain.
4. The many types of road choices.
5. That electricity carried over to connected areas without needing wires, but that I could use wires if I didn't want to connect sections.
6. That the energy and water information sheets showed the separate sectors, if there were any.
7. The look of the high density towers.
8. The real life landmarks with interesting information in their descriptions.
9. That planting trees counteracted both pollution and noise so I could put commercial and residential closer to industry than I would have been able to otherwise.
10. The elevated train system is the shiz!

What could have been better:

1. Descriptions were greatly lacking, as was overall guidance.

2. I also wish there was education past high school with the option to research things that could be applied region wide.

3. The removal fade was nearly impossible to see through on some maps. I was constantly deleting things I didn't mean to because I couldn't see what was what.

4. I think there are keyboard shortcuts but I only found a few by accident. it'd be nice to know what those are. I'd really love hotkeys for Move and Remove. I use them a lot.

5. I wish that moving things didn't cost.

6. I wish that items with a radius of effect would still show the radius of effect when moving it.

7. I wish that it wouldn't set the grid to just density colors when moving things. I can't tell where I am. (I figured out that if I zoom in closer, I can sort of see things.)

8. I wish the item wouldn't jerk many squares over when I pick it up to move it. Especially since it's so hard to see what's what while moving it.

9. I wish there was more to do on a superficial level, like coordinate with other towns in the region on a project, or export goods, or something other than just build (but no battles!).

10. I wish there was a way to control income zones.


OVERALL, it was a fun game that kept my interest for quite awhile and I probably will replay it a lot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is almost the equivalent of playing SimCity 4 Deluxe Edition but with downgraded graphics of SimCity 2000. I enjoy the fact that I don't have to buy ten packages of dlc to unlock metro stations or trains... (looking at you Paradox) they just come included in the base game and make it much easier.

Even still, the game has some flaws although that doesn't mean its a bad game.

The first problem is the damn interface, it tries to respect touch and keyboard input but fails at doing both. I often have to reopen the menus because I didn't see that button or the game thought I was finished. Also I can't manage my city because I don't see a manage city button, I have to press a key to open that menu up. I get the game is being worked on (and this should be set as early access tbh) but it appears sometimes as feature complete, and other times as half way there. For example, why do I have to go outside the game to make a region? It involves jsons instead of the game using the world generator as the region creator. I get its nice to make a single map as a choice, but would also be nice to have the same option for regions.

The second problem is the learning curve, the interface while not the worst thing in the world... makes it complicated to understand on how the simulation operates. Does it follow my command or do I have to constantly please the AI for their demands? What kind of consequences will there be if I fail? Even after learning the basics of placing a 8x8 city square (dont do that, it gets worse with traffic later) the game simply stays quiet. It does nag you when there are problems but sometimes it feels obtrusive and too late. Its small things that kinda ruin the experience, but its not a bad game... just has some flaws.

The last problem is... the music?

Wait, what!? Look, I enjoy piano music and instrumental... but the music seems off in this game. Sometimes it almost feels like a rave just started and have to lower the damn volume. Like I've said before, it feels incomplete or loses its identity by trying something completely different. I might be wrong or maybe this is what they intended, but the songs feel generic at best. Its not the bombastic SimCity 4's Soundtrack or SC3K and even SC2K OSTs.

Now that sounded bad, but the game offers lots of great things. Plugins which are mods, lots of settings to configure, hell you can customize the world. You can literally use specific tilesets to decor your world without Plugins, and that is awesome. I always got annoyed when a specific piece was replaced. Nice touch devs!

Overall, the game at least plays well and runs wells. Its also a lot of fun! Its just a matter of fixing these issues later on which then can really take off. There is also iOS and Android version of the game too, which is nice. For the price it asks, I think I can't complain much.

Its fair and respectful which is why I recommend this game overall for those seeking for a good SC2K-ish modern experience.\t\t\t\t\t
