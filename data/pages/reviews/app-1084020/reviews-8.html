TheoTown is a game developed and published by blueflower

TheoTown overall, however, is a fun game to play, yet sometimes repetitive and it does get boring if being played for a long time, but is a really good different take on the city builder formula and game to play in short bursts!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're a fan of city building games, then TheoTown is definitely worth checking out. It's got a certain vibe that's hard to find in other games - it's like you're playing SimCity 2003 on a crappy laptop, even if you weren't even born yet at the time. It's like you're experiencing the past through the lens of a crappy computer screen, and who doesn't love that?

But let's be real, TheoTown isn't exactly a deep game. It's not like you're going to be managing a complex web of economic and social systems or anything. No, this game is all about building the city of your dreams, brick by brick. And boy, can you spend hours doing just that.

The game's community is surprisingly active, too. There are plugins for days, and people are still making them. It's like a little ecosystem of city-building enthusiasts, all coming together to make the virtual world a better place. And it's charming as hell.

The mobile version of TheoTown is free, so you can try it out without any risk. But if you really want to experience the game in all its glory, you'll want to play it on PC. The UI is a little wonky on desktop, but as an avid mobile user myself, I don't really mind.

So if you're looking for a game to chill out with, something you can play while listening to Spotify or whatever, then TheoTown is definitely worth a shot. But if you're looking for something more serious, something that will really test your skills as a city builder, then this might not be the game for you. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI grew up playing SimCity 2000 - and TheoTown glides right into the comforting grooves that game furrowed into my mind. It's a bit rough around the edges interface-wise, but the vast scope of the project in terms of content more than outweighs the occasional frustration when playing. It's complex, confusing at times, yet extremely relaxing to play.

There's also a deep plugin community, wherein contributors design and upload their own creations. These are a breeze to install and many of them are rather spectacularly done. They range from the purely aesthetic, to overpowered, to slotting nicely into the difficulty curve of the game as designed.

As far as critiques go, because the game is an evolving project, there's not great documentation on strategy or the mechanics of every single element. Certain things you really do have to figure out for yourself (which takes me back to gaming in pre-internet days for sure). If you do install a lot of plugins, the menus can become unwieldy, as not every designer is great about where these get placed in the menu trees. These concerns aside, the game provides a tremendous amount of content at the price.
