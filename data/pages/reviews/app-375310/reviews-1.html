\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving recently played The Mighty Switch Force Collection, I had noted that this game was absent. I had bought and played it some time ago, but never finished. Having returned to it, I can see why.

It’s a simple game, simply moving pipe blocks around so that a water stream can reach particular destinations to put out fires; occasionally there will be a Hooligan Sister to rescue.

I will say; the game isn’t bad per say (thus my light recommendation), but it feels very half baked (ironic for a game revolving around fires), here’s a list of issues I had with it:

  • \tThe “mystery block” gimmick is okay, but almost immediately wears out its welcome and becomes frustrating just a few levels in.

  • \tThe puzzles range from brain dead easy to just fumble about until it’s solved, none are particularly difficult or thought provoking, though some come across as tedious.

  • \tOne of the great joys of MSF is the Par time gameplay. This game absolutely fumbles that. It appears that this game was designed with touch screen in mind; thus making it nearly impossible for the average pc player with a mouse to beat the levels within the time-frame. Despite having a pattern memorised for beating the level and redoing it dozens of times, I kept missing within milliseconds, and these were the first few levels. The game needed a little reconfiguring with mice in mind for the pc.

  • \tAdd to the fumbling with the mouse, Clicking on certain blocks, or on Patty herself, feels a bit… off? Like I’ll be clicking on Patty (the player character) to start her hose and I’ll have to do it multiple times before she often starts. This is fine when playing slowly like you would a normal puzzle game, but since there’s a par time and every millisecond counts, this lack of responsiveness is quite bad.

  • \tI mentioned how there’s Hooligan Sisters to save, it took me a few minutes to work out what I was supposed to do with them, as there is nothing telling you within the game. It wasn’t a long time, but enough to leave a bad impression.
    (by the way, you need to drop them to the ground, and then lead them to the back of the building-- now you know)

So overall, not a particularly great game; however I still can give it a recommendation (though light) because as a simple straight forward time wasting puzzle game, it’s perfectly serviceable – on top of the gameplay you have the gorgeous artwork and lovely beats MSF is known for, and this game’s whole vibe and feeling (outside of the game) is just nice. Perhaps if I played this on a touch screen the experience would be far more invigorating and all my gripes would have disappeared.

So if you’re looking for a cheap and simple puzzle game with a pleasing atheistic, and you’re not the type to 100% games (seriously, don’t bother, the par times are just too tight), though you can defo get better, this is still a simple fun time.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMighty Switch Force! Hose It Down is a short puzzle game where you move blocks to control the water flow of your hose so you can put out fires, and sometimes rescue a Hooligan Sister in distress. You move the blocks by dragging them with your mouse, and you shoot a stream of water with the spacebar.

This game has 50 levels in total: 25 normal levels, and 25 remixed "EX" levels. Overall the levels are fun, and the art and music are pretty good (they are taken directly from other games in the series), but I'm annoyed at this game not having any option to control the volume of the music and sound effects.

Let's be real: This game is a lazy port of the mobile version. However, despite that fact, and despite the lack of volume options, I still think this game deserves your attention if you enjoy puzzle games.

Recommended, but ONLY on sale with a 50%+ discount.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy main criticisms are the victory music, something about it is very grating. And that you can't continue to fire while moving blocks, it stops the stream entirely until you're done which doesn't make for very fluid [heh] gameplay. I feel like the times seem unreasonable given the mouse controls as well, and the previously mentioned thing contributes to that. However, I really did not feel like trying because it took me literally 7 minutes on some of them which had far less to get the start on the level. And as far as I can tell there's no benefit to even getting the par time other than the accomplishment of it.

It's a neat little game, and I would recommend it. However, it is relatively short. Just 10 sets of 5 levels. Most of which aren't that difficult to figure out. But to par time it, is probably a lot more challenging. I only ended stumped by two, and one of them I just was so dreading attempting that I didn't even really try. [4-5 in the special levels]. I managed to par time probably 4 or 5 levels. Only one of which I did multiple times for the express purpose of doing it until realizing pretty quick that the times and required movements would not be fun for me.

Cute visuals, music [other than victory] is fine. And the clear tubes add a nice memory aspect to things to spice up the 5th set of levels in normal and special sets. Not Wayforward's best game, but it's solid.\t\t\t\t\t
