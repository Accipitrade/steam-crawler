Expert+ on Beat Saber isn't hard for me anymore; I have no problem doing different patterns with each arm at the same time... \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the perfect VR rhythm game.

It seems unbelievable to me that this gem of a game seems to have been largely overlooked by both rhythm gaming and VR gaming communities. This game has already perfected VR rhythm gameplay and I don't think any other game has come close. The mechanics make great use of your full arm span, combining individual notes into big movements that feel amazing to execute. It has a high difficulty ceiling, ensuring you have many goals to work up towards, while the level design still ensures a good time. Even then, it doesn't forget its rhythm game roots, and has notes that require button inputs to mix up gameplay. Unlike certain other VR rhythm games, scoring on all notes is directly tied to timing accuracy only, so you can be assured that getting better at timing and executing movements will actually result in score improvements.

And yet, what really makes this game is all the personality it has. It's vibrant and colorful, with incredible environments that just feel uplifting to be in. The little hub world is a great space to chill in and wind down if you're feeling tired and need a break from the workout that the game gives you, with lots of toys to play with. The story is there for those who want it and provides good flavor, but can also be ignored for those who just want to get to the gameplay. The mascot character is cute!! She's cute!!! The way she extends her hand out for a clap at the beginning is such a great touch. It's just such a happy and good-feeling game to play, and it isn't preoccupied with needing to seem edgy or cool like other VR rhythm games seem to need to do.

Airtone really is the best of traditional hardcore rhythm gameplay combined with the best of VR tracked motion controller gameplay. No fuss around justifying the game with lightsabers, guns or punches. You're just going to touch circles and squares travelling down a line to you, and you're going to have the best time of your life doing it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very polished and content rich 'arcade' style rhythm game with a nice sense of 'progression'. The style is very colorful and tonally consistent, which is absolute eye candy inside the headset (and for anyone else watching you play). The songs are great, and the song difficulty scaling is smooth; going from jamming out on basic to a moderate dance workout on hard!

The one thing that stands out from other rhythm games is the controls, as most VR music games don't require any button presses, only hand placement and gestures, while Airtone requires a trigger press for two of the three notes in the game (tap and hold) which shakes it up but with the caveat of being a little difficult to adjust to if you're used to other games like Beat Saber or Synth Riders.

Highly recommended, Neon wants you to take care of the Earth with her!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great VR rhythm game with a predefined song list. It can be played in standing space, which makes it a nice choice for those with a small play area. Like many great games, it's simple to pick up but difficult to master.

AirTone is one of the more polished VR titles I've played. In my experience, the controls are accurate and well-adapted for either the Rift or the Vive, with the one exception of accidental screenshots if you happen to have a vice grip on the Vive controllers as I do. The art is clean, with well-defined models and fonts sized/spaced such that they are easy to read through the headset. Combined with the mostly-upbeat and occasionally wistful soundtrack, these elements create a smooth and relaxing experience.

While the songs are limited to those that come with the game, the selection has been wide enough so far to keep me playing for a long time. One benefit is that the motions for each track feel very distinct and generally fit the themes of their songs, especially as you move into Hard mode.

In comparing to other rhythm games like Beat Saber, Airtone will not have you move around quite as much for most songs but can be more technically complex in others. There is also a main storyline that you progress by improving your scores, and the further you get, the more tracks will become available.

I would recommend this to anyone who has enjoyed other rhythm games, or who is looking to try one for the first time.\t\t\t\t\t
