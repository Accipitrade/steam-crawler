\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThings I have noticed:

I have a pretty high end system (custom built) and I too have noticed that the lag is just a tad for me when doing any Farm DLC missions. Its not bad for me at all even though I can see a little lag. With that said I can see why others may complain about FPS and/or lagging especially if you have a lower end PC.

Building a green house is not difficult, however it is very awkward to do. When building one you need to be exact when placing pieces or they won't line up and sometimes you won't even be able to place them. This IS my only complaint.

To the DEVS,
Please make the snapping of the following pieces snap better into position: Molding, Green Houses, Fencing and gutters. There may be more but these are the ones I have had serious problems with getting them to line up or match in the right spots. It shouldn't take an hour+ just to build the green house from the Windmill mission.

I have read all the negative reviews and a lot of them I believe are not warranted. From past experience the DEVS do a pretty good job at getting bugs fixed (pieces snapping into place correctly... HINT HINT HINT). When releasing an add-on or a new game that's just how it is. Give the DEVS time and I believe they will patch some of the bugs others are reporting about (I myself have not noticed any but does not mean they are not there).

The rest of the game is amazing. It is probably the most "chill" design and build (thanks to Farm DLC) game on steam. I have been waiting for this DLC and got it from day one. So far it has not disappointed me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMay 1 Edit: Alright, so most of the major issues have been fixed it seems. Although, there are still some noticeable performance issues, though i'm not sure if it's just my system or still the system. I've played the first 4 jobs so far, and I'm definitely happy that things have been fixed. At this point, I'm able to safely recommend it now. Of course, I'll keep my original review up. I know I said i'd take it down, but I feel it's better to keep the original for the sake of archiving the issues had and how fast these issues were fixed by the devs.


Original Review (April 15, Not Recommended): I was very excited for the release of the Farm DLC. I absolutely adore farming stuff in games, so I bought it day one. I played it for maybe an hour in total. I've had more performance issues from this DLC on my gaming PC than I did with Pokemon Scarlet on my Switch Lite day one.

Obviously, the sheer number of bugs and issues is from a corporate decision to release it without giving the devs themselves adequate time for bug testing and fixing. Please don't send hate to the devs, because the folks who've spent so many hours of their lives creating House Flipper are incredible, and probably aren't paid enough for how much love and passion they put into the game.

For now, i'm uninstalling the DLC. I hadn't encountered the "photographing apartment" bug until the latest patch released today. I made sure the game was completely up to date and i'm still running into performance issues and bugs. Once the DLC is completely fixed, I'll take this review down, but for the forseeable future my words to folks on the fence about buying the Farm DLC are this:

Don't. Not yet. Wait until it's fixed before spending your money on it. I have no doubts it will be fixed, but until then i can't recommend you buy the Farm DLC.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI LOVE House Flipper, have all the DLC's, hundreds of hours in the game. The new items in this update are great, but the mechanical updates in this DLC are the most annoying and finicky things I have ever done in the game. I chose to build the greenhouse in the "sole solution" job, and trying to put the roof parts on was the absolute worst. You can't control what direction the roof part goes, so even the tiniest mouse movement makes the part flip and go vertical, or point the other direction, or turn upside down. Was so frustrating it almost made me cry.

I do love the new wall tiling mechanic of being able to do multiple walls at once, that makes it much faster. However, you can only tile the walls in segments of 6, 2, and 1, even though the stack you pick up is an uneven 15. It would be much better to have the wall tiles in segments of 5, 3, and 1, like it is when building walls.

I seem to be one of the lucky ones (so far) in that my performance hasn't drastically tanked, my game hasn't crashed, and I haven't had many glitches. I look forward to when they patch this up.\t\t\t\t\t
