\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was really excited for this DLC but I found myself feeling really disappointed. With every new House Flipper DLC, I mostly enjoy the guided jobs, but this time I felt like they were lacking. Though there are 10 new jobs, most of them took barely any time at all to complete so I was left wanting more even after finishing them all.

I understand the focus of the DLC was probably all the new tools they added (like the paint sprayer, drone, house building, etc), but I find it really odd that those are locked behind this DLC since they don't inherently have anything to do with farming. I'm sure a lot of the development effort went toward those features and the new animals, but truly, I just wanted to flip some farm-themed houses and it felt like that was a complete afterthought with this DLC. I would have preferred that the tools were a base game update, or even a different DLC (which honestly I probably would have paid for because I love House Flipper).

Speaking of the new tools... the interface for accessing them is really clunky (you have to click and hold, then press Q while still holding... not to mention that's right by the TAB button which, if accidentally pressed, will close the tool menu and you'll have to start over) and I found them to be mostly unnecessary or too cumbersome to use. I do like the paint sprayer, but I can't see myself ever using the drone, and the grappling hook felt too clunky to use regularly. I like being able to lay more than 1 tile at a time on walls, but the experience could use some fine tuning because it very much feels like an afterthought feature.

I didn't play this DLC until the first hotfix, so I didn't experience a ton of issues like some others did, but I have experienced frequent frame rate drops and freezing while playing with this DLC, which I had never had issues with before.

Considering that this DLC is the same price as Luxury Flipper, which came with 15 new jobs, and even the Pets DLC which had only 11 (but took much longer to complete) I can't say I recommend this. I'm a huge fan of House Flipper but I was really disappointed with this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this is my favourite DLC so far! I've had hours of fun with it already, and I haven't started farming crops or animals yet!

If you haven't bought House Flipper yet and are considering a bundle, get the "Everybody loves animals" one. The furniture and renovation-related items in the Farm and Pets DLCs go together exceptionally well, and come in a wide range of colours, patterns, sizes and wood finishes. The houses in both of them are very big, with many featuring basements and attics.

If you own House Flipper already, you will find a lot of "quality of life" improving items. A radio that allows you to play audio files from your PCs music library; mobile scaffolding, bulky but going up to any height; wall panels that look painted; and several double bathroom sinks which only need a single sink plumbing system. I could also mention the ability to sleep in beds and the new option in the gameplay settings to turn off the buyers' comments while you renovate, but you don't need the Farm DLC for those.

The Farm DLC two new skill tracks: Farming and Architect. And if you press Q while the tool wheel is open, you get an entirely new tool wheel. I haven't tested the hoe yet, or the drone's crop watering capabilities. However, I did use the drone for aerial photos when trying to get to the centre of the maze in one of the missions (for an achievement). The chainsaw is just an alternative to the axe from the Garden DLC. I haven't yet decided whether my favourite tool is the grappling hook, for climbing onto roofs and vertical walls, or the paint gun, for fast and economical painting of external walls, but I have already used them in several base game and other DLC properties!

Strangely enough, the biggest selling point of this DLC is also one of its weaknesses. Because although the Architect tool is quick and intuitive to use, the outer walls it makes are External, and therefore unbreakable. So after you attach your new extension to the old house, the only way to connect them is by placing a door. Some players are so disheartened by the inability to enlarge existing rooms via extensions, they use the Architect tool for building new houses only. Especially as the DLC comes with 3 empty plots of different sizes. There is hope in the future for more roof shape options and the ability to sell existing garage doors in other DLCs, because lots of people have been asking for those. Other minor annoyances are that placement of greenhouse roofs and new extendable fences is a bit fiddly (a friend told me the snapping system reminded him of Autodesk AutoCAD).
