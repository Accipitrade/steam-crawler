\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe short recommendation: If this interests you, then buy it sale. Don't pay full price for this. Don't. It's not worth it for the mess it is. Don't buy for the farming and the animals. It's a building sim and it shows badly.

In-dept:
First of all I brought this dlc in june of 2023. I waited on purpose as I do with many games to buy it on sale to save money.
I'm glad that I did, because it seems like there were many almost game breaking bugs, that ruined the experience for other players. I didn't experience that, only some few bugs, but nothing I would say was because of the dlc itself, though one was just really pissing me off, but I will elaborate later.

This dlc has felt... confusing... even maybe halfway through I felt like I needed to write a review, but decided wait until I completed all the jobs. The most confusing thing I find is the theme. It feels more like some last minute tool ideas smashed together with this half constructed idea about farming and adding farm animals... which feels like it should be in a completely different game.

Now, the price for the dlc is much lower than for some other games, but still adds alot *cough cough* the sims *cough cough*, but that doesn't mean that I don't want value for what I spend my money on. I'd rather have a smaller cheaper dlc, without the farming and maybe a different or just no theme at all.

Jobs, Tutorials and Tools:
The jobs were fun, but when it came to learning the new tools I felt very much left on my own. Sure, they are good, fun and mostly useful, but learning how to correctly and efficiently using them was another story. You get the pop-up that's it. Especially the house/foundation tool was confusing in regards to the job that teaches it. I was struggling putting it down, dragging it along (you can only drag where the arrow is, which was frustrating with even medium builds) and even learning where the walls actually go instead the space it needed also took it's time. More frustrating than fun.

One of the features beside building houses is building roofs! And after completing the maybe 8 jobs included with this dlc, I have still yet to use or be shown how to use this tool, which is a clear no-go for me. I can understand from a learning perspective not chugging this tool and feature at the player at the first or two jobs, but after that, you got no excuse. Teach the players how to play the game or they will miss out and not get the true valve out of the game. For me it just annoyed me and now pisses me off, because I actually wanted to build roofs on several of the job builds, but I didn't know how to and was waiting for the game to show me.

Theme:
This was the big downer for me. The theme and the farming just doesn't connect with the game, especially with the garden dlc, which already adds plenty of stuff to do outside of the house. It feels so off and like something that just doesn't belong here. It just feels... off, wrong and like something that should be in a different game all together, like a sims dlc instead.

What could the developers have done instead? Maybe some sort of sustainability theme, where the focus, along with the new tools, would be to renovate and build sustainable houses, that are better for the environment, greener and cheaper. Maybe even some recycling of materials? I know that's becoming really popular in Danish builds.
But this is a wish for what could have been and not what we got (and paid for)

Bugs:
I did encounter some bugs, were, as I said, I don't connect with the dlc itself, but they still impacted my experience.
Some of the garage doors let in light despite being closed, some jumping haybales and nothing more visual that jumps to my mind.

What did annoy me was the how not all the tasks show up, when you start a job. I got the little 'ding' at one job telling me that I was almost done and could just end the job then and there, but then I cleaned a basement full of water where a ton more tasks were added. This was not unique.
You know how you can't really plan where you want to put the shower in a job-clients home, because you don't know the shape or size of the shower? Some of the jobs had this frustration from beginning to end.

In several jobs tasks would be added to the total sometimes an hour in to the job, which honestly also pissed me off, because I was either
a) not having a lot left and just wanted to finish and move on with my life (studying or sleeping) and/or;
b) mess up my planning for the renovation and not in the fun 'opsie-daisy here is an opportunity', but in a way, where I honestly considered just closing the game and leaving, because it killed the fun I was having.

Conclusion:
If you want the tools, the items or the animals and farms (you do you, we don't judge in this house), then go for it.
But this was bad, even if I had fun and am now free to do my own thing. It is a confusing mess and would have worked better as a dlc for the sims, even if the new tools were fun.
