\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you haven't played it yet, I recommend getting SW1 first to get a picture. SW3 is basically more of the same with a bit more freedom to get sidetracked, but at it's core it's rather a new Star Wolves 1 campaign with a bit of bonus stuff and the SW2 ships than a completely new game.

It's also still buggy, poorly scripted and poorly described. You'll get a dialogue about the good guys getting blocked, find some floating blockers, shoot them down and then nothing happens. Turns out you need to do 3 things first and then hit them only slightly, which you could have gotten only from your objective log. At least quicksave and quickload is very fast and the game has a lot of autosaves.

That being said SW1 was still a lot of fun for me and SW3 was just the same (ignore 2). It plays a bit like a tactics RPG in space. You have some choices, your squad levels up, unlocking active abilities and passive perks in a skilltree and you gear up with ships, weapons and systems. You can micro manage your team a bit and it's worth doing so (paused), but you mainly pick target order, activate abilities or pull low health ships back, it's not a mechanically taxing game. The progression is still a lot of fun and SW3 gives some opportunity to get sidetracked or farm a little compared to SW1's more linear mission system.

Don't expect a good story btw, it's mostly giving you some superficial reason to fly around. There are a lot of open questions after the finale, the final boss came out of nowhere in my playthrough and a lot of "twists" are pretty unsatisfactory. The gameplay is still fun, so I didn't mind, but don't expect much.

The engine is from 2003 and for that still looks darn pretty. The soundtrack is good (sadly missing sw1's amazing shop theme) and SW3 doesn't feel dated to me in terms of controls, although fine tuning upwards and downwards movement is finnicky but rarely necessary. I could play on 21:9 without any issues.

Worth noting that you can and should speed the game up a lot, most of my traveling time is spend on x4 or x8 speed. The game has configurable autopauses on events like enemy sighted, so no reason not to. Travel times are fairly short if you speed on x8.

Overall it's one of the games that makes me wonder why there aren't more of this type because the gameplay just works really well, but could have used a higher budget for better writing and more polish.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, how should I put it. The game over all has some flaws. The camera is constantly moving even when the game is paused, which makes selecting things hard. The mouse movement itself is bad in the vanilla game but there is an mod (not an official patch!) available which could fix it. The space is empty and I wish there would be more traffic, meeting more NPC's, finding more secrets and be more side missions, but there aren't.
Overall I know it sounds bad and it is BUT in my eyes it's a unique game, which allows you to command and upgrade a mothership and some fighthers as a mecernary unit. Fights can be though if you are outnumbered or got the some areas too early when you don't have the equipment and experience to do so and the story splits up in multiple lines, so you can play it more often and choose another storyline.
These are all reasons why I like the game. Too bad they didn't gave it more time to evolve in something even better and more complete and the worst thing is that nobody is working on part 4 of Staar Wolves.\t\t\t\t\t
