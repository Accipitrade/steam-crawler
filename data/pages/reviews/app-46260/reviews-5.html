\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVerdict: For me, this game would be a 7/10. Recommended, but wastes a lot of potential.

As a game designer I can see two issues with this game which make it look bad after just a few minutes of play time. But I think one can endure both of them.

First Problem: It's kinda slow unfolding and seems to be very hard. It's old school. Save a lot and try missions more than once if you fail the first time. You got destroyed when you took the left route? Then try the right route or hire merceneries. Don't get demotivated by sudden deaths.

Second Problem: I see and feel a big lack of immersion in this game. I never felt as a part of the game world, nor did I care about the story or characters. That makes the small mistakes in the game machanic even more apparent.

One can definitely have fun with this game, but you have to embrace it's old school style.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely love this game...although without the very helpful "how to play the first few missions" FAQ I most likely would have lost interest and uninstalled the game before I got far enough into the game to enjoy it.

It is not a space sim, it's an RPG. If you played Freelancer and X3 and decided that what you really want it for those games to play as an RPG with squad-based real-time-with-pause gameplay, you might get something like Star Wolves 3. Character progression is somewhat limited, the plot is linear, but with branches, and there's a large world out there but you won't be interacting it in a freeform manner.

The writing is excellent and the characters have personality. The English translation was done in a rough "literal translation" style, which butchers English grammar something awful but leaves the flavor of the characters intact. If you've spent any amount of time on the Internet, this is not so shocking... I played the game with the feeling that this is a group of pilots brought together by chance, none of whom speaks English natively but who are using it as a common language, and that fits pretty well with the game world . If this bugs you, you'll hate the game. I really had no problems with it--a rough translation of good writing is infinitely preferable to a lot of the native-English bad games I've played. If the voices used in the ending movies are any indication, the game was significantly improved by the omission of English language audio. :)

I'm not so much reviewing as I am just rambling on about what I think of the translation, am I? I'll summarize: After 50-odd hours of play, I feel like the game ended too soon, and I'm ready to restart it and take a different career path. I am sad that the sequel/followup was cancelled. Highly recommended, but follow the FAQ for the first few missions.\t\t\t\t\t
