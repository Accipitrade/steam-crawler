\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is really one of the best little farming games out there in my opinion. I have played a lot of different farming games over the years. My favorite farming games are, of course, Farming Simulator (all the various years), more recently Dinkum became a favorite, Farm Together was a fun little game, and now Harvest Days. I would compare Harvest Days to Dinkum somewhat simply because of graphics and gameplay. Granted, Harvest Days does not have a battle/hunting aspect to it like Dinkum. There is just something about Harvest Days that draws you in and keeps you coming back for more. It adds elements that Farming Simulator just doesn't have which I always thought would be nice. I get it . . . Farming Simulator just wanted to remain in the simulator genre and did not want to add any outside or social activities. Harvest Days has fishing, picking flowers, mushrooms, etc. You can adopt dogs and cats and there is wide variety to choose from. There is mining and woodcutting, farming, crafting, you can build rental houses, and the list goes on and on. You can raise a wide variety of farm animals from cows, chickens, goats, sheep, pigs, rabbits, and even deer. Plus you can breed all of these animals, as well, to either keep or sell. You have the option to name each animal, if you prefer. You do have to keep all animals fed and watered. I almost forgot, you can build a stable and purchase a horse. When it comes to fishing, there are (3) different fishing poles, each designed to assist with fishing from common fish to rare to legendary. There are a lot of different fish to catch during each season. The various seasons are represented here each lasting one month. Each season, except for winter, you can plant and harvest plants that are specific to that planting season, unless you have a greenhouse (there are (2) different ones) whereupon you can plant year round as long as you the seeds. Speaking of seeds, there is a huge variety of seeds to choose from at the general store. You can create beehives which produce honey automatically, there are cheese makers to produce cheese from milk that you get from the cows and goats. There are preserve makers that give you the option of creating jam and/or pickled produce and once again there is large variety to chose from. Of course, you can decorate your house with items/furniture you either create yourself or purchase directly from vendors. You can plant and harvest your crops by hand or you can purchase tractors w/implements and a combine to assist in that planting and harvesting if you choose. You can also actually go into the various businesses and even other houses. The graphics, like Dinkum, are simple but yet charming and have a unique appeal all their own. The game is currently in Early Access so there are few bugs, yet to be worked out but you can passing any bugs you encounter on to the developers via Steam, Discord, email. Overall, I can't say enough good things about this game. I do hope the developers have a far reaching vision of where and what they want Harvest Days to become.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am teetering on the fence about whether to recommend this game or not.

If you ever played Stardew Valley, your first impression in this game would be "Okay. A 3D, third person, version of Stardew valley...." Excited or disappointing would be completely up to you.

You start in a house with measly furnishings, like Stardew. You get mail in the mailbox, like Stardew. With the same quests (almost verbatim), like Stardew. There is a chest to sell your items from your inventory, EXACTLY like Stardew - mechanics and all.

By this point I have not played more than .7 hours. So far, this game is cookie cutter Stardew Valley. However, there are some differences which lead me to believe (or at least hope) that the developing team will eventually add their own unique story and mechanics to this game as it progresses. Maybe since it is early access, they are simply using a template of what it is inspired by and will grow independently from there.

With all of that said. This game is very much early access. It takes far too long to load, even on my high end rig. The locomotion is rough. The NPC's have no soul or independence. The rigging leaves a lot to be desired. And the mechanics need a lot more than just being ironed out. Keep in mind. This is EARLY ACCESS! These are to be expected at this point.

However, I would have implemented a keybinding system before releasing it. I find it super frustrating to use someone else's keyboard layout as though it is the gold standard. Talk to any game developer and they each will have a differing opinion on which keys should be assigned to which actions, while insisting they are correct and everyone else is wrong. Allowing a player to customize this to their comfort is extremely important.

At the end of the day, I am rooting for the development team. I am a fan of Stardew Valley. As long as this game brings in it's own unique ideas, I think it has potential.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA pretty decent farming game that is in early access. If I had to compare it, I'd say it's a mix of the calm and friendly environment of Stardew Valley mixed with the far reaching feel of My Time at Portia. It is still in development, so don't go into it expecting the polish of either. That said, it is still plenty stable, with no crashes and only two or three bugs in about 17 hours of playtime.
More of a breakdown of components:
Farming - pretty decent with a wide array of options, the process can be repetitive during the early game, but that's to be expected.
Mining/Woodcutting - nice and relaxing, once you upgrade the tools for either it becomes pretty easy so there isn't much mid-late game drag.
Fishing - Who doesn't love a game with fishing in it? Again nice, but I'm not sure if it gets harder when going for rarer fish.
Animals - Huge variety of animals and they give you little hearts! Only saw one breed of cats/dogs, though
Townsfolk - decently friendly, but very 2-dimensional characters. A little hard to care much about, but it is EA.
Environment - very chill, a little large with a decent array of variety, you start off with scooter access to make it easy to get around
Vehicles - Didn't quite get to the point of using them, but it seems like the developers have put quite a few into the game for farming, transport, and other cool purposes.

Once more, it is a pretty good game if you are looking for a nice relaxing time without expecting a fully polished experience.\t\t\t\t\t
