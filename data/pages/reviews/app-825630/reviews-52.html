Steins;Gate 0 (SG0) is a.... "midquel" to the original Steins;Gate (SG). That is to say that it takes place during the course of that game, as opposed to before or after it. To say exactly when SG0 takes place within SG and its context would involve massive spoilers for the first game, but I will say it has something to do with the "true ending". SG0 does not alter or change the original game's final ending, but rather it tells a story about the road that led to it. In that sense, people who have finished SG already know where SG0 ultimately ends. It is the journey to get to that ending that is the real focus here.

For obvious reasons, you should play Steins;Gate first before touching this.

Gameplay-wise, this is similar to Steins;Gate but with a few added features. You still use your phone to make the major choices in the game. Here however, the text messaging (via a new program called "RINE") is really only used for conversation and some very silly emotes that I badly want to have. All of the game's choices are done through answering calls.... or not, or at one point deciding whether or not to turn the phone off entirely. The end-result is that there are fewer actual choices to make in SG0, with them only really determining what storyline you wish to follow. Kind of a shame, since the sorts of VN's like Clannad or Little Busters! with their multitude of choices has started to seem like a thing of the past. :(

As far as characters go, it is about what you would expect from this series in terms of quality. New characters like Maho (who is awesome and easily my favorite new character), Kagari, Yuki, Dr. Leskinen, and others are (mostly) welcome additions to the cast that play major roles. I say mostly because one character in particular, while not exactly terrible, has far too little development for their involvement in the plot - especially for a Steins;Gate game. The original cast also gets quite alot more development and screentime, with Suzuha and Mayuri being particularly noteworthy in this regard. Furthermore while Okabe is still very much the main character, SG0 shifts perspectives quite a few times that allows you to see things from the viewpoints of other characters like Maho, Suzuha, Mayuri, Kagari, and more. As you would expect from the original game, SG0 is very solid when it comes to its characters.

So too is it for storytelling. The new story elements added for SG0 are interesting and connect well with existing concepts from the first game. It is also a much darker setting thanks to its starting point and the world it takes place in - and the future that awaits. You get to see parts of the "bad future" that Steins;Gate talked about in vivid and awful detail - and so see exactly why certain characters want to prevent that from happening at any cost. It's not all doom and gloom though. There are still lots and lots of funny moments of the sort that helped make the first game as good as it was. But even with the comedy, the world of SG0 is still headed towards a grim future.

Steins;Gate 0 fills an interesting space in that it wasn't a necessary story to tell, but now that I've finished it I am glad that it was told. It led me to appreciate the "true ending" of Steins;Gate even further by seeing more of what it took to achieve that result. The story that this game tells is a very interesting beast that manages to add quite alot to the Steins;Gate series without detracting from the original in any way.

If you enjoyed the first game, then you owe it to yourself to check this out. Steins;Gate 0 is not a sequel that changes the plot of the original, but rather an excellent addition to the story of one of the best VN's ever made.
