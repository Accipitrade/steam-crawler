\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review will initially be for Steins;Gate 0 as a whole, but will be updated at a later date to include specifics for this release:

What is Steins;Gate?
Part of the SciADV game series, Steins;Gate is the story of a group of friends accidentally stumbling onto the power to change the past, but with each use of this power the world changes in unexpected, and possibly disastrous, ways. This altering of time eventually leads to the main character battling against the will of the universe itself with nothing but blind hope to drive him forward on a mad quest to make things right.

What is Steins;Gate 0?
Blind hope alone wasn't enough and the main character is now a beaten and empty husk of a man, left psychologically damaged and traumatized from the events that occured in the previous game. This is every bit as depressing as it sounds.

It isn't hyperbole for me to say I believe Steins;Gate is one of the greatest Visual Novels ever (only tied with Chaos;Child) to be released, but with that being said, Steins;Gate 0 was an experience I enjoyed more than the original even if I don't think it actually measures up to its predecessor.

Steins;Gate 0 has a number of issues. It introduces many plot threads, but a lot of them get dropped early on or are left to be solved by the reader using obtuse context clues layered throughout the whole story. Every ending has a deeper meaning in the overall context of the Steins;Gate story, but those meanings can be difficult to decipher, or possibly even realize they exist at all. There are also a few sections of the story that require readers to have knowledge of, relatively, obscure (for the West) side material which helps fill in gaps that are left wholly unexplained in the game itself.

With that out of the way, why did I think Steins;Gate 0 was a more enjoyable experience?
The characters. Steins;Gate's first half was an incredible, albeit slow, build up of character development that made you care about every single member of the lab. It was a deliberately paced progression of day-to-day activities which all helped round out every character, giving impact to the entire second half of the story.

Steins;Gate 0 feels like the first half of Steins;Gate throughout the entire Visual Novel, but with the pacing and story moments of the second half of Steins;Gate expertly weaved in. Every important character has their moment in this story, further enhancing and adding layers to what you learned and expect from them based on the original. The plot is constantly moving forward, always throwing new things at the player, but there are plenty of down moments as well which serve to make the characters all feel like real friends enjoying their time together.

If characters and their interactions were the driving force behind your enjoyment of Steins;Gate, then this is all fantastic news for you. However, if the air tight narrative and general ramp-up of the story from the original were your motivators, then Steins;Gate 0 may fail you in a number of ways. As mentioned previously, the narrative can be quite messy at times. It takes on more than it can handle constantly, and only thoroughly explains a subset of all the plot threads that are laid down. This is in stark contrast to the original which explains and justifies nearly every single move it took by the true ending's credits.

Is Steins;Gate 0 worth your time?
A resounding yes, but only if you have seen/played Steins;Gate. Steins;Gate 0 at its worst is still an interesting read and fleshes out the handful of unexplained details in the original (with the side effect of creating many *many* more).

Which version?
For now I've only played the PS4/Vita release, but as soon as I've finished the PC version I'll update this section of the review.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe mid-quel to Steins;Gate! I played this back when the VN came out for the PS4 and the PSVita. So this review is mostly about the game itself disregarding any edits that may have been applied to the steam version.

The interesting thing about Steins Gate 0 is that we already know how the story ends right from the get go. If you're here and haven't played or seen the original Steins;Gate, there will be spoilers below and I'd strongly advise playing it before playing this one, otherwise you're going to be really confused about everything.

S;G Spoilers------------------
In the True Ending of Steins;Gate, after Okabe goes with Suzuha back in time to save Kurisu and prevent World War III, in his attempt to save her, he tragically ends up becoming the one who ends her life. When he and Suzuha return he is distraught and refuses to go back a second time to try and save Kurisu. After a wake-up call from Mayuri and a video mail from himself in the future, he is able to devise a plan and he goes back for his final attempt. Steins;Gate 0 is the story of that future Okabe. The one who gave up. What this VN does so well is to showcase Okabe's abrupt personality shift and how he tries to cope with the mental fatigue that he went through in Steins;Gate.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Steins;Gate 0 is a brilliant exposition of future Okabe's journey through the world that he forced himself to accept when he gave up. The combination of the soundtrack along with the addition of first person views for other characters help us see Okabe both as he sees himself, and how others see him. While not everything was perfection for me, the game and the story it presented was very enjoyable and I would definitely recommend this to anyone who enjoyed the original.\t\t\t\t\t
