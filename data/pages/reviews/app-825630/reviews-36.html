\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLoved the original, but this is a convoluted mess. Way too much worldline jumping, too many heel turns for the sake of shallow twists, practically no overarching story, incredibly poor pacing with either way too much exposition or sudden timeskips up to many months in the future.

So many typos and absolutely bizarre grammatical errors, e.g. a character in a motorcycle outfit is named "Women's with riding suit", and not just once or twice but every time she appears. The original artist, Huke, wasn't on board this time around, and all the characters are redrawn in a generic anime style by somebody else. It's especially jarring when both art styles are used at the same time.

Get this only if you really really like the characters and don't mind turning off your brain when the story gets really stupid. It's always a worrying sign when the slice-of-life parts are more enjoyable than the actual plot development.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt should go without saying that this Visual Novel should not be touched without having finished the original Steins;Gate VN.

Steins;Gate 0 is hard for me to recommend, but also hard not to recommend to any fan of Steins;Gate, as it further explores one of it's endings while also having an overall good narritive. However, it's clear there were some time constraints while making this game, which can be seen in some re-used sprites (which don't fit with the new ones) and unpolished writing here and there. In addition to this, at times certain scenes feel incomplete. However, it's still recommendable if you watch the Steins;Gate 0 anime afterwards, as it surprisingly adds important scenes, while also lacking most context from this VN. Hence the VN should be read before.

All in all, I can recommend this, but if you end up playing it, I would highly recommend using a route-guide and watching the Steins;Gate 0 anime afterwar.ds, otherwise the overall enjoyment might fall flat in comparison to Steins;Gate.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMuch better paced than both S;G 0 anime (to rushed) and the S;G visual novel(too slow, imo). A most enjoyable read.

If you don't know anything about the Science Adventure series, I recommend watching thru the Steins;Gate anime first to get yourself familiar with the story, and then once you get to episode 23, if you want to continue to the end of the original series then just finish it off, it if you want to experience how the original series is even possible to begin with; if you want to learn more about the very first timeline (the 0 timeline)--then play thru this game after episode 23.

What makes this series great, primarily it gives a great sense of comraderie that more often than not, doesn't work in most stories being told today. The incentive to use all your will to save your closest friends, your lab members, your very unconventional family, is very present and feels very real throughout the entirety of the story (even in a lot of the other SciADV games). The science, in my eyes, is a bonus.\t\t\t\t\t

Disclaimer - you need to play Steins Gate before playing S;G 0

Everyone needs a hero, a person that will grab destiny by the hand. Pulling it out and changing the World around. Okabe Rintaro is that person or I should say... Hououin Kyouma. Sadly there wasn't much of that second character here. Story justified that though, we are looking at the guy who saw much more terror in his life than most experienced war veteran. Without spoiling the fun it feels great once you get hang on the situation.

It has different style compared to the previous journey of our lab members. It is much more gloom. Story has a lot of future visions as always but much more intensified on war, I believe and consequences of our previous decisions. On few grounds I find the previous title much more complex in decisions but less thrilling. S;G 0 does the opposite, and you won't need a guide to reach all 5 endings. There are different feelings packed inside, but again shocking, memorable and glorious.

We will meet few new characters, legal loli for the win!! Leskinen and few old ones that will shine even more than before Suzuha gets her spotlight moments and even Moeka (<3!!!). We have some new light on Kagari finally seeing her in full time role. Watching story unfolds was interesting even more then before since its length was properly adjusted, great mix of story and action since we didn't need long introduction it turned out perfectly balanced. I still hoped it was a bit longer game with more choices but oh well...
