\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAre you a fan of the original STEINS;GATE? Then you will most likely find this expansion pretty interesting, at the very least.

This is not a sequel per say, but you will need to have knowledge of the ending of the first game. Much like the Corpse Party: Book of Shadows release to Corpse Party: Blood Covered, it is a sequel that more or less takes place after a bad end/timeline/world line, not really adding onto the canonical ending... except it kind of does in this case. It's weird to try and talk about, but yeah just basically don't expect it to pick up exactly at the end of the True Ending. It's more complicated.

For as little as the interactability was in the original STEINS;GATE, being simply text messages and phone calls you could respond to or explore, it feels like there were significantly less options in this installment. And yet, if you were to try and find the true end and/or all the other endings, you would somehow be even more hard-pressed to find out which actions you need to take without a guide than before.

So I decided to put the show on hold, and turned to the VN instead.

Now that I've finished it I do not regret that decision. Characters, plot flow and mysteries feel much more natural in the VN. Maybe the reason for the strangeness of the show is that it is not following any particular route from the VN. It's doing its own thing. But maybe that was a necessary decision.

Plot
The entire story of S;G0 and its true route, or its essence, are not what I'd call easily adaptable to a traditional on-screen format. While an interactive game format is much more appropriate for this, this VN actually ended up a bit complex at times. It doesn't feel as streamlined like the original S;G. This does not make the game bad, the story just simply demands that complexity. So be prepared to use some of that grey matter, and you'll exprience one of the most non-linear time travel stories ever written. It is well woven into the S;G franchise (including the movie).
And maybe you even get an answer for something that raised a question for you in the original S;G. But prepare for new questions too.

Characters
Almost all characters from the original S;G return, and you learn new sides of them. The new characters are all very well fleshed out and feel real. None of them are either always grim or funny, everyone gets their fair share of seriousness and silliness.

Gameplay
The traditional VN gameplay. In addition to that, Okabe's famous phone is now replaced with a smartphone. Cou can still change background pics and ringtones, but no more webbrowser. Also, previous text messages (now RINEs with emojis) do not get stored anymore. Thus the functionality feels somewhat reduced - I guess they did streamline here.
Choices are made in whether you answer phone calls or not and also other, interesting, and unexpeted ways.

Music & art
The music goes very well with the scenes. It accompanies and emphasizes, but doesn't take over or distract.
The art is good where it is good. Huke probably improved over the years. It is less stylized than the original. But I ended up prefering the original's art. The reasons are the quality of S;G0 art.
I can't help but shake off the feeling that this game was rushed in the art department (not blaming Huke, this was likely a management decision). Some characters' faces vary quite noticably (Okabe's and Maho's in particular) over the course of routes (and not because of plot). What's more is that at some key plot moments, main characters didn't get any CGs. That was very unfortunate, espcially because I really wanted to see them in those situations. And third, sometimes, and quite noticably so, the CG models from the original S;G are used (again, not because of plot).

Sum up
A highly intricate addition to an already elaborate time-travel franchise, with lovely new character. The rushedness in the art department at places is a bummer, unfortunately.

A 9.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFunny. After finishing both, Original and Zero I realized how similar I am to both versions of Okarin. Only a few years ago I used to be as lightheaded and always on the positive? side as Kyoma, and now I'm as depressed and miserable as Zero's Okabe.

Okay, cringy stuff aside. STEINS;GATE 0 is the most unique sequel I've ever got my hands on. The way you have to experience both novels is insane: you have to start the original, play the entire damn thing (50+ hours if you leave True ending for dessert), stop at the VERY end (25 minutes, tops) of the original, play the entire Zero (leaving True ending last) and only THEN you should wrap things up by beating the original. Ridiculous (in a good way, of course). Someday I'll read it again following that exact pattern.

As for VN itself... I have to admit, I enjoyed it far more than original. Why? Unlike S;G, Zero goes straight to the point, minimum "filler" content. After the "common route" (more like a prologue chapter, really) there's always something that's happening. Don't get me wrong, I LOVE the original, but the stuff that's been happening before THAT celebration of Lab Mems ... I've been considering dropping the novel entirely, it was THAT slow and nothing's been happening. Thankfully it went from 0 to 100 rather quickly.

The storytelling keeps the novel fresh and you ARE eager to learn what will happen next. Unlike previous novel (hope I'm not mistaken), we follow not only after Okarin, but at least after 6 other characters, sometimes, the narration switches to a 3rd person, to keep it fresh. I'm only 24, but I'm already getting to old for videogames. I rarely sit playing for more than 90 minutes, not the case for S;G. 5 hours was my record, stopped only because my ass got stiff.
The structure of novel is quite fresh as well. While S;G's plot looks like a "tree"(A straight line, only branches out on 65th percent through the story), S;G 0's plot is a "bush" (one small common route, then 2 subcommon routes which brach out even more). I want to point out that subroutes are unique, meaning completely different outcomes, World Lines, if you please.
The story itself is... brilliant. There's nothing else to say. If you enjoyed the first one, without a doubt you'll enjoy Zero.
Technical part.While S;G is a diamond in a rough, S;G 0 is far more polished. Smoother animations, transitions between scenes, menus. Has a controller support, hence playable via Steam Link.
Artwork, music...I like both of them, Main Menu theme went straight into my playlist and the sprites\\backgrounds are pretty... there's only one thing I dislike about Zero: reused assets. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ they were lazy enough that they didn't even bother to redraw the old sprites, really? I'll take Faris for example: her winter variant is new, but the summer variant is the same ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ one from the first game, looking SO out of place. It's a minor thing, really but... for a AAA game of visual novels they should have done it better.

I rarely buy games at their full price these days. This was an exception. And I don't regret a bit.

STEINS;GATE 0 is a unique experience, every VN\\anime should try. Since there won't be any new S;G novels (let's face it, infinite worldlines=infinite sequels, although you can already make a sequel out of 0's True End, and I'd love to see Okarin as SERN's rounder in Gamma\\Delta worldline (can't remember which one)), I bet you'll going to miss these characters, as like they are your friends you're not going to see again.

10\\10, yes, Yes and YES from me. A true pearl in my VN collection.

A lot of extened scenes, a certain confusing story arc was simplified (well, probably due to the fact that the anime adaptation takes a little bit from every alternative ending there is in the novel), and a few more changes that made the story even better (even though there are cut moments as well). After credit scene alone make Steins;Gate 0 my personal favorite of the series. The story is 100% concluded.Sorry to keep you waiting, you two!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteins Gate 0 is considered a mid-quel to the first game. It leaves the entirety of its predecessor untouched and merely expands upon the story to give you a greater sense of how all the events leading up to the original's conclusion came about.
I've read through a large chunk of the reviews on here (strikingly positive) and feel the need to write a more fair summary of what you actually are getting from this game, because it's not the masterpiece that was Steins;Gate.

Storyline: Not even remotely close to as tight-knit as the original. The numerous endings the game offers do give a bit more background on certain characters, but they tend to be filled with unnecessary padded filler which takes some of the emotion out of the experience. Not to mention, because of the way the endings are structured in this game, you will have to sit through numerous scenes multiple times and not be able to skip them. I'm not sure if this was just a bug, but getting to the true ending had me mashing the enter key through several scenes that the skip feature didn't seem to want me to skip, even though I'd seen them before.

The ending to this game ties everything up extremely poorly and leaves a ("so that was it") kind of taste in your mouth. Once you dig around online and graph out all the minute plot details together from different forums, things will make sense, but a sign of poor writing is when you can't follow the plot from beginning to end within the content itself.

Characters: Not one character stands out in this visual novel over any of the original ones. Yes, Maho is written well enough and I did like the contrast the story sets between her and Kurisu, but because of the nature of how Steins;Gate was set up, she's always cast as the character that did these certain things, but never became relevent enough for you to care. Characters like Moeka and Tennouji play minor roles with little explanation. Kagari was the only character that 5pb seemed to want to really take a shot with, but she ends up being more of a plot complexity headache then anything else. And the connection with her and Okabe in the past is so contrived it just seems absurd and poorly throught-out.

Art: Fairly consistent all the way through. There's a lot more graphics with the extra company on board, and it helps to set a better scene. However, some of the graphics were really badly drawn, especially some of the scenes with Maho, and that really contrasts with the professional art style you're used to from this series.

Gameplay: Typical hit enter through dialogue fanfare that's common with every other visual novel. The only difference here is that they simplified the route choices down to either answering or not answering calls, instead of having to make right choices within texts sent from characters, If you want to 100% the game with achievements, you do still have to make proper replies to various texts throughout the game.

Is it worth it: Honestly, and this is just my personal opinion of course, I feel I wasted my time spending 40 hours reaching every ending in this game. The majority of endings just didn't feel impactful, and the true ending is very short and summarizes what you knew all along, just with more detail. There's some quality writing to be found here, some good AI science that was intriguing to read about, and the game did have some plot twists to keep the story moving, but this mid-quel just doesn't come close to living up to the hype.\t\t\t\t\t
