\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteins;Gate 0 is an amazing mid-quel to the original Steins;Gate, although it is a lot darker than the original one (really early game spoiler : mad scientist transformed into sad scientist ), it still is a great story unlike some could tell you. The pacing is slow, maybe almost slower than the original Steins;Gate VN, but the narration is just excellent especially on how it tells each character’s perspective. However due the non linearity of the events some could be confused. But for me it’s exactly what I could expect about this type of story, going time leaping doesn’t call for a whole lot of linearity and that’s why one thing that I disliked about the original Steins;Gate was the linearity of the events (you get what I mean if you played the original), in S;G 0 choices really do feel they matter since really early in the story you get to make serious (obvious) choices that affect the entire worldline and the ending in a significant way.
Each route is unique (although some are somewhat short, looking at you Maho’s route) in its own way.

For those who come playing the VN after having seen the anime adaptation (which was not that good), this is different experience altogether, some fans such as myself, consider the anime ending as happening on a different worldline (even though only one worldline can be active, it doesn’t mean the one we’re seeing on the VN is the only one where Kyouma succeed in motivating his 2010 self to deceive the world, in order to reach Steins Gate .

In the end I would absolutely recommend this to any fan of the Sci;ADV universe.

Operation HiddenHand is a success.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finished S;G0 recently and kept clinging on the feeling that I needed to write about it. The original had been probably one of my all-time favourite premises and I had been really interested in how S;G0 would expand on it.

I think, IN GENERAL, I would say that if you played or watched S;G and liked it, you will definitely enjoy this experience and how it plays out. If you have not played or watched S;G, this game is not for you; the plot content is so dependent on what happened in the OG that you will not understand what's going on or have any understanding of Okabe's character. If you like sci-fi anime weeb stuff, go play the original Steins;Gate (or watch it), it's has an amazing plot. The story the game is trying to tell is about how Okabe fails to succeed in his goals in the original, and how he struggles to cope with the fact that he now lives in an imperfect world-line, in which World War 3 is right around the corner and his loved one is dead. In 0, characters from the OG like Mayuri, Daru, and Suzuha are given a lot more attention than they did in OG, and personally I feel like they really shine to make up for Okabe's lack of motivation throughout the game. New characters like Maho and Leskinen were great additions, to the point where I actually wish they had been in the original because they're pretty likeable. The climax that the story is trying to build up to (and if you've played the game, you'll know what I mean) is great and seeing it all play out is hype.

However, although I felt that the VN was Good, I definitely had some problems with the plot and how some of the characters were handled. Overall, the route structures are pretty different from one another, and try to play off each other until you reach the true ending. But, I felt like some of these routes were rushed and didn't have enough time to really setup what they wanted to do, this lead to some reveals coming off pretty flat personally ( Ahem, REYES. Also even Leskinen after his reveal kinda become "resolved" soon afterwards so even he doesn't feel much like a threat.. ) Moreover, a lot of these routes don't tie into the true ending much, and compared to the OG (which admittedly, was more linear) it kind of made me feel like the plot wasn't as cohesive as it could have been. There was a weird moment I had where a plot cue was dropped in one route, and doesn't ever become relevant until a completely different route; wherein it doesn't even have much payoff and feels like it had originally intended to be important, but they decided against it ( Kagari having plastic surgery to look like Yuki ). I think the biggest problem I had was how the character Kagari is handled. Basically, this character changes significantly between routes. TBH, I can see why people would be fine with it, but i felt like I had really no idea what the character was even about, and the parts of their character that remain consistent felt shallow. I guess it felt like their character was designed to bring about some plot device for w/e route they were in, and there wasn't much direction beyond that.

With that in mind, I can understand why adapting it must have been difficult. It kinda made me salty that the true ending is literally only one scene, and that the anime does more to expand on it than the vn did. Overall, it was a fun ride, but I probably would never replay it like I would the original.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, even though I would recommend this game for any Steins;Gate fan, I can't recommend it as much as the original series. It's not because the story is worse. On the contrary, I think the story is a fantastic addition to the original and makes the series as a whole better. The problem is that when you compare this game to its anime adaptation, I feel like it wanes in comparison. This game feels very unorganized compared to the anime and certain events are separated when it feels like they should be together, but this opinion might just come from me watching the anime before actually playing this. Certain characters are ignored and there are some noticeable plot holes. Overall, I would still recommend this but only after watching the Steins;Gate 0 anime.\t\t\t\t\t
