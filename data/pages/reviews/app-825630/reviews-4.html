\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteins;Gate 0 is the sequel to the acclaimed Steins;Gate made by 5pb, it’s a sci-fi visual novel that involves time travel, conspiracy theories and mind control. 0 adds another technology that is significant to the story – Artificial Intelligence. It is out on numerous platforms but I am reviewing the Vita version I originally played.

Plot: The story continues from just before the ending of Steins;Gate and takes place in the alpha attractor field timeline, as opposed to the beta attractor field timeline the previous game took place in. In this timeline, SERN is not as much of a threat and Mayuri survives.. but now Kurisu dies instead. This timeline instead converges to WWIII. The protagonist Okabe’s mind is pushed to the brink between the deaths of two of the people he cared about the most. Two main subplots in this timeline involve Suzuha searching for a child who she had lost when time traveling, Kagari, and Rintaro testing out the Artificial Intelligence system Amadeus, which contains a copy of Kurisu’s memories. 

Gameplay: The main gameplay element, besides reading text, is checking your phone at the right times and responding (or not responding) to the correct messages, which alter the timeline of events. It’s a simple but effective means of branching the storyline. 

Characters: 

Steins;Gate 0 contains most of the previous game’s cast along with a few new additions:
-Okabe Rintaro: the protagonist of Steins;Gate who is a self described mad genius, who comes across as both eccentric and self-loathing at times. In 0, he also suffers from PTSD due to witnessing Mayuri and Kurisu’s traumatic deaths numerous times. He is also considerably more serious in this game, no longer using his mad scientist facade anymore.
-Daru: Okabe’s closest male friend, a pervert and a prodigal programmer and hacker who can bypass almost any security system. 
-Kurisu Makise: the heroine of the series, a tsundere, a scientific teenage genius who Okabe has a love-hate relationship with. In 0, she exists mostly as an artificial intelligence system nicknamed Amadeus, as an machine copy of the original Kurisu (who is dead in this timeline).  
-Mayuri Shiina: Okabe’s closest female friend, cute, innocent, a bit ditzy but always there in need for her friends. As a result of the attractor field shifting, Mayuri is alive and well in this timeline.
-Suzuha Amane: A time traveler from the future who works as a part time worker for Mr Braun (Okabe’s landlord). In this timeline, she is less joyful and even more serious due to the worldline converging to an even more destructive future with WWIII. 
-Faris NyanNyan: A cosplaying catgirl who comes from a well to do background and owner of a popular maid cafe. 
-Luka Urushiba: Biologically a male, but always appears and acts very feminine, leading many to mistake his gender. He’s one of Mayuri’s close friends.
-Moeka Kiryu: Nicknamed ‘Shining Finger’ for her preference for and speed at texting, a very introverted girl with sneaky intentions. In this timeline, she is not a ‘Rounder’ and is not a threat to Okabe anymore.
-Maho Hiyajo: Kurisu’s friend and research partner at the same university; she helped out with Kurisu’s research and introduced Amadeus to Okabe. She is also plagued by feelings of jealousy and insecurity due to feeling that she will never match up to Kurisu’s genius.
-Alexis Leskinen: Professor and chief researcher of the Brain Institute at Kurisu and Maho’s university and serves as their mentor. He is one of the main minds behind Amadeus and seems to harbor darker desires for the project.
-Judy Reyes: A colleague of Leskinen and fellow research professor at the university.
-Yuki Amane: Suzuha’s mother and Daru’s wife. Most of her involvement is limited to Suzhuha making sure that Daru’s doesn’t mess things up and that he ends up together with Yuki.  
-Fubuki Nakase and Kaede Kurushima: Along with Yuki they are Mayuri’s cosplayer friends.
-Kagari Shiina: A war orphan and the adopted daughter of future Mayuri. She escapes with Suzuha in the time machine and got lost somewhere in time. A big suplot of the game is locating Kagari and helping her regain her lost memories.

Graphics: Steins;Gate 0 uses the same pastel style graphics as the previous game and it’s a very visually distinctive style that people either love or hate. I find it very unique and while it won’t fit every game, it seems to work well with this series.

Music: I’ve always loved Steins;Gate’s music, and here is much the same; the composer definitely did a good job matching the background track with the mood of the scene and the environment.

Conclusion: 9/10. Steins;Gate 0 is a noticeably darker game and takes place in a timeline with even higher stakes. While Steins;Gate started off on a lighthearted note and only started getting darker towards the midpoint of the game, 0 starts off right off the bat with dark sombre tones and doesn’t really let up the whole game. There are light hearted moments, but Okabe for the most part remains much more serious in this game compared to the previous one. He no longer uses his ‘Kyouma’ mad scientist shenanigans anymore, and is more focused on his main goal of saving Kurisu, saving Mayuri and reaching the fabled Steins Gate timeline. Overall it’s a great sequel for Steins;Gate fans and a fitting conclusion for the series, and those who are into time travel, sci-fi, psychology etc will definitely be interested in this game, the only reason I gave this game a slightly lower score is because there are moments where some scenes seemed unnecessary to me in order to prolong the game’s length. This game is quite lengthy to get to the true ending, and there isn’t as many open ended routes in this game as the first one. The different endings in this game are often required in order to achieve the true ending, compared to the multiple possible endings of the first game. That said, it’s still a highly recommended game for me – just be sure to play the first game before you play this one as this game is a true sequel and builds off of the first game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the greatest strengths -- but also weaknesses -- of Steins;Gate is that it's a very "tight" story. It never leaves Akihabara, and everything that supposedly happens in various futures -- World War 3, SERN's domination of the world -- are things that only exist in the stories that we're told by other characters. Even though we believe they're real, there's little verisimilitude. It makes the story feel small, narrow, cramped. By design, of course -- but nonetheless, there's a part of you that wanted more, right?

One additional weakness of Steins;Gate was that Okabe wrote a whole ton of checks that he never actually paid the bill for, emotionally and narratively. In a lot of ways, the "True Ending" feels somewhat... undeserved, if that makes sense.

Steins;Gate 0 is a much, much more ambitious story. It goes many of the places the original didn't, and really does try to "pay the emotional bill" on those checks Okabe wrote. Overall, I think I enjoyed the experience even more than the original game, though YMMV. Anyways, If you liked Steins;Gate, you'll probably love this too. Also, it has Maho, who is so fantastic of a character that it feels like she carries the whole game.

A small side note. Steins;Gate 0 is effectively the story of "what happened between Mayuri's ending of Steins;Gate and the True Ending". That is, (Steins Gate ending spoilers) how exactly do we reach the point of getting Okabe's video call from the future?. As a result, the game is effectively "missing" its final ending -- because the real ending is simply the True End of Steins;Gate. Accordingly, if possible, I suggest playing the True Ending of Steins;Gate after finishing Steins;Gate 0.

As a disclaimer, since I played in Japanese, I cannot speak for the quality of the translation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: if you enjoyed Steins;Gate, Steins;Gate 0 is a worthy continuation. But expect depression and a very branchy story. Reading Steins;Gate first is required.

I want to give some tips to new players first. Compared to Steins;Gate which is pretty linear and the choices are made deliberately, Steins;Gate 0 is very branchy and that branching is decided by whether or not you answer certain phone calls. And you have no way of knowing which phone calls and what will happen afterwards. The first step towards properly enjoying this story is coming to terms with that. In Steins;Gate 0, failure and having things come out different than you want is part of the story.

Usually I would recommend just playing on your own to see what ending you get based on your own choices but I can already tell which one you'll get: Gehenna's Stigma. And that ending leaves a lot of questions unanswered. Actually that's true for most endings. So instead I recommend 100%-ing this game. Getting all endings. Especially True End. Only then will you get the full picture.

The story branches off into 2 main routes very early on and you won't even know it happened. Those routes are towards Promised Rinascimento and Vega and Altair chapters. In order to get the True Ending you have to finish Promised Rinascimento, which unlocks an additional chapter after Vega and Altair. So to get the True End you have to experience both routes.
The game quick-saves in all important scenes by default so it doesn't really matter which route you go first. You can always come back and skip everything you've seen already. However I think it's more efficient to finish Promised Rinascimento first, and then play through the other route with True End already unlocked. I also think the story flows better like that. Although once again, the differences between the routes are big and it's difficult to grasp them as a part of a singular story. So once again, don't expect a linear story like Steins;Gate. And DON'T BINGE

If you chose to follow my advice I recommend using this 100% guide. It's spoiler-free and has a convenient diagram to help you undestand the branching: https://steins-gate.fandom.com/wiki/Steins;Gate_0_(Visual_Novel)/Walkthrough

And now for the actual review:
As for the story itself it's pretty different from Steins;Gate but not in a bad way. To avoid spoilers I'll just say that it follows the Okabe who failed to save a certain someone. The poor guy now has trauma and PTSD attacks and completely stripped himself of his mad scientist identity and tries to live like a normal college kid. But some things are simply out of his control.
That is the premise of the story and I love it. Okabe giving up at that moment is quite realistic. You're used to characters being like "never give up! powa of friendship!!1" and suddenly gain power and motivation. But that's not realistic. Everyone has a breaking point. And Okabe's breaking point is placed at the perfect spot.
The same likeable characters from Steins;Gate also appear here plus some new ones who you'll also like.
Expect to be burned out though. You are following a burned out character after all. But there are moments that make it all worth it, a lot like Steins;Gate, maybe even more so.

My main criticism goes towards gameplay. As I said you have no idea where you're headed. I mean neither does Okabe but it's still a little unsettling. And the branching makes it very difficult to grasp everything. Route 1 has what Route 2 is missing. Route 2 has what Route 1 is missing. You have to experience both.

It is recommended by the community to watch the Steins;Gate 0 Anime regardless after you finish. The anime is a linear route unlike this one and it's a lot easier to understand. It acts as a 3rd route that has all of the strengths of the other 2 with none of the weaknesses. The story of 0 is handled in iterations. Every worldline you see is a failure that drives you a little closer to success. And the anime acts as the final iteration. Also the anime straight up does some things better. Anime's ending is done way better. It's recommended to watch the anime after reading the VN but I did it the other way around so that may have thrown me off and influenced my review a little.
Reading this helped me realize how god-tier the anime adaptation is. You can watch the 8h anime intead of reading the 25h VN. You'll still be satisfied. Still, it's nice to see additional content of the VN.

Overall I loved it. I love the premise of 0 even more than the original. But it's a little harder to enjoy fully beacuse of the branchiness.\t\t\t\t\t
