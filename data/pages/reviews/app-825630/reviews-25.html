\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recently finished up Steins;Gate 0, and since I did a review of the original Steins;Gate I only feel it is appropriate to leave a review of this game as well. After taking some time to fully form my thoughts on the game I've finally decided to write out my review for this game! I really like doing longer reviews, so this is probably going to be longer than the average steam review. If you don't want to read the whole thing the next paragraph is a pretty good summary of my thoughts, so you can just read that if you'd like!

The first question that most people ask regarding sequels is whether it was as good as the first one. So I'll just go ahead and get this one out of the way. If I am to be completely honest my answer is no, this was not good as the original Steins;Gate. However, that absolutely does not make this a bad game. In fact I believe quite the opposite. I thought that this was a great game that would probably be the best game in many game series that exist, but since it is the sequel to Steins;Gate (which is an easy 10/10 in my opinion, and my favorite game ever) that just isn't the case here. Even if I have some problems with this game that I will discuss in length later, this game is definitely a worthy follow up to the original Steins;Gate. If you loved Steins;Gate you will most likely love this game as well, it's that simple in my opinion.

What does Steins;Gate 0 do right?
  • Personally I think that Steins;Gate 0 has an excellent story for the most part (I'll explain this later), and I really thought that the side endings in this game were absolutely excellent. Twin Automata and especially Recursive Mother Goose were incredible and were some of the best this series has to offer. Recursive Mother Goose is my favorite side ending in the whole series personally, and I've always thought that the side endings in these games were top notch!
  • The returning characters in this game are definitely done justice. Okabe is still my favorite protagonist ever, and (slight spoilers, mainly for the original Steins;Gate) the depiction of his guilt and trauma were pulled off very well in my opinion, and add a lot to his character here. Mayuri and Suzuha were both excellent in this game, just like they were in the original. In fact I'd say they were even better and more important in this game than they were in the first game, and considering I thought they were both great characters already that is really saying something. Faris, Moeka, and Luka are typically less important and less prevalent here than they were in the original game, but I always enjoyed their screen time in this game! I think the biggest winner from this game, however, is Daru. While Daru still has his "perverted gentleman" side, this game shows a new and very interesting side of Daru that shows how much he has grown since the first game. I always felt Daru got kind of screwed over in the original Steins;Gate, being the only main lab mem to not get an ending and a character whose few defining moments felt overshadowed, but it seems like they tried to rectify that mistake by making him super important in Steins;Gate 0 and vastly developing his character.
  • For the most part the new characters are very good too. While some of the new characters are mostly irrelevant, most of the new cast members feel very new and interesting. Maho in particular was an amazing addition to the cast here, and I know I will absolutely miss her presence whenever I replay Steins;Gate. Leskinen was also a great addition. He wasn't as good as Maho, but this game certainly would not be what it is without him. The addition of Mayuri's cosplay friends in Yuki, Fubuki, and Kaede was nice, even if they weren't super important.
  • The voice acting in this game is, just like in the original, simply incredible. After playing the original Steins;Gate and this game I don't think I'll be able to not notice how mediocre most voice acting is honestly, because that's just how good it is in Steins;Gate. The feelings of these characters completely cross the language barrier due to the voice acting here, which is no easy task.
  • This games music is also very good. I think it's a bit less memorable than the original, but the OST of this game is still very good. The song that plays during the Recursive Mother Goose ending is probably in my top 3 tracks of the entire series.
  • This games ending was great! I feel like games where you already sort of know the ending tend to have disappointing endings, but this games ending didn't disappoint me at all! And that's not even touching on the anime adaptation, which IMO does the ending even better!

While it's strengths may not be as insanely good as the original game, there is still plenty to like in this game! This game certainly does more right than most other games that I've played do!

What does Steins;Gate 0 do wrong?
  • Personally I don't like the art in this game as much as the first game. A lot of the characters simply look different, and I very much preferred the style of the original. That's not to say that it is all bad in the art department, but if you loved the art of the original game just be wary of the fact that the general art (especially sprites) look different. The CG's are still excellent, however. Another thing that is strange about the art is how in some scenes (typically flashbacks) this game uses the art of the original, which very much clashes with the new style. Seeing how different Kurisu looks especially is just jarring honestly.
  • A few of this games story moments just really felt weird to me. I obviously can't say what they were, but one in particular near the end literally feels like it is breaking this games own continuity. As far as I can tell that has been fixed in the anime adaptation, but it is still really jarring in the VN and frankly out of place in my opinion.
  • This may sound like a stupid complaint, but this game really overdoes fan service. I'm typically not somebody that ever likes fan service (the type that sexualizes characters that is. I'm not talking about things like ME3 Citadel DLC), it isn't normally something that really bothers me. However, this game really overdoes it in my opinion. Within the span of approximately 6 hours there were 3 scenes that involved some kind of naked woman doing something that absolutely did not require them to be naked. It was a bit absurd and honestly just upset me that the writers/devs felt the need to include this in the game. They even try to explain in game why these scenes have to have the girl nakedl, but the reasoning is so nonsensical that I just found it frustrating. They could have easily rewritten these scenes to be CLOTHED women, and literally nothing in this story would change. Maybe this isn't a big deal to most people, but it was something that really bothered me.

In my opinion this is definitely a game with some major flaws. While these may not be game breaking to most people, all of these listed flaws definitely changed my opinion of the game for the worse.

[Overall Thoughts on the game]
Despite it seeming like I may have sort of ripped this game in the flaws section, I still think this was an incredible game. This game absolutely did not disappoint me as the follow up to my favorite game ever. Was it as good as the original? In my opinion no, but that does not change the fact that this is a very solid game that would be the best entry in a very large amount of series out there. If I were to assign this game a number rating it would probably be a solid 8 or 9 out of 10. There were enough things that I disliked that I justify can't giving this game a 10, but it is still a solid game that I would gladly recommend to nearly anyone. If you enjoyed the original Steins;Gate you are almost certainly going to enjoy this game in my opinion, and are just doing yourself a disservice if you decide not to play it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor fans of the anime, this Visual Novel is a must-read, even more so than the original Steins;Gate. The S;G anime was a much more faithful adaptation of the S;G VN due to the VN's relatively simple story whose alternate endings didn't matter too much.

S;G 0 on the other hand is a very non-linear VN with two main branches that each have multiple endings and the two branches interact in a way that leads to the true ending. Because of this complexity, the anime had to decide one one route to take to tell the story. They did this my mixing together multiple of the routes and endings in the VN to create a cohesive, linear story. This definitely isn't a bad thing, but it does mean that there is a lot of amazing content in the VN that wasn't present in the anime.

This is why I think that this VN is more "worthwhile" to read if you are a fan of the anime. Especially if you want a "jumping in" point for the rest of the SciAdv series, I think S;G 0 is a great introduction to visual novels for a fan of the S;G animes due to having a faster initial pace than the S;G VN, as well as being a great mix of familiar and new content for people who have seen the anime.\t\t\t\t\t
