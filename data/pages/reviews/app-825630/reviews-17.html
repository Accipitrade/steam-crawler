\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Positives

+ Great story considering how good the original was it’s a tough act to follow.
+ Some decent new characters, some great new characters - Maho is a particular standout.
+ Has a different tone and identity compared to the original (it's darker) while still feeling very much like a follow-on story.
+ Music is fantastic as always and really compliments the tone of the story.
+ Changes to other characters’ perspectives in parts which provides interesting new insight.
+ Artwork is still great and there's more of it compared to the original.

Neutral

/ Much quicker pace compared to the original in parts and frequent time lapses, e.g. now it's 3 months later.

Negatives

- The story is harder to follow because it branches out much earlier and the reasons for it branching are less obvious - I found it a bit harder to keep track of the various routes.

Conclusion

Overall a really solid sequel to a great visual novel that explores more of the Steins;Gate world, adds new characters, and has its own, darker identity. If you’re a fan of the original you should lap this up, although it does have noticeable changes in structure, such as the semi-frequent time skips and the change to how the routes branch out, that you may not like as much as the original. Despite me saying I wasn’t as keen on these changes compared to the original’s format, I think they did a good job of changing things up enough to give it its own identity while still keeping the core essence of what made the original so good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I think that this weakens the experience of the original Steins;Gate (which I thought was incredible). While the idea of it is wonderful--with no spoilers for this or the original, it explains the central plot point of the original's true ending and explores the ramifications of the events prior--the writing and plot development is significantly weaker, and it has far too much fanservice and fluff. Some scenes in this... really sucked, honestly, which is not a problem I recall having with the first one.

It also has a much broader scope than the original, which in my opinion misses out on the thing that made it so brilliant. The original tells the story of one man suddenly plunged into a world beyond knowledge or comprehension, and the agony he goes through as that world closes in around him. The key here is contrast. It's the stark difference between the "before" and the "after" that lets it work its magic. Here, the scale starts big, and... really doesn't change a whole lot. It's so much less visceral.

It's not all bad. It does have a couple great scenes and a couple great songs in the soundtrack, but I just don't think those scenes and songs are worth going through the rest of the VN. Just use your imagination to explain that plot point from the original's true ending, like you would before this were released. It's fine without having this game to explain it.\t\t\t\t\t
