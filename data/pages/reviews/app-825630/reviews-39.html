\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a little mixed on how I feel about this entry to Steins;Gate, but first and foremost, I really enjoyed it. Like I really, really liked it. It was absolutely amazing, just as the first one was. However, I will say that it is absolutely lacking when compared to the first. Pretty much each and every aspect of this one is aaaaaaalmost as good as the first. It's like it falls juuuust short on everything. Like I said, it's AWESOME, but somehow it's just...not as good. It feels almost as though they got a different group to make this game. Like it feels like a really good fanfic that doesn't quite give you the same feeling of the source material. Except for maybe the voice acting. It was spot on.

The emotions you feel are gripping, the sci-fi elements are all very thought provoking, and the overall plot is very interesting. If you liked the first one, you'll absolutely like this one, too, but it's just slightly less interesting.

One thing I do love about it, though, is how it connects to the first one. It had quite a few eye-opening moments where I was like, "ooooh, okay, so that's why that one thing happened in the first one!"

If I have one major complaint, it's the art. For some reason they changed up the art style almost entirely. The first one was extremely unique and had really cool shading and coloring. This one, while in certain parts attempting something similar, sorta just looks like cliche anime art. It bothered me. lol

It's like I give it a 10/10 because it's a masterpiece, but it's also not as good as the 10/10 I gave the first one. It's like...a 9.99/10 lol\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just barely recommend it. This thing is a mixed bag if there ever was one!

It's 50% a cool look into what happens to Beta Okabe, with the stakes cranked to 11, new insight into interesting characters (particularly Moeka), fairly intriguing mystery that could've been handled better, but your mileage will very depending on the order you happen to finish each route. Unfortunately, then there's the other 50%, which is just... *ugh...* I don't even know where to begin. Filler, rehashing, fan service, repeating plot points (2 routes are virtually exactly the same for hours of content, the only difference being you fail at the end of Leskinen's route). Then you have Amadeus, the only thing I can say is that it could have been worse? She's exactly like Chris, but everyone says she isn't for some reason, but she has to be sacrificed in every world line to reach Steins;Gate, so you'd think there'd be emotional weight, but they only bothered to give it any in Maho's route, which you're likely going to skip over if you're trying to be efficient since as good as it is, it doesn't matter narratively at all. Meanwhile you're going to be thinking in the back of your head "This is just an excuse to have everyone's favorite waifu in the sequel for marketing purposes." It's so transparent and I hate it, the entire premise of this world is that Chris has to die and the human race is destined to go extinct. There are more problems too, continuity issues. It's made clear at the end of the original VN that Moeka and Okabe still knew each other and were looking for an IBN in Steins;Gate, so I'm supposed to assume that not only do they not know each other at all and SERN is not looking for an IBN anymore, but Okabe is deathly afraid of her? It's dumb. One of the main routes that you *have* to completely disjointed because every time you get anywhere useful, the world line changes and nothing that you just spent hours and hours on matters at all. Another one is Okabe's attitude. I get depression, I get PTSD, I get what it's going for, but this portrayal of him is so over the top and ridiculous that I just want to die every time they rehash the same conversation about what they're going to do about the whole situation (which repeats at least once every chapter, it's exhausting). PTSD does not rob you of any sense of logic all the time. Even if he had been 100% convinced that a perfect world didn't exist, why wouldn't he at least jump back to the Alpha world line? At least that way humanity won't go extinct, and the attractor fields will most likely turn against SERN one day. It's not like he's still obsessed over Mayuri, when he went there by mistake, he was down with staying until Chris basically forced the issue. When you raise the stakes like Steins;Gate 0 does, those questions become important, it's not a matter of which friend to save in this sequel, it's the fate of the world! The story is generally just sloppy.

Story aside, there are a few other things to note. The voice acting is great!. Probably better than the original VN, but a bit worse than the anime adaptation of the original. The music is better than the original, hands down! I wish we'd gotten some classic Piano Gate of Steiner in the mix, but it's generally such an improvement I can't complain. I like the art a lot better than the original VN. It saves the whole thing, to be honest.

If you're a *huge* fan of Steins;Gate, buy it, it has some neat stuff. Every other spin-off is better, so buy Elite and Phenogram first, but you aren't throwing your cash away here either. If you don't actually need more though, best to pass on this.\t\t\t\t\t
