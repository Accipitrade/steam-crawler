The question that everyone asks:
How is this tittle compared to the Original Steins;Gate VN? Is this VN worth buying/reading?

The Steins;Gate series is like a Bic Mac. The original Steins;Gate being the burger itself and Steins;Gate 0 being that special sauce. They complete each other. So why compare them to one another? What you need to know is that Steins;Gate 0 isn't like any other VNs out there. It isn't sequel and it isn't exactly a prequel either. Rather, it's something in between. A "midquel", people call it. It is set just before the ending of the Original Steins;Gate true ending.

I have to make one thing clear for everyone. It's important so I'll say it 3 times. It is NOT. It is NOT. I repeat, Steins;Gate 0 is NOT an alternative end to Steins;Gate! and it is definitely not a "what if" spin-off either! It's not an add-on item, it's a necessity for the Steins;Gate series as a whole. What people should be doing is immediately start Steins;Gate 0 after reading/rereading Steins;Gate so you wouldn't feel "disconnected".

So, yes, the past and future of this Steins;Gate 0 are set in stone, there's no changing. We know what happened before and after the events of Steins;Gate 0 from reading the original VN. Steins;Gate 0 serves as an explanation to what happens right before the Steins;Gate ending and reading it will make you appreciate the original Steins;Gate ending much, much more.

Then, what is Steins;Gate 0 about? It's too easy to spoil the game so I'm just going to tell you that it's about Okabe who's weak, wimpy, depressed and almost pathetic. Doesn't sound too appealing does it now? but that's a human thing right? It's what made Okabe such a great character and believe me, its all worth it. And it's just more Steins;Gate right? Who wouldn't want more Steins;Gate?

But of course, the game still has it's flaws. Some things weren't as clear as they should be and sometimes down right confusing. And there's no major climax since it's a "midquel" (Tip: rewatch/reread Steins;Gate ending). It isn't perfect I know, but still, you gotta get it. You're not experiencing the Steins;Gate series at it's fullest if you missed out on Steins;Gate 0.

This is a D-Review from the year 2036 and I'm here to pass on a message to you:
The only way to reach Steins;Gate worldline is through Steins;Gate 0.
Stop reading reviews.

The story the game is telling feels unnecessary, especially considering that we already know how it ends. Most of the new characters feel like they shouldn't be there at all.
The new artstyle feels weird at first, especially if you played original and 0 back to back but you get used to it. But for unknown reason the sprites from the original start to reappear alongside the new ones, Mixture of styles doesn't look organic at all.
It's an alright game if you are a s;g fan and want to get all the content possible. But if you just liked the original - new game will feel like a filler episode for you and probably won't add to any experience you had with original game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr If you like the original you will like zero

If you played the original Steins;Gate you have to play Zero

S;G Zero is a bit different from the original S;G, It shows a depressive version of Okabe that gave up on saving Kuriso. The gameplay is similar, but zero uses branching timelines, instead of the more "choose your girl" timelines of the original.

If you watched the anime, and though It was weaker than the original S;G you have to play this game and see It for yourself, the branching paths are too complex to adapt to anime, so the show ends skipping a bunch of interesting stuff from the game.

If you havent watched the anime, play the game first and watch the show later, even though the show skips a bunch of stuff, they added some extra scenes that really makes It worth.

Some differences from the original S;G:
- Answering phone messages now shows three options, instead of having to click on hyperlinks
- You play as multiple character through the game, in the original you always saw the world through Okabe's eyes, in Zero sometimes you see through Okabe, Suzuha, Maho, etc...
- Sometimes the timeline change to some crazy stuff, I cant say much without spoiler

10/10 would cry again

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy expectations for the sequel of my favourite told story ever where high and thankfully where met and even exceeded.

The game is set in a strange time which I will not spoil and feels like at the start a what if story. But once you go through each non-linear walkthrough and get the six endings everything starts to make sense and answers the question you will be asking "why was this made when Steins gate's ending was perfect?".

The voice acting once again is top notch and Mamoru Miyana really outshines everyone with his top notch skills. The sprites all look fresh and great to look at, but once again there is a lack of many sprites for each character. The characters are even more built on and thankfully works in the overall story of steins;gate.

The "main" story is told with two endings and in my opinion are just as satisfying in different ways then the original. It even has possibly the best steins;gate moment.

Once again the creator Chiyomaru has knocked it out of the part with his perfect storytelling and can't wait to see what he does in the near future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was a real fan of the first game, however towards the end, while a great final ending, one of the branching paths was intriguing enough on its own that it could tell its own story just before the end. And this game is basically that taken to its logical conclusion, and fully explores the story leading up to the true ending of Steins gate.
In that way, it can be considered a sequel in almost all aspects except the first game's ending. I kind of wish I was able to play this just before finishing the first game.
There isn't too much different compared to the first. The user interface is more clean and modern, and the chracters often branch into segments that come from their point of view, not just Okabe's. The story also noticeably changes immediately at key points that lead to multiple endings, which does make the story feel less repetitive going through each path.
Still a great game, and makes the first game even better despite how wholesome it felt.\t\t\t\t\t
