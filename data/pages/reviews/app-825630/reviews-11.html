\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't really think this game needs a recommendation, this is a direct sequel to STEINS;GATE which continues the story by explaining the events that led to the iconic finale of the first game. If you liked the setting and characters of the original, by all means play this one too. It expands on the world and develops characters further, introducing some new characters along the way.

This story is significantly shorter than other SciAdv game, though there is still a lot of content to be found here, including the usual multiple fun endings.

I missed the sense of mystery and unknown that is so prominent across all games in the Science Adventure series. Although you know in advance where all the events are going to lead, the writers introduced many new threads that the reader will have no prior knowledge of. However, I found all of them very easy to see through, without unexpected consequences to the story, and not really contributing much to the greater picture.

Unlike R;N or C;C, there is no map to guide you and no geographical features participate in the gameplay, just like it was with the original S;G. However, you can still find the real-world locations easily by following the many hints left in the script.

I love how the original music themes return in revamped compositions, and blend nicely with the new themes in a very consistent style. The new art also matches the original style very closely, personally I prefer the detailed and crispy clear style of C;C, but I still can't say anything bad about S;G's dim, faded, brushed drawings.

To my surprise, this is the first game in SciAdv series where I can possibly imagine reaching the end with no walkthrough. It would still take a pretty long time (and the way the final route activation flag registers is bugged as ♥♥♥♥), but at least it does not require superhuman effort or brute-force search. Still, I recommend the CoZ's guide, as well as their translation fixes patch, which is really top notch.\t\t\t\t\t
