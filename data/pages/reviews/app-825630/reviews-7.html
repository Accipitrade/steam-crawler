\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, I'd like to point out that Steins;Gate 0 is neither a sequel nor a prequel to the original S;G VN, however they're connected in some way (to put it in a non spoiling way). The 0 in the title is kind of misleading. Play S;G before S;G 0.

Story
Honestly one of the best VNs I've ever played. The story can be a bit hard to follow with the different routes and world lines and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ are there lots of twists to it (which I like tbh). As I don't have anything more to say about the story, other than it simply being wonderful yet tragic in a way, I'll head straight to the "Gameplay".

Gameplay
The gameplay, if you can even call it that, is simply reading and listening to the voice actors' amazing works. The only way to interact or influence the story is to use your phone for answering (or ignoring) phone calls. There is also the menu you can interact with, but I digress as I think it's not that important to go over.

Art
As for the CGs and sprites, they're really well made. The art style is a different from S;G's and as someone who watched the animes first I was not used to those detailed sceneries and character designs it at the beginning, but grew fond of it after some time. I'd also like to mention that the artists seem to have an obsession with well proportioned and/or naked butts. Seriously, these guys did not cut corners when it came to that.

Soundtrack
Astonishing. They really add atmosphere to the gameplay. I especially love the title theme "Messenger" and Gate of Steiner. They reused some or the tracks from the original S;G and made new ones for this game as well.

Oh yeah and before I get to my conclusion I wanted to mention that for some reason, other reviewers have so little hours in this game. Did they speedrun the damn game or am I just a slow reader? Seriously, there's no way one can reach the true ending in less than 30 hours, right?

Conclusion
As you can tell by now, I really love this game and would definitely recommend it to those who are interested in hard sci-fi with a complicated plot and have enough time on their hands to play for possibly more than 80 hours. Just, please, don't rush it and make sure to play through at least two endings - it's worth it.

With that being said, thanks for reading my review of Steins;Gate 0 and...

El. Psy. Kongroo.
