\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished this about a couple minutes ago and I’m not going to lie when I say that I've been like a hermit and reading this night and day for about 2-3 days. The whole experience was intense and I loved every single second of it. The way the game is setup, it's best experienced when you 100% the game. Fills all the holes and gaps that might accrue over time, though you could always google it for a quick answer. Took me around ~25h to 100%. Right now, the anime is coming out for this and I haven’t watched a single episode yet. Going to wait for it to complete and then marathon it like I did this. Not going to see daylight for the next day or two when that happens.

More on to a quick rundown of my experience. This is more technical/gameplay related and has nothing to do with story or plot whatsoever as everyone in the review section will say the same thing: It’s a masterpiece, just buy it (I also agree with this logic). This game when I played it had no bugs or glitches. There were a couple grammar mistakes I noticed in it like having “complement” instead of “compliment” or accidently omitting a word from a sentence but the idea is still understood in the end. Trying to get 100% on the story was kind of interesting. I reached one ending and then realized that if this is setup anything like the first one, then I messed up and have to spam skip through a majority of the story to get to certain points to hard save to get different endings. I had a guide with me the whole time, telling me where to hard save and went from there (highly recommend this). The points where you have to hard save are kind of hard to skip through. You’ll fly by the dialogue and then hit one of these points that just stop you. The points it tells you were to stop diverge pretty drastically from the different choices you make. You may have a couple repeated words/scenes but majority of it is different. I only had to really fly by on the first ‘playthrough’ I messed up on so it wasn’t that much of a big deal. Keyboard controls, like the first one, are really weird when I looked at them. Trying to get second+ endings is where I changed to controller. Keyboard is somewhat easy to adapt to because you only care for about 3 buttons but I wanted controller so I can sit in my bed. And last but not least, Mayuri with a scarf is the cutest thing I've ever seen, and her and Maho are best girl.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros
- The story is very good, there's always things happening that you didn't see coming
- (Almost) every character is lovable
- The graphics are very good too, there's a lot of improvement since the first game, especially with sprites
- There's many different CGs

Cons
- The mix of new and old sprites (from the original Steins;Gate game) look weird sometimes
- There are scenes in the future where characters suddenly look 20 years younger too and it's a little off-putting
- Also in some CGs it looks like some characters are missing a jaw, but it's okay, at least the picture made me laugh

I'd highly recommend this game. One of my favourite visual novels, along with Saya no Uta and Amnesia: Memories. I think it takes a lot of talent to write such a scenario, going back and forth between past and future. Now I kind of want to play the first game again.\t\t\t\t\t
