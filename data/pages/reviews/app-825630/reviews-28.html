\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImmediately bought steins;gate 0 after finishing the first one at full price. I really liked the idea of having an alternate reality where everything is a lot darker and depressing. I liked how Okabe was psychologically affected after his failure, and how it lingers in the story. I think most of the new characters are a good addition to the story and you learn more about the other characters as well. The Sci-Fi aspect like A.I was well done and learned more about artificial intelligence.

The things I didn’t really enjoy (just a personal preference) is that there wasn’t really romance or there was little of it. In the first game, there were endings that had romance and it felt conclusive. In this, characters assumed that Okabe is in love with Kurisu. Which I don’t think that there’s a problem but I kind of wish that he moved on and liked/fell in love with another character, at least in another ending.

Another con I have in Steins;gate 0 some of the endings were inconclusive and it felt like the writers could of put a little bit more effort in the endings and actually end it without having it open to interpretation. The only routes I really enjoyed was Kurisu’s route and the true ending was okay but still flawed, at least for me on how it ended. A lot of the routes to me, felt like is this really it?

I wish the story focused more on potentially finding another method of steins gate. Would have been cool to experience gamma or delta worldline. Or even have an ending with another worldline and with at least having Kurisu alive. But I understand that writing it would be very complicated and I believe it’ll be difficult to implement, but the potential I think at least in the steins;gate universe is possible.

Despite the flaws, I still think Steins;gate0 is a very enjoyable experience and living up to be a great sequel to the story. There’s potential to be better but still enjoyable nonetheless. If you didn’t enjoy the first steins;gate then you won’t like this one either. If you’re a minimalist reader I suggest finishing Kurisu’s route then go to the true ending since I believe you need to complete Kurisu’s ending in order to unlock the true ending.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm writing this shortly after completion. Contrary to my expectations, this game is somewhat of a continuation of the story line of the first; I had heard it had a new cast and a mostly-unrelated story, but this is untrue. Nearly every major plot point from the first game is reiterated, which is good if, like me, you've forgotten much of it; but bad if you haven't played the original and decide to play it afterward.

The Good
Explores in-depth multiple concepts from the first game that are glossed over or have implications larger than their narrow role within the original story. The final chapters of the first game raised some interesting ideas which justified their own story, which is told here. Some additional backstory justifies and expands upon some major plot points from the first game. This exploration introduces some new but related concepts, some of which are quite novel by genre standards, and are fascinating to consider.

Great soundtrack, some of Takeshi ♥♥♥'s finest work. I'll often stop reading in order to listen, some of which is quite listenable outside of the game.

Plenty of content. The paths are structured more like a traditional VN, with each path being more substantial than in the original game, with some plot points unique to that path. You'll want to see all of them, although some are better (and more substantial) than others. Unlike the first game, you're unlikely to be left scratching your head (or consulting a walkthrough) because you can't find the one arcane path to the ending you want; they're all pretty easy to find.

The Neutral
There is plenty of content, but the game felt overlong to me, overstaying its welcome about halfway through. The small amount of filler is mostly a palate cleanser, but this includes some blatant fanservice, which I found ill-fitting with the tone of the rest of the game. The filler also tends to fall upon overused anime tropes.
All 'gameplay' choices are still made via your cell phone. There are phone dialog choices but they have no impact on the story.

The Bad
I feel bad for loading this part of my review, but there are plenty of nits to pick.
The most obvious is the fact that there are more than a few editing errors throughout the script. Grammar, spacing, dialog flow between lines, putting one character's name when context suggests another was referenced, putting the wrong word, missing a word, duplicating a word or sentence fragment. Sometimes it seemed like spellcheck changed something to the wrong word. It felt like the script needed another pass by someone with fresh eyes. To be fair, there is a LOT of text here, and it's easy for one's eyes to gloss over editing it all. Minor phrasing/terminology from the first game seems to have been changed (perhaps to match the anime, unsure.) The TIPS are edited to a lower quality, and dates seem to have an inconsistent format. It may be a game limitation, but the dates only display in the backlog, never in the main text window, leading to some confusion during scene changes.

One dialog with an antagonist was poorly written and/or edited, to the point that I had no idea what was going on or why things resolved the way they did. It wasn't terribly plot-important, but it did stick out.

Plot holes rear their ugly heads, a few times at the least. Character prepares X in case of contingency Y, which occurs, to which they say the only option is now Z, when X is clearly the better option. Especially egregious given Z is out of character. Another time, an event occurs when the established prerequisite is not present (and could not reasonably be expected to be present), with not so much as a handwave as to how it occurred or that it somehow was present. A different time, a character inexplicably surrenders when they seem to clearly have the upper hand.

A couple dramatic reveal scenes are overwrought, where I had already figured it out yet the story was trying... to... reveal... it... slowly...

Last but hardly least, the true/golden ending (it's a VN, of course it has one) highly disappointed me. The various paths set up interesting mysteries, the protagonist usually remarking on them, and a large fraction are never explained or resolved. It goes far beyond what is normally left as an 'open ending' or 'sequel hook', and they would be considered plot holes if they weren't remarked upon, implying that they had answers that would eventually be revealed. I couldn't believe it when I finished the game and learned online that that was it, there was nothing else. I was certain I hadn't yet found the true ending, which would resolve all of the loose plot threads, but I had and it hadn't. If you brush up on the ending of the first game, you will feel better about things, but not by much. I hope that a movie or something ties up all the loose ends, although that seems unlikely at this point. I was psyched for the possibilities that could happen in the true ending, yet the result felt weak and abrupt, as if there weren't time to write a proper ending.

Verdict
If you enjoyed the first game for its sci-fi aspects, you will enjoy this one for providing plenty of novel ideas to chew on. If you liked the major characters from the first game, they get plenty of screen time, although the minor characters mostly get sidelined in favor of the new ones. The tone is more serious overall. The (Japanese) voice acting is quite good, usually conveying more emotion than the text. Unfortunately there are plenty of missed opportunities, particularly in the way the plot is resolved, and the lack of expanded interactivity (yeah it's a VN but it's easy to imagine there being a little more with managing relationships or information.) Overall I'd say the highs overcome the lows to justify this VN, if you're up for the time investment.\t\t\t\t\t
