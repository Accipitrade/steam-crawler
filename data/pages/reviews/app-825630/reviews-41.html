\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteins;Gate 0 is not as good as Steins;Gate, but depending on what you're looking for from it, it's still a solid game. This review is assuming that the reader has played Steins;Gate (no spoilers though) because you really, REALLY should play that (or at least watch the anime) before Steins;Gate 0.

Pros:
- Great character development for certain characters, particularly Rintaro (this game dives into his depression and anxiety following the events of Steins;Gate), Maho (a new character in Steins;Gate 0 who is a surprisingly good addition), Moeka (you see a different side of her in this game), and Mayuri (I love her story in one particular ending)
- Subtle phone interaction matters more - things like not answering a call or shutting off the phone can change which storyline you're on
- More messaging features than just plain text, like stickers
- Some storylines and endings are enjoyable
- The game's art is generally quite good

Cons:
- Convoluted nonlinear plot that can be really difficult to keep track of
- One particular plot twist changes depending on which storyline you're on, but the game never really explains why
- Way too much unneccessary fanservice
- A lot of the returning characters (like Faris and Luka) don't really contribute much to the Steins;Gate 0 plot
- Reuses some art assets from Steins;Gate, but changes others, which can be really disorienting - especially when the game clearly wants characters to seem familiar but their sprites and expressions are totally different
- Shorter game than Steins;Gate, even with some padding
- Different localization/translation choices that are kind of weird if you play this right after Steins;Gate
- The true ending is just okay

There are a few other things that can be good or bad, depending on your preferences. For example, Mayuri's cosplay friends appear in this game and are either a fun break from the heavier story stuff, or a pointless padding device, depending on whether you enjoy them or not.

Overall, there are enough good things in Steins;Gate 0 that I would recommend it, but be aware that you may be disappointed if you go into this game expecting anything better than a mediocre spinoff.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving a chance to read/play this game first before watching the anime was a good choice, (the choice of steins gate?!) and it certainly didn't disappoint. It is a very sad and emo story, that tackles a lot of heavy themes and you WILL cry depending on how much of a soft boiled egg you are. The multiple viewpoints in the storytelling was a step up for me as it opened up another layer to the main characters lives instead of everything through Okabe's perspective, and that's something I really liked. I recommend going through all the endings, because they're easy to get if you know where/when the phone triggers happen, and you can delve into more of the characters motivations and backgrounds if you're a fan of the series like me.

It is also more streamlined as far as phone triggers go, making a more smooth gaming experience that lets you focus on the story which is what I think they were going for. SoOO much easier to follow and less complicated than the first installment.

There's some fan servicey bits sprinkled throughout the story which at times can be cringey(but that just me, you do you), but nothing is as bad as the "American" accents portrayed in the VN. It's just nails on the chalkboard bad but in a way, lovable once you discover the memes- or maybe you already have. Anywho that's probably my biggest complaint. but overall I really enjoyed this VN. If you're a fan of steins;gate or watched the *meh* anime and are looking for more, then this is a worthy sequel that I highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
