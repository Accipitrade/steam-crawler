\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy first review so bear with me.
SG0 is a sequel to the previous game SG which takes place from the "bad ending" of the first game.
As someone who never played a VN I didn't know what to expect so I can't define what's the standard for a VN should be.
It's important to note I didn't play the first SG VN and all my info about SG is from rewatching the anime few times to connect the dots.
Let's start:

Pros:
* The voice acting is top notch and high quality.
* The soundtrack is beautiful and memorable.
* The visuals makes you to stop for a moment and appreciate the artwork.
* The plot is really interesting and makes you want to learn more.
* The "tips" section is a nice touch and informative.

Cons:
* Re-using old models from the previous game which can be an eye sore.
* Some of the gallery art work looks cheap.
* Would need a guide to continue if you aren't familier with VN genre.
* SG0 doesn't connect all the dots which will make you to do your reasearch after.

Conclusion:
I really recommand anyone to give Steins;Gate and Steins;Gate 0 a chance if you don't play VN.
I feel I experienced the best VN I could have started with and really hope to see more out of SG.

The Steins;Gate Series\t

*Spoiler Free Review

I would like to start this off by saying this. The Steins;Gate series is PERFECT. I absolutely adored both games and it is probably my favourite series.\t

BUT.\t

It doesn't necessarily mean it is perfect for anyone. If someone had played the game for an hour and never played it again, or had called it boring, yes i would be insulted, but i would also understand.

Also, please play the first Steins:Gate game before buying this, or it literally will make ZERO SENSE.\t

Story Overview:\t
Steins;Gate 0 is the sequel to the story that takes place in Akihabara. The story unfolds beneath the eyes of a self-proclaimed mad scientist: Hououin Kyouma. As a sequel, we already know the ending of this game before going into it, so we already know the past and the future. In this game, we are only watching the events that led to the true ending of the first game.


Should i buy this if i played the first Steins;Gate?\t
Duh, if you've finished the first game, you would probably be craving this immediately. If you haven't played the other game, go buy it now, if you aren't drawn in within the first 2 hours, you can always refund it.

So what makes Steins;Gate such a great story?\t
In all honesty, i do not know myself. It's a good game. Buy it.

The Goods:\t

-Japanese Shaman Girls
-Voice Acting
-Storyline
-Music
-Maho
-Plot
-Literally everything.\t

Spoilers for Steins;Gate and some spoilers for Steins;Gate 0\t

Tl;dr\t at bottom. Read the whole thing if you are bored, have time, or something like that,

First off, if you have not played the original Steins;Gate or at least watched the anime, do so as this visual novel relies heavily on prior knowledge to the previous story. You can try playing it as a standalone, but none of the references, themes, plotlines, etc. will make sense. This review, while I am aware should be a review of solely Steins;Gate 0, what makes this visual so powerful for me was how well it built up and took things even further from the prequel. That is why I feel like comparisons have to be made as doing a standalone review for this visual novel will not give proper credit as nothing will make sense and the emotional appeal will simply be lacking.

Steins;Gate 0 continues the story of the original Steins;Gate where Okabe Rintarou fails to save Makise Kurisu and instead saves Shiina Mayuri. However, life for poor Okabe is far from over as the legacy of Kurisu still haunts the world, causing an inevitable occurrence of World War III over the race for knowledge of time machines that only Okabe and his acquaintances are aware of. What will Okabe do knowing that his failure will cause World War III to happen, yet he does not do anything?

Thematically compared to the first game, the focus is moreso on AI over time travel, though time travel still plays an important part in this story. One thing I absolutely love is how much in detail both the prequel and this visual novel goes into extreme depths describing each of the theories. In the prequel, it was the application and understanding of different black hole theories and how the transfer of memories work from a neurophysiology perspective. In this case, we get a reiteration of how memories neurologically work and how that can be applied to be making a completely independent and essentially living AI. What I really love is how much effor the developers put into making time travel and an independent and living AI seem completely possible. While obviously some of the finer details had to be skipped because these concepts are not possible (as of right now at least), I truly felt immersed and even thinking that these things could be possible.

Comapred to the first one, I personally feel like the game is much darker (e.g. appearance of multiple assassins and while it definitely has its humorous moments ( WHERE? WHERE ARE THE JAPANESE SHAMAN GIRLS, LINTAHLO?\t), the overall flow and feel for the story is more depressing, if that makes sense. There are definitely more action scenes (e.g. we can finally see Mr. Braun and Suzuha be super badass killers), which I personally have no problems with as it makes sense to me with everyone (i.e. America, Russia, Japan) vying for knowledge of time machines and will do anything to do so. Also, while without a doubt the first game had its tear jerking moments, for me, the sequel had even more, which dealt with unrequited love, destroyed love, abandoment, loss, and so on and so forth. Just like the first game, you get multiple endings that focus on different characters and each ending is quite different from one another that makes playing the game multiple times worth it.

Voice acting wise, all the voice actors/actresses reprise their roles and they all still do an amazing job, especially Mamoru Miyano as Okbae Rinatoru and Kana Hanazawa as Mayuri Shiina. For me, those two steal the show with their absolutely stellar voice acting. Also, major props to Yoji Ueda, who voiced Alexis Lesinknen, for having a scene where he only speaks English, which consisted of a lot of science vocabulary that was heavily focused on neurophysiology, (pretty damn good mind you) and has to speak in fragmented Japanese to give off the impression of an American speaking poor Japanese. It is pretty humorous, but still very good. In addition, I never realized Megumi Han, who voiced Shiina Kagari, could sing; her voice acting without a doubt is very impressive, but I did not realize her singing was that good too (she sang one of the ending themes). To me, it was because of such outstanding voice acting that made the emotional moments, the humorous moments, even the simple moments, that much more enjoyable.

Art wise, I feel like one could see it either an increase or a decrease in quality. For me, it is kind of a mix, though more leaning towards an increase. Certain scenes, like the opening theme, look absolutely amazing, and character models look great. Lot of small details and care were definitely put into the models to make them as unique and interesting as possible, such as Okabe looking constantly sad and hunched over, almost completely defeated due to the previous story. However, what really made me sad was that certain still images lacked the care and focus. In one scene, Okabe was supposed to look depressed and out of it, but the way his eyes were oriented made him look more cross eyed than anything and it was difficult not to find it silly. There was another scene where Okabe and two others were doing an interview and while Okabe looked alright, the other two just looked kind of silly and out of focus. I personally feel like more details could have been added, but that's just me.

Music wise, it still continues to impress me. They brought back the main theme of the first novel, Gate of Steiner, and is now used in a slow and sad piano variation whenever there is a depressing or emotinoal moment. The new theme song for this novel is Messenger and it definitely has a more ominous, dramatic feel to it and is used whenever a new revelation or epic moment is happening, usually around the end of an ending. Kanako Itō, who sang Hacking to the Gate in the original anime, Zwei, and Megumi Han all make an appearance as singers for either the opening or endings and, I am no music judge, but they do a pretty good job.

Tl;dr\t, if you have played/watched the original Steins;Gate and want more, Steins;Gate 0 will cover you. With great voice acting, fitting music, an intriguing storyline, more or less great art, Steins;Gate 0 will keep you interested and emotionally invested. While it may have plot holes and inconsistencies, it sticks together pretty well as a whole and to me, will hold a special place as an amazing sequel to the already amazing Steins;Gate.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteins;Gate 0 is amazing visual novel.
Its sequel to Steins;Gate. The story starts with one of failed attempts to change past in original game and explore possibilities from there.

In my case i like it even more then Steins;Gate, mainly because Okabe doesnt have the annoying mad sciencist personality anymore. He is slightly gloomy but otherwise behaves like normal human being this time.

Story is very good, thrilling, emotional. Our group of ppl isnt changing past deliberately this time, insted its done by unclear means and you can only imagine want was the reason. It means everytime world line shifts, you have no idea why or what changed and it makes story more exciting.
My only complain here would be that "true ending" isnt as good as i expected, it kinda has no impact. There are so many mysteries and unexplained things in this VN, if you expected true end to explain stuff and give you closure (i did expect that) then you are out of luck.

Both sounds and visuals are executed mostly flawless as you would expect.

I like this game a lot and would recommend it to all fans of Steins;Gate\t\t\t\t\t
