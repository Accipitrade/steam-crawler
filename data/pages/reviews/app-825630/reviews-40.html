\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf the Original Steins;Gate is the king among Visual Novels, this Steins;Gate 0 would be the Shadow King.

I'll write some mild spoilers so you've been warned. You shouldn't even be reading this Review if you haven't finished Steins;Gate.

S;G0 is best described as 'midquel' as it takes place exactly after Okabe arrives from the past and fails to save Kurisu. That's it, if there's no 0, there will be no Steins;Gate's True Ending. It is NOT recommended however to do a 'proper' skip by halting your S;G progress and play this then jump back later as it diminishes the experience/effect as opposed to playing it normally.

Quality wise, this is no less than the Original Steins;Gate's quality. If you liked Steins;Gate (Graphic, Audio/Soundtrack, Voice Overs, Steam Cloud/Achievements/Cards) then there's no reason not to like this 0. If anything, they now get the graphic/art up on par to modern's standard right from the get-go and it doesn't need fan-patch like S;G to make it enjoyable.

There's a bit difference in terms of gameplay though. In S;G, you're required to set flags which are easily miss-able as they're not prominent to reach the routes you want. In 0 they improved it somehow and requires you to choose certain visible options e.g answering calls or not to determine the route you take and reach the endings you want. This is better of course, but you will still need walkthrough most of the times. I still recommend you to play at least once, going in blind, no matter which ending you will get.

Overall, the game is about 50 hours long if you read/listen to the story, so get ready to (more) reading.

Score: 9/10 because I'm a bit tired with Okabe's endless whining in 0 before he finally knows the answer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for a long-a** review you've come to the right place. There are more hard-core fans than me, but I've gotten every ending in the original Steins;gate, the new Steins;gate 0, and watched the anime, so I feel I have a pretty decent grasp on it.

If you want the TLDR, I'd say that overall Steins;gate 0 is great, but for me personally it didn't live up to the amazingness of the original. Overall I'd give Steins;gate 0 about an 8.5/10 and would recommend it to most people.

Now let's get into the knitty gritty. If you haven't played the original, what are you doing here? Go play that masterpiece first and then come back. Spoilers for the original will be below. Turn back now. Ok, so as for the story of Steins;gate 0, it can be a little confusing at first, because it actually doesn't pick up after the true ending of Steins;gate. It picks up after the "Mayuri Ending" where Mayuri is safe, but you sacrificed Kurisu to get here, and WWIII is still on the horizon because the metal Upa saves Kurisu's papers from being destroyed in the plane crash.

However just the beginning is arguably my biggest problem with Steins;gate 0. I understand the game developers were faced with a lose/lose situation, but I'm still sad they couldn't have figured something out. Steins;gate was never made with a sequel in mind. So in Steins;gate 0, I had the feeling that nothing I did really mattered, because it was just another ending that deviated from the True Ending of the original. In the end, I feel that this was better than destroying the ending that everyone already loved, but it doesn't seem like they had any good options here.

Also in Steins;gate 0, there are a lot more alternate time lines and situations. For me, it was much more confusing that the original. It wasn't really clear how my choices affected the timelines. Admittedly, the texts in the original Steins;gate that you send are pretty arbitrary too, but at least when you make the time leap it is easy to understand who caused what, and what you are trying to fix.

In addition to the path of the story, after Steins;gate, we have no Kurisu, and we have depressed Okabe. From a realistic perspective it obviously makes sense. But, Kurisu was one of the strongest and most interesting characters from the original. Additionally, everybody loved Okabe because of his goofy Chunibyou antics and to see him be generally more depressed and reserved in Steins;gate 0 just isn't as interesting. Again, this comes back to the point that the original wasn't made with a sequel in mind and I don't think they really had any other options to make it better while still holding together the original story.

Of course there are some new characters to take into account. I'd say they are a mixed bag. There are a couple good new characters, but also several more that, more or less just feel like side characters. They don't have much of a personality and they're just there sometimes.

Dialogue has always been one of Steins;gate's strong points, and it is as good as ever in Steins;gate 0. Very occasionally it sounded a little too "anime-ified" to me, but that's very knit picky. The naturally progression of the conversations is amazing and makes reading for hours on end seem like no chore at all.

The art is similar to the original, with many scenes just being recycled from it, but I thought the new art was not quite up to snuff. In the original VN, Steins;gate had a strange but very characteristic dreamy artstyle. If you've played it you know what I mean where there are strange patterns in people's clothes, their hair, and whatnot. Steins;gate 0 is a bit more flat, for lack of a better term. Personally I thought the original artstyle was better. This one seems a bit more robotic like it was just made to meet the anime standard. Additionally, there were some CGs that I just found a little strange.

My last point is definitely knit-picky and my own personal preference, but there's almost no sciency talk in Steins;gate 0! I enjoyed in the original there were hour long conversations talking about different theories of time travel and many other things. It was actually interesting. I wish they had a chance to talk a bit more about fringe scientific topics and teach the player while reading and enjoying it.

Overall, the main cast and story are still definitely enjoyable. Obviously I've put a bit of time into this game to get all the endings, but to me personally the quality has sunk a little below the original Steins;gate, mostly due to the fact that they hadn't planned for a sequel. I don't feel that anyone is really obligated to play Steins;gate 0 after the original because the original "True Ending" isn't really affected, but if you want to explore the other World Line, it's definitely still fun and can expand your knowledge about the world and characters.\t\t\t\t\t
