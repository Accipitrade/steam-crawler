\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSTEINS;GATE 0 isn't uncontroversial. It did some things right, and some things wrong. But I am not here to discuss something that's been beaten to death already. I am here to answer the following: Is this worth getting, having read the original STEINS;GATE? Additionally, did it live up to its predecessor?

The answer to both questions is a resounding 'YES!'. STEINS;GATE 0 tells a compelling story. Okabe's efforts to overcome his personal demons and time itself take centre stage, but in classic STEINS;GATE fashion the rest of the crew are pivotal to such efforts. Hours of buildup, hours of struggle, hours of agonising; all build up to a satisfactory conclusion that ties in very neatly with the conclusion of the original STEINS;GATE. Admittedly, the actual ending sequence of the game needs work. Without spoiling too much, I can say that it felt really rushed and more like an afterthought. This doesn't detract from the rest of the game, but I leave that as a disclaimer.

What about the gameplay?
Just like the original, you do get to make decisions with regards to what messages to send and what calls to answer. These decisions dictate the flow of the story. I recommend a walkthrough after getting your first ending (one of those flowcharts, to be specific) since its plot isn't as straightforward as the original. There are two main 'routes', with numerous side endings. As an aside, you don't have to send a long sequence of correct messages just to get the true ending. It's much simpler this time around.

What if you've seen the 0 anime already?
It doesn't matter. The anime actually had to restructure the plot quite a bit, cutting out some parts, adding new ones, and even changing some plot points. You can probably imagine the difficulty in adapting multiple separate routes into one anime. Hence, while you can watch the STEINS;GATE anime and call it a day, for this one you really should go through both the anime and the VN.

Sale, full price, whatever. If you can afford it, and you've already either read or seen the original STEINS;GATE, then get it.\t\t\t\t\t
