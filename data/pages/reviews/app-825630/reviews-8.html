Instead of exploring Punished Okabe's inner struggles and his relationship with Amadeus Kurisu (which is what I expected after finishing the prologue), the game dives deep into lots of boring filler and some incoherent mess around the worst character in the entire series, Kagari, which could be easily cut from the game and it'd make it significantly better (actually, you could cut every new character except Maho and Dr. Leskinen).

Warning - This review (and also the game itself) will contain major spoilers for the original Steins;Gate. If you haven't played Steins;Gate or watched the anime, ensure you do that before reading this review or playing Steins;Gate 0.



Steins;Gate 0

The year is 2010. In a few months time, the world will be engulfed in World War 3. Why? Because of Makise Kurisu. A young scientist, known for her intelligence. She is dead. Killed by her lover in another worldline. Okabe Rintaro. This is how the game, Steins;Gate 0, starts. It follows on from one of the endings of the original Steins;Gate. However, there is one difference. Okabe gives up on reaching Steins Gate, believing it is impossible. Now he lives with his mistake and retires his "Hououin Kyouma" for good. This game acts as a sequel to a bad ending and explores how these characters have been affected by these circumstances and what consequences await Okabe and friends, as a result of their failure to save Kurisu.

Story

The story of Steins;Gate 0 immediately gives off a more darker vibe. Especially since WW3 is just around the corner. Many of the themes from the original Steins;Gate are carried over in 0, such as time travel. Events from the previous game are also mentioned. Steins;Gate 0 (S:G0) adds other themes to the plot, such as Artificial Intelligence, trauma and memories. All of these themes are key to how the various endings of S;G0 play out, some darker than others. The main plot point of S;G0 is the introduction of Amadeus, an AI that is able to use memory data from others and create an AI based off them. The main AI that is the key focus is [Kurisu]. This version of Kurisu is quite different to the one from Steins;Gate so players will enjoy uncovering the mystery behind the AI. There is also the other major plot line of a certain character needing to reach Steins;Gate, especially since WW3 draws close. It is important to note that S;G0 isn't meant to be an uplifting game, so players who prefer the more slice-of-life aspects of the original game would likely dislike this game. Overall, the story of S;G0 is very interesting and engaging as bits and pieces from different endings help to create a more rich narrative.

Characters

It is expected that the characters in S;G0 may differ from how they acted in the original. Old and new characters are present in S;G0, with some being less significant than others.
  • Okabe Rintaro - The mad scientist from the original game has become more of a sad scientist. The trauma of all the events from the previous game have made Okabe's mental health deteriorate excessively. Okabe is now a lot more "normal" in some ways, not referencing pop culture or using his mad scientist persona at all. This helps to show just how traumatic many of the events in his life have affected him. Okabe is probably one of the most interesting characters in the whole series and the way S;G0 develops him is very good.
  • Daru - Daru is still a pervert but now, after the events of S;G, he has becoming more compassionate and caring. He understands Okabe's reluctance to try and reach Steins Gate but misses how he acted before. Daru becomes more likable in this game, which helps players to understand how others have been affected by Okabe and his actions
  • Mayuri - She is still as lovable as ever. She often hangs out with her cosplay friends.
  • Faris - Faris remains pretty unchanged from how she was before.
  • Luka - Same as Faris. However, we do get some developments in his character
  • Suzuha - The Suzuha from most of the original game is not the same as this one. Suzuha is one of the characters who gets a lot more development. In the end, you really sympathize with her and the lengths she has to go in order to reach Steins;Gate
New characters are also introduced in this game. Some, such as Maho, are incredibly interesting. However, some, such as Kaede and Fubuki, feel wasted in this game, only appearing sometimes. It also doesn't help as, as a result of the various endings and routes, characters can change drastically. Some appear in one or two scenes. Some have quite a big plot focus. But, due to this development only occurring in some routes, it makes it very confusing to keep up.

Gameplay

The gameplay is almost the exact same as the previous game. Except, we have the RINE app and Amadeus. RINE is optional to use most of the time, being used as a way to see what other characters do, Amadeus, on the other hand, is the main way that influences the endings and routes you get. Overall, the removal of emails does make things a lot easier.

Conclusion

Steins;Gate 0 does suffer from problems, mainly stemming from the fact that certain development for characters is restricted in certain routes. But, if you want to know what it took for Okabe to finally reach Steins Gate, this game is a must. At the end of the game, you will understand just how much things can change due to one decision. Players will find it quite straightforward to get all of the achievements and endings, though some might need a little direction to get.

Rating: 8/10 (An extremely sad, but satisfying game that will leave you craving more from Steins;Gate. If some characters and plot points were developed a little more, this would easily be a 9/10)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMild spoilers.

It's decent. It's not as good as the original VN, but that's a lot to live up to. The old cast are still their likable selves (but what did they do to the art for Luka and Faris, it's hard to look at, and why did Mayuri's chest expand so much in like a couple in-game months?), although with the introduction of new characters a lot of them have reduced screen-time (As a Moeka fan I cry). The new characters are surprisingly likable: Maho was my favorite character in this game, and Leskinen I like on a gut level. Two of Mayuri's friends briefly mentioned in the original become major characters, although they aren't terribly important, but neither did I feel they detracted from the story. Kagari I don't even know what to say, because her character wasn't entirely clear to me. Your AI buddy could have gotten more mileage, but I liked what was there. As for our protagonist, I didn't really feel strongly about Okabe one way or the other, he's pretty inert most times, which is a step down from his likability in the first game.

Speaking of the story, it's kind of on the bad side, this is a VN you play for the characters. A lot of loose ends really aren't tied up in a satisfying way (basically all of Kagari), and there's a lot of events which feel fairly unnecessary to the story. The endings were mostly OK, Twin Automata, and Vega and Altair being my favorites. To be fair, I felt they added some more complexity to the story in a decent way, I didn't feel grated by the inclusion of more organizations and things like that, so it's not a total write-off, just could have been handled better.

The art is a step-down from the original. You'll get used to it, but some of the designs are terrible.

There's a lot to criticize, and I wasn't as satisfied after 100%'ing it like I was with the original, but it's still good. Recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
