\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteins Gate 0 approaches with a more serious tone but neverthless impressive, immersive and beautiful as the first one.
A story about carrying the burden, atonement, resolution, and responsibility.

As a sidenote: I would compare some of my opinions about this game from the first one to scale it.
I highly recommend grabbing the first one (Steins Gate) as it is a masterpiece of itself and the story of Steins Gate 0 pretty much continues on a part of the first one.

[Characters] SG0 showcases how the characters further developed from the first game of the series and introduces new ones. Most people won't feel a direct connection with some of the new ones at first but later on they show very promising character developments.

[Music] Music was well composed, catchy but doesn't grab too much attention to cause distraction. Personally I find it very fitting for each scene and adds a huge amount of immersion on the game. As you start playing the game for the first time, just listening to the main menu soundtrack would give you enough information on what's happening, what could happen and what had happened when it left off from the first game. There are times that I find myself just sitting on my back and listening to the bgm for minutes while my thought wanders. As always the series keeps its image of having one of the best OSTs for me.

[UI] The UI was better here compared from the first game, it's much more organized and was never confusing. You could reach where you want to go by only using a mouse, in terms of using a controller it feels the same as the previous one, clean. The beeping sound on every quicksave was a nice touch too.

[Voice] Magnificent as ever. Every character's voice was well toned, and every word said was filled with emotion that feels right and appropriate. Kudos to the VAs here.

[Art] The artstyle completely fits the theme of the game. Beautiful art, scenes that are memorable, well drawn background and matching filters that adds to that ominous, techy feeling.

[Gameplay] The gameplay was the same with a few new touches to the perspective, and how message interaction works now. First the perspective:

- The perspective of the game not only focuses on the MC alone. Sometimes the story would shift like a storybook, narrating what they are doing without Okabe. There are times that you will be put into the shoes of another character and know their thoughts. This gave me more idea about those characters and start to like them more.

- Answering messages from your phone this time uses stickers or phrases. Playing the first game, I always found that sometimes the choice handed out are too vague for me since Okabe adds something to say when I chose that answer, this is the same case for this one albeit I often got the response I wanted Okabe to say. Not a bad thing but I wanted to point that out.

I cannot add more since I am afraid at this point saying more would somehow spoil the experience but as a final conclusion, Steins Gate 0 is a masterpiece and if you liked the first one, you would probably like this more. I had a great experience so far and am yet to see everything the game has to offer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere's a few thoughts I've kept in the back of my mind since I read this masterpiece of a visual novel back in December 2016. Keep in mind I haven't finished re-reading, so I'm not judging the Steam version here. I will not be spoiling anything but I am assuming you have read/seen the original Steins;Gate. Anyway:

EDIT
Just finished it once again. I had no issues with this version and the game itself was just as incredible. Thoroughly recommended.

Story
Steins;Gate 0 is a companion story to the original cult classic Steins;Gate, uncovering the story of the future Okabe who sent the D-Mail explaining the details of Operation Skuld. This is the story of the Okabe that couldn't do anything to save "her", and in a cruel twist of fate, ended up living with the guilt of murdering Makise Kurisu. As you can expect, this is a grim and depressing journey into a previously unknown timeline. Even though we actually already know how this story ends thanks to the ending of the original Steins;Gate, this visual novel masterfully manages to keep things fresh and interesting thanks to the many unexpected plot twists you'll see unfold. Indeed, the world in which Okabe Rintarou is plunged into is a mysterious and dark one, with dangerous secrets lurking behind every corner. In addition to that, Steins;Gate 0 expands its themes further by exploring the subjects of relationships between man and disturbingly believable AI, the philosophy of the self and post-traumatic stress disorder. Pensive melancholy, sadness, regret, sorrow but also hard-earned triumph, satisfaction and pure joy are all things you will feel while reading this visual novel, it will make you bawl your eyes out without a doubt.

Characters
In addition to the previously established characters, some new ones make their entry and complement the original cast perfectly. The characters we already knew get some extra character development, giving you new insight into these already extremely endearing people. Okabe Rintarou is given special attention, as we get to see a completely different facet of his character that was only scratched upon in the original visual novel. The post-traumatic stress disorder and guilt for his actions are treated in a properly mature way, which is very satisfying because other stories that share these themes sometimes tend to overdo it in an unrealistic way. Anyway, if you liked the characters in the original, you will surely like them even more after reading this, along with the newcomers.

Music
I feel like they really nailed this aspect, there is a wider range of soundtracks compared to the original visual novel and they are used in a more efficient manner to heighten the atmosphere in order to bring out the correct emotions from the reader. Messenger, Isolation, Re-Awake, Amadeus, Tactics in Confusion and many others are absolutely glorious tracks, and there are also great opening/ending songs to complement it all.

Do I consider Steins;Gate 0 to be worthy of the original visual novel? Of course! Steins;Gate was quite the masterpiece but I feel like this companion story actually manages to do better than the original at times. If you were a fan of it, I cannot recommend reading Steins;Gate 0 enough because you will surely love it the same way I do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinished 2 times on PSVita already. When you'd expect that milking a franchise is a bad thing, then your expectations get surpassed so hard you cant believe it. This is Steins;Gate Zero. It is every bit as good as the previous one, AND more.
The plot is better, the characters get more development when needed, the routes are cooler, the drama is bigger and wider, the serious moments outweight the silly anime-trope ones, and the narration is overall way better than the original.
The overall package is better as well, both the physical version and the presentation as a whole.
My only gripe with S;G Zero is that it's so short. But maybe it's just me devouring it because I needed to know how it would end. Oh boy it was quite the ride.
The mere fact that the developers were able to squeeze out a whole new backstory with multiple alternate universes inside a closed loop is proof that Steins;Gate can still be expanded with its basic formula as premise for many years to come.

This is the "right" kind of milking. Just like Persona 4's spin offs were exactly the right kind of milking for the Persona franchise. Keep maintaining this level of quality and I'd even buy a S;G pachinko game if the story is coherent.
