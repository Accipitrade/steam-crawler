\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA surprisingly great sequel (midquel? sidequel? worldlinequel?) for a story that didn't really need one. It somehow managed to take something with a pretty well-wrapped ending and add to it in a meaningful way. Each new character introduced is mostly very strong and adds a lot of depth, the conflict is just as harsh if not more so than the original, and in my opinion the characterization, especially of Okabe and Mayuri, is much deeper than the original (while admittedly a few characters are pretty shallow in return).

A moderate flaw of S;G 0 compared to S;G is that the plot makes less sense overall. Once you've played through the whole thing, thinking on it allows you to put together a pretty clean picture, but it's harder and less obvious, which I suppose is bound to happen when the branches are more daring than what they were originally (if they could even really be called branches). Don't let that stop you though, the main plot is still quite solid, even with its missteps.

You probably don't need convincing if you're here reading this, since you already likely experienced the original, but just in case you do, do it! Unlike the original, where the anime is really strong also, I actively recommend the game over the anime, as the anime for S;G 0 is a little sub-par - that being said, feel free to watch it after this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImagine really bad fanfiction. Imagine it's filled with new undeveloped characters and soap-opera-liked twists. Imagine very repetitive storytelling, with lots of meaningless parts which could be removed without any damage to the story. Congratulations, you've got yourself STEINS;GATE 0.

I am really disappointed in this one. The first part was very well-written, and the plot was build thoughtfully. Everything had a reason and some explanation and no plot-twist felt forced. How did this happened?

And don't get me wrong, I started to play being very invested in saving Kurisu ASAP. I expected that we will see how Okarin builds his plan, but this was not it. This is 49 hours of angst of several characters and random hopping between the worldlines so Okarin could meet the spirits of Christmas Past, Present and Yet to Come and become brave again.

These 49 hours feel like a year, I can't believe I managed to complete this game. Such a waste of new pretty artwork.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would like to preface this review by saying that I am assuming you have already played Steins;Gate (2009). This review will contain unmarked spoilers for Steins;Gate.

Steins;Gate 0 is a "midquel" to Steins;Gate. The story picks up on the beta worldline with Rintaro Okabe's failed first mission near the end of the original story to save Kurisu Makise's life and finally reach the Steins Gate worldline, but now we see the path when Rintaro never receives the D-mail from himself from the future. Rintaro gives up on ever trying to save Kurisu and Suzuha Amane is furious with him for not even trying. Fast forward a few months and Rintaro is attempting to live a normal life of a college student, but also feeling despondent and anxiety over his experiences and failures. He meets the American neuroscientist Dr. Alexis Leskinen and his assistant Maho Hiyajo, both of whom are former colleagues of Kurisu. Rintaro learns about their work on artificial intelligence. He also learns that Kurisu's memories had already been digitized before she died, thus allowing him to speak to her again through the AI technology.

As a big fan of the original game, I was ecstatic to finally have the chance to play its follow-up. The original was nothing short of a fantastic experience, but does this game stand up in comparison?

As with the original game, Steins;Gate 0 is a visual novel, a game that is more about reading the story than traditional gameplay. There are certain decision points in the game, triggered through interacting with your in-game smartphone, that cause the story to follow different paths. There are six endings in total to achieve.

To start with some of the more technical aspects of this game, the artwork, for the most part, is excellent. As with most visual novels, Steins;Gate 0 sticks to a traditional anime-style. The style is definitely a marked difference from the original. While the original had a very distinctive look, this game has a more cleaned-up look to it. Everything from the CGs, the character sprites, and the backgrounds all look great.

As for the audio, the game's music sounds great. There are plenty of new tracks to enjoy while playing this. In addition, the entire cast of the game is fully voiced in the original Japanese. All of the voice actors from the original game reprise their roles here. Again, just as with the original, even if you do not speak Japanese, the voice work still adds much to the experience from the emotional reactions of the characters at various points to even Leskinen's humorously heavily-accented Japanese.

As is the case with any visual novel, the most important aspects are the story and characters. This is where the game was a bit of a more mixed-reaction for me. To start with the positive aspects, the characters are as strong as ever. Any fan of the original game, or even the anime series, will certainly enjoy seeing familiar characters, including Rintaro himself, Mayuri, Daru, Kurisu (as an AI), Suzuha, etc. embark on new adventures and new interactions. This is bolstered by the addition of new characters. I especially liked Maho who comes off as an incredibly intelligent, though sometimes feisty, young woman. The story is, for the most part, interesting. It does come off as a bit darker than the original. But still, there is a main theme of understanding the consequences of one's actions, or sometimes a lack of action, and what it means to do the right thing.

However, despite the positive aspects, I did find some areas of this game lacking. The game is structured a bit differently than the original. While the original had a fairly linear storyline, when going for the true ending, this is not true with Steins;Gate 0. In the original, the alternate endings form a series of "what if" scenarios if Rintaro had made a different decision at key moments. Steins;Gate 0 has more of a branching storyline. When playing through the different branches, exactly who the characters are and what their motivations are can change and it makes it difficult to keep track of who knows what and when. This issue is compounded by the fact that the game is not entirely told from Rintaro's perspective with significant parts told from Suzuha's and Maho's perspectives. Though it is interesting seeing the story from new perspectives, it can make things confusing. I was also disappointed with what I considered to be unneeded filler material that just does not seem to matter or go anywhere. Even the true ending felt a little underwhelming that just lacked the emotional satisfaction of the original.

Despite the aforementioned issues, I did enjoy the game. It does not quite stand up to the spectacular original, but perhaps that is more due to the fact that the original set the bar so high. Still, it is a game with good characters, a mostly interesting story, and some great art. Any fan of the original wanting more is sure to get enjoyment out of this game.\t\t\t\t\t
