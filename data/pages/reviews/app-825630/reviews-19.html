\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile this sequel does have the same time travel concepts and more, it tries to do too much and fails to capture the unique essence of the first game.

Story
The story starts off nicely and does sets the stage well. Okabe a once playful scientist that discovers the secret of time travel, now beaten by the dangers that be. He tried to forget the past in order to live a normal life but fate pushes him forward slowly, he can try to run from his fears or confront them.

Unfortunately then it slows down. It also gets convoluted as we get introduced to more more characters and events. For the next few chapters the visual novel tries to do more but fails to focus on the main story and main characters. My interest in the story dropped as I just got bored at that point. There are good things in the story like most of the endings are engaging but the problem is just getting there. I wanted to quit multiple times before getting to the end.

Game
The game is mostly split into 2 distinct routes instead of a single one like in the original visual novel. They should have stayed with the formula of the first game having 1 big route with multiple sub-route endings as it makes it easier on the reader. On the positive side, most of the endings are really well done and do engage the reader to discover more. That said, one ending in particular is a huge disappointment and a failed opportunity.

Visual and Audio
Overall good, The art is nice even if it's not as special as the original, the music is mostly taken from the first game. Voice acting is good.

Closing comments
Let me just say that I really like the first STEINS;GATE. Looking back on this review, my expectation were probably too high for this sequel. I wanted to love this visual novel but found it not as special as the first one. It's entertaining but felt like it was taking too long before getting interesting.

Low recommendation\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery good game, certainly on par with S;G and in fact explains one of the biggest what felt like deus ex machina moments in the original's true ending.

Despite this game being called sequel (sometimes prequel?), this game actually took place (took time?) in the timeline where MC decided he couldn't save one of the characters (trying to stay spoiler free here), kinda gave up, and what happened after that decision.

The best thing about this game is the lack of buildup time - or rather, just shorter lead-in compared to the original. The original spent like 8+ hours building up the series of events that resulted in the super interesting parts of the story - but that was because it had to explain and establish characters, characterization, and events, before it can start acting on those characters and events. If you're playing this game, you already know the characters very well, so there is less need to do that kind of pre-establishment.

I also like that this game have actually less formula to it - each of the endings/paths results in pretty vastly different events and ending, and I also really like that just because it's <insert name of character>'s Ending, it doesn't mean that MC ends up dating/living rest of his life with that character. Original game had the character endings made out to be like a dating sim, whereas this game maintained the canon relationship of the true ending of the original game, and make sure it didn't go off rail. The attention to detail and continuity is excellent.

New characters were introduced, and as mentioned before, because they had maintained continuity, they were able to establish and develop many of the new characters in such a way that they don't become annoying mary sue's. Yay! The thing I'm most impressed by the Steins Gate (and Chaos Child) games is that the writer managed to write all the characters to be likeable in different ways. I normally highly dislike chuunibyou characters, and I find a lot of anime female characters to be ultra annoying, but I didn't have any of that trouble in these games (okay Faris was rather annoying in game 1, but even then she was so well developed in her Ending that I ended up liking her).

The only thing I would do is add the original ending's True End at the end of this game - just tack it on at the end as a post credit series of scenes so that the viewer can get a rehash of what happened after MC receives the you-know-what. As it is, this game felt like it ended rather incomplete. Of course it had to, because again the actual resolution of the whole story actually happened in game 1, this game is an alternate timeline that he ended up having to live through before coming to the deus ex machine-esque solution in the original game to reach the true ending. However, for players to put a proper bow on the story, rewatching/rehashing that original true ending would've been perfection. (in my case i ended up re-downloading Steins Gate and fast forwarded through a save i had toward the end to rewatch the ending, but i was also lucky to have a save toward the end with all the True Ending flags, I can imagine how annoying it'd be for someone who didn't keep such a save, to be unable to revisit what happened in the true ending).

In any case, all in all this game was a great wrap up of the holes in the original game. This game's story is also more convoluted than the original game, but I can conversely argue that the original game's story was actually overly simple (formulaic, is what i felt of the original game at one point). Can't say I'd be interested in the dating sim spinoffs but I'm happy to have gotten to play these games!\t\t\t\t\t
