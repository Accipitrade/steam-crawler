In SG0 she's basically the second coming of Christ, solving problems that plagued science for millenia on the first day at the lab, a girl so smart that secret organizzations from all over the world want her genius.

If you thought that was bad, oh boy we didn't even scratch the surface, entire character arcs that should take a lot of time to elaborate on get solved in a few lines, characters doing superhuman feats and i don't mean logic feats, god forbid, the game is full of thoose, but actually phisical stuff that doesn't even make sense by anime logic.

This game prays on your fanboysm on your experiences with the previous title to get some more money out of you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, I'd like to say that despite S;G0's flaws, it is a nice sequel to the original Steins;Gate visual novel. Or maybe it's more accurate to call it a prequel to S;G's Chapter 11. Operation Skuld didn't exist in the beginning of S;G0, and all the events that lead to its planning take place in S;G0. All of Okabe's experiences in 0 contributed to reaching the SG worldline we see in the original S;G. I really enjoyed seeing the relationship between Daru and his daughter Suzuha in the separate S;G0 worldlines. Suzuha's memories with her father in 2010 in the alpha worldline were sacrificed in the original S;G, so it was nice to have some closure and see that she reunited with Daru even in the beta worldline. Also, Daru's interactions with his future wife Amane Yuki were hilarious and sweet. I feel like the Hashida family's interactions alone made this visual novel worth reading. Maho is also a great character, and I loved to see her interactions with Okabe, Amadeus Kurusu, and Leskinin. Kaede and Fubuki were also nice side characters that helped liven up the character cast.

But even with all the great things about this visual novel, I might have taken a lot of time reading because I paused a lot as I read to try understanding the sequence of events in each worldline. With my OCD causing me to overthink, I've noticed there are 2 BIG plot holes that bothered me a lot.

The Promised Rinascimento and Vega & Altair branches are separate, and V&A overwrites PR relative to Okabe's "chronological" experiences after the D-Rine is sent. This is what leads to the canon ending of S;G0. With that in mind...

(1): I didn't understand why Kagari was alive and lacked plastic surgery in 'Milky-Way Crossing' after the events of V&A. With the canon established so far, I can only guess that its a different worldline that us readers of S;G0 didn't experience. It feels like a "worldline skip" that isn't explicitly mentioned at all.

(2): The D-Rine was sent from the PR branch intending to be one of the steps forward towards the SG wordline. This branch was filled with a lot of worldline changes caused by Stratfor and DURPA abusing Kurusu's memories. Okabe and his friends finally end the cycle and decide the best way to reach SG is to start over again from an earlier point in time by sending a D-Rine. This meant that all the experiences (especially Kagari's time with Mayuri) would be sacrificed for the good of humanity. But the D-Rine itself didn't contribute to any worldline change that Okabe would notice with Reading Steiner -- meaning that he should still be stuck in the PR branch according to the established canon in the original S;G. And even if we ignore Reading Steiner, the V&A and V&A+D-Rine branches are COMPLETELY IDENTICAL; the D-Rine itself had ZERO effect on the timeline. The variable that determines reaching the V&A and PR branches is if Okabe turns off his phone on 12/15. [Phone On]--->V&A. [Phone Off]--->PR. The D-Rine can ONLY be received after Okabe's choice -- when he talks to Kaede and Fubuki. But the PR branch which sends the D-Rine already has the established fact that the phone was turned off, making it impossible to receive the message from the future. This inconsistency could've been easily avoided if the D-Rine was received at least a day earlier. Also, Okabe doesn't acknowledge or mention the D-Rine's existence until the separate MWC worldline -- a worldline that is obviously independent of V&A's consequences.

Because of 1 and 2, It felt like the PR branch's D-Rine didn't even have one small effect on the V&A branch wordlines; this cheapens the sacrifices Okabe and his friends made to start over again with a new hope. Also the V&A branch was loosely connected to the MWC wordline due to the lack of D-Rine significance to V&A and Kagari's death from that branch; this also cheapens the events and sacrifices that led to the resolve to finally plan Operation Skuld.

I mainly felt the need to write all this because I wanted to collect and organize all my thoughts for myself. I've finally finished reading the S;G series and I've been fascinated by the "science" behind time traveling, D-mails, time-leaping, worldline convergence/divergence, and Reading Steiner. This is my favorite time traveling series of all time. The original S;G, to me, is nearly flawless. And I think even after finally collecting my thoughts, I'm willing to forgive S;G0's inconsistencies because I absolutely loved the characters' interactions. I highly recommend this visual novel if you've read the original S;G, want more time with the lab members, and are willing to ignore any big inconsistencies that may bother you.\t\t\t\t\t

9/10
As a sequel to Steins;Gate, you won't be able to understand the content of the game without playing the previous game.

The sequel to the famous visual novel game Steins;Gate. Although some people think that Steins;Gate 0 is not as good as its predecessor, I think this game is a well-deserved excellent sequel.

A change from the witty and relaxed game style of the previous game, the overall atmosphere of this game is heavy, and personally I prefer this kind of visual novel work. The plot of the game is naturally wonderful, so I won’t comment here to avoid spoilers. If you are deeply attracted by the game’s background settings and characters before playing Steins;Gate, then this work must be worthwhile. The music of this game is also very beautiful, and it will never disappoint the players. The only shortcoming of it is that the description of some characters is too sloppy and the characterization is insufficient, such as Kagari.

In any case, this one is definitely a must-try work for players who love Steins;Gate, and it is an important addition to the series of plots. As a related supplementary sequel, its nature is also destined that players must play the previous game in order to understand the content of this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore the review starts, there are two things i should mention
1. It's been about a year since i completed the game, so forgive me if some details are vague
2. Steins;Gate 0 takes place in an alternate universe from Steins;Gate where main character Rintaro Okabe fails to reach the good ending of his journey, which means that playing/watching the original S;G is a must for S;G0. Unlike most of the other games in the SciADV franchise where you don't necessarily need context from previous entries, S;G0 actively expects you to know the events of the earlier entry. Needless to say that, because of this link, this review will contain major spoilers for S;G, so check that out first.


Premise
Steins;Gate 0 takes place in the β-worldline where main character Okabe Rintaro fails his quest to rescue the one he loves, Makise Kurisu. Suffering from severe anxiety and PTSD, Okabe decides to live out the remaining years of his life in complete normalcy all while trying to forget about the conspiracies he was so deeply involved in. But fate is a cruel mistress, and his plans are soon thrown into disarray as he meets with Hiyajo Maho and Doctor Leskinen, former colleagues of Kurisu. Using Kurisu's science paper as a base, they've managed to create an AI named Amadeus, which is based on the memories of the girl he'd forced himself to forget. Not only that, but Suzuha's prescense in the Future Gadget Lab serves as a constant reminder of the consequences of his failure: the international arms-race to build a time machine has begun, and the shadow of World War III looms on the horizon.
Faced with these events, Okabe is once again dragged into a world that he swore that he'd leave behind. Messing with time means entering the realm of God, and he knows all too well what kind of cruel punishment awaits those who dare interfere.

The Experience
The overall experience of Steins;Gate 0 is stellar like the original. The writing, atmosphere, and soundtrack are, as expected of a SciADV-game, fantastic. The general mood of the game is more somber than its predecessor, but that doesn't mean that the game is without happy or comedic moments. That somberness gives S;G0 an identity of its own, making it feel distinctively different. But it's not just the tone that makes S;G0 feel different.
First of all, the different endings are better executed than in the original. The main route and true ending in S;G was phenomenal, but the side endings felt like they existend purely for setting Okabe up with the different girls of the FGL. Because of that, most of the side endings felt very underwhelming. In S;G0 however, the endings are longer and have more variety to them, not existing with the sole purpose of romance, but rather giving a look into what would happen if things were done ever so slightly different at key moments in the game, and where that would take the cast.
Speaking of endings, the route system of the game has been streamlined. Rather than having send a specific set of emails, the routes in S;G0 are determined by wheather or not you choose to accept or decline calls from Amadeus in key moments of the story, which changes the somewhat chaotic mail system of S;G into a more easy-to-understand binary system of yes/no answers. The mailing system isn't gone from S;G0, but its relevance to route-selection is.
Speaking of the mailing system, it takes the form of RINE-messaging in S;G0, but follows the same concept, which is great, as the mailing system was, and still is, one of my favorite tools of immersion in gaming. Being able to interact with the cast and seeing the effects of time travel mess with the order and content of previous/future messages makes it easy to feel how skewed okabe's sense of time gets in S;G, so seeing it return in S;G0 was great.

Conclusion
Steins;Gate 0 is about as perfect as a "sequel" can get to an already amazing game. Following the offscreen journey of β-Okabe from the original is a blast, adding additional characters and events that compliment the parts of the story that have been previously established in Steins;Gate. Despite my praises of the route system from earlier, i'd still reccommend following a spoiler-free guide to make the experience as smooth as possible.
Finally, while the Steins;Gate 0 anime is good, it also removes key events and isn't consistant with the VN in its story-progression. Unlike the anime adaptation of the original Steins;Gate where both experiences felt similar, the anime of Steins;Gate 0 and the game itself are so different that they feel like two seperate experiences. I'd say the VN is undoubtably the best experience of the two, so even if you watch the anime first there's so much missed content that the VN covers that i'd say it's worth buying regardless.

tl;dr: Game is good, and is worth it even for people who have seen the anime\t\t\t\t\t
