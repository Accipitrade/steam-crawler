\r\n\t\t\t\t\t8.6/10. Great VN game. So-so Stein's Gate game. Spoilers ahead:
*
*
*
*
*
*
*
*
*
*

The good:
In the end, it's still a great game. I still enjoyed the characters, the art, and the overall story was very interesting. I liked the idea of having multiple POV characters. Achieving the different endings was also much easier than it was in Stein's Gate, and a welcome change. The new characters I liked for the most part, with Maho definitely being a standout - I disliked her immensely at first, but her characterization as the game went on was probably the best of them all. I also was intrigued by the AI ideas introduced, and felt like I actually did learn a lot just from playing. All in all, it's still a Stein's Gate game, which means it's a time travel game with a fantastic story and set of characters. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ cool.

The bad:
Unfortunately, it's a Stein's Gate game, which means that I was constantly comparing it to its predecessor, which I literally rated a 10/10. Honestly, I thought Stein's Gate was the better game from almost every aspect. For the most part I liked the characterization of the main characters better in their Stein's Gate versions, and I even felt that the art/sprites/music were better in the original. Furthermore, I believe that the plotting in Stein's Gate was far more coherent/cohesive, and I definitely preferred the linear plot over Stein's Gate 0's multiple branches. I thought that the antagonists' motives were murky at best, and incomprehensible at worst. I also don't like how multiple endings retraced back to scenes that I've already seen before, which turned the game into a slog at some points. I'm not sure this is some huge knock on Stein's Gate 0, either - I just thought that Stein's Gate was that good. I loved that game.

The ugly (basically personal preference stuff):
I loved Kurisu Makise. She's probably my actual best anime girl/waifu if I had to choose. I really don't think she belonged in this game. Yes, it pains me to admit it as an unabashed Kurisu simp, but it's true - I thought that a big theme in this game should have been Okabe learning to let Kurisu go/fall in love with Maho, but the game seems determined in bringing Kurisu back. I don't like how they did it, either - communicating with an AI/bringing a girl who looks exactly like her in Kagari is not healthy, period. Yes, I get that a main point of the game is Okabe realizing that he must save Kurisu/find Stein's Gate, but I believe that it's a realization that he should reach himself through self-reflection or through talking with friends, and not being semi-manipulated by an AI or other hokey/contrived means. Also, man I signed up for a time travel game, not an AI game - I found that I enjoyed the time travel sections much more, and those happened to be my favorite endings, too. The AI and brainwashing stuff I found to be far more unsettling/not enjoyable. Also, there were probably one too many shower scenes for my liking. Like, what are we doing, here.

And now, the rankings:

Best girl:
Kurisu (ofc) >>>>>>>>>>>>>>>> Mayuri >>> Maho > Faris >>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>> Yukl >>>>>>>>>>>> Kagari >>>>>> Suzuha>> Moeka

Best ending:
True ending >> Mayuri ending >>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>> Kurisu ending >>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>> Kagari ending >>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>> Bad ending >> Maho ending

Daru best boy. Super hacka B).
