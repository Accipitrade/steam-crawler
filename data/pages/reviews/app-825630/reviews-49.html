As the main character, due to a failures, becoming depressed and giving up, he finds himself back and becomes a mad scientist again.
And of course new characters, new plot twists. I had to sit, think and comprehend what was happening.

But in my opinion, the right branch is much more interesting than the left. And therefore it is better to leave it for last. Moreover, the true ending comes after her. I began to read myself without guide and constantly reproached myself for it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know if I can whole-heartedly recommend Steins;Gate 0 but I have to say, despite my issues with it, I deeply enjoyed certain parts of it.
I think it should go without saying that you need to play the original Steins;Gate before even considering playing Steins;Gate 0.

That being said, my issues with Steins;Gate 0 are pretty vast:
-There is one ending in particular that made me contemplate never playing this game again, for example.
-Some of the game's character developments are... really unfortunate and make next to no sense.
-The experience certainly isn't as tight and well presented as the orignal Steins;Gate.
-(Personal preference on this one) there's way too many action scenes compared to the original to the point where I feel like they missed the reason that made the original was so great.
-I'm not certain that Steins;Gate 0 justifies its own existence. It was a great experience and seeing old and new Steins;Gate character was great but I just don't believe the ending was worth it all.

But let's move on from the negative. What does Steins;Gate 0 do right?
+The writing, humour and emotional delivery is kept very much in line with the origjnal. Basically, if you liked Steins;Gate, you're going to have a lot of fun with Steins;Gate 0.
+The music is still great. The (Japanese) voiceovers are amazing.
+Lots of moments that'll make you laugh or cry. Steins;Gate 0 gets very emotional and although not all of it's punches seem to land, there's enough of them to make sure that you're going to feel something.
+Decent length that'll make sure you get what you pay for.
+Some really great CGs. Maybe not enough, but the art of Steins;Gate 0 is wonderful.

My verdict on Steins;Gate 0 is complicated to put it lightly. There were a lot of moments that hand me on the end of my seat or made my hair stand on end or hand me on the verge of tears but on the other hand, there was some parts that totally bombed. There were moments that left me scratching my head in confusion or walking away from my computer in frustration at the audacity and poor quality of some of the writing. That, coupled with the fact that the game lacks a truly satisfying and moving ends makes me really unsure of what to think of this product.

I'm going to go ahead and say if you're a huge fan of Steins;Gate and absolutely NEED more, go ahead and get Steins;Gate 0. But if you're satisfied with the true ending of the original and feel like Steins;Gate has given you all it has to offer then I wouldn't blame you for passing on this one.

I'm open to discussion on why or why I may not be right on some of my points, by the way so feel free to comment.
Thanks for reading. El Psy Kongroo.\t\t\t\t\t
