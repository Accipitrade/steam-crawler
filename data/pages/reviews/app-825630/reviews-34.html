\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, have you experienced the original Steins;Gate? If not, STOP! Drop everything and go witness the original choice of Steins;Gate!

Are you here after playing through Steins;Gate ELITE? I SUPPOSE that will suffice, but I must urge you to at least pick up a copy of the art book from the first game.

Here by accident? No interest in Steins;Gate? YOU ARE MISTAKEN. Your arrival to this page is the choice of Steins;Gate! You have no choice now but to go to the page for the first game and witness it for yourself.

All right, with that out of the way we can now assume you've experienced all the original story had to offer. Are you ready to do it again? Steins;Gate 0, although not quite the perfection of the initial experience (we'll get to that later), accomplishes a rare feat in essentially allowing you to relive your initial experience with Steins;Gate. Haven't you always wished you could experience a beloved story and all those feelings for the first time again? Steins;Gate 0 addresses those wishes by recreating their world through a new Worldline, and bringing you new characters who feel organic to the experience and are well-written--all without interfering with anything from the original story.

What's new?
Were you heartbroken as you witnessed the worldlines reset over and over in an attempt to save one life at a time? Well, get ready for World War 3. Along with the premise that Okabe did not save Kurisu, the atmosphere of this game is understandably much darker.

Other adjustments to the story experience this time around include slightly less perfect/complete storytelling, and a different approach to the various "endings" of the game. Our friends over at the Future Gadget Lab are no longer responsible for messing with time. Thus, the worldlines tend to change at times without the clear explanations for "why", as we were so spoiled by in the first game. I think this is a bigger issue for me than it may be for most, as I enjoy my complete explanations in a good story, but there's still plenty to work with here. Theories can abound, but they're not necessary. And they give themselves a nice out by not having Okabe causing the worldline changes. You'll just have to accept of a few of the more "convenient" ones at face value, but I don't think this unfortunate change in approach really subtracts from the experience.

The endings are no longer entirely romantically tied, nor are they somewhat complete on their own. There is still the inevitable "true" ending in which you secure the original true ending to Steins;Gate. However, this time around all experiences and explanations are spread across multiple ending routes. (Side note: I've not seen the anime adaptation yet, but I'm curious how they handle this while hopefully including all the new information gained from this game.) On the bright side, routes are more easily obtained, and the true ending doesn't result from a seemingly random series of text interactions.

Possibly the greatest emotional downside this time around is that you go into this already knowing how it "should" end (via the ending of the original Steins;Gate). In that regard, completing this experience could feel less meaningful/accomplished than the first game since you're there for the details, not the end result. But I'd still say it turns out to be an incredibly well-written and worthwhile experience companion to the first story. After all, a good sequel (prequel, side story, or otherwise) is one in which you can revisit beloved characters, add new ones without interfering with established events, enjoy even more world-building, and experience a new story that brings it all together. Steins;Gate 0 is a great sequel.

Final evaluations:
Music - 10/10. Another home run in this category, with excellent revisions to some original tracks.
Story - 9/10. Aforementioned complaints take it down just a tad, but still amazing.
Art - 10/10. If you couldn't tell with my opening comment to those coming from ELITE, I love the art direction.
Replay/Depth - 8/10. Forced replay inherent to the VN style to experience the whole thing, plus you'll likely come back at some point to do it all again.

Overall:
8.7/10 - Not enough "El Psy Kongroo"\t\t\t\t\t

0 is considerably more solemn than the original, for good reason. Okabe is constantly struggling to cope with his loss, certainly not helped by having a digital copy of his beloved assistant talking to him.
0 is filled with the memorable moments, intrigue, and tragedy you've come to expect - anyone who enjoyed the first should find plenty to love.
