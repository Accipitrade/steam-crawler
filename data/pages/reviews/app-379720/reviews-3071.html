\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have not reviewed a game in ages, but I can't keep quiet about how much I love this game, and how well it's designed. Everything from the map design to the weapon and ammo, powerups and monsters is designed well to keep the combat fast-paced and fun. The multiplayer is mediocre, at best, however I feel the game is worthwhile for the single player campaign alone, so that's what I'm reviewing.

The enemies have some variety in how they pursue you, but for the most part they keep you running the whole time. The way the maps are designed in the areas where fighting is heaviest means this includes a lot of jumping around on different levels and platforms. Armor, health and ammo are scattered around which further encourages constant movement. It feels very much like an old-school arena shooter. You're not given a great deal of ammo, and between that and the variety of enemies thrown at you, you're almost forced to switch between various weapons and use them all to some extent, rather than picking a favorite. (Or getting stuck picking which two you want to carry, which I'm sick of) It's great for the pacing and means that no two encounters are alike. Getting up close and finishing an enemy in melee means they drop health, and enemies drop more if you're low. It encourages a risk and reward gameplay that often keeps you walking the fine line between life and death. The weapons are each unique, and all have upgrades to unlock as you progress. Most have two options for secondary fire attachments to unlock, with a different set up upgrades for each. With additional upgrades for your health, armour and ammo capacity as well as various utility upgrades, there's a great deal of progression to allow for a steady increase in the difficulty of encounters.

All in all, the game does an outstanding job of blending old with new. Fast paced movement and combat similar to older FPS games and arena shooters, paired with a dark atmosphere and story that keeps it feeling very much like a DOOM game should. Pair that with a more modern system for unlocks, progression, and a few collectables, and of course the gorgeous visuals from a modern team working with a modern engine.

It's a tough call, with Overwatch and Battlefield 1, but DOOM gets my pick for game of the year for 2016.\t\t\t\t\t
