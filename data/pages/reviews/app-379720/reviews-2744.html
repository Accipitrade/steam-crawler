\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt looks like Id Software has gone back to their roots as the undisputed pioneers of first person shooters they once were. Doom 3 had its own gig and probably would have worked better under another name and another franchise, but DOOM really plays and looks like everything you'd want for a modern Doom game.

DOOM has the gore, the arsenal of weapons, the spooky environments that truly makes a Doom game. One thing that stood out for me was the amazing pace. You grab some ammo and armor in a hallway, go into a large room and brutally kill every demon in sight, then it's up to you if you want to stay and explore the area or just continue the mission. And the exploration really pays off big-league if you can find all the secrets, some are useful power-ups while others are cute, nostalgia-filled throwbacks to the old games. Something the old games didn't have too much of was platforming, and DOOM brought this concept with the right amount and with a smooth edge, works very fluently and adds to the exploration part of the game. The level design truly complements all elements of the game.

The enemies are varied in attacks and appearence, however, the way you play against them doesn't really change. Maybe you swap weapon to spare ammo on the more casual weapons such as the shotgun or assault rifle, but other than that you don't have to swich up your play-style too much. This isn't neccesserily bad if you want the speed and pace of battle to flow without having to do anything specific to fight a specific enemy, but if you want the play-style in battle to be more mixed you should definitely play on harder difficulty, (not saying it's a cakewalk on normal difficulty, but the harder difficulties add a lot more challenge).

While the enemies don't force you to change play-style, you can change your play-style yourself anytime by using different combinations of runes and weapon modifications. If you want to focus on punching demons in the face easier, just swap runes if they're available depending on where you are and if you did the exploration needed. If you want your plasma gun to stun enemies or blast a heat wave around you, just swap the mod if you unlocked it. It's a great way to choose how you want to play depending on your choices during the missions.

Personally, this is one of the best first person shooter games I've ever played, (not sure if it's better than Wolfenstein: The New Order, tough choice...). The story is revealed in a great way, the action is ripping, the exploration is rewarding, the pace is out of this world and the setting is spectacular. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was initally hardpressed to write a review for this game. The IP is so well known and this game in particular recieved quite a bit of media attention upon release. Its pretty well known that a lot of people really enjoyed Doom (2016). I'll even myself admit right from the start I too enjoyed it. However I noticed a bit of quality lacking from the reviews. Certain questions I had about the game myself weren't addressed. Im assuming if you're still sitting hear a year later wonder if you should buy the game, not all of your questions/doubts has been addressed either.

Story:
John Carmack , lead programmer for the original Doom was famously quoted saying: "Stories in video games are a lot like a stories in porn. No ones expecting it to be any good, but people are still expecting to see one" In other words, video game stories aren't important. For the most part modern day ID stuck to this belief. The narrative, overall has very little drive to the gameplay. Which in itself would be fine. The story to me however, brings Doom's (2016) strongest contridiction. Which also drives my chief complaint. From the developers interviews themselves to the various reviews. Most people say the story means very little. From the openning moments of the game this seems evident. Yet immediately after getting to kill 3 or 4 zombies right after the game starts. You the player are forced to watch a quick narrative moment. Completly haulting the gameplay and thus killing the momentum. This continutes to happen through out the campaign. One minute the game is giving you the "screw the story, im here to kill demons vibe" to completely halting the players ability to even move to watch some in game narrartive. Its this contriction that never gets less jarring. To me this wouldnt be so bad if the game didn't try to force feed that the story bares little to what you're doing. To go from one moment of allowing the player avatar to destory computer or break a "key" game item, halting the narrative. To stopping the player dead in their tracks to watch some in game cut scene just feels out of place. Yes the story scenes only last a 30 or 40 seconds a piece. But they are there and they do kill momentum.

GamePlay:
This is by far is the key selling feature to Doom (2016) and in reality should be the key selling point to any game. But that discussion maybe out of the scope of this review. Doom (2016) does in fact capitalize on what Doom 3 failed to do. Capture the feel and frenzy of the original Doom games while brining into the modern shooter era. In reality I feel Doom (2016) more closley mimics the Serious Sam or PainKiller series then its own ilk. But that doesn't take away from the fun or the "doom" feel of the game. The majority of the game will find you getting trapped in a large commonly multi tier arenaish area filled with hordes of demons. You the player will not be allowed to progress on untill you completely kill everything besides yourself in the room. This pretty much goes rinse and repeat untill the end of the game. However the game rarely if ever feels monotonous. Each "area" feels very detailed and very vibrant. Despite the samey overall assetetic each new area the player comes across has a distinct layout keeping the fights feeling fresh. On top of this the game offers RPG-LITE elements allowing the player to upgrade components. Traverse areas to find secrets which generally involve some light first person platforming. Normally I hate this in games. But Doom (2016) puts in places that allow the action to broken up a bit while still keeping the momentum going. Well untill it plays another bloody in game cut scene. The newest and flashiest feature of the gameplay has to be the glory kills. A subject of a bit of debate. I myself really enjoyed them. As it adds a bit of cinamatic feel to the combat without taking control out of the players hands. Not to mention it becomes to key to survival in the more difficult parts of the game. Netting these kills on the plus of looking cool offers both health and additional ammo. This all together mixes into make one really fun moment to moment FPS experience. Depending upon where you are, what guns you have, how much ammo you have and what enemies your agianst will force you to constently keep shifting your strategy. Each playthrough can require different moment. Say your in a room with 2 hell knights and a summoner in the back. Plus 6 or 7 imps shuffeling arround the back. You need to keep moving. Why not use a chain saw to instant kill the hell knight or summoner to make gettinng arround quicker. Finish the other hell knight with a rocket launcher. Switch to a more basic shotgun or assault riffle for the imps. Not only does Doom (2016) hold onto what made the classic games so great it makes many of these elements key to the gameplay. On a side note yes the game has quick saves. And yes they are blasphmous. Yet I can see that it partially went into the design of the games difficulty. Maybe just maybe we can get a mod or a patch some time down the road. Regardless of this crappy feature it honestly doesn't de-rail the gameplay experience.

Graphics:
Doom franchise has always been considered a benchmark for computers. Doom 1 and Doom 3 were new heights the rest of the industry had to catch up on. Doom (2016) looks great, is well optimised and runs on a great number of different systems. However this also means its no longer the tour de force the series has been known for. There are a great number of wonderous effects on offer and everything is modeled in wonderful detail. However all of these effects has been done in other games before. Texture wise the game seems bit behind the times. Especially compared to Battlefield 1, TitanFall 2 and Battlefront 2. But as I said the game runs amazingly. On a gtx 1070 I never saw drops below 80 FPS and generally saw an average arround 120 FPS.

Sound:
I love the music in Doom (2016) its a mix of industrly electronic music with hints of metal. Personally I'd of loved to heard more metal. But whats on offer more than does the job. In terms of effects the game is also no slouch. Giving the one area of the game where it takes a bit from Doom 3. Offer creapy omniance and some shrilling rawrs from the monsters. Particually from the pocessed when you hear them in the distance.

Multiplayer:
By far the most disappointing piece of the package. The game offers to modes to take. Standard MP mode which offers death match, team death match and several other modes. And the "mod friendly" SnapMap. Which allows players to build maps and levels which they can play with other players cooperativly or competitvly. The standard MP is by far the worse piece. Borrowing heavily from other main line FPS games like Call Of Duty and Halo. While the modes work, and are by no means broken. They themselves feel nothing like Doom, or Quake or for that matter old school. It offers a level up system and the ability to transform into a demon for a small stint. However it feels just outright soulless and forgettable. The maps all feel un orginal and aren't fun to look at. Not to mention all the paid DLC for the game comes in forms of more of these maps. I think I put in a total of 20 minutes into the mode before outright giving up. SnapMap is probably the most interesting piece of MP and the most orignal content. Offer the players to put together maps using simple to understand tools. Players have full control over making maps.The mode works and I've had some fun. However the inability to add custom content has already made the mode seem borring. Not a lot of people are making content and finding new maps that are fun are difficult. The game continutes to show case the same old popular maps players made months ago. The idea of doom co-op seems fun, yet not enough maps are longer enough or done well enough to warrent extensive play sessions.

Overall Doom (2016) is a worthy successor to the Doom Franchise.
