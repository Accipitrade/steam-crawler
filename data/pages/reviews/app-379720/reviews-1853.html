\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot even finished with it yet, but based on what I have played - and to give you perspective, I just restarted a particular level for the 6th ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ time to try to get all the unlocks and secrets - its really good.

There are things that are annoying, long platforming segments and about half of your upgrades are hidden secrets you feel compelled to find, yet there are often points-of-no-return, doors you pass through that refuse to open again once you trigger the checkpoint.... but right around the time I get kind of annoyed or frustrated about that stuff, I run out of ammo, press the chainsaw button, get an awesomely brutal kill that drops enough ammo to restock all my stuff, rinse repeat, and eventually I forget all the stuff that made me mad

I will tell you, I have spent HOURS on the Doom wiki trying to figure out how this game ties into the old stuff, and it is a fruitless effort, when something in the game makes you think about old id games just slap yourself and remember, this is Doom and story is for nerds, this is about shotguns and explosions and chainsaws. And its really good at being about those things while somehow still staying true to the franchise. a lot of the fights remind me of Serious Sam in that the real serious battles tend to be arena-style, once you trigger a thing you are locked into this area until you kill everything, with weapon/armor/health pickups and various powerups, its modern AND oldskool at the same time which is hard to do and still be awesome.

Its a huge download, but it runs really well at least on my setup....it does thrash my hard drive a bit by constantly writing to the page file, havent figured that out yet, but I almost never experience frame per second lags, even if, when I alt-tab out, it takes 10 minutes to open a new tab in Chrome (usually to find out, how the hell do I get this thing I can clearly see but cant get to)\t\t\t\t\t
