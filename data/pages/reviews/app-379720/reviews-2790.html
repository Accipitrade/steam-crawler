Reload - GONE
Aim down sight - GONE
Health regen - GONE
Limited sprint- GONE
Gimmicky set pieces - GONE

Over power rocket launchers - BACK BABY!!

In these simple statements, DOOM reminds us that FPS should be fun, fun, FUN! It feels like a response to the copious amount of derivative FPS out these days, getting rid of everything that slows down the action, and putting in two innovative mechanics in place to make sure you stay in the fun.

One, a problem of classic FPS is leaving the battle to find health packs. That's not fun. So Halo came along and popularized health regeneration. Revolutionary at the time, but really it just replaces looking for health pack into looking for a place to hide and waiting. That's no good either. So DOOM comes along and really fixes the problem: enemies drop health if you melee them! What a brilliant idea. Instead of leaving the action when you do bad, you must take more risk to get your health back. It ensures action, action, action every second.

The 2nd problem is ammo. Not having ammo in an important fight sucks! It sucks so much most games just gives you a ton of ammo right before those fights. Not DOOM. It gives you a chainsaw that spews ammo when used on an enemy! When you run out of ammo, the game doesn't want you to go and look for it, because that's not FUN.

Next, let's talk about enemy types. How many shooters are there where it's possible to beat the whole game with one gun? An insane amount of shooters that's how many. In DOOM, each enemy has a weapon they are weak to, meaning no weapon will get stale in your hand, and getting each new weapon is momentous. The enemies gets harder and harder but your guns get bigger and bigger. It's sooo satisfying when you get your hands on a new toy.

In short, DOOM asks these questions: What bogs down modern FPS? and how can we make classic FPS even more fun?\t\t\t\t\t
