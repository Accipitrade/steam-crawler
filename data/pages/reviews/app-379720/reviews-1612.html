\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom (2016) is a game I'd hesitated to buy through multiple sales. I've played a little of the modernized Wolfenstein and I didn't really care for it (weapons felt boring and sneaking is so overused now). I figured Doom was just another overhyped, modernize FPS rehash with little to offer to the already bloated genre. After finishing the game here's my take on it:

Cons:
-Levels lack variety: Enter arena area, fight waves of demons, platform a little, enter another arena, and repeat until the game is done.
-The pistol is boring and useless. Sure, starter weapons are supposed to be bad, but it's so bad I wish I could just remove it from my inventory.
-Story is mediocre and laughably predictable. It's all one big McGuffin to setup the gameplay scenario.

Pros:
-Rip and Tear! Seriously, the Glory Kill system in this game is what sets it apart from all the mediocre shooters on the market. There are few things in gaming that feel as badass as shooting a demon in the face with a double-barreled shotgun, staggering him, breaking his neck with glory kill, turning to his best demon buddy, ripping off his tusk, slitting his throat with the tusk, and then finally pulling out your chainsaw to slice open another demon's grotesque belly--spilling his entrails all over the floor. That's just one scenario.
-The guns are awesome. Each one (except the pistol) is fun in it's own way and feels useful and powerful. I was more afraid for the demons than I was for myself.
-Music and Atmosphere are solid.

Though the game can get a little tedious due to poor level variety, it is overall a great FPS experience. This game is easily worth 20-30 bucks, so don't be afraid to jump in when it goes on sale for much less.\t\t\t\t\t
