\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe pre-release marketing for this game was garbage. It mostly focused on the lackluster multiplayer, which featured unwelcome modern tropes such as a two weapon limit, loadouts, and XP progression. The beta received overwhelmingly negative feedback.

The box art was laughably generic.

The little single-player footage they did show looked slow, focused too much on the glory kills, and didn't show off the level design.

All of this led me to believe that this new DOOM game would completely miss the point of the original DOOM titles.

But it doesn't. The single-player campaign is genuinely fantastic. The combat is fast-paced, gory, and varied. The level design is fairly open and encourages exploration. The story is kept minimal and largely out of the way so the player can enjoy killing demons without interruption.

All in all, id proved me wrong. DOOM is legit one of the best shooters since Half-Life 2. Though with the current state of the market for AAA single-player FPS campaigns, that might not be saying much.

Nonetheless, I really enjoyed DOOM and I would like to see more games of this type - fun, gameplay-focused shooters; reminiscent of a time where FPSs weren't so po-faced and self-aggrandising in their narrative. Games unashamed of their old-school design sensibilities and unafraid to crank up the speed, throw ADS outta the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ window and shout "Let's rock!".

I don't think I'll bother with the multiplayer though...

PROS:

+ Fast-paced, FUN combat. Carry all your weapons at once, switch between them with the number keys, and blast demons apart.

+ Level design hides many secrets and encourages exploration.

+ Story is minimal, because honestly who cares about the story in DOOM?

+ What little story interaction there is basically just involves DoomGuy punching things. It's great.

+ Uncapped frame rate. Sad that this has to be a pro, but after previous idTech engines...

CONS:

- Although infrequent, interruptions do happen. There's a couple of times where you get locked in a room for a few minutes of narrative exposition. Yawn!

- Multiplayer plays it too safe, featuring modern tropes such as loadouts, the two weapon limit, and XP progression.

- SnapMap is kind of limited, and is no replacement for propper modding.

- A season pass that focuses entirely on multiplayer DLC like new maps and guns. Splitting the community with paid-for maps is crappy. Since most of the positive reception centres around the campaign, I think more people would prefer some new single-player content.

- No campaign co-op. You can make co-op stuff in SnapMap, but as said before it's limited, and also SnapMap uses the multiplayer gameplay as its base, so the co-op still has things like the two weapon limit.\t\t\t\t\t
