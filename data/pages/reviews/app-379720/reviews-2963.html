\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Brilliant and pleasingly brutal redemption of DOOM, updated for the 21st century. Within seconds of starting the campaign you are killing, as it should be. When you need health you don't hide behind a wall but beat it out of your demonic foes with your bare hands. Indeed the game learned a lot of the right lessons from the Brutal Doom mod. Speed and aggression are the keys to victory. Hell devours the indolent.

The multiplayer can be safely ignored unless you crave Halo style combat but don't actually own the latest Halo game for some reason. Oddly enough multiplayer is where they're pushing all the forgettable DLC. The built in "Snap-Map" level editor is more interesting, and continues to get some impressive free improvements. It still has a long way to go before it reaches the origional Doom mapping/modding community, but that's not entirely fair as that community has a 20 year head start.

Regardless, the meaty single player campaign is well worth the price of admission. Rip and Tear, untill it is done.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExpected it to be hot garbage since Bethesda acquired id Software, but I was pleasantly surprised.

Story isn't all that great but it's there, gameplay is fast and challenging (as challenging as shooting everything that moves and searching for keycards can be, that is,) and gunplay is very satisfying. All things considered the singleplayer part of the game is extremely fun and definetly worth a second playthrough.

Multiplayer, on the other hand, met my initial expectations and then some. But that was to be expected considering a third-party development team was working on it and they clearly had no idea what they were doing. Avoid at all costs.

Not really worth the price they're currently asking (though I don't regret cashing out €60) for it so you should probably get it when it's on sale, considering the multiplayer component is pretty bad and the campaign is rather short.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPaid $20 during the Black Friday sale. Great price for what I'm pleasantly surprised to say is a great game. This isn't gonna change your life or leave you with a new perspective, but it looks ♥♥♥♥♥♥' amazing, sounds ♥♥♥♥♥♥' amazing, and is a hell of a lot of fun.

Particulars: there are multiple upgrade systems for weapons and armor abilities. These are distinct and separate from the system used to upgrade your health, max armor capacity, and max ammo capacity. I expected it to end there but there's also a Rune system that's another layer of customization and/or Ease of Use kind of stuff. (Example: one rune increases the range at which you suck in pickups. Not a game changer but nice to have.) The weapons themselves have two distinct "special" abilities (all but one weapon, anyway) that can be switched on the fly and have their own challenges to complete to further upgrade and "max out" the weapon.

Surprisingly, there's quite a bit of exploration to be had. And there are armor upgrades that simplify this exploration in that you'll have the option to make secrets and miscellaneous map data appear without having to find it on your own. As someone who prefers to be thorough but is also impatient this was a welcome surprise.

Honestly, at high quality as this game is I'm stunned Bethesda had anything to do with it. I haven't encountered any bugs so far and I'm at Argent Towers(?) now, which I believe is the fourth mission. I'm even able to alt+tab out to write this review and nothing has crashed, no messed up colors, nada. Again: shocked Bethesda had their hands on this.

Related to that, don't spend more than $20 on this since you'll have to buy additional map packs for more multiplayer maps, more armor skins, more weapons and gear. If they're going to try and go the EA route with this nickel and dime ♥♥♥♥ save your money for a GOTY package. I was willing to roll the dice for $20 due to A) no interest in multiplayer and B) Steam refunds policy. If you're wanting this for death matches with friends and frenemies wait for a steep discount. Hell, if nothing else, wait for the discount and share the love. You'll end up getting 2-3 copies for a bargain.\t\t\t\t\t
