\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is metal. If you can enjoy metal, if you like the pounding kick and grindy rush of heavy, distorted guitars that create that feeling of raw power, you will like DOOM. However DOOM is not a game that gives you everything there is to enjoy on a plate. It is as fun as much fun YOU bring to it. If you play slow, and hide behind cover, it will get boring. If you are constantly moving, switching between weapons, combo-ing shots into eachother, the game is mad cathartic. I remember during my first playthrough just feeling a sense of exhaustion after big fights because oh how the HARD AS HELL music meshed so well with the rampage through demons by use of powerful, destructive weapons. The story is even handled as well as a DOOM story probably could have been handled. There is a cryptic nature underneath everything going on that doesn't water down the experience, and at times led me on to want to know more. The art direction and map design are really unique and there is just some imagery that made me go "THAT IS SO ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ RAD", a feeling which I really don't get anymore from games. There are tons of little secrets that were a joy to seek out and find, some really requiring careful observation and reasoning. I would say it is worth getting solely for the single player experience. There is some fun to be had with the co-op game modes but the pvp just doesn't feel quite right. It is metal. Are you metal? Play DOOM and BE metal. Rip and Tear.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to thank ID and Bethesda and everyone who has worked on this game to develop and publish it, and who have worked to get bugs corrected. I personally have had one-helluva great time with the game having logged over 230 hrs (to my wife's chagrin). A substantial amount of my time, early on in campaign, was spent trying to win one of the boss fights, and lately (since it came out) it has been in Arcade Mode. Don't think I have had this much entertainment from a game since Battlefield 1942 and the L4D's.
And before anyone starts to flame me, I had problems also; but have not had any crashes nor game-caused lockups in several months: That's not to say there might not be others having difficulty, just that for me the game is running really really well.
Again, Thanks Bethesda, Id, Steam, et. al.

Likes: Serious action - if you aren't moving and/or fighting you will be dead. Some difficult challenges. 5 levels of "pain" to choose from- best of luck on Ultra-Nightmare. Equipment earned/won carries over into the more advanced levels.
Arcade level (Wow!)

Dislikes: very minor, this one: Not as spooky and scary as DOOM 3/DOOM3 BFG. Still has an occasional glitch; though most seem to have been overcome, at least on my machine, I am now occasionally finding I can't shoot after switching weapons - though in fairness I may have worn-out my mouse. In Arcade Mode - Ammo Boost rune equipment count does Not carry over into next play through of a given mission or any other arcade mission/level.\t\t\t\t\t
