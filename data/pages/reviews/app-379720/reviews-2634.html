You need a good story to make a game truly enjoyable. Classic Doom and Wolfenstein did have stories, as basic as they were. Doom 3 was a great game, but Wolfenstein 2009 was an aweful game and I began to think ID had forgotten what good games were. While not devloped by ID Wolfenstein the New Order was a great game a a return to a classic FPS feel. I went into Doom 2016 wanting it to be great. I did like having the classic Doom HUD and the unlimited clip. Those were great features and ID got those right. I cannot forgive the extreme emphasises "glory kills". Doom is about shooter your way out, not meleeing your way out.

If you never played the classic first person shooters this is probably a great game. If this game was not called Doom, I might have a different opinion of it. However they choose to call it Doom so I have to judge it compared to the classic two games, and it is just not good when compared to them.

I know Steam does not do number based ratings, if they did I would give this game a 2.5/5 but they do not like half stars I would round down in this case.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA perfect example how to NOT make a game. 2/10
EDIT: Updates and vulkan mode makes it slightly better: 5/10 - Still not recommended; better play Doom Eternal

Graphics:
Who thought that OpenGL is a good idea for a fast shooter game like this? The over-colored envirements look like extracted from a childs book. textures pop up as you look at them because of that useless "prerender" option that just make the game unplayable at nearly 18fps with a gtx 750 and an i7. (other modern games plays fine with this hardware setup) Leveldesign is pretty okay but not as good as in doom 3.

Sound:
The soundtrack is great but becomes annoying as hell when you progress in the game. Every time, when enemies spawn, the soundtrack changes to a more agressive combat style until you killed the last enemy from said spawn. This is exactly the same as in painkiller or serious sam but they did it right and doom sadly did not. noises from enemies feel like they are played directly infront of you. I had trouble seeing enemies because the sound indicated a whole other direction than the enemies are actually standing.

Gameplay:
I had a very bad feeling about the "glory kill" moves at first but they are pretty okay. there are just a few problems with it. first of all, you're not invincible during glory kills.. at least not for the whole kill move wich leads often to death after glory kill animation because you can't move until the animation is fully played. EDIT: This problem got fixed. You can still get killed during glory kills but the input-off-time is now way more acceptable.

If you try to avoid the glory kills, you often end up shooting more than enough bullets till an enemy gets down without glory kill... I think without the kill moves you need 3 times more bullets then with a glory kill.

next thing I'll have to cover is the fact, that this game playes almost exactly like serious sam or painkiller. you enter an area, all doors get locked, a fixed amount of enemies spawn and after you killed them all you can continue to the next area. and with the changig sound, you always know when an enemy is still alive. this ruins the whole scary part that doom3 had. areas with enemies are mostly build in a circle and play like multiplayer maps. enemies spawn in almost every spot at almost the same time so that you end up running in said circles untill every enemy is down.

weapon upgrades are just there to make the s*** look a bit better. they're nearly useless in my opinion because the secondary firemode of the weapons do not enough damage to be usefull, I almost never used them.

the game offers ingame challanges to unlock some sort of talisman which makes enemies stay longer in the tumbling glory kill state or increases the radius in which items get collected but like the weapon upgrades, they're more or less useless because you just stand right next to the items, when enmies drop them (f.e.)

Story and Playtrough:
the story feels pretty rushed. you get the chainsaw and I believe the first 4 weapons in the 2nd level of the game. and thats almost everything you need to complete the game (I never used the gatling gun or the bfg which you'll get later) while doom 3 took care of slowly getting into something like a story, the new doom just throws you in hell and then you're back on the mars base and that was it. I knew hardly what I was doing besides clearing areas and walking to the next ones. At certain points you'll have to find a keycard or a switch which is pretty boring depending that this game wants to be an arcade game. I guess they tried to make something like doom 1 but horribly failed. The only good thing is, that the checkpoints are decent placed and there was never a situation where I had no idea what to do next.

If you want to play a creepy horror game with some action: play doom 3
if you want to play an arcade game like the good old dooms, just play painkiller or seriouse sam instead.

+ soundtrack is decent
+ movement is acceptable
+ great animations
+ interesting leveldesign

- graphics are too expensive for what they look like
- textures are too colorfull imo
- glowing "glory kill" effects arround enemies destroy the atmosphere
- no quicksave, just checkpoints
- enemies look worse than in doom 3
- a lot of useless feautures\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: DOOM is probably one of the better triple A single-player shooters that's come out in a long time, but as an actual DOOM title, despite being a little better than Doom 3, still doesn't feel like a DOOM game.

First off, let me point out that I have religiously played the original Doom games a hell of a lot (No pun intended) and when the initial teasers for Doom 4 actually came out, it looked impressive. Much more so than Doom 3 did which tried for a more spooky approach to what Doom in the current era should feel like. I watched a few e-celebs play it, and in all fairness, it looked fun. Emphasis on, 'looked' fun.

To start with, the game is a WHOPPING 58GB. For real? How unoptimised is your game, id software? Compared to other shooters that have more content and, arguably, look better, where did all of those extra gigabytes get thrown in? Are you seriously telling me you couldn't compress any of your textures or audio, especially considering most of the audio spoken in game is all crackly and filtered through to make it sound like it's come out of some bad signal radio.

On to the gameplay now, within the first hour or so I already knew where I stood on this game. Picking up the starting pistol, it was nice to see it take out the first sets of enemies in single headshots, I thought 'Oh, hey! This game's going to be quite fast paced then?'. But as I picked up the initial shotgun and the first 'imp' spawned in, I could feel my childhood almost disappear. Not only does it have a puny capacity and power, but the range on it was attrocious! All of the enemies feel like enormous bullet sponges, and the guns you get near the start feel pathetic. After slogging through even further, I started to see this big emphasis on using these 'equipment items' like grenades, and altfire upgrades to existing weapons that you could utilise, and even these felt very weak. Why couldn't you have just, you know, made the actual shotgun decent to use in the first place instead of making the player rely on extras to use? None of the weapons were satisfying to use at all. It doesn't help the fact that there are so few enemies during combat, and how slow the player's speed is compared to the original games. It actually feels slower paced and more sluggish than the originals, which, for a supposed reboot, is despicable.

I've seen a lot of people praise DOOM for it's core focus on the gameplay with few cutscenes, with the game poking fun at this by the Doomguy throwing away a monitor as it tries to spout exposition at him. But seemingly everything the Doomguy does has some kind of cutscene to it, for instance, pressing a button, finding a keycard or secret collectible, picking up a new weapon, pushing open a jammed door, all tiny little cutscenes that add pointless padding. Beating the second area, for instance, the game locked me into some unskippable cutscene full of filler that I couldn't skip through that lasted a good minute or two. So much for fast paced, high octane action. The glory kill system is also a big example of this as well, with the combat actually taking a heavy reliance to using it when you play on the hardest difficulties (Which I did, I ran through the first two levels on Nightmare) and the game simply does not give you much of anything. Ammo and health are scarce and you're pretty much forced to use these slow, boring glory kills to take out enemies for the extra health they drop. The chainsaw is also relegated to a 'get your ammo back' tool for this very purpose, being a cutscene only weapon that shows the Doomguy cutting an enemy to pieces instead of, you know, the player actually skillfully dodging enemy fire to dance around the enemy with the chainsaw like in the original games. The game just feels so... sluggish.

And it's a disappointment, really. As a big Doom fan I wanted to love this game. Compared to most shooters released today, it's a step up. But it's still not saying much. For a revival of the franchise you could have done so much more with this. The music is perhaps one of the best factors of this game, with Mick Gordon of Killer Instinct fame doing the soundtrack he really hit home what a gory, action-filled romp through hell should sound like. The game's just not Doom enough for me. Considering how much this game costs, as well, you'd get much more bang for your buck buying the original two Doom games than buying this.

EDIT: Considering that this game was full priced at launch and is a fraction of that now, I do recommend you give it a go if it goes on sale. The game's not awful by any means, it is a good game, it just doesn't feel very much like Doom to me.\t\t\t\t\t
