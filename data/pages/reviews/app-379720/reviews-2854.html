\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty much a master class in doing run and gun FPSes going forward. This is a review largely of the single-player mode, beaten on Hurt Me Plenty, unlocking most upgrades and secrets but not all. I played some snapmaps but not multiplayer. No DLC was purchased.

It's kind of weird to realize that the original Doom's legacy has faded over the years, with the general tendency going towards military-flavored games that are about cover-based combat and precision accuracy. DOOM, on the other hand, is messy. You don't take cover - you might exploit it for a moment - but demons can hop over nearly any cover and rush down on you. It's all about staying in motion and relatively close combat in small to mid-sized arenas. Line of sight is often broken to enforce this, and though a few weapons can reward 360 noscope headshots, most of the time you're just throwing death in the demons' direction. Killing demons rewards you with health and ammo, but only if you're close enough to pick them up. Many, like the classic pinkies or hell knights, will constantly rush you. The mechanics enforce the constant motion and close up-combat, with "glory kills" rewarding you tremendously for close-up executions.

If there's a drawback, it's that DOOM plays a lot of its hand early, and the mid-to-late levels start to feel a little like going through the motions. Thankfully, those motions take a while to become stale, and that's when it starts dropping bosses to mix things up. About the only criticism that can be leveled as its bosses is that it takes a good while to see any, and there aren't nearly enough of them. Hopefully a sequel can deliver more.

DOOM also gives a fair number of secrets and achievements to go through, many of which reward you with upgrade points to improve your armor, weapons, runes (passive upgrades), or actual upgrades themselves. For the most part these feel good to find, but the number of them is a little thick. Moreover, what constitutes a "secret" can be slightly obtuse at times, and a guide will likely be necessary to find them all. It's a good idea to start, but fumbles the landing slightly. Most achievements aren't too strenuous, though some of the weapon ones can be slightly annoying to go through. Overall, it's at least interesting in how it rewards you varying your arsenal and trying different things, but I can't say my 20th headshot with the pistol to complete its upgrades taught me more than the first 5 did.

There are also snapmaps, community-designed maps. Some seem to be honest attempts at offering new levels, but obviously all they can do is remix existing fights and offer added difficulty. Others are created just to give you cheap ways to earn "snap points", the multiplayer customization difficulty. I didn't get too deep into it because they struggle somewhat to offer something new I wouldn't get by diving back into the single-player campaign so far.

The characterization of the Doomguy is subtle surprisingly strong, a no-nonsense demon slayer feared above all else. Though there is a story, it's somewhat intrusive and leads to a painfully predictable and weak ending. In general it's a decent enough reason to keep Doomguy going forward, at the same time any time your controls are taken away from you it's a likely cue for an eyeroll moment. That isn't too much of a drawback- the lore is generally well done, but it could have led to something more enjoyable in the narrative. DOOM, ultimately, needed to make its narrative more about empowerment and self-indulgence than the disempowered note it ends on.

Overally, though, if you like the old games or if you just want to walk knee-deep in big demon guts, this is a very good game. Definitely one of the most satisfying single-player FPSes of all time as I write this, hopefully iD can serve up more of it in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

I prefer Doom 3 over this.
Played the sH*t out of Doom 3 while with this game I played about a few hours worth and couldn't continue.
The fast paced constantly needing to punch some demon's face in and ripping their arms apart to heal gets tiresome and quite frankly gives me a headache. The game's fast pacing is very nauseating.
