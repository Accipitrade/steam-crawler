\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom the very beginning of campaign mode, DOOM sets out to do what the original Doom did all those years ago: Be a bloody, fun, demon-shootin' time. The game succeeds with style, bringing new "Glory Kills" (brutal melee kills) into the fray and actually providing a reason to use them. A successful Glory KIll yields guaranteed health pickups, so getting up-close and personal with enemies is essential to survival. In addition, while chainsaw fuel is limited, its use grants guaranteed ammo pickups, once again ensuring that the mechanic is used to its fullest potential.

Multiplayer mode reminds me significantly of both the original Unreal Tournament's multiplayer and Quake 2's multiplayer. In other words, it's fast-paced arena-style combat with all the brutality you'd expect from a DOOM game. As far as competitive aspects go, leveling up doesn't grant you a true advantage over your opponents, aside from some unlocks of custom loadouts to replace the pre-existing ones. Still, the weapons available are always the same for every player, so the game plays more to skill than some shooters that require you to be leveled up in order to unlock the best weapons.

So do I recommend this game? Definitely. Is it perfect? No. The story could use some work, but then, that's not why you're playing DOOM, now is it? The game meets every goal it set out to achieve; and it surpasses them in bloody technicolor.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis Doom to me is more than just a remake. Doom 3 felt like a remake gone wrong and this feels like the successor the franchise was so desperately in need of. I can see how it grabs bits and pieces of various games in the genre but mostly I see/feel how much it caried over from older id games such as Quake and Rage.

Visually, you can see how much the id tech 6 engine has grown since id tech5 (Rage). Rage had terrible texture pop in regardless of system hardware, PCI SSD, and any other bells and whistles. Now that is gone with the exception of a tiny bit when you first load a level. Also gone is the use of shadows to cover low res textures and the flashlight in Doom 3. I say good riddance as the game's visuals truly shine.

The gameplay is as fluid as you'd expect from Doom and swapping guns and their mods during a firefight works beautifully. I also really like the addition of "glory kills" (melee) and feel the health/ammo dropped during them is truly rewarding. Speaking of melee kills, the chainsaw is still here and is balance/OP in the most perfect way possible. It has very limited ammo, can kill anything up to mid level demons in 1 hit, and causes a wealth of ammo for various weapons to drop when used. In short, the chainsaw is a superb item to have available when nearly out of ammo.

Level design is top notch. I will agree with various other reviewers in saying it doesn't shine in the very beginning but quickly grows in size, complexity, and of course the key count. If you feel the need for more levels, there's the addition of snapmap. Snapmap allows users to create and play custom maps in a manner much more simple than running custom WAD files in older Dooms.

Multiplayer is diverse and lacking at the same time. As of 5-29-16 you can easily find people playing TDM or warpath modes but the other modes tend to lack players. This probably isn't a surprise when you consider this is an arena shooter trying to gain love while people are still playing BF4, CS, COD, Overwatch, and various "esports" games. The level count also seems low, I don't know how many there are but you can expect to see all the levels in an hour or 2.

I highly recommend this game to anybody that has an appreciation for arena shooters and gore. I honestly hope this game gives Epic a reason to create Unreal 3 because Quake and Unreal are a couple of my favorite shooters from the late 90s.

Feel free to find and frag me if you buy this game, I hope to run into you in the fiery pits of Hell. ;)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA RETURN TO FORM:

TL;DR: Kickass campaign with sparse but fun multiplayer and snapmap; had several technical issues even after downgrading graphics. 8/10


PROS:
+Brutal Campaign: The single player kicks every ass you can think of. It does an amazing job of combing older mechanics (like no reloading except for the Super Shotgun) and newer mechanics (like double jumping). And the campaign is difficult , I played on Ultra Violence and there were so many times I running around looking for health, ammo, and armor, and I died at least 2-3 dozen times. Even in the beginning, when the enemy and gun varieties were minimal, you still find enjoyment in slaughtering all the demons around you. Plus the only objective is to kill everything in sight, no escort missions, no protecting the payload, and certainly no "PRESS F TO PAY RESPECTS". Just go on a demon killing rampage.

+Break-neck combat: the Doomguy is fast. He is always running and nothing will slow him down, not even a direct hit from a Mancubus fireball. Also, there is no cover system and no regenerating health, you have to pick up health in order to survive. if you stop moving around then you will get surrounded and killed. The game also encourages movement, as it scatters the ammo, health, and armor around the map so you run around trying to collect everything. Also the gun variety in this game is spectacular, you have a gun for every situation. Surrounded by Imps and Possessed Soldiers? Combat and Super Shotty. Ran into a Revenant? Heavy Assault Rifle. Hell Knight and Cacodemon giving you trouble? RPG. Barons of Hell and Mancubi won't leave you alone? Gauss Cannon. Being overwhelmed by all the above? Time for the BFG 9000. Plus each weapon allows you to modify it to your playing style by finding field drones, that let you unlock weapon mods. And the boss fights are incredible, you will feel the adrenaline pumping as the music begins playing and the boss begins his onslaught.

+Glory Kills: This system is perfectly implemented into the game as you can do these kills to get Health, and also keep the flow of combat going. I once glory killed a Hell Knight only to be confronted by a Cacodemon and was immediatly able to shoot the demon once the glory kill was done. Not a fan of how the game tells you when it's ok to do a glory kill? Turn off the Glory kill highlight so you have to pay attention to the signs to do a glory kill. Don't want to do a glory kill? You don't need to.

+Exploration: There are a lot of secrets to uncover which will extend the time of your campaign in a meaningful way. From finding weapons before you are supposed to, to finding and completing rune trials to become a more efficient killing machine, to finding Doomguy action figures. There are a lot of nooks and crannies to search and find goodies.

+Enjoyable Multiplayer: Let's get this out of the way, yes the MP Beta was blasted to hell (pun intended) but it was still fun for me. Just like the campaign, there is no covering and no regenerating health so you have to rely on pick ups to remedy your low health. The combat in MP is just as fast paced as in SP and just as brutal. The fact that the MP isn't fully reliant on weapon pickups (super weapons like the Gauss Cannon or BFG have to be picked up) actually works MP in my opinion, because now you don't have veterans who know where all the good waepons are and slaughtering new players. You also don't have people camping in areas waiting for their favorite weapon to spawn, you can start off with your favorite weapon in hand. However, not everything about the multiplayer is great ( I will explain what it is in the Cons).

+Snapmap: This mode is great as it allows anyone to make and play custom maps. Mapmaking is incredibly user friendly allowing you to make changes on the fly. The Snapmap tutorial is great at explaining how the feature works to anyone, from people who have never coded in their life, to veteran modders.

CONS:
+Leveling up in MP: This disappointed me because the game hides weapons behind a leveling wall. I wouldn't mind leveling up in MP so much if it just unlocked cosmetics and hack moduels, but now weapon access is restricted so you might not be able to start off with your favorite weapon. Another thing that upsets me is that the weapon balance needs a little tweaking, the Chaingun is super OP.

+Sparse snapmap: Snapmap will not replace modding. The rooms you are given are only from the Mars Base, nothing from Hell. and the 12 demon limitation is for the entire map, so even if you have a map that takes 1 hour to traverse, you will only be able to place 12 demons and every other room will be filled with easy to kill possessed scientists and engineers.

+Technical Problems: While playing the campaign I encountered numerous framerate drops and freezes. Now I don;t know if this is because my system isn't very powerful (I have an i5-3570k, gtx 960 SSC, and 8 GB RAM) or if my download was somehow corrupted or if the game wasn't optimized. One glitch I ran into was a delayed input, I would tell the Doomguy to move forward and he wouldn't but when he would he wouldn't stop until even after I let go of the W key. I also ran into some really bad frame rate problems, where the frame rate would drop down to 5 FPS and stay that way, even after I changed all the graphical settings to Low and updating the Drivers. Now this would be fixed by exiting the game and starting it up again, but it still happened multiple times. But the worst thing to happen was the game freezing. it froze on me 5 times so far, with only unfreezing itself twice. The other 3 times I couldn't do anything, not Alt+Tab, or Ctrl+Alt+Del. So I had to do a forced shut down, 3 times. So if your system is slightly underpowered, be weary.

So in all, the game gets an 8/10 for an incredible and in depth campaign and a fun MP and Snapmap. However things about the MP, a sparse Snapmap, and numerous Technical Glitches hamper the experience...only a little bit. Definetly a must buy for Old School shooter fans or people who want a change in their shooters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSince the game is an adreneline rush from beginning to end, I'll try my best to summarize everything.

Did you enjoy the original Doom games? Buy this game.
Do you want classic FPS elements without following glorified tour guides? Buy this game.
Do you want non-stop gunfights, access to ALL of your weapons, with the story aspects not interfering often? Buy this game.
Do you want a campaign with good length and lots of replay value, with multiple difficulties and upgrade systems? Buy this game.
Do you enjoy bloodbaths, lots of gore, plenty of guns with secondary effects, and a wide variety of enemies in your wake, capped off with some of the best boss fights ever made? Buy this game.
Did you come here for the multiplayer? Eh, it's fun, but that should not be the main reason you're here. Play single player.
Did you enjoy Timesplitters' level editor tools? Buy this game.
Did you enjoy Halo's forge modes? Buy this game.

I know this is all blunt, but I was genuinely surprised by how amazing the single player campaign was. Multiplayer is ok, but definitely stay for the single player. Snapmap is fun too, but I do hope some of the limits are alleviated and more options become available (such as removing that dumb 2 weapon limit from multiplayer), and that feature will be the closest you get to classic Doom multiplayer, including Capture the Flag.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter recently finishing the new DOOM, I decided I should share my opinions and thoughts on if you should buy the game. In this review, we will be looking at the Pros, Mixed, and Cons of DOOM (2016)

Pros:
+ Extremely fun and satisfying gameplay. As everybody knows, DOOM is a FPS, but DOOM (2016) is not Doom 3. The gameplay is extremely fast paced like original DOOM games, like The Ultimate Doom and Doom 2. As much as I liked Doom 3, I found it extremely slow and at some points boring. DOOM solves this greatly by making it extremely fast paced, which is something I think fans of the original game were looking for.

+ The graphics are extremely good, especially when set to higher settings. This game graphics much better than Bethesda's Fallout 4, which were showing the age of Skyrim's engine. Also, it runs a lot smoother than Fallout 4, which only adds to the gameplay of DOOM, allowing it to be fast paced.

+ An extremely long story campaign. The game will take you around 12-13 hours to beat, or at least for me it did.

+ Tons of replay value. In Wolfenstein: The Old Blood, I noted how I thought the game was fun, but there was this section that took me more than 2 hours to beat, because it had force stealth (When stealth is forced upon to beat a section or at least make it barely impossible) in it, which pissed me off greatly. However, very few sections in DOOM are frustrating. If I died, I felt that it was usually my fault and it was not extremely difficult to the point of making it a chore to play, except for the Final Boss, but what do you expect. I would honestly replay this game, especially since how fun it was.

Mixed:
- SnapMap and Multiplayer are not that good. I would have put this at cons, BUT I am thankful that they at least tried it. Wolfenstein: The New Order nor Wolfenstein: The Old Blood had multiplayer, which makes me happy that they at least tried it. I haven't really tried Snapmap, but from looking at it, it is something that could have potential in the future.

- No modding tools except for Snapmap. This could be considered a killer for some people, but I need people to remember if the core game itself isn't good, but mods make it better, than the game is not good.

Cons:
- Crashes. Throughout my entire experience, I had around 5-6 desktop crashes and one full computer crash, which was annoying. Luckily, I did not have to restart a major part or lose any of my data, but I would imagine it being hell if anybody had to restart the entire campaign because of a crash corrupting their saves.

- Glitches. At first, I was surprised at how I experienced NO glitches. UNTIL mission 12, where I experienced a major glitch, forcing me to restart at the checkpoint. Basically, an enemy was invisible and impossible to hit. I looked around for maybe 20 minutes, trying to find this enemy, but could never do so. I gave up and was forced to restart from a checkpoint.

FINAL VERDICT

9.5/10

That is my review of DOOM (2016). I recommend buying this game right now, by right now, I don't mean a minute, I mean RIGHT DAMN NOW. This game is extremely fun and it is worth every penny. I hope they make a sequel and I am damn excited for the DLC.\t\t\t\t\t
