\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBe damned if you dare to say that this is a worthy successor to DooM!

I've always said it since I say the footage shown at the E3 2015 that this DooM was going to be bad; I bought it, I spent 15€ to don't pirate it and say that yes: it's the worst DooM ever made.

This is a console FPS, not fast paced at all, that resorts to all kind of exploits to give the player a false sense of frenzy while having an awkward gameplay full of fatalities (which are mandatory to survive and progress at higher difficulities), and RPG mechanics that shift the attention from what made DooM a game remembered forever, to an endless hunt for secrets and upgrades.
The upgrades in particular, destroy what is left of the gameplay; forcing the player to complete tasks in each level, in order to grant access to the full potential of its weapons.

Finally, the game is a huge PvE arena FPS, where the protagonist isn't allowed to progress if all the monsters in the area haven't been killed. Way to kill the action, forgive me the pun, if you would like to dash through the demons as fast as possible.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7kRyjKlvLlA

Did you think it was all? Of course not!
What could top a casualised FPS for console gamers, other than a story full of clichés? (which, at the time I'm writing this review, I still haven't the displeasure of fully unfold)

The protagonist: A holy hero with a pure soul devoted to the higher good.
The bad guy gal: an old, scraggy, woman who wants to bring hell on earth for no other reason that being evil (you occupy a very top position in your social hierarchy, where techonology is so advanced you could live forever technically, and you send everything to hell, literally. Let's pretend that she just went out of her mind for review's purposes, which are "not writing a philosophical monologue out of a standard mythical story about an hero and an antihero").
The AI that magically didn't get corrupted/deleted/mulfunctions even though the bad granny took over all kind of technology in the Mars installation and the protagonist is forced to manually override everything. Convenient.

What about the upgrade bots flying around? As if one would design things such as that during the middle of a battle with an unspecified aggressor you would have to run around chasing flying robots that guard weapon upgrades you might need, rather than just giving you the weapon AND the upgrade when you leave the armory to go to fight.
I was forgetting... This is a fictional game, where I'm supposed to go beyond inconsistencies between its various aspects and enjoy the gameplay.
The crippled gameplay..
The gameplay I wrote about above...
I'm pretending to like this game, enjoying the action and all... but I progressed past a secret I need/want, I missed a collectible/upgrade so let me try to load... Ohhh right you can't save when you want, you can only submit to the autosave's will, and have the game saved when and only when the game decides (to hell your free will, along with all the UAC and the rest of the universe!).
We're talking about a PC game, I'd like to remind you.
We're talking about a PC game that works on consoles too..
We're talking about a PC game that was developed with consoles in mind, and whose developers didn't care bit to at least refine some techincal aspects of the PC version to make it less obnoxious to play...

This is NOT DooM. This is a first person shooter that wipes away any other console FPS developed in the last 15 years, but which is a very mediocre PC FPS.

If you need ratings:
Graphic: 10/10
Sound: 6/10
Longevity: 7/10
Gameplay: 4/10

Overall: 6/10

There is no "buy this game if". Don't -ing buy this game! Not even if you want to play a first person shooter from your couch with a controller.
This game is good only for its graphic. Just don't waste money on this junk: buy Serious Sam instead. If you really want to play this game: "family share" it with someone.
