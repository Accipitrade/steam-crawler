\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started gaming in the DOS era. I remember playing games on a monochromatic IBM machine that was yellow and black. When I played DOOM Shareware edition it was my first experience into first person gaming. Doom, Doom II and Wolfenstein were my favourite FPS games. Nothing would ever compare properly except Unreal 1 and Serious Sam TSE in terms of run and gun high speed slaughter... that is, until Doom 4.

Doom 4 is a masterpiece, a master-crafted work of art of the First Person Shooter. It prioritizes single player instead of that crappy multiplayer garbage. Doomguy has more character and personality through action and silence than most main characters of other games. It has beautiful homages to classic doom and tons of fun secrets to discover. Played in it's penultimate difficulty it's a fantastic experience.

By and far Doom 4 is no only the best DOOM I've ever played (previously a title held by Doom 64) but it is quite easily the BEST First Person Shooter I've EVER played of ALL TIME.

My only disappointment in the entire experiences is that the Unmaker isn't present.

If you appreciate history of gaming, the evolution of FPS and a finely polished gaming experiences (and also if you think multiplayer is stupid and too many games use it as a crutch) you must experience Doom 4 for yourself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr at bottom

Very fun, fast-paced, adrenaline fueled shooter from the long line of the same. DOOM makes some subtle changes to the series but doesn't really take away from the existing legacy.

I was expecting another DOOM 3, and I got that but this time I can actually SEE the beautifully crafted world that I'm splattering with demon blood.

The decision to take away reloading and make every game pull from an existing "bank" of ammo is a little weird to get used to, but comes in handy as there is a never-ending horde of demons for you to unload on.

The map - it's very confusing and weird. I don't know why they added it. Well, it's to help navigate through the more non-linearity of the levels but it's fairly confusing and disorienting until you get used to it. The map is clunky and overlapping, it's hard to tell where you're going if you're on a lower level. Overall I just used it to try to find hidden stuff.

Map design - The levels are more complex than previous DOOM titles. Some of them play out as more non-linear "open world feeling" maps. It's pretty easy to miss secrets and bypass ammo/armor spawns. Some parts of the maps can be bypassed entirely. That's where this new map addition comes into play. I'm indifferent to the map design. It's neat, but not really necessary.

Graphics / sound - As you would expect for a AAA title in 2016, the graphics are superb. Everything runs VERY smoothly (50+ FPS) on my, now showing age, computer. Every map is well lit (enough), animations are incredibly detailed, and the sound.... OH MAN the sound. This game is best played in a dark room with a speaker at each ear and a subwoofer at your feet - put it on the highest notch. This is one of the only games that I don't turn the music off (I hate playing games with music...) The hard-hitting metal-themed sci-fi music is one of the best parts of the game. If anything, turn SFX, dialog, everything down and leave the music on 100.

I haven't tried the multiplayer, and I have no intentions on doing so. If it's not co-op, then I don't care. I don't understand why games don't include co-op within their campaigns, but they'll put in waste-of-disc space PVP elements. Dumb. Especially a game like DOOM, co-op would be a freakin' BLAST!

tl;dr It's DOOM, buy it, play it, LIVE IT.\t\t\t\t\t
