\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI admit I was skeptical of whether this would be good or not up until lauch, but I decided to go for it once I heard the positive reception. Have to admit it was worth it. Single player is one of the most fun things I've ever played. The campaign was great from beginning to end. Loved the added RPG elements like upgrading your guns. So many secrets and collectibles, exploring the levels was amazing. Very few criticisms, mainly the lack of quicksaves was annoying. Granted, it wasn't as big of a deal as I thought, the game was pretty generous with checkpoints, but still I would have liked a quicksave or manual save. Also performance was spotty, although this is due to the shadow bug other people have complained about. I have a GTX 960, runs great at first then framerate eventually tanks because of the shader cache. Hopefully this bug is addressed. Also some texture pop-in while switching weapons. I hope once Vulkan support comes the game will perform even better.

Multiplayer is ok. I know a lot of PC players dislike it but it's not as awful as everyone says. The level of player customization is nice, and the hack modules make for an interesting variety each match, but my main issue is it's simply too easy to kill people with the weapon pickups. The basic weapons usually take a while to kill people, which would be fine, except the chainsaw, BFG, Gauss rifle and of course the demon are all extremely OP. There's no real middle ground, either you're killing people in one hit or firing many shots. Also don't like the glory kills, they're fine in single player but I feel they don't really belong in multi. I might change some of these opinions as I play more however.

Haven't used SnapMap much so I won't say much, just that I wish real modding tools were released. SnapMap looks interesting but the artificial limits on size and monster count seem annoying. Even worse, this game uses Denuvo so it's unlikely people will ever be able to significantly mod the game outside of graphics shaders.

Overall a great game. Amazing campaign, ok multiplayer, and a good-looking athough limited map editor. My biggest fear is that due to the DRM this game won't last 23 years like Doom 1. The problem with a game being "uncrackable" is it can die once we hit a new console gen and advance a few new operating systems. Doom 1 and 2 have lasted so long because of source ports and mods, and I'm afraid that won't be possible, or at least easily possible, with this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is great. It forgoes any phony sense of depth. You start the campaign, pick up a gun, and insert your fist through the brain stem of a zombified scientist. It's a simple concept with an elegant execution. Movement is tight; air control is satisfying with the in-flight mobility rune. Level design is open and vertical. Weapons are distinctive and complementary. Enemies are circumstantially strong and weak. The music pulsates with a bassy rhythm that breaks into crunchy guitar riffs when demons appear. A robot-man guides you to your objectives by radio, and you ignore him anyway because your character is an irascible boulder of meat. It's ridiculous, but beautiful in a way because it's self-aware. No tedious backstory or verborrhea—just demons and the meat boulder.\t\t\t\t\t
