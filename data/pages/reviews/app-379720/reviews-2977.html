\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is as DOOM as it gets. It's an amazing return to such a glorious game series that lasted two decades and continues to be active to date.

I want to go over the good and bad of this game, explaining each three parts that make up the complete package.

Campaign:

This game's campaign is amazing, it has so much to tell while never letting go of the constant action any good DOOM game has. The backstory and enjoyability of this game's campaign is so large that it astounds me. There was no part of this game's story that I did not love so much. Almost every level seems so memorable, and doomguy returns, being even more badass than before, and that is incredible. Doomguy expresses so much just by watching his arms and legs move, the animations are just so pleasing to look at. It's hard not to love Doomguy this time around, you will instantly feel the need to be him the way DOOM wants you to be. You want to be the Doomguy, and you want to kill demons. You will want to avenge the living hell (ha) out of all those people who were killed in the UAC. The game's backstory is very interesting, and is explained almost perfectly. You get an amazing fill of action from the game's new Glory Kill system, providing such a satisfying way to finish off any enemy that dares to stand in your way. The demons look great, the kills are satisfying and hell looks just as scary as it did pixilated. Speaking of pixels, the nostalgia this game gives you will make the experience only better for any old-time DOOM fan like me. The music gets you FIRED UP, and the game's soundtrack is so good you'll want to listen to it outside of this game.There's challenges, guns galore, and blood to spill. DOOM is back.

Multiplayer:

I played the open beta for this game's multiplayer, and god, I thought this game was going to end up horrible. Boy, did I not realize how different the game's campaign is from it's multiplayer. The multiplayer is alright, and can be thoroughly enjoyable (If you're good at it, otherwise you might as well just rage). Though the new loadout system over the classic pick-up-the-weapons-as-you-go style that gives you that Unreal Tournament feeling is gone. It's not so much fragging anymore, it's modern. And quite frankly it sucks. I don't play the game for it's open multiplayer, I find it to be a pretty unbalanced mess. I happened to like the customization, but I didn't understand the point of choosing your armor colors if you're always blue or red. (Actually, always blue. I don't even know if you're red or your actual chosen colors in that one gamemode that doesn't require teams.) Of course, you might find enjoyment in this game's multiplayer, I just think it could have been so much more DOOM than COD.

Snapmap:

This choice is brilliant. A way to create and play maps with friends and try out every single thing you possibly can using the game's engine adds so much playability and fun to be had. When I'm not playing the campaign, I play this. There is so much potential to be had here and it does not go to waste. Check out what other users create, you'll get just as good an experience as you would using DOOM Seeker and playing with mods for the classic games.

In terms of the gameplay, the game runs amazingly smooth (For me, at least. I get some issues when running it with Vulkan, however.) and looks fantastic. You will want this game, trust me. There's a reason it did so well. I've even looked for gameplay elements to criticize about this game, but I really cannot find much. It plays exactly how DOOM is supposed to be played. You stay still, you die. You move fast and consistently, you will be rewarded with those sweet, sweet Glory Kills. Just get this game, and see for yourself. It's worth the buy.

(P.S, I never bothered with DLC, but the free updates always add so much. This adds so much replayability to the game that you will end up playing this for a while. So buy it.)\t\t\t\t\t
