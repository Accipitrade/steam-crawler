I also read a review that mentioend the arena style moments, ie locked in a few rooms until clearing out bad guys, may suit hardcore quake arena fans but wasn't keen on this idea. The hand to hand glory kills are amusing to watch but become something of a novelty after a while. Then there's the optional time based challenges, which just stink of 'lets add something else' and detract from what doom is about. Whilst I realise I'm beginning to sound negative I don't hate the game its just not what I expected. Thankfully it looks in level design as we'll get the full compliment of levels (I was little worried after Bethesda's brill but far too short Wolfenstein). Overall a good game but don't expect the wow factor the original Doom game you when you first got that shareware disc on the front of a magazine.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Good:

The single player campaign is fantastic. The weapons feel great, the monsters are tough and downright scary, the atmosphere is amazing, and the story is excellent. It's a really well-rounded FPS game that gives me that nostalgia for old ID games like Quake 2.

The Bad:

Multiplayer is seriously lacking. There's no dedicated servers (the lobbies look like COD), and that means you can expect not so much for mods. It's sad to see Bethesda and Id come out with a game that's not so well suited to support a vibrant mod community.

I find myself wanting a coop campaign, or coop challenges, or other game modes that aren't straight arena combat.


Final Thoughts:

I'm definitely going to enjoy the rest of the story, but I'll definitely be looking out for improvements before I purchase any DLC.\t\t\t\t\t
