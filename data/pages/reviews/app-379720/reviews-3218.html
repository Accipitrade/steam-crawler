\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot perfect, but pretty good. Surprisingly, the lore seemed as entertaining as the action.

That last fact might derive from the problems of the game, though. Namely, the game really only has two gears; ghost town and meat grinder. It's highly linear, with rooms swarming with enemies one after another, separated by practically barren corridors. The lively rooms are literally swarming, enemies appearing out of thin air. Standing still means death, so the only solution is to just run around, landing shots wherever possible. The hectic pace diminished my ability to enjoy the enemy variety, as they would get placed in two broad categories, melee and ranged. As a consequence, the fights would become pretty much the same merry-go-round over and over. There are a few boss events that are fairly enjoyable.

The game is very self-conscious, which is a saving grace. The game doesn't explain why enemies just pop out of thin air, why there are maps, ammunition and even toys in Hell. Yes, some of the collectibles are silly. There is a story, but the Doom Guy just doesn't care. He wants to be done with this mess. Let's just say that it's told partly from the demons' perspective, which is pretty interesting.

The game runs okay most of the time, though it suffers from occasional, and sometimes really severe frame drops. Saving is done through check points, which I'm not a fan of. The check points and the arena-to-arena pacing seem to be a console compromise and my biggest gripes with the game.\t\t\t\t\t
