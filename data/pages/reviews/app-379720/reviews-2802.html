\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a younger gamer i was not really capable of experiencing the original DOOM games in their full glory. But this is personally one of my favorite games ever. The campaign admittedly is very linear and slightly on the predictable side, but i kind of expected that considering the type of game this is. The main story has lots of little secrets and challenges to complete, as well as the wide variety of enemies you will meet. The three bosses are pretty easy to beat, but it has been a while since i have had a proper boss fight so it was a great refresher for me. The game doesnt try to appeal to every audience and i like that. Bethesda knows what DOOM is and gave us an excellent omage to the previous days of gaming.

The multiplayer is very Quake styled, being fast paced and close range. it is different from the classic Call Of Duty multiplayer, with lots of pickups to keep yourself alive. Overall fun but nothing to wet your pants over.

Snapmap is another feature i really enjoyed as the ability to create levels and content is always a plus for me. It is pretty thourogh but limits the maps to being closed off dungeon style maps. You cant make large open areas outside.

Overall this game exceeds most expectations and is a win for Bethesda in my book.

Great job Bethesda and keep doing what your doing :)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, now that the high of actually playing this game is over with, let me go ahead and lay down my thoughts on this game.

So, firstly, I should start off with saying how much I love this game. It's everything that I would have wanted out of the ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• that was Doom 3. It's full of the rip and tear action that I could have ever hoped for, and the main character's interactions with the other characters is down-right hysterical. The reinvisioned demons from the first two games into a 3D platform look absolutely amazing (not accounting for Doom 3, because I really didn't like those recreated enemies). Also, good on you, Bethesda, for not adding the Archvile in this game and replacing them with something slightly less ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•.

With that out of the way, let me go ahead and say my gripes about the game. So, my primary problem with this game is the story. The cutscenes take away from the action of the actual game, and every time it shows up, it seems to be rather imposing. When you're not in a cutscene, it's a constant blood and gore action, always ripping and tearing your way through demon after demon. And then Dr. Samuel Hayden has to speak to you, and it's almost like a chore. Basically, this whole game could have been done without a story, and I would have loved it all the same.

My other gripe is the music. Now, don't get me wrong, it's not that I don't LIKE the music, it's rather that the music is...lacking. When you're on your murderous rampage, the music is great, it's the metal hype music that gets you wanting to kill more stuff. But, when you're doing anything other than killingthings, it's always the first 10 seconds of Doom 1's E1M2. And, if you're looking for collectibles and the like, it becomes an absolute drag. There's nothing to keep you motivated to find stuff or progress further. It's boring.

My last gripe is about the only two characters you ever interact with, outside of the AI, VEGA, that basically outlines every bad thing ever in the game. Now, I'm going to go into spoilers here, so don't read what I have to say here if you haven't yet beaten the game. The game sets you up to hate both Dr. Samuel Hayden and Olivia Pierce. Every time they are seen or mentioned, I wanted to push their ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• in, but guess what? You never get the chance. And the ending feels like it sets you up for a new installment, given the cliffhanger of Dr. Samuel Hayden betraying you. Now, I have no problem with a setup for a Doom 2 (again). Where I do have a problem, is how the ending gives no sense of closure. It leaves off on a cliffhanger in a self-imposed story that no one cared about. Everything else that happened in the game was forgettable other than going to hell and the sheer amount of carnage you sew anywhere you go.

Also, "Doomslayer" sounds like something I would have come up with when I was 12 and being really edgy. Even if "Doomguy" isn't much better. :awkward:

All of that said, I will still recommend this game because, if you are a fan of the first two Doom games or just a FPS lover, you will love this game for what it is. But if you're looking for a good story-centered game, I will gladly redirect your attention to Wolfenstein: The New Order for that. Bethesda did a better job of incoporating a story to that game than they did this one.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not a big fan of Doom 4. I was expecting a game like Doom 3 which I really liked. Doom 3 was more then Doom, it was an interactive dark ride and I'm a big fan of classic dark rides and 1970s style haunted house attractions. The atmosphere in Doom 3 was extremely forboding and it made great use of theatrical lighting effects. The music in Doom 3 was so good it blew my mind. It had much in common with French Acousmatic music like Francis Dhomont. Different areas would trigger different sound cues which was just amazing. The music in the haunted elevator scene was so good I recorded the loop to a cassette boom box to use for Halloween.

Unfortunatly Doom 4 has none of this. It's just a heavy metal album cover gore fest and is void of the creativity of Doom 3. Now, I knew this going in but, I had to see for myself. I played Doom 4 to the end but I really don't like it. I also don't like the Gauntlet-esk monster generators and I certainly don't like that you have to finishing move all of the enemys (this is especially _very_ annoying). I think Doom 3 was just too good for its own good. Several people I spoke with didnt get it. One person even argued with me about if Doom 3 had a soundtrack or not. The subltle electro acoustic sound track went over that persons head. Doom 4 is not sublte nor is it a haunted house. I believe the creaters just wanted to remake the Doom 1 vibe with modern graphics and I'd say they accomplished that but its not for me.

Hopefully Doom 5 will be a return to Francis Dhomont style music and 1970s Wildwood NJ style haunted houses because thats my jam.\t\t\t\t\t
