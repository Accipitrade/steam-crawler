\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't had this much fun with a singleplayer FPS since Half-Life 1. Actually...if we talk about sheer "fun", this might be the best SP shooter I've played, period. It's just so fast, the flow of combat is so smooth. I've never been a big fan of pure SP FPS, disliking all of the Bioshocks and most other offerings of the last decade or so. I also never really played any of the original Dooms, nor Quake.

And despite its grimdark trappings it obviously does not take itself seriously. It walks that fine line of camp and horror that even the story - shallow as it is - is quite enjoyable.

Do play this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis 2016 version of DOOM is incredible. The weapons are fantastic, the sound effects and enemies keep you wanting more and the campaign thoughly satisfied my FPS craving. The gamplay play is what makes this game great, is it smooth and fast. The levels have multiple tiers of levels to them and jumping, shooting and stomping is unmatched. The BOSS fights are very good and the available content is up to par with a AAA title.

I recommend a good sound card and headset to get the best available experience. This game does require a significant rig, but this review will not go into hardware specifics. I also recommend an Xbox 360 PC controller for this game, it will make it that much more enjoyable. The animation and 3D-Modeling is good and the variety of enemies that jump at you adds to the sense of surprize in this game.

I bought this game during the summer sale and it is worth every penny. If you like games like "Killing Fields 2" and blood and gore this game is certainly for you. If you are looking for a fast pass FPS with a good length campaign I recommend DOOM.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis Doom plays very fast, yet allows you to take it slower and explore, take your time and hunt for secrets or get those special bonus kills for upgrades. A Deus-ex like upgrade system with weapon customization allows you to modify your weapons to suit your playstyle. Suit upgrades allow you to bolster your weaknessess or add to your strengths.

Weapon wise and monster wise this game is awesome! So much stuff, variation all around makes it very interesting!

Horror wise I did not find this scary at all, perhaps due to the massive amount of fun had whilst playing.

If you loved Doom 2, you'll love this.

And, as a bonus the storyline is actually really good.

Movement wise, you are overpowered. People who dread hard jumping parts need not worry, Double jumping and wall grappling make it near impossible to fall into a pit of death.

Recommend this game for anyone who loves killing demons and getting more and more awesome weapons and finishing moves that turn your enemy into a giant health pinata. Rune customisation further allows you to mod the game to your style. You love glory kills and getting more out of it? no probs! You want infinite ammo when at a certain armour lvl? No probs! Find more loot? OK! Have more time to perform a finishing move, longer stuns etc etc.. combinations are so much fun!

tl:dr

Before I played this is thought Doom 3 was good.
Now I think Doom 3 is just.. well... pales in comparison!

Shortly:
Doom is all about killing demons and this game delivers just about that. Killing demons in the most brutal ways is enjoyable, but it can get quite repetive towards the end.

The story in this game is almost non-existence, but it is there. You wake up from a lab and you're immediately pulled into action. As you move on, you'll start to learn that what has happened in the facility, but you tend to forget the story for most of the time.. The overall story is weak, but the good gameplay mechanics compensates for that.

The multiplayer in this game is painfully average, but luckily I didn't buy this game for its Multiplayer. I managed to play the MP for about an hour before I had enough. It just didn't pick my interest as I had hoped it would.

As for the amount of content, it took me about 18 hours to complete the main campaign, but I spent about 6-7 hours from that finding secrets, collecting collectibles and upgrading my weapons/armor/runes.

Overall, the Doom was enjoyable game, but I wish the MP would have been little stronger.


Pros:
+ Enjoyable boss battles
+ Weapon mods and armor/rune upgrades
+ Decent amount of content
+ Smooth combat and gameplay mechanics

Cons:
- Almost non-existence story
- Campaign has no replay value, unless you want to kill some more demons again
- Only 3 boss battles
- No backtracking on several maps, which makes finding secrets and collectibles annoying, because you have to restart the map if you progress too far
- Kinda boring and weak multiplayer
- Campaign, Multiplayer and Snapmap have their own seperate startups
- Cliffhanger ending

Meh:
+/- Snapmap - building your own maps is nice, but it takes quite a lot of time to learn how to do that


Do I recommend?
If you want to buy the game for its campaign, then yes. But if you're solely interested of buying the game for its Multiplayer only, then don't bother. There are many other online shooters out there that are way better than this multiplayer is, but of course this is just one individual opinion.

Verdict: 7.5 / 10 - Enjoyable game

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: awesome!

The combat is so much fun in this game. The arena battles are intense and it is very refreshing to play a FPS where you can not just blast down one enemy at a time. Movement really is life in this game. You have to think on your feet about when and which enemies you use your chainsaw on, at what point you grab a power up, which weapon and mod will work best, which runes you need, and which enemies you should try to kill first.

I completed the campaign on Ultra Violence and I do not consider myself a particular great FPS player. It was challenging but you have so many tools that the battles never felt insurmountable. Nightmare is when have to get every last drop of value from each tool at your disposal, because making a mistake can, and will, cost you your life.

For those who are concerned with game performance, I am running Windows 10 with an Intel Core i-7 5820, Asus x99 Deluxe MB, 32gb DDR4 Corsair ram, and a nVidia Gefore 960 GTX (4gb vram) and with all graphic/video options set to High or Ultra, I maintained 60-90 FPS at a screen resolution of 1920 x 1080. The game has not crashed a single time for me.

I rarely play multiplayer FPS games, and I have no intentions to play DOOM on multiplayer. So, I cannot comment on that aspect of the game.

If you like FPS games, I definitely recommend DOOM.\t\t\t\t\t
