\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor those those of us who truly have that special place inside our hearts for the Doom franchise, we can truly appreciate a work of art like this. This new DOOM, or as I like to call it DOOM 4, does not waste any time giving you useless plot and or exposition. You literally hear a guy say one sentence and the game is on! When I play this game, I get this feeling like I truly am the hero and it's just so satisfying to watch as the hordes of demons before me are blasted to bits by my weapons! It makes me feel like a badass and it's so exciting to see my old demon friends all new and shiny in HD graphics right before my eyes! Its also something very special to see the remastered bosses and it really gets my nostalgia going to hear covered versions of the classic e1m1 theme.

Many people complain about it being to far from the classics and they believe it does not truly capture the feeling that you are playing a DOOM game. Although I myself felt like the game seemed to turn into another franchise I am still content with what has been presented before us. Really people, when a company is making a new game in the series, they have to change it in some ways or another. Would you really like it if all the did was change the graphics and pump out this game. The truth is, NO you would NOT. Next gen games require some sort of evolution to it or it is just bland. It is ok to keep a lot of the same things but it should noticably different. Can you imagine if they made DOOM 4 an indie game. Some might say that sounds appealing but it really wouldn't be great. Many gamers don't understand that especially for a next gen game that costs $60, the devs need to present us with something new and unique that we will be pleasantly surprised by and the only way to do it is to change this up a bit, but not TOO much.

I am still entertained with classic DOOM to today but people need to know that game developing is really hard and even harder to satisfy the needs of people like us. I strongly believe that these devs have done a more than adequate job of taking the classic concepts and plans and combining it with new ideas and concepts for us to indulge ourselves in. When I beat this game, I felt something great. Right before the scene cut to black, the game displayed the scene of the classic dooms with doomguy on a hill fighting off the surrounding demons with one demon grabbing his arm and when the music hit the right tone, the nostalgia hit me hardet than it ever had before! It brought right back to when I was only 5 years old sitting on my dads lap, pulling the trigger whenever there was a demon in front of us because I did not quite know how to use the controller by myself at the time. It all reminded my of how I grew up to become a gamer after feeling that satifactory feeling of playing through level after, defeating demons of all kinds, getting this feeling of accomplishment as I traversed throught the many settings of the several games, growing up to feel that sense of action and adventure as traveled through the many environments of The Ultimate Doom, Doom 2, and Doom 3, and of course, hearing that sweet soundtrack play on as I pushed forward looking for that exit!

The feeling I got from beating DOOM 4 was a feeling I don't experience very often and it was something wonderful! Some people might say I am biased for making a review like this since DOOM is something I hold very dear to me but all I do is try to look for all the good qualities I can. If you are as much of DOOM fan as me or someone completely new to the franchise, you will still get that same sense of satisfaction from loading those demons up with lead. Watch some footage if my review is not convincing but I assure you that this game will make you feel poweful and exhilerated. Have fun and as always, RIP AND TEAR THOSE DEMONS!


P.S. look up classic doom wallpaper if you do not know what scene I am talking about with the demons surrounding doomguy\t\t\t\t\t
