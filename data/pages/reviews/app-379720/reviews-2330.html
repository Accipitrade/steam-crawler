\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay:
The game handholds you a lot on the first half an hour or so, independently of the difficulty you pick. Speaking of difficulty, the first 3 are insultingly easy, it's like the game only starts after you go to hell, where you get to fight against multiple real threats and not just cannon fodder in small numbers. There's also bossfights (4 on them on the campaing IIRC), which felt easy as well. Jumping, moving and all the basic functions are responsive and feel good to execute, even if I dislike the modern ledge climbing thing)

The gunplay feels amazing, the weapons are good, they feel effective even on enemies with higher hp that take a lot of shots to take down. The glory kills aren't something I completely agree with but they help a lot with the rpg part (more of that in a second).

This game has RPG elements in it: You get weapons and armor upgrades, unlockables, challenges to complete, runes that empower you and that you can also upgrade. It feels tacked on and not well thought out, but it includes most of the stuff you'll need to noticeably change your playtroughs from one to the next. There's also a set of upgrades that greatly help exploration by showing more of the map, highlighting secrets, etc. The UI for the RPG system was clearly made for consoles, and it's a pain to navigate with KBM

Sound:
The OST is amazing, but they kinda failed at applying it to the game: The main songs only ever play on scripted combat areas, sometimes spoiling what's next, and glory kills not only have weak sounds, but they also tone the music down during the scripted motion, which makes them feel completely underwhelming sound wise. The ambient sounds are decent.

Graphics & Performance:
The game can look amazing, but even on the highest setting, the performance you get is unjustified compared to the looks and scale of the game. There's a good set of options, and they do change the look of the game, but none of them seem to affect the performance enough. There's no advanced physics usage, or aggressive graphic techniques that would make the game require the PC it requires to work well.

Story:
The argument is weak, the storytelling inside the game is weak, but you can expand on the lore with the data logs and other stuff you get from playing. The ending is pretty open

Level Design:
Kinda basic, but reminiscent of the original Doom: Main, easy to see path that gets you where you want, and smaller paths that take you to "secrets". The maps do look great, and are well decorated. However, secrets are very hard to spot and some times you get to the automap (the in-game lore friendly tool that reveals all the map) after a point where you can't go back and find the stuff the automap reveals.

Overall:
I'd give it a 7/10, it's a decent game, worth the discounted price, not worth the computer it requires, and by bringing doom to 2016 they also included the worst from this gen of games (parkour, handholding, cutscenes, QTEs, etc).\t\t\t\t\t

Gunplay is understandably great. Run and gun is shown at is peak with Doom. Swapping between your robust arsenal is incredibly rewarding and can make you feel unbelievably powerful.

Animations and controls are tight and response, as they should be in a fast paced shooter. Hit detection is a bit lenient, but there's really no fault in that considering the speed of not only your character, but enemies as well.

The story is there and actually pretty interesting. It's not developed all too much, but it at least gives you a reason for slaying hordes of demons. But a reason why isn't the important part of Doom. It's just firing BFGs and mowing down creatures left and right.\t\t\t\t\t
