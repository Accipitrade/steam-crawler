This is a review of both games Doom 2016 and Doom Eternal
Reason: I would put Doom Eternal on its own separate Review, but I simply don't have the money to buy myself a brand new pc along with Doom Eternal despite if it's on sale constantly. So I've instead played it on the Nintendo Switch and a couple times with a friend of mine. I can run Doom 2016 but I prefer playing on the Switch because it has better performance then my shi*ty computer.
  • Doom 2016
    Doom 2016 is the game that brought back old school shooters into the market. To me I say it's about fu*king time, because these oversaturated military shooters were getting out of hand.

    Anyway, this is an old school shooter at heart. Good classic level design, Gameplay is mostly similar to something like Painkiller or Serious Sam but mostly Painkiller, and a sort of good story. Gameplay can get really repetitive at times but it's not that bad.

    Overall, this is a great game and I would absolutely recommend it to anyone that's into the Old School shooters genre.

  • Doom Eternal
    I would say this. The game is good really good, but at times it feels very bloated with mechanics and the amount of enemies, which are all manageable but it's probably not for everyone.

    Doom Eternal is what I would say a budget version of Serious Sam but much harder. Where was Serious Sam it gives you some breathing space but in Doom Eternal it does not at all. Enemies are all up in your face in the way to counter that is to adjust to the new mechanics and utilize your weapons properly on certain enemies. This makes the gameplay more frantic and very enjoyable if you know what you're doing.

    Level design has been kind of slightly dumb down to make it more linear but also add in some platforming sections to have some cool down in between fights. Some of them are good, but most of them feel a little half-baked. Still enjoyable to some degree though.

    The term "Get Good" has been surrounding this game ever since it's release. In some degree that is sometimes true, but you can literally say that to any other game that's either hard or bullsh*t and get away with it. I would say they could of just added a customized difficulty option because most areas feel kind of bullsh*t depending on what difficulty you picked. Most enemy placements in some arenas feels like an afterthought and there's only one awful boss fight. But hey I'm sure someone out there has played over 10,000 hours of this game and said the game is very balance in every aspect, so who am I to speak.

    Overall, Doom Eternal is a good game but I don't see this game aging that well in the future. Some other game in the future is probably going to take Doom Eternal's mechanics and make them more polished and more suitable for everyone. But we'll probably know Doom Eternal as the game that started a new genre or popularized a genre of shooters. Let's just hope it doesn't go down the rabbit hole of expansions like Painkiller did.

    I would recommend this game to anyone that are into really hard shooters.
