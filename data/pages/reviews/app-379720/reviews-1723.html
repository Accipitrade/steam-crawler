\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA first person shooter. Don the armour of the Doom Marine, and rip and tear the denizens of hell apart as you push back their invasion.

The game has just enough front and center plot exposure to give context to all the action in the game, and a lot of background information to expand the world. It's interesting enough, particularly the bits about the UAC's organisational structure and politics. But the real star here is the gameplay.

Enemy projectiles are just slow enough to be dodged by moving or jumping out of the way, you have little to no options for taking cover, and you'll of course be getting swarmed normally, so you need to keep mobile to stay alive, and stay aggressive to make sure they don't stay alive. Getting low on health means that whatever you kill next will drop health pickups, so staying on the offensive in dire straits is very much the way to go too. Your tools of the trade are varied, starting with a useful but supremely underwhelming to use sidearm, and quickly expanding with more devastating devices of demon destruction that are all unique, powerful and satisfying to use. They can also be extended with modifications that give them new functionality and give you further options in combat. Your opponents are all unique and imposing forms, in both their wonderfully intimidating designs and their devious behaviour in combat. You also have unique ways to utterly brutalise them with this game's "glory kill" mechanic, which allows you to execute weakened demons, and also gain some extra supplies. This makes combat a truly intense and exciting affair full of awe-inspiring violence, typically backed by a hard rock soundtrack that's sure to keep your adrenaline flowing and keep you craving blood as you lay waste to all before you.

Exploring the levels is generally enjoyable, with them being combinations of arena segments that have you face off demons, and segments that have you advancing in the level, looking for the way forward, or hunting for additional items if you so desire. The game is beautifully designed and rendered overall, so taking in the slower moment is enjoyable too.

An amazing shooter overall and a must play for any fan of the genre, both new and old.\t\t\t\t\t
