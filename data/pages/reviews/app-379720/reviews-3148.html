\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing singleplayer campaign, beautiful graphics, killer baddies and weapons, and nostalgia to boot. This masterpiece has the makings of 2016 GOTY.

One major issue- I spent over 20 hours beating the campaign, finding every secret, collecting every Doom marine, unlocking classic levels, and collectables, only to have it ruined by a single crash that corrupted out my savegame. A cloud-sync'd savegame. There's nothing I can do to salvage the game without leaving me in Dev Mode- a gimped state that disables all achievements. Deleting the savefile, verifying game cache, even disabling cloud saves- nothing fixes it. And I've spent hours scouring Bethesda, Steam, and reddit forums for a fix, to no avail.

It's a very frustrating experience, and even several months after release there's no fix available.

Even if you play multiplayer and no longer care about single player progression, the same savegame bug may suddenly wipe out any progress and level progression.

That said, even with the corrupted save, I still recommend this. This game truly lives up to its venerable 1993 namesake. The action, the groundbreaking technology, the ultaviolence, and the over-the-top demonic references- it's all DOOM.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that the game has shaved off even more of its price, being under half of a triple-A release title (60usd/80cad), this game is absolutely worth the buy on and off sale. I'm not playing a whole lot of FPS games on PC, but this game was an absolute blast on release and still is now. Nothing complex about the game, no real cover based gameplay, very much a strafe and shoot game with no sprinting and some verticality and zero stealth (though of course putting walls between yourself and certain enemies is still absolutely necessary).

All the guns feel good, the glory killing/executions while over-the-top were still really fun to me, and playing the game on the second hardest difficult was extremely satisfying and waaay challenging for me.

Visually, I LOVE the game. It's a lot of muddy textures and dull-metal plating futuristic architecture until the dusty/sandy hell levels, but weapon fire, projectiles, and glow of enemies make for superb key visuals that make combat fairly visible and distinct, which is important for a fast paced shooter.

Overall a solid 8/10 carnage FPS game with pretty demons and chunky feeling guns. Good stuff all around, but nothing complex about the game-- which was perfect for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReturning back to Doom, man what a ride. I remember the original when I was a wee lad. So what has changed? Not much suprisingly, just a boost in graphics... problem number one. if you don't have a beast of a computer forget playing this e3 style. my computer was above the minimum and still it has potato graphics. now there are many optimizations, but you're better off getting a better video card and ram stick. problem number two, the original Doom was fast paced, with some exploration to find keycards and other things of that nature. If you were new, it was jarring yes. The new Doom seems to have a pacing issue, one minute i'm killing Hell Knights, the next i'm looking for the key only to find a collectible. It also telegraphs EVERYTHING. Every room I enter that has more than one ammo type, or an abundance of one (early game), I know there is gonna be a fight. Which leads to the next issue. there doesn't ever seem to be a large number of demons on screen at one time. ID made a beautifal game, but didn't really attack me with the hordes, like in Doom or Brutal Doom. The story is great, if you've been reading it like an old novel. this game does what I like games to do; expect you to know the story while telling it to you in another medium. The problem with this, if you miss a few codexes, you may be confused as to why the Hell Baron doesn't like taco tuesday. Multiplayer is eh... but it wasn't what I wanted when i bought the game. haven't played much of it, but it could be fun if you're in to that.
