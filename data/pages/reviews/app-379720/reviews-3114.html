\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom has a legacy to uphold, that much is clear, but beyond the shadow of the game itself, if we were to strip the entire branding of DOOM from this game, does it still hold up?

Pretty damned well.

Let's take it from the top, the combat is great, harkening to the true Doom/Quake days and their sucessors, Serious Sam and Painkiller (or the newer Wolfenstein: The New Order), fast paced, enemy-filled arenas that want nothing less than to kill you. The only thing that works against them, is your skill as a player. DOOM progresses pretty well, and the enemy you dreaded last level and thought was a miniboss, this level is in almost every encounter. Althought you can't boost your base stats too much to matter, HP/Armor wise, and while weapon upgrades are pretty powerful to the point of broken, somehow all of this combines pretty well.

The difficulty curve is skewed towards an easy late game, but whether that ends up because you're a better player or because you've unlocked the Siege mode on the Gauss Cannon is a bit of a wildcard. The jump from Hurt Me Plenty to Ultra-Violence can be significant sometimes, depending on the encounters, since from what i've seen, it deals more with player damage than enemy hitpoints.

Still, the game is fantastically designed, and no where more than anywhere does that show with the automap. I'm sure we've all played games whose maps are completely useless to get around if they're even there, but DOOM does wonders with it. For starters, secrets/collectibles in a limited quantity show up on the automap in short range, upgradeable ingame. The map being full 3D and showing height and shape often tells more to you about where you can find cool stuff than your instincts often do.

Speaking of design, a little thing like the ability to switch between pistol and chainsaw by pressing the same button shows the people play the game they make, as well as the ability to have a slow-down-time weapon-select wheel instead of chasing that 6 button on your keyboard to pull out whatever specific weapon you need.

Speaking of weapons, the player can upgrade them, as well as his suit, and using a Runes, a sort of perk system, can further enhance his abilities. These are universal across a save game slot, so you can freely rank them up in previous missions if you want to get more upgrades or you missed some secrets that grant bonuses or even early good weapons. This does lead to some farming issues, but nothing horrible.

A weird player experience pacing matter marrs the flow of the game though. The game starts with a short straight-forward tutorial/getting accoustomed map, then throws some pretty cool free-form/open maps, reticent of old DOOM. The Foundry is a great example of that. But it doesn't take long and they turn linear. That's ok, you still can backtrack to grab armor/ammo you left behind and any secrets you might have missed. But again, it doens't take long and the hell levels turn around which also start locking doors behind you and by end game you take it room by room.

I really feel the game could have used more of the opens maps, as well as less closed-in arena fights and more map-mode fights.

The story itself, while we weren't expecting much, is usable, but it has some great subversive elements that id should have followed on. At start you toss away the screen of the generic 'hello, this is a dude that will radio-help you the entire game' and various prompts and loading screen messages make it a point that you're the driving force, not them, but not long into the game you're lapdogging and following orders like a champ. I really think they could have kept up that subversive aspect a lot more, especially given how the lore is pretty interesting in itself and follows the same themes.

The graphics are pretty great, and the audio can be fantastic sometimes, especially with using low-level zombies as ambiance creators.. It ran pretty smoothly on a 2700k 4ghz OC'd I7/GTX980, but that's expected. Still, the game is scaleable and i've heard of video cards as low as 660's running the game pretty well.

All in all, i don't regret buying it, it's a solid game\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is going to cover only the single player features of Doom and not "the multiplayer that noone asked for".

First of all, this new Doom is worse than the original. Sure, it has better graphics and you can jump around.... BUT is that really an achievement for being released 22 years after the original game? So let's go over a few things that make the original Doom better.

1> The glory kill/melee system.
Every time you shoot an enemy it won't actually die. It will get in a stunned state where you need to go close to it and press a key to play an animation. This new system allows you to get more ammo and health from the killed enemy. How exactly you get ammo and health from a dead imp, you might ask. Well noone really cares... you just do... get over it. In the original Doom you couldn't get ammo from imps... which makes sense. You could get ammo from soldiers for the type of weapon that they carried. Different ammo from different enemies.
But let's get back to the glory kill system. Every time you press the key to play the kill animation the gameplay stops. You lose control of the player and while you wait anything can kill you. You cannot cancel the animation to save yourself or dodge a fireball. In the original Doom the melee system was by far better. You could do melee attacks in real time while you moved around dodging attacks at the same time. Never was the power taken from the player... and if you died it would have been your own fault and not an RNG fireball landing on you while you couldn't move. Also in the original Doom you weren't forced to melee enemies if you didn't want to. You had the freedom to do it if you wanted, in order to save ammo, so it wasn't forced on you in every encounter. But... but you don't have to do melee kills, you might say... and that leads us to the next point below.

2> Powerless weapons.
In combination with the glory kill system the weapons feel "nerfed" compared to the original Doom in order to force you to go closer to the enemies. If you try to kill an imp 10m away from you with the pistol then pray that you are lucky enough to kill it. Without even changing your aim the pistol will randomly hit around the circle of its target. Your enemy will be hit multiple times but the RNG of the aim will be so bad that it will stay stunned. And if it goes back from the stunned state to it's normal state again then you will have to stun it again.... and again... and again... an infinite amount of times. Well your pistol has an infinite amount of bullets... but even if you could use all of them (which is impossible) the enemy would never be killed. Just go close to it and punch it already!!! The same nerfed feeling goes on for every weapon of the game compared to the original Doom's weapons. You will have to be in melee range if you want to do enough damage with a shotgun... A marine than can't aim and shoot an imp 10m away is a disgrace to the original Doomguy.

3> The chainsaw.
This is as bad as the new Doom can get. A chainsaw that doesn't work in real time. You just go close to an enemy and kill it instantly with an animation that just like the glory kills you cannot skip. It doesn't matter how big or tough your enemy is, it will be killed in the same amount of time with a chainsaw animation. Chainsaw kills will give you even more ammo than the glory kills (yes I know it doesn't make any sense). When even a game from the 90s can have a better chainsaw that works in real time and needs more time to kill powerful enemies then something is wrong.

4> Gameplay mechanics.
Every few rooms you will be locked in a room and you will be forced to kill everyone to continue. This "lockdown engaged - lockdown disengaged" mechanic will have enemies slowly spawn in order to increase gameplay time and you will be forced to run around chasing them. There are times where you have to walk through empty corridors in order to get into the next lockdown area. At least in the original Doom you had all the enemies in front of you to kill and they didn't slowly appear to increase the gameplay time. So even if you were locked in a room full of enemies you could kill them all with one BFG shot or find a switch to open the door and escape. Now you are forced to parkour around the room while chasing them waiting for the next ones to appear...

5> The story.
Yes... I know there is not really a story behind this game but there are parts of monologues that are forced on you. You cannot skip or escape them. You will have to wait until the annoying person that talks finishes what he has to say in order to open the door and continue playing. At least in the original Doom the only story parts were in the end of each chapter and you weren't forced to read them if you didn't want to.

6> The save system.
In the original Doom you had save slots that you could use and rewrite any time throughout the game. Now you don't. The game automatically decides when to save your progress. You have no option whether to save or not. If you want to load a previous save then too bad for you because you can't. This new autosave feature is so bad that it's going to make you walk 30sec - 1min doing the same parkour stuff to get back to the area where you need to go. If you explored a bit and found some secrets or picked up some armor or health along the way then you will have to also do that again every time you die.

7> It's Bethesda/id #Bugs.
Have you ever had an imp hit you through a closed door in the original Doom? Well you can do it now! Not to mention that your save files can get corrupted for no reason and the only thing you can do is to delete them and start over from the beginning of the game. Have you ever had a corrupted save file in the original Doom? If it wasn't acceptable then why is it acceptable now? Why should you need to backup your save files in 2016 when you didn't need to do it over 20 years ago? If you know well the publisher then those bugs will never be fixed because noone cares.... #Skyrim #Fallout.


I do not recommend this game to anyone who likes FPS games. Do not support bad reboots. Play again the original Doom and you will see how well designed every aspect of the game really was.

Final Rating 0/Infinity

Edit: If you really want to see the difference between Doom 1 and Doom 4 then Bethesda/id has made it easy for you. Play some of the classic maps using the weapons and gameplay of this new game. See for yourself how much nerfed the new weapons are and how bad the gameplay is.\t\t\t\t\t
