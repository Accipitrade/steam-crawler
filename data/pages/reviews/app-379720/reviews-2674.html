\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played the original Doom back in '93 or '94 - installed from floppies and all. It was a blast. I have played with every version since, but not one lived up to the feeling. Unfortunately, this "reboot" is no exception. It tries very hard, and everything is perfect but simply it is not as much of a breath of fresh air as the original was. It's a bit like the "new" VW Beetle and Mini - they try to ride a nostalgia train and although they are very good, simply put, they are only remakes. Same here. I enjoyed the game, good graphics, controls, weapons, gameplay... just not that much different from any other good FPS today. Worth a shot, good game, but not special - with different characters and title it would not stand out at all.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell. I recommend this game, because it is probably the only decent sorta-classic shooter made in the last 10 years or so.

However, the game falls short compared to the original Doom in couple of areas.

I finished the game on ultra-violence difficulty, and there were couple of things that stood out:

Negatives:
  • Original Doom had an aspect where at later levels you were running out of ammo all the time and as a result you had to think strategically. In levels such as Slough of Despair or Mount Erebus killing everything on the map was actually quite tough (on higher difficulty), because you could easily run out of ammo, and had to make sure that every shot deals maximum damage. it is also possible to end up without an ammo at all at the beginning of a level face to face with a demon only armed with fists. This aspect is now gone. Enemies are pinatas loaded with health and ammo. When you start running out of ammo, glory-kill someone and you'll be showered with ammunition and health.
  • This leads to next problem: the thing is obviously balanced around glory kills. You're expected to use them to replenish ammunition/health, and missing a glory kill might make a demon recover.
  • Compared to the first game, the marine is incredibly fragile, so, you're playing a tug of war with health bar. Given the chaos that is happening in the levels, you have to constantly regenerate health with glory kills, otherwise you die.
  • Glory kills (aka "Fatalities") break combat fluidity. While you're running landing a glory kill throws you into a cinematic mini-sequence that pauses your combat for a second or maybe even two. It also mutes the music somewhat. It is extremely annoying, and those kills are completley unsatisfying, because it is a cheating "I win sooner and get free stuff" button. You'll have to deal with this nonsense to heal, whether you like it or not. The whole system feels like cheating, to be honest.
  • The first shotgun, compared to the original game is ruined and extremely underpowered. To one-hit-kill anything with it, you need to stick it into enemy's a... I mean face and pull the trigger, otherwise you might as well sneeze at them. The second shotgun is more similar to the original one, but the range is still greatly reduced. I think the range is between 10% and 25% of the original. It is extremely unsatisfying.
  • Chainsaw is ruined too. It is now one-hit-kill weapon that requires fuel. So you can't run around in circles and saw cacodemons to death anymore.
  • You can't punch demons in the face anymore. The only way to switch to fists is to get berserk powerup, otherwise you get infinite ammo pistol. In Doom. Infinite ammo. Sigh.
  • Maps have greater degree of verticality, which is good, but vertical movement is not fluid enough. Ledge grab animation significantly slows you down a lot, even with upgrade. in couple of places it is possible to get stuck.
  • I actually managed to find a map with an invisible wall. There were two places that looked like you could've jumped between them, but if you tried to that, you'd hit an invisible wall mid-flight, and fall into abyss and die.
  • The "Satanic" and hellish atmosphere from the original game is gone. It is partly because of music and level design. Instead there are piles of meat and gore everywhere. Well, gore isn't scary, and original game had several levels with heavily oppressive atmosphere. Nothing like that in the new game.
  • You think you won't be listening to someone giving you orders? Think again. While the game starts with a sequence where DoomGuy interrupts communication with someone, in later level you'll have someone communicating with you telling you where to go and what to do. They tried to make fun of it, but the element is still there, and it is still annoying.
  • The "UAC base" levels are mostly bland. It is the standard "metal everywhere" installation with piles of gore all over the place. Hellish levels are ... okay, but there aren't a lot of them. There were games that created better hellish atmosphere than this one.
  • You're supposed to collect powerups that increase your stats. In Doom. Without them you have 100 health max and 50 armor max, and severely limited amount of ammo you can carry - for example 20 shells total for a shotgun. I think this kind of nonsense doesn't belong to the series.
  • Once you've collected the powerups and some runes, however, ammo stops being a problem, because of infinite number of demon pinatas. I get through half of the game with a better shotgun and only run out of ammunition twice.
  • Maps feel small compared to original. Original levels allowed much more fluid movement over larger distances, here even the larges rooms feel cramped, and you can barely move somewhere without hitting a corner.
  • Speaking of which, I have impression that on higher difficulty they decided to up the damage you receive, instead of throwing more enemies at you (like original did). This is cheap.
  • Remember the moments in the original game where you walked somewhere, picked an item, all the walls lowered and enemies walked out of them? Well, no such moments here anymore. Almost all enemies spawn into the level, which gets boring after a while.
  • They also tried to make epic explanation for the DoomGuy... and it mostly sounds silly.
  • Also, I'm starting to seriously hate the new Bethesda/ID engine. This thing tries to stream textures all the time, which can lead to microscopic pauses at the wrong moments.

Positives:
  • Movement speed is okay.
  • Enemy movement speed is okay. Chasing imps around with a boomstick eventually gets old, but it is better that sitting in a cover while your health regenerates.
  • Visuals and music are okay. Music could've been much better, and lacks punch however.

Overall:
It is a decent sorta old-school shooter. Sorta and it is only decent, not great. Unfortunately, some of the design decisions in the game made it less fun than the original was. Tactical/strategic ammo hoarding is gone, punching demons in the face by yourself is gone, chainsaw and shotgun are now worse, and they thought the original was cool because of gore, so they threw in annoying "finisher" animations so the player woudl feel good about themselves. Also there's a "leveling" thing that increases your stats, which doesn't really belong. Basically, the game almost looks like whoever created it never played the original beyond first level on a higher difficulty.

Still... it is a decent "sorta" old-school shooter, much better than most of the modern stuff. That makes it worth playing.\t\t\t\t\t

After played this game more than 64h and acquired 26 achievements I can finally say one thing, this is among the best first person shooter game in this decade. Great gameplay and no complicated storyline. Kind of reminds me Serious Sam and Dead Space franchise.

PRO:
- Great campaign both in gameplay and story.
- Bloody gore.
- Big guns.
- RIP and TEAR demons.
- Energy balls.
- Secret exploration.
- Cute collectibles.
- Double Jump.
- Arcade mode and Snapmap.
- Silent badass DOOM guy.

CONS:
- Multiplayer are full of pro especially in Deadmatch or Team Deadmatch, so you might find it hard to win at the first time. But once you get the grip on it, victory is all yours.
- Sometimes find a match in multiplayer server is just like finding specific people in cowboy town, It's so quiet that you might think that multiplayer is dead.

So, you should try it. It really worth every penny that I spend during Steam Summer Sale. 9/10 would glory kill demons again.\t\t\t\t\t
