\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI grew up playing my brother's copy of Doom I. Never got to touch Final Doom or Doom II and its respective expansion as a child. My brother moved out, I grew up a bit, and as a teenager played Doom 3. Now I'm an adult, and the new Doom game has been released...and it's better than I thought it could be.

As far as FPS goes, this genre generally doesn't appeal to me in most cases. I'm more of an RPG/FPA/TPA gamer. However, DOOM helps redefine a dying genre. It removes the generic coridor shooting that has become so dominant and replaces it with fast paced, lethal, and explosive combat in arena styled areas (calling back much to the original style of DOOM) with a fluid movement and interaction system.

Graphically this game is fantastic, even at med/high this game is gorgeous at 4k and runs at a clean 30FPS on a GTX970 4GB (and it feels like 60fps).

Story/Lore wise - for us lore hounds you won't be dissapointed, there is plenty to read and find. Especially if you're a fan of the old Secret Area system that was present in the original DOOM games (though I DO miss having the percentage redouts at the end of each mission).

Campaign length - Appropriate for modern day games (even with a tacked on Multiplayer), though I'm hoping for expansions fitting the style of original DOOM. That is to say, I have my fingers crossed for Episode 2: Inferno!

tl;dr - 9/10 - Worth every penny.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis 2016 DOOM is a great game. Game play is amazing, when you can play.

I had video trouble where, if I died the game would slow down to a crawl and only a reboot would fix it. That appears to be fixed? Now it loads on the wrong monitor and the monitor settings don't work. They revert to default monitor 2. The only way to play is to turn off the other monitors. One of the hardest boss fights I had attempted 6 times, taking all evening, and the last monster spawned inside a wall so I could not kill it. So after many hours of game play, since you can't save and I haven't reached a checkpoint, I have made no progress of any kind. For a game that is supposed to be non-stop action this is insanely bad design. I've also been stuck for hours looking for keys on empty levels, finally having to go to the web for answers.

The game is gorgeous but cartoony compared to DOOM 3 which was terrifying at points. I think the conversion to an RPG shooter has taken certain elements away from the game but the constant upgrades are fun.

The gameplay is amazing. It's designed to be non-stop and, until it glitches back to the desktop, stops working or you can't find a key, it's really fast and fun. You don't have to reload. You have movement controls hinting at Tomb Raider allowing you finally to get over 1 foot obstacles. The double jump makes it feel like Megaman X. It's all good stuff. The game has just been released with too many bugs. Wait for the sale. I don't feel like I got 80$ worth out of the game and it's simply because of the bugs.

I'm giving it a positive review because it's Bethesda and they do this EVERY.... SINGLE.... TIME. All their games come out glitchy as all heck and then they fix them. I should have waited and in a year it'll be a solid game, finally complete with all the extra stuff that should be part of the basic game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlmost everything about the campaign is amazing! Everything from the soundtrack to the gameplay just works to create the best game I've played this year. Easily #1 in my books!
Multiplayer is admittedly fun, but is definitely not the main focus of this game, so if you're buying this for the multiplayer more than the campaign, you may be disappointed to find it a bit lacking in comparison to other modern multiplayer focused games (i.e. Call of Duty, Battlefield, CS:GO, etc), but it's definitely fun as an addition to the campaign.
SnapMap is a unique feature of this game, and gives it a sense of mod-ability, but I feel it shouldn't be forced as the only option for modding the game. It's fun to create your own maps and play others', but it's definitely lacking if it's the only option for modders.

Overall, this game is a must buy for the campaign alone. Multiplayer and SnapMap are fun additions that definitely help to extend the life of the game, but would need some work for the community to keep server population high for years to come.\t\t\t\t\t
