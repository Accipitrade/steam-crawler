\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's difficult to not enjoy this game.

The good:
-Excellent gunplay. The way each gun feels and sounds to shoot, the amount of ammo you have, the rate at which you acquire new guns and modifications for existing ones is the perfect balance to prevent you getting bored.
-Excellent level design. Some really fun to traverse combat arenas littered with pick-ups and secrets as well as large open levels with multiple paths to your objectives.
-Excellent soundtrack and sound design. I even went and bought the soundtrack seperately when it was released.
-Very impressive graphically. Had some issues running it on an older PC that just scraped the minimum specs but on a newer machine with everything cranked up it's an amazing game to behold.
-Cool enemy design. I mean that both in terms of aesthetically and mechanically. Each demon, even the regular posessed humans look terrifyingly awesome. Add to that solid AI that forces you to adapt your playstyle and weapon choices to whatever combination of enemies you're fighting, and it makes for a fun time.
-The multiplayer is solid. As with seemingly most people it wasn't exactly what I wanted (basically a modern Quake Live but shinier and more extreme) it is still an enjoyable game to play online on the whole with some decent game modes and cool levels.

The bad:
-Unfortunately the multipler still isnt great. The absence of a deathmatch mode for the longest time was a big negative. The weapon balancing, at least the last time I played it, was also total garbage. Depending on which level you're playing you can almost guarantee what weapon eberybody will be using. For example playing this game just after launch and in the beta, the majority of people on each team would be running round with a rocket launcher and super shotty because they were the most effective combination on most maps.
-Enemy variety. There are a realitvely high number of different enemies in the game. Unfortunately several of them are essentially re-skins of existing ones with changes to their abilities so slight it makes little to no difference as you wade through them. For the final few levels after you've seen all the regular demon types that aspect began to get a bit monotonous. "Gee whizz another 2 floating eye dudes and a couple revenants, just like that fight 10 minutes ago."
-Too many secrets/collectibles. From what I can recall there are preator tokens, argent cores, doomguy toys, codex files, audio logs, classic doom levels and just general "secrets". I enjoyed exploring the levels in this game but that's just too much. And while it's good that you're rewarded for finding them, what's not good is accidentally finishing a level only to find that you missed half the stuff and the only way to get them is to replay the whole thing.
-The biggest blue-balls ending to a game since Halo 2. No spoilers but if like me you think cliffhangers are cheap and lazy then you will hate the ending as much as I did.

Overall the gameplay carries it though to be one of the best single player FPS Ive played in years.\t\t\t\t\t
