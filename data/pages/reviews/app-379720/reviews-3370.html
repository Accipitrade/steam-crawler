\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM feels like a game for fans, from fans. It's fast paced, chaotic, bloody, and loud. Everything a DOOM game needs to be. The environments are stunning, colorful, and every bit as sinister. This is far from DOOM 3, it's better in every way and a great new beginning to the series. This time around a story is actually intact, and interesting. Like DOOM original, this game is about staying on the move, exploring, and destroying stuff with epic weapons. And it delivers every bit of it without mercy.

This is not Halo, this is sure as hell not Call of Duty, you explore beautiful and grotesque environments in search of power-ups while surviving onslaughts of enemies and monster closets to make your inner psycho squee with joy at all the bloodshed. DOOM runs a very simple gaming formula, but does so with grace: Explore > Power up > Monster closet > Boss. It begins easy but later on, as the monsters evolve, so too does the challenge. Evolving from a simple shooter to a fight for survival at each encounter. Enclosed rooms become bloodbaths and open paths become drive-by arenas.

The graphics this time around put tears to the eyes. Guns are sleek and shiny, environments range from beautiful to hellish, and demons look better and more sinister than ever before. All of this complimented by the high levels of gore to make even the likes of George Romero squirm. Not to mention you got yourself a sick soundtrack to rip apart demons to.

Weapon variety comes in the form of classics we know and love, with a little added something. Weapons can be buffed or upgraded, much like your armor, letting you play DOOM differently and to your unique style. Or you can forgo everything and just play this classic with no power-ups. Nothing is forced on the player, not even the routes you take. It's about freedom to express the insides of a demon on the wall any way you like. And it's damn fun to do it.

Multiplayer is not your usual Halo or COD. This is a shooter with guts. It's fast, chaotic, and bloodboiling. Say goodbye to quick one-shot kills and hello to group cooperation and outmaneuvering. If you can't move, you die. Best start developing those hand-eye coordinations because you'll need it as you pump lead or plasma into the enemy combatant trying to make your head into a pinata. The added toughness of each player, along with armor and health boosts, just adds to the fun of dogfighting your way through each kill. It becomes a race to see who can kill who first before a partner or new enemy jumps over to provide the handicap. And it works. DOOM never stops. You can't camp, you can't cheese. All you got here is skill, forget the patience. You're a hungry monster out to feed. As you play you progress levels and unlock swag armor to stylize, colors, and new weapons/gear. Win or lose, you are always rewarded handsomely for something enjoying yourself. So forget the pressure of getting them kills, enjoy yourself.... and kill a whole lot of people.

All in all, DOOM is the remake Duke Nukem wanted to be. Hell, it's the remake any game wants to be. It's beautiful, fast, violent, engaging, challenging, and, above all else, fun. I have yet to run into lag, yet to see my frame rate drop, and I'm running this monster on all Ultra settings. The only stops here are when I rip the head out a demon to shove down his throat, or when a demon beats me to death with my own legs.

DOOM fans NEED this game, shooter fans WANT this game, WHY HAVEN'T YOU ALREADY BOUGHT THIS GAME?!?!?!?\t\t\t\t\t

Playing the game was a family affair, and every once in a while my mom would grab me and my dad and get us to go play because she "wanted to shoot something". That's what DOOM means to me: there's a whole load of demons out there, and they've killed everyone, you go kill them back.

And this new DOOM gives me that feeling again, there's no creeping around with a flashlight, waiting for something to jump out at you; you ARE the something that jumps out at them. There's a whole lot of demons out there and you are going to kill every last one of them in the most badass possible manner.

Playing this game makes me feel like an unstoppable killing machine, but don't let that fool you, it's challenging too. I'd say I'm average at FPSes and playing on the Hurt Me Plenty, I've had a few scares. I'm sure the harder difficulties make it even tougher.

In conclusion, I used the pistol to make 2 zombies heads explode with a single shot, then I found a berserker pack and ripped an imps head in half vertically. Highly remmended.\t\t\t\t\t
