It polishes and perfects the gameplay formula so well, I'd say this game is the best example of it so far. Other games like Hard Reset and Shadow Warrior from Flying Wild Hog do this as well and they are incredible games on their own right, but the new Doom makes improvements even above the heights those games take it in terms of the quality of gameplay. There are types of enemy patterns and movements I've never seen in an FPS before and they require the kind of second-to-second planning and adaption I've never had to do before. It really makes the genre fresh and new again.

Even though I'm only a few hours into the game so far, I'd tentatively say this is the best FPS game I've ever played. I'll be updating my review once I beat the game to see if it stays that way. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely love this game. It feels just like the classic, but with so much more to do. The challenges are actually fun and I love the gun progression aspect. Very cool. I'm 9 missions in, skipped most of the secrets that I will be going back for, and I've been excited about subsequent playthroughs on higher difficulty settings for some time now. The logs and records you find laying around are fun to read as well. I found myself chuckling at quite a few. This is not horror survival or an army men fps, This game is all about murdering demons in brutal fasion and at a very fast pace. This is Doom.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't believe it, every damn reviewer in the world must be bought by id Software!
I played the game for like 1 hour before I refunded it in Steam.
The killmoves aren't even the most annoying thing as I immagined they would be after watching gameplay videos. The gamedesign is just bad!

Worst thing about it is the enemy placement. You enter an arena with some monsters, begin killing them and suddenly dozens of dozens of them spawn. They spawn in exact the same location as their predecessors. Multiple times! Imagine an arena with let's say 12 spawn points. That's it. It is annoying because in original Doom they didn't just beam in, but came from a suddenly opened Door or something. And you heard them! You heard them scream behind your back and you got terrified. Even worse so when the lights shut down in a claustophobic corridor or labyrith. In this Doom you hear nothing. Here you are fighting and boom, there you are beeing attacked from behind. They don't scream, you don't notice their appearence till they attack your back. It is just dumb and annoys me. It happens all the time. Sounddesign is really bad. That also is true for the weapons sound in my opinion but I guess thats personal taste for some degree.

Next thing is Graphics. Really nice overall but it isn't clear enough. I have difficulties overviewing the enemies positions. There are lots of effects and the monsters move really quick. Not Doom like at all. The fact that the monsters spawn in your back all the time worsens that: You can't get into a fight with a strategy or plan. Most of the monsters I encountered behaved more like Zombies than Doom Monsters. They dind't move until you got close, then they jumped right at you. But that can change in campain progress.

Here is my opinion about the killmoves. First: You have to do them. They are an essential part of the gameplay and gamedesign. They give you health and ammunition and not playing them gives you an enormous disadvantage. They're essentially the counterpart of heath regeneration in Call of Duty. You are low on heath and your best option is to go right back into the battle and punch a monster in the face. Yes they take control for 1-2 seconds. But they keep you in the fight, what some people might like and consider fluid gameplay. I don't like it. In original Doom you were forced to run away and get to the next medikit. You hopefully remembered it's position. That adds tactics and tention. Also true with ammunition. That transits to the next point:

In original Doom you weren't always in battle. In this one you go from one fighting arena to another. Once you are in arena you've got to fight! It only opens it's doors when you killed all enemies. in original Doom you could just avoid them with a secret path or run through them sometimes. Original Doom didn't have these arenas btw. Arenas with lot's of jumping are more Unreal Tournament like. Doom had big levels with free movement in them. You had to find your own path. In result there was a lot of backtracking, but it never was a problem because the movement speed was so high and the leveldesign so good: When you had to go back, you almost automatically took a other path then before. I hate these arenas and jumping in a Doom game.

I really could not enjoy this game and I am shocked by the positive reviews.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game where you are given a gun, pointed into a direction and have the idea to kill everything in your path, and I don't see a problem with that. Doom brought you back to old shool FPS shooters that don't have hand holding, cover, or regening health and armor. Always forward, never backwards. With an enemy that shows no remorse and mercy, and neither do you...which is fantastic. So much brutality and gore. Hell I didn't even get all that mad when I died.

The story was good. Plenty of action and very breif moments where you have to wait while to plot moves forward. I got a little taste of the lore and hope it's fleshed out later.

To be honest, if you bought doom for the multiplayer you might be disappointed, Feels too much like Cod/Halo got drunk and had a evil kid.

Overall its a great game. One I hope on putting a lot of hours into. Now i'd only be perfect if it gets blamed for some mass shooting.

Side note: If you find my skull in Ultra Nightmare....don't ask.\t\t\t\t\t
