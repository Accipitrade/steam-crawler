\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe DOOM series is one of the most important in video game history. And any game that bears its title has a hell of a legacy to live up to, no pun intended. By now, you've probably heard from everyone that this game is amazing and that you should play it. But it's the reasons why the newest DOOM is so great that make it truly special. This game has no identity crisis of any sort, which is something I was concerned about in the leadup to its release. DOOM knows that it is arcadey, no holds barred, relentlessly fast shooter that respects your intelligence in both story and gameplay. In terms of story, the game doesn't pretend to guide you along any complex narrative. It knows why you're here, to kill demons. And the levels are designed to make that as fun and interesting as possible without any cutscenes to interrupt your killing spree. In a similar vein to Dark Souls or early Resident Evil games, you can flesh out the backstory by finding codex entries scattered throughout the world, and it tells a genuinely intriguing plot. But you can completely ignore it and have an absolute blast. The shooting is tight and responsive, the levels are varied, well laid out and chocked full of secrets, including finding Classic DOOM levels in a similar vein as Wolfenstein: The New Order, which is also a spectular id revival. New types of enemies and weapons are presented at a near perfect pace, but if you're explorative enough you can find powerful weapons like the Chaingun and the Super Shotgun before you're "supposed to", making exploring the vast levels rewarding and fun. And no, the multiplayer isn't the greatest in the world, but it's still a fun enough time to be worth at least checking out. And people have created some pretty impressive things with SnapMap. No, it isn't as good as being given the game's source code, but its simplicity is done to make it easier for console players to use it (editing code with a controller would suck). I do hope that one day id does release the code for its PC audience, or maybe include a more advanced creator in a sequel. But with that aside, this game is never boring. I've beaten it 6 times across two platforms and I will continue to play this game until the day I die. So if you want to see what the hype is all about, pick up this game then Rip & Tear.\t\t\t\t\t
