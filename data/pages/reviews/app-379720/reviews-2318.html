\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll be honest, I thought this would be a cynical cash in on a classic title, and things like the killmoves didn't help that perception.

But in playing it, I was very pleasantly surprised. The responsive movement and flow are pure old school, and maybe even a touch more Unreal than classic id - but that suits me fine. The controls are simple, so there's no bunnyhopping to learn, and this is great since it means the joy of kinetic fragging is in the reach of more players. It takes a little while to give you the kinds of arenas where this works best, and possibly even peters out a bit midway through, but it's such a romp up to that point it barely matters.

The music is excellent, and the simple layering and build-and-release matches the gameplay well. Weapons feel solid in true id fashion and the upgrades are an interesting and sometimes fun distraction, but, like the story, not strictly necessary.
The enemies offer some variation in required tactics, but generally the aim is to keep moving and deal with the more immediate threats to your survival - the higher difficulties really accentuate this aspect.

It also runs really well thanks to Vulkan support.
Not tried online, or snapmaps.\t\t\t\t\t
