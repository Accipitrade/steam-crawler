\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most overrated games in recent years that I can only think of as a clash of generations.

The game basically has you going from one room to the next and getting swarmed by demons that you then proceed to slaughter to a deep bass beat, and repeat. Don't get me wrong though, it can certainly be mindlessly entertaining at times but there's virtually no depth with lackluster visuals outside of the demons themselves. If you are expecting some form of atmosphere while steadily descending into hell you should look elsewhere, ultimately I could only see Doom 2016 appeal to the younger crowd who doesn't have the patience to look for anything deeper.

I never expected Doom to be an RPG or even have much of a story so I am not being overly nitpicky here, in fact I think I tend to be quite generous in my assessments, Doom 2016 is just a bad Doom game.

On a bright side the game seems fairly well optimized and runs smoothly, however, the menu's are awkward and stinks of consoles. The online is similarly lackluster with a terribly stretched thin character customization where you unlock one boot at a time, one color at a time, don't expect to create anything presentable for a long time unless you like bright red characters with mismatching armor pieces. The MP also fails to explain what it's different modes do, why are my team mates turning into demons in team deathmatch? I eventually figured out it's just some kind of powerup but the game should have made this clear before I even began.

In the end I got 15 hours of entertainment out of the game and that included finishing the campaign at a moderate pace grabbing most collectibles along the way and testing out the MP for an hour or two. With all this in mind I'd not recommend this game to anyone unless it looks really interesting to you and you can grab it for 10-15â‚¬ on a big sale.\t\t\t\t\t

GAMEPLAY:

This is one of the few "push forward combat" FPS games available on the market that also have a stellar single player campaign. When you think of modern FPS games and look at Call of Duty, Battlefield, Bioshock, Dishonored, etc etc. A huge emphasis is put on taking cover, carefully measuring your actions before moving on, and sneaking whenever possible. DOOM throws this right out the window and actively encourages you to move at every moment and to actually close into enemies as fast as possible without worrying about cover. What makes this system work is that it encourages you with a shift in perspective; it is conveyed to you all the time through both visuals and gameplay, that you, the player character, is the actual monster in the room. A lot of the design in this game is made purposely to make you feel overpowered and motivated enough to bulldoze everything moving. Lightning is plentiful and enemy design promotes aggression rather than fear, not to mention attack animations are great, you have auto-reloading tech, and most of the ammo/health drops actually come from brutally finishing off enemies in melee. Even the soundtrack is exploited to this end, because the way you move throughout levels and how often you choose to punish enemies dictates the pace of the music, which is all dynamic and adaptive to your actions - leave a fight to scoop some items and you'll hear a bassline, go back into the fray and start smashing things and you'll get a very catchy and aggressive guitar riff chorus, and so on. If anything, this game is a good addition to any collection due to intentionally diverging from the contemporary industry standards with good results.

STORY:

Story in this game works as a good backdrop to engage you into the aforementioned push forward combat. Most of the exposition is optional through reading data logs. The game acknowledges and relishes in the absurdity of how powerful your player character is, with your immense speed, strength, etc. being explained through half humorous remarks in the data logs that still manage to not sound corny. The whole demonic stuff is also managed very well, with the game knowing it can't take itself too seriously but also letting you know that proper world building has been done. It is much less satirical than the Wolfenstein series in a good way and makes you care for the things happening around you without taking your focus out of the action. The most specific thing to be said without spoilers is: you are the living manifestation of vengeance and has spent centuries in Hell absolutely devastating the whole demon population. They have grown scared that you would actually exterminate their kind and so trapped you into a stone encasing full of sigils. A Mars based corporation rescues you from that dimensional plane and wakes you up, and that's when the game starts. Cue metal music.

SOUND:

Speaking of music, the soundtrack in this game is composed by Mick Gordon, who is a seasoned videogame composer you can look up on Youtube. It is a healthy mix of dubstep and metal that during gameplay can probably be enjoyed by people who don't like either, because it is all dynamic and adjusts itself to what is being done at the time. Let this sink in that the developers thought the music was so good and integral to the gameplay that they use it as a reward for being more active in combat. Don't play this on mute.

GRAPHICS:

This game has standard graphics for its year of release (2016). It is nicely optimized (id Software are excellent programmers regardless of their game designing skills) and anyone reading this past 2018 can probably play it without lag with reasonably high settings. As said before, the visuals are all supposed to play a part in conveying the feel that its your enemies that are the ones locked inside the room with you, and they do that very well.

SUMMARY:

DOOM is a great game to have in your list if only so you actually have a good FPS game that is unique in relation to the modern industry standards. It is great to play in short spurs from time to time and it gives a really good feeling of being overpowered without being too easy. It is worth the full price specially since the second installment is coming soon and said full price is no longer very high, but if you want to wait for a sale it is worth it even more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like old school shooters (which means relying on movement instead of cover and having great twitch reflexes as opposed to buying lootboxes full of nice gear)?

If so then get this game.

That's it. It's a fantastic, ultra violent action game. Challenging, but fair (ish). Addictive,

Goods
-Controls are spot-on. The best shooting controls I've felt in years. If you miss with your shot that is on you, if your movement and strafing isn't up to par that's on you.
-More verticality. At first I didn't like the double jump and climbing mechanic as they didn't feel like pure Doom to me, but they really open up the arenas.
-Graphics are great, voice work is sparse, but very well done, enemies animate fantastically, the music is Metal for people who don't normally like Metal.
-Tense and challenging, you will rarely feel more badass than when you play as the Doom Slayer.

Bads
-Some people won't like the 'arena' format for the big shootouts, but I thought it works well.
-This is NOT a story and cinema driven Call of Duty style game. Only a con if you are expecting that rather than constant, brutal shooting.
-I have no idea. Some of the challenges are kind of frusrating, but they only take like 30 seconds so who cares?

Get this game. Throw money at Id to continue making such great shooters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt has a lot of good points, but overall I was not impressed. I was expecting a fun, fast action game like the original Doom, Quake, Quake II. In the end it seem like they went with the melee combat a bit too hard, as it seems that is way too often the best way to fight. The levels are a bit tedious, and it has a very different feel in control from Quake I, Quake II, Quake IV, orginal Doom. It just is not a good run and gun shooter the way those games were, nor does it have the depth of story and level design that more story focused shooters had. Were this not a Doom game, maybe I'd like it a lot more, but it seems they took the doom name and themes and made game in the same genre but with very different gameplay, focus, and feel. I'll stick to Quake I & II and orignal Doom and the many well polished 3rd party engine available for them.\t\t\t\t\t
