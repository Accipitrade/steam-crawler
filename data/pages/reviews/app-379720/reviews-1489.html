\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, quick thoughts on this game with Eternal now out. I never played OG Doom. I want to. Even, like, the most vanilla way possible, bar reconfiguring the controls to be more intuitive. But I just never got around to it. That said, this game does seem to carry a lot of good things people cite as being why the original was good, albeit in a feel-wise more than 1:1 sense.
The player character is fast- I've seen comparisons that imply he's slower but I question these after seeing how fast he moves from a third person camera. Maybe it's because the camera's higher off the ground that it feels slow... In either case, you at least make up for it with very nice mobility traits, including a nice ledge-grab, double jumps, and airstrafing through a rune upgrade. Weapons are satisfying to use, and hitting things is generally good, which I think is important- poor feedback is a huge turnoff for me.
Elephant in the room is probably the glory kill system, which... Well, to sum up why I like it so much, it feels less like a QTE and more like a move, like, it's one of your weapons and firing it just has a flashy animation. Using it doesn't feel like handing over controls so much as the end result of a control input playing out. I think this feeling is facilitated not just by them being quick, but by the animations typically being a single, cohesive motion or one-two punch- fast or not, I think the feeling would be lost if there were glory kills where the camera moved a lot or he hit them many times. Because it doesn't take away from control for long, and because the actions done out of your control aren't overly complex, it still feels like you did it. Other than vomiting up health pickups, certain upgrades afford it additional tactical use, like lunging into enemies from a moderate distance...
...Which of course leads into the RPG elements- while I sort of lament that the health, armor, and ammo upgrades end with you maxing out everything instead of allowing minmaxing (though, as a rule, it's a good idea to prioritize the one you fill up the most while making your way up- you'd rather a health bar too big to fill than leave those precious pickups to waste, right?), and I think allowing players to choose attachments for their weapons on the fly eliminates an element of decision-making, the more choice-based abilities and upgrades through the Rune system are great, and maxing everything out is good fun. There's also a bit of a story- Doomguy's disinterest in it when it tries to sit you down for it is clever, but there is world data to be found, and a lot shown through the environment.
Of course, the game is short- the multiplayer, if it's even still online, is something I haven't touched, and the snapmap community stuff allegedly has some nice levels but the relatively short campaign might not have warranted the pre-markdown $60 if you're big on playtime and don't want to loop back through/spend extra time looking for collectibles, but it's $20 now. Runs well- my Y510P actually ran the game in somewhat smooth slow motion. Had I not played it again after upgrading, I'd probably agree with the speed complaints.
The final thing I'll address is difficulty: I played on like medium and I think I only died to a non-environmental cause like twice on my first (slow-motion) run through. I kind of stayed there on my later runs but took a screwier approach and died a lot because I just enjoy spamming the standard melee on Revenants. Kind of wish that was more effective... But, yeah, medium is quite easy and, the hardest difficulty being locked behind your first playthrough (I forget if that's the one where it's just "same as the previous one but also deletes save on death" or if it's actually the next step up), it might disappoint people expecting relentless challenge.

In any case, the sheer markdown the game is being sold at now may make it worth the price even if Eternal proves to be a huge upgrade in every aspect.\t\t\t\t\t
