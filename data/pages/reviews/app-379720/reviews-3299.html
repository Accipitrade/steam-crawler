\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's actually an absurd amout I could write for this game – get a doctorate maybe – but it'd be a little much to squeeze in here, so I'll try to keep this only rather lenghty.

In a short summary: RECOMMENDED. Good game. Whether it is a $60 game is a bit more of a touchy question, varying from person to person. However for a lot of people I think it'll easily clear that threshold; just not for everyone.

Here's the thing: the combat is very, very good. It's fast-paced without losing control, it's exciting and responsive, and it has an appeal that leaves you always looking for the next battle. It's a rollercoaster that feels right. Punching through the whole game and having a blast the whole time is not a problem since nearly the entire thing is composed of said great combat. If all you care about is the adrenaline, moving fast and blasting everything, this game has already been sold to you. The combat is bliss.

But the catch is that everything else largely falls by the wayside in favor of it. So the math works out as how much you value that exhilirating combat minus however many points the lesser things cost for you (if any). Fortunately it's not an unfair equation: there's nothing outright bad about the game, but there was plenty outside the combat that I found mediocre or otherwise meh. Hence, I really think most people would do well picking it up, especially if you know what you're getting into.

Won't go into longform detail, but here's a minor list of a few things that struck me as unremarkable, just to serve as exmaples:
  • Story (of course). But it did have its highlights.
  • The upgrade systems, though good for adding more room to the combat, weren't spectacularly integrated.
  • Game atmosphere doesn't get very varied. Explore scenic Hell and Mars techbase, and each doesn't get much variety.
  • Hallway, deathmatch arena where you're obviously going to fight, hallway, deathmatch arena, repeat. The predictable pattern leaves zero surprises. The arena environments also don't provide much to excite the imagination and really start to blur together, though they're more than suitable for the game's combat.
  • They're called 'secrets' in game, but, uh..., a lot of them aren't? Like at all?

There's so much more, but you get the idea. To spin it another way: the combat here is way more fun than Doom 3, but in the right state of mind I could find Doom 3 the more enjoyable game overall as an experience. But note: these 'detractions' are all things removed enough from the ecstatically beating heart of combat to not affect many people's decision about the game. If you're a rare person where $60 for this was just a little too high, well you've still barely made a mistake at all by purchasing. (You just wouldn't be harming yourself by waiting for a sale.)

Special mention: the soundtrack for the game is perfect. I was especially happy to catch a few returning motifs from the original Doom (the one most will recognize is the snippet of At Doom's Gate which plays when picking up a collectable).

Last note: I did not try the multiplayer as I'm not a big Quake-style deathmatch person. I have every reason to believe it resembles that kind of setup though. Do your own investigation there.\t\t\t\t\t

The multiplayer is great as it takes a lot of inspiration from quake live.
The marketing says fight like hell. That is shown in the gameplay. The game makes you fight like hell.
