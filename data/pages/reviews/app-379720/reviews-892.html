\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike someone said: Either you fall in love with this game or you stop playing it immediately. It's very much down to personal taste.
So many people claim it's the best shooter evar but no-one mentions that it's so very, very, very obviously a console, not a PC game. I'm not exaggerating when I say everything about this game feels console to me. "What's the difference between a PC and a console game" you ask? It's the difference between Deus Ex 1 and Deus Ex 2.
So if you love simple, action-packed pseudo-oldschool shooters and don't mind console game UI, console game world design, console game weapon design and feel and console game arcade-y gameplay on your PC, you'll probably love it. Reminds me of the recent Wolfenstein 1 & 2, which everyone claimed were the best FPS evar too and when I played them on PC I though: "These games are good, but if you claim they're the best evar you obviously never played a shooter that was made for PC instead of consoles or multi-platform (which is another word for console-first)".
If you're an oldschool PC gamer like me and don't like giant-ass crosshairs on monster size weapons and gamepad-focussed UIs and menues, learn from my mistake and don't buy this game and then wait a month before you actually play it so you can't get a refund if it turns out that you're just not the target audience for this simple console arcade shooter.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was there in late '93 when the original came out and I was blown away, many night shifts at work wasted playing this landmark game. It was incredibly basic by today's standards but a classic moment in gaming. I've avoided playing any of the new versions of DOOM until now simply because I didn't think they'd be able to compare with the feel of the original.

I found this on special offer on Steam and thought I'd get it just for laughs. Have to admit that it's one of the few times I've played a remake of a game that's genuinely been an improvement on the original. The game play is fluid and fast but not so fast you lose track of what's happening. The tracking of the bad guys is spot on and easy to keep up with, even for an old guy like me. On the easiest level of play it gives you plenty of time to react and fight off the horde while you keep going. Just like the original it's not rocket science to keep track of the path through the levels, very well designed levels keep you going and on track to maintain the pressure. There's moments where literally all hell is breaking lose, but as the dust settles at the end of each wave you get a few seconds to rest before you move on to the next wave, the frequency and timing are spot on.

Genuinely impressed by this remake, they've keep true to the spirit of the original game, fast, furious sometimes a little scary but really good fun to play and nothing beats mindless killing alien/demonic horde.\t\t\t\t\t
