\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game that finally forced me to write a Steam review. The true inheritor to the Unreal/Quake twitch shooter and a modern perfection of the form, Doom gives you a solid dose of exploration and customization without wasting one second of your time on fiddly interface or redundant storytelling. Start on Ultraviolence and let the game hone you into a rapidly reacting machine driven forward by the thunder of flawless sound design. The art style may be a little dated, but the graphics themselves are crisp and let your smoothly tear through a legion of different enemies across Mars and Hell. This is the rare game that makes both you and your foes feel powerful, a power fantasy that neither pretends to have anything significant to say or veers into problematic territory.

Doom is a pure game and purer dose of norepinephrine in dark times.\t\t\t\t\t
