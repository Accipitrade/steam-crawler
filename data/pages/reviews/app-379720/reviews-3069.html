\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk - First thing - really NOT happy that I dropped $60 bucks a few days ago and now find the game $30 cheaper. But that's a matter of policy. (How about a $30 store credit, Steam???)

The game - is awesome. Visually, the game is stunning. It's one of the most beautiful games I've ever seen. Id's coded it to take advantage of the best processors currently available (I have a GTX 1070). The level of detail is breathtaking, write down to dirty finger prints on your suit's helmet. It's been a long time since I had a top shelf graphics card and Doom is exactly the kind of game to put it thru its paces. On ultra/nighmare settings the graphics card barely breaks a sweat.

Id still knows what it's doing. This is FPS madness at its best. They stick the formula they invented so many years ago. If anything, they've pared that formula down to its basics - Doom 3, the last major iteration of the franchise - was a lot more "story" rich, with longer and more elaborate cut scenes. (And, old as it is, Doom 3 still looks great, much better than Quake 4. Unfortunately, I can't get it to run under Windows 10). In fact, there is, perhaps too little story here. The focus is clearly on the action and the action is basically killing demons in new, interesting, visually arresting and hyper-violent ways. The game works on the basic "Hulk smash!" level. Most of the exposition is provided by a character named Samuel Hayden, who gives you background as well as clues pertinent to completing various levels.

No long intro here - just immediately plunged in to the ♥♥♥♥♥. The mechanics are well done, as usual with Id. Switching between weapons is a little awkward - you can't simply scroll thru your arsenal as you could in the old Doom/Quake games.

It works wonderfully well with an Xbox 360 controller.

So far the most annoying part of the game is much too long intervals between check points. The game uses an auto-save system, which is another gripe of mine. I've never liked them. And, as with all Id games, it's easy to end up fruitlessly running around a level if you miss a left-turn or a door. Thank God for walk-thrus.

I will update this when I've finished the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is nothing short of a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ masterpiece.

The soundtrack is sexy as hell.

The mars environments are sexy as hell.

Samuel Hayden's voice is sexy as hell.

Glory kills are sexy as hell. (RIP AND TEAR)

Hell is sexy as hell.

And they did a great job of weaving it into existing Doom lore. There's some really nice and subtle iceberg storytelling that makes it even sexier.

And frankly, I really enjoy Doomguy's character. They did a great job of giving him personality. "But Johnny," people who have played DOOM might say, "he was a two dimensional character: he never spoke, and his only form of communication was basically violence." To this I reply: I KNOW ISN'T IT GREAT? But being serious for a moment, the way Doomguy interacts with the environment is pretty cool. The way he handles various objects is very expressive, and the persistent feeling of anger and indifference makes what few moments of hesitation or softness (and those moments ARE there) much more meaningful. Trust me, you'll know them when you see them, and if you go out of your way to find all the entries on the Doom Marine, then I think you'll agree with my assessment. Anyway, enough lore sperging, on to one of the slickest game mechanics I have seen in awhile.

It offers a new take on combat. Rather than having health regen (so you can hide like a ♥♥♥♥♥ when ♥♥♥♥ gets bad), you are expected to kill for your health. You have to be a non-stop badass. Because you are Doomguy. WHO'S A MAN AND A HALF? YOU'RE A MAN AND A HALF! BERSERKER PACKIN' MAN AND A HALF! So you need to act like one. RIP. AND. TEAR.

The shooting is good, the weapons are satisfying, and everything about it is just great in general. It is one of the few games that I chose to buy at full price, because I wanted to believe, and because my friends said it was so great. And it was. And it still is. So do it. Buy this game. Encourage them to make a sequel. Because ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, this game needs a sequel. I need a sequel. We all need more DOOM in our lives.

Disclaimer - the multiplayer kinda sucks from what I hear, but I didn't even bother with it. The single player is worth the price alone, imo.\t\t\t\t\t
