\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying on "Hurt Me Plenty". Maybe I'm getting to this game too late but it just feels... fine. If it weren't for all the rave reviews its gotten over the years, I feel like I would have enjoyed it more.

Movement feels like your guiding a block of lead across ice. Shooting is exactly what you expect, not great not bad. Combat is weirdly sectioned off. You fight a handful of demons and then nothing happens for a few minutes then you fight again. The collectibles, which are necessary to find to upgrade your equipment, range from brain dead easy to annoyingly tedious.

If I could just run through the level fighting demons it would be a million times better. I'm going to try upping the difficulty but that won't fix the first-person platforming or the collectibles.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finished this game when it came out in 2016 and then again this year and it still stands. Doom 2016 is one of the best single player FPS games of the past 10 years. It was worth every single penny when it came out for $60, which means it is an absolute must buy for 19.99. It's a game that makes you feel incredibly awesome the entire time you are playing, full of incredible visual design, lots of hints to the classic Doom games, and an incredibly compelling gameplay loop. The soundtrack is also amazing and fits everything perfect, which helps amplify those already tense fights against overwhelming odds. If you are a fan of trying to complete games 100%, the multiple difficulties, easter eggs, hidden items, etc will keep you engaged for a long time.

The only downside(s) to this game is that the Snapmap and Multiplayer modes don't seem amazing. This might be controversial, but if you are looking for a multiplayer game to match the intensity of this games single player campaign, I'd recommend checking out Quake Champions. This games MP options could be great with friends, but I didn't really have a great time any time I tried to hop into a match, and snapmap didn't seem nearly as useful as including an actual map editor with workshop support or anything.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's DOOM for a new generation.

After the slog that was Doom 3, I was very disappointed. That one was trying way too hard for the horror aspect at the expense of what made the original doom great: lots of enemies, lots of guns, and never stop running.

Doom 2016 doesn't bother with limited weapon selections, cover mechanics, or stealth like so many recent FPS. This is straight up run and gun from one fight to the next. Each big fight is separated by a little bit of travel/exploration for secrets/ammo gathering. Saves are done via checkpoint and seeing that message appear is often your clue that the final enemy in the area is dead.

This game ignores Doom 3, and instead is virtually a sequel to the Doom 2 in that you're the epic Doom marine woken up from stasis to do what you do best. Mars is once again overrun by the forces of Hell, and it's up to you to kill them all.

There is a little bit of customization via a rune system where you can complete trials to unlock runes. Once equipped, the runes will offer a specific benefit which can also be upgrade by completing tasks such as aerial kills, gathering a certain amount of ammo/armor, or kill enemies in specific ways. Up to three runes can be equipped at one time. Weapons can be upgraded and/or modified, and once you have earned all of the upgrades or mods, you can unlock the mastery for that weapon/mod combo by completing specific kills. For example, upgrading your sniper rifle is done once you get 50 scoped head shots.

The game is very straightforward - apart from a little bit of secret hunting you can expect 10-15 hours for your first play through, though once you get the hang of it you can speed run it in around 2-3 hours.
