\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, ok. Having spend a significant amount of time with the new DOOM, I can say that it is one of my favourite games of all time now.

Here the cliffnotes:

Pros
  • beautiful audio-visual design. Looks and sounds as you would expect a modern DOOM to look and sound
  • Fantastic single player campaign. Very straight forward fun. Non-linear maps. Lots of secrets. Weapon upgrades etc.
  • High-speed combat in kill rooms. The game will funnel you into these open areas with tons of enemies. If you want to survive you have to keep moving. Awesome fun.
  • SNAPMAP - Ok, this is great. It will give the game infinite replayability. It is like Portal2's steam workshop support. Basically, like a Doom version of MarioMaker. Plus it is fun and easy to use and you can play with your friends in COOP. Brilliant.
  • Performance. The game runs like butter and you have a gazillion settings to manipulate. On a single 980ti the game runs in Ultra/Nightmare settings at 3440x1440 (21:9) almost always at a 100+ fps, with occasional dips into the high 70s. Good stuff, considering we are not even playing the Vulkan version yet.


Cons

  • The competitive multiplayer may be a con for you, if you expect a full Quake 3 Arena or Unreal Tournament type game. Personally, I love the multiplayer. It feels fresh for Doom. It is like a mix between Quake 3 and Halo multiplayer. For me it works, but if you are going to buy the game just for the multiplayer. Understand what you are getting.


    Overall, I am extremely happy about how the game turned out. If you are a fan of DOOM, you will at the very least love the campaign and SNAPMAP mode.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInitial impressions of the game from publicity materials and limited participation in the closed beta weren't good, but I'm pleased to have been mostly proved wrong.

Doom (without the 4) is a fun single player experience. The story is wafer thin and, like many other ID releases, the game is essentially a rich tech demo for the purpose of licencing the engine. However it does have many pluses:

The graphics are silky smooth, even on a 3-year old card and look great with modest detail settings
The action is very fast and fluid, like the best of online PVP games
The game was rock solid. The only interruptions I had were from unannounced updates from Steam. A welcome change from the usual day one Bethesda bugfest.
It brought back memories of bot matches on Quake Arena and horde battles in Serious Sam

On the down side - and these are very much subjective - the story was unengaging. Despite the criticisms leveled at Doom 3, it was very atmospheric and palpably diabolical. Less so this new game. The story makes the same mistake as the movie in downplaying Hell and its hordes as just another alien threat. A waste in that respect.

Anyway. Try it. You may enjoy it.

This review was based on one complete run through the single player campaign.\t\t\t\t\t
