\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the greatest first-person shooter to ever be created. It's so good, that every FPS I played after playing Doom felt awful, even if I'd previously loved the game or previous games in the series (Crysis 3, for example, feels terrible now that I've played Doom).

Mick Gordon's soundtrack is godlike. Musician's could work 24 hours a day for next 200 years and not even come close to creating music equal to the quality of Mick Gordon's mastery, let alone better. Seriously, the soundtrack could very easily be put into the Oxford English Dictionary as an example of perfection.

Guns feel fantastic. The pistol you start with may feel very weak and useless compared to every other gun in thje game, but compared to other games, it's great. Instantly switching between multiple weapons, each with 2 different modifications, plus the chainsaw, plus the BFG, PLUS the finishing moves, PLUS the speed and maneuverability of the movement make every second in Doom - whether slaughtering hordes of demons or searching for secrets - more fun than anything you can possibly imagine. Don't be surprised if you turn down sex to play Doom instead.

The only bad thing I can think of when it comes to Doom, isn't even that bad. Multiplayer. Some people hate it, I think it's really fun, if a bit poorly balanced. The only reason I say it's bad, is because it's compared directly with the singleplayer which is infinitely more enjoyable.

Bottom line, Doom is flawless. Buy it at full price if you can and show id Software (& Bethesda) they did a damn good job.

By the way, you can buy Mick Gordon's soundtrack now, but it's not the full thing. There should be about 50 songs, but the album only has 21.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter finishing the campaign and spending about a satisfying 15 hours to beat it, it's safe to say DOOM is the best FPS I've played this year, maybe even GOTY. Everything runs butter smooth from the performance to the movement and combat. The glory kills are amazing and are viscerally enthralling to do. The selection of weapons is great and it is a blast to switch between them while fighting a demon horde. And I MUST mention the soundtrack. It is easily a 10/10 soundtrack and one of the best ones I've ever heard in a videogame. I'm always pumped to get to the next room full of demons to fight because I know I'll be slaying them to the tune of heavy guitar rifts! The story is barely there and the ending is kind of a let down but that isn't the reason you come to play DOOM right? I dabbled a bit in the multiplayer and it's a bit average, nothing I'm gonna come back to play when I have other better multiplayer shooters like Rainbow Six Siege and Overwatch. But I don't want the multiplayer portion of the game to deduct from the amazing single player experience. If the game is on a sale then don't miss the opportunity and pick it up immediately! You won't regret it.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t
