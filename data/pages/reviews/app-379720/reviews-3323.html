\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA worthy successor to the series that I have cherished for all my life. The core aspects that put DOOM into legendary status in gaming history are alive and well in this title. From the very beginning, you are thrown into the action with little knowledge of what's going on. The enemies are introduced similar to how they were in the classic DOOM games: thrown directly into your face. The speed of the gameplay is just like that of the classics and keeps you on your toes. You have to keep moving in order to stay alive! The heavy metal music is outstanding and hits almost every note in setting the essential feel of playing a DOOM game. The weapons are well made, feel powerful and satisfying, and the "Glory Kills" make it a joy to rip and tear from one end of Hell to the other.

The new release is very well optimized also - my 3 year-old laptop with GTX 770M can run it well @ 1280x720 on Medium settings. There are many other options to optimize the settings to fit your system picture-perfectly. I rarely have a crash, and it seems to be in specific areas. These crashes I'm confident will be patched out in the [near] future. Overall, the game is very stable and smooth, ESPECIALLY for a new release.

There are only a few things I can say I legitimately have issues with in this game. The main menu is a bit inconvenient - I'm unsure as to why it's necessary to restart the game in "Multiplayer" or "SnapMap" modes. If you want to play online, you need to select Multiplayer or SnapMap and the game has to restart. If you want to go back to the singleplayer game, you have to restart again. I've personally never experienced that when trying to play online with my friends in any other game. Also, SnapMap isn't finished at the time I'm writing this. It needs some quality-of-life improvements such as search filters as new maps enter the scene, a more interactive menu to find specific games, and just overall things that are standard in today's terms regarding what should be in a multiplayer menu. I'm confident that SnapMap will be refined and polished into a great tool to make amazing maps for endless fun. :)

This title is well worth your dollars in the end. It's DOOM, after all!\t\t\t\t\t
