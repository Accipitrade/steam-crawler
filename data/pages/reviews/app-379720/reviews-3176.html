\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow. Im really glad I bought this game, especially after the lukewarm release reception. Its run-and-gun fun from the first 10 seconds on. Great looking areas, excellent level design, brutally fun combat. So far, my only complaint is the music-- it works and adds to the excitement, but theyre pretty forgettable 'metal' tunes. Nothing youll be humming for days (let alone remembering for decades like the classic E1M1 theme). Although, thats small potatoes compared to how polished and attentive to detail- visually and combat-wise - this game tends to be.

The best part of this game is how EXTREMELY well-optimized that it is. I have a newer video card (Geforce GTX 970) but my board and processor are definitely aged, nearing 7 years old... yet I can run everything on medium/high with 0 lag, 0 screen tears, 0 load time in-level... and this game gets REALLY packed with things moving on screen at times. Even on medium, the game still looks high quality. Cant wait to finish building my new rig so i can set this baby to 'ultra' and really let it blow me away.

If you like classic (or brutal) DOOM, you should feel right at home here. Its just funny how, 20 years ago, this game could be put on a couple floppy discs. Now, it eats a quarter of my hard drive. lol......

666/10. Get it on sale though. In this day and age, theres NO reason to pay full price for ANY game. $35.99 on sale for a title like this one? Sure, Ill go for it, considering how Ive spent over 70$ on SNES carts back in the day. $36 bucks well-spent too, Id say. Really lookin forward to the inevitable DOOM II now!\t\t\t\t\t

If you love FPS games, this is the one. To me, this is a mix of the best things of Qake 4 and Doom.
Scenarios are gorgeous. Characters and monsters are awesome.

Rest of things are under ID's aura: blood, hell, mosters, big weapons, big monsters, big bosses, pipes, machines, etc...
At the very beginning, the sensation to be on Mars is just perfect: the sand, the wind, the light, everything together. Way far away of the sensations you can have with Doom BFG, by example.

Fights are frenetic, non-stop, constantly jump, shoot, dodge... total madness. You often go disoriented on the map.
The character moves really fast, as the monsters do, you have to be in constant movement.
This is funny but, the scenarios are that good that you would love to have a bit of time to enjoy the graphics.

Story is typical, on the line of ID' stories, not bad, not good but, perfectly suits this kind of game.
Anyway, best shooter available, without any doubt.

I personally hate the boss fights, but that's me. It makes me to waste a lot of stupid time. I would love an option to bypass the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ bosses (something I also hate in Namco's games). But that's me. If you like bosses, you certainly have some really challenging ones here.

On the bad side, lots of crashes, without any explanation (Windows 10 64bit), with the latest NVIDIA driver.
And the checkpoits. We are in 2016. Can't we save the game at any time, any spot?. That's odd.

I would suggest them to give some more autonomy to the chainsaw, it goes out of fuel really quickly and, it's one of the most funny weapons. As well, there is no way to enhance such a weapon. That would be nice against the big monsters.

Playability is total. This is gaming. This is Fun, with capital letters.
Re-playability is ensured. When you do your first campaing, you will probably miss lots of secrets and, parts of the map.
You will probably love to play once more every map.

The end is open so, I bet they are working on a Doom 2. Which is a good thing, for sure!.
Thank you, ID and Bethesda for bringing us such a cool game.

Still reading?. Buy it now, dude!\t\t\t\t\t

This essence is quite maintained in this new version. Killing enemies is not a excuse to make story advance like much other games. It is the combat what is really cool and you just cannot wait for the next enemies trap to try to survive.

Most of the weapons are well done and balanced. Like in traditional doom, you usually switch to a weapon and use it until its ammo is depleated.Then you just change to another and keep enjoying You will find yourself switching to another weapons, even less powerful just to play again with its combat style and kill some demon with them.

I didn't tried the multiplayer yet (don't expect much), but the single player campaign is what an old doom fan would expect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't think that the classic DOOM could be remade according to modern game design principles and still feel similar to the original, but Bethesda succeeded.

The key to the success lies in the optionality of many of the things. If you wish, you can just blast through the levels, in a frenetic non-stop action. Or, if you are so inclined, you can explore each level, every nook and cranny, to discover all the secrets.

The weapons feel awesome, and the possibility to upgrade them in different ways and choose which mod to use, adds a lot of variety.

Monsters are well designed and they work well together - some of them chase you relentlessly for a close range fight, while others hurl their fireballs and shoot laser beams from a distance, which, combined, means you will be constantly moving, constantly thinking on your feet.

===

Word of warning: If the game crashes while it was autosaving, then the save file may become corrupted, at which point you'll have to start from the absolute beginning, because there are no backups or anything. This really sucks.\t\t\t\t\t
