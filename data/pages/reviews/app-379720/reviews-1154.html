\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeing an original Doom fan from back in the day, still are to some extent, this just doesn't cut it for me. Maybe, if you are 12 years old, this would be real fun. Stunning visuals, great map design, don't get me wrong. Sadly I just got bored with it and uninstalled when I reached the Hell chapters.

SnapMap is terrible. I mean, really bad. A four-digit combination lock has way more possibilities than what you can rustle up with this. All content will look and feel the same, because it relies on pre-existing set pieces that can only be connected in certain ways, with no room for deviation. It's a shame developers have turned their backs on modding communities by introducing in-game pseudo-level editors that limit creativity - a Steam Workshop page would have been a game-changer.

And now there's this new Doom Eternal out... which is set in Hell.... and can also stay in hell, till hell freezes over, because I shall not be buying it anytime soon.\t\t\t\t\t

Final score: 5/5

“Welcome to Hell”, says a deep, growling disembodied voice as a bada** heavy metal song by composer Mick Gordon starts playing. “But really, it’s heaven – it’s video game heaven”.

In a sea of mediocre games and interactive movies, DOOM is the light at the end of the tunnel. It focusses on gameplay and fun more than anything, elements that I, an elite gamer, have always valued above all other factors in games.

That is not to say they slacked with the story writing and while some might be turned off by DOOM’s simplistic caveman story of “me big strong man blow out demon brains uga uga”, I found myself really engaged with the lore, as I too am a caveman and don’t care about life lessons like how revenge can turn into a vicious circle and you should rather forgive someone for killing your father in a golfing incident than drown them in a puddle. So, here is a better life lesson for you: the circle of revenge truly stops when you ripped and tore until it was done, and only until it was done.

Despite the simplicity, the story is somewhat deep enough to make you question whether it’s worth to play with a perfect energy source if it can and will destroy your planet every few years – a question too hard for my caveman brain to answer, as already established. But you got a cool robot to worry about those sorts of things.

But this is DOOM, ok? Who cares about a story, I just want to kill and that’s where gameplay comes in. You start with only a pistol and have to pop some weaklings’ heads to progress. Though soon enough they give you a shotgun and Doom Slayer just goes *click clack* and you immediately know what this game is all about. DOOM has almost a dozen weapons and a couple of useable items like grenades that you’ll forget you even have because the guns are too much fun not to use for even a second. They all have different modes and can be upgraded via completing challenges, giving you a reason to replay missions, plus cool little collectibles are a thing, so… By the way, this game has the most satisfying and iconic shotgun ever made: the SUPER SHOTGUN, my favourite weapon ever and I’m saying that as a stealth archer. Oh, right! The suit can also be upgraded giving you higher resistances and so forth, by completing gauntlets called Rune Challenges which reward you with abilities to equip and upgrade, making builds somewhat possible.

The enemy design is varied; they have a distinct difficulty and their own strengths and weaknesses, while some have more of one than the other. There are also a couple of bosses in the game, as expected, and they still make a nice addition but are not very hard once you figured them out.

The levels themselves are great in design and not as linear as imagined, while jumping from sci-fi labs to hellscapes to cities of ash as you progress through the story. All in all, you’ll be breaking bones and burning flesh in an amazingly detailed, gory and beautiful world, that might make you pause your slaughtering for maybe a second and just admire the scenery.

As already made obvious from my praise, pretty much every gun is fun to play with and can be used in most situations. The finisher moves are flashy and a brilliant mechanic to give you armour, health or ammo if you need it, while the rune abilities can make you OP as hell if you upgraded them. DOOM knows exactly what it wants to be and is confident enough to ridicule itself with over the top gore and sound effects at times. And all this is tied together by one of the best OSTs you’ll ever hear outside a FromSoft game.

My only criticism are some secrets can be super hard to find and make you run around a map for an hour, until you give up and just look it up and bash your head against a wall in frustration, because you ran past that exact spot like 10 times.

It’s currently 20€ on steam, which is super cheap for an amazing game like this, making the decision to recommend this mastapeece a no-brainer.

Happy ripping and tearing.\t\t\t\t\t
