Product refunded
\r\n\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYeah, I know I only played it very briefly, but there's a reason for that.


The game has numerous small issues that ultimately convinced me to refund it:

* It didn't have real ragdoll physics.
* There's this cliff you can loom over on the first level or whatever they call them this time.
* I decided to jump off that cliff and enjoy the visuals of falling to my death. Well, like half way down, I just died. I wondered if I got a fireball or something thrown downward, so I tried it twice more. Nope. I died in the same place every time.
* Now, that's not that big of a deal, frankly. I mean, if I were to refund over that, it'd be unreasonable.
* When you jumped up to a lot of things, your arms would come out to pull you up. But there were tons of these minicliffs that you could so totally do that with, but they weren't marked correctly. That is, the level designer either failed to whitelist it, or instead blacklisted it, meaning, what one would expect from the game's logic doesn't apply.
* I had to spend ~10min walking around these horizontal steel rope bars even though he could realistically just climb up, since he can pull himself up 2.5m small cliffs ad infinitum by jumping to them. This betrays that little actual thought was put into level.
* The monsters lacked the flavor you had from the original game, not in that the flavor was different, but in that it didn't really exist. Oh, this fleshblob model is for this monster, and that one is for this other monster? Okay got it. This slightly pinker one is for a third monster?
* The enemies don't have terribly different tactics. They don't seem to really have any personality beyond pew pew pew.
* The monsters were visually interchangeable and initially I actually thought they were just different models for the same kind of monster
* Monster corpses didn't gib, and blood doesn't splatter on the walls/ground. For a franchise that's in no small part about being a gorefest, this kind of seems ... lame.
* Not only did it fail to capture the "feel" of the original game, it also failed to replace it with something else that wasn't already done a thousand times. It felt like Quake meets Unreal meets HL2. It felt generic.

Bear in mind that I only played for about 1hr and 30min, but if what I saw is any indication, it's nothing to call home about.\t\t\t\t\t
