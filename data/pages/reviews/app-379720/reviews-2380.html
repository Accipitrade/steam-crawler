\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI liked Doom 3 but this Doom reboot is much closer to the original Doom in its game design.
You basically goes from one arena to the other fighting hordes of demons but with a bit of action and exploration in between.

I think it is the best arcade FPS I've played until today:
+ The game is skill based, never unfair even at the highest difficulty and a rewarding challenge.
+ It's an arcade shooter and accordingly, it play fast. It's full of adrenaline and feels stressful as it should be, at least if you play in the difficulty levels Ultra Violence or above.
+ Doom is very offense oriented. If you stay static you're going to die very fast and it feels just right for an arcade shooter.
+ Weapons are varied and feels great. I love that damn double shotgun.
+ they put a bit of character levelling in there. Not to much. Just enough to enjoy the progression.
+ It looks very good with great art design
+ The music isn't my favorite kind but it fits very well with the mood of the game.
+ The story isn't bad considering that it's a arcade shooter and the main character is very cool and badass.

- a few bugs here and there like falling through the floor.
- I regret that there is no difficulty level between nightmare and ultra-nightmare (same but with only one life for the entire game). I would have love to have a difficulty level with one life per level.
- It's probably not the most important thing but Doom doesn't have meaningful achievement. You got the useless milestone typical of AAA but no achievement for finishing the game in nightmare or ultra nightmare for exemple. I know it is so because they don't want to frustrate snowflakes who somehow wants to collect all the achievement but its a real shame IMO.

It took me around 40 hours to complete an ultra violence run and a nightmare run.

Rating; 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
