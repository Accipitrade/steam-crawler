Why play this game? Well, the answer is simple, because you constantly rek the face of demons. Tearing the hearts of your enemies out of their bodies (actually, literally) and ripping a chainsaw through their chest, not to mention exploding them and filling them with shotgun shells is just soooo fun. The gameplay is fast paced and exilerating, and the story/character development isn't half bad either. You can also unlock classic DOOM levels from the original game, which you can play, seperately from the campaign of course.

Its a traditional DOOM game, lets be real here. You're the voiceless DOOM guy, a badass supersoldier equipped with the latest in military-grade weaponry. Since its DOOM, you're obviously obliterating demons around every corner and jumping around to not get killed by that Baron of Hell that came from nowhere. Since its a modern title, there's a lot of exploration for secrets and optional items.

The progression and upgrade system is pretty neat, very straightforward and offers some awesome upgrades for your weapons (some are completely overpowered, infinite rockets anyone?). The weapons all have unique combat styles and are suited better for different situations. You'll probably end up playing some weapons more than others, and I'll use different weapons more often, it just depends on your playstyle. I can say with confidence though, that you will want to use the BFG 9000 as much as possible (ITS OVER 9000!!! sorry, I had to, but seriously its frickin' powerful). There are also special upgrades for your Praetor Suit called Runes. They can give you a longer range on pickups, longer range on Glory Kills, and much much more (I have been actively searching for all of them).

I haven't played multiplayer, so I can't tell you anything about that, but most other reviews say multiplayer isn't good anyways. All the DLC is for multiplayer, so it isn't worth your $$$ unless you like the muliplayer.

The graphics are actually pretty good, and the game has support for the new Vulkan API. With OpenGL being pretty optimized and hardware friendly, older or less gamey PCs can still get decent graphics without sacrificing 90% of their playability. If your PC is stronk enough, cranking it up to Nightmare settings can yield some really nice visuals.

In my opinion, DOOM 2016 isn't really worth a $60 price tag, if you don't play multiplayer. But if you do, then I can justify a standard AAA price. I got my copy a couple weeks ago when it was 50% off, definitely worth it. I highly recommend this title to any action and/or FPS junky, and to classic DOOM fans too. The difficulties can give you a hard time, and when you rek the face of big, powerful demons, you feel really awesome. Does relieve stress when on slightly lower difficulties, if that matters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I'm a bit late in reviewing this, but I have to say, this is the best singleplayer shooter I have ever played, besides it's obvious predecessor, which still runs on my toaster.

The game is so well optimised, with all the recent AAA titles having a huge amount of lag, frame drops and day one patches, this is a refresher. The graphics are beautiful, and it ran at 4k with 60fps on my 970.

If someone said to me, I'm new to PC gaming or gaming altogether, I definitely would not give them this, this game requires you to be sick of every other ♥♥♥♥♥♥ story based game with draining dialogue and long cutscenes, you have to be ready, you have to want to kill some ♥♥♥♥, speaking of, the gameplay; that essentially is the gameplay, kill ♥♥♥♥, and I, and the rest of the classic Doom fans wouldn't have it any other way, what we wanted from Id was what we got, a shotgun and a horde of monsters. The game is bright and colourful, and is short, linear, but yet has very open levels with lots of secrets to discover, great for speedrunners, it is also very engaging, you are always in a fight, you can't draw yourself away. Once you leave one area you are in the next killing stuff, your only escape is the end of the level.

Shame about the multiplayer...\t\t\t\t\t
