\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLooks beautiful. Sounds great. Play's amazing.

Worth the wait. This is the Doom game you wanted, heck it's the videogame you've wanted for a long long time.

Shotguns blast, blood sprays and body parts go flying.

Also the way "glory kills" and the Chainsaw function to return health and ammo to the player is nothing short of ingenious.

In my opinion - best shooter soundtrack since Quake II. Great use of short guitar stabs when doing things like discharging plasma gun.

Finished campaign on default difficulty in 11 hours, would probably take 10 hrs without dying as much as me.




Final Thoughts:

Upon finishing you will be able to select any mission already played, and play any old school maps you found in secret areas. There's a nifty 3D model viewer, but you will need to find a corresponding item in game to unlock these.

I found this to be an enjoyable and consistent experience from start to finish, although the further you get into the game the bigger the hordes you will fight and consequently the chance at death rises noticeably.

My only quibbles with the game are that the chainsaw kills do not renew BFG ammo, the pacing can sometimes be slow due to the size of some areas, and the bugs I experienced mentioned at the end of this "review".

In the end I would not classify this as a memorable game, it's more of a summer blockbuster popcorn action flick. Just that it's a very entertaining one, and while playing the action will consume your attention.

My final rating:

Graphics 10/10
Sound 8/10
Gameplay 9/10
Story 7/10

Overall 8/10 - solid experience, no buyers remorse, thorougly entertained, got my moneys worth, essential purchase when you find a price point you agree with.




Bugs experienced:

One instance of falling through game world and dying while performing a glory kill.

One crash to desktop (no error message) during my second visit to hell.\t\t\t\t\t

I had major concerns about how it would turned out... and my fears were put to rest for now... yet to go deep in yet, but so far it has delivered from all fronts except MP which I haven't touch besides the Beta which was OK only for me.

Great job!

And btw... it runs REALLY well on my measly setup :
G3258 stock speed
8GB RAM
2GB nVidia GTX 750Ti stock

Update :
Played more and this is summary so far :
Doom marine does looks like Doom marine.... updated :)

Level design :
very good and not maze like. It really feels like a facility/R&D thingy

Gameplay :
Ohh... where to start. DOOM all over again... and THAT my friend is a VERY VERY GOOD thing :) Speed is great and the new added mechanics works very well. Glory kills gives and alternate side to the run and gun aspect and is statisfying. Jump and grabbing edges to climb up adds new dimension to the gameplay but does not slow down the pace.

Monster :
Imps are pistol folders but still adds some nice touch due to their quickness and jumps. The design is updated well yet still reminisce of Classic Doom in-a-way kind way :D If that makes sence. Seeing the Hell Knight first time in combat was scary... I can only imagine the Baron and Cyberdemon :D The Hell Knight does reminds me of the Hell Knight from Doom 3 though. Summoner was annoyingly fun to take down :D
But the AI aren't all that smart which is understandable... this is a run and gun game :) Fits well.

Story :
Story? what story? Oh that story... so yeah.. completely optional and I didn't even bother following it XD

Overall up to now... 4.5 out of 5 :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing the MP beta I was sceptical, but purchased DOOM for it's Single Player Campaign (as it really should be).
It's F***ING good!

- Optimised (feels smooth unlike beta release)
- Fast Paced
- Feels like Doom but with a modern addition
- Gory AF
- Combat is fluent
- Guns just feel good
- Tactical play style required
- SECRETS! [Feel rewarded! but there's no gain without pain in DOOM]

You will definitely feel the pinch at times with a "Low Health" warning, and "Low Ammo" warning on your guns to be reduced to your pistol, or even the chainsaw just to gain those final few rounds of ammunition, whilst avoiding death.

They mix it up with confined area gameplay, and open area combat and the transissions feel close to seamless.

So what's the downfall?
- Storyline isn't exactly straight in your face like other games (but that's how I've always felt the original DOOM games were like so not really a negative for myself)
- Invisible walls? Some cliffs you can jump off and commit suicide, others you can't, sitting there like "LOLWTFBRUV!?"
- Checkpoints... legitimately this is one of the only gripes I actually have... sometimes you can clear a whole area, then find yourself attempting to find a secret, only to slip from a stupid move and die... and that's when you find out the checkpoint is located in a further section after the combat. So just because it's safe it doesn't mean you've reached a safety net yet. [Repeated 2 areas due to this]\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust got done playing through the first few levels and first impressions are.... Wow. Exceeded my expectations, as well as running 100 + average fps with my 970. id really understand the value of high framerates!

The game is a hybrid of old-school with modern elements, ie. with fast and verticle movement, quick but meaty gunplay, with leveling and perks that are not unlike the Wolfenstein reboot from Machine Games. Excpept this game is much, much faster.

The visiuals are a treat, and lastly I'll mention the enemies. It's a rare shooter these days where your AI enemies will dynmaically use the environment to get the drop on you. Imps climbing walls, etc. This adds so much to the arena battles in the singleplayer, and the buggers have caught me off guard a few times.

This is nothing like CoD, and it's good to see Doom hark back to its roots while also being a modern shooter. Well worth a look on the singeplayer alone so far.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start by saying that I started my PC gaming experience with the original Doom in 1994 as a kid, and I've also played Doom 3 in the mid-2000's. I now own all Doom titles in my library. This is only my 2nd major game review on Steam with currently over 1,000 titles total. I do not normally post reviews unless I have something compelling to say.

So I'll get right to it.

This new DOOM delivers the strengths of what made "Doom 3" successful (and NOT from the original Doom series from the 1990's which was what I was expecting, more on this later). First, many friends and myself would've preferred having the just the "option" of co-op play for the SP campaign, especially when the game is labeled as having co-op on the its store page. The only co-op there is is from its "Snap Map" game mode which allows the creation of custom maps, including DM, TDM, CTF, Survival, etc with co-op.

The gameplay's "glory kill" (1-hit melee kill against any enemey) option, giving the player invulnerability frames during the kill animation, is WAY overdone and quickly became repetitive and grew stale within minutes. Since when did the Doom guy start out with invincible melee skills? The closest to that was the "berserk" power up item from Doom 1, and it was a temporary-effect item that was usually found as a secret, had to be picked-up, did not have invincibility frames, and you'd lose it at stage exit.

These days, I spend more time gaming with friends than by myself. Gaming has always been and is increasingly becoming a social outlet for many, and DOOM would've been a great avenue to take advantage of that. I am saddened to see that it did not. That being said, if you like a graphically-superb and faithful reboot of specifically Doom 3, and do not mind a bit of "me" time currently, DOOM delivers precisely that. I just wished it had evolved to become at least slightly more than that after two decades since the first title. Since Bethesda had already chosen to make a reboot of Doom 3 and not the original Doom (1993), they might as well have added co-op to it, as Doom 3's co-op mod was one of the highest mods in-demand for an FPS title at the time. They certainly had to have known that.

This DOOM's multiplayer, specifically TDM, was dull at best. It lacked the adrealine-pumping soundtracks, twitch-reflex action that the original Doom series, Quake series, and the Unreal Tournament series were known for. DOOM feels like a slightly washed down version of its predecessors. This may have been Bethesda's decision to resonate more with the average teenage gamer who's last and only "Doom" experience was 2004's "Doom 3", as well as with mass-market FPS titles such as COD and Halo. Many of us are certainly not teenagers, nor am I an average gamer. I am a devoted FPS lover, among other genres, that fell in love with Doom 1 in 1993 when I first ran it on DOS via a floppy disc, as well as with the Quake 1, 2, and 3 on CD-ROM (for those who may be too young to know this, they were also developed by id Software and were widely hailed as the successors to the original Doom).

As a fan and worshiper of the original 1990's Doom series as well as other old school FPS titles by id Software, I cannot honestly recommend this title. Implement the option of co-op for the SP campaign, nerf the "glory kill" melee insta-kill option, adjust the multiplayer pacing, add multiplayer voice scripts, catchier soundtracks, then maybe I'll give it another try again. And oh yeah, WHERE the heck are the bunny hops and strafe jumps? Who makes a retro throwback FPS with neither of those core movement elements?

And why would I or anyone have to restart the game just to launch the Multiplayer or Snapmap mode? Unfortunately the restarts are mandatory each time. A bit of a PITA.

Bottom line: What I saw in this reboot was purely "familiarity", and not the "nostalgia" that I was hoping for, because its "soul" is clearly Doom 3, instead of the original Doom. If you liked Doom 3, you'll probably like this DOOM. But if you loved the original Doom series from the 1990's, then you will want to hold back until there's a sale or simply pass on this one. I will be one of the latter. The game really should've been called "Doom 4", rather than "DOOM" as it may inadvertently suggest (or mislead) customers that the game may have drawn its inspirations and qualities from the original Doom - which is obviously not the case. (And ironically, this game WAS in fact named "Doom 4", until Besthesda acquired id several years ago and John Carmack subsequently left.)

Out of my thousand titles I have rarely returned games. But this is one I'll be reluctantly returning.

To Bethesda and id Software employees and affiliates - go ahead and down vote me all you want. I've only been one of your biggest fans for the Doom, Quake, Elder Scrolls, and Fallout series in the last 30 years. ;-)

Happy fragging, everyone.\t\t\t\t\t
