\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR Looks great, plays great, get before playing Eternal.
Note: I have not tried the online multiplayer as my internet tends to hit over 5000ms ping (the max Discord will show) more often than you'd think, and I have only done a minimal amount of snapmap for a similar reason.

visuals
tldr: Yes, very gorey, very realistic. Read on:
I played this on 2 different computers. One with a GTX 1060, the other on a Vega 8 (integrated) GPU. don't ask why, but needless to say, the game looks great, at low or maxed out (that and you kinda don't notice the exact details when you're busy ripping and tearing). The overall aspect of the game was intended to go as realistic as possible, and while no, there is no ray tracing (at this time there isn't even on Eternal yet (great now this review will age poorly)) it still looks quite semi-realistic. The graphics (oh and trust me, it's graphical) are, really, as realistic as you would expect a game from 2016 to be (if not better).

story
tldr: Interesting but subtle.
Yes, this is a Doom game, yes, there is a story to it; and yes, there always has been, even in 1993. I'll try to avoid spoilers as much as realistically possible. The story is, simply put, intriguing, in a good way. You are a legendary mythical human from many years gone past. After years, the demons managed to put the slayer (which gets explained a lot in Eternal) into his "sarcophagus", to which he stayed in until the events of the game. The story as a whole, is very well written. If you would like to know more on specific enemies; and the lore behind them, I would recommend Roanoke Gaming. He, simply put, is that biology teacher you never had.

This is already getting longer than I thought it would... haha

gameplay
tldr: Fast, fun, not as fast as Eternal's (hence why play before it)
I remember when I played this through the first time, it was a mad heart-pumping rush. The hyper realism and ongoing action kept me on my... well not toes, per se, but kept me in the action. It's not quite as fast as Doom Eternal's gameplay is, however. Don't take that as discouragement though! Now, in terms of weapons, the majority will be answered with a super shotgun. I can absolutely see why they made balancing changes to Eternal. Other than that, there's a lot of various weapons at your disposal. If you plan on getting eternal, I would recommend to get into the habit of weapon-swapping)

whole take (out-take)
I know I've only played 50.5 hours at the time of writing this, which is nowhere near what I've seen others do, but in my farm-kid-so-free-time-is-rare opinion, this game is great. It runs on relatively low hardware specs (See LowSpecGamer if you have a quite low spec PC... hence the name), and still holds it's own in... whatever year this is, I forget at this point. It's fun, and while development for it has stopped, the servers hosting the online features are still running strong. I do intend on playing more of this, even if I do suck at it.\t\t\t\t\t
