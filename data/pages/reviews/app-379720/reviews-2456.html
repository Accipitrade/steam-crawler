\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOK I think it's time we set the record straight here, children.
1. I like this game (I liked the original and I was likely younger than you are now when I played it.)
2. This game is a reboot (and a dynamic one at that) of the Godfather: Doom.
3. It Captures, Streamlines and Delivers with heavy blows to the face all that was great about the orignal & more.
4. As one who grew up with this and it's clones, I can definitely say this: Shut up. The 90's were fine! But they weren't THAT fine! Anyone who remembers playing this game and the subsequent clones and bigger-better-badder's will recall that in the 90's we valued gameplay (this got it), we valued graphics that redefined what we knew about graphics (close enough,) and if we got a nice soundtrack to boot, all the better (this'll work) - and finally if it had a story and or lore we cared about; SWEET, bonus right there (check.) That being said, the hardcore difficulty WAS there (also here,) and it WAS just that. HARDCORE. But let's not kid ourselves here... - There was (and still is) a HELL and a WORLD of difference between playing against the CPU on Nightmare and then playing Multiplayer against a superior number of other Doom Entusiasts that were better than you. This principle, BTW, stands to this day, in any FPS. You can check. (They don't count if they're NOT better then you, hotshot!)
5. The new content is certainly an improvement. I can certainly "appreciate" the new snapmap function (even if I'm STILL not that big a multiplayer entusiast) it shows that the Devs care for those who do. Also, the lore's gotten more streamlined, even if it IS a bit of a "slam on the brakes" as I've heard tell, I still remember DOOM 3. Trust me, it's gotten better. (One con, I'm missing some of the "mood ligthting" from the older siblings. Especially Doom 3... I know, I know. It's DOOM, not a horror game and I wholeheartedly agree with whoever came up with the difference between Darks Souls and Doom. Still. Meeting the first Imp felt like sort of a letdown... No jumpscare this time... Aw well...) Anyway, the uh, upgrades and what not? - What for? I like the novelty but, come on? I played the original. We found secrets, sure, but aside from that it was Health'n'Armor'n'Guns'n'ammo'n'shoot'em'in'the'face. We wanted an upgrade? Hell no! We wanted a Bigger Friggin' Gun! Only we didn't use "friggin." Period. It's not a con, I just don't see why you wanna fix something that ain't broken. (Ok now I feel old.)
6. To summarize: This DOOM lives up to the name. I'm not gonna be standing in liine at the Devs with burning effigys. That being said: The Devs know this - Doom the godfather was lightning in a bottle and much much more - It was groundbreaking, it was redefining and it was teethgrinding, tongue-biting, blodthirsty grin-like fun! Any remake of it should rate a SOLID A+ or AT THE VERY LEAST a B- in all of the above and so far, I'm not convinced it deserves that - Jury's still out, I think, if nothing else until they put the smackdown on the Series with DOOM: ETERNAL or beyond.
Having vented my fury I'll get to the point: If you played the original you should play this. (I'll let you decide whether to own it. Kinda makes one remember, doesn't it?) If you HAVEN'T played the original... Hell, you should still play this game - Odds are, if somebody let's you play the original you'll likely think "WHAAAAAaaaat? WTF are you on about? This is so ooooold..." ok just stop it right there. Just play the game. And if nothing else, when the time comes you can sit down with the original and, instead, go "ooooooh. That's where it came from..." Leave it at that. Play the game. I will.\t\t\t\t\t
