About me and my gaming experience
I have played the whole doom series. The thing I loved from the very first doom game
into doom 3, was that there was some thinking involved. You had to figure out how
to proceed, and action was halted when you had to figure out how to proceed.

In all doom series before Doom 3, these was normally platforming puzzles in different
flavours, but in Doom 3, there was a lot of thinking involved, which I loved. Same with many
other games in the same genre, for example Half-life series.

Now to why Doom 4 (to not confuse it with the first doom game) is such a bad game:

The Intro
I have always loved the peaceful intro of many games.
Remember the calm intro from Half-life and Half-life 2?
Remember the nice intro of Doom 3, before the action start?
Even gunman chronicles had such a nice peaceful intro.

Such peaceful intros without any action, give plenty of opportunity to explore
the environment, talk to characters, just play around, before you load up and get
ready for action.

This is just taken away in the Doom campaign. Just action from the first seconds in the game.
Not even some initial puzzle, you are just thrown into a room with monsters, and to proceed
you MUST kill all monsters. So boring.

In Doom 3, such moments were very rare, you could very often skip the fights and just continue forward,
lockdowns were only used when you stumbled upon a new enemy or something similiar to that.

Platforming
The platforming is a nice thing in Doom 4, except for one single thing:
Theres actually green lights on every platform where you are supposed to go.

So you never get to think yourself, you are just told "go here", "go here"
like some newbie hint system. And this can't be turned off. So any
advantage the platforming would give the game, is instantly taken away by
not letting the player think for himself.

Interaction with in-game objects
Thats something I find it very boring in Doom 4.

You remember those cool computer panels you had to operate in Doom 3?
Where there was actually interfaces and menus you had to dig down into,
to accomplish your task. Some panels were just for fun, like the shutter control
in the intro or those news panels. And doors didn't open automatically, you had to
try to open the door and see if the PDA or card key you just picked up worked for
that door.

In doom 4? Yeah, now everything is automatic. Every panel is single-button, and
you never get to think. All doors open automatically, and all keys have colors so theres
almost no thinking at all.

And pickupable objects? They have a flashing highlight telling "HERE IS A PICKUPABLE OBJECT"
so you never have to search for yourself. And those highlights cannot be turned off either.

In Doom 3 it also was these code locks where you had to read emails, and listen to audio logs
to find out the correct code, or sometimes you only got a hint, so you had to figure out the rest for yourself, both to doors mandatory to progress, but also ammo boxes and optional secrets.

For example, the removal of filters would be a excellent puzzle. Press the levers in the right order to remove the filter. But instead they made that the guy don't know how to operate equipment and instead kicks the filter into pieces.

Humor
Where is all humor?

In Doom 3, there was a lot of fun moments, for example reading some emails and laughing at them.
There was a lot of humor.

In Doom 4, all that humor is lost. At least in the campain. I could just find some humor in the snapmap editor,
for example the description for "Large electrical spark" was pretty funny.

The whole campain
It was so boring. I played it through just to see if there came anything fun, eg some thinking or puzzle somewhere.
Basically, you only fight waves and waves of monsters. Just run'n'gun.
Nothing more. No thinking - the game helps you with that.
Even if you have every hint turned off, including compass, theres still holograms, "friendlies", green lights and signs that show you way through the maps.
The only thing you do is mash your mouse button. Everything else the game itself help you with.
Why did Bethesdia destroy the Doom series so much? Why couldn't Bethesdia released a sequel to Doom 3 / RoE instead? The Doom 3 engine could have been improved, instead of building a new fighting-only boring engine.

Where's the "minigames"?
You know that crane you had to put the toxic leaking barrels in the garbage to get the air nice in the room to unlock the sealed airlock doors, in doom3.
There was NOTHING such in doom4. How boring!

You know that crane with the cryo chamber bins you had to solve a puzzle in doom3? There was nothing such a thing in Doom4.

Actually, doom4 leaks "boringgas", that you need to use the crane to put the doom4 game package in the garbage before your apartment door opens, because else the air is just full of "boringgas" that makes you very bored.

The positive things
+ Snapmap editor can be pretty fun to play around with.
+ I love that the game senses you are low on health/ammo and then spawns extra health/ammo when you kill enemies,
so being 10 on health and kill one enemy = you are back at like 100 health again.
+ Beutiful graphics.

TLDR
In short, Doom 4 could be best described as a interactive movie, not a game.
Essentially, this is Doom 3 with all the fun parts removed.
Or in other words, a UltraHD version of Boring Sam 3 BFE Edition.

- Haven't tried out the multiplayer yet.
- Just finished the campain.


And can say: This game doesn't deserve 50 GB of my harddrive. If they made it smaller, it could have a place in my harddrive for its snapmap editor and the multiplayer might be fun, but now, 50 GB, and that for a extremely boring campain.
My 1.2 TB Intel 750 storage space is too valuable to store this rubbish.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished the main campaign on the normal difficulty, came in right at about 7 hours.

The only thing that I can really say negatively about the campaign is that its a bit short, however its a hell of a ride the entire time. They really nailed the feel of old school shooter combat with all the modern bells and whistles we enjoy.

Essentially its a corridor followed by a combat arena.. followed by some story and another corridor.. with the combat arenas coming in alot more near the end. That might sound boring but the combat and the frantic "oh my god there are so many of them" feeling of it all is great. There are strategic power ups and such scattered around the arenas so just when you think your getting overwhelmed you can find one and turn the tides.

The sort of odd thing introduced is the "glory kill" mechanic they decided to add. Basically when pretty much ANY monster in the game (including bosses) have taken a certain % of their overall health they enter a stunned stat and flash yellow and blue. If you can get over to them and hit the melee button you will perform a take down move. If you do so the enemy will often drop health and ammo. I found that in order to survive some of the fights I had to take advantage of this especially on the bigger demons because they just gush health when you do this to them.

I thought it would slow combat down but its actually done pretty smoothly. Wading amongst the tides of demons firing into the crowd and hitting the take down button to keep on going is really satisfying and on the bigger threats the animations are pretty elaborate.


The game is also meant to be played through several times and there are many collectables and timed trials and the hardest difficulty mode you are not allowed to die, but when you do a marker is put down in the world noting where you died so when you come at it again you can see if you can get farther. It keeps track of your friends progress as well.


The multiplayer is just ok.. i didn't expect much from a doom game multiplayer wise, its competent and does what it wants to well its just that there is a slew of other arena style shooters coming out and in reality something like Overwatch will be more fun mutliplayer wise.


The story of the campaign overall was.. ok.. its nothing mind blowing.. the expected twists and turns happen and alot of the lore is actually buried in the codex and things you find around the environment. I was a little confused overall because the way the story progresses its almost like we came in the middle part of a story and not at the beginning. Things have already gone to ♥♥♥♥ from second 1 of the story and they just continue to get more crazy as you progress. The story isn't really the focus here it just sort of gives you some motivation, but the combat is the shining star of this one.


If you enjoyed doom in the past and wanted to see a good modern take on it.. pick this up\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that i played the whole game, i will just tell you this if you've played Quake and the old Dooms. Buy. This. Game.

Just like in the trailer i've finished the game in Nightmare difficulty and i felt like i fought like hell.

10/10 Soundtracks

Some people said that it's kinda repetetive but it's Doom so i don't know what they expect. I guess some people prefer cutscenes longer then the game.

Multiplayer kinda seemed like really fast paced and boring at first but i got used to it and instead of playing Deathmatch i prefer to play the other modes cuz deathmatch won't do any good to get used to the multiplayer. The demon power-ups are balanced in terms of every demon's strenght and weakness, you just have to know how to keep up but i know that it's not that easy to keep up.

I was an old-school metalhead but i know listen to all genre. However, the soundtracks got me into the instrumental metal again and in the game everytime i hear BFG edition soundtrack it gives me goosebumps. So even if you don't like that kinda music i guarantee that you'll love the ambient created by the soundtrack in game.

I look forward to try the Ultra Nightmare but i'll try to play snapmaps and multiplayer more. And ofcourse unlocking all the achievements.

This is the Doom that the old school players always wanted. I was hoping that the doom franchise would be remastered but not be a whole new game. However, the tables have turned to be just like hell if you know what i mean. If you played Doom in your childhood and looking to play something like that, don't hesitate to buy this game.

This may be the reason why i quit cs go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs more of a casual gamer these days, I was looking forward to Doom, a bit to see what could be done with the old "blast from the past", and to see how much better it could be.. However, it seems that a bit of the old past leaps up to bite.

The parts I least liked about the original Doom were the "find the colour (x) keycard to get through this door", resulting in wandering round and round and round until you finally got it, then not remembering where the hell the door was, and the "Pixel jumping" platformer activities.. And alas, both of these still crop up.. After soldiering on through a few levels where the key cards were lurking round, and finding my way around the map, I come to the Argent Tower, and that's just plain frustrating..

On the plus side, it looks good, and on the whole doesn't lag (though I've had the odd crash or two).. But not a game I think is worth the asking price. After the good playability of Fallout 4, and the enjoyment I got from that, this rendition of Doom seems to be sadly lacking.\t\t\t\t\t
