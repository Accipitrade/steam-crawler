\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOthers have commented excessively on the game. I own the original Doom II on floppy disks and also Doom 3.

What this game gets right:
- Fast pace but not "twitchy"
- Feel like a hero
- Hurt me plenty was an excellent level of difficulty for me. Die occasionnally but not all the time.
- Great graphics. Excellent multisampling options (including temporal AA), give very smooth image.
- Very fluid engine, does not stumble when several enemies on screen. Vulkan codepath is a bonus for AMD cards.
- Levels are complex but not frustrating.
- Nice easter eggs.
- Audio seems to orient to enemies quite nicely.

Compared with Doom 3, well, it's completely different. Many more enemies, much faster rhythm. No need for a flashlight.

Although I don't particularly like the demon-related setting, this has not stopped me from enjoying the Doom series. This one is a great game overall and gets a solid recommendation. Buy it.

PS. I have tried multiplayer a little bit. Looks nice, feels great (Quake III-style). In my opinion lacks the sophistication of, say, BF4 levels. Can certainly spend a few dozen hours in multiplayer, but would not expect to spend hundreds...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe singleplayer campaign is absolutely phenominal and some of the most fun you can have with a PC in my opinion. If you play it in short bursts then you will not stop loving the fast paced, arena style combat and grotesquely satisfying glory kills. I had originally thought that this quicktime event heavy, canned animation crap looked immature and out of place, but once you get hands on and realise that it actually helps you survive the onslaught you will quickly learn to love it.

The story isn't amazing, but the Doom Marine's attitude towards... well... everything adds a good amount of comic relief.

One word of caution however. DO NOT buy this for the multiplayer because they utterly ♥♥♥♥♥♥ it up. Compared to the wild ride of singleplayer, the multiplayer is slow, repetitive and downright perplexing in some areas. Why for example, did they feel the need to limit players to two weapons? And you can't swap them out for weapons found around the arena, because there aren't any. You chose the two weapons you want before you spawn, and those are the only two you get...

In summary:
Singleplayer = Good
Multiplayer = Awful

Get it for the campaign, preferably on a sale so you don't feel cheated paying full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who's been playing Doom since our family got its first computer in 1994, I have to say that this game feels very right in the franchise and I love it as much as the original games.

One thing people don't get about the original Doom games when they say that they're "old-school games about mowing down tons of enemies" is that they're not giving those games enough credit. Doom 1 and 2 had a visceral pleasure of high action and violence, but they were also well-designed around examining threats and adjusting tactics based on level design, resources, and the numbers and types of enemies. This is what was missing in Doom 3 for me, as most combat in that placed you in tight rooms where enemies tried to give you hugs.

Doom 4 so perfectly encapsulates the strategic and tactical elements of Doom 1 and 2 in a modern way. Levels are tightly designed, and enemies have different abilities and behaviors in combat that you have to learn and adapt to in order to win.

There's exploration, incredibly gratifying and well-designed combat, beautiful visuals and sound, and interesting gameplay mechanics like finishing moves that provide extra health or chainsaw kills that provide you with extra ammo (to name just a few)

My only real complaints come in form of a progression system that kind of breaks the difficulty curve in a few spots and the fact that the multiplayer is very dull. The former isn't a huge complaint, but the latter is a big mark. The multiplayer is slow-paced and uses pre-set loadout systems that remove that fun aspect of old arena shooters of exploring maps to find different weapons to strategically blow your opponents the h*ck up.

I can heartily recommend DOOM's singleplayer campaign to anyone looking for an incredibly fun, challenging shooter. It's one of the best games I've played in recent years, and it's frankly spoiled me on a shooter games at the moment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere's the thing. Sometimes I hear people trashing this game that really loved old original DOS Doom. I get the feeling that they haven't actually played it, maybe only watched videos instead of feeling it for themselves.

This game absolutely reeks of effort. Everything in it feels deliberate; enemies are introduced in a paced, balanced way, guns are all equally fun to use but encourage differing your playstyle to have an easier time in a new situation, and most importantly, it makes your peen feel like it's the size of a god dang Arizona can. So many parts of this game feel like they're just in place so you can feel a tiny bit more like a badass, and the result of all of those parts is an experience that keeps your blood pumping and your eyes from blinking.

If I were to list a complaint, I would say the spreading out of the items and giving the player a map of where to find them kind of kills the pacing of a few levels and encourages groping around to try to find that little trinket, which feels just asinine after awhile.

Still, that doesn't ruin the overall experience I had with it. Good game, 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t
