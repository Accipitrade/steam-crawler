\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGlory kills? Ha, more like GORY kills, amiright?
For the longest time I didn't buy this game because the special death animations seemed too sick and disgusting and up-close-and-personal. Maybe they would give me nightmares? Perhaps I could tolerate them, but why should I have to? And wasn't I way past playing a re-make of Doom anyway?
Noooo.
I don't know if it was because the end of 2021 had been pretty hellish, or because I had free time and thought "to HELL with it", but I caved and bought Doom 2016, and now... no regrets.

Gameplay
By crikey is this an intense game. Enjoyable in the sense that one night you realise you're raging that the Hell Guard has smashed you into pixel pulp for the 15th time in a row and you're still going to back and try again RIGHT NOW DAMMIT. It's your traditional Doom run-and-gun against large numbers of enemies gameplay. Although the largest number of enemies you face at any one time is nowhere near the number pink demons unleashed in a single room in some of the old Doom levels. I suspect some games consoles and PCs might keel over in molten copper if there were 50 demons running around in full 3d animation, so dropping quantity and gaining quality is fine here.

I'm not convinced that some weapons work better at some enemies than other weapons. e.g. rockets are not a good choice against Hell Knights, as these guys move towards you too fast and take a lot of rockets before going down, but rockets are fine against Cacodemons. That's just because Hell Knights have more hit points. A more powerful weapon is more effective against every enemy, but some monsters are tougher than others, and the weapon type doesn't trump that. Just don't waste rockets and plasma balls against weak enemies.
And don't use up your BFG shots unless you absolutely need it.

Weapons and Items
All the old weapons are here, though they are not bound to the same keys as the original. eg '5' is not the rocket launcher. Mostly I used the mouse wheel to switch between them anyway.
Plus they all have an optional upgrade tree which add extra powers and effects. For example one upgrade available for the chaingun allows spinning up the barrels before firing starts, which helps puts a high rate of fire into the target when you only have a short time out of cover. I had a tonne of upgrades by the end of the game, but I don't think I used more than about 4 of them regularly.

The old powerups are also present in the set-piece battle arenas that occur frequently in the game. I would often leave them for later thinking I would only use a Quad Damage or Berserker if I was low on ammo and other options for big bad guys, and then would beat all the enemies without using any powerups. Kindof a lost opportunity, so use them any time you get more than one Hell Knight or Cyber-Mancubus at a time.
In a slight break from tradition one of the powerups is basically the Quad Damage from the Quake series, which was not in the original Doom, but heyyy we don't mind.

Enemies
Most enemies from the original 1993 shareware trilogy and Doom II combined are here, however there is (thankfully) no Arch-Vile in this game, and no mini arachnotrons either. The boss monsters are encountered in the same order as the original. Some originals such as the Heavy Weapons Dude have been re-imagined as lightly-armoured soldiers carrying laser-like weapons instead of the original chaingun. There are also some entirely new ones, such as the Summoners, which seem to teleport around the place and direct waves of destructive energy at you.
They are all very well animated.

Maps
All environments are quite detailed and realistically rendered, though finding the secret rooms with original 1993 style blocky textures is cool too. The levels are very linear in navigation, but exploration is not the focus of the game. Hell is done quite well, with decent fire effects and rather good looking lava in places. As if the levels weren't linear enough, the pattern of the way forward being lit by green glowing lights/candles is kinda clunky and unrealistic once you notice that's been put there.

Sound
Well this is almost the best part. The music is absolutely on point. Heavy metal riffs all the way. The demonic sounds are also nicely done. Voice acting is also good.

Bottom Line
Why buy this game? Simple. It's a good way to vent frustration and blow up some demons.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI waited many years to play this one, and my expectations were admittedly high. Sadly, they were not met, at all. The game is much slower than expected, the level design is a mess, and consists of finding your way to the next "arena" by using an unclear map and a destination pointer. When you find it, you're locked in until everything is dead. Then repeat, forever. While the music is amazing, it's often not there at all, and the sound design was a huge miss for me. The first 4 weapons all felt underwhelming, even after a few upgrades.

The "glory kill" system was always presented as non intrusive and "flowing perfectly" with the combat, but that also never really worked as expected, it creates so many unnecessary pauses, and sometimes break the flow of combat by making you unsure about where your character is actually positioned.

Also, the title screen still shows advertising to preorder stuff released 2 years ago. The overall menus were a bit of a mess too, took some time just to find where the settings were, and then even longer to find out how to set resolution to ultrawide. I'd expect games to detect and set that automatically nowadays instead of making you waste a long time in menus just to set the game resolution to match your monitor.\t\t\t\t\t
