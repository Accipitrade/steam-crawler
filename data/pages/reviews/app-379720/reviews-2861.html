\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is what games used to be back in the mid 90's to mid 2000's before all the DLC, microtransactions, online, unfinished products, "early access," pre-order, uninspired stories and stuff. A game made with love and dedication without the obvious love for milking $$$ that companies now put as their top priority. It's all about gamplay and enjoying the title at its full potential since day-one.

I'm in my 7th hour right now (I know, not much), but I already feel like if I'm back in the golden age of gaming. The only thing that kills this feeling is the BREATHTAKING SUPERB graphics.

Simple and right to the point, this story is fantastic at going right at the blood-pumping action with no BS. Game mechanics are fluid and responsive, sound will absorb you and graphics made you wish you could go slower to appreciate the magnificent details.

Only things to watch out: this game while amazingly optimized, won't run smoothly on potatoes as many say. Anything below the minimum will suffer. My spare rig with a GTX 750ti, 12gb ram and some 3.0Ghz i5 can't even get passed the Bethesda screen.

On the other hand, my main rig with a GTX 1070, 20gb ddr4 and an overclocked 6600k @ 4.2Ghz absolutely destroys this game with nightmare settings. Running constant 144fps (had to cap it because otherwise it would reach in the uper 170's, making my gpu scream for mercy).

This is by far the smoothest, and most beautiful game I've ever ran in my computer. This is also the most complete and single-player oriented game I've played in more than a decade.

Other companies should learn from this game... DOOM 2016: Making gaming great again!\t\t\t\t\t

But with the mass gushing about this game and a very tempting Steam sale, I decided that I must know.

This game is intense, I felt like if I played for more than an hour my head may explode, in a good kind of way this game made me into a fearless demon killing machine.

Everything about the shooting, movement level and enemy design was spot on, everything just felt like it was exactly as hard as it needed to be without being overly punishing, just fun, the weapon design and really everything about the weapons was great.

The story was fun, I think I went in with high expectations and while the story doesn't touch even mediocre adventure games, it was good enough and felt very new to me.

One hard crash and multiple instances where my weapon just stopped working and wouldn't switch was annoying.

What absolute garbage the multiplayer in this game is, Going from playing Overwatch daily to this is like a slap in the face. Any game where you can dump into an afk guy then have him suddenly jump into action and one shot me is complete garbage as far as I'm concerned.

Overall this game is an enjoyable experience and I'll happily play what comes next.\t\t\t\t\t
