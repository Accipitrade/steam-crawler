\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 2016 is almost universally beloved for a lot of reasons, but if you're like me and cut your teeth on early arena-style FPS games, you'll immediately understand why I loved this one.

Doom 3 was a mixed bag when it came out in 2004, and while I personally enjoyed it for what it was, I can comfortably say that it was not a Doom game. The cramped level design and cumbersome flashlight mechanic meant that it was deprived of one of the major things that made Doom enjoyable, which is to say its freedom of movement. Doom 2016, however, brings those elements back in a big way.

Combat flows in a remarkably smooth way. Weapon switching is quick, strategically-used glory kills can give you a decisive advantage with invincibility frames, and the amount of alternate weapon modes gives you a lot of choice. Bringing back arena-style combat with quick movement, platforming elements, and classic power-ups was exactly the breath of fresh air the franchise needed to come back strong.

There's a story, but it doesn't matter too much. Humanity has screwed everything up again by opening a doorway to Hell, and Doomguy, now called the Doomslayer, has to clean up the mess. He is immediately over it, and sets his sights on destroying everything even vaguely Hell-related. Along the way, bits of lore flesh out the Doom universe and give life to the story around you -- but if you so choose, you can ignore every last bit of it.

My one gripe with this game is that once you've beaten it, you've beaten it. 100% all the secrets, which isn't terribly difficult, and you'll be rewarded with neat goodies and a profound sense of accomplishment. Going through it again is just as fun, but won't really offer much that you didn't experience the first time through. A solid 20 hours of gameplay more than justifies the current price tag, which at the time of writing sits at twenty dollars, and will certainly decrease further with the upcoming 2019 Summer Sale. I bought it at release with no regrets -- it's great fun, and fun enough to replay it even after you know all its little twists and turns.

Gorgeous graphics, smooth performance with the Vulkan API, and good old-fashioned demon-slaughtering violence. Looking forward to Doom Eternal.\t\t\t\t\t
