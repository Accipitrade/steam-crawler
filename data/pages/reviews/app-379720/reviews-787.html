\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis FPS throws away several tropes from "regular" shooters. There's no weapon reload; aim does not get worse when you're walking; you carry all your 10 weapons at the same time; the chainsaw is an instakill which drops ammunition for the guns; and so on. Getting ammunition this way feels very "gamey" (there's even an in-game nod at this in the lore of Slayer's Testament IV), but the end result is an action-packed game that has you killing demons with your current ammunition then sawing them open to get more ammunition to kill more demons. It feels awesome getting the Berserk powerup and annihilating dozens of powerful demons in a couple of seconds.

The leveling system feels a bit unnecessary, but I liked that the last step of completely leveling up each weapon required me to complete certain challenges (for example, killing 2 demons with a single Super Shotgun shot 25 times), and the last step is the largest power spike.

I only played the single-player campaign. There are a few platforming sections which are annoying before getting double jump, but they did not distracted me from the rest of the game. And I found the collectibles to be non-obtrusive, too; maybe because the story revolves around the Doomguy, without events that "will happen now, act quickly"?

The game felt very balanced, and it is an enjoyable experience overall.\t\t\t\t\t
