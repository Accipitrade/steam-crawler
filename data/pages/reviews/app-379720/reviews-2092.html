\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is absolutely ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ incredible.
Played through countless times on Xbox and bought on PC partly just to write a review, also wanted to see what it was like to play with mouse and keyboard.
SO, why is doom "absolutely ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ incredible"? Because it easily has the best combat in any shooter released potentially ever, definitely in this decade. The campaign, bar around 5 minutes total of a bit of context, is as fluid as a poo after a curry. By that I mean it is flowing, brutal, satisfying as hell (pun 100% intended) and you are probably sweating when you are finished. The glory kills are brutal and there is enough variation to make you want to experiment and try every possibility. However, breaking one demons jaw and punching an other's head into a wall will always be satisfying no matter how many times you do it. They are bloody, and beautiful like mortal kombat without ever feeling like they linger on them. They always move on quick to whatever is next without losing the tiniest bit of momentum. The gun play is as incredible and satisfying as the glory kills, stitching the kills together perfectly. The upgrades to weapons also make sure that older guns don't become redundant unlike some games where you go through guns or other weapons like prawn crackers at a chinese. Add all of this to quick and precise movement, you have yourself a near perfect game.
The focus is purely on game play, with the story presenting itself and normally literally getting punched in the face by the doom slayer, so you can get to the action, but still allows the story to present itself so the player knows what is going on. The story is basic and clear, serving it's purpose of throwing the player into the combat which is ultimately what games are about, narrative serving as a reason to take part in the game play.
The multiplayer is fairly lackluster to be honest, so if you are going to get it for that, maybe wouldn't recommend as the campaign is the clear focus, and is phenomenal as a result, the multiplayer feeling forced, but still halfway decent, not making full use of the mechanics that are so incredibly implemented into the main story.
Their are collectibles and achievements that warrant multiple play-throughs if the game play somehow didn't tempt you back to play again.
The soundtrack is also incredible. I personally don't like/hate metal, yet somehow it fits so well with this game, particularly the visuals which make sure that if you were to take a freeze frame at any point, it could probably be used as a metal album cover.

To summarise, BUY THE DAMN GAME, YOU FOOL!!! It is easily the best shooter to come out of the industry, has won multiple awards for that exact reason and playing it you can see why. If you say you don't like Doom, and haven't played it, sorry to say it pal, but you are the least qualified person to deliver an opinion. Play the game.
PLAY IT!!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(I have not played multiplayer, so this is a review of the Campaign)
I cannot overstate how good this game is. I bought it in 2019 and it is definitely one of my favorites now.

Combat is great, period. Which is good because that's what this games purpose is. The game has a very satisfying game play loop which never overstays its welcome.

The graphics in this game are something else... textures are good of course, but that's not what I care about. The animation and stability of this game is what I absolutely love. All of the animations in this game are snappy and to the point. Exactly what you would expect from the Doom Slayer who would rather be shooting holes in demons than doing something like switching attachments on weapons. With stability, I don't know if its the talented engineers over at Id or a testament to the Vulkan api, but I ran this game at my native 144hz refresh rate the entire game on my Vega 56, even in the most intense parts. For how good this game looks and how hectic it can get, this was a lovely surprise.

Sound wise I have nothing but the best to say, except for two weapon. The shotgun and the super shotgun. Their sounds feel weak compared to what their models are showing. I hope in Doom Eternal they update the sound of the shotguns to give them a bit more of that shotgun feel that makes me love them so much in other games and in real life.

Speaking of Doom Eternal, I can't wait now. In the words of the wise Reggie: "My body is ready!"
Get this game on an SSD, trust me you wont regret it. Try to have 70-90 GB available for it.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t
