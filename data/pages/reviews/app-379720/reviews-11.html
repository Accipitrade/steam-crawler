\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimple running and shooting, how fun can it be? Here is the Doom--Cyber-hell monsters, Glory Kill, Sci-fi world saving story and heavy metal music. The level design is remarkable. Besides the arenas we are mainly hooked in, each map is carefully designed where we can slow down to search for upgrades and collectibles, I like the difference of pace. The guns are various but not redundant. It is one the most difficult game I have ever played, since I have never played souls-like games. Glad I pick the ultra-violence difficulty, I can struggle to finish it rather than give up halfway.

minor problems
1. We can be attacked during interaction with object or glory kill. I don't know, maybe I am used to being invulnerable while interaction in other games.
2. We have no idea how many monsters are left, so in the early missions we may run out of ammo due to "give it our all" at the beginning. After we become familiar with the monsters and acquire BFG, things could be easier.
3. Me and the boss both happen to be stuck once, seems a bug but not a big problem.
4. Last boss, I thought it may transform and I have to kill it twice like the previous bosses, but no, its death suddenly makes me feel easy after I get accustomed to the fierce battle.

Major problems
1. Like Ori, its checkpoints are fixed location rather than events, if we take a detour to explore and die before the next checkpoint(note there are some long checkpoints involving battles as well), well, sent back.
2. We can't go back in some non-boss missions, which means another run if we miss some collectible. Simply unfriendly.

I definitely enjoy this game but considering those problems and its sequel Eternal(still a single player game) requires to login an online account, I don't plan to play.\t\t\t\t\t
