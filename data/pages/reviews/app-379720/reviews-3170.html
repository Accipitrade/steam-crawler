\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit 13.07.2016 after Vulcan AMD drivers update 16.7.2

Praise to Vulcan!! This last update turned the game from utter broken and useless waste of space on the SSD to one of the best single player shooters! Still got couple of crashes, but at least now the PC can recover without need for a total reboot and can load in back quickly. Aaaleluya!
I hope this Vulcan thing catches on to further more titles. It feels that performance is higher in the multiples, much more smooth and less variable fps. Some bugs still occure like map overview fps drop, but that is not too worrying.

Thank you, id and AMD. I wonder what would the effect be if this was available at release. Would nVidia qq and boycot the game? Would there be less hate on how broken it is? I may never know, but I guess I couldn't care less at this point. Satisfied finally!

The hell, even the beta was running better. Actually, I had no problems running the beta. Not a single one (from technical point of view. game design is another story)!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, if you're a good FPS fan: BUY THIS THING RIGHT NOW.

This is a "back to basics" game, and is a direct homage to original Doom/Doom 2.
General level design changed (you wouldn't expect for the game to be a carbon copy of the classics, would you?), it's more of an arena shooter now. You can't just run around the map, pick off only a few monsters, get the key(s) and rush to the exit. Now, you get locked in a general area, with a lot of enemies spawning in, and cannot get out until you kill them all. In the end you're almost always forced to clean the level in order to progress. But that's not necessarily a bad thing, because of...

...combat mechanics. Oh boy, that changed very little. Old style at its core, just plain fun.
Let me detail this by listing what ISN'T in the game. There is:
- No regenerating health;
- No sprint button (just a "walk" key that serves no purpose whatsoever in SP);
- No falling damage;
- No "cover mechanics";
- No reload button. Yes, no reload button.

Gameplay is so old style that you could play the classic maps on this and feel right at home. Which they actually did, by the way - classic maps are unlockable secrets throughout the game.
Just run, dodge, aim and shoot. And jump. A lot. There's plenty of platforming to be done, but I never had any problems with it - there's a "grab ledge" movement that help things out a lot, and a few levels in, you also get a double jump. I can't remember a single time that I accidentally fell while platforming.

General aesthetics still resemble much of Doom 3, with that mix of modern machinery with Mars landscapes with gore-y hell flesh and fire. The enemies are a lot more faithful to the originals tough, in appearence and behaviour - more "demon" than "alien". And the levels, obviously, are way more open and spacious now, no more claustrophobic dark corridors.

Graphics are absolutelly stunning and detailed. And it runs so fast that I don't know how. On my i5 + GTX970 (which by mid-2016 is more mid-end than high), with everything maxed out @ 1080p, I've only seen FPS fall down from 60 ONCE - and that was to 50. Even during large battles it keeps steady.

The plot... well, there is a plot. Corrupt corporation does experiences on Mars blah blah... But it's just an excuse to slay demons - you can tell that even Doomguy (or "Doom Slayer", as he's sometimes known ingame) doesn't seem to care much about the people around him, just want to kill more bad guys.

About multiplayer... I can't say anything about it since I didn't try it out. But the SP campaign is really pretty solid, and in my opinion already makes the game worth the buy. MP would be just a plus

All in all, I largely recommend this game to everyone that likes a good action FPS - more so to fans of the originals.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a long time people werent too confident in new Doom, mainly because of Bethesdas horrid marketing that made it look like a brown and slow mess. Thankfully new Doom is nothing like this and is another addition to the great single-player FPSes of recent years alongside Dooms sister series Wolfenstein, Shadow Warrior, and Metro: Last Light. Its also pretty much the best Painkiller game too.

New Doom is a great blending of the old and the new just like Wolfenstein: The New Order. It has a lot of modern conveniences such as generous checkpoints but it still has some old school sensibilities in how it plays. There is no regenerating health, you got to find packs or get them off of enemies. The game gives some of the best mobility Ive seen in an FPS, theres no cowering behind waist high walls here. Doomguy runs by default and stuff like double jumps and ledge grabs add to this mobility even more. The levels are quite sizable and allow you to maneuver around the arenas in multiple ways. The weapons are real fun to use, especially the Super Shotgun.

Doom has a rather long campaign, if you blaze throughout itll probably take you around ten hours. But theres a lot of secrets to find in the levels, including ones that unlock classic Doom levels that you can play akin to playing through Wolfenstein 3D in TNO/The Old Blood. So that can definitely extend your playtime. Theres also a level creator so you can play other peoples maps as well as make your own. Theres multiplayer, and while I havent touched it yet, it seems like its already been dead due to Bethsedas misplaced belief that people wanted to have a multiplayer mode in a Doom game.

Dooms writing is good in the sense that its unobtrusive and helps the game along. Its not like New Order which actually had some solid writing, but the game knows people just want to rip and tear some demons so it doesnt take itself too seriously. Theres also shoutouts to stuff from earlier games as well.

While Doom is a great game I ran into several bugs, three of which caused CTDs and the other two broke the game. (I couldnt shoot my guns after going into a teleporter while switching my guns as well as one where the switch to activate a door didnt appear.) It didnt really mar the experience but its the only flaw it had. Also I wish Bethesda would make single-player DLC maps instead of just having the Season Pass be all multiplayer stuff.

So in the end, Doom is a damn fine game and a must play for single-player FPS fans.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI takes all the pros from games like the original Doom, and adds in the things tested to work from newer games as well without adding in the things in newer games that where horrible. Map design is great, it is what made the original Doom so good and these maps are no different, I only ended up finding 4 of the 8 secret areas in the second map when I went through it and I am the type of person who will search an entire empty map trying to find all the nooks and crannies of Doom maps. The weapons all so far feel really enjoyable to play with, even the pistol can be enjoyable. I mean my biggest complaint with weapons is just that I don't find that the scarcity of ammo is quit scarce enough atleast in the early levels on what would be normal difficulty.

With maps, how weapons feel, and ammo scarcity covered time for health. The way it is designed actually feels really good, there is a ton of health and a fair bit of armor around all over the place and you get health back whenever you melee an enemy to death but that in no way makes it easy. It is very much a game where you need to be constantly moving quickly in basicly large arenas or you die really quickly, yes there is a lot of health about but you loss it so quickly that you constantly need to be putting yourself in harms way to finish off that enemy with melee to get more health back, its very much a risk reward type situation, by no means does the ample amount of health drops make it easy.

All in all if you loved the original Doom and its map design and like constantly having to be moving where stoping to try to aim basicly spells death you will enjoy this game. Like I said, on easier difficulties there is a bit to much ammo to feel like the original Doom but I guess that was also true of the easiest difficulty in Doom as well it wasn't till higher difficulties that ammo scarcity and conserving good ammo for tighter spots was as big of a part of the game, so I guess I can not fully complain about how much ammo there is until I play it on a higher difficulty.\t\t\t\t\t
