\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is everything you liked about the original doom and more.

- Run around at insane speeds
- No wussy reloading or cover mechanics
- No ducking behind a chest-high wall waiting for a shield to recharge
- Wicked weapons
- Graphics that will blow your mind
- Leaderboards, challenges, and time attack modes to add replayability
- Demon smashing fun

Forget Doom 3. This is the sequel that Doom 2 deserved.

Unlike most AAA titles, there are very few cutscenes and no quicktime events to ruin the fun... and unlike most Bethesda games, this one runs just fine on high refresh rate monitors. The controls are razor sharp.

...and the engine? Wow. It supports my 144hz monitor no problem. HEY BETHESDA! WHEN CAN WE HAVE A PORT OF SKYRIM THAT DOES THIS?!

If you only play one shooter this year, play this. It's the best FPS I've played in years.

It's not perfect, though. The glory kills (brief noninteractive animations) do interrupt the flow a bit, but they're optional and you can get a powerup to speed them up a lot... and the game starts with an unskippable intro video that takes forever. Even if you mod the game to disable the intro video, you'll be looking at a black screen for a full minute before the game starts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI did not like the multiplayer much when I tried it and got destroyed, so I'm not going to be speaking about that.

The singleplayer mode, though, damn. I really enjoyed the crap out of that, and would recommend the game solely so you can play the campaign. The game feels very DOOM and took me back to when I used to play The Ultimate Doom as a nipper.

It is intense, I recommend wearing headphones with the volume slightly too high. It is smooth, it is fast, it gives you the perfect control that you want to have in an FPS game. You will use that control to brutally destroy one demon after another, using a plethora of different, interesting weapons. Including a chainsaw. And your fists. Which you will use to rip out the heart of a demon and shove it down it's throat. Brutally. Do demons have hearts? You don't care.

There is a story too, sort of. It's not very interesting. But I never cared about that in the first place when I picked up a DOOM game.

I mostly played the game in 1-2 hour spurts, after which I usually felt like taking a break. But then I was always excited to return to the game the next day.

I only got about 15 hours of play out of the campaign on "Hurt me Plenty" setting, but it still left me very satisfied with my purchase.

I sohuld mention I got the game when the summer sale was on, so it was slightly cheaper.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually post reviews, but I felt like as such a big fan of the original when I was a child - I had to nod my head at this game. If you enjoyed the original - this game somehow captures the acrade feel of the original.

As in all games - it ramps up the difficulty as you get farther in and it does so at a great pace. You build up your favorite weapons and armor as you go and the RPGish upgrade points you acquire along the way are fun too.

The story isn't the driver in this game - it's the pure fun. Either way I felt like the story was actually well done for what it could be.

I don't want to ramble on. Just buy this game if you enjoy fast paced FPS gameplay. It is great.\t\t\t\t\t
