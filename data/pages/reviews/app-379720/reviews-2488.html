\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 4/Doom 2016 is an excellent reboot/sequel for the legendary Doom franchise. It is a HUGE improvement over Doom 3. Doom 3, to me, was mediocre. It was slower paced and not as action packed as the previous Doom games. I think that fans were expecting that Doom 3 was going to continue the style of fast paced, intense action from the previous Doom games. It didn’t. So Doom 3, to me, was mediocre. Anyway, back to Doom 4. Doom 4 brings back the fast paced, intense action that fans loved. It didn’t take me too long to realize that Doom 4’s gameplay is not like Doom 3 was. The levels are well made. The graphics are great. The music is awesome. And the gameplay is top notch. Doom 4 also has good multiplayer and a SnapMap mode that allows you to create your own levels/maps, share them with others, and play other people's levels/maps as well. Doom 4 also has a lot of throwbacks to the previous Doom games that fans will recognize and like. All of the classic Doom weapons and Doom enemies are back. Doom 4 keeps you engaged all the way from start to finish. The gameplay pretty much consists of putting you in big areas and you have to eliminate all of the enemies in that area. Then proceeding on in the level and doing that again and again. It may sound like it’s too repetitive, but it’s not really that much different compared to the gameplay of the previous Doom games. The frequent enemy battles are intense and they get progressively harder and more intense as you progress through the game. It works so well for Doom 4. Doom 4 is easily one of the best games that I have ever played. Doom 4 is the game that Doom 3 should have been. Doom 4 is an excellent example of a reboot/sequel done right. Doom 4 is the most intense and engaging FPS game that I have played since Half Life 2. I highly recommend Doom 4! It’s phenomenal!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDear God, this version is absolutely gorgeous. I mean, in that "everything is cold and hard and technological, or harsh and deathly and oozing blood--or out to drink it" sort of way, I guess. The art, the textures, the physics, the music, the easter eggs and subtleties...absolutely amazing. I was a kid when the first games came out. I read the books and spent countless hours looking for secrets. I'm kind of surprised Doomguy never talks in this entire game, but the designers did a really great job with his presence throughout the story, regardless. I'm really proud of id and Bethesda's work in this version & I really, really hope the saga continues.

Mick Gordon's soundtrack is flawless. You can find it on Apple Music, btw.

There are some cheesier aspects to the game, such as the kinds of ledge-grabs Doomguy can make from some really epic falls...ones that, as a rock climber & parkour amateur, I can confidently say would rip the fingers off of any human--Praetor suit or not. Doomguy's not alone, though--seems like every Assassin and Templar running in the streets has superhuman fingers, too.

It's kinda funny how glory kills work in this game. They're brutal, and sometimes downright hilarious, but it's almost kind of ridiculous how you can crack open a demon and healing pods pop out. Or ammo. Or that a group of charging Hell Knights and Pinkies will kind of wait alongside until Doomguy finishes up beating a Possessed with its own arm. I like to think they kind of enjoy watching it enough to stop fighting for a moment...kinda like "ooo, nice."...that makes it seem more like a feature than a bug.
I also like how you have to be careful not to get too close to demons during a battle--and watch your back, because they can do glory kills, too. Way more exciting.

The Imps are finally super badass. I love it. They're smart, they're fast and they will ♥♥♥♥ you up if you aren't careful. I have a love/hate relationship with the Summoners...they're way more intense than in previous games and they, too, will make your life hell if you don't go after them, first. The Hell Knights got really impressive, and the new Barons of Hell have this dark majesty and grace about them that somehow distills the entire essence of the game's legacy. The new Cacodemons still reign supreme as the most bizzare mashup of scary and ridiculous that I have ever seen in a game or movie. I mean, wtf?? I remember being scared of them as a kid, even in the first and second games; now they're one of my favorites, purely because of how floaty and blimpy and kind of unfortunately endowed they are, but you still don't want to hug one. Anything with that many teeth and psychoactive loogies could still eat your head whole.

The backstory gets a really dignified treatment in this newer version, too. I missed some of the "middle" games that came out on earlier platforms, and I'm not sure how much they were able to cover, but this new version really starts to raise a lot of questions about Doomguy, himself: how long has he been at this? Is he some form of demon, himself? Is his life some long string of continuous demon extermination? Is he somehow stuck doing this for all of eternity--that he's forced to come back after each death and continue where he left off? I hope that subsequent releases can explore his backstory a bit more, and let the circumstances between him and Samuel Hayden mature into something really amazing.

Definitely get this game if you're a fan of previous releases. Play it with headphones if you want to be impressed with the sound design.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is extremely well developed:

Graphics - 10/10
Environments - 10/10
Gameplay (as long as you understand that this is a run-and-gun-kill-everything sort of game) - 9/10
Story line - 5/10

The game from the beginning to the end is centered around killing literally everything in your path. There are a few side challenges that you can complete, and also an abundance of secrets and hidden upgrades that you can search for in each level. You're offered a large variety of guns and very rarely run out of ammo (that can't be replenished quite soon following a fight). The decals and environment work is truly the best I have ever seen. Mad props to the developers and creative team - this game breaks barriers in visual aspects and is absolutely stunning at 4K on ultra. Only downside I really noted was that you're sort of immediately thrown into killing stuff with not much background information on why or how, and this persists until the end of the game (however the story does develop in a similar fashion to Doom 3).

Very recommended as a casual game. If you enjoyed Doom 3 you will enjoy this one too.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never rush to move onto the next gen. I usually hang tough awhile until a few titles come along and I say "ok, now I'm sold". Doom was single handedly that title.
The plot is pointless, I think "shoot demons until they go back or until their all dead or both" sums up the whole thing nicely.
Doom's all about the gameplay. You might pull the occasional lever and push the odd switch but that's all for the sake of moving you from one arena to another. You enter an area and all of a sudden the room locks down, floods with all enemy types and you literally don't let off the trigger until their all dealt with. No reloading, no health regen. Health and ammo pick ups are orbs dropped from the bodys of demons, if you glory kill you get a much better drop. So if your down to your last 5% health and only 1 shot left in the shotgun, you'll make a charge at the nearest demon screaming like a savage madman for that drop. Pulling that kind of banzai crazy ♥♥♥♥ off is the thrill that alot of other shooters are missing. You really feel like the badest ♥♥♥♥♥♥ on mars.
Graphically, it looks great, I think it runs unbelievably well, even with all the madness and fast pace it doesn't skip a beat.
If your into shooters I think this is an absolute must have title.

I've never played the multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t
