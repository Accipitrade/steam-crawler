\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
+ singleplayer campaign is fun
+ boss fights are fun
+ rune trials are a fun distraction
+ powerup system keeps the game interesting
+ nice to see Doom/DoomII classic monsters
+ the arch-vile has been replaced by the summoner (good call)
+ it has multi-player modes
+ it has snapmap for creating multi-player maps

Cons:
- missed having some of the classic monsters reintroduced (pain elemental, arachnotron)
- snapmap can only have AI in deathmath mode (seriously WTF)
- awards and medals in multi-player mode don't get awarded against AI opponents
- you have weapon loadouts
- NO COOP MODE OF ANY KIND

They took a game known for its multi-player and strengthened its single-player.
In the process they neutered its multi-player mode for people that want to play coop vs bots (which seems bizarre considering the emphasis on single-player)

EDIT: SECOND PLAYTHROUGH ADDENDUM

Pros:
+ single player campaign holds up pretty decently

Cons:
- most of the rune trials are ... not well done
- the lull-arena-lull-arena pattern deflates its re-playability
- no bot AI for snapmap maps and non-deathmatch modes (this is the same company that owns QuakeIII Arena, Quake Live, and Quake Champions)

UPDATE:
After playing Doom Eternal, this game shows us what the future of the franchise will be.
* huge levels
* arena-corridor-arena gameplay
* more platforming
* beefier weapons
* beefier enemies

After thinking about it some there are 3 "eras" of Doom so far... canonically, they're all a single timeline but to me, that timeline doesn't make a lick of sense, and they are all very different game play wise. So I tend to think of them as separate, but similar, realities.

Era 1: Doom/Doom II/Doom 64
Era 2: Doom 3
Era 3: Doom (2016)/Doom Eternal (or as I call them now Dewm, and Dewm Eternal)\t\t\t\t\t

The characters are written well for the dialoge that is in the story. But the gameplay is what takes center stage. Doom returns to the fps genres roots with health packs, extra armor, and WAY more then 2 weapons. It 100% works as well, the enimes are challenging and the gameplay feels so unrestricted. I don't want to go to in detail with it because 1. I hope that you'll play it yourself and 2. Its simplictity is the hook of the gameplay. If you were making the first fps ever with current day graphics engines and you made it well, you would get this game. The multiplayer is also fun it's nothing groundbreaking but it definetly has some replayability. Doom's gamplay I personally think is best suited for it's story rather then it's multiplayer. There's nothing particularly wrong with it but it's nothing that hasn't been done by every othe sci-fi shooter out there. It doesn't have that unique feeling the campaign has. Double jump, advanced mobilty, symmetrical maps, somewhat unbalanced weapons. It is a fun time despite this though with solid sound design, very good moment to moment encounters, and maps that at the very least work around Doom's movement style. But agian it has been done better elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts was around 20 years after the original doom came out that this game was released. The orginal currently runs on my phone and with head phones is still as impactful now as it was then.
Which is why the fact that I played this remake for twenty minutes and was disappointed about how muted and meh it felt.

Ignoring the fact that the orginal doom basically ran on anything and this version is unoptimised and strangly has high hardware demands, my main complaint is it doesn't feel like doom.

It's hard to explain as it is missing visceral part that makes it feel impactful.

Maybe comparing and contrasting the start of doom, with the start of the remake.

In the orginal it starts with you in an empty room, straight away high enemy heavy metal starts to play. You have complete control from the start, movement is fast. as you leave the room you hear the moans and groans of the monsters before you you see them, you enter a room with a path going through a pool of lava, across the pool is a hairy ape creature throwing fire balls, you fire at it. The pistol has slight recoil which has slight push back and clear impactful sounds. Following this you enter a room which is completely dark, accept for one area lit up in the centre. Starting there is another human. when you get close to the human you realise it is a zombie.
Again the shots feel impactful, something which is highlighted by the monster moaning and reacting to the shots.
This entire sequence happens in the first four minutes of the game. The music, the controls and how reactive they are, the monsters from the fore shadowing, to how they act, etc all give a high energy, high control impactful experience.

Now in this remake, it starts instead with your character restrained, a monster approaches and with out any real player control the main character crushes it's skull. The music is either amospheric or non exist depending on how charitible your feeling. The you are provided a gun and the room how four lubbering zombies who moan slowly and are no threat and can be shot to piece with the pistol with no real struggle. The gun in question does not have a significant recoil and the sound rather than the alarming bang of a gun, is basically pew-pew, making it feel like a pea shooter despite destroying the initial zombies. Rather than making the gun feel powerful it makes the zombies feel weak.
Moving is slow and feel like gliding through taffy.
This is the first 5 minutes of the remake.

As you might notice there is a significant disconnect from the high energy, high agency feel of the orginal, while the remake has none of it, down to flat out removing player agency for the first few minutes of the game completely.

The point of this comparison is to highlight the feeling that was missing from the game, so you could say that you get more impactful weapons later or maybe the music gets good later, I dont know how ever that isn't the point here, the point is how they interact and the feeling it inspires.

An example of a remake that I believe does a fanastic job of capturing the orginal feel, while modernizing the game play is wolfenstein: the new order.
Yes they changed things from the orginal but the kept the underlying feeling and theme athentic to the orginal.

It is worth noting I write these reviews for my own benefit so that when I come back to a game I can refer to them to let me know what my initial feelings were.
If you like the game, you shouldn't let my views ruin it for you, enjoy playing the game and have a good time. Why should you care if some random guy on the internet didn't enjoy it?
However for me, knowing my impression of a tittle later when deciding what I am in the mood to play is crictical to understanding why I categorized a game a certain way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving DOOM as pretty much my first FPS experience has without a doubt been...interesting.

First of all, no matter what you're gonna hear from me, you should give DOOM a try even if you feel like it's something you wouldn't ever play in your life. My first go at the game was about 5 minutes at the beginning of the campaign because my friend made me do it. Yeah, wow, straight in the middle of a ton of creatures that all want to eat you, shoot you, decapitate you...you get the idea. So there I was frantically bashing the keyboard looking for the right keys at the right time. Eventually I managed to get Doomguy killed of course and I was pretty shook about what I had just been doing. However this sparked my attention and with a little bit of persuasion from my friend I eventually bought the game.

Yes, the first couple of missions were really unusual at first and having a bunch of demons jump at me from behind the corners really held me on the edge of my chair. That's the case even now as I'm nearing the end of the campaign. But as I've been getting more familiar with the opportunities given to me and learned to use them it's really not that hard anymore.

+
* You can literally rip and tear your way through roomfuls of enemies thrown at you! They can of course do the same to you BUT since you're playing as a ruthless meatgrinder, no demon can stop you!
* You are given a pretty decent arsenal of guns as you progress through the game. Learn to use them. (Almost) every gun is good for a certain situation and it doesn't stop there as there are several ways you can use each gun.
* The game looks great. It's gory but great. While playing pay attention to your surroundings as you can find smaller and bigger details that look interesting and also help you immerse in the story.
* Speaking of which, it's not just about plowing your way through endless blood and guts. There is also a pretty good storyline to guide you through. That is if the seemingly endless ways of killing demons hasn't got you hooked enough.
* The game creators have been really creative with all of the demon 'species' you're gonna meet. Just as you begin to loathe one as being a bit too tough, the game has a new one in store for you.
* DOOM also offers quite a bit to all of you completionists and collectors out there. If you need to take a breath from killing, there are many bits of data, secrets and adorable collectibles to find.
* The soundtrack is also worth mentioning. Metal is not something I'd usually listen to but together with DOOM it's just in perfect harmony.

-
* You may get bored having to always "clean" every second room of demons before you can get on with your objectives. Although this is obviously the major way to confront you with enemies.
* Some guns become useless, especially the pistol that you'd probably just want to throw away after getting your second weapon.
* Everything loads for a long time but I guess that's to be expected.

Conclusion:
Despite DOOMs scary and gory nature it's still probably gonna be the game of the year experience for me. Yes, the game doesn't really hold your hand as you waltz through the missions and sometimes it can seem too difficult to manage but don't let any obstacles get you down!
Also I must say, DOOM is not for the weak hearted. Coming around a corner to have a screaming mountain of a demon jump at you, it can have it's scare moments. But what do you expect! You're sometimes literally in hell so get used to it. ;)
If you sometimes feel like you need a break for a day or two, do it. It also happens to me. But do keep pushing forward as it is eventually worth your while and effort.\t\t\t\t\t
