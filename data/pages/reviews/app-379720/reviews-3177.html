\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe addition of the classic view point for weapons is indicative of just how much this game is meant to pay homage to and revel in it's roots. A fast-paced, straight-forwards shooter with a hellscape aesthetic: it stands as a shot in the arm for the first person and shooter genres that have stagnated in the last decade. While admittedly the art style is hardly fully faithful as its most iconic elements are merely borrowed for recognitions sake and it suffers much of the modern visual trend of all blending together (though it does have some nice color contrast in areas saving it from completely drowning in single colors), and the gameplay is more reminicent of Quake or Unreal than the original Doom, nonethless it is a highly worthwhile addition to anyone's library and one that I've not only returned to a few times already, but that I'll want to play years from now.

That said the multiplayer is as generic and modern as you can get. Absolutely no creativity was employed in putting together the exact same gameplay you could get in Halo, Battlefield or Call of Duty. It should come as no surprise then it was made by the people who put together CoD's multiplayer modes. To be fair, if you like that then here you go, more of the same. However, it departs so heartily from the single-player experience it really makes you question why bother at all? Why play it so straight in the single-player and then revert back to the status quo for online play? I'm guessing despite Bethesda banking on the multiplayer (the season pass is all multiplayer content), we'll see a nice single-player map pack, or dare I dream, full episode expansions in the future so hopefully everyone wins in the end.\t\t\t\t\t
