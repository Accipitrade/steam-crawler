\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhatever made doom DOOM, is also in this DOOM. Only difference is it's 3D and doesn't have 1993 graphics. Mind you I bought it on sale for some 6-7usd, and that's a great price for what you get. Could be easily worth some 20.

Good:
1 - Climate
2 - Feel
3 - Yes you ARE the horror, we've heard this already.
4 - Game keeps reminding you that you're not immortal and invulnerable, while also providing tools to feel like the vengeance on rollerskates. With guns.
5 - Basically everything in this game is designed with a player in mind, so that you get help when needed but are never annoyed by it, nor is it particulary easy.
6 - The story, somehow, while having to be written for a "Here's a gun, you're superpowered, go shot up hell", is interesting, does not anyhow contradict what we knew about DOOM already, and is actually pretty interesting! I've read all the bits of information I could find.

Bad:
1 - Despite green lights usually pointing where to go, there are several open areas with many paths and reasons to backtrack in some instances, which made me genuinely lost for some good 3-5 minutes walking aimlessly.
2 - Mancubus glory kills seem to be limited to two, with front and above being the same animation, which got really repetitive after a few dozen. Rest was more varied.\t\t\t\t\t

Story: The story isn't AMAZING, but it's not the game's focal point. The story flows with the game's purpose: to ♥♥♥♥ ♥♥♥♥ up!!! It's a very basic story, consisting of, SPOILER WARNING, "I am the person who runs this place and she has betrayed us... you must stop her!... oh no, you need to go to hell to fix this! ...you need to come back to fix this!". If you want STORY, don't come looking.

Gameplay: The gun-play in this game is the most generic, but smoothest experience. It's left click to shoot and right click to use your modification, so not much to attach or anything. There isn't much shaky-hand feeling or realistic movements. I would compare how the gun-play feels to a modernized original Doom, which is kind of what you want from a game like this. However, there are ways to upgrade your weapon and make them feel super bad ass and overpowered, even though they really aren't. For example, one of the mods for the assault rifle is a micro-missile launcher that takes the place of your normal ammo. When you first get the mod, it can only fire six missiles at a time before needing a cool down; but, before you know it, you'll have already upgraded it to BOTTOMLESS MISSILES and just have 210 missiles at your disposable without and cool down. ITS AWESOME! There are also suit upgrades, but there are only a few that REALLY matter, like having items and things appear on your map and being able to climb faster. There are also runes that you can collect which give you minor enhancements and they're all balanced. None of them are more useful over the other, and it really depends on the player's preference.

Collectibles and secrets: If you're a completionist who wants a bit of a challenge, then this game should be able to sustain you. There are a multitude of secret rooms and little toy doom slayers to find in each mission. Additionally, the runes and praetor tokens (suit upgrade currency) are sometimes hidden away in these secret rooms so you'll have to find some of them to get suit upgrades and other enhancements

Length: I like to collect everything, so there's a bit of backtracking for me. I took about 20 hours (still playing at this time, but almost done) for me to play but length may vary.

For me personally, I like my games to have a little bit more background on all the characters. Even though there are data logs about all the weapons, monsters, and characters, it just all feels so empty that it's all pointless. However, when you get to the heart of the game, you just forget about it and just kill demons and ♥♥♥♥ ♥♥♥♥ up

I give this game an 8/10. If there were more backstory at all, it would probably be a 9/10 for me. It might have been a 10/10 if some of the power ups you get were a little more balanced, if some of the collectibles really did anything, and if most of the suit power ups were worth it to care about.
