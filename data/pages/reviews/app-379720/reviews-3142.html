\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, I recently got a new PC (about time) and I re-purchased DOOM and began playing it. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, is it amazing.

Now, I have not played any of the original games, but I don't think I need to after playing this. In a nutshell, this game is... KILL, KILL, RIP, TEAR, KILL, KILL. The campaign was very enjoyable, but I have one complaint which I will go over later.

The graphics are amazing. It looks very lifelike and it makes you feel like you're actually there on Mars. There were a few things which the textures looked sloppy and rushed, but it's hardly noticable because of the horde of demons that are coming at you. The demons and the locations are well-designed, creative and overall great. Some of the demons annoyed me at times since they were harder to kill then others (SUMMONER) but they were mainly cool ideas. One complaint I have is that a few of them bugged out at times, but it's very rare.

You have a very diverse arsenal of weapons that you unlock throughout the game, some of which perform better then others. Some are just downright over powered (Chainsaw). Weapon mods were extremely useful and fun to use. Again, some were better then others, but try mixing them up, switching them often, but towards the end of the game, use the mod you're best with, since you'll need them for the bosses. It's a shame it's so hard to find Chainsaw fuel sometimes.

The main campaign is fun, it's a good way to get used to the game, learn the controls and use all the weapons before hopping into a multiplayer match. There's a lot of secrets to find, making players explore and map and somtimes encounter an entire horde of demons, which can be good or bad, depending on a few things. One thing I really like is how you're invincible whilst performing a Glory Kill on a demon, I was honestly expecting to be torn to shreds, but nope. +rep for that. Now, towards the end, however, when you enter Hell, it feels the game isn't about combat anymore and it's mainly exploration. Maybe that's just me being unable to find anything, but there's less demons and more puzzles and exploring there, so I was very disappointed with this. The ending was a bit cliché as well, I saw it coming from a mile away, but the boss fights were fun. I died a lot, but I think it really improved my skills and it's helped me in online multiplayer.

One quick thing before my final rating of the game, I would like to mention how calm the multiplayer community is. When I join a game, I'm not finding people raging, or spamming the chat with stupid things. People are actually working together to complete the game's objective. Wether we're defending the allied Baron of Hell, or agressivly collecting souls, it's a great community. From somebody who has played games with a lot of toxic people, this is a great upside.

Now, my final rating of the game is about an 8/10. The complaint with the campaign I mentioned previously kind of ruined it, but the game makes up for it in nothing but fun, demon-killing action. I would definatly recommend getting this while it's on sale, though. To me, as great as it is, it is in no way worth the $80.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very, very enjoyable shooter with an oldschool feeling to it! The single-player campaign is awesome, and so are the snap maps!

Pros:

+ Extremely fast paced action, faster than anything else. If you try CSGO after an hour of Doom you'll think that your crouch button is stuck!
+ Almost no cutscenes. Your job is one - kill demons, no distractions!
+ Big guns, big demons, heavy fists. Almost all guns have x2 alternative firing modes (unlockable) which are neatly designed. The Siege Gauss Cannon has the greatest sound ever!
+ Community designed Snap Maps. I tried several survival and tower defense-like maps, all of them were great fun with a buddy!
+ My gf loves breaking the faces, ripping the guts, and dismembering the limbs of demons. She's not a gamer.

Cons:

- Most of the secrets are way too obvious, others are way too difficult to find.
- The single-player campaign is rather short. Luckily, hunting for secrets is fun and destroying demons never gets boring, so you can get quite some game time out of it.
- I wish there were bigger groups of demons to fight - if you progress the game and decide to go back a level to find that one secret you missed, you'll notice how scarcely populated the level is.
- Multiplayer sucks, but who cares? Get Quake instead.

Buy it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan what a blast.

With those four words I have easily summarized my experience with Doom so far. Doom takes you back, not just to the UAC facility as the DOOM Marine, but back to the action-packed game Doom was when it was first created and shipped on floppy disks.

At first I was a bit apprehensive about the new doom when I read that it was moving away from the action horror setting from Doom 3, as it was something I quite liked. My main concern was that I've seen a lot of games pull this move from atmospheric horror to outright slugfest like 'Alone in the Dark', with abyssmal results. Say what you will about it not being true to the original gorefest that was the original Doom, but from a story and atmosphere perspective it was excellent.

Doom, or Doom 4 if you will, is an inversion of Doom 3. The story element is still there, but it has taken a backseat to pure action and gore where in the end you feel like you're not really playing the game as intended if you're not continuously chaining together glory kills every second you're there.

My biggest and only gripe about the game is that crashed a lot on my computer. And while I can run the game on medium to medium+ settings on a Windows 7 system with updated video drivers, it wasn't uncommon in a one hour session for the game to crash eight times or more. Once I even had a random engine crash that shook the game so hard even the crash report crashed. I didn't know wether to laugh or cry at that point.

In summary; Doom is an excellent game that returns to the original formula. If you want a fast-paced, action heavy game with a light dose of story injected once in a while to guide the player along, this is it.\t\t\t\t\t
