\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR If you enjoy FPS games at all and haven't played this, then you owe it to yourself to buy and play this game. Like now.

What else can I say about this game besides that it's absolutely badass?! To this day I don't think I've ever played a better FPS game, the combat is fast paced and satisfying while cutting out nearly all the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ of reloading, waiting for health to regen behind cover, constant cutscenes and story dialogue grinding the pace to a halt. Don't get me wrong I love a good story in my games, but I definitely appreciate when a studio like ID knows when to not ♥♥♥♥ around with it if their game doesn't need it.

I know my playtime on steam is only about 20 hours, but this was a second purchase of the game (bundled with Wolfenstein II during a huge sale), I bought it on launch back in my Xbox days. So this was a second playthrough when I bought my gaming PC. In my initial time with the game in 2016 I must admit that I felt it dragged on a bit too long for its own good. But let's be honest, I had no clue what I was talking about. This run through the game on a higher difficulty, while looking for all the secret nooks and crannies, was just awesome. Not once did I feel bored, or like I was tired of the gameplay, completing rune challenges and upgrading runes for the benefits actually adds a good amount of choice in your encounters. The same can be said for just about all the upgrades for weapons and such. If you haven't played this game you owe it to yourself to buy it, I know you can find it cheap nowadays with Doom: Eternal just a few months away. So do it, ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ buy this amazing game.

The last thing I will touch on is the soundtrack; If you somehow haven't already heard anything about it then you are missing out my friend. Mick Gordons' work on this game, Prey, and the new Wolfenstein series is just amazing. But this game has to be his best work yet. The blend of metal guitars with electronic beats and the perfect ambiance when combat dies down is just amazing. I'm not normally one to listen to game soundtracks outside of the games, but this mans work is so good that I regularly visit his music in my free time. As someone who's always loved throwing on headphones to blast music all day, this games music is perfect for the gameplay on offer, you'll listen to some of the heaviest guitar riffs I've personally ever head while ripping and tearing your way through thousands of demons. And as a final note; I can't wait for Doom: Eternal to come out so I can crank the music up to max and sink my teeth into another great shooter by ID software!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn the 40 hours it took me to fight my way through the campaign on ultra violence DOOM has become a top 3 game for me. The soundtrack is perfect. The gunplay is some of the best I've ever experienced in a game. There is story here while light it is interesting. You have to dig a little and read some to understand the story of the Doomslayer but it's much more interesting than badass with a gun.

Levels keep varied decently well with the earlier few being much slower than the latter levels. By the end of the game with a full arsenal you'll be switching weapons constantly to engage demons in the most efficient manner possible. Everything in this game's campaign is balanced well. If you die it is your fault, not because the game is cheap.

This game is a masterpiece modern FPS.
Buy it if you enjoy fast paced action games.\t\t\t\t\t
