\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWasn't initially sold on the doom reboot. I had a fair few friends insist it was good, though, and so I figured I'd get it with the PC upgrade I picked up recently as well.

I like it, overall, but it has some flaws. I don't think you could make a game like the original DOOM these days without the charm being lost somewhere; people's expectations have changed a lot. So, I didn't go into this expecting it to be too similar.

It plays a bit more like an arena shooter than Doom 1 or 2. The level design lends itself towards this nicely, it's all pretty varied. Though the gameplay loop WILL get a bit boring if the levels start to drag out. And if you decide to explore, they almost certainly will. It's a nice nod to secrets in the original titles, at least.

The gunplay is really the strong suit here. You have a tool for every job, and a job for every tool. This is really where the game becomes most interesting for me. It's just a shame that combat comes in these irritatingly obvious and broken up intervals rather than a more consistent level of pressure - the pacing suffers a bit.

Overall though, it's a good action shooter. One of the better ones I've played in this particular niche.\t\t\t\t\t
