\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI liked DOOM and I loved to play some of the older ones.. this one however.. not so much.

Imagine for a second the classic game Frogger. A frog jumps across a street with cars coming left and right which you need to avoid. A nice game and a classic on the C64 back in the day.

What I do not need, is a 3D DX12 version of frogger. There is just no point. Same for DOOM.

This version of the game features well designed levels in which the same groups of enemies spawn until you have killed them all, then you go to the next room. Rinse and repeat.

There is no surprise, there's a bit of jumping up and down left and right.. but you get tired of predictable enemies very fast.

How this game got these awesome reviews I can only explain with nostalgia. If you are into nostalgia and seeing the newest version of a flaming skull or a beholder-demon is enough to get you excited, this game is for you. If you expect storytelling or anything except shooting the same enemies over and over again, it may not be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tpros -
- retains elements of older doom games while still introducing new and fun mechanics (glory kills add a whole other strategic element to certain levels)
- difficult, satisfying arcade-style boss battles
- plenty of content for completionists, and a solid length for a standard campaign.
- really nice to look at while still being well optimized
- the last 3-5 hours of the campaign are probably the best 3-5 hours i've ever played in any FPS campaign
- it's fun to do glory kills from really far away and pretend that you're a naruto character

cons -
- in early-mid game, missions are very unimaginative and consist of basically like "go to platform x to access tower y because it contains keycard z that is used to open a door over on tower a, which connects to elevator n..."
- doomguy's early ~3XTR3M3 3DG3~ is embarrassing. i get that the entire series is meant to be over the top but some of his antics felt like they were aimed at 13 yr old boys. characters can be hardcore and cool without being cartoonishly disobedient or obstinant
- doesn't have the same horror elements of doom 3. personally i disliked how doom 3 tried to be a horror game, but i can see why this tonal shift may put off some fans of it\t\t\t\t\t
