\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTrying so hard to like this game.

Game mechanics is very simple. There was a lot of eye candy to the game when they demoed it during E3 that made me thought this is a game worth trying. Unfortunately, playing each arena is downright boring. Sure you can toggle the difficulty up and fight with tougher AI, but I don't feel any incentive to progress through the game. Perhaps maybe it is the lack of immersion. Perhaps maybe it is the lack of good story writing that made this game feel cheesy at times. Perhaps this game has its place in the classics, and if so, I completely disagree with the pricing of this game. I think with very minimal features, lack of content, and absent multiplayer community, the game is definitely not worth a 59.99 MSRP.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is an incredible game. There's a fine balance between holding to its historic roots with classic gameplay, but not being too classic as to seem dated. DOOM's 2016 release strides that line perfectly in singleplayer, although the multiplayer seems pretty mundane. The story mode is the highlight of the game by miles and is very satisfying from a mechanics standpoint. The balance of the different weapons, upgrades and suit systems make for quite a varied amount of play styles.

It's a fantastic old-school spin on modern shooters that is as visually impressive as it is strategic in its gruesomness.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTruly the ultimate shooter. I've played games like Far Cry and Bioshock, and although they have their amazing pros, shooting was never fully explored in those. Sometimes, there just was too much storyline or it felt as if the game wasn't really meant to work that way.
DOOM explores the FPS shoot-em-up genre to the extent where every aspect of the game is designed to pump you up and have you kill demons in every possible way. From the visuals and the audio, to the abilities, guns, attachments, glory kills, monsters, levels, powerups, this game made shooting a gun not just an act, but an art.
The storyline is beautifully made, since it seems so unreal that there is little to care about from the start, so you focus on acquiring the biggest, baddest weapons on Mars and in Hell, to face enemies. These enemies, additionally, are so well-made that the ones that appear at the beginning are still killing you in your last battle.
The music, in my opinion, is a feature of its own. In a game like Skyrim, the battle music is just a bunch of chants and instruments that gets annoying when you have to hear it for more than ten minutes in a row. On the other end of the scale, we have DOOM, which has amazingly well-coordinated music that lets you know you need to ready your triple-barrel minigun attachment, while getting your blood pumping at the same time. There were some times during this game where I was physically exhausted after playing this game for a good two hours.
I bought this game to have my reactions and FPS skills tested and improved, as well as to have an intense gaming experience. It was exactly what I expected, and I absolutely loved this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is great. Well at least the Single Player is. I remember back in the early 90's by dad installed the Shareware version of the original on our first PC. The game was amazing, intense and scary as hell. This game very much reminds me of the 90's but with just more. The movement in the game is incredible. It feels fluid and responsive which is more than I can say for some other shooters out there.

The shooting also feels solid. Upgrading your weapons and modifying them really helps strategize the arenas that you fight in. The level design in very much based on arena’s and are very open and multi layered. They are really fun and allow you to change up your weaponry when you can. The demons are agile and move throughout the environment with ease. It provides a good challenge and keeps you moving throughout the levels.

The graphics are great. I am running this off an i7 with a 970gtx. I typically get about 60fps with some dips. The game visually looks great and runs great. I do recommend using the settings the game provides so that you can get the best frame rate which is a solid 60. Its smooth and makes it easier to shot demons when jumping and moving your aim around.

Overall if you love shooters, buy it. Don’t expect much from the story but the lore you can get into if you are really into that stuff. I was at least. I actually think it is my shooter of the year.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 is a $60 AAA title with a decently long campaign, and I recommend it on sale if you aren't the kind of person to frequently buy $60 games.
Now, this is Bethesda trying to continue a beloved (and old) FPS franchise, and also attract the modern gamer. These goals kind of counteract each other.

This game has an unlock and upgrade system that feels a little bit like pandering to the "AAA" crowd, who expect character progression and unlock systems, but it doesn't get in the way of gameplay too much (imo the game would've been better without it). In a nutshell, gameplay is hallway exploration combined with being locked inside a large room with waves of demons. Combat feels pretty good, but still a little rooted to the floor (you only get air control via an upgrade unlocked midway through the game). Either way, it's pretty fun nonetheless.

Multiplayer and Snapmap aren't worth $20 combined, they suck :/
This is a singleplayer game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played the original Doom games when they came out in the beginning of the '90s and was blown away. I played Doom 3 and liked it, even though it received a lot of criticism. So how does the new, rebooted Doom 2016 stack up?

First of all, it's a great game. The praise from the players and critics is well deserved. I feel it is the spiritual successor of the original Doom games. Even though they are worlds apart from a technological standpoint, you can *feel* the influence of the original games all over this game.

The combat is fast-paced. There is no hiding behind covers and waiting for your health to regenerate in this game. If you try to stay hidden, you die. If you try to escape, you die. The only way forward is to battle your way through the hordes of demons. The weapon balance is pretty spot on. There are a lot of upgrade options for weapon mods and your suit, which I first thought was completely unncecessary in a game like this, but I later changed my mind because they give you the freedom to upgrade your character according to your playstyle.

The level design and the environments are very good, you will have a lot of fun exploring and trying to find the secrets. The game engine is very impressive and the recent VulkanRT renderer update did wonders (at least for AMD video card owners).

What are some of the negatives? The random crashes to desktop are annoying, but most of all the bloody checkpoint system which the absolute cancer of the modern FPS genre. This has no place in a game like Doom.

Overall I would rate this game 9.5/10. I wish it was a bit longer. I had tons of fun and it is by no means a short game, but I just wanted *more*.
