\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is really about the metal aesthetic and the joy of excessive violence

Story? It's blunt and to the point. I kept waiting for the drop where we get betrayed or there is some complication. Nope. And that's a good thing.

You might think by the start that I'm reffering to the gunplay as what makes it awesome, not really. The shooting is too arcadey and kind of floating. The overall structure of fights works throwing swarms of enemies where you have to get in close to sustain yourself and your weapons, but it's not great. The general hectic nature hides some of the jank, but it would benefit from being more crisp.

The exploration sucks. I mean sucks. The arcadey feel applies to jumping and the game feels borderline unplayable for moving around without double jump. And even when you have that it feels bad once they push it whatsoever. And all of that is secondary to exploration sucking. There are secrets and a ton of unlockables, but the stage design randomly has points of no return. It doesn't tell you, you can't prepare. And in a seeming sadistic choice, it also establishes that backtracking is needed for a lot of the secrets. Without a guide, you can't tell if that secret you see can be obtained now. And you are actively punished if you want to come back and you are wrong.

It throws a bunch at you in terms of the upgrades and secrets, sure. But even then it's sloppy. The upgrades and challenges ask you to play in a specific, typically inorganic way. Instead of jumping around and punching demons in the face, I spent far too much time creating contrived situations to fulfill an upgrade requirement.

It's a laundry list of horrible design decisions, and honestly, I don't care that much. It's so blunt and to the point and has so much personality that Doom is undeniably fun. I look forward to playing the sequel because there is so very much they can improve on here. 7/10\t\t\t\t\t
