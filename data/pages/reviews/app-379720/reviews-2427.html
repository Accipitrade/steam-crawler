\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo reloading, keycards, secrets, no regenerating health, and the old health and armour system from classic FPS games. Like Doom.

This game is kind of like Doom, but prettier, and with a new mechanic called "glory kills". It's also probably going to melt your computer if you don't turn those graphics all the way down.

It's a ton of fun at breakneck speed with a story you can choose whether or not to care about. It's got some pretty fun bosses, too. You're probably best off playing it on Ultraviolent if you want an actual challenge, but you can still have a great time with Hurt Me Plenty. Every level has a fantastic setpiece or two, and they all feel different to each other, in a good way. There's some pretty funny visual humour that sometimes gets bloody gruesome sprinkled throughout. Like the skulls you have to shove into holes to open doors once you get to hell.

The gameplay is about as arena as it gets these days, and even if doomguy can't sprint as fast as he used to, he's still pretty fast. One of the ways the game gives you a strong sense of progression, aside from its weapon upgrade progression system, is the way that it slowly introduces new enemies and new weapons as you start to get the hang of moving and fighting in DOOM.

As soon as you've gotten used to dealing with imps, the game throws a hellknight in your face. And he can take a few metric ♥♥♥♥ tons of lead to the face before he goes down. But once you get used to dealing with him, there are bigger monstrosities on the way. And when they go down, doomguy makes sure they don't get up again.

The new element DOOM adds to the old formula (if we just ignore weapon and suit upgrades) is glory kills, and as much as it might seem like just a cosmetic addition, it does actually have a significant impact on gameplay. When you weaken an enemy enough, they'll flash orange, and you'll gain the ability to trigger a cutscene of you punching some poor demon in the face. Or stomping on his guts. Or punching his eye out. Or snapping his neck. Or, if you're using a chainsaw, cutting him in half. And, particularly if you're using a chainsaw, ammo will pour out of them, as it seems that a healthy demon's main source of protein is UAC-issued bullets.

You may be wondering if this breaks the pace of the gameplay, or whether it gets old quick. It definitely adds some flavour to gameplay, and because the game takes its sweet time introducing new enemies, it feels like there's a great variety of different ways to kill enemies. Once the entire ensemble of enemies has made its way on stage and we're 3/4 of the way through the act, and we've seen into the depths of all these demons, though, it kind of just becomes another element of gameplay. Press F to collect ammo. Pull out your chainsaw and cut the pinyata of choice in half - preferrably a smaller-looking one, so that you conserve more ammo for your chainsaw for about the same amount of ammo in return. It's just another thing you've got to keep in mind as you're ripping and tearing your way through hell.

That said, you can just kill enemies normally when you're low on ammo and they'll drop it all the same anyway. Clearly, the designers figured if you were low on ammo, you'd probably be low on health, too. And there's no time to waste fishing through an imp's entrails for the exact kind of bullets you need to keep the fight going. Glory kills are a great spectacle, at least for a good 8 hours. But once you've seen it all, they don't prove that intrusive to the gameplay. It's a more entertaining alternative to running around with your pistol, fully upgraded or not, trying to take down the biggest and spongiest enemies that DOOM has to offer. As long as there are enemies, there's more ammo.

I suppose I should mention the weapon and suit upgrades, since they're a thing that can make your weapons do more things. You can make your shotgun highly explosive or fire three rounds in quick succession, and you can fire three homing rockets at the same time at an unsuspecting enemy. You'll have to kill a lot of enemies to gain Praetor tokens to upgrade these abilities further or otherwise steal them from random guys lying down on the job. Or were those guys for the suit upgrades? Forgive me, I forget, because the suit upgrades are basically worthless, though the weapon upgrades are good fun. They're not necessary, but why wouldn't you want to make the game more fun? It's a nice addition.

The music's pretty damn good, too. Mainly because it's designed in such a way that it responds to what you're doing in combat. If you're going in for a glory kill, it'll slow up to an apex, and if you're tearing through enemy after enemy, it'll build up. It makes combat a lot more addicting and involving - if tearing a demon's eye from its socket wasn't involved enough for you already. The music also changes from level to level, and gets exceedingly more awesome. And if you don't like the electronic music specially selected to accompany your demon-slaying, I assume you can just turn the music down to 0 in the options.

The weapons sound great and powerful. There's really not much more to say but that they're as satisfying to see fire as they are to hear. Enemies also sound powerful and nightmarish as ♥♥♥♥ when they mosey up on you from behind. I don't remember them nearly as clearly as Doom's iconic monster sounds, but they're still pretty damn good. As much as I don't really give a flying ♥♥♥♥ about the story, Samuel Hayden's voice actor makes me want to care. Vega has a pretty unique voice for an AI, and other girl who's name I don't remember sounds suitably crazy. I've got no complaints there.

Oh yeah, and speaking of sound design, the new voice actor for Doomguy has a lot more grit.

You might notice I haven't dedicated a paragraph or two yet to mention the graphics and art style of this game, and that's because there's really not that much to say abou it. It's great. It looks pretty. Demons look like the stuff of nightmares; the bad ones. And the whole place really does look like hell. Or a UAC base. Depending on where the demons show up to have their asses torn out and handed to them. There's not much more to say.

DOOM's singleplayer is a damn fun 10-12-hour romp through hell and back and back to hell again. It's well-crafted, sounds great, looks great, and even has a story, if you're into that. It's worth $20USD, and it's definitely worth $10USD. If you like First Person Shooters, then buy it. It's a steal.

Of course, that's not all the game has to offer.

DOOM also happens to have a multiplayer. And it's not as well put-together as the singleplayer, and was apparently worse before I first laid my hands on it in mid-2018. I'd like to give you a more wholesome opinion of it, but because it's pretty unpredictable as to whether I'll find the only other person playing this game in a 30-minute search or a full lobby of people in 2 minutes, I haven't gotten the opportunity to play that much of it.

But what I have played, I'll tell you about.

You unlock weapons as you level up. And you level up pretty quickly, so long as you're not as horrifically bad at the game as I was when I first started playing it. Your best bet is probably the rocket launcher and super shotgun combo, until you have access to anything better. It's actually a pretty fun experience, if only for the novelty. You're probably not going to find a game in anything but Team Play, but that's a large variety of modes already. And you get to do glory kills in multiplayer, too! What more could one want for?

Maybe a larger playerbase would be a start. This game is pretty dead, you know. It has been two years, I guess, and even in the subsequent hype from E3, there doesn't seem to be as many people playing the game as, say, Quake Champions. Which kinda bugs me, since this game actually has decent netcode. But it ain't that bad. It's not the greatest, but it's pretty fun. That's my opinion from 4 hours of gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHated every minute of it.

Maybe this game is not for me. Maybe I'm getting old?
The weapons feel like plastic toys. The first gun in the game litterally goes "pew pew" with almost no effect on ennemies. The other guns don't feel better. I think sound design is partly to blame (the volume of a gun shot seems to be speaking voice volume), there's almost no recoil or feedback to the weapons.
The game really wants you to do combos, but you've seen them all in 10 minutes, so secondary objectives like "do X combos on X ennemies" immediately feel like homework.
The ennemies mostly attack you at close range. They seem to plunge forward at the last moment, leaving your field of vision, so they dodge your shot at the most frustrating moment. This also results in no damage feedback at all. The ennemies look like they're just going through you and you then don't know where they are. I never knew when I was taking damage or how much damage. The ennemies attacks don't knock you back or shake you or anything of the sort: They slow you down?!

Then, the weapons and armor upgrades and secondary objectives. First of all, the menus are as unergonomic as they could be. You have to unlock an upgarde to do headshots apparently? None of the upgrades seemed useful or attractive, so upgrading your stuff is just a chore, selecting whatever at random.

I was c o m p e l e t e l y lost most of the time. All the corridors and rooms look alike. I never cared where I was going or knew what I was supposed to achieve, the map does not help, so I ended up walking endlessly in the levels until I found the right door or key by chance.
The game seems to (want to) make fun of games that pretend to have a story. In the beginning, a computer begins to tell you some generic objectives, and the character knocks it down to immediately grab a gun. Fun! Except the game then proceeds to give you ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ objectives anyway. Not fun, ironic objectives, nothing funny, just the same "go there, unlock that" crap it's supposed to make fun of, with a constant recorded voice saying the most generic stuff, and holograms guiding you through the levels (never saw THAT before...)

I am the biggest fan of ID Software for Quake, but just thinking of playing this new DOOM again makes me feel angry, frustrated and depressed. Level 3 is where I stopped and I don't think I'm going back to it. I bet I'm missing something since the reviews are so positive. I only paid 9.99, but I would've been pissed to have paid more. Very disapointed.\t\t\t\t\t
