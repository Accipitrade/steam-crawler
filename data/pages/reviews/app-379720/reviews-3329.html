\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt feels pure DOOM: 75% oldschool and 25% modern mechanics, also you can mod the Multiplayer and make it a bit more competitive.

My score for this is 90/100. These are the good and the bad things imo:

· Pros:

- Great production, technically excellent with amazing sound and graphics.
- Smooth, fast paced, bloody like hell.
- Level maps and boss designs.
- Metal music with some really good tunes, and a bit of electronic and classical music for each moment.
- The game is so fun and addictive for itself but add a lot of things to do for a better replay value such as secret levels, different paths, upgrades, challenges etc. Nice Easter eggs.
- Glory kills (Melee Fatalities) looks and feel really good and you can disable the visual effect that indicates when an enemy is staggered if you don't like it. With this, you can get bonus ammo and health.
- You can turn off the crosshair, the compass, the objectives... for a more hardcore experience.
- SnapMap (Maybe the key for the ones who are looking for a more competitive multiplayer experience while waiting 'till next Quake game)
- Casual but really fun multiplayer with tons of creative modes and a well designed online world with cool skins, playable demons, new weapons and other stuff to unlock while you play.
- Perfect balanced difficulty (Nightmare, the harder one, is really hard) Also you have that same difficulty level but with Perma-death called ''Ultra-Nightmare'')
- Weapons beautifully designed with collectible modifications you can get through the game.
- Spoiler: Classic nostalgic levels hidden inside the campaign and you can play it later from the title menu.
- First time since 90's we see a real good FPS (I mean with an AAA production in the back) with no health/shields regeneration, no reload, tons of beautiful weapons, good maps, and fast combat speed and exploration.


· Cons:

- Disappointment achievements, all of them so easy to get.
- The... Story? (I know, is DOOM)
- No coop.
- Expensive Multiplayer DLC.
- Limited Strafe Jump as default (Don't know if you can change that with SnapMap)
- No Rocket Jump and no Plasma Climbing. (SnapMap maybe?)
- It seems this is another PCGame victim of the cross-platform because textures have been downgraded a bit. I can feeling it.

Overall I'm happy with the new DOOM. Games evolve and change, 90's were great but we should accept and love the new era because it does good things too. Not everything is awful nowadays.

---------------------------------------

TL;DR: Good multiplayer (Outsourced) and a brutally gorgeous singleplayer thanks to ID.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary:
DOOM(2016) is not only just a great game, it is a great Doom game. Unlike Doom 3 which tried to focus more on narrative and horror aspects, DOOM brings what fans always enjoyed about the series. Fast paced action, gory kills, sprawling level design, epic music, and wonderful boss fights. The story is minimal to non-existent, but that is a good thing, DOOM knows what it's doing, providing an avenue for players to experience the most adrenaline fueled gameplay of the past few years in gaming.

Pros:
-Excellent level design
-Return of "Secrets" across each level
-"Always Run" is back
-Old School mechanics (health packs, holding all 10 weapons)
-Epic score of industrial/metal music
-Excellent visuals
-Well optimized for PC
-Gameplay is stellar
-Enemy design is grotesque and gory
-Memorable boss fights
-Snapmap is a great addition to the game
-Good length campaign (~10 hours on ULTRA-VIOLENCE difficulty)

Meh:
-Multiplayer is just OK, some old school Doom fans will be disappointed

Cons:
-Some campaign arenas can get repetitive (Gore Bags)

Performance:

Rig Used: EVGA GTX 980 4GB of VRAM, 16GB DDR3 RAM, i5 3570K.

I was able to maintain a flawless locked 60fps at 1080p resolution on ultra settings. When unlocked, fps stays around the 90-100 mark on Ultra graphics settings. I tested at 2560x1440, and was able to maintain 60fps, but with a few frame drops during intense sequences.
