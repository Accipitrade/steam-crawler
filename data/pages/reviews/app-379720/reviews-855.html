\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought it at the 2020 end-of-year Steam sales, and played it through the following week. Well, I think I prefer Doom II (or rather, GZDoom + the tops of the Cacowards). I understand that they felt the need to renew a formula that is more than 20 years old, but now it's not the same gameplay. In this 2016 version, I had the impression of a wrestling fight, where I spend my time hurling myself on the enemies to tackle them to the ground. And just like in a wrestling match, the fights take place in a small, enclosed arena, with bouncy stuff on the edge. All mixed up with Benny Hill-style chases: I run behind demons, while other demons run behind me, and we go around in circles like that, going through the same places ten times until either life runs out. There's way too much unnecessary talk, and sequences where you get stuck in an elevator, and I also didn't enjoy the repeating sequences where I am asked to press E to trigger an automatic animation so that the hero shows his big muscles by forcing a door. Admittedly this new version has a lot more polygons and volumetric fumes, but even so, the levels are ultimately not very imaginative and quite repetitive. Overall, it's not catastrophic either, I still appreciated enough to play it to the end. But there's little chance that I'd come to it back again and again, especially with its 70 gigabytes that filled my SSD. Maybe I just had too high expectations of the many rave reviews this Doom had received?\t\t\t\t\t
