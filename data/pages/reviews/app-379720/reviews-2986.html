However, even the ever talkative Space Marine shows it, the plot is as important as sprinkles on ice cream. I mean, you still get the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ice cream even if you don't get the disgusting, colored wax called sprinkles (and the ice cream is scooperman; everyone loves scooperman). The gore appeals to everybody, regardless the amount, which, in comparison to early gameplay of Doom, is toned down; if gore didn't appeal to people, we wouldn't have Mortal Kombat or Pulp Fiction or My Little Pony. And the multiplayer: I might be extremely bias when I say this since I don't play any multiplayer of any sorts (I'm a lonely ♥♥♥) but the singleplayer campaign easily outweighs the multiplayer in terms of quality and replayability. There are so many things you can do in the singleplayer that there's no real reason to even go to the mulitplayer. I mean, if you wanted to good arena shooter, go play Quake III or Unreal Tournament.

Long story short: this game makes my jimmies all tingly.\t\t\t\t\t
