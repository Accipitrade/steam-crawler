\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a good game that holds up even to this day amongst many other titles but isn't without its flaws.

It's one of the best run and gun mindless shooting games out there right now, but that's all there is to it.

Multiplayer isn't worth your time to play more than once, game play feels choppy and sluggish at certain points many of the physics objects launching you 400 ft into the air or launching you forward, game crashed and froze often, at one point I fell through the map and died while using the berserk power-up on an enemy that was 1 meter away from me. Story was okay for what the game is, the hidden lore often felt more interesting than the actual streamlined story in the game which is disappointing knowing they could have done more with it. The soundtrack for the game was really well made, I felt at some points there should have been music playing when there wasn't any but would also play an intense song when i had to fight 10 imps. The game also felt dragged out at certain points, along with an extremely shallow upgrade system by today's standards.

To conclude, if you haven't played Doom yet I would pick it up before playing eternal, or even if you have no intentions of buying or playing eternal I think it's worth playing at least once.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 1993 was the fps that molded the genre into what it is today and with that legacy DOOM 2016 had a lot to live up to, and it came out just as good if not better than the 1993 classic. The amount of variety in weapons and upgrades is crazy and makes the fantastic combat system infinitely re-playable. And on the topic of the combat system there's the big new selling point of the game, Glory Kills. Glory Kills are quick attacks that you can use on enemies when they are staggered to do a gory finisher that drops health. There was a lot of debate on whether these would slow down the pace of the battle or not but in the end not only did they make them fit into the flow of battle but they also made them enhance the strategic aspect of the game. And then there's the music which as a not so big of a fan of the heavy metal genre i can say that i loved it. Mick Gordon did an amazing job at making music that fit the bloody fast paced action of the game and made it a million times more stress relieving to rip and tear through demons. But action isn't all DOOM has to offer, much like the original; DOOM has many secrets to find by thoroughly exploring the thirteen levels it has to offer. While they don't do much for rewards they do add a ton of replay value to an already very re-playable game. In conclusion DOOM is a fantastic game that defied all expectations and to some even surpassed the genre defining classic the preceded it. And in an age where the fps genre is littered with games of the same old over-saturated formula DOOM brings a breath of fresh air that will surely be remembered for years to come.

8/10\t\t\t\t\t
