\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a game that no one expected. It delivers an amazing campaign. After the corridor laden, single enemy at time, monster closet, and jump scare laden game that was Doom 3 id Software decided to try and recapture the feeling of Doom 1 and 2. Every environment is an arena filled with demons requiring quick movement and a steady hand to brutally destroy. This makes the game feel a lot like a character action game in the style of Devil May Cry more than a modern first person shooter. The story is simple and revels in that simplicity. You play as the reawoken Doom Marine finding yourself perhaps once again on a research base on Mars that has been overrun by demons after a research opened a portal to Hell. What makes the story fun is how the player character is presented as a complete badass who is going to do anything to stop the stupidity and destroy the demons that are more afraid of him than he is of them.


DOOM also has two other modes multiplayer and Snapmap. I can’t really comment on the multiplayer as I didn’t spend any time with it. Snapmap is a level creation and sharing suite. It includes a tutorial and puzzle mode which I loved as it requires some knowledge of game engines and teaches complex logic design. Recently id added in classic Doom map parts and more options for how weapons can be used. While I don’t have a real opinion on multiplayer Snapmap is a good value add and worth checking out when you are done with the campaign.
