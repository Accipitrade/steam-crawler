\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the record this is my first review

DOOM if the title isnt enough I dont know what is. I played the original when I was a little kid thanks to my father. He is a fan of the original and so am I. Yet again thanks to the same person I bought this one. I don't regret getting this game for a minute. It has everything a DOOM game should have.

-Ugly as f... enemies (Check)
- Blood and gore everywhere (Check)
- Big (redacted) Gun (Check)
- Fast paced action (Check)
-Repetitive Gameplay(check)

Now I'm going to be honest the meat of the game is in the campaign. The story isn't complicated but it is pretty interesting to follow, the gameplay is pretty much as the original with a few upgrades besides the graphics such as upgradable weapons, glory kills, upgradable armor, runes (which are basically perks) and more. Also if the difficulty ain't much of a challenge (which I doubt) there are literal challenges on almost every mission. And it wouldn't be a DOOM game if it didn't have Secret Areas there are a lot of them and they don't need to be necessarily for collectibles. My point is that the campaign has lots of features to experience with lots of gameplay.

Now the multiplayer is basically as the other shooter multiplayers out there with a feature that lets you become a F.... killer demon. You have customizable armor, weapons(cosmetics) and loadouts. Can't talk a lot about multiplayer since i've only spent 3 hours or so on it but it's enjoyable and features glory kills as well.

And like I've seen some people call it "the cherry on top" we have the snap-map where you can create your own maps and share your creations. It has user friendly tutorials and though it has its limitations it is fun to use. And if creating isn't enough you can also play other peoples creations and vote if you liked or disliked.

Anyways that was DOOM

Now for my verdict 10/10

It's Fun, fast-paced and Gory which is all wanted it to be.

P.S. There are secret levers that unlock some of the original levels to play with the new mechanics

*For the special prices the game is a steal\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t- Game sticks to the more classic feel of FPS (Simple objectives [including story], Powerups, No Health Regeneration, Health Pickups).
- Fast pace (no sprinting)
- Variety of different weapons (no reloading)
- Variety of enemies
- Great level design
- Smooth graphics (not the best I've seen)
- Lengthy FPS Campaign
"MULTIPLAYER"
-Loadouts (less classic FPS)
-Simple game modes (TDM, CTF, Domination) [Few other different modes]
-... I think that's about it
"CONTROVERSY"
- Glory Kills: The game is not designed to mandate glory kills. It is beneficial but not by a lot. The game mechanic is just there and you can also disable the colors that flash that signify a "staggered" opponent. It actually enhances your tactic on taking out enemies. (I.E. - Low health with two staggered enemies around the corner with a hell knight chasing you. Do you risk glory killing to gain a small amount of health? If so, how much time before they “unstagger” and how will you create the opportunity to engage the glory kill?) I am for the glory killing!

{MY VERDICT: 8/10 : Love the campaign despite the repetitive nature of the objectives. The main thing you do is basically kill everything (demons). There are challenges throughout the levels you can complete that will upgrade your character, but those still consists of killing demons. There is a story present within the game but I was more focused on the massive hordes of enemies that behave and attack distinctly which causes you to move, shoot, and think on the fly. The gameplay will really keep you going and can prove to be a challenge. If you miss the classic feel of FPS back in the old days along with a mix of modern day FPS this is definitely a game you would enjoy. Would not call this a MUST HAVE/BUY but definitely a game I wouldn't miss out on playing. The multiplayer is extremely basic in my opinion although you can have fun for a little while. The focus of this game is obviously the Single Player Campaign.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a refreshing (and desperately needed) take on the franchise. More than anything, I loved the return to the twitch-shooting we saw in the late 90s and early 2000s. This is not a game where you approach a level slowly and methodically. This is a game where you move from zombie to demon in a adrenaline-fueled fury- blood and ichor raining in your wake. It is brutal and it is awesome. I loved it.

I just finished my campaign on Nightmare, and one of the most refreshing things for me was the difficulty of the campaign. It has been a long while since I had a pure FPS title with a campaign that gave me the challenge Doom has. That said, it wasn't so much that I was frustrated and wanted to give up. The campaign really does an awesome job at scaling up the difficulty chapter by chapter as they introduce new enemies and compositions.

I know there have been some mixed thoughts on the narrative that Doom tries to pursue. I've read the criticisms that it seems like annoyance to some, and that the aesthetic is too over-the-top into the edgy occult. I can't really say I agree on those points. I really did enjoy the story elements that the game put in place. To the criticisms of the occult iconography and corpses everywhere I would say that it's Doom. You kill demons and go to hell. Taking that into consideration, I think it's perfectly fine.

I loved this game, but I think it's important to underline a point here. If you do not like the feel of a twitch-shooter, do not buy it. It's a beautiful game, with a cool little narrative, and awesome arena-style gameplay everywhere, but all of that will be lost on you in a white-hot explosion of frustration if you walk into this expecting a tactical FPS.

TL;DR- Awesome gore-powered twitch-shooter.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI came into this game excited for the single player campaign -- wasn't too concerned about MP, but I thought snapmap might be cool.

The campgain is everything you could expect in a DOOM game. Fast, gory, brutal, skill-centric. I haven't had this much fun with a FPS in a long time. I've beaten the game on Nightmare (died a lot, but running through the second time with many fewer deaths), and one of the things I like so much is that the difficulty feels fair. Every time I died, it was justified -- I made a mistake (or several) and deserved to get beaten to death with my arms by a Revenant. It never felt cheap, and it never felt like I couldn't fix the mistake.

By the end of my Nighmare run, I felt like a total badass -- dodging and weaving through enemy fire, jumping, spinning, and putting a Gauss round right through a Mancubus' head. It is visceral and awesome.

Also, I happen to like metal, so the soundtrack is AMAZING. I think I actually caught myself headbanging to the combat music while butchering demons. This game delivers on so many levels. The campaign is insanely good, MP sucks, and snapmap is actually pretty cool. I love this game, and it reminds me so much of when I was a kid playing DOOM and DOOM II. Same thing, just with a fresh coat of paint and updated mechanics (where it needs it).

If you are running an older graphics card (I'm on a GTX 780), make sure you crank your card fan up and turn off some of the post-processing effects, otherwise you might have some overheating/crashing problems. I am running on 1920x1080 with everything set at high, and the best AA available (amazingly, it doesn't drop frame rate much at all). Never even a hiccup with this setup.

Rip and tear. Buy this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just wanted to write a quick review for DOOM. This is all my opinion and most will not be based on fact.

I personally refunded this game because its not my type of game. I like the more strategy and slow paced fighting, like Squad and Arma 3. The game itself is amazing, with the graphics looking astonishing on my 2gb 960 (surprisingly); One of the best games with great graphics that doesnt suck up all your vram. I can see this game being very fun for the fast-paced FPS player. I would recommend you to play it on Console, but if you want more precision stick with PC (doesnt seem like you need too much skill to kill anything, just point, run, and shoot). I would recommend this game to trigger happy, FPS players that enjoy COD and other games of that nature.


Though I did not like it, the finishers were better than Mortal Kombat IMO.

Rating: 10/10
Why?: Finishers and music and overall gameplay was fantastic, just not my genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOK folks, the question. Is it good.

Well you see this game, and in it's programing to be pretty much broken into seperate experiences and program sets, probably causing some of it's redundancy in hard drive space usage, litteraly creates a YES and NO.

Some of us were looking forward to the CO OP experience. And sadly much in part like the multi player, it's got issues.

Multi player is forgetable right off the bat, so much to the point that people are saying that the PVP litteraly created in the co op set ups, is actually better than the actual PVP maps and then some.

However it doesn't really make it a good answer to the PVP mode issues because some of us, as in a lot of us, are finding DOOM will just crash for no apparent reason durring games of snap map. Like even in the quiet moments. Particularly in those quiet moments where carnage is handled just fine, but an update seems to be the culperate. Still I have to keep re launching doom just to get back into co op and I don't even want to touch the PVP with that issue persisting lest I get labeled a rage quiter.

But here's the good news.

The campaign, it actually does the DOOM franchise justice. Signficantly so. Even in moments when you're feeling a bit lost, you're coming across bonuses, some great vistas, and if you're lost while fighting, it's probably because you're losing yourself in the carnage of blowing demons away in various ways, sometimes testing said various ways, as you watch their necks squirt crimson on the walls. Incredably accurate blood squirts to markings on walls no less.

The campagin is worth it, and being that the game litteraly has to re launch seperate components for multi player and co op, it litteraly feels like it's own game with the others loosely tagged on like back when I had to hop from Photo Shop 5.5 into image ready back in the year 2000. Technically they sell them too you together as a program, but the button really just opens that seperate program and carries your work. Only here your campagin efforts don't seem to carry into the other modes and vice versa.

The map search functions are also kind of improperly done, and saving the maps lets you work on them, but you can't just use the save feature for easy picking of favorites to play with your friends? Did I just miss something as I would figure there would be some kind of favorite maps option for that very reason, but it's simply down to community favorites, most up voted, popular, and some other stuff. Your personal loves be damned.

But that campagin. Damn that campaign. Story is there, but rarely abtrusive. It's in fractions you welcome for the occasional breather but quickly you're thrusted right back into the action. It litteraly feels like the good old DOOM with the melee glory kill system that was created, and bethesda style BORROWED, to make this good experience.

Word to the mod creator of ye old Brutal DOOM, they may not have credited you, but your work has changed the world brother. Well met, and people will still find your grand work and how it's blatantly lead to the glory kill system used in this game. Your footprint has been left.

Plagerism aside, campaign is fun. And that's what matters. It's fun and you can even play detective a bit if you choose to learn what really happened to some of those laying around people. Yet it doesn't tie your hands often and force you to sit through each part of it.

The ways that youcan come close to deaththen pull it back with a few good super kungful looking kills, or even breaking out the good old chainsaw to really bring it back, keeps the ballance in how you can get hurt bad, but still skill your way out. I can't remember who said there's no pick ups, because they're there too, but your whole life dosn't need to hinge upon them alone, and to even go for them or that kill instead could be a life and death decesion in it's self.

Some enemies animations cycle a bit much too regularly, like our favorite fat boy with flame throwers, but the fact I'm knit picking at that is saying something as most of the others move about rather fluently with different leaps and swung attacks and still with reasonable visual keys to make you feel smart and on the ball with how to deal with what's coming if you're sharp.

And the soundtrack is rightfuly metal, still kind of surprised we're not seeing that OST offering right up a few inches to buy said OST. Maybe after this positing we'll see it. It's justified.









So in conclusion, right now as it stands. Snap is a neat idea, but by no means is it stable. What's going to hurt some of their plans is I think the DLC is mostly geared tword that and multi player, when really the hero here is the one player game, which I really wold've loved a reason to co op a story. I was kind of hoping co op could be treated by fans as a storry endeavor. And in a way it can. But again it's not stable, and only two guns instead of the full rack is still bothering people as well. Even now people are petitioning for mod support, but in time it's going to come weather iD wants it or not. They may go that angery legal path, but they don't want that Activision or EA vibe, because iD still kinda needs those fans they have.

Get this game for the 1 player, and maybe hopes that snap map will have a better search system and be made stable. Expect a sale on summer sale sooner than later, but youdo want to enjoy the story mode for yourself, and that's coming from a guy who often likes to mix his game time with social time in co op.

But if the DLC is just for snap and multi player, well, they may find those sales not do so well... The best thing they can do is fix snap map, push an ideal of squads going in to continue an endeavor, and let people create story after story, mission after mission, and find easier ways to find them and subscribe to them by working in STEAM WORKSHOP or a similar system.

IF they do that, this game could carry on for a VERY long time. But that's a major IF right now.

Buy it for the Campagin right now, the rest is a maybe. If that means waiting till summer sale, I can't say I blame you. But that campaign was impressive enough for me to still recomend it.\t\t\t\t\t
