\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTwo hours into this game and I was ready to give up on Doom. It seemed boring and too easy but what kept me playing was all the cirtical praise it was getting. "Surely this will get more interesting at some point."

Indeed, it does. Glad I stuck with it because Doom is one of the best games I've played in some time. I've been a longtime Doom fan (since the original) and still have my original doom games from the 90s. Glad to see the series return to its roots.

If I had any negatives to say, it's that I get some strange graphicial glitches on environment textures from time to time and they're pretty jarring. Other than that, you NEED to buy this game. It's a nice change from modern shooters and their unwieldly storylines and plot devices.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely one of the best FPS titles on PC. Campaign features gradually harder and harder mob fights that will test your awareness and aim. I was also pretty surprised at how well some of the boss fights were done, although I felt the final boss could have been a little more difficult. Snapmap adds a lot of replay value, its a great time looking and testing custom levels with friends. The multiplayer is decent but if it had been more fleshed out and polished it could have been great. In my opinion, the Demon Runes are way too powerful, as well as shotguns. Some of the MP objective modes, like Freeze Tag for example, are pretty fun. The campaign alone is what this game focuses on, and makes it worth the purchase.\t\t\t\t\t
