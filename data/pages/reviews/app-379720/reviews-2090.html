\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSingle player : Great and fun shooter with a badass BGM. a "Must have" if you like old FPS games from the 90's like OG doom, duke nukem, quake, etc..

Multiplayer: complete garbage. Doesn't reward good aim, but instead it's focused on spam with shotguns and various explosive weapons. That means rocket launchers, mines, electric grenades, all kind of various grenades, grenade launchers, secondary fire modes for shotguns, etc. Forget about sniping. you're literally forced to use the scope to charge the weapon (it takes around 1.5 seconds which in arena type shooter game is completely ABSURD) for max dmg, and it still doesn't one shot enemy with the headshot since everyone has armor 95% of the time. This is like Halo forge on steroids, where everything is exploding and nobody is sure what the hell is going on. Death matches are either double shotgun spam, or exploding hell. You're either stealing the kills or someone is stealing the kills from you. There are some cool and fun game modes, but the current weapon meta problem outshines the fun parts. I'm a huge fan of arena shooters and I've played the majority of them and oh boy, this multiplayer mode needs some SERIOUS tweaking for balance and weapon mechanics. Also, I have no idea what's going on with damage, meaning, I do 120 damage with a sniper, and land 5 more shots with a heavy rifle which is like 50 extra dmg, so that's 170 right? and the guy that I just shot kills me, and still has 90HP left, and the max is 200 with a power up. Might be that the netcode is bad, at this point I don't even care. One of the recent games that did the arena shooter properly was TOXIKK. It's a real shame cuz the potential is there. definitely not recommended for multiplayer, but single player is worth the price.

IMPORTANT: Multiplayer is LITTERED with RU hackers, usually using speed hacks and aim assist. Sometimes the speed hack is barely noticeable (since you can change the speed level), but enough to give them huge advantage when it comes to movement and reloading. Sometimes it's over the top and easy to notice. They will also usually respawn with max armor and health all the time. DOOM staff doesn't really care about it, so it's best just to avoid the multiplayer aspect of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM pokes your brain to a primordial level of fun.

Games nowadays are too complex for the boomer gamer that plays to please himself and DOOM understands that, so it gives you the solution.

DOOM is, in its entirety and with everything considered, a mixture of run and shoot, a lot of different weapons, extreme visual gory violence, explosions and limbs flying, with the heaviest of metal left there blending everything (composed by Mick Gordon and boy is he good). And that's it. That's DOOM. 10/10. It doesn't feel poor, it's not boring, it's not monotonous, it will not stop making you have fun splashing demon blood all over the place.

For some maybe scared this game is campaign only and that's it (For 15 dollars it's worth tho), well it's not just that.
Multiplayer is still pretty active august 2019, and can be fun for a while. Arcade mode keeps the game challenging. You can try going for the permadeath difficulty if you're some kind of brain damaged psycho (yes I am one of those), plus there is Snapmap which lets you play community created singleplayer and multiplayer maps, create your own or gather some friends and have mindless fun, which is also what DOOM is all about, having fun in the most primitive and profound way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is an FPS trainer that is also fun. If you play this game the right way, and try to improve on your weaknesses, the skills you learn here will correlate to other games too. My TF2 skills have improved dramatically, for instance.

Important things I learned:

* When you dodge, always strafe. Don't jump unless you are trying to get somewhere. AI in doom (and players in multiplayer) shoot where you are going, and when you jump it is hard to change your path to dodge.
* Be aware of everything. Keep a list of all the enemies around you and where they are. Listen for sounds, and make use of 3D sound to determine where they are coming from.
* Keep moving. Do not go in circles. Go in random paths all over the place. Hit and run. Do not stay close to an enemy too long before moving on. Don't kill things one by one.
* Gage threats. If something is more likely to damage you and you will have a harder time dodging it, make it your priority to kill as fast as possible. However, don't forget about the other enemies around you at the same time.
