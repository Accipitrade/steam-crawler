The first aims to recreate the cinematic experience of film. Winding story-lines, thrilling set pieces, vast treasure, uncharted islands, and tombs ripe for raiding.
Doom does not belong to this category.
Doom belongs to the category of action games that's main goal is to cause any players with heart problems instant death, and to introduce heart problems in its healthy players.
This game is pure frenetic action. The excellent and diverse combat system always stays fresh and exciting through the varied landscapes. It's engaging to explore each sprawling level, propelled forward by the angry soundtrack. For each new monstrous challenge you will find an equally destructive upgrade to your arsenal. This fuels a constant arms race that always leaves you feeling like a terrifying death machine, but without every sacrificing the deadliness of combat.

I had heard so much about this game before playing and it really didn't disappoint.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe sound mixing for headphones is not super duper rub your frenulum experience, but you won't play this game that way. Nono, you intend to buy some Klipsch speakers from NewEgg, complete with a sub woofer, grounding cables, and a Yamaha receiver so everyone can hear the Rip and Tear soundtrack.

The game play is very front and center, you do not walk in predictable paths and use the shotgun up close. A shocking amount of platforming and maze running if you want to find the hidden things each level.

Brutal art.

I've played this using a controller, and with a keyboard/mouse. Honestly it is forgiving enough that I just use the controller. They laid the controls out in a clever manner and it allows a more relaxed posture when compared to the other option.

Grab this game.\t\t\t\t\t
