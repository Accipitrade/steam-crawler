\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom did a great job in bringing the classic style of shooter into a modern day game. If you want a great arcade style FPS, this is probably what you're looking for.
\t
\tThe gameplay is tight. Running, jumping, shooting, melee, it all feels good. You play as the Doom Marine, 20 lbs of rage packed into a 5 lb bag. And the game does a great job making you feel that way. The glory kills they added into the game (melee enemies after they have received a sufficient amount of damage) make a great addition to the gameplay. It's a way to make the enemies drop health, but it also keeps things fluid and visceral. Running and then jumping at an enemy, blasting them with a shotgun to the face when you land, turning around and shooting another from a little further distance to set up your glory kill, running forward and then ripping their arm off to beat them to death with it. That's what a pretty typical line of gameplay involves. And it works without a hitch.

\tThe environment is well done to. This game is focused on action, unlike Doom 3 which had a lot of horror elements. Gone is your need for a flashlight. All you need now is your, well I was going to say guns but honestly his fists work just as well. Even with that being the case, the environments are still something straight out of a horror movie. Blood sprayed on hallways, corpses hanging from the ceilings, alters made up of gutted torsos, and that just on Mars, not even in Hell. Which the game does take you to, bouncing you back and forth from Mars to Hell. And Hell has some pretty interesting landscapes as well, like a tunnel that you're going to go through and then you realize the entire entrance is actually the open mouth of some dead ancient demon. It gives off a very “desert of war and chaos” vibe to it.

\tThe graphics are fantastic. The models for all the demons, weapons, and characters are well designed and interesting. Watching the different glory kills for the different enemies you encounter (of which there is a lot) was one of my favorite parts of the game. It also ran very smoothly for me, without any hitching or frame drops even when the action got very intense
\t
\tMy biggest disappointment was for the storyline. It wasn't because it was bad, it just wasn't enough. The team outright says that they focused on the gameplay first, and story second. Which is kind of what you'd expect from a Doom game based on the original. And for many people, that's fine. I like my storylines though and after a while I became bored with killing demons just to kill them. Mind you my boredom kicked it at about the 9 hour mark and it's only an 11 hour game so it wasn't horrible or punishing to finish. I hope that for a sequel they spend more time on developing the story but I can understand the challenge to it. It's probably hard to write a compelling story for a character whose sole purpose is to kill demons.

\tThere was some story behind the game though, and it's interesting. In this version of Doom you're not just some marine stuck in a bad situation. You are specifically the “Doom Marine”, and there's some backstory that goes with that. There is also a lot of attention to the demons that you fight and some cool things you can learn about them. The areas of Hell also typically have backstory that you can look into that is well done. All of this extra story is optional though, delivered in data pad type devices you find, so you can skip it if you just came to kick ass.

\tThe last thing I want to touch on is the nice details they put into the game and especially the Doom Marine himself. As I've mentioned, he is rage incarnate, and everything he does exudes that. Grabbing a mod kit for your weapon from a helpful drone? Why take it easily when you can grab the case and then punch the drone away. Getting a power up energy crystal? Why put it into your suit when you can crush it in your fist and absorb the energy that way. And probably the ultimate example, the Berserk powerup. You store whatever weapon you're carrying and you then one hit kill any demon you can get your hands on with, you know, your hands. And then there are nice Easter eggs thrown into the game as well. Like a skeleton wearing a Skyrim helmet with an arrow in the knee, or the collectables being little Doom Marine action figures or, my favorite, when you fall into lava him giving the Terminator thumbs up. It's little touches like that that show the developers put a lot of thought and care into the game.

\tSo yea, if you want to experience what it would be like to throw an Anger Marine from Warhammer 40k into Hell then go ahead and pick up this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAWESOME game!! Probably one of the best single player experiences I've ever had!

The single player campaign took around 15 hours for me to complete on medium difficulty. The enemies are very well balanced and provide compelling gameplay regardless of the environment. Usually I hate platforming sections in my first person shooters but in this game it genuinely adds to the experience like no other game of its type. The weapons are all very well balanced (except for the plasma rifle - for some reason they made it really weak) and if the game is too difficult, you can use a variety of runes to modify how the game behaves (my favourite is the one that gives extra armour on each kill - came in very handy on the later levels!).

I tried nightmare mode today (only for a few minutes on the first level, just to see what it was like) and got my a$$ handed to me - but I already know, I will definitely be returning for more!

I've noticed many people saying that the multiplayer part of the game was a massive disappointment - well, don't worry, the single player campaign alone is worth the money! Probably one of the best games I've played in the last ten years. Cannot recommend highly enough!\t\t\t\t\t
