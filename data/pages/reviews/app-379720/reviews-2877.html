\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay/Mechanics - 5/5
As far as FPSs go, this game is as crisp as it gets. DOOM (2016) goes back to the Quake-era of gaming where you are always holding down at least one movement key as you are snap-aiming enemies, plotting and timing rocket trajectories, and twitch-shooting anything that moves. The movement, which is already tight, can be further upgraded so that you can double-jump and even better control your movement in-air. If you are not used to twitch-shooters, you may have a hard time aiming at first. However within minutes, you naturally adapt to the mechanic. Also, some weapons in-game are more forgiving as you upgrade them: rockets can be air-bursted with an alternate fire or homed by holding down that alternate fire, etc. Overall, I had full control and never felt punished by clunky inputs.

Visuals/Audio - 5/5
This game is downright gorgeous and immersive, that is if you don't mind a gory aesthetic with demonic sounds (which why would you be playing DOOM if you didn't?). The cut-scenes are well made, some in and out of game, and are pretty. The big details of the geometry of the large maps down to the little details of the blood splatters and scratched-glass really help give you a sense of the story rather than just give you narrative. The audio is fantastic as well, each enemy is distinct in sound: helping you grasp your surrounding threats perfectly. An enemy type in particular which is one of the weakest makes a noise that blares loudly into your speakers/headphones; it literally sounds like thousands when there are normally one or a few. This further projects the player into the immersive world and increases that level of enjoyment.

Story/Plot - 3/5
I tried hard not to spoil anything here. If you think "any information" is essentially spoilers, stop reading here. The only mediocre part of this game was the story, and even then it was just as good as most testosterone-fueled movies. By the time you get to a certain point in the game, you can predict the ending with pretty decent accuracy. That's all I will say about that. The plot merely serves as a vessel for propelling the shoot-em-up protagonist, the DOOM Soldier, to kill and destroy literally *everything* and have no second thoughts about it. In a way, it makes the game more appealing though: these are *demons* that are trying to kill you... why not kill them all? Even with the story being semi-shallow, this game is still fantastic and worth a play-through.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimply excellent. On a technical level, in terms of gameplay, ingenuity and pure fun, this has to be the best shooter in year.

Single Player Pros:
- Looks and sounds amazing and runs very smoothly. Beyond the fidelity of the graphics, though, the environments are really detailed in a way that gives some insight into the world around you. Not that DOOM is a franchise with lots of deep, meaningful lore to consider, but still.
- Hugely satisfying combat. "Glory Kills" let you beat a staggered enemy to death (sometimes with their own body parts), and doing so grants you extra health or ammo drops. So combat is all about chaining together a series of gruesome kills. Combat is really fast, dynamic and unforgiving (no health regen - if you are low on health, your best bet is to get in close and do one of these glory kills).
- Great weapon variety. You have the run of the mill FPS tools like a pistol, shotgun, machine gun, etc, but each can be equipped with multiple mods that either change its function completlely or add unique special abilities. On the machine gun, for example - add a sniper's scope, or add a mini rocket pod. Swap between these mods on the fly based on the enemies at hand.
- Platforming, level design and exploration. My biggest surprise with DOOM was that it has a lot of relatively well-done first person platforming baked into the level design. Exploring the levels gives you cool rewards (and also lets you slow down and appreciate the details in the environments), so the platforming is crucial. The levels aren't open world in nature and aren't that large, all things considered, but many are constructed like a labyrinth with multple layers. In many respects the game reminds me of Far Cry 3 (in a good way), save for the open world aspect: There's the platforming, the way that information is logged in your dossier, the way you upgrade your suit, and especially in the "Trial Runes" you find in your exploraitons, which challenge you to a weapon-based mini-game, usually a time trial, totally seperate from the main game environment, and reward you with an ability to use in the main game.
- Great enemy variety and AI. You have your up close melee enemies, your sniper enemies, your heavy tank enemies, your mid range fireball throwing enemeis, your mega demons, basic zombies, flying heads and more. They all work together like a team and will swarm you if you slow down. They are always on the move, too, with the human sized-guys jumping and climbing all over the map just like you. Every skirmish is unique.
- Wit and humor. DOOM doesn't take itself too seriously, but it doesn't treat itself like a joke either. It's always very fun and very tense, while also not losing sight of the fact that there is something inherently comical about the whole premise and that hordes of demons are always very cartoony.


General Cons:
- Multiplayer is pretty basic in comparison to what else is available these days. For me this wasn't the selling point, but it may reduce lifetime replayability. I admit that this kind of classic deathmatch mutliplayer is not my favorite.
- Campaign difficulty may trend towards the easier side of things, but there are at least a lot of modes to choose from.
- Checkpoint spacing in campaign sometimes doesn't make a lot of sense.
- Some minor gameplay things are never explained, to their detriment. For example, when you get the BFG it may stress you out that you can't find it on the weapon wheel... did it glitch out? Is it gone? No, for some reason the BFG is available by pressing T, not by selecting weapons. Weird.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinished the campaign on ultra-violence just a few minutes ago and wow, I'm blown away.

DOOM perfectly marries brutal gunplay and agile movement in a way no other game has. It also has an amazing retro style with plenty of secrets and challenging boss fights, which to me sells that game as GOTY 2016. There's so much time and effort apparent in the design of each level, which is something rare to find in a game these days. Each of the 13 chapters are engaging and interesting, with final fights in each that put your skill to the ultimate test.

Several times during the story I was tempted to lower the difficulty, but I'm so glad I saw it through. All of the boss fights come close to Dark Souls in difficulty, with challenging movesets and attacks to learn through endless deaths. Taking down those smucks was such a pay off though.

On top of the immaculate game design, DOOM is the only game besides Halo that offers three fully develped modes: An amazing 10+ hour campaign, Snapmap for users to design and play their own PVE modes, and a smooth multiplayer experience complete with playable demons.

All in all worth $60 and then some, but with sales you'd be crazy not to pick it up for $20-30. I'll be playing through the arcade mode for many years to come, even after playing plenty of the story and multiplayer.

VIVA LA DOOM!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you call yourself a fan of the shooter genre, you should remeber that shooters of the early age of videogaming were not called shooters; they were called DOOM-clones... and current DOOM behaves and feels exactly like that.

No cover system, no regening health, no place to hide from the demons and no cutscenes that take you out of the action. If you stand still you die, if you dont shoot you die, if you try to run you die, the only way to live is to kill what comes from hell to kill you. Your character does not give a about what other people say in this game, his ony purpose is to up demons and empty hell from what ever lives inside it - There is no better way that I can sum-up the tone of this game.

The game encourages you to be very aggressive by letting killed enemys drop health and ammo, that you otherwise have to scavenge by exploring the map, which near impossible during combat. You have to keep moving since the great AI will not sit back and snipe at you, but will swarm and surround you if you stay on one place for too long. The different weapons are all very satisfying to use and you will probably have to use most of them at one point in the game due to ammo shortage. DOOM runs smooth as silk no matter how much stuff is jumping, exploding or shooting next to your face. The sound is fantastic, the weapons sound beefy and the rock music is exactly what you would want to hear during the glorious facemelting the game provides.

This game is what most modern singleplayer shooters are not: very fast, extremely unforgiving, technically excellent and most important: fun from the fist moment to the very last

If you call yourself a fan of the shooter genre: you are required to play this game, this quintessence of what a shooter should be.\t\t\t\t\t
