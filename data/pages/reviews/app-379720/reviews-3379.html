I have not had a chance to mess with SnapMap yet but pretty excited to try that out and see what other players have built.

campaign

the level design is awesome. in many levels you won't just go from point a to point b. you can actually explore the level and play it like classic doom, find colored key cards, open new areas and so forth. the gameplay is also really fast and fun, because it rewards for moving constantly, jumping around and shooting enemies with shotgun to their face! this is no call of duty or halo. you will not be hiding behind rocs. you will punch your enemies to utter death! the graphics look also nice, even if i use gtx750ti (2gb ddr5) and fx-8350. just run it at 900p and you will get around 40-30fps. And the music. oh, my, god. it feels like i'm playing quake 2 all over again! it is truly amazing. maybe not as memorable, but it sounds super cool when fighting against hordes of demons! oh yeah. and you are the original doom guy! f yeah!!

multiplayer

the multiplayer is at least in my opinion very fun, and just like the campaign, you must constantly be moving and jumping around if you want to survive. and you must know how to aim. this is again, no call of duty. it takes more than reflexes to kill somebody. not just one shot like in cod. usually takes like 3 shots from super shotgun to kill somebody. yeah. and it is fun because kills feel more meaningful. i would rather have 20 kills in doom than 50 in cod. (why am i comparing this to cod anyways. The only cod i own is ghosts and you all know how much that sucked xD) i feel like the leveling up is way too fast. you will level up after every mach. but you unlock a lot of cool stuff, like colors and markings that you can apply to your armor or guns, new armor parts, emotes and so forth. i don't relly care about the rpg style leveling systems in shoters. they just feel pointels and don't tell anything about your skill level. i would much rather have cs go style ranking system where you have a higher rank the better you do in competetive game mode.

i have not used the snapmap feature much, just played couple of levels. but that will keep the game alive if you are not going to be buying the 40€ season pass! c'moon. i'm a poor student. i already paid 60€ for this game!

but all and all, this is a great game, and i highly suggest you to buy it! and no, you need something else than a 200€ laptop and a touchpad mouse to play this...just saying, because somebody will do that for some reason...and that somebody will be on my team playing multiplayer -_-\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Gnome Seal of Approval*
So far, I'm enjoying this more than I expected. I feared id would continue down the path they took in Doom 3 with dark rooms and corridors, jump scares and a game not in line with the previous installments regarding atmosphere. I didn't mind the darkness so much in Doom 3, I just didn't think it was the right way to go with a Doom game.

In this new Doom game, the levels a brightly lit, the action is fast paced and fluid and your adrenaline is flowing.

This is what Doom 3 should have been!

The level design is fairly non-linear (at least compared to other shooters out there), but with some of the same backtracking we used to do in the original Doom and Doom 2.

The enemies are fun, diverse and nicely modelled and animated.

The weapon and suit upgrading system is fun and encourages exploration and experimentation. The map challenges are some minor goals you can do to earn a few extra upgrading points, but your main source comes from clearing the maps for enemies. This means that casual players can just run through the campaign, yet still earn upgrades. The Rune trials you eventually encounter are yet another way of customizing playstyle and upgrading your character.

The soundtrack also helps to make combat fun and engaging.

So, there's a lot to explore and fun stuff to do! I like it!

There's even classic Doom maps to unlock and play with the new enemies and weapons, but with the classic graphics everywhere else.

My only minor complaint so far is regarding secrets. In early levels, the game lets you backtrack to all areas so you can explore and search for secrets in peace. However, soon enough you will encounter doors that lock the way behind you so you can't go back and look for missed secrets until you finish the level. I've never been a fan of this in any game. If a game have secrets and collectibles, please enable the player to go back and search for them if he/she missed some.

I have not played multiplayer. This is purely a singleplayer review.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe only experiance I have had with the previous DOOM games is with the DOOM 2 Brutal Doom mod. I reallly liked it and I enjoyed the new Wolfenstein games so I got DOOM. First of all, disregard all of the negetive reviews from the goons who played the game for under 2 hours and passed judgement on the entire thing, the first hour or so of the campaign eases you into the game and does not represent full fledged gameplay. I've played mostly single player on ultraviolent so far.

PROS:
No minimap, health regen, or any other COD/modern casual shooter BS
Completely unrealistic
Decent AI
Combat feels good, faster than originals but works well by making fights tense and surprising
appropriately hard
well optimized and silky smooth even during big fights (Im running it on the Ultra preset at 1080p on a GTX 970, fps on avg 60 varies from 55-65)
Tons of secrets and easter eggs, many of which give you perks, secret levels from DOOM 1,2 included and harder to find than the secret levels in Wolfenstein
Upgrade system is simple but matters and adds variety
Weapon mods are really cool and each one has its own skill tree for further variation
Glory kills work well and dont feel like cheap gimmicks
levels are varied and more complex than original games
still has classic elements key cards, door noises, many classic enemies are reworked and improved
Snapmap-integrated level creation and modding tools
Power-ups work better
Limited story-Unlimited shooting

CONS:
Campaign is a little slow early on
Map shows locations of EVERYTHING (using it is optional though)
No campaign Coop
Multiplayer is eh but Im not really a fan of arena multiplayer, but from the bit I played, everything seemed to work well and finding a match was pretty easy
Multiplayer DLC- I'd like to see more official single player levels

Overall DOOM is gory, challenging, and not held back by story elements. The singleplayer is Great and makes the game worth it on its own. The multiplayers not my style but technically works well. Cant wait to go to Hell and back and back to Hell and....back again!
