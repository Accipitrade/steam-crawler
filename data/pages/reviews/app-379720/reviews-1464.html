\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith "Eternal" out I decided to give Doom 2016 another chance. When it came out I was put off by the extremely fast pace of the game, as it had been a long time since I played the likes of Unreal Tournament. In hindsight, if I'd thought of it like those "old school" FPS, I probably would have enjoyed it more. I think I bought it, played the first 30-40 minutes, said "man I suck at FPS now" and let it go till a couple days ago. Now, coming back with a controller (sacrilege I know) and a different mindset, I'm finding it a much better game than I remembered. There's a cool upgrade system, the level design really isn't all that confusing if you use the automap, and the relatively fast pace feels more like a call-back than a burden.

It doesn't hurt that my current machine is more than up to the task -- playing on a 65" LG B9 OLED with G-Sync enabled at 4K I'm averaging 100FPS with every setting but motion blur (off) maxed. You have to hand it to ID, they know how to build an engine.

If you're looking to relive the old school, look no further. Rip and tear, until it is done -- I know I will, this time.\t\t\t\t\t
