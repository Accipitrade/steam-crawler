\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a pretty freakin fantastic game. It's got a great story that can be easily ignored during second, third, or fourth playthroughs. The cutscenes (if you can call them that) last seconds at most and are far and few. However, in that short time, you can get this amazing world of demons and doomguys. The doomguy character in itself is amazing, the way he angrily reacts to Samuel Hayden's justification for the Argent Energy project (the one that caused this DOOM game, basically) tells an amazing story about your character, just by itself.

Aside from all the amazing story points this game has, it's actual playability is even better. There is nothing more cathartic than literally ripping in half a demon before doing a 360 and punching another one's head so hard it explodes. The difficulty levels are the first ones I've ever used in a game, because as time went on I actually got better at it. As of now, I've completed a nightmare run, a pistol-only run, every other difficulty run, and one kinda special run where you don't upgrade any weapons or your suit. They were all great.

Honestly I'm not sure why it says I have so few hours on DOOM. It feels like my most played game of 2017, and even though so far I don't have 2x the money cost in hours (which is my general rule for a good game), DOOM is definitely worth it.
