\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is essentiall the heavy metal big brother of Halo:
You are a superhuman in a specially designed suit that fights on an alien planet to prevent a total catastrophe and invasion.
To do this, you pick up weapons and upgrade them (not like Halo in this point) and smash demon's heads left and right.
In the multiplayer, it is generally a cluster♥♥♥♥ of rockets flying around, fps dropping, a giant demon frying your brains out and oneshotting everyone and that one annoying sniper that somehow always know where you are. But it's fun. Featured gamemodes include: King of the hill (or dominion), Kill Confirmed, Team Deathmatch and Protect the Payload.
All of that is supported by the heaviest of metals and coated in a beautifully designed character customization.

Overall a very solid game that has high entertainment value and can easily keep you captivated for quite some time.

Would I recommend purchasing this game? Well, let me rephrase that question for you: Can you kick the Devil's ass with just a shotgun? Hell yes!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - Buy this game.

All the gut-ripping goodness we love from Sgt. Mark's Brutal Doom, wrapped in super-shiny graphics, with music that sounds like Messhuggah trying to cover the original Resident Evil movie OST.

The narrative tries just hard enough to pass off as a story from "Heavy Metal", which works considering the whole b-movie premise of Doom, and honestly I was too busy feeding demons their own entrails to bother asking questions like: "why are those dudes wearing medieval armor?" or "why does hell look like outworld from the 90's Mortal Kombat movie?"

While Doom 3 brought a lot of jump-scares and survival horror elements into the mix (demon bug-babies anyone?), the new Doom campaign levels are essentially interconnected combat-arenas. You walk into a large space that looks suspiciously like a parkor course, all the doors lock, and then demons start teleporting in. This formula gets pretty repetetive...however the insanely fun combat, polished gameplay, gorgeous environments, and awesome variety of demons keeps things feeling fresh right up to the end.

Speaking of the end, though the game is short, new content is being churned out faster than you can say "snap-map". Check out the community-made solo maps, or my personal favorite survival modes for some "killing floor" style gorefests.
