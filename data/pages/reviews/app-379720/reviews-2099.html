\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's pretty good, but not more. I'd say that out of 4 main games this is the worst Doom yet.

+ Combat is great
+ Most weapons are really awesome, have good sound design.
+ First 5 or so levels have really good level design, they are pretty open and full of secrets.
+ Secrets are awesome.
+ Game has tons of different upgradable things, like weapons, weapon modes, Doom guy himself, runes and more.
+ Rune chalanges are fun, too easy though.
+ Last 2 levels are really awesome, they have best combat situations.
+ Boss battles.
+ It has somewhat storyline. There's nothing extraordinary about it, but best Doom game storyline yet.
+ Decent multiplayer
+ Snapmaps. Users' made maps. Pretty much endless content. Sadly, most of user made levels suck hard. I found some really nice ones, but most are horrible and I played best rated and most popular ones.

- Middle game levels are really bland and level design is boring. They are very linear. Huge stepdown from first levels.
- Colors. Game is mostly grey and somewhat pink. Looks ugly. Really missing contrast colors and much more red would be great instead of somewhat pink.
- Hell design is boring. Mostly mountain rocks and botomless pits. Where's lava, goryness and any kind of random stuff hell should have? Doom 1/2/3 had much, much better hell designs.
- Too much platforming
- Items looks ugly. I know it's weird of a complaint. They all look like not real, like holograms or something.
- Highlights for glory kills looks bad and not fitting. Such highlights are like holograms, if enemies would be robots, I'd say it's decent, but as for demons such highlighting design is really not fitting. At least I can disable them in options which is a huge plus.
- Shotgun is so underpowered and boring to use. On the other hand at least double barreled shotgun is really awesome.
- Can't run. This really annoys me. For the first few hours I was waiting for a running tutorial, but it never happened. Walking speed is good, but even faster for old-school game is never a bad thing.
- Horrible saving system. It's only checkpoints and those are one of the worst I've seen. In more open levels checkpoints will make a huge backtrack. It sucks.
- Gameplay too much relies on glory kills.
- How dare they use Dubstep instead of instrumental heavy metal?!? It sounds somewhat similar, but nowhere near as good. It's discrace to original Doom games.


7/10\t\t\t\t\t

The Mindless Bloody Shooting
Bloody hours of endless fun, thats what this game is all about. It took me about 8 hours to complete campaign, but I didnt seek secrets, they just came to my way sometimes. You can choose weapon wheel and you are not limited in the number of weapons you can hold. My personal favs are Combat shotgun and heavy assault rifle. The level design is great, theyre not same, they have many sideways and options how to strike, and they look gorgeous. You get rocket boots to boost your jumps, they give game more action and speed up fights. Frequently you will run out of ammo, but always you have your chainsaw which will after glory-kill give you huge amount of ammo. The glory-kills get repetitive, but sometimes you will need to heal and its best way how to gain HP and ammo. This game is Moronic shooter, but Its fun and thats what game are for. Also, music in background is cool and every time youll get into fight it changes immediately and clearly. Many times, you will hear that legendary E1M1 from very first DOOM. I made it on medium difficulty, but game gave me some hard time, but mostly it was very delightful. Cant wait to try harder difficulty. DOOM 2016 also has multiplayer, but I dont care about it, because there are better games that are meant to be played in MP but give it a shot. I got it 6,59 and I would pay even 20 for it. Great game, brutal fun \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of, if not the best game in recent history, and one of the best games of all time. The control is perfect, level design top-notch, music incredible and ingeniously integrated, and fun factor cranked to 11. Everyone owes it to themselves to play this game within their lifetime. The feeling of pure power fantasy where you are the most feared thing on the map makes it impossible to not crack a huge grin of joy while punching a demon's jaw off so hard that you snap its neck in the process. The weapon sandbox is varied without making any of them feel identical; even in the final level you'll still have a reason to pull put the shotgun over the super shotgun, or the heavy machine gun over the chaingun, because they have their own role to fill, and the attachments allow you to tweak them to suit your play style. Mobility is key both in combat and traversing the map. You're tested on your ability to strafe and maneuver around enemies while fighting, and your platforming prowess while exploring. The difficulty curve is superb; in the beginning, you're given a handful of basic enemies and weapons to get to grips with how combat works, and by the end you have to use everything you've learned to come out on top against combinations of every non-boss enemy type. Throw in unlockable rune trails and classic maps from the first two games, and you have one of the most complete singleplayer packages with no need for DLC or microtransactions to get in the way of pure, violent, demon-slaying fun.\t\t\t\t\t
