\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really, really wanted to like this. But it's hard to. The level structure and map layout for all major battles is quite poor and combined with the way the game is setup - for you to simply run around and shoot, hoping for the best - you quite often find yourself running into small ledges or barriers that prevent movement in confined spaces. This would be ok, except that you are woefully underpowered against all but the weakest enemies - and as soon as you are caught, you die. And you are just about the slowest thing in the game....

This could have been good. The enemies are good and varied, the weapons are familiar to anyone (although reloading times and fire speed - whilst quite possibly realistic, aren't suited to the run and gun style - as your ammo is very limited and some weapons are largely useless in most conflicts as they either don't fire quickly enough or don't have enough stopping power). The settings are good and there are lots of details and upgrades/challenges to complete. But when you can only play it one way - there is minimal replayability.

Maybe I'm just getting old, but I don't remember charging wildly into every level in Doom all those years ago shooting ammo randomly, jumping everywhere and just hoping I don't fall of a ledge to my death or get stuck against a non visible barrier...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust beat it a few days ago and had an absolute blast. Everything about this game was great.

Pros
- The sound in this game is wonderful. Each gun, monster, explosion, or even melee kill were perfect. Each gun felt like it packed a real punch and were very delightful to use.

- Lots of gun variety. Each fight had me switching to almost every gun (besides the pistol) to get the job done and to take down specific enemies. Each one is different and has a different monster/scenario that suits it best. You could use them all though if you really wanted.

- The gameplay is great. Moving super fast around rooms, dodging enemies attacks and switching to different guns to mow them down is a fun time. Searching for the secrets even was enjoyable to me and some of the references were hilarious.

- The music in this game cannot go unmentioned. The music would kick in at perfect times and would fit the scenario perfectly. Made a lot of these fights feel absolutely epic.

- This game ran perfectly for me on my system fully maxed out settings at 1080p I was getting 90-125fps depending on the location. It might be different for others depending on their PC specs of course. I was using an i5-4590, 8gb ddr3 ram, and a GTX 1060.

Cons
- The only con I really had with this game is that I felt it was just a tiny bit too short. I finished it on Normal difficulty and did not run through the game as fast as I could or anything and I finished it in 10.2 hours. I'm sure if you played it on a higher difficulty or replayed it, you would not think it was short at all. This did not ruin the experience of the game for me in any way though and I still absolutely recommend it.

Overall: I would rate this game an 8/10. Highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM.


Yes.


If you enjoy high-octane action, full of heavy metal riffs, dead demons on Mars, guns, guns, guns. Then go for this new DOOM, I REALLY recommend it, I haven't played the previous ones, haven't been really immersed into the DOOM universe (If you really can be in between shooting demons in the face) but this new iteration is on par with modern games (with codex entries, lore all around the place, modern graphics) but still has that taste of the good old father of FPS. (fast paced, visceral combat, satisfying guns, amazing responses from targets)

And the MUSIC, Oh. My. God. ... I love it. Great metal soundtrack, that has you pumped to shoot more demons. Mindless fun. That is most definetly NOT a bad thing.
