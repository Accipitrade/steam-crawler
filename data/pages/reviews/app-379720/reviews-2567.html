\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best single player FPS out atm. Alot of love went into this game, so if you've been sitting on this and not playing it for fear of it ruining the series, you've nothing to fear. Plus its optimization is godly and it can run on any potato or toaster

The level design tries to harken back to the originals and succeeds for the most part. It gives you reason to backtrack and explore your enviroment yet keeps you forward focused enough so as not to break its own pacing unless the player chooses to do so.

The gunplay is also extremely solid. You can even set the gun to sit in the middle of the screen instead of the side. That said, every weapon feels powerful and useful. They allow for approaching different situations in whatever manner you want and manages to avoid pigeonholing them. They really do an amazing job balancing the feeling of the weapons being overpowered, yet actually aren't, as well. There's also no janky animations in reload and moving that make the guns feel disconnected from the character

The only con I'd have for the game is the enemy spawning. While is isn't always true, It basically locks you into fighting arenas and you kill them as they come in. While I suppose this gives control to the developers on how each situation might play out, it just doesn't feel as good as seeing things naturally existing in the world and removes a bit of fun in randomness or strategizing a more in depth way of approaching a situation. Although, they handle it in a way that makes sense - you don't just feel locked in because it's the games mechanic, but rather that it's a natural thing to occur.\t\t\t\t\t
