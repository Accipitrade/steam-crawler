\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is one of those games I've grown up with & witness its evolution over the years; ssssoooo this may have some bias.

Performance:
First thing I noticed is how smooth the game runs, I have yet to see any framedrops or movement glitches while having some great graphics. The only time I've seen a drop in frames was during load times which are actually pretty quick. While going through the vid settings I was hesitant on removing the motion blur but as it turns out it really makes turning more realistic but at the same time doesn't hinder your aiming. Something I've had a bit of a problem with previous AAA titles.

Story
The story is paper thin but that was something I was expecting from a DOOM title. Out of all the releases of the series I think DOOM 3 probably has the deepest, which in itself doesn't say much. I haven't finished the game yet but so far I'm not seeing any philosophical deep meaning of the chick opening the portal to hell.

Gameplay
The pace is fast which I find kind of ties into why the game runs so smooth. If it runs like ♥♥♥♥ then the whole idea of a quick paced game is moot. When enemies surround you the freedom of movement you have is something that needs to be considered as a weapon within itself. Repositioning yourself constantly really gets you to think/panic the best choice of action in heavy enemy areas. Also the variety of baddies when you come to a horde is diverse so there are different things to consider when dealing with all of them together. Unreal players will appreciate the gameplay. The health/ammo drops I thought at the beginning were too abundant but as I progressed I found them as little nuggets of relief. The glory kills are cool but there aren't many for each enemy & the really cool ones I'm still figuring out exactly how to trigger them.

Multiplayer
Now I've only played MP during the beta so I am not going to go into much detail. It was fun but the map they had was small & only deathmatch. But becoming a Revenant striking fear into your enemies as you jump down from the second level blasting your rockets watching them turn into puddles of meat was awesome. And yes there is voice chat.

Atmosphere
DOOM has always had a kind of dread when it came to the feel of the games. When I read that Mick Gordon was composing the soundtrack my nipples got hard. I loved what he & Fredrik Thorendal put together for Wolfenstein: The New Order; even have the soundtrack & will probaly get this one too. It's messy, depressing, & fits what DOOM is all about. The levels tend to run together as far as in a facility....then outside on mars...then in another facility...back outside again, it gets redundant. I guess now I just want to go to hell which is something I shouldn't be thinking about as far as where I'm at in the game. Looking out at the horizon when on mars is really beautiful. The facilities appear to be in canyons or mountainous areas so it's not boring flat deserts.

Opinion
I love this game so far & with the addition of SnapMap there is some good amount of content. For any DOOM fan the $60 price tag I think is fair but if you are apprehensive I'd wait for a Steam sale or go to one of those sites that sells game keys but wouldn't pay more than $40. On a side note ID Software did a great job giving Doomguy some personality, jump into a lava pit & see what happens.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen i played the beta, I looked at the reviews and they were pretty bad. Honestly this is the game I personally have been waiting a while for and am very happy with the game. You feel like a TOTAL BADASS when you run around makin a fool of these monsters and the glory kills are pretty sick. They are satisfying and in a pinch can save your life.

All together the game is great. the only gripe I really have is the sound positioning is kinda weird. Without a general idea of where the enemies are, sometimes I'll be running around trying to find a monster for like, 5 or so minutes because you cant really use your headphones to position the monsters. I understand if there is a lot of them, but this happens when there is like, one or two left.

The graphics are very pretty and its really cool when you find your first secret room, you get to see the change in gaming eras. Personally remembering what it was like to play DOOM as a kid from the 90s, it really helps you appreciate how far gaming has gone.
