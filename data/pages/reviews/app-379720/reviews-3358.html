\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is without a doubt the best FPS campaign of the last decade. It already feels like one of the best of all time. The mechanics you loved about the first two are back, with best in class visuals and some modern tweaks. The gameplay is lighting fast and so frenetic. Its so much fun to play. I have not beaten it yet, but I am enjoying the road there!

As for multiplayer, I had read that a lot of players were complaining about it, but I have seen nothing to complain about. Like the campaign it plays very fast, so if you're slow on the draw you will have a bad time. The demon rune adds a fun mechanic and kinda feels like what Evolve should've been.

For both multiplayer and campaign the glory kills do not take you out of the action. They are quick and intense and you keep racing around immediately after. I really like this game and it was well worth the $80 cdn.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot a bad game but has many frustrations that means it wont be on high on my list to replay. Starting with the not so good, manily because there is more of it:
1.Way too many crashes. This is not some indie game funded online that cost only $20. This is apparently a AAA industry leader release. Crashing to desktop every hour or so is just unacceptable.
2. So many jumping puzzles. Mordecai was right to have a go at Tina for such noobish level design. Maybe the devs of Doom should have listened as well.
3. Character movement feels like an old style no clipping cheat. Its pretty crappy really.
4. Super shotgun is barely mild mannered.
5. Having glory kills as required mechanic to achieve some goals makes it feel like the game design was handled by a couple of 12 year olds. I know game devs are usually man childs but surely there was an adult somewhere supervising this.
6. The sound is just plain annoying. The atmosphere in hell is just white noise rather than terror. The sound editor needs to go back and play Ravenholm in HL2 for a lesson in creating a scary ambience.
The good:
1. Graphics are great
2. Monsters look awesome
3. Gameplay has moments of fun
4. Ummm thats it.
I ticked not recommended because at USD 60 to 80 there is not a lot value. On special for USD40 is about what its worth.\t\t\t\t\t

It's the thing right next to the number 1 and underneath the Esc keys. If you so much as nudge the thing, you go into "developer mode" which immediately disables multiplayer, achievements, SnapMap, and any Single Player campaign unlocks. Yes, it's that serious. Rip the thing out. The only fix is to delete your saves and profile, and start over. That' s the official response from Bethesda. Yeah, I know. I've started over twice now.
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

DOOM is pure and unadulterated adrenaline. It is both a refresh of the classic formula, and the carnage you already know and love.

The story is extremely scant, which may or may not matter. The player wakes up shackled inside what appears to be a sarcophagas, and finds his iconic armor shortly after. A brief explanation from a UAC bigwhig informs you that Hellwaves, yatta yatta, energy source, blah blah. Whatever. Kill stuff.

And kill things you will - it's not optional. Several areas in the game lock you in until the area is clear of enemies. "Demonic invasion at unsafe levels" is a real automated announcement in the game. That's about the level of seriousness we're dealing with here. Fortunately, the frantic pace at which the action moves means that these arena-type battles are seldom stale. What does get stale however are the finishers called "Glory Kills". A button tap when monsters are weakened will perform one of a limited number of kill animations on the creature depending on whether you attack them from the front, the side, behind, or above. The primary benefit of performing a glory kill is that they will you net you important health and ammo drops, so repetitive or not, you'll be doing it a lot.

There are a TON of weapons in the campaign. As you might expect, they are purpose-built for different battle scenarios. Of note, the chainsaw is a limited use weapon, which is a huge divergence from past games. The good news is that it's an instant kill. The bigger the monster, the more gas the chainsaw uses. After killing a monster with the chainsaw, they always drop a huge load of ammo for your other weapons. Speaking of which, your weapons don't have a lot of ammo capacity starting out. As the game progresses, you find ways to raise your maximum ammo capacity, but until you do it can be a bit constraining at times. Each weapon has two modifications that allow for additional strategic uses. The shotgun for example can lob explosive shells or fire a salvo of three rapid and focuses bursts. These modifications can be upgraded by earning tokens from killing monsters, completing challenges, or finding secrets.

The player is further enhanced by Runes. These are optional trials that when successful, grants a little passive boost to certain stuff like how far away you can perform a Glory Kill, or how far away you can absorb item drops. To start, only one can be equipped at a time, but after acquiring several of them, you can equip more.

The monsters in this game are interesting, even if I'm not personally crazy about their designs. The AI is without a doubt the best its ever been for this series. Imps will try to distance themselves from you and lob fireballs as they run, Soldiers use charged energy shots that burst to get you out of cover, and Hellknights relentlessly pursue you in hopes of pummeling you into the ground. Your success will depend on how well you navigate the environment and make full use of your equipment.

On a tangential note, I think the sound design on monster noises is lackluster. Is it functional? Yes, for the most part. I can tell what kind of monster is around by its roars, etc, but they aren't even as distinct and inspired as the original Doom.

The visuals are top-notch, as is expected of an id Software game. That of course means you need some seriously beefy hardware to run it. You have full control over the performance with several graphics options, but the problem is that you may have to dial a lot of it way down if you have gear that is older than two or three years. To run it full spec at 1080p, you'll need a GPU that has at least 5GB of memory - which is insane. The advice coming down the pipe says to lower your Shadows settings to get easy fps gains. Something is going on there that both nVidia and AMD are both trying to address at current.

Unlocks from the campaign come in two flavors: collecting Doomguy bobbleheads that unlock character and object models for you to gawk at, and secret levels by finding hidden switches in each stage. These secret levels are classic maps from earlier Doom games. They look precisely as they did in their day, and the secrets are (mostly) in-tact. Monsters are replaced by their current Doom counterparts where applicable, which actually means things are a bit tougher. For example, even though Pinkie demons have been around since the original, in the new game they are a monster to be extremely careful of. Thier inclusion in classic maps means that you'll have to fight a bit harder to survive.

Every map has tallies for secrets, collectibles, rune trials, and kill counts. If you missed anything, you can always revisit the map later and pick up what you left behind. You can then carry all that stuff with you back to where you left off. Very handy, if a bit OCD for completionists.

The oft-touted SnapMap feature gives you a bunch of pre-fab rooms and chambers that you can tinker with by adding monsters, objects, and exit points. There is a fair bit of depth, but the one thing it does lack is the ability to start from scratch and add tiles one-by-one as you might have in map editors of old. Sure, it's a quick and easy way to build environments, but it also lacks the creative freedom that map makers will be looking for.

All in all, if you can avoid any of the technical issues and actually run the thing, DOOM is a trigger-happy, blood-stained paradise. Is it my favorite game in the series? No, actually, but it is a helluva good time. If you're on the fence, drop the $60 asking price and laugh maniacally as you rip an imp's face apart with your bare hands.\t\t\t\t\t
