\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBethesda & id have done it. They've managed to breathe new life into the Doom franchise by keeping true to the series' roots, while injecting it with a healthy dose of modern design sensibilities. The end result: one of the best shooters on the market, and one of the best shooters of the last 10 years.

Doom is fast-paced, frenetic, and surprisingly challenging. Many gamers will be surprised to learn that the game doesn't really hold their hand through the journey, but that's the beauty of it. With its sprawling maps and challenging battles, Doom will test veteran gamers on the higher difficulty levels--and that's a good thing. In an age where game design is all about accomodating the masses, it's nice to see a publisher take a risk.

And the risk was worth it.

Pros
-----------------
Beautiful graphics
Superb controls
Highly configurable system settings
Lengthy single-player campaign (12-14 hours, and higher as you increase the difficulty)
Incredible replayability (weapon and suit upgrades and rune challenges)
An outstanding musical score
Old school multi-player that will remind you of Quake and Quake 3
Superb weapons
Incredible violence
No weapon reloads
Snapmap

Cons
-----------------
Some people just aren't going to dig the multi-player
Campaign is pretty light on story (I think this is perfectly fine, but others will not appreciate it)
Price of season pass borders on outrageous\t\t\t\t\t
