\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this game is all about diplomacy.

You wake up still chained to your desk due to your recent study in multicultural agreements until one of your soon to be clients comes to you in order to ask for your help. After a few seconds of clever negotiation your client is convinced and hands you your first own book about "remotely controled argumentation". Which will soon be replaced by a much bigger one, that you feel immediately fond of, so you pet it once or twice before putting it to use.

Just "thrown" into the world of diplomatic missions and maybe one or two topics of foreign politics one would say, that you are massacering your way to the top. The top being some princes, highly educated in the art of negotiation and compromise. But at this point you found some allies yourself, so you are able to counter every point of argument with only the most mild and necessary amount of diplomatic force you can think of.

On your way you even get to travel to different countries, which feel like another world, sadly always with a large amount of unnecessary heat and therefore bodysweat. Luckily this seem to only further improve your role as an envoy of the human race and your skills accross the many well designed negotiaion rooms, which are as usual well packed with secrets and things to find to improve your books and your business suit even further.

Everything is really beautiful and peaceful in this game, well designed, well written with a story... maybe not so rich... but a story.

Finished in 8 hours on medium difficulty.\t\t\t\t\t
