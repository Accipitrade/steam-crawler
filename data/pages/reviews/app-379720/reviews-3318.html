\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you heard that DOOM was being remade, and something to the effect of "Hey, they're bringing DOOM back," positively crossed your mind, then you need to play this game. Or rather, you need to play the single player mode.

It delivers exactly what you would expect as an answer to the question "What would DOOM be like if it were released in 2016?"

STORY: If you just said "Who cares?" then you're definitely in the right place. The story is there, and it's actually pretty deep if you think and read and listen while you're playing. But who wants to do that? Your character seems to feel the same way, so you won't feel alone. He'll throw a monitor out of the way while someone's giving him mission objectives through it. He'll punch through a speaker in an elevator while someone plot-dumps through it. As an NPC explains how dangerous an upgrade resource it is, he simply crushes its container in his hand without letting the NPC finish.

But, if you're interested, it would seem the original DOOM actually did take place before this DOOM. You, as Doomguy, are found in a sarcophagus in Hell, along with your suit. Humanity brings you back to Mars from Hell and keeps you in storage, intending for you to be a failsafe in the event that their Hell experiments go awry. Their Hell experiments involve harvesting Hell energy, an unlimited energy supply that can reverse natural resource depletion on Earth. Save the Earth by exploiting Hell's resources. Yeah, it really is that awesome.

MUSIC: This is the only aspect of the game that could be improved, meaningfully. The score is fitting--it's djent-y electro-industrial metal. What it lacks is groove--catchy rhythms to amp your adrenaline like those produced by Lamb of God, Meshuggah and the like. In fact, I have a three-step plan that could upgrade the music from "fantastic" to "perfect."

1. Fights with demon hordes are set to something like Meshuggah's "Combustion"
2. Boss fights are set to something like Meshuggah's "Bleed"
3. The final boss is set to a modern groove-metal master of the original DOOM's theme (search "Doom theme metal" on YouTube and just pick one)

MECHANICS: Gets a "♥♥♥♥ yeah" out of 10. The game involves you fighting a few demons in narrow confines while you make your way to the big, complicated open area. At that point, you fight infinity demons while running like Speedy Gonzalez, double-jumping from platform to platform, rapidly switching among your weapon set and Glory Killing demons when you can to get that precious, precious health back. Guns feel incredibly powerful when you shoot them, demon animations look really freaking cool (imps will run from you, only to jump, spin around and side-arm-fling a fireball back at you before running away again). Grab the "berserk" powerup, and every punch either explodes a demon, rips something off of them, or rips something out of them.

There are also four ways to upgrade your character. Badass Hell energy lets you upgrade your resource quantities--health, armor, ammo. Dead guys who wish they were Doomguy upgrade your support functions--map, equipment, etc. Little bots that you punch in the face give your weapons new functions--shotgun fires grenades, chaingun becomes a mobile death sprinkler, plasma rifle becomes a stun gun, etc. And finally, completing challenges and killing demons upgrades your guns's functions. Shotgun shoots deadlier grenades, mobile death sprinkler never overheats, stun gun kills cause stunsplosions that stun the guts out of all demon scum in the room. Awesome

This game is a masterpiece of FPS revival. Play it. Do it.\t\t\t\t\t

There's plenty user created levels and content to explore after too
10/10

Unfortunately the multiplayer seems to be dead with no players so i wouldn't bank on it being playable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou tear a zombie's leg off and stab him in his face with it. You rip a demon's horn out of his skull and stab him in the wound with it.

This game is gory, brutal, and simplistic as ♥♥♥♥, makes no excuses for it, and is all the better for it. No stupid 5 minute cinematic to start you off, no tutorial to teach you how to crouch, no ♥♥♥♥♥♥ stealth portions. Instant, constant, and incredibly gratifying combat. The weapons are all so diverse and satisfying as hell to use, aside from that ♥♥♥♥♥♥ starting pistol, but you ditch that pretty quick. Glory Kills fit the flow of the game perfectly, very fast, very smooth, incredibly brutal.

If you want a deep character with realistic motivations, look elsewhere. Doomguy doesn't give a ♥♥♥♥ about anything but killing demons. Not to say that there isn't a very intriguing story to the game, but it's told around Doomguy, he does not interact with most of it.

The layout of the levels in the campaign are incredibly well done, with a large amount of verticality and many crossovers, with little niches and secrets everywhere. Very fun to explore and find all the little easter eggs, powerups, upgrades, lore tidbits, and even callbacks to the original Dooms!

Campaign is a 10/10, absolute classic.

Multiplayer is pretty fun, I'm not that big into it. It doesn't seem to bring too much new to the table featurewise, but the different flow of combat and types of weapons is refreshing.

8/10 Multiplayer, but its Doom, you get it for the campaign and community maps.\t\t\t\t\t
