\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo amazingly different, except not! Id Software goes back to their roots with this game and it’s nothing short of amazing. They throw all the silly mechanics of modern FPS games in the trash. If you’re tired of the following things in your FPS games then DOOM is for you:

Rechargeable health? HELL NO! You have to rip demons apart with your bare hands to get more health!

Reloading weapons? Nope...you don't need that it takes time away from demon slaughtering. If you think this is unrealistic that is FALSE! The bullets are so afraid of the Doom Slayer they load themselves, DUH!

Want to take cover and aim-down-sights to pick away at the armies of hell? NO! You’ll die…quickly; imps don’t give a crap where you hide they will flank you constantly, hurl themselves at you with razor sharp claws or stand back and volley your location with fireballs. If that isn’t enough to get you moving the hell knights will rush and jump at you like roided out linebackers. They don’t care where you hide, just like real demons wouldn’t. To stay alive you have to run, jump, and climb your way across the environments scavenging for health, armor, and ammo all while blowing demons into a fine bloody mist or ripping and tearing apart the ones that escape that fate. Move, constantly, and show those hordes of hell why they should fear YOU, not the other way around.

Other awesome things about this game:

Epic metal soundtrack? Check.

Secret areas, collectibles, and old-school doom level Easter eggs? Check!

Really cool/interesting weapons? Check!

Fast movement? Check.

Original DOOM enemies remastered? Check.

Fun Powerups like quad damage and berserk? Check!

Really the only downside to this game is it can get a bit repetitive, especially once you start upgrading all the weapons, suit, and various other abilities that make you even more unstoppable. Up the difficulty and you’ll get more of the same just more unforgiving when you do make a mistake. Also, if you want to get into multiplayer save your time and just go play Overwatch to get that FPS multiplayer itch scratched. The multiplayer rollout was kind of botched and by the time they fixed things everyone had moved on. You NEED to play this game for the single player campaign though; it’s just so badass you have to experience it. That goes double if you played the original DOOM back when you were a kid like me on your family's first computer. This game is such a perfect homage to the original in all the best ways.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am half-way in, so my comments are based on my progress so far.

First, the game was absolutely worth the purchase. There are a lot of secrets to find, like in previous Doom games, and it is very easy to miss them. But I do like how they integrated everything into the map seamlessly. And the maps are very well laid out, even though they can seem confusing at times, with a bit of exploring you'll find that the maps are much smaller than they appear at first. There is only one level so far that was highly difficult to figure out where you are supposed to go next.

The suit upgrades are a bit meh, to be honest it feels like they had an "idea" for the suit upgrades but just ended up putting very little thought into the actual implementation. The weapon upgrades are a bit better, but most of them are just there to eat the weapon tokens (currency for upgrading your weapon mods).

The gameplay is much better, in my opinion, than the last Doom release. There is simply no need for a flashlight this time around, and anyone that played Doom 3 knows how much they used dark areas and "scare tactics" in the dark to give that game some life. Hah. It didn't work then, so they definitely avoided that mistake this time around.

The UI is very well laid out, and easy to use. The information (as you progress) is a very nice touch, even if you start out at the very beginning with not even a clue as to who you are, or why you are where you are. And there is NO explanation in the beginning as to why YOU are in the situation you are in. This is most likely done for a reason that will be explained to you later on, at least it seems that way.

Overall, this has been a very enjoyable game. I have taken my time on every level exploring and finding all the secrets and data, and am not rushing through. You have no choice in some areas, but in most cases you can backtrack over the entire map, and proceed only when you are ready.

I'd give it a 8 out of 10, and that is just my opinion.

From, A loyal iD and Doom fan.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started playing PC games with DOOM in 1994.

This reboot of the classic somehow manages to capture the essence of the original singleplayer campaign; it's visceral, white-knuckle combat that has just enough story to drive the action forward and give it purpose.

It cannot have been easy for the developers to stay true to that original recipe, while also incorporating modern FPS mechanics. ( Weapon / Armour upgrades, Collectables, etc.) but Id and Bethesda have done it, and it still feels damn cool.

Another defining example of the FPS genre. Well worth your cash.\t\t\t\t\t
