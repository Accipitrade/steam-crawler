\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 4 is a great addition to the series, for a modern rendition of DOOM it did a fantastic job.

TL;DR: DOOM 4 singleplayer is fun as ♥♥♥♥ and it's good, multiplayer is mediocre, snapmap is p gud.

Honestly when they announced and showed off DOOM 4 I wasn't impressed, what's this ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ glory kills? why is it slow? I play ALOT of doom 1-2 and still do to this day using Zandronum. But after playing through the single player and 100%ing it, I can say that it's a blast. It's a lot faster than it seemed when they showed it, the action is fast paced and brutal.

Yes it is a modernized DOOM, but that's not really a bad thing when it's done right, if you want to play classic doom, just go play classic doom man. Glory kills are almost completely optional (bosses require it to FINISH them off) but I never found myself really getting tired of all the different ways Doom guy can murder a demon, sometimes it feels good to get up close and personal!

The maps are mostly nonlinear in exploration and the secrets are all pretty damn good, I had to go to some maps several times to find them on my own, the game kept getting more interesting at it went on, none of the maps felt too similar to each other.

We see a lot of returning weapons with a modern flash, with some weapon mods to spice things up. The guns felt great to use for the most part and most of them have 2 different mods to choose from, allowing for a secondary function. Being able to upgrade your armor and weapons is a welcome change in my opinion, fully upgraded you feel stronger, but not cheaply invincible, especially on higher difficulties.

The singleplayer is a love letter to first person shooters in this modern age, maybe we'll see more of it. Multiplayer on the other hand is pretty forgettable, the net code or something feels off and the modern "load out" function is "eh" I don't really have much to say about it, it's fun in short bursts but it's not really something many people will find themselves playing all night.

Snap map is pretty interesting and has a lot of options on creating unique levels, I spent a couple hours in it and made a map and found it to be kinda fun, a bit clunky to use but legit none the less. I never really mastered it or played too many maps but I really liked it, don't have much else to say.

Also the added lore is pretty decent, doom boy is a fantastic protag, A+\t\t\t\t\t

This game was incredibly refreshing to play. I am not by any means a die hard DOOM fan, lets get that out there. I have played all other DOOM's to their maximum capbability and then some thanks to the modders of the DOOM universe. The first thing one must realize is that......this is DOOM. The name literally explains the game. Marine sees demons, marine picks weapon of choice, demons die. DOOM 3 in my own opinion was the black sheep of the series, this was a timewarp back to the old days.

Level design--->Honestly each level had its own unique design. It was not at all linear or predicatble. There are usually many ways to achieve that ONE path which leads you to the next area. Obstacles and jumping, climbing, and various thinking designs are used to get from place to place.

Environment---> It is quite.....DOOMY. LOL. Seriously though the environment is perfect. The areas get more and more dreadful as the game progresses. Amazing detail, textures, particles and very very few if any collision bugs ( none for me, there have been mixed opinions)

Gore---> Its DOOM......enough said

Enemy AI---> WAS FREAKIN AMAZING!!!!! I am sad to say that the Imps kicked my tail during the first level! They have multiple combat tactics from the Imp all the way to the Cyber Demon. Beginners to DOOM will be on their toes alot, where I and others who are used to DOOM and high super fast paced killing will usually be able to detect their next move

Story/Collectibles---> The collectibles are pretty hard to find. Especially the dolls, the proto chips for the suits are difficult as well, allowing for a rather impressive exploration concept. The story IS NOT given to you. The collection of Data pads, runes and more are needed should you wish to uncover the full story here.

Overall? ---> Will play on Nightmare and die alot.

Since the above are pros, lets get to the cons i experienced.....
1. DOOM does not like recording software. I didnt get it to work
2. Had 3 random crashes ( I did have recording software online......may be linked...)
3. MASSIVE....MASSIVE download time.

I recommend this game with all of my non existent heart\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM - the way a reboot/sequel was meant to be. I played through the campaign on ultra-violence difficulty and enjoyed every second of it, thoroughly. Doom 3 is forgettable as far as I'm concerned, especially compared to this one - it tried being a slow-paced, survival horror game. DOOM pays homage to the original Doom games much more with the fast-paced action, quick weapon switching, no reloading, hordes of demons, badass power-ups, good music by Mick Gordon (some of which plays on queue for executions/power-ups/kills), and nearly all the original enemies implemented for the nostalgia factor.

Multiplayer is passable but was developed by different people apparently - I bought it for single player anyway. SnapMap is pretty cool, especially since it has some logic/programming to it; I just wish the map editor portion of it was more complex/customizable.

If you enjoyed the original Doom games and are comfortable with a modernized version of them (not including Brutal Doom and Project Brutality), buy the new one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gameplay present here, while quite enjoyable, is actually far more reminscent of the original Quake, with it's more limited on-screen mobs and level verticality.

Other than the way it looks, It doesn't really provide the "Doom" experience: taking on a room full of monsters and prioritizing dangerous ranged attackers like revenants, chaingunners, arch viles etc., while frantically keeping out of the way of all the fireballs and melee attackers. The game that came closest to that playstyle was Serious Sam, which was only let down by its average level design.

Doom multiplayer is forgettable and stale feeling, as arena shooters have largely done their time. 8/10 for the single player with a negative factor being the lack of campaign co-op and a minor issue where there seems to be no pain sounds from the player when hit or killed (in space nobody can hear you scream?).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didnt think you could make a faithful Doom(93) reboot in todays gaming culture (Doom 3), but damn they did it. And it WORKS. Let me count the ways:

1. Story - The story adds interesting background and guidance but dosnt get in the way - it leaves you alone to explore. The marine dosnt seem to care about the 'story' that is happening around him, its interesting to watch him behave unlike a tradiational PC. This a genius way to introduce story to this type of game. There is a story here for the player, but the protagonist dosnt seem to care about it.

2. Exploration - the level designs are engaging and not cheap (such as having invisible walls everywhere) and encourage you to explore and find secrets. Thankfully, the map makes it easy to get around and explore and the secrets are everywhere! The environments are pretty to look at but not grimey like so many realistic shooters. Its a nice place to spend some time killing demons. But are there really bad places to kill demons?

3. Mechanics - exactly what you expected. Double-jumping around with a rocket launcher. Very Quake-ish in feel. They added a 'take-down' move but this tactic drops loot and works pretty seamlessly into combat. I think it works. Running around shooting and pulling yourself up ledges has a good flow to it, similar, to some extent, to Mirrors Edge.

4. Love - I feel like the developers really wanted to bring the spirit of Doom(93) to Doom(16) and they did so with love. I think it shows.

Cons (these are not deal-breakers):
1. I get a lot of Crashes to Desktop and lock-ups. I mean, once or twice a session. I dont think this is acceptable but the gaming community allows it so I just have to suck it up. *UPDATE - 3-4 CtD in a session, this is ridiculous.

2. Lack of options - there just seems to be a serious lack of options in what was a PC original. One complaint people have is the mouse smoothing not being easy to turn off (you can hack the start-up file to remove it).

3. It seems to be dumbed down for consoles (see lack of options up above). In a reboot of what the PC master race would call a Magnum Opus, we dont see a ton of enemies on the screen at once and the ones that do come in, port-in slowly. It just reeks of the developers worrying about someone playing with 2 analog sticks on a wireless bluetooth connection. Or I could just be wrong on this point. *UPDATE - and I didnt play the other difficulties yet




Anyway, Im having a blast playing Doom(16). Have fun.

*UPDATE - and I didnt play the other difficulties yet
