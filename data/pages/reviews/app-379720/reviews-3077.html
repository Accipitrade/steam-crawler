\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYES!! I grew up with Doom and Doom 2 and before Doom (4) came out, I decided to replay them and what a great decision that was. Doom 1, coming after Wolfenstein 3D and Blake Stone, introduced the z-axis to the FPS genre, and they made well use of it. Level design is where Doom shined, combining complicated sprawling labrynths with demon hordes in a convincely dark atmosphere. Doom 2 updated the engine to allow even more demons on screen and even more complex awesome levels. Nearly a decade later we were given Doom 3, which was also awesome, but highly relied on jump scares and darkness, while level design was relatively linear. It was a good game, but different from original Doom. Now again about a decade later we have been blessed with Doom (4). I was nervous. Was it going to recreate the feeling of the old Doom? Maze-like levels that test your navigation skills while throwing demons at your face with fire in the depths of hell? I became more nervous when I heard about skill trees and upgrading. I became terrified when I tried the multiplayer open beta. It was awful, slow, boring, and the brightly colored player/weapon skins were just slap in the face. People were like "yeah I was old school Doom multiplayer like CTF and DM!" Umm, you mean Unreal Tournament and Quake III? But the MP part was not made by id, it was by the CoD people. Ugh why Bethesda why?? But whatever, Doom is about singe player. So got the game and WOW. This. Is. PERFECTION!! The level design is complex and interesting and great use of z-axis like what Doom 1 was all about. The demons, the weapons, the fast paced enemies in your face explosion is all so great. The brutal gore, the f*cking heavy metal music, it's all perfect and gave me the same feeling as playing the old Dooms, well more specifically Doom 2. And if you thought the glory kill thing was brutal, jesus the berserker powerup will make your grandparents vomit and weep for the future of society, just like they did back with old Doom! The weapon/armor upgrades actually add a bit of variety and I loved searching for all the secrets. Took me about 30 hours to complete the whole game having found all items and secrets, and that's longer than Doom 1, 2, and 3 combined. My only qualm would be that the health and ammo from glory kills makes the game a bit easy, but I'll soon replay the whole game on nightmare. And if you thought the SP game was all, SnapMap is actually pretty awesome. The tutorial is fun and well made, and you can play with that, but there are plenty of crazy obsessed people out there making some impressive and pretty awesome maps. It can be difficult to find a good map since there's no real search function or way to preview a map but the rating system is a start. In the end, Doom (4) exceeded my already extremely high expectations!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a lifelong fan of doom, i was a little hesitant to buy into this iteration of the game. But after it released and had read a few reviews calling this "the best singleplayer of the year", I decided to get it and see if it lives up to the name. The singleplayer is pretty fun, but not because of the story, or the repeptitive combat, but for the tons of hidden secrets.

The multiplayer has already been critisized, but admittedly, I was sucked into it for a week or two, then started to realize that nothing would improve, and gave up hope on this doom's multiplayer.

My biggest complaint with Doom is the worst. First, I'd like to say that Doom was amazing in the 90s. One of the things that made it so amazing and is why it is still played even to this day, is the moddability. There are mods still being released for doom ~23 years later. Moddability is a key factor to a game's lifespan. If good quality mods continue to be made for a game, people will still play it. But this doom (2016) does not have that, and now i'm almost certain that this game will suffocate on it's own repetitiveness which could have been avoided by merely adding mod support. There are modders like me, who are capable of creating high quality content. So why not give us the tools used to create the game levels? Instead, we were given snap map, the worst level creation tool in the history of video games. Stare at your screen in boredom as you place pre-assembled room after pre-assembled room. Exhillarating. I call it 'nap-map'. And because of the tool being used, the maps are just as unexciting to play as they are to make.

So buy it when it's cheap, play it for a few hours, and move on.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou will love the new Doom if you played the original from the 90's. The game has the same unusually high player movement speed like the original. The weapons feel fun effective and accurate. The combat encourages strafing to avoid getting hit and going back in the level to search for power ups or ammo. All these simple things became uncommon in modern tunnel like first person shooters with self healing players. If you never played the early id Software shooters like Wolfenstein or Doom you will probably find this concept refreshing. Technically the game reaches an extreme level of perfection in graphics and sound. The id Tech engine had really a lot of trouble with graphic card drivers in Rage and the new Wolfenstein. Now in the new Doom it finally feels rock solid even on AMD hardware with the Vulkan API. Not much texture popping, nice anti alias etc. New game elements? There are enough of them to keep you motivated. There are weapon upgrades, challenges and skill upgrades. The levels almost always use the height as a dimension. They are fun to explore and navigate in. 10/10 for me. Get this game if you haven't done yet.\t\t\t\t\t
