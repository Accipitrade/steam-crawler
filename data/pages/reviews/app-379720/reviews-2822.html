\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot this on sale for half price. This is just my first impression, but it seems like it was totally worth it. The sound, graphics, and level design are amazing. I like that it has both shooting and exploration. The "interactive" music reminds me of the first time I played Unreal. I have a steelseries gaming keyboard and most of the keys mapped to good spots on the gaming keypad by default, which made it easier to get into the game.

I did not try multiplayer since none of my friends play it and I didn't want to play online with some random woodenhead who would pwn me in three seconds and then be a woodenhead about it. I did miss the simple drop into a deathmatch with bots like in Unreal Tournament series. Too bad I bet such maps for this game would be awesome.

I did try the snapmap tutorial, which shows you how to assemble your own levels, which looks interesting but I'm still in awe over the campaign.

I'm amazed at how well this runs on my system, I have a GTX 1050 but the rest of the machine is a few years old, and I run at the monitor's resolution on high settings. I was expecting to relive the 1993 DOOM experience trying everything to get it to run well on my machine (anyone remember custom DOS boot menus?) and was pleasantly surprised. The only issue I had was that I had to enable to Win10 swap file on D: as that is where my games are. Take note if you have games installing on anything other than C: enable the swap file on that drive or you'll hang up on exit.

Overall I'd recommend this game for fun demon-blasting and exploration fun, especially on sale, and I might even go ahead and try DOOM 3.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti've been turning to religion a lot more lately cause the brief glimpse of hell this game offers is quite the feeling thats expected.

so lets start off with the basics. the system requirements are not to be trifled with. if your pc is below the minimum, good luck running this pretty. i have a pretty nice pc, and this was the first game i ever had to move the graphics to medium on. i still shudder at the thought. thing is, even on medium graphics, the game looked great, so there's that.

when i started looking at the difficulties, i was wondering why name them these names. easy is "i'm too young to die" and the next is "hurt me plenty", followed by "ultra violence" and some others i cant remember. i was curious, so i figured middle is good, and went with ultra violence cause i thought "that sounds like medium". this is my favorite part of the difficulty titles. what it sounds like is exactly what it is. easy's too young to die title is like a cake walk. medium's "hurt me plenty" doesn't put any added toughness. it's a good experience. naivity was me playing ultra violence on my first run. it was ultra violent. I L O V E D I T

What the game felt like was pleasure. it starts off as you'd expect, flung head first into action, being able to learn as you go from your own experience, not spoon fed a tutorial. you actually feel like you just woke up wanting to kill things. it has objectives and a storyline which is very fulfilling but the bloodshed is just more fulfilling. great weapons, great environment, great enemy design, great feel to maneuver and fight with. it felt like the good ol' doom series got hd graphics, a bloody melee system, and the guns just kept getting more brutal. i wish the weapons were easier to switch in and out of, but after about a half hour with more than two guns, you kinda get used to it.

for me, every aspect of this game's single player campaign was well worth it, but i haven't tried the multiplayer too much yet. I enjoyed the original doom style of focus on the campaign, dont make it unbearable or boring at parts. just keep it guns blazing, and enemies slaying.
