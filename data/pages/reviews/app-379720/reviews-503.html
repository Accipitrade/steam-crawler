\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM kicks ass. It's excessively violent, bada ss, and metal as . There's been a resurgence of "retro" shooters recently, and I don't think it's unreasonable to say that this game played a huge part in revitalizing the entire FPS genre.

I've played parts of it elsewhere since its release, but finally picked up my own copy to play it in full, and I don't regret it one bit. The gameplay is tight af, and actively rewards aggressive play. Low health? Turn a demon's face into mulch with your bare hands to get back in the fight. Low ammo? Rip them in half with a chainsaw. All other problems? Bullets are the solution. On Ultra Violence (the difficulty I recommend for a first playthrough), you go down in just a few hits, but so does everything else; enemies are never passive, matching your aggression with their own relentless movement and attacks. As a result, the fights are quick, the weapons are impactful, and you can feel like a bada ss mowing down demons while still feeling accomplished for surviving the encounter. The environments and enemy designs are fantastic and detailed, and when combined with the soundtrack the game is famous for, they absolutely nail the atmosphere and tone that DOOM deserves.

Some details/minor nitpicks: About 20h of gameplay for me to beat the campaign with 100% secrets, and it ran extremely well in Proton, though it did CTD, rarely (once every few hours or so, usually when loading after I died). Secret hunting basically a requirement, as it rewards you with all kinds of goodies you need, which I enjoyed anyway except for 2 very specific ones: 1, a secret path not indicated in any way on the map, unlike every other secret in the game except for 2, a secret you have to shoot to trigger, a mechanic not used anywhere else in the game, before or after. Multiplayer exists, I guess. Base movement speed was just a little slow for my liking, compared to similar games like Dusk or Ultrakill. The pistol is the only weapon that I didn't really enjoy in some fashion (not counting the grenades, which might as well not be there) - only falling back on it when I didn't want to waste shotgun ammo on fodder enemies, but even then a Glory Kill is usually the more efficient way to deal with them. Runes for passive buffs are a great idea but could've been implemented better - a majority of them deal with Glory Kills in some way, a couple are super underwhelming, and I think it's a wasted opportunity to not have them meaningfully alter different aspects of the gameplay, instead.\t\t\t\t\t
