\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;WR: DOOM is one of the best single player shooters you could play right now, so if you haven't yet, go play it. but for those who want a little more of an in-depth analysis than that:

Pros:
DOOM's main strength is undoubtedly how aggressive the gameplay is. Everything in the game compliments this aggresive playstyle; Between the famous metal soundtrack (which is famous for a reason), the incredibly well animated and brutal glory kills, and the health drops that enemies give for said glory kills, there's plenty of reason to get up in the demons' faces.

The gunplay is also fantastic. The weapons feel distinct and are all useful in the right situation. The game also offers weapon modifications and upgrades to each weapon for further customization.

There is also signifigant variety in the enemy types. Which means you have to continuously both observe their behavior and create strategies for dealing with them. This is one great way that DOOM fights off the potential of getting stale.

There are also some elements of platforming, exploration, as well as a signifigant amount of collectables, giving some time to break up the action a little. These collectables also earn you points that you can use to upgrade your weapons, which gives some extra incentive to seek them out.

There are also some extra difficulty levels to add replayability (be warned though, when the difficulty is on nightmare it gets insanely hard). Also, a solid, Quake-style multiplayer mode as well as a level editor called snapmap. Snapmap is sort of a mini Little Big Planet, allowing you to make your own maps for the community, or explore levels that others made.

Cons:
There are a few bugs in the AI. Every once and a while they can be found in their natural habitat, shooting at a wall while you're right beside them. However, instances like this are rare and typically fix themselves once you shoot at the enemy.
Nothing else that isn't extremely nitpicky.

In conclusion:
If you haven't decided yet, pick up this game. The campaign alone is worth the price tag, and with the multiplayer and custom map library, there's plenty of value here.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is easily a thumbs up for me. The game is a very fun fast paste shooter with unique enemy types. The thing that I love the most about this game is the Ultra Nightmare dufficulty, in campaign that's still a work in progress but in Arcade Mode I love it to death. With other shooters like 343's Halo games, new Call of Duties, Titamfall 2 and so on I hate their highest difficulty because it's basically turning their aim up to 90% accuracy and you drop in a second to weak enemies. In Doom every enemy has a specific sound and animations giving you time to react. If you're an overachiever or guy who wants his name high on the leaderboard I strongly recomment Arcade mode.

The Campaign is pretty decent, nice upgrades and weapons as you go along. The only problem I have is it's kind of repetative. You enter a room and fight the emenies, and the keycards and skulls in hell are pretty easy to find because they'er literally on the trail to your next destination. I wish they could of added something unique specific to campaign like a speedrun section, a race to some point of the map, a flying section, just something that's not just enter and shoot. The bossed were the only unique sections throughout but with only 3 bosses but that's not really enough.

The campaign also has some secret data that gives you insight into the lore for the geeky type, I certainly liked it. Multiplayer is pretty insane, I played 78 matches but if you're new and click on "beginers only" prepare to meet the scummiest smurfs ever. I only saw one hacker but that's to be expected when the devs don't moniter the multiplayer as much as competative games. The game with players or AI, I never felt so much satisfaction from ripping and tearing through enemies in extreme gorry detail. This game didn't have to be called Doom for me to love it but man is it a fun experience.\t\t\t\t\t
