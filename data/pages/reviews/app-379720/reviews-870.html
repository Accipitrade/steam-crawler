\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are a fan of FPS games then this games needs no introduction. Doom (1993) is considered one of the pioneering first-person shooter games. Doom (2016) in my opinion is faithful remake recapturing the hearts of us gamers the same way it did so many years ago. The game has one of the best fps action if not the best this generation.

The gunplay is hands down the star of the show here. The guns feels like they have real impact which make them extremely satisfying to use every time. But don't be mistaken into thinking that this is just another corridor shooter, Doom does many innovative things to set itself apart. The glory kill mechanic is one of the best features i have seen in FPS games. Whenever you bring any enemy to low health they start glowing indicating to the player that they can perform the said glory kill which drops the much needed health packs. This in turn not only incentivises the player to move around the arena but also keeps the player aware of his surroundings. This combined with auto reload ie. that there is no need to ever waste your time on reloading, just walk over the ammo and instantly it is ready to use thereby always maintaining the feverish pace of the action. Then the excellent level design which encourages the player to dash and jump from one corner of the arena to the other like a badass killing machine laying waste to everything in its path.

This game has one of the most incredible soundtracks in all of gaming. Mick Gordon who is the composer has done exceptional job creating this master piece of a soundtrack. The thumping soundtrack instantly makes the player feel the intensity making it the perfect accompaniment to the relentless action of the game.

Such games come once every generation and hence are a must play kind of experiences and i myself looking forward to playing Doom Eternal next.\t\t\t\t\t
