\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI genuinely feel like I was playing a different game than everyone else. DOOM (2016) is a terrible nightmare of developers not understanding what they made prior. Gameplay is repetitive. The campaign is filled with "shoot all the enemies until the door opens" sections. It's the complete opposite of what the original DOOM games were and is not even worthy as a successor to DOOM 3.

DOOM (2016) does nothing interesting to make it worth playing. It's short, redundant, and unimpressive. This game is far from any of the great design of the originals. DOOM 1 and 2 used short, delicately crafted mazes to give gameplay variety and replayability. They were fast, smart, and incredibly influential. DOOM (2016) copies modern tropes from newer games, which includes the praised instant kill melee. The great graphics and art direction are ruined by the unmemorable set pieces and incredibly linear level design which does the complete opposite to recapture the true feeling of a "return to retro" shooter as others claim.

[2020 EDIT] I have played DOOM Eternal as it was reviewed very well as 2016 was. I actually think that DOOM 2016 has a better gameplay loop than Eternal during the ‘arena’ sections as there is less downtime looking for ammo.\t\t\t\t\t

there is no way i would recommend you buy this game at the full price or even half price, there simply isn't enough of it to be worth what they charge.

a little sorry i bought it but i really did want to see what Vulkan was capable of and in that respect it was interesting. as for the gameplay, the action is pretty good, very much like Serious Sam, run through and beat 'em up or shoot 'em up pretty much the whole way. the bossfights were boring. the upgrade system was fairly painless as those things go but i could have totally done without it. and it would have certainly seemed much more like old-school-classic-DOOM without that extraneous rpg element.

i'm not even going to go into the multiplayer nor the snapmap thing becuase i believe neither adds anything to the replayability. snapmap is extremely limited and multiplayer is a clusterfu_ck, as with any pvp.

bottom line: way too little content for the money. if i was you i would wait to pick it up for ten bucks. unless you just want to show off your hardware, it is good for that purpose.

ID software's Doom, Doom II and Doom 3 are all great games and i revisit each of them each to this day.

this game, Doom 4 or Doom 2016 or whatever they want to call it, really doesn't have much more going for it than lush graphics and a single play-through of the campaign.

the multiplayer is just plain ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ and there won't be a soul playing it, this time next year, if there is anyone playing it, even now.

the map editor turned out to be overly simplistic and severely limited, not really useful for anything. which leaves the prospects of co-op replayability pretty bleak as well.

i finished the campaign and i see nothing to return to this game for, nor can i recommend it to my friends.

my advice is to wait until the price of this game slips under ten bucks and you may not feel so bad about the purchase later. i think i paid thirty or forty bucks for it and i almost fully regret the purchase although admittedly my main motive for buying it was to witness the Vulkan performance (which was really the one thing that did not dissapoint).

the campaign was not altogether bad but it was very short and very repetitious with every level being set up essentially the same way and looking so similar that it's actually easy to get lost on a lot of the maps, which i almost never do in games.

ID software is now nothing but a namesake and it clearly shows in this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM has the blood & guts of a classic Doom game with the exterior of a modern AAA title. There's no reloading guns, no sprint button, no aiming down the sights - it remains true to the original core elements of the series while being modernized & refined to near-perfection. The gameplay is very taut, frenzied, and rewarding, especially as you get better at it and play through the campaign on higher difficulty settings. While the game essentially consists of navigating your way from one objective to the next, engaging increasingly thick & powerful waves of foes in astoundingly frenetic gunfights, its linear structure never becomes tedious or stale because the level design is fantastic, the atmosphere is thick and well-realised, the enemies and weapons you use against them in combat are well-varied and dastardly fun, and the game is superbly paced. There's a strong sense that everything belongs and there isn't a single shred of filler here - suit & weapon customization adds a lot of variety, allowing you to tailor your experience as the campaign progresses, and there is enormous replay value due to the large, layered levels, plethora of secret areas & items, wide variety of fun challenges, bombastic arcade mode, and, if that wasn't enough, online multiplayer (which, despite being supplanted by DOOM Eternal, remains active four years after its release), which includes an in-engine map editor that allows you to create, play in, and share your own levels.

While DOOM's plot is streamlined and character development done without any dialogue from or direct information about the player character (who remains a nameless, faceless entity throughout the campaign), it is clever in its execution, as his behavior and reactions inform us about him. Plot is developed almost entirely through text and pre-recorded dialogue you find; cutscenes are brief and rendered entirely in-game. Doom was never primarily about story or character development, anyway; its focus is the pure, undistilled fun of sheer carnage, of being armed to the teeth with a mix of conventional & sci-fi weapons and taking on hoards of demons by shooting, exploding, and ripping them to shreds to a fantastic Industrial Metal soundtrack (Mick Gordon's score here stands out as some of the best videogame music ever recorded). DOOM pulls this off in a straightforward yet highly dynamic & tactile way, and it's all topped off with slick presentation, immaculate optimization (using Vulkan at 1440p on Nightmare settings, it lingers around 120FPS with a GTX 1070 and beyond 165FPS with an RTX 2080 Super, but this runs so well that a potato could squeeze 60FPS out of it), and visual & audio design quality that blows away most other AAA titles of 2016. This game excels at both the old and the new, bringing them together in one of the most well-crafted, unpretentious, and wickedly fun first-person shooters and finest reboots I've ever played. A near-perfect game and my 2016 GOTY.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's simple, yet highly entertaining. It felt like playing the old Doom with new and improved graphics and I think that's what they were looking for. There is no real 'scare' element here, because you are this badass monster slayer revived to kick demon ass and everything that moves needs to die.

The gameplay is awesome even though you just run and shoot everything in sight. The plot is nice, the character's attitude awesome and every weapon needs to be used! It's a simple FPS game.

After completing the game I thought that maybe I would like to actually have something to interact with in the game, maybe a littlebit of puzzle solving or something. There should be more, I think. Nonetheless, a great game!\t\t\t\t\t
