\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend this game if you can get it for 30 dollars or so. Its good, but not great.

Cons:

1. The enemy types are limited and about half way through the game you will have encountered them all.

2. The weapons are overpowered. On hard mode, the game feels somewhat balanced, but on lower difficulties its much too easy once youve acquired all the upgrades.

3. You can use any weapon and it will kill the enemy. I like games where you need a certain gun to do a certain job.

4. The arena style game play works ok with doom but not really. The original games had more tight corners and choke points and more enemies everywhere. Replacing that system with a spawning and arena system makes this a different type of game altogether. Doom was about getting lost and running around blindly trying to find the exit while being assaulted constantly.
In this game, you have stretches of time in which there are no enemies and you can replentish your ammo before the next phase of combat begins. In orginal doom, you had to run through hoards to get a gun and then you had to fight tooth and nail to get away. There is less of a survival or horror aspect to this game. I know that you coundnt have an orignal style doom with current gen graphics because only people with really expensive set ups would be able to handle the constant stream of enemies.

5. This game feels like a demo or the first half of a game. This game is much too short.

6. The game sort of just ends and is basically just a cliff hanger for DOot 2.



Pros:
1. The music is really fitting. I get that doom marine face from the original doom when i hear the music and I start killing demons.

2. The glory kill system is fun and is the main attraction. I always feel bad for the fat flying orbs of death cause they look pretty confused and sad when you gouge their eyes out and shove it down their throats. I want one as a pet.

3. No endless exposition.

4. Story is decent.

5. Well optimized and smooth.


Conclusion:
I give this game 7.5 out of 10. The glory kill system and music are what makes this game decent. Otherwise I feel like this would be a generic halo game. The levels are repetitive and theres too much backtracking. Its like they developed the combat system and then plunked levels around it.
Its somewhat akin to fallout 4 in so far as they improved the combat but messed up the story and level design. I hope the next one improves on the story elements and level design. I was always on the edge of being absorbed into the game, but never quite reaching that point. The main problem is that once youve finished fighting the enemy hoards, you are dropped back into a kind of boring scavenging or exploration mode until you get to the next arena. It feels like going for a run and stopping suddenly without walking it off. It a bit jarring.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:

Amazing Graphics, art and design
Extremely fun and violent gameplay
Brilliant upgrade / weapon level up system, adds to gameplay
Unnerving audio

Cons:

The story started decent, but dropped off in satisfaction towards the end. The art is "beautiful" (hellish) throughout the game, but the gameplay, level design, and story seemed a bit rushed in the later levels.

The level design gets old at the 3/4ths mark of the campaign. You enter a new room, you know what to expect. Kill demons, next room, kill demons, etc.

That being said, the challenges, secret areas, completionist options and higher difficulties definitely add to the replayability.

I recommend mainly because the gameplay and art/graphics are top notch and worth getting. I waited till it was on sale for $30 and I'm happy.
