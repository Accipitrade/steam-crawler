\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is actually pretty much as good as people make it out to be. It's fun, it's pretty, it's well put together (mostly), it runs smooth on pretty bad hardware, it's tightly designed and doesn't have any glaringly awful decisions like Doom 3. Even the Glory Kills, which seemed like a terrible idea, work out well enough to not be a massive momentum killer in a fast fight. The soundtrack is amazing, the creature design is a nice middle ground between modern sensibilities and tribute to the classic, the weapon variety is good, it's basically everything you could want from modern Doom and it doesn't outstay its welcome.

The only blemishes on the game are the occasional bouts of backtracking with nothing to do and the upgrade system which is just... There and kind of blah, particularly the combat challenges which can get just a hair short of obnoxious.

Rip and tear until it is done. And then maybe spend twenty minutes with the multiplayer which isn't particularly great and is frankly a little unnecessary.\t\t\t\t\t
