\r\n\t\t\t\t\t+ Fast, fluid movement
+ Mantling and double-jumps
+ Maps rich with verticality and twisting spaces, not a linear sequence of setpieces
+ Varied enemies that aren't all just different types of "dude with gun"
+ Punchy weapons, and switching amongst a full arsenal
+ Plot that mostly stays out of the way
+ Colors (real *isn't* brown!)

Big-budget FPS that actually remembered all the highs of the '90/early-00s ones? Hell yes.

Merges in some of the more recent things like melee finishers and unlocks and actually makes them work together as fun mechanics without hurting the flow. Serious Sam 3, pay attention. This is how you move a "retro" game forward, keeping the good of the old, and making the new work too.

(CAVEAT: ...except the co-op multiplayer is garbage. It is literally a separate game bolted onto this one. You can only co-op community snap-maps, and the quality there is terrible. I never bothered with DM after the demo put me right off, and frankly it's the least interesting game mode. But the singleplayer alone is easily strong enough to carry it.)\t\t\t\t\t
