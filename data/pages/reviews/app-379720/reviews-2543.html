\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan, i cant belive i payed £50 for this. Okay, It's not a bad game. But I have seen pretty much everything in it done better by a mod for the original doom, yes thats right an ancient ass mod for an ancient ass game beats this hands down. Lets see why.

1. The soundtrack- During moments of calm and exploration, the soundtrack is quite nice with it ambience. Now, most people say they like even love the soundtrack when it comes to the more action-y moments. However, when the action music picks up the only thing thats left bleeding is my ears. All of the music that plays during action is absolutely awful, whoever is responsible for this travesty probably made it by accident while drunk, stoned on meth, having an epiliectic seziure all while coping with an odd temporary parkingsons disease.

2. The story and enviroments- The enviroments are great, just look at the pictures. No area feels like a copy or repeat of previous areas giving every level in the game a uniqueness to it. 9/10 on this, nice one devs. The story is half hearted and the protagonist makes a point of skipping exposition and cutting to the chase. There are PDA files that offer some intresting facts and lore about stuff in the game so explinations and what-not are there for those who care. A good job in my opinion. This is the parts where they ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up the least.

3. Snapmap- So, take dooms community. A community pretty much built on modding of old ass doom games. Now think, what would be the most destructive, harmful and painful thing you could do to that community. If you thought 'remove all modding support in your new game and replace it with a completely garbage sub-par level maker for kids' then you were 100% correct and thats exactly what the devs did. So, i complained my tits off about the abismal soundtrack already but all it would take is a simple soundtrack replacment mod and i could listen to something much better instead like paydays ost or MGRR ost. But no, no mod support. You done ♥♥♥♥♥♥ it up.

4. The gameplay- doom is always meant to be first and foremost about the gameplay. The gameplay here however feels linear, repetetive and dull. Certainly where the biggest disappointment is to be found. Every enemy in the game is a complete bullet sponge, almost putting 'the divisions' thugs to shame in the sheer amount of lead they can swallow even on the easiest difficulty. Or maybe doomguy is just using a collection of cool looking BB guns? That might be the case seeing as there is not a single gun in the game that has a satisfying impact or any notable recoil, he also never reloads but thats normal in Doom 1. The game pretty much forces you to 'glory kill' ever single opponent because killing them with bullets after they are in their stunned state takes 2/3 of your entire completely full ammo reserve even when you've fully upgraded the thing. Enemies are laughable most of the time, lobbing uber slow moving progectiles or charging you so predictably and slowly that you need down syndrome to get hit. Admittedly this is only on one of the easier difficulties, but by the time i had completed the story i uninstalled the game because i had seen more than enough to know this game is garbage.

Lets contrast this with the brutal doom mod for doom 1 that i mentioned previously. Incredibly satisfying power fantasy gunplay, every weapon feels distinct and powerful. Nothing like cutting down a whole corridor with akimbo assualt rifles or sawing a soldier in half with a single blast of the shotgun. Gameplay has greater depth, reloading is a thing now as well as kicking, throwing grenades. Enemies are plentiful and are no push over, unless you know what you are doing you can be cut down with ease. Actually, stop reading this and type 'brutal doom' into youtube. See for yourself and then compare it to footage of this game being played, you will see just how much better the mod is over this overpriced ♥♥♥♥.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBack to fundamentals! Doom is quite possibly the best reboot of a franchise I've ever seen.

Nonstop action, a surprisingly in-depth story, smooth and engaging pacing, and a variety of enemies, combos, and secrets to keep you going.

The roots of this game show through in the best of ways, though engaging and frenzied combat, fun level design, and really just that ever-present feeling that you are a total badass.

The guns feel powerful and despite being a fairly straightforward game, never does it feel repetitive or mindless. I would argue it's the perfect length and provides longevity in each level's engaging "challenges."

Multiplayer wasn't anything special, but I would highly encourage any FPS player to take this for a stroll.\t\t\t\t\t
