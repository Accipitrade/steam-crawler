\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is Erfwerms honest review of Doom.


Fun 5/5 - If you are a fan of quake style games, then Doom is for you. I played Doom way back in the day when i was a kid and back then, it was one of my favorite games. This game appeals to the people that played Doom way back then, and also grabs the attention of people that like fast paced run and gun type of shooters. If you try to play this like COD, your probably going to have a bad time. Grab the shotgun, run into room, assess the situation as fast as you can and identify the most dangerous targets, and go. Thats pretty much my stragety here.

Graphics 5/5 - I'm running this game on a gtx970 and it is smooooooooooth. I'm getting about 70 - 90 fps with no stuttering, tearing, or artifacts. Everything moves great with zero issues.

Story 4/5 - I was able to follow the story easily. There is enough dialogue to keep you involved and moving forward. My only complaint (and i recognize this is a weird complaint) is I just dont give a S***. Its no fault of the games, but i kind of found myself creating my own little backstory as i ran from room to room killing everything i could find. I guess the story they give is a bit too involved for me, where as it would of just made more sense to have a "Hey, your in hell, kill the demons and get back home" type of story. But then again, i guess if it did, others might critize the game on lack of depth.

Audio 5/5 - I typically dont review the audio when i write a review, but for this game, its a must. HOLY S***. I would give this category a higher number if my imaginary rules allowed it. Kill demons while listening to heavy metal. Its like eggs and bacon. Steak and potaoes. Peanut butter and jelly. It works perfectly. Its not some cookie cutter metal either. Its legit. I dont recognize any of the songs (they might not be on any cd, im too lazy to check), but the music just fits perfectly while running around and killing demons.

Overall 5/5 - I know i gave the story a 4 but thats really my own hangup. Everything is top notch, and doesnt require a gtx 1080 to run.

NOTE : As of right now, i have NOT tried the multiplayer. (I tried it in the beta, and i wasnt a fan, but it would be unfair of me to review it in this review without trying it first).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never been a major fan of the Doom series (though I have always had respect for it). This game is a lot of fun, especially on the harder difficulties. With the harder difficult levels enabled you're forced to go from "shoot and don't stop moving" to "shoot acurately and keep moving so you're dodging rounds AND getting a better tactical advantage".
For some reason Steam says I've only played this game for 16.8 hours. I've actually put more like 100-ish hours into this...because it's very addictive and very satisfying. There isn't a "reload" action you can perform at will. You either have ammo for a certain weapon or you don't. The health system in this game is very unique and well done. There are health packs through each map, but they're usefulness is geared more torward the moments just before or right after combat. The most effective way to gain lost health is kill your enemies.....the more brutal the kill style, the more health you'll get (and maybe some ammo if you're brutal enough...........ahhemmm.....chainsaw).
In addition to health there is armor which ranges between 0 (no armor) to 100 (full armor). Once you're armor has been depleted all damage received starts depleating health. Even if you dont have full health, you can still gain armor if you find, and you can have more armor than you do health. This makes for an interesting gameplay balance and also provides you with more options tactically....and you're going to need all the tactics you can learn if you're going to play this how it's meant to be played (not on easy or even normal difficulty).
.....and the soundtrack. (Un)holy hell! The soundtrack to this game is outstanding. The best way I can think of to describe the soundtrack is that it sounds like Trent Reznor and Meshuggah had a little too much to drink one night, and nine months later the soundtrack to this game was born. It's the only game soundtrack I know of that I would pay money for just to be able to listen to it anytime I want.\t\t\t\t\t
