Past meets future in a violent way
NOTE: THIS IS A MIXED REVIEW, BUT I'M VOTING "YES" SINCE MY OPINION LEANS TO A POSITIVE SIDE

At first, I denied that the old DOOM could be back in a modern engine like other recent FPS games use... until I decided to give it a shot.
This game plays like Quake or Unreal Tournament - fast-paced, you can avoid projectiles - while still maintaining characteristics of the old DOOMs - the enemies, the guns, the violence. A really curious aspect of the game is Glory Kills: deal enough damage and the enemy staggers for several seconds, allowing you to brutally murder it and get some health - and this mechanic plays a huge part in the game.

At Ultra-Violence (Hard) difficulty, there was a good amount of challenge in the 10-hours, 13-stage campaign. The story is just there in case one is needed, since it really tells you to kill demons to avoid the huge disaster.

Graphic-wise, the game looks fantastic and there's a good amount of detail in every stage. Many secrets are well-hidden, and it runs smoothly for the majority of time - I just had trouble during a small section of one stage.

DOOM also brings upgrades to your arsenal to keep you up with the challenge. Some raise max HP, armor or ammo; others give additional effects to weapons and there are suit upgrades - show secrets on map, explosion immunity, boosts to power up.

Sadly, Bethesda tried to push a subpar multiplayer mode into this, probably limiting the awesome campaign. Not only that, but the company raised the prices of their games in many countries, and I paid, with a 50% discount, AAA price ($60) for a 10-hour campaign with little replayability.

In the end, this is not a game to miss if you are looking for intense action. Don't hesitate to buy it on a sale (it's not worth full price)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome game! I mean, honestly i would say that if you had to pick a video game that is a perfect summary of 'video games' this would be it. classic campaign with pre-designed levels, tons of great enemies that almost feel procedural (don't want to say procedural as i think the actual number of specific enemies that spawn are pre-determined, but the way they attack you isn't) and there are lots of secrets to be found in each level, that all are somewhat rewarding. there are also TONS of easter egg secrets. like seriously A LOT OF EASTER EGGS.

the multiplayer is just as fast paced and visceral as the campaign. haven't beaten the story mode yet, so to be fair i really can give it a judgement just yet, but i am at least halfway through (been literally to hell and back) and it is insanely fun. i wouldn't catagorize this game as horror, but sometimes, in the right setting, with the right sound effects, this game can genuinely make you jump.

only played for about 15 hours so far, but so far so good! i personally love the multiplayer section, as it is tough as nails, bloody, explosive, and just as addicting as the campaign (if not more addicting, considering the expansive customization options). in the 15 hours i've split my time at about 70% multiplayer 30% campaign. will update my review once ive finished the campaign (w/out spoilers of course) but i would definitely say that so far, without a doubt, i would recommend ID Software's next addition to the DOOM franchise. BUY IT RIGHT NOW!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot a bad game. Worth a playthrough but if I were to sum this up in one word, it would be "overrated". The game looks fantastic and is very well optimized. The controls are great and the action is fun. Plus I really like the idea of the secret levels and just how well hidden the secrets are in general.

But there are a lot of little things that add up and keep this from being great. Cliche, deep character voices, unskippable intro videos, being locked in a room and/ or forced to watch something (more annoying when you just want to find the secrets and finish the level). Plus some of the weapons have weak sound effects as well as lackluster upgrades. Don't even get me started on the fact you can only use your chainsaw a few times and its only good for replenishing your ammo. What. The. Hell!!!

Plus I never liked the idea of being locked in a room until you "kill all of your demons". Granted, this is my opinion but I feel you shouldn't be "forced" to do combat in a game where combat is the main focus. That's part of why the original Doom series was so good.

Rant over, carry on.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a first-person shooter developed by id Software and published by Bethesda Softworks.

Story in a nutshell: In Doom you’ll step into the boots of a lone Marine a battle hardened man ready to destroy hell’s minions once again, who wakes up strapped to a table and is immediately tossed into the dangers that plague the surface of Mars, where an abandoned mining facility has become home to some of the worst assailants you only see in your dreams. Once the Doom Marine gets his bearings, he soon discovers a shocking truth behind all of the activity on Mars. Equipped with futuristic battle technology and a practically impenetrable suit of armor, you’ll face off against hell’s finest abominations all while trying to survive their onslaught.

The sound of Doom: The audio is probably some of the best I’ve heard in a long time. Everything from the environmental sounds-capes, the blasts of powerful weapons, voice acting, and music all sound fantastic. Let me start by saying that the weapons sound powerful and satisfying all the way down to the impacts each projectile makes. One of my favorite things is how scary the enemies sound, blood curdling screams, grumbles, growls, and even the sound of them dying, really made me smile. While you’re busy fending off hordes of demonic spawns, you’ll feel more obligated to do so as adrenaline fills your veins with Doom’s heavy metal music it’s almost hard not to. All the music was beautifully scored, timed, and composed, giving Doom the epic feel it always had. Voice acting was well done also, the characters felt alive and as though each of them have their own little story to tell. Voice acting isn’t easy, but the actors they chose to play the parts did a fine job at capturing the mood.

Frantic Game-play: I’ve been going on about sound it’s time to pay how the game plays a visit. Doom starts off with a neat cinematic and all the way through the game does things really well to recapture the old school feel of the franchise’s roots. Where this may seem like something most wouldn’t care for it’s certainly a step in the right direction. Even though Doom does several things right, it also falls flat in some areas. While you’re prancing through the beautifully rendered dark and gritty world Doom sets you on a warpath through you may find that some levels are rather difficult to navigate even with a simple to understand mini map and world map. The map leaves a few things to be desired. I did like how the map of each level was organized into floors, the layout of where ramps and staircases were, things like that but it often confused me to the point to where I had to back track to find a door or room I may have missed. During my play through I felt that I wasn’t let down in any way, shape, or form. It felt as fast as it should. The guns fired quickly, minor puzzle elements that send you into different parts of the level to collect skulls or key-cards to progress through. Mix that in with frantic gun-play, awkwardly jumping from platform to platform, pulling yourself up to other areas that may give you a height advantage. Another thing I was happy with is how certain power-ups that gave you invincibility, berserk, shield, or speed each of these things were cleverly placed to give you a fighting chance to survive the hordes. There were moments where the jumping felt a little strange and sometimes the ledge in which you were trying to grab was somehow missed.

During your time in Doom you’ll often be given challenges that take you into small arenas where you have to complete a series of tasks before a timer expires. If you’re successful in completing these you are rewarded handsomely. Some of the rewards however, proved to be rather useless in most cases but all that is made up for with Doom’s upgrade system. This system allows you upgrade your armor and weapons on the fly when things are quiet before you move on to the next area. Each of the weapons have their own exclusive alternate firing modes or improved stats to further better their effectiveness in combat. Doom Marine’s suit also has it’s own specific upgrades which improve level navigation, and survivability. I see this as a nice touch, who doesn’t like to upgrade their equipment? It adds a depth that Doom never set foot in and sort of adds similar mechanics you’d find in RPGs. During gunfights, aside from blowing demons to bits, you’ll have the opportunity to perform killing blows on them. Each demon has a collection of a few different moves you can unleash on them. However, it lacks in variety, seeing a lot of the same animations played over and over will start to get repetitive but that still doesn’t mean it isn’t fun to get up close and personal while disfiguring an already disfigured face. All that aside, Doom’s world is filled with plenty of secrets, easter eggs, and bobble head dolls. So if you want to take a break from frantic demonic slaughtering, take the time to explore the game world and have fun with what you’ll find. Often times the AI seemed a little stupid to the point to where you feel like you’re staring at a brainless thing that has no idea what to do with you or where to go to keep from getting killed. For the most part, the AI is well aware of their surroundings and you, so be careful.

“An old friend.”

The Multiplayer aspect of Doom is pretty common. You’re given some familiar choices such as Deathmatch, Team Deathmatch, and Capture the flag. Even in multiplayer, Doom feels fast and as smooth as butter here the developers did an excellent job at keeping things frantic and fast paced. There are also a smaller handful of game modes that may tickle your fancy. Even though multiplayer is great, there’s a limited amount of maps that don’t feel very big. However, it doesn’t stop there. Doom features a mode called SnapMap which allows players like you and me to create our own levels and challenges without needing to worry about downloading additional tools to keep the fun rolling. The features of SnapMap are very user friendly. It features pre-constructed rooms and hallways with walls, ceilings, and floors. It’s up to you as the designer to add in decorations, enemies, power-ups, and challenges.

Graphic sickness: Graphically, this game is beyond phenomenal! It’s beautifully rendered maps are chock full of disgusting goodness. The game is a beast, therefor you’re going to need a very modern machine to be able to enjoy this game’s visual feel or if you have the money, go grab yourself an Xbox One or PS4 and waive any chances of you dealing with sluggish game-play. Thankfully on PC, you have the option to switch between Vulkan or OpenGL 4.5 as well as a plethora of different settings for you to mess with. Vulkan, is a new collection of visual libraries that allow for the less fortunate people to be able to play Doom at an ideal setting with minimal chugs and delays.

The biggest thing I like about Doom is the modern re-imagining of classic Demons. Each creature feels legitimately scary, gross, and cool all in one package. There are times where you’ll find yourself caught in a dark area while fighting demons and the only time you see them is when the light from your muzzle flash illuminates them, that makes for a horrific experience especially if you’re playing the game by yourself in the dark. Environments are very well done! The lighting, fog, and the capturing of the mood is something that actually made me shiver. While the monsters and environments are fine and dandy, let’s not forget about the guns. All the guns feel and look like they work, the texture details are amazing, it’s almost like you can reach into your computer monitor and grab it. Each level, monster, and gun are well crafted.

Closing words: Doom is a fine shooter and despite the minor flaws, Doom will take you on a thrill ride which is well worth looking into. 8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile i do generally like the game and play it often, the spastic MP and re-binding problems coupled with the price makes me tell people to wait until its around $40 or so. Graphics are sweet, gameplay is pretty polished, runes and abilities to unlock, lots of Glory kills to see, original levels worked in as secrets, lots of weapon mods to unlock, figures to collect.

I like the story aspect explaining why our soldier is such a bad ass. And even why we can comeback, since in one level we see many of our type of "sarcophogus" laying about as if we just come out of a new casket each time. The story of the protaganist is okay. Same old marine, total mute. Never as much as a "yes" or "UMPH!". I have played through the SP probably about 5 times by now. Haven't gotten far on Ultra Nightmare.

As stated, the MP is a waste of time. They were too focused on making it fast instead of fun and balanced. The result is a jumping, spam fest. Weee.

Level builder is also a waste of time. No good levels to play. Making levels in cumbersome, time consuming, and unsatisfactory.

So for around $40 USD, it would be worth it. Much more than that, and i would be getting a little sour.\t\t\t\t\t
