\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been waiting to play this game since I saw the first trailer and boy, it did not disappoint. No reloading, no regenerating health, no cover. Massive intricate maps with lots of secrets. Just action packed demon madness. This takes DOOM back to the roots with a few new additions. The most important thing is, this game is fun, fast and action packed. I loved every moment of it. The glory kills might seem like a gimmick at first, but they provide a short reprieve from the endless onslaught. All of the old favourite weapons are there, now with mods, which you don't have to use but believe me you will want to. The classic double barrels shotgun is my favourite for sure.

On to what I didn't like. I didn't care much for the rune trials, some of them were fun but most were boring. The actual runes are cool can actually help depending on your play style. The sound sometimes cut out, but luckily it didn't happen a lot. By far my biggest issue with the game is the loading times, it's atrocious. My whole computer starts quicker than this game. The actual loading of the levels are so slow as well. I later figured out that the nightmare texture settings made it worse. The texture loading times aren't great either, but this is how the engine is made. I later figured out that reducing the anisotropic filtering to 4x made this much better, but it's sad that I had to.

All things aside, this is by far the best FPS in years followed closely by Wolfenstein The New order. It's a must buy for any FPS veteran and DOOM fan.

Nominated for my "Best Comeback of a Series" award!\t\t\t\t\t

However after playing for over 12 hours my save file became currupt for no reason. I looked online and this seems to be a common issue. I tried all the suggestion online for getting it working again, but they all failed. I was 4 levels from the end of the game and had been collecting all the secret items on my playthrough.

At the moment I can't see myself playing the game again. I really would like to complete it but I just don't want to invest all that time again doing the same levels, with the possibilty that the files might just currupt again.

It is a good game, but the developers need to do something about save files currupting before I can recommend it. If you do get DOOM, I would make regular backups of you saves so you don't end up losing everything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt had to be done, didnt it? I mean buy the latest version of Doom: the game that started the FPS genre of gaming back in the early nineties. I was there in 93 when Albion, in true Mephistophelian fashion, introduced me to the game. From the moment I started the demo version of the game in 1993, I was hooked and it was no less the case now in 2016. A few minutes into the demo, I was GBP 39.95 (USD 50) lighter and enjoying every minute of it. But is Doom 2016 worth the hefty outlay? Maybe it does, because I do have big reservations, but more of this later.

First, the game looks great. Beautifully rendered graphics and superlative sound skip along smooth as glass in spite of the fact that I dont have an extreme high-end machine; just an i5 3330, 8GB RAM and an Nvidia GTX 960. Back are the old villains from the original game along with some new ones along the way. Back also are the weapons from the original game, but now fully modifiable, all except two. The story is very much the same, such as it is: demons from Hell have invaded a UAC Mars research facility, which was in the process of tapping into energy from the netherworld as a new clean, cheap and infinite energy source of power for humankind. However, the inhabitants of Hell have taken a very dim view of all this and its now your job to wipe them out and put a stop to it all. As stories go, this sucks like an Electrolux vacuum cleaner on speed, but who cares? The proceedings seem to bear out ex-id CEO John Carmacks contention that a video game is like a porn movie: a plot is expected, but not entirely essential. And such is the case here: the action is second to none with our hero in permanent sprint mode and no shortage of dangerous zippy baddies to dodge and kill. All the power-ups from the original are there too with the addition of Quad Damage, a relic from the now sadly demised Quake. Forget the well-crafted story-lines and wonderful characterisation of other games, such as the Far Cry series, along with the broad sandbox panoramas of such games this is Doom, where claustrophobia rules and the horrific action barely lets up. Theres no humour to sugar the pill, so in a game where you can tear your enemies apart with your bare hands when theyre weak, Doom 2016 is definitely not for the squeamish.

So whats the catch? And theres always at least one, isnt there? For me, its the ubiquitous boss fights. The first of these is possible to win in single player mode, providing you level down the difficulty setting, but the next (and possibly the last) are, for all intents and purposes, impossible to win solo. You fight in a small enclosed arena with no cover, no power-ups and no NPC support. I made repeated attempts to progress beyond the Necropolis boss fight but after my twelfth failed attempt, I decided that the frustration was elevating my blood pressure so high, I was close to suffering a stroke. I tried every tactic I could muster hologram decoys, frag grenades and siphon bombs to regain health and armour, but not no avail. I jumped, ducked, dived, dodged and weaved between shots but the health and armour loss were relentless. At least in the Borderlands series, the boss confrontations at least give you a fighting chance (often with cover and other secondary opponents to kill for power-ups) and the really tough raid boss fights are optional and you are advised to take some friends if you want to attempt them. In Doom though, your opponents are little short of invincible when playing solo and you cannot progress beyond them. Even using a cheat code causes the game file to become corrupted and unusable.

Consequently, I cant comment on the latter stages of the game because I am unable to progress there and, for nearly forty quid, I am well pissed off at this.

This latter point is why I, reluctantly, cannot recommend the game for older gamers (and yes, were not all fresh-faced teenagers these days) who may experience serious health problems as a result of the developers penchant for making things so sodding difficult that we all suffer heart attacks or strokes playing the latest version of what has been in the past a much-loved and venerated game. I suggest that Bethesda/id should take note of this because we now live in very litigious times.

EDIT Since writing the above, I've managed to progress beyond the second boss fight to the latter portion of the game, but not without a considerabole amount of angst.\t\t\t\t\t
