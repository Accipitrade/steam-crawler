\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could recommend this game. I wish I could've actually played it to form an opinion on the game itself. Unfortunately I didn't get past the screen with all the user agreements.

I've requested a refund for this game, which I should get because I spent only half an hour playing the game reading the terms of use. Yes, I actually try to read these rather than just clicking "accept" without knowing whether or not I've signed away my immortal soul. Not that it does much good usually.

In the case of this game, the terms of use are presented vaguely and seem to give the game owners arbitrary power to revoke my right to play a game that I paid them real money for.

More detail on the issues I have are as follows:
1. The game page provides no information at all that would indicate ANY additional agreements are required. You are not made aware of their existence by the seller until you have already installed the game (a process that took my computer about 2.5 hours) and started running it. At this point you learn there are a whopping FOUR documents for you to read. Considering every other steam game with a special EULA has a big yellow notice about it, I think this is actually deceptive.

2. I can't really understand most of the information in these agreements, because they are vaguely written. Most of them appear to be standard, if in my opinion undesirable, stuff about how they'll spy on you and tell all their friends in the advertising industry basically every bit of info they can scrounge from your computer whenever you run the game. Yes, I know that's not what it actually says, and is likely an exaggeration of what they actually do. But it's all very general and is written in legalese so as far as I know, that is what I'm agreeing to.

3. I can understand the game's code of conduct enough to recognize that it does not state any scope for some of the actions it prohibits, which means it is logically possible to interpret as prohibiting me from engaging in them anywhere. One of the possible consequences for violating the code of conduct is banning me from playing the game. Not just from playing multiplayer, but from playing the game at all. This is theft-maybe not legally, but morally, it's theft. I am not going to sign an agreement that creates the logical possibility of me having consented to theft. And it's absolutely none of Bethesda's business if I name my character in some other game "HitlerBundy420blazeit." I'm not sorry for having a dark and off-color sense of humor; without that sort of thing we'd never have gotten Blazing Saddles.

4. One of the things prohibited by the code of conduct is "engaging in conspiracy theory or misinformation." This is a fancy, socially acceptable way of saying "making statements that the people who own the game don't like." Maybe that's not how it is implemented, but for me to believe that would require trust. I'm sorry, Bethesda and ZeniMax, but I do not trust you. It's nothing personal-I don't trust anyone I've never met. So I don't trust you not to use this as an excuse to ban me from playing this game, which I paid for, because I posted this negative review complaining about how your user agreements are too vague.

I hope the people who made this game read this review, because I honestly think this is an issue they should be aware of too. Some of us actually read the agreements you expect us to sign. And individually we are powerless to negotiate them. We have two choices: agree to your terms, or ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• off. Since you can set whatever terms you want, and play with our lives like dolls (that's what it feels like at least), I think you at least ought to present those terms to us in a clearly-written fashion that allows us to understand what we're agreeing to. I'm sure you would expect the same.

I think this is something people who buy games should be more aware of as well. When you click "I agree" on license agreements, you are signing a contract and actually stating that you agree to the contents. Do you agree that the makers of a game should have the right to block you entirely from playing the (mainly single-player) game you paid for because you said something they found offensive? Because you criticized them? I don't. You have the power to do something about this if you don't like it.\t\t\t\t\t
