\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Doom reboot finally arrived after years of development hell. Was it worth it? Yes, but only just.

Starting with the technical aspects, Doom 2016 shines. It looks great, moves great, and sounds great. What perhaps isn't so great, in my humble opinion, is its approach to the combat which is, in a word, repetitive: enter room, doors lock, 1st wave of enemies spawn in, kill them. Second wave arrives, kill them. Rinse and repeat two more times. Doors unlock. Enter next room. Doors lock. 1st wave of enemies spawn in... see above.

Sure, the fast-paced action and constant movement to stay alive is still there, but the original games weren't constantly confining you into a tight space and limiting the way you approached the combat. These games (especially Doom 2) had vast, open areas which allowed for greater freedom in how you moved around and engaged the enemies. Doom 2016's approach, by comparison, just feels cheap and lazy.

Lastly, the multiplayer took huge missteps by introducing Call of Duty style loadouts, lacklustre levels and a bare-bones selection of modes (errors sadly repeated in the disappointing Quake Champions).

Despite all of that, no other game makes you feel like the badass you are in Doom 2016, and that's its saving grace.\t\t\t\t\t
