\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA modern expertly crafted design of DOOM and it delivers it perfectly. The gameplay is still kept the same with added bonus's such as point tokens to upgrade your weapons, suit and others such as less damage from environmental damage, grenades recharging faster etc. Once you nail the gameplay it feels ever so satisfying to get health from glory kills aswell as rune trails/power ups to make you even stronger, this game is a true definition of a power fantasy and even on higher difficulties with all that power it doesn't disappoint as it's still has moments where it can beat you down despite all that power.

Snapmap is alot of fun to play around with to both create and also play others creations. It adds more content to the game and lets those creative folks have a sandbox to play around with.

Arcade Mode is my fav gamemode as it's basically the prototype for what's to come in DOOM: Eternal, the gameplay is more fast pace than ever as you're trying to get the highest score possible in all the campaign levels which is alot of fun.

Highly recommend it for those who want a modern experience of how old school shooters are like as the level design and gameplay is still there and it really captures alot of old school elements while also keeping it modern.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFeels like old doom, plays like a new doom. It's familiar in many way and the newer elements of this game bring out the adventure to this game. Even though it's not centered around adventure, the game provides it alongside intense action, satisfying gore and intense weaponry.

The campaign is a bit more linear than previous doom games (as in pathfinding and level design) but not too much as to be a "shooter on a railroad". There are about 2 cutscenes that last about 30 minutes, but most of the campaign is raw gameplay and carnage. It's easy to grasp the objectives throughout the levels and always fun to find secrets. The campaign is replayable if you want to go for score in the Arcade Mode and rack up points and beat others times on levels.

The multiplayer was a dissapointment. Constant cheaters, bad networking and lack of gamemodes. However, the multiplayer is fun, balanced and intense when servers and players are okay. As of now, there isn't many players online playing multiplayer as much as I'd like and a lot of gamemodes have 0 players in playlists.

Snapmap, the custom campaign/multiplayer map builder, is pretty neat too. There are various features that come along with making snapmaps and possibilties are large. It is limited to a Halo Forge-like editor and you get no access to mod-tools or map and geometry editor. There are plentiful amounts of snapmaps (if when you exclude free point maps), and theres always a map to play. Also a voting system governed by players to let maps that are liked get featured daily, weekly and monthy. I prefer snapmap since I do not like to play the campaign over and over again.

All-in-all, a really good game. Solid shooter, remains DOOM and is absolutely disgusting. The best game I played in 2016.\t\t\t\t\t
