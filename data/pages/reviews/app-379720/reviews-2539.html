\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great revival of a classic game. Very enjoyable fast-paced action, fun gore and good level and monster design. I played through the single player campaign as I usually do and had a lot of fun. Game rewards exploring and secrets aren't absurdly hard to find. Alternate modes and upgrades for weapons are really useful and there is plenty of ammo so you are almost never limited to only using one or two weapons.
Surprisingly Doom has also a lot of background info about the story, monsters and other various aspects of the game, but there are no lengthy action stopping cut scenes and if you are interested, you can pause the game and read about everything at your leisure.

Had also a quick glance at multiplayer, seems ok and has a lot of customization options and gear to unlock. Probably offers quite a lot of fun as long as there are enough players.

Snap maps didn't seemed very interesting, but I usually care only about the single player campaign experience, so I didn't explored this aspect very deeply.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played the single player aspect of this game and not much imagination went into this.....

OK.... PRO:- Graphics are gorgeous with tons of details...and mostly red.
-Some basic rpg characters build and weapon upgrades
-Lots of weapons and upgrades for them.

CON: -Very repetitive. You're tossed in multiplayer designed levels and waves of monsters appears in random location. Kill a bunch then move on to the next.
-The only AI associated with monster is that they generally won't approach a doorway where you wait for them.
-Ambience sound and Music is good if you like metal but all monster sound like clogged vacuums or sink vortexes.
-Not many way to approach this, run around and shoot without being shot. Can't use topography much or progress at your own pace because of monster wave patterns.
-Weapons are weak, even at the basic difficulty it take gobs of ammunition to kill something unless it's a basic possessed.
-Consolatis saving point, nothing rare these days, But killing the same 10 monsters every time to get to the hard one get old fast. You just can't quit a level mid term without losing all your progress.

Conclusion: I know they tried to bring back the original Doom feeling and maybe the multiplayer mode is great but the campaign seem to have been an aftertough. I'm halfway trough it and it feel more like a chore than a game. I play mostly because I've paid good money for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs good as they get!

In the world of high paced, sci-fi first person shooter games, DOOM is king. You get very nice graphics, intuitive controlls, no bugs at all, a lot of super-fun weapons, a vast variety of enemys and great overall gameplay.
If thats not good enough for you, there is actually an interesting story with quite a lot of background information on pretty much anything from your suite to every single weapon. I also like the difficulty of this game, its challenging enough without being tedious. Theres an upgrading system for your suite and every weapon, most weapons also have two modifications which both can be upgraded.

I never tried the mp-mode so i cant comment on it.

I do recommend this game a lot, even though normally i`m not a big fan of super fast paced first person shooter games.
