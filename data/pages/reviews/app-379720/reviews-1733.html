\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor some insane reason, when i first began playing doom back in 2016 on PS4, i played it on the easiest difficulty, looking back at that decision, that was the DUMBEST mistake i have made in a game in a very long time. this game is so much fun on the harder difficulties, every single fight in the game makes you feel like an absolute badass, coming out of every battle feeling more and more triumphant.

There is a rhythm of gameplay, each demon adds another level of the dance of death with the doomslayer. you never feel the different combat areas get stagnant, as everytime you have mastered the demons you already have been fighting, you get to try a new one on for size. The bosses are fine, nothing special, I don't find myself enjoying the bosses as much on secondary playthroughs, but the glory kills on them, more than makeup for that.

The rune trials add an extra level of depth to the combat, not a huge amount but it is a nice extra to make the combat a little more fun.

The snapmap, well... i've never really enjoyed custom map creation stuff built into games (aside from Super Mario Maker and games based entirely on the idea of community based levels) there's nothing wrong with snapmap, it just feels like i has more to offer. some mods would help fix this problem, being able to import your own models would be really cool.

Multiplayer i cannot give my opinion on as i have yet to play it

Overall, doom is a fantastic experience for both old fans and new, and this game helps combine the awesome arena combat of the original doom, and mechanics of the modern day (glory kills are the best thing ever)

8/10

Bring on Eternal!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been a fan of Doom ever since the first version came out on DOS, and spent a lot of fun hours playing solo and with friends and creating custom maps etc. As such i was quite thrilled to see the reimagining with modern graphics and techniques, and I was going to give it a solid recommendation after seeing the result and getting some basic gameplay in, finding the challenge interesting with pretty smart AI ranged aiming etc. (although once you figure out how it "thinks" it becomes less of a challenge).

That is, until it became painfully clear what the game mode of this game is; no matter what environment you are in (which are nicely crafted!) the basic gameplay is this:
1. Enter room
2. Room closes, locking you in
3. You kill x enemies in the room
4. Room opens up and you continue down the otherwise completely linear map
5. Rinse and repeat

Each room being relatively small, arena-style areas where the enemies get game-cheating advantages to provide "challenge" (in quotes).
The bosses are just bullet sponges to make them tough, but really, making them very bland endurance fights is pretty lame.

So, overall verdict: it's pretty, well made in terms of visual appeal (if you don't get tired of the repetitive nature of animations), but the actual gameplay just absolutely kills this as a negative. Sorry folks, nice try but I can't honestly recommend it after a number of hours of play.\t\t\t\t\t
