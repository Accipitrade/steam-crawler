\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR - if you liked Doom and/or Doom II or want a pretty straight forward run and gun shooter, get this game. Masterpiece of what an FPS game should be.

I was very nervous about this game going in - as a kid who grew up with Wolf 3D, Doom 1 & 2 as my early intro's to gaming this franchise (and iD as a whole) are an important part of my gaming life.

The game was hit by all kinds of problems and delays but when it came out it was more than worth it. A pure, run and gun, fast paced, mindless FPS. No covering for shields to regenerate, just pure 'enter room, kill everything, regroup, move on to the next room' non-stop carnage with a brilliant soundtrack to keep the pace going. The gore filled glory killers are amazing, so satisfying to rip the demons apart limb from limb, and allows a brilliant mix of gun play and melee combat. I was worried the glory kills would break up the flow of combat but the way they work is just seamless and smooth, allowing you just enough time to catch your breath and play who gets a face full of lead next.

Upgrade and menus are kept pretty simple and straight forward, easy to navigate and straight to the point. There's also the traditional secrets you'd expect in a Doom game and the addition of uncovering snippets of original Doom levels and then being able to play the full thing after (with modern enemies and weapons, but classic textures and maps) is just the cherry on top of this wonderful masterpiece.

There's a bit of story to the game, ultimately though it doesn't really matter too much, there's no massive cut-scenes to break up the game, you go from level to level and get most of the narrative as you're going through so you can keep the pace going constantly.

A game truly worthy of carrying the DOOM name and a call back to when FPS where simpler. Here's a gun. Demons are invading. Kill the demons.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the combat and gun play, fantastic, top notch. The levels and enemy design are visually interesting. It's great to see enemies from my childhood realized in today's modern graphics. Unfortunately the story is not interesting, it just serves to string levels together and I found it impossible to get invested in anything going on. Also Doomguy is frustratingly slow, so zooming around the fight arenas the way I want is not possible. The game requires you to complete finisher moves to gather health and ammo, which is fun, however you are vulnerable to attack while the Doomguy slowly goes through the finisher animation.

The music is excellent, battling enemies while jamming to the sound track never gets old. I love the difficulty, but the game's checkpoint system is abysmal. You just spent an hour refining your skills to complete a Gore Nest or Rune challenge on Nightmare, you finally win, you are thrilled, and then you die for whatever reason and the game resets you back so far you have to do the entire fight again. There was no reason to remove manual saves from this series, and I can't respect a game that doesn't respect my time and effort. Ultimately, I had a great time fighting through this game but would only recommend on discount.\t\t\t\t\t
