\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most fun shooters I have played in years. This game FEELS like a Doom game. The action is constant and unrelenting, the demons are unique and modeled well, the level design keeps the maps focused while avoiding becoming too linear and the gunplay...oh the gunplay. The shooting mechanics are extremely satisfying as well the fluidity of your movements. After you get accustomed to the speed and the gameplay style you feel like a Martian god blessed with the spirit of a panther as you hunt your prey with speed and agility. It's fun to say the least.

The performance is stellar with the addition of the Vulkan API. Even somewhat dated cards with low VRAM can manage a consistent 60fps on 1080p with medium-high settings. I'm playing with a GTX 960 2gb and my FPS is at a near constant 60 at 1080p with TSAA(8x) and almost all settings on high. Just beautifully optimized.

I could go on and on but I recommend this game to anyone who likes video games in general. It's just too damn good. This game needs to be out of your Steam wishlist and in your library already.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's DOOM. Not the kind of DOOM where you have a flashlight and you can't see a damn worth anything, not the kind of DOOM where a pickup is in the centre of a room and you just know half a hojillion enemies are going to teleport in behind you when you pick it up (next time, in front of you just to spice things up!)

No, this is the kind of DOOM where you live for the battles fought at a break-neck pace, flitting from a well-aimed rocket into a group of demons, to a shotgun blast in another's face before snapping its neck in a glory kill rain of much-needed health vials. And you do so because combat is fun. id have really managed to nail it in this game. There's a decent spread of death-dealing devices for you to use; some familiar, some new, but all potent and absolutely bad-ass. Weapon mods, which you unlock as you progress through the game, allow you to expand your arsenal's capabilities in ways that complement your play style.

The story, while mildly interesting from the perspective of being quite a different take from previous games in the series, isn't what should make you buy this. There's a bit right at the beginning of the game, just after you put on your Big Ol' Beelzebub Bashin' Boots, where one of the main characters guiding you along your way tries to engage you in conversation. This lasts right up to the point where your character takes the console and violenty throws it into a corner. I thought that a clever and amusing nod to the prospect that the story, here, is simply incidental and a framing device, rather than a pivotal part of the game.

For the 100%ers, there are also secrets to find and upgrades to acquire, as well as Rune Trials, which are miniature challenges in separate arenas which unlock additional buffs to your character. Some of the Rune Trials, however, can be keyboard-smashingly tricky to complete.

Multiplayer

I played the multiplayer beta prior to the game's release and was distinctly unimpressed. I gave it another go on the full release, and while it the "feel" of multiplayer is improved from the beta (this may be due to better maps than were in the beta), I doubt it's something that's going to draw, and keep, the crowds necessary to sustain a multiplayer scene. It ain't no Quake 3, that's for sure.

Summary

Buy it. Buy it to shoot every last thing that moves in the single-player campaign, and enjoy every last second of it. Don't buy it for an epic story-driven experience or the multiplayer.

Initially, especially after playing the multiplayer beta, I was skeptical that id would be able to pull this off. I'm glad they proved me wrong.\t\t\t\t\t
