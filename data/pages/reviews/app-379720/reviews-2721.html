\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom (2016) is, without a doubt, the best single player FPS I have picked up in years. I played through the campaign the first time in a single session (except for bathroom and smoke breaks) until noon the day after, and I still couldn't get enough. The level and sound design is awesome, the guns feel amazing and very impactful, and the glory kill mechanic is executed (heh) just right. The enemies are impressive and can even be a bit scary combined with some of the labyrinthian levels, but you're always perfectly aware of the fact that no matter what they throw at you, you are the worst monster in the room. Thanks to your high agility and the advantage you get from being on the move constantly, the combat is very fast-paced and smooth. It plays *nothing* like your traditional FPS with tactical elements, it doesn't bug you with misplaced sneak mechanics or lots of cutscenes and exposition, it's 120% action almost from start to finish.

The best thing about it, however, is that id software actually wrote a good story and progressed the lore as you'll find out if you explore the levels for intel and secrets, but they are entirely aware of the irony of having story in a Doom game, which is why the Doom Marine himself doesn't give a single tiny ♥♥♥♥ about any of it. Just like you, he's there to ♥♥♥♥ up demons, and all that is masterfully conveyed without a single voice line or facial expression.

Now excuse me, I need to get back to rip a Hell Knight several sizeable new ones.\t\t\t\t\t
