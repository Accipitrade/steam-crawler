-- ORIGINAL REVIEW:

Overall, very awesome. The retro gameplay style is certainly a breath of fresh air in a time when the only fast-paced arcade shooters are low-budget indie games.

In many ways, DOOM looks and fields like the next step in gaming. The overall visual quality is fantastic, but iD accomplishes this more with clever lighting and textures than with high-poly models, so the performance is buttery smooth. The user interface, and many other small details, are polished to a level I've never previously seen in gaming. This game has gotten a level of love that you normally only see in $300 million blockbuster movies. Little things like how your character adjusts the position of his weapon when you face a wall, or how the shotgun tilts when you activate its grenade launcher, just feel so right.

Gameplay is great; combat is very fast-paced and exciting. There's plenty of weapon diversity and you can customize your weapons with upgrades.

My main gameplay complaint with the game is relatively minor: there are several mini-objectives to complete in each level that unlock upgrade points or other abilities, and these often pull you out of the game by interupting the gameplay. Every time I get tasked with performing X melee kills from above or behind I start grinding my teeth. These require too much attention and distract from the regular gameplay; I want to have fun killing things, not be worrying about where to position a demon so that when he gets stunned I can jump onto him from a catwalk to perform the finishing move the game wants me to use.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR : This game is an amazing demon murder simulator filled with ridiculously satisfying and hilarious kill moves alongside some great weaponry. Just buy this game, you will not regret it.

______________________________________________________


Doom contains some of the most entertaining gameplay I have encountered in a long time. Within the first five minutes of the campaign I was hooked. You get access to guns which can later be modified with attachments which make killing demons very fun. There are also several unique finisher moves for all the different types of enemies, depending on where you initiate the move it will be different. Don't forget to try out the chainsaw and enjoy the rain of blood, gore, and bullets raining from the corpses of your enemies!

If you are looking for polished graphics and gorgeous skyboxes, this game will not disappoint you. Throughout all of the missions I have been taking time to enjoy all the details in the skyboxes.

The soundtrack for this game is another amazing selling point for me, it is a combination of metal and electronica. This works so well for setting the mood while you are playing the game it really brings it all together.

As far as multiplayer is concerned the maps are reminiscent of old school arena games. This contributes to the chaotic nature of each game. If you are missing the times of old school arena shooters and their unique gameplay, you will not want to miss this game.

If any of the above points sounds good to you, pick this game up!\t\t\t\t\t

Wow. Just, wow. Doom the reboot is FPS that relies on frenetic paced combat, lots of enemies flinging projectiles, a smorgasboard of weapons to tear said enemies apart, lots of milestones and secrets within the levels to customize and upgrade everything from weapon capabilities, to ammo, armor, and health capacity, to giving a one-off save from death.

Down sides, the map is borderline unreadable when hunting for collectibles, dying while platforming can be bad if it isn't right after a checkpoint (most checkpoints are right after big fights), and points of no return that are never telegraphed. You were looking for the way up to that field drone and accidentally went towards the objective? Oops, door's closed!

Overall, I'm genuinely blown away by the game. The game's about 8 hours long, SnapMaps (player made stuff) and Arcade mode (slightly altered campaign missions) add some replay value, and multiplayer is quite good. The difference in style from the cover-based shooter that most games have is very refreshing, and it's good to see another strong reboot alongside both Tomb Raider and Wolfenstein. I give it a 9.5/10, fantastic game that I would recommend anybody to buy.\t\t\t\t\t
