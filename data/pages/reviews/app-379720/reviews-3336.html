\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was playing DOOM II while waiting for Steam to finish setting up the game. As soon as it was ready, I went for it.

First playthrough on Ultra-Violence, no tutorials, no compass or objective markers, simple crosshair, minimal HUD (only health and ammo), no overlays, weapon changes only with keyboard shortcuts.

Let's just say this: the transition from DOOM II couldn't have been smoother and more positively surprising.

In the first 5 minutes you'll be already knee deep in it, blasting away at demons like in the old ones. Each demon has its own character and combat style: Imps are fiercer and faster than ever, and they come at you from all sides. Hell Knights are agile, fast and deliver some serious blows... and wait 'till you get to the Baron.

Level design is maze-like, combining linear corridors and sections with open ground arenas where most of the action will happen. Playing without markers and compass felt much more like an old school experience: exploring only by relying on visual clues is extremely satisfactory especially when searching for secrets, though the automap is still very useful.

The only complaints I have are related to relatively minor things: one is the presence of two or three story-related scenes that lock you in place for a brief amount of time and they cannot be skipped.
The second one that I noticed is that there are no running speed upgrades apart from the temporary Haste powerup, only combat speed upgrades (weapon and mod switching, faster glory kills, faster mantling) which do improve the pace somewhat, although I would still love to be able to speedup things a bit more, especially when comparing the pace to the old DOOMs.

It does feel like a DOOM with some Quake mechanics thrown in (platforming, air control), but I think those same mechanics have actually helped the game to step into more modern ground and at the same time feel like a unique, original game in its own right. In my opinion they did something that feels so good to play it's almost unbelievable. It's not about "franchise purity", it's about fun.

All in all I think the game feels just right: pace, power and enemy behaviour all combined to give an awesome "old-school meets new" feeling that hasn't been part of an FPS in a very long time.

Thanks id, you did it again.



Note: The mouse input is the best I've ever experienced in an FPS, after disabling mouse smoothing. I definitely recommend doing that.\t\t\t\t\t

The fixed checkpoint system makes the gameplay play out similar to a Souls game as you fight through the same situations to catch up to where you last died...this may not be preferable for some (no quick save), but for me I like being given the opportunity to attack the same areas in different ways.

Secrets are pretty good, though not quite as exciting to find as the original Doom's.

Minimap system works really well for the most part.

Havn't played multiplayer at all yet, singleplayer's where it's at for me.

Snapmap looks powerful but very limited in terms of environment design; you're given a bunch of prefab rooms that you can put props inside, but no direct control over room layouts. I'm hoping for a proper level editor down the line.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't really think there are many games that I can say are fun for all of the right reasons. There are plenty of games that I can point to that have some good points and have some bad points. And then there are those games were you wonder why they even bothered making it the way they made it in the first place. DOOM is one of those games that falls somewhere between the first two categories. On the one hand, the multiplayer is not worth anyone's time that has nostalgia towards the good old days of quake 3 arena or to anyone that enjoys a good arena shooter. But on the other hand, the single player is one of the most entertaining and just plain fun than any other FPS in recent memory. It seems that the game's single player was developed with the sole intention of being a fast-paced, action-packed and fun shooter that has so many call backs, references and old-school style secrets and pick-ups that even after my 25 hours of playing as of typing this review, I have not even found one-fourth of the secrets in this game.

So, in review:
pros:
+Extremely fun
+Fast-paced, action packed and visceral combat
+Oh so many secrets to discover
+Interesting story
+Long ( but not overly long) single player
+Just about everything else about the single player.

Cons:
-The multiplayer

In conclusion, I would strongly recommend this game to anyone who wants to have a fun time playing a single player in an FPS. Or, just to someone who wants to play a great game. Just avoid the multiplayer if you want to avoid disappointment.\t\t\t\t\t
