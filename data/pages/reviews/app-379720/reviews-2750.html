\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame itself looks fantastic. Graphics, level design, fluid combat, sound effects - really good.
Compared to Doom 3, monsters are better designed, constantly moving, very dangerous.
Still don't like trigger mechanic spawning monsters as you enter defined area or do action. Although is better then in previous Doom ( you don't need to go trough game backwards because of the monsters spawning begind your back) I prefer most AI opponents present in a level from the start ( original Doom 1 & 2 style ) and spawning should be rare.
But singleplayer campaign gameplay is top-notch.
Now what I don't like.
First and major complain - no campaign CO-OP. For me, it's a BIG one for me.
Some may argue that there is a SnapMap option as MP part. But after searching available maps (more then one year after release ) you will realize that maps are very simple, cartoonish, not interesting and far from quality of the campaign ones. Judging from my testing of the SnapMap editor, it is very limiting and you can't ( or is extremely hard to ) create anything more complex when you can only put predefined parts together even that monster behavior progamming is actually good. I'm not sure if the reason for this is technical ( MegaTexture technology ) or to allow create maps to consoles.
Deathmatch like MP maps are good. So if you like these mods there are good maps.

Pros:
+ graphics
+ flouid movement and combat
+ level design ( campaign )
+ sounds

Cons:
- no Co-Op campaign mode
- takes long time when switching/loading to MP part
- few quality custom maps (SnapMap limitations )
- MP menu interface designed for consoles
