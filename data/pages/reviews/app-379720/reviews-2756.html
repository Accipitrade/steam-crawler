\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPicked this up during the Summer Sale for $15. Well worth it! The newest iteration of the Doom franchise goes back to its roots in a big way by cutting out the attempts of introducing story that many lamented in the previous entry. I honestly was one of the few that didn't mind that in Doom 3, nor did I mind the attempt to be more of a horror themed game rather than an action game, but that's just me. As far as this Doom is concerned, I don't miss anything they stripped out of Doom 3's formula in the slightest. id/Bethesda made one of the most satisfyingly kinetic shooters where you can make your momentum count in a huge way. The glory kill system is a great way to get some instant satisfaction, and some health when you're running low, and when you switch to the chainsaw when - I don't know - you're running low on ammo, ammo comes out instead of health. Sure, it makes no sense that these death animations turn the demons into health/ammo pinatas, but if you care that much, you probably know well enough to stay away from any Doom game. This game was designed to be played, and played fast. I only had a few days off, so I marathoned it, which I would not recommend because I found myself getting a bit exhausted at times by the pace, but ultimately it was worth it.

The dynamic music was nice, and though limited to a few tracks, really added to the hardcore experience.

Definitely pick this up if you're on the fence!\t\t\t\t\t
