\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSingle-player first.

Nostalgic euphoria with a twist of modern day mechanics.

DOOM (2016) casts you ball's deep into pools of demon's blood, as you rip and tear your way through the gates of Hell itself. Rune powers, weapon upgrades, hidden secrets, alternate routes, and a seamless traversal system that adds vertically and momentum to every encounter.

Old weapon favorites can be augmented on the fly to provide advantageous firearm superiority, while "Glory Kills" provide the much needed health and armor for perseverance. Encompassing fear and doubt, is the so named, "Ultra Nightmare" difficulty. A mode where even seasoned shooter veterans fall to the almost impossible scenario.

DOOM (2016) manages to capture the essence of what made id Software's 1993 first-person shooter so remarkable, while adding a dash of the "ol' ultra violence." New concepts for familiar adversaries, blurs the line between the then and now.

DOOM (2016) is a must have addition to any "old school" shooter alumnus, or new generation undergrad. It's a hard-hitting, gritty, and beautiful shooter that should only be played on the most brutal of difficulties.

-Multi-player-

Although DOOM (2016) attempts to provide a multi-player experience, it feels shallow and out of place. An almost forced concept is a testament to our times, and how every shooter needs a multi-player feature. Held back by flaws in matchmaking, repetitiveness, and an unlock system that doesn't belong in what attempts to reflect an "arena shooter."

DOOM's multi-player falls flat in almost every respect. Snapmap is a nice, and simplistic concept; offering a quick glimpse into some of DOOM's mechanics and level design. However, much like the multi-player feature itself, it becomes another shallow concept that is anything but spectacular.

Fans of the DOOM franchise long for proper replayability, Co-Op, and modification tools. It is a wonder why these items were not, and are not, pushed to the forefront of design obligations. DOOM's longevity will be cut short by the emphasis of multi-player content and DLC, that essentially reflects the burden of capitalism in our industry.

Final thoughts and conclusion.

Even with the shortcomings in the multi-player aspects; DOOM (2016) is a fantastic first-person experience, that should be enjoyed by old fans, and new fans alike. I feel it marks the rebirth of one of the most iconic titles ever released in video game history.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall score: 9/10

Graphics: 10/10
Music: 10/10
Controls: 10/10
Replayability: 6/10


Pros:
- All around well polished fps, above average story.
- Extremely well optimized, played the first 80% of the game on a dualcore 2.7 ghz cpu.
- Custom map support through snapmap, not perfect but decent.
- Excellent soundtrack
- Weapon and perk progression, more of a meh really, for me anyways.


Cons:
- Very linear gameplay flow, go to area kill enemies, proceed to next area.
- Much more locked down than previous doom games, expected more openness given bethesda and id's history of allow thier games to be modded.
- Snapmap needs quite a bit of work, doesn't integrate snap map into the steam workshop....come on guys.
- Very linear game progression. Because of area based enemy spawning, very little tension when moving around the maps, something doom always had before.
Turn a corner and take a shotgun to the face or have a pack of boss type demons destroy you.

***SPOILER***
Cliffhanger ending could be considered pro or con....
