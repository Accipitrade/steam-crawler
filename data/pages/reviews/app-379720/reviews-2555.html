DOOM:
I just want to start off this review and say that before I played DOOM I strongly disliked metal,
well, that changed...

DOOM.. few words can really describe my experience with this game, it seems like the type of game that bores you to death doesn't it?
Continuously killing demons for no apparent reason seems like something that gets dull, well, you're wrong.

That's why it's hard to describe, DOOM's overall gameplay structure is basic but it's the details that make it so damn great.

Let's go through some things: crushing demons with brutal might whilst listening to a metal soundtrack that gets increasingly brutal as the game progresses is enough
to pump you with the adrenaline and testosterone levels of a pissed off gorilla.

Additionally, the game also features a story that really drags you in (even though it is not the main focus of the game), easy to use upgrade system for both weapons and your Preator suit,
intricate level designs with many secrets and collectables if you chose to explore them.

Pros:
  • Brutal Metal soundtrack
  • Crushing demon hordes with your bare hands and arsenal of weapons
  • Taxing high-quality graphics (well optimized)
  • Legit and completely badass lore
  • Punishingly difficult
  • You will lose your virginity after playing DOOM
  • You will have better taste in music
  • You will become an alpha male
Cons:
  • You can't get enough of this game
  • Why is it over?
  • I want more
  • Hard to describe to your friends
  • oh god they will never understand
DISCLAIMER: you might experience bone chillingly satisfying moments and[/i] your testosterone levels will increase... a lot.

In conclusion: It's brutal.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo put DOOM in a phrase, it's a "no nonsense" shooter.

The story is fairly simple and the gameplay is, ultimately, fairly repetitive - you kill things. A lot. However, its the gun mechanics and variety of combat actions that make the game fantasitically good fun to play.

It's a satisfying shooter. It really is. The variety of weapons is just about right - not too many to overwhelm you, not too few to be boring. The guns also feel 'heavy' and powerful - they're not little pea shooters or anything. Additionally, the upgrades that you can apply to weapons adds that little extra to combat in terms of providing a distinct experience. But most importantly, it's the flow of combat - no matter how I choose to fight, whether with a shotgun, assault rifle or rocket launcher, the game flow is smooth and satisfying.

Graphics wise, the game looks very good. It's atmosphere is dark, yet not overly horrifying to put you off playing. Most importantly, the game isn't full of jump scares like it's last incarnation! The game runs perfectly - I've had no crashes or frame rate issues whatsoever.

There is a multiplayer component to the game, but I've not played that. I've not really felt the need to as of yet.

I'm not sure what the replayability will be like with this game. I have to admit, I doubt I'd play it again on a harder difficulty (Hurt Me Plenty is my advised chosen difficulty for optimal 'fun' and challenge). Despite this, my first run through has been very fun.

I'd rate this a 9/10. If any DLC is created for this game, I'll be coming back to it.\t\t\t\t\t
