\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo Far, So Awesome.

I first played DOOM 2016 on an XB1. A damn fine reboot of the 1993 genre defining classic. Though the verticality and light platforming feel a bit more akin to 1996's Quake, I welcome the mix with some modern touches. "What modern touches?", you may ask. Well, we have an RPG-like upgrading system for weapons, armor, and abilities. Said upgrades aren't quite linear and require you to consider which weapon mods and abilities you wish to employ before committing. Stages are littered with hidden goodies, which entices you to explore and even revisit stages. The action is as fast and frenetic as ever. Enemy types are varied in appearance, movement, and attacks. The 2016 reboot of DOOM feels like what DOOM should be by 2016 standards and even surpasses said standards.
