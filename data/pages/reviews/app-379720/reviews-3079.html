And its fun, exilerating, liberating. An excellent core game without distractions of cinematic pretentions to cover a lackluster core anyone would normally expect of the expensive shooter title.
Ok then, nitpicks: boss encounter design is as can be expected bad (it forgoes all the movement stuff, preferring a flat arena) and the game runs out of new enemy types too soon. Also after the opening it does tend to have characters talking story stuff without doomguy able to ignore them which kinda disappointed me after the strong statement that was made at the start. And im not convinced of their preferred anti aliasing option (fine most of the time, but in certain circumstances, near the Samual Hayden character or near VEGA's core, it can go from fuzzy all the way to a kind of blurry). Oh and there are also a few bugs that popped up at some really awkward moments.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fan of the series, one who didn't much care for the original Doom, this review isn't negative because of the change of design. Contrary to a lot of the complaints I loved the game even if it felt lacking at times. As many have described it, DOOM is a mixture of Old School and New School design, intertwined to make a hell of a package. One though I wouldn't recommend for anything over $35.

The reason I am giving this game a negative review is because of Denuvo. Not its inclusion, but rather what it did to my game and my computer. I wasn't happy with Denuvo at first, it was obvious that whenever it was doing its thing as the frame rate would plummet, but thankfully it never does whatever it does during combat. The game always ran smoothly during the most intense moments, would at points with previously no problem just nuke the frame rate for a short bit of time before returning to playable status.

It was an annoyance, but one I was willing to overlook in a positive review, where I planned to recommend the game if you could secure it for a reasonable fee. That was until it crashed my computer, but not just any black screen or even a blue screen, but a System Memory Dump Error. After checking everything else, and in a panic backing up all my files, I have determined all my hardware, registry, and software is working perfectly. Without a single shred of doubt I can say Denuvo nuked my computer. Worse because it screwed up, it corrupted my entire save undoing hours of exploration, fighting, and progression.

As a result I cannot recommend this game. For the simple fact that if it can happen to me it can happen to someone else and that automatically makes it not worth anything.

Outside Denuvo as a game DOOM is okay. The combat is visceral, intense and comes with an old school vibe with no reloads, health pickups, and the ability to carry every weapon and switch between them. Yet as fun as the combat was, it felt lacking. Never reaching the heights it felt it was capable of, always over just as it feels it is beginning with hoards that feel way to small.

Visually the game is gorgeous, weapons, lighting, everything you could want to look good does. Gore is galore and the meaty bits and blood coat the environment leaving a spectacle to mark your most epic encounters.

Lore and the story is well crafted, not just for a Doom game , but for any game. All that said I wouldn't recommend this game for anything over $35, if you can overlook Denuvo that is. The game has a competent single player, but it's length is not there. Of all my time spent with the game 80% was on exploration and finding secrets, powerups, collectibles, and unlocking the original levels. Ignoring all that, the game would probably clock in close to 4-5 hours in length.

There is a multiplayer mode that you will either ignore, or give a chance before moving back to your preferred multiplayer game. Overall without Denuvo as a consideration I would recommend this game at a reason a price. It does a lot of things well, looks good, and while never reaching true intensity with the combat, the combat isn't anything to dismiss either.

Doom 3 really let me down all those years ago, but considering how strong the last 2 Wolfenstein releases were, I gave it a shot. I'm so glad I did. I can't imagine a better translation of what made the classic Doom games fun into a modern gorgeous shooter. Well done! It is just a blast to play and I don't get tired of it.

The action forward mechanics push you to play hard. If you hold back, you won't be getting the health or ammo you need. The secrets and gear leveling add a nice touch to dial in the approaches you take in mowing down demons. Doom has finally arrived in the modern era of shooters and it is a gloriously fun bloodbath.\t\t\t\t\t
