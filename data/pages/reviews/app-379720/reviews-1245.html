\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent game. Gunplay is smooth with no reloading and easy weapon swapping, with fairly bountiful ammo. Game is challenging on higher difficulties, but not untowardly so. Boss fights are exceptionally hard, but unfortunately can become fairly trivial with strategic use of the BFG, the most powerful weapon in the game. It's balanced-ish with having very scarce ammunition, but it isn't difficult to save up ammo for boss fights, and you're typically granted one or two rounds immediately before.
Platforming is mostly good, though some sections are rather ill polished and don't behave as expected. Level design is also good on a majority of levels, however there are some levels that push against the 'metroidvania-esque backtracking to find secrets' design that's taught to the player early on, and it can be frustrating being at the end of a level, looking over the map and seeing a secret area you missed around 60% of the way through that you have been locked out of, requiring you to replay the mission again. This isn't a problem for everyone, and I don't mind backtracking nor do I dislike metroidvanias. But it feels unintuitive and hampering that in a game that for the most part encourages backtracking to find secrets, that there are a few levels where this is literally impossible, seemingly intentionally so since many of these levels in question feature simple doors that permanently lock for a fight once passed through, when they could easily be reopened upon winning the battle.
The story is interesting if you read through all the lore, though it's not the main draw of the game. It's also rather difficult to pin exactly where and how this game fits in the overall Doom canon, if such a thing exists.

There are several game breaking bugs in the game that still haven't been addressed as of June 2020. Most are documented in the Steam discussions of the game via various complaints, most of which require rebooting the game or restarting the level from the beginning in order to 'fix'. There are one or two bugs that are much more game breaking and can actually erase your Steam account's progress, so it's probably a good idea to verse yourself on the bugs and figuring out how to address them or how NOT to replicate them for a smooth experience. The story is so straightforward that there isn't much reason to be concerned about running into spoilers while looking through bug fixes.

TLDR: Good game, some level design flaws and no longer receiving support for critical bug fixes.\t\t\t\t\t

-Satisfying combat
-Fun & straight-to-the-point weapon upgrading
