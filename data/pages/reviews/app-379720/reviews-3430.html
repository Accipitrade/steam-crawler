- The difficulty.
It's as challenging as you'd expect from a doom game. Hard if you want a challenge, easy flying if you wanna just chill and kill demons. My first playthrough was on Ultra-Violence, i'm not bad at fps games at all, and i still struggled in most parts. Now to finish Nightmare difficulty...

All rebindable, 95% fluid movement most of the time

Cons:

- No option to remove mouse smoothing (yet)
Had to turn it off through config editing

- The ability to grab onto ledges can be a teeny bit dodgy some times.
Hence why i said 95% fluid movement. Occasionally you won't auto-grab onto things when you'd think you should, or sometimes you'll do the grab-and-pull-up animation but the game won't put you up onto the ledge you'll still fall. 1/50 chance of that happening to me, in my experience.

Only in the specific parts few parts where tonnes of enemies spawn, do you finally feel even a teeny bit overwhelmed. Whilst just navigating levels, the lack of monster placement sucked. I expect to open doors to a room packed to the brim on nightmare mode. Not just a few monsters that deal absurd damage and can be dodged ezpz. There are many wide open spaces for example, filled with a mere few normal zombies... shame

- The level design again
If you were looking for a style similar to the original doom games, well they got it 75% of the time
It's almost as if certain maps have been stuck through a quake/unreal arena generator because of all the verticality, but i guess they're just making use of the fact that we can aim up and down now... right?

Baron's of Hell: yeah even they look weird despite still being the same. Something about them is just off, i think they're shorter/wider than their tall pixel versions.
Cacodemons: A mix of the original version and doom 3's, red balls of death now with arms and an empty ballsack dangling from them that just looks weird.
