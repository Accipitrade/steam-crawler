The graphics are fantastic and the framerate is buttery. Killing things feels empowering and no enemy dies with a whimper. Even multiplayer feels accessible. DOOM stands out in a lanscape littered with CoD wanna-bes because of how few frills are attached to the ridiculously enjoyable core experience. This is a modern take on the most old school of shooters, ditching inventory management and reload times in favor of balls-to-the-wall pacing and shooting all the guns. DOOM is a great lesson in gameplay design for studios like DICE, Treyarch, and Bungie: simple is best. If you are going to spend years developing a game, make sure your resources go into making your time in-game feel snappy, exciting, constantly on the move, never grindy or notably reptitive.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo after playing the game for 3 times (on my fourth and planning on playing it again) i realize that i should probably write a review on this game. I was looking through the negative reviews and found myself frustrated that people overlooked the amazing qualities in this game. First up is the graphics, although a game can be fun without good graphics, visually intruiging games can improve the experience and i have got to say this game is beutiful. Playing it in 2018 it still looks and feels like a new game with the graphics still on par with many of the triple A games out now. It is realistic with a subtle comic book and cartoon artstyle which is very enjoyable to look at.

Now on to gameplay! This game is the most fun i have ever had playing by my self. It gets right to the point
and throws you into multiple areas specifically designed to be an arena for killing. The arenas have a great sense of scale and offer more than just horizontal elements. They are designed perfectly to allow you to manuver flawlessly around the map, jumping up to a platform just to jump back down and smash a demons head in. This being a great segway into the gore. If you like gore you will love this game. The glory kill system is original and satisfying till the very end. Depending on where you look at the demon a different kill animation will be performed and i believe that there is enough variety to maintain that satisfying and brutal feel throughout.
Another thing i have to give props to are the demons. Each demon looks and feels different and has a unique outline that allows you to instantly identify them and act accordingly. The Ai is amazing and makes the demons actually feel threatening. They can climb on walls, jump and dodge and even flank you in a coordinated fashion. Again each demon feels unique with there own attacks and animations that make it fun and visually pleasing. Talking about variety is the guns, each gun feels completly unique and each has there own 2 different add ons that further increase there diversity. For instance the compat shotgun offers an adon that charges up a 3 round burst or one that can shoot a grenade that deals massive damage to a group of enemies. Did i mention that you can switch between addons swiftly and flawlessly with a click of a button? it makes the experience completly smooth as does the weapon wheel mechanic which when holding in the dedicated button slows down time and allows you to seemingly instantly switch between weapons in the heat of battle. Each gun works better against some demons with the larger slower ones being more immune to the rocket launcher and the faster more agile ones more weak to up close shotgun shots or far away machine gun shots.
The game sounds great and makes the gore feel even more brutal with the sound of bones crunching and blood filling the floors. The sound track is amazing and totally works with every battle you go through. It creates adreneline and almost directly increases your amount of testosterone transforming you into a real man.
The story is simple but i found myself intruiged thoroughly. The down times of the game are short and are very welcome considering the amount of secrets and collectables littered about the map for you to find post battle. You switch from hell back to mars and into an industrial setting. It feels diverse and changes throughout the game.
I can't recommend this game enough and if this helps atleast one person decide if they want the game or not then i think i acomplished my goal. Trust me if you like what you heard you will love this game. It has so much re-play value and i have found myself playing it for a fourth time.
With nothing else to say thank you for reading my review and RIP AND TEAR!\t\t\t\t\t
