\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDear readers,

This game is quite enjoyable. Fairly entertaining, I might say. A return to arena shooters, even if it is pretty much just in single-player is great, and it is nice to see the genre seeing something new. I hope this trend continues with more games (Quake 5??).

The gameplay in this game is really good, and that is what this game is really about. The story is just motivation to rip and tear, but that doesn't make it bad. It's no Bioshock, and that's a good thing. Combat is fast paced, with a wide variety of weapons that you have available at all times. It is quite difficult on higher difficulty settings, and is skill based.

The graphics are very good, especially for 2016. This game will also run on a potato, no problems with system requirements here. ID is always pushing the boundaries on graphics tech and it shows.

My only issues with this game is changing weapons can feel unintuitive as in the heat of the moment I end up scrolling through all the guns to just get the one I am looking for, but that's probably just a skill issue. I haven't played the multiplayer, but I have heard that it is not really that good, so do not go looking for multiplayer experience here, just pick up Quake III Arena for an online arena shooter experience (R.I.P. Unreal Tournament, you will be missed).

Gameplay: 9/10
Graphics: 9/10
Performance: 10/10
Story: 5/10
Multiplayer: 2/10
Linux Support: Platinum (Proton)

Overall, a must play\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is not a sequel to Doom 3, it is more of a sequel to Doom 2, if that makes sense. To me, Doom 3 was a survival horror game that was a slow moving experience and you barely scraping by the demons of hell and the biggest badasses of Mars. This was a disconnect for me and this is why I love Doom 2016, it fulfilled my fantasy for what Doom 1&2 were. You are not lone Marine that is struggling to fight back the demons of hell, you are an icon of rage and a symbol of terror to the demons. You are not slowly peeking around corners and carefully approaching combat scenarios, you play like an enraged, adrenaline junkie and the game rewards you for playing like that as well. The more you kill, the faster you move, the more aggressive you play the better off you are. Dooms pacing matches that of the player, making it clear from the get go what the protagonists intentions are with simple actions that don't clutter the gameplay. The weapons are varied and extremely fun to experiment with and I'm sure you will have a difficult time finding a favorite firearm among the armory of satisfying weapons, unless you are a double barrel purist and if that's you, I salute you. The game makes an amazing decision of implementing the mechanic that rewards you with ammo when you kill something with a chainsaw and rewards you health and armor if you brutally "glory" kill something, this makes the game a constant fast-paced blessing that I really can't get enough of. If you were dissapointed by Doom 3 and that's why you're hesitant about picking up this game, I want to assure you that this will not dissapoint.\t\t\t\t\t
