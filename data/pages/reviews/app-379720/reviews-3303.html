\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPeople like lists right? Well then here it goes...

NOTICE: This list is entirely based on the singleplayer campaign. Everyone already knows that the multiplayer is garbage.

  • Platform: Windows 7 Pro 64-bit, Intel Core i5-4670K @ 3.4GHz(x4CPUs), 8GB RAM, NVidia GeForce GTX 760 4037MB DirectX 11, game installed on traditional HDD
  • Display: AOC G2460F 144Hz @ 1920x1080
  • Controller: Logitech G100s + some old PS/2 port keyboard
------------------------------------------------

1. Features that function as expected:

General
  • No stylized aesthetic to mess with the visuals
  • Demon types returning from earlier games are recognizable
  • Mars looks like Mars

Options and performance
  • Fully rebindable controls
  • Full audio settings with 0-100% separate sliders for different audio
  • Desktop PC setup FOV setting that goes from 90° to 130°
  • Full mouse support, no acceleration
  • Steady 60 FPS performance
  • Supports higher framerates without issues
  • Screen space effects such as grain, blur and chromatic aberration can be disabled

Gameplay
  • Weapon wheel switching is optional, every weapon can be bound to its own key
  • Tutorials, handholding and highlights are all optional
  • Automatic item pickups (no interaction prompt)
  • Distinct sounds for different demons that help you anticipate what is coming
  • All of the weapon upgrades are useful in certain situations
  • Demons sometimes fight eachother (and it isn't scripted like some say)
  • Story and exposition are secondary to combat gameplay
  • No fall damage. Only instant death from obviously lethal terminal velocity falls
  • Sufficiently long singleplayer campaign (Longer than Doom 1 or 2) -about 10 hours with some exploration
  • No ingame minimap (Realtime minimap has adverse effect on spatial awareness)
  • No character development. Doomguy just wants to kill demons because he hates them so go**amn much.
  • Animated sequences such as melee attacks are nicely varied and sufficiently short to not interrupt the flow of gameplay

------------------------------------------------

2. Aspects that fail to meet expectations

General
  • Some demons' visual designs are uninspired, for example HellKnight and Cyberdemon
  • Upgraded double-barrel shotgun fires two barrels twice (stupid) instead of getting an extra pair of barrels (stupidly cool)
  • Hell looks a bit too comfy compared to Doom 3
  • SPOILER: Demons got cybernetics from the UAC (I just think it's stupid, even for Doom)
  • Original colored and detailed logo is better than new simplistic monochrome logo
  • "SnapMap" -level creation tool is FUBAR, totally ruined by the arbitrary limitations in the irredeemably awful Multiplayer. Absolutely useless. This needs to be fixed.

Options and performance
  • Occasional temporary freezing when (assuming) the game is streaming assets
  • One level often ends with the game hanging in a black screen indefinitely (probably will be fixed in a patch)
  • Level specific performance drops for no apparent reason
  • Opening movies can't be skipped
  • There is an option for inverted vertical mouse movement even though very few people prefer it, but there isn't an option to hold "Crouch" for those who prefer that over toggle
  • No lower FOV options for those with a Couch+TV gaming setup that requires a more narrow viewing angle (Who would have thought this problem could be reversed like this one day?)

Gameplay
  • Difficulty levels are just lazy adjustments of enemy health and damage instead of changing the amount of enemies you encounter
  • (The few and far between) cutscenes cannot be skipped
  • Inconsistent difficulty or tediousness of upgrade- and level challenges
  • Pointless optional suit upgrades with underwhelming passive effects
  • Overpowered "runes" that trivialize resource conservation
  • Contextual chainsaw with overpowered rewards of use (massive ammo loot drop)
  • BFG 9000 doesn't need to charge up before firing, making it a bit less satisfying to use
  • Berserk powerup is overpowered, kills everything in one hit
  • UAC holograms endlessly repeating their pre-recorded monologue with no option to punch them
  • Exploration occasionally denied by unexpected points of no return
  • Automap that reveals the whole map is found in every level and isn't even hidden
  • Optional exposition and lore is in clumsy text format, whereas a lot of it in Doom 3 was audiologs that you could play during uninterrupted gameplay
  • NewGame+ is the only new game. Only way to start totally over is to switch save profile. Speaking of that...
  • ...only 3 save profiles, no regular save slots
  • No difficulty option for "Pistol restart from death" -even though weapons are found in different levels just like previous Doom games

------------------------------------------------

3. Aspects that exceed expectations

General
  • Badass industrial metal soundtrack with decent throwbacks to Bobby Prince's original Doom tracks that works in a good semi-dynamic music system
  • Nice and powerful weapon sounds

Options and performance
  • Amazing realtime screen-space reflections even in complex geometry with supernaturally little performance impact
  • Options have tooltip explanations
  • Built-in performance metrics
  • HUD can be entirely disabled, including the crosshair
  • Borderless Windowed mode and primary monitor selection
  • The ability to enable mouse smoothing or acceleration doesn't even exist, thank goodness

Gameplay
  • Extremely smooth controls and movement mechanics
  • Fast-paced action with constant vertical movement and weapon switching is encouraged
  • No hitscan enemies. All damage can be avoided with skill
  • Nonlinear sprawling level design on par with Doom 1, with extra elements of vertical exploration
  • Clever placement of secret areas that reward all forms of exploration
  • Challenging, varied boss fights with multiple different and satisfyingly unpredictable stages
  • All returning weapons feel good to use and serve a purpose. Same goes for all added new weapons
  • Enemy variety is good and they have decent AI with distinct behavior and so far absolutely flawless pathfinding
  • Very clear and easy to use 3d map screen

TL;DR
It's a great shooter. Ignore what 4.c.h.a.n. says. Score: Great/10. Now go away and learn to be patient enough to read bullet point lists, you goldfish attention span waste of skin.\t\t\t\t\t
