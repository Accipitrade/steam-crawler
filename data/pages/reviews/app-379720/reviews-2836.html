\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Doom has been a a legendary series since its inception. The series pretty much created the first person shooter (well, Wolfenstein 3D came first, but it wasn'tas big, and had the same developers anyway). And today, when the first person shooter has become an endless cycle of regenerating health behind cover before you can shoot anything again, what are we to do? This new Doom has the answer: we use rocket launchers with three-round-burst-lock-on, we use chainsaws to cut our enemies to pieces, we circle strafe with exploseve-shot shotguns, or we use our fists to turn everything into a bloody mess. I've been playing shooters my entire life and nothing has ever come close to the sheer gruesome glory this game instills in me. Nothing is as satisfying as ripping and tearing through horde upon horde of Hell's demons in this game. It's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ wonderful. I've been waiting for a game like this for a long time.
"Are you only going to say positive things about the game?" Well no, it's not perfect, and as much as I love it, there are still problems. There are only three bosses in this game. I won't say what they are, because of spoilers, of course, but still, three isn't a big number, no matter how fun the fights were. The Vulkan Graphics API is incredible, giving my RX 470 performance I didn't know it could have - when it works. For a long time I couldn't even get the game to launch, until I added pretty much every launch option on the PCGamingWiki page for this game. Even then, the game would often freeze for around 10 seconds, I'd say around once an hour, however I feel like this is either because of my Pentium G3258 or my 5400RPM hard drive limiting the game, because I couldn't find anyone else with the same issue. Another small complaint - this game is an 80GB download. That's a lot of space. I don't really care much if the end result is this good, but still. 80GB is huge. Also, ignore the multiplayer, it's pretty worthless. Campaign is magnificent, however.
In summary, Doom is a game that allows you to slaughter huge numbers of things like few other games I have played. The campaign is absolutely wonderful, while multiplayer and snapmap are forgettable. Even though it has a few small issues, I would recommend this game over most of the other ones I've played - it's excellent. I got it on sale for under $20, which is basically robbery for an experience as good as this one. You should play this, everyone should play this.\t\t\t\t\t
