\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely one of the most fun campaigns I've played in a long time. Non-stop action all throughout. Ran into some minor bugs on the Vulkan renderer. If I hugged certain walls, occlusion culling would freak out and not render the level. Occasionally, collision detection would freak out on glory kills. Once, I glory killed a monster and was forced into a shipping container with no way out. Another time, I even fell through the map. These bugs were very rare, however. I could count the number of times these happened on one hand.

The RPG elements were a welcome addition for me, I had a lot of fun getting 100% in this game. Though in some instances, it distracted from the simple gameplay. I found myself reloading certain checkpoints a lot to farm for certain challenges to upgrade my weapons or runes, because it was more expedient to get them against hordes of possessed than actually go for them during normal gameplay. I spent a lot of time at the start of Advanced Research Complex getting my gear up to 100%.

Towards the end of the game on Ultra-Violence, the upgrades also ruined the challenge. I combined the runes In-Flight Mobility, Equipment Power, and Rich Get Richer and it made the game a sleepwalk. In-Flight Mobility can't be lived without once you get used to it (it probably should have been a base feature), but Equipment Power makes siphon grenades ridiculous. It was trivial to maintain max armor at almost all times, in turn meaning I could spam siege mode on the Gauss cannon with impunity, essentially neutering every challenge the game could throw at me. The final level and boss should've been the most intense mission in the game, but because I'm a completion and I already had maxed out my upgrades, it wound up being one of the easiest. Looking back, I much prefered when I wasn't fully upgraded where every encounter was a mobile, dynamic, visceral, intense fight for survival. I'd almost advise not using Rich Get Richer at all because the game is just so much more fun when you're managing your ammo and constantly swapping weapons, at least if you're already a seasoned FPS player and are playing on Ultra-Violence. I can't speak to Nightmare.

A lot of people say that FPS platforming doesn't work. I'd say Doom 4 proves that wrong. It was some of the most natural platforming I've ever seen in an FPS. It was great in between fights as pacing, and during fights it's an invaluable tool that must be mastered. It felt very natural climbing up ledges and double jumping to evade enemies, and the designers including so much verticality in their arenas helped the fights feel much more dynamic.

The story was both exceptional and minimal. The game wastes almost no time doing the business of the plot (with one exception of an annoying cutscene halfway through), and when it is exploring the plot they use the opportunity to add great characterization to Doomguy. His little actions during these scenes speak volumes about his character without saying a word, and they actually create a very visible and palpable dynamic between Hayden and Doomguy. There's tons of lore and backstory to read too, and that's always fun. It's also totally optional, so if you just don't care you can ignore it entirely. It simultaneously feels like a return to classic game development where the story only existed to get you to the gameplay as fast as possible, but they still made a very interesting narrative all the same. With the plot, they got to have their cake and eat it, too. They don't waste your time with the plot, but they also don't squander their opportunity to make it engaging with visual storytelling.

Very highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, I don't really play any singleplayer First Person Shooter stuff other than the two CoD campaigns I played. But DOOM really surprised me. If you don't get this game, you're practically committing a crime.

The gameplay is fantastic and there's pretty much nothing more satisfying than taking on a horde of demons and blowing them to bits. Enemie types are extremely varied and it actually gives you a reason to use different weapons. On top of this base gameplay, there is also a sort of "character building" esque feel. You can upgrade a bunch of various things about your character.

Perhaps the strongest point of this game though, is the level design. The levels are fantastic. They're each the perfect length, designed masterfully and truly open. While the game is a level based game, the levels themselves aren't entirely linear and provide plenty of collecting and exploration if you're into that kind of thing.

Overall, the game is great and extremely fun. Totally recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
