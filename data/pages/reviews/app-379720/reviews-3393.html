\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is Doom, through and through. If you were a fan of Doom 1 and 2, you'll enjoy this thoroughly.

There's a wealth of options to turn off any of the new additions you don't fancy; I myself toggled off the melee finisher flash, any waypoint icons, the "radar", HUD notifications for objectives and lore pickups, and finally I also toggled off cross hairs. Mission stages are expansive, have plenty of hidden secrets, and tons of enemies to kill. Weapon, armor, and rune upgrades are fun to use and if you really don't want anything to do with them you can just ignore them entirely aside from one mandatory armor upgrade pickup early in the game. I highly recommend using at least weapon modifications so you gain full use of each weapon's capabilities though, it's just more damn fun with each weapon's alternate fire available.

There honestly isn't much I could ask to be added or fixed. I've been playing Doom since Knee Deep's shareware and I happily can say the current team at iD knocked it out of the park. Took about 14 hours or so to complete my first playthrough on Hurt Me Plenty and I can't wait to go back in on Ultra Violence.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game doesn't have multiplayer....just keep telling yourself that and everything will be fine.

I was so hyped for this game until the open beta dropped and so did my hope. However, the single player completely lives up to what you would expect from this game. Within the first 5 minutes, the game establishes what it's all about:

Free yourself from a table, pick up a gun, start killing. Boom, intro done! And just in case you didn't take the hint, when someone starts trying to give you some story, Doom Guy rips the monitor off its platform and throws it across the room. You aren't here for story and neither is your character. This game is about one thing: kicking gratuitous amounts of ass!

If you played Doom in your younger days, this game is going to hit every nostalgia nerve in your body in the best way possible. The music, the guns, the gore, the pacing....it's all there. I absolutely love games with a great story to them but it's strangely refreshing that Doom says f*#k all of that and only concerns itself with the gameplay.

This game knows what it is and sticks unapologetically to it (so far). I've had to force myself to stop playing for now because I really want to savor this. It's rare for a game this exciting to come along!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is DOOM. Like, this is legit DOOM. You hit Enter, you load in, you get a gun within the first minute, and a shotgun within the the next 3 and that E1M1 music hit, and you're off and running.

This game is going to live and be remembered by it's single player.
The gunplay is vicious, every thing has a kick, everything feels meaty. The most of the mods for the weapons really do change up how you approach every encounter.

The maps feel like DOOM. Labyrinthine levels with lots of secrets and multiple floors. This really does look like a natural progression of what DOOM would look like brought into 2016.

Keep an ear out for the UAC audio diaries too, because those are hilarious. DOOM knows it's not about having a serious story. So the audio diaries are always in super try hard mode. Meanwhile Doom Guy gives no ♥♥♥♥♥.

DOOM had the potential to be a great game, or an utter disappointment, especially after the MP beta. But I really think id nailed it. Well done.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOTE: This review mostly pertains to Snapmap. The campaign is good (if a touch short and in need of some more monster variety).

This is a tough one for me. Doom was so hyped and I was so extremely excited to get it. Got it, played it through (on easy first, to see content, next HMP and then UV) and...while it all flows wonderfully, looks great and was fun - I was left feeling like it was half done.

The stupid cliffhanger ending combined with a final boss and achievement that basically knows it's over too soon, the surprising lack of enemy variety (where are my arch viles? pain elementals? cyberdemons that aren't just one set piece boss fight?) left me wanting more.

I was sure that Snapmap was going to solve it. My love of mapmaking in Build engine games when I was younger gave me dreams of lovingly crafing awesome survival modes, crazy multiplayer maps and mini campaigns.

No dice. Even on single player maps - two weapon loadouts, no alt fire, no upgrading, only very basic UAD tilesets (no hell, no outdoor areas at all, just a few hallways and rooms) etc.

Overall as much as I want to love and recommend this, the pricetag is just to hefty. Wait for DLC or Snapmap to be improved. Right now - you get a decent, but flawed 6-10 hour singleplayer campaign and the standard shooty multiplayer. Forget about Snapmap, it's more restrictive than Mario Maker.

I wish I could review Snapmap and DOOM seperately, but while the core campaign is mostly solid, a big reason I bought this was Snapmap. And...to be brutally honest, Snapmap is a travesty.\t\t\t\t\t
