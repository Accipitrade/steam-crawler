\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cant believe how well this game turned out and I can't recomend getting it enough. For those tired of the hum drum military fps that have been the standard in an over saturated market for quiet awhile DOOM comes in tand offers a fesh and overall incredibly fun experience with a lengthy singlepalyer campaign that throws you into the fray and has you killing legions of hell within ten seconds of starting up the game. The combat is extremely fluid, brutal and fast paced with weapons that feel right with one exception being the combat shotgun which is a bit underwhelming. There are secrets hidden throughout the lvels such as item dumps like health and ammo and collectibles. Cutscenes are few and far between and the game handles them like half lifes interactive custcenes where you can still look and sometimes walk around while a character dumps exposition, luckily these only happen about 5 times throughout the game. The music is also incredible and as I was dissapointed at first that it wasnt complete metal however it is a wonderful mix of industrial sounds mixed with guitar riffs and drums that goes perfectly with killing demons in 2016. I dont really care about the multiplayer or snapmap but to me this game is worth it just for the singleplayer alone which is what DOOM is known for. I give it 9 colored keycards out of 10 dismembered torsos. The game also runs great on a gtx 970 with everything maxed at 1080p, hovering between 70 and 110 fps.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is back baby! I can't believe id pulled through with this one. Solid gunplay thanks to modding capabilities of the guns, melee isn't so bad as I initialy thought, the level design is just superb, blows everything out of the water, the monsters the sounds oh god. I just can't believe it I actually lived long enough to see Doom come back and better than ever. The way the game was built feels like the natural next step of the franchise. It's built in the sense that you can do non-stop action without ever breaking combat (that's the point of the glory kills) and it feels great. Even the way doom guy interacts with certain story elements is just great, hehe. What a blast this was, I will surely go for multiple playthroughs of the game since they offer a ton of collectibles, hidden areas, upgrades, runes, modules for your suit etc. It brings a tear to my eye playing this game. But enough about what I feel, I could drag this revew for pages and pages let me break it down and tell you what Doom 2016 is going to give you
    The Good:
  1. Fast movement
  2. Solid gunplay, a large arsenal with kick-ass customization for guns
  3. Fluid animations across the board
  4. Jaw-dropping graphics. iD's engine combined with some guys from Crysis, this game really steals the show when it comes to graphics (so far)
  5. Expansive levels with plenty of exploration and space to fight in
  6. Plenty of monsters on screen at one time
  7. Collectibles, logs, expanding on the lore of the game (it's optional but nice to have it)
  8. Long campaign, includes the classic doom levels, snap-map, multiplayer. There's just a lot of Doom to be enjoyed here.
  9. A love letter to fans of the series, revitalized, bad-ass and metal.
  10. Awesome sound design
    The Bad:
  1. Snap-map is limited to prefabricated rooms, only 4 players can join a lobby.
  2. No modding capabilities (hopefully we will get mods in the near future)
  3. The whole DLC/Season Pass thing seems redundant (since they gave us SNAP-MAP to build our own maps), they are also segregating the online community.
  4. Not really a bad thing, but I was hoping to see some more classic monsters returned to this one (arachnotron, pain elemental etc)
  5. Multiplayer (while not developed by id) is simplified and casualised. No depth to the movement, loadouts are a no-no for me and overall it just sucks compared to the level of polish the singleplayer received(which is good)
It's the current year folks and we got a game from this legendary developer,id software. The kings are back, and they made sure we will never forget who makes the best fps games. Thanks id, for the passion that went into this, thanks for rebooting that horrible pre-Doom that was in production (before this one), thanks for living up to such a monumental amount of hype (let's not forget it's been 12 years without any Doom). This game blew all my expectations and I'm so happy just writing this review, I'm grining like a child, I haven't had fun with a singleplayer campaign like I had with this one in such a long time, I just can't wait to dive right back into it. Anyway, I better wrap this up, I can't help it, I fell in love with this game from day one, I purposely upgraded my gpu only to run Doom on ultra. That's how much I anticipated this. Hands down my personal GOTY already. There's nothing else I could ask from the industry this year, I have Doom thanks. I'll give it a 9.5/10 on the singleplayer while the base multiplayer mode gets a 7/10. A must own, a must play and probably one of the best games to come in the last 5 years easily. We got Wolfenstein rebooted, Doom, Quake when? Only time will tell.\t\t\t\t\t
