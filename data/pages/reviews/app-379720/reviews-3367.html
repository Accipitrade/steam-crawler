I have to be honest, I'm starting to hate this game. (In a good way. I'm getting my butt kicked.)

I have never take taken the time to review a game but this one has totally set me off. If I had to describe the game in one word it would be "ridiculous." When you are in combat you really can NEVER stop moving or you will die. Every other FPS allows you to duck behind cover and shoot to avoid damage. If you try sit behind cover, with the exception of being in the next room, you will die very quickly. It's just not realistic. (No rest for the weary!)

Furthermore, the checkpoint system is totally frustrating. Somtimes the checkpoint system makes you start a good ways back from the mob fight you just died in, so you have to spend 30 to 60 seconds to get back to where you were. (This is one of the ACTUAL frustrations I have with the game. Could have done a better job with this)

I know we are supposed to suspend disbelief but this whole health point things spilling out of the dead bodies is again absurd. It just doesn't let the gamer get immersed in the game because when does this type of thing ever happen? I could understand an occaisional enemy dropping a first aid kit but I just can't seem to get past the little blue pellets everywhere.

Edit:
So after playing for about 12 hours now, the game really is enjoyable, with some minor frustrations mainly revolving around the checkpoint system. Overall, well done.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHell Yes.
Now updated due to attaining a new graphics card.

Pros:
General
-Looks and Feels Amazing (even on low settings, in my opinion).
-The violence in this game is fun, not necessarily dark or gritty. Like Mortal Kombat!
-Weapons that have their own distinct "Oomph!" to them. The upgrades are cool, too.
-Mick Gordon's (who also did music for Killer Instinct and the latest Wolfenstein games) hard-hitting soundtrack, to which you can bang your head to whilst banging demons' heads on walls or with their own limbs.

Campaign
-The Demons aren't ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. They look awesome, intimidating, and don't EVER let up. Unless, of course, you're on "I'm Too Young To Die" mode.
-Story is kept to a minimum, and whatever exposition is shown to you can sometimes be played through instead of just strapping you there to watch in boredom. Otherwise, it's all there when you press the Tab button and go over to the database.

Multiplayer
-Multiplayer will kick your ass if you're not careful.
-But multiplayer is so fast-paced, so who the ♥♥♥♥ even has time to be careful? All you gotta do is kill anyone in your way.
-Getting the Power Weapons, Power-Ups (i.e. Invisibility, Quad Damage), or Demon Rune results in quite a bit of carnage.
-Taunts are very quick, but humorous.

SnapMap
-You can basically do anything you want, including naming a Hell Knight "Bob the Gate Keeper".
-Side Con: You can't see the name when you play the level.
-There's a whole selection of other people's levels that you can play on so that you can get inspiration.

Cons:
-Some glitches are bound to happen in new releases. That's including at the very end when you fight the last boss and it doesn't die even when its health has run out.
-Being on the receiving end of the Demon Rune or Power Weapons. Same goes for people with Quad Damage or Invisibility.
-The developers considered having players be able to put the Cyberdemon in their SnapMaps, but they axed that idea. Also, the SnapMaps can only have so much stuff on them.

666/666 would slit a Pinky's throat with his own tusk again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen your main complaint is that the campaign is too short, you've arrived at a great problem to have. Yes, the main game can be completed in about 10 hours, give or take. There are a fair amount of secrets and hidden supplies that are fun to hunt and can extend the game a bit, but not by much. The story? There is some, and it's not that bad. It's quite good actually. I won't spoil anything here, but the long and the short of it is this; you are a man trapped and released on Mars during a demonic invasion from Hell itself, and you are tasked with going in Hell to stop the hordes. A few NPC's will help you along the way or will try and stop you, but don't expect cutscenes and long-winded dialouge, as you get in the action very quickly indeed. The action and combat is phenominal. The guns feel heavy and chunky, they kick and rear like a real gun should. They impact and do damage in a very satifying way which makes them incredibly fun to play with, especially the larger weapons. Guns, however, aren't the only weapons at your disposal as your fists are just as potent and deadly. When you hurt monsters down to lower health, you have the ability to perfrom a "Glory Kill" with your hands. You rip and tear and crush demons in several quick and tidy, and yet gruesome way. The melee attacks are fluid and fast so you can keep up your movement and momentum, which is important in heavy combat. The selection of monsters will be familar with DOOM veterans and they look and fight as they should. The design of the demons is top-notch and they look and sound like the hordes from Hell should.

Long story short: Buy this game. It is a top-class shooter and is a Hell of a ride, albiet a short one.\t\t\t\t\t
