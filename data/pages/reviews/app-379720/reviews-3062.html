\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start off with this: Doom is a mess...a big bloody mess. This game is all about killing monsters with big guns. If you're looking for a story driven, cinematic shooter, look elsewhere. Doom is a super intense and fast paced FPS full of bloody and frenetic action from beginning to end.

Pros:
  • Fast paced gameplay; Keep moving or you die, simple as that
  • Large and nicely designed levels
  • Beautiful graphics; the game still looked good even after setting the video options to their lowest setting
  • Tons of secrets and collectibles
  • Your weapons arsenal is varied and fun to use
  • Sound design is mostly good except for some monster sounds which have inconsistent volume or cutoff abruptly when the monster dies. Overall, sound quality is pretty good
  • Lots of different enemies
  • Runs great on a variety of hardware
  • Almost* didn't see any bugs
  • Great soundtrack

Cons:
  • Lazy ending
  • Checkpoint system

*Bugs:
  • There's a bug that prevents level 5 from ending: had to lower my resolution to 720p to fix it, then changed it back to 1440p when the level finished)
  • The game takes a couple of minutes to start after a full pc shutdown

Overall, the game does a good job of channeling the spirit of classic Doom games and is a great game in and of itself. Any classic Doom fan should give it a try.

I haven't tried the multiplayer but I plan to in the near future.

Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's Doom. Like old school original Doom. Get a gun, go into a room, music comes on, kill kill kill kill. Fun. I remember playing the Beta of the original Doom way back in the day when the monsters were stationary but could still attack, I spent probably hundreds of hours with the original and DCK building levels. Doom and Doom2 are similar to this Doom. Doom3 was more of an exploration FPS with tunnels and corridors and hallways - this goes back to the original roots. There are still puzzles, keycards and other item hunting on the side - some classic levels here and there. But mostly it's about going into rooms, triggering a hoard of demons, killing them all, moving onto the boss level fights, killing them all, moving through the base and killing everything, moving through hell and killing everything..... fun fun fun - very frantic at times, yet good for stress relief

Details are great and amazing and if you remember back in the day, the original games, you'll see how far gaming has come over the years. I haven't tried any online or the built-in map maker yet, I did a full play through and went back for more at higher difficulties. Weapons and armor have upgrades to help you stay alive while killing more demons faster but it seems like no matter what upgrade path you choose, in the end you will upgrade everything the same way. A lot of great details in the graphics, even on my Evga 770FTW with 4gb on an i7 920 I can crank the details up pretty far and override the settings to display 2k and it runs smoothly and quick - a few stutters in large fights but that is expected.

I'd give it either 8 out of 10 or 9 out of 10, perhaps both. Hard to say right now since there are so many features I haven't yet messed with, no DLC, no online play, no map maker - so I'm sticking with an 8 for now. Great game for those that have been with Doom for years, probably a good game for those who never played before. Frantic action shooter survival game set on Mars and in Hell. Adds to the mystery and backstory from the earlier games and the movie that Dwayne was in. Sadly, nobody smells what he is cooking these days\t\t\t\t\t
