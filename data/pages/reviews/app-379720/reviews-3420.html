Is this DOOM 1 with an HD reskin? Nope. Does that mean this doesn't feel like DOOM? Absolutely not. From the strafing, to the exploring, to the power ups, to the music, this is DOOM. This is coming from someone who just replayed DOOM I, II, and III in the week leading up to this release. This is more original DOOM than DOOM 3.

This game has a lot of exploration and lot of wide open spaces that you can't pass until you complete certain objectives and find certain items (like in the original). If you liked the claustrophobic, survival horror, F.E.A.R. feel of DOOM 3, this is not what you're looking for. The campaign is fully fleshed out, with Ultra Nightmare difficulty living up to its name (you die = you start the whole campaign over, with saving only at the end of each level). SnapMap, which people have used to create anything from Harvest Moon in Doom to remaking the missions from the original games, is sure to give this game a long life span as long as the community keeps it alive.

tl;dr if you like strafey doom then buy, if you like locker-code jump-scare doom, hopefully you also like strafey doom\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game ended up being about as fun as I expected. So far, I've run into absolutely no issues running the game on a GTX970 at High settings; I even have the framerate to spare if I want to run on UItra. The single player is very fun, but nothing like the old school Doom you may be familar with. Think of it as a mix between Wolfenstein TNO and Doom 3. It isn't scary, but sure fun, and challenging if you want it to be. There is also quite a bit of depth (though not an unheard of amount) in the campaign. These include secrets, challenges, and various pickups. No alternate storyline, or side trips; it's as linear as Doom 3. Wish I could review the multiplayer and SnapMap, but I haven't played around enough with it. I highly recommend watching SnapMap tutorials first, as it doesn't appear to be very intuitive at first glance. If you have the patience for learning SnapMap and using it at it's fullest, grab it for $60. For singleplayer campaign only, $40 may be more fair if you are willing to wait for sales. Solid 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t
