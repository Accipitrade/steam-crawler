\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary:
It's amazing, and well worth your money. I did not play multiplayer.

Intro:
Another amazing title from iD (published by Bethesda) doing their franchise justice -- after being pleasantly surprised by Wolfenstein New Order (Old Blood too), this game knocked it out of the park. Doom2 was the first game I ever played on my Windows 3.1 as a kid, so there is a certain nostalgia I have whenever I play the old Dooms. Doom2 for me still holds up today: fast-paced, good sound/music, fun levels full of secrets, satisfying to shoot the guns and kill monsters.
The new Doom does an amazing job bringing these qualities into the current day and delivers a great game (I'll refer to Doom 2016 as Doom, and the older dooms as Doom2 for clarity, even though it seems counter-intuitive :P).
I took a lot of downvotes for negatively reviewing Doom3 earlier this year: the game was too slow, too few monsters on screen, and overall not a very compelling story... I found it boring, and outdated after playing DeadSpace 1 and 2 which improved upon everything Doom3 tried to be.
Doom does great job satisfying fans (like myself) but making a game that newcomers would enjoy.

Sound and Graphics:
The composer did an amazing job. The music during action pumps you up with some good metal tracks (for the most part). Otherwise, the ambience, the downtime music, the gun sounds, the monster noises, the death sounds are all amazing. The tearing of monsters faces, the cracking of their skulls, very satisfying.
The art team and graphics team deserve top marks. The game is beautiful and really well thought out.

Story and Lore:
There is a clearly defined story, it's not very fleshed out, but doesn't need to be. It is compelling enough to keep you interested, and there are plenty of little tidbits to pick up as collectibles if you're interested in reading them. Some are quite worth it (especially when hearing what the demons think of the Doom Marine).

Gameplay:
It's back to Doom2's fast-paced, arcady action. Except, in D2, the monsters are all spawned on the map. In this game, some are on the map, others will teleport in, but unlike D3, it is not just a couple at a time or boring. It's usually to throw you into an arena, where you must fight and kill them all before moving on, and this is an improvement on D2, where you would be able to pick them off one at a time and hide.
The brutal kills looked kind of stupid to me at first (slowing down action to watch preset animations), but you need to try it and experience it for yourself. It works and makes the action better. If you want, you can disable the flashing animation, and just shoot the monsters the old fashioned way.
Also, the chainsaw mechanic of refilling your ammo was a wonderful idea. Overall, combat is fun, and never feels tedious.
The maps are varied and large (13 of them), and each have a clear "theme" to them. You'll get the most out of them by exploring and finding the secrets (also fun). This will help with upgrades to your weapons and character.
Boss fights were also done generally well (something Doom2 couldn't).

Fan Service:
For Doom2 generation fans, there are a ton of nods back to the previous games without being too blunt. Moreover, you can unlock 13 classic levels and try them with the new monsters/weapons (they are much harder this way).

Overall:
From someone who said Doom2 would never be topped, highly recommended. I found myself smililng while battling in hell, blood raining from my enemies as death metal was pounding. This is the first time I saw my computer chug (free bullet time :P), but even still, it was a very solid experience from start to finish. This game is not better (or worse) than Doom2, but amazing in its own right. It seems weird to recommend, but if you enjoyed this game (and you will) you should try the Brutal Doom mod which this game borrowed a lot of elements from.
Overall, this game is made with love, made with skill, and chock full of detail and polish.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter a while, and still with the bitter taste of the multiplayer beta in my mind, I submerged in the Doom campaign and what it has to offer. And boy, it has a LOT to offer. From the beggining the game has stunning graphics, really good atmospheric, a world that´s been meticuously detailed and monsters that truly came from hell. In my case I have tried a first run in the Ultraviolence diffficulty to have a little challenge and enjoy the flow of the campaign, and it didn´t let me down. The monsters are relentless, have different aproach tactics, and sometimes the quantity of enemies on screen can feel overwhelming for some. For experienced FPS players maybe this difficulty level will feel "easy", for those the Nightmare difficulty will give them the challenge they need without sacrificing pace, which in my opinion it´s very important to do not fall in a mechanical gameplay and lose the ambience and feel that the game tries to give. The story itself it´s pretty basic although I enjoyed the backstory told in documents you find laying around in the maps, how some texts are presented as part of a "manual" for UAC, some are really creepy and absurd at the same time (what it really defines the tone of the game).

To give it a score, I will give it a solid 9 out of 10. It´s a great game with a very nice campaign and replayability for the ones looking for an extra challenge with the Ultra Nightmare difficulty, and not counting the Multiplayer and the Snap map.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom, the game that (almost) started the FPS genre, and is the 2016 successor worthy of the name? The answer is an emphatic YES. The game is just FUN, and fast, and loud, and awesome. Much more so than the Doom 3 attempt. This one is fast paced, always scurrying from room to room fighting monsters. You have all the classic weapons redone in modern style, with the classic feel, from the double shotgun, to the chaingun, to the BFG 9000, and they *feel* powerful and awesome with every shot, making the monsters that survive more than a couple seemingly all the more terrifying.

It's not a dark game, rather a gore fest as you fight the demons of hell and save humanity. There's no regenerating health, instead health packs and picking up ammo. Its biggest additions to the series are an RPG like leveling up of health and weapons, along with glory kills to finish off your enemies in an extreme fashion to be rewarded with health and ammo.

This is a game which I'd say every love of FPS's should play and enjoy. Takes only 11-15 hours to beat the Single Player.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou play as Doomguy, the protagonist of nearly every other doom game, who wakes up on top of a mysterious altar on a Martian base that has been overrun by demons. That's how the game starts, no backstory, no explanation as to why you are there, and I love it. It feels like a reimagining of the original doom games that just dropped you into the action as soon as you hit start.

The combat is fast-paced and extremely fun. The game encourages you to be constantly moving whilst attacking because staying still means death 99% of the time. Then there are the glory kills. Glory kills can be pulled off once the player causes enough damage to an enemy, once the enemy has low health it will become staggered, this lets the player perform a brutal melee kill to finish it off. These kills range from breaking the enemies neck to ripping out their own hearts and shoving them down their throats. Fair to say this isn't a game for the faint of heart.

The games campaign clocks in at around 11-15 hours long depending on if the player wants to find all of the secrets and collectibles. These secrets range from action figures that the player can find that unlock 3D models in the main menu, to levers that you can find in each stage that unlocks a classic DOOM level that you can play. There are 13 levels in total.

The only flaw that I have found with the game is the boss battles. There are only 3 in total and they are a little bit too easy unless you are playing on the hardest difficulty.

The multiplayer is OK but unnecessary, it feels a lot like the halo multiplayer with a doom skin painted onto it. However, there is one feature that I like about the multiplayer. Players can turn into demons for small amounts of time and use it to rack up more points.

Overall, DOOM is a game that I feel many players would enjoy, especially fans of the original games\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is back! The feeling of the original Doom with the visuals of 2016, it is the Doom we've all been waiting for. Single player is fast paced, full of monsters to kill, and visual stunning.

As for the multiplayer, it is also very decent in my opinion. I feel like the biggest difference between this and Quake is that this feels more grounded. In Quake, you run like a cheetah and rocket jump like a crazy grasshopper, which has its own charms, but this, you are heavier and slower. It is pure personal preference. I've found multiplayer more fun the more I play, because the fun comes from becoming better.

I highly recommend this game!\t\t\t\t\t
