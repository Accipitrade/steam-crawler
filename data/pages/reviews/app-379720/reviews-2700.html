\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is making video games fun again

You may feel like singleplayer FPS are a redundant sequence of Shoot-Hide-Shoot-Hide-Walk forward-Your closest friend dies or betrays you 2/3 into the game-Shoot-Hide-Shoot-Win. A month ago, I would've agreed with you. I like to think about the first F.E.A.R. when it comes to evaluate these games because the first person perspective is more than the gun and the ennemies (and waypoints, and cutscenes, and the "dying" red screen) you see. It's about the immersion and experience.

When my friend told me to play Doom with him, I said: You played one, you played them all, put it on a harder difficulty. I got destroyed in the first minutes of the game and gave up. Fast forward months later where I bought the game and realised the greatness behind it. If you felt the genre was more of the same in 2017, you need to play Doom. Here's what I like about it.

First of all: gameplay mechanics. The game will make fun of you for playing it the same way you finished Killzone 2. You see the low health sign, you run behind cover, you are killed. If you want to survive, you need to be aggressive, you need to run towards your ennemies, you need to kill. A lot. I would argue that, with this little change, Doom flips the genre on its head. They changed what a lot of developpers thought was the norm. More importantly, it's in tune with my second point.

Secondly: immersion. As I implied, in a FPS, I think the immersion is more important than the story, In other words, what happens to the character is happening to the player and I need to feel that. When a game needs cutscene and dialog to explain the character's/my motivations, it often lose my attention and I start playing the game just to finish it and check it under my To-Do list. The experience then becomes forgettable.

So, in a way, I really don't care about the story in a FPS. The Doom Marine doesn't like it either and every part of its caracther emphazies that. The interaction he has with story elements, the glory kills, little animations here and there tell you all you need to know about him: he is an insane killing machine. As the player, you don't really have to worry about much else. The game doesn't lose your time and always give you what you want: Fast-paced, violent, split-second decision making action. When the music kicks in (which is awesome, but you already know that), I have a demented grin on my face every time, much like I imagine him when he slices a demon in half with a chainsaw. And that's what the strong side of a FPS is. Immersion isn't about the cutscenes or your "important" mission. It's about the gameplay and how it makes you feel.

I see a lot of reviews on games about "how a game is worth your money". A lot of games have a great production value. The game looks awesome, story is adequate, game mechanics check all the boxes and it took millions to produce. In a way, they deserve our money. I feel that's why open world are so popular right now. Worlds get bigger, more and more sidequests and they take 70-80 hours to complete. People will say "you get so much content* for the price". But what if it's not fun?

I feel a more relevant question is: Is the game worth your time? In that regard, Doom deserves every praises it gets. It deserves your undivided attention. Doom respects your time by not spending half the game waiting behind cover. It respects your intelligence by not giving you a waypoint towards secrets.

Doom respects what a FPS is, what video games are and its community.
And I dont say that often.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM emphasizes the music and gameplay rather than the story. I've never played a game like this before and it certainly has changed the way I look at video games.

Pros:
- Super fun gameplay
- Pretty good story (it doesn't focus on the story much)
- Amazing campaign
- Very good graphics (they look pretty good even at the lowest!)
- the music tho
- the soundtrack is just.... mmmmhhhh yes
- the background music is soooooo guuuudd
- did i mention the soundtrack?

Cons:
- In the end it gets repetitive (slaughter a room of demons, find a way into the next room, repeat)
- the game ended on a cliffhanger

The multiplayer in this game is pretty meh, not bad not good, but it's something. Not something I woulNo pros or cons really to be said.

The snapmap mode allows you to create levels or even campaigns, and it's very flexable. My only complaint for this one is that it can be a little confusing for beginners since there is no tutorial.

Overall DOOM gets a 9.6/10 for it's pure action, great campaign and kick ass music.\t\t\t\t\t
