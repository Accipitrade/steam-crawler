(Singleplayer-only review)

10 years ago, I really started to get into the classic Dooms. Aside from the vanilla experience, I also played a plethora of WADs, pk3s, and mods in general, so while I'm no professional historian, I'm at least decently familiar with what makes Doom a fun insta-classic.

Does this installment hold a candle to the old games?

Yes, but on its own way. It's rather obviously not meant to be Classic Doom with pretty graphics, but it's a great modern version.

For reference, I really disliked the repetitive nature of the Painkiller games, and got sick of traversing levels only for my passage to be blocked and for monsters to start teleporting in. Rinse and repeat.

This Doom actually takes this gameplay loop I hate so much, and makes it fun and exciting, because of both good pacing and excellent movement:

1. Between every fight, you can backtrack, hunt for secrets, and stock up on your essentials for the next fight. Many oldschool-style games insist on pushing you forward and barely leaving you time to breathe, which irks me.
2. In the fight, your ability to (eventually double-)jump and grab ledges adds verticality and flexibility rather than just circle-straferunning around enemy groups like a confused herder. Most of the enemies being smart and avoiding bunching up into blastable groups also helps with the experience of making you believe you're fighting dangerously competent foes.

My only issue with this game is that the [weapon damage]-to-[enemy health] ratio leans to being borderline spongy, but I can definitely say I've had far worse, more useless weapons in this genre before (Shadow Warrior reboot's guns, anyone?).

Overall,
9.3/10\t\t\t\t\t

DOOM is a great game, although I wouldn't say it was as revolutionary as The Ultimate DOOM that came out in the 90's.
It's still worth your time and especially at the 19.99 it's at right now.
It's game play is smooth and fluid and you can really feel the weight of movement and the guns.
It's music is phenomenal and I'm almost sad I couldn't hear more of it because I was too busy either blasting through combat or dying in the first few minutes.
That being said I mostly find myself blasting through the game even on the hardest difficulty, maybe I'm just used to the a little bit more okay with death than others, but death seemed more of an unpleasant stop and less of a punishment for your mistakes.
Nightmare plus (you unlock after beating the campaign on nightmare) takes it a bit far and puts you back at the beginning of the campaign instead of the level. It DOES give you an option on normal nightmare to reset the level, but on your first playthrough that makes finding secrets a total ♥♥♥♥♥. so maybe do two playthroughs of the game, it's assuredly worth the time.

oh yeah, and the multiplayer is a thing... if you like that...

Not a perfect game, but leaps and bounds above more recent games in which we shall not name. It's a solid 8.5/10
