\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite the initial reveal making this game look like the clunkiest garbage ever, the game actually turned out fantastic. It's fast paced, difficult, enemies are varied and fun to fight against, it's overall a very fluid experience that really feels like a true sequel to classic Doom.

Despite being a great game, this PC version does have some issues which will hopefully be patched out soon. Custom keybindings for movement will reset to WASD if you enable and disable a controller, Snap Map does not use custom keybindings either and I know of no way to set them, that's why I use a controller when making maps. Mouse smoothing cannot be disabled for certain people and the UI and menus all feel like they were designed for consoles.

As for Multiplayer, it's nothing special. I would describe it as the type of multiplayer that singleplayer games use to have to add a bit of replayability, before devs realized it was a waste of time as their populations died within a month.

Snap Map is surprisingly powerful, but needs some updates. Namely the ability to enable a visible grid in the rooms for more accurate placements, armor shards and small health kits don't float and spin in sync, so it looks dumb when multiple are in a row. Other than that, it's great, but it's a horrible shame that it replaced modding which made classic Doom so long lived and memorable as it is.

TL;DR: It's really good, but has some minor flaws.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not an "oldschool" gamer. I wasn't born when Doom came out. So while I wasn't born into hell, I adopted it. After beating that beautifully-orchestrated first section of the game, I was hooked. I found myself being physically giddy when the game let me do something badass; I was a rock star during a fight, I was a rap god dropping the mic when I cleared a tough wave encounter. The demons from the pit were brutal, without mercy. But I was worse.

The game really makes you feel that way. The soundtrack is so perfectly crafted as a backing track to the non-stop beatings you deliver. The gameplay is structured so that the demons are agile and aggressive, they chase you and use the environment. And you have to constantly be moving, dodging, positioning yourself so that the demon horde has no advantage over you. Ammo for every weapon is very limited, so you're forced to use whatever you have on-hand, which may not be the best for the encounter. Sure, you pick up ammo all the time. But when you're surrounded by tough enemies and have no rockets left, you're forced to improvise - and that disadvantage makes you feel like a god when you punch the literal face off of the last demon standing, the last chords of an overdriven guitar still echoing.

And let me level with you, Snapmap is probably the most exciting thing I've seen in a game in a long time. It's a very user-friendly and accessible map editor - letting you easily build maps for either singleplayer, coop, or multiplayer all in the same system. The "scripting" is brilliantly easy (especially for non-programmers, I imagine). And the community integration is about as good as you can imagine - you can upload maps publicly, play with others whenever you want, and rate maps/players you've experienced.

When I was young, scripting was just programming with a weird language, mapping was a tedious process with a compile phase, and to get anyone to play your map required finding a way to host BSP files, convincing someone to let you use their server, and then actually finding people to play with you. The fact that a triple-A game has a first-class map editor built-in that's accessible is huge to me - mapping has always been about making game content creation accessible, and I'll never complain when a game has this good of support for it.

I expected this game to suck. I tried to not even get excited. But the experience that this game gives is easily the most remarkable I've had in a long time. Maybe it's oldschool, maybe it's an homage to a simpler time, maybe it's not enough to satisfy people who played the originals. I don't care about any of that - this is a hell of a game, one of the best.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've played the first two DOOM games and loved them, then this is the DOOM you've been waiting for. If you're looking for something more akin to DOOM 3, well you wont find it here. DOOM 2016 is a return to form with throwback level design and classic arcade shooter gameplay that requires the player to be just as fast as accurate. DOOM 2016 takes clear inspiration from its 90's roots and its with that inspiration that DOOM 2016 realizes its full potential. This iteration of DOOM is at its best when it tosses you into a demon filled arena armed to the teeth leaving you with nothing but a few armor, health, and ammo pickups scattered across the map, and your wits. A worthy addition to this version of DOOM is the brutal takedown system. Takedowns are all but required in any frantic firefight because they reward the player with health. The system is smart enough to reward just enough health to make the risk of going in for the kill worthwhile and not making the health rewarded feel useless or plentiful. Another useful change to the DOOM formula is now chainsaw kills reward the player with ammo. The chainsaw is a one hit kill for weaker demons, but you'll need to soften up the bigger baddies if you plan to cut them in half, however the chainsaw also has a limited amount gasoline, 3 to start with to be precise, and gasoline tanks are some of the scarcest ammo pickups in the game. DOOM is an excellent example of mixing gameplay mechanics and making them so that they blend well together. I used every weapon in my initial playthrough and felt each weapon had its strengths and drawbacks and while the multiplayer may not showcase these mechanics as masterfully as the singleplayer campaign; rest assured that its still a decent way to kill some time every now and then. DOOM 2016 is a sequel 20 years in the making and its been worth the wait. The singleplayer portion of DOOM is the best take on classic arena shooting in the modern era. The AI is smart and challenging, the weapons, gunplay, and movement all feel responsive, the upgrade systems promote a sense of progression and power without making the player overpowered, boss fights are fun, and the game both looks and runs amazing on up to date systems.

DOOM 2016 gets a solid 4 out of 5.\t\t\t\t\t
