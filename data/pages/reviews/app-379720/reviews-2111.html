\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I don't really think I can give this game a positive review. Let's start off with a simple question. Where did it succeed and where did it fail?

Doom (2016) succeeded in:
-Ripping and tearing
-Excellent gun-play
-Outstanding and impressive visuals
-Amazing optimization
-Eargasmic soundtrack. (Papa bless Mick Gordon)

And personally, that's where it ends. Albeit, I wasn't around for the games launch, and thus I didn't get to experience the multiplayer. But don't expect yourself to either.

As for where Doom (2016) failed:
-Creating an interesting story that holds throughout the game until the end.
-Level design feels slogish and boring, same repetitive map design over and over.
-Mid-way through you've seen every enemy, expect nothing new outside of the total of 3 bosses.
-There are only 3 bosses. And that's being generous.
-The game is really. Really. Easy. Even on Ultra-Violence it was incredibly simple. (Maybe I need to play Nightmare)

Honestly, I could've gotten over the previous failures if the game provided any form of challenge. Some guns are incredibly weak, others, ridiculously strong. You learn quick which kills the fastest. BFG ammo comes in troves, to the point where there's too much for me to even use. Sometimes 3+ per room later on. Gasoline is honestly harder to get your ripping and tearing hands on. Assuming you even remember the chainsaw is in the game. It's only used as a tool to restore ammo, or to utterly remove an enemy you just really don't feel like fighting. With a neat animation alongside it that you'll get bored of quick. The same could go for the BFG. With maximum efficiency, you really only bust it out when there's a load of enemies, or some you just really don't want to fight. Being a 1-shot kill to anything it hits/passes, it's easy to see why it ruins the gun-play of the game.

The runes were an interesting idea, however again, you'll learn which are the best quick. If you don't believe me, I will show you.

I used the following
-Ammo Boost: Increases the amount of ammo gained from items and enemies. - After mastery, it allows BFG ammo to drop from enemies, making BFG ammo spawns irrelevant and pointless, since you've just made the BFG a standard use weapon in your kit.
-Vacuum: Increases the range for absorbing dropped items (ammo and health) - After mastery, further range. Honestly, I could've replaced this one later on, but I just didn't as I hadn't found another rune that did anything remotely as half useful.
-Armored Offensive: Glory Kills drop armor. - After mastery, it drops more armor. This. I don't think it could be more obvious why you'd take this. It's damn near essential if you're going to use runes. And again, making it essential, defeats the purpose of choices.

All in all, runes made the game far easier and just defeat some of the core aspects of the game itself. The game provides as a good arcade game, to run around and do some ripping and some tearing here and there, but the main campaign is just that trying to be more, and failing. Hopefully, Eternal will prove to hit all these notes better and shoot for the skulls (stars) in it's November release. Either way, I had a fairly good time, just wish it was more challenging.\t\t\t\t\t
