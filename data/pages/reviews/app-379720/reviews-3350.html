Doom came back to that original concept with a vengance. The fash running, the strafing, the endless stream of bullets tearing into those demons. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI preordered this, then preloaded it, only to have to wait until today to finally play it (family stuff that takes priority over games). Well I am completely wowed by this game and I don't say that very often. It was more than worth the wait and in comparison to Doom 3 this one is so much better. This game rightfully can call itself Doom. It feels like an advanced alien civilization stumbled across an old copy of Doom, the original from the mid 90s and decided to just enhance everything. This game is stunning, yet horrific. The play is fast, the direction is usually clear. It's a fragging masterpiece. I like that your suit and weapons can be upgraded. I absolutely love the classic maps that you can find, they are ridiculously fun. I'm playing this on hurt me plenty and I'm progressing through at a very nice pace. It's Doom, I really want to enjoy it, and in this incarnation I am thoroughly enjoying it.

Pros:
No mistaking that it is Doom
Game progresses at a good pace without the usual frustrations
In game map is much more helpful
Fraggin is a literal bloody good time
Hurt Me Plenty is hard but still viable for the average gamer
A nice marrying of the old platform to a console style game
Controls are extremely intuitive
The HUD is simple and effective to managing what you need
Campaign mode actually plays out like a story making you progress in order to uncover more of the plot
Runs excellently on my Core i5
Feels like it's properly using my GTX 980 Ti
Sound environment is insane, yet at times too busy (1 demon sounds like hundreds)

Cons:
It has multiplayer, why? This is Doom
Game modes (Multiplayer, Campaign, and Snap Map) force a game restart.
At times it can be a tad confusing where you need to go until you realize you need to jump across something or pull yourself up to a ledge.

I won't discuss multiplayer since I hated the open beta. Maybe some day I'll try it out but for now I'd rather just keep it like the Doom I have loved for over 20 years.

This is a winner in my opinion. I didn't play it on Day 1 but on Day 3 I'm running, jumping, climbing, and shooting to a very good time. I can safely recommend this to my friends who love Doom.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBrilliant but broken.
The graphics and gameplay are great. Its a return to form for the franchise as this concentrates on fast fun gameplay than trying to tell a complex story.

The problem is that the game is unplayable, at least on my system. Yes I am below specs but I get the game running smoothly at about 40fps for a few hours, then, either at a change in level or if I quit and re-load I get 2fps. I get 2fps ON THE MENU!
Looking at the specs and the way the game plays, the specs are largely due to an un-optimied engine. I had to run game optimised drivers to even run the game and I frequently get booted out as the game tries to use more memory than my card has available.

Bad coding, bad testing, thats what it boils down to and its become a trait on the PC versions of multiplatform titles. Just wish I hadn't kept trying and run out of time for a refund.

DON'T BUY THIS GAME TILL ITS PATCHED! Its the only way to send the industry a message that we won't stand for crappy ports!\t\t\t\t\t
