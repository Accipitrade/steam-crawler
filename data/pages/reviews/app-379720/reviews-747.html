\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMay the 13th 2016, I was so hyped to finally play DOOM with 3D models and modern graphic. At the time I didn't really care about DOOM 3 and I didn't consider it a "real" doom game (now I find DOOM 3 different but good). I didn't have a PC powerful enough so I got it for Xbox at the time.

At first I was so disapointed. For what felt like half the game I was killing imps and Hell knights and that's it. After that Barons, revenants, mancubus and cacodemons came along. It was better, but still It didn't have enough demons. Something was missing. The re-appearance of quake-like soother with DOOM 2016 didn't stoke me as the big thing because I was constantly playing the OG DOOM and "boomer shooter" and I wasn't really awared of the state of modern FPS at the time.

After a while DOOM 2016 finally clicked with me and found myself enjoying it quite a lot. I still wished there was more demons but I wasn't playing it especially for the combat but more because it was a badass shooter with a lot of gore and metal music playing while slaying. It was great for the ambiance and design, but when I was craving a top notch game gameplay wise, this was not my first pick.

So DOOM Eternal came out and to be honest, I don't see many reasons to re-play 2016. I, personally, find Eternal better in every way than 2016 (Cacodemons everywhere again!). But 2016 still has the dark ambiance that fits well with the game but I'm glad Eternal didn't do the same. Now If I want good graphic and top notch gameplay in the same game, DOOM Eternal is the answer. But that's not to say that 2016 is obsolete, If you want to spam the super shotgun and turn your brain off while slaying demons and feeling like a badass this is your game. And it's great the way it is. You can still have a lot of fun. It's not a perfect game by any mean, but a very fun one.\t\t\t\t\t
