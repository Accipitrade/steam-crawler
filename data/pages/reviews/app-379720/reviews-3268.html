\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only just about recommended this game because the gameplay is fun. It is Doom, there is lots of demon slaying and plenty of guns to slay said demons with. But after finishing the game in 13 hours, and because of how it ended, I am beginning to wonder if the game manufacturers aren't attempting a bit of a cash grab.

The game is pretty linear. Get from A to B while killing everything inbetween that is not you. There is a villian whom you're trying to catch and you also get to uncover pieces of the story as you go along. The story is pretty much a side to the main dish: shooting stuff. And here it is fun. If you stand still, you're going to have a bad time. There are some areas where it is difficult to move around and it can be frustrating trying to do the thing they want you to do but in little space to do it in. However, on the whole, strafing, jumping and shooting your way through an area is lots of fun.

The bad parts though? The game is repetitive to the point that is predictable. You walk into a round/oval shaped room with lots of ammo, armour and health which means a fight is about to go down. It is not scary at all - Doom 3 was a lot scarier - and you'll find yourself wandering from area to area barely taking anything in. Every now and then you'll come across a log or hologram explaining some of the demented stuff they did to summon demons, but again it's thrown in more as justification for doing what you do. They could have expanded on the story a whole lot more because it's easy to feel like you're just running around and shooting stuff for the sake of running around and shooting stuff.

Many of the reviews I've read praise it for getting back to its roots or being the real Doom, but they could have done more than just sprinkle a story inbetween shooting stuff, climbing on stuff and melee attacking stuff. The Glory Kills (melee finishers) are not a bad idea, but they are really just soft quick time events. Shoot the demon until it flashes then hit the melee button to finish it off. You're then rewarded with health, ammo and armour, which sprinkles about like a pinyata has blown it's load.

The developers have a good game on their hands, but it seems like it's missing some stuff. For example, there are like only 2 NPCs you interact with throughout the game. It's pretty much demon shooting all the way. The game looks nice but the levels are pretty much the same: walk down a corridor towards a room or area where you're going to shoot stuff then proceed to the next level.

Here are some pros and cons:

Pros:
- Shooting hordes of demons and fighting demon bosses is fun
- Power-ups help you lay the smack down that much harder on demons
- You can upgrade your weapons, armour and equipment. You can increase your maximum health, armour and ammo capacities, which is a nice touch.
- You're constantly moving when fighting, so it forces you to think about what the best weapons are for the environment, as well as for the specific demons you're facing.
- Melee finishers come in handy, especially when ammo and health start running low.
- The game is rather forgiving when you do, allowing you to just reload from the last checkpoint, which is usually not far from when you entered an area.

Cons:
- Combat is repetitive, especially during boss fights. I remember finding myself just circle strafing and shooting many times because it worked rather well.
- Not much of a story, which is a shame because Doom 3's PDA system made the game more immersive.
- Level design tends to be similar when entering areas where you're going to fight some demons.
- The ending is a bit of a let down. They're probably looking to profit from DLC later.
- The game "feels" fast-paced only because demons are quick and you're constantly trying to avoid projectiles. Your character, though, can feel slow because he is set to one speed.

I'd give the game a 7/10 because it is fun, but it hasn't got much in the way of substance.\t\t\t\t\t
