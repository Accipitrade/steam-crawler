\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a 10/10 singleplayer, a 5/10 multiplayer, and a 8/10 level maker.

The Singleplayer - Oh my god where to begin with the single player, the level design is is filled with lots of secrets and flows incredibly well. Visually the game at points bloody disgusting and it works so well for not only the world but also the demons, like when you rip a demon in half you can see them turn into the blood soaked hunk of meat that they were born to be. Something that goes along perfectly with the presentation is the soundtrack, it's a never ending buckkake of awesome metal music that only ever stops for when you annihilate something. I just realized I haven't even talked about the gameplay yet, it's a never ending murder binge that makes you feel like a god but it at the same time never makes you feel like the game is too easy. The glory kill system at first is weird to get used to as it's harder to just shoot things to death but as soon as you become accustomed to it, it makes the gameplay flow like running water. The story isn't at the forefront of the singleplayer but if you want to learn more about the lore data logs are plentiful and you can delve into it on your own time instead of the game bringing itself to a crawl to explain the inner mechanics of Hayden Pierces robotic rectum. The Doom Slayer as a character bleeds personality through only animations of him being pissed off. In conclusion the campaign for Doom (2016) does everything flawlessly from the music to the level design everything was done meticulously and it has been fined tuned to perfection.

The Level Maker - The level maker has a lot of detail packed into a relatively welcoming level editor. You can do so much stuff like add NPC's, mess with the lighting, create scripts for how the enemies relate to each other, etc. The only disappointing thing about it is how generic it can feel, like you only really have one theme and , it doesn't even feel like Doom, it feels like it's from some low budget 2000's movie and the NPCs are mainly discount looking Spartans form Halo. In conclusion a lot can be done with the level maker but it's marred by how little personality it has.

The Multiplayer - It's worse Halo mixed with worse Quake but it can still be fun.

It's ALL good. Only near the end did I even find something that I'd say detracted from the game. It was tiny, incidental. Got stuck on a wall in VEGA's level. Same room I also got stuck behind a handrail. The latter is my fault. Hell I did it twice. :) But I died on the wall stuck in a gap in an incline of the wall as it meets the floor.
That's it!!!
The rest is friggin' dope.
The music...is by Mick Gordon so 'nuff said.
The level design is superb.
Weapons are excellent.
Navigation is brilliant in that you can scale higher walls by climing, & double-jumping is always a favorite past time
And the story is also compelling. A surprise because I thought I'd hate it 'til I found some demon lore. Then it got interesting.

Key details about gameplay:
There is an upgrade system. Weapons, your suit. And to earn these upgrades is done through a finding secrets & kill-count. Find 'em earn points to spend, slaughter them & get the same. It's more intricate than that, but all the same. A neat change to the series.
If you want to continue the fight against Hell's minions it's possible to do it in an awesome way. The Onslaught is on! in this game.\t\t\t\t\t
