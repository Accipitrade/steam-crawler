\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR version: Okay. Let's get this out of the way. The Singleplayer is hands down the most fun I've had with a Tripple A FPS in awhile. Fast paced, lots of customization, excellent level/enemy design, great gunplay, actually a cool story. The multiplayer is a decent attempt at modernizing arena shooters but is a bit shallow and very console orientated gameplay. Snapmap is fun and you can make some cool stuff. It is however, weak compared to what WADs and mods could do back in the days of DOOM 1, 2, and 3. Overall you might want to wait for this game to go on sale at around 40 bucks before you pick it up.

Now for a more in-depth singleplayer review. The story goes that you awake within a UAC facility and ♥♥♥♥ hits the fan with demons. Bust out of your confinement, smash a zombies head in, grab a pistol and start kicking ass. No slow cinematic introduction to set up world building or character development. Just a black ominious screen with a forboding voice telling you to rip and tear until it is done. And ripping and tearing is what Doomguy does best in this game. Between the fast paced default running speed you'll zoom by demons unloading lead, never having to reload, double jumping, and vertical gameplay being the core game mechanics here.

Hot damn it is astounding how well id modernized the DOOM genre without sacraficing much. The guns are unique, feel great to shoot, and their design is a mix of both old and new DOOM style. A new take on the genre is the addition of Light RPG mechanics. Just like the old DOOM games where it recorded how many demons you killed or secrets you found, this game does that but also rewards you for it at the same time. You'll gain tokens from exploring, killing monsters, and fulfilling combat based side task (IE: Kill 5 demons in one shot). These tokens can be used to both increase your suits passive abilities as well as unlock new and powerful mods for your guns. Not only that, but you can also find runes which give you passive abilities to help you on your quest (More ammo drops, faster glory kills, air-strafing and more). To gain them you preform special events ala Devil May Cry secret room style objectives. With the combination of both, weapon mods, suit tokens, and rune loadouts, there is a LOT of freedom for the player to play the way they want to play The glory kills at first might look like a turn off, but they are fast and completely optional; and on higher difficulties it is recommended you don't do them as they can lead to death. They do however feel VERY satisfying to preform and there is a ton of different animations for each individual body part on the demons you execute. There is a lot of love and attention to detail, it is very reminiscent of MKX's Fatalities. The monsters/demons you tear apart are beautiful and have lush, vibrant colors that make them easy to identify; giving them character. Just like the original DOOM games, they have their own set attack patterns, weaknesses, and even fight with each other. My personal favorite is the Revenant, who looks stupidly cute to me for some reason and I am in LOVE with how he looks in this game.

The level design is fluid, encourages exploration, eye appealing, gameplay orientated, and strays away from long boring corridor simulator syndrom which is abundent in most Tripple A games these days. There is SO much vertical gameplay in this game. Jump pads, platforms in places you wouldn't dare dream of trying to jump to in any other FPS and to top it off, no fall damage! Weapons, items, and enemies are placed in key spots to make sure the adrenaline and flow of the game are never halted because of some ledge you couldn't see that had to be hurdelred over or because of an invisible wall. The level design reminds me of Half-Life 1 with its unfriendly enviormental locations; such as scaling a mechanical tower with large gyros you have to transverse or even an assembly line with laser grids. A very good break from all the demon killing to put your platforming skills to use in a fun and refreshing way. Not to mention these levels look freaking GORGEOUS and deserve your time to stop what you're doing and bask in their glory and gorey ambience. You'll also be delighted as a lot of the levels are based off of original DOOM 1 and 2 levels, and are filled with subtle nods to the past games.

Finally, the music. This is a very iffy topic as most of the community is a bit split over it. The music is filled with heavy metal, industrial bass/beats, and a gothic choir to go with it all. The heavy sound of electronic screeches and distorted guitars for the early industrial levels set the mood for an almost dread filled Cyberpunk movie. Where tech and despair come together to keep you on the edge and warry at all times. Then once you get to the hell levels, heavy metal guitars become more abundant and get your heart racing, pumping with adrenaline. It is soon shut out by booming ominous echos of a moaning choir forboding an unending war you fight within hell that you can never escape. It's like Mask of the Phantasm had a baby with Killing Floors soundtrack and DOOM 1 was the caretaker. Aside from the combat music there is subtle hints of DOOM 1 and 2 sound effects/music within the exploration. Songs like The Dave D. Taylor Blues can be heard off the distance, DOOM 3's intro guitar rift makes an appearance, and even The Demons' Waltz can be heard. This is honestly a super fun, well suited soundtrack for both the games pace and history.

I could talk so much more about this game, but I think it's clear that I really love the singleplayer. The multiplayer and snapmap are a nice side attraction but they don't even compare to what the SP brings to the table. LIke I said, you should buy this game for the singleplayer when it goes at a cheaper price. In no way is this a bad game, but it is advised to spend your money wisely on the meat of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is ok...

Gameplay: I'm not a fan of arena combat and Doom wasn't really known for this, you've always had the chance to pick up a key and run like the dickens out of a room. Or you could teleport into a room and have multiple exits. Only a select few levels would actually lock you in an arena (Namely "Dead Simple" from Doom 2.

The earlier weapons you get don't carry any actual weight when you fire them at an enemy, it feels as if you are armed with a peashooter and eventually the enemies just die out of boredom and sorrow for the player character. The later weapons start to have a decent feeling but by then you'll be a little tired of just putzing around arenas doing the same thing over and over again (run around the arena backwards shooting)

Story: They shoehorn in a rather lackluster story to explain why demons were brought in. Its stupid.

The enemies in the game don't really have that great of AI either (They really didn't in the originals as well) but they still just run at you swinging like a drunk child at a strip club. You dont have to put too much effort to avoid the attacks and then counterattack.

Music: The music is straight up garbage, they decided to do a farty techno beat all throughout the game save a couple of tracks that have decent guitar beats. This, I guess you can say is remedied easily just by slapping in your favorite metal cd and playing that way... You shouldn't have to though

Levels: The levels weren't all that non-linear. You are still going in a linear line to the end in an ok sized map.

Enemies: The enemies are pretty neat though. Imps look like your stereotypical gremlin, Hell knights are just slightly prettier looking from Doom 3, Barons of Hell make a neat return... The main problem I have... they are all easily defeated.

Multiplayer: Eh

SnapMap: Instead of giving PC players the ability to mod the game openly (Hell even Doom 3 allowed this) you are locked down to Modding light... Snap map is what was brought to the table for us pc players to make new levels or new experience for other folks... Too bad its way too constrained to actually allow for gigantic levels with hordes of demons, or create an experience like Pirate Doom, Alien Doom, Batman Doom... Nope... We are stuck with the worst hand holdy process out there.

All in all... Its a game. But its going to be forgotten easily. id really screwed up by not allowing the modding community more power to play with this game.

The overall single player campaign was lackluster and really didn't offer anything passed "Kill stuff and go to next room"

I'd wait for this game to go on sale before you waste your hard earned money\t\t\t\t\t
