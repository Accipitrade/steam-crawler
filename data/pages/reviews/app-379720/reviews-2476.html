\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI initially wasn't expecting much, but this is a good game. I played the original Doom games, and am also a fan of Doom 3, but this game is a much more raw, pure action with an emphasis on speed and gore instead of survival horror. Except for a few short scenes, the story can be mostly skipped (background material comes in the form of pick-ups that the player doesn't have to look at), but there's a fun tale here and a lot of thought put into so much stuff that doesn't really change the course of the action. The environments are great, and there are great nods to the history of the series, but towards the end I grew a little tired of moving from one "enemy charge" scene to another; there isn't much variation in the fight setpieces. Thankfully there aren't any scripted scenes with a buddy yelling orders or anything; as the "Doom Slayer" you get to do things the way you want without someone else lecturing you. The implied attitude of the Doom Slayer has a lot of appeal.\t\t\t\t\t
