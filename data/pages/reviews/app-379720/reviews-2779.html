TL;DR
Doom is a fantastic game. It manages to pay homage to its roots without compromising the important lessons of modern game design. It goes against the popular, modern style for first-person shooters and does so with overwhelming speed and force. Doom is a brutal adrenaline rush of exciting combat and clever design with an intense soundtrack to motivate your murder. I barely touched the multiplayer aspects of the game, but I don't feel like I missed out on anything at all because the campaign was so good. If you have any reverence for the original games, or you are just looking for a new first person shooter to make you feel like a badass, look no further.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game. Lots of action.

Once caveat though. When I bought DOOM, my plan was to run it on my 2011 home built pc. The computer had served me well using two NVIDIA GTX 465's running in SLI. As Steam games evolved I would have to drop the game settings back to get a good frame rate but, until I bought DOOM I could always find a sweet spot between eye candy and frame rate.

That wasn't going to be the case with DOOM. My frame rates were a terrible 17 to 25 fps (sometimes worse) unless I was in the tightest of corners with not much for the GTX 465 cards to draw. That was even on the lowest on the low settings with everything turned off.

Frustrated, Doom forced me to make an unplanned video card upgrade. I'm just a working married man on a tight budget so I shopped long and hard for an affordable video card upgrade. I took a chance on a single GTX 1050 Ti card with 4GB VRAM.

Now, I couldn't be more pleased with DOOM and the video card upgrade it forced me to buy. The game now runs perfect on ultra setting with a rock solid 60 fps running at 1920 x 1080 (thats all my LCD monitor will support - which is more than enough for me).

If your a first person shooter fan then DOOM is a blast. Its fast, frentic and loads of monster killing (and being killed) fun. I know first person shooters can be controversial, but who in their right mind can complain if your killing monsters summoned from hell to wipe out humanity?

If any of you are in the same boat as I was wondering if aging video cards will run this game, well, i still suggest a vdeo card upgrade and the problem will be ULTRA solved. I had luck with the budget priced GTX 1050 Ti solution. Wifey never even noticed the cheap video card purchase I made.

This post is not an endorsment except for DOOM on PC. You'll love this game once you have the hardware to run it. If your an AMD Radeon fan I say go for it. I can't speak for AMD cards but I expect that the outcome of an upgrade will have you killing monsters from hell in no time.

Doom 4. Buy it! If Steam puts it on sale again then most definitely buy it. If you end up spending more money on hardware to run the game, well - you will love your computer even more.
