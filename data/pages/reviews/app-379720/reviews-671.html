\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM (2016) Review
[November 11th, 2021]

Doom is probably the greatest FPS game series of all time. The original Doom, which was released in 1993, basically invented the first-person-shooter genre. Doom II was a continuation of that game, Doom 64 showcased Doom in the brand-new N64 3D graphics. Doom 3 was a different genre entirely.

But DOOM 2016 completely revived fps. I have yet to find another modern game as fast paced and as entertaining as DOOM. There's no twenty-minute unskippable intro cut scenes. There's no bs. The second you enter the campaign, you are given a gun you are already shooting stuff. Within the next 10 minutes you already have another gun, all the equipment, and the actual intro starts. The protagonist, the Doom Slayer, literally throws a monitor against a wall when someone tries to tell him what's going on. He doesn't need to be informed of that, he already knows. So do you.

There's something new in almost every level, save for the last few when the game is throwing everything it has at you and you're running and gunning like mad and therefore have no time to learn anything new. There is some story, revealed mostly in real-time events or optional lore in the menus, but it's not great and forgettable. But what really matters is the gameplay.

DOOM's gameplay is (as I have said before) fast. Faster than any other FPS game (save for new entries in the series), and it's a hell of a lot of fun. Pun intended. Milliseconds after you press 'Q' you've already switched weapons, so spam Q and you'll continuously output death at your enemies. But the golden rule of every Doom game is thus: **never stop moving.** Ever. Most of the demons move just as fast (maybe *slightly* slower) as you, and the second you stop you will have hordes surrounding you. And how are you able to keep up with all this controlled chaos, you ask? The music.

Mick Gordon is a musical genius. When Bethesda/Id hired him for the job, he was told specifically, "*no guitars.*" So naturally, he did everything but. And it still delivered. Finally after recognizing his pure talent, Bethesda/Id finally gave him the OK to add guitars to the soundtrack. And he did not hold back. The DOOM 2016 soundtrack is unlike any metal I have ever heard. It's metal alright- pure industrial metal, straight out of hell. But it has a melody, a rhythm. It overclocks the brain, it's like taking drugs without the side-effects. And I absolutely love it.

The multiplayer leaves much to be desired, however. It's your classic arena shooter doomified. Custom loadouts, demon runes, etc. It's not great, but hey, it's there.

The SnapMap however, is a different story. You can make your own Doom levels, looking old or new, and publish them to the world. It's really in-depth, and arguably as good as Halo's forge.

Unfortunately (but also fortunately), Doom Eternal (which is the 2020 sequel to Doom) almost ruins this game. It's 2x faster and that much more entertaining, which on paper doesn't take away from this game, but if you play Eternal first you won't find much fun in this one. It's just so much slower. Definitely play this one first if you haven't yet, but if you already played Eternal I wouldn't bother playing this one.

In conclusion this game is WELL worth twenty bucks. I cannot recommend it highly enough, and it is a very worthy addition to any Steam user's library. See you in hell, slayers. Rip and tear.

VERDICT: 9/10 - Outshined by it's younger sequel, but a masterpiece by itself\t\t\t\t\t
