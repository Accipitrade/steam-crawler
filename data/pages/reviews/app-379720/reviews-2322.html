\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI guess I'm in the 8% that did not find the game fun (some negative reviews complain about bad performance yet it's the user's pc that sucks, not the game, so probably under 8% of players didnt like this, but anyways - to the review).

The game is definitely doom-like. You've got the heavy metal thrash music, gore, smooth aiming, cool guns and an interesting environment. However, the repetitive nature of the game (blowing demons away) simply bores the sh*t out of me.

1. You spawn in.
2. You kill demons in a room.
3. You move to the next room and some thrash music plays while more demons spawn around you, which you then kill.
4. Repeat step 3.

No, i do not like the game. However, there are some good aspects to it that stood out to me that i think i should note down:

1. Graphics truly are great, its some real eye candy provided you have the pc to run it.
2. Extremely well optimised. I'm running it on high settings with a 960 and pulling 70-90 fps. That is nuts for such a good looking game especially when theres so much stuff to render. And my fps didnt drop much even when in the middle of intense combat.
3. Cool weapons, NGL gives me a little buzz to see these futuristic weapons in such detail. Theyre futuristic and NOT stupid looking like some competitors.

So i guess my biggest gripe is the lack of a detailed story and interesting gameplay. But, it is true to the DOOM franchise, so if you came here looking for a mindless bloodbath, sure get it. But if you want a game with more depth, i would suggest bioshock.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis Doom is everything I was hoping it would be. This game is the perfect example of "modernizing a classic" done RIGHT.

The combat is fast and brutal. If you stand still in this game, you're dead. Cover won't save you. All of the games mechanics are designed towards keeping the flow of combat going once it's begun. There's no reloading, switching weapon mods on most weapons is reasonably quick (and can be made quicker), glory kills are fast and too the point as well as help replenish health, and kills with the chainsaw help replenish ammo. The game delivers a combat experience that feels truely authentic to the Doom franchise.

Almost every enemy from the original doom is here. Imps, Mancubi, Pinkies, Barons you name it. On top of that though, each enemy has their own unique attack style you have to fight against. Imps are quick, nimble and can climb walls, Hell knights and Baron's have this powerful leaping slam attack, Pinkies will just charge the ♥♥♥♥ out of you. These varied behaviors among the enemies also lend to the game's difficulty. As someone who beat this game on Nightmare, let me tell you this game will kick the ♥♥♥♥ out of you, it is not easy by any stretch, yet another feature that is authentic to the Doom name.

As much as I love the game though, it isn't perfect. There are a couple of areas it falls short in. My biggest complaint is for how brutal and satisfying the combat is, most of the guns' sound effects are very weak. Also the story isn't really anything special, though this is somewhat forgiveable in the sense that the game seems to mock itself in this regard, because the main protagonist seems to give even less of a ♥♥♥♥ about the reasons for the game's events than you do. Lets be honest, no one really plays a Doom game for the story.

Last, but by no means least, is the music. Holy hell, this game probably has one of my favorite soundtracks ever. When this game was first announced I was saying to myself "It had better have metal music", and it didn't disappoint. Anytime combat starts, the game seems to play just the right track to send you into a killing frenzy. I got a genuine rush the first time BFG division played.

In conclusion, Doom gives an experience that stays true to its roots. If you're a fan of most modern style fps games, this probably isn't for you, because this game doesn't have a pansy difficulty setting. Now go hide behind your cover and wait for your tiny life bar to recharge, the rest of you buy this game and get your asses into the fray.\t\t\t\t\t

Not Tomb Raider. But the ONE aspect which actually takes cash value away from the game's selling price is the silly forced checkpoint saving. That needs a patch to restore hotkey saving. Also there are some traps you can get into and can't get out. You'll stand there until "DOOMsday." So a clipping mode would be useful for that. Another thing which takes value away is that Doom IV seems to be locked against "easy" modding. After I played Doom III over and over many times I got bored and started enjoying it all over again by making all kinds of easy mods. That value seems to be missing in DOOM IV. Very good game overall, recommend buying it. For an Original Doom1&2 player, the biggest let down is the new cyber-demon. His new weapons and things are juvenile. What's up with the arcade-like weapons & effects? Sweeps the floor with some kind of high & low rays that you have to jump over? Very silly... And he sends up rock walls on either side of you from the Earth to trap you? How Juvenile. The Cyber-demon is "Supposed" to be a cold, trudging, menacing figure who pursues you with a rocket launcher. And this guy prances about. You MUST be kidding me. Can you PLEASE restore the Cyber-Demon to his familiar, menacing self\t\t\t\t\t
