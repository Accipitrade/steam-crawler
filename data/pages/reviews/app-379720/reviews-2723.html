\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe most recent first person shooter I really enjoyed was Left 4 Dead. Before that, TF2. Why? They're mechanically simple. (TF2 gets tactically complex, but that's irrelevant here.) There's just no ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ to worry about. It's see enemy, shoot enemy, and there isn't a lot getting in the way of that objective. There's also nothing off-putting about the narrative. It's simple. There are also few interruptions in actually playing the damn game. The point is shoot things, and I can get straight to the point.

Basically, TF2 and L4D (and most Valve shooters) are AAA quality games without AAA game ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. And that is what Doom 4 is, too. It flirts with some AAA game ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ (glory kills are kind of like QTEs, sort of?) but turns it down so far you hardly even notice. Little enough of a bad idea can be a good idea.

Doom was a game about shooting monsters. Who cared why? That's what Doom 4 is. You shoot monsters. There's a thin story there, but you shoot ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ monsters. The story doesn't get in the way of shooting monsters, and there's no sitting back and letting the computer show off your graphics card while you stop shooting monsters for a moment.

Oh, speaking of graphics cards: Doom 4 runs on weak hardware. There are a ton of options to adjust to get the frame rate to your liking. You can run it at ridiculously low res, with the resolution halved below that if you like. I can attest to it running playably on my Geforce GT 650M with an i5 3210 and 8 gigs of ram. It ran even better on my wife's i7 6700T with 16 gigs and HD 530 graphics. (I guess at the low end it ends up being more constrained by CPU?)

Basically, if your computer is less than 5 years old, you can almost certainly find a setting to run this game at around 30 fps. And you'd probably WANT to run this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"RIP AND TEAR, UNTIL ITS DONE"

For the context: i've never been a fan of DOOM series.
I knew how much the series did for First Person Shooters, it started the genre, but i just didn't like the game's style and i was way too late to the party with DOOM 3 which didn't aged well when my PC could handle it.
Also i was a ♥♥♥♥♥ who was scared of spooky demons.

This game, however, completely changed my mind.
This game is amazing. Its one of the best first person shooters i've ever played. Even better than Bulletstorm, which i absolutely adore.
Its incredibly fun, stylish and is just filled with love and appreciation for the original games, as i later found out.

This is one of the best games of this generation, of this genre and in general.
Its fun, its badass and its legit challenging on higher difficulties. Casuals beware, because even the lowest difficulty might not provide 100% immunity to deaths.

The only 2 cons of this game are:
  • 1) Extremely high system requirements. Even systems above average will struggle with keeping this game above 60 FPS with high graphical settings.
    I highly recommend to compare your system to the requirements before buying the game, and after buying and installing the game - google optimisation guides and fiddle with the settings a bit at the start of the game.
    First room of the game already puts the system to the test and its the perfect place to fiddle with the settings in order to find acceptable performance-to-quality ratio.
  • 2) The multiplayer feels a little bit wonky and unfinished, even unfair sometimes, but its still surprisingly fun (although not as fun as the single player). On my first attempt i've sink like 2-3 hours into it right away in the middle of the night. That counts for something, right?
    The only thing that i find legit annoying is a very weird, near to incomplete and barely working text chat, which means voice chat is the only proper way of talking to your teammates.

Verdict: 10/10 for single player, 8/10 for mutiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t
