\r\n\t\t\t\t\tClassic references galore; fast-paced, excellently thought-out movement and controls; awe-inspiring graphics. This is truly the reincarnation of a classic for the modern generation.

This is such an excellent game, I dont even know where to begin...Doom4 is like, if Stanley Kubrik directed and wrote the original Doom back in '93, then J.J. Abrahams got a hold of it this year and filled it up with modern effects and action-sequences, and added a story that made a bit of sense. It's that amazing and respectful to its origin, truly.

It pays ohmage to the originals (Doom & Doom2), as well as Doom3 (not it's clunkiness, its story). I'm not sure how many of you will be familiar with the originals...but...there really wasn't any kind of story to speak of. Doom3 was inherently cinematic...and poorly executed, but lets not talk about that. Point being, Doom4 is an excellent convergence of the originals gameplay and 3's story-driven cinema.

Starkly contrasted with 3, 4 doesn't rely on horror-house scare tactics (dark rooms and flashlights) to intensify gameplay...it's simply overwhelmingly intense, and thats enough to scare you, believe me. After you run around a football-field sized arena 3-stories tall for twenty minutes, demolishing dozens of hellspawn, the one you didn't see in your blind-spot will jump up behind you and kill you before you have a chance to get your reticule on him - makes me jump out of my chair every time, and return to the beginning of the fight with renewed fervor, drooling for revenge. "That little bastard is gonna get it this time!"

The guns, oy vey! All the guns that originally appeared in Doom 1 are here, along with about a dozen additions. Harken back to the old days with the Combat Shotgun or Rocket Launcher or Plasma Rifle (still has its slow, plasma-ball projectiles, which was revolutionary in Doom1 - https://youtu.be/6A4-SVUHQYI ), then realize the power of the new Gauss Cannon trained on a Cacodemon, or use a grenade to finish off a crowd of zombies. Fantastic array of weaponry, second to none!

I see alot of criticism of multiplayer, but I dont understand why... Doom 4's multiplayer is modeled a bit after CoD's multiplayer, with the ability to customize armor and weapons feeling very CoD-ish, and after the match the 3 best players of the round standing on pedestals and taunting the rest. The stats and the scoring and the rewards all feel highly reminiscent of CoD. But, the maps, the weapons, the gameplay, and the demon-runes all set it far apart from any CoD.

Try camping in a multiplayer round of Doom 4, and I will be amazed if you last longer than 30 seconds. Snipes are the easiest targets ever! Their rifle gives off a level-wide laser, which lets you find them easily no matter where they are. the key to success is NEVER STOP MOVING, get close to your enemy, and always pick up as much armor and health as you possibly can.

One-shot one kill? NEVAR in Doom. Remember, this is a team of Doom Marines against a team of Doom Marines - its like Halo in that way, a round of Master-Chiefs all fighting each other. Your character is super-human, so it takes a bit more effort than usual to put him down. Jump off a 50-foot cliff - who cares? Your armor absorbs the impact, like Halo. So...you might think of it as Master-Chief in a round of CoD that takes place in Hell (or on Mars) with Doom weapons....and dont forget the demon runes!

Does the game use a player-host model? Yes, unfortunately. Are there cheaters, slow lag-filled games, and people studdering across the screen? Yes, sometimes, however infrequent in my experience. But these are growing pains that every good multiplayer FPS has had to deal with, and I'm certain the folks at Id will resolve these issues in due time. Despite the model's imperfections, I still have logged almost all of my 100+ hours in multiplayer. The original game has a LOT of replay value, and I've played through it twice, working on insane-o-flux difficulty right now for the third time through...Nightmare I think it's called. But multiplayer keeps me coming back constantly...

All-in-all, this is easily one of my most-favortist games ever. It's excellent, and if you don't buy it, you're a dummy, because there's not a very compelling reason not to...it's worth every penny! Unless you dont like violence, then this is definately not your bag...and obviously, you always should be paying very close attention to system requirements. Realize that mobile graphics cards are like half-rated - a GTX960m is the same as a GTX540 in a desktop. I didnt realize that at first, and tried putting this on my laptop (with a GTX960m) to no avail. Here is a plethora of reasons why that happened http://gpu.userbenchmark.com/Compare/Nvidia-GTX-960M-vs-Nvidia-GTX-670/m27242vs2181 . Despite that frustration, the game runs FLAWLESSLY on my desktop with a GTX760.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom, in my opinion, is probably the best game of the current generation. A game that's so bold and confident in what it does that its old school 90s design looks and feels new after well over a decade of FPS trying to be "realistic". While very few FPS managed to make the best use of modern design elements, most of them in my opinion are dull, boring, have aged poorly and are doing nothing other than following a trend set by the popularity of Call of Duty, especially when a game in a franchise decided to follow suit with that old fad imlookingatyourresistance2andfear3. Those games have easily become stale and it's about time we finally had a game that shook the genre up after it went stagnant for years with modern military crap and along with titles like Overwatch, Doom has absolutely shook up the FPS genre with almost universally grand results. This is a steam review so I'm going to keep it simple by breaking it down into pros and cons:

Pros:
- Tons of content; lengthy and replayable campaign, fun multiplayer and a robust level editor with more free stuff on the way
- Excellent, fast-paced and meaty gunplay
- Awesome weapons and alternative fires
- Incredible balance, every weapon, enemy and map just feels so right - it's a game that delivers the perfect amount of punch, challenge and reward
- No story; it's just you, your gun and lots of demons from hell
- NO REGENERATING HEALTH, I love health packs <3
- Hyperspace arsenal; you can carry every single weapon in the campaign and most snapmap levels and you'll have to find ammo scattered throughout the levels
- No ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hitscan enemies - everything is fair and can be dodged, it's a game that truly values player skill
- Badass and unique demons to fight
- Great level design in campaign and multiplayer
- Amazing visuals
- Beautiful aesthetic, the dark oppressive environments makes the action feel even more awesome
- Superbly optimised and runs like a dream, I've never dropped below 60fps once
- Amazing soundtrack, very cool reinterpretations of the classic Doom music
- Lore is deep and engaging and full of references from previous Doom games
- Upgrades that actually matter (no boring stat boosts here)
- Lots of fun secrets
- Very fun and well-balanced MP (the balance was busted on launch and has been through a lot of tweaking and it feels great to play now)
- Snapmap is excellent and has a lot of UGC to play with friends be it deathmatch or campaign levels or something truly creative and awesome like how someone recreated Tetris for example
- Epic player customisation for both multiplayer and snapmap, lots of armors and colours to choose from
- id are incredibly supportive and add new free content on an almost monthly basis
- The DLC model for selling maps is actually genius, while you only get the cosmetics, weapons and demons everyone else can also play the DLC maps as long as at least one person in a public or private has bought the DLC; if you buy the DLC then you're also buying those maps for others to play on too so there is no community split
- And just so much more, there's way too much going on in this game and the campaign is worth the asking price alone

Cons:
- Occasional unskippable cutscene in the campaign (but not arcade mode), they're thankfully few and far between but they do suck
- Snapmap could do with more content and functionality but it's getting there
- The multiplayer may not appeal to those looking for classic arena shooter action
- Loadouts in multiplayer, I'm ok with them but I would like a separate classic mode that adds weapons as pickups
- No mod support, I wish id adds this even if it's the final update in this however long game plan
- DLC is too pricey for my liking and I feel that the multiplayer Cacodemon sold in Hell Followed is too OP, just lower their health to about 350
- Bots have been added but they are wonky and only work on non-DLC maps in DM and TDM only; hopefully id can make them playable on all maps and modes.
- This may or may not be a con but for some people, performance is almost unplayable for others but it's ran perfectly for me since launch albeit with very rare crashing; it's unpredictable so bear this in mind before buying
- Some people may not enjoy the glory kills but I personally love them because they are short but sweet
- I've noticed people have complained about the arenas in the campaign but they are blissfully short, full of intense action, great design and you're never kept in them for any more than a couple of minutes at a time, which is unlike other "old school" FPS that keep you fighting in the same area for upwards of 5-10 minutes imlookingatyoushadowwarrior2013yabastard
- And... Well, I can't really think of anymore cons because Doom 2016 does so many thing so damn right

Needless to say, I love Doom to absolute death and just like previous Doom games, including Doom 3, it quickly became one of my favourite games of all time and that purity of vision to focus entirely on the awesome action and cutting out needless fluff such as a stupid story and a moody ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ protogonist that nobody cares about was an excellent design decision. Doom is fantastic just the way it is and trying to add a "deep" and "emotional" story to that will only drag the game down because it doesn't need it and that was one of the downfalls of Wolfenstein: The New Order (another id property developed by Machine Games, ex-Starbreeze) and even though that story was fantastic, I feel it really affected the awesome action in a stop-start kind of way.

Doom hits every nail on the head on how to make an excellent FPS and a fantastic reboot as it defies the stale and dated tenants established by modern FPS and proves that 90s FPS design is absolutely timeless, there's a good reason why classic Doom, Duke Nukem 3D, Quake, Blood, Shadow Warrior Original and what-not are considered to be some of the best FPS games of all time and while Doom 2016 borrowed certain elements from modern FPS, it still brought that classic 90s FPS design foward into the modern day and is truly a triumph in modern FPS game design. Doom 2016 is absolutely a must buy and I urge anyone who's debating to get this game to buy it as soon as possible because you're missing out on an amazing title otherwise.\t\t\t\t\t
