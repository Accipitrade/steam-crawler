\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Story in a game is like a story in a porn movie. It's expected to be there, but its not that important." - John Carmack

Doom 2016 (Otherwise known as Doom 4 to others) is a very noble homage to the original Dooms in the 90s while still being a modern take to the franchise. It goes back to simpler times where the story was placed in the back seat, and gameplay and fast paced action was at the front.

Doom 2016 takes this to heart, and its very clear it does so within the first 5 minutes of the game with it beginning with a very simple 30 second cutscene. No dialogue, just a simple smashed head and a trusty pistol to begin annihilating the few enemies around you. The story is there, its explained through dialogue with the few characters in the game. But does the main character care? No, and the game is very open by this by his aggressive actions towards everything. And that's perfectly okay. Level design is also retro with secrets to be found in each level. And yes, some of them are actually very hard to find.

The graphics are horrificly beautiful, and the particle effects are beautiful to look at while the the violence levels are off the charts. There do exist games that are more gorey, but a satisfying level does exist in the game, and depending of what guns you use, change the level of gore. The once controversial glory kills, which was scared to slow the pace of the combat, are brutal and satisfying. 7 hours into the game and they are yet to get old. Yes, a lot of the same animations do repeat and your level of tolerance does change from person to person. But with my experience, they're fun and don't break the game or even hinder it at all. Should be noted that AMD users, such as myself, aren't getting the same level of performance as their Nvidia counterparts. For everyone to be aware, Doom uses OpenGL 4.3 when running on AMD systems while it uses 4.5 on Nvidia systems. According to benchmarks, the difference in performance is quite significant with one even showing the 960 beating my R9 390. Though, this may change in future updates and I can play some parts of the game completely maxed out with 60FPS. But it does drop a lot in some sections, sometimes to 20. The max I see on max settings is 100 fps. So that 80fps range is quite noticeable on my system. (This is dependent if the scene is in or out doors, and the number of enemies and particles. My game runs better when outdoors)

If you expecting anything similar to Doom 3 then you won't really find it here. Horror really isn't a significant factor in this version of doom. I will be the first to admit I completely lose it with horror games, so the fact I was completely okay playing this game just shows how not scary it is. So if you're looking for that, then this game isn't for you.

Also with the multiplayer, if you didn't like the beta, then just steer clear of it since you already know what to expect.

Overall, I'm impressed. I did get worried when they refused to give review copies, but overall they had nothing to worry about. They created a fun game which lived up to my expectations. I will edit this review when I explore Snapmap more and the updates are push through either the game or AMDs side to talk about the performance increases that will come in the future. This is a very safe buy.

Rip and tear.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Doom" is a remake of a very popular FPS (French Playground Simulator) named "Buckaroo Larry's Win Handsome Fun Zone" that was first released for the Commodore 64 back in 1974. It won many awards and critical acclaims from magazines such as "Computers And Stuff That Has Wires Attached to them Monthly." which described it as "amazing... I never imagined I would some day be able to mow my lawn in space, all from the convenience of my home computer machine!" Copies were sold at Babbages for $1.99, and if you purchased two of them, you would receive a cloth map showing Richard Garriott's face.

While this new "Doom" is quite different from its precessor, it does share many similiar features. For example, your character can move (I believe they call it "running" or "walking"). There are guns in this game, but I don't know what you're supposed to do with them or why you need them because I was homeschooled and live in a series of closets connected by a central hallway. I asked my friend Jeff K. what the plot of the game was, and although I coun't exactly understand him (I was blowdrying my hair), it sounded something like this:

"You... guy. Space guy. Make good (cramwitch) from a (fist slug forgery?). So NO NO NO don't do that, you need the Buttersworth! Yes. Parp. It's grunty (Fat Louie?)"

Sorry I couldn't be of more help, but it was late at night and I did not want to wake up mother because she might lock me in the training box if she heard me. Overall, I would recommend this game 9/10, but please make sure you have a PC or something that can play it before you buy it (this has happened to me so many times I stopped counting).
