\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll of the positive qualities of older shooters brought up to modern standards of quality, with good graphics, loads of callbacks and references to the classics, and plenty of blood and gore to keep the slaughter going.

Doom 2016 is a callback to a different era, and it proves in no uncertain terms why that era was so much better than the standard FPS fare of today. It's every bit qualified to bear the title of its' predecessor, a game that is effectively the grandfather of first person shooters today.

Doom is set apart not only by the sheer fluidity and ease with which it executes its' fast-paced gameplay, but also, ironically, with the clarity it conveys the Doomguy's character through. Even though he never even so much as grunts, you can understand his character and motivations perfectly through nothing more than camera movements and hand motions, and that alone deserves to be singled out as a point of recognition.

The only criticism I can find for this game is that even the easiest difficulties still expect a lot of you; if most of your experience with FPS games comes from newer titles, you may have something of a learning curve to adjust to, but I promise, if you can get over that, it is entirely worth it.

In summary, an absolutely superb FPS experience, a master-class in show-don't-tell storytelling, and a thoroughly fun time.

Rip and tear, until it is done.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this cause I heard the singleplayer was cool, then I got sucked into multiplayer. Fast paced, arcade arena style battles, with over a handful of game modes and some cool features (such as getting a powerup to become a flying demon with a jetpack and missiles). To me I think the multiplayer feels a lot like classic Doom, but not just that - it also brings back a lot of feelings and memories from Unreal Tournament 99 (and also a tiny bit of Halo, as far as the online competetive red vs blue style arena shooter with aliens and alien weaponry).

The maps are fantastic, the art style is fantastic and pretty, and the game feels fluid. I'm impressed, I hope the community doesn't die out quickly, and the game also adds some leveling up with XP, costume unlocks, and skin customization (both your character and weapons) which adds some depth to the MP experience.

The singleplayer I've played so far (a couple missions) is fun and I felt more closely resembles the original Doom & Doom 2 more than Doom 3 did. Doom 3 felt slow and boring, this feels fast, bloody violent, and colorful. I'll have to come back and give a better review for that in the future.

tl;dr: MP reminds me of UT99 & Halo, SP reminds me of classic Doom (better than Doom 3) - it's good!

edit: A month or two after owning this game, my multiplayer data has vanished and reset to level 0. This is quite dissapointing... I've tried all the suggested fixes online with no avail. This seems to have happened to quite a few other Doom online players. Be warned.\t\t\t\t\t
