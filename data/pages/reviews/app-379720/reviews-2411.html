\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIm really impressed with DOOM 2016. This is how you properly reboot an old videogame series.

Ive been a fan of DOOM since the original like many other fans and I really enjoyed DOOM 2016 for many reasons. The game itself seems to takes inspiration from both 1993 DOOM and DOOM 3 but also has its own original story and plot. The graphics are fantastic and the animations are smooth and fluent. Many of the classic weapons return but brand new weapons are also introduced. Alot of classic demon enemys return and new ones are introduced as well.

The gameplay is from the first person perspective; you run, shoot, strafe around the levels like a classic DOOM experience. The actual movement in the game feels more like im playing quake which is actually a plus in my opinion. The reason it feels more like quake to me is because of the climbing mechanics, strafeing and the double jumping ability you get later on. Glory kills were introduced in this game and they are very satisfying to perform and watch. The powerups return from original DOOM as well as medkits and armor. The game is very challenging on the higher difficultys too.

Mick Gordon really captured the vibe of DOOM with a heavy metal and dark grungy electronic style soundtrack. The sound design itself is also amazing, from the weapons to the demons to the atmosphere sounds and much more.

Highly recommend picking up DOOM 2016. If you want a classic doom experience while playing DOOM 2016 ide recommend turning off all the gui in the settings menu (except maybe the health and armor hud and simple crosshair) and putting the classic DOOM FOV on.

Im pretty hyped for DOOM Eternal as well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 4 manages to balance the thin line of appealing to the hardcore fans of the highly esteemed previous games by bringing over the elements which made them so great, while also adding new concepts that fit in perfectly, resulting in an updated new Doom gameplay experience that reminded me how it felt playing the original Doom for the first time in 1993.

Being a fan of the Doom series (especially the first two), let's see how the new Doom holds up in terms of the aspects which I think made the previous games great:

[+] Gameplay: Excellent. It's faster and wilder, thanks to the new Glory Kill mechanism. The game has also more depth, since there are many optional secrets and unlocks to hunt after (collectibles, runes, suit upgrades, weapon upgrades, weapon challenges, level challenges), inviting the players to explore the maps in-between all the action and just play around a bit to have some fun, preventing the game to become too linear and monotone.

[+] Soundtrack: Great. The composer did overall a really good job and it's clear that he wanted to go the extra mile, providing a soundtrack that adapts to the player's actions, paying tribute to the previous games in a subtle way and even implementing hidden messages in the songs. My only point of critique: The songs lack the catchiness of music the first Doom and Doom 2 had.

[-] Weapons: Since this is a First Person Shooter, the weapons and the feedback they provide in terms of animation, modelling and sound is crucial. And this is where Doom 4 disappoints me: While the indeed wide selection of weapons delivers exactly what every fan of the series expects, their implementation does not feel fun and "satisfying" enough to me. The option to switch between 2 weapon mods is an excellent new idea for refining tactics and adding some variety, but games like Killing Floor 2 or Farcry 3 did a way, way better job in terms of modelling, sound and animation. The famous fan mod "Brutal Doom" or the indie game Project Warlock are other good examples for how weapons can feel more gritty and satisfying in a First Person Shooter. Personally, I wish that at least the designs of the already series-established weapons would be closer to the original designs of Doom and Doom 2, since many of them are not only iconic, but they also look better in comparison to the new counterparts, which often look quite generic.

[+] Enemies: As with the weapons, the game delivers exactly what a fan would expect, plus it adds a few nice new additions. My personal point of critique is the same as with the game's weapons: I wish the designs and sound effects of the enemies would be a bit closer to Doom 2. Sadly, Doom 4 excludes 2 enemy types of Doom 2.

[+] Level design: Doom 4 also features well-designed and rewarding secret areas, and the game actively invites the players to explore and find them with a handy 3D map. Every level even features a small portion of a level from the first Doom and Doom 2 as hidden easter egg, unlocking the full classic map with old textures to play !

[?] Multiplayer: Can't comment, as I only played the single player mode of Doom 4 so far.

[?] Level edition: Can't comment yet, but it seems to be very easy to use and invites players to create, test and upload their custom maps.

Conclusion
As probably most people were, I was quite sceptical when Doom 4 was announced, as the old games are rightfully not only considered classics, but video game milestones. I still replay them to this day -- granted, with updated graphics and controls. Doom 4 managed to pull off the seemingly impossible task of not only pleasing the hardcore fans, but even surpassing everyone's expectations. The developer was clever (and very likely fans themselves) enough to look back and understand what the previous games' strengths are, keeping them alive and also adding new features that fit seamlessly to the previously established Doom game formula. Doom 4 indeed feels like the series has been reborn (but that's still no excuse that the official title drops the number "4" -- I hate it when a game or movie series do that, as it adds unnecessary confusion in discussions).

9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS IS WHAT ALL GAMES SHOULD BE LIKE, WORTH EVERY CENT

I just finished the singleplayer campaign on ultra violence, loved every second of it. The game is action packed and has some pretty good mechanics. I would not put it up to where Doom 2 is in epicness, but this is due to no fault of the DOOM game itself.

I loved the Glory Kills, I loved the guns, I loved the enemies and I loved the visuals and sounds. But I really loved the Glory Kills. I think if Doom 2 does one thing worse than this game, its the lack of Glory Kills. DOOM has very little of the usual movement slowness that has infested modern FPS and none of the whack-em-all cover mechanics. I think the game marries the low weapon count of modern FPS with the high weapon variety of old games pretty well; you have good weapon variety but limited ammo types. The upgrade system definitely adds to the experience, though I felt some abilities were mandatory to use if you wished to have success.

I played on nearly all graphics settings low, yet the game looked gorgeous. There is some loading at the beginning of each level, which in the beginning led me to believe my graphics card is not up to the task. After realizing this, not for a moment did I think on upping the graphics; the game just kept me going until I was done with the campaign.

The campaign itself is just the right length. Environments are great, though I hugely prefer the hell levels. Map design feels a bit too linear to feel Doom-like enough for me and teleport spawning enemies is not really my thing, but I think both keep a fast pace in the game which I'm a huge fan of.

Bosses are actually pretty nice, though maybe my opinion will change once I have played on higher difficulties. I'm somewhat reserved considering some of the attacks they use. There was also a big spike in difficulty in the last 2 levels, I'm not entirely sure why this was deemed OK. Personally I would have preferred a more gradual increase to the same level of difficulty.

I read very little of the Codex, I wasn't hugely impressed by it or by the story itself. But none of it was necessary to read or watch and it was never in my face so I can't complain. I play games for the gameplay, so I'm not really bothered by an average or below average game story. Though there definitely are great moments to be enjoyed, such as the doom guy destroying various expensive equipment and so on.\t\t\t\t\t
