\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, the new Doom is beautiful, fast, exciting, feature-dense, modifiable and one of the slickest games I've played in a while. Moreover, it does a good job maintaining the francise name and the feel of Doom. And I'm pretty sure it's more evil than previous releases as well.

That being said, there is one thing that bothered me about this release. It wasn't the $59.99 price tag, but it was the intense push in every forum, blog, advertisement, review, whatever.. it seemed that if it was about Doom, is was about how you needed a GTX9XX series card to even come close to being able to run it.

Well, don't go rushing out for the new Geforce GTX9XX or, now, GTX10XX card to be able to run Doom. I have the following:
3rd gen i3
GTX660
16GB fast ram

That's it. It runs, on ULTRA graphics setting, 25-45fps (mostly around 31-32fps and lowest at 25.7fps) and this had zero to negligible affect on the ultra graphics setting. No lag, no choppiness, no rubber banding, smooth as silk, actually. Running Windows 7 pro 64bit, btw.

So don't listem to the hype just yet about all the new video cards, my 3 year old GTX660 does just fine... put that money towards a PS4 instead and play some Uncharted 4....\t\t\t\t\t
