\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA truely great reboot. Like most gamers I was mantaining cautious optimism about a hit classic like DOOM being rebooted in the modern age. After playing through the campaign several times I now see that my concerns were not warranted. Gameplay is fluid, fast, responsive and unforgiving. All weapons are fun to use and upgrade. Environments (the later Hell levels not withstanding) are gushing with atmosphere and detail. Enemies are varied and unique across the roster. All levels reward exploration and have an almost intimidating sense of verticality. The game includes plenty of fan-service and even includes the original DOOM levels as secrets for you to discover as you play. Lore is a big part of the game as well, but unlike DOOM 3 its not a requirement to enjoy or play the game. Its simply there if you want to learn more about the game world and its well written, tying all of the DOOM games together in interesting ways. Multiplayer is included, but I never spent any time with it. In my opinion the game is worth the price of admission purely for its single player.

Perhaps the only criticism I have of the game is the fact that the later Hell levels are a bit weak in the atmosphere department. The pre-release gameply demo had a lot more ambient fog/thunder which really drove things home, but the Hell in this version seems a bit dry and underwhelming.

Beyond that, the game maintains a repetitive producure from start to finish in which you slug through levels with light resistnace up until you get to kill rooms (which are easy to spot), in which the doors lock behind you and waves upon waves of increasingly powerful foes spawn everywhere you arent standing and try to kill you. Aside from boss fights there is essentially no deviation from this formula, and while the enemies and rooms you fight in are always varied to keep the game fresh, the pure repetition will no doubt wear thin on some players.

Beyond that, this is a fantastic game and I would reccomend it to anyone that enjoys first person shooters.\t\t\t\t\t
