\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I recommend this game because I did have a good time playing it but it is not without some annoying flaws. I would also say it's a must-buy if you are a die-hard FPS fan or just a big fan of the other Doom games. The game is not great solely on its FPS gameplay, but rather from the combination of gamepaly with the atmosphere. Many of the mistakes made in Doom 3 have been corrected, but it is not as fun as the Doom 1 and 2.

Graphics (9/10): The environments are impressively detailed. This is a major boon for the game because it really adds to the feel and immersion. The game is genuinely creepy in some parts. The enemies are a bit less detailed, they still look good but are a little on the waxy side.

Music/SFX (7/10): Above average, and entirely adequate but could have been better. About 50% of the music is kind of "just there" background music. The other half, usually reserved for intense fight sequences is pretty good heavy metal style music that definitely adds to the immersion. My big complaint with the sound effects is that the demon noises are really uninspired, all they do is just roar or growl. This is something I immediately missed from previous Doom games, where each demon would have its on distinct sounds and a lot of them were very creepy!

Story (8/10): This game lucks out the fact that the bar is so low for video games. The story itself is nothing particularly original, but at the very least the diaglogue isn't written like a grade school play (like 98% of all video games). The plot comes from a combination of dialogue with a character via radio, various data logs you find, and these chirpy little corporate holograms that exist everywhere in the game except when your in Hell. It does not really make sense for them to be in some of the places they are in at the end of the game...but..whatever.

Controls (9/10): I'll be generous in this category because Doom is classically a PC game and therefore one should expect that mouse/keyboard is the default. But, the controller support is very limited (Xbox360 only) which is really disappointing for a modern game. The controls are responsive and they avoided an excessive number of keybindings that is common for modern FPS but would really be out of step with the Doom style.

Gameplay (7/10): To put it straightfowardly, the game is laid out like this: You spend some time exploring, you go into a room where the doors lock behind you and a bunch of demons pop out of nowhere, you kill them and then proceed to explore some more. So of course, this is not as fun as and not what fans of the first two Doom games would be hoping for. On the other hand, it is WAY better than where they went with Doom 3 where you were on a rail and only fought usually one, maybe up to three enemies at a time. At least when you do fight, you fight A LOT of demons. But it does become laughably predictable: before every "arena" is a bunch of ammo, armor, and health and you'll get a "checkpoint" notification. At which point you say "oh boy, the next room is a fight". In the beginning this is fine because you will be so enamored by the overkill gore, the environment, and the rapidity of the fighting. But by the end of the game it gets very monotonous and honestly I couldn't wait to just get through the last two levels to finish the game.
The second thing to know is that the combat has been designed to be very acrobatic. You are given double jump early in the game and most of the arenas you fight in will have jump pads. The demons will also come at you much faster than any previous Doom game, so essentially you are to NEVER stand still. It makes the fighting intense, sure, but it takes away from the gameplay for me because you are never able to utilize your surroundings or your own movement to gain a tactical position, beacuse there are NO tactical positions ( should also mention that every enemy can instantly climb or leap onto any ledge). But to the point, you die frequently in fights simply because you stopped moving for more than 0.3 seconds.

Summary: 40/50, way better than Doom 3 but not as good as the first two.

P.S. Don't buy this game for the multiplayer. It's alright, not great, just OK. But my review is already too long.\t\t\t\t\t
