\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM has provided one of the most fun experiences that I've had in a long time. The game, from start to finish, maintains a breakneck pace which helps to move things along and keep all of the increasingly long one-room demon fights interesting/engaging. The guns (other than the pistol, which is useless), all have their own specific uses, and you'll be switching back and forth often. The movement is fast and fluid. The glory kill system is both a fun way of brutalizing the demons as well as forcing the player up close to their foes, rather than taking shots from a distance. And I don't use the term brutalize lightly, because DOOM goes in and it goes hard. This game is gritty and visceral, allowing for some of the most intense fighting ever programmed. If you ever wanted to feel like a complete badass, then play this game right now. Absolutely worth the price, and absolutely earned my praise.

One man up against all of Hell? They don't stand a chance.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played the original Doom games and loved them. I wasn't sure if Doom 2016 would measure up but it turned out to be a fantastic modernization of the franchise.

The gameplay is some of the best I've experienced in an FPS. Everything controls smoothly and aiming is a dream. Normally I dislike platforming in an FPS, though I'll tolerate it if the game has a cool story like Metroid Prime. Doom has some platforming, but it wasn't much of a problem. You can upgrade your suit to make movement much easier and generally I didn't have any trouble until the Argent Energy Tower level. I also loved the boss fights as they provided a good challenge and a lot of variety. Doom is a bit different from its predecessor, however, as it's more of an arena shooter. However, many of the maps are fairly interesting, especially those in the beginning.

The story is kind of dumb and predictable, but I never played Doom for the story. I read some of the texts, but mostly I focused on blasting demons to bits.

The music is very, very metal and sounds a lot like some of the tunes in the first Mortal Kombat movie so they fit the game perfectly.

Lastly, the graphics look fantastic, though I don't have that many newer FPS games so I can't say if they are superior to other games that came out in 2016. The style worked well for me and I loved the gore effects.

This is a fun, fast-paced shooter that doesn't get bogged down with stuff like cut scenes, QTEs and audio log collections. Just like the original Doom, it's all about the action.
