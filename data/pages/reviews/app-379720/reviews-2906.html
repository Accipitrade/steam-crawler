\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo to start off i stopped playing FPS games shortly after halo 4 came out, COD, Halo and other games just felt slow even though in halo your a 9 ft tall super human, you feel slow. I bought Doom 2016 during the black friday sale but didn't start it till around christmas...i have no idea what iw as waiting for. I payed $30 for it, hell I'd pay the full $60 now and i'm not even done with it.

when you start off you arnt armored and all you get is your pistol, but quickly after you find your marine armor and they through a shotgun in your hands and say "welcome to DOOM M***** F*****" the combat is quick and fluid, back to the old days of if you stand still you're dead. clever use of environment and and "glory kills" will keep you alive. even on the highest available difficulty from the start it doesn't feel dauntingly impossible like COD on veteran does when you poke your head out for a second and die. once you start getting weapon mods and your double jump boots you'll be flying around an area just ready to destroy the waves of demons they throw at you.

like all games things get progressively harder, i've started on ultra violence which i think is the highest possible for first play through, and it just feels "right". even if i die 9/10 its my fault i dont rage or feel cheated "back to COD" once you get the hang of the fluidity and speed at which you're moving since there is no sprint you start making plans about how you're going to handle the current set of enemies spawning, only to have stronger ones spawn in ready to put a dent in that plan. which brings me to the guns.

they seem somewhat standard but they're fun, i find myself using the shotgun as my bread and butter saving ammo with the pistol for the weakest of enemies. they don't skimp on the ammo supplies for all you're guns save the BFG like they do in other fps games. if you want to use your rocket launcher you go right ahead and obliterate that enemy before he even sees you. each weapon has 2 mods that you can switch to with R, no reloading just keep going keeps you in the action. you can mod your shotgun to shoot a grenade round, or a 3 round burst by holding right click, turn your assault rifle into a missle launcher. pretty soon you'll know what to use for which enemy to effectively kill everything before they do you. aside from the plenty of ammo lying around you have 1 fall back weapon next to your pistol, a chainsaw. the chain saw can kill most if not all enemies(havent tested some) and when you do an explosion of ammo for all of your current guns will spray out of the enemy you just sawed in half making sure you dont stagnate and resort to pistol pot shots to get through an encounter.

lastly the attitude, if you're a fan of heavy metal get this game, the sound track is great, the main characters attitude tword his situation is a much needed change compared to other games, he doesn't care what your problem is just get out of the way and let him handle it. I was pleasantly surprised by this game and think it breathes renewed life into the genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHoly s**t! Doom got a reboot and its actually one of the most refreshing first person shooters in a long time. The good people at id Software went back to the roots of this fabled franchise to figure out what made it work so well. Unlike Doom 3 where they tried to reinvent the franchise, the reboot is a very tightly constructed spiritual successor all gussied up for the latest generation of gaming. The story isnt important, there are demons on Mars and they need to be dead, like really dead, dead as s**t and youre are going to do that because you are Doomguy. Thats right your character doesnt even have a name he just kills violently and efficiently as he sprints around like an Ethiopian marathon runner on cocaine wearing power armor and wielding enough weapons to perform a hostile takeover of a large town. There are explosions, fire, and blood everywhere but none of it matters to you because you are Doomguy and he thrives on it all. There is a pure zen I find in playing this game that I usually only experience in racing games and certain Dark Souls boss fights. New enemies bring new challenges and withstand more punishment but new weapons expand your versatility to keep up with them. Id also like to mention how freaking awesome the weapons are in this game, like I get excited every time I find a new one like I cant wait to kill something with this. Doom is the shot of adrenaline that the FPS genre desperately needed and if youre sick of hiding behind chest-high walls waiting for the blood on your screen to disappear before you can actually play again then this game will cure what ails you.\t\t\t\t\t
