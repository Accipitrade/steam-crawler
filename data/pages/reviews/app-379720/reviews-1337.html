\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can easily recommend this game if you like FPS games, gory games, great heavy metal soundtracks and if you were a fan of DOOM in general.

Gameplay is smooth, graphics are beautiful and it runs like a charm in even the most budget builds (at least 60 fps). I was very interested in the game since, when it was released, it was a benchmark for Vulkan for my GPU (AMD RX 480) and the fact that it could run the game at max settings at well over 150 fps sold me the game. I finished almost in one sitting.

However, I do have to point out some flaws:

- Gameplay, even though satisfying it got a little stale going towards the end, the same mobs, the same mechanics and basically the same gameplay until the end. But, since the game is relatively short (only 13 levels) it compensates a bit;
- Replayability is not something I do abide for in this game, the bosses were easy (even at the hardest difficulty at the moment) making sometimes the overwhelming number of enemies the real challenge but, after you get the BFG is just a matter of waiting for the bigger mobs and whipping them off;
- Game was FULL of bugs, I clipped through the floor multiple times with the chainsaw, I got stuck in walls, enemies got stuck in the ceiling and the blur effect didn't disappear near the end of the game which was easily fixed with a restart. The game crashed a couple of times while alt tabing too
- The environments while trying to be varied were mostly all the same style: mars; lab/ship environment; Or Hellish places. Made it a little stale, however, the graphics are beautiful and made the spaces look not so dull to look at, even if repeated
- The story is VERY weak, obviously hinting towards a sequel (DOOM Eternal) because the true villain was obviously not dealt with, I was waiting to get him in this game but that's ok.

I give this game a solid recommendation and a solid 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
