\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: This is a review of the single player campaign. I have not tried multiplayer nor SnapMap.

Tl;dr: Fantastic return of DOOM, with one of the best single player campaigns in years. Very difficult to find faults in the single player campaign.

Pros:
+ Fun gameplay, both in shooting and exploring
+ Awesome music by Mick Gordon (especially if you're into metal)
+ Badass protagonist, whose expressions and movements more than make up for his silence.
+ Huge levels with lots of collectables
+ Many graphical options
+ Beautiful and metal graphics/artstyle
+ Very interesting backstory, which is completly optional (it is not shoved in front of you. You have the option to read the backstory in the database menu, and it's actually quite an interesting read)

Cons:
- The look of the hidden classic DOOM maps don't blend well with this game's otherwise beautiful graphics
- Grenades are kinda pointless, as I never had much use of them (nitpick)
- No options for mods (I'm not really a modder, but I have had much enjoyment of the mods for Doom 1 and 2).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere to begin....

When I heard about this game at first I wasn't exactly on the hype train. I had played the "Survival Horror" Doom 3 and never got the chance to play the originals save for on a broken android SNES emulator.

Well, when the Great Warframe Content Draught began, I was desperate for a new title. Something that could captivate me as well as Warframe had. Doom happened to be on sale. The stars aligned.

I would have paid full price for this, easily. Its...♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ gaming perfection.

It keeps you on your toes, has lots of interesting secrets and satisfying weaponry. The mechanics are very fluid, despite how it seems on the surface with an antiquated pickup-based system. It executes it very very well.

Everything from the symbolism of Doomguy being representative of the player playing doom to the reboots of classic demons in modern realistic style and the immensely powerful weaponry is just perfect.

Bonus points for Reinhardt's voice actor playing Evil Chappie (Whom I look forward to killing).

My only complaint is that the BFG only had three shots, not expandable with ammo cap upgrades, and BFG ammo was only really prevalent in the last two levels. That satisfying heartbeat sound as you pull the trigger though...I can't un-hear that auditory wonder.

I really, really hope this game sees GOTY status, its easily the best game that's come out this year. I look forward to trying out the snapmap mode, really hoping to see what custom campaigns are out for this game (and if I get to BFG some more things at point-blank range).\t\t\t\t\t

but......

It doesn't: "corrupted save files"

I read somewhere that an automatic game update is the cause of this, but I really fail to understand why this is supposed to be relevant. When or who or what broke my game doesn't really fix anything, does it?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: This game must not be too bad for me to be coming back to it. I think it runs a bit better. The issues mentioned are real, perhaps a bit hysterical. There's plenty of good reviews for this game, so I'll leave this unedited negative review.

Well multiplayer is multiplayer, lack of mod support, dog $hit map editor, singleplayer is what you bought this game for.

It boils down to being an utterly bland arena shooter. The beginning of the game seems well paced and tight, a mix of corridors filled with enemies and short arenas, and as you start to max weapon mods and get new weapons you see that the game just throws cheap boring bullet sponges at you and you are using an arsenal of weapons that are laughably and insanely unbalanced and simply not fun to wield. A lot of the weapons seem to end up having similar DPS and burst capability simultaneously. The plasma pistol is worthless past level 3 and arguably sooner, the shotgun becomes a wait-to-fire potato gun, the assault rifle becomes a kaleidoscope, the plasma rifle never ends up scaling up at all and simply becomes underpowered, the rocket launcher becomes a potato gun with greater blast radius, the super shotgun I actually don't take issue with as it seems to be perfectly balanced in and of itself but perhaps it should consume more ammo, the chainsaw is just plain weird because you always have plenty of ammo for everything and this weapon is only designed to help you kill an enemy or two to replenish ammo, the gauss cannon will be your noob toob late game and the chaingun just wastes ammo. The BFG I can't find any proper times to use as there are never enough and/or powerful enough enemies on screen at once that you could justify a single shot, I suppose you waste all that potential damage enough to get yourself out of hairy situations just enough to justify all the ammo lying around for it.

Exploration was fine until they started randomly walling off parts of the game you couldn't go back to.

The game technically is an amateur flop and becomes unplayable late game and that is the main reason this game has become hated by me. Those random slowdowns which come usually after changing graphics up or down or reloading checkpoints or sometimes just randomly, and persist until you exit the game and restart.

Oh and the music is angsty and uninspired, mostly ambience and "wub."\t\t\t\t\t
