\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fine arcade FPS for its price in 2020. Pretty short though, about 8 hours to complete the story. Gameplay is overall very solid, if a tad repetitive. The levels only basically have three different themes with minor variations as well, not including some slightly more unique brief gaps between areas. Still, looks good, sounds good, and runs good, for what it offers.

But because I like to complain, the game does have some minor weird/frustrating design decisions. And I do mean they're fairly minor, personal pet peeves even, but they did irk me enough to point out a few:

Such as locking you out of secret areas (that might contain stuff like runes which offer fairly good upgrades) for doing stuff too early, like from opening a random door, which locks all doors behind you, meaning you need to be constantly sniffing out for secret areas before touching new areas, because the next door could mean no return and you need to replay the entire level if you want to get the secrets you got locked out on. (Seeing some of the secrets' locations on the map doesn't always help, because the secret could be shown to be in the next area, but access to it in the current area, so you might run towards the secret, only to lock yourself out of the access to it.) Surely the developers could've thought of better incentives to replay levels than artificial methods like this. Denying backtracking because of uninformed decisions is such a 90's concept.

Another thing is how getting your toe sliced by a security laser insta-kills you (funny, but not the problem here), while falling into a lava pit means you're spending 15 seconds wading in the lava, sloooowwwlyyy getting your health drained while there is no way out of the pit, then watching an additional 5 second 'burning to death' animation. Why not make the pit insta-death too if it's a guaranteed death in the first place? Luckily there are only a few of these in the game, but make no mistake, I spent my fair share in them because I like to (try to) parkour.

And then there's the default binding of switching weapon mods on 'R' key on keyboard. Seriously, 'R' is universally the reload key in FPS games, and all of the other games condition you to press 'R' before the next fight and generally between fights too when you take cover. Sure, there is no reloading in DOOM, and you can rebind the key, but who thought 'R' would be a great choice for a default? There are plenty of other unused keys nearby that you don't press on instinct causing unwanted actions to happen. Don't be stubborn trying to get used to it like me, just rebind it right away. :p

But considering that I can only complain about trivial stuff like that, the game must be doing a lot of stuff right. I still ended up wasting a fair bit of extra time to get most single player collectibles/secrets/achievements and replaying all levels on the next difficulty level. The game just flows nicely for killing time. An 8/10 experience, I'd say.

Definitely makes me want to try DOOM Eternal too, eventually.\t\t\t\t\t
