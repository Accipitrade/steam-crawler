\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't get me wrong, this game is okay. Just not great/ "game of the year" material.

Pros:
1. Beautiful visuals
2. Okay level design (this is sort of also a con)
3. Snapmap. This is actually fun creating maps and playing maps with others.
4. Good player weapon variety.
5. EXCELLENT soundtrack. I was actually quite suprised how good the rock and electronic mix together.
6. Weapon upgardes. These add quite a variety to cater to different playstyles.

Cons:
1. Too much action, even for a Doom game. It's just one zone of demons to the next, with barely any sort of actual intelectual thinking happening in between as you do nothing more than left click with the occasional Q and weapon switch.
2. Pretty boring once you get to later sections of the game. This is because of constant repetative and mind boggling shooting over and over again.
3. Level design can be dumb/ platforming is nothing more than a chore
4. Too long campain. This game doesn't even have a good enough story (which when ever should a DOOM game have a story) to have it this long.
5. id software, why the living ♥♥♥♥ did you include exploding barrels when most of your enemy placement is never near them??!!!

Final Verdict:
7.5/10 for its asking price of $59.99. HOWEVER, since at the time of this review's writing, I purchased it for about $20, I will bump up the score to 8.5/10.

If these games are more of your thing, go ahead and buy it at full price. Me? I like these games, and while I did enjoy some parts of this, I would only spend 20 max on this game.\t\t\t\t\t

I really didn't think much of it, even though it really did look cool.

Fast forward to now, and this is my personal game of the year. Despite my having bought up to 4 new games since I completed this game, I constantly find myself coming back to Doom. I've finished my SECOND playthrough of Ultra-Violence mode, it's hardest difficulty. This game is one of the most polished games in EVERY ASPECT that I've played in YEARS. The combat is absolutely glorious, it never gets old, and never gets dull. It's SO fast paced, and the weapons are satisfying to use. The demons are interesting, and really do stay true to the Doom games of old.

On top of all of that, it is optimized INCREDIBLY well, on both the OpenGL API, and the Vulkan API. If you happen to be a person who is worried that your PC may not meet the requirements to play this game on AT LEAST 1080p/60FPS, I would think again. System requirements are 100% of the time, just a guideline. This game runs so incredibly well, I am very confident you will be able to run it. My system is an Intel Core i7-6700, MSI GTX 1070 Gaming X, 8GB RAM and I can run this game at maximum settings, 1080p, and anywhere from 140-200FPS. No joke. TWO HUNDRED FPS. At maximum settings. That's incredibly well optimized.

Everything about this game is great. I am so proud of id Software. They really did good by the old Doom games. The games that started it all for first person shooters. Well, besides the even earlier Wolfenstein.

I would recommend this game one-hundred times over. And on top of that, steam constantly has it discounted now that it's been out for months. As of writing this interview on December 24th, 2016, you can currently pick this game up for $19.79... that's a 67% discount! There's no reason to not pick this game up if you're a person who's on the fence about whether or not you should play it. I absolutely guarantee you will not regret it!

The only con for this game is it's multiplayer. It's not bad per se, but it definitely is a huge letdown. In today's day and age of multiplayer shooters, it falls flat. It's sort of like Halo, but not as good. You can definitely find fun it in, but in my opinion you will find much more enjoyment from playing the campaign on every difficulty, and taking advantage of the hours and hours of extra content you can get from the user generated Snapmap mode. Tons of people have created incredible user generated campaign stories. You can even play some of the old Doom maps that users have taken upon themselves to recreate in this new Doom world.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a Doom'er at heart. The classics were what got me into the FPS genre when I was growing up, and even as I began to branch out to other genres when I got older, FPSs were still my bread and butter. Some time in the early 2000s though, I began to lose interest in them as a whole, as every game was trying to be like the other.

Then id software returned, first with Wolfenstein: TNO and now DOOM. Finally, FPS games are fun again for me, and I can stop waiting on new WADs to play on my modded version of classic Doom. I only hope that id can be a shining example of how a FPS is made; relying on fast-paced gameplay over the visual gimmicks used in Hollywood.

The Pros:
- Adrenaline-fueled combat of the old games, coupled with the art style of Doom 3. So you get a great mix of action and horror.
- Narrative that just says, "we know what you're here for" and throws you right into the game. Cutscenes are few and far in between, with some story elements being optional from the collectibles found throughout each level.
- Music that fits the atmosphere appropriately. Some familiarity to the classic MIDI soundtrack, and some unforgivenly heavy tracks for the combat.
- Low-key nods to both classic and Doom 3, such as the Soul Cube and the Icon of Sin.
- Secrets a plenty.

The Cons:
- Glory kills, although an essential mechanic for keeping the game's pace, it gets old. Previews and discussions for the game made it a point that there was a lot of variety, when in actuality there isn't.
- SnapMap is quite bland imo, as its design was focused on being accessible to anyone. Single play maps feel too arcade like, and most of the time they're just plain boring.

I haven't touched multiplayer, so I have no pros or cons to say about it. I just wasn't interested.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is hands down the Game Of The Year 2016 for me. It's truly fantastic and highly recommended.

I have been a Doom fan since the original, so maybe I am biased who knows :) But this game is thoroughly entertaining and I enjoyed every second of it.

Things I liked:
  • This game really feels like a successor to the original doom games.
  • Once I started playing the game, I couldn't stop! I played it for a whole weekend and wish I could have more!
  • The combat is extremely fluid and fast paced. It often feels like you're playing a movie!
  • The soundtrack works very well. I often found myself playing and bobbing my head as I blew demons away. Love it!
  • The glory kills are fun and really add a lot to the gameplay
  • All the levels are pretty fun, there are none that I wouldn't want to play again (and I have replayed them!)
  • Visually the game looks fantastic and runs extremely smooth
  • Great performance feedback in the options (geeky opinion I know)
  • Having the ability to replay previous levels to go back and search for secrets etc is a nice feature.
  • The game structure reminds me a little of Painkiller, another game that is great!

Things I disliked:
  • Doom isn't really about the story, however what story there was, wasn't super clear. However I didn't read any of the codecs so that may be down to my own laziness. But the audio and in-game story elements were nice, but maybe there could have been a couple more here and there.
  • The challenges are fun, however the time it takes to retry is frustrating (a few button presses and loading etc)
  • This isn't really a dislike, but nice to have kidna thing - Right now there is no feedback on the difficulty you have finished a level on. It would be nice if on the unlock page for each level, it could have one more criteria which is difficulty. It would be nice to be able to work on each level 1 by 1 to beat the hardest difficulty. Or offer a New Game+ so I can take all my abilities into a new harder difficulty.
  • Some Singleplayer DLC would have been nice. I would love another mission or two.
  • Achievements. Multiplayer achievements kinda suck in all games. I cannot play multiplayer games on my connection so these are out of my reach. If I cannot unlock all achievements I don't really bother trying for any.

Overall the game was a lot of fun and I highly recommend it if you like fast paced, shooters.

The developers did a really great job. Thanks for giving us another great doom!\t\t\t\t\t
