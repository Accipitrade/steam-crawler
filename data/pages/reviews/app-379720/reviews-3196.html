*****VULCAN UPDATE*****

ID Software you Glorious Bastards~!!! You've done it again~!!! So since the Addition of the Vulcan API, DOOM now runs seemingly BETTER than ever! Where I would get random frame drops in certain scenarios and my frames would COMPLETELY TANK below 20FPS in some levels (*cough* VEGA Central Proccessing *cough*), all on LOWEST settings to boot... I can now run the game on High Video settings with Anti Aliasing and Aniostropic Filtering cranked to the max, maintaining a steady 90-120 FPS throughout all levels that used to give my PC Hell before (*buruchi*). There's no better time than now to buy and experience the Gory Glory that is DOOM 2016. I'm now looking forward to another run through campaign, with a smoother and better looking game, thanks ID Software and Bethesda~!!! :3


*****UPDATE*****

On June 30th, 2016... an update was released that seems to have addressed this bug, but I will leave the note here for knowledge purposes (and in case the bug ever comes back another way...).

(ISSUE FIXED) There is currently a common but very serious bug that is currently plaguing this game at the moment, once it has been addressed I will switch this review to recommended as DOOM 2016 is a legitimately good game, but this bug just kills the user experience for me and many others it has affected. You don't even have to use a console command to activate it either, it happened to me when my PC suffered a hardware failure and rebooted, which unfortunately flagged my Save file as being in DEVELOPER MODE somehow and corrupted my save (just when I had beaten the game and was working on the final achievements :( ...). What this does is it Disables ALL achievements as well as multiplayer/snapmap access, with the only known work around being deleting your "savefile", disabling steam cloud save for this game, and relaunching the game. You can then re-enable steam cloud saves and overwrite the corrupted save with the now fixed one, but doing this also resets ALL your progression in both singleplayer AND multiplayer, possibly deleting your snapmaps as well (I had unlocked a few things in multiplayer, but I am now back at square 1, at level 1 and with no custom loadouts, I could feel my blood boiling when I saw this, but, well... I really love this game so I can at least give it a pass... I mean I was planning on starting over on Nightmare anyway...). Regardless, if losing all your progress is something that upsets you greatly then until this bug is patched I HIGHLY RECOMMEND YOU DO NOT PURCHASE this game until ID Software and Bethseda have sorted this bug out and patched it. For more information, please read here:

http://steamcommunity.com/app/379720/discussions/0/357286663672706978/?ctp=55

Now that this bug is out of the way, ONTO THE REVIEW~!!!

******************************************

I really love this game, as a Major fan of the original DOOM since the days that I used to play it on my Dad's Windows 95 back in 1996, I have nothing but fond memories of playing the original DOOM, and I am happy to say that DOOM 2016 to my surprise, turned out as amazing as I had hoped, not only did it live up to it's hype single player wise, but it also delivered a few new (or inspired) mechanics to the formula that neither alienated old fans (IMO) nor kept it the exact same as you remember. This is a new DOOM, but it does the old DOOM really well. Everything you remember from the first DOOM is here:

-Many guns carried at once
-Secret areas and easter eggs
-Fast paced gameplay (though the movement speed is slower than the original)
-Ammo and Health management

Along with new things such as:

-Runes that affect things such as faster glory kills or infinite ammo under certain conditions.
-Swappable weapon modifications.
-Upgrades
-Challenges
-and more...

The best part is, many classic enemies as well as bosses make a return. While the campaign length is not super long, it is sweet and to the point, as well as offering lots of replayability, just the way I like it :3 ... and with the new classic camera option, it feels more like the original DOOM than ever~!!! The only Gripe I honestly have is that getting the glory kill animation you want can be really finnicky so it can frustrating trying to get those specific Glory Kill challenges on specific enemies. But overall I give the Single Player a 9/10 totally worth your time~!!!

******************************************

The multiplayer is not bad, I personally find it fun, though it has been heavily consolized so that you only carry 2 guns in loadout form, and a few (though rather quickly) of the rest of the guns and loadouts are unlocked by leveling up (AKA Typical FPS Progression). While there is some kind of skill gap if you know how to quick swap and fire like in CPMA, or to memorize the power up spawns to give you an edge, it is also a very low skill ceiling thanks to lack of weapon control (since you have weapon loadouts), Overpowered Power Weapons and the Demon rune (though to be fair these are ok, I don't mind playing a low skill arena shooter, and they make it feel different enough), shared ammo, etc...

Now do I think it soils the whole game and devalues the whole package, well... it really depends on how you look at it, but I personally don't think so. The multiplayer is there for you to enjoy if you wanna give it a go, but it's otherwise an optional mode. The progression is nice and at least gives you something to do other than fragging other players. If I had to rate it I'd give it a 8/10, good old mindless fun :3

******************************************

I Tried Doing the SnapMap academy as well as a few custom maps, while they're very interesting and fun to play, this mode also seems Dead compared to the already struggling multiplayer, so unless you intend to make Dedicaded single player Snap Maps, OR you have a friend/some friends to play this with, don't bother with this mode. I really had high hopes with it to be honest, but I guess people just feel it's far too limited to really do anything worthwhile, despite there being really neat things having been done with it already...

Overall, if you can afford it, I don't see why you shouldn't buy this game (except for that bug I mentioned earlier). So come join us, resistance is futile!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWithin the first five minutes of the campaign, I knew this was going to be cool.

After an hour, it's still cool.

I think they did a great job of recapturing the action of classic Doom. It's fast and satisfying, even though I'm extremely rusty at FPS games. It is a bit disappointing that there is no co-op mode, seems like a huge oversight, but I'm still having a blast on my own. The tight maps have lots of multi-level stuff going on, and unlike COD with its invisible walls, here you can jump pretty much anywhere.

It's not perfect, and I'm sure we all have our opinions on what they should or shouldn't have kept from the classics, but overall this is a satisfying romp.

The graphics engine seems pretty good too. I'm seeing 40-50 fps at 2160p Ultra with TSSAA-8x on my GTX 980. Given all the action on screen, that's quite respectable. 1440p gives a solid 60fps throughout. Now, if only my pair of 1080s weren't backordered...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo if you've never played a game like Doom you're gonna really jump into a new experience. Think of Doom like Counter Strike with fast pace, kill or be killed style, just with demons, metal and blood everywhere.. so nothing out of the ordinary.
The soundtrack, gameplay and mechanics are all wonderfully done. You will get a decent amount of gameplay out of the Campaign with some replayability for focusing more on searching for the dozens of collectables throughout the game. Find Classic Doom maps to play or switch to Multiplayer and take it to other players. Its fast paced and actually quite spiced up in the Multiplayer, with Demon Runes being spawned to turn one of the players into a demon or runes like Quad Damage appearing to gain an edge, however for the Multiplayer I do find it sometimes difficult to find a game, leaving me searching for up to 20 minutes. If you are more on the creative side, you can even create your own maps in Snap Maps. This isnt like the Halo games where you have a default template and just put random objects down, you literally create the entire map, with the rooms you want to put in, the various demons or interactives. You can create a mini story of your own to play in Co-Op and publish it to the public and be rated/downloaded.
Overall Doom was really well made and extreamly entertaining.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off being said that FPS isn't my main genre. I just don't enjoy them for any longer period than several hours. This game, however, is quite different...
This is an example of a FPS game made right. It's not by any means complex, innovative, or high techy. It's just plain fun, and it delivers.
Instead of developing new mechanics that end up shooting the game in the leg (*cough* Call of Duty), this game got heavy investment on the simple mechanics, that aren't new at all.
The resault? An old school type game with a very new feel to it. The mechanics are awesome and fun to play. This game isn't friendly, however. It demands you to be reckless, fast, and intensely use your reflexes.
You won't be seated comfortably in your chair playing this. You will be highly stressed and it's actually quite fun. You may even play better when you're actually angry. Try playing some Dark Souls before this and you'll probably do better :)
Don't get me wrong, I absolutely love Dark Souls and it actually made my reflexes better. It's just Dark Souls has a tendency to make you really angry at some points.
Being said that this Doom is the first game I actually love that comes from Bethesda. I've never liked their games before, including their big-hitters such as Dishonor and Skyrim. Their games always seemed unpolished and soul-less. Even seemed quite dead. This however is very different experience, as the game couldn't be more alive. The graphics are very satisfying and the game isn't boring for a second.
Last point is this game is EXTREMELY optimized for PC, as I'm able to run it on ULTRA with a 2 years old GPU (Sapphire Radeon R9 280X Toxic edition). Runs at 60 FPS with drops at certain points but most of the time it's stable.
This really is a game done right.\t\t\t\t\t
