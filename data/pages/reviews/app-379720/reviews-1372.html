\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRip and tear, until it is done.

Disclaimer: Previously owned on PS4.

It's rare for a game to leave a lasting impression on me. What's even more rare is that I decide to re-purchase/replay a game. This game has earned both distinctions from me. Everything about this game left me wanting more.

The environments are well crafted and fit well with the gameplay. The enemies, while all basic variants of 'shoot or stab you do death,' feel unique and polished. Every gun felt good/fun to use; especially given their two unique alternate modes. Melee attacking an enemy is made unique thanks to glory kills. The lore, though retconned from the original games, was incredibly enjoyable despite how shallow it is. At it's core, this game is a bare bones shoot to kill game. Oh and the music is purely astounding. Mick Gordon did an amazing job making a soundtrack that not only matches the game but is metal AF.

Hardware wise; I'm running this game with a 1060ti, 16GB of RAM and an i7 7700. I was able to run the game pretty stably averaging 90 FPS on Ultra settings. I did not experience any bugs, major FPS drops or glitches. The load times with an SSD were only a few seconds. So I was able to rip and tear to my hearts content.

If you buy this game (and you totally you should), you won't regret the decision. While the campaign is short - 11 hours on normal finding all of the secrets - it has a lot of replayability. Between arcade mode, harder difficulties, multiplayer and the ability to replay missions; the only thing you'll be left wanting is more of well, the game.\t\t\t\t\t
