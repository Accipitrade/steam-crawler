\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me begin by saying that, having played the original Doom series, I was unsure as to how this game would turn out. As it happens though, it came out okay-ish, although there are some points I would like to make.

First of all, this game is beautiful. Graphically, the game looks polished, and the settings -both on Mars and in Hell- look brilliant. Whenever a gunfight ensues, the music quickens, turning from a steady heavy metal beat to a heart-pounding hammerfest of a metal rhythm. As regards the actual mechanics of the game, it plays rather well. Glory kills are fun to pull off, and there is such a plethora of weapons and upgrades that you'll never find yourself without a weapon capable of dealing with the current situation.

The issue I do think the game might have though is that the game is lacking any motivation to replay it. Sure, there are a myriad of secrets -field drones, praetor upgrades, Marineguy toys- but they aren't difficult to find even in your first run through. And I think the developers knew this would be an issue, because they've added a multiplayer mode and two unlockable difficulties. Moreover, the story feels forced, particularly ending on a predictable cliffhanger sinceDr Hayden is so obviously a villain its laughable. Finally, the puzzles in each level are rather boring, and get repetitive very quickly.

Overall, many of the positive reviews stem from nostalgia and sentiment of a time long ago, when the original Doom emerged into the gaming scene. Realistically, buy it when its on sale - it isn't worth the full price.\t\t\t\t\t
