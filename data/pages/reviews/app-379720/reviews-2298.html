The weapons and gunplay feel great. The graphics generally look pretty sweet. IT RUNS WELL, giving the user a good level of control over the options so that even a more modest rig can get this game running in a way that's rather enjoyable. So what's the problem? The cinematic takedowns, while they look badass, get repetitive and interrupt gameplay. The AI is unconvincing and immersion-breaking at times. A horde of enemies will very clearly take turns fighting the player, making everything fell somewhat artificial. The other problem is the existence of a game called Shadow Warrior 2. It's an indie reboot of an old series and ends up playing a lot like Doom, but just better across the board. It gives you a huge double jump, infinite dash mechanics, a handful of cool abilities, and tons of different weapons, each upgradable with elemental effects and general badassery, and it's existence makes what was once regarded in my mind as an extremely intense game feel boring.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe environments are kinda pretty, they don't seem to have any discernible logic to them, but they look kinda cool.

The monsters are ambulatory sponges in all shades of brown and tan, barely react to being shot and if they have any intelligence, I have missed it.
Not that they need any, because the main gameplay loop consists of being locked in cramped arenas with a bunch of these and you can leave when you have killed all. It also involves running out of ammo a lot for some reason.
Music consists of ugly unmelodic screeches.

I'm more than halfway through the single player campaign and I can't force myself through any more of this.

If you want an old-school shooter with great gameplay, level design and soundtrack, try the 1993 doom, it's still great.\t\t\t\t\t
