Final Verdict: Buy!

So Doom 2016 is everything we wanted, but also not innovative or a progression in the shooter genre. It is a good game that is well-polished, but not a great game. In essence, it is a really good arena wave shooter with AAA production values and lots of content. That is probably the best thing about Doom 2016 just the sheer amount of good (not great) content it has. It really is value for the money.

Not only is there a lengthy SP campaign with all the cinematics that accompany a modern AAA shooter, but there's MP that's still active and all kinds of unlockable content and challenges. Lots of secrets to find that usually lead to more content. Whether it's playing a challenge to unlock a perk or discovering a collectable that leads to unlocking OG - inspired levels of Doom, there's seemingly a never-ending reason to keep playing. Don't forget the ultra-violence and higher difficulties to try.

Despite all the good things, I felt more relief than joy playing the game. Relief that the game was over and I could go on to other things. This is classic Doom, with all the bells and whistles, but it's just not that interesting anymore in 2018. Don't get me wrong, it can still be mindless fun, but modern shooters usually have more going for them. The old run and gun formula just doesn't really work now. Gamers should expect more.

So in the end, I enjoyed my time but also didn't leave the ending excited to have played it. The last few levels especially just felt like arena shooters with just more of the waves. And the boss fights, while interesing, weren't epic. I certainly won't remember anything about the story.

Rate 7.5/10. Really excellent for what it is. It just didn't take any chances and kept with the same formula of old. Probably the best in its subgenre (arena wave shooter), but probably a genre that has no place in future FPS AAA franchises.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA masterpiece.
An FPS masterpiece.
A rare nearly flawless game.

It does what it sets out to do with the same pure awesomeness as the original Doom series but with some excellent lore and 'drive' added to it.

The game is gory. The game is violent.
The game is dark. The game puts fun at the forefront.

The story though simple is executed flawlessly. The main ally in the game is brilliantly voice acted and executed.
The main character is simple yet despite being a silent protagonist he oozes motivation and righteous anger.

Weapons are fun.
The kill animations awesome.
The game gets better and better as time progresses.
Except a few slower paced levels here and there the game almost fully has an upward bellcurve 'fastpaced action fun'-wise.

The lore and background fluff is oddly fascinating. And the slow elaboration on the history of the 'Doom Slayer' is awesome as you realize how Hell itself views you... and why.

So in brief:
A brilliantly executed fun no nonsense action rompe players have craved for many many years.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe most irksome feature of the Bethesda Id reboots is a tendency to turn them into item collectathons. In Wolfenstein, collectables will unlock some extra concept art or videos but in Doom your character progression and weapon upgrade paths are irritatingly tied to collectables scattered throughout the map. At first this is quite bothersome, particularly as it slows down the balls-to-the-wall demon slaying. While not exactly reaching the adrenaline fueled heights of the '90s shooters, it comes damn close for a Triple A big budget release, and is the king of the hill when it comes to Id's recent fare.\t\t\t\t\t

[▼] NOT RECOMMENDED [▼]
  • Bad map design.
    • Map is fixed to an odd angle. Hard to view.
    • Map is in 3D, with many levels stack on top of each other, blocking the level you are at.
    • Can't rotate ↔ or tilt ↕ map for clearer viewing.
    • Map is fixed to the direction Doom Guy is facing. Want to change direction? you need to quit the map menu, have Doom Guy faces another direction, open map again. Repeat those again if the direction is wrong.
    • Many secrets items & collectables are well hidden, thus studying the map can help locate them. So, most of my time was wasted on getting a good viewing angle on the map.
    • Can't place manual marker on map. Making exploring the environment a bad experience.
  • No directional indication when you were hit. It is similar to car with no speed or fuel indications. The designers clearly have no interest in your well being. As a result:
    • No idea where enemies' fire coming from.
    • Wasting precious seconds just to locate where the enemies are shooting you from, in an quick-action shooter game.
  • No enemy location indication (e.g. highlighted enemy silhouette), while enemy can spot you even from corner, and from afar.
    • Even worse, hordes of enemies usually teleport in from all directions to ambush you.
    • Enemies teleport in when you just arrive at a new place, where you are not familiar with. Not sure where is a good spot to shoot, where to run towards when enemies break your defense line.
    • Placement of explosive barrels are useless, since you will be focusing on shooting moving enemies which ambush you, rather than focusing on stationary barrels.
  • Brightness can't be adjust when you starts playing.
    • There are dark places where enemies are very hard to spot. They will shoot you from there, and you quickly lost sight when they stop shooting.
  • Environment design which is hard to spot (e.g. where can be climbed, drop down to, what lever can be operated).
    • Plus, the map is hard to view.
    • Platforming through these kind of environment (with no clear indication where can be landed) is a sign the designers are trying to kill your mood of playing.
    • No guard rail (or invisible wall) at edges, causing many accidental death when you are exploring or platforming through the stage.
    • Plenty of levers in the environment, but some can operated to unlock secrets. No distinct different between those. Wasted plenty of time back-tracking to find all the secrets. Poor map indication make the experience worse.
  • No indication of getting hit, or armor/shield is depleted. Only indication of low health.
    • imaging shooting hordes of enemies, then suddenly low-health sign pop-up. The moment you try to run away from enemies' fire, you were death. The designers clearly like to drive car without dashboard.
