\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is definitely a great successor to the classic DooM 1&2. While Project Brutality + a good old wads seem more entertaining to me, this game is definitely outstanding and superior to the most of the boring modern cover shooters.
Pros:
-New arena-ish fights idea around which the whole gameplay concept is centered
-Quick, brutal and satisfying gameplay
-It's interestingly challenging on ultra-violence, unlike most games on high difficulties, when they simply put you in an unfair situation or let AI cheat
-Lots of respect paid to the classical doom via level desighn, monser remakes, doomguy toys, etc
-I also loved the storyline and how Doomguy ignores all the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ :)
-Upgrades keep it fun and let you experiment with the fight style
-Player is mobile and all the levels are very well desighned in terms of movement freedom and visuals
-The music is BRUTAL as hell

Cons:
-Fights are pretty much the same all the time, while in old dooms situation depended on the room layout, amount and types of enemies you fight (remember that doom2 level with a lot of barons and a cyberdemon in a single room / section with lots of cacodemons?). I also miss 'you pick up the key/powerup, and the room suddenly turns into a deathtrap' thing.
It's no longer 'level desighner vs. player', it's non-stop running around and shooting
-Because of the above, I don't think it's very replayable
-Snapmap is... well, you know.

Overall 9/10. Absolutely entertaining in almost every aspect. RIP AND TEAR!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMolding the classic gameplay of the original DOOM 1 and 2 with a splash of DOOM 3, id Software created a modern twist to what DOOM can be. It is fast-paced and can be quite challenging. There are kick-ass weapons to play with, a whole lot of demons to kill, and the soundtrack is SO GOOD! The story is surprisingly done well, although not very meaty, but this is DOOM; fast-pace gameplay, killing hell spawn using big bad-ass guns, all with a cheeky grin on your face. The multiplayer on the other hand is...not very great. Granted DOOM wasn't a huge multiplayer game, a balanced arena-style multiplayer mode would have made this entry into the series a solid 10/10 for me. Other than an OKAY multiplayer experience, DooM (2016) delivered on all accounts. Newcomers to the DOOM series will be sure to enjoy this entry just as much as long-time fans will. id Software did a damn good job, overall!


9.0/10.
