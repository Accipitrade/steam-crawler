\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot recommend this game enough. The story is rather average, but the gameplay is phenomenal - frankly, unmatched in my opinion. One of the most fun games I've ever played. You'll see an odd review here or there that's sour because it doesn't stay true to the DOOM franchise, but the real question is - who cares? I mean, honestly...play this game for 10 minutes and you'll realize you made the correct decision in picking this up.

The campaign is quite long, actually, which is a huge bonus. And its replay value is extremely high, given that it is nearly impossible to pick up all of the secrets/collectibles and complete all of the mission challenges on the first playthrough without using a guide. Not only that, but the campaign offers 5 different difficulty levels (including a perma-death difficulty which has never appealed to me), so you can play it casually or you can challenge yourself. The game can become really frantic and challenging, and getting through some of the later battles is exhilirating. You are also offered a wide variety of weapons, and can carry all of them at once, with NO reloading necessary...so swapping between weapons mid-battle just adds to the chaos.

And after ALL OF THAT...you STILL have two other features - Snapmap and multiplayer. Snapmap is a mode where players can create their own maps/scenarios, either solo or multiplayer, co-op, PVP, whatever you'd like...and post them for community play. It's quite amazing and pretty easy to use. I haven't utilized it too much to this point, and I don't see myself actually making a map in the near future, but it basically offers unlimited playability of community-submitted content.

The multiplayer is also fun, although I've only put a couple of hours into it at this point. Hard to say if the PVP has any staying power, but I don't see any compelling reason why it wouldn't. The gameplay is fun, fast, and furious...being able to power up mid-game as a demon is pretty awesome, and there are multiple gametypes and maps to satiate the masses.

I think the campaign is reason enough to buy this game, but adding in Snapmap and multiplayer makes it an incredible bargain. Absolutely worth $60, but obviously you can get it for less. I picked it up for $25 and I think it's one of the best deals I've ever gotten on a game. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor me this is the new definition of addiction.

The fact that this is DOOM, an American cultural icon is just icing on the cake. I was hooked by the first level.
As soon as I took the first steps out of that elevator I knew I was in for a ride.
I feel that the incorperation of the traditional doom weapons along with the contemporary control style was honestly a very beneficial asset of the game. Reserving the chainsaw and BFG-9000 to separate buttons was a really good idea in regards for ammo conservation.

While unfortunate that the multiplayer was taken down, the game more than made up for it with the astounding Snap-Map function. The fact that players can design, engineer, and publish their own levels with scripted events and customized NPC actions and relations was an astounding development. The Snap-Map is almost like a free game engine that came packaged with doom. As a person looking into getting a job in videogame development I loved the overall idea of the Snap-Map.

I have recomended this game to all my friends and have almost talked non-stop about it to my friends and family whenever I see them. I like this game almost to an extent of LOVING it.

10/10
GREAT GAME.\t\t\t\t\t

I don't care for graphics to much, I still play Q3 engine based games from time to time and they look horrible. But the lowest graphics make this game look ancient, and it just makes it harder to see everything, basically a nonstop motion blur.

According to the debug stats my GPU is the bottleneck (20.0ish, cpu at 8.0-10.0ish), which is all fine and dandy, but it basically looks like the first DOOM... so, ok. Pretty sure a 64man Golmud is more resource intensive than the first few minutes of DOOM, tiny ass map, only a hand ful of mobs. I know you can't directly compare these 2 games, but the range of FPS games I enjoy is very limited. But BF handles a ton more entities with a whole lot more pixels to render, it just has a lot more going on and does it much more beautifully.
