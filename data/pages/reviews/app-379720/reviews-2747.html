\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hope my mom doesn’t read these reviews. It isn’t the first time I rip on her for not allowing me to play the cool games when I was little. Street Fighter 2, Mortal Kombat, she wouldn’t have it. DOOM; same story.

Sweet revenge now thanks to Id Software’s modern day take on the classic shooter. This game is awesome. It is a quintessential game. It abandons all semblances of reality and imposes its own rules for maximum enjoyment. No more stamina bars, no run buttons, reloading – none of that. DOOM is straight, balls-to-the-walls shooting action and it nails it.

You play Doomguy, a dude so fearless the demons of hell had him sealed away. Now someone messed up at the power plant and it is your job to horribly mess up the demon hoard’s plans.
It’s a little thin on story, but really, it’s just there for motivation’s sake. Even the secondary characters make it clear that Doomguy has a disposition for running and gunning before subtleties.

You always run; you don’t have to reload; you carry all your weapons on you at all times and you punch through bone like it’s sugar. Everything is about speed and aggression. You need to be more ferocious than the hordes of demons that beset you and it is a blast. The game gives you the ability to gruesomely finish off any demons you stun after a few shots and those finishers then drop a little health and ammo, allowing more time to be spent murdering and less time scavenging.

DOOM is retro is in its reward system with hidden power-ups and secret rooms, but really is a modern title with all conveniences in place. The quick paced and rewarding gameplay will have you coming back for more even after the credits roll. It looks gorgeous and gory, the music is on point and the sound effects wrap the whole thing up into a wholesome and complete game. Love it.
