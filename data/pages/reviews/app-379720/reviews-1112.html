\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a surprisingly fresh FPS that is definitely worth a check if you want to try something different than most modern offerings.
Doom's story is a sort of quasi-reboot of the series but is also more of a continuation of the first two games whose story was basically non existent. Here, the Doomslayer is tasked with the usual, killing demons that are threatening to take over our civilization. In all honesty, the story here is almost nonexistent, more than anything it is an excuse to kill a bunch of demons in a variety of places, including hell itself. That's not to say the story is bad or not interesting at all, it actually has some interesting moments. However, if you are looking for anything deeper than someone pointing you to a place to slay more forces of Hell, then you should look elsewhere. As a vessel for gameplay though, the story present works really well. The fact there are only three characters really in the entire game also doesn't help it's depth either.
The gameplay here is really really good, obviously. Unlike most modern FPS games which emphasize realism (to an extent) and cover mechanics, Doom is all about motion. If you try and stay still and pick off enemies in any form of modern tactical combat, you will die pretty quickly. You need to constantly be moving, switching between your arsenal of guns acquired throughout the game while you do so and weakening enemies for glory kills to refill health and sometimes ammo. This constant motion really amps up the adreneline of the player, and adds constant tension to the gameplay even though it isn't particularly difficult once you understand these mechanics. The glory kills and the the overall gunplay are incredibly satisfying, and moving around the environment killing a wide variety of enemies feels fluid and fun. The only mild complaint I have is that the sections between combat arenas are really boring and sometimes hard to navigate. The map for the game looks cool in that it is 3D, but it is dang near impossible to navigate properly. There were a few times where I was supposed to go was not clear and as a result, the pacing of the game was off during these sections. I can't complain too much about this though given there are no real puzzles, it is more of a UI complaint than anything. As far as replayability, the user levels are not really good IMO, just excuses to test out a few interesting concepts but you'll drain through them in about half an hour, while the multiplayer is virtually dead here and not really as original as the rest of the game. Basically, you are here for the campaign.
The game looks really good for the most part. Map textures are great, although Demons within their species have no varietty. While this makes it easier to identify combat threats and analyze quickly how to kill them on the fly, it can make the game feel stale in the later sections. The sound design here is AMAZING. I'm more of a hard rock fan than metal, but the soundtrack here really boosts the gameplay and makes you feel like a complete badass. The guns and demons also really sound great and it is amazing just how many sounds the developers fit into the game.
From a technical standpoint, falling out of the map will allow you to see the map in it's entirety. Also, the doom guy's death animations aren't great compared to the glory kills given sometimes they are taken from glitchy angles. Overall though the game runs great and has incredibly smooth framerates!\t\t\t\t\t
