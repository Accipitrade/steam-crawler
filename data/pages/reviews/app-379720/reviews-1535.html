\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a hardcore fan of the original Doom and Doom 2, this game blows me away. Once those games ran their mainstream course, I moved on to other shooters, but very few compared to the raw satisfaction and pleasure I get from the massacre and slaughter I incur on my foes in this title. It lives up to the original, and then some. Hell, I even downloaded mods to make the original play like this, just to see if I like it the same. Sure enough, I did.
An absolute masterpiece (not without flaws), that maintains the spirit of the original, makes combat feel just so satisfying, and has a simple yet strong story element to tie it all together.
The multiplayer is alright; feeling like a blend of Unreal, Quake, and classic Doom. Good ol' arena shooter style. Some of the maps are lackluster, but who cares when you can make your own?
Snapmap is absolutely awesome. Reminds me 100% of the old Timesplitters 2 map maker. Other games had these features, too. But none did the concept quite like D16 and TS2 do... Halo has Forge mode, but that's map editing, which I consider equally awesome, but in a different category.
The music is immaculate, and I ended up putting a few tracks on my daily playlist. I love the mashups the community has made, as well. Mick Gordon has made not only amazing music, but music others can add to, to make it better for them.

If you like gory, fast-paced, explosive gameplay that just lets you run wild in the power-fantasy (even on the hardest difficulties), you're going to feel right at home, here. I'm brimming with excitement for the sequel, and its more calming release date partner. I can't believe I waited so long to really dig into it, but I've forgiven myself.\t\t\t\t\t
