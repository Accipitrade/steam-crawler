Thoughts going in -

I have only played the original Doom for about half an hour, so I will say that this IS my first Doom game. I have watched a lot of gameplay of the OG Doom, though. I expected this game to be as gripping as the Bethesda walkthrough, and very hellish (of course.) I was skeptical after seeing beta reactions, but I bit the bullet.

Initial reaction -

I was first blown away by the visuals. This is definitely a top-notch game for 2016. We know that it has Vulcun support coming, which will be amazing. I am running the game with a GTX Titan X on a 1440p, 144hz screen at max settings. I was only getting 60fps (could tell by the mouse) but I do believe it was from the graphics settings, as the game does have an unlocked frame rate. The game is much more stable than The Witcher 3, and even GeForce Experience (LOL.) There are basic menu options, so it's easy to use.

Movement -

This game is fast. It's not as fast as Unreal Tournament 4, but it's fast. A long bunny-hop chain in CS:GO could describe this game's speed accurately. I tried bunny-hopping in this game but I can't tell if it was faster than running or not. Your character can vault up legdes really easily.

Level Design -

This is one of my complaints. They levels are very large in certain areas. There is a lot of verticallity to the game, which is interesting. There are "blue" and "yellow" keys that you need to obtain from dead characters to unlock doors. This is my issue. I spent a solid 15-20 minutes searching the entire level for one background-blending corpse. There was no marker on the map for it and it was just a waste of time. It's a good mechanic, but it can get annoying. Also, you need to open almost every door that you pass manually (at least to unlock it.) The terminals to do so look alot like the un-useable terminals so it can be confusing at times.

Gameplay -

I played this game on the standard, medium difficulty. You gradually build up your arsenal of weapons as you progress. As you get more weapons and upgrades, (you can choose different alternate fire modes for each weapon i.e frag rounds or choked burst for a shotgun) there are more enemies that get more challenging. I sort of felt like I abused the "Glory Kill" mechanic. All you have to do is get any enemy down to low health and you will incapacitate them. You can just walk up to them and "Press F to pay respects" their asses and mutilate them. From doing that, you will get more drops vs a normal kill. I did this very often. With the chainsaw, you get a TON of ammo. You can insta-kill bosses with it if you have enough fuel. All of the enemies are really different - attacks, health, speed, etc. are all really well balanced.

Story -

From what I can tell so far, it's another reboot of the original Doom's plot. From what I've read about Doom 3 this game seems to have a very similary story so far. I have not finished the story yet so I will update when I finish it.

Summary -

I think this is a very solid Doom game, and my first. I love it and I always feel like a god. The art style is great and you are always moving. 9.4/10 - It's fun, gripping, and immersive.

TL;DR -

Pros:
+Graphics
+Stability
+Style
+Level Design
+Movement
+Enemies

Cons:
-Blue and yellow key-cards are not easy to find in some cases
-Melee is overpowered, most of my kills are from melee because it is easy to spam and gives a ton of loot\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not Quake, but it will do for a while.

The game is not as bad as the BETA made it out to be, the multiplayer can actually be played with something resembling actual arena FPS with fast movement. I can jump into TDM games and use typical arena fps dodging skills and aim to outplay people, which is good enough for me and still fun. Compared to Quake skill cap this is about 6/10 of what Quake allows you to do.

I wouldn't take this game seriously competitively just yet, it's still a bit too casual friendly and skill capped. However they have done a good job on the PC release of the game, many of the things were hidden before the full release such as uncapped framerates, FOV, actually being able to bring down the game console. I was pleasantly surprised I could remove the gun model.

The SP is great and will be what shines though.

It has the potential to be a successor to Quakelive for competitive arena FPS under the right conditions, such as allowing weapon pickups, removing universal ammo, strafe jumping that allows you to build up speed, etc. But I have yet to see if this is possible, the default game mode servers in multiplayer definitely don't have any of this stuff yet.\t\t\t\t\t
