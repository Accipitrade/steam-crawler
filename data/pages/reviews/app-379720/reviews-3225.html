\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the start I want to say, this is a singleplayer only review.

I only played the singleplayer campaign but what a singleplayer it is. I didn't really expect Doom to be good. This could have easily been another Duke Nukem Forever. Thankfully it isn't. This game is awesome.
This is what an FPS can be like. Fast paced, great weapon variety, interesting levels that you are free to explore by yourself, no crappy QTEs, etc.
First thing that is really great, is the fact that you move fast (could be even faster for my taste). As soon as you get the Jumpboots, it gets even better. In one level I was stuck for a few minutes because there was a wall about 1,5m tall. First I thought i couldn't go there, because normally you have invisible walls everywhewre. Not in DOOM. You can just jump over walls. What a great and revolutionary concept. The levels are Rooms and not corridors with many different platforms, walls and great verticality overall. When you're in an arena with a bunch of enemies you can run around, jump in the air and avoid enemy hits and fire. Unlike in the latest Shadow Warrior - where all arenas were basically a flat surface - the Arenas have interesting architecture, you can use to your adavantage. Another thing is that the levels look great especially the ones in Hell. Really great design. The only thing that bothers me is the choice of the color palet. Sometimes it's just too much piss-yellow. But that's a minor point.
Another thing that should be great in an FPS is the gunplay. In DOOM it is. You have a great weapon variety and most importantly, you have all your weapons with you, unlike in most modern shooters. What DOOM does is limit your ammo somewhat (not too much though), so your are sometimes in a situation where you have to use your other guns. Which is great, because otherwise you might not tried it out. Most weapons (aside from the basic pistol) range from good to awesome, so it's good the game "forces" you to use them sometimes. Another great thing is that you dont have to reload your weapons (or they reload automatically). This realy helps with the flow of the combat. If you run out of ammo you just switch weapons. All the weapons have alternative fire modes an can be upgraded. The great thing is that pretty much every upgrade feels meaningful and makes your gun more awesome than it already is.
What also helps the flow of the game is the fact that your health doesn't regenerate. You never find yourself sitting behind a corner waiting for your health to go back up. You either have to find a health pack or to glory-kill an enemy, which keeps you going at all time. First I thought the glory kills would get annoying. But they are optional, don't take long and there are quite a few of them, so you don't see each one too often. Besides you can get an ability, where you can dash at the enemies from a greater distance, which accelerates your movement.
The enemies and their design are great as well. There are a bunch of different Demons to kill. They all look different and behave in different ways, so you can tell them apart. No dudes in power armor who all look the same. The bigger the beastie is, the more difficult it is to kill. Great stuff. You also have three major boss fights, which are all a bit different and and have their own gimmicks. It's good they have only three, because this way the all feel different and don't get repetitive. The AI of is also pretty good. The enemies run around, try to flank, get behind you or shoot at you from above. No hiding and sitting behind cover. What's also great, is that there isn't a single enemy with a hit-scan weapon. They all fire orbs/fireballs/etc which move faster or slower. Most of the range attacks also have start-up animations, making it easier to see when an enemy is going to shoot at you. This way you can avoid most enemy fire by dodging. If you get hit it's probably at least partly your fault.
The game looks and and plays pretty good. I only have 8GBs of ram and a relatively slow GTX750 TI, but A could run the game at constant 60FPS at 1660x900 with medium/high settings. And even on my machine the game looks pretty good. The only bad thing here is, that the game crashed a few times and I don't know why. What I've also read is, that theres is bug, making the game think you're in developer mode, so you can't start the game. I personally didn't experience it but maybe you sholud wait a bit if until they patch it. Or wait until the next sale, as of writing this.
The last point that really surprised me was the story and writing. The story is actually OK and fairly well written. Better than COD or latest Battlefield stories, that's for sure. There is a lot of additional information and story bits that you can find, that are genuinely well written and interesting to read. And if you don't care about such things, the game doesn't force you to read any of it.

In the end I have to say that if you like singleplayer FPS you should definitely check out DOOM. Even at full price it's worth buying just for the campaign. You probably will get 7-8 hours of singleplayer if you just run through the levels and a lot more if you look for upgrades, easter eggs, weapon mods and so on. Check it out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a big fan of all the doom games,
I even love doom 3 for having a more survival horror vibe to it which i can appreciate.

DOOM 4 dose not disappoint, the fast paced combat is intense and insane, I see some infulkecne from brutal doom,
the creature design and creature graphics are gorgeous, some really detailed 3D models here to gawk at.
Looking for Secrets is fun and rewarding; some of them are annoyingly well hidden.
The classic levels are a lot of fun and quite challenging because there are no checkpoints.
The bosses are great Would of been nice to have a couple more bosses at the end of some of the earlier levels.
The soundtrack is beast in some tracks and maybe a tad weak in others,
Probably just my opinion, It sounds a little too robotic and modern but I just really love the classic doom music and sound.

Story is average but is very scarce throughout the game so it's not that annoying.
Who decided to have optimus prime's voice in this game?
Multiplayer is average and matchmaking seems a little broken, why no server browser?
and Snap Map is a big disappointment so far, joining a snap map match is near impossible.

All in all The wait was well worth it.
It's hard to put a number on the experience...
I think it misses the 9 mark slightly, with a couple more bosses and maybe a little more variety in the level design and scenery to go along with the facilities/hell it would of been perfect.

8.5/10
