Needless to say after you get done fighting a demon and he pisses you off, you can now rip his arm off and shove it down his throat.
Good stuff.
There are secrets in the game for die hard doom fans. Even if you aren't, they are entertaining.
(little hidden doom action figures)
The weapons are badass, they really did a good job with the mechanics of all weapons. I couldnt pick a favorite, they were all unique and fun to use.
Classic armor and health, No call of duty bs where you go hide and you have full health again.....
Gauss canon has a kickback when you jump, I found this amusing and went for a ride with off the map and into oblivion a few times.
Enemies are badass. Especially the Bosses.
Enemies start fighting eachother sometime. It's nice to not be the only one they want to kill.
Level Design is on point. Fast paced. No hangups.
The game is reallyyyyyyyyyyyyyy SMOOTH. Its fluid, no bugs.



THE SOUNDTRACK IS OUTSTANDING! Its all the classic doom music but done in low tuning and creatively remastered!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been looking forward to playing this since it came out. And it definitely didn't disappoint.

There are so few First Person Shooters out there give me the rush I get from DOOM. Possibly because it's literally crush kill destroy; but that's how I like it, since the first FPS I played was Unreal Tournament.

The game looks great; even though I have to run everything on the lowest setting it still stands up as a good looking game.

The Campaign is a load of fun, especially since glory kills make every kill as brutal as possible, and the power ups are great fun (especially berserk).

Once you finish the campaign, you could play through again for 100%, do rune trials and there's even multiplayer and a map editor tool.

I highly recommend this game to anyone. Arena shooters aren't dead. They're alive and kicking.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played the single player campaign only. And while in the beginning I felt a little lost about "who am I, where am I, and what should I do", after the first few demons you littary rip in pieces (with your hands :-D ) I didn't mind it that much not knowing. Fortunately the answers to those questions came eventually, as the game progressed.

The game feels very balanced. And even though I had a bit of trouble staying alive at first, as the demon threats became larger with each arena, I adjusted play style a little and suddenly that impossibly big demon threat wasn't so impossible anymore.

And while the gameplay feels amazing, there's actually a pretty decent story beneath all the gore as well. The environments are very beautiful and the amount of detail is enormous. There were points where I just stopped tearing deamons apart and just enjoy the scenery for a minute.

Those beautiful environments didn't impact performance. This has to be one of the most smooth gaming experiences I've had in a while. No lag, no stutter, no fps drops. It just felt rock-solid.

It isn't entirely perfect though. I did suffer 2 'hangs' while playing. The game would just stop continuing, though the sound does. Still, only 2 crashes in the entire campaign, I've seen worse! Also, Alt-Tabbing back to windows doesn't work well allways. Most of the times it worked fine, but a couple of times the game speed would drop. Framerate was still steady, but now it was a steady 5 fps instead of 60 from which it wouldn't recover. Had to restart the game to get back to decent framerate after that happens. Reloading or returning to menu wouldn't work.

Still, those glitches can't kill the fun. I've enjoyed myself with this game a lot! I dare say it's my best gaming experience in several years! Definitly recommended!\t\t\t\t\t
