\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first played this when it was still new and I kind of rushed through it. Didn't really bother with the combat challenges or the secrets. I don't think that was a good idea because they help you to upgrade your suit and weapons. So, not completing them made the game less enjoyable. The challenges themselves were ok. Most of them were pretty easy. Some of them are more difficult. Then, some of them are stupid; like "collect 3000 something something" where there's no skill required, but it takes forever to complete.

In the end, I think taking the time to complete all the challenges and get your gear in order, then playing the levels with all your upgrades was much more fun.

Basically, the game pretty much forces you to complete the challenges in order to fully enjoy it. However, I'd say they're fair about it. You can reload as many times and play a level as much as you want and the challenge counters carry over. So, if you need to glory kill 5 imps (just for example) and you've only glory killed 4, you can reload the checkpoint or replay the level and will only have to glory kill 1 more imp. Then, once you get it, you can just exit the level with no penalty if you've already completed it in campaign mode.

There's an arcade mode that adds a few features, which some people find pretty fun. The story wasn't really anything special. Basically, someone tries to use hell for something, everything goes wrong and the protagonist has to clean it up.

Finally, I'm pretty sure most people know what this game is like. It's not a survival horror, like Doom 3, but I'd put it more in the Serious Sam category. Fight waves of enemies, complete objectives and find secrets.\t\t\t\t\t
