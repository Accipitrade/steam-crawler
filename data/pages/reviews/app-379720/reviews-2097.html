\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a pretty good game, packed with a bad ass soundtrack and an amazing combat + movement system to boot.

Notwithstanding all that, the main issue I have is how they handled the difficulty of demons (and the game in general). Apparently on Nightmare (the difficulty I played on), the spawn rate is increased vastly to the point where I died to enemies that weren't even in my line of sight before I would begin a certain engagement, leading to a lingering feeling of just straight up unfairness as I was given practically very little to no time to react to them. Demons are also transformed into bullet sponges (crazy how many games think this is an acceptable way to properly increase difficulty), and some enemy attack patterns are either not telegraphed enough or given a sufficient wind-up time.

Also a minor con, the game's story is actually laughable.

However chances are that 95% of players playing DOOM aren't looking for the most engrossing story.

Level design is super linear outside of some secrets due to not really utilizing verticality as great as it should outside of some elevated platforms but then again it is sorta just DOOM.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I finally got around to playing through DOOM (2016) and I must say, braaaaaavo!

This is an absolute masterpiece, in my humble opinion. The game starts off quick and begins introducing you to the new style and feel of DOOM. It feels awesome! I am obviously playing on PC and I have a pretty powerful rig so my experience was in 2k, 165hz with keyboard and mouse.

Now, it isn't perfect as I did run into a few glitches here and there but they were far and few between. For 98% of the game, it actually was flawless! Keyboard and mouse, as always, felt right at home, the same or even a bit better than previous Doom experiences.

If you like any of the Doom Franchise, and you are ready to take the next step in evolution, this is simply a must play. The story is pretty good (though its not the strongest part, but it IS interesting and I enjoyed it), the game play is 10/10, the music is hardcore awesomeness, and the visuals are 2nd to none. The enemy design is insane and the bosses are really well thought out and challenging (I played on 'Hurt Me Plenty' for the first play through). The gun design is also really amazing and the weapon mods and challenge rifts add quite a bit to the game. Your armor even has its own progression system and all will be explained in due time so no need to worry about it out of the gate.

Again, if you like Doom as a franchise, you really need to purchase and experience this gem! And DOOM: Eternal will be coming out later this year, I believe (2019) so I can only dream of what they could add/improve! I can't wait!\t\t\t\t\t
