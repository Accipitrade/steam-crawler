\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo put it bluntly, DOOM is a good game. It's worth a buy.

DOOM had a troubled development to say the least, with initial gameplay tid-bits making it look scatter-brained and life-less, with colorless imagery and vain attempts at showing off, but it turns out that the sum of its parts weren't all there in those promo's, and that the final product is actually, well, amazingly well done.

The weapons are meaty and satisfying, all the alternate fire modes serve their purpose and each offer a different kind of gameplay style for that particular weapon, the grenades are all very unique, the environmental traversal adds a new level of depth to Doom that wasn't present previously, and the executions are far from just the "pointless cosmetics" that everyone is so quick to jump on them about.

Basically, every negative preconcieved notion that you had about this game was wrong, thankfully, and they managed to make an experience quite unlike any other on the market for FPS's for this era.

Highly recommended

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for the old-school shooter from the 90's that you grew up playing, then this game just may give you that fix you've been needing. Right from the start you are dropped into the action, with the story taking a backseat until the later levels.

Pros
- Large variety of weapons, with various upgrades.
- Old-school maze-style maps with area battle areas and secrets to find
- amazing soundtrack that is fitting for a game about non-stop action
- lengthy and fun campaign experience, with a balance between mars and hell levels
- chainsaw and the BFG. these things will save you in a pinch.
- the difficulties are actually difficult!

Cons
- some crashes occur without explination (may be fixed in future)
- some areas are unable to be backtracked to at certain points. I made this a negative because sometimes you'll notice a secret area you missed in the map, aand the only way to get back to it is to restart the level. Bare in mind these levels can be anywhere from 20 minutes to an hour long.
- not enough boss fights scattered through the game. There are like 3 boss fights I believe, and they all happen at the end of the game.
- cliff-hanger ending. Definitely set up for a sequel. Not that I mind a sequel, but any game that doesn't give a satisfying conclusion to it's story in the first game is a "con" in my eyes.

Definitely worth full price if you're looking for some good old-fashioned gory shooting action. and replayability is high due to higher difficulties unlocking (not sure if there's a new-game plus yet).\t\t\t\t\t
