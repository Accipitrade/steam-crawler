\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt Â£20? yes this is a great sell for some of the old ultra violence. Anymore and I would not touch it.

I got this during the summer sale and completed it within a few sessions. 15 hours is all it takes to beat the highest immediatly available (none nightmare as that has to be unlocked, terrible move) difficulty. But with rare excepetion those 15 hours were fantastic.

The combat is pared down to a smooth flow of constant running, shooting, murdering set to an effective (if a little too disjointed) soundtrack.

You gain weapons throughout with a few extra mechanics introduced (a little late on realy) and that helps the game stay fresh till the end of your playthrough.

If I have any criticisms to level it is that that is all this is worth, that first playthrough will be a glorious blowout of guts and bullets. But it isn't realy worth repeating.

The collectable hunting just adds filler and acheivments and is often spoiled by the fact that you need to break up the flow of the game it indulge in it.

Upgrade progression is completely redundant with everything you acquired before the super shotgun being pointless there after.

The games story, which while well handled, will often force you to slow down and lose the flow to play a rousing game of "push the button" "follow the marker" etc which makes re-doing levels tiring, especialy once you know what to expect.

In short, Doom 2016 is a good game, but it pushes no bounderies and only just fills it's required runtime to cost value, get it, play it, move on.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFOR THOSE DECIDING BETWEEN THIS AND DOOM: ETERNAL

This game is very straightforward with very limited puzzle or platforming. Basically, this is much closer to run and gun than Eternal that does require to solve some basic level puzzles and platforming (shotgun + grapple = awesome!)

Would recommend starting off with this game then moving to Eternal.

-------------------------------------------------------------

What a fun game. As everyone knows by now, this is a very masterfully done reimagining of the original game. And its a brutally well done job.

Tip for new comers to the franchise or shooters in general, the difficulty scaling is very real with different options, but I found Hurt Me Plenty option is a good entry point, below with the game is just too easy and loses it's charm.

Basic introduction to the game:
You're playing the role of a near mythical soldier who has been resurrected/reawakened as a result of Hellish chaos unleashed by Corporate Force That Be on Mars. Basically, a portal to hell has been opened and the station has been overrun by demonic entities -- the bad guys.

In all honesty, I don't think this is one of those games that relies too much on story elements to move forward relying on core gameplay to that.

I like how there isn't a weapon collection module attached the game like many games tend, but you need to learn the few weapons that do appear as they are very situational.

I will update my review once I have played the game more!\t\t\t\t\t
