\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember the orignal PC DOOM. DOOM II lived up to the original. Then I guess somebody retired or moved on to greener pastures or something, because no other DOOM game since has even come close.

Tiny rooms. Claustrophobic environments. Doom III and this DOOM reboot both managed to have tiny rooms and claustrophobic environments even when you were supposedly "outdoors". I always felt confined to a very narrow path the entire time I was playing them. Oh, I won't try to claim the first two were really "open world", because they weren't. But you had a lot more paths, a lot more exploration, a lot more roaming, in both of them. This game was supposed to return to the spirit of those two games--ID knew it hadn't managed that with Doom III--and it didn't manage it here either. Not by a long shot.

"Glory kills." Really? Who even uses the word "glory" anymore? Two-bit cannon-fodder bad-guy Klingons in some of the worse episodes of Star Trek... that's just about it. That, and Chinese people who think they're fruent in Engrish. Last time I saw the word "glory" used the way ID used it in DOOM was when I was, oh, six, and bought a crappy toy machine gun made in china. "Glory Warrior... A Born of Hero..." Ugh. Puh-leeze. I just wanted the gun. Every time I play DOOM, I spend the entire time as disgusted as I did just before I hurriedly discarded the pathetic wrapper of that old toy.

I can't even finish this game. I got a fair distance once, but ever since then I barely make it past the first couple of levels, then I get sick of "Glory Kill: A Born of Hero" and go play something else. By the time I ever come back to DOOM again, I feel totally lost at whatever level I've gotten to, and have to start over.

Buy this game if you loved the original DOOM. But only to encourage somebody to try again someday, and maybe at least get it halfass right next time. Buy it and let it rot in your library. I still hold out hope of seeing a decent DOOM again someday, but forget this one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeen a fan of the Doom franchise since the 90s and I have to give thanks to the guys and gals over at ID who made this game possible.

Game is gorgeous - I have a gtx 970, the game auto set everything at a medium setting, and it is still one of the most beautiful games I have ever played. I had trouble shooting at the varying demons on their first appearances because I was, more often than not, taken by how damn awesome their new designs were. Again, the developers stayed true to the original dooms.

Game is bug free - I LITERALLY HAD ZERO BUGS! I beat the game in just over 12 hours and never, not once, did I experience anything close to a freaking GLITCH. No graphical errors, no clipping issues. NOTHING. Even when creatures die they stay plugged into the physics of the game until their corpses turn to ash and blow away. I just couldnt believe I didnt find anything and I explored EVERYTHING.

Game is FUN! - I remember reading an article that Bethesda put out talking about how they want people to play agressively and that the game awards a gameplay style that is mobile and in the enemies face. If you sit behind a corner and camp, or try to play like its a Call of Duty fps, you will die. This game is all about moving, using the terrain, and all the tools the game gives you to bring the demons of hell to their KNEES.

The Game and gameplay make you feel like a BAMF - The game literally FEEDS your ego by talking you up and basically reinforcing the idea that YOU, THE DOOM MARINE, are the demon that the denizens of hell tell scary stories about around campfires. Unfortunately for them, you are very real! The glory killing system has you literally wade through your enemies pulling them apart and just leaving you looking for the next demon to tear apart with your bare hands! (sometimes your hands are all you need!)

THE GAME IS A REWARDING CHALLENGE! - The boss fights were AWESOME. The game constantly pits you against waves upon waves of demons as you get further into the game. It also seems like the game scaled my exposure to the waves quite well. It never felt like i was in an impossible situation. The entire combat arena could be swarming, and i mean like 20-25 guys staring at me trying to kill me and the only thing going through my mind was..."Im killing that one first" and then you charge!

Item collection was a pleasure! - Alot of times you get games that basically give you the "option" of finding little treasure troves around the various levels with usually an achievement or a journal notation to reward you with. How does Doom award you for doing this? IT TRIES TO KILL YOU OF COURSE! You want to find every little secret on the map? Squeeze every upgrade you can? Get ready for puzzles, get ready for ambushes, get ready for challenges! How exactly are you rewarded? Well you become a far more capable killing machine of course! Going for all the unlocks in the game takes time but they are all interconnected with various "completion" based sub-objectives that further reward you for your diligence. Ultimately allowing you to stay ahead of the game with the best equipment and upgrades you could possibly have which you ARE GOING TO NEED!

I havent touched the multiplayer or snapmap yet because im probably gonna amp the difficulty up to its max setting and give it a go.

I highly recommend this game - the last time I felt this good about an fps game, I was on the Pillar of Autumn and my rank was Master chief.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI literally never write reviews for anything, but considering that Doom is perhaps my new all-time favorite FPS campaign, I felt I owed it to the developers and community to share my honest opinions. Doom, simply-put, is absolutely incredible. This game has greatly exceeded the somewhat high expectations I had set for it after watching a solid amount of footage before release. The visuals are absolutely stunning and the game runs very well on my 7/10 PC at max settings.

You're thrown into action almost immediately, the basic and near-perfect gameplay formula shining right from the start. The fluidity of the movement, the fast-paced, feel-like-a-badass combat, and the perfect execution of weapon indroduction, upgrade pace and variety. It's as the developers said, the game is a frantic chess board, of sorts, in which your enemies take their spots and you quickly formulate, execute and constantly re-evaluate your approach to every battle, as it progresses. When you become comfortable with all the pieces the game offers you as you play through it, every battle almost feels like a dance. Constant weapon swapping, leaping from platform to platform, never stopping to breath until the music slows down. It just feels so damn natural and perfect.

Halo 1 was the first time I played a FPS and thought, "this formula is just so perfect that I could play the game over and over again and never get tired of it"(which I did). Well, I'm here to say that Doom is as close to formula perfection as I've seen since the first Halo. That's not to say that the two games are similar in how they play, just that each design element has been so perfectly-crafted and results in such incredible synergy that something special is born as a result. Doom is special, there is no doubt about that.

I've played countless FPS games over the years and I'm confident in admitting that Doom is the most fresh and most fun FPS of this generation. This game is going places, and I couldn't be happier.

Pros:
-Incredible visuals, both technical and artistic
-VERY well-optimized, I'd estimate about 60fps average throughout most of the game at max settings
-Near-perfect gameplay formula, resulting in a foundation that could support an endless amount of content
-Frantic, gorey, visceral combat that NEVER gets old and plays out differently each time
-Excellent weapon and gear upgrade systems that encourage you to explore the gorgeous maps for secrets
-Fantastic arsenal, a gun for every situation. Even at the end of the game I found myself constantly swapping between 5-6 guns, not including the mods being swapped on each gun, which can drastically change the way they function.
-Surprisingly-long campaign with excellent pacing and environmental variety, considering the source material
-Dialogue is very well-delivered and never induces cringe or slows down the pace of the game to undesireable levels

Cons:
-Player and monster-created light sources don't cast shadows. This is a pet peve of mine when it comes to games with amazing lighting systems. I understand the implications on performance to have all those light sources casting shadows on nearby geometry, but the immersion factor gained from implimenting this type of visual effect is significant and often jaw-dropping. It's very rare to find this in games, and Doom should absolutely not be without it
-2 or 3 poorly optimized areas, resulting in strange framerate drops.
-Would have liked a 4th rune slot and the ability to further level up the power of my guns through more in-game challenges. The guns are fantastic, but at some point the earlier weapons are mostly phased out unless you run out of ammo.
-Certain weapons have their sensitivity changed either while firing or while zooming in/charging weapon mod fire. This can be VERY hurtful during combat. There need to be individual sensitivity options for these weapons/mods.
-The game ends\t\t\t\t\t
