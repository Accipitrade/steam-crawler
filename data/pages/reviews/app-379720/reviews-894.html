\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM - I never thought I'd be comfortable in hell
Pros:

+ The music and sound design is crazy good
I need to emphasize this, the game always gives you the best audio experience possible. The music makes you feel like a badass, and the devs know exactly what will keep your heart beating at a solid 200 BPS.

+ Game fluidity
The controls, menus, guns, etc are fluid. You feel great playing this!

+ Graphics
The game goes for a nice art style that will probably look good for many years to come!

+ Fits any mood
The game's straightforward concept just makes it such a great experience to take on no matter how you feel. There is nothing complicated about this game but there is a lot of depth to it if you wish to proceed.

+ Length
The game's campaign is a healthy length but for the avid player, there are bonus levels, a bunch of collectibles, extra challenges, gun challenges, and several multiplayer modes. There is enough to do for any kind of player!


Cons:
- Loading times
I'm not sure if it's my hard drive or the game but it usually took me a couple of minutes to load into the main menu, and sometimes loading times between levels were a bit long. Not to worry though, never ruined my experience as the game played out smoothly when actually in-game!

- Cant save mid-level
This is more of a pet peeve as I tend to multitask. Not being able to save mid-level made me lose progress a couple of times. Luckily that never hurt the experience!

In conclusion: If you like FPS games of any kind play this!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: I did not play the multiplayer, and I'm only considering the singleplayer campaign.

DOOM (2016) is the definition of what a game is suppose to be; it's a fun and entertaining time without taking things too seriously. I don't think I have to explain what Doom is about and why it's fun (lots of other reviews explain it, and the game itself is self explanatory).

One of the things that stuck with me is the collectable aspect of the game. I really love collecting things that rewards the player directly through gameplay in games. It just feels rewarding, and you feel like you accomplished something when you find these collectables. If you like collecting stuff in games, then I believe you'll like this game.

If you don't like a mindless FPS, then I recommend setting the difficulty higher as it changes the game. It goes from a mindless FPS to a somewhat tactical FPS at least in my case.

Overall, I recommend this game especially if it's on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
