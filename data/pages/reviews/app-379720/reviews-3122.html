\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe multiplayer is good for a chuckle but demonstrated severe imbalance when I last played. Snapmap is something you have to get invested into and really learn, if you want to have any fun with it.

The Single Player, however? Well.
I'll always maintain that the classic Doom and Doom II surpass it due to their hands-on approach, simplicity and sheer volumes of levels with crazy-good design.
DOOM, however, impresses just as much as the original, albeit for different reasons. To remember the original Doom differently to the actual game content is an easy thing. We remember the combat being faster paced, more chaotic and possessing more berserker gameplay than it actually had. DOOM 2016 is a faithful recreation of what we imagine Doom to be.

Mechanics wise, the only things I'd complain about would be:
a) Variations on the same weapon type are not bound to the same key, instead the game banks on you to use the game's weapon select wheel, whereas the classic Dooms had the sense to put the Shotgun and Super Shotgun on the same key, trusting you to know what you're doing when it comes to weapon switching. You could easily merge the Super Shotgun with the Shotgun, the Plasma Rifle with the Gauss Rifle and the Chaingun with the Heavy Assult Rifle, with ol-faithful rocket launcher still bound to 5 and everything would be fine and dandy, but oh, well. Maybe in a future patch.

b) Some of the weapons feel awfully weak, for instance the plasma rifle and the pistol. Mainly the plasma rifle feels like an issue. It needs to be stronger. The heavy assault rifle would also fall under this category if it weren't for the fact that the weapon mods on it make it incredibly useful.

Otherwise, the gameplay and the mechanics involved feel near flawless. Low on ammo? Use the Chainsaw to get ammo pickups. Low on health? Simply luck out on ranged kills or perform the less-intrusive-than-you-may-think 'glory kills' for health drops. The game doesn't flow perhaps as good as you might like at first, due to the inclusion of secret hunting for weapon and armor upgrade points, none of which you'll want to miss if you want achievements or powerups in general. Thankfully the map screen is very easy to read compared to most 3D maps in games, and once you know the secrets, the game really does feel like it flows quite well.

Gunplay is a lot more fluid, but also faster paced than the original Doom. Rate of Fire is increased across the board, and your mobility is just agile enough to not intrude on the shooting. Throw in a double jump and a quick climb animation, and some good verticality to the levels and you've got a real recipe for success. Every enemy feels fun to fight and never feels like a grind (see the lost souls and pain elementals in previous games), allowing you to plough mercilessly though waves of hell's minions.

The presentation is decent, with just enough exposition that it never truly roadblocks you. No riding on vaguely-directed platforms like Doom 3. No sections that really turn you off, as the action is the juice, here. The music isn't really classic Doom content, but it truly does feel like it belongs in a Doom game. Mick Gordon did a fantastic job, and no, it's NOT dubstep, dummy. A turning point in the game comes when you enter hell for the first time and you lay waste to demons on their home turf with the most berserk-inducing music egging you on. Before that, one may be forgiven for being bored of all the factories, offices and foundries, so don't let go of Doom until you actually arrive in hell.

Altogether it feels much less arcadey than classic Doom games, but it gives you both a familiar and different sensation. Doom is bound to please you, and keep you psychotically grinning like Richard D James as you revel in the carnage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to say, I didn't really expect a whole lot from the game at first but damn was I wrong to do so. It is classic DOOM with all the action and gore. It is fast paced and it got my heart racing. Had to make sure and stop playing right before bed because it made kept wide awake. The weapon upgrades, suit upgrades, and weapon mods really made the game a little more RPG-ish. I also really enjoyed the story. SPOILER - it ends on a cliff hanger.

All in all, if you played the old DOOM games back in the day like I did, you will love it. It is new but feels familiar.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt runs like ♥♥♥♥ on my computer even on the lowest setting, because I do not meet the minimum requirements. Yet I am having so much fun that I hardly notice it. Id software did an awesome job recreating the series in a new game engine without losing sight of what made the original games all time classics. I will be describing the single player campaign because, let's face it, who the hell buys a Doom game for the multiplayer?

The levels are large and filled with secrets, it is very easy to get lost without a map (Helps to have a map!). The weapon arsenal is pretty much identical to the ones in the original Doom and Doom II, with a few new guns here and there. You will also see some familiar faces in the demon horde. Imps, Pinkies, Lost Souls, Hell Knigts, etc. They are all back. The biggest surprise for me was (and this may sound ridiculous) that you do not need to reload your weapons. And this I am very happy about because I always found it annoying. Also shooting is extremely satisfying, every single weapon feels great.
This game also has a plot, although I beleive that even Doom Guy does not give a single ♥♥♥♥ about it. All he cares about is pouring lead into every hellish creature that have the audacity to stand in his way. So that is another plus for me.

The only thing that bothers me is the checkpoint system. Come on, id software! There is no reloading, so why not have a manual save system? As I mentioned before, the levels are enormous, so being able to save whenever the players wants to would have been much more convinient. Although, in the checkpoint systems defense, there are (hurt me) plenty of them in every level.

Considering all, I would definitely recommend this masterpiece of an FPS to fans of the classics and to newcomers. It is a breath of fresh in a industry filled with modern military shooters.


Tl;dr: would recommend the singleplayer campaing,
do not buy it for the multiplayer.
One of the best FPS-es of all time.\t\t\t\t\t
