\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is only one word to describe this game, and they used it in the title.

I thought using the name as they did made this a remake or a reboot or something, I was wrong. I thought there was no way this is a continuation of the series with that title, and no number attached or anything. I was wrong.

This game is DOOM. It really does follow on from the beautiful 90s shooter I grew up with, in some ways it is a renewal, starting something new, while keeping the old. It plays so wonderfully and is just so fun. They have dared to abandon tropes that have become 'required' for FPS, things that have become so ingrained in the genre who would dare not have them now? Well, they have dared. They have made a game just so much fun thats hard to believe. It's beautiful and it plays so well, I wish there was more~

I enjoyed the snapmap too, and i've seen some improvements, so I'm hopefully they'll keep coming. Creating your own map is fun and interesting, feels to me like writing a story or running an adventure in an RPG lol. I wish more people on my friends list had this game so I could make them play my maps~

This is DOOM. What else do you need to know?


EDIT - The last patch added classic doom level rooms to the snapmap and the one before, the Hell rooms, which is great. I still really enjoy the creation tools and creating a story on the bones of Doom.

They also added Arcade mode to the single player campaign which I have to admit is also really fun too, a points multiplyer score based version of the levels, with leaderboards. Its actually quite a lot of fun to run through trying to get high scores and avoid damage.

This is a terrific game and they keep updating it and adding more, I love it. The single player and snapmap that is, I haven't touched the multiplayer since the beta, and I don't even mind xD\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh, the Doom series. My favorite classic FPS.

This game goes back to the logic that made the original Doom great: gameplay. The focus (at least for the single player) is all about run and gun with updated technology and graphics. It feels like the same game in a way, but with the technological limits of the old days no longer an issue.

The single player is amazing. I want to play it over and over again. I want to get better. I want to hunt down all the secrets. I want to not feel like a pansy and beat it on Ultra Nightmare. It is just plain fun.

The story is pretty bare bones, and really, I don't care. This was never meant to have a great narative. There is just enough to get you from point A to point B.

The original weapons have made their return, and you can hold and use them all. No encumbrance, no inventory management; you simply switch from one to another. There is the addition of equipment (grenades and such), which I keep forgetting I have since I'm so used to the originals. I don't treat this like CoD, so I don't use them when I really should. That's how much it feels like the originals.

I'm not going to talk about the multiplayer. I didn't get this game for the multiplayer.

This is what Doom 3 should have been. This is the true heir of the legacy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM might not be exactly like it's old predecessor, but it's perfect for what it's trying to be. The high-octane violence with extreme maneuverability and over-the-top demonic theme set it aside from any recent FPS on the market. No crouching behind chest-high walls, stealth sections or escort missions. Just you, an objective and legions of demons in between.

PROS:
+Exactly what is says on the tin
+A plethora of weapons, upgrades and challenges to keep the carnage fun
+Amazing soundtrack and overall thematic feel
+Varying level design with impressive scenery and with increasing difficulty.
+Hideously-awesome monsters and bosses that you can study in detail with their logs and unlockable models.
+The combat system is fantastically balanced. Example: going in close and using a chainsaw to earn ammo for your empty guns isn't 'realistic', but it IS engaging and fun as hell.
+The game is self-aware and doesn't take itself to seriously. There's even some humour in small amounts.
+Change difficulty and controller style to what feels best for you.
EDIT: +The newly-added Arcade mode is fun and gives it tons more replay value then it had before.

CONS:
-The rather predictable story might not be your cup of tea at all. I found it pretty cool myself though. And funny at times if you don't take it very seriously.
-Backtracking, looking where you need to go and finding secrets in the confusing maps can sidetrack you too long from the action, but Your Milage May Vary.
-If you ignore all the secrets, challenges and extra's then the core game is only about 5-7 hours long depending on your skill.
-Replay value is pretty low unless you're looking for bigger challenges.I do hope to see more variation in enemies and how you engage them in future installments.
-Multiplayer doesn't feel tacked on. Has multiple game modes, plays smoothly and lot's of customisatoin. Has very little staying power however, and wasn't engaging enough for me to keep playing or buy the seriously overpriced DLC.

I for one can't wait for the sequel and see more cool demons and crazy levels!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne the best single player games i've played in a long time.
With todays game market flooded with games that over promise and under deliver, trying to create 'realism' and 'immersion' its very refreshing to play something that is metal, balls to the wall fun.

This game blends the beloved play style of oldschool shooters and modern graphics and performance incredibly well.
Things like carrying a ridiculous amount of weapons,
Never having to reload
None of this hiding behind cover or regenerating health bars.
Massive death arenas filled with monsters
Secrets and collectibles that yield massive worthwhile rewards

There is even some story and lore to the game which i found cool and interesting but you are in no way forced to pay attention to or care about.
Performance and graphics are perfect. Level design is great especially on the hell missions where you can really appreciate the work put into them.
Difficulty is well justified. Literally every enemy in the game is relentless with their attacks and movement. Standing still for longer than 3 seconds will often result in your death.
Lets not forget the kickass soundtrack that really enhances the fun and brutality of the game.


Fight Like Hell\t\t\t\t\t
