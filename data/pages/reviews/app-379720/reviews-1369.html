\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game knows what it is and does it really well. You're a grizzled space marine interested in killing all the demons, and throughout the game you basically kill lots and lots of demons. The levels are really creative and varied, jumping between Mars and the depths of Hell, and the in-game map system makes it refreshingly easy to see where you've been even with all the varied terrain. I played on the default difficulty and found things just challenging enough, I died a fair number of times but never felt like the enemies were cheap or broken. The game makes you feel unstoppable when you're doing well, and several of the design choices seem to reinforce this. First, you're encouraged to use the shotgun (or super shotgun) as your go-to weapon, which gives you a lot of punch and a preference for close range combat against durable enemies. This feels a lot better to me than Halo's assault rifle, for instance, which needs you to empty an entire magazine and then melee certain enemies to take them down. The health system initially worried me, since I've gotten so used to regenerating health that having to find pickups feels very old school, but the ability to get more health from close-up "glory kills" of enemies helps get you into character as the "doom slayer." If you're worried about low health, the best plan is to dive into combat and kill something up close, which is exactly what an armored marine intent on killing every demon would want to do anyway.

It can seem a bit gamey at times, with much of the game revolving around sealed "arena" areas where enemies spawn and fight you until you kill them all, playing very much like a single player variant of unreal tournament or similar games. I also advise you not to think too hard about why there would be shotgun shells, rockets, human sized armor deep in the bowels of hell. These aren't really downsides, but if you were looking for something that feels more immersive you could be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
