\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSick and tired of all the same FPS games getting released? Give DOOM a shot!
In this game, your health doesn't recharge and you won't get killed reloading, it's like the old school Doom games from the early 90s, story wise this is a reboot (again).

A new feature in this game is the Glory kill system (Inspired by Brutal Doom, a Mod for Doom 1 and 2), if you use this, you can regain a lot of ammo or health, so the game awards you for being a sadistic bastard.

Don't think that cover based shooting will help you in this game, the demons will overwhelm and slay you if you do that, so stay on the offence and pump the bastards full of lead.

As for replay Value, this game have several old DOOM levels from 1 and 2 hidden in the levels of this game which you access by finding hidden levers... you can also find Doomguy action figures.
You also have a multiplayer mode but unless you like Halo you won't find any enjoyment in it, It's fun for a side distraction.
You also have Snapmap which lets you build your own maps, It's fun for what it is, but it isn't Mario Maker DOOM sadly.

Overall, I loved my time with this game, and I hope they made another DOOM game... or how about a Revival of the Hexen series Bethesda?!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSometimes nothing feels better than being given a bunch of guns and a bunch of demons to slaughter. DOOM delivers that at top-notch quality, as a Doom game should. I will focus more on the campaign, as I haven't played much multiplayer as of yet.

Pros:
-Well-optimized, got a stable 60 FPS at max settings. It is more GPU based, as newer games often are.
-Satisfying gunplay, with lots of brutal finishers.
-Downright metal soundtrack, will make you headbang while committing genocide on the demon race. It also 'reacts' whenever you Glory Kill something.
-Good selection of weapons, a lot of modern renditions of classic Doom weapons (Super shotgun, rocket launcher, BFG), and a couple new ones (Heavy Assault Rifle, Gauss Cannon).
-Large range of enemies, all 'solvable' with enough bullets, but no pushover either.
-LOTS of secrets, which often give you advantages for finding them besides a shiny achievement.
-Ultra-Nightmare for those people who hate themselves.
-Decent storyline.
-Upgrades that feel like they mean something ('+5% damage? Cool, I guess...' as opposed to 'Fire your rocket barrage indefinitely? GIVE GIVE GIVE')

Cons:
-Said decent storyline requires a lot of reading to appreciate.
-The game is too short for its price (although I suppose the campaign isn't all of the game).
-Too many Mancubi, I don't know why they throw so many at you. They aren't tough, they just get boring real quick.
-The bosses aren't spaced very well, you do not encounter the first one until ~2/3 into the game.
-Berserk makes all other powerups obsolete, unless you stack Haste/Invuln with it, which then it make all demons obsolete. Haste and Invulnerability could use a buff.
-The ledge grab has made platforming almost give me heart attacks.

This game is great for casuals and champions alike (speaking of Champions...I'm sad now), with a wide difficulty range, a wide range of specializations, and good pacing to keep you engaged.\t\t\t\t\t
