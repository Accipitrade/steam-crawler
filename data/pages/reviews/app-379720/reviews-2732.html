\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImpression 8 hours:

After getting the jump boot upgrade (double jump) and the suit upgrades to display all the secrets the game became enjoyable, since there was less aimlessly wandering the map and the platforming got a lot easier. Level design also introduced greater variety of enemy encounter situations. I can't recommend the first 4 hours, but I do recommend the next four and after. There's a play-time investment before this becomes an enjoyable game.

This is a well designed game though. Not a betrayal of the franchise, but definitely not the epitome of the franchise' identity either. Maybe I'm just old-gen but I feel like all this weapon upgrading and challenge completing detracts from the game. "Kill 4 of this specific enemy in this specific way through the course of this level, we'll give you an ugprade token" isn't necessarily a bad mechanic, it just really, really doesn't feel like Doom. I never did Doom 3 but for me Doom was just the absolute terror of getting to the end of the level. I could put this on harder difficulties for some effect, but that wouldn't replace the "strange challenges needed for upgrading" mechanic with more pure gameplay instead, I have to do those either way.

Thematically it just feels too cute for Doom, too. It feels like a slightly more gorey version of Metroid.

Impression 4 hours:

A great deal of the game is a slow paced platforming/exploration style thing. All the combat happens in big rooms and all the monsters come out all at once when you push the button in the middle of the room (it strictly adheres to this pattern), none of the suspense of "there could be one around every corner" that the old doom games had. The other 2/3rds of the time spent playing is spent jumping over lava and climbing up ledges and checking the map and not shooting at anything. I can pull the trigger far more often if I just turn on Overwatch.

Oh, but the graphics are amazing. There's lots of metal too, if you want that.

Meh.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM (2016) is a solid first person shooter with frantic action that rewards a fast paced, in-your-face style of play. The graphics are good, there's an engaging enough story, and the variety of weapons is great. One of the few games where I didn't just select a favorite weapon and use that through the majority of the game; each of the weapons has their place and the modifiers can be selected per the player's preferences. Upgrades, ammo, armor, and HP were abundant on my "normal" difficulty playthrough. While it only took 13 or so hours to complete (not finding every collectible), they were very enjoyable hours.

A few downsides would be there isn't much variety in environments (two main regions) and the music can get a bit stale. I did not play multiplayer and all of the DLC is geared towards multiplayer so I cannot comment on either. The single player aspect of the game is great, though, and I highly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
