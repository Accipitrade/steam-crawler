\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I'd had this game back around 2005 or so.
I was just barely a teen back then, super afraid of anything even mildly scary. So, I figured the best way to overcome those fears was to play Doom 3 (Which I played like the slow, scary horror game it was obviously going for) Well, spoiler alert, I never made it beyond maybe level 6. There's one clear memory I have of spending over ten minutes dipping back and forth through a doorway to a dark room see if anything happened, and it basically describes my entire experience. In the end, I think I only ended up more afraid of zombies and things in the dark.

What I desperately needed back then was to follow that up with a game like this - where the player character is a demi-god of a man who can carry 20,000 pounds worth of guns & ammo and move at 30 miles an hour; one who the demons of hell and embodiments of all that evil fear and have written stories about detailing the last time he came through and and absolutely stomped them into the dirt. Even on "Ultra-Violence" difficulty and doing my fair share of dying, the heavy metal music, sound effects, beefy animations and grisly glory kills really sell you on the feeling that you're an (almost) unstoppable force of nature that sweats testosterone in thick, ropey strands and feeds on rocket fuel. When you add that to combat so fast, frantic and happening in so many directions that it starts to happen beyond your consciousness (just reacting on a split-second-by-split-second basis with, the gameplay often left me needing to remind myself to breathe a couple times during some particularly intense sections.

There are a few issues I have, like with the automap - which, coupled with the massive, sprawling (and occasionally obtuse) levels, often will leave you hopping around trying to find the way to get to the hidden spot before you give up and the level eventually leads you there 20 minutes later - and the grenades, which I can never find a reason to use. The glory kills also can get repetitive, since 90% of enemies will be stunned first (and ready for the glory kill) and it's the best way to replenish health. I also wish there was a little more variety in the levels, since it's either all science corridors, big machines, red Martian soil and grey/yellow hellscapes, all soaked in blood red - however, at this point, we're starting entering nitpick territory.

For a $60 game, the issues would make the game a cautious recommendation. However, for $20 (and cheaper during sales), I say if you have the hardware and storage space for it ... and especially you're looking for a (nearly) pure gaming experience with high-octane, heart-pounding action ... then this game is definitely worth adding to your library!\t\t\t\t\t
