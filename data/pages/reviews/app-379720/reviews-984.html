\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know that any comparison with its 2004 predecessor might appear pointless, but Doom 2016 demonstrates that id Software has been able to listen to its fans and has brought the franchise back on track. Forget the jumpscares and the horror B-movie dark corridors from Doom 3: this time, even if we're still on Mars and in Hell, it's all about shooting and making aliens explode in combos or finishing them with cruel well animated executions.
Levels are now unique and easy to distinguish from each other. I loved the Foundry one.
If you want to fully experience its satisfactory campaign, you can also focus on customization, collectibles and dozens of achievements and challenges required to unlock features of your arsenal or unleash the full potential of runes. Exploring levels does pay off since some of the secret areas are "levels-in-the-level" who bring you back to corridors and rooms fron Doom 1993!
If, instead, you just want to mindless shoot in a relaxed run you can replay each of the campaign levels one by one or select the arcade mode that gives you onscreen scoring like and rewards your killing creativity.

The gameplay and the AI are so good and fast paced, you'll never get tired of Doom 2016.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing, one of the best in my opinion. I've had so much fun with the campaign and will certainly reinstall this masterpiece once i have more space on my pc or once i buy an external ssd. This game gives you an adrenaline rush like no other especially during the later missions where you feel overwhelmed but not overpowered by the hordes of demons. I feel like the game is balanced overall, you will get punished if you play on harder difficulties and play a bit carelessly but the flow of dodging and moving feels most of the time natural, fun and enjoyable. In the beginning i felt that Doomguy was a bit slow but once i got used to the controls it felt natural, especially in the later missions. One flaw that i had to face in the beginning of the game especially was a bit of the platforming sections feeling that i didn't have the mobility necessary but with the unlock of the double jump boots i felt more in control. Every weapon feels more or less balanced and with its own purpose, even though it is possible to beat the game with just the pistol only or just shotgun I felt some satisfaction knowing where to locate the weakpoints of the enemies and using the right weapon. If anyone is new to this game and wants to buy it a good analogy to switching the weapons is building a table: you can in theory just use the hammer and some nails and some wood and make a table but once when you use an electric screwdriver, some rails you can make a nicer table with drawers and feel more satisfied with your craft.

Another thing that greatly improves this game is the soundtrack. I don't know what else can be said about Mick Gordon's masterpiece and musical genius but in case you read this review and never heard of this game and is soundtrack I can certainly tell you that you won't be disappointed. The soundtrack is so brutal and heavy that it energises you just like a power-up and trully makes you feel like an unstoppable destroyer. Check out his soundtrack on this game and you will realize on what kind of game this is and how fun it will be, even if you are not a heavy metal fan it will still pump you up.

The enemies are plenty and have well thought designs almost everyone, maybe specters are a bit cheap because they are invisible but you get used to them after you face them for the 2nd or 3rd time. Each enemy has its strengths and weaknesses and once you know the right weapon which counters their strengths or exploits their weaknesses then you realize the depth of the combat and how satisfying it is.

This game is a certain 10/10, no game is flawless yet this game has such good gameplay combined with the soundtrack and setting that it doesn't really matter that the storyline isn't that good like another AAA game. It's an fps after all, where you just go through hordes of demons feeling enraged and becoming more and more powerful. The multiplayer is ok-ish but i didn't play that much mostly because i didn't buy this game for the multiplayer. Totally would recommend buying this game, full price or on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
