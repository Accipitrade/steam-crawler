\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOozes gibs of gore-spattered charm. Gibs instead of gobs. See what I did there? ;-) Also, a game with the conceit of "Go to Hell and slay demons by the truckload" makes for a fitting 66th review.

"Doom 2016" is a good time. From its polished graphics, smooth movement and crunchy, metal/industrial soundtrack, the game feels every bit destined to be the 'arcade-y' version of an almost-but-not-quite-serious-face-sci-fi-shooter.

It's refreshing to see 'run and gun' style game play so prominently featured in a recent game. Many shooters incorporate stealth, cover or some other tactical element into the regular game play loop; not so with "Doom 2016". Here, it's a return to form a la the late 90s/early 00s: movement is life. There's even a loading screen tool tip to help drive this point home: "Hell punishes the indolent." "Doom 2016"'s departure from the more prevalent, modern formula promotes something else: engagements which feel chaotic and frenetic in a way that is often one of the signatures of 'run and gun'. While it's great in the moment...

...that frenzy of energy is a double-edged sword. Some times, those fast-paced, brutal encounters help push you forward to the next section... but there are times where you'll want to take a break. The quickly changing visuals, the demonic screams, the soundtrack thumping in the background, combined with the knowledge that the only way forward is by face-pulling the next group and basically ending EVERY demon in the pack, regardless of whether or not it's truly necessary to do so... That can (and does) generate game fatigue.

Speaking of fatigue... No one plays "Doom" for the story, but a game of its length could have used a bit more meat on its plot bones. I don't know how much it would have helped alleviate the above mentioned fatigue, but I wonder if just a bit more story would have facilitated a bit more engagement with the world. "Doom", while fantastic for what it is, runs a bit long for what it is. Hell, there are several occasions where the 'challenge' for part of a level is what seems to be an old-school multiplayer level packed with power-ups that spawns in demons at various intervals. Entertaining? Absolutely, when used sporadically. "Doom" makes prolific use of this sort of set-up, which sometimes reduces 'this is going to be great! BRING IT ON!' to 'right; another slog through 20 demons to the next checkpoint'. That's a shame. The mentality of 'shoot more, shoot harder' seems to be one that hasn't aged gracefully. At least, not when it's the meat and potatoes of the game.

The weapons feel distinct and none seem useless, especially after you acquire mods and/or upgrades. Weapons mods add diversity, efficacy and flavor to the weapon sets. The Combat Shotgun feels good right out of the gate. Sure, it only works at short range, but it packs a wallop when it hits. Later on, you'll get a mod for this weapon that will either turn it into a grenade launcher or offer burst fire mode. Those options expand your toolbox even further. The expansion of options continues throughout the game, as well. Nearly every weapon has two mods, each of which promote, encourage or compliment different styles of play. It's pretty awesome.

The weapon mastery objectives felt lackluster, however. On the one hand, certain upgrades were not only noticeable but offered nearly as much increased depth-of-play as earning the mod itself. For example, being able to move at full speed while using the Gauss Canon's zoom function, or increasing the area of effect for remote detonated rockets. These attributes further increased what was possible to do with the weapon. 'Upgrades' such as reduced cool down for the Pistol's charge shot or decreased time to charge for the Gauss Canon's precision shot, while noticeable, were incremental rather than truly meaningful. They didn't feel bad@ss or like they were opening up different styles of play. Rather, they felt pretty 'meh'.

7/10, thumbs up. Gobs of fun in small doses.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt least for the single player (which is the only thing I played), outstanding game, everything I asked for.

Enemy attacks are all projectile and can absolutely be dodged if you have enough skill. This I think is quite important and accommodates for more aggressive play and action. There is no passive regenerating health, instead you get resources from killing things, which also encourages more aggressive play and rewarding risk with appropriate reward.

While the numbers of enemies and their spawn locations are generally fairly predictable, I think this is a good thing, because it reduces the amount of RNG that goes on in the game and reduces the number of times you think you got killed by things outside of your control. You're the Doom Slayer, you're always in control.

Weapons are pretty good, all serve their purpose. Even the Pistol has its uses... but I wouldn't recommend upgrading that one first, let's just say :P The alt fires are pretty nice and all accommodate for different situations and play-styles. For example, Tactical Scope has very high damage output once fully upgraded for low ammo cost but requires usage of the Scope, which limits peripheral vision. The Explosive Shot for the Shotgun provides cheap explosive power and better range, however it still has lower raw firepower than the Charged Burst. Precision Bolt fires much faster than Siege Mode while consuming less ammo and allowing for movement at the cost of per shot damage when compared to Siege Mode, yada-yada... you get the point! Even the Gatling Rotator I feel has advantages over the Mobile Turret, you have full movement with the Gatling Rotator and the Rotator alt fire just feels snappier.

Overall, this gets a 9.5/10 from me; a movement-based fast-paced shooter that is fun and appropriately rewards bold gameplay? Why aren't more people playing this?\t\t\t\t\t
