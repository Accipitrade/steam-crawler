\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf there is a better shooter than this, I have not yet played it. I'll probably be eating those words when the sequel comes out in a few weeks, but for the moment I remain confident in them. Doom looks awesome, controls incredibly well and is full of powerful tools to use and enemies that know how to make you hurt. The best quality is how it drops the baggage that holds back so many modern shooters. Reloading? Nope. Moving super slow? Nah. Having to cower behind a wall for a while when your health is low? Hell no. In Doom the solution to being low on anything is exactly what it should be: changing your tactics and fighting better, using the glorious arsenal of weapons effectively and building your own unique style based on the upgrades and mods that you like. Snapmap is an incredibly smart addition to the formula too, because while the campaign is highly replayable due to difficulty levels, arcade mode and different tactics and builds, Snapmap takes it to an even higher level by throwing a ton of cool new content at you (yes, it's still reasonably active this long after release). The multiplayer isn't worth bothering with, you can really see why the id Software took it into their own hands for the sequel, but this game is a fantastic experience even if you ignore the multiplayer (Which I recommend that you do).

Ok so as far as I'm concerned, Doom Eternal is better but this game still kicks ass.\t\t\t\t\t
