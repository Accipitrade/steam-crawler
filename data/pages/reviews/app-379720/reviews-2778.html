\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played Doom back in the 90's and it has come a long way since its inception. Except this time, it's in complete 3D with all out ruthlessness you would expect in such a game. The game is very solid, but unfortunately the player base has died down.

DOOM's gameplay is very smooth and sound. I've played a few FPS in my day and I have to admit that DOOM's system is very fluid. Not to mention, the ability to double jump is also included (yeeeebooiiiii)! The single player campaign is definitely a memorable one and will take you on a journey ripping demons apart with your guns or bare hands. The melee system in the game is awesome. You are able to go on a rampage and literally grab the demons and tear them apar. The guns are varied and have some serious explosive impact when making contact. The shooting feels proper and becomes varied when different units start appearing further into the campaign. Multiplayer is just as good as single player and will give Quake players a familiar feeling. It's fast pace and very fun. They have added the ability to become a "demon" so to speak (which is a power-up/rampage sort of ability). To collect this special ability, players have to race to the deploy point and grab it before anyone else (especially opponents). Players can choose different demons as they continue to gain experience after every game played. Only issue with multiplayer is that the player base has drastically decreased since the game first came out. It was possible to find a game whenever, but only certain times of the week are busy.

DOOM has done a fantastic job for presenting its visuals. Everything from the level design, customization of suits (and weapons), HUD, interfaces around levels and enemies themselves are drawn with great detail. I absolutely love how everything came together visually. The sounds are also done absolutely right. Whether you are firing, decapitating, pressing buttons or jumping - everything is well suited to the game. Sounds may not be as important as the gameplay, but definitely do add the extra umph in a game to make it a memorable one. DOOM has done just that.

Overall, a very good game and worth a purchase on sale. I'm just a little disappointed that the multiplayer player base isn't what it used to be. The single player campaign will give you a solid 8~+ hours of fun.

RATING : 7.7/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWould I recommend this game? HELL yes!

There’s not really anything I can add beyond what others have already said: this is a fast and furious FPS with great graphics, a pulse-pounding soundtrack, kickass gameplay and smooth controls. DOOM does a great job of making you feel like a badass while simultaneously forcing you to keep running and gunning to stay one step ahead of the demons. My only complaint is that some of the “platforming” sections of the game feel forced and detract from what you really want to be doing: ripping and tearing!

It took me 17 hours to play through the campaign on normal and I’d expect the game to take about the same amount of time for most players. As such, I’d recommend getting it on sale if you can ($60 is a steep price to pay for less than a twenty hours of gameplay).

Finally, I’ve never played the multiplayer (partially because I’ve heard a lot of negative things) so I can’t speak to that. But DOOM isn’t a game you buy for the multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 2016 is a perfect homage to the 1990s classic Doom series. Maintaining the tense, run-n-gun gameplay alongside the cyberpunk labs and ravaged hellscapes.

The same cast of demons returns for the new adventure, along with a small handful of others. And WOW are they each their own monster! Lumbering, hardy Mancubi, Nimble Revenants, and Towering Barons of Hell all have to be dealt with in each their own way - not always just sidestepping with the shotgun. The pinky demon in the past was nothing more than a guard dog, a blast or 2 from the shotgun and you could forget about them. Now, it's like dealing with a rhino. An angry rhino. Most of my deaths were to the pinky demons, and I wasn't angry about a one of them.

The guns all feel incredible, and you'll find that each one is suited to a certain situation (no more just running entire episodes with the super shotgun...sigh) It is very satisfying to use each weapon (except the pistol. It sucks.) to rack up kills throughout the game. The newly implemented upgrade system allows to diversify your arsenal, nearly tripling the default amount of weapons available. Along with new grenades and decoys, you're sure to always have something to suit the situation at hand.

Doom 2016 is a rush, and though it is designed to play more like a sequential arena shooter, it is a worthy successor to the Doom throne. I will always revere Doom 95 for it's charm, and impact on my early gaming life, but Doom 2016 has earned a place in my heart.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love Doom. Every aspect of the gameplay is so satisfying and rewarding, fastpaced combat and interesting and diverse enemies keeps the game fresh throughout its campaign. The story ends on a cliffhanger, when it stopped I was actually pretty suprised that it was over, the ending kind of came out of nowhere. However, since I bought the game on sale for 20$, it still felt worth the price. Additionally, the multiplayer is really fun. I like how the combat translates into the multiplayer, and although the two weapon system is a bummer, I actually enjoy this multiplayer more than the Quake Champions beta. It reminds me a lot of Halo without regenerating health, and thats good for me. Anyway, this is one of the most fun games I've played and I expect to spend many hours replaying the game on harder difficulties, looking for collectables and challanges, and continuing to enjoy the multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t
