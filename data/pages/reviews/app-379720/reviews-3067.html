\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original Doom was a defining game for me, and I find this reboot to be a worthy successor, but with a few caveats.

I feel the level design focuses too much on large combat arenas rather than encounters organically peppered throughout the stages as per the original Doom. On multiple playthroughs the upgrade system feels tedius and tends to slow down the flow of action as I try to recall where each upgrade chip and argent energy orb is at. Finally, though snapmap has led to some interesting map concepts, it's simply no replacement for proper mapping and modding support.

Regardless, the core action of Doom 2016 is solid, frantic and fun. Hopefully the inevitable sequel will iron out the other issues.\t\t\t\t\t
