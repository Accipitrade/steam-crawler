This is a masterpiece 23 years in the making. The Original DOOM build upon the foundation laid down by Wolfenstein 3D and improved it for all modern day FPS, and this game has taken the next step in giving the middle finger to games like Halo, COD, Battlefeild, Duke Nukem, and Postal 2 & 3. If you have ever been on the sidelines of buying this game this is a game for both of the diehard, hardcore, fanboys of DOOM and for the infant DOOM fan who is starting to experience the nightmare fueled, demon throttling, sticking a chainsaw sandpaper dildo right up your ass after you have been killed hundreds of times by a pinky, specter, hell kight, baron of hell, cyber-demon, or reveant to the point of being completely doused in blood, gasoline and set on fire, while walking in a napalm factory. the maps both inculde a new challenge to the reurning FPS player but also a new improve ment to the DOOM Franchise the big open areas are exciting butt the tight corridors are a huge struggling challenge not only to a fan of the original doom but also the new players who are picking up this wonderful game gem.


That was the good news has been said but now for the depressing news and it has to do with multiple issues for this game. Down below are the darker side of this game.

1. The removal of mods support. Mods have kept to this day people inticed with a game. The fact is that Bethesda, id softwares, and many other video game companies see modders who happen to create impressive content for the games is a bit under whelming. This is an example for a senerio, If bethesda didn't say that they were going to crack down on mods, and a modder created a great mod, then the news gets out that a great mod for this game and a few of them don't have this game will then buy this game for that mod to see what is about and why athat video game is such a the big deal is. The games creators will be gettingmore money that anything and the mod creator will continue to improve the mode to keep people downloading the beautiful masterpiece that they have created so it is a double win win and yet big video game companies feel threated by modders for showing their enthusiasm for a game that they love.

2. Snap-Map. Snap-Map is a great tool but the resaon why it is on this list for the news is due to the amount of variety for demons the cyber-demon, the spider mastermind, the pain elementals, the arachnotron,the arch-vile to chose from hell even the newly added prowler isn't even available to chose when you start to creating map there are many other options to choose from to add like the classic soulsphere, megasphere, the heavy combat armor, to the weapons that can be seen in DOOM 2 Hell on Earth, even the idea to remove the iconic backpack from even being able to be picked up. The idea is great but still need a few tweeks before it can even be grand enough for consumer use in my opinion.

3. The weapons and ammo collecting. in many ways the weapons are extremely sexy for 3D models but the fact that the original game you had to wait a huge amount of time before you got the plasma rifle until close to the end of the game, while in this game it is the on the third level or the second level if you went out of your way to find it, and with the removal of the backpack was a bad idea in my opinion due to the fact that the backpacks were a key hidden item that players could find for a huge amount of fun within the game not only for a huge storage of ammo along with extra ammo to enjoy the brutality of demon annihilation.

4. Armor and Weapon upgrades. Its very strange that every weapon has two mods while some of the armor is a bit difficut to figure out while tring to upgrade it for the next battles. the cost is not too big of a deal but I strongly think that the tougher demons should drop more than just health and ammo. They killed many marines who were wearing the Praetor Suit and a few of them may have eaten a few too many Marines who wore a Praetor Suit so it would be wise to include a few of those in when you glory kill a demon or two.

5. Health and Armor levels. To me the 50% armor levels and the very low chance of collecting health viles which where a key part of fighting off the hellish spawns that have been sent through the portal from hell is a bit important while the fact is that the original doom you had no real armor level up system, but none the less the game is fun, exciting, and action pack to keep players occupied to keep comming back for more enjoyment.

With all that has been said what are my final opinions on theis game the game is both an enjoyable for every type of DOOM fan hell even those of the entire FPS genre will enjoy this game. DOOM has no only set the bar for future FPS games but also rasied the bar a bit more due to the fact that the game is always keeping you on your toes. This game I would highly recommend to those who are not only new to the FPS genre but also the diehard fans who has succmubed to the Call od Duty fatigue.\t\t\t\t\t

Doomguy: *Punches monitor*

10/10 would punch story in the face again.

DOOM is an awesome return/reboot to a neglected franchise. The speed and combat are fluid--even on older machines (i7/12 GB RAM/GTX 750 Ti) the game runs great and maintains a high enough framerate at 1080p to be easily playable (even though FPS is not locked at 60 all the time).

The environments are gorgeous and imaginative, the monsters move and react to you in ways the make sense. Even on lower difficulties (my first playthough is on Hurt Me Plenty), the monsters are still very aggressive and move in a tactically sound way. They'll jump on things to gain high ground, throw fireballs and duck away to avoid your return fire. The glory kills are highly satisfying, and haven't gotten old yet (I'm approximately halfway through the campaign). Every time I see a new monster, the first thing on my mind is "What horrible thing am I going to do to this demon when he starts glowing?".

The thing I like most though, is the tongue-in-cheek humor that follows Doomguy. Doomguy doesn't take your crap. Doomguy doesn't need your crap. Doomguy doesn't care about your lenses, or your Argent Energy, or helping you, or anybody. Doomguy kills demons. That's it. End of story.

In the past, id seemed to be walking farther and farther away from the high-speed arena shooters that they had been famous for (Doom 1/2, Quake 1/2/3) early on and towards things that were......well.....not that (see Doom 3, Quake 4, Quake Wars: Enemy Territory). Such a breath of fresh air to see them recapture what made old arena shooters great---speed, big guns with no clips, beautiful environments and level design that pushes you relentlessly from combat to combat.

TLDR: Described in 3 words...Doom is FUN AS HELL.\t\t\t\t\t
