TL;DR: A living classic, one of the best of action-games of the decade.

Okay, so I don't even know where to start on this one. If you've been living under a rock for past two decades, and you don't know who are guys from id Software, you're better start by playing the original Doom, dated 1993 -- a game that defined first-person shooters back in that time. This is the game by the very same devs, and simply put -- it's amazing.

Doom has everything that action-game needs to have -- intense and very fast-paced gameplay, lots of weapons, gory finishers, amazing story, badass music and sound effects, very immersive atmosphere, and multiplayer that noone would match anytime soon.

Putting it short, game consists of three main parts -- and you don't even have to buy DLCs to access them all, imagine that (looking at you, EA).

Part One: Campaign
This is single-player part of the game, which you definitely should play to get acquainted with the game. Learn about the Doom Trooper story, learn to use weapons, enjoy massacring monsters, gaze upon the glory of the Mars (and the Hell too). There are five grades of difficulty in the campaign, with first being child's play (monster missing frequently, dealing very little damage) and the fifth raising skill cap through the roof.

Each level contains secrets and collectables that help you upgrade your suit (or just enjoy levels from original Doom, 1993), making your life a bit easier, and allowing for more than one run through the levels.

I'm 8 hours in, didn't complete the main story yet, but I'm looking forward to -- judging by how things are going, the end is near. I won't update the review, nor would I spoil anything. Just play it, honestly.

Part Two: Multiplayer
If you ever played Quake III, this is basically what this is. Yeah, there are unlocks, profile level progression, various achievements, etc., but the main gameplay is as brutal as it is basic: you have to run around level, killing enemy team. Capture-The-Flag and the Domination modes are my favorites, as of yet. Really gets you back into the Quake and Unreal franchises wars times.

Part Three: Editor
Now, this part of the game is something I did not expect until I saw how powerful it is. Very simple, yet very powerful tool for making your own maps, campaigns, or even separate game modes (as in, Zombie Survival, for instance, or Tower Defense).

I won't lie, I did not spend too much time in it -- I've learned to use it, made one map, ran around it like a crazy person that I totally am, then got back to my campaign. However, I will definitely come back to this mode once I'm done with the campaign, because the concept of making maps so easily lures me in hard. I hadn't have so much fun creating maps since Starcraft 1, Half-Life 1 and Warcraft 3, by the way, though those editors were pain in the ass, for sure.

- - - - - - - - - -

Verdict
If you haven't played this game yet, but you love action games, this is a must buy. Price is pretty high (especially for some regions), though the game was on a 60% discount this Christmas -- but it'll give you back more than it's worth, in adrenaline and gory deaths of your enemies.

I'd rate this 9.7 out of 10.0, and would recommend to at least try the game to anyone who enjoys a good action game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTen out of ten; would play again. Actually, I already did... Would play again, again.

In all seriousness, 40 hours and two playthroughs in and the campaign is still a blast. I've barely scratched the surface of multiplayer.

PROS:
- Fast, addictive gameplay.
- Unique mechanics from other shooters that encourage risky and aggressive play.
- Amazing visuals. Looks great even on lowest settings.
- Adrenaline-pumping soundtrack and sound design.
- Weapons are all fun to use and have a real weight to them. VERY satisfying. The amount of customizaztion allows for variation in subsequent playthroughs.
- Absurd replay value. Once again, 40 hours before arcade mode, multiplayer, or any DLC.
- Though not the focus, the story is interesting. World is rich in lore.
- You can quit in the middle of a mission. Useful with longer missions or higher difficulties, as you may need a break.
- Perfectly captures what made the originals fun.

CONS:
- Too violent and bloody for the weak.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm gonna be real honest - new DOOM is nearly a better DOOM game than the original DOOM game. It manages to incorporate design elements of all the previous games, and actually pushes the first-person shooter genre forward in terms of gameplay.

You've got the heavy metal -esque satanic kitsch and the cyber future lab aesthetics, which are both wonderfully rendered.

You've got the wide open spaces and the dense twisty hallways; the puzzle-lite exploration-lite aspects of navigating the environments.

You've got all the weapons - rail guns, plasma rifles, double-barreled shotguns, rocket launchers, the chaingun, the chainsaw, and of course the BFG!

But most importantly: each battle is an excersize in spatial navigation, resource management, threat evaluation, and solutions to problems, on the harder difficulties you're constantly in a state of triage juggling which weapon you should be using on which enemy. The pacing not only works from a standpoint of keeping the game modern and accesssible, but feels familiar, linking you back to oldschool FPS like DOOM, Hexen, Heretic, Dark Fortces, Marathon, etc.

I seriously didn't want to like this game, I liked its predecessors, but they're dated - DOOM is practically perfect for the time it's made in, and the games that came before it.

... it IS a little glitchy sometimes. Deal with it.\t\t\t\t\t
