\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an arena shooter.

I'm not kidding. You basically get locked inside of rooms filled with ammo, health, and usually a powerup or two. Demons spawn in order of ascending difficulty as grungy metal music kicks in, and you have to kill them to unlock the doors so you can walk down some corridors and head to the next arena. The game consists of doing this about 40 times. You very rarely get to navigate hostile terrain and pick off enemies from a distance like in retro Doom, and when you do, it's mostly just zombies.

But hell, this game is fun. It takes what worked from Doom 3, and replaces everything else with solid homages to the original Doom. The crazy colorful environments with cliffs and steep drops are back. The frantic fights with a dozen enemies at once are back.

Interestingly, for the first time in Doom history, none of the enemies are given weapons with bullets (the chaingunners even fire energy spheres), meaning that you never have to hide behind cover and you can stay alive just by your dodging skills alone.

Also, there is no reload button. All your weapons automatically reload themselves and most don’t even have a cooldown, meaning you can just keep firing until you’re out of ammo. That’s about as awesome as it sounds.

Amazingly, someone must have realized that Hell was the best part of Doom 3, and this game sends you there not once, but three times. I especially loved the ""Necropolis" level which was almost pure fanserivce to classic Doom in ways I don't dare spoil.

But probably best of all, this game has some serious attitude and a sense of humor. Doomguy is a destroyer of worlds, and you can feel his aggression in all of his actions even though you only see him through his own eyes, thanks in no small part to glory kills.

I found that the notorious “glory kills” while odd at first, worked to the game’s advantage. For those who don’t know, when an enemy is almost dead, they can be finished off with a single flamboyant melee attack which will also spew a fountain of health pickups into the air. Not only do they humorously show off Doomguy’s rage, but they aren’t really all that new since they were ripped straight from Brutal Doom. In retro Doom, you’d have to run over dead bodies to collect the ammo drops, and it wasn’t unusual to give the enemies a punch or a grind with the chainsaw to conserve a little ammo. Glory kills are basically that, but just happen to make it look a bit cooler. They become a bit less important in the second half of the game, as the game starts throwing you into some ridiculous situations and you’ll probably just want to blast enemies to smithereens without worrying about carefully whittling down their health.

I give Doom 4 an 8 / 10. If you were hoping for the true return of classic Doom, I’ve got news for you: nothing is going to be like classic Doom. If you want classic Doom, go play that. ZDoom.org is on your right, Doomworld.com is on your left. Truth is that classic Doom never left; source ports exist which can add dynamic lighting and make the game look ridiculously modern, and the community is still alive and pumping out dozens of new maps and mods every week. But after the strange horror/corridor shooter that was Doom 3, Doom 4 returns the series to its roots in the best way possible: it will transport you to a colorful world of sprawling martian compounds and hellscapes, a place where you never need to stop running at full speed, a place where it just feels good to pull the trigger and watch hundreds of imps and mancubi crumble before you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf they didn't call it Doom, it would've been a nice homage to it, but as a Doom title, it doesnt live up to the series' standards.

2019 UPDATE:
Recently replayed the game on Ultra-Violence, this review still holds up. Not a bad game, but not a proper Doom game

The gameplay feels slow compared to the original Doom, with an attempt to mask it with flashy finishers that just get annoying after the first 30 minutes (did I mention that the game was designed with them in mind? Enemies drop ammo and health when killed via meele and you won't find much ammo if you ignore this mechanic). Enemies freeze as soon as you lower their health to finsher-levels and won't attack at all until they recover after around 10 seconds.

Movement feels like you're sliding around (possibly due to the lack of headbob) and not controlling an actual human being. There's almost no air control with the double jump, I'm not sure why they even decided to put that in, nor Doom or Quake ever had double jump (as far as I remember).

The pistol is incredibly pathetic, even by Doom standards. Shotgun doesn't kill weak enemies in one hit, ruining the flow while fighting many of them. Super shotgun feels like it has absolutely no range. The Rocket Launcher is slower and does less damage than it's original counterpart. The Chainsaw was reduced to a flashy finisher that turns enemies into ammo piñatas. The rest of the guns feel from ok to decent.

The muisc is horrible, I actually lowered the music volume because I couldn't stand the constant noise blast while playing. It's a bad attempt at bringing the classic Doom songs to a new generation.

Level design is pretty basic, nothing terrible but it lacks Doom's key hunting and maze-like levels. Every level is Fight Room to corridors to Fight Rooms and so on. Secret 1993 doom levels are blatantly scaled down so people dont realize the new Doom is actually slower than the old ones.

Art wise it feels as overdesigned as most fps these days, you can barely recognize enemies from the background since everything gets coated by a single colored light. Demons (for the most part) don't feel as hellish as the original ones (The Cyberdemon is the biggest offender here, he doesn't look intimidating at all, he even looks slightly cartoony) Hell is not covered in enough flesh and mostly looks like desolate ruins instead of the land of eternal suffering.
Doomguy is extremely overdesigned and overarmored, defeating the purpose of him being an above average dude who kicks hell's ass back to itself (And that's without counting the whole "chosen one" deal they had with him, about how he's the only one capable of fighting hell).

The modding capabilities aren't even close of what you can do with the original doom, although SnapMap was a nice attempt at imitating the original doom's moddability.

I didnt try the multiplayer, I was never interested in it, really.

Overall the game is mediocre (Not absolutely unenjoyable, mind you) and I'd wait until the game is at least 50% off to buy it. enjoyable but doesn't really represent what made me love the original game to this day.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen it came to gameplay, I absolutely loved the short time I spent playing Doom. The gfx were wonderful, the gameplay happened at a rapid pace, and I was starting to get involved in the SP story line. Unfortunately, I happened to be a victim of the developer mode bug. In a nutshell, out of blue I received a notification that I was put into developer mode, and as a result certain features of the game were disabled. According to the dev message, the solution is to delete my SP Game save and start from scratch. Unfortunately, this did not work. Even deleting the files and folders in C:\\Users\\me\\Saved Games\\id Software\\ did not solve the problem (there are a few more steps that I followed to try and resolve this problem, but I digress). When I first open the game after deleting the files I can play just fine. However, as soon as I quit Doom and then re-launch, I get the "you are in Dev mode" error.
Fortunately, I had played the game for less than 2 hours so I just ended up requesting and receiving a refund from Steam.
If it wasn't for this absolutely horrible bug, I would definitely recommend this game. In the end, I simply cannot recommend purchasing at this time until Bethesda releases a fix (as of 06-02 this bug has been met with silence).
Honestly, I have no idea how many people its affecting; for all I know it could be less than 1%. That being said, there is currently a 500+ post thread on Steam regarding this issue. Ironically, the thread was started by a Bethesda Developer.
Long story short, if the Dev Mode bug gets resolved, I will definitely purchase the game again. Otherwise, I am happy with my refund!
Just FYI, I strongly recommend any new purchasers test out their Doom install as soon as possible to determine whether or not you are affected by this bug (If you play the game for more than 2 hours, Steam will not give you a refund). Based on my experience, the best way to determine if you are affected is to simply exit out of the game and then re-launch it. If you receive a message about network services being disabled and dev mode being enabled, you are most likely affected by this bug. Honestly, you should perform the exit/relaunch process as many times as possible. Some people don't run into this issue until they are hours into the game. IMO there is simply nothing you can do to fix it at this time.
Hopefully Bethesda will at least post an update as to whether or not they are planning on fixing this.\t\t\t\t\t
