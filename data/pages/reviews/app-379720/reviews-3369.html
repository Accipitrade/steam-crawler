\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKeeping it simple and doing things right. That is the best way I can describe DOOM.

If you seek an engaging tale in your games, or if you are looking for innovation than this game may not be for you but (and it is a big BUT) if you just want to have some mindless fun and engage in a shootfest from start to finish DOOM may be right up your alley.
The game plays smoothly and battles are hectic, made all the more so by the remarkable fluidity of the control system. You will grapple upon any ledge in reach, dodge and twist all the while shooting at wonderfully rendered enemies while trying desperately to evade their assault.
Graphics are great with an abundance of particle effects and the game engine itself seems to be a winner.
Can't comment on the multiplayer since I haven't played it yet but for me the game is worth a buy on the basis of its single player campaign alone.

DOOM: a refreshing return to the days when games were all about fun...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first I had my doubts from the beta reviews... But this game is freaking awesome! I've clocked in 3 hours today alone. I'll say what I like and what I dont like. NOTE: This review will only cover Single-Player campaign. I haven't played the multiplayer more than one or two matches.

Pros:
+ It really is DOOM. I remember playing the original DOOM... Running, gunning and trying to get to that exit room. Looking for secrets.. and of course the action was intense. Usually being outnumbered 10:1 or more. This game really takes that and brings it right into modern gaming. Running and gunning is absolutely fluent and you feel like a real badass and like you really did a lot after a round of killing.

+ Collectibles. There are a small set of collectibles throughout the game, such as dolls and classic DOOM maps. I love that these were thrown in and they are a charm when you stumble upon them.

+ Exploration. Along with the collectibles, the game actually rewards you for exploring the area and finding tokens to upgrade weapons and armor. It's really nice to be rewarded for exploring... as if the visuals werent enough... Which brings me to:

+ Graphics. Holy hell this game looks great. The maps are very VERY well made, and the small attention to details everywhere make it feel like a living, breathing, place. The locations look incredible. The map designs are well made and it's so big, each level, you just cant help but climb and explore and dive and all while killing demons.

+ Customizability. I love that I can choose my playstyle based on the perks of my weapons and where to spend my upgrade points.

+ Monsters. Look great. Look fluent in animations. They feel like real living things and I just wanna kill em all!

CONS:

- Audio sometimes a bit choppy for me. I'm not sure why but it feels glitchy. Almost eerie. I still love the game and it adds a little bit of a nice touch as its a hell-themed game. But occasionally annoying. However this is probably just an issue on my end... But even so, ive had some minor performance issues.

-Repetitive finisher kills. They were awesome at first but quickly lose their charm as you see the same 5 or so finisher moves. One where he rips open the jaw. One where he smashes the head. One where he snaps the neck. One where he puts own foot through face, and one where he punches head off. I wish there were more variety there, but it's not too bad in the heat of the moment.

Overall my GOTY 2016 so far.\t\t\t\t\t

PROS
-Engaging and brutal combat
-Great controls and gunplay
-Lengthy singleplayer campaign with branching, semi-linear levels

-Buckets of customization
-Awesome music and sound effects
-Decent graphics
-Lots of weapons
-Murderable hordes of demons everywhere
-The abilty to have different video/audio/keyboard settings for the Campaign, Multplayer, and SnapMap

CONS
-FPS drops in Campaign

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing campaign! Just finished it on Ultra Violence and I can say it was so worth it! I fiddled around with the snapmap and while i had fun placing enemies and rooms I got very quickly frustrated at the games attempt to control it. Spawning into my new creation i immidiatly knew some thing was wrong by the fact that the enemies i spawned were not there and instead monsters that i never even placed were being teleported in all over the place. So i am not a fan of the snapmap. I have not tried the multiplayer because honestly that is not my thing. I bought DOOM to RIP and TEAR some demons. And it delivered.

On a sidenote, its depressing to see so many people from the brutal doom community smashing this game. Its like they expect the new doom to be BRUTAL DOOM instead of DOOM. That is not Id's vision. Sure they borrowed the glory kills from Brutal DOOM. But not much else. This is a DOOM title. Not a Brutal DOOM title, If brutal doom is what you want your not going to get it here.

We have been waiting way too long for a new DOOM and this was well worth it for those of us who still enjoy the old DOOM. It's has a great campaign and great gameplay. Much better than most shooters that have come out in the past few years. Besides Wolfenstein the New Order, this may be one of the best shooters in the past 5 years.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving play and beat Doom 1, Doom 2, TNT and Plutonium with Brutal Doom installed on "Black Metal" difficulty and beating them all again vanilla on "Ultra Violence" difficulty in the hype of waiting for this game to come out.

I think I know what I'm talking about if this game didn't feel like Doom.

This game did feel a little Alien at first maybe the first 5-10 minutes of the game opening but after that I fell right into the grove like I had just booted up Doom 1. Since this game had HUGE shoes to fill I was a little worry maybe the game couldn't live up to hype.

I have never been so wrong in my whole life THIS GAME IS incredible. This is pretty much everything I wanted this game to be, the combat is very fast which slowly begins building starting you off with the fast weak Imps that toss fireballs at you to the massive Baron of Hell which is huge and is the biggest baddest thing on two legs since T-Rex.

Pretty much all the orignal power ups and weapons have returned and are space throughout each level before you reach the king of them all the BFG-9000.

The Music is AMAZING very similiar to Killing Floor 2, having the Hell Choir mixed with heavy rock is prefect and fits this game like a glove.

This game does have a lot of large open spaces which when you break these demon nests the room or area is locked up and you need to defend yourself from the spawning waves of demons. I was worried that these would get annoying after a couple but they do not the great level design of theses rooms and areas makes each locked down moment unquie and different spicing the gameplay up.

My ONLY very small complain about this game is sometimes when you are walking around the world after dealing with a huge group of enemies weather it be on Mars or in Hell their is very little or no music playing making it a little too quiet since the rest of game is so fast and instense with heavy rock music blasting. I would have like some tone down rock music or just general level music playing in these areas like the old games and then when the instense moments of the game do happen have the loud heavy rock and metal start to play

This game is worth full price just for the single player alone. Now mind you if you buy this game thinking it will become 1993 and your Moon shoes will be hip again, you might be a little disappointed. But if you liked Doom from the 90's or like old school FPS's and would like to play a very will put together reboot then I can not recommend this game enough.

666/10 Rip and Tear\t\t\t\t\t
