\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHands down one of the best games I've ever played. Much better than Doom Eternal in my opinion. Rip apart demons to your hearts desire with no cutscene BS. Didn't care much about the story in the beginning but game did such a fantastic job of world building that I got very interested in the backstory. No game has ever made the player feel like an unstoppable force as good as this one. Stay still and you're dead. Move, and you're rewarded with unparalleled satisfaction.

Now this game is pretty bug free for a Bethesda game but very rarely expect issues like sound direction bugs and a monster getting stuck somewhere that you have to find and kill so you can advance.

If you've already finished the game and are wondering what was going on read on below:

The Doom Marine/Doom Slayer was the leader of Night Sentinals, a brotherhood of strongest warriors protecting the wraith beings from the outside world and vise versa. His son was killed in the invasion of hell so he betrays the Night Sentinals to have his son resurrected from hell. After a hell priest corrupts the wraiths and all the other Night Sentinals are killed, the hell lords betray their bargain with the Doom Marine and bring his son back in demon form as the Icon of Sin. That is when Doom Marine goes on his killing spree and swears to kill every single demon and end the dominion of hell. After a while killing demons and winning every fight, the heavens reward doom with more power and speed. Why does he wear a different armour than his Night Sentinal brothers? In his conquest of hell he finds "The one who shall not be named" who forges him an invulnerable armour (the doom armour) and unstoppable weapon (the crucible) from pure hell energy. But he also hides some secrets in the weapon without doom marine's knowledge to bring his downfall (I guess we will see what that is in future games)... nope they completely threw all of the story they built into garbage in Doom Eternal\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBethesda did a great job on this game, and now that I've moved on from it, I find myself missing playing it. It was a lot of fun.

Compared to DOOM3 before it, this game is much lighter and less terrifying. It is more arcade-y than DOOM3 was. I was hesitant to start playing it at first, expecting another nightmare experience, but I was very glad to find that this game does not rely on dark tunnels and monsters jumping out to try and excite you. I'd play through this game again happily, whereas I don't really have any interest in playing through DOOM3 again.

The graphics are top notch. The level design is awesome. The audio is stunning and immersive. The music was great, and the voice acting too. The story was engaging, though I might have preferred a bit more substance to the ending.

All in, I spent 36 hours on this game, redoing levels to ensure I found every secret, and seeing everything there was to see. I didn't even touch the multiplayer, it's not really my thing, but there is a lot to do there as well.

A good buy, especially when it's on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, here it is. I finally got around to skipping Final DOOM and DOOM 3 just to play the latest installment. And, honestly, I'm very happy I did, because (and this might come as a shock)... this is probably the best DOOM game in the franchise.

I know, shocking. Especially coming from me.

DOOM 2016 (or DOOM 4 if you want to call it that) follows the traditional DOOM narrative where you play as the unsuspecting space marine (Formerly Doomguy, and now The Doomslayer... which is a title I definitely prefer) is tossed into events far out of his control, as he must punch, shoot, bludgeon, annihalate, and eviscerate every last god**** demon on the face of Mars.

Oh, yeah, and on Hell too.

First off, DOOM 2016 has some fantastic presentation. The music, sound design, and graphics are miles beyond the first two DOOM games (as expected). I'm not one to talk about how new games are better than old ones based on graphics alone, but as far as presentation goes... this DOOM felt far more visceral and fast paced than its predecessors.

Not saying I hate them, I love both DOOM I and II, it's just that DOOM 2016 has a better soundtrack and better sound effects for enemies and kills. I just prefer it. If you prefer the originals, that's fine. I like popping in some classic DOOM when I'm bored and there's nothing interesting on ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.

This new installment expands on many of the foundational elements of the DOOM series and appropriately opens up the level design to suit these new updates. The levels are massive arenas where you can sprint, bounce, and jump using the new set of the Doomslayer's abilities to rip and tear your way through all of Hell's hordes.

DOOM also sets a shining beacon of how to make a damn good action game as opposed to the popular concept shown in games like Battlefield and Call of Duty. I actually played Advanced Warfare on my PS4 for the first time around the same time as installing DOOM 2016, and the differences between these two FPS's is astounding, to say the least.

Advanced Warfare includes new abilities similar to that of DOOM (weapon and armor upgrades, double jumps, sprint boosts), but the levels are still as linear as ever and the enemies remain stationary in most of those levels. With the increase in mobility, Call of Duty still blindly shoves these mechanics into their cookie-cutter levels which makes the game feel often awkward, like the game can't make up its mind whether or not you can run around like a rampant killer or stay stuck behind cover waiting for enemies to pop up.

DOOM takes advantage of using its streamlined story to keep you moving, but it doesn't just throw action sequence after action sequence. There are moments to calm the player down through puzzles, platforming, and even story segments. Advanced Warfare, on the other hand, continues to shove mindless exposition into its loading screens and keep players on a conveyor belt from one area filled with enemies to another, with little downtime.

Honestly, I've never liked Call of Duty. In the future, I might go and try out Modern Warfare and Black Ops on my Steam account and review them but I'll never play Advanced Warfare again. Count these few paragraphs as my fair review. It's a mediocre 5/10 game. Nowhere near the 9.1/10 that IGN gave it.

I digress. DOOM does have its faults. While it is certainly better than its previous installments and leagues better than the Triple-A FPS titles being released today... that doesn't make it perfect. For one, I think the weapon upgrades are a welcome bonus to the game but the armor upgrades are just kind of tacky. They never did a whole lot for me. And while I like HOW the story is presented and progressed, I'm not a fan of the story itself. It's pretty shallow and predictable which- while fitting Cormack's quotation about stories in video games- doesn't really align to what I think makes a video game a masterpiece.

I think DOOM is well worth the money its priced at. I got it on discount, but after plugging in 9 hours, I'm definitely going to put in another enthusiastic 9 more into playing the campaign again in the future and maybe even more hours scrounging around the levels to find every last secret. The replay value in DOOM 2016 is high and the amount of fun you'll have is even higher. I'm giving DOOM an 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t
