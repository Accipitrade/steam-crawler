\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first started it up (Ultra-Violence) I felt it was a bit too frantic and wondered if I'd even get through it. I found the controls a bit wonky and the level design was really difficult to grasp at first.

But..

I spent a couple hours finding all the secrets in map 2 and by the time I was done I realized that this game's level design is amazing and very much like the original Doom games. Once I mapped my controls to something more comfortable, the rest of the game was amazing. The levels are linear, but contain backtracking (especially for secrets.) Once you're a few hours in the frantic pace becomes more natural and you wonder why you've ever had issues. IMO, this competes for "best shooter experience" in a decade easy; and it is probably better than Half-Life 2 in that realm (though obviously HL2 has more "atmosphere")

I'd say the *only* complaint I really have is that sometimes when you're trying to figure out how to trigger a secret you can get locked into a later portion of the level and be unable to backtrack and find the secret. Your only options at that point are to restart or to leave it be; and sometimes this anti-backtracking mechanism happens from a door that locks behind you so you have no idea that it was going to occur.

Also, this is the first game I've played where "climbing" from a jump felt perfectly natural\t\t\t\t\t
