\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Note: Only played single player and Arcade modes, earning all single player achievements.)

Pure fun. This game is unapologetic and exactly what a Doom game made in 2016 should be. Rip'n'tear through the hordes of hell. Doom gives you a multitude of weapons to work with (the classic ones we all know and love), and each one has customizations that you can get in-game to further power them up. The game dispenses with what have become other first person shooter tropes such as cover, regenerating health, complex characters you play as, etc. You are Doom Guy, silent protagonist who likes to kill and doesn't give a flying feather about the actual plot. The NPCs care of course, but you are there to kill.

The game wastes no time in dropping you right in. You very quickly obtain a shotgun, ignore the plot being exposited your way (Doom Guy literally shoves it away) and are on your way doing what the game was built to do: FPS at its finest.

I won't go into much more detail, but it is what it is: A gory game where you shoot at all the demons until they die. Would love for this game to get a sequel or a singleplayer-mission expansion of this kind, as it's easily the best part of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

3) Lag, lag lag lag lag lag lag, players can be hitting you whilst you can't even see them, or you can line up a killshot with the gauss cannon only for them to teleport out of existence
4) Demons are stupidly OP, and it works even worse in conjunction with the above point. I've waited above a rune spawn only to immediately be looking up at an enemy revenant who teleported in using their Fisher Price laptop.
5) An indication to when special weapons spawn would be nice. It's not fun to have someone teleport the chainsaw I had no idea they had into my lower abdomen.
In it's current state, the MP just feels like something out of a mod community, that kind of enjoyable until the novelty wears off kind of feel.
-DLC seems kind of overpriced and worthless, just some new colours a few maps, some crappy weapons and a demon each.

But despite these, they aren't critical parts of the game, just little bits on the side that don't work as well as the SP
In short, get it.
