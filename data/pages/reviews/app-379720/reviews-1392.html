\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood old Doom sure changed a lot. I grew up with the original and was very disappointed with the 2004 remake.

This Doom is something of a mix of the two. It looks, feels and works like the original, but more than that: it looks and feels exactly the way we imagined it in 1993 when our fantasy was our 3D accelerator. In that aspect Doom is flawless.

However, it lacks the original dynamics. The levels are quite linear. There's nothing left of the old mazes. While most maps still offer a lot of exploration, it's a far cry from the classic maps where you could get proper lost. It's designed for a simpler minded generation who doesn't like to think a lot over their games.

The jumpscare is also gone. All fights take place in confined areas, reminescent of Quake's multiplayer arenas, and it rarely happens that a lone monster suddenly just pops up in your face. You always know when a battle is coming up, and in most cases it's up to you to decide when to begin. It gets repetitive and boring after a while. When you don't hear growls or see movement, you can be almost certain that there's no danger. This isn't what Doom used to be. It's boring and unimaginative after a while.

The gameplay is also fairly unbalanced. Some monsters are ridiculously overpowered, and unless you're playing on the lowest difficulty, you'll die a lot. At some points it's outright unplayable for someone not very experienced in FPS games. You know, not everybody has been playing this genre for decades. Even I got frustrated over the cyberdemon. It sure isn't impossible to beat, but it's like trying to have sex on a bicycle. The fun of it is gone.

The game ends when you reach the first boss. There it turns into absolutely mindless grinding and zero fun. The bosses are ridiculously overpowered and it takes a HUGE effort to grind through them. Don't even try as a casual player. This ♥♥♥♥ needs dedication, days or weeks of practicing and exceptional luck. Unless you have no life and don't mind spending all your time on a repetitive and boring part, loading and reloading over and over again, then hey, this Doom is for you. Otherwise that's where you stop playing and forget about the entire game. And no, it's not about "git gud". It's simply pain to play.

All in all, it's a superb game which slides back to mediocre after a while. It's still worth playing, but ultimately it loses its charm and fails to deliver the same thrill as its classic predecessor. I can't imagine today's kids writing fan fiction or demanding a movie to be made of it. It sure will happen, but not like back in the 90's. At the end of the day it's still a good game worth having, but not the true return of a legend. The unbalanced boss fight part is the worst party breaker ever in gaming history.\t\t\t\t\t
