\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played the original Doom and Doom II back in the 1990's I can comfortably say Doom 2016 is the Doom that the fans truly deserve. It's not a sequel or prequel to any previous Doom. It's more of a ''how the original Doom would have been made in 2016'' Doom 2016, is an extremely fast paced shooter with all the gore and gibs you can handle. However If you're looking for a rich fulfilling story, and prefer RPG-FPS games to FPS-FPS games you may need to look elsewhere.

Doom 2016, throws action in your face right out of the gate, you are simply a nameless faceless space marine (Doom Guy) who awakens inexplicably tethered to what a appears to be an operation table of sorts. Naturally you break free of your bindings and slaughter everything in the room. You're only given a very brief explanation of what's transpired in the time prior to your nap on the operating table. From this moment forward this game is an ocean of blood, guts, and explosion's from one checkpoint to the next. There is some story there, in short it goes something like this, The Union Aerospace Corporation (UAC) has somehow opened the portal to hell on the planet Mars, and in an attempt to solve man kinds energy crisis, is attempting to refine a limitless energy source supplied by hell itself known 'scientifically'' as Hell Energy. Naturally though, things get outta hand, demon's crossover into our dimension, from their your journey of kicking demon ass begins.

This game is fantastic at being a Doom game. The action is near constant, the gameplay is lightning fast, and the gore is beautifully grotesque. This game pays a lot of omage to it's elder brethren, many of the demon's in Doom 2016 are essentially hi fidelity version's of their 1993 selves, this also applies to the weapons and even Doom Guy himself. This game features a really unique health system in which your enemies essentially act like pi√±atas for health and ammunition. This discourages the duck and cover (rechargeable health) playstyle so many FPS games have adapted to these days, it's this system that really contributes the most to this games fast paced action, as you are CONSTANTLY on the move and plowing through horde's of enemy's not only to clear the room, but also to regain precious lost health and ammo, and in my mind that is the way a Doom game is meant to be. Kudos's to Bethesda for realizing this. This game also features a mechanic affectionately called (Glory Kills), this is the game's way of spicing up the melee combat. To perform a glory kill all you have to do is shoot an enemy until he staggers and stumbles around in a dazed state, then get in close and hit the F key. Depending on your angle of approach, you'll be treated to one of several brutal melee execution's of your foe, glory kills range from punch's and kicks to the face, to full on neck breaks and curb stomps. Either way, this type execution is also encouraged in game, as it leads to greater health and ammo drops from enemy's.

As of October 2016 the graphic's are top notch, having state of the art graphic's for their time is usually a staple for Doom games. I'm running this game on full ultra everything and ninety five percent of the time I'm getting a 55 to 60fps. However, I have run into an issue that's persisted throughout the game. Sometimes, not always but sometimes when loading into the next level, my framerate will inexplicably drop to around 15-20, essentially making the game unplayable. The only remedy I've found to fix this issue is to quit to desktop and reload the game (a relatively short process). Aside from that though, the game is buttery smooth, beautifully detailed and well optimized. Unless you want to count the unexplained FPS drop, I have not currently in my 11 hours of gameplay experienced a single glitch.

The sound is good, I've got a decent headset with 7.1 audio so I get to hear all the wonderful bone crunching and thumping explosion's. However, one complaint. The guns...Though Sound decent and hit hard...Lack A certain 'boom'' that I like. Overall they sound very good...But Not great in my opinion. I had (among many others) this very same complaint about Doom 3 when it released in 2004. However, in Doom 3 it was to a MUCH greater extent.

Another complaint that I have is the Chainsaw...Not The Chainsaw itself, as it's an awesome weapon and one of my personal favorites to use! However, that leads to my complaint. The mechanic in which the Chainsaw is implemented discourages you from using it as a primary weapon. The Chainsaw is immensely overpowered, and when fully charged (with gas) it's essentially a one hit kill on almost everything in the game, not only that but what you do kill with it, drop's a massive amount of ammuniton for you, a mechanic that is unique to Chainsaw kills. While this all sounds great, you only get about six charge's on the Chainsaw, and fuel for it is relatively scarce. So I found myself saving the Chainsaw for when I encouterd a true big bag like The Baron of Hell or maybe a Mancubus, when in reality I wanted to pull out the Chainsaw and just saw my from point A to Z in this game. A minor complaint, but a complaint none the less. Also, on that note. I am not a fan of disappearing bodies. Yes, I understand that ragdoll physics are very taxing and it wouldn't take long before than wonderful 60fps turned into 15fps with mountains of mutilated corpses stacking atop on another. However, Left For Dead managed a way around this, by taking all rag doll animation's out of the zombies (or in Doom's case demons) after death. This allowed corpses to stay on screen for some time after death and still not over tax your machine. Seems like Doom 2016 could have done something similer. Not the end of the world, but I would have enjoyed overlooking all the carnage after one of the many epic battles you will have in Doom.

Let's see what else...I've Invested around 12 hours of gameplay, and I don't feel like I'm near the end of the game, I've yet to face the first (of several bosses). Of that 12 hours however, probably about 8 or 9 of them are actual playtime. As I have the tendency to take breaks and leave my self in game for when I return, thus racking up false hours. But even so, I got this game on sale for about $35 and I am not disappointed. Even if I had paid the full $59.99, I think I'd still be happy with it, which is a rare thing for me to say about game's these days. Nothing pisses me off more than paying $59.99 for a game that give's me 4 hours of gameplay with little other content. Thankfully that doesn't seem to be the case with Doom 2016 as the campaign is rich and lucrative with action, and then there's the snap map level creation system, which allows players to develop and fine tune their own levels, though I haven't played with that much yet, I suspect that it'll provide me with more hours of entertainment even after I've gone through the campaign.

So, with all that said...It's Time to weigh this game in.

(Pros)
Graphically beautiful
Very well optimized
Fun action packed gameplay
Pay's omage to old Doom very well
Seemingly long single player campaign

(Cons)
Some Weapon's sound a bit weak
No depth to initial story (none expected however)
I want to use the Chainsaw more
Vanishing bodies

(Verdict)
9/10 - Solid fun modern hi fidelity Doom action.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile living up to the legacy that DOOM has is rather impossible; I have to say this reboot does a great job of capturing the gameplay of the original but also brings its own style into it. Id Tech 6 brings a excellent looking game, as well as the technical side of Id Tech being unmatched by some engines. Its runs very well and has a great amount of graphical options to tweak to your preferred experience and computers potential. The game also has some great sound design and music that cranks the action and atmosphere to the max.

The only problem I had with the games campaign is once the games credits started rolling I was a little disappointed. I felt the climax of the game should have been more intense, or bigger than the rest of the game. I was expecting maybe another couple hours than an intense final boss, but that wasn't the case.

SNAPMAP AND MULTIPLAYER:
SnapMap is a great addition to the game, and can add many hours to creating various maps, modes, and scripts to share with the community. It has great features, but also shows how limited it is as well. Being able to play with friends can add many more hours to the game. It shows how it was made with console players in mind however, and the opportunity to modding the game has come down to using SnapMap only sadly.

The multiplayer; while some might disagree, I found the online to be very lacking. The campaign shows much confidence in what it aims to do. The multiplayer however is in a middle point of having classic Quake/Doom pickups and "fast paced" gameplay, but then gets stuck on todays shooters with custom load outs, unlocks, and the main game mode being played is Team Deathmatch.

Regardless, single player is great and full of fun. SnapMap is somewhat limited but a fantastic addition. Multiplayer is there, and some fun can be had; but really isn't the focus of the package.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is an amazing return to the days of well built single player campaigns. The single player contains the fast paced gunplay that doom has been known for, as well as amazing huge level designs, full of secrets. The singleplayer campaign itself is amazing and child feature as a complete game. However, id/Bethesda have provided us with two more parts to this game. The snapmap is a custom level builder somewhat akin to halo forge. It is not insanely deep, but it is a well built tool that allows for the creation of some amazingly fun and creative levels. The multiplayer of this game is by far its weak point. The multiplayer is a fairly basic online multiplayer setup, and does not have the same polish and depth to it that the single player aspect did. The combat and movement are not what you would expect from a traditional arena shooter, and contain some aspects that make gameplay seem shallower. For one, some weapons function slightly differently in the multiplayer, and some are not even present in the singleplayer. Also, preset bonus spawns sometimes lead to camping around certain points. All in all, the game is very well done, if you are into sp campaigns, you will probably love this game, however, if you are looking for a competitive in depth multiplayer, I would suggest looking elsewhere, although it can still be very enjoyable casually.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen they released the Doom reboot, my first question was, if this old concept of simply find keys and shoot everything can still work in 2016? Also after not having liked Doom3 that much, I wasn't that excited for a new edition. But when the game was on sale for 50%, I thought it might be nice to get it - and I don't regret it. In fact, the concept really still works. There's not much story, it's simply fast and arcade, but that's what you expect. But even though the gameplay is fast, it still takes you quite a while to complete the campaign (took me 19h with searching for secrets).

The first couple of levels are quite big and the level design feels excellent (IMO the complexity of the levels decreased in later levels though). It's also nice to search for the secrets and to complete the additional challenges. The gameplay is pretty the same for all levels. Every now and then you enter a room or an area which will be sealed until you cleaned it completely. There's some variation in the locations. While the first missions are indoor, you'll later enter hell before you return to the UAC station again.

The Glory Kills are a nice addition to the game, especially if you're low on health or ammo because those kills will get you some rewards. If you liked to sweep out the levels in the original doom by only using the double barrel shotgun, then you may also like the reboot. It's still fun to try it with the shotgun only, at least on lower difficulty levels. There are parts of the game where you'll need the bigger guns though, especially with the boss enemies. Personally, I'm not a big fan of those, but they're simply part of such arcade games.

While I didn't feel like replaying the campaign in both new Wolfenstein games, Doom seems different in this matter. It's definitively a game where you can do a 2nd or 3rd run, simply because it's not about story but about turning off your brain and clean the levels after a hard day at work.

Being able to play some classic levels is also a nice addition.

I only shortly played the MP. For whatever reasons it didn't feel that enjoyable. So my thumb up is only related to the SP part.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tZero expectations for this game when it was released; haven't played it until more recently...

It's a real treat to play though--a near perfect modern take on one of the games that started it all. Sometimes you need to ditch the tactical assessment of the battlefield ahead and just go absolutely nuts, slaughtering everything in your way. Sure, it's mostly mindless, but it doesn't even try to take itself seriously. I think that's what I like about it the most... there's nothing special here, just a great shooter that is a lot of fun to play.

Mostly linear/corridor environments here but the different "arenas" where you engage the demons are mostly good. There aren't really too many spots where they just throw all the hardest enemies at you. When they do spawn a lot though, you are totally pumped to keep on killing. It is really nice to take a break from all of the new age shooters and just play something super gorey--like a huge middle finger to the censors which is great. Also, the soundtrack makes for some truly brutal gameplay; metal is the obvious choice here of course.

Overall great game, what you'd actually want from another installment in Doom. 3 was pretty cool back in the day, but this is a way better experience overall. Don't think they'll ever match the classics in terms of sheer awesome, but they did an excellent job in capturing the core aspects of the series. I really like it and for me it's a great tribute or throwback to a time where gaming was actually for players and not profit.

8 out of 10 all day
