\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM has been an incredible experience. I haven't had this much fun in a video game in years. There were so many things that it did perfectly, it would be difficult to list them all, but one thing in particular I have to rave about is the sound design. The music itself is dynamic and goes from loud industrial to creepy ambience and back again in perfect sync with the action. On top of that, whenever you come face to face with an imposing enemy or rush in for a gruesome melee kill, the music swells subtly with every action you take. It all comes together in such a way that when you are on your last legs, and you desperately grab that berserker power-up, and then one-by-one, you gruesomely tear, limb from limb, every last demon in sight, it feels like the soundtrack was written specifically for every moment of it, as if it were a choreographed Hollywood action scene. It makes you feel empowered, like you can overcome anything, which is a good thing because, especially on the harder difficulties, this game will kick your ass.\t\t\t\t\t
