\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo Doom...I am really impressed with this game.

It managed to capture the feel of the old games, but put a modern sheen on it. They made a couple of new additions, but it is still all about killing hordes of demons.

The combat is fast and intense. There is definitely a tactical layer to it, though. Stopping means death most of the time, so you'll be dodging and climbing through the masterfully laid out terrain while you're trying to keep track of where the big guys are, rationing out your ammo and setting up the glory kills.

The multiplayer is something I have been looking for for awhile. It is really similar to Quake Arena or Unreal Tournament, but with demons. Strafing and rocket dodging type of stuff. Really old school, and really fun. Reminds me of my LAN days.

Then there's the SnapMap mode. If you ever played around with the old school level builders in id games like Doom or Duke Nukem, it' a lot like that but easy mode. You have a grid, and a big selection of rooms, hallways, big set pieces, ect. You can arrange it however you want, and then populate it. You can make Team Deathmatch levels, Co-op survival type of levels, ect ect. There is a built-in client similar to the Steam Workshop where you can share all of the stuff you make, with like a built in community hub.

My experience was on a higher end PC, playing on the Ultra-Violence difficulty.\t\t\t\t\t
