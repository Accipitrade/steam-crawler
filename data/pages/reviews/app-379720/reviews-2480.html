\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like metal?

Do you enjoy tearing through waves of demonic forces equipped with hellish weaponry?

Do you like miniature figures?

GOOD NEWS! This game has all of that and THEN some! I had an absolute blast playing this - there was never a dull moment and when there was, it wasn't for long - the devs tried their best to redefine what 'fast-paced' means. As you make your way through the story, you start to feel yourself fit nicely into the persona of the Doomguy. You feel fast, you feel relentless, you feel like a demon stomping mother-father! Then you start to upgrade your suit and the hits keep coming, killing becomes secondary to pumping as much airborn death from the barrels of your gun as quickly and efficiently as possible - if any survivors remain when your clip empties, well, that's why we have the chainsaw.

Beautiful level designs take you on a visual journey from the deepest gore-holes of Hell to the desolate mechanica of Mars. Space and demons - get you a game that can do both!

Listen, if you think this is a game centered around multiplayer (which it does include) and lackluster on storyline, you're gonna have a bad time... Enjoy a good story for once. Don't worry about your precious KDA, no one is going to 180 no-scope you in this game except for that rocket-firing skeleton SPAWNING JUST BEHIND YOU LOOK OUT!

Get it, or don't - whatever.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 review :
This game is simply brilliant and very few games really give you feeling of actually being powerful(power fantasy).
Gameplay
Combat is fast , brutal and visceral and weapons pack a lot of punch. The game puts heavy emphasis on your movement and your skill using all of the arsenal provided. Levels are generally linear however each level provide hordes of increasingly difficult enemy to fight. I recommend playing the game on the highest difficulty because that takes the game to a whole new level.

Visual
Game's visual looks beautiful and very well done.

Audio
Audio is one of the strong point of the game as well as the combat. Soundtracks are dynamic and change according to your action. I really love Mick Gordon's work on the soundtrack in this game. Sound effects also pack a lot of punch and very clear.

Really having a blast playing this game and i am looking forward to continue playing it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game engine is great, graphics are good, gore level is decent. However some enemies are looking like a plastic/inflatable demon puppet and it kills the magic a bit.
Level design and gameplay for single player feels like an interconnected line of mini arenas meant for multiplayer and not for storytelling - and storytelling was punched in the face through the very first terminal our hero smashed instead of listening to what the hell is going on anyways...
Single player missions were over quite before I expected from a gigantic ~70GB sized game and for me none of the levels were as catchy as some in the previous three games. However adding the classic doom level bits as secrets to the game was a brilliant idea.
AI is strange, when you stand still the enemy, especially in big numbers will destroy you, however when you start to run around random, they seemingly do the same, and I could not identify any attempts from their side to corner me appropriately, no teamwork, and it felt like the chasing scene from any cartoon where everyone is chasing everyone by running elsewhere. Except the boss fights. Finally another game without bloody quicktime events (hidden in finishing moves)...
And then again the solace and te accomplishment after dealing with the final boss is washed away by a flimsy cliffhanger at the end which makes the whole game to feel like it was cut in half so the developers can spend on less brain storming under influence.
The soundtrack is one of the most badass I heard in years and all my respect to the composer. What was "missing" from Doom3, now is completely fulfilled. Frankly he should re-record some more old favourite doom1-2 level tracks...
Buy this when it is cheap, kids!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is really really fun, to the point that I sat and beat it in only a couple sittings. It's just the right length, has some fun bosses and just feels satisfying in almost any way. It's worth buying... if it's on sale.

The game is great, the music is really fun and worth actually listening to on its own, the game looks really cool with all the hellish architecture, fun chracter designs and even the look of the Mars base. The "story" is ther bu it's very much meant to be bare bones and it works perfectly that way, which does make it annoying when they try to do more like with the ending.
But besides that it's all good and while there are palces where I wish I knew more I also think that anymore would just feel sour.

The game only downside, in my unprofessional opinion, is its palyablitly. It's fast and fun and long and then it's over. And there are collectibles to collect but I just didnt feel compelled to, as much as I liked them. Going back through feels easy in the wrong ways because even if you don't have every weapon upgrade you're still untouchable. The multiplayer is just not that fun, it's there and that's about it, which is a shame because of all the other campagin heavy games with suprisingly nice multiplayer. The creation level stuff is fun too, I played a recreation of Halo CE's opening that was cool but even that wears thin because again, the combat becomes mundane and easy.

The repalyablity of Doom is aparant but I think it's the kind of game you come back to after a long while to play, I'd say pick it up on sale absolutely but if it's at its full price at the time, think on it.\t\t\t\t\t
