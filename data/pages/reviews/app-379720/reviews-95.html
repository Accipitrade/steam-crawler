\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game, wish they gave you the double jump sooner.

The game gets a lot more fun when you can move around a lot. (The midair control rune is awesome) If you feel iffy in the first few chapters just wait for the double jump boots and see how you feel. I like the game, fun to play, has a couple great enemies. The imps are the best of the bunch, they climb all over stuff and over railings and get to dynamic places. I wish every enemy was as interesting as the imps but eventually everything is just a flesh wall to cleave in half with the double barrel shotgun. Still fun by that point but the game really hits its stride in the middle and tapers off late game.

That sounds kinda negative but I think this is really just a solid and exceedingly fun base gameplay loop that gets a little bogged down by the difficulty curve it constructs.

Also a bit too gory for my tastes but I knew what I was getting into. Good sound design and animation. I like a little violence to really stand out and pack a punch, this title instead gives you buckets and buckets.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI initially played this game for 10 hours before I reached a point where I just didn't want to go further. My initial impressions were that this was more of a Call of Duty inspired FPS with some platforming sprinkled in, which ultimately left me with a craving for a more traditional FPS shooter like the re-released Quake or the newer retro shooters Prodeus, Wrath, or Amid Evil. So, I followed my usual rule of 'if you aren't having fun playing this game and you'd rather play something else, just go play that other thing' and didn't look back.

But something made me come back and give this another try, and I'm glad I did. My initial try of this game wasn't long after I was playing MW2019 and all the customization in it kinda had me in the wrong frame of mind. in COD-style games, you really aren't meant to get up-close-and-personal. You're given plenty of ammo, there's cover mechanics, and you really only use melee as a last-ditch kinda thing. But, after playing other 'retro-FPS' games long enough, where you could 'dance' with a Shambler wiith nothing more than a few pillars for cover and a healthy supply of shotgun shells, or use a Vore's explosive projectiles to save some ammo by keeping on the move and drawing enemies into taking the hits for you. So, when I came back to Doom 2016, I had a much more rewarding experience.

The gameplay is focused on staying in motion, and not hesitating to get right into the thick of it with a double-barrel shotgun. Knowing what weapons are best for which enemy is still a thing, and don't worry about that 'low ammo' count that pops up before the big baddie shows up to cap off the current fight, the chainsaw is there to get everything right back up to full inventory while also dispatching an enemy.

Bottom line, play this as your run-of-the-mill CoD/BF shooter and you're gonna have a frustrating experience that wastes your time. Play this instead as a man literally too angry to die, ripping and tearing through literal demons from hell.... and you'll have more fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom's gameplay mechanics harken back to classic Doom games while also allowing it to fit comfortably into today's popular gaming aesthetic, and I had a blast exploring its many quirks and features.

Like any good Doom game, the gameplay is exciting and tense. For the most part, I had a smooth gaming experience with no real problems. The story was straightforward and simple to follow. The graphics are also excellent and hold up well even today.

If you're getting it for the multiplayer, you'll be disappointed to find that there are only a handful of people still playing it (which I couldn't even find). So if you are wanting to get the game for this, don't.

The achievements are easy enough. The only thing is the MP achievements, which are going to be hard if you want to get them legit, but there are ways around this and it makes them very easy to get. So going for 100% is still possible today.

In my opinion, the single-player campaign is what makes this game worth playing, while the multiplayer mode is only worth it if you already have a good group of friends to play with. but as always, wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t They are rage, brutal, without mercy. But you. You will be worse. Rip and tear, until it is done.

DOOM is a fantastic return to form for the team at Id Software. If you are a fan of first person shooters, intense action and fast paced gameplay then this is the one for you.

My first introduction to the DOOM games started with the first in the entire series a few years ago. I thought it was a pretty fun time with fast paced action and solid controls for its age. This game is like they took that first game and gave it a major tune up and improves upon everything from its first iteration x10. There is even a really fun secret where you can find pieces to classic DOOM levels that you can play from the main menu. The respect it pays to the series roots is very nice and fun for fans of the whole series.

Gameplay is still just as fast paced and hectic as it was all those years ago. They do a really good job of scaling weapons and enemies as you play. Things are never too hard and you still feel like a force of nature here to unleash hell on the demons. The power creep really gets insane by the end of the game and can be very fun to experiment with all the different weapons, glory kills, and combos you can come up with. Honestly if you are stressed and need an outlet to take some of that out on, try this game. Something about being so unstoppable that the demons of hell can't do anything against you feels really good after a long day.

It should be noted that this game is not complete without touching on its music. When slicing and dicing hordes of demons, you need a good soundtrack and this one does not disappoint. Mick Gordon is a genius and he masterfully creates soundtracks that get your adrenaline pumping while you are in the thick of it.

This time around the game has a bit more story than its first iteration. There is plenty of lore in the articles you can find about characters, weapons and locations throughout the game. While nothing to write home about, it manages to set the groundwork for the sequel well and introduces a characters that I became quite attached to by the end.

This game was one that I had meant to check out for the longest time, now that I did I am ready to buy the sequel. There are no complaints to me. The runtime is somewhat short? I guess it depends on your definition of how long a single player FPS should be. Compared to something like Half Life I think it measures up pretty well, and compared to the original game it practically triples the playtime at least. For me this game was a lot like the Ratatouille meme. It reminded me just what I loved about the original DOOM while bringing freshness to it and making it even better. I would highly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
