\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoubt there's much to say that hasn't been said a thousand times.

It's a great game, it looks stunning even with it's age, combat is just addictive because it's so fun.
We know the gore, the splatter, the endless corpses. It has a touch of strategy with various enemies and their fighting tactics.
Doom is one of the games that just puts all the encounters everywhere and more of them later on. I prefer if games pair up certain enemies to make you use different strategies. It could have a bit of a faster gameplay for my liking, as it really gets fun when you get haste. Both those things are likely deliberate as you'd want the adrenaline driven stomping forward with weight and not a super fast rush while combining strategies on the run. That wouldn't be Doom. But as far as Doom goes, I doubt there is anything to criticize.

What caught me a bit off guard was the amount of jump'n'run. Why Hell is a place of hovering isles is a mystery, but it works well - making jumping your 2nd nature - which helps in battles as well. But yeah, lots of Crash Bandicoot in there, didn't expect that.
Another thing that wasn't as much a surprise as much as reviving a memory: That damn level design. The maps are mazes that you get accustomed to. It takes a while to find your way. Navigation isn't my strong point and the levels are sort of riddles to solve.

Which brings me to the design. The environment is gorgeous. If you want a vision of prototypical Hell in a western catholic sense, this might be a great example. Hell is designed so well, it's glorious. I get easily spooked and don't really like gore. So, a game that makes you think of the smell and disgust of this place does it's design very well. Although you wouldn't want to be there. It's horrible and that's a great achievement.

So it offers a dense atmosphere with a gorgeously designed world, peak gun-play, great music to get your adrenaline pumping and decent speed for fun. There's a reason why it's received as well as it is, because it's just that good.\t\t\t\t\t
