\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a must own! This review is coming very late, but after dealing with some issues at launch in May 2016, the game is now running very smoothly without any issues at all. The soundtrack for the game is what REALLY drives the action. The music would probably be the best music in any game I've ever heard (coming from a guy that likes hard rock). The weapons look and sound amazing, and they're upgradable which makes it even more fun. The single player is where the game really shines. Depending on how hard you like to look for secrets in the game which will remind you of things from older games (there are even secret levels hidden within levels) the game will take you around 13-16 hours.

I am a gamer that often will play a game for a while, put it down and play something else. But if I end up playing a game all the way through without stopping and trying something else, that means I was hooked and absolutely loved it! Now, a couple months later I'm playing it again on an even harder difficulty, and let me tell you this game can be A REAL CHALLENGE!

The multiplayer is average to good. ID Software is doing free updates to improve upon it though.

I would maybe say this is the best fps I have ever played. I have never played the other DOOM games. Old DOOM fans will defintely be satisfied.

I would give this game a 9/10 and as of 9/19/16, so far it seems like GOTY potential.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a new iteration into a classic game that is loved by many. This newest version does what Doom has always done best, slaughter enemies with some great weapons and straight up action.

Campaign will put through 13 levels of Earth and Hell. A storyline that was pretty interesting and leaves you wanting more. I had a blast trying to find all the secrets in the game which vary from little marine figures, super health, or a small section of classic levels which will then unlock and be playable from the menu. I was immersed into the game the whole time and not once did I get bored or worn out from playing. The ending left me wanting more and also a little mad but in a good way. I’m not spoiling it so you’ll have to find out for yourself.

You get the standard weapons that you usually see in Doom with two mods per weapon and then upgrades. The final upgrade on each mod is a challenge like get so many double kills with one shot. There are also perks you can find in the game that make you complete a challenge to unlock them. These will do things like collect more ammo or health from fallen enemies. Plus they added a thing called glory kills which are possible when an enemy is staggered and blinking. These were cool at first but lose their effects after a bit.

Multiplayer wasn’t bad but it’s just not that exciting to play. It comes with six game modes and nine maps to play on. You have boosters which you can pick for you loadout and can be activated each time you spawn. There’s also a demon rune that will spawn randomly that you can pick up to transform into a demon and then give yourself or team a small advantage. Other small things are armor and weapon customization to give your character a little more personal touch.

Then there is a mode called Snapmap that lets you create and publish your own levels. It comes with tutorials too to help you out on learning all the features possible. It’s a cool mode if you are into creating community levels and adds more life to the game. This isn’t something for me but I do appreciate all the people who take the time to create something I could have fun with.

Overall Doom is a fantastic game and a must play for any fan of Doom or this genre. ;)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't even know where to start with this one. I remember my Grandfather stealing the key to his boss' office on a weekend so I could play the original Doom on his computer. There it was, I was hooked.

Years later I would have my own computer and run Doom 3. It was nothing special, and quite frankly the flashlight pissed me off. It was a pretty game, but it wasn't quite doom.

Now here we are, 21 years later from the first time I played Doom. Holy ♥♥♥♥. You are immediately thrown into combat with very minor story and they let you go. "Here, shoot and punch these ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ in the face, but would you mind doing a couple of things on the way?". Doom at its finest.

Stunning graphics, amazing glory kill system (though a bit repetitive after a while), THE SOUNDTRACK IS THE HEAVIEST ALBUM I'VE HEARD IN 10 YEARS, AND I'M A ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ MESHUGGAH FAN.

I find it absolutely amazing how this game came to be only after every last man standing at ID software from the original team was gone. It's like a bunch of kids who grew up on the original are now at the helm and wanted to give this back to us.

♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Flawless.\t\t\t\t\t
