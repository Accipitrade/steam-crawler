\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGAMEPLAY: Amazing, fast-paced shooting. Good variety of weapons, feels extremely punchy. Exploration is well rewarded with plenty of collectibles and secrets (including permanent buffs and access earlier access to weapons). You and your weapons can be upgraded by completing nice little gameplay challenges (e.g. get x number of multikills in y amount of time). Level design is great with a good balance between linear, set-piece-y gauntlets; more open, find-the-blue-keycard affairs; and arena-based maps. Didn't play the multiplayer so no comment on that.

STORY: It's more 'setting' than 'plot' but what there is really supports the tone of the gameplay. Everything is grim and overblown. It's all designed to give you the feeling of being this unstoppable force that hell itself is afraid of. The narrative works well - no big twists and turns, but they aren't needed. There's plenty of lore in an in-game codex for those who are interested, but it leaves room for interpretation so as not to spoil the mythic atmosphere.

PRESENTATION: Graphics, music, SFX, voice acting are all great. The visuals are especially great, not just for their fidelity but their design. Everything is a blend between heavy metal album cover and contemporary sci-fi. Also, the gun sounds are outstanding. I swear I could drift off to sleep listening to the noise of the heavy assault rifle.

PERFORMANCE: Buttery smooth but for some frame drops (although that might be my machine). An odd number of bugs though, especially early game. I died by physics mishaps and bad clipping a couple of times. I even bluescreened once. In total not much to worry about but it did surprise me that a 5 year old game, and a modern classic, still had any prominent bugs at all.

TL;DR: Buy it if you like fast-paced, stylish shooters with exploration and upgrade mechanics. Don't let the graphics intimidate your card - you could probably run this on an Oral B toothbrush.\t\t\t\t\t
