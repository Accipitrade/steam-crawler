\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPfft! Do I recommend this to everybody else that's got a pulse?! DUH! It's DOOM 2016. There's nothing about this that needs to be said... But I'll keep it brief.

You're some nondescript guy that got woken up from a stone coffin, and from there, you grab a pistol and go on a massive demon killing spree. It's honestly one of the most Christian games there are out on the market.

Alright, now that the joke part of the review is out of the way, allow me to amend my previous thought and go into this a bit more. As far back as I can remember in an era when the dinosaurs roamed long, long ago... No, I'm just kidding, it was about 1993, I believe... Kinda been a while. But anyway, I remember that I was at one of my cousins' places and as I was passing by their computer one time, I spotted a game that I hadn't really seen or ever played before. Little did I know, of course, that that game would be one of the sole reasons as to why I got into FPS games like this one in the form of Doom 2. (Not DOOM Eternal, as an FYI, but that'll come soon, I promise.)

So then, when I remember watching that E3 for this, and I saw the environments, the carnage, the blood and the guts and all that fun stuff as well as the music, I thought to myself, damn does it ever feel good to get back into this again after all that time this franchise sat on the shelf for after Doom 3, huh? Now of course, I first picked it up on my XBOX One S first, and I loved every bit of it. But there was something missing. So then, I started looking around for this version and sure enough, the cardinal differences between this and the XBOX version couldn't be anymore different, despite their overall look is virtually one and the same. The only difference is - at least in my view, anyway - is that with a mouse and keyboard, your responses and movements are much more snappier, more free flowing and not quite as restrictive as you would be on your controller.

Speaking of controls... It may take a bit of muscle memory in order to whip out the right implement of destruction for the task at hand, but when you do... You will feel like an absolute god as you watch everything, from the simplest possessed humans to the biggest Baron of Hell fall before you... Either in a red mist or huge meaty chunks.

One thing I kind of noticed is that they get awful liberal with those upgrades, though. Complete a rune challenge, upgrade! Complete a challenge, oop! Here's another upgrade! Found a hidden secret, bam! Another upgrade for you! Now, I'm not saying this game's too easy or anything like that, as the challenge factor - even on the easiest setting, mind you - is still enough to make your sphincter tighten and maybe grip your controlling method of choice a little harder, but there is never a moment that feels cheap. If you made a mistake or you got too careless, you're more than likely to get royally curbstomped in a big hurry.

Then we get into the music. Mick Gordon, I don't know where you've been all our lives, but the simple fact that you've made music out of chainsaws and screaming, added some sick guitar riffs and blasting drum beats gave this game a HUGE boost. It's relentless, bloodthirsty, adrenaline pumping, 10,000 tons of industrial metallic majesty that I don't think - at least according to me - could ever be topped. If you ever come back into making music for games like this ever again, whatever you do, don't let them push you around and tell them that for this game to even have a decent enough soundtrack, you gotta give me the time I need to create and then you slam that door in their face.

Now, for the revised version of this review, do I still recommend this? I would have to be an absolute dumbass not to. This may have taken some liberties with the classic game we all knew and loved back then, but as far as I'm concerned, this was an evolution for the FPS genre as well as a reminder to all other FPS games out there that yes, it's still VERY much alive and kicking. It might not have mods for it, but then there's the Snapmap option where you can not only explore other people's map creations, but you can make one yourself!

As far as multiplayer goes... Ehhhhhhhhh. I'll say it's a mixed bag and leave it there. You might like it, you might not, I dunno.

So. What now? Do you people need another reason to get this? Matter of fact, what are you still doing reading this?! Go friggin' buy this already!\t\t\t\t\t
