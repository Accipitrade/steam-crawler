\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've beaten the game a total of 11 times, and that is more times than I've beaten Doom 2.

Doom 2016 is a re-imagining of Doom 1 that completely fits the bill. It adds in mechanics from Quake 1, 2, and 3, as well as alt-fires and double jumps. It's a loving tribute to a classic game that I love dearly, as you can see from my profile picture.

The only gripe I have with this game is that sometimes the game crashes after you beat the argent tower level.

The soundtrack is amazing, and deserved the award it won for best soundtrack at the game awards. Mick Gordon has done a fantastic job with every soundtrack he's touched.

Plus, the amount of personality that they've given to Doomguy (Slayer) is great for having no voice lines. The fact is, he chooses not to speak. He's so motivated by killing demons, that every other action he performs is brutally violent and concise. The only time we see him show mercy is to an AI named VEGA. Doomguy backs up VEGAs brain on a big SSD drive, effectively saving VEGAs life.

The rest of the time, little robots that store upgrades for your weapons get punched or demolished. It's a stark contrast.

This game is an instant classic, just like the original.

I have a problem with people remaking things and just slapping the old name on top, but in terms of remakes, there's really no other option. The problem fades in my mind when I remember how great this game is.

The only game that I don't have a problem when random people confuse it with the original Doom from 1993.
That is a big compliment, from someone who's been playing Doom 1993 since they were eight years old.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is how you reboot a franchise. 2016's DOOM is everything you could want from a true successor to the legendary original two games of the 90s. I bought this game on PS4 a few months after its release and loved it. I recently picked it up on PC and I've fallen deep in love with it. Of course the game is still enjoyable with a controller, but I have to say the game just comes alive when you play with a mouse and keyboard. The visceral, free-flowing combat feels really enhanced when you have the agility that a mouse and keyboard provides.
The first thing you'll notice about DOOM is the absolutely incredible soundtrack it has. In my opinion, the greatest soundtrack a video game has ever had. Its not only that the music sounds great, its that it fits perfectly with the gameplay. When you are blitzing through demons, bullets flying, gore splattering everywhere, and you have that heavy metal soundtrack in the background its just the most adrenaline pumping thing you'll ever experience. In fact, to say the soundtrack is in the 'background' is inaccurate, it is front and centre. How the music is woven into the gameplay is fantastic too, it ramps up in different ways as you do things such as initiating a fight, destroying a gore nest, or performing a glory kill.
Speaking of glory kills, they are so much fun. Games have had finisher moves before of course, but these are the best I've seen. First of all, they feel so incredibly satisfying; getting up close to an enemy and finishing them in a gruesome way is great and once again it feeds into that visceral combat. Each glory kill also provides you with your most consistent method of regaining health, as enemies spew out health with each glory kill. This encourages a more aggressive approach to combat, which is the only way to play DOOM.
Of course it wouldn't be DOOM without a variety of unique weapons to play with. Each weapon is so much fun to use, and only become more fun with each unique upgrade you apply to them. Most weapons have two upgraded variants, for example the rocket launcher has either remote detonated rockets or lock-on cluster rockets. These variants can be switched between on the fly, and can also be further improved. To go with the great variety of weapons, is a huge and interesting variety of demons to slay. From the numerous but weak imps, to the devastating hell knight, and the hulking mancubus. Each demon is great fun to take down, and you're encouraged to play around with different tactics and synergies in combat to defeat them.
The level design in DOOM is masterful too. Whether its tight corridors or large open arenas, the combat is perfectly complimented by how each level is constructed. There's much more than just combat in each level too. Obviously, exploration and secrets were a huge part of the original games, and they remain so in this game too. There are lots of items to find, whether they are upgrade items or just collectables, they are fun to find all the same.
There are two other modes on offer apart from the campaign in this game too. Snapmap, which functions as a sort of community workshop, for creating custom challenges and levels, and a PVP multiplayer mode that feels much like Quake and other arena shooters. While I don't think anyone really wanted a multiplayer mode for DOOM, its there and the game is no worse off for its inclusion, so why not. Though, if you never even touch these extra modes the game is still worth buying (if what I've said already hasn't made that clear).
I simply cannot think of a negative for this game. I don't use this word often, but DOOM is a masterpiece. It is in my opinion the pinnacle of singleplayer first-person shooters.

10/10

Rip and Tear.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a great game, the gunplay is quite satisfying and you can find a use for basically every gun, but you'll probably end up sticking to your favorites and the strongest guns. The rune system gives some variety to your playstyle but some of the more fun ones are only unlocked later on so you can't quite enjoy them for the whole game.

Enemies are varied enough with a decent pacing to introducing newer (and tougher) ones as you get more comfortable with dispatching the ones you already know.

There are a few things to criticize about this game though, some of them may be more of a personal issue I had with the game.

Firstly, while it only happened two or three times, there were some technical issues or gameplay bugs, mainly getting stuck in terrain, falling through the map and glory kill snapping you off the map and falling off a ledge. There were no crashes though and the game ran absolutely beautifully otherwise. Zero stuttering or lag or anything!

Second, the unfortunate lack of bosses. There were basically three boss fights. They weren't too difficult (on Ultra-Violence) I beat them on the first go each, but they were fun. Putting your vast arsenal to use fighting huge monsters and dodging their varied attacks was quite fun, so I wish there were more of them. A lot of the regular fights where the game spawns a lot of tough enemies at you were more difficult and challenging than the boss fights but they aren't as fun as bossfights. It could also be great if you got a reward like a new gun or basically anything for beating a boss but overall the boss fights themselves were epic enough that I don't feel too bad for not getting a new gadget for beating them.

Third is more of a personal irk for me that some may not be so bothered about but I was a bit annoyed at how often you ran out of ammo, especially for my favorite guns. Granted it adds an extra layer of challenge to the game to conserve your ammo and makes you have to use more than one or two types of gun, and by making you risk either running for ammo packs or taking out the chainsaw to get ammo back, but I found it also interrupted the flow of the fight for me, as well as my personal enjoyment of the fights. There is also a particular rune that gave you infinite ammo if your armor is above 100 or 75, which was my personal favorite rune since it allowed me to worry less about ammo and more about shooting the screamy bloody toothy things.

Fourth is again probably a more personal issue but sometimes I just wondered "Where the hell did you come from" as I got my ass slapped by an Imp's sharp claws and lost a good chunk of health. There's a lot of noise in the game so you can't always hear what's going on around you, the enemies can spawn right behind you, and the visuals of the game, while pretty, are also quite messy and sometimes make it hard to distinguish what's what, and it's a bit annoying when you can't spot the enemy. I also increased the field of view from the default 90 to 110 and I still sometimes felt like I couldn't see enough around me to not spot some imp coming up for a closer look.

Fifth: The Possessed and Unwilling ARE TOO DAMN LOUD. They're basically cannon fodder throughout the game but if they aren't the loudest sons of in the whole damn game, only competed by the Lost Souls but I understand why the lost souls are loud. On the first level the possessed and unwilling being loud did create a good bit of heart rate increasing stress but after that the volume could just have been toned down, it got increasingly annoying hearing all that shouting coming from basically walking health and ammo packs.\t\t\t\t\t
