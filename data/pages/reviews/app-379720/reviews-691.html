\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk ok, actual review now, this game is awesome and that's an understatement. If you loved the original 1993 release or DOOM 2, you'll love this as well. The game runs incredibly well, even on my ♥♥♥♥♥♥ computer I've had 60fps constantly. The graphics overall are great, the design of levels is fantastic, with nooks and crannies hiding secrets that can give you upgrades or weapons early. The gameplay is great, no reloading, no ADS, quick movement speed and a double jump allow for this game to fully embrace its nature as a arcade-y game. The demons are varied, easily recognizable, and damage dynamically. The push forward combat system encourages quick paced aggressive gameplay, and rewards you with health and ammo for it. One of the most talked about features of this game has to be the rockin' soundtrack produced by Mick Gordon and the rest of the music team. The tracks are heavy, using guitars and electronics, and it dynamically reacts to your gameplay, using ambient bits when you're inbetween arenas, and changing the intensity based on how many enemies and how tough they are, with small music stingers player for every glory kill you do.

In total the game is a technological, graphical, musical, masterpiece; with matching gameplay.
Highly recommend.
