\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMany reviewers seem to dislike DOOM's multiplayer. When playing multiplayer, your experience is shaped by ping, other people's skill levels, the community, game mode, team (if applicable) and gameplay mechanics. I could go into this a lot more but I'm not focusing this review on the multiplayer, but I am just giving an explanation why many reviews are less than positive of this amazing singleplayer experience.

It is my belief that reviews should not try to combine singleplayer and multiplayer into one rating, as the mechanics and fundamental experiences a player has are completely different and should not be treated as such.

Now that the disclaimer's out of the way, lets get into the review of DOOM 2016 Singleplayer.

DOOM is a masterpiece of a FPS.

It's atmospheric. There's a real sense of tension in between lulls of combat when you're scavenging the bloodied mars base. The ambient effects are brilliant at giving you a real sense of horror. Early on when you're fighting imps you'll notice they are very adept at crawling around on railings and scaling walls. After you smash in their skull and find your way into a supposed "safe" zone, you will still occasionally hear scuttling of claws among railings in the room above you, or something walking through the ventilation. You don't know if its an actual enemy or just the game messing with you. You're rewarded for exploring levels by gaining more upgrade points for exploration, but not enough to make it feel mandatory to be strong enough. That's a very good thing. If you want nonstop combat you can just rush into demons without worrying, but if you want the demon, scientist, artifact, weapon, lore / experiment reports, you're able to feel like you're really learning and benefitting from the insane twisted research.

There's also a real sense of progression. Finding new weapons gives you a feeling of relief and awe, every single time. It's quite impressive how the game keeps one-upping itself in this regard. It's an experience I don't wish to spoil, but upon obtaining the last weapon I squealed in joy and stopped for 10 seconds in orgasmic awe after firing it for the first time. This sense of progression is also mirrored in armor and weapon upgrades being obtained through completing specific badass challenges and time trials. You didn't buy those upgrades. You earnt them.

That said though, the enemies also progress at the same rate so you don't become an overpowered god of death. You are powerful, yes, and can destroy the enemies that were giving you trouble at the start of the game easily, but you're always threatened by bigger and more powerful demons with more crazy powers. It eventually becomes an art where you don't even need to think about situations like:
six rockets and a shotgun blast will stagger a mancubus
and let you rush to it and tear out it's heart and shove it down it's own throat to get some health back
and then you'll be able to turn and charge a gauss rifle precision shot
and one shot a cacodemon in the eye to turn it into a fine red mist.
Combat becomes second nature and you become intimately aware of your weapon's capabilities and the enemy demon's weaknesses.

Speaking of combat, it's f**king great. None of the weapons are bad, except for the starting pistol but that's more of an ammo conserve tool, and each have their own strengths and weaknesses. Movement is fluid, enemies are scary, you're surrounded very often and have to carve your way out yet you rarely feel cornered or trapped. Glory kills are a great tool to cover distance and function similarly to something like Bloodborne's blood rally system where they encourage you to keep fighting after you take damage, instead of playing passive and running away.

However, I believe the most important aspect of doom, my personal favourite aspect of the game, the thing that influenced my gameplay and made me feel the most emotion, was the SOUNDTRACK.

THE SOUNDTRACK IS ABSOLUTELY AMAZING. THIS IS A MASTERPIECE.

The soundtrack fits the game so well. It adds tension. It adds ambience. It sounds like it's "alongside" you during combat. It mimics your movements and shots. It crescendos upon the largest spawn of demons when the firefight is most intense. It really gets your heart pumping and adrenaline flowing. It gets extra metal during your gory Glory Kills. This game utilizes it's soundtrack to the fullest extent and should be best enjoyed with headphones at max volume. It also pays homage to some older doom songs as well, which older fans might pick up on, yet they're different enough to not be copies or covers. If I could stress one point about this review it is this. If you like game soundtracks, and especially enjoy when they mirror the events happening on screen, please check out this game.

Here's just a quick list of neat features that I liked in game that are not major, but neat nonetheless
-in game map is incredibly detailed. It's actually more like a 3D architect model than a map. It is also easy to navigate, rotate, and pan.
-quickswapping weapon mods
-level challenges
-customization with weapons / armor paths
-environment design is awesome

Here's one concern I have regarding the game
-can be too exciting

Seriously. I'd be honestly worried if you had a heart condition and were playing this game. This isn't a joke or trying to belittle anyone who gets scared. I'd really suggest seeing a doctor or something about this sort of stuff. You definitely get a surge of heartrate and adrenaline while playing this game, and that could be dangerous.

Here's some things I didn't like about the game, and think it could have done better.
-rune trials are a bit hit or miss. Some are good. Some are not so good. They are also a little bit hidden / require some exploring but provide your Doom Marine suit with a rune slot, which is a decent power boost.

-runes. The runes you can put into the suit are not balanced at all. Some are great and some are meh. You need to complete 7 runes trials to unlock all three slots, which means you can't get progress towards the rank two version. I think you should be able to put runes in the slots and gain progress towards their rank two version, but not get the benefit until the slot is activated in your armor.

-stuck. I got stuck five times in game over about 12 hours. One time I had to die to get out and lost 30 seconds of progress. The other 4 I fired a Gauss rifle and that pushed me out, but I'm gonna assume that someone without the Gauss rifle would have to die. This is super minor. Also one time I jumped and my camera clipped through the world for half a second. Even more minor.

-title screen was super slow, updated my graphics card drivers and that fixed it. That isn't really a negative, I should keep them up to date, but if your graphics drivers are out of date then you might have to wait 5 minutes on starting up or something.

-bosses. I wish there were more. However, that's purely personal opinion because I love fighting bosses, and making them more common would diminish the rarity that the few bossfights had.

-bethesda. Bethesda's an awful gaming company and they didn't make this game. They gave money to ID to make this game. However, by purchasing this game, you are giving Bethesda money. That's always a negative.

-keys. I get why you need key cards in a facility, but why do they have yellow and blue skulls to open demon doors. That's a little silly in my opinion. Could have been something cooler like a demon with a matching pentagram on his torso or something. I guess it keeps it simple.

-Glory kills. They're awesome, but it's not completely clear how many variations of them there are. It would be nice if there were some glory kill manual, they could be vague enough to promote experimentation, but I don't really feel like the information provided in game gives you enough info to get you experimenting yourself.

That's my review of DOOM 2016.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best single player FPS experience I have had in easily more than a decade. The combat and health systems are brilliant. Need health? Better run headlong into enemies cause they are your only source of health... genius. This simple game play mechanic defines the experience. You are never stationary in combat, no taking cover or stopping to regenerate health.

The need for health, ammo, and the requirement of dodging forces you out into combat. you are constantly running around huge and varied combat areas, stylishly decimating enemies, and narrowly dodging fireballs in the process. It's so incredibly satisfying, and those who try to play the game with some type of hide and shoot approach will get punished for it. There is no cover, no safety, get right in there mixing it up with the demon hordes, it feels so incredible. The style of running and gunning makes every combat encounter feel like you are narrowly escaping death at every turn. It's bar none the most exhilarating single player FPS campaign I have ever played from a game-play standpoint... and I really am finding a hard time putting it into words.

The game also played a lot like it's from 1995! Like a good Boomer Shooter the complex levels promote exploration and secret hunting. Each mission is like a puzzle to solve, locked doors, key cards, hidden weapon caches, collectables, health and armour etc. Don't let the linear design fool you, it allows the devs to create an awesome loop of exploration, and combat that always feels hand crafted and pitch perfect.

Suggest playing on Nightmare difficulty. It was a great experiance for me on this difficulty level, always felt fair and very difficult.\t\t\t\t\t
