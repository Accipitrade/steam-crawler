\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most kinetic, hectic and mechanically satisfying singleplayer FPSes I've ever played.

The controls (at least on keyboard) are responsive, the combat is actively engaging: managing your spatial awareness and mobility, the weapons you use, the types of enemies you're facing, and keeping attention to your resources, these are awfully rewarding to master.

The game is very Metroidvania-esque when it comes to hidden collectibles, it makes the game's replayability last longer to hunt those secrets out. Rune Trials, extra challenges akin to Devil May Cry's Secret Missions that reward you with passive upgrades are fun puzzles to tinker with. The side challenges, that is to use your weapons in a certain way to unlock the final upgrades are quite situational but manageable, I guess.
The game carries certain things from Doom 3 like the collectibles PDAs, certain demon designs, and how each level is segmented by the "Exit (insert location)" switch at the end of each level which is kinda neat.

The story's alrite. The boss fights are fun, mostly. The multiplayer's dead, at least you can do bot matches, which is rare these days. The Snapmap level editor is actually pretty extensive, accompanied with helpful tutorials.

I encountered a clipping glitch once, the collectible gallery didn't render the models properly. The platforming could be a bit finicky. Half of the achievements are tied to the multiplayer, so beware to all achievement hunters.
You can beat the singleplayer under 10 hours which is the optimal length for this type of game, making it more replayable than Doom 3 at least.

Great game. Rip and Tear away!\t\t\t\t\t
