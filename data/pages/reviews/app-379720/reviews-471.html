\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is almost the perfect Doom. The only thing the game lacks for perfection is a little more oppressive atmosphere and a little less visual farce, especially in the second part of the game.

The initial missions are just right. Exactly what is needed. They have the right pace, right action, right sense of immersion into the chaos and decay. But the second half of the game, unfortunately, contains less Doom than it should.
In addition, all these flashy finishings, bright bubbles of ammunition, fountains of colorful bonuses -- they simply do not belong here. That's not about doom (it's a pity that in the sequel there are even more such things, as well as completely inappropriate acrobatics + doom-parkour, wtf).

Nevertheless, the game is certainly a good example of a successful relaunch of the classic series. Very much fun.\t\t\t\t\t
