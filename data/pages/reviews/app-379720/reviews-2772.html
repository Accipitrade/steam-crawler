\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like fast paced, gory, first-person shooter gameplay, intense and badass soundtrack, and pretty graphics, then you will really like this game. I am almost done with this game. So far, the game has been really good. If you are familiar with FPS games, then play on the hardest difficulty. It is worth it as it gets pretty intense when there are plenty of demons out to get you and you are low on health/ammo.

There are three reasons why I won't give this game a 10/10.

1. It is fairly easy to get lost in the levels once you have killed everything. You will have to find a certain keycard to access the next area, and you basically have to search for it around the whole level. It's a pain in the ass and if Bethesda happens to make a sequel of the game, I hope that there are less of these moments where I just had to search the whole damn level just for a stupid keycard to get to the next area.

2. Personally for me, the replay value isn't TOO high for this game. Yes I know, there is multiplayer if you're into that, granted I haven't given enough of a ♥♥♥♥ to even play it.

3. From the point in the story at the time of writing this review (I only have like 3 levels left), the story isn't that exciting. I haven't cared for it.

Overall, I recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy favorite game of all time is the original Doom. I still play the originals today with the Doomsday Engine and never seem to get tired of them. Doom 3 caught some heat but I was a tremendous fan and loved the BFG edition even more. When I first saw the early release footage of this new Doom reboot I was highly disappointed and extremely skeptical.

Moral of the story? DO NOT JUDGE A BOOK BY ITS COVER.

This is arguably the greatest reboot of a game ever made. The quality of this title is beyond putting into words. The level design is as awesome as you would expect from a Doom title. There are hidden areas and secrets to collect much like the originals.

The sound effects are some of the best I have ever heard. The voice acting is phenomenal. They even slightly make fun of the fact that Doom 3 got criticized for being slow and story based.

The AI is fast and ferocious. If you stand still you WILL die VERY quickly. This leads to that classic fast paced gameplay we all missed from the original games.

I could go on and on but all I really need to do is say that if you liked the originals then you will 95% surely like this. If you never played the originals then just ask yourself "do I like action packed first person shooters?" If the answer is yes then buy this game.\t\t\t\t\t

Overall
DOOM is a surprisingly good continuation of a long-running franchise which meaningfully evolves the gameplay introduced in the first two-entries.

Score - 4/4

The Good
  • One of the many highlights of DOOM is the sheer joyous brutality of the combat. The glory kill system, which could easily have become tedious, flows seamlessly with the gunplay and means that even when not shootnig the player is still ripping and tearing through Mars.
  • Despite expecting him to be a rather bland space marine protagonist, the Doom Slayer himself is incredibly charismatic, always conveying his general emotions to the player without saying a single word. His animations really do a fantastic job at getting across his personality without using dialogue, something which more games could stand to learn from.
  • Mick Gordon's rip-roaring soundtrack perfectly complements the game's action and has a creepy industrial sound which suits the style of the game's environments perfectly. Some tracks, such as BFG Division and the remix's of At Doom's Gate's music from the original game are particularly excellent.
  • The design of the maps is really nice, with winding, labyrinthine paths and secrets seemingly hidden in every nook and cranny. Each of the levels is diverse and clearly different to the ones before it, even when traversing the same area multiple times over (although thankfully there are only a handful of instances when we do this).
  • Snapmap is pretty fun little level-making tool and is surprisingly deep, even if it doesn't quite replace a proper selection of modding tools.

The Bad
  • There's strangely no option to replay the introductory segment without starting a new game. I understand people might not want to see the tutorial segment again when replaying the first level but it was fairly fun and short, and could've been included as a prologue level.
  • The game doesn't really have a massive amount of replayability after you've completed the campaign, with nothing beyond a handful of collectables and secrets available. I think the game could've benefitted from additions such as an endless arena mode.
  • The ending of the game unfortunately falls a little flat, with Samuel Hayden betraying the player and surprising none of us while doing so. It felt like a cheap and unsatisfying way to end the game after making the player feel like a badass for the past 15 or so hours, and was an obvious lead-in to a sequel.
