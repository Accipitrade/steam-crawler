As another user put it: "first time playing I played until my eyes hurt". Which this was completely true for me, except not just with my first time playing it, but with pretty much every play session.

Again, normally I can't stick with and FPS longer than like an hour or so for a single play session, but man did this one have me hooked.

The gameplay is fast, so fast it feels like the gaming equivalent to doing speed or something. Not to mention the glory kill mechanic, makes every single kill just feel so rewarding with an insane execution not just for every enemy, but every direction you come at the enemy. Not to mention a "hurt me plenty" I feel like the game had a practically perfect level of difficulty for most first-time players, giving you enough challenge to make getting to every checkpoint feel so rewarding.

The soundtrack is god tier, not only does it do a great job at capturing the original feel of the OG Doom's soundtrack, but it pushes it forward leading you to behold some of the greatest sound design overall of the 2010s

While the story is just so so, the cliffhanger ending just gets you so excited to try out Doom Eternal next.

Can't believe I took this long to play this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"They are Rage. Brutal. Without Mercy. But you. You will be worse. Rip and Tear until it is done."
He wakes up in an unknown facility that is left in a bloody chaos and, with no hesitation, the very first thing he does is tear off the shackles holding him down, punch the closest demon in his proximity and vaporise its skull on the stone sarcophagus he was originally entombed in.
The rest of the introduction up to the elevator only deepens the impressions we get from our first minutes of gameplay.
The establishment of tone at the beginning of the game is probably one of the most rememberable beginnings of any story I have ever heard or played.
Doomguy is set up as an unwavering,unstoppable force of rage, that we get to play as and the story stays true to that all the way through.
Everything is viewed through his eyes in first person, which adds all the more to the immersion, while the already literally shredding gameplay is underlined by equally shredding soundtracks.
