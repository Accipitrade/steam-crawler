\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExceptional first-person shooter; one of the best action games that I have ever played. Blemished by minor technical issues, but it is an amazing value during steam sale discounts. An exploration campaign segmented with over-the-top, adrenaline-filled combat arenas.

I was a huge fan of Doom when I played on my 486 PC, and I remember downloading the demo for days through Usenet textfile encoding. The gameplay was shockingly good, for the time. The new DOOM game is just as impressive, even while harkening back to those awe-inspiring moments of the original. It hits all the marks for this fan, with the same cast of enemy characters, the same weapon arsenal, and even similar level designs. All these things are brought back with a technological refresh that made me cheer with glee. For example, the monsters are still horrifyingly scary, not just because of the incredible artwork, but because the AI is smart and engaging. I also want to mention that the level design and textures are so good, that I sometimes just stop and admire the immersive and detailed environment. I also love the weapon upgrade system, which makes you spend points on a gun (remember the chaingun?) until you unlock an in-game challenge that gives a final upgrade.

There are also some things that have been a headache. Firstly, all these assets come with a big download size. They take a long time to initially load, and even just getting to the main menu takes a while. Loading after deaths or level-ends also has some gameplay pause. This can be annoying when retrying an area repeatedly. Secondly, the game crashes from time to time, and other people in the steam forum are having issues at the same moment. I have seen it crash at the EULA, the main menu, level loading, and even the bug reporting form. And, when it takes several minutes to restart the game, these crashes are even a bigger aggrevation. And lastly, perhaps just a personal preference, there is a lot of jumping challenges in the game (maybe another call-back to older shooters), and there is an unconventional combat mechanic that gives extra loot for melee finishers. Both things I am not totally liking, yet. All in all, the game is what it is, though I wish some of these crashes could be addressed.

I waded through the blood and gore, using an ubuntu linux computer with RX550 radeon mesa graphics. Even with my old computer, this game ran GREAT with the built-in Vulkan graphic drivers (enabled in the settings). It ran through the Steam Play compatibility tool, which fired the game up without any configuration. The 74,000 MBs of game files took up a sixth of my harddrive space. The game uses an iD-specific game engine, with "bink" audio and video media files. I played this game with a keyboard and mouse.\t\t\t\t\t
