\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn starting this game, I was initially disappointed to see it had less of the tension and horror of Doom 3 and more of the full-on action of the original Doom games (I love the originals, but really liked what they did with Doom 3), but I gave it a chance and am glad I did.

When judged on its own merits, this is a great action game with amazing visuals. All the classic Doom weapons are there. The chainsaw (which I always saw as a bit of a gimmick in the earlier Doom games) is massively improved, giving you more reasons to use it, and the newly added glory kills are a lot of fun. The original Doom maps that you can unlock are also a very welcome bit of nostalgia thrown in.

The Doom franchise is nearly 30 years old, but by once again changing the tone of the game compared to Doom 3, the makers have managed to reinvent rather than rehash it, making the game surprisingly fresh and unpredictable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust completed my first run of the campaign, here are my thoughts:

I am completely new to the DOOM franchise. It caught my interest when a friend of mine showed me the soundtrack for the game, then it was recommended to me by another friend who loves the games.

And oh my goodness, this game was a blast.

I was initially worried that I wouldn't like the game, as I had never played a game like this before.
I wish I could go back in time, slap myself, and say "just buy the game already!" because I have never had such a fun experience playing a game that was like this.

The soundtrack, gameplay, and absolute destruction of everything around you combines perfectly together for a well-paced and well-made game.

The only issue I had with the game is that by the end of the campaign the gameplay felt very repetitive, but I believe the bosses to have been a good way to make up for this repetitiveness.

The bosses in this game make you adapt to them, learn their mechanics, and triumph over them my combining your knowledge, experience, and r e a l b i g g u n s in order to defeat them.
I just wish there were more of them, by far one of my favorite parts of the game.
(besides the soundtrack, because oh my goodness, Mick Gordon is amazing. Someone send this man back to heaven, where he belongs)

I will definitely be playing DOOM: Eternal once I'm done slaughtering demons in this one.

10/10, would Rip and Tear again.\t\t\t\t\t
