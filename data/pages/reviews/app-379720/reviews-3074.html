\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGruesome, spectacular fun and a seriously great campaign. Months after finishing the game, I still think about some of the awesome setpieces, memorable characters and the strangely compelling story.

FYI, there are several ways to cheeze the game's mechanics, especially near the end. I wound up with a perk that gave me infinite ammo while my armor was full, and another that made enemies drop armor. Spamming the fully-upgraded railgun with infinite ammo made me practically invincible, which lowered the difficulty of the game's later fights by quite a bit.

I don't think I enjoyed it any less - by that point, I was too into the story of this doom guy and his hell adventure. But hey, if you want to struggle a little bit more, maybe choose different perks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor all of you saying that you can't run this game, your PC's are either WAY below the reccomended specs or there's something wrong with them as I can play no problem on a system that doesn't even meet the reccomended CPU (I'm running an overclocked FX-6300 @ 4.2GHz) requirments. Onto my expirence in the campaign.

It was the best FPS campaign I think I have played yet. Since not only it lasts twice as long if not longer than the average Call of Duty or Battlefield campaign, and it has the audaicty to include over-the-top gory kills that make the parents on the ESRB ratings board cringe and cry with agony of their children's hellified minds. Now, to be honest, the only reason I don't play this game 24/7 is that when you take a break from it for a few days and you play it again, it makes the glory kills and the raw nonstop action that much better. The storyline and lore is very well thoughout, the characters are rich, but who really bought Doom for the story? The guns, the demons, and even the bosses are well thought out, and on Ultraviolence they were challenging yet not impossible. Now I played through the game on Ultraviolence, and I'm currently replaying it on nightmare, and it is hard as hell (Yes, the pun was needed).

Onto multiplayer. Its there and fun in short bursts. It is very reminiscant of the Quake 3 Arena style of combat and can be fun (yet frustraiting). It was developed in part by another studio, Certian Affinity, whom consist of ex-Bungie employees who helped develop the different multiplayer aspects of some other triple-A titles such as Halo and Call of Duty. This makes sense when some of the maps felt similar in layout to another triple-A title, Halo Reach. The class system isn't that bad, but if you would rather have the classic style of weapon pickups, Id recently added that in as the first update. Even with this, its still fairly lackluster. Most of my issues with it (lack of a party system, weapon pickups) will or have been addressed in updates. I feel as though they made the multiplayer look and be fun in theory, but actually a bit underwhelming in gameplay to promote the upcoming Quake Champions game. In summary it gets boring fast, but I bought this for the campaign, not this.

Snapmap. When I first heard of this, I thought it was cool. Like really cool, but at the same time, I didn't think it could live up to the expectations of the old style of Doom 1 & 2's WADs. I was right, but it's as damn close as you can get without them. I would've liked to see Workshop intergration rather than their own system, but it isn't bad. I feel like the menu navigation could be better, but its not impossible to use. The inclusion of the user-friendly tutorials was not only nice, but almost needed, as the level editor is really daunting at first look. After I went though all the needed training, the editor was a piece of cake. Not only is is a level editor, but it even has it's own built in logic and psudo-programming. This entire system reminded me a lot of a Playstation 3 exclusive that my younger brother really got into called ModNationRacers. I tried it out myself and the editor was very easy to use, but it wasn't as in depth as this. I've played DOOM levels that I thought wouldn't be possible, such as a full fleged zombies mode. The only thing that I wasn't happy with was the inability to import custom models for use in the level, or even creation of custom level pieces. That would make this level editor perfect.

Now with all this good stuff comes my main nitpick at this game: the fact that they used multiple developers for it. Now I have to say, unlike some newer games that have done this like Medal of Honor: Warfighter, they did a good job of blending this all together. Id did the campaign, and though they no longer have any of the original creators on their team, they did a good job. One of the original creators was on the team for the Snapmap creation, John Romero, done by a little known studio called Escalation Studios. The multiplayer was developed by prevoisly stated Certian Affinity, whom tend to help other big name studios with their games. There is subtle differences in the different aspects of DOOM that you can miss that make the use of different devs noticeable. The biggest is the different default key layouts between modes. Naturally, I expect everything to be seamless and the same controls as the campaign but no, I have to reconfigure my controls for each mode. Not a huge deal, still frustrating. The Snapmap definitly feels more like multiplayer with NPC demons. I would've liked it to feel more like the campaign if I was playing in either singleplayer or Coop, as even the death camera screams MULTIPLAYER on the singleplayer snapmap version. Finally, I don't know if it's my PC or if its just something with the different devs, or that singleplayer was more intensive, but I feel as if Multiplayer and Snapmap are better optimized in general. Again, is probably my system, but just something I noticed.

TL;DR Outstanding singleplayer, lackluster multiplayer, well though out and fun to use snapmap

My rating: 9/10, if only the multiplayer was as good as the campaign.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I don't normally write reviews, but this game motivated me to write one because of the awesomeness.

I am one of the old schoolers who enjoyed the Original Doom, Doom 2, and the various forms of Doom95. Doom 3 was a let down in my opinion as it went from fighting lots of monsters to more of a mano-to-mano fight with demons, which was fun but not as fun as fighting hordes of demons like I did in the days of old. This reboot brought back the mania of fighting against hordes of demons and they are much deadlier adding a greater challenge. This game is definitely one of the more fun and challenging first person shooters that I have played in quite some time. Let me go over the points on why you should get this game:

#1. Killings lots of enemies, nothing more satisfying than fighting a bunch of enemies and coming out on top.
#2. Smart AI and challenging fights in terms of managing your resources. I also like how they kept the AI attack your friend for the enemies intact because it is one of the few games that had enemies attack their own fellow enemies, which can lead to some hilarious one-sided fights.
#3. It is beautiful, the game just looks amazing and the sound is dead on. Incredible feel and look to the game.
#4. Weapon choices, armor choices, upgrade choices, there are so many choices and so many cool aspects to improve upon that it really adds a level of strategy/fun to the game that I thoroughly enjoyed and I am sure there are all kinds of fun builds you can make.
#5. Secrets and Easter Eggs, I really go out to find secrets because of the cool stuff they provide in terms of both valuable resources and some cool nostalgia that takes me back into the classic game. These kinds of things are growing to be expected as the gaming audience matures and when done right makes everything awesome.
#6. Some backstory on the Doom Guy is explained, which I don't want to get into any spoilers, but it is cool to learn something about the Marine that saved the human race a crap ton of times.
#7. The foreshadowing of danger in this game is so obvious. Maybe this is just my opinion, but when I walk into an area that is filled with resources such as health, armor, and weapons right next to a Demon Shrine I just can't help but say to myself, "So... these 3 power ups, armor, weapons, and health means I am not going to like destroying this Demon Shrine." For a moment I have to think and prepare myself for the onslaught that I know will be coming and the developers know that I have to be thinking that which makes it so fun to me.
#8. The bestiary for this game has some really interesting stuff and while some pay homage to the classic, it is also refreshing to see a few new additions that add an interesting dynamic to the fights.
#9. Humor. Throughout the game the Doomguy will do some pretty silly and hilarious stuff to get access to places and gets to see some of the havoc some poor souls suffered at the hands of the demons.
#10. Glory Kills, the addition of this mechanic has made killing enemies, especially annoying ones very satisfying and I like to see the different end results I can get.

Ok, so there are positives. Let's look at some of the things that I felt were off-putting when playing the game:

#1. Rune Trials: when you get to a certain point in the game you will have these Rune Trials that upon completion give you a Rune that provide some minor boost to your character. I felt these challenges were way too hard for the small benefit they provide. I only have 1 Rune that I am interested in and that is the very first one you receive, which lets you absorb items dropped by enemies at a much further distance. Which, I am not sure how far the distance really is since I am typically up in the grill of my enemies.
#2. Glory Kills feel almost required to survive in the game. I marked this as a positive because I enjoy the mechanic, but I also dislike that I have to rely on this to survive in the game. The more damage you suffer from an enemy the more health you get back when killing that enemy using a Glory Kill, this lets you recoup some of your health so you can keep going, but I rather not have to keep spamming for this just to survive. Maybe it is my skill level, but I feel desperate to get that Glory Kill just to keep my health up from some random enemy that I know is breathing down my neck ready to blast me with some ultra attack.
#3. Checkpoint Saving; I hate this mechanic and all games that use it. The classic Doom and Doom 3 games let you save whenever, which was awesome.
#4. No Co-op Campaign: The main reason I played and fell in love with the classic Dooms was the fact I could team up with my buddy and fight demons from hell in blazing glory using the starting pistol and dying over and over. That would have been a nice addition to this game and though they allow some Co-Op in SnapMaps it is just not the same.
#5. Story & Game Length: I personally do not care for the story from what I have experienced so far as it feels like it is a rehash of Doom 3, but maybe things will get more interesting as I get further into the game. I also feel that I am nearly done with the game and I have not played very long in all honesty, which saddens me because the olden days of Doom I could play for hours without getting anywhere near the end of the game. I remember playing from 8 p.m. on a Friday into 8 a.m. on a Saturday morning barely able to see the screen in front of me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom, at first I had countless issues just to get the game running without any hitches. This left a bad taste in my mouth once I finally had it fully running. However the bad taste quickly got swallowed and forgotten as I got pushed straight into the game with a punch to face from the games amazing visuals and sound. The first couple minutes sees your killing first and asking questions later XD
The game shoves you into the role of an angry super marine ordered to destroy all Hellsih beings on the Mars-ish planet.
From the simple pistols to machine guns that have exploding bullets to rocket launches that smear your enemies against the wall. This game has the full asrenal at your disposal to absolutely destroy your foes from Hell.

Doom has and dark and Hellish backdrop with lots of blood and killing, along with awesome heavy metal music that plays as you encounter your bad guys, with sudden "oomph" effects when you smash an Imps face with your bare hand using the melee finish off move. Secret areas litter the environment to explore and small side missions where your pitted against an event to kill as many imps in a time limit.

Doom never lets you sit and have quite moments instead it throws the whole army at you to beat to a pulp and move on. Quite simply its that badass game you want after a long day at work to just smash people faces while looking good at the same time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(This is about the singleplayer, I have no opinion on the multiplayer.)

A master class in shooter mechanics.

Swift character movement, impactful weapons and meaty gun sounds. Frantic combat with enemies that move fast and furiously. An uncomplicated run and gun experience, without aiming down sights, cover system or recoil. You don't even have to reload.

There's no headbob and FOV is adjustable. Vulkan mode gives me a considerable boost of FPS, even though I'm on NVidia.

The game does have anti-tampering measures and is slow to start up. You also have to be online to start the game. The game crashed 14 times to desktop so far, I don't know if the patch solved it.

Unlike Doom 3 there's little atmosphere or suspense. It feels more like playing singleplayer Quake Arena than going on a big adventure.

Nevertheless, I had a blast.
