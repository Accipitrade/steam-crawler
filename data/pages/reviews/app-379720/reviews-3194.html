\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFast, hectic, bloody, gutsy, skull smashingly, leg breakingly, arm snappingly, eye gougingly, jaw breakingly, chainsawingly good fun! Bethesda have brought back the true essence and feel of DOOM with a healthy helping of modern graphics and fun, involving gameplay.

They have also been cheeky enough to put some rather funny easter eggs in the game too, i ran across a Skyrim Guy with his horn helmet and arrow in the knee while in Hell and I couldn't help but appreciate that little touch. The game also has a sense of humour, when being told to gently remove some crystals to use them again later you simply smash them all up!
Or the "diary" account from the demons you fight talking about how this guy is going around killing them all mercilessly, it adds an amusing twist with the Demons actually fearing YOU and not the other way around. Always a good laugh to listen to those :)

Haven't tried multiplayer and probably won't, Doom has always been about the single player for me and this one does not disappoint! It not only looks stunning but it runs smooth as an Imps backside and the gameplay is nothing but sheer fun. modern shooters could learn a thing or two from this, like you don't always have to use cover based, hp regening, "tactical" gameplay. Sometimes us gamers just want a hectic run and gun marathon of death and destruction with a healthy helping of blood and guts along the way and that is EXACTLY what Doom gives you. Also the music is simply the best and makes those big gunfights all the more brilliant.

BUY IT. Buy it NOW!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not going to lie. I didn't expect much from this game. I feel like id software has lost some of it's magic over the years, and going into this, I was prepared to be disapointed.

Oh, how wrong I was. The compaign for this game is a blast. It's fast, aggressive, frantic, and full of secrets to find.

The combat is extremely tactile, every weapon feels unique and powerful in it's own right. No matter what your flavor of fps playstyle is, you will find a weapon that clicks with you. When the ish hits the fan in this game, it REALLY hits the fan, you can get overwhelemed quickly and have to react quickly and stay on the move. I'm not crazy about chaining the Glory Kills (mini-cutscene execution animations) as spamming the cutscenes can get pretty monotonous, but It's a price I'm willing to pay, as the mechanic itself helps to keep you in the thick of the fights and rewards you well for playing aggressively.

I'm also thrilled at the exploration factor they've put into the game. Every level is full of secret nooks and crannies that you are handsomely rewarded for seeking out. Many of them are quire a challenge to find. If you fancy exploration and some great platforming, this will scratch that itch.

The characters are great, they are well voiced, and each one feels fairly well fleshed out and written well. Even your marine, who without any dialogue at all, definitely has an attitude and his own personality.

The soundtrack is very well done, with the music getting aggressive and heavy during combat, it really does a great job at making the pace and aggression of battle feel cinematic and frantic. There are times where it can get a bit obnoxious with how loud and furious the music is. Although, it doesn't feel out of place, as the gameplay and the art mirrors this furious loud attitude.

I haven't had a single hiccup running the game. I see a few people complaining about crashes and the like, but my game has run smooth as butter without any issue at all.

As expected from a 2016 release, the graphics are fantastic. I particularly like the attention spent on backgrounds and skyboxes, these are often not given a ton of attention in FPS games, but here you see all sorts of things going on in the distance, really makeing the world feel much larger and active than it actually is. Of course, the game is exceedingly gory, as this is a doom game afterall, there are times where you can't help but roll your eyes at the over-the-top nature. Again, though, this is a doom game, you kind of have to expect it.

All in all, i was blown away by this game. I didn't expect it to be nearly as good as it is. It's definitely in the running for my personal game of the year. I picked it up on discount, but had I known how good it really was I would have gladly paid full price for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEarly note: You can absolutely turn off the "glory kill" indicators and ignore using them if you want, and with a mid game rune powerup combination (faster glory kills, speed boost after) they actually speed up rather than slow down the gameplay.

DOOM is fast paced and very fun. Easily the best FPS release in over a decade. Only the originals stack up to this game for heart-pounding mayhem. As someone who grew up on Doom in the 90s, I love it.

The multiplayer is ok; nothing to write home about. It's not the same deathmatch style of the original but you can make your own levels, speed up everyone, drop in only SSGs and Chainguns, and go to town. Only drawback is the player limit on your own levels. Hopefully they free us from these limitations at least on PC, but until then, Zandronum for your classic Doom DM needs.

The level designs are not Romero-tier but they aren't bad at all. I've enjoyed each one and replaying to look for missed secrets is a joy rather than tedium. I do miss monster closets though.

The pacing of demons joining the fray and weapon unlocks is pretty great, though if I was being nitpicky I'd say Barons of Hell show up a little late to the party. You're a serious arsenal by the time they arrive, and in comparison Hell Knights created a much more tense atmosphere in their level reveal. To be fair to id though, combat is never boring and they waste no time throwing several Barons at you in a row or at once. Later levels are hectic as all hell and it feels so good to go to work.

Given art, music, weapon feel, and level design, DOOM is an easy 9/10. I'm expecting many GOTY awards. They really went back to Doom/II's roots rather than Doom3 when they made this game, and it absolutely delivers.

Will be 10/10 when they let PC users write their own .wads and free us from SnapMap's limitations.

I really hope for the sequel they crank the difficulty like Doom II did after the original. We need levels with the demon population and density of Argent D'nur earlier in the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom singleplayer is fine and is as much as I would expect from a Doom game.

The multiplayer is in its own little hell however. Multiplayer almost caused me to give this game a bad review, its just cringey. They attempted to make a Doom a modern fps, which includes the CoD clone multiplayer with a few added things to make it a little different. Doom multiplayer feels slow, the player walks slow (for a Doom game). It shouldn't feel like im taking a casual stroll through hell, I should have my adrenaline pumping, yet in multiplayer its just too slow. The only appealing thing about multiplayer to me is the massive amount of customization. Armor customization is like a Doom barbie game.

All in all:
Singleplayer which im too lazy to go over, and other reviews have explained perfectly: 9/10
Multiplayer: 4/10\t\t\t\t\t
