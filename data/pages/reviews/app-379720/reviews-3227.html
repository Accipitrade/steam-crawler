Needless to say I was blown away by the direction that they took DOOM, it is almost a revamped version of DOOM2, stand still you die, shoot and move is the name of this game. Having a blast blowing away the whatever hell throws my way. By no means is the game easy, sure you can put it on pattycake and blow through the game, or yu can man up up and go to hell mode where every movement you make could be your demise or triumph.

Graphics on this game are sweet, movement is amazing, easy to control making you feel like your character can hop, skip, jump and doge well enough. It is action from the get go, story line, nothing too deep so far, but who needs to know anything more than killing hells spawnlings? For all you RPG players, this does hit that spot. Though mildly, but enough to keep your interest, whether you want to beef up your health, armor, ammo supply or adding sick mods to your weapons, it has it. the only downside is, there are just too many damn monsters, and not enough firepower to do it on my own, which is why I was wishing Bethesda was going to make it co-op. But that is what we can expect from Bethesda games, the "Almost factor". All-in-all it is an amazing game, great gameplay and optimization is out of this world. You will not be dissapointed with this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am going to give a seperate review for each mode of the game.

Multiplayer: This mode is extremely fun, with competetive as well as cooperative gameplay with a variety of modes, and apparently modes within modes. It is extremely fun to play. However, the "deathmatch" mode doesn't work for me. Whenever I select it the game doesn't load past the "connecting" stage. It isn't that big of a deal, however, as all the other modes still work.

Campaign: This gamemode is possibly the center of the game. The good things about it are: The gameplay is extremely fun, fast-paced, and challenging. The secrets are hard to find but very rewarding when you do find them (and very fun to search for). The bosses are easy enough to be fun to fight, and hard enough to not be disappointed. Also, the storytelling is very good. There really is nothing that I have to complain about this.

Snapmap: I don't have any complaints for this mode. None. So, why don't I talk about the good things? For starters, this mode is extremely fun. This is basically a community-powered multiplayer mode, and is fun to see what other people come up with. Also, it is extremely fun to contribute to the snapmap community and be creative with your level designs.

Miscellaneous: The game crashes. A lot. I have had it crash four times during normal gameplay, and it crashes almost every time when trying to load up a mode of the game. (i.e. Campaign, multiplayer) So basically what you have to do is Load up the game in campaign mode, load it up in a different mode, restart your computer, load up the game in campaign mode again, and THEN load it up in the other mode. It is really quite time consuming and annoying. Also, I haven't been a victim of this yet, but I have heard many complaints about the dreaded "Developer mode". If this is as bad as it sounds, I would recommend fixing it. I think it should quite possibly being your first priority when addressing the issues with this game. Also, this didn't affect me that much, but after the second crash during gameplay when I loaded the game back up I got a notification saying that some of my data became corrupted and deleted. The things that got deleted were: My classic maps in the campaign and all of my multiplayer data. It would be disappointing had I not only been level 2 in multiplayer.

In short: This is a great game. Excellent storyline, great graphics, fast-paced, and extremely fun modes. Also, you get to absolutely massacre demons. I don't think I mentioned that yet. Thank you for taking the time to read this extremely long and time consuming review.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's break this down into three parts, just like the game.

Single Player 10/10
This is the best twitch FPS campaign ever and is everything you’d hope for from a game of DOOM’s pedigree. The game is best played on the hardest difficulty you can handle. The rhythm of the combat is like nothing before. When you play the game on Nightmare you're not just running around the space, you're dancing in the space. Managing your resources and your position requires strategic use of weapons and the timely use of both the chainsaw for ammo drops but also the BFG when you get it later in the game. The utter insanity of the later levels on Nightmare difficulty is nothing short of exhilarating. As soon as I finished my first play through on Ultra-Violence I knew I wanted to jump into Nightmare right away. All I can say is if you're a seasoned FPS veteran you should have your first play though on Ultra-Violence immediately follow by the brain smattering that is Nightmare. You won't regret it.

Multiplayer 7/10
If only it didn't have loadouts and was more like Q3 where you have to pick up weapons throughout the map and positioning was the name of the game. There is still hope for them to improve it and what is currently here is fun. The standout modes are Warpath and Freeze Tag. Sadly the online community is already slowly dying. This one just tastes like lost potential.

Snapmap 7/10 currently, could be 9/10 with community support
This all depends on if the community can deliver exciting experiences. Snapmap in its current form is fun for about 30 minutes until you realize that it's impossible to sort the good stuff from the bad other than a few "featured" maps. They need a better way to do search as well as promotion so that we can separate the wheat from the chaff as it were.

Overall 8.5/10
The single player is totally worth the cost of admission by itself but with some decently fun multiplayer and the potential of SnapMap the new DOOM is looking like a hell of a deal at $60 and if you ever see it on sale for less than $40 you'd be remiss not to pick it up.

EDIT: As I predicted, the multiplayer has gotten much better with time. The new maps, weapons, and demons in the season pass are still annoying but they have provided an lot of new content in the way of new game modes as well as double XP weekends to keep the community alive.

EDIT 2: The multiplayer is all but dead with the advent of Quake Champions, but the Single Player will stand the test of time as one of the best FPS campaigns ever created.\t\t\t\t\t
