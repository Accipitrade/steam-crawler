\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell well well, look who finally finished DOOM after 4 years.

With Doom Eternal out I figured I'd boot this one up since it has been sitting in my library since it came out and I haven't touched it.

Yeah I'm kind of an idiot. I buy stuff and just keep it. I'm like the digital version of a hoarder, except I smell better.

Anyways this incarnation of Doom is friggen fantastic. Its an incredible throwback to the originals and I was surprised that it well, felt like Doom. Like classic Doom and Doom 2. Your default movement speed is fast as hell (get it, because you're killing demons? ah you're no fun) and your damage is also brutally high.

You also rip things in half with your bare hands. Its fun...just unsanitary.

You are also made of wet tissue paper and a one or two spitwads from the Barons of Hell trying to bully you and take your lunch money will tear you to tiny angry Doomguy pieces.

The primary idea around this game is movement. Unlike Doom 3, this version doesn't plant your feet to the floor and make you fight with lead weights around your ankles. Here, you dive and shoot and run fast as lightning as you blast your way through the levels. Enemies come at you in hordes and you have to react quickly in order to survive. For old fans like me, this isn't anything new. But for people who are newcomers or maybe only played Doom 3, this is a whole new experience.

This difficulty is a little harder this time around, even on normal, since demons hit as hard as you do so there is a lot of you playing dodgeball with Imps trying not to get out so you can impress that girl you like.

Stop trying. She's never gonna look at you.

Fortunately they added back in the ol' classic Doom mainstays that Doom 3 ignored. Mainly that enemies can hit each other, and demons being the angry lil boys they are don't like being hit by anyone.

If you are getting fireballed by Imps or Barons, you can actually use other demons as a shield. Do this enough and you can have your enemies in the middle of a civil war while you sit back and watch them tear each other apart.

Its fairly entertaining to see a little imp trying his best to fight a Revenant like he has a chance.

This emphasis on speed and strategic movement makes for an incredibly fast game time. I blew through this in 10 hours, and that was me looking for all the collectibles I could in ever single level. I could have probably blasted through this in 6 or less if I didn't bother.

I mean, there are a couple of gripes I have with the game though. For one, the aformentioned collectibles are an absolute pain to find. Some of them are standing right in front of you with neon signs and bright lights shining down from the heavens saying "HERE!". Others are buried in some obscure location halfway through the map deep down a shaft that you have to do a leap of faith to find or else you won't get it.

I wouldn't care if it was just some neato thing that added story or something, but a lot of these items will enhance your max stats or improve your weapons with new mods. These aren't small things you are missing, and if you go through an area that a sneaky secret is hiding a lot of times you can't go back if you get further along and now that item is lost to you forever.

Overall though, the game is still an amazing time even if it is short. Nab this bad boy on a sale or something and have some fun ripping demons in half to heavy metal.

Its probably a good thing you don't encounter any other humans in the game, because I feel like Doomguy would terrify them considering he is like a 6'5 guy who probably smells like blood all the time.\t\t\t\t\t
