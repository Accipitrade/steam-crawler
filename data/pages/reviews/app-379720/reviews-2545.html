\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you want to shoot and run, you will have some fun!

This review addresses the campaign, and not the multiplayer. I played it in Ultra Violent mode first time through to make it challenging, and to make a review apply to an expert players experience.

They tried to make more of a story, it is still pretty thin, but it is enough to keep a gunner engaged. The facility maps are pretty good, and plenty of chances to mod your equipment. Though everyone in the end has the same mods, so once you play through the game, there is no motivation to play through a second time and try something different. I like that "glory killing" give health and supplies, it is a real motivation to get up in close combat when your health is low, instead of dodging at staying at a distance.

The downside is the "hell" maps are pretty disapppointing as far as imagination and suprises. It's as if the art staff used all their imagination on the human facility assets, and went cookie cutter by the time the got to hell. If you imagine stone, lava, rock jumping, some bone looking pieces, that's pretty much it. You'd think as long as this franchise has been around, they would have a conception of hell that was deep, diverse, and creative. Unless part of their idea of hell is it looks boring as hell.

The general combat is fun and can get your adrenaline going, but the Boss fights fall very short. They are challenging, but it felt like the game went from an first person shooter to a video arcade game. Every boss fight takes place in a circular arena space, the most unimaginative play spaces in the entire map. And the boss attack tactics are made for the round space. thus there is no creative tactics for you as a player to use, just circle left or right, jump or duck when they shoot, and fire everything you have until they are dead. The battles are as exciting as my description. The big bummer for me was the game ended on one of these boss fights. The game ending on an uncreative map.

I do recommend the game, 80% of it being good ol' run and gun fps, and I think you will enjoy the diverse artwork and maps of the human and ardent facility. Unfortunately, it's the 20% that is lacking that makes the journey into hell and the boss fights more of a comfortable nap that a hellish nightmare.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a brilliant experience as far as the campaign goes! I have not bothered to touch multiplayer and probably won't. The mechanics feel solid and the combat is certainly visceral. Enjoyable gunplay with a good variety of weapons and good amounts of progression for each weapon that felt rewarding to max out a weapon mod. Kill demons so you can kill demons even harder.

Gameplay:
Going through the campaign was fulfilling. I played it on Ultra-Violence for a challenge and the challenge held true. Dying didn't really feel all that frustrating because you know it's your fault when you decide to jump into a Barron of Hell's fist trying to go for a glory kill on some other lesser demon. You need to learn the attack patterns of the various denizens of hell so you can ensure victory as each demon behaves differently. Bosses were also very challenging as well with their own unique methods of tearing you apart.

Level Design:
Each mission has checkpoints and none of them feel unfair. You know when you're about to get into a big fight and the game makes sure you don't have to slog to progress through the game. There's also secrets within every level and they are a treat to find as you often get items to help you along or fun collectibles like the Doomguy figures. Levels are very big and fun to explore. Secret hunters beware however, the game will block you off from backtracking too far and no doubt this is to keep the pace up. That said, you can replay old missions with your current loadout to find those collectibles and that's nice of them to ad.

Performance:
When it comes to performance, it's no secret that this is a well optimized game and it shows. The game is beautiful, albeit very grotesque in it's artwork at times. Very high resolution textures, great lighting and shadows. For a dark game it was never too dark. It also ran beatifully though that isn't saying too much considering I have a 1080 Ti and a Ryzen 7 so just about anything well optimized is going to hit high framerates but if I had a high refresh rate screen, this game would hands down have taken advantage of it and then some. Most scenes ran at 200FPS(the game's cap) and the lowest I saw was 150Ish and it never skipped a beat once. Resolution was 2560x1080. Speaking of resolution, game works flawlessly on ultrawide.

Excellent Game and look forward to ID's sequel. 9/10 well worth the 15 bucks on sale but would have been satisfied at full price too.\t\t\t\t\t
