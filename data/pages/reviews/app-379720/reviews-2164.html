While you are incredibly mobile, what really strikes me is how diverse the cast of enemies are and how good their AI and animations actually are. It makes for enemies that are the most satisfying of any other FPS to date.

And at least half of the levels in the game are very open, making level navigation and repeat playthroughs engaging even on your third or fourth time around. And while not every level is this open, the level design manages to be diverse and engaging enough to keep your attention all the way through. And yes, there are still keycards, switches, and boss fights to keep things interesting and faithful the classic 93' DOOM.

STORY/UNIVERSE
The game of course takes place on Mars, and of course, Hell (no spoilers there, this is a Doom game, what did you expect?). While you really wouldn't expect any more than an interesting enough setting, DOOM manages to have a very competent and interesting story if you pay attention. The story also makes the Doom Marine a very understandable character without saying a single word throughout the entire 12 hours of the campaign. His actions speak way louder than words ever could, with subtle glances and unsubtle smashing of other things tell you all you need to know about his opinions on everything going on. Of course, Id Software knows many of those who play Doom aren't here for the story and will never force this story on you if you don't care about it.

Here's the gist of the plot below, with a spoiler covering it:
You wake up from being encased in a stone sarcophagus, to immediately be handed a pistol and told to shoot the demon possessed zombies in the room. After re-uniting with your suit you are denied access to casualty reports over and over by Samuel Hayden, who introduces himself as the head of the UAC Mars facility. In the next level, you are also greeted by a message from the woman scientist Olivia Pierce who caused this whole demonic outbreak across the facility. While chasing down Olivia Pierce, you are instructed by Hayden to disable the argent energy (Energy taken by the UAC from Hell) filters carefully, which the Doom Marine completely ignores and decides to smash to pieces. During this smashing of the three filters, Hayden tells you that you have no idea what you are doing, and that this energy that took decades to perfect is the only solution to an energy crisis occurring on Earth. In what should be apparent Slayer affairs by now, the Marine does not care in the slightest, as anything that involves Hell always ends poorly. After this, you end up in Hell as a result of chasing Olivia. This is the plot up until about the halfway point, saying anymore would do this game's story injustice as a paragraph of text could never convey the story as well as the game has managed to do.


GRAPHICS
This is an Id Tech engine game, so of course it's going to be the best looking game coming out during it's release time. Incredibly detailed textures (thanks to a fixed megatexturing system), insanely high poly models, and unbelievably varied environments are what you are going to get. And all of this is beautifully optimized, running at 1080p 30fps on pretty much any low end system, or 1080p 60fps on any competent gaming desktop. And unlike previous Id Tech games, there is no frame cap present and of course nearly any resolution you can think of is supported.

ISSUES
There are two things holding this game back from being what I would believe to be the best game released in the last 10 years:
1)[\\b] Multiplayer is ok, but loadouts (that limit you to carrying 2 guns) instead of weapon pickups and launching without free for all definitely killed it for a majority of people.

2) Gore nests, and many instances where you initiate the fights. These are a small issue, but over time it's gets annoying having to initiate every fight. It makes the game feel less like you are just plowing through demons and more like you are locking yourself in a room over and over while summoning the demons yourself.

Extra) Weird jerkiness sometimes at framerates over 60fps when mantling, but this is so minor I barely consider it an issue.

TLDR YOU WRITE TOO MUCH
Great game about navigating through engaging levels, moving fast, using fantastic guns, and slaughtering an entire dimension's worth of demons along the way while listening to crazy good industrial music.

If my hours in game aren't apparent enough, I definitely recommend Doom 2016 and would give it a 9/10 with only multiplayer issues keeping me from giving it anything higher.


P.S. I love you and your games Id, and you bet your a** you'll see coming along for the ride with Doom Eternal!\t\t\t\t\t
