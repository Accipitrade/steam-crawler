\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fan of the original, I came to the 2016 Doom with very high expectations and I was still impressed. It is not hard to take notice that the developers were fans of the original Doom as well, because the core gameplay plays and feels exactly like the original. This may sound odd and critical, but I only mean to praise. There was something extremely satisfying about the original Doom -which to this date very few FPSs achieved-, whether it is the music and sound effects, the gunplay or the monsters. Doom introduces a new concept called Glory Kill. When you inflict enough damage, monsters start to stagger, triggering the ability to execute a Glory Kill, a gory visual that feels immensely satisfying. Music is awesome, even if it isnt as good as in the original Doom and I have found myself headbanging while doing glory kills one after the other. The whole time I played through the campaign, I felt the same sense of addictive gameplay and fun of the original Doom.

Is it perfect? No. The multiplayer was obviously not the main focus of the game designers (in fact the development thereof was outsourced), but it is still fun to play as a good old FPS deathmatch if you keep your expectations low. As for the single player, the levels lack variety and overall look and feel quite similar. I could not help but feel dj vu throughout the campaign. Gameplay can also feel repetitive at times, considering the entire campaign has a very obvious loop of throwing monsters at you. Story is also trivial, but chances are slim that you play Doom for the story.

But all its shortcomings are easily forgiven, because playing Doom is so much fun.

PS: If you are any good in FPSs, I highly recommend playing this in ultra-violence difficulty. It is challenging, yet never unfair. I have the feeling this is how the game is meant to be played.

9/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is one of the games that blew the FPS community away! Whilst I haven't grown up with Doom, I respect the series and ID Software (the developers of Doom), for making a positive change within the FPS genre!

The Positive's:

- Graphics are ahead of its time, even with medium or high settings
- The gameplay is fun ( It kinda reminds me of Serious Sams gameplay )
- The soundtrack is great and unique
- Good replay value for an FPS
- Depending on the games difficulty, you feel like a powerful figure
- The game is self-aware that you don't care about the story (not that it doesn't take itself
seriously)
- Has a pretty decent upgrade system
- Runs very well on any hardware (as long its specs are made from 2011-2016)
- A good amount of content
- Some cool mechanics
- The price, even when not on sale, is a great price

The Negative's:

- The game has minor but noticeable bugs
- The secrets may be cool and well placed, but rather than there being clues, the map tells straight up tells you where they are
- Checkpoint system only (you can't save at anytime)

My Score out of ten: 9/10

Conclusion

Even though there are better FPS's than Doom, I still had a great time with this game and it's certainly worth picking up! In fact, if you've never played DOOM, this is a good way to get into the series. Same applies for those new to the FPS genre.\t\t\t\t\t
