\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOTE: If you are enjoying the game a hell of a lot right now don't read this review just in case I ruin it for you. Also this review isn't about disliking the game, it is about what could be improved at some point and it is JUST MY OPINION.

Doom is no more than a pretty good game,
I had a lot of fun using all the death tools and enjoyed the animation and visual style.

The the few problems I PERSONALLY had was the glory kill system and the fact that it TO ME felt a bit shallow in terms of gameplay.

First of all, the glory kill system. as a huge fan of Strafe shooters, I really didn't like the glory kill mechanic because it stopped the flow of the 10/10 arena shootouts. I wouldn't mind if it hadn't made the player STOP and kill the demon as oppose to actually killing the demon with the same movement as the player. It would just make it feel so much less annoying and tedious. Despite this, I do actually think that glory kills are a cool concept as they drop health and items when you perform them but it just ruins the flow in MY OPINION.

Now for the shallow gameplay. The next problem I have is the fact that it is really quite repetitive towards the end because a lot of the weapons and weapon-mods are redundant. Pretty much this game goes... Go to this arena and kill those guys, new weapon kill those 5 guys in that linear tunnel and then go into the arena and kill some more guys. Look, it does have more than that to offer definitely, but still it got a bit old for me.

If I would give the game a score out of 10, it is probs a 7/10. but this game has something in it to achieve something incredible for it's sequel.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe pacing is exceptional, every time I felt like I wanted a new weapon, or I wanted more enemies on the screen, sure enough around the corner would be just that.
Gun play mechanics leaves you feeling like everything has a purpose. Run out of ammo for everything? Use the chainsaw to quite literally open up the belly of a demon to refill.
Players who go the extra mile to explore are rewarded. This game is riddled with Easter eggs, secrets, and other hidden gems.

Game runs buttery smooth, is responsive, absolutely no frame drops, no stutters, & no micro-stutters. Vast video options.

Unfortunately I can't play this game with headphones on, due to certain sounds that are ear-piercing to me. For instance, the double jump, and a few of the other pickup noises.
Throughout the game I have seen a lot of the low-tier enemies de-spawn and re-spawn, as well. That's it. Game is pretty close to perfect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA true shooter, as opposed to the much more common cover shooters out there nowadays, and a very solid one at that.

Just about every weapon "feels" right, and their specificities complement each other nicely, so that each weapon naturally becomes useful and actively used (instead of a go-to weapon & situational ones). On top of that, munitions, while PLENTIFUL, start with a rather low per-weapon cap, further driving the player to make use of the full arsenal. Special mention for the chainsaw mechanic, which essencially provides charge-limited full-ammo refills.

Level design is just as tightly mastered as the weapon gameplay. Arenas chain together smoothly and offer a variety of situations, usually permitting vast movement opportunities. Movement is quick and rather precise, making clever use of 3D. Getting up is made easier by fast ledge-grabbing as well as gadgets later on. The packed arenas encourage if not mandate making full use of these possibilities, leading to nervous but never overlong encounters.

Those excellent raw shooters parts are completed with an open map design, where many secret areas & collectables are deployed. While these are completely optional, and often quite easy to find, some do itch the completionist/OCD side in a bad way, distracting from the core gameplay for extended periods of blind "how am i supposed to get there ??" time.

Difficulty's rather well dosed in my opinion, with the default providing fights where letting lose & having fun isn't much punished.

Additional modes (beyond the official campaign) are present, including (if i'm not mistaken) player-created content, but i haven't touched these yet.

Overall, it's a VERY easy recommendation to anyone with some love for energetic shooters.\t\t\t\t\t
