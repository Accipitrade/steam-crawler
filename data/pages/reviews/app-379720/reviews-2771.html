\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot recommend this game enough, Id software has finally gotten it right. 10/10

Doom [1] was absolutely revolutionary for its time, and I use the word in its literal sense. It was something that I grew up with which helped push me towards CS (I modded the hell out of it and loved making levels when I was ~10). This game is a definite throwback to the original Doom in pretty much every category: gameplay style, level design, graphics, ambience, etc. As far as I am concerned, they did everything right and there is nothing they could have improved upon.

-----------------
Graphics
-----------------
Monster models are absolutely gorgeous and feel like graphically upgraded versions of old demons. Environment graphics are stunning, mostly due to the lighting effects. I have never seen ambient light cast so well. AND THE SHADERS! They even get it perfect with the volumetrics. Areas are purposefully flooded with sharp primary colors while still maintaining its general primary color for the area. You don't see that a lot these days, as most games go either grey (industrial) or green (Farcry).

Id's big texture engine also allows everything in the environment to have incredibly high texture resolutions.

-----------------
Sound design
-----------------
Sound design is also wonderful. My home system is 7.1, and I feel like I am in a horde of demons when fighting. When in enclosed spaces, it sounds like even a single demon is coming in from all directions due to ambient audio bouncing. I have NEVER seen this done before.

Voice acting is also top notch. And the silent protagonist role works perfectly, as Duke Nukem style commentary just gets annoying.

-----------------
Gameplay
-----------------
Gameplay is, as previous stated, pretty much the same as the original. You have your same primary set of fun guns and demons. You run around killing said demons with said guns. One minor annoyance is that the game takes an enclosed-fighting-area gamestyle. Whenever you are fighting demons, you are locked into an area of the level until they are all defeated.

Secrets work pretty much the same way as the original, but with/inside them you also get the added bonus of collectibles and datapad with game info [story, monsters, levels, etc]. There are also upgradeable systems for your power suit, guns, and stats [and runes].

-----------------
Environment
-----------------
The environment, another major factor of why I love this game, is just jaw droppingly solid.

The level design makes you feel like you are in natural environments. They are [generally] very open. It is rare that you hit invisible clipping planes. The old-style mapping system, while now in 3D, is also done just right.

All UAC facilities are decorated with high texture screens, gadgets, server equipment, and other such ambient fluff. At one point of the game I realized how amazed I was at the details of just piping for server equipment embedded behind glass on a wall. If you were to walk into a real futuristic facility like this, I believe they got exactly what it would look like, including all the scientific writing and mumbo jumbo. And then there is the blood/gore/demon environmental ambience. Again, perfectly done. Inside UAC facilities, there is just the right amount of dripping corpses, demonic-puss-filled-floor-stuff, demonic symbols, etc. These environments really come alive.

And the actual levels taking place in hell... feel like hell. I don't want to give away some of the surprises, but some of the non-enclosed designs in theses areas (Titan's Realm) are just breathtaking

-----------------
Story
-----------------
The story is fun, engaging, and [dare I say it] sometimes cute and funny. This game actually makes me laugh quite often (not just from the story aspect). I believe the characters and their motivations. They are interesting. And even better, this Doom is actually a full on sequel to the original. (Not sure if any other Doom games are counted in there).

-----------------
Game length
-----------------
The first time through the game, the levels all seem massively huge. At the end though, the game kind of felt too short. But I wouldn't really fault Id for it. Levels are definitely quicker to go through the second time (maybe 10-15 minutes each if not trying to rush it).

-----------------
Multiplayer
-----------------
I have not yet tried the multiplayer, but I also expect it to be a blast. More on this once I play it.

-----------------
VR
-----------------
OK, I take back the "they did everything right" I said before. This one is a major aggravation point for me. I LOVE using my Oculus Rift whenever possible for games. It really does make the experiences that much more fun. Doom has 0 proper support for VR, and I doubt it will ever get it. Id really pulled a ♥♥♥♥ move when suing over Carmack's help in developing Oculus, saying he stole their trade secrets. This is absolute BS because:
#1 HE [co-]STARTED ID SOFTWARE. He is allowed to go off and make competing companies if he wishes.
#2 Id wasn't doing anything with VR anyways. While Carmack wanted to delve into it, the company told him to go ♥♥♥♥ himself. Granted, Carmack is known as a self-absorbed douche-bag.
#3 I highly doubt he signed an NDA. His expertise and ability to keep inventing new methods to improve game graphics is what makes him valuable. And in general, he lets his ideas and implementations be known to everyone. Free exchange. And in that regard, there is no reason he shouldn't be able to be a free agent.

And Oculus and Valve were originally pretty close. This would also probably stop Valve wanting to work with them directly for the Vive.

I have to comment that it was kind of an exciting day for me when I heard Carmack joined onto Oculus (before Facebook bought them, which kind of screwed their pooch). Same as when Michael Abrash joined Valve's VR team. Hearing about titans of the industry working on this exciting new technology was a breath of fresh air.

-----------------
Sign off notes
-----------------
Ignoring the above point about VR, thanks for the great experience Id! May you rot in hell for all eternity.\t\t\t\t\t
