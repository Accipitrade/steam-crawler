\r\n\t\t\t\t\tusually the first thing i do in a game is open it's settings menu, turn off the ingame music, and only then start playing it. luckily, i was too curious and hit the campaign start button right away - oh boy, would i have missed out otherwise. the brutality of the industrial-heavymetal-electronic-something soundtrack, accompanied by the unbelievable fitting and high quality sound fx of weapons, monsters and surrounding objects is just beyond perfect.
for audio alone, i'd give this game a 10/10.
as i played the original doom back in the 90's as a 10 year old, i was of course very sceptical about the "modern" additions like glory kills and rune trials and the like. but they got everything right and i'm having a blast each time i fire up the game.
my only two complaints would be that corpses disappear and the amount of monsters onscreen at the same time is limited. mainly for performance reasons, i get it, just and especially compared with the original doom, this sucks a little bit. i miss the 1vs100 total carnage that, so far, only the serious sam series managed to do.

so, 9/10 in total, i'm back in the game and speakers on max.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've just [2016 05 22] finished my first playthrough of the campaign. Here's the breakdown of my impressions so far:

+ Excellent level design, both visually and functionally. I'd prefer more wide open spaces, but the game makes use of whatever real estate it has very well. Superb pacing between action-packed segments and periods of calm kept me invested in the game from start to finish.

+ Clean, unobtrusive, customizable UI with brief, effective and toggleable tutorials.

+ Loads of secrets, both easy to find and less so, bonus challenges and optional objectives that help the player to learn how to play better and reward them for doing so with various weapon and ability upgrades.

+ A map that is both cool looking and functional in 3D environment.

+ All around solid, dynamic combat with good variety of mobile, aggressive enemies that gives you enough weapon options to allow for personalised approach without being forced to rely on something you don't like.

+ Lots of tongue-in-cheek dark humor, easter eggs, references and poking fun at things.

+ Some plot, characterisation and setting building in case you happen to be interested in it. Surprisingly not terrible either.


+- The default keybindings are back***wards. If you jump in without trawling the option menu, you will most likely have to interrupt your playthrough the first time you try to crouch. :)

+- The saves are checkpoints only. Their placement is not too bad, but you are still liable to lose progress because of a random crash or an untimely death because you can't save manually.


- The music tracks used during the bigger action segments are too short and end up repeating endlessly, interspersed with frantic jamming of instruments that don't go anywhere. IMHO, gets old and tiresome quickly.

- Sound effects of the majority of guns are unsatisfying, often lacking in 'oomph' and aural feedback when landing hits on target. Melee attacks sound puny, with a side dish of lots plastic crunching. A number of enemies have sounds that are hard to distinguish from each other in the midst of busy combat.

- I've run into a number of technical issues. Some screen flickering when trying to climb where the game doesn't want you to go. Some crashes with no visible error messages. Some level sections have inconsistent or consistently low fps regardless of what graphic options you lower to accomodate for it. I've had to delete the game's video cache and force it to reset to default settings that way after I tried turning off motion blur in order to return to playable fps because of who knows what.

- There are all these extensive options to adjust all sorts of things, but we can't turn off mouse smoothing from within the game? You guys do all this awesome stuff and then **** the bed on such a simple thing? Really?


Overall opinion of the single player mode:

Net positive, despite the, in my opinion, several notable failings in sound design. I hope Bethesda doesn't stop supporting this game after release and all those nasty performance problems get fixed. Interested in seeing what they have in store for future expansions / sequels.\t\t\t\t\t
