★★★☆☆
Honestly, this game is not bad at all. Some might say its the best game of the year. Amazing graphics, smooth gameplay, lots of content, a solid, fun singleplayer and an old-school-feel multiplayer.

That being said, I bought into the hype and paid 60 bucks for it (back at launch). I should also note that the game currently takes up 71 GB of your harddrive. This is a lot, in terms of space on you drive as well as downloading it. This is a serious issue if you 'only' have 500 GB of space, and you like to play different games too.

However, I got burned by this game. I was never frustrated with it, nor in love with it. It just never clicked with me and I assume it won't with some other players either. The gameplay might be too simplistic and/or repetitive for you, as it was for me.

This is a game about arenas with tons of enemies chasing you while you run around and shoot at them constantly. No puzzles, some platforming and just some annoying 'exploring' change up the pace. But how can you 'explore' if the devs close the doors behind you if you wanted to go back for some health or perhaps a secret you noticed a bit too late? This was the biggest annoyance for me.

Try out the demo. It'll probably show you the essence of the whole 13 hour game in a mere 20 minutes.

*played the game at the most difficicult setting that is optional from the very beginning
*ran the game between 50-70 frames.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStandard reviewing process is as follows....


Pros:
- Pulse-pounding soundtrack.
- Epic finishing moves.
- Awesome story, with surprising depth upon investigation; the depth or lack thereof of the story is up to you.
- Cool death animations that make you regret yer bad moves...painfully...
- Awesome level design, very thoughtful and grants a surprising amount of strategies and options.
- Weapon customization taken to new limits, personalize your arsenal to your liking, even during combat.
- Action-packed combat, fast paced with almost no time for rest as it's a constant flow from battle to battle.
- Plenty of collectibles and fun secrets...
- Terminator 2 ref. 'nuff said.

Cons:
- VERY lackluster multiplayer. it loses it's novelty after a little while.
- Can't use custom gun paint jobs in the campaign. (minor issue. I'm probably the only one annoyed by this.)
- Multiplayer weapons get nerfed too much, too often. DLC weapons give (albeit slight, based upon skill.) advantages.
- Snapmap's game logic has a serious learning curve, very little would make sense to someone who doesn't study programming as a hobby or something.... (not all that much of a problem for me, but whenever I play on some random person's map, I find logic errors in many places, as confirmed by several of their creators....) Not to say make it simpler, just add a more in-depth, explainatory tutorial regarding non-enemy encounter game logic.
- DOOM 1 snapmap parts are adequate, albeit that it feels like something's missing here and there....*coughcough*actualbossesintheeditorsothatyoucanmakerealbossfights*coughcough*
- They replaced the archevile with the summoner (HORRIBLE CHOICE, albeit that the summoners are quite challenging as well...I dunno, summoners are pretty cool, but Archviles are more interesting.) and got rid of pain demons altogether. (as annoying as those were, I'm surprised they cut them out.) Also, they cut out arachnoids as well.
- Lost souls are both OP and nerfed. (they only hit once, but they hit INSANELY hard. in the original, they hit as many times as they could before dying, but dealt....a bit less damage.)

DOOM is back in this awesome reboot of the franchise, simply dubbed, "DOOM". I always call it DOOM 4 anyways, however. Too used to Quake, I guess. But anyways, albeit hardly a faithful reboot of the originals, (seriously. MAJOR. DIFFERENCES. EVERYWHERE...) it is amazingly fun in it's own right. There are 5 difficulty levels, ranging from "get back in yer crib and get yer pacifier, ya COD player!" to "oh look, you died. WELP, I'm just gonna delete yer save file now...and then laugh as you rage quit, again and again...", to go from having players enjoy the story while proving how whimpy they truly are, to challenging the most hardcore, most brutal gamers in the world! it's quite flexible, with loads of options for how to handle this next situation...and if that's not enough, there's snapmap, which grants you the power to build entire campaigns on your own! or just fun, weird and quirky things...


Story (the depth and clarity of the story): 7 of 10 stars.
Addictiveness (how hooked I am on it): 9 of 10 stars.
Inventiveness (originality): 7 of 10 stars.
Faithfulness (to the originals.): 6 of 10 stars.
Soundtrack (quality, awesomness, timing): 8 of 10 stars.


FINAL VERDICT: 8 of 10 stars!\t\t\t\t\t
