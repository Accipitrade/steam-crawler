\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEveryone has pointed out how great this game is. It is. Buy it. This is the best single player FPS since Wolfenstein. But, I want to comment on some of the stuff I didn't see mentioned.

The soundtrack is incredible. I can't wait until it is available. It was written and recorded by Mick Gordon, and he really outdid himself. When I can buy this soundtrack I'll probably listen to it daily for a few months while driving or working.

One thing that is a little disapointing in this game is the enemy design. IMHO the enemies in Doom 3 almost all looked scarier and more horrifying than in this game. Doom 4 seems to want to take cues from super colorful and somewhat cartoonish games like Sunset Overdrive and Borderlands. The imps were very scary and alien in Doom 3; now they are bright purple and orange elves. The Barons of Hell look like Tim Curry in Legend. The final boss was not scary or cool looking at all; in fact I was sure I while I was playing the last boss that they'd have something cooler afterward just because of how lame it was. With the exception of the Revenant and the Hell Knights all of the enemies in this new Doom look kind of cartoonish and silly. The lower-level cannon fodder enemies all look stright out of Sunset Overdrive. They look super out of place in Doom. It isn't a horrible thing, but it detracts a bit. This game with Doom 3's enemy design would have been better.\t\t\t\t\t

Even the standard Imp bears a stronger resemblance to the original.

Musically, this is phenomenal. The soundtrack gets my blood pumping during these fights and has, on more than one occasion, had me pumping my fists whilst yelling "THIS IS ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ AWESOME!"

I love the throwback to oldschool FPS archetypes: no health regen - only health pickups; no reloading - only one ammo pool; no crouching behind cover and taking potshots - FAST NONSTOP BLAMMO BRUCE WILLIS MOTHER ♥♥♥♥♥♥!

I can't recommend this highly enough. Insane amounts of fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t This game is totally worth $60 dollars! BUT, if you are planning to buy this game for it's multiplayer, you should wait for a sale or something similar. While the multiplayer is fun and can be addicting, it is definitely not a game revolving around multiplayer like games such as Call of Duty, Halo, or Battlefield. The real reason you should get this game, is for Campaign and Snapmap!

Campaign is all about gameplay. It is fast, violent, super gory, and addicting! This game does not focus on story, but more on super gruesome and enjoyable hours of slaying demons, which is a great thing. However, if you do find yourself wanting to know more about what is happening and why you are killing demons, the game has a very neat system where you can pick up devices that contain lore/backstory. Once you complete this insane compaign, there are still a plentiful amount of hours of pure fun.

There are a variety of difficulties you can beat the game with, and a ton of collectables/secrets in each mission including classic doom levels (1 per mission). With this, you also get access to the Snapmap feature which adds an unlimited amount of contain made by the community, and even you! The creation system is insanely easy to use, and so is downloading and playing other people's maps with a featured list, etc. While singleplayer has no Co-op mode, snapmap can support up to 4 players at a time! This game is great for both you and playing with friends!

Over all, i give this game a 9/10 but only because the multiplayer is not the best. But like I said, buy this for the campaign and snapmap features!\t\t\t\t\t
