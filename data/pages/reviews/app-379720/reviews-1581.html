\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell. I'm playing this game in 2020, 4 years later the first release of the game. I've played for almost 20 hours and i'll admit it has been fairly enjoyable until now. While a few parts of the late game missions have you face stressful fights (which are only a small amount compared to the rest) , I did not regret the money and time spent into the game. Each difficult situation you find youself in is rewarding enough for you to keep going. The variety of the arsenal of eye-candy weapons at your disposal is also nothing to joke about. The devs nailed both the level design and the enemies design in my opinion ( which HAS TO BE humble, since not having played the older doom games means i can't hold anything in comparison). Also I'm no hardcore player, so i probably won't delve into the whole range of difficulty modes the game has to offer, but i sense the detailed study that has gone in building the demon ripping and tearing experience which is Doom. DO i recOMend it? Definitely, especially if it's on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTook me 3 long years to finally beat Doom 2016. Just in time for the release of Doom Eternal.
Doom has a pretty forgettable story in my opinion. It’s basically humans-mining-on-Mars-finds-ancient-energy-oh-no-demons and your job is to kill demons and close the portal to Hell. But who cares? Doom is all about the fast, responsive gameplay which it certainly delivers.

Doom’s gameplay is visceral and raw and straight up balls to the wall which is a hundred times awesome. Played through the game in Ultra-Violence difficulty and it was a challenge to beat. You must think fast once the demons spawn in as you dodge and weave through gunfire, scrolling to find the best gun to use in that situation. Zipping and zooming through the levels to avoid getting wrecked by demons is exhilarating and gets you on the edge of your seat.

The weapon variety never gets boring, but I find myself using the same few most of the time like the rocket launcher, gauss cannon, plasma rifle and the super shotty. And when a demon starts glowing gold, you mash F for that sweet glory kill animation which is satisfying as heck.

Surprisingly, glory kills do not slow down the tempo of the gameplay. It also gives health and armour which is great as health packs and armour can be scarce around the arena. Ammunition can also be scarce but that is remedied with the help of the chainsaw.

Chainsawing an enemy, essentially, refills all your ammo (except the BFG) and is a guaranteed one-hit kill (except bosses). You must use it wisely as you only have 3 fuel (can be upgraded to 5 later on) and different enemy types take different amounts of fuel to kill.

One minor gripe I have with gameplay is that collectibles can get tedious as you are constantly checking the map to make sure that you don’t miss out on it which can dampen the experience a little bit. Other than that, the gameplay is solid as heck and keeps you on your toes.

The demons’ design is great as well. From Imps to Hell Knights to Cacodemons and the Baron of Hell, they all have individual characteristics that is unique and different from one another. The world design is good but can be forgettable as you would be busy trying not to die and looking for that sweet glory kill glow.

Another major strength of Doom is the amazing soundtrack that blasts in the background as you rip and tear through the demons. The djent-like heavy metal soundtrack gets you in the zone and makes you feel unstoppable at times.

Overall, Doom 2016 is pretty dang amazing with its adrenaline-fueled combat and I fully recommend it if you want to have a quick round of demon blasting and purging. Or just relieve some stress that's fine too. 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
