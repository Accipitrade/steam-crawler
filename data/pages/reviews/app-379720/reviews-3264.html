\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGLANCE RECOMMENDATION: 95% YES / 5% NO

GOOD
  • The sound design is my favorite aspect of this game. All the guns sound mechanical and crunchy, the demons have this nice reverb, and the environment is atmospheric with its clanks and industry.
  • THE MUSIC IS SO BRUTAL OH GOD MORE GUITAR
  • Everything looks stellar. The visual effects on the bullets are cool as sh** and the monster designs are rad as all hell.
  • Boss fights are surprisingly fine. Once you learn how to deal with the one attack that ruins your day, it's a cake walk.
  • Difficulty makes you feel amped. I'm no pro but I put it on Ultra Violence and did fine if I kept moving.
  • So many secrets that make me pan the horizon. I actually liked combing through the levels my first run through to try and find things myself.
  • Lots of mods to alter your armor and weapons. The little challenges to unlock the final mod attribute per weapon mod is super fun too.
  • Doom guy's personality is the best. No story or character background, just a pissed off dude who's here to close hell portals and smash demons in the mouth.
  • IT'S JUST SO MUCH FUN. Best single player shooter campaign in a long damn time.

NEUTRAL
  • The level where you climb the energy tower is a bit too vertical in design. Lots of down time and deaths when you try to experiment on what ledges you can and can not jump onto.

SUMMARY
I didn't bother with the multiplayer beyond what achievements I may go for. I'm a bit bummed to hear the season pass may purely be for multiplayer, but I may return if more single player content gets added. The single player content is just so dang tight in every way. It's hard to stop saying such good things about it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was very excited for many years about Doom. Weeks before release I dropped hundreds to play it on proper hardware. My Doom Career started in '93 and lasts today, still mapping, playing, etc.

This game is good. Combat is very addicting and you're always moving. A few slips and you're knee deep in death. The Graphics are fantastic, vibrant colors, many things happening at once. I love every monster design besides the CyberMoose, the one demon they couldn't possibly screw up they did and modeled him into a weird half-tard Moose (SPOILER) that you only encounter once. (SPOILER)

I was on the fence about Glory Kills and Arenas but after beating the game twice they're nicely done and complement combat very well. It's similar to a Serious Sam but more Doom. The stuff I miss from Classic is monster closets and a little more open-ended map layouts, but what's here is still better than anything we've gotten in years. Weapon mods feel fluffy and it seems design time could have maybe better spent elsewhere, Doom's always been about combat and quick heel & toe work.

Certain Affinty developed Multiplayer so you're getting a weird mix of Halo and COD, but neither of the two. It has no real identity, it's not bad, it's not great. I was hoping they'd revive skill-based MP with things like item control, timing etc, but that's just not the case, especially from a gang of hacks like Certain Affinty. There's a weird flip-flop here where the Singleplayer requires the skill Multiplayer should have but doesn't. So if you like Singleplayer shooters this is the one to get.

SnapMap is good but prefabs suck, along with console limitations. It's good for combat, though, and that's all I really care about.

The game is worth 60, and though the time of Romero and Carmack at id has passed, the new id team did a great job.\t\t\t\t\t

The gameplay is amazingly fast paced, and the gameplay is the focus big time. You're killing â¥â¥â¥â¥ before you even have control of your character. All the guns look, feel, and sound great, and the variety of enemy types make combat engaging.

The campaign is the obvious main attraction, and it's 13 levels took me about 15 hours to complete on Hurt Me Plenty (Medium Difficulty), but the gameplay was so compelling that I had no qualms with replaying levels to complete challenges, find collectibles, and get upgrades I missed in pursuit of 100% completion.
The focus on upgrading your character by finding upgrades and items in non-linear levels gave me big Metroid Prime vibes, and that's a very good thing. The campaign is basically rated-M Metroid Prime with way faster combat and a bigger focus on it.

The multiplayer doesn't deserve a lot of the â¥â¥â¥â¥ it gets. It's not the best, but it's good fun with friends. A lot of people went in expecting something like Quake 3, save those expectations for if/when id revives Quake (which after successful Wolfenstein and Doom reboots, is the logical next step for id). You can use logic code to define new gamemodes, and the level of customization is huge.
Somebody made a fully functioning Harvest Moon clone.
In Doom.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSIngle player - was fun but did get a bit repetative.

Online - Let me start by saying I play with an xbox elite controller. I say this to point out that I am well aware that I will seldom to never be on top of the leader board. Typically, with a little practice though, I can stay off the bottom in most FPS games. I'm fine with that. I play from my couch and it's worth the conveiniance and comfort to me to not be "the best". Now that all of that is out of the way... This game suffers from one of the most horrific cases of "worst connection" wins I have ever seen. Players frequently disappear and reappear 10-20 feet away (oftentimes behind you or over your head). Those same players also tend to win the "both guns fired at the same time but my bullet wins and yours vanishes" battle as well. And surprise, surprise, those same plays who disappear and reappear behind you just happen to consisently have a ton of kills. Argh. When the lobby isn't full of a bunch of people who are apparently still using a 3mbps DSL connection the gameis fast and fun. Sadly, either there are a LOT of horrible connections across the country, or there are a lot of weenies that have nothing better to do than download torrents while they play. Like I said: I don't ever expect to be at the top of the board, but getting rolled by invisible opponents who are likely messing with their connections to gain an advantage is just plain frustating. I don't understand why in 2016, devs haven't figure out a way to test connections in game and aggregate future lobbies by quality. I'm still playing, because like I said, when there aren't a bunch of crappy connections in the lobby it's a blast. Here' s hoping someone starts caring about the quality of the gameplay for those that spend good money on hardware and their cable bill.\t\t\t\t\t
