\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Doom series is the only FPS genre game series that interests me nowadays. Shooting people and/or things gets old quickly, Add unskippable cutscenes, uninteresting stories, bland weapons and fighting systems and more to the mix et voila all but few games are ruined.

Not so with DOOM: The unskippable cutscene is actually in-game and not footage, short and to the point, introducing me to the newest monster I can't wait to saw in half - on Mars! The story is beyond ridiculous but contrary to world war shooters and whatnot DOOM is not trying to take itself too seriously. You're supposed to have fun!

It comes down to a perfect blend of both visuals and acoustics. There are showdowns in the game where you won't stop killing for minutes at a time, and with the right setup, big screen, big speakers, you'll be engulfed in the madness, feeling the shotgun blasts in your chest, while suitable music rounds off this symphony of killing.

It reminds me of the old Doom, and Quake, in that it has this fast-paced action with no real slowdowns, technically perfected fluid motions all the way. The only real complaint I have is that I can't run any faster, but you do get double-jump in the first third of the game.

The levels are sometimes hard to navigate, very big. Despite the relatively linear gameplay (you can't progress until you have the yellow access card and you need the yellow one before you can get the other two.,.) you can always go back and take a wrong turn, losing yourself in previously played areas. The map helps somewhat, but I think the game could be more honest with itself and just go linear all the way, for example by locking a door behind me that I won't need anyways.

Still, I can easily look past that and wholeheartedly recommend DOOM.\t\t\t\t\t
