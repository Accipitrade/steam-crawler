\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Good:
-Excellent graphics and animation.
-Fairly immersive atmosphere and game world
-Beautifully tight controls and core game mechanics
-Easy movement that you need to use in order to survive
-Varied and powerful weaponry - the guns feel good
-Enemy variants are fairly few but are well done with very different strengths and weaknesses
-Gives you the feeling of being powerful but challenged
-Pleasantly surprised by a decent narrative with interesting characters to string together the carnage
-Lots of weapon and suit upgrades to give you a proper sense of progression as you take on more baddies

The Middling:
-Most fights with mobs take place in enclosed rooms. It is somewhat repetitive that you jump around shooting enemies until they are all dead and a door opens to let you out. If killing in this game wasn't as fun as it is, this would be a significant downside.
-Generally good level design that does leave you with hunting here and there
-Beat it in a week on Hurt Me Plenty (medium difficulty)
-Difficulty feels uneven but ramps up nicely toward the end of the game.
-Halfway through I had died three times. (Once because I wasn't paying attention, twice due to falls.)
-Must have died a couple dozen times in the second half between all the boss fights
-Music is ok. playing without music makes the game feel downright pedestrian.

The Bad:
-Can't save any time you want. Chekpoint system only. The good news is that checkpoints are frequent.
-Weapon upgrades failed to save between sessions multiple times, had to spend upgrade points all over
-Soundtrack would not load a couple of times leading to fights with lots of grunting and no music
-Not something that is at all kid friendly with all the gore and demonic themes

Overall:
-Got in it sale for $6 and it was a steal. Even for full price most people won't be disappointed.
-Pacing and length were near perfect. 9/10
