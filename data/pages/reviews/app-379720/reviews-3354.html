Guns are amazing, glory kill animations are awesome and never get old, the demons are great, and the boss fights are the best boss fights in a game i have played in an FPS. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tMultiplayer.

I'll be telling you about that, because I really didn't play the main story before I decided to get rid of the game.

The games multiplayer is pertty great, very fast paced. People have mentioned the 'Halo' look of it. But thats just the armor builds. (Which this game has like 3 times as many armors as Halo did). The game 'Feels' ... kind of like the Halo Pvp with it being a Fps with alienlike guns and glorykills.... But faster, and a lot more jumpy, So I guess it would be closer to Unreal Championship in pace and bloodyness.

The level desighns are well done, big; but not big to where you can't find people to fight. And you will have mostly tight hallways to fight in with the occasional 2-3 big rooms per a map. Gear, can also be found scattered around and respawns itself in time, giving a expirienced player the edge of drop locations. Such as the locations where the demon rune will spawn, making one player who grabs it into a killing machine. I would be willing to bet though, that the custom maps will offer a much better pvp expirience provided any of them become popular.

The guns. So you have a rather small selection in my opinion, of weapons to choose from. However a few of them and a couple additional tool items are super OP at the moment. The biggest plus comes from being able to set up your loadout before a match and paint your gear. You also have these different abilties slotted that can be activated during a Pvp match to give you a edge upon respawn. Armor and speed buffs, things that tell you where the person who killed you is, your team, enemy demons, etc. The buffs play a rather large chunk of your build and timing when to activate them is crucial, otherwise you might just waste them (they can only be activated for a set ammount of time, all 3 of them, then you cannot use them again for the match).

The game is gorgeous, maybe a little bit too so. My rig can only handle the fights with a couple dials at mid, then the rest of them set to off or low. Over alll I was getting 40Fps or so at medium graphics, which were just a little too blocky for me. (But the game was for sure playable, I just was not pleased, and didn't want to set things down to near low to get 60Fps.) For any reading this wondering about comparing builds, I will be attaching mine to help you.

GPU: Nvidia GeForce GT 635
CPU: Intel Core i7-4770 CPU 3.40GHz
Memory/RAM: 8Gig
Reso: 1920x 1080
OS: Windows 7
Driver: 365.19 <----------------- Which is the newest driver, that just came out for this game. All my other attempts to load it with another driver resulted in the game never even loading up.





Over all great PvP fights. loads of fun and blood. If you can handle the game, get it. The only reason I refunded is because I can barely handle running it at medium graphics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tID software has a history of trying new things in gaming. Each iteration of their engine adds something new to the gaming experience.

For those of you who have been following ID Tech games for a while, their v5 engine is the one that fueled Rage. Three key technologies were employed heavily in that engine and its tools:

1. Shockingly complex scene rendering (lots of texture-loading improvements)
2. More dynamic environments (nothing feels cut-and-pasted)
3. Dynamic enemy reaction.

These have all been improved and more in the latest engine in DooM 2016. The single player campaign is a blast with excellent pacing, awesome environments, and fantastic balance towards forward momentum in fights. I have not been able to pick out exactly what the IdTech6 engine brings new other than taking the cutting edge in fast-paced graphics technology and showcasing the awesome things you can do with it.

The multiplayer is shockingly well done. I had the original Quake shareware disk back when it came out, and the awesome NIN soundtrack. After my first match I was stunned, because the feeling of a match somehow captures what Quake multi-player felt like so many decades ago.

On a scale of Industry Changer to Shovelware, this would rank a "Must Own" (lower than Industry Changer but still worth every penny at launch-day price)\t\t\t\t\t
