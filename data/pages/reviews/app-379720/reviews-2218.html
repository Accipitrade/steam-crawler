\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a right way and wrong way to re-imagine a classic game in the modern era. DOOM is the right way.

DOOM focuses on what everyone remembers and loves from the classic games -- moving fast, shooting things (lots of things!), finding secrets, shooting things, descending into Hell and, most importantly, shooting things. It's DOOM with just enough modern polish, which is really all anyone wanted.

That's a bit simplistic, though, because there is a lot going on here to make DOOM work. The soundtrack and sound direction are top-notch, ramping up the intensity for combat arenas and setting the mood in the larger, expansive levels. There's an actual story(!) though not so much that you have to pay close attention to get something out of the game. A lot is answered very early on and everything you learn in the meantime is window dressing that doesn't compromise your ability to shoot things.

Perhaps most importantly, glory kills -- oh my goodness, glory kills -- are what makes the modern incarnation of DOOM really sizzle. Get an enemy to low enough health (and this includes bosses) and melee attacks become gory, canned animations that drop health. So, not only are you cracking skulls, snapping limbs and/or stuffing ripped out heats down mouths (oh, Mancubus, you poor fat soul), you're getting a potentially vital dose of healing that allows you to continue -- that's right -- shooting things.

There is a multiplayer mode, but it's somewhat of a ghost town. At least, I sat in multiple lobbies for tens of minutes and got nowhere. Your experience may differ.\t\t\t\t\t
