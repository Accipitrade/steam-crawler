\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM. Where do I begin? This game has done so much for the franchise and the community that it's almost unbelievable. This is one of my favorite games in recent years and I love this game. Like always, there's a reason why I love this game so much.
DOOM's gameplay is one of the reasons why I love it so much. The game emphasizes constant mobility and quick decision making (and sometimes hyper fast reflexes, if you're playing on harder difficulties), which is great for a game like DOOM. All of the enemies emphasize movement, as well. With the combat comes a completely revamped arsenal of weaponry that the Doom Slayer can use, with new additions as well as some old favorites like the Super Shotgun. The wide array of weapons makes the game interesting and unique, as it allows you to find new and creative ways to kill the demons standing in the way of your ruthless rampage.
The lore and story of the game is an excellent addition to such a high octane game. It centers around your character, the Doom Slayer, as he trudges his way across the UAC base on Mars with only one plan in mind: Stop the demons by any means necessary and close the portal to Hell. More of the story is revealed as you pick up data entries as collectibles, which can be read in the Codex.
This game seriously makes you feel like a badass. Everywhere you go, you decimate everything in your way. I just love the idea of everything in the game being scared of you, instead of you being scared of them. You feel extremely BA when you complete the many fights near the end of the game on Ultra-Violence, although any difficulty will do. Just the feeling of taking out everything in gory fashion and walking to the next area to do it all again is amazing.
Next, I'll address the multiplayer. The multiplayer mode is a lot of fun, actually! It's got a lot of different game modes and ways to play. Of course, you have modes like Deathmatch, but that's expected in an id Software game, but you also have new modes, as well. I'm unsure if anyone is still playing the multiplayer, as I played it back in 2016 when everyone was online. If there is, I hope they enjoy the many ways to play on there.
The Snapmap mode is arguably the most unique addition to this game. It allows you to create your own maps and publish them for other people to play. I've seen a lot of levels that people have made, and I'm genuinely blown away by what the engine is capable of. The possibilities are endless! To me, that's awesome, since I can be a very creative person.
The game is extremely addicting, and I would say that DOOM Eternal took the combat and perfected it. All the problems with the game were solved in the sequel, which is great. There were only a few problems with the game, but most of them are minor and come from me coming back to this game after DOOM Eternal, like the lack of dashing and grenade launchers. Other than that, this game is amazing, and I would recommend this game to anyone who wants a high octane experience that never lets up and delivers some of the best fights I've ever played (the Cyberdemon is amazing)! 9/10 A good game, but DOOM Eternal is so much better.\t\t\t\t\t
