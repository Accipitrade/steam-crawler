DOOM is the epitome of modern arcade action

Brutal, gory and relentless. Everything arcade players always wants: pure freaking action. id Software made a single-player campaign with agile and no-bulls**t pacing, so the player just destroys demons with lots of different weapons listening to some industrial heavy-metal (and some "plot noises" on the background). The story is predictable and id Software knew that, because they absolutely nailed the thought that people do not play DOOM for the plot, so they focused on everything else: dynamic combat mechanics, a good weapon/character upgrade system and Metroid Prime-style maps that makes every level worth exploring even if it slows down the pace a little bit. It's simple, rewarding and fun.

After so many generic titles, DOOM is finally back with the greatest ripping and tearing of the last decade.
