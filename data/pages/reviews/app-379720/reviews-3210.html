\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo what do I think about the new doom? Well there are three parts to judge, and I will review each part.

The single player, easily the best part of the game, the single player of the game is hands down the best of the three features. The single player of this game was so good I bet it will win many best shooter and game of the year awards just because of how enjoyable the single player was. The levels are non linear, have replay value are challenging, full of secrets, monster variety and guns. The game get more added in the later you play and you can carry all your guns through the levels with you making combat much more fun, you have no limits to the number of guns you can carry. the story of the game is great, you dont have to spend for than a few miniutes listening to exposition and most of the time when you do doom guy is angry and destroys anything in the way of him killing demons and that includes a computer telling you story. The game starts out and in a few seconds you get to start shooting things. You can read PDAs if you care about story and you spend 95% of the single player shooting things, the way it should be.

The snap map. How good is snapmap? Well there are two ways to look at his, for consoles it is much better than the map maker in halo forge and will get more added to it in the future so it might get a little better but the issue with it is that there are stupid map making limits in the builder the classic doom builder 2 is a better map maker because you can make a map with 100,000 demons on it while in the new doom game there is a limit to the numbers of monster allowed in game to be spawned at a time and a limit on the number of monsters allowed on the map total as well as a limit on the map size. Clearly the map maker was made with consoles in mind and that would be okay if the game had external mod support like classic doom did but sadly the new doom does not. The snap map builder is easy to use and and I guess a good introduction to learning how to make maps miles ahead of halo forge but if you have used any kind of good map maker snap map is not as good as map builders that are 20 years old sadly but on consoles it is the best map builder available.

The multiplayer.... The worst part of the game in my opinion. I got the game a few weeks after the game came out and the online multiplayer was quite barren with only a few thousand players on it. Why is the multiplayer not nearly as good as the single player or other online shooters? There are many reasons why the doom online multiplayer is bad such as being made by halo developers, having limitations like 2 guns only, loadouts, perks that the game calls hack modules that are basically like power up cards, the game is poorly balanced because a level 1 player has HUGE disadvantages and a level 50 has HUGE advantages. You do not even start with all items or guns, you can save up and earn hack modules that are basically just like the hated cards or perks in halo 5 that if you save up enough of you get unfair advantages over players who don't have these items. Some of the power ups include temporary unlimited ammo, extra armor and knowing both the health AND location of the player that killed you and there are 3 theirs of these power ups bronze silver and gold better the higher the tier. The multiplayer is very unfair and poorly balanced for 4 reasons,
1 The higher your level the more guns items you have that a level 1 player does not. This gives you more variety and different guns to use on players who don't have the same options that you do. Items are also given out to higher levels and they can give advantages to players who can use them on players who don't have them.
2 Hack modules are unfair because the more matches you play regardless of level even if your the same Max level a player who has more hack modules than you has things like knowing your Hp AND where you are on the map, infinite ammo for the beginning of the map and more armor. If you save up these and use them on new players or players who don't save modules you have a unfair advantage over newer players.
3 Demon runes, demon runes are fun the first few times it nice to play as a demon its fun you get a few free kills and you think everything is good right? Well no it is unfair because demon overpower marines. You might think oh its fair because they get some power if I get some power too right? Well you don't un lock all the deom runes at the start of the game you only get one out of 4 and you get 3 demons at level 15. So this is just really unfair to newer players because they don't even have the same runes, guns, hack modules, items, etc that you do. On the other hand haste and quad damage power ups from quake are fair because if you kill the other player you get to take the quad damage from him the second you kill him while the timer for the item is still active. You may or may not get the demon rune because it may not spawn even if you can kill a demon that has much higher dps and health than you. Also the most popular game mods are ones that have the fewest demon runes.
4 Community after a few days playing I have already heard about people cheating in the multiplayer, I haven't seen it to be sure but it might be in the player base. There are not very many people playing the multiplayer so as far as I can tell on PC it is dying. Another hug issue is groups and balance. If you have a few level 50 friends and you as a group go into team deathmatch you will all be on the same team even if the other team is level 1-10 and your team is leveled 40-50. Making the multiplayer even more unfair but at least it is really really easy to play with friends.

So do I recommend the game? I do but it is worth it for the single player alone, snap map is okay but could have been much better with just mod support, and I really don't like the multiplayer because it is very unfair to players in many ways but it is enjoyable and you can get kills easy with the gauss cannon, demon runes and that's fun but it is also unfair when you get killed in one shoot by heat seeking missiles, a BFG/gauss cannon blast or by advantages of hack modules. I payed 80 dollars for the game and I am happy I did. Buy it for single player and if you want to make a few maps or play a multiplayer few matches for only a few hours but you want a great singleplayer story.\t\t\t\t\t

Coming from a gamer (primarily single-player) who appreciates BOTH old-school titles like Quake and contemporary shooters alike, I can hands down say this is id's best game to date (and arguably one of the best shooters yet made).

Not only does it greatly improve on the things older id games already excel at (faster action, large complex levels and combat spaces), but it actually excels at things id traditionally sucked at (almost all enemies are highly agile and excellent at navigating the complex terrain to attack the player; with deliberate exceptions). The story is surprisingly engaging too (simple but very well executed). New features like armour and weapon upgrades add to the fun as well. To top it off, the boss fights are intense (id bosses are normally lame and easy- but these ones killed me three times... each.


If you're looking for great single player (but not MP- cough), this is a must-buy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBethesda and id nailed it. This is a great game.

The gameplay is fluid, fast-pasted, and exciting. The story is well-written with lots of extra content. The maps are huge and there is a lot to explore. The weapon arsenal is awesome and the demons are great. The new "Glory Kills" are very cool.

The devs promised a 13-hour campaign, which is nice considering that FPS games nowadays seem to tack on the campaign as an afterthought. I haven't played the multiplayer very much but it is definitely a lot of fun. The game modes are neat and the maps are awesome.

DOOM plays a lot like the old titles, which is a refreshing departure from the Halo/CoD format that's plaguing FPS games these days. If you're looking for a violent shooter that plays great and is a lot of fun, then this is it.

I've had a few problems with the game crashing during gameplay or failing to load. Perhaps these issues will be sorted out with a driver update for my 390x.\t\t\t\t\t

Old review: june 20th 2016.

I've spent 33 hours on this version of doom, in single player (one thorought playthrough of ultraviolence), tinkering around in the mapmaker, and playing multiplayer.

This is ABSOLUTELY A GOOD GAME.

I have spent over 2000 hours in the classic doom, developing mods, playing new mapsets, ect.

The difference is two things to me: 1.) Nostalgia. I played classic doom as a kid and it will always have a special place in my heart. Not much i can critique the developers on that. 2.) Linearity. This game is linear. I feel like they streamlined things from classic doom, which was already fairly streamlined. Maps are far from complex, and ultimately... repetitive. Sure there are memorable moments, and I will probably play this game on and off for awhile...

But the replayability factor and the complexity and linearity of maps are a hindrance. This would be no issue if people could make a single player experience in the editor, like in the original doom. the reason that the original doom is so popular is that there are literally 6 figures of mapsets to choose from, most as good if not better than the original maps, and dont change gameplay.

This is not so in the new doom. No single player Co-op is a drag too, but frankly, because doom is so stylized, you cannot create the same feel and playstyle of the single player game in the provided editor.

because of this, I fear this will not replace the classic doom for me. How could it? thousands of weapon packs, monster expansions, and even total conversions that keep you coming back for more. Doom can never be this flexible, and the vast majority of people will appreciate that its so streamlined.

8/10, can defintely recommend, even for the $60 dollars. just expect to bottom out at about 30-50 gameplay hours until expansions and dlcs are implemented. This is not a high replay value game, despite its advertisement to the contrary.

To be fair, Shadow Warrior is in the same bandwagon, yet another excellent classic remake. i'd say about the same thing, only i paid $20 bucks for that game. In that sense, i'm much more pleased with shadow warrior, yet my life would probably never be complete had I never been able to play Doom.\t\t\t\t\t
