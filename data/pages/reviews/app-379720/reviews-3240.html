I was hoping it\\'d be a return to form-which I'll admit I never experienced in the first place, being born the same year OG Doom was released. But I'd kinda built up a romantic ideal of Doom built up from word of mouth osmosis and that amazing comic.
So I was extremely pleased when "Rip and Tear" was in the first seconds of the game. The more I played, the more it became everything I'd wanted and more. I didn't have any real firsthand nostalgia for Doom's gameplay but as I realized just how finely-tuned this one was, it definitely seemed like it'd come down off the mountain to show others the error of their ways.
The biggest golden calf it upended is probably forcing narrative on the player at the expense of gameplay. Most of the characterization and humor(!) of Doom come from the Doom Marine\\'s outright physical hostility toward exposition, smashing monitors and frequently disobeying orders. I know The Verge said it in their review, but I thought it first dammit- Doom is the Fury Road of video games. Pure action, minimal exposition (but a well-built world if you really wanna dive in), all with the near-perfect execution that only comes with decades of experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile it was broken as hell at release, it got fixed, so I bought it again, yay. So as before, game is amazing, gorgeous, even BETTER frame rates with Vulkan (which show on my new monitor), and fun as ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hell. And the problems I was having, gone, No problems whatsoever. What was the problem? Who knows, probably Denuvo. How was the problem fixed? Who knows!

Anyway singleplayer campaign is amazing and there's some great story there if you want to piece it together. Or you can ignore it and just murder the ♥♥♥♥ out of demons all day. And there are plenty of reasons to go back and try to find all the collectables and secrets scattered about.

So you finished the game, now what? Well, Snap-Map. It's meh. Some interesting stuff has been done with it, but most of it is: Here's a tunnel with a few monsters that no effort has been put into. Or it's a room that lets you get the achievements in a few seconds. It's a pathetic attempt at matching the modability of the original Doom. In fact I've been playing some Doom today, the original, not this one. I've got about 30 mega wads to run through, and I can toss in some Brutal Doom or even Brutality to change it up even more, or one of the hundreds of maps, gameplay conversions, or insane joke wads (a wad where you fire baguettes and Steam sales at enemies, or you escort a barrel through levels), blah blah blah. Will Snap Map give DOOM 2016 the kind of staying power that will keep people playing it in 20+ years? Are you kidding? NO! ♥♥♥♥ no, why would you even ask something that stupid. Jackass.

Then, then there's the multiplayer. And you can definitely tell it was made by pathetic console peasants. It's ♥♥♥♥. It's a dumbed down version of Halo's multiplayer. And the demon pickup is broken as hell. Or maybe it's not. Who the ♥♥♥♥ knows right now, I tried it for all of 10 minutes during the multiplayer beta and I have no desire to ever go back.

So DOOM, is it fun? Yes! Is it worth 60 bucks? Eh, not really. I'd say, if you're a 100% completionist and you're going to be ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ about in Snap-Map, both building and playing, then yeah, maybe 60. But on sale? Snap it up! (Ya get it? Snap it, Snap-Map, eh ♥♥♥♥♥♥.)

If you really want the most Doom for the dollar, buy Doom Classic Complete and a source port and go nuts with the literally thousands of WADs available and wait for DOOM to go on sale. I'd recommend Brutal Doom if you're looking for a bit of the old ultra-violence, or even Doom 4 for Doom for a similar experience to the new.

TL;DR Get Doom Classic and GZDoom and play the ♥♥♥♥ out of the originals (and Brutal Doom), then grab this the second it's on sale.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game but it's not as great or timeless as the classics. The classic doom games could be modded to become pretty much anything you want. After you beat this game and play some of the multiplayer, their isn't a whole lot of reasons to come back. The glory kills are probably my favorite thing about this game but it's not enough to make me come back years later.
On it's own this game is good. The old doom gunplay is still intact and it even adds a new layer of verticallity to the game that's never been seen in a doom game. I'd give this a 7/10. It's great but in the end I don't think it's worthy of the mantle of Doom. Adding a steam workshop would multiply the fun factor. Allowing free customization without dlc (which feels more like microtransactions since you aren't actually downloading anything.) or credits would improve it. It's a solid foundation, now if they only added more. My final complaint is this slow ass launching system they use. If you launch the game and decide you want to play multiplayer, you have to re launch the game in multiplayer mode and the same is true for snap map mode, not even the original doom made you do that.
One word to sum up Doom 2016:
Unworthy\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been waiting a long time for this one.

Not to say I've been looking forward to this release as such. My story is thus: As a kid, my first FPS ever was the shareware Doom levels (the first part of the first game). I loved these levels. I memorized all the secrets, I mastered the level layouts, and I shot oh, so many demons and possessed humans. I even ran terrified from a couple of Barons of Hell.

This feeling got even better as I branched out into the FPS genre as a whole, playing a huge list of games. I went from Doom, to Dark Forces (people forget that the first was essentially a Doom clone in the Star Wars universe, with no "force" to use, and it was amazing!), eventually moving into proper 3D playing endless Quake II and Half-Life, up through Half-Life 2 and beyond.

Now, just before Half-Life 2 came out, there was Doom 3, the long, long awaited sequel to Doom II. I played it about a year late, having fallen in love with what Half-Life 2 was hyped up to be. What I got when I finally played it was... disappointment.

This is not to say Doom 3 was a bad game. It was actually very well done. It had an excellent, detailed aesthetic, artsy demons, a simple, predictable, but workable story, and some glorious guns. My problem was that they took Doom in the obvious, horror direction with 3. They tried to make a truly scary game (it got me a few times, and having to swap between the flashlight and a gun was a cool and frightening thing). Thing is, it wasn't really that scary, and even if it had been, I didn't WANT scary.

I wanted that Doom feeling again. I wanted a metal soundtrack, with constant firefights with wicked demons, in a modernized shooter. I didn't want a tight, dimly-lit corridor horror game. Some really liked it. I did too, to an extent, but was always left with that sad feeling that it wasn't quite what it could have been. With this game, I abandoned Doom as a whole for a bit.

Some nostalgia was obtained with Brutal Doom (an excellent mod of the original) but it wasn't something that lasted. I gave up.

Doom 4 was announced. Time and again, footage and leaked screens that I saw as I browsed the internet at random failed to impress. I was not hyped for it at all, as I had been for 3. It was looking to be way worse than 3, and every time it was spoken of, it was about how it was (ironically) descending into development hell, a place very few games make it out of (and those that do often SUCK. I'm talking to YOU Duke Nukem Forever!).

Then Doom 4 was released. Reviews starting popping up everywhere. Jim Sterling said he LOVED it. When I finally saw some footage of gameplay, my jaw dropped.

It was Doom. It wasn't trying to change even more, it went BACK. Back to Doom II. Back to what we loved... except this time, in GLORIOUS detail, intricate design, and flawless execution. It also modernized what it was without spoiling it. Everything that was added to this game was placed there to help players embrace what Doom really is: Unforgiving, relentless demon onslaught, and a protaganist that simply does not give a DAMN why what happened, happened. Your mission? Stop hell. How? Kill... them... all.

I loved exploring the original Doom for secrets, and those secrets often revealed a new weapon (obtained earlier than otherwise possible) or an amazing power up. In Doom 4, you will find the same sort of thing, only better. In addition to finding weapons early, and power ups, exploration rewards you with points to upgrade your guns... mostly the same classics as Doom, but now with even MORE kick.

The Chainsaw and BFG are now INTEGRAL to success when they arrive. Killing a demon with a chainsaw rewards precious ammunition, and the BFG... well, the bosses do not like it (and anything that isn't a boss ceases to be, as it should be).

And I have to mention, of course, the core new mechanic: Glory Kills. This is the coolest possible addition that can leave it still feeling like Doom. Glory Kills reward health. Glory Kills are Melee Finishers. This encourages brutal, constant movement as you RIP and TEAR through demon after demon, not only to kill them, but to sustain yourself. Standing still in Doom was never wise... and in this game, nothing is more imporant than learning when, and how, to be intimate with the nearest demon, be it a lowly Imp or a mighty Baron of Hell.

It has a sort of leveling system, in that obtaining enough power ups of various kinds can increase your ammo pool, hp, and armor, and enough elite guard chips give specific extra bonuses to movement and survival. These, however, are obtained by exploration rather than combat, and I refer you back to classic Doom secrets being AMAZING and satisfying to find.

There is a story, and it is very simple. It is also executed exceptionally, never trying to be more than it is, while maintaining excellent voice work, and interesting characters (and there are NOT many of those, thank goodness).

Finally, despite being a gory and constant barrage of demons and death, this game is not scary AT ALL. It had one (ONE!) jump scare that I can even remember in the whole game, and if you have played any horror FPS at all, you saw it coming so far away that you may have pre-emptively thrown a grenade.

I love the new Doom. It feels like it has achieved everything I have wanted since Doom II. So, now that I have shared my story and feelings, I leave you with:

TL;DR - If you loved the horror-styled Doom 3 better than Doom I and II, then this will not be your cup of tea. If you were looking for a modern FPS with simple wait-and-heal mechanics and realistic visuals, play a Call of Duty game instead (being serious, not sarcastic). For those that want an honest action shooter with no strings attached, you will have a TON of fun with Doom. Finally, to the many fans of Doom I and Doom II... it's finally time to finish what we started, and avenge Daisy, our pet rabbit.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is by far the best fps game I've played in a long while. The stunning visuals in this game are amazing. Nothing seemed to by downgraded for any reason. The bonus content in the game (collectibles and secrets) are so much fun to find and uncover.

Gameplay is amazing. It left me breathless when I started playing. I mean, who doesn't like shooting things and ripping them apart just for the heck of it?! Every enemy has different strengths and weaknesses so it always keeps you on your toes! One thing I've learned after playing through the story and finding everything is that you must move! None of that camping BS is allowed, and if you do you WILL die.

For all those that want a fun experience and a throw back game, I would highly recommend buying this. The story may be a bit short, but if you spend time uncovering all the secrets the story becomes very rich and interesting. It gets a 9/10 from me!

SIDE NOTE: There are a few bugs. I've noticed that artifacting occurs in some places of the levels, but nothing major. Usually you can fix it by exiting the area and entering back into it. Another thing that happened twice was the game crashing. NVIDIA software on my computer claimed that the driver crashed. I don't believe that this was caused by the driver software and GPU. Another thing to add is that NVIDIA Experience wouldn't detect the game in my library. I don't know why this would happen because NVIDIA advertised that they were supporting the game.\t\t\t\t\t
