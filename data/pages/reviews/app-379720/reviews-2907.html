\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM [single player campaign] has gone back to its roots. The result is the best old school shooter since the days of Quake and Quake II. Pacing swings between blood pounding mayhem and suspenseful exploration of the vivid, eerie environments. Players who enjoy exploring every nook and cranny will be rewarded with extra upgrades to their weapons and armor, but those who would rather carve a direct and bloody path will still earn enough resources to upgrade much of their gear by the end of the game. Throughout the single player campaign players will encounter terrifying, well-designed boss encounters that test players' skill and ability to literally strategize on the run.

The weapons in the game start out as standard fare, but as players progress through the story their alternate fire modes can be unlocked then upgraded. And, the final upgrade for a weapon is bought not with upgrade points but a test of skill which, when unlocked, can turn just about any gun into a weapon of mass destruction. Each weapon beyond your trusty pistol is useful in different situations. I found myself using almost all of them throughout the game. The pistol, however, never runs out of ammo and can be upgraded as well; don't underestimate it!

Armor upgrades use a different resource but offer a similar diversity of options to suit playstyles. And, completionists will find many "rune challenges" which allow them to equip runes of power on their armor which have a variety of extremely powerful effects. Explorers are really rewarded here, even moreso than with weapon upgrades!

Standard difficulty is challenging but fun; I only hit a few sticky spots that required a few tries. Overall I did not die too often but still felt challenged and threatened by the enemies. Some of the bosses also took a few tries (as they should!). I have not tried hard or easy modes as of yet, but it's important to note that you can change the difficulty at any time, so if you get stuck you can lower the difficulty temporarily (I never felt the need to do this but the option is there).

Having not played much in the way of FPS games lately (except for about 100hrs or so of Overwatch), I was quite surprised when I found myself devouring this game over the course of a day. And when I was done, I wanted more! If you like FPS games at all, I'd definitely recommend DOOM's single-player campaign.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom. I grew up playing the originals and it was the game that got me programming. For a long time since the originals (Up to doom 2) there hasn't been much good coming out of the studio. Doom 3 and Rage were.. Questionable. No, that's the wrong word. Forgettable is the right word. I played them with waning enthusiasm because they felt more like work than anything else. It wasn't the pure pleasure experience we all got from playing Half Life 2 for the first time.

So enter the DOOM reboot. What's satisfying about this game? The executions. Great fun. In fact it's the only thing that kept me going. The collectables also, (And I'm not that kind of guy, honestly) were a neat addition, allowing you to power up weapons and add some extras, but honestly, the hardcore nature of the game doesn't lend itself to the character meticulously searching for ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.

Doom, at it's core, isn't about vertical jumping sections (There was too much of that in this game) and secret collects. Doom is about beating the ever loving ♥♥♥♥ out of an entire demon army that actually seem a little surprised that you're not running the other way in terror. It's about being the ultimate badass because you're not afraid of death. This game captures the DOOMED SPACE MARINE better than any game in the past decade.

If you've got the kind of rig that can really play this game, it shouldn't be missed. It's a mere footnote in the history of gaming, but an important one as we move to the next incarnation of everyones favourite media.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely adore this game. It is easily one of my favorite games of all time. The gunplay is amazing, the enemies have great variety, and the levels are intutive. My favorite aspect is the gameplay style. The ideal play style is not to slowly pick off the enemies from a distance with a sniper rifle, the game is just begging you to run into the middle of the fight and start shooting demons in the face with a shotgun.
The story is also surprisingly interesting, tying in lore from the earlier games and creating a solid character for the marine without him saying a single word.
The multiplayer is a bit lackluster, but the singleplayer campaign alone is easily worth the asking price, and at the time of me writing this, DOOM is on sale for $20, an ablsolute steal.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCampaign was very repetitive and what variation it had was minimal. It was just proceed to an area and run around it for ten minutes killing boring enemies who despite their variety, don't require different tactics to fight each type. Just run around in circles and spam whatever weapon you have ammo for (except for the useless pistol and combat shotgun). The music is also pretty repetive, pretty much all just metal that sounds the same and just blends into the background. It took me about half a year to will myself to finish this borefest.

My first problem with multiplayer is that I am not given an option to launch into it which is stupid. First you have to launch the main game and then go into multiplayer which relaunches the entire game. I feel like this is really unnecessary and id should build their game better. Then the multiplayer itself is very stupid with tons of useless and small cosmetic and gameplay-effecting gear constantly making all your menus have a new notification on them. None of the weapons feel very distict since they are all balanced to the same general damage output and thus choice is not much better than what picking out a skin for other games would be. The demon rune is the center of every game because it is a overpowered ability that guarantees free kills. Playing with it and against it feels very cheap.

Not much to say about snapmap here, I just was not really willing to play much because of the other two terrible modes. Though for all the faults I find in this game I can't deny that it is very visually impressive and has really good art and lighting. I also liked the collectibles throughout the campaign and rune challenges which were the only source of real game design variation. I would probably only recommend this game on a deep sale.\t\t\t\t\t
