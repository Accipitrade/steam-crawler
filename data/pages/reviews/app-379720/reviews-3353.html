\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a steal at 50% Off. This game gets my vote for game of the year and brings back such nostalgia for old school gamers like myself who grew up on quake arena and the 1st doom.

Single player campaign is hella good. So good in fact, that after finishing the campaign, i immediately went back and started over on higher difficulty and played it again. No other game has enamoured me so much that i had to do a replay back to back. Single player is not heavy on story, but there is enough story to make it interesting. Where it really shines is game mechanics, graphics and pacing which is frantic, edge of your seat style.

I also really liked the multiplayer, especially now that it has more variety, CTF, DM, TDM, Point capture are all there. It has elements of new school, but is mainly old school with health pickups, and power ups and really reminds me of Quake Arena, with things like quad damage.

MP base is still strong, no trouble finding a match.


Graphics are 10/10, and game runs smoother than anything else out with the new Vulkan drivers. I'm getting 90fps at 1440p on ultra, everything maxed, even AA, with just a 8gb rx 480.

Honestly, I'd be really suprised if this doesnt get game of the year.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't expect to buy this game when it was announced. I wasn't even that excited about it. The more I watched gameplay leading up to release, the more curious I got. Suddenly, it was May 13 and it was on Steam. I didn't realize it was release day until I opened Steam and saw it in the store. What the heck. I bought it. Wow. This game is brilliantly executed. The visuals are excellent, the gameplay is fast, furious, and fluid. The minor gameplay mechanics like weapon and suit upgrades are there, but don't get in the way. It's full of easter eggs and doesn't take itself too seriously. They managed to capture the classic DOOM gameplay feel even with giving it a modern facelift. The multiplayer is adequate after a few updates, and feels a bit like the Unreal Tournament or Quake style and feel, but also stinks a bit of Call of Duty, which is a bit disapointing. I could do without the tacky victory dances and perks. That said, the single player mode is a true old school style FPS. You run and you shoot. If you stop, you die. Run, jump, fire, swap, fire, jump, run. Frantic fight sequence after fight sequence. Give it a try. If you don't like it after 45 minutes or an hour, you probably won't like it (it does get significantly more challenging after you enter Hell) and you can always request a refund.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far I haven´t seen anything I haven´t liked. The campaign can feel a little repetitive and there isnt much of a storyline to it but what do you expect when buying a doom game? The music doesn´t really pump me up much but puts me into that kinda focus mode where you don´t blink for a few minutes. Theres are lots of secrets in the game so you´ll be doing alot of running around trying to find upgrades and so on. I´ve seen a few people complain about the sound effects and I´ll agree with them that the shotgun doesn´t really sound or feel like something that would be capable to blow something to bits. There are a few things they could improve upon but nothing has stopped me from wanting to play more.

The multiplayer is fun and addictive, it´s much more fun than blackops, it´s its own game. I grew up with the first doom games and I loved them and I think if you´ve played the previous doom games you´ll love this one too. Regarding the price, it has a high price tag and it doesn´t come as a surprise that they´re asking for 60 euros, almost all games nowadays come at the same price. So overall is this game worth buying? Hell yeah.

((I7 6700 processor + Nvidia gtx 970 4gb + 8 gb ram, game runs like a charm and looks beautiful too.))\t\t\t\t\t
