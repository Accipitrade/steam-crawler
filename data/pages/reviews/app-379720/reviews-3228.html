\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes. Just yes.
Doom has a very similar pace to that of the second installment in the franchise (Doom II) which is a really important factor to making the game great. I still think Doom III was OK but the slow pace and the horror element of the game made me dislike it quite a bit. With Doom (2016) I got back the feeling of being the most awesome dude to ever exist. You go fearless into battle, and come out even more fearless. The monsters/demons in the game are pretty cool. We have some of the original demons such as the revenant, possessed soldier etc, and also some I won't tell you about because it's kind of a surprise element to encounter them in the story for the first time. Overall I'd say the game is really good and well optimised despite the gorgeous graphics. There might be a few texture rendering bugs in some cases, but I personally haven't encountered any of these.

10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game just made my week as I played it non stop, I was always looking for a game where the story didnt really matter until critical points and they would just hand you a massive gun and say "Good luck, have fun" and DOOM is that game.

Finally a game where the story isnt the focus, all they want you to do is have an absolute amazing time crushing, tearing, cutting and destroying any demons that get in the pathway to your objective which is just like the original doom.

The movement and animation is very quick and fluent without any sudden jerks or "lag", they captured the classic hoverboard with a shotgun type of feel and the glory kills. The glory kill is a very brutal execution that finishes off a staggared demon and drops extra health, I was skeptical about this first but after performing the first one I enjoyed them because the animation was one quick fluent movement that didnt take up too much time. This game is SUPER brutal if you have read other reviews, it is completely over the top which is why I like it alot with some of the simple touches of gore to outright ripping demons in half.

The campaign was brilliant, the story was easy to follow and understandable with most of it being told through holograms which if the player wanted could just run past and completely ignore. The game onnly forces you into cutscenes or dialogue during critical moments so that you actually understand the story. Doomguy once again was a complete badass as always, once again not caring about anything else except from shooting and killing every single thing related to hell he could find. He isnt invincible however and this adds to the brutality, you dont simply die in doom as most of the time the demon responsible will perform a proper and extremely brutal execution on you which adds a nice touch because you begin to feel what doomguy feels, and this once again captures one of the classic dooms effects. Another thing about the campaign I loved was what happened when you picked up a new gun, doomguy would examine the gun slowely like it was one of his old friends and another nice touch to this was depending on where the gun was found it could be immaculate or covered in blood.

Multiplayer will be the next focus of this review, its not nearly as good as the campaign and more as an extra to the game. However I still had alot of fun with it and dont fully understand the hate towards it, its a very hard job to cater towards the old and the new simultaneously and get it perfect. I think ID did an alright job of it with loadouts being one of the only things that was close to the new, you had to find health via kills or drops on the map along with armor. The scrabbles to get to the powerups and superweapons made the game never feel "safe", I was in one game where the enemy team was thrashing us by 50 points, next thing you know my teams picked up Quad damage, the BFG and the demon rune and we pull it back and win. The demon rune is just way to much fun, upon collecting it you will transform into a demon you selected and run riot, the demons you could select are as follows: The revenant which is a skeletal demon with a jetpack armed with dual rocket launchers able to bring death from above, The mancubus which is the tank demon as he has a ton of hp and is armed with grenade launchers to shoot at any unfortunate soul, The prowler which is the stealthy demon of the lot as he has much less hp than the others but posseses the ability to pounce and hang from walls and if a marine gets caught during the pounce upon colliding with a wall will die, and my personal favourite The Baron of hell, this guy is a huge demon with above average HP who only exists to run around tearing every single marine limb from limb. In my opinion the multiplayer isnt that bad and will help to keep you occupied for a longer amount of time without too much boredom, although I have only one complaint. The chaingun, this weapon should be a super weapon as it shreds the demons hp and anyone else without a power up can die before they even register the chainguns shooting them. It revs to full speed almost immediatly making it extremely powerful and killing the wielder relies on him not having any armour which is rare.

Snapmap is a very cool feature in DOOM, modding is a very complex and hard thing to do but Snapmap allows a player with no experience to create a map with relative ease, it is quite easy to grasp and its tools allow some really interesting ideas to flourish. There isn't much else to say about Snapmap although it will keep you occupied for a good amount of time wheather you are building a map or playing on those being recommended.

To conclude this review I would severely recommend DOOM to anyone wanting an FPS that doesnt focus on story but more on the aspect of making sure the player has fun and enjoys the game to its max. You start off at first against imps and the variety of demons increases at the same time as your aresenal, as the developer said "You take one step on the ladder and the demons take one step". The games difficulty and upgrades progresses at a nice rate and the areas are built for combat allowing for some creative strategies when engaging the hell spawn.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is officially one of my favourite fps games and what appeal to me are the Glory Kills and the music! The combination of these 2 will make you feel like a badass who only needs his hands and ammo!

Singleplayer:
Nothing that special all you do is one thing:killing demons! The campaign is decent and what is more awesome is arcade mode! Although not my thing arcade mode in this game managed to make me love it! Last but not least there are some classic maps from the retro games!

Multiplayer:
This ruined the game for me! In multiplayer you'll mostly be finding scrubs with rocket launchers that are too incompetent to use other guns! However it has a very good customization system that expands as you level!

Snapmap:
This was my compensation for the multiplayer! Snapmap is DOOM's (2016) mod support (kinda). Although it isn't as good as the mod support itself, you can manage to sunk some hours in it if not many. You basically create levels for different gamemodes and trust me...IT IS GREAT! I EVEN PLAYED TETRIS IN IT!



All in all DOOM is a game you'll love and what was also good in it was the fact that the game would be as difficult as you would want it. You can either make it a walk in the park or an unforgiving nightmare!

9/10 ('cause multiplayer sucked for me! ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ scrubs with rocket launchers)\t\t\t\t\t
