\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI regard this new DOOM as a faithful return to the original franchise. Obviously it’s not going to be exactly the same, as the original game is far from perfect. With a lot of reboot franchises, the core of the game is usually lost, but this isn’t the case with DOOM.

Id Software have really poured their heart and soul into making this reboot as amazing as it could be, and then some. It reminds me of the original games, yet introduces new and interesting gameplay features that keep it up to date with modern games. It’s a perfect blend of old school meets new school. The new Id Tech 6 engine is one of the most impressive engines I’ve seen. Both in terms of graphics and performance. Granted my PC is above the recommended specs, but I’ve heard it performs well on a variety of systems. The performance was “unchained” essentially, and my computer could be fully utilized to give the best experience possible. Id clearly haven’t forgotten their roots.

The shooting is enjoyable and punchy. Each gun has 2 distinctly different weapon mods to unlock, which add a decent amount of variety to the combat. 2 weapon mods per gun doesn’t seem like much, but they change the way a weapon behaves in such drastic ways. Each stand out on their own, and by the end of the game I had unlocked all of them, and felt comfortable no matter which I was using.

Glory kills are another new gameplay feature. Fast and fluid melee executions that add serious variety to traditional fps gameplay. Originally I thought these would slow down the pace of the combat, but I can safely say I was wrong. They add brutality and enjoyment to an already brutal and enjoyable game :P

The multiplayer is satisfying to an extent. At first glance people assume it was a tack on, but looking into it further you can tell Id have really put some effort into this area. The amount of customization is insane. From acquiring different armour types, to changing the material and colours of your weapons. The gameplay is pure old-school Quake III, slightly slowed down and missing a few features though. Definitely worth your time if you have any interest in faster paced multiplayer.

The integrated SnapMap tool (used for building your own levels and sharing with others), offers a lot more on top of the single player and multiplayer, but sadly the limitations are far too obvious. Clearly it was a tool built with consoles in mind, hence the limited objects, and options. You can only have 10 demons spawned at once, and the building space is far too small. I really wish they would have removed these limitations on the PC version, but sadly I don’t see it happening any time soon. Still, SnapMap is a fun extra, to an already great game.

Buy DOOM, it’s worth every penny.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are thinking of buying this game, there are only 3 simple questions you need to ask yourself.
1: Do I like First-Person Shooters?
2: Do I like games that have no stealth elements?
3: Can I accept some (not all) unclear "points of no return" in each level, in a game with many valuable secrets?

If you said "Yes" to those 3 questions, then you should buy this game without a doubt!

(Nr 3 is a minor inconvenience for most people and can easily be fixed in the future without much effort or affecting level design. An easy fix would be if they allowed you to tick an option in the Options Menu which - if ticked "yes" - puts a Portal at the end of each level that takes you back to the start of the level without restarting it or all spawns, for you to find your missing secrets without trouble before continuing on with the next level.)

[NO SPOILERS! Short review of basic information]:

This is easily the best First-Person Shooter in years. Besides the basics (which for some developers nowadays aren't even basic, since they ignore optimising etc) like that the game is running incredible well and the gameplay feeling amazing, it also manages to be one of the best games in a long time by its long list gameplay features.

I run it capped at 120 FPS, but otherwise I have between 100-140 FPS on Ultra settings at 1080p, the big difference coming from switching between no enemies at all to full on combat. Will mention that I'm not using any blur effects except DOF, since it makes the screen worse for less performance, which is especially noticable and actually affects your gameplay when you move alot while also panning with the mouse and you can't see crap. This is on a GTX 970 GPU with an Intel i5-6600k CPU, 16GB RAM and Windows 7 Pro OS.

The game also has an incredible depth EVERYWHERE you look! Level design, secrets, tons of different interesting suit, character, rune and weapon upgrades using different upgrade points (gained themselves from different interesting sources) or Challenges to upgrade them with, diverse enemy types and behavour etc. Easily one of the best games (of any category) in a long time!
.
.
.
[NO SPOILERS! TL;DR version]: Containing a list of all gameplay mechanics and non-spoilery info I can remember, each point being explained thoroughly. No story info at all. (If you have a Dark Souls mentality about all games and think gameplay mechanics are spoilers for you - because you want to go in completely blind - then don't continue)

-Complex level design with multiple paths to explore, paths either diverging from the main path to join back with it close to where you diverged or sometimes being completely alternate routes themselves, ending with you further up the main path.

-Valuable (gameplay affecting) secrets hidden everywhere. Somewhere between 10-20 per level, trying to find all of which will extend the gameplay extensibly. I have 24.1 hours played atm, all of which are only from my first Singleplayer Campaign playthrough (on the last mission now). Haven't touched any other mode in the game yet. This is mostly thanks to the many secrets and how many of them are very well hidden, but also a small part to me AFK:ing with the game tabbed down. My biggest time sink into this game applies only to people trying to get 100% of the secrets on the first run, I'll take why at the bottom of the review.

-Large arsenal of different weapons, all of which (except the pistol, a shotgun, the chainsaw and the BFG) having 2 different modes that can be unlocked by getting physical mods which you put on the weapon and can (if both are acquired) swapped between with a single key press, that often completely changes how the weapon behaves and it's area of speciality. Sidenote: Each type of ammo (except rockets, saw fuel and BFG) is shared between 2 weapons.

-Largest amount of different ways to upgrade your character I've seen in a campaign.
1: Every weapon can unlock 2 different mods that changes how the weapon works drastically (both mods can be obtained and switched between on the fly by pressing "R").
2: Both mods on each weapon have their own unique upgrade options, upgraded by using Weapon Points gained in many different ways (cleansing a room of monsters, discovering a secret area, completing each one of the 3 Challenges you gain per new mission etc)
3: The last upgrade in each weapon mod-upgrade section can't be bought and can only be gained through completing a mod-specific "Mastery Challenge", which becomes available after buying all other upgrades on that weapon-mod.
4: The suit you wear can be upgraded in 2 different trees, both using different points, none of which being the Weapon Points. One is using what we'll call Suit Points, gained from a computer chip inside the Red Armor Suits of the already dead and immobile soldiers hidden throughout the levels. Used in 5 different specialised trees, each containing 3 unique bonuses based on the name of each upgrade tree.
5: The other Suit upgrade (let's call it A-Cell Upgrade) uses something called Argent Cells (big orbs found in big rectangular chests) which lets you upgrade either your max Health, Armor or Ammo (for all weapon types).
6: You can get Upgrade Runes by finding hidden Rune Challenges, which tasks you with a challenge with a specific weapon, having infinite tries (which you'll need) to complete them. You can equip from 1 (at the start) up to 3 runes at the same time. Each changing how a gameplay mechanic behaves. For example: Glory Kills, Equipment (grenades), Pickups, Ammo drops, Armour drops etc.
7: Lastly, each Rune can be upgraded one level by completing a unique challenge written on them while having them equipped.

Rune note/tip: A favourite Rune combo atm combines one of the last runes you get - which makes you have infinite Ammo as long as you have over 100 Armor (75 if upgraded) - combined with the upgraded Equipment Power rune - which makes one of your grenades generate armor (the more demons caught within it's blast radius, the more armor you gain) - and Glory Kill Armor rune - which makes demons drop armor when Glory Killed.

-Diverse enemy types with different weaknesses, strengths, mobility, projectile types, attack patterns, specials etc.

1: Specials being everything from Stealth to Endlessly Summoning more creatures until killed, or a dangerous and swift Charge which if dodged gives room for you attacking a small weakspot, a dangerous and even more swift charge that CAN'T be dodged so the creature must be killed before or while charging etc.

2:Attack patterns are different between enemies, but they also have some enemies switching patterns on the fly, like changing between acting and attacking like a coward or being inquisitive or being aggresive depending on the situation (Imps), Idiots barely able to walk in your direction or swarming you (basic enemies), No-ducks-given monsters that will chase you to the end of the world (Hell Knights, Barons etc) or monsters staying back for their own reasons, etc.

[End note]: For people trying to get 100% secret completion on their first playthrough: The biggest time factor (and this is my ONLY - smaaall - con for this game) is caused by many levels (not all, but most) having unclear "points of no return", which becomes a even greater problem because there is no set or average number of how many "points of no return" there are per level (I've seen from 1 to around 8).

If you're trying like me to find all of the secrets on your first run, then this will increase your time quite alot. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe singleplayer is amazing!If you have grown tired of the handholding and console-minded slowness of modern FPS-games, the new DOOM is for you! The best FPS action i have played since Wolfenstein: TNO, just fantastic stuff!

There are a lot of things DOOM isnt though, mainly a good multiplayer game and a good story. The mechanics and audiovisual design of the singpleplayer is phenomenal, but do not expect anything more than that. Its pretty obvious that the devs knew what DOOM is, and prioritized the core to the detriment of everything else. I applaud that focus, but wish that they had the balls of Wolfenstein to cut MP entirely.

There where one or two technical mishaps too. I had the game crash on me 3-4 times during my playthorugh, and there was one occassion where my framerate dropped out of nowhere and i had to restart the game. Hopefully these issues will be patched eventually.\t\t\t\t\t
