\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShut up! Shut up! You’re all just a bunch of philistines!

(ah) Well I suppose we could deliberate over such a passé title, even though all is already been said…

You talk and you talk – let me tell you something, DOOM is a beast title. It has syphoned over 200 hours, and out of all the hours thinking somehow that I’ve lost my mind. The story is sharp, but the demon’s teeth are sharper. It is a mistake to think you can overlook Hell’s horrors by shooting enemies from a far and feeling clever for noticing that the real enemy, the corporation responsible for unleashing hell on Earth, is a deliberate metaphor for capitalism.

I said shut up! The earth swarms with people who are not worth talking to, and I will not let your incessant commentary about DOOM’s literary themes derail my focus on the blood and the gore. I will bury you fledgling! (sighing) ghouls…,ghouls...The choking, sweltering, curb stomping, chainsaw revving, eye gouging, dismembering, deadly, killing rule of no rule for Hell’s spawns is the crudest of men’s affairs – and DOOM assents to it in all it’s divinest.

Absolutely every single glory kill is bloody disgusting. They’re crisp, many, and uniquely different enough that they do not get tiresome. The visuals offend the sanctity of sight, but they go beyond ordinary finisher moves. Each glory kill rewards the player with health or drop, and combined with the game’s deliberate pacing, you have a mechanic that incentivizes player’s to be proactive, aggressive, and strategic. You have to move, and move fast, but instead of hiding, you attack – playing a small game of cost and benefit by risking health to survive and continue your righteous desecration of demons. This game mechanic is not limited to the campaign either, with an arguably more strategic role in multiplayer, and a more pressing one in arcade.

Hahh. No more talking – you’re all dead! Dead!

“The campaign premise in DOOM is the logical and demonstrable end to our current unsustainable economic…” AH! HAA HA HA! ‘Tis all in my head, well, in that case, ‘Hi! How are you?’ “I

I am well, but since life is not about second chances, I must finish my thought. DOOM’s story is about a corporation that taps into a lethal and unsustainable energy source with the potential to maximize profits but at the same time, it will destroy Earth. But alas! In the search for greed, karma is vigilant. There are supplemental themes more prescient of climate change, critical of corporate culture, but I am running out of time…

One last bit. The Static Rifle is one of the best multiplayer weapons in recent history. I run, constantly. I do not like staying still. When I loaded that weapon and saw it gained power the more it was in motion, I was stunned. It felt so right, so deliberate. DOOM managed to design a weapon that perfectly fit my playstyle. Loved it. Oh, I almost forgot to mention how the score is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ lethal. Nothing more kvlt than slicing up a hellspawn in half to a double pedal drummist in the middle of an exorcism.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI gave this a positive review but I just didn't like the overuse of the glory kill mechanics.

The game plays like a twitchy fps and platormer rather than a simple run and gun vesion of the original. Although the game looks great, the gameplay is stale because of the glory kill system which you'll have to collect for upgrades and challenges, as well as a bit of health and armor(rune upgrade) from each kill. You'll be glory killing so much it becomes tedious to do so but also a necessity.

The glory kill action forces a death blow animation that can sometimes feel jerky and jarring or even fustrating when the enemies are closing on you while you wait for the animation to end. This can be sped up by a rune upgrade later in the game, still, it felt a little wonky.

There's also way too much reliance on the gauss cannon for my liking and the sound it makes, well, I found it irritating after awhile. You really only need 2 guns plus a chainsaw to beat the game, the double barrelled shotgun and the gauss rifle. I just wish the other guns were more useful, seems like they were too weak versus the bigger guys and too powerful for the little ones. I didn't find the gun upgrades all that satisfying and some are way better than others. I didn't find switching around weapon mods on the same gun all that useful or necessary.

The boss encounters were the weakest part of the game. They weren't very challenging and basically the simplest of pattern recognition while just shooting with the gauss rifle until it's dead. None of the boss encounters levels were interesting, they all were basically an arena, circle strafe to win.

The story isn't anything special, cliche, and ultimately unecessary. If you watched NOCLIP'S Documentary on the making of DOOM 2016, the developer's admit they struggled with the story and only have one because of "focus groups". This is why you never let focus groups dictate game development. You don't need a 120 page script to find out why you need to kill demons, they are demons. I felt like I knew who the final boss was right from the beginning with no real surprises.

So why is the reviews for this game so high? Well, I think people like pretty FPS shooters with blood and gore. The game also has an excellent soundtrack, mix of metal and electronics that harkens back to the days of original DOOM. The level design is done well and there's many secrets to be found if you look hard enough. The game runs solid, I didn't encounter any bugs or freezes or crashes.

What could they have done better? Find another way to do glory kills that make them less crucial to the game. The game pretty much requires it and because you do so much glory killing, it is a bigger deal than it should be.\t\t\t\t\t
