\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnlike most of the people who have reviewed this game, I've actually played it in it's entirety. I've completed the campaign, spent a couple of hours in multiplayer, and tinkered around in SnapMap. With that said, let's get on with the review:


For 12 long years, people have waited for the return of the Doom franchise. It's been delayed, rumoured, hyped up, delayed some more, and even completely scrapped. It seemed like this game was never going to be released. Until late 2014 when a teaser trailer was released to the public. Doom had returned, and it returned with a bang. Trailer after trailer, interview after interview was released regarding the game. Covering every aspect, and letting everyone know how the game was going to work, and when it was going to be released. The hype train was moving at full speed, and it wasn't showing signs of stopping.

Until an open beta was released.

Everyone on the PC side hated it, Saying it was too much like Call of Duty. The hype train stopped, and for a while, no one really cared about Doom anymore. Everyone thought it was going to be a flop. But now, the game is released, and everyone seems to actually like it. But why? What happened in the full game that the beta lacked? Well:

The Singleplayer:
Basically the entire reason that everyone likes this game so much is the singleplayer. It's an excellent return to form for the Doom franchise, which was something that Doom 3 lacked.

Pros for singleplayer:
-It's fast
-It's tough as nails
-Glory killing is varied, and doesn't break the pace of combat at all
-Guns are diverse, and each one serves a unique purpose
-The Demons are abundant, and must all be approached and attacked differently.
-The level design is superb, and greatly rewards exploration
-Weapon and armour upgrades are well executed
-The plot is absurd, yet fun
-HUD options are highly customizable
-No health regeneration

Cons for singleplayer:
-The bosses are a bit too easy
-I personally don't think that dying sends you back far enough
-There is an objective marker on the compass, however this can be turned off in the options menu
-No Co-op

Overall score for singleplayer: 9/10

The Multiplayer
This is the part that everyone was complaining about, and while I do understand these complains 100%, I don't personally think that it's as bad as everyone says. Hell, I've been having a lot of fun with it.

Pros for Multiplayer:
-No health regeneration
-Gun and armour customization is pretty cool
-Very fast
-Map design is good
-Bonus weapon pickups are very fun to use
-The ability to turn into a demon is very satisfying to use, and can turn the tide of a battle when in the right hands
-Level cap is high

Cons for multiplayer
-Not a whole lot of weapons to choose from
-You get most of the major progression items at a very early point
-Some weapons can be unbalanced (I.E Chaingun, Super Shotgun)
-Season Pass
-No Private Matches
-Not a lot of maps

Overall score for multiplayer: 7/10

SnapMap:
I think that this is what everyone should have been complaining about. This was meant to be a level editor that would somehow replace modding tools. Why? Why not add modding tools alongside SnapMap? It's so limited, I wonder why it was even implemented in the first place.

Pros for SnapMap:
-Private Matches
-Game logic can be edited pretty extensively

Cons for SnapMap:
-You can only use premade rooms
-No Hell areas
-No outdoor areas
-2 weapon limit
-Premade rooms feel empty, even when filled with clutter
-No way to make custom assets

Overall score for SnapMap: 4/10


General game pros:
-Excellent music
-Fantasic graphics
-Great art design

General game cons:
N/A

Overall, this game is absolutely amazing. It definitely lives up to the hype. Even though SnapMap is absolute garbage; The multiplayer is fun, and the singleplayer is a masterpiece. Get this game, it is undoubtably worth it.

Overall game score: 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
