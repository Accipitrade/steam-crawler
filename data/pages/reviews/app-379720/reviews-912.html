\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played Doom in... I don't know, 15-20 years? I picked this up on a whim... and I wasn't disappointed. I love the adrenaline rush this game gives me. With the heavy soundtrack that engrosses you in every gory, blood-drenched, demon-slaying moment and accents the tension of the moment, I haven't felt so immersed in a game in a while. The gun play is on point and the game keeps you on your toes, rewarding you to always be on the move. The exploration and secrets is deceptively deep and it feels very rewarding to discover something. I'm only five hours in but this game is hands down one of the best shooters I've played in a while and I think I'll have to try out Doom: Eternal after this. I recommend this game to anyone who enjoys a good shooter. Furthermore, anyone who wants some blood-pumping action and anxiety inducing combat should give this a go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I were to rate this on a scale from 1 to 5 I'd give it a 3.

The visuals and the mechanics of the game are truly astonishing. The first 5-10 minutes are exhilarating. Then the novelty quickly wears off and it's just not that interesting to play.

The gameplay is basically like this: you walk around some hallways/paths with nothing much happening until you get to a large enclosed area. Then all the entrances to that area get sealed off and a bunch of bad guys appear. You battle them in arena-style combat until the last one is dead. Then the entrances unlock and you proceed to go through more hallways/paths to the next arena.

The game is also too arcade-ey for me. The super-fast movement, the token system for upgrades, the bonus challenges... it's just too shallow. I actually prefer Doom 3 over this simply because it had more substance.

It's not a terrible game. I would recommend getting it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
