\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, yeah I recommend it.
There's a lot I liked about it, but I also had a lot of nitpicks and because of it I'm honestly not in the mood to actually "Review" DOOM.

---------- SECTION 1, NIT PICKS

Alright, so now that I actually feel like listing a few of my nitpicks.

1. Audio Clashing poorly:
Specific Monsters, and specific tracks. Pinkies, Summoners and Lost Souls all have the most obnoxiously loud sounds (to the point where it overtakes the music, actually) and their respective audio cues (Spawning and such) always seem to play on top of you.

So basically you have these enemies spawning, making noise which doesn't give away what direction they spawn in or where they are attacking from, and when you add all the other enemies charging you down, pelting you with projectiles and what-not, making it harder to actually know WHERE they are without having to look.

Annnd when you mix in multiple pinkies/lost souls/summoners with a choir being obnoxiously loud as well... auditory overload, much?


2. The damnable BACK TRACK WALLS.

Now, I don't know how dedicated you were to finding collectables and what not for the upgrade points, but I had a BIG issue with these things to the point where I had to take a break due to the sheer amount of times I had to restart so many stages so many times.

There's nothing wrong with giving us a wall and showing us it's a point of no return with a, say, REALLY BIG PIT or something.
But giving us a door that shuts, and never bloody reopens and gives us NO indication of the fact it's going to stay closed? ♥♥♥♥ move if you ask me.

One of my best examples of this is the first time you go to hell. I got tired of searching for collectables, and since it was an open level map, I just decided to go ahead and wipe the level of enemies so I could come back to hunting.
What happened after I killed the last lot? A door fell down and never reopened. ONE Question
W H Y? Just ends up padding out the time if it takes nearly an hour to do with just ONE restart at the bloody end.

No more nit picks as of the moment.

---------- SECTION 2, POSSIBLE IMPROVEMENTS?


What is a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ joke though, is why this game is over 60~+ GB now. The campaign is NOT 60~+ GB big, so please for the love of CHRIST split it into Single Player / Multiplayer / Snap Map downloads because it'd give me a reason to actually reinstall the game for the Campaign.
That also allows people who wanna 100% The single player experience without having to do all the Multiplayer stuff they may dislike.
Bethesda, ID, I don't know who thought this'd be a good idea but please, PLEASE, separate the different game mods to make it easier for people to download. I don't have 60~GB of spare data laying about given the current state of my games library.

I know, something like Wolfenstein New Order is about 40GB (which encourages me to not re-install it) but at least that was for the single player. Unless of course there's some kinda multiplayer mode I'm not aware of.

DOOM wasn't that big when it first came out I think? Maybe about that size, been too long. But as the updates keep coming in and adding in Multiplayer DLCs and more tools to snap map, it's gotten larger. It'd help a tonne if you could just separate the downloads to Single/Multi/Snapmap.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot even 9/10 on Metacritic for a good reason: It's not a classic or even great, just good.

Completed the game with Heat Blast Plasma Rifle and Uranium-while-Scoped Heavy Machine Gun only, with two-throw, quick replenish frags from Praetor upgrade on bass.

Sink your Argent upgrades into Ammo all first, and your weapon systems from Praetor, and you won't need defense or health. Environmental damage is meh until exploding barrel immunity, I'm sure the Map Nav is glitched, and Dexterity makes so little difference I'd not invest in it even if I had excessive Praetor tokens just 'cuz, y'know.

It's challenging early, but by level 5 I was bored with how routine every demon and encounter was, how formulaic the big fights are organized and obviously presented, and just at times frustrated with this old school silly-straw level design that doesn't fly in 21st century gaming.

Upping difficulty doesn't improve challenge at all.

Enjoy a good 20% of your time figuring out what the eff, over, the level designers want you to do, and what opens what locked door so you can progress when there's no context. Just replayed the game on level 3, spent 10 minutes on a map I've played through before trying to figure out what opened that last damned door in the cargo bay. Turns out it's tied to completing 3 prerequisite objectives, but there's no way of knowing this without stumbling into it. Skyrim provides nearby context clues for all their puzzles, and the ones that don't are typically tied into a tome, conversation, or something similar to solve a puzzle or open a door. Doom 2016 is a Bethesda game that just says '♥♥♥♥ you' on figuring ♥♥♥♥ out, which I understand may be old-school Doom, but what year is it right now?

It's a fun shooter for a bit, full of carnage and a top-tier soundtrack that adapts to every battle, but you can tell when a battle is about to begin by listening for the music to change out of ambience.

If there's a Gore pit, look for a power-up nearby. They're almost always one there.

Kinda logged off after clearing one level again because I cheesed a two Hell Knight, whatever those ones are called that teleport and float about and shoot red floor waves at you, and imps and soldiers out the wazoo with a Berserk pack that took what should have been a challenging encounter into a 20-second do-nothing only memorable for how lame it was.

I don't recommend this game full value. Get it on a Steam sale or some other discount.

Id clearly rested on their laurels on this one.\t\t\t\t\t
