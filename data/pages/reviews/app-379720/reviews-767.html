\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot exaggerating when I say DOOM is one of the best FPS shooters I've ever played. Hot damn, is it great to see id Software still cranking out spectacular FPS games considering they pioneering the genre! The story is fairly straight forward scifi-fantasy fare, and it won't bog you down with the details. Which is great because DOOM's focus is on the gameplay, where you ascend to the level of an unstoppable killing machine with spider monkey-like feats of acrobatics. There are small fights and then there are arenas, where you take on swarms of enemies. These arenas are smartly crafted where you're given plenty of places to jump, grab, leap, duck behind and snipe to depopulate a room full of demonic abominations with hot lead. This is less like John Wick and more like Rocky Balboa but with guns where you don't karate chop weapons out of the hands of your enemies, but block their bullets with YOUR FACE and execute them with a Glory Kill if you wish. The difficulty is scaled perfectly to give you more of a challenge each level, preparing you for the higher difficulties like Ultraviolenceand Nightmare, if you dare. DOOM's arsenal includes the standard weapons such as the combat shotgun, super shotgun, plasma rifle, rocket launcher, gauss cannon, heavy assault rifle, and the pistol. Your special weapons are the chainsaw and BFG. You can switch between different grenade types as well to meet whatever battle conditions you encounter. Plus, you can upgrade your weapons, your armor, health, etc. by completing challenges and collecting items from secret areas. The game tells you which is which and what does what, and tells you fairly concisely so your ADD-addled brain doesn't get lost amidst all the endless murder and mayhem. Happy Demon Hunting!\t\t\t\t\t
