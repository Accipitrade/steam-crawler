Second review with the sole intention to recap
¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†I still don't see many friends with DOOM 2016 and also, I fear some oldschool still don't want the new stuff. Some may not know and some may be skeptical. Many free content has come, so it's time to redo my review. Just a warning though, my pleasing comes from the actual content not from blind love.

A glimpse of how they're respecting oldies. (No Romero's head was found inside)
http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=823395747

I was skeptic about DOOM for three reasons some might have now.
  1. They decided to reboot it again for the third time (Doom 64 counts)
  2. Jumping around horizontally and vertically just seemed incompatible or silly. People told me fatalities would worn off quickly.
  3. Bethesda was publishing the game and I could already smell their corrupt quality control.

Reboot isn't just good, it's necessary
¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†Fun with Doom never really depended on some incredible story and nice plot. I still believe Doom 3 should have been a survival, but this version is an honest no-brainer shooter, intelligently made to fit any ''canon'' from any version and feels welcoming for everyone. The script really resembles all the cheesy from movies like Evil Dead. ''Baron of Hell'' which has a familiar design is now catalogued as "the royal guard of the dark lord of the fourth age" and I know why, it is just fun without big mysteries, sounds like generic fantasy making use of elements to look promising yet purposely vague. Not always, there is a clear repetition of Doom's 3 plot. Remember Sargent Kelly's repetitive orders "Get to the Bravo team"? Samuel Hayden just assumed his boring role. At least his voice acting is marvelous and saves the plot.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=824264048

"We don't need story on Doom anyway" says another skeptical next door. So why you know who DoomGuy is? Plot is always needed, but Intricate plot is another league and Doom will never be intricate. So let us all shut up so we can hear the magic.

Straight Outta K.I.
¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†DOOM music is right on where it belongs; On the deepths of molten metal, and its glorious. Old games had to rely on some heardable distraction due visual limitations, but ID managed to rearrange that, music is again the shining star. Burning Media Player to hear your songs doesn't work that well. When you hear metal with synths and epic choirs in-game, you can feel just as bad@ss as DoomGuy. There are many remixes from the old versions scattered around, but don't google it. Let the game remind you. Mick Gordon from the 1st and 2nd season of Killer Instinct was definitelly the right choice.

Doom remains about combat and graphics
¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†The gameplay is a ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•#ng blast! Every single combat is a mix between Dead Simple & Ever Simpler (I mean its blood boiling ultra-violence) now perfected with brutal weaponry - all of them has second fire modes. Id Tech is on its devil number version and it doesn't impress me that much. You can evolve DoomGuy's armor, but some of those powers are optional and you can tweak many of them. Meaning if you just want to gun them down like old times, you Just have to turn the ''Glory Kill'' reminder off in the options. On Doom 64 you had to find 3 special powerups to become stronger Three Ancient Artifacts and that idea has evolved to the Three Runes here. Some fatalities animations are way too fast and sometimes it can feel mandatory. All previous Doom made this one, even Seargent Mark's mod Brutal Doom found its way and its so much fun I will abstain from criticism.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=827336849

Bethesda saved DOOM from... doom
¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†I hate blind love over Bethesda, but they get the job done. Bethesda actually saved that game and they did it with style. Even the Snapmap sometimes turns on my rusty level editing enthusiasm (like for .wad) back all over again. Who else would feed from some old gamey tags and feelings and renew that, but Bethesda? Whoever was the head behind publishing, he really knows what I would like to see and hear.

Its horror or what?
¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†Doom 3 never really worked for me but it had its moments. Demons and hell aren't supposed to be scary on DOOM now, they're actually a bit cartoonish all again. Things still darky and creepy so weirdness sometimes finds its way up your head, but fun is just non-stop so it collides. Tension might be the word for Doom 3 instead and Pace might be for DOOM.

But, you're still going to hell.

Last thoughts
¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†Exploration sucks on any difficulty. It happened with the old versions of Doom but not on Nightmare's due enemy respawn. You can wipe the entire map clean and keep looking for items with an entire empty level. You find some imps ocassionally, but there is absolutely no dynamism on exploration. The Lazarus Lab is actually the only level where exploration can be really cool, thanks to level hazards.

DoomGuy remains the fearless bad@ss ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• he used to be, Ask the Dragonborn about him. It was just the best FPS of 2016.

About Emoticons, backgrounds and cards.
09/30/17
¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†Backgrounds could have been better. The black & white Baphomet is missing. At least there is the homage to the classic cover, DoomGuy and even Cyberdemon. Emoticons are related to UAC and Tanaka logos, also bright red runes of evil good only to call for satan when doing sarcastic comments. Cards set is entirely based on enemies and they're always fluctuating prices, they're usually middle. It isn't your best option for quick level ups if you want the truth.

Multiplayer?
¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†¬†It is pure frag madness, leaving abominations like CS:GO looking like nothing but horse sheat. Tons of exclusive MP big ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• guns. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tClassic Old School shooter, it is what makes this game unique in this point of gaming history. It's relaxing and fun to just run around, shooting and killing. The creators of doom does an excellent job immersing players into the game. The graphics and audio are terrific. Each fight is exciting, I crank up my volume and enjoy the enticing visuals.

If you are expecting in depth story or creative rpg setups this is the wrong game for you. This game is all about unique ways to kill aliens.

I admire all the extra features this game has. The competitive multiplayer needs work, it isn't has well developed as BF or COD, but if you enjoy the play style then you will easily enjoy the competitive multiplayer scene. There is also map creation and editor which I was mind blown when I first saw its potential. I haven't used it a ton, but it allows for cooperative multiplayer and full level design.

tldr: Excellent slash and bash shooter, with many other features like map creation (cooperative gameplay) and competitive multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start off by saying that this game is extremely fun, one of the best Triple A shooters I have played in a long time. Without spoiling much you follow the events of the usual "UAC is ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• with powers that they underestimated and don't fully understand." You play as the "Doom Marine", as the codex refers to him. Doom Marine is characterized by a few things. Mainly unending rage, and a love for all things that end with either turning every demon into pink mist or ripping them limb from limb. The plot tries to pull the Doom Marine into the sterotypical shooter story where you have to do X thing while being told what to do by a dude half a world away, but he doesn't care about that, Doom Marine only cares about killing demons and doing things that allow him to kill more demons, thats it.

There is something almost relaxing about this game. Its something you can pick up and mindlessly play all day long and just have fun. At the same time it is complex enough to keep you engaged and wanting to do better; wanting to rip and tear better and faster than the last time. The gameplay is the best part of the game, a mix of classic doom with an emphasis on "the right tool for the job" approach to combat. This approach is extremely apparent on the harder difficulties where you can't just run around with the shotgun and expect to win evertime, you have to mix and match your weapons to the situation you are in. The combat is extremely fast paced and enjoyable in the campaign mode, I cannot comment on the multiplayer because I haven't even tried to play it.

For a game that is so focused on combat there is a surprising amount of detail that is put into the world of Doom, there is a lot of deep and interesting lore about the Doom Marine and the world that he inhabits. The best part is that it isn't shoved down your throat, it's there if you want to see it or read it but you could play the game all the way through without really knowing anything aside from what the campaign directly tells you.

All in all a great game and would highly recommend it to anyone interested in a unique triple A shooter.

TL;DR
10/10 "Like Skyrim with demon robots"\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOK, so I've had a few people asking me recently what DOOM is like. Is it just a nostalgia trip? Is it the next coming of JC himself?

The answer: DOOM is a masterpiece in the vein of Old-School FPS games.

The long answer:

If you liked any of the prior DOOM games, not counting DOOM 3 then you will love DOOM because it brings all of the fast-paced, side strafing, power-pickup gathering arcade fun of those games with a modern lick of paint and a movement system so joyful it reminds me of playing Halo for the first time.

Without this joy of movement, DOOM would turn repetitive a lot more quickly. There is no reloading of weapons, enemies deal high damage (on higher difficulties) and glory-kills can be performed on any enemy that is damaged to near-death but has not been slaughtered by your constant barrage. DOOM isn't about monster closets, or narrative. DOOM revels in demonic slaughter, bringing elements of Quake and the old-school arena multiplayer into a single player tour of Mars and Hell.

The enemy types are largely just reskins of the classic DOOM enemies, imps, mancubi, Cyber-Demons, Hell Knights, Pinkies, Cacodemons. You name it, DOOM has it, but this variety also forces the player to engage. When you enter an arena, you find yourself gleefully double jumping, changing weapons (no lame 2 weapon limits here), prioritising tougher enemies until you need the health pickups from the smaller enemies etc to efficiently and creatively slaughter demonic hordes. In the downtime, there are secrets to find, which mix great platforming with the joy of old-school problem solving and lateral thinking, and the "get the powerful weapon 2 levels earlier" secret doors within doors which make you feel like a demon hunting machine.

By stripping back DOOM to the essence of what it was, ID software have also succinctly expressed what we have been missing in modern shooters. Wolfenstein: The New Order proved that big dumb loud shooters could pull emotional punches while still delivering a fun core shooting experience, DOOM proves that big dumb and loud shooters can be big and dumb and still be a worthwhile and ultimately fulfilling experience, that asks us to consider how exactly slowing down shooters and making them more serious made them more fun.

A further side note. DOOM is not optimised particularly well, and performance can vary across different machines. I have found the framerate steady but dips do occur in particularly "busy" levels (think particle effects in the red dust of Hell and Mars and you get the picture). Generally, nothing impeded the feel of fluidity of movement and shooting, and nothing was game-breaking enough for me to stop playing.

Time: 27 Hours (Including DooMGuy/secret hunting)
Difficulty: Nightmare

** Before I begin, a thought; if someone was to mod in a bolter and chainsword from Warhammer 40,000 we would have a game which mastered the feel of the Adeptus Astartes. **

Two words perfectly describe this reboot of a classic: Pure. Entertainment.

Movement, combat, weapon feel and level design are all incredibly tight and fluid. Once you get a good pace set you are nigh unstoppable. Weapons and creatures from the Doom of old have returned - looking both excellent and handling exactly like you might have hoped. Paired up with a soundtrack that keeps the blood pumping as you are demon pulping this is easily looking to be my Game of the Year.

Do yourselves a favour and pick up this excellent addition to the Doom universe!
