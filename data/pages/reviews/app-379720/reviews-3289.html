\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor me this game is a 7.0 out of 10. Decent, but cannot describe it as being worth full purchase price. It's a consolified version of Doom, which, as it turns out, works kind of.

The Good

Graphics (might not run well for you, so no promises)

Gunplay has variety and is functional

Fair amount of content (crappy map editor and multiplayer)

Makes good use of jumping

Fast paced and somewhat old school doom...ish

Good boss fights

Somewhat open levels

The Bad

Multiplayer is absolute garbage (only listed in bad, since Doom is mostly about singleplayer)

The gunplay is marred by a forced press a button and watch an animation aspect for melee

The story (it's doom though, who cares?)? Nobody cares, but the kiddy voice overs for demons are annoying.

Gunplay isn't as fluid as in other comparable shooters (don't even get me started with the original). Annoying animations that will either get you killed, or bore you to tears after 10 hours of seeing them (thank you modern day shooters)

The UGLY

Map design is pretty bad. 90% of your fights with demons are in quake-style arena maps that have been copied and pasted over and over again. No sense of immersion, lame exploration (compared to old doom) and extreme laziness (reuse, tedious, predictable paths, you can tell a lot was automatically generated).

The soundtrack for this game is GODAWFUL. Once you get half way through it sounds like a cat getting murdered by a pair of scissors on a chalkboard. I had to turn it off. Thankfully the ambient sounds are tolerable.


TLDR :

It's alright, but it's not second date material.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game was about what I expected, but it still pleasantly surprised me in plenty of ways. As many have already noted, rather than aim for shock value as Doom's I & III did, this game decided to shoot for all-out ridiculousness, whcih they nailed perfectly to a T. To that end, they went back to the rapidly-paced style of combat the Doom games are famous for, adding in extra blood and gore and tons of secrets as a nice bonus. This latest installement didn't have all that many "wow" moments, but the few it did have were enough to impress me.

While the campaign was tons of fun, multiplayer feels a bit repetitive and confined to small maps while also lacking in gameplay strategy and customizeability for weapon mods. That being said, you have to constantly be on your toes while playing online, so it's hard to be lulled to sleep with boredom.

Great game, all in all, with oodles of challenging scenarios and an endless supply of blood to sate my inner demons. 4.4/5 stars from me.\t\t\t\t\t
