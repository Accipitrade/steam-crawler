\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought the game for ~$30 and I couldn't be any happier with my purchase. The single player campaign was one of the best I've ever played and would easily recommend it to anyone that likes fast paced action and feeling like an absolute badass. As for the multiplayer, many other reviewers accused the multiplayer experience of being lackluster and boring, and that it felt like an odd mix of Doom and other shooters. After putting a good amount of hours into multiplayer, I've come to find that it's not as bad as people make it out to be. The multiplayer is very fast and coming out on top of the leaderboards is dependent on your reflexes, mobility, and knowledge of the map. It took me a while to get used to the gameplay, but once I started getting the hang of it, I found myself really enjoying the game. Instead of listening to the reviews on youtube that all say the same things about the multiplayer, I encourage you to pick it up, give it a shot, and see if you like it for yourself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDooM is a great homage to its predecessors while keeping game mechanics fresh and up to date. I enjoyed the game from beginning to end which is not a common thing with FPS games these days. As for performance the game is well optimised but tends to be less so the further into the game you get. Some of the final missions have a tendency to crash or have various framerate issues. This is something that I am sure will be taken care of later on with further optimisation patches. The Single player is phenomenal and easily worth the purchase alone. Unfortunately the multiplayer suffers from a lack consistency and clarity needed to really tie the two modes together. Much of the multiplayer is unbalanced and the concept of loadouts in this game is counter intuitive to the games nature and overall feel. I enjoyed playing the multiplayer but only in short spurts. As it seemed to be less diverse with the same encounter repeating themselves do to the same loadouts inevitably being used. I would love to see id overhaul this in the future and add some longevity to the lifespan of the game and to revive that arena style fps that hasn't been as prominent for the last ten years or so.

Bottomline is that Doom is a worthy successor to the franchise and well worth the time and money spent. I cannot wait to see whatever expansions id has in store for the future.\t\t\t\t\t
