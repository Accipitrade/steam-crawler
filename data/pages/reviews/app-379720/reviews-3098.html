\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDemon was too tall to kill, so I sawed his legs off. 'nuff said.


... well, no there's more to say. Doom 2016 is an action-packed seamless action game that makes killing demons feel like an an art-form. You look at a problem (aka: "Demon Horde") and think "hmm - how can I do this with finesse?"

Now for the nitty gritty:
Good Stuff
  • Graphics - The obvious stand-out of this game is that it uses the full capabilities of Unreal Engine 4, with convincing PBR and lighting reflecting on highly detailed models and textures. I found myself stopping on several occasions just to enjoy the view, but when you're not enjoying the view, you're engrossed in the gameplay.
  • Gameplay - Push-forward combat system (as opposed to the more "tactical" combat of games like Modern Warfare) make DOOM 2016 feel a lot like old-DOOM. Weaving and dodging through hordes of enemies, wreaking havok until the horde is dead. This is due largely to the benefits of the glory-kill system, which was the pivotal invention to make modern game mechanics play like older games.
  • Environments - Each "zone" is unique stylistically, and is interesting. From giant forges lit by vats liquid metal, to demon-temple excavations, to the hell-dimension itself. There was always something new to look-at.
  • Visceral - The visceral combat, gritty and gorey, really makes the player feel present in the game, and like a total badass.
  • Story - It seems silly to mention this in a DOOM game, since Story is just an excuse to kill demons, but they introduced an extremely interesting and unique villain with interesting motivations, and the overall premise of the DOOM Universe also was quite interesting. I should note here that, despite the title "DOOM" - this DOOM seems to be a sequel to DOOM3.
  • Humor - They don't take themselves too seriously. From throwback hidden levels to fist-bumping adorable Doom Marine big-headed action figures. There are chuckles between the carnage.
  • Progression - Also perhaps a no-no for some fans, there's a progression system for leveling up weapons, max ammo, max armor (etc) as well as special abilities. I feel that this adds a lot of depth to the game, since you can tailor it for particular playstyles.

Bad Stuff
  • Glory Kill Animations - There aren't enough of them. After awhile it got repetitive seeing the same animations over and over again.
  • Level Design - Some of the levels (particularly hell levels) can be confusing to navigate. It can be difficult to know where you're supposed to go. Also, progression is largely based on finding secrets, which may require backtracking. But some levels cannot be backtracked, meaning you may have to play the same level several times over to be a full-completionist.
  • New Enemies - Some of the new demons are kinda dumb. Spoiler alert: I'm thinking the wormy things in armor.
  • Hoop-Jumping - I do feel like the progression system should not be linked to secret-finding. Finding of secrets shouldn't affect core gameplay. It should be for the hardcore fans. But instead, if you want to get all the abilities and upgrades, you need to find all the secrets.
  • Multiplayer & DLC - Id Software does not understand the draw of their own game. Their multiplayer-centric DLCs ignore the main draw of DOOM, which is the single-player campaign. The multiplayer is at-best mediocre. I hope future DLCs will be more campaign-focused.
  • Crashing - I did get a lot of crashing towards the end of the game, especially on hell levels. I'm hoping they've fixed this.

Conclusion
Loved almost every moment of it. I give DOOM a solid 9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStart to finish, DOOM's campaign makes you feel tough, agile, and absolutely unstoppable. I recommend it real hard, you guys.

I haven't touched snapmap or the multiplayer, but just in finishing the single player campaign, I absolutely got my money's worth. The combat loop in DOOM is an incredibly well-designed breath of fresh air in the shooter genre:

The glory-kill/chainsaw system forces you to actively engage enemies to regain health/ammo instead of hiding behind walls for a recharge timer to kick in. However, running around out of cover is never the death sentence it is in Call of Duty or Mass Effect. Every enemy attack is completely dodgeable if you pay attention. The combat arenas are huge, dense with layers of verticality, and demand the player to utilize every square foot to avoid getting swarmed by demons. The climbing mechanics are loose enough that you can get to any platform on the map without messing up a jump, but still tight enough that you feel in control.

DOOM is fast, tactical, violent, and really, REALLY good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you expect this game to be a protagonist that totally wreck demons in a non-stop battle in semi like arena well this game is for you!

PROS:

-Rooms filled with demons that need to be killed before going further
-Secrets to find that will allow you more upgrades on your weapons/armor (you dont have to find them but they really help)
-Armor progress with different "trees"
-Weapon progress with each 2 possible mods with their own upgrades
-Awesome Multiplayer experience which include lots of customizations... like a lot!
-A map editor with community content
-Awesome Graphics
-Bloody glory kills (melee finishers)
-Hours of pure evil madness of demons killing!!!

CONS:

-Very limited storyline
-A bit repetitive because of the room clearing
-Campaign can be very quick (also a pros since it wont be too painfull to start over with a new difficulty)

PS: i had a lot of troubles to make the game work (wouldnt launch) but after managing it i had a lot of fun. I recommend to not try the Vulkan graphic mode since its was cause most of my problems. This game was still worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
