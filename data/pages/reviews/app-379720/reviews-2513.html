\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNever played the original games, but Bethesda doesn't dissapoint when it comes to single player experiences. It's a shame that the majority of AAA developers are focusing towards a multiplayer pay-to-win experience because this is a rare gem that actually kept my attention the entire time I was playing.

The story is your typical 'go run and find/do this' system to progress. I don't mind that since you have to have some sort of will power to progress and do things in a game. The story was slightly lack luster when it comes to the overall premise of the game. Predictable, but easy to follow. But I didn't buy the game to focus strictly on the writing itself because in my opinion it could have been worse. I'm looking at you Assassin's Creed.

Gameplay is very smooth and I didn't find any ground breaking glitches in my single run through of the campaign. I even tried to break it a couple times to see how far I could push it, but the devs put a lot of work into making sure you couldn't go where you wanted or skip some major parts if you wanted. I didn't dive too deep but im positive there are some glitches out there but I didn't experience any playing it casually.

Classic run-and-gun premise where even the tips when loading encourage you to move around. My only gripe when it comes to the guns is the double barrel 'super shotgun'. Once fully upgraded that thing is a BEAST and destroys everything in your path, even on the higher difficulties. I chose the highest difficulty I could my first play through and i'm glad I did since I felt like I could have just ran through it no issues on the easier ones.

Now one thing that makes this game what it is, is the sound track. Dear god could they have not picked a better genre when slaying demons. The hard heavy metal in the background while you demolish demons is what makes this game what it is. Was never disspointed when it came to the music and i'm glad they did what they did with it.

OVERALL I got this game when it was $15 on sale and I am glad I did. I put 13 hours on my first playthrough and almost did all of the challenges, collectibles, runes, powerups, and secrets. I plan on going back and getting everything I can just so I can put as much into this game as I can. I may put another 2 or 3 hours into finishing everything making me spend $1 per hour which is A STEAL.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst let me say that I delayed buying this game for a while. The DOOM before this I found to be very unsatisfying and to me, boring. I used to love playing FPS's but since the old Duke Nukem 3d, Quake and Unreal I've lost interest in the genre as the games for some reason aren't able to hold me anymore. Painkiller got close but for some reason fell flat. Based on reviews I gave this a chance and I'm glad I did. The campain is amazingly long but doesn't feel drawn out or get boring. It has replay value! That by itself impressed me, that I actually want to play it through again! It brought back the fantastic feel and fun of Unreal Tournament. While the game has storyline the storyline isn't the core of the game. By that I mean that the story isn't so essential that replay is diminished by knowing the outcome. The story that is there is actually quite good from the get go and doesn't feel strained or bloated. How the managed to pull off this gem is quite surprising and all things considered, the graphics, size of the game etc. the price of the game is amazingly low. I know a lot of games that offer far less and charge far more. Well, what are you waiting for? Get your BFG and teach them who's boss!\t\t\t\t\t
