\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty fun.

Good: Music suprisingly. I mean I was expecting Doom, but I was blown away by the soundtrack. Most noticeably near the beggninng and end of the game. It really builds the atmosphere of the game. The pace of It crescendos with the frenzy of the fight, then cuts out when you blow away the last demon leaving this oasis of calm in the midst of the carnage. I'm not doing it justice, but it was awesome. Triple A game so everything's polished and nice. Olivia and Hayden make excellent villians, weapons are interesting and varied, fights are fun and engaging.

Bad: Shrug, It felt like the single player campaign dragged on a little in the middle. I liked it, and i'm enjoying the multiplyer side. 3/4 of the way through I just kinda wanted it to end. There were no new demons, weapons, same areas, it became kind of a slog to get closure to the story. The secrets too. I mean I full upgraded the sensor first to help and this is not to say it's impossible to find them all but I quickly got burnt out searching for where the heck that entrace to the etc is so I can reach this part of the map. Most were just annoying, and none felt like simple clever things to figure out, just a grind to keep retracing your steps. Oh the demon voice overs when you activate a lore tablet were kinda stupid. They used the stereoptypical bass rumble demon voice and it didn'twork at all. Didn't help the Demon's dialog was hokey too. Let me ammend that by saying I loved all the other voicework.

Final Impressions: Overall a great game. I bought it on launch cause I just like Doom, but otherwise i'd wait for it to drop down to $40. Great game, but I have to put my bias for it aside and say that despite all the polish if I wasn't a fan of the demon aesthethic I'd be dissappointed having paid 60 for this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a huge fan of the Doom and Quake games and loved this game. Lately I've avoided FPS games because of what I've been calling 'lack of innovation in the genre'. It took playing this game for me to realize what I've been missing from modern shooters. Chaos. It's been missing the chaos. The single player is the best single player experience I've had in an FPS since probably the original Doom. The story is not a super duper unique one but it is not a hindrance to the gameplay and it is pretty cool.

My biggest complaints are the fact that there is no out of the box: Free for all deathmatch mode in the multiplayer, this has been partially addressed with SnapMap as there are several enjoyable FFA maps people have already created, however SnapMap has it's own limitations.

In all honestly, my #1 complaint about this game is that it ended. The campaign is by no means short, it's actually quite long for a modern FPS (probably around the same time to complete as the original half life), but I wasn't ready for it to be over. I just want more because of how fun it was. I hate the general concept of DLC but if id releases any SP content via expansions or DLC I will buy it immediately.

Definitely pick this game up, the SP is refreshing and the MP is also very enjoyable and is somewhere between Arena Shooter and Modern Shooter.\t\t\t\t\t
