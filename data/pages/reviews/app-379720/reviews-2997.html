\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBetween DOOM and Wolfenstein: the New Order, Bethesda is showing how to take old IPs and update them to modern gaming without losing what made the original so good. Some might argue that their iterations in the Fallout franchise fall short in this regard (and I agree with that to a degree), they have not fallen into the trap that a lot of recent remakes/reboots have fallen into, such as EA's monstrosities that are Dungeon Keeper, Syndicate, etc.

Even if this wasn't DOOM, taking this game for what it is, it is probably the best FPS that has come out over the last half-decade. The gameplay is an effective update of the old-school run-and-gun shooters with large, open levels and puts games like CoD, Battlefield, and other modern shooters to shame.

As for multi-player, I dont care about that, and this game has such a strong single-player that even if you are into competitive multiplayer, this game is worth every cent.\t\t\t\t\t
