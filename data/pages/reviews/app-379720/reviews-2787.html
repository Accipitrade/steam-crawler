\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe multiplayer just straight up doesn't work. It gets caught up at 91% and after about 5 minutes of "loading," you're booted back to the main menu. Singleplayer's fine, but this is nowhere near $60. This game should cost $10 for the singleplayer and -$5 for the multiplayer. It's a nightmare to try to play. The level editor sucks, it's just pre-made level pieces you slap together and call it original. Imagine playing Minecraft, but instead of placing blocks, you can only place pre-made houses. But only 10, you can't go over like 10. That's the level editor in a nutshell. Multiplayer was kinda fun, for the one round I actually managed to get into. But if you really want the Doom multiplayer experience, go play Quake or Unreal Tournament. The singleplayer was fun and it has some awesome stuff in it, but make sure you only buy this game when it's worth the singleplayer to you, because the singleplayer's all you're getting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWas bound to happen that I dive into this. This trend of reboots in last few years has been hit or miss mostly. What developers are trying to do is capture the essence of old games while bringing them up to more modern standards. In some cases it works out, in others it doesn't, though it doesn't mean they don't have an entertaining game (see Shadow Warrior). While Doom might have not been the first, first person game it is still considered the grandaddy of shooters. Comparisons to the classic game (and certainly more recent Brutal Doom/Project Brutality) is inevitable but won't impact if this game is good or not.

Without beating around the bush I loved this game. I loved it so much that I think this is the best single player FPS game I played in god knows how long. Wolfenstein New Order was great so was Shadow Warrior, but this game was just phenomenal. It did so many things right, that the very minor missteps ended up mostly forgotten.

I played my first run on Nightmate and my fear was what every FPS developer seems to do. Make enemies bullet sponges that hit like a truck. That was not the case and I loved it. It had more of a sense how Brutal Doom does it. More spawns, more elite spawns, enemies are more agressive, AI changed and so forth...BUT they aren't bullet sponges. Which gives your guns a good sense of punch and doesn't change the pace of the action, relies mostly on your ability. That was frankly the biggest, most important thing in this game that I wanted. I wanted a sense of power from guns (and they are all awesome) while still getting a challange from mechanics and enemies that I don't have to shoot for half an hour (like something in Hard Reset hardest's difficulties, ugh).

Next was how guns were. In comparison to Brutal Doom (or better Project Brutality), the guns aren't as punchy, but you also don't kill quite that many enemies. Shotgun here is more confined to very close range, in comparison to one in BD where you can take a head off good ways away. Still this didn't bother me. All guns were great, they shifted little bit differently to what I expected them to be like (in expectations of how they stood in vanilla Doom). Plasma gun is more of a workhorse gun that can kill imps and possessed soldiers with ease but can't melt bigger enemies quickly. While the chaingun that was a more a workmanlike weapon in vanilla is pure carnage in this game (similar to BD version). Rockets are still awesome, BFG is still be all end all. Super Shotgun is a freaking monster. New Heavy Machine gun is a more versitle moddable weapon that can fit into many roles, and the gauss cannon is just one of those guns you can't stop smiling when you fire.

To balance out the awesomness (one shot nature) of chainsaw and BFG they made them special. They have a very limited ammo capacity (BFG is 3, chainsaw up to 7). Chainsaw also uses up to 5 ammo to kill some bigger enemies, making it more valued resource to when you want to use it, that you can't abuse them. Overall guns are fun as hell, and combined that enemy health isn't bullet spongey while still retaining difficulty should tell you everything you really need to know about actuall gunplay.

Movement is slower than older games (vanila, BD etc). But I wouldn't call it slow. In comparison to other shooters this is much more faster paced and with size of areas and maps I found it perfectly fine, with the tight and satisfying gunplay it fits and I have zero complaints.

The next big thing is level design. No I don't think it matches up to something like Hexen (which is still baffling how good it is to this day), but their levels aren't straight forward. You can turn off hints and highlights. I advise you to do so. The puzzle nature of it is still there, the levels are more vertical, but still large enough for player to explore, look around, figure stuff out. While it is fairly simple to clear the actual level, finding the many secrets can get involved. Secrets are how you expect them to be, they give extra resources, collectibles and they can get you guns early.

There is a progression system in the game, where you upgrade your weapons, runes and armor. I didn't originally like this at first, but thinking about it, it gives a far better sense of power progression as you go on. In original Doom you were stripped of guns between episodes, and you had to start over, here there is none of that, so they had to do something to constantly escelate the conflict. It fits well and you will be able to tell how crazy powerful you become by the end. The only thing I don't think is well balanced is "rich get richer" rune. Even on nightmare, as soon as you break the armor breakpoint you turn into a god. Mobile turret chaingun or Siege mode gauss cannon with infinite ammo and you can trivialize damn near anything. Normally guns like that are balanced by their crazy ammo consumption so you couldn't just murder everything with it, but that rune sort of makes you a bit too strong. Regardless you can always ignore the rune or slot something else.

The actual campaign is refreshing. It's the whole no nonsense, take no ♥♥♥♥ sort of approach to the game. Your protagonist doesn't speak, is there to kill ♥♥♥♥ and to screw the rest. The story is simple but it wasn't what made it enjoyable to me. The badass nature of Doom Slayer and how you just jump head first into ♥♥♥♥ with rage and death is what sold it. These people try to talk to you of the big picture, philosophies, problems, whatever. Your guy doesn't give a ♥♥♥♥. He just has the "kill them all" mantra in his head and that is that. From start of the campaign to the finish, it is blood fueled, adrenaline pumping gorefest. It is satisfying from start to finish. No hitch in pacing, no absurdly long luls, no turret sections, no gimmicks. Hop in and kick ass.

So what did the game do wrong? Not that much. The biggest thing I can think of is the checkpoint system. The game doesn't support manual saves and that is something that irks me but I can live with it. Checkpoints are generally right before fights and right after fights, letting you take a breather. Also if you find a secret and get collectible item you don't have to do it again if you got sent back checkpoint before you did it, however you will have the same ammo, HP and armor you had at the time of the checkpoint so might need to collect that again. The other thing were the challanges. On one hand none of them took me more than few minutes and maybe 2-3 tries. On the other hand it sucked me out of the level to do an optional thing to get a passive skill. Not a huge deal, but could have been better without it.

Yeah I'm gushing a lot over this game, and personally this is what I wanted out of it. Now you see how I didn't mention the multiplayer? Well I won't go there, since the word ♥♥♥♥, doesn't begin to describe my dissapointment, I could probably write an essay of what went wrong there, but I won't. The singleplayer is just phenomenal, one of rare few SP shooters that makes me want to jump right back from start after I finished. I loved it, through and through and truly hope they create more content for it (SP content), or their future games follow in this direction. Godspeed.\t\t\t\t\t
