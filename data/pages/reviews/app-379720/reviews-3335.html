\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSingle Player: One thing I wish the single player did better was retain some of the horror that Doom 3 established and up the difficulty. My one playthrough(I don't think it's worth doing a second) was on the hardest available difficulty and that felt rather easy. I didn't die once against these bosses and held on to my chainsaw and BFG ammo until about the final level where I realized there's an abundance of special ammo. I always thought "I'm going to need this ammo for when ♥♥♥♥ hits the fan" but much like the baddass your character is made out to be I basically punched my through an army and then beat them to death with their own limbs. Weapons each have 2 upgrade paths usually with one clearly better option but it provides a nice little extra when findng secrets. Although most of the weapons are fun I really question why there is a need for what is possible one of the most whimpy pistols I have ever used, I wish I could just throw the useless thing away. Story was meh, not that I expected anything more but like I said earlier I wish there was more horror. A few more enemy types would be welcome too. There's around 12 and 4 of them are more or less the same and one of them can hardly be called an enemy as they die in a single shot by just about everything and walk at the pace of a snail.

Multiplayer : Nothing special here. Loadouts of 2 weapons which you have to unlock by leveling half of which I think are ♥♥♥♥ and boring. Demons are just flat out OP, I killed 5 people with one right click as the Baron. They're hard to kill and insta-kill anyone they can catch(which isn't hard). Weapons feel largely unsatisfying. This so called "super shotgun" can't even hold a candle to the Halo:reach shotgun. In a game that prides itself on being the *father of FPS* I think this is a major problem. There's a rather random almost perk system where you are randomly given limited amounts of various buffs. Like spawn with extra armor or she the cooldown of item respawns. These if anything just add more time before you can get back in and shoot things because it forces you to choose one or choose none before you can respawn. Snap map is awesome.

TLDR Single player was fun but ended abruptly and was all run and gun with no horror. Multiplayer more or less feels clunky and lame but snap map is one of the best map editors I have ever seen. If you want fast paced shooting and ripping jaws off then there's a lot of that. But if you want more you won't find it here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright here is my review of this game.

Pros
-Singleplayer overall is really well done
-Fast paced and lots of action
-Action starts within in the first five minutes
-One of the few games to make me play the story nonstop
-I really liked the snapmap feature. Some of the custom maps and sutff you see in there are good.
-One of the campaigns I could come back to just to play it again
-You can learn how to use snapmap pretty fast

Neutral Things
-The campagin took me around 7 hours to complete so its a decent length i suppose
-The nightmare difficulty thing with making your game restart to the beginning if you die is a cool idea but i dont see myself ever doing that simply because I would not have the patience to restart multiple times

Cons
-The multiplayers seemed kinda slow to me, its still a little fast paced but singleplayer felt more fast paced to me
-This is just my opinion but i personally dont like the loadout system but I can manage with it

Singleplayer - 9/10 why? Just an overall great campagin. As I said its one of those campagins I would consider playing again.
Multiplayer - 7/10 why? For me theres a lot of upsides and downsides to the multiplayer. I am including the snapmap feature to this, so this is why it gets a 7.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original doom was the second big fps, wolf3d started it all, and wolf3d was about "shooting your way through rooms with treasures, dogfood and nazi soldiers to reach that top of the building where you meet hitler" Doom was about "reaching the mouth of hell while you go through reasonably explorable level full of demon posessed zombie soldiers, imps, demons and cyberdemos while you explore one abandoned mars installation after the other". There was not really a story.

So it's not very hard to say why for me, this version of DOOM is the definite sequel, or remake, or re-imagining (whatever you want to call it) of the early 90's classic. You see doom 3 (2004) tried to suck you in too much with story and linear narrative, and conspiracy theories and story happening around you, and ironicly it tried to be too much "horror". It also suffered from trying to copy gimicks that worked in half life 2 but felt "copied" and bland in doom. It tried to go away from the classic by bringing in modern (at the time) gameplay that deviatd from run, strafe, crowd control and shoot. That's why in retrospect, I was never incredibly happy with it. The guns felt too clunky and plastic, and it was too impact less. Doom 3 was more quake 2 in a sense that quake 2 DID have more story to go for it and that's fine.

The new DOOM is more brutal, more clean and more fecious at the same time, the way the weapon pack punch to the enemies, the addictive glory kills that make you maticulously dismember enemies, make it a very classic experience with a special modern twist.

The leveldesign doesn't hold your hand for 100% (just like in the classic, there were small branches and spaces to roam and get lost in whithin the level), and the story is in the background. The pacing of gameplay varries from that typical calm haunting moment where you look for security cards while you hear critters in the background, only to suddenly ramp up and and barely make it alive by "glory killing" that last enemy that is about to punch or fireball your remaining health off. That makes me feel like I'm back in the 90s. The game lets you make your own story about what Doom is about; that it's the only thing you are worried about, you as a nameless marine with his praetor suit in an abandoned base on mars, no less, no more: that's that is what DOOM is all about, and successfully brings back and makes better!

There is progress in this game but it's simple and straight forward. The suit and weapon upgrades are granted to you when fighting hordes of enemies. You get suit empowerment globes and weapon upgrade points, you can spend them at your disgression on more armor, more health, specific weapon upgrades for your energy rifle, shotgun, rocket launcher etc...

The in world pickups like green armor replenishment and small armor nuggets, as well as the classic health packs and cupcakes, make a return. And just like in the old doom. Temporary enhancements also make a return such as berzeker mode where you fistbump the enemy to a squishy death, but also powerups that are adopted from quake make an entry, so you can find haste and quad damage powerups.

Doom is simple and satisfying in it's modern execution, did I mention how satisfying it is to use the chainsaw ? Together with glory kills, it's one of these landmark doom essentials that made both the classic and the modern version so typically enjoyable. And as it adds allot to the overall gameplayto a point that it becomes a worthwhile satisfying mechanic on it's own, it becomes something you don't get tired of easily: DOOM certainly is a game you can replay, and it offers that extra value for that. Because you can findsecret "classic doom levels", and because you can stumble on runes that let you do challanges to receive extra passive perks for your characters. In level 3 there's a challange you can do by killing 15 imps with your shotgun in order to receive a perk that lets you suck in world items from further away as you pass them by. Thoe perks are purely optional but help a bit in roaming the levels.

The soundtrack is also a great throwback at 90's industrial/ambient type O negative / nine inch nails galore. And what makes it so amusing is that the music interacts, stutters and breaks while the pacing of the game changes and accentuates the arrangements as youperform glory kills. That really acknowledges your actions in a badass way.

It could be that when you are a modern player who is used to the sophisticaction of the division, call of duty, even dead space, and other modern first person shooters, that doom might be a bit too simple because there's no story. But for those nostalgic 40+ year olds like me, it becomes a throwback, and how more accuratly they can represent the original, the stronger the nostalgic feeling will get, bringing the cycle of our youth anew... just like the marine's new cycle of violent creature tearing on the surface of mars!

I don't review the multiplayer because I never play it.\t\t\t\t\t

After playing a few hours of the single player campaign I was in love again. It's the most fun I've had playing a FPS in years. This game runs at 1080p and around 60-70fps. It's on a weird mix of medium to ultra settings (I left it at AMDs auto-detected settings) - it's one of the best looking games I've played - up there with Witcher 3.

I can't say anything about the multiplayer (I hear it's really bad), don't let this put you off! The single player so far is worth the £39.99 by itself. Here's hoping for lot of community built maps on Snapmap.
