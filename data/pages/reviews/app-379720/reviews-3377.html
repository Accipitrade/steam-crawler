\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, so I just beat the single player. 14hrs, easy mode (because im a wuss, I know) and I did not actively persue collectables/side objectives. I am recommending this game, even though by the end of the single player I very very very much wanted the damn game to be over with.

Why?

Because the single player is literally an arena where you kill everything, followed by a brief bit of exploration, followed by another arena where you kill everything. Sometimes 2 or 3 arenas inbetween each explorable segment. Bethesda has designed some amazing arenas for this game but made literally no effort at challenging you gameplay wise in single player. Each arena simply has more of the demons you've already seen in steadily larger and larger groups making them steadily more and more annoying to deal with. Got a full rack of armor? Pfft, you'll be back down to health the first time that mob of 15 demons sacks you in a corner. The last four stages were literally just arena fests with no actual exploration areas.

Now, there is story here. Quite a bit of story infact so long as you listen to the narrative npcs throughout and take a look at your pda from time to time. I actually enjoyed the story, explains a whole lot I never felt 'thats stupid' cross my lips during it. Most of the story comes to light via stones in hell where a demon tells you things.

Gameplay wise this is a lot of fun! Fighting the demons, using the right guns, using the arena enviroments, fantastic. Upgrading weapons impacted them nicely and felt rewarding. Same for your armor and your own upgrades though as stated two paragraphs up, upgrading your armor didn't feel as rewarding mostly because the damn demons took it from you at every engagement heh.

My only wish was that they hadn't gone the 'throw more demons at you' route towards the end. Felt a whole lot like Dead Space 3 which did the same exact thing. Larger and larger groups of demons. That dosent make the game more challenging bethesda, it just makes it inconvienent :)

Mmmyes

DOOM Eternal review here. - NOT recommended

I don't know what this is, but it is not Doom.

If you just want a regular fps to pew pew some baddies, then buy it and read no further, this is a good game.

If you absolutely love DOOM and think that developers shouldn't sacrifice quality, then keep reading.

Since the franchise somewhat evolved in the hiatus and older games are subject to glorified past googles, I'll try to compare it with Doom 2016, which is the latest installment.

About Doom Eternal:
1. First and foremost, it shoves a story on your face, tries to give Doomguy a past and lore, makes it about some greater objective other than "you wake up and start shooting". Id's story franchise is supposed to be Wolfenstein, Doom is where you just don't care and want to shoot things.

2. Childish. This doom compares poorly in immersion, the graphics are a downgrade from 2016 and look a bit cartoony, add to that some weird toy like sound effects mid combat and you have not a "I'm fighting demons and this is all very serious" atmosphere, but a "look I popped this cacodemon eye out and it made a POP". Glory killing cacodemons breaks every bit of immersion you might have.

3. Platforming. This one took me a while to figure out. At certain sections of the game you have to use monkey bars, ladder-walls and dash jumping to pass difficult terrain. While platforming is fun for some, it feels weird in a Doom game, where you usually run from arena to arena while shooting everything in between. Some "puzzles" require you to stop and analyze how and where to jump to and from to progress. This interval cuts your momentum and greatly reduces the whole "earth is on fire" situation.

4. Melee. Not that melee is a staple of the series, but punching feels extremely weak. Even lowly zombies can't be killed by punching and just end up slap fighting you while you try to kill them, which brings us to...

5. Low Ammo. This is controversial. Personally I always switch guns when ammo runs out so I don't usually have a problem, but this is a valid complaint, ammo runs out CONSTANTLY even if you are using the chainsaw, which you should. This also breaks momentum as you often have to run around waiting for the chainsaw to reload so you can keep your killing streak.

6. Pickups, tutorials and in-game items in general. They all feel "gamey". If you've played 2016, which you should, then you've seen immersion. There are little to no tutorials, everything is intuitive and items are either on the ground, on corpses or terminals, blended in the background. In Eternal EVERYTHING GLOWS which is very weird. You don't pick this ammo that a soldier couldn't use because he died, you pick the glowy thing. You don't repurpose the plasma gun a defender still had on his cold dead hands, you pick up the spinny glowy thing that is there for no reason. This is true for all pickups, even data logs are just weird papirus floating around and glowing. Everything is constantly reminding you that you are on a game.

HOW IT COMPARES WITH 2016

Overall it feels like whomever developed this game ignored all that made 2016 great. It feels like it was made by a different team, that didn't like 2016. It feels like it's a spin-off made for a different target audience than 2016 was made for.
2016 felt very serious, very dark and very tense. Eternal feels teen, it play like some game with glowy neon to make your brain want more play. It feels like it was made to sell, not to be good.\t\t\t\t\t

DOOM is one of the franchises that never die. Is this a good Sequel or does it suck?

PROS:

- Unforgiving Enemies on higher difficulties
- Decent Graphics
- Glorious Kills of Glory
- Awesome Multiplayer
- Awesome Death Animations
- Upgrading System that doesn't feel Overpowered
- Easy-to-Use Map Creator
- Chainsaw (Because Reasons)

CONS:

- No local Co-op (Splitscreen)
- Mouse is too "smooth" (You can disable it. There are Guides on Steam that tell you how to do it)
- If you're not into this type of game, the campaign can get repetitive.
- Color Scheme of Demons etc. is very alike.

Overall: I like the game a lot. It's brutal, fun and makes you feel like a total badass. Would I recommend the game? Yes. If you like Singleplayer Campaigns, youll have fun with this one. The Nightmare difficulties are extremely challenging and unforgiving. The Multiplayer is a slower version of Quake: Arena, but definetly not less fun. You have a decent amount of Maps and Modes to keep it interesting and fun. Snapmap is very easy to use. It can be complicated at the start but that won't last long. And don't forget Rule #1: Never. Stop. Moving.

I'll give Doom a 9/10 at its current state.\t\t\t\t\t
