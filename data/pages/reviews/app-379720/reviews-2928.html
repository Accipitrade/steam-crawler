\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only played the single-player campaign, but it was brilliant. It felt like DOOM and DOOM 2, with many of my favourite demons and weapons looking and sounding amazing. The maps were well-designed - large fighting arenas interspersed between exploration sections, with plenty of items to collect and upgrade, and plenty of secrets (and even more secretive secrets) to find. My only gripes were that sometimes I couldn't work out how to proceed, and I often forgot about my support equipment in the middle of a fight - I hardly ever remembered my chainsaw, my grenades or my BFG. Overall, a brilliant back-to-basics first-person shooter, where your defence is keeping on the move rather than hiding behind cover (although you can do that too, if you really want, but a demon will almost certainly find you and bite your butt).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom da doom doom DOOM!

Everything I wanted from a Doom game, just about anyway. What's missing is the hectic nature of corridor shooting where enemies like at every turn, running into rooms, activating hordes and running out as you trigger the entire level and en up in a giant frag mess. Well, it could be done with modern gaming if everyone owned a super rig.

The flow for those who don't know, is, enter>arena gets activated>kill all the baddies and the gate opens. That's it. Does it get old? A little, is it fun? Oh Yes!

Everything here is done well..ish. Enemies aren't terrifying enough in my opinion. The revenant was a real menace in Doom 2, but how just has a bigger bark than it's bite. I found a few tactics that got me through every fight just fine so it did take some of the fun out, but overall a win in every other regard not mentioned.

Well, guess it's time to try Ultra-Nightmare, and stop being a massive casual.\t\t\t\t\t
