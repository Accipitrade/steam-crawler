\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith the doom plot, there is little room for creativity in regards to scenery. That being said I do have to say that the creators efforts towards the aesthetic is something that is felt as the game progresses. However, the beginning was a bit lackluster. All throughout there is a lot of landmark repetition in the layers that you are interacting with which makes this game much harder to navigate. I find myself getting lost frequently. There is a bitter sweet feeling when it comes to finding secrets because when you finally see the markers directing you towards an objective you feel elated thinking that you are on the right path and then "secret found" pops up and you suddenly realize that you are, in fact, so far from an objective that you've found a secret.
I feel that perhaps the creators must've really took to heart any criticisms about the story line in Doom 3 and they seem to have just scrapped the idea of having a story line completely. 99% of this game is just killing demons. It's very much akin to the 1994 games with modern graphics. I do wish that there was more to interact with. While story line in games can get sort of drawn out, I appreciate when games use it as a jumping point for mixed objectives just to keep the game play exciting and less predictable. But rather than doing that, the game seemingly tries to be spontaneous by going old school and just tossing in new enimes at random. An enticing as that may sound it's actually rather disappointing. None of the demons seem to serve a purpose or location and their introductions if any at all, are nothing more than a slow motion 2 second zoom in. All in all I would say that the game is very black and white. By all means it is a huge shift from Doom 3.
And they probably should have given a name that is easier to differentiate as I often found myself installing the wrong version and I even completely forgot I had the 2016 version in my library because I thought it was just regular 1994 doom.
All in all I do appreciate the effort put forth I do love a lot of the details in some scenes. The way that Mars looked in the sky when I entered mission 6 "Kadingir sanctum" had me awestruck. I was glad the game gave me a second to appreciate it from a few angles before making me jump back into the ol' rip and tear. Possibly my favorite part of this game is the music. There's truly nothing like knowing what point you are at in a demon battle based on what kind of heavy metal you are hearing. Although, when you can't find an objective and the same wandering music keeps playing it does make you want to take a hammer to your speakers.
One final criticism I have that has caused me to rage quit countless times on this game is the fall damage. Yes, you do have get the perfect timing on your jumps from ledge to ledge and while that can be exciting at times, it is also very true to the nature in much of the 1994 Doom. And it can be thrilling at times especially when you get it down perfectly. But the fact that falling is a near constant threat throughout the game play does get aggravating. Instead of adding any unique ways to challenge the player you are faced with the challenge of not dying from slipping on a ledge throughout 90% of the game.
As much as I hated things like the flashlight horror game route that Doom 3 took, it would be nice to have some interaction or unique challenges in this game. Different tools to work with for certain challenges would make things a lot more intriguing. I'd like to see a good balance of Doom 3 and Doom 2016. Something akin to wolfenstein "the new order". I do like the lever activated easter eggs that occur throughout the game but again, it gets a little repetitive. And you really can't call them "easter eggs" in all fairness. When we think about what makes something a true easter egg, it's that rare scarcity that makes the experience so unique. In wolfenstein "the new order" we experience a genuine easter egg similar to the lever activated throw back to Doom 1994, our character enters a dream where we are taken back to play 90s wolfenstein until we die at which point the character wakes up from his dream making it a one of a kind experience. And while I enjoy going back to play the 90s version of doom by pulling levers perhaps my criticism ends with just the lack of variety.
My only disappointment is that I feel this game had a great deal of potential to do so much more and become something that isn't just special to those in the fandom. I still recommend playing this game and of course I still think that this is by far the best doom since 1994.\t\t\t\t\t
