\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 is a simple yet very effective game that succeeds in becoming one of the best power trips i know of. It succeeds in making you feel like the ultimate badass through immensely satisfying gameplay and its absolute masterpiece in sound design.

Going over the gameplay, The combat loop is near perfect. Your base movement speed is much higher than in most other games. Coupled with your Ten different weapons you have a recipe for minus one demonic invasion. The game encourages you to be as aggresive as possible by adding glory kills, which are brief, satisfying, optional quicktime events that reward you with instant health. The result is an insane combat loop that throws you into a Dance-trance as you weave through the horde gunning, blasting, smacking or melting anything in your way.

Alone this would be a decent game, but what absolutely sells this is the sound design and reactive soundtrack. The former comes in the form of weapon and enemy sounds, all of which have weight to them and make them feel powerful. The later, is one i'd recommend experiencing for yourself. Effectively, through utilizing different riffs and independent transitions, the soundtrack reacts to what you do in game, using glory kills and specific cases in combat to change and adapt the track currently playing, its quite cool once you notice it.
