\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn ID game is always technically excellent; the models, animations, environments and effects are always top quality. The framerate is rock solid. The gameplay footage is designed to be super impressive and dramatic and actually playing it is much the same. However I found the game quite difficult. The pace of this game may be suited to a young kid on five cups of coffee rather than an old dude.

Every enemy moves massively fast and everything is attacking you at once. I am not sure why scoped weapon mods are even offered as you rarely have a chance for sniping. Control-wise you really cannot afford to remove a finger from a button to press another one, there's just no time. You pretty much need to have every action required happening at once. This will take much practice.

The imps inevitably get UNDER my character and I often miss at point blank range. Apparently I'm so bad that easy mode still doesn't feel all that easy. Running out of ammo is common and although the chainsaw is great, most of the time it doesn't have gas. I wish it had a timed cooldown rather than being out of gas. Yeah, I know this sounds pathetic because you guys are all super skilled. I am doing better on my second try, though.

The other problem is that I got lost a lot. Many minutes were spent wandering around empty areas having no idea how to get where I need to go. This happened on nearly every level. My lack of skill in combat can be blamed on me, but I think the level design bears some responsibility for being confusing. I guess I have to tackle the map as well.

It's possible that if you're old enough to remember playing the original Doom back in the days of CRT monitors, modems that made noises and floppy disks you may be too elderly to keep up with this lightning-paced, frenetic battle. Or maybe I just suck, but it's a nice looking game at any rate.\t\t\t\t\t

The visuals, the gameplay, the length, and (Especially) the music all feel just right. Put it this way: before this, I thought I would never enjoy any sort of FPS whose name didn't start with M and rhymed with "pet droid time", and this game basically flipped that ideology on its head. Even at full price (which was what I paid for it on PS4 back in the day (yeah,I have two copies...)), I still felt like I got my money's worth out of it, and at the price it's at now, I'd strongly suggest giving it a try if you haven't already.

Now let's just hope that Doom Eternal, when/if it comes out, can hold true to this game's good intentions.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a majority of my playtime with this (I've done two playthroughs from the ground up on Normal), this honestly does live up to the hype of being one one of the best first person shooters in years. The rhythms of combat in this are unbelievably smooth, almost to the point it resembles a ballet of gore and viscera more than anything else, with top shelf level design, perfectly balanced enemy difficulty and spectacular visuals. It's a shame then, considering that the game completely craps out at the end with a hamfisted cliffhanger that feels like it undermines the effort you put into the game. Not having a proper NG+ isn't a plus in its favor, either.

Do I recommend the game? Absolutely, without a doubt. But I do feel like this is a caveat one should know about if you're picking up the game for the first time. Hopefully, DOOM Eternal won't make the same mistake twice if it manages to ship out this year.\t\t\t\t\t
