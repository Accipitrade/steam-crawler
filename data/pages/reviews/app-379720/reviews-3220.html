\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe single player experince is mostly done right; fast paced, run and gun action that's very satifactory. Every other review under the sun will tell you that, though. I wish to address the major faults this game has because I don't think enough people are mentioning them.

1) The multiplayer is trash. It just is...Blantant Halo ripoff complete with teabagging, spammy weapons, cheesy taunts, and generic tech-armor. Not to mention it doesn't try to balance itself. I recently played a match where every single player on my team was below level 20 whereas every single player on the enemie team was over level 40. The game uses a loadout system akin to cancerous games like COD as oppesed to starting everyone out with a useless pistol and making them find weapons around the map; like an arena shooter should. Instead of having powerful weapons and teaching players to dodge projectiles, the game gives you weak weapons that have to be spammed over and over again until you or your oppenent finally dies. Only in DOOM's multiplayer can you sustain 5+ rockets directly at your feet and still live. It's a system that doesn't promote good aim.
2) "Snapmap" Doesn't replace true mod support: Snapmap is a cookie-cutter editor that's easyy to use, but that ease of use comes at a great price... You can't build the Grand Canyon out of Legos, and you can't build unique, fully-custom maps and gamemodes if you don't have access to powerful modding tools like Valve's Hammer Editor for the Source Engine that let you fully customize the geometry, textures, models, sounds etc. of your maps. The original DOOM games were famous for their modability, but Snapmap isn't going to allow for the same level of customization.

TL;DR: Buy this game for the single player when you can do so cheaply. Unless some actual mods spring up for this game, the single player story (which is about 10 hours long) is all you're getting for $60.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am love/hate with this game mainly because I hate seeing Doom at its most corporate. This game is a product. It was made to sell. Eternal shed a lot of that, and has more creative freedom. It's like a Doom game created by someone who's only ever had Doom explained to them. Doom 2016 is where Doom (and id) sold out - it's the Force Awakens of Doom games and it shows. This is a game made by teams with meetings, not friends bonding over media like before. As a reaction to FPS games during 2016, this was certainly a big deal when it hit, and is pretty much responsible for the boomer shooter revival. Credit where credit is due. It's a game, no microtransactions or any ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, it's just a game, and that was SO refreshing coming from a AAA dev.

It's a simpler and easier game than the superior Doom Eternal, but also a bit buggy with some questionable design, so it's not really a joke when I say that this feels more like an Eternal prototype or early beta. The object physics are incredibly janky to the point of me needing to ask "how did this get past id's historically tough QA and patches?" When the game is working well it's smooth and a good time, even if the meta boils down to using the SG/SSG 90% of the time, else Gauss Cannon/your best long range gun and nothing else. You can ignore two thirds of the weapons in this game and be better off for it partly because of the way that the upgrade system works. Due to the simplicity of the meta and occasional weird bug that usually involves clipping, this is the easiest yet most annoying official Doom game ever made.

Once you find the sweet spot in the meta, that means that you then get over the presentation and the music, and it almost becomes... boring... as much as I hate to say it, but the journey towards that boredom was pretty fun, I must admit. And it gives context for Eternal, which is one of the best FPS games ever made and easily the best of the current era.

My main issue with the game is that it strays too far from what I consider Doom to be in terms of atmosphere and that it isn't nearly strategic enough in the combat even compared to the original games. There's few quiet sections or navigational challenges, and the main crutch of the game is the combat to the point of it almost never letting up except for unskippable cutscenes. Those cutscenes are in the most irritating levels to 100% too. You cannot get lost and the automap is there to tell you where "secrets" are rather than for finding your way.

The engine is essentially a souped-up id Tech 5, or Rage engine, and some of the technical problems from Rage have migrated over. Texture-pop is a significant problem when using older or potato hardware, but it's still a thing even with my 2070S. It also means that the download is massive as the texture files are so large. However, the benefits of the Rage engine are also present in that it looks amazing at a distance, (although like **** up close) and is very well optimised even for lower end hardware, especially after the patches.

The multiplayer is okay but not amazing. There's basically no anticheat at all so it can be hard to find a real game, but I'm not sure it's worth getting into anyway.

I am aware that this reads like a negative review, but there is an honestly a very good 8.5/10 game here. I live and breathe Doom and this game is excellent at making me smile, even if I did put more hours into Eternal in four weeks than I did into 2016 in four years. It's a great game, but a mediocre Doom game more along the lines of Serious Sam or Painkiller. Doom Eternal fixed 90% of the issues that I have with this game.

No real modding and no quicksaving is criminal in a Doom game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf it weren't for the fantastic single player, I wouldn't be even recommending this.

Doom's Singleplayer is a must-have for anyone looking for a new, dumb shooter where you stomp in Demon skulls and blow up everything with a pulse. It keeps the original charm from Doom 1 and 2, fast paced action mixed in with the constant threat of Demon's plugging up your rectum. It's fast, it's fun, and I just wish it was in a seperate package instead of being paired with the braindead multiplayer.

I've heard people call DOOM's new Multiplayer "Remenescant of Quake and a bit of Halo". These are all wrong. The multiplayer is a manchild's paradise, giving you "Hack modules" which let you spawn with certain tweaks so you can see through walls, or you get more experience when killing someone. The addition of overpowered Demons to the multiplayer make the game unenjoyable, as everyone immediately rushes to grab the Demon rune once it spawns (Everyone can see when it spawns thanks to a countdown timer that shows up on your HUD. Gone are the days of remembering specific spawn times- The game dumbs it down for you!)
Let's also not forget taunts. Yes. Taunts. Why are these taunts here? Who knows. These Taunts are similar to games like TF2 or Overwatch where it gives you a five-ten second animation of being a huge prick after a kill. In a game about being fast and quick, a lot of people sure do enjoy spending extra time performing their "HAHA I KILD U!" animation.
Loadouts are another issue. In Doom 1-3, everyone started with equal spawns. You ALL start with a pistol (lest the game mode called for it). Now you can shove a Rocket launcher and a Super Shotgun on your character and be set from square one.

Snapmap is another feature I was looking forward to. Needless to say, I'm not very thrilled about it anymore.
The game supplies you with pre-made areas that you can adjust SLIGHTLY to your liking. It's boring, unintuitive and leaves out a lot of creativity that the DOOM community was known for.

I'm still recommending it, even if it is just for the campaign. They really should've just looked at that and said "How can we make this awesome in multiplayer?" instead of looking at games like Black Ops 3, Overwatch and Halo for their setting. It's boring. Everyone looks like "AN HALO" and the entire experience is muddied and just not fun to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is absolutely amazing, and by far exceeded all expectations for the remake (I grew up playing the original Doom and Quake series).

This is how you always imagined Doom (and even better).

Player movement and glory kills are super fast and enemies have the same speed and power as you do (many enemies can kill you with a single hit).

The campaign is really challenging (in UltraViolence) and at the same time so addictive. Environments and monsters are jaw-dropping, and if you complete the game wanting for more (and I guarantee you will), you can create you own maps with SnapMap and play it with friends.

Last but not least, the multiplayer mode is *absolutely perfect*. I really love every design decision they made with this mode.

Score:

Graphics 10/10
Audio/Music 9/10
Multiplayer 10/10
SnapMap 10/10

My final score for Doom is 10/10: a legenday sequel for a legendary title.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI need to open with the fact that I am not normally a fan of FPS games. I am more of a Witcher, ES, Forgotten Realms kind of guy. With that being said however this is some of the best fun I have had with a game in a long time. The combat is fast paced, the weapons feel great and the lore is rich. On the subject of the Lore, id did a great thing when they chose to not force it down your throat. For someone like myself who is a natural collector I love that there is so much to find.

I have seen complaints about crashes and a dev mode bug. The game has not crashed on me once and so far (crosses fingers) no dev mode bug for me. The game also performs beautifully on my machine. My specs for reference: CPU: i5 4590, 8GB DDR3 1600 RAM and GTX 960 OC edition 2GB VRAM.

The multiplayer is meh but I still enjoyed it and the snap map adds a lot to the game. That being said the strength of this game is is SP mode. People have also claimed that the SP mode is short...I have over 20 hours so far and I only now fought the first boss. I would not call that short, but maybe my expectations are different to other peoples. The solo mode is still significantly longer and more well thought out than any COD :p (yes, shade, I threw some shade).

Should you definitely, absolutely buy this game? I don't know, that is up to you. All I know is I have not been disappointed. *drops mic to goes and rek demon faces*\t\t\t\t\t
