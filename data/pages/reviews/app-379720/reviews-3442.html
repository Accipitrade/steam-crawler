\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is Doom! Keys, automap, health packs, and secrets!

It really captures the feels of the original Doom. Granted I would have liked to have seen a bit more of the elemtnes seen in Doom 3: some small puzzles, and some more complicated maps with a bit more to do. Not just go in kill everyting and move on. Doom 4 is about as true to the originals as we could hope for.

[rant]The "follow the green lights" addition to the doom series is not welcome and doesnt do it any favours, we already have an objective marker and a map to figure out where we're going. The game would have been better without it, or at least have the option to turn them off. Holding the players hand and leading them everywhere in games doesn't help gameplay. [/rant]

I had a few bugs along the way, there were a few weird looped sound glitches and
the last boss didn't die when it ran out of health, it just kept crawling around without attacking. So I had to repeat that section. Which is unfortunate since it is literally the end of the game and really took away from the satisfaction of beating it.

Snap map is okay. It is great for building a simple generic map quickly. There's a bit more control and potential than the simple Portal 2 editor but it feels like it still falls a bit short. Being confined to pre-determined map sections that snap together get's old pretty quickly. The pieces are not terribly different from one or another. As someone who as spent countless hours in making maps in Doom Builder or even the Cryengine sandbox, Snap Map doesnt have the same staying power.\t\t\t\t\t

The AI seems to, on one hand, know too much (like knowing exactly where you are, all the time) and on the other hand be somewhat predictable, but the enemies - even the early ones - are a good challenge nevertheless.
But, it has to be said again - the game IS fun. After the first hour, a solid 8.5 or 9/10 at least.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe single player campaign of Doom has exceeded all of my expectations I initially had toward it. My expectations were that I wanted it to be a great FPS game, and more importantly, a true DOOM title. I started the game on Nightmare difficulty right off the bat.

You will be fighting in the first 10 seconds of the game. Combat is challenging and very fluid with emphasis on speed, which they've certainly cranked up. Stopping is death on nightmare. Glory kills are satisfying, can be a ammo saver, and really don't hinder game flow at all, and can even be upgraded to perform faster (or toggled off in the options).

Level design is absolutely impressive. This was my biggest concern with Doom, and rest assured id FINALLY did it right. Non-linear, lots of exploration, plenty of secrets with hidden guns, secret level unlocks, and equipment.

The monsters are relentless. On nightmare you will get your ass kicked. This is a fact, and there's no way to circumvent this. I honestly felt at times that I was playing Project Brutality on "Last Man on Earth" difficulty. Monsters boast a variety of attacks, chase you down, flank you, and can be tough as nails (sometimes a little too good).

Weapons look and feel satisfying, great attention to detail. You will use guns interchangeably, each has their respective uses. You'll have to search the environment for health and ammo, aside from the chainsaw kills on enemies, they drop little items- but enough to keep you going (i.e +5 health occasionaly or like 4 shotgun shells). The BFG literally clears an entire room in one shot, and I couldn't help but smile at the results. Upgrades and progression in this game are entirely optional, but really help shift the game to fit how you play (the air control ability is the best imo).

The graphics are incredible too, although you better have a strong card if you want to play on Ultra with anti-aliasing, etc. Uncapping framerate, setting your FOV to 130, tons of options to customize.

And I have to say, Mick Gordon's soundtrack in this game is some of the best I've heard in a while. On the third level, I was literally head banging and rocking out to a soundtrack that felt like a crossbreed between Lamb of God, Celldweller, and Rage Against the Machine.

Overall, I can confidently say that this is the Doom game we've wanted. We all can breath a sigh of relief now that the main portion of the game is actually well-done. That beta we played is absolutely no indicator of how the campaign plays in the slightest, and id did it right with this one. Definitely recommend this title as a gamer, a die hard Doom fan (still play the classic to this day), and FPS fanatic.

EDIT: Since some of you do not take the time to actually explore the game's options and like to jump to conclusions, you can in fact start the game on nightmare. Ultra-nightmare is unlocked after beating the game, NOT regular nightmare. Fact.\t\t\t\t\t
