\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is the new DOOM archetype for the modern age. Re-envisioning the framework that made the original tick and become the phenomenon it is today.

Over the course of a 15-20 hour campaign which offers solid window dressing for a narrative, you will acquire an armory of tools, perks and guns you can upgrade on your way to killing the biggest monster you can find in Hell. After you've done it once you can revisit stages to find secrets to further max out your skills and guns or take your arsenal into the score attack mode working up the leaderboard or attempting a world record speedrun.

What you won't find in this Doom are mazes of hallways with keys hidden so well you will end up wandering for an hour trying to progress. Purists may find the maps too linear but they are far from Call of Duty hallway simulators and offer multiple playgrounds to unleash death upon varied hordes of hellspawn.

The multiplayer mode is solid enough for team based deathmatch. You can power up to assume a demon form in between several randomized modes of king of the hill, freeze tag, doomball, TDM, or defender. The free-for-all modes seem dead these days and this was never destined to be the next Fortnite but if you pine for the days of Quake III Arena this should be right up your alley.

The soundtrack is filled with all-new synth metal that would make it's daddy proud. Fuel for the road to ruin that will amp you up with every step.

Overall I have loved my time with this game and plan to come back to climb the multiplayer ladder and revisit the campaign. It is more than worthy of your time if are a fan of the genre and haven't jumped aboard the Eternal bandwagon yet.\t\t\t\t\t
