\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBADASS, HELLISH, MASTERPIECE

Keep in mind this is coming from someone who is not really a fan of First Person Shooters. With that being said, this game is awesome.

You play as an unnamed soldier or fighter or warrior and battle demons on Mars. That's pretty much it for the story. You'll uncover some lore, details about the facility you're on. I didn't really pay attention to the lore and plot of the game, since everything else is much fun than that.

The gameplay is fantastic. You have multiple weapons you can use and many ways to kill demons. The animations are bloody and violent. I love it. This protagonist, which is probably called "Doomguy", really hates demons, and it shows. Although Doomguy can kill a lot of demons, you, the player, must also fight like Doomguy, because this game ain't pulling any punches. Mashing buttons without any strategy will always lead to death. Although Doomguy is angry, he is not dumb. The difficulty is more on the difficult side on normal mode, but once you cleared a room or wave, that feel badassery will seep through your veins.

Other than shooting demons, you can also explore the environment in each level, filled with upgrades and secrets. Level design is wonderfully crafted. All the easter eggs throwback to the 90's Doom game. I haven't played the past game yet, but finding easter eggs like that gives this game some otherworldly charm.

The soundtrack is so amazing that it created a new genre. I'm more of rock kinda guy and not metal, but here it strikes a balance where I headbang everytime I pop a headshot. Amazing work by Mick Gordon.

Since I'm not really well versed in this genre, I don't really know what to complain or find some downsides. Probably the lack of bossfights. Bossfights can be encountered on the second half of the game. On the first half you will venture through levels and clear out rooms and areas. Repeatedly, until it is cleaned. Some may find it repetitive, but the animations and gameplay make up for it, atleast for me.

Overall, felt like a badass.

5/5\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game that got me into oldschool shooters. This is what I would describe this iteration of Doom - and there's more to this game than just shooting demons (...even though that this is the main course). Here's my short (almost) spoiler-free review:

Hand me my shotgun
The gameplay is easily one of the best parts of this game - it's simple, yet effective. You run, jump and shoot with a plethora of different cool-looking weapons - just like in ye olde times. The thing that makes shooting baddies different is the upgrade/weapon attachment system. Each weapon (...with some exceptions) has two weapon attachments you can chage mid firefight. Some are more useful than others and you'll quickly find your favourites. That's not it though - you also get permanent upgrades for your health, armour and ammo capacity; upgrades for your suit such as enviromental resistance and dexterity AND even different game-changing perks in the form of unlockable runes. Also - chainsaw, BFG and finishers - 'nuff said.

Welcome to Mars Hell
The enviroments are awesome. You'll travel from the more industrial-looking parts of the Mars complex to Hell and modern science-y-looking labs. Although every single place you'll visit is wonderfully detailed I personally felt that they can become a bit repetitive.

I sawed a demon in half
You'll fight great looking enemies. From the lowly imp to the glorious cyberdemon - all of them greatly detailed and with a great variety of behaviour between them so you'll want to prioritize some over others.

The Devil grabbed his fiddle
Music. THE ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ MUSIC IN THIS GAME. Combine industrial electronic music and really low-tuned guitars and you've got yourself a perfect fit for the gameplay. Mick Gordon did an excelent job here. I sometimes felt like playing just to listen to the different tracks - which wonderfully bind the gameplay and enviroments together. The soundtrack is also great by itself for long drawn-out trainrides.

Carmack would be proud
Yes, this Doom game has a story. It is there but it's not intrusive at all. If you don't want a story in your game, you don't have to focus on it but in my opinion - it really is a good part of this game (even if there is a lot of reading). It feels like a continuation/re-imagining of the old-school games' story. I won't go into detail since people on the internet already described it better than I ever could. The plot is fitting for the game, leaves on a cliffhanger and builds a universe for the other games in the series.

Friends are welcome
The multiplayer in this game is simple and what you would expect from a Doom game. You'll see some new mechanics like playing as the demons and weapons that aren't in the campaign but that's about it - nothing groundbreaking unfortunately.

Oh snap...map
Snap-map is a nice addition to the game. You can easily create levels thanks to the editor and play user-created levels. Together with the arcade mode this game has a good amount of replayability.

What's that?
The game is filled with secrets so exploration of levels is highly recommended. You can find both fun stuff like parts of old Doom levels and toys that allow you to view 3D models of both enemies and weapons and helpful stuff like upgrades and ammo.

The bad & the ugly
After the good we have to talk about the not-so-good. The game suffers from the occasional repetitivness, some linearity and the lack of a good multiplayer. I disliked the "rune trials" for unlocking perks - they break the gameplay too much in my opinion. Weapons are varied but you'll end up using only about 3 in the late-game after fully upgrading them which is a shame.

tl;dr
is gud; recommend; 9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo i would say this game is a solid 8/10. If you are going to play doom eternal, play this first. I played doom eternal first and it made a lot of this games problems a bit more evident. Is this game better than doom eternal, not in my opinion. The combat in this game is amazing, as expected, and its story actually makes sense compared to doom eternals "attempt to justify the maps after the level designed got high" type story, if you know what i mean. I feel like some of the features, like the to extra gear, was not easily noticable, and as such, most player would go the entire game without noticed that you can spawn a decoy. Also, I feel like a lot of weapons, like the heavy cannon and combat shotgun, are replaced rather quickly, and their mods arent really enough to justify using them over the chain gun or super shotgun. This is not an issue i would have if i didnt play doom eternal first. I also dont like how their are many parkour sections that kill you if you miss a jump. It, in my opinion, makes the doomslayer look a bit weaker than how doom eternal did it. Anyway, the bossfights were great and my last major problem with this game was that it only took my 8 hours to beat first try. Should you get this game, hell yeah. Dont buy it expecting something like call of duty. This isnt a tactical shooter, there is no reloading, and the crouch button is useless. This game encourages up in your face combat. Give Doom 2016 a shot, and if you like it, play doom eternal, to expand on everything in this game and more\t\t\t\t\t
