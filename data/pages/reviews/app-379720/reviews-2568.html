\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is what Doom 3 should have been: a slick, brutal romp that takes to hell and back- literally. Doom is an insanely polished game, fast-paced, intense, and surprisingly smart. The level design takes a range of forms, from the tight, dark corridors of the UAC facility, to the extremely vertical industrial facilities, and finally flying across the wide, winding canyons of hell. On top of the astounding level design, the game is just gorgeous, never sacrificing looks for functionality, nor the other way around.

All the weapons feel satisfying to use, and more importantly, remain viable throughout the entire game, something that many modern FPS games miss. The addition of runes (perks, essentially) allows you to tailor your abilities to your playstyle, but they always encourage to rush in head-first, there's no crouching behind cover every 10 seconds to wipe the stawberry jam off your eyes in this game.

The single-player experience was everything I could have hoped for and more, which only makes the extremely mediocre multiplayer that much more disappointing, seeing as it was made by a completely different team. It's slower-paced, less mobile, and the level design was rather uninspired. But then again, you generally aren't playing Doom for its multiplayer anyways, which made me all the more baffled at the fact that all the post-release content was for the multiplayer.

TL;DR: Fast-paced, brutal, polished, Doom is everything you could want in an FPS, with the only downside being its multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t
