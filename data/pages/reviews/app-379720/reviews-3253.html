\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter beta multiplayer fiasco I wasn't expecting much. But surprisingly the game turned out to be very, very good. I've been playing Doom, Doom 2 and Quaky a lot when I was kid, and never since than I've enjoyed FPS as I did with the new Doom. Fast paced, no-♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, no-nonsense, kill-em-all action. Beautiful graphics, solid performance, excellent level design (large and non-linear, but nowhere maze-frustrating as some of original Doom levels were), fun exploration, smart an tough enemies which require individual strategy for every single one of them. Hell, I even enjoyed the story, and read all codex entries I could get, revealing deep and somewhat mysterious lore. I didn't expecting an interesting story from Doom game, but they actually did it. Moreover, they did it in such a smart way, that if you don't care - it won't bother you. Game doesn't shove lore into your throat, there are no long expositions and unskippable cut scenes - all the story are told though world, characters and data entries found though the world and accessible from a game menu.

The only small nitpicking complain that I have, that the campaign was a bit too long for my taste. I know many people find it as good thing, but for those of us who don't have that much free time (which I suspect is large part of the audience for that game: grown up old-time doom fans like me) we prefer quality over quantity and short games to a longer ones. That said, I had fund though entire campaign, and even played few levels again on a higher difficulty for challenge and finding few remaining secretes I've missed.

I would give it 10/10 for singleplayer campaign, which alone is worth your time and mony. However multiplayer is somewhat lackluster, so I wouldn't even bother with it, but I still would highly recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA light, in this eternal darkness that modern day shooters have put us into. it's there, I see it. perhaps we are not DOOMed.

Quick review,

Singleplayer: Phenominal, Worth the asking price alone, Gore, exploring, good level design, fun gunplay, brutality, Doomguy, It's all there. with only two unfortunate parts where they lock you in a room for a short amount of time to tell you about whats happening, granted, they have to, because when they try to talk to doomguy with other means, he just smashes it. No talk. Only kill.

Multiplayer: Much like Halo Multiplayer, but with a doom twist on it! Fairly interesting and entertaining. Adds value to the game. Has progression, cool cosmetics, demon runes, Loadouts? in a doom game.. eh okay. It's a decent multiplayer experience.

Snapmap: Cool For creative people that like to do creative things. Like you can literally make a vending machine that sells you guns. Or just spawn like 50 demons and a bfg and go ham.

Conclusion, 10/10, Easily the best shooter in a long time. Do buy. I hope you do. I know I did.\t\t\t\t\t
