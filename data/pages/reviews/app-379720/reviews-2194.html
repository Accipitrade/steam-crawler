Frantic carnage with enough room for a simple, yet very well exposed storyline. Something the original did through text screens.

I'm not a particular fan of the djenty dubstep, but it's a fitting update to the metal .midi doots of old.

A perfect update of the Classic. Oldschool and new generation gamers alike will find something they love about this game.

By the way, playing the IDKFA album by Andrew Hulshult REALLY works with the atmosphere of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best shooters I have ever played.

I was a bit too young and Nintendo-fanboy-ish to know about early PC FPS games, and by the time I got into things like XBox, Doom 3 was out and got canned, so i only played military-style FPSes for a while. Doom 2016 made me wish I could go back in time to diversify the kind of games I played as a kid and teen. Everything about this game is near-perfect. From the way everything is designed, to how action is fast-flowing without feeling twitchy, to how every gun feels, to how versatile you can make weapons, to the story itself is amazing.

Being able to customize and upgrade weapons gives the game some variety on the player's end as well as the game world itself. Your guns essentially having different "modes" means you double your arsenal in a way, giving some room for experimentation, especially on repeat playthroughs. The glory kills thankfully do not overstay their welcome, have a gameplay purpose, and are by no means mandatory. Similarly is the chainsaw, and while it's disappointing that it's based on "charges" rather than just using it as a melee weapon, the way it integrates into gameplay as a last resort "use when out of ammo" weapon is a fitting purpose. Grenades, however, feel like they serve no purpose, and don't feel nearly as punchy as any of the guns.

Even the story has some depth to it without getting too in the way. It's a shame most of it is relegated to paragraphs of text that you have to get from collectibles randomly in the levels. But since this is a DOOM game, I know story gets but on literally the lowest priority. But while I'm on that, the automap feature is great, in that it finds a near-perfect way to map out a 3D level, but relegating the various upgrades to collectibles to be found in levels seems wrong to me, like it's rewarding the player for exploring rather than shooting, which is the game's focus. And going back through a level to get an upgrade breaks the game's normally smooth flow immensely.

Overall, Doom 2016 is an incredible experience, and is especially worth buying now to prep yourself for Doom Eternal, which promises to be a sequel matching or superceding in quality.\t\t\t\t\t
