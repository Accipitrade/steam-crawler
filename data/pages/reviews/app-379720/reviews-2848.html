\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you love looking for secrets, doing obscure challenges and occasionally killing demons without a care in the world, then this game exhibits an incredibly high level of polish, a great mapping tool, tools you can upgrade to reduce the annoyance of searching for secrets if you so wish, interesting weapons - it's just as everyone says.

Personally I feel like the challenges get on my nerves more often than not. I'm somewhat of a completionist, but I also don't enjoy going out of my way to accomplish obscure challenges like "Kill X number of Y demons using a very specific glory kill from their right leg." What? I just want to kill demons and look for secrets, damn it. Let me kill demons the way I want to kill them!

That gripe is more of a personal issue rather than anything else, the rest of the game is so well polished and enjoyable that I couldn't not recommend it for that reason. If you hate loredumps in FPS games you'll especially love DOOM since the marine that you play has absolute contempt for anything the NPCs ask him to do. He's there to absoultely murder demons, stop their invasion and doesn't care what anyone else has to say about how he does it. Unless it's a challenge his suit gives him.

The lore hidden away in codex entries that you gather automatically/from searching for them adds nice flavor. Most of it is technobabble fluff, but it's interesting technobabble fluff that isn't hurting anyone by being in the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wasn't a fan of Doom series, but after playing the demo, this game got me.

Pros:
- Single player is great. Action is fast-paced. Weapons and melee feel powerful. Character movement is fluid.
- It runs smoothly on my PC (i7-6700, GTX1060 6GB, 32GB RAM, SSD, Win 10 64bit). No bug or crash.
- It plays great with a 360 controller.
- Visual and audio are top-notch. They nailed all the designs, especially the guns.

Cons:
- Waypoint system and some mission objectives can be unclear sometimes. You might end up wandering around empty levels trying to figure out what to do or where to go next.
- Platforming sections where you die from falling. Personally I don't think FPS games need that.

More unlockables for New Game+ would be nice, but the game is already worth its price as it is. So, no complain.
And I haven't tried multiplayer.

Definitely recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
