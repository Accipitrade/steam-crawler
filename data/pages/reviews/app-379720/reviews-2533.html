\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom single player is outstanding. Mutliplayer is pretty irritating.

Single Player: THUMBS UP !!!
Combat is very much in your face with the vast majority of the fights being in locked down arena battles with a mob of mixed bad guys. I'm not a big fan of being locked into a room with a mob, but doom's areas are well designed and often have choke points or bottle necks that you can exploit, as well as powerups like quad damage.

Weapons, nades, mods and runes are varied and give the player a lot of options. there are maybe six useful weapons when modded and upgraded, but those weapons vary in range, fire rate and DPS. It feels like the player has a lot of different combinations of weapons. No reloading is great and should be adopted by all FPS game designers.

Skill trees are minimal and consist of upgrading suit powers and ammo/health/sheild capacities.

Maps tend to be mazelike and can be difficult to get around in. Platforming is well implimented and easy to do, but always gives me sweaty hands. Secrets/collectibles are well hidden but easy to reach once you find them. I recommend majorslack walthroughs of Doom for pathfinding as well as combat tactics. i always keep a slack walkthrough up in the background in case i get lost or can't find a secret area. very helpeful.

The feel of the single player game is very polished and thoughtful. The game feels like old style shooters but with great graphics and a few mod and skill trees to play with.

Multi Player: thumbs down!

multiplayer is dying and hard to get matches at some times during the day. when you're in a match, you may get random disconnects. random disconnects are frustrating in the middle of a match. a lot of people own doom and very few play multi.
mulitplayer is fast and a bit irritating... you have powerful weapons and low health and armor pools. so everyone is a glass cannon. add to that vastly overpowered powerups which enable the player who gets the powerup to kill everyone on the map, and you have a pretty unbalanced mess. everyone mobs the place where the powerups will spawn and whoever gets the powerup kills everyone
in doom you don't lose accuracy by jumping, moving or running. so most players are madly double jumping and strafing while trying to land a shot... it's chaotic and annoying.
Also after 30 odd levels at the beginning of multiplayer, you have all the weapons and equipment unlocked, so playing more only unlocks colors and character customization items ( or hats as TF2 vets call them). many of the colors, patterns and armor items are almost identical.
The bottom line is that it's more fun to play bots in practice mode than online multi in doom. bot difficulty can be set and you can select the map you want to use.
online multiplayer can be boring and irritating, if you can find a match and don't get disconnected.
there are 15 year old multiplayer games that are much more fun than this.

Single player Doom is great, but it is not a casual game, you have to have your tight at all times because 15 big bad guys will jump in your face every time you walk into a new area and get locked in.
Multiplayer is interesting to mess with, but turns out to be more irritating than fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWelcome back to DOOM. It's a great game and I applaud the approach taken. This game is a dance of death, destruction and gore and a return to form.

While DOOM 3 tried to do an atmospheric unsettling environment for its environments for its property to usher it into the realistic shooter markets. Reloading, a slower pace and a "serious" story. DOOM (2016) has gone back to its roots and reimagined what would the first "Doom" s would have been like have they been made today.

Just like Doom back in the day, you get the same arsenal set from the chainsaw all the way to the BFG. The twist this time is that mods can be found to give your weapons additional functions. The bestiary is the same as well but has different behaviors which work different in the environments. The environments are lush and varied and takes into account for vertically. They are also better allowances for finding collectibles and little secrets here and there was part of the charm of the first Doom.

For the twitch/ snapshooting FPS gamer, this is everything that you want. The Marine is very quick and able to use finishers as a way to avoid some of the incoming damage. Everything mixes very well together along with its soundtrack to give a visceral rush of (sort of) mindless combat.

At this point, the conclusion should be drawn that the Doom franchise is known for its frantic gameplay along with its setting of Hellspawn on Mars. The Doom franchise has achieved the same status that the Mario and Zelda IPs where the story is the same but every iteration will evolve the mechanics in which it was born from. Doom should probably only evolve in this space and if iD studios wants to do mechanics that are different, it should probably use one of its other IPs to be the carrier of it in order to be successful.\t\t\t\t\t
