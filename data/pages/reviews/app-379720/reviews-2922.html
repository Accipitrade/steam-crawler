\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wasn't really sure what I was expecting - I played (and loved) DOOM and DOOM 2 when I was a kid and Doom 3 was a survival horror experience which was good, but felt like a completely different game series. So when I saw the original images and trailers for DOOM (2016), I wasn't entirely sold on it. And then I got my hands on it.

A lot of times, your mind plates the lead of the past in gold: things you remember looking one way, look way better in your head than in reality. So hopefully this will make sense: DOOM (2016) plays like I remember the original DOOM. It's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ intense; a thrill a minute bloodrush that doesn't stop. It's a trip to the most awful part of Hell and the most desolate part of Mars and it's the best roller coaster I've been on in years.

Two things are particularly worth mentioning. First, the story is narrative brilliance - they took the original game's one dimensional story (you were the last Marine on a Mars outpost that's been overrun by demons, kill your way out) and blown it into a fullblown epic tale that is STILL the same original plot. The story is literally the definition of doing more with less - I only wish I could come up with a story this sharp and terse. Second is the soundtrack: Mick Gordon took Slipknot and Nine Inch Nails and used both to weave something that is both easy to listen to by itself and that fuels your every movement during play, making you feel like a profound badass.

Easily one of my favorite experiences of the year - while Dishonored 2 is probably objectively a better gaming experience, I can definitely say I had more fun in Hell. Well worth the price of entry and highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
