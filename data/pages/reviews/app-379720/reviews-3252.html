\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is going back to the days where Doom was just a shareware floppy disk that would be sold on the counter of your local computer store. If any of you remember CompUSA or Computer City, (might be just a New England thing) you will remember how the game was an awesome step up from Wolfenstein 3D. I remember Doom II also being in a huge bundle set of floppy disks just to install, but I found it funny how ID Software just jumped from E1 Shareware Doom to Doom II, then I drew to the conclusion that ID waited for Microsoft to come out with Windows 95 to release "The Ultimate Doom" but anyway.... Without getting into the whole history, this was the game that started it all on the FPS genre. The new generation Call Of Duty, no skilled console gaming snobs will never experience what we've experienced. They'll never be able to say "Hey I was there with the first wave FPS genre was created, I lived it!"

All I want to do is give a huge thank you to Bethesda for picking up ID Software and releasing the most nostalgic feeling Doom that I remember, mininal story telling, classic silent protaganist, the nightmarish feeling as you advanced through the game. It's all there, everything that I remember from playing the classic Doom is all there, but I have also come to notice that somebody in the development team had to been keeping an eye on the game modders that have created Project Brutality. The weapon mods and the fatalities (Glory Kills) was definitely a Brutal Doom idea, regardless.. Still a classic run and gun as Doom was intended to be. I have not one thing bad to say about DOOM 2016.\t\t\t\t\t
