\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough this is completely different than what i expected, i must say i found this doom reboot a pretty fun shooter and overall a nice game. I got this at a heavy discount during the autumn sales (just 20€) and i feel it's worth the money. I would probably be a bit disappointed if i paid full price though, since the campaign took me only a little less than 10 hours in ultra violence difficulty.

What i did not like:

- the game is a series of big rooms full of demons connected by corridors and/or cutscenes and/or other elements that, in my opinion, break the flow of the game (i.e. runes challenges, powerup managament). After the first few level i simply stopped caring about those and did the full level without ever stopping (except for the mandatory cutscenes). This is its biggest flaw in my opinion: however, even if sometime it feels like a slow shooter, this changes after the first few levels.

- in a few occasions i felt like the maps had a very lazy game design. Although overall the levels are amazing to look at, the layout is not very clear and unique as any map of the original doom. I rarely had to get back to a previous area to be surprised by a new wave of enemies. Just go forward, kill everything that moves.

- the game implemented a one-shotting chainsaw, which i didn't really like. I used to love to chainsaw everything on my way in the original doom! On the bright side though, chainsaw kills are amazing to look at!

- thankfully there is not a regen health system, however i'm not very happy with the kill a demon -> get health mode. Still, the game throws at you so much enemies that after a while you don't even stop doing the glory kills for health.


What i liked:

- it's gory as hell (duh)

- it's a fun shooter!

- the attention to detail is staggering. It's an id production afterall, and it shows.

- the soundtrack is great

- ironically, the multiplayer (for the few games i tried) gave a strange UT/Quake vibe. I liked it, although, no music? wth?

So, even if this is absolutely not the game i expected, it's still probably the best shooter i've put my hands on recently!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game plays much like Wolfenstein: The New Order does - fast, lots of killing and a ton of fun. I ended up playing the demo for this game earlier this year and enjoyed it. When the game went on sale, I picked it up and don't regret it. The graphics are gorgeous, the game play is solid, and the campaign is immense. Each mission in DooM will take you to explore something new from half-destroyed laboratories to interpretations of Hell; moreover, each new mission sees an increase in difficulty in the number and variation of demons one must destroy and herein lies my only complaint – it gets tedious.

DooM, as I understand it, is setup to be a big arena, arcade-like game, so you go from mission to mission fighting your way through hordes of demons and on the way the difficulty in beating these hordes increases as you go along. On some areas within a mission, it can take awhile to beat it depending on the level of difficulty selected when starting the campaign. After playing the campaign for more than eight hours, I’m ready for this adventure to be done and over with as I find myself enjoying it less and less due to going to one area, destroying all the enemies, and rinsing and repeating in another area. The story is mildly interesting to me, but I feel like all of it could be explained in fewer Missions – there are 13 missions altogether with each so far having taken me around 45 minutes to an hour to beat.

Altogether, though, this game is definitely worth the buy. It lives up to its reputation of being a great game. There’s much to enjoy about it, including the multiplayer community, which is still alive and active. If you enjoyed Wolfenstein, then you’ll enjoy playing this title.
