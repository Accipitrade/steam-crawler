\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr - Combat is a blast, story is pretty simple and straightforward but the worldbuilding is surprisingly rich, pacing is a bit of an issue, and the game is kinda short. Not worth the original $60 probably, but a great game nonetheless, and probably worth its current price.

Full review:
Well, lets start with the main attraction, the combat. Fast paced, punchy, gory fun. Everything about it feels great. It is, by far, the main selling point of the game, as it should be. The combat makes it very clear very early that it's not like most shooters: If you try to hide behind cover, move carefully and keep your distance - You're gonna have a bad time. What you want is to be quick, always keep moving, get in close and bash some heads in. It's so much fun.

But then, there's the other part of the gameplay - Exploration and platforming. That's a complete 180 from the combat. While the combat is fast paced and fun, exploring is slow and boring. While combat is very straightforward, exploring the map can be quite confusing. While combat is quite forgiving, allowing you to recover health and armor quite easily if you screw up, platforming is a 1-mistake-and-you-die deal, and you have to wait for the loading screen to take you back to the checkpoint. The second combat ends, the rush is replaced with tedium. If you try to get all collectibles, you will spend more time running around trying to figure out how to reach some hidden area than actually fighting. That is quite unacceptable imo.
The plus side is, you don't have to get the collectibles, and the game is a lot more straightforward if you decide to ignore them. But, the collectibles are how you get upgrades, and the game loses the whole power-progression when you don't take them. What I did was google a list of all collectibles in each level, and I used that while playing to get everything without wasting time. This is obviously not ideal, and I wish they did a better job with this,

The story is quite simple. You're the Doom Slayer, there's an open gate to hell that's pouring out demons, and you're gonna do whatever it takes to close it. Doomguy is a silent protagonist, and there are a few side-characters who, while they do talk, are quite flat. Still, there's a bit of comedy through Doomguy's actions and responses and it definitely works.
Other than that, there's quite a lot of worldbuilding through text and dialogue, that is far richer than I expected it to be. This definitely left me wanting more.

The game is pretty short, but I think making it longer would have been a mistake. The game is quite repetitive. Most of the time, you're gonna be locked in a large arena-room and fight waves of enemies, and then the doors will unlock and you move on until you reach the next arena room. It works, but much more of it would have been too much, and the game would have overstayed its welcome.
The game also doesn't really have any replay value. The story is 100% linear, and you can upgrade all of your weapons to max in a single playthrough, so there's no much reason to ever play it again.
So, due to the limited time you'll spend in game, I'd say it's probably not worth $60. But, by the time of this review's writing, the price has already dropped significantly, and I'd say the game is worth it's current price.

So yes. I recommend it. It's really fun.\t\t\t\t\t
