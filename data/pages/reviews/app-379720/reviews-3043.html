\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI made the mistake of playing the 2013 version of Shadow Warrior just before playing this.

The graphics are great and the gameplay is fast but overall, I enjoyed Shadow Warrior more just because it was more fun interms of style and in gameplay. Nevertheless, this is still a good game.

I played the original Doom and loved it at the time. This has the same overall feel in terms of atmosphere and gameplay so no complaints there. I couldn't help but shake the feeling that games HAVE moved on since those old days and I don't really like the idea of scrabbling around for keys to unlock the next area. I'm not totally into platforming in my shooters either - it annoys me at times because I had a few needless deaths because I ran off a ledge and had to go back quite a way.

There's a good variety of weapons and they all have a great FEEL to them. The idea of glory kills is great, giving the game variety as you are encouraged to get up close and personal even with the heavies.

One complaint I have is that there is a lot of similarity to the levels. I mean, if I needed to ask my friend for help and he asked where in the game I was, I'd be a little bit stuck to explain it to him. They all kinda feel similar - especially the early ones. Later, there is more variation but this feeling did resonate throughout. You move and you shoot some stuff. Many modern games have great world building moments but stepping into this game really felt like I was there just to commit murder and there was definitely something lacking in terms of real atmosphere.

There is a story here but I wasn't exactly captivated by it. Someone unleashed some demons and you have to sort it out. Generally it moved me on to the next place. There is backstory about Doomguy and you can find out more information about the denizens of hell if you are interested but lets face it, the main protagonist is just an empty shell.

Overall, if you like good graphics, fast, intense and challenging gameplay, go for it. If you haven't already bought it, try Shadow Warrior too, though - it's actually more fun :p\t\t\t\t\t

One of the best FPS campaigns ever crafted, DOOM is a must play, even for those who typically don't enjoy FPS.

Small tech thing: DOOM is extremely well optimized and has an incredible amount of options.

Long review:
DOOM is an anamoly among my enjoyed games. I typically don't enjoy FPS games all that often, the closest I'll come is something like Call of Pripyat or a first person RPG.

To say I enjoy DOOM is an understatement. Doom demonstrates some of the most well done first person shooting mechanics I've ever encountered. Hardly do I ever think about how great shooting feels in a game. In Doom, I notice.

There is simply something gratifying about lining up a shotgun blast to an imps head while side strafing at what seems like 50 mph to dodge gunshots from a mancubus. There is elegence to the bulllet-hell nature to the way one dodges enemy attacks while simultaneously picking up some gauss rifle ammo just in time to take out two hell knights in a single charged shot.

Moments like this line the halls of DOOM's gameplay and make it an extremely memorable experience. In truth, the game could just have been arenas with combat scenarios and it still would have been a superb game.

But,
(and this is a good thing)

There aren't just combat arenas, though they do take up a majority of the gametime. There are moments of downtime in doom. Just after an intense battle the game will routinley ease back the throttle, either to let you refuel on armor, health, and ammo, or to allow you to explore.

And the exploration is just as good as the shooting. Secrets were a mainstay in the days of "doom-clones" and return in the newest iteration of Doom. From collectibles to hidden weapons and great recurring easter eggs, the levels are packed with things to find during and after combat. I routinley found myself bunny hopping around the level looking for small vents I could climb into or seeing ledges which I could clearly reach that had me figuring out a way to reach them. In a sense, Doom has a bit of light platforming, something oft done terribly in first person games. In Doom, however, moving, jumping and the like are a joy, in and out of combat.

The newest Doom also has a bit of customization. You collect ruins which give you passive abilities, such as letting you initiate glory kills from an absurd distance away; Addons can be collected for guns and upgraded; Your armor can be upgraded as well. All of these things let you customize the way you evicerate demons and give a good sense of progression as well as a reason to explore.

The music is also very well done. The soundtrack is the fast pased chugging heavy metal score you would expect from a DOOM game that is clearly trying to recreate the feeling of the first DOOM in the modern day. Its not overbearing but when you get those magic moments where the ripping guitar seems to line up to your gunshots as your gliding about the arena it definitley enhances the experience to a large degree.

The story, for me, was something that I bairly noticed. I was fair to busy gliding about the level. In some spaces I felt like the momentum was disrupted a bit when I was stopped from running and had to stand around to listen to dialogue. This is the only real negative I can think of for the game. Except...

The multiplayer is fairly lackluster, but, the game is worth $60 for just the single player campaign.

In the end DOOM is one of the finest FPS's ever made and possibly the best game of 2016. Simultaneously acting as a call back to its classic roots with it's fast-paced gameplay & secrets, while also bringing modern sensabilties with its progression and upgrade system.\t\t\t\t\t
