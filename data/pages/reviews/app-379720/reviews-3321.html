\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an absolute gem this game is. id shows nobody can outdo them in the genre they invented.

First off this game is absolutely gooorgeous. No brown hallways here. Everything is super vibrant and the vast mars landscape views are really something. And it plays even better than it looks. Long gone are the dark hallways and spooky scares of the old games. They've been replaced with high octane encounters full of a variety of monsters requiring the player to think on their feet while blasting away.

Each gun and the player himself is also customizable in their mechanics, allowing the player to really tweak their experience to their play style.

It's just, a marvel. Seriously. I'm in love. Probably the best pure fps flow/ mechanics game ever made. Period.

Absolutely perfect 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's get the okay out of the way. Multiplayer is good but for a game like DOOM it is a change. At least I won't have to die 5 times before I can kill Kyle who is camping the BFG spawn with my puny pistol. I do under stand it however. Weapons do not often kill in one shot (unless it's a power weapon). This encourages players to leave a fight or go around to grab that health pick up instead of thinking "1HP, 100 HP same difference!" There is a mode for everyone, and if there isn't snap map will probably fill your need. Snapmap has a game finder search function; it is here you can play a few modes not in the multiplayer function for the sole reason that the game modes do not function well enough on the maps. Deathmatch is one of them; leave the hard DM maps to the community. I will say this: once you find your favorite loadout, you will grin like the DOOM Marine in the bottom of the screen in the first and second games. You know the one that says either "I'm about to kick ass" or "It's raping time".
NOW IT'S TIME FOR THE THING DOOM IS ALL ABOUT: KILLIN' DEMONS.
Story was all wrapped up nice. This may be DOOM but this is "DOOM 4" as the artifacts you uncover will hint at what happened in DOOM 1 and DOOM 2. Story was nice. Main antagonist was satisfying to beat. There are 13 total levels with some being longer than others. the shortest one I played was I think the first (I was looking for all the secrets!)
-Gameplay in the story: Compared to other first person shooters pretty fast. As said before, you're "Bruce Lee on a skateboard with a shotgun" or whatever weapon you roll with and skateboard-nah. Ever try playing an arena in campaign? It's great! In terms of progress with in the level here is the breakdown: kill demons until you find a nest of demons which triggers a demon overpopulation-warning which sends the area into demon containment lockdown until you kill all of the demons; repeat there is no rinse. For some reason you will find out your character stays in the same dirt he has when he wakes up. IF YOU ARE WORIED ABOUT STORY!!!!!!!>>>Within the first 10 second if not a maximum of 20 after the loading screen you are killing hellspawn. The only time you will have unskipable cutsceens or story is when it's necessary to explain what you need to do which is, besides killing demons, go here and activate this to close or get near closing the hell portal. So anything that prevents you from saying "Wait, why am I here and going there?"<<<. To put in perspective how badass you are: VERY MINOR SPOILER a badass voice has seven hidden items/texts that he reads about the events of when your character first came to town. Surprise! Surprise! You kicked major ass! END OF LITTLE SPOILER!!!
You will never stop killing and your cause is noble. Taken from a text that was put into the loading screen of the very first level:

"So you walk eternaly through the shadow realms, standing against evil where all other falter. May your thirst for retribution never quench, may the blood on your sword never dry, and may we never need you again." But they will need you again, always and especially NOW. FIGHT! LIKE! HELL!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom. It's a game about shotguns and rocket launchers and plasma rifles and gatling cannons and BFGs and demons and monsters and zombies and heavy metal music. It's a game that hands you a pistol in a room full of zombies ten seconds into the game and assumes you understand what you need to do. It's a game where, when some dork comes over a computer monitor to explain the backstory to you, your character just picks up the monitor and smashes it into a wall. It's a game that doesn't waste time with magazine reloads or regenerating health or cover mechanics. It's a game about constantly moving and never stopping; about split-second threat assessments; about always maintaining strong spatial awareness; about keeping up a steady rhythm; about translating nervous energy and adrenaline into celebratory fireworks displays of blood and demon flesh.

Doom. It's a game you should buy right now.

Oh, and is the multiplayer fun? I score frags with a rocket launcher painted up like the American flag, then do the Carlton to celebrate my victories. Of freakin' course it's fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 is a game I was really, REALLY wrong about from day ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ one. When I saw the initial gameplay, I was disgusted and turned off- Glory Kills I felt broke the flow of gameplay, the game looked incredibly edgy and the weird speed of the main character turned me off. As the game approached, I realized I was being too harsh on the game, and as such decided to give it a whirl. The verdict? The game is great! I'm shocked at how poorly Bethesda marketed this game, because this is one of the best first person shooters i've played in a long time.
* Story 7/10
This is perhaps the biggest suprise- DOOM 2016 actually has a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ story, suprisingly, and it's decent. While it's no Shakespeare, it occasionally has some funny writing, and Doomguy is absolutely hilarious all throughout the story despite never opening his mouth. Another suprise is the wealth of lore from item descriptions and logs, all of which reveal a somewhat complex backstory with a lot of depth. Who the Doomguy is in this universe and his purpose in the story is not only an excellent interpretation of his character, but a wonderful sendup to all the fans who played these games throughout the years. Unfortunately the game ends on a brutal cliffhanger that resolves absolutely nothing, and leaves a lot to be desired.
* Design 8/10
The level design of this game is really something special. Most levels are open and able to be explored, and the game encourages the player to backtrack and explore hidden areas. Goodies are all over the place, and finding a hidden cache or a collectible feels rewarding. The game will often drop you into 'kill rooms"- large, locked off rooms full of health, armor, ammo, and a LOT of really angry demons that want to eat you. The only way to leave is to slaughter everyone that isn't you. The design of these rooms is simply excellent- moving from section to section, jumping, dodging and weaving your way and sprinting along, collecting everything you see while pumping lead into your enemies is an exhilarting experience that seldom gets old. The enemies, while not smart, are DEADLY even on Normal, and must be skillfully evaded using the design of these rooms to your advantage. The weapons you have access to are large, mean and fun to use- shotguns, rifles and laser weapons punctuate a fairly large arsenal. These weapons add an unexpected level of strategy to the game- for instance, the chainsaw will instantly kill any enemy provided it has the fuel for it, spilling a huge amount of ammo for each gun. Do you use the weapon on a weak enemy now and harvest his ammo, or save it for a big enemy and reap the rewards of a tough foe fallen AND the added bonus of some nice ammo? The game also includes a levelling system of sorts- by finding things in the enviroment, Doomguy can upgrade his max health/ammo/armor, his weapons (including adding altfires) and his suit to deadly effect. You'll need these too, as the game will frequently swamp you with enemies in the later levels. Multiplayer fares a lot worse however- it's a weird amalgamation of old-school map and movement design with new-school design that just doesn't work all that well. Weapon loadouts exist and you can only carry two (!!!!!!) weapons at a time, along with a sidearm and a demon. This is insane- the game would be much better if it allowed you to pick your starting weapon and forcing you to pick up others scattered around the field, ala Quake III. The demons are horrifically overpowered, the Glory Kills don't work all that well, and the weird, garish armor designs and colours make for a multiplayer that feels like it's caught between the singleplayers excellent design and Call of Duty. At least it's still kinda fun. Snapmap feels like a mini-Forge from Halo- I was never into map creation, but it seems fun, and having the ability to do co-op is a blast.
* Gameplay 9.6/10
DOOM 2016 is fun, man. It's really fun to blast away demons with a shotgun, rifle, or more. The game just feels really ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ good the whole way through, from minute one, and never stops feeling good the whole way through. Glory Kills are an interesting mechanic- get an enemy's health down enough and you can instantly kill them in a brutal animation that rewards you with health. They're super short so they never break the flow of gameplay, and there's enough of them (all of which are context sensitive) that they never feel old. The bosses, enemies and weapons all come together to form a really fun experience that excites from start to the finish, and the campaign, which lasts 8-12 hours, can really suprise on how fun it is. Multiplayer and Snapmap will give this game a decently long life, but otherwise it's a blast.
* Presentation: 8/10
The game looks great. Lighting effects are excellent, atmosphere is perfect and the music and audio design are both stellar. The game had some framerate problems on my medium-high spec laptop with a GTX 965M (really wishing i'd sprung for the GTX 970M one), only getting about 30-45 FPS in most locations but otherwise (aside from one or two crashes to the desktop) the game ran fine.

*Overall: 8.1
A fine game that Bethesda really should have had more faith in.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tnow see the thing i love about this game is that they didn’t try to reinvent ♥♥♥♥

this is straight up ♥♥♥♥♥♥ doom

you run around shooting demons and ♥♥♥♥ and it’s ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ great

that’s not everything though, they didn’t try to reinvent ♥♥♥♥ which is good but they did add a few new things that, in my opinion, are great and welcome, and those things are ledge-grabbing, weapon mods, and various character upgrades as well as challenges that give a mix of weapon and character upgrades

everything in this game feels great to do–jump, glory kill, pick up items, find secrets, traverse the stage, and most importantly shoot and move, it all feels really smooth and it all flows together very nicely, it’s all very seamless and fun

i think my only complaints are there aren’t enough gamemodes? like i haven’t checked out the multiplayer because i heard it sucked but I haven’t heard anything about a mutliplayer endless wave survival game mode which, now that i’ve played this game plenty, is something that I think it would’ve greatly benefited from and would’ve been really fun to do with friends

my other complaint is that there’s like… 1 mission where you go to Hell? Hell looks really ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ awesome but you’re only there for a single mission, unless i’m wrong i actually haven’t played the WHOLE game yet but based on some upgrade finding help videos i’ve been watching there’s only 1 Hell level.

now that I think about it, i have one more complaint: snap map apparently isn’t that great? like I read that you can’t create open environments, or big environments, so basically you can only make narrow hallway/mazes which I guess is somewhat ok because that’s very early DOOM level design i suppose, but still it would’ve been great to create bigger environments beyond hallways and such

DOOM 2016 earns a 9/10. It just exists, and what it is is great and it’s just plain and simple fun. Through this entire game I haven’t gotten frustrated at all, though some puzzles made me scratch my head a while but that’s more my fault than it is the game’s.

Plain and simple, like I said. I really like this game because of that, there’s nothing else to it besides running around shooting dudes and getting upgrades. I might try out Snap Map for myself, given how basic yet fun this game is I think it’s great that I can potentially never stop playing it with the community-created levels.\t\t\t\t\t
