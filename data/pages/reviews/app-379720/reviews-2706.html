\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a very hasty recommendation.

Doom or 'New Doom' as I'll call it, is an excellent shooter- one of the best on the market today as of Q2 2017.
It looks gorgeous and the gun audio is on point, everything looks and sounds even better than you'd expect.
Doom gets nothing wrong when it comes to shooting demons with every different kind of upgradeable weapon
you can think of- plus the glory kills are rad.
Saying that, going into this game I expected the glory kill system to get old quite quickly, yet it was the game itself which
did that for me. Glory kills switch up most of the time so you don't see the same repetitive animation too much, the developers understood this and made it a great compromise. Why didn't they understand that about the game itself?
DOOM is an excellent and mechanically brilliant FPS in a Groundhog Day style loop. Every mission is fairly similar
-cutscene
-shoot minions
-destroy gross demon thing which spawns boss demons
-destroy all demons
-progress
The only break in this rule tends to be when the game gives you a new weapon or sends you on an obligatory platforming mission which well and truly breaks whatever pace the game had left.
Overall, this is a great callback to the original DOOM- repetitive slaughter of demon after demon for self mastibatory satisfaction of the player.
John Romero, creator of DOOM once famously said that people did not buy video games for story or substance, simply for the experience of the game itself- something I profoundly disagree with, but if we adhere to his maxim 'New Doom' entirely fails in being a worthwhile experience. If you want to scratch that ID Software itch, maybe return to Wolfenstein the New Order or wait for Quake later down the line. However, if you're only interested in repetitvely shooting demons- this is the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original changed my life back in 94. It got me into modding which eventually led me to software engineering. It's difficult for me to be objective about DOOM but I'll try.

This 2016 remake is a worthy successor in the series. It takes the strengths of the original (spooky atmosphere, rooms full of demons, power fantasy) and wraps them in a beautiful engine with modern gameplay traits. These modern traits, like a progression system, FPS movement changes, never feel tacked on mind you. Instead they add extra depth we've come to expect nowadays.

The campaign is not overly long but the skill challenges and (with a later patch) the arcade mode give you plenty to do. The story is pulpy as you'd want in a DOOM game. The characters take themselves seriously but the protagonist goes through it as we, as players, should: fast and unconcerned.

A true come back from id Software, focusing on gameplay while not losing sight of a beautiful presentation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom was just about perfect before the 6.66 update but I had stopped playing multiplayer due to the DLC pricing and splitting of the player base. Now that the expansions are free I can easily say that Doom is the best FPS game since Doom.

The campaign is straight to the point, with minimal story. Awe inspiring set pieces all over the place. Great variety in lighting and mood throughout. Never got boring for a second.

And now the multiplayer is up there with Halo 2 as the best DM or TDM game ever. I've seen the bad reviews and the good reviews of the multiplayer, the bad ones are just plain wrong usually because they don't understand how to excel in a game like Doom, and even the good reviews never seem to fully grasp the complexities on display here. There are four things to select in your loadout (5 if you count a demon for team games), 2 weapons, 1 equipment, and 1 rune. You can make kits that play drastically different from each other. You can do standard brute style kits but you can get more creative and use the grenade launcher alt fire to make a smoke bomb, and then teleport out of it to the teleporter that you just threw on the ceiling, leaving the opponent shooting at an empty smoke cloud while you rain down rockets from above and get all taht sweet splash dmg. Even the most positive piece I saw on the multiplayer talked about the runes and how Vital Signs (shows enemy health) is good to see if you should engage or not, that is true, but it's really great in FFA to see which players can be picked off the fastest for multikills and good rating at the end of the match. Every little decision made for the multiplayer at this point makes the game more exciting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a game that has a good storyline for a game that isn't supposed to have a storyline. The game gets you into action within the first 10 seconds of the campaign. I have completed the campaign and have to say it is one of the best I have ever played. With the RPG like mechanics of upgrading your suit and weapons to the FPS style game. The multiplayer is very fun and provides thorough customization, with an array of weapons you will not be bored. Being able to customize your multiplayer character feels amazing. In the campaign there are many easter eggs and throwbacks. In each level you will be able to find a level from the classic DOOM. It is very dark. Not that dark, but the kind to where you will have to turn your brightness up. The endong is very satisfying. For only $15 (on sale) you will be provided with one of the best first-person shooters. After the campaign there is an arcade mode, and snap-map. Arcade is a survival whorde mode to see how long you can survive and snap-map allows you to create your levels or play ones that the community has made. There is a lot of content in this game. The only flaw I can say is that some people will not like the campaign because it does not have a great story. Besides that, I reccomend you pick up this game. 9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
