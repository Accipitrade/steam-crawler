\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEasilly the best shooter of 2016, i bought it back on ps4 in 2016 and loved it and now on PC i got it again during a sale where i love it even more with the improved visuals and far,far,faaaaaar better controls.

I Initially got the game on release day for PC in 2016 from a friend as a gift however the game on release had an incredible amount of problems, from weird crashing issues,audio crackling to horrible low fps even on low settings on my previous older card the 760 GTX [Got a new AMD RX 580 8GB now which runs Doom like a dream], so i ended up refunding it and instead saved up some money and bought it for myself on ps4 a few weeks later since i couldn't play it on PC at the time but now i finally can.

Doom [4] 2016 is a return to a much better time for shooters, when levels were more open ended and had secrets to discover which would reward you with ammo,armor or even allow you to get new weapons more early. It was a time where you wouldn't have to spend 5+ hours watching cutscenes instead 90+% of your playtime was actually playing the game.
There's a large variety of demons to brutaly murder and a strong variety of guns to use, each of which has two different secondary fire modes to unlock and upgrade.
The campaign has a pretty solid lenght of about 10 to 13 hours if you chose to spend your time hunting secrets and beating all challanges.
Both my PS4 and PC playthroughs were a blast and I'll most likely keep replay it again and again in the future years to come.

The game does still have some odd technical issues, but nothing as bad as it did on release. Game runs very well now, maintaing 150+ fps on Ultra settings in 1080p without a hitch but for some unknown reason, the main menu and 3D map screen at random times gets weird frame rate fluctuations that make mouse movement sluggish, it's a minor annoyance as it only happens sometimes in the menus but never in game where it ofc. matters the most.

Multiplayer on the other hand seems to be a mixed bag for people, a lot fans were disappointed because they were pretty much expecting it to be just like Quake's MP but it wasn't, instead it's a hybrid combination of Quake and Halo's MP. I myself am an old school Quake fan but i honestly didn't mind this hybrid combo much at all, so much so i even spent a good month or two playing the MP on console however on PC now after a year it seems to be dead as it takes a long time to find anyone and at best you can get 2v2 matches going which aren't fun much as i dislike such small player counts, sadly PC version also has no anti-cheat what so ever and there never will be since the MP has been completely abandoned after the devs released the final map pack dlc which is my personal bigest problem with Doom 2016's MP; The horribly overpriced map packs which split an already small community into half. They could've easilly just monitized the cosmetics in those dlc packs and left the actual maps free for everyone but they didn't, some publishers and devs still haven't learned that paid maps in MP only does damage to that game's player nase, especially if it's a small one.

In short, don't bother getting it for MP, it's dead on PC and the few people on are people buying it during sales and just going on a hacking spree since there's no anti cheat to stop them.

Game also has a mode called snap map, it's pretty much like a sandbox mode that lets you create your own maps and custom game modes, think of it as Forge Mode from Halo Reach in a way or TimeSplitters 3's level edditor but far more complex and better. I've seen people get pretty creative with it, making pac man game modes and even re-creating levels from other games pretty well. It's a fun lil' side thing to check out alone or maybe with some friends since it lets you play those custom player modes/maps with friends. Still i wish we had gotten proper modding tools instead of this simpler sandbox tools but better then nothing i guess, it is kinda neat that all custom content created is shared between all three platforms so if someone on Xbox One makes a pretty cool game mode and map, people on PC and PS4 can also play it.

Bottom line apart from the dead multiplayer, game is kick a**, if you even remotly enjoy first person shooters than you're doing yourself a disservice not picking up Doom [4] 2016.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know how to explain how good this game is to people. It just feels incredible. Everything just fits together. The graphics are gorgeous, one of the best looking games in a long time, but the animation is great too and really fits the feel. Despite the incredible quality it runs better than... well anything. I'm running a decently OCed 970 and I have everything to max at 1920x1200 except for nightmare settings which I cannot do without 6BG of video ram. I haven't even seen a slowdown. It's a serious achievement.

The SP is fantastic, even the glory kills and progression all fits the feel that I was talking. Progression is rooted in two main things: finding secrets/items and killing a lot, so it still works very well within the Doom ideology of old. And the upgrades let you tailor the experience a little, they dont' get in the way at all.

I played the beta for MP, and oddly enough this does seem a little better. I'd still prefer something a little faster and closer to the SP, with quicker deaths, but I'm actually finding myself wanting to go play it.

Perhaps the biggest compliment I can give is that I'm extremely happy that snapmap exists. After playing the game for about 5 minutes I was hooked by the way the game felt to play, I knew I was going to want to do that for a lot longer than even the SP or replays of the SP. So while I'm not happy that full mod support doesn't exist, the ease in which people are able to make new and fun places to play with these fantastic mechanics is appreciated.

I give it a 9.5/10 because I'm not done and maybe I'll feel alittle different by the end. If you like twitch shooting this is about as good as it gets for SP.\t\t\t\t\t
