\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat is DOOM? DOOM isn't just your ordinary shooter, no, DOOM is a fast-paced, action-packed shooter that is never calm. You will always be on the edge of your seat slashing through demons with the large arsenal of weapons you have. DOOM is for people that want to not only kill and shoot but completely annihilate their enemies. This game is the definition of gore but with the amazing soundtrack, it's impossible not to get pumped and enjoy your time.

What doom gets right is the way the game is centered around YOU, the player. In other shooter games, you must take cover, plan your movements strategically and quickly shoot down your enemies before they shoot you down. With DOOM it's different, very different. Here you just rush in like a maniac with your preferred weapon of choice and just all out slaughter everything in sight. Cover is for girly girls, you don't need or want cover while playing DOOM. The healing mechanic allows you to focus solely on killing demons without even worrying about your health. If you like going all in like a madman and just destroying everything in front of you this is a game for you.

There's not much to say about the story or level design. This isn't a game for that. This is a game made to appeal to your primal sense of rage and destruction, which it does perfectly. While the levels are cool and definitely do their best to keep it fresh, most of the time you won't be paying attention to the tiny details. The game also offers a bunch of different enemy types for you to literally slash through each one with its own unique look and attack. In essence, DOOM isn't a deep game, quite the opposite, but this is its strongest point. You just hop on and immediately start enjoying it, no matter if you're a hardcore fan or just a casual playing for an hour on the weekend. The developers did a great job by keeping it simple, yet entertaining for hours.

Verdict: 8.5/10.If you like shooters and destruction give this game a try, you won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

After four years I finally got around to playing this game, and it's really good, even today. Now, I haven't played Eternal, nor do I know much about it at all really, but I can say that if you want a good time killing some demons, this game certainly won't disappoint.

Instead it becomes more about resource management - the game sends a ton of enemies at the end and you must be careful how you defeat each one, lest you run out of ammo or health. This endgame is actually really cathartic and really makes you feel like a badass demon slayer, and you truly can rip and tear your way past this part of the game.
