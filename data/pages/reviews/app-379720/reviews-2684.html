DOOM runs very well, which is a blessing in this age of rushed, unfinished games and buggy early access titles that never seem to come out of beta. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo i've just finished this game and want to give a review:

First of all it's overall a pretty solid shooter game with really nice graphics and sound. The pace of the game is fast (unlike the Doom 3 protagonist who felt as slow as a guy in a wheelchair).

What I liked:
- All the original monsters from the real Doom games (part 3 excluded) are there and they feel basically like them.
- The original weapons find a place in this game and they even put some on top.
- The gore level is what I would expect from a Doom game and it delivered.
- The pacing of the game is fast even though sometimes I was kinda missing a sprint key.
- The story is sufficient enough to give me a second motivation to kill them all (the first motivation is pretty obvious with a Doom game I think)

What bothered me:
For me Bethesda is the 80% company: They have some really nice games in their portfolio and those I played I really liked. But it feels like they are never really giving it the final polish that a game would need to become really great. I see this in Fallout and Skyrim but the fantastic modding support more than redeems that. In this game though I don't see the same modding potential and that brings me to the following list:
- The game is short. I guess there is no need to sugar-coat that fact.
- Weapon upgrades are not a bad idea in general but in this game I think it takes the wrong implementation. To REALLY upgrade all the weapons in the game you need to find a buttload of secrets or have to do special challenges with the respective weapon to unlock the highest tier upgrade.
Personally I see the necessity to upgrade my weapons as much as I can to unlock their true potential. Too bad though that I found myself breaking the flow of the game and roaming around the empty maps after killing everything afterwards. In a game like that this is NOT wnat I want to do.
The weapon challenges are especially annoying when you have to do something to a special kind of enemy that is not overly present in the current map or is surrounded by other enemy types that block you from quickly achieving this challenge.
- To the above mentions point comes that I think there are way too few enemies in the game. I played on the easiest setting after getting my ass handed by the enemies in the first maps on a higher difficulty due to their bullet sponginess and extreme damage output. I think this is very Doom-unlike.
- In my opinion there should be enemies that are as strong as on the second hardest setting but more.. way more.
- I was shocked to see how pathetic the DOOM-guy was in the first maps. Just 20 shotgun shells and you have to get really, really close to kill an Imp.. c'mon.
- In later maps (and after scrounding alot for secrets :/) I became almost godlike which made fight too easy. You don't have this in the original Doom games and it should not have been included in here.
- Calling this game just "DOOM".... I mean really. Everyone knows that MicroSoft cannot cound correctly, but I expected more from you guys. Don't listen to the marketing monkeys. I know that monkeys through feces around when they want to demonstrate dominance but that doesn't mean you have to catch it all and re-use it.

All in all I would have given this game a higher rating if it was a bit more like the original Doom games in a better graphics but i'm still willing to give it a 7/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a gorgeous looking FPS that very much epitomizes the "run and gun" style. Literally. The game punishes you for being a static shooter and benefits you if constantly in motion, yet gameplay never feels choppy or a blur.

The campaign is done extremely well for this style of game. A fantastic stream of brutal takedowns, fun gunplay, challenging battles and steady drip of a well done story that doesn't feel skimped but also does not bog you down from playing. This is a shooter that a lot of other shooters should take notes from.

Additionally the map creater system is easy to use and allows for creation of some pretty fun maps.

My only negative is a partial one towards the multiplayer. Graphics maintain well and gunplay remains pretty much the same. Strangely this doesn't entirely translate well I think mostly because the style is too familiar and the game doesn't offer any particularly unique game mode to stand apart. This isn't to say it is bad, it just isn't the point I could sell this game on as it offers nothing unique for FPS games.

Overall my scoring would be 9.5/10 with the half point demotion for the well done but non-unique multiplayer. Everything else from gameplay, graphics, music, story and map creater I give this game great credit for and highly recommend its purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest fps as always... very intense and you must be good if you want to survive, as usual in DOOM games. Loved all that gore that somehow, dissapeared from games. It's not so claustrophobic like DOOM 3 (the only game I couldn't play for more than 45 minutes per session when I use to play 13 hours straight), and there are no secrets rooms activated by a switch which usually released many demons to fight with, or a change on the floor level, sending you to smaller bottom floors to start the carnage, or to wide open areas, there is no back fire either, soooo usual but absolutelly unexpected changes of the world of all DOOM games. Suprised me quite a lot that enemies appears always in front of you and almost never behind or above. That actually takes out from the game a lot of suspense to become a more gore-action-like game, so this DOOM doesn't give the creeps, nor I have not seen any inverted cross or the face of Christ as in older doom games but it has lots of action and raw gore. Hope next time id sofware be more risky and do something really obscure, like they used to do with the atmosphere of the game (as well, mouse sensivity should be increased more than the 100%). Game's music is SUPERB at its best; it is raw, fast, accurate, brutal, powerful, owhervelmingly destructive just like DOOM. Hope id hire him again for next DOOM game.

Of course, this game worths every penny, and it will enssure you lots of fun and intense action with tons of gore as never seen before.\t\t\t\t\t
