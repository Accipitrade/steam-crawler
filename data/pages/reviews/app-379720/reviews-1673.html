\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat's to say that hasn't already been said? Game runs like a dream on a 1050ti despite everything else being pretty much 10-15 years old in my rig. The guys and gals at id know what they're doing and it's exactly what you want out of an updated Doom. There is probably some intense pressure at HQ to ensure that anything with the name Doom on it is really good, and each entry has been a phenomenal release. While it had a pretty lackluster multiplayer open beta, the full release Doom 2016 is a landmark game in its own right.

Whatever distracted me 4 years ago from playing it has now been rectified. I had loads of fun looking for secrets and just clearing out rooms. Unlike the Dooms of old, you go from arena to arena and more often than not, they get you all patched up and filled with ammo before they unleash the horde on you. Obviously, you can play at harder difficulties if that's not your cuppa. It's always interesting how they craft a narrative around "demons from hell are invading". Where Doom 3 took the survival horror route, this one was all about getting that classic aesthetic mixed with a modern feel. No stopping to rest until the horde is dead, no hiding somewhere to heal. Keep moving and shooting whatever the hell is in your pockets, switching on the fly as needed. It's pure adrenaline bursts mixed with short moments of downtime, and it's gorgeous as hell. The texture work is absolutely mind-blowing in its attention to detail. Get this under your belt before Eternal drops!\t\t\t\t\t
