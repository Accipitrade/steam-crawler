\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, first of all, as you can see, I have 800+ hours in this game. The majority of those hours are spent in multiplayer. I believe this MP to be one of the most addictive and fun MP games in quite a long time. The day one reviews of the multiplayer were made by people who were really bad at the game and didn't understand the idea of an arena shooter, and as such, bashed this multiplayer as being, "nothing special". Which I believe to be 100% wrong. They didn't understand not being able to regen health or armour, and actually having to participate in a large scavenger hunt to find these things, as well as competiting for the mega health (100 health/100 armour).

A lot of people don't like the idea of demon runes being in the multiplayer, but I think they play a large role in making the multiplayer what it is. It helps newer players stay hooked to the game and helps even the odds against us veteran players, seeing as if you can control the demon, you can control the flow of a match.

There's a loadout system in play in this DOOM, which strays from the original DOOM element, but takes hints from COD and other newer FPS games on the market, which some people like, and some people dislike. I personally love this, seeing as I don't have to go and control a zone waiting for a vortex rifle to spawn. In all actuality, the only weapons that spawn on a map are the BFG, Gauss Rifle, and Chainsaw, which all spawn at 8:15 in their respective locations. These locations are typically quite combatted. The same goes for the Powerups that spawn at the same timestamp; this includes, Haste, Quad Damage, and Invisibility.

Now here's the issue of the DOOM servers, which are actually pretty bad, making it one of the few issues I have come to find in this game. The servers crash on me probably once in every twenty-five or so matches, which may not sound like a lot, but it's frequent enough to take note of. There's also an issue of using the in-game party system, where you can invite friends from your Steam list to join you; while this feature works a lot of the time, it will sometimes completely crash the joinee's game. causing them to have to force close it. The only way to combat this issue is by reforming the party and having a different player send the invite.

There is also no anti-hack dectection in this game, and while the hackers are extremely obvious, there's no way to kick them, or report them (aside from knowing devs and sending them messages), even then, it seems as if nothing is done about it. This makes for some frustrating gameplay, and can completely turn off new players to the game. On the bright side, there doesn't seem to be too many hackers from what I've noticed.

Lastly, there's the issue of the small MP player base. This can make it hard to find matches in anything aside from Team Deathmatch and Deathmatch, completely blowing off all the other great game modes. As well as making it difficult to find full matches or different people to play with, meaning if you're getting your *** handed to you by a maxed player and you're brand new, it might not be too easy to get away from that maxed player, try as you might.

Overall, I would rate the MP in this game as a solid 8/10, only taking points away for the Server issues and lack of Anti-Hack detection. I would still highly reccomend this game to anybody looking for an amazing FPS shooter who wants to change up the pace from the modern day shooters.\t\t\t\t\t
