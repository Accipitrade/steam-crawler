\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a fast paced platforming shooter. I find it odd that platforming isn't one of the tags for this game considering it makes up a large portion of the game and there are entire segments dedicated to it.

Doom is a decent shooter and it plays well for the most part. The problem is it has a ton of small issues that weigh the game down. There are more issues than what I'm listing.

1. Gameplay. Most of the game is you going into a room, room locksdown, you'll kill a bunch of enemies, room will unlock, you'll roam around a couple rooms collecting stuff then go into a new room that'll lockdown and repeat ad infinitum. The campaign is essentially a giant horde mode where you get to run around a couple rooms collecting stuff in between each round. It is lifeless.

2. Most fights in the game feel the exact same as they play out in the same exact manner and leave the game feeling devoid of any thoughtful twists in the gun play. Wave 1: A bunch of weak enemies spawn, a bunch of mid-weak enemies spawn, 1 or 2 mid strength enemies spawn. Wave 2: A couple weak enemies spawn, a bunch of mid-weak enemies spawn, a couple mid strength enemies spawn, 1 or 2 mid-high strength enemies spawn. Wave 3: A few weak enemies spawn, a few mid-weak enemies spawn, a couple mid strength enemies spawn, a couple mid-high strength enemies spawn, 1 or 2 high strength enemies spawn. End of round. Every..... single.... round. Once you start facing off against Barons of Hell (supposedly the biggest baddies in the game) guess when they're going to spawn in? At the final wave of a round.

3. Joke enemies. Remember the Revenant from Doom 2 that was a glass cannon enemy capable of firing heat-seeking rockets at you and messing you up. He was an enemy you'd focus your time on taking down because he was a big threat. For some reason they decided to turn him into a clown in this game. He spends most of his time shooting a barrage of 4-10 rockets out at absolutely nothing. I've sat there and watched him shoot a barrage of missiles and none of them came close to me. He's the last enemy I focus on unless he happens to be right in front of me. The basic zombie enemies are a bigger threat than he is. Likewise, Barons of Hell typically only show up during the final waves of battle and are incredibly easy to leave til the very end and then take down. This is supposed to be the big baddie of the regular enemies and he's a joke. Who designed these two and who approved them? You could remove them from the game and lose nothing.

4. It's an easy game even on Ultra-Violence. By two thirds of the way through the game I just wanted it to end.

5. I get that nowadays mindless ADD gamers need some gimmick to keep them hooked into a game so that they'll keep playing and say it's amazing but it's simply annoying for those of us who want actual good gameplay. I'm of course talking about the glory kills which you will mindlessly and repetitively use throughout the game as it is greatly encouraged by saving you ammo as well as giving you health and armor every time you use it. It also prevents enemies from regenerating health, which they'll do if you fail to shoot them to death or glory kill them during the glory kill duration. Just like every other game that has a gimmick like this, it's ok the first time or two you use it, but by the 8000th time it's tiresome. Of course, mindless gamers can't get enough. â€œOH WOW!!!!!! Did you see what I did to that enemy?â€ â€œYeah, I saw it the first 300 times you did it, it hasn't gotten any better.â€ Especially annoying is that sometimes the game finishes a glory kill by having you come out of the animation staring at the ground or the sky. Thanks game, just what I wanted to be looking at in the middle of this firefight and now have to reorient myself.

6. After the 1st level, every level has 3 optional challenges for you to complete that gives you points towards upgrading your stuff. Some of these challenges are incredibly annoying, not because they're difficult, but because the method of completing them are broken. Some challenges want you to perform the â€œdeath from aboveâ€ glory kill on certain enemies. The problem is that the game decides at complete random whether you actually did this or not. You can be goomba stomping an enemy while performing a glory kill on them and the game will simply not count it as a death from above. Likewise, another challenge requires you to snap the necks of certain enemies but this is again completely broken. You can have your reticle directly pointed on the front or back of the neck/head of these enemies and rather than snapping it's neck, the game will do some other random glory kill instead. Another challenge has you doing leg sweeps (or some leg related glory kill, can't quite remember) on certain enemies. Of course, you can be staring directly at their legs and press the button to initiate the glory kill and it'll perform some other random glory kill instead. Simple suggestion to the developers. If you can't get your game to recognize specific glory kills, then don't make specific challenges to complete them. Should be simple game design decisions 101. Apparently these developers slept through that class.

7. This game is a platformer as much as it is a shooter. I've got no clue who out there was demanding that a Doom game become a platformer but you and the developers should be shoved into a brazen bull and have it lit. The platforming is annoying when you latch onto platforms you don't mean to latch onto or fail to latch onto platforms that you do attempt to latch onto. There are instadeath pits everywhere in this game and it gets tiresome having to deal with them nonstop. Going for any secret over an instadeath pit is an excruciating experience since you can't manually save the game. Ooooo, I see some green armor over there. Should be an easy jump. Oh, the bottom of my foot apparently touched the railing after I jumped and now my guy has dropped out of light speed and has come to essentially a dead stop over this insta death pit. YAY!!!!! Now I get to play the last 10 minutes again.

8. The game contains some bugs. Not sure if any are game breaking, but they're at least somewhat annoying. Do you like falling through the map? Because you could be a winner in the fall through the map contest. Your reward? Death. I also apparently blew the game's mind one time. I went into a building, got lockdowned, killed the enemies inside, lockdown ended. I decided to leave by going back through the front door instead of exiting out the back door like the game expected me to. I found an alternate way to the back door of the building while running around the environment. I decided to go into it and it was an airlock. The outdoors side of the airlock closes and the indoor side of the airlock opens into the building and a bunch of enemies suddenly spawn outside the building, causing the building to lockdown and me to be stuck inside permanently. Had to restart my last checkpoint. Apparently the game figures you're going to exit through the backdoor of the building through the airlock and so it's supposed to spawn those enemies as the airlock is opening to the outside.

Doom is still an above average game even with all the problems I've listed here. The issue is why would you bother playing an above average game when you could be playing good, very good, or great ones? It's a game that could've been much better if the developers hadn't gotten lazy in their design.\t\t\t\t\t
