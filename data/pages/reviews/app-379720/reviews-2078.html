On a scale from 1-10 where 5 is average, DOOM is an 8.5

The gunplay is tight, every kill feels rewarding, and you are constantly blasting around grabbing powerups and health. You are constantly rewarded for being AS AGGRESSIVE AS POSSIBLE, through the glory kill mechanic. If you get any demon low to the point where they start blinking and you melele them, a masterful mini cutscene plays. Punching through a cacodemon’s eye, curb stomping a demon's face, f***ing slitting a demons throat with its own horn, and after health rains out of the exploding carcass. DOOM is graphic, it is fast paced, you are going to have to do some clutch saves, and you will die A LOT. Do not let that discourage you, play on a higher difficulty than what you are used too, it feels amazing when you finally get good at plowing through a horde of ugly a** monsters.

The exploration is exactly at the level it should be. DOOM is not an open world game, it is strictly level based, but this is a great thing. There is rarely ever a boring moment, unlike in open world games where you are sometimes just wandering around, wondering what to do next or endlessly searching for collectibles. Speaking of collectables there are a couple of secrets and a handful of collectables in every level, just enough to scratch that itch. It never feels monotonous, and most of your exploration is when you're doing parkour from one area to the next.

The story is about as complex as this sentence. DOOMGUY is here to kill destroy ANNIHILATE as many demons as possible. In the first sequence, DOOMGUY literally pushes the story out of his face to go destroy demons. If story is important to you, this is not your game; however, DOOM is amazing without a story, if not because of its lack of story.

I have not played much of DOOM’s online multiplayer, maybe around 10 hours. My experience with online is that it is great, the gunplay is just as tight as the singleplayer and it feels really balanced. Having the game be 3 years old at this point means that many players are max level and will kick your s*** in, but having the game being so balanced means you will still be able to get the jump on these high level players every once in a while.

Biggest problems with DOOM. Being an 8.5 out of 10 means there are a couple things holding DOOM back. It can be a little buggy at times, there have been a decent amount of times where I have cleared an area but the game didn't realize it so I was stuck in purgatory forcing me to load a previous save. I’ve clipped through the floor a couple times, and even died for what seemed like a weird collision bug. The biggest problem I had was the final boss. After this amazing journey I went through tearing my way through Mars, its moons, and hell, why was the final boss a godd*** spider? Spider Mastermind was just kinda lame! She didn't look cool, and the fight was relatively boring. The previous bosses were MUCH better

Buy it. Buy DOOM. You’re gonna have a good time with your trusty chainsaw tearing demons into pulp.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a first person environmental puzzle game where you go from one arena to the next with some platforming in-between. It is soooo based on a simple formula that sometimes it even took me out of immersion because it slaped me in the face. Non the less, Doom is a great game if you are into some gory demon slaying.

Pros\t
  • Great, action packed soundtrack
  • Good looking game that runs well too (especially with Vulkan)
  • Adrenalin pumping action arenas in single-player and servicable multi-player
  • Lot of secrets and side activities for those who like collectibles
  • Boss fights are awesome (it has 3), except...

Cons\t
  • The final boss, who is a push-over compared to the first 2 (those had multiple phases; the final one didn't)
  • Movement was strange at first - you slide more than you walk
  • Whole game is made with one very simple formula and it shows sometimes (can get stale)
