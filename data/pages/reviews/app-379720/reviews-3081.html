\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*** Cannot Reccomend because of always on DRM ***

Wow is this game good.I have been down on first person shooters for years. With crappy regenerating health, two weapon slots, etc etc yes everybody's gone here before. I have to confess that I don't do multiplayer games so I can't comment myself in that regard. So yes this is old school runny round shooty fun, key card hunts, secret areas and well executed first person platforming sections. I'm running the game on ultra settings, with the GTX 690 and for the most part it silky smooth. Unfortunately there is frame dropping at the most inconvenient moments and although I haven't had a hard crash, after an hour or so gameplay, I have to restart the game because it throws hissy fit and runs like a PowerPoint presentation.

So it just breaks my heart to say: that when I started the game today I got a message which said "unable to connect to Doom servers" – so it's got always on DRM it seems. I hope I'm wrong and the game needed to contact the servers for another reason and it would not start-up because Bethesda totally wouldn't have included always on DRM, but as it stands I CANNOT RECOMMEND THIS GAME. Yes I think it's brilliant, but I personally will not entertain an always on DRM scheme and I will not let it fly no matter how good the game is.

I find it sad that game reviews do not point these sorts of things out. It was the same with Grand Theft Auto Five. Great game let down draconian DRM. However, as far as I'm aware not one game reviewer pointed this out.

If Bethesda do patch the online DRM out I will gladly recommend this game to anybody, place it on a pedestal and worship it to the end of time!\t\t\t\t\t

DooM made it into modern times
(My impressions of the Single Player mode)!

The original DooM made it`s way into my heart as the first PC game i ever played and i had a ton of fun with it. Many people disregard DooM as a mindless gorefest but for me it was always a test of my skills and reflexes as i dodged fireballs and bullets alike in my quest to kick demon ass and revenge Doomguy`s pet.

The reboot is basicly just that. And i love it for it! Fast paced action, exploration and secret hunting are back, just like in the good old days. DooM avoids many modern mechanics like the plague and for the better if you ask me: no forced characterisation of your protagonist for example. Doomguy actually could care less for the motives of the other characters and the UAC in general. He`s just pissed beyond belive that he has to deal with hell´s forces again. A welcome diffrence to the "patriot-one-man-army" of many shooters today, especialy anything with a military touch. Just to give you a example: after getting up and reclaiming your armor you are contacted by one of the caracters via a small screen. Instead of listening Doomguy just smashes the thing. No monolog about anything. How refreshing. And cover is overrated, just don`t get hit. No tactical wall-hugging here!

No regenerating health, of course. Heathpacks are there but not in a huge number. Don`t expect any handholding in this game. Keeping your self alive can be prolonged with the help of Glory Kills. Basicly a finisher after staggering the unlucky hellspawns. This will grant you a certain amount health (a small amount if not in critical condition, like 5 HP on UV per pickup, spawning not too many thou) and if your a bit lucky, ammo as well. The whole Glory Kill System is very neatly implemented: not once i felt i was stuck in a animation and about to get obliterated by the next enemy. It`s fast, bloody and suprisingly dosen`t get old. Even when unscrewing the head of a Possesed for the 100st time it still feels fast and fitting.

Speaking of finishers: the chainsaw is a beast. it is more of a tactial element instead of a standard weapon, since fuel in finite and kinda rare (because chainsaws on mars, thats why). It`s nicely balanced with the rest of the arsenal and can save you from the brink of death if used correctly. Also the fastest way to get your ammo back if you are with the back to the wall. What about the BFG? Just get a hold of it and see for your self... trust me, it`s worth the wait just fireing this beast for the first time yourself. I couldn`t stop grinning after that.

Movement feels realy smooth and fast, no need for any kind of stealth. Remember, we are talking about DooM here. The addition of a double jump feels nice and adds to the over all feel of the game. Since movement is the only thing that keeps you alive most of the time, it is nice to see that the devs gave the player enough possibilities to ensure they get around and don`t get toasted.

The weapons are realy cool looking, my personal favorite is by far the Gauss Cannon. And they feel powerful. Fireing the Gauss Cannon for example will force you back one or two steps since the recoil is massive, but the projectile impact is massive as well. Newtons law of forces or something like that! Like i said, the gunplay is just as satisfying as looks, and many upgrades wait to be discovered.

Whitch brings me to the modernisations of DooM in this reboot: Upgrades. Collect certain items you can find in the levels to upgrade your suit to your liking and earn weapon upgrades by first finding drones then earning points for finding secrets, combat and for the completion of challenges. Collectable secrets also earn you weapon upgrade points and keeps you invested in your arsenal. You can compete in Rune challenges to get a further edge against the demonic ♥♥♥♥, like better control over your double jumps for example. And keep looking for those classic DooM levels!

The level design is realy old school, but with additional verticality. Secrets can be overlooked if not careful. The artdesign is not to serious about itself, don`t think this one is edgy or anything. Sometimes i can see some form of twisted humor in it that i can deeply appreciate. The monsters are all instantly recognizeable with their old-school counterparts but are unique in their own right. Animations are smooth as butter and over all the game just looks gorgeous. Hell is without a doubt one of the visual highlights in my opinion.

Sound design is on top of it as well. Explosions sound full and volumenous, the guns sound like an orchestra of destruction and the demons sound menacing as well. The music fits the game and dosent sound tryhard or anything, in my opinion. But music is always subjective so keep in mind this is my own personal view on things.

The battles against the demons are no walk in the park as you can guess. The game puts you against compositions of enemies that get progressivly tricky to deal with as the game progresses. Sometimes it can get quite frantic and hectic but in this moments the game realy shines as it IS quite hard at times but never unfair (played on Ultra-Violence so far, Nightmare is next!).

But it`s not perfect. The flow of the maps can be quite predictable (corridor with pick ups - arena fight- corridor with pick ups - arena fight... you get the idea). But otherwise this game is a truly faithfull reboot of the source material and is highly recommended for everyone who is looking for a single player experience like in the good old days but with the shell, feel and look of a modern title.
If you liked the latest Shadow Warrior or Hard Reset you HAVE to give this one a shot!

Singleplayer: 9/10, would go to hell again.

Editors note: Please be patient with me if you find any spelling mistakes since english is my 3rd language.

Edit: I can not verify that this game has an always online DRM. I played just fine without any network connection at all. NO ALWAYS ON DRM, for me at least.\t\t\t\t\t
