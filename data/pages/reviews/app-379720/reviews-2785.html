\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game that perfectly embraces its origins and mixes with just the right dose of common additions in the genre. Doom 2016 brings back the fast pace gameplay many fans knows from its first game, and although with a basic plot is easy to ignore as you can rushdown through the areas excluding a very scarce cinematics. Like in the original you will carry several weapons not requiring to reload, keeping the adrenaline pumping as you devastate hordes of enemies. Is a bit formulaic as most action sequences will be in closed rooms until you defeat a number of waves of demons, but even so you will be waiting for the next one while passing through a brief relaxed area.

With various secrets and unlockables to find in each level, the online community still lively in both multiplayer and custom maps, and high replay value as the game leaves you asking for more, Doom 2016 is a must have even if you are new to the genre. I have a bit of a problem with the campaign and multiplayer being two separate programs but running in the same menu instead of being individually in your library, but is more of a small nitpick. The true negative however is for completionists, as some achievements are only available in the DLC which is very optional as is just multiplayer content and you can still play DLC maps without it.

Even though i am not a fan of single player FPS, Doom 2016 is a wonderful game that brings back what many games of its genre lacks nowadays.\t\t\t\t\t

But no, this one is boring. I almost gave up a few levels in because nothing really happened. You kill a few demons here and there and you move on. It's not all bad though. The mouse control is the best I have seen in years. No need to get used to insane mouse smoothing or other crap, it just works.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a game franchise that is known for fun, fluid, and pure visceral gameplay, this one certainly delivers it on a whole new level.

Pros:
+ The entire game is jam packed with action. There are absolutely no boring and drawn-out segments. You are here to kill demons from hell like a badass, and nothing more.

+ Your entire arsenal is useful, so there isn't a single gun that I find inferior and don't use during combat ( aside from the pistol ). You'll constantly be switching between each gun while you're plowing your way through each level. This keeps the gameplay interesting and fluid.

+ The level design is great. Each area has its own environmental structure.

+ A pretty good variety of enemies.

+ Doom has a story, but it never feels intrusive to the gameplay. There are barely any cutscenes in the game.

+ Loads of secrets and little collectibles to find throughout each level.

Mixed:

+/- The campaign has a pretty decent length. I managed to beat it in around 10 hours on the "Ultra-violence" difficulty ( which is the highest difficulty setting on your first playthrough. You unlock harder difficulties after you beat the game ). I personally wouldn't spend $60 on this game if you plan on playing through it only once, but that is obviously up to your discretion.

+/- The performance could dip sometimes. A handful of people seem to have major problems with the optimization, but for the most part, it runs pretty well for me.

Cons:
