\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe single player experience of DOOM might be the best for an FPS in years. I played then original DOOM and DOOM 2, but they were a bit before my time and I grew up playing more modern shooters. So when I say the DOOM single player experience is the best in years, I'm not looking at it though nostalgia goggles. For pretty much the past few years or so, every single FPS has ether been a clone of halo or cod. The mechanics might have been framed in different ways, but they were at the core, the same. DOOM is different. In DOOM when you're health is low you don't take cover and wait for regen. Instead to recover you health, you need to find a pick up or kill a demon. Mechanics like this facilitate fast and aggressive gameplay. You will feel like a badass when planning this game. Match the mechanics with sprawling levels full of exploration, and multi level beautifully designed combat arenas and you have an FPS that is and entirely a fresh and new experiences, but still manages to be a call back to the originals in a genre. The DOOM multiplayer gets a bad rap, but It is miss understood. Transitioning from the DOOM campaign to multiplayer and vise versa was difficult even through the mechanics were basically the same. Unlike the single player, the DOOM multiplayer is much less aggressive. If you health is low, you look for pick ups before engaging enemies. Unlike other modern FPS, gun fights in DOOM multiplayer aren't just dependent upon who pulls the trigger first. With larger health pools and the potential for pick ups to instantly restore lost health the tide of battle can turn pretty quickly, and the fight isn't determined within the first second. Is the DOOM multiplayer the best? Probably not, and it definitely wouldn't stand on its own, but it's a good addition that rounds out the game to modern FPS standards. The map editing tools are pretty fun to use, and I've played some pretty awesome user created maps. Snapmap is probably the part of DOOM I put the least amount of time into, but it gives people to opportunity to create and play with some awesome things, and as far as I'm concerned it's just extra to an already complete game. If you need a deep narrative that pulls at the heart and a protagonist that you can bond with, DOOM probably isn't for you. If you need a game that's easy, calming, and relaxing, DOOM probably isn't for you. How ever if you are at all a fan of the FPS genre and believe, like I do, that the most important part of a video game is that it should be fun, than you need to get this game.\t\t\t\t\t
