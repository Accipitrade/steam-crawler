\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was expecting not to like it, but it was surprisingly good.
It doesn't quite feel like Doom 1 or 2, but it is leaps and bounds better than Doom 3.
It is a great single player game that for some reason the devs keep trying to make into a multiplayer shooter.
The multiplayer is mostly crap.
Seriously they cramed that into the game then tried to throw three multiplayer DLCs at players, no idea what they are thinking.

Graphics are great and runs great on PC, which was a surprise as my PC is starting to get on with age.
Pacing does a good job at throwing new areas, weapons, and enemies at you when things start to get boring.
lots of secrets and collectables.
perhaps too many secrets and collectables, kinda got a bit distracting chasing down Doomguy Figurines and staring at the map in the middle of an invasion from hell.
Weapons have satisfying secondary attacks, but unfortunatly the primary attacks feel a bit underwelming.
Enemies also seem to have a bit of a bullet sponge feel to them.
The scripted "glory kill" press F to kill enemies in a silly fashion felt a bit forced and out of place, it is almost required for health and ammo. Would have made more sense if it only happened with Berserk...
As the game goes on you get forced into an increasing number of situations where you get locked into large rooms that feel like Arena shooter multiplayer maps and enemies teleport in wave after wave. It feels stupid and forced and unlike the classic Doom games where enemies didn't just materialize into existance until you killed enough of them.
Supershotgun is still the best weapon
A lot of talk about the soundtrack, but it didn't really have any great effect on me. No track is memorable unlike those from the classic games.
They included the classic levels as fun little easter eggs, unfortunatly when occupied with the new bullet sponge enemies it just makes them feel wrong.

Despite the few misteps it is overall a pretty enjoyable singleplayer game and worth buying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMeet the new boss, same as the old boss. Lacking innovation is often viewed as a detriment for series that haven't seen any installments in a decade. Somehow, though, this seems to work in DOOM's favour. id Software distilled the franchise down to its essence and improved on it. It hits that sweet spot of swift movement, meaty gunplay and melee combat, and updated graphics and sound design. Even after hours of repetitively blasting and punching through hordes of demons, the game still maintains its edge, aided by the inspired soundtrack by Mick Gordon. The OST features chugging guitar riffs alongside industrial synths and dynamically adjusts itself according to the situation of the player.

The Single Player campaign is about 13 hours in length and includes an unlockable Ultra Nightmare mode. The game has traditional multiplayer modes and SnapMap, which allowed players to try their hands at user-generated levels. Multiplayer also features an upgrade system which the developers (unfortunately) chose to lock customization options for SnapMap behind, making it more difficult to differentiate maps.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDamn it, this game is incredible. But, it has some huge problems.
To me, this game is 50% perfect, and 50% garbage.
The perfect part is the actual combat. You know it, I know it, this game is so amazingly awesome on that front.
The other 50% of this game though is a dealbreaker.
Okay, so, the mapping system is a mess. The levels include verticality, which is great, but the maps in this game include the entire map at once and you have a hard time figuring out where you're going or where you need to go or where some items are. Guys, come on, other games had this figured out already! Show the level by dividing up each vertical point! It makes things so much less cluttered and confusing.
Another huge thing: this game only allows you to save via checkpoints. ♥♥♥♥. That. ♥♥♥♥. I should be able to quit out of my game and resume exactly where I left off so i don't have to find all the ammo and armor i found earlier again.
This is especially a pain when the game forces you to traverse half the level every time you die in a difficult fight. Yes, that happened to me.
And the third horrible part about this game is the secrets system. The levels are scattered with tons of various things that will help you in some way. My problem with this? It's BORING! No one wants to spend an hour looking around the map to find collectible dolls, a 50 armor pack and whatever else the game provides. Sometimes, secrets are hidden too well, and it's a pain trying to figure out reaching some other things like argent energy, the rune trials or those things that allow modifiers for your weapons. Yes, you can mostly avoid these things but that leaves you as weak as a piece of paper later in the game. Also, I really like how the game refuses to allow you into earlier parts of its levels after reaching certain points. 10/10 game design guys really. It's not like I wanted to come back there later.

*sigh* get this game if you want I guess. I for one can't forgive it for the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ it throws at me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh Doom, many a teenage hour wasted on this bad boy. I even remember its multi player being my first ever Dial up multi player game played vs my mate on Death match mode.
All this said I got the reboot on a sale and decided to play it on Ultra-Violence. I was a bit rusty and could finish it on the hardest setting as a kid so as an adult I should go for the challenge.....

Right so lets get this straight.. Doom is fast. Really fast.
Usain Bolt comming at you in a rocket propelled Bugatti Veyron fast.
In the later levels its very unforgiving as well. You have to time your quad damage / berserk power ups to perfection or get overwhealmed. BFG and Chainsaw anything that gets close or certain demons (for me Summoners and Pinkies.)

This is also the first game where i had to limit my game play, anythng over half an hour and I found myself seriously fried. I would come off the game feeling quite shaky as the adrenaline still pumped. The speed of the game coupled with the plodding Industrial sound track made this a real treat to play.

My one complaint might be the lack of government health warning and the pretty lame story line... wait this is Doom.. story line not inluded / needed.
Quite enjoyed my play through but after rushing the last 3 levels its time for a cup of camomile tea and to listen to the Carpenters before calling an ambulance for my inevitable heart attack in about 20 minutes time.

In short... Doom rebooted still gives the thrill it did when I was 13.\t\t\t\t\t
