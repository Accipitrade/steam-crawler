\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne Of The Most Satisfying FPS Experiences To Date - 10/10

1. Gameplay - 10/10

Comment:

Smooth. One of the most satisfying experiences. More than half the reason to play this game is for how well it plays...hands down. It's an experience.

2. Story/Lore - 10/10

Side note regarding story elements:

To be clear this is not a game changing story arch. The character development is questionable and the story progression is organic at best. However you DO NOT pick up a game like DOOM for its story. Having said that, the story is absolutely perfect for what context you have in mind when wanting to play something like DOOM.

In order to fully immerse yourself in the world and its story elements you have to read these data modules you pick up throughout the game. I honestly didn't mind reading this stuff once I became invested into the game.

Depending on how quickly you read or if you like reading at all could really make or break the story and the lore for you. You will be picking up a ton of these data modules throughout the campagin. I noticed if you read each module as you acquire it, which is fairly quickly, it could interrupt your gameplay experience if you read slowly. If you are a reading maniac you can get through them fairly quickly and it would probably add some overall satisfaction to your overall experience.

3. Music/Audio Production - 10/10

Comment on music & sound:

The music matches perfectly with what you're looking at on screen. The sound FX and overall atmospheric sounds are really satisfying as well. There's nothing quite like ripping a demon in half while hearing 20 others howling in the background running towards you among the blasting music. So satisfying.

4. Level Design - 10/10

Comment:

Nothing ever felt bland. These were well thought out levels with enough areas to explore and secrets to collect. The choice of colors and props really added to the mood in each level.

5. Steam Achievements - 10/10

Comment:

Not really a big deal. But it pains me sometimes when games have near impossible achievements to acquire. DOOM had fair enough challenging achievments which were fun to work towards throughout the experience.

Final Note:

I enjoyed this game much more than I thoguht I would prior to buying it. FPS campaigns have been a hit or miss for me at times. I'm more strictly about the multiplayer aspect of FPS. So this was definitely a breath of fresh air when it came to a quality single player FPS campaign.

DOOM has reinvigorated FPS campaigns in myself once again!

Highly recommend this game for anyone looking for a riviting experience which requires your attention, focus, and creativity.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf Doom 3 was a reboot of the original Doom, then Doom 4 - or Doom 2016, or DOOM, or whatever I am supposed to call it- feels more ike a reboot to Doom 2. For all its gory and fast-paced action, the first Doom game was equally drenched in atmosphere; with its (at the time very sophisticated) lighting effects, it was at times a genuinely creepy game, with moments of quiet suspense that inevitably lead to balls-to-the-wall gunfights as soon as you crossed some invisible trigger. Doom 2, on the other hand, forsook much of this flavor in favor of gimmicky levels and a focus on the gameplay. Personally, I consider the former the better game, but I can understand the appeal of the second.

Doom 4 is not without its own character and mood, but it is obvious that these played second fiddle to the combat. The story is shallow even for a Doom game (although interestingly, it appears to be a sequel of sorts to the first game), and its environmental design never seems to stretch much beyond the basic "hi-tech base covered in blood" motif. It's all very well detailed, but not particularly imaginative and it would have been nice to see the developers stretch a bit beyond the tired tropes that have been carrying the game since 1994.

Instead, the developers focused on refining the gameplay, and I'm not sure it was entirely to the game's advantage. In order to make the game play "faster", they added in a "glory kill" system (essentially, a coup de grace melee attack) which is the primary way of earning ammo and health pick-ups. Unfortunately, melee combat in first-person shooters is generally awful, and Doom 4 does little to improve it; the only difference is that it melee combat is mandatory in this game if you don't want to die. I was never comfortable with the system and made as little use of it as I could, finding ranged attacks both more effective and less dangerous. Oddly, I found that while the glory kill system made the combat feel more hectic, it did little to speed up the game (much of which was slowed down by the inevitable after-combat crawl for power-ups and secrets anyway).

In another interesting similarity to Doom 2, Doom 4 also featured a tremendous amount of platforming. First-person jumping wasn't any fun back in 1996, and it hasn't improved an iota in the two decades since. I was flabbergasted at how much jumping over bottomless pits this game contained, since first-person platforming is almost universally loathed; it just seems such an unusual addition.

I was not particularly impressed by the graphics. Don't get me wrong; Doom 4 is a pretty game, but Id's tech has been the benchmark against which other engines and games are measured. Doom 4, on the other hand, was only average in appearance; there are far prettier games on the market. Having said that, Id does deserve kudos for how well it OPTIMIZED that engine; even running on a three year old GPU (and a five year old CPU), the game ran at a constant 60FPS with almost all the settings on Ultra.

In the end, I found Doom 4 a mixed bag. Like Doom 2, I can understand its appeal to some people, but it felt far too one-dimensional for my taste; even Doom 3 - for all its problematic monster closets - felt a fresher and more interesting game. Nonetheless, I must admit that for all that the game did not appeal to me, it is generally a well-developed title, so I reluctantly give it my recommendation, albeit with the caveat that it is an acquired taste.\t\t\t\t\t

I just wish there was more content in the storyline and some good in game cinematics. Its an old skool game with a retro style. Yes it is as if you were punching Satan in the balls. Doom is one of the best single player game out there that doesnt hold your hand.

There are occasional confusion when running around the maps and looking around for key cards. However this doesnt detract you from the real game, which is action pack and difficulty gets harder and harder as you progress. The end game is really worth it, you would have to use everything in your disposal and use every skill and effort to get through, its true hell.

Buy this game or atleast support it and have it in your library.Mind you that this game may cause disorientation because of the speed and pace, but when you get used to it, it doesnt effect you anymore.\t\t\t\t\t
