\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo begin, I need to make clear that my opinion of DOOM varies based on which of the three parts of this game is being discussed, for that reason, I'm going split this review into three parts.

Campaign: 10/10
The campaign for DOOM is spectaculer. Prior to buying I had heard about its excellence, and had attached an almost unfair level of expectation to it. Despite this, it still delivered. Upon the first five minutes gameplay the player is already in a fast paced battle situation that's involves 3-dimensional fighting with the forces of hell. Never is there a slow down experianced that is too long, any cutscene witnessed always lasts less than two minutes and is worth watching simply for the attention to detail. Any complaints I could think to add about the campaign are not game breaking, and honestly aren't even worth the time to mention.

Multiplayer: 3.5/10
Looking back on the hours I spent with the multiplayer, my most common thought is, "was this made y the same people who made the campaign?". Honestly I don't know what the heck they were thinking. While the excellent 3d map design from the campaign can still be seen in the multiplayer, there's not much else to commend it on. I should mention that none of the elements in the multiplayer are bad, those being loadouts, unlocks, and other uninspired gaming cliches. But that's just it, thier cliches, and when their is such an amazing an unique campaign sitting right next to it, the multiplayer is grossly underwhelming.

Snapmap: 8.75/10
While it doesn't hit Gmod levels of absurdity, snapmap sure does have its trolls. Honestly though, there are only two major causes for concern in the snapmap gamemode. One being the community, and the other being the learning curve. The community, while it offers a great few selection of maps to be played and enjoyed, is mostly just full of trash. There are plenty of users whom simply published an empty room. Even worse, sometimes auto lose maps are made and put up making those who play them hurt their win lose ratio for no good. But who can blame these poor creations, the systems tutorial is almost devoid of all the the higher level knowledge a newb will need in order to put their dreams to reality.

Overall though DOOM is worth the money and 200 hours is easy to lose to it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game surprised me; in a good way.

It's an unapologetic, almost retro FPS. Crazy power-ups; a protagonist that carries an entire arsenal of weapons; ammo ammo, health and armour litter the ground; keycards unlock doors to progress to the next area etc etc. The graphics are excellent, the sound design and music really add to the experience and the storyline is actually a lot more involved than it needs to be. There's loads of flavour-text in collectible data logs too and a dark sense of humour running through it.

The violence is horrendously over-the-top, but as you're blasting apart demons from hell rather than humans, the absurdity of tearing an imp in half with your bare hands becomes quite cathartic rather than disturbing.

The level design is good, the guns are all unique enough to have their own uses and iconic weapons from the series make a welcome return. Collectibles and secrets provide replay value and the length of the single player campaign is generous. The difficulty seems about right (a few boss battles took more than one attempt to clear, but that's fine really). The autosave checkpoints are just frequent enough to save substantial repetition if you do die (sometimes accidentally by mis-timing a jump or due to environmental hazards).

All in all, it is a very polished product that it is a lot better than it really has a right to be (being a remake/reboot of probably one of the most iconic gaming franchise ever made). By sticking to its roots and avoiding a lot of the pitfalls of modern games (microtransactions, day-one DLC, under-developed single player campaign etc.) it is a stronger product for it.

I don't play multiplayer so I can't comment on that aspect of it, but the single player campaign was excellent and I'd definitely recommend this to any fans of the FPS genre, or anyone that's nostalgic for the original Doom but wants something with a more modern feel to it.\t\t\t\t\t
