\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen this game was first anounced back in 2014, I was beyond pumped. The doom series (excluding the disapointment that was Doom3) has always been one that's catered to my playstyle in an fps, run and gun, rip and tear. As time went on however, it began to feel as if though I was alone in that snese. Everyone had already boycotted the game, screaming from the heavens that it was going to suck. I however, in my undying hype hoped... no... knew! It was going to be awesome!
Yet, on playing it at release, and even to this day, it still baffles me that it is as good as it is. So let us begin.

Narrative:
I often find the narrative aspect of games to be quite lacking in this day and age, and don't take that the wrong way. There are far to many games with story's and narratives, most of which are painfully underwhelming, and obnauctiously intrusive. This games narrative however, is not only none intrusive, and undeservingly fascinating, but also hilariously self aware.

You play as the DOOM Slayer, a warrior blessed by an archangel with incredible speed and strength, specifically for the purpose of horrifically slaughtering the denizons of hell. In fact you become so feared, the devils minions carve your name in stone, and practically crap themselves at the mere mentioning of your name.

One day however you are overpowered, and locked in tomb for a milenium. After that time you awake on mars to find a company called the Union Areospace Corportaion, has created a rift to hell in order to siphon it's energy as earth is currently going through an energy crisis. But things have gone wrong, and the head of the corporation, Dr Samuel Hayden, has awoken you for what else but to slaughter Lucifers army as you once did.

The game however rarely shoves this in your face, instead preferring to only rarely give you quick bursts of the base essentials in regards of what you need to know. Most of the narrative is revealed in data entries you find scattered in the levels, all of which are surprisingly fascinating, and this is coming from a guy whom hates to indulge in most forms of literature. Not only that, but in case you haven't noticed, this games plot is... well... a tad bit ridiculous. But the great thing about that is the fact that this game knows that, and has fun with that. It doesn't take itself to seriously, and there are soe legitimately hilarious moments this game has to offer for those whom pay attention.

I dare not blabber on about this games protagonist, I'd be here all day trying to put to words how awesome he is, all I can really say is... just watch this. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AphprlpAVyE

Presentation:
This game is by far one of the best looking games ever made to date. I remember when I first got my knew gtx 1070, I cranked all the settings up to nightmare, and set the resolution to 1527p, I kid you not, my eye's were honestly knid of in pain for about the first 5 minutes of playing. There is so much detail down to the stupidest most minute of things, its incredible! But whats even more amazing is how well this game runs! I have a fair few games who's settings I can't even crank up to ultra without the framerate dipping to a painfull 60 or lower, but this game runs at a constant 100 or higher, rarely dipping below that.

Not to mention the sounds, every weapon sounds fantastic, every enemy sounds distinct, and the music is as if though God came down and gave man a peice of heaven, it is bloody glorious! So I can undoubtedly say this game nails it in the presentation department.

Gameplay:
If you've never played a DOOM game, then all you need to know is that these games, (again excluding the 3rd) prioritzes gameplay above all else. I have dumped more than a hundred hours into this games campaign, and youn wanna know something? I can't wait to play more.

Right from the get go your thrown into the action, and this game never lets up for a moment. I've heard this game been called dumb by many, and am fluffing sick of it. Every enemy has it's own strategies and weaknesses, every weapon its own distinct functions. This game has so much microstrategizing that it demands your full attention whilst you are playing.

You carry all your guns at once with no reloading, no regenerating health, and no cover system to keep the pace of this game as fast as possible. All the enemies shots can be dodged if your skilled enough, meaning that you constantly have to be moving, jumping, strafing. It's a ballet of destruction to the enth degree. And this game is challenging, especially nightmare difficulty.

The maps are large and non linear, allowing for plenty of exploration, with dozens of secrets and easter eggs, all of which are fun and useful to find. Some contain weapons you wouldn't get until later levels, some upgrades for your armor, and some attatchments for your current weapons that give them alternate firing modes. Just for finding the secrets, exploring and even engaging in combat you are given upgrade points which you can use to upgrade your weapons secondary firing modes. upgrading is simple and quick, easy to understand and yet complex enough that it offers another layer of depth and replayability.

Glory kills, a feature even I was skeptical about, have practically become one of my favorite parts in the game. Instead of interrupting the games flow they do the exact opposite, encouraging you to mush forth into the oncoming horde, and rewarding you for doing so. To add to that they're all so brutal and all so much fun to pull off.

Alot of the battles however are quite ammo costly and as a result you'd need a quick way to regain some of that amunition. Thus is where the chainsaw comes to play, it one hits most enemies and gies a large ammo dump after doing so. It does have amm itself though, meaning it's best saved for when you need it most.

Every weapon excluding the pistol which also sucked in the original, feels amazing to use. This games gunplay is currently unrivaled by any other shooter on the market today. The gore compliments this, cementing the power of your arsenal, as your foes are reduced to a pile of viscera... though I kinda do wish it would stick around, but eh preformance, yatta yatta.

Cons?:

So don't I like about this game? Well, I could say the multiplayer, but in all honesty I don't hate it, its not even half as well designed or balanced as the singleplayer, but its still fun, and some of the game modes I genuinely enjoy playing. Though in all fairness DOOM has never been a multiplayer focused game, and its presence here is really only an additon to the main meal that is the campaign.
I kind of would have liked to see a few more new enemy types, as most are either amped up versions of the foes from prior games or just replacements.
Aside from that, eh... um? I can't really think of anything.

One thing i would recommend is indulge in every aspect of this game, if you purely blast through it via only combat, you might find that you think it a bit repetitive, it's a complaint I've seen pop up every now and again from a few people who've played it as such. But in all seriousness, this is my favorite game, and I can't recommend it enough!

Score:10/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: if you have doubts about this game.. don't. this game is good. go get it. now. do yourself a favor.

pros:

this game is good. like.. really good.

they managed to get that fast-paced run'n'gun feel from the first two games done perfectly in this game. this is what doom 3 should have been.

there are a lot of new things added into the formula this time around, like the execution moves, a double jump feature, a progression and by extension, upgrade system, but none of it seems tacked on or gimmicky. everything blends together perfectly.

upon seeing the reveal trailers, i was catiously optimistic regarding the execution attacks. people said they'd become old and monotonous really fast, and i thought so too, but there are like 20 different execution animations for every enemy depending on which bodypart, and from what angle you attack, and they're all very.. visually satisfying, and also go by really quickly, so it doesn't feel like it slows down the game.

another thing about the execution moves is that enemies killed in such a manner drop health and other items when you kill them, so you get rewarded by going up close and personal. nothing feels quite as exciting as running between the legs of 3 barons of hell to get to one vulnerable mancubus while you got 20 health, just to rip its heart out, and forcefeed the damn thing its own innards, then get enough health from the ordeal to survive a bitchslap of green plasma.
regaining health from melee attacks eliminates my one and only gripe with the original games; having to stop fighting when i get hurt to backtrack through the map to find leftover health items. this new system helps keeping the pace going fast, and the action high. love it.

i mentioned the double jump right? if you've played metroid prime, then you know what this means. verticality. and lots of open ended exploring. the original games had secrets all over the damn place. this game is no different. and no different from other "new" ID games, like wolfenstein, and rage, you can unlock secret levels from the original games. also there are doomguy bobbleheads. and upgrade chips for your suit to hunt down, as well as new attachments for your weapons.
finding secrets and doing certain challenges on each level will earn you upgrade points, which allows me to segway into..

..the upgrade system. what? you don't think doom needs an upgrade system? think again!
you can upgrade your guns with said attachments, like a multi-missile launcher for your assault rifle, which you can then upgrade to remove the need to reload it, and make it fire almost indefinitely. you can get a burst-fire attachment for your shotgun and upgrade it to do more damage depending on your accuracy. most weapon attachments have three upgrades you can buy for upgrade points, and one final upgrade you get for doing certain kill-related challenges with the weapon in question, making you work your way to the final upgrade by shooting monsters in the face.

suit upgrades include things like.. carry more grenades, take less damage from your own explosives, swap weapons faster, and stuff like that. no sleight of hand though, since this game doesn't have reloading, except from the super shotgun, plus some weapon attachments having cooldowns equivalent to reloading.

speaking of perks.. there are demonic runes hidden around the levels. interact with one, and you can enter a little challenge. which is usually along the lines of "kill these demons with this gun within this ammount of time". upon completing one, you get a rune you can equip. these give bonuses like "make ammo drops worth more ammo" or "enhances mid-air movement" and stuff like that. unlock more runes, and you unlock more slots put the runes in. and continued use of a rune, will eventually unlock an enhanced version of the rune with an additional bonus on it.

and again, as with metroid prime, and a bit like dark souls.. there is a lot of story and lore in this game, but it doesn't really like to slap you in the face with it. instead, you can gather hidden PDA's, and entering a new area, or killing a new enemy will unlock codex entries for you to read at your own leisure.

cons:

the secret doom 1 levels are impossible. you don't get to keep any of the weapons and upgrades you've acquired in the campaign when you go into them. also, the enemies don't work the same way they used to, so copying the same enemy numbers and placement just doesn't work.

they decided to make the front of the pinky a brick wall that is invulnerable. you have to attack it from behind. also it charges at you. and if it hits, or gets within 10 lightyears of you while it's charging, you'll get hit, and you'll stagger and be stuck in place, unable to move just long enough for the pinky to get a few extra pot shots at you.
combine this futtbuckery with copying the original maps to perfection, including the enemy placement and numbers.. you will come upon more than one pinky quite many times. and usually in VERY CONFINED SPACES. it's physically impossible to survive if you encounter 2 or more pinkies. ded. finito.

also, no quicksaves. and the ledgegrab feature likes to stop working when you're over a bottomless pit.

and some challenges are crap. giving you a global maximum of 6 seconds to do your stuff. and if you manage to get four 4-second bonuses in a matter of three seconds, you won't get 13 extra seconds to play around with, cause the time caps at 6 seconds, so you won't see a reward for being tactical and fancy in the challenges. which is dumb.

those are the only things i dislike about this game. they might seem imposing, but the pros FAR outweigh the cons, and this is one hell of an enjoyable game, pardon the pun. go get it right now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is for the SINGLE PLAYER CAMPAIGN ONLY.

As a gamer, I have no qualms about eating my hat if my preconceptions about a game wind up being false. I was among the many who were sure this game was going to be mediocre because Bethesda held review copies until launch.

There have been very few other times where I have been happier to have been wrong. The single player campaign is everything I wanted it to be. I was afraid that Glory Kills would just be a gimmick and get stale after just an hour or two, but there's actually a bit of strategy to be had with it. Never before have I seen a shooter encourage you so strongly to be insanely aggressive. I just completed it on Hurt Me Plenty, and am immediately going back for more on Ultra Violence.

In short, the campaign is incredibly respectful to old-school Doom, it's nostalgic as hell (pun absolutely intended), and the violence and gore are so intense and unforgiving that I'm looking forward to a school shooting being blamed on it in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
