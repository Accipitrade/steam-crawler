\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an awesome game.

Pros:
- The weapons are really fun to use and I didn't really have a favourite; they all have their different use-cases
- Running and jumping around during combat is a lot more fun than hiding behind cover hoping to not get shot
- Lots of secrets and collectibles
- Classic Doom maps to play after you finish the campaign (if you can find them that is)
- Only 2 unskippable story "cutscenes", the rest of the story is told through audio transmissions and holograms, and you can just ignore them
- Actually got a playable framerate with a Radeon HD7950 at 3440x1440p, and it didn't look too bad
- Loading times were bearable, though I do have it installed on my SSD

Cons:
- Crashed quite often for no reason at some points. thankfully it saves after you get collectibles and stuff, so it wasn't too big of a deal
- Previous sections of the map will close off sometimes during the mission, which is frustrating when you're just looking for collectibles

Overall, if you have $80 (Canadian) to spend, I'd recommend this game. It took me around 12 hours to beat the campaign, and I haven't even found all the collectibles or played any of the classic maps yet.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHmm.. I wish there were a conditional recommendation.

The game's single-player is great. They did a great job with the single-player mechanics overall. There are some things I'd tweak, but it's a good game and if an old-school FPS-like experience with some new trappings are what you're looking for, then this game is a good buy for you.

However, I really can't recommend this game's multiplayer.... it's Call of DOOMty... or Call of Dooty if you prefer.

If that's what you're looking for... well, there are a lot of other games that do it better. What we have here is watered down mess with modern tropes ripped from all kinds of other titles. This is NOT a Doom/Quake-style arena shooter. It's a CoD/Halo/Battlefield play-alike with limited options, sniper-domination, and balance that basically strips everything good out of the single-player content and replaces it with garbage.

The customization is pretty lame. The overall experience feels clunky in multiplayer with a clear focus on the console crowd... completely leaving old-school PC online multiplayer trappings behind.

For a person who cut his online teeth playing custom WADs with arena trappings via sirdoom on my local BBS, what we have here is a major letdown.

It could be a great buy once it goes on sale... or if they fix the broken multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is definitely not for everyone.
I played on Ultra-Violence mode for my first run and, to my surprise, it was actually fairly difficult and fun.
I thought it would be boring and repetitive but the constant introduction of new items/concepts made it refreshing.
If you are someone who really likes story and not-dying this is definitely not for you, the story is interesting but it's not necessary to follow and is only there to progress the crimson tsunami

However, saying that the game doesn't get repetitive would be me lying, Doom isn't a game where one can expect to NOT fight something. So, you can expect to have a fight every 30 seconds or so, depending on if you want to go around finding the secrets on a map or not. Glory kills are faster than I expected and are satisfying to do, also rewarding.

I tried the multiplayer for about 2 hours, it has the game-modes you would expect: TDM, CTF, Dog tags, etc.
There was one mode that was refreshing: Ice Tag. The two teams try and freeze (freeze upon death) the other team and the two teams can thaw teammates by being in vicinity of them. This is really fun because it really relies on team work and being able to shoot around frozen enemies and taunting in front of them is really great. Other than that, it's similar to Unreal Tournament's multiplayer.

Overall, this game is great, the campaign lasted the perfect length.
However the real highlight of this game is the snap map mode.
If you ever played Halo 3 with Forge, snap-map is very similar but has an advanced system of logic functions.
What I mean by logic functions is, for instance, Door(x) > On opened > Spawn Custom Demon Spawn > On event finished > Unlock door(y)
This allows for a complex system for level makers to design intricate progression to maps previously impossible in things such as forge.

This is my favourite part of this game, making mediocre maps and testing them is very fun.\t\t\t\t\t

Instantly hooked
After an hour of gameplay i figured that this, is already one of my favorite games. This game is a non stop action filled experience where i feel powerfull.

Singleplayer mode
The singleplayer mode is fantastic. Blood, gore and violence pulls me through the gameplay like a kid on christmas eve. I have nothing to complain about when it comes to the singleplayer mode, its absolutely amazing, and i always get hyped when i get a new weapon, excited to see how much blood and gore it can spill when killing my enemies. ID did a really good job.

Multiplayer mode
The multiplayer mode is filled with non stop action against other players who want so desperatly to kill you. The weapon system is awesome, I really like the customization of the armor and guns. The only thing i can complain about (as a beginner of this game) is that its a bit hard for newcomers to stand a chance during their first hours of combat, but it isnt too hard to get a hold of the maps and such. I really like the multiplayer option a lot, almost perferct for me so fat.

Snapmap
I havent tried the snapmap option yet, but the idea of creating your own maps in this game makes me want to try it soon. Player made maps can really get creative so i look forward to getting into this!

9/10: ALMOST PERFECT SO FAR

I like the upgrade and customization aspects, even if it's generic these days. Multiplayer is also really fun. People saying its bad because its too much like quake 3 are crazy. That's a good thing not a fault.

What I like the least are the Rune Trials - super lazy add-ons to the campaign that are too easy and just interrupt the story - most just sit out in the open so you can't miss them. They could have put these in the secret rooms as a pickup and just replaced the useless doom dolls or data logs. My second least favorite thing is how incredibly pandering the game is. Anywhere there is green light, that is the direction to go in. Ledges, doors, torches, even candles. They don't give you any opportunity to discover things for yourself. Even the 'secret' rooms show up on the map. The most challenging part of the game is finding the nearly invisible lever to open the old school doom rooms, but there's little reason to bother since they're mostly empty rooms with a few ammo items. Game format is repetitive as hell, the same thing always happens just in different places. You get locked into an area and have to kill a bunch of demons to get out and as the levels go on you do this multiple times. Then eventually when you reach a boss the fight is too easy, even on Ultra-Violence.\t\t\t\t\t
