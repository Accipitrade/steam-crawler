\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely fantastic.

Long time doom fan here, which one could think makes me biased, but in a way, is quite the opposite.

In 1993 we had Doom. Then we had Doom 2. The quintessential shooters of the 90s before quake arrived. They were very action packed. However, when Doom 3 came out in 2004, while it was praised for its technical achievements (of the engine, actually) the gameplay took a big turn. It no longer was focused is lightning fast action. It was story driven, slower, moodier. This made a lot of people (including me) wish it was more like the original 2 games.

With that in mind, I didnt have the highest expectations of Doom 4 when it was announced. I certainly was pessimistic about it and I could only hope, and hold on to that hope very, very hard. Could DOOM (4) be like the first 2 games? Ignore the modern FPS formulas and bring a more visceral gameplay like it used to be before military shooters were this big?

Well... I WAS NOT DISAPPOINTED. AT ALL.

I already invested 12+ hrs playing this game, and I have loved it.

But then again, this wouldnt be a fair review if I would only praise the game. So I'll just give a pro/con list and let you, whoever is reading this, decide if you want to play it.

Pros:
- Really fast pace. In the first 2 minutes of playing, I was already shooting. It remains like that for the entire game
- No stealth sections. Is all about shooting demons in the face, baby.
- Incredible gunplay. Almost all weapon feel incredibly satisfying to use. Sounds dumb to mention this point, but so many games just get it wrong.
- Excellent level design. All levels are big, super detailed, and they allow and encourage to explore. This is no rail shooter. You can run around and find new paths all the time.
- Great design/animations. From weapons to monsters. They look gorgeous, even if some are a bit of a mismatch of the general style of the game.
- Long and entretaining SP campaig. How many levels? Im not even sure. Like 12 or so. Who cares. I wasnt counting. I was playing them and enjoying each one of them.
- Horned demons. Okay... Im just joking there... kinda. Doom 3 brought all the hornless demons. Respectable, but those old school demons straight out of a 90's metal artwork cover with big horns and hooves are the stuff. That's how we remembered doom.

Cons:
- Weak sound design. This is quite subjective, but when you compare the visuals vs the sound, it doesnt match very well. Some weapons sound weak, the monsters are hardly recognizable by sound (which was a very importan thing in the original 2), the environments sound better than monsters do.
- No oldschool MP. Dont be mistaken, THIS ISNT AN ARENA SHOOTER. Is a modern take of it, but is no Quake3/Unreal Tournamen. Loadouts, no weapon pickups. I mention this as a CON because for a game so focused in bringing back the "soul of doom", adding a modern FPS multiplayer formula, makes it somewhat contradictory.
- Slow Multiplayer. When you compare the speed of the campaign to the multiplayer, you have this sensation the the MP is just slow.
- No FFA (Free for all) MP. Everything is about teams.
- Lackluster matchmaking for MP. Just that. It has issues connecting to people, it creates unbalanced teams of 5 vs 2.
- "Meh" soundtrack. It has a very dubstep-ish sountrack (may the record show I had it ON through all the campaing). And I like dubstep, but this soundtrack fails to bring the "action" to your ears through music. The ambiant music is fine, barely noticeable, but the action music just doesnt cut it. I will definitely put some other soundtrack in the background for my next run.

Right in the middle:
- Repetitive gameplay. THIS ONE DOESNT BOTHER ME AT ALL. But I expect people to get tired of running around full speed trying to kill stuff, all level, every level. I think is a PRO. Some might call it a CON
- Simple story. Let's face it. This game is NOT ABOUT STORY TELLING. There are no huge cinematic cutscenes and long dialogues. This game tells you it's story through a few super short sequences and some text you find in PDA like objects. No character development for the hero other than... well, I wont spoil it for you. But really, dont ever think this game is about it's story. It has one, and it works, but it wont WOW anybody.

That's all I can think of right now. I loved the game and I recommend it for any old school FPS lovers out there.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll split this review into three parts: Campaign, SnapMap, and Multiplayer

Campaign:
Pros:

*Good variety of weapons and mods (mods are an alternate-fire mode for the weapons, there are two for each weapon and each mod has their own upgrades)
+Each weapon and mod fells unique, and are better in some situations than others. I particarlly favored the Plasma Gun/Shotgun combo

*Plethra of secrets
+Secrets are divided into three catagories: Hidden Pickups, DOOMGUY dolls (ressembling POP Vinyl dolls with different colour schemes, some referencing the monsters, some referencing iD's older games) and older DOOM levels (each level has a hidden lever that opens a secret area representing older DOOM levels, which can then be played from the main menu)

*Excellent level design
+Each level has open arenas that doesn't hinder your movement in combat

*Excellent Soundtrack
+While it may not be as hard rock as the original DOOM's, here it's a solid mixup between the old-school rock and new rock, which often fits perfectly during combat

*Replayability
+Each level can be played again with all your current weapons and upgrades, allowing you to find secrets you missed in your first playthrough, or even to simply help master your guns or runes

*Story
+Majority of the story is off to the side in your Codex and Codex entries you find in the world, perfect for those who simply wish to get on with the killing. Story itself may not get an award, but it's fairly interesting overall to see how the UAC went bottomsup

*Combat
+Combat is fast-pased and can be quite chaotic depending on the demons that are in the arena with you. You will need to be ready to use all your weapons at your disposal, though often times the lesser demons blend in with the enviorment, causing you to spend time looking for them

*Optimization
+I'm playing this on a old-spec system under minumum requirements, and I still had a stable FPS. I did have some dips, but even with them the game still played excellent

Neutral:

*Upgrades
=Upgrade system is nice, but personally I felt you recieved too many upgrade points. Points for your weapon upgrades are recieved by exploring, completing level challenges, and simply killing them all. Upgrades for your Preator Suit are recieved by finding Elite Guards throughout the level. Overall, some of the upgrades are nice for your playstyle but some are just lackluster. I wish they would've limited how many points you get, so you can actually decide what paths you wish to go down, instead of all the paths

*Secrets
=As much as I love the DOOMGUY dolls you find, it would be nice if you could do something more with them, such as allowing the colour schemes being a custmization option in Multiplayer or SnapMap

Negative:

-Nothing really negative comes to mind at this momment, might update this part after the honeymoon phase is over

SnapMap:

Pros:

*Accesibility
+Great tutorial for new users, easy to use overall, nice and simple UI

*File Size
+Most games that use a feature similar to this require you to download maps that can be of a decent size (ex. some Portal 2 maps can be about 1gb or more from what I have seen) SnapMap doesn't do this however, the game simply downloads the instructions for the game to construct level on its own, with the asessts already in the game. This means you can try out many different maps without taking out a good part of your HDD or SSD

No negatives or neutrals at this time

Multiplayer:

Pros:

*Custimization
+Plenty of it, almost too much in my opinion. You can make each weapon and armor piece unique from other players. Armor pieces and colour options are awarded after each match depending on how well you do, though it's random what you get

Negatives:

*Identity Crisis
-Honestly, it feels like a CoD, Halo, Quake mashup. Nothing wrong with it on paper, but for this game, it's extremely noticable. There are loadouts you can customize, which are fine in other shooters but here it doesn't fit. Instead of picking up the rocket launcher and super shotgun, you can spawn in the match with them. For some people this is fine, but for me this isn't what an arena shooter is

*Netcode
-The netcode in this game is very tempermental. It can work well one match and horribly the next. There have been times where I've been glory-killed by an enemy when he was a fair distance away, too far for them to really execute the glorykill. There's also times where I've been killed around the corner by a non-rocket launcher weapon, and vice versa. I will say this, the netcode works well about 3 games out of 4, that that 4th game can be really bad

*Weapon Balance
-Some of the weapons are still too weak. The plasma rifle only does 5 damage per projectile. This would be fine if it's rate of fire was hire, but that's not the case. The super shotgun is the go-to weapon for most situations becasue of how fast paced it is. You see an enemy rushing up to you, pray, as odds are you are going to have a double-barrel in your face. The weapon mods for some of them are kinda pointless, ex. the Tac Scope for the Heavy Assualt Rile. Aim-down-sights in an arena shooter? Kinda pointless in my opinion

*Demon Rune
-I love the idea of turning into a demon. In this game it's pretty much the same as a powerup, however it can be overpowered in Deathmatch and Team Deathmatch. In those modes, basically whoever can get the demon rune can pretty much be garuenteed to be at the top of the scoreboard. It's great though in Objective based modes, since you can use your demon transformation to help hold down the Warpath or Domination point, or use it to get the points back. My personal opinion is that it shouldn't be in Dm or Tdm. There's also no real way to stop the demon, except with your team working together to take it down. That's what the goal is, and while it works when you have a competent team, most players I've played with simply run away and wait for the transformation timer to run out. I just wish there was more ways to help you stop the demon

Overall: I'd give this game a solid 9/10. The campaign and SnapMap are simply wonderful, with replayability for the campaign and the potential of excellent community-made maps and modes for SnapMap. The Multiplayer, while fun at times, is a bit lackluster to me. However, some of you might enjoy it. This game is easily worth full price

Disclaimer: This game does use Denuvo, however I have not had any problems with it, nor has anyone else I've talked to. I know there are some where that would result in a no-buy from them, so I felt that needed to be said\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom's single player is great fun. It has a dash of Pain Killer sprinkled on top (not a bad thing). When people say they it plays smooth they weren't kidding. My only gripe is that the environments aren't as interesting as the original. They're grounded in reality but something is lost in translation with the shiny and gorgeous sci fi look. I Can't quite put my finger on it.

I haven't played much multi player but it's not quite grabbing me yet. Also Doom has a built in level editor which is a nice bonus.

Looking forward to Quake next. You just know it's going to happen once you've played this. Personally, I hope it's Quake 1.

Grab this game and have some stupid old fashion 90s gaming fun. It's a labour of love.\t\t\t\t\t
