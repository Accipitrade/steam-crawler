\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't need to tell you this is a great game, you already know that. Despite the fact that it wasn't what I wanted (I wanted a continuation in the tone and atmosphere of Doom3), it ended up being ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ awesome anyway. Somehow I find myself not minding that this didn't go the way I wanted, even though Doom3 was one of the most intense videogame experiences I've ever had.

Gameplay is fluid/fast as ♥♥♥♥, brutal, and the presentation in every aspect of the game is completely unified in what it wants you to experience. It wants you to be an unstoppable demon-slaying machine that hell fears. It succeeds. The music really heightens the experience when you need it, dynamically dropping djenty riffs when you start snapping necks.

Snap map was also a pretty cool feature, with a few noteworthy coop modes too. Just a shame they never included an official coop mode to give the game a bit more longevity as the MP is just standard boring pvp arena stuff you've played a billion times before. My only real complaint other than that, was a total lack of horror. It's in keeping with the tonal shift/power fantasy, but it would've been nice to see some hellish stuff that wasn't just played for comedic effect.\t\t\t\t\t
