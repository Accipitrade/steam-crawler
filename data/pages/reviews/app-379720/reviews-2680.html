\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComing into this game, all I knew it was about is you shot stuff. What I experienced is some of the most fun shooting stuff I've had. In this game, everything but having fun takes a step back. The story? From what little bit of it you have to experience, all I could tell is people wanted to use Hell for energy and it backfired for some reason (Reason is told to you ofc). But there's very few cutscenes, maybe 10 minutes of gameplay where you are forced to view story related stuff. There's audio log type things you have to stand still to listen to, or you could click them and leave to go kill more stuff. The majority of the story is told by voice comms from two people. The original half life has more character models than this game does story characters, which means not counting the demons you kill.

But, they took the shooting aspect and made it as fun as possible. Reloading? Not fun.(Okay, the super shotgun reloads and that was my favorite gun, but it's dope) Running out of ammo? Not fun. Shooting demons? Fun. Non stop shooting demons with metal music? Super fun. It took me roughly ten and a half hours to beat this game and I never got bored. Demons explode, you can glory kill demons where the doom guy rips them apart in some fashion depending on where you initiated it, and this game is chock full of secrets and easter eggs. There's probably about ten guns in this game, a chainsaw, and a BFG. Earlier, i hinted that you don't run out of ammo. In the first hour when you only have two guns, you'll probably run out a few times but a glory kill usually gives you enough. After you get about three guns, running out of ammo is rare especially once you get the chainsaw (A chainsaw kill drops tons of ammo, but it uses fuel which does not drop from said chainsaw kill).

In all, this game was amazingly fun. More fun than I expected. If you like shooting stuff, this game is great. If you like story, I've heard you can read about it but don't expect to experience it like The Last of Us or Firewatch.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInstant classic.
Masterpiece.
Genre-defining game.

None of these relate to DOOM.

After acquisition of id Software, Bethesda started to make remakes of classic id games - Wolfenstein, Quake and Doom. Somehow, they managed to distort formulas of fun in at least two of these - Quake (with character skills) and Doom.

Development of DOOM started with Doom comic - https://www.doomworld.com/10years/doomcomic/ . Somebody really liked the RIP AND TEAR page, so he decided that new DOOM should be about ripping and tearing. Therefore, shooting became a necessary mechanic to let player rip monsters. Players are given health and ammo for tearing, but you get almost nothing if you shoot enemies without ripping - a subtle way to move away from the core doom gameplay.

After choosing the main game theme, developers discovered that some players still chose to shoot monsters avoiding melee contact. More limitations were added such as RPG elements. Now Doom guy is a weakling, who can carry 10 shotgun bullets without any upgrades. He also cannot wear armor in proper quantities, his health is 100 out of possible 200 - he is crippled in any way possible. Lack of ammo boxes almost forces you to rip and tear - as intended.

After crippling the main character, developers found out that player can just run around looking for supplies to shoot monsters and avoid ripping! So they decided to place you into tight multilevel arenas without exit - just like Painkiller, but with more jumping and ledgegrabbing.

Game also has a plot with missions. That's right, doom guy is a mission guy now. He has a mission to do, with someone else above him (which is total heresy). He is not a free independent marine anymore, now he needs to save everyone. Of course Bethesda tried to hide that via character's actions - like constantly destroying important stuff. And yet he still follows the orders.

Guns are somewhat nice, but without ammo upgrades they feel underwhelming. Not to mention the amount of upgrades - weapon tokens and argon energy balls for ammo (maybe something else? Too many upgrade categories to keep track of). Was it really necessary? Doom needs no feeling of progressing aside from getting new guns and meeting stronger monsters. Now player is faced with decisions - do I need more hp or armor or ammo (protip: you always need ammo)? Acceptable for any shooter, but not for the shooter with DOOM in title. Not to mention that game ends like a level after you feel really powerful. Of course there is an arcade mode with all upgrades, but why not make it the default mode without RPG crap?

Monsters are fine, they do monster things and do them as they should.

Graphics are fine, nothing bad about it either.

Music belongs to the garbage bin - wubs are not music. Occasionally there is some guitar - when a strong monster enters the arena.

Bethesda and remains of id Software managed to make a good shooter, good enough to be a proper Painkiller sequel. Unfortunately, it has almost nothing similar to original Doom in gameplay terms aside from Hell setting and weapons. Sonic-speed running marine was replaced with jumping ape, who has shotgun without ammo and forced to rip and tear. Get it for 75% off and play arcade mode, avoiding campaign. And please ignore DOOM in the title.\t\t\t\t\t
