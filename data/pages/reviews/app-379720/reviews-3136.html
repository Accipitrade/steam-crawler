\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not very fun.

The "glory kill" takedowns are dumb. It's meant to be a health regeneration system where you get enemies down to a certain health point and then press a button to get armour/health/ammo but on any difficulty higher than the lowest setting you'll rarely get a netgain from that. Also the way the camera moves around when you press the takedown button isn't very satisfying since the camera just teleports around. It's not very comfortable and also somewhat disorientating.
You can also briskly walk around levels to pick up health packs and other goodies to refill your stocks. But since doors like to lock behind you until you clear a room of demons, that's not always possible.

Also there's several different pseudo-rpg upgrade systems all spread across several different menus with various different points for each. I don't know why a lot of modern "classic" shooter games have these systems, it's not very good.
There's also this obnoxious-tier heavy metal music that plays throughout the game. Sure that worked back in the 90s but it's the current year and that stuff is just cringe.
Certain enemies like to jump and move around the small rooms, that'd be a neat idea in other games, but in this it just adds more mess to the pile.

Remember when Half Life 1 had that "interactive cutscene" thing where allies would talk and do stuff while you could run around the room like a headless chicken? Every game essentially copied that after Half Life 1's release. Even the new Doom does it. Except it does it in an extremely terrible way. Whenever the game wants to throw it's boring and tedious plot at your face, it plonks you in a room, makes you stand there motionless and forces you to watch some nonsense you don't care about. It even tries to poke fun at itself in a ~cool and ironic~ meme way. Occasionally the player character will just disregard the plot and destroy something. "haha so cool and ironic lulz ecks-dee". It's like they know their story is lame and nobody cares, but they force you to sit through it anyway. You also have audio logs and other nonsense thrown around the levels.

On top of all this the level design is awfully linear. You have rooms interconnected by hallways with the occasional big room. Sometimes you get outdoor rooms and hallways, sometimes there's hell-themed rooms and hallways. It's all very lame. Sure it might get better later on in the game but I can't really be bothered finishing it.\t\t\t\t\t

RIP AND TEAR!
RIP AND TEAR YOUR GUTS!

In all honesty the game is really good but having just recently beat the game, there are some things to point out.

- The game offers little in the way of new enemies or weapons. That may be good for some players, but I was left wanting a little more. It's nice to see new versions of all our old pals, but I would liked to have seen more than the few new enemies we did get (and no, the variations of posessed don't count).

- Speaking of enemies, some of them feel a little spongy. Not to the point where it's annoying, but to the point where some of even the bigger weapons lose a little of that... "ooompf" that they should have. This is usually fixed with the weapon mods, but it doesn't fix all of them. Mind, I played on the "medium" difficulty, so I don't know if they're even spongier on harder difficulties or how they handled that.

- The game makes it clear almost from the beginning that getting "into" the story is entirely optional, with the way that most of the background lore and monster bio is hidden in codexes, and through the way that Doomguy disregards the other characters. That said, the game still sits you down and forces you to pay attention to a few story moments. There aren't many at all and they don't last long, and I get that they're trying to give us some down time, but I think we get enough of that with the key and secret hunting.

- Speaking of which, key/secret hunting is back! While you're not often searching through some labyrinthian abyss trying to find that one damn keycard that opens that one damn door, the secrets can be another issue entirely. I personally don't mind taking time to hunt for some secrets, but many may find it boring or arduous. This is compounded (somewhat) by the fact that many of these secrets also give weapon upgrades and upgrade points, which are almost necessary for doing well in the game in some cases. So ignoring too many of them can leave you feeling ill-equipped - something you don't want when you're staring down a snarling Cyberdemon.

- The story, while interesting, is pretty predictable. And while it does make a token effort to catch you off guard or get you to chase a red herring, most people will know the big twist as soon as they meet the character associated with it. It's a minor nitpick - especially in a game where the story shouldn't matter as much - but seeing how as they occassionally hold you down and force you to participate in the story, I'm still going to hold it against them. But only a bit.

- It can feel a bit boring toward the beginning. At least before (spoilers) you get to go to Hell for the first time. Many of the early enemies are variations of posessed with a few exceptions, and a lot of the truly memorable enemies don't appear until during or after the Hell level. Again, only a minor nitpick, but something I thought I'd bring up.

- FInally, the game plays less like classic Doom and more like Serious Sam or Painkiller. What I mean is that the game will sometimes lock you in a room and have you face off against a few waves of enemies. This isn't horrible, and I downright enjoyed it, being a fan of SS and PK. I also realize, however, that some people may miss the more fluid progression of classic Doom or even the more recent Wolfenstein: The New Order. They do include classic levels though, and if there's always snapmap.

I haven't played multiplayer so I can't make any kind of analysis based on that, though I've been told that even post-release it's not the best - a worrying sign since rumors have it the DLC will be mp only.

This isn't meant to turn you away from Doom. It's meant to highlight a few of the problems that some people may have with it. If any of these turn you off from the game, then I would still recommend it a few years down the line or on sale for a lower price. If you're like me and you still want to RIP AND TEAR, then I won't be the idiot to try and stop you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started playing DOOM after hearing many people praise the game for its fun, action packed campaign. I walked into this game with low expectations, as I didn't want to completely overhype myself. Having never played any other prior installment in the franchise, I must say I walked away beyond impressed. DOOM delivered a campaign of epic proportions, delivering a true sense of brutallity as I faced off against demons in hell and on Mars. Its fast paced fighting and amazing graphical fidelity instantly had me hooked. If you're looking to have fun and as marketing goes, "Fight Like Hell", then DOOM is definately worth your time. The multiplayer aspect was very enjoyable, bringing the all out mayhem to maps with multiple people. Some of the map designs seemed a little generic and bland, but the combat itself felt great and fluid.

If you're looking for all out action, mayhem and chaos, DOOM will more than satisfy you. DOOM is definately one of the best shooters of 2016.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a Bethesda game, so let's just get this out of the way

- This game crashed on me several times over my playthrough
- For one of the hologram director guy's audio, the sound cut out and made a really spooky screechy sound. I thought it was intentional, but when I reloaded my save, it didn't happen again
- On one level, the music completely cut off. I had to reload my save

Buggy $60 games are never excusable, but you could at least dull the pain by reassuring yourself it made sense, since Bethesda deals in YUGE open worlds like TES and Fallout. Not so with a linear first person shooter. And ♥♥♥♥ anyone who says "who cares, it's a Bethesda game". As if being consistently ♥♥♥♥ is an excuse to be ♥♥♥♥. You would expect them to learn their lesson after one, two, three four or five buggy releases ¯\\_(ツ)_/¯

The Praetor suit upgrades are boring, too. Grab ledges half a second faster? Wow sign me up. Despite that, overall, DOOM is still fun. I mean it's Doom after all, hard to go wrong there. I can't hate this game too much, fun is fun, even if it is flawed.

Three out of five Lennys

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite CTD several times now, yes it does crash, I still recommend it. Much like the old doom it has a certain map by map charm. There is a story, which much like the game, comes in over radio and can be safely ignored. This game almost fall forward all the time, in and out of combat with some almost dishonored like parqour moves makes it a brutal fps mashing, gnashing and crunching your enemies as you push through.
The tempo is ferocious through out, the story is made for a silent and furious protaganist who rips and bashes anything within range and shoots at the rest. The level ups in terms of armor, weapons and so on are all just making the game feel alive and fresh as you move along, or through it.
The music shift into high gear as you start to hit enemies and turns probably anyone into a frenzied killing machine in their own mind. Add the guns and all that and you have your perfect d-beat to it. The sound and feel you get for it is classic doom. There is a certain rush to it, this game begs you to blast through it and then again. While the TES and FALLOUT begs you to idly sift through the world and take your time in your exploration DOOM tells you bash through it one more time. There is no mecriful lull or careful planning a mission, grab that gun and have it!\t\t\t\t\t
