\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me preface this by saying the classic games (Doom, Doom II, Final Doom and Doom 64) were my favorite games growing up. They still are in 2016 due to the vast collection of community maps and mods available. I therefore had really high standards when it comes to what constitutes "a Doom experience".

I wholefully expected this game to be a misguided sequel in title only. Thankfully, I was wrong.

Not only does it follows the sequence of events at the end of the classic timeline by starting you in a sarcophagus brought back from hell, but it also contained a hearty amount of throwbacks and very Doom things.

I found it enjoyable at first but it wasn't until I reached hell that one fact became exceedingly clear: they managed to capture Doom's *soul*.

Circle strafing, prioritizing targets, quickly learning the level layout and hunting pick up items while dancing amidst the attack of lesser demons and picking the Best tool for the Job. Exploring complex, maze like maps in which it is possible to get lost, for secrets, bonus items, challenges and keys.

Classic demons make an upgraded appearance, in recognizable form, and it's like greeting old friends.

It managed to capture the essence of what made the classic games fun, the flow of combat, split second decisions that can turn a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ situation into a victory. A wide variety of satisfying weapons, demons that complement each other and offer different amounts of challenges in various combinations, and a slow burning but eventually steep difficulty curve.

The game not only understands all of this, but also tries to improve upon and meld the mechanics with modern gameplay. The added vertical aspect with quick mantling and platforming is meshed in almost flawlessly, and demons can and will go wherever you can go, with added AOEs and surprise attacks.

It's a ton of fast paced, captivating fun, peppered with throwbacks to the originals, a million secrets and challenges to find, convoluted maps that are just a pleasure to explore and replay.

It's not just a fantastic value for the money, it's also a certain breed of fun I had not experienced in many years.

Also, it's gorgeous. The new id tech 6 engine really shines, both in terms of lighting quality and performance.

If you too, yearn for something like this in 2016, I believe you may have found it. Exceeded all expectations.\t\t\t\t\t
