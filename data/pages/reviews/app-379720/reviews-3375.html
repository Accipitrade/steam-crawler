\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow. Before I start the Review off: I have as of this moment NOT Completed the main Story.


<This is a Spoiler Warning. Do not proceed if you do not wish to see the Main Plot Spoiled.>


DOOM 2016 is here to bring back the classic fast-paced gameplay of Id's original DOOM released in 1993. but Just how well does this game hold up? From a Story perspective It involves you in the Story much more than the Original game did. To a point of having moments where the Pace of the game is broken by, Say. being locked in a room while you're spoken to. Although from a Gameplay perspective, Ignoring all moments of Pace-breaking Dialogue and In-game Cutscene (That is to say, a Cutscene where you are free to Move Doomguy around the map.) This game is VERY Fast-Paced, You can tell right off the bat that the Devs wanted to keep the player constantly on the move. and it Shows. taking a hit in this game is Punishment in it of itself, as much like the Original 1993 DOOM. Your only method of repairing health damage is to pick up a Medical Kit. although they have improved upon this with the Glory Kill system, which Plays a fancy little animation in which an enemy dies in a Spectacularly gorey way by your hands, Said enemy drops a Set amount of health (Dependant on what Percentage your own Health Bar is at.) Singleplayer has also handled Bosses quite well. (Well. the One boss I have faced.) As the Cyberdemon helps to both bring new things to the Table, while retaining it's old feel. For example, the CD keeps his missiles as his Main method of attack. but starting with his Second Phase, he begins slashing with his Blade Arm, making you constantly have to Move.

(Multiplayer.)

From a Multiplayer Perspective (Bear in mind, I have only played Ten matches of it to formulate this Opinion.) DOOM Brings back the Gameplay of Arena-Based Shooters, While incorperating the Custom Loadout functions of Modern FPS games like Halo, Call of Duty. Battlefield, you get the Idea. And much like these other games, You can also customise your Multiplayer Character to look 'Unique.'
Now to the gameplay. It's an Arena Based shooter, where sadly you cannot Pick-Up another Enemy's weapon upon their death. Which I find to be an interesting choice as Many other First-Person Shooters allow this function. Another thing of note is that you find Powerups. Be it Partial Invisibility or Quad Damage. You can also find two 'Power Weapons' on the Map. one of them is the Classic BFG-9000. while the other is the More recently-added Gauss Cannon. Both of them are Equally Devastating (With the BFG Being slightly moreso.) The last point of note is the 'Demon Rune.' in which If you pick it up. you are Transformed into one of four demons of your choosing. This I feel is a rather Irritating addition, as if an ally picks it up. Your team is Denied kills as this Ally Gets the Instant-Kill abilities of whichever Demon they have become. And if an Enemy gets it, Your team will be Respawning constantly. unable to kill this Unstoppable Demon. That's why they had added a Newer function. If the Demon Perishes prematurely. you can pick up the Rune, and become a Demon yourself. with the leftover Countdown of the Other Demon. this cycle continues until the Pickup dissapears, Or the match ends.

[Snapmap]
Snapmap is a very limited level creator for DOOM [2016] Which has several hard limits, Such as the 'Only Twelve Demons spawned at any given time' Limit. which for some is a simple enough workaround, and for others a small irritation. Snapmap as a whole is not bad. There are some very great things creative people can do with it, However - and this is the big however - Snapmap like most creative efforts, takes time to figure out and even moreso to master. I've seen some people make tributes to E1M2, Completely rebuild the Icon of Sin's bossfight, and even make interesting use of the Demon Runes from multiplayer, all in the drive of adding more fun levels to play on snapmap. I will say that if some hard limits were removed. Snapmap could have the capabilities of being great fun.

Overall. My review.
Singleplayer - Highly Enjoyable.
Multiplayer - Needs work, but I do Enjoy it.
Snapmap - Very fun, does need work, But can be enjoyable to a point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere was a lot of things I was skeptical about in this title at first but it's managed to reall surprise me now that I've had a chance to play it.

The singleplayer is what DNF should have been. No boring, linear or slow gameplay, bogged down by scripted events. Instead you are treated to an extremely fast run-and-gun experience with challenging hordes of enemies, lots of cool looking weapons and exploration-oriented level design. There is no story or cutscene to sit through. If you WANT to learn about the backstory of the game, you are free to read about it on your PDA (or Cortex, as it's called in this game) for exposition on the UAC, it's personnel and their experiments leading up to the start of the game.

The multiplayer is also excellent. The old feeling of being able to circle-strafe jump around people is back, while shooting mid-air detonated rockets to blow them to bits. There is no regenerating health, and power-ups like the Quad-Damage and BFG9000 make a welcome return. While you 'technically' need to unlock weapons before you can use them, I found myself having everything unlocked in a matter of an hour or so, and that's how it should be. There are only two annoyances here. You can only start with two weapons and you cannot pick up fallen players' weapons. This is probably for balance reasons and it does not bother me too much. When my current load-out I simply pick one of my other ones and mix it up. The other annoyance is the demon rune, which transforms you into an overpowered OHK machine.At first I was really against it, but I am slowly learning strategies on how to fight the demons effectively, so it may not be so bad.

Just be wary of bad teammates who can literally feed the demon-transformed player 10+ kills before the power-up wears off! Latest there is SnapMap the level editor, which I haven't even played with yet, but it looks very promising and user friendly. Overall I would say DOOM is one of the best and most fun values you can get for your dollar at this point in time, and I would recommend it if you like viceral and lightning-fast singleplayer and old-school twitch multiplayer action, with a few updates to bring it into the modern day.\t\t\t\t\t
