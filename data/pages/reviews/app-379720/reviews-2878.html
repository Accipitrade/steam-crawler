Pros:
-Amazing graphics (i5-3570k and GTX 660 Ti for specs reference)
-Amazing soundtrack
-Amazing combat
-Amazing level design
-Great story
-Runs extremely well
Cons:
-A bit expensive at its normal price of CDN$ 79.99

I brought this game on sale during the winter sale for CDN$ 26.49, and it has been worth every penny. It's full price is perhaps a bit too much unless you're a huge fan of the series or love the genre, I'd recommend waiting until a sale of at least 25% to purchase it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 is unapologetically savage and gloriously true to its roots. This is no survival-horror adventure; this is no tactical cover & snipe shooter. There's no regenerating health, no dialogue options, and - hell - not even a reload button. Your usual omnipresent guiding voice gives Doomguy instructions - and he completely ignores them. Doomguy is on his own agenda, his only goal... which is to gun down all demons, grab them by their jaws, and shove a mailed fist down their throats. Fearless, brutal, and relentless, Doomguy tears through rank after rank of demons, ripping out their innards and literally yanking ammo and health out of the busted-up bodies of his victims. Whereas in other games a grotesque Mancubus would be a horrible terror, Doomguy just sees a plump ripe ammo-cherry for the picking and comes crashing into battle with glee.

It's incredibly refreshing to play. Bethesda has chosen to simply cast Doomguy as essentially a demi-god, and therefore completely disregarding 4th wall logic like "where is Doomguy stashing all of these guns as he flies around the map?" or "how many rounds can that plasma rifle possible hold in the magazine?!" or, "why are all these health and armor items conveniently placed all around the little nooks of Hell?" Yeah, Bethesda doesn't care, and neither should you. You're Doomguy!

Doomguy moves faster than most FPS protagonists sprint (there's no run or walk button, although you can lightly tap forward to go slower), and every single action is visceral. Land from up high (Doomguy never takes falling damage), and Doomguy thunks solidly into the ground, one knee down, Batman-style. Glory kill (melee finishing a staggered enemy) from above, and Doomguy smashes the Imp's skull to smithereens with an armored boot stomp. Fire a charged Gauss Siege Cannon, and the beam's event horizon leaves a trail of carnage in its wake while simulataneously hurling Doomguy back several feet from the recoil. Grab a Berserk powerup and - o, divine carnage! Think Batman-fluid combat in First Person, and with the enemies being torn to shreds by your rage-fueled fists.

On top of a very satisfyingly long campaign (13 maps, each one easily taking up to an hour or two to finish), Doomguy can also complete ingame challenges (Headstomp 5 Possessed Soldiers, etc) to unlock weapon upgrade points, weapon challenges (kill 30 demons with a Plasma Heat Wave) to unlock special weapon abilities, find rare Praetor suit upgrades to gain special perk ablities that can be swapped out at any time, and of course discover secrets that range from items to portals that unlock classic Doom maps that can be played on their own from the main menu!

Suffice it to say, then, that Doom 2016's single player campaign is a great game well worth the price of admission. As for the multiplayer - I haven't bothered. Perhaps it is because I am too old to play dash TDM games anymore (although I remember Unreal fondly), but I bought Doom entirely for its single player campaign.

This is the best quote from the game:
"Samuel Hayden has offered to help you close the Portal. You don't need his help. You just need ammo."

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM

In the first minutes of gameplay, slaughtering all my fears and cynicism that I had towards the game, DOOM fast became my favorite remake of anything ever. Being a huge fan of the classic DOOM I & II from the '90s, this game went against the odds and blew my expectations out of the water.

On top of the absolutely stellar gameplay and everything surrounding it, I must first mention the music. The soundtrack to this new DOOM was composed by Mick Gordon, and boy did he do an impossibly good job with it. Brutal, merciless and headbangingly fantastic metal, with soundscapes that meld with the game (and especially gameplay due to the dynamic sound/music design linked to in-game action) perfectly. Damn well done.

You're dropped straight into action, and it never lets you get bored, it's adrenaline pumping demon slaying extravaganza for 10-18 hours, in just the same vein like the classic DOOM games were. Plethora of weapons with really well done upgrade customizations, there's so many ways to play the game, meaning destroying everything in your path exactly how you wish. The meaty punch the weapons have, the sound design on them, the effect on the enemies exploding into gibs of gore and fountains of blood, no other game makes dealing with enemies this satisfying.

Environments, level design, secrets, in so many ways this game manages to honor the classics and still build on them. Blazing through the industrial maps and fiery hellscapes, it all combines into an exhilarating experience. The joy of quickly dodging through enemies, blasting them left and right, using verticality and seeing how well the ledge grab animations for example mesh together with the levels. The action just never stops, it all flows together so well, perfection in design.

The story of the game is good and never bogs you down with excessive cutscenes or any time stopping hub world breathers. You're thrust in the middle of cultish UAC crazies who are fiddling with Hell, meeting some characters and having very one-sided dialogue with them (don't worry, the Doom Marine is still very much our favorite silent protagonist) and an army of demons (that later on will reveal to have a bunch of nice story to them as well).

We also get a new backstory to our protagonist, and it is a good one, oh boy if it isn't a glorious celebration of how badass you are in the game. The story doesn't take itself too seriously, and the main character certainly isn't, except really damn serious about killing every single demon they can get their hands on of course. I don't want to spoil anything, but the new lore behind the Doom Marine and how it relates to Hell and the demons, is so damn good, you don't want to miss out on any of this. This is one of the best games ever made, simple as that. The king is back. Rip and tear.

Recommended to anyone who likes 1st person shooters, you need to play this one.

(As a last note, you probably noticed I only talked about the single player gameplay. The multi player isn't good in my opinion, it's generic, doesn't have interesting game modes as of now and the single player is so good that one just doesn't need to bother with the multi player side.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMean Mr. Mustard's short DOOM review

1) Essential - buy it NOW
2) Recommended - buy it on sale
3) Avoid

Time to complete – 12 hours to complete campaign on 'Ultra-Violence' difficulty
Played multiplayer? Not Yet
Worth revisiting? Yes, still got a load of secrets and collectibles to find, still want to do a playthrough on 'Nightmare' difficulty and still want to try the mulitplayer and SnapMap modes.

Best part – Very true to the original DOOM, the devs should be applauded for retaining the important landmarks of the classic titles in the series: the iconic setting, the demons and weapons and most importantly the blisteringly fast speed. But should also be commended for making sure the game can stand on it's own two feet as a modern First Person Shooter and not rely solely on past glories. A truly remarkable accomplishment which appeals to and satisfies both DOOM veterans and knew-comers alike.

Worst part – That we've had to wait 20+ years since the release of DOOM 2 to get another 'proper' DOOM game.\t\t\t\t\t
