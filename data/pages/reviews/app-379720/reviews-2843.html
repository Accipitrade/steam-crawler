\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM! Gore galore, be prepared for the onslaught of DOOM!

With crisp visuals and even crisper gameplay, it's time to eviscerate hell with a penchant for doom. DOOM appreciates its roots while acknowledging modern game design, packaging it all together in a remarkably thick coat of gore. As you weave between the horde of evil, carefully finessing your way through the gore, you'll feel both empowered by your agility whilst overwhelmed by the masses of evil to overcome.

The enemy design is top-notch. Fluid movement and fluid bloodspray, DOOM is an excellent example of the modern first person shooter genre. Further heightening this is the backbone to it all, the Id Tech 5 engine, powering the movement, gore, animations, and visual effects. There are really no complaints to leverage against its visual design - it is simply DOOM in the goriest of form.

And more, the visuals are matched with perfectly pitched audio-design; as the rampage begins, the guttural rock is cranked to an 11, intensifying the entire experience; and when the siege has finished, silence is welcomed, but only for a moment, as you are once again thrust into the hordes of hell with the staccato of percussion pumping into your ears. Round and round it goes, as hell and hell abodes.

Unto ye, DOOM!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is one of those franchises that brings with it high expectations. Luckily DOOM has managed to be a highly enjoyable game all the way though, While there are some negatives, they are overridden by the positives.

Positives:
The combat is a load of fun. The glory kills were a great addition, but the emphasis on moving around as much as possible emulated the original games well.
The suit and weapon upgrade systems weren't as intrusive as I worried.
Difficulty remains well balanced as Doomslayer gets more powerful.
Some enemies were nerfed and others were buffed, though their choices make sense.
Alt fire modes force more strategy to be used in choosing the right gun/mod per enemy.

Negatives:
The 'lock you in a room for action set piece' progression got tiring at times.
The plasma rifle seems overly nerfed.
With the excpetion of last few levels, level design is overly liner and rarely has 'hub' style level design.

Ultimately the overriding issue is that the game is absurdly fun to play. It's high paced, exhilarating, and just a delight to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I enjoyed this game, I'm too angry to finish it.

I went through the game casually at first as I tend to do, blowing past most of the collectables and secrets until I was virtually at end game. I was having some difficulty towards the end so I decided to go back and do everything from previous missions, complete all the rune trials, get every possible upgrade before attempting to complete the game. I did certain maps like foundry 3-4 times to clear everything.

Days later after investing a rediculous amount of time, I finally completed everything. I returned with all my upgrades to the same section I was having trouble with, ready to rock, when I noticed the autosave had left me with no armor and insufficient ammo for the task. So I decided to reset the map. Having logged on and off several times, fully completing every map multiple times, naturally I assumed all of that progress was saved. There was an instant of apprehension about reseting but I reassured myself that the warning message displayed about losing your progress is the same message you receive when exiting a mission replay, which doesn't reset items collected during the replay. So it's reasonable to assume when it says "progress" it is referring solely to mission progress and not item progress.

All of my progress from replaying earlier maps was apparently saved within the same instance as the current mission I was on. Everything reset to where it was when I first entered the map. It had to have been something like ~40 upgrade points, I don't know how many hours, all the toughest rune challenges, probably half the total content in the game, gone. Instant rage uninstall.\t\t\t\t\t

The game gives us so many new experiences while still giving us that raw Doom feeling, making us feel empowered as this Doom marine as we rip and tear past a amazing fun, challenging and fast paced variety of enemies keeping you on your toes and an upgrade system to keep you feeling powerful!.

As for performance I have played this on low and high end rigs and with the introduction of Vulcan API this game is amazingly well optimised smooth fast paced gameplay all throughout the game on lower end rigs I noticed some dips below 60 fps but that could be fixed with lowering some settings! Would give you those extra frames for a smoother experience while still looking incredible.

All in all the best single player fps campaign I have played in a long time and worth the money alone on that! But with the multiplayer lacking it's not the perfect game but I still love it!
