What makes the combat work so well is that it's all about movement. It feels good to move around, and all the enemies have differing optimal positioning required to deal with them, making effective combat about controlling your distance and movement around multiple sets of differing opponents. Unfortunately the game insists on frequently dragging you through constrained levels where movement doesn't feel good and you'll find yourself getting stuck on geometry you can't see while circle-strafing, and the "glory kill" mechanic, designed to draw and keep the player closer to the target, quickly becomes tedius yet necessary to exploit.

Overall, quite enjoyable though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLiterally the worst shooter I've ever played. A game with so much potential, so much to do, so many great features wrapped into one game with two major problems: the gunplay is awful and the progression holds your hand. Do you like exploration? Too bad! You already know which direction to go. Do you like first person shooters where weapons matter? Too bad again! Glory kills mean you never need to be good at shooting. Just fire your weapons in the direction of the enemies and they'l eventually flash blue and orange for you, because why would you want to use your own intuition playing a video game when the game can visually hold your hand through everything? "Oh, don't you worry deary, we'd NEVER let you use your brain, you might hurt yourself." Half the game is finding pointless weapon upgrades that completely detract from any difficulty the game was supposed to have. The level design feels like they took Doom 3 and used asset flip tactics to make a mess of a linear experience. If you like mindless violence, then you'll probobly enjoy this game, but that's about all I found in this game and it got old really fast for me. Not even worth the $15 sale price I paid for it.\t\t\t\t\t
