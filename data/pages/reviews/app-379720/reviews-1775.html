\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA long time ago I had an Commodore Amiga for many years this computer was superior to the PC. Then a friend showed me his recently built PC and we played Doom. I was sold and ever since I am a PC gamer :-). The original was great in so many ways because it was one of the first FPS games with good sounds, an action pact environment and great weapons.

Now fast forward to 2019, I picked up Doom 2016. At first I couldn't get into this game because I mostly compared it to modern open world shooters. This game is different: you are a space marine and the Mars facility is invaded by demons. Many times the levels resemble more of a maze than anything else: just like the original. At first this frustrated me a bit but I got used to it. Then I uncovered the true gem: it's a brutal game that rewards slaughtering demons in the most gruesome way: be as aggressive as possible otherwise the demons make quick work of you.

There are so many upgrades and secrets that findings these at times becomes more of a priority than actually finishing the levels (at least for me :-)). The graphics and effects are done very well and the sounds brings a lot of atmosphere. I didn't care too much about the music track although I suppose it does fit the theme well. They did include some of the original songs and levels which is great. In its heart it is definitely an old school game (but updated to today's standards). It might not be for everyone but if you can get into it you will definitely have a good thrill ride.

I also played hours of multiplayer and while simple compared to other games it does provide enough fun to continue playing: game speed is fast and you definitely need to play a lot to beat many of the more experienced people. Nevertheless it is good fun and a nice option is that you can unlock more items the more you play.

All in all one of the better FPS games out there.\t\t\t\t\t

Because of this game, i fell in love with the DOOM franchise, and with gaming as a whole!

9,5/10

Doom 2016 is an absolute triumph, a resurrection of classic FPS excellence that redefines the meaning of fast-paced, adrenaline-pumping action. From the moment I stepped foot on Mars, I knew I was in for a wild ride!

The game encourages aggression and rewards players for getting up close and personal with their foes. The arsenal of weapons at your disposal is both varied and satisfying, with each gun packing a punch and offering unique strategies for decimating the hordes of Hell.

The level design is a testament to the game's brilliance. Each environment is expertly crafted, striking a balance between atmospheric exploration and non-stop action.
The pacing is superb, with moments of intense combat followed by brief moments of respite that allow you to catch your breath before diving back into the chaos.

The Glory Kills are brutal, satisfying, and add an extra layer of visceral excitement to the combat. They're not only a fantastic way to replenish health but also a spectacle to behold as you rip and tear through your enemies.

The soundtrack of Doom 2016 is a standout aspect of the game, elevating the intense gameplay and immersing players in the hellish atmosphere. Composed by Mick Gordon, the soundtrack captures the essence of the franchise and delivers a pulse-pounding mix of heavy metal, industrial, and atmospheric tracks.
The game's audio design is nothing short of breathtaking. The environments are richly detailed, and the hellish landscapes are both terrifying and awe-inspiring.

Doom 2016 is a love letter to classic first-person shooters. It successfully revives the spirit of the original games while injecting modern gameplay elements and stunning visuals. It's a game that embraces its roots and unapologetically celebrates the joy of fast-paced, over-the-top action.\t\t\t\t\t
