\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a fast paced First Person Shooter taking place in a futuristic setting where the demons are invading a Martian facility. The 10-ish hour campaign will take you on a violent and fun demon mashing journey.

Released in 2016, Doom will run quite comfortably on any modern machine. I played it through on AMD Ryzen 5 5600x, RTX 3060 Ti and 16 GB of RAM at 1440p resolution with high settings, and the game ran smoothly at around 200 fps. Also the game did never crash.

The graphics look like what you would expect from a modern triple-A game. Even though Doom is a few years old now, I think the graphics still hold up pretty nicely. Something to note on the graphical side is the color palette, because (almost) everything in this game is either red or orange or their mixture. I would have liked to see more varied locations with different themes.

Metal fans will be delighted to hear the heavy soundtrack, which greatly accompanies the demon slaughtering gameplay. The best moments in Doom are tough battles with the track "BFG-division" playing in the background. I often found myself cranking up the sound during some fights just to enjoy the music.

The gameplay is like in old-school shooters. The player is put in a somewhat linear level where battle arenas are located behind almost every corner. Between battles the player will hunt down for supplies, collectibles and secrets.

The gunplay feels good. Over the course of the game you'll get a decent arsenal of weapons, and almost every weapon has two upgradeable secondary attacks. The player gets challenges in every level and completing these grants upgrade points for weapons.

Pretty much every weapon has some purpose, or at least I liked to use a specific gun against a specific demon. For example I usually finished the last bullet spongy demon with the chainsaw, which grants the player ammo, and shot imps once with combat shotgun and finish them with a melee attack. When a demon's health is low enough, it will enter a stagger-state and if hit by a melee attack, the player enters a cool looking finisher animation and gains health. To indicate demon is in stagger-state, it flashes with bright colors. This looks stupid and breaks the immersion, but luckily you can disable those flashing colors in the settings.

The story is quite boring. You wake up in some research facility and notice everything has gone wrong, the demons are invading Mars and you are the only one that can stop them. You don't really play Doom for its story.

This game also has a multiplayer but I couldn't be bothered to try it out. It also has a Snapmap mode, where the player apparently creates their own levels. That didn't interest me but it is there.

The base price for Doom seems to be 20 nowadays, and I could say it is worth it if you are planning on completing the game at least twice. For context, my most recent playthrough on hard difficulty took about 10 hours. During a sale Doom is a no-brainer since it goes for around 5.

I give Doom my recommendation and a score of 3/5. This is a game that you can complete in the time span of a weekend and it's great fun if you are into fast paced FPS games and metal music. I would have liked to see more wacky weapons, weapon upgrades and a chance to acquire those upgrades earlier in the game. I barely got the last upgrades to late game weapons during the final level. Also I need a break from the color orange for a while now.\t\t\t\t\t
