\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo one ever told me Doom combat was THIS addicting. It has made me fall in love with boomer shooters, a whole genre of game I never knew I needed. I highly recommend playing on at least "Ultra Violence" difficulty. It's your standard hard mode. (Nightmare is for masters where it feels like certain enemies oneshot you.)

Doom's combat is invigorating and exciting. You're quickly running around dodging projectiles and enemies, figuring out which weapons best kill which enemies, and managing your health and ammo resources. You get health back from melee executions and ammo back from chainsaw executions. I used to think these "glorykills" were annoying interruptions, but on higher difficulties they actually provide a brief moment to catch your breath and plan your next move.

But it's not just the combat. The music, atmosphere, and story are all TOP notch as well. The music is this amazing mix of electronic synth and metal. The atmosphere is thick and immersive, like you're really exploring this huge facility on Mars. And the story is honestly just as cool as the gameplay; the Doom Slayer has one simple goal and is completely apathetic towards all the internal politics, while Dr. Samuel Hayden is trying to balance a grey line of necessity and effectiveness, while Olivia Pierce has completely been brainwashed and given herself over to the demons. The lore documents are equally engrossing, a very scientific approach to all these monsters.

If you love challenge and intensity, play Doom!!!! It is some of the most fun I have ever had with a game.\t\t\t\t\t
