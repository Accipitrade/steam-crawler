\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is Doom, I don't have to say more. If You want to feel like an absolute badass, go to literal hell and slaughter hordes of demons while listening to great heavy metal music, give it a try. Audiovisuals are absolutely top-notch, the guns are well designed and feel great, and so are the demons. It is also nice to have an old-school health and armour, instead of just screen going red, and miraclously healing wounds behind a cover.

That said, if you're not only in for the action, the game gets repetitive with time. Same if you're not a big fan of exploration, as character progression is tied with that (although you can get upgrades that show you where stuff is hidden). Also, not a con, but if you're familiar with action games and want a challenge, you should pick a higher difficulty setting, as "normal" is too easy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this is the second or third time I've played through DOOM 2016. I originally had 100+ hours on Xbox, where I went and unlocked every achievement.

DOOM 2016 has been a go-to for me over the last few years. Combat is tight, fast paced and intense. Weapons all feel amazing to shoot and swapping between them is quick and painless.

DOOM's story mode is unquestionably there but it's far from in your face. You can truly choose to reach each Codex entry or just ignore them and blast until your hearts content.

Absolutely recommend this game to anyone who is tired of the online multiplayer only shooters.\t\t\t\t\t
