\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot a reboot, not a sequel, not a prequel, and not from another universe/timeline. DOOM is just a hot, fresh new game by Bethesda just waiting to be played. Playing as the iconic DOOMed Space Marine, the player must travel across Mars, Hell, and back-around to stop the Hell spawn from being released into the solar system. This first-person shooter was what id wanted back when computers still had floppy drives.

What I liked about this game:

Of course, the single-player campaign is the strongest point of any DOOM game. Period. The player controls the most savage being in existence. Hell was so afraid of him, that they trapped him in an accursed coffin; never to be released. But of course since you’re the Doomguy it doesn’t matter what box he’s in. The player travels through Mars and Hell to defeat the demons before it goes out of control. There is little to no dialogue, and most can be skipped entirely. 90% of the levels are fast paced and keep the player on their toes and their adrenaline pumping. Plenty of guns, powerups, and of course the BFG 9000. Glory Kills is the most prominent feature of the game, where players can perform the Art of the Kill in different UNIQUE positions on EVERY demon in the game. Playing on Nightmare difficulty is fun to get through, but with Perma-death on Ultra-Nightmare mode will leave players testing their gaming skills.

The SnapMap allows players to create their own levels and missions. They can play other players maps through cross-platforming, however, not WITH other players. Some are straight-forward, while some are even considered another mode.

Their latest update added a new single-player mode: Arcade Mode. No dialogue, no cutscenes. Just you, and the demons. The player must accumulate as many points as they can to get the Gold medal. However, one doesn’t have to settle for Gold… This is my favorite mode in the game. It pits the player with nothing-but adrenaline fueled shooting and killing, just like the good ol’ days.

What I didn’t like about this game:

The Argent Tower platform jumping section. This may be everyone’s hated level considering it was just climbing a tower for ¾ of the level without killing a lot of demons. Unfortunately, one slip from the tower is certain death, and the player would have to climb up again from the last save point. The ending of that level, however, is definitely worth using all the powerups and ammo.

The multiplayer is distinctly dead on the PC. I’ve waited 15 minutes for at least one guy to play with, and I got nothing. The taunts and skin customizations are nice. HOWEVER, the latest update allows players to play with bots and still have some sort of fun with what’s left of the multiplayer mode. The DLCs don’t do it justice either; I don’t recommend getting the DLCs unless you love playing as the demon, new skins, weapons, and taunts.

Overall:
One of the best shooters of all-time. I highly recommend buying this game, but I highly recommend buying it when it’s on sale (I got mine for $30 on Steam. A well-respected price for the single-player campaign). Its replay ability will fall, but it certainly won’t die like the fire in Doomguy’s heart.
[Here’s hoping for a DOOM II]\t\t\t\t\t
