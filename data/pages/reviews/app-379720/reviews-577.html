\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst things first, I absolutely ADORE this game. As a metalhead, this game was a gold mine for me because of the soundtrack. Mick Gordon was a GENIUS when it came to composing the soundtrack in this game. He blended Industrial Metal with Djent, and added a little Doom (The Genre, not the game) to it to create a stunning soundtrack. The 9-string guitar that Gordon used to make iconic songs like ‘Rip & Tear’ and ‘BFG Division’ pair so heavenly with the game’s gore, grit, and overall barbarousness (which are all great qualities to have in this case) .
https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2902773649
I could go on and on about how the soundtrack in this game is an ABSOLUTE masterpiece, but for everyone’s sanity I’ll keep it at that.

  Doom (2016) is a game that revitalized an already amazing franchise, and I praise the Devs for doing it correctly. The game’s story mode provides give or take 13 hours of gameplay. One of the many reasons that I like this game is that you don’t need to invest yourself into the actual story to enjoy it. You can load it up to sit back, relax, and Rip & Tear without a second thought. If you’re the type of player that plays games to invest themselves into it, this game also does that extremely well. The game has many collectibles, secrets, and achievements that can provide an extra layer of difficulty. Trying to fully complete this game to 100% was a pleasure since there were no annoying parts about it. The multiplayer was also a pretty nice addition to the game along with the couple of achievements that were tied to it. Overall, this game is one HELL of an experience, and I whole heartedly believe that everyone should play this game, 9/10.

TL;DR Metal Music in this Game is Beautifully Paired With Gore and Guts, 9/10. \t\t\t\t\t
