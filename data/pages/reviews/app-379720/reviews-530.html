\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFast-paced, high adrenaline game. It's really good and fun game. Moving constantly, adjust weapons for every different situation constantly and killing demons really give enjoyable feeling. There's not much of a story, but in this type of game nobody pays too much attention to it anyway, you just want to shoot 'em up and that's exactly what you get. Level designs are amazing, really well done, open areas where battle begins are done just right, you can move constantly, use some environment to your advantage, there's enough recourses too to restock after each skirmish, even on higher difficulties, not too much of platforming, just the way I like it.

Basically, when you get into the new area and demons starts spawning in, you just forget everything around you and just start blasting.

With that said I have some major issues with DOOM. First one is weapons' upgrade challenge system. I just hate it; getting first upgrades on weapons are easy but to get last one is cancer for me. It kinda forced you to use specific weapon constantly to get that last upgrade.
For example lets take super shotgun - you have have to kill 2 or more demons with single shot 15 or so times and only then you'll get that last upgrade on your weapon, Well f**k that b**ch, I want to upgrade my weapon as usual - with upgrade point earning it by killing demons. If they wanted to implement this system into game that's fine, but at least they could've done it optional, with possibility to turn it off completely.
And note, that some weapons (for me it was plasma rifle) are pretty much useless without that last upgrade (especially if you're going to play on Nightmare difficulty).

My second issue with this game was - there's just too much exploring, for secrets, hidden doomguy's suit upgrades, weapon mod upgrades and all that s**t.
In this type of game, me personally, I just wanna grab my gun and shoot enemies, then resupply, go to the next area and repeat all over. But then again, you are forced to do this boring map exploring because you want to get those upgrade points, upgrade your stuff... Cmon man, it's not Witcher, just make it straightforward.

My third and last issue was game's length - on Ultra violence difficulty it roughly took about 9hrs for me to beat this game. Even for 20 euros there should be more. But I get it, you can replay it all over again on different difficulties, there's also MP (I haven't played it) etc.

Anyway, even though it's game from 2016, DOOM still delivers good quality, game doesn't feel dated at all, graphics are lit, gameplay really fun (aside ♥♥♥♥♥♥ challenge system), definitely worth it.

8/10\t\t\t\t\t

On a more serious note... yes, I had a ton of fun playing this game.
Yes, I recommend this game.
If you want a throwback to classic Doom with some welcome modern updates, buy this game.

The graphics are nice and gorey, as Doom should be.

The story is just enough to tell you WHY, without getting in the way too much.

And the gameplay is gloriously gung ho, with an "always move, always be attacking" mentality that fits Doom perfectly.

The classic weapons are back, with added options for more capability for anti daemon destruction. The new weapons are also quite fun too, and I found myself enjoying finding my favorites, and sometimes swapping to weapons slightly better suited to certain enemies.

Doom is just plain fun, and I heartily recommend it if you want to spend about 15 hours blasting away at ever increasing numbers of demons, and scouring the levels for secrets, just like the original.\t\t\t\t\t
