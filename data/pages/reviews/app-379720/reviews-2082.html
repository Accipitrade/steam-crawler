\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOriginal DOOM is a great game. They managed to copy it alright:
~~PROS~~ (what they got from the original DOOM)
- Fast movement, no reloading
- Heavy Metal
- Carnage
- Varied enemy types which require you to think about which weapon to use
- Health and Armor pickups

~~CONS~~ (what they missed)
- The level design is very linear with almost no exploration
- Many paths or doors are one way (lame)
- Glory kills get old fast. (there's a rune to speed them up at least)
- The rpg elements added very little while making it harder to balance (felt cheap to use special skills all the time)
- The rich get richer rune (infinite ammo) breaks the game
- Every battle arena has power-ups, but you often don't need them. Makes the victory less satisfying.
- Most levels are just a string of battle arenas, the enemy placement isn't very interesting. It's all super obvious and uninspired.
- You never get cornered, never have to run, never get ambushed
- "Hurt me Plenty" is not normal mode, it's easy mode
- It's never dark. It's never scary. Over correction from doom 3
- Buggy physics engine
- The audio compressor is a little edgy. You can hear it cutting every time any ui sound happens.
- Every space is super open. Really easy to dodge everything.

All in all, it's a pretty fun game, but I really don't think that's saying much. They were trying to copy one of the most inspired games of all time. Bethesda and Id could have done so, so much better by being a bit more aware of what makes DOOM such a classic. I really never got the feeling that the level/game designers actually understood DOOM, they were mostly just trying to copy it (and missed some of the most important factors).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat can I say? ...it is Doom. One of the first First Person Shooters. They set the standard quite high with the original.

The good thing is... they brought us the bigger brother of it. Nothing totally different, it is like an up-to-date version of the original.

The same weapons... but with more options, more variations to create gore.

There are not many tactics needed to play this game. It is about moving forward and attack head on. Of course you need to dodge a lot of enemies and their projectiles. But this game does not come with a stealth mode.

New things added are the finishers, challenges and upgrades for weapons. The maps are fun to explore to try to get all the secrets and the items that come with it. A few maps lock halfway so there is no backtracking.

All in all I am happy with my purchase, it feels nostalgic because it is like the same as before but bigger, badder and meaner.
