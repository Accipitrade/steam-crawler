\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played the original Doom the week it came out in 1993 on a 386 computer and loved it. 2016 DOOM is a worthy reboot that is bags of fun, not perfect but overall a great game experience.

DOOM is well optimized, getting 60FPS on my old GTX750ti and dual core PC. I had occasional minute long periods of 6FPS and sound stutters but I attribute that to my ageing hardware. Glad I had a SSD game drive, brings load time way down.

I beat the campaign on Hurt Me Plenty and it was fairly easy except for a couple of the bosses, I'm a scrub though.

The download is really huge unfortunately, just about the only downside. I bought it on Steam Sale super cheap.

Environments are pleasingly spectacular and the monsters are excellent tributes to the originals. Some of them have been buffed notably the Pinky which requires absurd amounts of damage to kill.

The hidden classic levels are a great feature. Levels in general are linear, suits me fine. Weapons models and variety are acceptable. Rune challenge minigames were either very easy or stupid hard.

Gameplay is fast, fluid and overall enjoyable. You figure out the play style quickly which is always pushing forward. Not a game for snipers, what we used to call "Ramboing" is now the order of the day.

The biggest change to gameplay is the glory kill, which along with a few other attacks yield dumps of health and ammo. The first few times its fun buuuut I found it to slow down the pace of really intense combat. The animations were long enough to interrupt the flow of the melee, especially when you are tearing into a big crowd of monsters.

Played a few games of multiplayer which is not really my bag, seemed good enough but what the heck do I know. I'm here for the single player.

Pros:

Fun, fun, fun
Runs very well
Updated monsters
Cacodemon mouth

Cons:

70 Gb DL
Questionable Glory Kills

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a reminder of what an excellent FPS single player campaign can be. ID software suceeeded in capturing the spirit of the original while adding a modern flair to it that doesn't detract from the essence of what DOOM is. DOOM casts a spell that many refer too as the shooter trance. Once it sinks its claws into you, it doesn't let go.

The gameplay hinges on high mobility and pushing forward. Its mechanics work so well together that it creates a synergistic rythmn. It wouldn't be out of place to liken DOOM to ballet. You can gun down a pack of imps, climb a wall or scale a ledge, perform a glory kill and then jump back down and chainsaw a hell knight.

The more you keep moving the better your odds of staying alive are. The game rewards you for being aggressive. Its incredibly satisfying to be on your last legs only to peform a gruesome glory kill and watch as health packs spring out. Low on health and ammo? Take a chainsaw to an enemy and recover both. It creates a satisfying loop of adrenaline pumping action.

Doom's story is simple but it doesn't have to be overly complex. How much context do you really need to punch demons in the face...(or through the chest). It gives you enough to get going, and lets you have at it. DOOM knows what it is and knows what you want to do. The way the story is told is through subtlety. You see Doomguy's no ****'s given attitude on full display and its those subtle cues that characterize him.

The game initially gave me some trouble getting it to run until I updated to the latest game ready driver. I still get some stuttering and slowdown in the menus initially but once in the game my GTX 750 ti handles it well at medium settings. I get a stable 50-60 fps. The game runs buttery smooth outside of those few problems I mentioned.\t\t\t\t\t
