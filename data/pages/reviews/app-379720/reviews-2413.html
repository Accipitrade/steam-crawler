\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the better FPS games released in recent years. If you like swapping between weapons, bouncing and dodging projectiles from every direction while exploring semi open levels and Killing a grand variety of demons, this is not one to pass up.

The level design isnt the same as old Doom games. Instead of moving and clearing rooms one at a time, you'll enter an arena, doors lock, demons spawn, you kill them. In some ways, they've improved and modernized it in all the right ways; there's a lot more verticality in the arenas to move through now, where in old doom they had to keep it pretty flat due to engine limitations. The secrets range from too obvious, to clever, to obtuse, so it's every bit as exciting to find a secret now as it was before. Unfortunately the focus on Arenas led to one issue that I'll go into detail with later

They also went full quake/unreal tournament with the jump pads and portals, so your never really at a lack of options with your movement.

Weapons, new and old, pack a decent punch(except pistol, but who cares about pistol?) the shotgun is pretty useless once you get the super shotgun, but the rest of them remain in use all the way to the end.

Updates to the classic enemies are also a welcome addition. I was glad to see Imps jumping all over the place, Pinkys charging like bulls, and the mancubus doing parkour(because why not). Barons of hell are my favorite, they are now a menacing and real threat instead of just bigger Imps

Unfortunately it comes with some baggage. The most obvious of which would be the checkpoint system. There's nothing fun or even challenging about spending 5 minutes clearing a room, only to be killed from out of nowhere and forced to do it all over again. It's 2016, Manual Quicksaves or bust.

The soundtrack ranges from alright to WUB WUB WUB WUB WUB, bang your head against wall, obnoxcious. Doom games deserve a metal soundtrack, not a techno dubstep soundtrack. No excuses.
Even Doom 3 had one.

Animations for everything. I miss the days when pushing a button to open a door was instantaneous. Doubly so when picking up a weapon, and tripply so when falling from a height.

Glory Kills. They are obnoxcious, tiresome, break the pace of the game, and worst of all: downright mandatory. The game was clearly balanced in such a way that you are supposed to pick up a majority of your health and ammo from glory killing enemies and secret areas, but during the longer fights in arenas you wont be looking for secrets, thus leaving the responsibility entirely on the glory kills that you've seen a million times.

When i'm knee deep in the dead and low on health, the last thing I want to be doing is stopping, shaking the camera around, then repeating four or five times until health and ammo are full.

I'd be fine with them if they were an optional move that you rarely have to avail yourself of, but in many cases, failing to glory kill the enemy will refill half their health after you rightfully widdled them down to zero.

And finally, a nitpick: Weapon upgrades in a doom game?

Sounds really cool on paper actually, but how do you get these upgrades, I hear you asking. You progress through the game like it's an RPG and "level up," your guns.

Would it not make infinately more sense to just hide weapon upgrades in the various secret areas you've sprinkled throughout the game? Would that not be more like a DOOM game? Would that not make exploration into a more nessessary part of the experience now that health and ammo are infinetly spawning from enemies?

Ultimately, the game is fun and well worth your time. Just wish they'd stuck a little closer to the older games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter years of playing Call of duty and months of Rainbow six siege, coming back to DOOM is an experience that is both new and old to me. This is from someone who unfortunately never played the original.

Despite so, I can at least liken it to two other games that provided me a very similar experience: System Shock II and Deus Ex. They both have you being the random commando going in and kill everything. That's about as much as it gets similar to DOOM. I never had a thing going on for solo campaign games where its just you because as aforementioned I was very accustomed to the illusion of teammates, singleplayer in COD, actual people in R6 (Ashamedly, its because the lack of such gives me the scares)

However DOOM is different. It empowers you.
It doesn't matter the difficulty or whether you die frequently or not
It doesn't matter if you run out of ammo and the demon horde is still there
It doesn't matter if they want to kill you

What only matter was that you can kill them
No matter what you'll always find a way to kill
There is no stealth, you're not empowered to hide and act with discretion
You see them, they see you, and before you know it
You both are embroiled in a battle to the death

I have to give DOOM a special kind of recommendation, for making me coming back to a "no-teammate-whatsoever" singleplayer experience where I feel I am the boss. With a wide variety of armaments and arsenals at your disposal, melee or ranged, with plenty of area to discover and admire ( the graphical fidelity of ID tech 6 ) and ESPECIALLY the number of demons, you will come to realise that buying DOOM comes with an exhilirating experience.

Absolutely DOOM.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow.

I can't say I've had this much fun with combat in a first person shooter since ever. Everything makes you feel like you're really ripping demons to absolute shreds. The visuals are eye popping, everything looks awesome, and the atmosphere does a really good job of pulling you into the creature-ripping madness that this game loves.

Some of the areas in some of the levels tend to run a little iffy, at least in my experience. That didn't take away from the game overall however.

The music is awesome, the gameplay feels good, and overall I loved every moment of this bloodbath.
