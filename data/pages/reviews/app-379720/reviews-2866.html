\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 possesses one of the best campaigns of any shooter made in the last decade. Its shooting and movement mechanics are sublime, smooth, and incredibly fast-paced. The extraordinary soundtrack by Mick Gordon enchances every aspect of the game and the bombastic combat themes propel you to kill the demons of hell as fast and violently as you can. At first glance the Glory Kill system seems like a curious mechanical choice but ends up greatly benefitting the momentum of the game. The maze-like level design is a wonderful bit of nostalgia for anyone who played shooters of the 90's and is benefitted by an excellent map system.

The story and exposition in DOOM are minimal and that is a good thing. I found myself intrigued by the world and the characters and was happy to take a few moments to read the collectible lore entries, but these are easily ignored if you prefer. The collectible items and level challenges give nice replay value to each map and harder difficulty levels tempt you to push yourself to overcome some truly foes.\t\t\t\t\t

This is how a shooter campaign should look, sound and play.

A single playthrough with decent exploration (secrets/rune challenges) on the third difficulty setting takes about 7-8 hours if you're an experienced shooter player, which is a bit too short for my liking.

  • Story:
    Simplistic, if you're looking for an epic adventure and huge cutscenes, you won't be satisfied, still interesting enough to care for the conclusion.

  • Graphics:
    Insane, this game takes place in many confined corridors and well.... Hell, yet it looks really pretty and the performance on a GTX 1060 is really good (1080p/high details - always 60 fps, BFG can drop it to 57 or so, lol).

  • Audio:
    The surround effects with a decent 5.1 setup are insanity, whenever I activated one of those "Doomslayer lore stones" I got freaking chills, loved it. Oh and the guns, grenades, demons and the blood flowing from the ceiling, all have perfect directional sound effects.

  • Gameplay:
    Movement feels tight (no strafe jumping sadly)
    Gunplay is working flawless, upgrading is fun, i wish the earlier weapons wouldn't share ammunition with the more powerful versions.
    There are a ton of secrets to find (runes that let you collect health/ammo drops from farther away, give more air control etc.)!


  • Multiplayer:
    In the era of Overwatch it feels kinda lackluster to be honest. I would probably play Quake Live again before I even consider Doom. I wish they added Singleplayer content instead of 3 MP DLCs.

My personal rating for DOOM: 8.2/10\t\t\t\t\t
