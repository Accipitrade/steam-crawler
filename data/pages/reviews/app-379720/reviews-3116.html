\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a teenager, I vividly remember blasting thru the halls of Doom and Doom ][ and their expansions, not to mention Heretic and HeXen. Doom 3, for me, was kind of a letdown in playstyle. While I do appreciate a good horror/survival game, Doom it was not.

Doom (2016) - or DOOM, as it is now stylized - marks a return to the heyday of Doom games. Run and gun, pick up equipment, ammunition, and health. While the various informational objects make a return from Doom 3, it does add to the gameplay and storyline more than the ending text of the original two Doom games. It's nice to find out some background on UAC and the experiments they had going, while leaving out a lot of the mysticism that Doom 3 brought to the table.

Much like a lot of other people, I have found the multiplayer in DOOM to be lacking. This is where DOOM does NOT return to its roots. While the maps offer enough variety, and the weapons are there, I don't find any significant reason (like enjoyment) to go back to it. The tiresome levels and experience systems that seems to be more and more popular these days has replaced the good 'ole Doom Deathmatch's "shoot anything that moves and good luck, sucker" type of gameplay that was always a hallmark of any good Doom Deathmatch. Multiplayer feels more like a tired Quake Arena mod combine with cheap-looking armor skins.

I would say that the single-player mode itself is enough to warrant buying DOOM, and you shouldn't have to think twice about it. Multiplayer mode is going to depend on each individual person. I suppose there are just as many people that like it as there are people that don't.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThat was awesome! Super fast paced, enough of a challenge but not too over bearing. Overall the campaign is fun and really engaging, but mildly predictable since you can tell you're going to be locked in an area to fight off hoards of demons, but it doesn't take away from the flow. I went into this blind, never having played a past DOOM title and the story was easy to follow. This is the game that never ends, you fight one boss and think, "Well that must have been the most powerful demon in Hell, I must be close." Then another boss comes to challenge you, personally I put my shoulders down and gladly took on the fight. I'm looking forward to the replayablity of looking for the secrets.
