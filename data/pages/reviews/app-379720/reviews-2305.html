\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat I expected to get when I purchased DOOM was a dumb straightforward shooter with no ifs or buts, which it is... for the first level.

What I got beyond that was yet another game with a "Challenge" system. I f***ing hate challenges. I hated them in Fallout: New Vegas, I hated them in Far Cry 5, I hated them in every other game that has them, and I will always hate them for as long as they exist. I hate it when a game pretends to give me the freedom to play it as I see fit, only to then force me to play it in specific ways so I can unlock all of its content and upgrades.

Did I mention there are collectibles? Countless collectibles freakishly well-hidden in each and every one of the huge labyrinthine maps? Collectibles that're mandatory for upgrading your guns? Collectibles in a game like DOOM? I mean, WHAT THE HELL (pun intended)? I don't care how humorous the game is meant to be, but this just sucks.

Don't get me wrong, the game certainly has its merits. The gameplay is fast-paced, the guns rock, the graphics are great, the glory kills are awesome, and the music is neat, too (although pretty lame as far as Metal is concerned; a game like this would've deserved something much more hardcore). Still, all in all there's far too little time spent shooting ♥♥♥♥ and waaaay too much time wasted searching for useless crap (action figures? Seriously? What's next, flags and feathers?) just to scrape together the upgrade points you need to improve your gear.
The upside is that, in a minute or so, I'll have freed an additional 60GB of SSD space on my rig for (hopefully) better games :-).

Verdict: 4 / 10 - greatest disappointment of the year. Yeah, I know that isn't saying much in January, but that doesn't make me feel better about the 20€ I just flushed down the drain. At all.\t\t\t\t\t
