\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf it wasn't for the ending this would be one of the best FPS I have ever played. It is one of the best arena shooters I have played so far, that's for sure.

Guns: Good weapon variation because you can modify 7 of the 9 guns you get in the game with 2 different secondary functions. The gunplay is really nice even though I have to say that I would've liked the energy weapons to have a bit more heft behind them.

Enemies: Also good variation here, enough to not get bored with the same enemies over and over, but also not too much so the player can still distinguish them in a fast fight...most of the time.

Movement: You're always running, the real way to go around in the older generation of FPS and in my opinion the better solution, instead of the stupid low stamina our game protagonists usually get today. You get double jump 1/3 into the game campaign and it works as intended and is pretty fun to play around with. The only issue I have with the doulbe jump, when you jump too late while running at a ledge and are already in the air before pressing jump the first time, you will only get one jump and that normally means you don't get to your target. The grabbing to climb onto ledges also works pretty well even if you can occasionally bump into ledges where the Doomguy just doesn't want to grab if you don't look at it in the perfect angle.

Arenas: Most arenas are well designed, mostly with some bigger open spaces and some corners where you can get yourself in trouble if you don't stay in motion. And some classic elements are also there like portals and powerups. I had one bug where I defeated all enemies in the arena, but the game didn't realise it and I couldn't progress until I loaded from the last checkpoint and played the arena again.

Checkpoints: As far as I have seen all checkpoints are well placed and you don't loose much progress if you die.

Story: I don't really care for the story but except for the ending the story ist still pretty decent. What I really like are the little story fragments you can find in the hell -level, where the interactions of the demons with the Doomguy are described from the perspective of the demons.

Ending: The Olivia Pierce-spider is a pretty bad endboss, it's not interesting to fight against it and I had no fun with it. And then Samuel Hayden, who did nothing but give you condescending orders throughout the whole game, gets the super cool sword with you just watching him take it from you. This ending feels rushed and it's clear, it's only there so they can setup another game. It really dissapointed me with the bad end.

So overall, a really good FPS that can restore part of the classic Doom feel. I had many fun hours with it and will most probably play it through a 2nd time to get all or at least most of the secrets. But with a pretty dissapointing ending that is only there to setup another game and not to end this very good game appropriately.\t\t\t\t\t

And that would be at full price, imo.

However I did wait until it was on sale, but after completing the game I would have had no regrets had I purchased it at full price. But I mean come on now, who passes on a cheaper price for a game they are interested in but not 100% about??

Pros:
- Runs super smooth and looks absolutely stunning (Radeon Rx580 gpu and 8-core 4.3 ghz for referrence)
- The combat is really fast and the transition from one enemy to the next is just as fast but feels natural in its quickness and gruesome brutality
- Collectibles and secrets are fun to hunt and find, but have no impact on the game imo
- Variety of enemy spawns during heavy engagements was refreshing
- Enemy A.I. while nothing to gawk at, is still fairly sharp and manages to make almost any enemy no matter how basic or elite feel as if they pose some legitimate threat to you

Cons:
- Caused increased heart rate, and choking on drinks when pinning me in confined spaces with large and tough to kill elites
- The story of the game ends in a very cliche and expected sort of way, an ending I called within 2 mins of starting the game


9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM (2016) ... the only FPS to give meaning for single player.
Let me sum my review to pros and cons and then (if you want , you can read my whole review for my reasons as to why the game is so GOOD !!)
9/10

pros
-Great single player
-Weapons are unique and and awesome
-soundtrack is pure BADASS
-Enemy variety !! something that's missing in alot in FPS shooters these days.
-Fast pace combat that feels like Devil may Cry with guns rather than swords
-custom maps by users is always great

cons
-multiplayer is not competitve
-very unbalance to be honest
-needs more boss battles

DOOM delivers what game should be like ... fun and chellenging.
It kinda feels like playing DMC4 with its fast pace combat.
You always need to move , different weapons behave different and camping on enemy AI is discouraged by how the game was create for you.
Get close and RIP AND TEAR UNTIL ITS DONE !!!
dogde, shoot , jump , glory kill and move to the next objective.

This leads me to the story. I'll be honest I didn't listen to the story narrative given by the robot guy , the evil woman and that demonic voice.
The game gives you the option to simple speedrun throu the game if you just care about playing the game.
How ? well , instead of being stuck in a cutsence or a walking section , you get listen to the narrative played in stations around the levels' map.
This goes very well with the main character , Doom guy/slayer (You).
So far , I heard that the story was great.

Of course , I can't avoid the big plus that this FPS game has over TONS of FPS shooters which is enemy variety.
Each enemy type fights different and you need to know who is the most threatning before you go and slay the others.
This comes to play heavily in hard difficulties , so get a grip on your mouse and GIT GUD !

Last is the multiplayer.... this wasn't heavily developed with thought.
Its too chaotic.

I was looking forward that Doom online might be like the old Halo 3/2 multiplayer.
No class loadout (ONE default "loadout"), just weapon spawns. this could have made the online much better rather than coping the old COD formula.

I can already tell you this , the ability to give players to transform to demons that instant kill you will enrage you more than being killed by BFG blast.

luckily , replay value has its root in arcade more and custom maps made by users.
I played alot in arcade more to get S ranking (Slayer not Stylish XD).
Kinda of the reason why I think this game is DMC with guns rather than swords.

so overall .... the first FPS game that is par with the Halo games (except 4 and 5).
