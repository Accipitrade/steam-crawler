\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a fantastic mix of oldschool and contemporary packaged in the best of ways.

The fast, gliding movement and lack of sprinting, ironsights, or even magazines for that matter work well with the brightly colored item pickups that really let you know this has the mechanics of more classic FPS.
And yet its still quite modern with good visuals and animations, and even a few modern mechanics such as context-sensitive ledge-grabbing and contextual executions. There's also a nice character progression system, which seems to be quite popular in shooters these days.
And to top it off, the hidden retro stuff and collectibles satisfy the completionist in me, and yet are completely optional for those who are not interested in the little things.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBy far the best shooter I have ever played. It has the only story line I could play. CoD ruined the trust in campaign modes for FPS games, but doom brought it back.

It should also be noted I have encountered 0 game breaking glitches during my playthroughs of the campaign. Thats right *ZERO GLITCHES*

Bullet Sponge Demons are a common thing in this game, especially on nightmare difficulty. It got to the point that in the later games that I would enter a room with full ammo, and I would end up having to use only my hands and my pistol to dispatch the last demoms because of just how much ammo a running little meatball of a demon can take to the face without even slowing his roll.

That being said, this game is the standard that every FPS will have to live up to in years to come. And I can assure you this.

Nothing will ever be as good as DOOM.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLate in buying this game and bought it on sale. I'm an older gamer who grew up even before the orginal Doom and Wolfenstein but played the heck out of them. I always loved them for their just straight forward unashamed kill everything approach. Sure, I enjoy the new shooters too where you sneak and use tactics, but sometimes that's just over done.

So, I started up Doom and was pleasently pleased. Right off the bat you just start killing. Plot? Sure there's a plot. Kill everything. Your thrown into room after room where you don't sneak around like you're afraid to get seen. You boldly crash into the room ready to just kick ass. If it moves, kill it. Heck, even if it's lying on the ground already, just fire a few shots in it to be sure. And, the more gruesome and hands on your kills are you get rewarded with health boosts or ammo if you cut hem up with the chainsaw. Nice touch. Even the music drives you forward to just go forward and be a machine of death. Don't run. Don't hide. Don't sneak around and be a baby. Jump out into the light and just start killing everything with glee.

The games just a nice throw back to the days of what a shooter used to be. I can see myself havin many and many days of wasted retirement hours at the keyboard on this game, and I haven't even touched multiplayer yet.\t\t\t\t\t
