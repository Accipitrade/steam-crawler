\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing DOOM Eternal, you realize just how slow this is. Not that that's bad, it's actually good. It's slow but not enough to be boring, it's decently fast-paced, especially the farther you get in the campaign. I also like the atmosphere of this game much much more, as it feels like just two interconnected worlds you're exploring: Hell and Mars. In Eternal, it feels very distinct in the fact that each level is essentially isolated with almost zero connection with the previous or the following ones. The levels feel much more grounded in reality and closed in comparison to DOOM Eternal's very vertical and open levels that are very fun, but they feel much more fantastic and aren't very realistic. (What's a big open arena thing doing in a research facility? UAC Atlantica, I'm looking at you.) If the third DOOM that we're gonna get is something of a mix of the two, I think it'll be the perfect FPS. A decently grounded shooter with believable levels that is still fast-paced and has lots of action.

This is not to hate on DOOM Eternal, though. I think that game is absolutely great, but it does have its flaws when we compare it to its predecessor. In general however, it is much more fun and dynamic than DOOM 2016. Where DOOM 2016 wins is as I said, in the atmosphere of the game. It's also less hands-on than DOOM Eternal. By that I mean that it isn't as limiting as its successor. Weaknesses are a good idea, but when it feels like the other guns are debilitated to compensate, it feels a lot more restricting. Then again, it may be because I'm not the best player. I've only managed to complete DOOM Eternal in Nightmare- this game in Nightmare is a lot more difficult in my eyes. I've only passed a decent chunk of the game (around 80% or 70%) in Ultra Violence, so there's that.

Would I recommend this over DOOM Eternal? I'd say so. It's akin to dipping your toes in the kiddy pool- you shouldn't start off by doing a 20 meter jump into one hell of a swimming pool. Don't misunderstand me though, both games are good on their own right. You should also take into account the fact that I played this as my first game on my first ever gaming PC which definitely adds some bias to this conclusion, but I love both games and play them every once in a while to relax from my friends' shenanigans or just to have a bit of shooting fun without the competitiveness of multiplayer FPS's.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen you want to play a nigh unstoppable bad ass there is little that can compare to running through Doom. The last Doom I played was Doom 3 and I enjoyed the hell outta that game, but the big difference between that game and this one was the atmosphere. In Doom 3 is was a shooter with the horror aspect still there (I would say a good comparison was Dead Space but with FPS controls) where as this Doom was more of an action shooter vs a horror sf shooter. The plot is also no where near as complex or creepy than Doom 3 so for me its like a gun-toting romp more that the atmospheric shooter it used to be.

I'm hoping the Doom Eternal is more along to lines of how Doom 3 was presented but I am not holding out much hope with the success for this installment.

Overall, a fun game to play especially if I just want to run around and shoot things without giving much thought to anything.\t\t\t\t\t
