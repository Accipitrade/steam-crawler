\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is one of the most badass games ever created.

After playing the beta, I was a bit apprehensive and thought that perhaps it would turn out to be disappointing, but I've never been more happy to be proven wrong. As a reboot/remake of the original, it more than delivers. The action is fast and frantic, with battles quickly culminating in absolute chaos, putting your reflexes to the test and making you feel like an absolute juggernaut as you dismember and obliterate one demon after another, and it has one of the most complete weapon arsenals in any FPS. The addition of glory kills, which function as brutal finishing moves, makes the game all the more visceral and satisfying, and is way better than what Brutal DOOM tried to pull off - plus they're completely optional if you ever get tired of them. The intensity and horror angle of the original is also on full display. The sound effects are unnerving and the atmosphere is oppressive and bleak. I play the game wired on caffeine, so I was constantly jittery and on the edge of my seat, which resulted in some great jump scare moments like when you turn around and a pinky is directly in your face, which also happened in the original masterpiece.

The pulse-pounding soundtrack adds an extra layer of awesomeness to the game and hits at all the right moments. It's so good, you'll want to listen to it outside of the game as well, and with a decent pair of headphones or sound system, the game stands out as an audio masterwork as well as a visual one, with the stunning and detailed graphics containing the true definition of the wow factor. Some have complained that the game gets repetitive towards the end, but I didn't have the same experience. In fact, I found that after I defeated the epic final boss, I wanted the game to continue, but was instead greeted by an interesting cliffhanger ending that hints at the inevitable DOOM 2 and a terrific credits sequence, which is nothing to complain about. The pacing is fantastic and the game constantly takes your breath away with its thrilling set piece moments and challenging enemies that swarm you in numbers. Plus, unlike most other modern shooters, this one is open ended and both rewards and requires exploration. No linear nonsense here apart from a few exceptions, and even in its most linear moments, it's a heck of a lot of fun.

The multiplayer is an entertaining enough diversion, but definitely won't replace your favorite multiplayer game, nor will it blow you away, as it doesn't do enough to set itself apart from the likes of Unreal Tournament, Quake, and Halo, but looking at it as a bonus to the fabulous campaign, it's hard to complain, and it can be fun in short bursts.

DOOM is everything I could've possibly wanted in a modern take on the series, and it returns the series to its roots while also making notable improvements on what came before it and also putting nearly every other modern shooter to shame. This is the DOOM game we've all been waiting for.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is mindless shooting, over and over and over, and it is perfect because of that.
It does not have the tension and fear of Doom 3, it is all action, fast and furious with great graphics and a great soundtrack.
As FPS go, this is about is purely entertaining as it gets. There is no reason to hide behind things, you must keep moving, jumping, picking things up, all while selecting weapons and blasting everything that moves in to messy bloody chunks.
If you want a little bit more depth, there are things to find, both optionally as secrets (models, and more useful "rune challenges" which give selectable upgrades) and because you have to find keycards.
It's much less stress than Doom 3, because you don't wait in fear of being attacked, you are almost always being attacked, there is no time to think, just shoot.
If you liked the orginal doom, you will love this.
If you never played the original doom, you will probably still love this.
If you kind of liked doom 3 but didn't like that feeling of things jumping out at you (like alien isolation), this is nothing like it. Even on the hardest out of the box setting of ultra violence, there is still plenty of ammo to keep blasting. You never die because of a reload either, if you run out of ammo it swaps weapon for you and you just keep shooting.
Perfect FPS, the only problem is it crashes every now and then. There are frequent checkpoints though so if it does crash you don't lose any progress.
If you enjoy shooting things without any tactical thought other than using ledges and platforms, this is for you.
It's easy to learn how to play it in less than 5 minutes, and then it just gets better and better the more you play.
There is a bit of replayability if you want to get all the collectibles, and anyway, once you finish it you get harder modes unlocked.
I didn't try the MP yet, but I have plenty of MP games nd it is nice to have an old school single player game to just blow stuff to bits, and a choice of two shotguns :)
It's very fun and very gory.
Oh, and the chainsaw is awesome.
UPDATE: Now I have finished the game, I will add there is replayability in this game and it carries on being fun. When you are not rushing through and have time to find all the secrets, there are loads of nice little touches and attention to detail.
For example, I am getting the collectable models, which involve collecting little mini marines. When you find the doom marine collectable... well I won't spoil it, but let's just say he does something different to all the others :)
The challenges on each level are all varied and different, the secrets, some of them take a lot of exploring around, and the weapon challenges are the most fun of all.
One of them involves killing 5 hell knights with a headshot, another is killing 5 summoners with locked on missles (they teleport away when you try this). There are dozens of challenges between the weapons, level challenges, and secrets.
The game is not overly hard even on ultra violence, apart from the boss levels (espeically Oliva, really hard). Normal demons are easy enough once you have the weapons, but it's good to know there are extra difficulties for when I have unlocked everything to give me another go at it with more challenge.
Superb game, and best of all there are hidden maps from the orignal doom as easter eggs.
It's the best Doom ever made, and feels like playing the original, but everything bigger and better.
Great job ID software!
Uodate 2: for all those who say the game is hard to beat, I did this on ultra violence on my first play through, but if you are struggling... now i have all the upgrades and rune challenges, here is a way to make it VERY EASY! Get the Mobile Turrent upgrade for chaingun, AND mastery so it never overheats, along with the Rich get Richer Rune challenge, and upgrade that.
You can then spam everything with the chain gun and never run out of ammo. It is so overpowered it feels like a hack, but it's still fun :)\t\t\t\t\t
