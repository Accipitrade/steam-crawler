\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I certainly enjoy the Rip'n'Tear lifestyle and the refined feel of well executed classic arcade combat, I think the attention to the player experience is what speaks most to me. It's something that has become secondary or even irrelevant to many developers, and it's most sorely missed once a game like this brings it back. Some examples include:

-Very forgiving ledge grabs.
-Frequent checkpoints with minimal downtime after deaths.
-Secrets that require effort and intuition, rather than blind luck to discover.
-The option to get upgrades to reveal the map and secret locations.
-Well designed time trials! Destroy 30 barrels! Oops! Missed 1? Now it's highlighted!
-Tastefully packed with humour and pop culture references that don't distract from the game.

I'm not a huge fan of shooters, which is why it took me 4 years to get to this title despite buying it at launch. And while I don't think it ticks all MY boxes for an amazing game. I'm having an absolute blast because that's what they designed it for. This game was designed with passion and made FOR the players. Absolutely recommend it!\t\t\t\t\t
