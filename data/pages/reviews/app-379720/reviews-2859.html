\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn one hand, I can see the care and craft, at least in the single player campaign. There's a sense of humour here, the graphics are gorgeous, the combat feels like what a fast first person shooter should be, and the first five minutes is just great, turning every FPS trope on it's head. This game is respectful of it's source material, but somehow manages to update one of the first great FPS games from the early 1990s into something that plays in 2016. That's a massive accomplishment.

But on the other hand, as odd as this sounds, this game... well, it's just more Doom. For somebody like me, to whom FPS games are hit and miss at best, it's just... okay. I'm personally not a big fan of the jumping puzzles, and I can recognize the technical quality of the combat, but I don't feel any visceral feel while being involved in the combat.

I'm not a big fast FPS fan in general, but, after picking up Titanfall 2 and loving that, I thought I'd give this a go. But, it just didn't work out for me.

If you love Doom, or fast First Person Shooters in general, you'll love the Doom campaign. (Didn't even get as far as the multiplayer, so no clue if people who like that will want to play it.) But if you're kind of meh on fast FPS in the first place, this game probably won't change your mind.

I have thought and thought and can only come up with a single negative thing. The menus and loading times are a bit slow. THATS IT.

Please buy this game. It's worth it even at full price.

Ultra Nightmare difficulty can keep you busy for quite a while in single player campaign mode. You might never even need the multiplayer.

The multiplayer itself is rather different from single player, and kind of a disappointment for those of us who played more team-based shooters like csgo. As far as I can tell, in team modes people don't really care about teammates much, at least the lower skilled players. And with the lack of proper ranking and matchmaking, you get totally random players, which kind of sucks. Still, it's relaxing to sometimes play a fast-paced deathmatch.

There is also the Snapmode, which is about community created maps, so if you're sick of campaign you can play various (very different) maps, both alone and with friends.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is like Painkiller or Serious Sam moreso than the original Doom games. Brutal, fast, constantly in motion, and repetitive. It's a lot of getting locked into horde battles in Quake 3-ish arenas interspersed with bits of linear platforming to the next battle. Things like keys and skulls are pointless because the linear levels aren't designed to be mazes or with backtracking in mind.

I think the music is the biggest letdown. The original Doom has some of the catchiest, head bobbing music in an FPS. The game has an impressive dynamic soundtrack that adapts to the action, but it ranges from ambient to just violent and punchy guitar. There's nothing memorable and nothing to hum along to.\t\t\t\t\t
