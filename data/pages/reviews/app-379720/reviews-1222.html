\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is rated just as well as good as it really is. A large concern for me is that the combat would become repetitive and boring when really the game is constantly throwing new features and upgrades at you to keep you invested. Another great solution to this concern is that the hack and slash combat has several different varieties of animations, and different uuuuhhhhhh parts that you can hit. Another problem I thought I'd have was no focus on world building or story (which yes that's not the point of the game). But I'm again surprised to see myself becoming even more invested in the story, and the several different environments that can change the way you go about destroying your demonic enemies in the hell-scape in DOOM which doesn't fail to satisfy. Another great thing about this game is that I find very little situations where the game feels empty or under developed. The game has a history of popular and successful titles which has classic enemies, and combat that inspire the future generations of games to fit the rapidly exceeding expectations for newer games. A few negatives is a hard topic because like I said this game is very well developed: The combat progression system is quite gun focused even though the game has a great opportunities to create intuitive melee options for the traditional rip n tear type playthough. Also the suit has so many different upgrades but I feel as serious changes in the environment could result in a more unique interactive experience with your suit, and how you go about the game, like possibly a frozen level with different themed demons, and a little more exposure to different environmental conditions. Those two things are pretty damn nitpicky, but coming up with complaints for the game was quite difficult. Overall this hell of an experience is an 10/10 (I haven't finished it so this review is a little dry, but I hope to update this when I progress a little more).\t\t\t\t\t
