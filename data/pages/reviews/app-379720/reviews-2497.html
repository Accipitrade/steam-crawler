\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played Doom for about two hours after I bought it and thought it was the worst game ever and that the story was boring.

Over Spring Break 2018, I decided to give it another go.

Doom is the best game I have ever played in my entire life. The story and fast-pacedness of the game make it so much fun to play. Glory Kills are so damn cool! Keep in mind that you can aim at different parts of monsters' body's and perform different Glory Kills based on that. You'll need to know this late into the game, if you want to do the challenges. Best story and gameplay ever. Not having to reload also makes this game extremely fast paced too, nothing takes away from the action, ever.

Every achievement (for singleplayer), challenge, and weapon upgrade can be obtained without having to go out of your way and are extremely fun to get. I have never played a game where doing everything was not tedious or time consuming. I have 100% completion after only about 15 to 20 hours (I played the multiplayer for a bit after the 6.66 update), so it's really not that hard to do everything and, as I said, it's a ton of fun doing everything.

Please buy this game. Bethesda and ID need to know we want a Doom 2!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played Doom back when it was originally released. While I was in awe of the FPS experience it pioneered and the amazingly smooth graphics for the time I never really thought it was a very good *game* though, with levels that rarely made sense (Dark Forces had some great levels). I skipped Doom 2, being more of the same, and tried my best to like Doom 3 (with the duct-tape mod) but did not succeed.

This version, on the other hand, is a very good game, a very good FPS and also feeling very much like Doom. It has solid controls, a badass protagonist, nice visuals, sounds and level design and rock-solid framerates.

+ it's not pitch black
+ you can climb (no longer is the champion of humanity thwarted by waist-high ledges!)
+ weapon upgrades make for interesting options as you progress
+ glory kills actually offer a relevant choice: a chance at extra health while potentially leaving you open
+ colored keycards make a perfectly reasonable re-appearance

- the boss fights are boring and feel dated (and take place in the most boring areas of the game)
- the rune challenges are a bit do-it-THIS-way-or-else (but they are entirely optional)

Note: I have only played single-player.\t\t\t\t\t

Unlike the old DOOM, however, it gives you breaks between combat sequences for exploration and admiration of the beautiful world and stunning graphical programming and design. This is not a complaint. The game finds a good balance between trying to hack you to pieces and letting you breathe in the atmosphere of its world.

On top of being a good, wholesome game to its core, being programmed in the Vulkan API made gameplay so much more smooth. WIth the multi-threaded rendering system offered by Vulkan, the game runs much better on multi-core CPUs like my own. Thanks to Vulkan, in most of the areas, I was able to run this game at 1080p on Ultra at a smooth 60 FPS with my GTX 1060 6GB. Only the largest of the areas induced a slowdown - and by slowdown I mean I went to about 45 FPS. (That said, be sure your desktop recording software will capture Vulkan games - you may need to switch to OpenGL mode if you want to record)

Do you miss the good ol' days of raw chaos, thrilling combat, and wholesome fun? DOOM 4 is for you.\t\t\t\t\t
