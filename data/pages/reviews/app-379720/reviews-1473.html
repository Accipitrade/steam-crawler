\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016

DOOM got a reboot, and what do I think of it? Well, I think it's great! I never got to play the original DOOM, it was past my time. When I heard about a reboot, I got excited. I get a chance to play a series that is legendary for starting the PC Gaming Scene (well, it kickstarted it atleast). While id software didn't have John Carmack or Romero to assist in the reboot, I felt that maybe their visions would've made the game different that what we got. Not sure if it would've been in a good or bad way.

As for the game, it's a recreation of the original DOOM's story. You awake as Doomguy, naked in a laboratory on Mars. You kill a few demons then get your hands on your Praetor suit. The rest is history. Fighting your way through demons to try to fix a mistake made by power hungry (literally) scientists.

Gameplay it can be slow at times, but when the combat happens it is very intense. Whenever a demon inevitably runs your health, ammo or armor down, you gotta run around the arena area to find pickups. This actually incites panic a bit, and can get your heart rate going. This is what one of the core mechanics of DOOM is. It was in the older games from what i've seen, and i'm glad it stayed.

Story is good. Ends on a cliffhanger, but we all knew there wasn't just going to be one game. I felt the final boss was a little too easy, but I admit I died at least twice to it.

The art style of this game is fantastic. It sets a dark, gritty tone that almost makes you believe it left off on DOOM 3. Redesigns of demons and Doomguy are good. Level designs of Hell and Mars are great, and the added bonus of secret DOOM chambers are funny and add depth to level exploration.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t
