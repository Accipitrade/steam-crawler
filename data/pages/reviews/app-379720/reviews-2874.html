\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat makes Doom so fascinating in the modern gaming era is it's level design that wonderfully mixes current-day complexity with retro accessability. You aren't moving from cover to cover like other modern FPS games, you instead feel constantly moving in zig-zag lines bloodily finishing off each foe. And the gameplay? Solid. Fun guns and close-range melee "finisher" moves that are cool enough to make you go "whoa" and are quick enough to not ruin the action, combined with a very quick player character, makes for a fast and bloody FPS. Visually, story wise, and music wise the game offers little unlike any other AAA game. Standard, but the gamplay is great.

TLDR - Innovative level design and fast and gorey gameplay combined with a lackluster but non-intrusive story make for a great AAA shootfest.

7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"In the first age, in the first battle, when the shadows first lengthened, one stood. Burned by the embers of Armageddon, his soul blistered by the fires of Hell and tainted beyond ascension, he chose the path of perpetual torment..." -Doom Slayer's Testament

- Epic story and characters.
- Satisfyingly brutal yet crisp and smooth gameplay.
- Challenging fights and bosses.
- Each weapon feels very unique.

Something that really struck me is that every enemy attack can be avoided so every death (and you will die on the harder difficulties) feels completely fair. I could easily see 'Oh, I didn't sidestep that fireball' or 'I screwed up jumping over that attack'.

This game takes everything good about retro shooters (fast, action packed gameplay) and spiffs it up with some modern additions (glorious graphics and art style, suit and weapon upgrades, fun minigames, collectible items).

10/10 buy it. I love this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnlike its older counterpart, Doom 3, DOOM manages to stay true to its fast paced fps action roots while still innovating with some interesting ideas such as glory kills, power ups, and other features to keep the action fresh even towards the end of its around 8 hour campaign. The story won't be winning any awards for originality, but it isn't a necessity for this type of game to have an amazing plot.

Multiplayer was developed by a different studio and it really shows. The two gun limit is really out of place in this style of game and it just doesn't feel right for DOOM. Snapmap is really neat if you want to get creative and design your own maps or if you just want to have an endless stream of user-generated content to keep you busy after you complete the campaign.

While it is worth the $60 asking price, try to wait for a sale for $20-$30. Overall, it is a very good game worth the attention of any fps-junky. Just stay away from the multiplayer unless you want to play COD with a DOOM paint job.\t\t\t\t\t
