\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh . . . DOOM.

I haven't finished it yet, but I've gotten a pretty good taste so far.

A new entry in a franchise, in a genre I stopped enjoying about 10 years ago. But is it fun? Oh, god yes.

I was expecting Wolfenstein in hell, and basically got it, with the exception of a few basic differences between the franchises. The exposition is generally kept to a minimum - that does make it more intrusive when it actually stops the action for it, since your character is pretty much a voiceless incarnation of rage. I'm just imagining the 'duh' expression that must be on his face when someone starts monologuing and you can't do anything.

That's a minor ding, though, against what's otherwise a great game. One subtle example is that 'run' is always on. That might be able to be changed in settings, but it's on by default, which gives you the feel of the old school game right out of the gate. The weapons are plentiful and varied enough that running out of ammo is never really an issue - just running out of ammo in the gun you like the most.

With the sheer variety of games available, I'd wondered how they'd alter the fairly one-dimensional gameplay of the original in order to keep it fresh. Modding your weapons and armor fills that gap nicely. Aside from making your personal playstyle more viable (unless you like stealth, which ain't happening) it doesn't actually seem to alter the course of the game much. I haven't found a single area that could only be accessed with a certain build, or that was closed off if you didn't take particular mods.

The gameplay is fast-paced, linear, and the levels (once you know your way) are surprisingly brief. The battles are visceral and brutal, and the addition of Glory Kills (the ability to rip apart and/or beat an enemy to death once they're weakened) are a fantastic way to make you feel like more of a badass. Unfortunately, they do take place in real-time, as does most collecting of items, which can result in getting a Glory Kill, picking up a power-up, or getting a mod, or interacting with something and immediately ending up dead if done at the wrong time.

I also like that by the end of the level, if you've missed something, you can pretty much always backtrack the full length of the level. It's been that way in all of them so far that I've noticed, but again, haven't finished it yet.

Definitely recommend, WELL worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t
