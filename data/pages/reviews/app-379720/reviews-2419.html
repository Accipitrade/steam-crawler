\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you liked Ultimate Doom and Doom II, and If you like Brutal Doom, you will love the campaign of Doom (2016). This is a garuntee. This is an experience that doesnt care about lengthy cutscenes, shoving exposition down your throat, trying to be realistic, or trying to make you emotionally invested in the character's backstory.

This game is about one thing: Killing demons with a bunch of sick weapons on Mars and in Hell. If you require more of a plot, you can read data logs found in missions at your own pace. The game doesnt shove the story in your face, but it is there and pretty interesting.

The game is worth buying for the campaign alone. Seriously. Not many better ways to say it.

You may have heard concerning things about the multiplayer, and you may not have even heard of SnapMap, and it is true that these modes, while fun in thier own ways, leave much to be desired. However, with the price being what it is even while not on sale, it was an easy decision for me to buy this game for PC after already buying the game at launch and all of the DLC seperately for PS4.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM, a reboot of the franchise that aimed to be a modern shooter with the heart and soul of the previous games.
As someone who grew up playing the old Doom games along with awesome build engine shooters that came after them I was wondering what am I getting myself into with this game. Doom 3 was too different from the previous games and I didn’t like it nearly as much, I was worried that I’ll feel the same way about this game, and honestly in the end I kinda do.

It’s definitely different than the old Doom games which I enjoyed a lot more due to level designs which for me were more enjoyable and fun to play through. This game’s placement of secrets wasn’t as good either in my opinion and secrets didn’t really feel rewarding or fun to find most of the time. Given that good deal of them are missable in which case you have to replay the entire level, I ended up using guide for secrets to spare myself the pain.

Challenges system where you get simple tasks to perform in each level in order to get more upgrade points was interesting, but for some of them I had to reload the levels from the checkpoints up to several times in order to complete them, which wasn't fun and took away from the should-be quick pace of the game.

Combat was relatively decent, you have to move around a lot while shooting like in the old games, however some of the monsters felt a bit too tanky. Most of the fights were arena style with monster waves which I didn't like. The glory kills were fun at first, but once the game starts throwing lots of demons at you they get a bit risky to do. Sure you can’t be damaged while performing the glory kill, but it can happen that imps come right next to you and then attack you immediately after you finish, or monster shoots at you while you’re performing the glory kill and the projectile reaches and hits you after you finish.

Story was okay, I would even say I found it interesting and that I am looking forward to seeing what happens next in the DOOM Eternal which should be as far as I am aware a direct sequel to this game.

In the end while I didn’t enjoy it as much as the first two Doom games, it was pretty decent and definitely a lot better than Doom 3. I don’t regret buying it and hope Doom Eternal is going to be better.\t\t\t\t\t
