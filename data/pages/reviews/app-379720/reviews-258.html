\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't like Doom 2016, and I will probably not be purchasing Doom Eternal as a result, but this dislike is rooted in preference and my own physical limitations. I can still recognize that it's a great game.

Firstly, I'm old enough to have played the original (1990s) Doom games in their heyday. The modern incarnation is just too fast. I literally got a headache from the player character's floaty movement and overall speed, which made completing the campaign a challenge (but I did finish, to my surprise).

Secondly, this kind of shooter was never my thing anyway. I have fond memories of Unreal Tournament in my youth, but that's about it. I was wondering how that old formula would feel in a circa 2016 reboot. Turns out it feels about the same, with updated graphics, only I'm older and even less prone to enjoy shooting hyperactive bullet sponges while practicing WASD-fu.

Again, none of this is the game's fault. It is what it is, and it is executed very well. If you want to slaughter demons at a breakneck pace—in the stylish, blood-splattered architecture of both a Martian research base and the fetid depths of Hell, with sweet weapons and upgrades, hard-hitting combat, and obnoxious boss fights—then Doom has you covered.

If you're like me, and Arma and Ghost Recon are more your speed, then maybe think twice.

Addendum: Glory Kills and the Berserk pickup are awesome, though. Rip & Tear ... is not a metaphor.
