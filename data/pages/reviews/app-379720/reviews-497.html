\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 2016 is a surprisingly deep game. Yes, deep! On the surface it's all chainsaws and glory kills, and it's easy to assume it's just some silly ultra violence. But there's actually some serious plot and lore underneath that which adds enormously to the satisfaction and joy of the game. Without giving spoilers, let me just say if you played the earlier doom games, don't mistakenly think this is a reboot. It isn't. There's a great plot line which links together that character from the first games up to this point.

Plot aside, this is a beautiful game, visually, with solid mechanics and great gameplay. One small bonus that people might not know: Doom links to the Razor lighting system. So if you have a room full of Hue lights you can get even more immersed. It's fantastic.


I argue this is a better game than it's sequel, Doom Eternal. Doom 2016 lets you choose how to flip between weapons and approach encounters in a range of different ways. Doom Eternal is more prescriptive, pushing you to use specific weapons and tactics for certain opponents. Doom has a little bit of "platforming" and exploration which makes the levels more interesting. Eternal takes this too far, IMHO, with prolonged sections that feel more like Super Mario than Rambo.

I'm here to be Rambo in hell, and Doom 2016 let's me do that.

A really great game, and I'm amazed I initially ignored it.\t\t\t\t\t
