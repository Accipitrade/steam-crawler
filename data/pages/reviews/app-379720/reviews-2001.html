\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's Doom. If I need to explain more than that, you're too young to play it without your mommy.

*sigh* Okay, I'll say more words at you. Remember when Doom was first released back in nineteen-eighty-something? Remember how there were dozens of games really a lot like it? Wolfenstein, Catacomb Abyss, Aliens of Gold, Heretic, etc? Doom wasn't the "best" one but it was the one by which all others were measured.

Then came Doom 2 and it was essentially just more Doom. For real: I can't tell the difference. Decades later, when Doom 3 was released, we were all tentatively hopeful. When id Software finally dropped the playable demo, we knew we had been rewarded. I remember the first time I played D3, just standing in one spot for at least a full minute, saying to my friend "♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥! Look at my gun! You can see shadows moving across my gun!"

While Doom and Doom 2 had been a blow-em-up blast-fest, Doom 3 was the game that redefined the genre. Again. Personally, I never found the original games to be at all scary. Doom 3 was HORRIFYING! I'd never been actually scared by a video game before and it's a high I've chased ever since. I don't typically care for scary movies but horror video games (good ones) are most definitely "my jam."

This new Doom fails to deliver much in the way of horror. It has a dark, gritty feel to it but there are no jump-scares, no dimly-lit hallways that are probably hiding several monsters waiting to eat your brain, no visceral sense of impending... well: Doom. Personally, I blame Carmak. Now here me out. I blame him but I don't blame him completely. John Carmack literally wrote the book on video games. It's one sentence long. You've read it before but maybe you don't realize it. It goes like this:

"Story in a game is like story in a porn movie. It's expected to be there, but it's not that important."

Last year, he wrote a new introduction to The Book of id. Here it is:

"This old quote still pops up, but I caveat it today -- there are undeniably lots of games where the story is the entire point, and they can be done well. I do still hold that the most important games have been all about the play, not the story."

Doom 4 (this game right here) seems to care too much about its story and not enough about its play. Unfortunately, the story doesn't hook me early enough. don't get me wrong: I plan to have a few more hours fun with this game but it's nothing special. It's on the order of Doom 2 or Quake 4. Just one more in a sea of same. Just as Doom was only distinguishable from Wolfenstein because you were killing demons instead of Nazis, Doom 4 is only distinguishable from Wolfensein 3 because you're... uh... killing demons instead of Nazis. And nobody really cares why.

If this game is an intentional attempt to recapture the feel of Doom and Doom 2, where all you care about is finding the biggest gun and blasting as many demons with it as you can before you die, I'd have to say Bethesda has succeeded in publishing an entertaining shooter that borrows elements from every other shooter and has essentially no horror value.

As an action-shooter, Doom 4 is solid. The controls are good; the graphics are... not breathtaking but really very good. This isn't the industry cataclysm that Doom 3 was in terms of visuals.

In summation: I wholly agree with John that the most important games are about the GAME, not about the story. Through that lens, Doom 4 is an utterly unimportant game. I was looking forward to Doom: Eternal but it seems Bethesda has turned it into a DLC-fest on par with Borderlands.

If you liked Doom and Doom 2, you're sure to at least appreciate Doom 4 for a few hours. If you loved Doom 3 as much as I did, you'll probably be pretty disappointed. It's not "bad;" it's just not special. And it sure as hell isn't scary.

Doom 4 gets the 'ol "gentleman's C." It's just good enough that I don't feel cheated having paid six dollars for it. If I'd been able to pay full price for it at launch, I would have been pretty pissed off. For a game with such big shoes to fill, Doom 4 just... doesn't. I'll still play Doom Eternal. Eventually. When there's a GotYE with all the DLC included for twenty dollars or so. But if that's just more of this... that might be the end of the franchise for me.

I feel like I've written a pretty negative review here and that wasn't initially my intent; I kinda talked myself into it. I really DID have a lot of fun playing this game for 105 minutes just now and I really DO plan to play it some more tomorrow. I was just hoping to be terrified like I was with Doom 3.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is about demons on mars. You have a shotgun. Send them back to hell.

Fast. Brutal. Unyielding. Powerful.

DOOM 2016 is all of the above. A perfect mix of the old and the new. A worthy successor to the games that essentially created and popularized the first person shooter genre as we know it.

Fans of the seminal DOOM 1 and 2 will be happy with this game's mix of classic DOOM elements (weapon, ammo, health and armor pickups, fast base movement speed, etc.) along with new additons like the glory kills and weapon upgrade system.

In addition, the game supports the new Vulkan rendering API, allowing it to run better on low end machines. I have a fairly new powerful PC I just built in late 2019 (3.6 GHz cpu, 16GB RAM, M.2 SSD, and an RTX 2060 GPU) and it even helped me run the game better. With OpenGL, I was getting between 110-160 ish fps. But switching to Vulkan nets me a nearly unstoppable 200fps even on Ultra or Very High settings.) Regardless of your computer specs, you may be able to run this game with some visual sacrifices or tweaking of the options.

And the music...to put it bluntly is METAL AS IT GETS. If you're a fan of heavy metal music like I, you will probably love the crap out of this music. It is not for the faint of heart. Mick Gordon created some one of a kind dynamic music for this game that ramps up and down to match the flow of gameplay. It's a very original mix of modernish metal combined with crushing electronic elements and influences. Think super low, heavy guitars and the builds, drops, and drones of modern day dubstep.

I could say so, so much more, but really in short:

It's DOOM.

Rip and tear.\t\t\t\t\t
