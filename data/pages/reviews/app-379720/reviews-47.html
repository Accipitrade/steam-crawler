\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got it for cheap in a sale, I didn't expect too much, but it turned out to be really good. The gameplay is very arcade-like, even more so than it was in the original DOOM (1993). The campaign has a very good balance between calm exploration and frantic combat. The skill curve is mostly fair, but there is the occasional super-frustrating nearly-impossible-to-win battle, especially near the end of the campaign.

The plethora of upgrade options and bonus items can be overwhelming: Argent Cells, Praetor Tokens, Weapon Upgrades, Weapon Mods, Rune Trials, Secrets, Collectible Toys, per-level Challenges ... that needs quite a bit of unpacking, but in the end, it turns out to work just fine. The same goes for the controls, which take a bit of getting used to, but are quite OK.

Graphics are more than adequate, and the game does a good job at keeping the framerate steady most of the time. I like that you're not locked into the blurry TAA mush that some other games like to produce, but can opt into an actual sharp image.

There are, however, two things I absolutely dislike about this game. First, the lack of a quicksave/quickload option in favor of a checkpoint system. Second, the fact that to use the in-game console, "dev mode" has to be enabled (which is fine) but can't be left again (which isn't) and also disables achievements (which is outrageous).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed on Medium difficulty (Hurt me plenty). Took about 7 hours to finish the campaign.

The good:
- Have to mention the soundtrack. Even though i wouldn't listen to most of the songs outside of the game (because they are too repetitive for my liking), they fit perfectly in game to set the mood. The few times when BFG Division kicks in though, that's when you feel like you're able to take on the entirety of Hell all at once. Good stuff.
- No complaints about the rest of the sound department.
- Gameplay and controls are tight. It feels really good to mow down demons (while listening to the soundtrack)
- Weapon mods and armor upgrades are a good way to customize your playstyle. For example the Assault Rifle has a scope for those who want to snipe enemies from afar or micro missiles for those who want to take it up close.

The neutral:
- Story. I don't think it's good or bad. I didn't feel bored when i was watching the cutscenes but i didn't feel any excitement either.

The bad:
- Some levels are badly designed. Whether you have to search for keycards in maze-like areas or do platforming, it's quite painful. I don't feel like it belongs in a game like this where you're supposed to slaughter as many demons as you can. This is probably the reason why i've never finished any Doom games in the past.
- It's short. I mean how long can you drag on a game that's basically arenas with waves of enemies? I guess they could send us looking for keycards so- oh wait..

For what the game is, it's pretty good. Mindless demon killing. Though i got action overdose and could not finish the game in one sitting. 7/10 wait for sale.\t\t\t\t\t
