\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh boy, uhm... where do I even start. If you're looking for one of those "OMG IZ NEW DUUM GAEM 10/10" reviews, keep scrolling - this one isn't it. As a DOOM fan from the very first (two) game(s) into which I sunk countless hours as a kid, a teen and again as an adult, even a fan of DOOM 3 and what it set out to do, I felt it would be dishonest to my favourite franchise to not give it a proper review. So here we go: as much as I want to give it straight As, DOOM 4 (I refuse to call it DOOM "2016") is a mixed bag.

First off, as usual, presentation. Marvellous - I can't really stress how unbelievably well this game runs, how good the controls feel, how great the music is, how pretty the levels are, how punchy the guns are, everything just roars on like a well-oiled chainsaw. Some people don't particularly care for Mick Gordon's modern metal/electronic music mashup of a soundtrack, and I'm sort of one of them in the sense that it's not really good music on it's own, but it's AMAZING video game music. I'm an 80s metal fan and 9 string guitars and djenty one-string-only riffs don't do it for me either, but I'd be lying if I didn't say I felt a ferocious tinge of adrenaline when the music kicked in every now and again during intense firefights. Besides, the original DOOM had electronic-ish tracks as well as faux-orchestral pieces, so it's true to the formula if nothing else. Shows I'm a muso for devoting almost the entire paragraph to the sound, heh - just to be super-clear again, DOOM is technically almost flawless (for more on quite a big "almost", see below).

Now the gameplay, the core, the most important bit. Here's where DOOM both shines bright and simultaneously starts to lose me a bit. One of my favourite things about the old DOOM was the fact that every level felt like its own unique situation. Some levels were more populated, some less, some more arena-like, others claustrophobic corridor mazes... but the important part was that these two factors - the way the level design and enemy placement interacted and the levels themselves - dictated, for the most part, how a level would pan out. Here... the formula is very repetitive. Explore a little first, flip a switch or two, find a gore nest, get forced into a fight by "Demonic presence at unsafe levels" lockdowns, rinse, repeat, over and over again. Here's the catch though - the shooting itself is AWESOME. Weapons are powerful and effective, enemies have attack patterns you need to learn, health is not regenerating, no hit-scan enemies, all things that make a good shooting experience, at least in my book. What I'm getting at is - I never caught myself thinking "ugh, not another closed shooting arena", but... why not just make levels and put enemies in them instead of ripping off Serious Sam/Painkiller? It seemed unnecessary and to me it kind of ruined the exploration aspect because I knew I would inevitably get locked into a demon-slaughterhouse until I can run around the awesome maps again. The danger and rush of going into a dark server-maze (a la E1M2) and getting swarmed by imps and soldiers disappears because you're forced into fights. In short - shooting good, the way it's delivered... not bad, but to me, it could have been done differently and still retain the high-octane fast-paced feel.

The upgrade system I really don't have much of an opinion on. It does admittedly add some depth and it keeps the gameplay fresh and it's nice to experiment with the rune "builds", but ultimately I think they could've just given you some of these trinkets from the get-go (like scope for the HAR or static siege mode for the Gauss Cannon).

Just to get this out of the way too - the story is just a means to an end. It's leaps and bounds better than DOOM 3 which took itself way too seriously. It's campy and tongue-in-cheek without being too dumb or pretentious; in fact, I think they NAILED the tone they were going for perfectly. However, Olivia is a very weak villain, if you can call it that, and she annoyed me to no end. I know the demons are the bad guys, but just seeing her on screen acting like she's this badass antagonist made me cringe. At least she could've literally become the Spider Mastermind a la Betruger in DOOM 3: Ressurection of Evil. And to everyone saying "DOOM HAS LOAR NOW", I actually loved the overly-nerdy faux-sci-fi/fantasy writeups for every character. It gave the world a lot more depth.

Now the slightly bad to merely annoying. The checkpoint system. It sucks. The end. Especially if you're like me and love to run around and look for secrets and stuff, and then one "hey, not my fault" ledge-grab fail sets you back a solid 30 minutes. Yeah, the secrets save regardless of checkpoints, but it's still annoying. Speaking of secrets... the fact that some of the levels are designed in a way that you can't backtrack after a certain point is atrocious. Replaying a level from Mission select is an option, sure, but it's a cheap way to waste your time and suckers like me will fall for it. One more thing, which is admittedly a minor nitpick or maybe an over-attachment to the days of yore - the movement speed. Yes, it's faster than most FPS games today. But when you come from strafe-pwning everything in the old DOOMs it still feels kind of slow. IMO the Haste powerup could've been removed and that movement could have been the "hold shift" run speed (maybe with a generous "stamina" bar for a little balance).

Lastly, the downright ugly. Bugged achievements. Most notably "The Circle is Complete" and a few others. Bethesda's response is "start over". Then, there's a weird thing that happens if you press "Restart mission" it just resets your progress to a previous point in time. Also no fix. Unacceptable. These issues have been present since launch, no fix, nothing. Not to mention folks with corrupted saves and so on. This stuff isn't prominent in any way, but it left a slightly bad taste in my mouth, especially since it's clearly apparent from the lore, level design, weapon design, gameplay and general vibe that a lot of love went into the game.

Ultimately, DOOM is a must-play title with some minor annoyances and some completionist-infuriating bugs. It's not the be-all-end-all of FPS games or a second coming of old-school shooters, but it's a very good game nonetheless and I will be coming back to it without a doubt.

EDIT: I have spent some time in SnapMaps and I kind of love it, to be honest. As someone who played quite a few DOOM mods and TCs, I can appreciate the idea and I love the way they're organized by category, votes etc., it just makes so much sense and I would've killed for a system like this back when we were loading WADs manually. If you want to squeeze out some extra hours, look no further.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a perfectly blended mixture of insane action, brutal visuals, and hardcore music. The combat rejects all modern fps tropes of ducking behind cover, ADSing for every shot, and weak enemies with no variation. In Doom you are asked to run around the arena taking down different enemy types with different attacks and weaknesses while also managing a variety of weapons which are all at your finger tips and seconds from containing no ammo if you aren't careful. The music and visuals enhance this, blending perfectly with gameplay to make you want to rip apart every demon in hell with your bare hands. Anyone who enjoys a good fps campaign (opposed to modern COD and BF) owes it to themselves to play Doom. The last fps campaign released to rival its quality was that of Halo 3.\t\t\t\t\t
