\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFast paced shooter game with extreme violence to the point of silliness and a focus towards extended battles in designated battle zones. Some character and weapon customization (new for Doom) and exploration (a major feature from the first couple of games was discovering secrets)

Presentation (5/5) for 2016 - Due to unbelievable graphics optimization, the game's visuals do not demand the best gaming computers. You can play with a mediocre or very old piece of equipment, lower graphical settings, and have a perfectly playable FPS averages. The ultra-violence of 'glory kills', the distinct prog metal soundtrack that shifts around what you are currently doing, the powerful feel and sound of the weapons, the outstanding redesign of the old demons, the useful user interface, they really nailed it.

Story/Dialogue (3/5 but not important) - Story is minimal, some of which you learn via podcasts and reading notes. There's a cheeky tone to it that reminds me of campy horror movies like Evil Dead or Army of Darkness. The narrative is sufficient to give you a slight purpose and explain away why you are such an efficient doom-slayer but that's pretty much it.

Gameplay (5/5) - The gameplay is extremely good. Most of the combat revolves around staged battle zones that feel like multiplayer deathmatch arenas, but instead against AI demons. The demons are highly varied and it is up to you to determine how it is best to handle them. A few of the demons have pretty good AI, such as the bosses and surprisingly enough, the imp. The key of the battles is to keep moving - this is not your typical hide and shoot tactical game. This is a 'learn the battle zone', find what pick ups it offers, and move move move while shooting. The game rewards mobility and heavily punishes players staying in one spot and struggling to dodge.

There are some exploration (finding secrets), which I think hurts the momentum a bit and character development that is not exactly necessary other than for balancing the increasingly difficult battles and giving you the feeling that you are becoming closer and closer to a god.\t\t\t\t\t
