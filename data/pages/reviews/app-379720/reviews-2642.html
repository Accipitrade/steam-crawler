\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely overrated, but still not bad. It's enjoyable, strengths would be its simplicity is easy to pick up and play, weaknesses would be the lack of real depth to the gameplay makes it feel repetitive over time. You might love the gameplay enough to warrant a purchase but I would wait for a sale.

The strengths of the game is the graphics, the violence, and the music. The game's soundtrack is really unique, not many matches its style. The graphics is really well done as well, very easy on the eyes and even the up close and personal attacks feel rewarding. The violence the game has makes you feel like you're in a metal music montage, some melee attacks consisting of you just smashing the skull of a demon with a stomp. It's satisfying and the learning curve is very accessible.

The weaknesses is replayability and content. The replay value, as you will see from about two thirds of the reviews here, is you will get about twenty to thirty hours out of this game. This is good amount of hours, but I feel simple fixes could make it even longer. It is a matter of preference, but I don't like personally mechanics like the chainsaw giving you ammo and melee attacks giving you health, it makes it feel like the game has one correct playstyle and that's just rushing through enemies. It's fun for the first while, but then evetually you just want to kill enemies off with your arsenal, being forced to melee attack ALL enemies just gets stale for me.

The multiplayer is disappointing as well. It could have stolen a mode like Call of Duty Zombies or Killing Floor, but it did something else that more than half of the players just don't feel is special because it isn't; it's generic in nature. Skulltag games back in the day, for example, had invasion modes, where are they here?

This game overall is a positive experience, just don't get hyped up for it and you'll be pleased.\t\t\t\t\t
