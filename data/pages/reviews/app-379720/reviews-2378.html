\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a truly refreshing shooter, meticulously crafted to remind you of an old game, but "feel" a lot more modern and updated. Much like Shovel Knight did for platformers, DOOM brings back all the things you loved about fast 90s shooters, without the frustration of actually playing one.

The visuals are amazing. The amazing Mars and Hell landscapes will terrify and amaze you. Blood pours from ceilings as you come across disgusting piles of what used to be bodies. The horrible experiments that were done will make your skin crawl. The terrifying symbols from the rituals, along with the altars used and sacrifices made are there for you to wonder at.

The monsters are also horrible and grotesque. Each of the updated monsters from the old game bring the same dread when you see them as they did back in the early 90s, but now they are meticulously rendered in 3D. When killed, they will explode into bits of blood and gore, which looks amazing. Or, they might spew a fountain of blood. Each visceral, disgusting death is gloriously satisfying.

This brings me to the gameplay. Gloriously satisfying is an excellent way to describe it. The first DOOM game had tough enemies, but sometimes the real challenge was finding your way to the exit. You won't find much of that here. Sometimes there's a key for you to locate, but for the most part that part of the gameplay is kept to a minimum. This game keeps you busy with fighting. You will fight some guys.

Most of the game, you will walk around some corridors, arrive in a big room and either find a "switch" of sorts, or just be ambushed by bad guys. Unlike the older games, here you will see an initial swarm of guys, who will be gradually replaced as you kill them by other, usually stronger guys. This kind of "paces" each fight, where you can't just blow through all your ammo quickly, because there might be a super-strong guy at the end of the fight that you might need it for. Careful planning is always necessary. It never gets old, though, because of the constantly varied enemies and the great variety of weapons you have. As the game goes, you gain more and more weapons to use, and you will use them all. Each one is best for a certain situation. It's really well done.

One reason you can rely on planning is because of the checkpoint system. When you die, you go back to the last checkpoint. Usually these are placed after each big fight. This makes the game feel like you learn something after each fight you get through, without feeling punishing because you grabbed the quad damage too early and then all of the Cyberdemons spawned and you got killed. Luckily for you, you can try it again and use your time more wisely, without having to replay the entire level or something.

There are a ton of secrets to find. I'm not sure if I'll go find them all; I'm not much of a "100%" type of guy, except with Mario games. However, I can say I won't be playing it on a higher difficulty. I played on Hurt Me Plenty and died plenty enough times, and the bosses seemed incredibly difficult. I can't even imagine fighting them on Hyperviolence.

Please do yourself a favor and play this wonderful game. Let the loud heavy metal fill your room as you settle into the zen of fighting a huge wave of demons, and as the fight ends, and the music fades out, I hope you will lean back in your chair, as I did each time for the whole 13 hour campaign, and sigh, "That was AWESOME".\t\t\t\t\t
