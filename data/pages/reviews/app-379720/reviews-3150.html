\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to be bringing up the original Doom a lot in this review, so I'll refer to this game as Doom 4 for the sake of clarity. If you want a fast paced, violent shooter with music that gets your blood pumping, Doom 4 won't disappoint you. Even if you're one of those poor souls that hasn't played Doom, you'll still enjoy Doom 4. For the rest of us normal people that have played Doom, this game is even more of a treat. The BFG 9000 works just like it did in 1993. The Super Shotgun makes a return, and is the only weapon that needs to be reloaded, unless you count the need to operate the pump on the combat shotgun. There are secret areas on every mission, and all of the demons are recognizeable as 3D versions of their classic counterparts. The graphics are impressive, despite the fact that my computer can't handle much higher than medium settings. The sound design is perfect, and feels right at home in a Doom game. If you enjoyed Doom 1 and/or Doom 2, you owe it to yourself to buy Doom 4. ID returned to their roots with this game, and it shows.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first played Dark Souls a few years ago, the first thing I noticed, and the thing that kept me coming back to the game, was simply just how smooth and natural the controls felt. Even if there were pre-set animations and limitations based on equipment, it felt fluid and natural. DOOM is just like that -- I've never played an FPS that just felt so ... good. When you're playing DOOM, you can get into the zone so easily -- run, jump, shoot, glory kill, rinse, and repeat. This isn't even to mention the fact that the game is just really, really fun -- it's super cathartic to be able to literally kill a hundred demons and get a little "Checkpoint Reached" pop up in the corner. You can kill a hundred demons in a single fight and it won't even be the end of the level. It's a fantastic game. It's gorgeous, the demons are hideous, the music is great, but most importantly, it just feels so, so good to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSingle player alone is worth it. I have not played / expected much from other modes.

You are the Doom guy. You shoot everything non-stop.
Gunplay is the point here and is really well-done that I have never played a shooter this intense.
No reload means no interruption. Tons of enemy firing at you means you have to move around to avoid getting hit.
The combat, especially in the later part, always put you at the edge between life and death.
You kill something and you get health, then you lose them from getting bitten by another demon.
This goes on and on but (at least me on normal difficulty) mostly ends in victory, and that leave you the awesome feeling of being super bad ass for surviving the wave.
Even in down time between waves, you still want to look around to find secret / health / ammo for next wave. Very little filler in the ~12 hour campaign.

How fast is 'fast paced'? Let's just say that Titanfall feels slow for me now.

+
Awesome graphic.
Not many cutscene. Story don't get in the way of shooting stuff.
Crazy option menu to suit your PC spec with build-in performance-analysis info.

-
Multiplayer / Snap map fun-factor is questionable.\t\t\t\t\t

The ambiance is very immersive although the story line is pretty basic, but who cares? Mars outdoor is prettty impressible, with weather evolution, winds and stupefying skies, I stopped many times just to admire the designs. Indoor bases are also very impressive, doors motions, computers, lightning and sounds are very realistic. But HELL is definitely the most impressive world, with pentagrams, candles, and various evil signs everywhere, the gigantic huge skulls are pushing the nightmare one step ahead. And the sound line! Continuous growling and screams add up to the oppression, creatures have very peculiar screams, you can tell before seing them who is going to be crushed by your massive firing power! DOOM 2016 must be experienced. You will enjoy slicing monsters with your chainsaw again... A big UP to ID developers, many thanks for this ultra violent and perfect game play, it was worth the wait. I can now tell that I have visited HELL and survived! My favorite DOOM of all times, raw, rough, pure.\t\t\t\t\t

DISCLAIMER: This is a full review, and NOT a first impressions review like all my other reviews, if you want a better full review, then please read better reviews then mine

Doom guy is back, and he's ready to kick some demon s*it!

DOOM (2016) is a reboot to the classic first-preson shooter Doom developed by id Software and published by Bethesda Softworks and Zenimax Media

When DOOM was announced in 2015's E3 Bethesda press conference, I had mixed feelings. I was both happy and excited that a new Doom game would be coming with much more gore, blood and violence but also worried that they would mess everything up and I was extremely worried when the Multiplayer Beta rolled out and I got to try it, I was not impressed and the multiplayer portion of the game will be mentioned later on in this review

Thankfully, I was not dissapointed

The first Doom game I remember playing wasn't the original Doom, or Doom II nor Doom 3 but a mod known as Brutal Doom which took the level of violence from the original game and increased it by 100% and I have got to say, the new DOOM is like Brutal Doom only much more gorier, bloodier and more violent... and prettier graphics

The game drops you immediately into the action, no backstory, no cutscenes explaining how you got there. Nothing! Just dropped straight into the action of busting demon's brains and that's what I loved about DOOM. It got straight to the point with no long cutscenes and no backstory, although the game does explain how you got to Mars in the first place

They also made Doom guy really not give a flying f*ck about anything. He bashes things around, he throws monitors that are in his way and shoots demon's guts out. Again, this is what I like. The story as a whole isn't the best but I didn't really come for the story, I just came to shooting demons and running all over the place

The story starts with Doom guy on a slab in a testing facility on the planet Mars. The testing facility has just been overun by demons from Hell thanks to a portal the testing facility was using for energy. You then get to meet Samuel Hayden and Olivia Pearce, as well as the computer of the facility known as VEGA, throughout the game you travel to and fro the testing facility and Hell via portals

The graphics are amazing and very vibrant and the attention to detail is stunning. I was very surprised that the game ran at a very solid 60FPS with hardly no dips in frame rate and all graphical settings were set on Ultra (GeForce GTX 980 Ti) and it comes to show you, a game like DOOM which came out in 2016 ran far better than a game like Batman Arkham Knight which was released a year before DOOM

The music is brutal. I normally don't like heavy metal music, but done right, I will surely enjoy it. And although the music is mostly heard in the background and not straight in your face, I really did enjoy listening to the music whilst shooting around. The gameplay is quick and easy to master especially the glory kills. They are the most magnificent kills I've seen in all my life, they are brutal and ever so glorious

I encountered no bugs or game breaking glitches. The only problem I had was that sometimes the demons would get stuck on the environment and just stay there or enemies don't show up at all and the heavy metal music (which starts when there are enemies in a level) just kept on going, so much so, I had to restart from the last checkpoint

The boss battles are really good and unique for a modern day game. There are no quick time events, nothing. All you do is shoot and find the weakness of the boss. This happens to all the enemies, some demons have certain weaknesses and some demons die quicker with a certain weapon. The guns all have different attack styles and health and shields do not regenerate, if you're losing health, you better find a health pack, quick

The game never got boring as it always introduced new enemy types and although nearing to the ending the enemies did get a bit repetitive, the game offers upgrades and power ups to make the game not boring. And for some reason just ripping off some demon's head or ripping their guts out was so satisfying

DOOM's singleplayer was the most satisfying game I've played in quite a while and I have absolutely no complaints to the game at all, the only thing I would comment about is the ending, although I am not going to spoil anything, the ending is heavily sequel bating and although I'm happy that a new Doom game might come in a couple of years, I really wanted a satisfying ending to the game, but overall the game satisfied my need

Now onto the multiplayer. This by far was the weakest portion of the game

The multiplayer portion was not developed by id Software but by Certain Affinity and for me the multiplayer was exactly the same as every other multiplayer shooting game I've played. It's all the same thing. Shoot, level up. Shoot, level up. Shoot, level up... only this time with demons and Doom guys instead

When I played the multiplayer beta of DOOM's multiplayer, I was very dissapointed and I am surprised they didn't change anything between the beta and the release of the game

DOOM also offers a level editor known as snapmap. With snapmap you can create mutliplayer levels that you or your friends and others can play in. I personally tried the snapmap level editor thingy and found it too confusing to even make a simple level

For me personally, I do not know why DOOM needed a multiplayer and level editor included into the game, the singleplayer portion would have been just fine

Overall, DOOM is a gritty, bloody, violent and satisfying game and anyone who likes shooting should try this. Hell, I would even recommend it at it's full asking price. DOOM was a successful reboot to such a beloved franchise and I cannot wait for the next Doom game

Now, let's kick some Hell!
