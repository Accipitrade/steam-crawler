The multiplayer is a fun expierance with lots of customization to armor and weapons. everymode except TDM(team deathmatch) feels like a new way to play with the doom mechanics. The game has great graphics, a detailed and awesome enviroment, but a slightly mediocre story with a little repetive stuff going on, The multiplayer is entertaining but I would suggest playing something else other than TDM.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing Id software games since I was a child, started with the Keen games, got Wolf3d and Doom when I was old enough in my parent's eyes. Got Quake on release day, one of the few games I set the alarm for and was waiting for the store to open in my life. To this point every Id game had wowed me, I could not believe the graphics and the purity of gameplay made the games so easy to pick up but hard to master.

I played thousands of hours of Quake 3 multiplayer on my dialup, mostly instagib modes.

When I heard there was to be a new Doom game I dismissed it as FPS in general has become a stale cover shooter by the numbers genre in recent times. I didn't preorder the game specifically because I feared the single player would be a cookie cutter cover shooter in the modern style.

I eventually caved in and bought the game at full price on release day because I was curious more than anything. Good grief, shooters have been reborn with Doom. You run around at what feels like a million miles an hour compared to a modern FPS, you can carry a small army's worth of weapons all of which are amazing in their own right(except the pistol).

You start the game with a small scene with the Doomguy smashing a zombie's head in to his stone bed, getting up and proceeding to murder anything that has the temerity to be standing in his presence. No long intro cinematic, no reasoning for why you're here. You just start killing.

Everything about this game is designed to make you feel like a killing machine, you are a scythe against a field of demonic wheat. From the shotgun to the BFG9000 every weapon is capable of turning the demonic horde into a fine red mist. The glory kill system is both a valuable way to regain health and really sets the tone for the mute protagonist, with each glory kill you enhance the feeling that the protagonist really hates the demons, and goes out of his way to give them gory deaths.

The silent protagonist is generally a cheap way to encourage self-insertion into a character and enhance immersion, but here Doomguy voices his disdain for the story characters attitudes very clearly by throwing and smashing anything of value to them. Doomguy has his own motivation, and that is to destroy anything demonic and anything that stands in his way of that goal.

The single player is an amazing run with plenty of old school hidden secrets, boss battles, platforming sections and a slowly descending into hell asthetic that sets the mood brilliantly.

Multiplayer is subpar, the loadout system and lack of weapon pickups really bring it too close to being a modern shooter from an arena shooter.

Snapmap is fun, I'm not much of a creator so I don't see myself getting great mileage from it.

Overall this new Doom game scratches an itch I've had for a long time, a high mobility, fast paced shooter that makes you feel like you're the most powerful thing in the game. This is a game which bridges the gap between the modern shooter with it's upgrade systems and power creep and the old school which just dumps you in to a level and says have fun. I'd recommend this simply for the single player mode. Hopefully we get an old school expansion pack. A special mention for the music which consists of several remixes of classic doom music and new compositions.

PS: It's nice to play a game with a SUPER shotgun again.
