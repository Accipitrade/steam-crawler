\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor context, I played Doom and finished the campaign within ten hours on Hurt Me Plenty.

The gameplay loop is delightfully simple. It's a classic first-person shooter with minimal platforming and a solid assortment of weapons to get your hands on. Adding to this classic appeal, the game provides opportunities and incentives for learning combat combinations, upgrading combat options, and generally improving through player skill (examples include rune trials, tangible rewards for exploration, sick glory kills, arcade mode, intense difficulty options). As it relates to the campaign's narrative, it too is delightfully simple, with less talking and more shooting, which is exactly what folks are expecting for a Doom game. Levels are wonderfully designed, expressed through gore-ridden floors and hellish arenas built for massacring demons, but I would have loved to have seen the game explore different environments. Alas, given the narrative, it is consistent, so I cannot fault them for that decision.

Ultimately, I recommend Doom (2016). If you wait for a sale, you'll get a massive discount, but it's worth it for the full price as well.\t\t\t\t\t
