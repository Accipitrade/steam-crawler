\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt’s Doom, except it’s 2016 instead of 1993. We’re talking video cards with Gigabytes of RAM, actual 3D environments instead of 2 and a half dimensional levels, character models with fifty times as many polygons as pixels two decades ago.
And none of the wimpy survival-horror ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ from a decade ago; this time the demons have to survive you.


The Good:
  • The best simple-straight-forward run around and kill ♥♥♥♥ game ever.
  • Not only do you get to run around and kill ♥♥♥♥ but you get to look for secrets also!
  • The game looks amazing, the visual graphics are sharp, the creative aesthetics are awesome, and the best part is you don’t need a Los Alamos supercomputer to run it.
  • The main campaign is by far one of the greatest single-player gaming experiences ever, and after you have finished it there are community-made single-player and multi-player coop missions.

The Bad:
  • Not everyone is a fan of simple-straight-forward run around and kill ♥♥♥♥ games. Other than some cool secrets in each level there isn’t much depth here; even the best thing about this game gets a little tiresome as the enemy encounters get a little repetitive (run into a room, enemies spawn all around you, rinse, repeat).
  • The main campaign is less than 25 hours long, and the community-made maps? Some are pretty good, some not so much, but even the best aren’t nearly as good as the main campaign.
  • The studio that made the single-player campaign? Not the same studio that made the multiplayer game… it shows.
  • It probably won’t run on an old Gateway 486.

The Ugly:
The main villain gave me flashbacks of my High School Chemistry teacher.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only have a few hours in, but I can tell I'm hooked already.

I haven't done much with FPS games since I played Wolfenstein 3D all the way through back in 1995 or thereabouts. That was a fun challenge of a game. This game, Doom (2016), is like that... only WAY better. I love that the basic gameplay is straight-forward and familiar to me, but the graphics, sound, and all the rest of the window dressing is just out of this world better. I will say that after spending some time with Resident Evil on my Wii that I miss the ability to do a shooter with something more like a gun than a joystick. I'd actually be surprised there isn't some great controller that would give that same "gun in hand" feeling... that would be the only thing that would improve this game for me. Perhaps the keyboard + mouse approach would be an improvement? I don't know.

But I can't imagine how this game fails to entertain. I mean, you're just constantly blasting monsters/demons from Hell? It's wall-to-wall carnage. There is obviously strategy to surviving each level... same as it was in the old days with Wolf 3D: don't waste your ammo and don't get killed. Easier said than done!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game. I played the original Doom as a kid and it was my first horror shooter game. This version is a good game overall with lots of action and satisfying execution. And it looks great graphically.

However, that only lasts for about 5 hours for me. After that you start to see how clunky the controls can be and how annoying the platforming can get after the 6th hour even with the double jump. I'm the doom guy in a heavy exosuit and yet a slight breeze pushes me off a ledge for some reason. I've died more from clunky platforming than from the enemies it's so bad.

While the action feels fairly fast paced the Doom guy feels slow except when you execute a scripted melee kill animation which can be entertaining for awhile. There's no sprint unless you use a power-up which can be frustrating in some situations with some enemy types especially while playing higher difficulties. A lot of the enemy variation is comparable to the Halo series with the similar tiers of enemies, aka not a fan of the shield guys. Really? Demons become tactical to use high tech shields? Where's the horror survival aspect? Ammo and health drops a plenty which removes the survival tension completely.

Overall, while it's great action, the sense of tension from the horror/survival aspect that the original Doom had is not there. Most of the world is well lit, the enemies are loud and announce their presence, and the enemy spawns are usually the same scripted ecounters everytime you have to restart at a checkpoint. Which makes dying more frustrating and boring than it should be.

The loading times are still horrible on high-end PC's unless you have a top of the line SSD. Which can be expected from Bethesda.

If you like a good action shooter that makes you feel like a badass for about 5 hours. Then you might like this game. However, don't expect to get more depth than that. You wont find the horror survival side in this version. Frankly, it's my opinion that this game is a bit overrated.

The snap-map level creator is fun and has more depth than the actual game....sad. Once again Bethesda has to rely on fan/modder made content to make the game more interesting.\t\t\t\t\t
