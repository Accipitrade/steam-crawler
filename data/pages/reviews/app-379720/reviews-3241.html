\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"New school" id software unexpectedly delivers on nearly all fronts with their Doom reboot. Hectic, relatively traditional shooter gameplay paired with double jumps, mantling and "glory kills" (melee finishers) as novel additions to the old formula. You can expect a decently sized (and very expertly paced) campaign which will, in all likelihood, take you at least 10 hours to complete. Searching for secrets and the completion of challenges can add quite a bit of playtime on top of that. I was not expecting large sprawling levels, but nonetheless more than a couple of them ended up being just that - And the presence of (nowadays rare) features like a 3D automap was almost refreshing. Despite having to rely on successive spawns (you will not be seeing much more than 10 active enemies on any given encounter) to recreate the numbers usually associated (often wrongly) with the classic Doom games, the game never feels downscaled, gimped or cramped - You maneuver about at a brisk pace, seeking new hotspots of combat and taking the occasional detour for health and ammo pickups. This game's multiplayer mode has garnered a fair share of criticism, and while it does come across as very intentionally "casualized" (in many respects it feels like the FPS equivalent of a party game), it's nonetheless quite fun and even unique compared to the current crop of more modernly styled shooters - Think "Quake Lite" instead of "Quake Live". Doom's third leg is SnapMap, a module oriented level editor with some surprising depth and equally surprising limitations. I appreciate it existing at all, and it's certainly a lot more than the majority of other shooters even bother offering, but in the long run it's still too restrictive and all but guaranteed to constitute a creative dead end for user made content. It would be great if Bethesda was willing to allow more advanced modding (like they do for TES and Fallout) on the PC side of things, but I'm not holding my breath. Nonetheless, get Doom - A masterfully constructed shooter. I think id software is back to stay.\t\t\t\t\t
