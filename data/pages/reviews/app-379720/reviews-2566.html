\r\n\t\t\t\t\t8/10: It's really good. Not perfect, but really good.

Pros
+ GORE
+ FPS Done right, fun combat, feels nice
+ None of this stupid "armor ability" style BS that makes every game feel like CoD but worse
+ My only regret is I haven't played it more
+ Collectables and secrets

Cons
- Story/plot is a bit mehh but it's DOOM GUY BEING DOOM GUY, not a telltale game series.
- Kinda takes a while to load (I am using a mechanical HDD though, I'm sure with an SSD it would be much faster)
- Snapmap has some limits on it, most likely brought on by console porting. (Snapmap is still pretty decent though)
- Glory kills can get repetitive as you will kill a LOT of the same enemy in similar positions.
- If you're bad at finding collectables and secrets you're going to get frustrated trying to get to that ONE thing you see but can't get to.

Overall it's a good bit of fun, with nice graphics that doesn't try and "make more classes with more abilities" because that sucks. It's a straightforward kill them all FPS, and it's really good at being that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThree old-school shooter franchises have recently been revisited. Shadow Warrior, Wolfenstein and Doom.
Doom is the one I'd say most sticks to its roots. The objective of the game is simple. Go from point A to point B, and kill every demon bastard that gets in your way. The secrets and potential for trying to beat your own time in generally fast-paced levels offers a ton of replayability to this game. It's a simple formula, it doesn't offer much in terms of story, and it's not super hard, it's just pure murderous mayhem, and if that's what you're looking for, this is the game for you.
