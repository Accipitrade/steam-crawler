\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot imagine anyone still reading reviews for this game. You either already have it, or you decided not to buy it for whatever strange reason.

Nonetheless, this game is great. Not to overuse the popular metaphors, but it is a great contrast to current conventional "grounded" shooters.
You are literally a murderous monster with one desire - to kill demons.

You are so successful at fulfilling that desire that legions of demons have legends of you and run scared when they see you.

Your name is carved into ancient stone and industrial rock music emanates from the air as you walk the earth.

And boy is it a design marvel... the music responds to gameplay just fluidly enough so that you get into a rhythm that clicks well with shotgun reloads and bunnyhop timings.

There are a handful of videos online discussing the AI in the game - it is ingenuis. Long story short - the AI in the game is designed to literally be the opposite of cover based shooting. Every cover is a corner to die in, every moment of hesitation is punished by increased enemy accuracy.

Yes, the game is a string of repetitive formulaic arenas.
Yes, the narrative is a little forced over gameplay.
Yes, exploration is advantageous at the expense of gameplay pacing.

But holy-freaking-heck is it an enjoyable experience... Completed it three times by now. Will come back again to rip terrified imps in half with bare hands.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR -
• Story: 7/10
• Characters: 6/10
• Gameplay: 9/10
• Quality (glitches and bugs): 10/10
• Graphics: 9/10
• Sound: 9/10
• Music: 11/10
• Un-repetitiveness: 5/10
• Replay Value: 5/10
• Personal Enjoyment 9/10

Its the perfect example of how to reboot a very old franchise, especially one which shaped PC Gaming in it's start.
It's a high adrenaline, high speed and non forgivable game on the higher difficulties (which you should play it on in my opinion). The music is on the top of the gaming food chain of soundtracks, and you can even safely say that it actually is the main factor of making this game as badass and exciting to play as it is.

The game doesn't have to offer a lot of story and lore mateial, but it's there and it's really good, and there's a lot of hype added to it as now Doom Eternal is about to come in the near future.
And my take on the combat is like this, its repetitive aside from the vast variety of level design, but it manages to make repetitiveness actually good as weird as it sounds.
The music shaped this game to being one of the best and most fun offline shooters, the athmosphere it gives is uncomparable to other games, they try to make the music suit the boss or how powerful a certain being is or how sad something is and etc, in doom the music makes you feel like you're the boss battle and the demons are the ones who are in danger, like the very overused joke in the Doom community - Doom is the equivalent of "I'm not trapped in here with you, you're trapped in here with me".
The graphics and the effects are gory and... red. Everything is red and brown, obviously you're on mars and hell, so its understandable. But aside from that graphics are actually really good even though they use kind of the same theme for almost the entirety of the game (I'm not blaming them, you can't really make these places with different colors).
The gameplay is overall the embodiment of badassery, to heal and to get ammo you have to kill demons, making the best and only good tactic to be agressive all of the time and always be on the move, making the game really dynamic and fast paced. Although I'm tuning the score of the gameplay by 1 because of the reason I'll list below.

The combat as good as it is, has a few flaws.
Some of the guns are unstatisfying to use, while some are fun as hell (hehe), other ones you use only when you're out of ammo.
The crosshairs I feel like could've been made better, they're certainly not bad but not to my type if I were to make them. Playing without hud and crosshair is really immersive though, so I recommend you give it a try after you get a bit good with the mechanics and the idea of the gameplay.
Also the default FOV of the game is really low, so watch out for that and make sure to change it in the settings if you're not into it.
You don't get the double jump until a few hours into the game, making the game feel a bit "slow" compared to when playing with the double jump, but it's only a factor on second playthroughs.
I personally found exactly one glitch throughout my playtime, and that was an imp glitched in a wall. Thats it, nothing else so I'm giving it full points for quality.
Loading times are just a bit long, but its not a huge deal.
