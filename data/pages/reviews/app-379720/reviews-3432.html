\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter completing the campaign on Ultraviolent I have updated this review with my current thoughts on the game.

Pros
+Fast paced gameplay
+Excellent level design (For most of it)
+Many reimaginings of clasic Doom enemies and equipment
+Secrets hidden around the level
+Many options for customizing your gear to suit your playstyle
+Weapon variety, plenty of options here

Cons
-Melee System, some upgrades make it more bearable but it still takes a while to get used to and could have been cut altogether
-Shared ammo pools can make some weapons irrelevant later
-Late game (Last third or so) Level design becomes rather basic
-While DOOM has never been about its story this iteration falls short
-Color pallets are mostly gray and orange brown like most modern FPS


WARNING the rest of this review contains spoilers for the main campaign as I go into detail about specific topics.

First I want to address my biggest issue with this game, the Melee 'glory kills'. The way it works is after an enemy has lost most of its life it will enter a 'staggered' state and will become vulnerable to an execution move. This seems to be an invincibility frame (Its honestly hard to tell at times) in which you kill your target and they explode into health/ammo/armor depending on which upgrades you've taken. Because of this its generally in your best interest to pop these loot pinatas whenever your low on ammo or health. The problem is jumping in to squeeze the health out of a foe slows the normally fast paced combat so you can watch one of the 4-5 execution animations over and over. You can acquire runes that will speed up the animations but its still an annoying break from the game. There's quite a few supplies scattered around the levels but early game your pretty much forced to make heavy use of this system until you expand your ammo caps a bit.

Weapon variety is fairly good, you'll see most weapons from the doom franchise make a return. In the Chainsaw and BFG two of DOOM's most iconic weapons are treated more as periphrials in a good way. The Chainsaw is basicly a melee instant kill(no need to even injure the enemy first) that causes the enemy to shower you with all types of ammo, usually restoring your entire inventory. It uses fuel thats scattered around the map and depending on what enemy type you use it on will deterime how fast you burn through that fuel. Its a nice little system that gives the chainsaw a place among your arsenal that you'll be using fairly often to restock or simply kill a high priority foe.

The BFG is acquired fairly late in the game and basically does what you'd expect. It can one shot most enemies and clears out a wide area, its ammo pool works similar to the chainsaw and has a separate place on the HUD that informs you of its ammo count at all times. You can only carry 3 shots at any given time but its easy enough to replace them.

Each weapon has 2 alt fire options you can swap back and forth between during the game. Once you spend weapon points to purchase all upgrades for a weapon mode a 'challenge' unlocks giving you a final upgrade that's generally very potent. Overall the only absolute losers in this category are the pistol and basic shotgun. There is no reason to use these at all late game. Ammo is fairly easy to come by with the chainsaw and as long as your swapping between weapons frequently you shouldn't have any problems once you move past the first two levels.

Enemy variety is quite good, most classic enemies return and there are a few new ones that are welcome additions. Overall the enemies seemed 'fair' in the sense that any damage I took was mostly my own problem, it never felt like they were being cheap though a few arena's towards the end of the game liked to stack as many high HP mobs as possible into a small area.

One last thing to touch on is the bosses, there are three separate encounters. The cyber demon returns, looking more like the villain of Darksiders than its classic counterpart. Boring model aside the fight was interesting, nothing too complicated but provided a fair challenge. The 'Hell Guards' were the second boss not long after the Cyberdemon and were more annoying than difficult. They also looked totally out of place for a doom game in my opinion looking more like some strange crustacean bugs with silly hammers. Finally the last boss is the Spider Mastermind and it was a bit too easy compared to the rest of the game but otherwise a very faithful reimagining. Overall the bosses were mediocre and had extremely simple patterns to exploit.

The games pacing is good for the first half but quickly goes downhill. Most of the levels are very well designed up until after the first mission into hell. Then once returning to the Mars facility it just seems like everything becomes more linear and less exciting. The enemies quickly accumulate more and more HP over the course of the game which outscales your weapon upgrades leading to long drawn out battles at the end of the game. In addition there are at least three separate levels I thought would be an end to the game but the designers felt it necessary to keep dragging the game along. Its a rather short game even with that only around 9-10 hours but somehow it feels a lot longer. Whether thats good or bad will probably depend on how much you enjoy the combat.

Finally I'll touch on the story. I'm not a huge fan of the decisions they made here but DOOMs never really been about the story so feel free to skip this section.

So basicly our hero is named DOOMSLAYER. He's basically an 80's Sword and Sorcery warrior who's really good at killing demons and breaking expensive things. The UAC has taped into hell because nuclear power is just too dangerous and unsustainable. So Hell is their alternative. Sure its UAC so we've come to expect this kind of stupidity. A cult forms on mars lead by an old crippled woman who can hear the demons making promises to her. She opens up a direct portal to hell which DOOMSLAYER has to seal up with the help of a computer AI and a Robot scientist with a creepy voice that basically spells out he will betray you in the first 30 seconds of the game. Despite this DOOMSLAYER trusts him and goes on a long adventure to seal up the portal. After all of this is said and done old crippled lady turns into the Spider Mastermind and DOOMSLAYER stomps him. Upon returning Creepy Robot Face takes the McGuffin you spent 6 hours going after and turns it into a lightsaber and drops the Sequel/DLC line and walks off while the credits roll.

Its basic and functional but Bethesda should have either not bother, or tried a little harder because the whole plot comes off as half baked. No one was really expecting a master piece of story telling from DOOM but this story is filled with characters with no motivation real motivation and there are maybe 4-5 characters in total depending on if you count the mysterious demon voice or not. So what if we cant close the portal? Everyone's dead anyway. I guess its implied by being on Mars the demons will somehow threaten Earth but we're never shown anyone in need of rescuing. There just don't seem to be any stakes at all. As I said before DOOMS not really about story so I guess I can't really fault the game for this but not having any clear sense of urgency or motivation makes the game just drag eventually if you play for long sessions at a time.

Overall I would still recommend DOOM despite its shortcomings. Its a face paced shooter which is a refreshing change of pace from most modern FPS's that are published today. The campaign is short but the mechanics are extremely fun. If you don't have a lot of money in your gaming budget, you may get better mileage elsewhere but DOOM is an extremely fun if short game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely worth $60 IMO. Very reminiscent of the classic Doom, gameplay-wise. I like the fact that they didn't try to make it a tactical or cover-based shooter. There's definitely strategy involved though, you have to keep moving at all times. Stop, and you're dead. Combat rhythm is key, you can't spend too much time on one demon at one time. The melee finishers and chainsaw are badass!!

Performance-wise it runs great. It runs smooth on my Asus gaming laptop with most of the settings on Ultra. If you have an Nvidia card the latest driver is required though. The game stuttered badly and wouldn't start until I updated my driver.

If you've ever liked Doom at all, it's worth the purchase. You won't be sorry!\t\t\t\t\t
