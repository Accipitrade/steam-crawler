\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWelcome to hell! Doom is a game that has a near perfect mix of frantic combat (featuring the so called glory kill) alongside a surprisingly interesting story coupled with plenty of lore.

Gameplay wise the game is a nonstop adrenaline rush in the best possible way: Weakening enemies results in them becoming stunned (they'll start flashing blue/orange) which leads to the player being able to perform a melee kill on them and get rewarded with a shower of small medkits. This concept has a profound impact upon the way the game is meant to be be played: This game clearly gives the finger to all the 'hide in a corner waiting for your health to regenerate'-shooters of years past.

In short, the only way to have any chance of survival is to constantly keep moving forward slaughtering demons as you go. Encounters usually happen in a medium to larger sealed off area that almost feels like a multiplayer level (meant in the best possible sense) where various power ups and platforms can be used to the player's advantage. Understanding what routes to run, what enemies need to be dealt with first and using the glory kills to replenish health (or the chainsaw in order to turn a cut up demon into an ammunition fountain) is key to victory.

So all in all an excellent shooter, short and simple. From the beautiful and clever level design, the energetic combat to the first person platform sections (that actually work and are fun in this game!) Doom is a blast to play. Had it not been for the rather lackluster multiplayer, which tries too hard to look and feel like a demon version of HALO and comes with a rather cumbersome interface to form a party with friends, I would have given this a 9,5 out of 10 instead of the current 9.\t\t\t\t\t
