Presentation: 9.5
The graphical and audio quality are extremely well done. The level, character, and weapon design all feature intricate details that most won't even notice without a closer look at the models. The sounds of demons and guns play against a backdrop of heavy metal music one would expect in a Doom game. The voice acting and voice modulation of the few speaking characters are well done and fit the setting. The engine/game are optimized and it runs very smooth even on max graphics while streaming.

Gameplay: 9.5
Carry all the guns at once, you'll need them. Health and armor do not regenerate, so find them where you can. Do not expect to be dragged through hallways by computer controlled squadmates. You may get lost in what feels like a maze trying to find a key to open a door, but while you're searching you're likely to find supplies or secrets.
Rip and tear. Kill all the demons.

Story: 7
Story isn't the focus of the game, but it manages to be entertaining while not intruding on the gameplay.

Total: 8.666/10\t\t\t\t\t

That took 5 minutes. Literally, 5 minutes into this game and I knew it wasn't for me.

I'm a fan of the old-school doom from the 90s (which became succesful because it PUSHED MULTIPLAYER - REPEAT MULTIPLAYER- REPEAT MULTIPLAYER boundaries to the limits, and essentially created MP fps as a genre and I think that the last entry in 2003 or 4 was a step in the right direction (though beleagured by bugs and a lot of inconsistency) but I don't want to play Doom 1993 with upgraded graphics and child-like maps (in their simplicity, compare them to the maps of the original, I dare thee). Doom was not a success because of it's SP campaign, despite what some may wish you to believe, a quick Wiki-check would prove this or any basic research. (Don't trust me, take it from the words of one of it's two genius creators http://www.theverge.com/2013/12/10/5195508/doom-20th-anniversary-retrospective) http://kotaku.com/memories-of-doom-by-john-romero-john-carmack-1480437464

This... looks more akin to a Borderlands style cell-shaded game (graphically, as in it's heavily cartoony for a modern day FPS - i.e. don't expect the 2003 atmosphere or textures, expect serious sam) stitched up in Doom clothes.

I found the first 'locked' room battle to be an epic indicator of what a drudge this game was going to be. After reading/watching dozens of other reviews and giving it a try I can firmly say no thanks. - To those that haven't read other reviews or played the game, envision a series of 'arena' style rooms with hallways connecting them. 90% of the game's combat will take place in essentially the same exact environment, with different window dressing. That environment is a small-medium (with a couple larger settings later on) enclosed room with spawning bad-guys forcing you to constantly run and shoot at the enemies (mainly because they are constantly running and shooting, if you stand still, you die). Which would be bearable if that same combat wasn't offset by this ridiculously boring melee 'execution' which simply requires you to 'stun/stagger' the enemy. Now the amount of damage/stun changes per enemy so there is 'some' variation, but really... it's a kill combo move without the combo. Also, in a First Person Shooter...

It smacks of what would happen when you mix in the doom lisence plus the modern day "instant gratification" desire. The sheer fact that it forces you along like a rollercoaster is intended to blind you to the fact that ... well, everything is kind of shoddy. The environments look like they came out of a game from the early 2000's, (but the UI is fantastic), the story, well, there isn't one and while some may say that is refreshing, I find this highly reminescent of serious sam... without the witty(?) comebacks.

If you like this games graphics, and find it's simplistic and boring combat up your alley, well more power to you.

I on the otherhand, don't.

TLDR- If you are a fan of hectic instant gratification games with no story or substance to speak of, you may like this. On the other hand if you grew up gaming in the 90's and have expected your games to evolve (rather then merge the worst ideas of modern day shooters and throw a doom cover on it and call it "retro" and a return to 90's doom)... you probably won't.

Such a shame, because there is so much potential in the Doom setting.

Again, please if you enjoy this type of content, I encourage you to wait because there is SO little of it, you are doing yourself (and the rest of us gamers) a disservice at purchasing this at 60, Now.

Repeat, THIS TAKES THE WORST ASPECTS (THE SP CAMPAIGN without it's incredible and complex level design) of 90's Doom (which was NOT an emphasis, hence the lack of any part of story, because it was a game intended to be shared online in the days of dial up), while convienently ignoring what made Doom an international success (it's innovative marketing through shareware word of mouth MP gaming - repeat MULTIPLAYER)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS IS A REVIEW OF THE CAMPAIGN. NONE OF THIS REVIEW IS TOWARDS MULTIPLAYER.

I never did look at anything related to Doom preview wise, besides trailers, though I heard a few things from friends that played the beta, so I don't know if ID didn't deliver certain features that were initially promised.

I'm extremely glad I watched some twitch streams on AU/EU release and took the bait on buying the game. This game is exactly what I was looking for in a modern, twich fps. I never came across any game breaking bugs, frames stayed at 60+, the soundtrack is sick (if you ever played the most recent Devil May Cry, the soundtrack in Doom plays in the background during combat like DMC did) and the few graphical glitches that did happen were more hilarious than they were cringe.

This is a Doom title and a very worthy successor. I canno recommend this enough to anyone that enjoys a challenging, extremely fast paced game. Do yourself a favor, buy the game, and massacre all the demons of hell you can. It feels amazing, it looks amazing, and plays amazing. You won't regret your purchase.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf youre wondering whether or not the glorykill feature would get redundant quickly the anwser would be yes. I cant say Ive progressed to far into the game yet, Ive completed three missions so far but there doesnt seem to be any option yet on altering the executions, upgrading them or toggling the options with it. The missions have so far beeen lengthy but I find the pace of combat to be significantly and quite ironically slowed due to the glorykill feature. Its just too frequent. You need to rely on it for a gauranteed health pick ups in battle. The attacks enemys land are devestaing at the highest available level at start( ultra violence) but its just too frequent. While the animations of the executions are coded to be rather seemless it makes the combat flow not so much. I find it annoying that all the demons beyond the basic zombie type will stagger with point blank shotgun blasts (havnt gotten doubleshotgun yet) and direct hits from rockets rather than just explode into gore like youd expect. Its like earning a kill but the enemy doesnt quite die. Its almost as if Im waiting for the narrator from mortal combat to come in and say "FINISH HIM" for about 75% of my kills gving me an option. i just want it to die in the midst of battle without feeling like I have so much control over the melee kill. I dont want to decide whether i want to waste another shell or not. I just want the fight to play out more randomly.

I dont like the demon designs as much as I thought either again Ive only reached mid level demons but the common imp and possesed marines dont have a really intimdating look or style to them. They went with the doom 3 look on the imp design, they just dont look hellish or creepy to me and dont do homage to the original concept of doom. They are lanky and look more like an alien species Ive seen in mass effect series. The possesed marines look like big flesh blobs more than undead soldiers. While these are low threat enemies they are still Doom staples and should at least maintain the style of the original especially for a game that is simply calling itself DOOM. And they should still pose a challenge when they are grouped like in the original. So far taking on scores of them keeps my doomguy fed on health upgrades with all the glorykill staggers being so consistent. And why is every person on mars a bald male? seriously nobody on mars has hair.
I will say the first human antagonist you are introduced to in the plot is quite creepy. its too bad more so than the actual "demons of Hell".
I like searching for secrets but they put these collectibles in each level which are like little dolls or more specifically exactly like the bobbleheads in fallout except they dont grant stat boosts necessarily. It just seems out of place and incongruent. It reminds me of the game of thrones bobblehead merch they produced, the show is violent,sadistic and tragic so whats with the cutesy cartoonish toy thing. I dont see the humor in it, it breaks the tone. Its probabkly a minor thing to most to complain about but I want to feel like im playing doom. I dont need a branding reminder from a bethesda trademark. I get it you paid for Doom to be possible now I pay you so you can keep it Doom, so get it right.

The game is not unenjoyable however or completely unchallenging, I do get defeated and the player deaths look cool and add to my incentive to not be wreckless and survive. Im going to keep playing it but another thing you should know is that theyve included a heavy emphasis on upgrading the weapon mods and praetor aromor, so there is a huge RPG element to this game. I actually dont mind it so much but I think many will not like it. I think its fun to complete the challenges they issue each level, and the enviornments are fun to explore for rank up tokens but at the same time, and I believe for many, this is going to be more of a distraction from what should be the games main and purported focus and that is fast paced seemless combat. Bloody, intense,seemless combat. I hate to say it as Im a big fan of both doom and bethesda but it falls short of that goal, at least so far in the beginning stages of the game. With RPG upgrading emphasis it just plays almost like a metroid game instead. ugh. and with the rune trials it reminded me of a concept in the original turok dinosaur hunter for n64 with those blue portals(now that was a stellar game for its time, but again what made it successful was not preserved)
It would probably be more fair to write a review until I get a little further in the game but Im already annoyed especially at the glory kill so I had to post now. Sorry if this lets anyone down or discourages you from buying. I suggest you look up youtube reviews as well before accepting my opinion as one you would share.\t\t\t\t\t
