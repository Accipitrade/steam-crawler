\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBy far the best Metroid Prime game I've ever played.


It seems like what's old is new again. It feels more like a throwback to Quake II than to the original DOOM, from level design to gameplay, but it works. Take DOOM/II and strip it down to its bare qualities, frankenstein it back together with everything 1990's FPS, then turn the knob to 11.

There wasn't as much psychological strain when it came to overall creep factor though, you can see that the goal of this game was to not repeat DOOM 3. There wasn't a whole lot of fear-inducing moments, and majority of the demons had a very cartoon/caricature quality to them. The level design was superb, but again, a lot of it felt very Biblical Hell, as if I was walking through a 19th century painting. I only wish there was more absurdity/surrealism to the Hell environment, most of it felt like I was just on another planet.

And I couldn't help but notice the extreme similarities to Metroid Prime I & II though. Replace Argent Energy with Phazon, and Hell with Dark Aether, and boom, you have Metroid Prime.

Would grin like Doomguy when finding the chaingun again, 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a blast.

I am through most of it, so I have a pretty good idea what the game is about.

This game very much reminds me of old school shooters - no reloading, fast paced, strafe and run shooting. I have not had this much fun with a FPS game since the HL games. Every weapon (aside from the pistol) is useful, and the glory kills are satisfying and useful. There is a story behind it, but in the end, just have fun shooting all the demons!

The game is very smooth and looks great - I was compelled to find secrets in each level, but I don't think it's neccesarry.

Absolutely buy this game if you want a bit of a throwback to the run and gun style of game - no hiding, no recovering, just shoot demons! I liked Doom 3, but his is much better IMO.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
+ Fast paced, exhilirating combat
+ Looks great, runs smooth as butter
+ Wide variety of weapons and enemies
+ Focused, lean storyline

Cons:
- The hardest difficulty setting initially available is rather easy


The Bottom Line:

Ya know, I really did try to come up with more Cons but honestly - Doom does what it set out to do and does it extremely well.

id Software comes back to us following a marginally successful new IP (Rage) in 2011 and twelve years after our last taste of anything Doom-related, and man did they come back ready to stun us. As has become typical of id Software (save a few initial kinks with Rage) the game runs smooth and looks fantastic. Reinforcing this is a great artistic direction and level design that makes each stage an experience in and of itself.

Refusing to conform to today's endless cover shooter or "realism"-centric titles, Doom brings the old-school, high-octane linear bullet-fest back from the 1990s and introduces it to a new generation exceedingly exhausted by every game needing a grim, emotionally driven story-line. Doom is all about mindless fun and killing demons with everything from a chainsaw to rocket launcher and watching hellspawn disintegrate into piles of blood and guts.

There really isn't much more than needs to be said honestly. Doom is fun, plain and simple.

Score: 10 out of 10 Pinkies\t\t\t\t\t
