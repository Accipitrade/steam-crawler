DOOM - THE POWER TRIP

+ The latest iteration of id Tech; unparalleled graphic fidelity and performance with Vulkan
+ Classic weapon arsenal
+ Combat is just visceral and satisfying all around
+ Glory kills feel as good as they look
+ The Sounds of Mick Gordon
+ Hell levels are beautifully themed with complex geometry and some oldschool keyhunts
+ Alternate "Arcade" mode synergizes nicely with glory kills/upgrade systems
+ Snapmap creation system
+ Centered weapon option (although SP only)
+ Fun upgrades

- Level flow is irritatingly stop-and-go - fighting is done inside the dedicated "arena" areas and very little else
- Extreme overabundance of sidequests/secrets accentuates the above point
- UAC levels packed with corridor shooting and extended story-related sequences
- Enemies generally very vulnerable to kiting and stagger extremely easily
- Low health values for most major demons (only enemies with a sizeable amount are Pinkies and bosses)
- Absurd power creep through upgrades destroys any semblance of challenge the game has on normal difficulties
- Almost all secrets show up on the minimap, invalidating their intended purpose
- MP locks gameplay content behind progression system
