\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA testosterone-induced genocide of the vile creatures of hell. Definitely recommended.

Any game that manages to get me immersed or break apathy is one I don't regret experiencing.
This one got me so pumped while playing, that I had an urge to break the face of every demon I met. When I ran out of demons, I wanted to find more, just so I can torture them too.

This game is like Metroid Prime with extra violence and gore, or Half-Life with way more power, or Duke Nukem if the last game was made the way we anticipated. In other circumstances, it'd be a survival horror; but here it's only horror for the demons - you're busy being furious, never on-edge or afraid of what's around the corner.

I have some gripes; there are some small defects, like some random glitching tap-dancing furniture or 1 zombie that sounds like an entire horde. And it might be a matter of personal taste, but I dislike the search for all the secrets (you get upgrades if you find them, so it's not a choice for me) - one moment I'm beating things to a pulp, and at the other, I'm looking behind the table or at the ceiling from different angle to see if I can find a hidden path to a doom-guy toy. It's cute, but breaks the build-up from the fight, and it's sort-of mandatory. At least the stages can be re-visited.\t\t\t\t\t
