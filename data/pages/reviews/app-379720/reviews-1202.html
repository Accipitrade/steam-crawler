\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an absolute masterpiece, and I mean that with all the weight that word holds.

The Combat:

For not only a DOOM game, but an fps game in general that is on PC, the game feels way better on a controller, at least an Xbox One controller. Never used the Dualshock 4 controller so I can't say anything on that. The combat is done very well, possibly the best combat in any game I've ever played. There are things called Gore Nests in this DOOM which are surrounded by the Possessed (The low level, easy to kill Demons) which once you clear all of them out, you will proceed to rip out the Nest's heart (Kind of like a Spire from Saint's Row: Gat Out Of Hell). You will be surrounded by dozens of Demons which all spawn in the same location each time you play the game (No RNG) and are well placed as can be said for the Gore Nests and overall Demon placement. Not too many where fighting is boring, not too little for when you crave it. The only thing that I don't like is the standard melee (Not the glory kills, those are perfect) which is a lot of the times you attempt to melee, you will miss and be put aside of the Demon allowing you to be hit.

The Demons:

There are 18 standard Demons and 3 Boss Demons that all have their strengths and weaknesses. There are plenty of them around and each have multiple types of unique glory kills for you to experiment with. The bosses can be hard even if you know how to beat them and are experienced at the game but overall the difficulty options are there for if you find them too hard or too easy. Which leads me on to the next topic.

The Difficulty:

There are five total difficulty options in DOOM 2016: I'm Too Young Too Die, Hurt Me Plenty, Ultra-Violence, Nightmare, Ultra-Nightmare. The first four difficulties affect how much health and armor you pick up, how much damage you take and how much health the Demons have. The odd one out being Ultra-Nightmare which is the same as Nightmare but this time, there is a Perma-Death mechanic (Once you die, then you have to restart the entire game) I would recommend playing at either Hurt Me Plenty or Ultra-Violence for casual play or if it's your first time playing.

The Weapons:

Just like the other DOOM titles, you start with a pistol and once you get to certain points in the game (Either through secrets or just plain progression) you will build up quite the arsenal which you can even upgrade through the new feature of Weapon Upgrade Points that are earned through finding secrets and combat. A cool new feature is the chainsaw's ability to give you ammo however the chainsaw needs fuel unlike the other DOOM titles. Each Demon requires a certain amount of fuel to kill but is east to find around the map.
Another thing is that the BFG works way better than in any other DOOM game and takes separate ammo compare to just taking Plasma Rounds.

The Story:

The Story is fantastic. I'm not going to say much on this so I don't spoil it, however the story isn't the main reason you would be playing this.

The Achievements:

Overall, they are not too difficult to earn including the Multi-Player ones. Take note there is a bug with the ''The Circle Is Complete'' Achievement where you have to earn them all in one run. If you don't do this, then you will need to create a new game just as I and many others have done. The Multi-Player achievements are very time consuming. It took me 20 Hours (Almost) to get them all, even though I am pretty decent at it from playing it a lot on Xbox. In the end, the achievements are worth completing despite being rage inducing (Which is actually kind of helpful considering what do in this game).

The Soundtrack:

I left this one for last because words cannot describe how amazing this soundtrack is. I don't typically listen to game soundtracks outside of the game itself but this one, I just can't help myself. It is professionally put together on its own and when in game. When you listen to the standalone soundtrack, it is in typical song form but when it is played in-game, the music reacts to how you play. Meaning the way you play and who you are fighting is monitored.

TL;DR

Combat: 10/10
Demons: 9/10
Difficulty: 10/10
Weapons: 9/10
Story: 8/10
Achievements: 7.5/10
Soundtrack: Too good for a rating
