What the hell...

The fact that Doom doesn't value a big story, the game doesn't take itself very seriously and our marine doesn't value any gibberish is shown by the fact that Doom uses the communication panel that Dr. Samuel Hayden talks to him, just throws it in the corner.

Even if there are always one or two story scraps in the game, this is negligible and actually not relevant for me. Since when do you need a story in Doom?

We're on Mars, researchers are messing around with hell again and we're supposed to fix it. That's enough for me because I know why I'm here...

Already after the first few seconds it gets down to business and you quickly realize where the journey with the reboot should go because there is no great intro stuff at all.
Well-known and iconic content, such as putting on armor or receiving well-known weapons, is really celebrated.
For those of you who enjoyed the older Doom titles, you're in for a killer party. But that doesn't mean that the game is only fun if you know the originals. no way.

So why play Doom right now?

I definitely recommend it as it's a great first person shooter that leaves nothing to be desired in terms of graphics and gameplay.
I can understand that some people might find Doom boring, and the old shooter joke "kill all enemies and run to the end of the level" applies here as well.

Doom neither wants to stage a believable world nor convey the drama of a military operation, let alone tell a profound story.
Doom is an entertaining and action-packed game and uncompromisingly subordinates everything to this goal.

And that's exactly why I have so much fun with it, especially when I'm jumping into the middle of a horde of demons and swinging the chainsaw, then the pure game mechanics captivate me.

They are rage, brutal, without mercy. But you, you will be worse... Rip and Tear, until it is done ...

Since I wrote a review of the absolutely amazing sequel, DOOM Eternal, at a very high quality, I have decided to do the same here.
(most of these things were from the PS4 version, but I will talk about the PC ver too)
Also note: If you plan on buying Eternal, do NOT expect the same gameplay as in here. A lot of people are buying Eternal, realising the gameplay is different, refunding, and trying to convince everyone this is a 50x better game and that Eternal is horrendous. Remember that the gameplay difference between this and Eternal is like night and day)

Plot

Basically you're this guy who was sleeping for like a thousand years then humanity discovered you and while you're in there a demonic invasion breaks out sending everything to Hell(pun unintentionally intended).

Gameplay
The gameplay here is fast, fluent, and most of all, brutal . You have a large assortment of upgradable weapons, allowing you to survive on Mars(and Hell). There are also runes, which 3 can be chosen at any given time. However, you must do a challenge, such as killing all demons in the challenge map with just 1 HP, to acquire these runes.
Your suit can be upgraded with Praetor Suit Points, found from dead members of the Elite Guard. There are 5 categories to upgrade.
Resource managment is slightly out the window. You still have to grab HP with Glory Kills, but you have a lot, and I mean it, a lot of ammo.
All these things can be found in secret areas, which also give you:
- Classic DOOM levels
- Collectibles which give you character and weapon models
- Something else idk.
Also powerups exist such as Berserk.

So you walk eternally through the shadow realms, standing against evil where all others falter. May your thirst for retribution never quench, may the blood on your sword never dry, and may we never need you again...

Graphics
The graphics are good. Though for me, I had to lower it a bit to get a good framerate, but its still good. Hell also looks good, even though Im convinced they retconned its floating rocks.

Multiplayer
Holy fricking moly , we've got actual MP! Deathmatch and all the other modes that were removed in Eternal(refer to my review for a summary of that games MP). Loadouts(yes, loudouts in a DOOM game), Playable demons and a lot more.

Snapmap
Build your own levels! Yes, thats right. You can cobble up a level with preset rooms, join them up with other rooms, add other gameplay and maybe even make a brand new experience. Its an easy editor too, but holy moly I commend those who made the complex stuff because I'm too stupid to understand or make that.

Score

I hereby give DOOM 2016, an 9/10, same score as DE.

Until we see eachother again...
