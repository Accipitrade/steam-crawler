\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Fun, but dull..
Very satsifying gameplay. The "glory kills" which are pretty much just pre-recorded quicktime events get pretty old fast. The graphics are pretty nice, and not too excessive. They went a bit too hard in my opinion and made the game take up a solid quarter of my hard drive's space. Don't buy the game if you dont have a solid 80+ gigs to blow. The multiplayer is pretty fun and offers some solid customization options and gamemodes. The storymode is alright but I didn't pay attention to it, as I was too busy focusing on the more apparent goal of the protagonist: To destroy everything possible. The game features many instances where your character is told "You should probably not do that!" and the only option you are given is to continue your path of destruction. As for the story: My understanding is that some company was harnessing energy from hell (seems kinda unnecessary) and that was, as it turns out, not a good idea. The gate to hell is opened by some mean lady who was from hell. You go to hell to make everything better again. All in all, I give it a 7/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best reboot I've seen. With this game Id Software proves it understands why people like Doom. (spoiler) It's killing demons with shotguns. Doom 2016 translates the superspeed and brutality of Doom 1 and 2 beautifully into full 3D. Id also took some pointers from Painkiller and will frequently lock you in rooms full of demons. That's what you're there for though, brutalizing them.

On a different note, Doomguy is one of the better realized characters this year thanks to some lovely arm animation and not much else other than what you do with him. Murder the legions of hell. What more do you really want though?

The game looks, sounds, and plays beautifully. There's not much else to say. Go murder some demons with your fists. Or shotguns. Or rocket launchers. Or whatever.\t\t\t\t\t
