\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest shooter game in last decade. DOOM simply disobeys almost every classic FPS game design and sets a new standard.
Unlike traditional FPS DOOM encourages the player to be active and rewards accordingly by different means.

But what is different ?:

- Enemies force you to actively move around to keep the game pace high.
- No regenerating health bar.
- Instead of taking cover when hurt, brilliant enemy AI forces you to perform a glory kill to regen health.
- No aiming down sights.
- Strafing is a lot faster than traditional FPS games.
- Battle arena based level design.
- Very diverse enemies with different abilities, different weaknesses and with very diverse fighting behaviours/pattens.


What I didn't like

- Level design flaws in just some parts of the game that are forcing the player to revisit some areas can get discouraging for some players.
- Some level's design feels very linear.
- More diverse in-game collectibles could be added. Just collecting small doombots or finding dead soldiers are not satisfying for the amount of exploration DOOM offers.

Due to increasing production costs of AAA games and stakeholder promises, game studios are forced to rush and sell their games to higher prices than ever before. We recently have seen very well designed games like Dishonored 2, Watch Dogs 2 and Prey to not achieve the minimum estimated sales and disappear. I definitely don't believe id Software is ever going to receive the full potential credits DOOM deserves due to the same reasons that applied for AAA that were made in 2016.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe announcement of this masterpiece recalled some great memories... Doom was my first MP game.
I've enjoyed years after of demon-ass kicking sessions alone and sleepless nights of gaming with my friends at that time.
I bought the collectors' edition (not from STEAM, of course) which is absolutely great. The statuette of the Revenant is well crafted, exactly as the computer model of the game, and I tell you without being afraid to mention this... is one of the best collector editions I have bought in my life.
This game have, at least of all the games I've ever played, one of the best optimized graphics engines in the business. Runs ok in my seasoned Alienware m17x R3, HDD and RAM upgraded, from 2011, and in my most recent rig (X99 ASUS MoBo, Intel Core i7 5960X, and dual SLI GTX 980 Ti) is a silk-smooth, gut-ripping rave party. Textures, models, sound, gameplay, Metal dynamic background music... everything is extraordinary. The most amazing thing is that this Doom feels like the old Doom, even with the tremendous technological and graphical differences between them.
The new feature called Glory Kill is violently satisfying, great addition to the parkourish-style of this new iteration of Doom, and well implemented in the challenges. Some levels are a non-stop gory action-packed, guns-blazing festival... then the silence, with the welcomed feeling of satisfaction at the end. This kind of experiences makes you going back again, to taste the emotion. Action so addictive... you'll feel like a diabetic in a candy store.
I fully recommend this game... you won't regret it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is way too easy and the two hardest difficulties are locked until you finish the game. It gets even easier the more you progress with all the upgrades you get, and the secrets aren't even secrets anymore since you can easily find them just by checking the map. Ammo isn't very important in this game since you get a pistol with infinite ammo that can kill most enemies with one charged shot when fully upgraded. Most enemies decide to run away and climb walls instead of trying to attack you, and when they do attack you it's by shooting slow projectiles that are very easy to dodge.

There's no challenge to this game, it's just a mindless bland shooter that's probably an attempt to get casual console gamers used to games like Call of Duty and Halo to get a taste of what a FPS used to be like in the 90's.

The game is also filled with bugs as expected from a Bethesda game, there's one part I had to replay around 6 times because I kept falling through the floor in the same area while trying to climb and the chainsaw is so bugged an enemy might disappear in front of you when the kill animation is supposed to play.

Let me go in details as to why it's too easy:

1.You get health (and armor later in game) from killing enemies which basically makes you invincible as long as you're killing

2.Some weapons like the railgun are way too overpowered and can kill most enemies in one shot. Even the shotgun which is the first weapon you get can kill most enemies with one shot of its alternate fire when fully upgraded.

3.Secrets can easily be found by looking at the map and you can make it even easier by getting suit upgrades that puts an icon over every secrets on the map

4.Enemy projectiles can easily be dodged by simply sidestepping

5.Challenges progress stays even if you die, that means if you need to kill a certain enemy in a certain way you can kill yourself and repeat as much as you want.

6.There's tons of health packs, armor, ammo and health stations in every areas where you fight enemies as if getting all those from killing enemies wasn't easy enough.
