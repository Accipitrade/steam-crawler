\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty rock solid game that feels like the original Doom game but with obvious better visuals. Gameplay takes me back quite a bit to the first release from the way the player moves to the enemies and story line. I enjoyed how some powerups are hidden and take some risk to find and get. The secrets are a nice throwback too (they're Doom plushies :D).

The weapons are pretty much what I'd expect out of Doom and I find them fun to use. I would suggest playing on the highest difficulty you can when you start as it makes the game more enjoyable, I think anyways.

Not many downsides to list aside from the use of the kill glory mechanic where you have to basically hit a key when enemies start to turn orange which shoots off a kill cinematic. They tend to get old real fast as there aren't that many cinematics (atleast per enemy) but you're somewhat forced to trigger the kill glory mechanic as it causes enemies to drop health and in some cases resources like ammo. I feel like if more cinematics were added to address this then the kill glory mechanic wouldn't get as stale as it did for as quick as it did.

The powerups are fun so far but they seem to be few and far between although it may be that I just haven't figured out a good way to unlock them yet.

The soundtrack is very much alike that of something from a Nine Inch Nails album which positively adds to the experience. Nothing like killing multiple enemies from hell while some NIN-type music is keeping up the pace with the mayhem. Parts of the game have an eerie vibe to it as well due to the music which gives it a nice atmosphere.

Pros:
- Fast gameplay
- Feels like the original Doom
- Story is entertaining enough to warrant following
- Music matches the gameplay and style of the game
- Weapons are pretty fun to use, very Doom-like

Cons:
- Kill glory cinematics tend to get old somewhat fast, especially given your reliance on them to get health and ammo

Overall, real positive throwback to the original Doom game. Would recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSingle player:

Really good. One of the best single player campaignes in recent memory. Make no mistake this is a Doom game. And it is a blast. From the weapon upgrades to the creatures. To just the speed and mobility you have.

Multiplayer:

I know there were a lot of people that did not like the beta. But the full multiplayer with everything in is really a lot of fun. Sure there is a progression system with unlocks. But the levels come fast and the unlocks are really quite cool. 2 or 3 hours and you have all the weapons unlocked. I think it is actaully quite fun. It does remind of of old shooters. Yes I would have like to have had all the weapons be pick ups. But there are some weapons to pick up Chain saw, BFG, one or two others as well. And the Demon Mechanic is done pretty well and is not super overpowerd.

All in all this is a game well worth 60 bucks. I have played every ID game since Return to Castle Wolfenstein, and I think this game might be my Fav.\t\t\t\t\t
