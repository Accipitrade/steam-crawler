\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was deeply skeptical about this new Doom game following the multiplayer beta a few months ago. The reviews came out and were overwhelmingly positive, so I decided to pick it up on sale. I didn't play any of the multiplayer, just the campaign and a few snapmaps. id has takensingle player first person shooters and reinvigorated them far beyond what I expected. The last time I had this feeling playing a FPS it was the original Serious Sam - not that the games are similar, but that they both take a concept that has been bogged down with years of building gameplay premises on top of one another and starts from a relatively clean state to deliver an astonishingly fresh shooter. Years of being sneaky, slowly assessing threats, making sure your guns are reloaded - just not something that happens in Doom. Yes, one could argue that it is mostly moving from arena to arena, killing all the enemies and moving on, but it never feels like it. Each encounter is fresh. The game favors constant movement, both in the horizontal and vertical planes, which is something else. It also rewards agression with the glory kill system. Sniping isn't an option when a half dozen demons are up in your face. The speed, the movement, the aggression, it's all extremely refreshing in a genre that seems to have gone for slower, more deliberate encounters. In the process of writing this, I just realized that Doom very closely resembles the Just Cause series in its pacing in that it's full throttle, nonstop action and the player is always doing, about to do, or having done something awesome.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really liked this game. The graphics looked awesome and one could see that this was optimized for the PC. The textures, effects, and the art direction inspire you to push forward. Gameplay is incredibly fluid and fun without feeling tedious. Doom uses an arena-style approach to combat but it doesn't feel repetitive - there's a right amount of difficulty and monster volume, and makesyou feel like a complete badass. Story is simple but believable and enjoyable. The story is about two things - stop Hell from invading our universe and discover who the Doom guy is - and is delivered well through progression and from finding documents/recordings in-game.

Stuff I didn't like - lack of people on multiplayer and gun feel. On the former, it was unfortunate that Doom was launched pretty close to Overwatch, so it was competing with Overwatch for multiplayer time. The latter isn't too big of an issue, but some weapons (e.g. plasma cannon and heavy machine gun) feel very similar. Would have been great to have better differentiation across all the weapon types.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDISCLAIMER: I quit playing around the Argent Tower. Further reading will tell you why.

Doom 2016 is a weird thing. It features some of the most fun FPS action in a long time... but said action is unfortunately wrapped in a ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• sandwich of a game.

Okay, so let's start looking at the game at a zoomed-in-extremely-close level. The shooting is fun. Like, seriously, viscerally fun. Don't let anyone tell you otherwise.

Now, let's zoom out to look at the course of a battle, say, one following the destruction of a Gore Nest. The enemies will teleport into the room in small increments, which on one hand makes combat continuously ferocious and manageable.. and on the other hand manages to be woefully predictable (in that it's going to happen) and feel stupidly random (what exactly you're ambushed by) at the same time.

And as fun as the Glory Kill mechanic is for the first couple of dozen times, being essentially forced into doing them in all their flow-breaking, um, glory, is just plain stupid. The same goes for the chainsaw, with the added bonus of it being a limited-use super inventory booster.

Glory kills and chainsaw attacks aside, I have a personal pet peeve with the way combat is forced into close quarters. See, I like to hide behind corners and snipe off enemies one by one. Not in this game, buddy. You try that and an enemy will teleport in right behind you and kill you faster than you can say WHY THE HELL WAS I HOLDING A ROCKET LAUNCHER ANYWAY?! You have to keep moving, keep getting in the fray and throw caution to the wind. I'd probably agree with this more if the level design was the reason behind it and not the game design.

Speaking of level design... The designers went so far out of their way to pack the levels with hidden goodies that it sometimes feels like you're playing an MMORPG instead of an FPS. This is also what made me throw the game away. There's platforming. Mandatory, seemingly aimless platforming. In a Doom game. You even get double-jumping shoes, as if this is some cutesy-but-impossible indie platformer with pixel graphics and not a game about shotgunning demons in the face.

In the last hour that I intend to play Doom, I managed to inadvertently throw myself out of bounds and into an insta-death. Twice. The first time I was chasing a checkpoint down a dark alley that apparently wasn't actually there; the second time I was trying to navigate a platforming section with the Double-Jump Boots of Infinite Stupidity and took a turn that the level designers didn't expect. Even before those happy accidents, I had managed to get lost in the gigantic levels a few times because I had managed to miss or ignore the direction I was supposed to proceed into.

As for game design in general, it's a hot mess if you ask me. There's collectibles, weapon upgrades that mostly seem like crap, HUD upgrades that also seem like crap, secret mini-challenges to unlock even more crap, boosts to weapon/armor/ammo maximums and this and that and oh stop it already.

It's like this game tries to do all these things that are supposed to be good for games (in order to be fun for everyone, because one of them is eventually bound to click with whoever you are) that the core gameplay goodness only manages to present itself in brief glimpses in the middle of everything else that's more of a chore to get through.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's Doom...rebooted.

Take the frenetic gameplay, non-regenerating health and the search for powerups and secret areas of the first doom and add better graphics and some RPG like elements where you powerup your weapons and armor to make them and you stronger. You've got yourself a winner.

The guitar heavy soundtrack is back. The demons look really good. The game is fast paced.

I've not played multiplayer yet but I'm working my way through the single player campaign.

It's still really easy to get lost and I've found myself having to think outside the box when navigating through the levels. This isn't a bad thing as it's something that was done a lot in the first game too.

I do recommend this game and I can't wait to see what the guys over at id add to this game in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOMG! I have been playing DOOM franchise since they began. I now have a capable rig that allows me to run in 4k with no stuttering. The game looks awesome. Setting gama for a darker game makes the creep factor even better. I play in a dark room with headphones (no surround sound capablities) with the music down a bit. Startle the heck out of you moments galore! Music is adrenaline pumping and frenetic.

I have never been and most likley never will be a multiplayer gamer. At first I though the new version was *only* MP. So glad I checked again! The glory kill mechanic is really cool; the drone drops and weapons upgrades are excellent; the praetor suit upgrades and how you earn them is very nice. Secret rooms and the DOOM legacy rooms are a riot.

I love the snap maps. Even though I got totally toasted on the first three I tried, I was able to find one I could handle with great challenging play.

All in all.. DOOM began the FPS style of gaming and this installment does not disappoint or dimimish their place at the top of the heap (of hell spawned bodies!) of this genre!

Fully recommend the game to any gamer who is into FPS, likes lots of action, or just likes DOOM.\t\t\t\t\t

In short, it's a game that takes the best of both and delivers a hyper violent ballet of death. Honestly the only gripe I'll offer about this game is the music, now don't get me wrong, I love the ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• out of some Heavy Metal but what is on offer here is over produced, metal electronica.
