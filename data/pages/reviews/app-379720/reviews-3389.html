\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf we ignore the annoying and frequent load times, the glitches experienced in the first few hours of the game, and the story that has as much depth as a kiddie pool, we're left with a game that claims to be all about running and gunning but is so hesitant to let you just do some running and gunning.

The bulk of the game is
Enter an area
The doors close
Enemies spawn
Run in circles using the shotgun and the plasma rifle
Doors open
Repeat

The levels felt like they had been designed with movement and momentum as an afterthought, there are awkward jumps and the climbing feature is often glitchy enough to be unreliable, enemies will spawn on platforms to hit you off severely limiting your running choices, there are occasionally environmental elements that run on a timer, so you'll have to wait for them to proc before moving through, its very hesitant to really just let you go ballistic, to drop some powerups and explosive barrels around the map and let you be Doom guy. That's not to say that there's no room to go out and jump on things, but by far the most effective means of clearing a level is running in circles with a plasma rifle and shotgun.

The bosses felt uninspired, even annoying. A boss battle should feel intensely satisfying and piggyback off new mechanics or weaponry or play styles introduced prior to the player, but they were just enemies with a bigger health bar. Their "stages" that they cycle through as they lose health are essentially just the same attack with a different animation, and I found myself bored and wanting boss fights to be over so I could move on.

The weapon upgrades start out satisfying and incredibly rewarding, getting your shotgun upgrade is a game changer in the beginning, but after getting the super shotty there's really no need for other upgrades. I found myself forgetting about upgrade points and accumulating upwards of ~20 points before remembering I needed to use them. The suit upgrades were a little better, offering more useful upgrades, but they weren't game changing by any means.

The story is one of the worst i've ever heard - somehow they managed to make a portal to hell on mars combated by a space marine boring. Without giving too much away (there's not much to give away) they introduce a character who I *believe* you're supposed to be attached to and kill him off in less than an hour or two. I say "believe" because they were seriously there for such a short time that I had no feelings about them one way or the other, but the death was supposed to be some tragic sacrifice. Maybe they really were just a filler character, but very anticlimactic either way. And of course they introduce a character who you know is evil from the start and turns out to be, in fact, evil.

All in all, I understand why people like this game, I really do. Some of the kills are satisfying (although they get quickly repetitive) and the music is an absolute blast, but I found myself wishing the game would just end already. The first hour or two once the game kicks off is pretty fun, but it just recycles content from there. If you can pick this up for less than 10 dollars, it's worth the money, but with around an 9-10 hour campaign, and as tedious and mediocre as it was, I can't recommend it at full price. 5/10\t\t\t\t\t
