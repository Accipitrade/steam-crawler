\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest Doom Game!!

Most fans weren't expecting much when this game was announced, myself included, but this is the best and most fun Doom game i've ever played.

The singleplayer is the best mode this game has to offer it's absolutely flawless with large well designed maps, lots of secrets and collectibles, nice visuals, great demon designs and wide selection of weapons. The small details like ledge grabbing, double jump and glory kills are what make this game so fast paced and so fun. Doom 2016 is what Doom 3 should've been and so much more.

The singleplayer has a story which can be ignored for the most part but it still manages to force itself in your face, personally, i think the game would've worked fine without a story because the characters are boring anyway and you just want to run and gun everything. The game also has in game challenges which are all fun and accessible some may find these too easy to complete but it depends on what difficulty mode you're playing.

Some nitpicks are the music, it's not bad, it has some good tracks but overall not memorable like the original Doom games. The sound effects seem to fade away when glory killing too many of the bigger enemies at once.
Another nitpick is that there is no sprinting which is very weird for a Doom game not even in multiplayer.

Finally the multiplayer is fine it can be fun but don't expect too much it's mostly the same old modes with the same old modern leveling up and unlocking customizations it's nothing new and lots of people are still playing. Demon runes are fun if you manage to be first but otherwise it's a lot of work and time consuming to unlock everything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, that was disappointing...

I brought this game at the bargain bin price of around 20$ AUD (11 Pounds) and despite the low price, this game was one of the worst i have played in recent memory. A few things to remember about this review.

1. Just because i bought it on sale at a lower price, doesn't mean i will be reviewing it for that price, i am looking at this game as a 100$ next gen title of a pre-existing franchise.

2. I do not have much experience in triple A games.

3. For 100$ i expect at least 200 hours of playtime minimum.

With these in mind, lets start with the positives.

Visuals:
As i previously stated i had not played many triple A games prior to this, and DOOM 2016 is the most recent game i have played as of writing, so i was very happy to see that despite not needing to look all that good, this game was gorgeous, and for the most part i didn't have trouble with performance (Apart from one hilariously bad occasion which will be discussed later). Some things were somewhat low poly but i cant complain with a lighting engine as good as DOOM's so oh well. The visuals could have done with something innovative and new but even then it wouldn't be necessary. Visuals: 83/100

Level Design:
Coming into this game, i expected the levels to be like a large arena with smaller rooms to explore all with unique assets. This was not the case in the game, however i wish it was. The levels in actuality are mostly multiple small arenas that connect through corridors and small rooms. This could work by itself but alas ID thought it would be a good idea to hide secrets at every few corners, and this might have been a good thing if there was a middle ground between secrets for blind people, and secrets you need to look at the map every 6 seconds to find. To make this worse, there are many points of no return meaning you can miss some things altogether. And as for the hordes of demons, they never last all that long, and they are usually not much of a challenge (On normal), in fact i turned the difficulty up later in the playthrough and had a little, albeit not much more fun. Level Design: 38/100

Gunplay:
For a triple A game i expected the gunplay to be on par with Killing Floor 2, however instead i got a what felt like a slightly updated Fallout 4 combat system with an admittedly excellent takeout system called 'Glory Kills'. While glory kills are an excellent mechanic, it couldn't save this games lacklustre gunplay from feeling like you are firing nerf guns instead of those fancy lead propelling explosive based hand cannons known as 'guns'. Its just such a shame too, i mean you would expect DOOM of all franchises to rewrite gunplay in video games but no. Gunplay: 41/100

Bugs:
For a game published by Bethesda, this game sure is polished. The only minor bugs i can note are that sometimes demons get stuck running in circles, once getting suck in moving collision, and getting stuck in a room despite killing all of the demons. However there is one bug that while may be uncommon, is so hilariously bad its hard to believe. Keep in mind that despite this bugs weight, i will not factor it into the review due to its unlikely nature. So, after a long download i booted up the game and found that my FPS was going from 20 to 40 to 120 to 10 and so on. So naturally i thought i bit off more than i could chew, seeing the age of my PC, so i turned everything to the lowest settings for testing to find that it had no impact on the FPS counter having a seizure in the corner of my screen. I then looked for help online and was told to install the newest NVIDIA drivers which didn't work (Also took 6 hours due to NVIDIA's awful customer service). So i kept searching until i found your stereotypical crusty tutorial with Paralyzer playing in the background (I'm serious) which told me a trick to fix my FPS. It worked. That trick was to disable Razor Chroma from being used in the game, due to DOOM attempting to run a script 600 times a second for some reason. Like i said, unlikely, but still worth noting. Especially if you are using Razer Chroma.

Length:
This is the factor that kills this game (With the BFG 9000). ITS THE LENGTH OF A CHEAP INDIE TITLE. When i saw how many levels there were i felt rather sick, so i kept playing while hoping that levels get longer. They didn't get longer, they didn't get better ether. Selling a product this short for as much money as it was sold for is insane. Worst of all i got 100% on all levels except the last, so in other words, no i didn't rush the game, i extended it for as long as i could! And i like games that are short, but THIS?! What's the point! The game is worth 15$ AUD, or 20$ If you are a hardcore DOOM fan not 100! Remember that this is a next gen, high budget title made by a large company and published by an even larger one. More importantly its replayability is limited, as going through one level again is already a slog and feels uneventful. Also this game has multiplayer, it is dead, therefore not worth mentioning. Besides, this is a singleplayer game primarily. Length: 9/100


Conclusion:
Not only is DOOM 2016 shorter than what should be allowed for sale at its asking price, it is also unengaging. And unengaging is the last word you think of when playing the original DOOM. If the game were 10x longer, i still wouldn't recommend it, due to the fact that DOOM 2016's gunplay is awful for a game this new and high budget. Rating 28/100\t\t\t\t\t
