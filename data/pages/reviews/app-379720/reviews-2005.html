\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProtips:

- It's really easy to forget since it doesn't appear when you select your weapons with your mousewheel, but remember that you have a BFG (once you get it). Absolutely essential to get out of some of the tougher spots, the default key is 'T'.

- Same goes for the chainsaw. Remember to use this when you need ammo/health as it causes enemies to spew. Default is 'G' or press '1' twice.

- Remember to use your equipment! Grenades and holograms are the most useful, default is F1/F2 to select what to use, and middle mouse to use it.

On to the review... Loved this remake, and everything about it has been so well done with probably the best optimization in a game i've seen to date!

+ Great single player campaign and maps
+ This is a remake, not a sequel - Doom actually has a proper (and interesting) story now!
+ The game looks GORGEOUS.
+ Rewards fast and smooth gunplay, none of those stealth and conservation hijinks here.
+ Really liked the RPG-esque weapon/armour/ability levelling and customization.

- Boss fights can be a little underwhelming...
- Didn't really get sucked in by the multi-player aspect. Lots of options but kind of boring.

All in all, one of the best games i've played in a long while, made me realize how much i missed the adrenal injection that was the original DOOM - highly recommended! :)\t\t\t\t\t

Story: It's there, but the main attraction is the combat. (7/10)
Gameplay: RIP AND TEAR UNTIL IT'S DONE! (Fluid, fast combat, great controls, amazing weapons, exploration gives a breathing time between fights so they don't grow old too fast, great rewards for dedication towards challenges and the map design is good; but some areas being locked to avoid backtracking after you progress through the map can get annoying if you're a completionist. Despite that, I still give it a (10/10).
Music: A f***ing AMAZING experience. It syncs flawlessly with the action and fits perfectly. (11/10)
Character and enemies design: Perfect transition from the classic to HD graphics age. Also gotta love the protagonist interactions. You'll see. (10/10)
Replay Value: High. Harder difficulties and different game modes give a lot of content. (10/10)
Multiplayer: I would describe it as... "Modernized". The only point falling short. It's fun, but doesn't feel right; and also **** the emotes. (5/10)

You really are getting your money worth buying this game, and more.

This is a true game, and a must play if you love action and exploration.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately this game isn't for me. Which is a pity because, as many have pointed out, it looks real pretty and the combat is super fun.

But I couldn't really click with it because out of the hour or so I spent playing it I was wandering around cleared out maps looking for various collectibles and secrets for more than half the time. None of them being super hard to find, but there's enough of them that it still puts a good 10-15 minute break of wandering between combat. While these are 'optional' objectives the UI heavily reminds you of them, even providing you with a handy checklist on your map that will constantly bug anyone who hates seeing unchecked checklists. On top of this, these secrets and collectibles are also entirely linked to the upgrade system. So if you wanna make your shotgun go even more boom... best spend 10-15 minutes between combat looking for random .

Plus there's the codex, which I read a few entries off. It's there and it's... fine. It would also be fine if it wasn't there and it would be more than fine if the little prompt to hit tab and read to codex whenever you get a new page (approx. 10 pages every step you take) didn't show up. It'd be super if the dumb little yellow exclamation mark didn't appear in the menu when you still have unread codex entries. Few games do wordy codex's well. It requires great writing, something genuinely interest to write about, and of course needs to enhance the gameplay experience instead of detracting from it. Personally I don't think pausing to read a million mediocre pages of codex entries adds anything good to DOOM.

Having said all that, I'm clearly in the minority so if you love fast, brutal, gunplay interspersed with wandering around to clear a map of secrets and such then this game might be for you. Alternatively if you're able to ignore the urge to go collecting them all and instead power on through the story then you'll probably have a good time.\t\t\t\t\t
