\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRun. Shoot. Kill. Repeat.

Seriously that's the review. Right there.


Since I'm a maroon who can't make a serious review, I'll at least run some specs by you, if your curious about how the hardware handled it.

For reference, I ran the game at Ultra everything at 1080p, using Vulkan.

i5-4690k @ Stock:

All cores maxed, even while being able to sustain the 3.9GHz boost clock, not a particulary horrible bottleneck, but it is there. Probably would do better with more cores/higher clockspeed

GTX980 w/ 100MHz Factory Overclock:

Absolutely hammered the GPU, with it always pegged at it's max boost, never dropping utilization below 96% at all times.
However VRAM utilization at 1080p doesn't seem to be too bad, averaging about 3GBs usage.

Can't speak for the RAM usage, (because I'm too lazy to go back and monitor it).

Overall managed 120-130 FPS, with dips into 80 in intense scenes. Curiously enough, cutscenes seem to be locked at 60FPS, even though they are rendered in-game.

!NOTES!:

Vulkan did cause some issues for me, causing minor artifacting. I switched to OpenGL to test, and no anomalies happened. So perhaps try Vulkan, but probably stick to OpenGL for the time being.

Also 65 GB on disk! Sorry DOOM, but as soon as I'm done with the campaign, you're going to the mass storage HDD...


Overall pleased with the game. Don't buy for multiplayer, but don't avoid it like the plague either, it's really not as bad as they say.
