\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's short but sweet as hell. The gameplay is great and fast pased, focused on moving and dodging rather than sticking to cover (though that plays a small role too).
Each level is pretty big with lots of exploration available and lots of collectibles and power-ups to be found.
The story is simple with a "plot twist" that can be seen a mile away, demons are on mars and you have to kill them.
Regular playthroughs take between 9-14 hours, depending on if you go for all the collectibles or just spend some time exploring.
There isn't a whole lot of variety as you'll end up using the same collection of guns in every playthrough since they are clearly supperior to the other options, but they're fun to use and it's stil 4-8 weapons you can jugle between.
Multiplayer exists and all the DLC is multiplayer content.
If the multiplayer doesn't interest you, you might want to wait for a sale before buying it, $30 seems a fair price for the single player due to its short length. It was a blast to play through and I had a lot of fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis might be just me, but I found the game to be rather boring. This is my first Doom game so I wasn't entirely sure what to expect. The lack of story makes the whole game feel pointless. For this reason I couldn’t bring myself to push through this game. It’s pretty much: run into this room, kill all the demons, run into next room, kill all the demons, get keycard to progress to the next area where all you do is kill more demons again. The save system is absolutely horrible.

Now that I’m done ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ all over this game, I will admit I had fun at points playing this game. The combat is pretty fluid and enjoyable for the most part. The platforming was also quite satisfying.

Those two pro’s however were not enough to make me want to go back to this game. I wouldn't recommend this game unless you just wanna do some mindless shooting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game left a lasting impression on me. I have been replaying it again on a higher difficulty after finishing on the medium one. The reason this game shines so much I think is due to several factors:

- Introduces a combat style that I've never experienced in an FPS before. It forces an adrenaline rush. It's fundamentally different from how most other FPS games are played, trying to hide or staying far away from any harm. Even though it's classified as an FPS, it changes the formula so much IMO, it really can't be compared to other modern FPS games. The glory kill system and its related incentives is a game changer.

- Amazing multi layered level design. While the game is sort of linear, it still gives you a lot of freedom to explore different parts of the level. I like how vertical the levels are, with the map showing a complex maze of multi story paths. Levels vary a lot in their aesthetics and form. And some levels look beautiful. When I entered Titan's Realm, I just stared at the surroundings with a sense of awe. I really wish they had gone further with that concept though since it gives way to more standard level design very quickly.

- The guns! DOOM has an amazing line up of weapons. Each gun feels very different from the other. And over time you start realizing that some guns work better in different situations. They are a lot of fun to use, and I just love the overall feel and kick that you get from shooting a badass weapon.

- Fantastic upgrade system. Upgrades in DOOM are not just more damage or more powerful guns. They add to the existing functionality in very meaningful ways, that can really make a difference in the harder battles or boss fights. Eventually everyone gravitates towards certain upgrades more than others, and that's part of the fun. Personalizing your demon slaying experience with a great variety of guns and upgrades.

- The upgrade system also has interesting achievements which once accomplished give an extra level of functionality to your weapon. Each weapon has a separate achievement. This makes it interesting to adopt a certain play style while playing levels and adds some fun variety to the demon slaying.

- The concept of runes. This is quite innovative for a game like Doom. These are mini challenges that will give Doom slayer interesting powers. These challenges vary quite a bit in difficulty and were pretty fun (and sometimes frustrating) to complete.

- Interesting secrets. The fact that they went through the pain of including the original Doom levels as part of the secrets shows the level of polish and detail this game has received. You can play the full levels from the main menu. It adds a nostalgic twist while playing.

- Ending credits were kick ass. Really some of the best I've seen.

- I personally found the lore and story interesting, much more so compared to the original Doom games. It's not too intrusive, but there is a coherent thread of events that tie the whole game play together. There is a lot of stuff to read if you are into that kind of stuff and appreciate the details.

- Snapmap is a really interesting part of the game. I've tried a few. Some are fun diversions and others are full levels. They vary in quality. You will never run out of variety in terms of re-playability. I haven't tried designing a level yet but that's something I want to do get my creative juices flowing.

- Arcade mode is another nice but a very meaningful gameplay mode. It makes it a score/skill focused shooter, where focus is on chaining kills, with multiple skill points and medals. It's quick to get into, and forces you to improve every time. There are no secrets or exploration required. I really enjoy it when I just want some mindless Demon slaying good time.

- I haven't even touched the multiplayer. From what I've heard, its now really good as well.

- Sound fits the mood of the battles. Though I personally found it be not as good as the original Doom game since it is mostly heavy metal elements, without really a solid melody in most instances. They sort of sounded very similar to me. But it does the job, which is to get you super amped up.

And I must mention, the game looks gorgeous. With various graphics settings set to high or ultra, the mosaic of a very atmospheric color palette, amazing light effects, fluid and fun animations, detailed demon models and their actions and so on makes the whole world come alive. I found it quite immersive. If you can play it above 60 fps, I highly recommend it. This is one game that does justice to 144hz, even in singleplayer.

Id software have really hit the ball out of the park. The attention to both depth and breadth is staggering. They could have left out a lot of elements without sacrificing the basic gameplay, but they didn't. It makes sure every type of player will find something to enjoy. And it has almost infinite replayablilty with the Arcade mode, Snapmaps and Multiplayer (It includes bots in multiplayer in case you were wondering)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM (2016) is a reboot of the original DOOM made in 1993 by id Software, and is, in my opinion, one of the best first person shooters we've seen in modern years. Just like in the original Doom, you take control of an unknown soldier sent to dispatch the hordes of hell with a bunch cool guns. That's essentially the plot to the game, which isn't all that important here. The main thing that sells Doom is the gameplay. This game has non-stop action with fast paced combat, tons of different enemy types and some rather big levels to explore and find secrets. The game has some of the best level design I've seen in a first person shooter since games like Halo 3. On the more technical side of things, the game is a marvel of graphics and technology. It is one of the best looking games out there right now and it can do it on some less powerful machines. The game is incredibly optimized and the new Vulkan graphics API boosts that even further, allowing it to run on machines below the minimum requirements. Sure you might not get all the best resolutions or even achieve 60 fps all the time on one of those, but it is a great achievement for the game. Some downsides to the game I can see are its lacking multiplayer and limited snapmap tool. Snapmap can be great if used properly, but there only a few really good levels and only 12 demons are allowed to be alive at one time. The multiplayer is quite lackluster feeling more like a call of duty shooter rather than a classic deathmatch game like Doom, Quake or Unreal. The singleplayer is the key part of the game and is worth every penny of the 60 dollar price tag (or even as low as 20 if you find it on sale). One thing that some more experienced players might find as a bit of a downpoint, is that the game is just too easy for some (even on nightmare) as the game throws you a lot of upgrades by the end. But to them I say, play Ultra-Nightmare for the hardest setting plus Permadeath if you're that much of a masochist. The game also has the an absolutely kick-ass soundtrack to accompany your slaying of demons (go listen to Rip and Tear for a taste) which actually makes the gameplay feel even better.

9.7/10-some cuck on the internet\t\t\t\t\t
