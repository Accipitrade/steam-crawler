\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMultiplayer: Haven't touched it, won't claim to know whether or not it's improved from people's impressions during the beta.

Snapmap: Far be it from me to object to a developer giving players the tools to make their own maps and levels, especially after Starcraft and Mario Maker became things. Potential is there for some really fun maps, and I'll definitely be checking on it from time to time to see what people pull off with it. Would love to see a good co-op survival map (picturing something like the Firefight mode from Halo ODST and Reach; this game would be even better for that sort of mode).

Campaign: I could gush about this for a long time. After you hit Start Campaign you're on your feet and shooting demons in less than a minute. The gore is present in exactly the amount you would expect from Doom, and the combat puts focus exactly where it needs to be: on mobility and always staying on the offensive. Need to not get hit? Keep running, as cover will quickly get overwhelmed. Need ammo? Grab your chainsaw and slice someone in half, the bullets/shells/energy cells you need will spill out of them. Need health? It ain't gonna regenerate, so snatch some pickups from the room you're in --- or better yet, stagger an enemy and use a melee finisher, the health will gush right out of them. Doomguy is on permanent sprint, so you'll have no problem covering ground quickly.

Game looks beautiful, even if you're not trying to push the upper limits with them. Story is present and engaging but takes a backseat to the action; very few cutscenes, which stop you for a short as possible before getting out of the way again. The music...well, it says something that I'm playing a shooter and feel no need to mute the game and have my own metal playlist going; some great tracks in this game that fit the action to a T. Levels are are all pretty big and a bit maze-like at times, though designed such that the most obvious path is often the one that will progress you forward. If, however, you go off the beaten path and use the map there are all sorts of secrets: armor pickups, upgrades to Doomguy's weapons and abilities, various unlockables, all sorts of rewarding goodies to be had. Between the motivation to find secrets you missed and mutlitple difficulty settings (from fairly easy all the way up to a perma-death variation of Nightmare mode) there's plenty of replay value here.

So, recommendations. Played the first two Dooms back in the 90's and loved them? Get this game, they nailed it again. New to the franchise? Get this game, it's as fun as they come and a real change of pace from most shooters these days.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am currently resisting a huge urge to just write a one liner review like "Doom is back" or "This is doom" or "All you haters, DONT go to hell" But theres enough of those, so ill try to give some substance

Ill start with how faithful the game is to its roots.Its astounding, period. The gameplay feels like doom 1 and 2, the exploration is back, the powerups, the teleporters, the arenas and weapons, no reloading, hell yes, this is doom. I never once felt anything but challenged, a huge grin on my face as the next hell knight fell to my super shotgun. strafing and double jumping my way out of the energy balls flung from cacodemons. This is where the game pays homage best. And when you arent filling mars and hell with pools of gore, you are exploring to find upgrades that actually make you FEEL more deadly rather than being "+4% speed, you move barely noticeably faster" The gameplay is solid and wonderful on almost all accounts, however since i need to not be biased in review there are things i will point out specifically, good bad and neither, in the order that they come to me because im lazy and dont care to categorize with my currently shaking hands

-The early weapons (Assault rifle, shotgun, and plasma rifle) are basically utterly useless once you have their upgraded versions (At least on my difficulty level, which was one under the hardest, ultra violence?) I would occasionally pull out the assault rifle to humor myself in the later half of the game (Where all the former bosses now make up 90% of the enemies) but there was no doubt they were clearly INFERIOR to the better versions (Chaingun, for instance) theres no "They have their purpose" argument to be made here, the grenade upgrade to your shotgun doesnt even match the damage of the unupgraded super shotgun, the missile upgrade to the assault rifle doesnt match the DPS of the normal chaingun and wastes more ammo.

-The game has no "Pity" system, many games give you a "hey, idiot, you are about to die" grace period to go and hide or whatever, this game does not, i love it. You can feel like the biggest badass on mars and turn to run into a cacodemon shot at high health and instantly die, no pity, no sorry, no mercy, this is how doom should be.

-Onto the "Story" yes, i know, doom+story=? but they got this one right, you can play through the whole game with no exploration and get a pretty darn good idea what is going on and why you are killing things, but explore some and you will find a rich and cultivated universe that takes place hundreds of years after the original doom (Not doom 3, i mean DOOM) Somehow they tied a pixel game with weird mixes of guns and no story up well with this game that has a well defined universe and a great-but-easy-for-doomguy-to-keep-up-with story. (Im talking a full multiple pages for each monster beastiary, a gun database, tons of content for weridos like me who take breaks from killing demons by reading about the guns im using to do so)

-This game doesnt just pay homage to doom, mind you, the super popular and free "Brutal doom" mod has equal parts in this games pedigree, from ripping monsters apart for health to a powerup that lets you decapitate a hell knight with your bare hands, brutal doom was not only acknowledged, but totally included.

-Doomguy is epic, just epic, no other man could carry more reverence than B.J blaskovich himself while not ever saying a word.

-The game pays homage to alot of amazing boss battles in the DOOM canon, except for my and many others favorite... Ill bet that DLC comes to give us that back (However I must note the "Season pass" ONLY mentions multiplayer, there may be no hope for me after all unless theres a sequel) SPOILER The icon of sin was only mentioned and his skull is part of a level. you cant battle the behemoth in this game, can battle spider mastermind and cyberdemon though!

-The games ending and alot of the content that feels vaugely not-all-there probably suggests the games story will continue in a sequel. Remember how the "Sherlock holmes" movies opened up with a badass first film, that oddly was designed to set up the next one to be even better? thats kinda how this feels. The game was great and complete but felt like "Just the beginning" My bet is that a "Wolfenstien, the old blood" type standalone game comes out (That way since its standalone they dont have to include it in the season pass, see) That gives a little more substance and background to this game before they begin working on a sequel.

all in all, This is a great game whether you are a hardcore doom fan who sleeps with a cacodemon plushie or a gamer who missed out on that generation but wanna feel the nostalgia with modern graphics. this is the doom you have been waiting for.

Yea, it was a ranty, awful review, my hands ache and shake from all the strafing and jumping in the constant never ending combat this game offered me. When a game leaves you grinning and thirsty for more, its good, simple as that.

EDIT: MULTIPLAYER

The multiplayer is fun, but not nearly as well made as the singleplayer, the matchmaking system is atrocious about matching top level players with level 1-3 players, and most modes heavily rely on "Demon runes" that let one player become a monster that racks in points, and often these are the sole decider of each victory. While the runes are fun and playing as a demon is badass, the fact they impact gameplay so much and depend so heavily on one players ability to use them (Cuz dying with one drops it for the other team to steal) makes them a bigger nuisance than feature. All in all however, the multiplayer is entertaining and mostly twitch based, which is how doom should be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you loved Doom 2 and like playing the crazy survival-fests that are like the zdaemon MP spin off for doom 1&2 like I have. Then you can rest assured that this is the new DOOM for you :P DOOM is too cool for school this time around. It's just like "DOOM 2" but with improvements. I will recommend this to anyone.

The game comes packed with secrets, old school style, plus they added collectibles and upgrades which are well done IMO. The upgrades arn't like other games where they make you stronger....they just increase your chances at surviving, but that means nothing if you dont got the skillz.

Snap map is a great game mode to play on weekends. It's twisted creations from xbox one/ps4/Pc players, and it is fun stuff. Creating a map is easy, and its easy to create a GOOD map. That is important to mention.

There is other stuff too that would sell you on this game! but i dont want to give any spoilers!

Multiplayer is decent. It's a step up from the beta 100%. Movement and gunplay have been improved and it shows.

Trust the 'Squid on this one ;)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to love this game and it has some very positive aspects, but mostly, it is disappointing!

I played Doom 1 & 2 when I was young, back in 1992 when they were released. For many years, me and my friends enjoyed playing this on LAN and had a bunch of fun with it.

Despite the fact that doom follows the concept of a fast paced FPS, I think that games have evolved during the last 23 years and that you have to offer more content to make a great game.

Look for example Wolfenstein the new order - there you will also have to shoot like every second on some enemies, but there is a great story behind it, which makes the game interessting.

Even in Doom 3 you had some stroy parts, that made the game less boring. Here, you run like crazy and just shoot what ever comes in front of your weapon... I mean, in 1992 it was cool, but is it still funny in 2016? For a couple of minute, or maybe an hour, yes - but for more, no...

I'm not impressed!\t\t\t\t\t
