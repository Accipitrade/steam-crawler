\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn many ways, the 2016 incarnation of DOOM is the perfect shooter. It's got a great premise (you shoot demons in the face, what more could one want), it's fast-paced but not frenetic, it has a great atmosphere, and gameplay designed purely to make you want to shoot even more demons in the face. Level design is very solid, weapon types are numerous and varied, enemy types are numerous and varied, enemy AI is actually sensible, the soundtrack is wicked, and the game runs smoothly af. There are also tons of upgrades in several forms (weapon and armour upgrades and runes that provide extra perks), which allow you to tailor the gameplay to your style (to some degree), making it even more fun to shoot demons in the face.

In terms of gameplay structure, luckily there were only 3-or-so boss fights in the game, which is fine by me because boss fights are often underwhelming. And, to be fair, they were a little underwhelming here too - much less of a challenge than arena fights with swarms of lesser demons. And this brings me to my favourite aspect - the arena fights. SO [REDACTED] FUN!!! They're extremely dynamic, forcing you to run and jump around trying not to get surrounded, then getting surrounded anyway and shooting faces in every direction - left, right, above and below. And they're perfectly balanced with ammo, health and armour pick-ups, so that they generally require a real effort, but are never frustrating.

My only real complaint with this game is the depth of the story and character design. Or, rather, the shallowness. And yeah, I know I know, it's DOOM, what do you expect - you shoot demons in the face and that's it. And yes, the game is really good at what it is. BUT, because of the excuse plot I lacked an emotional connection to it, which some great shooters do provide without a detrimental effect to combat (e.g. BioShock Infinite, Wolfenstein: TNO, or from what I've heard - Mass Effect). With a more engaging story it could be not just the perfect shooter, but the perfect game.\t\t\t\t\t
