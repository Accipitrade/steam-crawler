\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I'll rate DOOM 2016 10/10, Campaign-wise, at least.
I've been playing this since a few days after it first came out back in 2016.
And Let me tell you, the campaign is magnificent.

The soundtrack is spot-on, inter-changing in certain spots/places,
Where I guess you could say, the places that have less demons, have usually a very quiet tone.
And when you're Ripping and Tearing through demonic guts and obtaining your health or armor, whatever it may be,
The music really decides to kick in. It really flows.
Especially when it changes between when you're Ripping and Tearing or just by yourself as the DOOM Slayer, whether it be pressing a button, extracting vega, or something else.

The gameplay is outstanding.
Keeping the flow of the gameplay through Glory Kills, a couple second-timed animation to give you a second to breathe, without slowing down the pace, then continuing your rampage in a slaughtering Demons. Glory Kills also give you health, so that adds in a little bit of resource management (not as much as DOOM Eternal, though). Choosing the weapons you want to rip and tear the demons with, is really good, though it doesn't require you to use certain guns to pressure you.
They've kept the speed of the gameplay around the same as the originals.
They've also given argent energy packages to permanently increase your health, ammo, or armor, as well as weapon upgrades and mods that you can change at any time on any weapon.
A map as well, that you can use to explore to find secrets or runes.
What the great thing is about the map, weapon upgrading, etc., is that you do it on your own time, instead of on the game's time.

The story is self-explanatory, Rip and Tear demonic threats, stop Olivia Pierce, and prevent the invasion of hell from continuing on Mars.
If you want to learn more about a specific enemy, character, map or secret, all of that information is contained within the intriguingly-written codex.

As for the multiplayer, well, let's say that's just not as good, as it was expected to be.
It reminded/gave me vibes of Halo 5: Guardians, with the armor customization & design of the armors available for you, as well as containing loadouts, instead of all of the weapons given to you when you spawn, I just thought it was.. not as good.

Now let's move onto snapmap.
You can create your own custom maps, play others' maps, which contains maps such as classic throwbacks, snapmaps with DOOM Eternal mechanics, or just custom maps made that could (possibly) interest you. Add in specific songs to your map that are contained within Mick Gordon's DOOM 2016 soundtrack
The variety of creation is NOT EVEN close to limited.\t\t\t\t\t
