\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start this review off by saying first, I really hate when a developer does a reboot of a series with the same name as the original. Doom is starting off with negative points already because of that in my mind, but don't you worry folks, it mostly goes well from here.

Doom is a shooter. A really good shooter. It's the kind of shooter where you don't have to get sexually involved with chest high cover. I'm not an older gamer, I'm 23 at the time of review, and I grew up mostly playing Call of Duty and Halo. These games frustrated me to no end sometimes because of all the running, hiding, and waiting I had to do. I understand that games are trying to be more realistic and such, but why have games focused on being more realistic instead of being more fun? Doom threw realism out the window in favor of fun, and for this alone, Doom has gotten far better reviews than what most people thought it would get before the release. id Software focused entirely on making a shooter, then making it fun to play. You are always on the move, always changing directions, changing plans, changing weapons, it keeps your mind engaged during the fights so all you have to focus on is killing as efficiently as you can, and never once do you think, "I better get behind this wall and sit for a bit otherwise I'll die, lose 5 minutes of my time, and run through crap that I know is easy as ♥♥♥♥ but AI aim is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ perfect and I hate it! Oh ♥♥♥♥, a grenade. I'll just throw tha- Oh ♥♥♥♥ another one. I'll just move over here an- HOLY ♥♥♥♥ STOP THE GRENADES!" Things like this aren't fun to deal with. They're frustrating. Realism is frustrating. The reason video games exist is because the real world is frustrating and people who play games want to get away from that. So a video game that focuses on throwing out frustrating ideas and replacing them with high octane action, such as Doom, are very welcome on the gaming front.

I don't have rose-tinted glasses from the original Doom and Doom 2, I didn't play them growing up. I played through some of the original Doom when it released with Doom 3 BFG edition, but I never really got into it. I enjoyed it, but at the time, I was sprinting through life in an energy drink caused haze, still certain that graphics were what made games great and had zero concept of fun gameplay. I also played a lot of MOBAs which I think is a serious mental disorder now, something along the lines of self-hatred. I also didn't like Doom 3, which was infuriating because it couldn't decide whether it wanted to be a horror game or an action game, because it kept trying to do both, just poorly. It's hard to feel afraid when you're packing super weapons in your butt pocket, but it kept trying to scare me with constant dark hallways and sudden monster encounters. So none of these games are affecting my opinion of the new Doom.

I've already said that Doom is good. I'll say it again and again because there is very little of Doom that I don't like. The multiplayer is better than Call of Duty, but that's not saying much in my opinion. I want multiplayer more like Unreal Tournament, so it's hard to compare loadouts with only 2 guns allowed to UT, even if the movement speed is cranked way up the way I like it. Fast movement means it's harder to hit your targets, which means more skill is required on both the movement part and the shooting part. Move quickly and unpredictably to avoid taking damage, and aim better and faster to deal more damage. Simple as that. Still didn't care for the multiplayer too much though, so that's another negative, but I'm not factoring that into my review very harshly at all, it's not that big of a deal.

The story is decent and not too intrusive, there are a couple points where it forces you to stand around waiting for annoying voices to talk when you just want to shoot, but it's not too bad. I keep getting reminded of the slow walking from other games which I hate too because your movespeed is a little faster than the NPC you're following and you're forced to just keep tapping the forward button in the most annoying manner. So it's better than that. When fights end, you are given whatever time you want to either go hunting for secrets around the map or go on to your next fight, the pacing is well done. Fights are exciting, and the not fight moments are important to give you something to compare the adrenaline rush to and scream with joy after extremely tense fights if that's what you feel like doing. The only thing I disagree with is the glory kills, but at lower difficulties it adds to the kill crazy fun, and at higher difficulties, a glory kill can very easily get you killed, as monsters have intelligent AI that I am almost certain will time their power attacks to hit you just as you come out of invulnerability but before you hoover up the health drops from whatever you just killed. So they're not a great idea to go for at harder difficulties which keeps the pace going during fights. I don't like scripted take down animations, I don't like things that take control from me for any amount of time, but this kind of compromise feels acceptable, it pleases people that like it by having it in the game and it pleases people like me by making it extremely dangerous to pull one off in the middle of a fight with demons surrounding you.

Finally, the graphics. Usually when a modern game is incredibly good, there's some textures that show that the priorities were on the gameplay instead of graphics. Doom throws that idea out and instead has some of the most detailed environments I've seen. There are extremely few moments when I looked at something and noticed some details looking blurry or pixelated.

So Doom is fun. It looks great. It plays great. I enjoyed almost every minute I have played so far, and I'm 120 hours in. This is the easiest recommend I've ever given.

Also, there's snapmap, a map building thing, which tools to add in AI, objects, text, monsters, whatever. It has mars base and hell rooms that you can use to create your own maps that can be played by up to 4 players I believe, but it's not the same thing as the campaign, because once again, 2 weapon slots. I really hate that weapon slots are a thing, just leave a bunch of ♥♥♥♥ lying around and let us pick it all up, variety makes for a better game. Still, another tiny issue compared to the extreme fun of the entire game. I give Doom 3 thumbs up. I'd give 4, but I can't find another dead guy that has an arm still attatched that I can tear off to use.\t\t\t\t\t

Constant movement, faster movement, lots of demons and the guns I remember with some twists. Honestly had trouble setting this game aside which is very unusual for me.

The only negatives for me would be the secrets, enhancement system and the bosses. A have no problem with secrets and challenges but they are tied to the upgrades for weapons and armor. They were like bosses from other games, huge health, small arena with attack patterns to figure out. Anyway, small quibbles really, if you like the old doom games, the campaign does a good job of bringing back that style of play.\t\t\t\t\t

Doom Is What It WAs Meant To Be

Game-play: This has a smoothness that is very stylistically Doom. Always moving, always dodging, and there are zen moments when it all comes together. The Glory Kills(flashing Enemy signals a Fancy Finish) gets you health and sometimes ammo. It took me a bit of time to include the Glory Kills as part of the rhythm – and not something separate. When I did, it added to the game.

Levels & Design: There was a good mix of inside/outside environments (without the ice planet/lava planet tropes.) Not all sections were corridors and a lot of it made sense. The story was told well and you got a good sense of the main human protagonists and the hell-gate itself. A lot of what they disclose about the Doom Marine made me interested in his story for the first time. With no dialogue, he finally had a distinct aura but, fortunately, no actual personality.
The Monsters are excellent re-works of some very basic types that we are used to and had lasting power through the game
Multi-player – didn’t pay much attention to it. Nothing extraordinary like Quake 3 Arena. I did like the Packages that they offer to give you distinct play-styles you can choose from.

Weapons: The original DOOM had great set of weapons to start with. And from that they refined them to the maximum. The upgrading system gave lots of options and extended the basic weapons far into the game. But there is probably too much fiddling about early in the game with that stuff and that detracted from the smoothness. There’s always a worry that you haven’t chosen correctly. Fortunately, late in the game, your options are simplified by not wasting time on weapon upgrades and you get back to the basics of DOOM.

Graphics: All good with excellent art on the environments and environmental effects. Top-notch miasma, that’s for sure. The monsters always had interesting things that they would do. And you could get the unique sense of the netherworlds - Bestest Hell evaar! Taxing on older machines; that smooth weaving movement is the dividing line between a good and not-good-enough machine.

Rating: I tend to divide things into two categories: Worthwhile and Not Worthwhile

Overall, with my current graphics set-up, a very worthwhile buy that creates a lot of the great moments that are very memorable. Good story - told well. Classic motifs, fun secrets to search for (sometimes too much) and a memorable game.

Where it’s not worthwhile: If your machine has not aged well, it will limit the experience a lot. I had a much older machine that I was going to upgrade anyway(8 years old – 4GB RAM Ati HD7870) and had to give it up after 10 minutes each session. You can trade off a bit by reducing the screen size and reduce effects. The key would be how smooth the weaving is.

Recommended as Worthwhile
