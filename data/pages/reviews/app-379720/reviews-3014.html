I really enjoy the brutal killings, the weapons and upgrades, the general theme of the game is fitting since you are supposed to be in HELL. Thus far I highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"They are ruthless. Without mercy. But you, you will be much worse. Rip and tear, until it is done."

DOOM 2016 pretty much captures what a reboot should be on the dot. Not the same game from several years ago, but instead its own, new game that still embodies the same things as the original. At its core is an incredibly fast and visceral shooter about recklessly blowing through the legions of Hell, and yet DOOM 2016 adds several new elements that bring the gameplay 'up-to-date' such as the vastly expanded verticality (as well as bringing lots of vertical mobility along with it) and Weapon Mods, which means the already expansive weapon library gets even more expansive with how different many of the mods are from eachother.

The levels are wholesome, with plenty of challenging encounters, particularly crafty secrets, lots of world to explore, and good attention to detail that pulls everything together. The game's intense industrial-metal soundtrack is phenomenal, being put together by the critically-acclaimed artist Mick Gordon, famous for his work on Killer Instinct Seasons 1 and 2 as well as the Wolfenstein reboot series.

My biggest complaint with the DOOM reboot is that the multiplayer is very underwhelming. It's playable, but it could be so, so much more if id themselves had worked on it instead of outsourcing it, as well as if it didn't have game-changing content locked behind a paywall.\t\t\t\t\t
