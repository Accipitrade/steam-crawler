Great game, but...
It saddens me to say that weapon/body impact sound effects and any attempt at a gibbing system in DOOM completely pale in comparison to their Brutal Doom counterparts. You'll know what I mean with a quick YouTube comparison. With that aside, however, this game is truly a flawless modernization of the classics' gameplay style, with combat upgrades to boost its replay value (two lines per weapon & five suit categories).

It's frantic
The demons will chase you no matter what, making constant movement a must. But they vary greatly in their HP, speed, effective lethal range and movement and attack patterns. There are wall climbing imps, charging “pinkies”, jetpacking skeletons (revenants) and sluggish mancubus, the ultimate bullet sponges. You will have prioritize your targets wisely to avoid getting overrun.

Samuel Hayden is awesome
The story is typical: demons show up, you kill them. What sets it apart from previous Doom games is the introduction of three characters other than Doomguy. Two of these are typical genre staples; the central lab helper AI, which doesn't seem to have a sentience, and the evil scientist who is inexplicably hell-bent on achieving total world destruction. The third character is what facilitates the game's dialogues (or more like monologues) and creates a somewhat entertaining character dynamic, because he's neither good nor evil. He is a stoic and morally-grey character who simply wants to do the most sensible thing, stirring as little dirt in the puddle as possible. He will be your guide through the game and you can expect to hear his voice transmissions on a lot of levels.

Final remarks
The aesthetic design is modern but incorporates a lot of elements from the original Dooms, which I really loved. While a few classic demons are missing in this version, I think that the new bosses are very well made and can be quite adrenaline-pumping to fight against.

If you enjoy Quake-likes, get this game.\t\t\t\t\t
