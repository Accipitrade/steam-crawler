\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is my favorite video game franchise of all time. The impact it's left on the gaming industry cannot be understated. Video games would not exist as they do today without DOOM.

DOOM (2016) is arguably the second best in the series. It's got a fantastic atmosphere, fast-paced combat, excellent music, and is incredibly well optimized. It's just, fun. Fun in a way video games of the same genre struggle to be.

With that being said, it has two major shortcomings: repetition and identity. DOOM (2016) isn't entirely sure what it is. It wants to be the reboot of a beloved franchise that hasn't had a major installment since 2004, but it also wants to comply with modern design principles and aesthetic so it's accessible to a broader audience. You can see this everywhere, from the upgrade systems, to the narrative, and especially to the production design of the entire game. Everything UAC related has a very generic, sci-fi/future humanity vibe to it that can work in a vacuum, but in a DOOM game feels, off. The weapons feel like cobbled together bits of future tech, and the environments are mostly just shiny highways or dusty mars surface without anything interesting going on. Fortunately, this is somewhat remedied by the insane amount of gore spread throughout levels. It's honestly hilarious how incredibly violent some of the rooms and hallways can look, like something out of a Dead Space game.

My second major issue is repetition. The first 1/2 to 2/3 of the game super work for me. The combat is engaging, the story is very simple but compelling enough that you want to keep pushing forward, and the environments are pretty generic but there's enough verticality and alternate routes that keep things interesting. I vividly recall being shocked the first time playing when we're teleported to Hell and have to fight our way out. Then, you get back to Mars, to an area you've already explored. And for the rest of the game you're either running down a gore-filled hallway or some underground, hellish crypt where each room blends into the next. All the enemy types and weapons have already been introduced, and none of the upgrades are meaningful enough to keep things interesting. The experience is somewhat saved by the introduction of bosses, which can be incredibly hit-or-miss in the FPS genre. Fortunately, all three of them absolutely hit, with the Hell Guards being a standout.

All in all, DOOM (2016)a good game, which is held back from being great due to an identity crisis and repetitive gameplay in the latter half. Fortunately, DOOM Eternal came out 4 years later and completely knocked it out of the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ park.\t\t\t\t\t
