Multi-player

I played 20 hours of the open beta, and now after release, for the most part, multiplayer is about the same. And for being an arena shooter (Quake, Unreal Tournament, the old Doom games), it's not nearly as enjoyable as those games. I love the customizing, except you only see other's customizing in the match making, when someone kill you, and the three best players of the match, so it's not very visible in the game.

There doesn't seem to be a free for all mode, where everyone just kills just everyone, no duel mode, you start with two pre-selected weapons and a tool (grenade, or other thing), and you can only pick up one weapon in-game (the Gauss), if you switch away from this, you drop it. There should have been modes with the old kind of weapons all over the maps, and you have to run around to pick them up. I also miss knock-back, so people are moved by being hit like in the Quake games, now the weapons feels like they have no impact, but you see damage numbers, so you know they do damage. It's all very 'meh' in my opinion.

And from what I can see, the DLCs will be new maps, weapons, a new demon and hack modules (and some other visual stuff), so you will get a split community with those that have these things, and those that doesn't have them. I don't think this is a good idea.

But that's multiplayer. Single-player is completely different.

Campaign

Here the game really shines. The single-player is worth the purchase price on its own. This is not a game with a deep story, with lots of interaction with characters in the game, it's not an open world. But not all games need to have these things, and to be great, it certainly does not need it.

What it is, is an action game with fantastic gun-play and enemies. The music is fantastic, I wish they sold this separately so I could buy it and play it outside of the game. When the action starts and the musics starts going as well, the adrenaline is rushing and everything is good.

The way you upgrade and modify the weapons is great, two mods for almost all weapons, so you basically get even more weapons, and I think a lot of people will find a way to use any gun in a way that they like. There are so many ways to play this due to this modification system. On top of this, if you keep glory killing (finishing the demons with melee kills when they're ready for it), you can keep getting health and armor, and if you use the chain gun, you get more ammo than you can use as well.

There are also some secrets on the maps, but you have a map you can use to keep track of where you are, where you have and haven't been, and it also find secrets (for the most part) for you, but it's not what the game is about, it's an additional thing you can do if you want to.

The graphics look amazing. The maps are so beautiful, you have great amazing vistas to look at, I have over 300 screenshots after one play through of the game. The weapons look great, they have great details, and the demons and effects are great too, though some of the design of the enemies are a bit weird (Pinky, Cacodemon), but they all play great, which is more important than the looks.

SnapMap

Oh, and SnapMap looks fun too, I've only done the tutorials and played a few of other people's maps, and there can be some great stuff there. There are some nice things now (played a map called parkour for over an hour, only one run, but hey, it was fun), but in time, more things will come, I'm sure. If you want to build maps and kinda make game modes that the game doesn't have, go ahead and try it, you will have fun.

Conclusion?

This is a very well made action single-player. Great fantastic action, go play it. I hope for a Doom 2, I want more of the story, and more action.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter spending time in multiplayer and beating the campaign I think I can give a well-rounded recommendation for this game. WOW, this is everything that doom has been culminating to. I have not had this much multiplayer fun since Quake 2 and 3 and I also find myself replaying campaign levels to find hidden items. Each and every level of the campaign fun and every fight is a struggle (kinda dark soulish feeling sometimes, trial and error). Victory is a breath of fresh air and rewarding when you go to the next part of the level/map. I recommend if you play this game DO NOT PLAY IT WITH A CONTROLLER!!! I found myself getting killed all the time because the controller cannot react fast enough to everything coming at you. I tried an XBOX One and Steam controller, neither helped me. In the end I resorted to my keyboard and mouse to get me through the very brutal fights. The glory kills, weapons wheel/slow mo add to the fast pace and the no reloading is such a relief and keeps you from getting distracted during the high tempo fights for survival. Plus, the multiplayer makes me reminisce of high school and LAN matches for Quake 2 and 3. Yes it’s that fun and intense and a very bloody good time. Becoming the demon is a great advantage and not too overpowered. My only complaint is the glory kills during multiplayer, you are vulnerable during the sequence which usually ends up with you dying in the middle of the kill. I cannot wait for DOOM 2 Hell on Earth.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, cut to the chase, I'd give it 8.5/10.

Campaign
Modern elements like multiple equipment upgrade systems are embedded into this old school shooter, giving players a "practicle objective" to play it through.And the weapon modding system refreshes the gameplay which varies your butchering fashion.
However, the level design is traditional arena-style rush with sparse story. Sure, the entire experience feels smooth and badass, yet it lacks variety and is stuffed with repetition after a while.

Multiplayer
Personally I find this really entertaining. Fresh elements like demon possession is genuinly intriguing, and even the set game modes(say, Domination) which is commonly referenced by other muptiplayer shooters have been tweaked. And yes, it's as fast and ferocious as campaign.

Snapmap
Think of this as the in-game editor of Portal 2. It's easy-to-use and intuitive, letting you finish a map within hours. From what I've seen in the community, players can be rather creative with it albeit the functionality is constrained while compared to professional editor like Unreal or Hammer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you remember Doom I and Doom II? Do you remember fighting endless hoards of teleporting demons in "colosseum style" battles? Well I first played Doom when I was 6 years old in 1994 and have since replayed it again and again, as well as Doom 3...

Well this version of Doom had me on the edge of my seat. I loaded it up and finished the game on ultra violence and lets just say that the action and nostalgia worked great together. There were many aspects of the game that remained "classic", such as certain weapons, tunes and monsters (all modified to a degree).

The music really had me captivated. Each level had its own ambient sound as well as combat music, each one progressing the previous, based on the overall theme of the zone - Hell had some choirs, for example. The composer, Mick Gordon, did a great job in combining what sounded like, Nine Inch Nails, Slipknot, Disturbed, (old) Korn and many other 90's and early 2000's metal/industrial bands' sound.

Opposite of Doom 3, this was no survival horror with a hint of action. This version of Doom replicates the feeling of the original two; a run and gun shooter with immense gore and over the top action. In every level there are stages in this game where there are tons of powerups and ammunition strategically placed all around an enclosed area, sometimes with objectives to complete. The music "drops" and "kicks in" and you're literally swamped by stages of progressively more difficult monsters and you're constantly on the move, jumping from pillar to ledge and underpass, always looking left, right, backwards and forwards.

I highly reccomend this game to anyone looking for a modern version of the past. It has a story similar to all the rest, hell on Mars, but this story in particular is very easy to follow and the mission objectives are not intrusive; the story will tell itself, and the cutscenes, premonitions and little bits of story are brief and did not bother me at all. Overall, in my opinion, this game was very fast paced, well-thought-out, full of action and the story was simple, cut and dry.

9/10 - - My only con is that the replayability may not be high unless you haven't beat it on a more difficult setting. I don't play these kinds of games on Multiplayer but the experience of the Single Player is well worth it.
