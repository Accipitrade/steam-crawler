From there it looked extremely promising, and it is. Personally, I grew up on traditional FPS games and what I loved about those games is that they were hard and unforgiving; having large rooms and corridors filled to the brim with enemies, pick-ups. Although I've never actually played the original DOOM games in their natural state, I did enjoy them via other means. I guess I'll call this DOOM 4 since this would be very redundant. Skipping school for this game was worth it, I really enjoy this game. I've put almost 7 hours in already on the "Hurt me plenty" diffculty and it's challenging to an extent.

-Gun-play is fast and hard hitting, although can get repetitious.
-Several upgrade trees (?) and tiers allow you to tailor Doom Guy to your play style, more or less.
-Demons are true to the first DOOM games with the original cast along with some additions from DOOM 3.
-Glory-kills are fun to watch, add a little bit of an edge with minor health drops and they can be toggled on or off.
-Environments vary depending on size whether you're in the UAC base or Hell.

Overall I enjoy the Hell out of this game, it has good replayability and a lot of nods and fedora-tips to the originals. This game may not revive the traditional shooter genre, but atleast it does something Call of Duty might not rip off.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 is a fantastic reboot that somehow lives up to the insane expectations set by the legacy of the series. A game that is a celebration of the DOOM brand as a whole first and foremost, it proves that classic game design meshes with modern technology very, very well. Let's put this game into context:

DOOM 2016 is a game that has no reloading, no ADS, no sprinting and instead a fast base movement speed, no regenerating health, it allows you to carry all of your weapons at once, gives the player powerful movement in the form of double jump and mantling, adds tons of off-the-beat secrets, proudly shows off bright armor suit pickups and floating power ups, showcases keycard hunting and a wide range of enemy variety (more than just fighting slight variants of soldiers - a rarity among contemporary FPS) and to top it off, a classic 90s rocket launcher that isn't a cumbersome RPG-7. At the time, it couldn't be further from the status quo, and thank the lord for that.

Just like DOOM 1993, DOOM II and 64, this game is about fast movement. There's no cover to speak of. Dodging enemy attacks and staying on the move is key to survival and the design philosophy of push-forward combat is made to force the player into an aggressive playstyle. Although DOOM Eternal accomplishes this idea much better, your primary source of resources are the enemies themselves. Are you at low health? The next enemy will drop a bundle for you. Are you low on ammo? Use the chainsaw for a confetti of all ammo types. Armor acquisition is restricted to pickups only, but the basic health+ammo combo is enough to fulfill the purpose of push-forward combat. Being aggressive is rewarded, and playing defensively is discouraged as pickups are scarce. Yes, glory kills are neat and the weapon mods are an incredible addition, but push-forward design is DOOM 2016's triumphant innovation that ensures most players are playing the fun way.

Besides that, the level design and combat is top notch. In the levels, there's a certain amount of nonlinearity and an emphasis on exploration the first three games became well known for, and the combat arenas take a huge amount of inspiration from Quake 3. id has always been the leaders in level design and weapon feel so that shouldn't be much of a surprise, but it was so refreshing to play 2016 when it came out. The aesthetics are a healthy mix of DOOM 3 and the classic titles, depicting a lot of dark industrial UAC facilities on mars mixed with glorious bloodsoaked landscapes in hell, the lore is surprisingly extensive and the codex a joy to read through. The soundtrack is at this point iconic... and, well, you get the idea.

With this entry, id Software rightfully reclaimed their throne as kings of the genre and revitalized a legendary, landmark IP for new and old audiences alike. Once again they have shown the world how to make a proper videogame... and miraculously, they managed to outdo 2016 by a leap with Eternal.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is after having finished the game on "Hurt Me Plenty", in 10 hours and with around 70-80% collectables/secrets found. This review won't contain spoilers.

Tl;dr: Is it worth it? Yes, it's a solid FPS and a worthy DOOM title. The gunplay is amazing, the sound design is beautiful, the levels are big and filled with secrets. Haven't touched MP and SnapMap yet, so no opinions on that yet.

So, after the extreme blunder that the Open Beta was, I went into the full game extremely skeptical, but came out very satisfied.
SINGLEPLAYER: The singleplayer is amazing. The gunplay is solid and the weapons feel as though they have actual weight, especially the Super Shotgun. All the weapons are good in every single situation, except for the Pistol which is a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ piece of ♥♥♥♥ and you only use it for the first 5 mins (though it might be better in higher difficulties, gonna update review once I finish Nightmare diff). The levels feel well thought out, not too linear and not a lot of backtracking happening. Of course, there's the usual secrets, easter eggs, collectables etc.
STORY: Lmao, who gives a ♥♥♥♥? Not gonna spoil or anything, but it's like Romero said, story in a video game is like story in a porn movie, you expect it to be there, but it doesn't really matter.
PERFORMANCE: I got a old ass pos PC that coughs everytime I boot it up (not really, but it's pretty old). Point is, I ran the game on max rez, 120 fov, but lowest possible settings except for textures (left them on high) and I got solid 50-70 fps in 99% of the areas, exception being one small part where I hit 15-20 fps for like half a minute while I passed through an area with no enemies. So yeah, great performances.
OTHER: Sounds and music are 10/10. No need to say anything else on them.

In conclusion, I don't know. Do I recommend it? The review has a thumbs up, so I guess I do. Should YOU buy it? Idk fam, your money... but yeah, go ahead, buy it. I pre-ordered it and I'm very satisfied with it. And to be completly honest, I might even put a few extra cash for a season pass if I were to get more SP content, maybe an expansion like Doom 3 had, that might work.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGuess it says a lot about me if I don't feel an ounce of sympathy for the demons DoomGuy brutally murders, yet feel absolutely horrible for those robots he punches in the face after they give him gun upgrades :(

For those of you who are kind of pulled one way and the other because of the reviews of the multiplayer beta: don't be alarmed. The campaign is definitely everything you asked for: the speed (definitely fast on 120 pov), the guns, the demons. All checked off, plus a few bonuses:

-The environments are GORGEOUS, even with medium settings (yes, my rig is modest).
-Secrets that get you looking all over the damn map, and are fun to look for
-The difficulty of Ultra-Violence (almost) matches Doom II's difficulty on the same level; safe to say, you will die a lot.

I do have nitpicks, of course. The upgrade system is definitely a bit cumbersome; I want cool guns, but having to pull off tricks to get their upgrades feels bleh. No, game, I really don't want to pull off 4 sweep-the-leg Glory Kills on ONE specific demon. Glory Kills in general, I'm very neutral about. The rune trials are weird and out of place, and I do feel a bit bleh about replaying one map twice (the second time, it's destroyed, which is dumb).

Still. They are nitpicks of a colossal, fast, brutal game that I can easily see myself sinking in ungodly amounts of time. I have yet to sink my teeth into Snapmap and Multiplayer outside of beta, but if it's the campaign you're here for, pick this one up. It's a worthy successor to the grandaddy of fps.\t\t\t\t\t
