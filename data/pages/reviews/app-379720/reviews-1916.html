DOOM:

Is an FPS game about brutally killing demons, it was originally a 1993 classic game that was first released for The MS-DOS and then it was ported for Sega 32X, Atari Jaguar, and Mac OS in 1994, and in the next year it was ported for The SNES and The PSX/PlayStation 1, and many other ports came after them.

The Gameplay:

You have a gun, and shoot it at the demons, that's for the basic gameplay introduction.

There's a little bit much more to it than that, there's a mechanic if you damaged a demon enough, you can insta-kill him, and you'll see a brutal kill, this mechanic is actually useful, it's not just because it looks better than just shooting him, one of the things where you can use this mechanic is for movement, it moves you to that demon closer once you're able to do it, so it's useful if you damaged a demon from the high ground, you'll be able to go to him, and I think that you become immune to damage while you're in the brutal kill animation, but be careful because once it's over, you have to keep on moving.

You can also find and upgrade weapons, upgrade your character, who is known as Doom Slayer or Doomguy.

You can find some runes around each level, if you find one and go to it, it'll take you to some sort of a challenge room, if you complete it, you get a perk, for e.g, one of runes increases the distance to insta-kill a stunned demon.

Also it has some story, but the story is about average in my opinion, though keep in mind that most people play DOOM for the gameplay and not for the story, this game isn't a game you should buy for just the story.

This game has something really bad about it, which is the amount of bugs on it, especially game-breaking bugs, I have a screenshot where I just jumped and got stuck inside an unreachable place, which made me respawn on a long wave of demons, what a waste of time it was.


The level design:

To be honest, I don't like the levels' visuals, a lot of them look the same or forgettable, and it's obvious to know where is the next wave of demons are going to spawn once you find it.

There's some platforming when there are no demons or exploring, exploring in this game is just grabbing your hands, you can open up your mini-map and you'll see the where to explore, it's not on your own, so you have to keep up on opening the mini-map or radar.

The difficulty:

I could've played this game on the hardest unlocked difficulty, but I went for the 2nd hardest unlocked difficulty just because it had a tutorial, and didn't wanna miss a single thing, most people play on the normal difficulty, and for playing the 2nd hardest difficulty, it's not that hard at all, maybe you'll die once or twice at a boss or a new enemy, but only probably once, once you easily figure out the way they work.

I've died twice on the final boss, that's how easy it is if you're an FPS experienced player.

I recommend for all FPS experienced players to play on the harder difficulties, otherwise it'll be too easy and boring.

My thoughts:

There's something really fun about this game, which is the guns, it's so satisfying to use and aim, this game could possibly be good for small-practicing an FPS game, you have your projectiles, hit-scans, and of course a sniper rifle.

And this game also has a multiplayer, though it has low players, it's really fun, it's kind of like Quake, it's a fast-paced arena shooter, you can have your own loadouts, and what demon you want to be once you collect the demon power up.

It's fun, but it really isn't competitive, the demons are straight out OP and not fun to face against, especially if you're playing with people that doesn't communicate.

Rating:

9/10

I would give it higher if it wasn't for the bugs, but other than that and if you're lucky enough to not get any game-breaking bugs, this game is really worth it, especially during sales.

It's worth it for the campaign mode alone, and the multiplayer mode is also fun, so try it if you want to.

I'm interested on seeing what they would do with Doom Eternal, which is coming soon as of typing this review.

Slay those demons.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is, bar none, the best FPS game of all time. For now. With that out of the way, let's see if we can't figure out why exactly.

Doom, preceded really only by Wolfenstein 3D, was the original. The Original. It's the one that truly made First Person Shooter a real thing. Since then, the genre has been massively improved of course, through a whole lot of amazing classics like Quake, Duke3D, Blood, Shadow Warrior, Half-Life series, and so on. Along the way, they sometimes lost something. Burned on the pyre of "realism" went high speed mobility, massive arsenals and ludicrous enemy counts, etc. Higher graphics demands turned a dozen on-screen monsters into three or four. Moving roughly the speed of sound went to the wayside of slow slogs, chest high walls, cover shooting, low weapon choices and so on.

Occasionally we had throwbacks, claiming to be 'old-school' like Serious Sam and such, but for the absolute very most part they weren't. Horde shooters where the gameplay primarily consists of holding the mousekey down while backpedaling isn't 'classic'.

Doom2016... is old-school.

It's not like the original, or like Doom 2. It's not like Blood, Duke or Shadow Warrior, etc. No no, it's not like Half-Life or Quake either.

This is... this is New Old School.

Doom2016 manages to capture the spirit of old-school first person shooters, without sacrificing anything in terms of modern improvements to the genre. You have a massively beefy and incredibly meaty and satisfying arsenal of weapons. You have joyous fast and high mobility. You have an absolutely ungodly amount of incredibly satisfying enemies to fight. You have huge, expansive and gloriously beautiful levels to explore and fight in.

The core gameplay loop is the simplest thing. Advance. Kill everything. Repeat. And it never gets old.

Doom was always the original and the classic. It was the absolute height of the genre, as it spawned it. This? Doom2016? This is the absolute peak of the genre right here and now, until something comes along to surpass it. Four damn years down the line, nothing else has.

Doom Eternal is coming. Get ready for it.

Rip and Tear. Until it is DONE!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing. A campaign that is exact opposite of what Doom 3 was, and is more in line with classic Doom games. This is everything a reboot should be. From the Glory Kill system, to the weapons, and the Doom Slayer himself, this game encapsulates everything that made the old games amazing and modernizes it. There is no sprinting, for that would get in the way of the combat if you had to lower your weapon (Something the newer Halo games could learn from). Instead you move very quickly by default. It feels amazing to dodge and weave between your enemies' projectiles and close in for that Glory Kill on a Hell Knight you weakened. The story is there if you want it, but it's not the focus. The Doom Slayer doesn't care why it happened, he's only there to kill demons. The unlock system rewards performance and finding secrets in the levels. There are also optional challenges to complete for extra progression as well. With all the updates, this game has also become very content rich. Snap Map is okay, but very basic compared to something like Forge from Halo 5, but more accessible, unlike Halo 5's Forge. The single player is where this game shines. However it all falls apart when you get to multiplayer. While it is a serviceable experience, it is so far removed from what made classic Doom multiplayer so fun. There are custom loadouts and the demon powerups, while cool, are overpowered and can make the game feel like you are being destroyed. Or the opposite happens and you are steamrolling the enemy team. There are a few weapons and demons exclusive to the multiplayer and Snap Map. Sadly while a few are cool, most of them were underwhelming. They also made a huge blunder with the dlc. It was all multiplayer focused and they charged for it originally. I played this game on a PS4 originally and got all the trophies/achievements on that version. I also got the season pass thinking they would do an expansion or two for the singleplayer. Instead we were treated with lackluster content for a lackluster multiplayer. The customization in multiplayer and Snap Map is fantastic however. You can change the materials your armor and weapons are made out of and apply patterns and different armor pieces to your character. Id did eventually come back and rebalance the entire multiplayer and even made all the dlc free. They changed the progression system for the better and even added an Arcade mode for singleplayer. This game is well worth it at full price or on a sale. Id has confirmed it won't make the same mistakes they did with the dlc for this game and have confirmed that if Doom Eternal gets dlc, it will be for the singleplayer primarily and the multiplayer looks to be shaping up to be better than what was on offer here. Here's to Doom Eternal. Hope it turns out to be great.\t\t\t\t\t
