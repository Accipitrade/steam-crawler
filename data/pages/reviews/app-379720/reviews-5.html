\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am something of a bony old dinosaur, albeit prematurely at 32; the most modern game I'd played prior to this was Far Cry 4. When I first played Doom's demo (mostly out of curiosity to see if my new PC could run something from the 8th gen) I was a little bit dubious about whether I could hang with the detailed menus, '3D' automap, and the complex visuals, but I'm glad I stuck with it.

Doom is a the type of game I've come to love - something that does a couple of things really, really well, as opposed to trying to do a whole spectrum of things kinda well, like that newfangled GTA V where they had you doing yoga, and the rest. Once you get into the rhythm of the game - always moving, always switching weapons, always glory killing (pressing F to execute a stunned enemy,) something really clicks. You don't think about the combat, you just feel it. You prioritise the worst threats, use the right guns, and move down the hierarchy until you're left with mopping up weak stragglers.

I also love the story. I play a lot of first-person shooters, and I've always felt that they always suffer in terms of their narrative for the disembodied voice in the headset and the audio-logs you're expected to care about, but Doom acknowledges that you're here to rip and tear, and the voice in the headset can go ♥♥♥♥ itself. That being said there still is quite a lot of demonic technobabble about various types of arrays, and wells, and systems, and cores, but it never lasts long before getting back to the action.

I did hit a wall with Doom at one point where I thought that I had exhausted my appetite to jump around and kill demons, but I checked online and found that I was at the arse-end of the penultimate level. To me this speaks to great pacing, if I had that feeling half-way through, I'd have sacked it off, but getting that feeling right at the end of a 10 hour campaign that comprises of little else than room-clearance, well that's impressive.

I think Doom is sorely lacking new game +, I'd love to have started over with what I'd unlocked and unlock the rest, and THEN go again, but you can only select one level at a time when you're replaying. I guess that's a minor distinction, but I really love new game + and this game seems to be a perfect candidate for it!

Mick Gordon's score, I don't need to tell you, it's awesome.

Anyway - very cool game that requires you play very aggressively, does what it does really well, and leaves right when you're getting full. Nice one!\t\t\t\t\t
