Out of ammo? Chainsaw will insta-kill and drop massive amounts.

Even passive abilities called "Runes" can be adjusted to suit the play style. And their presence with additional challenges and weapon upgrades, alongside lots of hidden secrets will keep the flow of the game complex enough to always have multiple goals in mind while progressing through the game.
All while the game never pulls cheap tricks. No slime that slows you down. No screen effects that mess with your vision and aim. No dark areas that hide enemies.
There is only one lengthy monologue that does not allow to progress until finished.

And all of that with simply put one of the best 3D in-game maps. While being a bit inaccurate here and there, it provides all the information required to understand and explore the surroundings. With only a few spots in the game that do not allow to go back for additional secrets.

And outstanding soundtrack. Not simply as a piece of art, but also technologically. As the music not only adapts to the scenario but also to player action. The way how music is played and structured is always different, on any playthrough.

But unfortunately it is also the buggiest AAA game i have played in a while.
3 times did my inputs stop working. With that i could no longer shoot or climb - effectively turning this into a duck hunt, where the Doom Guy is the duck.
2 times did a required event not trigger at the end of a combat arena. In the first case i could locate a hidden bugged Imp hanging from a wall that needed to die. In the second case i had to reset to the earlier checkpoint as no enemy could be found.
And multiple times was i unable to look at extras in the menu. As the 3D models went invisible.

Overall an excellent shooter with some technical drawbacks.\t\t\t\t\t
