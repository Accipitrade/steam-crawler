\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI generally don't write reviews, but this game was so amazing, I had to share my thoughts :)

I am really impressed at the direction ID decided to take with this game. They went back to their roots and made a game that'll, no doubt, be a classic for a long time to come.

The SP campaign throws you straight into action. This really surprised me as I was expecting a cutscene as a filler to begin a story.
Instead, you see the Doom Marine awaken, break a metal cuff binding him to a concrete block, grab a zombie-like demon by the skull and smash its head against the said concrete block.
The Doom Marine then breaks free, picks up a pistol and immediately starts kicking demon-ass!

There's no time wasting i.e. you camp, you die!

The fact that there's no health regen at all is one of the best features about the game. I love the fact that you can't just hide behind an object for a few seconds to gain some HP. If your health is critically low, you better make sure you move fast and dodge every attack you can and preferably perform a Glory Kill to regain some HP.
The Glory Kill system is also absolutely amazing. I think they could have made it a little more gory but a lot of folks would have had issues with too much gore.

The weapons system is also brilliant. Your arsenal isn't there just for show.
The game doesn't force you to use every weapon available, but it's a bad decision if you don't. I switched weapons many times during combat, especially battling larger waves of enemies. Every weapon has a purpose and it's absolutely fun to use all of them.
I like the way they limited the amount of ammo you can carry per weapon as well. It's neither too much nor too little, but you can't run around using only one weapon...you will run out of ammo and often realize the said weapon would have been useful during the same fight against a different enemy.

Boss fights are difficult but not impossible. It's awesome because even on the lower difficulty settings, boss fights aren't a walk-in-the-park. This provides much needed challenges over other FPS games.

The level design is also very well done. It's fun to explore each level to it's fullest and find all the hidden secrets and Classic Doom levels. Besides trying to be a completionist, you're rewarded for exploration which is useful because you can upgrade your Praetor Suit and arsenal with the points you gain from exploring. Besides, sometimes it's a welcome mechanic if you need to take a break from all the monster-slaying you'll be doing :)
I appreciate the way they put Doom 2016's monsters into the 1993 version's levels. It gives the Classic version a refreshing feel.

ID deserves all the positive praise they're getting from this game. They did a fantastic job breathing life back into a long-overdue game we all loved!
The graphics, animations, art, level detail, direction and gameplay is really worth the money spent!

Well done ID! 10/10!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst Impressions:
(Yes I was around playing the first floppy disk doom.)

This is an interesting game, It sorta feels like a blend between Doom 3 and the originals...In a good way.
The awkward to read maps are there, the cool weapons and nice looking monsters are there.

For me currently the only thing that is dragging this down is that the world isn't feeling alive; more like every monster is waiting for Doom Guy to arrive in the next area (Sorta DMC or Bayonetta style.) Kill them and search then move on. The original Doom games had a "Level" Feel and exploring them was a treat!
My MAJOR criticism is that the music is just nothing!! Remember the original Doom soundtrack? Based on Pantera and gnarly music. This game just has some ambient BS and some teasing of the original music. TIP just play the original soundtrack in the background or some metal of your choice and turn the in game music to 0 (Now it's better isn't it?)

All in all 6.5/10 currently. A fun game for 12 hours but isn't going to win any awards. Hardcore Doom fans should pick it up just for the nostalgia. New fans just get the original 2 games if you want to enjoy a great FPS.

Edit-
After making it a little further this is a decent game. Probably a 7.5/10
The levels have become a lot more intricate although the arena style is still an issue. I would say the best way to enjoy this game is in two playthroughs though. The first time go through and just shoot things in the face and then wait until you play it again (which you probably will.) to find all the secrets. Espcially considering some of the areas behind you lock off??? This makes exploring the first time frustrating. Treat this like the first game, plug in .... turn the music up, play and enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t
