Maybe that speaks to the improvements made to the combat systems of Doom Eternal more than it does the level design of Doom but my point is - if you liked Doom Eternal then 2016 is still a worthwhile experience that feels unique from Eternal, just with its own gameplay quirks, enemies, and environments.

Also just to touch on aesthetics - Doom 2016 is actually ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ sick. Sure Eternal has awesome unique environments but there's something that's just so clean and pleasing about the grittier graphics of 2016 that are more grounded in realism than fantasy. This game is much, much more grounded in reality than Eternal is. That game takes the fact it's a game and just runs with it to the fullest extent, and while Doom 2016 isn't exactly realistic or anything all the lore and environments seem real and plausible and the Doom Slayer feels especially badass in this iteration. In fact the Doom 2016 Slayer is my favourite iteration of the character - So much personality is portrayed through his first person actions, the glory kills feel so much more brutal, you walk through hell itself blasting your way through hellspawn as ancient tablets tell of epic tales of your past that exist only to build up your character as unstoppable and feared among even the demons of hell. It's genuinely cool - and even though there is a lot in this game that is so over the top the Slayer is a genuine 100% no joke a badass. In Eternal he's still ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ sick - don't get me wrong, but I feel giving him a face really humanises him too much and compared to the no faced-opaque visor murder machine from 2016 he doesn't nearly feel as cool. That might just be me - but you have to admit there's a layer of over the top cheesiness to Eternal's Slayer. The man has his biceps out. I love that, but something is like, GENUINELY badass about 2016 and it's raw, gritty presentation.

Not to mention the music - OH MY GOD THE MUSIC. This is a purely subjective thing of course but I feel it's more brutal and heavy than that of Eternal's. The electronic elements of Mick Gordon's music are on full display here with stuff like Vega Core and Mastermind. Vega Core specifically is so cool, showing you can perfectly encapsulate the violence and impact of DOOM without a nine string guitar, even though Mick Gordon certainly does. The combination of absurdly low nine-string guitar riffs and busted up electronic crunches and lurches in Flesh and Metal are insanely good and results in my favourite DOOM track, period, across the whole franchise. Ironically the Doom Eternal soundtrack which was intended to double down on the heavy riffs of doom 2016 feels a lot tamer and less absurdly heavy in comparison due to its smaller emphasis on experimental electronic sounds. Nothing in that soundtrack hits as hard as the best tracks of Doom 2016, but of course, that's just an opinion.

Anyway, buy the game. Good game. Even though its sequel is widely regarded as better by many I'd argue Doom 2016 still has a lot to offer for anyone who is interested in Doom. I for one adore it.\t\t\t\t\t
