\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original DOOM was originally never released in my country so I played a little bit of it at my friend's house who owned a pirated copy of the game. I remember being shocked, scared and even repulsed by the amount of blood and gore I saw in a video game and it was too much for me to handle at that moment. I never completed the original game due to this reason.

Fast forward to 2016 when I heard about the remake of the original Doom, my childhood thoughts immediately went towards that distinctive memory. Now I was much prepared for the actual game and I gave it a go. And what a blast it came out to be.

The combat was everything I hoped it would be. Its intense, challenging, fast paced and makes you fire on all four cylinders. The game has a lot of enemy variety and they are extremely hard to kill forcing you to always be on the move and focus on razor sharp fast skills and tactics to level down their numbers. The main character is a total bad-ass and you actually feel his rage and intensity as he goes slaughtering Hell's various minions. Though he is actually silent for the whole game you get to see his motivations through various actions he does throughout the game. He's not a character who beats about the bush, he gets the job done, plain and simple. The plot is also extremely dense and thick for players who are interested in the story and all the characters, enemies, locations and lore are fleshed out through various collectibles scattered all around the various levels. There are tons of secrets hidden so it also scratches that exploration itch of players who wish to do so. Also, you earn points through exploration which can be used to upgrade your never ending arsenal of various weapons as well as your combat suit.

What I really liked about this game was how extremely well optimised it was and how well it ran even on a mid range PC while at the same time running with an amazing visual quality. This is coding at its finest and I really appreciate the time and effort ID Software took to fine tune the game to run on acceptable levels of hardware. This is a perfect example which shows how almost any game can run when properly tweaked by the developers with proper love and care.

One of the best things about this game is the soundtrack and how creatively it blends in with the game's atmosphere and settings. Its a mix of heavy metal, electronic and tempo beats which is absolutely a delight to hear and experience, especially with a good pair of headphones during intense combat sequences. It pushes the player's adrenaline to the extreme up to the point that there is nothing between you and the game. That's how immersive it is.

There is nothing more I can say which is already not been said about this game and this review is already 7 years too late. This is one game I would recommend to buy even at full price for the extra support to the developers. Its fun, addicting and filled with lots of Easter Eggs to the original era of Doom games.

I rate it 10 Lost Souls out of 10.

One small performance tip which helped me play the game much more smoothly than when I originally played the game in 2017 was running the game in "Vulkan" mode and disabling "Razer Chroma keyboard" option in the game settings. Back then when I played the game on a GTX 1070 the game was ran super smooth but I was having a bit of stutter lag when running the game on an RTX 3080. So if you are having issues with this game and if your hardware is not at fault you can try the above settings and I am pretty sure it may help you.

For more details on this issue which I faced, I am posting a video link which helped me so I am putting it here for anyone who may need it-

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u0aRrBSDKR8&list=PL4q6uD2KDczjxbMyRH3eHW5KJsp5R6p2Z&index=2
