\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: Doom Eternal DOES NOT DO THIS GAME JUSTICE. Doom 2016 crafted a perfect high-octane combat experience with its fantastic array of weapons and phenomenal map design, complete with satisfying enemy design and boss battles. Doom Eternal rejigged this formula too much - the magic found in this game is lost in its sequel, not to mention that the story found in the gameplay of each DOES NOT tie together. Eternal is a sequel in lore only, which is a travesty.

==============

"What happened to the glory days of immersive, rewarding, single-player campaigns?" I've been asking myself this question ever since the gaming industry transitioned into MMO and pay-for-access episodic content, leaving gamers like me high and dry.

It seems that id software asked this question too, and their answer is "The genre is alive and well - here's DOOM 2016."
This game scratches every itch I've had since Halo 3. The weapon design and execution is incredible, giving you a true feeling of power and domination, and the gore-saturated battles are juxtaposed perfectly by sprawling level design, exploration rewarded by secrets and achievements, and a driving narrative that compels you forward. Although the game is Hellish, of course, its tone and gratuitous violence gives satirizes its context just enough to contribute a light-hearted element that keeps things fun.

This game is simply perfectly balanced. No regrets, and I haven't even tried multiplayer!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have invested almost 60 hours in this game at the time of writing, and I think I can say that I've fully experienced it.

The Campaign:

The story is intriguing and the information to learn about the world is copious. The gameplay is fantastic, the guns are satisfying to use and there is a good amount of variety to them, the glory kills never get old, the game was always a good amount of difficult: never frustrating but always tense, and the controls are snappy and responsive. My only complaint is that several of the achievements are bugged, and if you get some collectibles at certain times, it will lock you out of them unless you want to start an entirely new campaign. If you're a completionist, tread carefully, or you may be in for a world of pain.

8/10

The Multiplayer:

Once again, the controls are snappy and responsive, and additionally, there is a massive amount of customization available. Much of this customization is walled behind challenges that you have to complete, such as killing a certain number of enemies with a certain weapon, or what have you. All non-cosmetic options will be unlocked by about five hours in, by my guess, so the overall investment to get all the fun toys is not huge.

The multiplayer, despite having team-based modes, is not an amazing team-based game. It suffers from a lack of communication much in the way that many modern shooters do. If, however, you don't care about teamwork and just wanna shoot some people and look cool doing it, the multiplayer is perfect for you.

8/10

tl;dr:

The game is worth the money if you like killing things in lots of fun and gruesome ways, or if you want an interesting story-based campaign mode. If you are a completionist or a team-player when it comes to shooters, you will probably be somewhat disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
