\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is way late cause I finally bought DOOM 2016 and eternal in a bundle, currently going through 2016, and going to do eternal afterwards.

And trust me, I played a ton of DOOM.

I have no idea if Bethesda succeeded or not in making this a DOOM game, but I guess it's a good thing. Graphically it is somewhat of a rehaul of DOOM 3, gameplay wise it's closer to DOOM 1 and 2. I find that mix a bit weird. It's like the game can't decide if it's classic DOOM or a story driven horror survival vibe. I would have preferred a different lighting, but that's just me.

The gory kills are fun, but I played brutal doom so no surprise there. It is quite required to use them cause that's how you regain life and ammo.

Overall it's fun, and for old timers like me there is a classic weapon display in the options which solved the weirdness and finally gave me that DOOM feel.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9/10 FOR EVERYONE ELSE.

11/10 IF YOU LIKE RUN N GUN SHOOTERS AND DEATH METAL/FIGHTING FANTASY HORRORS

I will eventually catch up with modern gaming.

All my mates were going nuts over this and I have to be honest I never got into the first buzz of Doom. I was more into RPGs and the ideas games of that ilk can generate aren't generally dependant on fast and flashy graphics - Doom is/was and the older versions often looked comical and that stopped the ideas (that there was on the plot front) catching on in your mind.

As time has gone on, Doom now has come of age.

I have of course played many modern shooters - this kicks the ears off all of them. The mood of terror and being outgunned doesn't dial down until the last real.

The graphics and animations are mind blowing.

The sound track is totally spot on for the hell glazed, blood gorged spectacle that plays out in front of you.

It is like someone read the intro on the instructions to the antique originals and said "Make it like you are running about in a death metal video." and used modern computer art (and it is art in many ways) .

It is realy quite something on the mood front.

The game play is frantic and at time I would say down right stressful. It is rewarding once you get the combos down and offers variety of attacks building up to 'glory kills' which means getting dangerously close but keeps your character ticking over.

Only downside is that the missions are repetative - but the chaotic unfolding story keeps you hurtling through.

And now I think about it, it does seem to work the hell out of a laptop when running it.

It is not for young or sensitive eyes, but those folk aside, this is really an essential game.\t\t\t\t\t
