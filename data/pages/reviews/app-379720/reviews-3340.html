\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA lot of people are ragging on this game for being a "halo/COD clone". Waaaaaay back before those games even came about, there was DOOM. A game about mega-violence, awesome guns, and blowing stuff and demons up. That is EXACTLY what this game is.
Just 2016-ified.

About 15 hours through the campaign and first off, it's a challenge. And not the "shoot rockets until it falls over dead" kind. More like "shoot rockets at it til it's dead, watch your back, sides, up, and down without stopping" challenge. The bosses require some thinking, familiarize yourself with the area, and always know that if there are buttloads of ammo/health/armor laying around things are about to get real REAL FAST.

And the game is a challenge in terms of difficulty. It's hard, but not give up halfway through hard. It's the kind that is a real challenge but you learn to think on your feet really fast or die and start over.

The soundtrack is AMAZING! In the pre-release videos and such they said they wanted hell to look like a metal album from back in the day. And that's what the music sounds like. It borders on slipknot, tool, and a few other heavy metal groups all rolled into one. The weapons and combat sounds are rich too.
The enemies have character and yet you can identify what monsters are in the room by the sounds they frequently make and it helps build a mental kill list so that baddies have trouble surprising you.

The level design is great too! There's all kinds of places to search, hide behind, exploit, and red barrels in all the worst places. Everybody LOVES red barrels.



Overall, if you liked DOOM 1&2, get this! This game is a long and rewarding thrill ride with lots of things to search for if you're a completionist.





Also, if you see lava, jump in and wait to die. Trust me. It's funny as hell.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere do I begin? I'll be updating this review as I explore more and updates happen.

Campaign:
I completed the Campaign and only had the game crash twice on me from my PC overheating. Wasn't the game's fault. I have a AMD FX8350, 32GB of RAM and a GTX660. With the settings all put on the lowest, the game ran butter smooth at 1366x768. Maybe the game was running at 1440x900, I don't know for sure, but that's what my TV was telling me. The graphics were a bit slow loading, which is why the GTX660 isn't on the minimal requirements, but i'm thankful that the devs didn't lock out systems that didn't meet the listed requirements.

You will be spending a good 7+hrs on the Campaign, even on the easiest difficulty. If you want to learn about the storyline and enjoy the experience, I recommend doing that first. There are a lot of secrets and you have to be pretty thorough to find everything. The game has it's bonus challenges that give you benefits in the game. Don't pass these up as you want these. I did a live stream of each level.

Discovered Glitches:
Enemies can disappear at random, though these are very few, it was only ones that were unresponsive and the game just dumped these out, or respawned them, I'm not sure.
Some objects and surfaces do not have a physical wall and you can pass through them.
If DOOM crashes while Snap Map is saving, the map will become corrupt. Cannot find a way to delete corrupt map.

A possible guess on the pistol. Since it's a infinite energy pistol, why does it discharge bullet shells? My guess is that they were going to have the bullets take ammo like the first game, but decided to scrap it, forgetting to remove the discharged shells.

Added notes.
Multiplayer is similar to Quake, though damage given/taken is much higher. You'll die pretty quick if you're not paying attention. You have to unlock other things as you play, which in turn makes for a more desired attempt to play multiplayer. The snapmap is separate from the multiplayer in terms of customization.

My own issues.
The game has to relaunch itself if you want to play multiplayer or snapmap.

Update.

Build a new rig with AMD Ryzen 7 overclocked to 3.8Ghz, 64Gb DDR4 at 2133Mhz and GTX1070. This game runs at 45FPS at 4K(3840x2160)
This is one of the games I ran in 4K and was stunned at how detailed this game is.

I had swapped the other computer's video card for a GTX1070 as well which brought up the performance to about the same framerate in 4K. The FX8350 is ideal, just don't worry about clockspeeds with the RAM. 1700Mhz vs 2133Mhz is only a 2FPS difference. The only concern with this game is CPU core count with clockspeeds, RAM and graphics are secondary.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf ID software thought that this new "Doom" is a return to it's roots, I'm not so sure there is anyone left at ID that actually remembers the original game. Sure, the automap makes it's return, the 3 color key fetch as well as the existance of secrets (if they show up on your automap both in number and eventual location (with the appropriate automap upgrade of course), how can they be secret), Despite the marketing spin ID software may put on this game, this New Doom is no closer to the original than was Doom 3.

Many people, criticized Doom 3 for how "undoom" like it was. However, I did appreciate the fact that the Doom Universe was now more grounded in reality. Random med kits and weapons caches were replaced with lockers. The System Shock 2 method of story telling through logs and email's was very effective. The Space Marine against the demon horde of the original was more of a decision borne out the lack of effective AI. And the whole three key fetch was simply an obvious way of making the player explore more of the map before exiting.

And yet Not only does Bethesda seem to harken back to the worst aspects of the original game, it decides to make even more head scratching decisions. This new Doom has no basis of reality whatsoever, and as a result, leaves the gamer playing with very little at stake. Some how the space marine gets health by doing Mortal Combat finishing moves on his prey. And ammunition is dropped primarily if you kill a baddie with the chainsaw, (if you have enough fuel to kill him that is). All these little things just draw you out of the absoutely stunning atmosphere that the game tries to create.

At least with Unreal Tournament, there was a commentator that issued a running play by play, and so you felt like you were part of some big televised bloodsport. But in a game where supposedly you are trying to save humankind from destructions, All the gimmicks for weapons upgrades, health and ammo drops (glory kills, Doom Marine dolls, argent cores, etc...) seems just like what they are gimmicks. And confusing ones at that.

I would much rather just play the game through without "gore hives" and "rune challenges". I mean what reality are we based on here? Granted, it's a big enough leap to believe in demon hordes, do we need all this extra fluff taking you out of the moment? And when you are not fighting bad guys, you're busy upgrading everything from you suit, to your weapons to your automap, all with their own "currency". I mean how many different upgrade points can you earn and where do you spend them, it's exhausting. And all this occurring, of course while you are running for your life killing hordes of baddies.....Since when did Supermarket Dash becaome a part of Doom?

And while the designers were busy being "old school" why not just go the full monty and have scores for each hit come bubbling off the corpses and add to a huge running score counter in the corner of the screen where one could earn extra lives.

Doom has always been a "survival" shooter, whether it was against hordes of aliens due to limitations in AI, or more atmospheric as in Doom 3. In the Doom's of past, you bought into the world you were in, there was suspension of disbelief, and that added to the tension and horror of the game, whether you were fighting a couple of demon spawn, or a couple dozen..

This reimagining of Doom is visually stunning and has some absolutely amazing fights in it, There are times where I am just overwhelmed and the sheer beauty and technical brilliance of the game. And that they can get it to look so good on what is basically an entry level Alienware Alpha to boot is incredible. Kudos to the programmers for that.....That is one impressive engine and amazingly efficient at that.

But despite the overall brilliance of the engineering, the game comes off as empty and shallow because it never once stops reminding you that it's a "just a game".......Checkpoint completed.....Weapon power up point earned.....Gore Hive destroyed....ad nauseum.....What a wasted opportunity....\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM makes no attempt to be anything but a fast-paced shooter with one goal: knock-down, drag-out, no-holds-barred chaos. From the jump, it is honest about its intentions and delivers on that promise throughout. DOOM is one of those once-in-a-decade remakes that manages to faithfully preserve the magic of its namesake, while also doing some new tricks.

The progression is well-paced, and I find the difficulty curve to be spot-on. The game introduces new, more powerful enemies, presenting them first as boss battles, but then including them in the normal line-up of spawned creatures as the game moves forward. This adds a continuous and ever-increasing sense of helplessness as enemies that were once considered "bosses" by the player are now just a part of the run-of-the-mill roster.

Enemies themselves are not exactly the most clever specimens of artificial intelligence, but they do pose a serious challenge in numbers. They do not seem to work together so much as they behave as a disjointed arrangement of individuals. I would have liked to see more cooperation between enemies. The variety of enemies is good, and the pacing at which new enemies are introduced keeps the player interested.

Player customization is excellent with many different options available from which to choose. The sheer number of add-ons, enhancements and power-ups that are available means that the player has to very carefully consider how he/she wants to spend those hard-earned upgrade points. Exploration is well-rewarded here as taking a moment to trod down the path less traveled usually yields some sort of upgrade point or secret weapon/collectible.

The game runs smooth-as-butter on my GTX 970 and i7 6700k at ultra settings, 1080p. It stays pegged at a constant 60 FPS without so much as a hiccup (as long as you update your graphics drivers!). The controls (mouse + keyboard) are some of the best I've ever used in a shooter. Hands down. The graphics are absolutely stunning, and iD's implementation of OpenGL is truly a new benchmark for the framework. The upcoming Vulkan API has me even more excited to see how much more frames my little 970 can crank out. This is the best-running, best-looking game I've ever installed on my machine.

I've not played an arena shooter I've enjoyed this much since the original Unreal Tournament launched in 1999. How iD managed to translate this iconic, decades-old series into a modern-day FPS with all of the usual trimmings is a monumental achievement, of which iD should be extremely proud. I could not have imagined in my own mind a better tribute to the original DOOM, the shoes of which can only be filled by a giant, which is this title. Fast as hell. Scary as hell. Cool as hell. Bravo, boys!\t\t\t\t\t
