\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a game about shooting demons, and that's all you really know need to know about it. The thing about Doom is is that it excels at the one thing it sets out to provide to its player. It perfectly blends old school FPS style gameplay with some modern features to create a fast-paced action game with a focus on highly mobile combat that forces the player to be aggressive in order to survive. Outside of combat, each level has a multitude of secrets to be found, some of which comes in the form of collectables, while others as power-ups or extra life/armour/ammo. The story takes a back seat in favour of the gameplay, but it's there for those paying attention and/or are willing to read the various lore entries that you'll collect throughout the campaign.
Doom is a no-nonsense type of game. It knows that you, as player, comes there to shoot demons, and it won't let anything get in between letting you do just that. It gets my highest level of recommendation, as it's easily the best game I've played in years.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 is near perfection in the FPS genre. It takes what made the classic so fun and adds a bunch of modern day details that mix in with the classic DOOM from the 90's perfectly. All the levels are designed so that you can move around the arenas swifly and fluidly while fighting a huge variety of demons. And with variety in demons comes variety in weapons. There are so many ways to annihilate the hordes of demons that "shooting the thing till it's dead" never gets old. Glory kills are extremely satisfying (most of the time). I feel like a couple of the glory kill animations could've been more intense but it's not a big deal at all really. Glory kills persuade you to play more aggresively by rewarding you with more health than you would get from a regular kill, and since you dont auto-heal like in a lot of other FPS games, you'll need those health pick-ups desperately, especially on the higher difficulties. Although I do feel that it would've felt more rewarding if you couldn't glory kill every single demon you fight. Weapon upgrades force you to use all of your arsenal rather than sticking to one or two guns for every encounter because in order to get the final upgrade for your gun you have to complete a certain challange with that gun. However I feel that forcing the player to use certian guns kind of ruins a bit of the freedom that comes with having such a large amount of weapons to use. You may be in a situation where the most practical or fun choice to kill the demon(s) in front of you would require the gauss cannon, but you need to kill demons with your super shotgun in order to fully upgrade it. Overall these are really my only complaints, and they are very nit-picky things. I've played through DOOM 2016 2 times on the PS4 before now, and since it was on steam for only 10 bucks, I couldn't pass up the opportunity to play through again with a mouse and keyboard. After about 14 hours I finnished the single-player story mode for the third time, and it was just as fun as my first playthrough. There is an arcade mode that is pretty fun and a multiplayer mode that's... there. To be fair, the multiplayer mode was average, but it's definetly not the main part of the game. Though this may be an unpopular opinion, I believe that DOOM 2016 is even better than the 90's classic. And it is absolutely worth the full $19.99 it's at now.\t\t\t\t\t
