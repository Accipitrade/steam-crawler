Rip and Tear


DOOM (2016)'s Pros:

*Great graphics
*13 levels that are relatively long
*Best soundtrack ever
* A variety of enemy types
*Gory Executions!
*Fun boss battles
*Armory of BA weapons
* Movement in DOOM feels smooth and responsive
*Paid homage to the classic doom titles by adding retro levels and fan favorite super shotgun
* Character and weapon design
*Searching for secrets (in my opinion)
*Minimalist story; No need to have a story if you are going to instantly kill whoever is talking, a
mindless shooter that's not boring.
*The sound design/effects of the demons
*The robotic voices of Dr. Hayden and Vega

DOOM (2016)'s Cons:

\t*The entire multiplayer (Its dead)
*SnapMaps; they could've used the resources in this gamemode to further enhance the main campaign.
*Campaign becomes a grind and almost a chore doing the same thing over and over again, gameplay
doesn‚Äôt change nor does it improve after the first level.
*Platforming; platforming in DOOM felt off and it's because the single player campaign feels so much like
the original DOOM games that adding platforming and double jumps just doesn‚Äôt give the original
games justice.

DOOM is a great 11 hour game that you can pick up and play for the weekend. I highly recommend that you only play the campaign because the other 2 game modes are a waste of time. But if you want to blow off some steam by ripping and tearing through everything you see, DOOM (2016) is a game for you.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tidk, not really all that fun. Games have this thing called "mastery" or something, you achieve it over time and it makes the game feel good to play. Alongside that, this game has a lot of issues to pair it with. The entire game is just shooting aliens and watching unskippable cutscenes (ofc), but there are points in every damn level where you finish clearing a room and have to backtrack through this labyrinth of a map, trying to figure out which doors you can and cannot open with what keycards you currently have, none of which is easily labeled on the level map you can pull up.
On top of that, there is just way too little ammo in this game. You can find the ammo pickups (specific to each weapon) or you can get RNG drops from enemies when you use a finisher, which you should basically be doing on every single alien to get enough ammo even on the easiest difficulty. I can't imagine how often you'd be using the nerf gun of a pistol if you were playing on a difficulty where the enemies were even greater bullet-sponges...

The momentum is nice, the shooting is ok, the gameplay is a little less action-oriented than something like Half Life, to my surprise, which also isn't really my cup of tea.
