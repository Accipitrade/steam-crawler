\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJudging from the cover art and the first few hours of playing, this game just felt like a demon-themed Halo derivitive. Once I got to hell though I was like, OK, this is more like DOOM. I can even forgive the obvious System Shock 2 ripoff with the holographic flashbacks.

The Bad:

I wasn't too impressed with the tired and boring story. One lesson from the original DOOM, if you can't do something well, don't do it at all. At the "honesty" speech near the beginning of the game, I found myself looking for an option to turn messages like that off, and ended up shouting STFU at my monitor as it played over and over. The story didn't really get any better, either. I had a serious eyeroll when I heard a low, gravelly voice talk about "the DOOMSLAYER".

The levels are also kind of too big. In some respects it's great, but not when you see all the things you could get on the map, and it takes forever to collect everything, being OCD this can make you unhappy if you chose to get everything, and unhappy if you chose not to. Which leads to my next gripe -

I don't like the upgrade system at all because it makes you slog all over the place to get stuff. Making the slog worse, were tons of doors that needed keycards (skulls in Hell). That's one part of the original game would have been pleased to see go.

Lastly, the map. It's otherwise done well and is a necessary part of the game, but there are many cases where it doesn't reflect where you can go and where you can't at all. Bars, barriers - all absent. Anger-inducing because you have to try to get the upgrades, and the levels are so big. And this is made much worse by portals, which don't even show up on the map (last Hell trip, I'm looking at you).

The Good:

While there is some monotony, and some areas are just ugly, many of the environments are absolutely gorgeous. Mars outdoors, the foundry. Beautiful. You got a modern graphics card and a 4K monitor? Yep, this is a good game for that.

Movement is supurb, and the levels take advantage of that making them a pleasure to move around in. You can mantle and climb over things taller than you are. So can your enemies. They crawl over everything to get at you. This completely changes the character of the game from the original DOOM and its many successors, and in a very dramatic and good way... which leads to the next point -

Oh boy does this game not f*ck around. Different from many other FPS's, including the original DOOM, no longer do you move through levels a little disdainfully, staying fairly safe and saving ammo, mowing enemies down with little stress. Oh, no. Right from the beginning, you gotta pay attention. You end up taking every fight seriously, and it IS a fight. You may find yourself swearing a lot.

Conclusion:

Thumbs up. The meat of the game - fighting - is superb. But it could have been better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start off by saying that I may only have 34 hours into DOOM but ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ if it wasn't one of the best made games of this decade. The storyline was excellent, badass, and fulfilling to complete. The music in the game just adds to the hyper-violent and satisifing combat in the game. It has exceeded my expectations tenfold.

Combat is fluid and just amazing. The glory kills are really fun and the weapon selection, while a bit limited, is just enough to make sure that the combat is not repetative. On top of that, the dynamics of every mission change and you get a harder and harder demon horde the further into hell you get. This is the game to get when you just want to go out and kill mindlessly. The music seemingly matches the intensity every level. The music plus the intense gore and adrenaline fueled killing sprees you go on are just so satisifying, it's worth getting the game just for this. Multiplayer leaves something to be desired but that shouldn't be anyone's focus in getting this game.

In total, this game is 9.5/10 and I'd highly recommend this game to anyone and everyone.\t\t\t\t\t

Incredible pacing, excellent feedback on the weapons and intensely satisfying blood drenched kills, great enemy variation, well-written but minimalist storyline that's there for those who want it but can easily be ignored by those who don't much care, exemplary level design. And to top it all off the level editor provided is incredibly powerful and the community hub is full of very well crafted levels made by the fans and even a handful made by the devs themselves that encompass single-player, co-op and competitive multiplayer.

This game's a bargain at any price, you'll be hard pressed to find anything better if you have any kind of love for fast paced, arcadey shooters of ye olden days. I grew up on them, DooM and Duke Nukem 3D were among the first games I ever laid eyes and hands upon nearly 20 years ago now, and this reminded me of all the things I miss so much from the game design of those old shooters. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: I have this game on 3 systems and i played it most on XBOX and PS4, but that should stand testimant to how much I love it.

DOOM has one of the best singleplayer FPS experience's around. In an industry which focuses on K/D and DLC weapon skins it's refreshing to see ID go back to their roots rather than creating another shooter to be forgotten about *Coughcough*RAGE*coughcough*.

GOOD ♥♥♥♥

- Finding keycards is back, though not a dominant gameplay element, still nice to see as the entire obective of the original is finding the bloody things (AND KICKING SOME DEMONIC ASS!)

- Fast, punishing gameplay (if thats what you're into). DOOM and Wolfenstein have always had an attitude, encouraging the player to go all out or to ♥♥♥♥ off back to MS Paint (E.G: "Easy mode? u little baby" and "NIGHTMARE???? *sick thrash metal plays") so playing easy is a breeze but if you really want a challenge then better buckle up your underpants bro, cause nightmare gonna blast ur ass.

- There's seemingly a lack of boss fights, but do not be fooled, within the last area of the game you fight like 5? 4? I don't ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ know man but they were hard, talking Dark souls styled unfair for at least one in particular.

- DOOM 3 had this horrible habit of pretending to be a horror game, this IS NOT SPOOKY, it's a shooter with awesome music and LOTS O' GORE! I mention DOOM 3 because it puts you in the shoes of "mr marine man army guy" with your big stupid grey suit and its all very serious and piss. HOWEVER, DOOM starts of like "AW ♥♥♥♥ BRO DEMONIC INVASION U BEST be TEARING UP SOME ♥♥♥♥♥♥ DEMONS!" and thats awesome!

- Snapmap and Multiplayer for when you beat the game and need more DOOM? Yes plz!

BAD ♥♥♥♥

- The multiplayer hitboxes are hideous, weapons still seem unbalanced, E.G the super shotgun is an unfair mistress! and it doesn't look like theres much in the way of an anticheat going for it so be weary of hackers.

- £12 for a demon, gun and some paint? yer havin a laugh ID considering some devs can create entire worlds and sell them for less, you ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ gonks.

- Hosting snapmap games with your friends seems oddly complicated.

- game load times? make up ur mind, sometimes your quick, other times it takes half an hour to load the main menu.

So that's my two cent's.

I love this game, the singleplayer is incredible but the other content feels lacking in really small ways, still totally worth picking up when you can.\t\t\t\t\t
