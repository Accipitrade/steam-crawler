\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe bottom line is this game will probably win every award there is for a FPS. Outstanding game play, storyline and flow. Outstanding audio, music and special vocal effects. Nice and long campaign, fantastic bang for the buck. (I didn't even try multiplayer yet) Outstanding graphics, artwork and "environments". Weaponry, weapons upgrading, the Praetor Suit thing...well just about everything in this game is awesome. Truly a work of art and technical expertise.

If you like PFS games, if you liked Doom 3 and Quake 4 (both of these were very good for their era), then you should really feel impressed by this iteration of Doom. Like challenges? Then this is it for you. Even the default difficulty can be very challenging for the average, ordinary player in the "big fights". Elite players may not think it so challenging, but there are few of these players actually. Most players are solid in their skills, even though they are not top tier. Whatever level you feel you are at, you will become a lot better player after this game. Because you will forced to improve at fighting while moving continuously, you will probable get better at the standard FPS Deathmatch, like the new COD BO3...seriously.

As the game progresses the fights get tougher. Like the reviews have said, keep moving or die! You simply cannot stay still much at all. Run and gun all the way. Fortunately the game is such that there is a very good balance of increasing challenges and weapon-Praetor Suit upgrades that keep the fighting in balance. You get to choose the nature of every upgrade to suit your style. There are plenty of choices. The last few missions have some very tough final fights, even on the default difficulty. Especially that last two missions. The "boss" demons are challenging enough that many players will probably check out a youtube video to see how they're supposed to play the mission ending.

Then there are the "Rune Challenges". These are not necessary to play the game, but if you succeed in each challenge there is a reward in powerups, weapon enhancements and such that help the player handle the fighting a bit easier.

I honestly believe that the "replayability" of this new Doom iteration is higher than anything I have ever owned. You could try playing at the next higher difficulty than default, or try different Praetor Suit configurations and weapon configurations. Maybe just replay your favorite battles and missions, or even only do the Rune Challenges for a quicky before you go to work. The Rune Challenges are actually hard enough that everything needs to be done just right to succeed.

All in all this is an exceptional fine game. I had zero glitches on the first play through on the default difficulty. The initial release is quite polished, probably more than any other FPS I've ever played...and I've played them all going all the way back to FarCry 1. Many awards await this new Doom.

At the initial $60, it's worth every penny. ANY sale pricing is a steal, litterally. You can't go wrong with this guys.

My comp specs might help in the enjoyment and emersion as I have a very strong computer. Corei7 4790K, 32 GB DDR 3 at 2400 Mhz, EVGA Nvidea GTX 980 SC, Asus Z97-A MB, NEC monitor playing at 1920 x 1200 resolution using the games chosen video recommendations, which it cranked up quite abit. A super nice headset SteelSeries Siberia Elite rounds out the show. The SteelSeries headset sound is absolutely incredible, so my game emersion as far as sound effects go is outstanding. I highly recommend playing this game with a high end headset to take advantage of the incredible work that Id and Bethesda did on the audio in this game.

Thank you Id and Bethesda, great job! This one will go down as one of the very best FPS of all time.

Played some other FPS since this one...STILL feel the same way. What a game !!!\t\t\t\t\t
