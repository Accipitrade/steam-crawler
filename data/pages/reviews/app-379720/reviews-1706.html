\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith Doom Eternal on the way and after going through many of my other FPS games, it was time to go back and finish Doom 2016. I had put it off over the checkpoint system; a PC FPS game should always have the option to save pretty much anytime. F.E.A.R. had checkpoints but also allowed you to save so it worked and I hate the idea of checkpoints only it kept me from playing this till recently.

I'm glad I did as Doom 2016 is an excellent game. As a fan of old school shooters over more modern ones (with exceptions), it scratches that itch for interesting level design, secrets, and lots of running and gunning. Doom Slayer runs a bit slower than old school Doom Guy but he still moves (no garbage stamina meters here) and once you get used to how he jumps and grabs ledges, you'll find his maneuverability a treat.

The guns are all great, except the starting pistol that I barely ever used. They are all so big and chunky you'd swear you could use them as deadly melee weapons. The upgrade system for your armor and weapons sounds obnoxious at first, but they're well done and add some nice options. The chainsaw is even here as a fun option that can one shot almost anything if you have enough fuel, turning enemies into ammo pinatas! Glory kills are satisfying as well and in some of the tougher fights can really help as the enemies spew health you can pick up.

The enemies are callbacks to Doom and Doom2 with decent AI and varied abilities. They jump around, use special attacks, flank you, and otherwise try to make your life miserable. Between the great weapon selection and how fun they are to use and how good the enemies are to fight, the focus of the game; fast combat, is brilliant.

My only real negatives (besides the lack of saving which I still loathe), are the rune trials that take you out of the game and can be highly annoying. They do give nice benefits so they're worth doing, but they are still jarring when you have to load in and out of them and back to the normal level. Some of the challenges for weapon upgrades are also tedious and may require the reload of checkpoints to pull off. Having to glory kill imps in 5 different ways on a single level is just obnoxious.

The problems are very minor here though. On max settings on my rig it ran like a dream. I had zero crashes, lag, or lockups, An old school purist may find issues with this game, but as someone that loves the classics, I highly recommend this one. It's glorious.\t\t\t\t\t
