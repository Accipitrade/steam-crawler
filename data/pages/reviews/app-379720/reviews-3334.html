I only really have 1 complaint about it: music is mostly just... noise, rather than actual metal. + it only plays when you're fighting. You don't get to "enjoy it" while you're exploring and looking for secrets.

If the multiplayer would be like the single player, ther wouldn't be a problem with it. In single player you can carry as many weapons as you want (they have upgrades too), the glory kills reward you with a small amount of HP so it's less risky to do them, while still being super-satisfying.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's get something out of the way right now. Doom has never been about Multiplayer. It has always been the single player experience that has driven the game to it's heights.

While this is the fourth Iteration of Doom, it is not Doom 4. It is not a continuation of the jump scares that were used to great effect in Doom 3. Unfortunately the scene deformations are lacking this time around. So if you were hoping for a corridor shooter, You won't get that here.

What you do get are waves of enemies à la the original Doom 1 and Doom 2. Hordes of them. It's fast. It's frantic. It's a bloody good gorefest. Some people might hate the new Glory Kills, but I find them to be gratifying. All the weapoons have impact. If there is anything to be said about a game with great gunplay this has got to be it.

This is a game that is meant to be enjoyed for the sake of enjoyment. It looks gorgeous on my system running at 2560x1440 all settings maxed out, Motion Blur is turned off, on my GTX 980Ti. At these settings, I get a solid 90fps. 4k is somewhat playable at around 47fps all settings maxed out, but I'd take the higher frame rate any day.

I can appreciate that perhaps some people wouldn't like the metal soundtrack, but you know what. That's what DOOM is. It's Dungeons and Dragons, Heavy Metal, and Grit. It's tough as balls.

As far as the multiplayer...I'm not interested. It doesn't (yet) have Local LAN, so I couldn't care less about playing with Randos.

TL;DR
This game is extremely satisfying. Buy this game for a fantastic Single Player experience. Don't buy it just for the Multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only played the single-player. The atmosphere, graphics and sound are great. The combat is a breath of fresh air - you always have to keep moving and being aggressive. However, non-combat parts of the game ruin the pacing. While the cutscenes sometimes have elements of humour and the protagonist just being annoyed and wanting to get into the fight, those occurances are rare - most of the time it's standard exposition that you'd rather just skip. Using the slow pistol is the best way to clear an area of weak enemies before you enter - because when you enter, stronger enemies will spawn. You have to spend time searching the map for secret areas to get various powerups. I would be okay with this if not for the fact that there is no "New Game Plus" where you start on a harder difficulty but with all the powerups from your previous playthrough. I would also have liked it if there were speedrunning modes (complete the level as fast as possible, with and without 100% kills etc.) built into the game, but cutscenes/scripted fights ruin the fun in that.

This is not a brilliant revival of Doom where the game goes back to its roots. It's very close to Wolfenstein: The New Order in terms of gameplay. It's still very fun throughout the first playthrough, though, and I might come back to it in a year or two to complete it on Nightmare difficulty.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know why my post can not be posted.

Many of you may ask: is this DOOM?
And I will answer: yes.
I still remember that in 2005, when the DOOM 3 finally get released after a one year delay due to some hackers. And then, many people around the world are crying: this is not DOOM!
But it still is DOOM.
DOOM 3 is DOOM. No matter how many people ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ about it's atmosphere comparing to DOOM II or DOOM 95, that's 20 years ago.
DOOM 4 is DOOM. No matter how you may like DOOM 3 or the original DOOMs, that's 10 years and 20 years ago.
Time is changing, you can't expect someone keep inventing something whole new all the time all these years. You should respect all the developers who are trying their best to keep DOOM 4 feel like DOOM but not Half-Life, not Unreal, not Serious Sam, not Medal of Honor, not Call of Duty, not Battlefield, not even Quake (well in my opinion Quake 1 does is more like DOOM in too many aspects).
THIS IS DOOM, our special ass-kicking DOOM. Yes, DOOM, not anything else.
Graphics: 8/10. Not groundbreaking, but very amazing comparing to every previous id Tech 5 games like Wolfenstein, The Evil Within, Rage. Dynamic lights and reflections are what amazes me the most in the game, though they are not all real-time rendered (You can't see yourself in the mirror like what in DOOM 3). Some textures are very highly detailed, I guess they used at least 8192x8192 resolution for those textures, while some others maybe only 2048x2048 and you can tell the difference very easily. Overall the graphics is very good especially in intense combat with a lot of dynamic lights.
Sound & Music: 7/10. Not so amazing, but it's OK. 1 point plus for the metal music during combat. The environment sound may be a little flat, but weapon sound is much better than in DOOM 3. Can't tell much now cause I play it on desktop speaker and haven't tested headphones.
Game Mechanics: 8/10. I like this DOOM mainly due to its monster design. There are various different monsters that each of them have their unique identities, their advantage and weakness, and their own movement and combat style. I think the developers really does take some time to study how the monster should do when you encounter them, and I like their effort. Meanwhile what amazes me most in DOOM 4 is that all the monsters could hurt each other, and in many cases you may find yourself sitting around having nothing to do watching them fighting each other. The monsters do fight each other, and after they finished you can clean up the rest. This is the most fun part in DOOM 4 I think. The combat is very intensive and explosive in higher difficulty, requiring fast moves, precis aiming, smart dodge, knowing of the surroundings, understanding of weapon features and monster characteristics---all in all, you must be highly skilled to get into hell and come back alive, it's not COD, neither hide and seek Battlefield. And yes, it kind of makes me feel like playing Serious Sam, but that's OK.
Story: Just DOOM---Hell happens, you are alive, you kill the demons, you get out of hell. Atmosphere is OK but not match that in DOOM 3.
Multiplayer: Sucks pretty much cause I can't get access to the node server since alpha test. I tried everything but nothing works. Just can't play multi, maybe it's due to my location(China)?
Easter eggs are everywhere and sometimes it's more challenging to find than the combat.
Fallout 4 fails the expectation in mission and story design. But DOOM 4 keeps the DOOM spirit in my opinion.
I will add multiplayer review if I can get to play it in the future.
This is DOOM, stop ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.\t\t\t\t\t
