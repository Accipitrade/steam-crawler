\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game. Plenty of room for improvement.

id has struck gold with this 'resurrected' vision of DOOM. They have found an angle that works while also finding a way to innovate on one of the most stagnant genres in gaming.

[THIS REVIEW IS LONG]

Let's get the cons out of the way first:

Extremely monotonous environments. The game takes us back and forth from Mars to Hell and has very little to offer in terms of set-pieces and environmental diversity. There is little between the industrial grey and beige hallways of the UAC and the red and brown hellscape that is admirable or unique, although it tries to be at times. The layout of the arenas are somewhat lacking as well, as they don't do much to encourage moving around within them. Between the arenas are hallways that lead to more arenas, and these hallways have a demon thrown about here and there. This issue with environments ties into another core issue with the game which is...

It feels just a bit too repetitive. While DOOM's core gameplay loop is extremely satisfying and some of the best the modern FPS genre has to offer, it starts to seem like a one-trick pony at some point through the campaign. You might begin to wonder: "Is this all the game has to offer?" and I would argue you are correct in that observation. DOOM runs out of tricks to throw at you at about halfway through, it simply runs out of content. This issue is made more prominent by the fact that the environments get stale fast. Usually, these epic FPS campaigns keep trying to one-up themselves and introduce new content with each level, whether that be with story, environments, set-pieces, enemies, equipment or challenges. That simply isn't the case here. You're fighting the same enemies, in the same way, in roughly the same areas, and this I think is the key thing that keeps this game from being a true masterpiece in my opinion, while many may already consider it to be.

The progression system is flawed. DOOM offers a decent progression system as the rules of modern gaming mandate. You will be finding weapon mods and suit upgrades throughout the campaign, some of which are great while others are not -and that's the problem. When it comes to weapon mods and upgrades especially, some are far more powerful and useful than others. There are some you should outright avoid and that begs the question as to why they exist in the first place. You can tell id Software struggled with putting this progression system together throughout the games very troubled development. They simply couldn't nail down consistency in terms of quality, and this leads to imbalances. The game can be made very cheesable with the right mods and equipment. The point is, there are boring ways to play this game and the game does nothing to encourage you to play it the correct way, which is to say the fun way.

A disjointed direction. It's no secret that DOOM went through a tough development, at some point requiring a hard reset as the devs simply weren't pleased with what they were making. Although the game changed course for the better, there are still signs of that confusion and indecisiveness. This complaint is more about the overall feel of the game and nothing particularly specific. I guess an example of this would be the way the story elements are delivered on occasion, or how the game sometimes shifts tone. You can feel the trepidation as the devs weren't sure what was going to work and what wasn't.

The multiplayer. While the game's multiplayer mode has managed to garner an audience since release, I have to stress that I don't think it was worth even putting in to begin with. For starters, its development was outsourced to a different company and it was basically built like a classic style arena deathmatch mode with some modern contrivances shoved in, such as loadouts and emotes. The only thing it has to remind you that it's a DOOM game is the fact that you can play as a demon on occasion, but that's it. Otherwise, its Halo with a DOOM coat of paint.

There's an in-depth view of all of the game's flaws, in my opinion at least. The reason I went into such detail with them is because this game is fantastic, it managed to pull off the impossible and I want to see id perfect their vision with DOOM Eternal, which it seems is exactly what they're going to do. I don't want this review to be much longer so now I'm just going to list the reasons why you should definitely play this game if you consider yourself any sort of gamer.

The visuals and graphics are great.

The soundtrack is absolutely phenomenal and iconic.

The core gameplay loop is extremely fun, fast and a breath of fresh air.

There is interesting lore that is optional to read.

The story is second to the gameplay, although what little there is is well-handled.

It makes you feel like a total badass.

It features a snap-map mode, which let's you create your own levels.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying the original Doom way back in the 90s was what got me in trouble as a kid. I mean, my parents didn't want me playing that sort of stuff with all the blood and gore, so I had to play Doom at a friend's house.

Probably the best rebellious act I've ever committed.

My children certainly won't be allowed to play this game until they're "of age", but part of me hopes they'll eventually have a friend who owns this or whatever Doom game is out at the time. (I hope my wife doesn't read this.)

Anyway, this game is without a doubt one of the top reboot titles I've ever played, the best being God of War 2018. Doom 2016 was at the top until then, but it'll always be a phenomenal development and worthy of many replays. I nod in respect to those who spent countless hours working on this game as well as the highly-anticipated Doom: Eternal.

Buy this game. Now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM (2016) is the newest (as of Feb 2019) game for the new DOOM reboot made by id Software and published by Bethesda Softworks. It features an absolutely astoundingly optimized game experience that runs fantastically on Vulkan that is noted to be playable on even the oldest of systems at high framerates, and as a result makes for a pretty great experience ripping and tearing through demons. Take the role of Doomslayer, and fight the demon invasion back to hell. However, this is not enough for the burning blood hungry vicious unstoppable feared weapon you play as, and you decide simply sending the demons back to hell isn't good enough, so the only reasonable thing to physically go to hell yourself, and kill every single demon in hell, and absolutely destroy everything in your way. There is no way you can play this game and not feel completely badass. The music is not only phenomenal, but noted for winning Game Awards 2016 for Best Music/Sound Design. The music is entirely comprised of Mick Gordan's badass heart-pounding guitar work that can be set to the same bar as Steve Vai's work in making Halo 2's Mjolnir Mix Halo Theme song, but, imagine EVERY song was just that badass, or more. It's pretty great. Play with an ever expanding, upgradable and easy to use arsenal of weapons at your disposal to help you kick ass.

Overall this game deserves a solid 9/10. I would highly recommend anybody who has not played this game or does not own it to own this game on Steam. It is a must-have title to acquire.\t\t\t\t\t
