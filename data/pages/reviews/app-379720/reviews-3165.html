\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a fast paced shoot-em-up that offers a large variety of weapons with which to obliterate your enemies. I don't think I've ever played a game that screamed "badass" so much or in so many different ways. The gameplay is fast and smooth, and by combining the weapons, the effects, the enemies, and above all, the music, an incredibly satisfying experience is achieved.
The single-player campaign offers some degree of customization in the shape of weapon mods, distributing upgrades between health, armor, or ammo, general suit upgrades, and runes.
The multiplayer wasn't entirely my cup of tea, but it was still enjoyable. It is very fast paced, and strongly reminds me of great classics along the line of Unreal Tournament.

All in all, I think Doom is well worth it even at full price. If you can catch a sale, and want to have some fun wreaking havok, don't think twice about picking this one up.\t\t\t\t\t

TLDR Version: Perfect reboot of a classic game franchise

Let me clarify, the single player campaign is "Classic Doom" remade and reimagined to run in a modern engine. The game looks beautiful and runs smoothly if you have a powerful enough graphics card.
Id Sofware not only met, but exceeded any expectations you should have for this game. It captures everything great about the original games. The pacing, the intricate level design, the secrets, and the satisfying feel of turning demons into much smaller bits of demons. If you've ever enjoyed Classic Doom, or any '90's era Doom Clones for that matter, you should absolutely pick up this game for the single player campaign alone.

Pros:
  • The level design. Classic Doom all the way. Exploration. Exploration. Exploration. The new Doom (nuDoom?) has intricate level design that hides secrets, blocks off portions of the map with keycards, and has a perfect balance of demons trying to rip you apart while you explore. Running down a hallway you might see a gap or a window that you could easily walk right by, but if you stop and look, you see that hidden through there is an armor power up or a weapon you would not normally get for another few levels. Now you spend 10 minutes trying to figure out how to get to that hidden area.
  • Collectibles. There are two Funko Pop-style doom marine figures hidden in each level to collect, they don't do much besides serve as a neat collectithon sub-game, but they also unlock character models to view in-game. Also hidden to collect are RPG-style upgrades for your suit/guns and hidden trial courses. The Trial courses are small challenge maps that give you something unique to do and then grant a reward if you're successful. Stuff like put you on a small map with a bunch of demons and you have to perform x amount of a certain type of glory kill, or you have 1 health point left try not to die, or kill all these demons using only explosive barrels. Some of the unlocks include runes that let you collect ammo/health drops from further away, initiate glory kills from further away, or fire your weapons without consuming ammo if your shields are over a certain threshold.
  • Combat. This is a fast-paced game. Literally, you move fast. You run through Mars and Hell collecting all the classic Doom weapons, that have all received a nice fresh coat of paint, and graphically mow down Hell Demons. There is no reload mechanic, you run over ammo packs to refuel, you run out of ammo and swap to your next favorite gun and keep chewing through Demons, if you run out of ammo entirely, and you probably will, that's why God invented the chainsaw.
  • The Chainsaw mechanic is interesting. If you run out of ammo in the middle of a fight you can use the chainsaw to cut a demon apart and spawn a bunch of ammo and health packs. Depending on the size of the demon it may use more chainsaw gas. But you can pick up more gas as you find it in the levels.
  • Similarly, there is no taking cover and waiting for health/shields to recharge like most modern shooters. Health packs, and armor pieces dot the level, go run over them to pick them up. And you're actually encouraged to enter the fray and stay in the fray constantly moving, because as demons take significant damage they become staggered, which allows you to perform a "glory kill". Basically you perform a fatality on them which usually involves brutally ripping their head to pieces or beating them to death with their own severed arm and stuff to that effect. BUT, performing glory kills also drops small amounts of health pickups. So as you run through waves of demons it's beneficial to always be moving, shooting, and performing glory kills to keep your health topped off.
  • The Campaign story. While NOT a 40 hour+ epic saga of epicness, IS a fun (and increasingly macabre) story that unfolds as you progress from level to level. For the first time it totally makes sense why you're on Mars, why everything went wrong, and why demons are pouring out of portals to Hell. It even has enough hooks to build a direct sequel that not only shows what happens after the last event in the story but could also expand the origins of the Wraiths, the Doom Slayer, who is the voice you hear controlling the demons of Hell, and the real origin of Vega... it's hinted at that he was just as much found as built.

Cons:
  • Co-op campaign would have been nice?
  • The multiplayer. Doom was never about great multi-player, that's what Quake was for. That being said there are better multiplayer fps experiences out there than what is included in this game. Not to say it's necessarily bad. There are customization options to unlock and there is fun to be had. But you're buying this game for the single player, and the multiplayer is just the gravy on top if you're really in the mood for an ultraviolent deathmatch.
  • Single player campaign could be considered short if you're just looking to speed-run the game. Not a con if you're only looking for about a 10 hour experience, and it will take much longer than that anyway if you're looking for every power-up and secret.

Protip, do yourself a favor and look for all the argent energy caches and use your weapon tokens to fully upgrade a couple weapons. Assault Rifle with the mini-missile launcher attachment pairs nicely with the super shotgun. Plus if you upgrade the assault rifle attachment all the way you can release barrages of mini-missiles without reloading until you exhaust your ammo. Aaaand if you unlock and upgrade the don't spend ammo when you have over a certain amount of armor trial rune, and put argent points into your armor capacity, you basically can just fire these missiles non-stop with impunity for huge chunks of the later levels if you manage to avoid enough damage. Just saying.


One extra thing, not necessarily a pro or a con as I don't think anyone buying this game is under any illusion about it being non-violent. But this game is NOT for the squemish or for very young children. The graphic demon killing is only the tip of the iceberg on this one. There are literally piles of gore and depictions of satanic human sacrifices vividly detailed enough to make a grown man uncomfortable in a few places. It's Doom. Good job Id Software.

I went into this game as a skeptic. I didn't care for Doom 3. The Doom movie is literally the only bad thing the Rock has ever done. But this game's single player campaign is the best single player fps experience in about a decade. If you're a fan of classic Doom buy this immediately. If you're a fan of fps shooters in general buy this game, and if you've only ever played CoD brace yourself for something very different. If you prefer your games slow and stealthy, maybe wait for a sale.

10/10 Would Super Shotgun demons in the face again

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome gunplay, has something resembling a story which is uncharacteristic for a DOOM game and a pretty decent soundtrack Also the gunplay. Did I mention the gunplay? No? The gunplay is awesome. And now that word has no meaning to me. Good job.

But other than that there's nothing else, really. The level design is very corridorish, and you basically go to one mini-arena to another, rinse and repeat. The gunplay carries it, but at the end the upgrade system makes your guns so powerfull, you just blast through everything without a thought. Bosses take 2 minutes and you pretty much only need to use one or two guns to kill them (and that's on Nightmare, the hardest difficulty. It's not that I'm good or anything, it's just that the mastered weapons are way too OP).

There's little bit of exploration with all the classic DOOM maps and secrets, and because you can get an upgrade that pretty much shows all of them on your map it's not too much of a hassle to do, which is nice. Thing is. that the environments have a massive problem of only being made up of two colours, and boy does the colour red get old after 10 hours. Good thing it's only 12-15 hours long (!), 20 if you go all completionist.

Also apparently it has multiplayer. I can't really explain it, but it's really not all that fun. I paid money for this game, why is content behind a leveling gate exactly? What's up with this initially confusing perk system? Meh. There's also SnapMap, which is basically a miniature map editor. It's alright, although time will show how sucessful that is.

Performance wise this game's pretty damn good. It crashes sometimes. Quite a lot after the last patch, but assuming they fix the crashes, the game performs quite well. It's shame that they use Denuvo, because since they're using OpenGL, they could've easily released the game on Linux (the original Multiplayer demo worked flawlessly through Wine). There are a few glitches, but I've seen nothing gamebreaking.

It's an okay game overall, but it's definitely not worth the asking price of €60. If it's on sale, consider it, but full price - nah.\t\t\t\t\t
