\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer: I haven't made much progress in the campaign yet, but I have familiarized myself with everything (including all the weapons and enemies).
Synopsis: You play as Doom Marine -- a generic soldier without an identity. In campaign mode, you blast your way through claustrophobic corridors and industrial arenas on Mars. The enemy? Demons. Your goal? Hell.
In addition to campaign mode, Doom features SnapMap and Multiplayer modes. SnapMap is a very intuitive and flexible level builder. Multiplayer is... multiplayer.

Pros:

1. Optional Story. Doom has always been about blowing demons away. Story and narrative have typically come second (or third), with the exception of Doom 3 (which had quite a bit of story and cut scenes). This latest edition sticks to its roots and focuses primarily on fast-paced gameplay. For players who enjoy an immersive story and lore, don't worry: Doom gives you the option of collecting and consuming information that helps build the overarching narrative.

2. Lightning fast gameplay. This game runs seamlessly on my PC. The framerate held at 60FPS, although the game gives you the option of uncapping it. As far as the actual speed of gameplay, you literally feel like you're floating on air as you zoom through hallways and zip around arenas (You "sprint" by default). Takedown animations are fluid and quick, and everything generally feels super responsive. Oh, and you can double-jump. That’s awesome.

3. Nostalgic. This is truly Doom for the 21st Century. Yes, Doom puritans will have a million little nitpicky issues with this reboot, but let's be honest: The only way they wouldn't complain is if ID repackaged Doom II and sold it to them as a new title (although I'm sure they'd still find something to whine about). Regardless, Doom retains the basic feel of Doom I & II. It somehow manages to capture that original feeling of "gliding" through levels as you discover secret areas, weave through waves of enemies, and desperately hunt for health, ammo pickups, and keycards. Aesthetically, Doom draws inspiration from both Doom II and Doom 3. The enemies look classic and are easily recognizable, but the creepy environments (UAC facilities and Mars) definitely feel inspired by Doom 3.

4. Good level design. Although I'm only on the 3rd level, I already appreciate the level design. It's actually quite brilliant. There's no shortage of secret areas, alternative routes, and strategic vantage points. The maps are intuitive to navigate, but big enough that you will get lost on occasion (and end up going in circles).

5. SnapMap. For players who were disappointed with Doom's multiplayer modes, the SnapMap level builder presents endless possibilities. I'm sure it's only a matter of time before someone creates multiplayer modes + levels that appease even the most puritanical Arena Shooter fanatic. Personally, I'm just stoked for all the user-generated Survival co-op levels (think Killing Floor but Doom).

6. Badass. This game is ultimate power fantasy. Your upgradable suit is bulky and powerful, your guns are giant and penetrating, and your foes are worthy adversaries (with the exception of Possessed Scientists/Workers). Everything in this game is big, fast, and bloody. The violence is over the top. Your weapons can be modded (choose wisely). The soundtrack is the perfect blend of industrial metal and ambient soundscapes. The loneliness of Doom's campaign is offset by your arsenal (and the accompanying upgrades); In Doom, your weapons are your only friends, and they have your back every step of the way.

Cons:

1. Repetitive. Sure, Doom's demons are badass and challenging. But after just a few hours of gameplay, I could already imagine getting bored of killing the same demons using the same takedown animations. Luckily, the diversity of weapons helps mitigate this problem to an extent.

2. Bland multiplayer. It's not that the multiplayer is BAD. It's just that there's a huge disconnect between the campaign and the multiplayer. I mean, sure, you're using the same weapons, but multiplayer just doesn't FEEL like Doom. A common criticism I've heard is that Doom's multiplayer lacks an identity. I couldn't agree more. Luckily, SnapMap will most likely rectify this issue. Personally, I think the developers should have focused on co-op multiplayer modes and less on competitive modes like Deathmatch, etc.

3. No Campaign Co-op. Really disappointed about this one. I mean, sure, the SnapMap will allow for all sorts of co-op type gameplay, but it would have been nice to play through the campaign with a friend on Nightmare. Oh well.

4. Potentially low replay value. Once you've completed the campaign, then what? What if you don't like the conventional multiplayer modes? Well, tough sh!t --- you're stuck with user-generated SnapMap levels. Fortunately, we can expect to see some really amazing user-generated maps and game modes. But will that be enough to incentivize players to stick around for 1-2+ years? I mean, I hope so.

Final thoughts:

I recommend this game to fans of the Doom series, as well as newcomers who are simply enticed by the gameplay footage. What-you-see-is-what-you-get, and there aren't many (if any) shooters out right now that deliver what Doom does. I can't guarantee this will be a title you commit to for years, but the campaign alone is definitely worth it and the SnapMap has quite a bit of potential.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll in all, a great, fast paced shooter that looks good, sounds good, plays great and is just pure, simple fun. There's some great easter eggs for fans of the original, including an unlockable mode of play ;)

I played the original Doom way back in '93 when I was at college. You could only get it as shareware back then so I didn't know anyone else with a copy. Me and my friend pretty much played it to death over and over again. We'd take it in turns, swapping over when we'd finished a level or lost a life.

Doom 2016 has the spirit of that game. The story seems to be the same and the action follows the same pattern, even down to the colour coded keys.

Basically, it's lots of shooting with periods of recovery where you can hunt down ammo and health that you've missed, just like the original.

There's plenty of modern stuff, like weapon upgrades etc... which add depth, and bits of story to piece together. However, the core of the game is still lots of fast paced shooting.

The game runs at 100+ fps on my GTX970 (@1080) with most of the setting tricked out, and it looks great.\t\t\t\t\t
