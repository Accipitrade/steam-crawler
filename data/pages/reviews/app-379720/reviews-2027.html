\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this at release and played it for about 16hrs and got kind of stuck at the cyberdemon boss and quit playing it. I got stuck because I had used all my ammunition to kill him in the first of the two phases, then died in the second phase. When you resume, it doesn't give you enough ammunition, so completing the 2nd phase just wasn't going to happen. Perhaps they fixed that, because a few days ago I decided to re-install it and start a new game. When I got to the cyberdemon, I did die in the 2nd phase and was resurrected with enough ammo to complete the 2nd phase. But just in case, I didn't use any BFG-9000 rounds on phase one.

That is really the only negative thing I have to say. After re-installing it, and going from the beginning again, I am just in awe of the game. I can't imagine a better reboot of the original. Some people don't like the gore nest concept, but I really think it fits well. Basically, you come across these nests and when you kill it, a gazillion demons spawn. These can be quite challenging, especially later in the game, but you will find some power ups placed in the area that really help. Like berserk, quad damage, etc.

I don't care for the runes concept though. Well, as a concept it isn't bad, but I found the challenges to be a bit too much for me. And each time you try an d fail, it takes some time for the game to reload. This is the main reason I quite investing time in these.

The early part of the game you are on mars. The missions aren't terribly hard, and some will say too easy. It can be a bit challenging just to find the things you need to complete the mission. If you miss one dead body with a key card, you will not be able to open doors needed to progress. So you have to go back through the last section you finished to find what you missed. That causes a bit of a break in the action. But if you go into the game knowing that you need to really look around and not rush through, you won't have such a problem.

Towards the later stages of the game, when you get transported to Hell, it is a big change. The difficulty ramps up. You will need to learn to use all your weapons and develop good tactics to do well.

The game really didn't bring me back to the same feeling I had experiencing the original DOOM back in the early 90's.... until later in the game in Hell. Level design and layout is more like in the original, the demons are fantastic reboots of the original, and the artwork and sound are just amazing.

There is a good amount of puzzle solving and parkour in the game. Yeah, back in the day we had to do more than just run-and-gun, and platform style games were the norm at the time. It was nice to see some platform elements in this reboot.

The bottom line is that I would say you will enjoy this if you know and liked the original. If you didn't, then you probably won't be very impressed with this reboot.

Regarding multiplayer, I can't really speak to that. Haven't touched it. From the forums I gather that cheating was a huge problem -- and probably still is -- and that chased most away from the game. So if you really aren't into single player campaign type games, then you might want to research this and be sure it has a multiplayer experience and enough players.\t\t\t\t\t
