\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 is a milestone in the FPS genre, game development, video games as art, and pretty much anything else related to its medium.

The Best Reason to Buy DOOM 2016:

The combat and gameplay are immensely satisfying, challenging, and fun. They have a rhythm and pace that feel completely unique to Doom and in large part define the personality of the game. Navigate doom guy through waves of demons while dodging projectiles, teeth, claws, plasma, poison, and so much else, as he traverses layered maps, each textured with corruption, and backlit with a color template straight from hell. There's nothing quite like it. Making it through requires moment to moment strategic decision making. The game tests your strategic vision, challenges your reflexes, and ultimately forces you into developing your own combat style and refining your approach to annihilating the forces of hell.

The Most Memorable Part of the Experience:

The chaos on screen, player inputs, and the most metal soundtrack the industry has ever seen, combine together to create a rhythm and flow that's almost musical in itself. In a way it feels like playing a musical instrument. Not practicing, but jamming with friends to songs you know, getting high on finding variations, improvisation, and the thrill of being a crucial part of so many moving pieces. DOOM has great music. DOOM is great music.

Finally, the best praise I can think to lavish upon DOOM is this: I wish there were some way for Hugo Martin and Marty Stratton to handle Reboots for any franchise trying to start over. It seems almost impossible how successful that team was in crafting an experience that nearly every fan of the genre and the original games can enjoy.

Buy DOOM and PLAY IT.\t\t\t\t\t
