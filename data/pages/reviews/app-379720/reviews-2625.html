\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoomguy is back (this time, you're called the Doom Slayer, which is even cooler), and you're at it again.
You remember the saying that the demons should be terrified of you?
Well, in this game, they canonically are.

The demons know of you.
They are terrified of you.
And you make their fears of you real.

Being the monster that demons are afraid of is awesome.
The Glory Kills, which are the unique melee kills you can execute, remains fun and satisfying from start to finish.
Mowing everything down with your bullets, swimming in the blood of demons without uttering a single word, not even a grunt, is such an amazing experience.

One thing that I was afraid of was the repetition.
I mean, you can only shoot and melee demons in so many ways, right?
Well, yeah, but it never gets old.
Bethesda has somehow made the repeated massacres of hordes of demons enjoyable the whole way; which is impressive if you think about it.

Graphically, amazing.
You can see 1 map before and after being corrupted by Hell, and it adds a lot of flavor.
Mars and Hell itself has fantastic landscapes; especially Hell.

The sound effects is so satisfying.
Gauss Canon on Siege Mode; everytime you ready this beast, you feel like a badass.
Every time you fire it, you get the oh-so-satisfying feeling of power.

The music is amazing.
Just listen to the music in the menu alone and you'd understand.
If fits the game perfectly.

Story wise, it's quite minimal if you just go at the demons with nothing but murder in mind.
You will get some story, but it's super straightforward, and just plain Doom.
Go find demons, kill demons, close down Hell portal; same old wonderful plot. xD
But if you are interested you can read the flavor texts, and they are plentiful.


This is game that revels in what it does best.
Doom; it's where you go kill demons, and everything else is secondary to that.

Bethesda, you've outdone yourselves with this.
Bravo.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBetheda made the right choice when rebooting the series. DOOM 2016 is a fast paced FPS shooter that runs smoothly with a wink to nostalgia. The campaign mode is interesting and offers an alternative narrative, sometimes you stop just to look at the beautiful environments. Contrary to DOOM 3 you actually have a chance to fight without being too cramped by narrow corridors and the prospect of playing a glorified Dead Space.

The multiplayer mode is a bit limited but... the modes and weapons make it even more straightforward. A huge asset is the Snapmap principle: you can enjoy retro maps, mini-games, new storylines and some real interesting game concepts. As long as there is a supporting community it has a good replay value.

The weapons are related to the classic arsenal but you can upgrade them along the way. The amor or Praetor suit in itself is a nice overall tweak. Personally I liked the campain and especially the Arcade Mode were true speed and mastery of weapons excels.

Contrary to Quake Champions this feels like an old skool shooter powered by a state of the art game engines. The Vulkan API shows a lot of visual potential and a more efficient use of your hardware. I enjoyed playing Doom 1 and 2 and I consider the 2016 version the one and only true direct successor and a piece of art!\t\t\t\t\t
