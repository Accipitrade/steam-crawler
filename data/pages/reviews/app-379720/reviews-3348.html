\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 4 is fast, gory, chaotic and blast!

One of the best single player campaigns I have played in years, perhaps one fo the best ever. The momentum and the pacing of this game are freakishly good. The level design and added depth is just fantastic. Each level is a sprawling open map that all last more than an hour. Theres half a dozen secrets per level as well, I only ever managed to find 1/4 of them. You can also find parts of classic DOOM levels and play the full levels outside of the main campaign.

I could only describe DOOM 4's singleplayer as near perfect.

If theres on very small issue I had with its the fact that some of the jumping puzzles in the early and mid game drag on for abit too long and are just kinda annoying.

Overall multiplayer is not worth bothering with, it hasnt really improved from the BETA and its not even by Id anyway. It was outsourced to a studio that does Halo and CoD stuff. Snap map on the other hand has some potential to be a really great tool for DOOM modders and level designers to make some really great stuff. I cant wait to play some of the levels by the community soon.

Finally If you dont have a 900+ plus card you can expect some preformance issues. For the most part with my 770 the game ran fine on high settings at 60 fps but I would experiance the rare frame rate drop which brought it down to 15fps for some bizarre reason. Across all cards and systems there have been some random and really annoying crashes.

All in all its a great game, RIP AND TEAR

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHad no idea I was buying a platform game....

I like the game, let's be clear - everything but the level design.

When the combat kicks in Doom absolutely rocks, but the combat is rare, believe it or not, and punctuated by far too many quiet and frankly very boring moments where the game becomes a "how do I get over to that ledge" platformer more akin to Tomb Raider. This creates huge problems with the game's pacing. A few minutes of superb, visceral combat, followed by many more minutes of ledge-hopping drudgery; then repeat. I think the level design is to blame here. It is also chock full of jumps you need to make that are instant-death if you miss them. Something that should never be in Doom.

Don't get me wrong, the levels are actually beautifully designed, and I admire the work put into them, but they're just not Doom levels. Too complex for their own good perhaps, they get in the way of the action rather than enhancing it, imo. I'd rather play Doom 1 and 2 levels with these graphics and be done with that. So wish they had just done that, as a reboot perhaps. The game as it is is just not pulling me in the way Doom 1 and Doom 2 do (both of which I replay right through quite regularly).

I really can't see me coming back to this game again once I complete it (IF I complete it?), as the levels are seriously stifling my enjoyment. Whereas I'm happy to play Doom 1 and 2 any number of times, from start to finish. They're just more fun for me because their levels are simply spaces where you have amazing arcadey combat in, rather than dominating the entire game. Hope that made sense.

Finally, from a stability point of view, like many others I get a random crash to desktop every so often. Approximately one crash every couple of hours. Not hugely frequent but it can wipe out a fair bit of progression if you're unlucky.

Since I do not believe the game has any replay value, at 40 pounds its way over-priced, so I simply cannot recommend it. It's borderline though, as the combat - when it occurs - is fantastic and the graphics are amazing. However you'll get more fun buying Doom 3 BFG edition and just revisiting all of Doom 1 and Doom 2 remastered.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros
- Fun and Interactive
- Great Campain
- Graphics and Acting Superb
- Did Not Screw Around, Brought The Player Into The Story Automatically
- Lots Of Mysteries, Twists And Turns

Cons
- Glory kills and campaign can be repeative, however it's still great
- Multiplayer could be worked on more
- The shotgun needs more worked done (It's supposed to work well close range, not long range.)
- Chaingun needs to be nerfed tremendously (The gun is one of the most used weapons in the game and keeps being used to get the most kills off of people)

DOOM (2016) is a great way for id Software to get back on their feet and publish more DOOM titles. This game definitely raised the DOOM series back from the dead after 12 years and 8 years being in development hell. I'd really recommend this game to any one who loves DOOM or loves to battle undead and hellish creatures.

Despite the poor Multiplayer gameplay and weapons performance, I still have to say that the campaign story, graphics, and mechanics saved the game from suffering horribly wrong.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAftering hearing mostly negative reviews from the beta, I heard some astounding feedback after its release. I decided to check out the game, mostly for the Campaign. This game is great! It's easily the best FPS campaign I've played in years. The difficulty is high (but you have plenty of options to choose from), there are plenty of well hidden secrets, and the Weapon and Armor enchancements are the perfect addition. If you're a fan of the Doom 3 style story, they did a good job with a similiar setup here, however the story isn't as forced.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is back and Bethesda did it once again. I was in love with Doom the second I installed that shareware disk that I picked up from a cash register display at Staples. This newest iteration of Doom managed to preserve its fast-paced, badass feel that made me fall in love with this franchise all over again. I really felt Doom 3 kinda stuck you in the position of taking things slow and strategizing your movements, leaning more on story and adopting the horror genre that was very popular at its time of release. Everything about this game is love-letter homage to the floppy disk original -- equipped with modern, polished and very immersive graphics, the inception of the Glory Kill (enough said), weapons upgrades and secondary options, incredible level design, grappling mechanics that allow you to reach high ledges and more. Bethesda does an amazing job with the level design and strategically placing enemy spawn points to always keep you on your toes. This is NOT the game where you dig in behind cover. Actually there's very little by way of cover, and if you want to survive, you have to run head-first into the demon hordes. This game is fast, it's in your face, it's everything it was back in 1993 save for the floppy diskettes. Now for the setbacks:

1. No local LAN/co-op
2. No private game mode
3. 6-player maximum party online

So, if you're looking for a good LAN party game, Doom has sadly exited that arena. We had set up a LAN party for release night. We had to split up into 3 parties and could not all play together. Doom was the game that introduced me to LAN parties! Sad to see it go! Wonder how they're going to adjust this for official competitive gaming?

All in all, single player is a wonderfully-emersive experience. Doom purists will certainly be more than satisfied. I certainly was, and new gamers will undoubtedly go into this iteration of Doom experiencing the feel and the spirit of the original.\t\t\t\t\t

Graphics are next gen, light and shadows, reflection are dynamic, particles seem to use some sort of apex engine (think Nvidia physX) post processing is also very dynamic (motion blur, depth of field tone mapping and HDR) very movie-like. Sound is incredible, every room has its own reverbation, If you play this with a home cinema get ready for a treat, the use of low frequencies in this game is incredible. Soundrack is the best part, fits the game like a glove. Gameplay goes from being a fast pace FPS shooter to a take your time and explore type like Bioshock/Metroid. Map design is very well done multiple pathways to the same objective, lots of collectable and hidden secrets. Combat is the best part of this game. Weapons and enemies have a learning curve to them and the AI design is done right as eneies chase you down and attack/flank you from blind spots....! Its well worth the 80$ I paid.\t\t\t\t\t
