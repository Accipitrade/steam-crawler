F*****G BRUTAL FUN

- Game runs like a dream with the Vulkan API setting: Constant 60fps on Ultra 1440p with an RX 480.

- Visuals are immense. The details in the world and the overall design can be gob smacking at times. You need to remind yourself to stop and smell the demonic roses dripping in blood though 'cause the pacing is so driven.

- Gameplay is frantic, fast, and sometimes rewardingly difficult, but never unfair. You really have to learn to adapt to the situation and think on your feet, it's a great challenge even on 'hurt me plenty' mode. Someone else said it, it's like a Dark Souls fps in terms of difficulty.

- Sound is top notch. Music is as brutal as the gameplay and is programmed so well to fit with the ebb and flow of a fight. General sound effects such as monsters, ripping things apart, and gun sounds are scintillating.

- Story is perfectly good though not the star of the show.

Conclusion : Intense challenge that's rewarding, fun, and awe inspiring at times. 9/10 \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe perfect video game. Story takes a backseat to gameplay, giving you fun, often nonlinear levels with a variety of enemies and weapons to murder them with. Secrets are legitimately hidden and hard to find. Ultra-Nightmare is for the kinds of people that like to shove pencils down their ♥♥♥♥♥.
Performance is pretty good too, although you will need a newer rig to play 60+ fps, and moderate pc's / laptops can give you a solid 30-40 fps.
The music can be good at times, but for the most part I'm a bit disappointed that they went with a more electronic/dubstep mixed with metal rather than classic metal like the original Doom soundtracks. That can be fixed by turning off the music in the game, alt-tabing and turning on a remastered Doom soundtrack on youtube.
The multiplayer isn't anything special, but it's not as bad as people say. It's more fast paced and vertical than say Call of Duty, which honestly makes it better. But ultimately you'll play for the singleplayer.
The snapmap seems underdeveloped, there's a lot of restraints (probably for console players), and there's no real way to mod the game, which is unfortunate considering the reputation the classic games had. Of course, making WADS is much simpler than making a mod or level these days, what with the increase in complexity of the engine.
Overall, the game is super worth it. DOOM is the best game of the decade.
