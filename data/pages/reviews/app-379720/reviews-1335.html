\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a solid FPS that has a big emphasis on mobility in combat, and an even more solid soundtrack. The arsenal you acquire throughout the game provides a variety of flavors. Every gun works a little differently and sometimes your survival depends on which gun you swap to at a certain moment. The levels are mostly pretty linear, but they are cram packed with little secrets and bonuses for you to find which makes them feel very satisfying to explore. I do recommend that people who are familiar with shooters play on atleast Hard mode, because the normal mode (hurt me plenty) really does feel too easy even on the first play.

What I really like about this game are the visuals of your environment. The facilities on the surface of Mars feel authentic and have a very Dead Space sort of vibe to them. A lot of the levels have you platforming around the inner workings of giant computers or Sci-fi machines. The visual design in Hell is also pretty pleasing, but especially highlighted by the fantastic sound effects and ambience.

Now this is a Bethesda game, and so it does have a few minor bugs. I have to say though, I'm pleasantly surprised with the very low amount of bugs in the game. It's never anything totally game breaking. Usually something visual like an effect that hangs in the air. The worst that could happen is you may need to reload from your last checkpoint because a certain enemy didn't spawn or something.

If you're just looking for replayability, this game is probably not for you. It is a fantastic game to play through one time, maybe again in the future. But it offers no variety between playthroughs, it's going to be the same experience every time (outside of maybe playing on hardcore mode next time).\t\t\t\t\t

Rip. Tear. Collect.
My personal experience with DOOM has no room for praise like the mojority of people that give this game. The game is well designed in almost everything, be it the soundtrack, enemies & weapons or story. But there is one mistake this game has, that ruined the flow and the feeling it beautifully builds up for you and that is the secret collectables and powerpoints spread all around the levels. You are allowed to ignore these secret items but sadly they give you points that you need to power up your suit and weapons especially early on in the campaign, you want as many of these points as possible plus you feel satisfied with all collectables found on each level. But the time I have spent searching for these is unbelievable and unnecessary, I finished the campaign in 15 hours in my second attempt with the game because I quit after 60% progress in the first run becuase of the frustration I got from killing the flow of a game that normally is known for words like RIP & TEAR .
Considering the price and the rest of the game that is wellmade I'll still give a dissapointed recommendation in hopes that the sequel will fix this.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:

• Excellent soundtrack. Mick Gordon carries the entire audio design on his back, and then some more.

• Very fluid animations, especially for monster parkour/movement

• Engaging, fast-paced gameplay. Not as fast as the Classics, but still miles above any other modern game.

Cons:

• Inconsistent audio, with explosion sounds being way too low and almost no sound telegraphs for many important attacks. You'll occasionally get one-shot by out of screen projectiles that were inaudible. Even enemies as big as Mancubi or Hell Knights can walk without making any noticeable sound, often sneaking up on you and deleting you with a single melee attack.

• Worst shotgun I have ever used in around 25 years of shooter experience. The SSG at least saves this, but the basic shotgun needs two headshots at point blank to kill an Imp, and has such awful spread that outside of melee range you might as well not use it. Even the regular assault rifle has better TTK than the shotgun... at point blank, too.

• Extremely tight health and ammo economy, forcing you to use the glory kill gimmick which punishes clean kills with headshots and rewards not dealing overkill damage so you can glory kill a dude. Glory Kills also have invulnerability periods but they're inconsistent, having me die mid-animation sometimes to an offscreen attack.

• Above points create a very unfun early game, with the game only really picking up after you have upgraded yourself enough to have a less Resident Evil experience and more of a Doomslayer experience

• Massive guns that block most of the screen. There's a reason why a lot of pro UT/Q3A players had weapons invisible ; screen space is precious in a shooter. The Gauss (the game's sniper) occupies around 40% of my screen when equipped. Yikes!

Game overall could've benefited from better telegraphs to create a more consistent, skill-based experience, and toned down the importance of ammo upgrades in early game since most enemies are very bullet spongy.

Worth buying if you're looking for some "arcade" shooter, but don't expect a flawless product.\t\t\t\t\t
