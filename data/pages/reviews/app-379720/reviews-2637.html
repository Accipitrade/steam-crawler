\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finally got around to playing doom. After 25 years of waiting, I'm blown away by what people could do in the 90s with graphics technology at the time.

Oh of course this isn't the original doom, and of course I've played Doom MANY times on many different systems. But I also did grow up in the 80s and 90s and gave Doom to me as an impressionable child of only 12. And ignoring what Jack Thompson would spout for years later I grew up to be a game developer and not a murderer. Doom was one of those revolutionary games.

24 years later, Doom came out again, the original team is no longer at ID but... if we grabbed that team and transported magically through time, Doom would probably look, play, and perhaps feel the same as this game. And it's really DAMN brilliant.

If I tried to sell a company on "DOOM" as it is, and called it anything else, I think I would be laughed out of an office. A FPS where the story isn't the focus. Where it's just room after room of fighting the same enemies, simple weapons that were made 20 years ago, and just the most ultra violence I could make.

The thing is, DOOM is a game you can't make any more for all those reasons, it's probably TOO violent, it's mostly simplistic, and it's repetitive. But this isn't any game, this is a vitalization of a franchise that started the genre and honestly anything but what this game is, would be a crime.

There's really too many brilliant things to mention. There is a story, but it's clear through action (not words) the main character isn't having any of it. There's repetation but my god the action is INTENSE that even going through the same enemies thirty or forty times is intense. Even the gameplay is run and gun, not "War run and gun" But this is DOOM classic run and gun. An attack is coming at you? Move out of the way. Do that again and again and return fire and that's honestly a bit of brilliance in the modern era. Raycasted bullets are the standard, but dear god, this breaks that mold.

Then there's the violence, and it's QUITE over the top, but you know what? You're fighting demons from hell, using all sorts of weapons on them, and while the violence is visceral and in your face, it fits with the game's themes.

There's the fact you fight enemies over and over, but this is a masterclass in level design. The enemies have a good variety, but change slowly, the locations though are always epic, and you feel awesome ducking behind different areas, and seeing different elements of level design come into play, like the verticality and circular levels. It's really exceptionally well done, something Romero would only have been able to dream of all those years ago.

There's just a solo problem with the entire game, and that's the music. E1M1 (At Doom's gate) is a masterpiece of audio that may not have been topped in video games, it gets you ready, puts you in the fight and gets your blood pumping, just listening to is just so awesome.

New Doom however. The music isn't "bad" but there's definitely a lacking edge to this music and it's the single area that the game hasn't topped the original, but being unable to top a masterpiece isn't a horrible problem. And while the music might not live up to it, the entire rest of the game gives with both barrels.

There's Snapmap, and multiplayer and while snap map is interesting as an experiment, and multiplayer is decent, I didn't really feel a reason to return to either of them even with the improvements. What you want to do here is play Doom, the most excellent of retro shooters, perhaps the only game that should be this retro and does so with the perfect style.

But to be honest, this is a game that's going to be retro, and I really wish everyone could and would enjoy it. The fact is much of this game is a return to form of a 25 year old game, however gaming has moved on in those 25 years, yet every "return to form" is exactly that. It's returning to the great old days, not the rose tinted nostalgia days that were "better back then" but it returns us to what was great about those days and makes us glory in it.

So while not every single person will like it (Some people will never be able to accept the super fast pace action), I can't do anything BUT recommend this game as to me I'd call it the perfect sequel to Doom. And god I can't wait for the next one.

Just please don't call the next one Doom 2, it's confusing enough calling this one DOOM.

Still not as good as I thought it would be, but it is a damn fine game. I bought it thinking my mate would pay me back. That was half a year ago. Caleb, if you're reading this, quit ignoring me and pay me the ♥♥♥♥ back. (Little did I know 2 months later he stole my debit card and tried to spend $1500, and he spent $60 on a porn game the pathetic little ♥♥♥♥)

Anyway, it feels like an older classic FPS with modern tropes, unlike other modern FPS games that claim to harken back to the era. Far better than I tink anyone could have hoped for, but after all the hype after release I was still
expecting a bit more, although I'm not really sure what since this does tick all the boxes.

The Great:
++Incredible performance on a GTX1060, i5 4460, 8GB RAM (120+ FPS) with many graphical options
++Finally a true triple A FPS that really holds up to classic FPS gameplay
++Exploring is lots of fun and very rewarding
++Weapons have real weight and impact

The Good:
+Findable readables are plentiful and add lots of interesting stuff to read
+Finding secrets is fun and rewarding
+Suit upgrades are great
+This game has a map system, doesn't have reloading and doesn't have aiming
+Weapon upgrades change up gameplay in an interesting way
+Bosses are fun to fight
+Enemy designs are true to their original concepts and look, sound and play wonderfully within the environment
+Animations are amazing
+I like the time trials aspect
+Arcade mode is a fantastic addition to the series, even if I think it is a bit rough around the edges
+An FPS with a sense of humour in 2016
+Campaign is a good length

The Okay/Not Too Big of a Deal:
+-Game looks good enough, but not as good as maybe it should
-+The story is boring but I can't really fault it. It's DOOM, not The Last of Us, and the majority of the backstory isn't forced on you whatsoever.
+-Besides the main theme, I don't like the music. I'm in the minority I know. I ended up turning it off honestly.
-+Arcade mode has minor issues
+-Once you've gotten the hang of the gameplay, this game is hell easy on Nightmare

The Bad:
-Takes a while to boot
-Playing this for a second time shows how annoying waiting for useless dialogue to end is
-You'd think it's a small issue, but enemies make very little sound so I'm constantly getting attacked by Imps because I can't hear them coming up behind me. Sound is important, especially on Nightmare when a single Imp attack can take off so much health.
-The whole 'go to an area, interact with it and then have a battle orgy' concept does get a bit tiring after a while, but it's a very minor issue

The Horrible:
--To be honest, and this isn't some ♥♥♥♥♥♥ brag over a video game, but this game is a bit easy on Nightmare. I mean I still die, but usually only on specific bits every once in a while. It takes a little while to get back into the rhythm but once you do, there's few times you can be stopped. That being said, I wouldn't even be able to do a quarter of the game on Ultra Nightmare.
--The checkpoints were placed by an idiot
--Multiplayer is atrocious and no one plays it
--DOOM guy refuses to climb way too often which gets me killed a lot. Also, he constantly gets stuck on little objects in the worst of times. In a game about moving constantly stopping for a single second on some random piece of ♥♥♥♥ on the floor while a big ♥♥♥♥ chases me is unnacceptable when it's this prevalent.

Sound: 8/10
Graphics: 8/10
Gameplay: 9/10
Content/Replayability: 7.5/10
Overall: 9/10

Awards:
Runner-Up Game of the Year - 2016

Conclusion
The game still kicks serious ass and the guys at iD should be proud. I honestly don't know if anyone else could have made it as good as it is. A damn fine game for sure. Just wish it was a little more difficult. Starts off unfairly hard with stupid checkpoint placements, but once you've gotten into the flow it's butt easy.\t\t\t\t\t
