\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 2016 is one of the best FPS games ever made, and actually probably in my top 10 favorite games of all time. Specifically because completing the game on Ultra Nightmare difficulty is one of the most challenging and fun things in gaming in the past 20 years.

It does have things that don't really mesh well with the DOOM franchise, though. For one, the use of exploration like keycard collecting is oversimplified, making me yearn for earlier games like Doom 2 and Doom 64 with their labyrinthian mazes that require careful exploration to unfold. With that said, everything else is just about perfect. The graphics are fantastic, and it's still very satisfying to blow enemies into chunks of flesh using the variety of weapons the game gives you.

I actually didn't care for this game that much at first, but it was because I didn't really see the nuances in the game play. If you sink a good 40 hours into it, you'll start to notice a lot of details about the game play that make it top notch. Basically, there are a few difficulties, ultranightmare being the hardest. You can be killed in one hit, and you must complete the whole game without dying because it deletes your save file if you do. Now normally this sort of challenge would be insanely annoying, but because this is such a polished game it's actually fun spending hours figuring out different strategies to speed through the different sections.

Doom 2016 is part RPG since you have all kinds of upgrades, and it takes time to understand how it all fits together. FIrst off, the suit has tokens that upgrade certain abilities. Then you have weapon upgrades, of which you can pick from two. Killing enemies or completing certain activities unlocks points you can use to upgrade these skills, which then gives you the option to master the weapon and unlock some final perk if you complete a certain task. On top of this you also have argent cells which increase certain attributes like max health, ammo and shields. So there's a quite a bit here and you have to use some advanced thinking and logic to figure out specifically how to beat the game on ultra nightmare, since if you die all your efforts are lost. When I had a near death moment a good five hours into the game my heart legitimately skipped a beat, it's the most tense game mode I've ever played in a video game.

It helps that the heavy metal music is just brilliant, and the enemies have this weird way of confronting the player and looking truly menacing before they attack. There is a bit of a formulaic nature to the way they attack, it isn't really random other than perhaps their movement patterns. For example, the imp will charge up a fireball that will lock on and hit you 100% of the time unless you change your movement pattern a second before he throws it. But since there are so many enemies and they are constantly moving around, you are never really safe, and that's what makes the combat so tense. Compare this to a game like Halo: Combat Evolved, which is also tense, but also has the safety net of letting the player retreat and recharge his shields if things get too challenging. In this game the shields don't recharge, although there are various nuances that make it possible to increase health and armor during combat. It's pretty useless on ultranightmare though, since those precious half seconds you use to collect the extra bit of armor or health will probably get you killed.

There is also something to be said about how the game play is streamlined. The BFG, or the ultra weapon, is relegated to one button, and the chainsaw is relegated to another. The chainsaw is a brilliant weapon because using it causes ammo to spill out of enemies. So you really save the chainsaw ammo (It takes fuel) for when you are low on other ammo. Each enemy has a specific strategy for taking it out. For example, the cacodemons wlll float and follow the player all over the map, so you really want to kill them first. The hell knights and barons of hell will pursue the player relentlessly, so you need to be constantly running and jumping until you have the time to take them out. There's 13 levels, and even after I learned to speed run most of the sections on my own it still took me about six hours to beat the game without dying once. Well worth it though, since the content is a lot of fun to replay and develop new strategies. For example, there is a really hard section in level 8 where you have to fight a lot of demons in a laboratory. If you used the token to extend the life of the power up and also activated the rune that drops armor shards when you kill an enemy, this section is much easier because the rage powerup lasts longer and gives you tons of armor to survive through the battle. It sounds complicated, but that's because it is. It's not something that I would've figured out without testing a bunch of things to figure out what works best.

And lastly the multiplayer is OK, at least on the PS4 version which is how I played it. It's nothing like the single player mode though, and mostly just mindless run and gun shooting for the most part.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat FPS game, great action game. This is easily the best singleplayer FPS released in from the mid-2000s until 2020.

The guns are varied and feel good to use. The enemies are also meaningfully different from each other. The environments are a good mix of open space, closed rooms, and verticality. This all combines to give a ton of variety in the game. I normally get bored playing singleplayer FPS campaigns and the last few levels are usually a slog, but Doom kept me interested until the end.

The music is excellent. The music and gameplay are perfectly crafted to fit together and support each other.\t\t\t\t\t
