I paid $60 for this game and I am very disappointed. I really wish there was a way to return the game. If you're someone who likes story or horror, this game is not for you. If you're someone who likes running around in power armor and punching and shooting every living thing in your path without consequence, then you may enjoy this game.

I have only played DOOM 3, which I understand is a very different game, but it's a game I actually enjoyed. There was a cohesive story and DOOM 3 had an almost Half-Life pacing to it. You still got to run around and shoot things, but you also had times where there were smaller puzzles or actually entertaining bits. And the light horror aspect of the game made it very enjoyable. If you're someone who hated that about DOOM 3 and you want a rampage simulator, DOOM is the game for you. But if you actually enjoyed DOOM 3 because of the reasons I named above? Leave this game be and save your money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the Doom series, all the way from the originals, so this was a definite must for me on my xbox. Of course, after I built my computer it was a must on here too. The campaign is great and hails back to a time where you didn't need a story, just a gun and something to shoot. Along with that, snap map is really fun and simple to learn, and contrary to many reviewers, the multiplayer is fun. It is the complete opposite of your modern day fps ducking in cover and waiting for your health to regenerate. Instead, the more damaged you are the more you have to move to find health pick ups. Add in a fairly large skill gap, and you have a challenging and fun multiplayer experience. In all, if you are a fan of Doom and first person shooter genre in general, I can't recommend this game enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you like shooting things? Do you like punching things? Do you like chainsawing things? Welcome to Doom. The new Doom. It's been a few years since we've had a good Doom game. Doom 3 was pretty good, but it was more an attempt to be horror than it's roots were attempting to be. But this, this is ACTION. Action in the wholest of sense.

You play as the Marine (Doom Guy) who fought back the denizens of Hell. But eventually was captured and place is a massive mausoleum in Hell. After a few centuries, scientists eventually reach Hell in an attempt to discover unlimited energy. You awake on Mars, and after punching some demons, you regain your marvelous armor and the real game begins.

No one plays Doom for the story, and really as such it doesn't go really far in its development. The Demons are attempting to take over Mars and head back to Earth, and Doom Guy must shoot them. All of them. And that is where the game excels marvelously. Shooting Demons has never been this fun. Each gun, and you get a lot, feels good. The visceral thrill of the super shotguns blast, the chainguns belt fed nonsense, the pistols small pop, they all just sound great to use. They feel even better.

The best thing that can be sad about Doom is how different it is from Doom 3. Where Doom 3 had you wandering darkened hallways awaiting horrors that you must shoot, Doom 2015 MAKES you that horror. The speed that you move is amazing, so quick and elusive. That speed is neccesary when you face off against the myriad hordes of Hell. The enemy design is great, and the way they slowly trickle in more and more enemy types throughout game is great. The more and more enemy types you meet, the more they change up which type your facing. Eventually you face every one, in a massive gauntlet near the end. Having to learn these enemies throughout, and eventually how they react with other enemy types is great fun and keeps the game fresh.

Lets go back to the weapons for a moment. You get plenty, but everyone has two alternate fire modes to unlock. The regular shotgun can unleash a few shots in a row OR launch a grenade. Each one is fun, and adds tactical knowledge to your aresenal. Both the weapons and the Doom Guy can be upgraded using special items hidden in the levels. This can be more damage, or a small change in the way the gun works. Having to upgrade your Doom Guy is great, as your playstyle will determine which you want to upgrade first. His health, his ammo, his special abilites that cause health to drop of off meleeing enemies?

There are context sensitive moments where an enemy is weak enough that a melee attack will kill it. These leave health behind, or ammo, after the enemy dies. This is handy for when your up against several foes, and can quickly kill minor enemies while replenishing health. You also get a chainsaw. While not a regular weapon (you have to find gas to fill it up) it is a devastating weapon. It also has the unique ability for enemies to drop ammo, health, armor or what have you. In the middle of a fire fight, to whip out the chainsaw and murder several towering demons to just shotgun shells is both empowering and excellent game design.

I was surprise by how much I liked about Doom 2015. The combat is fast and ferocious. The weapons are excellent to wield. Its almost a throwback of sorts. Its a game that could've been released in '98 and people would've loved it. But instead we have it now. And it sure is a blast.\t\t\t\t\t
