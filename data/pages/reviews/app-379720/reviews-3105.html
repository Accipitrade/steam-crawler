\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a good shooter. It doesn't quite live up to it's DOOM name. It's not as fast, levels are rather linear and the soundtrack is underwhelming imo.

Gameplay and movement feels great, just a tiny bit slow for my tastes. Most of the demons fire projectiles which can be dodged. This inspires a fast-paced run-and-gun style of play. Along with the loud and energy-packed soundtrack, most of the encounters gives a great surge of excitement, in an adrenaline-rush sort of way. The story stays in the back seat most of the time.

The game looks great, both art and graphics. Some vast areas I've had fps-dips below 1/2 of my regular. So it's so-so optimized. The sound seems rather compressed and lacking in dynamics. Fine details are completely drowned out by the soundtrack and demon-howls.

One thing I missed from classic DOOM was the sense of danger when exploring a level. Few demons are lurking the level. Traps feel more like chore. You hit a trigger, which locks you in, until every demon is slain. Makes the game feel repetetive. Some levels will hinder you from backtracking by closing a door behind you. Not uncommon for modern shooters, but imo a step down from classic DOOM.

Dispite my complaints, the game has been a lot of fun. Campaign took ~10 hours on medium. And I see myself coming back to challenge harder difficulties a few months down the line. Multiplayer is also great, but given that it has paid DLC, I wouldn't recommend it.

DOOM IS BACK


Pros:
+Graphics
+Music (Heavy Metal at the right time = love)
+Controls
+Level Design (nice structure and hidden paths)
+Nostalgia (OMG DOOM IS BACK!!!)
+Secret levels + Hidden Items (those little guys are fun to find)
+Guns (HOLY S**T BFG IS NUTS!!!)
+Glory Kills (Punching a Demon in the face or ripping their eyes out feel good)
+Difficulty (Challenging when needed to be)
+Story (It's simple, and to the point, no long cliche filled cutscenes)

Cons:
-FPS Drops (i74790 - GTX770 - 12gb Ram on Med/High settings @1080p = 20-50fps depending on level)
-Falling through the map (happened a few times during glory kills)
-Bugs (Had to restart from checkpoint a few times due to game not moving forward after demons die)
-Multiplayer (don't buy this for the multiplayer)

SCORE: 9/10

If you're like me and only bought this game because of how much fun you had playing the original, then you're in luck. This is the first Doom game since the original to capture that feeling you had while playing. Is it a great game? No not by a long shot. But it will give you 10+ hours of feeling like a bad-ass.

The Guns are massive, the music makes you want to head bang, and the demons are jaw dropping. I haven't had this much fun playing a Single Player FPS campign in a very very long time. It may be short, but it is sweet. The game gets more challenging as you go, but you also become more of a ass kicker with it. Tack on a upgrade system and it's constantly making you look forward to search the entire map for upgrades. The secerts are nicely hidden so when you find one you get excited. The Old Doom levels are fun to experience again make you want to search every single corner of each level to find them.

Some may say 10 hours is a bit short for a AAA title that costs $60 at full price. Which may or maynot be true. I guess that's why I waited for a sale. I spent $30 on this and I don't regret it. If a game can keep me entertained on the edge of my seat for 10 straight hours of heart pounding action I'm in. I would rather have non-stop fun then fun every couple of hours and needless boring in-between.


So if you want to murder Demons and break through the planes of hell, then look no further then DOOM! The only Single Player FPS in a long time that isn't boring and filled with the same old cliche ridden story. No long drawn out cutscenes. Just ass kicking and big guns with scary demons in your way. Pick it up on sale. $60 is too much for this short of a game in my opinion. However $30 felt just right.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGiven it has very large boots to fill, Bethesda's DOOM proves itself entirely up to the task. It's a bit on the short side for 80 bucks, and my 2-year-old middle-line machine had some serious slowdown in a number of places, but a fluid, well paced, engaging gore fest packed with callbacks, chaos, useful powerups and secrets.

The new features are a bit overmuch, giving you such a stellar spread of swappable abilities that the hordes of the damned barely stand a chance once the Rocket Launcher's up to snuff. The Glory Kills are a good idea and actually keep the pace moving and make you consider how much damage you're doing- but since they give you a substantially larger amount of hp when you're low, health ends up being of transient importance. The upgrades, at least, are optional, however, and the game can certainly be beaten without them. It doesn't interfere with jumping over an imp, shotgunning them from above and then turning and assassinating them with your boots, though, and that is a wonderful thing. Overall, the balancing act is successful.

Finally, despite the fact that my computer's exhaust probably could have cooked me dinner, the game didn't crash once, which is a landmark occasion for a Bethesda title in my books. DOOM is a great and memorable shooter, worthy of the series name. Oh, and it has multiplayer, too. Not that I played it.

DOOM.
DOOM is a game like no other with its phenomenal graphics and unique gameplay.
It has three gameplay modes: Singleplayer, Multiplayer, and Snapmap.
Singleplayer:
So far, the storyline is amazing.
The fast-paced gameplay keeps the player busy.
If you look closely around the map, you can find secrets, field drones (weapon upgrades), argent cells (suit upgrades), rune trials, and UAC information logs, which add a sense of adventure to the game.

Multiplayer:
If you are a fan of the cliche multiplayer that can de found in COD, Battlefront, and many more, then this is for you. Otherwise you will get bored easily and wonder why Bethesda didn't make a CO-OP demon slaying mode.

Snapmap:
I have not yet fully explored the capabilities of snapmap, but it was a nice toutch for Bethesda to add a level editor and let the community share content that is enjoyed by many other players.


I will update the information on this review as soon as I can if I discover something that I haven't mentioned.


Overall, this game is the best in my library.

I don't have a lot of time for video games in my day, so while I really like games like Witcher 3 and Fallout 4, I'm not really albe to dig into them an appreciate all the great things they have to offer.

Because Doom has almost no plot and nothing that you need to remember from one session to the next it's perfect for me, and it's tons of fun. I've been playing on "Hurt me plenty" and it has been challenging enough without getting frustrating. It might take dying once or twice in an arena in order to learn where all the stuff is that you need to survive it. The enemies are diverse and each one seems to paired with a particular weapon or combo of weapons and once you know those pairings you can really strategize your fights.

I would not say that this is a horror game. Doom 3 had me scared out of my skin at times which was great. This isn't scary at all. When greeted with a new enemy you may be like "WTF IS THAT!!!!" but thats about it.

Anyways, I'm really enjoying this game. It's a game to play if you don't have the time to invest in an RPG or you had a crummy day and want to turn off your brain for a few hours\t\t\t\t\t
