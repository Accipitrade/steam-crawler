\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been anticipating this game for years. Ever since small details started coming out for Doom 4 in 2007 I was excited. My foundation for PC Gaming started with the classic doom when I was a kid. It was my first FPS followed by Half-Life and Counter-Strike. As time passed and set backs occured I had started to lose hope that Doom 4 would arise from development hell and then a private screening of the game occured at Quake Con and all my hope and hype was rekindled. I picked up Wolfenstein: The New Order and saw that it included access to a closed beta and I knew this game was definetly happening.

Back at E3 last year when they showed the gameplay I sat in front of my TV like a child on Christmas and couldn't help cheering as they unveiled this awesome looking game. As time passed though I started to have doubts that this wouldn't be what I had hoped for. That it wouldn't have that classic doom feel too it. With playing the alpha and beta I was worried that the game would be too limited to content. But I was so wrong.

The Doom singleplayer feels exactly like Doom should. It's fast and it's bloody and challenging. I learned really fast that movement is key. If you don't keep moving you will die as hordes of demons pile on top of your postion. I absolutely love the movement. The fast verticality of moving up walls and boxes above demons to drop right back in. It made for some cool moments and some of my best shooting. I was intially worried that glory kills would become old and tiresome quickly but they don't cease to become satisfying. You kill a few demons and lose some health well just knock a imp's head off and you're good to go again. It never got old, the game was fluid and fun each second of the campaign and I couldn't put it down. Aside from the fun this is a goregous looking game even on medium settings. I found that I could get away with high settings at 45-60 FPS but I found that you want Doom consistently at 60 to really enjoy it.

At the end of the campaign I felt satisfied but I hope they don't make us wait as long as they left the game WIDE OPEN for more. I won't spoil it but I hope there's much more Doom in our future. I'm not normally a MP guy but I will have to give it a go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe new Doom is absolutely one of the most fun games I've ever played! It's true to the old school, no nonsense kind of 1st person action games of the 1990s. There is no quick time events, reloading, aiming down sights, buddy NPCs to babysit, or any of that. It's just you, your wits, and hordes of demons to slay. The game encourages exploration and is filled to the brim with secrets to be discovered. Weapon and character mods add depth to the already intense combat which really pushes this game into the stratosphere. Above all, it's metal and dark and gorey and everything Doom fans have been wanting for years. If you're a fan of games like Duke Nukem 3D, Hexen, Doom 2, Blood, Quake, Painkiller, or Serious Sam you owe it to yourself to buy this game. I've loved every minute of this new Doom and will be playing it for years to come! DOOM is BACK!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is more akin to the first Doom, than doom 3. This is a welcome sight to me as they seem to have made it more action packed (like 1 and 2) and less of a jump scare game (like 3). They found a way to get the old "Never needing to reload system" back in, that still makes it plausable by limiting the ammo you have in one clip, but allowing players to refill it by walking over more ammo packs.

They expanded upon the mod system from quake 4, and introduced the ability to select weapon upgrades. So you can now attach extra effects to your weapons, like the shotgun you can change from a 3 shot charge or an explosive round. They allow players to upgrade their doom suit as well.

The Developers based a lot of the game on the brutal doom mod, which is refreshing to see they care about the community. Its also quite nice that they added a new map system and a lot of reasons to explore the map, all of which are masterfully crafted.

I would say they have done to doom, what Nintendo did to Metroid. If anything, this is as close to DOOM Prime as we are going to get, and it is GOOD! Highly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike Wolfenstein 2014 before it, Doom 2016 merges the heart of the original Doom games - their addicting explore/slaughter/loot loop - to a modern character evolution mechanic that encourages the player to play the game like a rock star badass. Completely explore levels, and use your abilities in specific ways, to unlock new abilities. It turns the old Doom into a new game and it's absolutely brilliant.
The game runs well even on cheap hardware and scales well if you have powerful hardware. I use mostly Medium to Ultra settings, with Shadows and Reflections set to Low. 55-60fps at nearly all times on an i5/GTX 960!

Update: after overclocking my 960, I've been able to get a stable 60fps with occasional brief dips down to 55fps, using a mix of mostly Ultra, some High settings. What an engine!

The quest is long, especially if you play it right (you set out to find everything), it's gorgeous, and the mechanics will make you wonder why there aren't more games like this. Bethesda published another winner here. If you ever loved Doom, then Doom 2016 belongs in your library. Strongest possible recommendation.\t\t\t\t\t
