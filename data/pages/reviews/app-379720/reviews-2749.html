\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWOOOOOOO! DOOM!

This is one of the best FPS's to come out in the last 10 years. The action is high-octane insanity that keeps you grinning like you just picked up a BFG.

If you miss the good ol' days of Armor and Health systems rather than a the recharging shield system made institutional by Halo and COD, then this is the game for you. No reloads, unlimited weapon carrying capacity, health and armor, all packaged in a super well-made FPS that will scratch all the same itches the ID games of the early 90's did, with a tinge of Quake in it.

The single player adds something really unique with the "Glory Kill" system which becomes an important game mechanic rather than just an unnecessary melee animation, and helps this game to stand out a lot. You'll be choosing if it's worth it to sprint across a battlefield to Glory Kill a big baddie for some extra health and armor, or if you should just shoot the bastard and get on handling all the other baddies around.

Combat is chaotic, but never seems wholly unmanageable. They throw lots of enemies at you, and almost all of them are bullet sponges, but they give you plenty of bullets to throw at them in a variety of weapons that really feel different from each other, and offer different advantages in different situations.

The multiplayer is pretty excellent as well. It does have a leveling system, but unlocks all the important stuff within the first 10 levels, which only takes 3 or 4 games depending how well you do. After that it's a very even playing field and is fun for all.

If there's one downside to the game it's that they just said "♥♥♥♥ it" to the plot. Yeah, they go out of their way to explain how you're the "Doom Guy" and there's some token bad guy character motivations there too, but really the concept is: "Earth is out of power, so clearly the answer is to go to Mars and open a gate to Hell," so you can really only take it SO seriously, and thankfully they don't try to shove unnecessary plot down your throat. They know why you're here, and that's shooting demons, and the game never stands in the way of that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game would be even better if the FREAKING VULKAN MODE WORKED!!!

Anyway. this game is pretty much as good as its predecessors. Full of awesome demons to completely obliterate in the bloodiest way possible, sufficient powerups, stunning graphics (even in medium detail, which is about all my rig can handle in 1440p because the hardware's like 5 years old).

Now. Onto that Vulkan issue:

It's been reported in a couple threads on Steam's support forums that this game after its latest patch has issues with displaying in Vulkan mode. The game responds and you can still interact with menu elements, but you can't see a damn thing because it leaves you with a black screen!! If you could fix this issue it'll make a lot of us that bought this game and use/want to use VKN happy!

Now. I'm happy enough to overlook the black screen issue with Vulkan and recommend this game. Just remember that it takes up 67GB of space so make sure you have a decent amount of space handy!!! Damn! these modern games are getting absolutely massive eh!?\t\t\t\t\t
