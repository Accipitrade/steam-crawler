\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome gunplay, diverse enemies and fast paced gameplay with the oportunity to slow down every now and again and search for secrets.

The soundtrack really drives the action forward in the this game and the finisher mechanic, which allows you to execute enemies who are near death for bonus health and ammo ensures that you never feel like you have to play safe.

The multiplayer is also solid, if you're a fan of quake style arena shooters then Doom 2016 is a solid offering.

The game is very well optimised, achieving well over 60fps (often around 90) at all times with graphics set to ultra with vulkan enabled, with 7700hq i7 at 2700ghz and a gtx1050.

My only criticism of this game is the frequency of set animations and the occasional cut-scene which has the gameplay grind to a halt while Doomguy checks out his shiny new collectable or the game attempts to progress its (fairly mediocre) story.

This is a veeery small gripe with what is by all accounts, a super solid fps.
