\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI honestly hesitated to buy this. But my memories of playing the originals years ago sucked me into it.
I am not dissappointed. I am having a blast playing this. This is hands down one of, if not the best looking game I have ever played. The graphics are absolutley wonderfull. Bethesda's got a winner on their hands!

The game plays smoothly and I havent run into any glitches save one. An imp fell halfway through a floor and got stuck.
other than that one instance it seems to be clean at this point.

If you read down through the reveiws you will notice people talking about needing to play aggressivly. They are 100% correct. You get rewarded for being aggressive and swarmed if you are too conservative. It's alot of fun though!
Some parts you can hang back and snipe to thin them out a little but in the end you knee deep in demon guts!
.....and yes there will be button mashing and the bad language will insue,,,,,,,,,

Frame rate wise it's been nice. I am running an i7 4790k with an asus 290x Direct cu II. All setting maxed I have been from 40-198 FPS @ 1080p. average around 98fps. I have not noticed the rates drop below 60 very often.
I am only mentioning this because at first I had used vsync. What I found unusual but in the end helpfull was that if the fps couldnt maintain 60fps, I would cut and hold the frame rates at 30fps. I havent ever encountered this before. I really like it that way. It really is a seemless transition and I just happened to notice the FPS counter otherwise I wouldnt have known it was doing it. It makes for an extreemly smooth experience.

EDIT: With the latest drivers the lowest FPS I have encountered is 62. But that was in a cut scene. Gameplay wise I did see a drop to 86FPS.

Campaign Gameplay 9/10
Multiplayer ?/? (not into the multiplayer)
Music and audio 10/10 (techno death metal........ it works wonderfully for this game)
Graphics 10/10 (Top of the heap with room to spare!)


There is one con to this game. It is UBER VIOLENT..... and bloody,.... and it has GORE AS FAR AS THE EYE CAN SEE.
Thus I am limited to playing this when my kindergardener is in bed........and I have to sleep sometime.......... Sigh.........

Well worth the 60 bucks on this one. Good job Bethesda. VERY GOOD JOB!\t\t\t\t\t

There is a bit of exploration and secret finding that is actually really fun, not overly complicated, and not super hard. Definitely could feel some of the Bethesda influence on this front. If you're looking for a survival experience that is story centric that was Doom 3, you may want to give this one a pass or wait until it is on sale. If you're looking for a very simplified arena shooter that is easy to pick up, easy to play, and is a hell of a lot of fun, pick it up. Especially if you're a DOOM fan.

TL;DR: Plays like Quake / Unreal / DOOM / anything id, very simplistic, no crazy mechanics, just a fun run and gun shooter.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOK, so I really do love this game. Worth every penny, enjoy it a lot. Beat it in two days grinding and ignored my family the whole time. However there are some things you NEED to know before you buy.

Pros:
1. Fun campaign, feels very classic. Graphics and gameplay are stupidly fun in single player if you like shooters.
2. Level editor is simple and easy to learn and the tutorial is easy as cake. Monkeys could make a decent level with this thing.
3. Multiplayer is fun, especially when you get the demon runes and can rampage the other team. Lots of customization of your character.

Cons:
1. BUGS!!! Seriously, this has a lot of bugs that shouldn't have gotten out of beta, for instance, the constant issue with the game telling me that my saved games are being flagged/achievements disabled and online play disabled due to being in developermode, despite me not doing anything of the sort. Do a search in Google, you won't have to go far to see what I mean. Not to mention that it occasionally lags in single player so much I have to restart.
2. Level editor has only one theme so far, which is indoors UAC. Would have liked more variety. Word has it that more themes will be released in the future, but I smell microtransactions. This is Bethesda, which once tried to charge us for horse armor in Oblivion. Never forget, never forgive.
3. They have this currency in the map editor called snap points or something to unlock additional features, including color schemes for your character in custom maps which doesn't automatically match with your multiplayer character.
4. Multiplayer has no FFA deathmatch, which is silly considering that this is a shooter. Who leaves this out? Also completely defeats the purpose of customizing your character if whenever you play it only shows as red or blue.
5. Speaking of multiplayer, it feels like the multiplayer was made by a completely different team than the single player to the point where half the weapons available in multiplayer don't even show up in the single player, which makes no sense. Also, the key bindings for equipment/melee are switched betwen multiplayer and single player. I can change that, I know, but its wierd and I'll be damned if my lazy index finger is going to take two seconds to change the key in settings.

All of these can be solved with a patch or two. If they patch it right, this can be a fantastic game rather than just an awesome game that will piss you off every few hours.\t\t\t\t\t
