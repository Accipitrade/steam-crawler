\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is visceral -- intense. It's actually the first first person shooter I've developed a long-term interest in. I've purchased others before, but they'd tended to be games I wanted to like more than anything else. Doom is different. The hyperactive gameplay with the glory kills for health mechanics is exhillerating. Each stage also has numerous secrets to discover, and even in the very first stage, finding all of them is no small feat. I'm surprised both aspects of a stage can be enjoyable at the same time. Some of the secrets border on point-and-click adventure hidden objects in terms of difficulty of discovery. Parallel to that is some of the most adrenalyn pumping carnage I've ever experienced. One trope I think must be common to shooters that Doom doesn't shy away from is locking every other door behind you after you enter a room for a locked arena experience. Doom does this a lot. I began expecting it. Fortunately, the no-escape arena combat doesn't change how you'd confront a battle, anyway. Doom is 110% charge into the fray, guns blazing in its gameplay. There's no taking cover in a traditional sense.

I dabbled in multiplayer maybe 5-10 times, all Deathmatch. Other players never miss. I have a long way to go. I also seem to go down in a lot less hits than I have to dish out to take down another player. I think it might be my choice in guns, or there might be a tactic in gathering all the armor upgrades straight off. I'm new to it all. Fortunately, I don't think there's cheating - I did get a few kills each time. I just need practice. Eventually, I'll amend my review when I've done some team multiplayer. Right now, I fear I'd let down any teammates I had. Maybe I can wrangle some friends to buy this? Wouldn't be the worst thing.

Anyhow, in summation -- best FPS ever.\t\t\t\t\t
