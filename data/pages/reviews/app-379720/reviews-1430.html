\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: This is on a list of hall-of-fame FPSes somewhere. Go play it right now. Turn up the difficulty if you're not engaged.

The first time I played it, I didn't like it that much. I played it because it felt like an obligation, not because I really wanted to. Don't get me wrong, I enjoy FPSes as much as the next guy, but arena combat after arena combat got repetitive fast even if I had to admit it was the best FPS experience I've had since I first played couch coop HALO. DOOM16 masters the art of making you want to kill demons; not because of some grand narrative (which really could have used some work pacing-wise), but because when you boot up this game you see demons and you press Q and you get to smash their arm into their face and you remember why GAMES. ARE. AWESOME.

XP bars have kind of dulled games as a whole for me. The incremental dopamine rush of knowing you've made your character better isn't ever progress, but a prevention from obsolescence. Basically, the Apple tactic (allegedly, don't sue me). The Doom Slayer has no experience bars. When he gets out of the sarcophagus at the start of the game, he has a dinky-ass pistol; very marginally useful in every situation. Shoot it as much as you want (you don't want to). When he fights the boss at the end of the game, he has a BIG. Friendly! GUN. Shoot it three (3) times. Make them count, and watch the ribbons of green tear the blood out of demons.

But when the arena combat is over, you're left looking for the next one. The adrenaline subsides, and you traverse DOOM16's mazelike levels to find more things to shoot. Occasionally, you'll find a hole in a wall with a Doom Slayer action-figure at the end of it, or a permanent health boost, or a big armor pack, but you won't actually care. The game isn't really hard enough for the secrets it hides to really matter. If I were to review this game when I stopped playing it two years ago, it would have been a negative review; go buy something else on your wishlist (or metal gear rising revengeance if it's empty). "DOOM excels at what it's great at; shame it's only great at one thing."

Fast forward to the release of DOOM Eternal, and I can't justify buying it even though it looks so good; I still haven't finished DOOM16. So I finish it grudgingly, taking about eight hours to finish the whole game. I tried the multiplayer, found it to be everything I wished Quake Champions was, despite both being very dead. Then I saw I had unlocked the Nightmare difficulty. There was still two more days to Eternal's release, I may as well give it a shot.

I died in the first level (before the title card!) twelve times, and I loved every second of it.

Suddenly the zombies slowly milling about weren't an annoyance--well, they still were, but they were also instant glory kill targets and sources of precious health. The Imps' fireballs, if allowed to charge, deal SEVENTY FIVE DAMAGE. That's almost enough to oneshot you. The soldiers' massive charged shots deal similar damage and have a similar windup, but they fire a three-shot burst of them, and they EXPLODE. WHAT!? I'd played this game for eight hours already and I'd never noticed them doing that! During my first playthrough I had never even touched my grenades; now they were the only thing to reliably and safely kill these slow glass cannons. For a game about mindlessly shooting demons, you think a whole lot about which demons to shoot. The flying Cacodemons' spit deals 105 health, which is your maximum health at the start of the game, and the Hell Knights go from being bullet-spongey annoyances to being legitimately terrifying when they do their leap-slam.

As you progress through the game, Nightamare mode will teach you exactly how to have the most fun playing it by killing you if you do anything else. Stand still and disrespect the demons? Death. Fail to use the right weapon for the right situation? Death. You ride this line constantly as you progress, and you start to actually use the weapons you find. I only used the shotgun, rocket launcher, and chainsaw the first time I played. Now, I can do the super-shotgun/gauss cannon combo with my eyes closed; a severely damaging and ammo-efficient combo for tanky enemies which also thrusts you backwards across the stage and into safety.

Earlier I mentioned that in my first playthrough I disliked the exploration. That dislike goes right out the window as you're frantically scrambling for early unlocks of weapons, more upgrade points for your suit and weapons, and oh my god I haven't mentioned the Argent Cells. There's only about one of these spheres in every level, and they ask you whether you want to increase your health, ammo, or armor capacities. In Nightmare mode, when approaching it, I realized I had no idea which one I wanted. Sure, the chainsaw gave me ammo, but I had no idea what I'd find next. Sure, glory kills had kept me topped off so far, but shouldn't I be a little safer? Not to mention, whenever you upgrade a system, you gain enough of whatever you upgraded to bring you to your new maximum, adding another element to your decision. I can now say that exploration certainly belongs in DOOM16.

This game has no weakest link (okay, except the story, the interruptions are really bad). Buy it the next time it's on sale. Hell, don't. Buy it now, it's that good. If the game is too easy, turn up the difficulty. You won't be disappointed.

Okay. I'm going to go back to playing this game now.\t\t\t\t\t
