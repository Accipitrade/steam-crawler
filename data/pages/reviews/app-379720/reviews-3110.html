\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never written a review, but I'll try to cater this to those that are on the fence about this game and it's DLC like I was. I BADLY want to like this game and it's DLC because I long for the days of the simplicity of Quake III Arena. I think enough has been written about the single player campaign that justifies buying Doom by itself. My biggest gripe is with the multiplayer. It isn't too overly complicated, but there is a big problem: you don't get certain weapons until you reach certain levels! Why they don't have traditional weapon pickup from arena shooters is beyond me and panders too much to the Battlefield/Call of Duty crowd.

Since multiplayer already has a dwindling population you're either a level 500 (maximum level) or you're not. This means that when you get teamed with/against a 50[X] player, they're substantially ahead of you and will pretty much unfavorably stack a 4v4 game (since it's extremely rare to find a full game of any mode) immediately. If you try to find a new lobby, odds are you'll also see the same 15-20 people depending on what time of day you play, too. Sure, you can tolerate it and "git gud", but the DLC isn't worth it because they bundle *all* of the different play modes together to hide the fact that there simply aren't enough people playing and those that are tend to be drastically ahead of anyone new to the game.

Get Doom for the single player campaign with the expectation that Bethesda won't be offering any single player campaign DLC, but wait until the season pass is marked down to $10 if you want to try out the multiplayer because at this point it simply isn't worth it for what you get.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game on sale because it was cheap, and because I have fond memories of chainsawing Barnie the purple dinosaur on the CNC computers in shop class. (Yeah, I'm old). When Doom 3 came out a few years back, I got it, thinking I might relive some of those good memories.

I didn't.

I never even finished it, because I got frustrated. Frustrated by the dim lighting, by the dearth of resources and by the scary freaking demons jumping out of every third shadow to ruin my just-charged-back-up-to-full armor before I could get a shot off. The game used the mechanics, the environments and the enemies to keep me scared, and to its credit, it did just that. I felt like I was playing a slightly tongue-in-cheek sci-fi survival horror. But I didn't buy it to play a slightly tongue-in-cheek-sci-fi survival horror game. I bought it to play Doom, damnit!

I think the whole experience can be summed up by my reaction to getting the chainsaw. I thought "Well, this is kinda fun, but honestly I want something with some damn range so I can keep them from climbing all over me." So I switched to the plasma rifle and slogged back into things until I finally gave up.

So you can imagine my jaded approach to this game. This wasn't even the same studio, let alone the same guys who made the original Doom and Doom 2. Plus, I'm older now. I have kids who are too young to play this game, or even to watch me play it. I figured I'd have something other than Fallout 4 to do after the kids are put to bed and me and the wife are just relaxing in the evening. This just isn't the game I loved so much when I was a teen. It's just a new game that got good reviews and looks fun. That's all it is. It's not really Doom.

So I downloaded it, put the kids to bed, popped on my headphones and fired it up. I played it for a couple of hours before I got far enough to get the chainsaw. My wife then stuck her head in the room and asked me to turn the volume down. "Um, I'm wearing headphones, babe." I told her.
"I can hear the demon things laughing maniacally in the other room, hun," she replied. "It's too loud."
"Oh, uh.... That's me."
"Well, you sound like a melodramatic villain. Cut it out."
"Okay, babe. Sorry."
I went back to playing.

Hehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehehe

Sitting here writing this, I realize what the disconnect between these two titles is. In Doom 3, they tried to introduce survival horror elements. They tried to make sure that you, as the player, stayed scared and on edge. In doing that, they forgot one of the most fundamental elements of the Doom franchise, something Bethesda remembered and clung to.

The demons should be scared of you.\t\t\t\t\t

Earth has an energy crisis so amoral corporation Weyland Yultani decides to siphon energy from Hell and it turns out it really was as bad of an idea as we all thought. Doom is the type of game made to pander to those who grew up with the orginal two but in a good kind of way where the people who made it don't suck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR review first:
Do you like endless running with no reason to slow down or stop? Play DOOM.
Do you like demons that will scare the living crap out of you (Revenants, *shudder*)? Play DOOM.
Do you like stupidly powerful guns (looking at you Super Shotgun)? Play DOOM.
Do you like parkour? Play DOOM.
Do you like nonregenerating health? Play DOOM.
Do you like super gory executions that reveal all the blood and guts you could want? Play DOOM.
Do you like nameless heroes that don't know why they fight? Play DOOM.
Do you like cliffhanger endings that sets Bethesda up to make a second DOOM? Play DOOM.
Do you like awesomely powerful bosses? Play DOOM.
Do you like secrets? Play DOOM.
Do you like challenging combat? Play DOOM.
Do you like Satan and believe hell should rule the universe? Don't play DOOM.
Do you like Call of Duty style shooters with linear progression and slow-paced combat? Don't play DOOM.

Nice Long Review:

I never played the original DOOM. I was a little young for it. But DOOM is the grandaddy of all shooters, and, from what I know, this DOOM pays massive homage to the original. Combat is fast. Super fast. Lightning fast, snap reactions are essential. You may find yourself wanting to use an assault rifle, or the rocket launcher, but you'll always go back to your trusty super shotgun. There is no regenerating health, only health pickups and armor pickups. Executions give you back some health, but won't give you back much unless you're around 50 health. Demons are varied, and force you to prioritize the threats that give you the most trouble. Your thinking will need to become instinct to survive. Combat is challenging enough to make it frustrating even on normal difficulty. (I would probably die of an anuryism if I tried higher difficulties.) Everything in the game is perfectly designed to immerse you in the game, and it's beautiful to boot! I am a self-titled Bethesda fanboy, but DOOM is a genuinely good game. There are a few glitchy spots, and some textures tend to snap in and out of view oddly, but other than that, I really can't complain. 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
