\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn this review I'll just write about the singleplayer campaign, since I don't play multiplayer a lot.

Perfect combination of old and new elements, like on one hand the fast paced gameplay with a variety of guns for different situations, varied enemy types and some open levels which can be explored non-linearly (including secrets!) and on the other hand the weapon and character upgrade system which lets you pick your preferred playstyle. The graphics are a mix of mostly Doom 3's dark atmosphere and a few of the old Doom's colourful enemies. The only thing I didn't like as much as the rest was the design of a few of the enemies, like the cyberdemon which looks like it comes straight out of a Blizzard title, but that's just a very minor flaw.

Technically, the game is also very good. The environments look great, indoor as well as outdoor. The enemies are extremely detailed and wonderfully animated. The framerate is stable at 60 (GTX 970, 16GB RAM, i5-4690K) and the loading times are not even noticable on my SSD. The only flaw is that the game crashed 2 times so far.

I can recommend this to old and new FPS fans alike, it's just great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, for everyone wondering whether to buy Doom 2016 or not: I have completed the SP and I will not spoil anything, but it is in some sense of the word a remake of the original Doom (1), so in case you where wondering about the "story".

tl;dr: its a good, solid, fast shooter with more under the hood that you might think.

Speaking of which: there is some very thin story running in the background just to tie set pieces (and "objectives" - and I use this term extremely very lightly here - together). In the end, save for one or two rooms maybe this game will throw you into one arena after another and pit you against an increasingly difficult monster mix. Rinse and repeat. Sounds boring? It definitely should be and I can see how this formula can overstay its welcome, But the game manages to keep the action itself extremely interesting. For one: shooting stuff in Doom is extremely fun. The Weapons feel right and powerful, the enemies react well, you get a good sense of damage. It just works, the people who made this game knew how to make it work.
The other part are the arenas and how tactics work in the world of Doom. The action is very offensive, fluid and fast paces. I you ever played Quake 3 you will get flashbacks, since it is not rare that you make some cool acrobatics just to hit that one target just right and it all fits together perfectly. As for the tactics: there is a lot of that here. Just running in blindly will get you killed, especially in the later fights. Using the territory to your advantage and especially making use of the glory kills for health, sometimes ammo or armor and also to save that one bullet, or getting in that one chainsaw takedown for ammo is crucial for survival. Since all this has to do with the enemy placement and enemy (re-) direction, these kills do not slow down the action, but reward you for risky maneuvers in sticky situations. Its a fluid, organic part of the gameplay and works extremely well as a complete package. It also forces you to make good weapon decisions. Not splattering that one imp with a rocket, but instead going for a glory kill, because you a low on health, or even saving him for an easy glory kill later on when you might be low on health or ammo is the stuff that you need to think about and that the game will teach you. Resource management is what its all about and this makes even the pistol a essential part of your armory even in later stages. Ammo is spread out juuuuust right, so that you cannot blast your way through everything with just one gun, but need to change up your tactics according to the situation, enemy type and ammo available.
To change up the pace a bit, there are many, many hidden secrets. Some extremely well hidden, others a bit more obvious, but its always rewarding. be it a Doomguy Chibi-style collectible, or a mega health and armor, exploration is rewarded and will make you keep your eyes peeled at all times.
Finally, there are challenges for each stage that will reward you with weapon upgrade points. This will make you try out different stuff in the levels and go for risky kills in heat of battle. Sadly, most of the upgrades don't feel like much and in the end, I simply left that part out, since all weapons where almost completely upgraded already and there are simpler ways of getting weapon points.
Is Doom difficult? Well, it certainly is no Dark Souls, but the difficulty can ramp up, and I did have my fair share of deaths. I would not call it hard though, but the later battles on higher difficulties will most probably be a challenge for some people.

So, while on the outside, Doom is just one arena fight after the other, there is a lot more to it than you might see at fist glance. The game is just long enough to not overstay its welcome, but it is on the edge. the final few battles where already borderline tedious, since there are no new enemies or weapons and you start seeing the same situations a couple of times over.
All in all it was a blast blasting my way through Hell and back, but before I even think about touching Snap-Map I need a break.\t\t\t\t\t
