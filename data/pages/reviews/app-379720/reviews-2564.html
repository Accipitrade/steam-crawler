\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy trust in id was waning in the past years.
Doom 3 was good but didn't feel like a Doom game to me and Rage was average with an awful ending.
Then the marketing for Doom 2016 started and it seemed lackluster.
The E3 demo looked like a uninspired corridor shooter with canned and long finisher animations that take away the momentum from your movement. The public beta was only for multiplayer which felt like it didn't know if it was an old-school arena shooter or Halo.

So when the game was released I wasn't expecting much. Fortunately, it turned out to be one of the most pleasant surprises of 2016.

The Glory Kill finishers looked like they were there just to look cool for trailers in the pre-release footage but they turned out to be pivotal in some brilliant game design.
Low on health? Instead of hiding behind cover to regen health or scurrying off from combat to find medkits hidden behind some crevice, this game urges you to keep fighting. If you want health you need get close to the danger and kill some demons. You turn into an unstoppable force and RIP AND TEAR through everything.
Want a power fantasy? This game has it in spades.

Even the story is surprisingly well done which is pretty unexpected for a Doom game. If you don't care it won't get in your way. But if you do check the journals there's some well written lore there,

Add in a superb dynamic soundtrack that makes those special moments even better, excellent visuals with great optimization (id Tech 6 is significantly more matured than id 5) and you got probably my favorite shooter in a decade.

I don't often have particularly strong reactions with games but this one always makes me happy.
I see a screenshot, hear a snippet of the soundtrack or just talk about the game and it just brings a smile to my face and when I sit down and actually get to play it - it's pure bliss.

Thanks id, you have redeemed yourselves in my eyes in an absolutely gloríous way.\t\t\t\t\t
