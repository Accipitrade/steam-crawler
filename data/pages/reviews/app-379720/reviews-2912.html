\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy only problem while writing this review is that I could only give the game one thumbs up. I mean, there's literally no flaws here, and as anyone reading my reviews can attest, I'm a deeply critical person.

Performance? Amazing.
Graphics and design? Amazing.
Music? HOLY AMAZEBALLS.
Gameplay? Intense and awesome and excellent.

If I had to critique anything about this game, it's that I wasn't tired of playing it yet when the campaign ended, and that I'm miserably aching for some singleplayer DLC rather than all the MP DLC they've been cranking out. I need my ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ fix, Id Software. I need my ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ fix.

Well, okay, there's a bit more. The one thing I could nail down on as a genuine issue is the weapon selection. All of the weapons are useful, all of them are interesting, but there were some that I just found myself... not really using. In particular, the basic shotgun completely disappears from use once you have the super shotgun, but the super shotgun sadly lacks the interesting utility upgrades of the combat shotgun. The minigun also feels... out of place a little bit? It's a great weapon, but it seems more based around being stationary than being constantly mobile, or at least requires less cluttered combat arenas than what you're given.

Using the minigun feels like it would expose you to way more fire than the rocket launcher or super shotgun would.

Which is a shame, because I love miniguns in most games.

Uhhhhh... I guess I got a bit lost on the Foundry level this one time? And some of the levels with more backtracking sometimes have you running in circles a bit because you're not sure when you're going to arbitrarily unlock access to a collectible or rune challenge or something.

But seriously. GET THIS GAME. If you like running and jumping and mantling stuff while shooting things with fun guns, you will like it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this with the expectations of a overall great game, and I can confirm this is 100% true.

The singleplayer campaign is one of the most interesting experiences in a first-person shooter in recent years. Where games like Call of Duty abused the linear patriotic theme for far too long, seeing Doom nowadays is totally refreshing. It has the best of both worlds, incorporating classic design elements like keycards and no weapon reloading (save for the Super Shotgun) and modern features. The story is long enough to keep you interested in finishing the game.

SnapMap imo is a nice tool for creating new maps. It's pretty easy to use and the tutorials are extremely helpful if you don't know what to do. So far I've been trying a few user levels, even one that was literally 3D Pac-Man, it's amazing to see the levels of creativity unleashed with this tool.

Multiplayer does not actually deserve that much hate (remember the open beta?). Yes, it has some team balancing issues here and there and some DLC-locked content, but it can be pretty neat. It's even better with friends, so if you can find a friend lobby, you will have a lot of fun.

I believe this game is not just one of the best shooters of this year, but it also is one of the best FPS of all time.

Buy this game now, and make sure you RIP AND TEAR!!!
