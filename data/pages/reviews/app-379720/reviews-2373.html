\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou have not seen a true high octane action game until you play this masterpiece.


First, a pros and cons list:-

-Awesome Graphics
-Good Sountracks
-Superb Gameplay
-Decent Story + Lore
-Superb Optimization (playing Ultra on 1600x900 with a GTX 1050)

Cons:-

-Game length is short :((((


Overall Rating: 9/10


This game is the definition of what you want from an action game. The gameplay is fast, it is brutal (in nightmare), it is satisfying!!

The glory kill mechanic is an amazing innovation in action gaming and I hope to see more in the sequel.

My one and only gripe with the game is it's length. As somebody who plays games for the story (I played a little Arcade but I got bored of it), I would have loved more levels (even if they are just filler levels in the story). The original DOOM games had a ton of levels (like 36-40 if I remember correctly). The meagre 13 levels in this game feels lacking.

TLDR: Literally one of the best high paced action games available whose only flaw is it's length.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIF A DEMON'S HEAD ISN'T SPLIT APART BY YOUR BARE HANDS, DID YOU EVEN KILL HIM?

What a lovely reboot. What a lovely old school FPS updated for the current generation. What a lovely piece of media in every fathomable way. DOOM succeeds where most current day FPS miserably fail. It both brings the genre back to its roots while simultaneously innovating with bold new mechanics and a gameplay loop that is exciting, compelling and one might even say addicting. Two years prior to this Id did something similar with Wolfenstein: The New Order. Creating the best of a series with all the advantages of modern gaming, amazing graphics, interesting new mechanics and a general production value that simply wasn't achievable 15 years or not even 10 years prior. Now, while WTNO is certainly a masterpiece, DOOM kinda blows it out of the water in every sense.

The game looks and sounds incredible and I'm not only talking about graphical fidelity and good sound design and mixing either. No, what I'm talking about is the overall art direction of the piece. While high quality graphics are par for the course on any new release, getting better and better every year, truly beautiful design is something rarely achieved particularly in AAA games. Just think of COD as an example, better graphics every year but nothing particularly striking artwise. In DOOM though we have something truly beautiful. Let's consider the demon designs for example. They are strikingly updated while also maintaining the iconic looks of the first two games. Everything here is pretty much found in the original DOOM but now we get the chance to experience them as they should have always been. That was greatly missed chance with DOOM 3. While the graphics were impressive for the time the overall look was simply uninspired. These designs along with the somewhat varied enviroments, amazing lighting effects, imaginative kill animations and some of the best gore ever portrayed in gaming make the optical experience of DOOM a feast for the eyes, especially when your in the zone clearing a room of demons at break neck speed and watching the whole thing unfold like a well orchestrated music piece.

Speaking of music, oh boy what a soundtrack! This is probably my favorite soundtrack written specificaly for a game. It's one blasting metal tune after the other creating the ideal soundscape for slaughtering the hordes of hell and truly augments the experience. It keeps you tense and focused at all times with the suitable, if a bit underwhelming, game sounds being your paintbrushes to the canvas that is the sound of this game. The only complaint is that after about 15.5 hours that took me to finish the campaign I've pretty much heard every single sound about 1000 times and they simply didn't have the same effect the first hours of the game. Picking up a weapon for the first time and trying it though provides an acoustical satisfaction almost every time. The sounds are good but just not exciting enough to keep you engaged in contrast with the soundtrack. I also loved the voice acting of the four voice acted characters in the game. They simply found the perfect voices for all of them conveying their personality and boosting the bare bones plot in the process.

Story in a game is like a story in a porn movie. It's expected to be there, but it's not that important. John Carmack will be definetely pleased to know that his remark echoes in the reboot of his iconic magnum opus. The fact of the matter is that you can play DOOM without paying any attention to the story and you won't have a lesser experience and I don't think this is a bad thing. Still the plot is nice in the way that it services the game, giving motivation to the Doom Slayer while also finally constructing a very interesting and thorough lore around him. The other three characters are pretty much sci-fi cliches but the writting is spot on for everyone so they end up playing their parts perfectly while also surprisingly giving us quite a bit of humor through the VEGA AI. Point being, the story is simply gravy on the full course meal that is the game.

And what a nourishing and fully satisfying meal it is. A game like DOOM lives and dies on its gameplay and it truly is pitch perfect. It feels like an old school fps. The movement is fast as hell(pun inteneded), the guns have weight and are simply a joy to use, there is no regenerating health so you have to rely on pickups which always keeps you on your toes. That being said the developers did something innovative which is the true selling point of the game, that being the glory kills and the always moving forward combat. If you hit an enemy long enough they will get staggered and then you can finish them off with a glory kill which rewards you with health(and armor if you get an upgrade later on). This makes the game more accessible and most importantly super fun to play. You could have 20 points of health and with a single glory kill be launched back to the hundreds. It is a fun challenge and makes the game a lot more forgiving than say the original DOOM which is a great thing. Think of the game as a gateway drug to games like Quake, the original DOOM and Shadow Warrior.

Another great old school part of the game are the weapons. There are many weapons and while every one is useful you have to really mix and match during a fight for optimal performance. That being said, all weapons can be upgraded so almost any of them is pretty useful even in the later stages of the game if you have fully upgraded it. The upgrade options are also plentiful and you can have a general experience tailored to your absolute liking by having favorite weapons and choosing the best upgrades for you. The most important thing about the weapons though is that true to their roots they don't need to reload which again reinforces the non-stop action. A thing that was missing from previous DOOM games are the suit upgrades that you get. These can range from having more health, armor and ammo, to passive abilities and boost on all manners of things. Between the weapon upgrades and the suit upgrades it's a match made in heaven to customise your game style just enough to give you a good reason to replay tha campaign just so you can experience the game differently. In the later stages of the game your Slayer ends up being a truly OP beast and while in other games the protagonists are trapped in a room with the monsters, here the monsters are trapped in with you. That being said I would have liked for the boss fights to be more and harder. I played in normal difficulty but I beat almost every boss on my first try, even the final one.

To hit the old school trifecta all the aformentioned upgrades are either linked to good combat pefrormance but most importantly to exploration. Modern FPSs tend to be on rails experiences with levels feeling like corridors rather than mazes. Not in DOOM. In DOOM you have varied and well thought level design rife with secrets to unlock. These are mostly upgrades but there are also easter eggs that will unlock artwork, lore or original DOOM levels. It's really a whole part of the game trying to find every last one of them and you can get suit upgrades that make them easier to find, again making the game much more accessible than the FPS of yore. I believe that was the ultimate goal the DOOM had. To make the old school FPS genre relevant again and, in my opinion, it truly hit it out of the park.

Long story short GET THIS GAME! If you love FPS games you are missing on the best of all time. If you don't love FPS games this might be the best way to do so and open up a whole new world of gaming variety. DOOM is truly a unicorn, an overachieving game in every way. I didn't touch the multiplayer or the snap map tool(but the arcade mode is pretty sweet) on this review because I didn't try them but the campaign is more than enough reason to get this game, especially during the sales you can't go wrong with this one.\t\t\t\t\t
