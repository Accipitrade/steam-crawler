\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor anyone wanting a truly worthy successor to the original DOOM, this is it.
DOOM was quite clearly made with an enormous amount of talent and passion - as is apparent within the first 5 minutes of game play.

With extraordinarily fun and rewarding game play, exquisite attention to detail in art and animations, interesting, exploration-centric level design, high-quality music and sound effects, it's the DOOM we've all been waiting for.

I should mention that nowadays, I only make time for FPS games written as richly imagined narratives with discrete, start-to-finish story arcs - the original Biosock, or the Deus Ex series, for example. But I was pleasantly surprised by the quality of story in DOOM. It's a balance that seems quite difficult to get right for this kind of action game. Too much story and you run the risk of alienating your players with pretentious rhetoric. Too little story and your game may become aimless and trite. DOOM does an excellent job finding a middleground.

I highly recommend this game. And Cheers to everyone at id Software!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is quintessentially Doom as far as I'm concerned. The original Doom was my first FPS when I was a teenager and my true introduction into PC gaming and deathmatching. I remember when Doom 3 came out and being very excited, and while I enjoyed that game, it didn't hit me in the nostalgia quite so well as this iteration.

The game has a somewhat interesting story to it with only a couple of characters to keep track of. Being a reboot of the series, it has a whole new kind of origin story for the Doom Marine that is rather interesting. The best part is that he gives 0 ♥♥♥♥♥ and just destroys everything in his path, sometimes to the dismay of the character that is helping him fight the armies of hell. All the Doom Marine cares about is destroying the legions of hell in the most brutal and efficient ways possible and that's what's great about this game.

It's fast paced action, just about everything you feel like you'd need to see from the original games is here, from weapons to enemies to level design philosophy. It does contain some modern gameplay tropes like an equipment upgrade system but these mesh very well into the more classic gameplay elements of the original games. The glory kill system adds a new level of brutality to the combat that is quite welcome, and it isn't just cosmetic either. Glory killing an enemy causes it to drop health pickups, and these become very important in harder difficulties.

If you were a fan of the original Doom games, or even if you've never played them but like fast paced action shooters, this game is for you. It's gorgeous and gory, frantic and merciless. I was impressed with the whole presentation and look forward to more installments of this type to continue the Doom tradition.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game and so much fun. Been playing since the original DOOM was released. This "reboot" goes back to the basics that made this series become classic...just pure, intense, run & gun mayhem.

The look and "feel" of the new id Tech 6 engine is top notch. Ive always been a fan of id's engines....this one is a much improved version of Tech 5, which I thought was great to begin with anyhow.

The plot serves it's basic purpose...it isn't anything too deep or complicated, but keeps the game moving suficiently.

I enjoy playing multiplayer also. Like the original, its simple and fun. Customizable lodaouts and characters, etc. I actually like the fact that there isn't a million different weapon configurations. The area few different multiplayer games modes like Team Deathmatch, Soul Harvest, Domination, etc...

This is a solid game overall and I would recommend to anyone, especially those who really enjoy arena shooters and classic id games.
