\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a run-and-gun action-FPS game with platformer elements mixed in here and there. It reminds me of the older Half-Life games in many ways, but is different in that it lacks the strong story and interesting world lore that these games were known for.

While Doom has a story, it's essentially only good enough to perform the basic functions of a single player campaign story. It's not particularly memorable or interesting. But since nobody plays games like this for the story to begin with, that's acceptable in this case.

This version of Doom is an outstanding value for the price and gives you a lot of single player campaign gameplay for the money.

The soundtrack is a nice match for a retro (think: 1990's style) run-and-gun action-FPS platformer game like Doom that doesn't take itself too seriously.

I was pleased with the utter lack of annoying political content in this game. It provided a wonderful escape from the craziness of the modern world.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's nothing too new to talk about this game from 2016, but I was surprised at how forgiving it is. There are enough difficulty options so that even a baby could complete the game, and the upgrade system rewards exploration and combat prowess both, so you can ignore secrets and just play well to get your guns upgraded, or explore really well to make up for your poorer aim. Lots of options too in how to approach combat, with the Rune system complimenting however it is you want to play this game. If the game looking too hardcore for you was ever a worry, it shouldn't be

The one thing that annoys me is from a completionist standpoint, that some stuff just... didn't count, for whatever reason. Yeah, Steam, it sure makes sense I was able to upgrade all Runes from the single player without getting the achievement for unlocking them in the first place\t\t\t\t\t
