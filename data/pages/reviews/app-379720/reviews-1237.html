RIP & TEAR UNTIL IT'S DONE

This was the very first DOOM game I've ever touched. I never even touched the classics and this game is making me want to try them out. I did my first play-through on Ultra-Violence and oh boy was it rage inducing. The amount of enemies and bullet-hell I've had to go through kept my adrenaline and heart rate going at a fast rate, and I had to constantly keep moving quick. This game is so fast paced that I even fell off some stages multiple times and kept running into walls.
I'm definitely picking up Doom Eternal right after this review. For the price tag of $5.99 during this Steam Summer Sale was an absolute steal. I didn't know I missed out on such a bad-ass game until now, 4 years without playing DOOM 2016.

VISUALS

The graphics themselves were absolutely stunning. Even though it was created and published 4 years ago, they still hold up today, especially on Ultra Settings! When I first launched the game, it ran so smoothly than some other games and it looked completely beautiful. It seemed as if this game came out in 2020, that's how stunning the visuals are in this game! 10/10

If you're looking for a Casual experience, definitely don't go Ultra-Violence since it requires you to go 'tryhard' at times haha. Overall, this is one of the top games in my list, and now it probably will hold a special place in my heart and set a higher bar for upcoming 'DOOM' inspired games.
For $5.99 and hell, even for full price, I recommend picking this game up if you're in for a bullet-hell, gore-infested, demon-slaying adventure!


THE ONLY THING THEY FEAR IS... YOU...

DOOM 2016 is one hell of a jam packed game. The amount of content you get is amazing, and the replayability is beyond amazing. For a full price of $19.99 and sometimes even dropped down to $5.99 during sales, this game is worth it. It is a graphically stunning (even on LOW settings the game is beautiful!) demon bullet hell. Many aspects of the game has changed, with the addition of many new game mechanics added in for the player to intimidate and dodge the demon's attacks; all with keeping the same feeling of ripping&tearing demons to heavy metal music made by the beautiful Mick Gordon.

THE PROS

• Ultra fast paced FPS combat. You can't underestimate the pacing of this game, especially on the harder difficulties like Ultra-Violence / Nightmare / Ultra-Nightmare
• ALL ORIGINAL DOOM WEAPONS ARE HERE. All of the fan favorites such as the Super Shotgun to the BFG-9000 are here, with an addition of a couple revamps done on them.
• One of the most optimized games I've ever touched! No frame rate issues 'nor crashes ran into the game
• All of the controls are re-bindable, meaning you can adjust / customize them to your liking!
• Level design is amazing! Gives you a lot of opportunities to run around the map, dodge the bullet hell of demon attacks they throw at you, and collecting a bunch of power ups / health / armor to help keep you alive during combat.
• Almost ALL of the familiar enemies from past DOOM games are here, including the Pink, Caco-Demon, and more! With an addition of a couple of new ones that are kind of annoying to run into whilst doing a first run-through on this game.

THE CONS

There really isn't any cons for me to write, except:
• The campaign for this game would be roughly around 10 - 15 hours, which is alright.

THE FINAL VERDICT

The combat is very fun and addicting! Especially with a little mix of heavy metal by Mick Gordon, it feels like you're a complete badass! If you're considering of buying this game for the full price of $19.99, go for it. But I'd really recommend you to wait for a steep sale like $5.99 or even less.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this four years late to the party, but man... what a party this was!

At first I found the collectables a bit annoying, but you don't need most of them to get the important upgrades. I ended up getting everything anyway. I felt It detracted from the classic fps formula at first, but that faded quickly and I actually started having fun hunting them down.

I enjoyed the story as well, even though I didn't buy game for it, but I was pleasantly surprised despite knowing an endgame spoiler.

Performance is great using the Vulkan API and stability was pretty good, I've only had 3 crashes in 35 hours and one of them was probably my fault for rapidly alt-tabbing during loading.

The SnapMap part of it was interesting. I played one or two pretty straight forward campaigns, hopefully there are more interesting ones out there. It reminded me slightly of L4D2's custom maps. I said slightly because L4D2's custom content scene is probably much larger and varied.

Playing multiplayer feels like a good arena shooter, not sure how balanced it is, since I haven't played too much of it, but even without it, the game is worth it.
Especially now (1 July 2020) since it's on sale (-70%).

12 August 2020 Edit:

I played more multiplayer, it was very fun, and to my surprise... still alive, even though if you play frequently enough you start to notice that you're playing with and against the same rather small pool of players in the Team Play mode, however I rarely had to wait for players for a match to start.
The opposite is true for the Free For All mode, which is essentially dead as far as I've seen, every time I tried to play it, the lobby wouldn't start because you need 4 players to start and the most I've seen were 3 total players.

There doesn't seem to be a kick option, so if you get a cheater in your match, you have to either put up with it or leave the match. And depending on the time you get one in your game, if you requeue, chances are you'll be put back in the server you just left, having no other options than to either wait or change the server region setting.
The game feels decent playing from Eastern Europe on North American and European servers. I haven't tried the other regions.

Thankfully, I've only seen one speed "hacker" thus far.

Also there doesn't seem to be any autobalance system, so if no new player connect you're stuck in 1 vs, 4 scenarios etc..

The gameplay itself is nice, your equipment, runes and weapons being limited until you get to level 50 the first time (you've got a prestige/echelon system, but you keep everything as you progress).

Leveling is pretty fast, especially if you do well in matches.
Unlocking cosmetics is a chore, sadly, especially if you want to get all the achievements, two of them being particulary grindy.
The achievements in question are "Computing with Style" and "Beauty is Pain", requiring you to get Echelelon V and VIII, respectively, and then doing some challenges that only become accessible when you've reached those to levels. Even if you meet the criteria before, it won't count.
Alternatively, you have to reach higher levels if you don't complete the challenges, the last one requiring Echelon X.
The challenges aren't very difficult and I've done both soon after I've reached the minimum required level.\t\t\t\t\t
