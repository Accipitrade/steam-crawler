\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAllot of people will disagree with me in this statement but I honestly believe this game is really boring. Which is a shame seeing as the graphics are amazing, creativity is there, there exists the making of a great and exciting game to play but this isn't. After a few hours in my opinion there is little fun to be had in moving stage to stage in a level killing waves of zombies. There is not enough after a few hours to keep it interesting, in titles like Bioshock you had vigors/plasmids that made the wave more exciting and creative. Here in Doom there lacks that creativity instead the developers just throw in powerups like Haste, 4x damage, berserk, which have there moments but ultimately don't freshen it up. Allot of the guns feel as though they fall off and go from being balanced and effective to killing enemies to just a peashooter, IE the combat shotgun, plasma gun, etc. This isn't a bad game IMO it just lacks ideas when it comes to the final few level missions, it had felt like I had done it all and that there wasn't much for me to do in terms of creativity in killing of these waves, only the rooms I were fighting them in were new not the ways in which I could kill them. Ultimately I think this game had allot of potential but just ends up being a snooze fest. Yeah the glory kills were cool but after doing them so often and having them be an integral part of the gameplay the effect just wears off.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort and sweet: If you like a single player campaign focused game with constant moving and shooting enemies, interaction animations with buttons and levers, and "cutscenes" that are few and far between with the longest clocking in at 5 minutes, this just might be the game for you.

Sure, the ratio of arena fights compared to killing enemies you come up along the way is a bit too skewed on the arena side. Sure, there should be a few more old school mazelike wandering levels to balance out the more linear ones. Sure the challenges can slow down your pace in the levels when trying to complete them. Yet, despite all this, this is by far the most solid single player campaign in the past 10 years.

Oh, there's also multiplayer arena (that appears to have a somewhat low population) and community made snap maps if you're into that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of, if not the tightest shooter I've ever played, and one that knows exactly what it is: A game about destroying demons. There's no bull. The game opens with a thirty-second cutscene of you grabbing a demon by its eye socket and slamming it into a stone altar so hard its head explodes, finding a gun three seconds later, and beginning the gameplay. You don't sprint, you just move insanely fast by default. You don't sit around and regen health. You don't even have to reload. It's just 100% violence and satisfaction.

Finding new weapons is joyous, because each one is dramatically different and hilariously powerful. Modding them is even better, as almost all have two available mods which do ridiculous stuff. The shotgun, for example, can have either a three-round burst or grenade launcher, which you can freely swap between at any time.

It's hard to put into words why killing things in this game feels good. It's just so beautifully destructive. Shotgunning an imp doesn't make it keel over, it liquidates its entire torso. It's very hard to actually defeat an enemy without dismembering it in some comically violent way, and it's something no other game has gotten right in being at the same time gritty and darkly humourous. And yes, there is a rocket launcher, and yes, it does completely destroy things.

Glory kills reward you with health for beating the ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• out of weakened demons with your bare hands. You're rewarded with ammo instead for doing it with a chainsaw. The game gives you many creative and badass ways to destroy the myriad of enemies, which include cacodemons (Giant floating orbs), hell knights (Ten-feet-tall bodyslamming machines) and the Possessed, which are incredibly slow, weak and exist only for the entertainment of destroying them.

I could waffle about this for hours, but the point is, nothing's ever going to be quite like Doom. It's that flavour of wiping out the hordes of hell with super powerful guns and/or your fists that no game since the first Doom has ever been able to match - and this masterpiece brings that feeling to the modern generation. And it feels pretty good.\t\t\t\t\t
