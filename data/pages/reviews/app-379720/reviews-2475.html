\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou can never go home again, but I guess you can shop there

SCORE: 6 out of 10

PRO: Gives you much more DOOM than DOOM 3 - they right many of the wrongs by going back to source

CON: they let you down by some of the monsters and weapons not having the devastation dynamic you recall

CON: story and text elements here are a grind and singularly unrewarding

PRO: the Half-Life style irony is welcome, if unoriginal

CON: the music is unmemorable... the original DOOM/2 game music is burned into our souls

CON: difficulty and pickup levels are all over the place - no consistency to the challenge

PRO: the finishing moves are not QTE and actually do work, though can be exploited to break the flow of dangerous crowds, which is a bit of a cheat

CON: the classic levels are not integrated into the main campaign beyond a pickup room and the classic levels with the new weapons and new monsters isn't satisfying. In The Old Blood you play these side missions in-campaign and fight the classic enemies with the new weapons. That works much better, this aspect of DOOM is a fail

PRO: the Vulkan optimisation with Nvidia really can aid performance and is well worth doing

BOTTOM LINE: if, like me, you waited for a sale to get it and then even longer to bother installing it, do play it - when it's fun, it's really fun. But it's all referential, they've not reinvented the wheel here and it's not a remotely deep experience. If you want solid single player arena FPS, this delivers a good few hours of that. Not desperate for a sequel.\t\t\t\t\t
