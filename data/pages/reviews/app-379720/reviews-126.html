\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - 8/10 - Very enjoyable first person shooter. Loved defeating the forces of Hell unleashed by Kyle's mom.

And now the longer review which covers the single player experience and nothing else...

STORY
A group of scientists at the insistent urging of Kyle's mom and Not Optimus Prime decide to open a portal to Hell with only the best of intentions: to harvest Hell's resources for money, power, and the sheer sexual thrill of it. Then Kyle's mom spoils it for everyone because she is... Kyle's mom. Not Optimus Prime, believing in the mantra of working smarter, not harder, decides to outsource the cleanup of the cluster***k Kyle's mom created to a professional cleanup crew consisting of one muscled up guy they found locked in a sarcophagus in Hell. Because he's non-union and technically an illegal immigrant to Mars, Not Optimus exploits him for free janitorial maintenance with those futuristic mops and buckets we refer to colloquially as, "guns."

This game takes the "scientists playing God" trope and runs with it, and then doesn't stop running. It's pretty much satire how f***ing insane these UAC nutbags are, and I enjoyed reading all the logs and lore bits. I especially enjoyed the very corporate, politically correct, company first speeches which companies in real life use and the application of that style to some the most horrifying things you can imagine. In this case, opening a literal portal to Hell, which make no mistake, Elon Musk WOULD do if he wasn't so busy f***ing up Twitter.

GAMEPLAY
Encourages you to play aggressively and be as violent as you can possibly be. The glory kills are exactly as brutal as you want them to be, my personal favorite being ripping out the fat demon's stomach and shoving it down his throat. 10/10 fatality. Customization options in game are gained with points rewarded for killing more things than you have to, or, you know, playing aggressively, which is what I just said. They're also rewarded for exploring and finding secret areas, which provides a welcome break from the intensity of being locked in a large room for 5 minutes trying to survive while simultaneously killing hordes of demons spawning around you. Except when it doesn't. Backtracking and looking for secrets in the level can be a bit tedious at times, and it REALLY annoyed me when the game locked off the level behind me (first level in Hell, I'm looking at you) when I wanted to go back to try to find secrets that were locked off earlier. But, that side stuff is optional. Unless you want to upgrade your guns and find runes and upgrade your armor to enhance the experience, in which case, you should absolutely be looking for all the hidden things. The hidden classic Doom maps were a nice touch, though I haven't actually played the original Doom games, so some of the nostalgia is lost on me.

GLITCHES
I only really experienced two relatively minor glitches. The first was: occasionally when killing enemies they continued to make death animation noises until their corpses disappeared in a puff of smoke that I imagine smelled like burning tofu. The second was that every time I picked up a new log entry in the later levels, the logs for Titan's Realm and Necropolis would appear as "unread." Neither of these affected my enjoyment of the game. They were just weird little quirks. I didn't experience any game-breaking crashes or serious issues, though on systems with less optimal graphics cards I know the game slows down and freezes to the point of unplayability. So make sure you have plenty of RAM, and good processor, and a good graphics card.

FINAL THOUGHTS
The story and gameplay are exactly as brutal and ridiculous as you want them to be in a Doom game. The story isn't groundbreaking by any means, but it was very fun to play. Strongly recommend the single player with an 8/10. I personally played it on "hurt me plenty" which I found offered just enough challenge to keep it interesting on my first playthrough without feeling like i was being slammed repeatedly in the d***. There's also apparently a "mega being slammed in the d***" mode which offers even more masochism than "being slammed in the d***" mode. If you're into that sort of thing. There's also a multiplayer which I haven't bothered with. Maybe I'll try that at some point. (But probably not.)\t\t\t\t\t
