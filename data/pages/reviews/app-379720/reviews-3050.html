\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a first person shooter which for all of you who aren't hardcore gamers like I am means that you control the character and watch him from the front as he starts to murderously gun down everything in his path. The original game was said to have instigated violence in real life and I'm somewhat nervous that some real life shooter will copy the violence in this game because its highly realistic. However because I enjoy highly violent video games I ignored that fact and enjoyed this entire game.

Now onto the review. This game is more intense than duck hunt that should give you an idea of how blazingly fast you can gun down anything in your path which is also a means of solving real world problems as the makers of this game make very clear. One thing that is so great about this game is that you can murder various demons and monsters and send them straight to hell and then actually teleport to hell yourself and kill them again. This was one of the only parts that 'jumped the shark' and became slightly less believable. Doom has highly photo-realistic graphics and effects, I just pray some spree shooter is not carefully studying this game to plot his evil violence.

The objective of this game is straightforward so that even a liberal can sort of barely follow along: You control a marine who has to violently gun down any creature who crosses his path. Thats the plot.

This game is fun as balls and I'll be a witches moose knuckle if it isn't my favorite shooter to come out over the past 3 to 5 years.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot a bad effort and certainly an improvement on Doom 3, and dispite having the look and the layout of the original series, the feel of the game lacks any real punch. Couple this with how you always feel underpowered and meek, dispite constantly being reminded of how you're a super powered, badass space marine, it really makes the game feel lack luster in its attempts to make the player feel like a super powered, badass space marine. The weapons have no oomf and you constantly strive to find that feeling of awesomeness you got from the shotgun and other weapons of the original. They all feel so under powered and blank, especially when you're shooting the same Imp 6 times to kill it. You have to be standing right up in the guys face to make any real impact and even then, it feels hollow.

In short, it looks like the original and is layed out like the original, but does not have the same feeling. Where in Ultimate Doom the weapons feel like an 11, this remake, the weapons feel like a 4, and the glory kills get quite old after the first handful.

The levels look great and layed out in a semi-liner fashion at first, but once you get into the game properly, it really does seem like you're playing Doom. With exploration and free movement, hunting for keys and secrets, the meat of what made Doom the great game of the centuries are all there. Right down to the blood soaked floors and human offerings. Some of the deamon reskins feel wrong with what they looked like at the start, but you get over it in no time. After all, all deamons look the same when they are gibed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPicked this up on sale, half off. Totally worth it. If you like classic Doom, but modern graphics, totally worth it at full price. Some people have complained that it's short....it's like any other Bethesda title. It's as short as you want it to be. You can blow thru Fallout 3 in 2 hours apparently, but most people spend 30-40 hours. Same deal here. For me, I'm only a couple levels in and already 5 hours played. I expect to have 20+ hours by the end of my first playthrough which puts it on par with Crysis (OG Crysis, one of the best shooters ever).

Edit: having said that, I am now 20 hours in and still not done, got 2.5 levels to go. In earlier levels I was being a completionist, but the further you go the larger the levels get, so I kinda lost that. Now I'll have to go back to get all the trinkets, weapon upgrades and whatnot, which means more game time. The people saying it's short simply aren't playing the game.

As for action, I can't remember a PvE campaign with such fast paced action. It's thrilling run and gun gameplay, no cover, no puzzles, just keep moving and shoot. Also, smash, melee is prominent and soooo satisfying. The more aggressive you are, the better things tend to go. This isn't a shooter for the patient sniper or tactical genius. It's run, smash, get soaked in blood and hope you don't run out of ammo awesome!!!! (But if you do, there's always the chainsaw....heh heh heh, /EvilDoomGrin)

There are also lots of extra content modes which add to the value. Multiplayer (havent tried it yet), Arcade just released (is fun once you've done the campaign), classic Doom maps to unlock and play (weird but fun), built in map editor and custom built player content (also havent tried yet). So, lots more than just the campaign and I think the campaign alone is worth all the demon skulls you can gather.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never really sat down and wrote a review, I'll keep it short. DOOM was one of the first games I played when I was a kid (thanks Dad, for scarring me) and this is definitely worthy of the DOOM name.If you have a powerful PC and even relatively enjoy the FPS genre , get this game. With it being 50% off there's really no excuse not to. The melee is extremely satisfying. I have this arsenal of future weapons and rocket launchers, but for the most part I find myself beating down the enemies with my bare hands. It's amazing. The combat is fast-paced, but not intimidating for newcomers to the genre. Unfortunately the online scene takes longer to find matches than I would like, that's to be expected for its until-now $60 price point and high system requirements.

So I'll wrap it up like this, if you've got $30 to spare and a decent PC don't pass this deal up. I was on the fence about it, but Betehesda really pulled through on this one. I'm going to go fist some demons now. Ciao.\t\t\t\t\t
