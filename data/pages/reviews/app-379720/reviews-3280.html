\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy gawd! If I ever have to blow my frustration out, i would have to play Doom everytime! This game plays so well and you feel so powerful when you land your punch in every demons's face, ESPECIALLY in Berserk mode! I had a mix of uncontrollable laugh and a dopamine rush with full of excitment when I used that mode for the first time.

Most guns in the game have their uses, so i don't feel like i'm using the same gun over and over. With the weapon upgrade system in place, I was surprise by the variations you have in your arsenal. Moreover, it doesn't feel like a weak addition to the weapon system. It adds a depth of tacticality in the combats.

The music fits well in this gory mess of a game, and it keeps your focus on whats ahead; it is paced with the action on screen and complement the atmosphere sometimes.

While i like following stories in most games, i'm glad they focus more on the actual action rather than making long cutscene, which could've hurt the experience. As i said earlier, this game is well paced.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is impressive. It's not intricate, doesn't bring a rich story (Though I am very intrigued by it), and doesn't have beautiful cutscenes, but the most important part is there: Gameplay. And reviewing by the gameplay alone, it's amazing. I keep coming back just because the battles feel so epic, they keep getting harder, but still manages to stay incredibly fun.

As far as the technical side of it, it's got beautiful graphics and plays much better than I would've expected from my machine. Not saying the graphics are groundbreaking, but they look damn nice and you can tell it's well optimized. The gameplay is fast and brutal, you could be doing fine until you get caught on a corner of a wall for a second, and the whole fight could turn on you.

While I grew up on Duke 3D, and have played Doom and Doom II (On emulator) I can say that of all the games I've played with a weapon wheel, I don't think I've ever managed to use every single one of them on a normal basis like this. Add in the BFG and chainsaw, and that's a lot of tools to switch between, and the game still gets me to happily swap them constantly.

The campaign is fun in that it does have the older feel of those games, but it's not a game stuck in the past. Still got secrets, but while they're challenging to find, they don't make you pause the game to hump a wall for 10 minutes and bring the pacing to a brutal stop. The powerups are all helpful and can be used sort of strategically. Originally when I saw the cyberdemon in the new one, it reminded me of too many other boss fights like it that are simply too easy, but Doom gave me a solid difficulty curve that was neither too steep nor too shallow.

Snapmaps, the map-making mode, and quite fun (depending on who made it of course) especially in co-op, and multiplayer feels like it should be fun but I could not personally enjoy it. Maybe I'm just no good at it.

All in all, I would definitely recommend this title for anyone that likes a lot of action in their games. There's not much else to it, but it surely won't bore you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic game. The campaign is a faithful love letter to the originals, while still being innovative. The mobility coupled with the high pressure combat means that not only do you always have to keep moving, but you always have lots of interesting options to keep the iniative. Keeping the iniative is key when you're trying to slay 20 demons at once.

The levels are expansive, and reminiscent of the original levels, with high level doors exposed early, and making backtracking necessary (and full of nasty surprises). And then there are the secrets, and the easter eggs.

It's basically a perfect DOOM game.\t\t\t\t\t
