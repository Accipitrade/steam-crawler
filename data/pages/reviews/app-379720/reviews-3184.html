\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDude ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Doom is a pleasure to play. To quote Penny Arcade, "it's like playing sugar." I cannot gush about this game enough. It's just ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ impressive. It's a masterpiece. Never again will I say that id is an engine developer only, and here's why:

What Doom lacks in story and context, it MORE than makes up for in sheer brutal, spectacular, horribly gratuitiously gory combat. Doom takes everything that was beloved from the original game and just made it better in every single possible way. Loadsaguns? Check. Disgusting gory demons? Check. Awful, violent, gut-wrenching deaths done quickly, efficiently, mercilessly, and satisfyingly for every single one of those demons? Absolutely ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ check. Doom's combat is the most beautiful single player combat experience I've had in a long, long while.

The concern initially going in was "will it replicate the original game's frantic, non-stop energy of combat, or will it 'move with the times' and ♥♥♥♥ everything up by slowing down combat with reloading, regenerating health, and heavy use of cover?" Well all concerns were immediately allayed when one of the very first things Doom told me to do was to rip the heart out of a disgusting pile of gore in an arena-chamber and then kill everything inside using just the weapons and items found within the chamber. From there on, the game would throw me into arena after arena with plenty of exploration in-between, with the fighting accompanied by a beautiful metal soundtrack that fits the gameplay like a screeching glove.

Despite the frantic nature of Doom's combat however, it maintains a relaxed, unrushed atmosphere. Upon clearing an arena, the option of looking back over the map and checking for secrets is absolutely there and absolutely rewarded with optional collectible items, levels, and weapon/armor upgrades. Additionally, hidden within the map menu is a dossier system for *literally everything* in the style of Far Cry, except not boring and ♥♥♥♥♥♥. The entries are exactly as long as they need to be, and don't take any more time to read than the demons take to kill, which is exactly how it should be.

As a closing point, during some of the hell bits there are sections where you can hear the testament of a demon who survived a fight with the Doom Marine centuries prior, and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥* is it satisfying to hear a demon speak about the player character in reverent tones, filled with pure terror. Like ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, gives me chills bruh. Overall the game's amazing. I love it to death and highly recommend it to any fans of the shooter genre. Or fans of killing demons.\t\t\t\t\t
