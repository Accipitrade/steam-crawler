\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was very surprised by DOOM. In general I was unsure what to expect, but once I fired it up, started on a bed and within a few seconds obliterated the head of a nearby zombie, only to watch an introduction to what was going on on a 3D GUI monitor, only to be cut off right then and there by the protagonist smashing the monitor, I knew I made the right choice.

The gameplay is extremely solid, fluid, and fun. The glory kills are hilarious - which other game allows you to end a demon's life by smashing his face in with his own leg??

I was then hooked, played the singleplayer campaign through from start to finish, enjoying every second, looking forward to every combat area. This all while listening to Mick Gordon's epic musical talents, as part of the game.

I have not tried multiplayer yet, truth be told, but I was mainly in for the singleplayer aspect anyway. In this regard I can whole-heartedly recommend this fantastic game. Every glory kill animation, every story sequence, every weapon, every level - everything makes it clear the developers must have had a ton of fun creating this polished title.

As a developer myself I can really appreciate the effort and tech that went into this shining gem. To the developers at ID and Bethesda - Thank you for the many hours of laughs, fun, adrenaline, and exploration.

A must play!\t\t\t\t\t

Review: DOOM ( 2016 )
  • Graphics:
    The Game looks simply amazing and if you would ask me its one of the best current games graphics wise. Textures look sharp with amazing level design. Its huge that they were able to capture the original DOOM feeling with current gen graphics. The Characters / Enemies all look great and even better in glory kills ( close combat ). Overall i do not have anything else to add.
    []

  • Gameplay:
    The Game is built like an older game which sometimes can go wrong but not with this one. No Regenerating health, no quest marks and a decent challange creates an amazing experience. Its one of the Games the more you play them the more you "need" them. Aiming is smooth as is movement and enemies are not easy to kill or immpossible to kill which is just flawless. Soundtrack is a straight A too.
    []

  • Story:
    There will be no spoilers or not any major ones.
    To be honest its basically a classic DOOM story, doom guy comes to earth or hell and kills everything, the end.
    Might not sound amazing or life changing but you really do not play DOOM games for the Story.
    Still the Story keeps you entertained.
    []

  • Performance:
    The Game runs easily on High End PCs and Runs Great on the Nvidia GTX 760-AMD FX6300 so mid range PC will be able to play it easily. Probably doesnt run on Low End Laptops.

  • Multiplayer:
    It is still active and in my opinion i had a lot of fun playing it.

  • Price:
    The Game is extremely underrated for its price on key sides or on sales which is great for us.

    I Just have to Recommend this game and have no idea why it did not win GOTY 2016

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSingleplayer Gameplay -- 8/10. Solid score.

Multiplayer Gameplay 2/10. Absolutely atrocious.


Singleplayer review: Now, I'll make it a point to mention that this is where 95% of the time spent developing was. This campaign is very polished for any standards, and engine limitations. The singleplayer campaign is also one of the most brutal and engaging campaigns that I've ever set upon. Revived and ready to kill, you set out to brutally murder every demonic entity that crosses your path. That being said, the glory kills were a neat way to regain health and add finishers, but after the first hundred times of punching a Cacodemon in the eyeball, it got more routine than remaining a fun option, especially at higher difficulties. The powerup system feels pretty cool, even if the only must-get powerups are Quad Damage and Bezerker. The upgrade system is pretty neat as well, but it feels like there is a heavy lean towards one upgrade path over the other. However, even with the cool upgrades, powerups, collectables and gory campaign, the game begins to feel stale very quickly. Unfortunately this is unavoidable with the developer, but putting that aside, a majority of the gameplay revolves around removing demonic presence (a fancy way of saying "go here, kill this") by destroying Demonic Altars. When you do this, a large horde of demons spawns and you have to cleanse the area by hacking, slashing, and shooting your way through the hellspawn spawned by the altar. This might not seem too bad from a first look, but every level has at least one of these altars. The combat becomes tiresome, as I had breifly mentioned before. Lots of the time you're just sidestrafing, jumping, and emptying magazines into the larger baddies (sometimes with rockets too), only to finally have them glow that oh-so-sweet amber color.... And then stamp their skull into the floor, solely to get back that health you lost. It quickly becomes a bullet Hell once you reach the Hell maps, dodging projectiles from Imps and Cacodemons, with the occasional Mancubus. That to me is just a poor way to make gameplay more challanging, by adding a bunch of different objects you must avoid while focusing down an enemy mini-boss.

Single Player TL;DR: Repetative but still entertaining.


Multiplayer: Where do I begin on this one? The arena mode matches players of varying skill levels (yes, there is a new player MM, but it takes upwards of 10 minutes to find a game, if at all), the most iconic weapon (supershotgun) is nerfed to the point that it's absolutely laughable, the higher-level unlocks decimate (Looking at you, static rifle), and it's an overall frusterating experience for new players. The Rocket launcher even sucks ass, taking 2-3 direct hits to secure a kill. Where the â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥ is the promised "fast-paced arena brawling"? If I wanted to play with nerf guns, there are blasters cheaper than $60.00 sold at Wal-mart. I do have to give the game kudos for the characters though, the customization (albiet sort of limited) is a nice way to change up your character in multiplayer. However, that doesn't affect anything gameplay wise, and can easily be overlooked. Next thing I want to bring up are Demons. These things are incredibly powerful, and once a team secures one on a mode like Team Deathmatch, you can kiss that game goodbye. The Revenant's rocket launchers are what the stock Rocket Launcher should be; Giant bazookas of death and destruction. There's about 10 choices (don't quote me on that) of demons in the loadout menu, each powerful in their own regard. Some have high health, some don't, but as soon as you get that rune, the other team (or players) have to watch their asses and plan their shots.

Multiplayer TL;DR: Gameplay sucks â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥, guns are pretty much jokes, and to call it a "fast paced arena shooter" is a serious mistake.


Conclusion? Buy it when it's on sale for the Singleplayer story, and forget that multiplayer exists. If you're lucky, you can speedrun the game in under an hour and get your money back for what feels like a half-finished game.\t\t\t\t\t

I'm kinda glad I didn't. This was such a great surpise.

What a ride! Insanely satisfying game to play, unexpectedly interesting story to it too! Simplistic yet it knows what it is and delivers. Gameplay is fluid, story delivery does not get in the way of said gameplay, and the pacing is juuuuuust right. The game knows when you need a breather, and when to tie you down. Masterful game design.

Game looks f**ing gorgeous too. Sounds badass as well.

Only downside? I WANT MORE! That c**ktease ending, my god!

id Software, make more of this and you'll have all my money.

I didn't try the MP, and don't really plan to, but the coop stuff seems interesting. Still I doupt ill meddle with that too much.

SP - 20/10, would slaughter the hordes of hell again.

Buy this game if you like having fun.\t\t\t\t\t
