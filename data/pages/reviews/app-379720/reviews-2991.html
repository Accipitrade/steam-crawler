\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGiven the early press which focused on the uninspiring multiplayer, I wasn't expecting this game to be very good, but it turned out to have a solid singleplayer campaign, with a lot more vertical movement and jumping puzzles than older Doom games and a focus on set arena fights over clearing out pockets of enemies or ambushes. There are secrets, powerups, and upgrades to find and use, some of which are nods to classic games while others are just plain entertaining to discover. The game also seems to want to be placed on sale quite often, and if you aren't squeamish and are prepared to shoot, punch, and chainsaw demons and explode them into item pinatas, I can't recommend the game enough if it's on discount.

Well, I do have to say the game is fairly demanding and is prompting a refresh of my years-old computer, so while it does have large vistas of alien planets, hellish chasms and tech bases it can push older hardware to their limits. It is also pretty annoying waiting for the game to reload if you die.

Overall though, considering the amount of saturation the genre has had over the last decade, it's almost refreshing how well the developers managed to create something that not only held true to many of the design aspects of past games but managed to make a game that feels great to play even now.\t\t\t\t\t
