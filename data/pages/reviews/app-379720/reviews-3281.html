\r\n\t\t\t\t\t===SINGLE PLAYER REVIEW ONLY===

So, you wana play Doom, eh? You wana play a good game? You're in luck. When they said the levels were linear, they lied. I got lost for a bit on level two. If that level is representative of the whole product, the level design is on par with the original. When they said it was slow, they lied. DoomGuy isn't as fast as the original, but he's fast. The weapon switch wheel is optional console garbage, and the infamous glory kills are decent speed.

Doom's all about the quick and dirty, so instead of writing a retrospective, I'll bombard you with bullet points, and I won't skimp on the negative aspects of the game.

The Good:
* Modern FPS hand holding is 100% optional. Compass markers, tutorials, "press 'E' here" shimmer, HUD pop-ups. You can turn it all off in the option menu.
* Level design is top notch. Don't be fooled by the tutorial level, it's not all halls and boxes. Looks like Metroid Prime.
* Like wise, the devs seem to have taken "Show, don't tell" to heart when designing levels around game mechanics.
* Almost no waist high barriers. It's all fast action strafing and pillars, just like the original. More verticality though.
* Varied player death animations, gore effects, and Glory Kill animations.
* Weapon upgrades are simple, yet useful and fun.
* I heard the game had an auto-aim feature. If it does, I couldn't feel it.
* The game is self aware. It knows what made the original good and seeks to emulate/expand it. It also knows it can never top the original, so it isn't afraid to get creative.
* The choice in music is excelent. Industrial metal, I think it was called? Very nice.
* Doom loves its secrets, and it isn't afraid to rub it in your face.
* Also, a lot of levers and computers lie around that look interactable, but aren't. Very cheeky devs, very cheeky.
* Half-Life-esque aproach to story. The only cutscene I saw was the 8 second intro. Backstory delegated to logs.
* On that note, it's better than Half-Life. No bunny hopping around waiting for people to shut-up.
* RIP AND TEAR

The Bad:
* You need a good "gaming" computer to run it. I bought a GTX960 GPU just for the occasion. Now I find out my CPU is bottle-necking it anyways. Overall I spent 350$ just to play the game.
* That awesome kickass music it has only comes in during combat. Why.
* Also, I kind of miss the knock-off Metalica MIDI's, but this is purely objective.
* Corpses fade. That's fine and all, but it could at least leave piles of gore or something. Backtracking feels empty because of the corpse fade.
* The game has a small over-reliance on arenas. A few more demons bursting out of vents or doors would be nice.
* Demon palettes are not as varied as they should. You have to look for the baddies movement, not their color.
* Glory Kill pinatas are fine, but the Chainsaw pinatas are overkill. So much ammo, my FPS dropped a few frames.

The Ugly:
* An environmental bug forced me to reload to a previous checkpoint. Not cool, Doom.
* It may just be me, but there shouldn't be 90 second load times for respawning/reloading.
* The "Glory Kill" mechanic necessitated the removal of the "fist" weapon. They replaced it with a hearty shove action. It may do good damage, but I can't tell. I almost never used it because it looked and felt so damn weak.
* The biggest sin of all, the Chainsaw weapon sucks, hard. It guzzels fuel (its ammo) on each kill. It also drops so much ammo on kill, it just looks ugly how it all spills out. It kills the mood. Also, the ammo-on-kill reward seems stupid. The bloody mess of a dead demon should be its own reward. God knows I lost so much health in the original just to hear that chainsaw hit demon flesh again. Its animation takes way too long and locks you in place. It's like 4x longer than a Glory Kill. In fact, I would classify the Chainsaw as an over-glorified Glory Kill, and not an actual weapon. Gone are the days where you could mow down zombie hordes 12 strong in just 20 second. Thank the Lord that one ♥♥♥♥♥ melee weapon a bad game does not make.

Over all, I would recommend this game to everyone who likes bloody fun, even the hardcore Doom fans. Doom 2016 is to Doom 2 as XCOM2 is to XCOM. An awesome sequal in the franchise that manages to be good, but not completely outshine the original, giving us TWO good games to love instead of one strict improvement.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI highly recommend this game. I have been playing Doom since I could get my hands on it, back in the 90's. This game is just a continuation of a great series. Doom 3, with as much flak as it had received, was still a great game. This is much different than that game and more like Unreal Tournament meets original DOOM. Original Doom was fast paced during combat and online play was much like an early version of Unreal Tournament.

This game, though, brings back the oldie. It brings it back with a hardcore feel to it. It's so badass that when you play it, it'll make you feel like you're the one doing all the hell-slaying instead of Doom Guy. The games single-player campaign is so satisfying that you may actually feel disappointed by the multi-player version. If you were to compare the gameplay of single-player to that of multi... You'll soon realize it's not the same game. It will take time to get use to how the multi-player engages you. It's actually a little slower than the single-player and has no music. You end up playing most of the matches with people who played during Beta (and thus are much better than you).

If the multi-player could be improved, it would be to have a team-horde mode or to have hell beasts vs. doom marines. It doesn't have the feel of "♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, I'm going to die". Rather, it has the feel of "if I round the corner, another person playing this is going to kill me, so I'll wait her-- oh ♥♥♥♥ he came from behind me and killed me".

Single player 10/10
Multi player 7/10 -> Could endure a lot of improvement and extra content
Snap map -> ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ it's awesome/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 30 hours in the game, completing the Campaign, Getting Echelon 1 in Multiplayer, and going through the SnapMap tutorials and beginning to design a few of my own maps, I can without a doubt recommend this game to any FPS fan, DOOM fan, or anyone looking for an incredibly polished and exciting ride for a video game.

The single player is fast, action-packed, pretty straight forward, but as with all the original DOOM games, this one is about the moment to moment and frantic encounters when you step foot into a room. I loved 99% of the single player and would have bought the game just for the single player being a huge DOOM fan. 10/10

The multiplayer is the weakest point of the game, and it STILL is very fun. People come into a game series with preconceived notions that a multiplayer should do what other games that have done well should do. Well the DOOM multiplayer does this to a degree, but what it does well is cherry pick some aspects from Halo, COD, and Quake to form a mismatch of strangely-paced, frantic, and often times frustrating swirl of fun. If you don't get hung up on your idea of it should have this, this, or that. It will be fun, it's a traditional Arena FPS with TDM, Warpath (moving king of the hill), Domination, and Soul Harvest (stockpile hybrid from Halo). Overall, not as bad as many make it out to be.

SnapMap! SnapMap is awesome. Close your eyes and imagine a game mode or map you'd like to have in DOOM. Whether it's a player vs. monsters encounter. Maybe it's a Co-op experience vs. monsters, or even a cool CTF map you've always envisioned. It gives you the tools from the ground up to create, edit, and publish your own maps and campaigns how YOU want them. It gives you a lot of conditional tools and player use stuff from cool mechanics. Examples of this are: DOOM beat pad and piano map, Original DOOM map remakes, Tower Defense Maps, and the list can go infinitely on.

Overall, the gameplay and gunplay especially for DOOM is what makes DOOM, well, DOOM. And personally, I think they nailed that on the head. It's fast, it's frantic, and you constantly are dodging enemy fire both single player and multiplayer. It's a learning curve as this is a VERY different experience than most FPS games coming out nowadays and that's fine. It's good even. It means that they knew this was a different breed of monster that could come out on top. And I think this has shined pretty bright in comparison to the many FPS games that have launched this year. Easily a GOTY contender.

Overall - 9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf there was only one word to describe DOOM, it would be "joyful."

This is a game that basks in the fact that it's a video game. In no other medium would this story be able to achieve the same amount of enjoyably bloody and bloody enjoyable action and set pieces. Even in a game about blood caked hallways and bowels of hell, never does the player feel grim and mournful, and it's all thanks to the unrelenting badassery that is Doom Guy.

If Far Cry: Blood Dragon is a love letter to 80s synth and action movies, then DOOM is surely a love letter to 80s heavy metal and horror movies (and action movies, for that matter).

The game gradually ramps up the speed of the combat so you don't feel overwhelmed out the starting gate. You begin in darkly lit hallways with a few shambling enemies, and eventually graduate to sprawling arenas with jump pads, teleporters, power ups, and a never-ending supply of demons. But you wont feel exhausted by Hell's relentless onslaught; in fact, when the Mick Gordon-composed music dies down, letting you know there's no more demons left to kill, you'll say "but I was just getting started..."\t\t\t\t\t
