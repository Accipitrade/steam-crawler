As someone who played Doom 2016 on release and was thoroughly underwhelmed, finding it sluggish and uninspired.
I later played Eternal and loved it, I decided to go back and reevaluate Doom 2016. And with a clearer picture of what they were going for I very much enjoyed Doom 2016 the second time around.


Some tips that I took from Eternal to enjoy this game more:
  • Swap weapon- It keeps the variety up, and some weapons are better against certain enemies making them feel less bullet-spongey
  • Ignore some weapons - Some weapons are just crappy. Just ignore the combat shotgun, the assault rifle and the plasma rifle. The will just slow you down
  • Use specials - Always use the weapons mods whenever possible and spam grenades
  • Move around - Donâ€™t try to focus fire on one single enemy too much. Move around. Find opportunities. Grab pick-ups. Strike hard and keep moving to stay alive
  • Skip the collectables - And I mean all of them. Runes, argent cells, praetor suit upgrades, challenges and dolls. You will find enough to upgrade what you need without exploring side-paths. Everything else is a waste of time.
  • Raise the difficulty - In order to get the most out of Doom and engage with all its systems you have to play on a difficulty level 1 or 2 steps above what you are comfortable with. On the lower difficulties you can get by with just using the plasma rifles primary, stand still and soak all damage. It will counterintuitively make enemies feel more tankier and the game feel slow.


There are still some gripes:
  • Lack of movement options - No dash, no monkey bars and no shotgun hook
  • Confusing level design - For some reason there are a lot of large open spaces and somewhere there is a random door to find, It ruins the flow and pace between encounters
  • Weapon balance is off - The super shotgun is too good while some weapons are useless. Some weapon mods are especially useless
  • The chainsaw and BFG are cheap gotchas - With plenty of ammo around for normal guns, the chainsaw is just a insta-kill with no strategy or need for skill. Same goes for the BFG but it can wipe a whole room and stun bosses

I think Eternal is better in every way, a tighter second draft for the same idea, but there is still plenty to do like here. The formula is good even if there are some hiccups.
