\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the funnest single player FPS games I've played in a decade.

Fast paced, no reloading, great action, and awesome level design. This did feel like a modern Doom. I would recommend the game to anyone who is a fan of classic FPS.

I didn't touch multiplayer, since that isn't what I play a Doom game for. I'm sure I will hop on now and then with some friends to mess around. The snap map is really cool as well. It has co-op for up to 4 friends. I've spent a little bit of time playing user created maps.

A few pro's and Con's (only focusing on single player)

Pros
-Quick to the action, not too many dull moments
-Weapons feel great, almost all were a blast to kill demons with (Other than the pistol, which you never need to use)
-Speed, you run fast and kill fast
-Great frame rate, and it ran smooth the entire time. I never ran until noticable slow downs (GTX 970, 16 RAM, i5 @ 3.4)
-Collectables done right. In a few secrest areas you can find upgrades and little collectables. Game isn't flooded with them.
-Levels design. You can attack and jump around. Easy to find you way. Bunch of secrets in each level.

Cons
-Little repetitive towards the end (But I always still had fun)
-Story (was just completely meh) it was obvious and Doom doesn't need a strong story but give us something unique or cool
-Wish Doom guy had a little more interaction. I loved moments when you could see his range and he smashed a monitor when someone was talking to him. I wish there were a few more of these moments. One of my favorite things from Doom and Doom II was the face came and seeing his reactions. To me small things like that is all Doom guy needs.


The game isn't perfect but I would say it is great.\t\t\t\t\t
