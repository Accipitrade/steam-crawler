\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat visuals, fast shooting. This game goes against the grain in the modern fps genre and stays true to its roots. The graphics are visceral, the gore is really good. The level design is really good and interesting to look at, the story is also straight forward. I think the level design is both the main feature of this game and its biggest drawback. The game feels like it just throws you into one arena fight after another, which is where most of the level design goes. It feels like less of a journey because less attention is given to the areas in between the arenas except in a few areas. In general the game comes into its own best towards the end when you spend more time in Hell. Aside from that the levels pretty much all look the same. You're either inside a hallway in the argent facility or you're in hell. Not much diversity. But the gunplay really makes up for it. The game does throw alot at you towards the end. It will really get your noggin jogging figuring out how to spread your ammo out through a combat section to survive, that's the biggest strength of the game. If you've played alot of halo or old 90s shooters like original doom or even marathon you will realize there is a lack of puzzle solving in this game. You just move room to room, survive the onslaught, grab a key and proceed. There's lots of cool secrets throughout the game as well. Worth playing if you like old school shooters.\t\t\t\t\t
