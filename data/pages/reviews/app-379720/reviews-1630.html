\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is an absolutely wonderful experience.

I will go over 3 points. Gameplay, Music, and Visuals, with quick sidenotes for each.

1) Gameplay
The gameplay is fantastic. Prioritize targets, take them out. Need health? Smash their skulls in, break their leg and make them eat their own foot, rip them to pieces, and do it fast. Need ammo? CHAINSAW THEM IN HALF AND MAKE THEM BLEED AMMO PACKS. Need a break? Look for collectibles. There is no shortage of action or speed. You sprint at all times, and you don't have to reload. If you stand still, you die. End of story.
SIDENOTES:
I play the game on Hurt Me Plenty usually, but switching to Ultra-Violence made the game feel completely unfair after a certain point. I know, call me a wuss, I don't care. If a game is difficult, I should at least be having fun. I don't like games that relentlessly kick my ass.


2) Music
GOOD LORD THE MUSIC!! It's amazing!
Composed by Mick Gordon, the music in doom is what happens if you mixed Heavy Metal music using a chainsaw. It sounds absolutely amazing, and it's designed to make the player play more agressively. The music is programmed to follow the actions of the player, and it's brilliantly mixed.
SIDENOTES:
Mick Gordon made a GDC presentation talking about how he made the soundtrack. It's really interesting, and very insightful for game developers.

3) Visuals
The visual design in this game is so good, that when they ported this game to Nintendo Switch (which runs at lower than the lowest settings on PC), it still looks great. In this version of Id Software's game engine, ID Tech 6, they focused on lighting. Geometry count does suffer a little bit, but who cares, the lighting in this game completely overshadows that. Despite being so graphically gorgeous, the game runs at 60 fps at all times, on consoles and PCs. You should have no problem getting this to run on your dad's work computer.
SIDENOTES:
Admittedly, the polygon count for the environments is a little low sometimes, and they used Megatextures (A technique where the environment uses one texture for the entire thing) again, so the texture resolutions for the ground in Mars looks a little fuzzy compared to the interiors.
Sometimes, objects such as rocks can look a little jagged and strange, and the physics of the objects in some areas are janky as hell. Objects will constantly collide with the ground, making weird noises.

so yeah 665/10 would definitely buy DOOM Eternal if i had the money to do so

but make sure you play this first, because you will never be able to go back

the game gets repetitive in the third act btw\t\t\t\t\t
