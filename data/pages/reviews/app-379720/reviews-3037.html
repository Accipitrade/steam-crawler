\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM (2016) is a fast-paced, gory first person shooter packed to the brim with hellish environmental imagery and literal demons emerging from the game's every nook and cranny. Set on Mars in the chaos of an "Argent Energy" research facility recently devastated by a demonic attack, the player is thrust headlong into a world of nonstop action in a quest to put a stop to the madness and kick some serious demon butt along the way.

Like the 1993 original, DOOM's greatest strength lies in its combat. Once again taking on the iconic mantle of Doomguy, the player gains access to a diverse set of weapons over the course of the game, each of which are designed to keep the combat experience continuously moving and intense. There's no place for awkwardly hiding behind barriers mid-fight in DOOMâ€”if you try to stay in one place, you're dead. The number of enemies contributes to this greatly; you're rarely fighting just one or two demons in the main area of a level (expect more like twenty!), and that also forces you to remain keenly aware of your surroundings at any given moment.

The game's staple weapon is a powerful shotgun best used in close-quarters combat. It's my personal favorite weapon: id really did an excellent job making you feel like a god using this thing. The amount of kickback is perfect, and the audio for every shot is immensely satisfying. The player also has access to a heavy assault rifle, a rocket launcher, a plasma gun, a chaingun, and of course the classic BFG, among other weapons. You can get away with sticking to one or two of these for the first few levels, but the game's robust enemy design encourages the player to make use of several weapons in different situations. Ammunition is far from scarce, but it's very easy to use it up if you're careless.

What I find fascinating about DOOM is how it's able to take what is effectively a blank template for a character (Doomguy) and use the thematic material in the rest of the game to create a unique persona from that. Most modern games aim to actively give the protagonist a dynamic or subjective role in the story; not doing so is often dismissed as lazy writing. Yet DOOM does not attempt to convince the player that Doomguy is anything more than a vessel for their inner desire to go on an insane demon-killing rampage. This approach is very neatly executed and keeps the game's focus clear all throughout.

In addition, the other two things DOOM got absolutely perfect are its art direction and its soundtrack. Mars is rightly depicted as a barren, red wasteland, not unlike the locales in hell itself that the player visits later on. It feels alien enough to keep the player visually intrigued, but not so much as to be distracting. Likewise, DOOM's choice of music ties in perfectly with the intensity of the combat, keeping your heart racing for the duration of each battle. Together, these things produce an adrenaline-pumping sense of hyper-bloodlust that I've never experienced in another game. It's extremely fun, if a bit exhausting!

The story is nothing out of the ordinary (I found the gameplay the most comepelling part by far), but it holds its own. The voice acting is solid, and the small number of actual characters helps to convey the theme of desolation and inhumanity that accompanies the barrages of demons around every corner. I found the level design quite straightforward, for the most part; it's rarely unclear where you have to go, although there were certainly a few moments of confusion in my first playthrough. The game performs excellently on a hardware level, too. I could consistently reach above 100fps at 1080p ultra with an i5-4460, GTX 970, and 8GB of RAM. The one thing that I feel is annoying about DOOM is the UI for the map and weapon upgrade screen, which were both a little clumsy. Otherwise, though, it's pretty solid.

If you're looking for an exciting shooter to really get your heart rate up, I would give DOOM a try. I found the combat to be the most enjoyable part of the game, with the art direction and music adding to the experience significantly. DOOM is certainly one of the more memorable shooters I've played, and I would happily recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
