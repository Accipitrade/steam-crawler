\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy first actual review...

TL;DR:

Awesome campaign, easily one of my favorite single-player experiences in several years.

Positives:

A+ for graphics, textures, animations, music and sound design. Gunplay is great, huge arsenal, no reloading, fast switching. Movement is very fast, you must run-and-gun constantly to avoid melee damage and projectiles. Very reminiscent of the old DOOM games along with well-executed modern mechanics.

Level design is excellent, both aesthetically and mechanically. Lots of secrets and significant rewards for observation and exploration of the environments. Despite the huge levels, backtracking to horde items or find a secret is pretty quick (often an option, but not always) thanks to Doomguy's high movement speed. Otherwise, levels are all replayable for the completionist to collect all the upgrades and extras.

Many reviewers knocked the story, but I found it enjoyable, certainly more than sufficient for a gun-centric DOOM game. Taking time to read flavor text in the DOSSIER -- particularly the artifact and character sections -- adds a lot to the story and lore experience, if you're interested.

Upgrade system is great. Weapon mods are fun and add utility. Lots of customization options, but everything is very straightforward, no need to spend tons of time in the menus equipping runes and weapon mods unless you want to.

Glory kill system is awesome. Not intrusive or interrupting to game flow like I thought it would be.

Level challenges, weapon mod challenges, and rune challenges are a blast, and act as a tour-de-force of combat approaches while simultaneously rewarding your efforts with upgrades.

Negatives:

My only negative impression is that lots of the encounters feel similar. You get locked in a "kill chamber", heavy metal music starts, waves of demons spawn, and you fly around the room gibbing everything that moves. When you're done painting the walls with gore, a door opens and you continue on. But just because I could smell these encounters coming doesn't mean I didn't look forward to them. The kill chamber rooms are always cool -- designed for you to platform around arena-shooter style -- and you can get really creative with how you wipe up the monsters.

Also:

Remove the awful mouse smoothing, add to steam launch options: +set m_smooth 0
Skip the unskippable intro: +com_skipIntroVideo 1

It's low by default, but you can slide FoV very high if you wish (I like 120).

I played the Ultra-Violence difficulty setting and recommend starting there. Only reduce difficulty if you're absolutely stuck. Think old-school DOOM, strafing around those kill chambers to avoid being cornered. Standing still or trying to take cover is rarely the right strategy.\t\t\t\t\t

I enjoyed Doom3 a lot, the story seemed more in depth. Something was pushing you forward other than killing. More survival I guess.

If you like what I've said, you will enjoy this game. I did not.\t\t\t\t\t

Note: I'm only marking this game as recommended pertaining to the single player, NOT THE MULTIPLAYER, WHICH IS TERRIBAD.

As of this writing I have completed the single player route on ultra violence and played enough multiplayer to reach level 20-something (after putting 8+ hours into the open beta, and being very little different from that, I didn't feel like playing multiplayer anymore.)

The good (SP):
  • The music KICKS MAJOR DEMON BACKSIDE. (even the menu music is amazing.)
  • The guns are nice and varied, but each one has its strong and weak situations.
  • The game isn't EZ-mode walkabout like some big-name FPS games, and it doesn't hold your hand TOO much.
  • The audio (aside from the music) is VERY good, very punchy and visceral, it makes the game sound alive and ..well, DOOM-y.
  • The enemy AI isn't scripted. It's also not stupid. They can do just about any move you can, and then some. Some are combat closers, some rush, some hang back and shoot from range, but they also change their roles depending on what you are doing to their fellow bad guys. In short, VERY well done.
  • The glory-kill system is fun, and varied enough that you don't get sick of using it.
  • The maps, look, amazing. The DEMONIC maps even more so, there are varied details everywhere, the overall feel of the maps change as you get further toward key places, giving a solid feel of progression through the world and into the pits of hell, and then back again.
  • The animations all across the board are some of the best I have ever seen in an action title.
  • The character design going WITH those animations is also top notch.
  • The writing and world building entries are thick and plenty, clearly showing that Bethesda does in fact care about game lore and whatnot even if its DOOM. I enjoyed reading every thing I unlocked/encountered.

The bad (SP):
  • There is a little too much micromanagement, upgrades to your suit and weapons and whatnot. It's not really what a DOOM game needs, but its not done poorly either.
  • Periodically the glory-kill system will put your camera thru a wall or map object for a few moments (however, never getting stuck when this happens is nice),but its also very rare.
  • Strange bug when loading a map/restarting a map. Might be driver related.. game will choke down to exceedingly low FPS and stay there until you alt-enter the game to a window and then alt-enter it full screen again. (this also happened in RAGE as well, and was later fixed.), but again, this happens rarely, and only at the very start of a area, so its fairly minor.
  • A few CTD's here and there. (Crash to desktop) Only had about 4 the entire play through, showing this is very much a Bethesda game... :) This will likely be fixed.

OVERALL:
I highly recommend the crap out of this game for the single player, its as good if not WAY better than Bethesda's work on Wolfenstien, and I'm looking forward to more playthroughs and secret finding and unlocking of all the goodies. I might even make some stuff in snap-map.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

If your interest in this game is single player, stop reading, from here on in things get nasty, mean, and ugly REAL fast.


I had a scathing review of all the problems in the multiplayer beta, it sat on the front page of the beta review for four days and had a %87+ ratio at the time the beta was taken down. I pointed out the blatent halo-ism of the multiplayer, the problems with the audio, netcode, the weapons balance and the, frankly, lack of effort put into making the multipayer. Now that the game is released, I have gone back and re-evaluated all my previous points of concern and..well, here you have it.

The good? (MP):
  • It looks nice, being as all the game assets are from the single player, that is to be expected.
  • You can unlock stuff that looks neat. (It even looks like they yanked some of the halo-lookalikes and replaced them with variants that fit the new-doom-lore better.)
  • It has guns..?
  • The pings seem to be better, but that doesn't mean a whole lot since the servers are all entirtely hosted by Bethesda and ID, they just added more capacity on their end.

The bad..(MP):
  • NO CO-OP? WHAT THE EVER LOVING HELL BETHESDA? No, really. Which one of you guys-in-ties thought that was a good design choice? Because you need to be slapped upside the head with the north end of a southbound pinkie demon.
  • Every game mode is basically team deathmatch. No, really. In this game mode you chase and guard a circle! In this game mode there are more than one circle and they sit still! In THIS one you are playing team deathmatch and you just have to pick up an extra thing people drop when they die! And in THIS one.. Well, you get the idea. Do you LOVE team deathmatch? Because Certain Affinity sure as ♥♥♥♥ does, and NOTHING ELSE. This is DOOM.. and there is no straight up ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ deathmatch anywhere. And I couldn't even test Clan arena because *no players were found*.. just a few days after launch.
  • The Audio is even *more ♥♥♥♥♥♥ than the beta*.. Before, you could hear firefights clear across the map.. but not rockets exploding on your back or in your face. Now you can't hear really anything. No, really, you can't hear a rocket explode 2 inches on the ground behind you. But you can hear this... really strange.. pitter patter sound that I think is supposed to be.. bullets hitting things? In the far far distance like somebody was tapping away at the nearest wall with a BB-gun. It literally makes no sense.
  • Weapon balance is still completely hosed. They basically didn't change anything from the beta they just unlocked the rest of the weapons you can use. Combined with the fact you can't /hear weapon fire around you/ for beans, the super shotgun is pretty much an instant kill gun. Supershotgun+glorykill-button-spam=win. That's how to multiplayer.
  • Explosions still basically do nothing, nothing has knock back in this game.
  • The little numbers that pop off players when you shoot them.. seem to be calculated client side, and therefor sometimes highly inaccurate. (When I hit a guy for 25-25-25-25-25-25-25-15.. and he kills me, and the post-kill screen shows him as having 85 health left at the time he killed me, I call ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ on your game code skillz, K?)

I could go on, but frankly.. I don't care anymore.


Multiplayer Roundup:
This is not the next 'doom multiplayer' or even the next 'Action FPS multiplayer' experience, its just 'generic multiplayer FPS with a doom skin and a demon spawn', its not even a contender for competitive FPS play, its strictly casual nonsense and frankly its not even really that fun after the first hour or two.


Remember all that 'feedback' they were gathering from the multiplayer beta for 'development reasons'? Remember how I warned you it was all a big fat marketing lie? Yeah, it was. They changed one thing, the stupid audio bias, and they didn't even fix it, they fudged it even more. That is it. The weapons are still jacked, the game modes people all asked for are not there, nobody can host a dedicated server. Yep, that's the list. So the console kids who think this is as good as FPS multiplayer can get are gobbling it up, and will abandon the game as soon as the next shiny thing rolls past. The multiplayer is more or less as forgettable as I feared after the beta.

I give it 6 months and it'll be a ghost town unless there is some severe intervention by the developers. (which IS possible, and if that happens, I'll update this review!)\t\t\t\t\t
