\r\n\t\t\t\t\tINTRO: This game is absolutely fantastic! If you like old-school fast-paced first-person action-shooter games like Unreal Tournament and Serious Sam, then you'll love this.

OVERVIEW: Regardless of difficulty, this game really knows how to make you feel like a bad***. Despite fighting literal demons from Hell, you feel like YOU are the monster. It works like this: In DOOM 3 (and in other games) you are locked in with a bunch of monsters. Here though, the demons are locked in with you.

And you don't leave survivors.

Basically, it's like a gory arena with emphasis on movement. On the tougher difficulties, this game is brutally unforgiving (in a good way). Fortunately, it is very possible to play on an easier difficulty and still have a lot of fun if you're a casual player. I recommend starting at a medium-ish difficulty and increasing it until you hit your sweet-spot, since the harder you play, the more boss you will feel when you manage to kill a horde of demons and survive.

Replayability is also excellent, since few things are as fun as literally ripping demons apart that are five times your size. I'll be playing through this game many times in the future. And there's a "Snapmap" feature which, while being a poor substitute for true modding, adds an extra area of replayability. Snapmaps are fan-created "skirmish"-type maps you can play, from serious challenges to a literal remake of Five Nights at Freddy's (not kidding).

Not that there aren't issues. Small things. Little tweaks could improve this game hugely. For one, your character's footsteps are nearly silent and your camera is nearly completely static while running. Beefier footstep noises and a small view bob would make you feel more like you're the deadly super soldier you see on the game's cover. Speaking of an armoured super-soldier, when you first put on your suit's helmet, you'll notice this awesome helmet's interior overlay on your screen. But it disappears immediately after the cutscene. Personally, I'd have liked the option to keep the overlay for immersion's sake, like good ol' Republic Commando's helmet. Like I said, little things.

There's just one issue to address if you're thinking of buying this game.
If you can think fast on your feet, and can move your fingers over your keyboard's WASD and number keys rapidly, then you will benefit most from this game. But like I said above, more relaxed gamers will still be able to enjoy this game on the lower difficulties. Just don't play this game if you play like my dad. Meaning, slow to react and/or push movement keys (he got eaten alive by a radroach in Fallout 4).

I'll be playing this game for years to come. 9.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

BOOM DOOM BOOM

Let's start of this review by quoting John Carmack, programmer of DOOM in 1993, "Story in a game is like a story in a porn movie. It's expected to be there, but it's not that important".

That is entirely the case in this DOOM, you are living on Mars, trying to avoid HELL being unleashed. This Reboot is exactly what you expect from the game, from the begining you understand that this game will let out all of your testosterone, even if you're a girl. Watch the blood, guts, big guns and exploding demons as you're growing that manly mustache. We have here the perfect recipe for a DOOM. The action never stops and if you aren't finding you are losing yourself into some levels trying to find secrets, collectibles and ... old school levels !

The implement of glory kills really help you feel like you didn't skip legday, some sort of Mortal Kombat fatalities that you can use very often and are making Scorpion look like an old granny. The only question you have to ask yourself is: whenever you will bash their skulls in two, which eye will close first?

GUNS
This is a FPS, you need weapons. The range of those g

Because yes, that says FPS said weapons. These range from chainsaws to Rocket launcher. Every weapon can be upgraded with at least one mod, for instance, the shotgun has two mods: a triple shot gun and a grenade launcher. To switch from one to the other, simply press the "R" key and explode more demons.

But you can also boost yourself, in upgrading your armor or completing sets of challenges throughout the game that will let you develop new passive skills.

Difficulty
You can finish the game in under 10 hours, and it gets very challenging in Nightmare mode, you have to be constantly on guard and rushing to be able to dodge.. absolutely everything. You can also try the no-respawn mode and schedule an appointment at the closest clinic.

Multiplayer
Multiplayer is incredibly fun, but will bore you to death after a couple of hours. Even though the weapon design is incredibly well done for single player. You feel extremely weak in Multiplayer and everyone seems to be using the rocket launcher.

The levels are a bit disappointing and it would be great to see the community use the level editor mode.
