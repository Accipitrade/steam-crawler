\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh, DOOM, one of the most satisfying games I've played since Witcher 3 and Undertale, and definitely the best game I've played in 2016 up to this point. Sorry, Overwatch.

I have no prior experience with the DOOM series, as I don't recall ever playing the games. However, I may have played a teeny tiny bit of the first game at some point. I don't know for certain if the game I'm thinking of there was DOOM though, as I think I might have mistaken it for Wolfenstein. But let's just assume that said game is Wolfenstein, and I have no previous experience with this series.

Graphics

DOOM's visuals are quite pretty actually. When rooms aren't covered in the blood and guts of demons you just punched to death, the game is colorful and it fits very well with the game. I especially love how Hell turned out. Not much else to get into detail with here, because this is DOOM. Why do you care about graphics?

Gameplay

This is why you're here, aren't you? Well, let me tell you, buying this game for the single player alone is a worthwhile purchase. The gameplay is incredibly smooth, fluid, and very fast paced, If you're looking for an old-school shooter, look no further. DOOM is one of the best in that category, as well as Wolfenstein: The New Order. The boss fights are challenging in a way without feeling unfair, the difficulty options make the game more interesting, and I felt compelled to go for the collectibles. SnapMap is also very fun, and while I didn't spend too much time with it, I'm glad it's in the game for those with creative minds. Where DOOM falters, however, is the multiplayer. It'll only appeal to a very niche audience of gamers, and I don't happen to be part of that audience. I could get into more detail aside from things like hackers, and there's plenty of places to look if you want more in-depth reasons as to why the multiplayer falls short, but it just doesn't feel right in a game like this. I couldn't enjoy it at all, no matter how much I tried. That being said, I will give credit where its due, the customization for your character and weapons are awesome. Choosing the wear of your weapon makes me quite happy, because having war-torn guns work in something like this. Besides the multiplayer, DOOM is outstanding in the gameplay department.

Story

Okay, let's have a quick discuission here. If you're reading this, you're probably looking for a reason to buy the game, right? Well, if you want a story-driven game, look somewhere else. DOOM has a story and it doesn't suck, but it's clear that this wasn't the main priority to the game. The important part was that this game was just like old school DOOM, and that aspect was done almost to perfection.

Audio

We don't need to discuss sound effects for a shooter game about beating the ever living ♥♥♥♥♥ out of demons, now do we? However, the soundtrack to this game is awesome. I found a few that I quite enjoy, While it won't reach levels of awesome that the original DOOM reached (even I knew about how awesome those songs were) this game's soundtrack is excellent regardless. It seemed oddly familiar to me for some reason, and when I looked up the soundtrack, I realized that it was made by Mick Gordon, who I'm a fan of. He didn't disappoint this time around, and that's a good thing, because I would've let Doomguy slice the HELL out of him. See what I did there? I'm here all week, folks.

Replayability

DOOM's replayability is surprisingly strong actually. While the other aspects to the game besides singleplayer may seem gimmicky, SnapMap was done very well. Not to mention you can get quite a bit out of playtime from the singleplayer, as there's lots to do. You'll definitely be able to get over 10 hours of this, but depending on how much you like level creators and collectibles that might increase/shorten your playtime.

Verdict

A game where you can punch, blow up, slice, or however you prefer to kill demons and get rewarded for it? Count me in! DOOM is the most fun I've had out of a game in 2016 so far, and I can see myself coming back to it from time to time to play through the campaign again. It's a worthwhile purchase if you're a fan of old-school shooters!

Graphics - 9/10
Gameplay - 9.5/10
Story - 7/10
Audio - 9.5/10
Replayability - 8.5/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe story starts off with a little bit of worry, I was afraid the amount I paid for game was too much to become my main source of play, that was until I started doing it every single day.

Just kidding. But for real, I was pretty upset about spending $34.99 on this game and was honestly scared that I wasted my money. That was.... until I pressed play and actually started the game!!! (A few days later.) I left the game running to listen to the main screen intro music - and I don't even like Rock. The game's soundtrack will intensify its amazing display of graphics as well as the demon-shredding that comes with it. Passive destruction is all I can say. I started the game on the highest difficulty that I could and haven't looked back sense. With a combination of exploration and extreme gunfights that occur, they merge together to incorporate the DOOM atmosphere with an excessive amount of butt-kicking. Overall I've spent TEN HOURS ON THE FIRST TWO LEVELS (and some multiplayer) and all I can say after looking back is that it was well worth it. I would have gladly spent the $59.99 original asking price. However dull season passes may be now a days, I actually don't find it worth it. The DOOM multiplayer comes with Freeze Tag and if you've ever played Quake III Arena, this reminded me so much of hours spent playing Rail Freeze on rAz3r's server that I actually bought that game too.

The criticism for multiplayer may be that after hours spent it becomes repetitive, but then again, what game doesn't? Spend six hours playing Rainbow Six Siege and I'm pretty sure you're spent - unless playing with friends. It all depends on how you take in a dark, gritty atmosphere packed with a massive amount of Demonspawn that endlessly aim to destroy your every being. Unless you're actually playing multiplayer, then it's a massive amount of DOOM guys, shredding through each other with a plethora of online weaponry and "hack modules" the enhance the action even further. It is not a typical FPS shooter game online multiplayer. There is a strategy to it and whether or not you choose to believe or accept that will reflect in how many deaths you have each game. Run and gun and get lucky, or play Freeze Tag and actually thoroughly enjoy what you're doing. If you're the type that needs fast-paced action, believe me: even taking this game slow is an extremely accelerated battle between DOOM guy and DOOM guy, or DOOM guy and Demonspawn.

I not only recommend this game, but I have been trying to get friends that hate this type of shooter to buy. Think that it looks like Halo? HAHA. All I can say to that is: QUAD DAMAGE.

Anyway, I do seriously recommend maxing out the graphics (with NightmareFX) if your system can handle it.. I've been getting 144 FPS (frames per second) out of combat while exploring and during combat averaging roughly 100-120 FPS with a few ill-optimized dips into the 60s. With maxed out graphics, please use the GRITTY rendering option as it just makes the game overall more-like a DOOM game should look. Badass. And there I was avoiding using that word throughout the review so that I could impact its readers when it meant most.

You'll love this game so much you'll want the soundtrack to never end, the demons to never stop spawning and the frames to never stop counting.

This has been my first Steam game review ever, and there are many other games I love to play.\t\t\t\t\t

I suggest you look elsewhere - HOWEVER, Dooms multiplayer is still a hell of a lot of fun for the casual player (Like myself.) who only hops on multiplayer shooters every now and then to get a competetive match under your belt, Look no further because like the campaign, In multiplayer it still feels heavily gratifying to fight head to head with your enemy.

Guns feel awesome, shredding enemies apart with great force, the only bad weapon is the first shotgun.
Also game has exploration, looking for secrets rewards with weapon attachments, upgrade points for them, upgrading basic stats, etc. Some levels have non-linear progression like the old Doom games, but most levels are straightforward run'n'gun action, making this feel more like a Painkiller game.
Multiplayer, on other hand, is poorly thought-out mesh of arena shooter and bad modern game tendencies(2 weapon limit, loadouts).\t\t\t\t\t
