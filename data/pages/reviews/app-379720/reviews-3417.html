\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is just gread overall and it accomplishes what it set out to do, reviving a true classic and introducing it to the current generation of games by boldly blending the old with the new with great success.
Doom is finally here!

The story isn't much to talk about, but hey, this is a reboot of a game that didn't rely on story at all, the single player campaign is more about running, jumping, finding secrets, getting bigger better guns, shooting and punching demons in the face while avoiding getting your own face smashed in, Just pure fun.

The gameplay is great, a smart well balanced mix between features seen in most modern first person shooters and the classic old school arena shooter type of play. Really fast paced action that requires you to always be on the move.

The graphics.
The game looks and performs great, but, i did notice that this iD Tech 6 engine shares some of the downsides of iD's previous engine (iD Tech 5), enviroment textures sometimes look really bland and in my opinion not on par with the rest of the game's graphical qualities, also, texture streaming issues while far less noticeable when compared to iD Tech 5 games, it still exists here. Those aside, the lighting and shadows are fantastic, gun and demon models are very detailed, decals and effects look great, but most importantly it performs really well, smooth at all times, probably the most optimized game to come out on PC this year, no bugs, no crashes, excellent performance even with all graphic options set to maximum. Great job iD Software.
(At the time of this review there was still no proper SLI support, iD has promised to fix this in the coming days)

The sound is great as well, just listen to those heavy metal riffs while you cut an Imp in half with your chainsaw!

That's it, now i have to go back to kill more demons and finish the campaign.

Buy this game and have fun!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a long time Doom fan, Brutal Doom fan, and many other mods... I must say, the single player experience is by far worthy of the title Doom. The optimization is by far the best I've seen in a long time, the pace of combat and speed is phenominal, note that it's not ice skating fast like old Doom but it is exceptionally fast paced. It has many arena shooter elements, IE, you'll hit an area and need to down the monsters in the area before you can progress or regress back from where you came, but it forces a pace of combat that is chaotic as opposed to being able to fall back and snipe the map with a pistol. There are very few one off opponents...

As to the melee aspect, once you turn off the highlight/mob flash associated with the stun, they flow smoothly and while at first I thought it was gimmicky, you stop caring when you are at 10 health, no armor, low on ammo, everything on the floor is gone and need to specifically glory kill for some health.

My only real gripe is the chainsaw, it's an instant kill weapon that on causes a massive ammo supply explosion on kill. So you get limited uses and have to find gas. It's great for drawn out fights with a certain pain in the butt enemy but overall it's a shame I can't haul through the map spamming it into enemies. Brutal kills though, can't complain, no real way a chainsaw wouldn't be this devastating in combat.

If you are in it for SP, I would pay for it. Look forward to doing Snap Map SP content too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt appears they actually did a great job putting the spirit of Doom in a 2016 game.

The 1993 version still exists (and holds up, particularly well in co-op!) and did not need a strict 1:1 gameplay remake - such a remake wouldn't even make sense today. Do recall, for example, that for all its speed and enjoyable traversal of interesting locations you couldn't actually even jump. Which was, and again still is, perfectly fine for what that game was and the design of those levels, but in a new game today I do prefer that ability present and taken advantage of.

This new take on Doom makes sense. Things like ledge climbing and "glory kills" did not appeal on paper I admit, but in practise they do in fact serve the game quite well. They serve the action. Seriously, I know many of us are cynical these days (and for good reason), but it seems these things were actually thought through and implemented (and iterated over and over, clearly) as part of a good ol' fashioned, bona fide vision.

And the vision seems gloriously simple and uncompromising; frankly refreshing in 2016.
It's just Doom. A self-aware and honest power fantasy about using crazy weapons to make copious amounts of even crazier demons dead. Rip and tear.

...I feel like that was the sort of paragraph to end with, but I'm no writer and have no standards so let me add three things that are worth pointing out:

1. The PC version seems to have gotten the love and polish it needs. Great performance in general, and of course for those of us who are into that - extensive options (both menu and console commands).
2. Only time can tell how well it will hold up; barely anything even has a fair chance to hold up to the original - but Mick Gordon apparently can do no wrong when it comes to soundtracks and has, subjectively I suppose, knocked it out of the park again. \\m/
3. It's also too early to tell how the multiplayer stacks up. Again, pre-2000 id Software (and/or the modding community) set some pretty ridiculously high bars that I just can't see how this could even come close to reaching, however - what I do know is that, for now, I am actually having a lot of fun with it.\t\t\t\t\t
