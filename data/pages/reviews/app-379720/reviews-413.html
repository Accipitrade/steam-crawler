\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe title that revived the DooM franchise and a truly incredible game made with overwhelming love.
It does few things and does it better than the explosive majority of shooters from this decade.
As the catchphrase goes, RIP AND TEAR. That's what you are going to do... most of the time.

The gun feel is insane, the fights are amazingly fast-paced and felt right at home for this arena shooter fanatic. I played in the highest available difficulty from the start and plowed through the game, with a huge smile on my face every time one of TooT's banger combat tracks started playing.

Gun variety, enemy variety, environment variety and pacing that jumps back and forth between exploration and encounters, all of the DooT boxes are ticked, and speaking of environment... what eye candy!
Killer art direction and masterfully made art assets immediately plunge you into suspension of disbelief, and the game's exposition is done in the best way possible for such a title: Deep, unlockable, and out of your way. The only cutscenes are short and to the point, as displayed in the very first one, as you start the game. You'll see what I mean :)

The only truly bad thing about the game is the checkpoint system. Checkpoints are to far between and do not reset if you backtrack, which very heavily punishes exploring before a fight and then dying in it or missing a step and falling to your death while platforming.

Additionally, I actually felt like two things were very detrimental to DooD's pacing; Glory kills, while central to the gameplay, take control away from you for a few seconds, which tends to kill your state of flow during tenser fights, and the overabundance of well hidden secrets in very expansive maps tends to make my perfectionist head spin, making me spend more time inspecting every map's nooks and crannies than actually fighting.
It feels like the game is really afraid of exhausting you through too many fights and pushes a ton of "breathing room" moments on you. Keep in mind that this is how I view and feel things as a quake player on crack. If I am not firing a gun while bunny-hopping for more than half a second I tend to fall in a deep coma due to lack of dopamine.
In all seriousness, I would have liked MooM even more if it had 4 times the enemies. Fortunately the last two missions kinda do just that so I don't mind it!

BooB is a goog game.
Anyway, see you in Eternal!\t\t\t\t\t
