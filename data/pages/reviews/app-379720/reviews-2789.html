\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow. Truly amazing. 9/10

I bought this game on the one year anniversary on may 13 on sale, not knowing that that was the one year anniversary of its release. I was blown away, this game truly is amazing.

The campaign is a blast all the way through, that is if you enjoy blowing up demons almost constantly. Honestly the levels are a little bit repetitive but I personally am not picky and enjoy the gameplay so that doesn't matter to me. The gameplay is fun, fluid, fast paced, and overall satisfying with the glory kills. The weapon variety size is just right for me, it's not too big but not too small. The story is actually very interesting and I was quite intrigued by it. I don't see many people talking about it, as the gameplay may overshadow the story, but it is very interesting and is a good concept. I have only played a little bit of multiplayer, but the time I did it was really fun, I just can't play much because it takes a while to find a game.

The graphics are truly beautiful and I am running it on a laptop, I'm running it on a custom preset with most of the settings on lower and medium. I get around an average of 40 fps with 8gb RAM, Intel Dual core i5 7200U and Nvidia GeForce 940MX.

The only problems I have are that I wish that the campaign was longer, I feel like as soon as you really get pumped and good with it, it's just about over. I bought the game a year late and I'm way late to the party which sucks. Also the game file is around 66 gigs which took a while but the game is worth it.

Overall worth it and I'm happy with it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very good. There is no in-your-face story, and the game can get slightly repetitive, but when all is said and done, the game is well worth its price, in my submission. The fast-paced, gut-ripping gameplay, accompanied with some of the best metal music I have heard in a video game, makes for an intense and worthwhile gaming experience. If you are looking for simple, easy to understand, yet intense and badass gameplay, this is the game for you. Having had experience with the franchise, I believe this game deserves to be called DOOM, and, frankly, the argumentation to the contrary that I have heard since the game’s release is not justified when one considers the game's core merits as a shooter, and as a DOOM game.

If I were to run this game through a percentage/ grading system, the game would receive a solid 95% from me, as the game delivers on many aspects of game design in the single-player mode alone. This is without speaking on the Snapmap and Multi-player modes, which are just added goodies. If you are a fan of the first-person shooter genre, this is a 'must own'.\t\t\t\t\t
