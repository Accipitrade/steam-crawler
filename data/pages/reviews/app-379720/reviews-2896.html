\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great game for people who just want to punch things to death. 8.5/10

Pros:
+The lack of story is great. It's just you, with a bunch of guns, and a bunch of demons. This game doesn't need a huge storyark where you develop characters and plot points or whatnot. Your a guy who likes to kill demons. Boom! Done! Storytime over, now kill some more things. There is, however, story to be found through data logs and other similar things, which you can choose to read in your codex. So if you really are interested in the backstory of say, the Doom Marine, then this game has you covered. It just won't shove it in your face.
+Whilst I wouldn't say it's nonlinear, DOOM does a great job encouraging exploration. There are many collectables, such as Argent Energy which is a health, sheild capacity, and ammo capicity upgrade, drones that provide weapon modifacations, which add a secondary function to your gun, and even small DOOM Marine dolls that give you access to 3D models of items and characters in the game.
+The combat is amazing. This game does a great job keeping you at your toes. Cover won't save you, you'll have to keep moving to survive.
+The guns are all great, and you can feel the impact of each one. Though, a few of them loose their usefullness late game.
+The game looks beautiful, I love the amount of detail developers put into their worlds these days. That also includes enemies, which are all unique and grotesque in their own ways.

Cons(Theres hardly any really bad parts of this game so most of this is nitpicking):
-Glory kills get kinda old after a while, but are almost required to survive most of the bigger fights, since enemies killed this way provide extra health. There is a rune that makes you do it faster, but almost every other rune provides a better, more helpful benefit.
-The bosses aren't really spread out well, there's three(technically four) boss battles and they all take place in the later half of the game.
-There's not enough things to upgrade when it comes to weapons. I ended the game with about 30 upgrade points(The most a single upgrade can cost is 6). This is because I only used one mod per gun(You upgrade the mod, not the gun), but really most of them only had one useful one. I would've liked to be able to upgrade the weapons themselves, because some guns just loose usefulness late game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the moment I'm still sending demons back to hell, but after playing for 10 hours I have a pretty good image of the game:

DOOM is amazing. It is passionate and truthful reboot of the pioneers of FPS games, sticking true to its source material while keeping it fresh with modern FPS trends such as upgrade systems. The game takes you from shooting arena to shooting arena as you take on whatever hell spews at you. As in the original games, movement is key and what ensues is an adrenaline-filled action-packed experience. The game takes you from a martian facility to the gates of hell itself. Far from a corridor shooter like its predecessor Doom 3, the areas are open and feel open, allowing you to take to the air and have hectic battles with hellspawn as you scale from platform to platform, a feature that is surprisingly succesfully implemented. Add to that the more-or-less optional to follow lore and plot, and you end up with what is probably the best shooter since Wolfenstein: The New Order.

For €20 this game is well worth it; if I had known this game was this great I would've picked it up months ago for €30.\t\t\t\t\t
