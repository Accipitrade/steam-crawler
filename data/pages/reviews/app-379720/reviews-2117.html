\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a worthwhile experience. It's a love letter to FPSes of the past through its speed and arcade-like gameplay compared to what comes to mind when we think of the modern shooter, i.e. serious war simulators or regenerative health/shields. Its style is therefore uncommon, but not out of place today. Make no mistake: the technical aspects of this game are on point, from often gross-looking graphics and repulsive sounds to intricate level design.

Modern FPSes turn me off so much, especially when they try to be serious. I don't play games to feel the melodrama of a B-list movie. DOOM is classic, and it proves with this iteration that it's far from outdated. At $20, you get your money's worth with or without a sale.

Some cons to look out for:

- The worst part of DOOM is also its most generic. I spent the bulk of my nearly 33 hours so far on one playthrough of the Campaign. I'm a thorough player, but that speaks of both the quality of the Campaign and the mediocrity of the game's multiplayer. I was a couple of team deathmatches in when I realized this would not be why I play the game.

- Buying this game means you give money to Bethesda. They're a slimy company, but for the most part they kept their damage to Fallout and whoring out Skyrim to every platform imaginable. id Software are good guys, though, so just think of it as you supporting them. Having said that, obviously don't pre-order DOOM Eternal no matter how much you love DOOM (and I love it). Wait for it to come out first.

This is a terrific experience. Buy it.\t\t\t\t\t
