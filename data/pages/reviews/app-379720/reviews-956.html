\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty good. Definitely what the DOOM franchise needed at the time to revitalize the series. I think if they stuck to their classic roots—in terms of level design and progression—this game would not have been nearly as popular. That being said, there are still numerous callbacks and references to classic DOOM. Just about every weapon, enemy and power-up was featured in prior installments, making this game instantly familiar to veteran players. As someone who considers themselves fairly familiar with the series, I can without a doubt say that longtime fans will be incredibly satisfied. Killing enemies in a plethora of gory ways is undeniably fun and the music really amps the player up. It was nice to even hear the original soundtrack remixed and reworked into this game's OST. Regarding game feel, this game had to redefine character movement to traverse a more modern 3-D environment. I would say that they mostly succeeded, although later upgrades to your suit can make the player slightly overpowered, as dodging enemy fire becomes too easy. It still feels very fluid though and I believe that the developers recognized this, as more platforming challenges are strewn throughout levels. No complaints in the gun department, everything is kick-ass and looks very, very cool. I especially enjoyed the extra upgrades and secondary weapons that guns are able to equip now. The story was okay, but most players aren't really playing DOOM for the story. I thought that the characters were more interesting than the actual plot. I do think they missed their chance to have the player take revenge on Olivia Pierce. She definitely needed to have her ass beat. Overall, the game was fun. I encountered a few more bugs than I'm normally comfortable with, but the gracious checkpoint system made sure I didn't have to track back too far. Hopefully, whenever I get around to playing DOOM: Eternal, it's as good as this game. 8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
