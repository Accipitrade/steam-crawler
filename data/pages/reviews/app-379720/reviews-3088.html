\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou know those clutch moments in FPSs where you're pushing an objective? And you're in the middle of a fight? And you get that rush? That rush of adrenaline as you slam on your mouse and keyboard harder and harder, and you struggle to come out on top. THAT is what DOOM is like. This is not a CoD reskin where you can hide behind cover for a couple of seconds and regenerate all your health. This is a frantic, raw shooter game where there aren't any gimmicks that help you play, like auto-aim or regenerating health. Whether you live or die depends on your skill, and not if the dice happen roll in your favor.

There are two main components to most games: gameplay and story. Some games are mostly focused on story. Tales from the Borderlands for example. Others are more of a mixture, like Mass Effect or Half Life. DOOM is a primarily gameplay focused experience, with short snippets of story in between long bouts of gunplay. This is not to say that the story in DOOM is bad; it is very good and interesting and you can read up on the lore if you so desire. The point is that DOOM doesn't try to force-feed you story; it tries to keep the gameplay in the spotlight, but provides tools for reading up on lore if you want.

For a game like DOOM, with a large campaign and a 60 dollar price tag, replayability is a very important thing for me. I'd say that DOOM has a medium-high replayability value, for a couple of reasons. First, people are afraid that the game will get repetitive because of the cycle of run, shoot, kill, that you will often find yourself in. However, in my experience, this is not the case; with the constantly changing environments, variety of enemies, many weapon options, and challenges unique to each level, I never found myself getting bored. Secondly, the game has FIVE difficulty settings, ranging from "I'm too young to die" to "Ultra-Nightmare," where if you die, your playthrough is over. The only thing that will get repetitive in DOOM is some of the story dialogue, but only after you've played the game through several times.

Overall, I think that DOOM 2016 is an amazing game where the story may get old, but the GAMEPLAY never will.\t\t\t\t\t
