\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHoly crap! Just finished it. Epic game! No holds barred, pure unadulterated, non-stop action!
Just keep running and killing everything till the end, don't ever stop!

They removed reload, speeding up the gameplay.
The glory kills are fast-ish (you get a rune that make them faster), but it doesn't stop the flow of battle, and it looks cool killing demons in different ways from different angles.
You can quickly toggle between weapons (and you should) - which makes the battles very versatile and fun.
The music that blasts when there are demons to fight really pumps you up.

I have to note (in this age where we grind in all games for everything): The only grind is for achievements, if you want them (like me).

I highly recommend this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is amazing, but for a few issues. First, the positives....the game is brilliant at what it sets out to do, i.e, create an intense and gory fps game. Its refreshing to be able run into enemies and take them on face to face, rather than hide and take cover. Its most important and successful element is mobility, and it sets the game apart from the scores of other fps games available.

Level design is really good, and takes advantage of the game's focus on mobility, as you run around huge maps jumping around and dodging enemies while trying to take them out. More than once, I found myself disoriented during combat, which I take as a positive towards the game's intensity.

Enemy AI is fantastic, taking advantage of their strength in numbers and their different powers combining them to great effect. You will be forced to strategize to some extent, on the higher difficulties, as death will be quick to find you otherwise. If you are impatient or clumsy like me, expect to die a lot.


My quibbles,

Enemy design feels dull, there's nothing really memorable or scary here (well except the mancubus); considering that these are the denizens of hell, well, it leaves a lot to be desired.

Level design in terms of looks feels uninspired for some reason. While I can understand the levels on mars being somewhat "clean", I thought that they could have taken advantage of hell and been really imaginative.

The story picks up near the end but still feels like standard fare and it isn't told very well also. Normally, I might forgive this, as fps games aren't really known for their story lines, but after playing the excellent wolfenstein and shadow warrior remakes, I don't think such games really have an excuse any more. Shadow warrior was brilliant when it came to making the story unobtrusive, but still managed to tell a poignant one anyway!

In later levels, indoor fights tend to instill fatigue. Combat in these maps while hectic, can be really annoying as the mobility that has been the best aspect of the game doesn't really work here. I've frequently found my self falling of platforms and getting stuck in corners as I was attacked relentlessly by enemies. This isn't much of an issue in earlier levels because of the lack of too many close combat demons or huge indoor map sizes...but as the variety of demons increases and map sizes decrease, it can cause issues. Another problem that I noticed was that my character would sometimes not grab ledges. Of course, this complaint could also be attributed to my lack of skill or impatience, as I usually found myself able to get through an area after taking a break for a few hours.

In my opinion, the game needed to have better sound design. Demons don't seem to have individual calls (or they do but they sound similar, except for a few), which makes the game feel unfair in small and hectic maps with multilple vertical levels. There are certain demons that you want to try and take out as soon as they enter the fray, but there's a good chance that they'll have walloped you before you realize that they've spawned. This is maybe the only part of the game that has sometimes made me feel like I've been killed unfairly...There are a few areas, that I've only beaten after dying a few times and memorizing spawn times and spawn points - It breaks the fun.

Finally, and this is my greatest quibble, the checkpoint system is really annoying. The game frequently, doesn't save after or before an enemy encounter. This leads to a lot of time waste as you go past an umpteen number of enemies again...the gameplay might be fun, but it isn't fun enough for me to go through three different encounters to get to the encounter that I died at again and again, or worse when I die while platforming -_-. I've almost broke my mouse, not because I feel my death was unfair (it wasn't), but because the checkpoints are.


Overall, an amazing fps actually with a few minor issues! Also, the game is not for the squeamish...:p

Edit: Now that I've beaten the game, I must give the developers credit for the final mission. It was designed beautifully and allowed you to fully utilize all that you'd learnt throughout the campaign! The game also has an amazing credits sequence :D\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaven't played it through yet, but I don't really think that my impression will get worse, now that I already played for 11 hours.

I'm a big fan of the Doom games,and Doom I and II were the games that made a big impression on my gamin life. :) So when finally Doom 3 cam out, I ordered , was excited, and ... got very dissapointed. Graphics was ok, but gameplay really sucked.
Now when this new Doom came out, I was a bit skeptical. However, after playing it for a short time, I noticed the big difference. It is much closer to the original fast shooter gameplay and really feels like a worthy successor. To be honest though, to me it feels more like a very good successor to Quake, but that doesn't matter. Reason is, because in Doom you never had jumps and climbing, which really adds to the gameplay. I always liked the exploration of maps and Doom 2016 allows for that.
The gameplay is very fluent and fast paced, what you expect from a good shooter. Graphics is excellent and there are new ideas which really improve on the game. Though I must say, that the Gory Kills feel a bit like cheating, because they give you new health whenever you are close to death. Without it, it would have been much more hardcore, but it doesn't break the game.

I really hope that the community will jump on the editor like they did back then, because this will add a LOT of gameplay. I don't know how many time I spend with the original Doom, playing and also creating maps, and this Doom really has the potential for a long lasting gaming experience.


And one of the really cool features of this game is, it includes the class maps as secrets. :)

Don't know if all of them are included but still, a very nice reminder of my youth. :)

So if you are a fan of shooters then this is a must buy and definitely if you grew up with the original series. :)\t\t\t\t\t
