\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would recommend this to any fan of single-player FPS, as the campaign was very enjoyable to play through. The initial play-through (on the hardest difficulty it lets you start with) took around 40 hours, if you include going back to complete all of the challenges and find the secrets. My 2nd play-through on Nightmare took around 20 hours by comparison, with the majority of the time spent retrying from checkpoint on levels 10 and above.

I have not played the multiplayer, so I cannot comment on that aspect.

Although I think Doom 3 was the best in the series thus far in terms of suspense and general "scare you" factor, this game does a very good job of bringing back the constant edge of your seat "OMG I'm going to die!" aspect of the original. I was happy overall with the general difficulty of the game when played on Nightmare, enough so that I wouldn't consider wasting my time on the Iron Man aspect added by Ultra-Nightmare.

I was slightly disappointed in the rune aspect, given that it felt like you were forced to use the unlimited ammunition rune as soon as you obtained it. The "rune challenges" were kind of lame too, although I'm not sure what they could have done to improve them.

The boss fights left a little to be desired too, as the only one that was moderately difficult to figure out was the Cyberdemon (hint: use the hologram). The only time I was forced to turn to a guide for help was on a few of the very-well-hidden secret areas, but never for boss fights.\t\t\t\t\t
