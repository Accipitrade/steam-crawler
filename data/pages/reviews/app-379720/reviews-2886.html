\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverything about Doom is masterfully crafted, making it one of the best, if not the best arena shooter in recent years. There are a lot of positive things that I can say about this game, so lets start:
+ Its one of the very few triple A titles that is well optimized and can run smoothly on a computer with the minimum specs ( granted there will be a few FPS drops during the course of your playthrough )
+ From the very beginning the game slowly drags you in, even if you are a newcomer to the franchise like I am, you'll love this game the moment you killed your first demon
+ The demons are well designed and each are incredibly unique ( only a few are a rehash of another demon ), they each have a different set of abilities and the player is presented with a number of ways to kill them
+ Each movement you make have momentum and contributes so much to the gameplay, especially during combat
+ The difficulty settings are just right and provides a fair amount of challenges on each difficulty which you can choose to your liking, whether you are a casual gamer who only plays the game for the experience, or a series veteran who wants a challenge, there's always something for everyone
+ On higher difficulties you'll die, a lot, but don't worry, because the checkpoint system is fair and with each death you get better and better at the game
+ Glory kills are fluid, gory, well animated, and never gets old. They never diminish the tension of combat and actually enhance the overall experience of the game
+ The game offers you a variety of weapons to utilize in your demonic onslaught, and every single one them feels powerful and a blast to use
+ The secrets in the campaign are well-hidden and encourages the player to utilize the map ( which is brilliantly designed ) and explore
+ The soundtrack fits just right with the flow of the game ( especially during combat ) and adds intensity during the game's action packed moments
+ The story, for what it's worth, actually works and you can delve deeper into the story by reading the collectible files foudn throughout the campaign
However the campaign does get SLIGHTLY repetitive, but that doesn't detract the overall experience which is phenomenal.
I haven't even tried the game's other features such as multiplayer or arcade yet, all I know is that I can't wait to sit down and play Doom again\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Best single player FPS campaign I've played since the wolfenstein reboot. Fast paced demon slaying action, with an incredible soundtrack to boot. All the guns are fun to play with and the game is challenging enough to be incredibly fun. Easily worth it for the campaign alone, the multiplayer is alright but not the best.

Pros:
-Gun variety and gameplay
-Level design and secret finding
-The codex's have pretty interesting lore(Surprising for DOOM)
-SOUNDTRACK(Rip And Tear might be my favorite video game song)
-Variety of enemies and the fun boss fights
-Perfect Length, didn't drag on past its welcome

Cons:
-Sometimes fell through the planet from the platforming. Twice I got stuck on the terrain and had to kill myself and reload.
-Voice acting was pretty uninspired. Not a big deal for a game like this but still a con
-Multiplayer felt ripped out of Call of Duty. Very meh

Absolutely incredible single player campaign. You'll get 11 hours minimum out of it, 15ish if you look around for all the secrets.\t\t\t\t\t
