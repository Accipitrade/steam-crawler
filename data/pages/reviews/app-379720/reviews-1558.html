In basic terms, it's the perfect translation of the '93 original being modernised (for 2016).
A story you could choose to involve yourself with, read through the extended lore and all that fluff. OR. Put the muzzle to the nearest demon's head and get trigger pulling without paying attention to whatever the NPC's or codex's say. Choice is yours.

The only real issue I find is that the game suffers from exploit fatigue in the third act, whilst still extremely fun don't get it twisted, the Super Shotgun is the be all, end all of most engagements, something I hope Eternal really irons out. Coupled with the upgrades for weapons and armour, anything below Ultra-Violence isn't worth your time in all honesty. The Super Shotgun makes fights trivial and in DOOM that's a bad thing to have happen, as it doesn't promote the player to learn and evolve their strategies.
It becomes a crutch device, and somewhat kills the learning experience for newbies as its so painfully broken that there's no reason to not use it most all the time across lower difficulties, directly affecting the combat system on return trips at higher end difficulties, making it jarring to have to re-learn how to fight properly.

Even with Eternal round the corner, DOOM 2016 is still relevant and well worth it's asking price. Packed with content (theoretically infinite with Snap-Map), collectables & secrets, hidden levels from classic games, pop-culture references and tasteful humour, it's the complete package.
Pick this up for the singleplayer elements, and you won't be disappointed. If you're looking for something more competitive in the multiplayer, arena shooter try looking around the Quake & Unreal Tournament scene as this game's MP has pretty much flat-lined.

My personal opinion is to play this game no lower than Ultra-Violence, it demands enough of you to learn how to play, but isn't so punishing it stops being fun.
Issues with weapon balance and upgrades by extension negate the need to learn how to actually play the game, hurting lesser experienced players on lower difficulties, as it will negatively impact how they may see the game, being either repetitive or boring.
Judging DOOM on the content packed in it, my own personal criticisms and what it's managed to achieve. It's easily an 8/10.
It's on sale regularly enough, which makes it even more of a no-brainer. Looking forward to see how iD improves on this when the sequel is finally released.\t\t\t\t\t
