I must kill the demons!

DOOM is one lucky franchise that is blessed with having no bad entries in its long blood-soaked history (yes, I do love Doom3) and the tradition continues with a game that revitalizes not only the series itself, but reminding us of simpler times when if anything moved you just needed to shoot it. The "daddy" of FPS games still got it. And how can you go wrong anyway, with such a simple premise: Rip and tear.

Ripping and tearing through hordes of ghastly demonic forces from hell to save the entire universe from peril, DOOM(2016) tones down the horror aspect that was prevalent in the previous entries - Doom64 and especially Doom3) and takes more of a "James Cameron" approach to problem solving. Gathering tons of guns, from beloved classics to some very interesting new additions and all coming with a twist of adding numerous cool upgrades it is one of themost fun gung-ho experiences in recent years, the epitome of going "guns-blazing".
This wouldn't be as much fun if it weren't for the beautifully designed and atmospheric environments which, even though not the maze-like labyrinths of the 90s, offer grand arenas with tons of big and small rooms and corridors to help you in your constant speedrunning as you avoid countless spawning enemies thrown at you, in a space base that really gives you the feeling of a grand scale experiment gone wrong.
But that's only half of what makes it so good, the other is the amazing and bombastic soundtrack that goes from hardcore heavy metal riffs, to slower more atmospheric tunes with quite a few nostalgic callback to the old 90s OST for good measure.

And perhaps the cherry on top is the funny and always angry hero, the Doom Slayer Doomguy. The devs knowing his place in pop culture, present him not as the ordinary unlucky marine of past games but as an eternal superman, fully aware of his mythical status and destined to "rip and tear" the demons' huge guts in an never ending struggle. This combined with the huge amounts of backstory info and lore you learn throughout the game was a complete surprise and at the same time quite funny and ironic, considering the famous words from one of the original creators you know who! about the importance of stories in video games.

Besides the big and juicy single-player campaign, DOOM offers your always welcomed multiplayer modes but also some new extra fun modes like arcade where you can try and top your friends' score in the leaderboards and also a Map Creation tool that you can use to make and share your own maps with the community.

I didn't encounter any bugs during my time playing this and the overall quality was great and well optimized even for my slightly weaker PC with high settings and no loss of graphical fidelity. The game can also be played with a gamepad if you are a madman.

DOOM is a reboot done right and one of the most exciting FPS games of recent years and I highly recommend it for fans of the genre, but in all honesty you've all probably got it already and don't need me to tell you that.
One final friendly advice: Be careful while trying to kill the demons. Because in the end, maybe you are the demons. And then you were a zombie.\t\t\t\t\t
