\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is SO good so far. I've beaten the game in Nightmare which was too fun. The story is there if you want it to be, if not you can skip past it. It's the only FPS that i've played (besides the original DOOM's) where you're punished if you try and take it slow, in order to survive you have to be as fast as possible. As you progress through the 13 levels they get bigger and more complex as you go and there's tons of secret areas, challanges, hidden pathways and doors for you to discover.

I'm currently half way through the second level on ultra-nightmare, I'm too chicken to go any further until I practice this level some more in nightmare.

The weapon and armor upgrade system lets you play how you want to when you want to.

The graphics are top-notch if you have the GPU horsepower to push it on ultra settings.

Replay value is huge with ultra-nightmare, snapmap, and a solid multiplayer.

Doom was my first game as a kid, here we are more than 20 years later and it's better than ever. I love this game so much that this is the first review I've written. Rip and tear.\t\t\t\t\t

Good;
Great graphics
its doom!
story is decent
The SP draws you into a different world; very immersive

Bad;

The biggest disapointed factor was the run and gun arcade feel; in a dark and stormy world of hell; going the more horror and tatical route would of made this game so much better. Doom 3 fans; look somewhere else... This mindless run and gun and kill endless randnom spawns.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving gotten approximately halfway through the campaign, I feel I can safely say that this is one of the best first-person shooter campaigns of the last decade, maybe even decade and a half. The game doesn't waste your time with overlong cutscenes or forced exposition, instead throwing you DIRECTLY into the action within ten seconds of the game starting. One of the biggest worries for many people was player speed, and thankfully, while it's not quite as fast as classic Doom, it's definitely fast enough that you'll soon be getting into the Doom groove, circlestrafing enemies and running around, avoiding fire and darting from enemy to enemy, blasting them into beautifully-rendered piles of flesh and guts. The weapons feel incredibly satisfying to use against the hordes of Hell (minus the BFG, but only because I haven't found it yet), and the game's new Glory Kill system is nowhere near as pace-breaking as I had feared it would be. Animations are quick, there's a great variety of animations, and best of all, they actually give you an incentive for risking your life by diving into a crowd of enemies to punch the head off of the one unlucky zombie in the crowd to get stunned by your awesomeness. Much of the rest of the game is oozing with classic Doom tropes: hordes of enemies, no reloading, mazelike level design with oodles of secrets to discover, powerups and ammo pickups and deathtraps galore. The best part about the whole thing? It's actually a challenging shooter (a rarity these days) that will push your reflexes to their breaking point. Enemies no longer simply shamble around, occasionally throwing off an attack and waiting to be pumped full of lead. Instead, they are lively, angry and aggressive as all get-out. Imps dart around, constantly switching vantage points to get potshots at you with fireballs, Pinkies charge like rhinos towards you, safe in the notion that you can't damage them from the front, the new Summoner enemy teleports all over the map, throwing wave after wave of energy, Hell Knights and Barons chase you down like rabid lions, Lost Souls kamikaze towards your position before exploding in your face, all while you stand alone against the toughest incarnations of our favorite hellspawn to date. Thankfully, you're armed to the teeth with all the classic weapons as well as some new ones, which I won't spoil so that the elation of finding them can be preserved. What I will discuss is the upgrade system, which is a modern-ish trend most people didn't want, yet it works extremely well in this game and adds depth and variety to a tried-and-true formula. The better you are at the game's combat, the more Upgrade Points you will earn, which you can use to alter your guns to have different attributes. At the same time, scattered throughout the levels are mobile Drones that will provide you with a special weapon Mod which acts as a secondary fire. For example, the Shotgun gets a grenade-like Explosive Shot as well as a 3-round Charge Burst, the Rocket Launcher gets lock-on missiles and remote detonation, the Plasmagun gets a Heat-Blast which essentially acts as a plasma grenade as well as a Stun Bomb, etc. Each of these mods can be upgraded, increasing blast radius, reducing cooldown, adding enemy penetration, yadda yadda yadda, and although you may only use one Mod at a time, you can switch between Mods at will for all weapons, creating a very versatile character with a plethora of methods for dealing with enemies. God, there are SO MANY things to talk about with this game and only so much space to fit it all in without dragging this out, so I'll go ahead and say that for the campaign alone, this is a MUST-BUY. You are doing yourself a disservice by not playing this game the first chance you get. It's a return to form for the faltering Id Software, and a damn spectacular entry in an already incredible franchise.




Did I mention the game runs fantastically as well? No crashes, very little slowdown, consistent FPS for the six hours I played, and to top it all off, the graphics are stunning and striking.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t
