\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a really damn good game despite all the flaws in it and I think that's all thanks to id completely nailing the gameplay loop and game feel.

The two original games came out over two decades ago, yet aged so well that even today most people can enjoy them without any real need to adjust expectations. Shooting was fun, it felt good, it looked cool and it sounded metal. Despite all the progress games made through the years, it's still a tall order to follow up on these classics (hell, they did kinda fail at that in 2004).

If you play DOOM however, the first level alone demonstrates right away that they know what was expected of them. The intro cutscene is short, but manages to introduce you to the Doom Slayer and just how ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ cool he is, as he rips out the chains that hold him to a the tomb he was encased in and smashes a few demons with his bare hands. For the most part the story bits in this game are all like this, short and to the point, as if aware of how little people care about the plot in a Doom game and how mediocre the writing is. The only exceptions to this are maybe a dozen or less points in the game where it locks you in and forces you to listen to dry exposition about ♥♥♥♥ that doesn't really matter.

Shooting is where it's all at though, so who cares about how bad the story is. All the classic weapons and enemies are back but are reworked in a way that makes them more complex and interesting. Aside from pistol they're all really satisfying to shoot. The sounds are impactful and juicy and I'm particularly happy about how energy weapons, which are often done poorly actually sound like they pack a punch. The animations and effects also just scream power, for example assault rifle has a slow rate of fire but bullets leave the gun with a flashy bang and leave a trace leading to the impact on the enemy model where bullets lodge into them with miniature explosion and sparks and blood. This is further amplified when you upgrade the gun and zoom mode gets "experimental" ammo that hits enemies with a flasher explosion.

Weapon mods are a huge difference between this game and old ones and it's also one of the things that feels like it wasn't all that well thought out. You get mods from field drones found on the levels, usually you'll be getting about 1 or 2 per level until about second to last level of the game and you can later upgrade those mods using weapon upgrade point that you earn through combat, exploration and level missions with final upgrade requiring you to complete some task, like doing x headshots and such. These mods are fun to use but they're very poorly balanced so in the end there's only 1 actually good one per gun. It eliminates any need for mid-combat swapping or any decision making in what to upgrade first. You might as well even skip half the drones and get half as many weapon upgrade points cause you'll only be needing them for an achievement.

Another big difference are the runes. Just like with mods they're not really created equal. Some are just boring if sometimes useful upgrades like enemies dropping more ammo on glory kills, dropping ammo on glory kills, there's one that makes you faster after a glory kill. Meanwhile there's one that literally makes you not die when you should have or gives you infinite ammo as long as you have 100/75 armor. The whole system is kinda clunky and there's not really a significant enough impact from any of them to really get you swapping runes in and out for maximum advantage.

As I said, all the enemies are also back and they all got updates. In general they all concern making them more distinct from one another. The only real huge change in my opinion are the imps who were made super mobile. They can run up and smack you or they can turn around to fire a cheeky fireball at you if you chase them or they can climb up somewhere to get a good vantage point to fire at you from. The best part of enemy changes overall is that they all serve some distinct and different purpose and demand player to consider their priorities or best way to deal with them (though super shotgun seems to be great at dealing with all of them).

I've also come to have quite a few gripes with the game along the way:

1. RPG nonsense
DOOM's progression is entirely dependent on you going out of your way to find upgrades, some of them are intentionally placed along your main path but that's a small part of them. You can absolutely beat the game without them but I wouldn't say it's good design to make a huge part of the gameplay ignorable. If you do however try to get all the things to make your weapons or suit better, the game will demand over and over that you go out of your way and search absolutely everywhere or do things that you wouldn't normally do. In the end it led me to just cheesing some things to get the upgrades since otherwise I could've not gotten an opportunity to fulfill the conditions for some upgrades. DOOM is a fantastic game when it gives you full freedom of action, when you decide how to move, who to shoot and what to use for that. These challenges however force you into doing something else and that something is usually less fun.

2. Slapdash design of most encounters.
I think the sheer fun of shooting saves a lot of encounter design in this game cause very few spots in the game felt like they had some direction behind them when it comes to enemies thrown at you and arenas you encounter them in. For the most par the game just loves to go from weak to strong. At first you get to shoot up zombies, soldiers and imps, then the game will drop in something beefier and maybe cap off with big boys. The enemy roles are distinct yet there's rarely synergy to how they're used. I think part of that is related to how enemy spawns work. Instead of them all being in specific positions or coming in waves from natural spots, they just pop into existence with a short portal animation and it can be related to killing a certain enemy or stepping into a certain trigger or clearing the room. It's incredibly basic and while it can lead to very hectic combat there's usually no strategy to it aside from keeping your distance, always moving and picking off whoever is more annoying,

3. Homing attacks and enemies ignoring verticality.
Hell Knights and Barons of Hell are the biggest bastards when it comes to this. Sometimes their attacks don't make sense in terms of physics, I had a hell knight jump almost entirely horizontally over a damn long distance and that kind of stuff never feels right. It's also annoying when they spawn out of sight and decide to hit you with a slam you can't hear or see coming. Other point is that a lot of enemies can simply jump up any distance which makes vertical positioning irrelevant.

4. Poor difficulty balance.
I beat the game on Hurt Me Plenty and Nightmare and while nightmare often provides a challenge I want it also scales enemy damage in a way that leads to a lot of cheap deaths. A lot of attacks are nigh on impossible to see coming like soldiers energy blast, pinkies rounding a corner on full speed or imp's arched throw from where you can't see them. There's also a problem with glory kills that lock you into an animation which can lead to someone killing you without ability to dodge for daring to use a major game mechanic.

That's pretty much it. Despite these things I still had a blast with the game and there's a lot of content here for people who want it. From higher difficulties to replays with upgrades, classic levels, arcade, ♥♥♥♥♥♥ multiplayer or snapmaps.
I'm running out of characters but I want to give special mention to amazing adaptive soundtrack in this game. It's heavy as hell, it fits levels and pace of battles to a t and didn't get tiring in over 30 hours.

If you love FPS most definitely give this a go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is how you reboot a franchise people. DOOM is a masterpiece of Action, Gore and blazing fast combat. The game is a must buy for every FPS fan. And you reall should'nt have an excuse not to buy it, since its only 20 bucks and goes on sale regularly.

With its roughly 13 hour long campaign, DOOM has a very simple goal: Kill demons and have fun doing it. And it does achieve exactly that, without getting stale or repetetive. The variety of enemies ensures to keep the battles fun and engaging, since you also have to use your brain. Making good decisions and using every weapon in your arsenal to its fullest potential is something you need to learn on the higher difficulties.

Speaking of which, the weapons in DOOM are nothing short of iconic. Of course there is the rocket launcher, chaingun, super shotgun and BFG, which need no introduction. But the new additions like the Gauss Canon and the Heavy Assault Rifle fit perfectly into the game and feel awesome to use.

In short: Just buy DOOM. Treat yourself.\t\t\t\t\t
