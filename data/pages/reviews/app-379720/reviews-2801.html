\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSolid and fluid gunplay plus eyepopping environments and effects make for a very satisfying DOOM reboot.

Nice bits
- Masterful optimization allows the game to run on Ultra at a constant G-Synced 75 fps on a mere GTX 1060
- Impactful, satisfying and super-smooth gunplay, learned from the Wolfenstein reboot and improved upon
- Special "glory kills" blend seamlessly into intense periods of combat
- Fluid and responsive controls allow for fast three-dimensional movement (terrain-dependent)
- Beautifully rendered and atmospheric environment, envelopping and immersive visual and sound effects
- Excellent variety of weapons and weapon mods, including the extremely satisfying chainsaw
- Bits of tongue-in-cheek humour and comic relief inserted between periods of combat
- Good variety of secrets and collectibles

Less nice bits
- Megatexture has been vastly improved since the days of RAGE but occasional pop-ins still occur, even on an SSD (though not an NVMe one)
- Some textures still look blurry/jaggy at the edges close-up, even at TSSAA 8x and Nightmare virtual texture size
- Suit upgrades, weapon upgrades and special abilities may not please the old-school DOOM purist
- Storyline is mostly an excuse for the action
- "Glory kill" animations can get boring, though the finisher move you get depends on how you approach the enemy
- Takes up nearly 80GB of disc space\t\t\t\t\t
