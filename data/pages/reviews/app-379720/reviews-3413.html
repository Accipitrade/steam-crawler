\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's Doom.

Fast, gory, fluid action. Jammed with secrets and bonuses. The upgrade system isn't over-complicated like in so many games, and does what it is supposed to - it adds variety to the combat.

Classic Doom, at it's most exhilarating and particularly most exercised in the more excellent community maps over the years, is about overwhelming odds being handled with grace, a combination of movement, awareness and weapon management. Doom 2016 taps into this well, with chaotic set pieces that are breathlessly paced and require the most capable reflexes. It's a modern FPS that is challenging but not excruciating.

The monster design sits somewhere between the colourful aesthetic of the original games and the more contemporary iD tech style of recent years (Doom 3 onwards). The Baron of Hell is very much a beefy, slightly silly looking pink behemoth that leaps around and smashes the ground as if it's having a tantrum. The Hell Knight is a faster, angrier take on the design seen in Doom 3 (which was pretty scary in that incarnation). Imps are fast like in Doom 3, but with added movement dynamics, as they scurry, climb and crawl, throwing fireballs behind them as they run for cover. Catching them mid-leap is very satisfying.

I'm still playing the game, and haven't played the multiplayer as of yet (beyond the BETA anyway, which I enjoyed), but I have sampled snapmap and I am looking forward to really experimenting with it. There is clearly a lot of potential within the logic system to create some great maps and scenarios. I hope more snappable areas are made available in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

From first level onwards I found myself running around the level, shooting anything that moves, jumping on higher ground while grabbing health/ammo/armor desperately trying to stay alive.

What was probably the most positive surprise is the level design. You are not just thrown into a linear corridor after linear corridor like in Doom 3, instead you get open large levels quite similar to original games. Also yes, there are numerous secrets to be found, I think I missed 6 in second level alone.

What I thought I would hate were the glory kills, however they are quite quick to pull off and keep the pace up by giving you some extra health (which you will need on higher difficulties). Perhaps the game relies too much on this system, them again whenever you take damage it is 100% your own fault, almost any source of damage can be avoided by a skillfull player. This doesn't make the game easy though, UV is definitely giving me a run for my money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith DOOM, ID Software perfect the formula they started with Wolfenstein: The New Order and The OId Blood. Probably the most fun i've had in a single player experience in years. I'll be updating this review once i've fully completed the game and ran through more hours of multiplayer (as well as adding review for snapmap). These are my impressions after about 6 hours.

PROS
  • Movement is smooth and fluid. Reintroduces old school mechanics like double jumping and removes dashing for a classic approach. Your character moves fast without any interuptions through reloading or sprinting for a constant barrage of bullets. Fast without feeling floaty.
  • The glory kill system was implemented in a way that serves a purpose. When low on health, it will give you a boost to continue the fight. They also introduce runes you can equip to speed up the animation to a split second, which you can use once you get tired of seeing the animations over and over.
  • Guns all feel unique and powerful. The progression system is nicely done as well, where you can equip mods for secondary fires, and level them up with points to make them even stronger. Most of all, none of the guns suffer falloff for late game: I'm still using the regular shotgun during lategame.
  • Maps are perfectly streamlined to keep you moving for run-and-gun action, as well as allowing the player full freedom to backtrack and grab a missed secret or powerup. Its easy to tell where you need to go without getting lost so you can focus on combat.
  • While there are several times where the game wrestles control of your camera to focus on a story element, they are almost nonexistent, and are exclusive to bosses or important character scenes. All story is relayed through audio clips and text logs that you can go back and read to your pleasure. You can either get the full story, or just ignore it entirely and keep chugging along.
  • Last but not least: Replayability. You have the ability to go back to older missions to grab secrets and bonuses, while keeping all of your weapons and items from your progression. Being able to go back with your upgraded gear is something that few games these days let you do, but adds so much more to the experience. It removes the stress of leveling up weapons, and you don't feel like there are constraints to your enjoyment of late game pickups. Most games give you the most powerful addons or perks with just the final mission left (Looking at you, Dishonored). This game lets you get your mileage.

CONS
  • Every way the single player succeeds, I feel the multiplayer fails. This is Doom, not Quake. Your character is floaty and every weapons range goes to the extreme: You either have to be up in their face with no long range, or you have the weapons that can one shot from across the map. It feels like medium range is nonexistent. Also the armor and health system doesn't translate as well in multiplayer nowadays. Too many times I've emptied my entire clips into enemies only for them to just run up and two-shot melee me to death.
  • Weapon loadouts in multiplayer. This isn't COD. Doesn't make as much sense since you can't even add mods to your weapons. everything you can change is cosmetic. This is its own bullet because I feel this is the crux of my frustrations with the online mode.
  • Game has crashed twice on me on my high end system. Seems to happen when i'm switching tabs too fast between the mini map and weapon mods.

If you buy this game, but it for solo play. Multiplayer is a bit too chaotic and feels like it needs balancing before it settles into an enjoyable experience. Otherwise, this is easily one of my favorite games this year.\t\t\t\t\t
