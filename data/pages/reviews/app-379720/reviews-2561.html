7/10
DOOM's core gameplay carries an otherwise forgettable game. The soundtrack being incredible adds half a point.


Notice: Review is only for single-player campaign. I was in no way compelled to explore any other part of DOOM.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLong story short: It's like Masterchief from Halo starred in Peter Jackson's Braindead, it's an unhinged and unchained gorefest.

"They are rage, brutal, without mercy. But you... You will be worse. Rip and tear, until it is done."

DOOM is a pure first person shooter, that's it, that's all. You run, jump and gun down anything in your way with inhuman strength using the classic Doom arsenal. Finding secrets along the way will give you upgrade points, which you can use to power up your guns or enhance your armor. There are also Rune Trial, which give you special perks.

At the end of Doom 64, the Doom Slayer closed the portal to Hell behind him after going inside... After "aeons" of tormenting demons in hell and being trapped there, a deep demonic voice wakes him up from his long slumber with a single trigger phrase that makes manchurian candidates look like kindergarden kiddies. Unlike Doom 3, where you felt trapped in a Mars base with demons, this one is the opposite. I think at this point, the goal of the game is obvious.

The art style and overall graphics are excellent. As mentioned in the "long story short", this game pulls no punches and displays the goriest, bloodiest and juiciest Doom of all. Brutal Doom's good and all, but this one here goes full 3D and doesn't give a damn about ESRB's rating system. Rip an arm off an enemy and cave his skull in with it? That's ok here. Bash an enemy's brain open with your foot? That's ok here. Saw an enemy in half with a chainsaw? No problem. On a technical side, DOOM is coded very well. I'm using a potato PC. I'm not supposed to meet the requirement to play this game, both with CPU and GFX, yet I can still play it on Medium at 60fps using 1920x1080 resolution. It looks and feels fantastic to play.

The sound and music are also well produced and nicely complements the game's atmosphere. The music is a fusion of electronic beats and heavy metal guitars, sometimes adding heavenly choirs to the mix. It's guarenteed to keep your blood pumping and keeping you entranced. Some of these tracks reminded me of the first Resident Evil movie soundtrack which was composed by Marylin Mason.

https://youtu.be/ZUNNXecb6nA

The overall feel of the game, when taking every aspect in consideration, is simple and straightforward. It's a bloody roller-coaster ride. The idea of taking the ride is hyping you up, riding it is spectacular and when you get off you'll want to ride it again. Sadly, a roller-coaster ride can sometime be fleeting. After taking the ride for a couple of times, the thrill might wear off. The same can be said for DOOM. I'm not saying the game is short or the maps are small, I'm saying the basic gameplay mechanic might get old fast for some, especially veterans of the genre who've seen it all. Guns go pew-pew, demons go boom-boom, oldest story in the world. Don't get me wrong. If you go in this game with a fun-loving mindset, you'll get your bang for your buck.

One detail that annoys me about DOOM is the fact that it's kinda late to the modern FPS party. 12 years to make a sequel to Doom 3 is almost criminal, kinda reminding me what happened with Duke Nukem Forever. At least, DOOM sticked to the original source material, both in gameplay and story, while DNF didn't really seem to know what to do with itself. DOOM feels like a dinosaur time-traveled in the future. Primal and brutal, yet bold and simple. Not sure if you should take it seriously or just laugh comically at it's prmitive nature. But if DOOM is a dinosaur, it's a T-Rex on steroid.

Lastly, I lament how Killing Floor beat Doom to the co-op fps market. In many ways, KF tried to continue Doom's legacy in it's absence and it gained a lot of traction. KF was a mod for Unreal 2004. Then it turned into a full game release and spawned a sequel. Both games have been played more than Doom in the last decade and have been lucrative for Tripwire with their cosmetic catalog. Because Doom is a famous franchise, maybe ID didn't want to tarnish the brand name by trying to be like everyone else, pushing out titles every 1-2 years, like the Call of Duty franchise... Hopefully DOOM Eternal won't go down this cursed path. I'm praying hard.

It's hard to not recommend such a well-polished game, even with it's sincerely simple gameplay. 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t
