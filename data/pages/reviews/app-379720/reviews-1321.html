-=-Overall-=-
This soft reboot of the DOOM series really does its job well. The frenetic, fast-paced combat really proves that a simple mistake can be the difference between life and death. The combat system is good, and the game is really enjoyable. There are a few bugs here and there, but they likely won't result in you punching your monitor. If you want to grind and get through the game all in one sitting, get your stress ball ready because you're gonna be here a while and you're gonna get pissed at the game. The replayability is high if you want to go through again and collect all the Doom Slayer toy collectibles or access all the Classic Doom maps, or even if you want to try and go through on Ultra-Nightmare mode (really difficult, don't recommend). The multiplayer mode is fun, but personally I preferred playing the game for the campaign. All in all, if you're looking for a good campaign around 12 hours long, DOOM 2016 is a must have. Not to mention, DOOM Eternal recently came out at the time of writing, I'll be getting that one too.

Rating
Gameplay Loop
9 (Excellent gameplay. Possibly one of the best shooter campaigns I've played, because it goes the opposite direction and promotes an aggressive playstyle rather than hiding behind a cover all the time and waiting for your chance to shoot. The fact that getting glory kills gives you health and you can use the chainsaw to get ammo whenever you want as long as you don't abuse that is genius. Then, why am I not giving it a 10? TBH, I would have. But the game doesn't make too much effort to have variety in its general gameplay loop. All games revolve around doing the same thing, but do something or the other to have some variation with every loop. In Doom, every fight begins to blur into each other and not have anything unique in itself. They could have done a lot with the terrain, some aberration or uniqueness with every level, or having some new enemy getting introduced every loop, but because of the lack of that the game gets very repetitive. But that wasn't that much a trouble because I finished this game over the course of 2 months. Allowed me to take my time and enjoy it more than what I would on a week long grill. But what I disliked even more was the bits with parkour. The visual design didn't make it too clear where exactly to go. The mechanics aren't made for parkour. And there's random platforms moving at some points, or random holes in the ground or roof that you need to go through which makes it weird.)
Narrative
5 (I was excited when my guy broke the monitor that was telling me what to do. I was like "Damn, this is different". But then you keep hearing either Vega or the Samuel Hayden guy talk. Their voices and words are as generic as can be. Please don't tell me it's on purpose to make some commentary because I wouldn't mind it until the game locks me in a room and forces me to listen to story that is not good enough for me to care about. It's basically a cutscene even if I can move, because, heck, what is the player gonna do in the room you closed him into? Jump around, be a boob. It's not just the kind of game that needs a story, and the promise in the beginning of the game when I smash the monitor isn't really followed up too much when I keep doing what the voices want me to do.)
Visuals
7 (Now, the game looks excellent. But just like I said before - everything that is related to the combat is great and everything related to explore is garbage. And that philosophy permeates the visual design as well. Enemies look distinct, and so do their attacks. Which was my favorite part of the whole art direction. But there are so many doors in the level that will never open, but look identical to the ones that will. The visual design is such that the "blinking button" is not even an aid, but a necessity. I like games where the visual design will automatically tell you where to go.)
Mechanics
9 (The shooting. The movement. The behavior of the enemies and the interaction with the environment. Everything was impeccable. This is the most important thing about a shooter and they nailed it. Only thing that was off for me was the parkour aspect.)
Challenge
9 (Would've given it a 10 if not for damage spongey bosses a lot of times, on Ultraviolence. For the most part, the difficulty was very well balanced though. There can only be 16 or so enemies at one time across the level which is a very fair amount to both have it be a challenging playground without being overwhelming. It could be very easy to make the game unfairly punishing which this was not. The difficulty was enough to promote me to use all weapons in my arsenal depending on what enemies I was dealing with. Which brings me to...)
Variety
8 (Great enemy diversity. Every enemy has a personality of its own. It's own attacks. It's own behavior and health gauge and damage gauge. And I have a good amount of weapons to deal with them. Loved the diversity here. Would've even given it a 9 or 10, if so many of the weapons they introduced didn't feel kinda useless despite the variety. Another thing I didn't like was too many upgrades. Weapon upgrades. Rune upgrades. Suit upgrades. And one that makes you choose between Health, Ammo and Armor - which, by the way, is the only one which would have any noticeable change in your experience of the game. They added "too much" in those aspects, and used that as an excuse to propel me towards exploring the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ level design, which again brings me to...)
Mapping
6 (I'm VERY conflicted here. On one hand I loved the combat levels as they promoted freedom of movement which is how the game is meant to be played. But the traversal and platforming levels were ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ garbage. Maybe I had more trouble with them than others did, but I remember getting lost every once in a while because there's a random hole on the wall that needs jumping into, or a random door that has a small crack that needs to be opened. Or a button in the corner of the map that you need to press to open this door. And the compass is pointing towards the final destination not that corner? Eh, just really wonky work there IMO.)
Sound Design
9 (The sound design is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ phenomenal. The music that goes with the combat consitutes to a lot of the intensity of the battle. The gun noises are all distinct from each other. The enemies sound different from each other. The sound design is great. But the only reason I don't give it a 10 is that I also include the voice acting in this parameter which was, if I put it mildly, terrible. No personality and so easy to just hear and not listen at all.)
Interface
6 (Only reason I give it a 5 is because they were able to make every button shine that was supposed to be pressed to open the next door. Which happens a lot. But the interface was really shabby too. The 3D level map was shabby to look at and try to make sense of. The upgrade trees, all of them, were clunky. Sure, you do figure it all out but it's needlessly complicated. The overall user interface was "just" serviceable enough IMO.)
Pricing & Performance
6 (The price could be lower seeing how old it is now, but is still a must-play game for people who love shooters to pick up on a sale. Had a bad experience with performance though. The game doesn't work well with Steam Cloud Sync. So once I played for 3-4 hours (my longest session) offline because my net dropped. And guess what, next time I open the game - I'm back to where I'd started the last session. Almost dropped playing the game, but did pick it up a couple of weeks later and forced myself to play all that again. I had no trouble with the optimization, but the game definitely crashed on me a couple of times, after I Alt-Tabbed for something else.)
Replay Value
7 (Halfway after the game, I was playing to "finish" the game more than "play" the game. But I can see myself enjoying it say several months later again when I want some brainless blind action. While I'm pretty much done with Doom for a while - it definitely has all the ingredients to make me come back sometime.)
Online/DLC
-

SCORE : 7.4/10
