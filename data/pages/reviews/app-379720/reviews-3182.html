Price & Performance
Really good price for what it's going for during the Steam Sale. I don't neccesarily agree with the 60$ regular price, but i'll explain that later on.

Performance-wise, despite what the listed requirements are, I still manage to run medium settings using what I have currently:

Proccesor: AMD FX-6300\nGPU: NVIDIA GTX 660\nRAM: 16GB\nWindows 10 (64 Bit)\n

While I obviously can't run ultra settings, I would say there is still some leg room on lower-end rigs to still run the game well. Definitely a very-optimized game; It even runs better than other big-name titles in my steam library such as Fallout 4 or GTA V.

I've seen some reviews criticize the game for crashing issues, although I haven't experienced any crashing since i've started playing; i'd assume the crashes relate to driver issues that are now fixed.

While relatively smooth, There actually was a point in the campaign that I fell through the map & died after glory killing a Mancubus. I'm primarily pinning the blame that the game attempted to throw me up over after the kill, however since enemies do occasionally clip through the floor/objects, issues like this are still possible. However, it only occured once & I would likely not be able to recreate the glitch.

Gameplay & Story

While I'm not describing the whole story, I can easily say that the campaign really does live up to it's legacy. Very fast-paced, heart-pounding action that requires a quick trigger finger. Glory kills encourage you to use your mobility as much as possible even when in a critical state of low health. With that, the game keeps you very involved & never gets old as you explore Mars & the depths of Hell.

Story is really interesting as well. There are secrets throughout that will definitely be worth the time finding to add to the experience overall. Just make sure even though you are pillaging through the game, that you take the occasional moment to look for the secrets, some are pretty interesting.

Main Criticism

While I still find enjoyment in it, I must say that the current state of multiplayer is really atrocious compared to everything else. Some weapons are terribly OP, along with it being difficult to find anyone playing anything besides TDM. Not to mention unlesss you partner up, you are matched based on location since there is no server list of any kind. Would really like to see some major improvements to multiplayer to match the quality of everything else this game has to offer.



In the end, I'd highly recommend a purchase while the price is 40% off. The campaign is defnitely worth it especially for Doom fans. I'll be looking forward to future improvements in the game, as I don't see any reason to why the little things won't get fixed.\t\t\t\t\t
