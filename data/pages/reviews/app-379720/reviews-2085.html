\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the game. I enjoyed the graphics and sound, gameplay is fun and entertaining. Level design is impressive and you can play SP several times, discover new spots and secrets. And the best «in the first day, in the first battle» experience you will ever had. The Mars atmosphere and UAC-facilities are awesome. Story line is simple. All dialogues are well-written and not overwhelmed with unnecessary information. Everything is pretty straightforward.

The only major flaw of this game is multiplayer. Yes, the game has entry levels mode (up to 10-level players) for novices and beginners. It lasts very short and ends very soon. You will be faced against skilled players of 50 level each rank. I never had good balance in battles. Some of the skilled players in game said me they spend up to 100 hours in it be really good in multiplayer. You may win with convincing victory or lose it with big score (e.g. you:2 enemy: 10 or something like that). I almost never had close-to-victory games. Anyway, if you are good in Quake-related games and you are ready to spend a lot of ours to master weapons and techniques feel free to join multiplayer battles.

Otherwise stay in SP and enjoy the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was not impressed with Doom when it was first released, but I decided to get it on a sale due to the nice reviews, and my initial impression was right. Doom is a stupid console arena shooter, and other than the theme and the name has nothing to do with the original Doom games. I would describe it as a dumbed down version of Doom 3 where you run around and alternate between executing poor retarded enemies that just stand around and let you shoot them, or executing poor retarded enemies as they keep teleporting in if you are in an arena and the developers felt that there were not enough bullet sponges around originally.
I feel that the enemies have no chance to fight back as long as you keep moving, and my general feeling when playing the game is of being insulted and patronized every step of the way.

I did not get very far before giving up, as it dawned on me that this game really isn't worth my time or money, but from what I gathered the game features wildly inaccurate weapons, enemies are bullet sponges that rarely hit you, but when they do it hurts a lot, so it seems like gameplay almost entirely consists of keeping enemies out of melee range, dodging a few projectiles that they shoot occasionally, and spraying with the weapons you have ammo remaining for until they drop.

I would like to take some time to mention that the game automatically detected that I have a joypad connected to my computer, so of course that means that I want to use it to play an FPS with, and changing controls displayed in the game or remapping the keyboard and mouse controls did not seem to even be an option.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great game that managed to get the feeling of the old doom in the contemporary gaming scene, adding anything you could expect from a modern FPS while keeping the originals’ spirit alive.

While I liked doom 3, there where a couple of things that felt wrong, like the more alien looking demons and the luck of monsters infighting, it was by all means less demonic and cyberpunk mix than the original dooms.

This doom however seem to have fixed that, and really I don’t feel like there is anything I have to say against this game. Everything feel like the original dooms upgraded, more detailed but always true to the originals spirit.

I have to mention that I managed to play in medium settings with Vulkan in my below minimum specs, potato system that is an i3 2120, 8GB 1333 RAM and a 1GB 7850 OC to the speeds of 7870. The game played fine with frames from 25-45fps. The only area that I got terrible performance was once outside of VEGA’s building with the snow and all those particles dropping me down to 15fps, but it is a very small portion of game so it didn’t affected my gameplay much.

Also the music is great, very immersive and very cinematic, playing the right tune at the right time can make all the difference in the world.\t\t\t\t\t
