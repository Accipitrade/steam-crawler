\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM does a great job following in the footsteps of its predecessors while improving upon certain aspects. It serves as a technologically jaw dropping showcase, just like every other Doom game. This is nothing like Doom 3, closer to the first two but still a bit distinct from them due to its focus on vertical gameplay at times.

What separates this game from most other shooters is how stylistic combat is. It is like a dance, extremely coordinated and on higher difficulties it requires such coordination from the player. Enemies work together remarkably well, different enemy combinations of enemies in different types of environments require different tactics.

I don't know why this game even has easier difficulty modes, that defeats the point. Play on Ultra-Violence at the bare minimum.\t\t\t\t\t
