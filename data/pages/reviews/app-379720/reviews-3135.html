\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the BEST FPS of the last 5 years. I can't remember when I had so much fun in an FPS... and I play a LOT of FPS'. The last FPS to really stand out like this was FC3 Blood Dragon. Even then, this game is a one of a kind. The gameplay/action is more fluid than the 2004 tsunami.

This game runs like Terry Fox and is silkier than Scarlet Johanssen's butt. Graphics aren't the best I've seen, but you'll be running around so much that you won't care about static graphics. I'm usualy a graphics slu+, but that's only because most FPS' are shoot and cover. With this constant movement gameplay, there's no time to sit by and smell the roses. It's kill or be killed. The only stationary graphics you'll see are the guns you're carrying - rest assured they're all gorgeously animated and brutally effective. The variety leaves nothing to be desired.

I'm usually not a big fan of metal/rock, but the music in this game rounds off the experience and makes it the total package. DOOM as much of an assault on your eyes and ears as the Shock and Awe campaign... only this really is "Mission accomplished" and in a good way ( ͡° ͜ʖ ͡°)

Simply amazing game. DOOM is GOTY and simply a must buy.

I do disagree with their choice recently to require constant online DRM, but I've had few issues with the game so I can't complain. Besides, I want to support the devs for this mind blowing experience.

DOOM is life. DOOM is Love. DOOM is FPS nirvana.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom was recomended to me as "Awesome." So I just started playing it and it really lives up to my expectations. It feels exactly like when I played the original Doom as a kid- the graphics are better, the sound is better, and the action is true 3D... but other than the 'modern-ness' of it, it recaptures the 'giggle factor' and nostalgia of playing the original. It's very fast paced and action packed, gory, has fun weapons... an awesome chain saw... and is in general a great FPS. It's the FPS those of us who grew up with the original Wolfenstein/Doom/Quake expect from ID.

It's also the first game I've really used the Steam Controller on- and it has a learning curve but ends up being awesome! I REALLY tried to use the controller in Fallout 4, but Fallout's controller engine isn't set up for simultaneous use of keyboard and mouse (unless I missed something somewhere), which meant the Steam Controller's touchpad had to use joystick emulation. It was a HORRIBLE experience and after trying it for a few hours and screwing around with every setting I could think of I went back to the 360 controller. I decided to try the Steam Controller on a brand new game and hope the experience was better. It absolutely is. This is the first game where I've found the Steam Controller to really shine. Admittedly I'm a casual gamer so the adjustment may have been easier that way, but the touchpad + gyro-control experience is awesome. If more of the new games are designed with this type of control in mind and people are willing to actually take the time to get out of their comfort zone and learn a new control style I think they'll be very impressed.\t\t\t\t\t
