\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot sure how many times I have played the original Doom, but it was a lot. Then I heard last year that this was coming out. I admit that I had my doubts about it, considering that Bethesda was involved and my thoughts that they would somehow f^&k it up. Well, thankfully, that didn't happen. I bought this one just yesterday and I'm hooked, just like when I first played the original. For those of you not familiar with Doom(however few of you there are, my condolences), the premise of the game is simple: pick up everything you find, shoot everything in your path, and try not to bite it in the process. I'll admit, I started the campaign on "I'm too young to die" and there's still plenty of demons to smoke. Can't wait to see how much "nightmare" lives up to it's description. I also liked the new glory kill feature; in fact, I try to finish off a lot of demons in that fashion(considering I usually lose a lot of health in stand-up fights; you know, the way Doom is meant to be played). Haven't tried multiplayer yet, but I probably will once I get through the campaign a few times. Until then, I'm definitely going to enjoy the everloving f#@k out of this one.\t\t\t\t\t

You get a weapon, you blast the crap out of demons, you get another weapon, you blast more demons differently. It's quick and extremely well optimized, and the action is incredibly satisfying (you just cannot get sick of ripping a demon's head apart with your two hands). This is the perfect reimagining of the classic Doom that spawned the FPS genre. Id always makes games to showcase their engines, but this one actually stands on its own as a very fun game.

1. Runs like melted butter on everything with a GPU
2. Looks incredible
3. Frantic non-stop demon killing action
4. A bunch of unique weapons and upgrades to those weapons
5. Just enough story to make you care
6. Gives you a nostalgia trip by proxy\t\t\t\t\t
