\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf the vast selection of games available today are like all the different meals of the world, Doom is pure comfort food. It doesn't need to be terribly innovative, exotic, or immersive; it just needs to perfect the ages-old recipe its based on (and it does).

If you played first-person shooters on PC in the 90's, you might consider this a homecoming. All the elements that made those classic titles so enduring are in place here:

*Satisfying, well-designed single player campaign

*Silent protagonist with little or no backstory

*Big weapons that trade realism for over-the-top (and creative) functionality

*Arsenal that comes with you (i.e. no trying to decide which weapon to swap out and carry)

*Levels with lots of platform jumping

*Health that depletes, and must be collected from pickups

*Traveling to other dimensions, for a mid-game change of environment

*Narrative that unfolds mostly while on the move, with minimal interuptions to the action

*Insane powerups that let you wreak havoc for a short time (hearing the Quad Damage noise again after all these years was fun)

Now, I'm not saying that FPS's need to adhere to these conventions. We always need games that try to revolutionize their genres, but once in a while, a shooter should throw everything out and get back to the basics, like Doom does. Even if you have no nostalgic attachment to the franchise, there's still a very solid, smooth-running, nice looking action game here worth your attention. You need no prior knowledge of Doom lore to jump in.

(Note: I'm only rating this game on the single player campaign.)\t\t\t\t\t
