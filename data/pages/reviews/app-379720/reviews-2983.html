\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I haven't really been a huge Doom fan. I played the shareware version of the original way back in the day, which I liked, but never got into it beyond that.

I started hearing some good things about this game and grabbed it on sale.

Holy. Cow.

This is one of the most fun games I have EVER played. The controls and mechanics are ultra tight and focused, the visual and sound design are as good as it gets, and the soundtrack rounds it all out and brings it together.

Surprisingly, this game has a REALLY good story, and not just good "for a Doom game", like ACTUALLY good. The writing in all the codex entries is fascinating and gives you insight into the Doom universe.

The way the powerups and upgrades work keep things interesting and mix up strategy. Each battle is intense and you feel awesome when you wrap up clearing out an area and the music winds down. Glory kills are ALWAYS satisfying. This is one of the only games that once I beat it, I immediately started a new game. It's that fun.

The game runs insanly well. I have a fairly old AMD 8 core CPU and a Radeon HD7950, which ran the game at 60fps with quite a few settings turned down. However, they put out the Vulkan API update and HOLY SMOKES did I see an instant and insane improvement. I'm able to run the game, with nearly ALL settings maxed out, at a solid 60fps. It's incredible.

I still haven't even tried multiplayer.....

I can't say enough great things about this game. It's crazy fun, runs well, and replays are satisfying. My personal game of the year for 2016.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom's campaign is lauded, and rightfully so. It has just the right amount of action and down times. Being trapped in a room as enemies pour out of the portals does make for some amazingly tight gameplay. You'll be jumping and running like a madman trying your best to take out the enemies. There's a lot of fun to be had exhausting the ammo on a room, then chainsawing some demon to refill and doing it over. Pretty much every demon room also has a power-up, which if used appropriately can help clear the room. The arcade mode they just added also allows a lot of replay.

The multiplayer isn't good. I've barely played it and have no interest in going back to it. I'd recommend most other games before this if you want multiplayer.

Snapmap is a cool concept, though it doesn't really replace modding. There's some really cool creations available, and I can see it adding a ton of value (though you won't be able to see any total conversion creations, which is disappionting).\t\t\t\t\t
