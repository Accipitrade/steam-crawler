\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOK, I admit that as expected this game is a total bloody mess. But my favorite Doom is still Doom 3.
This new game might be dark, but Doom 3 was freakin' pitch black. (And it's a shame that the BFG Edition of Doom 3 was ruined due to the new ability to use a weapon and the flashlight simultaniously).
Also, what I do not like in this new Doom ist all the sillyness and broken logic. Being almost dead and running towards nearly dead enemies to hit them with a fist in order to beat more health pickups out of them just doesn't make any sence (And one of the great features of the previous Dooms was a constant lack of health that forced you to search for hidden health pickups. Beating health out of monsters is still better than the infamous regenerating health but just not as good as having to desperetely search for it.) OK, the game is fun, but all that sillyness just ruins the dark atmosphere. Doom is not supposed to be silly. Doom is supposed to be scary. And Doom 3 was the best Doom in that aspect. The newest Wolfenstein was also an excellent game due to it's dark and serious atmosphere. So I wish that the id-developers give up on all the sillyness and let the indie developers be silly instead.
I almost want to give this game a thumbs down because of that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could give a neutral grade, because it's an average game and really pretty, but... why does every ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ modern FPS has to be a Serious Sam rip-off, and what I mean by that is when every enemy encounter turns into 3-step 360° wave spawn arena endurance matches. The few times you can see cacodemons etc. flying in the distance etc. made me wonder why couldn't they just make it *all* like that, pre-placed enemies wondering around, acting demons. Where in original doom games it was like: 90% pre-placed enemies, 10% teleportspawned, in this it's reversed.

The way game has character upgrade system, as in, increase your maximum ammo, health or armor is... ok. I usually like these kinds of gimmicks, but for some reason, it was more annoying than fun, propably because the game hides 1/3 all of the upgrades behind some very obscure secret areas.

I'd say this is a discount game, pick it up for 15€, I paid full price and really was not worth it. I managed to spend 12h by loitering around a lot, but it's not that long if you are just running through, and I can't say I see much re-play value in this. If you want a game where you fight near endless hordes of spawning enemie in endurance fights, play Serious Sam, it's just better.\t\t\t\t\t
