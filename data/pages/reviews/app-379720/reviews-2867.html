\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo make it simple: For me personally, Doom is by far one of the best shooters since Bulletstorm. The game-play is great, fast paced combat that makes you run like a lunatic while shooting at anything that moves and if you then kill an enemy mid air and finish it with a glory kill before hitting the ground, that's gonna make you feel like a total bad-ass. But beware, the later you are in the game the more you have to focus certain enemy types first while choosing the appropriate weapon. Well, not just the weapon itself but the right weapon mod as well which actually does add some kind of tactic to the fights. On top of that there are many collectables to uncover, challenges to master, runes to gain and upgrades to unlock. In simpler terms: there's a HELL(!) of a lot to do here. PS: whilst some may disagree, I loved the glory kill system, it feels very rewarding and does have strategic value.

Okay, it ain't all just sunshine since some arenas are a bit too small or have weird placed smaller objects that will both block you and hinder your overall flow. Though... this is the only real complaint I can think of. The multi-player is kinda like "play a match every once in a while and that's enough". Unfortunately, I experienced a lot of disconnects where the connection to the server was lost meaning, I played several minutes for nothing. It happened pretty much every second match so that's that about the multi-player.

But overall: if you're interested in a great single-player campaign that just rocks and makes you feel like a total bad-ass, get DOOM!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a game that knows exactly what it is - a metal af game where you play as a badass who kills demons with shotguns. Not a complicated premise, really. The thing that makes Doom so special to me is the awareness of what it is. It knows it's a bit over the top and almost silly in a grotesque sort of way. It's ridiculous to the point where the blood and the gore becomes laughable, and this game relishes in that fact. The gameplay is where this shines the most. It's fast pased, frantic action with high-powered weapons to fight high-powered demons. The glory kill machanic helps with this a lot. You spend most of your time shooting demons with your wide range of awesome guns, but once your health gets low, you meelee staggered enemies using satisfying glory kills to get health back. There is no standing behind cover to regain your health. Low on health? Headfirst into battle with you. This keeps the action on its feet at all times, always moving quickly and rushing with adrenaline. I have to assume that when making this game, the designer's priority was "What made the original Doom fun, and how do we recreate that?" Not trying to create the game itself, but to recapture the elements that made Doom so fun and make a game that was fun in very similar ways, yet new and more fitting to the modern FPS (mostly meaning truly 3D). So what made the original Doom fun? Fast-paced shooting, blood and gore, big guns to overcome big challenges. The new Doom employes all of these to the fullest degree. It's refreshing to see a FPS throw realism out the window in favor of fun. Cover? Who needs it! Reload? Screw that, guns fire until they're out of ammo. Stopping to duck and regain health or to reload would kill the momentum that Doom has, and thus have no place in this game.
Now, there is a sense of pacing to it all. It isn't literally nothing but shooting demons (if that's what you want, I recomend Devil Daggers). There's small bits of story, optional notes to read, but most of all secrets to find. You can spend a lot of time exploring portions of the map once you've killed everyone in your way. Some secrets will give you upgrades to make you stronger, but a few are just for the sake of finding them. They're almost all optional though, so you can search for upgrades and secrets as much or as little as you like. This helps the player set their own pacing, deciding if they want to spend time getting the secrets or just gun it to the next area (this also somewhat lets the player pick their own level of difficulty by opting to skip the optional secrets and miss out on upgrades, but their is also an actually difficulty setting for that).
The story is also a good example of this game's self-awareness. There is a story, with a few other characters you meet and talk to, and their story is rather serious. Your character, Doomguy, on the other hand, doesn't seem to take their story too seriously. The game never spends too much time on cutscenes, only enough to give you context about the next makeshift arena to fight your way through. Overall, the story is good, but it knows that you're not here for the story, and is content to play the supporting role while the action takes center stage.
Doom is a game for anyone who wants blood-pumping action without any bullcrap to take away from the experience. This is a wonderfully crafted game that knows exactly hwat you came for and deilivers in spades. Every aspect of the game is crafted around making killing demons fun, without compromise, and it works. It's really freaking fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS is what Duke Nukem Forever should have been. A classic game revamped with modern fetures and updates, while still retaining what made it so damn great. Is it as unforgiving as the originals? No. Does it get your blood pumping and excitied for bloodshed? HELL YES. Some would say Doom is just a rehash of the original with modern tweaks slapped on. They are wrong. The first person vaulting, weapon wheel, and great graphics combined with the Doom formula make for a unique experience. But no feature was as important as the Glory Kills. Without this, the game would never reach the epic adrenaline rushing levels. When an enemy is about to die they light up, and with a tap of a button you rush in and execute them, brutally of course, which then rewards you with ammo and health. Even when your one lucky hell blast away from dying, a lowly hellspawn can be the difference life and death. I'd say the whole game practically revolved around this feture. A flaw I found was that the game felt a bit short. Perhaps it's because the game was such a fun ride I didn't want it to end, but do feel there should have been one or two more levels to run through. I feel like the studio should have dropped multiplayer and focused on adding more to the main game (Co-op would have been badass tho). I do hope a sequel comes soon. The "overpowered guy in a power suit FPS" subgenre is kinda dead (RIP Halo and Crysis). Who better to revive it than the original?

Overall Score: 8.75/10

Would recommend simply because splattering Demons is good, clean fun (suitable for the whole family even!).
The Glory Kill mechanic was pretty cool at first, but the novelty wore off and it became a neato gimick. A very satisfying gimick though, and useful on the higher difficulties.
Gameplay was satisfyingly simple and straightforward, fastpaced, clean.
The background info on the DoomGuy is unneccessary, but woven into the game really well. Just the right amount of plot for this sort of game.

Major downsides/things I think could be better:
* There were not enough demons, especially early on.
* Campaign could be longer (but I would say that about any good, fun game)

I wouldn't pay $80 USD for it, but then I'm a cheapass. It is a good game.
