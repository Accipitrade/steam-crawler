\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite being a short game its a very fun thrilling challenge. There are a lot of guns and offensive/defensive options to choose from but its not quite too much to handle. Good story, seems like a remake of doom 3 with some changes, or maybe both of them are based on one of the books i didn't read. Art, levels and effects are very nice. Its still impossible to find half the secrets, true to doom form. I dunno.. 7.5/10?

Complaints: Main complaint is that some of the objectives don't trigger when you do them forcing you to do the level again to get the perk points. There are a few other shortcomings like somewhat poor damage notifications. Maybe its the armor hit sound effect that is really weak but I couldn't tell most of the game when I was getting hit or dodging properly and it only works because of the gratuitous amounts of health you gain back from kills and how easy it is to dodge things most of the time. The enemies are a bit quiet, not a lot of grunting or sound queues to let you know if someone is behind you. There are no shotgun zombies in this game so there is no need to stay in cover ever like original doom games. You can really just run around and kill whatever you want in any order. I hate how tanky pinkies are in this game. I'd rather there was twice as many of them with half the health. Sometimes they make 90 degree turns up stairs while charging at you! Pretty sure that wasn't intended. Backtracking lockouts in many levels were very annoying and a lot of levels end abruptly. A lot of the weapon mastery unlocks require lots of groups of small enemies to achieve and later levels are quite full of larger enemies and highly fractured paths so it ends up being easier to die over and over to farm an area. There are a lot of guns and the default controls are not the same as scroll wheeling through them, and also the quick wheel weapon order is different. I wish they had kept the traditional double number press, like 2-2 for super shotgun and added 3-3 for chain gun 4-4 for ion canon cause its really hard to reach the 7 and 8 buttons while you're in combat looking at the screen. One time I respawned at a secret and assumed I kept the armor upgrade I picked up before because that's how the game works, but noticed near the end that I no longer had the upgrade. One time my hud disappeared and I couldn't do anything until I get an imp into execution and killed it, at least I was able to recover game function quickly and predictably. Sometimes the movement is really bad, like arbitrary ungrabable ledges or losing all velocity when you do a 180 in mid air or there's this level near the end where its 3 floors and almost every platform/staircase has thin rails surrounding and if you accidentally jump/land on one of the rails you get stuck in it and your movement is limited for a short time, or if you land on certain slanted terrain you lose all momentum and are stuck there for 3 or 4 seconds (aka you're dead) and going down stairs on an angle changes your trajectory like it's optical refraction. That is all I have to complain about, fun game otherwise. Oh, also it was annoying having to open the map constantly but the alternative is a completely linear game with no exploration at all so I guess that's what we want.\t\t\t\t\t
