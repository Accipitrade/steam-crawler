\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was one of the many who was very unimpressed with this game when it was first shown. It looked generic, boring, too much focus on over the top "quick time" melee kills and very derivative looking gameplay. Mighty **** was I wrong though. This game will literally punch a smile on your face as you dance around slaying every demon and/or zombie that looks at you funny in an amusingly gory manner.

My main initial criticisms of the game are ironically the most entertaining things about the it; the flow of combat from ranged to quick time melee and back again has a very natural flow to it. The melee insta-kills are very short, violent, gory and varried enough that they never get old. The chainsaw is increadibly useful and powerful, but has only enough fuel that you will have to choose the proper time to use it. The weapons have a solid feel to them and their alternate firing modes add some extra strategy to the gameplay; every weapon and fire mode has its place on the battlefield, and as you upgrade them their strategic use only increases. Finally, although there are less enemies on screen than the originals, there are more than enough enemies with a good variety to keep you on your toes and force you to prioritize the your targets. There is obviously a lot of influence from the highly popular Brutal Doom mod in this game (I might even say straight up copying of certain features), but it adds enough twists to the forumla that it stands on its own very well.

This is a game with gameplay that demands to be mastered before its true glory will show itself as you leap from demon to demon; shooting, punching and chainsawing your way to the end of a level. It takes a bit to get used to the fast paced combat and even faster weapon switching, but it will eventually become second nature. After a while, you will look forward to the ensuing fight that will occur after you crush the heart of a gore nest, and enjoy the random ambushes your enemies will set for you. The heavy metal soundtrack certainly helps with it's awesome covers of classic doom music that changes with the flow of combat. Finally, the game rewards exploration with secrets in every level for those that want to slow down and enjoy the scenery.

The game runs surprisingly well, considering my ancient system (GTX660 and a 2.8 GHZ Intel i5 that is over 6 years old). I am well below the minimum specs and although I occasionally run into some serious slow downs, the game is still playable and looks fantastic even with the lowest available settings. This game is forcing me to upgrade so I can experience this game at its full potential.

If you are on the fence about this game as I myself was, go ahead and take the plunge. Although I can't speak for the multiplayer portion and have yet to dabble in snapmap, the campaign by itself is more than enough fun for any diehard Doom or classic shooter fan to find hours and hours of enjoyment in.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've got mixed feelings about this game.

Pros:
-Graphics are superb
-Gameplay is intense and full of action
-Levels are well thought-out and huge
-Multiplayer was fun and challenging for a few days after offical release

Cons:
-Loading videos are choppy or freeze and can take up to 10 min to load to main screen
-Game will freeze at certain points
-Black screen crash requires reinstall of video drivers to reset the video res in the game, otherwise it wont play at all
-Certain lockups are during level loading and saving. I've had it lockup my machine and a hard reset corrupts the saved game file. Basically, you have to delete the corrupt file and start the game over.
-Sometimes a crash will set the game into DEV mode.
-Dev mode basically locks the game into single player and doesn't allow you to save any progress past what you've achieved, play multiplayer or use Snapmode.

Conclusion;
For some reason, the game was fine when it was released.
It never crashed, no stutters while loading the game.
Multiplayer was fun.
In fact, it ran fine for 20 hours of gameplay.
I'm not sure what happened, the first time it crashed, it was loading into another level when the whole machine locked up.
I did a hard reset, and that corrupted my saved game. I'm not sure if the game had updated over night, or me installing new video drivers. But after that, the game turned to crap. It would crash to desktop randomly, lock up like it had done before and corrupt the saved game files. I've started the game for a third time, and honestly, I'm so pissed I don't even feel like playing it anymore.

I want to play this game anymore. Starting over is very annoying, and never sure when it's going to crash is frustrating too.
I'm going to leave this game alone for several months. Maybe they'll fix it.

I never experienced this personally, but I heard there are a lot of hackers in multiplayer now.

If this game didn't crash like it does, corrupt the saved game and have hackers online. It would be by far the best game of 2016. But until then, it's one of the worst.

I don't recommend buying this game at this time. I'd give it a few months. Or wait till christmas for the steam sales.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game runs on an engine that is a joy to experience. Optimized for speed, an expression of love for the pc gamer with keyboard in one hand and mouse in the other. With much attention to detail on how the world looks when you move your mouse, or strafe, or pull that trigger. I hadn't experienced such a glass smooth response engine since the days of quake. We've become accustomed to inferior games with their instant hit mechanics, de-emphasized movement of projectiles in space, dumbed down 'trust me you hit something' hit markers. Doom brings back something we've been missing and raises a bloody middle finger to all recent first person shooters that came before it. Aim matters, weapon switching matters, distance matters, and you the player have become an artist. At your disposal is an arsenal of destructive weapons. It's up to you to paint that canvas with blood. Doom is a jolting realization of what we've been missing out on. Other games have taken shortcuts and denied us a pure visceral experience that our computers, our internet connections, and our skills were capable of all along. To hell with hide and cover mechanics, it's time again to kick some ass. I highly recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the third time I've rewritten this review because the more I play it the more I learn about the game and the more my opinion changes. This is going to be a long one so bear with me.

When I was a 6-year-old kid I always wanted to play my older brother's video games. However, he'd say “No” every time. One day I saw my mother playing a game on her computer. Again I jumped at the opportunity and asked “Can I play?” This time the answer was yes, and my mother sat me in her chair and showed me the game. That game was the original Doom from 1993. I'm still surprised that my mother plays Doom; she even knows the cheat codes. To this very day, I'm still play Doom I and II. I've put over 3000 hours playing the original games, fan-made levels, and I even started making my own levels with Doom Builder 2. Doom has been very influential on my life and my work as an artist/writer. If it wasn't for doom I wouldn't be the man I am today. When I heard about Doom 3 I was excited, of course, but the game was a little disappointing. Don't get me wrong, I still like Doom 3, was pretty good for what it was, but... it wasn't Doom.

That said, when I first saw the teaser trailer for Doom 4 I still got excited, but this time I was more skeptical about the game. Every new piece of footage, from trailers to gameplay, old questions were answered and new ones came up. In the end I did end up pre-ordering the game. At first my PC couldn't run it very well, but after awhile of tinkering with the settings, I found a good balance between the graphics quality and resolution size to where I get a healthy 30-50fps without the game looking flat out terrible. Soon after, I discovered there was a driver update for NVIDIA; now I can run at high settings without compromising the frame rate.

Pros:
  • Combat feels a lot faster than the original games
  • Most levels are loaded with secrets
  • Boss fights are challenging, but not to where you feel under powered (Bosses have multiple attacks and there are different ways to avoid them)
  • Monsters from the original game are beautifully redesigned
  • You can use the scroll wheel or number keys to switch weapons if you don't like the weapon wheel
  • Enemy behavior is more diverse (Revenants can fly, Cyber-Mancubi can make acid pools, etc.)
  • Game includes new weapons, enemies and power-ups, but very few
  • You don't loose upgrades or collectibles if you are killed
  • SnapMap, although limited, has great potential in terms of “programming”

Cons:
  • Level design gets a little stale in the last quarter of the game (Arena rooms start to look similar to each other)
  • Snap map is pretty restricted when it comes to level design
  • Game crashes without warning occasionally
  • Dev-Mode bug (game thinks you enabled cheats) forces you to delete all your save files in order to get achievements and play online (this happened to me once on the second level, but not since then)
  • Currently, there is no mod support (SnapMap is okay, but it doesn't scratch that itch that modders have)
  • You leap forward whenever you melee an enemy, but you can still miss (sometimes resulting in getting smacked in the head)

Personal likes:
  • Lost Souls explode on impact, making them less annoying
  • You can replay any mission you've completed and still have all your weapons and upgrades
  • Despite their flaws, I still enjoy the multiplayer and SnapMap
  • The gameplay and combat
  • The weapon mod system/upgrades give me a sense of progression
  • Doomguy's “I don't care” attitude towards the other characters
  • Those Doomguy toys that unlock monster and weapon models
  • The silly little easter eggs that add a bit of humor to this hardcore gore-fest of a game
  • The game is filled with (optional) lore that doesn't distract from the gameplay
  • The Hell Knight is no longer just a palette swap of the Baron of Hell

Personal dislikes:
  • Arch-Viles are now called “Summoners” despite obviously being Arch-Viles
  • The Rune Trials are way to hard (probably because I'm bad at time trials)
  • I fall to my death more times than getting killed by demons during the "Argent Tower" level
  • Guys with shields... they are a pain in the ass sometimes

In conclusion, I love this game. It has been WAY too long since I've played a game like this. I think that the FPS genre is over-saturated with tactical shooters and we need more games in this “run-and-gun” style. This game may have flaws, but the pros definitely out weigh the cons. Since this game is pretty new, I'm sure there will be a patch or two to fix the game's bugs. I'm glad I pre-ordered this, but if you haven't gotten this game yet then you may want to hold off for awhile until the game-breaking bugs are sorted out. However, if you have the same mentality as Doomguy (you don't care and just want to kill demons), then go ahead and buy it.

My rating for DOOM(2016): 8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
