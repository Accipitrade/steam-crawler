\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe following review was written by my beloved girlfriend Spitha. I recommend this game as much as she does. Please make sure to visit her profile.

I played the game 3 times (twice in 2019 and once in 2021) so instead of going through all the details I will mention the elements of the game that stuck out to me the most.

-The synchronisation between combat and music is masterful. This is one of the closest experiences to being in a John Wick movie you will get. The music reacts to your movements and actions during combat making for an end product that is directly tailored for you and your playstyle

-The enemy variety makes for a very fulfilling gameplay that will never get old. It is not unfair and it always allows you enough breathing room to strategise and pick your targets while never becoming too easy and boring (except if you fully upgrade Siege Mode in the Gauss cannon and you have the "rich get richer" rune, at that point Doom 2016 turns into a point and click experience, but don't tell them I told you that)

-The variety of weapons allows for all sorts of playstyles. Of course some more advantageous than others but none truly "useless". All weapons and weapon modes have their place in Doom 2016 if you know how to utilise them

-A lot of games have tried to do the whole "execute the enemy with low health" gimmick and it always failed because it always slowed down the experience. However Doom 2016 somehow was able to pull it off and make it not just fit in with the fast pace of the action but actually drive it in many cases. The glory kills are fast, satisfying, they rewarding the player with health (and armour later on if you so choose) and they are generally a highlight of the game.

-The story is unironically good. Yes. In a Doom game the story is actually very good in the way it functions. (Warning: some light spoilers ahead) It does not overstay its welcome. It is not complicated and the Doomguy's disinterest to it mirrors the one most players will experience. Doom is a game that directly tackles Climate Change in a very elegant and non intrusive manner and places the blame squarely on the ones responsible, those being, the corporations. The Doomguy (or slayer) has little time for UAC's nonsense. He does not care about saving UAC and its energy production. He is just there to contain the problem and prevent it from happening again. In a lesser game the main character would follow Samuel Hayden's instructions like a good little puppy, however this is not a lesser game and the way the subject is treated is beyond masterful in my opinion. My only criticism is that in some very few cases you are forced into heavy dialogue cutscenes which kinda undermines the way the story is meant to function. \t\t\t\t\t
