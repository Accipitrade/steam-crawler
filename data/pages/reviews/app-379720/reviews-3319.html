Then the game released, and I saw literally nothing but praise for it, so I figured "I have time to kill I might as well give it a chance."

This game has no right to be as fun as it is, none at all. There are maybe ten unique enemy types and eight weapons (six have two alt fire modes, one has only one alt fire mode) and somehow I've gone 10 hours without losing interest so far. It baffles me that the game can remain entertaining while literally moving place to place fighting arena style then looking for secrets. I honesly don't know how they've managed to accomplish this, but if you're thinking of buying it there's a significant chance you'll enjoy it (might wanna wait for a sale if you only want to play single player).

Have not tried multiplayer, only barely tried "snapmap".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, now that I have completed my 30-40 hours of pre-review game time, it's time for me to throw my hat into the ring.

Doom is an exceptional game, hands down. During the time I have played, I was in a glorious state of euphora as I slaughtered the denizens of the Nine Circles! I have played many FPS, a lot of them being AAA titles, so I think I know a thing or two about these kinds of games. I also happened to pick up the previous games on sale as a bundle, so I have experince with the other games to compare with.

Here is the Pro-Con list to give you the short, short version:

Pro:
+Stays true to its heritage
+Fast (we are talking REAL fast!) and fluid combat
+Lots and lots of secrets to find
+Graphically beautiful
+Consistently high frame rates with 970 or higher (will have to tone down some settings to match that with 960 and lower)
+Guns are solid and gunplay is quite deep with the various upgrades
+The AI is very intelligent, sometimes frustratingly so (you'll get what I mean once you play)
+Glory kills are GLORIOUS!
+Map design is expansive and makes you think about where to jump to next
+The BFG is back!

Cons
-Multiplayer is...decent? (More to be discussed further down)
-After the campaign is finished, feelings of wanting more arise
-A season pass for multiplayer (again, talking about that below)
-Pauses in the action (minor annoyance more than anything)


So...the campaign was pretty much flawless. It is rather lengthy for an FPS sitting around 10-12 hours per play through, but it's just so damn good. In a very Doom like fashion, id* just drops you right into the thick of it and tells you to start shooting. No beating around the bush, no hoops to jump through, just go in and start killing s**t! The combat is rather smooth and fluid. The mantling and auto-climb is quick enough to keep pace with the tempo of the fights. There is never a pause or stop to the motion. If you do stop moving, it's only for a split second otherwise, you are already dead. The graphics of this game are well done, with each of the demons having a distinct and profound look to them. The same can be said about each of the weapons. the models show the amount of detail that went into the making of these and it's nothing short of impressive. Speaking of guns, they are pretty bad-ass. They can kick ass by themselves but once you find a flying drone, the upgrades you can get turn it up to 11! They range from lobbing explosives to homing missiles to three mini-guns in one! These upgrades allow you to more easily move forward with their added destructive abilities but you can also upgrade yourself as well. Throughout each level, you can find tokens to unlock various upgrades for your "Preator Suit". These are largely passive but can be helpful with certain aspects of the game. One example is drastically reducing the chances of blowing yourself because you thought firing a missile point-blank at an Imp while at 20 health was a good idea...Oops. The others affect equipment, your map/secrets, and power-ups but you can learn more about those for yourself since I don't want to spoil too much. \\

Going back to the combat, the "Glory kill" system is very flushed out and ties itself in as an important aspect of the game without hindering the flow of the battles. Health cannot regenerate, period. That being said, you can use "Glory kills" as a means to get health back by executing low health demons and they happen quickly so as to not stop your rampage. As an added bonus, they are over-the-top gruesome and somehow very satisfying as you break a demons leg to smash it's head with it's own foot! (Talk about putting your foot in your mouth, amiright?) The map design is very elaborate and a far cry from the days of old but that is to be expected since times have changed and so has level design. The maps are very vertical, but not Dark Souls vertical. You will use your double jump and mantling quite a bit to get around. It does allow for greater depth to fighting the hordes since you can get even higher and use that height to rain hell on the enemies below.
Secrets! What would a Doom game be without secrets? Well...crap. All jokes aside, there are a plethora of secrets to be found in each level and with their own rewards to boot. Most times, you usually find Preator tokens for your suit or other stuff that will help you in the level you are currently in. Other times, you can find little collectible Doomguy figures. These little guys unlock the various models for demons, weapons, and characters that are found in the game. There is one other type of secret but that is a reward all its own so I'll keep quiet on that one.

The enemies are horrifyingly great! Scary looking, nasty noises, and intelligent enough to know how to surround/kill you quickly is a welcome feature. The demons come in various sizes and forms, from the shifty Revenant to the fearsome Cyberdemon. Each demon type is unique in both their appearance and fighting style, with only a tiny amount of overlap. For exapmle, the Imp is a demon that is best known for, both past and present, throwing fireballs at you from all directions. But, they simply won't throw fireballs. They can rush you, retreat to a safer distance, climb pillars and walls to throw s**t at you! They act to always keep you on your toes and will never let you find cover or rest (if you try to hide behind cover for any longer than a few seconds...what are you doing?) The same goes for the other demons, as they have their unique styles and when all thrown together into one area, it's going to be chaotic! But, that is the beauty of Doom! Pure, chaotic, uncensored, gory, ultra-violent fun!

Now, for the one true disappointment...multiplayer. I'm sure you all heard about or even played the beta. It was meh at best. Guess what? Not much changed, outside of better framerates and severs. From what I played, which was very little because I simply could not keep playing it for long periods, the multiplayer mode has some sort of identity crisis. One the one hand, it has it's Unreal Tournament/Quake arena qualities. On the other, it adds load outs, hack mods (think of them as the death boosters from MW2), and the weapon balance is out of whack. The vast majority of the time, you see people running around with the Minigun, shotgun, or Rocket launcher. Everything else can be used, but you will simply pale in comparison. These two aspects clash and it takes away from the idea that is is supposed to be an arena shooter. The matchmaking is bad. Often, you are pitted against people who had dumped many more hours into the multiplayer than you have and other noobs dot both teams. One could say that this goes to show the amount of skill one player has over the other but it becomes frustrating when you die to the same two or three people over and over again. It's not fun. The unlock system hands out randomized rewards and the better weapons are locked behind level restrictions. Also, demon runes allow for players to become a demon within the game. You can choose from a the Revenant, Mancubus, Baron, or Prowler. Playing as the demon is fun but being the guys on the other end sucks. The demons are too powerful and one-shot players almost 100% of the time. Frankly, the multi-player feels half-assed and indecisive.

Snapmap is probably the second coolest feature, since you can create your own levels with the level editor given to you. It hasn't been fully realized yet, but this will eventually give us levels that we created that are parallel to the actual campaign. It has a lot of potential and is fun to tinker with!

I do recommend this game! Its fun and the time spent playing the campaign is worth it! It has all of the features of Doom with modern twists. Snapmap, while still being figured out, is very interesting and fun. Multiplayer is...okay on its best day. Definitely do NOT buy it for only the multiplayer but if you enjoy it, power to you!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOTE: This is only a review of the single player campaign.

Doom 2016 manages to blend retro style run-and-gun shooting with a (admittedly fairly limited) degree of modern customization to match your particular playstyle. there is NO cover other than what you manage to put between you and the enemies, but why would you do that when you can run around at a speed aproaching the land speed record and blow them away with a wide variety of well rounded and cathartic weapons. Its not perfect, there are of course flaws as with any game. The sheilded guards should not be that annoying, a damage limit to their frontal sheilds would make sense (they should not be able to tank more rockets than a demon the size of a small house). Additionally there are some balance problems with the armor "mods" where some are massively useful while others just let you pick up stuff from slightly further away.

GAMEPLAY
This game has a horror tag... it lies. There is nothing really scary about this game. Violence? Dear god yes. Gore? I dont think any other game has more if im honest. But at no point was I dredding the next encounter, even against 50 foot high monstrosities the gameplay is more exhilerating. Its not perfect though, the weapons have some balance problems, along with the runes as stated above. It is also very linear (outside of one or two levels) which is not a bad thing in and of itself, but is worth mentioning for those who love to roam around a massive open world.

PLOT
You are a super powered space marine fighting the armies of hell, what else do you want?
Seriously though there is a mildy engaging plot that uncovers what happened and why you wake up on a slab with zombies trying to eat you, but its honestly a very cliched "meddling with powers you dont understand" story. The ending was unsatisfying with a predictable twist that feels like a DLC/sequel set up as opposed to any closure.

LENGTH/CONTENT
It is decently long, about 13 missions of varying length and increasing difficulty. There are only ever two sceneries: space complex (inside and out), and demonic realm ancient architecture; and of course its all covered in excessive blood, bodies, and satanic symbols. The levels have interesting challenges which add a nice twist to each: kill X enemies by blowing them up with barrels, find Y secrets, kill Z enemies at once, etc etc. Though some of the Glory Kill ones get annoying when you have to crouch down and attack them from the lower right leg X number of times for example. In a similar vein to the challenges there are a number of secrets on each level to find ranging in difficulty from "marked on the map" to "in a hole in the wall 50 feet down an otherwise fatal drop".\t\t\t\t\t
