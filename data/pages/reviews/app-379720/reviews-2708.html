\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, firstly, this game is a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ work of art.

Smooth, well-made gameplay, combined with the ability to rip enemies apart with your bare hands, all while listening to heavy metal. This is unironically the most immersive angry man experience I have ever experienced. Those Gory *Glory kills were the best idea conceived in recent game development.

This game also has one of the best examples of proper difficulty I have ever seen. Instead of opting to make damage sponges like almost every other game does, the devs of this game simply made enemy damage scale and nothing more. This, is, VERRY, well done. Instead of waiting for a damage sponge to drop, the player instead has to carefully maneuver, plan, and avoid incoming projectiles. Requiring actual “skill” in the planning of engagement, reflexes and general flexibility. This is an ingenious, and yet greatly underused way of scaling difficulty.

Now, praising aside, I do have one issue with this game. That being its collectibles. Collectibles, work in slower-paced, crawler games like The Evil Within for example, in Doom, I find them to be a major flaw. This is a HEAVY action game, and having senseless collectibles that are also the way you get upgrade points gives this game an unpleasant change in pace. You will go from a heavy metal gorefest, to looking for action figures after almost every fight. This 100 to 0 change is quite unpleasant considering how good the games combat is. Now yes, the first Doom game had a tone of hidden junk, but that game was more linear and compact in its design, rather than the large, more open areas of this game. Now, I can’t complain too much since there are people who like these easter eggs, though I will stick to my opinion that cutting the action down to nothing for several long minutes of searching at a time is not the best.

Overall, it would be a crime not to recommend this game. It really is one of the best games out there. If you like goory shooters that is.\t\t\t\t\t

Btw, here is the recipe for a apple pie:

Ingredients:

3 pounds mixed apples (such as Granny Smith, Gala and McIntosh)
2/3 cup granulated sugar
2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
6 tablespoons unsalted butter
1 tablespoon all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
3/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
1/8 teaspoon salt
2 disks dough for Basic Crust
1 large egg, beaten
Coarse sugar, for sprinkling

Procedure:

Peel and core the apples; slice 1/4 inch thick. Toss with the granulated sugar and lemon juice. Heat 4 tablespoons butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the apples and cook, stirring, until tender, about 10 minutes. Stir in the flour, cinnamon and salt; cook until thickened, 1 more minute. Let cool.

Roll out 1 disk of dough into a 12-inch round on a floured surface; ease into a 9-inch pie plate. Add the apples, mounding them slightly in the center, and dot with the remaining 2 tablespoons butter. Roll out the remaining dough into a 12-inch round. Lay it over the filling; press the edges of the crusts together, then fold the overhang under itself and crimp to seal. Brush with the beaten egg and sprinkle with coarse sugar. Cut a few slits in the top crust. Chill 1 hour.

Place a baking sheet on the lowest oven rack and preheat to 425 degrees F for 30 minutes. Place the pie on the hot baking sheet; lower the temperature to 375 degrees F. Bake until golden, 1 hour to 1 hour, 10 minutes. (Cover the edges with foil if they brown too quickly.) Cool on a rack.
