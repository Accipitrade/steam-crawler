\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my in-depth review of DOOM:

TL;DR:
Yes, if you are a fan of FPS games at all, I'd highly recommend DOOM. It is one of the best of its kind in the last decade will definitely be enjoyable from start to finish.

Gameplay: 9.5/10
DOOM is quite possibly the most satisfying game I've ever played. Every single glory kill feels like a rush of serotonin which makes the whole game a blast to play. Each level is around 1-2 hours long and involves making your way from A to B whilst constantly fighting off hordes of man eating demons with a wide variety of weapons, each with their own unique upgrades and abilities. With 13 total levels, a full playthrough of this game should take a fair bit of time depending on how much you play per day. I usually like to play 1 level per day in a playthrough since 1 hour of DOOM a day is more than enough for me. My only gripes about the gameplay are the keybinds and collectibles. Yes, they are a great addition to the game but there a certain few that are located in really stupid locations that would be really hard to find normally without the use of a guide which can make some levels tedious if you plan to 100% the game. Switching weapons with keybinds rather than the weapon wheel is the more optimal way to play the game but I highly recommend changing the weapon keybinds as it utilizes the 1-9 keys which are uncomfortable to constantly reach over to when quickly trying to swap weapon in a fast paced game . The game is difficult enough to keep you on your toes and focused at all time but isn't hair tearing and infuriating (unless you play violence or ultra violence).

Graphics: 8/10
I think the overall design choice for this game is far superior to DOOM Eternal in terms of how the enemies and environment are supposed to make you feel, that being they make you uneasy and want to kill them. DOOM's enemies are truly hellish looking and look like something straight out of a horror movie. The soulless look they have in their eyes (well, the ones that have eyes) gives you more of an incentive to kill them. The environment feels really realistic as the lighting is quite dim and gloomy which adds to the immersion of the game and feels as though the levels could be real places. The models aren't really faithful to the original DOOM but I prefer the design choice they made in this game compared to the more faithful-to-the-original DOOM Eternal. My gripes with the graphics would be that some of the guns don't look that great (looking at you, plasma rifle) which for me at least, gave me less of an incentive to use them.

Social: 4/10
Eh, its ok. At the end of the day I'm sure 95% the developers' focus was to make the best singleplayer experience they possibly could which (in my opinion) they succeeded in doing, however the multiplayer option in the game is quite underwhelming. I haven't played much of it since it bored me quite quickly but the multiplayer isn't anything groundbreaking or revolutionary and is definitely not the main reason people buy this game.
The multiplayer modes are: Team Deathmatch, Soul harvest (basically a variation of deathmatch), Clan Arena, Freeze Tag, Domination and King of the Hill.

Narrative/ Story: 6/10
Whilst DOOM does have an ongoing story throughout the campaign, it isn't the best thing in the world. You wake up chained to a coffin you previously slumbered in. Olivia Pierce has opened a portal to hell from the UFC base located on Mars, and it is up to the DoomSlayer to stop the invasion. That is basically the story in a nutshell. There are some interesting characters such as Samuel Hayden, who was never really able to be identified as ally nor enemy, and he helps you progress through the game (only for his personal benefit however). The ending feels a bit anticlimatic and doesn't really have any closure, plus does not fit with how DOOM Eternal begins and leaves a lot of questions in the air.

Audio: 10/10
DOOM's soundtrack is absolutely incredible. Mick Gordon went absolutely above and beyond in making one of the most perfect fitting and sounding OSTs of all time. The entire soundtrack is a composition of heavy/death metal tracks that incredibly immerse you and really makes you feel like the DoomSlayer, a badass guy ripping and tearing his way through enemies. I like to listen to my own playlists when I play games but there is no way in hell (pun intended) I would ever skip these bangers. Playing DOOM without the music blaring in the background doesn't feel like DOOM.

Value: 10/10
£15 is all I need to say really. It is nigh impossible to find an FPS game with this much level of enjoyment for such a low price tag and I believe it is absolutely worth the money. Not to mention this game goes on sale every time there is a major sale and usually goes down to £5 which is an absolutely insane price tag for such a good game.\t\t\t\t\t
