\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed DOOM. I think most shooter fans would absolutely enjoy DOOM. I played the originals way back in my childhood, I played that one crappy one, and I saw the awful movie with The Rock. I never really thought of myself as a huge DOOM fan. This game brought me to be a huge DOOM fan. The campaign is campy and fun as all hell (no pun intended). It doesn't take itself too seriously. It's fast paced, weighty, and meaty as prime rib. The multiplayer is a whole different kind of beast. It almost feels like a seperate game. Fun to play alone, even more fun with friends. There is some imbalance with weapons in multiplayer, but hey... since when is perfect balance actually fun right? The DLC is nice if you want to buy it, but you really don't have to. I never did. If you enjoy fast pace, meaty, and fairly simple shooters, then this game is for you and you should buy it.... and make your friends buy it too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is overall good except for a few gripes i had:

1) Many enemies are bullet sponges. While not over the top, it gets kind of aggrevating, esspecially when 20 of them are thrown at you. 90% of enemies mechanics are variations of throwing fireballs at you or charging you.

2) The story's ending was bad. I'm not going to spoil anything but its a blatent lead up to something final that just.... ends.

3) Most of the guns are fun to use, but you barely get to use them because you are either saving their precious ammo for later, or you dont have ammo when you finally use them. Instead of making you cycle through all the guns (which i assukme is their intention) you basically stick to the shotgun for the random enemies and use the beefier guns for the bullet sponges.

4) Maybe it is because i havent played any other doom game, and the only other arena shooter I've played is halo, but I found that the repeated arenas you encounter in the campaign left much to be desired. It felt like the story was basically pushing you through multiplayer maps, and just sprinkling enemies on them telling you to clear it, then move to the next one. This is alright as the base gameplay is still fun, its just somewhat lacking, but again what did i expect from doom?

*Note*: I didnt play multiplayer, maybe I'm missing out on content that will make it worth more in my eyes but after beating the story I've had enough of this game.

Overall: It is a good game, but not $80 worth (not even $30 for just the campaign). The game looks gorgous, and the mechanics are fun at first, the game nearly outstays it's welcome with them, only not doing so because of its short campaign with an abrudt ending.

Overall i got it on sale for $27 and I still think that was a little much. Go pickup titanfall 2, you'll probably like that more if you are looking for something besides a arena shooter.\t\t\t\t\t
