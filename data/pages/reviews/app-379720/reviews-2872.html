\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile a little mindless and would have been a better game (and 100% better story) if it was not doom. :) (i.e., replace dumb demons with aliens or something.)
The shooting feels good and the game is fun. Pick it up on sale or something. It is a very short campaign too, steam says under 15 hours for my game time, but it think it was longer than that.
The engine is not very demanding which is nice for people with older cards. The music (like all heavy metal) is painful. There are a bunch of weapon and I found myself switching between all of them and their different alt modes.
Most of the fighting takes place in rooms/areas that are clearly "arenas" for fighting. You walk around and enemies magically teleport in – and you happily murder them. The few boss fights are a bland. One just strafe around shooting, no real ticks. Just dodge some attacks and shoot.
This is a fun, but forgettable game. It seems people are like it/forgiving it just because of the doom name.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was prepared for Doom to be good, but not THIS good. Doom outclasses recent entries in the FPS market by a landslide. The combat comes in waves of hectic, gory dismemberment heightened by impressive sound design and flesh-melting heavy metal. It's thrilling, visceral, and offers the right amount of challenge for any player. The enemies of Doom are familiar to the series, but a welcome sight in a mess of war games against human assailants. It plays like a classic FPS game, but without the clunky controls and antiquated level design that bogged down the era. Fights spiral into frantic run-and-gun scenarios with little time for aiming and no pause to reload. Doom doesn't try to be realistic; it tries to be fun. And in that respect, it succeeds beyond a shadow of doubt. Most importantly, Doom incorporates elements into its gameplay that marked the "Golden Age" of the first-person shooter and are sorely missed in modern titles. 1.) Doom has level design - ACTUAL level design. All the action is concentrated into well-crafted arenas with plenty of secret doors to uncover and platforms to traverse. It doesn't feel like playing on rails, but it lacks the empty spaces and pointless sections of an open-world game. 2.) Doom offers impressive replay value. From searching for overlooked secrets, to unlocking hidden levels, to completing challenges, players will find plenty of reason to revisit the game. And did I mention multiplayer and arcade mode? 3.) Pickups, power-ups, and ammo drops encourage players to actually explore the map and manage their resources in combat. Nothing in Doom is taken for granted. 4.) Doom features an array of enemies and weapons, all with unique abilities. This includes boss fights that are actual brawls and not just glorified cutscenes. The enemies are massive, difficult, and exciting.

Unfortunately, some of Doom's best qualities end up working against it. The battles may be frantic, but they become exhausting after a point. A lack of variety in gameplay means Doom Guy will do nothing but shoot. And when the game runs out of ideas, it tosses in more enemies, until battlefields are a slurry of Hell Barons and Mancubuses. Some of the challenges border on frustrating, and it's easy to wander through levels for long periods of time, looking for a hidden spot on the map. A few tiring moments aside, Doom maintains its energy throughout an impressively long campaign. A built-in level designer means the modding community will keep this game alive for years to come. Very few titles in recent memory are put together so well. Doom is excellent, plain and simple. 9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy review will be just like most others. DOOM was one of the first games I played on a computer. It had me hooked with its graphics and 3D game play way back when on a 486. I played DOOM, DOOM 2, Quake, Quake 2.

The original DOOM and Quake never got old to me.

This is DOOM in 2017. Hit tab for the map, find power-ups, ammo, health and secrets. Kill the demons from hell.

The atmosphere keeps you going with an adrenaline rush. The landscapes and details have depth. You come across enemies that are fighting amongst themselves, just wait for them to kill each other. You can start a level and hang back and pick off the enemies, rather than just rushing into battle. Red key, blue key, yellow key. Shotgun, double barrel shotgun. Chainsaw.

You will die, a lot, and watch as the demons tear you apart before you restart from your last checkpoint. It will be frustrating because they keep coming and coming. Change your strategies and explore the level before you unleash hell on yourself.

I don't know how far I am into the campaign, but I have been to hell and back. I have not touched multiplayer yet, but I have already replayed campaign missions to look for more secrets. When you replay missions, secrets count towards your overall progress - a nice touch.

They really did well with this one: If you like DOOM and FPS in general, there is a good chance you will like this game. This has become my goto game. I can see myself learning all these maps in great detail, just like the original, finding all the secrets, perfecting how to kill all the demon spawn in the most effective and fun way.\t\t\t\t\t
