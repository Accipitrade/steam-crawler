\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a Doom player and fan since Doom 1 on PC and SNES i feel bad for buying this at release.

Why? I will explain because i am 30+, have played hundreds of games now in my lifetime and for my taste new AAA game releases are getting worse and worse every year.

Good looking? Mostly yes! All the other stuff is mostly mediocre or worse.
Gameplay is way to casual these days, not challenging at all, repetitive and shallow.

So let's start this and i know a lot will disagree because they enjoyed the game BUT i don't care - i try to be as honest as i can.


The good:
Graphics are good, performance and optimization is nice and rare these days.
Sound design and soundtrack is nice too - no question.
The maps design is ok, but i wish there was some more "material" or "reference" to the first two Doom games.
Enemies are nice designed but it gets boring after the third time killinge the same enemy - AI is not that great.

The bad:
Only 10 hours of gamesplay in singleplayer and i wasn't even fast.
It's boring and - to be honest - i had to force myself to play it till the end.
It was fun for like 1-3 hours, following 3 hours were ok but i thought every hour "hell, i hope there is something new coming soon - this game is so repetitive and i am bored!". But there wasn't. So i forced myself to finish it the last 3-4 hours. And there is no way i would replay this again.
It was so boring and i don't know. Lack of everything Doom has been for me up to now.
The "new" mechanics killed it totally for me - simply some "OH WOW!"-stuff to get some casual kids buying and playing it, nothing else.


Multiplayer is nothing to talk about - it's not worth any minute.
Maps are boring, map editor is again a thing i would like to forget as fast as possible.


So - now let's come to my decission why i can not recommend this game for 30€ or even 20€. At 10€ maybe worth a look.

I do compare price and playtime for myself.

If a game totally blows your mind in 10 hours for 30-40 hours - i'm ok with that. ( Hellblade: Senuas Sacrifice )

But such shallow gameplay with lack of everything Doom has been before ( because it was something new back then ) is not worth 30 or 20 bucks.

Grab it at 10 $/€ - that's ok.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first played this game I was not very impressed. Spoiled is more like it. It has very little to do with the original Doom, but thats probably a good thing.

This game is like pie. You have to embrace pie for it to work.

For the money, especially on sale, there actually is quite a decent amount of content. Don't play it merely for the story line, although its not too bad and Lance Henriksen is a badass. Let the game guide you. The challenges aren't an annoyance, instead they teach you how to play the game as the developers intended. Significant effort was expended for the secrets, so it could be argued they are actually a part of the game and not optional.

The MP is probably DOA, but thats not their fault. It a good addition, if you can find people to play with. Same with the snap map toolkit. Worth your time if you decide to make the effort.
My advice would be to play through on lower difficulty for the experience and then replay on higher.

Runs smooth on decent hardware. I encountered zero bugs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's going to be awkward reviewing this game while avoiding what Steam considers "inappropriate language" so bear with me.

There are a lot of things about this DOOM that are very different from classic DOOM. There are more things, however, in my opinion, that resonate with old school DOOM than there are things that don't fit with it. It especially feels like a return to "real" DOOM compared to Doom 3. The biggest difference though, between this and classic DOOM, is that this time around, the Doom Marine is definitely more of an agressor. You kick the unholy crap out of the demonic horde in this DOOM. Sometimes the Imps even flee from you.

The violence in this game can be best described, I think, as brutal to the point of being horribly comical. When using the new "glory kill" feature, where you hit the melee button(yeah, it has one now) when close enough to a staggered enemy, you proceed to beat them to death in horriffic fashion. Its even badder when you are under the effects of berserk. Maybe it's just me having a twisted sense of humor, but during this game I often was reminded of Bart and Lisa Simpson laughing at "Itchy and Scratchy" by my own laughter while brutalizing the demons. Seriously, you really rip 'em a new one in this game, like few others. Try using the chainsaw on the mancubus.

Unlike old DOOM, there's not a constant background music for each level. Instead, heavy metal begins to play when you get into a big fight. There's also some atmoshperic music in hell.

The weapons in this DOOM are pretty good. Like Doom 3, there's an assault rifle class weapon before you get the bigger chaingun, and there's a new, additional energy weapon that offers power and accuracy instead of the plasma rifle's better rate of fire. I found myself using all the weapons at different points, but felt like the maximum ammo capacity could have been higher. There are also grenades, and, conveniently enough, a grenade button.

The bottom line is, I thought this game was cool, and it felt right for a DOOM game. I recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
