\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 is definitely worth playing, especially with its current prices and with how low it goes when on sale. And if you don't need the OST offline, just find it on YouTube when you want to listen.

-----

Firstly, a summary of what I thought about the game.

The good about DOOM 2016: combat, the general pace, the writing and story, mood and atmosphere, sound design, level design, the Arcade mode, and so on. Nearly everything, really. And let's not forget about the music of course.

The bad about DOOM 2016: mostly the game installation size, some bugs relating to replayability, the sequel.

Points I'm neutral about.... The achievements, multiplayer and snap-map, but mostly because I haven't played them enough. When I last tried multiplayer, I had some very bad FPS drops on some of the maps, which made them impossible to play, so it's something I might revisit some day if it still has people then.

Also, I was amazed that the game ran decently enough when I was playing it with a GTX 760. I have since got a better card which makes the game quite enjoyable. You may want to install the game on an SSD to reduce load times and such, too.

-----

Combat in the game feels a bit like a dance and is probably one of few games that I can think of that does choreography-like gameplay in a great way. I'll admit, when I first started playing the game after buying it, it felt tough, even on a lower difficulty. The game guides you just enough and doesn't shove your face full of forced tutorials like the sequel, and it forces you to learn to understand the fights and memorise spawn patterns quite clearly. The level design is great because it's exceedingly rare that you'll get stuck on any objects or enemies.

Once you are familiar with the general feel of the choreography, things start to get much easier, even on nightmare difficulty. The exception being that the campaign ultra-nightmare is completely unforgiving of mistakes, of course, since it puts you at square one on death, presumably why so few people have even completed the first level on ultra-nightmare.

Through the campaign, weapons have an unlockable progression, with most of the weapons having two separate alt-fire modes, save for the BFG and the super shotgun. There are two other progression systems, namely praetor suit upgrades and runes. All of these have some element of choice with regards to what you want to upgrade/unlock.

I feel that DOOM 2016 actually does secrets properly; it doesn't do what its sequel does. There's no highlighting or pinging when near secret areas, you have to find them for yourself based on how you observe each level. So if you like to hunt collectibles with a bit of thinking involved, this part should be enjoyable.

-----

About replayability, the campaign doesn't really have anything that's supposed to make you play it again, but I enjoy going through it because of the story and just because most of the fights feel fun.

That's the same reason I like playing the Arcade mode too, except that also has the extra element of trying to beat your own score, while mostly skipping cutscenes that interrupt level flow, which is great if you're only in the mood for combat, since you can also pick whatever level on Arcade. Also, maybe there's no point trying to beat the very best scores (top 10 to 50 or so usually) because they have likely used either exploits or cheating to be achieved.

Achievements also give some further replayability value if you're a completionist but be aware that some require multiplayer and the game probably doesn't have a huge multiplayer community at this point, I wouldn't know though.

-----

I said there was a bug with regards to replayability. DOOM 2016 has a bug where, I think if you're playing Arcade in ultra-nightmare and die and have loaded a save previously in the same session, that save file may bug and become prematurely unusable, either for continuing or selecting previous missions.

This didn't really bother me when I first played the game because I only played arcade after finishing the campaign, so I was essentially never affected by this bug until then.

So for one thing, if you'd like to play Arcade at top difficulty, be wary of this bug. Otherwise, just make sure you have completed what you want to in the campaign (i.e. achievements) and then blast away at Arcade.

The main effect of this for me, is that if you forget that this can happen, and you like going back to campaign levels with/for the photo mode, you might find that you have to start over a campaign save just to be able to get some shots of something.
Not like it's a huge problem since the campaign is enjoyable to play, but it's annoying on some level if you just wanted a shot of something specific, especially from one of the final levels.

I can understand why photo mode isn't present in the Arcade, but it would be nice if it was optionally usable there, even if it stopped you from being able to score or something.

-----

If you want to know about some specific part of gameplay I haven't mentioned in detail, maybe ask I guess.

-----

About the sequel, DOOM Eternal; it's not a horrible game, but if you've played 2016 first and enjoyed the story and the rest of the atmosphere, Eternal may leave you with a bad taste overall, despite its fun aspects.\t\t\t\t\t
