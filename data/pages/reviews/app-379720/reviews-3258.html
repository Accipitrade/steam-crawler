\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 2016 lacks a compelling story. The characters are pretty bland. The main protagonist doesn't have any defining features except a green powersuit, a lot of muscle, a gun, and a load of aggression.

Doom is easily the best FPS I've played in years. This game deserves a place in the pantheon of the greats. Quake, Half-Life, the first 2 Dooms, the first 3 Halos... This game deserves all the praise it gets.

For anyone who's a fan of the genre, this is almost required play. You don't get lame cutscenes and stupid stories. You get a gun and you blow off the enemies head, or rip their ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ head off. If you're a fan of the old Halo games and find the last 2 (or whatever, 3. Who cares?) to be awful in terms of playstyle, I'd highly recommend giving the game a shot. It has all the glory of Doom, the familiarity an old school Halo fan would recognize, all with the added bonus of not being exclusive to consoles.

Rip and tear, cannot recommend enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing the mediocre multiplayer beta, and seeing Bethesda fail and fail with pre-release PR, I had very very low expectations for this game. Thankfully after finishing the first level of the Singleplayer campaign my low expectations were shattered.

Doom 2016 is probably the best single player shooter I've ever played, beating out Wolfenstein TNO, Shadow warrior(2013), FEAR(the original and the only good one) and even Half life 2. The action, the smoothness, the enemy and weapon variety, the lack of any enemy using hitscan weapons, the verticality of the levels and arena's all make for a top notch shooter.

Graphics are phenominal and so is performance. On a single 980ti I played through the game at 2560x1440 with everything including AA and reflections maxed and never had the FPS drop below a vsynced 60. Also speaking of Vsync, there is no stuttering or noticable input lag when using the game's in game vysnc so feel free to turn it on if you have bothersome screen tearing.

Thanks to the game's snapmap feature for easy level/map creation, the nightmare/ultranightmare difficuties and the likeliness of an expantion I imagine DOOM will be a game I won't stop playing for years.

For the best experience the first time around, I recommend setting the difficulty to "Ultra-Violence" which is not too difficult, but does punish you for your mistakes and for standing still. Also turn off the compass and objective markers/hints. This forces you to simply explore and use your head which will lead you to finding more ammo/armor/health, more ammo upgrades and of course SECRETS!! Yes this game, like the Doom of yore is chalk full of secrets to find!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo i got to say, they actually made a doom game that feels like the old school doom we all love. The singleplayer is SUPER strong. Fun, fast, brutal, loads of guns and a decent story telling with big rewards if you explore. You're a badass, go kill some monsters. The storyline wont tell you everything, but you'll collect info while playing and decide for yourself if you want to read the story in detail. I like that, give the player a choice to read it all or leave it be. The game is also remarkable well optimized. I was amazed at how good the game looked with 8gb ram, a bad graphic card (GeForce GTX 560Ti) and no SSD hdd. It still runs smooth on 30-60fps after the loading, witch is nothing more than amazing.

Over to multiplayer. It's not that great imo. It's fast, but messy and it just don't give me the same awesome feeling of being a badass. It looks a lot like a version of unreal tournament tbh, with the twist. And some of the modes was not that fun. The melee instant kill range also seems to be really long. It's possible to do a finish move from far away.

Anyway, to sum it up. If you want a singleplayer shoot'em up game that's made of awesomeness, buy this. It will be worth it if you enjoy the old school doom style. If you want a multiplayer game, this will probably be a dissapointment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: inconsistent map design, lots of points of no return and stupid checkpoint system, otherwise would be a great game. As it is, it only offers enjoyable fights and a nice story. Still, it's a good game even if not really great.

Doom:
* Fast-paced fights. Movement could be a bit faster IMO, but is still fast enough to dodge projectiles. Not enough to run away, though.
* Good weapon selection. My only complain is that the chainsaw requires fuel. And you can't carry much of it, even with full ammo capacity upgrade. But it's a balanced trade-off, since it can insta-kill anyone except bosses.
* No health regen. Instead, when you are low on health, you have to quickly kill someone, they'll drop lots of health for you.

Not Doom:
* Map design is terribly inconsistent. It starts like Half Life, suddenly turns into Doom, then jumps to Jedi Knight 2, then back to Doom, then slowly deteriorates to Half Life and stays that way to the very end with some minor backtracking in some levels.
* Lots of points of no return. They are placed randomly and not recognizable. You just enter a room, the door shuts behind you, AND your save game is overwritten. Sometimes there is a way to reopen it or teleport back, but often there is not. This obviously prevents exploration which is a shame because the secrets are great. You have an option to replay any mission, and you even get to keep items you earn on your replay.
* Ambience is boringly realistic. No that crazy mix of styles, when you could have blue sky, wooden houses, cardboard boxes, hallways of stainless steel and walls of skulls and faces, all in one! Not only it created a special Doom mood, but also helped to navigate. Here, Mars looks like typical sci-fi, Hell looks like a random horror scene.
* Enemies also look like random demons from a random horror movie. Even though most of them are taken directly from the classics, their design is much less cartoonish and doesn't feel special. Pinkies are no longer pink, for example, but just, well, pinky.

Other pros:
* Good secrets, but they get slightly worse towards the end.
* Classic maps! These are just like the old Doom, but with new weapons and monsters. Even music and sounds are just as you remember them. Too bad they have modern monsters which can be a real pain because they are more powerful and yet their numbers are like they were in the classic Doom.

Other cons:
* Stupid checkpoint system. No option to reload the previous save. No quicksave or any way to save the game manually.
* Non-skippable cutscenes. Reportedly, there is a workaround using the console. But sometimes you're locked in a room and have to listen to some boring speech for 17th time, and I don't think the workaround will help because technically it's not a cutscene. Thankfully, there are only few such moments.
* Toggle crouch! Makes the first boss fights especially frustrating since you have to crouch to dodge a certain attack and then you'll have to quickly stand up to jump and dodge the next one (jumping while crouched doesn't quite work).\t\t\t\t\t
