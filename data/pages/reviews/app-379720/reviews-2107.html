\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best shooter of this generation (so far). There's nothing quite like Doom, and here's why.

The story: There isn't much of one, and that's okay. This game is an excellent example of what video games can achieve compared to films and novels, they can tell the story through player interactions just as much as through dialogue. Doom does this perfectly, choosing to through the player right into the thick of the action rather than monologue about the current situation.

The sound: Mick Gordon delivers one of the greatest soundtracks of all time in this game. I could make my whole review about his work here and it still wouldn't do it justice, I highly recommend watching some of the various YouTube videos on it to further appreciate his masterstroke. To but it shortly, what he does is match the soundtrack to what the player would currently be doing in the game, then code the soundtrack to play certain things when certain actions are made. This simple detail makes every fight feel like a dance of death if you will, jumping, shooting and strafing around in bloody rage in perfect sync to the metal score.

The gameplay: Everything about Doom works together to make the ultimate gameplay experience. The arsenal of guns is well balanced, each providing a different use in combat, most don't become obsolete as the game goes on, which is good. The powerups also create exciting moments, but aren't too common, keeping those moments special. As mentioned previously, the soundtrack sets the mood perfectly, almost acting as a character itself. Last but certainly not least is the glory kill system, which is by far the best use of a quick time event before. When a demon is at low health, the player can melee them, showing a brutal kill animation and giving the player health. The glory kills not only serve the Doom Slayer but also the player, giving them a sort of adrenaline rush (it did this for me at least).

In conclusion, Doom is a brutal, unique and downright fun experience that any fps fan should experience. It captures the magic of the original games and improves on it in pretty much every way. Not only that, but it almost serves as a giant middle finger to many criticisms of the gaming community (violence in games causes violence irl! satanic imagery makes kids satanic! oh no!) rather than appealing to these frankly ridiculous statements as many other games choose to, Doom takes an unforgiving stand against them, something I'm sure we can all get behind here.

Also it's just plain awesome lol.

9.7/10 (would be 10/10 but the multiplayer isn't up to par with the campaign)\t\t\t\t\t
