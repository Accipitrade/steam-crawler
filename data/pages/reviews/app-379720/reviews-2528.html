\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot to be said about this game, which is surprising to me, because I thought this was going to be a pretty straight forward game.

The single player mode really feels like they thought about every kind of player. First, you get all kinds of difficulty levels to choose from - new gamers can play an easy going game, old gamers can have a real challange. Then, you get a full story to drown in, but you mostly don't have to stay and listen to the diaolgs - you can run through the game for a quick-paced play if you want, or stay and listen to every word if you wish. There are many secrets to be found, which i found fun on my second run, but you don't need any of them to enjoy or finish the game. You can really play the game in your own style, and they game kind of feels like it took your style in consideration.

The levels are well designed, and fun. The music is amazing, but i found it quite fun to turn it off and just play Slayer instead. I havnt played alot of the arcade mode, but it seems pretty fun to me. The snap map mode is fun, especially with friends - you have maps that can be played by up to 4 players. The online multiplayer is pretty good, not perfect, but you can spend a bunch of time on it.

I had a lot of fun running through all the campaign levels, and then going through them again, trying to find all the secrets and getting all the fun achievements that steam has to offer.

All in all, i expected a fast-paced but shallow game, and im happy to say its much more than that - yes, there's alot of gore and demon-bashing, but there's also a lot of sense of accomplishment to go along with it.\t\t\t\t\t
