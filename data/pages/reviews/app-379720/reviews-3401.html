Overview

An extremely enjoyable experience, however limited the length of the game is. Expect to beat it within 8 - 15 hours, depending on your difficulty choice and exploration habits. It's not a very lengthy game but there are a lot of enjoyable aspects to it. If what you're expecting is a demon filled apocalypse of non-stop action, you came to the right place.

However if what you were expecting was story infused with your game, you might want to look elsewhere. While it has sustenance for those looking for reasons for what you are doing -- it isn't much. There is PLENTY of lore through text and some audio logs and in-game holographics, but it's not very deep.

As you should expect, it's a mix between doom 3 story and modernized doom 1 and 2 gameplay. It's serious, but in a corny kind of way.


What worked
  • Guns
  • Pacing (Except for a few spots)
  • Atmosphere (Tone, Setting)
  • Sound Design (Music, SFX, Etc.)
  • Graphics
  • Voice Acting

What was alright
  • Performance
  • Secrets
  • Upgrade system
  • Story
  • Bosses (Not enough of them)

What didn't work well
  • Runes (Many were useless or overshadowed everything else)
  • Equipment (Lackluster or just niche usage)


Summary

Despite a few shortcomings and parts of the game feeling somewhat elongated or drawn out, it's an enjoyable experience and well worth a purchase. I won't spoil the ending, and if anyone cares AT ALL about even the smallest hints: Ignore the next bolded sentence. The season pass is seemingly forced when it comes to the single player, it shows itself very blatantly. And if there is no single player content, the game shows itself to a sequel very obviously.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been a really long time since we've had a DOOM game to talk about and write reviews on, lets start with the positives since in my opinion, this DOOM doesn't have many.

The first thing is that this game looks great, just amazing, defiently a modern updated version of the doom games, not that i expected pixel graphics still, but none the less, the games graphics are probably the best for a FPS style game, and surprisingly, i think they look much better than Fallout 4, kinda makes you wonder what Bethesda did to make Fallout 4, oh well, on ward to the rest of the positive notes on DOOM..... oh yeah, there is no more positives.

Yes DOOM has amazing graphics, but thats about it when it comes to how great this game is, when i got this game, i expected it to be a constant firefight and just an amazing soundtrack and blood and gore everywhere and just sheer badassness from being the DOOM Guy, but what i got was a game with sections of action with almost no soundtrack, actually, there is no soundtrack, its just instruments, no singing or vocals to make it sound more badass than it already is, in fact, if i wanted to play a game with more action, and an actual soundtrack, i'd go play Killing Floor 2, that game in my opinion has a much better formula than DOOM does in terms of action, i mean heck, look at all the trailers for DOOM, the cinematic trailer has an amazing song playing while DOOM Guy is going around killing all sorts of baddies, and speaking of killing, that special finishing move system they have so you can kill people in a "cool" way after you've shot them enough, its just like 3-5 different ways to be "cool" on just the basic humanoids, which like 3 of the kill moves overshadows the rest because 90% of the time, you're infront of the enemies instead of off to the side or behind them, it's just alot of repitition and it makes it seem really bland.

All in all, i was hoping for an action packed FPS with tons of blood and gore, instead i got a damn good looking walking simulator with some action that seems very bland, like thers something missing, just some umph to the game to get it going, the chainsaw doesnt feel that great and in fact i think its stupid what they did with it to make it special. In the end, from the little bit of time i put into the game, i'm going to give it a 4/10 below average, great graphics, but lacks in everything else, no soundtrack to make you feel badass, the action is sub par and you really dont feel that much of a badass, and heck, it just seems really bland, the originals were much much much better than this, save your money and go buy Killing Floor two if you want a super action packed game where you do feel more of a badass than DOOM Guy, atleast this DOOM Guy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow about point/counterpoint:
DOOM is back! / DOOM is back, again.
Graphics are amazing! / Could probably be more optimized to run on lower end systems.
The melee kills are awesome! / The melee kills are kinda gimmicky, and most likely the sole reason why this game has high system requirements.
The action is fast paced and relentless! / Clear a zone of bad guys, move to the next, repeat.
The sound is fantastic, must wear headphones or use 5.1 / The sound is great, but the cheesey heavy metal riffs must be turned off. I feel like I'm playing Kingdom Under Fire.

I could keep going,but... Basically, if you are a fan of the series you will be pleased. If you are a super-fan of the series, you might be disappointed. It's basically the same game with a new twist on an old story. And like I said before, the melee kills are satisfying at first, but soon lose their appeal.

Buy it anyway. Despite my gripes, it's fun.

On a side note...This game is CPU heavy, My vid card barely broke a sweat with default settings, v-sync on. But my AMD FX-8350 was working overtime and running a little hot.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom the multiplayer beta alone, I felt like I was playing a $15-20 game, something functional & enjoyable, if a bit lacklustre. After the beta ended, I was hoping that the campaign would bring up the rest of the slack.

It easily has so far.
-The AIs are just smart enough, and are surprisingly mobile at times. (I never would have guessed that a Mancubus could jump, let alone climb...)
-Combat is fast-paced, and mentally challenging at times. (I'm looking at you Revenent-Cacodemon Dream Team...)
-A very traverse-friendly game world, with lots of verticality and not much linearity. ALWAYS a good thing in this genre.
-Aforementioned game world also has a lot of little secrets that feel rewarding to find, regardless of how useful whatever you find is.
-The weapons feel a lot more visceral and brutal than expected from almost any title anymore.
-The customization options feel like they're there only if you want them to be. They don't feel forced into the game.
-The same customization options are also rather well done IMO, they make most weapons a lot more flexible.
-The Glory Kills. It's Brutal Doom's Demon Strength Rune, IN HD!!
-Also, surprisingly short of bugs on launch day. Only found two minor bugs, one relating to being in the way of an extending walkway and the other being an enemy that must be killed to unlock the room clipping into an unshootable spot.
(Edit:
-The game world feels natural. It doesn't feel artificially closed or artificially open. It just feels right.)

All in all, despite the varied AIs, far and away my worst enemy is myself. I have died more often to my own splash damage & to gravity than anything else. That said, being cautious when jumping is more advisable in this game than quite a few others...\t\t\t\t\t
