If your coming to this game for the single player I say you will have an amazing time. Combat is fun. Finishers are brutal. Story is alright, but lets be honest, you're here to blow ♥♥♥♥ up and the character seems to know this too.

The weapons were an absolute joy to toy around with and I am currently having an amazing time with it.

But then there's the Multiplayer...
God do I ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hate the multiplayer.
Basically at the starting point you will be pounded in all your crevices.
Sniper weapons and rocket launchers galore.
♥♥♥♥ spawning, literally I spawned next to the demon five times in a single match.
Borderline insulting matchmaking. Lv 1's and 40's in the same game.
Uninteresting maps and objectives. Most of which are just team ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ deathmatch with various quirks.
If I could buy this game without the multiplayer for more money than the original game, I would.

All in all I fully suggest this game for the single player, but I can't bring myself to recommend the ♥♥♥♥ multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: When the first boss appeared, I laughed - he had no idea how much of a pounding I was about to give him.

I was dubious when I saw the trailers - I thought this was going to be a mindless action game with no thought or depth to it, pandering only to people who still boot up Final Doom at the weekend. Armour and health pickups? Power-ups? Cheesy insta-kill melee system? Silent protagonist and minimal plot?

It turns out that the single player campaign for DOOM is actually really good. It's an extremely satisfying game to play and goes right to the core of a good shoot-'em-up - interesting environments, varied enemies, and excellent weaponry. Everything else is just icing on the top of a very solid cake.

The story is present, it's just given less of a focus. You can largely ignore it or you can ponder the plot between firefights. The game would not have been improved by adding a supporting cast or giving the protagonist an invested opinion, witty one-liners, or defiant retorts.

The weapon/armour/rune progression is a nice touch, too; it rewards exploration and creative use of your arsenal. The chainsaw and BFG both serve a purpose, and you'll find yourself thinking about when best to use them as you progress. They are tactical tools, not unlike a character's powers/abilities in games like Mass Effect or The Division.

For me the only underplayed element was the utility items (grenade/hologram/siphon) - they don't really add much value and could have done with further development.

The game is about being a bigger badass than every demon in hell, and then punishing the demons for thinking they could possibly take you on.

The glory kills never get old and once you develop your bloodthirst there are never enough enemies onscreen at a time to sate it.

If there is going to be a sequel to this game with more enemies to rip and tear, I'll be on that bandwagon faster than a guass cannon round through a cacodemon's eye.\t\t\t\t\t
