\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is the game your mom was worried about in the 90's.

There's very few games that I've played that give me such a visceral thrill as DOOM. The combat is so punchy and satisfying, and it makes you feel like a stone-cold demon killer. It's a fast-faced high-octane romp that really tests the FPS capabilities of the player through countless waves of demon hordes. The weapons are easy to use and vary pretty significantly in their function. The upgrades system is simple, but feels a tiny bit bare-boned.

Aesthetically the game is beautifully grim and evil. The environments themselves can get a little bit samey after playing a few hours, but they do an excellent job of maintaining the atmosphere. The sound design also helps the atmosphere, but the music isn't super varied and you'll often hear the same track multiple times.

The story honestly isn't much to write home about, but that's not really what DOOM is all about. The game is littered with collectibles and secrets; enough for completionists to enjoy but not so many that the casual player gets overwhelmed.

In short, this is an excellent addition to any FPS fan's library. It's simple, gory, exciting, and a HELL of a lot of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
