\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was an absolute pleasure to play.
The flow and weapon upgrade mechanics work very well, and feels overall rewarding.
Monsters are quite different and have specific patterns. On higher difficulties all monsters can be dangerous.
I decided to play on Ultra-Violence and it felt like a perfect difficulty level for me.
I probably died around 30 times total over the course of the campaign, with about half of these deaths from bosses as i was figuring out their mechanics.
The gameplay is amazing, it is basically a fast-paced arena style game with a huge focus on mobility and twitch moves.
Both areas and monsters are well designed and look nicely futuristic & hellish with a hint of cyberpunk.

I do think that upgrading would have felt better if there had been less lock-in of mods/runes and more unlock by challenges similar to how the mod mastery and rune upgrade is done.

10/10 game! play it\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is such dumb fun. It's something akin to power fantasy, in that your character is well and truly the bane of all demons... But that's only because of how the gameplay is built; you can shoot until your ammo runs out with no reloading, and you can do so while moving faster than your foes, for the most part.

If you don't do those, you die.

That's what makes it only "akin" to a power fantasy; it isn't one because you're not almighty, only... Mostly almighty? Either way, this successfully makes you feel like a total badass. There's enough diversity to your foes and weaponry, there's a straightforward upgrade system meant to add replayability, and the story, while nothing too impressive, is there to serve the gameplay well.

A great romp involving plenty of ripping and tearing. ...I mean, Glory Kills. My bad.\t\t\t\t\t
