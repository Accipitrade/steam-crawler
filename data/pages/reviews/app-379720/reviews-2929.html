A Fantastic Co-op Game, that isn't Co-op.
I never played the original Doom games. I heard about them, but just never got around to playing them and I always felt that I had missed out. So when this game came out I added it to my wishlist and waited for it to be fairly cheap. I paid £13 for it, and think that it is too much. I wish I had waited longer.

I don't play multiplayer.

Pros:
- It looks good. I enjoyed the graphics and liked the look of the game and the setting.
- It reminded me a lot of Aliens Vs Predator in terms of setting and story, particularly UAC instead of WY.
- The gunplay is good, well done and fun.
- There is a wide variety of weapons, most of which I never used, some I forgot I even had (chainsaw, grenades).
- Reminded me a lot of Dead Space.
- Reminded me a lot of Left 4 Dead. The game would have been fantastic to play Co-op.
- Decent First Person Shooter.
- The game ran, very, very smooth for me even on the highest settings. I don't think that I have ever played a game that ran so well. No fps drops, lags, slowdowns or glitches.


Cons:
- The Sci-Fi setting was a little too much 80s sci-fi. It is basically your run of the mill spacy settings as seen in games like Dead Space (2008) and Aliens Vs Predator (2010).
- It gets very repetitive, very, very quickly. Basically the game is horde mode in Left 4 Dead with demons and with a story tacked on. Kill everything till the door opens.
- The storyline could have been so much better, the film had a better story line.
- You have to collect key cards to get through doors. The dude who smashes everything, apparently can't get through doors without key cards. But demons can. I found this really cheap and annoying, just like it was in Resident Evil (1996). 20 years later and we're still looking for blue and yellow key cards to pad the game out.
- In Hell you collect yellow and blue skulls, instead of key cards - that's as far as the imagination and creative process got, apparently.
- The game isn't Co-op! The repetitive gameplay would have been ideal for Co-op and reminded me a lot of games like Left 4 Dead, Zombie Army et al, yet it isn't?
- Very, very linear.
- I expected something so much more from one of the most famous FPSs of all time.
- I found it boring and really couldn't wait for it to end, so I can't complain that it is too short (but it is a bit short).

Conclusion
It was fun in parts just a bit samey both in terms of story and setting and also gameplay. I had a go on multiplayer (not my thing), that seemed OK. But again I wish this was Co-op, I would have loved to have played this through with a friend like HALO, it would have been a different game then.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, I need to say I am blown away how Id Software pulled this off so well and I praise them for their excellent work. I was expecting another failed reboot like so many other games, but instead, I got the exact opposite. It is so refreshing to see so much extra care put into a game for the first-time players and long-time fans. The trailer is so accurate to the product. It has three parts of the game: Campaign, Multiplayer, and Snapmap.

I can sum up Multiplayer as an arena shooter with loadouts but the weapons are balanced VERY well and the Power Weapons/Power-Ups are actually powerful. The loadout weapons are balanced in a way that they are all effective at different ranges. Arena shooters where you have to control the map and time your weapon pickups are nearly gone. You also have the ability to play as demons from the campaign in multiplayer as a power weapon.

The enemy designs are great. It features all the classic enemies in the other Doom games but they are redesigned to fit the demands of a AAA title. All enemies have distinct abilities and shapes so that you can quickly assess what weapons to use at what particular moment. For combat your fps skills should carry over from other games, but you will need to relearn how to do footwork. This is not your typical cover-based shooter. When they said they were going "back to their roots" they actually meant it. In combat you need to play aggressive and either face enemies head-on or kite them around the level. YOU MUST ALWAYS STAY MOVING.

The difficulty is spot-on and the names for difficulty are a fun little treat for players of the original Doom games. "I'm Too Young to Die" is easy, "Hurt Me Plenty" is medium and "Ultra-Violence" is hard mode. There are also two more bonus modes for the most hardcore players who want a more punishing experience. (they are unlockable by beating the game or by entering a cheat code.) I would recommend playing on Ultra-Violence for the first time playthrough. Easy Mode is beatable with one hand and will leave you bored. Its nice to see games that have accurate difficulty levels.

Snapmap is like the Halo 3 Forge mode of DOOM. Modding has always been a big thing for Doom. Id Software pays homage to this by adding Snapmap. It is a custom games lobby that also comes wtih a map editor that allows you to easily snap map rooms together and make Metroid-like labyrinths. You can also add weapons, ai, non-agressive ai, switches, and terminals. You can even save the maps for offline play. Recently, the free updates included hell portals and retro rooms for making your own retro doom levels.

Lastly, I love the Easter Eggs and collectible to the originals in the campaign. There are a few easter eggs hidden in levels but you have to look hard. Sometimes they will reward you with a weapon earlier in the campaign. On Kadinger Sanctum there is a skeleton in a dark cave with a skyrim helmet on and an arrow in his knee. Its not a funny meme anymore but it really tickles me to see Id Software making fun of Bethesda's dialoge. Doom Slayer can find a bunch of bobbleheads of himself that unlock viewable models of guns and enemies in the main menu. There are also hidden "retro doom" levels hidden within the actual game's levels. (Like Wolfenstien: The New Order) It is very fitting and it unlocks full retro levels accessable in the main menu.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe multiplayer is quite dead, and pretty lackluster. However...

EASILY one of the best single player experiences I've had in a looong time. I couldn't stop playing the "story" and since I've completed it, I want to play through it again on the hardest difficulty. It does not hold your hand, and it some parts it will even beat you mercilessly over the head. It will not seem too difficult at first; maybe even for the first few hours if you're a good gamer. However, trust me it gets difficult, almost comically difficult... But NEVER boring or overly frustrating. Only at a few small instances did I ALMOST get bored - more like frustrated - at a couple of minor "puzzle" areas in which the exit or next step is not clear, but those were pretty rare breaks from the intense action. The weapons are fantastic and the mechanics are smooth. And the music... The music is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ fantastic. 10/10 soundtrack.

My only complaint is that I wish you could always control the direction of your jumps/double jumps without having to unlock that ability later in the game.

All in all, I love this game, would recommend to anyone who loves action and shooting things in their ugly faces or mercilessly slaughtering demons in Hell.\t\t\t\t\t

The levels are beautiful, well designed, and filled with brutal demons. The secret classic levels are a very nice touch. I don't particularly care too much about the 'collectible figures', that sort of thing is very much par for the course these days. The enemy design is largely good, though Imps are way too brightly coloured, as are Barons of Hell (The latter being the bright pink of the original, when they and knights were just different colours of the same sprites).

Action is fast paced, and a harkening back to the original's focus on speed and brutality rather than the more modern focus on cover, driven by the fact you get health from melee execution moves. This is helped along by a pumping electro-industrial metal soundtrack that kicks into overdrive during large-scale encounters, which really got my blood pumping.

Regarding my complaint about additional content.... Yesterday there was an update which included a large amount of new content. The update is over 11GB in size, and because it's a patch/update rather than a free content DLC, the game will not play until I delete other programs to make room for the additional content I don't want. And, of course, it's a long download time because it's a large update. It's a pretty awful approach to deploying new content and features instead of smaller more frequent updates, which, while mildly inconvenient, are nevertheless a much smaller inconvenience than this, or the free voluntary DLC approach that other games use for large updates. It's sufficiently frustrating to me that I am deeply drawn to not recommend this game, but I'm not going to ♥♥♥♥ over the people that worked hard on the game and succeeded so well just because the publisher's deployment team is such a bunch of wankers.

In short, round of applause for the game's designers and programmers, round of boos for Bethesda's ♥♥♥♥♥♥ customer service management.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a great return to form from id Software by doing what made them famous back in the 90s: super fast action, ridiculous guns, and an army of grotesque hell demons to deal with. If you've played the original DOOM, you'll feel right at home as this is essentially a re-imagining of the first game in the series. There are no jump scares or slowly crawling through dark corriders like DOOM 3, instead you are running around at breakneck speeds with guns blazing, finding blue keys and blowing up explosive barrels to instagib mobs. The plot is pretty predictable, but it's just an excuse to keep you moving forward. Collectibles abound, but you can use the helpful map and suit upgrades to make them easier to find, and they're actually worth your while as they'll help you upgrade your arsenal. The big arenas filled with enemies, the thumping industrial metal music, and the boss fights all had me screaming with frustration and then shouting with victory when I finally overcame the challenge. Turn the difficulty up and enjoy this retro-inspired high adrenaline experience!\t\t\t\t\t
