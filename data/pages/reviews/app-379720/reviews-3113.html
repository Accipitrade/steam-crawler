\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReturn to the classic fast paced keycard fps shooter with a few modern tweaks for good measure. Fantastic soundtrack but more importantly them nostalgia feels from over 20 years ago playing doom on my brothers 486 PC. Wow has it really been that long?! The game looks beautiful and runs so well on the Vulkan graphics api. Fantastic rock soundtrack to boot.

Look forward to a sequel. Expect roughly 12 - 14 hours for the campaign playthrough. Re-play value is there if you want to try all the different weapon upgrades and power up routes and find the various secrets.

The combat relies on the old school fast paced arena combat movement to survive as hordes of demons overwhelm you. If you are from the Quake 2 generation it will come naturally to you, none of this popping up from cover to make careful bursts as enemies slowly move in on you. You will get rammed in the butt by a invisible bull demon if you don't embrace the adrenaline fuelled pace.

BONUS
Fans of Overwatch , once you get the Jump boots, you can double jump and grab onto ledges just like Genji !!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell... this is it. After Bethesda released Fallout4 I gave up hope on this company. It just looked like a very bad joke - a company, that created TES franchise (especially Morrowind), bought the rights on Fallout, made pretty decent Fallout 3 and let Obsidian make a flawless jewel "New Vegas", finally released a... Call-of-Duty-Sims post-nuclear role-playing game - come on, mr. Howard, this is not an RPG.

So I didn't expect anything good from Bethesda, as they have usually been failing at the 4th parts in their franchises (Oblivion, Fallout), but the new Doom cinematics were amazing, and it was little beacon of hope, that ID software will create something really nice.

ID absolutely hit the spot. They made an amazing FPS with beautiful graphics, great gameplay (still the killings are reminding me of Serious Sam 3, but that doesn't bother at all - they have been made perfectly), strong level design (you can wonder around the whole map, search for secret places or just look at the landscapes) and, of course, easter eggs referencing to the previous Doom parts. To put it bluntly, everything was made as canonical as it can be - monsters, weapons, doors, key cards and skulls - everyting (can separately name one thing, that actually impressed me so much - Baron of Hell. He's just absolutely amazing). Another thing I liked pretty much is the weapons and armor upgrades. IMHO, these are really helpful on a higher difficulties giving some extra possibilities to win the fights. The storyline, not to say "weak", but it's just standard - we heard it many times before. But still, as John Karmak said, "Story in a game is like a story in a porn movie. It's expected to be there, but it's not that important." - it is made very solid, so nice to see that ID didn't fail at this point. Another outstanding point is a difficulty. I played on Ultra-violence, I died so many times, and that's my separate thanks to developers: I see that games nowadays are made very casual and tolerant - so that was a breath of fresh air and a very nostalgic feeling when you are trying to beat the game over and over and it feels sooooo good, when you finally win. A feel, that you really need to make an effort to win game, stands really high for me, so thanks again.

The last thing I want to tell about deserves a stand-alone paragraph. It's a SOUNDTRACK! It is absolutely terrific... The music speeds and charges up in the beginnig of battles - guys, who made the tracks, you are just insane - THESE ARE ABSOLUTELY WICKED SICK TRACKS! Thanks a lot for these emotions, I don't have enough words to describe all the feelings this music gave... A very grim, ominous and oppresive dark ambient instantly being replaced by brutal and feral metal riffs - my favourite kind of music mixed up together.

Conclusion: 100% recommended game, that is worth its money up to the last penny. We'll have a look at Dishonored 2, but for now Doom stands for GOTY in my personal list.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame of the Year 2016

An incredible, violent romp across Mars and the depths of hell. DOOM should be viewed as revolutionary in regard to the modern FPS. ID and Bethesda have hit a home run with this one. Here are a few reasons why I loved it:

+The campaign's story isn't overdone or forced down your throat
+You find youself using every weapon in your arsenal throughout the game (except maybe the pistol)
+The soundtrack amps up your adrenaline during difficult battles
+The level and character designs look amazing
+The game mechanics work and smoothly integrate
+Multiplayer is fun and Demon Runes can turn the tide of a match
+GLORY KILLS

If you like FPS games, there's no reason to pass this title up.

10/10 Doom Slayers would hell walk again\t\t\t\t\t
