\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a reboot, DOOM (2016) managed to reinvent its own formula by allowing you to rip demons apart, limb from limb, head from neck, with your own bare hands. It loses some of the charm that the original games had, but compensates by giving you the opportunity to dig a chainsaw into the beating heart of demon-kind, to the sound of Mic Gordon's exquisite soundtrack.

It's not the old DOOM, but it's the DOOM this generation needed.

The game's story is easy to understand, and, although it stops your carnage a couple of times to feed you exposition, it happened so rarely that, overall, it makes the story nice and to the point.

Guns feel responsive, chunky, weighty, great sound design. The upgrades are a slog to get at times, but you don't need all of them to enjoy a good slaughter. The game is very fun as is!

Rip and tear, until it is done.\t\t\t\t\t
