\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA surprisingly good reboot for the original DOOM that had, in its time, laid the conventions for FPS games.
What I enjoyed:
- the ultra violent aspect of the is completely over the top and within the premises of the game.
- the gameplay is easy to control, and yet manages to be rich
- the level design encourages you to be mobile in order to survive and to explore all the corners of the map
- the design of the various enemies are well executed, and everything has been animated to look ultra badass and violent. It's even ridiculous sometimes. It becomes fun because it's so grotesque.
- some side quests / challenges that are fun to complete and to search
- micro management, skills to unlock and weapons to upgrade. Really not the focus of the game but adds a nice touch
- last but NOT least: sound design and MUSIC! I'm not so much into metal (I wouldn't even know how to describe this genre) but right from the opening title you're sucked into the mood of the game. The music evolves as you're fighting or not. The sound design gives you great feedback to let you know what's going on in the game.

what I did not like:
- the story is close to being non existent. But I don't know that it's such a bad thing. When you try to provide an experience that is more leaning towards gameplay, you probably want to pull away from other aspects. So I don't know that the game would've been any better with a better story. Still, I can't say that it's any good.

So if you like FPS and fast gameplay, give it a try! I personnaly come back to it every now and then.\t\t\t\t\t
