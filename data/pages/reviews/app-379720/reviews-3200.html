\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's difficult to review this game. It's all about gore and violence. The recipe is decades old and if you approach this first person shooter from a perspective of modern gamer it falls flat on its face compared to something like Borderlands. But Doom isn't trying to be that type of a game.

All attempts at putting a story, any background and lore in this game are as pointless as they were in Doom 3. So set your expectations right. This is "enter the area, kill everything, find the key, enter another area, kill everything, find the key..." rinse-repeat game. Doom is about running and gunning, about killing hordes of monsters and it does it extremely well. It has none of the issues of Doom 3. There is no stupid flashlight or any kind of other annoyance to slow you down.

There is no lengthy introduction and no character customization other than improving your stats. You're a Doom Slayer who just woke up from long slumber and you start killing things within seconds of the game's start. You also add mods to your suit and weapons to add functionality to your gear.

Weapons are mostly fun and very well done with highly detailed models and satisfying punch. There is the most satisfying shotgun I have seen in a game in a long time. Other weapons feel and sound mostly solid. The two exceptions are the plasma rifle which is effective but not much fun to use and the rather weak rocket launcher with almost no splash damage to speak of. The launcher has a very useful upgrade/mod though that fires a burst of three missiles that lock on the target. The railg.... er, gauss rifle is awesome too. You get the BFG late in the game and you will always be out of ammo.

The game is very vertical. There is lots of climbing and jumping but it never feels overdone and excessive. Sure, you will sometimes miss a jump and die, in which case the lack of Quick Save is a real ♥♥♥♥♥. You will have to reply from the last checkpoint. You will find yourself climbing giant, tall structures, scaling rocks, jumping over chasms, some are really cool and I didn't mind repeating them.

Enemies are tough, can soak up lots of damage, they move fast and attack you relentlessly with CHARGE!!! being the only tactics they use. They do have weak spots you can exploit though. I actually found the fights too difficult on normal setting. And I hate a checkpoint save system.

Doom is mostly an old school closed-world, corridor shooter but it does have a fair number of large outdoor Martian landscapes with some nicely designed, highly detailed industrial structures and backdrops, as well as huge industrial indoor areas with many levels, catwalks, jump pads that are really fun and you get to use the sniping mods for your weapons to pick demons from afar. It's ultimately a linear shooter but it does give you some freedom, and actually encourages you, to explore the maps for upgrades and secrets. So there is a fair amount of wandering around before you get to the next area. You can even reach some of the objectives by taking various paths. However, you can't go back to the previous maps. So if you are the completionist type you will have to find all the secrets and upgrades before you progress to the next area. You can't go back.

However, the parts that take place in Hell are booooooooring....

The combat is fast, frantic and satisfying. No stealth stuff here, no taking cover, no hiding, it's shotgun-in-the-face. You stop moving, you die. Glory kills, i.e. particularly gory melee kills, range from awesome to stupid to hysterical. The first time I ripped monster's arm off and pummeled him to death with it I laughed for few minutes. Glory kills regenerate your health and are mostly fun, do not get old. Also, you don't take damage while performing a glory kill. If you do enough glory kills you will keep your health up and won't have to run away from encounters in search of health packs too often. Some may think this is lame, but it is a reward for killing fast and killing plenty.

THE BOSS FIGHTS ARE COMPLETELY STUPID, RIDICULOUS and boring. I had to enable god mode to pass them.

Controls, movement, targeting, weapon handling are smooth, responsive, I'd say perfect. Typical id tech under the hood.

There are Rune Trials along the game, off-game mini games that are supposed to gain you something but I found them difficult, boring and not worth the time.

The sound is well done, scary ambient noises, machinery, screeching monsters and all. No complaints here.

The music is an aural orgy of industrial metal which is very close to the NIN music from Quake games of the past. It feels very appropriate to the game. No complaints here. Well done.

Now. Graphics. This is the most insanely optimized game I have ever seen. Sure, it doesn't have to render large areas full of trees, grass, water streams, birds and rabbits the way Skyrim or Witcher do. There is very little in terms of environmental and physics effects. It's all pretty static. But the game does not hiccup even once during the most busy battles. Graphics are not exactly next-gen but pretty nice anyway. This game was obviously build for speed and fluidity of combat and movement.

There is one head-scratching bug in the game that makes you wonder how the hell it passed through Quality Control: if you assign "jump" to the the right mouse key, or any mouse key, you will not be able to get past loading screens :/ WTF? You need to assign a secondary keyboard shortcut, such as space, to be able to progress through the game. The game does not listen for mouse input during te loading screens. I have not encountered any other bugs or crashes.

Final verdict? A mixed bag. It would be a better game without the stupid boss fights and without the parts in Hell. Unless you're a die-hard Doom fan $60 is too much for this game. It simply does not have the content of most modern games. It's fun, but it's not $60 worth of fun and the old mechanics eventually get boring. But for $35 it's probably worth the 10-15 hours you spend frantically blowing monsters to bloody chunks. I finished it in 11 hours on easy (lack of Quick Save is annoying) and without finding all the secrets.\t\t\t\t\t

2.The game is even full of upgrades that quicken and speed up almost all the aspects of the game to make the game feel EVEN FASTER.

3. The combat system is great, no enemy feels like a bullet sponge or too weak or broken. but keep in mind I only played the game on Hard mode.

4. Every level's map is huge and full of secrets and unlockables. playing casually and hunting for secrets took me about an hour for each level. that being said the game has a satisfying amount of playtime if youre playing casually and trying to find most of the secrets and upgrades.

5.The multiplayer is surprisingly fun despite what ive heard across the internet and from friends.
the multiplayer is very fast paced and simple. shoot whatever moves thats not on your team, doing that in every gametype wins you most matches.

6.the multiplayer also has the BEST character/weapon customization I have ever seen in a FPS and I mean that whole heartedly, the cosmetic changes you can make to your character and guns are very very satisfying and fulfilling.

7.The OST of DOOM is great. they tried a change pace from original doom/brutal doom with an interestinf DnB (drum and bass) soundtrack I cant say that I love it , but I cant say that I dislike it. It gets me moving and it fits the scene of the game. It makes you want to go on a neverending murder spree as a doom soundtrack should.

-THE BAD

most of the things i mention here are only minor isssues I had with the game

1.In the campaign weapon upgrades are specifically tailored for every guns alt fire. I found this to be an issue because alot of times I found the Alt fire for most weapons to be really good already (on hard) without much need to upgrade them , Ive only upgraded certain weapons alts because they significantly make already good alts EVEN BETTER.
the Super shotgun is the only weapon in the game where you can upgrade the core ability of the gun because there are no mods for it.
this aspect honestly shouldve been given to all weapons!
the starting pistol would do so much better if it had better fireate. or if the rocket launcher had less self damage.
core upgrades to guns would have been better imo then only having upgrades that make alt fire's more usefull then they already are.

2.multiplayer is good but it couldve been so much better. multiplayer completely rebalances weapons to be compatible to be equipable in player made classes. at first i really didnt like the idea of it but overtime it grew on me. tho I know Doom vets were likely expecting Arena focused combat where you have to pick up guns around the map and make do with what you have. this is why I feel that they shouldve added a free for all arena style gametype where everyone starts with pistols and have to make use of there suroundings and learning weapon spawn points and choke points.

3.Multiplayer is also hindered due to the "become the demon" aspect of it. becoming the demon is a major game changer like becoming the big daddy in boishock multiplayer OR if there was only 1 spawnable titan in Titanfall. Demons in multiplayer were designed to be curbstompingly strong, the Demon can easily change the tide of battle but thats not the core issue, the issue is that most if not all (I havent played all the gametypes) have the Demon in it.
this is annoying because sometimes I just wanna play barebones TDM or freefor all without any gimicks giving the enemy team/my team an advantage.

overal it was a pretty dope game.\t\t\t\t\t
