\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI grew up with DooM. I think I was 6-7 years old when I first fired up that sweet game back in 1996/97. I absolutely loved the fast paced, arcade shooting that it provided, along with delicious graphics, gratuitous gore and a top-notch level design.

When I heard about DooM 3, I couldn't be more excited to play it. However, when I first got my hands on it, it turned out to be kinda disappointing: what I was expecting was a true-to-the-roots DooM with delicious graphics, but instead all we got was a survival horror inspired fps, which looks cool, sure, but it's got nothing to do with how I see DooM.

12 years after the release of DooM 3, though, I can finally say that I'm back to enjoying DooM just like when I was a kid.

This new chapter in the DooM franchise takes the best out of the Classic DooM series, throws it back into the frying pan, adds new flavors, and it makes up for a fine, delicious dish, which tastes familiar, yet fresh. Fast paced, arcade shooting is back: there's no health regeneration, no covering, no weapon limits, there's health and ammo pickups, and a load of aggressive demons, even more so that the original: don't expect sluggish and slow enemies. Demons will chase you all around the areas and will relentlessly try to kill your ass. Movement is key to stay alive, especially on higher difficulties. In addition to fast movement, there's also a nice sense of verticality given to the maps, as a new vaulting and climbing mechanic is added to the game. You can flank enemies however you like to, and they can do the same to you, making up for fast paced and challenging fights against hordes of demons.

Level design is very clever: it encourages exploration and secret finding to find upgrades for your armor and weapons, and also to find so-called Rune Trials, which, upon completion, will award you a Rune: these artifacts give you small perks during gameplay, allowing you to gain certain advantages during gameplay. There's a fair number of them, but you can only keep 3 equipped. These small factors increase gameplay customizability, and therefore increase replay value.

Soundtrack is back to being pumped, with both industrial and djent metal and atmospheric tracks. Mick Gordon really kills it this time, adding badassery to the overall experience.

All of the above describes the Singleplayer campaign, which is absolutely lenghty and a glorious love letter to the classics.
But all that glitters ain't gold, unfortunately; Multiplayer feels generic, and mixes arena elements, like no health regen or cover system, with modern MP shooters elements, such as loadouts, but it doesn't please either of the player base. While it might feel fun to blast some fellow players in a TDM match, Soul Harvest match or even the brand new Warpath mode, there just isn't much content to keep you wanting to play for longer than 30 minutes, and the aforementioned problems with the way MP was conceived detract from the experience.

Snapmap could've been great: a prefab-based, scriptable, inbuilt map editor would allow unlimited content within the game, and let people play the game virtually forever. Unfortunately, this didn't turn out as expected.
Snapmap feels extremely limited, with very few environment customizability options and the same limits as Multiplayer on the weapon limit (that is, loadouts).
I'm a Classic DooM mapper, I've made some huge maps for a few projects, and it feels extremely satisfying to just keep your creativity ramping up, clear of any kind of worries about limits et al. Snapmap, however, forces extreme limits on both map size and active demons. You can NOT have more than 12 demons active at a time, you can't hold more than 2 weapons, and maps can only go so far as to be medium-sized, all with the same 120 prefabs available in the set. You might want to give it a shot to build some funny stuff, but after that, it devoids itself of any real usefulness.

TL:DR: Singleplayer highly recommended, MP and SnapMap not so much.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start this off by saying that I grew up playing the original two Doom games. This game is definitely not either of those. This game is, however, the sequel they needed, and it's packed full of references to the originals. It's a modern FPS with varied environments, more colors than brown, lots of movement (if you stand still you WILL die.)

Glory kills at first are incredibly fun and satisfying, but towards the end of the game I found myself just using them strategically. I think id focused a bit too heavily on them, with so many upgrades and achievements being based on them, but it's entirely possible to go through the game not using them at all.

The levels are filled with secrets that you will miss if you are not paying very careful attention. They hide items, collectibles, upgrades and story items. The story is very non-intrusive. There are a few cutscenes that cannot be skipped, but they are only ever a minute or two. Most of the story is found in data logs and descriptions of enemies and weapons that can either be read or completely ignored. However, this was the first time I actually found myself reading all these descriptions because I was genuinely interested. The main character is the same as in the original two games, and you discover much more about him, and about the story of the Doom universe, but only if you care to. If your goal is to run really fast and shoot demons, that's all this game has to be.

Would DEFINITELY recommend picking up this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOther than watching old DOOM games on youtube and watching the 2005 DOOM movie, this DOOM is the first game of the series that I've played and here's my review :


PROS :

1. Awesome Single Player
The single player is an awesome experience that you will have if you have played or watched this game in youtube. The Gory kills are incredible and the level of details are also nice

2. Good soundtrack ( especially during fights with enemy AI, the Heavy Metal in the background is good )
The Heavy metal music that bangs in the background during a fight will sure do keep you rolling.

3. SNAPMAP, which is the second main reason, the game gets positive reviews
The most liked feature of SNAPMAP where you learn from basics to the advanced level of creating a map, and with much time and dedication, you can also create a HUGE MISSION by yourself. The tutorial and handling is easy.

4. Overall a good experience
Overall the game is good and if the Dev's fix all bugs and patches, then it would be incredible.
Dont get fooled by those Pro's , lets look at the

CONS :

1. Frequent game crash
Sometimes, the game crashes unexpectedly and without any indication or warning during. This is really one of the biggest problems in that game and imagine the RAGE you will have when the game crashes in the middle, while you were about to kill the final boss in Ultra Nightmare.

2. Needs more soundtrack
The sounds are good but we need more metal songs even when not in a fight and it must always run in the background. The music only plays when you fight a large number of enemies

3. Dev Console
The most hated thing to happen in the game. Hitting the Tilt " ~ " button ( the one to the left of the number 1 and above tab, if you really dont know what that is ) will unlock Dev Console and will mark your game as cheat and block your access to Multiplayer along with blocking all achievements from being received and completed unless you delete your savegames Sometimes, even after deleting savegames, and restarting the game, this bug still exist.

If you have the same problem just follow these steps :

1. Seach for " Saved Games " folder on your windows search bar.
2. Click on id Software > DOOM > base > savegame
3. Move them to some other folder
4. Delete the consolehistory.txt file in the base folder ( folder before the savegame folder )
5. Open steam, go to your library and right click on DOOM and select properties
6. In the local files menu, at the bottom uncheck Steam Cloud Sync.
7. Open the game and make sure you dont have the Dev Menu opened bug.
8. Start a new campaign and as soon as you have reached a checkpoint, close the game and exit to desktop.
9. Now move your previously saved game back into the DOOM > base > savegame folder
10. Now open the game and you should have your old saves back and no longer have dev console open problems.




CONCLUSION :

Overall, apart from those cons which are RARE and will be fixed or patched soon, the game is solid FPS with all those gory kills.

ONE SENTENCE : GET THE GAME. ITS WORTH THE CAMPAIGN ALONE !!!! ( and SNAPMAP )\t\t\t\t\t
