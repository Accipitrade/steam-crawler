\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn the surface, it looks like glorified violence. Underneath that thin surface, DOOM is so much more than a simple shooter. Rather than try to find cover and funneling enemies through as narrow a hall as possible, the demons of DOOM will frequently come in many flavors, including climbers, fliers, long-ranged, and armored. Enemies drop health on death, but that requires making one's way to them to collect, all while being swarmed from all sides.

This is where DOOM's genius starts to come in - being aggressive is not just for the sake of satisfaction, but by breaking an encirclement at its weakest point, you can prevent yourself from being surrounded. Not only that, but this also ties into the Glory Kill mechanic, where by finishing off a demon in close range, one can acquire more health items than normal. This creates a game where aggression is required in order to prevent being flanked, heal up, as well as closing the gap with sensitive targets.

The 'exploration' leaves something to be desired, and feels kind of thrown in. Once you've collected a certain amount of weapon upgrades and settled on the ideal runes you want to use, further exploration feels like it's just there for kicks. The first couple feel great - get access to a weapon early, get that precious early weapon mod, but after awhile it feels like it's just there to tick the boxes.

That being said, this game definitely deserves the praise it has been getting. It may not be the pioneer of an entire genre like its classic roots were, but it's certainly good for a fun change of pace from cover-fire mechanics.\t\t\t\t\t
