\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBethesda really hit the nail on the head. I remember back in 1996 when I got my first computer and one of the first games I got was doom. As many people will tell you doom was ahead of its time spearheading FPS gameplay. This genre spawned a host of titles we all know and love today but I feel as if one of the most important concepts of the original doom (which is extremely fast paced close quarter combat) has been diluted as games become more ranged and slow pace... Sure you can throw in games like COD into the arguement, but when it comes to the single player aspect even games like COD isnt that CQC. This Doom really brings me back to the old days, where you are essentially in a 15x15 ft room with nothing to cover and LOS around but the occasional crate or wall and all you can do is quickly learn to exploit that room and figure out what patterns you can run to stay alive because if you stop, you die. And sure you can run back but chaces are youve already exhausted all the supplies from where you came from so your only option is to get back into that killing floor and fight or die.

When it comes to the story sure its basic, Doom always had a pretty basic story line, its always been about the gameplay (forgetting doom 3 because in my eyes doom 3 isnt really doom, its an ok game but its not doom). Basically the story is "theres a bad guy, bad guy wants to bring bad. but good guy is there to help!!! wait.. good guy is kinda bad too.... atleast theres you, and you dont give a f**k" All in all this game isnt winning any awards for story but I never expected that.

In the end it was always gonna be the gameplay that would determine how I'd rate this game, and honestly its everything I wanted. I was hoping this game would good back to its roots with the expectation that because it has been over 20 years that there would be some new elements to the game. People have been complaining that the glory kill system gets repetitive almost as if they expect there to be an unlimited amount of different glory kill types when in reality thats a ridiculous expectation. I like the glory kill system for what it is, another way to speed kill an enemy in a environment where you have very little time to actually stop think and kill. Its very risk reward, the risk is during a glory kill you have to stop and you lose sight of whats ahead of you, losing your bearings and often times (on a higher difficulty) that split second of doubt can cause you to run in the wrong direction. Most of the times this isnt that much of an issue, but when you're in the heat of a battle it really can make a difference. The reward ofcourse is saving ammo, an insta kill and alot of the times a bit of health... This system also help you delegate which enemies you're gonna kill first and possibly use the bulk of your ammo on and which enemies you are gonna save ammo on, to kill on the fly or to try to get health back... Just another way this game pushes you to be creative and express critical thinking. All this with the uprade system which is as I stated before, one of the new-ish elements I expected from a title thats existed for 20 years, this upgrade system isnt anything special but it does what it needs to do to revamp an old title. So all this mixed in with the classic fast paced CQC FPS really sold this game for me I'd give it a 9/10, honestly the only way they could of made it better is if they really did create a mind blowing story, that would of been a bonus and would of made this game a 10/10 but the lack of this hasn't really hindered my enjoyment at all.\t\t\t\t\t

Anything that is wrong in multiplayer and/or snapmap is instantly fixed in single player campaign. Although the multiplayer is still pretty fun, the campaign is definetely the highltight of DOOM 4.
Pros and Cons of Doom Campaign:

Pros:
-Graphics are beautiful
-Gorey, bloody, hectic, and fantastic battles
-Well Optimized
-Challenging
-Glory Kills are spot on
-Fast gameplay
-Non-repetitve environments
-Secrets
-Story (although this o doesn't matter to most, I still think its cool)
-Enemy types
-Fast loading screens
-Hell area
-Sounds
-Fluid weapon use

Cons:
-Can be a bit repetitive at times

Pros and Cons of Multiplayer:
Now this one is a bit contriversal so I am just going to say I am a huge fan of Quake, Doom, and other arena shooters like unreal tournament and I dont think its half bad.

Pros:
-SNAPMAP!!!
-That thing about the gorey bloody fun again
-Character customization
-Weapon custimization
-Fast
-Addictive
-Demon Ruins
-Load times
-Graphics
-Hack Modules
-Glory Kills (again, because I love ripping other peope's heads off)
-Decently fun

Cons:
-OP weapons like the Chaingun and Rocket Launcher
-At times reminds me a little to much of Halo, not really of CoD though
-No skill or ranking system system
-Pointless gamemodes

Overall, DOOM is an amazing game, with beautiful graphics, fast and fluid gameplay, it is definetly worth the $60 I paid for it. Multiplayer can be a bit shotty but in my opinion it can be loads of fun, along with SnapMap which is awesome! I cannot stress enough though how amazing single player is, this reboot really knocked it out of the park with that one. In fact, I feel the game would be worth $60 even without multiplayer. The gameplay is fast and violent and fun as hell (literally) as the originals from the 90's.
I would give it a solid 9/10. Hope you enjoy this game as much as I do!\t\t\t\t\t
