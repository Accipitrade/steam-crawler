\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. It doesn't shove the story in your face and give you the option to hunt it down and read it yourself.
It give you lots of action with maps that seem to be derived directly from the original doom.

The weapons are so darn satisfying. Throw in wonderful movement and this game is beauty.

I thought prior getting this game that the glory kills would hurt this game much like how it hurt other games in past that used this mechanic to spawn health. I figured I would be going around and glory killing everything and never die.
But the amount of action that occurs in the game and the amount of health that you get from glory kills ensures that doesn't happen unless if you have some planning in how you play.

None of the weapons are useless for me. I found myself using the pistol to take down a Cacodemon just because it was fun to do so.

That was single player. I haven't tried multi-player or the snap maps yet.\t\t\t\t\t

Doom is one of the best linear shooters I have played in quite a long time. However It's quite different from most modern shooters in the sense that it returns to a fast paced movement and momentum based arena shooter just like the original game. It rewards speed and flow, never stop moving otherwise you will die. Environments are gorgeous and well designed with many hidden upgrades and secrets that reward exploration.
