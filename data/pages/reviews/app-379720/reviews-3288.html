\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCAMPAIGN - 10/10
Just wow..... I'm blown away. I bought this game having all of the skepticism in the world but I couldn't be more pleased with how it turned out. The campaign takes off immediately and doesn't stop going for the remainder of the game. This is the Doom you remember. Incredibly fast paced, devastating weapons, gore galore, enormous enviroments and no overcomplicated story like certain previous releases in the series. ID took a hint from how Doom 3 was recieved and decided to go back to basics, and holy hell did they do a good job at it.

MULTIPLAYER - 6.5/10
There's really nothing inherently awful about the multiplayer, I just feel like I've played it before. If you've ever played Call of Duty or Halo on multiplayer than you've already played Doom multiplayer. You've got your typical Team Deathmatch which is still fun in small doses. The only really cool mode is Warpath, which is like a Base capture but the base is constantly moving.

SNAPMAP - 7.5/10
This really could have been so much worse. I'm surprised at how in depth the tools really are but of course it's going to be somewhat limited no matter what they include with it. If you're not the gam devs, it's impossible to design a truly unique level. One big blunder however is how they made the in-game mechanics identical to the multiplayer mechanics, which are very different from the single player campaign. It's annoying when you're trying to build a map intended for only one player.
It's pretty fun to put levels together though. Playing other people's maps is easy and quick but I already see a lot of total crap out there. There's a massive amount of "xp farming" and "easy snap points" cheat maps. I think the mods need to get on that shizz\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is an amazing return to a series once thought to be 6-feet under other triple A shooter titles like CoD, Battlefield, and Fallout. Although the multiplayer is by far nothing to gloat about, that one aspect of the game is completely redeemed by its masterful SnapMap and Campaign game modes.


The campaign has a basically non existent story because the Devs for DOOM felt it would be more appropriate to focus on the gameplay, which I applaud them for. If you want story its there, its just hidden under a lot of codex's, audio logs, etc. there also arent any cutscenes and character development is non existent, which also isnt a bad thing for this game. There are only 3-4 characters to mention

DoomSlayer; the main character (DOOM guy)

Olivia Pierce; the crazy that opens a portal to hell

VEGA; the UAC's AI assistant

Some robot ; IDK the owner of UAC I guess.

None of these characters really develop, the only development this game really needed was combat, and that's exactly what it got. Doom Guy is the most notable of all, and this is just because of his movements and how he interacts with his environment. He punches things unnecessarily, he snaps things because why not, he performs very brutal executions on helpless Demons after they take enough damage, and all around you can just tell how much DOOM Guy is pissed at the UAC for the events that are unfolding. This is most notable when interacting with objects, by punching, smashing, and tearing guns out of dead officers hands. Because of this DOOM Guy is probably one of my personal favorite videogame characters.

The combat in this game is amazing. The only way to stay alive is to kill, keep on killing, and dont stop killing until every demon in the arenas are dead. Kills give you bonus health packs and armor, and it's really easy to get adjusted to this "push forward" combat system. Executions provide the assailant with even more bonus health and armor, abd the chainsaw provides the assailant with a surplus pf ammunition flying from the mangled corpse once called a demon.

SnapMap is amazing. Whether its you destroying hordes of Demons while you yourself are a demon, or farming up enough crops to get new gear for ypur character, there is always something new you could do with SnapMap. And I mean this. You can change entire game scripts with just one click of your mouse and like Todd Howard said about the latest fallout's building system, it just works.

TL:DR

DOOM is a masterful reboot to a series once thought dead, with amazing campaign and building modes. The only thing that bogs your experience would be somewhat repetitive mission design and repeating arenas in Snapmap.\t\t\t\t\t
