\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my first Doom game, and I gave up on the campaign after 10 hours or so at 2/3's completion.

It was fun for about 6 of those hours. The combat is excellent, the enemies are diverse and killing them is gratifying. So why did I give up on it? Not because the campaign was too long; I've happily sunk dozens of hours into Prey, Dishonored and Mass Effect, because there was so much complexity in the campaign. I love good dialogues, interactive environments and compelling stories, but that isn't what we have here: the game is just too long for what it is.

After about 8 hours you have all the weapons unlocked, and at least most of the enemy types have been revealed, but the type of gameplay is the same. The game comprises a dozen or so separate levels, and in each case you the player have to get from one end to the other, often for no other reason than to progress to the next area. (Viva la dirt league have satirised it as 'pressing buttons' on Youtube, which is essentially accurate.) Likewise, with the enemies, you enter an room, fight through a variety of enemy types that start relatively weak and get stronger, then enter the next room and rinse and repeat. There's nothing to hold your interest: there are practically no dialogues, but instead monologues - your character is mute - and what there is of that is minimal. There are only 3 characters in the game that I encountered, excluding you, and the lore is poor and generally lacking.

What confounds these problems are the collectables. In each level, there are a variety of collectables and unlike, say, Tomb Raider, most of the collectables bear directly on your combat ability, which means that you spend about the same amount of time clearing the level again on finding collectables, just so you can hold your own later, It also means that you cannot just switch your brain off and go kill things because your character won't progress. It's an anticlimactic conclusion to each level and it becomes increasingly annoying as you play. I like hunting down collectables and I enjoy challenges in games, but I don't like it when I'm forced to do it. Like many things in life I guess.

tl/dr: The game is too long for what it is - it doesn't have the necessary complexity to support its length and so I got bored and didn't finish. Also the game has problems with pacing and progression.
