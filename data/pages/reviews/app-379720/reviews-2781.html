\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen you absolutely, positively NEED to wreck ♥♥♥♥ and feel like the most badass person in the world, you play Doom.

+ Fun and fast paced gameplay.
+ Weapons and their alternative fire modes are fun to use.
+ Temporary powerups add enjoyment to hectic combat, especially the Berserk powerup. This is when you REALLY feel like a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.
+ Progression system that works neatly with the progression of the game.
+ Excellent sound design.
+ A story that doesn't get in the way of the game's fast paced gameplay. The game's lore goes deeper than most think and can be explored if desired.
+ Dynamic soundtrack that reacts to your actions. (Check out Raycevik's video on it)
+ An extra point for Mick Gordon's soundtrack because it is just so GOOD. Even if you're not planning on playing Doom, I highly suggest listening to it either way.

- Weapons can feel a bit underpowered at times, ending in a pattern of using "the best" weapon most of the time.
- While the game is mostly bug free, I encountered a weird bug where my weapons "jammed" and refused to fire. Swapping weapons does not work either, forcing a checkpoint reset.
- Trying to 100% the game on your first run is most likely not going to happen due to the all the nooks and crannies you have to check for hidden loot. This also slows down gameplay drastically for little to no reward beyond some ammo and collectibles. This is of course a personal opinion, as some may find it fun to look for secrets.


Additional notes: I have not fired up the multiplayer, and I don't think I will be doing so considering how poorly it was received. Doom is a singleplayer experience through and through, and no matter how good the singleplayer is, tagging on a quickly thrown together multiplayer just because people will moan about a "singleplayer only" will always end in tragedy. Stop whining about this, which goes both ways. (MP only, SP only, etc.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a fast paced First Person Shooter, set on Mars. The demonic minions of Hell are invading, and only the legendary Doom Marine can stop them. This reboot stays true to the franchise’s old school roots, while adding a few updated mechanics.

The Good

•\tGreat weapon variety, including shotgun, assault rifle, minigun, plasma rifle, rocket launcher, chainsaw and BFG. They all feel powerful and fun to use. You can carry them all, and you find them all before the halfway point of the campaign, which means you get plenty of time to enjoy each of them.
•\tWeapons can be upgraded with extra fire modes. For example, the assault rifle can have a zoom scope which effectively transforms it into a rapid fire sniper rifle (and there are plenty of wide open spaces to make use of it), the plasma rifle can stun enemies (extremely useful, because aiming plasma against a moving target can be tough) and the rocket launcher can lock on targets.
•\tHealth doesn’t regenerate automatically, but there’s another really cool system in place. When enemies are near death, they will be stunned for a short time, which leaves them vulnerable to a melee finishing move. Any enemy killed by this method will drop some health pickups. Also, any enemy that you kill with the chainsaw will explode in a shower of ammo pickups. The effect of these two mechanics is that when you’re low on health or ammo, you actually run towards danger, rather than away. (You can of course find health and ammo pickups lying around the maps as well).
•\tLevel design is fantastic. Lots of verticality and flanking opportunities.
•\tLots of secrets to find, which encourages exploration. You can find energy which increases maximum health, armour or ammo. There are hidden portals to challenge rooms which reward you with runes, which you can equip to gain passive buffs. And you can find armour upgrades which grant even more buffs.
•\tLots of different enemy types, including many old favourites. Their movement and attack animations are fantastic.
•\tHeavy metal soundtrack is a perfect match for the intensity of the action.
•\tDifficulty level 3 of 5 seems perfectly balanced for a veteran FPS player. Some encounters last several minutes, throwing dozens of enemies at you, and they pushed my skills to the limit, without making me feel frustrated even when I died.
•\tControls are fully rebindable, and work great with mouse and keyboard.
•\tFlawless technical performance.
•\tCampaign is a decent length at just under 10 hours.


The Bad

•\tThe story is kinda basic. Obviously its not the most important thing in a Doom game, but Wolfenstein New Order showed that FPSes can have good stories.
•\tFirst person platforming sections are occasionally annoying.


Overall

This is quite simply one of the best First Person Shooters of all time. Probably in my top 3, alongside Half-Life 2 and Wolfenstein New Order (please don’t ask me to name a #1 from that list). Essential purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

- THE ENEMY COMBAT BOT CODE IS THE BEST I HAVE SEEN. It is varied, challenging and etertaining.
- Mission scripted events are frequent and well designed.
- Enemy bestiary is varied and well designed.
- The combat is very active, you need to stay in motion because there are always projectiles coming your way and they will hit you if you do not move.
- Graphics, sound and animations are the highest quality available in 2016.

If we had been told that the reason for all the brutality was the anger of the Space Marine because the demons killed his pet rabbit, we would accept it just fine.
