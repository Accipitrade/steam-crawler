Really good classic doom gameplay with new gfx, huge maps, and nice monster variations.
Well worth a single campaign playthrough.
I initially thought this would be another asinine reboot, but it's not.
It's a very polished gameplay loop. Excellent work. RAGE was fine, this is much better.

I initially had some problems with proper configuration so that it runs well, since this game needs to be played with a minimal framerate over 60, no matter what gfx quality. I now play this on a phenom II x4 with some 2.8ghz and a gf 1050 with 2gb ram, so cpu quite potato, gfx okish. The game engine (ID tech 6) is quite scalable, it runs really good (60-120fps) with render resolution full hd * 0.65, medium to low settings, especially for shadows and lights, 1x temporal aa and motion blur high quality. The game plays and looks beautiful at these settings.The (beta?) vulkan render mode introduced some weird constant stuttering issue, so i use the gl4.5 mode.

Also, the motion blur effect actually works very nice this time, first time i preferred it to no motion blur in any game.

Gameplay is simple but complex:
You kill monsters, retrieve keys to certain doors and find secrets.
The monster killing is done in a way i haven't seen yet in this kind of perfection.
It's all very fluid and seamlesss, with nicely tuned mechanics that grow as you play the campaign.
The base gameplay is totally opposite to the currently dominant slowed down sniper/cover shooters.

Move and shoot, while doing so you get quite some options to continue.
Stand still and die.

Again, very nice ID tech openGl/Vulkan engine and a really good revival of classic doom gameplay without any corporate horseexcrements attached.

Very solid singleplayer campaign with complete replay value around 100%.

Individual pros and cons:
+ very polished & extended classic doom gameplay loop with nice gfx & sound
+ plays very nice with mouse + key
+ nice guns and very polished gunplay
+ nice and scalable engine running on opengl/vulkan, looks beautiful even in low render res
+ lots of options and a level editor
+ no weird microtransactions or dlc ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥
+ no jumpscare-dark-horror-theme - it's brightly-lit
+ nice and varied map design (although the map themes are limited to 2)
+ nice and fluid movement, nice gunplay (although i mostly use the shotguns like in classic doom)
+ the quite-interesting-non-story has been pretty unintrusively wrapped around the gameplay
+ high replayability (mostly for the classic levels)
+ fair price, at discount it's a steal (really nice 2016 game)
+ no more denuvo drm

- initially included some ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ denuvo drm
- no linux release
- no moddability further than snap map
- needs some initial settings tuning (default settings were much too low on FPS)
- quite long load times (on mechanical hdd)
- mediocre and sometimes confusing interface (but tolerable)
- vulkan renderer introduces constant microstuttering (nvidia1050_2gb)
- there are only 2 environments (mars & hell)
- enemies can sometimes be hard to spot (not a problem on the classic maps)
- checkpoint system (it's tolerable)


As an end note i want to add that you really need to appreciate the fast paced classic gameplay loop and find a setting for your machine that gives you the 60+FPS at minimum, else you might be frustrated, because the game could be quite impossible to beat and very punishing with a cover-based-mindset approach.\t\t\t\t\t
