\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is, by far, the best shooter I've played for a long, long time.
I admittedly played it first on the easiest difficulty - which still had it's challenging points, to be fair. I'm going to pay through it again on Nightmare, now that I've got the mechanisms figured out.

If you loved the original Doom and Doom 2 but felt that Doom 3 was trying too hard for "Horror" but loved the modern imaginings of the demons, Mars and the UAC then you will love Doom 2016. It's fast paced and hectic, but it's perfectly balanced with Doomguy's agility and sheer firepower.

I'd also like to talk about the soundtrack - a LOT of games have got brilliant soundtracks, but Doom has probably surpassed most of them. Picture it; you're about to go into combat with a literal horde of demons; they're tougher and stronger than you. You've got guns, skill and speed.
Then a metal-as-♥♥♥♥ soundtrack powers up, and you suddenly become a thousandfold more confident in your ability. As each demon falls before you, the soundtrack pumps you up more and more, and before you know it, you've annihilated every demon in the area. You're low on health, armour and ammo - but you've conquered the eternal foe.
Not many games can match a soundtrack perfectly to gameplay, but Doom can and has.

I've found no faults so far with it (no serious faults, anyway) and would heavily recommend it to anybody, whether you're a FPS veteran or a complete newbie to Doom.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is strictly a Single player review, I haven't played any of the multiplayer.

This game is incredible. The developers knew exactly what kind of beast they were dealing with and exactly how to make it dance. Since the days of the classic Doom that so many people know and love things in shooters and gaming has evolved. This is a magnificent blend of the old playstyle that people loved with the new technology allowing for even more versatility.

That said: the gunplay is solid, each weapon has a specific function (except the pistol). The story, while there, isn't being shoved down the player's throat. There are data pads to pick up that you can read at your lesiure and the occasional break in the action so the few characters can get a total of about two minutes of exposition into the game. The core of the game is shooting dudes and that is all you will do. There are bosses in the game that each have different and interesting mechanics, while complex they aren't brutally unforgiving (on standard difficulties from my experience).

The movement speed is fast, maybe not fast enough for some of the more die-hard fans, but solid speed that will be easy to slide into when you start doing chain Glory Kills and jumping across ravines to punch some airborne demons. For players who are used to something more like Rainbow six or halo it's going to take some getting used to but nothing that fifteen minutes can't fix.

The level Designs are varied and interesting, a lot of the levels do their best to make sure the corridors and connected rooms for some early levels, while tight and complex aren't completely labyrinthian. More commonly the rooms will open up into large and versitile fighting arenas with hazards and enemies galore.

If you like old school shooters or high pace action (or the 90's) this game is essential.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis game ohhh this ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ game i tell you its such a good game, i dont like FPS but i gabled and man am i glad i did.
ALright so this isnt your typical FPS, it has the basic FPS elements but it adds much more with its style and atmosphere.

First of all this game knows what it is its an FPS where you move from setpiece to setpiece and kill ♥♥♥♥ while remaining on the move, and it doesnt try to take itself too seriously in any way.

The game is focused on making the player have FUN, this is accomplished by removing coverbased shooting due to a great AI (yes it ♥♥♥♥♥ up sometimes) that will find you and get you out of cover, as well as removing reloading on weaponry.
Campaign: the campaign is simple you start, you kill you get your armor and you kill some more untill nothing is left to kill. the campaign adds a nice throwback to the old game by having powerups, and pickups all over each battlefield making the game across the entire experience feel as if you are playing a modernized version of the good old DooM.
BE ADVICED : the campaign is heavily based on collecting keycards, or skulls to open a doors and move to the next fight, for some this may become tedious or boring, but i would say its a good way to lead you to the strongest Point of the game = its combat. the campaign also contains secrets, collectebles and upgrades for the player to collect and the perfectionists to complete :)

Multiplayer: currently the multiplayer is lacking people it can take time to find a match depending on time of day, but i found myself lost in the action once we got started with a lobby, with tons of costumization for weapons, player model, and even taunts which adds more rediculous fun to the game. though sadly WAY TOO FEW MAPS in its current state.

Overall i enjoyed my experience with this game and i will continue to enjoy the multiplayer for a long time to come, this is a SOLID piece of FPS gaming, that is quite worth the money, or at least later on when the price drops 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
