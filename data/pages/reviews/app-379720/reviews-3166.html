Single Player
While I am not near done with the singleplayer I can say I am having a blast with it. I have not even come close to finding every secret and I look forward to finding them on my future play throughs. Doom has insane amounts of replayability and I would give the single player alone a 10/10.
Multiplayer
Now for the multiplayer; I will say I see a lot of potential but with classes and cheap powerups being a huge factor of it I have to say it was less than enjoyable. With arena shooters like this being able to have loadouts with whatever weapon you see fit does not work and it completely ruins the multiplayer and map balance. I don't understand why they felt they needed to add these 'Call of Duty' like elements to the game this could have been the best multiplayer shooter of the year. While the multiplayer has its moments I have to say it was a disapointment and the multiplayer by itself is a 4/10.
Conclusion
Do not let my distaste of the multiplayer scare you away from the game. There has been a lot of time and passion poured into this game and it is genuinley a fresh breath of air. I needed to give the Singleplayer and Multiplayer seperate ratings because I felt this game would have been more than enough with the Singleplayer alone so the Multiplayer is just an addition and doesn't bring the game down at all.\t\t\t\t\t

Single player only review.

Doom (2016) is truly worthy of carrying the legendary name. It feels like a spiritual successor to Doom 2, with a few bits of Doom 3 and Quake (Quad Damage baby!) thrown in.

This is in the same fine caliber of remakes/reboots as Wolfenstein: New Order, which is one of the best single player FPS games I've played in recent years.

The FPS mechanics are close to perfect. At first I frowned at the double jumping and ledge climbing, but soon realized how these mechanics helped keep the pace of the fights high, as your great freedom of movement on all 3 axis pretty much always leaves you somewhere to go, even when you're not familiar with the layout of the map.

The level design is actually pretty reminiscent of Doom 2, with both narrow corridors and large open areas. Plenty of secret areas to explore, which gets rewarded well.

The good old gang of monsters and weapons are back (with some additions), and they all look, sound and handle superbly. This is pure no-nonsense, balls to the wall, Doom action with just a bare minimum of story and cutscenes.

Generally you'll face fewer, but tougher versions of the hordes from hell. The "Baron of Hell" is pretty fearsome now, so it feels better than ever to pick up a "Beserk" powerup, put a grin on your face and blast your 1-hit death machine fist into his sorry face.

The game is impressively true to it's herritage, even though the old dog HAS learned a few modern tricks...

Health and ammo no longer has to come from traditional pickups.
Damage a monster so it almost dies, and it staggers for some seconds, during which you can perform a "Glory Kill" by using your melee attack. This triggers a badass gory kill animation, and results in some dropped health.
The trusty old chainsaw has become a fuel guzzler, but in return it makes the sliced foes drop ammo. The tougher the enemy is to cut through, the more fuel the saw consumes.

All weapons (except for the pistol) have 2 unlockable modifications, and each of these mods have 4 upgrades. The first three upgrades are unlocked by spending weapon upgrade tokens, which are rewarded by fulfilling challenges, finding secrets and having some sort of algoritm evaluate your performance at the end of each level.
The final upgrade level of each gun is unlocked by completing a challenge related to the usage of that specific weapon mod. Some of the mods are friggin' kickass, and really help keep things fresh and varied as you progress.

Your suit too has upgrades. Four categories of them actually, and these are unlocked via Praetor tokens, which are found on the corpses of Elite Guards.

Even passive "perks" are introduced in the form of "Runes" which are unlocked on special challenge maps accessed through portals. These runes can also be upgraded by fulfilling challenges. You start with 1 rune slot, but can unlock up to 3 slots.

And finally your max health, armor and ammo capacity is upgraded by finding and collecting "Argent Cells" which are spread across the maps.

At the end of each level you can see how many exploration items you missed.

Overall the presentation is great. The id Tech engine performs excellent, so the awesome detailed artwork never once stuttered on MAX settings in 1200p on my GTX 980ti. I loved the industrial/metal themed soundtrack, but I reckon it might annoy the more faint eared folks out there :) It sets the mood perfectly regardless.

One of my few complaints is that I would've loved a thick layer of badass cutscene driven story full of colorful characters. If it can be done in a Wolfenstein remake, it can be done in the Doom universe as well.

The game crashed to desktop 4-5 times during my 10 hour playthrough, which never happened for me with previous id Tech powered titles.

That aside, it's my pick for best single player FPS of the year. Well done id Software - you're still a force to be reckoned with.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother game that unfortunately doesn't live up to the hype.

"Best shooter in years!"
"The Triple A game makers remember how to make a fun FPS again."

By that, I suppose they mean they just made a game with verticality and a fast-paced atmosphere. While the game is fun, it is nowhere near worth sixty dollars. With a campaign that scrapes eleven hours straight through, any percent, I'd say that a sixty dollar price tag is rediculous. Buy it at twenty dollars MAXIMUM. The multiplayer is some kind of joke, though the one that you laugh at for all the wrong reasons. Apparently "remembering how to make a fun FPS" means "let's take Halo 5 multiplayer and make it faster". Intuitive, to be sure. With no actual modding (thanks, ID) and instead an amateur "Snapmap" garbage, there's no room for modding and therfore leaves you with no choice but to continually play through the same campaign on multiple difficulties. Once again, not worth sixty dollars. If you're truly craving Doom again, go play DOOM I or II and get a couple of custom WADs. Hell, there's a Hentai iteration of the game, it's not too much of a stretch to imagine that there would be something to fit the fancy of any player.

In all, if I haven't said it enough, this game is NOT worth sixty dollars. I returned this immediately and am going to wait for it to drop again during the Winter Sale. It's already dropped by half within a month of release, I'm to hope that it will be a tolerable price come December.\t\t\t\t\t
