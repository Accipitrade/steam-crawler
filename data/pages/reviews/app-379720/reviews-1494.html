\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReturn to the classics of ye ol' absolute carnage with every tool in the shed.
I played the classic Doom and this was an absolute joy to return to.
Decided to tackle the game head on with Hurt Me Plenty difficulty because why not, boy it hurted me plenty.
Game was fast-paced and action-packed as expected, some of the secrets and easter eggs were immediately obvious and some aren't. I think I walked out of the game with only two or three gun mods missing because I found the bots and didn't find the access to them. Oh way, replay value right there!
Also didn't get some of the weapon masteries, so I guess I'm returning to Hell for that.

The story? Well, I wouldn't say it's a masterpiece but it definitely paints Doom Guy in that glorious demon slaying light. There's always the evil and it's obvious, but you're not really playing Doom for deep story and meaningful character development (at least I hope not). They point and yelled, and you march on in guns blazing, because you are the Doom Slayer.

Still a little saddened by the game-breaking bug at the end with the Spider Mastermind, but I managed a workaround and got to the end.

Now that Doom Eternal is out, we can pick up where we left off.\t\t\t\t\t
