\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10/10. Best game of 2016. The campaign is virtually flawless. The multiplayer is flawed, but I still had fun playing it on the Xbox One. I haven't beaten the game on PC yet, but I have on the Xbone. The graphics are good. Not the best around, but still enough for those seeking to test their GPUs. I love this game enough that I researched the graphical settings to find the best way to optimize FPS while still having great graphics. Although I love this game, I haven't bought any of the map packs. I'm hoping that Bethesda will announce a Resurrection Of Evil/Wolfenstein The Old Blood style expansion pack for this game. The single player is replayable, although not as much as Old Doom. Snapmaps are surprisingly fun, although painfully limited in some parts. Snapmap is great if you don't want to go through the hassle of making a classic DOOM WAD. I give the multiplayer a 7/10, Snapmap 8.5/10, and the campaign 11/10. Obviously, that must average out to 10/10, right? The campaign alone justifies buying this game. I have a ton of hours on xbox, making snapmaps, playing the campaign, and playing the multiplayer. I look forward to adding another Countless Hours to the PC version of this game over the summer.

PROS:
-One of the best single-player campaigns made since the 90's.
-Snapmaps are entertaining to make and play.
-Multiplayer is still fun, tho.
-Graphics are pretty good
-Runs well
-Game is fast
-Faithful to Old DOOM while still feeling refreshing
-SnapMap has a surprising amount of detail.
-SnapMaps are cross-platform!
-Single player is long, but doesn't ever get that fatigue that long games have (Post Anor Londo in Dark Souls, anyone?)
CONS:
-SnapMap feels a bit limited when creating in terms level size. I understand console has to have limitations, but there should be "PC Only" levels that exceed the boundaries that id Software placed.
-Although SnapMaps are cross platform, your DOOM ID is not. I was disappointed to find that I couldn't use the same SnapMap account on PC that I used on Xbone.
-Multiplayer has LOADOUTS.
-Multiplayer feels... a little slow, when compared to classic Quake, Unreal Tournament, or even Quake Champions.
-The Single Player isn't endless.

10/10. My only complaint with the single player is that it comes to an end at some point.
