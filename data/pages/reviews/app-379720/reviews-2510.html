\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA stark departure from DOOM 3, id software really outdid themselves in their reimagining of the DOOM franchise. By replacing the dark and jumpscare horror tactics in D3 with butt-kicking and beautifully disgusting demon curbstomps, DOOM (2016) puts the player in the gore-covered boots of a nigh-unkillable death machine. Give demons nightmares for a change as you cut them in half with a chainsaw, blast holes through their chests with break action shotguns, and disintegrate every particle of them with the BFG 9000.

On the easier difficulties, you cut through hordes of the damnable races without so much as a batting of an eye and you enjoy every single moment of it. Bump up the difficulty and you get to explore the tender love and care that the studio put into the massive and complicated combats. The gunplay is simple, smooth, and rewarding. The level design encourages creative platforming and rewards maneuverability with advantageous sight lines and avoiding damage. The secrets range from “in the open” to fairly puzzling, rewarding you for going off the beaten path without robbing less careful players of any core game material.

Visually, the game is stunning, even if most of the game is a viscera-covered hellscape. The lighting effects and texture details place you on Mars and in Hell like you wouldn’t believe. It was a bit taxing on my system at max settings, only getting 40fps in the biggest fights, so I played at medium-high and achieved a solid 75-90fps. YMMV

And personally, the soundtrack really takes his game from a great shooter to a masterpiece. Any apprehension you feel as you approach a large open field covered with ammo and pickups that you KNOW is going to have a massive horde will melt away as the driving and raging metal guitar screams at you to get bloody.

10/10 for an all around well-made singleplayer FPS.

FWIW, picking it up in February 2018, the multiplayer is largely abandoned at this point and every lobby I’ve gotten into is filled with hackers. I was excited provided the gunplay but ultimately disappointed there. Oh well.\t\t\t\t\t
