\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOutstanding.

Doom is a gift from the past, where shooters where fast paced, your quick reflexes where well rewarded and gameplay first was the norm.

The singleplayer campaign's plot is a B Movie quality at most, but that doesnt matter. It doesnt matter because the gameplay is so fluid, the controls are so responsive and the maps are so well designed that you just slide through the campaign like you would slide on a gigantic cube of hot butter.

And then, the soundtrack just wraps everything like the tastiest burrito you've ever eaten and creates a frantic and adrenalitic experience that will let you wanting a lot more. But in the good way.

The multiplayer is a nice add, but definitely not the game's main forte, although the Co-Op missions are very engaging and entertaining, specially if played with friends.

Doom breathes quality. It's a game about love. Love that the developers put into it, and love of shooting demons in the face with a shotgun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've reserved my opinion until I finished the single player campaign, I was kinda worried it would be another "RAGE" ending....

In short, just about everything about this is great, for an FPS, it's exactly what you'd expect, constant action (mostly), no dull spots even when you're exploring, man this game is tight!

What I noticed....

Graphics - Well always the first thing you notice. On my dated FX 6300 and much newer Radeon 480, never missed a beat at absolute full settings on 1080p, smooth as....something very smooth at 60fps. Visually, its vibrant, environments feel alive even though there are dead things everywhere, half the time I just wanted to explore and look at how lovely everything looked.

Sound - PUNCHY!!!! The design of all the sound, the guns, the slight drop in noise when you glory kill, even when you turn the music off, the ambient noise is fantastic.

Music - You'll either like it or hate it, not everyone likes heavy metal guitars mixed with dubstep, well it probably wasn't dubstep but there are so many genres out there, who cares. The music suits the mood "Rip and tear...until it is done!"

Gameplay - So it's a shooter, you're going to blow up a lot of stuff, melt many demon faces and look for secret areas when there's no action. The only problem I really found is that when the action is about to start, it's almost predictable. You won't find too many enemies in random spots unless they're low level imps or possessed. You can say in certain areas " oh, this spot here, there's going to be a bunch of enemies spawn as soon as I click this ". But that's almost a good thing, you can prepare. It just didn't seem to flow as well as I thought it should have, too many quiet spots followed by masses of destruction....you could look at it a few ways.
On another note, there are a lot of upgrade options with guns and the actual suit and the replayability is great, especially if you miss a secret or two and want to go back and play an older level. Before I even finished, I put it on my "replay" list.

Absolute must buy!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlease note: This review is about the Singleplayer Campaign only. I did not touch the Multiplayer (yet).

I expected this game to be something like Diablo 3 in First-Person. Meaningless killing of the same enemies over and over again while barely hitting any real challenge. I was proven SO SO wrong.

The systems used in DOOM are one of the most thought-through ones I've ever encountered in a video game. Every type of enemy that you encounter, stay meaningful threads throughout the entire campaign. Imps that are introduced in the first levels, can easily cause your death in the last section of the game without having changes values like HP or Damage (The AI might change tho). The same goes with weapons that you unlock and receive overtime. Every weapon has it's own purpose, upgrades and effect. Useful throughout the entire campaign. I like how id Software accepts that this is still a game and not a reality simulator. By that I mean, things don't always make sense, but it's there for a purpose. Best example would be the DOOM puppet that can be collected as part of the exploration design.

The art direction is, as the screenshots suggest, superb. But the screenshots do not even sell the whole product. The atmosphere within the game is amazing. Not only the great environmental art, but also the effects and animations playing a huge role in making this game incredibly beautiful. Although there are not a lot of different locations in the game, id Software manages to spice things up every now and then. Their approach to what "Hell" looks like is stunning. Polished sound effects and a great soundtrack make this a top-notch art product.

I hereby highly recommend purchasing this game. 12h of fun and challenge (I played the most difficult setting available at start) speak for themselves. Go get it, slay some demons and make the world a better place!\t\t\t\t\t
