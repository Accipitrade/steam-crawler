\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn a nutshell, this title is surreal. Honestly, if you like FPS games, buy Doom; if you like devilish/cosmic/gore horror themes, buy Doom; if you're picky about building a good plot, with immersive characters, buy Doom; if you are a fan of the franchise first and foremost, buy the damn game...

The gameplay is sensational and gives you the option to adapt certain elements, so that they are even more in line with the classic gameplay of the series (with the centralized weapon, for example). Don't buy it thinking it's an adaptation, a remaster, or a standalone title: assume it's a Doom title, produced with great dedication and care by Bethesda. I'm impressed with the way in which many of the classic Doom elements (such as villains, items, weapons, and level progress) have been brought to the incredible graphical quality of our current generation. All this composition, supported by a surreal inclusion of the game's soundtrack and current gameplay elements in multiplayer in server formats, make you not only want to play Doom (2016), but also give Doom 3 (2012) a chance. and Eternal (2020), if you haven't already.

I think that the standardized value is quite ok, but I suggest that -- even so -- wait for the distributor to produce (which tends to happen a lot). The game requires average graphics performance, but delivers an undeniable experience for users with good processing power.

If there are still doubts, buy the game.\t\t\t\t\t
