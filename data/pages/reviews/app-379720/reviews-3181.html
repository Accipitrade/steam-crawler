I copied those specs from a Speccy report just to provide context to the crashes I have experienced. I am playing this game in one of the harder modes (its been a while since I started, so I don't remember the specific name of the mode), and therefore there are sections that I have found to be particularly punishing.

I never experienced crashing in the first half of the campaign, and the fact that I have experienced so much in the second half is really disappointing for me. All I want to do is beat the game. I enjoy the game and find its frantic pace to be fun and rewarding. But when you get to the ending portion of a 15 minute fight that took you five tries to get to, and you're fighting the very last enemy and you seem to have a handle on him and then...desktop. Well, that's grounds for a not recommend in my book. Especially when it happens around six times in various stages of the second portion of the campaign.

It's a fun game, but I can't handle the heartbreaking crashes. My own experience I know, but forgive me for asserting that my specs should be able to stand this kind of game. If stability improves, it will be able to shine like I know it can.\t\t\t\t\t

The Good:
- Fantastic engine: even with my average spec machine, the graphics are beautiful and very well optimized, with reasonable performance.
- The action is absolutely fantastic. It's the way a classic-style DOOM should be: fast paced, endless demon blasting with no BS
- The difficulty: it will very A LOT depending on the level, but it's the kind of challenge that's just fun enough, and not too frustrating.
- The quality assurance seems to be solid. I've yet to find any kind of bugs after my third playthrough.
- The music. The fact that they included parts or the original Doom soundtrack and adapted them in the best way possible for the new game... it's fantastic.

The not good:
- The weapons do not give you an impression of power. For example, in Doom 3: Resurrection of Evil, when you acquired the Super Shotgun, you KNEW it was a SUPER shotgun when you used it. In this new game, the weapons aren't underpowered in effect, but rather in feel.
- In my opinion, I prefer the style of Doom 3. Some parts of the gameplay in this new one feel kind of "rushed".
- The game is way too short.

In the end, I do recommend this game, but definitely not at its release price. When it hits a 40%-50% special down the road, that will be a worthy price to pay IMO.\t\t\t\t\t

-Amazing gunplay

-Great graphics

-Great and very fitting metal music

-Refreshing focus on gameplay with the story a very low priority, just enough to give meaning to what your doing/is happening (While this may seem like a bad thing it works very well for what it sets out to achieve, the story doesn't feel forced like in many fps games nowadays which focus on multiplayer while still trying to put a story driven campaign)

- Khorne approves this game

Cons:------------------------------------------------------------------------------

-SnapMap severely limited on PC by the fact that the consoles are connected to the PC snapmap thus maps made on the PC have to be able to run on the consoles

-Multiplayer feels like it will very quickly get repetitive (Will have to get more experience personally to determine otherwise, take with a grain of salt)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
As someone who does not often play FPS games anymore this game drew me in right until the end. Go in knowing that the Campaign is definately this games strongest point.\t\t\t\t\t
