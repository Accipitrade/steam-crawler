\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr -- All-around excellent game -- lovely graphics (with the advanced option to use Vulkan library for top performance), frenetically brilliant music, and top notch single/multi-player gameplay (save for a couple bugs) that will have you ruining the plight of demons for many, many hours. Unless you're ISIS, you will not regret buying this game.

You can avoid the most major bug which will never be fixed as long as you don't _ever_ use the "restart mission" option whilst in the middle of a campaign (single player) mission. If you do, you will lose anywhere from some to all of your progress.

Aside from this and another minor bug which precludes access to "Classic Maps" when switching from multiplayer mode, and another which will rob you of a couple Steam achievements (really, who cares?) the gameplay is impeccable and leaves little for the avid FPSer to desire.

Speaking of Classic Maps (as in the original DOOM/DOOM II maps) -- yeah, you'll be able to play those, too, along with an unlimited number of community-submitted "Snap Maps" which can be single-player, co-op, or head-to-head and range from "bada$s" to "God why can't I strop playing this game." If you think you can make a better Snap Map, go for it -- it's almost more fun than playing someone else's.

The multiplayer experience in Doom 2016 may have been rocky early on, but since the 6.6.6 patch, it has been nothing short of a riot. Very active community of non-idiots -- reminds me of the early days of playing the original Quake online. Older, experienced crowd who are willing to help you get your potatoes together so they can subsequently turn you into a puddle.

Unless you absolutely hate the idea of killing demons with powerful weapons, in which case you are probably a demon yourself or a member of ISIS, you will not regret forking over your hard earned cash for Doom 2016.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a fantastic example of just how to execute a pitch perfect "remake" in a world full of pathetic attempts to do so. I will admit to having a deep nostalgia for the original Doom, and I think that fact makes my lens of criticism much more focused upon any short-comings the new game might have. Truth be told - I played this game ass backwards, Console first, and that was my biggest mistake.

The console versions are impressive, true, but compared to this they can't even hold a candle. The controllers on all new-gen systems are incredible, I love all of them for specific types of games, but for shooters they are a mixed bag (of guts). Having a computer with a keyboard, mouse & the muscle to run the game on Ultra at 100+ FPS (and a high refresh rate monitor, I highly recommend a 144hz display) transformed my experience with it fundamentally! The music, graphic design and story mesh perfectly with the insanely over-the-top gore and action. Better still, I find as I notch the difficulty up on subsequent playthroughs, I discover new layers of combat complexity I haven't encountered since my time with Dead Space and Dead Space 2 on xbox 360. Simply Gore-ious. I love this game, more than I had anticipated, and it's only truly experienced with the volume, graphics and gameplay turned up to "11"

If you love old school shooters, hell even if you love new shooters, PLAY THIS GAME!!\t\t\t\t\t

graphics, performance, port, physics, arts, visuals...
8/10

Game is really pretty, although not crazily detailed. Runs very smoothly, and looks gory and dark as needed. Hell feels just like it should. The whole environment still feels a bit repetitive maybe, but I guess that's what a Martian base has to look like.
AUDIO

music, sound effects, voice-over, audio balance, subtitles...
6/10

It's okay, yet a bit disappointing. Music is not punchy enough in my opinion, we are far from the original Doom slayerish theme, it is more like ambient metal junk... Also sometimes the audio gets a bit buggy (monsters yelling louder than the master volume or than they should based on their location).
PLAY

controls, difficulty, level design, luck-strategy-skill balance...
7/10

It's good, level design feels oldschool and deep (huge levels!). QTE-ing most of thr monsters to receive items still gets really tiring in the end, I don't understand why they thought it would be a good addition in a nervous FPS... And difficulty is high so that's a good thing!
CONTENT

story, world, solo immersion, multiplayer depth, overall idea...
8/10

That is good classic Doom stuff, also probably less dark than it could have been, but still the nice gruesome combo of Sci-Fi, demons, indus, metal, gore...
OTHER

length, replayability, price, community, economics, devs...
7/10
Good length (actually surprising, at the half of it I thought it would be finished!), replayability is big if you want to complete everything in every level, and secrets/upgrades are funny and necessary enough.
TOTAL

Average sum with a secret formula to adjust nuances...
79% - a very good game!

A very good Doom, a good time spent for any fan, maybe less for new comers? The game could have reached the Excellency of 80% if they had spent less time on the uselessly complicated story (or make it more interestingly complicated? Who am I kidding this is Doom!). Or maybe if they had made less backtracking in the levels. Or again if they had polished the gameplay with more smoothness. Or maybe if they had put better music. Anyway: still a good Doom to satisfy our needs to bash up demons.

Disclaimer: Never tried the Multiplayer mode and never cared for it in any Doom, sorry!
