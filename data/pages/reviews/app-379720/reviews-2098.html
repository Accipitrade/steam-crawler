If the current FPS shooters are too complicated or too hard to play, this game will in a way teach you how to play and improve.

The soundtrack is one of my favorite aspects of the DOOM franchise, this game continues that trend.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am not the biggest fan of shoot'em all without discrimination type of FPS but I was extremely impressed by this game. Graphics and sounds are fantastic. Story line is good too, although Doom is not played for it's story line. What I love the most about it is how engaging this game was. The worlds / maps are well done. It is definitely longer than most of the games I have played lately - you get your moneys worth. The game introduces enemies, weapons, upgrades, rewards etc at a very good pace. Weapons.. man they get so big, some take literally the lower third of the screen. Kudos to that. A nice trip on the memory lane, to the times when I was playing the first "id" games. One last thing, the music is the best I have ever notices in a FPS. While in some hairy battles with demons around you the music switches to aggressive speed metal and a voice choral. Brilliant. You just want to crank it high up. A really good game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA big Meh.
I'd recommend this game on a good sale only, the gameplay is good enough with some fun exploration, but I honestly expected much more from this by how many people praised it as basically the machine gun messiah (which is a good song to listen to while you turn down the ingame music).
The fights are rather boring, because enemies are pretty stupid and can usually be outmaneuvered without much thought, while also being extremely bullet spongy. The weapons you get seem at first glance extremely overpowered but combine that with the sheer stamina of the demons and they just become clunky. The weapons that actually do pack a punch are so limited even on max ammo that you won't kill much more than a small handful of demons.
Here we have another problem, some encounters, especially in the first half of the game, are so devoid of enemy numbers that you don't even want to waste your seemingly precious ammo, while in later encounters there are many demons that keep trickling in in small groups which makes you use your weaker weapons for most of them because you run out of ammo on the big guns.

I have to say that despite my complaints I found the combat decently entertaining, what I listed is what's keeping this game from having great combat. I also quite liked the exploratory part with some very nice level design, which is very atmospheric.

Last, I want to express a bugbear that I have with the music in the game. It is bland as hell (which ironically isn't bland in the game). The music is mostly for people that don't listen to a lot of metal on their own, and while having great production value, is more akin to a generic pop song than something really unique or impressive.\t\t\t\t\t

Game looks beautiful on max settings and I am getting a solid 144fps with no drops. The gun play is quick and gory. This feels like Doom! The game isn't just point A to point B, there is some exploration with secrets scattered across the map. Field drones allow your weapons to be modified that change how the weapon is used. Gives options for different play styles. I can't find a negative thing to really say about this game. Some would mention the story here, but let's be honest with ourselves here....we play Doom(at least those of us who grew up in the Doom 95 era) for the guns, monsters, and gore. There story is just kinda there to take you to the next map...AND I AM OK WITH THAT. The story is average at best, but it doesn't really matter. Yes worth a buy!\t\t\t\t\t
