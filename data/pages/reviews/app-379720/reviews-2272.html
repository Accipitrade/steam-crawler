\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIT DOESN'T GET ANY BETTER THAN THIS!!!!!!!!
After three (maybe more, maybe less) years without a PC, I finally purchased this game! It is everything it's hyped up to be, and is a surprisingly relaxing game. Difficulty settings feel right (if you've never played an FPS, this isn;t a bad one to start on.) Controllers work very well (I use one myself), and keyboard and mouse is supported.
Haven't tried multiplayer yet but the snapmaps are really cool and fun. Completionists will feel right at home with TONS of collectables and secrets.
Only real downside is the relatively long load times on my mid-grade gaming PC, but nothing unbearable. Only noticed one bug where it took enemies about 40 seconds to spawn in, instead of instantly like the normally do. DOOM ETERNAL is coming out this year (fingers crossed...), this is a great way to get into the series.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOriginal Doom was the first game I played on my first PC back in the days and I loved the game, as well as the sequels and the follow up games of ID Software (Heretic / Quake, … etc).



It took me a few years before I finally bought this game (I like to play games when they are polished after a few patches), simply because it was Doom and I knew I had to have it either way, but it exceeded all my expectations by a significant margin. It is a masterpiece, totally lives up to its name and fully respects the original game ; it is just simply enjoyable without being too serious or being overly difficult and the achievements are all pretty doable (I gave up at 90% because I didn’t want to grind Multiplayer for too long, but I surely could have reached the last 10% by investing 10 more hours).



For those of you who have played through the first two Dooms (and the various spin offs, like Final Doom), you will love this game. Everything in the game has been faithfully recreated, from the weapon textures, monster attacks, to the relative difficulty of the game. All the gore, all the vulgarity, all the stupidly over the top violence is there. The only real difference is how they altered the gameplay to be comparable with modern gaming. It's DOOM, from the 90s, brought back better than ever.


In the old days, there was no regenerating health. You didn't need to reload your weapons. There was no plot line in games like Doom. You were a space marine and you killed everything in sight. All this has been updated with the times - Health does not regenerate; there are health packs, but you get health from killing monsters (and even more by using "glory" kills or by ripping them apart with the chainsaw). Weapons function like they did in the old days, except they are all modable - but you'll actually have to be careful with using ammo, as you'll frequently run out and will have to end up using your chainsaw to get more. The plot line, while not unique, does exist this time around; It has to do with limited amounts of energy on Mars...and it's basically the stereotypical bad cooperation that is being destroyed by a single person. However Doom was never meant to have a story, and they did this one well.



Doom is without question one of the most fun and satisfying shooters I've played in years. It's fast and frenetic gunplay against hordes of demons and monsters that's honed to a science, harkening back to the idea of relying on speed, skill, and a ton of powerful guns and items to get you through combat instead of cover and exploiting rock stupid whack-a-mole enemies. It eschews the idea of waiting for an opportunity to pop off a couple shots and slowly work your way through levels by literally telling you in the first five seconds to rip and tear until it is done, and it's as viscerally satisfying as it should be. It honestly feels like the perfect blend of classic Doom sensibilities given a modern retooling, where it takes what it needs from its roots for most of the core gameplay ideas and then adds onto it with more recent developments in gaming like character progression and customization. And it all works and feeds into itself remarkably well.


There are plenty of easter eggs, difficulty settings (even a hardcore mode), online game modes (with the ability to play custom maps!), and time trials for you to gain extra powerups (the power-up system has seen a modern remake as well).



Multiplayer is also well done, with a leveling system up to Level 500 (Echelon 10 / Level 50) and a multitude of customization options which are slowly unlocked by leveling ; this gives us a continuous motivation to play, even for bad players (like me).



In short, this is a masterpiece and I look forward to its sequel, Doom Eternal, later this year.\t\t\t\t\t
