It's a must have game if it's on sale, you woun't regret it at all. Just buy it.

Campaign

It's awesome, the ammount of progression awards you get is unbelievable. Every mission is totally different from the other atmopherewise, each one has so many secrets and unlockables to play around with. If you try to play the campaign without rushing the missions, but by collecting all secrets and finding all collectibles, it will take a lot more than 1 hour to finish each of missions.
It kinda gets repetitive as all you are doing is running and gunning demons room to room, but the complexitity on the level design, atmosphere, demon type, AI actions, all the explorability, unlocks and customization keeps it interesting to play.

9.5/10

Multiplayer

It's honestly one of the best multiplayers I have ever played! There are SO MANY UNLOCKS from taunts, armors pieces, colours and patterns and the way you level up being 1 or 2 for each game, keeps you getting new items each game until you eventually get to level 50 prestige 11, which will take you plenty of time.
It's fun, modes are awesome and new from other games, too bad you'll end up using the same meta weapons as everyone else as the combat shotgun is so beast, but besides that you can really have a lot of fun here.
So far on my 8hours+ on the multiplayer I have only seen 1 cheater.

9/10 (no autobalance, players can't move teams, even when it's a 5v2 or 4v1)

Snapmap

There is so much replayability here you woun't end up without having maps to play on here. It's really fun, you can be extremely creative and this honestly is the most time consuming element of the game. Creating a good well designed map with over 5 objectives takes me over 6 hours and that is really short considering how easy it is to chain events.
You must watch some tutorials first, but it's easy getting used to.

10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen RAGE was released, it was considered a major disappointment from a company who was known for impressive FPS experiences for the time. Even compared to the divisive DOOM 3, RAGE was just a shell of the company's previous efforts. Thus, it was easy to see how fans felt betrayed and didn't see much coming from the company in the future. However, once the gameplay for the new DOOM game was released, those feelings changed.

The 2016 version of DOOM, while not exactly like the original game, brings fans back to what they loved about the series. That's the fantastic amount of violence, lots of demons, and fast and frantic gameplay. Indeed, DOOM comes at a time when big-budgeted FPS experiences tend to focus on more slow-paced, linear gameplay. DOOM, while linear at some points, is entirely fast and has levels that vary in their layouts. The game works differently than the traditional DOOM games. It works more akin to Serious Sam, where you're stuck in a room and are forced to fight a horde of enemies of varying degree. That's probably my biggest gripe of this reboot. Once you get adjusted to the formula of the game, it becomes a bit tedious and boring at some points. This is especially true if you're playing on the easier difficulties, but it should be an exciting experience if you're playing on the harder difficulties.

However, the gameplay still feels fine and the gunplay is fantastic. I think the Super Shotgun rivals the original Super Shotgun in DOOM II in some areas. The best part of the game is the glory kills. The various ways you can brutally destroy those demons is amazing. It offers a lot of experimentation. It's not just for show, however. As you play on the harder difficulties, it becomes a necessity, as the glory kills result in the player earning health for their efforts. This can make a big difference in how the player traverses the arena. It gets to the point that you don't even realize you're doing it after awhile. It works quite well.

To vary things up, you're encouraged to scavenge the level to earn upgrade points for your armor and weapons. This includes finding secrets and accomplishing tasks, such as killing enemies in a certain way. It really helps with upping the replay value. Not only that, you can find areas that mimic parts of the original DOOM and DOOM II levels, and if you find these, you can play the original levels utilizing the gameplay of the new DOOM game. It's a neat bonus for those (Like me) who like to break the boundaries.

Other than that, you have the multiplayer, which is nothing to write home about. It's the gameplay of DOOM, but compromised. You have a two weapon limit, and the speed doesn't feel as fast as the campaign. I guess this is to help with skill boundaries on multiplayer, but still, it's a disappointment considering the legacy of DOOM's multiplayer. SnapMap is a good bonus, and the community offers up a great selection of unique mods for the system. It's a shame it's not going to be in DOOM Eternal, but I guess this is so the team can focus their efforts on what they want people to play the most in that game.

Overall, the 2016 version of DOOM is a great return to what the series is known for, and it's still a blast to play to this day. It can be a little repetitive as the game goes on, but the gunplay and secrets make the replay value just that high. I recommend this to anyone who is a big FPS fan and wants a great challenge.\t\t\t\t\t
