\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery fun gameplay, and very challenging when it comes to playing the highest difficulty.


The Father of FPS returns back with it's super fast action from the 90's, but also along with a few common features we gamers see now in days with the FPS games of today, such as:

weapon attachments,
weapon upgrades,
suit upgrades,
ammo upgrade and health/armor upgrade.

I can understand that fans say that this DOOM kinda does kill the traditional gameplay of Doom 1&2 or if this wasn't a Doom game, especially when the areas lockdown in every level in DOOM when I engage in a arena fight, but I think that's the whole point of the game. The developers wanted the players to have a speedy/brutal battle, countering and evading your opponents in numerous ways by constantly moving around, using the environment to your advantage, only to find yourself on top of all the dead corpses killed by you. Heck - "Kill them all." :P

Honestly, I love the game's battle system, otherwise the whole weapon attachments wouldn't be fun to experiment with and would be in vain to even use one of them, because we'll all just be speed running through all the levels again like before. The weapons are very devastating, makes me feel like a killing machine, and the Glory Kills are just... SATISFYING, refreshening for my mind, only to see more ways of how to Glory Kill a Demon! I'm very pleased with the action in this game, how they added an Arcade Feature, and I'm happy how they added a handful of Classic Doom Levels to play, hearing those good old 8-bit tunes (but they need to be found first in the story levels! :P )


The plot of the story was generic to me, because I already knew what was going to happen in the end to the DoomGuy, but it's not bad either. I mean, I had a blast of exploring the levels, finding secrets, and finding logs to read about the UAC Facilities, the Demons, even about the epic Doom Slayer - DoomGuy. Oh yeah... I need a suit and a shotgun like DoomGuy.

All in all,

8 out of 10 for the story.
10 out of 10 for the action.
