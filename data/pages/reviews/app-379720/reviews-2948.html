\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBadass. From start to finish, there's no other word for this game.

No game in recent memory provides the same level of in-your-face, break-neck-pace, blood-and-gore action as DOOM. Gameplay is the crown jewel of this game. Polished to a mirror shine, the movement and combat are flawless. The design of the areas and enemies you face force you to constantly be running, jumping, and shooting. Hunting down your enemies with brutality and reckless abandon is made all the more gratifying by the glory kill system. Stunning a demon with a shotgun blast, then beating them to death with their own arm is a thing of beauty.

The upgrade system is well rounded in that, although you may have a particular love or affinity for a certain gun, it won't be the only one you use throughout the game. You'll constantly be learning each enemy-type's weaknesses and how to exploit them with the brutal efficiency of your full arsenal. The system is a well thought out part of the game, providing you with incentive for exploration, but not crippling you should you choose to forgo it in favor of a quicker-paced experience.

This game also has a lot of replay value. Playing through the difficulties is a worthy challenge. I, myself, am on my 3rd playthrough of the campaign, currently at the VEGA facility on Nightmare difficulty. My previous playthrough, I contented myself with a perfectionist run, finding every secret and completing every challenge. Reaching the end with 100% completion was a rare and fulfilling experience. I've never found myself doing that with a game. Most collectible systems in games I've played seemed asinine or the thought of going back to find them all elicited feelings of boredom and disinterest. Thankfully, DOOM's savage gameplay kept me entertained well through my 2 previous playthroughs. Now, on Nightmare difficulty, it's the unrelenting mercilessness of each room and wave of opponents that holds my competitive interest.

All in all, I went in to this game unsure what to expect, never having played any of the previous entries in the series. What I found was an instant favorite. This game is one of the few in my library that will remain installed on my system as others come and go. If I'm having a bad day, stressed out, or just feel like blasting some demons to bloody pulp, I know DOOM will be there to fulfill that desire.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is easily the best single-player I've ever played in an FPS.

The movement is fast, but responsive. The combat is brutal, and absolutely satisfying. The maps are very well-designed and are filled with secret areas that, alongside having multiple difficulties, give DOOM a huge amount of replay value.

This isn't your typical "cover > aim > shoot" single player that plagues the typical modern shooter. No sir, you gotta get in the demons' faces and tear them limb from limb, skillfully dodging (or tanking) any attack they throw at you. These demons don't around -- the AI will certainly catch you off guard multiple times, particularly later in the game.

The music makes you feel like the most badass demon-slaying being in existence.

Play it play it play it.

NOTE: Despite having nearly 20 hours in this game, I've spent none of that time in the multiplayer. So yeah I can't really say much there. From what I've heard, the multiplayer is far from the highlight of the game. Get it for the single player.\t\t\t\t\t
