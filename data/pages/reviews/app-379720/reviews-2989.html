Phenomenal Campaign


People have been praising DOOM since it released and I'm no exception; DOOM has been the most fun I have had with a shooter in several years. This is a surprise given that most of the pre-release marketing I watched was for the multiplayer which left a poor first impression and made me want to give this game a pass.

But damn was I wrong. DOOM has somehow managed to blend classic arena shooter conventions like constant movement, unrestricted inventory, and labyrinthian maps with more modern shooter conventions like mantling, and character upgrades so as to not alienate more modern players. Aside from a couple pacing issues involving cutscenes the action never lets up. Which is DOOMs biggest strength, it knows how ridiculous it is and plays it straight. You're the Doomslayer and you murder demons like its nobodies business and have neither the time nor the patience for anything else. This unfortuntely makes the cutscenes which break of pace even more jarring, but other than these small issues DOOM never lets up ushering you from one arena to another.

DOOM boasts many pieces that makes it stand out. The environment which ranges from the sprawling high tech facility of the UAC, to the exposed wastes of an inhospitable planet, and the otherworldy holds of hell. Weapons all feel satisfying to use as one would expect of a AAA shooter, each managing to feel unique with a weighty response fitting of the hardware you utilize. Upgrades for weapons also help further divesify weapons with most providing interesting tools to fuel your conquest of the demons, with only 1 or 2 feeling lackluster or straight up inferior to their counterpart (here's looking at your heavy assault rifle scope.) Enemy design is similarly fantastic, with the various enemies covering a good range of threats that each have a different way to approach. From the fodder of the possessed to the more specialized barons of hell, hell knights and summoners. Nothing here feels cheap or unfair any hitscan weapons having a telegraph or fitting the style of the enemy in question (possessed soldiers.)

Finally the gameplay itself where aggression is the name of the game. Nothing is more satisfying than getting a good run in on an arena strafing around enemy attacks making uses of glory kills and juggling weapons to take advantage of the enemies you encounter. Slowing down tends to be a recipe for taking damage that could be better avoided by never stopping, utilizing the mantle to clamber up ledges, so long as you do not slow down. Demons respond to your weapons staggering when appropriate giving weight to your weapons. Finally the soundtrack which is djent plain an simple helps make DOOM an experience that can be descirbed as distilled adrenaline.\t\t\t\t\t
