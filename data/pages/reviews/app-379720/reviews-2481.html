\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA true successor to the classics in almost every way. The campaign is pure violence and speed, while leaving the plot deceptively simple. You hate demons. Kill all the demons. Stop the invasion. Get guns, kill demons, get bigger guns, kill bigger demons, GO TO HELL, kill the biggest demon with the BIGGEST ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ GUN.

But there's way more going on here if you go out of your way to find it....

The multiplayer is also SERIOUSLY underrated. Each player gets a two-weapon loadout plus an Equipment item. It's all about "weapon swapping", and mechanically speaking works a lot like a Mortal Kombat move - a series of properly timed button presses can absolutely destroy a player in a split second, possibly even groups of them if you know what to do. But plenty of players know how to do the same to you. It had its flaws at launch and the less-than-familiar Arena Shooter newbs that reviewed it didn't play more than two games to make a proper judgement, but it's a blast once you get the hang of it.

I paid full price for the base game and all its DLC about 2 weeks after launch (that's $100, btw) and I absolutely think it was worth it. Now that it's cheaper, it's an even better bang for your buck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this is my favorite single player game now... It didn’t take a super long time to beat, but it was intense! I feel exhausted from how focused I was, and how hard I was gripping my controller. X_X

The gameplay was incredible. It’s been a decade since I’ve played a shooter that had controls as satisfying as this. (I think mainly because there are so many controller layout options, I was able to pick one that fit me perfectly)

The graphics are impeccable and seem very optimized. I never had any frame rate issues.

The story is interesting but not so up its own ass that it sucks. The character could care less about the story most of the time it seems though, which I love! It’s like he and the player are always on the same page. I found that even though the story could be considered optional, it still made me interested to learn more about what was going on. It’s not forced down your throat like every other game.

Sound/music is amazing and equal parts awesome and creepy. I love the heavy metal sounding spin that the tracks have, how it syncs up with your glorykills, and the scary sounds the monsters make.

The level design is very good; I only got lost once, which I actually enjoyed since I had to search for the correct switch, and exploring the different maps was fun! Especially when exploring rewarded me with the doom guy figures lol!

Overall I would say this game is near perfect in my opinion. Even the bugs have a certain charm that makes you appreciate the love that went into making this game. I was in an intense fight and managed to get stuck inside a box after I fell through the top of it. It was empty but even the insides were very detailed. I appreciate stuff like that, and it’s just one more thing that makes me love this game.\t\t\t\t\t
