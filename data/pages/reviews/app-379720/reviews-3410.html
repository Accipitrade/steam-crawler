\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been waiting to play this game for such a long time. I've played quite a bit of it and I'm already Knee Deep in the Dead and I'm ready to share my thoughts on it.

Summary

Doom (stylized as DOOM) is a reboot of the original games and mixes elements of Doom 1, 2 and 3. You wake up from a tomb and upon waking up, you discover that hordes of demons have invaded Mars and a UAC Scientist (I will not name that person because I want to avoid spoiling the game for people) had enabled Hell's invasion. You get your suit and you rip and tear through hordes of demons.

Now it can be said that this game takes some inspiration from Brutal Doom, but they did their executions in a fast and fluid way and they did it using a new melee style called "Glory Killls". To Glory Kill, you damage a demon to the point where they are stunned and when they are stunned, they start lighting up red and then when you get close enough to glory kill, they light up blue. You press the melee button and you perform a brutal finisher on them and that rewards you by dropping health and ammo from it's corpse.

When fighting demons, you need to constantly keep moving because if you stop, you are dead. A lot of naysayers have kept saying that the game is slow but on the contrary, the game does introduce fast gameplay and there are even upgrades in the Campaign that allow you to speed your character up to some degree and in Snapmap, you can change the speed of players so you can create that classic Doom deathmatch that you've been dreaming about. There are a variety of enemies and bosses to fight in the game ranging from the simple Possessed and The Unwilling to the giant Cyberdemon. The UAC Facility and Mars itself is dark and grimy and the style of these two environments really fit the atmosphere of the game. Let's not forget that Hell itself is also present in this game. The homeworld of the demons looks really terrifying and it gives you a feeling of uneasiness when you are there and the style of Hell and the architecture that is present does fit the style of the game and it let's you know that you are in demon territory and that you should keep your back watched at all times.

The game rewards you for looking around levels for secrets and upgrades because they can greatly benefit you as they make your marine stronger, it upgrades your weapons and by finding and completing rune trials, they change the gameplay up.

I've only played the multiplayer through Doom's beta so I may be missing out on some details, but I believe that it is decent. While I do agree that the player models do look generic and the game somewhat copies from COD's model (loadouts, taunts), I believe the multiplayer still has some outstanding gameplay. There are powerups and power weapons scattered throughout the map and you have to get them before the enemy team does, but the powerup that stands out to me the most is the Demon Rune. When you pick up a demon rune, you basically get turned into a demon (The Revenant, Baron of Hell or the Mancubus), and for a limited amount of time, you get to use that demon's ability against other players or the enemy team and you get a boost in health when you do become a demon. There are a variety of gamemodes that you can play so you constantly have a variety of different experiences in multiplayer.

Snapmap however was the very reason that enticed me to purchase Doom. I have used it myself and you can build a fully functional map and gamemode within five or ten minutes. You can place a variety of objects down and you can place down demons throughout the map (excluding the bosses), and you can use modules such as the AI Conductor and you can change your atmosphere of your own rooms to spice your map up. However, I do feel that Snapmap is missing some features as you can only use setpieces from the UAC Base to create your map. What if I wanted to create a map set in Hell? What if I wanted to create a deathmatch experience where you can use a portal to travel between the UAC Base and Hell? I know Bethesda did say that they would keep updating Snapmap after the game's release but I couldn't help but bring that up. Overall, Snapmap is a pretty good feature and I'm sure that the game will still be played as you can create different experiences with Snapmap.

Pros

+No Microtransactions.
+A variety of enemies to fight.
+The game's very rewarding when you explore levels.
+Snapmap is very flexible, creates a variety of Doom experiences.
+A variety of gamemodes.
+The game is fast and fluid.
+The upgrades are pretty nifty.

Cons
-Snapmap is limited to Mars.
-The multiplayer kind of copies from COD's model.
-The Marines in multiplayer look generic.
-Notice some performance stuttering in some areas of the game.

Conclusion

Wow! You really nailed Doom on the head ID and Bethesda. I'm really impressed. As a long time Doom fan, I'm going to say this game â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥ing rocks! While Doom may have some of it's flaws, I will still highly recommend this. If you want to get away from the generic first person experience like Call of Duty and you have a lot of money (cause this is very expensive), you should definitely pick this up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou know a game does an exceedingly good job of being "atmospheric" when the environment alone takes away your will to play it. I've descended into hell in the game, and honestly its terribly gloomy and creepy, not that I imagined it would be pleasant. The campaign has little plot but thats probably already obvious and I dont know if anyone was even really expecting one, at least aside from fighting like hell.

After playing through this for about 20 hours or so, Ive come to a point where I dont necessarily regret my purchase, but I am not thrilled with the game. With that said, Im an RPG fan and usually enjoy games with depth or a good story, I just bought this because of the legacy of Doom and that whole new appeal games have when they first release. The gameplay is shallow; at least if your expecting or looking for something meaningful or whatnot. This is a run and gun shooter at its core, and the game engine and gameplay succeed with that, not many games nowadays can successfully make a fun run and gunner.

The graphics and performance are good, Im on a gaming laptop I purchased a year ago (a bit aging hardware) and I can play on medium HD res. at around 30+ FPS. Looks great, runs great. Multiplayer I will not be commenting on as I do not play competetive online games, and have yet to even touch it. From what Ive heard many are dissapointed but to me I could care less if it was amazing or not, either way I wouldnt play it.

Snapmap is... Dissapointing for me. I was expecting a Far Cry-like map editor which was unrealistic because this is a shooter that takes place in corridors hallways and cramped internal structures. Even in the more open area of Hell it still is compact and feels cramped. So sprawling maps arent exactly doable in a game like this. But for a dungeon crawler type shooter, the Snapmap is great, lots of corridors and rooms. Its basically just snapping rooms together then populating the maps with enemies and guns, and there are some unique gameplay modifiers that make powerful options at open ended gameplay, ive seen ridiculous stuff like raccoon simulator maps and farming/settlement type gameplay maps, where you feed your demons and upgrade your equipment.

Overall if you arent expecting more than non stop hellish action, then great buy it. At $60 it can be hard for it to feel totally worth the cash when compared to other games released recently, at least if your not a huge FPS fan. If your an RPG fan like me who has played/enjoyed Fallout 4, Witcher 3 etc, then this will not feel complete. Its impossible to compare this to any of those I mentioned but obviously these type games arent my favorite, but even then its still fun and I decided to play past the original 2 hours and pass on a refund.\t\t\t\t\t
