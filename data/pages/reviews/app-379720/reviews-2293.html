\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Demons run when a good man goes to war."

There are not many games which strike me with pure awe and joy. Most which do are grand-scale simulators or masterful sandboxes. DOOM, however, gives me a sense of power and glory I have never felt before.

In most games, demons are powerful deities aiming to terrorize you and everyone you love. Not in DOOM; in a sense, DOOM is a classic survival-horror, but with a twist:

You are the horror.

You play as DOOMGUY; the Doom Slayer; the boogieman of every demon you shall ever face. From the mindless cannon-fodder to powerful titans of ages long forgotten, the realm of Hell trembles with your every step. Every bullet you fire, every skull you crush, sends fear down the spines of the corrupted and diabolical spewing from the ever-encompassing wasteland. The demons may show aggression, may rally their brethren to fight, but the look on their face is nothing short of pure fear.

This fear is not ill-founded. You are no god, but a man. The demons could swarm you with ease, gut you like a fish. Or, you could be crushed by the faceless Knights, or the abominable Barons. However, they do not. They may wield the forbidden knowledge and powers of Hell, but they fear you still. You have no magics, no power, no hidden knowledge; your strength swells from nothing but the gun in your hands, and rage quelled only by the blood of the unholy.

Feel the fear of the demons as you saw their lords in half. Smell the bloodstained walls of eternal damnation, and live to tell about it. Feel the kick of lead and steel, and fire to the beat of a glorious soundtrack. Buy this game, and never look back.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can say plenty of good things about this game. It's a lot of fun in singleplayer and multiplayer, provides a decent challenge, isn't glitchy at all, and has a great soundtrack. It's also extremely well optimized. Lower end machines can run this game surprisingly well, as long as you are willing to adjust the settings accordingly. And if you have an AMD Vega graphics card or any Nvidia RTX graphics card, this game should rarely ever drop below 200 frames per second. It even runs great on Linux if you play it through Steamplay, Valve's way of getting Windows PC games on Linux. Doom 2016 is a great example of how well games should be made, and I can't wait to see how Doom Eternal improves upon what this game has done. Anyone who likes shooting games should try this game.
