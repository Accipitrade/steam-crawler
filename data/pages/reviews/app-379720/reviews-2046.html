\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a game that takes the concept of guided gameplay and exposition... and thoroughly destroys it.
When you begin the game, you immediately you ain't f-ing around. You kick demon ass harder than ever before. Challenging fast-pace gameplay, with insanely well designed music, graphics, and background. With its story being a secondary aspect you can delve into at your own will, the shootin' is never interrupted, and if it is; its probably to quickly show you the atrocity you are about to annihilate. DOOM also has a well made progression, with difficulties ranging from the introductory "I'm too young to die" for those new to fast pace shooter, all the way up to the extreme "Ultra Nightmare", where in the blink of an eye you can die a horrible death. The arcade mode allows you to challenge yourself to beat scores and improve at the game at your own pace, and the Snapmap feature lets you utilize the vastly complex range of environments and enemies the game has to offer, to create your own level. The multiplayer, while DOOM's weak point, is still a fair bit of fun, with clever map design and original gamemodes. In conclusion, DOOM (2016) is a game that truly nails the aspect of giving the player complete and total control, with the music even changing with your actions, creating an ever-driving motivation to kick demon ass like there is no tomorrow. The potential this game had to ruin the Doom franchise was immense, as many assumed it would be a mere cashgrab on nostalgia; but DOOM is indeed the opposite, a remake of everything that made the original great, and beyond. I give DOOM (2016) a 9.8/10 .\t\t\t\t\t
