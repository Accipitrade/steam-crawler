\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTook me a while to get around to playing it, and I'd never completed the original DOOM, or played it much. I had two playthroughs, on my first, I kept getting stuck, like all the time, I couldn't work out which door to go to or where panels were and how to navigate around, nightmare of a game so I put it down. But recently restarted, remembering almost nothing and somehow finding it easier to find bits. There's the occasional platforming bit, or moments where I'm constantly bringing up the map (mostly to find hidden collectables), but an all-round pleasant gaming experience. It does well to make you feel powerful and the guns really carry weight to them when dispatching demons. A thrill ride that's deep in its lore that I didn't pay much attention to, but also filled with little secrets and in-jokes I was vaguely familiar with. I think I completed this in about 10 hours, over a weekend. Fairly straight forward and if you put some time on each level to find all the little power ups and upgrade parts, the game gets a little easier, though I was playing on the Normal difficulty. Only major downsides are the platforming, as I was constantly overshooting a jump or falling off the edge of the map when running and gunning, and this set me back to the previous checkpoints. Similar too when doing the 'Glory Kills' on weakened enemies, I would sometimes glitch through the floor and fall into the void, and have to restart checkpoints again. Overall I'd recommend the game, as it really performs well and is a nice easy game to get on with as its very much a classic video game. The story unravels nicely, there's occasional moments of intrigue but it's not the core gameplay - which is nice, I'm not sure many people play this for the story, but its a nice extra. Not played the sequel DOOM remake yet, but it has made me want to give the original DOOM an actual attempt now.\t\t\t\t\t
