\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: get - it it's rather good. Easily recommended.


-Review from a big fan of the original-

Had to stop myself from playing now. They called the game Doom rather than Doom 4 and it's like returning to its roots. It has more color and stuff und unlocks and fancy bits and animated mojo but the DNA is there right in the sprawling levels, the pumping industrial metal soundtrack, the sudden death when falling from an unsafe ledge or casually touching a moving container.
Yeah there still are cutscenes and talk and lore but it fits neatly in and adds to the game. This is no slowmo fighting like it was feared when the finishers were shown, game plays as fast as the original nowhere near the bore of Doom 3 with its linear corridors.
There is much fan service regarding the original: hidden playable levels with original textures, revamped soundtrack -if you listen closely the title track resembles the original also some of the others, the skull switches, the collectables which play the E1M1-tune and more easter eggs.

Good stuff:
Level design, you even need the map, also not too much hand-holding. Feels much like the original.
Secrets are really secrets even though they show if you found the area map.
Story and characters are sound and fitting to a Doom setting.
Fast action, died 20 times in 3 hours, 5 times to a moving container in a row.
Good classic weapons each with two selectable right-mouse-button mods to diversify the loadout.

Negatives (minor):
-Platforming but not as bad as it sound. Ledge-grabbing is also automated.
-Instadeaths along with checkpoint system
-Checkpoints, however fighting is fun so I don't mind redoing a quick battle if I fall down somewhere.


I'd give the single-player a 92-95% rating, then add the multiplayer, custom maps etc.

No problems technically (3 year old gaming PC).

Edit: several crash-to-desktops, falling through the geometry (found a secret there for consolation) and hang-up in the menu (press esc to exit not working) so if that's a problem wait until they fix it. (GTX 960 with latest driver)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recall sitting in a dark theater, Dec 19, 2015 as I anxiously (and somewhat skeptically) awaited the moment to see if someone, anyone, could actually do justice to an american cultural legacy ... and J.J. did not disappoint and Star Wars was a hit. Doom is to the gaming industry what Star Wars was to the film industry. Could it be, would it be, that lightning would strike twice... could it be, would it be that someone, somewhere would remake the beloved "first real FPS", the defining game that launched a genre', the breakthrough gaming experience of yesteryear and actually make it good once again? YES, yes, and YES! EDIT: The more I play it the more I just really like the game play, the weapons, everything, very well done id/Bethesda, well done!

After coming away so disappointed with the multiplayer beta, I was not very eager until I saw initial feedback. So I whipped out the old PayPal and took the plunge. I am certianly glad I did. Bethesda/id did a great job of capturing most all elements that made Doom ... well, Doom and made Doom!

+ Fast pace
+ Loads of demons
+ Demon crunching weapons
+ Even some music is brough back into the mix
+ New elements like the oddly gratifying Glory Kills bring new joy to an old gamer's heart
+ Graphically exquisite
+ Runs great on mid-range HW (though have read problems with AMD HW, and trying out on my AMD machine I can see why -- best experience is with Intel/Nvidia rig no doubt)
+ Simple in approach, for the most part. Easy to configure my keyboard and do everyting from the keypad.
+/- Story line is weak, but of course, there is really not much story line in Doom other than you are a space marine out to kill an invading army of demons on Mars. EDIT: As I progressed a llittle further, there is a good plot developing. EDIT 2: I moved this from neutral to a positive, in fact, it is the meager story line that makes this Doom even more Doom. The original Doom had just one paragraph of story written into it's intruction text file and that was all. This Doom has a small back story as you progress but never gets in the way of the action which is a refreshingly great feature of this game as too many FPS games today are so marred in story that it spends more time telling it than actually getting into the action.
++ The Glory Kill concept is just spectacular, I have seen some other games attempt to do similar actions, but this time Doom really nails it, it is truly a great added experience IMHO and I hope future games incorporate and build upon this concept. Glory kills are just superbly implemented.

+/- Snap map is cool though limited in my opinion, need to wait and see on this. No Glory Kill in snap map, sad -- that is missing for certain -- EDIT: Discovered the key mapping for SnapMap is NOT all the same compared to Campaign, Bug? Not sure, but default keys for Glory Kill are different between the two modes.

+/- have not played enough to really comment on level design, so far so good. Levels so far are getting redundant, meaning textures and objects feel replicated, design is good in places, takes time to really explore.

- Multiplayer is lacking, if you did not like the open beta (as I did not) then you will not like it here most likely.

There is only one word for this game: Masterpiece!\t\t\t\t\t
