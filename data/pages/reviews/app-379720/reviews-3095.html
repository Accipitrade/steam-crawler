\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played the campaign twice in this game, once on Ultra Violence and once on Nightmare. It is certainly a challenging FPS shooter, as it differs greatly from generic shooting games.

I found this refreshing, and hard to adjust to at the same time. You never have to reload, which seems less realistic, but thats not what this game is about. Movement is key, and defensive strategies are non existant. The game is like a harsh but practical father that teaches you painful lessons... if you're dying, fight even more to keep living.

I absolutely loved the combat system in this game... pummeling demons with projectiles until they stumble, then rush up and melee them to death. The glory kill system is very well done. The story line is pretty cool, and I thought the mix of action to story was perfect. I enjoy some meaning to why I'm killing everything, and this was just about right.

So overall, the campaign is a masterpiece and I really REALLY hope another side story or campaign is released via DLC. However, the multiplayer is garbage IMHO. As Honest Trailers puts it, it goes together like skittles and fish. It just feels forced, aggravating, and like a mish mash. Snapmap seems pretty cool, I've played some pretty fun maps there.

Even if just for the campgain, I'd still get this game. Overall, quite fun!

UPDATE: So, changed my mind on the multiplayer. It takes some getting used to, but I've since come to actually enjoy deathmatches and especially their new modes, Posession and Bloodrush!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. In short, if you like fast-paced, high-octane, bloody violence, buy this game. And the soundtrack. I love the soundtrack.

I found the gameplay very enjoyable. The guns have a very solid feel to them and were interesting throughout the game. The pace is kept up beatifully. This is primarily due to the absence of any need to reload, dodgeable enemy projectiles, and glory kills. These all reward highly mobile and active playstyles.

Further contributing to the high-octane atmosphere is the excellent soundtrack. It gets me pumped for every engagement.

The pacing is also noteworthy. While the meat of the game is certainly combat -nothing wrong with that- its broken up by small platmforming sections, challeneges (technically combat I guess), and, rarely, story.

As for the story, it's pretty easy to follow, and there's probably more to it once I read the codex entries, but my first playthrough was all about action, and that was what I got.

As far as graphics and performance go, my computer is on its last legs and will be gone soon, so read this with that in mind. While on higher settings, there was lag, it was still very smooth. No choppy frames, so I'll count that as a plus. Loading times are a nightmare, but the environments are large and once they load they're great. Because of my limited capacity, I can't comment on the game's graphical fidelity.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first saw DOOM at E3 2015 I wasn't too hyped about the game. I knew that DOOM had kicked off the FPS genre and all three core DOOM games were great, but I just wasn't very excited. That is until I saw more of the campaign and the single player live-stream, then I boarded the hype train.

They say hype can ruin a game but DOOM was well deserving of the hype it got. The campaign is fast and brutal with epic guns and tough enemies. I don't have a monster PC by any means but it ran well. Sure I was running at the lowest settings possible and even then I had framerate drops, but the game still looks amazing. The music in DOOM is absolutly fantastic. It has a nice blend of electro and heavy rock. I'm not a fan of rock music but even I found the music to be great!

The best feature of DOOM is a new graphics API called Vulkan. Compared to OpenGL 4.5, Vulkan runs much quicker. Combine this with a high end GPU and you will have non-stop action!

My main problem isn't with the game itself but more or less the demo. DOOM's demo dosn't include Vulkan so people with lower-end hardware maybe turned away if the game isn't running smoothly.

Overall DOOM is a must buy! The combat is fast paced and the Glory Kills are just beautiful, the graphics are outstanding even on low-end hardware and the icing on the cake is that it IS just DOOM. There's no reloading, you always move at full speed and ammo is as common as flies. If you're a bit sceptical try DOOM's demo. It lets you play the first mission of DOOM.
I highly recommend this game (Just be careful who you're playing it around :P)\t\t\t\t\t
