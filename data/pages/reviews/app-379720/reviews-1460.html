\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fun. Gameplay is solid with surprising depth. Story and characters are actually entertaining. Soundtrack is fantastic. Feels like a real quality reboot with plenty of respect towards its predecessors while revamping in its entirety.

A minor flaw is its platforming. Jumps feel unforgiving, and edges feel slippery. I've fallen to my death more times than I've been killed by demons. Beware: Lost Souls will stop you cold mid-air, and they love to spawn in jumps.

The major flaw in the game is the secrets system. Some are practically out in the open. Plenty more you can walk right past without noticing. Scouring the map is an exercise in frustration, and the upgrades for sniffing out secrets feel practically mandatory if you're trying to 100% without a guide right next to you. They're a significant source of upgrades, so you really do want to do them. Especially on the harder difficulties where an early weapon or upgrade can make a certain enemy type go from mini-boss territory to a gory boot-print.

Doesn't sound too bad, right? Well, it's a major flaw because of the save file system. You either reload checkpoints or you restart the entire mission over again. The vast majority of levels are designed in a way that only segments of the map are accessible at a time, so if you noticed that you missed a secret before you're about to finish a mission, chances are practically guaranteed that you're not allowed to backtrack to look for them. So you're forced to redo missions if you missed anything, and missions are fairly long. They can take from anywhere from 20 minutes to upwards of 30 to complete. That's a lot of time redoing content if you miss anything even once. While you don't have to start from scratch and maintain all the mechanics you've gained in that save file, it's still a lot of very annoying busywork.

Overall, 9/10, really solid everything except for that save file system.\t\t\t\t\t
