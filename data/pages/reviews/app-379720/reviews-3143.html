\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is the latest instalment of the hit franchise of the hugely popular Doom, which was released in 1993; and was arguably the ‘original’ FPS game. The series had a very popular sequel and reboot titled Doom 3, and even a Blockbuster (I use the term loosely) movie starring the Rock and Karl Urban. Bottom Line Up Front (BLUF), DOOM is a refreshing and fun FPS, resetting the genre back to the golden days of its predecessors, as well as Half-Life and Quake. In a sea of CoD and Battlefield, it is invigorating to have an ‘Old School’ shooter remade right (unlike Duke Nukem).

DOOM has outstanding graphics, with a great balance of shadows and ambient light to get your heart pounding ready for combat after each corner. Unlike Doom 3, the fear factor of this game is minimal. The sound track has more of a ‘hell yeah’ metal feel, which helps to drive your thirst for demon blood as you run through and chainsaw your way to the end. The storytelling is not all that important or original, but the lore and archives found through the game do create an interesting back story to the events prior to and around the game.

The mechanics are similar to most FPS games, with a bit more emphases on ‘run and gun’ rather than ‘camp and wait’. Each weapon has unique properties, with two different upgrade streams to modify a weapon to create havoc on the forces of hell in your own method of choice. There are a large number of weapons to choose from which is fantastic, however this also creates a burden. With no weapon wheel, players are forced to use number keys or Q and Z to swap between their chosen method of destruction which can become difficult when being chased by multiple Hell Barons or Knights.

Much like the campaign, the multiplayer is hardcore and fast. The MP has a levelling premise with no real scaling, which means low level players will be destroyed by the more experienced and higher level players. The MP play style is very similar to the SP but requires a more attentive and focused attitude. There is also a big emphasis on character and weapon visual customisation with plenty of options to make your own unique Doom Marine.

Each level is surprisingly big, with multiple secrets and hidden easter eggs around every corner. A few occasions you revisit areas before and after devastation by the hell forces. At first I thought this to be lazy, but it actually shows how well the developers have adjusted the ambience to give a refreshing feeling to a familiar atmosphere. There are several, step back moments which make you go ‘oh wow’ when surveying the map ahead. There are also plenty of challenges along the way which help hone your skills for the increasing difficulty of the game and test your resolve.

The large level size does tend to get a little annoying for those completionists like myself. As I spend two hours looking through every nook and cranny for a Doom Marine figure on each level, I become bored and move onto a different game. There are a number of difficulties to choose from when starting, Myself choosing to go on with the Ultra-Violent difficulty setting as I set to unlock the extreme difficulty ‘Nightmare’. My experience on this difficulty has been that combat has been tricky but rewarding. There never seems to be enough health and armour drops to maintain 100% HP, which always makes you extra cautious prior to entering an open area.

DOOM is a great break up to modern FPS games. Following the classic and unique style of its predecessor, DOOM is a BFG of fun and frustration. DOOM is a great go between as it doesn’t require much brain power or strategic thought process.

I give DOOM the following ratings;

6/10 Story (Violent fun doesn’t always need a story)
9/10 Gameplay
8/10 Graphics
8/10 Soundtrack
6/10 Multiplayer

8/10 Overall

This game is well suited for those that enjoy the following franchises: Doom, Half-Life, Quake, Borderlands, Wolfenstein, Shadow Warrior, Pain Killer, F.E.A.R.\t\t\t\t\t

Personally, this taught me to be more strategic with ammo, and made it more of a treat anytime I got to use these toys. However, I can understand if this is a turn-off if you're a fan of the original games

Fun, fast, and beautiful

The Verdict
9.5/10 Simply put, I thoroughly enjoyed this DOOM. Having grown up playing the originals in the mid 90's, I wasn't sure what to expect with this release. In fact, I cancelled my original preorder because of how much I disliked the multi-player beta. However, after picking up DOOM during the summer sale, and recently completing the campaign, I now highly recommend it. Never before have I played a game that felt so classic, yet looked so modern. It pays respect to the originals all the while bringing DOOM to 2016.


The Bad/Needs Improvement
  • Multi-player.... boring, graphically weak, and unbalanced (become a demon to win) -DOOM is not a multiplayer game, nor should it be IMHO. DOOM has historically been, and should always remain a single-player campaign based game. Again, my opinion.
  • Demons are (too) silent when being killed. Unlike the original DOOMs, there is little to no satisfying sound produced by demons while they are shot at and dying, with the exception of the Cacodemon moaning once before death. This seems odd to me, against conventional standards, and it takes me out of the game momentarily.
  • Lack of unique level soundtracks. The original DOOMs had amazing soundtracks that differentiated each level. Not so much in this DOOM. Heavy metal music only kicks in during short periods of condensed demon spawning waves.

The Good/The Great/The Fantastic
  • Gameplay. Wow is this DOOM ever fun to play! You are a badass demon slayer and DOOM makes sure you feel like it!
  • Weapons and weapon mods. Fantastic variety, really fun to use, and the mods enhance the uniqueness of each weapon.
  • Graphics. Oh the graphics! :D High amout of detail and fantastic lighting contrast in dark areas. Graphics are simply stunning. Be aware that two texture settings require a graphics card with at least 5 GB of VRAM to max out. With two GTX 770 4 GB cards in SLI configuration, I was able to max out all settings except for those two previously mentioned. With vsync set to "adaptive", I was able to consistantly maintain 60 FPS throughout the entire game on a 1080p 60Hz, triple monitor set up. FYI, I found setting "motion blur" to max actually improved visuals and gameplay! Weird right?
  • Level design. Well designed with lots of room to run around, jump from platform to platform, and take cover. Double jumping to ledges is fun. My favourite feature is the hidden levers.... and secret items and rooms :D
  • Replayability. Unlock the original DOOM levels to experience what made DOOM define the genre in its day. Increase the difficulty and use that BFG baby!
  • Demons. Intelligent and persistant AI. Beautiful character model design and graphically pleasing.
  • Storyline and Codexs. Easier to follow and understand than previous DOOMs. Codexs offer a good amount of background detail about weapons, demons, locations and more. Compliments the storyline very nicely.
  • NO PLANNED SINGLE-PLAYER DLC. I started playing games during a time when you bought a game, the whole game, and nothing but the entire game. Period. You didn't buy an "access pass" that allowed you to add content to the game for more money later on. I highly praise the developer's decision to not release any single-player DLC. This DOOM has everything it needs. It's a fully finished and polished product and that's the way all games should be released.

Final Thoughts
I would like to thank the developers, ID Software and Bethesda, for making this DOOM so damn good. You have delivered a fantastic game to fans of the originals, and I can't stress that enough. This DOOM is relatable for original gamers like myself, and yet so modern at the same time. You all did a great job. Even rolling the credits is such a treat! Thank-you again so much.\t\t\t\t\t
