\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSingle Player:
This game has the insanely fast movement of old Doom and Quake games, power-ups, glowing health and armour pickups, and an unlockable double jump. None of the weapons need to reload! The melee kill system works way better that you would expect, the way the chainsaw is implemented is genious, and the game rarely slows down or stops for exposition, and when it does it's breif, and mostly involves you smashing things, which is your characters sole means of communication. The levels are mazelike, like the old games, but not so much that you will get lost very often or for very long. There are tons of challenges, unlocks, secrets, collectables, and it all works together so perfectly. This game is an incredible achievement. 10/10 Cacodemons.

Multiplayer:
Now that I have finished the single player campaign, which was epic, I have tried the multiplayer, which sucks anus in the gayest way possible, and hardly anyone is playing it anyway so you will be waiting a long time to find a match for a multiplayer experience that no sane person would pay money for.

Snap Map:
The controls are consoletarded, mouse interacts poorly with the menus, and for some reason when playing the snap maps the game limits you to holding only 2 weapons at a time, which is odd since the single player campaign does no such thing, using keys one through eight, plus T for the BFG. It is a shame since this removes alot of the old school feel from the game and decreases the strategic element of switching to different weapons for different situations and opponents. Snap map also contains only one tileset. Also you can't quickly melee-then-glorykill the possesed like you can in the single player, which feels very off putting once youv'e gotten used to it in the single player campaign. Even if you lower their health in the settings there is a long delay after the melee attack before you can glory kill, unlike single player where you can just do it with a quick double tap of the melee button, which feels awesome in the single player, so it's a shame the game seems to be playing by slightly different rules in snap map mode. Makes the game feel alot less responsive and satisfying.

Season Pass:
The season pass is for more multiplayer maps lol. Don't waste your money. Nobody is playing the multiplayer now, they certainly won't be in several months time when they start pumping the DLC out, unless the multiplayer gets a drastic overhall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is touted as encouraging exploration and discouraging halted action. It fails on both items because of some very poor design choices.

Many parts of the map became inaccessible because of a faulty checkpoint system. To get around this I started a new campaign in the second of only three game slots, and enabled god mode to save time, but despite my carefully heeding all warnings that appeared and avoiding progress to my "good" run while in dev mode, doing so marked BOTH saved games as "cheat" games and permanently disabled certain content and achievements. Only way to get around this is apparently to delete my good run, lose all of my progress, and start over from the beginning a third time.

Aside from those two game breaking issues, the in-game menus are an enormous headache. Instead of the promised non-stop action you may find yourself trying to navigate walls of weapon options and useless walls of text.

I am enjoying the glory kills and the otherwise fast pace of the game. But it's not enough to make up for its glitches and infuriating design failures.

I strongly recommend against trying this game until the developers replace the debilitating checkpoint system with a traditional save function, and address the corrupted saves. Until then, if you're a fellow Doom fan, download some megawads you haven't tried and open up Chocolate Doom for a little while.\t\t\t\t\t
