\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't expect to like this game, having tried playing it before several times but losing interest quickly. But, it seems I just needed to be in the right mood for something new because once I got used to the style of gameplay it was a real joy to play through. The one small issue I had at the beginning was not being able to jump around much, but as it happened the next level solved that for me completely. The guns are all fun to use, each one has it's own niche and on Ultra Violence I rarely found myself in a position where I felt like I was forced into using a gun I didn't want to.

I was surprised by the level of progression and customisation, the runes and weapon mods provided both interesting challenges and great rewards that allowed me to enhance my specific playstyle whilst also tempting me into trying new things. I managed to complete the majority of rune/gun related mastery/upgrade challenges without going too out of my way, save for a small number I used cheese strats for after completing everything else.

If you're coming from games like Halo or Call of Duty like me, the one important thing to realise is that your aim is far less important than your ability to move around the map - great precision is rarely important and with time it'll come as you get used to the movement. If you're having trouble I highly recommend grabbing the explosive shotgun mod, as not only is it fun to use but it's extremely useful for dealing with enemies you'd rather not get close to.

The only real complaints I have about the game after finishing everything on Ultra Violence is how the collectables and hidden items are tied to progression, and how many times whilst looking for them you can progress too far and be unable to go back and look for them. Thankfully this is largely mitigated by the mission select option where you can go back to completed levels to grab items and finish challenges without having to reach the end of the level if you don't need or want to.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a pure FPS shooter. No reloading, just running and gunning and punching demons in the face. Its not trying to do anything but be a fun adrenaline filled ride from beginning to end. If I had one complaint about it is that the game relies a lot on going to one place and doing a thing and surviving constantly respawning enemies. I mean do not get me wrong its fun blowing them to kingdom come, but a lot of the levels felt kinda samey about halfway through. Doesn't really detract from the game just gets a bit repetitive in the long run. The story is kinda nonsensical but it is just there to give an excuse to obliterate demons because they bad and you are the baddest demon slayer on the face of the universe. The game even tells you so. This is a game for any old school FPS gamers out there. Worthy update to the original Doom games.\t\t\t\t\t
