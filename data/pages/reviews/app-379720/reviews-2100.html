\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM starts out as a stylish, brutal, but kinda shallow corridor shooter with lots of exploration and hidden collectibles. You unlock new abilities and weapons as you progress, but also bigger enemies. Eventually you realize the game has morphed into an over-the-top arena shooter, right under your nose. It takes its time evolving into its own source material, like a gritty reboot should.

The game's a ton of fun as long as there are new enemies and weapons to discover, but about two-thirds of the way through you stop seeing new things and start seeing old things in greater numbers, and then it gets tedious. I'm glad I played it, but the end didn't come soon enough. There's also a robust map/scenario/campaign editor and a multiplayer mode that gives HALO a run for its money. No vehicles, but also no lootboxes, so a net improvement over HALO 5.

For the campaign alone, I'd recommend it. The multiplayer looks great too, but from what I hear there aren't a lot of players. Too bad. It's the best of what it is that I've seen in a while.\t\t\t\t\t
