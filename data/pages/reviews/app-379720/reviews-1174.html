\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is DooM, but its not DooM, its DOOM.

Aside from being one of those reboot titles that completely screws up by just releasing part of a franchise with the same title as the original game, DOOM (just Doom from here in) reboots the series in a nice manner, building on ideas of the original games with some modern elements but not enough to bog it down and lose its original identity. However it is far from perfect and has some choices that mar one of the best shooters in the past decade.

On a boring objective level: Yes its a first person shooter and yes it is absolutely a technical powerhouse running smooth as butter while looking glorious on an engine that seems to run on black magic. Its an Id game, this is their thing and they rarely deviate from these elements (cough Rage cough).

Secondly I have not played the Multiplayer. I did not like the look of it.

At the heart of it Doom has enough of the older elements of the originals that most remember it for so as not to put off older fans. The usual staples are here: Imps, Cacodemons, Mancubus etc being shot with Shotguns, rocket launchers and Chainguns etc both utilising the variations given over the years as well as building on them. Imps and Cacos are instantly recognisable to long term fans even with the redesigns and they act as you would expect them to. There are some new items but they dont rock the boat so hard it capsizes. In this regard Doom is very much on brand with what has come before it and quite interestingly adds to what came before. However aside from the theming Doom rolls back to what made the series well known. Unlike Doom 3 which debatably wet the bed and became a survival horror game, Doom here retains the old focus on running and gunning while exploring sprawling levels for secrets and bonuses. In this regard this is possibly the best AAA FPS game of its ilk, sticking mostly to the fast action and rather than just having a linier map you can poke about finding bonuses. In many ways the exploration was the glue that held the whole thing together; while the shooting is fun and impactful, the character upgrades, hidden ammo and collectable fluff kept me coming back. Back when I bought it Doom was the first game that made me want to 100% all the collectables in about 8 years and while a typical Ubisoft open world game may have more, most of it is bloat, here everything seems on point.

I did mention upgrades, and upgrades and leveling up is, well, not very Doom really. Again though Doom here handles it well. While the upgrades may seem like they have stripped elements away from the game to give them to you later, they work in something of a nice learning curve. Initially you have barely any ammo to get by, so this encourages weapon swapping mid fight. Out of shotgun shells? Use the Plasma gun. In many ways the soft upgrade curve where the currency is earned via combat and exploring (no microtransactions here). In this regard Doom here has nicely evolved Doom to a new level.

Despite all this praise there are a few annoying choices and changes. First is the story and how it is delivered. Doom originally treated story as fluff that does not need to be noted. Here it tries to have its cake and eat it with a Doomguy who really does not care for the human drama involved: He is here to kill demons and that is it. Despite this the game frequently stops the action to waffle on about the ongoing situation. This is a minor gripe, but it does seem very anti-Doom.

Secondly there is the movement. While old school Doom was lightning fast, this Doom seems somewhat ponderous. Both character movement and mouse inputs feel somewhat off and sluggish, seemingly designed for a pad and it makes the exploration a little cumbersome when moving between areas. Where the original Doom had Doomguy strafe running at warp speed, new Doom feels like a slightly more energetic Master Chief with the weird, floaty jump included. If you want a truly oldschool feeling game from modern times, try New Bloods Dusk or Amid Evil as they are way more on brand in that regard.

While Doom is now somewhat outdated due to Doom Eternals release it is still a very fun title. It works due its core elements being the parts it buffed up the most, ignoring the misstep of Doom 3, but at the same time the modern story telling and console movement scheme may be offputting to older players. Worth a look when on offer.\t\t\t\t\t
