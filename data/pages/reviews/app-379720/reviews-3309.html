\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing for any fans of run and gun. It's a great stress reliever and can make you feel like a really strong, overpowering bruit of a human fighting an army from hell. The game has fluid gameplay, which is it's really only strong part. The story is ludicrous and goofy (what you'd expect from DOOM), the graphics are awesome but I found there to be some drops every now and again (my computer is pretty good but not the best so it might be on my side). The multiplayer is very fun, but it is not as good as other multiplayer games currently out there. It's a quick quake-y multiplayer, really reminiscing on the old doom multiplayer days. The weakest feature is the Snapmap feature. "Instead of offering mod support, let's give out customer's Forge from halo" said the devs while trying to find a way to make the game seem like a 60 dollar game. Snapmap, while made well, is a feature that replaces something that users would much rather have. Imagine the mods there would be on the market, what a glorious thing that would be.

In total, DOOM is worth it, buy it if you can. I bought it with all my CSGO money and some stuff that I earned (what do you expect, I am still in high school, unemployment ♥♥♥♥♥♥) and with that, I really think this is a great purchase, worth every penny (except for ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ snapmap). This game gets the Hot Bluey seal of approval, being the first of it's kind. Good job ID software, you are the first to receive this prestigious reward.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell done Doom team! You managed to win over an original Doom & Quake fan with extremely high expectations--at least for the Single Player portion of the game.

Doom has wonderful atmosphere, excellent pacing, and truly exceeded my expectations. This edition modernizes the franchise and pushes it to new heights in wonderful gory, glowing, spewing, smashing ways--highly recommended if you like fast, furious, romp-fest style FPS games with attitude. The single player campaign isn't long (about 16 hours I think), but I already want to play it again on the hardest mode. Believe it or not, the ending battle and even the credits themselves are worth the reward. Many of the classic elements are present, many with new twists. You have a LOT of freedom to mold your character throughout the game (or do no character growth and really ramp up the challenge even). Weapon and armor mods, "runes" that modifiy or enchance actions, and plenty of throw backs and fun secrets spice things up and keep the fast paced campaign fresh and enjoyable from start to finish. Bravo ID Software!

Only a few minor crashes while I was completing the game the week of launch. Nothing that caused me to lose progress. And I'm sure those will be fixed. Mostly happened when I was loading the next level.


Multiplayer:
I fundamentally disagree with the choice to use weapon loadouts. I wish there was at least a classic style game mode where we had to pick up weapons from the map itself. Map control was a huge part of Doom and Quake games and that is dimished somewhat by this decision.

Otherwise, Doom Multiplayer is fairly fast paced and fun and given the single player campaign is so enjoyable, its nice to have the added replayability of the multiplayer maps. Plus, there's a lot of room to grow here with Snapmaps and patches/expansions.
