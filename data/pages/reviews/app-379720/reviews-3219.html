\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is about the singleplayer only, I do not do MP so much...

Mars is great, nice rooms, nice lay out, nice graphics, nice everything. You really feel you are on another planet and there is something wrong. I have the feelling that later on you only get 'deatmatch-rooms' with a very boring Hell. Like you are going from a big A-movie to a bad C-movie. I had better memories from the original Doom. There Hell was full of skulls and 'gore-art'. Hell here is very boring to see and just one of the other multiplayer-levels...

The gameplay is very smooth, the framerate is very smooth, the action is very smooth. I never enjoyed such a boring concept: we put you in a room, you only can get out when everything is dead... And then over and over again. It is not boring because the way you kill and how the weapons feel like. An big animal is running towards you, you shoot it with the shotgun, it goes a bit and then push onwards again. It feels so good that you can kill something after a few shots. You really have to work for it. And you need to keep moving! It really feels like Unreal Tournament. At the end of the game it is getting boring. Hmmm... we have a room and enemies... What can we do more... Oh yeah! More enemies and bigger enemies! And if he can finsish that we just teleport even bigger and more enemies. And if he dies... He just has to start all over with the 'stage'. It is boring after a while, but if you do not think about, it is great fun. Every 'stage' puts on more pressure and it is like an Unreal-tournament-undurance. Every time a bit more, bit bigger, bit crazier.

And the enemies... First I was not thinking this was Doom, just because the enemies are looking so different with these nice graphics and the update. When playing the original, for me the Cacodemon was just a big fluffy thing with a lot of hitpoints. Scared of it? No... But now... I can really imagine why it is big, evil and fearful. And so are all the enemies, much better and less cartoony (DUH!), but in a good way. Also the AI has an update. They are really fast and hunting you. It is not that easy anymore like in the original you can outrun them or something. You really have to work and run. And the sound they make while you cannot see them and you are in a big room... :).

I cannot go in depth with every aspect, but one last thing. There is no relaodbutton... I really had to get used. I just explained myself that in the future, you do not need to relead weapons anymore :p (Except the super shotgun, but that is old ofcourse ;) ). It really works great. The gameplay is all about speed, action and adrenaline. Long time ago I had so much fun, although it sometimes feel like I am playing a Unreal-tournamentgame. Well that was not a bad game either but I mean, it is not really a single player like you are used to... But it is high quality (most of the time) and not too short. It really leans on the great action and gameplay. That is getting me trought the boring parts. Impressive.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is the newest game in the DOOM series. If you don't know anything about DOOM, it's about a marine shooting demons to save the world from demonic invasion, eventually closing up the portal between the human's dimension and hell. I know it sounds outrageous and I was skeptical too to the gameplay when I first heard about DOOM 3 (I'm too young for the original DOOM, DOOM 64, and DOOM 2). But it turns out to be beautifully written with several plot twists and I find the story very engaging and enjoyable. Unlike most other FPS, the combat style is VERY fast-paced where you will literally have to keep moving all the time if you don't want to get burned by fireballs, lasers, or any other kind of lethal stuff thrown by the demons. I'm not a gaming god that has 2000 games in my library so there might be other games out there that has the same extremely fast-paced combat style (like Bulletstorm?) so I assume this combat style as a unique aspect. In short, DOOM is a fast-paced, story-driven FPS that offers unique combat style with a lot of gore and GREAT metal musics that accompanies you whenever you're butchering demons.

Now, I'm going to talk about the good parts of the game. First of all, it's the combat system. I.Love.It. You get to do something called 'gore kill' where you'll kill a staggered demon with an extremely cool kill (and very bloody) animation. You can do a gore kill from above, from the leg, from the hands, from the chest, etc and each of them will show you different killing styles. As I said in my introduction paragraph, the combat style is overall extremely fast-paced. I find myself sitting on the edge of the seat everytime the combat 'session' starts. Graphics quality does not need to be questioned - it's epic. I finished the game on maxed out graphic settings and it was one hell of an experience. The gameplay has a recognizable pattern - you'll start with walking around trying to find a way to get to the next area. During this 'no-demons' part, you get to explore the environment to find various collectibles items like weapon upgrades, DOOM Marine dolls, Praetor suit (your badass armor) upgrades, classic DOOM maps, etc. And then, when you found the next 'area', the demons will start spawning and there you go - the metal music intensifies as you jump around from ledge to ledge, jump over demons and gore-kill them from above, shoot stuff, blow up stuff, lasers, rockets, fire, bullets, chainsaw, blood, blood, blood, blood, more blood, MOAR blood, and boom, you'll start with trying to find a way to the next 'area' again. This pattern will repeat over and over again as you progress through the story. Each combat 'session' is extremely challenging and I found myself scavenging for health, ammo, and armor desperately as the demons throw or shoot stuff at me (or try to crush me). Each demon has their own unique combat style with unique weak spots, since ammo management is critical in this game, it's very important to only shoot them on their weak spots, which makes it more challenging.
The soundtrack is also very good. I'm not a fan of metal or rock musics myself but I enjoyed listening to the soundtrack. You also don't need to worry about not being able to understand the story because you didn't play the previous games. It might be pretty confusing at the start but everything will be explained as you progress.

Now, the bad parts of the game. Just like the Wolfenstein series, it has the fps drop bug (FPS stays between 10 to 20 fps on certain levels). I know it's not a serious issue since all you have to do is just restart the game and it will be back to normal. But I find it kind of annoying, especially because I had to wait for around 5 minutes for everything to load and to be able to click Exit. I also don't really like the fact that weapon and armor upgrades are hidden in secret areas. I'm not a fan of exploring stuff or finding secret area so I still have a lot of stuff locked when I finished the game. The BFG9000 (your ultimate weapon) puzzle also lack of clues. I stood on one of the platforms for around 15 minutes trying to figure out what the hell am I supposed to do. I eventually gave up and looked for the solution at YouTube just to find out that all I had to do was shoot stuff. Navigating around the map is also troublesome for me, some areas can only be reached after doing several parkour stuff, so whenever you accidentally fall, you'll have to re-do all the parkour stuff again.

In conclusion, IMHO, DOOM is a game worth every penny. The adrenaline rush during the fast-paced combat is the highlight of the game and I love it! I heavily recommend this game for any gamers looking for a casual gory FPS that doesn't require too much thinking.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA VERY long awaited game for me, having played every game of the DOOM franchise and being an attendee of QuakeCon for about a decade, the anticipation was KILLING me!

I'm not a technical reviewer, so many details are escaped that are covered in many other reviews.

A (Graphics): The graphics are absolutely amazing! While I think recommending 4GB of VRAM is absolutely ridiculous, there are benefits to having the entire game loaded onto the GPU's memory to speed up rendering because the game is VERY fast paced. That is to be expected from iD Software. I do feel that they left out the ability for players with older cards to play, because the game loads and immediately eats up 1536MB of VRAM without having started a match or the campaign. I suggest you give players the ability to tweak their settings a little more so even older cards can load the game, but alas, that is the way of gaming in the modern day. After I upgraded from a GTX 480 to a 7970, I saw no problems whatsoever.

A+ (Multiplayer): I'm not too fond of the Snapmap (but that's just because I don't use it much), but the Deathmatch and other MP modes are spot on. They're fun, challenging, and full of gore! I see very little wrong with the way this was done. I do however wish that with the arena mode, you would start off with no weapons instead of the COD-loadout style (which has given people a mixed feeling about the game). My other issue comes down to levelling WAY too fast. People have already hit the highest level of Echelon and the game has not been out very long. It simply makes you say "What next?" but the game is simply so damn fun that you keep going anyway!

A (Campaign): I have only played the first level of the campaign because I have found the MP so fun, but from what I have seen it was very well done. Not only is it challenging, bloody, fast paced, but it shows the dedication iD Software put into the re-make to show everyone this franchise isn't done yet, and it was time to make it ready for the new era of gaming.

I have very few reservations about the game, and I would HIGHLY recommend this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou dont play Doom for the story, thats for sure...

Buuuuuuuuut, If you long for frantic gameplay and have ridiculous reaction times, this game is for you. If you want to spew unbridled death and destruction upon the legions of hell, this game is for you. If you want to use the BFG again for old times sake, this game is for you. What Doom does, it does well.

Lots of different weapons and mutiple mods for all of them give it good variety there. A couple-ish dozen enemy types keeps it interesting. The only thing that got boring was the arena type gameplay. Basically you get to a point and you get locked into an area against a swarm of enimies and you fight to survive. This happens the entire length of the game. Regardles, the pucker factor is always there, and letting your breath out after winning a hard fought battle is always rewarding. I plan to go through it again on Nightmare, and see if I can get every secret, pickup, and challenge, of which there are a ton. The ending was lackluster, but hey, see the first line.

The graphics are good, and it runs supurbly at 60 FPS on Ultra. Never had a single hiccup, aside from one crash that seemed completly random when I got an Argent pickup. I also played multiplayer for a while, just doing team deathmatch. It was fun but there was nothing to make it standout.

Overall 8/10. Would use Gauss Cannon again.
