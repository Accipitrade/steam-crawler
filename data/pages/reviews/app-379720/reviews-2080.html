\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComplicated gameplay. Horrendous story. Poor game design. Boring. These are all things that DOOM is not.

DOOM is something special. In an age of AAA studios spewing out hot garbage rather than AAA games, id Software (not Bethesda, they're just the publisher (thank God)) has truly produced what I would call one of the best games of all time.

It starts you right from the get-go with the introduction (and soon destruction) of demons taking over wherever you are. This game wants you to get into the action as soon as possible. The main character, the Doomslayer, himself tries to get out of heavy exposition when necessary. That's about as far as I'll go in the main campaign. I don't want to spoil any surprises for new-coming players.

The goal of DOOM is to be the most bloodthirsty run & gun shooter of all time, and I'd say it accomplishes this goal very well. Speed is key as you dash around dodging vicious demon attacks while also getting up close and in the face of your enemies to bash their brains into their chest. With a full artillery of guns and your brute strength at your hands, this game gives you tons of freedom to just kill things.

Story wise, there's not much. It's good, but it's not too heavily focused on. But that's expected of DOOM. You're basically annihilating a race of Hell spawn. Who cares that much about a long, strung out story? For those seeking lore, however, there is a codex to read on your own time as you wish.

Level design is very precise in this game. To make you as mobile as possible, there are several ledges to grab onto, platforms to jump on, launchers to, well, launch you, and much more. As long as you're moving and making split-second decisions of where to go, you'll be fine. A good tip given to you in DOOM is that standing still=death.

Other than the campaign, the multiplayer and snap-map modes are honestly pretty fun. Not as fun as the main story, mind you, but they're nice to do when you want to try something a little different in the game. At launch, which was not when I got this game, multiplayer supposedly sucked, but I've played it many, many times, and found the rounds to be fun, quick but not too quick, and rewarding. You will practically never exit a multiplayer match empty handed (the fact you get some of the best customization I've ever seen in a multiplayer game and all for free really helps too).

The snap-map is basically a level designing tool where you can upload your own customized DOOM levels or play already created levels from other users. There are some pretty fun options that you can toy around with, and even play with your friends if you wanted to do so.

Okay. I'm almost done. Hang on. I could go even deeper into the specifics of this game, but I've already probably bored you to death.

There are downsides to DOOM that I never notice but should probably mention. Multiplayer matches are (sometimes) hard to join. People have been playing more and more recently, but still. I've had one or two times where I waited for ten minutes or so trying to get into a game. The level design, although very detailed and well mapped out, is pretty bland. The mars settings don't differ too much other than the obvious building placement, but you kinda get a common theme of brown, orange, and red. Even the levels in Hell felt a little too similar in some cases. But, other than that, there's nothing much to complain about. Again, I don't even notice or care about these imperfections 95% of the time.

DOOM is a great example of what a studios like Bethesda, Ubisoft, or Activision should be striving to be putting out. It has developers who care about gameplay and fun first and foremost. DLC and microtransactions are something that the people at id Software avoid at all costs, and the result shows. DOOM lets you go on a bloodthirsty power trip to rip & tear through the hordes of Hell until it you are done, and also giving you a hell of an experience doing it too.

I'd recommend this game for anyone who has the lust to obliterate evil in the most gruesome and satisfyingly gory ways possible, and once you're done with that, look into the upcoming sequel, DOOM Eternal, coming this November. It's basically DOOM, but improved in every single way. I just love it.


9.5/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t★☆☆☆☆ Terrible!

Action

The blandest game I saw in years since modern military shooter craze started to fade away.

The "plot" is so bland there really isn't even one to speak of, which would be fine if the gameplay was great, but it's not!

The graphics and performance were all pretty good. I did not appreciate a couple of design elements that really ruined the game for me - zombies and glory kills. Zombies are wandering around many areas where there are no other enemies. Considering that zombies are mobile pickup generators. This felt really stupid to have entire areas with so many boring and easy to kill enemies.

The gameplay is kinda like Painkiller, only more generic. The graphics are okay, but there are some pretty low resolution textures in here that look like something from 10 years ago. The environments are static and noninteractive. The music is generic tech-metal stuff that's completely forgettable. The game's atmosphere and setting are cheesy and overwrought, nothing like the true terror that the originals and even Doom 3 managed to evoke. It's almost like an unfunny joke version of Doom.

Don't get your hopes up for the multiplayer, it's a absolutely awful. Flying cheaters everywhere, wallhackers, you name it!

Painfully boring, dumb as a bag of rocks, repetitive mindless shooter!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 while originally believed to be a reboot of the series, has recently been revealed thanks to DOOM Eternal's story that it is in fact a continuation of DOOM and DOOM II. The game begins with you waking up tied to a sarcophagus, in a UAC research facility on mars currently in the aftermath of a demonic invasion, leaving no human survivors other than Dr. Samuel Hayden, the cybernetic man who runs the facility, Olivia Pierce, an elderly woman who had a high rank in the facility, but became corrupted by Hell during her research and ultimately started the invasion, and finally yourself, the legendary DOOM Marine, Guy, Slayer etc. This game is a call back to classic old school style and death match, with a gameplay style that more resembles Quake 3 Arena than classic DOOM, but ultimately works very well for the game. Movement is lighting fast, reloading is non existent, trying to take cover will only get you killed, you can perform glory kills by dealing enough damage to an enemy until they become staggered and flash different colors depending on if you are in range to perform a glory kill or not, and you can use the chainsaw to refill your ammo when you run low. Learning how to quick switch, learning what enemies are and aren't weak to certain weapons, and utilizing everything in you arsenal as much as possible is the key to becoming an absolute beast in the somewhat short but infinitely re-playable single player campaign spanning about 16 hours on average. While this game may be heavily out dated by DOOM Eternal with that game basically improving upon everything this game has to offer. This game in my opinion will still go down as one of the best shooters ever.\t\t\t\t\t
