\r\n\t\t\t\t\tToo early to give an accurate review but I have to say this game put a smile on my face quite a few times already! Late to the party as usual as I'm pretty casual at gaming (every couple of years I pick it up for a few weeks). The arena style kill-everything-to-progress dynamics really work here; you forget about health and armour and just concentrate on crunching your way through enemies, and the game's fluidity makes you feel like a hero. Interestingly ammo is a constant concern: in the style of game where you're normally able to heave around an entire supply depot's worth, 20 shells and 90 rounds seems almost implausibly little, even if it's in plentiful supply. I guess it's in an effort to make players switch weapons. Enemies are good, just intelligent enough and the glory kills are constant fun.
♥♥♥♥♥♥♥? The irritating shotgun trope of buckshot vanishing into thin air after five yards; slightly muted weapon sounds (everyone else take note of Crysis 2, 3 for satisfying weapons); annoying secrets that you spend too long wandering around trying to find; too much "tech" to deal with in the name of upgrades etc..
But it's managed to be super fun - most of that stuff doesn't matter when you've just jumped on a bad guy's head to finish him off.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimply put, a creatively done re-imagining of a timeless classic. The original DOOM was a revolutionary creation, setting the stage for a new age of shooters. We've seen a lot of great, innovative new titles in the decades since, and, sure, we've seen a lot of un-creative clones and cop-outs too.....

DOOM 2016 is no clone or cop-out. It is, as I said, a re-imagining of a timeless classic DONE RIGHT. The game's mix of atmosphere, music, and visceral game-play give it a theme that will draw FPS enthusiasts to it for probably decades to come, assuming there isn't another amazing remaster or reboot sometime in the distant future. That and there's something satisfying about shooting up demons and monsters, and giving them a good ol "eviction notice" from the mortal realm. Give it a go. It's so worth it. Just remember to keep it in moderation. Addictive as this game is, we should still live a good chunk of our lives away from the computer too. XD\t\t\t\t\t
