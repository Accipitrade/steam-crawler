\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the better FPS games of the last 10 years, Doom (2016) remains an awesome exercise in badassery. While the occult demon worshipping scientists story is surprisingly interesting, the real meat of the game is the moment-to-moment gunplay, which is consistently awesome throughout the experience. Every weapon looks and feels amazing, and the insanely good soundtrack only further enhances that rip & tear experience. Movement is quick, reloads are almost nonexistent, and enemies behave ultra-aggressively, making the whole game feel like a careful balancing act between fight for your life and demolish hordes of pathetic demons. While the games campaign does feel a bit too long, especially in the middle section where the narrative starts to drag its feet, the overall experience is still fantastic.

My only real complaints are that the boss fights are nothing special and usually just an exercise in how many bullets can you shove into this guy while jumping up and down. Standard enemies also become pretty bullet-spongey in the late game, with certain varieties just soaking up huge amounts of damage, which I guess is required for balancing but still. Also, there are a ton of different upgrades in this game but most of them feel pointless. Praetor Suit enhancements, in particular, felt like they might as well be nonexistent. Overall though, Doom (2016) is an easy recommendation for anyone who likes fast-paced FPS action in the style of old school arcade shooters.\t\t\t\t\t
