A First Person Action Shooter
Do not watch the video below if you don't want to see Spoilers
https://youtu.be/f8_K92q59_o
This review focuses on the campaign only
+ The gameplay is incredibly fun! Honestly, this has to be my Game of The Year choice or at least best shooter of 2016. Basically, it is just non-stop action from start to finish as you slay hordes of demons in the most brutal and grotesquely satisfying of ways with a huge arsenal of overpowered weapons.
+ The story and lore is surprisingly interesting even though Doom focuses far more on the core gameplay. To put things simply, humans were trying to create a transdimmensional protal between Mars and Hell to harness the power of "Argent Energy" to send to Earth. However, a demon outbreak occurs and everyone on the Mars station was turned into demon spawn. You are a super soldier who is awoken to clear out the demon outbreak and find a way to close the link between Hell and Mars.
+ The graphics are amazing! Definitely AAA quality. There is so much detail given to each and every level. I often just wanted to stop and stare as it is all quite amazing to take in. The hell dimmension especially is facinating with tons of sculptures and satanic imagery everywhere you look. It is very atmospheric, and I felt engrossed in the world.
+ The controls (at least with a controller) feel natural, responsive, and ever so fluid. Switching between weapons in the middle of combat, clambering ledges, jumping and shooting at the same time, everything just feels great!
+ Large number of weapons to unlock which all have their own unique weapon modifications which you can then upgrade even further! This allows you to essentially pick and choose your favorites and make them stronger as you progress and customize them a bit to your liking. Not to mention they are all INCREDIBLY FUN to use! Mowing down demons with a 3 barrel gattling gun, blowing heads off with a gauss cannon, or disintegrating an entire room with the BFG never felt better!
+ Glory Kills or Executions are so freaking sick! You will be punching heads off demons, cracking there skulls, ripping them apart, chainsawing them to bits, and so much more! It reminded me of the type of gore you see in Mortal Kombat, and not to mention you get awarded health packs for each and every glory kill you do!
+ Apart from weapon upgrades, you can also upgrade your suit which will give you a ton of additional perks. You can also find Runes from Rune Trials (short challenge stages) which will give you addiional perks. All of which you can customize to your liking at any time. This adds a layer of strategy, as certain runes and weapons can be used together for great benefit!
+ The music is great and changes depending on if you are in combat or not. The metal soundtrack certainly adds to the overall feel of the game.
+ TONS of secret areas where you can find upgrade points, hidden collectible items, armor/health, and even a sort of Easter Egg on each level which will allow you to play a classic original Doom level.
+ A few absolutely epic boss fights that are challenging, and simply thrilling

- The map is a bit hard to understand at first and be quite confusing at times. (especially when trying to find secret locations as doors and walls are rarely shown on the map)
- You don't unlock the ability to double jump until about half way through the game which I found to be very annoying. They really should have given you the thrust boots at the beginning as they really do improve movement capabilities considerably and make the game more enjoyable overall.
- There isn't enough backstory / lore to define your character. By the end of the game I still had far too many questions, and did not quite understand who I was or how I came to be so powerful.

~ While not a downside, I feel like there should have been a sliding, rolling, dashing, or thrusting movement mechanic to allow you to dodge enemy attacks more easily. Sometimes it can be hard to move out of the way of a fast incoming projectile.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is so straightforward. No excessive cutsenes, no distracting storylines, no unnecessary game mechanics, just raw destruction and fun. This game won't win any awards for writing, but it is definitely one that will spawn an endless amount of awesome clips and losing track of time. The wide range of difficulty levels are fantastic. The lowest difficulty is 100% managable while still getting your heart racing at times, the standard difficulty will challenge you without making you spend hours trying the same checkpoint over and over, and Nightmare mode will make you want to break your keyboard, but with a smile as you do it. I recommend this game to anyone looking for a good time without much extra stuff slapped on the side.

On a hardware/software note, this game is beautifully optimized, will run well on almost any rig within the specified requirements, and has tons of options to make the game exactly how you want it. The only downside is that it is upwards of 60 GB to download, so HDD/SSD space and internet caps could become an issue.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought this game during the 2016 Autumn sale. I gott say that it completely took me by surprise. I was expecting Duke Nukem level regurgitation, but the game somehow holds onto an arcade charm that I thought 25+ years of gaming had destroyed.

The visuals are beautiful. The audio is well balanced, and the heavy metal soundtrack makes you feel like a badass. Also, the game is uber optimized. I am playing at Ultra settings on Vulkan API, and at 4K resolution at well over 70FPS w/o any hitches. Well done Bethesda, where was this for Skyrim and Fallout?

The techie options feed even further into my need to geek out and feel proud about my rig.

I haven't played the multiplayer yet, but that's not my thing after X-suck Live and its toddlers sucked the fun out of online gaming for me 10+ years ago. I do appreciate the support for it though.\t\t\t\t\t
