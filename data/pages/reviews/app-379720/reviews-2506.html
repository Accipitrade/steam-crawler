This game is so good. Seriously. Get this game. The combat is super satisfying, the soundtrack is amazing, and it's a really great experience. I want to focus on the campaign more because I feel like that's what most people buy doom for. The campaign kicks ass. The upgrade system is amazing and you really feel like you're getting stronger when you upgrade a gun and its mods. I like the praetor suit and its upgrades. Though I wish there was a bit more to it, I honestly thought that it was still great. The overall atmosphere of this game is incredible. Very dark and ambient. Going to hell for the first time was such a great experience and I feel like if hell is a real place, then that's probably what it would look like. The team did an amazing job on the level design and I feel like they really captured that hellish vibe. I finished the campaign and went for another go because I wanted to try out some of the other mods for the guns. Very fun game that I'm in love with. The multiplayer is ok but it feels like your generic multiplayer mode for any fps. Snapmaps were pretty fun and I thought that some of the map ideas players had were great. Overall, a really great game for anyone who wants to kill lots of things.

I give this game a 9.5/10. Can't wait for the sequel.\t\t\t\t\t

You might think that 13 hours is little, but every ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ minute was fun.
(I only finished the story, got lots of fun maps to explore with my friends!)

Gameplay wise, it's too much fun.
The variety of weapons and their uses, their mods and upgrades, makes it all awesome fun.
Lots of blood and crazy animations.
Movement is pretty simple for the most of it.
The powerups that you can find are ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ AWESOME!
The maps are full of interesting things to find and explore, adding more to the killings you do endlessly.

Story wise, it's pretty good.
While it's not Witcher 3-36-possible-endings-GOTY-tear-spawning-heart-crushing-love-making story,
It's good enough to give you purpose and some things to think about.
The characters are interesting and each with their own ideas and ♥♥♥♥ whatever I like killing stuff.

Personally it felt a bit slow at the start, but it was probably just to get new players used to the game.

To summerize,
It's an experience every gamer who thinks he likes FPS should try,
an experience every gamer who thinks he likes to kill should try,
an experience every gamer who thinks he like to have fun should try.

Truely, a masterpiece.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, that can I say that hasn't already been said.
Let me start with that I did not play the orginal Doom games 1 and 2,
but jumped the fence and played Heretic and the Spear of Destiny and really enjoyed those "O so many years ago".

That said, I did not like the rencarnation of Doom when it popped around with it's 3 version.
The jump scare semi horror game did not tingle my senses. I dislike horror games in general and although that one looked good, it had a lots of elements to it. It just did not play enjoyably.

But this game. Boy... I love it, absolutely adore it.
The combat is meaty and powerful. Everything combat wise is enjoyable, the graphics are fantastic, sound effects just complete the whole picture front and back.

Only complaints I have is that I did not use half of the runic powers... I just can not be bothered to go deep end in to menus and scoure the map to discover them while there are always more demons to kill.
And yes, exploration and freedom in the envoirments feel good. It also takes the speed away from time to time.

Appart from the collect a ton their is not a lot going for the quiet bits where you are going to the next objective or seeking for some extra loot. And for the collect a ton itself I am two sided. Yes the collecting is enjoyable, especially with the right map upgrades it adds a bit of a puzzling element to it and some nice secondary objectives and they are hidden enough mostly for you to be really searching for them. But not that badly you keep searching for hours to get anywhere.

With that ther absolute top of what you can have: max health, armor, ammo, infinite health (because of a > 100 armour room makes it so you do not run out of ammo as long as you keep the armor up). To the point you exhaust your weaponry and eventually die while you try to circle strafe your opponents and try your very best to try and kill them, but suddenly fail because you run in to a couple of deamons you missed around the corner or because you do not look well enough. That drain your armor and health in seconds. The gathering bit for that part did not feel all that rewarding, the parts for the weapons and upgrades was. But don't get me wrong, the hoarding of Ardent upgrades to put in to max health and armour are paramount. It did not feel like I was getting that much more powerfull when I really needed it.

I conclude this game is mostly skill based. Your skill as an FPS god must be to dodge, keep an eye out for any placements, spawning and movement while switching between the right weapons and keep moving at all times. Hit up powerups later in fights to recharge and kill the larger baddies and sometimes just take your time and keep rattling you 'infinite ammo supply' (mind you the rune and > 100 armour) till you get the result you want.

The weapons are varied and work as expected. They are so much fun. Though I wish I could have the traditional full weapon menu display because keeping track of 10 weapons + the BFG (that you can only equip with a separate key and is not on the weapon wheel) and the fact that every weapon has 2 different modes, makes it more complicated then I should be. I wish I could suspend weapon upgrades and all have mostly just 1 special weapon mode per weapon. Don’t get me wrong, it’s not that half of the weapon mods are useless, on the contrary most really add to your possibilities, but you won’t know until you try them. That is one of the gripes I have. I would love to have been given a sort of try out modes where I can freely switch between the weapons and possible weapon modes and see what the weapons modes actually do. Still I would love to just switch out some of the mods I had with just to buy the ultimate upgrade for a weapon instead of earning them through another challenge for the weapon mastery.
All in all it has been a blast. It has been a long time a game had me so excited during me playing it and keeping me so intensely engaged.

My vary last gripe. The music is MOSTLY amazing. I say that because there are bits I actually identify as music because it sound like something I would listen to from a Metal bands album, but other parts I describe as ambient noise. Not that it is bad, but they are shorter sounds that sometimes sounds like demonic possesion, chain saw noises and such. And the ones I did not like sound like alarms or monsters. It distracts, which is fine when I know there are no monsters in hoards, but I'm going through corridors and such. But when I'm in the middle of a fightd I feel it is pulling my leg. I would love for some more songs that are actual songs for the most important bits. Boss fights and such, songs I would happily enjoy listening to outside of the game. Like the orginal Doom theme song for example would (in my honest opinion) be the crown. The final boss could have a bit more different waves / stages and just end with the Doom theme reimagened and go more frantic and epic untill that final kill.

But all of that said:
Thank you ID Software for an incredible adventure once again.

I will definitely do another play through again on higher difficulty to get more out of this game.
Keep em commin :-D
