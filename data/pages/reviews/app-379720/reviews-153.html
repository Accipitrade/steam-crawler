\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter finishing the game, I have to say, it definitely is a good game, but it does not live up to the immense hype surrounding it. It's a fun game, where you get to destroy demons. It's iconic, I mean it's freaking DOOM. But when looking at it through a critical lens, there are a lot of problems.

For a game about killing demons with sick weapons, the gameplay and gunplay are somewhat unimpactful. The movement feels rather floaty, and even though speed and traversal are supposed to be cores of the game, it does not deliver. Aside from just walking fast, there is nothing else you can really do to go faster in this game. That might be fine if the game didn't have such open levels while also having spread out enemies, but when you're far away from a group of enemies, and all you can do is just try to walk towards them, it feels kinda underwhelming. Depending on who you are, the movement might just be a minor gripe, but I don't know how people can praise the gunplay in this game so much. I mean, it's fine... I guess? But it's nothing special. It leans a bit more towards style than substance. Sure, there are cool finishers and tons of gory effects, but the shooting is so "whatever". There just isn't a lot of visceral feedback from shooting, or from killing, and when that's the whole point behind the game, that's a major drawback IMO. There are so many other modern day shooters that handle gunplay better, where just shooting a gun feels better. Maybe my standards are too high but I expected more.

Still at the end of the day, I was able to get through the game with at least some fun. The music was banging and that did live up to expectation which was nice. The story is whatever, but let's be honest, no one is playing DOOM for the story. The campaign was a fine length, but because of my relative dislike of the gameplay, it did feel like it dragged on a bit too long. Also, the bosses suck which is a disappointment but it is what it is.

No matter who you are though, I would recommend at least trying it, just to experience such a critically acclaimed and iconic game. You might enjoy it more than me, and fall in love. At the very least I'm excited to try out DOOM Eternal because it seems to address some of my gripes with this game.\t\t\t\t\t
