\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOutstanding. Not a glitch or bug and perfectly balanced. THIS is what FPS was always supposed to be. The first time an FPS was worth purchasing since, well, since Doom. If you're not old enough to remember Doom, then you want this game. If you remember playing Doom, then you NEED this game.

Playing the vintage levels brings back memories, and if you think for a minute that they will be a cake walk, you'll get schooled pretty quickly. Actually surprising how well they hold up over the years. A testament to the craftsmanship of ID's developers. They wrote the book, and it's obviously held up to the test of time.

Multiplayer has its issues, but I'm sure a balance can be achieved at some point. Snap maps are interesting, and certainly much easier than the old fashioned level builders from the early 90's. Nonetheless, singleplayer is the star of this show, and well worth the price.

For the first time in ever, I'm looking forward to DLC and will be buying. Holding off on the season pass because it just seems to expand Multi, and as I said that is a bit weak. But give me some levels and we're off to the races.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEarly stage review - It's really just a gorgeous remake of Doom.

Wander the big, gorgeous maps.
Walk into areas and gorgeous monsters spawn.
Kill monsters, now with the ability to do up-close GOREgeous finishing moves (this is almost required, as these finishing moves provide you with much needed health and ammo drops)

Some objectives let you get permanent upgrades (weapons, armor, personal buffs) but not anything close to the skill trees you get in a modern RPG game.

So no, "choose your own path" no "accomodates different playing styles" - No matter what you just wade in and kill the monsters.

Mind you. I'm only on the thrid level after about 8 hours of playtime. No idea how long the game takes to complete, or if I'll eventually get enough power ups to try and specialize a bit.

As of right now, I give it a "Wait until it's on sale." review.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI highly recommend the Single Player campaign! Sadly, the multiplayer is not worth your time.

I like the campaign because:
  • The gameplay is very solid. The enemies follow the paradigm of the original Doom: demons are quite distinct and simple in their behaviors, but complexity and challenge come from having rich mixes of them, and in different room layouts. Your locomotion abilities and weapons are similar to the classic, with a few modernization you may expect (double jump + ledge grab, secondary fire on weapons). Speedwise, there's no advanced movement a la quake (this runs on consoles after all) but with the right runes the game gets pretty fast. Weapons upgrade in interesting ways, and the game almost becomes a combat sandbox, as you try out new stuff.
  • At the dramatic level, the doomguy is super badass and makes you feel awesome. There's no petty attempt to characterize it other than the killing machine you would expect. The way he interacts with the story characters and enemies is just perfect. I wish more games were this honest.
  • Artistically, the environments are crafted with love, animations are cool (solid work on glory kills and doom guy deaths), and the music creates the perfect atmosphere. Technically, the game runs smooth and loads very fast. I have encountered few non blocking bugs and one crash.

Honestly, I did not have so much fun in an FPS since a very long time. I played this on nightmare and had a hell of a time. I may recommend to turn glory kills highlight off, once you are used to how it works. Have fun!
