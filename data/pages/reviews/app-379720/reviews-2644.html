\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't get me wrong, I love DOOM and ID software but this game was just... underwhelming. First of all, I absolutely adore the new graphics and the music, but only one hour in I can see where ID and Bethesda failed.

Guns like the Shotgun and Plasma rifle are pathetically weak - with even the weakest enemies being bullet sponges - taking multiple point-blank shots to the head and still surviving. Combat is less fast-paced and more focused on longevity and ammo preservation, with the same engagements repeating over and over again. The game seems to rely on Melee functionality being more powerful than guns, rendering some weapons defunct, leaving the player with only one strategy to play - Shoot, punch them in the face and continue to the next one. Sometimes a grenade or burst fire is added in to spice things up, but everything else just becomes stale and repetitive. Sometimes the game grinds right down to a halt as the linear map design stops and turns into a platformer. The game becomes really frustrating with the addition of the climbing mechanic. Not only do I have to search left and right when I get stuck, but I have to worry about up and down, too. Though it does add some interesting possibilities for secrets and combat manoeuvrability.

When it comes to story, I was pleasantly surprised by the addition of a Human antagonist, though the story still seems a little underwhelming compared to the previous games. The length of the game is appreciated, though.

The Graphics and Sounds are astonishing however, with the accurate portrayal of Weapons in accordance with their power. Each crunch and splatter reverberates satisfyingly in High quality surround-sound. A welcome addition.

All in all, the game is definitely engaging. Although the gunplay is a bit iffy and the glory kills look like I'm stomping on a wet sponge, there's definitely an experience to be had, just don't expect any similarity to the previous game, as new DOOM takes the series into a whole new (Somewhat unprecedented) direction.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. I saw the videos, when it was first being introduced and didn't think my old PC would handle it. I planned to get it once I've upgraded. A year later, I'm still using my old LGA775 PC with 3ghz q9650 oc'ed at 3.7ghz and 8gb of ram. I bought a GTX960 4GB ssc GPU to run my older games and then I saw that DOOM 2016 was available to play for free, one weekend.

I said, what the heck and after spending a day and a half downlading it, I boot the game up, expecting myself to laugh for a possibly super choppy experience. Whatdaya know Joe? The game ran BEAUTIFULLY on it's default settings (which was a mix of medium to high settings, with textures set on ultra thanks to my 4gb gpu) and I WAS JUST BLOWN AWAY.

Fast, furious and pure rock and roll! I felt like a kid again!
The single player campaign is action packed fun, with amazing visuals (even at med settings, this game looks fantastic!), speed that took getting used to, wild demons and bad ass weaponry.

I finished the game recently and can now say, that the experience was great from start to finish. I'm a big fan of the multiplayer mode as well and though it's not a perfect mode, I've had a lot of fun with it.

The Snap Maps mode, which is a creator zone, where fans can create their own maps and share them with the gaming community, deserves way more love than its getting now, because it is enriched with great features to keep you coming back to DOOM, even after you've achieved everything in the campaign and MP modes.

Bottom line is, no game is perfect. But there's always that one game that you know you love so much, despite any of its drawbacks. For me, it's DOOM 2016. I definitely recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
