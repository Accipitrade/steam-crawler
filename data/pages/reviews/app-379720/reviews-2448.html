\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAN ABSOLUTE MASTERPIECE.

40 hours in and I still scream and laugh like Alex Jones at this gameplay. Nearly everything about it is absolutely perfect.
The first time through the first level I was absolutely aroused (no kappa) beyond belief as the level demonstrated glory kills and the abject RAGE of the DOOMslayer.

I cannot recommend highly enough. Probably my favorite game of all time, over even the original AceCombat Holy Trinity. Any gamer without this title in their library is NOT a gamer. Develop your righteous crusader hatred against the forces of the doom, avenge the innocent, and drive the fear of hell into the demons with barbarous cruelty.

*Soundtrack and Sound Design? 20/10. I bought it for $10 on Google Play and it's worth every penny. Channels the rage and flow of the game wordlessly and effortlessly, Mick Gordon's experimentation with Synths crafting an absolute masterpiece. The hatred of the demons from the silent protaganist rivaling Goblin Slayer, as he mercilessly fulfills all of the hellspawn's worst nightmares.

*Game mechanics? Top f**** notch. The entire gameplay is a rush of testosterone and endorphins, as you throw demons to the floor and they look up to you in absolute terror before you crush their skulls with your bare hands.
(GO TO 5:24 ON THIS VIDEO: ( https://youtu.be/9SGiI0_AA4o?t=324 )
A powerup called 'berserk' where you can instant kill any demon with your bare hands, their screams of terror and pain as you remove the filth from existance. The chainsaw instantly killing any demon you have the fuel to take on, spawning tons of Ammo and health pickups to refill your weapons, as it screams in terror while you cut it open alive.
Some of the demons, like the Shield Shotgunners or worse, Pinkies, are armored from the front requiring you to either deal 5x+ the amount of damage or flank them. The Pinkie can instant kill you if you are impacted by it's charge. Annoying as hell, ended many an arcade run for me, but makes you feel accomplished when you put the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ down, or better, rip off their own fangs and cut their throat.

*Lore? Badass as the game itself.
The lore spins the story about you, called "the doom slayer", where demons only talk about you in hushed tones after you spent Eons in hell killing billions of the hellspawn, destroying a demon the size of a skyscraper without even wearing power armor, to the point where they ♥♥♥♥ themselves just thinking about you. A horror game where YOU are the horror, and you are made out to be just as badass as you are to human and demon alike.

*Level design?
With the exception of Foundry (F**** foundry on every level, it's full of bloody glitches that end Ultra Nightmare runs when you glory kill a demon and teleport under the walkway, and cornering encounters with the tankiest demons out there), outstanding. Health and ammo pickups, demon portals so you never feel cornered in death arenas that you're given time to explore before beginning the encounters- in a way that fits the flow of the game, clearly taking queues from Half Life 2- jump pads to give the shooter a very sci-fi experience. Double jumps and mantling perfectly mesh with the game, impact compensation so you never take fall damage. Secrets hidden throughout every level that you're rewarded for encountering and finding, full of charm and old school references.

*Weapon lineup? Badass for the most part. No reloading, some fustrating cooldowns especially early on (looking at you, Shotgun grenade), but each weapon has it's uses and the game gives you wordless tutorials on almost all of them.
A bit eclectic and some of the mods are questionable in usage (cough cough Tactical Scope/Remote Detonation cough), and the game is too reliant on Super Shotgun towards the end once all the mods are unlocked, higher difficulties are filled with Artificial Difficulty second only to the Half Life and COD franchises. As much as I'd like to try and beat Ultra-Nightmare, I stick to Arcade where all the upgrades are given to you so I don't feel f-ed over by killing myself with the rocket launcher from a blast 15 feet away that would have only tickled anything higher than an Imp. In arcade or Ultra-Nightmare, panic firing the BFG is an absolute must to save your run if cornered, if you encounter a Pinkie in a hallway (a sadistic encounter from ID), etc.

*Technically? Game is optimized very very well, utilizes 95%+ on both of my GPUs despite having a Ryzen CPU with limited peak clockspeed. Gets 185+ FPS in Vulkan (throttles at that point) on 2x 1080tis and a Ryzen 1700x @ 3.9ghz on 1440p, maxed out everything. I imagine 4k is like ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ in two ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ at the same time.
BUT. If you have ANY problems with your PC, the game will absolutely let you know. I had RAM errors from trying to force my 3200MHz kit to run at 3200MHz (maximum for Ryzen), and blamed the Game itself for crashing on startup with unhelpful console dialogs, and Windows 10 for the OS crashes and boot failures (downgrading to Windows 7 out of annoyance) before I discovered the real issue. SLI settings a bit finicky but above all supports SLI much better than other games. Runs on Windows 7 like a dream, ID is committed to using Vulkan and other open source graphics APIs available cross platform.
And the game even supports Linux through Steam Game OS, although my linux machine is a potato that struggles to run World Of Tanks and I didn't test the game there. Comes with built-in Steam Controller support but couldn't get much enjoyment out of it, mostly bashed imps as aiming with what is a de-facto trackpad on the controller is too much for me.

If they had the option to buy an upgrade to the collector's edition I would in a heartbeat. And the sequel coming out in 2019, with Dialogue comparing the demons to Syrian and Somalian refugees?
SHUT UP AND TAKE MY MONEY!

Deus Vult, Comrades!! Hell awaits you!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - Do you want to murder waves of demons while heavy metal music plays in the background? If yes - buy this game.

Overall, doom is a great experience. I clocked in at around 11 hours on the medium difficulty. On sale, the game is definitely worth it.

The music is what makes doom. It sets the tone for your fights, filling your ears with heavy metal music, making the perfect soundtrack to kill wave after wave of demons.

Combat is fantastic. Its an extremely fast-paced FPS. no reloading, just firing all you got at the various demons that come at you.

It does, however, get a bit repetitive towards the end. Kill all demons in an area, click a few buttons, go to next area, repeat.

Enemy types have a decent amount of variation. However, I wish there were more types of demons. As the game progresses, it just throws more demons at you, instead of different types. There were a few "boss" fights towards the end of the game. These were really fun, and I wish the game had more of them throughout the story.

The story is good. I didn't pay much attention towards it, because a majority of it is read in audiologs. I don't mind reading in a game, but in a game like doom where your mind is completely set on where to go next, rather than trying to piece together a story from the various audio logs, it gets a bit distracting.

The length is really good for the price, especially the sale price of $15. If you aren't completely broke after this sale, this is definitely worth your time and money. It is easily a 10 to 12 hour experience. If you decide to go for all of the collectibles, and explore every inch of every level, it could probably double that amount.\t\t\t\t\t
