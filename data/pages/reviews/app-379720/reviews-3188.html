\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fun over the top game that has captured the old doom feeling very well. The game has fast paced action with some quite parts in between. These are only meant to get you ready for the next hectic gameplay. Standing still is NOT an option while fighting and the melee is brutal and efficient. It is a joy to see how the old enemies made the transition from sprites into these visually stunning monters with their movements and attacks. A personal favorite are the Hells Knights and the brainspider. A feast of recognistion for the gamers fortunate enough to have the age to boast the fact that they have played the original on a 486DX2 66MHz with 4MB of ram and an S3 trio vid.card 1MB.

So is everything just great?? Nope no really for it has some flaws like the hunting for secrets part. It feels out of place for it requires a lot of map reading and puzzeling to figure out how to get to certain parts of the map. Maybe it is done so as to not only walk from 1 fight to the next but it does interrupt the speed and flow of the game. Adding to that is the problem in case you move to quickly across the map and forget to check if you missed a part of the map. You may well find yourself cut off from secrets not yet explored.... so far for that achievement!
The melee attacks known as glory kills, are a nice touch for it is nice to see the doom marine rip apart a skull of an enemy or brake off an horn and stab its owner to death with it. But these pre-recorded cutscenes tend to get boring to the end cause by that time you have seen all of them a 1000 times already.

In hind sight the game feels like you have seen it all before but with some different textures. A Hint? Very well; the search for the hidden Old Skool Doom parts in all levels......... Any ideas??? If you would say Wolfenstein you hit the jackpot. Here offcourse it is the first Doom levels instead of the Castle Wolfenstein that kicked off the FPS genre. The upside is the fact that these old levels only contain a small part off the original instead of a whole map like in Wolfenstein. That is the only upside to that feature cause searching for the entrances (accesable by levers) is a hell of a job and feels out of place in the game Doom is designed to be.

I had fun playing it but it is very obvious that ID software got its inspiration from the Wolfenstein games that were reworked by Machine Games. Its a shame that the once uncontested champion of the genre is not trendsetting but following the great example of others. If you have played Wolfenstein this game may feel a bit too familiar but if you did not the scenery and gameplay are certainly worth checking out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you play this game for the multiplayer, you're a maniac. The single player, however, is worth every little ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ coin you can fetch from your wallet. This is one of the few quality single player experiences, that make you feel like a true badass. (Did steam censor that? no? who cares.)

So what is the single player? Some idiot in the story used some energy found in hell. Bad stuff happens. Portal opens, demons everywhere. Your job is to kill every single one of them, and shut the portal down. Challenging, rewarding, and ridiculously entertaining.

Multiplayer? Bleh. Don't bother.

And then there is this snapmap-thing. My tiny brain still hasn't made, or seen, a decent map yet, but i expect, that there is some genius in the community, who is currently making a masterpiece of a map.

Still, this is a brilliant game with the single player experience alone. Definetely recommended.

Oh, and there are big guns. These things make the Ratchet & Clank weapons look tiny nerf guns by comparison.\t\t\t\t\t
