\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game took me totally off guard. When I heard there was a new Doom game out I was like "oh... neat." I enjoyed Doom 3 and I was a fan of the old school Doom games, but I never felt like it was something I had to own.

Now, I am converted.

This game is not the spiritual successor to Doom 3. In the words of JonTron, it's Doom 2: 2. It brings the crazy arcade shooter insanity that you loved from the first two Dooms and brings it to 2016. Heck, there's even an arcade mode where you're trying to get a high score.

The game sets an intense tone and hardly ever lets up. You're shooting, jumping, chopping, and Glory Killing your way across each level. Each enemy zone is a skill challenge you have to unravel before moving on. The levels are designed to be visually appealing and fun to interact with. Even the platforming is fun, giving you interesting puzzles to solve as a breather between action sections.

The story sells itself as a throw away, but there's more to it than it appears at first glance. Doomguy never speaks (of course) but his interactions give him a lot of personality. If you are interested in discovering more about the story, there is a lot of lore to be found here. Also, the backtracking can get a little annoying, as can the wandering around to find the next area. Thankfully, those are few and far between. Even then, most of the time you're backtracking to find a secret you missed.

The game was a blast. Most times I only play through a game once on normal mode, but this one I played through again on the next level up. Now I'm playing through again on Nightmare (and can't beat the first level) and it's so much fun. If you don't own this game, you absolutely should.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been suffering from disinterest in games for what seems to be years, with most of modern titles failing to hold my attention through to the end, or for more than a day even.

But this game, this is something different. I have had so many hours of fun playing this, it is fast paced challenging and makes you feel like a total badass after a long day at work, and has just enough story to be engaging yet not overbearing.

It is a worthy sequel to the DOOM, the game of my childhood, it embodies the kind of games I grew up on. It does not take itself seriously, and does not try to make you either. It is just unabridged, pure, unadulterated fun for hours and hours on end, especially it's single player campaign.

I am not regretting the purchase one bit, and now that I have finished it, I wish there was another sequel.\t\t\t\t\t
