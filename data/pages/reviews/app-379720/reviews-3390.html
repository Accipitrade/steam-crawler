\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I was seven years old my father came home from work with a copy of Doom on a pair of floppies. It was the coolest game ever. The reason why I didn't like Doom 3 was because it just simply wasn't Doom (or Doom 2 for that matter). But I can assure you, THIS IS DOOM. The redness, the blue and yellow keycards, flipping the switch at the end of the mission, the monsters, the weapons, it's all here.

PROS:
- It's DOOM, and it certainly feels like DOOM 1 and 2 (and NOT DOOM 3)
- Graphics - I get 60 FPS on ultra with my 2 x GTX970s
- Controls - feels good, nothing to complain about
- Multiplayer is awesome. If you like Unreal Tournament or Quake Arena, you'll like this

CONS:
- Single player is a bit linear. I'd prefer a little more control and wiggle room, feels a bit like CoD to be honest
- Can't connect to multiplayer servers from Mainland China (So I have to connect to Hong Kong VPN servers instead)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe cerebral slaughterhouse has been resurrected after 12 long years, and it's now morphed into a slicker beast. This time, the emphasis is more on pace & strafing than Doom 3, but in doing so sacrifices any proper narrative and removes the fear-factor it's predecessor had. Also, you no longer encounter any other survivors at the UAC facility, or get to wield the Crucible Artifact, making the whole experience feel a little hollow. None the less, hoards of Doom fans will still lap this up as it up's the brutal violence & Satanic sites, fusing it with thumping hardcore metal tunes. The new melee Glory Kills are a joy to execute when you're in a tight spot, and doesn't affect momentum. In it's current state, the single player campaign is a far more appealing experience over multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original Doom made waves in the gaming community not because of speed, guns or gore alone, but because its developers understood what the market needed and didn't quite have at the time. As a veteran Doomer, I have to give id Software props for being able to make that call again. The gameplay here is a perfect match for the technology and tastes of the current era.

This new entry in the franchise preserves the classic arcade combat we've come to expect while adding a few layers of customization to both incentivize secret-hunting and keep demon encounters fresh. It's a reward system like that of the original, but buffed to meet the ambitions of the modern gamer. This is a recurring theme throughout the game; everything feels Doomish in that it's lean and kinetic, yet there is AAA-level substance as well. Not an easy balance to achieve as we've seen with hundreds of failed '90s-inspired titles. Respect.

Add a decent in-game editor to the package and it's a great value for the money. Rejoice; Doom is back.\t\t\t\t\t
