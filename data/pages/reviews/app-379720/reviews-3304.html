\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember I ran a BBS back in the late 80's, early 90's. I was a PC enthusiast and always was jealous that consoles had such really cool games -while the PC had some good ones, most were adventure style games like King's Quest or Ultima - great games of the day, but the PC was lacking high end action ga,es. My BBS was the only local Apogee distribution site, and I remember when my modem spent hours downloading a game called Commander Keen at 2am from the distribution hub. I woke up as the modem initiated its handshaking sequence. And thought, what a stupid name for a game.

Then I played it. Things changed on the PC from that day forward. Thanks to the developers at id Software, the PC was no longer relegated to backwater gaming. The consoles had direct competition - and the PC could be on the same footing. Then Wolfenstein 3D came. While not the first FPS game - it certainly took PC gaming to new heights. Then came Doom - a game that forced many to upgrade from 286 or 386 to 486 machines - and that started the trend of PC Gaming driving new computer sales. It was that important of a game. Many forayed into Networking, or Modding - a game that drove people to the IT industry. Myself included.

Ok, sorry for the mind numbing nostalgia - but I'm sure a lot of people who grew up in that golden age of PC gaming get the same feeling playing the latest Carmack and company installment. A feeling most didn't get with Doom 3. But here is a game with an engine a cut above what's out there (as we should expect from id), and displaying what made early FPS so popular in the first place --- fast pace action, twitch reflexes, not a lot of thought above "wow, I'm really enjoying this."

Doom is an absolute great game for me. Look, I'm middle aged, I have more than a full time job (in the IT industry), kids, and other RL commitments. I've tried to like games like Destiny (which btw, has some great shooter mechanics itself) - but the campaigns were needlessly drawn out in that game. I'm not so interested in multiplayer anymore, mainly because there is no pause button. Doom scales so well to gamers like myself. I can go in for 30 minutes and have glorious gluttony of frenzied hell spawn killing action, and then get back to my frenzied kids trying to kill each other over a minecraft sword real life. But like I said it scales - so when I do have more time, I have the option of exploring a little more of the nooks and crannies of some superb level design. And I'm not even that far into the game - but I knew right away that I'm playing Doom and not something that just bears its name. It has the feel, the atmosphere, the design, and the modern technology the original had in 1993. Luckily I didn't have to upgrade my hardware for this one, because it plays absolutely stunning on my GTX780 with high details. Not a single dip below 60FPS with averages in the mid 80's.

As a bonus, this game scared the hell out of my kids. They walked in on one of my gaming sessions - and promptly ran the hell out of the room. That means I may get a few extra minutes while playing it, cause they will probably think twice before coming in and asking me if they can have ice cream for dinner while I'm playing Doom.

I haven't even tried multiplayer yet, and even if it does disappoint, it won't change my overall thoughts of this game because the solo aspect of it is just what I've been itching for. My only wish is they port it to SteamOS/Linux so I can play it on my steam machine in the living room.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have not yet played the game, just the map editor. This review is only based on that.

I played original doom and loved making maps, building real life settings (like my house, or my parents place out in the country), or setting cool traps with fake walls and really customizing the whole experience...

This snapmap garbage does nothing like that. Basically you can create a map by linking together premade rooms, those rooms you can then play with the lighting effects and sounds, but you are stuck with the basic structure and size. You want a big open space to fill with stuff? Too bad, even that is impossible to do with this editor, the closest you can get is someone elses premade, oddly shaped and styled room.

I've only invested 2.5 hours into it, but so far that is 2.5 hours too many. This editor pales in comparison with the power and ease of the original doom level editor from 20 years ago.

Don't buy this hoping for a good editor.

EDIT:

So I hopped into the game with a friend to finally see what the gameplay is all about... only to find out that co-op is a lie. You can't play through the game with a friend. I don't know how this game was ever compared to the original doom as it has brutally failed.

So don't but this if you want a good editor, and don't but it if you intended to play through it with a friend, you will be sorely disappointed, I've requested my refund (which I have never done with any other game)

The single player aspect might be great... seems to be great from the reviews, but I did not buy this game to play with myself.\t\t\t\t\t
