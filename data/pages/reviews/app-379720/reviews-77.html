Review
Im one of those guys who played basically through every official Doom game. Doom 1-3 and then those weird niche titles most forget, like Final Doom and Master Levels. I was absolutely hyped up for the game due to its soundtrack, action premise and interesting multiplayer aspect. And man did they deliver.

Gameplay
Its mainly a fast action game, where you can go around and find secret collectibles and power ups in form of stat upgrades, weapon and armour mods or runes that add a new affect. The game makes no secret about how many you have, as you can go back anytime and redo levels and see how many and which items you missed. Although there is an achievement related bug that present in even the play station version. If you do rune challenges from the main menu opposed to when finding one in a map, it wont count.

So there are a lot of upgrades and mechanics around the game aside the normal gunplay, which in turn has a couple of weapons who use the same ammo type and have different mods and upgrades to get. Beating the various enemies is so much fun due to the awesome movement and gunplay. Additionally its arcadey but not too much, striking the perfect balance between modern and oldschool gameplay.

There is also an execution system, where enemies blink and you can execute them to get some pick ups. This can be upgraded in various ways through the runes aswell. I really liked this system, mostly or solely due to the amount of different animations you have for each enemy, especially the humanoid sized ones. Game even has two bosses!

The map design is great, making for very memorable spots aside hell. Which in turn, is mostly just random floating islands in a nothingness. Nothing completely obscure and unique like in the first and second doom game. What a shame. The only other negative aspect I might have is the weapon balancing. Due to some weapons using the same ammo, there is rarely a reason to use one over the other. The double barrel shotgun is superior in almost every situation, same with the gauss cannon compared to the plasma rifle and so on. The new weapon you find usually replaces the old one which has room for improvement.

Multiplayer
After the awesome main campaign you might want to try the multiplayer. On one hand you have snapmap, a halo esque forge where you can create and play singleplayer, coop or multiplayer maps to play with people. Except the coop and singleplayer its almost dead, not because its bad though! There are a lot of interesting creations out there if youre into that.

The regular pvp main multiplayer that isn't in snapmap works in the same loud out based leveling aspect like call of duty. The thing that I absolutely disliked about it though was the runes. In almost every game mode you can find demon runes. They spawn every couple minutes, giving a single player who finds it one shot capabilities and is literally unkillable by one person. Working basically like the heroes in modern star wars battlefront. The modes that don’t feature them are great, gunplay is fun. There is even a super hilarious football esque sports mode. The multiplayer is semi dead, you can find players during peak hours easily. Although it doesn’t have anti cheat. You can cheat as much as you want and wont get banned, caused me to find the same speed hacking cheater in a span of playing it for a month. Avoid.

Story
On one hand its a really neat story that kept me intrigued on the other it is adding a lot of questions that never get answered, even the ones that are basically the main questions you have around the protagonist. Although the main story about the mars demon infestation follows the same route basically like Doom 3 and is story wise apparently connected loosely to Doom 1-3.

Sound
I even own the soundtrack as a special 4 disc vinyl edition. Its not only having a really good mix between electric and metal sounds in the OST tracks but also the transition between those is made in a way they seamlessly go into each other. From combat to non combat track towards exploration and new song transitions. Wow. Also is since then a trusty fellow on my playlist for the gym.

Graphics
Not only does the game run on most potatoes it can also be tweaked to look really neat, in addion to the (mostly) memorable maps despite being on a marsian space station.

Conclusion/TL;DR
Seriously, just the main campaign alone is worth its money. The gunplay and movement is just so well made and map design is for the most part really fun and credible. Only hell and some weapon balancing might be the only downside of it. The multiplayer itself is also really fun, wouldnt it be for the cheaters. Gameplay can be extended through a very neat ingame map creator and very fun ideas in every type of gameplay mode. Not to mention the incredible soundtrack, satisfying executions and the fact that it looks great but also runs on almost every system.

FPS that i consider almost a masterpiece\t\t\t\t\t
