\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM, much like the games that made it what it has come to be, is a fast-paced arcade style shooter, that while now in a much more modern look and condition, still holds true to what we all knew playing the original Doom games. Many of the old characteristics, like the flowing movement, the weapons, and the level design, have all taken what was already present, and in the very least, added onto those mechanics.

Now, going into this, I completed the game in about 9 hours, and given how it had an original triple-A price range, I'd have to say I was a little frightened, but now the game goes for much less and (in my opinion) it's very fitting for the price you can get it for now, ESPECIALLY on sale, I got it for 5 dollars, and for the experience I got, it was a pretty decent price point.

Most people that play games in excess use a price point system of some kind, I like to reason that MOST games should be able to double the hours for the amount of money put into it, so for me, 5 dollars, nearly got me 10 hours, which nearly filled the bill, while also providing me an interesting gameplay experience. For those that use a direct 1:1, you would get double the money spent on a game like this, but for those that paid 60 dollars? I could only ever imagine someone logging 60-120 hours if they were either a diehard fan, or were a big fan of the Mulitplayer.

The game is not bad, I am giving it a positive review after all, but the game comes from an Era where "Arcade" was a much more prominent genre, a timeframe where games were more of a pass-time that relished you with something interesting and fun, whereas many games today have serious undertones, complex mechanics, or engulfing storylines; DOOM does none of this, the stakes are as high "as usual" considering how much of an absolute badass your character has always been, the mechanics are simple to be reminiscent of the old games, and the storyline is utterly shallow, there is a VERY faint amount of backstory being provided for the game in terms of optional information, but it feels a lot like you would either need to really already be a fan of the game to care, or the information purely exists to give enough of a foundation for a sequel that they could take it in the direction they want.

The Gunplay is solid, enemy variety is pretty okay, although most enemies are pretty boring in the sense that blasting them away with gunfire does the job 99% of the time, and you're in no short supply considering you're kitted with a multitude of weapons, some of which clear whole rooms in a single shot (albeit at the expense of precious ammo that isn't gotten back for kills with the Chainsaw) and with every glory kill or chainsaw kill, you can restore health and ammo like it's nothing. I played through on the Normal difficulty and really only ever died to not paying close enough attention to my positioning and/or health, situations where I would be blasting the third wave of enemies in a room in a row, and all of the sudden I would be running up to something huge with a shotgun because I didn't really feel threatened at all, only to find myself getting nuked to bits, and that's another issue the game struggles with.

There isn't much tension, nothing is REALLY all that difficult. Most projectiles can be dodged, you always have a way to restore health and ammo, and no enemy does a great job at really killing you quickly on its own. I imagine (Though I am not stating that such is the case) that with each increase in difficulty, the enemies can take more hits and do more damage per hit (artificial difficulty) and realistically, I don't see how that would do anything against most players besides cut down the amount of mistakes they can make or how loosely they can play. I found that maintaining a basic amount of situational awareness while running in circles strafing enemies pretty much did the job on its own, and that would include dodging projectiles and charging bullet sponges alike.

Maybe I'm reading too much into it, overall if you like the old games, you'll like this for sure, you won't find anything that really stands out to you besides maybe the Easter eggs or the Combat itself, and while the combat is a really fun aspect of the game, the "Gore" which is often referred to when talking about the game and what is cool about it, just really isn't all that cool, I mean there are a few glory kills that are pretty nice, but most are characterized by punches or smashing the enemy into ♥♥♥♥, and it feels so weightless it's hard to get any satisfying feedback from it, especially when you can do it all the time and should so that you can get health and save ammo,

TL:DR I'm going to give it a 6/10, almost bordering a 5 out of the nature that this game is supposed to cost 60 dollars for the content you get, I think that if you're going to create a game that heavily resembles an arcade game, that selling it at 60 dollars in the first place at all is somewhat insulting, because even though the game sold well, it started dropping in price immediately, and I think this was really just an attempt at a cash-grab from the players that were so excited to play it that they couldn't wait. The amount of content you get isn't really worth it (in my opinion) and it would've been better to sell it at 20 or 30 dollars and release 3 DLCs at 10 dollars each that added more content to the single player experience or something (and I don't mean to Gatekeep the campaign by unlocking it with money, I mean actually add-on to the game itself). If you like arcade-style shooters that get your blood flowing, for the price it is now, you should get this, put the difficulty all the way up, and go wild.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just got done with the game and all I can say is: DAMN, THAT WAS AWESOME!
This was pretty much the hardest singleplayer experience I've ever had, and at the same time
the MOST satisfying one as well. You can really feel the rush whenever all the demons in the room
run after you and you just blast them away. Doom also has a great variety of guns which mostly have 2 equipable mods (except the pistol and super shotgun). The demons also come in many versions with MANY kill animations. The only thing thats kinda boring is the locations. Most of the time you're just in a futuristic looking base with a lot blood over it. Everytime you enter hell is a highlight here. That's pretty much everything about the singleplayer, but we're not done here

There's also a multiplayer PVP mode and a map creator called Snap Map. I can't say anything about Snap Map since I haven't tried it but I have played the multiplayer and I know that's this is kinda a controversial opinion, but I REALLY like it! People still play it (at least the TDM modes) and the singleplayer gameplay translates pretty well into the MP. If you like fast and gory shooters, you may really likes this as well.

And honestly the Soundtrack is pretty much a reason alone to play the game. Mick Gordon is a GOD. The new Wolfenstein series (for which he also made the music) proves this as well.\t\t\t\t\t
