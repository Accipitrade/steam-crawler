Our generation is saturated with fps games that is promotes regenerative health and "camping like a little b*tch" mechanic. Most people come in to the MP with that mentality and gameplay style and its no wonder that they got owned. DOOM's mp is different in a good way as i promotes smart aggresive play and fluid locomotion. In other words, never stop moving. The weapons in Doom is also really balanced (except the BFG of course but those are rare). You can really own with all the weapons and the lack of reloads also helps to increase the fluidity of the game instead of you just cowering in the corner reloading somewhere.

The multiplayer is really fun and frantic, it doesnt really put and emphasis on your K/D ration so you just go in some matches, frag some dude, get fragged and frag some more. Really do try if after you finish the single player, for me...it is really good way to leat out some steam and just enjoy my day after a long day of stressful work\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHoly **** does this game ever NAIL input feedback!

Every brutal disembowelment, every shotgun blast, every secret collectible, even every machine gun round. You just FEEL it.

And it feels so good. The joy of this game is in the visceral, addicting adrenaline rush power trip.
The back-to-basics simplicity of "it just feels as good as possible" also extends to the story, visuals, and music.Despite stunning visual fidelity, the game is very well optimized and ran incredibly smoothly on my machine (I usually have to sacrifice visuals a bit to get a 60fps framerate on these sorts of games). Literally every aspect of the single player is a blast to experience. The developers are to be applauded.

Didn't play the multiplayer since that's not really my thing, but I do not regret my purchase one bit. I played through the single player campaign twice, fully powered up DOOM guy and found all the secret collectibles.

Bugs encountered: The game did unexpectedly crash every so often. Thankfully, these crashes were only mild pauses in the action since the game has very sensible checkpoints. Also, the menu storing weapon/environment/monster lore text became a bit out of whack on my save file, but that's barely even a nitpick.

Overall, I give this game's single player campaign a 10/10 - It delivers an unrelenting barrage of unfiltered fun, the kind that can only be had through video games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave this game on PC and XB1, the PC version wipes the floor with it. The fluid movement given by a keyboard + mouse make the constant movement and gymnastics a pleasure - I cant say the same for XB1 or via a controller.

Runs nicely on my potato masher* at around 45 - 60 fps at low settings, 1680x1050 full resolution scaling. There are frame dips in certain areas but as I understand it thats standard for most systems.

*AMD Athlon(tm) II X2 220 Processor
NVIDIA GeForce GTX 750
16GB DDR3

Game has a hugely enjoyable single player campaign with around 8 to 10 hours for a first run, lots of additional challenges and maps to unlock aswell.

Multiplayer isn't my cup of tea... bit too Halo/COD for me.

**UPDATE**

Update 6.66 just came out adding all the season pass goodies for free, amazing value.

Updated my computer to a Xeon 3520 quad core with Nvidia 950, Now I can access Vulkan video render the game hits north of 120+ fps... if your card supports it try it!\t\t\t\t\t
