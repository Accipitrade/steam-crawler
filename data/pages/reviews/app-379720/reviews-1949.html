\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM and DOOM 2 were my childhood. I consider myself something of a "purist", too. DOOM for me is what a first person shooter is all about, with its super fast action, light puzzles, tons of secrets to explore, and memorable enemies to rip and tear. I hated DOOM 3, and I don't even like brutal DOOM that well. So what about DOOM 4/2016?

When I saw the trailers for this game I immediately cringed. Seeing Bethesda was the publisher gave me cause for concern to begin with, but I knew in my damn soul it would be a perversion of everything I loved about DOOM. I knew that not even Id could do it right again,

For the next 3 years, I fit. Yeah. I somehow managed to bury all that amazing great publicity and all the praise that got sent DOOM16's way, I occasionally listened to Mick Gordon's amazing soundtrack, but I never actually bothered to pick up the game, never even watched any gameplay. To this day I have no idea why.

Anyway, the black friday sale rolls around in 2019 and you know what? I decide to go ahead and pick up new DOOM, because I was grabbing the steam releases of classic complete anyways, so why the hell not. I've only played a little over 3 hours at the time of this review, but I already know several things.

First of all, I suck at DOOM now. More importantly though, I'm a dumbass.

I'd be lying if I said I liked it as well as I liked the original DOOM... But I'd also be lying if I said I didn't know that was 100% my own weird bias and nostalgia talking. Even being blinded by my love for the original and the countless hours I've wasted in it, I've still had an amazing time so far. It feels brutal, and metal, and heavy, and fast, and everything else that DOOM should be. The weapons are as big and meaty as possible and are pure unadulterated satisfaction with every click, the enemies are iconic, distinct, and still feel exactly like they should(Bonus points for getting rid of all the damn hitscan ones!), and the changes made to enemies only make them cooler and better in my opinion. It still feels challenging in all the same ways old DOOM did.

Do I like it as well as old DOOM? No. But I also acknowledge that I'm wrong.\t\t\t\t\t
