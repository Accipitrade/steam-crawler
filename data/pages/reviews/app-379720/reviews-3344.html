\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a singleplayer game, I love the new DOOM and would definitely recommend it. The campaign is very fun and doesn't waste your time with too much exposition, it thrusts you into the action right away. However, if you're looking for a multiplayer game, look elsewhere. While it isn't bad per se, it feels a bit generic, especially compared to the campaign.

Pros:
-Fast paced, challenging gameplay
-Feels like a modernized version of the classic (in a good way)
-Looks amazing, even on the lowest settings
-Tons of secrets
-Minimal cutscenes/story
-Good variety of weapons, both classic and new
-Great mix of exploration and combat
-Pacing feels almost perfect
-SnapMap works great, and there's already some pretty awesome fan-created levels

Cons:
-Multiplayer is mediocre and feels a bit generic
-SnapMap has some unnecessary limitations (such as only being able to carry 2 weapons)
-Regular deathmatch can only be done through SnapMap (which is limited to 4 players)
-The ending is kinda lame (not that it really matters)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs an old DOOM guy who played back in the old 486DX days and survived the DOOM 3 reboot I was naturally a bit skeptical, however I was blown away by this latest DOOM release from id. The key to a good DOOM game is movement which this game nails. Fast, fluid and precise WASD controls make the parkour elements of moving around the environment a joy. The envrionments themselves are a playground for you to enjoy while madly fragging the demon hordes. Each level breaks down into several smaller arenas with as much vertical movement as horizontal. Note, new levels are quite large and will take time to fully explore.

The classic doom weapons return in top form, but with a twist. Each weapon has two upgrade trees. These weapon upgrades don't seem tacked on or gimicky, but rather they support the unique individual play style you choose. Players who prefer an explosive heavy play style can take several weapons down that path, while players who prefer the boom headshot style can focus on pinpoint accuracy. The new weapons also add additional variety and offer new ways to splatter hellspawn across the martian landscape.

Much like the weapon upgrades, the additions to the traditional green DOOM guy armor (now called the Praetor Suit) add to the feel and play style of the game without feeling cheap or gimmicky. This also touches on the lore of DOOM. This iteration of the franchise seems to be delving into some world building which I find interesting but not overpowering. DOOM is first and foremost about mad fragging, story be damned.

Note: for some reason id decided to change the default map of the shotgun away from number 3 on the keyboard. Old school DOOM heads are going to want to change that first off. The default key map will drive you to distraction.

Update': After several months of fun play I continue to be impressed by the progress id has made with this game. Multiplayer has moved from a Halo clone to an exellent mode in its own right. The snap map community continues to be active and creative. Still recommend. 10/10 would frag again.\t\t\t\t\t

Doom 3 was closer to this line of thought. "Doom" (or Doom 4) so far has not been scary. Where in Doom 3 you feel like a Marine barely surviving, Here the feel thus far has been closer to that of being a Super Hero. (and Super Hero's are not scared of the enemy.) Is this a bad thing? No, not for me. You should just know that this isn't going to have the heart pounding fear and dread Doom 3 (and the first 2 Dead Spaces) gave you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best single player fps experience I've ever had. In the interest of your time:

VISUALS: Stunning. Possibly the best looking fps available.
ART DIRECTION: Hell has never looked so heavenly. every environment is hand crafted brilliantly.
SOUND: SO &%^#ing metal it will melt your face. Sets the badass mood perfectly.
STORY: The plot is simple, but it's delivered in a way that makes it entertaining, and it pushes the game on nicely.
GAMEPLAY: From the beginning to the end, DOOM is a non stop thrill ride made of guts and glory. Fast, brutal combat, and a fairly deep progression system for your character, guns, and armor seperates DOOM from the competition.
SUMMARY: All of these things combine to make an incredibly immersive FPS experience that absolutely should't be missed.

MULTIPLAYER: The multiplayer feels like it was tacked on, and doesn't hold up well at all with the single player. If you are looking for the next great multiplayer/arena shooter, look somewhere else. DO NOT buy this game for the multiplayer.

SNAP MAP: The snap map feature offers a near endless amount of DOOM content with varying levels of quality. There is some great co op fun to be had, and it does add some good value/replayability to the experience.

  • The movement feels as if there were absolutely no weight to the character.
  • The speed of the game feels as if it may be overdone a bit too much, and i feel i would have enjoyed the game much more if it were slowed down just a tad.
  • The new melee system, is just gore for the sake of gore, and not in the "this is Doom, so its a good thing" kind of way.
  • The melee system get way too repetative after around an hour, and you will find yourself using it to clear out entire hallways at a time, so you will see the same animation over and over again.
  • The demons show up in predictable waves. and it turns into:1)clearing a room from 1-2 waves. 2)a bunch of filler in a few hallways to get to the next room. 3)repeat the first two steps.
This is just my opinion after an hour and a half. But, as it sits now id have to say this will be a better game when its at a smaller pricepoint. just not right now.
