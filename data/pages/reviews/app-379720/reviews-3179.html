It took me a couple of hours of gameplay before I started to fall in love with DOOM (all over again!). Apparently, games and gaming develop new ideas relatively slowly, because although I thought I remembered exactly what it felt like to play the first generation shooters, I was initially stunned when faced with a cutting edge, modernized version of the simplistic format in the DOOM reboot.

Like so many have already observed, it's basically fast-paced closed area arena combat - one after another. Repeat. Prepare for the next battle. But as your skills improve, it grows on you. You start to see little things. Like how much the devs must have enjoyed putting this or that over-the-top display of gore and violence into the design. In the end, It's all about the attitude of the protagonist. Anger could hardly be simpler as a theme and motivation, but it just works. Cranking up the volume, tasting blood in my mouth, I charge into the face of the towering monster, tear it to pieces and love it all!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great tribute to the original Doom game. Weapon upgrades, suit upgrades, plenty of secret areas / easter eggs, and hardcore demon killing action with extra gore thrown in makes this a very enjoyable game. It has been a long time since I grinned like a kid in a candy store when picking up a new gun in a game, and the BFG made it happen.

Game did crash a few times, causing some loss of time due to checkpoint saves, but overall it didn't happen enough to worry about.

My GTX 760 can barely pull off 30fps on medium details but its surprisingly playable at that framerate.\t\t\t\t\t
