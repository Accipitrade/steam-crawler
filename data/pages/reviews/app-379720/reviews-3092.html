\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is an absolute masterpiece. It is easily one of the best games I have played this year if not the best. In fact, I would say that it is one of the best FPS games to have come out in last few years.

The game has incredible pacing, it gives you wide range of weapons and then throws hell at you, the FPS elements are absolutely nailed on. The Glory kill system works and it is an excellent addition to the series. The game has amazing soundtrack, every time combat starts, you would find yourself enjoying the breathtaking soundtrack while your shotgun makes meal out of demons. The Game is gorgeous with amazing environments and ambience. It is a complete package. I am not going to talk about multiplayer as its not something I associate with DOOM.

Game of the Year for me. ID software have done it again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very solid reboot of the original doom.

There is story and there is lore, but your character is the sodding doom marine so he just ignores it all and smashes his way through when he can, which is particularly enjoyable. Straight from the get-go you're shooting stuff and within about 1 minute you've got a shotgun to get some real work done.

Combat is very fast paced and keeps you moving and jumping from place to place to avoid the bigger baddies and collect pickups, which is very doom like. The upgrades for weapons, challenges and "rune" upgrades keep you changing playstyles and trying new things and the same with the various enemy types. Keeps you on your toes and makes sure you try all the alternate fires and powerups.

Collectibles and secrets are there, but they are significantly more muted than the original doom, which is a real shame. But the ones they do have are quite cool and some give you access to guns before you otherwise would have them.

I'd recommend if you're anywhere near competent at shooters to go as hard as you can from the very start, it killed me a few times but not to the point of getting annoyed or frustrated. It's a solid all round game and a full campaign playthrough trying to find as many collectibles as you can is about 25 hours plus or minus a few, which ain't bad at all.

Didn't play the multiplayer, so can't comment on that. Still worth it without that IMO.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is what every single-player FPS should look to for what makes a game fun to play, while still excelling at being creepy as fffcking-Hell. No stealth B.S. No pitch black rooms. Just always-running, always-gunning with some double-jumping and RL-fist-fffcking-pumping while you search every nook and cranny for secret spots. This is exactly what you would have expected the first Doom game to be if the original team had a couple of GTX 980s in SLI to play with when they were making the game.

I grew up playing Wolfenstien 3D, Doom, and Quake (I missed out on Hexen, sadly), and this game takes me back two decades to the feel of those early FPSs. I can only hope this kind of action can make it into a new Quake arena type game, even if the multiplayer in this game fell short.

Worth every penny of the full price.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game. Doom 3 was okay, but this is on a whole different level.

The creators have clearly learned many lessons from the success of the Serious Sam series in how to properly create a modern run-and-gun FPS. In fact, the game is basically a version of Serious Sam 3 that has a smaller scale, artistic coherence, more nuanced design, and an element of vertical gameplay.

Although heavily inspired by Serious Sam, the game certainly has its own unique flavor. The combat is fast and intense. The maps are interesting and thoughtfully designed, testing the player's skills on a variety of terrain. None of the guns are frivolous. The metal is heavy. And it's smooooth. Smooth as butter, smooth like the Fonze. All of this combines to make an experience that feels... complete and fulfilling. And that is something you can say about very few games these days.

There are a couple of problems worth mentioning that prevent this game from reaching perfection.

First, the game's cues are inadequate. It is difficult to hear where the enemies are and what they are doing. The enemies do have auditory cues, but the action around you tends to muffle them out. Oftentimes, instead of maneuvering tactically in direct response to an enemy, the player adapts the general and boring response of sticking to enclosed areas while moving in erratic formations to avoid projectiles. This prevents the player from making full use of the beautifully designed maps. I suspect thta this was an artistically motivated choice on the part of the developers. In Serious Sam, by contrast, the cues are so loud and distinct that they are cartoonish. This is good for gameplay but bad for the overrall aesthetic of the game. That said, I'm sure you can make use of the auditory cues that do exist, but it requires more conscious training, which may not occur to the player on their first play through.

The other issue I have is with the projectiles launched by the imps. The projectiles lead their target when launched and fly through the air very quickly. I oftentimes felt like these hits were cheap.

Oh, and the multiplayer sucks. But whatever.

I give it an 8.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t
