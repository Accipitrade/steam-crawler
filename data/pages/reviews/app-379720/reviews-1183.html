\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom has lots of great stuff to offer a variety of players. Fantastic experience all around.

I exclusively played the campaign. No multiplayer, snapmaps, or arcade mode. I finished the campaign, fully upgraded all the weapons, fully upgraded the armor, found every secret (a few I needed help on), and finished every map challenge. I did not upgrade one of the runes.

Everyone should know at this point that the game offers amazing fast paced FPS action. It is a common criticism that most of this action takes place in locked arenas sprinkled throughout the level. I cannot think of any other way to pack in such interesting fight sequences, and so this didn't bother me through the game. The action was even more satisfying than I expected, and has left me wanting more.

Everyone should also be aware that the game brings back the classic secrets. It's extremely helpful that each map has a tracker for how many secrets there are left to find within the level, so you have some idea of where to look. This does come with a gripe from me though, because many maps will artificially lock you out of early parts. Meaning if you missed a secret early on you're going to have to replay the map. The game is very well set up for replaying maps, even in the middle of the campaign any upgrades you get trying a map on your second or third run will be added to you arsenal when you resume the campaign where you left off. But there was always a little twinge of annoyance when I was prevented from going back to explore at some points.

Overall the maps are very satisfyingly laid out (except the aforementioned gating). Though one honorable mention goes to how badly made and annoying the map is for the second mission. Early in the game I began to worry that it would become the norm. Mercifully it did not.

One of my favorite aspects that I was not expecting was the weapon upgrade system. You earn points to upgrade your weapons by killing lots of demons and by exploring the map. Everything I want to do basically. Most of these upgrades are simple numbers improves, not damage or capacity though; they decrease recharge and reload times, allow you to move faster when charging, or other things. But the best part is the final upgrade. The final upgrade is always something more unique and interesting, but you can't simply purchase the final weapon upgrade. Rather you must complete a specific challenge: get a certain number of headshots, kill groups of demons with the weapon certain number of times, etc. Each challenge forces you to try using weapons and approach demons in different ways, and helps keep combat refreshing all the way to the end.

This is one of those times where a game exceeds the hype. It's not perfect, but it is awful damn close.\t\t\t\t\t
