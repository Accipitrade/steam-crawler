However, dont expect snapmap to be any good. its a very limited piece of trash that ultimately will only end up spitting out the same, bland looking maps because people simply can't put enough into it to make it look unique. the limitations on each map are rediculously bad.

Bethesda pulled a quick one. I pre purchased when it was 79.95 USD.

Today it has been suddenly reduced to 59.99 USD.

If Bethesda doesnt compensate people, this will definitely hit them hard.

EDIT: This price decrease has been applied for Australians only. Australians have officially been scammed by Bethesda.

EDIT 2: The price has been reversed to 79.95 USD. While this has stopped them from their ♥♥♥♥♥♥ business tactic, the game is still now insanely overpriced on steam. Strongly can't recommend this game for Australians on steam, buy it retail to get it cheaper.


Since i'm getting tons of complaints about not actually reviewing the game, here goes.

Gameplay(8/10): Fast placed, brutal gameplay. its all there, the demons are rather agile little bastards that keep you on your toes, and the guns feel really smooth and easy to use. There are minor issues, like the map design is a bit plain (Its usually just corridors and an arena, before more corridors) but the fast paced combat definitely makes up for it. Secrets make a huge return, which is a huge plus, doom has always featured secrets, something I felt doom 3 lacked.

Graphics (9/10): The game is beautiful (For a hellish apocolypse) the graphics look very smooth, and its fun to climb up on things and just explore everywhere. In between monster fights I personally like to go explore around the area i've just cleared, try to find little secrets and get everything.

Sound (8/10): The sound in the game is very nice. I do really like the enemy sounds and such, really gives you that feel that you're being hunted. the only reason I give it an 8/10 is due to the fact that the music is lacking. it doesnt have the hard rock feeling that doom 1 and 2 provided, and it could have easily been added.

Overall (8/10): The game is fantastic. if you liked the original dooms, you will enjoy this. its clearly newer, and more modernized, but its still doom and you will most likely enjoy this. If you dont, doom 1 and 2 still have millions of maps you can play.

Extra reviews

Snapmap (9/10): Snapmap is fantastic. its very easy to make a map, but very hard to make a great one. there are some limitations, like on how many things you can put on the map or just how big you can make it, but there are plenty of ways to work with it. Another issue is that it can be very hard to place props on top of each other since theres no clipping when placing them.

There are tutorials for the basic and more advanced things, and theres even actual puzzles related to snapmap for you to play. Strongly recommend you give it a try.

Multiplayer (NA): I haven't played the multiplayer component yet, so I cant give an accurate review. will update this when I do.

Ultimately, the only reason I have downvoted this game is due to the shoddy business tactics bethesda have pulled. the game is great and you should definitely give it a shot if you like doom.\t\t\t\t\t

Good God was I wrong! Other than a few performance issues (drops from 70-80 to 40-55 fps here and there, less in multiplayer) -Hope they fix this soon- The game runs well enough to get your money's worth. The campaign is brutally satisfying. I haven't played much, but from what I have... it just feels right. So good.
The Multiplayer is a mixture of halo 3 and call of duty (The good call of duty, not the new ones) mixing the gameplay from Halo 3 and the progression of call of duty.
This is a must have if you're pc can run it.
Here's a reference.
On mostly high settings (Doesn't change with lower settings) I get around 50-100 fps usually in the 60-100 range with the occasional dips into the lower regions.
Multiplayer has the same issues but less frequent on same settings if not higher.
Specs:
GPU - r9 280x 3gb
CPU - amd fx 9320 (8core)
RAM - 16gb
Win10 at 1920x1080\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy Easy Purchasing Guide:
Do you enjoy videogames?
If yes, buy this game.
If no, uninstall Steam.

This game is it. This is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ it. This is a return of the good, old id software. This is the best game they've produced since Quake 1 and the best FPS in decades. I cannot overstate how amazing this is. This is coming from an old, hardcore gamer who has spent the vast majority of my life playing Doom1/2: I've made levels, mods, sounds, played Deathmatch and followed id since I was a kid. I made friends with a few of the old id developers way back when. I've had Doom2 installed on my computer for nearly 20 years and I replay it more often than I'd like to admit. Doom is one of my favorite games of all time. Doom3 was a huge letdown and we all know it. So, what about this new Doom?

Absolutely incredible. Mind-blowing. Maybe the old guard (Carmack, etc) leaving id was the best thing to ever happen to them. This is oldschool id. This is the id software I grew up with.

The graphics are, hands down, the most impressive I've ever seen. RAGE was visually impressive but there was way too much texture paging and the static environments felt... static. idTech6 (this game) is unbelievable. This outshines Unreal, CryEngine, everything. This is jaw dropping. It looks, in some places, like those fake videogames they show in movies. It's that incredible.

The gunplay is fantastic. Unbelievably good. It puts every modern fps (except, maybe, for the new Wolfenstein games) to shame. The sound design it amazing. The music took some getting used to. I like it, but it doesn't feel like old Doom. It feels more like a strange mix between the Quake soundtrack and KMFDM. It's good, just perhaps not what I was expecting. The maps are oldschool FPS design: backtracking, multiple paths, enormous layouts. A bit overly confusing, perhaps, but a far cry from the "walk in a straight line, see cutscene" way it's been for so long.

Also, seriously, someone should fire Bethesda's entire marketing division. Their advertisements for this game were abysmal and made me absolutely positive that my preorder was wasted money. This game outshines their incompetance to levels I'm not sure I've ever seen before.

I haven't tried multiplayer and I'll update this review when I put some time into it. The singleplayer, though, had me laughing and working hard to stay alive. I'm an oldschool Doom player and this game is hard, brutal, and methodical. Just like Doom should be. This is id software at their prime.\t\t\t\t\t

Single Player is just what you'd expect from any Doom title (i've grown up with all ID software games) and this one doesn't disappoint.

Multiplayer is fast and furious and the unlockables give you goals to achieve while mass murdering your opposition.

Now onto SnapMap, first time going in you are presented with tutorials and various AI challenges (mini logic games) and does a great job of teaching you the map editor and all it has to offer... Which isn't much. My main gripe about the SnapMap tool is that all the assets are pre-made (including the level pieces). It works like a mobile app rather then a full blown map editor. I image this was done for Cross-Platform compatibility reasons, however it severely limits what you are able to do. Only real customization and creativity they allow you to have is item placement.

I'm giving this a thumbs down because of the SnapMap editior alone, the game itself is a good game, but wish I could give two thumbs 1 up and 1 down because the sever limitations the SnapMap tool gives perplexes me as to why they would go this route, when custom maps is what has always made ID games (Quake, Doom) replayable and IMO awesome. Community made maps is where the true gold has always been, and this just extremely confines map makes to a very small box. If you had hopes of recreating great maps of old from Quake 1-4 or Doom titles... good luck it's not going to happen unless the SnapMap has major changes made to it.\t\t\t\t\t
