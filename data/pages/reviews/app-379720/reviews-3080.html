\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSKIP TO THE BOTTOM FOR TL;DR/PROS AND CONS
I considered myself a DOOM fan before this game came out. I wasn't really allowed to play FPS's at a young age, but I always wanted too. Well, my parents gave me their old peice of junk laptop, and it couldn't run ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. What it could do however, was emulate the N64 semi-decently. And DOOM 64 ran like a charm on it. Of course, before I even played that I had known what DOOM was about. DOOM was a game about killing demons, and it was incredibly critically acclaimed. I remember a few years after I got DOOM 64 on the emulator, a friend wound up buying DOOM 3 (Which I didn't know much about). He said he did it cause it looked scary, and when I looked into DOOM 3... It looked like ♥♥♥♥. It didn't look like my prescious DOOM 64, it looked like some edgy teenage junk that I wouldn't give a second thought to. But it still bugged me that survival horror was the genre DOOM ended on... Then came the reboot. I saw the very first trailer at E3, and my reaction was somewhat lukewarm. It looked like it was just going to be Wolfenstein: The New Order, with a DOOM reskin. I definitley didn't understand why people were skeptical, Bethesda has proven themselves with things like this... This game blew my expectations out of the water.


GAMEPLAY:
I don't think there has ever been a game quite like this, that encourages pure run and gun action to the extent of DOOM. This game has essentially taken the old bullet-hellish gameplay it used to have, and updated it. Now bullet-hell in modern engines sounds like it would be weird and clunky, if you can see the bullets to dodge them it just won't look right. But nothing here is weird or clunky in the slightest.

You run faster than most cars in other FPS's, and there is no duck and cover system. You're forced to think fast, and git gud. There's no regenerating health either. If you shoot enemies enough, you'll stagger them (Don't worry, you can still shoot to kill too) which opens them up for a melee finisher. If you finish them with melee, no only do you get a kick ass first person execution animation, but you also get health. If you shoot enemies, they have a chance to drop ammo. Or, you can use your chainsaw (Which has incredibly limited fuel) and get a garunteed ammo drop. I mean there's always the option to wait til the next ammo and health kits you find, but c'mon. That ♥♥♥♥ is for ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.

Speaking of ♥♥♥♥ that's for ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, yes, there are secrets. No, I didn't care enough to find them in either of my two playthroughs. If secrets are your thing, you're going to have a field day. I probably will go back for them, but I was a bit wrapped up in playing the awesome game that was laid out for me.

This is basically how the entire game goes, with new enemies and rooms constantly forcing you to change up your strategeys. Towards the end of the game I felt a little overpowered, I mean especially once the game made me take on two bosses at once. But I was never boerd, even if things were a tad repettitive. It's pure, violent, bloody, gory, messy, game juice. And I want the whole damn bottle.

The only complaint I would have about gameplay, is that the campaign is way too short. It's like a shot glass of awesome, but there aren't anymore shots lined up. Fortuneately though, you can get a cheaper drink in the form of snap-map (Think Mario maker, but for doom). I'm a bit busy trying to beat the game on hardcore mode though, so I couldn't tell you how well Snapmap or Multiplayer work.


GRAPHICS:
I played on the lowest settings because my computer can't handle the grafficks. I didn't even notice, or care. They definitley get the job done, even on low. Everything is realistic, in a completely hellish way. If you're a heavy metal fan I̶ ̶h̶o̶p̶e̶ ̶s̶o̶m̶e̶o̶n̶e̶ ̶p̶l̶u̶g̶s̶ ̶y̶o̶u̶ ̶i̶n̶ this is going to be your game 100%. Everything from the titans bones to the cracking skulls of the imps sucked me in and didn't let me go.


STORY:
Doom Guy hates demons. Okay so there's some junk about an energey crisis, and since we can't use solar power the obvious answer is to harness hell eneregey. And then Doom Guy was kidnapped-- Blah blah blah. The writers cared about the story quite a bit, everybody else on the team didn't. This shows through in gameplay.


SOUND:
For as Metal as the rest of the game is, the soundtrack is surprisingly not very Metal. I mean don't get me wrong, it's still somewhat rock, but it's more synth sounding instruments than heavy electric gitaurs and drums. There was never really a point where I felt like "♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, the beat of the soundtrack just hit me almost as hard as Doom Guy hit that demon!" However, it's still really good. Unfortuneately, there's nowhere to buy it at.

Oh yeah, and the sound effects are good too. If that mattered at all...



In conclusion, this is a glorious comeback for oldschool FPS's. Yeah I'm young, and yeah I still enjoy CoD. But you have to understand, CoD is fun because it's a shooting gallerey. It doesn't want to be anything more than a carnival game. DOOM wants to be more, and it is more. I would go so far as to say, this is the best First Person Shooter of all time.


PROS:
-Gameplay is incredible
-Graphics are really good
-Level editor adds endless replayibillity
-Soundtrack is super slick


CONS:
-There are DLC only achievments (♥♥♥♥ move)


NOTES:
-The game is somewhat short for a 60$ game, it's about 12 hours
-I heard multiplayer sucks, which doesn't matter. I have no intention of playing it.


[A++] - Worth the full price, if you're reading this and you don't own the game: Buy the game right now.


OTHER GREAT GAMES LIKE THIS:
-None
-Okay, Shadow Warrior is close, but it's not nearly as good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the Doom game that every fan was waitting for. Doom 3 left a bad taste in gamers mouths but this one takes the cake. The levels are large enough for a bit of exploration while still being linear, and the environments are beautifully detailed and stylized. The movement is fluid and smooth, the climbing isn't jaring and the platforming element as a whole fits in nicely. Your movement speed feels fast, and is perfect for the game's style of combat. The combat its self is also smooth, the weapons feel very impactful and switching between them is easy. The introduction of weapon mods and the ability to switch beween those is also done very well. The enemys are diverse and unique; they help encourage players to use your variety of weapons and discover which ones work best against certain enemys. They are equally terrifying and cool as the game sowly introduces the bigger an badder ones. The music is truly the icing on the cake. The heavy metal soundtrack works perfectly along side the gameplay. Huge, chaotic combat scenarios are made even more epic by the crazy hard-rock/ metal instrumental playing overtop of it. The story is fairly simple yet somewhat intriuging, but, as its not the main focus of the game (that being the combat/ overall gameplay) its gets a pass. Overall, DOOM is a great accomplishment for id softwear and I would love to see another Doom game like this in the future. --9/10--\t\t\t\t\t
