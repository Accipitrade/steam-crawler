\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very worthy remake of the classic DOS game.

Presentation:

The graphics are absolutely outstanding. Not exactly vibrant, but the horror is there, it's bloody, gorey, and hell looks just like I imagined it would. The level of detail is outstanding. The game is full of visual flair, graphical effects and feels real sometimes.

The game is optimized EXTREMELY well. USE THE VULKAN API! With the highest settings possible on 1080p I crunch 100+ fps on my GTX 1060. Even if you have a low end system, the game with the Vulkan API runs well as long as you have a somewhat capable GPU.

The special finishers are just as violent as gorey as you would expect from doom. Ripping heads clean in half, using their own horns to slit their throats, using chainsaws to rip demons in half, and even so far as to ripping out their hearts and shoving it down their throats with their entire upper body exploding soon after. Awesome!

Sound? The music is jaw dropping. A perfect mix of glitch and djent hammers you during combat and the ambience is spot on. The demons are terrifying and sound brutal and threatening. The weapons all sound loud, powerful, and like weapons should sound.

Gameplay:

Repetitive, over the top, low class, foul, despicable. And it's amazing all the way through.


There's a suprising amount of verticality in the game and for the most part it feels good platforming, especially during combat. Occasionally the verticality can be confusing and can obscure the obvious from vision, but it's only an issue in a couple areas.

Combat is just as invigorating and violent as before. It doesn't get boring for one moment, because the game throws new challenges at you throughout the game. The enemies are all powerful and formidable in their own way. They all have a weakness. Imps are weak and easy to dodge despite their high mobility, Pink demons are very resilient but can be kited. Cacodemons are stupid and only hard in numbers, etc. The hardest demon in the game is the baron, who takes copious amounts of abuse.

The bosses are good and provide a challenge, but sometimes it's difficult to tell why you got damaged. Luckily, the BFG staggers them, opening them up for heavy damage and they drop health and ammo (Trust me, you will run dry and the pistol's damage is so low that you'd be here for days). The final boss is truly something to behold. You WILL remember it.

There's a medium-depth customization to your weapons and character. It's nothing you need to spend hours on, but it can enrich the gameplay and make you plan out attacks. A very welcome addition.

There are plenty of weapons, but your main arsenal is pretty typical of doom. You'll be using the shotgun, chaingun, rocket launcher, etc. for most of the journey, but there's also the high damage gauss rifle and plasma rifle to keep the variety.

Value:

I bought the game for 60 dollars, and now it's half that. That's a very good value.

The single player isn't too long, with deaths it took me 10 hours. It does have replayability, with new difficulties and finding extra secrets and content.

The multiplayer is alright but snapmap will keep you busy. It's loaded with player content and offers fleshed out, huge new levels. You can even build your own!



If you enjoyed the classic DOS game or are just a fan of shooters, please pick this up! It's very fun and is well worth the price. Of course, this is steam, keep your eyes peeled for a sale!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*** Poor/No Support for SLI & Triple Monitor gaming (so far) ***

This review will likely only be applicable to certain users and I imagine the game is great for the single GPU & single monitor users.

Would prefer to leave a positive review, but experiencing SEVERE performance issues using SLI 780s and triple monitor configuration. 80+ FPS experienced in other games with water cooling keeping max temps between 50-60C, so not likely due to hardware limitation.

- 20-30 FPS with all settings on LOW/OFF and playing in full resolution of 4320x2560
- Vulcan API does not allow for 3rd monitor to be used with SLI (2 cards required to run all 3 displays @ 120hz)
- Playing in 1440x1280 works fine, but is equivalent to an 18" screen (portrait, not landcape)

Waiting for a response from support. Will change review accordingly.

Testing shows power usage for GPU1 & GPU2 to be 48% to 70% and could potentially be a limiting factor whereas Valley Benchmark pushes 100% to 108% power usage
