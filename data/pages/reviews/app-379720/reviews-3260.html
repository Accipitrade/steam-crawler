\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have mixed criticism about this game. As much as I recommend it, I think like it's lacking something. The guns just dont feel right. Feels a bit too "halo" like... The atmosphere still just doesn't feel very doom like... The music sounds like some sort of new age generic heavy metal band.

The push for melee kills and the flashing monsters just feels a big strange.

The atmosphere isn't creepy, it's so bright and perky. The level design feels like a playground you jump around.

The enemy design and enemy AI saves face of this game bit time. The mass of enemies swaming you is really fun.

Yet the graphics in motion simply don't look very smooth. You will find yourself often swining your guns around, missing your target in a mess of motion blur. It's awfully disgusting for a doom game. They should have focuses on producing a crystal clear smooth 60 frames per second instead of copping out with the choppiness and motion blur cop out.

Truth is... team fortress 2 looks much better in motion than this game.

I still recommend this game because it is enjoyable as the AI is simply awesome to play against. Yet as for it being a doom game... it's really something else with a doom skin.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t The DOOM campaign is absolutely astonishing. I tried not to get too hyped up for what seemed to be just another FPS game. To my surprise, the game exceeded my expectations, and provided a lot more gameplay than I originally thought.

The game throws you into the campaign and has you "Ripping and Tearing" demons apart within a good thirty seconds. Personally, that is my type of game; one that throws you straight into the action, instead of giving you a whole background. Its fast-paced play style makes for a fun, but challenging game. However, there is Ultra-Nightmare mode. This rage-inducing difficulty makes you restart the whole campaign no matter the level you were currently on.


DOOM's Multiplayer definitely lacks the most of the three. The multiplayer is rather dry, consisting of mainly the same things over and over again. Very few of the gamemodes are unique, warpath being one of them. Warpath is basically an objective-team based game that makes teams follow a point around the map and holding it to reach 250 points (each second counts as a point.) This gamemode is pretty much a moving version of King of the Hill. All of the other gamemodes are found in COD; or renamed versions of gamemodes you would find in COD. The replayability is not high, but it is still entertaining with some friends.

There is another gamemode that is called SnapMap. This gamemode is arguably the most replayable of the three gamemodes. Many people say it is like Forge from Halo: Reach. I agree, but it has more than Reach. You can make your own story mission, or an arena to duke it out in with some friends (or for those without any friends, you can just pick matchmaking and select a map.) I know without a doubt, that this gamemode will last the longest out of the three.

In conclusion, would I recommend this game? YES, this game is definitely worth the $60 you would pay for it on Steam, or the $30 you'd pay for it on ♥♥♥. The campaign is great for the casual gamer, or for the old school DOOM fan that wants to speedrun it. Snapmap is a user-friendly version of code. It will revolutionize how gamers see games. The DOOM multiplayer is fun at first, but quickly becomes stale as you do the same thing over and over again.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is not DOOM. It's more like doom . . .

The campaign is great, probably one of the better FPS campaigns I've enjoyed. That said, it is glaring this game is respawning monsters in chains constantly and reusing the same maps over and over. I'd give the campaing a 9/10. If you are just buying Doom for the single player experience, and not mods, buy it. For anyone else, think twice.

The mod tools are skeletal at best. Nobody can import assets and create their own levels like we were able to do in all previous iterations of doom. The snapmap system is obviously hampered by only being designed to allow 2 weapons per character, 4 player MP (oh did you want to make a CTF map? doom just gives you the finger). Snapmap is separate from other Multiplayer options so it's just a tacked on addition that does little. Mod authors are stuck with around 40 box style rooms that have little customization options. A mod creator can't even place all the weapons in the game in their mod.

Multiplayer is a call of duty / halo clone that feels nothing like any previous version of doom. It feels tacked on and also terrible. They didn't even include CTF in the gametypes. One of the all time classic match types for all previous id games.

I cannot recommend this game, even with the great campaign, it's not worth the money. Had I known about the 2 hr limit on Steam, I would have asked for a refund. It's that bad.

Don't buy this unless you just want the single player campaign and then want to pay for locked out content that Bethesda/id will shell out to you at 40 bucks a pop.\t\t\t\t\t
