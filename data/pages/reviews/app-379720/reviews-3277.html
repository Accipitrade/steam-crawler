\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as I was looking forward to loving this game, I am disappointed to say that it entertained me far less than I anticipated. The game didn't really speak to me with its shallow characters or 'kill this Olivia person because she's bad' story. The whole story was essentially 'go here, kill everything, grab this keycard, come back, open this door, grab another keycard, go all the way back, open this door, kill big bosses, go back and find the automap and collectibles hidden in obscure places' ad nauseum. I felt like each level was rushing towards the end as quickly as possible, preventing me from enjoying the any actual immersive experience of being at the UAC base on mars. Everything felt meaningless, and the end was unsatisfying and disheartening as the entire end level was a lag spike fest.

The game has no replay value. I found replaying levels to find the last weapon mod more of a chore than an exciting experience to revisit a game environment with more powerful weapons and abilities.

I don't /hate/ DOOM, per se, but the game doesn't feel like it was worth the $60 I payed for it. Is this a $20 game? Absolutely. $30? Yeah, I guess you could push it that far. For me, it wasn't a great game. It wasn't awful either. Maybe you'll love this game, though, so I'm not saying it's a total miss. Will I still get the sequal? *sigh*, probably, yeah. The ending is a mother effing cliffhanger that feels like it should be a level transition, then the game does a title drop right in your face and that's it.

Check out Replica's review of this game. We have largely the same feelings about it, but his review is more detailed if you'd prefer that.

Optimisation Note: I'm running this game on a HDD with plenty of RAM and a more than adequate processor with a GTX 760. I let the game pick the graphics settings for me (it's more than happy to do that) and it was fine for the most part, with occasional lag spikes forcing me to quit the game and relaunch.\t\t\t\t\t

Is DOOM a worthy successor to its famous lineage? Absolutely, though exactly what will appeal to you in this game will depend on what kind of FPS you are looking for.

From a single player perspective its hard to imagine someone not liking, dare I say even love, this part of the game. The campaign isnt just good, its the best PURE FPS in a very long time (5+ years). What makes it pure? Its adherence to combat and revealing the story and world through your actions, not through canned cutscenes or meaningless plot devices where they clearly arent wanted. The actual combat is varied with interesting weapons and just as interesting ways to use them. Enemies are more than just meat bags for you to slaughter, the first time you die will make it clear to you they arent just trying to kill you, they are trying to rip you apart.

Suprisingly the game manages to establish somewhat of a story despite trying not to, which might be bethesda's heritage rubbing off on ID. Due to the Doom Marine's legendary status as a complete badass, you get the feeling from both codex entries and subtle reactions from demons such as the Cyberdemon (not a spoiler, if you dont realize that a Doom game has a cyberdemon in it you might need help) almost imply that they've fought you before.

The pacing of the action really lends itself to the feeling of reward when you clear a room or level. When dozens of powerful demons rush you in later levels you feel a sense of accomplishment when the last enemy falls. Which leads me to my next point: this game is HARD. I will admit im not a very good FPS player but I think even seasoned players can confirm that nightmare mode is downright punishing. As a final note to the single player the soundtrack is simply phenomenal. If you dont like metal, screw you.

I absolutely recommend this game. Its the kind of game that you dont see around that much anymore and personally I hope this old school style makes a comeback.
