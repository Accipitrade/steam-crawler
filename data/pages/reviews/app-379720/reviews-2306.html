\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom has successfully captured what we loved from the original game and the brutal Doom mod and gave us something to remember

The Good
  • Soundtrack- This game has an amazing score that makes ripping and tearing through enemies a blast. Blast beats and double bass pummeling you all the while djent guitar crunch blaring sets the mood to destroy all in your path. While just exploring the score has these ambient electrical noises that buzz around and capture the feel that Hell is powering the place around you.When you start fighting the music builds tension and normally all hell breaks loose at the perfect time.

  • Gameplay- Fast paced,unforgiving,and brutal, this game really gives it to you and wont hold your hand. Not having sprint or health regeneration in the game really captures the originals feel while you blast enemies and look for pickups. Audio and visual cues help you know when to dodge and when to perform glory kills. Glory kills are a special kill when the enemy gets stunned or damaged to a certain extent, the player can execute them getting health and ammo in return. They are all very satisfying and cinematic.

  • Graphics- The game is very visually stunning. The environments are detailed with the aftermath of the demon outbreak. Blood and demonic symbols litter the facility and really build the tension. Hells environment is spanning littered with death and blood and it feels like a work of dark art. The lighting and textures all just fit and look amazing.
Needs Improvement
  • Relaunch for different modes- The game offers multiplayer and Snap-Map on top of the single player campaign. The problem is if you want to swap modes it relaunches the game into that mode.

  • Long load time- On launch I feel like it takes ages to get the game started. It's fine after its loaded initially though.This makes swapping modes from Multiplayer to snap-map a process.
Overview

Doom is a fantastic homage to the original game and the brutal Doom mod. The fast paced arena shooter mechanics from the past meshed with the new ones like secondary weapon modes and player progression make a very enjoyable experience. The multiplayer is fun when you can find players in matchmaking and snap map is a nice addition for player created content. Replayability is there with secrets to find, hidden original maps and lore just in single player. I loved this game, it felt like playing Doom again for the first time.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t1300 hours, can it be true? Yes. I stay busy with work/life, but somehow, in a little over a year, this game has consumed 1300 hours of my life. I have played thousands of titles in my time, but this is by far my favorite game. Overall number 1 any genre. I don't play it as much anymore, but I still fire it up from time to time.

My hardware consists of an i9 7900x cpu, 4000mhz corsair ddr4 ram 32 GB, Zotac GTX 1080ti Amp Extreme, and an evo 960 pro m.2 nvme drive. I run a 32" 4k monitor.

Graphics are beautiful, colorful, clear, and have a slightly gritty look that helps immerse you into this game's wonderful threat-filled world. I play ultra settings on 4k and can get 150 FPS. BE WARNED, if you wish to crank the graphics/resolution on this game, you'll need the right horsepower. My pc is overkill for any game currently on the market, but my kid's pc with a 1050ti sweats to run this game smoothly at 1080p. Turn down some settings, and resolution, it is playable on a 730/1030gt. I play it on my ASUS laptop with a gtx 1050ti at 720p, 1080 is lag on the laptop.

I have always loved FPS games. My early titles were the old doom pc game, chexquest (Seriously Fun), unreal, later graduated to games like goldeneye007 on the N64, which was fun but the controller was the most god-awful way to experience a FPS. Unreal tournament for the dreamcast was 2000x better than the PS2, faster, sharper, 4 controller/splitscreen local co-op. The I played the mid 2000s Doom reboot on pc and thought it was *ok. F.E.A.R on PC, of course the Half-life games. Then between Modernwarfare 1&2, Blackops 1&2, I easily played more than I had on all of those previous games combined on either individual game... until Doom.

Hands-Down, this is my #1 FPS. After playing it and hoping for something similar to quake, I could make the rare claim that a game I purchased with high expectations, was everything I was hoping it would be times 100. It is like Bethesda looked into my mind and built my perfect shooter, even I couldn't have envisioned something I would enjoy this much. Blazing fast, challenging, yet rewarding, decently healthy online player poppulation. Similar to call of duty, there is equipment. I prefer the teleporter. Throw a dart-like beecon, hit the button again, teleport instantly to that location. Hit an enemy and teleport, TELEFRAG.

I could go on for hours abou why you should buy this game, but if Multiplayer perfection is what you are after, you have found it. At the end of each match in multiplayer mode, the top three players are put up on a podium and you can dance. Equip 3 dances and use whichever you want. There are hundreds. This small consideration is one example of the attention that was paid to detail by the developers of this gem. BUY IT. Stop Reading and BUY IT. My name is Bucket, and I'll see you in the servers!\t\t\t\t\t

me when i finished it: *soaked in blood, muscles pulsing* AAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH



alright, serious review. This game succeeds in a lot of ways. It successfully captures the spirit of the OG without replicating it. It is it's own game for sure. It combines classic, open-ended, arena-based level design, key hunting, secrets, and lots of other classic bits with tasteful, more modern flares, like weapon upgrades and power-ups. It doesn't get in your way, and it doesn't hold your hand. There is very little in the way of story, but the story that IS there is just enough to progress the game and justify what's happening. It doesn't take control away for longer than like 10 seconds, and it doesn't puke exposition all over your face like a lot of other games do. It cherry picks different elements of other great games, this game is very much a summation of it's influences, and is far stronger for it. The OG doom was inspired by cheesy action movies, DOOM 2016 is inspired by the excellent games that preceded it, and also cheesy action movies. The devs literally cited "The Last Boy Scout" as being a primary influence.

It can get a bit repetitive though, and there are some bugs that are nearly game breaking at times, but those are few and far between. To put it simply, the game isn't perfect, but it's a giant leap in the right direction, and it shows that not all game designers are out of touch. It's fast, it's violent, it's brutal, the soundtrack is GOD TIER, it's just mindless, demon slaying fun, and if you're a fan of FPS games, i cannot recommend it enough.\t\t\t\t\t
