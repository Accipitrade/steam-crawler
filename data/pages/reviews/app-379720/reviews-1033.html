The campaign is legendary, the multiplayer has mixed reviews, but is actually quite fun with lots of customization, and the arcade mode adds fun and extra depth to the game. Let's talk about the underrated aspect of Doom 2016. SnapMap. It's a pretty awesome tool for creating levels/maps/modes/etc. for Doom with all the provided assets. I wish ID would do a standalone release and add additional assets from other games like Doom Eternal and Rage 2. It offers lots of creative freedom and is basically a built-in mod system that allows to simply download other peoples creations quickly and with ease with no additional software needed. I've seen people make little animations with snapmode, recreate levels from other games, and create fun little arenas that allow you to do things you normally wouldn't in the campaign. Please give SnapMap a chance and I guarantee you'll find some great content. Here's a recommendation:

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEasy recommendation for shooter fans, but not a ton of appeal outside of that.

This was my first foray into the DOOM series and I must say, I missed out on some classics. This game has heavy metal running through its veins and the action is both fast and a blast to play. The core of the experience is in the frenzied combat that is a joy to watch and a true spectacle. The controls are very tight and the excellent performance options the game offers you should make this a near perfect gameplay experience.

On top of the combat, there's also tons of collectibles and secrets to unlock. The game very much hearkens back to the old arcade-style shooters and ironically, this makes it stand out a lot to many of the modern shooters these days. It's pretty clear that this game was made with a lot of love and care both in itself and in the past of the series itself.

But if you aren't a fan of ultra-violence and shooting then this game doesn't have much to offer you. The plot, though not necessarily bad, is fairly bare-bones and basic and there's basically nothing else you'll be doing BUT shooting and ripping heads off. This game knows its audience, serves them a five course meal in it, and gets out.
