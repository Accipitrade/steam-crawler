Uncut dumb fun: 9.9/10

What is the most basic form of entertainment in a First person shooter game? You keep on shooting anything that comes at you until it dies while squirting its internals on the surroundings. Well, as to appeal to a wider audience the modern FPS games have became more militaristic and complex that masks what makes the above example as a good FPS game. Features like reload, support vehicles, aim down sights, stealth, support NPCs that kills the enemies for you, reasoning for why the hell the enemies need to be shot etc... These things might be appealing for the setting those games provide but at the end of the day all those gimmicks don't make up for a good FPS and will probably make it look ugly. So why don't just make a game that just kills enemies when you point and click them. That's what just Doom 4 did.

Doom 4 is an arena based FPS where you rip and tear the monsters the game throws at you. The game does not expect neither care that you understand why they need to be shot. Because the demons looks ugly and you shoot them. Its just uncut dumb fun. You can't expect a stealth option or to just hide behind a brick wall and shoot the enemies that are scripted to jump in front of your crosshair. Instead the A.I is so aggressive that if you try to play this game like a typical modern shooter, the game will rip you apart, eat you and spit you out.

Some people have asked me whether the mindless killing can get boring after a while? No, Doom is more that just a mindless FPS shooter. You cannot expect to just point and click to kill enemies at higher difficulties with your pistol. You need to choose the right weapon for the right situation. You will be equipped with a huge collection of weapons upon progressing and the game keeps on adding new enemy types and ramp up the enemy count as you progress. The game forces you to attend the situation and use the correct tricks at your disposal to play the game. There is no breaking of the basics here, you are still just shooting with another weapon based on the situation. Pulling this off is not an easy task. It requires great dedication, love and care from the developers and the game is just dripping with it every moment, which is predominantly missing from most other FPS titles releasing now.

While most FPS shooters just dulls you with gimmicks, Doom just gives you a weapon and ask to do what this weapon is suppose to do, which is simply shooting anything that the game throws at you. This simple concept requires great dedication and thought at each moment of the game's design to pull it off and also while knowing that they are sacrificing a great chunk of audience. Because the modern gimmicks exist as there are consumers who demands it. I am glad that Id software just made an epic moment in gaming history with this one on and I am proud to say that I am a huge fan of Doom franchise\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you made your way to this game's store page, there's probably very little you need to know about it.

Its visuals and atmosphere are fantastic. I honestly think they're still superior to Doom Eternal in a way, as this title has a much darker ambiance to it. Plus, the game is so well-optimized I first played it on a 1st-gen i5 (paired with a 1050TI though) with over 60fps on medium/high settings.

The soundtrack is so good there are memes about every single TV show, film or other game using it. Modern metal done right. Thank you, Mick Gordon.

Enemy design is great, variety is more than enough, and there's a payoff for learning their strengths and weaknesses. Level design? Hot damn, it's astonishing. There's a lot of verticality involved, hidden areas, secrets to uncover. Not to mention the immensely satisfying arenas you'll go through in the campaign.

Now, where this game really shines is with its gameplay. It's just perfect. Doom has always been a classic IP, but can you bring that old-school feeling to a modern title? You sure as hell can. While at first you will feel limited, both towards movement and weaponry, the game's pacing is impeccable. After two or three missions, even on Nightmare difficulty, you get into "the flow", and there's nothing stopping you then.

This is, undoubtedly, a very high recommendation for anyone who enjoys FPS titles. Put your feelings towards the topic of gore and demons aside, this is just an incredible game. My tip? Get out of your comfort zone. Play on a higher difficulty level than what you're used to. It's worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
