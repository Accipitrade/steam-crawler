Within the first 5 seconds after some guy is talking, immediately you wake up naked, kill a demon, suit up, and keep on killing more demons after that; I went from cautiously optimistic to "HOLY HELL (no pun intended) THIS IS AWESOME!"
The gameplay is fast, very much like that of Quake or even the original Doom with a lot more jumping around involved in bigger, much more open areas.
The guns are beautiful, nuff said.
The plot is as basic as the original Doom was, you live on Mars, demons from Hell show up and now you gotta kill them; screw BioShock, THIS STORY IS ART!
I wished Bethesda could've done a much better job at promoting the game before hand when showing at E3 of last year, cuz when you play it (especially on PC) it's the polar opposite of what Doom 3 was.\t\t\t\t\t

Fast, frentic, fun

Like a lot of people, I was kinda turned off by the beta and said to myself "Oh well, if the SP and SnapMap doesn't live up to the hype, I can always refund." Oh man, did I eat those words.

Singleplayer is exactly what the community wanted - super fast paced, intense fights, and contant motion. On UV and up, standing still means death, and the health and occasional ammo drop from glory kills ensures you keep that momentum up.

It's got something for everyone, too. Want to chase down story bits? Go right ahead! (They're more background fluff than anything) Want to explore the sprawling levels? We got secrets out the ass for you! Just want to stomp on some demons, heyo, combat score and challenges! (The more demons you kill, the more upgrades you get for your weapons).

The weapon upgrades themselves can be broken down into basically two categories for non-explodey stuff - a short range upgrade and a long range (I.E. the shotgun's burst or grenade launcher). It's a pretty linear tree to go down, but finishing the tree for a particular upgrade unlocks a challenge for an additional effect - like say, cluster grenades for the shotgun's grenade launcher.

Now, the Rune Challenges are where you can start going crazy. Manuverability runes, weapon runes, you name it, you can mash up to three to play how you like - plus the rune challenges themselves are a little bit of fun away from chomping through hordes of demons.

SnapMap is, so far, pretty sparse. It is day 1 launch, but what maps are there and features are amazing, everything from wave survival, to becoming a Baron of Hell and fighting your way back to hell through an exploding facility. I'd love to see what more can come out of the community as time goes on.

Haven't even touched multiplayer, and probably won't. Kinda burned from the Beta.

Pros
  • Everything you wanted from Doom in 2016
  • Challenging and extremely rewarding gameplay on higher difficulties
  • Massive maps with plenty of secrets to find and demons to fight
  • Silky smooth performance, and completely gorgeous. my rig is sitting at about the borderline of Mid-High range PC and runs between 50-60 FPS with a mix of High and Ultra settings
  • SnapMap has a lot of promise, and what's there right now shows a bright future

Cons
  • Sometimes the game will load the campaign with no sound, probably a bug that'll get patched out soon
  • There's some pop-in passing from expansive areas to corridors, but that's more of an "eh, whatever"

Overall, I'd say 9/10, probably the most fun I've had with a shooter since Wolfenstein:TNO\t\t\t\t\t
