\r\n\t\t\t\t\tid did an outstanding job in this modern approach to the classic fast-paced, arcade-style first-person shooter.

DOOM is balanced differently than most other FPSes. The overarching principal of gameplay is "push forward" combat: demons move fast and can quickly inflict lots of damage, there is no regenerating health or cover system, and your ammo limits are lower and can be quickly exhausted. To offset this, enemies drop significant amounts of health and ammo, often in types you're low in. The result is that you need to actively fight to survive—while there are situations where you can snipe a few demons to reduce their numbers before moving in, you need to stay in motion or you'll get mauled quickly. The ammo limits and fast-paced combat also means you need to be able to make tactical decisions like choosing weapons quickly—you cannot just rely on one weapon for most fights. The end result is a dynamic and intense shooter experience that tests both your reflexes and your wit.

Levels are remarkably detailed and the game plays well even on older (albeit high-end) hardware. On my laptop (Core i7-4800MQ, overclocked GeForce GTX 780M), the game plays smoothly on (auto-detected) medium settings at 1080p, maintaining a stable 40-60fps. Vulkan support helps a bit as well, though not as markedly as it does on AMD GPUs (detailed performance metrics indicate the game is GPU-bound on my machine regardless of whether OpenGL or Vulkan is selected).

One caveat for those picky about DRM: This game uses Denuvo Anti-Tamper. While this is not an issue for most players as it is vastly less intrusive than the old StarForce or SecuROM DRM systems (it certainly isn't an issue for me), it does need reactivation on hardware changes and imposes an activation limit of 5 per 24 hours, which may impact players who switch computers or change hardware very frequently. Edit: Denuvo protection was removed in Update 5.

This game is a must-buy for anyone who's a fan of fast-paced shooters. Recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
