\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinally got around to DOOM '16 . . . and it's one of the best reboots ever made. It takes the original game's premises and brings back the core of them - arena shooter action against lots of monsters who mostly shoot dodgeable projectiles in fairly complex maze-like environments - while solving one of the fundamental problems of how to make a shooter reward aggression rather than running away - by rewarding health and ammo pickups for killing enemies with special melee attacks.

It looks great, plays fast, and with its core single player campaign, is 13 levels of delightful cathartic carnage and destruction. Especially with the absolutely amazing metal/electronic soundtracks that play while you destroy demons like a dervish of death.

There's more to the package - Each level has an unlockable original Doom Classic level, there's an arcade mode where you go for high scores, "Snap Maps" where you make and share other people's maps in a map maker, and a fully featured MP mode - and all of that is gravy on top of the perfectly cooked meatloaf that is the main campaign.

If there's an issue with the game it might be that glory kills do get repetitive as you'll do them a lot, and that most weapons have a clearly dominant upgrade path making some of those choices pointless. But that's really all I can think of and those are nitpicks.

So as much as I hate recommending support of Rodd Toward's Skyrim Sales Emporium Studios these days, I do have to say that DOOM '16 is good enough to buy a copy if you haven't already despite the fact that there's an arrow to the knee joke jammed in there to remind you that Bethesda got your money for it.\t\t\t\t\t
