\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother game I'd give a thumb sideways to.

Beautiful graphics, excellent design, and I actually DO like the level designs despite a fairly large portion being linear in some maps.
The game is smooth and stable.
The story is straighter than a rail shot into deep space off the ecliptic. Not a GREAT story, but not a bad one either. No surprises, but Not Bad At All. (though the WAY the story ends makes the opening for continued story feel more like this was only half a game)

No.

The only PROBLEM I have with the game is something that the developers did intentionally.
They did what a LOT of developers are doing now with the idea of making the game "more fun".
They went to great lengths to control HOW the players play the game. That works for a lot of people, but not all. By effectively forcing players to play a specific way, developers alienate some players entirely. I get it, up to a point, but the way combat is structured in this feels far, far too much like Firaxis tight time limits in XCom that are made to force players to play in ways that some players simply despise.

Doom is NOT a bad game.
While it does cause me some fairly serious nerve issues, I beat it and am glad I played it...
I just won't ever touch it a second time or play the multiplayer or go looking for the secrets I missed because... the gameplay was just... not fun TO ME.

If you like (very) fast paced games this is fun. If you like being in fights so close you can't see what you're punching, this is fun.
If you like to wander and explore this game has a couple of levels that will appeal but most of the game will make you extremely paranoid that you're just MISSING something.
If you like playing tactically or taking your time - you know, using your brain to enjoy a game - then odds are you will hate a lot of this game with every fiber of your being.

Basically: Meh.\t\t\t\t\t
