\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMaybe DOOM Eternal's not compatible with your wallet at the moment. Maybe you'll have already played it by the time you're reading this and just want more modern DOOM. Whatever the reason, you won't be disappointed in getting DOOM (2016). Just when you think you've mastered some part of this game, there's always another demon, another weapon, a higher difficulty (even turning the freaking HUD completely off [which I absolutely love btw]). Something else that, if faced and overcome, will make you even faster. Even more strategic. Even more deadly. For my age I got into properly playing FPSs very recently, and this is one of the games that's given me a sense of mastery therein. I've played it on the normal difficulty, and then a year later played it again, except this time with no HUD. Then the next highest difficulty. Then Nightmare. This game is fantastic at gradually (sometimes in seemingly large leaps) pushing you to be better than you were last encounter, in ways that you can easily quantify and describe. At its best, DOOM keeps you teetering on the brink of annihilation, but still slaying. "I don't know if I'll make it through this level without dying, but I'm damn sure I can." The best gamer high I've gotten since I got myself a PC 6 years ago was playing the last level of DOOM on Ultra Violence, and managing to do it, including the final boss, all without dying once.

You really shouldn't be reading this honestly. You should've already gotten the game.\t\t\t\t\t
