\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I don't normally write reviews, however, this game is something special. First of all, it's gorgeous.......absolutely gorgeous. I played it at 1440p @ 144 fps and its amazing! The gunplay is outstanding, very fluid. The retro doom levels (shhhh....they're secret) really remind me of how far video games have progressed. I remember getting the original doom for pc when I was a kid. My dad used to get copies of games on disk ( hard disks ) from his buddies at work and bring them home for us to play. This is a totally different ball game though. The action is fast-paced, Stay moving or die. The soundtrack is phenomenal, among the best of any game I've played ( seriously, A LOT of deep bass heavy metal and it really brings out the adrenaline in the game. You know s***'s gonna hit the fan when it sounds like Mick Thompson is punching you in the ear-drum). This is not an atmospheric horror game like Doom 3 tried to be. This game scares you by the thought of dying and breaking a nice combat flow apart. It throws so many enemies at you, a few times I seriously doubted I had the ammo to kill them all. Thats another thing, between armor, health and ammo Doom will have you scouring the environment mid-fight just trying to stay alive and equipped. There are breaks in combat that satisfy the exploration itch because Doom is filled with secrets........so.......many......secrets. The campaign lasted me roughly 10 or so hours, your mileage may vary depending on how much of a completionist you are. The few boss fights that are there are nail-biting at first, had to die a few times to get the patterns down ( not really patterns, more like the signals each boss gives before they do vertain attacks so I knew what was coming).

The flip-side to Doom is the multiplayer, which feels a lot like quake with a Doom twist, you get to control demons! Some reward a brute force approach to combat while others require a bit more stealth and thought to approaching a combat situation. There is a beginners arena of sorts for newcomers to Doom's multiplayer, once you hit level 10 tho (im pretty sure its level 10....it might be 5, not 100%) you better put your big boy pants on and get ready to learn.....through dying.....A LOT! It doesnt hold your hand and give you matchmaking based on your level or skill ( at least it didnt for me....I'm assuming it won't for you.....if not, then thats just nonsense and I'm going to write them a nasty email letting them know just how much nonsense it really is but also that I loved their campaign). Most of the weapons have a skill curve to them and each requires their own play style to master ( like any other multiplayer game, duh). I like that the shotguns aren't overpowered, you feel like you're fighting other Doom Guys, its take a few rounds to put them down.

All in all, I'm very pleased with my purchase of Doom. I dont play this game as much as some other ones in my library but I came to the show late. They released the trailer for Doom: Eternal like 2 weeks after I bought this game, if thats any indication as to how late I was. Definitely pick this up if you're a fan of action, great gunplay or Doom in general. You won't regret it.
