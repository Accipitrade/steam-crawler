\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you enjoyed Doom 1 and/or 2 then you're probably better off playing Doom 1 and/or 2.

Doom 2016 is kind of like an Adult Swim sketch, its' close enough to the source material to know what its' meant to be parodying but there's just enough distance from it that the writers have some creative freedom. If you're a fan of the older Doom games then you may not enjoy the new mechanics such as using "glory kills" to recover health rather than finding pickups, the kind-of-samey gore nest encounters and the fact that mobility and agility mean less when many enemies are accurate to the point of negating any benefit. The system of upgrading your character, his armour and his weapons, plus the platforming elements detract from the game's pacing. The respawn system can be difficult, as sometimes it will spawn you after a fight or in a new area if you die in the wrong place, and the feeling of power and strength that the original Doom games provided is never quite attained.

If I try to imagine it without the Doom title then I'd probably have enjoyed it more.

If you're new to Doom and you're looking for a challenging shooter then you'll probably like this game. If you liked Doom 3 then you'll probably like this game, but if you liked Doom 3 then you'll probably also enjoy drinking bleach.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely fantastic game. Glad all the years of them going through development for this paid off. I just wish I had the means to play it when it launched. Got it on the Autumn sale.

Pros:

-The gameplay is superb. It's super fast paced , high energy , quick shots , really tight mechanics. Just a lot of fun. Gives off an oldschool shooter vibe but made with modern FPS standards.
-Zones are usually really huge and you have plenty of space to work with. Some are small but it makes sense for the story.
-Lots of awesome guns. A staple of the series. Pistols, Shotguns, Lazers, Miniguns , BFG , etc.
-AI is freakishly smart in certain situations and will try to group up around you in certain spots. Makes for a solid challenge and I promise you even on Normal , you'll die a lot till you get something right.
-Lots of checkpoints and save spots. Remember I said you'll die a lot? It's not too bad.
-Story line is a lot of fun , doesn't take itself too seriously yet is still very Interesting.
-The game has a ton of collectibles and secrets to discover. Powerups, Model viewers , Lore logs , etc. If you're into learning more about this universe/world the logs are great reads.
-Glory Kills are basically free kills when a demon glows. You can beat the living sh*it out of them in various ways. Even in the air. It's amazing and it rewards you with health and ammo. Feels great.
-Powerup system is nuts. If you're in a tricky situation and you grab one you become a god for like 10 seconds or more. Beserking is ♥♥♥*king awesome.
-Gun/Armor/Rune mods really shake this game up and let you play how you want. There's a challenge system in play as well allowing you to unlock points for these upgrades. I'll talk about that down this list.
-This art team is amazing. I was so impressed by how everything looked , the attention to detail was insane. So many little nooks and crannies to stop and look at it. Everything from high tech sci-fi to demonic shrines and blood pools with bodies hanging around everywhere. Crazy sh*it.
-Goes without saying but the enemies look and respond fantastically.
-The music is freaking bad a*ss. It's just faced paced metal and it's so sick to just run and gun while it's blasting. So good.
- THAT CLIFFHANGER ENDING. Damn son.

Cons:

*Some nitpicking*

-Challenges in this game range from fun to really annoying to pull off. Theres more annoying ones in this game. Like "glory kill this demon on this side , on this leg" or "kill three strong demons at the same time". It's easier said than done sometimes even with mods.
-Hell Knights. They're just so good compared to all the other demons. I'm not saying you can't kill them but sometimes they'll magically show up near you and backhand smack you. It's like a one or two shot kill on you. Other than that they're fine.
-Rune challenges are just "ok". Some of them are alright but the rest are not really fun. The runes themselves are great idea.
-I wish I could pick up and throw barrels. There I said it. Please add this if you ever make a sequel. Thanks.

Neutral:

-I haven't tried multiplayer yet as I type this. But I hear mixed things. Maybe I'll update this later if anybody reads this.

Anyways guys I highly recommend it. Solid game. Pick it up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fine-- it's objectively fast-paced and intense in a specific way that's an antidote to most other shooters going right now, and that alone is probably enough to recommend it to anyone looking for a different flavor of FPS, or anyone who already loves that style of shooter and wants More. There's a lot of extra content too in terms of challenges and secrets that are actually hard to find and making your own maps and the arcade mode and all the rest of it, so if you like the game you'll find a lot to keep your occupied. But writing from the perspective of someone with limited time to play games who thus values more than just gameplay, i would say give this game a pass if you're in the same boat - it's fun, but it's JUST fun. And it's not even all that fun, to be honest.

Numerous fundamental issues start to build early in the game: a game engine known specifically for texture pop-in is blatantly the wrong choice for a game so fast-paced, and you'll be reminded over and over; enemy design that doesn't take into account the environments the enemies will appear in, so the enemies don't stand out from the backgrounds, and moreover the game is too fast to ever really take in their detailed designs; the weapon balance is completely off, in the way that it wasn't in 1994 Doom, and so you'll just end up using one or two weapons instead of picking different guns for different scenarios; similarly, the enemy balance is off too, too many things are Fast And Nimble, hit points seem to have been allocated randomly so some enemies are way too tough and some too easy; some of the weapons take up way too much room on the screen so it looks like you're piloting a spaceship when you pull out your chaingun; the ammo and other pickups look boring and too similar; unforgivably, the game has LESS atmosphere than Doom 3, largely due to a tongue-in-cheek presentation (the UAC hologram guys) that saps it of all seriousness and leaves everything as just set dressing; and most unforgivably of all the game knows that nobody cares about the story in a Doom game and then includes one anyway - a severely half assed one filled with boring, motiveless villains and bush-league writing - thus pleasing neither the people who care about stories nor the ones who don't. Fundamental issues, one after the other, leading to a game that you WILL find annoying if you care about attention to detail or thoughtfulness or story or anything beyond sheer gameplay. Issues that make this game not worth your time if you need art to be thoughtful and inspiring and well-crafted. It's maybe the last time i try a AAA game, to be honest, because it's always the same story of acing the easy stuff and then doing such a thoughtless job of more nuanced things because even the designers have accepted that "it's just a game."

Again, in terms of sheer gameplay Doom is a winner -- every game does one thing better than any other game, and Doom has fast and intense gameplay that you will NOT find in any comparable modern FPS. But that's all it has, and moreover it has fast and intense gameplay that you can easily find in many OLDER games - games like the Serious Sam series that do not have a huge list of fundamental design problems - and even if you specifically like the sci-fi concepts of futuristic Mars bases and demon portals and all that creepy stuff then you are, improbably, much better off playing Doom 3, which has its own issues but at least brought those concepts to life with a very convincing atmosphere (including a much Hell-ier Hell). Play this game if you want more of this kind of gameplay, but not for any other reason - it won't inspire you with its attention to detail, it won't convince you that games are an art form, it won't impress you with knowing when to shut up and when to tell a story, and it won't change your life by showing you a perfectly balanced game experience that could be built upon in the future. It'll just show you why the original Doom is still good today: because it got the fundamentals right in a way that way too few people have chosen to learn from. Just because it arrived later doesn't make it evolution.\t\t\t\t\t
