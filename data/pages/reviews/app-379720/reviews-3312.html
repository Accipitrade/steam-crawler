Rating: 10/10

Single Player

  • Fast and smooth gun play and gun switch menu.
  • Has amazing loud metal music whist blowing demons heads off.

    This DOOM is 100% art, from start to finish on the single player I found myself wanting more. The difficulty levels are done just right, being able to feel like a pure god before nearly being slapped by a demon and shooting his head off is the best. I have never seen so much blood in a game before. DOOM is by far a game of the year materiel, I honestly have no issues with this game I truly love it and is right to be the price tag it has.

    Multiplayer

    The multiplayer was worrying me before release due to how it could end up being on release, however after playing a couple of matches I also cannot find any issues. Bethesda have done everything right in my opinion by keeping the multiplayer as fats paced and action packed as the singleplayr. Whist playing this you can tell that they did not focus on just single player or multiplayer.

    SnapMap/Overall

    SnapMap bring something different to the table anf allows everyone to take part in creating DOOM levels. This for me is amazing allowing everyone to have a chance to create stuff. Not only this but being able to see what people make can keep the game fresh and full of fun. Overall DOOM is 100% perfect I cannot fault anything about it and I look foward to putting in several more hours into the game.

It is absolute carnage and you will love every minute of it, you have to jump, dodge, duck, weapon swap, melee finish, manage about 10 different enemies coming at you from every direction - it's freaking insane, and bloody brilliant! None of this 'hide behind cover' nonsense, you will be constantly moving and fighting. All of the enemies have unique problems you have to solve and when they are all thrown together and rattled around in a can it makes for some seriously high octane gameplay, this stuff is intense.

The difficulty curve is really good, the introduce the right guns at the right times and personally about a third through the game I was like, meh, I don't think I need all these guns... cut to about three quarters through, I NEEDED ALL THE GUNS. If you are looking for the best single player FPS campaign EVER - this is it, and it doesn't rely on long winded cutscenes, the gameplay and level design carries itself, LIKE IT SHOULD!

Nostalgia wise, from the recognisable weapons, the nod to old sound effects, the nods to old art assets (the barons of hell hung up on the walls missing a leg, anyone?) and level designs, the suit... everything, it is executed so perfectly. On several occasions I laughed out loud and clapped at my computer screen, several times I found myself actually feeling a bit anxious with the nostalgia - if you played doom 1 and 2, this is a must buy - they have raised the bar so high I doubt James Cameron could reach it.

I didn't realise I wanted this game so much until I played it and couldn't put it down. They have really sorted out the lore with this one too, the voice acting and writing is actually really good, and they sort of sprinkle that stuff in just the right amounts to keep you interested and pushing forwards.

TLDR;

It's a masterclass.

If you are looking for a first person shooter with a single player campaign that'll blow your socks off this summer, this is it.\t\t\t\t\t
