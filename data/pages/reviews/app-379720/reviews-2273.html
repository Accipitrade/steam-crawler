\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gore, the monsters, great music score (highly recommend you check out video of Mick Gordon on youtube, where he talks about how he wrote the music for the game), action - it’s all there!
I purchased Doom right when I got my new rig and it was a must for me because the game was considered to be THE shooter to have installed on your device. And obviously, the popularity and age of the game speak for themselves. So, it was a no-brainer for me.
Once I had picked it up, I put it back shortly afterwards - the music was great and so was shooting, and the monsters looked badass. But I just was not lured by it. So I would come back to it to play here and there a few or so times.
After Doom, I had tried playing other shooters and also got into multiplayer first person shooters, where, obviously, I was not great. So I thought to myself, well, I would just get bored practicing shooters in the same games over and over again, so I thought to myself that I would go back to Doom and try land as many crispy headshots as I possibly could, to better my skills.
And this is when it all began - after about, one-two hours into the game, I started adding new skills to my Doom guy, upgrading the weaponry (that required to do certain tasks), and those headshots did begin to land better and the story was not too bad! Also, playing the game on “Hard” difficulty was a good and fun challenge.
And next thing I know - I completed the game. It was...awesome! Just go along with the flow, play it for at least an hour for things to properly kick off, and go into it with the spirit of bettering your headshot counts and you might love it as I did. I’m really looking forward to Doom Eternal and cracking this Doom on Nightmare difficulty in the meantime!\t\t\t\t\t
