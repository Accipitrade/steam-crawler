\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, I've played every Doom game made. I like this one but as games go it isn't the best thing ever. It's just kinda average. Perhaps I've been jaded by more modern games and the attempts to do new things. The stealth games, open world stuff etc... But this game and it's throwback feel, while cool and nostalgic, left me feeling like - well, nothing. I played the game, it was technically good, it had a somewhat interesting story, graphically it was absolutely stunning in some places but beyond that it was nothing to write home about. I finished it and immediately uninstalled it. Took me about 7 hours to complete it. At first i really liked things they'd done with this game (* Note - the chainsaw and the BFG were exceptional weapons) but after a while the game became an extremely repetitive bore. Go here, find ♥♥♥♥, go there, unlock that, prepare for onslaught of enemies, kill them all, door unlocks, stop for story/cut scene, repeat. Also, the atmosphere of the game really didn't give me the impression that it should. One would expect that if hell were unleashed in a place that you'd have the sense of fear that would bring about. While I know the character is a badass that is fearless, I think the game should at least be able to convey the dread and fear to me the player better than just blood and guts on the walls and ugly monsters. Which also brings me to the absolute worst thing about this game. The soundtrack - GOD - turn that ridiculous music off! It's not that I hate rock and heavy metal, it's just that I don't feel like it adds to the atmosphere of the game. By the way, I wish game developers would come up with another tactic for music in general in games. Why does the music have to come up loud (in this and any game) when you're in combat. It's so overdone and repetitive. So anyway, you get my take.... Initially engaging, but overall boring repetitive game with a so-so story that does nothing to draw you in. But, don't let that stop you from buying. The game for what it is, is very well made. Fun to play if you like doing the same thing over and over again in different locations. I don't feel bad for buying it nor playing it, but I think it will be something I don't remember or rave about for years to come either.

Graphics - 9/10 (I couldn't give it 10 because the look of hell was more like a desert/mountain than what you would think a place of absolutel evil would look like.)

Soundtrack - 1/10 (hot garbage and adds nothing to the play or feel of the game)

Story - 4.5/10 (story is there but it's not interesting or even critical to the gameplay. They leave on a semi cliff-hanger which had me thinking, crap they're going to make another one of these - ♥♥♥♥, take my money!)

gameplay mechanics 10/10 (fast paced, no reloading, really nice)

gameplay weapons 9/10 (some of the early weapons are just, meh! But the chainsaw makes up for it - loved it)

gameplay enemies 3/10 (graphically interesting but overall just too many and too repetitively used)

collectibles and secrets 5/10 (well I'm not a stupid kid anymore so this doesn't interest me in most cases)

overall 6/10 (better than avg, but not by much)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a full and all out review of DOOM. This review will be divided into three parts: Singleplayer, Multiplayer, and Snapmap. NO SPOILERS

Singleplayer: 9/10

The singleplayer of this game is amazing. The monsters are fun and intresting and welll placed and balanced. There is a suprisingly strong story plot for the game and can be as strrong as you want it to be. What I mean by this is that there is a minor story, but if you explore a lot and find things, it can make the story more in depth and intresting. The characters feel real and realatable beacuse of this.If you want to play the game only for the shooting and don't care about the story, that is fine. If you want an adventurous game with a story, that is also fine. This game suits both parties. The audio of this game is amazing as well as the AI and mechanics. I would have given this game a 10/10 if is was not for a few things.

1. The Framerate would drop and times there were a lot of monsters.

2. The load times between levels were unbareable and very long.

3. !!!MOST IMPORTANT!!! The game crashed on me once. I know this has hapened a few time to multiple people but it only happened to me once. This is unacceptable for a tripple a game and needs to be fixed imediatly.

Multiplayer: 5/10

The multipliplayer is hard to rate. It would be fun but there are a few issues.

1. VERY FEW PEOPLE PLAY MUULTIPLAYER (at least on PC) because of this, I have only been able to play deathmatch, which is pretty fun but can get repeditive. I would like to play other modes but barly anyone is on them.

2. Lots of the weapons are unbalnced in the game. People can run around with the minigun or rocket launcher and kill people easy. They should be power ups or nerfed.

3. There are very few weapons and maps in multiplayer; I think titanfall had more weapons than doom. It is stupid that they are coming later with a $40 DLC pack. They heavly efficised the multiplayer on Doom yet have it limited?
BUT I have to give doom props for the amount of weapon and character cousiminization(look wize). There is a lot to cousimize your character and weapons with in Doom.

Snapmap; 8/10

The snapmap feature in Doom is to where players can make their own levels and maps to share with the community. The map making feature is very cool and awesome (10/10) but what puts snapmap down the board is how confusing it is. They need to reorginise the map finding features and put a tutorial on map making.



So with all this said, should you buy it?

Yes BUT only when it goes on sale for the time being. This game is currently only woth $40 in its state and that is being nice. The singleplayer is not worth $60 even though of how good it is. The singleplayer can take you 10 hours to beat causually or 2 hours if you speed run it. There are lots of secrets to look for in it so that can help you extend its playtime but thats still not enough. The singleplayer is a perfect time length its just the other two feature need to be worked on.

I will update this review if doom changes in the future but for now in total id give it a 7.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t
