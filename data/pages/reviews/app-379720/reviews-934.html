\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEncountered some minor startup issues, something to do with an HDMI adapter I have, but after solving that, this game is an absolute STEAL when it goes on sale. got it for 6 bucks! Controls felt good, you move where you’re supposed to, none of that slippy sloppy “gotta wait for the animation to start/stop” bs, aim sensitivity was on point immediately, power scaling makes you feel like you’re growing but you have to earn it, not by grinding, but simply by just playing the game more. overall it just feels like a good game. It’s a great mix of using classic video game stuff that you can intuit, like “oh that looks like somewhere I can climb to unlock a secret” and it actually is every time, but also, on the other hand, it mixes things up and subverts a lot of your expectations with new enemies/game mechanics and various things that don’t go as you expect. You feel powerful in every animation, even opening a door gives me this like rush of this like badass adrenaline almost. You have a massive toolkit to use in different situations that you have to use effectively, and if you don’t the game will punish you HARD. I set the game to “Hurt Me Plenty” which is their mid, unaltered difficulty, and having never played the game I feel at odds with every encounter I come across. The places you go are like if Dark Souls and Portal both got a humongous makeover. The labs like Portal and such are alive and have storytelling within them, the Hellscapes are terrifying and desolate like DS, but they still have color and pop in the shapes and shading of everything and it feels alive. It’s a serious banger of a game in every aspect.\t\t\t\t\t
