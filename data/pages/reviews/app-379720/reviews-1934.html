\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who has played every single Doom iteration up to this point, my first few hours with this title were a real disappointment. The game has utterly dreary and weak map design; dull generic environments that I feel I've played through far too many times before and gun-play that doesn't feel anywhere near reactionary enough. Monsters will barely register shots, there are few audio cues and simply put, the combat doesn't feel at all satisfying. The shotgun is just pathetic for instance and simply doesn't pack enough wallop. Glory kills is a travesty of a concept and doesn't belong in doom. Taking away control from the player simply to register "cinematic visceral murder" is flash and all but isn't justifiable and forcing the player to rely upon it for health gain is an unfortunate design decision.

The soundtrack is not doom at all and isn't memorable in the slightest. Compared to the original games it's just not in the same class of fun. Doom 3 was also the better game; its atmosphere and carefully crafted design was certainly for me, far more satisfying.

If I wanted to play an FPS with RPG leveling elements, I'd play Fallout New Vegas. The token item level up features do not interest me in a doom title. I want to play Doom; not a game masquerading as something else or pretending to be "hip and modern".

I'll persist and finish the campaign; but I was close to refunding this as it has been quite disappointing.\t\t\t\t\t
