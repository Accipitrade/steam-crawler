\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:
- Glory kills, chainsaw and berserker powerup are possibly the most satisfying things you'll ever do in your life
- Weapons and mods are freakin' sweet, both performance-wise and visually
- You don't duck for cover to regenerate. You split demon heads in half to regenerate.
- When you run out of ammo, you don't fall back to resupply. You dice demons with chainsaw to resupply.
- I did not even care for a story to begin with but this one is well done and told
- Doomguys personality is awesome

The bad:
- Base game is missing any sort of coop mode. I mean the only thing better than slaying demon hordes would be slaying demon hordes with your homies. Learn to make coop Bethesda, Jesus!
- Remember how in old Doom you'd occasionally have a huge room just filled with absurd number of cacodemons and nothing else and you'd just strafeshoot the ♥♥♥♥ outta that swarm until your fingers hurt and ammo ran out? There are huge, satisfying fights in arenas here, yes, but it's not quite the same thing.
- Remember how in old Doom you'd get ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ lost for hours and just looked at the map like dude what is this ♥♥♥♥? Well, it's not THAT bad but it's there.
- Multiplayer. Cool unlockables, mediocre gameplay.

Overall:
Bethesda went far beyond my expectations with the new Doom. If I were to make an all-time top FPS list based solely on the action quality, Doom would definitely go in top 10. Otherwise the game could have had a higher replay value if multiplayer were different and coop were at least there...\t\t\t\t\t
