\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRestarting Doom turned out to be a pleasant surprise and one of the best (if not the best) shooters in recent years. The entertainment here is created not by pre-prescribed scenes, but by a huge number of opportunities in battle, and it is precisely because of this that there is a desire to either completely replay the game, or to launch individual levels and once again shoot at demons. The developers from id did not come up with anything special, but simply offered the audience an exciting shooter game about shooting at crowds of enemies, and they did great. It is a pity that the multiplayer failed and the support of the game will be associated with it, but we hope that Bethesda will change its plans.

Pros: excellent single player campaign in the spirit of the classic parts of the series; huge amount of secrets; very high replay value; SnapMap Editor offers many features, including cooperative missions.
Cons: near the end of the game, finishing off is annoying; multiplayer offers nothing new, and the design of the cards in it is depressing; in the future it is planned to release only add-ons related to online.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom put this genre on the map, and this is a worthy reincarnation of the brilliant original. It has received careful updates to game play in addition to the expected updates in graphics. Of course, one expected change is the addition of a third dimension to the maps, which is handled superbly in the automap.

But other game play changes are more subtle. Glory kills encourage a rough and tumble style of play. Ammo is made a much more important resource constraint. An upgrade system introduces a lot more player choice into how your powers increase over time as opposed to the simple "I got a new weapon." system from the original Doom. The enemies are also a lot more intelligent than the original, which makes the game a lot more interesting.

Also, I play this game on Linux through Proton, and it works beautifully. :-)

One complaint I have is that permanent upgrades come from discovering secrets. I really dislike it when games do this. Secrets should be rewarded with temporary bonuses or bonuses (like artwork reveals or achievements) that are not part of game play. Permanent upgrades don't have to be forced on the player, but they should never be hard to find.

But, the overall quality of this game is very high, and so that one complaint doesn't earn a down vote from me.\t\t\t\t\t
