\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 3 we waited for.

This game plays nothing like original Doom 1/2. Doesn't matter, because it plays great.
For me the most amazing thing is how well thought over everything is. All these stats, weapon upgrades and such made me thing "no way in hell they will fit" and yet they did.
Squeezing healthpacks out of enemies made plinking from behind cover pointless. Instead it rewards charging ahead and staying close to enemies, while avoiding their attacks.
Monsters redesign is another beautiful thing. They look freaky, both creepy and hillarious. Love it. Enemies are less numerous, but tougher, faster and smarter.
Maps are big, and worth exploring. Earlier new guns, secrets and collectibles wait for someone to find them.
Story is simple, but sweet and full of fanservice for Doom fans. During entire game there were maybe 3 cutscenes that lasted more than 5 seconds. I approve, I'm here to play game, not to watch a movie and play QTEs (yes, I'm looking at you Wolfenstein 2014).
One more thing: optimalization: game ran with no issues on 2500k and gtx 770. Not max details, mind you, but postprocessing effects make it look worse anyway.

What I have to complain about is lack of New Game +. Sure, you can replay levels, but they don't get any harder, actually easier, since you'll have more unlocks and upgrades. Game sure could use mod support as well, but at this point I doubt it will ever happen.\t\t\t\t\t

The Pinnacle of FPS

One of the longest more linear campaigns I have played in my time. Together I got 15 hours out of Ultra-Violence, and it was perfect to keep me engaged throughout, yet feeling satisfied when I finished. I really enjoyed the pacing, with a good mix of story, parkour, easy engagements, tough engagments and bosses. I strongly recommend you play on Ultra-Violence if you can handle it, because it gets boring and leaves little room to be creative on lower difficulties. So many awesome and detailed enemies, where executions never got boring. Executions are meaty and delicious, but personally I would wish that the demons expressed more despair when executed. Then again, it is probably difficult to do on a beast where their only face muscles are their jaws.

The weapons are very varied, and all feel incredible. My personal favorite is the Gauss Cannon with the Siege Mode modification. The models are sexy, and the animations are crisp, like every other animation in the game. I do think the Heavy Assault Rifle is pretty underpowered.

Multiplayer is a lot of fun too! How ever, I would suggest you play Quake Champions for the multiplayer, instead.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best game to come out in 2016.

Doom is an introduction to the franchise for a new generation and came into it like a duck to water without having to change any of the core things that made the franchise an icon of old PC gaming. It's all about killing every single demon in the most brutal manner that makes you lean forward towards your monitor after pressing 'F' on a low health enemy to obliterate them viscously for extra health.
It remains linear, action-packed and simple for one demographic and yet acheives a sense of labyrinthia for those with a more explorative mentality in its level designs that are chock-full of hidden secrets, weapon upgrades and even a decently developed lore for those who wish to know more about the mysterious characters that the protagonist 'Doomguy' couldn't care less about.
This brings me onto the fact that the Doomguy himself manages to be a more developed and relatable hero than many games that actively try to so in such subtle ways. While he may just seem like a generic brute, it's this generic brutality that forms his attitude towards the demons and the people he's working for (to not ruin the moment). He truly believes the only way is to kill them all. There's quite the clever juxtaposition in being the hell of hell so to say; the demons fear the one they call 'the doomslayer'.
The environments are truly spectacular, whether it be the cramped corridors of the UAC, the barren red desert of mars or the raging infernos of hell, you'll always feel at home obliterating demons with the awesome weapons (upgraded to your choice), mobile preator suit, power-ups and glory kills to the appropriate and pumping metal soundtrack responding to your gory massacre.

Play this game\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the only AAA title I truly believe is worth the $60. Despite the fact that I bought it for $30 during a steam sale, I would happily buy it for $60. The developers have put a lot of effort into making the game a seem-less demon-crushing good time. If you take the time to read into it, the story is extremely rich and has a lot of lore behind it that will make your mind turn, but the campaign still provides a great experience for all types of players if you don't care for the deep story behind it. The campaign is also fairly long which will keep you engaged and entertained for a while. The multiplayer, while very different from most shooters, is hellishly fun once you learn the mechanics of it. Customization, varying demon types, and different game modes also make the multiplayer more enjoyable. Snapmaps are also a great addition to the game and the you'll never run out of content from the workshop. The guides are super helpful, as well! Overall the combat is very fluid and fast paced in all parts of the game. The UI is very straight forward and clean. The gaphics look great and it runs great on lower end systems like my own when you enable vulkan. The music is SO underated, too! It works great in all aspects of the game and I recommend listening to Mick Gordon's soundtrack for the game. I believe more game developers should make their games like Doom. You will not regret buying this game. [BFG] 9000/10\t\t\t\t\t
