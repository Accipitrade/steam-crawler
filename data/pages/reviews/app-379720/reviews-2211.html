\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a semi casual gamer who lived through the days of the original Doom series, Heretic: Shadow of the Serpent Riders and Hexen, when there was just a story with your imagination to add to it, I'm definitely happy with this purchase. It has a story with a few small twists but easily ignored. If you want the whole story you have to do a bit of searching for stones that give the back story to Doom and your, as the players character, history. Story or no story, it brings back true run and gun, without the need for reloading and the many ways to die, it will keep you on your toes until the music changes and you can hunt for secrets with throwbacks to the original Doom and upgrades that could spell the difference between victory and defeat.

Gameplay: As I've mentioned, it's a true run and gun when combat begins. Each weapon has two modifications that allow for a bit of customization as far as gameplay is concerned. The lack of reloading will definitely have you cursing swapping out weapon mods in the middle of a fight, and probably die, but once you're accustomed to it, it's a rush of combat from start to stop with nary a break between. On the harder difficulties even an Imp (low class demon, easily killed) can kill you with simply 2 fireballs. Add the other 10 or so demons around you and it makes it hectic, and deadly to stop moving. With a variety of ways to kill, from stunning and using a melee which changes based on the angle of attack, or maybe putting a well placed shotgun blast to the face, I rather enjoy every moment of killing and the non-stop action. It's a good change of pace from hiding behind a wall. Demons hunt you down, until they're all dead.

Story: It's worth listening to for the lore. Hearing the tales of the original hell slayer killing every demon in sight and even downing the enormous ancient demon (the skull in the background of the original Doom cover, yeah...) they tirelessly worked to summon to end their living in fear, down to waking up and hearing how they thought that maybe endless, renewable energy was good enough to risk demon corruption and all hell literally breaking loose. It's tied in to the original and given a new twist and adds a few things to think about. The road to hell is oft paved with... clean, renewable, unlimited energy. Oh, and good intentions gone wrong.

Overall: Fast paced combat, constantly running from demons and then on to the next ones to kill, well developed characters commonly ignored for the next blood bath, no reloading, weapon mods for customized combat and a variety of ways to kill every single enemy with unique kills for each will leave you either satisfied or out for more blood. The difficulty does make it difficult and it's a true run and gun. I highly recommend it. I'm also pre-purchasing Doom Eternal. Thank id Software for bringing back a classic all shiny and blood spattered!\t\t\t\t\t
