\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a game that I have a love/hate relationship with. As the first game that took my DOOM virginity, this game definitely provided that adrenaline-fueled, action-packed gameplay that was advertised and is why I recommend it. However, I can't help but notice the glaring undersides of the game as well. Throughout the game I have had countless moments where I felt underwhelmed and it took the experience down from a 10/10 to somewhere near a 7/10.

My biggest gripe with this game is, contrary to popular opinion, the music to be honest. There is essentially only 1 song that repeats over and over it seems for the majority of the game. The game purposely makes the music scarce for some reason and it actually even felt buggy to me. Sometimes the music doesn't even start when an encounter starts and it randomly happens from getting a glory kill and it just feels really inconsistent and, for lack of a better term, bad. The music itself is actually really good but there is not nearly enough of it and it just irritated me throughout the play through.

My second biggest gripe is optimization.The game when you first load it up is HORRENDOUS. The main menu is incredibly choppy and stuttery, loading screens drop as far as 15fps, the game constantly freezes and take forever to load maps, and it takes a good 30 seconds after loading in-game for the game to finish rendering all the textures. The FPS once you're in-game is fine and very smooth, but everything else just felt like a game from the 2000s. It wasn't until I turned on Vulkan API (which should be on by default btw instead of openGL) that a lot of the optimization issues solved themselves.

My third and final, yet minor gripe with DOOM is the story. While I am aware that this game is not supposed to be driven off story, it is still present in the game and I have to give it recognition. The story, while adding a nice minor element to the game sometimes, is super underwhelming. The game starts off strong in the beginning of the game with the story and it feels so intuitive, but as the game went on it progressively got worse and worse. The ending is an atrocious cliffhanger for the next game, and it was way too short overall.

All of my gripes aside however, I'd still recommend this game. It still delivered what was advertised, and it's a great run n' gun game to blast music and just shred hell demons to. It's not perfect by any means, but I'd say it was worth the money. I'd only really recommend picking this game up for $15 or below though when it goes on sale since it's only around 10 hours long.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRIP AND TEAR!

Doom (2016) is an FPS game that tries to bring something old school to the current FPS games. By old school I mean: less story, more action and less repetitive bullcrap that the genre is full of nowadays.

The Shooting

The shooting is intense and frantic: you are usually locked inside a large arena-like place and the monsters start to spawn from demonic portals non-stop until you destroy em all! You have a variety of weapons at your disposal, such as shotguns, chainguns and laser beam weapons. They are usually pretty powerful and ripe the demons apart, exposing their vicera in a pretty gory and inbstense way. It’s really satifying to explode a demon into pieces or to rip off a Cacodemon’s eye just for fun. Violence and gore are garanteed in this game

The Pacing

The pacing in this game is divide in two moments: One which you are exploring the environment looking for weapons and stuff and Two when the demons appear and you start to run and gun like crazy, making you heart pump non-stop. This game will wake you from whatever state of numbness you are and put you into frenzy!

The Story

The story is pretty simple: Crazy scientists found a way to extract some kind of energy from Hell through a portal. But something wents wrong and the demons break loose. Now it up to you, as the Doomguy, to close the portal and the kick the demons’s azzes.


The Bad Side

This game has only a few flaws. One being that the action might be kinda repetitive and predictable sometimes, as you are usually locked inside and arena to kill the deamons. This scenario repeats itself over and over, só it might get tiresome after a while. Also, I got some performance issues and crashes with this game, but I can’t tell exactly if it’s a problem with the game or with my crappy potato PC…

Overal, this game is amazing and must be played

I give it a 4.6/5.0\t\t\t\t\t

Doom 3 had a better story and the environmental design was amazing. The darkness, creaking metal sounds, Demonic breathing (yet no enemies to be seen) all contributed to creating a scary and interesting atmosphere. Going to outer Mars used to create a completely different atmosphere, heavy breathing sounds from the marine, muffled sounds through the helmet, limited oxygen...

What's the difference between indoor level and outdoor level in the remake? You can see the sky... That's about it. There's no difference other than that.

All levels are basically arenas. Kill all enemies to unlock the doors and go to the next arena. Yes, I understand that they were trying to recreate the simplistic gameplay of Doom 1, however back then there were technical limitations that lead to this design. There are no such limitations now so what gives? Wolfenstein 3D was also just as basic and simple, yet the new Wolfenstein games manage to combine great shooting experience, level design AND STORY into one package. That’s what the original Half-Life brought to FPS all those years ago. It changed shooters from basic arena shooters to first person adventures. Yet the new Doom pretends like Half-Life never happened. What’s up with that? Heck both Wolfenstein and Doom are published by the same publisher too!

I’m very disappointed in id, especially since Doom 3 was so good. I think the IP should be handed over to MachineGames.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game gets a recommended from me.

It's a very good example of how old school and new school can live side by side. I think this DOOM is a worthy successor to the orriginals. Personally, I got no problems with stability; the game never crashed once, neither did the fps drop, even in the most monster packed situations where you will be living through a literal bullet hell. This game has got a very good polish, bar a few minor exceptions, and a very high technical and graphical optimization. It feels butter smooth for me all the time.

DOOM is not the quickest action fps out there. It is very hectic, though. When fighting multiples of enemies this game is very fun. You will need a decent approach to fight them efficiently, too. That becomes especially true on higher difficulties, but you can do casually alright on the lower ones, if you just want to blast stuff. You will be able to see every action of the well animated enemies, respond accordingly and, naturally, aim for the head. If you want to be efficient you will need to choose weapons according to the enemy type you're facing, how far they are from you, how clumped up together they are, ect.,ect. The way this game comes together is a pleasure to play. It is highly enjoyable.

Progression is decent, you have got level secrets--some harder to get, some almost obvious; nothing worthy to write home about. Through those you can get some weapons early plus additional upgrates, which turns your arsenal into a powerhouse of fun. You can do a purist run, if you like, but weapon mods and upgrades really make it just more FUN.

DOOM fullfills all of my expectations and always gives me a good mood. It's a good game choise.\t\t\t\t\t
