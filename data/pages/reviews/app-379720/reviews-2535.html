\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game play in this iteration of Doom is very much like its early predecessors. You have to run and gun in this game. It’s all about dodging, double jumping, and frantic shooting in a room full of demons. If you stand still, you die. Yet, as hectic as it is, the game makes you feel tougher than most other games. It’s a horror game in which you terrorize the horrors. The little bit of narration that exists tells the story of a legendary character, and that character is you! For what little story there is, it is a very cool story.

It’s quite metal. There’s tons of gore. The environments, enemies, and weapons are all very well varied and fun to experience. I couldn’t wait to discover the next level. There’s also a surprising level of RPG elements in which you can choose certain strengths in your suit’s effects and attachments for your weapons. These level ups come about through your performance and your discovery of secrets. I missed quite a few secrets, so there’s some replability there.

The controls are very smooth. The visuals have a lot of depth and wonder. The sounds and music pound into you. Everything is highly polished. I experienced no technical issues.

This is as far from stealth and slow paced games as it gets, and I usually prefer those slower types of games, but I also found a sort of mindless enjoyment in this and highly recommend it for the carefree guilty pleasure that it is. My only complaint was that I would've liked there to have been more. A very meticulous play through of the entire game, looking for secrets, enjoying the excellent scenery, reading everything, etc, took me 19 hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM... where to start...

Doom is a fanstastic game, its unlike anything you have ever played before! I have spent 11 hours in the game and have only played 3 levels. I have taken my time and have died on purpose (many times) just to replay parts I really enjoyed over and over again.

This game in incredibly well optimised, especially for the amount of enemies on the screen at any given point (only losing between 4-11 fps)

This game is so much fun. Trust me when I say ''YOU NEED THIS GAME!'' because you really do need to play this masterpiece.

Having said that there are a few issues I have encountered...

- Clipping sometimes seems abit wonky on enemies (glory kills that are no where near enemies sometimes teleport you 10 steps back to complete the animation)

- There is a map in the game (to see where you are going) however I would have prefered for this option to not be in the game, as finding your way around the level is usually guided by the amount of enemies you encounter. THIS IS JUST MY OPINION!

Other than that this game is truely worth every penny and you will spend many happy hours in DOOM!
