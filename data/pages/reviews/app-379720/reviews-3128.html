\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDooM is very much in the vein of an old school shooter but with modern sensibilities.
Fast movement speeds, double jumps , no reloading and no iron sights are the order of the day.
The levels are wide and open and filled with secrets

The graphics are awesome and the game runs well in general but its the gameplay that makes it worthwhile...
Its fast , frenetic and at times brutal, it kind of reminds me of the original Quake from 1996.
Cover does exist in this game and you kinda have to master the trade secrets of old school fps to be good at this game.
All in all I think DooM is brilliant and I would recommend it to anyone who likes a challenge and test their reflexes in fast paced action.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter finishing the campaign on Hurt Me Plenty aka "Normal mode" I admit that I collapsed with a great big smile on my face. Through and through I had no issues aside from a glitch that put me inside a crate in mission 1, a game crash in mission 4, and a frame rate drop in the snow level. I didn't spend a lot of time looking for loot, nor did I explore every nook and cranny on the first time playing through, as in doing so always takes the enjoyment out of the game for me on the first time running through. I wanted to experience DOOM: 2016 in all of it's glory: a fast bloody good time. I was not disappointed.

The mechanics blended into the game quite nicely too. I found that whenever I needed a breather, I would glory kill a baddie, take in a deep breath and go charging off to blow apart another monster with my Super Shotgun.


After finishing the game, I do wonder if part of my enjoyment is relative to my nostalgia, or just a desire to play a super fast, arcade FPS again, but either way the game hit all the right buttons for me. When I delve deeper into the experience, I know that I'll have more to say by then too, but on a first impression, it was just the shot in the arm I needed to revitalize my love of the FPS genre again.

Is it a power trip? Sure. The game is all about being a tough grizzled space marine who has a grudge against hell. Tearing out teeth to slit a beasties throat, lifting several tons of big turret to use as a man-portable weapon, loud thumping ballistic guns with some good, though generic gun mods like a few varieties of grenade launcher, a scope for your Assault Rifle, a missle pod for your assault rifle, or "Turret Mode" on what is Doom's single player answer to any modern FPS' sniper rifle. It was fun, and satisfying to glory kill every monster I found, and enjoyable to test out the varieties of ways I could "pop the top."

Is it pretty? For a gore-fest, battering demons into a bloody paste has never looked closer to art. The sound quality is top notch, the movement and fluidity at which actions are done, the thumping of the guns and dramatic guitar stings in battle kept me pumped to keep fighting.

Is it shallow? Yes, but in a good way. It cuts away a near to all of the chaff that modern FPS utilize to edge towards gritty realism, like regenerating health, cover mechanics and reloads on firearms, and replaces them with jackpot-styled glory kills, chainsaws massacres, or BFG burn-scars. Additionally the game is not heavy on story aside from "Go do the thing over there now." Game upgrades are not extensive, but add to the enjoyment significantly, with the exception of suit upgrades, and most runes. I did however enjoy the rune challenges that I chose to play, though upgrading my suit or finding the argent energy cells was largely neglected. I also almost never even remembered I had my grenades, I was too busy running circles around "Pinkies" to really give a damn about it though.


Complaints? Sure. The one thing that probably didn't sit well with me so much was the platforming sections. As the all-mighty Doomguy, well... platforming seemed alien. I understand that this game was a love letter to games like Doom and Quake, but still, as much as I appreciated the breather moments, I found I didn't much care for them. It was good to experience the breaks in the tension though through some light (Very light) puzzling.


If you're considering buying DOOM: 2016, know what you're in for: a game dedicated to fast, snappy gameplay mechanics that fuel an adolecent desire to rip and tear, until it is done.

It has great, large maps filled with daemons, fun weapons, great sound, some nostalgia, easter eggs, and some cool new features. These include weapon upgrades and other stuff you can unlock by playing the game and searching for secret areas in the maps.

The multi-player part is only so-so in my opinion, and the SnapMap level editor is a joke if you're a level designer. What DOOM really shines at is the SP campaign. Get it if you like Doom or great single-player FPS games in general. it's worth it. If you're looking for a replacement for the multiplayer action of Quake 3 or QL, go look somewhere else.\t\t\t\t\t
