\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe better Doom game. The 2016 shooter is based on the 1993 shooter, and has some elements from it as well in hidden classical rooms. Doom from 1993 upset some parents at that time, especially the chainsaw. In the beginning of the 2016 version, which is drastically improved, there’s no chainsaw. I felt a huge disappointment, only to discover that it appeared in the next mission. If you use the chainsaw on fodder enemies, you will get enough ammo. The use is limited, though. It’s also important to increase ammo. I prefer to upgrade ammo to health to shield. These upgrades must be found. The weapons must also be upgraded with triangle points. There are scarcer round points, which are used to upgrade immunity against explosions, duration of the classical berserk mode, better map, and grenades (equipment). The hologram is very useful. You can pass the enemies and shoot from a safe angle. The grenades are useful against mirror shielded troops and slow moving targets.

The game has a practical map. You can see where you have been and the direction of the tip of the nose. On Mars, you can double jump after a while. This is how you proceed in the game. You don’t have to find all the secrets. There is no demand of progression. You can die as many times you want and go back to the last checkpoint in the menu to do all the challenges. Some time limit challenges must he found on the map as secrets. The rest comes when you fully upgrade your weapons. If you need 50 headshots, just load checkpoints over again. It comes handy anyway, since you have to learn to handle a lot of enemies before you get the biggest gun. The BFG can be used to handle difficult situations and the point is to not hit anything, actually. But it’s not that fun. To get more health and shield eventually, you may need the smallest gun. Therefore, it’s best to the use the smallest gun often. You’re in trouble if you just knock out as many enemies as you can. It’s best to stagger enemies and press F. You’d better run and slide and sometimes double jump. But don’t let the enemies multiply either. Choose the right gun for the right enemy. The worst enemies, besides bosses, are the (invisible) pinkies combined with hoards of enemies. You must jump twice or slide when they charge. If you encounter just one with a wall behind you, then you can just shoot in the flesh from behind with the double barreled shotgun close up, almost kissing it’s softer parts. Otherwise, it’s a menace in this game (not in Doom Eternal). Run after the smallest enemies. The imps are typical fodder enemies. They’re ideal for health and shield among walking enemies, which are causing almost no harm. It’s tempting to throw a grenade, but that may be a loosing condition.

I recommend this game as a shooter. The story is ok, but not worth remembering: Argent energy, the doomslayer, and the sentinels (guards). Rather learn about your enemies: How to beat them. Then it’s more fun.
