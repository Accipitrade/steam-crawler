1)Great movement similar to that of other arena shooters such as the new unreal tournament game.
2)Amazing backing track of heavy metal that really ramps up the intensity and excitement
3)Great level design that has been clearly thought out which a skillful player can use to clear levels of many enemies

1) Reasonably short game as I reached the last level within 12 hours
2)Difficulity did not feel very reasonable as besides the larger boss battles the only way Doom increased its difficulty was by increasing the number of enemies in later missions which leads to longer and more frustrating check points
This lead me not to finish the game, only getting to about 2/3 through the last level where there was a very long checkpoint

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are a classic shooter fan that somehow made it a year without playing this title, fix yourself. Update your hardware, set your system up for blazing-fast framerates (play in 720p or 1080p instead of 1440p or 4K if it means getting your framerate near or above 60fps), and whisk yourself back to a different time regarding theory of gameplay.

A time when players carried truckloads of weapons on their person.

A time when cover didn't exist.

A time when healing referred to healthkits and not merely hiding for a few secons.

A time when levels might consist of more than simple, linear corridors.

A time when developers encouraged players to tear through enemies with a rapid, divine fury instead of laboriously peck at chunks of enemies on the opposite end of the room.

Embrace the game that embodies the spirit of one of the most influential shooters ever developed.\t\t\t\t\t
