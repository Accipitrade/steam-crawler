\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA genuinely incredible game. It's one of those games that kept me practically glued to it for a week. I have little affinity for the original Doom games - they were before my time -, but I knew this game was supposed to be incredible. I bought it on PS4, and tried it, but to be honest did not immediately see it. The game kicked my ass in its very first combat room two or three times, and when I got past I had a hard time tracking the enemies. Everything was happening so fast, and I couldn't keep up on my Dualshock.

Fast forward to a couple of years later and I tried it again on PC, with a mouse and keyboard, four days ago. It clicked immediately. Make no mistake: the game is pretty hard still, but using mouse and keyboard for me showed how fluid this game feels to play. It may be the most fun moment-to-moment gameplay I have ever experienced in a shooter. All of the weapons feel straight-up perfect to use, and there is just something about the way Doom guy glides through the environment, and the ease with which you can mantle onto stuff. You run fast, and switching elevation levels while you shoot and evade demons is just a joy. Hitting that balance of freedom and limitation of movement and abilities just right is something all developers strive for, but so few manage to hit. Games like Rocket League, the Arkham games (during combat), the Burnout games, and Doom 2016 all feel perfect in how they play to me, and that is some of the highest praise a game can get.

Somehow, Id Software also managed to make each level look interesting while never straying away from the unyielding red, dark red, and dark yellow color palette. It's all hyperindustrial sheen, or organic ♥♥♥♥♥♥-up looking hell landscapes, or various stages in-between, but the environments always look interesting, and the effects are fantastic. The game runs like a dream all the while, which helps to give it that satisfying sense of water-like fluidity in motion.

I also greatly enjoyed how the secrets worked. Each level is designed very cleverly, both in terms of how the combat arenas are set up, and how the various corridors interweave with each other. Due to the difficulty, getting upgrades is very important, and the game gives you the option to have the map show almost every secret at all times. There is still some digging involved to get to some of the collectibles, but knowing where they are makes them feel do-able, and thus more satisfying to me. The only minor issue is that sometimes the levels lock you out of backtracking as a door closes on you, and this will not always be apparent when you step through it. You can replay levels, but replaying 30 minutes to get a single upgrade point isn't all that feasible. Regardless, using the mini-map (and a few bouts of cheating with online guides, to be fair), I got 90% of the collectibles on my first time through. So there is that.

The downtime to look for upgrades and collectibles was also a welcome pause, because during the battles this game just won't quit. I found myself clenching my jaw and tightening my fingers on the keyboard as I desperately tried to manage the insane speed of the combat. During some of the harder encounters, you are flying in circles around the map, shooting at enemies as they flit by your peripheral vision, because you are low on health (which happens often) and cannot afford to focus too much on any one of them. Doom guy is insanely powerful, but so are his enemies. The combat is supremely engaging and rewarding, though I have to admit that (playing on Ultra-violence, the medium difficulty) there were a few battles that really tested my patience, as I died 6 or 7 times.

I considered turning the difficulty down a few times, but then I figured the intensity and joy of the combat also came in part from those enemies being so aggressive and you always being in danger of dying. You can never allow yourself to be cornered, but at times it can be hard to avoid it during some of the more intense scenarios, as you are low on health and ammo and need to engage with enemies to get some of it back. That said, the melee glory kills that give you back some resources are a genius mechanic, as it forces you to completely give up on standing behind a doorway shooting dudes in the head as they pop in -- a sensible but boring tactic that you are often forced to use in many shooters, even the great ones like Wolfenstein and Half-Life.

This game is fair but very punishing when you mess up, and extremely rewarding, but also sometimes a little frustrating. When you do get past these very tough encounters though, it is all the more orgasmic for it. It's one of the best first person shooters I have ever played, and most certainly the most fun one to play moment-to-moment. And everyone who has a mouse and keyboard should indeed play it. However, I hear Doom: Eternal is supposed to be quite a bit harder still, so I think I'll drop the difficulty down on that one.\t\t\t\t\t
