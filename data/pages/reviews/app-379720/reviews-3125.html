\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is awesome.

It stays true to what DOOM is really all about. The story is simple but intense, and the campaign is a non stop demon-slaying rage-fueled KILL EVERY ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ I SEE and SHOOT IT TIL IT DIES damn good time. The campaign throws just enough at you so that it never feels repetitive.

This is hands down one of the best FPS games I've played.

The multiplayer is surprisingly fun too. The gameplay is fast and a lot like the Quake: Arena games.

The Snapmap mode lets you create custom maps and custom game modes or play other maps that users in the community have uploaded. These people get creative and I've played some really entertaining and challenging maps.

As someone who has played all DOOM games (even the disappointing DOOM 3) and most of the Quake games, I have to give this game a

10/10

(I have not touched multiplayer, purely single player review)

Pros +
This game offers (if your computer can handle it) amazing graphics, attention to detail on all levels (Demons, ambiance, gore and weapons), to such a point where the big title games what brag about how good they look are left in shame.

What type of soundtrack do you put to a game of a lone Marine utterly massacring demons horde on a whole different level? No worries, these guys got it right. Walking through hell while listening to a beautiful choir of sirens or ripping demons in half to metal that makes other metal melt, makes this game's soundtrack one of the best soundtracks i've heard; it completly goes in sync with the game and enchances the experience like no sountrack ever has.

The level of gore and action? My Satan have mercy; this game isPURE VIOLENCE. Every level, every room, nearly every minute of the 32.4 hours i've played is me murdering demons on a genocide level. It is none. Stop. Acion. If you want to pay for an action game, well they stay true to what they say this game would offer.

Probably the best and most surprising aspect for me of this game, I thought that it would litreally be one giant gore ball with little emphasis on story and dialogue, more and more violence but the story that this game has to offer (if you explore) and that it tells is far more immersive than 9 out of 10 FPS games released now a days (expection, probably Wolfenstein that made me shed tears of Nazi killing bullets, made by the same company by the way). I would put on hold steming the tide of hell to read about demons and locations, listen to audio logs and stay to witness holograms. If you want to pay for a game that offers everything it says PLUS MORE, buy this game.

Cons - (cannot be a review without cons)
If you buy this game for what it is, a fast paced, arcade style FPS of pure savagery, you will have little dissapointment but if you want to play this as something more, your basically have no impact on the game besides murder; you cannot make decisions, everything is linear (not surprisng of an FPS, though).

The game is very demanding on graphics. I have a 'good' gaming laptop, decent specs that run most games on Ultra but the amount of detail, quantity of monsters and particles (OF BLOOD AND DEMONIC BODY PARTS FLYING OFF) that to play this game with anything more than in "Low" quality, you need a good gaming rig with a lot of memory.

Conclussion:
Honestly, when a game I pay for with my hard earned money gives me everything I want, I made a good buy. This game as a lot of reply value and I have 32.4 hours on an FPS singeplayer without and multiplayer or original Doom maps played (yeah, thats a thing). Most replayable SP campagins are RPG's or sports game but you bet your ass I am going to go to hell, over and over again!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst saw there was a new Doom when an ad popped up on Youtube. I heard the hard rock music play and thought "I wonder what this will be." I watched the ad and was a little bit shocked at how bloody violent it was. I generally don't go for the gory games, but eventually decided to give the free demo a go. I was impressed at how much fun I was having and wished I wasn't broke as hell so I could get the game as fast as possible. I must have played the demo at least five times before buying the game.

Things I like:

+ It's fun. I don't remember having so much fun in a game in a very long time.
+ It's intense, especially at higher difficulties. The rush you can get from throwing a demon to the ground and curbstomping it is incredible. It's like cocaine, but marginally more bloody.
+ The gameplay fits together pretty well and lets you play how you want to play. The weapon mods are pretty cool and varied. I found out that I like to run around with the combat shotgun and fire explosive shots all the time, and the game lets me do that. If I wanted to mow everything down with the chaingun, I could do that too. This helps with replayability too, since there are so many different ways to play.
+ It runs! For such a nice looking game, it runs pretty well on my 4-year old PC. It didn't do so well at first, but after Vulkan was implemented, I'm getting a solid 60 frames AT THE VERY LOWEST SETTINGS.
+ The secrets are pretty difficult to find (for me at least). I may just have the awareness of Helen Keller, but I couldn't find all the secrets in any level. Granted, I wasn't looking for them and was a little busy focusing on the Baron of Hell bullrushing me. I originally put this in the cons section, but I realized that secrets are supposed to be hard to find. Since I couldn't find many of them, that must be a testament to how well they were hidden.
+ Easy to sync mouse sensitivity with other iD or Source games. They're literally the exact same value.

Things I don't like:

- Still pretty difficult to run. If I crank the settings any higher, I'm afraid of frying my PC.
- Apparently multiplayer is awful. I wouldn't know since I don't play it, but beware of that.
- This isn't so much as anything to do with the game, but a warning to "completionists". Be careful of going online to find the locations of the secrets. I did this, and ultimately ruined the game for myself and made it more about the secrets that bashing the skulls of demons. Again, not a problem with the game, but a problem I caused myself. Beware!

Summary:

Game's fun. Give it a go.

Everything I just said is my personal opinion. If you have a problem with that, take your anger out playing Doom.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon’t Under Estimate DOOM.
Upon first glance this game may seem bare bones and simple, mainly because the games most advertised and best point is combat. However, make no mistake. This oatmeal has much, much, more flavor than you think.
Let’s start with the campaign. DOOM’s campaign easily surpasses every shooter I have ever played on all levels—yes, even story. The reasons being:

1. Fast fluid performance on both console and pc.
2. Tactile and ridiculously fun combat,
3. A well done story that does little to interfere with the games rage-fueled, adrenaline-inducing carnage
4. An incredible soundtrack; by far one of the best I’ve ever heard.

This game is not an overbearing shooting gallery of waves of enemies. It’s much more momentum-based. You are always doing something, usually hunting for secrets, health, ammo, power ups, upgrades, weapon mods, etc. In addition to all of this, the game has a surprisingly functional and equally fun level of verticality—in short, platforming in this game feels great and easy to navigate. There is never a moment in the game you are bored and the game constantly gives you the option to do other things than just progress and kill. However, you’ll grow to love the combat so much, you’ll wish it never ends. When it does, you can explore the level and the path of destruction you left behind.

The story is short and sweet but often leaves you asking questions, owing to an intentional design choice to keep it very cryptic. Once you realize there is more to the story than what is given, you’ll want to know all of it. The fun of the story is hunting for connections and putting the pieces together. Basically, this game does not hold your hand in any shape or form, it gives you all of your tools and lets you decide how you want to play whether you want to pursue the elusive yet interesting story, and explore to find all that the game has to offer or if you’d just rather crack your knuckles and kick ass.
There is no true way to totally express this game’s excellence. You must play it yourself; reviews, gameplay, etc., will not truly convey the game in all its glory. It’s like trying to read braille without having a sense of touch. There’s a free demo of the first level—try it! However, keep in mind the first few levels are nothing special, they intend to get people into the swing of the soon-to-build momentum of carnage I have referred to—but it will give you the a taste of the tactile combat.

Now that’s just the story, I can go on with the multiplayer and the SnapMap editor, both of which are also amazing flavorings to this already wonderful oatmeal. So in short, the Multiplayer is very fun and is an interesting mix of old school and new school, the part where people tend to disagree. To me, this makes no sense since it's something different and fun maybe not the best but still very enjoyable. In addition the SnapMap editor is surprisingly detailed while it does have limits incredible things can be done with it and leads to many many possibilities from your own levels, multiplayer modes and even new game modes entirely.

For $60 this is a must play just for the story alone, and with the Multiplayer and SnapMap it just makes it clear you are getting your money’s worth and more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFar better than it has any right to be.

It's amazing how keeping things simple and true to the DOOM 1/2 formulae somehow makes it feel fresh and new. Graphics are especially fantastic given how well it performs.

I'm not a huge fan of the art direction they chose, it seems like they gutted the gothic elements and made it very cartoony, but it grew on me over time. Small bits of humor in there make it clear they were not being very serious about it.

Combat in the game is fantastic, relying on constant movement to hunt demons. And you are the predator here, not the prey, which seems entirely intentional given other narrative bits in the game. The monster designs and feedback are exceptionally well polished and unique. I love the imp fireball charge. The base monster classes all operate very differently, something rare in games these days.

My only real complaint is the audio mixing is just bad. It's way too compressed, you can barely hear barrel explosions, and extreme ducking during events tends to cut out the (excellent) music. Physics effects seem to have no parameters for any sort of impact intensity and the smallest barrel refusing to settle makes continual annoying banging noises.\t\t\t\t\t
