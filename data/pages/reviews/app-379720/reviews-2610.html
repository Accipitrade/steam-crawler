\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis, to me, is unequivacally "Doom". Not the jump-scare, survial horror-esque ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ of Doom 3, but the "I'm gonna tear Imps in two while I shove a shotgun down a Cacodemon's throat while heavy metal guitar riffs play in the background" feeling of Doom and Doom 2. A flimsy, vaugely self-aware story frames a single soldier kicking the everloving ♥♥♥♥ out of horrors from Hell and it's awesome.

I had no idea this game was even in the works before I saw it on the store page, but I bought in on a whim and have no regrets. I couldn't stop smiling at its over-the-top presentation and the nostalgia wave it evoked for the budding days of the FPS genre plus it looks good and plays well. What shortcomings it had to me were its short length and fun-but-kinda-meh multiplayer, but I quickly forgot about them the minute I crushed a Revenants head with it's own rocket launcher for the umpteenth time.\t\t\t\t\t
