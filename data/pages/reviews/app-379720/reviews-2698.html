\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like DOOM, you will love this. A great single player campaign, and plenty of other options (Arcade mode, Snap Map (user generated content), decent, fast multiplayer) to keep this one on your hard drive.

The content is quite gory (it's Doom) so if that's not your thing, take a pass on this game. I personally loved it, as coupled with the soundtrack, it reminded me of many a night spent in Hell in the original Doom and Doom 2. The levels are well done, the graphics are top notch, and the game moves well.

(This line is put into the review because badge completion)

There is a lot of stuff to "level up" between your weapons, armor and runes, giving the game a good bit of replayability even in just the single player mode (the campaign is great, just enough content with very little "filler". I didn't collect all the things during my campaign playthrough, so I will be going back to Mars/Hell to get scratch that completionist itch.

I have only tried a very small bit of the Arcade Mode and Multiplayer, but what I played I enjoyed. I have not even looked at the user-created Snap Maps yet, and I can see where those would be fun, especially with friends. I am no level designer, but the tools for creating levels seem very intuitive and easy to use.

I picked this game up for $15 during a Steam Sale and would have been quite satisfied with the game had I paid twice that. If this is your type of game, I highly recommend adding this one to your library.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best games of the decade and probably the best reboot of all time.

Unlike Doom 3, this one is MUCH closer to the original games and a much better reboot than Doom 3 ever was. All the weapons and almost all of the enemies from Doom1&2 return (along with a few new ones!) and while for the most part they're implemented in ways you'd expect, Doom 2016 brings some fresh ideas to the table. All weapons now have 3 firing modes, enemies have new looks, attacks and patterns, you can find upgrades for your guns (most of which are fantastic, but a few are kinda meh) and even for Doomguy himself, secrets now reward you with unlockable extras & upgrade points and even the story is good! You now also have an excellent 3D map, which nullifies all chances of the player ever getting lost even without any upgrades.

One of the most fun new additions is how much more vertical Doom has become (with one level being a giant tower you have to climb and it's extremely fun). You can jump (!) and climb ledges (!!) and that's not only used for platforming, but it is also used extensively in combat! Surrounded by imps? A Pinky is charging at you? Why dodge when you can climb higher! It adds countless ways to aproach the combat of the game, especially when not two levels look the same. Every level throws something different at you and the game never loses steam. Unlike Doom 3, the boss battles are outstanding and very difficult.

But this is Doom, I hear you say! It's good that they nailed the gameplay and look of Doom with some new ideas, but what about the violence and gore that Doom is so well known for? And what about Hell? There was barely any Hell in Doom 3! And did you say that the story is GOOD in DOOM?! Slow down, friend, we'll get to those very soon... Sit back and relax, there's still much to be told.

Doom 2016 makes Mortal Kombat look kid-friendly. It's got blood and guts all over the place, you can pull off Fatalities on ALL the enemies (Yes, ALL of them!) and they're actually different depending on what part of the body you target! And yes, they're GORY as hell! The chainsaw might be my favorite weapon in the game, I cannot describe how satisfying it is to cut up demons while their insides are being sprayed all over the place and I hear the glorious sounds of flesh being sliced and bone being crushed. Hands down, the best chainsaw ever. Even when you merely shoot down enemies, the game still feels the need to spread Kill Bill levels of blood everywhere.

As for Hell, there are several long Hell levels! It doesn't just look or feel like Hell, it IS Hell! You're in the home of the demons, so now they're extra pissed, which make the Hell levels among the hardest in the game. They're also a welcome change of appearance, and feature much more asymetrical level design than the UAC levels. The music also changes to creepy ambience and chants for when you're not fighting.

Speaking of music, Mick Gordon is GOD. His music enhances the game in so many ways and a lot of his songs feature melodies from older Doom games! Even when you just listen to it, you know it wouldn't fit any other game but Doom and that's what makes it so good.

Before I mentioned that even the story is good and yeah, it's surprisingly good. The game gives you just enough exposition to keep you going without ever placing the story over the gameplay. For more exposition, you can find hidden items which unlock logbook entries Metroid Prime-style, giving you info on the enemies and the backstory of the game. The ending also sets the game up for a sequel, which I'd be more than happy to play. WHEN'S DOOM 2????

Now that they made all the multiplayer DLC free it's better than ever! Multiplayer was pretty fun from what I played, although there weren't many players. Snapmap is also pretty fun, although I can't imagine it will replace Doom WADs.

All in all, it's an extension and an improvement over the classic Doom formula. A must-buy and a testament to how reboots SHOULD be done.\t\t\t\t\t
