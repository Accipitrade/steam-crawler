\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe FPS equivalent of a hack and slash. Kill sh*t, upgrade your weapons for killing sh*t more gooder, kill more sh*t. Incredibly addictive though. And at the highest difficulties, it is skill-based FPS at it's finest.

Things I liked:
-Metal asf.
-Weapons feel *powerful*
-High difficulties buff your enemies/increase enemy count rather than nerfing player with unnatural feeling handicaps.
-Movement is simple, but fluid.
-Old school arcade game feeling but with modern engine and graphics. Peak gaming.

Things that were meh:
-The story. But you don't play Doom for the story.

Things I didn't like:
-In the beginning, some levels felt more like puzzles than anything as far as trying to figure out where the keys were to advance to the next section. Tried to do it on my own, but a few times I got stuck and had to consult Google and I hate doing that. I realize it is trying to stay true to the '93 version, but Doom is at it's best when you're blasting demons. It was a bit annoying to hop on Doom to blast some demons and let off some steam, then spend half of that time wandering around looking for those damn keys. (I will say mid to late game this became less of an issue... Thankfully.)
-Jump puzzles. Alot less irritating than the keys, but again not what I'm looking for in a game like Doom.\t\t\t\t\t
