\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"They are rage, brutal, without mercy. But you. You will be worse. Rip and tear, until it is done."

That quote sums up the game in a nutshell. DOOM hails back to old school shooters, like Doom 1 and Wolfenstein, but with a modern look (there's even an option to position the guns just like the old games). You are given a huge arsenal and rooms full of baddies; your objective? Kill them all. There is no point in taking cover in this game. The Doom Slayer doesn't need that baby stuff. The blood of his enemies is more than enough to keep him going. Just keep running and you'll survive.

The graphics are gritty. The finishing moves are brutal as one would expect from a guy who's day job his slaughtering hellspawn. And the lighting in each level is superb, adding atmosphere to each location wheter it be the different parts of Mars or Hell.

And the soundtrack. Mick Gordon is a true genius. What I mean is that every battle soundtrack is dynamic; it revolves around the actions you perform, sucking you further in the madness and choas you bring to these puny weaklings of Hell. So while the base music is still the same, there are little tidbits that will vary player to player.

Go rip Hell a new one.\t\t\t\t\t
