\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you would ask me what are my favorite games of all times the original Doom would be on the first spot. So much time spent on the game, WADs, level creation and early multiplayer. I love how close this one is to the original. Love the original demons recreated the way they are. Love the music with subtle hints of tracks from the original.
Once you get used to the new glory kill mechanics you are good to go. The weapons handle good a kind of a mix between the original ones and ones you may find in new games. No reloads! how cool is that! It is like a time machine ;)
I like the level design - sometimes when the carnage is over I like to zoom in on various details. Sounds are amazing. All in all I highly recommend this franchise reboot and If you feel the full price is too much for a game form 2016 put it on a wish-list and wait for a offer - there are plenty.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 2016 was my introduction to the historic Doom franchise and is quite possibly my favorite game I've played start to finish for the first time all year.

Doom 2016 is technically a sequel, but serves more as a reboot of the series. The story revolves around the player, a mythical soldier known as the Doom Slayer, repelling a demonic invasion of a facility on Mars which had been exploiting Hell as an energy source for humanity. If you think that sounds interesting and a really neat idea for a story to explore in-depth... that's not the point. Those who play games purely for story will find little praise for Doom; the plot of the game only serves as a backdrop for the actual purpose of the game: killing ♥♥♥♥.

The game is essentially a middle finger to cover-based shooters. In games such as Halo or CoD, if you get injured you simply find a safe spot and duck behind cover, waiting for your health to recharge. No such system exists in Doom; the primary way to regain health is with the Glory Kill system, which has you performing Mortal Kombat-esque executions on demons after dealing enough damage to them, showering you in health pickups. Glory kills, along with the layout of the arenas, ensure that you're never taking cover or avoiding the fight; unlike other FPS games, Doom has you diving headfirst into groups of enemies. The combat moves at breakneck speeds, having the player slay enemies at the same pace as the absolute banger of a soundtrack by Mick Gordon, which is an experimental metal masterpiece that defines the game.

The atmosphere of the game is great as well. The moody aesthetic of the game is perfectly matched by the ambient parts of the soundtrack, which is at all points breathtaking.

Every aspect of this game is fantastic. Each combat arena is fun and demons are extremely satisfying to kill. The game manages to be difficult while never failing to make you feel like a badass. The atmosphere immerses you in the worlds of the game as you explore the levels searching for your prey. I can't even begin to describe how hard the soundtrack slaps. Even the story, while not the main appeal of the game by any means, is decent enough and quite entertaining.

If you're a fan of FPS games in any capacity, I would definitely recommend purchasing this game. It's fantastic, and is one of the best FPS games I've ever played.\t\t\t\t\t
