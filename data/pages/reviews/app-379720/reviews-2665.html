\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best games i've played in a long time. Tight controls, beautiful graphics, killer soundtrack, fast action. It also has a decent storyline, which can be largely ignored, if you're oldschool like that, without really taking away from the experience. Very unintrusive, so to speak. I also have to say, id software really nailed it with the megatextures, and that's also the reason, why this game is so large. RIP, people with slow internet (me). They may seem blurry, when you really get up close to the landscape and stand still, but this is not a game where you just stand around and wait. While moving, the maps look vivid and lifelike, if life took place on mars/hell.

As somewhat of an old dude, approaching 30, i loved how this game payed hommage to doom 1 and 2. Be it gameplay wise, or in the soundtrack. Those midi-cowbells still haunt me, and you will find them here, as well. Sound design in general is phenomenal, and they well deserved that award they got for that.

Also i have to applaud the optimization. I don't have the worst of rigs (i5 7600k, gtx 1060 6 gb, 16 gb ram, win 10), but not the best either, and it runs very smoothly at 1080p with everything cranked to to the highest settings.

I totally recommend this to anyone who takes even the slightest of interests in the shooter genre, especially at the current price.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChecklist:
Gore – check
Fun – check
Bullet hell – check
Mindless brutality – check
Story driven plot – not really
Cut scenes – so minimal it doesn’t even matter

Absolutely one of the best games I have ever played. The single player is what you are buying, so let me explain. Multiplayer feels to me like a very poorly made game mode. I have experienced Call of Duty, Titanfall and Battlefield and nothing in the Doom multiplayer fits the spot. Now, now, maybe I am doing it wrong, but it doesn’t feel right to me. I can’t really put my finger on what it is, but I feel cheated.
But that is more than made up in the ridiculous campaign. You MUST play the campaign, regardless of liking or disliking the multiplayer. Doom is most definitely a mindless run and gun game, and it punishes you for not doing so. You are encouraged to use every clip of ammo you have and to, well, give them hell. You have no reloading, so what is your excuse for not emptying the RPG into the boss. The music is one of my favorite parts, as it always ramps up to just the right bass and tune to fit the battle. You can’t go wrong with this level of detail and feel to such a game. I have wasted a good amount of time myself (don’t listen to the steam playtime stats) and am loving the game.

Happy Hunting\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts short af (7-8 hours first playthrough, probably less than 5-6 if you know where you are going and ignore some of the secrets). The collectables and secrets (including some that unlock perks) are ridiculously well hidden. The game overall starts a bit slow.

Yet this is still easily the best single player shooter experience to be released in the last 10 years.

Everything is slightly faster than you are used to including movement speed, there is no peek-a-boo cover mechanic, gameplay does not only involve hitting the bad guys but also dodging enemy fire. Everything that the game throws at you can be avoided by good movement, there is even a difficulty level that expects you to finish the game on 1 hp. The game is incredibly fair, if you die in combat, its always because you ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up.

Enemies and weapons offer a solid amount of variety, the level design is brilliant (although gravity might kill you a few times), and the soundtrack and overall sound design are kickass. There are 3 (+1 somewhat changed moveset) excellently designed bosses.

Grab it for cheap so the playtime is worth the asking price. Great game.\t\t\t\t\t

This game is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ excellent. Weapons are very varied, but all have a purpose. You won't be able to just stick to the same one through the campaign - you have to know what works and be able to switch on the fly. Normal mode was still nice and challenging, I died a lot but it never felt frustrating.
