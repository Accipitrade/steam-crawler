\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt should be known before you read this review. Although I have played the previous Doom games, this is an objective review, it will not be compared or contrasted against the Original games. I have also held off on reviewing this game before I completed it.

Now then...

Doom 4, or as it's known now, simply Doom, is the fourth main installment of the Doom franchise by id Software. The last Doom game that came before this was Doom 3, which came out in 2004. Meaning, fans of Doom have waited for at least a decade before this game was finally announced.

Gameplay wise, its quite the same as many fast paced twitch shooters. Fast movement, no reloading weapons, lots of blood and guts. Unlike many other present day games however, this game is many comprised of large spaced levels. Some of them even took me at least an hour to complete one level. Almost all of the weapons in this game have two attachments, which you can swap between at will. The chainsaw will soon become your best friend, especially late-game. Killing an enemy with the chainsaw drops a small amount of health and drops a metric ton of ammunition for you. An interesting feature in this game is the Glory Kill system. Damage an enemy enough, they will start glowing. You can then move in for a melee attack and disembowl or many other ways destroy your enemies. If you're playing this game first time, it is recommended you play on Normal difficulty. It'll give you quite a challenge, especially late-game, where it starts getting pretty brutal. The campaign length itself is about, 8 hours long. A good length for killing demons.

Music wise, People will compain about the choice of music in this game, they will say 'It's not very Doom-like', but in this case it fits. It's a dystopian future, with a dystopian sounding soundtrack. It seems to be a mix of Nine Inch Nails, Celldweller and Rammstein.

Story wise, You awake from a tomb, and have to kill demons and stop the invasion... Doesn't get any more complicated than that to be honest. If you want to play for story, you're not going to be entertained for long.

Special Features, This game does have a multiplayer, if you're into that kind of thing. But what really has my attention, is the SnapMap mode. Player-created arenas and maps made for Competitive or Cooperative play. You can even use your multiplayer character to play and destroy the demon hordes in these maps, and you are rewarded with points to customize your character even further.

I do believe that this game was worth $60 dollars with its campaign and SnapMap modes. It is definently not worth buying the Season Pass, because it is mainly for multiplayer.

Now get this game, play it, and kill some demons.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original Doom(s) for many gamers is a holy throne that nothing can compare to. I may not be as biased towards it but Doom is a very fun, fast paced fps with varied enemies and weapons. Most fps games nowadays don't follow suite with the pacing or the replayability the Doom franchise had. So when this game was announced there was so many high hopes and doubts to come from it. Finally they can be put to rest because this new Doom, this one delivers. Everything I've played so far in it's campaign and it's snapmap gives me the feeling that I won't be playing too many other fps games for quite a while. I found it hard to go to sleep the first night after playing because nothing but Doom was in my mind. It strays a little bit in multiplayer from the traditional arena shooter, but I still found it fun and exciting nonetheless.

TLDR: This reboot to a beloved franchise is top notch quality. Delivers on almost every front they promised, runs very well at a good fps, fast paced, gory and has spectacular visuals (not to mention it is a complete blast to play). Doom is back and I hope it stays for a very long time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - 8/10, very solid game, must-have for FPS fans. Just don't buy it for multiplayer only - it's decent, but don't expect miracles.

So, I've finished the game, played some multiplayer, briefly checked out SnapMap, and now it's time to share my Very Important Opinion with random people. First of all - singleplayer is great, id still know how to make a shooter. The combat feels fluid, it's fast paced, and enemies are vicious. While there were some concerns regarding the amount of monsters on screen, it's about the same as it was in the original Doom. Also, each demon is now much more dangerous by itself, which makes fighting even tougher.
The map design also deserves a commendation. As opposed to a linear CoD-ish design that is borderline mandatory in all of modern shooters, maps in DOOM are branching, have multiple levels, and every one of them have secrets for you to find (if you want). Exploration is rewarded with additional weapon upgrade points. Speaking of which...
The weapon upgrade system is a nice touch that gives you a bit of new opportunities here and there, preventing you from getting bored with your guns. And every upgrade is meaningful and powerful (even the scope for the Heavy Rifle - when fully upgraded, it makes the gun quite a bit more powerful). The suit upgrades, however, are kind of gimmicky - only the mobility upgrades have a real use you constantly feel, the rest are mostly situational. On the other hand, the runes are massively helpful, and they give you additional options to shape your playstyle.
Also, I have to talk about the graphics, optimisation specifically. The game is incredibly optimized - even with my aging i5-2500 and GTX780 it ran at pretty solid 60 fps without sacrificing too much eye candy, and that is great.
The game has its flaws, of course. First of all - some of the monsters, Mancubi and Barons in particular, have too much health, being borderline bullet sponges. And while it's excusable for the Barons, as they are somewhat rare and supposed to be the toughest non-boss enemies, there are many, MANY Mancubi in the game. They're not particulary difficult, but shooting them over and over and over and over and over becomes frustrating after a while. There are also some sound glitches in the main menu, which is slightly annoying, and the game crashed twice in about 18 hours, although I expect it be fixed soon.
______________
Moving on, the multiplayer. It's not bad, but it's nowhere near as great as the singleplayer. Unfortunately, the developers did nothing with the issues there were so many complaints about in open beta. The movement is still slow. It's faster than other modern FPS games, but it's just slow enought to frustrate you. The weapons are still unbalanced - both shotguns are better than about any other weapon in the game, and the Heavy Rifle is still crap. The demons are also unbalanced - the Revenant requires no brain, the Baron is forced to spam his secondary attack, the Mancubus is nearly useless compared to the Revenant, and the Prowler is much more fun to play than any other demon (and he's also highly OP if used correctly - come on, wallhack? One-hit charge? Clinging to the walls and ceiling?). All that said, the multiplayer is still fun to play, just don't expect anything extraordinary. If you're just looking for an old-school online arena shooter, you should probably just try Quake Live or UT4 alpha.
______________
Finally, the SnapMap. To be honest, right now it's pretty much empty - there are mostly just test maps. I can see the potential in it, but I can also see why people might get bored with maps made of the same rooms. Time will tell. Maybe I'll update this part once I get used to SnapMap better.
______________
All in all - definitely worth the price. If you want a good singleplayer AAA-FPS with oldschool elements - you won't find anything better anytime soon.\t\t\t\t\t
