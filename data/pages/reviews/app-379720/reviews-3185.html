\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst Ever Steam Review

20 years ago I was playing the original Doom on a Sony Playstation. We didn't call it a PS1 back then because we didn't know that there would be any more. It was the best game that I had ever played to that point. My first ever 'gaming computer' was a 48k ZX Spectrum which takes us 10 years even further back in time. So I'm a bit of a crusty old bloke whose been addicted to computer games since the time that there were no personal computers, no mobile phones and my Hanimex pocket calculator attracted a lot of attention in the playground.

Unlike many of my generation who love to wallow in the past and say that things were better when Maggie was PM and remember how we used to talk to each other instead of playing with our phones, I believe that things are always best in the here and now.

I built a new gaming rig late in 2015. It is quite modest, based around an AMD FX-8320, 16Gb ram and an MSI GTX970. I've just treated myself to a BenQ XR3501 35" 2560x1080 ultra-widescreen gaming monitor and bought Doom last night in the Steam Sale (3 hour download, yikes).

I know I've only put in just over an hour so far but the 2016 Doom is simply brilliant. It is utterly faithful to the spirit of the original and I am getting insane fps on max settings. The only issue is my underwear drawer which has taken a bit of a hit; third pair of pants today already. I am very scared but very happy.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo after playing a bit of DOOM, I do think it is a good game but I think people are vastly overstating its similarity to the original. Some critiques:

1) They went full on orange/red with the color scheme instead of using the varied colors of the original. Honestly, I'm getting tired of games having "a color". It's refreshing that Elder Scrolls Online (my main game currently) doesn't do this.

2) The game feels way more melee focused than OG Doom. In the original, you could totally use the shotgun to pick off baddies from a substantial distance, whereas in the new one, it seems like you basically have to be in a monster's face to use it.

3) The combat pacing feels more "mindlessly frantic" than OG Doom ever did. Doom was fast paced but just slow enough that you could think about your surroundings, enemy positions, etc. and make conscious choices about how to handle the situation. The new DOOM tries to deliberately avoid this. This could be a pro if you prefer this, but it does make it less like the game it is a successor to.

4) Glory kills are more silly than fun, and many of them are so pointlessly elaborate that it is immersion breaking. It also discourages you from taking out enemies from range, which goes back to the combat being too melee focused.

In terms of capturing what made the original Doom great, I think they missed the mark again, just in a completely different way than they did with Doom 3.\t\t\t\t\t

A Perfect Symbiosis of Old Style & Modern Shooter

In the months leading up to its release I was worried about the state of the game as it had seemed to enter development hell if you'll pardon the pun. Their initial direction had me very dubious about whether I would enjoy this game or not. Fortunately someone at ID software decided to reboot the entire thing and led to the current game that we have today. It is simple in concept yet is suprisingly effective once in motion.

This is one of the strong points of the game and is crucial to performing well in this game. Many other shooters would have you stick to cover hiding behind a convenient bit of waist high cover, but DOOM has forgone all that and brought back some elements from older FPS games such as Quake, Unreal Tournament and especially the original DOOM where motion is key to combat. You must strave, jump, dodge and run to avoid being shot, dismembered and gnawed on by a variety of demons.

Bringing back this one simple concept has made the game a thrill to play, and ID Software came up with some very interesting ideas to make sure that the player is punished for trying to play this like a modern shooter.

There's no regenerating health, and one of the most effective methods to actually regain health is the Glory Kill feature where a Demon after taking a certain amount of damage will be stunned for a short duration enabling Doom Guy to kill this demon in a variety of brutal ways depending on the angle of attack.

I imagine some would have been worried that this would interrupt the flow of combat, but the way it is implemented into the combat is seamless and flows incredibly well.

Next there are the weapons which is essentially what DOOM is all about, the gunplay and combat. There are a variety of weapons that Doom guy has at his disposal and unlike plenty of shooters, these weapons perform and act completely different from one another.

This fact is only further proven once your weapons have been fully modded and the masteries have been completed, which are essentially challanges to perform that will unlock a weapons ultimate ability. Each weapon is satisfying to use and there's plenty to experiment with here.

The campaign is roughly 15 hours in length, probably more if you spend sometime trying to find the collectibles hidden throughout the sprawling maps. There's plenty to do in the single campaign from finding secrets to completing optional challanges.

One other standout for me throughout the game was the soundtrack, there's not many ways to explain it except that the soundtrack perfectly fits DOOM. The moment the first Heavy Rock/Metal riff starts and enemies start spawning in you know you're in for a treat.

Overall I would say DOOM is the best First Person Shooter that I have played in many years and I hope that some more developers take note of what ID Software have done and it brings back a resurgence of old school shooters. Maybe another Unreal Tournament or Quake. This game is a welcome relief from the tedium that many FPS games have unwittingly found themselves in and will eagerly tell anyone who asks that DOOM is an essential must play.\t\t\t\t\t
