Preface
Now that I have done everything that this game has to offer I believe I can leave a review on it. (The runes glitched but I'm working on it.)

I would like to preface this entire review by saying that I DO NOT LIKE DOOM. I thought the campaign was boring and it took me numerous tries to get through it. I put around 60 hours into multiplayer before I even decided to bother again for this reason I believe the game should be split up into two reviews. (The formatting on this might be [redacted] up.

Campaign
Where to begin man so many highs and lows and pros and cons. The campaign is...alright. The story is fairly interesting and the collectibles are cool. The collectibles, not the secrets. The secrets become tedious with many being super easy to miss unless you are following a walkthrough second for second. The amount of collectibles you're looking for become burdensome keeping up between challenges, runes, praetor tokens, collectibles, data pads, and secret areas. Data pads and secret areas aren't even on your map so you just kind of have to look for them. The challenges can also be highly situational so if you aren't constantly on top of them they can be easy to forget. Then what do you get to do? Replay the level. Replay. Replay. Replay. I do not want to replay linear levels. I've seen it. I've done it. I don't wanna do it again. It rips the sense of "pride and accomplishment" right out of your hands.

Upgrades are another thing to watch out for. Some of the gun upgrades are fun, others will leave you scratching your head until you find trash to complete them on. Trying to complete them will mean certain death for you on occasion. The rune upgrades on the other hand are perfect. You can do them on pretty much any demon. Any horde. Any level. Any time. You have to carry out specific actions and they are a lot of fun to upgrade and play with.

The length of the story are where my real gripes come into play. Does the story feel whole? Yeah I suppose it does, but it becomes tedious. It feels like it could have easily been cut down by 2-3 levels bringing it to 10-11. Those extra two were just brutal to the pace of the story in my opinion. With all the replays and collectible searching it becomes pretty grindy.

Overall the Campaign gets a 3/10. There's just too much going on when it could have easily been simplified down for a more carefree blast experience.

Multiplayer
The multiplayer on this game is a hidden gem. I think it says lengths about Bethesda that the servers are still up, and there's a pretty active community around it. You are pretty much guaranteed a match on either the US or UK servers when you get on. That alone should tell you all you need to know about Multiplayer after 5+ years.

There is a bit of a learning curve and you're gonna get plenty of players with 100's to 1000's of hours of experience to go up against. They know the strategies for each map, they know the pick up locations to stay at full health, and they have their loadouts perfected and field tested. Still multiplayer is an experience and while it's a grind for the cyber demon equipment it certainly has it's ups to get there!

The guns feel good, and different maps call for different loadouts. The demons are pretty one dimensional with the Harvester, Spectre, and Revenant dominating the spawns. Still it makes for an enjoyable time trying each of them out. The runes aren't too crazy, but add a little something extra. Finally this might come as a bit of a shock, but the customizations for multiplayer are kind of insane. At first glance they may seem lack luster. You receive quite a bit of uninteresting garbage at the beginning. The more you progress the more you start to take a look at the customizations you can do.

Multiplayer is a solid 9/10. I feel some better/more maps could have been added into the mix, but it is truly a gem.

I will never forgive Bethesda for not making an elite guard as a usable skin.


Bonus: Arcade and SnapMap
Arcade in my opinion is more of what the campaign should have been like. Is that the DOOM format? No, but I thought it was a lot more fun. Going for the higher ratings on the arcade maps, and looking for the relics is a blast. Moving up to higher difficulties on a level feels fresh. Arcade mode is truly shadowed by the campaign and it's a tragedy.

The SnapMaps are actually pretty cool. They have a lot to offer, and a lot of people actually get pretty insane with the levels that they offer. The complexity and simplicity of the tool is pretty ingenious, and I'm pretty sure you can play every Doom level before 2016 off of SnapMaps. Feel free to correct me if I'm wrong.

Arcade and SnapMaps 8/10

Despite not being a huge Doom fan this game has a lot going for it. I almost guarantee one of these facets is going to have what you're looking for. There's just so much packed into this game that I can't give it a thumbs down review. The campaign is tedious as hell, but if you don't form at least some type of love/hate relationship like I did I'd be surprised.

And that's it. I will be going back through to collect all of the ruins in a new playthrough and I might max out my multiplayer Echelons, but since this is completed I will be off to conquer Doom: Eternal and Deadspace.
