\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed it a year or two later after the release, yet totally LOVED every minute of it. BADASS is the word.

+ properly (fast-) paced gameplay, with cutscenes timed well enough to take a breather before the next slaughter, true DOOM-game feel to it
+ apparent influence of Brutal Doom, that wasn't too over the board, yet brought the long-awaited... "outlet"
+ great level design with demonic presence felt and seen in all its disgusting details...
+ ...and level to level progression of UAC's true "mission" with this whole Mars endeavor
+ gritty and gripping soundtrack
+ ledge-grabbing, double-jumping features only adding to the pace and gameplay style, not an overkill like the D Eternal with its monkey bars and other arcade nonsense...
+ great "tribute-to-original" final boss
+ to-the-point story and in-game lore that's not overwhelming and totally skippable if you don't need it
+ great way to honour classic maps
+ all enemies from classic games...

- ...that could still do better with DOOM Eternal's "soldier" enemies, the original Cyberdaemon (Tyrant) and Archvile instead of their 2016 counterparts. The Hell Knight could have had at least some horns, otherwise his head's too similar to the usual gun-wielding soldier
- maybe I didn't quite liked the direction this (and the next) installment took - arena-like challenges, instead of just levels more or less evenly packed with monsters, but this one is minor\t\t\t\t\t
