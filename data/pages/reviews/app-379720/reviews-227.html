\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen you think about the Doom franchise, you think of bloody spectacle and fast-paced gunplay. Doom '16 tries to deliver both, but the level of complexity and amount of subsystems added to keep an eight-hour game from getting repetitive end up distracting you more than not. I think I really would have enjoyed a stripped-down version of the game with similar graphics and a halved runtime, but the actual product at least kept me from getting bored.

I had a lot of trouble deciding whether to recommend Doom '16 or not, but in the end I think it's unfair to the game to tell people not to play it at all. It's pretty mindless entertainment, but it's a 20$ game at the time of this interview that goes on sale pretty frequently. Grab it at 10$ and you're getting as much stimulation as a superhero flick that will keep you busy for three times as long as said movie.\t\t\t\t\t
