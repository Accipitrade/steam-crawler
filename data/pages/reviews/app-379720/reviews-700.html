\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm writing this review after binge-playing Doom 2016, Doom: Eternal and all the way to Ancient Gods 1/2.

This particular installment is a decent attempt to reimagine the old-school FPS genre with some gameplay mechanics borrowed from modern games and Brutal Doom (a popular mod for classic Doom).

The game has great references to its roots (both in gameplay and as easter eggs) while being a solid game on its own.

There is some big underlying story, but the Doomguy doesn't particularly care about it, and, in this case, neither should you. You can read it in the Codex if you want.

Lots of secrets. The weapon/suit upgrades don't feel forced and can be helpful.

The glory kills mechanic seems a bit repetitive, but it can be freely avoided if you don't need its benefits.

Visually the game is great even five years later - for some reason I found Eternal to be more cartoony and less detailed than the original 2016 game.

The music is decent and does not distract you from battles, but I didn't find it memorable enough. I liked the homages to Robert Prince though: every now and then you'll hear references to classic Doom soundtrack.

There are some annoying moments, such as being unable to return back to some areas to grab missed secrets (the only way in some cases is to replay the entire level from scratch). The monster design was also a bit weird, but still much better than in Doom 3. That one was really, really bad.

A must play if you like FPS, fast action and Doom as a series.\t\t\t\t\t
