\r\n\t\t\t\t\t So I find myself sitting here, having just finished the game with half a feeling of 'that was awesome' and half 'that was really dissapointing'.

Let's get a few things out of the way, the soundtrack is phenomenal, the graphics exemplary and the setting is fantastic.

However, I felt I was unable to really immersive myself in the experience. Whilst it may seem like a small detail one thing that kept ruining my immersion was the game map. You have such fantastic environmental and artistic assets but when you go to the map it just shows you how truly linear the game actually is. "Oh wow, i'm in the bowels of hell" suddenly becomes: "Oh it's just another series of corridors...". This is exacerbated by the fact you are rewarded for finding the various secrets shown on the map, forcing you to constantly keep checking to make sure you haven't missed anything in an area before proceeding.

When I actually think back on the game, most of my time was spent wandering around the map, silently checking for areas I might have missed (as once you "clear an area" no more demons will spawn). This really took away the 'on-edge' atmosphere and left me a little...bored.

Hmm, if that was my idea of a small detail I should probably stop there and cut to the chase.

If you want a 10 hour experience of shooting a roster of demons with awesome metal music then I would recommend picking this up in a sale. If you are looking for anything deeper than that then I would instead advice looking elsewhere as the story is poor, the weapon customisation feels meaningless by the end game and the experience left me wanting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGraphics: 5/5
Characters/Story: 4/5
Environment: 4/5
Combat: 5/5
Replayability: 4/5

Graphics is great, even relatively weaker machines should be able to handle this fairly well and still look okay.

The speed of the game vs. the relatively few number of expressive characters left me slightly disappointed. A better story could have been told without sacrificing game time with just a few more character interactions, but then even the devs made it fairly clear they were making a run n' gun. The story as it is, however, is fascinating and ties into older DOOM games.

The environment is pretty amazing, though this seems to be accomplished by having an otherwise bland Mars base get slowly more demonic and blood-coated. The more ♥♥♥♥♥♥ the environment gets, the better it looks, though I would have appreciated a less bland normal area.

This is DOOM. The combat is fun. The weapons give you plenty of options. Enemy variability is decent, and powerups are fun. Much like old DOOM, movement is a must.

Replayability would be better if once the game was over, replaying didn't basically require me to select levels one at a time, rather than beginning another story progression. Also I would have liked a new game + style boost to the weapons on a second playthrough, but I understand that's not what they were going for.

Overall, this is a fantastic game with fun weapons. Collectibles were fun but slightly tedious. None of my criticisms make any part of this game unpleasant (however pleasant mowing down hellish enemies in a blood-soaked Mars base is to you). Ultimately it left me wanting more of the story, and anytime I just want to blast away waves of crazy enemies, I find myself playing DOOM.

EDIT: I forgot to mention. Multiplayer feels like Unreal Tournament. Fun but somewhat forgettable. And, CLASSIC LEVELS.\t\t\t\t\t
