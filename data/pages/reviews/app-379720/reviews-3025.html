\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is fantastic, frenetic, brutal, bloody good fun! It's been a while since I've played a game that so perfectly hits the sweet spot between challenge and enjoyment, and it's such a rush when it all clicks and you clear a room from the slavering hordes of hell using all your wits and weapons.

[Almost] No Nonsense
The first thing Doomguy does in this game is murder demons with his bare hands. The second thing he does is rip off a vidscreen of an audio log. Clearly, DOOM does not want to waste your time - and apart from a few tram rides and conversations you are completely in control for the entire game time. Doomguy isn't much for talking, but nevertheless bits of his hardbitten personality shine through his actions and reactions.

Tons of Guns
DOOM hearkens back to the classic FPS days of an ever-increasing arsenal of progressively more powerful guns. You start with just a pistol and shotgun, but quickly avail yourself of weapons ranging from machine guns to railguns. Upgradable weapon mods add even more variety - explosive shots, missile tracking, stun bombs, and more. Whether it's simply blasting away with a twin-barrelled shotgun and a chainsaw or chaining electric blast kills together, turning demons into piles of gristle has never been more fun.
And, as a bonus, you can center the guns in the middle of the screen for that true old-school feeling.

Speed and Creativity
Doomguy is light on his feet for being a giant slab of muscle, and the game encourages this maneuverability with its level and game design. Arenas are designed with both horizontal and vertical motion in mind, so constantly moving and flanking to frenetically gain the upper hand on the hordes of hell is the way to go,
Different upgrades allow you to further tweak your gameplay style. Double-jumps, quicker weapon swapping, and siphoning health from enemies all allow you to further push DOOM to be played the way you want it to.

Depth and Replayability
Not to say that digital murder isn't fun, but DOOM isn't all just brainless bashing. Hidden within the labyrinthine levels are many secrets, ranging from action figures to bonus upgrades to classic DooM levels to just fun visual easter eggs. You'll find yourself playing levels again and again, both to find all the secrets and to just try out your new guns on familiar faces. Rewards range from practical (weapon mod upgrades) to visual (viewable 3D models), rewarding for casual and completionist alike.

Not to mention this game regularly gets discounted quite heavily! Within months of its release I was able to get it for half-off, and now it's already being cut down to 1/3 the price. An incredible deal that you shouldn't pass up!

P.S. I highly recommend you play it on hard (Ultra-Violence)! Even though I typically play games on Medium, I found that this was the perfect difficulty for fun and challenging play.\t\t\t\t\t
