\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTwo of my old time favourite shooters were Unreal Tournament 2004 and Halo.
That was, until I played DOOM.

This game brings the intergalactic story, weird alien tech and mysterious relics from Halo.
And mixes it with the fast-paced, multi weapon gameplay of Unreal Tournament.

-You can find a great range of weapons almost all upgradeable with different mods to suit your playstyle.

-The game forces you to be agressive to get your health by having more health dropped when executing enemies up-close.

-Creative environments, with the exception of hell, all looking fairly different from each other.

-New enemies being introduced on the regular by first showing one of them and then bombarding you with three more the next room.
_________

Negative things include:

-multiplayer, don't buy for multiplayer. It feels slow, has a two weapon limit and couldn't be futher from the classic arena shooter gameplay if you tried.

-Snapmap is ok, but has some odd limitations. Meaning some nice maps with creative mechanics, but a lot more straightforward maps that bore quickly.

-difficulty options needs more choice.
In each difficulty, the enemies do 2x more damage than the previous difficulty, meaning that you can find one option too forgiving while the next one too brutal.

-Two perticular enemies that break to flow completely.

Shield guys, with a shield to strong to attack long-distance and a shotgun and turning speed to quick to attack him up-close.
(Becomes a whole lot better when you get some strong, easy to aim explosives)

Lost souls, they aren't a problem on their own, but the enemy spawning mechanics makes them homing missiles that spawn out of nowhere.
________________

Overal I strongly recommend this beast of a game.
