\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHE GOOD: You move relatively fast for a modern fps (slower than Quake 1). No Iron sights or reloading. The soundtrack is alright, once I turned it down it didn't bother me too much. Sometimes it's a halfway decent arena shooter.

THE BAD: It is nowhere near as fast and smooth as many games that used much older id-tech engines. The movement is kind of floaty, in a bad way (not terrible but enough to be annoying). The level design is pretty bland and doesn't offer dramatically more than older arena shooters, like Painkiller or Serious Sam so don't expect interesting or creative map design like in classic Doom because you won't find that here. The art design is mostly just bad. Everything is bright, shiny and cartoony. The monsters are mostly over designed and comical. The way glory kills are tied directly into the game design completely destroys the flow and pacing of combat. Just run around like an idiot shooting things then pinata them by locking yourself into an animation sequence when you're low on health or ammo. Secret hunting and exploration is entirely pointless because of that, not that there's much to that anyway. There is literally no tension in this game. It also has many modern design elements holding it back. There are tons of animated sequences you can't skip, bad checkpoints (no manual saving), you use upgrade points to upgrade your weapons and armor, collectibles, codex entries, scripted dialogue your forced to sit through (it even tries to trick you into thinking it won't do this), little annoying messages popping up on the screen all the time and arbitrary 'challenges' that require you to complete repetitive mundane tasks. Everything about it just screams console game.

THE UGLY: The classic Dooms are easily among my favorite games and I've been playing them regularly since they came out all those years ago, along with all the other classic id and 3D Realms games. Just because this doesn't have iron sights or reloading doesn't make it a return to the classics. None of the exploration, environmental puzzle solving or satisfying strategic combat is present. It's only remotely similar to those games on a very superficial level and I don't think the devs truly understood what made them great. It's like they only ever played them on lower difficulties or with cheats. Also the 'lore' is beyond terrible. The Doomslayer? Mythology? It's all just cringey. The demons don't need to be explained and shouldn't be. Being an overpowered bad ass alien/monster/thing does not make you feel like a bad ass. You know what does? Being a regular human overcoming insurmountable odds does. It's like the devs actively tried to prevent me from playing this or even thinking of it as a Doom game. I've tried to suffer through it multiple times to figure out why people loved it but the whole time while playing I'm constantly thinking 'I don't care, shut up, go away, you're dumb, just leave me alone and let me play the damn game' followed by 'oh great, another arena battle'. Then I uninstall out of frustration, every single time. Even Doom 3 is more of a faithful Doom game than this.

FINAL VERDICT: Don't be fooled by the hype this game had. It is not a true return to form for Doom as many claimed. It's a halfway decent arena shooter with modern mechanics made for consoles. Nothing more, nothing less. Put quite simply this game does not look, play or feel like a Doom game. If you're an old Doom fan and want an oldschool experience I wouldn't recommend spending any money on this since you can get a lot more bang for your buck elsewhere with games like Amid Evil, Dusk, Ion Fury and hopefully Wrath when it's released. Or just play some classic Doom with one of the many awesome megawads out there and forget this even exists.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKind of mediocre. If you are looking to play this some years after it's release; just skip it. My advice is to play the original games with something like Brutal Doom mod.

I appreciated the arena-like gameplay, but it wasn't done particularly well compared to earlier games like UT99 or Quake Arena nor does the game engine feel like it was made for it. Player movement is pretty floaty, guns (even when upgraded) do not feel very satisying, various small bugs/glitches, storyline not great obviously, but at least it was kept simple -- some of the demon voice over monologues were kind of cheesy and not in a good way (I expected better gameplay assuming the story was not a focus of any sort). The glory kills/violence are not as impactful if you have ever played something like Brutal Doom (which is a better play, IMO). I'd describe the game as not having any 'heft' or 'weight' on multiple fronts or aspects, if that makes any sense.

You can play the game fast, but it feels like it was poorly designed to do so. Textures and music are nice, map design is OK...there's a number of odd environment interactions that make it feel like it's somewhere between a linear corridor shooter but switched to an arena style shooter along development. Your character will sometimes get stuck on inclined steps or small thresholds (fault of mappers), which you'd think would be noticed on a playtester's run through the game. There's just a large number of small things that show some of the most important parts of the game they advertised (like the old school shooter fluidity, combat, etc) needed better care/development.

The first half of the game on a new playthrough is by far the worst part. Not until you can double jump and have more weapon mod variety does it actually start feeling slightly enjoyable. Unfortunately, once you get to this point, you're done with over half the game, the process of killing enemies feels incredibly stale (you'll find you just end up doing the same efficient way of dispatching them over and over).

It's worth a play if you don't have any better games at the time; perhaps it was some sort of refreshing action game at release -- but I found even Wolfenstein The New Order to be a better executed game technically and gameplay wise. I actually found that game more visceral than this one.

I played through multiple times in my 20+ hours, not playing the easy difficulties -- and there's not much incentive to play any more than once or so. Arcade mode, seems neat...bit it's really just arbitrary number popping up on the screen. I found a good number of the secrets/bonuses, but they really are worth finding or do anything interesting once you've upgraded most your weapons.

When I tried multiplayer, it would not load...my local save data had to be deleted before I could make it work again -- apparently save game corruption and local file corruption issues aren't uncommon in the game (I had this happen once with iDs other new game, Wolfenstein TNO too). I didn't take much time to play it, so maybe the multiplayer was good? Well over half of the game's Steam acheivements are multiplayer based -- so maybe that was ID's goal, to create a large multiplayer base for a new arena game.\t\t\t\t\t

It's not like your average "kill demons until you get to the final boss" type of game. The enemies vary hugely, their health, weapons, damage type and tactics make you have to think carefully about who to deal with first. The map design of the places you battle in make each fight unique. The environment also adds a nice touch. The arsenal of weapons make the game unique, as typically, at the last or 2nd to last mission of a game's campaign and it gives you the 'get out of jail free card' weapon. Where you just click and the gun will do the killing for you. But the BFG and Gauss Cannon siege mode are different. The BFG has to be used sparingly, because it can only be shot 3 times before you have to find more ammo. It also doesn't completely incinerate every enemy on the field since they come in waves. Each weapon is badass and whether you kill a lowly possessed scientist or the Spider Mastermind, you feel like a god. The campaign's gameplay is perfect in every way. And the argent cells and weapon mods add another layer of replayability. And if you're a legend at FPS games and want to take on a big challenge. Nightmare and Ultra nightmare difficulty is no joke. Most games I play, Nightmare is just a small step up from hard. But that's not the case. I completed doom on Hurt me Plenty and Ultra violence. And didn't die often until Argent D'nur. But it took my seven tries to get past the first gore nest. And many more to get through the stuff afterwards. And Ultra violence resets your campaign upon death. So that's even harder! Plus the snapmap feature is super versatile and you can program your own A.I.. All that rolled into one game makes Doom worth every penny. And probably more. BUY THIS GAME!\t\t\t\t\t
