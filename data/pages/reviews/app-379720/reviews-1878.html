\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI definitely recommend DOOM, not being a big fan of FPS games myself due to how similar most of them are, DOOM is something old in a fresh new coat. I've never played the original 1993 DOOM seriously, but having played the first level, I'd say that they are comparable. But with this DOOM ofcourse, it's full with next-gen features, next-gen graphics.

Gameplay:
This DOOM is fast paced, chaotic and has a great amount of challenge. It has the usual difficulty settings depending on how much challenge you want. I chose the standard difficulty. The first few levels serve for you to get used to this sort of level design and gameplay. However, later in the game, the game makes sure to give you a challenge. There are multiple weapons in the game, although I found the shotgun to be most reliable against most enemies, there are enemies that require you to use the big guns. In the game, you can collect secrets and upgrade points, The secrets are there as collectable, but these upgrade points can definitely make your game easier and more fun. You can purchase weapon mods and suit enhancements, for example. There are several more gameplay features, but it would be best if you discovered them yourself for maximum amount of enjoyment.

Performance:

Being a Next-gen game, I expected it to run fine on my PC, considering I have an outdated CPU (i5-3470) but modern GPU (GTX 1060 6 gb). However, I found out the game runs better than expected and even amazing on the highest settings. On everything maxed out on 1920 x 1200, I found that my FPS was around 100 - 120 fps. Loading times usually take about half a minute to a full minute when you are using a HDD.

General experience:

I definitely had tons of fun in this game. Nothing was a chore, (except collecting secrets, which isn't really my thing) and I had a blast terminating every demon in sight. The game felt fair for the most part. Generally in the later stages, you get bigger demons, which find ways to trap you into a corner, and so, you will most likely lose health and armor. But I'd say my general experience was pretty good. The game is extremely well polished, and contains no microtransactions.

Features in the game, but not tested yet:
- Multiplayer
- Snapmap (Custom community made maps)

Conclusion:

Buy this if you want to shoot up some enemies. You won't find an amazing story here, but you will find lots of endless shooting fun. I rate this game an 8.5 out of 10.\t\t\t\t\t
