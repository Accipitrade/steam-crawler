Campaign - Absolutely ass-clenchingly perfect. DOOM is a retro shooter with massive, secret filled maps, fast paced combat and the game forcing you to play very aggressively, which creates a very violent and testosterone fueled high of demon genocide. This is mainly enforced by your high speed, no reloading and glory kills, which are quick pre-animated takedown moves which drop more health. Demons are also more accurate when you stand still and are considerably less accurate when you're constantly moving. It can get a bit slow and maybe boring after the combat dies down and you wonder around the map like an idiot trying to find whatever you're looking for but that rarely happens. Expect a great experience through 13 levels of badass, running through hell and back (multiple times) glorious boss fights. 5/5

Multiplayer - Only fun on the beginner servers. Once you can't play on those it just becomes a demoralizing blur of losing and getting killed 2 seconds after spawning. The aggressive system that works so orgasmicly in the campaign is kinda ♥♥♥♥♥♥ in the multiplayer, especially glory kills which are outright impossible to pull off, not only because getting an opponent to low health without either killing them or them being killed by another player is hard enough, performing a glory kill is pretty much suicide, since unlike in the campaign, you are not immune to damage while performing a glory kill, so you're very likely to be killed while performing one, which the game doesn't count as a glory kill. Expect to be paired up with veteran players 485 levels ahead of you while you barely know how to play, said veterans insta-killing you the second they spot you and getting 30 kills while you barely get an assist in. 0.5/5

Snapmap - Fun little addition to the game which allows to make your own stages. Expect a level editor in which, as the name implies, you snap rooms together, put objects, logic and enemies inside them and whatever else you might desire. Although it is enjoyable it still isn't much and could get some help with more content, it's main faults being the relatively small scale most snapmaps can be, the max player count only being 4, the lack of a weapon wheel (you can only have 2 weapons like in the multiplayer) and the small amount and variety of rooms (there are only 4 themes for rooms and only the industrial one has any variaton within it) 3.5/5

Overall I recommend this highly, although I don't recommend the multiplayer unless your hobbies include rutinely hammering your balls with a sledge hammer\t\t\t\t\t
