\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is a fun, fast-paced shooter that harkens back to the day and age where you didn't aim down sights or reload in shooters. Doom is, at its core, about killing demons. If you're looking for an enriched story with compelling characters, turn away. Doom does not have it. If you want to blow away demons in a challenging campaign, you've found your game.

The campaign takes a decent amount of time to beat. The campaign is worthy of a second playthrough on one of the higher difficulties, but good luck on Ultra-Nightmare: once you die, you have to start the ENTIRE campaign over. The campaign also has a bunch of secrets and unlockables you can always go back and get, adding some depth to the game. The Campaign is definitely a 9/10.

Doom falls short on its multiplayer. Having not played the beta, I was expecting an arena shooter, much like that of UT or Quake. I was wrong. You pick your starting weapons beforehand, and you can also spawn with bonuses/mini-powerups (infinite ammo, temporary wallhacks, etc.). I did not like this, and it really detracts from the fun that this multiplayer could have been. At its current state, the multiplayer is just average. I wasn't thrilled nor pissed while playing. I just felt like there was nothing innovative at work. Multiplayer is about a 4/10.

Snapmap is a deep experience. You can spawn weapons, powerups, monsters, and basically create your own levels from scratch. There's no end to what people can create with snapmp, and you can usually find something fun to do in Snapmap mode, as you can download other creations and play them easily.

Overall, Doom is a decent game, but I would wait for the price to drop before buying it. 7/10

Imagine if Metalocalypse and Korgarth of Barbaria had a cocaine fueled, rage induced, blood soaked baby. I present to you the most metal, satanic killing, demon slaying death simulator to ever grace my gaming library.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I can't really describe how the game is itself, but what I've experienced so far really peeves me. First, I had hoped to share the game with my family because they love DOOM too, so I bought the physical copy so we could play it on multiple computers. I look on the back of the box at the system requirements and start to get nervous because my laptop isn't the newest, but after checking, I still surpass the minimum requirements. I put in the disk and run the installer, only to be directed to steam to finish the install. Wondering why I need steam for a CD. I click play. An update starts downloading and it's like, what, 18 gigs? I then look again to the back of the box and see probably the most evil thing I've ever seen on a video game box:

"Download of additional files from steam is required to run the game." Why even offer a physical copy of the game in that case?

5 and a half hours later it finally finishes at 10:30 at night and its too late to play, because I have to get up early the next day. So today, when I finally boot it up to play, it gives me this wall of coding jargon that is summed up with the line
"FATAL ERROR: wglCreateContextAttribsARB failed", basically saying my graphics card sucks too much for me to be allowed to play. I roam the internet for a while and see plenty of other people having the same problem with DOOM and Wolfenstein, and the horrible, horrible, evil answers I've been given were:
1) Get a better video card, which I can't do because this is a laptop.
2)Update the drivers, which they all are.
And worst of all, 3) Buy a new computer.
I couldn't afford one of those higher-end graphics cards/video cards, much less a whole new computer, so what am I to do?

Some of my first memories are of my mom playing The Plutonia Experiment on the XP (which is probably why I can't stand Revenants or Arch-viles). I've been waiting for this new DOOM since it was announced in 2008, and the gameplay footage from Quakecon only reignited my love for the game, only to be (practically) spit on at the gateway because I don't have the newest, most expensive, top-of-the-line gaming computer tower out there. Words cannot describe how disappointed I am. Hopefully, I still have the reciept so I can return the game, or else that $60+tax is just out the window.

However, there are a few good things I have to say. The cover of the box was so blad and generic, I was overjoyed when on the inside was a secret cover that was an updated version of the original cover for Doom 1. And from what little gameplay I've seen of it, the game looks amazingly good, like brings-a-tear-to-my-eye good. So if your computer has the horsepower and graphics to run it, get it. Otherwise, just play it at a friend's house or something, but don't bother buying it.\t\t\t\t\t
