\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Run around really fast, kill hordes of demons in several dozens of ways and feel good about it. Just don't touch multiplayer, or the magic will fade away.

Not tl;dr:

1) Good parts:
- Fast-paced combat. I really felt like I was in slow motion for a while in other games after playing it.
- Everything being entirely optional. You may ignore weapons you don't like, glory kill system, "secrets" and whatnot, and still feel satisfied by ripping demons to shreds.
- Nice playstyle customization / singleplayer replay value - especially if you skip challenges and secrets. There's so much stuff to upgrade and try out, that you can base your run on any weapon combo you like. Or you can be like me and tryhard to get almost all things upgraded in a single run...
- There are no such thing as "useless" and "unfun" on you weapons list.
- Possible to separately turn off different HUD parts to get rid of undesired features (compass, upgrade and story/lore noifications, objective markers).

2) Bad parts:
- NOT A SINGLE RED KEY THROUGHOUT THE ENTIRE CAMPAIGN. Blue is still my fav, but I'll take red over yellow any time of the day.
- Community content (Snapmaps) is somewhat limited in scope and based on MP. It's still killing demons, but now with all the worst strings attached (carry only 2 weapons, no mod swapping, hitmarkers, onscreen damage numbers etc).
- Loading times randomly go from super fast to complete garbage (not using SSD). Textures sometimes fail to load fully, even though the game already throws you into action, leaving you with a horrible view for another 5 seconds or so.
- No further SP support planned (aka don't buy Season Pass if you're not into MP).

3) Ugly parts:
- The entire multiplayer. Played 4 matches, 3 of them were 2v2-turned-2v6. Because it sure makes sense to add 4 people onto one team. Nice way to make sure I don't touch it ever again. It's also apparently region-locked or something.
- Mandatory MP/DLC updates even for those who don't own them. Prepare for 12 GB patches with stuff you possibly don't give a damn about.
- Garbage online DRM, which has been reported to lock people out for BIOS setting changes or swapping out parts. Didn't happen to me, but still, beware.

If you're wondering at this point "is this even a positive review?":
- Yes for SP. I had a really good time with several campaign runs up to this point.
- No for MP, because the cursory glance was enough to disinterest me completely. Consider looking elsewhere for comprehensive MP opinions.
- Can't really say anything constructive about performance, because I've only ran this game on i7-6700K/GTX 1080, which kinda means I overpowered the hell out of any possible performance issues, getting stable 120+ FPS in 2k resolution. There are lots of performance tests on YT, so I recommend looking there to find something closer to your rig.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I'd say I'm tired of the reboot genre. Admittedly, when I heard that a Doom remake was coming, I didn't pay any attention. Granted, it was the game that started it all - but it had tried to start it all again too many times.

Not this time.

iD produced one of the finest shooters I've ever played from a gameplay standpoint. Every shot, every kill, every death felt incredible. Nothing ever felt unfair - the controls are so tight, they almost felt like an extension of my arms near the end of the game. If I died a grisly death, it was because I wasn't playing hard enough.

Normally, I find this sort of hardcore experience annoying. I usually want to enjoy the story and shoot some things instead of needing to prove my skill to advance, but this game feels so good to play, I didn't mind in the least.

If you are a fan of shooters for either story or gameplay reasons, you should get this game. You will not regret it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA nonstop romp across Mars and Hell, punching, ripping and tearing your way to the heart of it all. I've not had this much fun since Halo 3 released for the Xbox 360. The game is extremely atmospheric and fun, going to great lenghts to reinforce the idea that you are DOOMGUY, back from hell to rip and tear once more.

When this game first had a trailer and gameplay video released, I sat in silence and slight disappointment due to DOOM 3, and how I felt it was going to inevitably be something akin to such, or a very simplistic and non-fun game would be the end result. I've never been more wrong in the entire 24 years I've been present upon this earth.

Pros:
- Absolutely astounding OST. I've streamed it in my car whilst hyping myself up for an all-night demon crushing spree.

- Highly customizable weaponry. From a triple fire attachment for the shotgun, to infinite micromissiles. This game has every weapon and customization that an aspiring demon hunter could ever desire.

- Very basic, but successful upgrade system. Allowing for upgrading of your ammunition reserves, your overall health, or your armor.

- Fast paced, adrenaline pumping combat. The hordes of demons rushing at you with only your weapons to trust will make you rethink the bland combat perspectives of new titles being released to this day.

- A plethora of exploration and secrets.

- An actually intriguing storyline, based upon a company called UAC which has roused the ancient DOOMGUY marine back to his senses to squash the demon hordes underfoot once more.

- The presence of OLD SCHOOL DOOM MAPS (<3) with updated enemies, but the same secrets, spawns, and so on. Carry your brand new arsenal of badassery into the past to face the demons once more with all new tricks.

- The returning weapons of the series feel fresh, and as devestating as they always have, further advanced with modifications found and applied.



Cons:
- DLC for the game seems to be centralized around Multiplayer (Whilst DOOM has always been about the campaign), and the Snapmap. They may become actually interesting at some point, or may decide to add Singleplayer DLC's, but for now they're focusing on Multiplayer.

- Save file shenanigans, resulting in some loss of progress even after the autosaves have supposedly triggered.

- (Minor complaint with non-remappable weapon slots on the 1-0 keys)

- Large complaint with the game freezing at random intervals. After spending an hour twice on a specific level in snapmaps, only to freeze on two different occasions. Only started happening after most recent update.\t\t\t\t\t
