\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe single-player campaign is excellent, featuring pulse-pounding action as you gleefully blast apart the demonic hordes and explore vast sprawling levels in search of secrets. The multiplayer is decent; the matches have a somewhat similar feel to Halo but with the addition of demons, powerups, health packs and loadouts. I haven't tried Snapmap yet, I may update this review when/if I do.

The game runs alright on my computer (which is a couple years old and has a high-res monitor). The graphics and models look good even on medium settings. My framerate dropped in certain areas during the campaign, but it generally wasn't a big issue. As far as glitches go, I did not notice any during the campaign or in the multiplayer.

Overall, it's a good game. It sets an example for what every AAA game should be like. I highly recommend buying it during a sale if you have any interest in shooters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA DOOM game at its core. A core that lays in its gameplay, it is all in the way your character move, the movement is nervous and non-stop. As soon as you enter the first room filled with ennemies you feel it, there's nothing like it; it's the way, the old way, no cover, no reloading; your life is to sprint on demons' giblets like a butcher on redbull / cocaine / exctasy.
-> THIS - IS - DOOOOOOM !!!!!!
One last word on the gameplay; the gore part is here, you'll have to rip off your ennemies with your bare hands here and there, it's an addition that never existed before, considering this feature genitor was an excellent mode created by an amateur (BRUTAL DOOM) I find it satisfying as hell and it's does not tarnish the game at all.


Special mention to the Soundtrack: the music is just too good to be true; I am not a metal head at all but here in this context it's just...perfect; relatively speaking the best achievment of the game's production.
Graphics are just top notch no complain whatsoever, but it do not impresses me as much as the musics' choice.

The sens of progression and replayablity are there. The game has a very nice set of difficulties, easy is easy and brutal is brutal. Last but not least, another very rare feature nowadays is the way achievments are handled in this games; it is a goold old school trait as they mean something to the game for they are the way to upgrade your weapons which is freaking awesome. Not being contempt enough with that single feature, achievments give you 2 trees of upgrades on each weapons for the replayability and your own challenges.

It's not perfect, it's not the 90's DOOM the father of all FPS; but I mean comon this game has so much well though details it's really hard for me to find a bad point to it except for the mutliplayer which looks like Unreal Tournament and for which I am not a fan at all. Quite an achievment for a big studio taking the time to thouroughly think about what has differentiated the original DOOM from any FPS you could have played in the last 2 decades.\t\t\t\t\t
