Introduction:
DOOM 2016 is a first-person shooter developed by id Software and released in 2016. As a fan of the DOOM series, I was excited to see how the game would modernize the classic formula.

Gameplay:
The gameplay in DOOM 2016 is fast-paced and intense, with players fighting against hordes of demonic enemies using a variety of weapons and abilities. The controls feel responsive and intuitive, and the game does a great job of making you feel like a powerful, unstoppable force. One of the standout features of the gameplay is the "glory kill" system, which allows players to perform brutal melee finishers on weakened enemies.

Story:
DOOM 2016's story is fairly straightforward, with players taking on the role of the "Doom Slayer" as they battle their way through a facility on Mars that has been overrun by demons. The character of the Doom Slayer is a silent protagonist, so the story is told largely through environmental storytelling and the interactions of the supporting characters. Overall, the story is serviceable but not particularly memorable.

Graphics and Sound:
DOOM 2016 has a gritty, industrial visual style that fits well with the game's tone. The graphics are generally solid, with some nice lighting and particle effects. The sound design is also strong, with excellent sound effects and a pumping soundtrack that perfectly captures the game's high-energy action.

Replay Value:
DOOM 2016 has a decent amount of replay value, with a variety of unlockable weapons and upgrades, as well as hidden collectibles to find. The game also has a number of different difficulty levels, so players can challenge themselves to complete it on higher difficulties. In addition, the game has a number of multiplayer modes, including traditional deathmatch and a mode where players must work together to complete objectives while being constantly pursued by powerful demons.

Conclusion:
Overall, DOOM 2016 is a fun and exhilarating shooter that stays true to the series' roots while also adding some modern gameplay elements. My main criticism of the game is that it has a relatively small number of environments, with the entire game taking place either in a generic facility, on the surface of Mars, or in hell. Despite this, the gameplay is satisfying and the visual and audio design are top-notch. If you're a fan of fast-paced, gory shooters, DOOM 2016 is definitely worth checking out.\t\t\t\t\t
