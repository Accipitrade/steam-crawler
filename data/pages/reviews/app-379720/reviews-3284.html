\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing through the first level, I can confidently say that this game has exceeded it's expectations in delivering the best of Modern Shooters, and Classic Shooters alike. Doom is a fast paced first person shooter that takes place on a Mars research colony, which has been invaded by demons from hell. You are the Doom slayer, or Doomguy, who has just woke up from an eternal sleep. After blasting through your prison and making your way outside, you are greeted by a Scientist who tells you about the attack and gives you instructions to survive.

The gameplay, is a revival of what made Doom, Doom. You can fast switch between weapons using the number keys, and you move at a very fast rate, compared to other modern shooters. The level design is very intricate and feels very real, yet is complex and gives a wealth of secrets that can help upgrade your gear. The weapons from the classic Doom are back, with futuristic looks, yet the same functionality as the old ones. Storytelling isn't a large component of Doom, but it's told with ghostly holograms, apparitions that are reminiscent of System Shock 2 or Bioshock. There are also a number of Logs which record the downfall of the facility itself.

This is the singleplayer. While the original games provided hacked on tools that were used to create maps, this game instead a dumbed down, simpler map editor known as Snapmap. Snapmap, does not allow you to designate your own verticies, or allows you to create your own structures, it instead gives you a pallete of 150 or more pieces and tells you to utilitze those to create a map. While this makes creativity dull, it's possible to make complex maps as 60% of the prefabs are hallways, junctions, and 90 degree turns. Snapmap also gives you a full logic editor, which allows you to set demon spawns, keycards, obstacles, weapons, custom enemies, and if you know programming, Booleans and variables. The only problem with this is, that there is a limit to what you can place. And you can reach that limit very fast.

The multiplayer, despite not being a modified singleplayer preset; is instead a entirely diffrent type of game. The movement is slow, it isn't exactly much of a arena shooter like Quake, and is more like Halo. It's likened to a free for all that promotes mindless, non - competitive fun. It's easy to get into, simple, and while somewhat slow, it has complex and large maps.

As a conclusion, if you're into singleplayer that includes a in depth campaign and fast paced action, go for it. If you're here for multiplayer arena shooters and a in - depth level editor, well we still have the classic games.

In other words, 9 / 10, for being fresh and original, breaking the COD shooter mold. -1 for a multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHearken back to the days of yore, before Call of Dudebro's, when a FPS was you, your infinite number of guns stored in who know's where, and a horde of monsters and undead abominations. When it was about how fas you could move and gun, while turning rooms into a bloody mass of body parts and pools of bodily fluids. This game brings back that feel while teaching new tricks to old dogs. The addition of mods to weapons, upgrades earned via missions on each level, a variety of quick kill moves, and double jumps with ledge climbs, all while seamlessly flowing together in the hectic run and gun feel of the game. There were a few hitches at first, updating drivers, slow load time for the first play, etc., but personally, I had about as much trouble as any new release game has. If you want that nostalgic feel of FPS, the need to listen to bass heavy rock style music while slaughtering rooms full of various demons, daemons, and undead freaks with any and every weapon imaginable, all with over the top gore and killing, than this is for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI understand why this game has a lot of people complaining, but this game was really enjoyable for me. The story had a lot of repetition; the glory kills get really repetetive, the enemies dont have too many attacks that they use, etc, but I found that the nearly non-stop action during that combat, and the platforming in between fights made up for the lack of variation. The weapons felt powerful and the combat can be difficult on the harder difficulties. (The object physics were apparently very weird, but I was too busy shooting enemies to notice) I liked the ability to go back to previous missions and collect certain collectables and secrets without needing to then finish the mission in order to apply those to the story. The ending really sets it up for a sequel/dlc addition/snap-map custom story, but it was done well.

The multiplayer wasn't bad, other than more guns and modes I didn't see much difference from the beta, and I'm good at it, which isn't something I can say often. Lots of people have a problem with the beta, but I was happy with just the campaign mode, combining that (regular and nightmare difficulties) with snap-map should make for enough gameplay to be worth $60.

tl;dr fast paced combat, not much of a story, snap-map isn't bad, plenty of secrets
don't buy if you hate games that take place on mars.\t\t\t\t\t
