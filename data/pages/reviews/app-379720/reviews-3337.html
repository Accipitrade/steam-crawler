\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSingleplayer = DOOM. 10/10

Seriously. The speed and intensity bring you right back to DOOM. The guns are interesting and varied. The levels are amazingly designed coupled with a good map. So far so good. I feel like I'm back in the Doom Marines boots.

The only issues I have is the demons that still teleport in. Very DOOM3 style. Oh and seriously limited ammo. 20 shotgun shells and 90 MG rounds? Yeah. Try 50 and 200. *edit - So the armor upgrades help here. So it slowly fades away as an issue* Last but not least the Pistol. It's a basic energy based, infinite ammo, charge it up sci-fi handgun that doesn't really fit DOOM. Nor does it sounds great. Very light pew pew pew.

Multiplayer = Decent but not DOOM. Now. Take your time to set up all of your controls and such. Make sure to adjust things. Interactions are different than singleplayer.
Its fast jumpy fight for power ups in close quarters. Has the card system (hack modules) from titanfall. Everything else is standard call of battleduty type stuff. Silly patterns on guns, plastic looking armor with lvling up, unlocking and two gun loadouts. If fast paced and fun enough I'll probably play it when I'm done with the campaign. 7/10. not bad not great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow! DOOM. This is exactly what we wanted.

For anyone looking for an action focused single player game, I can't recommend this enough. The combat is satisfying, gory, fluid, and NON STOP. The arsenal is big enough to support any sort of playstyle, and the progression is fulfilling enough to keep you exloring each level looking for upgrade drones. And speaking of which, the level design is some of the best I've seen for a non-open world shooter in YEARS. Yes, it is a corridor shooter, but the levels are not JUST corridors. The game is not bogged down by story, or cutscenes. DOOM puts you in the action and does NOT take you out for any reason. Most "story segments" can be completely ignored as they are told through holograms or voice comms, so if you just want to spend hours killing, GOPHER IT.

As far as multiplayer, if you didn't like it during the Open Beta, you aren't going to like it now. If you did like it, or didn't play it, it's definitely worth a try! I find it to be very fast paced and skill-based, including the Demon Rune pick-ups. The loadout system is annoying, and it's hard to justify, but it is easily ignored once you find your favorite weapon in your own loadouts... *cough*Super Shotgun*cough*.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM isn't your typical shooter. DOOM is not DOOM 3. DOOM is DOOM.

What I mean to say is that this DOOM feels so much closer to the originals than DOOM 3 (which was also a good game). The game is breakneck fast and unbelievably brutal sometimes. The story keeps you afloat and carries you onward, but its more of a canoe than a cruise ship. But that's O.K. DOOM makes up for it in gameplay over and over again.

DOOM was, for me, a refreshingly complete product. No missing content. Everything worked from first load. It looked beautiful and was well optimized. DOOM feels like a game made for fans for the single purpose of being fun. You know how sometimes movies end up being weird, often lesser than they could be, due to the studios making 'edits' and such? DOOM feels nothing like that. It feels it was designed exactly how they wanted it.

Going into DOOM I'll admit I was a little concerned to drop my $60. Was I going to enjoy the gameplay? I've always been more of a fan of slower 'tactical' FPS games. I started the game up, threw it into Nightmare! and watched as the game hurled me forward into this great atmosphere. There was very little hand holding. Barely any cutscenes. Just a constant invisible force behind you saying "GO! KILL EVERYTHING! BE AWESOME!"

And that's what DOOM does for you. You feel awesome playing it. It's amazingly brutal, often times incredibly hard, but so rewarding. Despite my numerous deaths during Nightmare! I never felt cheated. Every time I died I knew it was my fault. Very rarely do I feel like a game outplays me.

Speaking of which, I can really appreciate the time put into designing this game. The levels are extremely well designed. The mechanics are solid. The guns are fun and each have their own identity. Running around on Mars I noticed so many small things that really helped my experience that I fail to see in a lot of others games. For example, games will often shove a direction down your throat. "GO HERE!" in giant neon letters. DOOM, funny enough, was subtle. The levels are so well designed, you knew where you were going. Even then though, many of the levels are hinted with little green lights placed within the environment to let you know you're on the right path.

Enemies are great, varied, and never become useless. The game slightly goes back to the old days of 'learn, die, repeat' in video games but not so much so that you can't defeat something on your first try with enough skill. On Nightmare (I did not play any other difficulty) even the lowly Possessed Scientists (aka zombies) could take some serious chunks of health from you if you weren't careful. Imps, arguably the easiest demon within the game, are terribly effective in the late game when mixed with others.

The game builds off of itself in a superb fashion. While it never holds your hand the game design is so good that you learn as you go and are provided with the tools necessary to do the job. Every weapon, every upgrade, and every lesson is needed and was always 'on-time'. Never did I feel the game was too easy nor too hard.

DOOM is likely to be one of my favorite games of 2016 I imagine. Not only was it solid in every way and extremely fun, but it was also incredibly full of content compared to many AAA titles I've seen lately. Having just finished the game I have clocked in 25 hours of gameplay, much of it in long 8+ hour sittings. It was addictive and one of the few games I've felt deserving of the $60 price tag of modern AAA titles. I'm so pleased to see this. While the content may be the same, I'm incredibly appreciative to the studio for saying "♥♥♥♥ it! Add another level!"

The last thing I'd like to hit on with DOOM is it's unrelenting brutality, not only inside of the game, but on you- the player. Towards the end of the game I felt myself saying "Good God! Please let that be the last big insane battle. Surely nothing can outweigh THAT! That was almost unbeatable. That was insane!" only to have DOOM ♥♥♥♥♥-slap me across the face and tell me to "GO! KILL EVERYTHING! BE AWESOME!" again.

Having just finished the game I feel a wash of relief and sadness. On one hand, I'm finally free. I won. I did it. I have finished DOOM on Nightmare. On the other hand I'm sad that the game is over. I guess there is always Ultra Nightmare. Haha, J/K.

Go buy DOOM.\t\t\t\t\t
