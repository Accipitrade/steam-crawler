\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust beat the campaign around 20 minutes ago.

WOW.

What a complete and total adrenaline rush that was!

Gameplay: 10/10
Virtually perfect in the way it's designed, I never got frustrated or was bored. The frame rate was fast even at Ultra settings, and the variety of enemies kept you on your toes the entire time.

Story: 7/10
I took points off simply because it's not at the same level of other modern games, BUT this is the most lore-intensive Doom game ever, I love the Codex entries that you can optionally find.

Audio: 9/10
LOUD, that's all I can say! Some weapons I felt could sound better (Super Shotgun I'm looking at you), but overall the guns feel powerful and if you aren't satisfied with their current sounds I'm sure future audio mods can change that for you.

Visuals: 10/10
The Demons, the weapons, the environments, the Doomguy... all reimagined for a modern age while still keeping the classic looks perserved.

Overall: 10/10
The original Doom came out in 1993 and shook the world of gaming (and workplace productivity). Alot to live up to, and Doom 3, while enjoyable, didn't quite capture the original frantic gameplay of running around, dodging, and shooting mass amounts of demons. This new reboot of Doom I feel has finally brought back the original spirit of Doom with a fresh, modern style that will bring in new fans and please old ones!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR for those in a hurry. Few edits after finishing SP and playing some MP.
PROS:
+Looks good and runs good
+Combat fairly quickly ramps up into fast paced action reminiscent of older style FPS games
+Movement is fast and feels good
+The boss fights are all pretty fun and, more importantly, varied
+All the weapons feel unique and have their uses/quirks
+The soundtrack when in a big fight gets you pumped, has a very Djent sound to it mixed with some techno (which I could've done without but what can you do)
+All the old school enemies are back and they've been revamped to (in general) be much more dangerous. ♥♥♥♥ pinkie demons.
+There's a decent number of callbacks to the original games
+They expand on the lore of the Doom Marine and his origins which I found interesting
+The environments are all stylized and quite nice
+Lots of discoverable secrets
+♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Super Shotty
+SP DLC maybe? Sequel? Too early to tell

Cons
-No offense to Bethesda, but we all know the glitches that happen with their dead bodies. Only had two freak out so far but it does happen.
-There's a good bit of handholding going on in the beginning. In 5 hours of playing I only died from enemies twice, and one of those times was introducing a new enemy at the tail end of those 5 hours. EDIT: In the late game the difficulty both gets harder and gets a little easier at the same time. Harder cause you're fighting many more big guys at one time and a little easier because of some of the runes/weapon mods. You will die later on.
-Outside of big fights the soundtrack is virtually non existent

Neutral Points (things that you may or may not like):
+/-I've heard people say the multiplayer is great and others say it's not so great, yet to try it myself. EDIT: After playing a few games myself the multiplayer isn't bad. It's not quite as continuously fast paced as the single player because of the max player count/map size but It's still enjoyable with only a few minor issues so far. All in all I enjoyed the games and plan on playing it again.
+/-The platforming sections are mostly meh, particularly the longer ones. They're not especially challenging and mostly just serve as a way to break up the action and as filler.
+/-Glory Kills seem to be liked or hated, I'm neutral on them
+/-There's not a whole lot of story, but you're playing Doom. What did you expect?
+/-This game, much like the originals and older FPS games, has a predicatble pattern to the levels in that it mostly devolves into: kill, go hit button/pick up key/whatever, go to thing that you can now open, repeat. Thankfully the fights are fun enough that it works but some people may not like it.
+/-The levels aren't as maze-y as they were in the original Dooms.

Onto the in depth review.

There's not much to say about this game that it doesn't say for itself. It's Doom and it feels like a modern take of the original Doom. Combat is fun and while it doesn't start too fast paced it quickly picks up speed until you're fighting a couple revenants, a summoner, a hell knight, some imps, and some zombie dudes as you switch between weapons spraying them into the mass of bodies while sprinting and double jumping out of the way of the various projectiles coming at you. It's fun in the way only an old school FPS title like Doom or Serious Sam could be fun.

The weapons themselves are all pretty fun, my only complaint is that the base shotgun feels pretty wimpy and later on you'll just be using it for it's special fire mode. But the Super Shotgun more than makes up for this by being just as devastating as before, potentially even more so. Besides that you have the Pistol, Chainsaw, Heavy Machinegun, Chaingun, Rocket Launcher, Plasma Rifle, Gauss Cannon, and the BFG. Each one has a couple different mods that you can unlock and put points into to improve their versatility in combat and then upgrade further from there. All in all, they all feel different and unique and you'll really want to use all of them in some way. One downside is that your ammo count starts out very measly with the shotgun only being able to hold 20 shells and you have to use collectible items to increase that count. This wouldn't be an issue if it wasn't for the fact that those collectibles could also be used to raise your max health or armor as well. Personally, I just maxed out ammo before putting any into anything else.

On to Glory Kills. People seem to be fairly torn about them with a lot of people not liking them. Admittedly, they are kind of annoying in the beginning because a big push is made for them in the first couple missions instead of just letting them happen. But I quickly got used to them, and some are fairly fun to do in that Mortal Kombat Fatality kind of way. Like grabbing a guy by the leg when he's on the ground and then shoving his own foot into his face so hard his head explodes. Or ripping someone's arm off and slapping them across the face with their own hand. There is a lot of them as well with different ones triggering based on where you aim at on the enemy, with one enemy having at least 4 different ones based on if you aim for the head, legs, back, or arms. The downside is that you're likely to only end up seeing a couple for each class on any sort of regular basis. But, if the various reviews are anything to go by you'll either be ok with them or hate them, so be warned.

My only real issue with the game so far is that there are certain times where it seems like they're trying to make the game too easy. If you're low on health and gib a lower level guy they erupt in about 50 health, more if it was a glory kill. They do the same for ammo, though it's only one ammo drop per kill. These sorts of things kinda take the challenge out of the game, especially in the earlier levels. Later on this becomes much less of an issue because there's less little guys and your fights are often against a lot of bigger guys who can deliver a beat down if you're not careful. But, all the smaller pickups on the map respawn after a set amount of time or you go far enough way and come back, I'm not sure which. This means that there's a lot of times where you could go forward, get in a fight, and then retrace your steps back to ammo, armor, and health pickups and be totally fine. While this isn't an issue unless you abuse it, it is seriously easy to abuse. EDIT: In the late game enemies spawning health when you're low can be a godsend in the middle of a big fight. I'm still not sure if it was necessary considering the respawning supplies but later on it does help.

EDIT: The multiplayer is actually pretty fun. It's not as hectic as the singleplayer, though some of that might have to do with the lack of music. However, the lack of it feeling fast paced doesn't have anything to do with the game's mechanics (because they're the same from the singleplayer) but it seems to have more to do with map size and max number of players. Ultimately, the matches would feel more hectic and fast paced if the maps were a bit smaller and the teams a bit bigger. As is you can go without seeing someone in a full lobby of TDM for a minute or more, which in an arena shooter is a bit weird. Albeit going a full minute without seeing someone is a bit out of the ordinary but it happened about once a game for me. That is fairly excusable, but the main issue I think a lot of people will have is that the weapons aren't all equal. Guns like the heavy machine gun and sniper are likely to just sit in your arsenal never to be used while the Super Shotty, Rocket Launcher, and Chaingun will likely make it into every loadout and you will certainly see every one of those used by your enemies every game. That said, it's still pretty fun and worth playing a few games of.

In the end, I would reccomend it. It's fun to play with good action and just enough of story/background lore to keep you interested. All in all I think it deserves an 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely what I hoped it would be. It's a no nonsense, heart pumping, pupils dilated ride into hell. The campaign starts immediately and from then you don't stop. Everything is satisfying. The soundtrack is atmospheric and adrenaline inducing, the environemnts are gorgeous and every gun and glory kill feels visceral, powerful and heavy. You really feel like your ripping an arm off something. The multiplayer is equally as exciting, it's a fair balance between twitch shooter and some allowence for newer gamers. You'll find guys, who opt for the dirtier, scratched up armour, with subtle Quake logos who you know come from a time gone by and they will tear the place up - unless you call on a demon and take back control. Tables turn, I've lost games by one point and demolished a team 250 to nothing... Every game is new. I'd suggest playing it on the default difficulty first to enjoy the game, the story - or what there is of it. What I love is DOOM wants to have fun. Times when you think 'Oh look, another typical stereotype', you do the opposite. What a guy. What a Doomguy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay guys,

This game is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ badass. The game lives up to the hype. The game is a worthy successor of the most important video games to ever exist, DOOM 1 & 2.

Right away, you're shooting.

You tear demons apart with your fists.

The Campaign is wicked fun and violent as all hell.

The level design is so crazy. You don't realize just how good it looks unless you kill everything first, then stop and look around for secrets. You see the rocks of mars, jagged and sharp, the sparks flying around, the intricate level design is at the forefront, but the game is so fast paced you can blow through super detailed and cool spots simply cause there's always action going on.


The Multiplayer is the same as the Open Beta, but with more gamemodes, more weapons and pickups. The Multiplayer is fun, but I'm pretty damn pissed about this; There's no Deathmatch. Only Team Deathmatch, as well as about 7 more gamemodes. Did they just forget or something?

Bethesda pls. This is DOOM. DOOM invented Deathmatch. Why...

Finding a server is fast, but the big problem I have with the Multiplayer, is that there is absolutely no server choices. Your only option is Find a lobby. No option to host the server, customize, etc. My problem with TDM is that the rounds are capped at 75 kills. The average TDM game lasts about 7 minutes. It takes about 3 minutes of finding the lobby, filling it, waiting 1 full minute for the intermission to pass, about 15 seconds of scrambling players into teams, and a minute long loadscreen. THEN there's about 30 seconds once you load in, where the players choose their loadouts and finish joining the game. FINALLY you can begin playing.

Once you're playing, it's one of the most fun, chaotic, fps multiplayer shooters you've ever played.

The weapon selection in Multiplayer is fantastic, and there's a ton of gun appearance customizations. Nice feature.

The fact that it takes about 5 minutes to get into the game, for a 7 minute match, for 5 more minutes of waiting. Please for the love of god id Software and Bethesda if you're reading our reviews, allow us to set preferences, or some kind of server browser, where the server will have whatever point limit.

I have always mainly played Counter-Strike: Source and Counter-Strike: Global Offensive. The Source engine has a simple, but perfect server browser. You can find Valve servers or hosted servers. You can host the server yourself, and customize the server however you please. Some kind of server browser, or filter settings. Anything so we don't have to wait so long for a short game. I like endless Deathmatch mode.

My rating for DOOM 4 is:

10/10





10/10 because the Campaign mode is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ badass, the multiplayer is great, the graphics are the best I've seen in a video game. The game definitely is a great sequel to the legendary DOOM series. Everything about this game is badass. Soundtrack is perfect too.
