\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt goes without saying, but Doom 2016 does the FPS formula right in a way that games like Halo don't. No 2-weapon constraints, no regenerating health, and an enormous emphasis on mobility make Doom a heart-pounding love letter to Id's original shooter.

Every encounter you get in will feel unique, and you can't do things like camp in one spot and expect to survive; you can't heal by taking cover. The demons of Hell are relentless, and you must be the same in order to win. This works fantastically and as you zip through a combat zone, dismembering demons left and right, you're going to feel like a god of war.

Upgrades and weapons are a core part of the experience, and they are all done well. You're gonna want to experiment with a lot of different weapon configurations, as all of them have their uses, especially when mastered. Whether it's using homing rockets to take down agile foes, the Super Shotgun to blast down anything that gets close to you, or sniping the enemy with the HAR, every weapon feels powerful (well, except the Pistol, but you often won't need it). And never you fear, the BFG and the Chainsaw are prime parts of your arsenal. The Chainsaw, in particular, is really strategically designed in Doom, having very limited fuel, but allowing you to instantly kill any non-boss demons if you have enough fuel, while also providing a huge ammo resupply after you kill something with it.

Glory kills are another core part of the experience, letting you perform some absolutely grisly executions while also providing a good means of keeping your health topped up. The catch is that you have to get into melee range to do them, and you have to damage your victims enough without killing them outright. This means it isn't always wise to instagib a target at range if you're taking a lot of hits; you might want to pull your punches ever so slightly so you can have an opportunity to feed that Mancubus his own stomach.

The story for Doom isn't bad either. It's quite minimalist, and the Doom Slayer doesn't get any real personality, but the game hardly needs a lot of story. The setting, atmosphere and tidbits of lore you get are just right. It's dark, grim, and fits like a glove.

Overall, Doom 2016 is a much-needed shot in the arm for the FPS genre, and shows all of the pretenders how it's done, just like it did back in 1993. It's hectic, gruesome, and leaves you constantly wanting more. In my mind, rejecting this title would be an act of heresy.\t\t\t\t\t
