When I have played all the way through the campaign, my opinion may change, but thus far, its a campaign that I'll finish, and want to play again and again. It has well and truly revitalised a pure love of gaming. Its a game I don't play just because I'm bored, it's a game I play because I love it. I don't think everyone will feel as strongly about this game as myself, but it seems to be id's best work since the very first DOOM.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompletley different from Doom3 in a really good way. This one pays homage to the original doom games from the 90's by having a fast paced and intense gameplay experience, a simplified set of controls, and a ton of cool little details that only a player of the original games might notice (including classic maps that can be unlocked). However, a player who is new to the series will still apreciate them. They also did a really good job of linking the world of doom all together here with familiar monsters and environments that are seriously upgraded and crisp so it still feels like doom. This game has over-the-top amazing graphics and plays really smooth on high settings on my mid-level gaming rig, so the optimization is solid even this early after launch... props for that! And as a refreshing touch, the "Doom Guy" is no longer alone in the dark trying to survive, but a brutal incarnation of death and retribution hell bent on tearing as many demons limb from limb as possible. The gore in this game is... well, breath taking! The multiplayer is kind of a reskin of Quake Live, sadly. Which is fun, but pretty lack luster compared to other online shooters. The Snap Map faeture is really great. With tons of user created and published maps it seems like a way to endlessly play new content... as well as create it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was torn between buying this on pre-order, and getting Dark Souls 3. I settled on Doom mostly because it came bundled with Wolfenstein: The Old Blood.

Even without that bonus, I was not disappointed with my choice.

I personally always considered the original Doom a fairly overrated title. Not bad, and its mark on gaming history is undeniable, but there are other FPSes of the same era that simply did the same thing better. Doom 2016, though? I think this is what the original must've felt like to those first playing it in the early 90s. It is fun, fast-paced, intense, and utterly brutal. Within ten minutes I found myself remarking that I could absolutely buy this incarnation of the Doom Marine marching straight into Hell and kicking its ass. The game opens with you chained to a stone slab with zombies crawling towards you, and by the time your vision's cleared and you've figured out where your pants are, you've already killed half a dozen of them.

If I had to think of a way to describe Doom '16, I'd say it's like the original Doom and Metroid Prime had a baby, and you had that baby dress up like Master Chief and retell the plot of Dead Space. In between all the carnage and mayhem the game is pretty nicely atmospheric, with lots of gorgeous vistas and a silent protagonist who still manages to fully convey character through his sheer presence and lethality. Halo TOLD you that Master Chief is a 7-foot supersoldier, but you don't really get that feeling apart from the fact that all your marine buddies are a head or two shorter than you. Doom makes you FEEL like an unstoppable giant of a man, a solid wall of muscle and rage wrapped in metal.

The plot as said feels a lot like Dead Space, with the demon invasion the fault of the UAC, a Unitology-esque cult wrapped in a veneer of scientific study, who found a rift to Hell and realized could be used as a limitless font of clean energy. Things get out of hand, the wrong things are poked with sticks, and someone in charge goes entirely off-reservation and all Hell literally breaks loose. The big difference of course is that instead of a scared engineer with power tools having to clean up, it's the freaking Doom Marine, and he's having none of this.

The game isn't perfect - particularly late in the game it gets a bit too fond of using Points of No Return, something of a design faux pas when it comes to games with exploration elements in my book, and the platforming is occasionally frustrating with all the instant death pits - but the combat is easily the most visceral, satisfying, and intense I've ever seen in an FPS. I won the majority of my fights, only having significant difficulty with the bosses, but nonetheless I found myself letting out an audible sigh of relief every single time the dust settled. To me, that tells me that the game is wonderfully balanced to be incredibly taxing without being unfair. I'm left feeling I've earned every single victory, rather than having it handed to me by narrative convenience or through sheer bloody-minded repetition until the law of averages falls in my favor.

I'm not going to go into further analysis, all I can say is absolutely give this game a shot. Easily made it into my Top 5 FPS games, long before I got close to finishing it. Can't wait to see what comes next.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery much yes!!

The 90's Nostalgia is strong with this one. If you grew up playing doom on a windows 95 machine back in the day, this feels like a nice nod back to what made shooters good.. speed. There needs to be a resurgence of skill based speed shooters, and perhaps a agme like DOOM can show that the model is still viable.

There are two things I take issue with the game but are relatively minor compaints.

-It's not that the soundtrack is poor, however the pacing feels a bit off. For every awesome pumping metal track that plays, there are fights where there is a very consipicuous lack of any music and DOOM should to be constantly playing fast and visceral tracks to keep up the momentum. Nothing a good thrash metal playlist on spotify can't fix.

-There are several arena style boss fights toward the last 3rd of the games campaign that throw all the fun and frantic combat to the back burner. These fights dont rely on careful ammo conservation and hot weapon swapping and utilizing the glory kill system to survive. They are standard, paint by numbers, learn the rotation encounters that feel out of place.

Thankfully there are only a few of these throughout the game and the game does a good job of tossing you into ever more chaotic wave based arena fights as you hunt all the hidden powerups and weapon upgrades. There is a lot that could be covered in a long review but after having played through the game and feeling very satisfied overall with the finished product I can definitely recommend this to fans of the original who want a new entry that keeps the essence of what made DOOM popular while refining a few mechanics to keep things fresh. I was pleasantly suprised :)\t\t\t\t\t
