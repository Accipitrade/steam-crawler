\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWelcome to DOOM, a lightning-fast virtual reality adventure where you are the toughest space trooper ever to suck vacuum.

When I first read those words many years ago, I knew this game was going to be something different. That's the intro to the original Doom manual circa 1993. And over two decades since those words were first printed, the legacy is still going strong.

DOOM (2016) is everything that I expect from a Doom game. Big guns, much bigger demons, and one lone tough-as-nails Marine using the first to obliterate the second. Rest assured that this is an epic and action-packed assault through Mars, and hell, and back to Mars, and back to hell. (Repeated many times over a 15 hour campaign, twice as long as most FPS these days.) DOOM is a fantastic return to a time when games were about fun gameplay, story was something you had to dig for (instead of being spoon fed to you), bosses were challenging, and protagonists knew when to shut the hell up instead of yapping through the whole campaign.

While most shooters today encourage you to move cautiously, keep a finger on the reload key, and kill maybe a dozen or so enemies in a mission, DOOM takes things a different route. In DOOM, speed and aggression is the only way to get things done. If you back down from a fight, if you try to camp your enemies, if you try to hide in cover, you will lose. Over and over again. Play offensively, and always be on the move. This applies equally to campaign and multiplayer.

In DOOM, you will be moving 99% of the time. To help keep you from having to stop, DOOM does away with sprint-keys and weapon reloading. Instead, your weapons never need to be reloaded. So long as you have ammo in your inventory, you can fire your weapons. Rather than a sprint-key, your default speed is running and you can hit a key if you wish to walk. Movement overall is much less restrictive than in most shooters. By jumping towards any ledge, you can grapple on and quickly pull yourself up. You can also rapidly climb railings and vault over low obstacles. These mechanics are very fluent and far more versatile than they seem on the surface. While some games have mechanics like this that feel gimmicky, in DOOM they feel natural and work exceptionally well. In addition to this, fairly early in the campaign you will acquire a set of boots that let you double-jump (double-jumping is standard in the multiplayer). This combined with the ledge-climbing enables you to reach new heights.

But you aren't the only creature that can traverse the world with ease, nearly all of the thousands (literally thousands) of demons you will fight are just as agile as you. They can, and will, chase you all throughout the combat zone with surprising intelligence. Killing these demons is frantic, hellish, and very satisfying. The ability to melee weakened enemies for a quick finishing move (called "glory kill") is a welcome feature in DOOM. These glory kills might sound clunky and repetitive, but they are neither. Glory kills happen very quickly (so as not to slow down the action), and they have many variations. They are even contextual, so that what part of the enemy you are looking at when you hit the melee key will determine which glory kill you preform. These are not quicktime events and do not detract from the speed of the game. To aid in your assault, you will find numerous powerups that give you temporary abilities such as haste (makes you even faster), invulnerability, and berserk (allows you to glory kill demons instantly).

Completing various optional challenges during the game will allow you to unlock upgrades. Some of these upgrades take the form of runes which require you to beat a brief combat arena under specific instructions. (Example: Kill every enemy with only the super shotgun in 12 seconds.) You can equip up to three runes at a time and swap them out as you wish. Finding "field drones" throughout the game will unlock weapon mods which can be individually upgraded at your discretion. Once unlocked, weapon mods can be hot-swapped without pausing the game. Some of these are basic (burst fire mode for the shotgun), others are a bit more complex (chargeable 360 degree heatwave blast for the plasma rifle). As you play you will also find the corpses of fallen UAC guards, whose suits contain a power chip that can be used to improve your own suit's abilities. Also scattered around the environment are caches of "Argent energy", which allow you to choose one of three specific things to upgrade (armor, health, or ammo capacity). These upgrade systems allow you to tailor the game to your play style, and offers some replay value beyond the obvious fun factor.

id Software knows that all work and no play makes DOOM Marine a dull boy, so your experience isn't strictly limited to killing demons (unless you want it to be, then you can just do that). There are dozens of secrets to be found, all of which unlock something interesting. These are not easy secrets, either; they will require you to search and think. The most notable of these are the secrets which open up a hidden door to a small chunk of a level from the original 1993 Doom. These levels are classically textured and feature items and demons exactly where they should be. These are much cooler and more dynamic than the boring and static E1M1 in Rage. But that's not all, finding a small section of a 1993 Doom level also unlocks the full level which you can play from the menu. And in addition to the true secrets in the game, there are countless Easter eggs, some obvious and some not, that call back to earlier Doom games.

On top of this lengthy and challenging singleplayer action, you will find a variety of unique and adrenaline-pumping multiplayer modes and an impressive amount of character and weapon customization. playing the multiplayer rewards you with various hack modules which can be equipped and swapped out during the next match. As you level up, you will earn new weapons and equipment as well as available demons. Pick up the "demon rune" to transform yourself into one of several demons, each with their own special abilities, and decimate your enemies. If you find yourself on the wrong side of the demon, you'd better search for a power weapon to assist in tackling him. You will also be rewarded with different armor pieces, colors, patterns, and taunts, which you can use to customize your character. Oh and don't worry, you can glory kill both humans and demons in the multiplayer when they are low on health.

I could go on for much longer (there is ten times as much content in DOOM as there is in most FPS games), but this review is more than long enough already. DOOM is exactly what a Doom game should be: fast, hellish, violent, and very fun. id Software (as far as I know, Bethesda did not make this game, they only published it) has done an outstanding job of bringing back a legendary classic. This game absolutely looks like, plays like, and evokes feelings of, the original Doom. If you have even a remote interest in FPS games, DOOM is an absolute must-buy.\t\t\t\t\t
