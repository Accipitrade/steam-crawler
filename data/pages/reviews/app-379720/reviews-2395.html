\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game disappointed me. I can see why people like it but there's just so many little naggling irritations that all pile up to make the game almost unplayable for me. Which is a shame because there's a good game in there somewhere. The game, while beautiful, is often a visual cluster . I've had instances where enemies were literally right in front of me yet I wasn't sure if I was aiming at an imp or a bulkhead.

Even the weakest enemies in the game feel like absolute bullet sponges who often absord absurd amounts of damage while able to also deal absurd amounts of damage back to you at range. Then there's those damned glory kills. Yeah let me just rush into a bunch of enemies while getting shot from all sides for 5 health and 2 ammo. The ammo capacity is criminally low as well. There's some bad hit detection here and there.

The game is stupidly dark in certain areas and there's no flashlight to be found. There was a point where a very obnoxious audio log began repeating itself on an infinite loop that could be heard no matter where you go in the level. That's what made me quit playing. It started playing again for about the seventh time while I was killing zombies and I just said "Nope!".

Oh yeah and the game likes to pop up these helper things on the border of the screen every time something new happens. It removes all control from you and I'm not sure what you're supposed to press in order to get rid of it. I just kind of hammer keys and it eventually lets me keep playing. While the repeating audio log was what made me rage quit I'd say the frame rates are the real nail in the coffin for this game. The engine is pretty well optimized but I was getting 80+ fps in open areas with lots of stuff visible and then my frame rate would suddenly drop below 30 in an enclosed environment because of a single fire or, something. This was especially fun while trying to fight enemies that move like they are on crack.

So yeah. That's DOOM 2016. I was expecting simplicity and got a bunch of crammed down my throat. I feel like I need to boot up ZDoom with the Brutal Doom mod now just to cleanse my palette.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is beautiful. I remember playing the original as a kid (and wondering at the mystery of the vertical aiming), and loving it. The campaign is awesome, and we all know how rare high quality AAA single-player campaigns are these days, especially in the FPS genre (which is often multiplayer-only now).

The story is exactly what one wants from a Doom game. A powerful super-soldier slaughters demons in order to save a base on Mars from experimentation gone wrong. Without saying a word, the Doomslayer has a better characterization than many videogames protags: from the way he looks at the destruction wreaked around him as his guide talks about the greater good, to the way he smashes the precious machinery that caused the disaster with impunity, he conveys everything you need to know about him. Nobody wants a Doom game with characters whining, indecisive second thoughts, or with some bullsh!t drama story - we wanted a man who solves his problems with brute power and guns, and that's exactly what was delivered. The lore is great too, read to you by demonic voices that narrate the terror your character has inflicted upon Hell itself for millenia.

I want to mention that I do not consider this a horror game, as I generally feel that horror games are meant to make you feel helpless, scared, and weak. In a horror game, one is trapped with some set of monsters and is trying to escape; here, the monsters are the ones trapped with you. I can imagine the sadistic glee felt by the character when the music ramps up, because that's how I feel. And that's exactly how a Doom game is supposed to make you feel. I rather love the atmosphere of the abandoned, corrupted Mars facility and the roaring, fiery, occult, desolate lanscapes of Hell.

Anyway, graphics are perfect, particularly the glory kills (only minor wish is for persistent bodies); gameplay is the speed we all wish for (no sprint button, because why would you want to "saunter" in a Doom game?!) and controls are as smooth as can be. There's even easter eggs to throw you back to the old Doom games! About the music... I literally bought the album and have consistently listened to it for months. That's all I need to say I think.

In summary, this game was made by REAL GAMERS who love Doom and SKILLED DEVS & ARTISTS, not a bunch of hipsters and idiots infesting studios like parasites who care nothing for the franchise itself (or even dislike what it was). This game is a homage to one of the greatest and most influential games in history, and, in being so, will itself go down as one of the best games and highest quality additions to the franchise of all time. It's certainly one of my favourites.

13/10
