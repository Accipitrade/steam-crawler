\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was 13 when the original Doom was released, and I played that game to death, even going so far as to spend all summer recreating my school and playing deathmatch in it (here in the UK we can't get hold of machine guns at the corner shop, so we have to pretend we're shooting up the school). It was fast paced, visceral and fun to play; three criteria which seem largely missing from modern shooters. This reboot recaptures what it was which made the otriginal Doom so much fun - You need to keep moving or you'll die. You need to be firing almost constantly or you'll die. You'll definitely need to watch your health or you will certainly die. None of this "hide behind a rock and you'll go back to full health" nonsense. If you land a stylish kill on an enemy you'll get bonus heath pickups, but you'll need to be careful not to accidentally off them before you can make the glory kill. Make no mistake, this is a game which requires skill. You WILKL die, but you will be having so much fun you won't care. The engine is also very well optimised, running a solid 60fps on Ultra settings on my GTX780 rig. There was one level where for some reason the framerate dropped to low 40s, but the majority of the game was as smooth as pulped giblets. Buy this game or you will die.\t\t\t\t\t

I mean what's not to love?

Wide open, clever, level design with tons of ways to approach each level and many secrets to find

Rip and tearing demons apart whilst listening to the heaviest of heavy metal

Stunning graphics and art style

More gore than you could ever possibly need

Extremely fun and satisfying weapons

The only problem people seem to be having with this game is the Multiplayer, which I don't really understand. I've had a ton of fun, albeit only a couple of hours, playing it. I think if you're playing multiplayer, you need to stick to the team and objective based game modes, and avoid the basic ones like TDM.

Overall, one of the best games in the last 5 years. Highly recommended to anyone to loves to blow up to heavy metal
