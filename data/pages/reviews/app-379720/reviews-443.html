\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe 2016 id Software game is the first Doom game I have played since Doom 3. The premise of the game is familiar to those who have played Doom. The protagonist, a space marine, is silent from start to finish. There is a monotonous feel that comes with going from one level to the next, but some of the level designs are masterfully done. There is also the appropriate soundtrack inspired by the 1980s metal bands.

Doom is a first-person shooter set on Mars. The default pace of the space marine is run, so the walk is a toggle on the keyboard. It illustrates that the game is designed to be fast paced. The object of the game is still the same as it was in 1993: shoot everything that moves and keep moving. The opportunity to crush demons after shooting them enough times feels satisfying, as it results in glory kills. It may look gross to see a demon explode, but the rewards are irresistible because you gain health and ammo while other demons are attacking you.

While the levels in the single-player campaign feel mainly linear, there are occasions that require you to backtrack so that you can find the proper keycard to move on. There is also a break in the action when you find secret platforms and secret areas that reward you with critical resources that will help you survive the onslaught of diverse demons. The resources can help your character’s suit, armory, and weapons. The ability to improve your character’s abilities is what gives the game a bit of an RPG feel in an FPS game.

There are some levels where powerups such as berserk, quad damage, and haste are conveniently placed, but they give you the feeling that many demons are lying in wait to ambush. It is this part of the level design that challenges your FPS abilities since these are the tensest moments. All demons great and small suddenly and desperately attack you. Some Imps throw fireballs at you while hanging from walls while different variations of the Mancubi attack you, Revenants fire rockets while on the ground and in the air, and agile Hell Knights try to chase and pummel you. Cacodemons no longer look like the tomato heads that they looked like in past Doom games as they look uglier than ever. It is this type of action that makes you care less about linearity or monotony that accompanies these levels.

The BFG, the most powerful weapon in the game, is available again. It kills many monsters at once, but the ammo is scarce, forcing you to use it wisely. Your strategic use of the BFG is crucial in determining how you will survive tense moments, including boss battles.

The graphics are part of the masterful design of the levels. The skies have great shades of various colors. Some of the secrets include the classic Doom levels that are stark reminders of the original Doom. You go from 3D design to 2D design upon entering the classic Doom levels, but the 2D designs look great in their own right.

Doom may seem like a simple-minded, straightforward game, but the tense battles make this game compelling, especially for fans of the Doom franchise. The level designs are appealing and the battles against diverse monsters make Doom worth playing from the first chapter to the last. The RPG elements make the space marine noticeably more powerful which makes this protagonist more fun as the game progresses.

My Score: 8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSomeone's mediocre idea for an original FPS, shoehorned into doom, possibly the single least appropriate franchise vehicle for it imaginable. Who remembers Doom, kids? The game where you do loads of melee animations and have to be careful not to shoot too much or hurt any of the enemies too bad? That's the one.

Before: ripping through maps at lightning speed
Now: loads of samey sealed off kill rooms
Before: lots of contextual audio clues for enemies, all of which are distinct behaviourally and can be easily provoked to shoot each other for more mayhem
Now: pretty much everything warps in immediately around you with zero announcement, pretty much anything that shoots does so at low volume, pretty fast speed and pretty accurately
Before: shooting enemies dead to emphasise multi-kills and prioritising groups, enemy HP, range, etc, using the appropriate weapon
Now: shooting enemies dead deprives you of i-frames and HP, and you're constantly locked in tiny rooms with no range shooting possible

The combination of constantly being sealed off in a tiny room with teleporting enemies, and being punished for overkilling/exceeding HP limits by losing your i-frame executions and HP, means the game is basically a series of start-stops, punctuated by even more annoying canned mini stops for executions. The pace of the game is basically that of the equally mediocre Doom 3, without any of the more interesting tools the expansion pack added.

Genuinely, incredibly suspicious of the tastes of anyone who rates this as good.\t\t\t\t\t
