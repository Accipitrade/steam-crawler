\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh man, I said I'd make this review back in 2019 and now here I am. A significantly different person, probably in more bad ways than good. I feel like I feel like I'm supposed to like Doom 2016 more than I do actually like it, if that makes senses at all.

I first played it on Xbox, good game. I got it on here and played through it again, still a good game. But, like, it's a decent to strong 7, not a solid 9 or 10 like people act like on here. Eternal is better, no doubt. I'd give that a strong 9 or even 10. But 2016 just isn't that impressive to me? Like, again, it is quite good. But I can't see myself playing through it again like I can Eternal. And that mostly comes down to a couple of things.

First, the first couple levels are just not being that fun to replay. The horizontal progression is NOTICEABLE in this game because the first couple levels are so barebones, to the game's own detriment. The game also starts you with the dinkiest pistol in modern gaming, which is honestly just a bad game design decision. It's not a fun weapon.

Second, Doom (2016) doesn't so much have push forward combat as push sideways combat. The best, safest way to play Doom (2016) is to circle-strafe and backpedal around enemies. You aren't playing a juggernaut, you're playing a rat that scurries around and sometimes has i-frames. You can beat the whole game just with the super shotgun, which is fun sure, but it's also boring. It does not challenge you to be a better gamer. You are not forced to git gud. You get strong gear and then you circle-strafe around every arena because the ways you get health, armor, ammo, etc. are not directly tied to being in close range to enemies, unlike in Eternal. Eternal forces you to make use of your whole arsenal, and it so much more fun for it.

Doom (2016) is 100% worth buying and playing, just make sure you don't play Eternal first.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort version: Oh cool, they made a whole game based on Mick Gordon's DOOM album!

Long version: The familiar Doomguy returns after a painfully long hiatus with some new tricks up his sleeve. Similarly, this game feels like the next step in the natural progression of not just the DOOM series, but of the FPS genre as a whole.
Just like classic DOOM, constant movement is the key to survival; you move or you die, and there's no cover system, regenerating health, or weapon reloading to slow you down. Unlike classic DOOM, however, these enemies are faster, stronger, smarter, and can swarm you from every angle in mere seconds. That all may sound intimidating at first, but the new Glory Kill mechanic rewards player aggression with the perfect incentives to keep you pushing forward: health pickups, stylish animations, and a quick moment to catch your breath while you strategize which infernal baddie to blast next.
While the combat itself gets a little repetitive, it's never dull, and new enemies and weapon mods are introduced at a steady pace across the campaign's 14-ish hours. And even though the maps are littered with reused assets and the same dreary color palette, the surrounding scenery is always breathtaking.
Oh, and the soundtrack is pretty okay, I guess.
Minor gripes aside, this is one hell of a game (pun only partially intended), and one every FPS fan should at least try once.\t\t\t\t\t
