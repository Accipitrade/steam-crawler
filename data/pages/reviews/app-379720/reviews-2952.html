\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the perfect successor to the original. If you preferred Doom 3, this game is not for you.

Be warned: if you liked custom .wads in Doom, that's not a feature, unfortunately. No modding, and SnapMap is kind of disappointing. Multiplayer is not really Doom, either. Clicking the multiplayer button actually closes Doom and launches an entirely separate program. It's quite literally a different game using the same assets, not a good thing. Guns feel powerful and brutal in singleplayer, but are basically nerf guns in multiplayer. Battles are epic, fast-moving, and acrobatic in singleplayer, but in multiplayer it feels a lot like a more boring Halo, point your gun at the guy and hold down the trigger until one of you is dead. What should have been a fast-paced Quake-like experience is instead an unecessary CoD clone, and the unique Demon Rune mechanic isn't good enough to save it.

I want to reiterate that the single player is INCREDIBLY GOOD. In my opinion, that alone is worth the full price of admission. It looks, it sounds, and it feels like everything I wanted a new Doom game to be. The guns are the main characters, the chainsaw is fun as hell, the armor, ammo, and health pickups feel good to use in a world of cover-based regenerating health, and holy crap that motherlovin' BFG...

But if multiplayer and SnapMap are important to you, you will be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t
