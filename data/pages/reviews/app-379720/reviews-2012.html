\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't like the demo, but I bought it on sale for $6.59 on the off chance I'd like the full game.

I do.

I tweaked the game settings to not have motion blur or depth of field, and I like the immediacy of that look MUCH better than default. DOOM runs smooth as butter almost all the time on my Pentium/GTX 1050Ti, except for the odd occasion where TONS of monsters and/or smoke effects are on the screen at once, then it stutters into I'd guess 20s for a couple seconds, but it's so infrequent I'm mostly absorbed by taking in the DOOM universe.

Love the art direction, the music is uniquely suited to modern DOOM while hailing to its roots, the movement is WAAAAAY slower than old-school DooM yet fluid, and the story is thus far actually interesting.

Much like old-school DooM, I get lost all the time, especially when back-tracking to open doors with the key I just found. Unlike old-school DooM, DOOM has crazily multi-dimensional combat and navigation, so the awesome map is a must for me personally.

Gonna finish it, gonna do it again on progressively harder difficulties.\t\t\t\t\t
