\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy first review, but I thought it necessary. First and foremost, two thumbs up! I am a man born in the 80's but very much of the 90's. I have fond memories of quake arena, the quake 3 engine (it's countless powered games), and unreal tournamnet. DOOM feels like a modern reimagining of those games. That is, it brings that silky smooth, fast, chaotic fun with nice touches of modernity (melee system, and a limited attribute progression system). However, at its core, it is a non-stop demonic bloodbath that makes the player feel like a one man/woman recking crew cleaning up hell on mars. I can't recommend this game enough. AND, I think it's obligitory if you want that late 90's/early 2000 FPS gameplay that's been quite lacking as of late. I went into this game not really looking for much, and in a somewhat passe manor. I've not read any reviews--so this might not be congruent with you're favorite reviewer. I'm also playing this on ultra, on a GTX960--modest card, with 8gigs of Ram--and runs with a smooth and steady frame rate. The video options allow for a great deal of customization. My machine is not a gaming machine--but the game runs fantastic (a testament to the developer). I would urge you to do a quick look over the web if you have concerns of running the game. You'd really wanna play this at a high framerate-it's very fast/twitchy.\t\t\t\t\t
