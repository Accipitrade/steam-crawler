\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe campaign is really intriguing to play as it is a lot of fun to upgrade and improve your weapons and to experiment. There is a special touch to the gunplay in this game because you can upgrade not only the weapons but also your armor, equipment and general stats. It is amazing to create your own playstyle and improve it by spending upgrade points.
The gameplay in general is fast-paced, but you will get adapted to this very quickly. The enemies are well-designed and interesting to look at and fight against. Only some of the boss monsters are too boring and too generic in my opinion. They should have been way crazier and special. Also, some levels (especially in Hell) are underwhelmingly unspectacular.
But overall, I love the fast pace and gory style with a lot of blood, brutal glory kills and chainsaw melee. The entire singleplayer campaign is a wonderful, back-to-basics FPS game, that every or action fan should play.
Unfortunately, as this has already been released in 2016, multiplayer matches are sometimes difficult to find. There are not that many players left (or maybe there have never been many players online at all). It should be fine to spend some hours online and the constant levelling, achievements and unlocks are motivating and will make you keep going. But I don't think that you can have fun in multiplayer with this for too long.
So you should buy it to play the campaign several times on several different difficulties and have some fun in mulitplayer but you should not expect hundreds of hours of playtime.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has quite a few features that will appeal to everyone. If Doom Eternal doesn't have SnapMap or the ability to make custom user content, I won't bother getting it because that is a feature of THIS game that keeps me coming back.

Doom Eternal adds in the sync kills of Brutal Doom (a fanmade mod for the original Doom 2) where you can get ammo, health, and armor from killing enemies in case you need them. Sadly, this seems to be the excuse not to have more melee weapons, so if you were trying to make a Medieval-Themed snapmap level you will be disappointed you can't create custom weapons. It also did away with reloading that was in Doom 3, which some people liked, I felt indifferent about either way.

What Doom has that Doom: Eternal is doomed to not have is the community that grows and is nurtured by SnapMap and how easy it is to make fun levels by anyone, for anyone. Instead of a few overrated map types being the only thing you can do (which I fear will be the case in Eternal) you can make many types of levels you want in DOOM's SnapMap! Though outdoor levels are quite limited given it chiefly uses the campaign's assets and assets from Classic Doom.

I've had this game since Christmas of 2018 and play it on and off, but the Snapmap is what keeps me coming back. If the people making Eternal are smart, they will bring SnapMap back because what kept the original Doom series and Quake series communities alive for so long was the communities built around the ability to make custom user content such as maps, mods, and custom models. The fact that the glory/sync kills were inspired by Brutal Doom are evidence that taking away the ability for fans to add to the experience with their own creations and spin-offs would not be a wise decision. Even if not everything that came out of SnapMap wasn't a work of art, we would have never seen the maps that were by not permitting them to exist in the first place.\t\t\t\t\t
