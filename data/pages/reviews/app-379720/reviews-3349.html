\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll in all when it comes to the game for Single player, Multiplayer, and SnapMap features this game is worth every penny.

Single player,
In depth environments that have plenty of atmosphere and little hidden places for you to scavenge goodies and dolls (Fistbump). The attitude of Doom guy as you progress within the game is literally him giving zero ♥♥♥♥♥ about anything. You play as a badass, that’s it. The weapons and upgrades you get for them are pure awesome, the armor upgrades can assist you for much harder levels and difficulties, the Runes are decent adding more of a 'perk' thing within the game. More than plenty Demons to kill and it makes me wanting more, which leads to SnapMap features!

SnapMap,
What’s better than killing hordes of demons by yourself? KILLING THEM WITH YOUR FRIENDS OF COURSE! The features of snapmap go way more in depth than I'm even able to explain. You could do almost anything with what it offers and from what I've played, I haven't even scratched the surface of the possibilities. Not even a few hours ago before this review I was playing with a friend and the game mode was literally playing Hide-and-Seek with 2 Hellknights and 1 baron. Craziest thing I've ever done in a game and it was perfect. Dark rooms, custom smoke clouds everywhere, and the only thing keeping you from getting caught was when you could hear the stomping of their feet and their gnarly ass growls as they got closer to you.

Multiplayer,
I played Closed-beta and for the most part it was OK at best. Definitely had some balancing issues and to be honest it still does to an extent. Open beta was improved with running speed but that was the only thing I noticed. Finished game weapon balancing is noticeable and much appreciated because when I played the beta it was nothing but Vortex Rifles, Super shotty, and Rocket launchers.
