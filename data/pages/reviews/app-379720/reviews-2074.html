\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA beautiful, lightning fast shooter built around the old fashioned formula of games like Quake and the original DOOM, but with some modern action thrown in there to spice things up. DOOM has no shortage of action, sure to please any fan of the FPS genre.

There are a lot of things this game does well, and only a few things it doesn't. Gameplay flows really well and the action is fast paced. Glory kills are insanely brutal, but have a learning curve where you can choreograph your kills in a way that feels fun and stylishly lethal (and the chainsaw oh my GOD). Map design is very well done; maps aren't too linear and there are secrets everywhere to provide a break from the action and allow you to upgrade your weapons and armor past their expected lethality (as well as find some classic maps). These secrets and upgrades add a tremendous amount of replayability to the game, as well as some depth to the levels to make the doomslayer more lethal.

While still on the topic of upgrades, the game's sense of progression is really meaningful and lets you feel really powerful. Finding secrets give you better guns earlier in the campaign, completing challenges allow you to upgrade your guns past their normal potential (called mastery challenges; how does firing a 200 rocket volley at helpless demons sound? Yeah, it's pretty freaking awesome.)

But that's not to say that DOOM is mindlessly violent, either. The game's mechanics are very carefully incorporated in a way that you have to prioritize targets to keep you alive. If you find yourself running low on ammo, pull out the chain saw and replenish it (but the chainsaw has very little fuel). Running low on health? Maybe use the shotgun for a bit so that you can glory kill your next demon and guarantee some health. Everything has a purpose, but it's not intrusive and keeps you on your toes and in control.

However, DOOM does have some flaws. Action can (occasionally) get repetitive, you tend to fight a lot of the same enemies over and over again. The devs do counteract this by tossing in weapons, upgrades, and secrets to chase, but it does happen on occasion. Multiplayer feels a bit generic and doesn't really capture what made the campaign so great. There are still good times to be had in the multiplayer, but it isn't the game's main selling point by any means.

Overall, DOOM 2016 is a fantastic game. It's a ton of fun, and the brutally fast-paced action is extremely fun and satisfying. I recommend this game to anyone who enjoys a good shooter.

9/10. This game will probably be one of my "yearly playthrough" titles.\t\t\t\t\t
