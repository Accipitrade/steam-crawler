Doom (2016)

Not only one of the best first person shooter games I played in 2016, one of the best games of 2016. Period. A perfect example of how to create a successful reboot, Doom (2016) captures all of the magic of the original Doom games, and makes it better, gorier, and more bad ass! Fast paced and hard as hell, this game will keep you entertained for the full ten to fifteen hour campaign.

Play as the Doom Slayer, an ancient warrior who has been freed from his stone sarcophagus, to send all demons of hell, back whence they came. You don’t need anyone’s help, just ammo. When that runs dry, chainsaw enemies into a fountain of ammunition. Health running low? Rip demons aparts with your bare hands for extra health drops. A fun and entertaining mechanic that keeps the action fast and bloody.

While the ending is lackluster, especially compared to how well the game begins and peaks towards the middle of the campaign, Doom (2016) is still a great overall experience from start to finish.


Multiplayer

If you like classic, fast paced, arena shooters such as the original Doom, Quake, and Unreal Tournament, then you might enjoy the multiplayer of Doom (2016). I say might because the multiplayer is a strange hybrid of old and modern day shooters. Weapons and power weapons are not dispersed across the map, you create loadouts similar to Call of Duty. Loadouts, weapons, and abilities are unlocked as you rank up. Players with more time in the game will have a distinct advantage compared to new players jumping in for the first time. It wasn’t my cup of tea, but your milage may vary.

Arcade Mode

A very fun take on the campaign that adds a decent amount of replay value. Shoot for the highest score on any of the campaign missions across three different difficulty levels. Increase your score multiplier by chaining kills, collecting pickups and powerups, and performing various kinds of skill kills (from above, gory kills, etc).


SnapMap

SnapMap is the custom map and game editor in Doom (2016). Surprisingly very deep, you are only limited by your own imagination. One of my favorite SnapMaps was a Call of Duty style zombies mode. Similar to Call of Duty, you would earn money per kill and per round survived, allowing you to purchase bigger and better guns and armor. Provides many tools for the creative, and provides a lot of replay value for those who love the Doom (2016) engine.


Summary and Target Price Point

Doom (2016) is a solid 8.5 out of 10 first person shooter providing between ten and twenty hours of gameplay for the completionist. While I have nothing but praise to say about the game, it is rather short. For that reason, this game is an absolute steal at $10, and a great deal at $15. Still a good purchase at $20 for fans of the series and nostalgia.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou are the thing that goes "BUMP" in the night. You are the jump scare, the fear, the dread that keeps all the little demon babies awake at night. You are the curse the dark ones utter between trembling lips. You are...really good at killing things!

I love this game, and I can't say that about many other games. If you like adrenaline-soaked, fast-paced FPS action, then this game is for you. If you like taking your aggression out on the demon hoardes with eloquent brutality, this game is for you. If you are a red-blooded member of the human race who doesn't mind games centered on killing demons, and if you have a system that will allow you to admire how strangely beautiful this game looks, then please, don't waste any more of your time and get your DOOM on already!

Doom is a first person arena shooter with a very simple (yet engaging, in my opinion) story meant to hang the action on. You are the Doomslayer, an interdimensional being who was so good at killing demons they locked you away. You fight your way through various levels with the purpose of closing the gates to hell.

The level and character designs are wonderful. The sound effects are amazing, and the score is simply the best. Gameplay is smooth and satisfying, and there are a lot of customizations and unlockables to help your character fit your play style. I, for one, enjoyed reading all about the backlore of the world, and I found myself relishing the time spent in exploring, which is something I rarely enjoy in games.

Two more things to run by you: The pacing of this game is perfect, and I that is not hyperbole. I really do find the pacing of this game to be perfect. The battles kept me on the edge of my seat the entire time, and each one was followed by a brief respite that was just right. And after a little exploring, right when you feel like you might get bored, that's when the action picks back up. Similar to this was the actual mechanics of each fight. Doom rewards aggression and movement. You cannot sit still and look for cover. They will find you! And the ammo seems to run out in just the right way, never quite leaving you defenseless but forcing you to switch those guns around and try some new strategies. Astounding.

The second thing is how funny Doom is. The whole adventure is very tongue-in-cheek, and I had several belly laughs at the subtle humor the developers threw in.

All in all, Doom is an extremely satisfying experience, and well worth your time and money. But if you don't want to spend the whole $60, Doom has gone on sale quite a bit, so remember what your elders taught you: All good things come to those who wait. (Unless you wait too long and the sale ends...)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite simply, one masterpiece of a game and an extremely enjoyable experience overall.

Doom I and II are my favorite games of all time, yet when I heard about this installment back in early 2016, I thought it wouldn't have much to offer and it that it would be an empty game that either relied too much on nostalgia or just followed every trope from other recent FPSes.

Boy was I wrong. And man was I blown away.

This game takes every good thing from FPS of the nineties and the current century and mixes them into an unforgettable trip. Once again, you assume the role of the Doomguy, a badass super-soldier (although there's a twist in this game, play it and you'll find out ;) ) who goes around kicking the ♥♥♥♥ out of demons in the industrial facilities of Mars and the tortured landscape of Hell, using chainsaws, double-barrelled shotguns and big ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ guns. DOOM rarely ever stops dead in its tracks as plenty of monsters will pour from every direction to try and rip your guts apart. Many of your beloved guns are back to help you fend off your favorite monsters.

One very cool addition to this game is the upgrade system. By finding little robots throughout the game and winning optional challenges, you unlock special abilities to make your character even stronger than he already is. From your armor, to your weapons, to other passive capabilities, you can pretty much evolve in the way you want. Another fantastic addition are the Glory Kill sequences: when a demon has been critically injured, they will stagger for two seconds, allowing you to run up to them and perform some crazily violent finishing moves, from ripping their horn off and clobbering them on the head with it, to tearing their heart out and stuffing it down their throat. Delightfully disgusting. These animations are gory enough to make an impression, yet short enough not to interrupt the gameplay.

The atmosphere is spot on. There is a (very detailed) storyline in this game that you can uncover through diaries and codexes throughout the game, but the plot is rarely ever forced upon you. After all, this IS DOOM. Yet most of the characters you'll meet are likeable and there is some subtle body language that the Doomguy is prone to express.

The level design is very good. The main game is made of 13 levels, and they are all huge. These maps are littered with secrets, traps, rooms of all shapes and forms filled with demons, and they will keep your attention (Well at least they kept mine); although I will admit that it felt a little linear at times. The level design certainly is not as complex as in the earlier Doom installments, as you may sometimes notice that you are walking along a straight line, but fortunately there are enough secrets, backtracking (MEANINGFUL backtracking) and monsters to keep you on your toes.

This game took me about 20 hours on Ultra-Violence. Many parts were quite difficult, but I never felt overwhelmed or powerless during battle sequences. The monsters' AI, while not stupid, are not that smart either.

The graphics look really fine. My settings are mostly on High, and it looks gorgeous. The environments, animations and textures are A+. Now, in terms of aesthetics, DOOM does not have as great a palette of colors as Doom I had, but never does it overindulge in browns and greys like Doom II. About the framerate; I did experience some severe framerate dips when I had maxed-out settings, but then I lowered them a bit and it did not bother me again.

One thing I have to criticize is the soundtrack. To me, it did not really stand out in this game. Most of the time I couldn't hear it during the game, and when I did, it was some kind of electronic-industrial hybrid tunes that didn't immerse me into the experience (NB: I don't know much about musical genres, so if I'm full of ♥♥♥♥ on that, I apologize). One exception is the game's main theme, which is straight-up industrial metal: this one is AWESOME and got me into the mood.

In conclusion, I absolutely recommend this game to anyone who wants to rip and tear like they used to 20 years ago, or people who never heard of or played DOOM and are willing to, well... rip and tear. Play it to blast ugly creatures. Play it for the good level design. Play it for the atmosphere. Play it for the mix of the aura of FPSes of the nineties and the graphical ergonomy of FPSes of the current century.
Play it for the catharsis.
