\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a surprise hit for a lot of people and one that I've been following and anticipating for a while. The singleplayer campaign is one of the best I've played in many years. It's a strong throwback to the old doom games, from the keycards to the fast paced action, to the secrets, this game deserves the name DOOM. The weapons feel beefy and powerful, the enemies explode into fountains of blood and gore, and the sound design to go with it is top notch. The level design is what really shines, always giving you plenty of chance to explore and find secrets and always leading you along without playing the game for you and telling you exactly what to do (ala CoD). The singleplayer for DOOM is a breath of fresh air in today's gaming climate.

The multiplayer isn't quite as strong. It's still good and has plenty of game modes, customization and progression to keep people busy. It isn't for everyone but it's still quite a bit of fun if you like arena shooters. I should mention that the game uses a loadout system instead of weapon pickups (power weapons are still pickups).

Snapmap is a useful tool that people can use to make custom singleplayer and multiplayer levels. It's easy to use and powerful enough to make some fun, decently complex levels but not the same as having actual mod tools.

My only major gripe with DOOM is the lack of actual mod support and mod tools. It seems they made snapmap to fill that void but it's really not the same thing and especially with it being a DOOM game, that just doesn't feel right. Overall though DOOM is a fantastic entry into the series that surprised a lot of people by being unarguably one of the best games of the year.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is aggressively old school and eschews a lot of modern shooter conventions (recharging sheilds/health, cover, radar) in favor of a game where you are constantly sprinting and shooting and punching demons. There isn't even a reload button. It's a big contrast to Doom 3, (which I actually really enjoyed) which had a much bigger focus on storytelling (or at least mood setting) with audio logs and slow pacing. It's not that; demons aren't here to scare you, just explode in gore. The story is pretty non-consequential, and I wish they held onto the audio logs idea so I wouldn't have to pause the game and read. But you're here for the shooting, and the shooting is good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is amazing and it's the best game id Software's pulled out of their asses since Quake 2. The gameplay is insane, the level designs are fabulous to explore (they aren't linear and they also introduce some vertical gameplay), the way you can customise your weapons with certain types of alternative fires and the way you can customise your character's abilities + the health, armour & ammo upgrades are just a great touch which you surprisingly don't even have to address if you choose not to.

The atmosphere of the game is a lot like DOOM 64 and DOOM 3, the sound effects and the soundtrack are top notch. The themes and the addition of the ambient mixed with industrial symphonic metal greatly contributes to the atmosphere, and it all blends together perfectly.

There is a storyline to it and a lore you can read about in the game, but it does not push it in your face and makes you do certain things like most modern military shooters do now.

The multiplayer while fun is not reminiscent of DOOM or Quake as much, it's got a 2 weapon limit, no strafe jumping, no chain jumping, no rocket jumping and is noticeably slower in pace (the way your character moves) than most arena shooters-- especially in comparison to the original DOOM and Quake. Add on to that, you can't create and customise your server the way you can in let's say Quake Live.

It is unfortunate that id Software didn't develop the multiplayer, but the single player campaign really is faithful to the original DOOM franchise while implementing nice, new additions.

The idea of SnapMap is great but a little too simple in some areas, such as the lack of ability to create custom geometry and custom themes thus leaving you with geometry and texture presets only. Hopefully id Software will make some changes in future updates.\t\t\t\t\t
