\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe ID as they are now had huge shoes to fill when rejuvenating the classic series, DOOM. Many were skeptical, but faith was rewarded and then some in 2016 when DOOM finally launched. Despite embargos, a meh beta, and fears that the combat was too slow or the new additions like glory kills would ruin the pace of battle, ID managed to make a total winner of a campaign that has you step into the boots of a primordial death machine known only as the Doom Slayer, a demon's worst nightmare that speaks through violence so intense it makes Hell itself shudder collectively. The gameplay is a near perfect throwback to the classic Doom style of exploration rewarded with goodies and secrets, and guns only fit for a murderer's row of demons. The story mode doesn't need your approval, it just commands your attention from scene one and goes from there. Sure, the multiplayer was farmed out but the campaign is where this game truly shines. An absolute winner, necessary for fans of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
