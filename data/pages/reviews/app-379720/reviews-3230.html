\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have my issues with the game but those are also a lot of the same issues I had with Doom 3. From a purely gameplay perspective this game is phenomenal. I had severe doubts going in and had reasoned with myself that this was id softwares last chance to make a fantastic game or they were finished and I think they knocked it out of the park with this one. I enjoyed Doom 3 quite a bit and the slowed down pace etc. but this feels more like the original games I grew up with updated for modern gaming sensibilities. The combat is just so wonderful and fluid and dynamic. Any time I've run into trouble the solution has always been to fight harder, faster, and smarter. And I effing love that about the game. The story is kind of trash but I enjoy the subtle parody they wrote into backstory of the Doom Slayer.

(So my favorite thing about the original games missing from both this game and Doom 3 was the enviromental storytelling that did the bulk of plot presentation in the original Doom, i.e. the slow transition of Phobos and Deimos bases into the hellscape of the Inferno chapter.)\t\t\t\t\t

Yep, Doom was my first multiplayer experience, before the days of the internets. Hundreds of hours spent mastering the mouse and keyboard; wait, playing an FPS with a mouse? - Crazy high octane, DOOM meets QUAKE style Multiplayer [✓]

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is great.

It's full-on fast paced fun with lots to discover, there's a great and meaningful variety of weapons and unlocks, the health / ammo system is brilliantly balanced, the melee glory kills system is graceful and balletic in the midst of frenetic combat, the levels are chock-full of secrets and bonus challenges, the enemy variety is both interesting and meaningful in combat...

The campaign story is played just the right way, there's just enough to keep you invested, it doesn't take over, the protagonist is angry at everything, and your main goal is to blast everything in the face until it dies. There are some big moments, but nothing earth-(or mars)-shattering, but it is varied and interesting enough to draw you through. The art style is great, with a great variety in colours and map styles, and the weapons and models are all gorgeous.

The multiplayer is not so great, whilst a throwback to Unreal Tournament style of gameplay, it didn't hold my interest for long, and I'm not a big fan of the snapmaps system (which creates maps that feel clunky and like they're made on a tile system, because they are). Maybe some will get some great fun out of these options, but I considered them some underwhelming extras to dive into if you so wish. The meat of the game is the campaign, and it is glorious.

DOOM is great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI remember the original Doom games. A friend of mine at school who went on to work on one of the biggest Doom source ports out there, and created a scripting language used in several other source ports of the original Doom's engine as well, was kind enough to copy me The Ultimate Doom onto floppy disks, after I played the hell out of the shareware version over and over.

We were a poor family so couldn't afford a lot of games, so his gift was always treasured. Until very recently I still had the floppies, though I've not gone back to it in some time. You know how it is, old games are nice through rose tinted glasses but when you come to play them again you realise that modern games have had time and experience to build upon and correct the mistakes of old, leading to a far more fun gameplay experience, and richer stories and environments.

But regardless, the original Doom and Doom 2 always were special to me. They were the codifiers of the FPS genre, and classics in their own right. I even got hold of Doom 3 BFG edition for a bit to try it out, and whilst I wasn't disappointed, it really didn't scratch the itch the original Doom did when I was a kid. It was a capable FPS but it lacked so much heart and I didn't really feel like it was Doom, as opposed to just a good FPS with demonic invasion themes.

This however?...

When the multiplayer beta came out I was at first excited, and then I started seeing gameplay and that excitement quickly turned to dread. When the single player gameplay started getting reviewed however all that excitement came flooding back. My other half bought it, and played it, and loved it, and that sold me on buying this at full price.

I regret nothing about this purchase. Within the first ten minutes, the game immediately makes you feel like a colossal badass, and it only gets better from there. Don't be a wuss, play this on Ultra Violence AT THE LEAST. Secrets are actually secret and well hidden, the glory kill system is really well done and not overbearing or a liability given that it moves you TO the enemy you glory kill (and they have some i-frames), level design is phenomenal and they managed to make Mars look imposing and glorious at the same time, the game engine is fantastically well optimised (though it does struggle on AMD chips apparently), and I have encountered only one really annoying bug. If Dev Mode gets turned on somehow via attempting to cheat or by a crash which locks up Doom during a save, there's no way to turn Dev Mode off, and you'll have to delete your saves. Which is really, really frustrating.

The game looks, plays, sounds and FEELS like Doom, but about six hundred times better. And yes, it has a story, and yes, the story is actually really engaging and really good. And yes, as a fan of the original doom games, this was worth EVERY PENNY, even at full price.

Masterful work. It's like id software never left.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM revives this old franchise with a game that captures the magic of its predecessors (well, not DOOM 3), while modernizing it for the modern day. It's a fast pace, high octane, visceral shooter that demands that you have reflexes. Its not perfect. The multiplayer is there for no reason, and it holds to some older game design concepts from the original DOOMs that, while modernized, still would be better without, primarily the large amount of platforming and collecting key cards. DOOM towards the end starts to run out creative steam, but for the vast majority of the game, it'll constantly keep throwing new enemies at you to deal with. It also has a solid story to boot and the game looks amazing. Overall, I absolutely recommend DOOM. It revitalizes the series and makes me care about DOOM again, which is saying a whole lot after DOOM 3 made me write it off as trash.\t\t\t\t\t
