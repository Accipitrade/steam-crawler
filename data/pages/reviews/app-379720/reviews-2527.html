...


It’s all been said before, but the way DOOM kicks out the entirely linear shooter cover/hiding gameplay trend is killer. Having no reloads was something that took me a bit to get used to. It works so well though. Why punish firing a weapon with vulnerability when your job is to be destroying everything?

Ridiculously detailed environments and snippets of lore add so much polish to the world. This isn’t just some generic facility to shoot stuff in, it’s a corrupted research facility backed by cultish scientists that has become your death-labyrinth. While you wander around, you’ll notice bulges in walls, torn fences, and gruesome displays of bodies. No areas in Doom feel copied and pasted. Lighting everywhere is fantastic. Skyboxes in hell are something to behold.

The game’s approach to metroidvania-like exploration is actually really well put together. It pushes you to look around while ultimately keep your focus on the path ahead. You can’t backtrack to older areas, everything is on the map and plenty of myriad bonuses/collectables cross your path. It means you’ll never have to get bored if you don’t want to. This fulfills the needs of both completionists and action fans. Upgrades don’t seriously buff you too much and so missing out on a few points doesn’t hurt. I’m sure I missed more than my fair share, but by the end I was running out of things to look forward to upgrading.

For as much as I adore the campaign, I don’t think I’ll be replaying it any time soon. A lot of the excitement comes from being able to quickly understand new arenas, enemies and weapons. Some of the least heart-pounding moments were towards the end where you’ve got all the guns, most of the upgrades and levels are linear between arenas.

Doom is supposed to be challenging. You’re supposed to feel powerful but not in control, so maybe I should’ve turned the difficulty up more at the end to make those less important near-finale battles more stimulating. I would recommend starting on a higher difficulty than what you think you’d be comfortable.

Behind it all, quick and damn satisfying animations and Mick Gordon’s mind-bending, aleatoricly designed ear-shatter tunes melt on top of the insane gameplay to create Doom’s core. That holy trinity makes Doom an experience like no other. DOOM is totally worth experiencing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very average shooter which has pretty much nothing in common with the original Doom 1/2 except the name. Should have been better called Tomb Nukem 3D because of its emphasis on jumping around and sillyness. Played it almost half through until dropping it.

Pros:
- Looks at least half-decent even if the monster designs are inferior to the original
- You can jump now (Even if the game is too obsessed with it)

Cons:
- Gunplay overall sucks because everything is designed around the glory kills
- Weapons feel more like booze throwers that cause monsters to stagger like drunk idiots
- Weapons feel weak because demons regenerate while staggering
- Engine can barely handle more than 10 enemies at the same time (Original could handle hundreds without problems)
- Monster infighting technically exists but barely matters anymore
- Boring level design focused on arena fights with spawning opponents
- Monsters almost exclusively spawn which makes tactical planning almost impossible
- Corpses vanish, which reduces immersion and kills gameplay aspects like the monster resurrection of mages in the original
- Monster attack patterns are just a joke compared to the original (Homing missiles from skeletons, three shot volleys from Macubi, Mages sent you flying, etc.)
- Gameplay resembles more Quake than Doom
- Cheap trash metal sound track without any atmosphere
- Whole weapon upgrade system more annoying than interesting => Not every game needs 'RPG elements'
- Has just about 10% of the gameplay depth of the original Doom
- Monsters aren't scary because they are too sillyfied by glory kills (Demons I can beat in a fist fight aren't worth to be called demons)

The original Doom 1/2 is still played today - for reasons. But nobody will remember this one anymore in a few years.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, so this is the first game I've ever felt the need to write out a review for because, well, HOLY HELL...
DOOM is basically a love letter to what makes a good Videogame great, and that is the fact that it's lovingly made and is bleeding with creativity and enthusiasm, from the atmosphere in-game to the kick-ass feeling of taking down a baron of hell. Everything is made to work together, no nonsensical bugs that shouldn't be there, and they are made to work in a manner that makes you truly feel like a demon-slaying badass.

While i'm not necessarily one for the story it presents, the experience along the way harkens back to the days of difficult and rewarding gameplay. Everything feels fair, but you have to fight for it, and fight you most certainly will.

So in summary, if you've got some cash to spend and a good PC you want to push to it's limits, get this game. It WILL run, and dammit it WILL look OUTSTANDING.
Good luck, and remember...


Fight like hell!\t\t\t\t\t
