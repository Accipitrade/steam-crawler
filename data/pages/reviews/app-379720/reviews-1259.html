\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM (2016) is a highly entertaining adrenaline rush that I just couldn't put down. I had put off playing this game for a few years because it looked a bit too fast and chaotic for me, and I had never played any other Doom games before. I had even bought the game and tried the first mission several years ago, only to get overwhelmed and quit playing. However, after giving it a second chance and committing to playing through to the end, it becomes hard for me to articulate how much I loved my time with it.

The gameplay and combat just feel as solid as a rock. Every gunshot, every jump, and every kill just felt so reliable, responsive, and satisfying. I never once felt cheated out of killing an enemy demon, or in suffering a few hits myself. Any time I failed or died, I was never once upset, nor did I want to leave. I just kept wanting to play to try to get better at killing demons and mastering weapons, to the point that I would say it was addictive. The different weapons are fun to play around with, the environments are fun to leap off of, and the different enemies allowed for me to switch up my gameplay tactics whenever I could. It made me feel like my brain was actually working as I played, which is pretty rare for me. I'm pretty good at playing games on autopilot where I can almost get by with my eyes shut, but DOOM allowed me to feel like I was solving puzzles and problems just by moving and shooting. Whenever people talk about old school shooters being more fast and intense, I feel like this is the wave of calculation they are talking about. If that is the case, DOOM excels at going back to the roots of the genre. Nothing matters except for you, your gun, and your target, and it never got old. My last kill was just as satisfying as my first, and I didn't want it any other way. It was even still fun upon immediate replays of the campaign. It's pretty rare for me to play a single-player campaing more than once, and DOOM was able to keep me glued to my seat while replaying it. As much as I am complimenting this game, I must stress that it is not some rocket science breakthrough achievement. They were just able to do a great job at keeping things simple, and therefore understandable.

The graphical and audio design of the game was also excellently done. Every sound effect was quick to become recognizable and then aid to my awareness of everything around me. The demons all have clearly unique sounds, as well as weapons, and it was so easy to dive right into a room and just know what awaited me without having to scan every corner. The few pieces of dialogue in this game also feel so large and powerful. There's such a weight to them that feel important and badass. Samuel's deep and commanding voice, contrasted with VEGA's reserved and slightly optimistic sounding voice, and even Olivia's snide demeanor, they were all memorable to me. There's also several visual clues like this that I appreciated, with very clearly saturated colors and clear lighting placement that showed where something important was, even though there are some more hidden secrets. On top of being useful, the graphics and sound were very pretty and crisp. I loved how each level looked, and the weapons and monsters all had some nice grungy detail put into them. It actually ran pretty decently on my not-so-great computer. The music is also cathartic. I couldn't see this game without the lyricless industrial metal, it just feels so raw and empowering, especially when paired with the demonic Hell aesthetics.

I do have to admit, I did not get into the multiplayer mode, nor did I play or make many custom SnapMap levels. My praises come strictly from the single-player campaign, as that's pretty much all of my playtime. There didn't seem to be anything wrong with them, I just got less out of those modes personally. Perhaps you'd enjoy those modes more than me.

I can't sing my praises of DOOM enough. If you enjoy the classic shooter days of Doom, Wolfenstein 3D, or Quake, I feel as though there's a ton for you to enjoy in this game, as well as upping the ante from the classics. Also if you enjoy more modern shooters, I believe DOOM adds a nice speed and design that you can discover on your own and appreciate. I believe it can appeal to every audience just through its ability to be a solid, fun experience.\t\t\t\t\t
