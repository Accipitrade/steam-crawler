\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game will make just about any DOOM player from veteran to "The hell's a Doom?" absolutely cream their pants. Despite the rather intimidating file size (I'm sorry, SSD) the game will run very optimized at moderately high graphics and will rarely ever drop below 60fps. After beating the entire original Doom campaign, I can reassure any doubters that DOOM 2016 does those games justice and more. The violence mixed with amazing level design will without a doubt be able to satisfy gore-lovers, but if you're a true doom fan, y'know that your thirst will never be quenched, and oh boy did this game prepare: Finishing blows based on the limb you're looking at & your position provides numerous ways to absolutely destroy those Demons Alex Jones warned you about. Oh and the music... Ohhhhhh the music, no one could have done a better job for the soundtrack than Mick Gordon. A large combination of audio bytes that seamlessly compose before your very ears that ensure that no matter how many times you hear THAT song, there's always something just a little different that makes you want to come back for more. It's aggressive, it's metal asf, and it's even beautifully ambient on many occasions. The only flaws I have with the game are extremely minor and aren't even worth bringing up (i.e the finishing kill audio clip for a song interrupting a track one too many times since it is a great way to aggressively receive hp/ammo. Or the fact that the plasma rifle feels very weak compared to the earlier titles) so. Bottom line: Buy it.\t\t\t\t\t
