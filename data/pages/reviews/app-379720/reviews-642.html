\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn almost perfect fps game. Nothing much to say other than experiencing only three bugs in the whole 13 missions walkthrough. The first bug was sinking in the ground to land on the bottom floor of the same spot while clicking buttons during a fight. The second bug was instead of dying normally and having a death scene, I had an alive third person shooter character just standing there. The funny thing, it wasn't the Doom Slayer. The third was a game freeze while playing The UAC mission (First Mission) on Ultra-Nightmare while I was upgrading my praetor suit mid-air. I closed the game and opened it again to continue playing from the last check point with no problem. The game has a good sound system and its music was made by Mick Gordon. Visuals are pretty decent with cool animations. Gameplay is just high-tier when you compare it with other fps games in the same year. The best thing about the game was that it always had me looking for more because of its weapons mods, data logs, argent upgrades, praetor tokens, and rune trials. Not to mention, the nice challenges in each map and for each weapon mod.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely fantastic remake of DOOM. This game is a modern homage to the beloved original. It has all the best elements of the original game, and even includes original levels as unlockable secrets.

Highlights, for me:
- Level design -- the maps are huge, beautiful, diverse, and fun to explore. Lots of hidden goodies. The old system of grabbing key cards to progress through the level is totally fine, love it. The double-jump and platforming elements are awesome. Similar to the original DOOM, you get a scorecard at the end of the level that tells you how well you did, so you'll know if you found all the secrets or not.
- Demon design -- it's fresh, but it's still DOOM. The imps are very nimble and tough to hit, and the standard soldier has a lot more firepower than he used to. The new hell knight is scary! They did a great job of revamping the old demons. Plus they added a few new ones, like the Summoner, and the Hell Razer. Very fun to battle.
- Music and sound design -- the game is thrilling and scary thanks to the excellent soundtrack, which has a lot of bass, so make sure you play with good headphones or speakers.
- Story and pacing -- I think the story is awesome. The voice acting is great too. It's exactly the kind of thing you need in a DOOM game... just a massive excuse to go to Hell and blow a ton of demons to bits.
- Weapon upgrades -- The weapons are all fun, plus you get 2 unique mods for every weapon, and you can spend points to upgrade them. The only trouble is picking the right one!
- New game mechanics -- The rune challenges are super fun, and the mission challenges help to make the fighting a little more interesting.

Criticisms:
- There are 2 shotguns... I don't feel like I really need 2 shotguns. I guess they wanted to include the double-barrel shotgun for nostalgia, but I find that it doesn't really add much to the game.
- Tough to select weapons sometimes. I forget which weapon goes with which number, and I get stuck switching back and forth trying to figure out where my rocket launcher is. It does make the battles a little more frantic, but I think I'd prefer a system where you can stop time for a sec and pick a different weapon from a quick menu. I guess I could remap my keyboard to make the weapons more accessible.
- The secondary equipment is lacking. I like the frag grenade, but I feel like there should be some different types of grenades. The holographic dummy is pretty lame, IMO. I haven't finished the game yet, but so far that's all I've seen is a frag grenade and a hologram.
- The levels are great, but they are suuuper long. I like to explore and take my time finding all the secrets. If I am 50% of the way through a level and would like to go to bed, it would be nice if I could save it, and then come back later and pick up where I left off. Unfortunately, the checkpoint system does not allow for that.
- Glitches... I've found a few places where it's possible to fall through the geometry of a level and get killed. Gotta be careful not to jump when you're on some moving platforms, especially in the Argent Tower level. Not that big of a deal since you can just start at the last checkpoint, but a little frustrating.

Overall I love this game and I'm having a lot of fun with it! I didn't buy it for a long time because I am cheap and I have a very long backlog of games that I haven't played, but I've been wanting to play DOOM for a long time, and I'm so glad I did!\t\t\t\t\t
