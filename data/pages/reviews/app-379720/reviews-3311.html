\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was too late to get on the Doom train in 1993, being born just under a year later. Because of this, I was unable to fully appreciate Doom for what it was at the time. Yes, I played the orginal, yes I enjoyed it for what it was, but while playing I never got the sense of awe, or was dumbfounded by this newfangled "FPS." I was too young to see Doom for what it truly was.

But with the release of this Doom reboot, I can see what the fuss was about. This new Doom is the epitome of what a first-person shooter should be on a basic level. The gunplay is fast and fluid. The enemies are equal parts terrifying, and creativley designed. The levels are huge and open ended. All of the components that need to work do so flawlessley.

Initially I was skeptical, previous old-school throwbacks never did it for me, i.e. Duke Nukem Forever and Shadow Warrior. Along with this I was more used to the slow paced gamplay of tactical shooters such as Rainbow Six, Arma, Red Orchestra, or Swat 4. Coupled with all of the hate on the beta, this purchase was a gamble for someone like me. Despite this, I can say Doom is a bloody glorious romp through hell that has exceeded my expectations. Graphically, mechanically, and artistically, Doom checks all of the boxes.

Now I can see all of the fuss people make all over the internent about "old school" FPS, and their desire to bring back the game design tendencies of old. If this is how most PC FPS played throughout the 90s i say bring 'em back, because i'm having a blast with Doom.

Rip and tear my friends.\t\t\t\t\t
