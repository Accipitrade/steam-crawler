\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM IS...good. Doom is a good game, but I feel like those gore nests(aka: the glorified demon clearing mission starter) bogs down the fast pace behind the game. I would much rather prefer enemy encounters to be placed along the path of where I need to go and the challenge to come from that and to it's credit doom does have that in every level of the game. But as said previously, it seems like bethesda wants to corall players into one small environment in order to takeout hordes, so they won't have to actually think about having to create levels that acommodate all the different demons that you come across, as well as your combat abilities in ways that don't just boil down to:
kill X amount of demons until we get that you have a massive penis
rinse and repeat because, good enemy placement and level design? Never heard of it. Not to say that doom has inherently bad game design, the combat proves otherwise and the environments are both stylistically and in terms of the way you traverse through them enjoyable to play. Those gore nests though, are literally the only reason why I don't consider doom 2016 a masterpiece. Good game, held back imo by in my opinion, a lazy design choice which makes it so that the devs don't have to think as hard in order to create meaningful combat encounters. Rip and tear. I have tears in my eyes. Good game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't played a shooter game more satisfying and intense than this.

Graphics- 9.5/10: If you came to this game expecting great graphics, then you are in for a treat. This game has really good lighting, textures, and effects that are sure to dazzle you, even if you are playing on low settings. Sometimes if you look behind you really fast or switch weapons the textures look weak for a moment or so because the game uses a very unique rendering technique to only show high quality textures where they matter, and sometimes the vram (esp. if you are using integrated graphics) doesn't show the texture fast enough for perfection.

Gameplay- 10/10: This game pretty much rips the heart (gameplay) out of the old 1993 Doom and shoves it down the throat of a modern graphics engine. Sure, it isn't really the old doom since you can jump, perform glory kills, and look up and down, but the level design, secrets, fast paced gameplay feel, and plot are really doomy to me. In DOOM 2016, the gameplay is very fast paced, so if you stand still, then you will die fast. You will be constantly performing the finishing glory kills to get the extra bit of health to shoot the next demon and then finish him off to get the health so you can keep moving on, and so on.

Soundtrack- 666/10: Mick Gordon really nailed the soundtrack. When you are playing the game, the intense, beating metal soundtrack feels like it fits perfectly with the combat every time. It seems to get more intense based on how intense the gameplay is. I found myself trying to play seriously but the soundtrack is just so intensely good i got distracted.

Replayability/game time- 8/10: This game has a wickedly good campaign mode that is usually between 12 to 15 hours in length with varying difficulty. Past that you've got a decidedly mediocre multiplayer mode, which is nearly dead and mostly forgettable. Finally, there is a snapmap mode where you can build your own levels and play them with friends. They have this snapmap thing instead of mods, and although snapmap is flexible and good and all, it's just not as good as mods because snapmap can only use assets in the base game, which means no new content, really.

Difficulty- 8/10: DOOM has various difficulty levels, which are just as hard as their names suggest:
Im Too Young To Die- Very easy. Only play this if you are a wuss.
Hurt Me Plenty- Easy. Suggested for players who are looking to kick back a little.
Ultra-Violence- Normal. Suggested for most people, it's a good challenge.
Nightmare- Hard. There are no tutorials, and you can't change difficulty halfway through.
Ultra-Nightmare- Brutal. It's the same as nightmare, but your game is OVER if you die even once. (Only one life)

Story- 9/10: The story is just like the old Doom; hell portal opens on mars, people die, you are brought in, you kill all the demons. Thats really all there is to it that you need to know. The game doesnt have any forced cutscenes or forced story.

Optimization/performance- 10/10: This game is crazy well optimized, and is one of the games I show to prove that games can have fluid, dipless, and fast fps w/o taking high quality textures and effects. I tested it on a few of my graphics cards, such as an r7 360, RX 580 4gb, and vega 8 integrated graphics at 1600 mhz.

R7 360 2gb- Low preset, 1080p, OpenGL, SMAA. I got 30 to 45 fps most of the time. Vulkan got 30ish fps.

RX 580 4gb- Ultra preset, 1080p, TSSAA x8. I got about 70-100 fps on OpenGL, but about 144 fps on Vulkan.

Vega 8 @1600 mhz, with DDR4 @3000 mhz- Low preset, 1080p, TAA (1X). OpenGL gave me about 25 fps, but switching it to Vulkan gave me consistently 40-50 fps, which is respectable considering it is an iGPU in a 99$ cpu.

Bugs/glitches- 10/10: You can use the glitches for speedrun, but there is nothing too bad that a normal player would find.

Value: Get the game when it is on sale. I typically only buy games if the dollars paid is less then my hours I will play. I picked DOOM up for $15, which is what I would consider a reasonable pricing.


Overall I rate this game 9/10. I highly reccomend this game to anyone who hasn't already played it. Anyway thanks for taking the time to read through all of this lol.\t\t\t\t\t
