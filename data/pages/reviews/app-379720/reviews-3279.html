\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy first experience with the DOOM series was on an ancient computer with DOOM II and Floppy Disk installers. Intense violence, fast action, easily one of the great games I remember from my childhood. DOOM 3 came out, made it through... a very different although not all together bad experience.

Enter DOOM 2016. I end up completing very few games but this one I couldn't put down until I made it through the campaign. This game really captures the essence of what DOOM and DOOM II were and puts a modern-era spin on it.

The game is slow at the start as you learn the controls, what enemies do, etc. But this really ramps up towards the end with intense, arena-style slaughterfests that, if you have never played before, would look impossible to survive. The maps that encase these arenas are extremely well designed and allow for a lot of close-quarters combat without feeling trapped or gimmicky. The only complaint here would be that towards the end, I ended up doing a lot of strafe-style combat and would occasionally get "stuck" in an odd spot that it wasn't always so easy to get out of.

The story is alright for what the game is. Not as hokey as I expected and is bearable to get you from level to level. But let's not kid ourselves, this is a game solely about ultra-violent demon slaughter.

Voice-overs were well done, especially the stones retelling some of the lore of the Doom Slayer.


If you are a fan of the old DOOM series or are looking for a refreshing shooter that is not "run of the mill", you definitely will not be disappointed by this purchase. 9/10, thanks for the great game id.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 3 was a good game. It was just really friggin' dark and a crappy Doom game. What I mean by that is the original Doom is very rarely about not being able to see, and jump scares. Rather, it's about intense action, strafing wildly, wielding heaps of cool weapons, and not taking it too seriously while having fun. Doom 3 has some of these elements, but tries much too hard to be mature and gritty in the wrong ways.

This 2016 version of Doom is Doom done right. It's a reboot that I expected to be garbage, but ended up having a lot of fun with. Each weapon has a job, each enemy is dealt with a different way, fast melee finishers which I didn't think I'd like surprised me and added a fun new element to the game, and although it feels weird at first, the further I got into the game, the more this felt like the original Doom games. It's a fun reimagining of the original with a story that you can pay as much or as little attention to as you like, with delays for exposition being very rare. The enemies are very detailed and redesigned, but some of them, such as the cacodemon, revenant, pinky, etc., while looking so new, also have that nostalgic look to them as well when you take in their overall shape, purpose, and aesthetic.

However, it's not just Doom with a shiny new coat of paint and melee finishers. Almost every weapon has upgrades, as does your Doom Marine suit. You are awarded upgrade points for finding various collectibles, killing large numbers of enemies, and completing challenges (such as kill X number of imps from behind in melee, etc.) on each level. Completing these challenges, finding the secrets, and upgrading your guns and suit are practically a mini-game in and of themselves, because I spent a rather large number of hours combing through levels looking for goodies. It's definitely worth it, though, because having better guns and such make clearing later areas much easier and give you more options.

That said, if you aren't the kind of gamer who has a lot of spare time, you may not want to spend a terribly large amount of time looking for the secrets. Although there's a really rewarding feeling about finding some of the tougher secrets on your own, Youtubing to find secrets you're missing isn't a bad idea if you really do need to cut back on how much time you're spending playing, and videos of that are already available from several users on the aforementioned site.

If you like games you can get all the achievements in, this one's doable but not super easy. I have 100% achievements now, and most of them are for finding all the secrets and upgrading everything. I got the competitive multiplayer achievements in under an hour easily, despite me being terrible at it, and the Snap Map achievements can be obtained within an hour or two depending on how much attention you pay to Snap Map's tutorials and playing legitimate maps to entertain or simply trying to finish maps fast to get achievements.

The competitive multiplayer seemed alright, but I'm hardly the person to ask. It's just not really my jam. The mode I played was a lot like Transformers War for Cybertron's mode where you try to hold command points,except I could turn into a jet on a whim whenever I felt like it in Transformers, so for me, Transformers all the way. ;)

Snap Map is surprisingly robust. Basically, you have an overhead view of an "x-ray" of a room, and can place different types of rooms, key cards, locked doors, secrets, etc. If you follow the tutorials and mess around with the features, I think a person could turn out some impressive maps. I played a few that people published, and as short an amount of time as it's been since launch, someone had already posted a "tower defense" sort of map. No towers, but the enemies run in lines to an end point you're trying to stop them from reaching, killing them quickly yourself and spending money in a "store" between waves for new guns, permanent damage bonuses, score multipliers, etc. I didn't play these maps too long, but I could see some amazing things getting made eventually.

All in all, Doom is quite a good game, and if they make a sequel, I'm definitely down for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review will encompass Single Player only.

It's fast and brutal, with many of the better trappings of modern game design. It rewards brutality and speed, and is very much about the age-old formula of "strafe/jump and shoot". There are 4 of 8 guns with Aim Down Sight/scope functionality, the rest sporting (and two of the previous, ADS is simply a part of the alt fire) a "mod" system which is basically a changable alternate firing mode. It doesn't always make sense (shotgun shells becoming a grenade launcher, anyone?) but it's creative and adds variety to the combat.
If I were sum the game up in a word, it would likely be "violence". The DOOM Marine is a silent protagonist, and interacts with the world in very nearly only one manner: destruction. Demon? Destroy it. Malfunctioning door? Rip it open. Critical infrastructure? Stomp on it. Upgrade-providing NPC? Punch it.
It's an underlying theme, and were this a different company I would expect there to be a commentary in there somewhere, but as it is, it's just pure entertainment.
As for the monsters, there's not much new, and of what IS new... well, it's not very interesting. However, all the old favorites got shiny new makeovers, and were significantly fleshed out, lore-wise. The story and backstory are all buried in the Codex section, but it's all at least moderately interesting; if worldbuilding is something you're into, I'd say you'll have a good time if only from the various codex entries.
But as to the gameplay; it's fluid and fast, the Glory Kill system an interesting if unexpected addition. After finishing the game on Ultraviolence, I can't help but think that the Glory Kill system (which offers increased chance for enemies to drop health pickups, ~+3-5 HP) makes the game too easy. Then again, there are a number of modifiers (Argent cores, which increase Ammo, HP, or Armor caps), Runes (which modify various things, like Glory Kill speed, Ammo pickup amount, enemies drop armor pickups on Glory Kill, etc), and Weapon Mods (increase weapon stats in regard to alter fire, like faster reload/charge up, additional headshot damage, enemy penetration, missle-lock on, etc) that once you get a few of any of these under your belt, you're a Demon Destroying Machine. (It's all optional, of course, though; if you're looking for a good challenge, try not using ANY of that. It's tough.)

My final verdict would be: It's a worthy successor to the old DOOMs, but it's short by comparison, and unless you're a completionist who has to find EVERY secret/collectable, it's hardly worth $60. That is not to say it isn't a well-made game, with fun mechanics. If you have one, it'll scratch that twitch shooter itch quite nicely; you may not be a one hit point wonder, but if you're not on your toes, you can expect to be demon fodder in short order.
Numerical rating: 7/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuake, Doom, Duke Nukem 3D, Shadow Warrior... similar games that rocketed the first person shooter games into a popular thing. I played all of those old games and this newest game has stuck to the formula to a measure of perfection

It plays and feels almost exactly like the old doom games only with prettier graphics and a few quality of life improvements.

The story is entirely in the Dialog and the little bits and peices you peice together from the game... it tells you like... everything and nothing all at once. Run, gun, get the things, shoot the other things, find the loot and guns, then get corrnered by uglies and melted for it... Respawn and say "screw it" and blow up the whole darn room only to regret wasting so much ammo when you get to the next encounter.

If you loved the first dooms you will like this and it is a landslide improvement on the doom that came before this one

Tips: If you have played any first person shooter to a point where you are good or you have been playing shooters since you were young, do not play on any difficulty lower than "Hurt Me Plenty" I found that it is a fine mix of you still being a bad ass hero and still having to worry about getting shot or bitten. also remember getting lost and finding things is part of the experiance. upgrade the sensors first and you will miss a whole lot less special stuff

20+ hours worth of gameplay and then multiplayer and custom maps so definitly a value\t\t\t\t\t
