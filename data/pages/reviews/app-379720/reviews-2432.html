\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen this game came out I was still in college. I had a calendar that listed the number of days until this game came out. I stayed faithful to id software for almost 5 years waiting for this to come out. Every day that drew closer I was more excited. 300 days, 200, 100, 50, 25, 15, and then came May. The game released on my birthday, May 13th, and my suspicions and anxiety about the game was at its peak.

You know what I got? A 90's fps with updated graphics, mechanics, proper scripting, and the classic DOOM experience. And it was PERFECT. I got to relive 8 year old me's joy when I played DOOM in 2001 for the first time. People of this day can't appreciate the movement based, action packed, dodge and weave combat of the classics like DOOM. Recreating that feeling and environment in this day and age is a hard thing to do, what with all the popular titles nothing like it now. The best games get remakes, leaving you with something like this. It's not just another episode of your favourite kids show- It's truly something amazing, new, fresh, and it feels epic.

With the community's support and what all has been said about the game's development hell, id software took the right path and used the fans' advice to make, shift, and shape the game around the original. Some of the spirit carmack, romero, etc had back in the 90's seems to live in this game. If you enjoyed DOOM, Quake, Duke Nukem, anything like it, buy this game. It's well worth your time.\t\t\t\t\t
