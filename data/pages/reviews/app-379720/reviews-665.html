\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo unless you've been living under a rock for the past decades you know what DOOM is about.
Im going to skip all the obvious positive comments about the campaign and Mick's soundtrack and talk about the only thing about this game that i ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ DESPISE: the multiplayer.
Now, i normally wouldn't give a single ♥♥♥♥ about multiplayer doom, but since i liked this game so much i decided i'd have to 100% it and, therefore, earn every single achievement.
Even the one that requires you to play a multiplayer game wearing a cyberdemon (cosmetic set name) armor piece.
The only problem is that you unlock those cosmetics by reaching Echelon X (basically you have to prestige by reaching lvl 50 TEN times).
Leveling up is fast and satisfying, but for someone that doesn't care about multiplayer it's like undergoing hours of ♥♥♥♥ and balls torture.
And that's not even the worst part.
It's been a few years since this game came out and, as i'm writing this, pretty much the entirety of the playerbase moved to doom eternal or just simply left, making fair games IMPOSSIBLE to find (If you are lucky enough to find a populated lobby in the first place).
There are only 3 categories of players that you'll find if you decide to give MP a try:
1) Salty 2016 veterans: tryhards which will simply not accept the fact that they've been killed by a new player and will come to your steam profile or add you just to insult. They probably haven't felt the touch of a woman since the game came out.
2)New players that just got the game: like me, some people got this game only recently and maybe wanted to 100% it as well or just see how the multiplayer is
3) Cheaters: Bethesda has moved on and, from what i saw, is no longer interested in banning blatant cheaters from their servers. Wich is ok, i guess. As if they did, you'd probably not even have enough people left to play with.

Not even talking about how busted some weapons and how unbalanced demons are, who gives a ♥♥♥♥.
Still a great game tho\t\t\t\t\t
