\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA surprisingly good game in the style of doom, although different. Still it's in the spirit of doom - running, strafing, shooting with the regular action-slowdown rhythm. No leaning or aiming down sights (with a few exceptions). It really well executed. The level design is considerably linear, with the occasional sidetrack for pickups and secrets. It varies from level to level.

It's a game where you skill as a player increase and you can attempt a replay of higher difficulties. Although with rising of the difficulty the death comes quicker, it never feels like cheating against the player. Finding the secrets tends to help with the higher difficulties.

To anyone who likes the gameplay of the original doom, this game will appeal.

I haven't played pvp multi yet. There is a cool style of semi map editor which allows you to create and play custom made levels, already integrated in the game. Some player-made levels tend to be lackluster, but others are pretty well done. Basically, it's like custom WADs for doom, but supported in the main game.

Now that the game is discounted, and the Eternal is coming up, you can get your fix without any remorse.\t\t\t\t\t

But when I played the old Doom game again recently at a UK video game exhibition I couldn't believe there was no way to lift or lower the gun sight! And yet it felt so natural all those years ago, now it feels so limiting!

This game seems a bit more complex and the direction through is not so clear as earlier games, but of course it's a modern update. There were a few areas where I couldn't figure out what I was supposed to do but I got through them eventually once I realised the nature of the game is more 3d than it was, you have to climb more! So I recently started playing this game again with the anticipated arrival of Doom Eternal lurking. I've managed to progress a fair amount into the game now, started getting the annoying brutish Hell Knights appearing and reached the furnace and have to say it is great fun. I loved the old games and this is a great update to those! I am loving the level design so far and although it complex it really is satisfying when you progress to new areas, I do love the large open levels.

Looking forward to seeing what else this game has to throw at me in later levels!\t\t\t\t\t
