\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe new Doom is the classic we've come to know and love. It's still the same old thing - corridor based shooting with waves of harder and harder enemies to defeat with the occasional bossd battle thrown in for good measure. Having said that, there's a lot going for this version of Doom.

1 - the graphics are spectacular, even a good few months to a year or morre after it's releaser.
2 - the gore system is pretty intense, brutal and satisfying. This adds a new level of violence demon killing that adds a new and enjoyable dynamic to the gaming franchise and makes you feel like an utter bad ass.
3 - the new Doom is like the old, it is what it is and doesn't care what think. Where other modern games might force you to only carry two or three guns, Doom lets you carry all the guns you could ever need and enough ammo to close the gates of hell. And that's exactly what you're going to need.
4 - the chainsaw and BFG are still in the game and they're immensely fun to use and kick ass with.

Though I personally find corridor based shooters and boss batttles a bit dull in 2017, I was not bored with Doom one bit. It's a beautiful bit of mindless fun and brutal gunplay. Sure, there's onmly a semblence of a story line and the ending was a bit disappointing, but I had a blast, so it was, in my view, worth every penny.

However, they said there was Co-op. There is no co-op. False advertising dammit.

----------------------------------------ACTUAL REVIEW-------------------------------------
ID and Bethesda one hundred and ten ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ percent NAILED this game! Fun, fast paced no reloading, no health regen action combined with some RPG/collecting elements from modern shooters. And a kickass rock soundtrack that you would expect from a DOOM game.

EDIT: The GAMEPLAY is spot on and the graphics are also gorgeous... but the game is pretty poorly optimized. Unless you have a really high end computer you will notice texture pop-in and some FPS slowdown near particles at points. Having Vulkan enabled and shadows at low made it smooth for me though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't get deep into it but after 20 minutes requested a refund; I suggest you weight my review accordingly, as I did not get deep into the game and the things that bothered me might not bother you.

I have a relatively modern gaming rig and have had no troubles running other games, and when DOOM finally loaded it looked just fine. But it took me three tries before it would load, because it would stutter and stop during the open studio-icon cinematics. When I finally get in, you can't access the menus right away -- what the ♥♥♥♥ is that? I had to wait until I was into the story and shooting people before I could make the mouse not terrible: They slather on a thick serving of mandatory mouse acceleration (really?), and the only way to turn it off is to put it as a startup option in the shortcut target path. Guys, no, this is 2016. I don't have time for that ♥♥♥♥ anymore, and same goes for the long, glitchy intro cinematics.

The game does some terrible motion-blurring effects that make it nearly unplayable IMO, especially in combination with the mouse accel. The 'glory kill' system is awful; not only do the blue outlines around enemies look totally out of place, but it means that the player has no access to a reliable, snappy melee attack (it just might take a few seconds when you do one of the same 'glory kill' moves on an enemy. Cool-ish maybe, but I was over it by the third time I did it, and really would prefer a quick melee).

So, yeah. Glitchy first 20 minutes and I felt like the developer and I have a very different idea about how to build a good game that respects the player's time. The first few minutes of gameplay made me never want to play it again; it looked fine, and it looked like a lot of people would think it's perfectly fine, but man, I just ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hated it. I feel like they made what is probably a very solid game and then just ruined it with a few really terrible UX and mechanical decisions.\t\t\t\t\t
