\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTake some awful modern-day marketing tactics, only publish slow-paced gameplay footage that was obviously made on a console, shove a crappy multiplayer and shallow map-making utility intended to replace modding down everyone's throats, and then make sure you don't send out review copies until launch. I don't think I've ever seen so many red flags for a AAA game.

And yet, this is probably the best shooter I've ever played.

The single player campaign is worth the $60 alone, "dollar per hour" rule be damned. Fun, fast, challenging, hilarious, visceral, and as a bonus they built a compelling and thoughtful story around the "Doomguy" that completely changes your understanding of him. He's more than just a boring Space Marine, and he amusingly doesn't care. They actually imparted some meaning to the DooM world and it keeps you engaged. Design of levels, weapons and monsters is easily the best out there right now. Only the upcoming Shadow Warrior 2 has a shot at competing with this game this year.

Runs incredibly well on id's new Tech 6 engine that's a massive improvement over the painful mess that was Tech 5. Not only a jump in graphical fidelity and visual effects, but I experienced a huge boost in framerates and texture pop-in was barely noticeable. The high LOD on distant features while maintaining said framerates was particularly striking to me, especially when ogling Hell's massive gnarled awe-inspiring architecture and skyboxes while edging cliffs.

This game is an absolute triumph. I laughed like a damned child numerous times throughout my first playthrough, and my heart sang with the final visual you're provided at the end of the closing credit sequence. DooM is back, and better than ever.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike with any classic gaming franchise brought back from hibernation, I had my doubts about Doom initially. Those doubts weren't helped by a rather lackluster multiplayer beta and Bethesda's decision to withhold review copies. You can imagine my surprise when release day came and everybody was singing its praises. I immediately dropped $60 for it, something I rarely do nowadays, and have absolutely not regretted that decision in the slightest.

To put it simply, Doom is fast, gory, filled with amazingly satisfying weaponry and just all around ridiculously good fun. It reminded me why I fell in love with the genre in the first place. The campaign is a little short, but very replayable. The fact I've gotten over 70 hours of play time out of this game without even touching multiplayer or SnapMap should be testament to just how bloody good it is.

The package is sweetened even further by the engine. My slightly subpar PC was able to run this at ultra settings with no frame rate dips whatsoever. Even if you can't run at ultra, though, the game still looks great, and it has all the options you would expect from a solid PC game. It even allows you to enable a ridiculous detailed performance overlay.

If you haven't played Doom yet, what the heck are you doing? Get this game! It's an absolute blast and you won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was cautiously optimistic when I first learned that the new DOOM was announced. I almost lost hope when I played the multiplayer beta -- although I thought it was fun to play, I had to agree with the majority in that it just didn't feel very DOOM. The bad news is multiplayer is about the same as it was in the beta; you'll either love it or hate it. The good news: Miraculously, the campaign has turned out to be the best gaming experience I've had since StarCraft 2 and Bioshock 1. Being blown away like that after being skeptical for so long is reinvigorating and doesn't happen often, especially with AAA franchises.

The depth of the campaign defies the formula of every FPS title in recent memory in an amazing way. Its old-school Doom with keycards and gut wrenching battles, but with many twists that leave you guessing about what you'll find next. There's so much to discover on every level, at times its almost overwhelming. I can say with a smile on my face that the bestiary is unrelentlingly nightmarish, the weapon arsenal and your combat abilities are rediculously badass, the levels are masterful in visual and mechanical design, and there's some deviously well-hidden secrets in there for you to find.

EDIT: My God! I almost forgot -- the music is incredibly awesome. No review of DOOM would be complete without mentioning the heavy industrial tunes of Mick Gordon!

The single player alone is worth the $60, easily. id Software has proven to me that they've still got that magic. Now they just need to make Quake V in the style of the first Quake and I can die happy. As for DOOM, I assure you won't be disappointed. Rip and tear!\t\t\t\t\t

1. Overall
--------------


- OK I first played doom when I was 10 years old. - I played doom2 when I was 12 years old, QUake1 when I was 12 and final doom when I was 13/14 etc etc. I know doom + this is an attempt to revamp doom, its about 10 years since doom3. and we all know doom 3 , and , we all know "rage" for that matter. We also are well aquainted with the current trend of bethesda and zenimax. - And thats what this is , this is a trendy modern xbox/console game that has elements from DOOM2.
This is not bad, this is great !!!- the graphics are next gen and it has a unique approach to gameplay.


2. In depth
-----------------


The zombieguys, shotgun guys and chaingun guys are gone! PINKY is completely different and looks like a creature from Pacific rim (*Ahem). The cacodemons look like doom64 or doom3. THe hell knight was just copied and pasted from doom3 and doesnt really fit at all. -THe hell knights and barons are animated very badly, when they move, and do their attacks it's pretty tradgic :( The mancubus are ok-ish, looked kinda "cuddly" and friendly. The new hellrazers are cool. and the zombies everywhere were just too generic. The imps were good. the lost souls needed alot of tweeking !! The spider deamon at the end was a fail. I doubt there are any arachnotrons, archviles, pain elementals etc why not, bethesda thats why not.


WHat happened??? Well the game is pretty modern, it plays like halo on legendary mode, so, the levels are straight forward no brainers with a few jump around on platform sections to slow you down. the "Hit boxes " on the explosions are massive and this means its very hard to play the game like doom2 becuase you will die no matter how hard or good you are on nightmare mode :(. They didnt put enough items balanced around the levels so on ultra-violence you do run out of ammo and its lame, becuase there is a lazer pistol with a sight that has infinite ammo "yes -thank you bethesda".


Also to make the game even more of a departure from doom and quake, you cant progress in a level untill you kill the current "wave of creatures" so doors will be in "lockdown" preventing you from running around the level with 100s of creatures, running and screaming for your life, running running around like the original doom and quake. - Its that bad. It's that bad. The story is appalling- I mean appalling. And, its not even lore friendly to doom becuase doom had a lose story if you read the manual, back in those days thats where you would find the story. - The story for "this" doom was just not warranted !!- It was the spider brians that came through the portal and learned to use human computers, they created the cyborg demons, but who cares. Obviously not the managers and directors of this game.

The revenant is really cool and would have made a better story narator than the two main characters BECUASE THE STORY IS TERRIBLE and the oneplayer IS LINEAR AS HELL.- after level 5 it just feels like halo, the one player just got worse and worse after the first 3 levels, and the jumping and climbing sections they threw in just feel like mario 64 or some thing.


3. Conclusion
-----------------------
THis game is not a fail, but , it suffers from having a very large dev team probably several teams that never even met in person out side of a dropbox and email conversation.
The art is fantastic, it has a 2000AD feel to it. It's not "too dark" or too violent, there is alot of colour which is what doom was famous for.

positive note:
-Lets hope there are some mods to improve imersibility("immersiveness)becuase that is what this game needs, a thriving community of mods and fan levels!! Becuase doom was the game that started that whole movment. - Tweeking, nerfing, patching and some TLC will make this last a long long time.\t\t\t\t\t
