\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original Doom sits on a high pedestal as the originator of the Run n' Gun genre. ID software took what it learned from Wolfenstein and dialed it to 11 (for the 80s, nowadays the game seems downright tame). For years that was the gold standard, until games such as Half Life and Halo introduced elements such as storyline and variable weapon loadouts (IE you can't carry everything.)
For a very long time that became the norm for FPS games. Doom attempted similar with the ill-received Doom 3, and that fallout from that hurt the developer greatly.
Doom 2016 is a return to form, taking what made it such a monument to gaming and only adding what would improve upon that particular style. The emphasis on aggressive shooting can be a stumbling block for many younger players; the game flat out punishes you for trying to find cover. The typical shoot-find ammo-shoot some more method will leave you with little to no ammo, and learning to balance glory kills and chainsaw attacks to pull ammo from enemies is a must.
There is a storyline, but it is expressed in ways that, aside from a few scenes, avoid watering down the gameplay.
I bought this game on release for the PS4 and ended up staying up all night playing non-stop, then going back to collect all the collectibles.
For the cost on Steam, it was worth picking up just to enjoy again,\t\t\t\t\t
