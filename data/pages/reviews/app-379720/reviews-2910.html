\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought DOOM the day before it went on sale, so naturally I was bummed that I paid full price for it when I could have gotten it for $19.75. However, after playing it, I'm glad that I payed full price. The developers deserve $60 for every copy. Absolutely amazing game; the most fun I've had playing a video game in a long time. DOOM brings back the raw excitement of playing a FPS; every gun is fun to shoot, every demon fun to kill, and there are actual tactics that you have employ when higher difficulties are chosen. The movement feels very smooth and the mechanics of the game push you to kill more and move faster because there is no reloading, you get ammo and health from the enemies you eliminate. Plus, the soundtrack is flawless, suiting to every situation. I don't normally write game reviews, but I felt it was necessary for this one. Anyone who is sceptical about buying this game shouldn't be; if you enjoy big guns, dead demons, and have the money to dish out, buy it now!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played doom first around 93-94, when i was in primary school and been swapping games on 3'5'' floppy disks :)
Bethesda is one of the worst companies for me. When they bought Fallout first and ID later i lost my faith for the second time in PC gaming (first one was when Interplay closed and Black Isle Studios not much later) and really wanted to hate this game too. To be honest so far im having a great time! The devs really remade the original DOOM experience, very similar tempo game with nice smooth movement and intense gunfighting, but now you are able to jump and grab ledges and move around more freely than in the original. Also there is some minor experience system so you dont start out with full stats and can beef yourself up during the leels. By the way levels. So far the design is excellent and clever. The 3 key system is still there and alltogether the levels do have a lot in common with the ones in the original game and some clearly recognisable parts. The maps are not overhelmingly huge yet (not sure if they get any bigger) but they feel just the right size. The whole atmosphere oozes DOOM, music is gritty, game is bloody and you shoot monsters to pieces!
Highly recommended!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wish there was a "so-so" option instead of just thumbs up or thumbs down. This is actually a success when you consider what they set out to make. It looks great, plays smooth, and is very solid, but, I still didn't like it. That doesn't mean other people won't like it, nor would I ever tell anyone not to buy this.

I played the original Doom until my eyes fell out of my head, memorizing every monster and every pickup in every room on every level. Doom 3 was okay for the creep/scare factor (although I never made it through the Hell level), but this remake is just another shooter with pretty graphics. Very pretty graphics. I give it a thumbs down only because I found the game to be, at it's core, very boring and repetitive. Some folks may be looking for that, but I just can't do it any more. I only got to mission 3 or so and had to quit because even the idea of seeing more pretty graphics was not enough to make me want to slog through more of the same mindless shoot-em-up. I also found it to be a bit difficult, even on the lowest difficulty setting, so there's a frustration factor, which doesn't help the fact that you just keep doing the same thing over and over. Hunting for secret areas is kinda fun though, so that's a plus.

They added upgrade options for your suit and armor, and yeah, those are kinda cool, but nothing revolutionary. It also kinda forces you to figure out which weapons work best against certain monsters, but that just gets annoying more than anything else because of the constant switching between weapons, including ones that aren't all that much fun to use. Even the "glory kills", while fun to watch at first, get old after a short while. For all the shooting they force you to do, ammo runs out far too quickly, so you end up using less-than-ideal weapons anyway.

I dunno... if you like the idea of mindlessly running through pretty space scenes with lots of blood and guts, and endless pew pew pew for little reward, then this game is for you. If you like games that respect your intelligence, look for something else.\t\t\t\t\t

I can't believe it.. it's him! He's back!.. and he's more bloodthirsty than ever before, holy christ! Id Software truly nailed it with this sequel, the one and true successor to the original Doom in high resolution graphics (The "highest quality pixels" amirite), tremendously huge and well detailed levels and satisfyingly violent combat mechanics coupled with finishing moves and an arsenal of weaponry that leaves absolutely nothing to be desired. It is one of the best first person shooters I have ever played. It's so incredibly fast paced, straight to the point and as brutal as it gets, it's just breathtakingly amazing.

Introduction

Welcome to hell! No really, welcome to the space station Union Aerospace Corporation or short UAC, an organization specialised in the exploration of Mars, Hell and its precious energy source: Argent. Despite the controversy of the material, it is a much needed resource on Earth, with a power output far exceeding the efficiency of conventional nuclear power plants.

But with that surge of power come dire consequences. As the relentless experimentation and exploitation of and with the material advanced, the creatures damned into the realms of Hell were set free on Mars. Overrun by the hordes, the space station crew were eviscerated by the creatures or later morphed into them. With no man left alive and no hope left for liberation, you are released into this world. Fitted with your Praetor Suit body armor and a burning wraith for redemption, you set off to annihilate every last one of the demons.

Gameplay

This game just gets straight to the point. No nonsense tutorial stage, no holding hands. The moment you wake up from your millenials of sleep in the tomb inside the UAC facility, straight away you jump into the action. The gunplay in this game is just perfect, using the firearms feels enjoyable and as you further upgrade the weapons they become much more powerful and devastating.

Now once you've unloaded a sufficient amount of rounds unto one of the foul creatures, they become staggered. This is where the "Glory" kill system comes into play. Pick a limb, press the action key and watch as the Doom Marine violently rips the creature apart, smashes their head to bits or shoves the dismembered limb down the creatures throat, those moves just never get boring... or, alternatively, just launch another rocket towards them as sendoff or send a ridiculously gigantic orb of plasmatic energy their way, ensuring their definite annihilation.

With every progress the game throws a new class of demon at you, each with a different set of special moves and different approach to best them. In this regard id Software again stayed true to the original, with many of the classic's monsters making an appearance in this sequel.

Level Design

On a rather negative note was the sometimes monotonous stage design of the battle areas against waves of demons as it sometimes felt like you'd advance into the same section of the game and that sometimes it felt confusing to navigate through the stages without looking up the location in the game's Codex map view.

Nevertheless, the levels are filled with lots of small details, containing collectibles and secret rooms scattered throughout the stages, even including unlockable missions in the style of the first Doom as a throwback to the good old times.

Music

Metal. Lots of it. As your chainsaw revs up, your Plasma Rifle is charged up and you are all ready for the imminent battle, the music builds on top of that momentum and fuels the adrenalin rush through the commencing slaughter. Flawlessly integrates into the sceneries you traverse through and makes the murder time a fun time.

Conclusion

This game is all in all a truly fun and satisfying experience to get away from heavily plot induced video games, to jump right into action and gun, rip and tear your way through the baddies. Id Software has shown us that the simplicity of the classics doesn't have to remain a thing of the past and brought the experience of the original to this 21st century release.

Definitely recommend checking it out.

I'll start by saying I like the old Doom games. I heard tons of good things about this game since its release earlier in the year. I've been wanting to pick it up for awhile and now that I finally have I just simply don't like it.

The biggest and primary reason is the game gives me a mild headache and hurts my eyes a bit. No clue why. I've never had this problem with shooters before. I played the first mission popped and advil and took care of a couple things around the house and tried to come back to it a few hours later. Started mission 2 and started having the same problems again. Done with it after that.

Even what I did manage to play I did not enjoy and was disappointed. The graphics look beautiful of course. But there isn't much enjoyment in the game beyond that (FOR ME PERSONALLY - IF YOU LOVE THE GAME GREAT). The glory kill system is absolutely horrible. It feels gimmicky to me and seems like they wanted it in there just to have some other kind of way to speed the game up and add an additional hook to the running and gunning formula. Unfortunately it turns this "shooter" into half shooting half melee game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 14 hours gameplay and finishing the game I can do my review:

Technical aspects: Flawless, Very gorgeous looking game. Very good graphic design and overall in 4k settings it looks stunning, I have nothing bad to add to the techincal graphical aspect. The performance in game is very good too, stable 60 fps the entire game. The only thing here is that sometimes randomly the game game crashes but I fixed it and it does not happen anymore.

Story: Doom has never been a game famous for its story. The story is quite simple. You are that one man army guy that have to kill the entire hell with no mercy. But DOOM is not a game famous for having deep stories. It is famous for slaying demons all around.

Artistic aspects: It has no beutiful landscapes, all you will see is destroyed instalations, Mars and Hell. But this 3 aspects is exactly what DOOM is about.

Content: The content is basicaly weapons and enemies. Thats it. There are a lot of ANNOYING enemies all around and a lot of weapons to kill them with.

Dificulty level: If you play on nightmare, the game because a bit of a challenge. It is not a rush and shoot game (well it is) but it is not so easy to kill enemies and the fact is that 75 percent of all enemies can perform a range attack which can be very annoying. It is not as hard as dark souls but sometimes you can stay 1 hour in one phase because 1 hits almost kills you and you will have to run and gun fastly to get ♥♥♥♥ done.

The enemies in this game are not dumb, in fact they will aim very you will be running at so it can be very annoying to as you have to change quickly your patch if you want not get killed.

This game is an easy 10/10 for a DOOM, it is all what a DOOM needs you will have a lot of fun and you wont never exit the game until you finished. The boss fights are good enough, they could be like in WoW where you first have to find out how it works. But this game feel like that the developers have put all their love in this game and on top of that, that they put in all fans of DOOM wanted in it.\t\t\t\t\t
