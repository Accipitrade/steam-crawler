\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to clarify up front: I would recommend buying this game for the single-player alone. I am willing to give this game roughly a 95% based on the single-player campaign. Alone. I have played first person shooters all my life and this brings the perfect mix of nostalgia and adrenaline-fueled mayhem that modern tweaks and graphics bring. They did everything right with the feel of the game. No more kinda-scary-ish slow parts from Doom 3. If you aren't a completionist, you don't have to find all the secrets. You can just shoot stuff while your heart races at 240bpm. I usually just sit in my chair expressionless, but I found my jaw literally dropping at the fights they put in this game. This is arguably one of THE BEST single-player FPS experiences you can ever have.

The only, ONLY reason I wouldn't give this game a full 100% as a "complete" game is because I would LOVE to see a legitimately supported co-op version of the main game, OR full mod support so we can add that in there ourselves.

Don't get my wrong; playing this alone is worth the money in itself. But that co-op support (don't care about multiplayer myself) would put this thing so over the top that I wouldn't know what to do with myself. Except buy 3 more copies for my friends to play with me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFast paced no-nonsense shooter. The concept of the game is very simple: Here are the bad guys, here is a weapon, have fun!

Just like its 1993 predecessor this game brings fast paced gunladen carnage that knows no bounds. The weapons available range from simple shotguns and assault rifles to chainsaws, rocketlaunchers and the iconic BFG9000. While there is a story the explain why you have decided to commit genocide on all hellspawn, it has taken a backseat to the absolutely phenomenal gameplay. It has been years since there was a shooter so well made and balanced such as this one! The weapons feel powerful, the enemy forces are not mere bulletsponges and you spend most of the time ruinninng and gunning which is a nessecety to stay alive. The enemies rely on massive numbers and firepower. What this translates to is that the game punishes you for cowering behind cover and rewards you when you ram a double barreled shotgun down the throat of a hellknight.

Dispatch the enemy as fast as possible by any and all means nessecary. To further enforce this type of fast paced gameplay, you get rewarded for kills. The fastest and by far most reliable way to re-stock your ammo and HP in a firefight is to kill the enemy so that they drop said ammo and HP. The HP drops increase in size if you use the glory kill system and thge ammo even further so when using the chainsaw to split someone in half.

The enemy forces have great variety with some of them being ranged, others having shields and then you have the ones that are bigger than a house and wants to repeatedly introduce their fists and claws to, well, all of you. The enemy AI is fun and challenging on all difficulty settings and will keep you on your toes, especially in those big firefights where you happen to stumble opon half a battallion of enemy troops.

The maps of the game are incredibly well done. The atmosphere is spot on and instead of boring linearity you are on the constant lookout for secrets, bonuses and special items which takes you off the beaten path time and time again, serving as a breather between rampages.

Oh and the graphics are just amazing, courtesy of the id Tech 6 engine.


Pros:
You feel like a badass.
Good graphics.
Insanely fun single player gameplay.
Lots of exploration.
Lots of fun and customizable gameplay.
Lots of custimization in multiplayer.

Cons:
The whole run and gun can get repetetive against lower tier enemies.
Multiplayer is one massive rocket launcher fest.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is THE game for me. I loved the late-90s/Early-2000s ultraviolence FPS genre. Any attempt to replicate that after those times seemed half-hearted and unfulfilling... until now. DOOM is the essence of that genre, made better and amplified.

Positives :
- Graphics : It looks amazing. My jaw drops everytime I enter a new Mars level.
- Music : MY â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥ GOD that music!!! BFG Divison melts your faces and takes your virginity.
- Art-style : Amplifies the ambience. Completely unapologetic and in-your-face. Blood, guts, gore, GLORIOUS!!
- Mechanics : Fast movement, air-strafing, relentless action, no â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥footing, no walking, no "rubber-banding", no â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥.
- Difficulty : Ultra-Nightmare gets pretty difficult, which means it's that much more rewarding to play. Yet, the "I'm too young" difficulty should allow unexperienced FPS players to enjoy the game to the fullest without being intimidated.


Negatives :
- Multiplayer is underwhelming. The loadout system and levelling-up system undermines it. But I have a feeling they will listen to the feedback and fix it with the big upcoming multiplayer patch. This game absolutely has the potential to be as good as Q3A, it just needs to be unlocked.
- The in-game points/upgrade system in single player is annoying, but thankfully, it's completely optional. You can play through the entire game without bothering yourself with them, so this is only a minor gripe.
- Single player campaign not long enough. I was sad that I finished the game in roughly 8 hours. I LOVE IT AND I WANT MOAARRR!!
- Expensive season pass just for some multiplayer maps and playable demons(???). Should be free, but I'll reserve judgement for that when it comes out. But once again, it's completely optional.

Verdict : Best single-player FPS game EVER for a certain demographic (myself included) and a "must play" classic for every other FPS fan. This is THE 10 out of 10 game, the game that all other single-player FPS games will be benchmarked against. Rip and Tear!!!

PS : If you think it's too expensive for ~8 hrs of gameplay, I can somewhat agree but I will say that the game was thorougly utterly satisfying and to me, you can't put a price on that. If you can't buy it at full-price, please do yourself a favour and buy it during one of the sales.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf this is what FPS games have become in 2016 then I'm done. If you have never played a FPS before then you might enjoy this game. I had heard that the multiplayer in this game was a mess (and it is) but I figured the single player would be fun. Wrong. This game is so painfully generic and repetitive its like a cross platform FPS from the early 2000s but with better graphics. I felt zero immersion or emotion the entire 8hrs I played up until my save file became corrupted. It's as if EVERYTHING that made the original doom games fun has been completely forgotten. So why bother calling it Doom? Even Doom 3 was more doom than this. Performing glory kills and watching the enemies explode health and ammo everywhere like a pinata was a terrible design decision. Id rather have health regeneration, and I hate health regeneration in games. The pacing is all wrong, you have your â€œexploreâ€ segments where you walk around and do nothing, then you have your â€œcombat segmentsâ€ where you walk into a room, the doors lock and you must defeat hoards of enemies that spawn from thin air and disappear when killed before the doors unlock. And you repeat this over and over and over until your mouse hand starts to fall asleep. And in these combat rooms you get to listen to the same combat music over and over. That's right combat music, as in Everquest. And when all the enemies are defeated the music returns to the level music. There is no tension or buildup knowing that the area is clear or not. Why does Doom have upgrades for weapons and armor? Why does Doom have side quests? Why does Doom have a cluttered HUD full of hand holding information. Why does Doom try and tell a story when it knows there shouldn't be one other than the simple premise. Why is it so hard to make a old school mindless shooter? Shouldn't that be easy to develop? Mods might have helped some aspects of this game but no, no MOD support, and no SDK. They give you snap map lol. This game is so bad its enough to make a veteran gamer want to never buy another new game again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tId really captured the frantic combat from the original classics, Sure there is a story, but honestly, you won't care much about it and that's OK the combat is that good! Pacing is good, the graphics are outstanding and the sounds are spot on and greate good ambiance. There is some good replayability with TONS of secrets and easter eggs which I won't spoil for you here. Snapmap the level editor lets you create your own levels and its very good with relative ease of use and many features.

Multiplayer... I don't know, to me it feels like a floaty poor man's Halo multiplayer clone. Seriously its painful to see. It feels off when compared to the excelent feel of the single player campaign's combat. It remains to be seen if patches and DLC eventually make it good enough to stand beside the single player campaign and the excellent Snapmap.

If you don't mind lots of gore or demonic themes and an overall wacky story the game is great.

Note: At the time of this review the game had issues with the framerate being locked at 30FPS if you turn V sync on. If you have a beefy system that can keep it well above 60FPS The frame tearing is not too bad. You have been warned though. (If you have a freesync compatible monitor and an AMD videocard card that supports it you shouldn't have any issues though as it plays very smoothly.)

If you are a fan of the original this will definitely remind you of those days. Buy it!\t\t\t\t\t
