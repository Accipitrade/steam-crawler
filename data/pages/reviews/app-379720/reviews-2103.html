\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWriting this review after i just finished the SP campaign, and i can't help but wholeheartedly recommend this game! I don't think there's a lot i can say about it that other people haven't but it's just really good. I'm just gonna mention how awesome the combat is. Do you like blowing demons' heads off with your super shotgun? Or maybe you're more of a rocket launcher kind of guy. How about ripping through dozens of demons with your trusty chaingun? Or maybe you like being more up-close and personal, in which case chainsawing through a Baron of Hell sounds ideal for ya. And aside from the SP campaign, there's also the multiplayer, which is pretty active at the moment. I am able to get into a game and start grenade launching people in the face in less than a minute.

As a side note, this game also made me want to go back to its origins with the 1993 Doom game, and after playing that as well, i came out with a newfound appreciation for a classic. Definetly check that game out as well if you can help it, it's a must play!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat can you possibly say that hasn't been said in the last 3 years about this amazing shooter... Let's try this: launch Rage (1) and I dare you to suffer through 10 minutes of its clunky shooting mechanics. Doom (2016) feels light years more advanced, aiming is perfection, raw, no acceleration, no smoothing. I bought 2 copies of this game, one on PlayStation 4, and props to id Software they nailed controller look/aim mechanics too. They deserve credit for that, even the mighty Blizzard failed miserably with their sad Overwatch aiming on controller/console.

The engine is a marvel in other ways too. It looks stunning and yet at all the highest/ultra settings it still hits stable 144FPS at 1440p resolution with ease.

The only issue with the game is that it's too short, with such amazing aiming/movement/graphics/performance you can't get enough of it. They tried to fluff it up with secret challenge platforms and the Arcade Mode.

To be fair maybe development of the multiplayer component took away a lot of resources, but I find it hard to believe they honestly thought multiplayer would take on in popularity. You can't possibly know the modern gaming audience that poorly; The era of fast paced, no health-regeneration shooters is over. Modern players want to camp in their head glitch spots for minutes at a time and they want to recover their health after every engagement crouching in some mouse hole.\t\t\t\t\t

The goal of this game is to shoot demons and then move to the next area to shoot more demons. This game takes that simple concept and fine tunes it so well.

It was so easy to make it overwhelming and frustrating like Rainbow Six Siege Outbreak or boring and repetitive like Killing Floor 2. But the enemies come in small waves and the fluid movement and great level design allow you to feel like you're in total control.

The variety of weapons also allows you to change your approach on the fly once you've memorized the weapon hotkeys.\t\t\t\t\t
