\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been finding it hard to finish. Disinterest has set in.

The game is very pretty, but has some glaring audio glitches unless you ensure the music is at an overwhelming volume.

What bothered me was the quake-like combat arenas, and an advertising effort around that, but not quake-like speed. The game is slow. Compared to modern games it's fast, but compared to other arena shooter gameplay it's sluggish. In addition the pacing is bad. No hedging. The pacing is bad. Hectic combat in a quasi-arena followed by wandering very interesting but empty, lifeless environments then another arena.

The last bit of criticism I have is the exploration-based advancement system sucks. It's a way to extend the life of an already very short game. Want that upgrade? Want more health? Well better scour every. last. little. piece. of the map. Watch out! Some common map aesthetics like vents and hatches suddenly have one interactive version hidden away for those unlocks to be stuffed behind. The game is far better if you ignore the unlocks entirely- or just watch a youtube video like 90% of players did.

Oh but the arcade mode is surprisingly fun. Probably due to the forced speed increase. In addition, the aesthetic and story is quite nice. However some part of me made me feel like some of the set pieces were subtle advertisements for Prey.

It's worth playing if you have low expectations and want a 10-15 hour long shooter.\t\t\t\t\t
