\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA worthy successor to the originals, as each installment in the series has been. No other interactive electronic experience will ever come close to how mind-blastingly bodacious this is...Well, maybe the sequel will, but that's besides the point.

Overall, if you play video games for fun, here's the thing for you! I, for one, can't think of the last time I enjoyed a single-player FPS so thoroughly. The game honors it's predecessors with the feel of the originals, and the look of it's later installments.

Sure, it's not as revolutionary as the first game was, but who cares? A fun game is a fun game, and that's that. Just slay demons, don't think about it too much! Although if you DO want to think, there's a huge amount of lore documents to dive into. The campaign is riddled with secrets and hidden things to find; and, surprisingly, the lore behind it all is actually worth getting into!...Who'da thunk it?

The game does have it's flaws, and a few of them. Multiplayer is fun, but hilariously undercooked for what it is. The level editor is intuitive, but frankly, it lacks the fidelity to make exactly what you want. Also, only custom levels have co-op functionality, so that's a shame. Thankfully, the campaign makes up for most of that, and I recommend the game solely for the single-player.

tl;dr: If you want the game, get the game. It's fun, relatively low-priced, and the new one is coming out soon! There's no better time to get into DOOM than right now. This is probably the most hype the series has had since the 90's, and that's saying something. Don't go in with giant expectations, and you'll be pleasantly surprised! Overall, huge recommendation here, and I myself can't wait to play DOOM Eternal.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 2016 is one of the greatest properly executed power fantasies to come out in the last decade.

In my opinion they got damn near everything here down to a tee, and because of this I feel like this raises the bar for this genre and single player games in general.

Mars.

For the story, some people would say that there isn't one. There is, clearly the more important feature is the game play but the story that ID Software crafted to push the narrative and game play forward is cut, clear and precise. It is no Red Dead Redemption 2 but that's not what you play Doom for. Mars has a portal to hell on it now because someone was convinced by the devils lies, and they just happened to be researching and siphoning hell's resources at the same time. The Doom Slayer is summoned, sent back and forth from hell and does what any Doom Slayer does.

Murders demons.

Speaking of murdering demonic creatures, there is actually a large variety in this game to massacre. Their varieties and types are what keep the game from getting boring along with their smart placement and wonderful set design by ID killing demons has never been so satisfying or fluid. Challenging at times even on the lower difficulties, but as soon as you learn which weapons in your massive arsenal are for which situations is soon becomes a breeze. With their new movement improvements and Glory Kills mechanic it is an incredibly polished experience. When it's going off on all cylinders its like a symphony in orchestration.

Synthesized chainsaws.

As a Audio Engineer and part-time Producer I can tell you that this soundtrack was expensive. Just by listening to it. It must have some world class people working in the audio department because this is the most solid soundtrack I've heard in a ridiculous amount of time. The layering, the sound design, the mixing and mastering. The way the dynamic music kicks in and gets more intense as the fight sways from side to side over time. This is one of the greatest soundtracks a game developer has ever released, and it only heightens the experience to make you feel like the one and only badass there ever was. It's what keeps you going, what keeps the blood pumping and forcing you towards towards the end of the game.

Pacing.

I believe the only thing this game struggles with, and I use the term "struggle" lightly is it's pacing. The Beginning of the game may come off as a bit abrasive because of the lack of weapons and the repetitiveness but that is because the game is still showing you the ropes. But to a seasoned player this becomes a slight bit tiresome. Overall the level design and the way the map works keeps you pushing to find hidden secrets and extra goodies that'll help you towards your progression of your character. Unfortunately I think the game suffers in it's pacing specifically towards the beginning of the end of the game again. The game took me about 13-15 hours to complete and around the 9th-10th hour before the game started ramping up towards the climax I could find myself a bit bored doing the same thing over and over again. Having to take breaks and getting tired of the same power fantasy. But after they cranked it to 11 to the scale of WHAT THE ♥♥♥♥ IS GOING ON during the climax it makes it entirely worth it. I believe they mentioned this specifically on stage at Bethesda's E3 Conference while talking about Doom Eternal's pacing and story. Which was followed by showing us an insane amount of new and refreshing mechanics for the second coming game.

(Oh yeah there is multiplayer too for matchmaking and deathmatch and the like, but I only played it during the beta before the game released. It was a lot of fun but after trying it now there's a lot of bogus mandatory game modes that are in the playlist. And the player base is completely dead, even though the multiplayer was leagues ahead of Quake Champions.)

Overall.

I picked the game up for $15 from the Steam Summer Sale and it was worth every penny. If I was aware how good this game was when it came out I would have easily paid $60 for it. And I will be picking up Doom Eternal.

9.7 / 10

Will slay demons again. \t\t\t\t\t
