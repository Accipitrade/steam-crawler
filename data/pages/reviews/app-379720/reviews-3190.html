\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI grew up on doom 1 and doom 2. Blasting baddies on my old 386 took up a lot of my time way back when.

Doom 3 came out and it wasnt a total disappointment, but it was quite a bit different than I expected. With a slower pace and a focus more on the story (which wasnt exactly enthralling)

Doom 2016 brings us full circle back to the formula of yesteryear. The story is minimal, just a wrapper that gives you enough narrative to keep moving forward. The guns and weapons are satisfying to use and the game play is fluid and snappy. It's as gory as ever and once the killing starts it doesnt stop.

Theres no regenerating health or shields here, the comprimise they made was the new glory kills system. If you damage an enemy enough without blowing him to a pulp he will be stunned briefly, giving you a chance to go up and rip him a new one which will make them drop small amounts of health to keep you up during a fight.

The only real complaint I could say I have is that the game tends to abuse the 'oh you walked into an area and all the doors are gonna be sealed until you kill a few waves of enemies' trope a little too much, but it's a minor complaint.
