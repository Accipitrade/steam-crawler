\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me preface this review by saying that I really like games like Borderlands, where there's lots of roaming to do, sidequests to break things up, and (of course) copious amounts of weaponry to play with.

That said, however, there's a lot of pleasure to be found in the clean simplicity of a game like Doom; you start at point "A," your goal is point "B," and you kill every last Hell-spawned, demon sonofa***** in between those two points. No messing around with some goober's fetch quest, no grinding levels to improve your loot drops or what-have-you--and no mucking about with deciding between two guns that only differ by 5% to two stats--just go in and boot demon a**.

That's not to say that there aren't things to keep you invested while you're slaughtering your way across Mars. It is Doom, after all, and that means secrets to sniff out for a start. And while the game throws you a map that'll show you a lot of things, figuring out how to actually get them is still on you. Then there are the challenges, some as simple as finding a set number of hidden things in each stage, while others can be remarkably tricky to manage (like killing a certain amount of specific foes in a very particular manner).

To help make you a better kicker of a**es and taker of names, you'll have the ability to upgrade both your armor and every weapon that you find, letting you rampage into the horde in the way that suits you best. You can turn a shotgun into a delivery system for crowd-shattering micro-explosions, improve your armor's ability to resist those same explosions (for danger-close fun), and many other things besides.

It looks great, the sound is suitably atmospheric, and the controls are (for the most part) very smooth. However, I use a controller to play and that did bring up a couple of issues. Foremost, the movement is a bit stiff and heavy on the default sensitivity, and I had to dial the horizontal and vertical both to about 65% before it felt as smooth as I'm accustomed to in other FPS games. The other issue is with the minimap, where the controls are quite heavily restricted in what they'll allow by way of manipulation; the controls you're shown are all that you get, while the mouse allows for precision tilt and rotation to better make out or focus on areas that would otherwise be difficult to see.

I'm also disappointed that there's no sprint, a feature that I've grown to enjoy from other games, and I still find myself periodically fumbling with the typical sprint toggle in an effort to get my armored a** moving. But that is, overall, a minor thing and doesn't detract from the gameplay as a whole.\t\t\t\t\t
