\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHE GOOD-

Instead of Phobos and Deimos, it's Mars and an ancient Necropolis. Instead of a teleportation incident gone wrong, and willfully repeated for some reason, it's a powerful energy fracture that's exploited, despite the obvious risks, and a series of trips into wonderland for the "gifts" that keep on giving. There are actually other human-ish characters to drive the story forward, sort of. The demons are fully fleshed and meaty versions of their once sprite-ly selves. But this is no time to weep. In truth, this is a time to rejoice! For this is the Doom of yonder, in all its run and gun glory!

The addition of the Glory Kill is the true hallmark that, despite its non-traditional nature, is right at home in the gory, bloody, non-stop adrenaline inducing, awesomely dangerous cardiovascular trial, that is the Doom we all wanted back. No offence to Malcolm Betruger intended, of course...

Now strap on the minigun, jam in those shotgun shells, and get ready to deliver some medical advice to the demons: "I'm afraid the diagnosis isn't good, sir. Indeed, the only hopeful course is: IMMEDIATE MYOCARDIAL INGESTION! Allow me." Then scale a cliff and, for the first time ever, experience the sheer euphoria of getting to rip the eye out of a cacodemon, FROM ABOVE!! "Take that ya floating freak! Mwwaa ha haa!!"

Yes, it's true, the chainsaw is now for emergency use only. That said, gazing at the shimmering trails of ammunition which burst longingly forth from an imp as you kindly relieve it of half its torso, is one of the most gratifying experiences next to boiling a Baron of Hell alive with the BFG. You thought you were going to pull my legs of again, did you? Eat plasma, you horned horror!

THE BAD-

The campaign felt a mite short. I was somewhat deflated when the final boss arrived seemingly all too soon. Granted, I'll say that about a lot of games, but this one did feel particularly epigrammatic. Great, but brief.

If you're considering picking up a copy this late in the life cycle, be aware that, while the community generated content is full to bursting, very few players are still on the net for a quick deathmatch. Don't be surprised if you can't get a multiplayer game going.

THE VERDICT-

It's Doom as we all remember it, brought new life. If you are into FPS action games, I can't recommend it more. If you haven't played the original Doom and are into FPS action games, play both. Unless you are really excited to play the multiplayer, that is...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a very underrated fps with its roots firmly in the old school shooter mould. The shooting is basic as is gameplay but all in a good way. You aren't troubled with IQ tests in the form of puzzles, you just work your way through each gloriously rendered level one frag at a time.
The graphics are top notch but without the performance hit that usually comes with such a good looking game. If you have a fairly decent mid spec system from the last few years you should be able to max this game out or get close to it.
The movement in this game is amazingly fluid and it just feels so responsive and enjoyable to play. The shooting is very satisfying, let alone when you finish enemies off with 'glory kills' which are just plain impressive.
I have been playing the arcade mode a bit lately, which is pretty much just playing through levels without the story element but rather a focus on getting max kills in as many gory ways as you can manage. With the shooting and kill animations being so well done, it is all a hell of a lot of fun.
I tried the multiplayer a while back but could not find any matches, which is a shame as it probably could make for a classic MP game with its awesome shooting and accessibility due to low system requirements and easy to learn gameplay.

It goes without saying then, I give this a big thumbs up and id software remains a legendary dev team\t\t\t\t\t
