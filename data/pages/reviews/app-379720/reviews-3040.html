\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis games tries to be a remake of the original DOOM games that focuses more on action rather than the horror atmosphere of the third installment. And at first glance this works allright: no friggin flashlight, well lit levels, no reloading, fast platform traversal ... it should have all the things that made the original games good ... and then falls flat on its face.

Two reasons:

1.) The original DOOM games were a fluent runner through a demon filled maze with only some heavier foes that hardly stopped you in your tracks. This new installment is an arena-fighter were you move from one arena to the next, having to clear spawning waves of demons before you can move on to the next larger location for the next battle, which completely kills this sensation of flow. The developers might have thought that this gives the player more space to fight in and search for power-ups that help them in the battle. My approach is rather more simple: dodge and just kill everything. 98% of the time I obly found the hidden power-up when the room was already cleared out. Yeah ... very helpful, thanks.

2.) Also, the developers decided that they needed to add an upgrade system like all modern shooters have these days. Unfortunately that's in complete contrast to the whole motion/action-character of the game, because ever so often you have to pause, open the in-game menu and make a decision on what to spend your upgrade points on. Not only does this take you completely out of the action each time, you also feel compelled to try the new "tactical" secondary feature of your weapon and die a couple of times before you realize that the simple primary function of your weapon (you know ... the one where you just point it a demon and do damage) still simply works the best. This game would have been actually much better without customizable weapons, armor upgrades and stupid skill challenges which noone really asked for. DOOM shouldn't be a tactical RPG shooter like Deus Ex, it should just be a simple-minded yet fun slaughterfest.\t\t\t\t\t

This review is only of the single player campaign of DOOM.

DOOM is without a doubt probably one of the best and most fun 1st person shooters to come out in the recent years. It balances new and old mechanics with some excelent optimization to give you a dose of enojoyable entertainment. Gory entertainment that is.

Pros:
+ Looks good
+ Runs at 110-120 and sometimes more FPS on my 144hz monitor with a 1.5 year old GTX 970
+ It's a fun to play mindless shooter
+ Weapons feel varied and feel cool to shoot
+ Upgrade system does not feel like a modern gimmick forced in the game - upgrades are actually pretty nice

Cons:
- Story is there just to give you an excuse to go from point A to B
- Towards the end it aaaalmost felt like it was getting repetitive
- If the Multiplayer is not what you want (or if it is as bad as the internet was saying), this is very hard to recommend at anything above half its launch price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tid Software is back to classic form with this absolutely cracker of a game. Insanely fast, tactical, old school combat focused on movement and aggression with a host of beefy weapons and menagerie of classic and new demons. Any new additions to the combat system just serve to make the core combat even more fun.

The level design harkens back to the classic non-linear and expansive maps in the original games. Full of secrets and alternate passages, they encourage exploration and the progression through them feels just like classic Doom.
