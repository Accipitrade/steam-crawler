Enter an area, the doors close, mobs spawn, clear them all, the doors open. This is basically most of what goes on in the game. The "fatalities" got boring fast. In the last third of the game I was so bored I had to force myself to push it to the end. The plot and characters were unengaging to the degree that the game might have been slightly more enjoyable with no plot at all. So while most people seem to love this game, you might want to try the demo first to make sure. For me it was not worth the money.

This review will cover the following:
  • Gameplay
  • Story
  • Graphics
  • Sound
  • Technical
  • Sidenotes
TL;DR at the bottom for those who are too lazy.

Welcome to DOOM. The game where you get to angrily punch everything in the face while heavy metal assaults your ears. Note that I will be focusing on the campaign portion of the game for the most part.
Gameplay:
The gameplay is great fun. Fast paced and demanding focus. Seriously, if you stand still for about five-ten seconds, you’re gonna wish you didn’t stop to take a breather. So if you don’t like fast paced jumping around and getting attacked from all sides. You may want to look elsewhere. The game rewards you for your mobility with health, ammo, and armor. Glory Kills are awesome and satisfying. Each level is open ended with plenty of secrets to find. The level challenges are fun to pull off most of the time. Upgrades to your suit, weapons, and runes add some advantages based on your style of play.
Story:
This is a DOOM game, so there is not that much in the way of story. Basically, you’re a ruthless warrior who sat around in Hell for a little while eating demons for breakfast when you were put into a sarcophagus. Then the UAC goes to Mars and find a way to exploit Hell as an energy source. The demons get angry and invade. The UAC then wakes you up to help them murder all the demons, so get to it. There is some deeper stuff if you go and read all the in-game logs and stuff. There definitely is a nice going on here. Basically the story just serves as an excuse for you to be in these locations.
Graphics:
This game is pretty nice looking. It has its flaws, but it’s still nice to look at. Lighting looks amazing and visual effects are awesome. Model and texture quality could’ve improved in some areas, but I can’t really complain as they are small details. There are lots of options to change certain details as well. Although, I have to ask: Why do we need so many anti-aliasing options, like WTF?
Sound:
I probably drool over the sound a little too much. The environment sound great. The guns sound beefy and awesome. Enemies sound distinct from each other and sound great while doing it. The voice work is pretty great. Dr. Hayden is one of the coolest sounding characters ever. AND THE MUSIC! Mick Gordon’s no Martin O’Donnell, but he’s pretty close. The soundtrack is top notch and its implementation into combat is awesome. You’ll hear the music pick up when you get a glory kill or when more enemies come in. The music itself is worth listening to on your own.
Technical:
Not much to say here. I haven’t encountered that many bugs and a crash (maybe two). The game is optimized very well. I’m surprised that with my GTX 1050 Ti, I’m able to get a stable 60 FPS on Ultra settings. However, your experience may be different based on hardware or maybe OS.
Sidenotes:
I haven’t played that much multiplayer, but from what I remember, it’s nothing spectacular, pretty run of the mill. So honestly, you shouldn’t buy this game just for the multiplayer. Snapmap is pretty cool, but fairly limited. There is plenty of user created content there that you can play with friends or by yourself. Or you can make your own maps. It has potential and can definitely be expanded in the future. Maybe with DOOM Eternal.
TL;DR:
Gameplay: Fun and challenging.
Story: Good story, but does not interfere with the gameplay too much.
Graphics: Pretty.
Sound: Eargasm.
Technical: Stable and optimized (Your results may vary).
Sidenotes: Multiplayer is nothing special. Snapmap is pretty neat.

Have a nice day.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort version: this is an amazing reboot of the original game, and shouldn't be missed. 9/10

Long version: Obviously, in a reboot of a series this old, there are going to be huge improvements to sound, graphics...pretty much everything. This brings me to the most interesting addition: the glory kill. An enemy on the brink of death flashes, allowing you to perform a finishing move that heals you. This healing mechanic combined with the gore of the finishing moves makes for some bloody fights that feel strategic and fast-paced. Classic weapons have been reworked with a mod system, that gives your weapon an extra ability.
It wouldn't be totally inaccurate to call this a twitch-shooter, although that's not always the case. It has slower moments, and times when you're expected to explore, or solve some sort of puzzle (good luck with the lasers in the BFG room). Mastering platforming is key, as one off-target jump could mean death. So, all in all, it's a fast paced game. Oh, and it's really damn good.
The enemies are varied, and numerous. Boss fights feel appropriately challenging, and they are visually really cool.
There is a point system, partially related to level specific challenges, that allows you to purchase weapon upgrades. A similar system exists for your armor, health, ammo. And for the suit your character wears.

Now the bad stuff...
This game has almost 0 plot. I get it, it's DOOM, it's a mindless demon killing spree. Still, when there's only one weak link in the chain, that one link is gonna stand out. The rest of the game being so fantastic just makes it clearer that the story is the weak point. This lack of a true story will take its toll, as you'll likely find yourself burnt out after 1-2 levels. Without a story to constantly drive you forward, it starts to just feel kind of repetitive. Because it is. Kinda.
Certain enemies are a real pain in the ass, even if they pose no real threat (looking at you, imps).
Even though I couldn't take it for more than 2 levels without feeling bored, I always found myself wanting to come back to it.\t\t\t\t\t
