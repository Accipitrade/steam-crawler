\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is perfect.

I bought it months ago on a big sale and just let it sit. I didn't have a PC as a kid so never really played Doom until Doom 64, which was no Goldeneye to say the least. Doom 3 was good but eh, I respect the series but never considered myself a true fan.

Well that's changed now. This is the most fun I've had in a game since Arkham Asylum. There are actually surprising parallels between the Doom and Arkham experience. Arkham was amazing because it made you Batman, period. The combat system was so fluid and fantastic and awarded you for skill, while incorporating all the classic gadgets and just making you feel like an absolute bad-ass clearing rooms full of enemies. Doom's combat does the exact same thing. The rush of being surrounded by dozens of demons, with rockets and explosions and plasma and blood flying around at all angles, as you constantly sprint around swapping between weapons and launching into glory kills as heavy metal pounds in the background is just...it's pure gaming perfection.

The level design, like Arkham, is fantastic. The game is gorgeous, the levels are huge, and there are tons of secrets to find. But the mini map is great and really helps you track them down vs. just wandering around a huge map aimlessly. And again, like Arkham, you're rewarded for your exploration with suit and weapon upgrades and points. The game really encourages you to be a completionist cause damn is being fully modded out worth it. The upgrade system is just deep enough without feeling overwhelming or worrying that you're following the wrong path. It lets you customize to your play style, while still being able to max out everything in the end.

And it's genuinely fun to go back into levels and work on your weapon mastery or rune upgrades. You'll achieve a lot of them just by playing through the campaign naturally, but once you've found all of a levels secrets and power ups, going back and ripping through them for combat alone is a breeze and just keeps the action fast and exhilarating.

I haven't even played multiplayer yet and honestly, I probably never will. Don't really care for most MP shooters, so this campaign is enough for me. If MP's a big deal to you, you might not love it as much cause I've heard the death match is mediocre. But for a SP experience, I truly can't say enough good things about it. Looking forward to diving into the snapmap community campaigns and classic Doom levels, and probably will replay the campaign on a harder difficulty. It's just that much damn fun. I wish I could grow more thumbs to give up for this game, but alas, I am limited to two.

Buy it, and get pumped for Eternal.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is extremely fun and lives up to the hype. I had messed around only lightly with the first game, but I was floored with the presentation of this sequel. It charges you up from the introduction all the way to the end, and satisfied me well on pretty much every front.

The game is a fast-paced first-person shooter, similar in some ways to arena shooters like Quake and Unreal Tournament. You have about 9 weapons to play with (with most having two variations), which can be switched between each other easily at any time, and which are progressively given to you through the campaign. Your character can also be upgraded with various weapon modifications, magic rune powers, and armour upgrades, based on how many collectables you found in the different levels. The objective is to kill every demon you see, and get to the end of every level--occasionally fighting a powerful boss. The levels are gigantic, not always directly linear, and have many secrets, unlockable doors, and multi-leveled arena spaces laid out for a brawl with a large crowd of enemies. The rule of the game is to never stop moving, and the feedback on your guns is incredible, violent, and exciting. You smash through one enemy, and then you run up and get to smash through another with little in the way of downtime. It finishes almost as quick as it begins, well before it gets boring, and allows for a high level of replayability thanks to its engaging combat system and expansive world.

As far as the visuals go, it's absolutely stellar. After only short loading screens, you get dropped into a beautiful sci-fi world, full of intelligent and believable renditions of a base on Mars, and accented by gratuitous blood-splatter and horror imagery fitting for a demonic invasion. Given how good the game looks, I was amazed that I never experienced any slow-down, and the game was easily able to keep up with such fast gameplay and still render cool enemies and cooler worlds. Everything about its visual design is just that: cool -- imparting a kind of feeling like what a high schooler would think is awesome mixed with genuine passion that brings it to life. Several elements in the enemy and world design are also inspired or drawn straight from the original series of games. When you get tired of the base on Mars, the game will hook you back in with a trip to Hell, where it presents a vision of agony and brutality that could live up to the works of Dante Alighieri. No two levels look or play alike, and I was constantly refreshed with what I was seeing, despite the hypothetical stagnation of only visiting two large locations.

A major selling point in this series is music, and this instalment well delivers a heart-pumping metal track to fit its themes. You may hear a few songs that you recognize from the original game, and beyond its BGM is a satisfactory mix-up of scary noises, good voice-acting, and atmospheric placement. As far as the story goes, I quite enjoyed it, and it exceeded my expectations. You are the "Doom Slayer," a human marine who was imprisoned in a sarcophagus in Hell itself--not because of any wrongdoing, but because the denizens of Hell feared you more than anything. The intro tells it well, "They are rage; brutal, without mercy. But you, you will be worse. Rip and tear, until it is done." I could wish for nothing cooler than that, and playing the Doomguy is so fulfilling from the first five seconds right to the very end. You are the predator, and you are tasked with destroying every demon and ending the invasion on Mars. Thanks to the Doomguy's character, he is fierce, unflinching, and doesn't care at all for the narrative or well-being of the science facilities, making a better protagonist without a single line of dialogue that says so much more in his actions alone. I think you'll be sold on this game after playing it for less than two minutes.

Don't miss out on Doom, it's one of the best first-person shooters I have experienced in years.\t\t\t\t\t
