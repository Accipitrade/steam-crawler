\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSince Steam decided to delete my installation after the last update, and I'm not going to spend a week downloading 60 gigabytes all over again, I figured I might as well review the damn thing.

DOOM is the best game in the series since DOOM 64. And, unlike Doom 3, which was mostly a reboot, DOOM feels like an actual sequel, set hundreds of thousands of years in the future, when the UAC have succeeded in colonizing Mars, going as far as tapping into hell as a power supply. The doomguy has taken on a sort of mythical reverence (as it should be) and falls into the middle of the latest demon invasion as a kind of deus ex machina... or has he?

DOOM is more faithful to the older games in its approach to action and map design; gone is the flashlight, and back is the labrynthine levels and frequent key hunting. All the old monsters are back with some new variants (the Arch Vile can teleport now, for instance) and some welcome additions. The same goes for the weapons, which, in keeping with the times, are completely customizable and pack a meaty punch. Another nice touch is the ability to "stumble" enemies and perform contextual executions, all of which look fantastic and reward you with extra health and ammo. Running around a room tearing guys apart as fast as they can teleport in never gets old.

DOOM is one of those special games that I found very underwhelming when I first started playing it, but like a big-boy gamer I adjusted my standards instead of ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ about it on the forums. It gradually grew on me and somewhere around the halfway point in the campaign something clicked and I realized I was having a blast. While always grim and foreboding, the satanic imagery is placed firmly in the background, looking over your shoulder and breathing down your neck like an old friend, never overplaying its hand like it did in Doom 3. In fact, the game becomes less "hellish" and more flat-out Science Fiction as the campaign progresses (and it's a long campaign), culminating in an intriguing and unconventional showdown with UAC's suicidal computer system, which I actually really appreciated.

Lastly, DOOM is rife with unlockable text logs, difficult challenges and special abilities. The game is jam-packed with secret areas and homages to the franchise, up to and including some of the original levels. A lot of love clearly went into this one.

It's also worth noting that I found the use of a diabolical robot and a severely crippled woman, although completely one-dimensional, to be a very progressive choice for the main villains. (Yes, I am an SJW, have been my whole life, and wear the label with pride.)

Shame the whole thing runs like ass.

There is a multiplayer mode. As in, it's there. It involves running around trying to go more than five seconds without getting fragged, and occasionally fragging someone else, as it is with most online FPS. It's alright, I guess.

Is DOOM worth your time and money? If you like first-person shooters, absolutely. If you don't... absolutely. Just make sure you can run it. Seriously, I haven't had a game run this poorly since Splinter Cell Conviction.

8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I was only 4 years old, my family was poor. We lived in a rental home, the only table and chairs we had to eat at was a childrens play set, my sisters and I all had to share the same room, we had no cable, no tv - we were broke as hell and after Gamestop bought out the game company my father worked at, we thought it would only get worse. But luckily, he got hired as a manager someplace and we could finally afford to live in a house that wasn't so cramped and trash - it was only a trailer home, but it was still an upgrade.. While we were opening the boxes and getting stuff out, I got to a heavy box marked "Games" and inside I found the copy of Ultimate DOOM. I had no idea what a video game was and when I asked my mom about it, she got all happy and plugged everything up for me, and when she did, she showed me what became my most favorite game that's ever existed.

Now, so many years later, a new DOOM has been made and it amazes me they stayed true to what DOOM is. I was afraid it was going to be "Call of Duty" styled but it isn't, it's all DOOM, from the RIP AND TEARing gameplay to the music to the level design to the story to the lore to just everything, everything about this game stays true to what DOOM is supposed to be (unlike DOOM 3) and it's the most badass game I've played in years. From the very first lines of dialogue in this game, to the brutally challenging levels of demon slaying in hell, to the final battle, this game was perfect all the way through and I couldn't have asked for a better way to bring back DOOM. Seriously, thank you id Software for bringing my childhood up to such an amazing experience. If this game doesn't win Game of the Year 2016 I'll be completely shocked.

Update: The multiplayer is sadly dead, but the new Arcade mode makes the single player experience even better than it already was, making you compete with players around the world for the best time completed across all the levels. The faster you RIP AND TEAR through the demon horde that higher your score is, and the more you get hit the more you lose a chance of getting the score you're aiming for, making it slightly more challenging that the original story. Basically if you want to play through the levels all over again without the story the Arcade mode is perfect for you.\t\t\t\t\t
