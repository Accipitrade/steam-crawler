\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are looking for:
a. A mission based game, with story elements?
b. A game that's fast paced, with brutally unforgiving gameplay? (On higher difficultys, easy is quite forgiving.)
c. A title with a plethora of secrets to keep you feeling rewarded around every corner?

This game is for you! I went into it being told that unless you play at the highest possible difficulty you weren't getting the full effect, but I am a casual gamer and couldn't quite meet the chops. I went back on the games medium setting and I now definitely feel like a bad-ass demon killing machine. (Though if mortality is your thing, it would be recommended you go as high as possible.)

The Pros:
=================================================================
Stunning graphics which while wearing headphones really make you feel like you are one with the environment.

Amazing sound fx and a killer soundtrack to keep you on your toes and pushing through.

Despite all of this, I am not playing on a ridiculous rig or anything and get stable fps.

Don't let the first couple missions fool you, there are so many secrets in this game it's almost silly.

The main character has some silent, quirky sass to him which makes the cutscenes a nice fun break from the brutality.

The option exists in game to monitor pretty much any part of your graphics unit without 3rd party tools.

I haven't tried it yet (still playing the campaign) but with their SnapMap mode you can make and publish, or play custom missions to keep this game feeling worth your money even after you have found everything in the base content.

Spoiler: I enjoyed not knowing this until I got in-game but I guess it's not really a spoiler. The game sports a full 3d map making the search for unexplored areas not so dull.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Cons:
=================================================================
If you are a casual gamer who enjoys shooters this game may be too fast paced.

It's just a bit of a nit-pick as it hasn't slowed me down yet, but the ledge grabbing doesn't always work and may lead you to believe an area is not traversable. Always pay attention to the map and keep trying if the area seems noteworthy.

The game has a PVP arena mode, however in my experience it has really put me off. I'm not saying everyone will hate it, but I wouldn't pick this up solely due to it being a feature. Though demons are involved which make things quite unique in terms of gameplay.

Gameplay con spoiler: I am a completionist so it doesn't bother me, but the only way to upgrade your equipment is by finding secrets. They aren't hard to find at all, but I know there are some out there who just want to run and gun through their missions. In this game it will most likely leave you underpowered.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

I have yet to finish the campaign so I may update this review as I go, but as it stands right now this game is probably one of the best single player shooters I've played to date, and I would highly recommend anyone who is in the market for a good one grab this game. As well, as opposed to a lot of single player fps the gameplay in this title makes it quite the nice tool for sharpening and honing your fps aiming skills.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a bit hard for me to give this a downvote, because the campaign is great and snapmap is fun but primarily because the multiplayer is borked beyond belief mainly because there simply isn't an anti-cheat system, i mean it doesn't even use VAC for crying out loud! The only way to enjoy the multiplayer completely is to assemble a group of trusted players who won't cheat. This is so typical of Bethesda games, half measures! Aside from the cheaters the wannabe-rpg "player progression" is just downright annoying because it keeps weapons and equipment out of reach for a long period of time if you're a casual player. Which means you'll be getting rekt by Kinetic Mines for a good while until you end up being able to place your own, not the end of the world but pretty freakin' annoying, took me 2-3 days of playtime to unlock most things at lvl 41-42. I also don't want to forget to mention it's typical idtech netcode, meaning no matter how fast your connection is it'll suck in-game you'll experience frustrating results where lightning guns seem to bend around corners, they aren't technically, but what is represented on screen may not be represented on the server, so you see slight rebound but at least they cleverly found a way to smooth it out as you play heh.

Now, I will say, if you can get this game on sale and you don't plan on playing too much Multiplayer (snapmap the exception) then go for it, the campaign is awesome, the gameplay is awesome, snapmap is fun to fool around with, graphics are beautiful, the game runs very smoothly but the failure of multiplayer has been very disappointing, between this DOOM and QChampions bethesimax can't seem to get it together.

Take from this what you will but I don't see myself changing my recommendation until I see an anti-cheat implemented into the Multiplayer.\t\t\t\t\t
