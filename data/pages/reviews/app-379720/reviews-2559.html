Action is broken up with worthless sections where a character talks at you while at most you can look around or quicker moments where you faff with some consoles and crap like that. Level design gives the illusion of being labrynthian when in fact it's simpy arenas connected by linear hallways for the most part. Had a moment where backtracking down a secondary hallway triggered a checkpoint for the arena I had just completed which reset the open doors, but didn't respawn the enemies. This forced me to restart the whole level.

The multiplayer is just a gimped version of Quake with Call of Duty metagame ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ tacked on and should be avoided. DLC is nothing worth mentioning as it's all MP maps and some gimmicky weapons for said MP.

Overall pick it up on sale and you might enjoy the campaign inbetween the unskippable cinematic annoyances.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finally upgraded my computer and monitor (144HZ) after 6 years to play this. It was worth it!
This quick review is for single player campaign only.
Great game on so many levels.
The movement and animation is very fluid.
Audio :)
Visual quality is awesome.
Mounting mechanics are awesome and really change the dynamic of gameplay.
Glory kills turn enemies into slot machines that payout.
Chainsawing a Mancubus really pays out!
Hell knights and Barons are fast. Pretty much steroid versions of previous games :O
Revenant is the crazy guy of the bunch.
Dr. Hayden's voice is awesome!
VEGA sounds like C3PO's brother (kinda makes you feel safe in a world filled with demons)

The exploration and challenges have a purpose so seek them out.
Doomguy doll discoveries help break up the fast pace.

Have to admit that watching the videos especially for the hell levels and Lazarus labs I was thinking that it looks like too much work to go through all that, but when you get into the battle it really is fun!
If you see a Beserker powerup..... yep....

You really have to play the game to appreciate how much a step up from other games this is.
You truly feel like a badass when you play it.....

Looking forward to the next id game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMust play for all old school FPS players!!!

If you grew up playing Wolfenstein, Doom, Quake, Duke Nukem, etc., then you’re going to love this game. This is simple, fun, fast Gameplay.

Now don’t get me wrong, it does have its challenges but I also think It has a great balance of difficulty being not too hard and not too easy. I played it on Hurt Me Plenty and I had a great time.

Very well designed campaign. The game knows when to throw enemies at you and when to give you a break. It also knows when to make you feel invincible and when to make you fight for your life. They designed every inch of this game with the player in mind.

The enemies are perfect, very well designed and fun to kill.

Cons:

Or things I would’ve liked to have seen: (This is just mostly my personal subjective view)

- I would have liked to have seen more enemies from the original games. I know they have a lot but I would have liked to have seen more.

- Not a big fan of some of the guns. I’m not sure what Halo/Destiny guns are doing in a Doom game. I just really want to see that old school handgun again.

- I want more boss fights.

- I would have liked to have seen more melee killing animations. It would have been nice if you can attack a certain body part while melee attacking and get different animations that way.

- Multiplayer sucks, so I’ve heard...This isn’t really a problem for me since Doom has never been known for it’s multiplayer. I bought the game for it’s campaign. The same reason I bought all the other Dooms. It doesn’t need online play in my opionion.

Other then that the game is an FPS masterpiece and definitely belongs in your Doom collection.

Oh, and make sure to get some good speakers because the sounds effects and music is amazing. The music cues in at the most perfect times, as well as the sound.

Now go buy the game and kill some demons!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed the campaign and loved each and every bit of it. It does somewhat good balance between exploration and kill all living things, even though I am not much of an explorer type but still loved the nostalgic classic doom maps and cute collectables thrown into the game.

Loved the way they threw in the story of the game away from action. Like face it, this is doom, you do not want anything to interrupt your killing spree.....though progression bound to exploration kinda did that but you can avoid exploring completely if you don't care. They also threw in some challenges in each level which made things fresh everytime. You get the whole picture and lores by reading through the interestingly written codex and artifacts which you gather throughout the game. You can just keep reading the codex for hours only

The gunplay is super fun! No spirint button, no reload button, excellent mod system and the shooting makes you feel like a complete badass.

LOVED THE GLORY KILLS! Ripping apart demons one by one makes you feel so damn powerful like nothing in the world can stop you....and suddenly you die to an Imp lol

I also liked how they made the rune system in this game, some of them synergizes together very well

This is a definite buy from me for single player. Multiplayer seems like "meh" though the single player campaign makes up for it thorough and thorough.

Definitely gets my recommendation\t\t\t\t\t
