\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's really fun, though maybe not as incredible as the universal praise would make you think.

The weapons and all their upgrades are great, the enemies are well-designed with one exception, the environments are cool, the music is excellent. On the other hand, the game has a major emphasis on secrets, collectables, mini level challenges, etc. and it's all kind of bad.

The biggest change from the old games, in a way, is the tone.
Classic DOOM had a horror vibe to it; you were fighting in desperation against endless terrors. "Doomguy" was seriously just some guy. He knew how to shoot a rocket launcher, but this was in no way the situation he was trained for.
In this game, you're not Doomguy, you're "The Doom Slayer," a sort of demigod of vengeance, a being whose capacity for violence is absolute. At least, that's what the story tries to tell you; in terms of the actual game experience, there's no sense that you're divine or indestructible. You have to dodge fireballs like anybody.
I guess I am in the minority, but I find this change to be a bit stupid. It's like you took the "tongue in cheek macho man" protagonist, like Duke Nukem or Serious Sam, and made it into a religious figure taken seriously by the narrative.

Anyway, I don't mean to dwell on the negatives. I liked this game pretty well and I'm looking forward to trying Eternal next.\t\t\t\t\t
