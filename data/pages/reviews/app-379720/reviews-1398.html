\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far, the best first person shooter i have ever played. The game takes heavy inspiration from the originals, and has put a lot of effort into character and enemy design. Seeing all the classic DOOM enemy's in such high-quality is breath taking. A huge amount went into the design. Every stage is desktop wallpaper, and every weapon, armour, and creature is exquisite.

The gameplay is unique in comparison to other shooters, where rather than search for cover or high ground, the player is expected to move around the stage quickly and often, whilst using a variety of weapons including several guns, explosives, and a chainsaw. a key feature to the games combat are glory kills; melee attacks performed on weak enemy's which kills them with a unique animation. Another point is that the game is full of animations. Every enemy has a dozen well-made animations for multiple scenarios.

The game is full of collectables of several different fun secrets. In comparison to other triple A games, it does not have that much story, but is packed with lore about characters, places, organisations, etc. This includes files documenting information about characters, weapons, and monsters. The best part about the collectables is the ability to collect new stages to play from the original DOOM game, using the same textures and designs, which is really fitting and nostalgic.

One thing to be aware of is how "tense" the gameplay is. The first time i played DOOM, i grew incredible nervous, tense, and on the edge, due the music, sound effects, fast paced gameplay and of course, gore. Whilst i quickly became used to it, I can say it definitely increased my heartrate. There are also very rare occasions that have quite tedious platforming, but its infrequent and is a small complaint that did not bring the experience down greatly.
