\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAUGUST 2017 EDIT
---------------------------
Prior to this iteration of review, I had given the game a negative review based upon it's lackluster gameplay, coming out in a time wherein CoD was "King" of FPS and how it detracted from what DOOM was. This was after my first 30 minutes of playthrough, having detracted a shoddy experience from what I expected; Played one, you've played them all.

However, bought the game again out of sheer curiousity and to show love to the grand spectacle of FPS that is the DOOM series, as well as Bethesda for coming with a game that isn't a special edition of a certain game, almost like getting a bad breakup really.

ANYWAY, changing my review to a positive considering;

- Gameplay, while inheritently repetetive, is fun when blasting the soundtrack, ripping apart demon skulls and losing a shotgun barrel up the rear end of a Hell Knight.
- ALL DLC, FOR FREE!? Take a hint, EA!
- Soundtrack, for it's own merits.
- Somewhat linear level design also had me rather confused at times.
- Kickass backstory for Doom Guy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I hate this game. I just can't get into it for two reasons. 1) I'm a sucker for a good story, and Doom doesn;t have one. 2) While I played the Doom games when I was a kid, I don't have this huge emotional attachment to the franchise.

The game looks great, plays well, and feels like an old-school shooter. And I imagine that if you LOVED the old Doom games, you'll probably like this game. If you just want to run and gun and kill demons, you'll like this game. But if you want more than just mindless shooting with no real purpose behind any of your actions, pass on this.

To me, with no serious nostalgia for the old Doom games, this feels like a soulless, boring shooter hiding behind a prominent name. If it wasn't called "Doom", this game would have been passed over for far better games by most people who bought it.

That's a real shame, since Bethesda did, in my opinion, an awesome job with Wolfenstein. I played that on PS4 and loved every minute of it. Doom, well, I hated it. I gave it up and won't be finishing the game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom/Doom2 was among the first FPS games I ever played, and I played it a LOT. I later moved on to the Unreal Tournament franchise, and also did a lot of Quake 3/Quake Live.

This game revives the unforgettable feeling of Doom/Doom2 and puts it in the Unreal Tournament 3/Quake Live style of break neck high paced speed. Some elements also reminds me a bit of Halo, but any similarities between the main character and Master Chief is plagiarism on their end. Don't get me wrong, the main character in DOOM would snap Master Chiefs neck without breaking a sweat. He is absolutely brutal.

The single player campaign alone is worth your money, but if you are into building maps or playing multiplayer this game is tons of fun. Nothing beats bringing out the classic double barrelled shotgun and blasting some demon kidneys... Oh wait, the Gauss rifle is really nice though...

A tip of advice is you might want to skip the DLC if you are not all about the multiplayer prospects of DOOM, since DLC is 100% multiplayer stuff so far.\t\t\t\t\t
