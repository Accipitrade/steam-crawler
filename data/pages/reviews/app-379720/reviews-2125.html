\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is an FPS classic, old school, brutal and unrelenting.

I wasn’t interested much for the story, I was overwhelmed with the fight, moving from an arena to an arena fighting an exaggerated amount of monsters which the game is all about, While it was all overwhelming, I had my friend with his awesome speaker that this game need to help each other and to fight through the end, much more enjoyable than bunching the keyboard in the mouth.

According to my friend who played the original Doom, they kept the same flavor in it, the monsters are mostly recycled and remade from the original one, what the original lacked though is the awesome heavenly music this one has which is made from Death Metal, Goth Metal and Hard Rock, it was so sweet I could have been a Metal dude my whole life if Metal was always this cool.
There is so much Doom does right, the adrenaline, the gameplay, the graphics, and the general quality in every corner, glitch and stutter free from start to end, also the feedback of the weapons I feel is of a better quality than usual.
Don’t be fooled, the game is not perfect though, for a start it leans toward a shorter side, 10 hours to beat the game is not fully satisfying especially it’s repetitive in its nature, I expected more.
The maps are confusing to read, the locations are so twisted you wouldn’t know where the heck are you (I mostly blame my friend for that as he’s not usually a directions champion which this game really need), also the secrets hidden in each level are not balanced, some time you would find a secret in your face without searching, and sometimes you swear to god this game is making you feel like an idiot on purpose.

All in all, nothing effected my experience over all, this game proved my manhood again, veins on my testicles are way thicker now, after beating this game I give it a “Pinky” 9 out of 10.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're here you probably want this game. It's badass, it's fun, it can definitely be challenging but in a way that rewards you for your success and doesn't necessarily slap you across the face for failure.
The graphics are intense, so you might get some jitters while playing. Make sure your machine has got the min specs and maybe turn down the graphics if problems persist.
The SnapMap feature is great, because there's an endless supply of demon-killing fun to be had, either making your own maps and playing/submitting them, or playing others' variably rated maps. Some are cool and well made, others... sometimes people just want the Snap Points. Not a big deal. It's a little annoying to swap between Campaign and SnapMap, but not enough to give a negative review by any means.
Haven't personally played multiplayer, so I can't comment on that.

TL;DR - You're here deciding whether to drop the money and play Doom, that's all. You know it's good.\t\t\t\t\t
