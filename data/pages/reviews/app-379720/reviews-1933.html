\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. I don't know how many hours I've spent on this, more on that later. Where to start? Well, you can rip your enemies to shreds with your bare hands. That's a good start... The ripping and tearing is accompanied with music that makes your blood boil and some tracks should top any "try not to headbang"-list. Exactly what else do you need to know?

The gameplay is rather smooth and mostly intuitive, though some maps are a bit maze like. I believe the whole idea here was to create a modern game that hasn't forgotten the strengths of the past and at the same time doesn't neglect the improvements that have been made over the years. I'd say they succeeded well enough. There's a multitude of powerups, weapons and mods that you'll need to master and there's no reloading, just spray and pray! I keep getting back to the Arcade mode and sometimes even the campaing, which has enough replayability for you to complete all difficulty levels and try your worthyness with the hardcore mode Ultra Nightmare. I recommend starting at the highest difficulty possible from the start, because even though I like the campaign, it's a bit short. There's also lots of collectibles scattered throughout the game, from pieces of lore to "secrets", and references to other pop culture etc.

Okay, I'm going to adress the elephant in the room. 1333 hours. There's something about this game. You'll come across something called "Argent energy"... I think that energy seeps into my computer as soon as I run DOOM because that's litterally the only thing that keeps my PC from having a BSOD. I know, sounds weird, but I've run all kinds of tests to diagnose my poor PC with no luck so far. Only thing I can think of is to rebuild it from scratch, and that costs money. HOWEVER - even with an otherwise crippled and weathered PC I can run DOOM smoothly at max settings, which means you probably can too! And it's a pretty damn good looking game!

So what do I dislike with DOOM?

Rather short campaign
Demons might have possessed my PC

That's it I think...

This game provides great escapism.
Tired of other trafficants in your daily commute? Rip and tear.
Tired of your boss and/or co-workers? Rip and tear.
You have an exam soon? Rip and tear.
Politics? Rip and tear.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 is like your favorite Mom&Pop Restaurant: you know you ain't going for something revolutionary, but you're going for a strong classic that never dissapoints.

The controls are very responsives and well chosen. Camera never is an issue. The run n gun aspect with the glory favors a very aggressive gameplay and a rotation of your arsenal, with constant movement.

Over time, you unlock different weapons using similar bullets. It is nice over time to get a feeling of progression. Unfortunately, some weapon feels obsolete after some time, like the regular shotgun or the gauss automatic mode. I recommend unlocking the "reduced damage on self" buff for your armor as sometimes you can get trigger happy and a demon pops in your face, resulting in a self kill with the rocket launcher.

The game sometimes offers you puzzles and platforming to change the dynamics. they aren't really fun, but it changes the pace a little and aren't very long. There is also bosses, but i haven't found them very hard compared to some very steamy arenas in endgame. This was done in about 7-8 hours in ultraviolence with some moderate search for secrets, maybe 2-3 hours less if you're a regular shooter fan. This is the last shooter game i finished since ps3 era.

replayability is surprisingly good for a shooter. it wouldn't be uncommon for someone to get back on a mission on go blow of some demon's head for fun.

Overall, a game that won't try to be something else, but will sure will hold his ground in what it knows best in it's genre.\t\t\t\t\t
