\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't buy this EVER even on Sale. Whoever told you, whenever from and if you heard in any way that this is a masterpiece and the best modern first person shooter... You have been lied to. I hope never seeing anything so horrible, infuriating and boring as I did playing this.

Not a single weapon is satisfying to use, they sound extremely wimpy, and all enemies are just bullet sponges.
I only found 2 weapon combos and both of them include Gauss Cannon as main DPS weapon.
1)Ammo pool you can carry with you is pathetically low; Gauss Cannon has lots of damage and ammo and IS reliable and pin-point accurate.
2)Most of the low tier enemies can survive shotgun blast to the face. I can't think up a reason for that other than compensating for not having many health pickups in the entire game, so player can Glory Kill low-hp Enemy as frequently as possible to get health from it, more on that later.

There's also Weapon Module-Upgrade system which is a good idea in theory, my gripe with it however that it's kind of tedious to switch a module mid-fight, and it mostly works as "restricting player from doing stuff until it doesn't" instead of providing new interesting mechanics to play around with.
But the fact that you HAVE to explore 100% to get upgrade points to upgrade your guns and your health and movement and so on felt really off the entire playthrough.

In my eyes it doesn't require you to think and adapt, only shoot, strafe and "Press F to Finish Off a Demon".
---Overreliance on glory kills, those get repetetive extremely fast.---

It's kickass at first when it actually shows how badass the setting the intensity and gore can be but then it becomes extremely boring.

It's very slow paced, you just don't move fast enough, only with "Haste" power-up movement becomes somewhat fun but most of the time it's really slow, as well as having larger chunk of the game centered around FPS platforming.

After first few badass cutscenes - others are so extremely out of place and take themselves so seriously... i'm kind of speechless. They just put you in unskippable cutscene where you can't do anything, it might've been slightly more forgivable if characters were any good, but I completely forgot names of antagonists.
The only thing I remembered about them:
First was Bastion from Overwatch and second was a woman, probably.

There are quite a few audio logs, basically NPC talking to you through them - frankly, I couldn't care less about any of this stuff.

Every time you kill a new demon you get Text log, and it constantly jumps in your face "PRESS TAB TO READ" yeah...I totally playing this game to read stuff.
And I wouldn't nitpick it if it wasn't for the fact HOW FREQUENT it pops up, in the beginning of the game it pops up in about once every 2 minutes.

To be honest the game is terribly easy, let me show what I mean by that.

Most of the game composed of arenas. Area/arena gets blocked off, enemies start spawning and you won't be able to proceed until every single thing is dead.
(By the way that's how you do Serious Sam in the wrong way)
On highest difficulties you mostly only die when the game spawns the enemy right behind you or throws some really high-tier enemy just around the corner so you don't have any time to react at all, or which is the most likely cause of death - you blow yourself up.
Because that's what happens when CQC arena is paired with Grenade Launcher and bullet sponge enemies.

(Subjective: Soundtrack is more of dubstep than metal doom - which really really blows it sounds awful.)
But people seems to like it, so I don't deny it's just my personal preference of having Metal in Doom instead of industrial-rock or whatever it's called.

There's a vibe of power fantasy but then it fails to do that, you not ripping and tearing, what you doing instead is always watching your back because enemy can spawn right behind you, always trying not to hit yourself with explosives because health packs are so rare you can't afford hitting yourself.

If player has even basic knowledge or experience in FPS it's basic strafe formula, but in Doom you are forced to Glory kill after Glory kill because that's the actual only reliable way to get your health back - always glory kill. Otherwise you are stuck with barely any med kits and ammo scattered around.

So basically what im saying: Hammering F button most of a time with Dubstep playing in the background.
Doom game...96% positive - I applaud you, you stupid idiots.

Whole point is, and I know it's a long rant - everything was hyped around gore and gameplay and badassery. But this much quantity of stopping and pressing F prompts belongs in Call of Duty:ShootA Guy Generic Warfare, and from what I've before purchasing - not in a DOOM title.

Multiplayer - never played it. Seeing how only 1.000 people are playing on average tells me that it's pretty much dead. And most likely because of how boring and simplistic combat is.
I mean, you shoot. And jump. And only 2 weapons allowed.

Last but not least - level design, just look at the screenshots, everything is orange. It's orange on orange, and lighting doesn't look that great either, everything is bright as hell. *bu dum tss* Bunch of platform puzzles, even vents like it's Half-Life game or something, it's like level designers were confused or rushed to do everything at once.

In conclusion: This game is awful. It's boring. Sounds terrible. Looks decent enough. It's nothing more than a Console shooter masking its shallowness behind legendary Doom title. And that is the actual reason it's called DOOM instead of Doom4 is pure marketing to appeal to people who would feel good about themselves because they played DOOM.

Game design and repetitiveness make it seem like it was made for people who come after long day's work to kill some stuff for 15 minutes, turn the game off and return to it later.
Aka Skyrim without open world / Fallout 4 without radiant quests. Which doesn't work in FPS linear shooter whatsoever.

By the way I played classic Doom and Doom II about 2 month after playing Doom16 for the first time in my life, expecting it also being overhyped. But in the end the Classic Dooms forever going to my Favorite Games list, it was bloody amazing and gorgeous and I loved it (except for the second half of Doom II, those City levels can go delete themselves)

Doom2016 I will forever remember You as a waste of perfectly good 20$ earned with blood and sweat to get bored and angry instead of at least getting fresh pizza.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM (2016) is the first mainline DOOM game since DOOM 3, so quality-wise, the only way to go is up. Luckily, DOOM (2016) goes above and beyond all expectations; it takes the best parts of the first two DOOM games and adds modern-day polish and sheer resourcesas well as plenty of love and careto create something truly magical.

The basic gameplay is, on a fundamental level, the same as the first two gamesyou run around in first person and shoot demons with guns and look for keys to progress (truly, the essence of Old-School FPS). It feels great, and a lot of that is because of the movement. It's fast, fluid, and really intuitive, in such a way that everything from getting from place to place to avoiding rapid-fire attacks to the occasional platforming section just feels right. The shooting is also excellent; it's punchy and cathartic and varied and exceptionally, fantastically gory. Besides just shooting demons with your ever-increasing arsenal, you can finish them off after damaging them enough with Glory Kills, which are little takedown animations where you beat the out of the target in increasingly over-the-top ways. They're a lot of fun, and really help to keep the game's pace fluctuating just enough that it gets neither overwhelming nor boring.

As I said, there's been a fair amount of modernity injected into the formula, the most obvious example being the upgrade systems. There are four in total, which can take a little time to wrap your head around; luckily, the game introduces them slowly and keeps them pretty intuitive. The game executes upgrades well, in that the player progression isn't so much " to lethal" as "lethal to more lethal." You get more guns, different alt-fires for those guns, improved movement options, and increased health, armor, and ammo capacity, but they always feel like they're making the game even more fun, not just basically enjoyable.

There are some other changes and updates, too. Unlike the first two DOOMs, DOOM (2016) isn't really constrained by its engine, so levels are allowed to be really vertical. This leads to some fun map designs, and a lot of the aforementioned platforming. All the old DOOM weapons and enemies are back, which is great, but there are also a fair few new additions (my favorite being the Heavy Assault Rifle, with its mini-missile attachment that eventually supplanted the Plasma Rifle as my go-to rapid-fire DPS weapon). There are also now more powerups than just Berserk (although Berserk does return, and it is glorious). Armor, instead of mitigating damage taken, now absorbs all damage entirely until depleted, like...Turok 1, I guess. The Chainsaw now requires its own special ammo, but it's an instakill that makes the enemy shower other kinds of ammo, giving it a nice niche as your desperation tool when everything's going to . More importantly, enemies now regularly drop small amounts of health and ammo when they die, which is like grease that keeps the game's momentum going.

The levels are also a great staging ground for all this fantastic gameplay. As I said, they're really vertical, but they're also vast and interesting and filled to bursting with incredibly-difficult-to-find secrets. (Like in DOOM, finding the right secrets early on can amusingly completely break the weapon progression.) They're also a bit Dark Souls-y in that peeking down one small side path can lead to exploring an entire other chunk of level. Importantly, you can go back and replay them whenever you want with all the stuff you've gotten since then, which is a fantastic addition.

Even DOOM's story is surprisingly solid. It makes an effort to tie together the entire series' canon up to this point, which is a weird choice but one I fully support. It's a bit vague, but as far as I can tell, DOOMs 1, 2, and 64 (or if not that whole game, at least the ending) happened, then the DOOM Marine spent a long-ass time in Hell, during which point he got a new set of armor from some treacherous demon before eventually getting tricked into getting locked away in a sarcophagus. He stayed there for a while, but then eventually some people from a parallel universe where the UAC was getting Hell energy instead of studying teleportation found him and freed him when demons started invading them, beginning the events of this game. DOOM 3 also may have been a parallel universe, but if it was nobody cares about it. This is all conveyed, along with tons of information about enemies and weapons, through a lot of really quite fun flavor text that you can find scattered around the levels.

The characterization of the DOOM Marine, conveyed entirely through animations seen from first person, is really superb; all he wants to do is kill demons, just like you, the player. You and he are completely in sync, and the way you both interact with the world around him is both funny and gratifying.

Unsurprisingly, the game looks great, both graphically and aesthetically.

Most impressively, the game manages to take a few of DOOM 3's ideas (and a couple of weapon/enemy appearances) and make them goodfor example, Imps are really agile and jumpy like in DOOM 3, but here it's fun instead of frustrating because you're fast enough to promptly react to them. It makes a world of difference, and really drives home just how good DOOM 3 could've been with just a couple tweaks.

I do have a handful of quibbles. There's no manual save, which is weird considering the game's lineage, and the levels can get pretty labyrinthine; this isn't much of an issue because they're quite intuitive and if you keep going forward you'll eventually get where you need to go, but it does make the map next to useless and hunting secrets a bit of a chore. The game also isn't great at signaling when you've taken damage (honestly, I feel like developers should just make that more obvious than they think they have to). It additionally feels more scripted in that doors more often seem to open based on whether you've killed all the enemies in an area, not whether you have a key or have pressed a switch. Finally, Cacodemons bleed red instead of blue, which is just wrong.

Overall, though, DOOM (2016) is amazing. The movement's great, the shooting's great, the upgrades are great, the levels are great, and it's so full of variety and cleverness and clear love for the original games. Just assume that everything I didn't mention is also extremely well-done; it's just an incredible piece of work all around, and one that you should absolutely play.

P.S. I didn't play the multiplayer or use the level editor; I heard that the campaign is the main reason to buy the game, so I just stuck with that.\t\t\t\t\t
