\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBottom line - D00M is a fusion of the old Doom games and Doom 3 game styles, plus a great gameplay improvement.

You have this horror film scenario style from Doom 3 fused with the butchery of the old games (when lots of mobs just appear all around and you can't catch a break until the last one of them is dead).

There is an additional element of choice when upgrading your gear, without really swaying from the well defined FPS style. Basically, seasoning the dish to your liking, yet the dish stays the same.

The maps/levels are very well designed with much to explore - and as a recommendation: take your time and explore the map!
In exchange for the time invested in exploring, you'll be rewarded with tokens to imrove you gear (and you're going save some time by not dying later in game).

The game mechanics are great and invite you to think of how you'll be going through different parts of the map. Along your journey you should manage your ammo wisely and invest the gear improvement points likewise.

I think that these are the main things I can and should say without spoiling much. After all, being surprised by stuff is part of the experience.

HURT 'EM PLENTY!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t+ Fast, fluid gameplay succesfully capturing most of the old school DooM feel
+ I love the fragmented metal-industrial-glitchy soundtrack. It's tight, modern, dynamic and brutal. It's not as rememberable als Doom 1 or 2, but it gets me in the mood all the time.
+ You know what I despise about modern shooters? The fact that you can't backtrack from start to end of a level. None of this in Doom 4. You can fully explore back and forwards at your own damn leisure just like old times.
- [edit:]...at least, that's what I thought. Unfortunately there are some too many points where the level designers thought it *would* be a good idea not to let the player backtrack all the way to the start. A weak, infuriating and ununderstandable design choice. [/edit]
+ And there's a lot to find and explore on the levels. I would recommend disabling the compass and using the map to a bare minimum because that makes it a bit too easy to discover everything, but on the other hand.. well, suit yourself.
-+ There are some very hard to find secrets, if you're set on discovering all of the classic maps. It's fun to actually discover one yourself without looking it up on the internet, but it's not likely to happen with every one of them.
+ I actually like the weapon and armor upgrade system. It's redundant, but at the same time fun enough to keep yourself occupied with so why not. Same goes for the challenge levels.
+- Enemies come in waves instead of placed thoughtfully throughout the levels. However these waves themselves have been orchestrated pretty well and are fun to beat, it's not too bad at all (one has got to love Painkiller after all).
+- There's still something left of the chunky, too shiny too clean too round environments that made me insta-dislike Doom 3 so much back then. But many improvements have been made over it, it does look and feels good this time around.
+- Also I'm not the biggest fan of the lighting used in this game. Sometimes it's perfectly fine, sometimes it gets very oversaturated and foggy. It's very nice and thoughtful that you can actually change the rendering to gritty or cinematic, although I find myself switching between the modes depending on where I am instead of just using one setting the whole time. It's a bit of a mixed bag to my taste.
- Still too much exposition. Why the hell do we need a boring generic seen-it-before-too-many-times storyline? This is not Doom 3 (thank god)
- This is Doom 4. Not Doom, not Doom 2016, Doom 4. Seriously, name your game properly guys.
- Just ignore the multiplayer. It's not Doom, it will never be Doom. Snap Map seems interesting though, but I yet have to dive into that at this point.

All in all this game actually turned out to be great. It was really hard to believe anything good would come out of this game after that very mediocre multiplayer open beta, but you better believe it: Doom 4 is awesome. Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was hesitant to pre-purchase Doom since there were practically no reviews for the game and I have been burned so many times by AAA titles. After seeing the game released, I read nothing but great reviews so I chose to give it a shot...

It is Doom.

This title in the series has taken the graphics from today's generation of video games and applied it to the classic FPS game play of the old Doom. While the single player aspect of the game will seem a little short, it makes up the difference in replayability and secrets.

Unlike most of todays games, this one has a little story with some minor cut scenes sprinkled into the game play to keep the story moving along from loction to loction. The game play moves most of the games story forward and the plot is true to the classic Doom and goes something like this:

SPOILER ALERT:
A portal to hell is open and demons are pouring forth.
Kill them.

I know, with a plot that runs that deep it can be hard to follow...

Graphics 9/10 (Great, alomst perfect)
Sound 10/10 (Fantastic)
Game Play 10/10 (Sublime)
Story 9000/10 (This has the right amount of story for Doom)
Multiplayer 5/10 (Average. Still I enjoyed it, but nothing complared to TF2/Overwatch)
Snapmap 7/10 (I was not impressed, but it is still new so this could easily go up as more maps are created)

The only issue I have with the game is sometimes it will crash to desktop.
Additionally, I have seen lots of complaints about the ending... If you have ever beaten Doom/Doom 2/Doom 64, then you will be pleasantly supprised since the game will not end with a still shot and some text.

Overall: An enjoyable experiance. Worh the price of admission.
