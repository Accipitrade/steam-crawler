\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoomstein, now complete with dubstep metal! You know you're playing a modern FPS when the soundtrack goes BOOOuuuu.... while doing nonstop takedowns. You'll be doing a lot of takedowns certainly. Or glory kills, as they're called.

The catch is simple - you weaken the enemies enough with your weapons to make them stagger, which allows the player to swoop in, press a button and watch a few second long kill animation. Doom encourages for this takedown frenzy because it's a quick and necessary way to get health. The action is more of the Quake 3/Unreal Tournament variety than Doom, but has a charm and a good amount of challenge. Every weapon becomes useful and you'll be switching from one to another all the time to make sure you have enough ammo for more powerful weapons.

Visually stunningly beautiful, but static, just like The New Colossus, and I was running 120-144 fps through the game. Wasn't a fan of the boss fights though, because learning patterns to win isn't the most appealing way to defeat an enemy. I suppose that's a lesson of the 90s for those who weren't there. Play at least on Ultra-Violence and it'll be fun while it lasts.\t\t\t\t\t
