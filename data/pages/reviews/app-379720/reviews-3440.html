-Graphics are fantastic. Playing this on a ATI R9 380 (GTX 970ish equivalent) and it's looks great. Everything is on ultra and I never dropped below 45FPS. Hovered around 60-70 almost consistently.
-Controls are fluid. Only tweak I had to make was to mouse sensitivity. Default was too low.

Cons

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWelcome back "Doom"! First off.. the graphics is awesome, the speed and fluidity of the game is spot on. So if you have been waiting for a fast paced unforgiving shooter, this is it. I played this game during the beta as well of course, so I was a bit hesitant in the beginning, of how the final release would be, but I am happy that it I was proven wrong.

Now, this is a reboot of the franchise.. so there will be changes. I know some have irated about this is not immediate action, what is this? Why are they talking etc.. guys (and gals) this is a reboot.. a reboot.. got it? Once more then.. a reboot! So if you wanted the orginal, then by all means.. play the original. I also noticed that many missed the fact that the orginal doom actually is included in the game (or great parts of it anyway) Look around in the game for hidden levers. Pull them, and a door will open. go through and... ;) But I digress, the compaign does start slow, but it is for a reason. Instead of having tutorials, the initial levels themselves are tutorials. They also give some character development for you, and others around you. You get a back story that wasn't really there in the first Doom. You as a player act dismissive of the fact that a story gets explained to you. Going as far as throwing monitors away, and smashing them. Which is a nod, to exactly those who complain that this isn't immediate action. It is a funny meta joke, that even those whom are supposed to critique the game missed (most of them anyway) which is in itself even more funny. So it isn't like they didn't 'know' this reaction would come ;)

A small note: I've played all doom & quake games, Rage etc.. id games on the PC (sorry I am vehemently against playing this type of game on a console) so I could compare to them, but I won't.. because it is a reboot. Just in case someone still didn't get that part.

Regardless, the story is thin, but does set the setting quite well. And it isn't all that intrusive (except for a few places where you actually are forced to listen, which does suck a bit) But in all, those interupptions are small, and well placed. So I have little doubt most people will enjoy the campaign. I haven't played through the entire campaign yet, but I don't think I will experience anything that would make me not recommend this game. Which is awesome.

And again.. this review isn't finished. So I will wait for a while, as not to spoil things. ..

But as usual there are of course issues. And here they come (so far)

I really don't get the issue with the initial loading screen freeze. The first time you play the game, the Bethesda intro, and ID intro.. freezes to a standstill. Just wait and let it go through whatever process they are doing in the background (unlocking content perhaps?) because subsequent launches of the game will be normal. However, a first time user might wonder WTF is going on? Not good, that part is a terrible first impression. It is not the game, but still. People will react to it. They should fix it ASAP. Or at least actually inform that content is being unlocked, if that is what it is about.

The profile settings follows the user to the new computer. But wait, how is this a bad thing? Well, it is bad if that other computer is a laptop.. a less powerful one. Which you configure to play well on that. Now when you get back to your beast.. all is set for that lower end one. Seriously, didn't they think (or realize) that it would be a bad thing? Well, it is! I really hope they move it out of the tracking profile, because it frankly sucks.

The difficulty settings, the lower settings (including normal) are far too easy. I know I am a bit more advanced than the majority of players. But still, they really are far too easy. I would set the difficulty setting higher, if you want a challenge. Unless you only want to play for the story, in which case it is OK of course. But even then, only the lowest setting would be OK. But.. just so you know.

The upgrade drops.. points or whatever. Could we please just have a point based one? Please? Running around, trying to find places for suit tokens, robots to upgrade your gun etc.. isn't what I want to be doing in a running shooter. I want to play, not play Indiana jones for treasures. That is really one of the most (highly actually) annoying things in this game. Hidden levers for the old doom experience is one thing. That is actually cool. Finding tokens.. not so much. Not to mention that 'buying upgrades' in the field is really breaking it. -Oh sorry deaddies... upgrading my gun here (or whatever) play some poker guys.. I'm gonna be here a while. Jeez. Do that between levels, in a locked space or whatever. So it actually fits. Standing in the middle of a battlefield, shopping from a robot? WTF? Now, I realize it is not going away. I also realize a good number of people are OK with that, or even that they think it fits into the game. I don't, so there.

Note:
This review, isn't complete yet. If I find more breaking stuff I'll make a note of it here. And I haven't dived into the final multiplayer yet either. But that will come later. It won't change my recommendation.

Turn off that motion blur, DOF etc.. you don't really need it. It is also taxing on the GPU. If you want those extra FPS when playing on a less decent card. Lowering shadow, and AA settings are of course also good for that. I would try and keep the texturing, and anisotropic settings high, or as high as possible for a good experience ;)

Good luck doom soldier ;)

P.s and what is up with Valves gametime played counter? It is off by hours.. :/\t\t\t\t\t
