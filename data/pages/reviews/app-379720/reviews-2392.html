\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: This review is pretty much exclusively about the game's campaign. I have not experimented with the multiplayer/level builder too much yet.

First of all, I'm typically not the person who is interested in games or anything for that matter with extreme gore/demonic themes. But I must say, I really, really enjoyed DOOM. The flow of the game and how it progresses was perfect for me. Chapter based progression defeating waves upon waves of monsters in some of the most intense ways possible felt so fulfilling. The combat is also really fun. The weapon mechanics are really well put together, the SFX for the weapons are on point, and the glory kill system was especially satisfying. Oh, and the soundtrack is phenomenal (coming from someone who does not typically like metal either).

As you progress the game, you collect all types of weapons, encounter new zones, and discover new types of monsters. All weapons and your armor has several awesome upgrades and modules to be unlocked throughout the game. In addition to all that, every chapter has numerous hidden secrets to discover and challenges to complete, which adds even more challenge and spice to each part of the game.

With all that being said, there are VERY few things that I have to complain about this game, and they are pretty minor. For example, I thought in comparison to the two other bosses in the game, the third and final boss was pretty easy, short, and somewhat underwhelming. On normal difficulty, and with all my weapons and upgrades, the fight ended fairly quickly, and first try as well. But aside from that, I loved the experience that DOOM had to offer. And for anyone who likes singleplayer chapter based FPS games, this one is totally worth the buy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought, played, requested refund. If you like dated 90's era gameplay maybe you will like this game, I don't know. All I know is when I take up the mantle of the DOOMSLAYER I don't picture tissuepaper thin armor, the inability to take more than 3 hits, a pistol that politly suggests an enemy perhaps lose some health, and a ducking videogame-tastic shotgun: 20 whole rounds of ammo, and completely flacid after 15 feet. But that's OK right? They aren't going to stick you with two completely useless weapons and almost no ability to survive melee combat in large open areas for an hour+ are they? Oh yes, they are. Have fun! But, you say, when you get things low enough you can kill them in style for health! Sure. You can get that 5 health from them. Too bad being locked into the animation cost you 40 when the demon that spawned behind you hits you once in the back while the camera transitions.

If you like running backwards plinking things with a peashooter, grinding upgrade points to bring your equipment up to "at least it works now" levels... have at it. This was beyond not fun for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing open world multiplayer pvp/pve games and generally went the pve route. Since you can't always avoid the pitched battles and ravaging wildlife having a good gun hand is very beneficial. I found the singleplayer DOOM to be pretty good as a way to keep tuned into gunplay, tactics and using the environment to stay alive. Since you can replay as many times as needed it also allows you to practice difficult moves until you get them down. These moves can be migrated to the other games when similar situations arise. One other thing I noticed is that as I got used to being the target of a bunch of monsters with and without guns I was able to increase my level of calm which improved the outcomes quite a bit. Much less adrenaline jitters when engaging in combat.

I recommend the single player DOOM game.\t\t\t\t\t
