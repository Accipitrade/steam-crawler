\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA $20 metal album that comes with an awesome free game. Pretty tough to beat that.


But in all seriousness, I'm very glad I finally got the chance to play through this game. I originally bought this game close to its release, but never had the time or interest to really go ham on it. I loved every bit of the game on Normal difficulty, will definitely give harder difficulties a go. A first-person shooter where movement and awareness of your surroundings are key. Simple to learn, and once you master the weapon switch combos, you'll feel like an unstoppable force of nature -- just as it should be. Finding Secrets is a fun side activity, but you don't really feel punished by the game for not doing a 100% run.

A couple crashes here and there, a couple times when I happened to clip through the walls. Something I'd rather not have in the game, for sure, but not even remotely enough to stop the constant flow of dopamine from unleashing absolute carnage upon the beasts of Hell.

Right then, I guess it's time for Doom: Eternal.\t\t\t\t\t
