\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSumming up my game experience: 'I see an open room is ahead; I bet the doors close and you have to fight 3 different waves of monsters.' This is especially true for the last half of the game. The early stages are fun finding the secrets and the occasional new weapon. You start out weak and have to move through the maps slowly. Once you start unlocking better upgrades, it becomes "Run, double jump, shoot" until the room is cleared. It starts feeling monotonous.... done with Mars, so I have to go to hell? OK, Hell level completed, back to Mars I go.... and then I need to go back to hell. Then back to Mars, then hell, and finally back to Mars.

Not a bad game, but you will end up favoring only a few of the weapons making grinding out upgrade points through multiple campaign runs mostly unnecessary. After about halfway through, you will no longer find unique monsters, but instead they will have a mild variation in color and attacks. Half of the game feels fresh and exciting, and the last half felt strangely tedious. I played around 30 hours so it was worth the $15 I paid. I am glad I did not pay full price.\t\t\t\t\t

Typically with a lot of games the combat gets tedious or repetitive(cough cough Gears of War), but I didn't have that problem with DOOM. The storyline was easy to follow, the graphics were amazing, the guns were dope as hell, and I had an overall good time getting some anger out with glory kills. I can say with full confidence that DOOM is easily the goriest, most violent, most intense game I've played to date, and it was beautiful because of it. There's nothing better than running around murdering demons with powerful guns and a chainsaw. This game has taken the place of my #1 Favorite Game, above Halo 3: ODST AND Dead By Daylight, both of which I've played religiously.
Seriously, if you need a quality first person shooter, this game is perfect. Very satisfied with DOOM. Even if my dad says Quake was better than DOOM back in the day lol
