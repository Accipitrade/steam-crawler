\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat to say that hasn't already been said. This is a good game. It has good graphics, its fun to run around and fight demons. If you liked Doom 3, but wished it had larger environments and a faster pace, this is that game, but without a flaslight. Personally, the only complaint I have is that this game doesn't have a flashlight. All of the environments are sufficiently lit so that you don't need one, but I would like to have one to peer around the detailed environments.

Visually speaking the game is amazing. It does a great job of making you feel like you are in a large and complex world and the automap helps that world be navigable in a very gratifying way.

It also has a 'glory kill' system which reminds me of Brutal Doom. I encounterd one spot where a glory kill pushed me through the floor and I fell to my death. This only happened once. Overall, I am not sure the glory kill system is totally necesary. It is a little... gimicky... sometimes and doesn't really fit with the run and gun feel, but maybe that is just a matter of taste. Sometimes it feels like it interupts the flow of the game. I mean, it is totally optional, but the enemies light up and flash, and it kind of distracts from the cohesion of the game. Part of me wishes the HUD elements did not overlay on the monsters, but this would be my only real issue with the game.

There are times when the checkpoints do not save in an optimal way, but this has been rare.

The demons are challenging and satisfying to combat and have improved bot navigation and combat behavior from the variety present in Doom 3. Demons jump between discrete areas and close gaps quickly. Player movement is both rewarding and necesary. I was concerned, due to the loss of many of the original team, that this game would not be faithful to the original or would change too much to set itself apart from Doom 3, but it seems to have struck the perfect balance.

Demons drop health and ammo when they die (may be dependent on difficulty level) and this solves a similar problem that the Soul Cube did in Doom 3. In both cases this provides a way to give the player health outside of the normal health pack system and without resorting to regenerating health.

The music system shifts the background score to set the mood. If you are in an intense fight, the music is a mix of industrial and heavy metal/hard rock. If the fight is over, the music recedes to let the atmospheric sounds and ambience fill in the space.

This is a good game.\t\t\t\t\t
