\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is pretty dang good but there are some problems I have with it. First off the combat shotgun sucks compared to the original one, but I understand that they want you to use the other weapons. The pistol is garbage, it doesn't sound or feel threatening at all. It's missing the sort of dark atmosphere the original Doom had. The music is fine but I prefer the rip-offs of Metallica and Pantera because they add to the atmosphere. But the biggest gripe I have is that there are way too many arenas, especially near endgame and I just sit and stare at the Gore nests and it gives me a feeling I only get when playing Winnie the Pooh's Homerun Derby. But besides my complaints this game's soundtrack and level design is great, one of the few first person shooters to get platforming right. The only reason I am complaining about these things is because this game shares the same title as Doom (1993), I would be almost completely satisfied if it was titled Doom 4 or something but for me this game is Doom 2016 and original Doom is Doom. This all being said, the game's singleplayer is the best first person campaign to come out recently. The game's texture work is beautiful. The Super Shotgun easily the best weapon in the game just under the BFG. It's fun to get upgrades for your weapons and the upgrades are fun to use.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never got to play the original DOOM games, so I looked over this game when it came out and picked it up when it was on sale. It's so, so good. If you're a fan of FPS games and want a change of pace from other shooters out there, you have to give this game a shot.

The gameplay is fantastic. I've spent a lot of time in a variety of shooters (Metroid Prime, Team Fortress 2, Overwatch, Halo) and DOOM is unique in the same way the original games are; it's fast paced and an engaging fight in every situation. I'm used to a lot of games having breaks in fights where you pull up your inventory or run to cover, but DOOM doesn't have any of that. A fight ends when there's nothing left to rip and tear.

The weapons are good. The original DOOM games are known for this, and that magic carried over it seems. I was a huge fan of the heavy assault rifle and the gauss cannon.

I'm not too picky with graphics in games so it doesn't take a lot to impress me, but the graphics are really good! There's a specific moment in the game that was absolutely phenomenal looking, involving the AI core VEGA.

Speaking of which, the game does have a story, and it's very campy. I really liked it, but not because it did anything special, it was just really cheesy.

The music is also really good. Mick Gordon did the game justice.

Overall, if you're a fan of shooters, and felt like you missed out on the original games, DOOM 2016 is a fantastic game to play!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverything I needed to know was told to me when I realized Doom Guy was moving with the same sway and momentum of the original. A good sign for a fella like myself who has played classic Doom and a good sign for anyone interested in seeing if D44M has eclipsed Doom 3. DOOM is a good looking modern FPS without any modern claptrap holding it down. You kill demons and stoically ignore your mission control robot whining at you that he just did what needed to be done as you break his toys. That covers the story, although there are some good bits that establish a Doom Guy *mythos* in the Hell levels.

If you've played Doom classic or newer Wolfenstein games you'll be able to appreciate that your health is a number you want to keep high and reinforced with armor instead of waiting for a shield to recharge and give you permission to exchange some shots. There are FPSs with harder difficulties that train you to meticulously approach a fight and dissect it with proper application of your tool kit arsenal from the safety of cover, and then there's DOOM where the only survival option is aggressively destroying the opposition. You get a lot of guns and a lot of options for them, you get bonuses(quad damage, berserker, etc) in the levels that can be timed for destroying enemies en masse or saving your bacon with proper timing, the only real mix up is a double jump that seamlessly blends into that sweet Doom gameplay. This is a game about establishing a vicious rhythm of fighting where you strafe around fools and put them down quick lest you get overwhelmed. You might think the specter of modern game nonsense is here with the new Glory Kills function but in a pleasant surprise they are woven into the gameplay. Intelligent use of Glory Kills and Chainsaw Kills to provide brief windows of immunity, bursts of mobility and fresh resources turns the usual designer indulgence of "watch this cool kill animation 200 times" into a strategic consideration.

Good levels, good soundtrack, great gameplay. There's a few bosses, they're good fights. A few I wish were worked in as enemies but that's just an old salt missing when the Cyberdemon was a regular level feature. The game is full of the usual backstory and lore for those of you that love soaking in a setting and reading about the interesting technological and economic build up to mining Hell for energy and good laughs at all the obvious signs everybody ignores that half the facility is part of a cult. For the explorer there's a good set of secrets in every level to find and they're part of the nicely woven tapestry of combat, exploration, challenge stages, mission challenges, and equipment challenges. The game gives you a lot to chew on if you want to do everything and it rewards you well with extra power for doing so. Most of the challenges are not grievous pains in the butt, though a few are and encourage checkpoint farming. On the bright side none of it is mandatory unless you've got Collection on the brain.

Good game and at a perfect price point these days.\t\t\t\t\t
