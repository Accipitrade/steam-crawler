Thy Free Time Consumed

Pros
  • Good soundtrack
  • Game looks great
  • There's an alright story
  • The enemy design is awesome
  • The glory kills are dope as hell
  • Can upgrade your praetor suit
  • There a several weapons in the game and they all look awesome, Especially the super shotgun and there's also a Big ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Gun. Also, no Doom 3 shotgun.
  • Secret Doom I&II areas
  • Rewards you for finding collectibles and secrets
  • Rune trials, which are quick challenge levels that give you runes which you can equip. E.g. There is a rune that once you fully max if you have more than 75 armor at a given time you have infinite ammo.
  • There are also weapon upgrades, which are awesome
  • There's lore, which is pretty neat, and at one point a demon gives a narration about the doom slayer.
  • Data logs, which give information about every weapon, character, and enemy in the game
  • All the levels are top notch. The lighting, atmosphere, and level layout are pretty solid. Now, I am no level design expert, but I can't recall any level where I felt like it was absolute garbage.
  • The boss battles are enjoyable, and they also look really cool, especially the cyber-demon
  • Each level has its own set of challenges, which adds a little something extra. E.g. perform 3 glory kills from behind
  • There are power ups just like in the previous games
  • The in-game map is pretty cool, it gives a 3-d layout of the level and indicates if you've been in an area or not, and if you find the auto-map, it shows everything.
  • There is an arcade mode if you are into that
  • There is multiplayer and it is fun. You can play as doom slayer or as a demon. With doom slayer there are several weapons you can choose from and have your own personalized load-outs. As a demon you can play as any demon that is in the game, except for the boss battle demons, and every demon has their own special ability/attack.
  • There are several multiplayer game-modes
  • Also there are multiplayer cosmetics, colors, and patterns for the doom slayer and his weapons. All of which you can unlock by playing.
  • There are also SnapMaps, which are custom maps made by the community that you can play, you can even make your own map. There is also a in-game tutorial as to how you can make your own custom map, which is awesome.
Cons
  • The parkour can sometimes be wonky. He just slams his face into a ledge instead of grabbing it.
  • The multiplayer isn't too popular, so at certain times of the day finding a lobby isn't going to happen. Or you'll be seeing the same people in your games.
  • If you're trying to unlock all the cosmetics, colors, and patterns, its going to be extra difficult since it takes forever as is, and on top of that not many people play the multiplayer.

Conclusion
The game is fantastic. The only real negative about the game is the multiplayer, but that shouldn't be the reason you want to play this game anyway. Instead, it should be about you wanting to kill demons while listening to the kickass soundtrack. Doom 2016 is a great game and a load of fun. It is definitely worth the $20.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all: the games looks awesome! The best looking and sounding Doom so far.

Personally I'm disappointed though. I mean I'm not a big enthusiast of fights on open spaces, without ability to use environment to fight tactically. The deamon generating monsters frustrated me to the point of quitting the game before I completed it. So - if you like bullet sponge enemies, super intense combat on open spaces without hiding behind walls and boxes - this is your game. Not mine though. I loved Doom 1, 2 and 3, I loved Rage 1 and 2, but exactly the one type of monster made me quit Doom from frustration. I even tried playing on "I'm too young to die" skill level. It's funny, I still complete Brutal Doom 1 and 2 on Black Metal and Hard Realism skill levels. When there is a finite number of monster to kill - I kill them. When they keep comming in infinite numbers until I kill the teleporting generator - I would say yes if I had inifinite ammo. But I end up with basic pistol soon enough. Maybe it's my lack of those new over 60FPS monitor. IDK. Maybe I'm too old and the game is a constant race. Rage 2 pace is just right for me. BTW, I also hate jumping and other dancing. I prefer running, shooting and taking cover. Well, Doom is not for people expecting to take cover.

I'll definitely get back to it when I find a mod changing damage making both monsters and player less of bullet sponges. I mean bullets should kill. Rockets should definitely kill.\t\t\t\t\t
