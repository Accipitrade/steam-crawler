\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI grew up playing the original Doom on PC back in the day and I was one of the odd ones out in that I wasn't a big fan of it at the time. Don't get me wrong, I enjoyed it for what it was, but I didn't think it was anything that great. Well, fast forward to now and I have those same feelings. The game is beautiful, and I certainly think it is a good game, but I got bored with it really fast. Some of the levels felt too long to me, and it got to the point I looked up how far I was online just so I could see if I was getting close to being finished with the game. Combat lost its appeal by the mid-way point for me because of how samey everything was. Regardless, I'm still giving it a thumbs up, because it'd be unfair to do so otherwise, especially considering its rare to see a game with Bethesda's name attached to it that isn't a buggy piece of ♥♥♥♥. If you liked the original Doom or just gore-riffic action in general, you're going to love this game! You should get it. Even if you don't, or if you've never played Doom, this is a great modern way to get into it. Now I'm going to list a few of my complaints that make it impossible for me to come back to it, but these are more nitpicks (except my first complaint) than anything. Despite my complaints, I don't regret playing this!

1. Gatekeeping. Now, the original Doom and others had some gatekeeping with trying to find the keycards to advance in the level, but there were so many times when I'd get tired of the combat and just want to hurry and finish a level, but I couldn't advance because I'd have to clear out a wave of demons before advancing. This happens a lot. And since there aren't very many demon varieties, this gets old fast. When you go to Hell for the second time, I dreaded it because you could see these huge gates as you're approaching the wraiths and I knew it would mean I'd have to clean out wave after wave. For some people that is the joy of this game. I personally just don't find it entertaining.
2. Mentioned above, but there aren't very many demon varieties. Now, the originals didn't either, and I'm not asking for them to animate a bunch of different demons, I get that animation and modelling is time consuming, but something to vary them up. Maybe some would come in wearing different gear or even just be a slightly different color, I don't know, something different.
3. For many, the story is irrelevant, and that's fine. I think maybe this game doesn't do it for me because I love a good story (vast majority of my game time is spent playing vast RPGs with sprawling stories and worlds). While this story is interesting, to really appreciate it you're going to have to visit third-party websites to get a grasp of it, and even then they're not certain either. It seems to be that Doom Slayer IS the same Doomguy from the originals, but then there is room to believe he's B.J. Now, I am not saying I want everything spelled out; I think part of good story telling is living some things up for interpretation, but this game opens the door when they bring up the legend of the Hell Walker and the Night Sentinels.
4. I think the idea of Night Sentinels is kind of lame, but I can't put into words why. There is a theory that Doom Slayer may not actually be a member, but just worked with them at some point. I hope that's the case.
5. Movement speed was kind of slow. I was surprised at how slow you moved in this game, compared to the classic Doom.
6. Environmental story telling, there wasn't enough of it. Got to a point where it seemed like ♥♥♥♥ was just everywhere for no real rhyme or reason. I didn't really think about it until much later in the game when you find the secret-ish (not a real secret, you have to pass through it to advance) evil church where there is a sacrifice being made with Olivia watching and I really wanted more moments like this.
7. Regarding the ending: Samuel's heel turn was so painfully obvious. I know subtle isn't Doom's strong suit, or even their point, it's about shoot and rip things apart, but come on...

I might come back to edit this more coherently, but for now, there's my review. Not hating on anyone that loved this game, I get it. It's nice return to form for FPS, but I've just been more of a story-based player. But I'd never give this a thumbs down just because it isn't a game FOR me. I hate seeing when people do that. This is a well-made, competent game, with some incredible music. And despite the flaws, I am curious to see what happens next and will be buying Doom Eternal.\t\t\t\t\t
