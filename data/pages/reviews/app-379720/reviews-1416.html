\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinally completed the campaign....4 years later. It's a fine game, but it's not Doom and I won't be playing through it again. Doom I and Doom II will always be the best, and while this one looks great and plays fine, it doesn't have the same appeal for me as the originals. The multi-player aspect is kinda trash, and that's disappointing, but I really dislike how most of the levels are just this: "Enter a new area, look around briefly then trip an invisible line in the sand and dozens of demons spawn, cue the industrial heavy metal music, and blast your way through them while searching for the hive"t o disable the spawning. Once you disable that, you can clear the rest, then the demons stop spawning and you can search the area for secrets and collectibles". There are a few variations on that formula, but that's a big chunk of the game. The old Doom games weren't like that.

The areas are very imaginative, good level design and the graphics are great...the game looks and plays absolutely fantastic on a 165hz monitor. The snapmap feature is pretty killer too. There are plenty of secrets and cool little touches throughout the game like the glory kills and upgrades, and that's cool, but again..it's a fine shooter, it's just not Doom to me.\t\t\t\t\t
