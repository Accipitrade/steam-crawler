I was born in the 90s and I start to dwell into gaming in the year 2000ish, the first thing DOOM related stuff that I was introduced to, was DOOM 3. Unfortunate to say that I don't like DOOM 3, and i have a feeling a lot of people feel the same way. So when Bethesda showed the news of DOOM 4 in their E3 presentation I was not that interested. In fact the first thing that went into my mind was, "Here come another sad attempt to revive the glory of the 90s".

Not to mention the game is not sandbox. In my mind a game nowadays need to be a sandbox in order for it to be good, take a few triple A games for example: GTA V, Far Cry 3, Fallout 4, etc. Games like Halo, Call of duty, and Battlefield do have linear campaign, but nobody buy those games for the campaign (okay maybe that's not 100% true for Halo, but with how... underwhelming the campaign is I doubt people still buy it for the campaign). So hopes weren't high when they open the multiplayer BETA and almost all youtubers who played the BETA was disappointed with it.

But then, after the game came out for a while, I saw a lot of youtuber commenting on how awesome, amazing, fun the single player is. How it is a "revive to the classic 90s FPS", this got me interested so I start watching a few videos of it. At first I don't really get the appeal, it's just a guy shooting demons while moving really fast, what's so good about it? Luckily, there was a DEMO for it, so i can at least try it and figure out what so good about it.

And I played it... Oh my god.... I PLAYED THE HELL OUT OF IT! (no pun intended) It was so... good.. the simplicity, the action, the speed, the gore, the carnage, and how it never break the pace! This is... this is FPS goodness, this RAW FPS goodness. So when summer sales hit and DOOM get a discount, it was a steal for me.

//REVIEW BEGIN HERE
SINGLE PLAYER: I'm going to divide this into 3 section combat, map design, and Story.

Combat: 10/10
The thing that makes DOOM standout more than others FPS is its combat. In DOOM you will always be in sprint mode, the shift button is there to slow down your movement, so it's like the reverse of your usual FPS controls. Then there is the no reloading, the non regenerate health, and the glory kills. First off the glory kills is one of the best thing about the game, now some might wonder if this quick-animation stuff will break the pacing of the game. It doesn't. Glory kills feels great, the brutality and how fast it is actually encourage you to be more lethal and brutal. Glory kills also serve as a mechanic to get more health drops than your standard weapon kills, so yes, this game encourage you to kill your enemies in horrible ways. And it is great. There's also power up that buff your character for a while, like increase speed, increase damage, you won't take damage for a while, maximum health, and the best of all of them berserk. Game developer nowadays tend to give player all kind of options to play their game, such as you can be stealthy, you can go gunblazing, you can be tactical, etc etc. The thing is they try to create all these options and yet none of them feel satisfying, be it the AI don't know how to react or the map isn't design for such play. However, there's only one way to play DOOM and so every decision and design are created to further enrich this experience. That is why every wave of demons you destroy always feel great.

Map design: 9.5/10
If you read my "point of view" you know i have trouble getting into a game with a linear single-player, unless of course I'm in it for the multiplayer. However, after playing DOOM I realized how a linear single player game can be more satisfying than a big open world sandbox single player. Take GTA V for example, I played it for 55 hours and i still haven't finish the single player. Cause it fail to motivate me and all I do is driving from point A to point B. But DOOM i finished twice, the first one in ultra violence and the other in Nightmare mode. And I'm actually planning to play more of the single player. I keep looking forward to play the same map over and over again because each map is design perfectly. Now to give DOOM more credit, the map in each level is not linear, each map is open for exploration kinda like Dishonored, for me this is great because it give developer control over what the player should be doing and give the player freedom to explore.

Story: 9/10
Yes. DOOM has a story. It actually has a pretty good world building if you decide to pay attention to all the codex. The thing is, DOOM guy/slayer/marine does not give a single f#ck about it. WHICH IS GREAT! How many of you dream of a protagonist if, let say, there's a evil guy with high political standing that no one can touch, this protagonist would just kick the door to his house, grabs him, pull his head off, throw his head like trash, and then move on. And there's nothing anyone can do to stop it. DOOM guy is like that, there's a deep twisted story in this game but DOOM guy doesn't give a single f#ck, he just want to kill all the demons and destroy anything related to HELL! This might make him sound like a 1 dimensional character but that's okay! In fact this is great, the player can immediately feel attach to DOOM guy because his motivation is just that simple! He has no hidden motive, sobbing past, hidden personality bla bla bla all of that crap. All he wants is to kill demons and all the player wants is to start killing demons, so really from the very beginning you feel attach to DOOM guy and you understand his character immediately. And all of these without him a making a single sound. With all of his rage he stay silent. And let me tell you, that's the scariest kind of rage. There's already a lot of youtube video discussing this topic if you're willing to search, worth the watch.

Multiplayer: 5/10
After finishing my first playthrough I decide to play the multiplayer. I played a few matches, then I change back to single player. The multiplayer is.... not that great, the thing is DOOM core gameplay is build to kill demons with big guns, as fast as possible and then move on to kill the next demon. This game is not build for fighting another player. First, if you're not playing with your friends, chances are you and your team will be in total chaos. Second, unless you're owning hard, every time you get killed it break the pacing/immersion, the "keep on moving" saying does not really do anything when you get killed by someone with a rocket launcher (which is a starting weapon) every 3 second, and then wait to respawn. Maybe I just suck at it, maybe. But if I want to play some fps multiplayer chances are I probably go play Overwatch.

Snap-map: ?/?
I haven't really try it, I would talk about it but i haven't really venture too much in it, my 16++ hours gameplay is mostly me playing the single player, the rest is checking out the multiplayer and the snap-map for a bit. So i can't really say anything about it.

SO SHOULD I GET THIS GAME?
If you're looking for a fun multiplayer, with good balancing that never get bored. Go buy Overwatch.
If you want to play the most awesome singleplayer with the protagonist that define badass, awesome map design, and endless RIP AND TEAR. Go buy DOOM.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst Wolfenstein, now Doom. It seems like the 90's are coming back bigger and better than before.

Doom 4, as I will stubbornly continue to call it, its the best single player experience I've had since Wolfenstein The New Order. In sharp contrast to EAs battlefront it is a game with a solid single player with a tacked on multiplayer. Its season pass only has content for it as well, so I suggest skipping it.

It manages to blend old school action and exploration with newer features such as perks and leveling up in a way I found very enjoyable. It is a game of sharp contrasts, sometimes being very forgiving and other times kicking your teeth in.

It also has an interesting way of telling a story, making it completely optional how invested you wish to be. Personaly, as the games-are-art hippie that I happen to be, I paid close attention to the story and mythology and felt really engaged as a result. Samuel Hayden is foreshadowed to be the next game's villian in a really satisfying sequel hook and I hope they do his character justice as more of an anti hero than a standard villian

This is well worth the $60 triple A price tag, and even more so at the summer sale $45 that I got it at.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall I'd say the game is okay.

The single player is pretty fun and straight forward in what it tries to accomplish. Go in and have fun with blood and gore. Don't expect an interesting story though.

The multiplayer on the other hand is dull. I got to level 10 within a few hours and even though it can be fun at times I still think it's not very exciting. The loadout is preconfigured and you only look for ammo and health/armor. The demon system sucks because you don't need a lot of skill to actually get kills. On the other hand there are some demons that are extremly hard to kill if you meet someone that knows how to play it well.

In addition there's only a lobby system without proper chat functionality. If you want to talk you should have a microphone ready.

For the SP I'd give a recommendation. The MP gets a "meh!" from my end overall it ends in a slight thumbs up.\t\t\t\t\t
