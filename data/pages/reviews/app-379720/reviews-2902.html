\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was skeptical of this game at first, as I am with most reboots/remakes. As a fan of the previous Doom games, I assumed this wouldn't live up to expectations or the hype.

I was dead wrong.

Right off the bat, the game drowns you in atmosphere, mixing the classic techno-gothic hellscape design with modern visuals. The redesigns for the monsters are great, they're a fresh take on the original designs, but each one is still easily identified (my favorite redesigns are the Cacodemon and the Revenant). Every time a new monster is introduced, it's a welcome addition to the game's (very large) enemy roster. Each one feels fresh and gives the player enough time to learn how to deal with the new threat.

Weapons are dealt with very similarly. You have your old favorites, like the Shotgun and the Plasma Rifle, but there are new additions as well, like the Heavy Assault Rifle and the Gauss Cannon. These are incorporated seamlessly into the game and the player constantly finds themselves switching guns to deal with ever-changing threats and enemies.

Unlike its predecessor, Doom 3, mobiliy is vital and learning to shoot while running and jumping is key. It feels like a weightier, more realistic Unreal Tournament. It echoes the original two titles more while keeping the more horror-themed pacing of 3. It's a blend of genres that feels fresh.

There's even a story this time around, and while it gives a wide berth to the action, it's still a welcome addition and has an interesting premise. The player can expand on the characters with collectible data packets found around the game. Which brings me to the collectibles: There's many easter eggs and cool things to find if you have a good eye and like to explore the massive levels.

All in all, DOOM looks and feels like a modern take on classic Doom.

It's definitely one of the best shooters I've ever played, and probably my favorite Doom title.

Honest to God 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, I'd like to say that this game is absolutely gorgeous and runs without any issues on my computer. I bought the game hoping to return to the Doom I loved playing for hours. But I think Bethesda dropped a 50 ton steel brick on this game, in my opinion. First off, the singleplayer is alright. It doesn't have a reload system like Doom 3 had, but had a classic Doom ammo system. I think I only got to around level 4, and got bored because of the repetitive level design. A basic explanation is small corridors, where you would come upon a large area and fight hordes of enemies, and so on. But I must say, the design of Mars was so gorgeous. The health is hard to keep track of, though, because you'll be too busy facing multiple enemies, while also trying to dodge their attacks, and the only way to know you're about to die is the words at the bottom of your HUD "Low health" and some red around the screen. For example, in Doom 3, if you were low on health, your character would make a different hurt moan then when you had full health. But, I do like the gory take downs. Punching the head off of an Imp makes me grin. This isn't a game I would recommend for the players that hoped it would be something like the Doom they loved. The replayability is iffy. If you wanted a challenge, then it's good to replay on a higher difficulty. But overall, it's similar to Skyrim. You get bored after playing it a second time.

This is where I started to dislike the game more. The multiplayer. There is absolutely no ranked setting in the multiplayer, or no way to turn off some of the obnoxious power ups. You will join a game of high leveled players, which honestly reminds me of Halo. But at least there was a ranked setting for Halo. The high level players will completely sweep the area in a matter of seconds giving the new players zero chance. The bots are bad, too. If you're a low level, they will have weapons you haven't even unlocked yet. I would also like to mention that you can barely hear players walking past you. The walking sounds are either muffled by you shooting, or the other players shooting. Which was quite disappointing, because I got snuck up on multiple times. Also, the take down system is a fail in the multiplayer. If you miss, they can counter your attempt. And it is rather humiliating. Oh, and don't forget it's small level design. Most of the levels are too small to have decent team deathmatch games. I think the only game type that would work with these maps is Infernal Run.

I tried to get myself to like the game, but at it's core, it's nothing compared to the earlier Doom games. This game made me miss Doom 3, which to some people wasn't a great game. But compared to it, I'd rather play Doom 3 and it's expansion then this.\t\t\t\t\t
