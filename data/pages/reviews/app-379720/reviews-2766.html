If Doom 3 had a baby with the original Doom games.

It's got the story/campaign of Doom 3 with the fast-paced, action-packed combat of the original games.

There's a massive amount of weapons to use with most of them coming back from previous titles (Chaingun, super shotgun, rocket launcher, BFG) Some of the weapons have similarities to their Doom 3 counterparts in terms of appearance but with weapon mods (you can change on the fly) they play very differently.

I think all of the demons from the original Doom games make an appearnce in this one. (Not including bosses) There's certainly a large variety. Some of them look and act similar to the ones from the original Doom games (The Mancubus' for example) and others are a lot like the ones from Doom 3 (Hell Knights). Then there's a few that are pretty unique in terms of appearance and even playstyle. (The Imps are a bit different, and the zombie marines or whatever are very different)

You play as the original Doomguy in this game, (not as some marine recruit like in 3) so you're a total badass. This is demonstrated at certain points in the story, (literally he gives zero ♥♥♥♥♥ xD).

I enjoyed this game alot. It's lots of fun to just go in and mindlessly shoot up the legions of hell. It kept me on my toes quite a bit and the story itself wasn't bad.

I was pretty chapped at the ending because I found out that The Crucible was a FREAKING SWORD and we never got to use it. That would have been waaay too much fun, oh well.

I guess in short, if you wished Doom 3 (Personally I loved Doom 3) was more like the original Doom games, get this. If you're a fan of Doom period, get this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a reboot of a classic game, Doom does everything right. It captures the original feel of being really fast and just constantly shooting things, inside large levels packed with secrets. After many years of flawed releases like Doom 3 (impressive tech, but too focused on a “horror” presentation) and Rage (solid core mechanics and fun world, but highly erratic graphical quality), Doom puts id back on top in both gameplay and technology.

The newer game elements added to the old-school shooting give you some fun options. You can upgrade your weapons in various ways, culminating in a special mastery task you have to perform to gain each weapon’s final ability. You can change the rules of the game with equippable runes, and these can also be upgraded if you do certain things with them on.

The game runs really well—I have hardware similar to the recommended spec and I get 90+ fps at Ultra settings using the Vulkan API. The demons look amazing; almost all of the classic creatures are back, and they are often faithful to the original games visually. So much attention to detail has gone into the animations and behavior of each monster. For example, imps will leap up onto ledges, scurry up walls and pillars, and look back over their shoulders at you while they run away, hissing and screeching and throwing fireballs.

In Doom, you are a badass, but you can also die really fast to anything if you’re slow or not paying attention. This makes the major encounters a constant adrenaline rush and an exercise in rapid tactical planning (you might want to save some chainsaw fuel in case a Baron of Hell shows up). The game is ridiculously violent, but this violence is directed at demons and is presented in an over-the-top, almost comical way. There are even some funny hints that the demons know that they are screwed. In Doom, Hell is afraid of YOU!

★★★★★ (5 out of 5 stars)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a game about feeling like a complete badass, and it does that really well.

It is hard not to compare it to Bethesda's other badass shooter, Wolfenstein: The New Order. TNO does everything better than DOOM does, and I really recommend you play TNO first if you are looking at DOOM. DOOM is still a great game, it is just that TNO is better, and perhaps the greatest FPS campaign of all time.

If you already played TNO though, DOOM should be your next FPS campaign.

The game ditches the horror approach of Doom 3 to return to carnage of Doom 1 and 2. The soundtrack and brutality of the game combine to give you an awesome experience. The game design of linking melee finishing moves to giving health pickups is a great idea that keeps you in the action all the time, and gives you an exhilarating feeling of triumph by giving you a way to survive when brought to low health. The weapons all feel powerful (thanks to excellent dismemberment) and are fun to use - the game never shies away from giving you powerful weapons, even if they would be too powerful in other games. It makes up for this by throwing a surprising variety of enemies at you, and making them exceptional at traversing the levels. Demons will climb walls, leap up boxes, vault over railings, and not let anything stop them from reaching you. This is especially impressive with how well they all animate, always looking completely fluid and natural.

On top of all of that, it has one of the (if not the absolute) greatest soundtracks of all time. The music in this game is not just a background, but kicks up as the action picks up, and is so front in center that it is arguably more noticeable and memorable than even the games visuals. You will look up and listen to songs like "BFG Division" and "Rip and Tear" again and again well after you have complete the game. Even if bombastic industrial music is not normally your jam, the glorious experience of slaughtering demons to the absolute perfect soundtrack will elevate the already fantastic songs for you.

DOOM is a great game, and the changes made to the old DOOM formula are all welcome. If you are craving a game that will give you a rush, DOOM will do it. If you are looking for an FPS with tight and polished controls, DOOM will do it. If you are looking for incredible visuals and sound design, DOOM will do it. Pretty much the only thing DOOM does not do well is the story, but you really will not give a damn as everything else is so well done.\t\t\t\t\t
