\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first saw the previews for the game, I thought that it would be a nicer looking Doom 3. I actually didn't liek the idea of the special kill animations, I thought that it would take away from the simplicity of the game that made the original so good.

After playing I found that the game plays very intuitivley and lives up to the original while also adding a lot. In spite of adding a lot of customizations and mini games, the game doesn't feel like a convoluted mess. Everything is well thought out and smooth.

I was really happy with how the weapons were done. It feels much more like how doom should be. The artwork for the enemies is outstanding. They look new and badass while still very iconic. I would say this is worth the retail value.

I have to really try to point out a negative. I guess some check points could be in better locations here and there. And too many place swith cheap fall deaths, granted I may just be clumsy.

9.8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I really never cared about the development of this game. I didn't even have a passing interest in it, because I just really don't play that many shooters.

Fast forward to a few days before release and I started watching videos and looking at screenshots and I was impressed. I liked the fast paced nature, the old-school level design, finding health packs etc. So I took a chance on DOOM, and I'm glad I did.

Not quite finished yet, I reckon I'm about half way (at a little over 9 hours) and DOOM is glorious. I haven't had this much fun with a shooter since Half-Life.

It's single player is as good as everyone says. The story is minimal, but you don't care about that do you? Even Doomguy, your player character doesn't care. He crushes, smashes and chainsaws his way through everything. He doesn't speak but his actions speak for him. All he wants to do is kill demons and you will kill thousands of them.

The game runs great on my computer, and looks amazing as well. The combat is fast and challenging, and the levels are massive with multi-tiered maps, secrets, key hunting complete with power ups and pickups. They even threw in weapon upgrades, suit upgrades and challenges. If you look hard enough I'm sure you will find all sorts of Easter Eggs as well.

DOOM is a great game for single player but it also has a Multiplayer mode and a Map creator I haven't even touched yet. It's a full package and worthy of a spot on your Steam library.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are a fan of the DOOM series, you owe it to yourself to buy this game. I admit, at first I was not convinced; poor multiplayer beta and was underwhelmed while watching videos of reviewers playing the beginning of the game. Despite my negative initial impression, I decided to take the plunge and bought the game on release.

I'm glad I did! This feels like a DOOM game - keeping traditional elements such as fast-paced action, monsters, and weapons, while taking advantage of modern developments today. The combat feels fantastic; at first I thought the gore kills were gimmicky, especially when I saw other people play it. In fact, it doesn't just feel great ripping a demon apart, it adds to the gameplay and keeps it unique; most modern FPS want you to take cover and reload. Not DOOM. It encourages you to dive into a demon mosh pit by ripping them apart so you get more ammo and health!

The game also makes good use of weapon upgrades, allowing them to be used in different ways for multiple battle scenarios. Although the format of combat gets repetitive, it always feels fun. Also, upgrades to both weapons and your marine are optional - if you want to play it without them for a greater challenge, go right ahead! The multiplayer is basic and pretty uninspiring (perhaps it will get better in the future), but DOOM is all about the single player experience and modding community!

I admit I am a DOOM fan boy. The original DOOM is one of my favorite games of all time and I have enjoyed all the others (including Doom 3 and Doom 64). I feel this one is particularly strong and with the addition of SnapMap's user-generated content, I believe there will be plenty of replay value.\t\t\t\t\t

I would wait until this game gets actual modding, but since that's likely never going to happen- get this when it's on sale.

The only thing this game has going for it is a fun single player campaign- which suffers from a clunky mechanic they borrowed from Brutal Doom (glory kills). You can't play the game without doing it, as you get significantly better upgrades for performing them all the time.

This game has cross-platform play. This means that PC's are restricted to the limitations of consoles for the sake of being able to play with them on some Snapmap levels. Greeeaaaat.\t\t\t\t\t
