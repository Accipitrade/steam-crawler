\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game hit me close to the heart, in a good way. Ticks a lot of the right boxes I wanted out of a DooM game that I was not certain would be delivered:
* Intense, violent and visceral close quarters combat, challenging but rewarding. This game goes the extra step of making the player feel like a vengeful god through story interactions and the creative glory kills, something I appreciated.
* Great, punchy soundtrack (it's dynamic, glory kills change it)
* Innovation of design while preserving the elements that drew fans to do DooM in the first place.
I didn't expect Berthesda to win me over, I've become less of a fan of their games with time but this was a pleasant suprise. I also liked a lot of the cute little homages to the era of 1980s and 1990s of gaming such as the random secrets with useless but amusing collectables. This game stands on its merits to be worthwhile to newcomers while also entertaining the older lizards who remember it's origins, a worthwhile addition to a gamer's library.\t\t\t\t\t
