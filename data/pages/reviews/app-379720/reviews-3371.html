\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh...my...freaking...god. So awesome. So brutal! This leaves behind the Horror game that was Doom 3 and goes back to it's roots; Shoot all the bullets, kill all the things, revel in the glory of your triumph. Then get your face splattered in the next room. Which makes killing everything in that room all that much more fun.

The weapons are awesome, and have a healthy feeling of the originals, while still feeling new, effective, and awesome. iD has also added in several upgrade systems, from suit upgrades to help you switch weapons faster or find secret areas easier (not too easy, mind you.), to weapon addons (charged stun shot on the Plasma Gun, for example).
Each weapon I've come across, sans two, have 2 addons available, and if you unlock both of them, you can switch between them in battle.

Also harkening back to the old days, you no longer reload. All your ammunition is available to your gun at all times.

But just the sheer brutality of the melee kills you can do, from curb stomping an Imp, to ripping a soldier's arm off to bash him in the head with it, in the middle of a huge firefight, at that! Chaining back and forth from fighting to brutalizing enemies.

And I'll end it off with this..... an Imp sprinting sideways in front of you, suddenly jumping up and spinning sideways to fling a fireball at you. Absolutely awesome designing.
Get this, but be warned that it's HEAVY on the system.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere to begin . . .
First off, I've been an avid DooM fan since I was 4, playing the originals with my dad. Every Doom game that came out (yes even 3, loved the mod support for it). I'd been wishing for a new contribution to my favorite game seires for another 10 years, and when they finally showed it at Quakecon '14, I was ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ HYPED. So I've been aboard a dangerous 2-year hype train, dangerous because I really, really didn't want it to flop. I tested the multiplayer at Qcon '15 and participated in Alpha and Beta, thought was pretty decent, never quite got the hate for it, but I didn't really care to get into it either.

So with my over-hyped-ness and crazy high expectations of this game, I finally played thorugh it on Nightmare first (go hard or go home). Long story, short, IT exceeded every expectation I ever actually had for the game; it just feels right.

It's so hard to explain without one actually playing it, because it is truly a fresh but old school experience. Think of it as the Wolfenstein TNO, but 10x better.

11/10 I'm constantly recomending it to my friends with the hardware able to run it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust beat the game and here is my two cents...

True to the doom franchise you get kick ass guns and kill demons with a lot of gore. The combat is very fluid and the environments are very cool. I feel like the game was primarily geared towards multiplayer only because the levels are very well done as far as detail and are definitely setup to be combat zones.

If you are looking for story this game is not for you. The story is very weak and obvious. The voice acting I found to be sub par. Especially since only three characters have dialogue.

The game feels quite repetitive at times, especially end game when you stop getting new guns and fight the same demons over and over.... with just more each wave.

-- The classic doom unlocks were honestly my favorite part.

For 50 or 60 dollars I cannot not recommend this game. Wait for it to go on sale. I would say 30 dollars or less is a good price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, after a long debate on whether or not to get this game I decided to give it a go, and here's my opinion.

The single player is decent, it's DOOM.

there are a few things I do not like about it though. because they added double jump and climb onto ledges many parts of the game have turned into jump here double jump there..... can't figure out where to go??? probably because you didnt notice a ledge somewhere you need to take.

every single mission I had a TON of downtime with nothing to kill because I couldnt figure out where to go.

The rest was pretty much what you come to expect from doom.

now for the disappointing part: MULTIPLAYER.

god how aweful....
being a total retro fps guy, doom, duke, quake, UT, this multiplayer just missed every mark for me.
the demon rune is total utter ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ and has no place in a doom multiplayer in my opinion.

im glad the single player offers a bunch of playtime as the multiplayer is something I simply will not be able to get into AT ALL, and that's where most of these types of FPS should really shine IMO.

so even with all that negative feedback, I still give this a thumbs up, but ONLY FOR THE SINGLE PLAYER!\t\t\t\t\t
