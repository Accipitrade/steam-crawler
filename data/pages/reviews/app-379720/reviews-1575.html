\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOF COURSE I've gotta recommend it. 'Bout the only people who can't recommend it are those who can't play above the "hurt me plenty" difficulty. Frankly, it'd be a waste of time for me to go on and on about the details in the game because tons of people know what it's like, but I'll at least give it a general rundown.

The story is simple, clean, and easy to follow, but it also branches out into some other details on the side that are pretty fun as well. Personally, I'm not a fan of the narration taking a breath once every three or four words (they CAN at least cut out the breaths without losing the sentence flow; it only sounds better when it's not chopped up as fake as possible. I'd compare it to, like, those spray-painted images on local carnival rides as opposed to professional imaging on an official ride at a proper amusement park).

The gameplay is almost perfectly ideal in my book. Most people will tell you it can get kinda old because the variety of demons stops receiving additions about 60% of the way through the story, and yeah, that can be the case. Personally, I love the run and gun feeling. The higher your difficulty, the faster and more efficient you need to be, and unlike many games out there, this is one where skill is truly its own reward. Hey, it can be fun if you're not good at it, too. It's just not AS fun. It's a great game for the phrase "go big or go home."

Its personality and style feel like something generic turned into one of the most gorgeous masterpieces gaming has to offer. It feels amazing to shut your brain off and let your instincts run wild when you're playing a game, and, again, it's just fantastic if you're good at it.

There aren't many bugs or other iffy instances, but players can expect some flukes when it comes to platforming (especially grabbing ledges from below) and some weapon functions (for example, the combat shotgun's explosive-round secondary fire will sometimes initiate for a shot, but there is a remarkably brief casting period before the shot can be fired, and this can often result in the player just not firing anything; it feels more like a bug than an intended problem).

All in all, this is an absolutely fantastic game for players who want to feel dominant and destructive or those who want to improve their raw instincts and mechanical skill without worrying too terribly much about strategy.
__________________________

On the other hand, online gameplay is rather terrible and dysfunctional. When you've got a rapid-fire, spray-and-pray weapon like the plasma rifle or assault rifle dealing 30HP damage per pellet at pretty much any range as opposed to the super shotgun dealing 40HP per shell about four feet from the target, you've definitely got some mechanics to not only polish, but completely rethink. Other than that, I honestly don't like that playing as a demon wields such ungodly and unfair amounts of damage. In my very first match, I wrapped up the game on my own with a ten-player streak as the revanant, and all I did was click M1 repeatedly. No aim, no positioning consideration, no mindfulness whatsoever.

I've seen some bad online games, and this isn't the worst, but I would definitely put it on a top 10 list.\t\t\t\t\t
