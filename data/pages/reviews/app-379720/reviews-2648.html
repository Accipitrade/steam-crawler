Don't buy if you are a demon, seriously !!

Step 1: rip and tear.
Step 2: don't stop - until it is done!

(Note: lengthy but detailed review, please consider reading half way - before you decide whether to proceed or not)

YOU ARE THE DOOM SLAYER - TERROR OF ALL DEMONS.

This game is incredible. Hands down one of the most fun, exciting, entertaining, extremely satisfying and addictive games I have ever played. Definitely my favorite 1st person shooter of all time. It masters the shooter formula like no other and succeeds where many others failed- in creating a captivating, gripping and rewarding gameplay. Pure blood pumping action and impeccable atmosphere.

The game strikes a remarkably fine balance between challenge and fun. Between the danger of death and the thrill of demon slaying.
You will conquer seemingly insurmountable odds coming out as a complete badass hero. The game demands a lot of the player - tactics, smart and quick thinking, improvisation, adaptation, reflexes and a lot of skill - that you build up along the way.

Its systems are simple yet deep. They call for strategic thinking, planning before and during fights. Deliciously old school style - yet with a fresh cool modern take.
Coming out victorious after a well executed battle plan is extremely satisfying.

The gameplay is frantically fast and intense. The action is over the top and the AI is incredible: super relentless, dangerous and aggressive in ways that will impress again and again.

Yet as the Doom Slayer - as the demons call you- you will be the harbinger of pain, fear, agony and death to the minions of Hell. Defeating hordes upon hordes of demonic adversaries of all shapes and sizes.
The enemies will attack you all at once, and throw everything they have in remarkable set piece and sadistic battles.

Your feel STRONG in DOOM. The weapons pack a serious punch- and all remain useful and viable from start to finish. You will tear enemies to pieces in melee with your bare hands and legs - or occasionally - with their own body parts. With wonderfully animated brutal and gory takedowns called Glory Kills.

The game plants that fury and bloodlust into you - I have often finished an insane fight - asking myself- "is that all?". While other fights were "what the hell?" or "how did I make it out of there alive?".
The game has this incredible adrenaline rush and thrilling feeling - especially as a deadly fight shifts to your favor - and you realize that the demons are the ones that are DOOMED here.

-----------------------------------------------------------
Some more detail:
The good:
1. Grpahics are great and the performance is excellent.
2. Animations are fluid and outstanding. Very notable for a shooter.
3. Sound design: powerful heavy metal and electronic soundtrack fits the action well. Especially the metal part which I prefer.
4. Length: the campaign is quite long. The missions/levels are very big, complex and mostly different. Some are partially non linear. exploration is rewarded by upgrade points and other benefits. Completing all challenges in-mission, getting all the secrets and upgrading your suit and weapons lengthen the game too.
5. Upgrades and progression: the upgrades are, for the most part, impactful and significant. Every upgrade ir Rune ability boost or downright changes gameplay.
6. Replay-ability: plenty of it across different difficulties.
7. Challenge: very challenging but very fun.
8. Glory Kills.
9. For the most part the world has this grim, dark and gritty tone. Arts style matches very well here.

The bad:
1. The story is bland, often annoying and quite clich√©d and predictable. It doesn't even try to be surprising. Luckily it serves its purpose giving you an excuse to go after the demons and kill them all.
2. Ending boss and story resolution was quite disappointing. There's a cliff hanger ending. Setting up a sequel. Not the end of the world though.
3. Some upgrades to weapons or Runes can take a lot of time to unlock, often forcing a grind which can slow down the pace and force you to restart checkpoints and levels to unlock them.
Also making a new playthrough at the highest Nightmare difficulty demands that you start from scratch.
4. Some of the secrets are too damn hidden or far fetched -and getting them all is hard, time consuming and will slow or break the flow of gameplay and action.
5. Some of the levels or parts of levels are designed in a more sluggish or frustrating way. Others are not very memorable.

That's all. Luckily the good outweigh the bad by a great deal. And most of these issues are minor.

I came into this game knowing nothing and seeing zero gameplay. And my, what a pleasant surprise.
Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust one common misconception I'd like to clear up about this game. You see, Doomguy isn't actually a badass. Being one of something implies it's possible to compare him to others and rank him in relation to them. Rather, badass is just a phrase for things that want to be Doomguy but fall short whill still managing to be awesome in their own right.

As for the game, it's as near a perfect capturing of the spirit of DOOM as is physically possible to get. It manages to capture the 1993 vibe of "to hell with the plot (pun definitely intended), let's just liquify demons with a Chainsaw", while also providing all the graphics capabilities you would expect from a modern game. In the intro, Dr. Hayden is a metaphor for the plot with Doomguy (wordlessly) expressing his distaste and indicating he's aware of how the situation is simultaneously ridiculous and deadly serious. It's painstakingly made clear that this is a game about shooting first and maybe getting around to asking the questions later, but maybe not works just fine too.

TL;DR: Two takeaways here -
1. Doomguy isn't a badass because badass is just what you call a watered down Doomguy.
2. Buy it, now.\t\t\t\t\t

The graphics are amazing and the optimization is top notch, which makes the game really enjoyable on almost every hardware.
The movement is really fluid, the guns are so hecking awesome, gunplay is fast and shooting monsters and demons have never been this fun before.
Exploring is fun and rewarding since finding secrets and upgrades really helps you beating the game.

The singleplayer experience of the game is amazing and is the reason why you should play this game.

Though, the multiplayer part of the game is quite forgettable. It might seem fun for for a while but it doesn't really live up to it. It's nothing compared to the singleplayer part.

Overall rating is 10/10, not even multiplayer can slow down singleplayer. Amazing game!\t\t\t\t\t

- Starring: AngryDoomGuy
A random super-roided dude with the attention span of a squirrel and enough anger management issues to fill an entire encyclopedia.
His brain doesn't have a tumor anymore...there is only the tumor left.

- Weapon designs for the new EHRMAHGERD generation.
Shotgun ? Here have a grenade launcher instead !
Machinegun ? Now it farts rockets all over the place.
Minigun ? Nonono...TRIPLE MINIGUN so you can froth around mouth and roll your eyes back into your head while the thing eats ammo like a hippie with the munchies at an "all you can eat buffet" !!!
Railgun ? Here, design by Apple and at the size of a living room table !

- Sound is cool enough, lots of crunching, munching, squishing, squeeking noises and roars from gnarly monsters.

- Music is current gen AngryEmo-Step complete with electronic toaster farts...perfectly suited for the PCP induced gameplay.
You aren't headbanging to the music...you're headbanging because it's the only thing that allows you to see what's actually happening on the screen.

- Monsters ? Yeah, shiny roided action figures.
They sound menacing, but as soon as you see them you start snickering because of how stupid they look.
I can never take the pinky seriously again.
Cacodemon got arms...look more like dangleberries to me.

In final...
Do you have ADHD ? Buy the game !
Do you not have ADHD ? Get Doom 2 and download BrutalDoom for free.
It takes special talent to make such a brutal and gory game boring, could barely keep playing for 45 minutes straight before I keeled over thanks to my brain going into sleep mode.

3/10
- Would take Ritalin again.\t\t\t\t\t
