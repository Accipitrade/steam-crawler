\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWorth the $26 I paid for it.

-Cool music
-Kind of repetitive and soul less (by-product of having to please the codified expectations of the modern Reddit “gamer” I suppose)
-AAA caliber game
-Runs great on my out-dated gaming machine. Specs are an Athlon II X4 635, 8gigs, and a GTX760. Installed on a 7200RPM external drive. About 10 minutes to boot the damn game though. Once you are in it's pretty normal, 2 minutes a level.
-Has a 4:3 aspect ratio option so you can play on old CRT
-XBOX one controller support

You can tell a lot of work was put into the game and I respect that. It felt boring, but it's fun to play because it's pretty. I wouldn't say it's game play is better than DOOM 3 (or DOOM1) but it's still cool. If gameplay is your thing and you own a million other modern FPS games this will likely seem bland. There is no twitch, involuntary neurological not the streaming site, factor to this game. I've beat all the original doom games (including 3) on ultra violence several times. Playing original dooms on Ultra Violence for long periods of time will ♥♥♥♥ you up and put you on edge. DOOM 2016 doesn't do these things to me (thank god). You can play this game and have a good night sleep. It's boring and codified, but that's what you get in 2016. I like the music a lot, but it lends itself to the codified nature of the game. It has a DJENT 9 string guitar sound, which is cool but at the end of the day Slayer is 1000 times better than Meshuggah so ♥♥♥♥ that dubstep groovy metal hybrid garbage in a Doom game. Whoever did the music has a hard-on for Nine Inch Nails because you can tell he took every page from Trent’s book for the sound (not a bad thing, NIN is ID SOFTWARE canon so it works). Who ever produced the game is probably a giant baby who told the music guy to tone it down and add all that Mountain Dew sounding garbage. They should have stuck with the NIN sound stuff. That said, it all works, I like the sound track as a whole for what it is.

The game is phenomenal but has no soul. If the goal was to create a Madden 2016/Call of Duty type game they achieved it. If you look at the old days of ID the thing that set them apart was the originality. American Mcgee, Adrean Carmack, and John Romaero's out of box creative designs. This Doom game is so perfect and sugary I can't add it to that list of greatness. HOWEVER it achieves greatness in other ways. It won't be remembered the same way Quake or Doom will be but it's a great game none the less. It's faults are that it is too perfect. Doom wasn't perfect. Quake wasn't perfect. DOOM 2016 is perfect. Take that as you will. $26 is a steal for this game, it is well worth the $40.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor so long have I yearned for gristle, claret and curor-the true gore that I've always needed. I have only played other games to yet fill the voids of what was missing.

I got my painful executions from Wolfenstein-The New Order. When knives dissapeared deeply into Nazi skulls, the shivers of joy of such executions died down after awhile.
I got my grisly, bloody messes from Dying Light. The hollow sounds of zombie necks shattering under hyperextension of their range only satisfied me for so long.
I had only seen executions in gifs and videos and heard only parts of the great music in the game.
Then one day, I decided to dive into the deep, ragged, and soot-filled bowels of hell.

My hands couldn't control themselves.

The demons of hell didn't flinch at my presence until I began to dismember them all, one by once, piece by piece. They began to fall at my feet, in heaps of lead-filled, half charred piles of guts.
They could only guess what was next when my hands grabbed their low hanging jaws, tearing them off in one clean yank.
There was no resistance when my fingers dug into their skulls, tearing them in half and throwing their worthless corpses aside.
Helpless weaklings, spilling through portals only to be met with tarnished green gauntlets that twisted their skulls and mangled their bodies into wretched, bubbling pools of gore and bloody refuse from which I bathe in, laughing wildly at their demise.
Now here I sit, upon my throne, ready to atone-
For my sins I have comitted
With hordes of demons against me-pitted
To seal my fate, to assure my death
So that I do not leave that last room
Assured to meet my DOOM.\t\t\t\t\t
