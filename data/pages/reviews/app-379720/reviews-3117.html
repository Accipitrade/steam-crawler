I've been playing FPS for 20+ years at this point. I've found there are any number of games that I can find fun from the silly and over the top to the gritty and realistic. But what I don't enjoy is a game that has _hours_ of boringness with the occasional highlight.

From reading reviews, it seems that ~8 hours into the game, it really comes into its own and becomes a blast. That really doesn't surprise me as there are glimmers of a crazy all out shooter buried inside the slog of the first part of the game.

As of the time of writing, I have not made it to the 8 hour mark. I don't know if I ever will. And at least for me, half of a game being primarily boring is not one really worth playing.

The biggest problem I have is the time between the battles. You could call it a recovery time, but the battles aren't utterly insane in the beginning either. The larger they are, the more enjoyable they become. But such battles may last a couple minutes followed by many time more of wandering to the next battle or objective especially if you're like me and have an interst in searching for secrets.

Secrets can be fun to find, but they also slow the game down even further for limited rewards.

The worst and really last straw where I just didn't want to continue was the addition of the Summoner enemy. It's not the challenge they pose as they don't summon so quickly that the additional enemies are a big deal. The problem is they constantly are running away which generally translates to waiting for clean shots or potentially wasting a lot of ammo on the empty space where it used to be.

This is DOOM. The Doom Marine does not wait. The Doom Marine does not hide behind chest high walls. And this enemy seems to always be the sole survivor from an otherwise enjoyable fight that just prevents me from continuing while I pretend like I'm in a traditional cover shooter.

If I manage to get farther in the game, I may update this review, but unless the payoff at 8 hours is so good that it makes me want to call into work to keep playing, I have a really hard time thinking it could really be worth it. I cannot recommend paying to have to work a full day in order to have fun with a game.

=== Edit 2016-08-29

I have since the time of writing completed the game clocking in at 10-ish hours (there's some amount of that time where I was sitting at a pause screen doing other things, so I'd say at least 8+ hours)

So, did it actually pay off in a big way? Well... sort of. Very soon after where I stopped before, I reached hell and started experiencing what the game seemed to be designed to be. Chaotic and intense battles with many monsters all trying to kill me in different ways.

I jumped. I dodged. I actively swapped between many of my weapons as I moved between enemies. And I was having fun!

Sure, there still was the time after the battle in the arena to scour for pick ups and secrets. Sometimes a jumping puzzle appeared seemingly to make me long to kill deamons again. But overall it was a great amount of intensity with the occasional breath of air.

And then there were bosses! Actual giant enemies with health bars to empty every bullet you could find into. Patterns to recognize and dodging that mattered all came to be!

Sure, there is only 3 bosses in the entire game all of which exist within the last ~2 hours of the game, but they're a fun different challenge.

The first boss battle was a good third act turning point where battles went from intense and chaotic to more tedious. In that battle, I started changing weapons not because they were more effective, but because I simply was out of ammo for others. The end of the game starts running against this as the last challenge they could come up with: limited ammo.

I actually had maxed my ammo carry amount long before, but that didn't change the drops in the actual battles. Frequently I would stop fighting to run in circles around the generally not well marked arena looking for an ammo cache to keep me going (sometimes actually pulling out my pistol to kill some things at the same time). This starts to feel like you're running scared when really you just want 50 more rockets to drive into deamons faces.

So after all of that and the final boss and everything else I experinced, do I think the game was good? Yes. I am happy I finished it after putting the time in before I had.

But do I recommend it to (functionally) past me? No. The first part is a grind to find the fun. The end is a grind to find enough ammo and just let the fight of the same monsters you've killed hundreds of times by this point end. The middle is golden, but the balance doesn't last and requires hours of play to get there and you don't get an ending without a couple hours more grind.\t\t\t\t\t
