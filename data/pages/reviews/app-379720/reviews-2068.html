\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game looks great, music is just brilliant, really makes you pumped up for the fight. Gameplay is exactly what I'd expect from a Doom game: quick, exciting, brutal. Story is standard: demons on Mars and to me it's enogh.

Why the negative review then? Bugs and shortcomings.

My saves got corrupted twice. Luckily I've got a habit of backing up my saves manually so I didn't lose too much progress, but still it caused a lot of frustration.
On the last boss there was some kind of invisible wall which prevented me from finishing the game. Apparently it's a common bug and still not fixed.
Multiplayer was really fun initially, reminded me a bit of Quake 3. But the more I played the more I disliked it. I've never seen a game with more cheaters than in DOOM. With the community as small as it is now, no ability to report players, no anticheat mechanism and broken team balance (it's not uncommon to see matches between beginners and veterans in separate teams instead of mixed up) multiplayer is pointless really.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tabove average shooter by today's standards but doesn't even come close to the original doom. movement speed is WAY too slow when compared to 1993 doom, making the gameplay boring and at times frustrating because of your inability to maneuver around projectiles and enemies. guns don't feel like they have any weight or impact behind their shots. the designs of the enemies are not distinct enough from each other to be able to easily tell them apart in the heat of combat, making it difficult to quickly identify the biggest threats and prioritize them. the game takes control away from the player much too often, usually for a cutscene or a "glory kill," which is frustrating and something that never once happened in the original doom. glory kills also allow you to just hit a button and watch "doom slayer" kill the enemies for you instead of having to rely on your own skill. levels are bland and the graphics look nice, but the brown-and-grey color palette makes everything blend together and feel the same, and also makes it even more difficult to spot enemies. overall, it's clear that there was way too much emphasis put on vapid, gory spectacles and conformity to modern shooter game design over the elements that made 1993 doom a masterpiece, like fun, fast gameplay that required quick reflexes and mastery of the game's movement mechanics, well-thought-out, intricate level design that encouraged lots of playthroughs, and ease-of-modification, among a ton of other things. doom 2016 stands out among most of the popular FPS games of today, and i might have given it a positive review if it weren't claiming to be a doom game, but it catastrophically fails to live up to the high standard set by the original doom.\t\t\t\t\t

Spoiler-free review

One of my all-time favourite games, DOOM is a traditional, old-school FPS that stays true to its roots and to the genre. There's something to be said about the simplicity though which DOOM delivers a pure, adrenaline-filled experience that makes this game so fun, or should I say, has made the DOOM franchise so fun for over 20 years.

DOOM is simply no-nonsense, straightforward fun; everything works perfectly here, with zero superfluous, half-baked fluff that's crammed into modern FPS games. id Software knows that a classic, tried-and-true formula never fails to deliver, and man does DOOM deliver!

The gameplay, graphics, sound design, music (I repeat, the heavy metal music by legendary Mick Gordon no less), glory kills, super shotgun, the doom slayer... Everything is just so satisfying and badass.

The story is an afterthought, but for what story there is, it's at least well-written and the dialogue is spot on. But who cares about the story?! This is DOOM. Just get this game already!

Score: 10/10
