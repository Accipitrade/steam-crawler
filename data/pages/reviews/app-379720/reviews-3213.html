\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really fun game. Every day after purchasing, I looked forward to playing it, and even now, after a 2 week break now I've completed the campaign, I feel a twinge of desire while considering whether to play The Witcher or this. I haven't played multiplayer at this point, just the single-player campaign.

I'm going to keep it brief.

Pros:

- While playing you feel the chaos of enemies surrounding you, and there's realistic chance you will die
- Gun fights are fun
- Boss fights are fun: you have to use your brain
- Completing optional miniquests within each level allow you to upgrade your weapons sooner
- Each gun has 1-2 modifications which, when fully upgraded, have a miniquest which unlocks a special upgrade of your weapon; these are really fun to unlock, completely optional, and not always easy
- Exploration yields rewards, but it isn't totally necessary, which makes it more appealing
- Glory kills are fun, and rewarding
- Chainsaw kills are even more fun, and even more rewarding

Cons:

- Loading times could be a little slow
- I wanted more! The campaign took me 19 hrs

Overall: Great game. If you love FPS' and have the disposable income, get it now. If not, your experience won't be tarnished by picking it up on sale in a year or two. I'm excited to play it through again on a harder difficulty finding all the secrets I missed the first time round.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: With the 6.66 patch and lots of rebalancing/redesigning of certain multiplayer elements I decided to give the game another try. I change my verdict to positive. Even without the current discount the WHOLE package that is DOOM after a year is definitely worth it.

As this was my second and still very amateurish review I'll leave it as it is. I will review SnapMap more thoroughly in the coming days. I can surely say right now that multiplayer is actually a blast.

It's very difficult for me to review this game. I'll look at the three games that are packaged in and presented as one called DOOM but first:

TLDR; The price of DOOM doesn't match its content at all. It's way to expensive for what it offers. The singleplayer is super amazing and has great replayability and even it alone doesn't cost the full price. You're basically forced to buy the multiplayer, which is an entirely different game by itself. It's not DOOM, it reuses DOOM assets to create a generic and forgettable multiplayer experience. The other piece of a game you get is SnapMap, which is a poweful editor for creating single and multiplayer (coop) maps. It is a great idea but only time will tell if it realizes its full potential. It could have been a separate DLC. I cannot recommend paying so much money for so little. With disappointment and bitterness I don't recommend the package DOOM that is presented as the DOOM game.

THE REST OF THE LOONG REVIEW;

DOOM: This is the singleplayer game. It's awesome. In no particular order:

Gameplay. Constitutes of alternating segments of arena battles and exploration. There are bosses :) All are immensely fun. Battles are actually challenging if you play on ULTRA-VIOLENCE and above. I recommend this difficulty setting for an optimal experience. You die if you don't plan and execute well and that's what I greatly appreciate in this game. Exploration is hugely rewarding. There are numerous secrets on every level that give you experience points that help you upgrade your weapons. Without your weapons you are done. There are also upgrades for your suit, which is the next crucial thing in your arsenal.

Music. TADATADDAADRARRARDDAADDAA Music intensifies with the amount of violence happening on screen. It's just badass and I totally loved it! When exploring, the music matches the environments in a way. All in all, a very strong aspect of the game.

Weapons, mods and runes. Probably the most interesting aspect of this game. I'll try to lay it out but I'm sure I'll miss something.

There's not a single weapon (probably except the pistol) that you'll ignore. Every weapon has its strengths and weaknesses and you'll notice how important the choice becomes depending on the situation very quickly. The same is true for the weapon mods.

Weapons have mods that change their operations. Some have two, some - one. Simply put you'd want all ways of killing demons at your disposal and the game allows you to do it by giving you ways to change mods on the go but you'll have to make choices: which mod to upgrade first, etc. You can mix and match the utilities of all weapons at hand in order to achieve an optimal configuration tailored to your game style. While weapon mods are upgraded by collecting experience points, the last upgrade of each mod has a skill requirement, meaning you have to kill with the mod in a special way a number of times in order to gain the upgrade. Super fun.

Finally the runes further allow you to customize your game style. I dare to say there are actually builds because you can equip three runes at a time out of 12. Every rune has a special effect and some effects play exteremely well together. For example, a rune makes demons drop armor when you glory-kill them. Another rune gives you unlimited ammo when you have max armor. Rolling killing machine if you can balance glory kills with good movement and picking out strong enemies. Runes are upgraded similarly to the last upgrade of mods - by doing an action a number of times.

Suit. There are 5 categories of upgrades with 3 levels each. All are useful but I recommend not upgrading your scanner because it automatically reveals secrets to you, which is very disappointing. Finding them on your own is what this kind of gameplay is about and by getting the upgrades you are literally giving away half of the gameplay experience.

Difficulty. I mentioned this but still: the difficulty is what makes this game. Higher difficulties require skill, learning, planning, reflexes, etc. To me this is what makes this game more than what it seems to be.

Graphics. What can I say here? Beautiful. Some people say it's too red but guess what... the game's happening on Mars and in Hell. What color do you expect to be dominant in these settings? Green? There are actually lots of other colors and even environments that don't have red in them at all. Demon models are disgusting but at the same time stylized and that makes them good. Weapon models are awesome. Dying animations are delight to watch.

Level design. This is actually pretty good. It reminds me a bit of playing Half-Life back in the days. You can see the end of a level in the distance and it's cool to look back when you are there to see where you've come from. Very satisfying. Finding secrets is great fun, too. I cannot say anything negative here.

Story/Narrative. There is a story and unlike most reviews say I've found it to be actually pretty good. Pay attention though: it's not deep or anything. It's just there and it ties perfectly well with what you're doing. It's coherent and not out of place. Which brings me to my next and final point.

Lore. There's lots of lore in this game. It's hidden in data logs and other places but it actually supports the story very well and expands on what you're seeing - weapons, demons, the socio-economic structure of the Mars facility as well as the state of humanity at that point in time. Also, who you are playing, why he's there and so on. It tells you who the characters you're encountering are and so on. At the same time, it leaves you to connect most of the dots. An important part of the lore (about the DOOM Marine) is not very obvious. The best thing is that all this is completely optional. If you don't want it, you can skip it entirely.

10/10

MULTIPLAYER
It's generic and boring. No reason to not be sold separately but I guess nobody would buy it then. This is just my opinion.

* It's dynamic, fast, you die easily
* It's generic in terms of mechanics with two exceptions. There's only one unique game mode and it's a moving king-of-the-hill control point. I guess there'll be more with the Season Pass DLCs. There's this mechanic where you can play as a demon that is super powerful but killable. It introduces freshness but I couldn't see it changing the game too much.
* Most of the uniqueness comes from the weapons, which frankly, are not THAT unique, and the textures of the maps.
* There are loadouts. DOOM is a game that I've known in a time where multiplayer was a survival battle where weapons were disperesed on the map and that was a very strategical element. I expected the same here but it wasn't. Only the BFG is on a timed cooldown somewhere on the map.
* Finally, there's a season pass and it's only for multiplayer. I don't know if that's good or bad but I'm skipping it entirely.

5/10

SNAPMAP
This is a very interesting game mode but sadly I couldn't enjoy it much. Basically there's a powerful editor that allows you to build and script almost everything you've seen in singleplayer. Also, the made maps can be played with other players, i.e. in multiplayer. This sounds awesome on paper but as of two weeks ago I couldn't play a single multiplayer map and the singleplayer ones that I've played were very bland. This has great potential that could extend the longevity of the game but I didn't see it realized. I guess the popularity of the game is not high enough to incite the community.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvery time I receive a weapon upgrade from a flying robot I punch the hell out of it for absolutely no reason. This is Doom.

The campaign does everything right that the multiplayer (and Doom 3) did wrong. This is the classic Doom experience dropped into the excellent id Tech 6 engine while still preserving the rules/gameplay that defined the original. Modern updates to the formula, such as weapon/suit upgrades, serve only to enhance the core experience rather than re-defining it. Anyone for whom Doom was a formulative gaming experience will quickly fall back into the rhythm here, while those who cut their teeth on Halo/COD might feel like fish out of water temporarily but will fall in quickly with a bit more investment.

Gameplay is fast and furious with the original Doom "glide" in full effect. No reloads, no cover shooting, this is a game that rewards aggression and constant forward momentum. That would be a problem if the engine/mechanics didn't support it, but the movement and shooting are as tight as any arena shooter in recent memory. The new execution mechanics fit perfectly into the aggressive/fast gameplay style, and along with the chainsaw (which generates ammo) incentivize a pace that comes as a breath of fresh air after a decade of duck-and-reload shooters.

Bottom line, if you have any fondness for the early PC FPS era or you want to try out a new experience that feels significantly different from other modern shooters, you can pick up Doom without concern that it throws the baby out with the bathwater. This is a labor of 90s-era FPS love, and you can feel it every time you shotgun an imp and then explode its head with your bare hands.\t\t\t\t\t

It's Doom. If you are looking for suspense similar to DOOM 3 you will be disappointed. If you are looking for a tactical shooter you will be disappointed. If you are looking for a throwback to early days of FPS when body count and survival were all that mattered and games scoffed at trivial things like reloading them you have come to the right place.

This game plays fast and furious and doesn't hold back on filling a map with baddies ready to overwhelm you. The guns feel great to shoot and with their alt modes of fire provide plenty of opportunity to tailor their use to your play style. If you are looking for a deep an invovled story you won't find it here. This game is all about shoot it until it dies. You can flesh out backgrounds and story lines should you really wish to, but it's DOOM. You can hunt secrets, which sometimes will give you a weapon a little earlier, but don't have too.

This is probably the only game to play where you can snap a demons neck, chainsaw his buddy in half, and then shotgun another one in the face. Also, let us not forget the pure joy that is the BFG 9000 when you eventually retrieve it.

All in all, I would say that this game is a true successor to the DOOM franchise and worth the money if you are looking to relive the glory days of old school action shooters.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery satisfying game!

I spent 16 hours playing the single playger campaign, and it was worth the full MSRP. Old school run and gun, item pick ups and no automatic health regen. The enemies and the maps are well designed for the incredibly fast gameplay. Some of the newer game mechanics, such as the double jump and the ledge grab fit right at home with this run and gun master piece. The finishing melee kill, the Glory Kill, adds so much visual satisfaction and it balances the breakneck speed of the game very well.

The game also looks fantastic, and it supports 21:9 aspect ratio out of the box. I had everything pretty much maxed out running on 980Ti hitting 60fps 95% of the time at 3440x1440. I would say this game is on par with Crysis 3 in the eye candy area.

On the flip side, the game did crash 4 times during those 16hrs of play time. I don't believe it was my hardware.

HIGHLY RECOMMENDED!\t\t\t\t\t
