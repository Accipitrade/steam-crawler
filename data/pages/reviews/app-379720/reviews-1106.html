\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYeah, I got to go with the majority on this: Doom is fantastic. Not only as a video game remake, but a pure homage to the past. The thing that gets me is that it’s a game with a clear design philosophy. A philosophy that permeates the game from start to finish; throwing you into wave after wave of baddies, never letting go.

It’s a relentless loop of action, with very few lulls.

You don’t even get to reload your weapons, or hide behind cover. Just a constant feeling of momentum where you bounce from monster to monster; getting to know your arsenal like the back of your hand. There’s a lot of love put into the pacing of it, and a clear sense of the timeline between when you step into an arena, and when you leave it -- blood soaked. And even though you get to breathe a little and look for supplies and secrets between each set-piece, you’re always wishing for that next encounter, fantasizing about what configuration of hell-spawn the developers have cooked together. And they always deliver, even when the level design itself gets a little stale.

And if you’re still unsure about the developers attention to their lineage: you can unlock the classic Doom levels here, albeit with new trimmings like the comparably hyper-modern monsters and guns from the new version. It’s nostalgic and so, so awesome if you’re into the old-school stuff.

It’s also one of the few games I’ve worked my way through achievements and extra objectives, just to give me more reasons to get back into the heat. Even when it's for the nth time.

Aside from all this, there’s actually a ton to say about the streamlining, design principles and layers of Doom as a pure action experience. But if you’re serious about these types of games and the attention they demand from you, this will suffice: Buy and play the ♥♥♥♥ out of Doom.\t\t\t\t\t
