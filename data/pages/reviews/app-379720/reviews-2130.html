\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played through the story a long while ago, but I keep coming back. Here's why.


Sure, the plot isn't the best one you'll ever see in your life, but it's existant, coherent and -most importantly- doesn't get shoved down your throat. And you're not playing Doom for the plot, are you?

Combat is fast paced, especially on higher difficulties. Running from demon to demon while crushing limbs or exploding heads with a shotgun feels rewarding, especially because the phenomenal soundtrack from lord and saviour Mick Gordon compliments the gameplay fantastically.
The game rewards replaying levels by handing out points if you find secrets, raise your combat-rating or accomplish tasks, making your weapons and your suit permanently stronger.

After all that said, the campaign clearly is the main game mode.
While there is multiplayer, for me it's "meh" at best, because the game isn't really designed for it.
Also, there is Snapmap. Sure, creating levels or playing community levels is fun, especially when people come up with new mechanics or game modes, but in the end, the devs of id Software are best at making levels.\t\t\t\t\t
