\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I just finished DOOM and I have to say... it's not my cup of tea.

I went into this hearing a lot of good stuff about the game. How it's supposed to have good gameplay (which it does) and finally be a great shooter again in a world of grey-to-grey military shooters. But as the end credits started rolling on my screen I could just sit there with a blank face saying "That's it?". A little disclaimer on the wayside, I never played the original DOOM or any other DOOM game for that matter.

So the story is kinda.... I don't know actually. It starts you off with telling you to "rip and tear until it is done" and then sends you on your jolly way. From a mission name I was able to find out you were on mars at some point and I was also able to tell that I was in hell in some parts of the game. There's a story that has to do with argent energy and how the Doomguy wants to destroy it but the robot guy doesn't. That's pretty much it. I wasn't invested in the story one bit which is pretty disappointing for me.

I was actually ready to just lay the game down and shrug it off after the first half hour. I wasn't having any fun whatsoever. When I finally got a few more weapons, I started getting the appeal though. It is very fun to jump around and shoot enemies while breaking every speed limit on the planet. (Does mars have speed limits?). This fun, however died down after 2 hours of doing it. It just basically is the same thing over and over again. Go in an arena, "rip and tear" and press "E" to activate a context action that probably has the guitars shredding with lots of explosions. That just doesn't... cut it for me. after 3 hours of playtime I was just waiting for it to end, and thankfully it did after 3 more hours. That makes 6 hours of total playtime for the main campaign. I'd love to talk more about gameplay but there isn't more to it. There are a few jump n' run sections which aren't very fun and that's it.

On the technical side, this game is a marvel. The graphics look great, it gives you tons of options you can tweak for performance and visual fidelity. On top of that, this game runs like a charm. There were no crashes or performance issues whatsoever in my playthrough. It even gives you a few extra options to choose your rendering style. You have the choice between "Default", "Cinematic" and the one I used; "Gritty". So from a technical standpoint, this game really is one of the best. I did have some problems with triggers not working correctly after killing every enemy in an arena. Sometimes the door that was supposed to open, wouldn't open. So I had to restart from the last checkpoint. It got very annoying the third time it happened and onward.

I'm sure there are people that enjoy this game, if you like this particular type of game, that is. It is very simple and straightforward. Gameplay definitely is the focus here but it isn't nearly entertaining enough for me to keep doing the same thing for 6 hours. If you're looking for a short adrenaline ride to hell, give this a shot but be prepared to turn your brain off and just enjoy it like you'd do a summer action movie. If you expect anything more, you'll be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a scene in the movie "Watchmen" where one of the main charcaters, Rorschach, defends himself in a prison cafeteria and proclaims to the audience "I'm not locked in here with you; you're locked in here with me!" That pretty much sums up the DOOM experience from the first scene to the very last.

If you're in the market for a "shoot anything that moves" FPS, this is it. There's basically no story to speak of but there are lots of supporting lore in the form of data logs which can be read while you're taking a break from ripping apart some poor demon's jaws. It's pretty much - see demon, decimate demon. Rinse and repeat.

You may think this would get boring after a while but it doesnt. There are weapon mods that change how each weapon behaves, there are unlockable runes which alter the game play altogether and there are melee "finishing moves" that change up pending on what and which direction the killing blow is initiated. This mixed in with some suicidal demons hellbent (no pun intended) on pummeling your corpse into powder makes for some exciting gameplay.

This is a must play for any FPS fan - especially so for original DOOM fans.

Positives:
+ Immersive environment
+ Upgradeable weapons / armor
+ Runes to enhance / modify playstyle
+ Various melee "finishing moves"
+ Soundtrack
+ Secrets


Negatives:
- Some pacing issues between "arenas" - especially with rune trials. Not to give too much away but you can get "stuck" on a trial and this really breaks the tempo of the core game.
- Parts of the map become inaccessible after a set point in progression. Be aware, completionists!
- I personally found the map interface to be janky and confusing. Luckily, this isn't critical to the core game but if you're trying to get 100% secrets/items, for example, this feature is necessary and navigating the map controls were confusing and unintuitive.
