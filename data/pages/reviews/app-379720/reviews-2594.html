\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood but has some major problems.

It's faithful to the original Doom's run and gun style, but it has some really weird problems. It would get 10/10 for gameplay if it had a proper save system.

Inventory:
Right at the beginning of the game, it says F1 and F2 are used to switch inventory items, and LCtrl is to use items. There is also an upgrade tree for inventory items. I'm currently 8 hours into the game and I have never found an inventory item. I'm still hoping I eventually find one.

Saving:
There's no way to manually save the game. This is a problem because the auto saves are extremely far apart. Dying can easily set the player back by 30 minutes. All of the found secrets are lost, all of the enemies need to be killed again, all of the items need to be picked up again. Although the game has a lot of secrets and areas to explore, the game severely punishes players for exploring. You might die because Doom Guy didn't grab onto a ledge you expected him to grab, and now you're back to where you were 30 minutes ago. This forces an ultra conservative play style that clashes with the intended run and gun theme of the game. Imagine playing the original Doom where there is no saving or loading, and dying sent you back to the start of the episode. This entire game is like that.

Data Corruption:
The game crashed when I was about 2 hours into playing, and my save file was corrupted. Since there is no manual saving, and the auto save only keeps 1 save state, this means a single crash can wipe out all of your save data. All of your secrets are gone, all of your little doom guy trophies are gone, all of your upgrades are gone.A few people on the internet have complained about losing save data when they're deep into the game, and those people are justifiably angry as hell. This also means you shouldn't play Doom if your computer has any stability problems or if your neighborhood has power problems. If you get a blue screen or a power failure while playing Doom, there's a good chance all of your data will be corrupted. It's just bad game design to not allow manual saving.

Difficulty:
The game's difficulty seems to be all over the place and largely based on luck. It has happened multiple times where I'll get stuck on some imaginary bump on the ground and a hell knight kills me in 1 hit. Timing enemy projectiles is nearly impossible because the timing appears to be random. Sometimes they shoot right away, sometimes they don't. Sometimes they aim at where you currently are, and sometimes they aim at where you are going to be. There is no way of knowing where or when a projectile will be at any given time, so it turns much of the combat into cover based shooting.

Graphics:
It has that problem where the graphics are very ..... vague. You'll find yourself in a brown environment with brown ground, brown sky, and the enemies are brown. You're never really certain if you are facing an enemy or not. It seems like most modern games are infected with this kind of visual cancer where a game is completely unplayable if the monitor has any glare on it or if one plays in a room with no curtains. Do you think the original Doom would be a commercial success if the enemies were exactly the same color as the wall textures?\t\t\t\t\t
