\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI usually don't like FPS games, but I was running out of games so I decided to try this one out. I am really glad I did because this was a great experience.

The main difference between this game and most FPS games is that the "bullets" that enemies are shooting are slow. That means you can dodge enemy attacks. Not only CAN you dodge enemy attacks, but you HAVE to dodge enemy attacks if you want to succeed in this game. You can't just sit one corner of the screen shooting down enemies as they approach. One reason you can't do this is because there are a LOT of enemies in every battle (like 20+ enemies) and you'll get overwhelmed if you're playing so passively. Another reason you can't do this is because enemy attacks are designed to punish you for doing this. For example, Imps, one of the most basic enemies in the game, will shoot fireballs that will CURVE to hit you even if you're in cover, while not exposing the Imps to damage. A lot of enemies have attacks like this, so it's a seriously bad idea to sit in one place.

Instead, you're supposed to be constantly running around dodging attacks while at the same time killing enemies. You're not playing as the prey who has to cower and hide from the demons. You're the predator who has to seek out and kill these demons with extreme prejudice >:D . The developers have done an excellent job of designing various mechanics so that this fast-paced "run around shooting enemies" game feels as smooth and fun as possible.

In regards to the dodging of attacks, it's actually decently challenging. You can't just run around mindlessly and expect to not take damage. You have to keep in mind the enemies you're facing and the type of attack they're using and dodge accordingly. For example, some enemy attacks will fire in the area you're running to, so if you keep running you're going to get hit. Therefore, in order to dodge you have to either stop for a brief period of time and change directions. Other attacks, you have to go TOWARDS an enemy to dodge them. Other attacks you have to double-jump in order to dodge them. So basically, dodging is fairly involved and keeps the game challenging and interesting.

In regards to the gunplay, everything's designed to make it as easy to hit enemies as possible while running around like crazy:
\t There's no recoil to any weapons.
\t There's no reloading.
\t The aiming reticle (size of your attacks) is huge so you're almost always going to hit the enemy as long as you're aiming in their general direction.
\t Enemies get stunned by your attacks. In order to stun an enemy, you basically have to hit them. The basic enemies can get stunned with almost any weapon whereas the later enemies will require more hard-hitting weapons in order to stun them. This encourages you to keep shooting at enemies and not care about landing perfect shots (i.e. head shots) since it makes dodging easier (i.e. you can stun an enemy to interrupt their attacks).
\t Enemies can be "glory-killed". In order to glory-kill an enemy, you have to get their health down very low. Then you have to go up close to them and hit the action button. This causes you to brutally kill them (think God of War) and you're GUARANTEED to get health drops (with an upgrade, you can also get armor). This is what allows the game to be constantly fast-paced. Because it's about dropping in 20+ enemies for you to kill while dodging their attacks, you're going to take SOME damage. So glory-killing allows you to refill your health as you're killing demons. Even better, the demons drop more health the lower the health you have. This was an excellent design decision by the developers.
\t You have a chainsaw weapon that can be used to instantly kill an enemy in melee range. When it's used to kill an enemy, the enemy drops a large amount of ammo. The stronger the type of demon, the more ammo you need in the chainsaw in order to be able to kill it. Therefore, you actually want to use the chainsaw on small enemies in order to re-fill your ammo. This allows you to refill your ammo as you're killing demons. Again, another great design decision by the developers.
\t You have a "BFG" weapon that basically kills all enemies on the screen. While it has limited ammo (3), it is very useful in the later levels of the game where you can be facing like 50+ enemies.

So both the defensive (dodging enemy attacks) and offensive aspects of this game are designed in a great way. The level design is also great because you're not just facing enemies in a corridor like most single-player FPS games. Instead, levels are more like squares with a decent amount of verticality to them (i.e. you can jump and grab a ledge to go to the 2nd floor in order to run away from enemies on the ground floor). Later in the game, you also have portals that can take you to the opposite side of the map. So even the level design is top-notch and is always interesting and unique.

The gunplay feels great. Because glory-killing can only be done in melee range, there's a lot of emphasis on killing enemies in short range. Therefore, the shotgun really shines in this game. Not only that, but all the weapons are unique and great to use. While you do have the standards weapons such as the pistol, shotgun, and assault rifle, you also have a minigun, plasma rifle, rocket launcher, and the gauss cannon (my personal favorite). Later on in the game, you will be faced with enemies that you're going to want to stay the away from and kill from range as much as possible. So the game is always challenging. There's also 3 boss battles in this game. They're all fun to fight and have unique attacks that require you to dodge attacks that you've never had to dodge in that certain way before.

My biggest gripes with this game come from the platforming and the progression system. For some reason, the developers decided to put platforming in this game. While that gives you something to do apart from killing demons (which I don't know who asked for), it becomes a problem when it gets in the way of killing demons. And it happens a LOT. This is because a lot of the levels are high up in the air (because of the platforming sections) so that if you fall, you die. I remember one time I killed all but one demon, missed a platforming jump and died, and I had to fight all the demons all over again. It was a pain in the ass -_-. I appreciate the platforming when it allows the level design to be vertical, but not when it leads to instant-deaths.

The progression system is kind of annoying because it detracts from the gameplay. You can upgrade your suit by exploring and finding tokens. That's fine. You can also get 3 passive abilities by completing "rune trials" that provide unique challenges. That's also fine. You can upgrade your weapons by completing various tasks in order to get tokens. THIS is NOT fine, because these tasks are annoying. For example, in every level the more demons you kill, the more tokens you get. In 2 of the levels, I couldn't get the last token because apparently I didn't kill some demon. I spent like 5 minutes exploring trying to find them but to no avail. This was annoying. Also, you can get tokens by completing arbitrary tasks in every level such as "kill 3 demons in 1 explosive barrel". The problem is that demons don't respawn, so if you want the token you have to focus on trying to find areas where you can complete the objective instead of focusing on just killing the demons however you want. This detracts from the gameplay and focus on something that's not particularly fun.

Overall, I really enjoyed this game and this is probably my favorite FPS in regards to gameplay (Bioshock is my favorite in regards to story). While the platforming and progression system annoyed me at various points, it can't be denied that the developers did a great job designing a fast-paced single-player FPS experience. Looking forward to Doom Eternal :)\t\t\t\t\t
