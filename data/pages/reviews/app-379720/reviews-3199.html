\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is awesome. Even at full price is worth every cent.
This remake of the classic shooter finally came back to its roots with mindless killing of monsters, adrenaline pumping gameplay and unrelenting action. The monsters are smart and most encounters feel like multiplayer with mobile monsters that shoot on the run, climb walls or charge in to melee range depending on what they "feel" doing. Thus every encounter feels different and even the same encounter play significantly different it played again. I finished the game 3 times and I am still playing it.
You are an unnamed warrior that is to demons what demons are to normal people. Yep, hell is scared of you and with good reason.
The story is nice but secondary to the gameplay which is fluid and non stop action like old kung fu movies. You have a variety of weapons and you are encouraged to switch often so as to bypass their short cooldown/reload time. You have to constantly keep moving, shooting, dodging, changing weapons and charging in for melee kills. Every melee kill feels useful as it drops some health and also awesome with various gruesome animation of enemy dismemberment (one animation is pulling a monsters hand off and beating it to death with its own hand).
A typical attack goes like this: trigger monster teleport by pulling an organ out of an organic teleporter, shoot large damage weapon, launch grenade, change to another weapon and shoot it, watch out for the blue flash of a dazed monster, grin as you melee it for health/chance of ammo drop and sheer pleasure, change weapon, kill, maim, explode, jump, dodge, shoot... oops out of ammo, take out the chainsaw and split that monster open to replenish ammo, get back to killing.
There is a heavy vertical component to the gameplay as climbing and jumping makes dodging a lot easier. There is also a double jump mechanic that feels good and integrates with the need for agile gameplay either for evasion or getting to a vantage point or simply getting a drop melee special kill.
You can unlock special skills for the weapons by finding upgrade robots and upgrade those skills further by investing upgrade points. Once fully upgraded you can unlock another "ultimate" skill by performing certain feats (for example kill 3 monsters with one shot on several occasions). These feats are related to the ultimate skill so that achieving them will actually help using the ultimate better. The upgrade points are obtained by finding secrets, performing feats specific to each level and killing monsters.
You also have equipment items: two grenade types (damage and life leech) and a hologram decoy. You can also upgrade you character in various areas, for example take less or no damage from explosive barrels, climb faster, enhance equipment items etc.
The game is unforgiving on higher difficulty. If you stop moving you die, if you don't anticipate correctly monster movement you die, if you get stuck because mobile monsters surrounded you... you die, use the wrong weapon (like a rocket launcher in close quarters) you die, allow summoner monsters to live too long and you get overwhelmed and you die.
There is a variety of enemies with different attacks: melee, shooting, area of effect, launching slime that leaves a puddle with damage over time effect, summoners of other monsters, invisible monsters. Monsters have their own personality and if you can get them to attack each other they will fight each other to their death.
The NPCs are memorable with one of them being an AI that... well no, I won't spoil it. And no, it's probably not what you think.
You can find all sorts of goodies in secret areas and also entirely new levels for the classic Doom. Yes! you can play the old Doom and part of Doom 2 in this new engine.
The engine is smooth and it ran for me decently on a machine that was under the requirements listed. The only loading times are between levels and there is no framerate drop.
Enjoy the game!\t\t\t\t\t

DOOM (2016)
------------------------------

iD Software and Bethesda revive the DOOM Franchise, and bring back an engaging shooter based around killing demons from hell.

Campaign

So far, I have played the campaign, experimented with SnapMap, and gotten a few nice frags in multiplayer. At this point I can honestly say that I've really enjoyed what I have played currently and will continue to play. I haven't accumulated that many hours in the game however, I can say from what I have achieved, I have enjoyed my experience throughout. I will probably update my review of the game as I go on. DOOM achieves the atmosphere that they were going for, properly acknowledging the world that they wish to take people into. From the OST, to the spectacular visuals regarding the blood that sprays from the demons you kill. The game's story is definitely not something to buy the game over however, it does support the fact that they didn't intend to create a story-rich world. The lore in-game provides structure to those who wish to read more information about the game world. While I personally didn't care for the lore, after reading a few of the excerpts I had discovered, I saw the effort that was put into place so that those who care could have a spot to learn about the world. Level design seems incredible so far and I was surprised by the multi-level minimap. There is a lot of in-depth parts to the game world, including many secrets and collectibles, and the flow within the levels providing different ways to take on the demons. Glory kills provide a takedown for enemies that are close to death for a reward of bonus pickups. They have unique animations depending on the circumstances of how you initiated the takedown. After awhile, they began to feel kind of stale and repetitive but, they still were worth initiating due to the rewards provided. I am currently playing on "Hurt me plenty", which is the normal difficulty. I played the first level on Nightmare difficulty, and glory kills have proven more useful at the higher difficulty due to the fact that the demons hit much harder, and taking them out faster is much more beneficial. The weapons that I have played with so far have all been fun to use against the endless hordes from hell. The upgrading system provided a way for players to pick between a path that better supported their playstyle. The challenge system created a few unique goals to attempt during the levels, in exchange for some upgrade points towards making DOOM Guy even stronger. The scoring system that gives upgrade points for killing demons provides even more reason towards exploration and hunting down every last demon. Overall, my current experience with the campaign has been very enjoyable and provides plenty of reason to come back and play the game on a higher difficulty.

Multiplayer

My multiplayer experience has been overall meh. I enjoyed playing the game's campaign more because the multiplayer felt kind of unbalanced. Not only did I struggle to find a match at first but, I was lost in the frantic action of players fragging each other, and the demons raining hell down upon others. A few of the demons felt a little strong. In the matchs I played, I found a high level player that understood how to fully utilize the demonic powers that they had possessed. In one game, there was a demon that lept from wall to wall, one-shotting any players caught in it's path. My team was also struggling greatly with taking down this beast of a player. In my opinion, the multiplayer in it's current state is hardly a reason to purchase the game.

Overall

I reccomend DOOM for the campaign, but I feel like if you want to purchase it for the multiplayer, you might be a little disappointed in what you find. I didn't cover SnapMap in my review because I don't think I am knowledgable enough about the tool.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is quite simply amazing. There is no telling how many times I've beat the original DooM across multiple engines (Brutal DooM was very nice). Weirdly enough, this 2016 game with state-of-the-art graphics and vastly different mechanics still manages to evoke just enough of the original to put me back into the feeling I had the very first time I played DooM. The maps are designed very well and remind me of old iD games. This isn't a CoD "run to this checkpoint and press E to watch another cutscene". No, there is real depth to the levels, and they beg you to explore them throughly.

I'm not sure if this is what the guys at iD would have made had the technology existed when the original came out, but it definitely is worthy of the franchise. More so than anything that has come out since DooM II. I give this two severed thumbs up!\t\t\t\t\t
