\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS REVIEW IS NOT INCLUDING DLC OR MULTIPLAYER

It's been a while since I beat this, but the memories are still there.
The story to this game was a tad bit of a let down. Not to say the game wasn't entertaining, but it's not anything blowing your mind.
Without a doubt, the music is the best part of this game. It's tied with Payday 2's soundtrack. It makes you feel like a legit badass, and it keeps you playing.
Combat in this game was good, but it is insanely repetitive. The gore system can make up for it, but it's pretty much "clear a room, go down a hallway, repeat".

75 / 100

This game definitely deserved that "BOOM" Steam Award thing. It's definitely one the goriest games out there, but its repetition can become a major problem. It's a game worth trying, but get it during a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

These feats of skill typically revolve around killing a certain amount of enemies with a particular weapon before a timer expires, and completing them unlocks new traits that you enable in one of three rune slots. My favorite runes focused on enhancing movement, allowing me to pull off glory kills quicker, move faster for a short time after performing a glory kill, and have more control in the air while double jumping.

This is a single-player campaign game, Doom is a welcome return to form for the genre-defining franchise. By sticking to time-worn traditions in all the right places and deftly employing some modern flourishes, id has rediscovered what made this demon slaying series so successful.
