Argent energy is described as an "unlimited energy resource" that is extracted directly from Hell itself. As you may have guessed, tampering with Hell is not a good idea. Olivia Pierce becomes influenced by Hell, and opens a rift between their world, and Mars, and in a similar manner to the Resonance Cascade from Half-Life 1, demons begin warping into Mars, and basically everyone is ♥♥♥♥♥♥. So in a nutshell, its your job to rip and tear through all of these demons, and put a stop to all of it.

The level design is flawless. You really feel immersed in these environments. They also did a really good job with their "Level of Detail" system (lowering the detail of models and geometry that are far away), you can't even notice when they change. These levels are not linear by any means, you can find dozens of secrets by exploring the area. They even have these cute Doomguy action figures that you can collect, and these "Praetor Tokens" that can be retrieved from the dead corpses of Elite Guards, which allow you to upgrade your suit's abilities. There are also these "Rune Trials" where you get to complete these specific challenges in order to unlock Runes, that give you special powers, and these secret levers in every map, that open up a room from the original DOOM to be explored, which then allows you to play the actual classic level from the menu later on.

One of the other things that the game has introduced, is the "Glory Kills" feature. While some of the animations for it are a little bit repetitive, they are incredibly satisfying and dynamic. When an enemy is at critical health, they will act stunned, sort of like Mortal Combat, and then when you look at a certain part of the enemy and press F, you will perform a badass animation of you basically tearing the demon to shreds. The animations are quick, and to the point, they don't take you away from the action any longer than a second, and it grants you with health, so theres a plus to it.

I've been writing for awhile and theres still much more to talk about, but I'll come back to it later. Hopefully this has at least made you interested to go and give the game a try. Go check out the demo!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis Review is for Single Player Campaign Only:
Having played every DOOM Game in the Series, this one is more like the original. Fast Paced, Intense Action.
I found that the gameplay was done quite well with Mouse and Keyboard and pretty intuitive and easy to learn. The game does notify you when you need to learn a new technique etc. The Map functionality and the Archive for all the beasties in the game was a nice addition. The addition of Secrets, although not too terribly hard to find, are also welcome.
Armor and Weapon upgrades are a really nice addition, fun to fiddle around with and attempt to achieve bonus functionality. Also, a must have for fighting the final boss.
The game on Normal mode was not very challenging, except a few areas. If you are a FPS fan, you may seriously want to consider playing at a harder level than "Hurt Me Plenty".
That being said, it took me several tries to get past the final boss which is an excruciating test of dexterity, accuracy and timing with the right weapons.
Overall, I would recommend this game for anyone wanting a fast shooter with lots of gore, but limited storyline (as opposed to DOOM 3) in the style of the Original DOOM!\t\t\t\t\t
