\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI used to hate on this game nonstop back when I played it on console. It was very clunky, the controls felt slidey to me, and I felt the pace of the game was slower than Doom 3 at times. As much as I loved Doom 3, the classics always were incredibly fun to me. The high action pace, the constantly playing bit music, the noises, even today the game holds up to me in terms of fun. When this game was releasing, I was actually really excited, the promise of a game made in the original games set up. And when my dad went out and bought it for the PS4, I hopped on, played it, and was sorely disappointed. Ambience and a story that gets forced into your face (Which honestly isn't that great), and fights that ended up cutting off the good music right as you beat the enemies and then throwing you back into that ambience. I don't think of Doom games as ambience filled, I think of them as a high action packed music blasting heavy metal demon exploding gameplay. Yet, I never feel that with this game. Recently, when I finally got a PC, I was able to play this game at a constant 60 FPS and actually enjoyed myself. The puzzles of finding secrets are pretty good, even though mostly obvious if you go through the levels right. The combat is fluid, though I could do less with the brutal finishes that are very repetitive. The upgrade system I'm not a fan of, as I could care less about all that and just want to relax in blowing stuff up. The music is annoying, and I could do with less ambience. But if we are going off of gameplay and such, this game is really cheap for a 12 hour long story. Don't play this game for Doom, play this game to experience it as its own. Though, if anyone has any idea where to find mods to play the original DOOM music over the ambience, please message me or something, I definitely need it.\t\t\t\t\t
