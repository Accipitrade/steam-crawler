\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is too much fun.

I've played a bit of the original doom games, but i have played and completed the original doom on IOS (dont laugh please) and have played a metric butt-load of brutal doom, which i still love very much.

i was very sceptical of the new doom title ever since the first trailer was released, and my skepticism increased even more when i played the... lukewarm multiplayer beta. Now that the game has released, i can safley say that those worries and doubts have been completley diminished by what is a stellar first person shooter, worthy of the doom title.

Doom builds on what has already been established by the past 20 years of doom games, paying homage to the series when necessary while adding new and exiting mechanics that seperate this game from the other titles in the series. one of the most noteworthy changes to combat is the new glory kill system, which allows players to stagger enemies, get close to them, and begin to rip and tear them to tiny pieces for health and ammunition. this new mechanic keeps you in the action, and lessens the need to run from combat to find armor shards and medkits. this offers a great risk-reward system of attemting to close the gap between you and a horde of monsters for health, or to fall back and avoid further damage.

the chainsaw was introduced in the first trailer caused alot of worry and controversity, and for good reason because it seemed to just be a simple 1-click kill weapon that played a long, boring cutscene of Doom guy ripping and tearing an enemy to bits. thankfully, the chainsaw has been very well implimented, and rewards its use with a shower of health and ammunition, at the cost of fuel for the chainsaw, which is very hard to find.

the level design is better than ever with giant, labrynthian levels with tons of nooks and crannies to explore and tons of different paths to take towards the destination. due the larger and more complex level design, with a greater emphasis on verticality, the map has been updated to include a 3d model of the map which is easy to read, so even players that have poor navigation skills can keep track of where they are.

another fantastic feature is the game's built-in map creator called Snapmap, which allows players to build any level they imagine with the tools the game gives you. you can upload and download any map you want, but keep in mind that a seperate account is required to upload and download maps. Co-op is avalible in snapmap, but not in the singleplayer campaign which is perfectly okay.

I havent played much of the multiplayer, but it is pretty good. dont expect the deepness or complexety found in the likes of CS:GO, Quake, or Reflex, instead expect a fun distraction from the main meat and potatoes of the game. (I may update this portion of the review as i put in more time in the multiplayer, which i intend to do.)

this game is fantastic, and is also a great chalange on the higher difficulties, demanding proper movement, accuracy and reflexes to combat the forces of hell. This game is a love letter to doom fans everywhere, and although some extreme doom zealots may be disgusted by the improvements made in this game, others will be blown away by one of the best FPS games in years, next to games like Wolfenstien the new order and Dying light. this game is a must buy from me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Fight like hell is an understatement. You will start immediatly crushing skulls in the first seconds of your awakening. The game itself isn't very deep story wise but it doesn't have to be. They took more of a badass Kill. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. Everything. approach which is completey fine by most people including me. The environment/maps themselves are beautiful and interesting... beautiful being : covered in dead bodies, pentagrams, and hordes of pissed off demons. The game is challenging, exciting, ridiculously gorey, very violent, and never ever boring. I personally am one of the few people who actually enjoyed the previous game taking a more horror approach to the series. That being said this game perfectly blends the original doom games with a little bit of that horror from the 3rd and a touch of modern shooter features. The graphics are amazing and the level designs are even better. I highly reccomend this game to everyone. Even if you don't necessarily like super fast gameplay I would give this one a shot. You won't be dissapointed.

The only con I would say is the sort of short campaign.. If you think around 10 hours is short that is.. \t\t\t\t\t

The fact you are forcing me to play gamemodes that I don't want, has earned you my negative review again. Never force a player to do something, just to spread popularity to unpopular parts of your game.



Edit: At some point, an update allowed me to run the game and play it; and oh boy did I play it.

DOOM is a wonderful modern reimagining of the classic that I played with my older brother in the mid 90s. Combat feels solid, fluid, and fast. The game has just enough story to drive it forward without feeling like an interactive film experiment (I'm looking at you COD, Metal Gear).

My only complaint is that the game's performance feels sabotaged on older Nvidia hardware, and I know I'm not the only one who believes this. My GTX 770 Should at least be powerful enough to play on Medium or low with a constant 60fps, but I find it at times dipping into the mid 20s on the absolute lowest settings, at 720p which is even more disgusting; Some people are finding that their 780 Ti cards can't even handle the game. Not upset however, because I purchased a new GTX 1080 (not the crappy founder edition!) to power my DOOM experience.



ORIGINAL REVIEW:

I payed $60 for a lead paperweight. The game crashes before so much as showing me an intro video, and without giving any errors or logging anything in the windows event viewer. I tried with and without the latest Nvidia drivers, to no avail. I'm running on Win10, setting my compatibility mode to Win8 or 7 allows the game to start and output sound, but I am left with a blank screen.

Fix your damn game and I'll change my review to a positive one.\t\t\t\t\t
