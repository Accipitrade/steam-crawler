\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM takes me back to a time where games could be games. Games used to be able to have ridiculous, unexplained, crazy things thrown into the mix for the fun of it, like powerups. Nowadays, there's an expectation for games to have a cohesive world with some amount of explanation for the game mechanics. DOOM does a beautiful job of working the formula into current game expectations, while throwing in some really juicy "game-y" elements that are just fun.

The game design of DOOM is very thoughtful. It has a beautiful way of starting you off in a more open environment that people are used to in modern-day shooters, but slowly adding in surprises and new environments that are more claustrophobic and close-quarters more similar to old shooters. It coaxes you into becoming a rampaging maniac rather than dodging and hiding and taking shots right before ducking behind cover. It becomes crazier and crazier, the stakes skyrocket higher and higher, the heavy music accents the intensity and fuels your blood-lust, yet as it becomes so chaotic, it doesn't necessarily become overwhelming and you still feel reasonably in control in this mess. It is a very empowering and badass feeling.

It's so refreshing to see this formula brought into a big AAA title like this, and I only wish there were more games like DOOM that really break the mold of the genre and just make the game FUN.

Try this game out.\t\t\t\t\t
