\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery well optimized from a performance viewpoint, very poorly optimized from a perspective of how much it offers in terms of experience.

It runs great with no so great hardware.
It doesn't offer many aspects other technologies like that of Crytek offers, immersive surfaces, destructible environments, open areas, etc.

The solo campaign is cool and lots of secrets, runes, mods for weapons, a good rooster of demons and bosses, long enough, lots of action.

The downside for me is the policy of not releasing more content in he form of expansions, like Resurrection of Evil a decade ago.

MP has been getting all the love, I'm not interested in that part, plus the fact that you have to restart the game to switch to either MP or Snap Map is a big showstopper for me. And it takes loooong to start, big point down.

Purchase it if you got the hardware and you can find a good discount.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is... I don't really have proper words to describe it. It is not just good it looks too good. The amount of details and customization in this game is beyond comprehension of a human being. The game play is fast passed and dynamic as it should be in a classic DOOM game. The worst part though... it is so dynamic and fast passed that all those details do not matter that much... Your character has more than a hundred well made taunts, though it feels like no one will ever see them because the battle is too dynamic to stay and watch... It seems the game lacks some good sound tracks and weapon sounds seems to be weak. It also runs well on my hardware(well optimized) and runs in Linux through staging wine out of the box.

EDIT:
These were my thoughts about the game when I played only multiplayer and did not check the campaign... Now, when I played the campaign I can say it is absolutely amazing and I am happy I bought this game, because it worth more than its price. It is done well and has lots of replayability. This game is everything a good FPS is. I mean it and want to clarify why exactly I think DOOM is much better than other shooters. Your guns, you have lots of them and each has 2(not 1!) alternate fire modes. Ammo, it is balanced, so you will HAVE to use different guns and you might to run out of ammo if you miss too much. Your character is already quite powerful at the start, and there are lots of upgrades, that you have to earn before using. Upgrades are really nice, but if you miss them all you still can complete the game, it will be just less epic and slightly harder. Level design is really good. Most FPS lack level design, DOOM has it all(it is not just better than others, it is really good by itself). There are secrets, well hidden secrets. Some secrets are so well hidden that it is still a challenge to find them even when you have an upgrade that allows to locate them. There are many ways to kill and there are many ways to die, so you will not get tired of repeated animations. You can perform different fatalities depending on surroundings, targeted part and angle of attack. I have played Crysis games before and thought that suite is so powerful, it is not... Preator suite is! It is like "Maximum Everything" on Crysis suite. You are fast, strong, tanky, and have jet boots(and you can get even faster, stronger and tankier)!!! You are that powerful to tear enemies apart, so are they if you make a mistake! Few seconds ago you've smashed that poor imp's head into a create and now you are on your back and some other imp tears limb by limb from you. Have you seen a video "What if DOOM was made today"? That is not about this game! DOOM makers know what they are doing: they made great games in the past and remind us what games should be like even today.\t\t\t\t\t
