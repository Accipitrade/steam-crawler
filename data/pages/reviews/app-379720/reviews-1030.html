\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 2016, this is it. in 2020 you should know what to expect. I have played the multiplayer quite a lot which is excellent area style shooting, you can even turn in to so of the key demons as power ups, which was a nice touch and is balanced OK all things considered. Sadly the games population is small now but you can still find matches.
Campaign is actually quite long, there is a story but its not in your face, I pretty much skipped it and still had a basic idea of what's going on. But the reason you want to play this is for the fast paced, intense, non stop action. The missions are well designed broken up in to several arenas which are well designed, especially once you get to Hell. Personally I had to try 3 seprate times to enjoy the campaign, it does start slow and basic, just keep playing until you have done a few missions and things really get fun, by the last third of the game the difficulty really picks up and you will be enjoying the action for sure.
I will say running on my more than capable laptop GTX 1060 8GB graphics card, 16 GB RAM, intel core i5-8300H CPU @2.30 GHz (8 CPUs) the game does struggle, it seems many have had issues with NVIDIA card so you may not get the best FPS on max settings. A shame this was never optimised. Over fun game buy it now its cheap, the story will link in to DOOM Eternal and the gameplay is mostly the same.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very straightforward and simple FPS game. No story ♥♥♥♥, no overbearing gimmicks. Just you, a lot of demons, a lot of guns and a lot of incentive to turn said demons into red mist using said guns. Glory Kills are a not very unique, but well implemented mechanic that rewards aggressive play with health back, and some very slick pre-animated take-downs that as comically over the top as they are brutal. They're a flashy and brutal expression of the player character's borderline slapstick hatred of demons that couldn't be more apparent if the game put in font 150 comic sans red text "THIS GUY HATES DEMONS BTW" on screen whenever you kill something. The game expertly walks a fine line between taking itself seriously enough to be a cohesive experience, but also doesn't take itself seriously in the slightest, being only one tiny nudge in the direction of self awareness away from rolling it's eyes at the player and breaking the 4th wall to acknowledge that it gives no ♥♥♥♥♥. The difficulty levels are well balanced to give different levels of challenge to players of all skill levels, and the levels are extremely open ended with well hidden secrets and easter eggs, with weapon upgrade points rewarded to encourage the player to find secrets. However I do think that the game becomes too easy once you reach the final levels and have everything upgraded, at which point everything becomes a cakewalk even on higher difficulties. The game also has issues with weapon balancing, the Super Shotgun, Gauss Cannon, and Rocket Launcher are all noticeably more powerful than the other weapons and stifle the competition. The game caters to both the maniacs who want to bounce from kill room to kill room like a crackhead tweaking in a dumpster and blitz through the game as fast as possible, while also catering to more methodical players who want to find the forbidden Happy Meal toys strewn throughout each level while also ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Hell with a shotgun.

It's not got the complexity, nor speed, nor ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ aggression of it's sequal, but regardless of that comparing the game to other FPS games on the market is like comparing F1 to a Fisherprice baby car race. This game is not about huddling behind a chest high wall with your thumb up your hole waiting for the indistinguishable samey enemy to finally poke it's head out from cover, it's about dancing in and out of reach of a 12 foot tall monstrosity with horns and an 8 pack while blasting it's chest out with shotgun blasts. Unlike other games, where you commit war crimes for breakfast but it's ok because in spunk gargle wee wee shooter land brown people are all very very bad terrorist people. DOOM has no need to justify it's violence. Demons are bad, Hell is bad, so kill them. It contrasts DOOM: Eternal with a much grittier atmosphere, pickups aren't brightly coloured pieces of dangerous confetti, they're actual ammunition and other pickups like guns and grenades are generally looted from horribly mutilated corpses in bloodstained cubby holes throughout the UAC. It also has a much more angry soundtrack in comparison, like way more angry, even DOOM: Eternal's angriest tracks don't come close to how ridiculous the likes of Flesh & Metal, the infamous BFG Division, and Rip & Tear are. And that's not to mention the absolute slab of raw anger and testosterone turned song that is Skullhacker.

This is the paragraph where I shill the soundtrack. It's very good. It's very appropriate to the game. It also builds up as the game goes, starting as low and simple mostly electronic tunes that echo through the carnage of the early level, before building into the complex, angry, and proudly bombastic metal cacophony of the later levels. The fame Mick Gordon received for this absolute gem of a soundtrack is more than well deserved, because it fits the game like a glove, adding so much depth and catharsis to the unrestrained carnage. Relocating a Demon's spine to it's ballsack just isn't the same without the backing of this masterpiece of a soundtrack that makes nutjock religious mothers all over the world tremble in fear.

DOOM 2016 is a celebration of what makes FPS games good. It pairs the best fundamentals of any shooter game in history with a tight gameplay loop that doesn't overstay it's welcome over the 6-12 hours of campaign in the game, on top of some interesting lore (that is entirely optional and accessed through codex entries), brilliant and subtle characterisation of the DOOM Slayer in particular, a fantastic soundtrack, and brilliant level design. Where DOOM 2016 shines is simplicity, you aren't bogged down by tactical decisions, or precise positioning, or advanced movement options. That's a negative for some people, but the absolute feeling of giddy joy when experiencing the power-fantasy that this game turns into in it's later sections is not matched elsewhere in the industry. The only FPS game out there that makes this game look tame is it's sequel, which is no knock on it, because this game makes CoD, Battlefield, Titanfall, and every other shooter game out there, AAA and indie alike look like a game of laser tag being played by toddlers. When this game dropped, it was daddy coming home and sitting all the little entitled runts of the modern FPS genre down and showing them how the ♥♥♥♥ you do make a FPS game. It wears it's over the top violence and soundtrack on it's sleeve like a badge of honour, and never has to shut up to let the gameplay do the talking, because it never started talking over it in the first place.\t\t\t\t\t
