\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had only really purchased this game because of the insanely good soundtrack. I knew I would enjoy the game itself, just not as much as I expected. This is probably one of the best FPS games I have played, but the music helps push it up.

I love that they included optional exploration bits. Finding secrets and collectibles. A lot of it don't really improve gameplay, they are there to find and give a reason to explore. Exploring the maps is very fun, the graphics are insane, and it runs very well on my machine. I am using an AMD R9 390 8GB along with an i7 4790K 4GHz.

As of posting this review I have only completed the campaign, I haven't done any of the arcade, multiplayer or the snapmap feature. I also need to revisit the campaign to get all the secrets, collectibles, etc.. I plan on putting more hours into this game very soon.

10/10, easy.\t\t\t\t\t

With that in memory, and no interest in multi-player, I waited quite a long time before I caved in and tried this new DOOM and I have to say that I'm a bit disappointed... I had high hopes since the two new Wolfenstein games were awesome, but this was not to be. Repetetive is the best word to describe DOOM...

If it'd been called Quake Arena 2.0 then it'd be a better description but DOOM it ain't if you ask me.

If you like multi-player bash 'em all then perhaps this is the game for you but the campaign was weak and frankly boring. Pity because the technical quality is outstanding and so is the graphics department!

Thumbs down for single player campaign, and that's how I score this game.\t\t\t\t\t
