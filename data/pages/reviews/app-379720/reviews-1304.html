\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying this game is like gliding naked through a sea of warm, smooth butter. Everything about it--the movement, the animations, the graphics, the music, the level design--just feels so, so GOOD. For pure fun and minute-to-minute enjoyment, it's a 10/10.

As an overall game, though, it's more of an eight due to a few irritating problems:

-The sound effects don't seem to do justice to the brutality of the gameplay. The guns sound watered down and the impact noises are underpowered in a way that defies the explosions of blood on screen. They aren't bad, they're just noticeably out of alignment with the rest of the game.

-The suit and weapon upgrades are almost completely superfluous after the midway point of the game. There are a few satisfying paths on the guns, and it's always nice to have more health or ammo, but many of the options are so "meh" that I eventually stopped spending points entirely. It was like shopping in a store that sells nothing but plain cardboard.

-Glory kill animations are incredible... the first hundred times you see them. After that, you realize how limited the animation pool really is. The 167th time you stomp on a demon's skull in exactly the same way, the system begins to lose its appeal.

-There are a couple of boss fights in the game that were bad enough that I almost quit playing. Doom has a great rhythm to it in general, flowing smoothly from area to area and fight to fight. So when you have to stop to dump 45,689 bullets into a monster in boring, uninspired, and unnecessarily frustrating boss battles, you really feel it. It's like being ejected out the front window of a Ferrari going 200 mph.

Other than these quibbles, though, Doom is fantastic. If you like shooters--or just pretty games that run great and require very little brainpower--you should give it a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
