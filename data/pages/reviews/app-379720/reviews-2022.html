The game keeps things interesting by limiting the ammo types you find, not so that you will have to hoard it and save it for when you really need it, but to make you use other weapons. It's hard to run around with the plasma caster without any ammo for it, y'know?
The difficulties offer a challenge to those who feel like they've done all there is to be done in the normal modes, and there's also an Arcade mode if you want to test your speed and skill against others.
It runs fantastically well at high graphic settings, and I have encountered VERY few bugs, for a game that has anything to do with Bethesda.

Finally finished DOOM so let's start with that!

DOOM is the perfect mix between old school shooters and new skills/upgrade system, there isnt so much so you get super confused but there is enough to make a difference, it wont interrupt the flow of gameplay or mass slaughter at any given time which makes the blending of both new and old amazing.

Weve seen a lot of games attempting to do FPS correct and trying to finetune it to perfection but I believe DOOM reached that level with this installment, I will, however, not return to it, which is a bit sad that there isnt much replayability unless you are a 100% achievement farmer which Im not (Honestly too many games to do that in every game) but it is a game worth getting for the singleplayer alone (I mean why else would you get a DOOM game if not for the singleplayer) especially considering that there is a sale right in writing moment where it is down to less than 7 euros like WHAT THE HELL!!!

Overall I had great fun with the game and got sucked into it quite a lot, the music, graphics, and general atmosphere really dragged me in and kept me stuck to my seat while I completed the game, storywise DOOM is well DOOM, daemons lose on Mars go kill them all so like the old days!

For sure a game I would recommend for people that love FPS games and is tired of all the newer games that are overcomplicated.\t\t\t\t\t
