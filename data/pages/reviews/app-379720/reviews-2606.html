\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM (2016) is a soft reboot (meaning, a new chapter for a new audience, but keeps the continuity and established universe of the previous entries) of the legendary first person shooter series. Now, I never played the original version of DOOM nor its sequels, rereleases, mods and whatnot, because I was young and not a PC player, but I was aware of its popularity and quality. It's literally part of gaming culture and history.

So when Bethesda and id software showed up on stage at E3 2015 with a game they simply called "DOOM", I raised an eyebrow and looked for reactions from my pals who did play the originals, and they were less than impressed. The game fell off my radar until a few days before release when Bethesda released a multiplayer beta that failed to impress people so hard it got one of the worst review scores in Steam's history.

Cue release day and reviews drop, and both the gaming press and public seemed to have done a complete 180 overnight because all of a sudden, DOOM was considered good. Scratch that, people called it a worthy successor, a refreshing shooter experience, and a masterpiece, and by the end of the year it would make it into many top 5 games of the year. I was intrigued, so I bought it during a sale around summer of 2016, around the same time I built myself a powerful new gaming computer, and gave it a shot.

And I wish I could swear openly in these review because holy ♥♥♥♥♥, nothing could have prepared me for the shotgun blast to the face this gem of a first person shooter this game is. DOOM (2016) is now one of my favorite games ever, in no small part thanks to its incredible single player campaign, but also a solid multiplayer, tons of post launch content, a high replayability, a stellar soundtrack and the ability to rip and tear like nothing else.
  • Fun, fast paced ultra violence: Could you believe it, a first person shooter in 2016 throwing every single design trend of the last fifteen years out of the window to focus on just one thing, namely fun ? Because DOOM (2016) is a joy to play, offering enough challenge to satisfy every kind of player, even the not-so-good-at-aiming ones like me, while delivering high speed action with a huge variety of weapons (Siege Mode Gauss Cannon FTW), tons of upgrades for exploring and completing challenges, a solid enemy pool that keeps you on your toes throughout the adventure, and the story elements really don't get in the way of the action aside from maybe two small sections that are a minute long at worst.

  • Gorgeous visuals: I really can't emphasize this enough, DOOM (2016) is downright gorgeous. It faithfully updates classic designs while incoporating new ones, and manages to make it all look consistent with a superb artstyle. But what really elevates it is just how superb the game looks, thanks to its very high resolution textures and assets, a carefully crafted rendering pipeline and a truckload of graphical options to choose from. The id Tech 6 engine runs beautifully on just about every system and I can't remember a single dip below 60fps in almost 50 hours of playtime.

  • Terrific soundtrack: Before this game, I had never heard of Mick Gordon, but now, I can't get enough of his work, and I was never a fan of the genre to begin with, that's how good the soundtrack is. Every track is excellent and makes you want to get out there and shoot demons until you're knee-deep in the dead. Better yet, the soundtrack evolves based on your performance in combat, and it's all seamless !

  • A solid multiplayer: Aside from the excellent single player campaign, there's also a multiplayer mode, which is nothing special, but I got 20 good hours out of it, thanks to a variety of modes, and a retooled progression system. After noticing the split playerbase, id software decided to make all of the DLC free in what they called Update 6.66, which retooled everything, from the progression system to the way players interact with each other, and also drastically decreased the size of the game to a more respectable 67GB, as opposed to the almost 100GB it was before. This update gave DOOM (2016)'s multiplayer a second life, and it was worth it, because I haven't had any issues finding matches since.
So here you have it, a breakdown of why DOOM (2016) is a fantastic experience, which you can get for a pretty good price nowadays. Multiplayer still has a decent population, the single player campaign is worth the price of admission alone, the soundtrack rocks your ears off and the gameplay is nothing short of superb. I can't recommend this game enough, and if you're still on the fence, it's time to pull the trigger. Rip and tear, baby !\t\t\t\t\t
