\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt actually took me a while to get around to finishing this, mostly me being way too lazy and dabbling on other games (and also entirely way too much TF2).

I can't really compare this game to the classic Doom games, given how I haven't played them at the time of writing, but let's leave that aside and get to it.

Doom 2016 is the franchise's return after 12 years of having been dormant (and several of those being developer hell, with a scrapped version being basically Call of Doom, thank f*ck it wasn't what we got), and boy, what a glorious, gory return it was. We have this game to thank for motivating stuff like DUSK, Ion Fury and Amid Evil.

Story

The story concerns you, Doom Slayer, awakening after a long slumber (In a way that is somewhat reminiscent of Caleb climbing out of the grave with raging morningwood) in the rundown UAC after the demons were released into the facility. You team up with Samuel Hayden, the head of the facility and its sentient AI, VEGA (Who is actually kind of a total bro) to get to the bottom of this and stop Olivia. But really, it's not the main focus of the game. You can also collect data logs scattered around in each map to learn more about the monsters, enviroments and characters.
The actual point of the game is, to quote Yahtzee, "YOU MAN KILL DEMONS GRUNT GRUNT FIRE HOT", which leads us to

Gameplay

It's a fairly faithful update of the classic style of shooters, which mostly emphasizes movement, a respectable arsenal of 9 weapons, all of which are very useful, dodging projectiles and prioritizing enemies according to their level of threat, as well as secret hunting. There are some updates to it, though, most of which are welcome enough, such as mods and upgrades for them, giving the combat and your playstyle a lot of variety, and equipments, like grenades (one of them which gives you HP back) and holograms.
The roster of enemies provides an enjoyable fight, and encourages you to always keep moving, as already stated. The standard recommendation is usually to play it in Ultra-Violence as to experience a hard but fair challenge. I completed it on Nightmare, and while it was a very fun time, it can be very punishing. One fireball from an Imp can easily one-hit you early in the game. However, if this kind of thing is your jam, go for it. Who am I to stop you?
From the third mission onwards, you also get optional challenges. Upon completion, grant you an extra point towards your weapon upgrades, with them being up to three per mission. They're not really necessary, the weapon upgrades aren't overall, but they do heighten the power of your arsenal considerably.
Finally, this installment of Doom brings forth runes. These provide passive bonuses and you can use up to three at maximum, although you will need to find a lot of them through secret-hunting in order to get these two extra slots. They can also be upgraded, but some of them can be a chore (I'm looking at you, Armored Offensive)

It's worth noting that, while I can't write enough about it, the music does tons to enhance the gameplay, especially in the heat of combat. Even for someone that isn't really into metal, the heavy djent/electronic mesh adds to the punch of your weapons and the general carnage you'll be ensuing onto.

Criticisms

Honestly, my criticisms really aren't much in the way of dealbreakers, but I feel the game does become a bit easy towards the end, though that might just be me. I also kind of dislike so many of the rune upgrades and optional challenges making Glory Kills a requirement. I personally don't have an issue with it, but I also would just prefer being able to shotgun a demon back to the lowest circles without finding that I can't fulfill these conditions, but again, they're optional, so at this point it's more nitpicking.
There's also a bit of repetitiveness in the color palettes of the game with reds, oranges and browns, reminding me a lot of Quake, particularly with the last color, but given the devs' statements on how much time they actually had to finish this, colors couldn't really be a priority, and it honestly doesn't detract much from the overall art direction of the game.

Conclusion

For ARS$370.00 (and even less on sales), this game is an absolute steal. The weapon mods, runes and overall secret hunting add a lot of replayability to it, and the core gameplay loop is solid.
If you have it already, then go play it and don't be a procrastinating imbecile like I was!\t\t\t\t\t
