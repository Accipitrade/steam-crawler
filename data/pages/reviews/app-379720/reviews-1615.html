The story is exactly what it needs to be, simple and congruent, giving you direction without getting in the way. Few games are as seamless in mixing the elements that make up a game, from level design to graphics to world building. Well, one seam might be the main menu, it's kinda janky, but once in the game, everything is more or less where it should be. There's not much else to say; outside of some minor gripes, everything about the campaign is great. The multiplayer and snap map components aren't nearly as well put together, though I never got much into them, they aren't what this iteration of Doom is about.\t\t\t\t\t
