\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 4 is one of the most pleasing surprises available to an fps fan like myself. The game was launched in 13th of may 2016. So, at the date of this review's release, wich is 19th of september, is DOOM 4 worth it's full price tag? I will answer this question by adressing my points about campaign, multyplayer and soundtrack.

Campaign:
THIS is the single reason you should buy this game. DOOM 4 has the BEST single player campaign i have played since Halo Reach back in 2010. The amount of satisfaction and pure gamplay that we wanted in a fast-paced game is all available in DOOM's campaign mode. I am 98% certain that if someone picks up this game, they will more than likely play it until the credits screen. And i am willing to bet, they will play the game again, either for the colectables, for completion or just because it's an amazing game.

Soundtrack:
There is a reason DOOM won the 2016 awards for the best soundtrack of the year. Raycevick has spoken about this topic and has an in-depth video about DOOM's soundtrack. You can watch it here:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7X3LbZAxRPE
In short, DOOM has one of the best soundtracks that complement it's gameplay.

Multiplayer:
If Bethesda would've nailed this one too, the game would gave been game of the decade. But, it isn't. DOOM's multiplayer is easily it's worst part. It's not THAT bad, it's just bad. Loadouts and progression-based unlocks, unbalanced weapon combination, overpowered demons and power weapons make DOOM's multiplayer quite a miss.

In Conclusion, DOOM 2016 is a jaw-dropping single-player experience combined with an briliant score that gives you an amazing time for the 20 Euros it costs. And unlike other games, i would recomend this game even if it was 50 Euros. It's that good.

Definitely game of the year 2016, and my personal pick for the best game of all time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me say up-front that this is not a review of DOOM - it can't be, because I never got to play it. This is a review of Bethesda's technical support, which might as well not be there for all it's worth.

I downloaded and installed the game, then ran it: so far, so good. Up comes the standard "you must accept the terms and conditions to continue" screen, with no fewer than four different agreements to accept. Now, unlike most people, I actually read every license agreement I'm required to accept, especially privacy policies, so I clicked the option to view one of the agreements... and nothing happened. Clicked on another - same thing. It didn't matter what I clicked on, nothing happened.

I exited (everything was actually running fine - I just couldn't view the agreements before accepting them) and sent a message to Bethesda tech support. I won't bore you with the detail of what followed - it would take too long... But suffice it to say that over a twelve day period I was shuffled through three different script drones, two of them allegedly "escalation team members", although I don't know how you could tell. I endured the endless scripted messages with their fake bonhomie, and complied with all the meaningless scripted support demands (send us a DxDiag, send us another DxDiag, send us an MSINFO, reboot your machine, update your drivers, reinstall the software, do a clean boot, run it as another user, check the steam cache - you know the drill) until the point where they basically demanded that I disable my firewall, at which point I had had enough. Refusing to disable my firewall was, I suspect what they were waiting for, because I immediately got the "well, if you're not going to co-operate with us, we can't assist you any more" brush-off, and that was the end of that. It took twelve days and twenty-two mail messages to get exactly nowhere.

This is a modern, powerful system with 84 steam games, none of which has any problems (even very recent ones like Prey). Furthermore, the problem is not a crash, and does not involve gameplay. Had I checked the "accept and continue" checkbox, I believe the program would have run. I even asked them to send me links to the agreements that I could read outside the program, but when they finally actually read that (I had to suggest it four times), I was told that that wasn't possible. Give me a break! A company the size of Bethesda doesn't publish its EULAs somewhere?

DOOM may be a very fine game - and I'm truly sorry that I can't play it... But this incident has produced some of the worst, most scripted, depersonalized tech support I have experienced in my 30 years in software, and I wanted to warn anyone else who might end up needing it what they are letting themselves in for.

All told, a very unsatisfactory experience.
