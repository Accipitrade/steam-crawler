But yeah, DOOM 2016 kicks monolithic amounts of ugly demon posterior, you don't really need me to tell you that. Fast-paced, hilariously gory, tons of secrets to discover and upgrades to choose from (possibly a few TOO many to be honest) and basically a game that encourages you to revel in the carnage of guilt-free slaughter as you massacre hellspawn. You still should play the originals, don't get me wrong, nothing substitutes, but this is still a great playable essay on the joys of pure, unrestrained violence.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt took 12.5 hours to beat the game on Ultra-violence casually going for collectibles and what not. It really was a blast playing through because the combat is ahead of it's time.

The variety and diversity of weapons is really impressive. You get to upgrade two upgrade trees for about every weapon which changes how the gun functions entirely. I say two because you can change the tree your weapon is using by the press of a single key.

I am normally not a collector, but it is relatively easy to find the codexes, which are essentially the lore. I found the lore to be intriguing and engaging as you realize that you're playing as a bad ass demon slayer.

There are of course other things the game does great, but those are the main points. I personally (being someone that typically doesn't replay single player games until a year or two later) wouldn't pay $20 for the amount of hours I got out of it, but it was on sale for $6 which is a steal (This is a review for the campaign. I have no intention of playing multiplayer). This game is for the collectors, demon slayers, and everything in between.\t\t\t\t\t
