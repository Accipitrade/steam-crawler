\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an amazing game, I don't understand why you're reading the review section, seriously go buy it now.







ok fine I'll do a review for you

So what's to like about this game? well there's fast-paced almost non-stop action that'll have your blood pressure spiking in ten seconds flat, the enemy destruction is glorious, the fact that you can blow apart enemies without having to use a grenade is awesome, the powerups are really cool (I particularly like the beserk powerup) The difficulty curve isn't too steep but steep enough to keep you on your toes, plenty of collectibles to find (I haven't found all of them yet but I did manage to stumble across a few) oh, I forgot to mention that you can PUNCH YOUR ENEMIES TO DEATH AND BATHE IN THEIR BLOOD!?!?!? Seriously though the glory kills are ' gorgeous man, it really shines with the beserk powerup, there's also challenges which give you upgrade points which you can spend to make your guns more badass, there's a snapmap and multiplayer to keep you interested after the campaign is over, and also BFG, 'nuff said.

'But what are the downsides?' I hear you ask, well, there are a couple, to start off, the upgrades feel like they don't do , (except for the mobile turret upgrade on the chaingun, that is pretty awesome) the glory kill animations do get kinda boring after a while, and the game ends up coming down to a kill/grindfest towards the end, which on top of that the ending feels like a let-down in terms of story, and from what I've heard the multiplayer is unbalanced, and the snapmaps aren't entirely golden either, and if your computer runs on lemons then good luck running this game. But despite all the negatives I've mention I'd still heavily recommend buying this game at least for the campaign, it's just too good of a game to pass up and not play, I've heard a lot of good things about the original doom, and it seems like they put everything good from the original and made it look and feel awesome, so well done devs, ya did good.

EDIT: after playing multiplayer & snapmaps for a little while I can confirm that it is a little unbalanced, partly because some guns are just OP, but mostly because the multiplayer plays like call of duty multiplayer, as in you can rank up and unlock new guns to choose from, giving players who have been playing the multiplayer much longer than others an even greater advantage on top of all the experience from playing the multiplayer for a while, now there is a beginner's lobby, but that place is a ghost town, I waited roughly 10 minutes in a beginner's lobby and only 2 maybe 3 people joined and left because the game wasn't starting. Even with the new maps and demons from the free (I think) DLC I still can't recommend buying the game for the multiplayer. Now for the snapmaps, after playing a few snapmaps I have to say that some of the community is pretty talented when it comes to making them, challenge may be a little lacking in some maps (by which I mean they just toss you on an open field and toss enemies at you) while others really do shine, all I can say for the snapmaps is only play the recommended ones, because the rest of them aren't all that good. again after all I've said I still recommend DOOM mostly for the campaign, and partly because of the snapmaps.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot even begin to describe just how intoxicating the sensation of power is in this game. At first I was skeptical, I wasn't 100% on the art style or demon design, and I thought the single player really might not stand up on its own. I was unbelievably wrong. This game goes above and beyond in making you feel like some ancient, terrifying harbinger of death that the demons were so frightened of, they sealed you away to never return. But return you have, and you don't care about anything or anyone in your way. Nothing destracts you from your singular goal of nightmarish slaughter and horrifying pain to be inflicted on the denizens of hell. The details really sell it. The demons act slightly afraid of you, the contempt you have for the things you interact with, down to the glorious glory kill system. What I thought was going to be a half baked preanimated takedown system wound up being one of the most entertaining parts of the game. Almost Bulletstorm style, there are real rewards to these quick, punchy, visceral finishers, and they actually transform the game from the traditional "backpedal and fire" into something that really permits you to just rampage like the reaper of blood that you're made out to be, by providing enough health to keep things flowing. The chainsaw is a great method of restocking the ammo supplies midbattle, further adding to the pace. Controls are tight and responsive, the shooting is rewarding and satisfying, the weapons are punchy and visceral, and at the end of the day, It never gets old beating a revenant to death with its own guns. The soundtrack is fast, sufficiently camp, and perfectly coded to accentuate the combat and then back off between it.

I'm not the biggest fan of having to hunt for secrets, but I'm aware that I'm in the minority there, so no points off.

It runs like a dream, max settings on my moderately powerful computer. Truly an excellently coded game.

Overall, 9/10. I do wish there was a bigger variety of animations for the glory kills, I wish the weapon secondaries were a little more varied and I wish the gun sounds were a bit punchier. But in the end, I had an absolute blast, and this game is worth every penny.\t\t\t\t\t
