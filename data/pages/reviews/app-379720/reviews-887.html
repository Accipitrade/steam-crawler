\r\n\t\t\t\t\tquick review / early impressions:

SOME MILD SPOILERS

installed a couple of days ago and, for someone who doesn't game that much anymore, i put in a good 4 hours continuously last night.

I REALLY like this.

Is it sophisticated and deep?
No. You just have to slaughter demons / undead

How does it compare to other doom and quake, etc, games?
Very well IMHO. I wasn't looking for how it stacks up but one naturally reflects on this. It captures the essence of run/gun and fighting hordes but does it in a bleak, desperate manner. It's not just mechanically testing but there's a nice immersive vibe.

What's it like?
It's a visceral, simple FPS, but slick, nice graphics, and is testing on high difficulty levels. The player can move quickly (thankfully) - you can potentially react and dodge incoming fire but you have to be switched on. I've only played the first 2 levels. It's set on two moons of mars. I actually thought it was mars, due to its red nature. You start inside, then venture outside, then go back inside, etc. I really like the wide vista / imagery. Everything's shades of red and feels apocalyptic. I like the wind effects. Feels barren. It's got nice nods to quake machinery (mega pumps and stuff) and big industrial installations.

AI?
IMO the AI is good. You obviously have different enemies with different behaviours, weapons, etc. On nightmare level, you die very quickly. i have had to play the first major encounter with demons outside multiple times and each time it plays out a bit differently. Some demons spawn at the same location. I've not checked if this is true for all enemies but how they respond is definitely different. The imps are highly agile and will kill you if you're not careful. It's great that they move so quickly actually and use the environment well (trying not to spoil here). The possessed solders are high risk with their plasma guns. I've only met a few demons. I'm sure they will only get bigger and nastier/tougher/more armoured/more powerfully armed. It's a sandbox encounter but i was impressed with no obvious repetition of patterns. They respond to what you do.

Yes it's a series of kinda set piece encounters i.e. you fight some demons, etc, then move on a bit. This is limited but it's the nature of the game genre.

You have secrets to find and upgrade points you can earn for your weapons, suit, map, etc.

You ultimately have to purge the environment / levels of all demonic presence.

FPS
I've a 4 core no HT 4670k and a 1060 6GB and i'm getitng well in excess of 100fps (approx 120 on average i guess). Seems well optimised and very fluid.

Value
I bought this probably a year and a half ago in a sale. Paid £10 for it, which seemed good at the time. I expect it can be found for less. Can't quite believe i've waited all this time to play it. Very enjoyable.

boredom
i'm sure i'll get bored of the grind / repetition of finding keys to move on, exploring levels, looking for secrets, hitting a room and clearing it etc but i've not played this genre for a while (last one was wolfenstein new order, which i enjoyed as well). As i said earlier, it's not a complex or deep, strategic game. It is what it is and is unashamed about its purity :)

If you're old enough (like me) to have played the original wolfenstein and quake, etc, when they came out, i'd like to think you'll enjoy this. Does the series justice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh yes, Doom '16. A reboot of the boomer shooter franchise, attempting to take the old style and make it shine once again in the modern times. Everyone probably already knows how successful this game was, but you came here to hear my opinion on it, so here we go.

There's nothing to complain about in the graphics department. The environments are rather rich in detail, with a lot of objects strewn about in most rooms you come to, dead bodies and blood included. The game is a bit on the dark side when indoors in certain areas, but that only enhances the mood. About the only thing I would point out is this game is not for those who dislike the colors red and / or orange - given this takes place on the surface of Mars and in Hell, these are the two predominant colors that will show up whenever you're not exploring some demon-infested lab. There's a lot of red and orange tint in this game in general.

In the sound department, when it comes to sound effects they range between good and alright. The explosions don't sound as meaty as you'd expect, but the sound of the shotgun firing, or the gauss cannon, or the chaingun going BRRRR, or a demon's neck snapping... perfect.
And that is also the word I'd use to describe the music. Mick Gordon's OST has become legendary by now, and for good reason. Nothing fits ripping and tearing demons apart than some unrestricted heavy metal. Just take a moment to listen to BFG Division or Damnation and you'll know what I mean. It's a bit more problematic if you personally don't like this particular genre of music, I could see that, but for this kind of experience it's a perfect fit.

Gameplay is perhaps what you'd expect: high-speed demon-slaying experience, with focus on movement, switching weapons, and non-stop visceral action. You have a high, but not ridiculous base movement speed, as well as the ability to climb ledges and (later on) double-jump to add some good verticality to combat. There are also launch pads strewn around in a lot of combat arenas to further extend what you can do. As such, every hit you take - particularly on higher difficulty levels - hurts a lot. The game balances it out with frequent health pickups, and the glory kill mechanic - a demon that falls low on health without being killed first will be stunned and begin to glow, allowing you to finish them off by meleeing them when close enough. Depending on what body part you target as well as the angle, the finisher will be different, even on the same demon. Doing it from above will cause Doom Marine to descend down, crushing their head. Doing it from behind will snap their neck. Targeting their arm will rip it out and smash their skull in with it. The game is not cartoonishly gorey, but it will get creative with these. Every single one of these will also drop a small health pickup for you - and if you're low on health, drop a lot of health pickups as a way to keep you going and encourage being aggressive even when near death.

As far as your arsenal of weapons goes, switching weapons is extremely quick. You gain a plethora of weapons to switch freely between, starting from basic weapons like the handgun, your trusty shotgun or the assault rifle, through the rocket launcher and plasma rifle, ending on the mighty beasts like the super shotgun, chaingun and gauss cannon, culminating with the mighty Big F-ing Gun, or BFG. You also get a chainsaw, which works more like an emergency instakill that drops a metric ton of ammo for you, but runs on limited fuel. Most of the weapons can be outfitted with a secondary mod that gives it some alternate fire mode, like the underbarrel grenade launcher for the shotgun, or turning the chaingun into a multi-barrel turret of moar dakka. These initially are fairly underpowered, but as you gain weapon upgrade points from completing challenges, finding secrets and destroying demons, you can use them to upgrade specific aspects of a weapon mod, finally being allowed to take on the mastery challenge that requires you to do something specific with it, and after completion majorly powers up the modification (often as simple as "the modification never overheats so you can keep firing as long as you have ammo" which results in total carnage). On top of that there is also equipment which recharges over time, primarily in the form of the hand grenade, but you will receive a few more toys to switch between on the go in that category.

There is a big variety of enemies with different behaviors, be it the zombie-like Possessed that are basically cannon fodder, enemies that try to keep their distance and launch projectiles at you like the Imp and Summoner, big things that rush you constantly in the form of the Hell Knight and Pinky, or just big slow lumbering things that soak up damage in the form of the Mancubi. The game mixing them all together often forces you to learn how to handle multiple different threats at once while prioritizing some of them over others.

In-between fights you are free to explore the big sprawling stages in search of secrets, usually coming with pickups, collectibles, or getting a specific weapon a bit earlier than you normally do. There are also Argent Cells used to upgrade one of your basic stats (health, armor or ammo total), and Praetor Tokens for adding new passive abilities (such as less self-damage taken, or faster weapon switch).

People don't play these games for the story, though there is one, with a lot of optional reads from collectible data pads and such that tell you more about the enemies, places or other things related to it. Let's just say trying to harvest the hellish energy spewing from a rift between the worlds is something you should be very careful about.

Besides the campaign mode, there's also multiplayer, which I don't really have anything to say about besides the fact that from what I understand it died rather quickly, as this is not really what most people play Doom for. There's also the Arcade Mode where you take on a Campaign mode stage, with the goal being killing as many enemies as you can to increase your score, with taking damage penalizing you. There's also SnapMap, which is basically just a way for users to create their own levels and share them with others.

Lastly I am impressed with the level of optimization this game has. It runs at a stable framerate on my fairly old gaming PC, hardly ever dipping down. However I did have it crash on me when loading a level once, and froze twice when using the BFG - probably too many particle effects and splatters at once for my poor rig to handle, so a fair warning to anybody trying to run this on a potato machine.

Overall, if you like a modernized version of the old-school shooter, and enjoy (or don't mind) tearing enemies apart in creative ways and / or heavy metal - this game is a perfect fit. I'd consider it a must-play for anybody wanting to broaden their FPS perspectives after tasting the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
