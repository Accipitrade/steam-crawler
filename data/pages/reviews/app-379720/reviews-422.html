\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 is Id software's return to classic FPS game design and I loved every moment of it. Being stuck in an arena full of enemies and chaining together kills feels like playing a fighting game in FPS form; giving this feeling of power by giving you the tools to do it if you use them well. With a wide array of weapons to cover a variety of situations, there's rarely a moment where the player feels improperly armed for the task. The arenas themselves all hit really good beats in the flow of combat and there's no stage in particular which sticks out as being unfun or frustratingly difficult. Atmosphere is on point and Mick Gordon's music, both the ambient and action tracks, lends itself too well to indulging the player in their gore-filled murder spree. The game takes full advantage of its old school game design with new tech, being the perfect blend of both eras; which helps it stand out in the crowd as its own type of singleplayer FPS. Considering how often it goes on sale for $5, the game is a steal.\t\t\t\t\t

It is hard to express how much this game has the exact same feeling as playing Doom back in the when it came out. Original Doom made everything faster, more frantic, more challenging, revolutionary in level design, enemy design, weapon variety, everything! And this Doom (2016) does taht for the modern FPS genre exactly that while still keeping most of the roots of that original game. Killing Demons never felt this good, and it is hard to believe how it could be any better.
All the while, you if like challenges, you can master each weapon to make it more effective, ammo efficient, mobile, and also master runes that make Doom Slayer even more of an unstopable force.
I can't recommend this game enough for any fan of FPS.\t\t\t\t\t
