\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 2016 is an incredible audio-visual experience that every single FPS fan should play. No hyperbole. Everything about the way this game handles, from the movement speed to the gunplay, is basically perfect. Locales are varied, level design is very much in-line with that of Doom 1/2 (albeit less labyrinthine), and secrets that reward exploration abound. Oh yeah, and id Tech 6? They've struck gold yet again with strikingly beautiful and optimized tech. I can only hope it won't go the way of IT5 and make it into only a handful of games. Never bothered with Snapmap or MP, honestly, and I never intend to. Nobody should buy this game for either of those features. Buy it for the best damn FPS campaign to come around in years.

(Quick note on performance: the game ran pretty much flawlessly for me on a 970/4790k. Bumped everything up to high to keep my framerate locked at 60, but I'm sure you could crank it up to max on a similar setup and have a reasonably smooth experience. It's worth noting that I experienced a handful of crashes and bugs along the way; nothing game-breaking, though.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my first steam review so bear with me... since I haven no specific format I'll start with the prompt at the bottom...

Do you Recommend this game?

F#@% YEAH I RECOMMEND THIS GAME!

Why?

Simple... I've been a Doom fan for as long as Doom has existed.. I was there in 93.. started with a free shareware CD then came the mods you know... WAD files plain Wads, I Wads, P Wads... DEH files you know the package.. some good some not so good... some downright terrible but still you played them because you just loved the game and it was a different spin on the greatest thing ever. Then I got Doom 2 and finally got to play the full Doom 1 (not shareware) on my sister's office computer... then mods mods more mods you know typical 90s kid experience..... What does this have anything to do with Doom 2016? everything... the way the game played, the experience downright to the shareware-esque demo period we just had it feels like it's back in 1993..

Doom is back... no not that 2004 quasi turn based survival horror nonsense of Doom 3 (which was more of a prequel more on that later) but old school Run and Gun FPS goodness.. no cover, no quarter, no turning back... from the start this game is DOOM It's all about GO but thanks to modern graphics it's both Show and Go..... This really does the spirit of Doom justice... forget Battlefield, forget Halo, forget CoD... this is Doom and it is back...

So what else makes it great? Graphics this is pure eye candy (from a technical standpoint unless you're weird like that).. and they are optimized so that the game runs great on a GTX 760.. Then there is the integration of modern gameplay elements like the runes, collectibles and... the secrets which work very well and will make any completionist happy. The gameplay system with glory kills and the upgrades also works.. by the end you're a fully optimized battle tank on two legs but you can strategize and fine tune your experience depending on how you want to get there....
Then there is the sound aspect..... outstanding!! Playing with headphones you can hear the action and spot where it is coming from... fantastic music score from dark and tense to thumping and racing just like the action... lots of good easter eggs... when you end up in hell... only the real fans will pick out a choral version of the soundtrack to Phobos Anomaly (E1M8)... it's glorious. Finally bonuses like the snap map and a multiplayer mode with demon runes etc. is cherry on the cake... Snap map is particularly awesome because there are very creative people out there who have taken some of the original maps and recreated them to the best of their ability and done a phenomenal job at it.

What didn't work so well for me? At times I thought DoomGuy was a bit too badass... take the glory kills system and the whole bag of tricks and you feel more of a god among demons than a lone marine who got left behind to mop up the UAC's colossal screwups (and running on fear more so than adrenaline). Speaking of demons... they are very well done and look the goods BUT.... something about how they come across don't quite have the scare the crap out of you effect that they had in the classics... (look up the Pinky or the Revenant and you'll see what I mean) Also either I've become desensitized ore the whole Hell/satanic motif was a bit too subdued compared say Doom 3 even.. at times Hell/Mars where almost indistinguishible but I didn't let that ruin it for me. ... What did on the other hand was the ridiculous enter the console mode and you get locked out from achievements and MP bug which happened a little more than half way into the campaign... the game is so good I'm already replaying but still a crash triggered it and I hate that a technical issue condemned my savegame to cheater's hell but whatever...

Any last tips for enjoying the game?

Just play it you'll love it but if you have to ask this Doom should fit somewhere way after all the prevous iterations... from what I gather the timeline is Doom 3, 1, 2, Plutonia Experiment, TNT Evilutionn, and Doom 64 and if you've seen the Battlestar Galactica reboot think about events that are recurring and it'll all make perfect sense...

So there you have it.. my first steam review and its DOOM.

10/10 would buy again!

Enjoy\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a game that I think despite it having a meh muiltiplayer that isn't going to have a very long life span is worth it specifically for the single player experience. Honestly in recent times I haven't felt like actually finishing games all the way through in story modes. DOOM being an exeption. Not only is this game enjoyable, but it's also somewhat admirable for the risk it takes. Those risk being taking all of the tropes of today's singleplayer fps campaigns and pretty much giving the middle finger to all of them.

Right into the first minute of the game you get straight into the action and your main character purposfully neglects dialoge and would rather survive on his own, oppose to constantly having to follow around some other hardheaded dumpling and taking orders to "stay frosty" and "hold your fire". Sure, you do eventially team up with somebody out of obligatatory circumstances, but even then the story is never a vanguard of the game itself. Funny enough, I actually started getting more interested in the story than the protagonist himself! But hey, if thats the decision ID software wanted to go with, then I'm all for it.

The game stays consistent with the style of play all the way through. Constant platforming, shooting, and scavanging. Speaking of which, there are a ton of hidden collectables scattered throughout the non-linear levels of this game. So that adds a lot to the reply value. Another reason to go at the campaign a second time is the various weapon upgrades. Which there are a lot of, and its not likely that you're gonna get them all on your first run. And I think this game has a great upgrade system when it comes to weapons.

In a lot of other games that have upgrade systems the abilities you gain seem to be very arbitrary and useless, and while it's not as if you're experience will be all that tainted in DOOM if you decide you dont want to upgrade, it certainly will make the combat a lot more interesting and experimental. Which is a lot more than I can say with other games that try to do the same thing when it comes to upgrading.

I will say this, DOOM is not the type of game you want to play in long sessions. Clearing out rooms filled with demons is fun, but only for two hours at a time before it starts to get a little repetitive. As somebody who did pace myself while playing this game, I had a blast. I havent even started toying around with the level editor this game has. But from what I've heard from other people, it seems to be a pretty good and compatent editing system. Once you finished a level, apparently you can play survival with friends, and that sounds amazing. The game has great optimization, great gameplay, great background music, great graphics, and is probably the best chaotic fps single player experience to come out in recent times. Check it out.\t\t\t\t\t
