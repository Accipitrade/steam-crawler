\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI AM GIVING THIS A THUMBS-UP, BUT I AM ALSO GOING TO SPEND THE ENTIRE REVIEW B*TCHING ABOUT IT. SO STRAP IN, FANBOYS.

This game felt like going out on a date with someone who was so desperate to impress me they wouldn't stop constantly impersonating other people. I wanted to slap them and say, "Dude, just be yourself!" Or in this case, "DOOM, just be yourself! You have GREAT gameplay, so why do you keep distracting me away from it with all these pointless gimmicks from other games?"

What am I talking about?

*Do I really need to be standing in one certain spot to be able to pick up a Red Guy Token, just so the game can grind to a halt and play an animation of me looking down at my hands and going "Ooooh!"?
*Were the Praetor Tokens even necessary in the first place, when the Argent Cells already upgrade your suit?
*Do the Rune Trials really need to be floating there in-game? Why couldn't they be optional tests that you go to the main menu for?
*Does the game really need to grab my head and force me to watch the pretty cutscenes?
*Do I actually need to watch the boss go 'rawr' at the start of every time I fight him?
*Does the game ACTUALLY let you get right into the action? Or do you get to kill three weak demons and then slog through all the unskippable crap that starts off all of these games? (Even though there's nothing in any of it that couldn't have easily been conveyed without cutscenes)
*Are the graphics not good enough that we need to add in a dozen or so lighting options? Bloom? Lens flare? Chromatic abberation!? FILM GRAIN!? Did we forget this is a video game, not a movie?
*One little Doomguy would have been a fun easter egg. Having a truckload of them is pointless and breaks immersion. Why would these things exist everywehre in-universe!? The two arcade games were great because there were USED SPARINGLY.
*Did this game really need three tiers of weapon upgrades? Why not just find a repair bot; get a mod? The upgrades are fun, but it would have been nice to concentrate on gameplay and not trying to remember whatever circus tricks I need to be doing to make my gun pew pew more. And did the game need to give us three MORE circus tricks at the start of every level?
*For as linear and open as the levels are, did it even need a map?
*Did we really need to have clunky, obvious 'Hey, the UAC is a cult!' dialogue playing repeatedly in the facility levels? All they do is feel like ripping off Dead Space.
*Do you remember when rooms in games just had monsters, and they didn't keep apparating in as if they were all standing outside waiting to watch you press The Current Important Doodad? Do you remember when, if you were feeling overwhelmed, you could just run through, grab the key, and get through the door without having to kill every monster first? Wasn't it nice to at least have that option?
*Did this game need Samuel, Olivia and Vega at all? Aren't the demons antagonists enough? Did we have to be TOLD why we were fighting them? Couldn't that have been conveyed visually through the environment? Do we have to be told exactly what a thingy is and its history, when we'd go up and push it anyway if it's already glowing blue? Did we need big talking stone slabs that told us things that could have easily been SHOWN to us instead? Wouldn't some Doomguy murals and hieroglyphs in the Hell levels have been cooler?
*Did this game need any of the floating on-screen text explaining 'push button make door go open'? Does ANY game need that!? I do have to give Doom huge congratulations though, because after hunting around in the options I found that you can actually turn this hand-holding baby stuff OFF!
*And my biggest single problem with the game: CHECKPOINT SAVING NEEDS TO DIE. Autosaving is fine, so long as you can also choose when you want to save. I do not enjoy when the game makes 100% of those decisions for me. I fought the Cyberdemon and halfway through the fight I realized I should have saved my BFG ammo. I restarted, and it took me to the start of the second half where I'd already used up my ammo. I didn't know if trying to restart the encounter would take me all the way back to the start of the level. And there were MULTIPLE times when I'd just finished a big battle and noticed a Haste or Quad Damage afterwards, or just thought I could have done better, but I couldn't go back because the game decided to save the instant the last demon died. Gosh, maybe if the game hadn't been overburdened with so many extra variables, it wouldn't have been a resource drain to save whenever you wanted? (Shadow Warrior 2 had all this extra kaka and let you save whenever. Just saying.) Or if the goal of checkpoint saving was so they could craft certain encounters around the checkpoints, then maybe the GMs could stop being full of themselves and let the player choose their playstyle.

I cannot be too upset with Doom for all of this. I really can't. My problem is with the whole of gaming right now. Doom tried to do LESS of this stuff I'm complaining about, and in doing so, it just highlighted that none of it needs to be there at all. Why do we need SO MUCH of it? Isn't finishing a level or finding a secret enough of a reward? Shouldn't we feel that triumphant feeling from the progression of our skill through the game? Do you want to know why this matters? BECAUSE THE GLORY KILLS, THE CHAINSAW=AMMO, AND THE RETRO LEVEL SECRETS WERE ALL BRILLIANT AND CLEVER AND AWESOME AND _THEY_ SHOULD HAVE BEEN THE FOCUS, NOT ALL THIS CARNIVAL MINIGAME CLUTTER!!! It's an effing crime that gameplay this good keeps getting interrupted with the equivalent of dangling keys.

And why? Because AAA companies are terrified to release a game that doesn't have everything in it that made every other game popular. They know that eternally-unsatisfied crybaby "fans" will WHINE if it's not in there. Feelings of progression and reward that used to be conveyed through gameplay are now not enough, so we need redundant BS to hotwire the same thing directly into our fidgety little 'I want it now!' brains. Features that were fun and innovative when they first debuted are now seen as necessities (whether they fit the current gameplay/tone or clash awkwardly against it). What repulsive entitlement. Do you remember the Simpsons episode where the Itchy & Scratchy guys ask a bunch of kids whether they want gritty realism or cartoon wackiness, and the kids are just dumb kids so they want both? And the staff is even dumber so they try to DO both? The result was Poochie. Nobody liked Poochie. There's a reason why nobody liked Poochie.

And there is a reason why Portal is called a perfect game. Not just the gameplay, not just the humor, not just the leave-em-wanting-more running time. It is because THERE IS NOT A SINGLE THING IN THAT GAME THAT DOES NOT NEED TO BE THERE.

Think about that, devs.
