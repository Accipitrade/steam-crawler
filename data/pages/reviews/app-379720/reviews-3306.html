\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBig fan of the original Doom and Doom 2 games. Was a tad sceptical about this reboot but I'm impressed. Whilst not an unnecessary trip down Notalgia Lane, it contains sufficient elements from the original games that you are taken back in time, albeit with enhanced graphics, gameplay and audio.

The old staple monsters, platforms, armour and weapons are here, along with new additions. The gameplay is frantic, if you are not fighting then you are in the wrong area! There's a token effort of a plot but, much like a loveable and much watched B-movie, it's more a tongue in cheek caricature of gaming plots, it's all about the gore and the weapons, and neither dissappoint.

One thing that really pleased me in this modern era of slipshod releases, is how well optimised the game is. I've experienced zero bugs, and can run at 120FPS in 2K on a GTX980Ti and Core i7 3770K. I wish other developers would bother to optimse their code this well, it's something that makes a buyer feel all the more pleased with their purchase.

So yes, I think this is a great reboot and certainly for all old-timers, it's a must have.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the Doom game everyone has been waiting to play since Doom 3 killed the franchise. But I cant help that it was pulling punches. What do I mean? Well, I'll list them off to you.

- Upgrade/Leveling system is unnecessary and slows down the flow of the game in favor of artificial progression

- The campaign uses WAY too many monster spawn arenas instead of strategic enemy placements

- Weak and terrible Shotgun spread

- The run speed is too freaking slow, it really hinders the entire combat of the game making it feel sluggish

- The story is STILL shoved into the player's face with NPC's holding you back so you can hear generic bad guy monologues. It just kills the replayability (even in the arcade mode)

- Pretty much removed keycard hunting (literally having the colored keys/skull keys right next to the door that it needs to open)

- Dull cookie cutter level design that tries too hard to make sense of the levels function to the story setting
(Until you get to Hell)

-Player actions and movement feel clunky and inconsistent at times

Now for some good old nit-picks that I have with of Doom 2016's graphic design.

- The Doom Marine resembles too much of Halo's Master Chief

- No iconic green haired (or bald) Zombie Soldiers (instead we have generic testicle monster humans with white armor)

- Identity Crisis (The Doom identity is barley present in Doom 2016 and rubs off as generic)

The Campaign is good but as it stands Doom 2016 does not have a good replayability factor because of the NPC's non avoidable dialogue. The Arcade Mode WOULD have been a god send but unfortunately you STILL get stopped by the NPC's having to listen to them talk for 3 or 5 minutes every time you replay the campaign.

With all that being said and done, Doom 2016 is better than what these modern shooters have to offer and for that I commend it, but I am still saddened that it lost its iconic classic personality.

If the things ive listed off dont sound like they'll bother you, then by all means, get Doom 2016.

If they sound like it would rub you the wrong way, dont even bother buying Doom 2016, get Doom Eternal instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHE DOOM WE HAVE ALL WAITED FOR, THE SEASON PASS WE HAVE NOT

The single player campaign so far has honestly been the most refreshing immersive gameplay and the balance between plot and classic deathmatch style killing is perfect. Not only has this release featured throwback gameplay combat elements, it has completely out shown the recent trend of monotonous red screen duck and wait shooters that have become a fps genre norm.

Bringing back the classic health and armor pickups was genius, but I was not looking forward the the issues this classic system brought with limited item availability per level, the search this involved, and limitations it made. With this release ID has completely revamped the mechanic and has incorporated them into a driving force to live and push the player to take increasingly higher risks to succeed and excel in combat.

A problem with many classic run and gun shooters was in many ways the monotonous non stop action. There were rarely moments for the player to stop and be immersed in the story or environment. The constant push to continue moving the entire game caused in a large part a haze over the experience. Going through a level would often simply involve mashing keys and constantly clicking the mouse until the next loading screen. It even popularized buying a mouse with auto-fire button so you would not tire your finger and hardware.

With this release of doom it has not only brought back the positives of that classic gameplay but has remastered it in a way that brought a balance to combat, plot, unlockables, secrets, and character progression that is a evolution in a lot of ways doom 3 was with it's unique 2 way lighting engine that inspired a more reserved horror survival style incorporating the ability to choose between seeing the horror that befell UAC Mars and actually being able to combat it to survive.

I am 12 hours into DOOM and constantly remarking how impressed I have been so far and continue to be.

THIS IS THE DOOM WE HAVE ALL WAITED FOR.

Unfortunately, the season pass is not. At this point it is shown as only featuring content for online combative multiplayer and snap map features.

As a classic DOOM fan, I am not interested a COD online rehash or Halo reach style unlockable armor skin dress up features.

I am here for the campaign. The continuation of a world I have grown up with and continue to want to learn more about. The one that I am consistently learning more about every time I replay Doom 3 and re-live emails, audio logs, video training sessions, and immersive plot elements.

This recent release of DOOM brought back the classic gameplay I loved with the originals and paid respect to Doom 3 and continuation of the revolution in immersive content and gameplay.

The Season pass does not, but there is hope.

As doom 3 had a full game expansion, and wolfenstein the new order also has a full game expansion "bad blood" I am hoping that if there is not going to be included either several small dlc levels, expanded single player content included campain levels or secrets, weapons, mods, or the ability to co-op the existing single player campaign with increased difficulty included in this upcoming season pass then hopefully an additional full game expansion will be released in the near future.

As a long standing doom fan I have waited years for a new release. So often with new pc releases in the past couple years we have not only had sub par performance with console ports, bad hardware support, horrible optimization issues, locked or not available settings,but the fact that most games for pc upon release will crash and not even run until patched months later by the video card manufacturer as developer support after the base release is continually dropped soon after collection of pre-order funds.

ID delivered on it's promise to the doom community and brought a sequel we can stand by and continue to play for many years to come. They brought the PC gaming community a stable release including everything we need and continue to lack from most recent releases.

All I am asking for is the same I have been asking for many years now. I WANT MORE DOOM.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, straight out of the gate I'll just say the game is a solid 80/100.

I played it for roughly 45 minutes, and spent another 45 minutes burried in menus, console commands, diagonstics, driver installs, forum posts, and Bethesda's technical support. After about 90 minutes I reluctantly gave up and requested a refund.

I'm not kidding.

Grade: B
4 out of 5 stars.
Certified fresh on Rotten Tomatoes.
Pick whatever metric you like.

Here's the thing:

I had massive technical difficulties that made the game practically unplayable. There was a consistent and cronic stutter that hitched and skipped both audio and framerate. I mean look at this sentence...

"I ho ou know wh ou're g et t ing y oursel f int o."

That, right there, was CONSTANT. Not only that, but duuring fire fights these lapses in time would translate to falling of ledges or cliffs and plenty of lost targets and unintentional deaths. Between those gaps? 60-70 fps. Completely inexplicable. It's not like I have old hardware either... I run an i7 4770 @ 3.5GHz, 16GBs or RAM, a GTX 960 4GB, and a 2TB SSHDD with 6GBs of transfer, along with the most recent drivers available. Fallout runs a respectable 40 fps in Ultra quality. *shrug*

So, why 8/10?

Two reasons. First, the game itself - while unplayable for me - still looks fun as hell (pun initially not intended but now that I think about it, I'm totally intending it) and I can imagine how awesome it would be whipping around the well-crafted levels that circulate action around like a waterpark ride at break-neck speeds feeling like an absolute badass as I splash-damage and glory-kill my way through demonic hordes. The promise is there, but either my system or the software is unwilling. It looks like the best kind of throwback carnage I used to relish in my early gaming days, and given the so many other broken promises of other reboots and homages claiming to recapture that nostalgia, it's a feat in itself that DOOM appears to succeed at this.

Second, It is totally self-aware of it's absurdity. Doomguy is an ill-adjusted individual in a world of ill-adjusted gospel. Hell is the joke in Doom in very much the same way corruption is the joke in politics, capitolized upon in - I assume - almost every way possible. Hell is "worth it."

Now, here's the downside: where Doom succeeds, it simultaneously falls flat. If you ever played the original Doom or Doom II during their heyday, this will be an easier concept to grasp. If you only played Doom or Doom II within the last 10 years for the novelty or simply to experience gaming's roots, then you'll likely miss what I'm getting on about.

Doom in it's heyday was REVOLUTIONARY. No one had seen anything like it, and it was this sense of awe along side the addictive gameplay that made Doom the phenomenon it is known for. Capturing that sense of awe - of something truly new and exciting - is impossible, and in that respect it will never outshine the original - certainly if you covet those fond memories playing for the first time nearly two-and-a-half decades ago. As nice as it looks, it just doesn't recapture that magic.

Assuming there will be a patch that rectifies the performance issues, I will happily pick this puppy up again and charge headlong through those firey gates of blissful carnage. Until then, I'll be living out my demon-slaying fantasies vicariously through twitch streams and youtube speed runs, pining away until the day my machine decides it's ready to play.

Eight out of ten, would suffer through tech support again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first half of the game is a bit slow. There's not so many enemies and it feels a bit lacking in atmosphere and excitement. But it really changes later. This is one of those rare games that gets better as it goes along. The last few levels in particular were fantastic! I played the first few levels slow and made a big effort to find secret areas thinking i'd need the extra ammo and upgrades, but it got boring and frustrating. I realised it's more fun to just charge through the level shooting everything instead and the game doesn't penalise you for this at all. Ammo is rarely a worry as you'll find plenty when you need it most of the time.

It does suffer a bit from console influences. There's too many upgrades - you've got two gun secondary weapon choices to unlock and upgrade, your suit, yourself and then runes on top of that. Most of it seems a bit uncessary. Rune challenges arent very interesting. There's also random challenges for each level that feel very gamey and out of place like kill x demons using barrels.

Level map always seems to open at a useless angle and it slow to rotate

Gameplay can be repetetive in that most of the fighting happens in a large room triggered by you destroying a gore nest or pushing a button etc. This is not like the original Doom. Areas are very obviously arenas and you know as soon as you enter that a huge number of demons are going to teleport in. It get's more balanced out later in the game.

The environment is very static. Not much moves.

Snapmap is the biggest dissapointment. Clearly, they did not make the game's levels using this tool. Please give us a proper level editor with a good interface. The current one has been designed for a console controller.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcerpt from my Critique:
"The overall gameplay is pretty straightforward as a First Person Shooter and is very much a call back to the original style of the 90s style First Person Shooters coupled with some much needed modernization. In this game you wont have a HUD getting in your way or a map overlay holding your hand, no. In this game you are expected to PAY ATTENTION as it will NOT coddle you in any way. The game isnt without its modern conveniences though as you are able to call up a map menu and the game does have a compass across the top of your screen with a directional point to help guide you to where you need to go without getting completely lost which CAN happen in this game.. and thats ok. Doom encourages you to explore each level thoroughly to find secrets and power ups to be able to customize and improve your weaponry, which is done in meaningful ways that arent completely over-powered."

I gave this game a 9/10. It would have been a full 10/10 except for the weak storyline. Laying that aside, this is a fantastic game that deserves your attention!
