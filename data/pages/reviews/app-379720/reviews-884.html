+ Checkpoints are used for saving, it rises tense if save is not available all the time.
+ Completed map challenges are still done if you got killed and checkpoint reloads.
+ Items are picked up automatically if moving over.
+ Possible to replay missions, after last level you can replay all the previous missions and continue rising your weapon masteries and rune levels and so on.
+ Arcade mode, you compare your mission scores with your friends and other users.

+ Multiplayer mode keeps you playing for hours and hours and it's fast! Still has players in 2021, five years after release - but select normal levels not beginner ones when joining and takes some time to wait others.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter more than a decade of silence, the King of FPS returned in 2016 in this glorious instalment of the iconic franchise. Even though I haven't touched this game since the release of its near-perfect sequel, I still want to encourage people to give it a try: it might be impossible to come back to DOOM (2016) after playing DOOM : Eternal, but as this entry laid the foundations of the new DOOM formula, it is a great introduction to the masterpiece that is DOOM Eternal (and even did a few things better than the sequel...)

Narration - Quick overview of the narration : it doesn't matter. DOOM's story has always been an excuse to allow the player to slay demons... and the game is perfectly aware of this? when the Slayer punches a monitor talking about the story, or when he does the exact oppisite of what he's told to do. DOOM (2016) is indeed a middle finger to narrative games. The story in itself is pretty simple, the main problem comes from its lack of impact and scale (especially compared to its epic sequel) : the DOOM Slayer wakes up after the Union Aerospace Corporation oppened a portal to Hell, unleashing the demons, and his mission in this game is simply to recover a powerful artefact, kill a poweful demon, and that's about it... Simple story, with a few memorable scenes, but crippled by its lack of impact

Music / Sound Design - DOOM won the "Best music and sound design award" at the Game Awards in 2016, thanks to Mick Gordon's incredible OST and its integration to the game. This game is full of memorable tracks, a delite for any metal and rock fans, paying homage to classic DOOM tracks by giving them a new modern touch, and expanding the list of iconic DOOM musics with some headbanging, breathtaking tracks (BFG Division, Rip and Tear, Hellwalker, Cyberdemon, Damnation, etc...). Mixed with the combat, the music makes the player fell like an unstoppable force of nature, and is perfectly integrated with the gameplay, adapting not only with the settings and intensity of the fight, but the player's actions as well. Sund design aside from the music feels great, guns feel powerful, demons sounds... well... demonic, and the quiet sound design for the settings makes Mars and Hell feel haunting yet magnificent. The atmosphere of the game is fantastic (despite the repetitive nature of the settings, problem corrected by the sequel with more location than just Mars and Hell), maybye even better than Eternal's one. Do yourself a favor, and go listen to DOOM's OST.

Combat - Combat is the core of DOOM, and by far the best part of the game with the soundtrack. Brutal, gory and satisfying, the combat takes some element of the old entries (like pickups, powerups, push-forward combat, speed, the classic demon types...) and add a fresh new spin on it : more verticality, the glory kill system that encourages player to fight for health instead of hiding and waiting for it to recover, improved gunplay with weapon modes and runs that define your gameplay, upgrades to your armor and weapons... DOOM's combat is great, easy to learn and hard (but oh so satysfying...) to master, making you feel like an absolute god of carnage, all of this in some meticulously crafted arenas with impeccable level designs. It's not to say that it's perfect : a lot of arenas are still pretty flat, some weapon modes are basically useless when compared to the other ones, the game is sometime unbalanced, some weapons like the super shotgun are so powerful that you can destroy everything in your path without switching to another weapon... This game laid some very solid yet imperfect foundations for what the combat will become in the sequel.

Other - The progression in the game is excellent, with its armor and weapons upgrade, the discovery of cool new weapons (done in a more fluid way than the sequel), the exploration is rewarded with more infos about the complete and interesting lore, cute little slayer figures, 3D models of the demons and characters of the game, and even secret rooms referencing the original DOOM games, giving you access to some of the levels from these classics. Talking about content, DOOM is incredibly generous: aside from the 15-20 hours campaign with its different difficulties, the player have access to classic DOOM maps, Arcade mode (replay the missions of the campaign to do the best score in any difficulty), Snapmap (an excellent mod tool that allows players to create their own maps, where they can control the assets, rooms, weapons, AI, narration, etc...) and the multiplayer, who might be imperfect but gives you a ton of fun content (skins, maps, eapons, unique game modes where you can play as the demons...) to play with, a real treat when you play with friends.

Conclusion - DOOM (2016) is one of the best FPS of the 2010's, and one of the best reboots of all time. Bringing a fresh take on this now over-saturated genre, the King of FPS reclaimed its throne with this thrilling, brutal and simply awesome game. At 20 bucks or on sale, its well worth its price, so don't hesitate

Verdict : 9/10 - Excellent\t\t\t\t\t
