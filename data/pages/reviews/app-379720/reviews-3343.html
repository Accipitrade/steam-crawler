\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a long time Doom fan, I have been eagerly anticipating the return of this venerated series, and finally that moment has arrived. Unfortunately, I didn't have the opportunity to play Doom the way it is meant to be played. My notebook could barely run the game and even on low graphics settings, the framerate would dip into the single digits on occassion. However, despite not being able to play this latest iteration of Doom at a buttery-smooth 60 frames per second, I still had a great time with the single player campaign and absolutely loved it.

Make no mistake, this game is certainly DOOM. No reloading, fast paced action, lots of gore, hell demons, big guns, level exploration, it's all there. Id took a chance by going back to their roots with this game, and hopefully it pays off because this game needs a sequel, preferably a remake of Doom 2.

Anyway, as mentioned, this game has all the essential elements that a Doom game should have. It also has some new elements as well, including weapon upgrades, upgrades that you can make to your "praetor" suit, glory kills, double jumping, etc. However, the new elements blend quite well with the old Doom formula and are welcome additions.

There is an actual story in this Doom title, however, it isn't too intrusive (I could proabaly count the number of cutscenes on one hand), keeping in line with the way Doom games traditionally handle their stories. It is pretty interesting and there is some depth to it. It does quite a great at explaining some of the lore behind Doom: origins of the Doomguy, origins of the monsters, etc. However, if you don't care for any of that, you can skip all of it and keep on killing.

There is quite a bit of repetition in this game. Most of the combat takes place in arena like areas were you run around and shoot the monsters until they're all dead. That may be the one main con of this game. Unfortunately, the developers at Id just remember Doom as being a pure arena shooter, which it wasn't. However, there are breaks in between the arena portions which allow you to go around and explore.

Despite the repetiveness, I still greatly enjoyed this game and managed to finish it all the way to the end. If you are a long time Doom fan, you will most likely enjoy this latest iteration of Doom as well. I highly recommend it to not only long time Doomers, but also to everyone that wants a break from the montony of COD clones that populate the FPS market.
