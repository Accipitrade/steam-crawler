Unlike most modern shooters, where every action is planned in advance, Doom embraces the chaos and uncertainty as waves of enemies create an unrelenting and incredibly fast paced experience. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't really like the original Doom game, I have to say; I was more into Quake and then absolutely fell in love with Quake 2, and Half-Life. DOOM is a very refreshing experience for a large budget FPS in 2016.

When Doom started to interest me was with the Doom 3 released in 2004, however with a mixed reception from the fans Doom 3 was generally a great atmospheric game… but it wasn't Doom, even I could recognise that. The extremely high detailed models and textures for it's time forced the game-play to be designed around more 'scary' moments with smaller fights (presumably to avoid FPS problems). This, fundamentally, was fighting with the spirit of the originals; which had fast movement, lots of guns with variety, lots of monsters on screen, and so on.

ID Software has masterfully taken the Doom IP back to the roots, and reinvented it with respect for the content, understanding of the original games, and a huge amount of passion.

So enter with DOOM (2016). This development team yet again has advanced the state-of-the-art in computer graphics (and also implementing the new 'Vulkan' rendering support API). Quite simply, the game looks and sounds absolutely amazing. Even on the lowest settings the game looks great. Wonderful effort has been put into polishing this game.

The campaign is a visceral romp into the UAC space stations on Mars, with teleportation into HELL and back again. The story writing works well, with a demon worshipping scientist unleashing hell onto Mars on the pursuit of some sort of deal with the devil. Your main mission is to close the Hell gate and destroy her (just as she is gifted her demonic form of the Spider Mastermind).

There is some interesting social commentary on 'what will mankind do to make life easier' with a hilarious take on this concept, with the UAC pumping energy directly out of Hell (and Demons too). You will come across background audio-spam from UAC hologram in visitor centres, pleading for you to just accept what they are doing.

The over-all theme is very DOOM. You got your fast movement; You got your wide selection of weapons with different utilities; You got your health and armour, and ammo resource management to consider as well.

But also takes from the Half-life era of gaming, which followed suite from Quake 2. The story plays out with your Doom Guy as a mute protagonist (just like Half Life), and they have some great exploration and platforming mechanics going on here; which is buffered with the double jump boots that you find half way along the journey.

Map design is crafted with a lot of attention to detail, bodies, notes, computer screens all dripping with the theme and horror of Hell opening up. The maps have multiple level segments, sometimes criss-crossing around, giving a great range of tones for an FPS title. Other games just stick too horizontally, and become boring fast. In DOOM you are expected to move around (all demons have strong melee hits), and jump/edge grab to different levels of the map. These simple but perfectly utilised mechanics enhance the game-play and add to the visceral nature.

Battles are sometimes from traditional means, and some fights are locked in a room with spawning-in demons. What DOOM does here is 1-to-1 what you see is what you get for monsters. Other modern titles seem to have stuck to an idiotic system of infinite re-spawning soldiers that doesn't stop unless you hit invisible triggers by running forward. This is not what happens in DOOM.

You get a feeling that you are sweeping through the levels, removing the taint as you go along. You are the Doom slayer, after all. This is emphasised throughout the game-play too, from boss monsters and final bosses (that we used to have in games!)

The close combat is granted via glory kills, which you can activate when a demon has staggered from a lot of damage. While time still plays out, and demons can move around while you are camera locks into the glory kill, they don't attack until you are done. These mechanics work extremely well as they reward you with health/armour and sometimes ammo.

The special items return in this instalment of DOOM. Quad damage, invulnerability, haste, and also the new berserk mode which sets you on a spree of glory killing madness.

Weapons are found across the campaign, and so are support drones. These bots carry a weapon mod system that allows you to pick one of two paths for each weapons (usually, since the pistol and super-shotgun have no mods). The mods allow you an alternate firing mode, giving you some more flexibility in combat.

The rune system grants you bonuses (usually associated with killing and using glory kills for more rewards). The Praetor armour can be upgraded by exploring and finding tokens; Argent cells are used to boost your characters health, armour and ammo limits; and then you also get weapons modifications. None of these things were in the original games, all of them are great and work well together in this game. I got a Bioshock vibe from this game, since they allow you a wider variety of how you want to play out the campaign by offering you some choices with respect to your gear.

Overall, the single player run would be about 12 hours just gunning to the end. I played a second time on the hardest difficulty available by default (2 higher ones are presumably unlocked piece-meal). Exploration is almost a necessity, otherwise your character is significantly weaker than if you found all the upgrade mods, praetor tokens and argent cells.

I completed a second run in Ultra Violence finding all items and secrets; completing all combat missions (for extra upgrade points), and finding and upgrading all runes. This was a blast, and probably took about 22 hours to complete by itself. Some gripes: restarting a level removes everything you earned from it (how annoying), otherwise you can load to previous checkpoints and re-do some map content to level up your weapons upgrades or complete challenges). I would have liked a few more characters in the game to flesh out the plot a little more.\t\t\t\t\t
