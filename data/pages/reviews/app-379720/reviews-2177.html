This game is fast. VERY fast.

When I picked this up I was honestly afraid this game would give into modern shooter tropes and take realism over fun; however, I was quickly proven wrong.

The original games (1993-1995) were very fast paced for multiple different reasons, and it appears that all of them have been carried over.

1. The movement speed.

The original DOOM games had your running speed incredibly high and fluid, with very fast acceleration and deceleration. This hasn't changed here, other than the acceleration being practically non-existent, being more so like QUAKE (1996).

2. No reloading.

While this statement is technically incorrect if you count the Shotgun and Super-Shotgun's cool-down animation, there is no reload button in the game, and none of the other weapons have a reload of any kind. As soon as you pick up ammo, it is in the gun.

3. Most standard enemies do not take a lot of shots to kill, and the ones that do are more scarce.

The amount of damage some of the weapons in this game do are terrifying. It's an absolute BLOODBATH. Most enemies go down quickly, and as a bonus, this game has added a feature called the Glory Kill. It allows you to perform a Fatality-styled finisher on all enemies once they are weak enough, and it becomes essential for gaining health-pickups.

There are also more things that made the original DOOM games great that have also been carried over.

4. There is a large variety of enemies.

There are a lot of very distinct enemies in this game, that all have their own strengths and weaknesses. From the shotgun wielding Zombie Soldiers, to the Imps that can climb walls and throw fireballs, to even the Hell Knight that is large and fast, but only possesses Melee Attacks (note: May be incorrect.), there are a lot of things to fill with bullets.

5. There are a small selection of weapons, and they are all balanced.

There are only a small number of weapons, each carrying their own purpose and identity. It is practically impossible to confuse one for another. Each weapon also has the ability to be modded and upgraded to have very useful and deadly secondary attacks.

6. The maps are large and semi-linear.

Unlike many modern shooters which have a set path you MUST follow, this game has chosen to let you move to places you don't even have to go, and will have you looking for things in many places to progress (mostly in the form of keys and locked doors). However, they are so well designed that you most likely will not get lost.

Over all, this is an absolute must-play, and for only $20.00, you couldn't get much better if any better.



Also the soundtrack made me explode.\t\t\t\t\t
