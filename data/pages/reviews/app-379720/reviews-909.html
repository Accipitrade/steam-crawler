\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRIP. AND. TEAR.

I think it was the blood, but for some reason this game was really rough on my PC. I have a NVIDIA GFORCE 1070 card, and it could just be my PC's design, but it felt like my PC was about to take flight constantly and explode into demons from hell. The closest comparison I have to this experience is the Oil Rig battle from Divinity OS 2, where everything is on fire. So, uh... just be prepared for some wear on that graphics card.

Gameplay is pretty balanced, played on one step below Nightmare bc I can't handle the tension of losing a save file. Upgrades are kinda weird, but general strategy mostly consists of strafing enemies while shooting them. Simpler than DOOM Eternal, but I personally like the art style and enemy design in this game better - it's fleshier, while enemies in Eternal appear more metallic. Also prefer the plot in this game over Eternal's; it's basically an old man whining at the Doom Marine tearing everything apart for hours. It never lost its comedic effect, even as I waded through the waves of dead bodies sacrificed to Satan or something. Plot is simple, acting is wonderful and darkly humorous, sound effects are real crispy, controls are smooth.

Rip and Tear. Until it is done.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPROS:

Runs incredibly well, was able to maintain a consistent ~160FPS on max settings at 1080p.
Looks pretty good.

CONS:

Rather short campaign.
Boring core gameplay loop.
The music gets annoying after a while.

But the real reason I leave a negative review is the excessive amount of bugs I encountered in the last two levels of the game, which completely soured my otherwise "not for me but a decent game" sentiment. Almost every surface or staircase was causing a huge snag (or sometimes causing a physics glitch that made my character catapult as if I used a jump pad) and even locking me in place at times. Additionally, finishers were causing me to fall through the map or also get stuck in place.

For those reasons I cannot recommend this title, as these bugs became easy to reproduce and hard to avoid at what would be well past the point of a simple Steam refund in terms of hours played.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter completing the campaign, I felt it was time to write a review.

I will start by saying, that this game is fantastic. I bought it during the Winter Sale and the amount of fun I got out of it for the price is amazing. The game is very simple, you go from area to area and clear out the demons before moving on to other areas. The areas have a fair amount of platforming but the main priority was using the arsenal of weapons that you collect along the way to slay demons.

The game also contains a few boss fights which add to the experience, and they differ from the rest of the common demons that you see along the way. Each demon you face has different weaknesses and abilities which make them a unique fight. At times, it can feel repetitive as you are essentially doing the same thing for the duration of the game. However, the awesome soundtrack and satisfying gameplay make it worth the time.

I enjoy my time playing the Doom campaign. I wholeheartedly recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never played any previous Doom title but this was a lot of fun.
It's not very difficult (unless you opt for ultra nightmare difficulty) so it was the perfect game for me to turn my brain off and just enjoy mindlessly slaughtering everything.

The game's narrative is also complementary to the gameplay instead of the gameplay being driven by the story, which in my opinion was a great decision. There are many "hidden" areas that provide more insight on the lore and grant additional upgrades to weapons and your Praetor Suit, but are totally optional and can be ignored completely if the player wants to remain focused purely on combat; allowing the player to progress at whatever pace they want.

The soundtrack also absolutely SLAPS. The music that plays during combat sequences only help boost immersion, and the dark ambience that plays during the more passive sequences can be genuinely creepy at times and is very reminiscent of Metroid Prime & Prime 2. Huge props to the composer Mick Gordon.

The story took me about 12 hours to complete, and while I purchased it on sale for 7 bucks I'd say it was money well spent. Purchasing the game at full price may or may not be slightly less worth it based on the hours I put in, but you can easily get more than double the time I spent playing via the Arcade and Multiplayer modes.

Highly recommended to anyone who enjoys ridiculous amounts of violence and death.
