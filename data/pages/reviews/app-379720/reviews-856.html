\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review will be split up into three parts due to the content available

The story is absolutely stunning, you jump straight into the action, no exposition, no bs, you just pick up a gun and shoot zombies, toss the speaker screen then shotgun the demons to death and you find Mars in a state of chaos.
Once again in this timeline the UAC are conducting unethical experiments and open hell up to the world, threatening a larger invasion if the now named Doom Slayer somehow fails to stop the bad guys and kill everything, so instead of worrying about cutscenes you just get blasting, the enemy redesigns are good for a 2016 game and are still mostly faithful to the older games, but with a few new ones tossed in for variety's sake.
Hell is much larger too and not as abstract as the classic games, its more like its own living, breathing dimension where you have to kill familiar bosses in increasingly challenging and hostile battles and at the end you are left with a surprise. Story is an absolute 10/10 kings and queens play this

As for multiplayer I'm more mixed, i grinded my way to the top and its a rough experience, no anticheat measures so its not uncommon to see people moving fast or instantly killing, server stability can also be iffy at times but you can do it with patience, online is a 6/10 experience.

Snapmap was the least impressive, you can make your own levels or play user made levels and rate them, as well as getting currency to buy multiplayer pieces, I personally found it boring and it loads slow but you are still free to try it as you wish, but snapmap gets a 4/10

Overall though Doom 2016 is absolutely one of those must play games, it gives you a sense of accomplishments, and you experience the exaggerated swagger of a literal hell destroyer\t\t\t\t\t
