\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is definitely one of the best single-player campaigns I've ever played. It has short periods of calm punctuated with some of the most intense, pulse-raising episodes of action you'll ever experience. These are times when the game just throws everything at you - more enemies than you can count - and expects you to come out on the winning side. After surviving these hordes, you feel amazing and suddenly have the confidence to maybe conquer this very challenging but brilliantly rewarding shooter. If you can beat these unfavourable odds, then perhaps you might even be able to win against the boss fights, which are some of the most thrilling I've played in any FPS.

The game's method of keeping you in the hunt is a great one. There's no regenerating health, no cowering behind cover, and few health pickups. Instead you get recovery by being brave, diving into the action and killing enemies while dodging all these attacks. Kills give you small health and ammo pickups and ensures you're never in a useless situation despite how panicked you are. The glory kills are great fun and are a useful way of keeping you afloat in the fight. These hordes of enemies are also prime opportunities to trick demons into fighting each other. On a couple of occasions I thought I was done for, only then to realise that the last two (really tough, I might add) demons in the wave of enemies killed each other, meaning I somehow won the fight, and was then finally able to search the arena for some much needed health. After the battles are done, you'll have the opportunity to explore parts of the level further and look for secret areas, collectables, and hidden ammo pickups. The level design on all the campaign's missions is excellent, mixing tight, claustrophobic areas with more open, non-linear stages that provide great opportunities for intense firefights.

The weapons are great. My favourite is the Gauss cannon, a powerful plasma gun that can act as a long-distance sniper as well, and the BFG returns as the game's most devastating gun. There's an impressive variety of enemies, from Imps to Barons of Hell to Cyber-Mancubuses, and each one is a real threat, especially when the game is throwing lots of demons at you at a time. DOOM looks and sounds wonderful, and has a robust checkpoint saving system that never once saved me in a bad or unfair situation (I'm looking at you, Metro 2033!). It seemed to save after you've destroyed all threats in each horde, and when you complete each objective, so it works well and you don't often have to replay too much if you die or fall off. There's also a map editor for those that want to try their hand at creating a level. I tried out the tutorials but I suspect I would need a lot more training to be able to build something worth sharing. Nonetheless it's a great feature to have and allows you to play other users' builds.

I haven't played DOOM Eternal yet, but if it's anything like this, then I'm sold.\t\t\t\t\t
