\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd Love to recommend Doom but offer a "buyer beware - technical issues caveat":

Although I quite liked the 'Scary' Doom 3, Doom (4 ) 2016 is a fitting return to form for the Doom series.

Focussing on the story Campaign :

- It doesn't take itself too seriously, yet has quite a bit of background story for those that want to hunt around for it
- Absolutely littered with Easter Eggs, Secrets and Challenges
- Combat that is fast paced and challenging.
- Lovely to look at even with a mid range card
- Damn fine Metal-based soundtrack.
- Shooty Shooty Bang Bang

18 May 2016

===================================

Updated 28 Dec 2016:

There's even more to like after recent additions:

The recent addition of arcade mode in particular is a hell of a lot of fun: cutting the majority of slow platforming and plot from the campaign, boiling it down to it's juicy core, and breathing further life into what was already a lot of fun.

It feels like Unreal Tournament and Doom had some fantastic offspring.

However, buyer beware:

Despite going through several driver updates (and in fact a whole new Graphics card) and config tweaks, something was still causing crash to desktop on occasions (which has been a long term issue for a number of users across various Graphics cards and setups, even where the game appears to be performing well). I've found the end of the "Argent Tower" chapter in the standard campaign often does this.

Despite these technical problems, I can't recommend Doom enough for it's single player Campaign and Arcade mode alone.\t\t\t\t\t
