\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the most epic first hour of any game in a long time. The insane Thumper-like "violent EDM" music and DOOM title appearing on the screen when your character, the DOOM marine completes the first quick area gets you pumped for a rollercoaster ride of insanity hops and dodges. I was so looking forward to the boss battle with the Cyberdemon, a two story tall hulking behemoth and I wasn't dissappointed! This game screams polish at every turn and makes you want to get all the hidden DOOM dolls so you can see the immense model detail up close. Some enemy types look like pure evil. I'm running a GTX 1070 on a fairly old computer, but get everything maxed out at a smooth 67 fps at 1080p or 60 fps at 2560x1080.

I stopped playing shooters after Halo 4. Just kind of burned out and prefer twists, like in Dead Space. I was skeptical of Doom 2016, but got the demo and was hooked right away! This game is pure and focused without any nonsense. The last 2-3 hours are perhaps a bit repetitive, but there IS some story there if you want it, but you're never forced into it.

Only cons are considerably long load times after death and when starting the game even on my EVO 850 SSD with "rapid mode" enabled. It's a pure nightmare if you have a standard 7200 RPM HDD though! Also like many modern games, there are invisible walla everywhere and the "secrets," are pretty clearly marked on map after a suit upgrade...which...I suppose doesn't waste your time but are they really secrets then? lol. The game wants you to explore, but only on its terms.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA refreshing game if you want to take a break off Rainbow Six Siege and Battlefield 4. Ever feel that you want to be a total badass in a game and just go on a rampage ? This is the game for you. ID Tech 6 engine is fantastically optimized, on R9 390 on 1440P all on Ultra settings i am getting 60 to 80FPS. Bethesda has done a wonderful job on the PC port.

The graphics are superb, and this is return to basic for the DOOM franchise. The rock-heavy metal music in the background makes you feel like a walking tank and the melee kills are so satisfying. Multiplayer is nostalgia as it reminds me when i was gaming on window 95 so thats about it, nothing special about the multiplayer. The weapons are fantastically done and the sound engine in this game is awesome.

9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBringing Old School back

This is a refreshing return to form for the DOOM series and shooters in general. Id deftly weaves together outstanding first person controls, smooth and exhilarating gameplay, a lean yet engaging story, and beautifully grotesque art design and graphics.

DOOM is devoted to classic shooter principles, but it also embraces new ideas, while never compromising its old school vision. A simple yet surprisingly deep upgrade system for weapons and the doom marine’s suit offer a nice layer of progression. The fear here is that such a system could possibly detract from the game’s hardened, old school demeanor. However, where some games may require upgrades to progress, DOOM never forces the player to make that commitment. The upgrade system is there if the player wants to use it, but it never gets in the way. It simply adds to an already tremendously engaging package.

This is a masterclass in how to construct a shooter -- a FUN shooter. And it’s a great ride. Everything from level design to enemy AI is impressive. Weapons are impactful and satisfying. And while saying this is the best shooter of 2016 may not be saying much, considering the playing field, a loftier statement can surely be made: this is one of the best first person shooters to come around in years.\t\t\t\t\t
