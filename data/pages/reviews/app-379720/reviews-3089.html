\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt looks nice... No quick save. Massive amounts of enemies, sometimes appearing out of nowhere. No ability to pick up and store first aid kits, which only heal 8 point in most cases. If you are at 99/100 health and bump into one of the rare 30 point first aid kits it will consume the entire trauma kit to heal your little scratch. No ability to carry sufficient ammunition to allow the use of a weapon as you are always thinking, "I only have 20 rounds for this, so I better save it.". Stealth mode is not so stealthy and any action other than walking in a straight line on even terrain will take you out of crouch. If they are going to throw that many enemies at you they should have a quick save and a system like VATS (Fallout) to allow you to aim. The charge up function is cute but makes it impossible to strafe wile using it; not enough digits on the left hand. Bugs. I was stuck on an errant pixel and unable to move or jump so I had to load from the last "checkpoint". A warning about checkpoints, you may get the message saying checkpoint found at some location only remotely near where you re-spawn. I will give this a Not Recommended for the time being and if I have any luck surviving the easy mode game I am at the beginning of I may change my recommendation. I am glad that I purchased it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game. A lot of fun and honestly feels like a modern take on classic Doom. The game is incredibly well optimized and barely strains my PC at all, even at max settings. Fun, fast, and deadly.

However, be careful of bugs. This game frequently crashes to desktop on load, but otherwise runs smooth. It's a common problem, and there are many theories out there as to why this happens. I can usually get it to load in 0-3 tries. Annoying, but you can work around it.

Also be careful when replaying old missions. It's really easy to erase all the progress you made on old missions by restarting your latest mission or dying before a checkpoint. Apparently this is a 'feature,' but I haven't seen a single person who has been anything but incredibly angry when this happened.

Still, worth playing. Just be careful.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite being around (albeit very young) when the original Doom games were released, I never actually played them before. Indeed, the first "shooter" game I can recall playing is Half-Life 1. In other words... nostalgia for the "old school shooter" doesn't really factor into my thoughts about this game. If anything, this is the first of this sort of game i've actually played. XD

So, how is it? Well, it's alright. Pretty much exactly what you expect: non-stop violence, body parts everywhere, and a narrative that, while present, you can freely ignore as you kill everything you see. Whee.

This really isn't my sort of game, but I can't deny that even if that is the case, DOOM succeeds in what it is trying to do. Aside from the endless hordes of hell getting a little repetitive to the point that I couldn't really play this game for more than an hour at a time until I decided it was time to finish it once and for all, there's nothing really wrong with this game.

So for those out there who want to run at full speed, fire guns endlessly with no reloading, and basically do nothing else but kill the legions of hell, have funs. :D

Woohoo violence!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was fantastic. It was exciting the first run through and had high replay value as well. The secrets and collectables are what give it the replay value. I like that you can also replay missions to complete challenges and have it count towards your campaign progress. The story was good but isn't the deepest story I have ever seen, but thats not why you play Doom and the storyline was still plenty interesting. The music was also great and I like how it would pick up when you would encounter monsters because it gets you pumped. The map sizes werent too small and left enough nooks and crannies to have fun looking for secrets, but not so large that you go insane when you couldn't find something.

Snapmap mode left a lot to be desired. The creating interface feels clunky and limited but you can still make cool stuff if you put the time into it. There are some neat maps that have been uploaded but the servers feel like a ghosttown by now. Making maps with friends and playing with them seems like it would be a blast if you could coordinate a large enough group, however.

Multiplayer uses the same as campaign mode, unlike snapmap mode. The default loadouts are lame and seems to encourage noobtubes (but there has been a patch released recently limiting splash damage and also making the assault rifle stronger so that you can actually use it.) Once you get to level 5 it starts to get fun because you can customize your loadouts and start getting cool equipment. I have not played the DLC but want to say that much of what is in it seems like it should have been in the game originally. I hate when achievements for the main game require a DLC, since you cant get 100% completion without paying more. There are three maps, some extra taunts, and a couple other things that don't seem worth 15 bucks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is purely based on the single-player game, I have yet to even open the multiplayer option.

That being said this game is awesome.

Adding a whole new meaning to "juicing it up", every second is full of non-stop shooting and punching to an amazing metal soundtrack. Whether you like getting up close and personal with a shotgun, or spamming rockets from a distance, this game has intense battles from beginning to end.

The game is designed to suit a multitude of play styles. Even with just a handful of weapons, each one feels truly unique and not just a texture overhaul of another. As you grow more familiar with your weapon of choice, you'll also travel down a short skill tree that unlocks new utility. Fortunately this doesn't have to change how you handle the weapon, it just gives you more damage. Ammo is basically endless, and barring one gun (the best one btw) you don't even have to reload.

Combat is very fast and fluid. As the game says during loading screens, staying still is death. You will find that in order to surive you have to be an endlessly moving demon killing machine. The maps are usually very three dimensional, allowing you plenty of options for fast movement in any direction. This also gives you plenty of oppurtunites to jump off a ledge and kick a demon's head in. Overall I found the combat extremely fast paced and incredibly fun.

One of my favorite aspects of the game, as I mentioned before, was the soundtrack. It has a very soft, not very noticeable soundtrack during the few parts of the game where you're just moving from one spot to another. Then, when you enter a serious battle, the music starts up slow and gets heavy right with the action. The absolute best part is that when performing a "glory kill", essentially a finisher melee move, the music pauses while you beat the demon down to the beat.

In the same vein as Dark Souls, DOOM has a very "show don't tell" storyline. Want to completely ignore the story and spend hours turning demons into red mist? No problem. Want to go around collecting data logs, trying to find out what the hell happened to turn this huge sci-fi facility into dawn of the dead? Go right ahead. Die-hard completionist who's going to spend hours trying to find every little secret and easter egg? You're crazier than the guys who play on ultra-nightmare, but that's fine too.

In conclusion, this game was very enjoyable and a good waste of a few evenings. I'm sure that in a little while I'll pick it back up and play through again, maybe this time on nightmare difficulty. I'm definitely glad I spent the money on it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t20 hours of gameplay for me on the single-player with a moderate amount of wandering around. It reminds me of Doom3 with better graphics and physics. Linear hallway shooter, maybe a bit more expansive than that. Nothing super innovative. But hey, it was great fun. I would not recommend spending too much on it, but as a fun nostalgic play, it's great. I greatly appreciate the return of the health pack instead of hiding behind a rock to "regenerate". Fighting is encouraged to get ammo and health from slain foes. The "Glory kills" is somewhat fun, although I didn't find it super innovative. It was a nice way to catch up on health and ammo. Thanks to ID software for an enjoyable game. From an FPS game lover. I would buy this again. Worth a look. Oh, and did I mention controls on PC worked well and I use a joystick, mouse and keyboard (I know, weird). Bad ports are so annoying and ameteurish but this game was definitely no "bad port". Good job ID. Way to keep the PC community proud.\t\t\t\t\t
