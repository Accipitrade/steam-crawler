\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, I got this on sale and I haven't beaten it yet, but that shouldn't matter. This game is so damn fun.

Gameplay is the shining star here. Super fast paced gunplay, and the glory kill system forces you to constantly be in the face of demons. The movement is simplistic, but works really well, and all the weapons seem to be pretty well balanced. The challenge stages, called "Rune Trials", offer some great differentiation from the basic game, putting you in a stage filled with enemies, one weapon, and challenging you to defeat a certain number before the timer runs out. The only flaw here is that for me this isn't the type of game you can spend hours and hours at a time in. I really can only play for about one hour before I start to tire out, it really is something that you have to be in the mood for.

The story is for the mostpart basic and relies on you just killing whatever you need to to get where you need to go. It's not bad, but doesn't do much more than give you a small reason beyond staying alive to kill demons. Menus and overall polish of the game, is great. There isn't much to complain about, the textures are great, the menus look good and function how they're supposed to, and the game looks great in general. The only major issues in terms of graphics are that your fps can tank in certain parts of the map out of nowhere. In a scene with almost neon light effects, my frames dropped from a consistent 130 to around 40. The other graphics option that bothers me is the lack of a true windowed borderless option. There is one in settings, but it doesn't function quite how other games do, requiring an alt+tab to move your mouse beyond the game window and on to another monitor.

Despite these minor flaws, the game is still fantastic. One of the best triple-A games I've played in a while. If you're on the fence about this game, buy it. If it's on sale, buy it. If a friend gifts you the game, send them $19.99. It's that good.\t\t\t\t\t
