Story is self aware but not eye rollingly so (it just knows its a Doom game so just get out there and kill ♥♥♥♥) The campaign alone is worth the price. It's challenging and chaotic. Nothing like current shooters tend to be.

Only curious as to why this isn't called Doom 4 because it's clearly a sequel to the original 2 games.

I only played the beta when it came to multiplayer and I wasn't as impressed. Leveling system and two weapon minimum? No place in a game calling itself Doom.

My only real complaints are that the music is not my taste (like some kind of bass heavy techno metal ... meh it may grown on me) The enemies are all kinda samey looking as opposed to the originals, and the ledge climbing can simply be a leap of faith and hope the game grants you the mercy of not dropping you. This last one could also be a pro because it literally makes me hold my breath in hopes that I will make it every time I'm forced to jump somewhere I know the fall will kill me.\t\t\t\t\t

It's infuriating to have to first: be forced to backtrack through the entire map to get to some of the Rune quests that were inexplicably gated before then afterwards forward track all the way to where you were (before they locked you out) which can take several minutes. And if you die during that new section (that you haven't played yet because you had to backtrack or get locked out of the secrets)? You get to walk all the way back again for several excruciatingly boring minutes!

Are you kidding me? The devs haven't figured out serialization yet- i.e. you can't just save wherever. Highly frustrating and annoying. The system should simply checkpoint at major gateways whether the player has visited them already previously or not. Or make a game that doesn't require backtracking for unreaasonable times for us completionists.

Also regarding usability (on PC); the Rune quest challenges have multiple keys to restart them. Since they're ill-described in the first place you will need to replay them many times until you get them which requires combinations of the SPACE and ENTER keys. Make it easy to retry- ENTER to retry and boom your back to playing. This isn't business software where you need to indemnify yourself of data loss. Let us retry the quest quickly or at least with the same keys. As it is you end up spamming the SPACE and ENTER keys until the game finally returns you back to the retry.

Regardless, I'm recommending the game. 10 for 10 on fun, graphics, and sound. But a 5 for 10 on usability.\t\t\t\t\t
