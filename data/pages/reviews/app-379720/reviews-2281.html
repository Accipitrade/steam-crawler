\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"DOOM 2016 Review"

DOOM is back. This game returns to its classic roots in a time where modern first person shooters have been reduced to bland, generic Call of Duty wannabes.

It plays exactly like the classic DOOM titles where you go around shooting demons while finding hidden secrets and collecting power-ups, armor and more powerful weapons including one more overpowered than the Storm Tornado from Mega Man X or even the Falcon Punch. DOOM fans can pretty much guess which one that is. And the bosses are as tough as nails, especially the Cyberdemon.

Adding to the gameplay are Glory Kills, in which you must weaken demons before giving them a brutal dismembering for more power-ups, and weapon mods which gives your weapons secondary attacks, such as the homing rockets for the rocket launcher.

The music by Mick Gordon is awesome and gives off an intense vibe, especially when facing off against a large horde of demons. As some who enjoyed Gordon's previous work on Killer Instinct 2013, it has one of the best soundtracks I've heard in recent memory.

Outside of the superior singer player campaign. are two additional modes: multiplayer and map creator. The multiplayer is okay and creating maps for multiplayer can be creative.

Bottom line: DOOM is back! Buy it.

Now if id Software can give QUAKE a similar treatment (With the Stroggs in it)...

Overall score: 5 Doom Slayers out of 5.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBy far my favourite FPS in the last decade; not much to really be said that hasn't already been. Back to basics of a run and gun style of it's roots (namely Doom and Wolfenstein). For those who are new to shooters, i can't recommend a better introduction; simplified by foregoing some of the more tedious aspects of most modern shooters (such as reloading), which creates a very intuitive learning curve. The story is there, but in no way impedes on gameplay, and is very much supplementary.
I find myself returning again and again, if only to feel like a friggin badass--fluidly jumping up on a platform, shooting an imp in the face, throwing a grenade over at another badie mid-air, to land and blow up a group of the demons, and hitting that glory kill ripping the heart out only to shove it down the grotesque beast's own throat.

My only stipulation being a bit repetitive at times, but that really is a minor nitpick-- as i can't say I've ever been fatigued, and i wager you'll never have a bad time playing.

I was hoping for more of a horror game like Doom 3 from 2004. as that was a special game to me - I bought a Logitech G15 keyboard just so I could play it in the dark (I think I even had to upgrade my graphics card too lol). Anyway, I went back to it last weekend and it's one of the best things I've done in a while. What a game, it's so much fun, and thinking back to it, it's a return to its roots; Doom was never really a horror, it was a face paced shooter and this is exactly what Doom 2016 is and more.

I cannot recommend this enough for anyone who enjoys a great FPS. I can't wait for the next one!\t\t\t\t\t
