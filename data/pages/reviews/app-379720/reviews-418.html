\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs Doom³ decided to crank up the "ambience and creep" factors of classic Doom games, DOOM (2016) decides to crank up the "arcade-shooter and rockin' tunes" dial instead.

  • Gunplay: solid, with no one weapon truly being sidelined in favour of a superior option.
  • Difficulty: decent progression, and notably tougher than idSoftware's previous Doom game in their timeline.
  • Music: solid, even if sometimes it fades in and out by accident thanks to the game not knowing if I killed all the guys yet.
  • Graphics: it performs well, the liberal use of blood, meat and bones making a grand return from Doom³; though monsters are built around the "beady-eyes and needle-teeth" look, so whilst their general morphology is easy enough to differ at a glance, close the distance and their faces all end up looking rather similar.
  • Plot: s'what you'd expect from Doom, though they've at least leaned harder on the satirical side of things, that of "We discovered Hell and found it to be commodifiable"; has some interesting pacing comes up when it's shown that Mr Doom S. Guy (Esq.) gets especially frustrated and impatient with anything involving justifications for the invasion from Hell... but then gets made to sit still and listen at random times, as robot men and cyborg women pour exposition over his head and he can only sit and listen. I can only imagine him like a shockingly well-armed hornet in a poorly-sealed jar that people won't interact with unless they're damn certain it won't break free and sting 'em.
  • Stability: generally glitch-free, though one moment wouldn't progress properly until I restarted (possibly miscounted enemies?), whilst one achievement wouldn't unlock because I didn't fulfil the requirements properly (Unlock All Runes doesn't count if you do it from the main-menu rather than "in-game")
  • Multiplayer: I didn't touch that, so don't ask me for info!

On the whole, a solid entry to the series, and worth the current £15 asking price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 is a nice medium between Quake and DOOM.

There's a heavy influence on how quickly you can swap between weapons, how well you know the sandbox, and how agile you are on the battlefield, all elements taken from Quake.

However, artistically, the game is wholeheartedly a DOOM game. It takes the best elements of DOOM 3, Ultimate DOOM, and Final DOOM and presents them in a modern fashion yet while still staying faithful to the things about the franchise that were always beloved.

This is a game that's worth every cent, and in my opinion did some things exceptionally better (and exceptionally worse) than its sequel, DOOM Eternal.\t\t\t\t\t
