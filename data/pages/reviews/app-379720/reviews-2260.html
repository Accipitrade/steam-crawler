\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: I absolutely LOVE this game's single player campaign and I enjoy snapmap and can highly recommend it for those aspects, but the multiplayer is hot garbage.

The singleplayer campaign is an absolute masterpiece of an FPS. Even on the highest difficulty the game never feels unfair and death always feels like it was your mistake. The weapons all feel hard hitting and powerful, hell even the charge shot on the dinky little pistol has some kick to it. There's enough secrets hidden throughout the game to give it plenty of replay value if you're a completionist, and the different combinations of weapons and ways to kill enemies means you'll likely never have the same experience twice. You're actively rewarded for aggression and punished for playing passively, you cannot cower behind cover or camp to funnel enemies towards you. Perfectly represents the gameplay philosophy of the original Doom and Doom II. The story takes a back seat to let the gameplay really shine, but every character and enemy has a considerable amount of lore that can be found and read throughout the game. I absolutely reccommend this game for the singleplayer alone.

I don't have much experience with Snapmap, but I've heard it's one of the best level editors in a modern FPS, and the few times I've gone into it were great fun.

On the other hand, I can't praise this games multiplayer. Doom created deathmatch and the arena shooter, that's a big part of why the series was so influential, without Doom, we wouldn't have Quake, Unreal, Halo, Half-Life, etc. As much homage Doom 2016 pays to its roots in the single player, the multiplayer does not do this and it disappoints me immensely.

For one, this game has a Call Of Duty-style rankup and weapon unlock system, and due to this, a fresh account is at a pretty blatant disadvantage to someone who has gone through the grind and unlocked everything. Now I can't speak for everyone, but myself and many others don't play Arena Shooters to grind out levels so we can experience the full game. For cosmetic unlocks, this really doesn't bother me, but for weapons and demons, this is a major issue to me. Not to mention a couple of the weapons that are unlocked later down the line are just overpowered and not very fun to play against.

Two, because of the CoD inspired rankup, this game also has a build-a-loadout feature. This just completely throws away the core of what Doom's multiplayer was in the 90s. In this game there aren't even/fair starts and not all weapons are equal. You don't run around the map trying to amass an arsenal faster than your opponent, you spawn and if your opponent has put more effort into the grind and gotten a better weapon than you, tough ♥♥♥♥. It takes away a level of strategy and skill that is a core part of what makes an arena shooter interesting. This multiplayer is pretty similar to Halo 4's (the worst one) in that aspect, and wouldn't you know Halo 4 and Doom's multiplayers were both made by the same Developer, Certain Affinity.

Three, the power weapons and demon runes (and powerups depending on what weapon they're paired with) are literally just skilless, effortless, free kills until they run out. If you pick up a BFG, Gauss Cannon, or Chainsaw, you're effectively guaranteed 4 kills unless you get rushed by every enemy at once, unlike in other arena shooters where the power weapons may be stronger, they also take a modicum of brainpower and effort to get kills with consistently like the Railguns in various games, most of Halo's power weapons, etc. etc. The same applies to the Demon Rune. When you're a demon, you get free, easy kills without much thought or effort because every demon can oneshot and they're not particularly challenging to play.

Also, it's not hard to find a cheater in this game. Speedhacks, wallhacks, aimbot, you name it, and because of the aforementioned loadout system, you can't even prevent them from getting their hands on the better weapons so you might have a chance at shutting them down. You're pretty much screwed out of a good time completely when a cheater is in your lobby.

Although, once in a blue moon, you'll wind up in a good, balanced lobby and have a decently fun time. The gameplay overall is solid if you're willing to put up with all of the BS. I don't hate this game's multiplayer completely, but it's just not the Doom experience.


Dear id, don't let Doom Eternal's multiplayer become Halo 4 gone to hell.\t\t\t\t\t
