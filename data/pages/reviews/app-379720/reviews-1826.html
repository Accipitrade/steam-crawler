\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt went in a very different mechanical direction as compared to the original, but stays very thematically faithful. The glory kill mechanic forces you to get in the faces of demons instead of running away and circle strafing. This keeps the you in the fight instead of running away and picking them off from a distance. However this is my least favorite portion of the game as the game gives more health if you are damaged so you can stay alive, but this means that healthpacks have severely less value when you are at low health because you can regain the same amount of health from one glory kill. Non regenerating health is effectively invalidated by the entire mechanic as loss of health inherently allows you to regain health easier which is conventionally a dangerous situation. Despite my own distaste for glory kills this game is extremely fun with fast movement and a requirement for quick reactions and will be an example for arena shooters into the future.\t\t\t\t\t

However after putting 14 hours into one ultra violence play through I can say this game is one of the best games I've played in a long time. The combat feels invigorating and fast paced, the weapons feel satisfying to fire, and performing glory kill after glory kill makes you feel like an unstoppable monster. "Ultra Violent" is the perfect way to describe this game.
This is definitely worth buying, if you have the money to and you liked the older DOOM games or DUSK buy this.
