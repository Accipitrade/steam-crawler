\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a mindless arcade shoot-em until they turn into a pile of bloody goop, Doom is the best I have seen in quite a while. Unfortunately for me, about halfway through the game, the action became repetitious and boring. Maybe I'm getting old, but running into the same set of monsters in the same order in closed arenas time after time, just gets boring.

On the good side, this revision adds some really nice new touches. Glory Kill is a new and proactive way to collect health ammo and armor. There are also lots of secret areas and tokens to find and collect. This is integrated very nicely with the map system. Especially fun are the hidden levers that open up portals the rooms from the 1996 version of Doom. You can also rerun/retry completed levels and specific tasks called runes.

Unfortunately, this splendid game is IMHO crippled by the lack of a save/load option. When you die, and this happens often, you go back to some preselected checkpoint. Plan to replay parts of the game over and over. Finally, beware of the cheat mode. Once enabled, the game will not save correctly. You loose all progress and have to start over from the beginning. What were they thinking?\t\t\t\t\t
