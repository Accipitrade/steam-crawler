\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI always want to state my review right after finishing a game. As I am holding the shotgun, standing right in front of the corpse of the Cyber Demon, I want to state everything before I continue.

-\tI saw the hatred in the eyes of the Cyber Demon… It was obvious that he remembers, it was obvious that this is not the first time as he throws me other side of the hall.

As I stated before I am not an fps guy and I played Doom 3 mainly because of nostalgic reasons. The story was ordinary, the gameplay was ordinary. It was just nice to see and shoot some old fellas with good graphics. Having several NPCs around in order to improve the storyline was also nice. Horror elements are ok just the problem was the AI of the monsters. The soundtracks were good. Overall it was a nice and ordinary game.
Nothing is similar to the DOOM…

-\tThe Hell is full of warnings that say “Do not raise him!”

From the very beginning of the game, as the very moment that you raise from the sarcophagus and do not listen to any warnings that Dr. Hayden gives and start to kill around immediately, you feel that the “I am back and will kill you all!” feeling all the time! Furthermore, the storyline and the atmosphere of the game keep that feeling fresh all the time!

-\tYou can feel the rage against demons in every moment…

In a normal Doom game, you try to find some guns and bullets, you try to keep your distance and shoot them while protecting yourself… In this one, you just try to get closer in order to break their bones or crush their skulls. They will all scream with fear…

-\tBFG 9000 will be nothing compared with your chainsaw. You all will be looking around for more chainsaw fuel…

The fundamental part of the game is completely the same as the previous ones. However, there are lots of new details that improve the fun, atmosphere, and gameplay

Pros:
-\tThere are different types of improvements. You can improve your guns, suit by finding some equipment and with killing experience. Plus you can find and use some magical runes to improve your skills.
-\tEvery gun has two special firing modes besides the standard one. In order to achieve them first you need to find parts than you need more killing experience to unlock specifications. Reaching the full power of each mode needs you to complete a specific challenge for each.
-\tYou can improve your suit too. Just you need to search for the parts at every stage.
-\tThere are more than several runes that you can find and use in order to improve your skills. However, locating them is an issue, and completing the challenge they require is another. So there are plenty of mini-games involved.
-\tWhile writing this review, the game is 7 years old. Still, the graphic details are mesmerizing.
-\tSoundtracks of the crowded fight sections are legendary. It just boosts the rage.
-\tI never liked to lose my way while playing previous Doom games. Hence, the map is a lifesaver.
-\tNever gets boring!
-\tMars scenery in open areas improves the loneliness feeling.
-\tWe are in 2020 but I still like the visuals. I mean Cacodemons still look pretty cute and cuddly.

Neither Positive nor Negative
-\tSecrets are very very easy to find. Just being patient and searching around gives you all.
-\tSome challenges are not easy to understand.
-\tSome stages are very short.
-\tThe highest difficulty is only for psychopaths.

Cons
-\tNo expansions for the storyline.
-\tIn the Lazarus Labs stage I experienced a few bugs.
-\tNo face cam in the middle like Doom I and II. You feel the absence.

@developers: I had to play this game earlier. Sorry. My bad. This thing is *ucking legendary.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSO GOOD. Finally got around to playing this and what a great job they did with it. I love that it's not just another run-of-the-mill FPS. Super high-paced & high-action with perfectly appropriate music for extra stress during battles, one of my favorite elements is the use of melee that makes it almost required to get close-up on the raging monster spitting nuclear fire-whatever at you. It's so difficult to avoid getting hit by incessant projectiles that your armor and health is often guaranteed to decrease, thereby making it necessary to weaken enemies with your guns, and once they're weak, tearing them apart with melee, which rewards you with pick-up health. Then there's the chainsaw, which drops both health and ammo (also greatly needed) with its violent use. This doesn't require weakening the enemy before use, but requires gas to use, making it a challenge due to limiting how often you can use it, usually only when you're low on ammo.

So taking high-paced action, constantly trying to avoid getting hit, while killing everything coming at you, while trying to monitor your health and ammo and decide when to use these tools all at the same time: it definitely makes for a challenging and fun game. Highly recommend it.
