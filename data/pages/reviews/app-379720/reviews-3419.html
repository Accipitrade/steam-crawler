\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTwo thumbs up for this reboot that actually satisfies. ID has remembered what made the original DOOM games so much fun in this intense, fast-paced shooter game. The campaign starts off quickly without holding you back, giving almost immediete access to weapons and lots of demons to kill. The difficulty ramps up well within the first two missions that I've played, offering new demon types and challenges around every corner. The story is there, but deliberately shallow so as not to interrupt your nonstop killing action. Even the main character, a silent but clearly angry protagonist doesn't feel like listening to dialogue, as each time someone tries to present information to him via radio devices, he destroys them before they finish talking.

The graphics are decent enough. Nothing exceptional really stands out aside from some cool reflective lighting on the sides of the guns that actually reflects flames from nearby fires. What's good about this engine is how smooth it performs. Absolutely flawless performance on the very highest graphical settings. No stuttering, no bugs, no problems whatsoever, something that's really hard to come by in PC games as of late. The one technical issue I ran into so far was related to a 3rd party recording software (Nvidia Shadowplay) and it's inability to record footage unless I recorded from my desktop first and then maximized the DOOM.exe. Sound quality is exceptional with a heavy rock soundtrack playing in the background while you obliterate hordes of hellspawns. The weapons sound loud and powerful while impacts with the monsters cause disgusting sounds of flesh and gore to echo through the hallways.

In addition to the, so far, excellent campaign, DOOM offers a competitive multiplayer mode. Now this is where the game loses its footing. The multiplayer seems fun at first, but quickly gets old and repetitive. The full game offers far more weapons and maps than the multiplayer beta offered which is nice, but it's still severely limited in scope. The biggest flaw in the multiplayer is the entire "Loadout" style of gameplay. This type of gameplay just doesn't work for an arena shooter and its surprising that ID thought this to be the way to go. What they should have done is made it so everyone starts off with a crappy pistol and needs to run around the arenas picking up weapon spawns, and losing their weapons when they die. I'm hoping there's a gamemode that I missed somewhere that allows for this classic style of arena shooter gameplay, but from what I played I couldn't tell.

The saving grace to this tacked on multiplayer is the 3rd mode, "Snapmap." Now this is a pretty cool feature that I hope is continously added upon. Snapmap allows anyone to easily create custom DOOM levels using several premade level assets like hallways, arenas, deadends, and an assortment of props, weapons, and enemies. The tool is powerful enough to even allow you to set up logic based events like boobytraps or key-activated doors. With the power of this tool made readily available to anyone who buys this game, DOOM has some significant replay value. Not only does the tool let users create single player levels, but they can also create multiplayer deathmatches, cooperative survivals, races, or whatever else people can think of. The one issue I noticed early on with this snapmap option is that there's a pretty significant limit to what you can make in terms of memory space. I can see this filling up very quickly with more complex level designs. And I also noticed a lack of enemy types that I would have come to expect from the DOOM franchise. I didn't see the spiderdemon (arachnotron) or the arch-vile in the game's demon category. Hopefully these are added later on.

So that's about it. I'll be updating this review with a video review soon. So far I'd highly recommend this game if you're a DOOM fan. It's definitely a must buy for the single player alone, however, if you're not interested in the "Snapmap" or "Multiplayer" I recommend waiting until the game drops in price, which it likely will within a few months.
