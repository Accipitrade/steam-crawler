\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, I should start off by saying I'm not typically a FPS player. Sure, I enjoy an FPS now and again, but mostly I do adventure, casual, walking simulators, and RPGs.

Firstly, I played on the "normal" difficulty setting...but with a bit of uncertainty. My reaction times aren't what they used to be, but the pacing of combat difficulty progression turned out to be SPOT ON. Even when I fell in combat, I was able to come back and try again and make progress towards the "encounter". I also noted during my deaths that the reload times for post-death are very quick on my aging computer. I don't have a SSD and they managed to keep me in the game and ready to try again, rather than stewing in frustration.

The music is also phenomenal. I wouldn't consider myself a fan of death-metal or hard-metal-rock...whatever this is. But man did it fit the game perfectly. I found myself flying around the arena, blasting and glory-killing and feeling like a bad ass. And the music was a large part of that experience.

But there is also some strategy here too! Which monster is the biggest threat? What can I face head on, and what do I need to play evasively? How do I best use the terrain to my advantage? Also, my adventure-game mindset helped out too...because poking into the various corners of the map uncovered all sorts of items that powered me up and made me more likely to survive the hellferno of combat that was to come. Don't worry if you aren't great at this though...because there are unlocks and upgrades that will help you. Even if you miss something, you can go back and replay the map to find what you missed!

On a unrelated side...I never realized how little I would miss reloading weapons until it just wasn't a thing anymore! Sure, some weapons fire quickly and some fire slowly. But reloading as much FPS games know it really isn't here. You stay in the fight!

And I haven't even touched the map maker or player-made maps yet!

All things said and done, they have masterfully managed to maintain the original DOOM's overall feel and gameplay, but have brought it into modern times and even improved on it. Even as someone who is not especially a fan of or skilled at FPS games, I would GLADLY recommend DOOM to anyone. RIP AND TEAR!\t\t\t\t\t

Pros:
- fun campaign
- really cool, adrenaline-pumping music
- fun collectibles (not necessary, but I found it fun to go out of my way to find them)
- nice graphics
- VERY FUN gameplay
- a lot of extra content (collectibles, replayable levels, arcade mode, classic levels...)
- fun multiplayer

Cons:
- kinda hard to find a multiplayer match; my guess is that players moved on to the multiplayer in Doom Eternal; I guess it depends on the time and day you sit down to play multiplayer
- ...and no more cons I can think of!

Doom 2016 now makes me want to play the other Doom games, so I'd say Doom 2016 is a good place to start if you want to get into this series.\t\t\t\t\t
