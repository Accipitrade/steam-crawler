\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA word of warning: I never played the original DOOM games. Yes, I'm a filthy casual, so everything in this review will be from a filthy casual's perspective.

I bought this game when it was on sale after much persuasion from a steam friend who happens to be a gigantic DOOM nerd. I didn't know a thing about the franchise other than that they're classics and involve shooting and killing enemies. So, with a somewhat blank slate, I began my journey into the DOOM world. What a ride it has been.

I would like to start off by saying that this is probably the only game in my library right now that I have to lower my graphical settings for. Im currently using a GTX 960, so it's a tad outdated but can still run most things on high/ultra settings. I would recommend making sure your PC has some muscle before buying this game.

As for the actual game, it's first impressions are fantastic. From the opening menu, the game is very atmospheric and draws you in (but man the volume when the game starts up. Prepare your ears). The music in game is amazing. Fits the setting very well. The game's pacing is wonderful and can keep your attention even if you die repeatedly (which you will on harder difficulties, I guarantee it). The control setup is very smooth and fun to use. Graphically, the game certainly enjoys its particles, which probably adds to the need for beefy pc specs. It's a very pretty game, and I have often caught myself zoning out while looking at the vistas frequently found throughout many of the levels. While it's a linear FPS (which is not one of my favorite genres), this game has a really unique ability to hold your attention with it's variety of enemy encounters. This essentially means that while the setup for most of the fights are the same, the large variety of potential enemies in each fight allows for every encounter to feel exciting. I really enjoyed the game in this way.

Now to rant about how much I suck at this game. The harder difficulties in this game are not for weaklings. I don't know if the orginal games were like this, but you need to be in to self-abuse to enjoy this game at it's most difficult. I'm not saying this to criticize the game, I'm just saying that if you intend to play this game on it's hardest difficulties, be ready for a rough ride. On a scale of 1 to Dark Souls, it's probably about an 8. But I probably just suck at it. Who knows.

Overall, I highly recommend this game. I would give it a 92/100. Definitely in the best game of the year conversation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the best FPS games i have played in a long time. It gives the old nostalgic feeling with the environment, monsters and play while using newer engines and technology to make it visually stunning and epic sounding. I have played through the SP and MP extensively. The SP campaign was awesome, challenging and fun. The well thought out environments, obstacles and puzzles keep you going for a long time. MP is a hoot. Between the character and weapon customization and fast paced action, it can leave you knee deep in the dead if you're not careful. With the latest DLC update (as i type this), the new MP maps and gameplay bring more excitement. Arcade mode in the SP campaing can be quite the challenge as well, forcing you to think your strategy a bit. Overall I give this a 10/10. It made the young kid in me who remembers getting Doom shareware happy, and the old kid in me excited to continue playing this amazing game. \\m/\t\t\t\t\t
