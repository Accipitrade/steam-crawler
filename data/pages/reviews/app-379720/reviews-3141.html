This game is awesome, 9/10. 1 point down for minor technical/performance difficulties.

DOOM (2016)
(apologies for my poor English)
I didn't have high hopes for this game, because I didn't think it is possible to replicate the old DOOM games gameplay and atmosphere. Oh boy, how wrong I was!

This game has it all. The gameplay is perfect, it is fluent, fast, unforgiving. You either move or you are dead. You shoot midair, do 360 turns, do whatever it takes to stay alive and send some of those bloody demons back to hell (oh wait).

The weapons are pretty much perfect. Some of the alternative fire options are not extremly useful, but each of the weapon offers unique gameplay style, so it brings a diversity to the game. My favorite was the Plasma gun. Well besides the BFG and Chainsaw.

The level design is gorgeous. It is weird to say such thing about game from Hell, but it is true. The environment is very detailed, the textures are sharp and the lighting is carefuly crafted. The game lets you explore and there are multiple exploration rewards in each level. The developers managed to split player's time between fighting and exploring perfectly - first you fight, then you rest and explore.

The difficulty, the difficulty.... for a lack of words, it is just perfect. I played on Ultra-Violence difficulty which provided very balanced experience. It was hard, maybe very hard at times, but if brains had been used it was always doable without any big frustration. Sometimes when I finished a very hard section, my heart was beating like crazy, I was covered with sweat and my hands were shaking, but the joy from beating another checkpoint was overwhelming.

Same goes for bosses. They are hard, but not frustrating. It is possible to learn their strategies and with every death the player gets closer to beating them, finding their weaknesses. Normally I hate boss fights, but I really enjoyed the ones in this game.

Are there any negatives? The game itself is flawless, I can't complain about anything. However I have to drop one point for some minor technical difficulties. The game does not support SLI. OpenGL mode is very stable, but has some weird issues on max settings. Vulcan mode has better performance, but occasionally crashes and at my system it also caused very long load times (I didn't have this problem with OpenGL). However these problems are just minor, they did not spoil my experience.

9/10

Well done id and... thanks!\t\t\t\t\t

DOOM Campaign
-
The story is very basic, with backstory being told through what is essentially Dark Souls item descriptions. Combat is flawless, seamless, and fun. As long as you're not looking for collectibles or trying to meet requirements for Challenges/Masteries/Runes, it's a very naturally-progressing linear shooter. Glory kills and chainsaw kills provide a fun and quick way to resupply, and the different powerups add another element when things are getting samey. Guns are varied, unique, and unlocked at different points in the game. There are three kinds of upgrades, and while only one of the suit upgrades are useful, most weapon and player upgrades are. These upgrades can only be found by exploring, encouraging adventurousness and thereby making the levels feel less linear, less forced. Some of the Mastery weapon upgrades and mission Challenges DO feel forced, a common one being "kill X enemies with one shot Y times" where X is 2-4 and Y is 15-30. This is lazy game design that takes you out of the action, but at least you can grind these out with mission select.
It took me 27 hours to complete EVERYTHING in the Campaign on the easiest difficulty.

Campaign mode is fun, balanced, and natural; unlike the next mode...


DOOM Multiplayer
-
Multiplayer is barebones and random. There are few maps, and all of them have bad spawns, where you'll be killed from behind or the sides. You will constantly get killed for looking in the wrong direction, and your teammates steal the Demon transformation powerup you earn. It's horrible. Success is mostly dependant on the use of rockets at close range, and the random drops you get at the end of the game. Unlike in Overwatch though, these affect combat and range from stupidly OP to downright worthless. There is no progression to speak of--as the only gun you need is the Rocket Launcher--and the combat itself is not fulfilling . The developers did not add an anti-cheat, not even a basic system like VAC. This is combined with a lack of player reporting features; cheaters have free reign. But even if you don't encounter cheaters (and how would you be able to tell?), you'll be treated to one mess of a multiplayer experience. Additionally, all of the future DLC will be multiplayer-only, and many more multiplayer achievements will be added--if you happen to be a completionist. The DLC packs will be $40 for 9 new maps, fracturing the already meager playerbase and glorykilling your wallet.

Some may like the multiplayer, I do not.

DOOM Snapmap
-
Snapmap is like many other custom game editors, but with an important addition: programming logic. With controls simple enough to be used with a console controller, you can develop your own DOOM Campaign levels (well, about half the size) and play them with up to three other people. The tools supplied are robust, and if you have the time and patience you can create just about anything. There are limitations, however. You are given a "budget" for objects and AI, so that the inferior console versions of this game can participate in cross-platform play. I understand that id likely did this to swell the number of players in Snapmap, but we PC players get a cut-down version of it. Finally, the base for all Snapmap levels is the UAC Mars Building. This means no outdoor levels and no Demon world levels, the two most interesting kinds!

You can play custom Campaign missions with friends, but are limited in their scope.

Performance
-
The game looks fantastic, runs well, and isn't hampered by an FPS cap. I would say it was made with PC in mind, but the multiplayer disproves that theory. With an i5 and GTX 970, using the most recent drivers and patch, I am able to run the game at 1080p with a constant 110FPS or more (on auto settings). Both high graphical fidelity and frame rate are possible in this game- very important for an FPS on PC, and specifically in this game where fast movement speed always on. Just the other day, the developers added Vulkan support, which brings even more performance gains, espectially for AMD GPUs- but even though I run Nvidia, I still saw an FPS boost.\t\t\t\t\t
