\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM (henceforth referred to as DOOM2k16,) one of the best shooters in years, and one that still captures the original DOOM's visual style and feel: easily distinguishable enemies and fast-paced combat in a secret filled hellscape.

The story has never been DOOM's focus, but the one present in DOOM2k16 is well crafted with enough contrivance to keep the narrative woven with the gameplay. There are plenty of audio logs and codex logs to read if you wish, but the game knows as well as you that gameplay is the main focus. The cutscenes are only there to give the primary expoition, but the Doomguy (and presumably you, the player,) couldn't care less about what needs to happen, as long as you get to kill demons. Hell, the doomguy even cuts the main ally off in the intro stage to prove his point, not once, but twice!

Speaking of gameplay, the combat is well crafted to suit a very specific playstyle: monsters drop more health and ammo if you have less of those resources, both of which are guaranteed by using the glory kill system and chainsaw (health and ammo respectively.) The lack of any reloading allows the assault to continue without pause, and the enemies stay in a general area to prevent baiting and killing them from afar. There are a variety of weapons and modifications for each that allow versatile play that can be used to make certain enemies easier to kill with the right weapon. The runes system allows passive buffs that slightly alter gameplay but never in a gamebreaking way. There are enough difficulties to encompass a large variety of players, from new players to those who eat danger for breakfast.

By far the best part of the game in my opinion is the sound design and music. Every track has been crafted to build the atmosphere instead of fit it, the slow buildup of a modern rendition of Knee Deep In The Dead (DOOM's E1M1 theme) as the intro level ends gives the perfect indication of what the game will be like, and the ambient tracks manage to encapsulate the ominous feel of being on Mars with demons whilst being calm enough to indicate that the combat is over... for now.

To boil down those points into a summary that doesn't do the game justice, I'd put it as such: It's a solid game with passable narrative with a fluid combat system and a killer soundtrack to boot.\t\t\t\t\t
