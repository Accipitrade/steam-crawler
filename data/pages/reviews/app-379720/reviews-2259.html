\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is the soft reboot of the renowned FPS franchise, published by Bethesda and developed by what's left of id Software.

The Good:
+The presentation stays in the safe palette, but has gruesome gore and crisp sound effects.
+Mick Gordon's soundtrack is, for the most part, spectacular. Although, the industrial screeching can get a bit much at times.
+Guns are impactful making it very satisfying to deliver shotgun colonoscopies to demons far and wide. However, this is somewhat hampered by the reliance on arena-style combat.
+The campaign, while short, is littered with secrets and collectibles. Moreover, the Doomguy is now the Doom Slayer, a mythological figure blessed to rip 'n' tear.
+The multiplayer can be frantic and a lot of fun.

The Bad:
-An overreliance on some truly awful platforming. The ledge grab will fail to register, you will hit invisible walls, slide into death pits, etc.
-While the game doesn't have regenerating health, the Glory Kill system might as well be the same thing. And sadly, Pinballing between enemies to execute 1-2 second kill animations gets tiresome.
-While multiplayer is fun, it suffers from poor matchmaking, horribly broken abilities, and awful spawns that rival early CoD games.
-Some truly terrible boss fights.
-While the premise of the story is fun, the campaign frequently contradicts itself. Doom Slayer ignores the other speaking characters in the beginning of the game, until you are locked in a closet for some unskipable cutscenes. The worst one being when the Slayer stares a woman opening a portal to hell, yet doesn't even attempt to stop her. Lastly, the story ends on an unsatisfying cliffhanger.
-Upgrade trees and tokens bloat the game with awful modern design. Moreover, I would have removed grenades and simply given weapons secondary and tertiary firing modes.

Bottom Line:
DOOM is not a bad revival, it's just overrated. It personally reminds me more of Quake 2 than any previous DOOM game, but it is still a solid game brought down by modern design choices. If you DO get it, play on hard. And if you get bored after the first level, I suggest you get a refund.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know why but my review always bug. I had written a hugeass review to explain my points but I don't feel like redoing everything. I'm only writing this from the perspective of single-player campaign.

Pros
+ Sound
+ Some choices of game design (weapon upgrade)

Con
- Guns are not satisfying
- Monster design is bad. No palette distinction. Sound is all the same. Bad AI and patterns.
- Level design is atrocious and the main flaw of the game. Corridor, battle arena, corridor, battle arena, etc. No exploration, maze-like structure or anything like original Doom.
- Game design. Poor execution of FPS design IMO.

Sorry for not being more lenghty but I don't feel like rewriting everything again.

EDIT 2020: I replayed the game years later to see if it was better or if I appreciated it more. Here are my new impressions.

Overall score: 5/10, not recommended
Summary: Although some aspects are solid, they are generally solid across all game of the same genre so it's not a buying factor. Things that are weak are things that are important, ESPECIALLY for a Doom game.

-> Positive
+ Guns are good and useful, except for the first three (shotgun, grenade and pistol) which I forgot immediately the existence. The Super Shotgun is super amazing. My previous review comment was linked to the fact I didn't played enough to see the "advanced" weaponry.
+ Graphics are good, although I have a few problems with environment design and shading that makes everything look "samey". It's a solid AAA game with regards to that.
+ Music/sound is good, although sometimes the dynamic aspect of it is weird because of how abrupt it switches (or how long it stays because you can't seem to find the last enemy of an arena).
+ Higher difficulties are difficult. No hand-holding. Good stuff.
+ Upgrading system is good. Every game nowadays have an upgrade/level-up system, so it could have been horrible and bland. It's quite good considering it's on multiple front and is helped by both game challenges and exploration during gameplay, linking everything together well (except for the weird challenges that teleport you to an arena, creating an even bigger problem/disconnect with regards to my next point).

-> Negative
--- Level design i.e. Serious Sam marketed as Doom (i.e. arena fight centered). That's my main grip (as it was years ago). Everything is about doing arena action in a room (-ish) where you (almost always) HAVE to kill everything before proceeding to a boring corridor/linear portions. The game is predictable and not engaging on that front, although it sometimes have very good loops (the Argent Tower portion where you desactive the filter is very good) and foreshadowing (especially with new enemies) going on. Of course some of the arena are engaging (especially the first Mancubine one at the Argent Tower), but it still doesn't help overall the game.
-- Controls (move too slow, delay on the jump, lose control of characters for glory kills, etc.). Some features helps (wide "hitbox" for the ledge climb, upgrade to make you do stuff faster, etc.), but it still felt more clunky than Quake, which should be the default go-to to test your controls.
-- Platforming. Mostly because of previous point, but also because of bugs, slides, objects with weird colision detection, etc. I've died more to platforming than enemies. Also, no rocket jump (and insane splash damage to yourself also).
- Enemies. Both AI and design; I much preferred old school take on enemies that each had their own very distinct sound, movement and colour pattern for easy recognition in the frame of action. Also, I know that technically monsters can fight between themselves, but in reality I don't remember seeing it once (whereas in original Doom it's a very valid and common strategy).
- Long cutscenes, boring stuff.
- Chainsaw with ammo? No thanks. The whole system of ammo replenishment I didn't like (see below).

-> Neutral, i.e. random thoughts
# Glory kills and the ammo system in general I didn't like, but I know some did. It creates all kind of strange situation (such as not wanting to destroy too fast your opponents or not performing headshot, which is both detrimental to the gameplay IMO) and keeps the total ammo count low for you to use it.
# I seemed to have a few bugs (clipping, falling through the floor, etc.). Most if it came from the Glory Kills and bad platforming, which didn't help my appreciation of both.\t\t\t\t\t
