Doom 2016 is a masterpiece of FPS design. For one thing all the enemies save the Hell Razer (which is weak) are projectile based. This makes everything easy to dodge and rewards skill in dodging projectiles. The enviroments are beautiful. The first few levels are dark and industrial, and the Hell levels are strange and organic. There are a lot of secrets to be found, some taunting you as they're flashed in your face, but others are way out of sight and need specific perks to locate. The guns feel amazing (except the pistol) and all are useful up to the end.
A few problems I have with this game is that it's too easy to just pull out the Super Shotgun/Rocket launcher and cheese enemies. But to master movement and stay alive long enough to get up close balances this out.

U/N: just go play Nightmare 100 times, you have to know every single spawn location, every single ammo pickup, every single wave trigger, every single secret, etc.
And dont blow yourself up like I did on Level 3...

Hardware Wise:
I've tested on these systems:
FX-4300 @5ghz + GTX 1050
FX 4300 @5GHZ + R9 Fury X
FX 4300@5GHZ + R9 Fury X Crossfire
i5 2500K @4.9ghz R9 Fury X
i5 @4.9ghz R9 Fury X CF
Ryzen 7 2700X + GTX 1660 SUPER
Ryzen 7 2700X + RX 5700XT
Ryzen 5 3600XT @4.6ghz + RX 5700XT

With the FX 4100, it ran at 1080p high at around 100 FPS with the Fury X. With the 1050 at Medium it as around 50-70.
The 2500K fared the same with the Fury. Crossfire was stuttery and neither GPU was being used above 10%.
With the R7 and 1660S at 1440p High, it was around 150FPS.
With the R7 and 5700XT at 1440P Ultra, I frequently ran into the FPS cap of 200.
Same goes with the 3600.
Overall: Runs well on any system - potato or high-end. Just remember to turn down settings as necessary, you'll want to maintain atleast 60 FPS at all times.

A few more things:
Some of the rune trials you do to unlock different runes are pretty lame. They generally involve some sort of timed sequence where you have to kill a certain amount of enemies in a certain way. And they always come down to the last second. They aren't really fun to play through.

The remade classic levels don't play well because of the updated enemy types and balancing. For example, in the original Doom the shotgunners could be killed in a single shotgun blast and you could mow them down easily. Now they've all been replaced with shieldguys, who are much harder to deal with. There are numerous other examples of this in the various levels, but hey, it's still pretty cool.

And finally, the multiplayer. It really should have been called "Call of Doom" or something. It feels like a generic multiplayer shooter, with all the same exhausted game modes, loudouts, perks as you level up, an insane amount of cosmetics, and so on. It's mediocre.

But the singleplayer campaign is beyond amazing with some of the best shooting action ever created.\t\t\t\t\t
