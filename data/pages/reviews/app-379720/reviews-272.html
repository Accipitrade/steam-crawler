\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEveryone and their circle-strafing grandmother has already explained why Doom (2016) is a great game, so I'll use my review to talk about:

unfixed issues and bugs.

If - before finishing the last mission of the campaign - use Mission Select to complete previous mission objectives, find secrets, earn Masteries, level up runes, or buy upgrades, and then go back into the current mission and pick Restart the Mission... you will lose ALL THE PROGRESS you have made on that save file since you started the current mission, and not just the stuff you did in the latter. I've lost 2 hours that way.

If you use Mission Select (or load up a previously unlocked Rune Challenge) before acquiring all the Runes exclusively from within the current mission, your save file will become unelligible for The Circle is Complete (Earn all Runes) achievement. Which is why I don't have it, despite having IDDQD (Upgrade all Runes).

Twice in 24 hours of gameplay a demon fell out of bonds of the map, becoming invincible even to splash attacks, and I needed to reload the checkpoint. Which is not a load, but would qualify for that one Doofenshmirtz meme, right?

The secret-finding upgrade is a bit buggy with one or two specific secrets (one false positive, one false negative). And it won't detect the levers that open doors blocking Classic Maps.

...which is a shame, since most other secrets are reasonably findable, but the levers are hidden without rhyme or reason in some completely random, unremarkable spaces and blend in so incredibly well I once spent several minutes trying to find one in a tiny room despite reading up where to find it.
(Have you ever read one of these Harry Potter fanfics where Voldemort turns some random grain of sand into a Respawn Beacon phylactery Horcrux, so nobody will be able to ever find it and destroy it? Yeah, it seems like the dev that placed the levers was a fan.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore you buy this game, you need to be aware of what it is. If you expect it to be "just another nice shooting game", then you are dead wrong. You are in hell, and creatures from hell wants to rip you apart. The scenery, the sound, the gore - all of it combines to create an atmosphere that says "maybe this is what hell would look AND feel like, if I was a super soldier with big guns."

If this is something you'd like to experience, then I say purchase it, because it does the job well. However, if you have a faint heart (I'm not even joking on this one) or if you do not like to get scared by the demonic voices screeching your ears off, then stay away.

I would personally recommend the single player campaign and to play with headphones on and at a high volume.\t\t\t\t\t
