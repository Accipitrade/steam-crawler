\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(NOTE: all of my hours are based on the campaign. the multiplayer aspect is irrelavant to my opinion as by and large i don't care about multiplayer aspects, never have, never will.)

Before this game i was checked out of FPS games. they were boring, limiting, and incredibly short experiences. As emphasis was placed (i feel wrongly) for years on maintaining a sense of "realism" and focusing on multiplayer experiences.

One of the last new FPS' i had played was KILLZONE 3 and i was so bored by it i didn't finish it. The last FPS that i had bought for full retail (79.99) was CoD: World at War and i finished that in one 4 hour sitting and quickly returned it. I had dabbled and returned to the genre occasionally (notably i bought the newest AvP) but overall i hadn't seriously played an FPS or expected to like an FPS for the better part of a decade.

Then i bought DOOM

I feel this game is the cure for all of the issues that plague the current AAA shooter genre:

Are you sick of generic military settings?
Are you fed up with boring cover based shooting galleries?
Are you sick and f***ing tired of only being able to carry 2 guns?
Are you tired of guns in fps games all feeling exactly the same?
Are you tired of your character moving at a "realistic" speed?
Do you come home from work disatisfied and angry?
Do you feel like you sometimes want to revel in brutal violence in the comfort and safty of your underwear?

DOOM is for you.

The key to all of this is that the game rewards aggression, eg. slow methodical sniper tactics will likely get you surrounded and killed, the game boasts 2 different shotguns encouraging you to get as close and intimate with the hordes of hell as possible, get within chainsaw range and you get the bonus of having your ammo refilled. As a result it strikes that fine balance of making you the player feel vulnerable and yet totally invicible. Its combat refreshingly feels less like choreographed ballet of death and more like a improvisational beating with a toilet lid.

If you don't like it, that's fine. but i'm never going back to the tedium of coverbased, modern military, multiplayer focused shooters ever again.

I paid 80.00 for this and i don't feel ripped off, and that is reason enough alone to recommend it, and i can't do so enough.\t\t\t\t\t
