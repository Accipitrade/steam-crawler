\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSINGLE PLAYER: Fast paced, fluid in its mechanics as well as gameplay, story that is immersive yet at the same time doesn't try too hard and doesn't feel cliche. The enemies are diverse and you can see the hard work and detail the dev team has put into each and every enemy, from how they react to bullets, to their AI patterns, as well as the flesh wounds; they all surprised me for each and every creature. Atmoshpere is amazing, this engine is just the perfect match for a traditional DOOM reboot, it feels believable and just the overall color pallete for all of the areas in the game are just a joy to run through. Map design is almost close to genius. There were some combat areas where after I was done blowing through a horde of demons, I found myself to never have been so impressed and giddy over a map that welcomes running, jumping, and ducking through things to dodge my enemies in possibly the best executed way. Bottom line, it was just done extremely well, everything feels polished, I cant complain.

MULTIPLAYER: I dont want to get too in depth, but I found the multiplayer to be surprisingly refreshing. Somehow as I was watching videos of gameplay in multiplayer before games release, I left feeling "eh" about it. In fact I bought the game intending I would probably be playing singleplayer for the most part; the opposite is true. I dont think ive ever been so immersed in a multiplayer shooter. I can only describe it as the same way counter strike always felt to me. Simple. Balanced. Fluid mechanics. To me that is all these multiplayer games need. The most succesful ones are those that take the time to balance a handful of weapons and make them interesting, instead of throwing a bunch of trash weapons that nobody uses, then the meta takes effect. Im happy to say this game does not have that problem, for I have had many killer leaderboard scores for almost every weapon.

CONCLUSION: BUY THIS GAME. THIS IS WHAT A 60 DOLLAR GAME SHOULD BE LIKE.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHi everyone,

First of all, like Marty Stratton told at the Doom E3 Presentation - "..Ladies and gentlemen, this is DOOM.". And I totally agree with that. The most impressive (besides great graphics - it is not Unreal Engine 4, but very near to it) thing is how the modern game features are combined with game mechanics of the most classic shooter ever. Weapon upgrades (and switching between them), runes, suit improvements, and so on are not hurting your eyes, and really change the gameplay and how effeciently you shoot demons. But still it looks and feels very classic, very fast and extremely exciting.

Yeah, like in every 3D shooter outhere there is some smell of repetitiveness, but that is what shooters are about. Shoot the nearest weakest demon so he becomes stunned, smash his head, grab some HP that he dropped, switch to rocket launcher, shoot the biggest bad guy and delay his long range attack with that shot, jump higher, shoot the red barrel under you, get back and gather some other sh*t dropped out of foes that were nearby that barrel, and so on. I like such games exactly for these very dynamic scenarios. When you plan your battle, you may get hurt or loose, but then you load from checkpoint and play better and smarter. Honestly, I can`t remember last game that gave me so many emotions and rage at the same time :D Also, the levels design is very cool and corresponds to gameplay at 100%.

Other thing that surprised me - is that I am playing for 27 hours. I am at the end already, but still this amount of hours amuses me. That`s not very common nowadays, especially for shooter`s singleplayer part. This is one more big plus.

One more important thing for me is that the game is not so frightful as Doom 3 was. I just couldn`t play it (the same as Dead Space, for example). I cannot say that I am a p*ssy, but games can scare me very easy :D Yes, sometimes new Doom made me fear a little, but this was not a disgusting fear that made me quit the game immediately and remove it from my PC, but a totally different thing that gave me more rage and wish to go further and kick all the asses on my way.

What about technical part - at one level when I was destroying one cooling system (don`t want to spoil anything, who played the game will understand) FPS has dropped to 13-15, and game slowed itself down about twice (normal FPS was >30 on my 4k display) - all animations, movement, shooting, everything. That was pretty surprising, and I am not very sure what to feel about it, but it was still much better than playing with honest 10-15 FPS and loose because you don`t see anything. Several times (2 or 3) in the middle of the hard battle the game just silently crashed. That was annoying, but not very long, because I cursed the game engine a little, launched game again and forgot about that crash immediately.

Someone may wonder, why there is no single word about the storyline and the plot. Well, I am not playing Doom for plot twists, so please ask someone else about this part :)

Conclusion.
As an Unreal and Duke Nukem (I was not so used to Doom when I was young) series fan and hardboiled Unreal Tournament player I recomment this game to anyone who wants to test his skills and see how many asses he/she will kick before dying on one another level. 97% of masterpiece!\t\t\t\t\t
