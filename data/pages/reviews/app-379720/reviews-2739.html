\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has no shortage of fun! DOOM returns to its roots as a simple, mind numbing, and violent, gore-fest! No thinking required, which is a nice change of pace sometimes when you simply want to relax and destress.

I had originally tried this game on Xbox One, and quickly became bored with it. For whatever reason, the game just didn't feel right to me. So I was hesitant to try it on PC when it was on sale. Thankfully, this is one of the few modern games that has a demo available. So, I downloaded it, only to discover that it is the only game on my machine that takes full advantage of my GPU (1050 Ti) without the CPU bottlenecking it (Q9550 Core 2 Quad OCd at 3.07GHz). On 1080p ultra, with Vulkan! And boy does the PC version have a much better feeling!

Having the monitor so close, I really feel like I am part of the action. And DOOM never fails to deliver consistent action, with only short breaks lasting a couple minutes each as you move towards your next objective, before thrusting you back into the fast-paced meyham!

And don't get me started on the multiplayer! I'm not usually the biggest fan of arena style multiplayers, but this one takes the cake. It is just as action packed and fast-paced as the single player, making it super fun to play against other people online, and a nice break away from the likes of COD.

Honestly! Even if you have this game on console already, I think you owe it to yourself to play it on PC, even if that means waiting until it goes on sale again. And don't worry too much about your PC being below the minimum requirements. So long as you have someting along the lines of a Core 2 Quad, or newer, and a decent GPU, this game will probably play fine. Try the demo first if unsure. Hell, I'm even playing this on only 4GB of RAM. Though, it does occasionally stutter, which would probably disappear with the required 8GB (which I will have shortly, just waiting for it to arrive).

DOOM, you are the reason that I have decided to move over to PC gaming!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is Sonic the Doom-Hog, and it works pretty well.

In around eleven hours (minus some time spent idle while taking a few breaks), I beat the game on Hurt Me Plenty, while only finding one classic Doom level and finding maybe a third of the collectible toy Doomguys. So, the single-player campaign has a solid amount of content. And the combat is quite fun.

I have a few reservations, though.

As someone who played the original Doom when it was brand-new (I was only 5 or 6, and my dad brought home a shareware version first, given to him by a co-worker, before he bought the whole game), I have to say that this a tremendous departure in the pace of the action. Which has its benefits, but, good grief, does it need to be quite so non-stop?

It's really, really jarring how this one got paced out... not necessarily an issue much of the time, but it's so much faster-paced than the original Doom games from the 90's, as well as the Doom 3 game from the early 2000's. There's just not quite the same feel in terms of pacing... everything encourages going as fast as possible without being out of control, which is very different from the Doom and Doom II of old, where it often paid to snipe with the pistol rather than use the gatling gun. I just wish there were a few more moments of really having to conserve ammo. Which brings us to...

... the Chainsaw and BFG. Two classic weapons, two major changes. The chainsaw is nowhere near as fun in this game, as you need fuel for it, and until the final few levels, you might not even have the ammo capacity to really use it on the big guys. It sure is useful, though, now that chainsaw kills drop ammo like you're chainsawing a pinata. Then the BFG... it's still pretty satisfying killing everything on screen with it, but you get 3 shots or so with it, and the ammo doesn't deplete the plasma gun ammo. It's just three seperate shots.

For other changes, I personally like the double-jumping... I actually wish it was just something you started with in the very first level. And the Glory Kill system is pretty reasonable... you get a smattering of lesser enemies in almost every engagement, so you can use grenades if you like (another new feature) to clear them out, or use Glory Kills on each one for a boost of around 20 health, and possibly ammo and/or armor, depending on your perk choices.

The skill/perk system is paced fairly well. It keeps you on par with the challenges you encounter. But it feels forced... and arbitrary. Why, oh why, am I allowed to carry 20 rockets but only 40 shotgun shells? Weirder still, if I don't take the perk for grenades, I actually am allowed fewer grenades than BFG shots... you read that correctly. Yes, fewer grenades than BFG uses. Sure, grenades respawn automatically at a slow pace, but, just, why allow fewer grenades to be carried than BFG shots? It's not like the grenades are all that powerful, either.

All-in-all, I'm mostly nit-picking, I guess, because the game's good, and it brings the series back to what it started with: metal music, very little in the way of cutscenes and dialogue, arcade-style power-ups, and atmospheric level design.

But if you get this, get it for the single-player campaign and user-made levels... the multiplayer scene looks pretty quiet already, and it's barely a year-old game.\t\t\t\t\t
