\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRather than tell you how awesome Doom is as a game, which I'm sure plenty other reviews have done it better already, consider this a love letter to id software's game design.

So I finished Doom back in 2017 on Ultra Violence. And I was left mostly satisfied. I came back to it a few months later to play through the Nightmare difficulty.

And I got blasted to heck by the very first imp you face just because I tried to use a pistol to kill it to conserve my shotgun ammo. 'Oh so this is going to be one of those difficulties' , I thought. Everything does double damage to you. 'Man what a cheap way to increase the challenge!' I exclaimed and left it alone.

A few days later I found myself hankering again to play Doom and this time I stuck with it. And then I started to notice the changes.

Sure the big baddies are tough but now the greatest threat are imps. They dodge, skitter around, cling to pillars just outside my view and pelt me with fireballs. Oh a Baron spawned, ok. But where are those imps?! Gotta kill those buggers! Usually I greet a hell knight with a smirk and a fully charged up Gauss cannon in siege mode. But I can't afford to slow down to use my Siege Gauss because I can hear those damn imps flanking me getting ready to melt me once I commit to the attack. It's amazing.

All the runes you considered lame are now worth a second look. The larger range on Glory Kill activation? Pfft who needs that right? But on Nightmare, it changed into this teleportation ability that might just pull you out of a surrounded situation. I still remember the time I just zipped up a platform to an imp to bash it to death with a glory kill,barely managing to avoid the bevy of hell knights that had charged me. Things like Mid Air Mobility became very useful as one of the few times you were safe (comparitively), was when you use a jump pad. Now with more mobility you can do crazy crap in the air and touch down in an advantageous position, ready to wreak havoc.

And here is my second nasty shock when you find out that all those tasty health pickups after a hard fought battle just give you just miniscule amounts of health. That's right pickups are nerfed too. The amount of health, armor and ammo that you get are very small especially when compared to the previous level of difficulty. That felt like a cheap shot to me at first. But then I realized the amount of ammo and health dropped after a Glory Kill are buffed. Doom on Nightmare doesn't encourage you to strafe around pillars, sniping at enemies. It wants you to wade into the thick of it.

That brings us to grenades. Honestly, I used to find them weak and ineffectual. But here we have a versatile way to take out a platform full on enemies without showing yourself, having a hologram draw their fire while you reposition for a better chance at killing them and finally, siphon grenades that restores not only health but also armor with the relevant runes equipped. I had looked down on Stun Grenade on Plasma Rifle but here I'm stunning a group of enemies, throwing a Siphon grenade into their midst and regain some health and armor. And BFG ammo? A miracle for some of the tough fights.

This is a near perfect game in my record. To be this awesome with the game design for a mere another level of difficulty is amazing!\t\t\t\t\t
