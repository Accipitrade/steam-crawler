sound(non music) seem to be consistant and defintly adds to the whole feel of it being ....well hell on Mars...

the demon mechanic is very interesting and a fun ride when you get your turn

overall i would give this game a........8/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS IS A REVIEW IN PROGRESS, IT WILL BE FINISHED ONCE I HAVE PLAYED EVERY OUNCE OF THIS GAME.


Hell.

Hell is real, and when you awake from what seems to be an eternity since the last game DOOM takes you right back in from the start. However this Doom is not like the last we had, which was a blend of horror and action, no this Doom goes right back to the roots of the series and pulls no punches in the process.

From the get-go you can see how great the game looks from weapon textures to the bloodstains on the wall, and even though the enemys de-spawn when killed, its still a beautiful bloody mess before they do. when you do go outside on mars it still holds true and looks fantastic, I dont let graphics dictate my purchases though, so lets get down to the good stuff.

I didnt have much of an idea how the single player was going to be, since they havent shown too much, but in the time I HAVE spent with the game has been an absolute blast to play, and it feels SO MUCH like the original dooms it almost makes me sick. The gunplay is smooth, fast and punishing if you slip up. the armor/health around the map is just enough to keep you going, but not enough to hold your hand. THIS IS NOT A HORROR GAME and honestly im torn between hating that and loving it, while I will miss the horror factor from the last game I cant help but welcome my old friend with open arms.

The game has a few new things as well, upgradable weapons, armor, and weapon mods and new to the series and a welcome addition as it matches the gameplay this game has taken.

As of right now the game feels like a solid 9/10, however this will be updated when I finish the single player\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's exactly what I wanted it to be.

I finished 2 levels of the campaign, played one multiplayer match and played one SnapMap level.

The campaign is going to be insane, I can already tell just by the first couple of levels. There are some cool stuff for fans of the classic Doom games as well that's apart of it which makes it that much more awesome :)

Everything is fast paced, there's no reloading, there are plenty of suit and weapon mods and loads of collectibles/secrets in each level.

The multiplayer is more in-depth than what the beta was and I got into a match almost instantly, there are plenty more game modes now and I can tell that multiplayer is also going to be a blast but I'd rather finish campaign before venturing into everything else.

I think I'm most excited for SnapMap because I'd love to make my own co-op scenarios to play with friends and see what the community thinks of maps that I could create, can't wait to explore all of the options with SnapMap. Might edit this review when I start using it.

I never take time to write reviews about the games I've played but Doom deserves every bit of a positve review from me because I just love simple, classic style shoot em' up games and Doom delivers exactly that. Not to mention that it runs very well. (with a 970 - 60 FPS at all times)

Buy it, especially if you've played the previous Doom games and looking for a more modern take on it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe DOOM MARINE is back!

This is old school FPS. But what does that mean...

First - You have a health meter. You take damage, that meter drops. If you want to get health back you need to grab one of the limited supply medkits in the level.

Second - You have an armor meter. you take damage, that meter drops too. Luckly that meter stop some of the damage that would have been done to the Health Meter. If you need more, you grab some of the limited supply that is spread throughout the level. Oh, and it is in short supply.

Third - Weapons have a limited supply of ammo. This is not a spray and play game. Need more ammo? Grab some ammo boxes, don't worry however, these are in limited supply too.

Fourth - Cover... you have none. A cover mechanic in this game is pointless. Once you are in combat, it is nonstop until you kill all the demons, or they kill you. Taking cover would slow you down and give them the advantage.

Fifth - Reload...you have none. This isn't a missing mechanic, it is a blessing. Reloading takes time away from killing enemies that are relentless in their desire to see you die.

Sixth - Sprint... you are always sprinting. You do not jog, you run. If you don't want to run, there is a keybind to make you walk.
....
The game is brutal... it is not only a savage game, but a hard pushing and punishing game too.
The game is challenging... the game doesn't do much hand holding.
....
What about the story? The game starts, and then you start slaughtering demons. Why? What do you mean why? Because they are f-ing demons, that is why.
.....
My advice?

"Take a deep breath, and find your balls. And when you find them, finish the F-ing job!"
