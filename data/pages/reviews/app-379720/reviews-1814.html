\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI went into playing DOOM with a bit of a bias, but not a positive one. I haven't played a DOOM game in quite some time, and I've always perceived the game to shallow in terms of game play - Run. Gun. Rinse. Repeat. If that's what you like, this game can be that for you, and you can do that the end of the experience, and you will probably clock in between 12-13 hours, start to finish. I finished the game in 16 hours. If I paid full release price, I would probably be a little peeved by this, but I got it on sale for under $10 dollars - BARGAIN!

However, if you like to explore, and upgrade armor and guns, there are some clever tricks here that extend the experience a bit longer, and enable you have a bit more fun with the weapons. Throughout each level there are a multitude of secrets to uncover, and several "instanced challenges" to complete - the former provides you with a "currency" of sorts that you can spend to mod your weapons and upgrade your armor.

When you purchase all the mods available for a gun, it unlocks a weapon specific challenge that if completed, unlocks a special feature for said weapon. I found the more powerful the upgrade and gun, the more challenging the task to complete. I was able to complete all but two of the complete gun upgrades, and nearly maxed out he armor and equipment upgrades. I probably could have gotten all the gun upgrades if I had played a little smarter, and only realized this after I was close to the end of the game.

For a game that doesn't have a lot of mechanics, the gameplay is really fun!! Surviving a huge wave of demons is quite rewarding. The glory kills (which can be upgraded to provide ammo, armor and health) are cool, and there's a variety of ways to use them on each different demon type depending on where your sites are facing when you execute them. There is also some platforming, and it works surprisingly well, even though it is an FPS. I think I missed one or two jumps, but only because I messed them up. I never found myself fighting the controls. Boss fights are challenging, but not frustratingly so, and when I got the patterns down and beat them, I had a good feeling of accomplishment.

I'm typically not into the story or the lore of games, especially in an FPS - I mean, how deep can it be, right? I gotta say that I read nearly every codex, and I sat through every monologue and explanation. A lot of love and effort went into writing this, and it certainly shows. With James Spader voicing the Artificial Intelligence guiding you through the game, there's not much to complain about.

Graphically and sound wise, what can you say except this game looks and sounds great! The music the sound effects and environments are all masterfully created.

I don't have any interest in the multiplayer, but from what I've seen, it is probably a fun experience.

For someone who got this on a lark (probably because I was drunk and it was cheap), and wasn't expecting much. I really had a great time with DOOM. It actually has me looking forward to DOOM Eternal to see how the story progresses. Two enthusiastic thumbs up.\t\t\t\t\t
