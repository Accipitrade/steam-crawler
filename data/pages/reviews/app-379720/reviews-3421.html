Just finished the Campaign on NIGHTMARE difficulty, gonna try ULTRA-NIGHTMARE...

The story finishes SO FREAKING GOOD. I want to play it again & again...

All levels are pretty well designed, some are mind boggling... But overall, BEST FPS in a very long time.. I dabled with Multiplayer, it's okay, but not really why I bought DOOM.. Unlocking the classic levels is a bit of a chore after a few levels, some of the Doom Guy dolls are in IMPOSSIBLE locations w/o using a NO CLIP cheat or something like that...

The weapons, are great once you've spent time upgrading them, unfortunately the little bots that you have to use to unlock modding are far and few between.. By the time I got a few weapons upgraded the credits were rolling.

Runes are a VERY DIFFICULT challenge, example: one rune has you shoot IMP'S with a super shotgun, each kill only give 2 FREAKING seconds.... You only start w/ 6 measly seconds. Thanks Bethesda/ID you couldn't POSSIBLY have made the initial timer 30 seconds? really...

Those few things aside, the overall game is pretty fun, I never tire of slicing demons in half with the chainsaw...

Online is a whole other animal, too crazy...

Snap Map is REALLY COOL, I'm just learning the basics but I can see myself making some good co-op levels which are strangely missing in the core game....

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are looking for Bullet Storm meets Unreal Tornament, then by all means get this game. If you are looking for DOOM, go download the DOS Original.

It's not all bad though, the graphics are pretty and the sounds are very well done. This is to be expected with Bethesda games and full points and thumbs up to the graphics and audio teams. That's where it stops.

Controls
DOOM guy slides around the stage like he is ice skating the whole while, which is decently smooth, but it handles like being locked on TURBO in old Doom. The absurd footstep sound effects that accompany his rediculous Dhalsim yoga strides are laughable.

Sound:
So remember how I said they had great sound, well it is great... the first 40 times you hear it. If you'd like to experience, what i can only discribe as the "cacophony of disgust", stay in a room with 2 or more zombies. Their overlapping, hideouse, rapid fire, cries of "dear god shoot me so you don't have to listen to me", will make anyone want to mute all sounds. The music also is nice and has a nostalgic feel but it falls prey to the same blunders of only playing during the wave fighting and just ends into static ambiance as you enter a very dull stage till the next fight. I can understand that you need high impact sound tracts during the highest points of action and/or tension, but dont drop me like a the ball Bethesda was holding when they OK'ed this game to ship. I guess the scenes do fit the lifeless and dull atmosphere of mars when you are out of combat.

Combat:
I can't tell if they intentional wanted to take an epic crap all over DOOM or if they just thought wave combat made Doom 3 good.... I'm suprised one of the level designers didn't slap the developers upside the head with a 2x4 when they desided on wave battles. They lock you in tiny arenas in which you have to run around in circles like a constipated weener dog while firing idiotically at anything that happens to cross infront of you. I mean, if i wanted to play Quake Arena, I WOULD HAVE BOOTED UP QUAKE ARENA. I'll get to thier AI in a later section.
The regular level enemies, be they few and far between are a nice DOOM touch, it almost convinces me that this game was inspired by the original Dooms. I can see why they had to go with the atrocity that is wave combat. It's due to the awkward level design that just doesn't support original DOOM style play.
They give the players grenades and a backpack full of weapons (i'll get to those later), to try and sell the "You can play whatever style you like". Its a lie, a straight up, bold face, "it's 4 player co-op", LIE. The designers have forced players into the previously stated, running around like a constipated weener dog style. You can try and play it like an older Doom, Duke 3D, Blakestone, Wolfenstien or an other early era shooter, but no... you will die insanly fast due to the way that drops and AI is dispatched at you. Basically they have made it "play our way! or face the consequences!".

Weapons
So you're hoping for that sweet sweet Plasma Rifle or a Chain Saw to dispatch some demonic justice? To bad, so sad, devs gave you the finger. Not in the, "that's rudde" way, in the "THAT'S NOT WHERE FINGERS GO!" way. Plasma Rifle, now #3, and worthless. It's basically like opening a bag of Skittles and throwing them in the face of a stampeding Rhino. If the bolts manage to hit, they do little to nothing at all. Chainsaw? Nope, runs on gas now so you will rarely be shreading some demon meat, to the melodious reving of a Gore Machine. The Shotgun? well that's about the only thing that can stagger a Knight... works even at long range. Odd, cause 20 rounds to the dome piece with other weapons and he doesn't even flinch. The Heavy Rifle is nice but is worthless till you can get some ammo upgrades. Really it would take to long to go into deatail about every gun and how they blow a walruses enourmous equipment, so I will just give you the good gun. The Pistol... that's it... the Pistol. Had to be one of the Halo fan boys watching too much Screw Attack and getting upset, but the pistol is the best gun in the game. Upgrade it and you can plow through enemies faster that a Backpack full of guns on the new DOOM... go figure.

Multiplayer
Well if you heard the devs talking about how there was Co-op, might as well take your hopes and dreams of a fun time with your friends and bury them out back with gran-grans missing coarpse. That's right, they lied to us all. No story mode co-op, in fact, you wanna play co-op? then go build all your own stuff in this bull crap knock off of Doom Fever for 20 hours. The Devs just couldn't be bothered with making Doom, true to Doom. So take your sobbing and utter disgust for them elseware. But seriously, I like that I get a Doom game builder but COME ON, WHERE'S THE CO-OP CAMPAIGN?! Some one should be punched in the balls until they die for that one.
Seeing how this game has gone so far, I refuse to play this Deathmatch online.

Final
So, the only reason I got this game after I swore off Bethesda (the new EA) was because I was nagged into getting it. I would have just pirated it and not felt utterly ripped off. On the EXTREAMLY off chance it was good I would have appologized and paid real money for it. But this attrocity, of a sham, of a garbage heap left in the summer sun for 3 weeks, force fed to people with pretty words and broken promises, should not stand.
I would be completely fine with all this games innumerable faults if it just had the campaign co-op. I want to over look the terrible forced game play style, the atrocious sounds that should be patched, the bland levels and horrid wave combat arenas. I'd even ignore the fact that these ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• cads absolutly failed at capturing what Doom was, and instead gave us bobble heads of nostalgia and product placement. But I can't, and nor should anyone else.

There's only one way Bethesda can ever make it up, Seppuku. Take the blade, you know what to do... die with some form of honor.\t\t\t\t\t

Lore:-

The gameplay is just what a DOOM fan wants. Keep collecting ammo, pickups etc. There is NO RELOAD in this game. You need to keep picking up ammunation. Results:-

Overall from what I have played so far the game has receieved a massive positivity from me and just what I expected. It does indeed feel like a BLAST FROM THE PAST kind of thing. Secrets are back. You need to have pickups etc and collectables. I think so far DOOM is the game of the year at the moment. Bethseda has done it again.

My only qualms are the map and level layout. So far I'm loving it and would urge people to give it a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

Its everything I hoped for it to be and more, and it KEEPS GROWING.

This is the game that leaped over the pit that Duke Nukem fell into, and ducked under the bar that RoTT hit its head a bit too hard on, following the path the Wolfenstein TNO/TOB had trailblazed.

I just love and adore this game to death, its pretty much a modern poster child of what an accessible and paletable Old School FPS should play and feel like.

It is alone, far more than worth it for the 60 dollars, and you're in luck fellow gamer because I've noticed this game is regularly on sale for half that price on Steam. So if you still feel ancy about hoping in, then I'd wait for sale. Trust me though, every penny is worth it.

I've played 130+ hours and yet STILL, I don't feel even close to being done with it; I'm only half way through my Nightmare run. (Mostly cause I get distracted with building maps.)

Snapmap has grown up quite a bit from what it first shipped out as, but there is still a lot of room for it to grow. The developers down at Escalation Studios are very good at listening to the crowd, cause I'm pretty sure they added in the Berserk power-up into Snapmap because I had requested/suggested it to them over twitter awhile back.

I'm optimistic though that they won't abandon it as soon as the DLC is finished, but I'll be realistic and say that it'll probably stop growing soon after.\t\t\t\t\t
