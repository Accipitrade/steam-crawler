\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing. Surfing waves of enemies, in a gun fest of gore multipliers, all the while breezing through a maze of hidden paths and secrets.
Matching sound track to compose the painting, and an actually interesting story tying it all together.
Great game.
It's really hard to trasmit how good this game is by words, you really have to try it to understand it. No surprise that early videos were underwhelming. But experiencing the gameplay itself is like eating cotton candy. Doesn't look eatible at first sight, but it has a soft touch. Smells good, tastes sweet. Melts on your mouth, you feel sweetness all around and before you know it your grabing more and stuffing it into your mouth.
Doom grabs you by surprise with it's constant moving-no reload ballet, and before you know it, you will have sunk a few hours in it.
Until you finally stop to think about it and notice how good it is.
Also, early stability problems have been fixed for the most part, and after a few updates new SP and MP modes and maps have been added, making it an even more compelling and fullfilling experience and an essencial title in any gamers catalog.
If at release was a great buy despite the full price, at this point and after the price drop and with all the extra content, it's mandatory.

EDIT: And while all said above it's true, at this point (23/10/2016) after free update 4, the game can't be recommended. Campaign difficulty is bugged and reverts to normal (hurt me plenty) everytime you try the newly introduced arcade mode. And the game itself became an astouning 71 GB in size!!! Not a problem in itself if you have enough space, but be warned. When it gets fixed, if it gets fixed in an effective way it will return to positive, but for now the difficulty bug is progress breaking. And it's sheer size may leave people with poor conections deaming it not worth it for a SP game.

EDIT(31-12-2017):
Over a year later, at the end of 2017... Not Fixed. More interested on making bad DLCs, poor MP updates, and a Wii version, than actually fixing the PC version problems for early full-price adopters. I did find a blunt fix for myself. Not thanks to them, and not a great fix, but at least managed to finish it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is back!
Fans of the original Doom games feel right at home. It is very oldschool with modern implementations that contribute to the gameplay. It is fast paced and a tour de force with no punches pulled.
The singelplayer campaign is fun and the action only lets off for a few moments to give you some air to breathe.
Multiplayer is fun too. Feels like a new Unreal Tournament.
Snapmap is easy to use. And has well implemented community options. You cant build rooms from scratch, but it is complex enough to bring your own ideas and make your own levels.
Graphics and sound are just amazing. Everything in the game gets you pumped to kill demons.
I can easily recommend this game to all Doom fans and to FPS fans in general.

TLDNR: Doom with modern upgrades but still oldschool. One of the best FPS in years for the fast paced, action packed singelplayer campaign alone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to write a poem to honour this magnificent DOOM game, but all I could come up with was "I shoot, I grab, I mutilate, I dance, I sing, Cacodemon appears, and I am a sheeeitpants king". Cacodemons are scary man, despite their plushy toy alternative.

So all I want to express here is that if you are lucky to have played the original Doom and Doom II, you can now see what those game would have been if they were released today, by the same people who know how to make good games. This sentence probably made as much sense as the story in DOOM does, which is a good thing - because there is no time for it! You can't carefully sneak your way through this game, and the sooner you understand it, the better of you will be - you are what the demons fear, including the bosses. Don't respect them. Don't even try to fear them. Get in their faces and shoot them right in the mugger with the double barreled shotgun. Scream something vile, and turn to the next poor demon-guy. Remember to double jump.

Doom is really well made raw bass-filled fun, that truly does justice to the old school shooter. Runs smoothly on maximum settings on my three year old laptop. Oh, and when you are done bashing demon skulls in, you can, if you want, look around for secrets, which are hidden everywhere and introduce a fun exploration element into the game.

Play this for single player, by the way. I never managed to find a multiplayer game.\t\t\t\t\t
