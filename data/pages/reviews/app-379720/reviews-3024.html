\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll speak only to the single player, because to me, that's what Doom has always truly been.

They get everything right that Doom 3 got wrong. The music is driven and angry, the enemies are driven and angry, the setting is driven and angry, and the hero is driven and angry. Everything about Doom 1 and 2 is here - the manic pace of ludicrous speed sprinting, big weapons that are the perfect foil to the endless supply of demons. Then a few things were added, a sense of story in the game, beyond just a cut of text. A gory method of melee murder that maybe the very prototype for was around in the early games, but now really shines. Flowing in and out of the melee executions is gleeful and, while a bit repetitive as you go, still pretty fun.

Where does it not quite get there? I think it's lacking those moments of suspense and foreboding that hell clawing its way in to our universe should have. Not every demon is just gonna plop in to your face, not every detail is going to be just an in your face onslaught. This game series wallows in the christian tradition of hell, and hell has subtle undertones and true horrors. They missed that from Doom 1 and 2, and they missed the foreboding, the suspense of Doom 3. I think the scenes of agony and torture we saw in Doom 1 and 2 were treated not quite right for this doom - they lack the sense of existential torture, of unending pain. There should be no dead in Hell, only those who wish to be.\t\t\t\t\t
