\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, i enjoyed this game.

The controls feel nice, although once you have all the weapons it's a bit of a pain to reach across your keyboard and hit the number keys for them.
Movement feels great, and i don't think i missed a jump even once during my playthough, the ledges are very forgiving.

I did encounter a few bugs, though nothing gamebreaking. A few graphical glitches on some static objects, an area or two where performance dropped dramatically. Screen tearing is also TERRIBLE without v-sync enabled, but there are plenty of graphics options to fiddle with, so that's not an issue. The game performed very well in almost all areas, and i never noticed any hitches or stutters outside of those couple glitchy areas.

I will say that some of the skill challenges kind of suck, as it can be difficult to accomplish them even when you're focused on doing so. Enemies don't respawn once killed, and opportunities to kill them "naturally" as required by the skill challenges can be rare. How often are eight demons going to line up in a row, so you can kill them with a single shot? In my experience, pretty much never.

The weapon balance is also all over the place. The combat shotgun is the very first weapon you get besides the starting pistol, and it gets a grenade launcher upgrade, which can be further upgraded to a cluster bomb pretty easily. The cluster bomb uses a single shotgun shell, and does more damage than the rocket launcher.
Meanwhile, the rocket launcher gets an upgrade that lets it fire three homing missiles, but it takes so long to charge up that you could have fired three missiles in that time anyways, and if you don't constantly hover over an enemy, it loses it's lock and has to charge up again.
Likewise, the starting pistol is literally useless. Even fully upgraded, it's only good for killing possessed and imps, the two weakest enemies in the game. However, you can do that just as easily with the melee button, which also gives you health, so there's no point. Several weapons also use the same ammo, like the combat shotgun and super shotgun, or the chaingun and the heavy machine gun. I personally wish they had their own ammo stacks, because the divergent paths of the various weapons makes them useful in different situations. The heavy machine gun for example, has an upgrade path that lets you fire mini-rockets continuously, which is useful for clearing out large groups of enemies, or quickly taking down tougher demons. However, if you use up all your machine gun ammo for rockets to take down tougher enemies, you now have none left when you want to switch to the chaingun and mow down all the weaker enemies.
And the super shotgun... well, compared to the combat shotgun whose every cluster bomb blast is stronger than a rocket launcher, i can honestly say firing it even once is simply a waste. A super shotgun should feel super, and it just doesn't, which was very disappointing.

I did quite enjoy the level design, at least during the mars base segments. It felt polished and fun to explore, but once you get to hell, it's a different story. It's like they blew their whole design budget on the mars base and phoned in hell, which is mostly just bare rock and skulls everywhere. Very little fire and magma, nothing much at all to see or explore. Literally just floating in a brown void. Disappointing.

Another thing that was somewhat disappointing was the enemies, or more specifically the bosses.
I never really felt anything when fighting them. No adrenaline rush, no worry, nothing. The regular demons quickly become big and tanky, and you fight them in the dozens constantly, so all the boss fights just felt like an extended version of that. There wasn't really anything that made the bosses special, which made for a VERY disappointing ending. I was expecting something like shadow of the colossus, clinging to the body of a giant demon (i'd seen the skeletons of huge demons in the level design before) and destroying parts of it at a time to bring it down, but nothing like that ever happens. You don't get to fight satan, you don't get to bring down a giant demon, nothing special or interesting really happens, and boom, game over.

So yes, although i have my gripes and i believe it could have been done much better, in the end i still had a lot of fun. I played for eight hours over the course of two days, and rarely got bored. It does get repetitive, and i don't know how often i'd play it again, but it's certainly not a bad game by any means. Buy it on sale and you'll have a good time.\t\t\t\t\t
