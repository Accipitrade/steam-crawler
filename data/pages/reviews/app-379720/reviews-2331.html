\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA while ago I wrote a review of DOOM 3, a game that is part of a peculiar Christmas tradition for me, and though I knew there was a critically acclaimed remake of the game around I did not dared to approach it, in aprt because I was not sure if hardware could handle it , in part because Iw as afriad it woudl take the place of a game that is most dear to me. I will not pretend to elaborate a unbiased review of the game, because Im biased, Doom 3 is a darling to me, it is part of memories that are most dear to me.

But with that ou of the way DOOM 2016 is awesome. A fast paced, gory , slug-fest that clads you in mighty armor and makes you wild the most fierce of weaponry to exile the denizens of the nine Circles back to the fiery pit they came from. It satisfactory at every turn, it doesnt meddle into pointless escatological questions of good and evil(something it is appreciated in teh current political climate) and operates on very simple premise: Demons are evil, kill demons until tehre no more demons. End of Story.

This however pose the question of if actually surpassed Doom 3. For most people the answer is an absolute YES. For me? It was no so clear cut. Doom 3 and DOOM 2016 are very different beasts altogether: Doom 3 pus in the boots a regualr Marine, a rookie at that, that find himself in the middle of extraordinary events and has to do with all the manmade tools he can find to survive the hellish horde that has invaded the UAC complex until you find a Mystical artifact, forged with the sacrifice of an entire civilization, to be wielded by their greatest champion. There was a feeling of trepidation around the progression of Doom 3, you could see the corruption of this futuristic facility, a place where a brigther and better future for mankind was being forged, now being strangled by the coils of fleshy growths and blood spilled in unholy ritauls, the cacophony fo screams of those still living being silenced only be replaced by the cries of ethereal voices that plead for help...

The Hell of Doom 3 was different from DOOM 2016: It was not only a palce of limiteless rage and cruelty, but a palce of everlasting sorrow and sadness. And you , willing or not, were the last champion, wielding the weapons product of man´s ingenuity and of ancient sacrifice, replaying the role another brave - or desperate - soul played so long ago, reafirming that the notion that great conflict againt Evil is eternal, for Evil is Eternal. And only through the will to fight it , to save ourselves from it, it can be contained.

DOOM 2016 puts you on the skin of the Doom Slayer, a warrior so mighty, so beyond Hell´s ability to fight against, that through arcane ritual and subterfuge could only be given pause. An imposing demigod, that Evil not only opposses, but is afraid of, like a futuristic Archangel Michael, who stomps over Satan who in-turn thrashes , impotent, beneath his mighty greave. The only who could save the sane world from the hellish horde. A savior, if not the most beatific one.

And in the need for a savior, his existence was a statement in itself, that Evil can indeed, overwhelm man to a point in which it cannot save himself. That his will can be corrupted into believing that good can be made from evil, as the attempts to weaponize demons and the explotation of Argent energy demonstrate, That a little here and there can harken so much good, that offset the sacrifices being made. Thus paving the path to Hell on a road of good intentions. The Slayer cares not for goods intentions, nor for whatever good could be made from exploting Hell and its resources, unlike the Marine in Doom 3, he does not act out of necessity, but out of principle. Consequences be damned. He will not relent nor compromise.

After playing both games I could say that both in their atmosphere and themes, they are indeed different, very different, but at the same time, complementary. Both incarnating the very human paradox of the willingness to save ourselves and the need to be saved. Powerless and Powerful. Saved and Savior. But unified in the persuit of what is right and the defeat of what is wrong, principles seared into the soul of Mankind by a flame far more brighter than Hellfire.

That is why only recommend DOOM, emphatically, but I will continue to recommend all other entries in this venerable franchise (some which are dirt cheap). Remember my friend, you are also a Doom Marine, every day you fight your own demons . And I wish you all the luck in the world to perform Glory Kills on them.

Merry Christmas!!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis isn't a game, this is a work of art. Now, this could be listed as a "Sparkle, Sparkle, Where's my plot?" game. But why play a FPS for it's story? The "S" doesn't stand for "Story". But what ID gave us for a story is easy to understand and concise. So it all works out in the end.
The actual "shooting" aspect is amazing. And the enemies aren't Nazis or Zombies, so it feels good (but why would either of those be in any version of DOOM?). The gunplay is great with the roster of weapons provided. While it is good that the concept of reloading was removed, there is a drawback: ammunition is split amongst shared ammo types (to put it in proper terms: if the Combat Shotgun has 30 bullets remaining, the Super Shotgun has 30 shots remaining). Would it be too much to ask if all weapons had individual stock? The Gauss Cannon, Super Shotgun, and Chaingun consume ammo very quickly. And while you do get a boost to your max ammo limit, it is still a problem (albeit a minor problem).
The concept of Weapon Masteries was implemented quite well and offer a different play-style for different people. The Praetor Suit Upgrades are nice, although I do wish that there was a bit more of a "branching option". Although I have no clue how you'd implement it. Runes, I'd say, are borderline uneven. The effort you have to put in to unlock them isn't always worth the reward. The collectables (datalogs, UAC figurines, and classic maps) are there just to give you something to do if purging the world of demons isn't enough, and ultimately just gives completionists something to do.
Now I will say that the game is kinda short, and does leave you wanting more (the final boss could have been better), but what it does have is worked to it's absolute best (mostly). I enjoyed this game, and will forward to DOOM Eternal!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 2016 (Opinion)

The first Doom was my back up game in 1999, actually it was the only game my poor PC could play at the time. That and Wolfenstein. Let’s not forget my dad’s Age of Empire which he plays to this day. While everybody was busy exploring Black Mesa and PS 1 great array of games at the time, all I had was Doom. And Wolfenstein. With that being said, it sure was pure frantic fun blowing demons back to where they once came from. From the standout fast pace shooting and the ridiculous secrets you can find, the original Doom 1 and 2 was game I could always run to. Despite getting bored occasionally from playing it too many times, it was always fun to use the chainsaw or the BFG wiping everything out including my stress from classroom.


Summary
Playing it in 2018, I am pretty surprise that it’s pretty much the same game that keeps the same frantic shooting spree and ludicrous premise. And it’s definitely better for it. Obviously there have been new additions like glory kills and improvements done but it sort of throws away the horror elements that it was in Doom’s 3 reboot emphasising on adrenaline pumping first person action of old instead. This new 2016 DOOM starts with a bang, with the marine or Doomguy being strapped to some sort of altar and soon attacked by what I assume is zombies but apparently they are called the possessed. You are very soon creating carnage with melee kills and the pistol while exploring the Union Aerospace Corporation (UAC) which has been attacked. I was expecting a shooting and violence galore from what I gather from friends and reviews which it is but was still surprise by the developers when it came to world building and lore of Doom. There have been some added depth to the lore of creatures and this world through its codex as well as various exposition cut scenes. The plot is as generic as it could get. Really it’s about you stopping the forces of hell from coming over and the UAC from taking Hell energy to save Earth energy crisis. How many twist can you add to that kind of plot really but it’s delivered quite well especially the person who voiced Dr Samuel Hayden. The real blast of DOOM however is sending all these critters back to their hellhole anyway you see fit. Most of the time by shooting everything that moves and it does that like its 1993.

Detail Overall

DOOM’s shooting is as solid as it was in 1993 (Well for me it would have been 1999), strafing left and right firing away. It almost feel like you are constantly being pump with adrenaline. Over the course of the 8 to 12 hour campaign, you will be using an array of weapons to dispatch your enemies. From the iconic super shotgun to such classics like the plasma rifle and rocket launcher. Gone are the present day shooters of holding two fire arms, here you will be able to carry all your fire arms like Rambo. The weapons are a great blast to use and there is level of depth to using each of them depending on the layout or the amount enemies thrown at you. What makes it more fun is experimentation of these weapons. Almost all of them has an alternative firing mode and they are blast to experiment. The heavy assault rifle missile pod firing is like a rocket launcher chain gun once it’s upgraded to its maximum capabilities. While the plasma rifle has an alternative firing mode that allows you to stun enemies allowing you to use new strategies to attack your enemies. The addition of challenges to upgrades weapon mods to its final capacity is also extremely rewarding like sniping head shots on Hell knights. It almost feel like Bloodborne finishing a boss bottle.

Of course the most important addition is the glory kills mechanic. When enemies get stunned you are able to perform a glory kill that provides health, which creates a new strategy for some desperate attempt for a second breath when you are low on health. The glory kill animation are pretty good and different for all enemies. It also depends on which body parts you are looking at but it is satisfying (a bit funny too) tearing an imps arm and whacking it with its own arm. However they do get repetitive by the 6th or 7th hours of shooting and tearing limbs. I have heard comments that this mechanic slows the game down but personally I don’t think it does though. The chainsaw and BFG takes a somewhat different approach though now. They have limited ammo and the chainsaw kills provides ammo which becomes quite important in later stages when ammo gets a bit scarced.

Level design is pretty good and varied with secret areas as well as Easter eggs for fans of the series. Such as introducing classic maps and Doom guy dolls. The inclusion of classic maps is also pretty cool for newcomers who want to experience a bit of gaming history pioneer in FPS. The enemy design is good because they look great being transported from 1993 to 2016 especially the Cacodemon and Pinky. They still have a lot of their original design and they looked absolutely gorgeous now. The pinky now have some upgrades as well as they heavily armoured in front but weak from behind. The boss fights are remarkably thrilling, asking to use all of your weapons and platforming abilities. Yes this new DOOM has some platforming and for the most part it works quite well. DOOM can get a bit easy at times but playing at ultra-violence adds some difficulty to master. Lastly the music is excellent from the ominous dread to synth wave heavy metal music, the music flows remarkably well with its combat. I wish steam included the soundtrack with my purchase.

The inclusion of an arcade mode and multiplayer is icing on the cake. However I have heard that the weakest part of DOOM is its multiplayer, I have yet to test it. The ability to create maps is another bonus allowing for some creative level design experimentation. At the end of the day DOOM is a blast to play because in so many ways it plays like the DOOM of old with some great additions. Of course better graphics too. Its emphasis on single player is also a plus, yay no micro transactions to disrupt me. DOOM is definitely buy for FPS fans and especially for those who grew up with it. Watching all your favourites enemies brought to life with today’s graphics is awesome. The barons of hell towering figure while it throws that green flame at you is still a blast to watch. Overall DOOM was worth it. Ohhhh that satisfying super shotgun.
