\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game quite fondly reminds me of my childhood. While I was at a young age my parents were farmers, we owned a horse farm but we quite often raised pigs. While you would think I would fondly remember these memories, I often look back at them for these crazy times I had.

Ever since we had horses, which was before I was born, my dad would feed them methamphetamine on a daily basis in order to get them to be trippy, which would cause them to fall over and allow my dad to euthanize them via gunshot to the cranium. He did this almost every day in a desperate attempt to get away from this farm that he hated with all his cold heart that was quite literally made of stone because his pacemaker was that ancient. While looking back at this now in my later years I do think this was a great idea, it did not work on my mother, who was another story on her on. Often when this would happen we would just buy another horse.

Because this method did not work, we had to continue to do this in hopes of it working, eventually to where my dad "passed the responsibility" on to me. I remember it vividly, my first time shooting a horse in the head. I was 7 years old, the horse was on the ground, slowly breathing, in great pain, probably wishing it was dead, I was used to this as I had seen it many times, but then my dad passed me the gun. I didn't know what to do, I had seen these horses euthanized plenty of times, but had never done it myself.

There I was, at the minuscule age of seven, holding a gun being told to shoot a horse in the head. I slowly prepared myself to pull the trigger. After what seemed like an eternity, I pulled the trigger. Horse brain shot everywhere, it was all over me and it was horrible. While this sounds like a horrible moment, this was by far not the worst moment of my childhood.

At the tender age of nine, my parents got divorced. This was a horrible moment and was one of the worst moments of my childhood. After the divorce it was my dad that I got to see on weekends. He lived in a high-rise mansion, quite intriguing for a man with no job as of then I must say now, but there was a balcony right behind my room, and there was a tree that hung over it with its long branches that looked like it wanted to molest you, but I digress, one night, I believe it was five weeks after the divorce, I went to bed on Friday night, thinking about all the cartoons I was going to watch and the cereal I was about to eat. I eventually drifted off to sleep after what seemed like an eternity, which was most likely five minutes. My father had said good night to me before I went to sleep and nothing had seemed out of the ordinary. When I woke up on Saturday, I went to the bathroom and came back to my room to watch Power Rangers on my Television while waiting for my father to wake up, however I wanted a breath of fresh air, so I went to the sliding door and opened the curtains. I looked outside and saw it. There was a rope on the branch, and a dangling body. I was dumbfounded, it was the first time I had ever seen anything like that, not in a cartoon nor a movie, I was unaware of the concept of suicide or hanging. So I went up to my father looked, I thought it was a funny attempt at trying to scare me in the morning or something. I told him he could get down now, but he didn't move. It didn't click until a minute later, he was dead. I didn't know what to do. I got down on the ground and cried. I didn't know my parents phone number as this was the age of land-lines and I didn't know 911 was a thing. Eventually, after what seemed like hours, my mother had called the land-line. I picked up the phone and didn't say anything. It was my mother saying that she regretted the divorce and wanted to get back together because it was better for me. I sat there, crying, thinking, eventually I told her, he's dead. This is one of my most traumatic moments of my childhood.

Now, for one final story, at the age of eight is when I started helping with the care of the horses that hadn't yet fallen and got euthanized. The routine for caring for them was as follows: Gloves on, hand up horse ass, squeeze, feel if anything was wrong in the anus, stick thermometer up the ass and take temperature. But, when I first did it, I pulled out my hand, it was completely covered in fecal matter, all the way from the fingertips up to the elbow. My dad told me this was normal, however, the next few days I started feeling sick, eventually I went to the doctor and they had told me I was suffering from Cholera because of it. For the next while, I was deathly sick and could barely move and never did anything, my sickness lasted until roughly around the time of the divorce and after that, well you know.

Overall, this game really makes me look back at these childhood memories. I realize that, while traumatic, maybe not all the memories are horrible and should be a time that I forget. I love this game because it has really helped me to remember my lost years of my life.\t\t\t\t\t
