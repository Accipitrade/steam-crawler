\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDO NOT BUY THIS GAME.

Do not get me wrong, the game is tons of fun, and all around one of the best shooters I've played. However, after playing it for a bit on my buddy's PS4, I decided to get it for myself once it got on sale during Halloween. Imagine my surprise when I installed the game only to find that it straight up refused to start. Steam would give me the popup message saying that the game was starting, then five seconds later it would shut down. No error message, no window, no nothing. Hell, the only way I could even tell that the game was running and when it had stopped was when I was directly looking at my Steam Library.

I have tried everything I could think of to fix this issue. I have tried running the game exe as an administrator, I have tried to verify my game cache, I have tried to update my drivers, I have tried to uninstal and reinstal the game multiple times, I have tried to make sure that my antivirus wasn't blocking the game, and it goes without saying that I made absolutely certain that my gaming rig met both the minimum and recommended requirements.

Nothing I have done has worked, and the worst part is that this is apparently an issue people have been having since an update that came out in the spring of 2016. The fact that ID and Bethesda haven't fixed this issue after over 18 months is nothing less than unforgivable and I cannot in good conscious recommend anyone buy this off of steam. If you absolutely MUST get it, I highly suggest either getting the console version, since that won't run into any sort of issues when trying to run it, or better yet, pirate it first to make sure you can actually play the game before you spend any money on a game that doesn't work.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're expecting a carbon copy of the original Doom games (I and II), you won't be happy.

This game is fast paced, immensely fun, and differs greatly from the original Doom games. It has a perk/skill system, weapon upgrades, glory kills, and a few other "new style" game additions. Coming from an old school gamer who has been gaming for about thirty years, this may seem like a bad thing.

It's not. This game is a sequel done right. They have made changes to increase the replayability, and ensure the pace of the game is kept up. They nailed it.

DOOM (2016)

Great shooter, probably the best I've played in the last 4 years. But the platforming makes me wanna tear my hair out! The way the game registers whether you've grabbed onto a ledge, or not is â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥, and has probably caused most of my death's so far on any difficulty mode. Advanced platforming in a first person shooter just doesn't work Half-Life already proved this decades ago.

Rant aside though this is a pure-blooded shooter at it's core and a darn good one at that. Wide-variety of weapons to take on a variety of enemies. Combat stress's reflex's, and movement control over sheer aim like CS:GO for example. Level design is constantly changing from wide open arena's to claustrophobic environment's forcing you to adapt or die. The story is understated, subtle environmental narration is what pulls you in to read codex entries that flesh out the lore. It's a wonderful balance that doesn't hinder gameplay, or ever pull control away from the player.

Sound design is top-notch, surround directional audio could've been better, but the raw quality of FX captured is phenomenal. Demons are harrowing, and voice actor's for characters like Samuel Hayden demand authority. Weapons are punchy focusing on the mid-low to low frequencies, basshead's will get a kick out of this.

Visuals strike a perfect balance between looks, and performance with plenty of scalability. The implementation of Vulkan is a godsend in a sea of DirectX title's, offering better overall performance for the majority of graphics card's. Ton's of beautiful post-processing effects with standout's being depth-of-field, motion blur, and high-quality bloom. I tested Doom on a GTX 660ti (2GB Vram), and an R9 390 (8GB Vram). Both scaled superbly with settings being appropriately placed at low, 900p, on the former, and medium-high, 1080p, for the latter. Managing to hit an average of 100fps on both card's.

I invested about 13 hours into the multiplayer until I reached LVL 50, 1st prestige, or equivalent of it in Doom. Multiplayer is definiately an after thought, it's not balanced, some loadouts are more versatile and better than others, it's not a good competitive game mode either, but it is a fun casual experience. A large variety of maps, demon power-ups are great until your on the receiving end, it remind's me of COD killstreaks to a certain extent in which an already good player only get's more assistance to streamroll you even harder. As it stands I would consider it a poor man's Unreal Tournament, doesn't come close to being as fast as Quake for example. Ultimately your mileage may vary when it comes to enjoyment of mp.

All in all Doom (2016) proves that late 90's shooters are the apex of the genre, which since then have been bogged, padded, or distilled with silly mechanics and mediocre level design. It is the essence of what makes a great shooter, and is captured in a bloody bottle.

Final Score: 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
