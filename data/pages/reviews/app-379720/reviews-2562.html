\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe king of FPS returns with lightning quick gunfights and a killer soundtrack.

DOOM has quite possibly the greatest FPS singleplayer campaign in over a decade with tons of secrets and optional objectives. The level design is a highlight as well, ranging from big sprawling arenas to dark maze-like dungeons and everything in between. The game gives moments of quiet time in between fights for you to breathe a bit and solve some switch-based puzzles. The weapon arsenal is huge as should be expected, and each and every weapon can be upgraded, most of them in two distinctively different ways. You can customize your Doom Slayer to fit your playstyle, or if you're a completionist like me, 100% all the levels to reach godlike power.

The multiplayer is very enjoyable too. I found it to be highly reminiscent of old arena-style shooters like Quake III Arena and Unreal Tournament. There are loads of different gamemodes to choose from including capture points and freeze-tag combat, the old classics like Deathmatch and Capture the Flag are of course also here, although CTF has seen some alterations making it more resemble american football. Loadouts are customisable with 2 main weapons and additional equipment like grenades or mines, there are also runes that give you a distinct advantage in an area of your choosing. In this respect it could be likened to a mix between Quake III Arena and modern Call of Duty games, although the main gameplay is definitely leaning more towards the older style. Health kits, armor and super weapons like the BFG and the Gauss Cannon still spawn into the map, so map control is still highly useful. Some modes also allow you to collect a Demon powerup to become one of the game's many powerful demons. Taking down the current demon drops the powerup once again and allows you to transform into a demon of your own for the remainder of the powerup timer. This results in an often frantic game of cat and mouse, but where taking down the goliath is highly rewarding.

Lastly the game also supports custom maps and even campaigns which can all be built inside the game's engine itself. Creating maps is very simple once you get the hang of it, and there are multiple tutorials to guide you on your way. I'm very impressed to see such support for community creations, and thanks to the powerful tools there is some high quality user created content out there.

Id Software definitely delivered this time!\t\t\t\t\t

However, the developers of this game somehow managed to pull off a miracle that did it justice. If you're a fan of first person shooters, this is a must play.
