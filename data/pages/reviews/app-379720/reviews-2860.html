\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM!

Hole-lee *beep*ing DOOM!

*THIS* is the definitive first person shooter. Break-neck speed action. Variety of enemies. Variety of guns. Power-ups. Glory kills. Weapon mods. Secret classic levels. F*** yeah!

DOOM's graphics run better because of Vulkan than other 2016 titles that use DX11&12 (coughHITMAN).

I just finished my first play through of the single player campaign. I threw caution to the wind and played on Ultra Violence, which is the highest difficulty that comes unlocked out of the box. Most of the time, it felt like medium difficulty. There were a number of difficult battles that I beat on the first try, and even the really hard boss battles I beat after a few tries. There was nothing that was rage-quittingly impossible. I'm not exactly saying that the game is easy, but I think it was designed to be challenging but ultimately to keep you moving. There's just enough story to give you a reason for why you're fighting, but not so much that you sit around waiting to get back to the action, and that's how I like my story in video games.

The HUD is minimal, there is no reloading, there is no fall damage, enemies drop loot. Generally, as long as you keep moving and killing, you can steamroll through an area. When your health gets low, there are some health packs laying around, but if you glory kill an enemy, they will drop extra loot and that will provide you a health boost. Same for when your ammo gets low.

This game is a BLAST. Your heart will pound. You will swear. You will have to take breaks. And then you will keep playing. I recommend starting on Ultra Violence if you're experienced in FPS games. Take the time to find secret areas and upgrade your guns.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimple. Bloody. Loud. A fast-paced romp through Hell. Those moronic scientists let the demons loose on Mars again, and the only person who can stop this hellacious invasion is the legendary icon, DOOM Guy.

I really can't emphasize enough how after almost EVERY SINGLE encounter, when the brutal instrumental metal stopped, I realized my heart was racing and I was holding my breath in, letting out a huge "Whew!! Damn!" Honestly though, it takes A LOT for a game to wrap me up so much I literally lose sight of my physical self...DOOM managed to do that.

So take a generic, linear FPS. Guns, health packs, enemies, get from start of level to end etc. Now take everything that makes that shooter generic (linear level design, mediocre but usable weapons, handful of enemy types etc), and bump it up a few notches towards amazeballs status. DOOM doesn't offer much in the way of innovation, but it offers a HELL of a lot in doing everything that's been done right in other shooters and putting it in a nice little package for us to blow up in a multitude of ways.

How it works is you have a huge map. Seriously huge for a linear map based FPS, but each one felt like the perfect size. I never once said "wow, over already?" OR "damnit when does it end?" It always fell into place just as it should. Within that map you'll have an 'arena,' usually multi-tiered with lots of supplies lying around, where once you enter, all the doors close behind you and unsurprisingly "demon presence has reached unsafe levels." Defeat the demon horde, doors open back up, proceed. This is about 97% of the game.

Pretty mind blowing, right? No, not really. But damn did I have fun doing it.

9 weapon types, 10 if you include the BFG. In terms of weapons, they aren't incredibly unique. Plasma pistol, two types of shotguns, two types of machine guns, rocket launcher, plasma gun, plasma sniper rifle, chainsaw, and one Big ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Gun. Pretty standard. Each has one or two types of 'mods' you get down the line, adding little benefits like faster reloads, missile bullets or shotgun grenades yada yada. Again, nothing we haven't seen before.

But it's all streamlined so beautifully each bullet just feels so natural. And instead of slogging through a generic map, repeating to yourself which gun to use for which enemy, DOOM makes each weapon fairly equal and HOW you use them more important than merely using them at the right time. You can blow this monster up with whatever gun you choose (in general), however using that gun how it should be used will make it more effective than just spamming L mouse button. It's also pretty good about keeping you stocked up on ammo at all times, with supplies scattered everywhere throughout a map. Basically it gives you the freedom to blow ♥♥♥♥ up however you like, and I can appreciate that.

At least, for about 80% of the game. As you approach end game, enemy types you've encountered evolve in one way or another (who didn't see that coming?), in which case using the Gauss cannon to weaken an enemy instead of just spamming rockets speeds up the process and conserves ammo. But in terms of 'what works,' well...it all works. There are also 3 types of equipment, two types of grenades and a hologram. DOOM uses the system where equipment uses a cooldown timer instead of having to replenish. Personally I prefer this setup, keeps things interesting. And in a game like DOOM, you really aren't looking for realism.

But let's be real, nothing I described is something you haven't heard of or seen before. But I honestly don't think I've ever had this much fun using such a generic assortment of weapon types on such linear levels. Why is that, I wonder? Oh, I know...

Demons.

The enemies are what truly make the game and keep it from falling into the depths of mediocrity. VERY diverse group of enemy types that forces you to stay on your toes at all times...and I mean that quite literally. You have to move 100% of the time in this game to survive. Stop for a few seconds and expect to lose all of your armor and a lot of health. They all stay true to the DOOM universe of course. Some demons are incredibly powerful, others are cannon fodder. But each one looks bad ass af and are all incredibly satisfying to defeat. Baron's of Hell were my favorite. Just when you think you're God, turning around to one of those things in your face GLADLY reminds you that you aren't, but that in fact you are in Hell.

Bosses are meh and probably the most underwhelming part of the entire game. Basically just oversized baddies with a handful of different attacks and a lot of health. There is a level of strategy, learning the order of their attacks, when/where to strike. Typical boss stuff, so overall pretty meh. I much preferred when 'boss battles' were just incredibly difficult arena fights instead of an actual boss. Granted the bosses looked incredible and truly hellish, which makes it all worth it in the end.

Considering it is, at its core, a linear arena shooter, the level design is superb. You really feel like you're walking through a Martian research lab that has been taken over by true evil. It's the little things...the green fires, the bubbling blood puddles, the glowing pentagrams, and of course the sound design. Just the faintest hint of an enemy sound raises each hair on your neck and puts you in combat mode...even if it wasn't really there. General sound effects are, again, nothing crazy but good enough, but the ambience is just phenomenal. Combined with the distant dust storms of Mars or the burning fires of Hell, you are truly immersed in this world.

Story...I mean, it's a DOOM game, what is there to say? It isn't particularly deep, but it is particularly awesome. Research lab in Mars trying to weaponize the energies of Hell. What could go wrong? Yeah. How do you stop it? Close the portal with guns. K...though I will say voice acting is quite good.

Graphics. Screenshots do it justice, it looks fantastic and is fairly well optimized. Outdoor area strain is a little uneven compared to indoor, so you may have to adjust your settings temporarily when outside. I generally had 45-50fps indoors, but the same settings were 20-30 when outdoors. Though overall I had fairly little performance issues on my medium-high rig. In general I could stop wherever I was and take a minute to admire the beautiful environment around me.

Last but not least, side content. I'm yet to dabble in multiplayer though I look forward to doing so. In general I'm reading it's solid enough at this point in release, was shaky and dead in the past but has been fixed. However users can also upload custom maps and missions for other users to play FOR FREE, which is TOTALLY bad ass and I can't wait to try out some of those. Legit map editors are quite rare for a FPS these days and something I am sincerely impressed with here. Well played, sirs.

So in the end, I truly recommend DOOM. I'm not a sugar coater, the game really doesn't offer much in the way of innovation. Hell it's not even the only game to take generic concepts and put them together to make a great game. However it is one of them, and it does it very well.

Worth $60? Nah. I'd say if you're fairly well off in society and looking for a good game, it would be $40 well spent. If money is tight, I'd say absolutely pick it up on sale for $20 or less. For that price there's simply no way you'll be disappointed. $20 got me one campaign run collecting most secrets and side areas totalling about 22 hours. No complaints here! Also the demo is fairly representative of the game, though a little underwhelming compared to the real thing. I thought the demo was pretty cool, I thought the game was f'in awesome.

DOOM baby.

Played on PC (Using mouse and keyboard)
Reviewed 01/15/2017
Purchased ($20 during a sale)

Spoilers Warning

\tI pushed off writing this review for several days because I am having difficulty putting into words my feelings on the game. Even as I write this review I’m still struggling with how I will word things. To help eas this along, i’ll start out with whats easy. Doom generally runs smoothly and fluidly. Full disclosure, the game did crash on me 6 or 7 times. All of these crashes happened at 2 specific parts of the game (interestingly nothing intense was going on during these sections). Since these crashes all occurred during these two specific times, and the rest of the game ran flawlessly i’m willing to overlook them as just oddities of my specific computer build.

I’m not a person who obsessively watches framerates as this is not the most important factor to me, but at no point did I notice any framerate drops. The game ran really well and the graphics were…..fine. The textures were solid, the enemies were menacing, and the locations were detailed. So, I have no criticism of the “graphics” per se, nevertheless I would not rate the visuals of Doom particularly high.

Good graphics but not good graphics? What does that mean? The graphics are good, but the game suffers from a total lack of variety. The entire game takes place on Mars or in Hell. Every part of Mars looks the same as every other part of Mars. Every part of Hell looks the same as every other part of hell. Worse still, Hell doesn’t look all that different from Mars which means Mars doesn’t look all that different than Hell. Both areas are barren, dusty wastelands. The main difference being that Mars is a reddish wasteland while Hell is more of a yellowish wasteland.

The gameplay suffers from the same exact problem. It's very competently done, it works well, and I don’t have any complaints about its execution. Except, it's the same thing over and over and over and over. You travel through hallways looking for a dead soldier to get a key from. Blue, Yellow, Red. Notice how I said “a dead soldier” not “dead soldiers,” this was intentional as the soldier looks the same every single time. He is always propped up against a wall or something, wearing red and you rip the key off the center of his chest. Over and over and over. These keys unlock rooms. Once in the rooms, the doors lock behind you and you have to fight off 3 or 4 waves of monsters. Defeating the last one unlocks the door and you progress into the next hallway where you will look for the next key. You do this over and over and over.

My dilemma, and why I have pushed off writing this review, is that I feel the game is repetitive. Yet calling the game repetitive doesn’t seem fair because all video games are repetitive. Why is it a problem in this one? Is there a better word I could use instead to describe it? If there is a better word I have not come up with it. All I can say is some games feel repetitive while others don’t, and to me Doom feels really repetitive.

It's possible that it is a combination of everything above. Similar scenery, combined with similar key gathering combined with similar death rooms over and over again.

I’m left in this odd place where I don’t have all that much praise for Doom even though it's well put together. What’s there works, and it works really well, I just kept waiting for the next thing but the next thing never came. Doom isn’t broken, it's enjoyable but it left me wishing there was more to it.

Score: Good

How my scoring works: Most reviewers assign a numerical score to things. This doesn’t really make sense to me. If one game has an 8.5 and another has an 8 is one really better than the other? Or is it just the mood of the reviewer at that time? Are these meaningful differences? Is a 8 really that much worse than an 9? To me there is virtually no difference between these scores. There are just too many dividing lines. Now I get that In any review system you need tiers but to me you want as few tiers as possible. I seriously considered just having “reccomend/Skip” as my system, but decided that that was to vague to be of much value to anyone. That would combine outstanding games and just ok games under “reccomend” So instead My scores are:

Amazing
Good
Disappointing
Terrible

Yes, if something is given a “good” score you could say I gave it a 3 out of 4 but that really what i’m going for. Why convert a descriptive thing like good into something abstract like a number?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM pretty much brings the first person shooter formula to a fine well toned sharp point. On the gameplay side this game is as smooth as can be, though pacing can be a bit off if your going around executing every single demon in your path, but the mechanics serve their purposes and serve them well. More so DOOM removes two aspects which plague the typical modern FPS games. Regenerating health and linear level designs. The way levels flow, it feels organic with plenty of secrets hidden around. Plus you get a number of upgrades to both your weapons and abilities, though they're handed out hand over fist, but never really seem to overpower the DOOM guy.

On the technical side, this game runs incredibly well. For my rig which is pushing a few years old ran this game at it's highest settings with hardly any hiccups throughout the entire game. So for anyone who meets the recommend requirements half way, you should be able to not only play DOOM, but enjoy it in all it's brutal beauty and detail.

Pros:
-Proper health & ammo pickups
-Fast paced
-Looks incredible
-Performs incredly well
-Level maps

Cons:
-Executions feel like they break flow at times
-Multi-player doesn't feel as active as it could be

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI picked this game up in the Steam Winter Sale and oh boy, am I glad I did. So let's get started with the review.

Graphics and Optimization

First of all, it looks great. Absolutely amazing. It gives you a sense of the crisis that happened on Mars, with all the high-tech rooms covered in blood and bodies and demons everywhere. The blood looks great, the demons, from the zombie like "possessed", to the highly mobile imps, all of them act in different way. The texture work is amazing and all the post-processing effects look amazing. The game also supports the new "Vulkan" API, which makes the game run great on newer systems.

For the optimization, it's pretty good. Nothing amazing, but for the graphics and all, it's pretty well-optimized.

One criticism about the graphics is the menu. There aren't clear options and the tooltips don't explain what they all do very well.

So all in all, the graphics are great.

Gameplay

This Doom game is a throwback the the original Doom games. There is no story that is told by exposition. It's just the superhuman-like Doomguy, demons, and your trusty weapons. You just rip-and-tear through all the demons in a satisfying and fun way. There is, however, a codex for anyone who wants a plot.

The game really emphisises mobility, which I truely respect, as it is a break from the "shoot, get in cover, regenerate health, shoot again" kind of shooters. If you stay still, you're dead. You constantly have to keep moving to avoid the attacks of the demons. This was also the reason that the "glory kills" which are canned animations for a final melee blow to a demon that drops health and ammo, were worrying to most people. Luckily, they aren't long and don't get in the way of the fast-paced combat. This is the best single-player campaign that I have played in years.

Music

The music is amazing and it truely fits the style of the game. Shooting demons and moving from platform to platform trying to avoid attacks while you close in on your next target with metal music playing in the background gives a unique sense of badassness. The music is amazing.

Multiplayer

This is a point where the game fails. The multiplayer is bland at best, and severly unbalanced and frustrating at worst. It's an arena style shooter where there are ridiculously overpowered power ups that transform you into a demon. The multiplayer is lacking, and it feels like it doesn't need to be there.

Snapmap

Snapmap is Doom's map creator. Even though it is simple and decently powerful, the fact that it was made for consoles is really felt. In snapmap, you just "snap" rooms together and, ta da, you've got a map. An actual map tool for PC would have been great.

Conclusion

Doom is amazing. It has the best campaign that I've played in years and amazing gunplay. The full price is worth it, even for only the campaign. I don't like review scores but if I had to give it a score, it would be 9/10.

= The quantity is fantastic. The game length is good, many guns and enemy types to play with, many great songs, a good amount of hidden bonuses. And after you clear the main game, you also got multiplayer mode and map-making mode to play with. The multiplayer mode even has its own progression system for you to keep playing for more weapons and perks to play too.

= Very satisfied and fun to play. You will keep running and switching through many weapons to fight many types of enemies. And you can equip different runes or buff to suit your playstyle too.\t\t\t\t

= The overall art direction and how they build the world is very good. Still, the hell map needs more decorations to make it feel more "lively" and more interesting. = With all these guns, demons, songs, and multiplayer mode, this game is very fun. Pretty sad that the multiplayer is now very dead, if the player base is still lively like the first 2 years of this game, you can have more blast with this game for sure. (Don't worry tho, I won't reduce Fun score even if the multiplayer is dead)\t\t\t\t
