\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been waiting for this game for a long ol' time, and being a fairly big fan of the series and id software (I've the tattoos to prove it!) I was tentative about this release. This nature has been in play since the dissapointment felt throughout DOOM3 and it's expansion (something about it just didn't sit right - it just wasn't MY DOOM), and the early reviews of this title were far from positive. Something also to note is that I rarely play multiplayer FPS games, so my opinion is based solely on the campaign which is potentially "missing the point" I'm sure.

I rarely get the time to really pour hours into a title, but after a fairly hairy month at work I had bough an evening to experience one of my old loves - killing demons. All that said, I sat down last night and fired up the game and even from the title screen I had goosebumps. The game iteslf is certainly not perfect, and in the initial levels I had felt like switching it off and coming back to it as the story and gameplay hadn't really gripped me, however in around the third or fourth level the pennt dropped. This game is wonderful, gory, fun, and has a brilliant learning curve and drip-feeds you the mechanics throughout the campaign really well.

The games ability to embed; story, humour, cool mechanics, rapid gameplay and homage to the older titles is great and I can honestly say I enjoyed my experience thoroughly. If you are a fan of the older titles (DOOM and DOOM II in particular) I feel like you might just need to give this a go (and give it a couple of hours, don't quit when you feel it's just not right, give it a little longer, it might surpirise you).

The game made me laugh, scream in fear, think and nigh on break my keyboard it had everything I needed in a fast-paced, demon killfest - oh, and the classic maps are a touch of genius.

Thanks id, you've made and old fan incredibly happy.\t\t\t\t\t
