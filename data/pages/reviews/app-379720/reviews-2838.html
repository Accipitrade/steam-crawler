\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never really liked Arena-Shooters. Clear a room, go to the next room, repeat...I never really got the appeal. That may be the reason for me liking DOOM 3 quite a bit (even though I am very much aware of it's flaws).

Now here comes along DOOM 2016 with all its gory glory. I heard so many good things about it, that I just had to give it a try. And it turned out to be one of the best purchase-decisions I made in 2016.

Despite my dislike of these types of Shooters DOOM somehow manged to keep the shooting fun and interesting from the first minute until the last second of the game. A huge amount of different, satisfying weapons (counting the mods), fun and gory face-to-face glory-kills, excellent level-design and a movement-system which feels almost as fluent as a perfect run in Mirrors Edge just make this game a definite must-have for every FPS-Fan.
On top of all that the story - thankfully - doesn't take itself very serious and manages to rip on other games' ham-fisted seriousness without beeing in your face about it too much.


The one thing that made me put the game aside for a bit were all the collectibles and secrets you could find in the maps.
While it's fine for them to exist, it always gives me anxiety to know that they are here and getting it shoved in my face at the end of each level didn't help. It made me endlessly search all the nooks and crannies of each maps until exhaustion which deeply impacted my apreciation for the level-design and even the fights itself. "Gotta go kill all the demons so I can finally hunt for secrets again!". It took a while for me to just "ignore" them and have fun playing the game again.
But that said - thats just a problem I personally have with modern game design in that regard. And in the case of DOOM you can very easily replay any level with all your unlocked gear at any time if you choose to hunt for every secret in the game.



Oh and about the season pass - just skip it! The multiplayer - in my opinion - isn't worth mentioning and all the DLCs for this game are multiplayer-content only. Unfortunatetly even "Snap Map" didn't turn out as great as I hoped.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwesome way to bring back an old favorite. Outstanding graphics, great sound effects, music fits the gameplay very well. Upgrades to the suit and weapons are fun, welcome additions. New enemy types and new takes on the old ones make combat a ton of fun, although certain enemies are more annoying than they are dangerous (read: Imps). Perhaps my single biggest complaint is the lack of any real co-op multiplayer in the game. Yes, there are co-op "snapmap" modes - but good luck finding one. One of the things that made the old DOOM games so much fun was running around and killing demons with a friend, and that capability is sorely missed here.

The good news is, though, that this game doesn't take itself too seriously at all. They tried the "make everything creepy and scary" route back in DOOM 3. This time, it's just an all out, over-the-top action game that almost makes fun of the franchise itself.

Overall, solid 8.5/10. Definitely worth a buy on sale for $20-30, but not quite worth the full $60 due to lack of co-op in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM. A REBOOT OF A FRANCHISE THAT PAYS HOMAGE TO THE CLASSIC VERSION WITH MORE.

If you were a PC gamer in the 90's then you'll remember the classic FPS Doom. Doom was a game changer at it's time with it's fast paced gameplay, numerous weapons at your disposale, and hordes of different hellspawn to kill. Doom (2016) offers that experience yet again but with more added.

Before I get into that let's talk about Doom 3. Doom 3 was a fun game but it was missing so much of what made Doom and Doom 2 special and loved by many. With Doom 3 id decided to remove the fast paced gameplay in exchange for jump scares, dimly lit corridors, and slow moving enemies with only a few exceptions. Doom 3 was entertaining but it just wasn't the Doom we deserved.

Doom (2016) is what Doom 3 should have been. A fast paced killathon that was just as chaotic, if not more, than the original installments of the franchise. What id/Bethesda has done here is captured the spirit of the original game and quite honestly perfected it. With Demons running in all directions, climbing walls, leaping over obsticles to get to you it's just what the FPS genre needed, a serious kick in the balls. There is no cover system, no health regen, and no sections bogged down with cinemtatic scenes trying to tell a story. It's just run and gun, don't stop, and kill everything that moves before it kills you.

Doom does have a story though and it's pretty straightforward. You are the Doomslayer, a sort of knight that the minions of hell fear, but instead of running around with a sword and shield you run around with a chainsaw, a multitude of guns, a BFG, and a sense of being a total badass.

The gameplay does differ from the classics though. You are rewarded for finding secret areas with a points system to upgrade your weapons/armor/heatlh and other properties. Once you max those weapon upgrades your are given challenges to to add more effects to your weapons. For example I was using the standard shotgun. I selected the grenade launcher alt-fire. Once I had maxed it with points I had to complete a certain challenge with that weapon in order to get an added property where if I fire off a direct hit at an enemy it will spawn a few extra grenades to do extra damage.

The Glory kills are what makes the game very interesting and at times makes you feel like an unstoppable badass. No matter what direction whether you're dropping from above, or facing your opponent you'll pull off a brutal kill that can either be pulling off an enemies arm and beating them with it or smashing their face into the pavement until there is nothing left. Every enemy has a different way of pulling off a glory kill. Like the classic doom that are boosts that you can find that help you to take out hells baddies, the classic Berserk being one of them whitch I may add performs some awesome looking kills.

Enemies are varied and none of them feel the same as each other. They all have there own attack/movement style in the game. From the Imps that leap from side to side throwing fireballs and climbing obsticles to the Hell knights who barrell at you with shear brute strength.

The final thing I'll talk about is the sound design. The sound of Doom is spectacular from the sounds of weapon fire, demons roaring at you as they attack, or Mick Gordon's phenominal death metal/electronica sounding music that I have never heard in a game before and it works with the gameplay. I found myself itching for the next battle just so I could listen to more of that awesome soundtrack.

There are a ton of secret areas to explore in Doom. There are even area's where you can unlock classic levels from Doom and Doom 2 to play though by selecting them in the games menu.

Final thoughts:

Doom (2016) is a meatgrinder and one hell of an FPS that breathes much needed life into the FPS genre. It feels like old Doom but with a touch of new. I cannot recommend this game anymore. This is absolutely GOTY material.

Doom (2016) gets a solid 10/10\t\t\t\t\t
