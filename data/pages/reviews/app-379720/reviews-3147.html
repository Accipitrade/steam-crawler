‘We Will Send Unto Them...Only You’

Doom goes back to its roots with this instalment, and it does so in spectacular style, so if you love FPS games, not just a specific franchise but first person shooters in general then this is a must get.

Campaign:
The single player campaign is brutal.
Set in stunning locations within various styles of buildings on Mars and in the depths of Hell, arming you with a vast array of unique weapons & following a story arc far beyond any previous instalment. With more demons you can stick a chainsaw in; you will be shooting, blasting, popping, shredding, vaporising, ripping & tearing the souls of your enemies from their flesh in droves.

Whereas with most shooters you feel like you’re stuck in a room with the enemy, Doom is more like the demons are stuck in a room with YOU.

Multiplayer:
Contrary to initial reviews [and those from players who just don’t get it] the multiplayer is neither lacking nor hard.
Granted just like in the single player you have to pick up health, armour and ammo boxes to stay an effective member of the fight as sitting in a corner won’t regenerate your health.

With all the weapons unlocked early on and movement speed locked to all players you are on a par with everyone else out there. Your only advantage to any fight will be your choice of weapons and your skill.

Having to balance pushing for the objective with picking up ammo, health & armour makes for a fast paced shooter where pushing forward is rewarded over hiding in a corner.
With every hit & decision you make having an impact on the game nothing feels futile and a good game is not always the same as a win.

Snap Map:
This is a cross platform workshop/creation kit. You can with relative ease create your own single player or arcade maps and upload them so players from PC & console can play.

Overall this is a very strong game, a perfect example of how ID created the FPS gaming genre with Wolfenstine and the first Doom. This should be what other developers set their standards too.

Here is to a new generation of “Doom Clones”. 666/666 \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start off by saying I am not usually a fan of the shooter genre, but I have enjoyed the Doom series throughout the years. So here is my two cents,

The Pros


The game is REALLY fast paced. At the start they set the tone to be kind of scary, the monsters are intimidating, but as the game goes on that quickly fades and you're ripping the heads off of the most powerful demons out there.

The scenery and graphics are amazing, you can see that in the trailers.

The is great creature, weapon, and enviornment diversity.

Plenty of secrets to collect after you've cleared the room.

A must have if you enjoy shooters.


The Cons

The absolutely worst thing that happens to me when I am playing this is I will finally be killing the last few demons in a room that has taken me forever to clear (the game will save a checkpoint after the room is clear) and then BOOM the game will crash wasting a LOT of time. When you reload, you will have to start from your last checkpoint and reclear the entire zone. Extremely frusterating.

As the game got near the end I started to just slog through it just to finish it. I am not a fan of shooters so this is purely subjective and others may like this, but the last half of the game quickly became,

Go into room

Intense music plays and lots of demons continually spawn

Spend 15-20 minutes killing mosters in room

collect ammo and health then head into next room and repeat.

And last but not least it only took me ten hours to beat and I collected most of the secrets playing on normal difficulty. While this may not be a dealbreaker for some I am just putting it out there.
