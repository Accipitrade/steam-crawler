\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts a good Fps, and i appreciate how the game was still very playable ignoring the finisher moves.
Also Kudos for including a centered 'old' style weapon position and small dot crosshair.
I think aside from the nostalgic factor, it's also very practical in a game where aiming down at sights and scopes is ditched for 'shooting what is in front of you'.

Still, I wish enemies were less of bulletsponges at times.
I also think the game could've done without the shielded units, like the pinkies with frontal protection.
For Me, original Doom is about timing shots, level maneuvering, and firing the right guns at the right situations.

Sometimes this did not translate well into this new game.
Instead this was more of the Quake style shooting, where planning the running routes, and aiming in/ ahead of motion was prioritized.
Enemy placement and level design lacked because of this, it made it all less significant.
The play got a bit dull after each handful of arenas, and it does not really surprise anywhere.
The first Dooms could do a lot of interesting things, even with basic enemies, on the later levels as well.

Doom(2016) is comfort shooting.\t\t\t\t\t

Note: The following review is only for the single player campaign.

A game will only be genuinely enjoyed by its players, if there are multiple aspects/elements of it that work well together. One of those aspects is the soundtrack. Thanks to Mick Gordon for creating this aural masterpiece that works really well with the game. The fact that it is not completely metal, but incorporates distortions & synth sounds to give it a distinct sound, just feels right. The adaptive music changes seamlessly when the gameplay changes from "just roaming" to a combat situation. The ambient tracks and the hard-hitting ones do a great job in conveying the "feel" and pace of the game.

Now, to the combat in Doom. As someone put it, "Combat in Doom is a dance". It keeps you moving from one enemy to the next and the centerpiece of it all - the â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥' Glory Kill. Ripping & tearing demons apart takes a whole literal meaning now. I can't remember the last time I had so much fun with a game's combat system. The Batman: Arkham series is one which I remember having fun beating the â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥ out of enemies with bare fists.
And, of course, weapons make it more fun. Notable weapons include the super shotgun, chain gun, plasma rifle, gauss cannon, rocket launcher, chainsaw and our devastating old friend, the BFG. When fired, most weapons feel visually and aurally gratifying (more so, with the weapon mods and upgrades).

As with the music, player movement too feels so seamless and has a smooth pace to it (as the Doom Slayer, you are constantly moving at a frantic pace, in search of the next demon to put your fists through.. lol).
The game feels well optimized and runs smoothly on an entry-level gaming rig.

Other aspects worth mentioning are the level/environment design, demon design, the leveling up system, the story/lore, Snapmap (map editor), Ultra Nightmare difficulty, arcade mode.

Congrats to the team at id software for a job very well done. Take a bow.
