\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was worried about how this game would turn out. id Software delivered a great sequel to the DOOM series. Badass powerful guns, fantastic level design, a great PC port, very good AI, and fun and fluid combat.

The campaign was the main reason I got the game, since I'm a long time DOOM fan. It went beyond my expectations of a new DOOM game, even though I've finished it, I've already found lots of reasons to go back and play it again. There are lots of stuff to look for in levels, mainly secrets, quite a bit that are well hidden. The glory kill system blended in well with the combat, saving me some ammo also plus a nice reward. I also thought the Chainsaw and BFG system were an amazing implementation. Weapon upgrades make the guns even more fun to use.

The multiplayer wasn't necessarily something that I'd see myself playing, even after a few matches, but that's my own opinion, if you like the more modern type of multiplayer shooters, this might be for you.

SnapMap was a fun tool to use, for a bit. I tried my hand at making a map and found it easy to use, but I found it quite limited. Especially with the maps not looking like they've been invaded by Hell, I'm also not too big of a fan of only using the AI conductor for enemy spawns. Another huge problem is the fact that you can only hold two weapons, the chainsaw, the pistol, and berserk are missing. I was hoping for another single player type of experience. This still adds quite a bit of content that should be checked out, but needs some tweaks, or just mod tools (which I'd prefer).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the most nonliniar, action packed gorefests of an FPS game ive played in a while. Every level feels like a section of Metroid Prime was cut out, complete with a 3D map to tell you where things are in the massive maps you have to explore. I HIGHLY recomend playing on the hardest starting difficulty to begin with as every fight is more energetic and epic when your inches from death and glory kill that one big guy to regain all your health. Doom 3 was scary to begin with due to its jump scares, but when this game start hurling bigger enemies at you, that fear is forever constant! Also, while people have beat the campaign in about 9 hours, there are so many secrets in the levels that to 100% the game feels like it will take multiple days (if you intend to sleep and eat in that time...)

Multiplayer is just like the Beta but with more customisation, maps and weapons to play with. Due to its controvosey, you can make up your own mind about that. I personaly enjoyed it, but what do i know, I also enjoy Black Ops 3...

Snap Map seems to be based on a set of basic rooms which I can see getting repetative form excessive plays, but hopefully the ability to edit logic and events will make it more interesting

Overall I think that this is a game for single player FPS fanatics, loving everything so far!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM claims to be reboot of the classic, but they removed one of the key features, co-op. I remembered running through it with my buddy having a hell of a time.

The Steam features list claims co-op but ID/Bethesda confirmed it would not be present. Recommend Steam REMOVE the co-op, or at the very least identify that it is in Multiplayer maps only and NOT the campaign. In my book it is not co-op unless the main aspect of the game, the campaign is co-op.

The initial Region Locking issues can be worked through with a little adjusting as well.

The game itself is smooth and beautiful but reminds me a lot of Painkiller and some of the games that like to spawn/teleport creatures into the play area once you've killed a bunch. You can't assess the situation in a room; you look in and see 4 Possessed so you start shooting. As they die more just spawn on the fly so you may end up killing 20 before the game lets you through. Die through any of it and you get to start over again at the last checkpoint which could be a ways back; so very Console-like.

Key binding is a little strange for you ESDF, South Paws, and whatever else. You will need to play with the bindings a bit since it will allow multiple keys to an action but you can't remove one unless you rebind it to something else which you may not want either.

You will also want to look online for the command lines that let you turn on mouse smoothing, skipping load screens etc.

Still it's pretty good, just not the classic reboot; if you want that you still need to go back to the original.

In many ways I think the release of this game mirrors the release of the original Doom in 1993. A game where you move fast, mow down demons, and don't look back. Something which at the time was brand new and nowadays is a breath of fresh air from the sea of generic military and slow paced shooters that have overtaken the genre. In addition the performance of the game is equally as impressive as it was back then, achieving incredible fidelity and smootheness on low powered hardware. This game is the perfect reboot, capturing the soul of the original games while improving on it for a modern audience.

I really have to rant about the performance here, it's so good it doesn't fully make sense to me. I'm running the game on a 980ti at 2560x1440 resolution, everything cranked up as high as it can go. My average framerate is in the 80-90 range, regularly jumping up into the 110-120 range during less intensive areas. The absolute lowest framerate I ever got was 58, and only in 2 isolated areas throughout the entire game. Id clearly knows something that other developers don't, considering that most other games would struggle to reach 60 with similar settings, let alone 90 or 120.

"But you have a high-end rig!" I hear you say. Consider this: The game runs at full 1080p and 60fps ON BOTH CONSOLES. If an Xbox One can run a game that looks this good at 1080p/60, it's damn well optimized.

The game is paced such that every combat section is followed by an exploration section, which is then followed by another combat section, and so on. You run around, murder some demons, then go on your way to look for secrets and to accomplish objectives.

Honestly I don't have too much to say about the game other than it's really really good. Everything they attempted was well executed and the game keeps your blood pumping the whole way through. I was playing with a friend and sometimes after an intense combat section he would just turn around and laugh, a laugh of relief but also a laugh of pure enjoyment and adrenaline. This game makes you feel like an invincible badass, but without being a cakewalk. You have to earn your victories with skill and planning. Admittedly, the normal difficulty mode is pretty easy, but more on that later.

Instead of just gushing about the game, I'm going to talk about stuff that I didn't like. That way I think it's easir to make a decision. If you don't think you can deal with the stuff I mention, maybe skip this one. If you think it's not a big deal, go for it.

First of all I think the upgrade system could have used a slight tweak that would have made the game less of a headache overall. In the game each weapon has 2 mods that are unlockable through finding upgrades, and you can further upgrade each mod with weapon mod upgrade points that are earned for progressing through each level. Unlike the other upgrades in the game, when you find a new weapon mod upgrade you have to either spend it on the spot or leave it for later. You don't get to keep the upgrade point to use whenever you want, and you don't get to try before you buy. You basically have to hope that the desccription of the mod matches your interpretation of it, and that it complements your play style. The consequence of not being able to use the mod upgrade whenever you want is that in the early game before you find all the guns, you might find an upgrade before you find the new gun in the level. Obviously you don't know yet that there is a new gun in the level so you can end up spending your upgrade on an old gun instead of getting to try out a mod on your new gun. Even worse, you can end up spending it on a mod that you are totally disinterested in just for the sake of completion. This is frustrating, and after I realized this was happening I found myself leaving the upgrade points for when I had a gun I knew I wanted to use it on. This involved having to backtrack to a previous level and navigate through it just to use a weapon upgrade that I already found. All of this trouble would have been saved if the game just let you store your weapon mod upgrades to use them whenever you want. This would also let you try them out because you could just reload your save if you don't like the one you picked.

The second thing that started to annoy me a bit was the repetition, but every pure shooting game suffers from this to some extent so it's not a big deal. There are ways to remedy this as well by using different weapon mods and different rune loadouts to try new playstyles.

Lastly I kind of have a problem with how the game really doesn't want you to die. I get that you're supposed to be a nigh-invincible one man army, but the game makes it pretty obvious that it's doing it's best to stack the odds in your favor, at leat on normal mode. In the game you can glory kill enemies, which is done by using a melee attack on them when there are critically low on health. This kills the enemy and causes them to drop pickups, usually health but they can also drop ammo and armor. The thing about this is that I quickly noticed that the game will give you substantially more health from glory kills if you are low on HP. Generally a glory kill will net you a modest 10 health. But if you are about to die all the enemies become health pinatas, dropping many times that. I think the most that I got from a non-boss enemy was 65 health from one glory kill. The same principal applies to ammo drops, but less so. In this way it almost feels like the game is disrespecting you by giving you a get out of jail free card. Aagin, this is only in normal mode. Those wanting a bigger challenge have 3 higher difficulty options to explore.

That's pretty much it, everything else is good enough to not warrant any complaints. The game really feels like you are descending to the bottom of hell. Bigger hoards of more powerful enemies are thrown at you towards the end, as the levels get shorter and the time in between combat sections wanes. For me the game took roughly 25 hours to complete on normal mode, 100% (including alt-tabs and fooling around). If you don't care about completing everything, expect to spend about half that time playing the game.\t\t\t\t\t
