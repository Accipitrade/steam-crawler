\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I'm not a FPS guy. My idea of a good time is creating a custom character and roving around Dragon's Dogma or making little plans to take out baddies in Metal Gear/Assassin's Creed. But man did this game pull me from all of that. This is one of the most empowering games I've ever played. I usually have no need for ultra violence, as I'm a fighter irl. But this game awoke something in me. The beautiful, simple level/art direction, astounding sound design that never let you rest, secrets that felt cheeky/well-earned and a gameplay loop that never, ever, disappointed. The music is insane and I felt like I gained 10 lbs of muscle every level.

It's quite a simplistic game. They don't try and make you sit through a story. They know what you're here for. It's not terribly long either, but I felt it was a matter of ending things on a good note. There were 3 bugs throughout the entirety of my playthrough. One hard crash, and two times being stuck inside an object and needing to reload my checkpoint. That's all. Besides that, this was an incredible reintroduction to FPS. It's totally worth $20, even for a non-FPS person! If it's on sale, grab it. You won't regret it. I'm currently loading up Doom Eternal after beating this one. Hope it's just as good.\t\t\t\t\t
