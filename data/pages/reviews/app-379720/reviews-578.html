\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am not even that big of a FPS game fan and this is already one of my favourite games.
Any other game I can think of, it always takes time to get into, there always some story/cutscene/tutorial you have to get through but not DOOM, it's straight up action start to end. A must play!

Overall- 9/10
Story-7/10
Gameplay-10/10
Graphics/optimization-9/10
Achievements-6/10


Gameplay-
Wow, well if you already don't know it's just the best there is. I can play this game in loop, anytime and not get bored.
++Glory kills are SO satisfying.
++Rune trials are a total adrenaline rush although second last one was sh*t
++Game rewards you for always moving, loved that I don't have to take cover or calculate anything.
+Weapon customization is pretty good so that everyone will have something as per their taste.
+To me exploring map, finding all the secrets was fun as well, it is rewarded and is a good incentive to replay levels.
-Only minor complaint is game got easy by the end (on normal difficulty), BFG felt too OP. I guess I need to try it on Hard and it will be more fun.

Graphics-
Recommend tuning the sharpening up, made game look much better.
+Graphics are pretty good and the framerate is AWESOME.
+Monster designs, level designs, colour themes- red of mars and hell, real nice.
-I wish there was a photo mode but I understand it not going along with the fast paced gameplay.
--No HDR

Story-
When I started the game I had watched a video which had summed up all stories of the previous DOOM games, so I had idea of what to expect. I personally just loved idea of Doomguy/Doomslayer. The story cutscenes are minimal which makes replaying whole lot easier. If you want additional info just read up on information points/logs/lores which I found really interesting.

Achievements-
I wish it was single player only or in case of multiplayer had only handful of them.
Also, Runes achievements is bugged and can require you to have a careful playthrough which goes against the stress-free philosophy of the DOOM I like.

"They are rage, brutal, without mercy. But you. You will be worse. Rip and tear, until it is done."

Update 10 or so hours later-
Arcade Mode- A must try, I have only really done UAC so far but mastering it for a perfect run from lower difficulties to all the way ultra nightmare has fun quite a fun.
Multiplayer- May be if you have friends to play with, it is not bad, I had good moments from time to time as well but grind for achievements is real. I wish those achievements were for single player/ Arcade mode instead.
SnapMap- Yet to try\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI haven't tried multiplayer at all. I mostly play games for the single-player challenge these days.

I think there are some valid criticisms when people bring up the map. It's not very easy to use because there's not a way to rotate or otherwise control it. There's also a lack of a minimap, however there is an objective compass that keeps you on track. The levels are also fairly linear, so it's really not too bad.

A few reviews complained about repetitive gameplay. I don't think I agree. I felt there's enough weapon variety, enemy variety, and challenges that I have felt genuinely engaged. A few people complained about the "QTE" of a glory kill. I REALLY disagree with that. These systems were placed so that you could use them if you choose to, and replenish in-game resources. Alternatively, they can be completely ignored.

I'm not sure what to think about weapon balance. I haven't notice anything that really isn't fun to use, and most of the weapons seem to have a situation where they work well.

Further, exploration shines in between the action sequences. I've enjoyed the collectibles and being rewarded with upgrade points or codex entries for methodically searching the maps. It's been an actual treat.

Granted, the upgrade system for armor or for weapons isn't particularly deep. There's no real choices to be made, and I don't think any of the unlocked perks fundamentally change gameplay. They're there. They're supplemental. They give a reason for completing challenges and doing the exploration.

All in all, I'm pretty happy with the experience and I could feel comfortable recommending this game to a friend for the campaign.
