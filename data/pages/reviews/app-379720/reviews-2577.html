\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat can I say that hasn't already been said? This game is a love song to the original Doom. It retains the spirit, while upgrading the technology. Sure, there is some story and a bit of a narrative... but it's mostly inconsequential and shoved aside (sometimes quite beautifully even in-universe), and is not annoying nor does it get in the way. Doom is about killing demons in absolutely hectic battles, and none of that soap story stuff. And this game does it beautifully.

id Software has lost their edge over the last decade or so, releasing game after game (and not even so many of them) that tried to improve technically, but forgetting what makes games actually good, and thus the results have been relatively "meh". Not bad, but not that good either. Just meh. However, Doom is a great attempt at getting out of that creative slump, and returning to the basics, to what id Software was formerly good at.\t\t\t\t\t
