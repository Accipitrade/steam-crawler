\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had extremely high expectations for a game essentially hailed as the resurrection of a dead genre. Indeed, given that I had just come off of playing Half-Life 2 for the first time, I was looking for something satisfying.

DOOM ... disappointed me. It was cool, it was fun, but it wasn't compelling in a certain way. I played it on and off, a little bit here and there, for two weeks. I'd play maybe 20-30 minutes at a time. I think, in particular, the problem was the Shotgun. I expected something with some meat. The Assault Rifle felt good enough for me to keep playing. I think it was partly my own fault -- inspired by ByteMe's GDQ run, I felt compelled to get as many collectables as I could find, which tramautized the pacing.

Yet, that also reaped a great reward.

I unlocked the Supershotgun. Then, I unlocked the Supershotgun mastery.

And like a spring-time blossom, DOOM bloomed into a beautiful, gore-filled powder-keg. With the right runes and the right masteries, the ripping and tearing accelerated into the stratosphere. This was first person shooting done right.

This, like Halo CE shoty and HL-2's SPAS, this was a _shotgun_.

10/10
