\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom 2016 is singlehandedly the very best game to come out in recent years. It takes the modern FPS shooter and flips it on its head, with stunning gameplay and gourgeus visuals.

Pros:
Gameplay. Flowing gameplay that never gets old, with creative twists.

Barely any story. While this is a bad thing to some, this game doesn't waste it's time trying to hash in some ♥♥♥♥♥♥ story.

Graphics. Probably one of the most eye popping video games of all time. Whether your'e in hell or in the labs, you won't be disapointed

Weapons. Every weapon is different from the last, each bringing new ways to play the game.

Enemies. Every enemy in this game is satisfying to kill, while still challenging enough to keep you wanting more.

Details/Secrets. There are insane amounts of secret things to find, all hidden away cleverly.

Cons:
Short. This game took me around 20 hours to beat, and left me wanting more, while multiplayer isn't enough to satisfy me. There is no where near enough time to level up everything.

Boss fights. The boss fights were fun, but there were only two, both right next to the end of the game.

Overall, Doom 2016 gets a 9/10,.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGuess, we all remember our first video games ever played, right? All these memories stay forever like tatoos. And when it comes to a simple question regarding first FPS you ever played, most of the players will say Wolfenstein or DOOM, obviously.

And mine first experience was DOOM, as well. I never had a PC back at my place, so I spent a lot of time at my friend's houses and school computer classes just to play a bit more of it. It was priceless, I think if you were born somewhere around late 80s - early 90s you should remember that feeling when you were looking at it, thinking "Jeesus Christ, nothing will be ever better than this!" Yeah, we couldn't even imagine Uncharted graphics 20 years later, but this is completely different story, let's get back to my review.

So, DOOM (2016) was a game that every single experienced player and FPS lover was hyped about. People had a really big expectations regarding it and I wasn't the exception. Plus, I really felt like playing something non-stop action related and this game was on my first place on the list. Sadly, I wasn't able to check this game on the release date. Staying abroad, I was checking steam reviews and metascore every few minutes and, of course, all I saw was optimization moanings (no surprise here) instead of detailed reviews regarding the game experience itself.

At this moment, I was eager to come to my place ASAP and bust this another myth about bad optimization. What I really did three days later, game ran smoothly on 60 fps without any problems.

From the first minutes of the game, they show you that you are going to deal with an insane action game, with a wide variety of deadly upgradeable weapons. Tons of monsters chase you all the time and your ultimate mission is to survive in this hell. The story is pretty much simple and almost the same as it was in previous DOOM games. UAC ( Union Aerospace Corporation ), based on Mars resulting unexpected outcome of their experiments opened a portal to Hell accidentally. One of the squads, that was sent to investigate it, found a sarcophagus with a warrior in there and they decided to send it on Mars. And at the same time main antagonist Olivia Pierce decides to sabotage the whole complex, making a deal with a Hell demons, which leaded to some serious consequences - the whole crew died and turned into hell creatures. And at the same moment, one of the main guys of that complex, who was managed to survive during the explosion, opened a sarcophagus, releasing a Doom Guy and this is where the game actually starts.

As a Doom Guy, your ultimate task will be to explore complex facility on Mars, travel to Hell and back to take down Olivia. To be honest, in a games like this, plot is the least I would care about, because it's a classic action scenario with a charismatic bad bad insane guy, who makes a lot of trouble all the time and you are the only one who can stop him, carrying dozens of different weapons with a different weight, picking health boosters just in the right time and rushing through tons of monster creatures. DOOM is definitely a game about having fun, lots of fun with a non stop killing, explosions and all that action stuff that you love and missed so much!

Simply, what you have to do is to grab this game and ignore the multiplayer, having lots of fun in single player. Multiplayer is really boring in there, what they were trying to achieve is to take players back to 1999, to the era of Quake/Unreal face paced deathmatches and it didn't work for them. The whole community accepted the multiplayer in such a bad way, that they ended up with giving away all the DLC for free, which were chargeable previously. Not even to mention it's online.

To sum everything up!
You should grab this game if you:

+ DOOM fan!
+ Looking for a fast paced action FPS game
+ Eager to have tons of fun in a high quality action game
+ Love the variety of different weapons in games
+ Enjoy exploding enemies and throwing guts all over the levels
+ Fan of a quality products. This game shows a great combination of well designed levels, great variety of different monsters, nicely done animation and, of course, unforgettable soundtrack

Recommend to avoid if you:

- Expect a complex game, where you will enjoy a single player and then dive straight into amazing multiplayer mode for many more hours. Multiplayer is dull and pretty much dead here, however this game has its own fan base with faithful players, who play it a lot desperately trying to attract other people to play it. But after all, it’s up to you, who knows, maybe you’ll find it amazing
- Want to play a game with lots of open locations and activities. DOOM is pretty much linear with its corridor levels and its repetitive mechanics - rush through the levels, kill thousand of monsters, deal with a boss, watch a cutscene, proceed to the next level.

Enjoy!
