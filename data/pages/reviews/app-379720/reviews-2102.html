\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying DOOM (2016) on PC is the definitive way to play the game. My favorite FPS of all-time! 10/10

I first got DOOM on the Switch back in 2017 (putting over 150 hours into it), and I came to greatly love the game, especially for its fun and fluid gameplay that encourages aggression and experimentation with the combat. What's also amazing is how there's so much optional content in the main campaign: If you're a completionist and want to find every piece of lore and upgrade everything, then go ahead. If not, then don't worry about it; although the game will be harder. It just felt refreshing to have the freedom to seek out the extra upgrades and complete optional challenges, or learn more about the story via lore. The story is fairly non-intrusive; it's there and you can flesh it out with lore and holo-recordings, but if you don't care about story, then you can just get back to wiping out demons.


This game has been getting a lot cheaper as time has passed, so pick this up if you want an exhilarating and fun FPS that ditches a lot of annoying tropes that bog down a lot of FPS games these days. (Intrusive story, always hiding behind cover, hand-holding, microtransactions)

The game runs insanely well on PC (A powerful one, that is. You'll want at least a GTX 960 if you want a high resolution and framerate). Having a steady 60 FPS makes the combat easier to keep track of, and the more-detailed environments are amazing as well. I love this game on Switch, as Panic Button did a great job at optimizing the game for it. The resolution and framerate of DOOM on Switch aren't nearly as good the PC version, but it's good enough to retain the incredible gameplay, combat, and visuals of DOOM. The Switch version of DOOM got me to love DOOM, and I recommend it to any Switch owner, as playing DOOM anywhere rules, but if you want the best way to experience DOOM, then check this game out on PC. (Or double dip, if you're a Switch owner)\t\t\t\t\t
