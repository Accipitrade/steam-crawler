\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe original DOOM revolutionized gaming- it featured incredible levels of violence and gore alongside puzzles and a very high but not outright unfair difficulty, and was one of the first First Person Shooters to reach popularity, largely due to the fact that it was on multiple consoles, including the Super Nintendo Entertainment System. The sequel, Doom 2, expanded on the original. Doom 3 attempted to reinvigorate the series, but ended up using jumpscares and darkness, which, though admittedly a staple for most horror games, detracts from the game by being so omnipresent.

DOOM 2016 returns to the series' roots: brutal violence against hordes of demons, and none of that pansy horror stuff. It even omits something that almost every newer game features: reloading. Much like the original DOOM, your weapons will keep firing until there's no more ammunition. Because you're fighting against a very large number of enemies, this is a boon, as you don't need to waste time reloading before you keep firing.

The new features fit right in to the DOOM franchise- Glory Kills make killing even more fun and restore health, weapon mods that add secondary features to your weapon (all of which are very useful), and collectibles. There are even levers that unlock classic DOOM stages, adding to the nostalgia factor.

The game features very little story itself, instead preferring to use collectibles, brief custcenes, and even the levels themselves to explain what's going on. This keeps the action flowing and the less-exciting segments few and far between, something that has been missing from modern games for a while now.

Multiplayer is also fun, having special demon transformations that make you far deadlier but drop when you are killed. You can't choose your weapon mods, but it makes up for it by having more weapon variety and a customizable character.

My only real complaint is that the DLC only contain multiplayer features, and that the season pass is more expensive than the individual DLC.

My final score is 9/10. It feels too short and the DLC are junk, but the game itself is glorious.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wasn't sure I could still enjoy a game like this. First person shooters have changed since they first arrived as virtual shooting galleries in the early 90s. Games like Deus Ex and System Shock added a heavy dose of narrative and choice, and eventually led to modern classics like Bioshock and Dishonored.

The idea of a first person shooter focusing on the *shooting* almost seems quaint in 2016. Sure, Doom gives you a few side missions here or there, and the ability to upgrade your weapons a bit. But this is hardly an RPG in disguise. This is a chance to dive in, move fast, and shoot demons in the face.

I thought I had outgrown that.

I was wrong.

Doom is an easy contender for the best game of 2016. It's unapologetically fun. It never takes itself too seriously. The weapons are powerful and cathartic. It provides a stiff challenge without being unfair. And, amazingly for a Bethseda product, it is almost freakishly well optimized, running smooth as silk with the settings cranked up high.

It's a must-play. And that's coming from someone who rolled his eyes when it was first announced.\t\t\t\t\t
