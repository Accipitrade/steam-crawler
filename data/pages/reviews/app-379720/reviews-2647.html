\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll finally review this game.

First of all, you may be wondering; is it better than the original games?
Short answer; no.
Is it any good? Short answer; Of course.

Here's what I have to say about this new Doom game.
The graphics and textures are just all right.
The new weapons are honestly, worse than the original ones, especially the pistol, I don't like it at all.
The shotguns are the only weapon that are good in my opinion.
But you can use original Doom first-person mode (weapon in front of you) which is way better than the normal thing but that's my opinion.
Snapmap is pretty great and this time, it's inside the game, instead of out, snapmap is the thing that'll make you play more and more and it is really easy to use, anyone could use it and make a somewhat decent map but only of course, if you take the time to make it properly.
Enemies are pretty cool overall but for some specific reasons, I prefer those in the original Doom games.
Why some of them have shields? Is it me or it takes more bullets to kill them? Even though I played the original Doom games in highest difficulty, even played Brutal Doom and Brutal Doom 64 on highest difficulty, this game isn't hard but it enemies seems to be harder to kill, or perhaps it is just me .
Music is great, that's all what I have to say about that.

Multiplayer? Honestly, I don't care about it, if it were more of a Quake III or Unreal Tournament multiplayer with bots, then I would probably love it, but it's not the case, they shouldn't have made a multiplayer gamemode, instead, they should have made more content for the single-player such as new emies, new weapons, etc...

Well, If it was just of the campaign and the multiplayer, I would honestly think that this game would not be worth the 30$+ I paid for this game but... the snapmap probably makes it the game of the year.
Wolfenstein levels? Check!
Oriignal Doom levels? Check!
Original Doom 64 levels? Check!
You should get it now, the community seems to be making lot of maps and you can even create your own and it is pretty easy.


8.5/10 - Verdict, not as better as the previous games but a great game anyway, it ain't a "failed sequel".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: The game goes back to playing like an old school first person shooter, your character is a badass that punches the demon's balls so hard it switches places with their eyes. There's lot of guns. Small guns. Big guns. Bigger guns. Big ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ guns. It's really fast, lots of demons to shoot, lots of cool things to find. Awesome bosses and pretty graphics. One of the best shooters in the last few years imo. It's like a 100gb download, so it might take a while. Be advised. Other than that - 9/10

Doom is a great game! Definitely the best shooter I've played in the last few years.

Now, take this as a warning, if you really enjoyed the new style of Doom 3, you might be disappointed or not like this one at all.

This version of Doom drops the slow, more story driven, horror-esq gameplay of Doom 3 for a fast paced, action based one that reminds me a lot of the old school shooters. You move really fast, enemies shoot projectiles that can be dodged, and you're rewarded for your quick reflexes. You have really big maps to explore, even though your objectives are pretty simple like, "Grab the yellow and blue key cards to progress", "Kill everything to progress", "Destroy gore nests and kill enemies" with an occasional boss and mini boss fights, and some dialogue progressing the story here and there. The game combines old school mechanics with modern graphics, upgrade systems and features. It adds a "glory kill" feature, that allows you to execute a sunned enemy. You'll do this a lot as they're more likely to drop health pick ups when you do it.

"Wow that sounds boring" One might say. It does sound boring, and had it been any other game I think it would be boring. Doom stays fresh with a lot of exploration, a huge arsenal of weapons, most having two upgrades that completely changes how they function and adds a new depth to the gameplay. There's also upgrades for your power armor that you can search, some including better map tracking, shorter cooldown for grenades, switching guns quicker to make the gameplay even faster, reducing enviromental damage, among others. Each map also has challenges that award you with an upgrade point, rune trials which are also upgrades for your character, including glory kills dropping armor pick ups along with health, a better ammo auto pick up. If you upgrade those runes by using it, they can unlock new features such as dropping BFG ammo from normal demons. You can also find argent cells to upgrade your health/ammo/armor.

The exploration consists of:

* Praetor Tokens to upgrade your armor
* Argent Cells to upgrade your character
* Field Drones to buy upgrade for your weapons, most can have 2 upgrades
* Super Health/Armor (Maxes yours to 200/200 regardless of your upgrade level)
* Toy Marines (Unlocks enemy models for you to inspect)
* Classic Doom Maps on each stage. Nuff said, really cool and most of them are very well hidden.

The weapons are:
Shotgun
Heavy Rifle
Plasma Rifle
Rocket Launcher
Chain Gun
Gauss Rifle

***Special: BFG, Super Shotgun, Chainsaw, Pistol

Normal weapons can have two types of alternate fire upgrades for your right click, such as the heavy rifle firing mini missiles. The Plasma rifle overheating and shooting a blast of energy at max heat. Etc. After all upgrades for one alternate fire is completed, you get a challenge like "Kill x number of Summoners with the alternate fire" Then you unlock a super upgrade like the plasma rifle overheating on its own so you can always use the charged blast.

Special weapons have only one tree of upgrades, or none, and could use some type of special ammo. Chainsaw uses fuel charges that deplete based on how strong the enemy is, but enemies killed by the chainsaw always drops bullets for the other guns.

Doom's classic enemies are back and my god, it's so cool to see them with modern graphics. It's really awesome. The boss fights are very well done, even though they are short and the spacing between them is kinda funky (all three are closer to the end of the game).

I feel like the Multiplayer and the Snapmap features are underappreciated. The singleplayer campaign is extremely fun with a good 12 hours of content, which you'll really want to replay as soon as you're done in order to get all secrets. It is so well done that I feel the game could have been single player only, but they still added a multiplayer and a map creator on top of that.

Graphically the game is very pretty and well optimized. I ran it at 1080p, Nightmare settings with a GTX 1060 6GB and it almost never dropped below the 60 mark. 60fps in a game like this is crucial, and it being so well optimized is an achievement. It can even run at 60fps on consoles with mediumish settings (which looks pretty good, possibly even one of the best looking 60fps titles on console). If you can run it at the Nightmare quality however, you won't be disappointed in the looks. Great textures, the spark particles, the gore, everything is gorgeous. Even when you're zipping around shooting non-stop.

The story feels like a reboot but not really. It's kinda hard to understand what they were really going for. It's not a bad story though.

THE SOUNDTRACK IS AWESOME. No need to say anything about it, just listen to this: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pNkQMtZAMAw

With all that being said, pros and cons -

Pros:
+ Gameplay is so much fun.
+ Big maps, lots of exploring and upgrading to do.
+ The arsenal is amazing.
+ Glory Kills are a lot of fun.
+ Great Boss fights.
+ Amazing Graphics + Performance.
+ Awesome level design.
+ Snap Maps.
+ Multiplayer.

Cons:
- A couple of bugs which includes falling through the floor when executing an enemy on a specific staircase, nothing major.
- Awkward spacing towards the bosses. Would rather have one of those bosses earlier in the game.
- The game drags a little towards the end.
- Might be a bit repetitive depending on the type of player you are.
- The Download. Seriously. I had to download about 115GBs for this game, and that even on a good Brazilian internet takes an entire week.

Aside from the download simulator and those minor problems, the game is very, very good. Not only one of the best shooters of late, it's also my favorite game of 2016. I kinda feel bad now for not playing it back then. The game is an easy 9/10 for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort version: Check out the demo, definitly worth a look if you like FPS and you get a good idea of what to expect in this game. Not just an okish game done with a great engine, maybe not perfect but it was really fun.

Did not play the multiplayer game, writing this after finishing the game on ultra violence difficulty. One gripe I have is the strange mix of arcade like elements with some story telling and more 'serious' immersion. Nothing too bad except some mastery challenges for the last upgrades for weapon modifications, these were rather distracting for me for a while. It involves killing demons in certain ways which is not always easily done on the way, maybe I'm old school but I care about not dying so I simply gave up on some of these challenges later and focused on survival and actual progress. If you want you have much to accomplish, secrets, weapon mastery challenges, level challenges, upgrade tokens for your armor, rune trials, argent cells (upgrade of max health/armor/ammo). Add some distracting steam achievements on top if you like and you successfully have wasted some time in hell V4.0.

Grahpics: Nothing to complain about as expected, well, changing to vulkan reduced FPS by ~10 here (Geforce GTX 1060 6GB, Win10), was expecting a boost instead. But opengl graphics and performance were great, never dropped below 90 FPS I think in the first level on custom high/ultra settings.
Music was even better than expected, I like hard metal music, it may be dead as hell and was quite fitting in combat.
Level design could be a weak spot for some, some elements felt a bit repetetive but it does not hurt the overall experience too much imo. Maybe a few runes are a little too powerful, I avoided ammo boost for example, seemed to take out some fun.
As a detail, demons drop way more health if your health is low, maybe a way to fine balance difficulty, in contrast to most other things these deus ex machina like events when close to death don't feel like an old school hell at all, like it or not. Still waiting for the days when some advanced difficulty settings are the norm.

It's 2017 and I'm still counting myself, I'm talking about the one and only 'score': Your number of deaths. Maybe a task too personal to let a machine do it for you. You're not in hell AND lying too yourself, right ?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game knows a very fast pace that needs to be matched by the player. Rune challenges are even faster and secrets are extremely difficult to aquire because of its timing element. Visually, it looks good with enough detail and the level layout is far from repetative (unlike Fear with it offices or Doom 3 with its corridors).

Multiplayer is fun to play when you are evenly matched to the other players. A casual Doom player will leave within five minutes when the other opponents are die-hard Doom players who are able to match the game's speed.

I still recommend it, since it's not a bad game. It's just a game I can't phisically play due to its pacing.

In short: Great game, looks good but not for people with rheumatism or derivative.
