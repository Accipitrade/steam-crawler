\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDuring college in the late 90s I was the kid with the aging 286 PC while everyone else was sporting the sexy new Pentiums. Weird Al was writing demeaning stories about the "Dorito" powering my machine and I found myself locked out of many games that required a 386, to say nothing for games like Quake 2 requiring 3D graphics cards.

At least I had DooM.

Ok, so storytime's over. There's my creds. Buy DooM (2016). Do it. Not because it's a nostalgic trip back to the fast-paced slaughterfest that was the original (and it is). Buy it because it's the harbinger of a new breed of shooter that combines that breakneck speed with modern innovation. DooM provides an arsenal of varied weapons. Each one can be upgraded by doing what you wanna do anyway: killing and poking through the massive levels. There's no cover system. The DooM Marine doesn't need to hide. You're the hunter here and your kills fuel your survival and at times you will find yourself killing tens of creatures in mere seconds. Sounds really easy? Well, it's not. However, if you think you're some kind of Slayer you can plunge yourself into a permanent death Ultra Nightmare mode where you will see the hundreds of dead player markers litering the bloody ground. You won't succeed, but you'll LOVE it.

Nostalgia drives a lot of my purchases, but I was sceptical of this purchase until I watched some footage and smiled that manic smile that fans of DooM would associate with finding a shiny new BFG. Now the hordes of Hell fear me like they did so many years ago. Grab your shotgun, marine. Hell needs culling.\t\t\t\t\t
