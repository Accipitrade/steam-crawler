\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay:
When previous generations think of video games, games in the DOOM franchise are probably what comes to mind. Awful, gore-filled scenes of shooting bloodbaths, killing demons just for the hell of it. The DOOM Franchise has always been centered around fast-paced, simple shooters, and the 2016 version follows suit. While very simple, DOOM feels very different than any other game in its genre. It truly does feel like that stereotypic first-person shooter, with plasma guns and gattling guns blowing up your unsightly, demonic enemies. While simple, the game includes three different upgrade systems which give it a sense of progression. Adding to the progression, the guns that you receive throughout the game are very fun to use, each in their own right.
-
Narrative:
Unfortunately, this is my first experience with the DOOM franchise. Therefore, I don't know the character's origin story or any context to the game. This is something Bethesda overlooked and left the entirety of the game to the gameplay and its features. To me, it seemed like we were a man obsessed with killing demons. We were sent to Mars where a portal to hell was opened and the planet was infested with demons. He seems to have no regard for life of any sort throughout the whole game, but that may be his schtick. He closes the portal and it ends happily ever after. Personally, this is a massive weak spot for this game.
-
Visual:
For a 2016 release, the game looks average for a AAA developer. It doesn't have a special visual style, but it also doesn't seem to exactly mimic a realistic style. The demons are gruesome and awesome-looking, but they lack an element to make the game darker and heavier. Of course, there is a ton of gore in DOOM, which defines this game, and presumably the franchise's style.
-
Audio:
To my surprise, one of the places in which this game excels is the audio. The soundtrack is heavy, with awesome riffs that fit the gameplay perfectly. The other sounds sound good as well, with solid surround-sound support and well-done sound effects. Those sound effects don't stand up to the previously mentioned soundtrack, though.
-
Technical:
There is not much to say about this game apart from the fact that it is very well optimized. My computer has always had a fairly easy time with Bethesda games, and the same be applied here. High frame rate is especially important in such a chaotic, fast-paced shooter. Just make sure you turn off motion blur. No one likes motion blur.
-
Rating:
7/10\t\t\t\t\t
