\r\n\t\t\t\t\tits just not good. bought this due to all the hype and praise, and getting many goty nominations. but after finishing the campaign, just gonna say its not a good game.
i've played better, i've seen better. this isn't even goty contender material. a lot of praise and glorifying of the game comes from its "LETS ROCK!" intro, but after thats over, you got 10 hours of boring kill rooms.

its not even smart about the systems it incoporates, most of the skill/unlock systems in place seem like an afterthought and not well implemented. I remember feeling that way about the 50% mark of the game.

and my god, the checkpointing (because there are no manual saves or quick saves) is god awful. the only it saves via story beats, or via kill room encounters before and after.

the music is bad, just bad. like uninspired , unoriginal, just generic, like at no point did it feel good to hear the game's music.

graphics-wise, its OK,

and its clearly made for consoles, lots of the game is designed to be console friendly. i mean just in general, the levels are designed as such, lots of long narrow corridors, lots of press X to open door, press the melee button to do a "SICK FINISHER" , like a lot of this game is gimmicky, even through its weapon unlocks, gunplay, collectibles.

reminds me, the cutscenes are awkward, the game will often stick you into a room you go in, cant leave, until a cutscene/dialog happens, and you just sit there waiting for the game to continue, like just hopping around, because some longwinded badly animated exit/enter sequence is playing out.

i'd sum it up as, this game is just average, doesn't deserve the praise, doesn't do anything original and doesn't go anywhere with the story or premise. definitely do not buy it until its bargain bin price, you aren't missing anything special.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is for anyone who have the assumption that Doom 4 sucks.
I had the same assumption. As a massive fan of Doom1-2 who was entirely repulsed by Doom3 I just thought that the Doom series is over, they are just doing the money grab now. It was further reinforced by the overwhelmingly negative feedback on the beta, which in fact even prompted Steam to change their policy by erasing all beta reviews when the final game releases.
Here are a few of my assumptions about the game:
- Yes, its flashy, but the level design is crap
- COD-style 1 dimensional maps ie straight line
- ♥♥♥♥♥♥ spoonfeeding gameplay
- It just simply lacks the charm of the originals (again level design)
- The soundtrack will be a generic modern FPS crap

Then came Steam sale and seeing the very positive reviews, I gave it a shot.

In the beginning: "oh god this game sucks, ♥♥♥♥♥♥ level design, WTF enemies dropping health, lol easy peasy"

Then I played more and my prejudices have quickly disintegrated. The early levels are indeed ♥♥♥♥♥♥, but then it becomes excellent. Non-linear maps, very challenging fights even on Medium. The glory kills are not just for show they are an essential gamplay element to fight the seemingly unfair number of strong, fast and hard hitting enemies.
The game art is excellent and while does not even look similar to the originals in terms of feeling, it is perfectly good on its own right.
The music is excellent too - if you like metal that is. While I prefer the more melodic Doom1-2 soundtrack, I have to note that at its time it was considered heavy music being based on Pantera, Slayer etc. songs. Doom 2016 plays modern metal. And it's heavy as ♥♥♥♥.

All in all, this is a well crafted game and the best FPS to come out in many many years. Worth a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
