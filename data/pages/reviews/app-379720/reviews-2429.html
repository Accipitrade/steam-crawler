\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit. This first paragraph was written post-doom eternal. I think the game stands out even better now, because ironically enough, eternal showed us why doom 2016 is so good. 2016 has very few story breaks, no stupid forced lore mongering, no unnecessary characterization of the protagonist, and great writing where there is any. Most players don't give a damn about the ancient celestial lore of whatever the ♥♥♥♥, and 2016 stands by the player in this by pushing you past all the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ and skipping to the double-barreled slaughter. There is no handholding; instead there's creativity in your offensive approach. Eternal moves the gameplay forward by making it faster and tighter, but by doing this it imposes many rights and wrongs into the gameplay-- arguably more than it needed to. So in conclusion, I love this game even more now for what its sequel lacks, and I highly recommend playing them both in order.

My original review:

Most fun I've ever had in a singleplayer shooter campaign. This game captures the intense feel of the original fast-paced arena shooter of 1993 with additional modern mechanics that fit right in. It has a very good power and difficulty curve, constantly keeping you on your toes while staying a fresh and interesting experience throughout the whole game. Very good, instructive level design that avoids hand-holding. Much potential for different styles of play. Nightmare difficulty makes for a great challenge if you like dying a lot, or aren't as bad as me at FPS.

Very little story is forced upon you during gameplay, but finding ingame codex entry thingies around the levels reveals details through texts that are quite well-written, unlike this review. Most complaints of this game are regarding its pacing, calling its non-stop action too repetitive. I disagree-- the player can slow the pace by reading/listening to the story material as they come across it.

The game is also well optimized, and features cpu-gentile Vulkan rendering which my puny i5 4790k and GTX 750 TI handled like a champ while still making slaughtering hellspawn with shrapnel and plasma look absolutely glorious. This is further backed by an outstanding heavy metal soundtrack by Mick Gordon that both complements the gameplay perfectly and stands great on its own when you feel like triggering adrenaline overproduction without requiring a gaming pc.

Overall, not including multiplayer which you can pretend doesn't exist, ♥♥♥♥ 9/10 best game of 2016 dont @ me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom is the hidden gem of 2016. Not only is it one of the best FPS experiences I have had in a long time. In my opinoin, its the best game from 2016. I have not played this game in some time, and since I first got it back in 2016 on the PS4 i decided with the steam sale, and the upcoming release of Doom Enternal to pick it up and get back into slaying demons.

The graphics, sound and combat are still jaw-dropping, even for 2018. Doom excells on having an outstanding single player experience that makes you come back again and again to find all the hidden secrets, and to continue to upgrade the Doom slayer to be even more badass.

The muliplayer is extremely traditional. Old fashioned Doom style, small maps, weapons, armor and upgrades are scattered around the map. Nothing in my opinon to write home about. However does have a dedicated, but small, player base if you want to play.

Do I recommend? Hell yes. This is such a fun game to have on your PC to fire up from time to time to max out your PC and slay demons. What isnt there to like then running around and light speed with a double-barrel and chainsaw while heavy metal is blasting in the background with demons crawling everywhere in glourious 4K?
