\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe are not enough words to praise a game like the one I am about to review, Doom is perhaps the best reboot of a franchise to date. So, I will try to be crystal clear when it comes to detail my experience with this game.

Story

We play, obviously, as the Doom Slayer or Doom Guy, as the fans call him, a being with super human abilities and a set of murderous skills whose only purpose in life is to slay demons and wreak havoc in hell's legions. The storyline is quite simple, Hell breaks loose on Mars and its moons after some human scientists investigate and try to control some sort of unlimited energy that comes directly from Hell, by making a pact with the demons and take over mars, Olivia Pierce is the cause of the disaster, as well as humanity's inability to control the invasion.

Here is where the Doom Slayer steps in, one of the many things, as well as artifacts and demons, the scientists retrieve a coffin that contains the body of the main protagonist, sealed by the demons to contain his rage and hate, he wakes up amids the chaos. Filled with hatred, we start our relentless killing and frantic adventure.

Gameplay

Straightforward and honest, Doom 2016 is a game that doesn't waste time in boring and slow introductions and takes you directly to the action the moment we start wearing our armor, the gore, the blood, the guts and so on will acompany us until the end of our hunt, to get the job done we will have at our disposal a variety of weapons that will have different characteristics for certain scenarios and enemies, which I must say are very smart and aggressive and never give up on see you dead. From shotguns and LMG's and rocket launchers and of course the BFG, the fact that none of these weapons needs to be reloaded makes the game even more fast-paced, you run out of bullets for certain weapon, switch and the problem is gone.

Of course, Doom needed new level design and they nailed it, the different stages we visit are spacious, giving us the freedom to jump from platform to platform, run, dodge incoming attacks, all of these new details make the game to constantly feel renewed and the fast-paced sensation of running and jumping make none of the repetition feels like that at all.

With the new design brings slight changes that make it all even better, our armor or the Praetor Suit comes with some new mechanics that will allow us to traverse the new levels with ease, this changes can be made by spending points on our suit and improving it and other characteristics, the same goes for weapons, each weapon has new modes that can be unlock as we progress through out the game.

Graphics

Doom is beautiful and detailed, the levels vary as we advance and fight our way through Mars and its moons, from the reddish dusty atmosphere of the red planet to the interior of the UAC facilities, we encounter a great design and the in-depth details of how slowly the dominions of Hell take over the base creating these sort of fleshy and bloody mess smeared all over of what once were facilities for human technology.

Not only Mars is the main scenario, we can also travel to Hell and continue with our killing spree making demons and high lords of hell try desperately to end you once again, it took them a lot of time to catch the Doom Slayer so now that we have the opportunity to wreak havoc on their domains. We can finally see how the devs gave free reign to their imagination by creating a great and harsh environament that only the Slayer can survive, spikes, fleshy and stoned walls, multiple paths, blood and a feeling of hoplessness make for Hell a great stage for fighting.

The effects do not fall short as they can show how great the new engine works, explosions of blood, guts and fire alike drown the fears and shrieks of the many demons we eliminate, this and more with not a single bug or glitch and an unstoppable and killer 144 FPS, th engine runs smoothly and with no problems at all on ultra, of course using Vulkan, a marvelous way to play one of the best games of 2016. Top notch graphics and effects.

Music and Sound

Good lord, maybe one of the best soundtracks for a game I have ever listened to, it is outstanding how Mick Gordon kills it with every track that gives us this limitless sensation of being in control all the time, atmospheric when it has to be and then an endless avalanche of riffs and drumming that will pump you up to get more, to surpass anything you have done before, the music will make you want more and more blood and chaos and you would not want that specific moment you are in to finish, it is like that, a feast for any metalhead and gore fan, the two go hand in hand to deliver a frantic blow of power and violence. Absolutely amazing.

Regarding voices and other aspects of sound, it is well done, the voices are believable for the few characters that have dialogue in game, since the Doom Slayer doesn't talk and only shows his mood by destroying anything and killing all that looks a bit like a demon. In relation to sound effects, everything is on top, the demons make all sort of noises and grunting sounds as our bullets rip through their flesh, everything feels sticky and disgustingly satisfying. To add more ambient, I totally recommended to wear a headset with great sound and bass to blast the crap out of your ears, it creates immersion and a total feeling of endless addiction.

The weak point

Doom also offers a multiplayer experience that can be fun and all, however it kind of falls short, at least for me, I had fun playing a couple of matches, but in the end the connection quality and lag make the MP a no go for me, short and unoriginal concept, repetitive as it can be found on multiple other games that offer the same kind of modes, it remains fast-paced, despite that, I'd stick to the SP campaign if I were you, unless you have a good connection making your experience smooth.

The game comes with an option to create maps and whatnot, useful for those who like to create and express their imagination, I did not try this mode so I have no opinion.

Conclusion

Doom 2016 is a clear example of how a reboot of a game should be done, it is implacable and remorseless when it comes to gameplay, music and atmosphere, by the time you finish you will want more gore and maybe a greater challenge by choosing a harder level of difficulty, I strongly recommend this game to any fan of FPS and frantic gaming, it is an unforgettable experience you will remember for ever and as the first few words we heard when starting the game, just "Rip and Tear, until it is done!"\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty much the perfect balance of exploration and non stop, lightning fast, metal-fueled, blood bath demon slaughtering.

It is a shooter first, and a phernomonal one at that, and platformer second. It has great explorartion to it, but you won't find as much depth to it as say the Metroid Prime games. The platfomring/exploration however has a wonderful supporting role, and keeps the game from feeling like a hallway shooter, and only going from point A to B

Lore is great, lore is completely optinal, but if you do decide to read deeper, the game world unfolds and everything you're doing has a deeper purpose other than, look, demon, I'mma shoot em.

Graphics are top notch, and cranked all the way, it's some of the best i've ever seen. It also runs incredibly well (120 fps on a GTX 1070). I was disappointed that it didn't support SLI on the Vulkan API, but my FPS is already so high, it surpases my monitor's refresh at 100hz, so it really doesn't matter.

Theres something in it for everyone so its an absolute MUST PLAY from me.\t\t\t\t\t
