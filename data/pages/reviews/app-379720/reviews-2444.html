\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoom (2016)

Gameplay (10/10):
I hesitate to give Doom a 10/10 on gameplay because it's basically a perfect score but I struggle to find a reason not to. It's your typical old school shooter, plenty of targets and not enough toothpicks to get the giblets from your teeth after you're done scoffing them for breakfast. The weapons feel varied and good to use, the upgrades for them are nice and the levels are designed very well with some incredibly well hidden secrets. I struggeled to find the secrets in a lot of them and never came close to getting all of the upgrade points. The difficulty is great, it provides a nice challenge and isn't afraid to smack you down if you've gotten too big for your space boots. You've got to be able to navigate your way around the arena well enough, use your weapons to your advantage, power ups if you have any and glory kill all that you can to get your health back up. Which in the end is a double edged sword, needing to get in close to recover your health will end up with you possibly losing a bit so you need to weigh your options. Possible health and kill or just snipe them off from here?

It blends very well altogether and is a fantastic time.

Story (5/10):
The story isn't what you've come here for but it's nice to know that it was included. However, I'm giving more points here because not only is there a small, simple story to give context to your actions but there's lore to the creatures that you kill should you want some extra details and a bit of backstory as well to your characters previous actions if you want to listen to audio logs. It's a nice touch but it's not a story that takes priority over anything else.

Art (7/10):
There's a lot of detail that has been put in to the game, perhaps a bit much detail than I wanted to see in certain areas... such as disembowling a mancubus. Though what is there looks good, not exactly stunning. There's a washed out, semi-cartoonish look about it, at least to me that's what it looked like, that gives it a feel like it won't age in time. Each of the games three zones (Facility, Mars, Hell) look very visually distinct from each other and have a nice feel to each of them, polished and everything is conveyed clearly. The enemies all have distinct differences in their design so you'll never be confused about what is fighting you.

Soundtrack (8/10):
There's two types of tracks to this game when listening - there's the atmospheric tracks for when the game has died down and you're not helping yourself to a second course of demon entrails and the battle tracks for when you realise you could go for thirds. The atmospheric tracks are very good at setting up the scene and the battle tracks are great for getting your blood pumping and setting you in the scene and getting you in the mood for a fight. I'd pick out specific tracks to listen to but you should just listen to all of the fight tracks, they're all so good to listen to if you're a metal head.

Overview (30/40):
Doom is a very fun game to just sit down with, play and have stupid fun to enjoy yourself with. It's a completionists dream, having plenty of secrets to find and fun challenges to complete along the way and provides plenty of content. It's also one of those games that you can replay at higher difficulties to challenge yourself and season yourself more, absolutely worth the pick up. If you're a story heavy player you won't find much in the way of it here but even as a story heavy player myself sometimes you just want to watch the world burn.
