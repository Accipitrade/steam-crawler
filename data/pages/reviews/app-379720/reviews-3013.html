\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt had some serious awesomeness and some serious not awesomeness. I beat it on nightmare.

You've got amazing fun weapons, a great upgrade system, a great secret system, tons of sweet enemies that move about the level in an extremely unique way all of it denched in gore with some spectacular level design that somehow still managed to feel like the old games all the while incorporated the one of the best keep-you-playing narratives ID has ever done (I know, I know, that bar is not very high)

And then you have these extremely out of place boss fights where you get dropped into a single room with an extremely powerful old-school multi stage boss. You learn a stage's pattern, beat it, and the boss does a new thing that kills you. You repeat the frustrating slog untily you beat the boss. Considering how fast paced, mobile and unique normal combat is, combined with the brilliant level design, these boss fights are extremely out of place and really mar and otherwise great experience.

Lastly, the gore pretty much vanishes immediately. Seconds after brutalizing an army of demons the room looks just like it did before you started shooting. Anybody who has spent even a little bit of time with Killing Floor 2 knows that you can get tremendous amounts of gore strewn about the level before performance is a concern. It's just crappy that peeps with a powerful rig cant enable such a feature.

Overall, yes I recommend this game. It was a massive step in the right direction that did some truly unique and refreshing stuff for the FPS genre while kicking me right in the nostalgia, despite those bafflingly stupid boss fights.\t\t\t\t\t
