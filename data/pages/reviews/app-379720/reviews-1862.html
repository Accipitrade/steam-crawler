\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start by saying I didn't grow up with the DOOM series, and this game was my first dive into it.

As a noob with little knowledge of the classic version of this game, I found the secret DOOM levels to be a nice touch for veteran players, although it didn't really did much to me in terms of nostalgia. The little action figures were also cool and fun to find.

Challenges and Runes are a good addition, some are easy, some are hard, some make you question if it's even possible.

The graphics are awesome... as long as I'm not in an area swamped with too many demons; otherwise the lag commences.

Story's pretty good, nothing too special.

The glory kills are fun to do, but after a while of spamming 'F', it kinda gets a bit boring.

Weapons are good, I prefer the Super Shotgun.

Overall not too shabby, but just to be safe, I'd get it while it's on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM is a very innovative game. One, because it used full caps for its name on Steam. Two, because it uses its satisfying "glory kill" system to get you up close and personal with every single one of the game's many detailed and ravenous enemies. The benefits of this are twofold: it's super satisfying and it forces you to get closer to enemies when you're on low HP (instead of retreating behind cover like in other games). This keeps the pace of every fight skipping along and the variety of animations (one for each limb, extra ones when near walls, and one for on a ledge as well as one from a jump) make sure that you never see certain moves too many times. This issue ruins a lot of "finisher" systems, especially the one in Focus Home's The Surge, where every enemy has basically the same animations. Luckily, DOOM has different ones for each enemy, varying wildly in technique.
The combat system never stops, with weapon swapping being snappy and no reloads to slow you down. Upgrades can make it even quicker, allowing you to swap between your mods with better efficiency. Even after you acquire all of the base weapons (ranging from a weak pistol to a massive chain gun), you get several sub weapons, and two super powerful support weapons. The chainsaw utilizes the sparse gas canister ammo to execute an instant finisher on any enemy (ranging from 1-3 fuel consumption based on its size) netting you lots of 'munitions and a gruesome animation. The other support weapon is the BFG, which utilizes energy cells. It's basically a one shot kill. Every one of these weapons can be upgraded, either by adding ammo through the Argent Cell system, or using the weapons modification system.
Here is where I started getting interested in the game, the RPG mechanics. By tracking down armory robots scattered throughout each level, you can use your upgrade points (acquired through various means) to add more bang to your arsenal. Each mod feels significant, and you can fully master your favorite weapons by completing challenges. This allows you to specialize in the ones you like best (for me the Super Shotty and the Heavy AR). You can also use armor upgrade points (also found by scouring the map) to improve your classic Doom Guy Suit with various enhancements. There's also a Rune system which allows you to beat specific challenges to add certain upgrades to your character in the form of boosts (which can also be upgraded by completing longer challenges in the main game). There's a lot of upgrading and modifying, and it might be jarring to someone who's just in it for the running and gunning. As an RPG fan, I can say I enjoyed this addition, and I wouldn't want to see another Doom without it.
On the negative side, levels can often be winding and I found that the 3D map was very hard to control. The game also features a lot of collectables, but also gates that cut you off midway through the level. This forces you to either exploit bugs (like Gauss jumping or skips) or play the whole level again until you reach the stuff you were looking for. Luckily, the game is lenient with its replay system, allowing you to jump into any levels in the campaign you've completed with all the progress from your most recent save in the campaign. This is a godsend for completionists, allowing you to run through the game normally, then comb each level individually for secrets and collectables.
All in all, Doom is a fast paced and fun shooter with RPG elements that set it a step ahead of the pack. The familiar ebb and flow of combat harkens back to the original, while also expanding on the systems that made it great. The story is weak (but that's to be expected with most FPSs) however, this is compensated by the amount of lore and backstory you can find if you dig around hard enough. A good romp, and one that'll bring you back into the spirit of the classic shot-'em-ups. Rip and tear!

Update: This review was made a few hours before I completed the game. While it has been a significant amount of time since I finished, I feel I need to put in some thoughts on the ending. No spoilers, but it won't knock your socks off. There is a "twist" but is the most completely obvious and totally expected things ever. Not like this game was ever meant to be story based, but maybe something a bit better would've gone a long way. Doesn't drag the experience down much at all.
A little bit on the multiplayer too. I dipped my toes in it a bit, just seems like the typical multiplayer mode for single player FPS's (aside from the transforming into demons bit). The customization you can put into your character is awesome and all of the cosmetics that I can tell of are unlocked through gameplay. It's always nice to have a character creator free of pressure to spend money, and that's pretty cool. All around good game and I can't wait to test out Eternal when I actually buy it.\t\t\t\t\t
