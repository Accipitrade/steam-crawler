\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a bit of an enigma for me. It is really well optimized, gorgeous, and has an I-don't-give-a-f*ck attitude that other games could definitely learn from. I was initially really disappointed that they chose not to do metal for the soundtrack, but I understand that metal isn't for everyone. My main problem with this game is the way reloading, ammo, and health are treated. In other games, there is a delicate ballet of combat in which you must juggle shooting, reloading, melee, and grenades (or equipment). Doom has a gritty realism to it that really makes gameplay feel great. To get health or ammo you generally must kill an enemy, then a glowing shower of ammo and health explodes out of their corpse. It is a great gameplay mechanic that keep the combat fast paced, but it collides with the gritty realism and just feels out of place. Glowy ammo and health that falls out of corpses? There is no reloading or looking for ammo, which I find much more satisfying. Maybe in the future they can have two modes for the campaign - one set up like this Doom where enemies drop health and ammo, and one that has more traditional ammo pickups and health you must find (Like the health stations throughout this Doom game). All in all it is still a great fps and I hope they look into these options in the future.

Well Optimized
Beautiful

Cons:
No reloading or ammo boxes to hunt for :(
Enemies are pinatas full of glowy ammo and health\t\t\t\t\t

Multiplayer: Its gonna die. Maybe for like a few hours it will be good but i played one game and it wasnt mind blowing. iF YOURE GOING TO PLAY THIS GAME FOR MULTIPLAYER, DO NOT DO IT!!. The mutiplayer will get dull very quickly with the only progress in making your character look better and different gun skins. If You want multiplayer, there are better alterantives like CS:Go, BF4, etc that will fullfill your desire for multiplayer.

Overall: campaign (10/10) Multiplayer (5/10)
