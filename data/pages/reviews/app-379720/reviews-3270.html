\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was skeptical at first, given my experience with the multiplayer beta. I was even more skeptical following the misguided ad campaign. I was sure that this game would be a solid 5-6/10 at best.

I'm pleased to find that the single player experience is far better than I thought it would be. Controls are super tight, everything feels perfect mechanically, the game is a blast. After beating it twice, the second time on Nightmare difficulty, I find it hard to imagine a more ideal Doom game. In fact, it's one of the best FPSs I've ever played. Hopefully Id can keep it up.

10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMaybe I will update this review at a later time, but all I want to say is that this definitely is a worthy successor to the original Dooms, no matter what anyone else says. If you don't like the MP, that's no reason to say the game is bad. What happened to a game being judged by its campaign, which is where this game shines the brightest? Hell is like Hell should be, Doom Marine is like he should be, and the UAC takes itself and the plot as seriously as it did in Doom 3 which makes for a fascinating clash of chaos vs order. This game is about the progression of Doom as a franchise. I certainly have complaints, no game is perfect and I am patiently awaiting the first patch to hopefully fix most of the bugs (in this day and age games are so complex, can you really blame them for missing some?). Huge reccomend. I promise, the adrenaline is there and it does not let up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn excellent throwback to an older style of First Person Shooter. It has a modern take on the design of a fast-paced, constantly moving shooter combat but it feels Doom-like (and very Brutal Doom -like) and none of the additions of modern design hurt the campaign or the combat. The campaign has a couple of moments where the action grinds to a halt, but otherwise is composed of either combat or player's own exploration and travel, both of which are done very well.

Combat itself is very fast-paced, with demons either running, jumping or flying around a lot or slowly but surely walking towards the player in case of the heavier ones. The player himself will most likely also be running or jumping around the demons and circle-strafing them whenever possible. The weapons feel very impactful and fun to use (starter pistol excluded) and the battles are brutal projectile- and beamstorms. Both the player and the demons can die very quickly, even on the easier difficulties, so evasion and never stopping is the preferred survival tactic.

Most of the old things are still there. The collectible hunts and secrets are just updates on the old secret hunts and wall-hugging. The weapon upgrades are a new thing but they offer a lot of depth to the weapons and upgrade-dependant additional challenges, while not being necessary since the Doom Marine is a badass anyway. The armor upgrades are less interesting but they do offer some customization in the early game when the upgrade limits are still relevant. The Glory Kill system is actually very fast and works as a health regeneration mechanic, like Brutal Doom's fatalities, and helps keep the health hunts to a minimum.

The story is nothing to write home about, but aside from the gameplay-halting moments, I like the fact that there is a story. It gives the levels and the campaign a nice continuous feel more than the original Doom's much more bare-bones story did. The datalogs and heard conversations round the whole thing out and give the more lore-oriented people an additional aspect to enjoy, while not bothering those who don't enjoy lore stuff.

The only real negatives in the campaign are the few moments it stops completely to play a cutscene for the player. Most of the time, those cutscenes could have been replaced with radio conversations and the game could have kept the action going and the pace up. At the very least, the player could have explored the surrounding area for stuff while the conversation goes on in the background.

All in all, a very enjoyable 15 hour single player FPS and a return to form for Doom.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I played the original games A LOT when I was a kid, they were the first video games I ever played and I remember them fondly. When I first heard that id was making a new addition to the doom family, I was very happy, happy and concerned. When a company decides to breathe new life into a franchise, it's extremely easy to screw it up; any number of small issues can anger the veteran fanatics, after all. So I kept my excitement in check and my fingers crossed, hoping a new addition to the series wouldn't tarnish my old friend's name.
As soon as the campaign loaded I could feel a smile growing on my face, and that smile would only get bigger as I proceded. The weapons felt great, and the enemies looked good and unique, yet with a big nod to the monsters I fought as a child. I can very happily recommend this game to newcomers, who have never played a doom game; but more importantly, I can highly recommend this game to anyone who liked the original installations. This game will only make you love the series more. The gameplay is great, and the nostalgia is everywhere.\t\t\t\t\t
