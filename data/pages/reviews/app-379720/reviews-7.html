\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot recommended. Great graphics and gameplay, BUT id Software has a profound disrespect for the available free time of its gamers. The game is ludicrously difficult, even on easy settings. I gave up and played it on God settings in dev mode after losing far too many times in row. It also becomes the equivalent of Super Mario Bros with a shotgun for much of the game, jumping around from one platform to the next, and falling to your death more often than not. This is supposed to be Doom, not parkour!!!! The majority of your time playing Doom will be spent running around incessantly lost, simply trying to figure out how to get to the next checkpoint and level, then getting killed, and starting over at a checkpoint again. The developers put almost no hints or obvious clues that would help gamers move along quickly throughout the game, so you spend nearly all of your time lost in space, retracing your old steps to find something that must have been missed previously. Even online walkthroughs aren't very helpful, because all the levels are a confusing mess that look nearly identical everywhere you go. Doom has always been a great franchise through the years, but they really dropped the ball on this one unfortunately. If you value your free time, and are a casual stress-free gamer, avoid this one completely....\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI’ve never really had a HUGE itch for FPS games, but every so often I really want to play one. This was one of those times and this game absolutely did not disappoint. It knows what kind of game it wants to be and is so perfect at what it does.

I didn’t play any of the multiplayer, because fast-twitch Doom is not my idea of a great time. The campaign was a rip-and-tear, roaring good time. The controls are so buttery smooth. The cadence of the game almost feels like a clicker at certain points. There's a great variety of demons with different abilities and ways of engaging that requires different spacing and weapons to deal with them best. Luckily there's about a dozen different weapons available and half of them have 2 different mods that change the way you can use the gun. So each encounter can be a gun swapping, mod swapping rifle.

The game is at its best when you get in a groove of shooting this guy, tossing a grenade into a swarm of smaller guys, swapping guns, stunning this guy so you can focus the one charging you, shooting the first again until you can get a Glory Kill (up close melee gory kills) and armor and health pops out, then shoot a bunch more guys, run low on ammo, pull out the chainsaw and cut a demon in half, which spills out ammo refills like some kind of bloody, gory, colorful ammo pinata.

But there were other moments of exploring and finding some areas that made you a little nervous, like the good space Sci-Fi horror games (Alien Isolation, Dead Space, etc). There were entertaining challenge runes that asked you to kill demons in a very specific way, in a specific time. There were also so many little easter eggs and secrets that it rewarded you for walking around the environment a little more and not just getting frustrated that you weren’t constantly killing demons. The game is visually stunning, if not a bit disgusting. It was a very fun game. Honestly, it was just crazy, chaotic, running, shooting, bloody fun. Well done id Games and Bethesda!\t\t\t\t\t
