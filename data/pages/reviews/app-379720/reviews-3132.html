As long as it works.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI picked the game up on a sale for about $35, I have no real complaints besides that about 9 stages in I got severely tired of the whole "lock them in a room and make him kill things and let him out". While the original dooms also have the endless slaughters it allows you to keep moving without being locked in a small room. In doom 1 and 2 you kill monsters on the way from point A to point B, in Doom 2016 you walk thru a corridor and get locked in a room for 3-4 minutes till you clear out multiple waves of enemies and then it unlocks the door/elevator and you proceed to the next corridor to repeat. Mixing in the very similar stages on mars (where most of the game takes place) and the ever repeating glory kills (look amazing for the first few hours, usually exciting to try a glory kill on a new enemy) it can get a little tiring and repetitive, I myself find it a chore to play at the moment and probably wont be finishing it as it has gotten severely boring. I also played on nightmare (cant change difficulty once you start) and the game can be very difficult at times so i often die a few times per locked room but it isnt too terrible, it does get annoying in the more difficult areas sometimes. Overall the 13 hours i did play were very enjoyable but it just got tiring. If you can pick it up for ~40% off or so it is a solid pickup if you enjoy the doom games, or if what i described seems enjoyable to you, but for full price I cant see a reason to recommend it. It isnt a bad game but as far as content and level variety it is surely lacking\t\t\t\t\t
