\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDOOM 2016 is easily one of my favorite games ever:

The graphics are stylish and beautifully rich with detail. While it might be a little underwhelming in the endgame it's still really gory, disgusting and hellish looking in all the right ways.
9,5/10

I have an average PC at best and even i can run this game at really high settings and still get more than 60 fps constantly.
10/10

It doesn't have the deepest nor the most original story but for what it is, the story has alot of detail put into everthing and doesn't bother you during gameplay.
8/10

Do you like the original Doom Games? Do you like Quake or Unreal Tournament? Would you love there to be a combination of both of these styles of games? If you do, than you'll love this game.
It's quick and reaction based gameplay including recource management, dodging projectiles and having to always move around. It goes against every AAA Shooter in the last decade and i love it, it's so refreshing.
10/10

You know what i didn't expect would be included in a Doom game? RPG like elements, upgrading your Weapons and Armor, ...
Does it fit to the game though? Yes, surprisingly well. The way you become stronger over the course of the campaign through improving and upgrading your suit's abilities, completing Rune trials, finding Weapon Mods etc. It makes it much easier for you to complete the game, but you can do it without the upgrades, if you choose to do so.
10/10

The new designs for some Demons are wonderfully original (like the Cyberdemon), whilst the Throwback design of others is a nice bit of fanservice (like Barons of Hell). My favourite might be the Hellknight, who looks like it came straight out of Doom 3, showing that ID atleast ackknowledges Doom 3. Plus is gives the HN a distinct look to make him stand out against the Barons.
10/10

The Soundtrack, IS THE MOST METAL VIDEO GAME SOUNDTRACK IV'E EVER HEARD IN MY LIFE! On top of the quality of the music, it's also synced up with your actions ingame, meaning it never sounds quite the same.
The sound effects are top notch aswell. The gory and weird noises Demons make, the power you feel everytime you use one of the Guns, it's just perfect audio design.
666/10

This is easily one of my favorite Singleplayer Campaigns ever!
10/10



There might be a few problems with the Multiplayer and i might not like it as much, but it's still a great time. If you like it, great for you.
8/10



Snap Map is what most games should have these days. This is pretty detailed and deep Mapmaking tool that ensures this game stays relevant for a lot longer. Again i'm not massively into these Mapmakers, but i can see potential in it. I still would have prefered true Modding, as that's what DOOM is known for, but Snap Map is a good comprimise.
9/10


Overall this game is one of my favourite games ever, awsome gameplay, amazing graphics, a brutal Soundtrack by Mick Gordon and overall a HELLISH good time and a must play for fans of fast-paced Shooters.
666/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"This game isn't 1993's original Doom"

Well, no, it's not and it was never supposed to be. 2016's Doom is what i think the original Doom would have looked like if it was first released in this age.

Doom 1's gameplay allows the player to completely speedrun levels like a madman if you are skilled enough and this also allows the player to skip most demons and parts of the map which makes the gameplay feel fast and adrenaline-fueled. But 2016's Doom is not Doom 1, Doom 2016 is it's own installment that despite having to live up to it's predecessor it still does a great work at holding the name DOOM. Some parts of the game require you to fight against waves of demons after activating action, concept which, to be fair, repeats itself a tiny bit too much throughout the game.

Another topic that Doom 2016 can be criticized for is it's multiple new features such as Argent Cells: cells which power your HP/Armor/Ammo up permanently; Runes: perks that allow you to increase your overall survival and/or combat mechanic; Weapon mods: mods that allow you to have the upper hand in certain cases (Example: the Plasma Rifle's stun bomb that gives the player a breather or the Chain Gun's Micro Missiles that allow you to burst down a beefy demon in just a couple of seconds which can serve as a panic button). These features can be seen as almost disgraceful to the original Doom by some fans because the original didn't have all those features, but is it truly disgraceful? In my opinion, no. Yes it may seem a bit like CoD sometimes alongside with the point mentioned in the first paragraph but, as i mentioned previously, Doom 2016 is NOT Doom 1993. It does a good job in keeping the old school adrenaline-fueled demon-killing-spree you feel in the original but it also does it's job at "being modern" with all these new features.

The movement feels smooth and so do the weapons. The large variety of demons keep the gameplay interesting and forces you to be cautious while also being on the move constantly (Specially against the Imps and their tricky fireballs).

10/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShoot an imp, fill a flying demon with lead, launch rockets into a creature from Hell, and dash towards him to tear off its horn and stab it in its neck. WOW, that felt good!
That’s DOOM.

This game’s cool, it lets you go berserk and ravage every creature you find without reason. Oh, what? There is a story, a lore and a lot of backstory? I so don’t care! Let me kill demons, I don’t need no reason!

The main interesting mechanic from DOOM, as stated by Mark Brown (GMTK), is the fact that you can recover energy by finishing enemies at close-range with your fists when they’re low on health. First, it feels really good, there’s so much juice in each animation it’s really enjoyable to do it even after hundreds of these “glory kills”. But the main point is to make the player always in the center of the action, never sniping from far away, but instead go all in, especially when at low health. High risk, high reward.

The worst thing in this game is the sound design. Enemies that are below you appear to be right next to you. Sometimes I could hear enemies all around me, but when I looked, noone was there. They were all in another room below or above me. This is so bad to hear this in a game from 2016. While talking about the sound design, the metal-electro-indus music will engage you into the fight when there are enemies… but won’t stop until you find the last enemy in the zone, which is sometimes hidden or far away. So you run around, without anyone in view, but still listening to an hardcore music. Disturbing.

While playing, you’ll have a lot of possible challenges to complete, like killing 4 demons in a single gatling charge, or kill a big monster only with your sidearm. They change the way you play, making you try new weapons, new tactics, new ways to slaughter. But there may be too many challenges, and I found myself distracted a lot by these (because I wanted them all), which broke the flow. But when finally I unlocked all challenges, I was free to play the game any way I wanted, and I enjoyed it again at full potential, going as fast as possible, using the powerful arsenal given to me.

The game also has a lot of secret areas, so if you want to find them all, you can’t just go from a killing spree into another, but you have to carefully inspect each room, and use the map in the pause menu to detect secret zones, which also break the flow. At first, I finished the levels normally, then went back to find the secrets. I really appreciated that I could do that. But at a certain point in the game, I wasn’t able to backtrack anymore, so I had to stop this technique and examine the rooms after each massacre, breaking the flow each time. Too bad they didn’t design the later levels like the first ones.\t\t\t\t\t
