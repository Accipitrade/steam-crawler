\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEng: It's a nice "Crazy Taxi" inspired game, with the twist of driving a flying car. The controls are not that bad, it takes some practice, the music is very good (although not that many songs) and overall a very fun experience.
The problems are: Tutorial is painfully slow, no options for controls (very few options in general), many bugs with collisions and minimap, and each game gets stale after some time because the map is a little bit repetitive. Also the broken glass effect looks really bad.
It needs some work but it can become a great game with some patches.

Spa: Es un buen juego inspirado en Crazy Taxi, con el giro de manejar un auto volador. Los controles no están tan mal, solo se necesita práctica, la música es bastante buena (aunque no hay tantas canciones) y es sobre todo una muy divertida experiencia.
Los problemas son: Tutorial DEMASIADO lento, no opciones de controles (muy pocas opciones en general), muchos bugs de colisiones y del mini mapa, y cada partida se vuelve monótona con el tiempo, ya que el mapa es un poco repetitivo. Por cierto, el efecto de vidrio roto se ve horrible.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is pretty damn good. Expect Crazy Taxi arcade like time and fun. For a day one purchase it needs some polish. Id be upset if this was the final product, but normally in this day in age games get updated and maintained. The core game play idea is solid, it just needs more time to bake in the oven for polish.

What it does great at
- The game looks amazing
- The gameplay is solid, the core idea of the game to me lives up to the arcade experience and the legacy of Crazy Taxi
- Movie references galore!
- Great game to pick up and play, not a lot strat, straight to the game play fun after your initial first run through.

What it needs help
- More polish to the game
- Change in music or a radio function from youtube music or spotify
- The Camera angle and car orientation changes when the rider jumps in is disorienting when it changes back. You think you were facing one way when the rider was getting in, then when it changes back your back to your original position.
- Able to boost vertically or dive bomb down. Or a way to do 180s in combination with going in different altitudes.
- Nav arrow needs a better way to tell you if the drop off point is high or lower then were your car
- Voice variety. Fun impressions, just needs a little bit more. Or an add on tool to let community to add their own?

I'll update my review down the line, as of the first 24 hours and still playing it.

All in all, worth the price of purchase as long as it stays alive and updates.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI’d just like to acknowledge first that this was made by one person - so despite my review, this is a great achievement of a game for a solo dev.

This is more of a MIXED review than a positive review, but since there is no mixed option and although I don’t feel this game is quite up to scratch yet, I will leave it as recommended since it still delivers on what it promises and can have its fun moments, although it’s price tag may exceed its content value at the time being.

The pros:

-Good looking visuals and sense of life. The rendering of the game looks quite nice in the Unity Engine, and the overall futuristic aesthetic is vibrant and eye-catching, with dozens of billboards, streams of traffic and little pockets of life.

-Voice acting from customers is fun and entertaining and makes the city feel bustling and full, despite some repetitions in lines. It adds to the retro aesthetic from its predecessor, Crazy Taxi.

-The delivery gameplay. The actual gameplay loop of picking up passengers and delivering them to their (often rudely) requested locations is spot on. It works just like Crazy Taxi, with the additional verticality. Although it can be a bit daunting at first figuring out where exactly you need to go, but you’ll quickly find your way through the skyline.


The cons:

-Lack of audio settings. The game offers no way of mixing audio levels, or On/Off buttons for music and sound effects.

-The reversing and elevation shifting are slow. When I find myself at the wrong level or just a bit off, awkwardly trying to turn around with the clunky reverse and slow elevation shifting really takes you out of the fast paced rhythm of the game. Reversing should allow for quick sharp turns, and being able to elevate or drop at quick speeds seems vital in a game that relies on verticality and a fast paced tempo.

-City repetition. Although this is a minor note, it would be nice to see more variation to the overall city as I find myself getting lost in the overall blend.

Things that would be nice to have:

-Drifting or rolling. Currently making sharp turns in Mile High Taxi is not possible, and feels like such a maneuver could save a lot of in-game countdown time.

-Traffic collision. I will say that currently there is already a lot to bump into in this game, so I could see why the developer may have chosen to leave this out, but it would be a cool challenge if traffic collision was an in-game option.

-Some sort of ranking system, like a D-S class rank, or a level up bar.

Overall this game has a lot of potential, but right as this moment feels like it’s lacking in a few departments and could do with a bit of a beef up before I completely recommend it. I’d recommend buying this one on sale with where it is at at the moment. Looking forward to see what the developer brings next.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOpen world Cab Racing games are probably one of the most forgotten and rare genres in gaming. So few, that the latest big hit was 2003 Simpsons Road Rage for the PS2, Gamecube and OG Xbox. After that, we would only get Taxi Chaos in 2021, but that was not the break out hit we expected.

Mile High Taxi, however, is a great step on the right direction: With an incredible setting, a great choice of soundtrack (very important for this genre) and tons of references to 90s sci-fi, Mile High Taxi oozes with personality and give you plenty reason to explore it in roam free mode.

But the controls are not there yet. They feel great sometimes, others don’t. Repositioning yourself on a 3D environment is tricky but rewarding, however you will lose some time when you realize you are a couple of floors right ABOVE your target, and there is no easy way to simply drop on it.

Even if I’m still hoping for the controls to improve in the future, I definitely recommend this game and if you are a fan of crazy taxi this is a MUST BUY, not just because its very rare for we to get a new game on the genre, but it’s also a great and fun twist on the formula.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t3/13/23
FIRST IMPRESSIONS REVIEW

-From the elevator entrance to the tutorial, the Voice Acting makes me smile a LOT. Audio style is pretty much a perfect nod.

-Art style is really almost perfect. Like it's almost there but instead of triggering nostalgia, some things look kind of cheap, maybe some of the stuff like main menu and whatnot could be improved upon, Once specific example that's jarring to me is the main menu with the two people- the way that they have bold black borders around their outlines is somehow just wrong. I don't want to come off super critical because i can't do any better myself- like I can see you were going for the homage to original Crazy Taxi and it's hard to say what's "wrong", or broken or what would fix it, i think it just comes down to my own preferences there.


I think there's a lot of potential here, and I hope you keep working on polishing it. it's a really good start. If you had priced it higher, i would be rating it lower, but I think $16 is a decent price point for what you're offering to start with.

Especially, well done if you're one guy doing this, it was a lot of work, and it shows.

EDIT: So it seems like I'm having some bug problems related to the tutorial and that not working right made up the majority of my negative points in here. I've removed them to reflect that it was on me, not on the game. The dev got back to me about it and will be looking into it. He seems like a pretty good guy.\t\t\t\t\t
