\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn short, it's exactly what it says on the tin - a quick-paced, fun and often nostalgic ride that reminds me of the good old days of arcade gaming. Nothing complicated. If you're into that sort of thing, you should definitely check it out.

Controls are pretty straightforward but there's a bunch of special moves that have been challenging and fun to master. I use maybe half of them. The repetitive nature of the game is a strength and I'm finding it easy to get sucked into the rhythm of picking up and dropping off passengers. Also there's some great fitting music too. Repetitive music and play make it awesomely hypnotic when I'm in the zone.

I like games I can play in short bursts and don't have to commit hours at a time, so this fits that bill. There's also a certain joy in just driving around the city at my own pace [roam mode] without a specific objective. But in the normal modes, it's fun to try and beat my previous high fare scores. Speaking of which, one thing I'd like to see added is the leaderboard. I read the dev is adding it but it wasn't there at the time of this review. It does require a controller but I'm totally fine with that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been having a lot of fun playing Mile High Taxi since mastering the controls. What was initially a visually-compelling addition to the taxi racing genre, I now consider a step forward towards a bright future for those of us that fell in love with Crazy Taxi.

Compared to the other recent iteration of a game in this niche genre, Taxi Chaos, Mile High Taxi offers something unique (and far better IMO) by building on the original concept with a futuristic vertical city and a flying car that's designed especially to navigate the Fifth Element-inspired landscape.

To get the most out of this game, I highly recommend players to do their best at mastering the controls (specifically the special moves) in Free Roam and then go Achievement Hunting in arcade mode. I'd also suggest players spend some time familiarizing themselves with the in-game map - while it is a little challenging to get used to at first, it's surprisingly intuitive after a while.

Like with any game in this somewhat constrained genre, you can get bored easily by repeatedly trying to beat the number of passengers dropped off. I think this is where some additional game modes could really help, and partly the reason I've taken to speedrunning $1000 in fares.

It's also worth noting for would-be buyers of this game, this is an investment. The solo developer has been very receptive to the community's feedback so far and has hinted at some of the changes and adaptions to the game we can expect in future patches.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot fun yet. Has potential to be, but needs more work.

Moving up and down is a pain instead of the fun unique feature it should be. You can't turn (or even look) all the way up or down, best you can do is tilt 45 degrees or do a painfully slow "strafe", so it's usually just a time-sapping frustration when you reach the destination then have to slowly and awkwardly spiral without even being able to see what you're aiming at. Please, add some proper flight-like controls: ideally bank, roll, spin, end up upside-down... at bare minimum, at least let us actually point 90 degrees up or down. It looks like 180 turns were added recently so maybe there is hope? The "jump up 10 floors" thing should address this problem but does't really work, it doesn't always happen, feels unnatural and you still can't see where you're going. How about making the unused "east" button turn off the car's auto stabilisation, allowing unlimited up/down steering including vertical handbrake turns, then if we end up upside down and disoriented, turning it back on rights everything? And please add an option to use the right stick to look around so if we're above or below a target, we can swivel the camera and see it.

The main race-the-clock mechanic manages to recreate the worst feature of arcade games - the sudden stop, frustrating the player into putting in more coins - but we can't even do that, it's just bam, game over, restart or quit. Please, rethink this - if you're committed to recreating the arcade experience including its worst aspects at least add a game mode that doesn't just end, like maybe "your shift's over" then your earned money starts draining until you get back to a taxi point, then you bank whatever's left, see how your passengers/money per shift compares to your record then go again, adding to you bank (or getting in debt if too slow getting home)? Right now the game end is just so unsatisfying, and it doesn't need to be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWho are these critics praising this game? "The best Crazy Taxi clone I've ever played" and "Love the vibe!". These statements are just not credible for this game. This is, quite bluntly, some of the worst rubbish I've purchased in a long time.

It is rudimentary in every way. Not even so much as an audio volume control in the game settings and it's stupidly loud. I'm not adjusting global volumes just to play this game (I don't even use a volume control on my panel because those are "set and forget" settings to me.

The sounds are an aggravating jumble, such that I can't even make out what my car's computer is saying over the music and passenger admonishments etc. The last few attempts to play this have been with my audio muted to cut down on the annoyance.

The game requires a controller (as stated, but usually there are still keyboard mappings so you can at least see the game, if not playing it properly). I can use it, but I don't like game controllers and if I absolutely have to plug one in to play a game for a short time, I'm probably not going to launch that game very often, if at all after the novelty wears off.

The controls are in no way customizable and there isn't even so much as a button map to look at. I had to play the tutorial to see if I was missing anything (I wasn't, it's just that rudimentary) where he said confusing things like "use your right trigger to accelerate and the south button to brake". Do I need a compass? It took me a few seconds to realize that he meant "left" as in "southpaw".

There is no reason why this game couldn't have keyboard mappings, it's not as if the controls are complex. It's sheer laziness.

The game lacks polish in other ways, too. For one example, my language somehow got changed to Chinese in game and I couldn't read the menus anymore. Some fumbling button press? Being rudimentary, it doesn't seem to store that setting and prompts for language at game load so I just had to quit out, though :-)

There is not much to this game, it's just feels like a bit of an arcade game using the Unity engine. The "Free Roam" flashes "Restricted Area" on my screen and hobbles my altitude controls every time I turn off the main path to explore the city. What is "Free Roam" that doesn't let you roam? You have to stick to whatever paths are legitimate I guess. I thought I'd do that to get used to the car, but there is nothing to get used to. It goes about as fast as drying paint, relative to the game, even with a boost pickup and while the car is easy to control in flight, the limited controls make it ridiculous to drop altitude and coast in for a pickup. That's not fun, and I don't want to practice that arbitrary stupidity.

Even following the game's rules, don't turn off, stick to the limiting altitude range, try to follow the waypoint before the timer runs out, it's boring. Not a very interesting city, it's mostly just visually the same everywhere you go to the point that I wouldn't be able to discern where I am in the city. I see the same Toronto street names on the blue signs, they aren't much help. The same billboards come up in multiple places too so they aren't much navigation help. The behaviour of the waypoint arrow is terrible, shrinking, pointing up at odd angles when inappropriate, turning colours that are then hard to see against buildings etc. The actual gameplay proper isn't fun either. I pick up and drop off two passengers in the 120s time limit and I'm "fired"?

The dispatcher and the passengers are obnoxious and insulting... I want to eject them at altitude.

I'm not laughing... I'm going to come right out and say that Valve should not allow rubbish like this to be promoted and sold on their service because it will turn people off on buying games. That's just enough, I don't have any more time to spend on this.\t\t\t\t\t
