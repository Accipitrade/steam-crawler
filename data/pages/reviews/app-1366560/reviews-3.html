Crazy Taxi it is not, but at least its better than Taxi Chaos. Unfortunately, it's still not fun

It seems that, for the second time, another game has proven just how much of a balancing act Crazy Taxi is to be as fun as it is... and how hard it is to replicate. Mile High Taxi isn't bad but it's just... severely mediocre. And I hate to put down an indie developer's first attempt at not just a Crazy Taxi like game but their first attempt at a game in general, but this game is honestly insanely boring and I simply can't find a reason to justify my purchase.

Let's start with the good; there's a decent selection of music, a good sense of speed, and the controls do feel on par with Crazy Taxi in their tightness and and refinement. There's a ton of character in terms of the NPC interactions, dialogue, memes and pop culture references all around the world.

As for the criticisms... there's a lot to talk about there.

Graphics are mediocre, which is to be expected from a small project like this, but it's ugly. Like, really ugly. The graphics in the trailers and pictures do not look like that. There's horrible jagged edges everywhere even with AA turned on, there's no graphics option other than post-processing, there's a horrible green glow to things, and it seems like anything not directly in front of you is rendered at half the resolution of your monitor.

Graphics aren't really that important, but how about the gameplay? Well... while the controls are good, this spin on taking Crazy Taxi into the sky is really not utilized in any meaningful way. Sure, the car can go up and down and all around but the city layout is just a basic grid, so every fare ends up feeling the same. There's no interesting locations, cool shortcuts, unique layouts, nothing. I feel the developer was limited on resources, which is why the city looks so samey, and was really hoping the fact that you can go up and down would be enough to carry the game. But it isn't, especially since that's really the only thing you can do outside of accelerating and braking. Part of what made Crazy Taxi's driving so fun the gear shifting mechanic that allowed you to do insane launches and crazy drifts. There's nothing like that in this game or its own unique spin. It becomes mundane, repetitive, and boring really quickly especially because the game is so easy.

One of the most jarring things, personally, is the fact that none of the sky traffic has collision. What could have added an extra layer in complexity with needing to dodge traffic is completely absent because you just clip right through cars. Every drive feels like a casual Sunday drive around the park. The only obstacles in your way is the city, it's infrastructure, and signs that are more spectacle than designed to challenge the player in any meaningful way.

Your car's orientation is never how you parked it so transitions between the scripted sequence of passengers jumping in your car and gameplay are a bit disorientating. How far you stop from the pedestrian isn't a factor either, which was another cool feature of Crazy Taxi - you were rewarded for parking closely by cutting the distance the ped had to travel to get into your vehicle by saving you crucial seconds. That's not the case here. Everyone has a pre-determined path and the timer is very forgiving towards that.

The most obnoxious thing in this game is the limitation of the world. You can only go up and down a certain number of "levels" and that leniency is very, very small. If you go beyond the limit the AI in the car will start flashing and complaining that you need to ascend or descend or it's going to shut the car off on you or something to that affect. The problem is, some of the fares are actually within that limit. So the car will yell at you, while the dispatcher reminds you from the umpteenth that there's boost for you to pick up, and the passengers say one of the same 4 regurgitated lines. It drives you f*cking mad.

Speaking of the passengers, there's no coherency in how well you're actually performing. You can make it to the destination flawlessly and quickly and the passenger will get insult you. Then you can pick up another passenger, hot everything on the way to their destination, and they'll sing your praises. There's no indicator telling you that you're doing well such as how Crazy Taxi had a separate timer for the pedestrian that was color coded; if you delivered them while the timer was still green you got a performance (time/money) bonus. If you delivered them on yellow that was standard performance (time extension only), and red was poor performance that rewarded you no time bonus. But here it seems like they just have a few lines of recycled dialogue they just say over and over.

Lastly, the worst thing by far, is the graphic effect they decided to use for damage. As far as I can tell, there's no actual damage that can be inflicted on the vehicle. Rather, it's just a visual effect on the player camera of a cracked lens. It's a neat effect, and the developer is clearly proud of it because it even reacts to lighting in the map. Unfortunately, the effect can actually get to a point where it clutters up the entire screen if you crash enough and it becomes near impossible to see where you're going. Couple that with the annoying tidbits I mentioned earlier, the poor visuals, and the boring gameplay and this game is simply just not worth your time even for it's low price point.

Additionally, and this is just a nitpick as I'm not one to complain about culture war garbage, but the Skyler character is just an offensively bad gay stereotype which absolutely sucks because he has the nicest car of the roster.

Overall, you're honestly better off spending an additional $13 on a copy of Crazy Taxi 3 for the Xbox off eBay (or even emulating it) as that has both of Crazy Taxi 1 and 2's maps along with the 3rd map that game introduced and all of its features.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to enjoy it. Conceptually it's enough to make me want to own it but the price does not match the gameplay value.

Given the attempts of recreating the scenes & vibes of Fifth element + all-time classic arcade game Crazy Taxi, I can't say that there wasn't effort put into this game but i can say not enough time was put in, at least with respect to the gameplay mechanics. There was some understandable, polishable stuff like no keyboard support (gamepad only) & more control styles but then there's the big holes like no ability to go manually upwards or downwards or even have an impactful crash with other flying cars in what should feel like an interesting world. There's just so much potential in it's verticality in which the player cannot engage.

For 5 bucks this would be a cheap fun romp but at the launch price you would do much better to just play Crazy Taxi while simultaneously watching Fifth Element on another screen. Refunding, unfortunately.\t\t\t\t\t
