\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSpoilers to follow.

OK, I got this game for cheap on sale, and I didn't really know much about it, but I am still pretty disappointed.
At the start, I thought it would be one of those basic "trapped in a house, can't open some doors yet" short horror games, but later on it tries to be a souls-like?

The controls are clunky, and many mechanics aren't explained. There seem to be various enemies with their own triggers and workarounds, but it's really unclear what those might be most of the time, because its really hard to see anything unless you have a candle or cross active, but switching on the cross makes your candle go out, and you have to relight it at a torch, but the cross drains your "cross meter" while it's active, which may be the same as your life meter, but maybe not....UGH!

If you get killed in a dungeon, you have to recover your body, or you lose all of your souls. I died without recovering my body and lost almost 10000 souls, which I may need to either complete the main game or simply unlock special areas, I don't know.

It's pretty hard to avoid dying if you get close to an enemy. I figured out that one of them won't move if you look at him (a la Weeping Angel/SCP-173), and one hops slowly, so you can avoid her, but that's about all I figured out. Maybe the cross or candle ward against certain enemies? Maybe they attract those enemies? Who knows.

Also, the visuals are repetitive and the sound effects are often incongruous. I also had no idea how they determined where to put the checkpoints and various acts.

Lastly, if you have OCD, you are going to spend a LOT of time opening drawers in this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk visuals.
Really bad sounds (get a photo on a table you will see how it sounds like you pulled a brick on the asphalt).
Character is slower than a turtle when walking but moving his head is so fast when looking around that it provoke motion sickness... I played with a controller since it is mentioned fully supported.
It seems you can arrange that from the menu, but the menu is not accessible from the game (or I did not find how?) even by pressing the esc key (full controller support does not permit to do what esc key does...). So, you need to quit the game using esc, adjust your controls and restart the game from a check point... annoying... especially when you need to repeat the operation to really adjust the controls the way it is fine for you...
Atmosphere in the house is ok and some of the things you can read here and there in books feels promising.
However, most happen in dungeons and when the jump scares chain is ending, you are rushed by some monsters it seems you cannot fight... The only crucifix I have was doing nothing. Even not slowing the bully creature. So you die and start again in the first room of a dungeon. Goal seems to do a maximum of things, exploring and looting objects while the monster is not around and do not see you. When it does, then it is over, you will not really escape.
I also do not get why, from a haunted house, I am projected into dungeons. All should happen in the house, garden, streets... following the stories you can read by hanging around the different rooms.
I know dungeons is in the title but then, why starting in a haunted house with a very ghost like atmosphere and which is promising something else,,,
The house is the best part. It is more puzzle oriented with some nice events. But unfortunately, you don't stay in there...

If controls were better, movements more fluent, I would have given a longer try. But in the current state, it is more tiring than scary.
I am going to wait for the final release which is announced to be this year 2021. Then I'll give another try and see if things were improved in between. I will also perhaps update my reviews if it changes for the better,
In the current state, I can only recommend to, at least, wait for the end of the early.\t\t\t\t\t
