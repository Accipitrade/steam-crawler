I had been dying for a good dungeon crawler for a while now. When I saw this one coming out in the upcoming games list, it was an instant wishlist and purchase as soon as it dropped. Well, this isn't exactly what I expected, but man do I not regret my decision.

Part 1 (puzzle footage)
https://youtu.be/-kdDaabtw-k
Ergastulum is an odd style game in that it seems to tread between an adventure puzzle game a la Myst, then thrust you into a survival horror style dungeon crawler where you need to acquire souls and survive the nightmarish landscapes in a very Dark Souls manner. The Christian overtones laced with lucid dreaming and mythology leads to a very jarring experience in all the right ways.

As it stands I have only finished the first act, but the dungeon area there alone had me on edge the whole time. The idea of being helpless with only a crucifix and a candle to light your way makes most decisions vastly important in your quest to find souls and advance the story. It also led to one of the incredibly rare times a jump scare in a game genuinely made me jump so abruptly it gave me a headache (granted it was partly my fault I got startled but it still got me).

Ergastulum is kind of hard to explain. It blends two different atmospheres together incredibly well, creating one of the most unique games I've played in a long while. Yes, it's early access and has some problems. Characters clip through the wall, effects disappear based on where you look, and I got stuck at one point unable to move until I closed the door I opened that locked me in place. But the overall product (from what I've played so far) has been immensely enjoyable and I cannot recomend checking this one out enough.

NOTE - the link/video above is only the first half of act one, which is the puzzle aspect in the house. The next video will show off the first dungeon. I am including a link below to the Twitch vod that has both parts of act one unedited.
