\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you enjoyed the ps2 release, you'll enjoy this remaster. That being said, you'd be pressed to spend the asking price for this title - 30 dollars for an hour's worth of content? This game has as much content as the original, (eight stages with two difficulties). Online leaderboards are a welcome addition but that aside the game will not hold the attention of newcomers for very long.

The gameplay itself is satisfying and almost addicting, though the updated visuals and music aren't very memorable. Even simple options like the ability to toggle between the old and new soundtracks are absent from this game.

If you're morbidly curious about this game, the ps2 release goes for dirt cheap on eBay. Those who have played the original and want to try this version, maybe wait for a steep price drop.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSteam, I still need that neutral/informational review button. Please.

tl;dr "should i get this game"
if you're a fan of original: stick to the original, wait for a patch on this version
if you're new to fantavision: this version is probably fine? maybe?? id still say the original is better though...

Anyway, Fantavision 202X. It's Fantavision, but not quite how you may know it. The base gameplay is exactly the same, sure, but things are...different.

1) the game opts for a stock photo/video based look for its menus and cutscenes rather than the specially filmed retro-futuristic ones of the original; personally, I think it fits the vibe and mood just as well, but you may not agree.
2) the music wont be what you expect either; it's a re-arranged soundtrack based off the ost of the original Japanese version of the game. Still by Soichi Terada, mind you, so it's still good. But it ain't gonna be the one you remember.
3) Starmine mode currently doesn't function remotely the same way as it does in the original PS2 versions, for whatever reason. Shells don't properly get chain-exploded, the way multiple colours come out is very cleanly separated instead of mixed together, etc... Also its possible to select a shell and cause it to never get chain exploded, which is SUPER not how it's meant to work. See also: breaking chains during Starmine. ain't supposed to happen unless you mess up really bad in it, but here, it can just happen randomly as you're playing it properly.
4) the difficulty feels way higher here than it does in the original versions. new rounds of firework shells fly out rapidly but expire just as fast. weirdly, it spews out way more bonus items than the original versions too, which doesn't remotely help the difficulty outside of perhaps regaining energy which now depletes way quicker than it probably should. the higher speed of both firing and expiration makes it obscenely difficult to even attempt getting a 4 daisy chain.
5) it's been mentioned in other reviews, but you can't exit the game normally through the main menu button that is labelled "exit". it's alt+f4 or nothing, currently.
6) also noted weird control issues where pressing the explode button wont actually explode the shells you've selected, only doing so when you press a second time. no clue what that's about but it made things a bit frustrating to deal with.

Cosmo Machia does seem to be checking the forums every so often and occasionally responding so there is definitely a chance they'll see this and or my thread on the forums so hopefully a patch will be released that can fix these issues. They're only a small team however and this was clearly made on a budget lower than even the original game so I can definitely forgive them for that. I'm super glad they brought Fantavision back as I adore it so much. But currently I'm not sure I can happily recommend this version in its current form.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played the heck out of Fantavision when it launched on the ps2, and I have played it emulated many times over the years.
It's a chill puzzler game about chaining together explosions of like colors. It takes a few tries before the logic clicks, but it's a very zen game once you get the hang of it.

I'm reviewing the 1.002 version that just launched. This is an Unreal 4.27 recreation of the original game. The music and kitschy videos from the original game have been lost to copyright issues, but the core game is here.

As the game is right now, it's very rough around the edges.

The good: This plays near perfectly. The original was a ps2 launch with severe interlacing issues, and sometimes the visuals would lag the whole system making the music skip. Even emulated it's a struggle to find a balance between HD visuals and a lag free game.

Fantavision 202x at 165fps feels wonderful. It's so fluid and it plays just right. Everything snaps like it should. The music and announcer are good enough.

The bad: there are no config options at all and by default the game runs with an uncapped framerate with no vsync or resolution options.

But, it's an Unreal game so, if you open up your %LOCALAPPDATA% browse out to \\fv\\Saved\\Config\\WindowsNoEditor and open up GameUserSettings.ini you can edit these lines:

[/script/engine.gameusersettings]
bUseVSync=False
FrameRateLimit=0.000000
DesiredScreenWidth=1280
DesiredScreenHeight=720

I set the values to match my monitor, and then it with a proper vysnc. The anti-aliasing is still a little ugly.


I would recommend the game if you loved the original and have muscle memory for it.

If you've never played the original, this version is still a little unpolished. The original had a very cohesive kitschy design of retro-futurism, and this has that, but a bunch of stock photos over the parts that had to be removed for legal reasons. There's no option to exit the game, it's just assumed you know Alt+F4 exits a program.

Still, I'm very happy that someone took the time to go back and make one of my favorite puzzlers playable on modern hardware.
I want to support this dev just for the time they took to bring back a hidden gem. I hope they keep updating the game.
(Now can we get someone to port Sky Gunner to modern hardware? please please?)\t\t\t\t\t

I can say this does work on the Steam Deck, but only with GE-Proton, because this uses video files that don't work with regular Proton!
