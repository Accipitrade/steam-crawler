\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy time with this game has been great so far, though It does not run the level editor well, which is kind of disappointing.
Maybe its my Pc being garbage, or it could very well be the game's fault. I will have to look further into it.

Pros:
1. It's a fun, well made platform fighter, with tons of replay value, and a good starting roster. (I love Granite :) )
2. Feels fluid and fast, without being over cumbering and braindead, unlike NASB.
3. Level editor is packed to the brim with fluid and cool things to tweak to your character.
Cons:
Game is somewhat buggy, (As to be expected)
No online, at the moment.
While great so far, I do think that the Character Creator could be tweaked to run better.
Also this game is 20 bones, for an unfinished game.

I would recommend this game, especially if you have Parsec, which allows you to play online with friends. Maybe if you don't feel like paying 20 bucks for it right now, you can wait till the game releases.
But even with it's flaws, It's still a great time, with friends, or by yourself. I will be spending a lot of time in the character editor, that's for sure.

This is worthy enough to be called "Super Smash Maker" in my book.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSmack Studio is a platform fighter with very smooth mechanics that feel almost like Super Smash Melee. Compared to the other platform fighters available on steam Smack Studio definitely has the best feel and roster of original characters.

Been following this game since the alpha and the largest improvement has been the visuals. The game looks as good as it feels with a cool mixture of sprite art and 3D modeling.

But perhaps the coolest feature is the character creator. I have no experience with animation and I was still able to put together a character in around 4 hours. They make it very easy to manipulate sprites so that you can get a fluid animation and spend more time trying to come up with cool character ideas and less time in the nitty gritty of the sprites (unless you want to).

Overall, Smack Studio is both an amazing fighting game and an amazing tool for animation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSmack Studio has come a super long way from its humble origins. The hook of creating your own character sounds too good to be true, but it works as advertised. What's fantastic is the easy with which you can jump in and make fully functioning characters, both with preexisting sprite sheet assets or custom creating your own.

The surprising thing is most of the time, games that tend to be intuitive and simple in the way they manage user created content suffer from simplistic choice syndrome; i.e. You can make his special attack a fireball, a punch, or a spin attack! This is not the case here. In Smack Studio's character creator, you can start extremely simple and go as complicated as you need, creating intricate interactions easily. It's hard to stress how well this creator balances depth and simplicity, giving anyone the access even without the deep knowledge, but also providing toolsets for users to go deeper into complicated interactions and commands.

All this doesn't even touch on the gampelay, which feels extremely satisfying and fun. While at the time of this review there's no official online multiplayer available, even playing over Parsec feels extremely fun and addicting. The core mechanics feel as good as Super Smash Bros Melee, Rivals of Aether, or Nick All-Stars Brawl (all, in my opinion, excellent platform fighting games with variances in control). Smack Studio fits alongside them in snappy, responsive, and satisfying controls.

The promise of a stage creator, and the vast multitudes of characters on the workshop *already* proves this game will have replay value far outlasting the cost of the game. I unconditionally endorse this game!\t\t\t\t\t
