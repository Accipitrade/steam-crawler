\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 53 hours of enjoyment, I am forced to stop playing the game due to being completely unable to progress past a particular boss, due to how this big major battle works.

The reason for this is one of three major flaws with the game that I can't ignore.

That aside, though, I'll still write out this review.



The Great Gaias (TGG) offers everything a person could want in an RPG of its like:

* Numerous party members
* Exploration
* Items hid everywhere
* Ability to acquire stronger items if you risk your life to get them
* You can steal stuff from enemies
* Out-of-combat skills like mining, trap detection/disabling, and lockpicking
* Sometimes-unfair combat if you aren't prepared or equipped properly

And the story itself is actually quite good! You can really tell the developers put tons of work in to this. It's not just another copypasted RPGMaker game using the same thing the majority of those $15 ones do.

This game is -well- worth the price.

PROS:

* Tons of gameplay here. I've put 53 hours in to it, but there are others with well over 80. (One guy has 180+ hours!)

* Open exploration. You can move ahead to areas with stronger creatures.. and if you survive, may acquire components and such earlier than intended for use in crafting.

* Speaking of crafting: you can craft accessories (rings/necklaces/bracelets, and leather/cloth accessory items), as well as upgrade relic and legenary items you find. Old armor and weapons that you have can, at a certain point, be broken down in to base materials as well.

* Alchemy: make your own potions and such! (You can collect herbs as well!)

* Combat is turn-based, but solid. Unfair at times, though.

* Story is pretty good.

* Devs are quick about fixing bugs that the community has found, even small ones like random encounters happening too often on a map with a puzzle on it.

* Item stores sell Repellants, so you can turn off random battles for a bit if you like. Later on you can get an equipppable item that turns them off indefinitely, too! (They get annoying sometimes.)


CONS:

There's only three major flaws with the game that I'll go over here.

1. Spelling/grammar mistakes.. they are everywhere. Mostly grammar mistakes, such as the misuse of possessive nounds and apostrophes in general. Normally I wouldn't complain about something like this, but from start to finish.. get ready to see a lot of this.

It's so frequent that, to me, it takes away from the narrative of the story as a whole.

2. Buffs and debuffs. Player characters gain skills which can debuff their targets.. lower their dexterity (speed), strength, magic, etc. Not only do these skills have a higher chance to fail than basic attacks, but apparently the debuffs themselves only have a chance to be applied if the skill hits.

This becomes much, much, much more annoying later on.. because these buffs, and debuffs, are monumental in combat and can make or break the outcome of a fight easily.

Thankfully there are food items you can eat outside of combat to help; meaning, you can enter in to a tougher boss fight with your party already buffed up.

3. Much like some of the old SNES RPGs, at times your party is split. Also worth noting is that there are.. 20? playable characters.. so you don't use them all at all times. This is bad because, like anyone, you're going to pick out some characters that you like more than others, and stick with them.. resulting in them being over twice the level of your other characters.

Normally this wouldn't be an issue. However, later on near the climax of the main story, your party is.. once again.. split. In to three groups of four characters. If any of these characters are too weak, you will be unable to progress and have no way to make them stronger.

Unfortunately for me, I found myself in just such a situation. I'm trapped inside of a tower, cannot leave, and the only way past is a boss fight with a commander and two random NPCs who deal far, far too much damage to my weakened party members. There aren't random encounters inside the tower, so I have no option to grind a little and get stronger for the boss above me.

The only way to alleviate something like this happening to use is to randomly bring in to your main party a lower-leveled character, and grind them up to within 5 levels of everyone else. That way you're behind, but not by so much that you have zero chance of winning.

This is the second time in the game that your party is separated in such a way that you have to use weaker party members and hope you survive. Thankfully, the first time this happens, things aren't quite so hard.



All in all, the game is great and I'd recommend it to anyone.. however, there are some things about it that I think the developers need to take a second look at, or find a way to make the issues not so apparent that your progress halts entirely as a result.

EDIT: Developers have added trash mobs to the areas I indicated, where your progress could possibly halt entirely if you're too weak. So now if players come to these points and the bosses there are far too strong, you at least have the opportunity to grind a little. Huzzah!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlmost 100 hours and going strong - great game!

I can recommend this already on a great opening, gets you interested in the events and characters right off. This continues with the pace of the story ramping up and keeping my interest for now over 90 hours and has gotten even better. Now I am recruiting more characters and having great fun with it. Sort of reminds me of the first 2 Suikoden games.

It has very interesting lore, and ties the characters into it in a very creative way. The game is visually crisp and colorful, lots of variation. Many towns, locations to explore and I've just scratched the surface. Music surprisingly good. Battles simple and go just like I remember on SNES/Genesis games. Will keep posted, but this game gives hope that finally, a modern RPG that actually feels like SNES era. Thank you to the devs! (You can only play old Nintendo games so many times in 20 plus years). This is a great treat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would rate the game 8/10 so far

I have played around 80 hours so far. If I had to compare it to existing games I would say the closest game it resembles is Suikoden (especially with all the characters and town building) followed by FF6 (especially in chapter 3).

Gameplay

Pros:
1. Lots of content. I am 80 hours into the game and I still have not beaten it yet.
2. Lots of secrets. Check everything! Every book case, pot, or piece of paper. You may find a good item, a rumor leading to a legendary item, or a secret door leading to a boss.
3. Freedom to go to places beyond your lv. At least in chapter 2 you get a decent amount of freedom which lets you go to higher lv places. I like rewarding exploration and strategic play by allowing the player to go places which test their skill. Once you get the sea ship and then the air ship it opens up even more.
4. Lots of items to find and craft. You can customize your builds based on what stats you like. You may focus on crit, dodging, raw physical or magical dmg, high HP, ect. You can't ahve it all though since you have a limited amount of armor slots.
5. Tough battles especially with side quest bosses. Some side quest bosses are quite tough, but they have good rewards. They may give you a summon of themselves, a good item, or multiple decent items and XP. Also with the ability to go places a bit above your lv you can run into regular mobs who give you a run for your money.

Cons:
1. Near the end of chapter 2 and into chapter 3 I started gathering a lot of gold. Before that it felt like I needed all the gold I could get and there was a ton of stuff I wanted to spend it on. Later on your limiting factor is mainly materials to craft gear rather than gold.
2. Sometimes an abundance of fights can be annoying, but many RPGS have that issue. I guess I could flee if I really wanted to. Later on you get ways to avoid fights if you don't want which is quite nice.
3. The game got easier later on in the game. However I found a good place to level and overleveled a bit so this is probably my own fault.

Characters

Pros:
1. There are a lot of characters which alows you to put together a team you enjoy for many different play styles.
2. Some characters can be customized multiple ways. For example with the druid you can focus on physical dmg and gear him accordingly with his shapeshifting or you can focus on his magic and go the complete opposite direction. Similarly with the summoner you can focus on summons or his more monk like punching fighting style. Some characters don't have duel roles, but you can still customize their focus quite a bit. Throughout the game I switched my parties characters around as I figued out better strategies and got more gear options.

Cons:
1. You get a lot of characters pretty quickly in the beginning and that doesn't give you a lot of time to digest them. It kind of felt like Suikoden where I kept getting new characters before I got a feel for the abilities of ones I just got. I ended up thinking of my characters by their class rather than their actual names because of this. However unlike Suikoden there aren't 100 characters, there are only 17 so you get to know them eventually. I felt like I started getting to know the characters in chapter 3 after a specific event happened which I won't spoil here.

Story

Pros:
1. Toward the end of chapter 2 and into chapter 3 the story starts getting into some interesting situations and scenarios. Cities alternating between undead and semi living, gladiator tournaments, ect.

Cons:
1. The main story seems to move at too fast a pace in the beginning and can make it a little hard to grasp everything. I would have liked a little more filler light hearted content, perhaps to get to know the characters more in between some of the main story elements. Note I am doing all of the side quests I find, but they are usually fairly short and don't delve into the characters too much. This does improve in chapter 3.
2. I think the children of light vs the dark lord and his group of high lv subordinates is a little too standard. I like more mystery and unconventional plots to stories.
3. You begin to understand the history and a lot of the plot quite a bit later in the game when you delve into the past. I feel like I wish I knew some of that information earlier on.

Misc:

Pros:
Dev interaction is good and they provide quick bug fixes, Most of the time within 24 hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is quite reminiscent of older RPG titles along the lines of Suikoden and the SNES Final fantasies. I would not say there's 20 hours of main story line, it is more along the lines that 15-20 hours is required to meet the storyline requirements

ZERKER GIVES: 7.0/10

THE STORY/SCORE

The world is well setup with various Lore and interesting enough characters with what is currently released. The protagonist has an interesting story explored in the prologue. Several interesting themes are presented that may prove a very solid basis of future content release. The second chapter focuses less so on these elements and bulls ahead with the struggle between good vs evil at an alarmingly fast, nearly dismissive pace for some keystone events yet still presented in a way that invites curiosity to see it through.

The minor writing elements of NPCs and other scripted events are fairly well done, with a good balance of lore elements, humour and filler content. However, Horizon should go through their transcripts to correct various spelling and grammatical issues prior to their official release.

The score itself is well varied and enjoyable, with tunes fitting to the area and situation.

GAME SYSTEMS

There is little in terms of explanation of the various attributes, certain spells or status effects and combat elements that may leave a newer player a bit lost until they experiment for themselves. This is particularly an issue when it comes to party composition, as the game does not make it clear you can change your party members at a Great Gaias book.

CHARACTERS

The party members themselves are varied enough in how they function and mesh together, allowing for a decent amount of experimentation and preference. Certain members make a significant difference in certain fights or parts of the game so do not write anyone off too hastily.

THE COMBAT

I find that the combat system itself is rather fine, if overall a bit slow for these first 2 chapters. Combat speed can be adjusted to a player's liking in options and combat becomes paused when it's time to decide a character's command. This is then followed by a much shorter charge up time until said character executes command, allowing a window for interrupts and being K.O. before that last second heal. Enemies who have completed their ATB charge are indicated by a brief sprite flash so one can plan accordingly. The various resistances, traits and tactics of enemies favors a dynamic party composition to best tackle different areas.

THE CRAFTING SYSTEM

Crafting in the Great Gaias is organized in a simple tier system, where products of the previous tier are required to create the next and so forth. It provides a good means to supplement your equipment beyond shop content. By the end of chapter 2, it becomes increasingly clear the necessity of top equipment unless you plan on overgrinding content. The system does not save you any money or grinding, as items needed are often monster specific drops or stolen and require multiple encounters to A. get the drop then B. get the gold needed to perform the synth.

THE CONS

Beyond some more minor elements previously discussed comes one of the biggest issues of the genre, the grind. Since your Protagonist is always a partner member, he ends up significantly ahead of your other members as you rotate out to level or try them. Every other character seems balanced and on par in party contribution, but the Protagonist pulls miles ahead due to his class and design. EXP is not shared outside those who participated, so you can find yourself with level 8 characters in the Gaias while your main party is in the mid twenties.

Some encounters and story fights are great challenge, downright difficult but completely dismissed with a bit more grind. The grind erases the need to be tactical or try out other characters better suited to the content. You can win everything simply by grinding more.\t\t\t\t\t
