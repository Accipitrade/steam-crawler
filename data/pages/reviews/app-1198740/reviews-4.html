\r\n\t\t\t\t\t5/10 - A TD game that has just enough to like to make me wish it was better. Despite a lot of fluff, it doesn't do much to elevate itself beyond the competition. Full review below:

= Intro =

It's a TD game. I like TD games. Was initially interested due to the advertised RTS aspects.

= Gameplay: 6/10 =

So, yeah. TD game. Build towers to take out advancing enemies before they reach your base. The big innovations here are that you can also produce units and build a few structures to unlock special abilities, increase the tower cap etc.

It all functions...fine. Most missions are your standard TD fare - build towers, upgrade towers. There is a definite emphasis on 'mazing' here. Sometimes a mission will require you to destroy an enemy base or repair an objective, using your 'commander' unit. As you progress, you unlock more missions and can buy new towers, units and the like.

However, there isn't really anything to raise CS above other TD games already on the market, and even at it's price range, it falls badly short.

It lacks the polish, interesting loot, variety and personality of DG2, the visual splendour and scale of X-Morph (or the upcoming Riftbreaker) or the blinding perfection and endless progression of the Gemcraft series. That isn't to say it's a bad game, it just feels like it's missing something.

Some of this has to do with the hodge-podge of ideas that either feel like fluffed up versions of established TD tropes (here are the standard cooldown abilities, but you need to build something for them) or the RTS aspects which feel superfluous, until you have to use them to finish a mission, at which time they become cumbersome.

It feels like a bit of a missed opportunity here - a system where you build AI controlled units to attack an enemy base while you defend from theirs...see, that's a game I'd be interested in playing. Even something as standard as different upgrades for towers would have helped.

= Graphics & Audio: 5.5/10 =

This is sadly not a pretty game. Honestly, it feels like it would have worked better as a 2/2.5D game, or perhaps a simpler graphical style like flash or pixel art. It looks like Ashes of the Singularity on Low. Very flat, very bland - and the overly subdued tower effects don't help matters.

The music and audio are nothing really to write home about either. Nothing awful here, but nothing special either.

= Style & Value: 5/10 =

Again, there is little here that is innovative and the areas which are feel either mostly pointless, or more clunky than enjoyable.

There is a decent number of missions on offer, I'm just gone halfway through (I think) after 8 hours of play, but it's already starting to drag and I don't think I'll end up finishing this one right now. A big part of that is a lack of variety - maps all start looking same-y after a while, likely due to the amazing lack of enemy variety, both visually and mechanically. The bosses in particular are amazingly bland.

= Overall =

+ Some interesting ideas
+ It all works, I guess.

- Poorly implemented RTS aspects
- A severe lack of variety
- You can play better for less

At the end of the day, I'm sad I can't recommend this more whole-heartedly. It feels like there is a very interesting idea here. That said, if you've already played DG2, X-Morph, Gemcraft: Frozen Wrath and similar games to death, this may at least somewhat scratch your TD itch.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tColony Siege, a tower defense hybrid with a strategy undertone and a dash of base building. Does it work?, yes it does.
I have completed the campaign on normal difficulty and if anyone's interested my journey through the levels can be can be found on youtube - ( https://youtu.be/HGJxNlXHUeE ) and I'm now going to start playing through again on hard.

The graphics are very good although not amazing and music and sound effects are on point. The camera can feel a little restrictive but it's not a game breaker.
There is a story line to all this but if i'm honest i wasn't really taking it in but it's nice that they made the effort.

My one slight complaint is after 22 great and varied missions the final attack on the zeno home world is a little bit disappointing.
Survival and endless levels are available on certain levels and these can be unlocked as you progress in the game.

Bottom line is i have had a great time with this game and it really helped scratch that tower defense itch especially if like me you ignore the vehicles in the game and concentrate on the tower defense aspect and maybe add a dash of air support and long range missiles :)

I highly recommend this game 8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, what can I say. It's freaking tower defence plus RTS!

I was hesitant about this, I thought it wasn't gonna be good, after all an RTS + Tower defence hybrid sounded like it was gonna be a nightmare, also because it sounded like it was going to be pure multiplayer and/or a wave system of attacking then defending, which I wasn't too keen on, thankfully the biggest point is that it's singleplayer, there is a bit of attacking stuff, but these are limited to a handful of missions, and you usually only do this towards the end of said missions, where you get a ridiculously OP unit to mop them up, so this counteracts that last point rather wlel.

Now, I put 15 hours into this game, and this costed me about £15 as of typing, so really you get what you pay for.

However despite this the game does have some flaws, they're minor so I won't go into much detail otherwise this may look more like a negative review (Any devs that want me to go into more detail I'll happily do so), so rapid fire:

- Not many levels in the grand-scheme of things (23, not bad, but when you look at it all at once, it feels so small)
- Perks being useless except for 2, everything else is a gimmick and is basically a disadvantage
- Bosses being absolute nightmares unless you have certain buildings
- Not being able to see enemy range (Especially when attacking when the enemies have turrets of their own)

Now these aren't game-changers for me, hence why it's positive, but I think if the devs expanded more on the game (Seriously why stop at 23 missions, sure you beat the game at that point but go crazy! Survival and endless modes exist sure but comeon) then this game would be a solid 10, a chefs kiss.

However I know what you're probably wondering, how's the RTS? Well, the RTS is a side-thing, so you can place towers anywhere you want, I'd consider this the main TD thing, the RTS shines in the form of having your own units and buildings, so you can have an army of mechs/tanks guarding an area, while you send out waves of drones to just kamikaze into an area, or carpet bomb an area while sending nukes and lasers from satellites (Sadly there isn't any proper 'air' unit except for your commander unit, they're all abilities.)

It's not perfect, but it's do-able, if you wanted to go pure vehicle only and forgo the towers, I'd say as long as the map gives you what you need (a vehicle factory and housing, because population limits*), then it's 100% possible, I've done it on some maps, only using towers to defend the base when some stragglers got through.

All-in-all, it's a really great game, though I will say that I don't think I can get much replayability out of this sadly. I recommend it either way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe more you play this game the more you feel that it's a passion project, and it's true. I've been looking for a good TD game to play, and this hits many marks that I was looking for.

Co-op mechanics are very well done. There's enormous flexibility in what you and your partner can do. In our game, I focused on unit and commander ship upgrades, while my friend prioritized towers and traps. I handled the RTS aspect of the game, while he took care of maze and TD. Except for late-game where both players have everything unlocked, you feel the synergy through the entire campaign.

RTS mechanics don't drown the TD side of things, but are a bit lackluster. Units could have a broader selection, but this is not an explicitly RTS game, so that can be excused. Riven battles are spread within the game to make sure there's variety, though the maps feel like they're following a trope selection (ice, lava, sand, grass etc.). It's not monotone, but except for the Riven battles, I didn't feel like doing anything different between maps. The game's difficulty starts at normal, and gradually lowers as you unlock more and more upgrades. Hard isn't a challenge when you're fully upgraded.

Unfortunately, being a passion project does not mean it doesn't have any negative aspects. The music is forgettable and adequately bland; there's nothing special about it other than being there. The story is non-existent except for text and voice over during loadout selection of a map, which is a shame, because it feels like there are elements of a bigger story, yet they never get explored. While the enemies have quite a bit of variety, they all get lost in the big red blob that's going through your maze. Riven units are able to fight back in the campaign, and certain Xeno units have special characteristics (like cloning itself, shielding, spawning units upon death and similar), but I couldn't tell you which enemy has what property. The player should be able to get information, like how there's some sort of "codex" in most TD games, but it's not present here. What information you have doesn't help much either. Damage is listed as "DPS", which is absurd for slow, heavy hitting towers and weapons. I'd like to learn what an upgrade from 25 to 50 "DPS" means. Faster fire rate? More damage? A bit of both? Unless you keep track of a tower specifically, you have no way to know. As for the end-game, the game could use a more thought-out difficulty instead of relying on Endless games, and if money is to be awarded at the end of every game (including repeats), there should be some opportunity to use it, instead of letting it collect dust because you've unlocked everything.

These are minor gripes though. They don't change the fact that this is one of the very few solid TD games where co-op is well thought-out, instead of being tacked on like an afterthought. With a bit of polish it could very well be my number one go to game when I think of tower defense games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile there have been some good top down tower defense offerings in the last year or two, I haven't seen very many 3D TD games that have piqued my interest. That changed as soon as I saw this game— right away I was getting Defense Grid / Sol Survivor vibes, both games I enjoyed thoroughly. A friend and I quickly purchased it and went through the entire campaign.

Does it live up to my expectations? I can easily say yes. The co-op campaign was a lot of fun. The game has full maze building mechanics with a mix of tower defense elements and RTS elements via a ship you control. The range of towers and abilities is fairly diverse and I certainly find myself adapting my build and overall strategy as I tried out different towers. This is further expanded by the upgrade system, which lets you and your co-op partner specialize. In our game this lead to me building tower arrangements and my co-op partner developing units and upgrading his ship. With a good number of maps, all of which vary both aesthetically and with their layouts, the overall game offers a lot of room for experimentation.

It does have it's problems though. It certainly lacks some of the lasting appeal that I found in Sol Survivor, even in co-op, the story was very bland (and the fact that the enemy who ruins the protagonist's life is an alien species called "Xenos" was not endearing to me at all giving the general political climate of past years, but I digress), and the final level was a tad anti-climatic. Aside from that the only other problems I had were just nitpicky such as difficulty tuning (I personally found the game to be a bit lacking in challenge) and perhaps some tuning on abilities and towers, but none of these issues were detractions from the enjoyment I got out of the game.

Overall this was an enjoyable TD that has filled the void where there has been a relative lack of 3D tower defense games coming out.\t\t\t\t\t
