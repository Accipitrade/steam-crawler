\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR at the bottom.

I enjoyed playing this game but it has some problems.

Colony Siege is a tower defense game, specifically one where you can influence your enemies' paths by placing walls and towers in their way. Although it has some RTS elements, the gameplay doesn't focus on them and even in the specific missions in which you have to engage with it I didn't find it very compelling.

The biggest impact the RTS elements have on normal gameplay is the unit count, that gives you a cap on how many towers and units you can field at one time. This is a bigger problem for towers rather than units since everything your enemy destroys is refunded in full, and units are generally faster to rebuild.

Neither the TD nor RTS gameplay style feels fleshed out and polished. Controlling enemy paths is a pain on some maps, feels very unintuitive, and overall it feels like you shouldn't rely on it like in a normal TD game of this kind.

The units you can build are fine, but none of them feel that impactful as building a tower. I used them as utility and the only one I found really effective was the one that stunned enemies. The large tanks were fine but I could only feel comfortable using them if they didn't use up unit count (which you can achieve with a perk in late game).

While I feel that the unit cap is a good addition, I feel it's an unnecessary constraint in most missions, with the exception of survival missions. You are already hard capped in what you can build by the fact that you have no revenue outside of killing enemies and getting wave clear bonuses. If you could have some kind of passive revenue that went past it, you could funnel it into better control of enemy paths.

Currently, there is little reason to make the enemies' path longer (outside the one kind of enemy that does suffer from long paths), what really matters is to make that path hell for the enemies to go through. This would not be a problem if most of these maps were designed with the clear intention of forcing the player to restrict the flow of enemies into a single path (not to mention building things in your base also changes enemy paths).


The game is fun, but there's some design decisions that I don't understand from either an RTS standpoint or a TD standpoint. It almost feels like there isn't enough of either TD or RTS, and that introducing more mechanics from one or both of these genres would make this game better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed through it with a friend and honestly its pretty fun for a simple Co-Op tower defense game. I wouldn't say that this is a revolutionary title in any way but you can def have some fun with it with a friend. Cant comment on the solo play though.

The Good:
Good balance in Tower defense and some decent variety in the missions. Some of the traps and towers seem useless at first but honestly me and my buddy found some hilarious ways to use them (In particular the spinner and spring traps are almost broken if you figure out how to use them. Launching entire groups of units into water or lava or off map is hilarious to watch.). Its a smaller game and I didn't hit the boredom line before beating it since each mission keeps giving you access to new stuff to play around with. Each player gets their own resource pool so that you don't affect one another when trying to build stuff, but when you build the base out both players get the increase from it no matter which player built it. This is great for team play!

The Meh:
Building felt a little limited in the end. Bigger maps with bigger waves is more fun at least in my opinion but overall they could have just been a little more varied or interesting. The perk system definitly exists, but i never saw a reason to switch from the liscense to maze perk and you can only choose one perk. I think that if they had done some balancing and moved to letting you select two perks it would have definitly made the game a little more Customizable and interesting as several of the perks would have paired together nicely. Perks are per mission, and in multiplayer the 2nd player is stuck with whatever perk the other player chooses.

The Bad:
Honestly, in my play through of the game I never really found anything that i would consider to be bad. Just stuff that could have been better,\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: If there was something between Yes or No to recommend I would choose that but seeing as there isn’t, I can’t really recommend this game. It starts off promising but is soon let down by an overabundance in towers, building and abilities as well as erratic difficulty during the latter part of the game. I think if this game focused more on quality over quantity it would be far better. However, as it is, it’s a bit of a mess and something you should only pick up during a sale.

I’m a big tower defence fan and this one caught my eye a while ago. I picked it up not too long ago on a sale.

This game takes place across 22 maps. For the most part you have to build towers and survive a certain number of waves to win the map, each map giving you a secondary objective (such as build a certain number type of a tower) which will award you a perk you can use for the following missions. While there is a perk per mission I really only picked one and stuck with it through-out my entire play through.

While there is a Story, which is told via a narration before each mission, the writing and voice acting is so bad I stopped listening after a couple of missions and just skipped over. While I can’t tell you the specifics, you do have to collect 6 relics before proceeding to the invaders home-world in the last mission to stop them.

When you start the map you have a Command Centre which, if destroyed, you lose the map. You also have a ship which you use to build your defences, repair them and can be used to attack the enemy. As the game progresses you’re able to build more structures around your command centre. These include crew buildings which allows you to place more towers. Power generators to allow your defences to be upgraded and various other buildings that allow the use of timed abilities, such as bombing runs, laser strikes and missile attacks.

For each wave you’re shown the route the enemy will take towards your command centre. You can place towers to force the enemy to take a certain path. You can also build traps, which allows enemies to move over them while doing damage to them.

While there are a few stats that come with each tower, there isn’t anything that negates damage or does more damage to certain enemy types. Some of the enemy units and towers talk about “armour penetration” but as far as I can tell it doesn’t make any difference. For the last few levels the best strategy I could find to win was to build and upgrade Laser towers as they had the best balance of damage and fire rate.

The enemies can be split into 3 categories. Ground units, flying unit and boss units.
The ground units aren’t that unique in what they do, in that they rush towards your command and try to destroy the first building they come across.
The flying enemies are a little different. Some will fly straight to your command centre, ignoring any towers you’ve put in place. Some will target certain towers and some will target your ship. At first I thought these were a good idea as they shook things up but, by the end they became very annoying. As they fly over your towers, you can’t really funnel them along a certain route, so if they fly towards your base you’re going to have to come back with your ship to fight them. Doesn’t sound much but when you’re having to constantly fly your ship back and forth between your base and your towers it gets old, fast.
The boss units are super tanky and deal really big damage. Facing these is really the only time my towers were destroyed. They’re interesting but over simplified and not that interesting.

The last level gets rid of towers altogether and has you commanding a few ground units. It’s for story purposes and not much else. It sticks out as an odd level, which is very easy and quick to do. I would have preferred another standard level with a bit more story at the end.

In-between levels you’re awarded money to purchase new towers, building, and ship upgrades. Levels can be replayed to earn more money but by the end you should have enough money to have everything you need.

Overall, to complete the game on Hard and get all the achievements took me just under 22 hours.

This is a game that has a lot of potential but it has both too much going on and somewhat over simplified mechanics. You have loads of towers/traps to choose from but I found myself always sticking to the same ones. It would have been better to have fewer towers which varied greatly from each other. The same goes for the levels. While some of them are interesting, they’re just too big and would have probably have been better if they were scaled down and more refined.

This game certainly has a “more is less” feeling about it. If it’s on sale it might be worth picking up but there are better tower defence games out there (Such as Defence Grid, Dungeon Warfare(s) and the Kingdom Rush games)
