\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll say this right up front: this is a "No at full price, but buy it on sale" review.

So, the basic issue here is that at its core, you have some competent building blocks. A variety of different enemies, an interesting way to both build and fight, a bevvy of different towers and traps and other units to play with, and so on.

However, the fundamental balance of the game has two big issues:

1) The waves are all extremely easy up until you hit the boss. If you play endless mode, the boss, who's already a challenge, gets multiplied ad infinitum, rather than leaving it as a single boss with more other units, or otherwise scaling linearly. So, the game's typically very easy up until the boss, which you'll still usually be able to push through, but you can't enjoy endless mode because it just quickly multiplies.

2) You run out of units and materials long before you run out of space. Despite maps that clearly have some mazing in mind, ways to redirect the enemy, you really don't need to optimize there, just pick one short-ish path, make it move back and forth in lines, and you'll be fine. You CAN'T set up large, complex mazes, because you will just never have enough materials and people for it, and per the above, you simply don't need it most of the time, either (a multitude of bosses will just punch a hole through your towers anyway).

These are two things that could be very easily fixed with some tweaking, as the underlying engine and gameplay is good, but until then, I can't recommend it at full price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttldr; If you are a tower defense game fan, this is a must-have game!!!

The game starts slow, with little credits and towers able to build in the first levels. But wow, what does it get heavy and intense later, with massive amounts of enemies and increasing difficulty. That's what I call a proper tower defense game.

While in the first levels the same strategy seems to work always, later on, one has to rethink strategies and not all may work equally good. Which makes for a good and interesting challenge. While it may not seem so in the first 10 levels, later it does matter which towers are used and combined together to gain the required advantage over the enemy.

The graphics are nice, sounds are good, effects just fine. Nothing exceptionally great, but also not bad, rather they are all on the same level and working good together. The interface though is awesome and very informative, other tower defense games could learn from it.

Gameplay however rocks and that's where it seems to have been put more effort in to achieve a good balance.

The story adds a nice touch to it as also let us understand and participate in the personality of the hero quite well.

The game has many other features, like those perks, which may make very well a difference on some levels or difficulties. It has a survival and an endless mode (which is unlike endless modes known from some other TD games though, you may only make a handful more levels through and at first just one or two).

You can build base enhancements to gain access to more towers (in amount), vehicles and bombardment features. Towers can be damaged and repairing plays also a role.

Overall, the game is rather one of the more advanced tower defense games which manages quite well to combine all these features into a working set.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tColony Siege did a lot of things right, but...

This is a decent TD game - but some design decisions are holding it back from being a good or even great TD game.

First the good -

1. The checkpoint system is very well done, and when you're not prepared, you're not back to square 1.
2. The commander flying around and participating in the battle, building & repairing things is a good change to the TD formula. You feel more vested.
3. Ability to place towers in the mini-grid like arena is done well, and I like how towers can join each other to create a barrier that allows you to maze.
4. The bonuses on the maps gives a good reason to try out new traps / defenses that you might have otherwise skipped.
5. Map diversity was good, progression with different wave entry points on each map with different spawn areas I liked.
6. I liked how some of the maps had environmental things (lasers, lava, mercury) that would impair the mobs so it is wise to try & maze them into these. It was another good map decision.

The muddling -

1. Unless you replay maps, you will not be able to purchase everything by the last mission - and this is something I came to loathe that so many new toys and abilities are held back so far in the game. I was over 1/2 the way through before I even got T3. I treat everything where I can't build everything as the tutorial. So this is one huge tutorial, with no maps because I can only play with everything once I've completed the game? What? Why was this decision made? Unless you're coming out with a map pack with 20 more maps where I can use all the weapons / traps / commander abilities from the get-go, I'm not liking this.
2. So many traps / defenses / toys and the map's are so big, you will hardly ever build a real maze because the "metal" market and the "capacity" limits are too burdensome. Here I have these maps that have all these areas, and I'm so limited. Build a chokepoint with 10-15 towers and that's it. So much wasted potential.

The outright bad -

1. Endless mode is just pathetic. +1 meatshield. There is no point to really do this because they destroy mazes so quickly.
2. Putting a pause between waves *and* seeing the wave composition behind a perk is stupid. I guess you really don't want me to use any other perks eh? Having to rebuild, rethink, is often necessary and this is the only way to get that.
3. The meatshield boss. One thing I really detest is when a TD game makes the final boss nothing like the rest of the waves, so all the build-up / refinement through waves is kind of pointless when he's just going to crash through your maze is really infuriating. The only counter is to use the commander ability to disable the unit, and the cooldown on that makes it tedious.
4. In the early maps, when you don't have the god-all-mighty railgun that makes bosses an afterthought, succeeding or not on the final wave is really down to how good you can estimate something will move before the air strike gets there. This is so damn tedious.

The game is what it is, and it's sad that it has a lot of potential, but without some fundamental changes, I'd be hard pressed to recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tColony Siege is a tower defence game which also includes some real time strategy style base building. Earth has been destroyed by aliens, and the player must ensure Humanity’s survival by defending colonies located on other planets.

The Good

•\tBuild towers such as shotgun, laser, missile, flame and sniper. When towers are placed close together, an energy barrier between the towers will prevent enemies moving through, allowing you to build a maze to shape their path.

•\tPlace traps on the ground to either damage or slow down enemies who move over the trap.

•\tAdd mobility to your defence with the command ship, which can quickly fly around the map, as well as building vehicles like tanks and mechs at a factory.

•\tEnemies will spawn through multiple portals around each map and attempt to destroy the colony’s headquarters. They’ll also attack towers along their path, and if you’re not paying attention they could open a hole in your maze. Damaged towers can be healed by repair towers, as well as the command ship.

•\tEvery defence item requires people to function, and you can increase the maximum limit by building houses in your colony.

•\tBuild a power generator to upgrade towers to more powerful versions, increasing their range and damage.

•\tThe aircraft hanger, missile launcher and satellite uplink will let you target areas of the map with a variety of air strikes, nuclear missiles, orbital lasers and asteroid bombardments. And I especially like the railgun, which will automatically fire at enemies on the other side of map, and can kill bosses long before they even reach your base.

•\tYour command ship can be upgraded with better weapons, armour and movement speed, and gain special abilities like a shield, or an EMP to temporarily disable enemy weapons. You can also unlock perks such as unlimited build time at the start of each wave, increased housing limit, gain more resources for every kill, or increased armour for towers.

•\tSome missions end with a final stage where you have to capture a powerful alien artefact and use it to destroy the enemy base, while they continue to send a few units to attack your colony.

•\tThe number of available towers, traps, vehicles, buildings and perks allows you to use many different tactics. Do you want to build a big maze of cheap towers, create a single chokepoint of expensive but powerful towers, build an army of vehicles to patrol around the map, or sit back in your base while gradually nuking the map?

•\tEvery map has a different bonus objective (usually build a certain number of a specific tower, trap or vehicle), which encourages you to experiment with different tactics. Completing these objectives rewards you with extra money to unlock all the different things from the shop between missions.

•\tSome maps have environment effects – lava will damage units (friendly or enemy) that pass through it, tar pits reduce unit speed, and meteor showers can damage your towers.

•\tIt took me 12 hours to finish each mission in story mode, and most maps can also be played in endless and survival modes, which increases replayability. Endless is exactly what it sounds like, just survive as many waves as possible. In survival, you have about 10 minutes to build up defences while the aliens constantly harass you, and then survive a single massive wave of enemies.

•\tControls work great with mouse and keyboard. I haven’t had any problems with bugs or framerate.

The Bad

•\tThe enemies look like simple vehicles, mostly the same shape and colour. The only differences are some are bigger, and some can fly. This means they lack personality, and don’t look very alien.

The Recommendation

Colony Siege is a great game, the developers have taken the basic tower defence gameplay format and added a lot of strategic options on top. I definitely recommend it to anyone who enjoys tower defence games such as Defence Grid, Sol Survivor and X-Morph Defence.\t\t\t\t\t
