\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm relatively new to RTS games.

Though I've tried a few of them out in the past, most of them couldn't hold my interest for very long. So this is the first one I've played long enough to even complete a full session. Which is already quite a good thing, in and of itself in my opinion.

This one, however, is fairly straight-forward and the short tutorial is a great introduction to the genre. I had no problem playing the game and the instructions were clear enough.

That being said, I would like to be able to toggle labels on selectable objects? Like have a much bigger 'title' or 'label' to what the thing is and whether or not the player would like to have more or detailed information in-game.

Seeing it right then and there instead of having to go to another window to look at a guide would be quite good, I believe.

One thing to note however, is that I wish it was a lot easier to spot 'buildable' things. I find indicators in general and the focused selectors are not very easy to spot. It's especially harder when you're fairly zoomed out so I'd suggest to make the icons, highlights, etc either be brighter or scalable (if it already is, then make them bigger) when you zoom in and out.

I'd also suggest this for some of the important menus too.

Thank you for creating this game and I wish it all the best!! Good luck! :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Videos of my gameplay

I was skeptical at first, but have become pleasently surprised by this RTS / Tower Defense hybrid. As of this review, I have played the first 3 missions on Hard mode. You'll find missions turn the tower defense genre on it's head, by seamlessly weaving RTS concepts into the missions. For example, after defending the classic 10 waves at a base and defeating the boss, you might be required to "go on the offensive", or claim strategic parts of the map for a bonus.

The graphics are modern and look like what you would expect in a Science Fiction setting.

The controls feel modern, including even control groups and unit selection hotkeys, like you would expect in an RTS.

There are voice acted briefings before each mission, outlining the story of the game.

The progressive nature of the game, continually gives you more and more access to an increasingly more interesting set of passive perks, ground/air units and towers.

I would recommend this game to anyone who loves RTS games, and a sci fi setting.\t\t\t\t\t
