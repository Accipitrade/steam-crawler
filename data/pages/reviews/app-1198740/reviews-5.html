This game is not worth a Thumbs Up, it is worth a Maybe. But sadly Valve does not give us that option, and somehow has come to the conclusion that rating a game is a simple Go / No Go affair.This game clearly demonstrates why there needs to be a middle ground option.

There is definitely potential here, and I am getting some minor enjoyment out of it. I think the Defense Grid meets Supreme Commander thing is a bit of a stretch, but I can clearly see what they were referring to. There is certainly the aspect of building your own path and shaping the battlefield that Defense Grid did so well. And you can build a vehicle factory that allows you to build units like Supreme Commander did. But that is where the SupCom similarities end. Ok, yes, you can upgrade defense towers, but that wasn't unique to SupCom, it is a normal part of any Tower Defense game.

So the good:
  1. You can shape the battlefield, to some extent, like Defense Grid.
  2. All the normal aspects of a Tower Defense game are in place, with upgrades and variety.

The bad:
  1. Because of multiple enemy spawn points and paths, and limited resources, you really only have a very small window for success. In other words, it is less about tactics and a LOT more about figuring out how the devs wanted you to play a given mission. For many missions you have very few meaningful choices. This is almost the complete opposite of Defense Grid.

  2. There are towers and traps that are not useful at all, so the devs have instead elected to give you bonuses if you use them in specific quantities on certain levels. So you buy them just to use them once. This applies to the vehicles you build as well, to a much lesser extent.

In conclusion, if the devs can figure out a way to give the players some actual meaningful choices with regard to tactics then this game could be great. As it is, I give it a 6 out of 10.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, Colony Siege. A Tower Defense/ RTS hybrid. Is it worth it? Yes. I've had 20+ hours for £15, and I'm completely satisfied, with a couple of minor whinges. (And I'm aware they're whinges.)

Cons -

Some weapons could use a tweak. "Pounders" are supposed to be area effect, but don't seem like it? "Spinners" seem largely ineffective.
The usual TD trap on one or two levels with there being a single "optimal" solution that requires foreknowledge. It makes the first play of that level a little frustrating as you get rushed by something new.
Bosses - both a pro and a con. Ties in a little with the above point about foreknowledge. Huge hit pools that can also destroy your towers. They're crazy hard to kill, but the satisfaction is solid!
The warning alarms at the start of each wave, and when your base is under attack make me grind my teeth, but I have audio processing issues. The rest of the sound/ music is great.

Pros -

Nice graphics.
Genuine different options - ground units, satellite weapons, missile factory, each with own benefits.
Environmental hazards.
Generally satisfying weapons, especially late game.
Black hole traps <3
Strikes are lots of fun once you get the bigger ones.
Solid TD gameplay.
Interesting perk mechanic to tweak repeat plays.
Bosses genuinely intimidating!
Good audio. (See above)

For a small studio, this is a great offering, and I would happily recommend it if you need a TD fix. Could maybe use a mission bonus randomiser for repeat playthroughs, but that's very much a nice to have for the future. Will I play it again? Yes :-)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much I love this game and tower defense alike, I have to down vote it because it makes the gravest mistake what no tower defense game should ever make.....mandatory progression.

What do I mean by that? Well in the game, after every mission you earn money and unlock towers/upgrades/units etc to spend it on. On later levels on MEDIUM difficulty (which is supposed to be the difficulty where you can have creative freedom as how you defend your base), if you didnt purchase the RIGHT tower/unit/upgrade you need to defend the wave with, you are screwed. You cant just make due with what you currently have no matter how well you use the terrain. When a tower defense game starts forcing one specific tower/strategy down your throat, it stops being fun. Sure you can go back to repeat previous missions to get more money and purchase everything, that still doesnt change the fact that you have to play how the game wants you to play it not how you want to.

Units are also useless in the majority of the time. They only exist because sometimes you have to move out and destroy a base mid mission and you cant do that with towers, but otherwise they are useless for any kind of defense, since units and towers use up the same population limit unless you pick a perk where they dont.

Also, most of the maps play out pretty much the same. You block all but 1 way in to your base, you set up a kill zone and thats it. (pretty much how you must do it otherwise you cant defend)

Overall, this game could be cool, but it is plagued with some bad balancing issues. Waves are set up so there is ultimately 1 way to defend against them, and the difference between difficulty is just that enemies hit harder and take more damage, instead of facing with a different variation of enemies overall making waves different and more challenging to defend against.

If you want a good tower defense game with a similar graphic style go play defense grid. If you want to play one of the better tower defense games, go check out the kingdom rush series.

PS: The developers do seem like they care about their game and are working on the balancing. I''ll keep an eye on the game in case they update it and fix some of the balancing issues.\t\t\t\t\t
