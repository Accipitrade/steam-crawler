\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy strong first impression of this game is that it looks and feels like an early access game. The open world seems lifeless, almost like a post-apocalyptic setting. I was expecting a happier world really busy and full of life and a lot of things to interact with. The boring music loop doesn't help and you'll be turning that off in the settings very quickly. The trailer does a great job making you think the game is very lively and full of excitement however the start of the game has nothing like it to reel you in.

The game needs a better opening tutorial covering the controls, missions/tasks, inventory, map screen etc. It was confusing off the bat trying to escape the house - my expectation was some heavy hand holding and telling me the controls and where to go but instead you run up and downstairs talking to cats with text-only dialogue until one of the cats let you leave the building. I found stepping outside the house for the first time into the dull city / harbour area is very underwhelming. There is a general plainness / lack of detail in all the buildings / backdrops and the colour scheme makes it all seem dull. With all the lack of detail the game somehow can only run at 50-60fps whereas I can play AAA games at over 100fps.

The cars drive like in similar open world games (wobbly life, goat simulator) though the controls feel clunky on both kb&m and controller. I tried to run over and npc and he got stuck in the bonnet unaware that he was hit by a car, which is one example of the many buggy things you'll see. If you drive into the harbour, witness your 1 frame car explosion and go get yourself a coffee as your cat takes forever to drown and it's faster to let him drown than to button mash him out.

The default controller layout is terrible (camera isn't assigned to a stick by default, b isn't crouch, x or press down left stick isn't sprint, map and inventory screens are on buttons which can be accidentally pressed), though this can be manually changed to be like most other games in the settings. I found getting out of the map and inventory screens on controller is a little mini game of remembering which button exits the particular screen or press every button until they exit.

If you've played goat simulator this may feel like a major step down unless you really like cats. My 5-year-old son enjoyed playing this a little bit though I'm pretty sure he's over it at 1.5 hours. As sad as it sounds the most fun we had was playing hide and seek in the tutorial house until I was bothered to plan our escape. We've played both Wobbly life (early access) and Goat Simulator 3 (full release) which have grabbed our attention for a lot longer than this game. I'm going to try and give this more of a chance, but its clear that this is an early access game with bugs and all labelled as a full release. I can only recommend this at this early stage to kids who really like cat games and hope the developer is going to keep giving the game love until it feels like a more of a complete game.\t\t\t\t\t
