\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI’m a relatively new player with around 50 hours on Layton and maybe 10 on Hirschfelden (the two starter maps). I had been thinking about a first dlc map, I was going to buy Silver Ridge Peaks in a sale, I’d hopped into a mp map to look at SRP but decided to wait. I’d watched a bit of the early access you tube partner content for this map, saw that this was in the steam sale 20% off (which was a nice surprise for a brand new dlc) so went for this instead.

I’m not going to comment on the updates to the base game that were released at the same time just the map dlc.

So far I’m really enjoying the map, I’ve explored about half and have been having fun which is the important part. It feels small compared to Layton (half the size maybe) and the need zones are pretty close together so I think the density of animals and need zones is much higher.

It feels like it was designed for 150m rifle shooting. The trees are pulled back from the water and the forests are open with not much cover for you or your target.

Compared to Layton this is a much easier map provided you’ve put some perks into aiming rifles and got the shotgun perks for faster up follow up shots. I’ve only done a few hours of bow hunting in total but I think, perhaps, the limited cover and open spaces would make getting close enough for a reliable bow shot will be more challenging on this map.

The boats to get across some of the lakes are great and save a lot of walking, just wish there were more of them.

The story mission is a disappointment just a tour with some fetching and carrying really but I wasn’t buying the map for the story missions.

Do you need any other dlc with this? Not really.

I bought tents in an earlier sale and having just one really helps with the exploring as real life keeps interrupting me (same as the base game).

A class 1 rifle (22LR in weapons pack one or the one in Modern Weapons) would help but that is the same as the base game and is a nice to have for offshore water fowl.

Other than that I’ve just been using base game weapons. 243 for smaller animals, unlocked 7mm for dear, plus the unlocked 338 for moose and bears. If I need a quick follow up shot, I’ve just switched rifles.

The starter shot gun upgraded with the unlockable reflex sight is fine for ground birds and hares.

The new flight speeds and random flight paths of fleeing birds mean that I’m looking forward to unlocking the better shotgun in the base game but I don’t think any of the other dlc ones would help much.

Overall it is a fun map and different enough from the starter ones to be revisited. I suspect I would also have enjoyed Silver Ridge Peaks. If you only have the base game and funds are limited, wait for a sale and perhaps get SRP, tents and Weapons Pack 1 instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wish there was a mixed option for this review, but I think this map has a lot going for it so I'll give it a thumbs up.

As of writing this, this is by far my favorite map. Open spaces make it extremely fun to hunt some of the big game species, and the lake hunting for whitetail is phenomenal (the best out of all whitetail maps). Grouse and Ptarmigan are also my two favorite bird species to hunt, so of course I was excited to finally see them make their debut.

THAT BEING SAID...

While I enjoy the new Waterfowl system (I think it's a marvelous improvement over what it was before) the shotguns in this game are clearly not designed to accommodate it. Over the many years I've played this game, both console and pc, I've noticed that the maps EW puts out vary in their perspective and distance.

Shotguns aren't really equipped to shoot anything accurately past 50 meters in this game, and yet it feels like the birds fly/spook quite a bit farther out than that. Especially with the upland birds, this causes significant difficulties in actually hunting any of these species efficiently.

Animals like grouse or rabbit I know, from experience, are nowhere near as attentive as they are in this game, and it makes the process of hunting them with their terrible shotguns all the more frustrating.

If they made it so that these birds spooked realistically and fixed the range issue with shotguns so that it doesn't feel like the pellets are rolling out of the barrel, this map would be a slam dunk. As it is, it's more of a 7 or 6 out of 10.\t\t\t\t\t
