Most, if not all, of the vegetation models are ones you will have seen in other maps. It does the job, though, and I can't complain too much - just be prepared to run into trees you've seen a million times before in Hirschfelden and Layton Lake, among others.

Quest-wise, there is not much - the main quest is quite literally a walk you can complete in a couple of hours, and there is only a small number of side quests with lots of objectives you can complete at your own pace. The story - which is just a walking tour - is narrated by The Most Finnish Man In The World, and I mean that as a compliment. He's dorky, endearing, and plays on a lot of the kinder stereotypes of Finnish character. His sense of humor might not work for everyone, but it worked for me - and I was honestly relieved to have a no-stress main quest, as a change of pace from some of the more intense ones in other DLCs.

So, if you are looking for challenging and dangerous animals, for very unique and unusual landscapes, or exciting and dramatic quests - I would recommend the Yukon Valley, Medved Taiga, or Vurhonga Savannah maps instead, as those hit all three. Revontuli Coast has none of those - but what it does have is a great chill vibes, relaxing environment, and the best bird hunting in the game. Bring your .22 and your 20-gauge weapons, and you'll have good fun.\t\t\t\t\t
