\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, it was inevitable they'd do this since they did one for carnivores. The choice of dinosaurs however were less obvious. So, let me start off with those choices. Homalocephale has stolen the title of smallest dinosaur in the game from Troodon firmly and has given a lot of people hope that Compsognathus, Microceratus, and other small dinosaurs can make it into the game. It's a tiny pachycephalosaurid and the first one from Mongolia that finally breaks the North American monopoly of the group. Hopefully, this means Prenocephale and/or some other Asian pachycephalosaurids can also make it in. It's a very cute little dinosaur and a JPOG veteran, so that helps too. Dryosaurus is another member of the JPOG old guard. With it and Homalocephale the entire base JPOG roster has successfully been translated over to JWE. Dryosaurus is a dinosaur we needed for so long. Small ornithopods were nonexistent until it came along, with the previous record holder being Muttaburrasaurus at 6m long. Dryosaurus is about 3 to 4m long and a little shorter than a person. It's arrival also makes it possible to make more complete late Jurassic Morrison formation-based exhibits. Also, the alternate skins are highly colorful and interesting for a herbivore. Nigersaurus is the third and biggest of the bunch, but with it marks the appearance of the first smaller sauropod in the game at roughly 12m in length. Because of this, it feeds from ground herbivore feeders as opposed to tall ones. The distinguishing feature is of course its wide mouth with many small teeth. Additionally, its alternate skins are very interesting and colorful for a sauropod.

Do I recommend getting this? Sure, why not? If you've been invested in JWE this long, it makes little sense not to. A few more worthy additions to the dinosaur roster of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's start first with: If you want more dinosaurs you will buy this dlc anyway. So what's in it:

All three dinos get an introduction from Cabot Finch and as always need to be researched first, then digged.

#Homalocephale "cutest addition":
Pop: 0-20, Social 3-10, rating ~54 (fully modified)
Is a small pachyocephalos, which after modifying has a rating just short of the bigger Dracorex. It is even smaller than it (smallest of all dinos so far!), but CUTER, esp its feeding animation.
Fast breeding, so small it fully submerges in bigger waterholes :-) Almost funny (I want swimming animations!)
For the Paleobotanic fans among us it has the same preferences than the other pachyocephalos (just other priorities).

#Nigersaurus "most useful addition":
Pop 0-25, social 2-8, rating ~151 (fully modified)
Giant herbivore (though not that massive), which feeds from the ground. It can be feed differently than other giant Herbivores and might be a nice addition in medium dino enclosures (for the paleobotanics).

For a large herbivore it needs a ridiculously small enclosure (about the size of a Dilophosus; 3000 gras and 3000 forest)!
It's fully modifyed rating is the lowest of all giant herbivores, still around 151.

I haven't seen any nice animations, despite its classification as 'giant' it is attacked by large carnivores. Not by small ones. It PANICS when around large carnivores.
It is better regarded as an ARMORED herbivore, which is not attacked by small carnivores and feeds from the ground. Don't let the 'giant' classfication fool you, it is not immune to large carnivores like all other giant herbivores.

#Dryosaurus: "least useful addition"
Pop 0-25, Social 1-22, rating ~11 (fully modified !! )
For paleobotanic purposes it is listed as member of the Iguanodon family (Iguanodon, Ouranosaurus). Just smaller. So small it has the lowest rating of all (but still bigger than the Homalocephale). Even after fully modifying it is about ~11.
But it is cheap and fast to bread. Unfortuntely it prefers forest environment (over 2.000 of it for such a tiny dino!), which is very unusual and doesn't make it easy to find a suitable existing enclosure for it. It is also unlocked rather late for the dino with the overall lowest rating. New tactics required, keep it with the Iguanodon family dinos.
Let me add that the in-game database list it as its own family (not Iguanodon anymore) now, while still keeping it in the Iguanodon family for food purposes. Complicated little minx!\t\t\t\t\t

Dryosaurus returns from it's JPOG days, and just like before it enjoys running around and just being cute, and sadly is defenceless like before, so be careful letting near this dino near your carnivores, it doesn't really do much, however it can make exhibits feel less empty or dead, have them running around when you have a pen full of larger herbivores, which I usually do for these and the other Ornithomimosaurs.

It's a super cute sauropod and is just a fun one to use.

Overall it's a fun pack, but compared to the rest of the packs, it is lacking, the Carnivore pack offers more interesting Dinosaurs, while the Cretaceous pack offers even more interesting Dinosaurs, and even some with new animations.
Again it's good, but I say get the other ones first if you want some new Dinosaurs now.\t\t\t\t\t
