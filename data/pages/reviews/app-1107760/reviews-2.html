\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe herbivore dinosaur pack as the name suggests adds 3 new herbivores to the game: Dryosaurus, Nigersaurus, and Homalocephale.

I will be rating these dinosaurs on a scale of 0-10 along with a rating of how often I personally would put them in my parks.

Dryosaurus: A small bipedal herbivore. This cute little dinosaur is unique in appearance compared to other small herbivores like Struthiomimus and Gallimimus because its small size and body structure. Dryosaurus also has a great variety of skins. Its uniqueness along with the fact that it is a returning dinosaur from JPOG makes it a good addition to the game. 7/10 will occasionally add them to my parks

Nigersaurus: A strange sauropod. This dinosaur is the smallest sauropod in the game, at about the same size as most hadrosaurs, and is the only sauropod to eat from ground feeders. Because of its small size it is also the only sauropod that can be hunted by carnivores other than the Indominus Rex. Its mouth is unique because of how wide it is and it has a couple good skins. While it is unique in many aspects, I find it a little pathetic because of its small size and slow speed. It doesn't have the grandeur of the other sauropods or the speed of hadrosaurs. 5/10 will only add to my park to feed Allosaurus

Homalocephale: The cutest Pachycephalosaur. This adorable little dinosaur is very tiny and has a unique animation hopping up to feeders as a result. It is another returning dinosaur from JPOG and has a great variety of skins. On top of being the cutest dinosaur in the game, it also has the most gruesome death animations from carnivores. Because of its small size it fits a unique niche among herbivores and as such is an excellent addition to the game. 9/10 will more often than not add them to my parks (will also probably add them just to be eaten because I'm a monster)

Overall, this DLC adds two small and one large herbivore. They each have some uniqueness, but I think it's just barely enough to justify buying full price.\t\t\t\t\t
