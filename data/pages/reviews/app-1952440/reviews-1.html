\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is so much better than it has any right to be. Don't doubt the trailers, they are spot on. Great artwork, good hack and slash combat. Tons of upgrades, levels, progression, story, enemies.

I love games like classic ps2 God of War, Darksiders, Dante's Inferno and the like. Something about a good hack n slash game with new moves, abilities, powers all while telling an engaging enough story. Not every game needs to have a super deep story or super technical combat for me, so long as what is there is engaging. What this game has it just that. The gameplay loop is simple, but engaging. The story is simple, but you want to find out more. Everything works this way and I'm over 3 hours into this game and it is still adding gameplay elements on, unlocking characters, giving me new trees to strengthen my character, unlocking new appearances and sending me to new areas. For the price of this game it is a fantastic package if you like the games I've mentioned above or consider yourself a hack n slash or beat em up fan. If
you like Norse mythology and creatures then you are in for even more of a treat.

Story

Nothing outrageously deep but it is all voice acted and you do want to continue to find out what it is going on. There is way more here than in your typical type of game like this. And while they aren't all that deep, there are even decisions you make during the game that change certain outcomes.

Gameplay

Just like you see in the trailers, it is all here. Combos, dodges, timed parries, special moves, quick time events, ultimates, powered up modes, they are all present and there are several upgrade trees to go down.

Sound

It isn't something that will blow you away but it is all for the most part well done. I wish I had more control over how loud the volumes were of certain things rather than high, medium, off, but aside from that everything here is pleasing. The attacks sound really good, and I applaud the developers for having almost the entire game voice acted. Everything I've played so far in over 3 hours has almost entirely had voices present. From in-game voices, to story, side story, and cinematics. Some of the music in the game is really good as well.

Graphics

Once again I applaud the developer for having higher resolutions. This game is like an upscaled PS2 game with some newer gen effects. Nothing that will blow you away but due to level, creative, and art design they all still feel higher than they should. For the price of this game, I definitely think the art direction is very good. Don't go in looking for a bunch of graphical settings you can turn up, but they do have higher resolutions.


I look forward to continuing to play this game and complete it, probably several times since there are different characters you can unlock and they have their own moveset. The game is engaging and the combat feels fun. While the levels are really short, they still feel just different enough and some of the boss encounters will either make you power up or really get good at timing. For people like myself that enjoy these types of games and still can enjoy them with less than current gen graphics, I think you'll really enjoy this game.

Well done job by the developers and I hope this game gets you enough money that you can make another game like this or a continuation with even a bigger budget and more open levels. The base you have here is so well done and in a year with so many great games that standout like Elden Ring, Spider-man PC, V Rising, and Vampire Survivors, just to name a few, this game is still something that begs me to play it more due to its good gameplay and great gameplay loop with excellent creative art direction.\t\t\t\t\t
