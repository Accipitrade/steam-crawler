Graphically, the game looks fine, nothing amazing but nothing bad either. The soundtrack was really good and probably the best part of this game. Still, I liked this game overall, and this is a 6/10 game for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough I feel as if the narrative didn’t quite move me as much as it wanted to, the sublime music and blended 2D animation do make A Memoir Blue an admirable piece of aquatic art. Interactive story art games, namely those also published by Annapurna Interactive, excel at using their limited gameplay as clever allegories for a narrative theme or to reinforce the character’s emotions. Aside from a few clever moments, A Memoir Blue falls short in fully capitalizing on the strengths of the storytelling medium that it’s working within. Most of the game’s interactive screens do little more than remind you that ‘this is a boat’ or ‘this concept is obscured’ or simply get you to initialize the next animation. As a result, the game is still serene and beautiful, just in a more passive and traditional sense.\t\t\t\t\t

DISCLAIMER: This is a full review, and NOT a first impressions review

A Memoir Blue is a game developed by Cloisters Interactive and published by Annapurna Interactive

The game offers some nice graphics and a really beautiful aesthetic, especially with its incorporation of both 3D and 2D graphics which blend incredibly well, it is pleasant all the same

The game does suffer with it being incredibly short and not offering much in terms of gameplay and more of a short, touching story. For me personally, it didn't bug me at all but if you're expecting a really deep, intricate game with some clever gameplay mechanics and such, A Memoir Blue isn't the game for you.

A Memoir Blue overall, however, is a short, beautiful game to play, it is a good interactive adventure game to play in short bursts!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was looking forward to this game since I first saw the trailer but it's is one of those games that need a "neutral" review because the good and the not so good things balance each other out.

The things that tipped my review to "positive" were the soundtrack and the fact that I'm a sucker for these gamified short stories.

Positives:
  • The game is very creative. As a budding game dev myself I appreciate all the little details, different interactions, animations and art that much more.
  • Stellar soundtrack.
  • It's just a nice little story.
  • Relatively cheap

Negatives:
  • Since it's more of an interactive short story than a "real" game, the little gameplay there is is only barely more complex than turning a page.
  • This game suffers from "mobile first" gameplay. The gameplay and the assets were clearly designed to target as many platforms as possible, primarily mobile ones (i. e. Android/iOS). The only input device you use is your mouse. You click and drag object around and wipe away gunk until the next cutscene happens which easily translates to mobile platforms. I don't know if this game will be available on phones in the future but it sure plays like that was the goal from the beginning.
  • My RTX 2080 was at 100% for the entirety of the game to run it at 2560x1440@144FPS which hints at something weird going on.

对于单鼠标点击, 很难在引导和交互之间达到一个完美的平衡, 提示过多救活打破沉浸感(光, 动画), 提示过少就会很卡关负面情绪增加; 而全部都是鼠标点击的交互更难去打磨好每一个体验
相比于艾迪芬奇的记忆(难免会做出来对比), 我感觉不管是体量还是交互都只能算得上其一个章节的规模

好的几个点:
