\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think anything from Annapurna is at least worth a look and "A Memoir Blue" was no exception, though a little "thin" at times.

I would say this is an occasionally-interactive-story-game, so if you're wanting a lot of push and pull you may be disappointed, instead you'll get a simple but well told story of one woman's path through life focusing on her relationship with her mother.
This was exactly what I wanted and I was happy to lose myself in the art and movement, occasionally pulling something or clicking a few times, it's a bit like a comic book, in that respect perhaps.

Anyway, I liked it though as mentioned, it might be a little "thin" for some. You can see it all in one hour quite simply; my play time was affected by achievements. I played this game the same day as "Lona: Realm of Colours" - if you want a double bill with similar games but slightly different approaches, that one would work.

Also this played perfectly on Ubuntu 22.04 with Proton 7 specified.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Drown into the depth of your consciousness to seek out forgotten treasured memories to resurface back main where they can be held as a trophy.

An attempt at a quick summary aside, Blue Memoir is one of my faviourite 'tell not me not, show me' type of games with sections of beautiful 2D and 3D style that works well for the narrative of the story. The 2D is used in flashback (for simpler time) and 3D use for the present (where it is more complicated). As for the puzzle, they are really easy enough to solve and help transverse from one narrative point to another. There are a few hidden achievements, thanks to the puzzle being interactive if any achievement hunters are interested.

Lastly, the story is quite easy to understand and leaves enough room for a different interpretation that other players to make up their own context, it is even worth it to replay certain sections of the game once you complete the full story to get the whole meaning.

Throughout the journey, I was pleasantly happy with how gorgeous and peaceful my experience was, whilst reliving memories between mother and daughter that I teared up a bit hearing that phone call towards the end.\t\t\t\t\t

The gameplay is essentially point and click, there are some very minor puzzles but they are extremely easy and not interesting or thought out. The game is only about an hour-long (the other 40 minutes were to get all the achievements) so it is short. There isn’t much more to say you click things to advance the story.

Story

The story follows a woman going through her childhood memories. It tells the story through no dialogue and uses 2d vs 3d animation for either memories or the present day. It tells a story of a difficult time for an athlete and how she developed as she got older focusing on her relationship with her mother. It is well done and tells the story in a unique way, it also feels very personal.


Graphics

The art style is gorgeous. The style is incredibly well done and matches the sadder vibe of the story well, each of the images is compelling to look at. The difference in the brightness of the 2d animation from childhood to the darker gloomier 3d animation of present-day works contrasts the two very well.

Soundtrack

The soundtrack is fine, the songs fit well I don’t really have anything to say about it.

Conclusion

A Memoir Blue has a compelling story and a great art style but the lack of interesting gameplay drags it down heavily for me.

Score: 4/10
