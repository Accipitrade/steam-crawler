\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm probably going to be in the minority on this, but while the game is beautiful, it's also REALLY boring. I played it twice through and both times I almost fell asleep.

Simply put, there's almost no actual gameplay. It's almost entirely cutscenes. The only real extent of your gameplay is recreating set pieces/memories. There are no challenging puzzles and the achievements are really easy to unlock. You don't even get to WALK. I don't understand why this is a game when this easily could've been a short film.

Now I don't mind games with simplistic gameplay as long as the story and characters are interesting, deep, or charming enough to carry it. This...doesn't quite cut it. Spoilers:

Basically, Miriam and her Mom flee an abusive husband/father and the Mom has to work very hard to support her daughter. Her daughter gets into competitive swimming and is really good at it, but the Mom doesn't always show up because, well, she's gotta put food on the table. This causes Miriam to TRASH THEIR APARTMENT and move out, becoming absorbed into her swimming and basically avoiding contact with her mother. Miriam realizes that she's been an ungrateful brat, but the Mom still loves her, and it ends with Miriam finally answering her call.

Like...that's it? I was expecting a twist that would give the story more stakes. Like maybe the Mom died at some point and Miriam regrets cutting her off like that. Or she sees her mother keep a collection of news articles on Miriam's wins. But nope. This whole game was basically an overly convoluted way of saying "call your Mom".

The game is pretty cheap and it wouldn't hurt to buy it to support the developers (especially since the game employs gorgeous hand drawn animation), but don't go in expecting anything super deep or engaging. Otherwise, I'd probably go pick up games like Florence or Gris. They're short with simpler gameplay but the emotional stakes feel much more real and are actually engaging to play.\t\t\t\t\t
