\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHi everybody,

I read a lot of criticism on the web because of Darksouls comparisons, so I wanted to mention a few things.
I recently played through the game for the first time. I also played through Darksouls 1-3.
Is this game like Darksouls? Only in certain general points: Saving only in designated spots, limited healing capabilities, your healthbar does not fill up by itself after a while and every normal enemy has enough power to kill you.

Is this a Darksouls clone? I don't think so. I am grateful for two things: That there is a game category where The Surge, Darksouls, etc. fit in and that studios like Deck 13 are not afraid to make a game like this even though they must know, that games like this only attract a very limited number of players who like being punished severly for making mistakes :D

If you want a challenging game, but don't like the Darksouls setting or monstrous enemies: This is a very good alternative. I enjoyed not having slimey monsters all the time, but instead a science fiction setting. Also while this had Darksouls similarities on the larger scale, on a microlevel it is quite different. Armor actually makes sense here and levelling is way different. By switching your augmentations you can change your experience all the time.

My advice: Give it a go if you like challenging games. Personally, I love it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn above average experience for a Souls-like game. The combat is enjoyable with some degree of innovation in how its executed.

The story line is much more direct than From Software games but told in a very mediocre way. There was a really good foundation for a really thrilling story of a handicapped protagonist but its not exploited and told properly.

The AI is decent, level designs and the bonfire ( ops rooms ) are placed good as well as really creative and decent shortcuts. There is only one bonfire per map instead of several however through placement of smart shortcuts to that singular bonfire.

The countdown to reclaim your scraps ( Souls ) adds a little bit sense of urgency.

There are a few really important bugs. Not game breaking but one of them is affecting a side quest early on. ( As of May 2020 )

Overall its a pleasant experience however missing some really aspects. I'd give it a 7.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
-♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ great feeling of control over your character
-Interesting story
Cons:
-Enemies actively work to limit your great feeling of control over your character
-Game is actively designed to stop you from progressing rather than teaching you how to progress

Summary: If you want a ♥♥♥♥ time waster, grab it. But if you expect more than anything other than a time sink this game is not for you.

This game really defines great game play but poor game design. This game, when at its right moments is incredibly fun feeling to play. However, the game as a whole falls flat. There is no real sense of personal progression. You can progress through the story, but the most efficient way to do this is running past enemies, which would boil this game down to a walking simulator. You could beat down enemies and receive little reward, go back and upgrade, and then repeat the whole process until you brute force your way through the game, but that makes the game repetitive and ultimately boring as well. If you have inexhaustible patience, you may enjoy this game, but I cannot recommend it. Bosses are disappointing and unrememberable. What little I remember about each boss is that there are strategies where you can perform one action over and over to defeat a boss (i.e. walking behind, baiting an attack, and then hitting once or twice, rinse and repeat). Lastly, enemies are purely balanced in way that indicates that some stat progression takes place, which results with enemies one or two hitting you by the second portion of the game, despite your not actually improving your stats at all (or at least your health).

The best one word description I can give this game is: arbitrary.
Everything feels difficult as if it is trying to arbitrarily live up to some difficult older brother, thinking that making a difficult game means a making an engaging game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere do I start? The combat, beyond the amputation mechanic which is dope as hell, sucked. Most enemies were incredibly cheap, and every single fight played out the same: dash attack, dash back, heal with the med-voitac, repeat. There were about eight different enemy types, and they get very boring. Even with the amputation, unless you try to create and upgrade 4+ sets of armor at the same time (you won't want to) you lose incentives to cut off limbs, favoring to use your energy to heal or use drones. I'm very disappointed, given how cool the game seemed. Most of the game's difficulty comes from inconsistent enemy attack patterns, lack of telegraphing attacks (ESPECIALLY with non-human enemies). horrible camera angles, and random pot holes in the middle of the map. If I had paid full price for this game, my fury would be unreal.

TLDR; The game is bad. Very bad. Just play Dark Souls\t\t\t\t\t
