\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed the game. The game runs smooth, no glitches at all. I ran the game on an Alpha 15 Laptop, AMD Ryzen 7 3750 H, RX 5500M 4 gb, 16 gb of ram on the highest graphic settings. It was running at an average 70 fps with no major drops, which means that you can run the game even on older systems, or entry level laptops without any problem at all. The graphics are nice, even if it's an older game, the location, an abandoned factory facility, gives the game it's own atmosphere. The game learning curve is ok, if you put your time in farming, and making strategies the game is not that hard. The hardest part of the game for me was episode 9 form the second DLC The Good, the Bad, The Augmented. Other than that the game was a Walk in the Park. I really enjoyed the game + Walk in The Park DLC. I used as my main armor for all the runs the gorgon set and the staff, twin rig and single rig as weapons of choice . The bosses are interesting, but easy to beat if you time your attacks correctly. The most interesting boss for me was Carbon Cat from Walk in The park DLC. I really liked the gimmick from which you procure your gear and weapons, cut different limbs from enemy in order to obtain schematics for armor parts, or weapons. I recommend this game to all the players that like a challenging game. I saw that people compare this with Dark Souls, but i can do that because i hate Dark Souls series. The only game that i play from From Software and enjoyed was Bloodborne, and maybe in some aspects like attack pace this game is similar with Bloodborne. To be honest i give credits to this game not as a Dark Souls clone like others said, but as an own game with interesting story, locations, weapons. Totally recommend. 10/10 for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSystem Shock Souls.

Overall I loved it. Design wise it's very reminiscent of Dark Souls 1, opening shortcuts back to central hubs, being able to see places you've been to in the distance, and the general difficulty level and progression.

Sound design is superb and it looks great. The story is fun and the game is very good at developing atmosphere. Setting wise it's reminiscent of System Shock with a grand project full of hubris and corporate cynicism, evolving into quite a disastrous situation as time goes on. In typical dark souls fashion there are few sane humans left and the few remaining are getting worse. Quite good voice acting for a game like this.

The combat is great, extremely fast and responsive when you use the fast weapons. Slicing off limbs to get parts and weapons is a neat way of making gear physical - if you want something you've got to take it. Boss fights are really fun and dark soulsy and feel very dangerous but not unfair. There's a load of subtle details and mechanics in the game that keep it interesting and challenging as well. Has NG+ and I'll probably play it again.
