\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI simply can't recommend this game. I tried and tried and tried to like it, but in the end I keep getting frustrated by poor design choices and glitches (like the targeting system not being responsive). The game has the foundation of a great game, but the developer's execution is TERRIBLE. They did such a poor job with this game.

Example of a glitch? I jump into the third boss where I have some scrap sitting there waiting because I died. I hit the button to pick it back up and the bar goes all the way across (indicating that I did pick it up), but wait I did not actually pick it up! As I give a second try to pick it up, I get killed and lose the scrap... Then, they decided for some reason to not let you hit this boss from behind? Strange and frustrating.

The levels are a frustration to explore. They pretty much all look the same, so getting back to where you made it can be a pain in the neck. You will be constantly wondering how you got to where you did. This is NOT because you forgot, it is because the design of the levels (everything looking pretty much the same) makes it difficult for you to find your way back. Again, this is a design issue and not a player issue. Yes, if you just didn't remember because you weren't paying attention, that would be your own fault. However, here the reason is the design of the level itself, everything looks exactly the same!

The enemies are inconsistent. With games like this, you need to have predictability so the player is challenged to learn new strategies, or level up, to beat a tough enemy. However, you will master one enemy (who is very easy to kill by now), but for some reason he one-shot kills you randomly.

The upgrade system for weapons and equipment is weak. I am still using armor from around the first level (might actually have been the first level) because nothing later is better... Again, really bad. You really should be chasing that better armor/weapon set. It helps keep you coming back to the game wanting more.

The energy system is REALLY weak. In part 2 I felt the need to constantly upgrade and use the energy system to advance. Here, the drones are weak and the energy system can be easily forgotten as it really is unneeded to advance. They could have just left it out of the game and it would have all been the same.

The bar is set pretty high with the Dark Souls games, The Surge doesn't even come close to that level. The funny part is, The Surge 2 is pretty good. I guess we should expect that though, right? The good foundation was there in The Surge, but the execution was terrible. The developers came back and fixed the execution in The Surge 2.

Don't play this game. If you play this game, you might never even try The Surge 2. Or, do like I did and play The Surge 2 first. That way you get to play a great game and not let part 1 affect your attitude about the series.

People are going to say these two games are the same, that is not the case at all. The Surge 2 is significantly better, as the developers pretty much fixed everything that I was critical about in this review.\t\t\t\t\t
