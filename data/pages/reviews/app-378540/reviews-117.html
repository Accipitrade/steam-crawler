\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would say The Surge is a largely enjoyable experience compared to Deck13's previous work. While yes it is good, it isn't without its flaws. The Surge nails its own identity, and is very much different enough to stand on its own. However, in certain cases I was left looking at some of the level design and questioning what the developers were thinking. I will get to that a little bit later, but first I'll list some pros and cons.

PROS:

*Great assets, and environments.

*World is large and in some cases very engaging.

*Various Souls-Like NPCs with side quests.

*Gear is procured off enemies instead of simply finding them in the world.

*Fun Bosses (For the most part)

*Dynamic Character Building Loadouts (Not locked to your Souls-Equivalent Stats)

* Combat for the most part is satisfying with Mortal Kombat like finishers

CONS:

*Certain Weapon Movesets are unwieldy and awkward to use.
I found myself favoring the Heavy Weapon, One-Handed and Staff movesets. Single-Rigged
was laughably awful.

*Weapon Proficiency only affects damage scaling (AFAIK), seeing this stat affect the actual handling of the
player's weapon would have made more sense to me.

*Boss reuse: A particular boss is (IMO) has poor implementation where you have to dodge a missile in order to break
a generator to prevent the actual boss from regaining health. Cool concept, I'd like to see a different implementation. (Perhaps using the Overloading mechanic instead to destroy the generators would be more interesting)

*Resource Hog: The Surge has some pretty hefty requirements, the game is pretty so it makes sense but other comparable games (Even more recent Souls Games) don't seem as steep which are equally pretty.

*Not enough bosses

*Labyrinth-like level design and no fast travel: The Surge LOVES shortcuts, and will absolutely make damn sure you remember the most recent one to get to where you need to go. However, The Surge HATES operations (The bonfire equivalent in this game), expect to see exactly one operations location per area. This wouldn't be as bad if each area had equally decent level design, but some areas suffer from poorly thought out layouts. For example in the Nucleus (the only place with a decent operations position) you get to move the operations location from levels of altitude 1-3. However, you can only move them at those respective levels. My issue with this, is that the game will require you to backtrack if you want to get more collectables, or farm certain enemies, and not being able to move the operations location after unlocking the respective level needlessly forces the player to go back to those levels and move the operations location (as moving operations also unlocks other areas). All of this is excess, because it forces you to remember a metric ♥♥♥♥-ton of routes, and could be easily remedied. I would say this in effect is The Surge's biggest flaw.

All in all, like I said I'd recommend the game if you enjoyed any of the Souls games from From Software. I would not buy it at full retail price however.\t\t\t\t\t

DISCLAIMER: This is a first impressions review, and NOT a full review

The Surge is a game developed by Deck 13, the same people behind Lords Of The Fallen and published by Focus Home Interactive. Deck 13 have had another go at the souls-like genre, and it's a better game definitely than Lords Of The Fallen. It's a challenging game, punishing even but never unfair and the progress feels just right

Combat feels good and weighted. It's also fairly unique that the gear and weapons you get are mainly sourced from cutting them from enemies. It's a nice system with plenty of scope for different builds and playstyles

The futuristic/apocalyptic setting is very well done and the visuals are excellent, with great animations and sharp texturing. The sound is also top notch. The level design is also very well done, however the game doesn't hold your hand what so ever so it can feel maze like at first

Size wise, it's a big game. Even after 10 or so hours, I'm still not even half way through it's post-apocalyptic world. The Surge is a huge step up from Lords Of The Fallen and if you like the type of games Dark Souls goes with, then The Surge has got your ticket to some bloodshed!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been called Soul-like enough times and there is definitely some soul in this one.

However in some aspects it is more, in some less.
You gain in generic level/power and not individual stats. Power is then converted to weapon effectiveness, armor, health recoverables and any number of other things in with implants which can be switched out at any time. Unlike a certain game you don't set yourself up for Mage/Archer/SwordAndBoard permanently but rather can (and sometime must) switch on the fly. Huge advantage for me.

Instead of fighting from save-spot to save-spot there is only one such point in a level and you progressively unlock more shortcuts, alternative routes and roundtrips. It makes the factory feel more like a real place instead of a disjointed set of environments that were haphazardly thrown together. This comes at the price of more difficult orientation and navigation. I sorely missed a map on occasion, but liked how the world was consistent.

Enemy variety depends on what you are looking for. They are mostly humanoids with a handful of robot types thrown in for good measure. They do tend to wear different gear and use different weapons and employ different move sets, so if you feel generous there is a very large number of different enemies. To me it feels like I have been chopping up the same bloke for the last 60 hours. Satisfying but slightly monotonous.

Bosses are largely amazing (except the very last one - I guess it took me 10 hours to get him right)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort review, as you can see I've put some hours into the game and it was my first "Souls" style game.

At the start it was hard to get a grip of things, but once you get into the "flow" of how the game works and learning the moves, the weapon types and their strengths and how to pair implants and armor it becomes a fantastic brawl. The screenshot function when you are doing a slowmo kill is nice too.

You are going to die, lots. But through those deaths you learn how to deal with each and every enemy type.

The robots you once feared, you now jump into and down them with 2-3 hits. You are still going to die, lots. (Sometimes on purpose).

The levels go from feeling huge to a bit smaller once you open the shortcuts, but I found them interesting to navigate and explore.

I've got all the achievements to date for this game, and loved every minute of it.

Definitely a game you should try!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I would recommend The Surge to those that enjoyed Lords Of The Fallen or Dark Souls. Having played them all, in my opinion this game is good enough to stand on its own. I favored exploring the vast complex of CREO. To me this was a unique setting. However, the tilesets can be repetitive. The variety of weapons keep the combat fresh but favoring one type of weapon for too long will make the combat feel repetitive. Blocking mechanic seems to be useless so dodging / evading is a must. The upgrade system transitions gear smoothly. My only complaint is because all gear requires Mk I components - backtracking to the beginning areas of the game is required at different points in time. For the price point of $19.99 however, it was worth the money to me.


+ Unique setting - vast complex of CREO to explore
+ Limb locking mechanics unique to genre
+ Challenge / Combat similar to that of Lords Of The Fallen and Dark Souls

- Environmental tilesets can become repetitive
- Combat can become repetitive
- Bumpy pacing\t\t\t\t\t
