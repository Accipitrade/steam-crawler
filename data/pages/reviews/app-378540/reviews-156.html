\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has one of the best core combat systems I have ever seen. Weapons feel weighty but not entirely unwieldy, actions are fast but require commitment, and if you ♥♥♥♥ up you can die in nothing flat.

Enemy variety and environmental variety is mildly lacking if you're used to Dark Souls, but individual enemies are higher quality and harder to beat than the ones Fromsoft serves up. The dog-like enemies introduced in the second area, for instance, managed to pose a challenge to me all the way to end-game.

There are only five boss fights, but all of them are at least reasonably challenging and interesting designs. The devs didn't feel the need to toss in "filler" boss fights, it seems.

Levels are insanely complicated, full of secrets and side-paths. There's only one Ops station (this game's version of a Dark Souls bonfire) per major area, meaning that every level is crammed to the brim with shortcuts back to Ops instead of new stations. One Ops station manages to actually serve as the checkpoint for TWO sections of the game, including two of the five bosses.

It's got a good story, but it is told in a very different way from the Souls series - it's much more traditional RPG feeling. Tastes may differ, but I personally really enjoyed picking up audio-logs and talking to NPCs to discover what was going on.

Overall, 8/10 would recommend to anybody who liked Dark Souls for the combat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: I did beat the game now, and can safely say that after the strong opening areas everything proceeds to go downhill until the very end. The final boss is kinda allright tho.

TLDR: The surge is not bad per se. I would actually say the first half of the game is pretty decent. After that, however the game falls apart rapidly and becomes a massive, frustrating mess. This,and a series of absolutely baffling design decisions make me not want to recommend this game.

This game prides itself in being a hardcore action RPG with high difficulty in a sci fi setting. And for the first two areas that formula works rather well. You dodge the mad shells that were you collegues, swinging around industrial equipment, chopping off body parts in a sprawling environment that invites you to explore and finding shortcuts that take you back to the ops that act as checkpoint. That part is fun.

As soon as you kill the first boss however you will realize that your enemies suddenly got massively upgraded. They no longer stagger. Their attacks will kill you in 1-2 hits. Their health pools got inflated. This sadly is the theme for the rest of the game. Your enemies' power level will rapidly increase while the growth of your character is heavily gated by upgrade materials. Leveling up your power core will barely do anything for you. This will lead to weird situation where you wail on your enemies with an industrial hammer the size of a man and barely scratching them only for them to power through your onslaught and deliver an enemic looking kick that will drain about 80% of your health.Sounds about right.

Speaking of enemy power- it is hillarious how effective their armor is. Armored body parts will gain up to 60% damage reduction and higher stagger resistance. This actually plays very well into this game's main mechanic of targeting specific body parts, as it directly encourages you to actively go for unarmored spots. This only gets incredibly weird when you get your hands on those armor pieces, and all of a sudden a piece of protective gear that could stop anti armor weaponry beforehand now has the defensive capabilities of a wet sheet of paper.

Damage in itself is also handled rather poorly. Dmaage types are split into three different physical attributes and a somewhat enigmatic 'elemental' stat. Elemental covering everyting from toxic gas to getting set on fire. Since phyiscal attacks are allways a mix of all three damage types ,there is no real feedback to which physical damage types are more efficient against which enemies, making the distinction somehwat pointless. Not as pointless as the elemental damage stat however. You see- the first wepaons that will deal elemental damage are available pretty much after you beat the second boss. On twin rigged and staff type weapons which most people wont have used extensively. Also it is AT THIS VERY POINT the game will throw mostly robots and humans in HAZMAT suits at you. Both of which are highly resistant to elemental damage.

Level design also takes a massive nose dive after the second boss. Open areas get traded for series of linear corridors with the occasional box shaped room thrown in. With the occasional really bad misstep. Like that one instance where a shortcut back from ops will lead you onto a small patch of dirt surroudned by acid and enemy ambushes waiting on both sides. Also enemy placement tends to go a bit overboard later on, making you fight groups of enemeis that can kill you with 2-3 hits frequently. This,paired with very long walks makes getting back to a point you died at a chore. After dying you will, as it is expected of these types of game, drop all of your currency which you can pick back up when you get to where you died. For some reason the devs decided to add a timer of 2 Minutes and 30 seconds to this process. If that timer runs out, your scrap is invariably lost. Between this and the massive amount of slow but powerful enemies, you will soon realize that simply running through the and ignoring a lot of the encounters is the best way to progress through the later levels. Also, expect a lot of 'lunge ambushes' where an enemy is covneniently placed out of your view behind some boxes or a doorframe only for the to hit you with a very powerful unblockable lunge attack if you walk past them. This is realtively likely to outright kill you. Dying to those allways feels incredibly cheap, but every area has at least one of those hidden somewhere.

The area where this game fails the msot however,are its bosses. Only one of them is somewhat decent. There is only five of them in the entire game. And one of them is used twice. But in hindisght that might have been for the best, as the ones they did come up with are all among the worst bosses i have ever fought. Every single one of them has periods of invulnerability where you need to wail on some part- or in one case another enemy entirely- in order to even make the boss vulnerable. This serves only to drag the fight out as long as possible. To add insult to injury here, every boss also deals massive damage to the point of them easily oneshotting you. Every single boss encoutner feels like a long, drawn out grind only designed to tire the player out until they make a simple mistake that immediately kills them. They aren't tests of skill but rather long, drawn out contests of atrition. And if that wasn't bad enough, every single boss also has a hidden prerequisite you have to fulfill in order to get their best weapon drop, which msotly consists of the player handycapping themselves.

All in all- the game confuses being 'hard' with incredibly handycapping the player and making them jump through tedious and frustrating hoops. There was potential in the beginning but terrible boss fights and a large amount of cheap deaths sour the experience to the point of it turning into a slow and frustrating grind.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRefunded this game.

The combat is just frustrating and unengaging. People draw comparisons to Dark Souls, but in that game the enemies attacks are telegraphed and you have a chance to outplay them, or outlevel them, or something. In The Surge, enemies ALWAYS 2-3 shot you, so your main goal is always rolling out of the way. Blocking is useless in this game. Heavy weapons are disgustingly slow and unsatisfying, and light weapons have no impact. This means you choose between painfully slow animations that leave you open to getting 2 shot, or fast animations where enemies can just attack you through them and kill you.

The game has a myriad of technical issues on PC. On a GTX 970 the game frequently drops to 30FPS from 60 in many situations. Even with all graphics options reduced to low or off, the technical issues remained. Clearly not optimised for PC at all, and a 970 is no slouch (Dark Souls 3 runs maxed out at 60fps, basically every other game is maxed and fine except this one).

Shame, I was looking forward to a futuristic souls-like, but this game is really just not worth the time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start off by saying, I have found some serious issues with the game that need to be addressed. So its a thumbs down for me.

Where to begin even... I wont waste your time explaining what this game is about or other games it compares to, thats already been done ad nauseum by literally everyone else and their mom. So straight into the meat of it then.

The blocking and dodging system is garbage. And even if its just because I havent figured out how to work it right or whatever, its still garbage. I have 12 hours into this game, lots of progression, a couple boss fights in, and I still cant block simple attacks and then sometimes, for no reason, I seem to block... like what is going on??

The attacking system is also garbage... I press the button once to swing my weapon and I end up throwing 3 or 4 combo swings, sometimes to my very own peril, often times with plenty of tech scrap on the line.

Leveling up does not increase your max health, so even at a super high level, the level 1 mobs can still one or two shot you, and oh, believe me, they will.

Speaking of mobs... the AI is absolutely horrible. I have not actually encountered a single mob that I have actually HAD to fight in order to progress in the game. I only end up fighting with them because I feel like I should have to... not that I actually do have to... which is super weird... Its completely broken in this regard. Literally the only time you HAVE to fight an enemy is when its a boss (obviously) or when one of the mobs like the ARC bot bugs out and literally blocks a super small pathway and gets stuck there.

Speaking of the ARC bots bugging out and blocking your path, the enemies are constantly bugging out. Sometimes you hit them with a certain weapon and weird things happen where the enemy goes flying or skidding across the room from a simple light attack... its very wonky and feels terrible when it happens. When an ARC bot bugs out and blocks your path, usually cornering you, usually you cannot even attack it. You will die. Its cheap, and poorly engineered.

The game is poorly optimized... I am running 4 GTX 680s, and an overclocked i7 4770k, with 32gb of ram, and I can only get away with 45fps on high settings. 20fps on ultra. To compare, Dark Souls III (and just about every other game I have) runs perfectly on maxed out settings.

Very little weapon and armor variety. Other games in this genre usually feature a multitude of tech paths you can choose... this game only has two paths, and very little variety to be discovered in the game world. What this means is, very little replay value. There are also no game changing decisons to be made either. So a play once, is a play 100 times.

Some things I do like about this game, is the theme, the animations, the level design (pure brilliance) and some elements of the otherwise terrible combat system such as the feel of the weapons having impact and weight. But with that being said, a "Very high impact" weapon will stagger enemies, unless you hit them in the wrong frame, then they will just power right through your massive hit on them like it was a paper airplane chipping their forehead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, so first off I want to say I have a bias towards games like this. I have spent many hours in DS, DS2, DS2 scholar, DS3, Bloodborne, and Lords of the Fallen. I recently beat the surge after 21hrs of gameplay so now I think I can give my opinion.

This game has some of the most fun combat I have ever seen in this type of game. Pulling or cutting someones limbs off for their gear is simply fantastic each time. The jumping and ducking mechanic when used on certain enemies feels incredibly rewarding when pulled off. However, this game has 2 glaring problems that makes it more of a chore than entertaining.

The first deals with the enemies themselves, now there are alot of LOTF esk features in this game such as The Champions rage bar, the "soul" multiplier, and the reviving enemies. However, this game has the exact opposite problem LOTF had. LOTF had really simplistic enemy designs with basic attack patterns (Think Dark Souls Lite) but its bosses seemed cheap and unfair at times usually leading you to cheese them out to progress. The Surge has the most ridiculous mobs I have ever seen. Most of the mobs in this game can 1 shot you regardless of armor (have tested fully upgraded LYNX and RHINO, on average the RHINO can sustain 1-2 more hits with a HUGE movement penalty). The bosses however are pretty fun, a little predictable but a fight you know will be tough but can still come out the victor. (Except 1 boss, random camera angle switch? What is this Nier Automata? lol)

Most of the small enemies are 10x tougher than ANY boss in this game and even other DS titles. Imagine if all the small mobs in bloodborne were replaced with Gascoigne (If any of u have played bloodborne). Its cool at first but after a while it becomes tedious to die to a new enemy youv'e never seen and have to fight ALL the mobs to get back to one guy and figure out in 3 secs what he did to kill you and how to dodge it. Im NOT against fighting an enemy multiple times to figure out how he works and eventually defeat him but if I have to fight 30 Gascoignes to get to the ONE guy I wanna fight it becomes very unfun. This leads me to the Duck/Jump feature. This feature works on 2 enemies throughout the entire game. Ones a Mini boss and the other is a regular mob. Pulling off one of these moves during an enemies attack and pushing in for a stun is fantastic! It makes you feel incredible, but it is utterly useless since you are given so much stamina that dodging left or right is safer and more consistant (Think of bloodborne... wow this game is kinda like that in terms of movement).

The second one I probably should have mentioned first but Im not re-typing this thing. The main problem with this game is the actual level design. I dont mean the interconnectivity, ive always enjoyed interwoven maps. I mean the color/art behind it. All of the areas minus the first and part of the... third I think... are the same mechanical industrial factories. Now I KNOW this is the style they are going for but to connect all the areas they use "tunnels" with lifts and doorways. The problem is the tunnels all have the same black tunnel/yellow guard rail design. They are almost impossible to tell apart from one another especially the dim lit ones. So on top of having to retrieve your lost "souls" from the new guy that one shot you while you were blocking, you have to navigate endless coridors all the same color and figure out where the ♥♥♥♥ to go. I really would have loved these tunnels to be color coded (think mirrors edge style) so I could tell one areas tunnels from the next since they are in all the areas. Ill be honest, the caticombs in LOTF was my least favorite area in the game, it was very similar in design and I would get lost down there all the time (Im not the best navigator) but I have completed all the DS games including bloodborne without an issue so im not that bad. Besides DS2, I did look up a guide for that one... Anyway Ill wrap this up with the pro's in a final thoughts paragraph and TL:DR for those that dont wanna read this... I know I wouldnt.

Final Thoughts (MY OPINION): This game has ALOT to offer with fleshed out combat, an interesting world, and weird people you meet along the way. Its problems lie in its repetitive art design/impossibly hard to navigate levels (Though I am not the best navigator), along with standard mobs that could be end game bosses. I really enjoyed the first 10 hrs of this game up until the 2nd boss. The weapons were interesting although few in nature along with armor sets not really giving you much in terms of actual armor lol. The injections/implants system is a really cool idea that kinda changes up the way you play without having to include a class system. All-in-all if you guys loved bloodborne/a really fast DS than this game might be for you. I would wait for a sale tho, as someone who isnt very good but has played all the DS games, I beat this in 21hours when most of that was me trying to navigate the nightmare hellscape that is The Surge. I would wait for a sale, this game was fun but not $50 fun. Its definitly better than lords of the fallen but id wait till its around $30. I doubt Ill go back to it unless they add some different or more interesting weapons and maybe tone down the 1 shots. Also PC port is good! I5 and 1060.

TL:DR- This game is essentially SciFi BloodBorne, Fast satisfying combat, Fun Bosses, Interesting story, Regular mobs that can out DPS all the bosses in this game, and hard to navigate levels due to repetitive art (could be me though). I think $30 is a good price.

Hope this helps! Let me know if you have any questions!

BTW: This is MY opinion. Feel free to have your own! (rating it down until it drops in price) sux theres no, "its ok" rating lol.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, I'm really loving this game. Disclaimer: I haven't played any Souls games, so I don't know how they compare. Would I recommend it to everybody? No, not quite. Not that it's a bad game but because it's not for everyone. It's very hard and you will die a lot. You have to learn the enemy's patterns and how to properly approach the combat. If you rush in and try to combo enemies to death by button mashing, you will lose every time. Button mashing is absolutely not an option here. You have to learn the combos and choose your attacks wisely because if you just spam attacks, you'll get locked into combo animations that may get you killed. You have to choose and commit to your attacks. With that said, the combat system if very flexible and fair. If you die, it's pretty much your fault. Only a couple times did I feel that my death was unfair. Don't fret too much about dying though, you only lose your collected scrap, which you get from every kill and can be farmed again. When you die, you can collect your scrap again if you make it back to your body within the time limit of about 2min. Killing enemies along the way adds to the timer as well, and generously so. I've never once had an issue getting back to my scrap within the time limit...but I have died on my way back, losing 5k scrap or so in the process. It will happen, it's frustrating, but you move on. Everything else you collect though from implants (abilities and stat upgrades) to weapons, armor, and crafting components are all saved right when you pick them up, so when you die, you still have those. This system is very nice as the only thing you lose is the most abundant resource in the game that you can get from anywhere, so although you can get set back a little if you lose a bunch, you can always make it back, like the difference between your paycheck getting lost in the mail and you breaking a priceless keepsake.

Overall, I really like it. I have about 15hrs of playtime and just beat the second boss and got to the third area. The combat is great and really reminds me of Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild in some respects. Locking onto enemies and dodging/parrying work pretty similarly, as well as needing to keep your distance and lunge in for strikes in between enemy attacks. Even the way enemies can power through your attacks to do some facial deconstruction is very similar. The way you unlock shortcuts also feels kinda like a Zelda dungeon.

A tip for people to avoid some potential frustration. Don't just go with whatever weapon has the highest damage number. Attack speeds and combos are super important. The first powerful weapon you get from the first boss has a very high damage value compared to the rest, but it's so slow that you will probably have trouble with more agile enemies. I'd recommend experimenting with different weapons and, like BotW, don't just specialize in a single weapon type, otherwise you'll probably have trouble with some enemies.\t\t\t\t\t
