\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat futuristic souls-like. I found out about this game from some reviers on YouTube and it didn't disappoint. If you're looking for games like Dark Souls with challenging (albeit not as challenging) combat and non-linear level design, then this is a great choice. If you're buying this specifically because of the comparison with Dark Souls, I'd say it's one of the best similar games out there. About as good as Salt and Sanctuary in terms of comparison, but better than Nioh (I wasn't a fan of the discrete levels in that game).

One caution that Surge is a bit short and doesn't have a ton of weapons, but I think it's just long enough, and some of the shortcuts you'll find are genius. There's one near the end that diverts you back to earlier areas perfectly in a nice organic way that allows you to pick up anything you missed and grab materials for upgrading if you need them.

Overall, a great game, lots of fun. If you come in expecting a souls-like to pass 20-30 hours, you'll be satisfied.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDark Souls III may be my favorite game of all time, but man I am sick of everything being 'souls-like'.


To be fair, a lot of this game IS like Dark Souls - difficult combat, frequent dying, collecting scrap/souls. Really though, it's a better Action RPG than souls-like. The combat is weighty and feels good, there are decent amounts of gear to be found/made, especially with the recent update. I do wish there was some sort of character customization or specialization you could work towards besides weapon proficiency.

All in all, it's not an overly deep story, the mechanics are easy to pick up, and the game looks and plays great. I'd definitely recommend it to anyone what wants to play an ARPG, but probably not to Souls fanatics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7/10

Pros:
- Visually appealing, great graphics.
- Great ability and armor customization concept
- Fun and challenging combat and boss fights. (Can die in 1 Hit)
- Can be played either with a controller or M&KB

Cons:
- Simple story plot.
- Poor characters growth in the story. You don't get to know well the characters in the story.
- Poor Soundtrack and background music sucks.
- Targetting system is bad. It takes time and a lot of practice before you can get used too. When it gets too crowded its really hard to kill enemies cos of the targeting system, may it be auto target or manual targetting.
- Bad camera angles sometimes on combat and even in boss fights.
- Lack of directional guides. The main character is equipped with high end technology exo suit with brain impants but it has no maps options. How sad.. :( The first thing I checked after I got the exo suit was the map but it has none. I was hoping I get one implant that shows map as I progress the game but nothing. The designers of this game must've really wanted this to be as difficult as possible.

Summary:
All in all it's a good game but it needs improvements, especially on the story and characters and some mechanics that I've mentioned above.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI give this game a recommendation with the clause you buy it on sale.
My synopsys of this game is: "It's alright".

More in-depth: There's nothing particularly broken in the game IMO. The combat is serviceable, the limb cutting is fun, the weapons look cool - but nothing really makes it to the point where it's very compelling. To me there's a few things really holding the game back.

1) I really dislike the sort of "rustic sci-fi" with chunky, scrapy designs that more things seem to be adopting. Dead space had elements of it, borderlands had a ton of it, and this game has it too. IMO it's just unappealing looking as the environments are quite literally just heaps of garbage.

There's nothing drawing the player with a sense of curiosity. Souls had gorgeous environments begging the player to explore and marvel at them despite the immense danger. The world was beautiful - at least for the most part. However, in the Surge there is nothing I want to go investigate. Big broken cement tubes, collapsed factories, dirt, patches of weeds, and chunks of metal collapsed everywhere. If I wanted to walk around broken factories with collapsed scaffolding I would just go visit Detroit for a weekend.

As far as I know, the environments do not change either. The souls games had incredible environmental and variety to keep it fresh - this just doesn't.

Further, this odd "mad max sci fi" look leads to a very odd juxtaposition in the lore where software and chemistry are so advanced you can implant chips in your brain that enhance your body, but mechanical engineering somehow stopped in the 1980s with hydraulic powered legs made of steel welded together. We have the technology to revive people from the dead but still haven't developed light-weight yet sturdy materials that are more practical than half a ton of steel on your legs?

I really think the game would have appealed to me more if it was a more sleek, advanced sci-fi theme with cool looking armor. Here you can really only choose between one chunk of dinged up scrap metal or another.

2) The combat could really use some tweaking. While it works, i just feel it's worse than the souls game. You cannot dodge out of any attacks. You cannot move while blocking. The arm lock-on sometimes does weird like you're aiming at the head and press down to target the body but instead it targets a leg. It just feels incredibly chunky, just like the rest of the game. I really wish some of the fluidity of souls combat was present.

In addition to this, the weapons are extrodinarily slow. I also don't really like the weapon proficiency scaling as it really discourages the player from trying new weapons - though that said there really aren't many weapon types, and what there are seem to all attack the same. Gone are the weapon specific movesets from the souls game, which really limits the utility of a weapon and that means you're basically just going to pick whatever gives the highest stats and use that.

I level these criticims cause there really is a good game in here, it just sucks it seems to fall short. It certainly does enough differently from souls and has some neat ideas - but in places where the comparisons are obvious it does worse than its inspiration in every single case.\t\t\t\t\t
