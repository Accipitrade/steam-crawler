\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs it possible to give a neutral review?

This game has a lot of potential. But it's also frustrating—and not in the good way you'd expect with a souls-like. I haven't used a walk-through tutorial since the days of BradyGames [oh wait, they still exist?], but some of the levels are so confusing. I also agree with the guy in said tutorial: you don't really learn after your deaths and having a timer counting-down until your dropped resources vanish is annoying (less so when you decide to just run straight to them).

And there were a few spots where you'd just fall to your death off a staircase during a fight like OSHA doesn't exist in the future. I will never forgive this game for those times.

Chopping-off limbs and heads and everything else is fun-enough, but don't ever get into a fight with multiple people because switching between those targets with little spacing is a pain, and sometimes a block just ain't gonna go your way. You can collect all those parts to build yourself a nice setup and continue killing in a Monster Hunter-esque grind. Murder. Murder. Murder.

Ultimately, it's like being in a Lyft or Uber with a five-star driver and them mistakenly missing a few turns. Does giving a four-star rating destroy their livelihood in some way? It's tempting to give a good review, but you also want to send a message. If only Gabe would let me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game and yes it has the "souls" feature of dying and dying again. But that is where the comparison should end. The Surge brings enough of its own unique ideas to the table without trying to be something it isn't. The melee combat is probably some of the best out there for heavier weaponry. Can't say I found the lighter weapons all that balanced, besides who wouldn't want to swing a gigantic hunk of metal around?

The story is interesting enough to carry you through and leaving you wondering what is happening with audio logs and journal entries.

There isn't really any ranged combat for the player however. There are enemies that are capable of ranged combat but the player can't interact or pick up these weapons even though just about everything else can be hacked off and utilised including armour and melee weapons.

I found the boss fights to be fairly ordinary and in fact most of the regular enemies you encounter will probably destroy you a lot more than the bosses. I found that armour had mostly a cosmetic effect and didn't really impact the amount of damage I was receiving.

Short, yet highly satisfying game. Get it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

You find gear and upgrade it similar to Dead Space and combat is similar to Dark Souls (heavy on the emphasis of dodge or else you'll get destroyed easily). The biggest downfall of this game, however, is that the plot starts strong but then it ends up being a little bit on the weaker side. A stronger plot would've easily made this game compete with AAA titles like the ones it borrows mechanics from but unfortunately the lack of solid story really takes away from the immersion of this game. Also, a silent protagonist would've been very welcome in a series like this. Overall, an 82/100.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had never heard of this game until the summer steam sale just scrolling through deals. I was hoping for a cheap game with decent combat, but what I got was so much more.
The combat: Similar in feel to Dark Souls III, I was able to quickly get comfortable with the combat.
Map: The maps are packed to the brim with shortcuts. Everything is interconnected, and reminds me of the map in the first Dark Souls. The maps have a great sense of verticality. From elevators bringing you to high areas to dark underground sewers, the map brings a lot to the atmosphere of the world.
World/atmosphere: There is a lot of tension created by this game's atmosphere. Corners hiding enemies from sight, encountering new enemies, and hallways with seemingly endless darkness are just a few examples of the way that the world draws you in and makes you feel like you are a part of it.
Unique mechanics: Acquiring new gear and crafting materials by chopping off the limbs of enemies makes this game stand apart from others in terms of progression and farming.

A lot of great things about this game. Devs took a risk in trying out some new, unique mechanics, and it paid off. That does not mean that the game is perfect though.
Cons:
You cannot teleport from one medbay to the next, like you can with bonfire in the Dark Souls series. This is extremely annoying considering the fact that you need to do quite a good bit of backtracking.
Enemies are slightly buggy. Nothing gamebreaking, but the ai can be really lousy at times. Sometimes they get stuck in walls, sometimes they just don't attack, sometimes they run away from you. Nothing that makes the game harder, just very minor annoyances here and there.
NG+ adds little to no content. I am enjoying my NG+ playthrough just because of how much I love this game, but it would be nice to have an actual reason to go into NG+, like upgraded versions of armor, weapons, and implants.
Difficulty curve felt like it was all over the place. Some of the enemies leading up to the final boss were more difficult than the boss itself. I felt that P.A.X., the first boss, was significantly more difficult than Big Sister, and Black Cerberus was hilariously easy.

I think I've listed more cons than pros, but I still absolutely recommend this game. I am incredibly excited for The Surge 2, and hope that some of the issues with the first game are addressed.\t\t\t\t\t
