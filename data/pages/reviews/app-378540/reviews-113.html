Most enemies in this game seem to have Goku's instant transmission...
Basically enemies will bug around on screen and sometimes just dissapear mid fight and re appear in the location they spawn in fully healed (I know how leashing works, trust me this isn't leashing.)
Anything that is supposed to go right in this game hasn't happened to me, the first 'mini boss' I encountered spammed the same spin move continously until I reset the game, and even then it still abused it, I had to resort to standing on a sandy hill every spin and then smacking its open panel like twice... rinse and repeat.
Then the first actual boss (if you can call it a boss) requires you to hit it until it shoots itself to death with its own missiles... I didn't think there could be an enemy more boring than the spin to win mini boss... I was wrong.

I equipped them for a total of over 55 core power and still couldn't open the level 55 core power door, because this game is a complete mess, and even its own mechanics don't work properly.
The only positive thing I can say about this game is visually it looks awesome, anyone looking for a cool robot souls like game should continue looking because this is far from that experience.
3/10 wouldn't scrap again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI liked it a lot! Bought it on launch day and had a great time with it. It's hard to talk about the game without mentioning that it's a Souls-like game because it is.

What sets it apart from Souls is the futuristic setting and the ability to target specific enemy limbs. The level design in the early sections is amazing with lots of shortcuts to discover, but later on I had a difficult time navigating the same-y hallways and getting confused by the same-y elevators.

Multi-player would've added more value, but I feel I got my money's worth at full-price. Check out the demo if you're not sure, or pick it up on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
