\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCons: Gameplay feels clunky, the trageting system was an okay effort at best and difficulty scaling is terrible and missguided.
Bland environments and badly designed bosses, on top of only having 5 total bosses in the whole game. Enemies get recycled over and over again. Just the first area alone has the same enemy recycled 50+ times as opposed to dark souls where enemies vary from area to area never having a feeling of repetitiveness and also poses a refreshing chalenge as enemies differ in their movesets and behaviour around the player. The story is forgetable, great settup and great vision but ultimately fails to deliver. The different power amours are great but you will be using mostly one kind as you upgrade it to the max and forget the rest. ABSOLUTELY do not pick heavy class, you will regret it. The ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ music at the med station... ugh. Progression feel very weighted and lacking any feel of rising to power over time. Enemies will eat through most of the armor given in the game just as easily as the starting zone enemies.

Personal cons: THE ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ CRASHES. I have GTX90ti i54690k 4.3GHZ Z87 PRO mobo 32GB of RAM and STILL get periodic crashes out of nowhere. I will be playing then the screen freezes, I can still hear my guy getting attacked and then as I die the game force closes on me. This made me livid multiple times especially backtracking deathless money hauls.

Pros: Semi fun combat, the finishers are pretty cool. Fantastic level detail and absolutely mind blowing graphics. Seriously, I would stand around just taking screenshots/ looking around at the detail and effort put in into these warehouse corridors and space age like rooms. The story has some meat to it, the main character is forgetable but his introduction feels good and powerful albeit lacking any sort of emotional attachment from the player. Great looking armour. I absolutely love the exo suits in this game, but as I said earlier, you'll mostly use one kind really.

I would wait for a sale on this one. 6/10 for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game. I really did. But it's such a piece of work....

-When you quit the game halfway through a level and load it up another day, the enemies will respawn and you won't get your regeneratives back. What....? Might as well cut the BS and place me back at the checkpoint, then.
-Regeneratives and utility items all share the same 8 equipment slots. You don't even get all of the slots until you level up a ton. It's hard to relate how bad this feels.
-Heavy weapons seem unplayable.
-You're CONSTANTLY worried about aggroing more than 1 enemy. Trying not to alert any enemies while fighting just one is half the difficulty in this game. Dark Souls 2 gets a lot of flak, but I enjoyed that game a ton and part of the reason is because it presented a lot of group encounters which were actually pretty fun once you learn to stop relying on lock-on. This game isn't designed like that. If you aggro more than one guy, you're swimming up against ****'s creek. And you CONSTANTLY have this threat going against you. A lot of the fighting could be actually enjoyable if you weren't forced to limit yourself into a tiny spot, for fear of aggroing another enemy.
-Not a lot of enemy variety.
-Some mobs are just a nightmare to have to kill - basically feel like minobosses - and really shouldn't be respawning.
-The first boss was awfully designed. The second one was alright. Uninstalled before the 3rd one.
-There are drone enemies in the game that aren't too dangerous, but since they're flying, they're a gigantic hassle to kill, as you're very ill equipped to handle them.

Overall, don't buy this above $30. The game's neat, but it could be so much better. The one pro I could say is that the visuals and atmosphere are actually REALLY good. But not good enough to save the game from the awful, "hardcore because Dark Souls is hardcore, right?" design. Dark Souls isn't about difficulty as much as it's about the adventure. It's painful that so many devs don't get this.\t\t\t\t\t

- Meaty, visceral combat

- Exploration is rewarded with gear, lore tidbits, and shortcuts
- NG+ adds new, tough enemies and lets you upgrade you gear past MK. V
- PC port runs well and has a lot of options; multiple gamepad faces supported natively

- Level design is sometimes confusing
- Promising story takes a backseat after the first area

Cons:
- Ugly gear
- MG Gorgon set bonus outpaces almost every other set fairly easily
- Some enemy placements are cramped or ganky
- Queued combos can leave you out of place if you're not careful

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGiven the experience the developers should have obtained from making a Dark Souls clone like Lords of the Fallen, I expected The Surge to bring new, interesting and fun things to the table. Well, I was very, very wrong.

They tried to incorporate elements from LOTF like the XP multiplier based on how long you're holding "souls" aka tech scrap aka experience points, and bring in new mechanics like the dismemberment thing, where you acquire new parts by dropping a blueprint from dismembered chunks from enemies, but that's about it. The progression in the game is so strongly gated, there's absolutely no real feeling of progression. Even though the dismemberment mechanic is interesting, it also limits the gear you acquire based on the segment of the game you are in. Even upgrades to already owned parts come from scraps with varying quality that is also based on where you are in the game, limiting options even more. "Leveling up" is a joke, as the player can only level up their "core", which doesn't really do anything besides ocasionally increasing the capacity for heavier parts or making so called "implants", which are very minor customizations you can make to your character(like slightly more hp, healing packs, etc), scale a bit better. The combat is messy, slow and confusing. Heavy weapons take WAY too long to connect and leave your character vulnerable, while lighter, faster weapons don't stagger enemies nearly as much and their combo system makes the player feel just as little in control, with neither dealing considerable damage, especially when compared to how much damage the enemies deal and how fast and unfair they are to fight against.

I don't recommend this game to anyone looking for a new twist on the still so very popular soulslike games, as it doesn't have one, and is, in fact, one of the worse and less compelling ones, and don't even recommend it if you're just looking for an interesting, fun and challenging action game. I might even take a risk by saying it manages to be a downgrade in terms of fun and progression when compared to Deck13's previous uninspired attempt at a Dark Souls game, Lords of the Fallen.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay:
The combat emphasizes aggression, making the game a bit more like Bloodborne than Dark Souls. As you get further in the game you'll handle battles with limited tactics: hit & run, pure aggro, and block/counter. Overall, the combat is fun, but the damage types could have been more exaggerated and the vertical/horizontal attacks don't make much of a difference (I plowed through NG+ barely using vertical strike).

The bosses are a mixed bag... The first one is fairly basic, another is hectic, and a couple are just slogs. Interestingly, you can earn improved weapons from beating bosses in special ways.

NG+:
Unfortunately, this was a weak implementation of NG+. Some new enemies are placed into the game (just existing enemy types with mismatched armor). The new enemies seem to have improved combat tactics, but they're a fleeting experience. NG+ was easier than the first run, with the exception of the last boss. Granted, some of this is due to learned tactics, but the gear scaling isn't quite on par (you scale a little too well).

The level design is good, but very linear. Thankfully, it doesn't feel completely linear, as the levels are built as a series of connected loops.

Story:
The main plot of the game is pretty weak, it's typical sci-fi tropes. The biggest weakness in the storytelling is that the game feeds you too much information, leaving little space for imagination. It starts off strong though, leaving you wondering wth is going on... but as you progress the game force feeds you too much information.

The strongest storytelling in the game is environmental. Everywhere you look is some tiny story about how others handled the events taking place. There's bits of information about how people lived before the incident as well... There's just a lot of character in the environments.

Audio:
Not much to say here, it's good overall. There's a few music transitions between areas that could have been better and a few audio glitches with executions (button press sounds).

Graphics:
It looks good and runs well on my old PC. I never noticed any frame rate problems while I played the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game makes me feel like I'm part of Daft Punk

You scoot around in your sleek new robo-booty through freshly apocolyzed settings, listen to heavy synth beats as you groove through industrial, laboratory, executive, and sci-fi horror landscapes, and are constantly collecting "samples" from your groove-less foes in order to mix them into a kick-ass new album.

Seriously though, this game is a glorious mixed bag of spectacular aaaaand less than great things. Please don't make the mistake that this is a "souls-clone". It takes gameplay elements from that glorious franchise, but it does not imitate. It improves and twists the formula. I won't be comparing this the DS in my meanderings.

The Combat is ON POINT. It places a heavy emphasis targeting specific points on enemies to either hit weak spots OR collect new gear from your enemies. Each weapon type has it's own very specific moveset and rather deep combo set. For instance, the twin-rigged (dual-wield) has your character balancing pin-point strikes to vital areas, while using a flurry of blows to quickly stagger or escape from a sticky situation, Where as the heavy-duty weapons focus on the concept of "one hit" where the slow moveset is tactically balanced between high dmg, stagger, and increased stability to incoming blows on your end. The Combat system is deep enough that a perceptive player can easily switch to any class of weapon and ultimately feel at home with it.

The combat system is paired with the concept of collecting gear from enemies by damaging their limbs, and executing them in a "finisher" move. This keeps combat on a constant "risk vs reward" system where you'll always be thinking " I can easily defeat this robo-zombie by hitting his unguarded head, buuuuut I really want his Jordans". The armors themselves all carry passive bonuses from wearing a full set ( i.e. increased attack speed, passive healing buff, badass-soundtrack). Sadly, this does not encourage being fashionable (which is the endgame of all games).

The environments are all predicated on the concept of "this is a high-tec future corporation". That being said, you will be scooting around incredibly awe-inspiring massive construction platforms one minute, and navigating increasingly claustrophobic and maze-like conduit tunnels the next. The almost rapid transition between the two will keep you on your toes and constantly give you the feeling of paranoia and gritted determination. Let me tell you, if there is a concept of "shortcut-porn", this game has mastered it. You will be finding small holes, access panels hidden from the light, caved-in entries, and other random routes that all miraculously lead you back to your hipster-fueled Ops center ( save point).

A few of my gripes with the game are:

1. That the enemies can sometimes be laughable, or ungodly difficult. There are no trash mobs in this game, every enemy is essentially a mini-boss and can wreck you if you don't respect them. The late game areas become LITTERED with heavy armored, proton-axe wielding psychos and ulti-death eldritch abominations. That's par-for the course, I suppose.

2. The environment can get too-maze like at times. Sometimes, I would just have to sit down and think of where the ♥♥♥ i was, just because i couldn't find that ONE DOOR that i opened literally minutes ago. Once all the shortcuts are opened, getting to anywhere in the level is quick and easy....but until then, good luck.

3. Lack of character. I like the premise, I like the setting, I like the interactions with the auxilliary character, but maaaan being stuck with Warren is kind of a bummer. It very much limits your RP options to Vanilla warren, Pissed-off Warren, Warren on holiday, Tom-Cruise, or Ripley in a Power-loader, or maybe DAFT PUNK.\t\t\t\t\t
