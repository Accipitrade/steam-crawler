Pros:
  • Combat offers a lot of opportunities for optimization
  • You can rebuild your character(weapon/armor/implants) very easily
  • Lots of weapon/armor choices
  • Weapon vertical/horizontal combos can make or break your weapon choices
  • Getting new weapons/armor pieces involves a fun mechanic that changes up combat
  • Lots of ways to build your character
  • Different weapon types play very differently
  • Characters are diverse and involved in worldbuilding
  • World is very immersive
  • Quest designs are usually more than just fetch-quests
  • Level designs make sense and map exploration is highly rewarding
  • Enemy designs are pretty varied
  • Game mechanics are explained in straightforward way, often through gameplay
Cons:
  • It's easy to "play on easy mode" (hit, retreat. wait. hit, retreat). You won't die but it'll take forever. N/A in NG+
  • Weapons within each weapon type are pretty similar
  • Many of the armor sets are very situational
  • Mixing armor sets is generally suboptimal
  • Difficulty is somewhat inconsistent - sometimes you'll fight lots of easy enemies and get blindsided by a very difficult/Elite enemy
  • If you can't switch off "Dark Souls mode" in your head, you will play the worst way possible(see above) and dislike The Surge!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed The Surge for my time playing it, but when I put it down for a day or so I found no interest continuing playing. I will be comparing this series to Dark Souls as it takes heavy influence from, unfortunetly I don't have anything else to compare it to.

The Good aspects of The Sure:
-Great setting
-Runs well, steady frames throughout. Smooth
-Intersting concept
-Targeting is a novel idea
-Combat is fun for a while
-Quick runs back to bosses
-Lots of shortcuts

The Bad:
-Clunky combat
-Enemies sometimes seem faster than player
-Enemies able to hit through your own attack combos, even with high impact
-Enemies have tracking on their attacks
-Story is pretty lackluster and empty
-Npcs seem to have little to no purpose
-Bosses are not quite a challege
-Clunky hitboxes

My main issues rely in the combat, too often I felt that regardless of my own actions my death wasn't due to my own inability to act or respond. Dark Souls isn't infallable of this but the vast majority of the time I felt i didnt do something right, where as with The Surge too often I felt the cause of death was unfair and out of my control. Targeting body parts proves to be fun in the begginning and a neat idea to aquire new armor/weapons, but when an enemy is entirely armored or the part you removed doesn't drop after a time it seems kind of unneeded. The surg seems to promote quick and heavy combos to kill your enemy, however many times i found waiting for the enemy to quit flailing about slow and laborious. The pacing just felt kind of off. The game also does a poor job of explaining all the combos/attacks you can do. It just simply labels them as "horizontal" and "verticle" attacks, while it isn't a very deep game it's deeper than it lets on.

The camera while not being horrible did prove to be often irritating and at weird angles. During Boss fights some of the bosses are off your screen when you move in to attack, making it very difficult to anticipate their moves and look for tells on certain attacks. While not impossible, or glaringly bad it proved an issue to me multiple times. One fight inparticular forces your camera to a perspective youve never had before which could be jaring.

By no means is The Surge a bad game, if you are looking to try out Souls-style this would be a good place to start. Its a decent game but just not for me. I'm most likely nitpicking at things that aren't a huge deal but having been a Souls plyer for some time the problems I had with the game were glaring. No pretentious tone intended. As I said I had fun, just not enough to keep me going through it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is another Souls-like, but in a futuristic setting and with an exclusive game mechanic: the system of body parts targeting. You can choose to target a specific body part of an enemy, to inflict more damage if he has no armor there, or to cut a piece of armor to recover it and be able to make it for yourself (or collect resources). The rest of the content is already seen.

However, know that the game has very few bosses, the Level Design is sometimes a little "sad" because of the industrial factory environment, and the balancing isn't perfect because some parts of the game are really too difficult for nothing. I recommend it, but get ready to sometimes be frustrated with this game, in the bad sense of the word, not because of the Souls-like inspiration.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7/10

Decent game, but missing the 'soul' that made Souls.

The levels are too constrained. Death is too quick.Enemies 'activate' into attacks too quickly. Blocking mechanic isn't well explained, and too fincky to be useful. Armor and weapons and varied, but not actually different. Either dodge in, R1-R1, dodge out or wait for animation, impact punish. Armor is worse and doesn't seem to have very drastic differences on play. Goliath and Operator feel exactly that same when the combat options are as limited as above. Implant system is interesting, but getting set-up with 'explore' implants, finding a boss, changing implants, beating boss, and re-seeting up 'explore' implants is a bit of a chore. There is also consistency issues where a tactic that works on a monster 90% of the time will get you punished at random. Makes praying to the RNG much to much of an issue for a game of this type.

All in all, a bit step forward from Lords of the Fallen. Next game should focus on consistent combat and more varied play styles. My Angel VI rigged Englehart build should fell 110% different than my Liquidator rigged Ignis build. I won't be beating this, as the chore to 'get gud' ratio is way off.

Also, random areas without railing leading to pits. I shouldn't be punished for walking too close to the bottom left corner of stairs. Yes, if I chose to fight on a narrow viaduct over an expanse please push me in, but don't make the run back fraught with 'lazy designer' traps.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those game i hate, that i absolutely love.

The game is like Lords Of The Fallen and Dark Souls. But with a big twist. Here you can target individual body parts. Chop them off and pick up the armor parts from that body part, and stick it on your own Exoskeleton.

The gameplay is fantastic, even with the few minor bugs i have found, like using different executions than chosen, and not getting the weapon the target was holding. Or sidestepping away from an enemy, just to fall trough a tiny hole in the ground and insta-dyeing.

Another irritation is that after beating the final boss, you are instanly pushed to play NG+. There is no option to go back and loot, or find secrets that you missed.

It took me 42 hours to finish the game + the DLC completely. All secrets found, and every gear and weapon gathered. Plus leveling my power to 105, and maxing all gear at lv.5. (In new game plus you max out at 10, so you are back to beeing a weakling if you start over in NG+)

Overall i really love the game. There could have been a bit more story, a map and a journal. So you don't get lost all the time. But overall i really enjoyed this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge starts off absurdly strong. You've got an immensely mood-setting intro, great art design, good voice acting, good music and ambient sounds, etc. it really just makes you FEEL that you're in a kind of unsettling, semi-horror situation... and then it drops the ball. A lot of the horror is lost as a result of the third-person perspective, which robs you of the fact that you're more or less fighting semi-conscious zombies, whose brains have been fried by their cybernetic implants. Occasionally you get a bit of it and it manages to overcome even the third-person perspective, but it's rare.

Secondly, there's the fact that you're going to be going through everything repeatedly. Either because you died to a boss, or because you needed to grind(and oh boy will you need to grind). Or just because you reloaded. As far as I can tell every area has a grand total of one save/respawn/re-equip point. So you're going to be seeing the nearby environs a LOT. Fundamentally not an issue, gameplay-wise, because you'll be unlocking a lot of shortcuts as you go through each area but it still undermines what could have been an INCREDIBLY strong mood.

Speaking of repetition undermining the mood, the game, of course, has ambushes. But it keeps re-using them, even with the SPOOKY MUSIC STINGS, every time. It's like, yes, this is the fifth time I've seen that collapsed robot and I know it's going to get up. Yes, this is the fifth time I've been down this hallway, I know a drone is gonna smash through those boxes and try to pry my eyes out, etc. it feels a bit tone deaf.

And the problem here is that the mood is really what the game has going for it. The fundamental combat loop isn't bad... it just gets incredibly repetitive, which isn't helped by the ABYSMAL level design. I mean, the levels LOOK nice, but it's like someone just scribbled some lines on a piece of paper at random in terms of layout. This is a serious issue because, after the first level, you encounter a couple that are 90% indoors, which means you have ZERO points of reference when trying to orient yourself.

Then an NPC very helpfully urges me to hurry to the Executive Forum(tm)! Well that's good except, uh. Where is it. I literally can't tell if I need to go back a level to find the connection there, whether I need to search the current level more or something entirely third. This is in general how any quests in the game will go: "I need a thing!" or "you need to go to a place!" and then you stumble around hoping you find it because there are zero pointers whatsoever. The only signage is to nearest elevator or back to the save point.

The Surge has the underpinnings, story-wise and mood-wise, to be a really effective System Shock-esque sci-fi horror game. It's just a shame that the actual gameplay tries to be something entirely different and it just doesn't work.

I'd recommend against buying The Surge, it'll impress you for one hour and then leave you regretting the rest.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGames like the Dark Souls series have extreme difficulty down to a science. Sure, I die a lot, but I feel like it's my fault everytime I do. I feel like my death was due to something I did wrong.
The Surge positions enemies in blind corners constantly, so that they can spring out in an attack before you even have a chance to see them. It wouldn't be so bad, but there's not a single one that can't drain your healthbar in an quarter second. It becomes a game of advancing a bit, dying, and carefully engaging the ♥♥♥♥ that suckerpunched you.
For someone wearing two tons of highly advanced power armor, I feel like an anemic twelve-year-old wearing a tissuepaper dress. The player's attacks plink off enemies, having very little visual effect or feeling of weight. Even the largest weapons in the game just feel slow, not heavy. The enemy has no trouble powering through your combos, but most of the time your massive, rig-mounted warhammer barely seems to affect them. Meanwhile, the player can be tossed around like they weigh nothing.
One-shots, stunlocks and being boxed in a corner all feel like they happen way more often than they should.
Both dodging and blocking take enough stanima that they're next to useless. They're also slow and don't seem to cancel attacks. It's actually more effective to flail at an enemy as long as you can, then backpedal than it is to try anything involving timing or skill.
If you're the sort that can deal with an end less string of frustration, the game is pretty fun. When you're allowed to actually play it, that is, and not sitting there looking at a death screen.
I purchased this on sale and I'm unfortunately still chalking it up as a waste of money.
