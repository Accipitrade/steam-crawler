\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this game for an hour, but allow me to head off the people who don't think that is enough time to understand a game. If I am not enjoying my first hour of a game why would I spend more time playing it? I am not here to suffer through a bad start for a possible later pay off.

So why don't I like it?

The developers put way too much time in making an elaborate upgrade system, and making the combat technical, instead of worrying first if it was fun. The combat is not fun, it's a tedious chore to kill even the most basic enemy. My character feels sluggish and uncoordinated, and I picked the agility/speed class. This is the combat: you do your little 2-3 hit combo, wait for the bad guy to flounder about with his and either move back or block, and repeat your combo. Eventually the bad guy dies, or you hack his arm/leg/head/robot penis off for a possible upgrade. You beat on basic scrub enemies for minutes each, and if you don't you can't improve your "rig".

So let's talk about that for a second too, because it goes back to the crux of what's clearly wrong with this game. So much time was put into a system letting you craft your own gear, upgrade it, and toggle around implants that may or may not matter. All this is great yeah, but if I don't want to play your game past the first few areas it doesn't matter. I do not want to put up with several hours of fighting boring crap to level/gear up my character just so I can hopefully fight some fun stuff in a fun way.

I get it, you want to build the plot and scale the gameplay; but you might want to add some actual plot and character building to the game so i want to see it advance. This game starts out super generic, you roll your character's wheelchair off a train after some speech about the company changing the world. Then you pick if you want to be a beefy tank class or an agile speed class. You see a cutscene where they drill a bunch of techno-♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ into your body and make sure it's known it's a big deal they didn't use anethesia. You then wake up in a garbage dump and carrot-on-a-stick your way to retrieve items to advance the plot. Some ethnic lady in glasses says things are bad, and doesn't know why. What a plot, ladies and gentlemen.

The devs took a few things from Dark Souls, and even invite the comparison. This was a mistake, but if they wanted it they can have it. Dark Souls was fun from the beginning. It had a challenge, but flowed. I wanted to explore this strange world, and I wasn't shoved in a specific direction to do so. Dark Souls has upgrades, but it only had a couple layers to it and the game was focused on fun gameplay. The Surge on the otherhand dumps the fun for an upgrade system, and puts you on rails to finish a generic plot.

Hard pass on this one folks. Especially for $30/$50 depending on when you saw it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're a fan of the Souls series you'll like The Surge.

Heavily inspired by Dark Souls, but by no means a cheap knock-off.

The combat is incredibly satisfying. Instead of the souls 1-2 style weapon combos, The Surge features an actual combo system. By combining vertical and horizontal attacks (equivalent to Light/Heavy attacks in DS) you can create some really cool 4 or even 5 hit combos. The tradeoff here is that there are 6 classes of weapon, and each class shares the same combos with only slight variation between weapons if any.

There's a big focus on targeting limbs and using executions to get specific gear parts for crafting, the executions are brutal and fun to watch, if you like them as much as me you can set them to be "slo-mo cinematic" every time you use them, sometimes, or rarely. If you're not into the whole finishing moves thing, you can turn that off and just let them play as a part of your combat.

I personally like the gear system, most of it looks really cool and they encourage wearing whole sets together for set bonuses. You gather schematics from enemies and craft the gear yourself, so you don't have to worry about not finding a matching set. The interface for crafting isn't particularly engaging so if gathering materials isn'y your thing you may not like it.

Don't get me wrong, the game IS difficult, but for a reason I can't place I found it easier to defeat enemies after you figure them out, very much the same way I felt about DS1. Like, the enemies will definitely whoop your ass until you really get them down, but once you have fought the same enemy enough times they literally become a non-threat, even the harder ones. I didn't really feel that way about DS2&3, there were plenty of enemies that would destroy you without careful timing and quick reflexes no matter how many times you had fought them before.


The game seems to have a decent length, I'm 22 hours in at the time of this review and I'm definitely less than a third of the way through. As of yet I can't really speak for environmental variety, but it seems fairly bland in that respect. It's hard to make unique environments when the game has such a niche theme. I don't hold it against the game personally, I think it makes up for it in other respects, but it's definitely something to consider.

I bought it on sale for 29.99, and I have seen it on sale plenty of times since, I'd say it's a definte recommend at the sale price, but for 49.99 I would say it's really only a worthy buy for hardcore fans of this genre.\t\t\t\t\t
