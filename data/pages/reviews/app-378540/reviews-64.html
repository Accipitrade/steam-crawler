\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game had a few problems but is overall pretty enjoyable. The biggest issue in my opinion is the repetitiveness of the game. You're introduced to new foes from time to time but you'll have to fight each of them a lot. And once you're used to them, it will pretty much always go the same way. The feature of cutting limbs also becomes very repetitive. The idea of chasing specific parts to improve your gear is fun but the act of constantly doing it on every single trash mob you see becomes very tiring.

The game has a couple of other issues (easy to get lost, slow story, the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ light on your first suit) but nothing too bad.

For the good part.

I loved exploring the maps. They have a lot of secrets and shortcuts to discover. The first two ones were amazing. The first is quite simple but well done. The second is mind blowing.

The look is great. The whole factory, robotic, exoskeleton style is very cool.

I liked the bosses quite a lot. (At least the first 3 ones.) They were a nice break from the regular mobs and a fun challenge.

I like how easy it is to switch your build around. You don't have too many options but still a couple of nice things to choose from as the games goes on.

Overall, a game much simpler than it looks. Amazing levels to discover and fine gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had a lot of fun playing this game. Not the first sentence you'd expect from a negative review, but it's true. The thing is, I've also had a lot of frustration and annoyance, and I'm fairly certain the amount of times I've said "F*** THIS GAME" is much higher than the number of moments of joy and satisfaction.
So, seeing as Steam has no "meh" button, I've decided to go with negative.

Now, why did I find this so frustrating and annoying? You're probably guessing it's because the game is hard - Dark Souls-like combat with bosses that require endless attempts to defeat and serious punishment for death. Well, no. That wasn't it. It was pretty much everything else.

First - a disclaimer: I haven't played Dark Souls. Don't know if I'd like it. But from what I've seen, it looks like Dark Souls can get away with a lot of the things it does, while this game can't.
First off, no minimap. In Dark Souls, the environments are different, each area has it's own unique design, sometimes you're out in the open sky and can see the view, tell where you're headed, and remember the layout. In The Surge though, everything looks the same. You spend half the game in tunnels that look exactly the same, fighting enemies that look exactly the same, and the level layouts get more and more confusing as the game progresses. It gets so bad that even if you give up and go online looking for an explanation on how to get where you need to be, you'll find threads where people genuinely struggle to give instructions because everything just looks exactly the same.

Next up we have the story. To be perfectly honest, even after finishing the game, I'm not 100% sure what the story was, or why things happened the way they did. I didn't care about any of the characters, and even the moments that were supposed to spark some emotion fell very flat. So that's not something you're going to find here.

The combat was pretty fun. It's your regular stamina-based combat with blocking and dodging (though honestly I gave up on blocking very early on as it takes up more stamina and isn't very useful. Dodging alone is 100% enough to beat the game), but there's a bit of a twist. To upgrade you gear, you gotta chop armor/weapon parts off of enemies, which means targeting their armored limbs rather than the unarmored one. That forces you to choose - make the combat harder by attacking armored parts, removing knockback and doing less damage, or make it easier on yourself and attack their bare bits but get no materials. It's an interesting mechanic that is used quite well, and is the main thing the game has going for it imo.
Boss fights weren't very interesting to me. Maybe I was expecting too much, seeing as this is a Souls-like and I've heard so much about how hard the bosses in Dark Souls are, but whether that's true for dark souls or not, it's not true for this game. Bosses aren't very hard, they just have a "catch". Maybe you gotta run between their legs while they're shooting you, maybe you gotta attack only after a specific attack, maybe you gotta trick them into shooting a part of the environment, etc. After a few tries, with the incredible power of trial and error, you'll figure it out, and that's that. Boss fights don't require you to be quick or precise, just patient. As a result, while they do take several tries, the victory isn't actually all that satisfying. Compared to, for example, Hollow Knight (which I really enjoyed), that required genuine skill, speed and precision, this type of boss fight is pretty lame.

And last but not least, there's just some stupid annoying crap all over the game design. For example, when you die fighting the final boss, you respawn facing the exit of the area. Naturally, your first instinct is to immediately run forward after respawning to get back in the fight - Which moves you OUT of the area and into a loading screen which you have to wait through before turning around and waiting through another loading screen. God that was annoying. Another annoying thing is that, at the end game, you get blueprints for better weapons and armor, but to craft them you need Mk.1 materials which you can only get at the starter area. So you literally have to run ALL the way back to the start if you want to craft them. WTF. I guess they're just given to you so you can build them during NG+ or something, but f*** that.

So yeah, that's that. As you can see, I enjoyed parts of it, I stuck around till the end (even though I genuinely had to force myself to get through the final section), but overall, I can't recommend it. There are so many Dark Souls clones out there that even if that's your thing, you're probably better off playing a different one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t+ The Surge takes the Dark Souls formula and transforms it into a Sci-Fi world with endless options to customize your build to your play style.
+The combat is very advanced and can be challenging for the first couple of hours, but it's smooth sailing from then on out. It keeps you engaged and forces you to adapt to each enemy move set in order to maximize your strike time windows , but there is very minimal grind and it never feels like a chore.
+The level designs are absolutely fantastic (although sometimes confusing, especially in the ventilation shafts), and enemies and boss designs are a breath of fresh air.
+The game has really pretty graphics

~ There are a few NPC quests, though the interactions can feel a bit lackluster and the world may feel lonely sometimes (but perhaps this was the intentional).

- Frame rates drops in the last area, and the enemies there sometimes glitch.
- Confusing and unintuitive back tracking is required, both to access hidden loot and to progress the story. Sometimes it gets really confusing and is hard to know where to go.
- The ventilation shafts all look the same and are hard to navigate.

Overall, this game is a solid 8/10 in my books and I recommend it even at full price. One thorough playthrough gave me almost 40 hours of enjoyable game play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI originally posted a negative review about The Surge around halfway of my playtime. After that I took some days off and got back to it with a new mindset and my view about the game actually changed quite drastically.
Now I enjoy it and consider it a really good game.
First you need to know that The Surge is truly hard and challenging which will repeatedly crush you if you don't willing to learn its rules.
But when you manage to understand its systems it becomes a lot more fair, and also the progression will come more natural.
I like the atmosphere of this game. The story is engaging, similar to the one of System Shock 2 or Prey, and it has some high points.
The level design is an absolute blast. Yes, it is complex, you need to put effort to understand it, however it is full with secret paths and loot, it is very intricate and really well designed.
The combat is fast, fluid and very methodological. You can change your armor, weapons and implants and it actually affects how you play the game.

The game is underrated and kind of underappreciated, because it is on the thin line where it is either a masterpiece or a flawed game. And sadly this goes more towards the flawed game category. A flawed, but still an enjoyable and worth to experience rough gem.

Just put the time in it and enjoy the challenge, the flow and the sense of wonder while you explore the Creo facility.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really enjoyable take on the Souls formula in a dystopian futuristic setting. Equipement can be gathered on enemies by dismembering them which is a really satisfying mechanic, it also allows a great freedom on your playstyle as you can change your equipement on the fly and your passive and active bonuses at the save room. There is a good weapon and armor variety and switching often is encouraged. Dealing blows feels impactful and fighting is almost never unfair.
The game's biggest weak points are a confusing map layout especially during the first hours after the prologue, poor enemy variety (mostly humanoid) and small amount of bosses.

If you're usually put off by the difficulty of the games in the genre, The Surge is one of the easier I've played as long as you use the right implants and keep your equipement up to date.\t\t\t\t\t

My biggest issue with the game is that it's mostly about solving mazes and puzzles in spite of all the hoopla about the combat. Anyway, the game bores me and I doubt I'll finish it, but if you like running around in circles for hours and brutal, unforgiving melee combat, this game is for you!\t\t\t\t\t
