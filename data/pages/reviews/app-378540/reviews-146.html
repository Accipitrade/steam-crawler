\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an absolutely outstanding game and after reading a lot of the reviews it seems that the negative reviews are based more around peoples inability rather than the game itself. So I will go into more detail if possible by giveing each section a score out of 10.


PROS!

-Performence

It is astonishingly good. I am runningt he game at over 100fps 1440p constantly with no drops below at all. This is on the highest possible settings and on an average computer (i5 and a 980ti). 9/10

-Graphics

They are beautiful. The world is fantastic to look at, and the characters are very well detailed. There are a few low res textures here and there but they tend to be off the beaten path so do not cause any isses. Enemies are detailed and have a nice amount of variety in looks. 9/10

-Armor

The exo suits are awesome. There is a huge variet in them and all of them have lots of detail. They also provide set bonus' which does help but if you want to mix and match you are more than able to with not much of a drawback. 8/10

-Weapons

There are several types of weapons and they all feel and perform differently, but all weapons within that catagory tend to be the same with some changes but I will get into that later. They look brilliant though, all of them look like the killing machines they are meant to be and have nice effects etc. 6/10

-Combat

Here is the big one for most people. I personally love it, if you learn how to use it you will be a monster in no time. There is a learning curve to it that will punnish spammers and people that do not pay attention to stats and enemies abilities. The 'Impact' stat is pretty self explanatory. The more you have the more you stagger so the more hits you can get in and the more damage you can do. Or in the case of weapons with low impact you can get lots of fast hits in and then move away.

It is pretty standard RPG stuff to be honest. On a side note if you got on with the Lord of the Fallen combat (like me) you will fall right into your groove with this game and not have many issues at all. Overall I would say the combat is absolutely flawless, if you learn how the game plays and what abilites to use in the situation you will get on with it very well. It rewards people that think about that sort of thing instead of spamming away. 10/10.

-Leveling

You level up in the same way as most games of this genre by collecting 'souls' or in this case scrap. You take that to a medbay where you can invest it into your suits core to increase its power. The result is a higher core level, this allows you to equip 'Implants' these are things like more HP/Stamina/Energy and your healing items and so on. Also your armor pieces have energy requirements so the better the armor the more you will need. This actually works incredibly well, especially with the ability to bank your scrap so you can acess it later. 8/10

-Looting Mechanic

I am a huge fan of this. Enemies have 6 spots on them you can manually target. 2 arms 2 legs chest and head. If there is a piece of armor you are after and you see a guy wearing it, beat the snot out of that body part and rip it off him. Theres a weapon you want and you see a guy wielding that badboy? Cut that guys arm off and its yours! You get the schematic for the armor and then you can make it at a workshop. All weapons and armor are upgradeable and each NG cycle you go through their level cap is raised by 5. Its simple enough to work out and execute. 9/10

CONS

I honestly dont think there are any of real note aside from the few I will mention now.

-NG+ onwards

After beating the game you go straight into NG+ and as you probably know the game begins again. All your armor and weapons can be increased by 5 more levels, you have more slots for Implants avilable and you earn more scrap. I personally found NG+, NG++ and NG+++ to be an absolute cakewalk. On my first playthrough I was smashed about constantly and I loved it. But from then on the game was too easy, aside from a few extra enemies everything is in exactly the same spot and behave the exact same way. Yeah they hit harder but at this point you have more HP and damage than they can cope with and you destroy them in 2 hits up until maybe 2/3 of the way through then its 3 shots for the rest of the game. Sadly this suffers with Lords of the Fallen syndrome where after NG+ you may as well start a new character since you get nothing out of it. 4/10

-Content is a little lacking

It is not a long game, my first playthrough where I explored every single nook and cranny took me about 40 hours. after that I did a dew NG+ cycles and added about another 20 hours and at that point I had every item in the game and all the achievements. It left me with a little bit of disapointment that I didnt have more to do. I wanted to keep tearing peoples limbs off because I liked their shiny armor. Hopefully there will be some DLC that adds more but thats not relevant at the moment. 5/10

-Story/Voice acting

I put these together since they ggo hand in hand really. Both are a bit 'meh'. The story is a cool idea has some great cutscenes but it is pretty thin. If you listen to every audio log then you get a better lay of the land but its pretty generic to be honest. The voice acting just adds to this since it feels pretty wooden and rushed, but since there isnt much of it then it isnt the end of the world. The main characters voice actor is pretty good though. 6/10


This game is great, if you ingnore the Dark Souls fanboys that hate the combat because they cant spam with their Carthas Curved swords or poke with their Estocs then you will see that this games combat is a masterpiece. The whole game from start to finish made me smile with the grusome fighting and the incredibly satisfying gameplay.

If you are looking for 50-60 hours of violence and feeling like a hero then this is the game for you. Overall I would give it a 9/10.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros :
- fun boss fights. Not that hard, not that punishing, but still a bit challenging with enhanced loots if you kill them in a special way
- lots of secrets, hidden stuff and a few nice sidequests (you can screw up quite easily ...)
- varied weapons moveset, giving different gameplays. All are viable (but... check cons), all are nice to play with
- not as vicious as souls on ennemies placement but still some traps you'll fall in
- drones are fun to play with, and electricity is a nice add on the ressources management side
- good scifi universe, cool story not as obfuscated as in Souls but still a bit hidden (you have to find items to get the whole plot)

Cons :
- quite short (~20 hours for NG spoiler free with a LOT of farming, 2h for NG+)
- every level is a maze of the worst kind. Zones in levels are too similars, shortcuts are numerous and you'll end up finding them easily but still... After almost 24hrs played, I keep getting lost everywhere, all the time.
- OP Gears. For real. It's ridiculous. Concussion drone is OP and push fights to the easiest side of the difficulty spectrum. Same goes for MG Centurion, too fast, too powerful and the MG Gorgon gear with its stam 5 pieces buff.
- frustrating glitches. Despite what I've read, my game never crashed but I got stuck once, fell all the time during executions or ending movesets (PAX 2nd & 3rd hit are the worst).

I still recommend it as it's a nice breath of air. I was restarting DS3 for the 5~6th time, stopped, bought The Surge and enjoyed the hell out of this game.
