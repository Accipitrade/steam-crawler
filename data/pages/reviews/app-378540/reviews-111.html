\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Let me give it to you straight.. Without relating it to Dark Souls or Bloodborne.. See, the game is beautiful in its own design. The concepts of the combat are intriguing and suprisingly fluid. However, the progression isn't rewarding.. The weapons you collect or fabricate are nearly worthless if they lack high damage. Each area, while breathtaking, is very linear despite very few shortcuts that are regularly short and dissapointing. Referring back to the fluid combat, it's so much so that it gets boring.. In short, each enemy is an encounter revolved around targeting.. Each enemy has their own weak spot based on their armor. This is entertaining at first, but quickly settles to a monotonous grind. Mind you, each area has two, maybe three different types of enemies that share similar weak points.

In the end, what really has to be said is this.. What I meant by the progression not being rewarding, is that you expect every item you're going to obtain and upgrade.. You know that in the very next area, you'll get the next level armor upgrade which practically upgrades only your base defense and health. You'll also recieve a new weapon, which would amount to something if it weren't only used for progression through dull combat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo... I do recommend it on sale (got complete edition for 25€) and I do kind of enjoy it, but it is frustrating and highly aggravating.

Should be subtitled "Falling Into Holes Simulator 2017". This is supposed to be a fighting game and it is, at its core, but the level design makes you believe there are two development teams behind The Surge. One that believes in solid, powerful exosuit combat and the combination of blocking, dodging and slide attacking, the other thinking this is more of a Jump'n'Run shooter like DOOM, therefore creating levels with holes you can fall into.

It isn't apparent when you start the game as everything is just solid ground and all you have to worry about is fighting enemies and cutting off limbs. Then in level 2 you basically fall to your death half the time. It's less about the enemies and more about not accidentally targeting the wrong enemy so your character slides into a hole. I've had enemies fall into holes and my character following suit because I pressed attack before the enemy stepped off the cliff. There are open pipes you can walk on that make you believe they lead to a secret area, then you see a ledge you could jump onto but it turns out it's just decoration and you fall through. Because movement isn't freeform and enemies have ranged weapons, it makes you believe that at some point you should have had a rifle. Not exactly sure where the messy level design came from but it looks like there's been coordination issues within the studio.

That aside, the game is mostly bug-free for me, with some quirky movements and awkward camera because of, well, level design choices like very narrow corridors to fight in where the camera will bug out and show you a closeup of a crate texture or something until you leave the area by intuitively walking in the right direction.

Good thing: Backtracking. There are only short-path points of no return, nothing is permanent. You can always walk back to the start and check for secrets. If you get the DLC with the proximity sensor and you do like a bit of a treasure hunt, I recommend you don't install it right off the bat as it's a huge spoiler.

This game requires a controller to play. I use mouse and keyboard but it feels a bit clunky. Standard keyboard layout suggests it was never meant to be played on PC. Controls are a bit awkward, medbay loading after leaving it, feels a bit sluggish, console-ish at times. Nothing too serious though as you don't exactly have time pressure or anything to lose.

When you die, you respawn and have to try again. Trial and error, learning enemy patterns, learning the map, learning the holes so you can try to lure enemies away from them before you fight. It's awkward but it's generally alright.

This is like a 5/10. Recommended but not really good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tToo long, did not read: It was a good attempt but lots of things took the fun out of it. Give it a maybe.



Shame there is not a meh option for recommending games. This was a valiant attempt at making a Dark Souls game, but there were just enough flaws to take away a large portion of the fun.

The story is pretty boring. Something happened to Earth and a mega-corporation saved it(?). Our hero, Willis or Harris or something is given zero characterization. The most interesting thing is that he starts in a wheelchair. Then we get what amounts to a boring torture scene and then you are dumped into a garbage pile. That's it. After over 11 hours, the most I have gotten out of anyone is "Sumpin happened idk." Since there was no world building or character building, who cares?

The combat system is a good attempt and adds some nice mechanics (material gathering is a lot easier) but it still has a rather clunky feel to it. Not much is told to you about the combat system in game other than vertical and horizontal swings (which could change later in the combo). The weapons also begin to feel VERY samey after a while. I found out about blocking from a loading screen and figured out canceling a combo with a block on my own. And yet, in the close combat moments, the game seems to give the edge to the enemy. I have had my block and dodge fail too many times when it should have worked.

This takes us to a rather big design flaw, your health and enemy damage. Like a Souls game, every enemy should not be taken lightly. They can do very serious damage if you are not careful. But no matter how much armor you have, everything seems to do a disproportionate amount of damage. Your health does not level up with you automatically either. You use implants to increase it, which would be fine if the boost was big enough to matter. Some of the bosses and enemies show this in a particularly poor light. With all the health and healing upgrades you can get, you can survive 3 quick hits rather than 2.

It also seems like the game is a buggy pile of jank. Inputs fail, chunks of the environment disappear (the Rusted Spring Tower is just gone), enemies teleport and hit when they should not, plenty of auto-tracking overhead swings, doors and lifts open when they should not and stop working afterward (tons of doors said that they should not open then did), and other things. There have been more than enough enemies that I finished off who just stand stock still upright and disappear. It also seems that a huge portion of all the enemies can literally be walked past without them ever hitting you.

Overall, It is not a terrible game but I have placed it firmly into the maybe pile. If you can pick it up when it is 80% off or in a HumbleBundle, go for it. But you will not be missing much otherwise. Good attempt Deck13.\t\t\t\t\t
