One of my favorite aspects is targeting different parts of enemies bodies in order to break off their armor so you can add it to your arsenal.

Update: I am about 20 hours in, and I can't stop playing. The game difficulty scales very well to your characters progress. The economy feels very balanced. I never feel like any items or upgrades are too difficult to obtain, or too easy to obtain. I hurried and bought all of the DLC before the STEAM sale was over, and as soon as I finish the main game, I will be playing through those. I can't say enough great things about my experience playing this game. I have read some of the negative reviews for The Surge, and I just don't get it. Giving this a direct comparison to Dark Souls is not fair. It is true that it adopts many of the same aspects of leveling and progression, but there is a lot to like in this game if you are a fan of the hardcore action rpg genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt was a bit tough to get into at first, but 20 hours in I somehow found myself recommending it to random people. (And now I guess I'm still recommeding it to random people here on steam...)
I'd say on a scale of Lords of the Fallen to Dark Souls - it's about a 7?

*edit* there is an option to turn off medbay music! omg i so needed that. I'm removing my complaint about that neverending loop. *

So once I'd realized turning off medbay music was an option - things went much more smoothly. I found a weapon I was comfortable with, I learned that blocking isn't so useless, and that I should really learn those enemy combos and wait for them to finish before jumping in.

Yes the environments are not as diverse or pretty as some other games, but you're on some industrial robot factory something or other site - can you really expect a magical forest with unicorns in the middle of it all? And level design is actually quite noteworthy, I think it very well rivals that of Dark Souls with all the little shortcuts that jump out at you when you least expect it. (Well, when *I* least expect it. I'm female so my sense of direction is basically nonexistent.)

There are only a few weapon types (I think 5?), which at first seems like a limitation, but once you get used to one - I bet you wouldn't care anymore if there are 4 or 40 others, since you probably wouldn't use all of them anyway. Either way, the movesets for the weapons are polished, damage increases with upgrades are reasonable, and battles with enemies last just the right amount of time (most of the time). Plus you can see a stun meter on enemies, which is great because you can tell if your next hit is going to stagger them! (This is actually a good example of the little things done right in this game that make it stand out to me.)

The bosses all have some tricks for defeating them which is nice. You know you hate it when you have to whale at a boss for 10 minutes with the same attack doing 1% of hp bar worth of damage, and well - this game is thankfully not like that.

Cutting off various limbs from enemies is always fun.

Length of the game is good. I was just starting to get bored at around 25-30 hours but still had enough energy to run through previous areas unlocking secret doors and farming for weapons/armors I didn't have. Finished the game at about 35 hours, moved on to NG+.

Overall - good buy. If you're a reasonable person and likes the genre it's definitely worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPoorly balanced, in theory lots of customization options, however, none of it has an ultimate effect (or so small that it makes you question whether it matters). The most grievous example being health, where irrelevant to what you equip (+health, higher armor) enemies will hit for more than they should (and far harder than counterpart games i.e. Dark Souls).

Gameplay itself is solid, in the souls-borne style - tight with its own twists - being able to decapitate limbs to effect loot is a fantastic addition. The weapons type are limited, however, with very few move-sets. Enemy diversity is lacking.

Areas are intertwined with many shortcuts; to the point where it is too much. Instead of a road that twists back on itself, it becomes a swiss cheese of passengers that constantly interconnect, causing the unlocking of a shortcut (which is usually a highlight of a souls-borne game) to become meaningless (and worse, and annoyance). Areas are differentiated by themes, however, are still earily similar in the industrial/scifi look which feels like it doesn't vary as much as it should.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is a souls-like game that tries to change things up a bit. Its atmosphere is hostile, cold, and desolate, all wrapped around a techno-futuristic dystopian theme. The game is supposed to take place in a single facility, in other words, you won’t see a lot of variety in the environment, and you’ll see a lot of the same corridors used throughout the game. The game space itself is interwoven in a way that is intelligent, and beneficial to the player. It always felt great finding a shortcut back to the OPS Center (healing/upgrading/saving station). The combat gameplay mostly handles like any other souls’ game: slow, rhythmic, and stamina-conserving. Something new The Surge adds to the combat dynamic is dismembering limbs from enemies for weapons, armor pieces, and building/upgrading material. As far as the enemies themselves go, they aren’t the brightest bunch, it’s pretty easy to figure out their patterns, just don’t get too cocky—they still hit pretty hard. There are a few bosses, each with multiple stages that really bring up the difficulty. Noncombat wise, there are little pockets, and some back-tracing secrets to explore if you want to deviate from the main story. I bought the Augmented Edition that was on sale, it includes all the DLC for the game, it’s not really required to fully appreciate the main game, but it was definitely a fun bonus to play. Overall the game was entertaining and well worth the money.

I have two cautionary advises for those that are wanting to play this game. One, watch out for catwalks with no railing, I literally died more times from falling, than from the enemy, either trying to dodge or performing a combo that would involuntarily move the character forward. Two is that pressing “F4” on a keyboard will instantly kill you; I was once stuck in between two pillars and couldn’t get out, quitting and restarting didn’t work, so “F4” was the simplest way to free myself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not a big fighting game fan, nor a big survival horror fan. I don't think I got past the first level of Dark Souls. It's weird I've enjoyed the Surge so much then. But I can say I definitely enjoyed it for being colourful, gory, placing an emphasis on timing, it's deep customisation features, wealth of secrets, story that has some mystery and good pacing. And no ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ enemy level scaling!

Every kill makes you feel like you bested an opponent. Yeah, the enemy telegraphs and they're scripted a certain way, but god help you if you get a sequence wrong or suddenly run out of stamina. Most of the time you run from groups too, but when you have one of those "rip and tear" days in a section and wind up on top, you feel like a kung fu master meets Doom Marine; complete apex predator ready to go in to another fray. You feel excited playing it and feel adequately rewarded.

The Surge runs well too. I've never had an issue with crashing, maybe one or two times the frame rate dropped to 0, but that's really it.

Highly reccommended.\t\t\t\t\t
