\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Failing at the core level while adding decent ideas to the genre.

Equipment system and leveling up your core is good ideas, but farming parts is really bad, since enemies are same but just easier/harder. There isn't any useful items you can get from different enemies while you are farming for equipment, so it's just a chore.

Weapon efficiency system is bad, it just pushes you to farm lower level enemies just because you switched to the new type of gear, even if it's stats are good, you are bad at using it.

I'm really sick of fighting in tight areas, especially when there is no room for the camera. These levels are for first person perspective, my camera was confused the whole game and I could barely see what's going on in front of me most of the time.

Enemy variety is poor, same enemies with different pieces of armor. Boss design is bad, you'll have to spam same technique to win, there is no room for different strategies, it's extremely focused on executing one strategy well.

You'll die by stepping on to perfectly safe areas, just for the sake of 'level design'. There is no multiple paths to go at one time like any other souls game, if you are stuck at one area, you won't have any way to have fun in the game. This made me realized that this game shouldn't be a soulslike, at this point I just felt like I should just cheat to enjoy the game. I didn't play and enjoy Dark Souls so much because it was difficult, I hated it's difficulty but I was in love with the game so I couldn't stop.

If you are looking for decent, enjoyable soulslike that isn't made by From Software, I'd suggest Remnant: From the Ashes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWorth persevering with. I initially picked this up, then put it down. The Surge is kind of like the dark souls franchise, except it is actually difficult and the setting/environment is rather boring. It is unfortunate that the game is set mainly indoors and in an industrial scene, there is little wrong with the graphics, but it becomes samey very quickly. However, the concept (exo rigs/cuts) is great, the gameplay is definitively there, there are lots of weapons, the camera works well and it controls well. The 2 DLCs are great, very creative and much more whimsical, they add a lot to the game. I have played both The Surge and The Surge 2 and I prefer this one, the balance is better in this one and the stackability of implants is definitely preferable. I hope they make The Surge 3 and balance it somewhere between its predecessors.\t\t\t\t\t
