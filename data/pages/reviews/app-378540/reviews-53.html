\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has the potential to be something great, but sadly fails to fulfill expectations. It's no question that this game takes heavy inspiration from the Souls games, but when it tries to do what Dark Souls 3 did with indirect storytelling, challenging gameplay, and it's main story it falls short on all fronts.

The Surge does one thing almost perfectly, the combat system. Targeting limbs for parts is cool, and I like that you actually have to think in order to target the limb and effectively sever it for your reward. But, sadly, even this system is flawed. Targeting legs is almost always a pain and can make you take damage just because actually moving your target onto them is difficult. On top of this, once you find a weapon you like, you pretty much never experiment with anything else. I would have loved to have more time with each weapon because there are so few.

Now on to the other stuff, man the story could have been soooooooo good, but I, and I assume many others, was not at all surprised or even intrigued by the endgame. Without spoiling anything, all I can say about the final area is that the gimmicks and enemies introduced are clunky and it doesn't feel finished. There are also a lot of "shocker" moments strewn without, which I found to either be 1) not shocking, 2) not built up to or explained in any way afterwards, and 3) in poor taste.

All in all, this game is playable, but cheeky enemy placement, bugs on bugs on bugs, and a lack of variety damn it to mediocrity. Honestly a shame, this game has a lot of potential. If I could get a refund I would, but collectible hunting and the game itself take quite a bit of time. Consider this review an advisory if you plan on picking it up. If you can get it for $5-$10 it's worth it but any more than that I don't believe is worth it. I mean it took me 3 YEARS to finally come back to and suffer through this game, hoping for a light at the end of the tunnel, saying that maybe the next area won't be as bad, and it always was worse.\t\t\t\t\t
