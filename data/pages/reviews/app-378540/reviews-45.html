\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDark Souls in an futuristic industrial setting perfectly describes this game. It takes many ideas from dark souls, like world design, level design, combat difficulty, story telling and places the game in a futuristic industrial setting. However, while I enjoyed playing this game, it doesn't hold up in many aspects to the game that clearly inspired it. The story is vague and meandering, the boss fights are few, the environments are mostly the same for my entire 26 hour playthrough and it just lacks that sense of wonder, apprehension and elation that is very unique to From Software games. Having said that, it is unfair to compare games to Dark Souls and Bloodborne that have set the bar so high. The surge is a solid game that is definitely worth your time and money. A solid 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never write reviews for games, and when I do it's for games I absolutely love and think deserve more attention. I think this game is the opposite. A game that was so seriously awful that I had to add my complaints to the sea of somehow positive reviews.

I expected The Surge to be the most standard, hyper-generic Souls-Like game, that I could run through just to have a Souls experience of some kind. Just the bare minimum.

This game does not even deliver that. The controls (for some reason I can't quite place) just feel TERRIBLE. Like, wading waist deep in molasses terrible. Combat is incredibly slow and unsatisfying. Body part targeting is finicky at best. Overall, the game is FAR too clunky to be enjoyable, especially as someone who usually builds a high dexterity character and enjoys the fast paced nature of souls-like combat. Like, WOW. I thought "Hey, $5 on sale for a souls-like game to run through for a few days seems like a good deal, even if it's the most generic uninspired thing I've ever seen," but it only took a few minutes with this game's combat system for me to realise that I'd rather pull teeth than try and see this game through.\t\t\t\t\t

The Surge is a brilliant game with excellent combo based combat and Souls-style exploration. It's fun and beautiful.

What really sets it over the top for me is the writing and voice work. The lore and characters are all very well thought out, the story especially ramps up in an area called the "Executive Forums" and from then on out things turn ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ crazy, haunting even, with lots of melancholy thrown in.

The "Lost in the Park" DLC is a wonderful addition, very fun to play. It has excellent level design and provides needed lore for the player character "Warren", it is a must get. It also happens to be quite humorous.

The "Good, Bad, Augmented" DLC also brings a lot of humor and LOOT, so if grinding for loot is your thing you have to get that too.

I don't like the sequel too much. Surge 2 did improve some things, quality of life stuff with the way gear and augments work, but overall I thought the world, writing, and voice work were dumb, passed over, hamfisted, having none of the excellence of this here first Surge. It also ended up looking graphically DOWN-graded from Surge 1 even though technically it's doing more graphically intensive things.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good: Combat can be fun, the look of the game is great, the weapon/armor stealing limb removal system is a fun idea.

The bad: Like a lot of Dark Souls wannabe games, the enemies all respawn if you go to any Campsite/Lab etc. to upgrade your gear. This becomes extremely tedious, and leads to basically just running past most enemies after you have enough material to upgrade. Add in the fact that combat is unforgiving(by design) so you can clear 40 enemies then get stunlocked and have to basically redo the entire level... this game does not respect the players' time, in my opinion.

Also, armor seems to be utterly meaningless, speed and stamina boosts are all you need, tanking enemy damage is nonexistent. Yes, you can block, with bigger weapons, assuming nothing goes wrong and the game lets you get hit through the block (or through the Limb Removal animation, etc.)

Overall, tedious design ruins what could be a great game, for me at least. Maybe I'll try this at some point again in the future, but I think this gets marked down with some other games to be wiped to make space for better upcoming games. A mod or built in option to have enemies STAY DEAD would make this game a lot better too. All that would be needed is a dropped resource tweak, or let them respawn when you go back to previous levels, not every time you go to upgrade your gear.

Anyway, I know a lot of people love Dark Souls type games, I am finding that I am not one of those people. Have fun!
