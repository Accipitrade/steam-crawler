\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell what can i say!

After putting in a solid 20 hours into The Surge i feel like i can definitely give my review and oh boy is it positive!!!
I came into this game with no expectations as that is how i like to begin the journey of new games to hit the scene and i must say im extremely impressed with what i have seen and played so far!

From the get go it got me hooked. I love the combat system, for me it's faultless. I have seen a few reviews saying you should be able to cancel attacks mid way through and at first i agreed but now i see it as if you just go into a fight spamming keys/buttons and die because you couldnt cancel and dodge an attack in time then that is your own fault.
The Surge is pretty unforgiving in that way, if you aren't willing to learn and adapt to each type of enemy and treat each as your last you are going to pay for it over and over again until you feel like throwing your monitor out the window in a fit of rage!

Don't get me wrong the game is not easy, it is most definitely challenging which is a good thing.
Enemies will surprise you even the ones you have fought countless times, never just try rush your way through a section just because you have been through it many times as it will bite you in the backside. Patience and good timing is the way to play and if you have played souls most people will be able to adapt pretty well and quickly in that regard.

As for graphics, the game looks supurb at max settings and is optimised extremely well. I have held steady frames the entire game time ive played so far so i am mighty impressed with that.
Sound also is really great, from the different ground types you step on, the sounds of exo suits moving, weapons colliding with enemies and of course yourself and all the way through to the enviroments and speech is nice and crisp.

Enviroment setting for me personally i really enjoy the look and feel of it. It changes quite a lot through the different area's you need to navigate through and the interconnectivity with all the shortcuts through each area is extremely well done so that's another thumbs up from me there.

Story, so pretty much you are just trying to find out what the hell has happened, i dont really want to go into it due to the possibility of spoiling it for others so i will just leave it at that. It's interesting and has me continuing on and on and on through a million deaths to find out everything.

I have only encountered one bug so far and its extremely minor i dont even care and that was a drone that went invisible just before i attacked it but i was still able to target and destroy it so it is nothing to really complain about.

So all in all i most definitely recommend this game. but i must say do not go into the game expecting dark souls, yes it has a lot of souls vibes coming from the gameplay but take into mind The Surge is it's own GAME!!! enjoy it for what it has to offer and im sure you will enjoy the game as much as i am!

Thanks for reading and i hope this review inspires many more people to jump on deck of this amazing game!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing through Dark Souls 3 on all available platforms (Xbox One, PS4, and now PC), I think i can safely recommend this game to anyone that appreciates the Dark Souls franchise. Listed below are the Pros and Cons that i have found so far during my time playing The Surge.

Pros:
- Feels like Dark Souls and Space Marine had a child and this was the result.
- A nice clean and easy to use UI.
- The game looks amazing and captures the Dark Souls Sci-Fi feel.
- Battles require thought beforehand, charge in recklessly and get rekt.
- The game has a decent weapon variety (one hand, two hand, staff, one rig, two rigged).
- Proficiency levels make weapons stronger as you increase it.
- Armor looks amazing and has set bonuses once all six pieces are equiped.
- A lot to explore in each of the levels.
- Nice execution effects on enemies.

Cons:
- Wonky hitboxes make you feel cheated when you die.
- Enemies seem to have hyper armor (all enemies).
- Enemies hit very hard and do a lot of damage (a lot of times i get two shot).
- Leveling up the power core only increases your power capacity.
- Most of the implants seem lack luster and do not do a lot.

On the Fence:
- Grindy (pro or con pending on the type of player you are).

A lot of the Cons can be overlooked as long as you play smart. The "On the Fence" item could be a Pro or a Con depending on the kind of gamer you are. If you like grinding scrap and mats you'll love this game however if you don't then count that as a Con. So far I am enjoying the game, and would rate it a 7.5/10. If the creators can fix the hitboxes and the raw strength of the enemies i would give it a 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me answer your questions.

Is this Sci-fi Dark Souls?
yes, yes it is.

Is the combat as complex as DS?
A little simpler but different enough to make it interesting. The armor, implants, and tactics you use make the biggest difference.

Is it as Dark as DS?
No not really. The lore and story is dark but not enough to make you want to dye your hair black and comb it over one eye, then wonder if anyone would miss you if you killed yourself. The actual scenery is colorful and interesting to look at.

Is it as hard as DS?
It is in that it will quickly punish you for any mistake you make. It does reward you for exploring everywhere, and for being careful yet aggressive. At the same time it does throw some curveballs at you that make you hate yourself. You will have to learn the game and git gud to get anywhere in this game.

Do you play these kinds of hard games because you hate yourself.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo it's not Dark Souls and that's a good thing. As someone who played Bloodbourne and the Dark Souls trilogy in it's entirety, i thoroughly believe that Deck13 made a great addition and alternative to the "Souls-like" genre/games.

My personal highlight of this game is it's great combat. All attacks feel very meaty and impactful. You really get the feeling of beeing a badass mofo in a futuristic exo-skeleton crushing your enemies with a huge hammer. Provided you don't overestimate yourself, because this game is as dificult and merciless as the games by FromSoftware. The Scurge puts a greater emphasis on fighting "regular" enemies rather than spectacular bosses. Regardless of your gear or level you still will be absolutely destroyed by your run-of-the-mill enemies, which i personally enjoyed but some people seem to view as a balancing issue. Similar like the Souls-Games you avoid damage by either blocking or dodging atacks. Although the latter option is the more reliable. Since this game has no shields (at least to my knowledge) there are only a handful of atacks that you can block without taking noticable punishment. Unfortunately the game doesn't explain which atacks are actually blockable, so this might lead to some trial and error related frustration in the beginning. But there's a unique twist about the blocking in the Scurge. While blocking you can either duck underneath an atack or hop above an low attack. The timing to pull this off is similarly tight as the parry mechanic in the Souls-game and those "mini dodges" can be followed up by powerful counter atacks. But since this is much harder to pull of correctly than your average parry it is oh-so more satisfying. The dodge mechanic works similarly as in Bloodbourne, where you esentially make a little jump in a direction where you want to go. This works and feels just as great as in Bloodbourne and when timed perfectly you will avoid pretty much all harm.
Purely by comparing the core combat mechanics i actually prefer the Scurge over the Souls-Games (it's heresy, i know...), but the feeling of combat is obviously something very subjective and cannot be potrayed by words accurately.

The only mechanic related to combat/character customization i very dislike is the Attack Proficiency System: Basically a Elder-Scrolls-like mechanic where you do more damage with a weapon type the more you use it. I really don't get why this mechanic is in the game since every other aspect of customization invites you to experiment with your build as you go.

There are no Requirements you need to fullfill stat-wise on any Armor or Weapon. The only "stat" you level up in this game is your core power. Every piece of armor/mod/consumable consumes a certain amount of core power, so you simply design your build around the idea of managing your currently available core power. This system seems very simplistic at first glance, but allows great variety in how you want to play your character. I personaly always like to play the Souls-Games with all types of different builds, and since FromSoftware likes to put odd restrictions regarding respecs, i really enjoyed Deck13's approach.

Even though this game definitely falls behind it's competitors in terms of variety in scenery and enemy design, the game is still very pretty for a sci-fi setting mainly consisting of metal corridors.
I still think that this game is great take on the Dark-Souls-Formula and is a must-buy for every Dark Souls veteran, who is willing to question some design desicions that are so engrained in our beloved Franchise.
Im looking forward for Deck13 future work and hope that they will fix the present issues with the game.
Finally real competition to the Dark-Souls franchise!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is a science fiction themed Souls-like.

PROS

-Combat that feels good -- weapons have weight (but not too much weight) and executions are brutal. If you enjoyed Souls combat but felt that there wasn't enough weight behind each hit, and liked Bloodborne dodging, this offers a similar experience.

-Interesting body part targeting system/weapon system. Focus on hitting enemy body parts and cut them off when you have enough energy to get armor and weapons.

-Industrial complex theme (also listed under cons) -- each area has a believable setting and flows from one area to the next. Weapons and armor fit the theme very well.

-Few bosses (also listed under cons) -- The Surge is less about boss fights and more about 'trash' mobs -- every mob that you would consider 'trash' in a Souls game is dangerous and should never be underestimated. However, each boss brings its own gimmick that most of the time can be ignored past the first phase of the fight. One reviewer mentioned that the Firebug required you to chop off all its limbs -- however, once you chop off 3, phase 2 begins, and the boss can be taken down without further loss of limbs by targeting the main body.

-Implant system allows you to change your build to suit each area or your own playstyle. Collecting implants found throughout each level is highly recommended.

-Typical Dark Souls-esque storytelling. There is a main story that you can follow, but will barely make sense unless you go looking for audio logs and pay attention to the environment.

-Graphics are crisp and the lighting is excellent, with a brightness slider for those that find it too dark.

-Energy meter used for various actions, such as operating companion drone, using implants, and executions on enemies, always feels relevant and rewards aggressive tactics.



CONS

-Few bosses (5). Most people consider bosses to be the highlight of a Souls-like. Unfortunately, there aren't that many bosses in the game.

-Industrial complex theme -- due to the setting, many rooms can feel similar -- it's up to you to remember where to turn and how to get to different areas.

-Not much music.

-Heavy armor may have too many penalties -- in a game that relies on dodging and rapid stamina regen, slowing down stamina regen is a harsh penalty. Medium or Light armor are far more managable.

-One or two late game enemies have annoying hitboxes on their attacks. If you've played FromSoft titles and have fought dragons/wyverns, you'll get a general idea of what I'm talking about.



NEUTRAL

-Scrap (souls) timer -- upon death, you drop your scrap and there will be a timer counting down the seconds until your scrap vanishes. This timer can be extended by 20-30 seconds per enemy killed. I didn't notice a huge difference in how I played the game in regards to the scrap timer. Sometimes I killed every enemy on the way to my dropped scrap and I ended up with 3+ minutes on the timer. Other times, I ran past everything and got to my scrap with a minute left.

-Voice acting -- Not terrible, not great. Mostly average. Some people will enjoy it, others won't.

-No guns -- While no guns makes sense for the setting, it does seem strange that only drones and very select enemies get ranged attacks. Your only ranged attack comes in the form of a personal drone.


VERDICT

Worth a buy if you liked the Souls games, but this is very much a different beast. Go in with an open mind and you'll probably enjoy it. In terms of buy/sale -- 35-40 dollars seems like a good price. Anything lower is a steal!\t\t\t\t\t

_____________________________________

The first few hours of this game are INCREDIBLY misleading to the consumer. Here is where the developers of the flop titled Lords of the Fallen have failed once again:

- Regardless of what armor you have on, enemies will 1-shot you later in the game. Dodging is the only mechanic that will save you.

- The weapon you use from the start of the game is the only type you will EVER get to use. This is due to the terribly designed weapon proficiency system. Want to use a two-handed weapon instead of a one-handed weapon? Too bad. You will have to grind the same mobs for the next 5 hours with a two-handed weapon to get near the same damage from it.

- Actions you take are QUEUED. Want to stop your combo to dodge? Better pray you only hit your swing button twice instead of three times.

- Level design is almost as lazy as Lords of the Fallen. Hope you like dark metal corridors and boxes.

- Five bosses, all nearly as boring and generic as the last. Similarly to Lords of the Fallen. Get it together Deck13. Make a boss with more than 5 moves.

- Lack of weapon/armor variety, not that it matters due to most of it being useless to the player. For a game based on cutting pieces off, you would think the game would be full of variety. Unfortunately, myself and many others players have only used the Lynx set and the same weapon/weapon type from start to finish.

_______________________________________

Yet another Deck13 disappointment. I am not sure how many failures these developers need to create before they figure it out. More effort was put into the options menu to get TotalBiscuit points than was put into balancing the game. Balance is incredibly important, and many souls-like games have been able to find a semblance of it.

The Surge does not even try.\t\t\t\t\t
