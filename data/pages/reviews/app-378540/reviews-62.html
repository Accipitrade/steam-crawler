\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is probably the best Souls game out there that isn't from From Software. That's basically all you need to know, but if you're interested in more...

The Surge is a science fiction action adventure game with difficult combat, where gauging enemy attack patterns and managing stamina (used for both evasive maneuvers and attacking) is the key to victory. While wandering a dystopian industrial complex, you'll level up your "Exosuit," allowing you to wield increasingly more powerful mods and (more importantly) more *combinations* of mods. Mods can do everything from heal you during combat to increase your attack speed, making them a clever substitute for traditional stat progression found in similar games. All the while you'll discover new weapons and harvest armor from your defeated foes (which you do by hacking off their body parts and claiming their armor for your own, one piece at a time).

The game has flaws. Some zones are too dark, some enemy attacks not well telegraphed, and enemy/boss/environment variety doesn't hold a candle to the Souls games. However, it gets the basics more right than any other attempt at the formula outside of Dark Souls or Bloodborne themselves. Combat is satisfying and visceral, there are interesting build possibilities, and what bosses they do have range from decent to excellent.

A special call-out for the story. Unlike Dark Souls, which remains predominantly arcane from beginning to end (that is: you've got to be looking for the story to find it), The Surge gradually moves the story line to the forefront. Even though the ending itself leaves a little to be desired, the final act still manages some surprising emotional notes. At least two story lines sucker-punched me pretty good, which was a pleasant surprise.

If you haven't played Dark Souls, there's something wrong with you. Go play that. If you have and you're looking for something that gets the basics right and adds a few twists to the formula, you're not going to do much better than The Surge.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis plays like the original Dark Souls 1. Clunky, but overall sloppier. I'm sure there was a way to unlock from an enemy, but well, on to the actual review.

The first area is actually pretty well designed and gets you into the game pretty well. Enemies are tough but fair, you can tell where you are and where you're heading. Even the boss fight was fair. Everything was going well for this game up until the second area.

And once you get there, you realize just how poor of a combatant our MC is. Those fair enemies I spoke of earlier, those were very sloppy enemies with very exploitable AI. Try landing a hit on the enemies in the 2nd area without taking one yourself. Fun times, especially considering those hits HURT. A LOT. Don't even try to 2v1 in this game when 1v1s are nearly impossible situations at this point in the game.

There was a unit that you can overcharge with 70 CP in the first area. Maybe that was the recommended level for that area? Because you probably won't even be that level by the time you're nearing the end and make your way back to the first area. Maybe "The Surge" refers to the huge difficulty spike of the game. Or a surge in grinding that you're going to need to do in order to maybe, just maybe, not die... in 3 hits. Maybe you can increased that to 4 hits in that 70 levels you gain.

The second area is all indoors and at some point, some areas start to look alike. The only way you can tell where you are is when you walk into another room that is considered another area. There is no minimap. With all the technology in this game, you can't download a map into your rig. Lovely.

At this point, I just decided to use Cheat Engine and turn this "game" into an interactive movie where I mash buttons to move enemies out of my way if I felt like it. The only time the boss of that area ever hit me was the really cheap spin attack it whips out with no tell. Looked weak, but who knows, that probably would've comboed me to death had I been playing legitimately.

The third area was pretty short. You find some lady who tells you to find some people... who are back in the area you just came from. But you needed to come here for an upgrade you actually needed. There's security doors that require security rigs to get through and you can come back here if you remembered to which I didn't and plus, who cares.

So you go back to a new area you can now access, I can't for the life of me remember what happened in this area, but I'm sure the maze-like aspect of the game still wasn't fully implemented yet. There was a boss at the end, it probably would've killed me in 1 hit assuming the fall damage I would've taken didn't kill me to begin with. And with the very crappy lock on system this game has, it was hard to defeat this thing. AND I HAD INVINCIBILITY TURNED ON LOL. Yes, still couldn't figure out how to unlock off from an enemy, once you're locked on, you have to take it down I guess. I was getting wailed on, I was questioning whether or not dodging actually gave you iframes in this game like it did in Dark Souls. Glad I wasn't fighting it legitimately.

Next area was where it started getting maze-like. First half was maze like as in, you know where you've been, but a lot of shortcuts lead back to that same area, also, you don't know where to go. And the second half was the labyrinth. Some dude gives you a quest. I'd like to say I completed it like 50 times over because I went through an "office" about that many times. But of course, guy is tweaking out and says "please help me man, it's in my office", as if I know who this guy is or where his office is. I move on and kill a weird bald dude that think he's a poet and won't shut up and then the area gets overrun by security and I think that "office" guy is dead now so I leave him.

You go through the second area of the game once more because the game likes to give you vietnam war flashbacks and now you really have to deal with security who is quite overpowered (you can fight them when you first arrive in the second area, if you really want, but you'll die in 2 hits. They won't aggro until you provoke them). Or you can run past them if you're playing legitimate I guess, but you won't be able to run past them for long. Because...

The next area is short. Boss fight is pretty much after a drop into a new "area" of that section of the game. If you ever wanted to do a 7v1 in Dark Souls, where you're the 1 guy, here it is... only because this game is clunky, I don't think you're meant to do this fight legitimately. Glad I was cheating. It must've taken about 10 minutes to clear these guys out while trying to dodge and attack. I took a look at the guide on IGN about where to go after this, because I didn't know where to go after this fight (didn't know about the thing in the medbay) and it looks like the boss fight was meant to be a 1v1 with just you and the boss so idk what I did wrong. I went down the exo-lift assuming I would be able to get back up it, but it was disabled afterward so maybe that's what it was.

The rest of the game was a blur. Stuff happened, maze-like areas were traversed probably 5 times over before I figured out where to go. I finally get to the end of the game, not knowing it's the end of the game, kill a boss and now I'm in NG+ where I'm FORCED to start over. I'm pretty sure there was an area of the penultimate area of the game I completely missed because I wanted going to go back to it later. Silly of me to assume I would ever find it again. I must've spent 2 hours looking for it by myself, with walkthroughs, with video guides, before giving up and deciding to move on. It's on the 2nd floor, that's all I remember.

I don't even have half the achievements which must have been due to the security doors I didn't go through, tons of bosses I apparently missed. I don't know what happened with any of the NPCs I helped. The only NPC I killed, the Dr. Evil lookalike, Edgar Allen Poe wannabe, was who I found out the most about. And I hated that guy. I understand that a good thing was turned bad. That's about it. Seems like our MC knew more about the situation than I ever did and that fact was actually implied in the game through the audiologs.

Overall, I would not recommend this game for gameplay purposes. I wouldn't even recommend watching a 100% playthrough of this online. Just watch the cutscenes for whatever story you can scrap out of it. I came into the game with people I know, not souls-like fans mind you, having played a couple of hours of it and not recommending it, but I still wanted to give it a try being a fan of the genre. I'd say the first area is a distraction to keep you playing for the 2 hours you could've refunded the game. I won't discredit it too much; it does look amazing graphically, it tried something new with the limb-targetting system and it does encourage customization for your character and reward your grinding for it. But gameplay-wise, it'll feel like a chore to keep up with the enemies very quickly and you'll dread seeing this game still installed in your library.

Having said that though, while I myself haven't played it yet, The Surge 2 seems like a more refined game that people seem to enjoy more and actually recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
