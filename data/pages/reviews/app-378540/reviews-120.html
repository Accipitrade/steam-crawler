\r\n\t\t\t\t\t-Work in Progress-
The movement is way too gamey for lugging around a hulking rig and exoskeleton.. Its too quick, and has zero momentum.. Its instant full speed, instant turning on a dime, no slow down, nothing.. I personally think Lords of the Fallen was superior in combat..

Once you get to the second area, you start fighting guys wearing Rhino armor, Ive noticed one o two who seem to teleport a bit.. Most notably, the guy with the one handed fire sword (similar moveset to the chainsaw).. The enemies using faster weapons, or dual wielding have almost no telegraph, and have perfect tracking, which becomes annoying.. Whenever Im fighting a Rhino guy, with a fastw eapon, its garanteed Im swapping blows..

The healing mechanic SUCKS... Allow me to elaborate.. You use injectors, which would be fine, except that there are multiple tiers, and then other items to inject.. Such as damage boosts, heal over times, elemental damage boosts, etc.. And theres only ONE key to heal.. Meaning during the heat of battle, you have to tap the right directional key, until you get to the one you need.. They have tiny icons, and it takes your thumb off of the left analog, which you need to move.. There is a kindve positive, depending on what kind of player you are.. They cannot be used while in attack animations, and cannot be spammed (small cooldown).. This makes perfect sense for potions, but these are injectors, attached to your bdy.. Its not like you have to stop, pop a cork, and chug.. I played from a controller, and its likely better on m+kb in this aspect, but I prefer analogs and triggers for this style of game..

I got it on sale, and recommend even at full price.. Its definitely fun, but is not without some minor issue.. I liked Lords of the Fallen.. Aside from its poor technical issues at release, I dont understand the flak it gets.. Though admittedly, i did have problems with its claims that enemies follow the same rules as the player.. They had no issue beginning a combo on you, while you were mid combo, staggering the ever loving out of them... Also a poor camera.. Which, persists in The Surge.



-UPDATE/Final Assessment-
After 70 hrs or so, and a 2nd character at rank 80, I absolutely adore this game.. However.. I now see the disdain for the final map, the Nucleus.. It is a maze, which I enjoy, but the blob enemies suck.. Not because theyre hard, but because theyre glitchy, and its sometimes impossible to tell which attack theyre doing, and even more difficult to tell how far away the attacks land.. Sometimes the long arms will remain visually, so it looks like it has permanent tree limbs sticking out 4 times its length. There are also FAR too many of them! Some areas have 2, or 1 and a Chrysalid enemy.. Other than the terrible blob enemies, I have less complaints..

Still has camera issues however, but the combat is top notch.. Possibly better than any other game! Hitboxes are superb(Except for staff enemies.. Theyre broken), and input is crisp.. You will have no issue with movement control in this game(though I thought it could use more weight/momentum in movement.. Somewhere between this, and LotF).. Except for the ridiculous jump/duck mechanic tied to blocking.. It usually doesnt work in combat, and is clunky as hell.. The map layout is awesome, although somewhat samey made better by the dlc which break it up.. Its understandable from a lore standpoint, as its all in the same factory.. The weapons and armor are pretty cool as well, and the level up system is simple, but effective.. I never really tired of fighting enemies, as they made a smart way of having to farm the upgrade mats and gear itself.. Works surprisingly well, and allowed for longer play before fatigue of enemy set in.. The small amount of boss battles is a shame, but the combat throughout makes up for it, as the normal enemies are always deadly, and make up for the lack of boss battles.. Implants do make for some differences in build, but there is no build diversity Im sorry to say.. Heavy gear, or light gear have minor differences, and implants add a little more, but all in all, each will play the same..

I enjoyed Lords of the Fallen, but I have to agree that this is leaps and bounds better! A must have for any 3rd person action rpg fan!

Edit: NG+ changed my mind.. There are new enemies, and they hit for FAR too much damage.. A staff wielder in the very beginning for example.. 170-200 dmg per attack, and has up to 6 hit combos.. Carbon Cat, is hitting me for almost 500dmg if I take on of his bigger hits.. I like a challenge, but the damage is tuned TOO high..\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlaying through the Surge has been one of the most enjoyable gaming experiences I have had to date. Everyone knows it's a souls like game but it is much more than that at heart.

It's easy to brush off the story at play here but I urge everyone to actually pay attention. Listen to the audiologs. Explore everything. Do the side quests. It's hard for a game like this - not focused on story but focused around it's difficulty and core gameplay loops to engage you in a thought out story, and while I admit it wasn't the best told tale it was certainly an interesting one. There are plenty of seemingly pointless audio logs or focus points that will all be revealed later on in the game and all of them will open your eyes to how grim the world of the Surge really is. Had it been a little more polished and perhaps given some background lore this would have been one of the best stories in gaming, in my opinion.

Aside from my love of the story, the game has many selling points.

- The combat is very, very satisfying. Yes it recycles the core dark souls style but it adds to it and IMO improves on it. With execution moves and pinpoint targeting you really feel in control in the fights you get into. The actual difficulty in fighting enemies I feel was greater than any other souls like game I have played (DS1,2 and 3) and I initially it a serious wall in combat. Practice truly does make perfect though and the game will reward you for trying again, because once you get the hang of your preferred weapon (of which there are tonnes) and can learn off some enemy move sets you'll be making your way through enemies like greased lighting.

-Customization is also plentiful. The Surge doesn't let you sink EXP points into any given skills or any of those familiar rpg mechanics you may be used to, but instead it has its very unique implant system. These implants allow you to drastically change up your playstyle and as you progress further into the game and find more you can adapt and overcome most situations. All of these implants rely on your core power which is in essence the exp sink of the game, but it doesn't increase your base stats, and will only allow you to add more implants or equip more powerful gear. It was an odd system to me when I first started but I like what they have done. I can choose to go into a fight stacked with HP implants and healing implants, or I can throw caution to the wind and only take DPS implants. High risk high reward gameplay is ever present in the Surge and the implant system simply puts the cherry on top.

-The game is gorgeous. Now I realize it is set indoors in familiar environments like 80% of the time but my god are those environments well done. The textures are beautiful and everything looks perfect. There are some sections when you walk through tunnels and I personally had to stop and admire my character model during some of these instances when the light hit me a certain way. It's easy to say that everything in the game looks the same because they're all robots and everything is mechanical etc. But looking similar doesn't mean looking bad.

-There are plenty of nifty little things that make the game so much more enjoyable. Set bonuses, secret areas, collectibles, easter eggs, ironic humour and more.


There is an abundance of things to do in the NG mode and then plenty of farming and upgrades and extra goodies in NG+, there is a DLC that just came out too so you won't be stuck for content in the slightest. Any souls fan is missing out big time if they pass up on this game, even if the setting doesn't appeal to you I urge you to try it because it will grow on you and you will love it.

10/10. I really don't have a bad word to say about this game. Say for the music that plays in the OPS centers, but that's so minimal it really doesn't matter.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe team behind Lords of the Fallen have another go at the souls-like genre, and it's a better game fullstop. It's actually more like Bloodborne than Dark Souls as the combat is faster paced with an emphasis on dodging, and trying to tank anything will result in a swift(er) demise. Yes, it's a challenging game, punishing even but never unfair and the progress feels just right.
Combat is good, with everything feeling meaty. It's also fairly unique that the gear and weapons you get are mainly sourced from cutting them from enemies. Upgrades require more parts, so do you go for that armoured chest on the enemy or the unarmoured head? The formers rewards are higher but comes with greater risk. It's a nice system with plenty of scope for different builds and playstyles.

The futuristic/apocalyptic setting is very well done and the visuals are excellent, with great animations and sharp texturing. The sound is also top notch, and you can even add your own music to the Ops centres, these are your basic hubs throughtout the game where you can upgrade parts and implants and take a breather. The level design is also very well done, however the game doesn't hold your hand what so ever so it can feel maze like at first. Size wise, it's a big game. Not the biggest but it still took me 45hrs to complete, and i missed some side quests/items which i'm going to do in NG+.

The 'Walk in the Park' dlc is also excellent, with a really different setting to the rest of the game, adds some really nice touches (you can have a go on a rollercoaster!) and it's also woven into the main game perfectly. So i recommend buying the complete pack for the full experience. Great game. I highly recommend The Surge.\t\t\t\t\t

TLDR: Buy Armored Core 6 and thank me later.

--------------

After 47 hours, I finally beat the base game. And... I'm torn. On one hand... this is a great souls-like game. On the other hand... it leaves much to be desired.

Pros:
-Cutting off an enemy's armor. It's a unique combat feature that pretty much ensures that you always get 'drops' from enemies. AND, they contribute to you leveling progress. It's such a simple and fantastic idea.

-Story and art design. While it may not be as thought provoking and visually striking as the Dark Souls series, it's still well done. Hell, just the idea of nanomachines + exo-rigs essentially zombifying people... it's a fascinating thought.

-Storing scrap. Probably the one thing I can say this game actually did better than Dark Souls.

Cons:
-NPCs are kind of lacking. You can do a few things with NPCs, and that's great... but their interactions are, for the most part, slightly boring. Only one of them shows any signs of personality.

-Clunky, often frustrating combat. Yes, I know, we play these games for the challenge. But there is so much to the combat that needs fine-tuning. Inaccurate hitboxes. Inputs don't respond during enemy attacks sometimes, even though you aren't staggered. Enemy lunge attacks sometimes push around corners, and despite their slowed momentum still hit you for massive amounts of damage (I'm looking at you, you four legged ).

-One particularly complex boss fight, which had an unnecessarily obscure mechanic to it.

-Level design. Don't get me wrong, I loved the art in this game. But... my God... I got lost so many times. Lifts were used way too often in attempts to create complex pathing within a single area. While I appreciate the thoughtfullness of adding in multiple shortcuts to help the player along, I ultimately ended up running around in circles trying to find my next objective.

-Traveling hurts. This goes along with the previous note. There's no universal travel system, just a tram/door which takes you from one area to the next. Yes, the shortcuts helped loop you back around to the safety of an OPS station (this games version of a 'bonfire')... however... there were so many different paths per 'level'... I would often find myself at an OPS stations trying to remember which direction I came from, and how to get back on the right path.

-TIMED SCAP COLLECTION. I can't tell you how much progress I've lost because I've died at the end of a stupidly long path and couldn't get back to my scrap in time. This last time, upwards of 200k all at once. It was sitting right by a shortcut door, which could only be opened from that side. This mechanic has no reason to exist other than to frustrate the player.

Final say:
This game started out so strong. The concept is solid, but the execution... well... it could use more polish. There are just too many flaws to ignore. It's not Dark Souls. Then again, I never expected it to be.

It's not a must-buy. But if you're a fan of the genre, I think you should still check it out.
------------------------------
DLC and NG+

Okay, let me just say this first: The art design is much improved over the base game. Level design still leaves something to be desired, but just visually the DLC stands out.

With that being said, the core game design and mechanics still hurt... and not in a good way. Artificial difficulty spikes are much more apparent in NG+. The inconsistency of enemy attack power, and... well... just combat in general... is really disheartening. If you've kept up on your leveling and gear management, no single enemy should be able to one-shot you.

Let me say that again.

If you've kept up with your leveling and gear management.... No. Single. Enemy. Should. Be. Able. To. One. Shot. You.

In a nutshell, that's the difference between 'difficult' and 'punishing'. I'm a patient man. I've made my way through some intense and challenging boss fights in other games. I couldn't even finish fighting the final DLC Boss because it was just so idioticly unforgiving. It didn't just kill me, it killed all the fun out of this game for me.

Final-final say: I've changed my mind. I'm not going to call the game out-right . But... yeah. I'm no longer recommending this game. If you're a fan of the Dark Souls genre, proceed with caution.\t\t\t\t\t
