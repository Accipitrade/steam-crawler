\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
* Interesting character upgrade system.
* Combat is pretty good and makes some enjoyable changes to the dark souls formula, particularly when it comes to the ability to chain different attacks together for different results.
* The story is interesting, at least in the beginning.
* Graphics are good, although not cutting edge or anything.

Cons
* Bosses are poorly balanced. I personally felt that just fighting the regular enemies was much more fun and allowed more variety. Fortunately, the bosses are few and far between.
* The ending is hands down the most disappointing I've seen in years. No closure whatsoever; so many threads of the story that could have gone somewhere interesting were completely abandoned.
* The DLC is too short to be worth it, even at the $10 sale price I paid.

I'd recommend it for the gameplay, but that's about it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInspired by the Dark Soul series comes The Surge from the creators of Lords of the Fallen. This game has a lot of positive things going for it in terms of combat, but the other areas are a bit lacking in my experience.

Pros:
The Gameplay: It feels great to control Warren during combat and execute enemies in brutal ways. Being able to target specific body parts of the enemies to obtain the gear they are wearing? That's awesome. Fighting giant robots? Awesome.
The Graphics: A Sc-Fi sort of setting usually looks pretty good. That's what drew me to the game in the first place. At best this game looks spectacular, and at worst, it looks better than average. Sadly, no HDR support on PC.

Cons:
The Story: Do you know what is going on here? Because no one else that I spoke to during my experience had any clue whatsoever. The dialogue was a bit stale, and I felt that it did not add much to the overall story.
The Environments: It was set in an industrial complex, and that lead to the scenery not changing much. There were some cases in which it looked a bit different, but it mostly left a negative impression on me.
The Sound Design: The sound is one of the most important things in a video game for me, and I felt that it was lacking in the sound department. I'd go as far to say that this game's soundtrack is similar to the Ninja Gaiden: Yaiba game. It felt generic and repetitive to me.

This game is a good game. I know that I have detailed negative connotations about the game, but it is easy to write negatives about something than positives in my experience. It has some flaws, sure. But if you are itching to play a Souls game. This game has definitely got you covered. Just keep your expectations low, and you will have a great time!

Actually a very enjoyable/good game
I'll try not to compare it to dark souls too often.

The good:

-Great combat vs mobs with a lovely dismember mechanic.

-Gear is acquired a fun way, through that dismember mechanic, no RNG annoyance.

-Looks good and performance is good.

-NG+ adds enemies with randomly equipped gear and allows for upgrading gear to new levels. (Gear can be improved everytime you start a ng+ all the way up to ng+++)

-A lot of weapons and armors (with the free dlc downloaded).

-Walk in the park dlc is a fun change of tone/atmosphere, which is much needed to avoid the feeling of endless industrial complex. (Kind of necessary for me personally).

-Map design is excellent, quite vertical and per area just 1 "bonfire" and just a ton of shortcuts.

-Special weapons from bosses if killed a certain way.

-A lot of different enemies, (even though some get used too many times).

-Artistic style is great on the setting it takes place in, an industrial setting, but that might make you a bit burned out on industrial visuals everywhere, which is why I recommended the dlc to switch it up a bit.)

The bad:

-Sidequests are kinda bad, often not very interesting and the rewards are not really worth it, except for the story/lore/achievement part.

-Music/ambience, the music in this game puts emotional pressure on the danger of the situation, this would be great if it didn't do it so many times I feel nausiated by it, I play this game with music turned off.
Also the medbay music was driving me to insanity.

-Boss music is kinda mediocre, just not memorable like other games.

-Bosses: This game, unlike the famous Dark Souls or even the previous Deck 13 game Lords of the Fallen isn't focussed on boss battles, there are only 5 bosses (plus dlc this number is 7).

Of these bossfights I enjoy 5 (including dlc), but none are like dark souls fights, because this game is not dark souls.

-Some map visuals/corridors are very similar looking, making you get lost easy.
I had more fun after my first run, because then I knew where to go and which shortcuts could be taken.

-Level 0 run, while possible, I gave up due to incredible frustration of the third boss, with the horrible camera in that fight.

Conclusion:

Great game, not something I'd try speedruns in. Fun is in the mob combat and dismember mechanic. But don't buy it for the bossfights.

I look forward to the next Deck 13 game in this genre with anticipation!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is interesting for the first few hours but it gets repetitive and boring later on. It is still a good game but it does not live up expectation.

Pros:
+ Good combat (dodge, execution, combo...)
+ Fluid control, movement
+ Good graphic + good optimization
+ Good and detailed gameplay settings (quite many options)

Cons:
- Boring and bad story narrative
- Character is plain and uninteresting
- Map level is complex and confusing (bad navigation, only one checkpoint per level)
- Uninteresting equipment customization/upgrades - too simple

Conclusion:
I still recommend this game because it is a good game with reasonable price. Pick up if it is on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHE SHORT VERSION:

1. Imagie Dark Souls with no leveling up
2.A Souls game where blocking is delayed
3. How can you play a Souls type game with
no leveling up & a blocking sytem that doesn't
properly work is beyond me

THE LONG VERSION:
I really wanted to like this game. As others said it is very Souls like. Like a Dark Souls in the future. And me being a fan of Dark Souls all the way back to Demon Souls. I thought I was going to love this game. But what makes the Souls games so special is the combat of coarse. Don't get me wrong the game looks beautiful & has some good idea's. But the combat system is just horrible. And it just completely kills the game. I can't help but think what was the develpers thinking? In a Souls game when you swing your weapon you know just what it's going to do...a light attack (fast attack) or a heavy attack (slow attack) a more powerful version of the light attack. You know when it should start & when it's going to end. But in this game when you press R1 one time to do a simple light attack. Sometimes you will swing once & sometimes you'll swing two or three times. In a game like this where you can get one shotted or kill in a instant. The combat needs to be very precise. Or you got it all wrong. And let me say they got it all wrong lol. It doesn't matter if your leveled up high & you have awesome gear. It's like the enemies get stronger as the game goes on. But you don't. And the attacks are insanelly slow. So when your attack decides to go through this slow animation & do some extra swings all own it's own. You just have to sit back & watch as you get one shotted over & over again. What sucks is it's not something you can learn from & correct.


My second gripe is you can't level up. Such as leveling up health or strength. They have something they called implants in this game. In this game you don't level up stuff like strength or health. You spend your points on increasing your power consumption level. In a Souls like game this is just stupidity. And the amount of grinding you have to do just to make it through a level is complete joke. There are even doors that need to be open & it'll have a number on it of how high you power consumption level needs to be. So be prepared to spend hours in like a 30 foot area just grinding... Kill all enemies. Go back to bonfire & start over. And do this over & over, hour after hour. And this is coming from someoneone who doesn't mind some grinding. In games like Nioh or any Souls game. Which are all good games in my eyes (for combat). And when you level up your power consumption so high it lets you use a extra implant. Implants lets you do stuff like being able to heal (estus flask). Which you can get a small amount of through these implants. If you even get the chance to use them. I'm on like the second level after you get off a subway after 1st boss. And I spend a couple days (16 hrs) farming to level up or should I say increase my power cosumption. All these for a level that should have took me 40 minutes to get through. And I'll still get one shotted like nothing. And the games ememy placement is a joke. They place these fast enemies that run fast as hell toward you & they can 1 shot you. And 1 is hard enough with this crappy combat system. And they will stack like 3 or 4 on top of each other. And they do this over & over. This game has so of the worst enemy placement I have ever seen. As a old Souls vet. I say to myself no problem. I'll play it like souls & bait the enemies out 1 at a time. But not in this game. What is really sad is this could have been a classic. There was a few new ideas that I liked such as being able to call in a drone. Cool idea be it really useless. All they had to do was get the combat right & make the progression more balanced. Games like Nioh had a crappy story but the combat was so good it's hard to stop playing. Maybe they will come out with a update & fix these issues. But until then I would keep far away from this game. It's not even playable in my eyes\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably my favorite game of 2017, and there were a lot of good games that year. Fantastic industrial sci fi art design that I find hard to believe was made by the same people who released Lords of the Fallen, immersive atmosphere, intriguing story that in a true Souls fashion will become more interesting the more you think about it, a satisfying and well though out combat system that integrates perfectly with the leveling up and upgrading system. Just make sure you learn the mechanics instead of rushing at it blindly like some people below, body part targetting and dodge counters are crucial to the combat.

One thing you should remember though, if you have a low end computer disable the vsync, it's absolutely broken in this game and will cut you FPS by about two thirds. Other than that the game runs great, even if you're well below the recommended specs.\t\t\t\t\t
