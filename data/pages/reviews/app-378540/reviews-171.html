\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood beginning and bad ending, in a monotonous environment. Different zones have their own styles, but inside one location you can't say where the one path and where another. If paths were color-coded [painted walls or floor, different color for each path], it could be much easier to recall where is what.

Scrap countdown is bad. Often I couldn't get back my tech scrap in time, because I just didn't know where to go.

Enemies are too tough for light weapons. Everything but greathammers can't deal enough DPS, even upgraded. This crushes replayability.

In the second half of the game it is not clear where to go to advance in the plot, and I tried to listen every dialogue and messages. You just don't know where to go.

Maps on walls are useless. Bunch of squares with green "you are here" spot. It doesn't show actual level layout.

Weapons should have infinite combos. Because they are already limited with stamina amount.

Armor set buffs are good. Often I used armor sets just for buffs.

I kinda like leveling system and implants and abilities. Reminds me of witcher 3 abilities system. Once you get better exosuit with more implant cells you are limited only with quality of implants.

Overall production quality is good too. Graphics, music, sounds, animations, overall artstyle is excellent, no doubt.

Aggressive backtracking and few gamedesign flaws made the game annoying in last 3-5 hours.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have completed the game, here is my review. In short I felt the game started off really well, and got let down by some bad game design decisons as it went on. To be slightly more specific, the emphasis on normal enemies, and hilariously long runs between the 'bonfire' and where you wanted to be.

It's hard to either recommend or not recommend this game. Rather than give a list of pros and cons, I'm going to say what's good about it, and then say how they managed to take that good thing, and ♥♥♥♥ it up.

1. The game is heavily inspired by dark souls. I consider this a good thing, as the dark souls formula is a good one to follow. If you don't like dark souls, you will not like this - in fact, you will HATE this. Move on now, this game is not worth your time. For the people still here who like Dark Souls, this game is a shadow of dark souls - I don't think you will get the same satisfaction you get out of playing dark souls.

2. The combat system is a pretty cool idea. You can select and target body parts, and those body parts can be armoured or unarmoured. You might think, "that sounds cool, does that mean that you get to strategically target different part of an depending on where it may be weak?". Well, not really, it's pretty much always the best idea to go for the head - armoured or unarmoured (at least, this is what I found to be most effective). There are two enemies in the game that I can recall where it's beneficial to cut off a tail of some sort but I really think they could have taken this idea further. So what is this body targeting system for you may ask? Grinding materials to make gear. Yes, they took this concept, and made it primarily into a grind mechanic. Cutting off different body parts gives you different items, which you need to make gear. This isn't terrible, but it's a shame that they took such a cool idea and turned it into a grind mechanic where I thought it would be cool to need to enemies in certain ways (the game does this to some extent, but for me, this should have been the focus).

3. The game is really, really hard. I would say it's probably harder than dark souls - albeit maybe a bit shorter. I think this would be a good thing, except, in my opinion, it creates challenge in the wrong ways. Unlike dark souls which has several bonfires sprinkled through an area, this game has a handful of areas with just one 'bonfire' that you unlock various shortcuts to. The paths from where you are to where you need to be become insanely long, with scores of normal enemies in between. And the normal enemies become LUDICROUSLY strong towards the end game. There is a need to go back and grind a little bit for items because it got to a point where enemies would one shot me with hilariously quick and cheap attacks with hyperarmour. This really becomes the focal point of my criticism with this game. I can look over everything else, but the strength of the normal enemies combined with the ridiculously long paths that you need to take creates a massive challenge which I didn't find very fun. Dark Souls takes the approach of creating challenge through difficult bosses that are varied and interesting to fight, while this game chooses to create challenge through long corridors of the same ridiculously hard enemies over and over again. To be honest, the only reason I kept playing this game was to overcome the challenge just to say I beat it, I stopped playing for fun.

4. This is sci-fi dark souls. Cool idea right? Except this games idea of the aesthetic is factory after factory after factory. The first level in this game really draws you in. By the second level, I just wanted out because I found the aesthetic to be almost unbearably boring, with the same enemies over and over. The enemy variety, and art variety are desperately lacking.

5. The boss fights are fun. I really enjoyed learning the different boss fights and they were very challenging and when you finally beat it, you really had to earn it. So what's bad about that? Well, in a 20-25 hour campaign there are only (if you don't want to know how many bosses there are, don't look at the spoiler) five bosses. And yes, they were really fun, and the body targetting system kind of shined here (although it felt clunky at times, especially on the last boss) but there were so few that it felt like a bit of a let down.

6. The exploration in this game looked kind of cool. There's a few gates you can only access when you reach a certain level which creates incentive to backtrack - I like that. I think I would have appreciated a fast travel between the 'bonfires' in this game since there's only a few of them, and navigating the game can be a pain with such a large amount of ludicrously strong normal enemies.

In conclusion, we have a good idea for a game, that is bogged down by creating challenge in the wrong ways. We just didn't need corridors with dozens of enemies that can each one shot you. Tone down the normal enemies, create more interesting set piece encounters, maybe put more focus on elite enemies that despawn after one kill or something. I love hard games, and I did get satisfaction from completing this game due to it's difficulty, I just found that difficulty was created in the wrong way. Finally, the art style absolutely needs more variation along with the enemies, which need to be designed to take advantage of the body targeting system. If you want a challenge, and you want to get this game purely for the challenge - this is the game for you. If you don't like grinding, factory art style, challenge, Dark Souls - stay clear.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis really is like Dark souls, but in a Sci-FI universe. The whole concept is the same, instead of collecting souls, you collect ''Tech scrap'' and that is what you use to level up at the medical bay (The Bonfire equivalent) and if you die, you drop that tech scrap and you have to go back to pick it up without dying. You progress through areas then you unlock shortcuts back to the medical bay, so like I said, exactly the same concept....No multiplayer though unfortunately, so no summoning or invading people. The combat is also very similar and very punishing if you try to get too clever and take on more than one enemy at a time. Graphics are really nice and it runs very smooth at 1440p at 80 - 100 FPS fully maxed out on a GTX 1080 and i7 7700k, and fully supports 21:9 widescreen, so I have no complaints at all there. and this plays perfectly with a mouse and keyboard unlike the souls games, keybindings are so easy to set up to personal preferences, so I think this will make a lot of people happy. This is more story driven like Lords of the fallen, I cant really comment much on the story at this moment, but you dont play games like Dark Souls for the story, you play them because you enjoy being brutally punished over and over again!....and this will happen to you on Surge. Personally I still think Dark souls is a better game, but this is definatly a welcome addition to my games library and would definatly recommend it to any Souls fan!\t\t\t\t\t
