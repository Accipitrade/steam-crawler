\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is one of those games that rode the Dark Souls bandwagon and almost did a very good job at it, but in my honest opinion, there are some points that it fails miserably.
The game takes place in a futuristic setting, where machines took over and people became mindless slaves. Pretty much clich√© to most things that we are used to seeing, but ok. The gameplay is very similar to DS games. Kill enemies, get "souls" (in this game: Scrap) spend souls on skills or items. Very standard stuff, but here comes one point that made me stop playing it for a while: the timer to get it all back if you die.
I know that most people stick with the "git gud" argument, and that's fine. But I think that this is beyond skill. Anyone can rush to the place that you died and retrieve your Scrap, but my question is: why? I think that one of the points that makes Dark Souls a great series is to go through the same path many times to memorize the place and get even more Souls on the process. I really don't see a point to rush, sometimes with a vague memory of the layout because you've only been there once and died once. And if you accidentally take a wrong turn... oof.. 4 relentless enemies behind you will kill you and you lose all your scrap for good. That was a "take a break" point for me. I was mid-end game, but had to take a break.
Another thing that I didn't enjoy so much was the setting in the game. Early game you have a very open area, with a little bit of indoor environment. But after that, it's all the inside of factories, offices, labs, etc. It's very bland and boring. Most games that have a dark and eerie setting don't really need to be all indoors. You can have exterior areas with that feeling. But, again, mho.

The game does have some positive points. I loved the mechanic of hitting specific limbs to do more damage and get the armor from that limb so that you can collect a set. I thought that was really fun and kept me looking for different enemies to kill and collect all of the parts of a set. Some enemies were very tough and needed patience and learning to take them down and get the prize.
I really liked the fighting as well. Parrying, jumping, stunning, it's all very fresh and satisfying to do when you're fighting a difficult enemy.

I gave a negative review because I think that if a game makes me stop playing it for a while because of a nonsensical mechanic, it's not a well thought game 100%. I understand that the devs want to have as many elements as possible different from Dark Souls, but if you're already going for that recipe, stick with the things that you KNOW that work. In my country we have a saying that goes something like this: Don't change a team that's winning. And I think that this game misses this in this critical spot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not a huge souls-like fan, I usually don't have the patience to beat my head against the gameplay until it clicks. That being said, I did get through and enjoy Bloodborne so I'm at least a little familiar with the style.

I really liked this game. I've heard that it's ridiculously hard (zeropunctuation, etc) and I have to think that's just because the reviewer was too accustomed to how things are done by another developer. I did try Lords of the Fallen and absolutely hated it, so I'm not saying Deck 13 have it all figured out, but this one sure felt great. Once I figured out how to make the combat work for me, and got a build going that I really clicked with, I really loved the experience. The environmental storytelling was much better than I have seen in other genre games, and the satisfaction you get when you see an enemy with gear you want and just cutting it off of them with a finishing move never got old, even after I had the armor I wanted maxed out.

The visuals are really good for a AA game, and there's some solid audio for the combat and all. The environments can get a little samey in each area and really are a maze at first go, but if you look at the signs around you, they are actually useful for finding your way. Some animations get a bit janky when there are objects nearby or ledges, but I only found it was 10% of the time. The pathfinding in the game once you know what to look for is a lot of fun, and I found myself doing completely unnecessary things just to try and find more secrets. You can find some pretty late game enemies hidden midgame if you dig around and aren't afraid to lose some scrap.

If you're having a hard time, just farm some scrap and level up. With the solid combat, that's not much of an issue, since I found it to be fun. Every now and then you will get completely destroyed by enemies and every time I found myself stuck in a corner in a stunlock cascade that I couldn't escape, it was my fault for not being cautious and getting too many enemies on me at once.

Given how cheap this game is nowadays, I highly recommend picking it up and giving it a try if you think a souls-like with better movement and somewhat easier combat sounds interesting.\t\t\t\t\t
