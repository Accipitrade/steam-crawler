\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was a suprise for me. For the record, I loved the Souls games and even really liked Lords of the Fallen for what it was. I bought this game but didn't get to playing it right away. I'm almost sorry I waited so long. I didn't have huge expectations and the reviews were kind of luke warm. Ultimately though, the game was really good from start to finish for me. I liked the combat system, I thought it was quite excellent actually. Similar to but not exactly like a Souls game. I ended up really enjoying unlocking new armor, testing out sets of armor to see what their set bonus was and liked trying out differnet weapons. The story was ultimately pretty interesting. The one issue for me is the audio logs and everything are actually way better on the second play through. Many speak of things and people you encounter way later in the game, but they are almost background noise till you know more about the characters. For example, there is an audio log in the first part of the game that talks about Dr Chavez. I had actually forgotten entirely about this by the time I met her. But on a subsequent playthrough I found the log enjoyable because it let on that there is more to this world than what is right in front of you. It let about a story where workers that were not you had thoughts and feelings about important events in the games story, and it felt more impactful once you actually had your own interactions with Dr Chavez.

I liked the gear upgrade and unlock system quite a bit. I liked going for the hardcore kills of bosses to get better weapson, I like how thinking about gear upgrades actually made me consider what part of enemies to attack. Also, in many cases I would think about whether it's woth going after the parts I needed or if my stims were in bad shape, I may just find a weak unprotected area to attack so I minimized my chances of death. It very much as that same effect of souls games where the amount of scrap you are carrying makes you think about what exactly you want to do in a given situation.

The game is not perfect though, as you can imagine. I disliked how confusing the maps were, which were one of the main complaints in other reviews. I appreciate how they tried to loop everything back around to the start of each area quite thoroughly as a way to get back to the ops center. But in the second area, which acts as something of a main hub to get to and return from other areas, becomes a confusing mess of corridors as the game progresses. There were multiple occasions where I stumbed back into areas I had already been. There is also some enemies in there at actually represent more annoyance than anything else. They are some kind of repair bot that have a ridiculous amount of health. You get no worthwhile drops from them and earlier on they can two hit you if you aren't careful. I would be fine with them as they are except they are hostile towards you like anything else it. It would have been way better if they had just made it so they weren't hostile unless being attacked by the player. It would be a nice ambiance effect of the area you are in rather than a hostile, hard to kill enemy that you try to avoid at all costs because the risk/reward is very lopsided towards risk with little reward.

Overall, I think I liked the game quite a bit though. There were some annoyances with the map and how it loops back on itself, but overall that meant very little in regards to how much I enjoyed this game. I would have liked more than 5 bosses though, perhaps more weapons or gear to try out as well. But what was there felt impactful and was worth considering how you wanted to build Warren around gear and implants. Combat felt very good, very responsive and active with much to consider in many encounters. I was a bit disappointed with NG+ only because I wish they did more to change the world on a subsequent playthrough. Little changes and tweaks throughout the world do exist, but I would have liked to see something like enemies being in different locations and maybe some optional hard enemies thrown in a bit more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who has enjoyed Demons Souls, Dark Souls and Lords of the Fallen, I can thankfully say this game brings enough new ideas to stand above them all. Having played this for a good chunk of time, lost sleep and eagerly going on, I find myself enjoying it a great deal.

Here is where this game mechanics mainly differs from the others; classes. In a the afore mentioned titles a player would be stuck on a specifc style of fighting (heavy, fast, magic) without being able to swap unless they started a new game and made a new build. Not this game.

The surge allows you to adapt in fantastic ways to new challanges by managing what your 'rig' or base suit is capable of through interchangeable nodes. I found myself struggling with some enemies until I changed my strategy, the freedom of this change is honestly a wonderful feature and I praise Deck13 for its brilliance.

I think of The Surge as a challenging souls type game but with a great deal more thinking. Strategies need to be made per enemy, maps are more like maddening mazes, and the satisfaction piecing it all together is sweet.

People complain that there are too few bosses, I disagree. While limited every encounter feels special and the fewer bosses have allowed some very creative ideas into what they can be.

Honestly speaking, I grew bored of the Souls series as it repeats the formula but in a different labcoat with some light adjustments, and I dont expect to make friends with such a bold statement. The Surge does have a different coat, but a different formula without being unfamiliar.

--------------------------------------------------------------

If you like a fun game, this is for you.
If you dont like the Souls games, this is for you.

Try the demo and enjoy this brilliant game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Good
Armor farming, this is an amazing mechanic that more games like this need to have. Farming for gear becomes a 10-minute side quest instead of 30-45 minutes of grinding.

Little to no grinding needed for scrap.

Great story and graphics

Really liked all the choice for armor and weapons

The Bad
Awful map design, the map becomes a cubic maze and with no map, going back to find something you missed becomes a pain. The number of times I’ve died because of the gaps in the floors is far too many. Taking turns too sharply can have you dying before you know what happened.

Poorly designed enemies, towards the end most the enemies have a stun ability that locks you down so they can get a free shot. The drones aren’t much better, many times they are far above where I can hit them and the drones you’re given don’t have much of a punch.

Timed scrap retrieval, this is just a poor attempt at artificially making the game harder. Being reckless and dying while getting your scrap back is fine but having it time out because you got lost in a maze isn’t.

Over all I wouldn’t recommend this game to a friend at the current $50 price.


Requested Edit
I've played Dark souls 1, 2, and 3 the challenge is similar but different. Dark souls requires you to study the enemy movements so you can stay close and avoid damage. The Surge has you gunning hard towards the enemy and backing off when they swing back (if not dead already.)

The bosses in Dark souls were fairly straight forward, very few times there were BS moments. The Surge also has bosses with no BS moments either, but the bosses have more puzzle components as opposed to straight combat. I'd say the only boss that isn't straight forward would be the first boss. I was stumped, it was only until I tried to do a kamikaze run did I figure out the secret.

Over all the difficulty is less than Dark souls but this isn't a bad thing. The surge still offers plenty of different challenges for the player to adapt to as opposed to DS.\t\t\t\t\t
