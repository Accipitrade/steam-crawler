\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCons:
- navigating through locations can be tricky due to the somewhat monotonous design
- bosses aren't very original of hard enough to kill
- infrequent troubles with hitboxes
- the drone could be more useful
- content shortage
Pros:
+ local sci-fi zombie-apocalypse as setting
+ the leveling system is based on the power of the core, which is spent on armor, stat increases, active skills and the drone
+ simple albeit enjoyable combat system with different weapon movesets
+ sectoral damage system, which is tied to the system of upgrading equipment by cutting off the necessary body parts of enemies
+ the vast majority of weapons and armor sets are also obtained by chopping off body parts
+ the presence of an intelligible plot, which isn't a frequent phenomenon for the soulslike subgenre
+ the DLC's are quite good (especially A Walk In The Park, which broadens the plot)
+ nice touches of black humour
+ backtracking isn't very tiresome

The atmosphere and gameplay of this game is incredibly addictive, but only if you can get used to the questionable structure of the locations (but to be honest, a lot of similar games suffer from this problem). A must-have for souls like fans.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA future where the world is on the brink of an ecological catastrophe. Many resources have already been exhausted. The seas and oceans are drying up. The only salvation for people is the "Utopia" experiment, which can save everyone.
We play as a wheelchair user who decides to get a job as a CREO by getting an exoskeleton and starting to walk again. But what can go wrong?

    Pros:
  • + Optimization
  • + Achievements
  • + Trading cards
  • + Works great on ultra-settings on weak PCs
  • + Built-in photo mode
  • + Possibility to collect the equipment of the character - in parts
  • + Each type of enemy has its own tactical approach
  • + Extraction of equipment by chopping off the limbs of opponents
  • + System targeting certain unprotected areas of the enemy to facilitate combat
  • + Colorful finishing off opponents (At times)
  • + Two finals
  • + New Game+

    Minuses:
  • - Very small text in item descriptions
  • - It turns out that the game has nothing to do with the film "Edge of Tomorrow", which starred Tom Cruise.
  • - Any change in graphics requires restarting the game
  • - No map
  • - Revival of opponents after saving
  • - No idea where to go next
  • - Complex and unpredictable system of combat and behavior of opponents
  • - Confusing, monotonous locations
  • - When conducting combo attacks, the player loses control over the character
  • - Increasing the level, only opens additional cells for implants
  • - F4 - suicide
  • - The plot is revealed only in the case of finding and listening to audio diaries
  • - Identical improvements do not stack (although they should)
  • - Unstoppable hero attacks (e.g. to jump away)
  • - Opponents are able to pull themselves up to the character during some attacks
  • - The hero is magnetized to opponents during finishing moves (and can fall into the abyss, because they are not interrupted)
  • - Executions in the narrow spaces of the corridors (Character and enemy in different places)
  • - Hounds are able to attack the character through heavy iron boxes
  • - The hero droid is able to deal damage to the enemy through walls/obstacles
  • - Attacks are strictly horizontal (Fight on stairs)
  • - When you exit the game with alt + f4, the game closes the steam client with it
  • - Freezing/closing the game when using alt+tab
  • - Unresponsive character controls
  • - Lack of an elementary list of tasks
  • - Final game
  • - Items for equipment/weapon upgrades are hard to find.

Total:
A rather controversial game that I bought thinking - that this is a continuation of the story of the movie with Tom Cruise "Edge of Tomorrow". After all, even the main character vaguely resembles a poorly made Cruz figure! But not the presence of this figure, nor the exosuits, and even more so the Nanite Opponents, are in no way connected with him.
We have an attempt to make Dark Souls in the future, and this attempt failed.
The player starts running around the location over and over again and constantly dies in order to start all over again. Loses the parts he has collected, which can help with improvements - this is especially sad on Bosses. But if he nevertheless conveys them to the medical station, when he leaves, he again meets all the opponents that have revived in this second.
The game has a plot - but you won't see it if you don't search all the corners and all kinds of rooms - in search of audio diaries. Therefore, you can go through the game without understanding what is happening around.
In addition to the main storyline (if you can call it that), there are additional tasks. Which the player can safely forget about, since they are not indicated anywhere. And after a while - they end due to the deaths of NPCs
By the way, there is absolutely no map in the game. Therefore, players will run for hours along terribly confusing corridors - to understand where to go at all.
Sad things are with the combat. So during the start of a strike or combo, the player cannot interact with the hero in any way. You can't even interrupt an attack to dodge.
An interesting mechanic of the battle, one can note the choice of the limbs of opponents - to facilitate the battle, as well as chopping them off - in order to pick up some of the equipment. True, not everything is going smoothly here either, and the player can fly over the map into the abyss - if during the execution, the enemy will fall there. By the way, executions look great in open and large spaces. But terribly ridiculous - in the narrow corridors where most of the game will take place.
If by some miracle you made it to the final, then you will find two denouements. Which in no way affect the beginning of the second part. What is sad.

I would like to recommend this game to fans of the Dark Souls series. But she is so dull and boring that even these hardcore people are likely to score on her and throw her into the far corner.\t\t\t\t\t

I really wanted to like this game, but the combat is just so bad. It's clunky and slow, enemies don't react consistently to hits, everything feels clumsy and unresponsive. Your attacks have these ridiculously long animations that freeze you in place, so if an enemy decides it just doesn't feel like being staggered you're stuck there while they hit you.

You also know the dev team has no imagination. Every enemy in the first couple of stages was some variant of "robot guy" scattered haphazardly around the level. Also, most of the mechanics are lifted straight from the souls series. Fog walls, "bonfire" where you level up, souls (they're called Tech Bits or something similarly uninspired) that you drop when you die and are recoverable, "this door does not open from this side", consumables that give you Tech Whatevers, the list goes on. It's painful how blatant is.

Also, the guy is a paraplegic who can only walk using a mech suit. If his legs don't work at all, then why does running drain his stamina? Is the mech suit getting tired?

Anyway, it's the same dev team that crapped out Lords of the Fallen, so I should have known better. I'll be giving them a miss in future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYour mileage may vary the review. Writing this review as I go,so it'll be updated with my notes as I progress.

I like Soulsborne games. I loved Remnant(Dark Souls with guns!) I liked DS1/2. They felt fair,they felt rewarding for my time,and learning how enemies work,and how to counter their moves properly.

Surge is..weird? I don't know how to describe it. The combat,once you get in the groove,feels great. The weapons so far are pretty unique and interesting(seriously,you get a chainsword like weapon.what's not to love?)

Surge feels like you are about to get fisted in the most unfair ways possible. One on one fights,are pretty managable usually unless the enemy just randomly decides 'no u' to your stagger attempts(yes,I've tried using the heavy blade).

The early game is..brutal so far. And not fun brutal. A single mistake usually means getting pummeled by 2 or(in the worst case if you misjumped due to the frustrating camera and didn't see them) more enemies at once.

The first boss is really..unintuitive. But most of the game sadly is. Not a whole lot makes sense,and dying and losing your resources is frustrating,especially when you're trying to figure out where to go,everything feels like it's circling back to itself,and it's kinda annoying at times when you're trying to figure out what to do.

The story and world are really neat too. While I don't expect the best VA or writing,it's clear they made a decent attempt.

The art is so cool! The world is interesting. The weapons are neat,the rigs are sweet. The cutscenes are so well done that I was thinking I was playing a much younger game at first. The combat beats are good.

I *want* to like this game,but I wish Steam had a meh icon. It's not bad. But it's clearly got some issues. But YMMV. I do think this game would be better with a controller that I don't have since I feel like Souls like games almost always play better on controllers. But I also might be a rusty old bolt who can't get into the proper groove.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBuy this game on sale, you will not regret it. Game starts out laughably bad... I can't spoil it but your character can't exactly walk in the very first few moments of the game... which walking is super important to a Dark Souls clone lol

IDK why people don't talk about that because in other DkS clone games, that would be a huge red flag. Anyway, this game is super fun. I only have 2hrs in the game and I'm still in the beginning area (The fuel station underneath the first area is too challenging for me at the moment; I prob need to go scrap hunting for another 2 hrs lol).

This game has fun combat and a dismemberment system (It's not like Dead Space; it's more like VATS from Fallout) where you can chop off someone's armour/weapon for a change to snag it (IF and ONLY IF the piece you want is highlighted in orange, meaning it is armored).

Take your time during battle; try to aggro one enemy at a time. This game is brutally hard, and this is coming from a guy who has finished half of the campaign of Dark Souls 1... which is a relatively easy game in comparison (don't judge me, I got stuck at the silverknight archers in Anor Lando because IDK how to flip them off the platform in order to pass; dodging the projectiles was the easy part). Dark Souls has a movement system where if you aggro enemies, they kinda slowly step back so you can strategise your next move; it's subtle but it's there. This game fails at that and if you have 2+ aggros on your ass, you will die 80% of the time, no questions asked.

Gear Upgrades/Core Boosts (Basically stat changes and level ups) really don't do much here either, but you might have more stability if you create gear rather than upgrade them, just because the stat boosts are minuscule compared to what you fight; with the exception being upgrading weapons.

If you hate grinding, go play Dark Souls and don't bother with The Surge. This game is grindier than Dark Souls because of how upgrades/mechanics work. This game expects you to grind, grind, grind, and only make small progressions as you do so. Prob one of the weakest points of the game along with the story.

The story.... hmmm. I spoiled myself and looked it up online knowing I'm gonna spend about 50hrs on this game and not understand it... because the game's story is the biggest complaint on the internet. I wasn't spoiled because the game's story is "Plotholes: The Video Game". Not even diehard fans of this game understand the plot, and the sequel makes things worse with the lore/story lol

All in all, this game is flawed, but has mechanics that improve upon the original Dark Souls trilogy and I commend Deck13 for that. I will be playing The Surge alot, I'll see you in Creo.\t\t\t\t\t
