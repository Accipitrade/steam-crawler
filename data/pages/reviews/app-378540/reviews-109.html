\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStop comparing The Surge to Souls games. Thank you.

Cons
- Level design is repetitive, lots of indoors area, hallways, etc.
- Boss battles are really weak for the most part. Only 5 bosses in the whole game and almost every one is way too easy. The last boss was a joke too.
- Didn't really enjoy the target-system, it gets repetitive fast. Dunno why everyone seems to like that, just because somethings new doesn't mean it's good.

Pros
- Combat is really fun. It's flashy and fast, paired with the overall looks of the game and the weapons it's marvellous. Additionaly, playing this on a 144hz monitor is bliss.
- Graphics are nice. Environment or Weapons, everything looks neat.
- Story's cool, it was a pleasant change for me since I've never really played a sci-fi game. I'd say anyone can enjoy the story. The audio logs that you can find throughout the game add another layer of depth to the story too.
- A lot of possible builds since the implant system offers many different boosts. Combined with the many different archetypes of weapons, armor and drones, you get a lot of options. It's also cool how you can basically change yourr 'build' anytime you want.
- This game is really hard. I never really felt safe up until late-game, because basically every little slip-up means death. I really enjoyed that kind of pressure the game puts on you.

All in all I definitely dont regret buying this game for 15 bucks. I'd recommend it to anyone who likes hard games paired with flashy combat and a semi-interesting story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTen hours into The Surge and it's very hard to describe this experience. The idea of a sci-fi hack 'n slash is outstanding and they've carried it off really well. The overall design is excellent, the environments, the gear, it's all very well designed and strikingly creative. The combat is also very good, it takes a fair amount of time to learn but most of the time it works fluidly and effectively and it's extremely satisfying.

But it seems they've tried to outdo Dark Souls in both size and complexity of level design and overall difficulty, particularly boss fight difficulty. Levels are huge and maze-like, full of hidden passages and shortcuts, and it's easy to become disoriented and completely lost. There's no map, no indication of where you're supposed to be going and what you're supposed to be doing. You have to break every crate because it's probably hiding a hidden door or elevator. And tough combat is constant, everywhere you go. You grind and explore for a long time and eventually find a switch, which you flip without really knowing what it's for. Then you grind and explore for another hour until you randomly discover that the switch turned on the power to a train that will take you to another area. But you only discover these things after running down innumerable branching corridors that loop around in all sorts of ways and getting killed innumerable times along the way. The level design is fascinating in it's intricacy but slows the game down to a crawl, there's really very little sense of progression, just wandering around for hours trying to find a shortcut back to a familiar area.

And the combat is extremely challenging, doable if you really practice and get the hang of it, but you frequently run into new types of enemies with unfamiliar attacks, and when they inevitably kill you it's invariably a long slog through numerous respawned enemies just to get back to that area for another try, more of a slog it seems than in the Dark Souls games.The combat can work well but nothing is really well explained in the game, and Iv'e had to do a fair amount of wiki checking to just to figure out some of the basics.

The key to it is mainly figuring out the upgrade system. You have a limited number of slots for health items and other buffs. You need to level up to get more slots. But before long you've got maybe six slots for items and twenty different items you could use. So what's going to be best, the item that gives you some health back on a successful finishing move, or the buff that make your weapon do more damage ? You have numerous choices and you have to find the right combination of upgrades that best matches your playstyle,

I think that The Surge really is a great game, like Lords of the Fallen it's more than just a Dark Souls clone, it has it's own unique character and challenges.In some ways it's like Dark Souls meets Doom. But I've spent more than a thousand hours on the Dark Souls games and I wouldn't put it on quite that level. The Surge places too much emphasis on constant, difficult combat and challenging navigation,without the intriguing environments and characters that make the Dark Souls games so engaging. It can be fun and engaging but only if you're psychologically prepared for a very different and frequently frustrating experience, particularly early on. I'm not yet that far into it and I'm planning on carrying on but I can see that this might very well just turn out to be one of the few games I can't finish.\t\t\t\t\t

+ Limb targeting system that works well into the immersion and setting of the game. Also allows the player to harvest loot from said dismembered limbs

I found myself using armor I got in the beginning of the game straight to the end because each gear set has a unique set bonus

+ Varied (enough) environments

but it also had it's flaws

- Kind of a disjointed story. Well..... That is more due to the story TELLING rather than the actual story

- No character costomization. I understand you want to make a narrative with Warren but at least let us change his face around

I could write a lot about the combat but in short, it's just not satisfying.

TL;DR i would not recommend this game, even more so if you like souls games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tObligatory ***YET ANOTHER SOULS GAME***.

With that out of the way, this might be one of the most underrated titles of 2017.
The engine is polished, the combat is slick and satisfying. The dystopian scifi aesthetic tickles me in all the right ways.

This is clearly inspired by Souls, but definitely not shadowed by it. It stands on it's own, and stands tall.
I'm about 40 hours in, probably nearing the end of my first playthrough and I just can't put this game down.

A lot of love and thought has gone into this game, attention to detail is abundant here.
One of my favorite things is the somber country song that plays whenever you're near or at "Operations" (Bonfire equivalent). It doesn't matter how brutal your last fight or death was, the music at Operations is like a soothing reminder of the fragility of your existence.

9/10, maybe 9.5. We'll see after I finish the game. But I just don't want it to end.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like Dark Souls/Bloodborne, you'll like this.

The combat is similar, yet more intricate. Can target individual limbs, etc. Faster too.

Many visually unique weapons and armor, and a drone that can assist in cool ways, like an aoe frost slowdown, a magnet-pull, long range laser, and more.

Some bosses are VERY COOL, perfectly executed (FAIR) with many attacks and great animations (Google Firebug or Big Sister fight!!!)
Addendum: only 5 bosses in game, with 2-3 forms each, next one needs 3x more!

The levels inter-connect even better than Dark Souls - shortcuts are not behind one-way doors, or ladders needing to be lowered, but just well-hidden. You WILL feel silly when a huge shortcut was under your nose, ready to be used the whole time.

The enviorments have great atmosphere, darkness and lighting is used very well.
Could use some more visual variety in the levels and enemies, though.

Graphics are great, the game play is tight.
Lots of voice acting. Story was good, but the ending was sequel setup.
BET YOUR ASS I BUY THE SEQUEL THO!

Give it a shot!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI debated over getting this game for several months because I tend to stick to only purchasing games with a 'very positive' rating or over. At this point, after having completed The Surge, I'm going to revise that approach....

Mixed...? what the hell?

Sure, it's relatively challenging so there's certainly a difficulty ceiling, and other 'Souls-like' purists will argue that the combat feels somewhat simplistic, but it's a solid game for sure!

Some other reviews have noted that the environments don't change to a great degree, and they're right, but the aesthetic overall and the level of detail is great that this wasn't really a deal-breaker for me personally. Others have also highlighted a lack of variety in enemy types. Most oponents encountered throughout the game are humanoids of either a heavy or lite flavour which can get repetitive.

To focus on its strengths though:
  • Fast, responsive combat.
  • A few dozen weapons to choose from
    (I only ended up trying out a handful of weapons so I can't say how much they might vary your play-style).
  • Interesting level design with plenty of irregular elevation to break up the monotony of corridors.
  • Delivery of the story is slow but it's a compelling enough narrative that kept me interested.
  • Good looking game! although the Rig (exoskeleton) textures were a tad too blurry for something you spend a lot of your time looking at
    (had settings at mostly 3/4 - high).

At any rate, there's a demo, so give it a shot. Don't miss out on this if you're a fan of action RPG's! \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat would happen if Elon Musk went hollow?

Inspired by the concepts of the latest action RPG's, The Surge puts the genre in a futuristic setting with strong focus on close combat.
CREO - scientific giant of a corporation - tries to improve humankind in various ways: exosuits, bio-engineering, rockets, you name it! You enlist as one of the personnel, but during your exo-rig installation something goes wrong. When you come to your senses, the whole facility has gone crazy and you're trying to understand the situation.

As you embark on your story-driven adventure, you aren't given too many hints or directions, except for hidden audiologs and rare NPC's. The NPC storylines seem quite similar to the Souls series, as you follow the people through their perils into a finale, caused by your actions. Listen closely and listen well, because your next objective might be lost in one sentence of a dialogue.

Pressured with the feeling of being lost goes well with the exploration aspect of the game. Intricate routes through the giant facility lead you far away from your safe haven just intertwine and return you back. Coming back through a new path gives you this feeling of discovery and offers a moment of relief after you start losing hope. Stellar level building.

The setting overall seems to have a refreshing "functionality over good looks" aesthetic. Everything seems clean and colour-coded, but very grounded and believable compared to other sci-fi games out there. This comes at a price though: with such lack of landmarks you will often get lost in a maze of similar service tunnels, especially in the second half of the game. Don't get me wrong, these are some sexy service tunnels!

Another game element that you'll experience a lot is combat. Sometimes clunky with targeting controls, sometimes not the prettiest camera angles and quite a learning curve. Behind these small frustrations lies close combat with tactical dismembering, which brings more and more joy, the more upgraded your gear is. I personally went with a single-rigged PAX weapon for most of the game, but in the late-game switched to twin-rigged Firebug Throttle v2.0 and had a blast!
The bosses are cool and not too hard to beat for the most part, but the Cerberus guy seemed cheap and gimmicky. Look him up if you struggle in this fight.

The roleplaying part is where the game truly shines. With character building through implants, weapons and armour-sets, The Surge abandons starting classes entirely and lets you "respec" at any moment, changing the way you play. The idea is simple: you have core power limit and cannot equip anything past it; to raise core power and upgrade gear - go out and earn scrap. Building on this simple idea all the systems interact and come together beautifully, and THAT was a highlight for me.

Now let's see if the devs will expand upon the good features of the game in The Surge 2.

tl;dr: really good game, if you can look past rare clunky controls, try it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game's intro is WAY better than it deserves to be - and it's too bad it was tied to a game this rough.

Dark Souls is almost sexual in the way it frustrates you - it kicks you in the stomach but then let's you cop a quick feel right before driving it's stiletto into your crotch. It gives and takes - back and forth - until you run out of sexual comparisons and you play it all over again.

The Surge is like trying to have sex with your blender - just as painful as previously mentioned but with none of the good parts. And at the end your smoothy is going to be VERY salty... and bloody... and - oh look - I think that bit was improtant...

There's a challenge to playing games like Dark Souls where the entire system is based around hardness and unforgivability. A specific kind of playerbase and fanbase required - granted - but Dark Souls, while not perfect, deserves the accolades and respect it's earned.

This game tries to garner and replicate that respect - but requires you to get wasted and then drag your nether regions over sand paper... and that's BEFORE you pick up the controller. By that point you'll either be too drunk or too miserable to realize - hey! This game isn't PUNISHING... it's just a mess.

The controls have all the responsibility of a paraplegic lap-dancer; I.E. it CAN function... just not as well as the others - really. Not only does the character respond a good half a second (sometime a whole second) AFTER you press a direction or an action; but you're CONSTANTLY surrounded by this 'aura' (like a cloak of limited visibility - points to people who get that reference) that pops open crates and busts the surrounding area... and... knocks over things you're trying to interact with - or pushes you off of ledges - see, here's the thing, Dark Souls is hard because the enemies are CHALLENGING and the puzzles are COMPLEX... this game is hard because it just WON'T do what you WANT it to do!!! See that picture - right there? You know! The promotional image that they cropped badly so it looks like the chainsaw is coming out of your crotch? That's what this game is like... trying to have sex with a chainsaw hanging from your scrote! This MASSIVE metal BLOCK just HANGING from everything that would NORMALLY be used for PLEASURING you - only instead it's the agony everyone ELSE told you it would be when you first strapped this thing to yourself and jumped into bed.

MEANWHILE...

The level design has all the organization and structure of... well... the average factory :P speaking as a manager for a local factory I can tell you - YES! ♥♥♥♥ REALLY is this all over the place... I wouldn't want to spend any time there if I didn't have to, but - there you go! If you didn't want to have a CLUE where you were - requiring there to be bright lines and maps all over the ding-dong place then; DING DING DING DING DING! Mission achieved! Unfortunately - those lines and maps that you have in the REAL world... AREN'T in this game. So you're going to be back-tracking a LOT. And, because of the lighting, there may be doors - or windows - or tunnels you can't even SEE until an enemy happens to throw you in that direction.

There's too few weapon options - VERY limited customizability - I mean, if you're going to cram Dark Souls, bits of Fallout and bits of the Witcher into ONE game... you'd at least want to make the bits you cram into the game GOOD. Unfortunately, this was not what was done. The DLC tries to mitigate this problem with more weapons and armor and upgrades - BUT - because of the way this stuff is added - you outlevel it almost instantly.

The targeting options - tied to the controls, but bad enough to require their own area - are a JOKE. You can target a specific limb - or you can rapid-fire your targeting; resulting in two problems. Neither of which that can be solved. If you target a limb - you're STUCK targeted to that limb. And it's it's armored it'll take FOREVER to get that health down and - early in the game - you can't WAIT for the health to go down. So you switch to the rapid-fire targeting option; but wait! Gear drops and loot are tied to which limb you hacked off... and with the rapid-fire option you've got one second - MAYBE two - before the enemy moves and your target switches and that ARM you wanted to chop off - with the gear you NEED for your upgrade - is now a leg and - ♥♥♥♥ ME I JUST EXECUTED THAT ♥♥♥♥♥!!!

So, you have to ask yourself, punk... do you feel like lucky?

On the whole the game is really REALLY rough - which isn't surprising. The developer is relatively new at their job - and the genre of sadism games is ALSO new - so, with time and training, Surge 2: The Resurging - Second Resurgance - Part B has the potential to be AWESOME... that being said, unless you're looking to be frustrated (and not in the aformentioned almost sexual way that Dark Soul's frustrates you) then this is NOT the game for you.

So, on the whole, put that bloody smoothy down and just watch a letsplay.\t\t\t\t\t

Maps are repetitive, they feel claustrofobic, and there is no sense of progression as each save point takes you back to the 'base' by unlocking a 'hidden' path to it (this is of course subjective).

Enemies are repetitive, and the 'target weak spot' and 'target armor spot to get the blueprint for it' gets pretty old after the first hour.
|Some enemies are way overpowered and too many for the zone they are in. If you've played DS3, it's like the first knight you get to fight, but 3-4 of them at the same time.
I understand that as you get to an area, you are 'underleveled', but I feel it's pretty dumb to expect playes to farm to progress in a single player game. If I waste 2 hours farming xp, I lose immersion. And farm for what? The next area will force you to do it again. And again.

Bosses are just hard and unforgiving. I would not call them uninspired, but pretty meh. Sometimes they feel unfair, and I did not get a feeling of accomplisment once I beat them. It was just a 'finally, hope I never fight it again' feeling. This coming from a complete newbie spending 2 hours fighting the first boss in DS - and that was hard but felt great.

In conclusion: if you like almost mindless farming and don't care about story/immersion, it could be an average game.\t\t\t\t\t
