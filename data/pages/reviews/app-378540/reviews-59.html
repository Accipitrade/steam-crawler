\r\n\t\t\t\t\t33 hours in and I'm not finished yet so my final score will probably change depending on the plot progression but overall it's a solid 7/10 for me in terms of enjoyability.

+graphic design is very nice, maps look great
+combat is definitely the best part of this game. Challenging to master but once you get it right it's really fun
+the story so far is OK. It's not revolutionary or anything, it's been done many times already but it's fine. Some HQ cutscenes would be welcome
+I bought it on sale and lenghtwise it's definitely worth its price
+I enjoy the difficulty although some tight spaces can be very hard to navigate when there are enemies, mostly due to the enemy-focused camera squeezing into small rooms; once you get strong enough basic enemies can be killed very easily but they still deal a good amount of damage if you make a mistake. Which to me is enjoyable beacuse I still need to stay focused even if I'm dealing with small fry

- could do with less gore sounds
- some parts do get really repetitive
- I'd really enjoy a map in inventory so I don't have to memorize all parts. I think it would be great if the maps were 3d since every location is multilevel
- I could do with more engaging plot
- maybe it's just me but even though there is a whole buffet of weapons I stick to MG Caldarium (single handed). I'll use a staff here and there but that's about it for me. Twin rigged weapons get interrupted way too easily, heavier ones have a long pending time and I never time them right. So even though there is an abundance of fighting equipment I pretty much don't enjoy it since it's not really well balanced (at least in my opinion, maybe I suck at combat)

Spoilers ahead
Once you get past the captain of security (Cerberus) bossfight the game gets significantly easier since you can equip more implants. It was at that point when I finally picked up the pace of navigating CREO without being paranoid about dying at every corner.
Sidequest can be quite confusing. I particularly regret killing Lizzie whom I attacked because at that point of the game I was paranoid about the robots and didn't even take a good look at her red shell. Poor Lizzie and her dad. \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf the problems with the combat weren't present, I would heartily recommend. However, there are so many small frustrations that all compound to make this a largely unpleasant game to play. In point form, the negatives:

  • Blocking is pointless. You can't move while blocking, and it takes MORE Stamina to block an attack than to just dodge it, or strafe or draw back. Literally no need to ever block
  • Stupid limb-targeting which BARELY works. I had to remap inputs all over the place to just get the F__KING game to target a body part, which it now does but only intermittently and only the body part the game feels like targeting
  • Cannot change your locked-on targeted enemy. Must unlock, then look at new foe, then HOPE the game locks on the right foe. Multi-foe combat becomes a nearly-impossible nightmare as a result
  • Tedious, cramped level design making is extremely easy to become disoriented. Very few markers are placed around to give you a clue where to go
  • Baffling interface for the upgrade modules. At one point I wasn't able to heal anymore, and it took me literally 30 minutes fooling around trying to figure out WHY. Even though the healing input (B Button) was displayed and the healing sound would happen, my guy wouldn't heal. HOLY SMOKES how could you make something so baffling when it's a core part of the game?
  • "Finishers" are a core part of the fighting, and certain modules proc when you do a Finish. Problem? Insufficient i-frames at the end of the animation and before control is given back to the player... so you better hope there isn't another enemy near you or you're guaranteed to get hit and hard. This makes the whole "Finishing" aspect of the game a punitive experience
  • I could go on...

The bottom line is that there are too many frustrations for me to keep going. Which is a shame because on paper, this game has everything I should want: Upgrade-able parts, optional exploration/grinding for upgrades, craft-able gear, build-expanding modules... And yet it all falls down in execution.

The limb-targeting should have been removed from the game before it ever got close to launch. There's nothing fun about it and this irritating mechanism takes center-stage in combat. Well no, I'm done.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, it is sci-fi Dark Souls, and yes, it is good.

EDIT after completing the game:

It is actually a GREAT game. I strongly disagree with most of the 'cons' mentioned by other reviewers.

First of all - the map design isn't bad, the map design is AWESOME. Very realistic (yes, it a sci-fi game, but you know what I mean), thought-through and attractive. And you think you need a map? Don't make me laugh. It would be a pain to use even a good 3D map with this since many of the doors and lifts can't be used until you unlock them. The easiest way to know how to get from point A to B is to actually remember it, no easy way here I'm afraid. Though I must say I never had issues with map orientation, maybe a bit in the last location because it was changing a bit as you progressed...

I also don't think it is a short game, for the current basic price 20 euro you surely get enough content and with some replayability value. Too little bosses you say? I am perfectly ok with the amount (and I like the design of most of them). Battle too hard? Just lol. No, seriously, it is true that later in the game when facing even just one single mob, you won't get away just with a good strategy and good overall knowledge of fight mechanics, you will also need to be fast. And even if your skills are too low, you can almost always just run through in the end.

I agree though that 80 % of the game you fight one type of enemy. Surprisingly it has almost no impact on the fun. And after all, you can say something similar also about Dark Souls:)

Finally, from the strong parts I would like to point out the story and atmosphere. To the end I could really feel all the depression and hopelessness and the horror elements worked on me. Yes, I listened to every audio log I found and watched all they showed on the TV:). It is true the ending wasn't terribly satisfying but I guess it was just preparation for Surge 2. And I am definitely looking forward to playing that one;)\t\t\t\t\t
