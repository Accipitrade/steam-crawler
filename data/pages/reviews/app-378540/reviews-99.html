\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike Lords of the Fallen before it, this game suffers from souls envy. I'll just put the most relevant points here and leave it at that:

- Combat is clunky even when it's meant to be fast-paced. Extremely similar to Lords of the Fallen in that regard.
- The story isn't particularly compelling. You meet a few people along the way but there wasn't much to it.
- The enemies in this game are nuts. I spent most of my playthrough figuring out ways to avoid them because you never when they'd suddenly just turn and take half of your life. Most of the combat involves enemies ignoring your attacks and trading blows...difference being theirs hurts.
- Not knowing where to go next becomes less of a fun puzzle/challenge to figure out and more a slog through the same enemies over and over again. You might say since I mentioned DS "Isn't that what souls games are all about?" To extent but I didn't ever get the sense of "Wow, I've really mastered this area, time to move on" like I did in the souls series. Again, feels very similar to Lords of the Fallen where it's more like "Oh yea, this enemy again...yey..."

On the plus side the combat is actually somewhat fun, especially switching around weapon types occasionally and mangling enemies. The bosses weren't bad...the first two or three were fun to figure out. After that I wouldn't say it wasn't terribly memorable and the bosses start to suffer from needing luck more than skill.

I really enjoy this genre of game and even liked Lords of the Fallen but I couldn't even make it through this one. When a game feels like a chore more than entertainment it's time to quietly uninstall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is an Action RPG in the vein of the Souls series, and the influence is very apparent. The setting has changed, but the gameplay remains very similar. You play a paralyzed guy named Warren who's first day on the job at CREO has gone horribly awry. You'd think the miraculous CREO tech that gave you the ability to walk via the Exo-Rig would be all butterflies and candy canes, but things have gone south. Quick.

You'll spend most of your time exploring the sprawling world and collecting loot, all the while either killing or being killed by the various baddies that inhabit CREO. You collect and spend Tech Scraps as your primary resource, which you can store and spend at Central Ops to increase your level and upgrade your gear. Yes, I did say STORE your Tech Scraps. You have the ability to store Tech Scraps in Central OPS everytime you visit, allowing you to wander around freely not worrying about losing too many scraps upon death.

The combat system is where this game differs from similar games in the genre. You have the ability to target individual limbs during combat, that you can ultimately slice off to collect loot. This system pretty much eliminates the RNG of random drops, allowing you to focus your efforts in gear collection. Weapons can be acquired in the same fashion and special weapons can be acquired by defeating the bosses in a specific way.

The combat in the game seems to be a lot looser than the Souls games before it. If you are used to I-frames and tight hit-boxes, you can just disregard that in this game. Being close enough to an enemy would qualify you for taking damage, even if you are in the middle of staggering the enemy. They'll still hit you. Blocking works well enough, but is a good way to get overwhelmed after your stamina loss (if you aren't Implanted for that playstyle). Some enemies will seem to learn new combos as you fight them, and follow up established movesets with an entirely new dumb move that instagibs you. Also there are several enemies that will kill you in one or two hits throughout the entire game. You're gonna get hit by enemies you were clear of. It's just going to happen, period.

The CREO facility is a really cool setting for the game. It's a huge labrynth of maintenance hatches and wide open hangars, and has quite the character to it. Corporate propaganda plays on repeat on video screens throughout the failing facility, futher concreting the bleak atmosphere. The world and setting is finely crafted, but the overall level design is a bit muddy and focuses too much on shortcuts. While Dark Souls had a way of linking all of the areas together in an interesting way with a shortcut or two, The Surge seemed to just take the concept of a shortcut and run wild with it, making some zones a tangled mess. While the setting and atmosphere are INCREDIBLE, navigating some zones is an uninspired chore (looking at you R&D).

When you die in The Surge, you drop your acrued Tech Scrap that you carried on your character when you died (banked tech scrap is stored forever, thankfully). This is all well and good, except in The Surge you have a limited amount of time to retrieve the scrap. In later zones, this is a huge chore, especially if you haven't opened the shortcut to that area yet. Thankfully this time increases with every kill, allowing you a little more time to return to collect. This time limit puts a rush on navigating the path back to your corpse in a game where patience is the key to survival. Later in the game you hit like a freight train, so recollection of your corpse isn't that big of a deal.

The story is fleshed out with audio logs scattered throughout the facility, audio e-mails found on computer consoles and interactions with NPCs and the environment. The game is relatively straightforward in this regard and allows you to pretty much ignore it all if you want to. There are no elaborate cut scenes outside of the intro and ending. NPC interactions occur throughout each zone and serve as side quests for you to get achievements, further the story and collect loot. Some quests persist through multiple zones, but can ultimately be ignored if you just don't care.

One of the selling points of this game is it's extreme difficulty, which can be frustrating at times. The difficulty only spikes up into the ridiculous category two or three times, but you ultimately get strong enough to take out powerful enemies quickly. New Game+ isn't really that much more difficult at first, but the inclusion of Elite enemies is really just a cheap difficult increase that isn't really fun. Enemies rarely come at you in groups, but there are definitely areas you need to be patient in.

Throughout the game, there are only 5 bosses outside of the 2 extra DLC bosses. Each boss has a "hardcore kill," which is a special way of dispatching it in order to gain a special weapon. I was disappointed at the lack of bosses at first, but the sheer nuissance of the regular mobs made up for the lack of bosses. Security Troops later in the game are the biggest PITAs of the whole game, including bosses. I would, however, like to see more bosses in The Surge 2.

On the subject of DLC, it's essential that you play it if you pick up this game. It's a fun, relatively quick DLC that compliments the main game quite nicely. The DLC zone is a theme park built as corporate propaganda for CREO, and has probably the most interesting level design of any section of the game. Entering this zone after level 50 or so makes it a cake-walk, but still super enjoyable. I recommend getting the Complete Edition if you are picking this game up for the first time.

One thing I am wishy-washy on is the weapon proficiency scale, which ultimately locks you into a single weapon type for the duration of the game. Once you get far enough along a weapon path, you can pretty much only use that weapon type due to damage output. Your proficiency grows as you use a weapon, which is all well and good, but if you find a cool weapon later in the game that's a different type, you'll have to grind the proficiency back up enough to do competent damage. So you have to figure out what playstyle suits you in the first area and stick with it. Luckily, all weapons types can be found in the first zone.

The influence of Dark Souls is very apparent, but The Surge offers enough to stand on it's own. It doesn't have the instant classic combination of atmosphere and gameplay that Dark Souls has, but it delivers a fun and rewarding experience. Overcoming the steep difficulty is satisfactory and the combat/gear system is actually pretty addicting once you get used to cheap enemy mechanics. I highly recommend this one to fans of the Souls series, and only recommend it to other players who are prepared to suffer through the difficulty.


Here is a quick tip:
One of the first armor sets you can get in the game, LYNX, is one of the best armors you can have. Focus on acquiring it and upgrading it from the start if you enjoy a quick attacking build. Slower builds may not benefit, but I haven't tried.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll in all, not a bad game. Pesonally I would rank it about an 85/100. Honestly, I gave it a break after playing for about a week because the story just wasn't pulling me in and I became exhausted battling some of the seemingly invincible enemies that would kill me in one or two hits.

Combat feels pretty good. Tight, responsive combat, and you are able to target specific areas to attack which is pretty cool. If you know how to use the system well, you can use it to your advantage to hit unarmored or weaker parts of the enemies and take them down faster. I'm sort of expecting future games in this genre to copy that system.

Story isn't bad, it's nothing new, but at this point it's very hard to come up with a truly new idea so I don't actually expect a very gripping story nowadays.

Bottomline, if you are into Dark Souls or Bloodborne then you will *like* this game. You won't fall in love with it. But hey, that's just me talking. Maybe you will love this game more than the Souls series. Nevertheless, this IS worth buying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I wrote this game off as another souls clone with zero originiality. Saw some vids online that kinda bashed the premise of it. The story seems really uncompelling. At first your just some dude in a wheelchair who can magically walk again via technology. Not much story right? However, as the game unfolds there are note or "audio logs" which gives first and secondary source information of the events that happened. As the game progresses and unfolds (at least in Creo Park) you learn more about Warren and his motivation, The Surge, and what is going on and why others and not yourself are acting erradic and irrational. At first its just a mess, the lvl design is good NOT amazing like DS1. While both give you the 'lost' vibe very well, The Surge lacks memorable "weenies" or landmarks that mark certain places. It's more of a confusing labryinth with a few landmarks that you MUST memorize to help you navigate, than better designed lvls in DS1 that are easily recognizable as "ohhhh thats that's the one place I was just at, so I know 'xyz' is this way and if i take 'n' shortcut...." The difference is Ds1 allows you to more quickly backtrack and "learn the map organically by geographic landmarks" while The Surge does this to an extend, lots of the short cuts are ambiguious/ modular and you will have to stumble upon some of these sometimes and their design is very modular so every hallway sometimes looks the same and its hard to orient yourself in the world.

Other than that, the combat and upgrade is very fun I love the resources systems they implemented, making lvling your source for obtaining upgrades that improve your experience tailored to your gameplay statisfaction. It really made you have to make hard decisions because BOTH armor and health, stam, abilities all require the same lvling resource. I rolled a MG Gorgon Suit with the Cat Claw, with overhealing implant, hp, stam implants, and adrenal implant for better dodging and many others. It relies on combos, but it's not button mashing combat. While you can do this the design will punish you for it but, like I said if you have the right customizations and implants you could make this viable.

Their customization of your gameplay is based on your preferences and is very flexible, the key is to experiment with all weapons in the beginning and find what you like best.

Overall, if you like the ARPG genre, I think you'd like this. I did not enjoy their Lords of the Fallen project so when i enjoyed this, from buying in on the recent steam sale, I was blown away and addicted to the systems and the challenge. I think some of the BS in the game is just, BS but it's highly overshadowed by the mystery/ story that drives the game forward forcing the player to figure out stuff. They also give the player more challenging and appropriate enemies. I think the NPC's could be better though. I guess they're there as more plot devices and to make you think? I am not even sure really but I wish I could find out more about them or make them more interesting or intriguing. They're just mundane NPC's either by design or fault.

PS
The boss fights could be better. Most aren't that hard and even a noob could figure it out in 3 runs. My fav one was the boss in Creo World (DLC i believe), the sequence of events blew my mind lol! Prob the best boss fight in game.

7.7/10
WOULD RECCOMEND TO THE ARPG HEADS ONLY!
I bought it on sale (with all dlc) over the Summer Sale for like $15 bucks and I feel with the amount of enjoyment I would have been happy paying them $25- 30 bucks. I am an ARPG fan so take it as my bias.\t\t\t\t\t

From minor things that could be done better:

- proper screen/mouse lock instead of target lock (dear devs, why do you keep tormenting us with console workarounds in KB/M context ?)
- allow to rebind 2 keys per action
- ng+ scaling seems really underwhelming - with nearly no new enemies (and very few they added don't even respawn ...); sure first 5 seconds of ng+ were super promising, but hopeful expectations quickly dissolved (so far at least)
- no way to throw away/recycle/remove/etc. weapons; not that it matters much, but still mini irritating

Either way the game is great, go get it =)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEssentially it's a Sci-Fi Soulslike game with some similarities but not quite the quality of the original Dark Souls.
Would recommend to gamers who liked Darksouls and want more in the same genre.
Needs a minimum of 2 playthroughs to get the 100%, keeping in mind you make sure to look out for missables.

Pros :
  • Open Level design, unlocking shortcuts to older parts while exploring
  • Fun Fighting system that has some weight behind the heavy weapons you use, but still feels fast enough due to the mobility of your exo-suite
  • Intuitive Drop system, giving you always exactly what you are aiming for, eliminating RNG
  • Risk rewarding Leveling, getting a higher multiplier the more enemies you kill before going back to a checkpoint
  • Easy to use Crafting and Gear system, even if its a bit barebone
  • Boss fights are refreshingly different to normal enemy encounters, with one being a really well designed fight not only by the standard of the game.

Cons :
  • Repetitive encounters since most enemy types are very similar in moveset, size, weakpoints
  • Limited selection of weapons, functionally only 5 different weapon classes with very similar animations
  • Very generic, predictable story. Especially the in-world stuff like TVs running in the background can get repetitive since you hear them every time you visit the area.
  • Easy to get lost in the sometimes maze like world since most is grey factory corridors, vents, hallways, etc.
  • Unengaging NPC subquestlines, mostly very short one task stuff
  • Even though the rig customization is nice most builds aren't viable to play, mostly because of the huge damage output of even the smallest enemies, making health and stamina the most valuable resources to upgrade.

Miscellaneous
  • Recommended to play it with a controller due to the strange but easy to get used to controls, in particular locking on to a specific body part
  • Having a timer every time you die in which you have to get your lost scrap (souls) back can get quite stressful and lead to more deaths
  • Some easy missable achievements due to stage specific events like NPC questlines and collectibles
