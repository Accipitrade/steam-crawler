\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game passed under my radar when it first came out, but it caught my eye during the fall steam sale this year and I picked it up for about $25.
WORTH. EVERY. PENNY.
If youre looking for a good souls-like game with a bit of a twist go no further. Honestly I would even consider paying full price for this game, but at a (current) sale price of $20 thats a freaking steal.

I'd watched playthroughs of Deck13's previous souls-like game Lords Of The Fallen and some things fell just short of the mark in some places. They must have learned a great deal from this experience though because this game just WORKS.

This'll be a longer review so I'll break it into sections about gameplay and mechanics and then follow with the overall pros/cons...

Combat:
As a souls-like game, I feel like this game hits the mark JUST right. While it may seem easier to some more experienced souls players, combat in this game is still relatively difficult but doesnt make a point to be overtly so. This is one of the areas I feel this game EXCELLS at, even over the souls games. Every time I died to an enemy in this game it actually felt like it was 100% MY fault, unlike many experiences in the souls games where sometimes combat deaths just felt random or unjustified. Boss combat, while still difficult, is significantly more forgiving than most souls games IMO. On average most bosses took 3-7 tries compared to my 8-15 on my first playthrough of dark souls. I feel like this made the game a bit more enjoyable and didnt feel like a complete time sink where I had to put aside a ton of time just to MAYBE beat the boss, and then have to spend 4 tries just refreshing myself when I came back at a later time (Im looking at you nameless king). So in that respect the game is a bit more "casual" and way easier to just pick up and start playing without 10-15 minutes of warmup, which I think is a good thing. But for players who are looking for even more of a challenge from the boss fights, there are also "hardcore" kills for each boss, which are difficult to accomplish but grant increased rewards and special loot. Plus the limb chopping combo finishers are satisfying as ♥♥♥♥ and literally NEVER get old.

Character Building/Progression:
Building your loadout in this game is based off of 4 main aspects: weapons, gear, implants, and core power. Weapons can be swapped freely, with no stat requirements, but sticking with one type of weapon class for some time will increase your proficiency for that class and grant increased damage based on proficiency scaling. Gear is basically your armor. At the begining of the game you start with a very limited selection, but you can expand your gear selection by chopping off the body parts of enemies wearing armor you don't already have (same with weapons). Gear peices can be mixed and matched to create the best combination of stat increases to fit your playstyle or worn as a complete set for a set bonus. Implants are basically chips that you can install into your exo for passive and/or active bonuses like medkits or passive stamina increaseses to even more complex applications. Both gear and implants have a power draw associated with them, and this is where core power comes into play. Core power acts as the limiting factor to your gear and implants, and is basically your level, which you increase in a similar fashion to the souls games by spending your hard earned Tech Scrap (souls). By increasing your core power you are raising the maximum amount of implants and quality gear you can equip.

Overall this progression system is super intuitive, and great for gameplay as you can freely tailor your build to each encounter as you play through the game. While this system does remove some of the replayability, I found it to be much more entertaining because I was able to try out any combination I wanted on the fly and could easily change from a tanky brawler to a fleet footed striker depending on the situation. This also means that The Surge is much more acessable than most of the souls games, requiring much less time consumption and min maxing to create the "perfect" build. Again this feeds into the sense that while difficult, this game is much easier to just pick up and play compared to the souls games.

Graphics&Audio:
Graphics are freaking amazing. Enough said. Plus I was able to max ALL stats and still keep 60 FPS with the GTX1050 in my laptop as well. Audio is amazing, the combat sounds are just so satisfying. Voice acting is wonderful as well, while conversations are few and far between, the voices from the NPCs and recordings did well to add to my immersion overall.

Endgame:
After beating the game for the first time you unlock NG+ which is acesseed by loading your old save after the end credits. This is actually the biggest problem I had with the game, after I beat it the first time I still wanted to go back and explore some more after beating the final boss, but instead I was forced back to the start of the game to replay it again. So make sure you do everything you want BEFORE beating the final boss. With that said, NG+ is JUST as entertaining as the first playthrough. You replay the game from the beginning and keep all your gear from the previous playthrough, with an increase to the gear and weapon upgrade cap. NG+ offers a MUCH higher level of difficulty in the form of much tougher, and SMARTER enemies, as well as the addition of new super strong ass beating super enemies scattered throughout the game armed with rare weapons and armor. In this respect the NG+ offers a much more satisfying experience for players who found the base game too easy, while still keeping the satsifying feeling of fair combat.

Overall Pros:
-Freaking great and FAIR combat mechanics
-Mixing and matching gear and collecting sets is fun as hell
-Audio&Visuals 10/10
-Provides a challenging experience but is still easy to pick up/put down any time
-Chopping off peoples arms is satisfying as ♥♥♥♥
-Badass mecha-man
-You work for alternate reality Elon Musk
-Deadmau5 confirmed super hero of the future?

Overall Cons:
-Ambiguous storytelling (some people like this type of thing but I didn't like it in the souls games and I still dont really like it here...)
-Forced into NG+ and can't return to end of game save after beating final boss
-not as many areas as I would have liked, wished for more & larger areas to explore
-numberous shortcuts and paths around the maps felt mazelike at times and occasionally confusing (probably trying to make the most of the smaller areas and limited maps, but sometimes I just wanted a straightforward path...)
-I've become addicted to chopping off peoples lilmbs with futuristic industrial power tools

TL;DR- Buy this game its ♥♥♥♥♥ good m8\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNice game for the right price (which is around 20€ for me).

It has some very fun combat, a unique artstyle (at least for a soullike game) and an interesting levelup mechanic (there are no skills, but only better gear and implants).

The story is... well it's not THAT bad actually, but you need to collect a lot of datapads if you want to follow it. And the good thing is, they are all voiced. So no need to read through all of it.
That been said it is definitley not a "story driven" game and cutscenes are extremely rare.

There are some secrets and a few miniscule sidequests. Also some characters you can talk to, tho they got not too much to say and the only interesting stuff is on the datapads.

Enemies can be pretty challenging, especially in the later parts of the game. The same goes for bosses.
Bossfights.. oh well.. honestly not the strongest part of the game. The fights itself are ok and some are quite fun and unique. But there are only SPOILER - 5 bossfights in total! - Spoiler and one of them is just an earlier boss together with a tough dude, which leaves you with 4 unique bosses.

The leveldesign on the other hand is much better. Sure there isnt really much for the eyes and after a while a lot of the areas look kinda the same, BUT safezone and shortcut placing is great imho. Each area has 1 safezone/hub/whatever where you can spend your scraps, fill up your injections and on occasion get a friendly respawn. The rest are shortcuts to these zones. Feels a lot better than the DS3 bonfire spam.

One last thing: There is no such thing as invasion or coop in this game. It's singleplayer only, which is absolutely fine for playing through the game once, but it definitely lacks replayability (at least for me, invasions are such a great mechanic no matter on which side you are).

I finished the game (no dlc) in around 22hours with some slacking and backtracking, so it is a lot bigger than its predecessor "Lords of the Fallen" but also has less bossfights :d\t\t\t\t\t

The surge is a hardcore rpg that aims to borrow all of the good parts of Lords of the Fallen and Dark Souls.
The gameplay mechanics around movement seems to be an exact blend between the two. It has the weight and feel of the larger weapons in LOTF and most of the strategy of dark souls.

You will be darting back and forth, in and out, but mostly estimating the impact of your weapons against the stability of your enemies in order to deal the most damage per combo.

Graphics, 9/10
Runs perfectly on max settings on a gtx 970 with a reasonable i5.
I was quite impressed on how they handled the background loading and transitions between areas. It's not flashy but it works quickly and efficiently. Also loading into an area that you have already been to is almost instant, a huge thank-you to the developers for keeping the majority of the assets loaded to facilitate this. - there is a current glitch with the position of the first security gear set after some animations that cause it to appear slightly behind you.

Gameplay 9/10
There are a few conceptual flaws here, but none relating to it's implementation.
When killing an enemy with a finisher off a ledge both the item and yourself will remain at the position to which the finisher started.
Darting in and out as people keep complaining about is partially correct as yes it works the majority of the time, but it is slower, less efficient, not possible late game (excluding the last boss which NEEDS to be completely reworked) and not fun. Putting it plainly, the combat around this game benefits from simply being COOL. I found the staff to be the most viable weapon by far, but that might just be personal preference. I do enjoy slower and larger weapons but they have this annoying mini-hit in between large swings that does nothing but expose you to being pummeled (This may be intentional). When using a larger weapon i want to take advantage of the huge impact rating, this mini-hit (until the black Cerberus) prevents this.

Level design 5/10
Oh my god some of these levels are moronic. Dark souls has a complex level design set in a similar manner, but it was done so in a way that granted a player great spacial awareness. This was done using open areas and the ability to look over the entire city from several locations. It does not work in closed spaces with tight and windy walkways.
The amount of bloody corners that have an enemy waiting for you thankfully reduces as you progress later into the game. There are in-game signs (no ♥♥♥ map) to assist you in getting where you need to go, but no painted floor lines like the ones found in some manufacturing facilities (which would have been a great idea). The site maps that are present are basically useless and completely void of useful information and this is assuming that you see them at all.
Honestly, if they present the player more information the level design would improve drastically, i get that they want you to explore but there needs to be better indication on where to go, or it's not exploring, it's just wandering about until you think you found that path you are meant to travel. - Ask NPCs where to go, they actually give useful information.
There needs to be a fast travel system that unlocks just before the last boss and only just before the last boss.


Overall this still gets an 8/10 due to the relative impact of each category including the ones not commented on in this review. It is an excellent game that is quite underrated. I was actually quite astounded at how long i could play this in one sitting completely entertained.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wouldn't call this game "hard" as lots of reviewers seem to, but I would definitely call it "unforgiving". It reminds me a lot of my WoW days. Learn the levels, pull the right enemies at the right times and your life is easy-going. But when it goes wrong, or that patrol you didn't see hits you, then it layers on the stress but is very rewarding.

The content is also really fun with lots of large areas to explore and learn the layouts, finding all the goodies hidden away (I would recommend playing the first map and if you enjoy it then pick up the DLC with the scanner in it as it's vital to find all the best goodies - you can re-scan through the first map to find anything you missed after picking up the DLC).

Enemy variety is good and bosses are exceptionally brutal, with one-hit-kills abound.

Combat is extremely satisfying, with each weapon feeling different levels of impact, sound effects and gore are all there to add to the show. There is a meaningful choice between using different armour sets and weapons. You can block low-impact weapons then counter-attack, or dodge high-impact weapons so they don't knock you over, then hit the enemy while they are off-balance. Because the combat is reaction-based some may find it overwhelming but it's possible to build around heavy armour and blocking more than dodging with enough HP and Regen implants.

Overall the game has a surprising amount of depth and replayability, I'm looking forward starting a New Game + after I complete my initial playthrough!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike Lords of the Fallen, but better.

Positives:
• Action feels great. Controls and movement are responsive, weapon movesets are varied, and everything from the huge two-handed weapons and the quick dual-wield ones are extremely satisfying to use.
• The Energy system is a great way to encourage you to stick in the fight, similar to Bloodborne vs Souls. Landing hits gives you energy, which can be used on a small estus-free heal, support attacks or abilities from your drone, or if the enemy's health and low, a dismemberment finisher. It works great as a way to encourage you to play aggressively.
• The dismemberment system never got old in my 25 hours with the game, which is surprising. It's an extremely satisfying way to collect new armor sets and weapons without relying on RNG loot drops, and as every successful finisher also boosts your tech scrap multiplier (your currency/experience in this game) you're encouraged to try and perform one on every enemy you find.
• The armored enemy and limb targeting system is great. To get new armor and weapons, you need to target that part when fighting the enemy and successfully perform a finisher to cut it off -- but if you don't need what that enemy has, targeting an unarmored part lets you deal more damage and stagger more quickly. The tradeoff works really well, letting you decide on an ememy-by-enemy basis what to do.
• The areas are *really* well interconnected. What feels like a linear sprawling megacomplex at first constantly grows more familar as you progress and unlock a huge number of shortcuts and alternate paths, more than any other game I've played in this genre.
• As mentioned before, successful finishers increase your scrap multiplier -- but this is reset when you go back to Ops (a bonfire) to refresh your injectables (estus). Alternatively, you can stop by Ops' equipment crafting station to respawn all enemies *without* refilling your injectables or restarting your tech multiplier. The risk/reward for farming is satisfying and player-friendly.

Negatives
• Enemy movesets are a little samey. If two enemies are using the same weapon type, they'll generally be using the same attacks. Not entirely bad, since it lets you capitalize on your mastery more, but a little more variety would have been nice.
• The critical path is linear. I don't care about this at all, but some might.
• The biggest negative for me, the aesthetic is similar throughout the game. While it looks good, it can get pretty confusing identifying where you are at times. I would have appreciated something like a color-coded wall design to highlight different areas, especially in the maintenance tunnels constantly connecting rooms and sectors.

The $40 base price now is a very fair price for the game. If you get it on sale for even less, you should be very satisfied. Greatly looking forward to what this team continues to do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is a great game, but one with large flaws that start to become apparent in the latter half. First of all, the game is very fun for the entire first half of it. It feels like a sci-fi Dark Souls with very nice graphics and fun combat. People criticize the controls as "clunky" but you're a guy in a massive suit of robotic construction armor, so I thought they made sense. For a lot of the game I was happily playing along wondering what the heck people were up in arms about.

Unfortunately, the game takes a bit of a turn towards the cheap in the second half of the game. As difficulty begins to ramp up, rather than throw new tactical situations at the player, they simply jack up the damage that enemies do. The worst offender of this was a humanoid boss that for all intents and purposes should do typical damage but somehow was able to one shot you even if you jacked up your HP massively. There are ways to deal with this, like parrying, but it feels cheap and out of place for a Souls game to kill you that easily if you make a slip-up, especially when there's little windup.

Ultimately, the game's design begins to miss the point of a Souls game, that it is fair but hard, and starts to put you in impossible situations that you have no choice but to "cheese" by exploiting enemy movement ranges and such. So ultimately the game is fun in so many ways, especially in the first half, but takes a bit of a nosedive that it can't quite pull itself out of.

There are some other minor ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. You can't choose the protagonist gender, which feels like they didn't learn much from Lords of the Fallen. There are also really annoying sounds in certain parts. The default medbay music gets old after about 5 minutes and one of the "operations" bases has an annoying voice over playing constantly (you can shut it off in the options, but the option is so obtuse that I didn't realize it until much later). My armor constantly glitched off my body, something that seems like it would have been easy to discover and fix. It feels like the game was biting off a bit more than the studio could chew.

The graphics of the game are pretty fantastic and the art design is top notch, incorporating extremely inventive futuristic designs. I did experience minor glitches like some unusual flickering with lighting. The characters are somewhat interesting, and there is a decent story, but it never truly grabbed me and sucked me in, and ends up feeling a bit like a mediocre movie that has a point it wants to get across but sort of flubs the execution. I thought the story was, at least, superior to Lords of the Fallen, but still not as engrossing as Dark Souls or Bloodborne by a long shot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted to like this game. It looks great in the first area (The demo) and the visuals don't disappoint. Weapons feel meaty and look great. The difficulty is on par with dark souls 1 and the level design is reminiscent of that dark souls 1 world. there are shortcuts back to the repair stations (read: bonfires) that are well placed. The story was interesting and I wanted to learn more about it. But then I started seeing problems.

The place that the game falls apart is in the animations and annoying level design.

Attack animations go on for a varying length of time depending on whether you held down the button for a long time, or a short time. That's how it's supposed to work. Instead it feels like you flip a coin each time you attack and then either do a 2-3 hit combo or a 1 hit attack. You can dodge like dark souls, but this is equally frustrating as the game locks you out of doing so while you are attacking until you're stunned by an enemy. There were multiple times where I tried to dodge out of the way of my enemy's attack while my character was performing a 3 hit spinning combo that I didn't try and do. I would then die due to the enemy having a small enough weapon (staff or sword of some sort) that didn't result in a stun to knock me out of the attack queue.

Another point in the combat that frustrated the hell out of me was that the game likes to give you deadly ledges fight on and to put enemies near that ledge. Well, the finishers sometimes will move you forward, sometimes to the side, sometimes you'll end the finisher where you started it. After the finisher's done, you might have thrown yourself off the ledge, or you might not.

The level design is for the most part great. Except for when it is just really bad. In one section you are being shot at by an enemy at a turret. The turret is on a raised platform resembling a tower. The stairway up to it hugs the tower structure along one side, and then has an approximately six foot gap between the stairway and the top of the small platform connected at the corner of said platform by a walkway. There's no guard rail that prevents you from falling in to said gap and falling down. To your death. It is also possible to use a finisher on that enemy and walk your happy ass into that gap and fall to your death. It is also possible for the enemy to knock you back into that gap and fall to your death.

I want to like this game. It does many things really well, but it feels like it was rushed out before having the amount of time needed to make a well polished and refined dark souls competitior. If they make a Surge 2 I will likely buy it as long as they learn from their mistakes and spend more time refining the game.\t\t\t\t\t
