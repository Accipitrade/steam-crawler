\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I heard this game was tough and supposedly Soulslike I thought "Great something to try out since I finished Dark Souls."

What I didn't realise was that playing the game was the hard part rather than the gameplay.

While the premise of the game is very intriguing and got my interest straight away the game immediately loses my interest within the opening mission.

What I was hoping for was Dark Souls or Nioh styled visceral combat what I instead got was something akin to Runescape's hit a person, wait for them to hit you and then hit back with the occasional dodge. Not only does it feel stiff compared to Dark Souls or Nioh it just feels slow overall like instead of actually fighting I'm getting a play by play recap of something that has already happened.

Maybe that's intentional maybe that's part of its charm to some people that it's not dynamic fast paced roll around a person and back stab but it certainly doesn't appeal to me.

My other gripe that others can feel free to argue is just complaining is the placement of the first boss. The first boss is positioned in such a way that it assumes the player will walk back outside the building they were in to complete an object to then leave the area however that wasn't my first instinct.

What I instead decided to do which most people tend to do in any game with exploration is explore a new area that had opened up. Unfortunately for me what ended up happening was that I got absolutely destroyed by an enemy I didn't see and without any health injections to spare I embraced death. Upon reviving I discovered The Surge's interestingly stupid mechanic timed retrieval of your Tech Scrap.

Which plays into my gripe about the placement of the boss because as it so happens the boss blocked my path to my glorious 3,000+ tech scrap and wasted my two minutes of time.

Not to mention that even at max settings the game runs about as well as your average PS4 game. And yes I know it comes down to my individual specs but DS3 runs gloriously in comparison to it also on max settings.

Overall if I could I would probably give The Surge more time to see if it gets any better but problems like these make it hard to give it another shot.

Buy the game if you somehow enjoyed Lords of the Fallen which had the dullest first boss in history. Don't buy it if you're expecting to jump from DS3 or Nioh into something similar.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely loved the game, it took me 27 hours to beat it.

The story is interesting (I don't want to spoil anything though), but only told in small doses, which can be a bit confusing at times. You're pretty much required to explore a lot to find most of the audiologs and failsafe consoles to find out what exactly happened in this game.

Combat seems to be inspired by the souls series, though the way you obtain armor and weapons is different, you're required to farm materials to build and upgrade items by severing the according body parts of enemies.

You level up by collecting scrap metal, much like collecting souls in the souls series. The higher your level is, the more implants you can use to increase certain stats or unlock some abilities or you can just choose to use heavier armor.

Things I didn't like too much were the bosses, as there aren't many of them and the fights could be a little more exciting, and the level design, which offers a great atmosphere, but can be pretty confusing so you can get lost easily.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoyed this game, and the "mech-souls" gameplay was a refreshing take on one of my favorite genres. I got it on sale for 20 bucks, and it was well worth it. The game presents a satisfying gear progression system and combat system that balances well with the scaling difficulty of the game's bosses.

There are no "levels" in the traditional sense, you upgrade your exosuit's power core by using scrap dropped from enemies in order to fit better implants and pieces of armor for your exosuit. The impants provide both passive buffs like more health and stamina, and can also act as usables to do things like restore health or add an elemental damage buff to your weapon.

In order to aquire new weapons and exosuit parts, you have to take them from enemies by cutting off the approprite body part with a special "execution" style attack once you weaken the enemy. Its quite satisfying.

I only wish there had been more game. I finished a playthrough in just under 22 hours, but I know that I missed plenty of stuff along the way. New Game + will be a good chance to revisit some of those other areas and round out the experience. There also seem to be multiple endings, which makes replaying it inviting. I hope to see more stuff from Deck13 in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMeh. Sci Fi Lords of the Fallen. Had potential but falls so disapointingly short.

Just finished it and just like Lords of the Fallen, it looks good, is graphically beautiful, had a lot of potential but is so clunky it loses any replay value. Decent game to get on a sale and play once. Ok story. Actually good implant and weapon proficiency systems. However just like Lords of the Fallen, all the weapons in the few archtypes are exactly the same. No variation in movesets at all. One does elemental damage. One does phsycial damage. One is the best in the archtype that you will end up using anyway despite potential resistances to elemental or physical. Lame.

The game tries to make it artifically difficult by making literally everything in the game 1 or 2 shot you, no matter what your build is. The only exception is when you have endgame equipment and go back to the very first area. Then they kill you in 3-4 hits. The combat is so clunky. Your combat moveset is locked in a 2-3 second windup animation for 90% of moves that you cant cancel while the enemies all have instant startup moves, unlimited stamina and non-telegraphed counters that will 1 shot you all game. Most weapons can't stagger enemies and when you do stagger, they can only be staggered once and then have some coded stagger immunity for the rest of the fight making big weapons useless. I finished SL1 runs in all the Dark Souls games and this is a clunky mess in comparison to how those games handle. The risk / reward system in this game is non existant. It's all (1 shot) risk and no reward to do anything differently. Hitboxes are terrible and it feels like there's no i-frames in your dodge at all which basically means you have 1 way to kill things in the game which make it trivial and boring which is:

"Slide in for the leap attack to get 1 hit in. Slide out so you're out of range because dodging close to an enemy will get you 1shot." Bait attack, slide in, slide out. Rinse, repeat Ad nauseum. Boring. All archtypes require you to do the exact same move until you have enough dps to kill things within the slide in / slide out window. Then you can spam R1 instead.

Horizontal and vertical attacks sound cool in concept but actually make 0 difference in the game. Doesn't affect how you can chop off limbs. Doesn't affect how you can fight or hit things. Doesnt' even have different movesets. Weapons literally have the same attack pattern between horizontal and vertical except 1 or 2 of the slash moves in the combo are, you guessed it, horizontal or vertical. Doesn't affect anything. Slide in, slide out, get 1 shot while out of range because the hitboxes hit you from across the room anyway and enemies do not have a limit to how far they can leap in to attack. The enemy leap attacks all take the same split second to move from where they are at the other side of the room to 1shotting you regardles of distance. No balance to movespeeds. Enemies seem to teleport across the room and hit you with those moves like the coding is done so that the time between original location and your location is constant (read instant) and the move speed to get there is calculated to make it happen.

Slide in, slide out, get 1shot. Rinse, repeat.

Interesting game. Exciting for the first area while you discover new stuff. Then you realize the whole game is copy paste and had no polish.

Oh well, back to Dark Souls.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is everything it advertises itself to be. Reminds me of devil may cry or the sort as you mostly just do combat, equip for combat, and move between zones. Some story is there but it definitely takes a backseat to the exo vs exo deathmatch that just continues while throwing the occasional giant robot into the mix.

I give it a 9/10 because it is a perfectly contained gaming experience that is worth its price - but my one gripe is that there is very little airborne combat and the few jump attacks you get move like they are on rails. Overall it's pretty minor considering the context of most combat but if they did a surge 2 I'd really like to see them expand on air combat and air physics for this game.\t\t\t\t\t
