\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMeh... its got so many good ideas but just isnt... put together well enough for me. The jumping is great, the body targeting is super intuitive, makes sense and is a joy to use but...

The whole combat kinda falls apart when there is more than one enemy. Due to lack of I-frames, and the game REALLY wanting you to lock onto a single enemy (for example, side dodging a lunging 2 weapon enemy without being locked on will sometimes just... not work where the same thing works while locked on), fighting 2 enemies falls into "Wait for both to do an attack, do a single attack against one enemy and backstep. Repeat until 1 enemy is left or you are against a wall".

Due to the I-frame decision, stunlocks are also VERY common and sometimes will drain your entire healthbar when you cant do anything to stop it, hell its happened once or twice for me from single enemies.

The hitboxes can also be super spicy... like the first boss REALLY needed some polishing because the hitboxes are terrible most of the time.

The world can also be needlessly confusing, half of the time im just wondering where the hell i am/need to go because everything looks the same, and doesnt link back onto itself very well. DS1 is usually called the most confusing dark souls game in world design but as someone who beat it fully blind, at least you know the general direction through areas most of the time. Here it feels like im constantly stumbling around waiting to fall into the right place.

I hope this doesnt come off as a rage post, im pretty experienced in the subgenre and this game honestly could be amazing, but its just... extremely iffy. Hopefully i get the drive to actually play on past area 2, and the surge 2 fixes some of these issues. But for now, its too frustrating to continue for me.\t\t\t\t\t

(TL:DR down at the bottom)

A lot of reviews are calling this game a souls like, and that's true but I think it shares more of its DNA with BloodBorne, it favors an aggressive playstyle rather than Darksouls' defensive opportunistic play. Though that might have just been my personal playstyle. The country song that plays in EVERY medbay (the medbay is essentially your bonfire/safezone) is an earworm, some people hate it, I thought it was fun. If you do hate it you can change it with a guide.

The Surge isn't completely open world, rather it's a large map separated by tram systems, each "Zone" has its own medbay that you'll find shortcuts back to throughout the zone it's in. Typical interconnected worlds though not as seamless as DS. I finished the game at around 20 hours, so it's definitely short, and most of that was accumulated through dying to bosses. If you already know where to go and how to fight the various enemies on subsequent playthroughs, the game could probably be beaten in under 8 or 7 hours.

The dismemberment mechanic is quite fun, and the animations never really got old for me, even if there's only one or two for each weapon type on every bodypart. So at 5 types of weapon, (1 Handed, Single Rigged, Dual Rigged, Staff, Heavy Duty) There's a good few animations if you diversify your weapon types, plus no more pointless grinding for that one really cool sword, just cut off his arm.

Replayability is a solid. meh. I didn't really feel a drive to go through NG+ like I did with DS and DS2, so it might be better to start a whole new character. The trash mobs are INSANELY strong in my experience, and can easily two shot me with my level 80 character.

The difficulty, now as a souls guy. I've beaten both DS and DS2 multiple times. This game never really gave me a wall like DS did. Though a few of the bosses are annoying if you don't know a few tricks, particularly the 4th. Combat is nowhere as smooth as DS or BB. the blocking system is clunky, (You can't more) and the parry system is not great at all. I mostly just dodged away from every combo as I saw no use for blocking. (Both of these systems are VASTLY improved with Surge 2)

The setting is great, atmosphere is very creepy and fun. The Cyberpunk genre fits really well in a souls like, dystopian megacorp fails to do anything good, things go sideways, welcome to your first day on the job. I mostly just adored the scifi elements, the RIG exosuit, different armour pieces. it was all very fun to collect and use.

All in all, The Surge is a fun few hours, getting addicted to this game for the first few days I had it before I beat it scratched an itch I had, and usually it was quite the fun experience. I'd totally recommend this game to anyone who's a fan of the souls or borne games. Might not be for everyone but for 5 dollars on a sale, you can't do much better for an experience like this. Its it worth full price? No, I don't think so. It's too short and clunky for 20 cad. But it was a fun little time waster.

I give this game an arbitrary score of 7 potatoes out of 11\t\t\t\t\t
