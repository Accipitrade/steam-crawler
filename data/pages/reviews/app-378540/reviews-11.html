\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRating - 7/10

Pros:-
Good deep combat and equipment mechanics. Has souls-like elements to it.
Good enemy variety. Graphics are quite good honestly. Difficulty level is typical of a souls like.
Story is pretty good as well.
Extremely good level design for the most part(except the final level).

Cons:-
You have to start and finish this game without taking a huge gap in between. The reason behind this is
that like most souls games, there is no defined objective marker, journal or map. And the levels are dark and looping with lots and lots of connectivity and shortcuts. So if you take a gap, good luck remembering which path leads where.
While the level design is good, a lot of places within the levels(especially shafts and tunnels) look identical at all levels. So its very confusing to remember the layout if you come back to this game after a gap.
The final boss is also one of the glitchiest bug infested mess(atleast for me). It would randomly glitch out and stop moving at times. The same attack would one shot you at times and at other times it won't. Blocking is the best option I found.
The hitboxes are absolutely terrible. You would get hit behind the boss while its attacking in front of it. And visually the boss looks "cool" but is terrible to fight because its a morphing boss so its quite hard to tell which attack its going to do if you are close to it.

TLDR:- A very fun game with very good level design, combat, equipment mechanics but with visually repetitive level design at places.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great addition to the soul-like genre - 7.5/10
I had a lot of fun with this game. While inspired by souls games, it add it's own twists to create a unique experience.

The main unique mechanics:
1. Leveling - you don't level by increasing your attributes (str, dex, etc.) but instead you increase your "core" power, which allows you to use better/more implants. So your character is defined by the implants it uses, and they can be swapped easily at checkpoints. this give a lot of flexibility and versatility to you play style.
2. Limb targeting system - when you locked on an enemy, you can target a specific limb (head, arms, etc.). reducing the enemy health and then perform a finisher on it with cut the limb and you will get the armor/weapon it was holding. this is the main way you get weapons, armors and crafting materials in this game. I find it very cool mechanic as you can get exactly what you need form an enemy instead of farming in hopes it will drop the correct item.
3. Drones - while there is no "magic" in this game (this is a sci-fi game), you have different kind of drones that can fill this slot. the drones can ram or burn your enemies, apply shield on you and more. Unfortunately didn't find the drones useful enough to actually use them.

Overall a decent game to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Is The Surge a perfect game?
No.

Is The Surge as terrible as so many people claim it to be?
Absolutely not.

This might be one of the most overhated games I've ever played And honestly.. I have no idea why that is as the game itself is solid for the most part.
The only complaint I have is the level design, which is very confusing in some areas, but that is about the only negative I can think of.

The Positives
> The game offers many possible builds with the implants mechanic, many different weapons and 3 different types of armor (light,medium and heavy) which all can change the way you approach encounters.

> Combat is responsive and has a feeling of weight to it, which in turn makes the combat more satisfying.
Unlike Dark Souls' dodge rolls you have a side step dodge, which has very minimal iframes so using block is crucial in many situations when dodging isn't ideal or possible (in cramped spaces).
In addition to stamina there is also energy, which can be altered with implants (for buffs at high energy levels) and is also used for executions, which sever enemy body parts and this is the main way of acquiring new armor pieces and weapons.
Targeting different body parts can give you an advantage in battle as targeting unarmored body parts will deal much more damage.

> Boss design for the most part is very solid and all the fights are fairly balanced (no one shot mechanics for example). Firebug is definitely a standout imo.

> Game progression and overall difficulty ramp up are both well paced and I never felt like I was put into an unfair situation.

> Map exploration is rewarding and you can gain an edge by finding better implants, scrap (souls) and new weapons. There are also a few side-quests you can take part in for some substantial rewards.

In conclusion
Yes, the game took more than a few ideas from Dark Souls, but if you're looking for another Dark Souls game then look elsewhere.
The Surge stands out as it's own kind of game and if you won't respect it's rules, you will be punished for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe comparison to a Soul's game brought me here. It isn't an unfair comparison. Instead of souls you collect scrap. When you die you lose that scrap but can retrieve it. Instead of respawning at a fire, you respawn at a med bay. Just like the Soul's games, the enemies reset whenever you leave the medbay, allowing grinding for scrap the way one use to grind for Souls in the Soul games. A Tech Soul's game. This scrap is used like souls in Dark Souls: buy upgrades and levels with it.

Unfortunately it didn't learn all the lessons Dark Souls had to teach. For instance, there are no stats or builds. At least not at first. I suppose you can personalize your character by swapping out implants and gear, but there is no such thing as a dex build, or a magic build. Not even a missile build. Maybe that stuff comes later. It seems melee is the only option. And this option feels heavy. Swings are slow. Attacks have no variation.

All movement in the game seems sluggish. I suppose that metal exoskeleton weighs a ton. But it isn't a ton of fun lumbering along at the speed of a WWI tank. Dodging is even more problematic. Verrrrrrrrrrry slowwwwwwwwwwwwww. Attacks tend to move at the speed of molasses. Not real fun.

However, these negatives don't make this a bad game. It makes it a game that one should research before buying. Watch some videos. Read a few reviews. Then get it on discount.



conclusion

the game hijacked the game mechanics of Darksouls and transported them into the future. I love those game mechanics. I love Science Fiction. I don't love this game, but I do like it a lot. I would suggest getting it on sale. And do not forget Steam's refund policy.\t\t\t\t\t
