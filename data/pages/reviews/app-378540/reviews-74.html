\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame looked very promising, my initial impression was great because of the graphics, the setting as well as all the mechanics.

It quickly turned into a repetitive slog not four hours in. Let me explain why

1. Enemies are all uninspiring and forgettable

2. Level design is poor, some levels have corridors filled with enemies, resulting in you needing to cheese the enemy in order to win. In a game where dodging is one of the core aspects to not dying, this is terrible.

3. Enemies not only respawn when you die, they also respawn when you go to the next area. This makes killing enemies utterly meaningless.

4. This game features a lot of cheap deaths. Did you fall from two meters? Better die and spend the next ten minutes reaching the same area again! Of course with completely fresh and respawned enemies! Hahaha! Oh, and you lose all your resources in the process!

5. Weapons are all boring and all melee

6. Drone is useless as it barely scratches the paint on your cybernetic enemies. A weird thing to have especially considering you need the energy for your injectables and finishing moves.

I don't mind difficult games, but when the difficulty is artificially inflated by cheap deaths and respawning enemies, then it just sucks all fun out of it for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had my eye on this game for a long time and I have to say I am really loveing it.
Its like if deadspace and dark souls had a child, and its uncle was a fallout game.

A lot of people have given it bad reviews for its levels. I agree they can be at times confusing but that is because its not a linear path. rather then haveing a save point every so offten, the levels revolve arond one set main save area with a lot of shortcuts that you open up by exploring. it makes the maps a lot more dense, but easy to get lost in. It makes things feel far more connected however, as it feels like you are going throuhgt a building. One area so far has lacked a save station and that really sucked.

The DLCs, as silly as they may look, are rather fun. the creo world one is probibly the better of the two storey DLCs (so far), and it offers a large level to explore with new gear. the western one is a bit out of place but its acttualy really fun once you know what you are doing. the rewards are pretty grate implant wise at lest. I will also say that the DLCs are interlaced with the main storey. You will want to do them alongside rather then after. moreso if it is your first playthoruhgt as some of the areas offer easy acsess to upgrade material and a few new things to help out. As a side note the western DLC testing area can also be used as a fast travle hub if you acsess the door at lest once form the other locations.

The game also has a NG+ that keeps ramping up things the more you play. spawns are changed, part and weps that things have are randomized, and incoming damage is incresed. this does mean at some point that it will get very very hard, but it also means that there is a lot of replay value in it.

Its worth trying out althouhgt I will also point out the game seems to crash... a LOT. this would nomrally be an isshue but it has a VERY agressive save system so you never loose more then a min or two of gmaeplay. There is in fact no way to save mannuely, but this also means all things you do are final. If it crahses during a fight, it also has a habbit of dropping you next to the enemy who is back at full health. I think it will remove you from a boss room however.

Other then the crashing, it also has a lost souls systme- you drop your exp on death and have like 2 min to pick it up, unless you die again (also to be clear its only your scrap. spent exp, gear, and consumabuls are not droped). you can bank scrap at save stations and haveing large amonts of scrap gives you a bonus to finding it. your scrap is the only thing you drop however, and aside from leveing up is used to upgrade gear. I wish it didnt have the time mechanic but its better then it just being gone.

unlike a souls game you can also target parts. limbs, body, and head. if you get there health down to a specific amont you can do a finisher, witch has a high chance of cutting off that part, and thus giveing it to you. its not fully garenteed, but its a pretty good chance. this means you can influance what something drops and collect gear a bit easyer then you would in a souls game, however it also means you have to to know what parts are what and what you need or do not have. usually cutting the arms gives you the wep as well, but it depends. this is also how you get crafting materials for the diffrent gear- cutting off the same part that you own the scamatic of altedy gives you a ruined part that is tunred into upgrade materials back at the save point. Some parts are "rare" some are just uncommon. all in all tho, its never been hard to find things. Plus when you rest at a save station, most things respawn (so far with the exception of the security guards at various check points).

I will also say, you are given a choice at the start of what armer you want. its a choice between light or heavy. you will get the one you didnt choose later. What I found was that I was faceing mostly light enemys at th start with the heavy units being used as mini-mini bosses with better upgrade mats. that said tho, iv used the starting armer for most of the game so far. each armer set also gives a set bonus when all prices are warn, the light one being that you move and attack faster.

Its been really fun. I highly recomend trying it, and looking out for the second game comeing out in 3 weeks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. I wish I had more of The Surge than what I've got.

+ Exploration was really awesome and rewarding, and the level design is just what I wanted to see in a Souls-Like game.
+ Attention to detail was everywhere in pretty much everything.
+ Great bosses.
+ Great soundtrack.
+ Great animations.
+ I liked the characters.
+ The combat and controls were really engaging and quite tight.
+ NG+ adds more implant slots, enemies are stronger, overcharge requires more core levels and you can upgrade your equipment further.
+ Full mouse and keyboard support.
+ The graphics and atmosphere are top notch.
+ RTX2070 was able to run it smoothly at 1080p, full settings and 150% resolution scale without any frame drops at all.
+ No crashes.

- So few bosses, really just five?.
- The story wasn't really that interesting to me.
- Most enemies will kill you in 1 or 2 hits through NG+. (I've had almost 720 health and some enemies still did one-shot me).
- The Medbay song can get annoying over time (You can replace it with any music you want).
- Most corridors looked similar (I was okay with getting lost as I considered that a part of the experience).
- Most enemies were humanoid, which could lead to a variety issue. (Wasn't a problem to me, as the limb-chopping for armor and weapons kept me going).
- Didn't really need to block, or do high/low dodges.
- Doing combos results in you executing a bit more attacks than what you asked for (I got used to it and it eventually was no issue).

If you liked Dark Souls or Bloodborne, you should try this game. It is definitely underrated.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as I like this game, it has some really really SERIOUS flaws.

7/10

Here is why: Weapons, Skills etc. are really good and all is fine and dandy until you get to fight something bigger than a human enemy. Drones and bosses are such a pain in the ass due to the controls and environment that it ruins all the fun.

There is a difference between a challenge and a nuisance. A challenge is something you have to work out, a nuisance is where you have to look up somewhere else how it is done, then repeat until the boss mechanics work as they are supposed to and you kill the boss. The bosses in The Surge are the latter, which kills all the fun.

Problem is, you need to kill the bosses to proceed. So you will find yourself with little else to do but try to kill the boss or farm scratch.

Although still recommendable only buy this if it is on special offer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd love to play more of this game, sadly I can't get past the first boss.

There are no difficulty settings and I understand that is due to it wanting to be a challenge to play but even so not every gamer has average reflex skills.

I'm thinking of those who may enjoy this game but due to a disability or some other form of impairment that slows their reactions will become frustrated (like myself) after dying constantly over many hours and days of trying.

Some will say "Well this isn't a game for you". Why shouldn't it be? It's a single player game, if it's easier to play am I having any less enjoyment from the game if I'm actually enjoying the game because I feel like I'm accomplishing something with a little less stressing out?

I feel game makers would increase their profits and player base for their games with just a little thought about those who may enjoy the game yet find it too difficult due to either a disability or having slow reactions or hand eye coordination problems.

If it wasn't for the fact it was on sale dirt cheap and wanted to try it I'd have never bought it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven though everyone keeps comparing this game to Dark Souls, I enjoy this game a lot more.

Combat seems hard, until you learn the combat moves of your enemies. At first, you have no clue where the weakspots are, how to tackle them, etc. Then once you get that handled, looking back you wonder why they ever seemed hard. Then it gets way easier. Slice and dice.

Speaking of that, yes, you do cut body parts off enemies, in order to salvage tech. But the parts that have tech are also more heavily armored, so you have to decided go for an easy kill on an unarmored part, or make the fight harder and hack at an armored part that will give more rewards. This is also how you can get new weapons, literally cut off the arm holding it. This part of the game I loved. There are slow motion finishers for kills that are incredibly viscerally satisfying, just a fun little payoff at the end of fight (which also means you got more loot). Cutting off limbs/parts also requires being a little careful you don't outright kill them first. You have to wait for a finisher prompt to appear, and then execute that. Means you're fighting a little slower then if you just wanted to outright kill them, which gives them possible openings to get off their own combos on you (and with some, those combos will kill you super quick).

There are implants scattered around the levels that let you customize how you play. My favorite was an implant that turned "energy" (think rage meter in other games. it builds up in combat, gets spent to do finishers, and drains to zero out of combat) into health, stacked with implants that reduced energy decay and cost. Meant that at the end of many fights I could heal to max without having to use the very limited number of charges of healing "injectibles".

The enemies drop "tech scrap" which you spend to upgrade equipment. If you carry a bunch on you, you get a damage bonus, but If you die it drops on the ground. Don't get back and recover it quick enough, you lose it. But, whenever you go back to "medbays" for healing, all the enemies in a zone respawn. At first this sounded horrible, but then I realized that just means you can FARM THEM INDEFINITELY! If you are stuck on a boss fight, just kill the normal enemies until you level up some gear. Or craft a whole different set of armor, weapons, etc, to try new tactics. I've played too many games where you can paint your self into a corner, where you run out of enemies to level skills/abilities, and you just reach a dead end. The endless respawning is actually fun. You learn to carve your way through enemies in the shortest path so you can reach a new area without using up your healing charges (the biggest reason you have to limp back to a medbay, heal up, which resets everything).

If you're curious, you can farm resource to build all the weapons and armor in the game. I liked that. Too many games make you choose what you spend scarce resources on. "Woops, I leveled up the giant sword, so now I can never try out the daggers unless I play a whole new game". There's only one resource for crafting that's has limited amounts, and by the time you reach it, you know what weapons and armor you like using the most, and it's a no brainer what you spend them on.

I'm having fun playing the game, that's all I can say.\t\t\t\t\t
