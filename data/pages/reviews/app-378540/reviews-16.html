\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat can I honestly say about The Surge...I wasn't originally going to review it because it pissed me off so many times through my playthrough that I just wanted to forget about it, whether it was a boss fight that just felt insanely unfair or gear feeling extremely underwhelming to a the point of pointlessness, investing so much time to upgrade armour and weapons and most enemies still dropping me harder than a cancelled Netflix series after 1 season, while dealing about as much damage as a wet noodle against...well anything. In a way it felt like the game was forcing me to play to perfection...don't get hit and just keep hitting, git gud. I raged harder playing this game than any other souls game to date, even the OG souls games were fairly smooth sailing during my first playthroughs of those.

....But there was something about it, an undeniable charm that made me come back again and again and see it through to the end, and after taking some time to cool off and reassess my stance on the game I decided I should put my thoughts to paper. It was unique in many ways, the added feature of been able to target limbs and perform decapitations in such cool and relentless ways and how they implemented crafting materials into this system. Want to upgrade your helmet? cut their heads off, want to upgrade your arm gear? decap their arms. You also gained schematics for new gear pieces by using this same method, same with weapons, and I found this to just be such a cool feature...it turned just mindlessly swinging at an enemy into something more. It did unfortunately increase TTK (Time to Kill) per mob because breaking an enemies armour took a fair bit and it was only going into the last quarter of the game where I felt like I could only just start mowing stuff down in a few hits. Fighting more than 2 mobs at a time was so much harder than It should have been and you're so easily stunned it wasn't even funny. Half my deaths were from me literally been able to do nothing about it due to some giga long stun animation. Combat had a satisfying flow to it though, fights were engaging and tough and the extremely limited healing items made me play in a very cautious manner, regardless of stability. Nice selection of weapons and armour with their own unique gear set bonuses and even better looks. I usually run my first playthrough of any souls game with heavy armour and big weapons but I just felt at a constant disadvantage so I had to change my mindset and try a more agile play style.

The Surge follows the general ideals of a soul game but without its cryptic nature or insanely deep lore. This is a very simplified version with a very straight shooting story where you can meet some pretty normal people who just so happen to be in the middle of the whole thing when the hit the fan and are just trying to survive. The game is something of a maze, its pretty easy to get lost sometimes and felt like I was going in circles for hours sometimes. I personally hate it when I feel like i'm not making any forward progress of any kind regardless of how big or small those steps are.

The Surge was tough, unrelenting and a test of patience, but rewarded you for perseverance, adapting to different scenarios and gaining the skill to overcome them. It unfortunately isn't for everyone, I've had souls loving friends tell me that they couldn't be arsed with this game after the first area...but honestly, despite the many grievances I have against it, I would still recommend giving it a go yourself and just push yourself deep enough into the game before calling it quits.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRight, so I played this, reviewed it, claimed it was a pile of janky junk, then came back to it after looking at some guides and let's play videos to give it another go. I have to say, with some ceveats, it's one of the best games I've played, at least from a story, environemnt and world building perspective. Yeah, it's kinda a high tech Dark Souls, but it nails the story and world building in a much better way in my view.

The game is fun, I suggest getting it on a discount, persevre, don't give up when you get insta-killed near the start, take some time to learn the mechanics and play the game, it's brilliant.

The caveats, too many enemies have insta-kill hit combos, you make one mistake, mistime a block and bang you're dead. So sure, get better, but the problem is it takes you 5 mins to run back to where you were for another go, making it an exercise in extreme frustration at times, I wish they'd done a bit more of an intro and some sort of tutorial fights, to teach you the mechanics of blocking, dodge/duck counter attacks etc.

Still I played it enough to platinum it, love it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most rewarding things about The Surge is its addictive gameplay loop that borrows lovingly from the Souls series complete with hard-hitting enemies, a risk/reward system of replenishing restoratives and health at the expense of resetting hostiles, and a sense of progression from being a total scrub to a near-god. Augmenting this already tried-and-true formula is an interesting limb-severing system that is reminiscent of the Dead Space series. The Surge is steeped in an on-the-cusp-of-the-apocalypse sci-fi horror setting that doesn't pull punches when it comes to its scathing critique of monolithic tech megacorporations and their often out-of-touch leadership.

While The Surge is solid when considered as a whole, there are a few noteworthy areas of criticism. Chief among these is the often-frustrating camera and lock-on system that makes fighting multiple enemies more difficult than it was likely intended to be. Secondly, the physics system resulted in a number of inadvertent deaths in the form of attacks involving lunges forcing your character off a ledge or platform into a "solid abyss" that visually looks like a short drop but was intended by the developers to be an out-of-bounds area. In addition, enemies would sometimes get stuck on seemingly simple geometry and behave in odd ways (e.g., gravity seemingly changing directions and them walking at 45 to 90 degree angles on clutter objects if launched into them). These technical issues aside, nothing felt particularly gamebreaking.

Audio-wise, The Surge is mixed; sound effects are quite good, and one can often find themselves dreading a certain enemy even from its sound cues. Objects, footfalls on various surfaces, and weapon sound effects are all well done, also. The soundtrack of the game is perhaps The Surge's weakest domain, which is highlighted by the fact that the ambient soundscapes in the game work far better at building tension and immersion than do most of the more coherent musical tracks on offer. One particular piece of music that you will often hear can become grating quickly, and you may find yourself hearing it in your nightmares after extended play. None of this is to say that the soundtrack is awful or detracts from the general experience, but it stands out as generally weaker than the rest of the game, with nothing being particularly memorable (at least not in a good way).

Overall, The Surge is worth its asking price. If you use a dollar-to-hour ratio to determine if games are worth your time and money, I finished the game in approximately 37 hours. There is a New Game+ option for the achievement hunters and completionists out there, but I found that I was ready to move on. The Surge does come with a couple of free DLCs that provide some extra armor sets (always welcomed), and the developers offer a couple of premium DLC options that add more weapons and armor in addition to new side stories and areas to explore (I did not purchase either of these at the time of this review).\t\t\t\t\t
