\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving a blast streaming this game!

Pros:
-It has hints of Dark Souls but with much tighter dodges and dashes.
-It has sections that bring the ambience of Dead Space.
-There are limited instructions on how to use your weapons, allowing you to discover your own combos and movesets with them.
-Head, Body, Left/Right Arm and Leg, adds variety in how to prepare yourself when going into battle as well as allowing you which sections to focus on the enemy mobs. Focusing certain parts of the enemy allows you to kill them faster, while other parts have a chance of dropping item/crafting parts. You can even attack different parts with each attack, resulting in gaining more energy so you can perform that "Fatality" on the enemy more easily.
-Makes great use of shortcuts back to your save/spawn.
-Fun and rewarding grinding, crafting and upgrading.

Con (very minor):
-Directions are not always clear, but this encourages exploration and analyzing surroundings.

Overall, I highly recommend this game for people who like to kill enemies over and over again, grind for gear, explore thoroughly and being faced with death every once in a while :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBe advised, I'm not a Dark Souls person. If you want a review from somebody who likes Dark Souls, I won't be able to provide that for you. Sorry.

The Good:
-The high level concept was great. Defeat your opponents, use their stuff to progress.
-Every enemy is a serious challenge
-I honestly enjoyed running around exploring

The Okay:
-The combat system is... okay. When I started the blocking option didn't work, and more often than not I was winning by luck more than anything.

The Bad:
-Too few enemy types/exoframes. There are around six of them, which is dissipointing.
-Weapons are designed in a fashion which rewards you with extra damage for using them. Which takes away any desire to swap to different weapons. Why would I switch to a new weapon which does significantly less damage? I used the weapon from the first boss until the end of the game.
-I hated the bosses. I never found them fun, and without a guide I just got my ass handed to me. Honestly they felt like a punishing roadblock to the only part of the game I liked, exploring.
-The story.... oh god.... I know people rave about the style of storytelling in the Dark Souls series. Where you have to learn what's going on by reading absolutly everything and you never get any real answers to everything.
For some people this is immersive, and realistic.
For me? I found it lazy and forgettable.
Upon the start of the game you know only that something went wrong, everybody seems to be completely insane (except the security?), and that you need to get to the main boardroom where all the answers will be revealed.
After you get to that point the only person who regularly talks to you... stops. You're given no answers, and bizzarely you're expected to continue on to trying to stop a rocket launch but nobody bothers to tell you why.
The secret experiments they're doing are barely hinted at, nobody botheres to explain what happened (in fact everybody asked says "I don't know what happened." Everybody!)

So why am I ripping on the story so heavily?

Well, without anything else to hold onto this game isn't really good.
A good solid story could have elevated this to something memorable.
And I'm not even saying that they had to tell me everything, but they needed to get me engaged. A story that encapsulates the main character's attempts as survival would have been fine.
But the main character barely speaks, has no character to speak of. You can save around four to five people, of which almost all will be dead by the end of the story with little impact. You won't know why you're going forward beyond "A woman told me to" and the end of the game really feels like a slap in the face with either of the endings not really showing any impact from your efforts.

Is it a bad game? No.
But it's not a good game either. It's long, and without a good story it's boring.

I could see this engine being used to make a better game.
But my suggestion is: If you want it? Wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t

Although I believe that only judging this game based on how it steps up to the soulsborne series is a detriment to what this game offers that the soulsborne games dont really do as well in my own opinion and thats boss and world design. I belive that the boss and world design of this game is its biggest strength, the game looks great not only graphically but also design wise, all the weapons and enemies bleed sci fi feel and all feel really great to fight against. I believe I'm about halfway through at this point but i cannot be certain although I feel i can already say what really made me enjoy this more than dark souls and I realized it during the fight with Big Sister "SPOILERS" Big Sister is a boss that is unique to me as someone who plays a TON of role playing games, I do not remember a single time when the boss is also the arena in which you fight, that design really sank in the rest of this games strength as it seemed to show them all off.

1. The combat is more dependant on movement, placement and dodging than powering through enemies.

2. The dismemberment system makes the game much more interesting, think of the last time you played a soulsborne game and saw an enemy carrying a weapon and thought "man I'd really like to have that weapon", this game allows you to use targeted strikes against a specific part of the enemy to gain energy and eventually finish them with a chance of dismembering them and removing that limb, thus gaining the weapon if you target an arm holding a weapon and/or the components and blueprint required to craft the armor they are wearing

3. Boss fights that actually require thought. The bosses in this game aren't simply going to stand there swing a few times and dodge while you widdle them down and run around them, these bosses are going to relentlessly attack you and unless you use your head and think of their weaknesses or ways to keep them from striking you, you are going to be hit and you will lose the fight, as an example. "BOSS FIGHT SPOILERS DO NOT READ IF YOU DONT WANT THE FIRST BOSS SPOILED" when battling PAX the first boss of the game he is going to attack you for a time while you simply swing at his legs, eventually he will enter a different combat pattern in which he will jump jet away from you and launch missiles at you. I'm sure you can imagine what to do, you need to stand under him so he attacks himself leaving him open to damaging blows.

In the end these are just my top 3 reasons to pick up this game I'm sure I could think up more but this is just for a review. I really think that if you enjoy role playing games and you enjoy the soulsborne games that you should give this game a shot, it might surprise you. 9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

TL:DR-
Gameplay (Overall) 8/10
Combat 10/10
Story 6/10
Visual/Auditory Appeal 8/10
Optimization 8/10
Bugs 7/10
Viewpoint Quality (Camera Angle Adjustments) 9/10

As a dark souls Veteran, it's hard not to compare elements of this game to the series, but it is important to remember that although people consider this game a souls-like, it still has it's own aspects that make it a good game without comparing it to what has already been done. Nevertheless, enemies and bosses in this game were amazing fights for me, there is more than one way to defeat a boss, usually with the harder way giving you some sort of a bonus item, enemies have variety in moveset and design, although the majority of enemies are humanoid.

Combat in this game is near flawless, it is fully fleshed out and there are many mechanics at play that make it just feel fantastic to execute.

As far as the story goes, it's not bad. It is not the greatest game story you've ever played, but it is not a terrible one, it's a simple arc and there are limited choices to be made which overall do not impact the end, even so, I still found myself immersed on what was going on, although at first it may be difficult to discern what is happening, I cannot stress how important it was to my experience by playing the game with no outside aid or any explanations until the end.

Auditorally and Visually the game is greatly appealing, there are quite a few effects used perfectly to give depth and make scenarios seem more dynamic.

The game is optimized well, running a pretty poor setup currently and only had occasional frame drops below 60 when users would watch my broadcast.

I've never had any bugs besides it crashing twice.

EDIT: Free DLC=Yes from me.

I loved this game, and I hope that you do not miss out on a chance to purchase it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDid you like Lords of the Fallen? If you did, this is basically a sci-fi "Elysium"-ish follow-up. If you didn't like LotF, what the @#$%! is wrong with you? Seriously, gameplay is very similar to LotF, with a unique and unusual progression system. What you have here is a Dark Souls-ish action RPG set in a dystopian future where characters wear "rigs" bolted into their bodies like the one Matt Damon had in the film "Elysium". Destroy body parts of enemies and craft new rig parts based on schematics found on their parts. Don't like crafting in games? Me, too... I hate it. But this game makes it simple and easy with minimal farming. This isn't an MMORPG thank goodness.

Graphics are excellent, and if you get "blue flashing lights", update your drivers to resolve it. The landscape is basically industrial with a lot of trash and debris, which unfortunately gets a little busy and same-y for my taste. Enemy designs are interesting. Music is dark and electronic, but sparsely used which is unfortunate. LotF had a great soundtrack which was well-utilized. Story is told through logs and holographic messages.

In all, a worthy sci-fi "sequel" to LotF, but not quite as good in some ways. B-

UPDATE:
Much like LotF, this game gets far more difficult right near the end. The second to last boss can be extremely infuriating, so be warned.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely excellent, everthing you'd hope it could be.

In comparison to Souls:
- Not as long (take that how you want)
- Combat is significantly more in depth with the limb cutting system, offers a lot more tactical options.
- Gear crafting and salvaging also takes a unique approach (to the extent of my knowledge)
- Leveling is also refreshingly different, and arguably more meaningful and a bit simpler.
- Ranged combat is severely limited but I'm fine with that.

Other:
- Wish there were more dark & scary parts
- I have encountered no bugs or performance issues
- Controller highly recommended (I used a steam controller)
- There is no multiplayer whatsoever

If you play Souls and want a Sci-Fi equivalent, look no further.

No disappointment 10/10\t\t\t\t\t
