\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is difficult, that is both a bad and good thing.

The maps start out feeling very well built. Their design starts out feeling very narrow and linear and you quickly realize that all those locked doors end up being quick ways to get back to base. This is great. I never felt like I was too far away from stocking up on med kits while still getting challenged at every fight. However as the game goes on, the ability to cut back to the safe room becomes more obscure and harder to figure out. This could have been fixed by either creating a better indicator showing where the base is like some sort of arrow system on the ground pointing "first aid this way" which would let players know at a fork in the road, head right to unlock the shortcut back here. Too many times I guessed wrong and ended up losing all my tech points because I died trying to get back to them. The other solution would have been to include some sort of map to let us know where we are in relation to the other areas we've already explored. Maybe include things like those in an easy/normal difficulty and strip them out for hard.

Combat - It took a while to get used to, and the notes on the walls telling you how to fight were perfect. Until I got to a boss fight, and had NO idea what the hell to do. No clue when I should attack him, just countless attempts of trial and error which I didn't find particularly fun. Once I knew how to approach a boss I found them challenging but overall a good reward for the effort. What didn't stop annoying all through the game were instances were you'd get hit once, and get chain stuck/stunned and just die. I almost wanted to give this game a negative rating JUST FOR THIS.

Story - Great start, really enjoyed the not knowing what was going on at the start and the bread crumbs that kept you moving forward. I started losing interest in the lab with the robots and completely hated the last level with the new enemies. Just felt like it was a turn/plot point not needed for the world you created.

Even with it's flaws I think it's worth playing for a fun challenging single player. I just hope the things I mentioned are fixed in 2.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is just great. I have played the first Dark Souls before this and I believe The Surge takes those elements and puts them with a more appealing style. I prefer science fiction over fantasy so this was already a plus. The combat is a lot faster than DS which takes some getting used to but makes the action feel amazing. The character customization really feels unique. There are multiple implants that you can swap out that affect your health, damage, stamana, or energy. The energy bar is a cool feature that builds up when you attack an enemy and decreases over time. Use this energy to use health packs, activate drones and implants, or execute opponents. This was something new that I think aids in this more fast pace DS like game.

My two biggest complaints are that so far (11 hours in) the bosses seem to fall off in difficulty and the game continually crashes. The boss fights are cool and unique but after the first boss the next 2 feel a lot easier. However this could be due to the possibility that I might be using a very good load out. In terms of the crashing, yes the game crashes every 10-60min. BUT it seems like it does this because it is not interacting properly with current Windows 10 updates. I fixed this by running steam as an admin and with windows 8 compatibility mode. But this was frustrating before so I at least want to put that information out there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t-- This is probably the 4th time I've posted a review . Perhaps I don't remove this one! ;p It may be a mess but hopefully speaks my overall opinion . Blap --

Weapon design is bad and probably the worst part of the game, you generally only given a lore description. There are several damage types but you have to test every weapon against every enemy to figure out which one is weaker to what weapon. This means you must farm to upgrade the weapons to the same levels or you'll not be able to gather anything conclusive. With all the various numbers and the 4 different damage types, it feels like going for just straight up high damage is almost the only choice as most of the time faster weapons drain your stamina too quick and do not do enough "Poise" damage to allow you to keep enemies staggered, allowing for less dodging as well as less damage. There are a lot of various weapon types and a large variation to play around with, but in the end any of the faster weapons are pretty much worthless, they are worthless for blocking and deal too little even when max upgraded. It seems damage on weapons were designed after DPS testing, but you can not just mad mash a lot of enemies or you'll get over powered.
With so many weapons, you'd imagine there would be some with unique "move sets" or cool special effects to be found, but it is either mostly for looks or slight variation. I don't know, with so little change there's no need for so many weapons.
2 / 5 - A lot more work needed so to make it feel like you have freedom to pick what you enjoy playing with, without making it feel like every scrub fight is a brick wall.

Armors are quite bad too, there are sets but the only way to figure out what they are.. again, you must build the whole set, which in terms means you must farm more. Only to find out the set you wanted to try is garbage. A set which gives more armor at high level doesn't give more than a better set gives without having to manage to stay at high energy. And while high armor may help a little bit, you'll never be a mad tank murderous machine even if you'll be a somewhat of a tankier murdering machine.
2.5 / 5 - One sort of gets forced to make sure to have high stability, or most of the somewhat survivable situations ends up with a visit from the grim reaper.

"Monster"/enemy design is alright, some boss(es) are awesome, others very dull. Combat mostly isn't great, even when you figure it out a lot of the time it becomes more tedious rather than fun. You do encounter various enemies and have to figure out how to fight them, but then they are just everywhere. So you found it tedious to fight one? Here's two, here's three.. If you were to get killed, the gathered currency for upgrading and so forth will be dropped (which is fine) and has a pickup timer. Getting to the currency in time most likely is going to push you to lose it all rather than make it to it in time. The timer choice I don't get.
2 / 5 - While nothing looks bad. Needs more bosses and enemies to encounter. Less "thicc" enemy spam and more interesting fighting would be a lot more fun.

Map design has been mentioned a little and I've got to say I find it quite terrible, there are some areas that feel nice to be in but it generally always ends up looking mostly the same. Narrow and dark. The flashlight doesn't do much as to face the flashlight forward you must look up and such your character blocks most of your view, so for a very long time I had the flashlights pointing down at an angle.
3 / 5 - Little to no variation in the general feel of getting to some new area.

Combat is lacking and quite dull, pushing through lots of enemies and later on tons of high health enemies with little to no variation. Rather than spreading some interesting boss fights through out the game and having interesting variation of both enemies and weapons. Curiosity helps as the story isn't bad. Inconsistent blocking, 100% stamina, guarding, get hit through guard. Next time, same attack, guarding, blocks. The same goes for many things, such as sometimes enemies has a delay between two different animations and sometimes there's no delay and you die due to expecting the delay between animations and try to use the window of opportunity. If you get knocked down, you stay down and keep taking damage but if you knock down an enemy and hit them, for the most part you reset their stance and they can attack almost instantly again.
There are several other issues with combat, making a lot of intended combos not work due to enemies being close thus making their purpose almost pointless as the few initial attacks hit nothing but air thus neglecting the purpose of using a combo. Each weapons type have other attacks if you are close to an enemy, but those attacks do not lead into combo moves and also if an enemy gets too close and you end up doing the wrong attack because of it, the combo will not execute.

Movement, I mean you do move but nothing special. Nothing great either. Some times dodging doesn't work because the enemy crosses to the opposite side of you, so your attempt ot jump away ends up jumping into the enemy. Which in some cases means death.

It is a first in the series and they did do better than most would, the game isn't horrible as there's a lot of interesting discovery, story wise and finding out your playstyle. Even if I do not like the map design some things do look nice. I recommend against it as I did not enjoy many design choices made, such as how they decided to make the difficulty work, map design and how little information the player is given.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWas looking for my next souls like challenge after beating Lords of the Fallen, The Surge did not disappoint, i think i am officially Deck13 fan now.

Graphically The Surge holds up very well, and i love the dark, creepy, at timed almost Dead Space like design, there where more then one instance i screamed like a little girl spooked by an enemy hiding in the shadows.
This sci-fi spin on a souls like genre feels very refreshing, combat feels very fast and fluid, a lot more forgiving than Dark Souls, which is great for coordination challenged people like myself who still want to enjoy souls like challenges. Story, just as in Lords of the Fallen case, very forgettable, it's definitely better, but it's not there yet, protagonist "Warren" is uninteresting and you learn absolutely nothing about him, but he gets Ripley's power loader suite, so he's got that going for him. Plenty of awesome and diverse weapons to satisfy your every combat style, bosses are very original and well designed. The Surge leaves you wanting for more Surge but with a better story and a protagonist you might care about.

The Surge is great and i recommend it to anyone like myself looking for their next souls like fix.\t\t\t\t\t
