\r\n\t\t\t\t\tnot enough bosses, confusing areas, possible to lock yourself in a higher level area unknowingly.
That's just a few things... The gameplay loop* is slow, painful, and often more frustrating than anything, the combat and promise of a souls-like experience hooks you in, and doesn't deliver at the degree you want it to. I wish I'd refunded this game when I had the chance. At least I got it for sale I guess....

*The main problem lies in the progression systems. Your level determines what areas you can access, what gear you can equip, and if you want any abilities (like healing yourself or using a drone), you have to level up for that too. In order to get abilities, you have to find them in the enviroment, and sometimes get them from enemies. Gear blueprints are attained by targeting specific portions of an enemy's body and finishing them, which is cool at first, but that is just the blueprint, you still need to farm the specific materials required to build it (often from the same body part on the same enemy or a similar enemy). AND THE PROFICIENCY SYSTEM, oh the proficiency system! It is the bane of my existence. You level up your damage dealt with a specific weapon type by using the weapon type, that means you basically have to choose a main weapon type or two for the whole game, and you have to choose early. Otherwise you'll be doing extra grinding.

These mechanics combine together to mess with you, the game incentivizes you to stay in one place for a long period of time to build yourself up, moving outside of your area without building better gear often leads to a quick death, and the level design doesn't help. In the first area, there are two medbays (bonfires). You'll be going back to each one multiple times, not just because there are multiple shortcuts to open, but also because you don't want to lose your scrap to a high level enemy that might keep you from them. (souls) Also, scrap is on a timer! If you explore too far from home and die, you might not be able to get there in time. This game isn't that difficult, just slow and frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall a fun if short game, recommended if it goes on sale.

Nice variety of gear that accommodates different play styles. You are encouraged to switch out your implants to deal with different situations. Your weapons will gradually be replaced by superior alternatives toward the end of the game. You can finish the game with your starting armor, but the game includes toxic gas/sludge which only a few armor sets protect against (I ended up using one of those for 80% of the game).

The combat encourages sprinting in getting some hits in then backing off. Not quite the dodge + R1 dance you would do in Dark Souls. You can still get mileage out of dodging, well-timed blocks (and extremely rarely ducking) for certain attacks. Main weakness of the combat is the lack of bosses in the base game combined with the degree of BS which they throw at you.

The story is another strong point, quite interesting without being obvious sequel-bait. I would say The Surge has a better story than the sequel, but The Surge 2 improves on pretty much everything else.

The level design is by far the biggest letdown, the recycled assets and areas end up looking too similar. It's very easy to lose your bearings, even if you're just trying to recover your scrap after dying. A basic portable map or fast travel between ops centers would have been nice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game. I really do. And I wish that I played this before playing any souls games. I feel like part of the reason I can't stick with it is because of the age. But there are many other reason that have turned me off from the game. Which sucks because I bought part 1 and 2. Coming from souls games, the enemies aren't particularly difficult. I actually think that's a good thing. However, the sheer cheese involved in fighting enemies is infuriating. I agree with other reviews that boss fights are pretty much about tanking hits in phase 2 and you will beat most of them. And I like that the game forces you to learn the mob enemies. What takes away from that is the stupid add-ins that, I suppose, is to increase difficulty. i.e. having a enemy that you can easily defeat, break a gas container and instantly set it on fire killing you and him. or opening a door and getting immediately boxed in by armored machines, or going to fight an enemy and there is a random hole in the floor that leads to nowhere inside of a basement floor lab. If you can stomach the cheese, you may enjoy this game. But every time I enter a new area, there is a new example of cheap foolishness to contend with.

Aiming on body parts is a cool mechanic, if you can get it to stay on the body part. It could be controller use but my lock often bounces to armored body parts and I have to fight with it to aim back on the proper part. Cool idea, implementation though...... could use some work.

Finally, I have to speak to the mapping system. Its nonexistent and its terrible. What people are saying is true, there is very little logical progression to the map and I can attest to running around aimlessly attempting to progress the story. I tried not to look up any spoilers online because I didn't want to ruin the first time experience for any new enemies but I didn't have a choice. The lady inside of the OPS room is helpful sometimes and sometimes she repeats hints that you have already solved.

Overall, it does quite a few things right. But if you are going to try for souls-like, you can build the difficulty into the enemies. There is no need for the extra cheese. The game can be plenty difficult and frustrating without it.\t\t\t\t\t
