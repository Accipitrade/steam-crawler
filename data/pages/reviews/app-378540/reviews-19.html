\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Very solid game for the low price. I personally have spent roughly 15 hours on The Surge and I just finished it. The Surge is like Dark Souls but less of the things that makes Dark Souls super tedious. Take this for example, If you do an execution move on an enemy in this game, and the execution move puts you character off a cliff; the game will not let you fall; it will instead return you to the edge of the ledge. In dark souls it would just let you fall.

However there is a few draw backs. In order to get your tech ( this games version of souls.) back you have to return to where you died within a limited number of time. You have about one minute and thirty second to get you tech back. There is usually only one OPS ( this games version of bonfires) in each area. The Surge remedies for this by making many paths back to each OPS which does get a little confusing. The last and most devastating lacking feature of all is the lack of direction the game gives you.

In the end this game is well worth the price. If anything the game is under priced. 8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst impression (this is not my definitive review) :

The story feels a bit vague (probably by design). You get the basics and that's enough if there's some more explanations by the end. It's a similar pace as Dark Souls but NPCs give you very basic directions on what you may have to do.

Graphics are unremarkable aside from cutscenes (which look great). The level of detail is bad even when you max out graphic options.

Gameplay is not bad but still feels a bit clunky.
You can dive under attacks and jump over some others but the issue is that you can do it as blocking only... blocking prevents you from moving so that remains to improve.
You can target limbs to get stuff for your exoskeleton and when an enemy is close to dying you can tear a limb by the press of a button.
I noticed that sometimes you suddenly are a meter or two away once the animation is finished (that's something to polish).
Overall combat is still manageable but ultimately flawed (you even have a DLC that basically gives you OP gear in the first level, that's ridiculous). Bosses are cool so far, they all have their particular thing to them (it's the fun part of the game imo).
I noticed once that the death of the last i killed (at the time of posting) insta killed me and on the run back one enemy type one shots me basically as soon as he deals damage (which is 3 dps and boom i'm dead even though i have over 150 health). Might be a glitch, idk but that pissed me a fair bit trying to run back to that dead boss location 6 flipping times.
All of that is nothing compared to my biggest gripe with this game :
The layout and interconnectivity. It's confusing and unintuitive as f*** to the point it feels like bad design (especially for a souls-like in that regard i'd be tempted to say pathetic). The first three Elder Scrolls are clear as day in comparison, that's saying a lot !

I'll try to cool down and get back to it but i could use a drink or ten and a smoke, my middle finger is stuck up facing my screen.

Update n1 : After 14 more hours in the game, i reached the end of the first playthrough.
The biggest challenge remained to know where to go 90% of the time. Story wise there are some cool twists i'm not sure i completely understood (including the ending).
Once finished i got booted to the main screen and hit continue which forced me to NG+.
There are some collectibles, not an excessive amount which is a nightmare without a guide.
I'd strongly recommend getting "The Good, the Bad and the Augmented" as it makes it possible to fast travel basically. Otherwise, you'll need to do a f*** ton of backtracking while hoping that you're not getting lost...

Update n2 : Second playthrough finished. While what i think might be the ending is still not clear, it gives a bit more exposure. I heard that the "Walk in the Park" DLC gives more information about the protagonist but i can't be bothered playing it now... maybe some other time. Finishing main game is really satisfying for the wrong reason -> How much of a nightmare the layout is. In case i didn't mention it, the game sometimes has trouble detecting dualshock imput when starting the game (meaning i sometimes needed to Alt+F4 and launch the game again until it works for some reason). One cool thing is that the game supports playstation prompts meaning i didn't get confused all the time when i saw prompts (that could have led me to far more deaths than i already got).

TLDR :

Story : 5/10
No clue what's going on.
Graphics : 4/10
Low quality details, overall unremarkable
Combat : 8/10
Dismemberment, jump or duck while guarding, souls-like, squishy...
Environment : 0.5/10
Labyrinthian, infuriating, confusing...

I give it a pass for the effort.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the start you think, that looks nice ... but then get stomped in the face.

+ Nice SiFi-setting.
+ Ran and uninstalled fine.

- Combat is clunky, You often get stuck in an animation, can't move / dodge.
- Camera is just meh when fighting several enemies.
- Levels are very linear, confusing and include a lot of backtracking.
- Some weapons to pick from.
- The game punishes you when you don't stick with one type from the start.
- If you get stuck in a difficult spot. Got luck, because you can't level up / grind past it.
- Only two armor types (light and heavy)?
- No manual save system.
- When you want to heal or save, all enemies reset.
- So you have to fight the same enemies over and over and over.

The game loop is not fun. Nor does the game reward the player (apart from wasting your time).

Score: 5/10\t\t\t\t\t
