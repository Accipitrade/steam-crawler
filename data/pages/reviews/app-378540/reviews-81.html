\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is, overall, a fantastic game. If you're into Dark Souls, then this is almost that with a future tech dystopia twist. I'd like to offer some tips before you start the game, though:

1. You will probably die a lot. I did, anyway. Enemies have unique attack patterns and generally hit pretty hard. They can perform combos, too, that are pretty much guaranteed death if you get caught in them. Be ready to get frustrated at times while you git gud.

2. You don't have to fight every enemy. Enemies are "locked" within certain travel radii. Sometimes it's just one room that they will chase you within. This means that running right past mobs to get back to where you previously were is sometimes the best option.

3. Spend time mastering your weapon's moveset. Every weapon behaves differently, and some weapons' attacks give you more mobility than you might otherwise. Additionally, learn how to consistently leap-attack. You are able to lunge at your enemy and attack, and doing so followed by a quick leap backward is great for many enemies in this game. Think of it as a poke.

4. Kind of a followup to all of these: be patient. The Surge is a game that plays best, to be frank, on New Game +. This means beating it once and then taking it on again. However the second playthrough, in my experience, is where you effectively become a Robo-God and can dispense cyberphobic massacre protocols like you were born to do so. Seriously, you will blenderize things.

So yeah, all that said, really fun game, good in spurts or long play times, and the sequel is coming out in the not too distant future. Grab it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough at first the art style and the settings did not appeal to me, that did not matter:
The combat is so much fun, very fluent and definitely a unique departure from the classic dark souls formula. The progression system works great and introduces interesting depth. Do I want more HP, or rather more healing items? Or do I want more damage buff "potions"? (The game calls these things "injectables")
The weapons are varied and fun to use. The enemies, although usually a variation of "a dude with a weapon", also feature enough variety to keep you on your toes.
There are not that many bosses, but the ones that exist are very different in design, and very memorable. Also they feature a more challenging way of defeating them called "hardcore kill", which usually yields a better reward (you should check the wiki on the exact reward for each hardcore kill though).
Deck 13 managed to create their own IP, but more than that they created a unique an fun way of doing a souls game.

In conclusion: Everybody who likes souls-gameplay with a unique spin on melee combat should check this one out!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:

1. Soulsborne-like gameplay and combat
2. Futuristic setting
3. Great atmosphere
4. Huge variety of armor and weapons
5. Huge variety of implants to build around
6. A lot of viewable builds you can go for
7. Boss fights are fun
8. Overall-Story is ok
9. At least 30 hours of gameplay on your first playthrough i would say (with Dlcs)
10. High replay value because of a great NewGame+ System
11. 100% Archivements is some work and grind, but still easy enough to do so

Neutral:

1. Very dark in some places, even with your light on. Can be a bit annoying, more so if there is some smoke as well.
2. There aren't that many places where you can fall off (not like DS), but when there are you will be falling from time to time
3. Some sidequests and NPCs could be written better
4. Overall Story could be a little bit more interesting
5. I encountered some crashes, but nothing devastating because of a great autosave-system
6. 2-3 more bosses would have been great
7. Enemy variety could be a bit bigger

Cons:

1. You can't just switch gear sets and weapons around as you may like. It can get very expansive in doing so (depends on the Tier you are) and you may need to grind a bit if you want to switch
2. You can get one of the best weapons from the first boss already, so that's a weird game design in my mind
3. The "A Walk In The Park" DLC is great, everything else is way too overpriced. I recommend the "Augmented Edition" on sale

In-Depth analysis:

As a old Soulsborne/Nioh/LOTF Veteran i think that might be the hardest in that genre to get into (well, maybe Nioh), but after a while i just realized i just have to adapt myself more. While the gameplay might be the same as other games in that genre, it's still different in a lot of ways, you just have to get into it and forget about everything you learned from the other games. It's nearly pure meele and no way to avoid it, like in other games. You can't just cheese your way trought, use ranged attacks, or overlevel yourself so you have an easier time. You really need to concentrate all the time and be aware what you do.

When you played said games before you will recognize some mechanics. When you die, you have to get your Tech Scrap (Currency) back and there is a timer to get them back, just like in LOTF. When you die enemies will respawn, same when you are using the Medbay. There are limited options to regain your health at first, but the longer you play the more ways you will find to regain health. Dodging is essential and blocking pretty useless. You have to be mobile and always have an eye on your stamina. When you get greedy you die really fast. 2-3 hits at the beginning are deadly.

Here is where the uniqueness from "The Surge" comes into play. First of all, there is no Skillsystem. Most of your stats will come from your Armor and your Weapon itself. From Stability, Impact, Attack Speed to Defense and Damage. The rest, like Health, Stamina, Energy and everything else comes from all kind of implants you can choose from. So all the Currency you get you will need just for upgrading your "Core Power", so you can use more implants and for crafting and upgrading your gear.

Setting and Atmosphere is just awesome. You are crawling through dark corridors, always with a claustrophobic feeling, enemies around every corner, nobody knows what's going on and no light at the end of the tunnel. The map outlay can and will be very confusing and it will take a while to get yourself orientated. There are a lot of doors you can't open until later in the game and a every map have a dozen of shortcuts. Overall level design is pretty great.

While the DLC "A Walk In The Park" is a bit short (around 2 hours) and more on the easier side, it's is totally worth to get. It's adds a fantastic new zone, 2 new bosses, gear sets, weapons, implants and new enemies. Great level design and atmosphere as well.

The other "real" DLC "The Good, the Bad and the Augmented" adds a series of intersting Test Champers. While the setting is very odd, it is fun for a few hours, but the replay value just isn't there, when you got everything. It is one of the best places to farm Tech Scraps and it contains a few of the strongest implants in the game though.

Long story short, don't compare it to a Soulsborne game too much. While it have the same overall mechanic, it's still quite different. When you like those kind of games, you won't miss out on that hidden gem. It's entertaining, has a unique setting and some great gameplay elements. Just give yourself a chance to get into it and you will love it. I actually liked the game so much i just played trough it twice on my Ps4 and then another two times on Steam, finally getting the full "Augmented Edition" with 100% Archivements. Highly recommended on sale.

Ps.: Also "The Surge 2" incoming. \\o/\t\t\t\t\t

I started this game expecting a decent experience, nothing great, nothing bad, but Deck13 managed to greatly exceed those expectations (especially after Lords of the Fallen, which I actually quite enjoyed despite its flaws). The Surge demonstrates that the guys at Deck13 understand the formula popularized by From Software, with immensely satisfying level design inter-connectivity and world building, surprisingly compelling story and characters, and a deeper fighting system than I expected at first.

The game is not without its flaws: voicing Warren, the protagonist, sadly ended up being superfluous to the story (which is a shame given the potential his background could have had), the moveset variety of the weapon is relatively scarce (an issue decently compensated by the animations' great quality) and the score and overall sound design, while serviceable, don't stand up to the same standards of the gameplay and feel of the game, in my experience.

At first the exaggerated damage output from certain enemies can feel a bit unnecessary, given that the fighting system and enemy placement provide a decent challenge by themselves already. Maybe in an attempt to live up to the expectations of the genre, the developers felt that it was better to risk making the game more difficult than intended instead of trivializing it. Either way, as the player gets stronger and stronger the further he ventures in the game's world ( with an upgrading system based on equipment and implants instead of the traditional levelling up) this initial shock of being taken by surprise gets replaced by a swift response from the player leading to satisfying sequences of insults to the enemies that once terrified them.

In conclusion, the guys at Deck13 managed to shock me positively, in almost all of The Surge's aspects, whether it was discovering the little nuances of the combat or feeling much more touched than I expected by the supporting characters' backstories. It's an experience I highly recommend to any fan of the genre, especially to those longing for the feel the first Dark Souls gave to them in their first playthrough.\t\t\t\t\t
