\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; It's fun, don't compare it to dark souls and your good. Beware the ♥♥♥♥ camera.
UPDATE: After making my way through the game and reaching the second to last level and having over 20 hours logged, the game crashed and i lost my save. Now i have to start from the begining. Needless to say, i am extremely upset by this occurance.

Fair warning, I don't have loads of experience with souls style games, so this is really a first for me. I cut my teeth on the souls genre with Lord's of the fallen, after it left a bad taste in my mouth I went for the surge, it's style, setting and feel captured my attention.

Before I identify what's wrong with the game, I want to take a moment and explain a glaw I have seen with alot off the other reviews I have read. Either they praise the game and don't call out any of it's flaws or they mercilessly compare it to dark souls, the king of this genre. Neither of those perspectives are fair to the game at all. The first thing to know is that unlike dark souls, this game does not emphasize the boss fight. Rather, levels are peppered with enemies that are challenging and require good timing, skill, and a bit of tactical thinking to take down. Comparatively, bosses are just larger, spongier, and stronger versions of the same mobs you fight throughout the level. Second, it's a lot slower paced that dark souls. It rewards slow, methodic thinking and the use of unorthodox tactics. You will be rewarded often for thinking outside of the box. The game is also much smaller than dark souls. The maps are far less expansive and vast and at times that is where the challenge comes from. But considering the game takes place in an industrial center, I think it's very fitting. My point is that the game is not dark souls so comparing them is hardly worth the time.

That said, I have spent a fair amount of time with the game, and comparing it to deck13's previous attempt at this genre which, in my opinion, is damn near unplayable at times due to camera issues, overly cramped spaces, geometry problems, and a general lack of direction. The surge doesn't suffer from those issues to as great an extent as Lord's of the fallen. While I desperately wish I could say that all the aforementioned problems do not exist in this title, that is sadly not the case. While the game is good, I overall would recommend it to anyone who enjoys a challenge, rage quiting at 3am, and anyone who is a fan of the souls genre, it does have glaring issues.

Camera movement and perspective:
this is perhaps the biggest issue that I have with the game. 99% of the times that I have died I find that it's usually my fault. Either I am not prepared for an area or I made an error in battle. Unfortunately, that other 1% really sticks in my ass. I can't help but get pissed off when I die because of the field of view or the angle that the camera is at and I simply couldn't react. This was not particularly a problem at the beginning of the game when things are more mellow while still being challenging. During the first boss fight it became a small issue and I quickly learned that you should not lock on to the legs of the boss while attacking. During the second boss fight I had the same issue but to a larger degree as the boss is highly mobile and has multiple phases of attack. Then there is the third boss. Arguably the hardest of all 5 bosses in the game. The camera in this section is so ♥♥♥♥♥♥. UP. it is rediculous that testers and designers allowed this to pass inspection and test plays. The camera zooms out because of the scale of the boss, affording you presuous little sight of your character. This makes movement difficult and the entirety of the boss battle feels cheap, particularly when you die.

Combat: there is no nice way to say it. Combat gets repetitive when it works. Though rare, I have had a hand full of instances where my attacks fail to chain properly despite my repeated input for more damage with the rappid tapping of the mouse button, leaving my character to stand like an idiot and neeldessly take a hit. But I also think that this may be an issue with my mouse from time to time.

Now that I have criticized the game fairly for what it fails to deliver, I think a proper run down of what it does well is in order.

Graphics: the game looks amazing. There is plenty of fidelity to all of the models, geometry and textures look infinatly better than they did in Lord's of the fallen. The over all feel of the game is astounding and reminds me slightly of Titanfall.

Sound: the sounds are spot in my opinion. Hitting unarmored flesh sounds like the most painful thing in the world, while armor sounds sturdy and gives a solid report when hit. The sound track is also wonderful, setting the mood well and giving a since of aw. Unfortunately, this is a double edged sword as I fail to be surprised by the enemy waiting around the cordner when the music suddenly goes from being barely noticeable to in my face.

Gameplay: gameplay is, on the whole, very good. Not only are the combat systems of slicing specific limbs off of your email for their precious precious loot amazingly gruesome, but they are rewarding. The game never fails to make me feel like I am progressing. Whether it be farming parts for upgrades, trying to collect new gear, or making my way back to previous sections after gathering upgrades and abilities only to find a few new challenging enemies and a while other half of the map I didn't know existed prior. The mechanics of the game are exicuted perfectly in my opinion.
Story: okay. This one is a tuffy. I think the developers played dark souls, saw that the story was hard to access and took that as a staple of the genre. Unfortunately, their exicution on this is Rocky, but not bad. I had the story figured out pretty early on after listening to some audio logs and (I'm not as ahsamed as I probably should be for this) looking up the ending on YouTube. Turns out there are two endings that people overlook as being to similar. But what I've learned through my play through of the game is this: their story is hard to access because of how subtle they are with it. For example: both endings are pretty much the same EXCEPT for one major factor, the diolog at the end of the cutscene. Since watching the ending prematurely u have gathered an appreciation for every since gle small detail such as the music in the ops center that plays on loop for ever and ever, it's done on purpose. Listening to the lyrics and beating the game will make you slap your forehead in realization as to what details where in front of you the whole time. While their method of story telling is different is most certenly is not bad by any means. It is very well constructed and I think does an amazing job at portraying a setting that we have seen many times before.
In summation: the game has problems. Deck13 is clearly trying their damnedest at making the genre more diverse with interesting titles that expand on the possibilities. Unfortunately, innovation comes at a price and that price happens to be their ♥♥♥♥ camera work. Does that make the game bad? Probably. Full disclosure, I'm hooked on the story even though I know the ending and I just keep coming back. All humor aside, the game is more than playable, just occasionally frustrating. It is a compelling title with loads of potential and after spending enough time with it you might be thinking that it was wasted potential. And you would probably be right. Deck13 seems to only be able to deliver a partial package. Bethesda does the same thing and people enjoy the ♥♥♥♥ out of their titles though so maybe it will work for them. Only time will tell.
I personally love the game, story, and challenges it has to offer and desperately hope there is a sequel. I think that people need to stop comparing it to dark souls, allow the game to stand on its own merrits and judge it fairly so the next time we get a game from them it's perfect.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t~~~Forward~~~

I don't typically do reviews, however this game having "mixed" reviews does not accurately convey the quality of this product. Due to this, I wanted to throw in my two cents in the attempt to add my voice to the sea of criticisms to this game.

~~~ END Forward ~~~
~~~ TL;DR ~~~

The game uses similar thought processes and logic to a Soulsbourne title however, like the PS4 game Nioh, uses different mechanics to showcase the "puzzle logic" Soulsbourne titles use to make their combat engaging. The difference with The Surge is two-fold. The first is it follows "Lords of the Fallen" from the same developer that was a carbon copy of Dark Souls with literal poor mechanics, the second is the mechanics of "The Surge" are close enough to a Soulsbourne game (unlike Nioh) that people misinterpret their own misunderstanding of the game's mechanics with 'bad mechanics'

~~~ End TL;DR ~~~
~~~ Graphics & Performance ~~~

PROS - Stunning visuals, modern showcase of technical ability, could have been made by a AAA studio. Sound design, animations, and polish are all here to create an immersive experience.

CONS - I have engaged one bug on an exo-lift that caused me to have to restart, and some exeucte animations can cause slow-down or hangs before completing.

~~~ End Graphics & Performance ~~~
~~~ Level Design ~~~

The levels are inter-connected in a way that is reminscent of Dark Souls but not as good as that one. Plenty of secrets, some of which will require you to come back to the zones later in the game when you have had some progression. The over-all progression from level to level is seamless and the well made visuals align with a very cohesive exploration your first play through.
~~~ End Level Design

~~~ Combat ~~~

The combat is engaging. You have a vertical and horizontal attack. You can create combos by combining these attacks with each other up to a 4th attack ( Ex, RB / RT / RT / RB - 4 attacks ). Each weapon can have different attacks - but not all do. The dodge mechanics is engaging and the counter mechanic works as intended.

CONS - The problem with the counter/block mechanics and dodge mechanic is there are some units that have undodgeable ( no invulnerability frame ) or unblockable ( you need to dodge/jump counter ) and there is no way of knowing which attacks are undodgeable or unblockabel without being screwed by this via trial and error. This is very frustrating for a blind playthrough. The mechanics themselves work well though.

Also, the stats are ambiguous and you have no way of discerning what each stat actually does. There is some intended obfuscation here which is not necessary or needed in this genre.

~~~ Story ~~~

Typical cryptic soulsbourne story with a hint of deus ex machina and cyber dystopia. You can take it or leave it as it's pretty much completely optional

CONS - Some of the sidequests are very poor in the "work vs. reward" for the sidequests. Also without any codex to track quests it can become easy to forget about them.

~~~ End Story ~~~

~~~ Summary ~~~

Give this game a chance. It is well worth your time if you like the puzzle logic -style of a Soulsbourne game. It's subtly different and the setting is engaging enough to give it a shot considering it's not the full AAA price even at full price it is worth the buy in. It has a demo which transfers your time to the full purchase and then you can get another two hours of the game before refunding it if you dislike it. Very good value propisition.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile this game is not ideal, if you are interested in souls-like games and sci-fi then it is worth a try. It does not do either particularly well, but it does not do either particularlly bad. Go in with low expectations; be pleasently surprised. Go in with high expectations; be dissapointed.

In terms of game play, it is a souls-like game where the main (yet, truly minor) deviation comes from the combat system. Instead of heavy and weak attacks, there is horizontal and vertical. To complement this, you can target specific parts of the body. While playing, you will not really care too much about horizontal or vertical, instead opting for specific combos. As far as the targeting system, you target the weak points. This variation is rather minor.

In terms of execution of a souls like, the game comes up bland once again. There are only 5 bosses, but you do get to learn there moves and patterns. And they are fair, for the most part. The combat pace is closer to bloodborne, and the game does require a few fights against multiple opponents. But, you soon become comfortable after you learn the moves and what to watcch out for. Overall, decent combat. Not great, not really memorable. But it fixes the souls itch for awhile.

As for the sci-fi setting, the game is rather straightforward. The only problem here is everything is just given to you, instead of dark souls which only shows you a piece of the entire picture. That is to say, do not expect to have a dark souls like intrigue with this game. On a dissapointing note, the sidequests very rarely have satisfying conclusions.

But if you want to play a souls-like game, and like sci-fi, it is worth a try.\t\t\t\t\t
