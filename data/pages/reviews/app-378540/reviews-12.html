\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttldr - This is a souls game, but lacks innovation and just mostly copies the same formula. Overall the combat is not satisfying and feels clunky, enemies and bosses do not feel rewarding to defeat, and although the limb targeting mechanic is cool it's not enough to make up for the various shortcomings.
For example, trash enemies on the map are either way too easy or way too hard, nothing in between to allow you to feel like your char is growing as you progress. The first real boss was beatable but frustrating, and instead of being impactful and raising expectations for the rest of the game, it lowered them since it was janky and poorly designed. I honestly wish i could get back the 6hrs I spent playing.


Full review:
I played this for the first time in Dec 2022, after dumping 400 hrs into Elden Ring, but I tempered my expectations since I knew this is not a FromSoft game and that it's from 2017.
Having said that, this very much follows the souls formula - get souls, use them to upgrade yourself, if you die, you go pick up your dropped souls, etc.
The concept of targeting enemy body parts for collect gear, and the various gearsets and customization is very cool and I liked that part alot, but that's really the only good thing here.

There are dozens of small gripes about the game that I overlooked as I played, they took away from the enjoyment but not so much to make me stop.
1- poor enemy scaling - starter enemies are slow and too easy, then the next tier of enemies are so intense and they one-shot you, there's nothing in-between, and that's with upgrading gear as I should
2- The edges of ground hazards like acid puddles so poorly defined, it's hard to tell where is safe and not until you actually take dmg
3- Some of the weapon movesets are just bizarre and dont seem to have combat versatility in mind, especially the slow weapons heavy weapons, it's fine to have them be big and slow but at least give the player one quick low dmg jab with the butt of the weapon to deal impact dmg and create openings and such. The overall combat just feels very basic and shallow.
4- Animation to raise your weapon for blocks is way too slow, especially with heavy weapons. When you do block, it easy so much stamina it's not even worth following up with an attack. Blocking seems pointless early game against the very strong enemies, which is a bad thing to teach the player imo. If you don't want the player to block, then dont have a block button, if you do want them to block, at least make it usable from the start of the game for the harder fights otherwise you're wasting a keybind and training the player to ignore one of the defensive mechanics in the combat system that is already leaving more to be desired.
5+- Many others small things but you get the picture by now - so lets talk about why i really stopped...

The first real boss fight against the big security mecha that looks like it's from Armored Core - this fight was just a bad experience, and is was really deflated the expectations I had for the rest of the game.
- Hit detection on the aoe stomp was bizarre, sometimes hitting me when I was not there and sometimes not hitting when I was.
- If you chose the agile field technician rig instead of the heavy rig, then apparently that means you have low poise and each time you get hit by the boss you get stunned so long that the boss will literally just combo you to death if you're in front of it. This even with full HP upgrades to raise max HP
- The mortar/missiles the boss fires, if they hit you'll be sent flying in weird directions, half of the time i was sent flying towards the impact instead of away -- felt really janky
- And the biggest let down of all - when you dmg the boss enough, it will kneel down into a vulnerable state so you can do real dmg to it's HP, however this just amounts to you doing 1 big combo and then you literally stand there staring at the boss while you wait for your stamina to regen for the other half of the window, then the boss would stand up before you can attack again - it is the most anticlimactic thing i've ever seen in a game. This was a huge missed opportunity by the devs, they should've done this differently, like have your char jump up onto the robot in a mini-cutscene where you mash the button to do dmg, then when the boss recovers from it's overheat it just shakes you off and you continue the fight. That would've been much more impactful and allow the player to get hyped for the rest of the match and even overlook all the other bad combat elements of the fight.
Overall it took about 10 attempts to beat the first boss, more than half of the time was spent fighting my way back to the boss which was not fun, I really wish they had a checkpoint nearby but whatever, let's copy everything from Souls formula, even the long run of shame after each boss death.

I played for another 30mins after the boss and then realized I was literally having zero fun with the game, the only good point was when i was in the medbay upgrading gear and understood the gear system. Overall the combat is not satisfying and feels clunky, enemies and bosses do not feel rewarding defeat, and although the limb targeting mechanic is cool it's not enough to make up for all the shortcomings.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t+ Unique limb-mechanic is a fun scavenger hunt for new equipment; makes finding new enemies enjoyable.

+ Combat is fast and smooth, with enjoyable weapon animations that have a solid hit behind them. Great finishers as well.

+ Each weapon type has an incredible variety of different chain moves that are fun to discover and utilize.

+ Implant system gives a lot of choice and variety to your build, and has quite a large variety (though I can't speak to the usefulness of many choices). Feels like a souls ring system expanded to be the main aspect of build customization.

+ Changing implants on the fly, easily acquirable materials for weapon and body upgrades, and weapon-type based bonuses that are easy to level make for a fluid change between builds and encourages experimentation.

+ I was worried about the story going in, it screamed US Michael Bay-style action movie, but came out with my expectations devoured. Strong character writing, presentation, and a consuming plot all are well done and feel a good tad closer to actual souls storytelling than I expected, but without abstract writing or certain soul-internal concepts.

+ Level design blew me away; while most of the game is inspired by Dark Souls 2, even development starting nearly after 2's release, the wrapping structure and backdoor-metroidvania take heavily from Dark Souls 1. The first time I found a shortcut right back to the "bonfire" equivalent, I was blown away, as was I every time thereafter.


~ Full gear sets reward the player with unique stat bonuses while wearing all of the gear. I have never enjoyed mechanics like these, as I feel it messes up both variety and character customization. However, certain global set pieces found in the DLCs lessen this issue slightly, and the popularity of these mechanics in jrpgs and the like lead to believe many enjoy this, so may not be a negative for some.

~ Completely lacks online play, co-op or pvp or otherwise. Not entirely negative, as build variety wouldn't really be great enough to encourage this anyway, and the game feels cinematic in a certain way where it would be fairly odd. Still, it's missing presence can be felt at times, and would have contributed to some degree of further play.

~ Since many of the generic enemies wield the same weapon types as you, as that is where you get those weapons from, they do use the same movesets as you as well. This means there's a sort of a closeness between you and them combat-wise which can feel jarring, but it does lead to a nice feeling of knowing exactly what they're capable of doing, even if you are not entirely familiar with them.


- Weapon variety is low, considering the difference between the majority of the weapons of the same type is just stats and appearance.

- Implant system and upgrades are essentially the only form of level and power progression; a complete lack of soulslike jrpg-inspired stats. Despite the addition of implants, builds tend to feel linear, and experimentation is not as wide as could be.

- Along with the lack of stats is a lack of magic or other secondary means of combat. Greatly contributes to linearity.

- Some level of lesser variety in enemies exists, and becomes more apparent the further in you go. Different enemy types are dropped in occasionally to spruce it up, but you can often tell when you've fought the same enemy so many times.

- Certain bugs exist mechanically, and others exist that can block you out of certain character questlines entirely (or this may have just been an odd choice related to character dialogues, but that would be a negative as well). However, these are not incredibly prevalent.

- Because of prior issues mentioned with variety in build, the replayability takes a great detriment. I did many different runs through my time with each souls game, but the difference in my hours between the games speak for themselves.



Despite some issues I mentioned that are directly tied to the positives, this game is more than worth your time and money. I didn't even mention the money above, but this game is an absolute and complete bargain at only $15 (though, as I mentioned, the DLC is well worth your time, but is some more on top). Absolutely recommended for fans of soulslikes who can handle some level of jank, but may want to reconsider if you're a particular fan of magic or other similar builds.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother game from Deck 13, with all the Deck 13 jank attached.

Hitting through enemies, heals not activating, being pushed off edges by enemies, 1-hit or 1 stun 1 hit kill attacks, map is hard to navigate with no level direction conveyance whatsoever.

The combat, loot and upgrade systems are all fun, so they got the main gameplay loop right. Unfortunately its just accompanied by a boring OST, awful story, lifeless characters and cut & paste scenery. The ending of the game is pretty bad as well.

The bosses are awful with the exception of the last boss in Creo World. So many 1-hit kill attacks, unfair mechanics and inconsistencies make them a chore rather than challenging.

I enjoyed most of my time with this game, but the issues above make it so I can't really recommend it to anyone.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUmm, i don't really know why when there is a Souls-like game, most of them failed to capture what makes From Software's games truly special despite it's hard difficulty.
Games like Dark Souls, Bloodborne, Sekiro, and Elden Ring, all truly special in rewarding the players with equipments, perks, treasure, new area to explore and else, while this game aren't.
The world design, the theme, the enemy design, the enemy move set are all boring.
I know it takes a truly mastercraft to build a world like From Soft has with all the amazing lore of what happened and the mysteries surrounding it.
Only a few manages to be special in the Souls-like genre, this one aren't to be sure.
I won't give a thumb down so be thankful.
6/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first 5 hours were confusing:
-You discover you have neat 4 hit combos but for some of the weapons each attack in the combo has a different duration for the windup and lasts for a different amount of time which makes it hard to time
-You're frustrated because even popcorn looking enemies will require you to spend your entire stamina bar attacking then wait for it to recharge and then spend all of it again to kill them
-Enemies will sometimes cancel their attack animations and will really trip you up (and it doesn't look like a feautre)
-The environment is dark and targeting enemy limbs and hitting them makes them glow (at least you can turn it off) which makes the overall readability terrible and you can barely even see what moves they're doing
-You need to enter a tunnel that is pitch black and if you have the Lynx body armor then the flashlight will be so worthless it makes the area unplayable, you practically need to get the Rhino body armor to even get out of the first area but there's only one enemy that drops it
-Many enemies have moves that are very unintuitive and it feels way more viable to just poise break them with heavy weapons because you really don't get the souls-like satisfaction from learning their move sets (ESPECIALLY the 1st mini boss)

After you adapt to the crap factor, level up, get better gear and find a weapon type that feels more consistent:
-It's a "where the do i go" type of game, they tried to make it easier to navigate with signs and big arrows on the floors but it's still unclear a lot of the time where you're ultimately supposed to go to progress
-All the areas look too similar and are so unmemorable which makes it even it harder to navigate despite all of them having different names. When an NPC tells you to go to a certain place, there is never a "oh that place" moment
-I think there was only like 5 bosses in the whole game (1 of which was just a regular enemy with more health and some extra attacks), 6 with the DLC
-Bosses will often spam the same attack many times in a roll because they have very limited and unimpressive move sets
-I don't think i completed a single NPC's quest (and not for a lack of trying) and it really didn't even feel worth looking up
-Many of the audio tape collectibles are incredibly boring and don't even feel like they add anything to the lore or world building (although there are some that do)
-There's switches in the environment that say they do things to the level but it's not clear at all what their effects are
-Many quality of life features and polish is missing, like sorting weapons by weapon type and swapping implants without having to unequip each one first

Overall, I only kept playing to get my money's worth, especially during the last 1/3 of the game i was just waiting for it to be over\t\t\t\t\t
