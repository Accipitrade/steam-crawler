If you are a casual gamer, stay away.
If you don't like the Souls games, stay away.
If you need a map, a waypoint and a primary/secondary objective description to be happy, stay away.

I'm just saying because:
- the game is hard as f****
- the atmosphere is depressing, you're mostly alone or occasionally talking to sad/crazy/holographic people
- the level layout is CONFUSING, I don't remember being stuck like this and walking around in circles in any other game I've played, including all of the souls games ... and I played my first video game when Ronald Reagan was president.
- the game tells you jack sh't and most of the time you have to guess where you're supposed to go.

But, if you don't mind things like that you'll be extremely satisfied because:

-it's a pretty fresh setting (anything else would be spoilers)
-the upgrade system is decent and lets you develop different playstyles
-every killed enemy means getting a little better
-every shortcut you unlock results in a huge discharge of endorphins
-the game isn't that long and the second playthrough will be more pleasant because you have a rough idea where to go
-and second playthrough opens up a whole new set of upgrades for your weapons and armor\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played 30 hours of the game to its conclusion and tried to do as much of the side quests as I could. Here's what I think.

This is a bad game. The only reason I played it to completion was because this game is so frustrating, janky and overall broken that I refused to let it beat me.

To begin, hitboxes in this game make absolutely no sense. There are times where you attack a target at the same time they attack you, only to see your attack go through their model and "miss" while theirs connects successfully. There are other times when you should be invincible while dodging (I-frames) yet the game ignores this and hits you anyway.

Second, are the weapons. Many of the weapons have a string of attacks depending on how many times you hit the attack button, standard fare. The issue comes with many weapons having a longer string of attacks than expected. For example, the game's "heavy weapon" known as "single rigged" weapons have an incredibly long wind up and take quite a bit of stamina. You are locked in this very long (for a souls-like game) animation which leaves you wide open for characters of equal attack speed to hit you. If you press the attack button once more after your first attack you are now locked into a follow up 2 hit combo that you cannot cancel. This has killed me more than it has helped me.

If the issue was only present for the single rigged weapons, it would be fine. However, I also tried what the game considers the "dual daggers" of the game, or "twin rigged" weapons. They hit fast but they do not hit hard. In order to be effective, multiple hits in the same chain are required. However, this also locks you into many combo animations and since these weapons have very little stagger power, the enemy can just power through the hits and hit you...hard.

This isn't the end, however. You, the player, are also gimped from the start and right through most of the game. There were many times where I had a powerful weapon that landed a 3 hit combo only to realize I've only taken a fourth of the target's health. On the other hand, even the fast hitting/low damage enemies were two, three-shotting my character.

This is made far worse by the targetting system this game has. There is no "free targeting" as the auto-target system immediately locks on when you first attack an enemy. This gets worse if there is more than one target. On top of that, the camera, when locking on breaks itself should the target sidestep past you, dodge or move quickly.

Then, there's the bosses. Good lord this is the part I believe was never playtested. The difficulty of each successive boss vastly shifts. First boss, P.A.X is actually a good way to test your skills as it challenges you based on everything you've learned up to that point. Second boss, however, Firebug is more frustrating than fun due to its incoherent, random AI and hitbox (as explained before).

Third boss, Big Sister is a giant assembly plant that gets easy once you realize you need to un-target each limb on each phase, everytime you attack. That way, you can see what's around you without the camera having a seizure. The fourth boss, The Black Cerberus is just a dude. Nothing hard about it, nothing interesting about it, it is the Wood Man of The Surge. And finally, the fifth and final boss (that's right, only five) Rogue Process. The boss has two phases. The first one is punishingly difficult while the second one is a joke. The difficulty shifts between bosses is so large it makes a terrible experience.

And lastly, there are the quests. I was not able to finish one of them due to a bug in the game which did not drop the item from a mob that I needed to complete it. I spent a good amount of time resetting the game, killing the speicific mob, even making sure I was doing it right by checking forums and youtube videos of the quest. Another quest had me bringing an drug to another NPC which, later, was supposed to activate an event but never did.

To sum up. Don't buy this game. Deck13 had a great idea that is horribly executed. Their second attempt at a "souls-like" game is better than their first, Lords Of The Fallen. Sadly, this feels like two steps forward, one step back. Maybe in the future, their next attempt will be a fair, and enjoyable one. And if you need another reason, not to get it. This game is so bad, it compelled me to write my second review on Steam, ever.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to start off by saying that this game is amazing.

A lot of people are comparing it to Dark Souls, which I can understand. However, it's also much more than 'Dark Souls in a Sci-fi setting'. The game has a bigger emphesis on the 'trash' mobs. There are only 4 bosses in the entire campaign. This is not a bad thing. The 'trash' mobs are in themselves quite challenging and can be quite relentless. Add on top of that a constantly changing area.
There are technically only 4 or 5 areas. The starting area is fairly straightforward. The next area you get to, is where I spent the majority of my time. It's very big with lots of shortcuts back to this game's equivilant of a campfire: The Ops. This particular level changes a lot though. It feels dynamic and the game as a whole keeps throwing new challenges at you, even in areas you thought you knew.
Add on top of that a progression system, which encourages exploring. The developers chose a Castevania aproach for quite a few of the 'secrets' in this game as well. Backtracking is essential, if you want to get all the loot the game has to offer. There are also conventional secrets that you can get to by either jumping, having a keen eye or just stumbling upon them. I have yet to find any ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ 'hit a wall and it disappears' secrets.

The combat is great. You can use both mouse & keyborad and a controller. I haven't tried a controller personally, but I know it can be done. The mouse & keyborad controls are excellent, and every key is rebindable! I've also been able to run the game at a full 60 fps, if not higher, for the entire game.

tldr;
This is a great game. It reminds me a lot of Dark Souls, but it also diverges away from it in key areas. It's not a clone, but it's got clear inspiration from Dark Souls. I personally liked this game enough to complete it in 3 days. The campaign is about 30 hours, I did a lot of looting, but I didn't find all the secrets. Moreover, there's a new game+ mode.
Buy this game if you're at all interested in the premise. It's bloody great, even if it is a bit pricey.

EDIT:
I've now played a bit further in NG+. I feel like the game doesn't scale well in NG+ and becomes way too easy. I have just beaten the third boss. It took me 3 hours to get to this point in NG+, where in the first run-through I spent 20 hours doing the same.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never played any Dark Souls game series, but I did enjoy myself with The Surge despite having limited time to play.

Pros:
  • Body part selection for chopping is fantastic!
  • Dodging enemy attacks is not easy, but rewarding when done right.
  • Accomplishing certain requirement will reward an unique upgraded item.

Cons:
  • Steep combat learning curve.
    The combat is highly punishing for any mistake made. Take a while to learn how to attack, dodge, or parry. Done right, you faceroll enemies quickly. Miss a swing, or forgot to manage your energy, you will either be instant killed or left with barely any health remaining while knocked to the ground. If they take a swift attack at you while you are getting up, you will be watching a slow death moment.
  • No ranged weapon
    Questionable concept of having no ranged combat, yet enemies have an option for firing laser beams at you until they either get destoryed or undock their weapon, and come square up to you in a melee combat.

Game is a little grindy for me, however I do understand there are people who love to grind at a game. If you look forward to a grind, and a combat challenge with an option to chop body parts, do give The Surge a try!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter seeing a few first impression videos I thought the game looked promising. I was looking for something similar to the soul series and its also clear that it was something that the developers where set out to do.

The game functions the same way in its core, but a lot clunkier. Attack seems to always connect if they are thrown in the targets general direction and you are also unable to pass through the hitbox of an other enemy.
But seeing as this game was made on a lower budget, you have to expect a lower level of combat.

My issues with the game are it's core design choices.

- Weapon proficiency, Who thought it was a good idea to add XP bars to your weapon? The souls game's have always been about experimenting. You're not going to start leveling up a new weapon mid game, grinding and gimping yourself for hours just to see if you like a new thing.

- Impact, poise damage is now amplified by the heavier armor you wear, meaning that you are forced to move slowly if you want to hit hard, again less choice to the player.

- Farming components, if you want to upgrade or create a new armor set, you need cut of the corresponding part of the enemy, how ever you wont get materials from hitting the enemies unarmored weak spots. So the game goes out of its way to encourage you to play smart, but then wont reward with any of the materials you need to progress in the game for doing so. Meaning that upgrading your gear is a slow slug fest.

- Lots of back tracking with no fast travel feature.

- No recall feature, the game actively goes out of its way to scum bag you out of losing your "souls", it will make you jump in to holes, it will lock doors behind you and with all this they had the decency to put your "souls" on a timer, so if you're not fast enough they are gone for good.

The game has a high degree of artificial difficulty, that it's so unfair, unpredictable and with a very slow grind.\t\t\t\t\t
