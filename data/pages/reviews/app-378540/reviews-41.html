\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinally finished it.

It is a shame, but this is definitely the worst souls-like game I played so far. It is a shame, because there are few things this game does better than Surge 2. For example, heavy-duty weapons are better in this game (better animations), and playing without stat points (like in second surge) is more interesting.

Anyway..

Playing Surge 1 feels like somebody tried to make a Dark Souls clone set in future with cyborgs in it, except for some reason the decided to replicate only the worst and the most annoying features instead. They succeeded, and they created a game which successfully implements every bad, awful or just annoying mechanic from souls series.

Here's list of complaints/issues with this game:
  • Ton of animations lock you in place mid-move, leaving you vulnerable to attack.
  • Those animations also feature complex animation driven movement, which will make you step into different directions.
  • Except the game will also happily place you on a narrow ledges blocked by enemies, where using those animations will make you fall.
  • In addition to the ledges, the game will strategically place nigh-invisible holes in the floor between you and position of the enemy, so if you attack the enemy you'll fall to death.
  • Finisher animations can teleport you in air, and guess what... can also make you fall to your death.
  • On top of that animation control is imprecise, for example, a dash can be followed by two or three different attacks, and in case of heavy weapons it can be a "poke", a lengthy spin or an uppercut. Those controls will generally work fine, until you fight a boss, in which case, the game will start consistently pick the animation that puts you in danger. For example, if you want t perform a "poke", it'll perform a spin instead, which will lock you in place for a couple of seconds so the boss has more opportunities to pancake you.
  • The first ever boss in the game is unkillable unless you use specific mechanic which is not explained. Actually I'm not certain how you're supposed to figure this out without a walkthrough or youtube video. I guess you'd get stuck
  • Because it is a factory, all environment will look the same. There are dozens of completely identical-looking corridors. Exploring this is not fun.
  • In addition to being bland, environments are constructed as a completely artificial 3d hamster maze reminding me of old 8 and 16 bit arcade games. This does not look like an actual factory, but some sort of obstacle course/maze. Expect to get lost, a lot. Did I mention there's no map?
  • By the way, the game tried to implement "Dark" part of the Dark Souls, and took its mission seriously. There are ton of places in the game which are completely pitch black, and you can't see a thing in there, despite your suit having pretty much military-grade projector built in. The puny light of your suit dissipates in just 2 or 3 meters, and doesn't light anything. So you see pitch blackness and glow of your lamps in the middle, which creates halos, but doesn't light the level. You also need to fight enemies in this environment, hope you find fighting using targeting markers fun.
    https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=2261041855
  • Certain enemies can shoot you through the walls. If you're lucky, that will be simply annoying, if you're not, they'll interrupt some sort of attacks and probably make you fall to your death.
  • Certain enemies are bugged and are completely immortal. There is supposed to be a prompt to kill them, but it almost never arrives, and there are locations swarmed by those guys. They can also kill you in one or two hits.
  • Certain boss has the worst camera/targeting mechanic in history of souls-likes, and on top of that glitchy stage. So and most of the fight, rather than fighting the boss, you'll be fighting the camera, which will look into walls, into objects that block you views, away from your character, at enemies you DON't want to target and in general will try to ensure that you can't see the part of the boss you really want to hit (I never managed to target it, by the way). On occasion the game also makes you target parts that would put you into kill zone, this is done automatically, typically one millisecond after you start a swing animation at the boss, so you'll turn around, take 3 steps into kill zone and get pancaked. It is horrible. Right, this boss also has parts of the stage you can fall through, even though they look like you should be able to walk on them.
  • There is at least one glitched quest, which I couldn't finish, because an NPC became non-interactive. If you catch this glitch, you can't fix it, the NPC will remain stuck.
  • As you progress further and further, despite increasing your damage output and power, it feels like you're getting weaker and weaker as the game goes, because enemies scale faster than you are. So if in the beginning you could die in 3 hits, at the end you'll sometimes die in one, despite having more armor, more health and stronger weapon. Kinda kills the fun of dark souls where you could revisit an old enemy which gave you a lot of grief, and realize that now you can go toe to toe with them or dominate them.
  • At every spot where you could possibly be ambushed, you will be ambushed. Somebody made a point to utilize every possible gank point in the game, placing enemies meticulously at every single one of them. This gets annoying.

Short version:

Basically it feels as if you were playing Dark souls, except the only location was blight town, the only enemies were black knights and silver archers, it was lit like tomb of the giants, you had no light source and only one campfire, no magic, no bows and only one weapons which occasionally would stun you in place so enemies would kill you faster, plus the camera would make a point to star at the ceiling when you fighting a boss.

It is a pity. As I said, there are few things which are done better in this game compared to Surge 2, namely, heavy weapons here have good animation set, which doesn't look as awkward as the one in second game, and your stats are comletely determined by your implants. You have no stat points. It is a clever idea.

But the bad sides of the game outweighs the few positive points it offers. Which is a pity.

I'd recommend to skip and not play this one. It is just bad.\t\t\t\t\t
