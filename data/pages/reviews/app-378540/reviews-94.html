\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is an action RPG with intricate level design, borrowing a lot of insperation from dark souls but with a sifi twist.

The plot is mostly a vague, hazy background to the rest of the game. All you can be certain of is that CREO, the company who owns the facility the entire game takes place in, is up to some shady behaviour. Warren, the player character, decides to join them anyway because of certain reasons I wont reveal, with him applying for a job that'll allow him to wear an exo suit, a robotic frame grafted to a person that allows them to perform super human feats. However, he isn't given a seditive during the graft operation and experiences such pain and trauma that he blacks out. Next thing you know you're in a dump and the game begins.

The gameplay is so close to being good, but just falls short. The combat is very dark souls-esque, with a decent portion of it being devoted to one on one duels, though this frequently blossoms out into one on two or sometimes three, if the game is feeling mean. There are five weapon types in all, one type is slow and high damage, another is fast and light damage, etc. I spent the majority of my playthrough using a single handed weapon because I found it to be the best trade-off in terms of power and speed. The other reason I used it is because every other weapon type, aside from the twin rigged weapons, have massive wind up times for attacks, leaving you vunerable. I also found that I could kill enemies faster and more reliably with the lighter weapons as well, especially if I got the first hit off. Your attacks take stamina to perform by the way, same goes for your dodges, your blocks and your sprinting, it recharges at a pretty fast rate, but can decrease just as fast if you're too liberal with your moves.

The actual controls are buttery smooth, there was never a death i felt was caused by poor controls. The targetting system itself is also really good for one on one combat, allowing you to target specific body parts on the enemy (targetting an unarmoured bodypart to deal extra damage, or an armoured one to weaken it and chop it off to get loot). Even jumping in this game felt pretty solid, especially with the way Warren can stop on a dime. The only time I didn't feel in control was when i swung one of the heavier weapons, at which point I lost control and had to wait a good few seconds for the animation to cycle through.

This would all be fine if it weren't for three things, We'll start with health. player health starts off small, and it doesn't increase unless you install modules into your EXO suit, at which point it gets boosted and does get a small increase based on your current 'level'. Keep in mind that you also use these same slots for your healing items, and your other special upgrades.

To make matters a bit more frustrating is just how quickly you're health can be removed. Even after getting a suit as upgraded as I could possibly get it during the third stage of the game, I was still being dropped in two or three hits unless I spammed my healing injections. Enemies will pound you relentlessly with their attacks, and you have no I frames between when you get hit and when you have a chance to recover, meaning it's entirely possible to lose all your health in under a second because of some cheap enemy AI tactic.

Lastly, the enemy placement is just god awful half the time. You'll be strolling along, minding your own business when suddenly you get smashed in the back of the head with a hammer from out of nowhere and lose half your health. This isn't limited to single, isolated incidents, this is a very frequent occurence, often times unavoidable, you'll be lucky to get through such an encounter even when you know it's coming. The games combat isn't designed for multiple enemies either, with the lock on mechanic having no way (as far as I'm aware of) to switch between targets unless you unlock fron enemy one, then hope it locks on to enemy 2 when you press the button again. Even then, the attacks most weapons do aren't AoE enough to hit multiple enemies at once, except for the staff class of weapons. With the small amount of health you have, and the sheer amount of damage they can deal, you're better off trying to split the enemies up, or hope you can kill one before the other starts swinging at you.

With all that said, the actual level design is, for the most part, fantastic. The visuals alone are wonderfully detailed, the sifi design of the interior of each of the various levels isn't entirely distinct from one another, but it's all very nice to look at. On top of that, the levels themselves have lots of intricate loops to them, with a lot of hidden passages linking back to the 'hub' area of each level that you never would have noticed on your first pass through. It all links together really well, with one level even having a spin on things and forcing you to move the hub around on an elevator up and down the level. It's really good design, it's downright impressive, with it being marred by some poor areas, especially in regards to how enemies are placed and how the levels were designed to allow them to hide in such awfully unfair places.

The way you get stronger is also pretty solid (if we ignore health). Instead of hoping for random drops, you can cut off the bits you want from enemies in combat, take them back to base, and craft them using the games currency and some materials you can also hack off of enemies. if you want an optical sensor, decapitate an enemy, if you want an arm component, slice off the enemies arm. Etc. It's a very good farming system and provides a tactical layer to combat, giving you a reason to hit those armoured areas on the enemies body. You can use the games currency to boost your level as well, there's no stats though, you're level just decides how much you can equip at any given moment, effects certain implants (namely health) and you need to be a certain level to activate certain panels, just nothing on the main path though, the level required areas are optional.

Back to the design of everything, the characters themselves look great, especially warren. The way his face is designed just really looks nice, as does the various exosuits you'll aquire over the course of the game. Most of the enemies are humans who are also wearing exosuits, so it applies there as well. The robot enemies tend to either look great (like the firebug boss), or be middle of the line above average, nothing looks truly bad until much later in the game when some of the final area enemies appear. The music is also pretty bland, it's all background noise, nothing truly stand out to my memory except the 'hub' area music, if only because it plays on repeat constantly and it's a country song.

This game really is a mixed bag, it's not as good as dark souls in terms of combat, but it looks way better than it, and there are rare moments when the combat mechanics align in just the right way to create a fantasticly tense situation that isn't ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, like the firebug boss, or with solo fights against some of the tougher humanoid enemies. It has poor enemy placement and not the best health and leveling system, yet it can still work at times. Honestly, if only one of the flaws i mentioned were fixed, I think The Surge would be much better and be well deserving of everyones time and attention.

Overall, I enjoyed my time with it, despite the massive frustrations I also suffered because of it. It isn't perfect, and I wont pretend it is, but I do recommend people giving it a look if they like dark souls, or various titles like it. You'll need patience and tolerance to endure through the bad parts to get to the good stuff.\t\t\t\t\t

I was interested in it when it popped up during a sale and picked it up, and I was blown away by this game! It has an interesting story, amazing graphics, a new take on the "souls like" combat system as it introduces the limb system, and horizontal/vertical attacks. The music when it comes up is well picked too, although most of the game is silent when it comes to music (something that I enjoy personally, but others may not). This is particularly the case with the choice of Prisoner by Stumfol being ued in Ops. The only cons I can think of in regards to the surge are the shortness of it - I could have and would have spent more time in it, but the game was pretty short once you really get used to the hostiles in it. But that being said, I currently have 69 hours in it, and still haven't hit up the second DLC, so who knows? Might be another 5 hours or so there~ ^^

TL;DR - More pros than cons, a definite must play! ^^\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a game what a game. The Surge is one of my all time favorite games. If you are a fan of Dark Souls this is a game you should play. It is souls like but still retains its own identity. The limb hacking never gets old and feels so satisfying, especially when getting new gear. The level design is on par with Dark Souls. You will find yourself being wowed with how areas connect back together with shortcuts. Bosses are also fun and feel quite different from one another. I didn't play through the DLC on my first playthrough but I look forward to it on my second.

I love how the story is presented in this game. It reminds me a lot of how DOOM 3's story was given with audio logs and messages. I feel this is a great way to give a story and stand apart from Dark Souls. The exploration and story go hand in hand and its great.

The only thing about this game that is a little negative is possibly the variety of enemies. 80% of the game you will be fighting people in exosuits with different weapons and armor. While this does give enemies a different feel based on what they have equiped it still is a little sad. Honestly I can let it mostly pass given the games location and story. Also there are only 5 bosses in the main game. I guess the dlc adds some more but even still I wish there were more in the main game itself.

Overall I absoletly love this game and it is one of my favorites of all time. Definitly worth checking out. So get out there and sever some bad guys limbs already.\t\t\t\t\t
