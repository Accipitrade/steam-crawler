\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I like some of the gameplay ideas of this game (like collecting weapons and exosuit parts directly from enemies, piece by piece), the controls and the whole fighting system is not good at all. The enemies that I've encountered before 1st boss are generally easy (especially after souls), but the very first boss shows everything that is wrong with the combat system by multiplying it. The controls are not good, the leashing is a thing, unfairly looking hits (from the animation point of view) are a thing, there is no real evasion (just some sort of a dash move), e.t.c.

Plus, I really don't get the idea of "weapon proficiency" and timed scrap - local analogue of souls. If you die and lose scrap, you have only 2:30 to go and get it back. Of course, you get about 15-ish seconds with every killed mob, but it's bad mechanics anyway, imho.

I've heard that the second part of Surge is far better, in terms of gameplay, so if you really want to try this setting, it probably would be better to pick up the second part.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first played it in 2017 I only got through 2 bosses and something else must have caught my attention. It always itched at me though, and with The Surge 2 out I've come back to finish this one first. I'm very glad I did.

PROS:
* Fun, smooth combat with great animations
* Bright, colorful visuals are a breath of fresh air for the Souls-like genre
* Executions and dismemberings haven't gotten old
* Trying out all the different weapon types doesn't set you back tooooo much. And they're all fun.

CONS:
* Hidden items are annoying. If you see (or hear) one, you don't know whether you should look around for a way to get it, or if it'll only be possible to get much later in the game.
* "Hardcore" Boss kills. The game doesn't tell you about them up front, so by the time you know about them it's probably too late and you've already missed out on one of the best weapons in the game.
* You encounter a lot of things that you won't be able to open until much later in the game, and coming back to them seems to be optional. If you hate FOMO and the idea of missing out on gear, you basically have to take notes as you play and refer back to them later

Play with a controller, if you have one!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI discovered this game mostly by accident. Was looking for something with futuristic tech feel to it so I decided to try The Surge. Only way later I discovered what kind of game this is.

I'm not a fan of this particular genre where each enemy encounter is at the very least small challenge. But I must admit that I liked the game pretty early on. The graphics were great too and I was interested in the story.

Boss battles and some enemies confused me a bit because I couldn't find any hints how to kill them effectively without loosing ton of health so I had to look it up on the net. It would be probably better if the game gave you some suggestions of the weak points of effective strategies maybe in some Codex or by other means.

The game became more monotonous just before the ending. I even gave it up for a long time before I return to finish it. It's probably just because it's not really my kind of the game.

Still I could recommend the game even though I doubt I'll be playing the sequel.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo begin with, I hate souls-like games and Dark Souls in particular, The Surge should not be compared with Dark Souls.

The Surge is very different. It's not a sci-fi clone of DS. Devs were inspired by some of the concepts from DS and decided to improve on every aspect of them, the most significant once are setting, UI, upgrades and core game rhythm and dynamics.

It was cheap on sale, I liked the sci-fi concept and decided to give it a try and it turned out I am really enjoying it, very cool multifaceted game, but clean and simple in it's design.

Most of the variety and depth comes from so many visceral patterns, their memorization and usage in different game situations. It feels very dynamic in it's control, as if something is flowing through you: wooom, wooom, bam, bam, bam, shoom at 70-90 BPM and you are drifting through this syncopated game beats. No matter if I die again and loos my scrap, the beat is so mellow and nice, that I am playing just for the sake of it.

What makes this game interesting and enjoyable for me:

1) Lots of interesting innovative gameplay twists, like targeting and scavenging different body parts, cool finishers, interesting and very well designed upgrade system.

2) Combat is very satisfying and visceral, camera works well. Everything flows smoothly, not like in Dark Souls.

3) Techy style, exosuite, sci-fi setting, game intro and premise.

4) Very clean game design overall, very tight game system with no bullsht

5) When you die and loose resources, it does not feels that punishing, cause the game feels good and you go grind again, sometimes it even feels like a small meta-quest to retrieve scrape from the tight spot.

6) UI design and it's UX are top notch, straight forward and clean.

Really recommended for people, who are not into souls-like, but actually want to experience and understand this overhyped genre.\t\t\t\t\t
