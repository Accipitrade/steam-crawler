**Warning** Do not buy this game based on this review unless you have, or are willing to buy a Steam controller, Xbox One controller, or PS4 controller for your PC. This is a highly action-oriented and skill-based game that requires a mastery of timing and synergistic button-pressing.

Ok, now the real review:

Forget about Lords of the Fallen and Dark Souls and Nioh and put all those out of your mind. You need scrap to level up. You lose it if you die. You have to run back for it before dying again. AND the similarities end there. Play for five minutes and youl realize this game has it's own heart and soul. In fact, if you're looking for something to scratch the Souls itch you might be disappointed in a way. So just let this title stand on it's own two servo-manipulators.

This game does not 'want to be' or 'try to be' any other game. My hat goes off to the developer for giving us a really unique, challenging, and skill-based game which will strongly appeal to science fiction fans.

Features I love:
1.) You can mix and match armor types
2.) ..But you get a bonus for wearing a complete set
3.) ..And you can upgrade your armor by finding components and using scraps
4.) (So far) there's no ranged weapons that the player can directly wield. It's all tools you pick up and 'convert' into melee weapons.
5.) Elemental damage consists of things that actually exist like Fire and Plasma, etc.
6.) It's difficult. If you don't use Google, you WILL die to the bosses the first time. At least the first couple of bosses...
7.) The trash mobs can kill you so fast. Unlike some games, you can't get sliced with a sword 35 times and live. You'll die in one or two hits unless you're moving or blocking.
8.) Stagger/stun is a thing.
9.) The game world/story matters and draws you in.
10.) The graphics are gorgeous.

Dev feedback:
1.) The level up curve (if that's a thing) from the first zone to the next is pretty extreme. So much so that I went back to the first area to just farm for a while before moving into the second very far. The difficulty is much higher in the second area. I went from mostly mastering the first area to being kicked around like a dirty ragdoll in the second. That's ok, I guess. It just felt like all my progress in the first part of the game was 'reset.' But the second area has alot going for it. So I'm not really calling this a 'con.' It's just how it is.
2.) There are button combos you can do in order to use 'move sets' for each weapon. But the game doesn't even give you the first clue about this. For example, double tapping RB1 then quicly tapping RB2 does a cool move set with some weapons. But you'd never know without experimeinting or finding out on accident. This isn't a con either. It's just something I'd tell new players - that they will have to experiment to figure out move sets.

Anyway, the game is money well spent if you like challenging and rewarding games. If you prefer casual games where you can just watch a story and press buttons every so often, you'll hate it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really tried to like this game, just as I tried to like Lords of the Fallen. I was excited about The Surge because I read reviews here stating that it was "like Bloodborne", to which I completely disagree. Certainly, as with LotF, there is heavy From Soft-inspiration in the combat presentation... but the feel is just terrible. The attacks feel clunky and overly-animated; the movement suffers from this as well. The entire combat feels proactive as opposed to reactive. There's nothing wrong with having combat that requires prediction - that's a form of tactics - but when 90% of the combat is based on proactivity, in a 3rd person action RPG? That's really boring. And it's really unlike Bloodborne in this aspect, because in BB, you are intended to react quickly with dodges, parries, and attacks.

Moreover, the difficulty when attempting to play this way is unreasonable. In order for something to be positively challenging, it needs to feel fair; it needs to feel like you are learning and getting better. With The Surge, it feels every other enemy kills you in one hit, so you have to resort to cheap tactics like dodging back, strong attacking once, dodging back, strong attacking once, etc. And that brings me to my second point: the AI is terrible. They're really easy to dupe into a lopping pattern. So the difficulty appears to only really come from their stats, which segues into my third point: they have way too much HP. I first tried using heavy weapons - which can kill enemies in a reasonable amount of time - but because the heavy weapons are so slow, it makes it very easy to be hit. And as I mentioned, a hit or two and you're dead. My second try, I played with light weapons. This made dodging and timing attacks easier, but they do so little damage that it increases the chances that you'll mess up and get hit in a fight, since the fights take that much longer. None of this would be an issue if the combat was actually fun, but it's not. It's just a boring monotonous grind that causes me to stop concentrating and mess up, because I'm so bored.

Overall, this feels like a very shallow combat-oriented game. The story has some interesting concepts, but even the plot points feel shoehorned in. If you love games like Dark Souls and Bloodborne for their combat, I don't believe you will like this. You MIGHT, as evidenced by some reviews here, but I honestly can't see why. If you do, great; I'm glad you found a game you can enjoy. Unfortunately, I didn't... despite my best efforts.\t\t\t\t\t
