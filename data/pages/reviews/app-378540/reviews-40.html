\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, yeah you could say this is a robotic Dark Souls and that would be pretty true, they share a lot a lot of similarities but The Surge has a lot of new twists and great ideas to add to the base blueprint of a "souls-like" game. There's a lot I like about The Surge, the combat feels super smooth and fluid and the ability to lock on and target certain body parts is a great feature that adds more complexity and depth to the combat.
There's a couple things that I really don't like though. Most of the enemies are so easy to kill, most of the time I basically just spammed RB and blitzed through 95% of enemies. The bosses also felt incredibly underwhelming besides the last boss. Every boss I essentially beat them within three or less attempts. I've beat every Dark Souls at least 4 times each so I feel like I have enough experience in these types of games and any time I went into a boss fight I was able to pick out every pattern they had super easily which made is super simple to get past most of the bosses. Once I knew the patterns it just seemed like the bosses almost gave up and let me beat them. Not to mention one of the FIVE bosses in the game was mostly a clone of the first boss in the game.
I really appreciate the map and everything it had to offer, there was a lot of depth and intricacies about it that made it so immersive. I found myself exploring the map a lot more than I might another game from this genre. The audio logs are really great too, building different stories away from the main plot and investing me in them. There's also quite a few AI characters spread out through the map though for the most part they do very little to add and expand the game, every AI I encountered was basically just another way to get an item.
I really enjoyed the progression system for the most part but it felt extremely easy to level up and become super OP which feeds into me running through most enemies.
For the most part I do recommend this game (I don't own the DLC so I can't speak for that) but don't go into it expecting there to be a lot of content when it comes to bosses. It has a pretty unique approach to the genre which is great considering how many games have attempted it. I'd recommend this game mainly to people who really enjoy exploring the map and the lore of the game and the world it creates because it does an amazing job when it comes to that.
I look forward to at some point playing through the DLC and the second game :)\t\t\t\t\t

The futuristic Dark Souls

Although It's common to compare these Souls clones to the originals, but I can't put them in the same league. No doubt Fromsoft is the benchmark, these should be compared to the other clones.
That being said, this game is above the average souls-like game, however, far behind the original magic of the true souls games.
Scraps are souls, there are NPC quests, several armors/weapons and few (very few) bosses among other similarities. And here come the problems:
- No co-op or PvP
- Farming materials is boring and slow, since you have to focus on a specific body part
- Only 5 bosses
- No fast travel (there is... something)
- Several weapons, but too many are almost the same
- I didn't enjoy the parrying system (and the lack of backstab)

Still, since it's not AAA game with a huge budget, the Devs did a good job overall. Any souls fan will like it, just keep in mind it's not from FromSoft.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge was a very ominous, freaky, disturbing yet fascinating all the way through. It is very gripping but frustrating experience for me but made all the more worth it when finally completing the game The sinister backstory that unfolds as you (the player named Warren) traverses this Dystopian future ironically called a Utopia by the organisation CREO whom idealises their product and the future of man kind only for things to turn south faster than you can say "UH OH."

The combat is the surge is very engaging. You always need to be on high alert in this game because it has more than its fair share of jump scares, similar to its likely inspiration Dark Souls However The Surge adds more to the Genre in the mechanic of limp and body targetisation. see a helmet, body or even a weapon that you think to yourself say i would love to have that. Well your in luck because at anytime you can sever the limps of your foes and have their gear as your own if your good enough to get it that is.

Now before getting into some of the aspects about this game that I dislike I want to go other some of the aspects about this game which stood out to me (WARNING SPOILERS AHEAD) starting with your quest with a woman named sally at the start of the game you think she is someone that needs to be be rescued whereas instead she is a actually part of CREO itself and her final encounter with you is in a relatively hidden room where shes dead or part of the machine im not quite sure what happened but its cool non the less. Aswell as the whole quest with Dr Barett. I find his whole project and research to be grotesquely frightening aswell as being very enjoyable and interesting to learn what happened from the start of his quest all the way to the end. the look of his experiments the (♥♥♥♥ Machinalis) remind me a lot of the monsters in dead space with is really cool to see. The Dr himself also turns into a Machinalis if you go back into his lab and kill him which is the kind of story telling that I love This also come with an achievement. Another scientist asks for your help to rescue is daughter until you realise she has been converted into a big machine very weird stuff I know. The various audio logs that you discover along your journey also give you a flavour of the world by listening to people talking to eachover or speeches about what you'd assume to have taken place in that area.

Now to point out some of the Negatives of this game. Some of the enemies in this game feel very janky to fight sometimes I feel that certain types of enemies are way too strong for a specific part of the game that your at. An example of which being one shot countless times in the span of an eyes blink. Instead of actually trying to find weakness in your opponant on your first try the game feels a lot and i mean A LOT! of trial and error. You face a new enemy die once or twice now you know how to defeat it and its too easy unless you get a bit lazy and get 1 shot again this is what the gameplay feels like to me while being fun and visually stimulating it does have some very annoying aspects. Bosses in this game look a lot like they are about to attack then they dont or they just stand there and you see your health bar get demolished out of the blue this isnt the only game that shares this aspect but it needs to be said by someone.

CONCLUSION I really love this game to the weapon variety to the satifying combos and executions, the mixture of Dead Space and Dark Souls makes for a very thrilling adventure. Ive always loved how you can take your favourite armour pieces from your enemies and wear it yourself to look like a badass. Then finally the Epic lore which can even rival Dark Souls and BloodBorne at times all an all a very good well made game\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOK this is very hard thumbs up from me. I have almost same amount of positive and negative stuff to say about The Surge. But it is the game and games are made to be fun and I had fun playing it.

Because this game has some real issues lets discuss them first:
- Some Boss fights are disproportionately hard (Thrust me I went thru DS 3 times, DS2 2 times, DS3 2 times, Lords of the fallen 1 time).
- Important game audio like what I just did cuts in the middle because I pick up audio log that is right next to point where "NPC" strats talking to me (you can play back that audio log any time, but words that "NPC" wanted to say will forever be lost).
- I like interconnected maps (like in DS) but when its done on interesting new ways. First 2 locations on the map you will see all tricks game has to intertwine its parts. It becomes boring same old, same old, and as game makes sections of the map more and more complicated it becomes really easy to get lost or not know where now. Lass and lass unique locations, and more and more stock halls, doors...
- In my case sometimes slow to respond controls (still better than DS)

AND now all the good stuff:
- It has nice and unique visuals.
- Fun to play thru story line.
- Amazing loot system.
- Fun and engaging combat system.
- Interesting LV up mechanics.
- exoskeleton visual upgrades are absolutely the best part, I had hours of fun just making the best looking combination (set bonuses king of kill that aspect... I would like if 3+2 gave you like some percentage of both sets, because at one point it just makes no sens to not use set bonus).
- all weapon types feel different.

All in all I had fun.
My price for this game would be around 10$

6/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
-Really cool concept. I love the world design and art style.
-Half-life style intro was nice.
-Concept behind attacking individual body parts is a nice touch. While it isn't the first time a game has explored this idea, it is nicely done here.
-I have a flight stick plugged into my PC along with a separate throttle with rudder pedals as well as a gamepad. The way everything is situated makes it a huge pain to unplug any of that stuff so when some games force one of those many axes to be used which causes my character to spin and an eventual config file edit to fix it, I get frustrated. All I want to use is mouse and keyboard. This game doesn't have that problem, and there isn't even a menu option to disable the gamepad. As soon as it detected I preferred mouse and keyboard it just rolled with it. Respect
-Game runs perfectly smooth. No problems with 21:9 and there wasn't a single hitch in muh giddy-up at any point.

Cons:
Combat is where this lets me down and it was enough to leave a sour taste in my mouth. The strikes are incredibly slow no matter how fast a weapon I found. Each strike uses way too much stamina. The worst part is that you can't dodge once you have started an attack or are in the middle of an attack animation. This leads to many times in which you will start an attack while you think it is a safe time but need to dodge after the fact but can't and you get hit. Makes combat sluggish and very annoying. Also, the dodge itself seems to have too much of a delay. I understand that if I worked on my timing a bit I might be fine with the dodge delay but what's the point when the combat itself isn't any fun for me. If combat wasn't the sole purpose I might leave a thumbs up and say it just isn't for me but with combat being so important in this I just don't think it is very good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fan of Dark Souls and Prey, I was told this game would be a Soulslike experience in or more less the setting of Prey. And while it is, there's something lacking about this.

Dark Souls is a series with fantastic boss fights, and The Surge has 5 bosses, one of which is a recopy of the first boss with an extra regular guy who comes out. There's also less than a dozen different enemies in the game.

The Surge did decide to introduce something new with limb executions and ducking and jumping, but I feel like they make the combar slower and clunkier. In the end it wasn't a positive additon.

The story is kind of a mess. While the story is told to you through e-mails and audio logs, the ending doesn't actually make sense. When I finished the game I immediate went to Google to figure out what happened and why, only to find out, no it doesn't actually make sense, no clue why they ended it that way.

Overall I just didn't come out of this with many/any good things to say about the Surge, it's not terrible, but I just can't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter years of hearing how hard Dark Souls games were (and nothing else about them) lead me to avoid playing that or anything like it, a friend introduced me to Bloodborne and I LOVED it. Once I had beaten it, I immediately wanted another Soulsborne to play. That's when I found the surge and I was immediately a huge fan!

Like any Soulsborne, character leveling is performed by besting enemies in combat and collecting an amount of currency from them which is used to upgrade character level. (This game uses "Tech Scrap") Combat in The Surge surprised me by how mobile it was, as opposed to other games I had played. Equipped with a powerful exo-Suit, you are now able to move at superhuman speeds and lift and swing weapons of inhuman size. This leads to many amazing combat feats which you may not be expecting. In any case, it's advisable that you take some time to experiment with the many available combat abilities. After playing the first few areas without knowing how to perform one of my most powerful attacks, it helped me a ton!

Gameplay and equipment leveling are very closely linked here as collecting/upgrading weapons and armor requires a certain strategic approach to combat whereby you target, attack, then sever the limbs which wear the equipment piece you want to collect. You can wear it yourself or use it as upgrade material back at the regularly placed "Operations" centers.

In addition to looting weapons and armor from enemies, you'll also be able, through combat and exploration, to find weapons, collectables, and implants which augment your abilities by affecting things like your maximum health and stamina, your damage with certain weapon types, and the amount/strength of healing items you carry plus many, many more

Since the game's equipment system is focused on the harvesting of enemy limbs for armor pieces, you can expect to very frequently be fighting humans wearing armor and wielding weapons. Luckily, The Surge has managed to offset this monotony somewhat, through the loot system but also through the inclusion of many robotic enemies. This would come as a natural step in a game set in a post-apocalyptic industrial complex, but their appearance in the environments you find yourself in throughout this game feel natural, as if they have been there doing their job steadily for years before everything came crashing down.

Speaking of, the world which is crashing down around the shoulders of your near-mute protagonist, Warren, is one that comes crashing down in beautiful, graceful style. The way the environments are crumbling around you as you fight and sever your way through your coworkers really sets the perfect background for the harrowing industrial nightmare you're trying desperately to survive through. This also plays into some challenges in the game in places where platforms are missing, an equipment malfunction adds an extra hazard to the environment, or even areas which are near pitch black

But even as sprawling, dark, and dangerous as The Surge's many varying environments may seem, you are still encouraged, as in many other games of this kind, to search every square inch, if not for collectables, then for scraps of story which, when brought together, unfold into a complete narrative. I didn't really understand this kind of storytelling at first but I've started to come around to it and get really excited whenever I find an audio log or a log entry in a game.

All in all, I've had a great time playing The Surge. I have damn near beaten it on PS4, but then I bought it on PC along with The Surge 2. So now I'm cracking on to beat them both! If you are a fan of Soulsbornes, it's a safe bet that you will enjoy this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first time I will ever review a game I brought in Steam. Why? To prevent unwary players from buying this piece of garbage. For what it's worth, I've played Nioh in NG+++, so this game was not my first soulsborne genre and I can say, not even Nioh with it's dual-boss stage, was this cheap. I will not list any pros in this game, just pure salt.

-Battle mechanic is garbage. You are at the mercy of RNG when you're trying to perform a combo, if ever you can. You want him to perform a quick slash? No, he will perform a haymaker instead, leaving you in animation lock and wide-open for a OHKO. This is unacceptable for a game where your moves have to be precise. There's also duck/jump mechanics, with clunky input, incorporated which is also utter garbage and doesn't reward you better and punish your hard for timing it wrong.

-There are a lot of enemies that don't flinch/stagger at all, and if ever they did, you can only do 1-2 hits or be punished otherwise. They also leave you with liiiiiitle opening to put in those hits to stagger them. So what do you do? You resort to cheese tactics. Dash-in > poke > dash-out until you kill them. I will contest, that everyone who has finished this game resorted to cheesing the enemies for the most part of this game. It defeats the purpose of a hack & slash game where you analyse enemy pattern and attack/dodge based on the situation. Don't even say "parry/block" because it's more economical, stamina-wise, to just dodge away on this game.

I did not add some other ways this game put you into disadvantage, I could add a few more salt just from its mechanics but these should be enough. This game will drive you up the wall not because of challenge but because of frustration. Game uninstalled after the 3rd map. Stay away from this game. Do not even buy this on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
