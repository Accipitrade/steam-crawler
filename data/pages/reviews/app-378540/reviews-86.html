\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely loved my time with this game. It was difficult to settle into at first. Some of the mechanics and the combos didn't really sink in (for me at least) as well as they did in DS or BB. I guess when you're holding a sword, like in DS, you have a certain idea of how a sword will be used. Some of the weapons types, such as the single-rig (my personal fav) handled strangely to me. The world was a little more labyrinthine than I expected as well. I tried the game for a while and, after feeling suitably flustered, I set it down for months.

But there's a certain groove to the gameplay. After picking it back up not too terribly long ago, I stuck with it. And I am very happy I did. I learned I actually enjoyed the environment. There is a certain excitement to finding a new passage (and there are soooo many) and following it to see how it surprisingly links two areas together. The combat was tough at first. But once you learn "the groove", you'll find yourself taking on 2 security guards at once (There's nothing like ducking or hopping over a swinging weapon and landing that follow-up blow).

The crafting is pretty easy and manageable. Nothing too complicated. Which I liked. I know what I needed and how to get it. Which just involves more killing. It can get a bit grindy but I think that's kind of expected.

One thing I didn't love was the overwhelming sense of claustrophobia in some areas. I mean, I understand the narrow access corridors. But some of those spaces are just not suited to handle the camera during combat. But that didn't happen too terribly often. At least your weapons don't connect with the walls. Dear god, that would be a damn nightmare. I do appreciate when they use narrow spaces and corners to sneak one in on you. There were a few times I let out an audible "OH S4!T". That's always fun.

I get it might not be the game for everyone. But if you like the soulsborne style games and just have that itch, catch this one on sale. I was certainly surprised with how much fun I had.\t\t\t\t\t

TLDR
A game in the Souls vein. Not as hard as Dark Souls 3 - I haven't really delved deep into the other 2 - and perhaps closer in spirit to PS4's Bloodborne (which is harder than this). Exploration is fun and combat, once mastered, can be incredibly satisfying; particularly as your character develops 'proficiency' the more he uses a specific weapon.

Random musings
As the first Souls-like I've ever played, and one that I credit with a deepening appreciation (hell, love) for the genre, I must admit to a soft spot for this game. Which in itself is immensely surprising to me because well before I encountered this game (and, in fact, throughout my first two forays into this game) I would have labelled the entire genre as antithetical to gaming entertainment (unncessarily, joylessly difficult; seemingly repetitious and grind-y play; stark, almost nihilistic, vibe). It did, however, take me three 'forays' into this game, over a span of 6 months, before things finally clicked and the love was felt!

Exploration
Quite a few reviewers seem to be exasperated by the level design and lack of map, and I was at first. But I eventually found exploration to be largely quite a rewarding experience in this game: particularly when it opened up new shortcuts to old haunts. I have less love for the final map which, despite it's verticality, still feels.... 'linear' in nature. But that might have been fatigue setting in (I was in this game an awful lot at the end).

Combat
I found this (eventually) to be immensely satisfying. The targeting system gives (to me, at least) an almost zen-like focus to combat: almost as if a life-or-death fight was too much and the micro-task of "securing this part" was much easier for me to grok.

Boss fights
Hmmm. Well, I've completed the game so they're all doable (while Lorian and Lothric can go do one, preferably off the side of a cliff - although, I suppose only Lorian has to, and Lothric would follow). They seem quite variable in hardness, and I found some required deep skill and thought, while others appeared destined to go down in my first attempt. However, compared to, say, Bloodborne and Dark Souls 3, I feel the boss fights here demanded less strategy and cognitive engagement, although I wouldn't call them bad.

Plot
This is, to my mind, a mess. I've read up on this. I can't make sense of the timeline. It seems to work more if it's largely internal, reflecting a broken mind, etc. But you know what - it didn't bother me. In fact, it's opaqueness has made me think more about it than other games that serve their's up on a platter. (And it's still way more accessible than Bloodborne where... something happened. Maybe. It's not clear.)

Suggestion
If there's still a demo available, and you are honestly on the fence about this, I would give it an evening (and the many, many deaths it entails). Perhaps read some advice on combat techniques, but otherwise approach it cold and explore with no prior knowledge. Oh, and don't picture winning battles as simply preserving 'souls'/tech scraps/points, but as a reward in and of itself. It really is!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of my alltime favorites - an awesome game.

It's a singleplayer Sci-Fi Action RPG with a nice story, stunning graphics and great sound effects (wear earphones or use a surround system for best atmosphere)
Every weapon has different combos/styles and effects, every armor type behaves differently so you can equip your character according to your playstyle.

I like the Sci-Fi setting, the long and dark corridors, the great halls, the atmosphere and everything else in the game - and the fact that it's a singleplayer game.

At first the game may seem a bit hard or even unfair. After a while I just noticed this game is just different from other games I played until then. You'll need to master evasive maneuvers, blocking, timings and attacking - then the game is just right and not too hard. The game does not have a map or a quest log or a quest marker but it's quite linear so you won't get lost too often.

"Character progression" is done via implants so you're able to replace your enhancements at any time to try something different.

I can't wait for The Surge 2. Really looking forward to it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, i played Demon's Souls, Dark Souls 2 and even Salt & Sanctuary. I never gone far cause i really don't like their spirit : die and lose all you've done, die cause you didn't knew they were trap, and you're suppose to learn of the game and do better after gnah gnah gnah. For me that is ♥♥♥♥, i don't want to be punish cause i don't learn perfectly the game.

Saying that, and on the other hand, i pretty like The Surge. Even these games share the same mechanic (very action - few rpg and hard game), there are few things that improve it a looooot.
1/ Graphics and artistic design are very great. Ennemies and bosses are well made if you like sci-fi and robot things. Remind of Elyseum of course with the exoskeleton, and other sci-fi stuffs with implants, disjoncted brains, after apocalyptic wake up , etc.
2/ Even you lose your xp (scrap) and can get them again on your corpse after you die, The Surge introduce few ideas that improve game comfort. You can stock your scrap for example, so you don't have the pain to rush the waypath with ennemies respawn (you ahe limited time to get back your losing scrap). Also quitting the game dont bring you back to the starting zone, they are autosave so you'll be back where the last save was made, with ennemies you lkilled still dead. Finally, the fight mechanic of focusing differents parts of bodie is the genius idea, of course. Mixed with bullet time fatalities, it make the game so "Oh yeah baby!". And not too punitive so you can keep the fun.
3/ Story. You discover the plot listening audio record, watching video ad and talking to NPC. Story is dystopic, remind a lot of Bioshock or System shock, I am a legend, survival zombie pandemy stuff, etc. True sci-fi scenario.

So in other words, if you dont like Dark souls, this game introduce few new things that can maybe suit you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSolid game, I enjoyed it. Never got the hang of when to use the mechanic to jump/duck attacks, but the rest of the combat mechanics are easy to grasp and feel good. The aesthetic I can dig, the out door areas and lab areas are cool and fun to explore, the catwalks and service corridors I feel are over utilized and not well distinguished from one another.

I really find this a significant improvement over Lords of the Fallen, and I'm glad the developer stuck it out with this type of game. While most of my gripes I can ignore as quirks of the game, they are all amplified in the Nanite Mass enemy and become irritating:
Enemies can instantly pivot their model to face you anytime they are not locked in an animation, even if you are behind them.
Enemy projectile damage is really high, especially for tracking projectiles from enemies placed in small rooms.
Lighting and color design feel restrained, and it can be difficult to tell what is happening while engaged in combat.

That all being said, this is a worthwhile game and I hope to see more from Deck13.\t\t\t\t\t
