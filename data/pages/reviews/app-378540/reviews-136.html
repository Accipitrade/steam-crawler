\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game literally came out of nowhere for myself and many others. It is from the same developers of Lords of the Fallen, which I personally enjoyed but it did have heavy flaws in terms of class balance. That being said it had great fights and unique maze like level design reminicent of old school dungeon crawler.

This game takes the best of Lords of the Fallen, the best from scifi games, and the best from Dark Souls and reinvents it all. While the game is somewhat short if you rush without exploring (20 hours rushing if you are good at these games. 30+ if you are new to the genre or go slow) it is still a great game. I had a ton of fun with all the boss fights.

My only real complaint is the low number of boss fights which I wont reveal. Those are somewhat made up for with the awesome maze like levels and the tough normal enemies to fight.

Is it worth full price? Honestly the game launched at 50 dollars which is all but unheard of now a days. I picked it up at 33.50 during a sale and have had no issues with spending that much. I plan on doing a new game+ run sometime which will give me even more time.

I highly recommend the game it really is a lot of fun!\t\t\t\t\t

Likes

- I really enjoyed the execution system (you get to tear off limbs) and by ripping people apart you get crafting materials and new gear.

-You can also put your own music in-game and listen to it while you're at a rest zone!

-The graphics looked appealing to me and I did not have any performance issues/crashes.

-As far as difficulty goes I had no issues with it being too hard or too easy.

-A lot of melee weapons ranging from very simplistic movesets to a bit more complicated.

-Fast paced.

-Quite a bit of BLOOD!

Dislikes

-My first playthrough was around 24 hours and my 2nd/3rd together took around 10. I wish the game had a bit more content but I'm pretty sure there will be DLC at some point.

-Your only real form of ranged attack is mainly for utility and besides that a melee weapon that can throw a beam of sorts. Because of the futuristic setting of the game I think there could have been some pretty kick-ass ranged weapons.

-Unfortunately I actually feel like I experienced everything that the game had to offer during my 34 hours play through. I used a different weapon type on each playthrough (except my first where I had used 2). I didn't find too many secrets but I won't spoil the one I did find.

Neutral

-I did get lost quite a few times but that's to be expected during my first playthrough of the game. But overall there are areas that do look quite similar.

-A lot of people dislike how the game has 1 distinct look throughout the game. There are a couple different areas for you to explore but they all have similarities. Personally I didn't really mind all that much.

-There are 12 sets of armor in the game and each one has a different set bonus for wearing every piece. It might have been interesting to mix/match armor sets but doing so just puts you at a disadvantage. Not to say that you can't beat the game with a set bonus but still.

-I sort of touched upon this in the dislikes section but the only true form of combat is melee. If you are looking for a game with ranged combat or magic then this probably won't be for you.

-I purchased the game at full price in $CDN and I don't regret. However some may want to wait for to go on sale or for DLC to be released due to length.

-You cannot customize your character or name him. He is Warren and always Warren. I didn't mind too much by the end of my playthroughs I wouldn't have minded giving him a moustache and mullet.

My Lazy Summary

Overall I could see myself going back and playing The Surge in a few months and if they release DLC. It was a solid title!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRight I'm going to be a little controversial here, so make yourself a cup of tea, get comfortable and I'll explain.

I ENJOYED THIS GAME MORE THAN DARK SOULS 3.

OK now that you spat tea all over your screen, I'll get into why this game impressed me so much. First of all, I have to say that Deck13 did an amazing job with the setting and Sci-Fi theme of the game. It's dark and gritty but oddly beautiful, which is only bettered by the graphical quality and performance of the game. Even on my somewhat low-end laptop, I was blown away by just how good it looked. The combat (which I'll get to later) is where the game really shines, it's visceral and incredibly satisfying to pull off finishing moves when it looks so good. It shows signs of improvement since Lords of the Fallen which also looked pretty good for a smaller development team. Visuals 9/10

In terms of controls and camera, The Surge feels quite fluid and combat is only occasionally clunky due to preemptively pressing combos (I think this can be canceled thanks to a recent update) or getting stunned whilst trying to attack. Your attacks consist of horizontal and vertical variants which then, depending on your weapon, form combos to apply greater damage and other additional effects. Limb-targetting is an important mechanic in the game but using the sticky-limb control feature I rarely had any problems. I must admit in the heat of battle I would often forget to target the weaker limbs but that's my bad. Locking on and switching between enemies was fast and the camera is quick but smooth to react to any changes you make. Controls and Camera 8/10

A lot of people seem to agree that the story was really lacking or that it was just non-existent. Although I did find it to be a bit shallow that was to be expected from a Souls-like game. I won't go into detail as to not spoil the plot but essentially Warren joins CREO as they can give him the ability to walk again amongst other things. However, much like a cliched Sci-fi film, it doesn't go very well for him. The story unfolds in quite a predictable way with CREO being a big corporation with ill-intentions, but I did enjoy the lore and back-story that the game provided. Most of this is found through audio tapes and scraps of information from characters. The characters themselves though, they weren't anything special. I assume Deck13 placed 1-dimensional people in there to keep the player from feeling too isolated. Essentially the plot feels like the equivalent of a Dark Souls story, just with a Sci-Fi twist for freshness. Story and Characters 6/10

Enemies in this game may appear to be generic and boring at first but thanks to the limb-targetting, each encounter can be different and require an alternative approach (More so in new game+ and beyond). You mainly find yourself fighting the "zombified" workers of CREO, some more capable then others. Later on in the game, this is switched up as new enemy types are added providing additional challenge and forcing you to choose different tactics. The most notable of these would be the Smelter mini-boss units and the Tripedal security bots (Damn those guys are annoying). The further in the game you get, the more advanced and smarter the enemies become. Again trying not to spoil, but the last lot of enemies were a good challenge as they exploit limb-targetting unlike anything else. In terms of boss design, each one is unique and requires you to learn their patterns and attack them when they're weakest or recovering from an attack. The set pieces of the later bosses are fantastic and taking them down provides a lot of satisfaction. The issue with this is the empty feeling you get when you realize there's only 5 in the entire game :(
Overall, I'd say the design of every enemy/boss was decent and well-suited to the Sci-Fi theme, which is important when you see them so often. Enemy and Boss design 9/10

Right now to the juicy part, severing limbs is so damn satisfying :D
The main bulk of the gameplay comes from Warren's ability to sever the limbs of his enemies and collect loot from their mutilated corpses. If that doesn't sound fun to you, you must be sane! Combat is fast, aggressive and fluid with enemies dishing out substantial damage if you mess up. More like Bloodbourne than Dark Souls, the combat requires quick reflexes and offers the player a risk/reward system based on how aggressively they play. Blocking is an option but I never found myself using it. Even high-def armor won't let you turtle for long. Essentially every humanoid enemy wears armor to protect their body, attacking armored limbs does less damage, but severing armored limbs has a chance to give you the armor schematics and components from the damaged part. The more focused damage you do to a part, the higher the chance. Alternatively you can target unarmored areas to do increased damage and sever them for faster kills. Every attack you make builds energy and this energy allows you to; A-Do finishing moves B-Use your drone to stun, knockback, damage or agro enemies (Yes you have a freaking drone) C-Use injectables which are basically items or flasks from DS.

The components and schematics allow you to craft new weapons and armor or potentially upgrade existing ones. This makes for a very fulfilling cycle of grinding and murdering then getting stronger through upgrading and murdering some more. Unlike DS this game doesn't let you upgrade yourself through levelling. Instead levelling up gives you higher core power which lets you equip more weapons, armor and mods (whilst also allowing access to other areas of the game and increasing mod effectiveness). Swapping mods is as simple as going back to a med bay for a completely free respec if you desire. Some mods known as hot-swaps can be switched on the fly too. This is an interesting take on the DS style of grinding souls then levelling as it gives much more freedom into what you want to equip. There's too many mods to list here but rest assured, you won't be limited by a lack of attributes and skill levels in The Surge. I feel I'm going on a bit so I'll just cut it short, this game may be Sci-Fi Dark Souls on the surface to most but it brings something new and exciting to the table in terms of gameplay. Gameplay 10/10

Now back to the matter at hand, I thoroughly enjoyed every minute of this game despite it's shortcoming's. I found combat very satisfying and overall more fulfilling than that of other games like Dark Souls. Being able to cut my enemies up and then craft myself a set of their armor with the accompanying weapon felt good, really good. Yet at the same time this game is very punishing and somewhat lacking compared to DS. As a longtime fan of the Dark Souls series, I'm not saying they're bad (if anything they're the reason The Surge was even made) but I feel that the combat of those games has grown stale over time. The Surge provides a new and fresh take on the Souls-like genre and for this reason I found myself enjoying it much more. However, The Surge is short and with only 5 bosses, you find yourself wanting more and new game+ isn't really enough. The DLC's are both a blast to play through, expanding upon the base game but unfortunately suffering from the same issues (lack of bosses, enemy types etc). The change of scenery and atmosphere is very welcome though, worth experiencing for sure. If this is your genre, definitely give the game a try!

The Surge 8/10

The story may not be extraordinary, but certainly interesting and well told. The last boss fight could be a little bit more challenging for "maxed out" players. There are a few glitches, but nothing that spoils the fun. I did not encounter gamebreaking bugs and frequent autosaves would have prevented any progression loss.
