\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA decent game with some good ideas and some really, not very good applications. Combat is solid but this staggering system sucks and being locked into a combo can and will get you killed a lot. Map layouts range from terrible to just "okay" where some larger areas have got far too few shortcuts for how many enemies you have to fight through and some smaller areas have so many shortcuts that it's actually difficult to navigate which one to take. Early and late game are really, very good but that middle portion is terrible. It's difficult to figure out where to go and when you get there you have to fight through dozens of annoying enemies to get anywhere and they are around every damn corner you can find.

I say buy it but wait for a discount. It isn't a terrible game, the devs clearly learned from the input they received for the awful flop of Lords Of The Fallen, but this game is no way a great game. Decent at best. Solid and playable. The DLC is pretty good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRecommended with a caveat.
This game is great, it looks great, it runs well and it has a lot of great components.
But it isn't without flaws.
I'd recommend picking this game up in a sale when you can and enjoying it for what it is.

The biggest pro this game has is the atmosphere and fantastic level design, it also has a very triple A style/look
Returning to OPS bases after finding new routes is rewarding, but sometimes these can build up and become confusing.

The biggest flaws for me were the poor bosses, which would have their stamina bar fully lowered and refuse to go into a stunned state, or how they'd instantly deal a large chunk of damage without telegraphing it at all.
The games weapon/armour upgrade system has one major flaw where the weapon that you use builds proficiency, meaning that switching from one handed weapons to one of the other weapon types meant a huge fall in my damage output.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is definitely one of the better Soulslikes out there. They absolutely NAIL the isolated/danger around every corner feeling that makes you want to just sit in your base and never venture out of it. So for atmosphere alone, this game is a 10/10.

The unique cutting mechanic is a great way to encourage you to try different methods of taking down enemies and keeps the combat fresh. The story is also quite strong and unlike Souls games, you will actually understand what's happening while you play without having to piece together the story. There aren't very many boss encounters but the ones there are are really well done and challenging.

The downsides of the game are a lack of enemy variety and a lack of environmental variety. A lot of the areas of the game feel kind of samey (interior of grungy sci-fi buildings) and new enemies are given to you on a slow drip (only a few new enemies per area).

Even with these issues, I would definitely recommend this game if you're into the Soulslike genre. I had an awesome time playing through it.\t\t\t\t\t

Robot souls with less polish than soulsborne but many unique features and good new game+.

Biggest cons:Very mazey map design easy to get lost,you can unlock so many shortcuts(without knowing them) but it is hard to remeber them all for first time players.
Stat "defence" barely reduce dmg even by souls standards,low implant build vareity on early-mid NG,takes a while to get your hp up via implants so you less likely to die in 1-2 shots on NG.Only 5 bosses not counting DLC.

No fast travel without augemented DLC.In game 2D maps are almost fully useless.
Once you know the map and the game,it is about half as long as dark souls 1.
Weakly balanced early game on NG.


Pros:Nice gfx and art style,VERY good finishing animations,MANY viable build options especially by NG+,many armor and weapons options,good drone utility,nice dark atmopshere,scrap(exp)multiplier reward risky non medbay/bonfire play.
NG+ has new enemy postion majority of them are non respawnable "elite" enemies wielding random sets.
New upgrades tiers and implant slots up to NG+++ opening up builds even further.
Few hidden secrets and sidequests.

Once you get past the first 5 hour of jank and unbalanced early game difficulty the game can open up.Very fun to play in NG+.

Over all 8/10 if you do not mind souls like difficulty.Not recommended for players looking for easy relaxing games.

DLC Walk in the park:Very good but short like 2 hours.Adds something to the main plot,I recommend it.

Augment DLC:Arena style cowboy themed grind,you can get the best implants mostly intended for high NG+ runs,it has limited fast travel,but the DLC is weak and grindy by nature.\t\t\t\t\t
