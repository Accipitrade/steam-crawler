\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA rereview of The Surge:

The Surge, although a short playtime I got... felt unsatisfying in regards to how it treats the player, and how it it essentially doesn't make good world building. Everything is too grey due to all the metal making it rather hard to navigate around, with some colour highlights as orange, green, etc... but are too small to even showpoint them.

Gameplay feels fresh, but feels half-commited on how it's built, targeting individual limbs are nice, but the attacks you do seem rather... has no impact, sound effects feel weak, unrewarding to what you do, and ultimately punishes you going with enemies that are only weak when behind them, or after an attack... with an "aura" that still deals damage.

It doesn't help either that there's a timer for your lost tech scraps, making it rather stressful to go through, in addition to enemies hiding unintionally by the environment, whilst they shouldn't, as you can clearly see their routines.

All in all, I'd say The Surge just doesn't make me want to play, as it doesn't really feel like the developers really wanted to commit to making The Surge a great game, but just an... okay game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is an action game, not an action RPG like the games it's usually compared to. There is much more emphasis on combat and defeating difficult enemies with limited tools, rather than in-depth character creation and long term min-maxing with builds. It seems like this game is either loved or hated for the combat, likely because it's more skill-based than Souls. I'll draw some comparisons to explain why:

Upgrades in the Surge are locked behind both region/discovery and player level together, so when progressing through areas and bosses you are restricted to whats available until that point. You can't spend tech scrap to increase your health or damage. Souls, on the other hand, allows you to improve your stats by grinding levels anytime, allowing you to grind until enemies are easier to defeat.

The few ranged attacks in The Surge must be charged by melee attacks before they can be used, so there's no possibility of cheesing levels or bosses with ranged. You have to progress by mastering the combat system.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKeeping it short and sweet, I have been enjoying The Surge. I'm a big fan of the "Soulslike" genre, and have played both the good and the bad. This one is definitely good.

"Is it a DkS clone?"
Yes and no. Combat is dangerous, you drop your collected currency upon death, and... well, that's really all that's carbon copied. Everything else has a rather unique and pleasant spin. Weight, for instance, is now handled through Core Power. If you want to wear heavy armor you need to be able to provide it with plenty of juice. You can wear lighter armor and go with plenty of powerful implants instead if you'd rather be more nimble. There are no stats, so "builds" aren't really a thing. Challenge runs are probably going to be more popular with this game instead of playing through it again with a different build.

"How's the combat?"
It feels good and the controls are tight. There are a few tutorials you can miss in the beginning area (I didn't realize I could jump out of a sprint until the third zone) so make sure to explore the controls thoroughly. Speaking of exploring, make sure to test out every new weapon type you find. There are lots of different combos that can be done by pressing light attack and heavy attack in the correct order. Some are quite vicious, and on the topic of viciousness you can gain power in combat by striking enemies to brutally execute them. It's quite pleasing to the eye! Besides choppnig off arms,, legs, and heads (you can target specific body parts in order to more effectively lop them off) cutting up your foes provides you with salvage and parts. Collect new armor sets by chopping up your enemy and learning the schematics, then build your own!

"Is it buggy?"
I've encountered one or two strange things in the game, though both happened with the first boss. Apart from that encounter I've yet to find anything game breaking or even inconveniencing.

"Is the story good?"
So far it has been. It's told through audio logs, conversations, and visual clues. I can't comment on whether or not the ending is solid, but I'm sure you can find out from other reviewers whether that's the case.

"Does it look good?"
As a lover of gritty sci-fi, I have really enjoyed seeing all of the environments and enemies. Keep in mind that a lot of the armor and weapons are tools, especially at the beginning of the game. Don't go into it expeecting to look like a space marine, since you'll end up looking more like a space construction worker. Seeing as how that fits the game's theme and setting, I think they did a great job.

Hopefully I've answered some of your questions in this review. In closing I'd say once again that I recommend this game is you're a Soulslike lover that wants a new twist on the formula. Dark Souls it ain't, and that's okay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t+ Dark Souls with exoskeletons
+ brutal and dynamic combat
+ nicely designed industrial locations (you might get tired of the setting if you're not a fan)
+ shortcuts
+ boss fights
+ optional grind fest, you can grind at the same spot, enjoying the combat and trying out different setups, but this is completely optional, the game does not require this to progress
+ difficulty

- sometimes cheap enemy placement (like two enemies spamming attacks in a tight corridor etc.), but this is rare
- sometimes it's too dark and the flashlights are just bad

+/- story / characters, it's ok but not outstanding, but I don't really care that much, the gameplay is fun.

final: 9/10
I recommend this game if you're a Dark Souls fan and enjoy a good challenge.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI almost never write reviews on steam but I have to for this masterpeice of a game. The combat in this game is innovative and creative, not only that the combat directly coincides with your acquiring gear and weapons. there are no stats in this game, to respec you simply replace your gear with different gear, same with weapon.
There are 3 gear types. you have operator, your extremely light but quick set, low stability meaning you may get knocked down more than you like, it can increase your attack speed, and also it can drastically decrease not only energy spent on your drone, but stamina consumed with running or dodging or even just stamina spent in general. Personally i just finished my playthrough wearing nothing but operator sets my way through. Secondly you have sentinel, sentinel is your medium set, his has medium stability, and dependant upon set can either add to or decrease the amount of stamina consumed, i never found a sentinel set that decreased or increased attack speed either, but the defense was higher than that of operator. So think of Sentinel as your jack of trades middle ground sets. Lastly you have goliath, and it is properly named. These behemoth sets can drastically decrease attack speed, and drastically increase stamina consumption in all areas and energy consumption on drones drastically once more. With all these cons youd think it was worthless but goliath sets turn you into a hulking tank, so if your plastyle is the slow smashy type. go for it.
Next up we have weapons. single rigged is a one handed weapon attached at the wrist, it has low attack speed, high impact (the higher your impact, the more likely you are to stagger and knock down enemies) and amazing damage, single rigged weapons from my playthrough showed to have the highest damage of any weapon in the game, maxing at 120. One handed weapons are swords and the occasional mace you find, medium attack speed, medium impact, medium everything pretty much, imo the damage isnt all to great but its much better than our next sad contestant, twin-rigged. Twin rigged is 2 blades attached to both wrists, with very low impact and exceedingly low damage (highest i saw was a mark 4 with 38 damage), it boasts extremely high attack speeds but with these your playstyle will become excessively hit and run.
After that we have staves, staves have some of the weirdest movesets in the game, they boast medium impact, not too great damage. next up, we have heavy duty, our last in the weapons category. In an odd twist of events, heavy duty weapons boast the slowest attack speed, but the second highest damage, under single-rigged. The highest damage heavy duty i found was 79 damage with an end game weapon. These weapons boost some of the highest impact tho, and knock down enemies quite frequently. Weapons also have certain energy gain levels which increase energy attained from attacking, in order from highest average to lowest average EN gain we have: Heavy Duty, Single Rigged, staves, one handed, and twin rigged. Also with weapons there is profiency scaling, as you use a weapon type more you become more profiecient with it, i imagine this increases damage done but its effect was never obvious. again in order of highest to lowest average gain of Prof scaling we have: Twin rigged, Single rigged, one handed, staves, and then heavy duty.
As you can see this game has quite alot of depth to it and im not really done explaining its most base parts, instead of stats you have implants, these implants almost always stack and increase stamina, energy, hp, healing potions, regen potions, and so much more, there are so many implants with different effects it would take we 30 minutes to explain them all. But to use these implants you need to upgrade your power score. your power core is your level, the higher level it is, the better gear and implants you can use. the only thing that doesnt use core power is your weapon.
To acquire all this gear and weaponry you have to make use of the games combat mechanic of dismemberment. If you see an enemy with something you want, target that limb, beat the ever loving ♥♥♥♥ out of it, and then when you have the energy if hes still alive, cut it the ♥♥♥♥ off. Its satisfying as hell. Your currency is tech scrap, if youre afraid of losing it you can store it at this games version of the bonfire, ops, which has a medbay and gear assembler.
The story may be very paced out, but it will always be at the back of your mind, wondering whats going to happen next as you journey through the Zombie(ish) infested halls of the CREO orginization.
You play as Warren, a cripple who saw an advertisement and decided to join CREO to help make the world a better place, but when he enters the surgical bay to get his RIG installed, the sedative doesn't work, and Warren is subject to machines drilling holes into his bones and even his skull, slicing his flesh as it welds peices of machinery into him. The pain is so intense that Warren passes out and you wake up in a junkyard, no idea what has happened. A drone dragging you by the leg you pick up a broken piston and destroy it. You make your way through the junkyard as best you can, when you find an Ops, a women is there on a holocommunicator, you find out her name is Sally, she will be as close to a guide as you get in this game.
And that my dear friend is where the journey begins. If you have been pondering getting this game, do it. Its well worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has the great tactical combat and explorative open maze style combat of a souls game with plenty of interesting mechanical twists that give it it's own identity. As opposed to souls games, the main focus of this game is intense situations while fighting the various small mobs of enemies. Although there is less of an emphasis on boss fights in this game, whenever you encounter one it is much more stressful than any of the bosses I've encountered in any souls game. I myself have never been a huge fan of boss fights anyway, so players like me will be happy to see a game more focussed on exploration and discovery, rather than traversing boss to boss.

Some may find the amount of content lacking but I've enjoyed all the time I've spent with it so far and look forward to spending more.\t\t\t\t\t
