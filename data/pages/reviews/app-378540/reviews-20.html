\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first time I played this game I was REALLY bad at it. I struggled through te first area and eventually gave up when I found the first boss to be too difficult. Some time after, I picked up the game again to give it another shot. Having several hundred hour of Dark Souls under my belt this time, the game was significantly easier and I got through most of it. However, I found there to be a big spike in difficulty in the later game and I ended up putting the game down again, shotly before the final area. Flash forward to recently, I wanted to give it one last go. I've gotten into Achievement Hunting and wanted to see if I could clean up the remaining few I needed. I ended up getting sucked into the game and found it 100% easier and more enjoyable. 70 hours later, I have the 100% completion!

Review: After being able to enjoy the game this time around, I feel I can confidently give a genuine review of this game. Overall, I can say that I enjoy it! The game looks fantastic - the factoy setting has a dark atmosphere that fits with the tone of the game. The 5 main areas are unique but can get repetitive when you have to backtrack, especially the 2nd area, which you circle back around to 3 times. There were few bosses (5) and they were okay. Their designs worked with the tone of the game but I found their fights were lacking excitement. The areas themselves were large with lots to explore but I found it easy to get lost - which sucked if you died and couldn't find your tech scrap!

There were lots of weapons are armor sets to choose from but I stuck with one weapon and one armor set that I liked through the whole game and just upgraded as needed (#IronMaus). The game can be difficult if you don't utilize certain features, like targeting weak spots when attacking or targeting limbs to chop off. Enemies can be really tanky if you don't engage each one properly. The story is okay, I'm just still not 100% sure what fully happened and I found I was always doing objectives without fully knowing the reason behind WHY I was doing them.

The 2 DLC's were okay. The first one, "A Walk In The Park", was short but offered new territory, which was a nice change to the typical factory setting of the main game. The 2nd DLC was different and very repetitive. But it was short so the experience was grueling. While the 2 DLC's were a nice change to the base game, they didn't really offer anything new of value when it came to items, weapons and armor sets (at least not in my opinion).

Overall, the game played really well. I didn't run into any lag and no game-breaking problems ever came up. The load times were excellent and starting p the game took seconds. I recently played part of the sequel, where load times got significantly worse. While it does have it's flaws, I would still highly recommend this game! If you're a fan of Dark Souls, this is a decent Souls-like for you! 7.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game really shines where pther ARPGs don't.

This is Dark Souls, but clunkier, but at the same time it isn't. Being able to control your attacks much better and more precise is a great feeling and slashing your way through the game finisher after finisher to collect new gear (you mostly have to cut limbs of enemies to get it) is really motivating.

You also don't have to worry about putting skill points into a category you'll later regret, cause there is no such thing. Character progression works as follows: You find tech scrap (basic resource to spent on things) and you either use it to upgrade your gear or level up your character. For each level your character gets +1 core power. Core power allows you to wear more and/or better gear and implants.

Gear and implants have values of core power. Usually the better the item the more core power it uses. If you want a really tanky gearset you might only take very light implants with you. If you want to have the best implants you should stick to the gear set that uses the least core power (or maybe even don't wear any gear at all).

There's a bazillion ways to build your character and you can all do it during the same playthrough, because you can always unslot all implants and set them back one by one to be able to wear that new set you desperately want, but it's core power usage is too high for your current set of implants.

Story is complex, but at the same time you shouldn't mind it too much - at least I didn't. I always understood what the heck was going on, but the gameplay was what kept me going for hours and hours.

I'd definitely recommend this to anyone who played Dark Souls, but may not be too happy with Sekiro and/or Elden Ring and enjoys a more grounded gameplay loop.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t TLDR version: good and underrated game. Shorter and easier, than a typical "Souls" game, but has a unique sci-fi setting and progression system that make playing the Surge an enjoyable expirience. 75/100 in overall.

The Surge reminds me of Prey. Both are sci-fi themed, both are good and both are somewhat unpopular among the masses for reasons unknown.

I'v spent quite a lot of time with this game, clearing both normal and NG+ modes, so buckle up – I will explain what The Surge is all about.

Let's start with an obvious fact – this game is a souls-like. At this point of time i don't think that anyone needs an explanation of what type of a game-mechanics this imply, instead let's talk what is different from the typical "From Software" games.

The first thing would be difficulty – The Surge is waay easier. I'd rate the game's levels at 45% and bosses at barely 30% of a typical "Souls" title difficulty. I can say with confidence that whoever has been struggling with, lets say Sekiro, - will clear The Surge without any troubles. More casual gamers might find the game challenging, but still doable, unlike the undless torrent of suffering of the true "Souls" game.

The second thing would be a unique "body parts" mechanic. You can target head, body, hands or legs independently and sever them off. This serves 2 main purposes: First - you can get weapons and armor that enemy wears for yourself by cutting them off. Someone swings cool axe that you like? Cut off his right arm and here you go! Second - is that most enemies don't wear a full set of armour – some body parts are exposed and by targeting those you can do much more damage, than when attacking an armored parts. All in all – a neat mechanic and also some perks do interact with this dismemberment system as well.

The third thing would be a "progression agility". You don't level up a particular attributes in The Surge – instead you increase the "power core" levels, which allows you to equip more and better modifications for your suit. Your whole build, power and playstyle is determined only by those modifications and armor + weapon choice. And you can swap your mods at a "bonfire" freely and for no cost or penalty as much as you want, which gives you an ability to experiment with many different loadouts and playstyles on the go. Wanna be beefy? Equip more HP and def modules. Wanna use drone attacks all the time and spam energy-related skills? Equip more energy generating and retaining mods. And so on and so on. Swap, mix and match em to your hearts content and find the combos that work the best for your personal playstyle. Honestly – i liked this progression system A LOT.

The forth thing – playing with the keyboard is actually very enjoyable and even preferable to the controller. Thank you very much for this, devs. I hate it when "Souls" games literally force me to play with the controller due to the complicated KB control sheme.

And the fifth thing would be graphics – crisp, clean, very detailed and beautiful. With all respect to the "Souls" series – those games always had a "murky" graphics. I hope that the Elden Ring will break this tradition, but for now the surge/surge2 graphics reign supreme in the souls-like department (no longer valid, check the post scriptum). And i think we can add an exellent optimisation here as well. The game runs super-smooth even on an ordinary PC, you won't need an RTX 30XX series monster to enjoy a good picture here.

Ah, and one more thing – a sentient, coherent and very powerful story/lore. You won't need to read hundreds of item descriptions to make vague guesses about what's going on in the game. No – you are presented with a linear and clear story and this is truly nice. Also the theme of exploitation of poor, vulnerable and disabled people by the megacorporations adds an another layer of depth to the game's lore.

But of course it's not “all the roses” in here.

The game has a bunch of annoying problems that... well... might annoy players. The thing that pissed me off the most was a lack of the fast travel system. Yes, you can't teleport to the previously visited and cleared locations on a whim. You will have to backtrack all the way back there by foot. The main reason for such a journey would be the upgrade parts for your gear – you need parts from the low-level, early game enemies to level up your cool new rig all the way to the max.

The devs have tried to somewhat resolve this with a “wild west” DLC, where they have introduced a separate “hub” of sorts, that has connected doors to every game's level. But to use it you have to own the said paid DLC and it's STILL cumbersome – looking for the single door on a level, going into the “hub” zone, exiting through another door (which is not even marked by the area that it leads to!).

Another problem is the enemy variety – 80% of enemies are humanoids, with only a few more unique types in-between. Personally I'v liked the “melting machines” the most out of 'em – that is exactly the type of an enemy that I would like to see more – big, brutal, industrial machine going crazy, but there are only 5 of them in the whole game lol. Also the bosses are few and far between.

The level design is not exactly terrible but it shows very clearly that the dev team was small and has been working on a budget – same walls, same corridors and textures in a lot of places. A few times i'v even got lost due to the corridors being exactly the same, without even a small variety lol.

As for the DLCs – they feel more like a “cut out content”, that should have been in the game from the start, rather than an “honest” additional content. “A walk in the park” is a nice additional level with a new boss and some intresting enemies and visuals. In my opinion this is the only DLC worthy a stand-alone purchase. A “wild west” themed one is good for exp and parts grind, thematical weapons and equipment, unique level challenges and most importantly – “hub doors”. But other than that it's entirely irrelevant to the main game. Personally i'd recommend getting the augmented edition with all the DLCs included, since it's not that much more than a barebone game.

Well, this text is getting quite long, so let's wrap this up.
The Surge is an “okay” souls clone, that stands out mainly due to the sci-fi setting and a good progression/equipment system. If you are into sci-fi it's worth a buy, if not – you won't lose much by skipping it. All in all – a mild recommendation, especially during a sale.


PS: I'v cleared The Surge and made this review before the Eden Ring came out, but simply forgot to post it. Now, that I have 200+ hours in the ER, playing on a NG++ there is one additional thing that I want to add to this text. While The Surge is not a bad game, I do not think that there is any point in playing it in the world where the Elden Ring exists. It's the ultimate Souls game, that utterly eclipses all competition. And the ER graphics and art direction is absolutely godlike – I have not seen a game more beautiful in years, the Soul's “murky graphics” curse has been broken. So the sci-fi setting is The Surge's only saving grace from now on. Is it enough? It's for you to decide.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLords of the Fallen was Deck 13's first Soulslike game. It was an absolute mess and the only Soulslike I actively refuse to ever finish. It certainly made me hesitant to try The Surge, thinking it was more of the same. Boy how wrong I was.

This is a pretty fun twist to the Soulslike formula. You can target any humanoid's arms, legs, head or body. There is a pretty interesting risk and reward here, attacking unarmored limbs will make the enemy staggers quicker, whereas repeatedly attacking and then dismembering armored limb will earn you that piece of gear. Aggression is also rewarded in The Surge: each attack will generate energy that you can convert into health, and later on you can earn an recover your health by finishing enemies.

My only complaints with this game are how much damage each enemy can deal in a single hit and how small the boss pool is. The early areas of the game aren't as interesting to look at either and can be disorienting due to how similar the environment looks, which is remedied on the later parts of the game and especially with the 'A Walk in the Park' DLC.

Despite this, the game's short length means it doesn't overstay its welcome. Can recommend for those who are looking for a different take on Soulslike, but probably not as your first game in the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
