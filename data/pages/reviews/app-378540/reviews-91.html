\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9/10 and one of the best games i've ever played. The Surge 2 - if done right, and if they use music directly from this spotify playlist caleld "retrowave - outrun" it will be the greatest game ever made.

What is The Surge? It's 90's game "One must fall 2097" mixed with dark souls. It's possibly the greatest idea for a videogame ever. Imagine a faster version of dark souls combat that makes you somewhat feel like you're playing devil may cry (just a little) where you are fighting other cyborgs to death with makeshift mad max style junk robot armor/weapons - and if you focus on the right body part and sever it - you can pick it up and take that robotic part for your own ensemble. Use scrap (like souls) to upgrade your machine masterpiece. It's intoxicating and I can't stop playing even though I should do real life things. Very addictive and very good combat. It's the game i've been waiting my whole life for.

Only problem is you probably need a badass rig to enjoy it. Even my 1070 struggles giving me a solid framerate with max settings and the anti aliasing is absolutely the worst. I am so sick of games using this garbage that makes everything shimmer and sparkle - it's absolutely horrendous. Luckily I have a powerful computer and can supersample my resolution - but it takes away from the combat when it's not max FPS. So to the devs I say: Take my advice on music choice - and fix your visuals. And u have something so special it could live forever.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first heard of "The Surge", I was amazed: A souls-like with a sci-fi setting? That's my game!
I then started to play a bit and learned some of the mechanics (yet I only managed to beat the first boss). After some time I decided to play it with a friend of mine (We already had co-op'ed DS 1-3 with DLC and Bloodborne) That was one of my better ideas. After the "Tutorial", which was fairly easy tbh, the real test begun.
New enemies with faster attacks, the first appeareance of the watchdogs (how we hated them...), new weapons, etc.
The difficulty rised pretty suddenly, yet it was never unfair. After a while the enemies started to repeat (they even recycled a boss, wtf?). Overall still a pretty decent game. I can't rate the DLCs yet but if it happens that I play them, I will update the review.

It is from the makers of "Lords of the Fallen", so they knew what they were doing. Combat is doing nice, especially since you don't have any shields that could block attacks. Cool idea to put the armor restricted to the player level but on the other hand you need your scrap first to level up and then also to craft or upgrade your armor, that doesn't feel so good. Also, the weapon skill level increases when you use that weapon type. So if it happens that you used just one-handed weapons but then find a really good two-handed..., well, it sucks tbh. Still a good idea I think.

If you liked any soul-like and the sci-fi setting, this is the game for you. I'd rate it 6.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is beyond amazing. The gameplay is good, the story alright (even though it acts as it is deeper than it is) and visuals are fitting and great. It definitely reminds one of Dark Souls, but that isn't bad. They took the concept, imporved it and changed the setting. So I am fine with that. Sadly, the game is a bit more repetitive. Dark Souls always managed to surprise one, and this kinda failed to do so after a while. I am still going to give it a second and maybe thrid playthrough, because the combat just feels damn good.

All in all. It's a buy. You won't regret it. It is not as frustrating as Dark Souls, but storywise also not as captivating. Looking forward to The Surge II next year. They know, that there is room to improve, so that is gonna be amazing!\t\t\t\t\t
