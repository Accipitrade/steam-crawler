\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's Dark Souls! It isn't Dark Souls! People can't seem to make their minds up about this one. The truth is:

It isn't Dark Souls, but the DNA runs pretty deep. It's an action-RPG where every enemy poses a threat and has to be approached carefully. You loose your money/exp on death and have to go and pick it up in person. You have light and heavy attacks. You have a stamina bar.

But it isn't quite the same. You move differently, more like a traditional action game. You can target individual body parts. Blocking is de-emphisized, in favor of dodging. it feels different, and looks different.

But if you like Dark Souls, you'll probably like The Surge. I do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game, and becouse of that, I hate it even more, for all the annoying stuff it has.
CONS:
1. Weapon proficiency system. Your weapon dmg scales with time you use this class of weapon. Which means, you better stick with the weapon class from the beginning, or you missing some damage. Reason for that is mystery for me.
2. Level-gated areas. Some areas can only be accessed if you leveled up enough. Again, why force the player? Stick some tougher enemies there, and people will get the hint.. or not, which makes things more interesting. Also, there is no "Low level challenges" possible, but I guess this game are not designed for them anyway.
3. Player have a bunch of stats, and the game is not very keen on explaining what the hell they actually do.
4. Remember those pesky enemies in dark souls, who dont telegraph their attacks, or trick you? Thats all enemies in this game. All of them attack from improbable angles, or suddenly, or with combination of the 2, and attacks in this game hurt a lot. Additionally, its bloody dark everywhere. Learning patterns just dont seem to worth it. All of the above clearly define a winning strategy: Go in fast, smack em once, and bug off before they hit you, which makes weapons with big damage simply better then the rest. Forcing the player to act in certain way seems to be the theme with this game.
5. Only saw 2 out of 5 boss fights this far, and they both can be described simply as annoying.
PROS:
1. Exploring is fun, and level design is very cool.
2. Cool stuff with exoskeletons, implants, and such. High-tek is something pretty new for this genre.

And the reason I recommend this: Cant play Dark Souls forever, might as well try this one. Its not _bad_, after all.\t\t\t\t\t

The game requires a certain amount of skill, but not so much that it becomes insanely frustrating.
