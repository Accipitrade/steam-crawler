\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNOT DARK SOULS

Yes, it is a 3rd person melee driven game, with RPG elements where you lose your progress when you die.

However, the pacing is very different. Souls games require quick reactions to enemies.
There is not nearly as much telegraphing in The Surge. You have to study an enemy,
allowing them to strike, either dodging or blocking correctly. You're not a half naked
undead, instead you're a broken man in a powersuit, and the mechanics help to enforce this.
This game is difficult until you understand that it IS NOT SOULS.
To layer this same thought process in, the mobs are harder, there are no easy mobs until you have
leveled quite a bit. The armor and weapons require different playstyles.
If possible, this game requires more patience than Dark souls.

So, what did it do right?

The combat has a very unique feature with limb targeting, almost like vats[Fallout], but in realtime.
The strategy to the combat is difficult, but each enemy has their weak points, their animations, based on
the weapon they are using. Becoming familiar with these weapons animations helps immensely.
Boss battles are fun and engaging, they don't feel cheap too often.
armor and crafting is intruiging and well done.

What did it do wrong?
NG+ progession seems strange, making it feel like you aren't as powerful as you should be the second time around.
Mobs are varied, but not in a unique way. There are many enemies with different armor,
but most of the enemies are humanoid, and it seems there was room for robotic variation
with the premise.

Middle of the road
Story line is run of the mill save the world
rpg mechanics don't stand out\t\t\t\t\t

The camera wasn't made for this enemy and it is constantly clipping out of frame, making it difficult to read its tells.

The game feels halfbaked. The devs are on the verge of finding their own identity but are still gaining experience. It is a definet step up from Lords of the Fallen and am intrested in seeing if they can make a good sequel to this game.

I've ragged on it kinda hard, but it isn't the worst game ever. Its kinda average, so I'd say get it if you want to kill time in between bigger releases but dont expect anything amazing and dont spend more than 20 dollars on it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a real good Souls-like game.
I've completed and loved Lords of the Fallen with all it's DLCs
It's also from the same developer (Deck 13), so this was no real surprise for me.

I think, the feature that stands out the most and even seperates it from other "Git gud"-games is:
The dismemberment based combat.
Yes... it's as much fun as it sounds!
Cutting off limbs and getting the gear you are waiting for, feels ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ rewarding.
Also the guts and gore does not hurt here. ;)

Pro:
- The combat
- Level design (most of the time)
- KI and enemy diversity
- Graphics (not revolutionary, but still pretty)
- The story and it's narrative was surprisingly good for that kind of game

Con:
- Level design (sometimes, especially the later stages were very confusing for me)
- The gameplay is a little bit more ruff compared to DS, BB or Nioh (just a little con)
- The Bosses (besides Black Cerberus... that fight was frickin awesome)

If you like those kind of games and you don't mind or even enjoy a good sci-fi setting then The Surge is for you.

7,75/10
