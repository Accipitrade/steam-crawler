I also love how you have to grind out damaged armor peices or alloys to upgrade your equipment! Honestly there is only one downside i can currently find for this game.

Focus Home Interactive...you gotta do something about your character running up stairs! Every time i try to run up a set of stairs trying to get away from an enemy or to try and get back to collect my lost scrap i get stuck! It's liks the running model wants to process that it has reached the top of the steps early and then tries to run straight even though it's only/not even close to the top! you can't even return to walking after it happens as you still wont move up! While i have not made it far into the game, getting suck on the stairs though is sucking out a lot of the joy i have from the game already. I do hope you read this Focus Home Interactive...because even though i can only play this on the lowest possible graphical settings I really do enjoy playing this game.

The stairs, no matter how much i complain, are a low threat to the game as a whole. Honestly, they don't really take away from the grand design that this team has created for our gaming pleasure, though i admit after a while they will irk you. Especially if you have an enemy right behind you dashing in to cut you in half to get your sweet sweet scraps! Anyway, i have said my piece. I enjoyed what i had played starting out and i hope i come to enjoy the game long after i have beaten it. I recommend that anyone looking for a good challenge pick up this game and give it a whirl. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like souls games, and I like Sci-Fi; This was the perfect mix. The combat is fun if you want it to be, and it can be a grind if you let it. This is a game where you make it what you want it to be, and honestly, I love it. It took some getting used to in the beginning, trying to understand the lingo and the complex-looking menus (It is sci-fi, after all) but then things became more fluid within a few hours of gameplay.
The level designs, although sometimes a bit confusing in tighter quarters, was very well thought out in terms of inter-connectability (is that a word?). Think: Dark Souls 1.
I like that you don't feel like you're missing out on any sort of special loot. You get all of the loot from the enemies, and if you fail to "dismember" the loot, well, that's on you. The upgrade system is cool, and you notice big power jumps in weapon upgrades. I like that the armor has set bonuses, which can really help you out in certain zones or in creating a unique build that fits you.
The graphics look good, but I have a bit of an issue with the temporal aliasing, as I do with many games like this. Increasing HDR will help, but it will also tank your system if you don't have the specs for it. When you're on the go in high action areas, you don't even notice it.

If you're on the edge trying to decide whether or not to buy it, I'd say go for it. I got mine on sale, and I bought the complete pack as well. The DLC seemed a bit short, but it was enjoyable nonetheless and ties into the game quite flawlessly. I'm nowhere near finished with the game and I can tell you that I won't be putting it down any time soon, as I find it quite addicting, especially when you get a hang of the combat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo saves, no do-overs is ridiculous...

The Surge hides away a huge amount of stuff from you, like the "premium versions" of weapons that bosses drop, but if you mess up while killing a boss, that weapon is locked away from you forever! (unless you finish the game and restart the game completely) You cannot go back BEFORE the boss fight and try again, because the games save file is automatically overwritten and there is NOTHING you can do.

I am all for high difficulty, but locking players away from having the freedom to do over certain things in the game is just dumb.

I know this is a sci-fi game, but it is not "realistic". You can clear a whole area of enemies just to get killed, and then you have to do it all over again! All your progress gets nullified in an instant. The game is a constant grind, to kill the same enemies you killed, over and over again. It is the whole point of the game. To kill the same stuff over and over until you level high enough to tackle the more difficult stuff.

The premise is cool. Sometime in the future, you apply for a position in a company that sounds awesome, and shortly after, everything goes to hell. You get a cool exoskeleton just like in Elysium(a movie that looked cool , but overall was laughably bad) and have to fight through hoards of enemies to find out what happened, meanwhile upgrading your suit, weapons etc. But you cannot enjoy any of it, because you constantly get slaughtered by constantly respawning enemies! Its extremely frustrating and annoying.

The bosses in this game are extremely hard. Their attacks are very random, there's hardly any pattern to it, which results to dying to bosses non stop. I had to do each boss about 50 times over until I got lucky enough to defeat it. That's right. FIFTY times over to defeat EACH boss. Challenging games are nice, but impossibly difficult games are not.
I seen many you-tube videos of people defeating bosses with ease on consoles, but on a PC, this game plays very different. Perhaps it was designed for consoles only and the PC port is just this flawed.

Combat is pretty clunky, and although there are cool finishing moves, it doesn't feel very smooth or intuitive. Overall, as much as I would like love it, I DO NOT recommend you spend your money on this game unless it's like $10 for both core game and DLC together!\t\t\t\t\t
