\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is from the perspective of someone who has 1000+ hours across the souls series on every platform.

Tl;dr - Solid game, but wait for a sale. Feels incomplete, mostly attributed to a small number of bosses and a repetitive feeling from enemies and some environments. If you don't have a ps4 to get Bloodborne or Nioh, than it would be more than fair to pay full price if you need to feed a craving for some "Dead Space Souls" fun.

+Souls-like combat
+Spooky dystopian setting
+Good use of lighting, ambience, and music
+Exoskeletons are cool
+Large amount of weapons and armor to collect, some which looks amazing.

-5 Bosses
-3 Environments
-Inconsistent Weapon Staggering
-No character creator, stuck as Average Joe.
-You will be at a disadvantage not using "Rigged" type gear.

It's an okay game. It doesn't feel complete, 5 bosses in a 20 hour campaign. The only thing that extends the length of the game is looking for items, unlocking doors with large power requirements in early areas. I'm sure if I got the game for free I'd still be playing it. Having the keep count of your minutes spent playing the game so you don't go over the automatic refund time doesn't speak well.

I've watched ~20 hours of gameplay from early access streamers and I've seen 3/5 bosses, and 5 enemy types in the first three areas. The third and the first area are also nearly identical. The game itself feels souls-like. The combat can go from realistic to ridiculous in one attack. You have dashing attacks that superman punch 30 feet away, and you have normal attacks which feel heavy and damaging. The quickstep/dodge also feels out of place in this setting.

The ability to lock on to specific body parts also removes the ability to target swap, having you lock off and re lock on a new target(since writing this review, I have found out you can target swap. The method is weird, but it's possible); which in some scenarios is quite annoying and leaves you at a disadvantage. It has platforming elements, but all are likely to use your humongous jump that doesnt even seem feasable in the exoskeleton you just moments early put a broken battery into to power on.

This game tries very hard to improve upon Lords of the Fallens failure, and it does just that. It falls short of something spectacular, but its a solid game worth playing. It IS NOT worth the $65 CAD price, however. Wait for a 50% off sale, if you want to purchase. Im gonna go get my refund and wait for Nier Automata to go on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
