\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI originally uninstalled and started the refund process for this game, then decided to give it one last go. I'm glad I did because it finally "clicked" and it's been a hell of a lot of fun.

The Surge is the best Souls-like I've played yet, by quite a wide margin. Combat is fun and rewarding with a good balance of complexity (targeting limbs, using abilities, etc) and simplicity so you don't get overwhelmed. It really flows when you get the hang of it. Very good. It's not too easy or too difficult; you can mow through entire levels without taking much or any damage if you git gud, and take just enough punishment when you mess up. The AI is consistent enough that you can run into situations and make plans up on the fly, and they work. The controls are perfect, and you have just enough time to react to unexpected attacks (even while already in a fight) that you can adapt and deal with the problem. These moments just make it all the more rewarding. There is nothing "clunky" about the controls despite what you may initially think. Give it a bit of time. Slow down and enjoy the environments. Maybe re-map your controls to better suit you. When you get the hang of it, it's great.

There are very few cheap deaths in this game. The camera is for the most part very good and the bosses so far (I'm 3 in) are extremely fair, just like the regular enemies. Everything is easy to read and you have all of the tools you need to win, and enough space to fight in.

Story is a little sparse but interesting. There are audio logs, Doom 3 style. Atmosphere is nice... kind of surreal, slightly creepy sometimes. You can progress as quickly or slowly as you like and backtrack all the way to the beginning if you want, which is nice. Graphics are beautiful and performance is excellent. Weapons are great, the upgrade system is great, and the item/ability system is great. It's a great game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm recommending this but you have to know what you're in for before you go in.

The games biggest issue is its crappy lock-on. The way it tracks enemies (including certain bosses) can be abysmal, especially given the game's penchant for tight spaces and its camera's inability to contend with such spaces. I defeated one boss by not locking on it at all in its final phase. I have no idea what it was doing above my head but at least I could see what I was doing and that clinched me the win.

Keep the camera's limits in mind as you play, and that will help enormously.

The Surge also has a punishing difficulty curve that absolutely drops through the floor by the end of the game *if* you select certain implant and weapon combinations. I used what I'm calling the COMPLY stick (you'll see why) because it murdered everything in the game and allowed me to buff my tank build in other ways. Won't spoil how, though. That's up to you!

The game can feel like a ungodly maze at first, but there really aren't that many branching paths. Keep running the loops, learn where the turns are, and explore. You'll find items, get in-game speech and so on to cue that you're on the right track.

And don't forget there is nothing wrong with not fighting everything if you're tired. Running through like a wannabe kamikaze is always an option. You'll get a chance to farm relatively easy yet high-worth enemies towards the end of the game if you really want to, but I didn't find that to be necessary.

The Surge also has a narrative to lead you forward. It's decent enough, if rote. I will say I prefer this to other games in the genre trying to be way more cryptic than necessary. Surge strikes a good balance for me.

TL;DR This game will frustrate you and yet you will want more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are so many things i love about this game.
-The Style: I Love the industrial style of the game. I enjoy the different exo-armors that you get and love thier aesthetics.
-The Combat: It's more simple than Nioh, but more complicated that Dark Souls. You have two buttons for attack (not counting the sprint move and dash attack) vertical and horizontal, combinig the two will give you different attacks that vary from weapon style to style.
-The Characters: I actually remember who they are and give a crap about their situation rather than forgetting them 5 minutes after I met them.
----This is basically a prelude to the TWO things that make The Surge an absolute chore to beat.
1.- The bosses: Every boss you fight is going to be exponentially worse than the last one. Deck 13 will take every fun thing they learned from the last boss and toss it out the window only to take the worse parts and refine them into even worse mechanics, which they will double down on and slap onto the next boss. I becomes the worst sort of feeling. Where you no longer want to beat the next boss to proclaim your superiority over the game but just to end your suffering.
2.- The level design: For about 80% of the game the level design is really good! Interesting places, cool sets only occasionally super irritating enemies that make you want to eat your fingers; but over all awesome!
Until you get to the last stretch of the game.
Then it stops giving a ♥♥♥♥.
You'll have the most irritating enemies stacked in onw spot with a boss that you've fought before as a cherry on top.
The last level contains more enemy variety than the last 70% of the game and they are all there to just be as irritating and frustrating as possible. It feels like the devs to a "How not to do this" class and then did the opposite of everything.
Seriously. ♥♥♥♥ the last 2 areas and ♥♥♥♥ everyone who signed off on it.
I had a great time for 90% of the game, but the last 2 areas and especially the last boss gave such a poor impression that I would advise skipping this game, despite all the cool weapons, armours, locations, and characters, ♥♥♥♥♥♥ design decisions just crash the whole experience into one of frustrated fury.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a soft / just barely recommend. It runs and looks fine, but it has some issues. I would like to say up-front that I'm terrible at these kinds of games. I lost count of how many times P.A.X. (the first boss) killed me. I did grind myself up and take him down eventually but it took an embarrassing amount of time.

After that, the game was too easy except those things that can insta-kill you. They get you the first time and maybe the second until you've seen all their tricks. Once the surprise is gone, it's not bad.


+Great fighting
+Using enemy parts for upgrades and new weapons is fun
+Looks and runs well on my sub-optimal machine

-My kingdom for a map! I'm probably just terrible with directions or remembering where I'm supposed to go if I don't play this every day.
-The story is literally your character running around asking people what happened and no one knows.

I had a good time fighting and doing the upgrades but I really hated the circular map segments that keep looping back to base/OPS.




That concludes the review portion...now on to the ranting and raving section. Stop reading if you don't want spoilers.




I did play through it twice hoping it would make more sense the second time around but it was the definition of insanity. The only thing that makes sense about this game is that nothing makes sense. It's the embodiment of "we're all mad here". This whole game feels like it was built then the devs asked 3-5 people to come up with some interesting story. Instead of picking the best one they just chopped them up and sprinkled the story bits throughout the game. It can be whatever you want or nothing at all. Maybe they just asked one schizophrenic to come up with the story? That makes the most sense really.

You have scientists creating toxic waste to save the planet. This leads to other scientists who want to save the planet by killing most of the people. In order to save more people, he wants to turn them into machines. Makes sense, right?

You have a medical hologram (like DS9?) named Sally that can't heal you. However, there is an audio log from Mallory that came before you talking about how all of her healed injuries came back after she told the hologram to ♥♥♥♥ off. So the medical hologram can hurt you but not heal you. Great.

Speaking of those that came before you, there are hints that others are leaping through the same flaming hoops as your main character. Why not team up? Why doesn't Sally ever mention she has other people doing the same things? A few people randomly mention "three blind mice" and that just adds to the lab rat in a maze feeling with the lack of a map. Not sure if they were trying to do some "no strings on me" Ultron crap or what.

Another thing missing is time. There is no concept of time. How long has everything been screwed up? Most of the stuff is automated. There are several audio logs about working the employees more and more so they have to rely on their rigs. Are these people being worked to death? How long have they been like that? The emergency log things go up to 96 hours past "the Surge" but you have no timestamp to know when the log was created. The crazy scientist lady that wants to fix Resolve has been gone for years according to Dr. Frankenstein and some other audio log.

Speaking of crazy scientist lady, who is writing the notes on the walls? At first I thought it was the crazy scientist lady or her minions as most of them are stuff like "stop CREO", "she is the cure", and "once you join CREO you'll never leave" but then there's stuff like "Earth doesn't need Resolve" which is exactly the project she's trying to fix (less toxic waste) to save the planet. Also there are a few messages in blood... what whacko is writing those? It can't be the run-of-the-mill psychos that are trying to kill you. I thought maybe it was Mallory as she mentions "Dr. Frankenstein stuff going on" with the cyborgs and there is a message saying "Dr. Frankenstein you're next" but there is one that shows up in an area you've cleared already if you go back that says "Sally lies lies lies". As Mallory is ahead of you, who did that one and why? As usual, more questions than answers throughout.

So finally, you achieve what Sally desperately wants. You lift the lockdown. NOW she tells us that she initiated a reboot a while back and then the surge happened and she was thrown out of the system. Still, no one knows how or why the surge happened. With the lockdown lifted, Sally can enter the board room which paradoxically leads to her demise but not before she defibrillates the last remaining board member to death which also starts the doomday countdown to launch what will end 95% of humanity. She helps the last board member and most of the human race to death. Is there anything Sally can't do? Great job!

So once you get to the end, you get to decide if you want to corrupt the Utopia program or not. This doesn't really seem to make any difference as the options seem to be quick Utopia spread or slow. it's like asking someone if they want to die quickly or slowly. They're still dead, but hey, you gave them options.

If you listen to all the audio things and all the billboards and you're really paying attention and trying to string it all together by this point, you might suspect that Jonah (the CEO) is the bad guy. You don't know how he did it, but he somehow orchestrated everything. He told the board to meet to vote on Utopia (he's not there in the boardroom) then he went to the launchpad himself to unleash the beast, causing the surge and killing the board. It's possible.

The story is so holy it's like Swiss cheese in church on a Sunday. Then you beat the boss and see your guy crawling back to his wheelchair he was in when he started this whole ordeal. The wheelchair that can't possibly be anywhere near the launchpad. The end.\t\t\t\t\t
