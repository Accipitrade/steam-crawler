\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved this game. The setting, the gameplay, the progression system, all is great. But the one thing that killed the whole game to me is the Level Design.

One on hand, I really want to praise the devs for creating complex levels with lots of hidden stuff for me to explore. But on another - they overkilled this initiative, brutally. The first level is okay. It has its fair share of hidden things, and it plays just great. The problems start at the level two, and what is most devastating to me - they evolve every next level onwards. The Labyrinthisism (If there is such word in English language) reaches its terminal state in the last location and if you could survive the pain of "wtf am I going?" of the previous levels, then here it smashes your balls with a hammer.

All these maintenance corridors, turns right and left, elevators down and up, they are EVERYWHERE, on Every. Stupid. Corner. And all of them take you somewhere! No map where you can see your placement by the way. So I mean - I get what the devs were after - they wanted to create an interesting level design so I could explore these locations to the living ♥♥♥♥ of them. But they drove it out of proportion to the extent where I cannot recommend this game.

You can make it through the game if you play it without taking a break. Then maybe, your short-term memory will help you out. But if you take a break somewhere in the middle, good luck with the pathfinding. Its gonna take some time. Oh and you'll probably die in the process and be revived somewhere else.

I am sorry, Deck 13. You made an outstanding piece of art and a very engaging game. But I lost my positive review on it somewhere in one of the maintenance shafts on level 2, right next to the corridor which leads to the seventh exolift. You can find it there.\t\t\t\t\t

I don't know how it scores in difficulty for genre veterans, but it was hard af. And I want to thank Deck13 and Focus Entertainment for setting me into the journey with Souls-likes. Finishing this game was one of the best feelings in gaming I've ever had!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like the idea of this game, that you need to cut of certain parts to get what you need, brilliant idea!

I also like the idea that stronger enemies can block your path and make it a hassle to go that way, or to show you that you're not supposed to go there yet.

That quickly loses its charm when all other enemies in the game can one-shot you.
It gets tiring really quick.

The combat overall is brilliant and engaging, but at times it is tiring when you feel that you're constantly one mistake from starting over, even at full health.

The world design is great, and frustrating. Seeing as it is set in a factory it makes a lot of the environment samey and hard to navigate at times as it is a lack of distinction there at times. But on the other hand, the amount of shortcuts and pathway leading to places is great and you always feel a rush of relief when finding one.

Bosses are creative and fits well in the world, although it falls flat on human sized bosses. They become trivial as you can approach them as any other enemy in the game.

Buy it if you like a soulslike combat with a twist.
But for me its a soulslike combat that misses the point of fairnes at times.

But I still recommend it for a challenging and a unique sci-fi soulslike experience.\t\t\t\t\t
