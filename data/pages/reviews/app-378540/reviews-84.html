\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fan of all things character-action, I went into this with high hopes. I've played every game in the Dark Souls vein that I could get my hands on, and none have disappointed me as thoroughly as The Surge.

Even the fastest of weapons feel slow and clunky, with the larger ones being so cumbersome it's near impossible to extract any enjoyment from using one. Not only are enemies all but unfazed by your strikes, but the attack patterns your character perform are random. Stripping down the consistency of the game to exclusively refer to the lack of satisfaction from combat.

The characters are generic, shallow, and most offensively: Bland. Interactions feel like an excuse for the shambles of a plot to continue more than anything, and maps are designed to facilitate the boring and linear nature of the game.

The UI is somehow both a cluttered information-overdrive and lacks anything useful, with many icons that are incredibly similar to one another. Especially for consumable items. It gets even worse with targeting enemies, where they have multiple targetable points and frequently pulse. This plays into the poor controls, where attempting to switch between said points is a taxing chore with very little reward involved.

TL;DR - 3/10, the intro cutscene was actually terrifying but so was playing the game.\t\t\t\t\t

But there are so many things that made me enjoy this game more.
Combat is FAST and brutal, mobs can finish you in a few hits if you're not careful and most of the weapons feel sooo good to play. you can make super fast builds with high stamina and WHOOSH around enemies like a cool mofo.
the implant system is great and really opens up a few hours into the game when you get more slots, i found myself constantly going back to the medbay and changing implants; mostly when i got my ass kicked.

the level design is great, but that's probably a taste thing. I love huge futuristic factories and lady announcers telling me to board the next train. DECK13 had a vibe they were going for and they nailed it IMO.

Overall, a great ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ game that surprised me. I am so ready for The Surge 2.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRight off the bat, the main reason I'm writing a review and NOT recomending The Surge is do to its stability, or lack there of.
While in some of the levels, I can play for two, three, or four hours between crashes, or in the starting level where I've never gotten one, other levels seem to have more troubling crash rates. One level in particular, I found myself often having the graphics drivers crash within 2 to 5 minutes of loading back up and playing in it. So if you want a game to just play and enjoy... you MIGHT be completely screwed over and find you can't realistically beat this game to do it crashing on you. Searching around I've found no fix for my issue, but its clear that a lot of others have serious issues too.

For the game itself, stability aside.

PROS:
-Interesting setting, decent attempt at a sci fi alternative to Dark Souls. Some of the characters and story is pretty good, though definitely not all of it. Still, I enjoyed trying to learn about the world.
-Combat is overall fairly decent. You have various weapons, and various options for attacking, and enemies you can learn to fight without taking any hits in most cases if you are careful. So it can be enjoyable for awhile.
-Visually, it's pretty good. Not great textures or standout effects by todays standards, though not bad, but rather it's the envioronments have some pretty cool areas and designs. That said it is a lot of repetitive corridors and maintenance tunnels too, generally lacking big open areas or places with scenic views like Dark Souls.


CONS:
-The equipment... In Dark Souls I had numerous weapons to use, all with their own styles, own elements, some with special effects or traits... In the surge it all plays pretty much the same. It's just a matter of attack speed, and stagger chance per hit, as well as the weapons reach. No weapon really stands out or plays especially uniquley, no weapon really brings about new options for combat. There's no magic, no bows, no bombs, no shields, just short range melee weapon to bash enemies with. The move sets and animations are a bit different, but that's about it generally. Slow ones require more timing and jumping back, fast ones require constant hits before jumping back (they just take longer to deal damage overall) when the enemy makes their own attack. Nothing feels as extreme as say the large hammers in dark souls, versus a simple dagger, it's different but almost superficially so.
-Stability, the game crashes very often in some areas. At its core, this is my biggest fault with the game for fairly obvious reasons since I might spend a full hour with only like 20 minutes of gameplay time made, and half of that redoing stuff from before it last saved.
-The world, while it is somewhat interesting, it does still lack depth. Most things feel like they are directly fed to you if you just walk slightly off the main path, down a very obvious side corridor to find it. Souls games have hidden areas, lots of bits of lore to learn and intuit on your own, interesting characters that tend to seem more relevant to the story or world, and also a world itself that is just awesome and full of so many far out there types of fantastical things. In The Surge, it's too samey, you get used to most of it fast, there's not much to make you go "WoW" or to take your breath away. There's not much to find by exploring around or looking for secret areas in the game, be it in lore or meaningful and interesting items that could affect gameplay.
-Breaking limbs for resources. This is an interesting method of farming for resources. It's kinda cool at first, but it's got some problems. First, it gets REALLY repetitive after the hundredth time. Second, there is only really only 4 resources to get, legs, arms, torso, head, and in a couple different "levels" depending on the enemies equipment level. So there's no variation, just fighting lots of enemies of X level to get all the parts you need. No unique parts or variety in the crafting, it's all just linear upgrades with the same parts used on all armor or weapons, just in slightly different amounts sometimes.
And as a side part, enemy placement. Do to this system, you need to fight enemies of the level you need to make or upgrade equipment, but all equipment is made as level one. So you must go back to the starting area, and grind and grind away at the same couple super easy enemies constantly in order to have enough level one limb parts to make the new armor or weapons you find as you progress through the game. That's right, get a cool new piece of kit (that honestly won't make much difference to gameplay), and you likely need to run back to the starting area and grind for half an hour to make the new set of armor or such. This part seriously slowed down my experience and made me dislike the core of their equipment system. So in the end I disliked equipment both on the side of making and managing it, and on the utilizing it sides.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty nice clone of Souls-games, which has its own features and a lot of shortcomings. It is recommended exclusively to fans of the genre, although those interested can also stop by.

Positive traits:
1) The limb clipping system allows you to choose a drop from mobs or kill them faster. Towards the end, it’s especially interesting because it allows you to understand how resurrected enemies will change.
2) Normal keyboard control. In general, normal, not counting the system of blocks and injections.
3) A lot of nice rewards. Increased drop, if not treated for a long time. The best weapon from bosses if you kill them without using their weaknesses. The ability to increase the drop (closer to the end of the first walkthrough) if you use fewer modifications.
4) A little bit of humor and references to mass culture.
5) No intro and explanation! Only hardcore! Gameplay five minutes after the first launch! The plot in the form of audio recordings only to those who really want to understand.

Negative traits:
1) Target capture system. Works with a 50% chance. It works at a distance of 100 meters, it does not work at arm's length. And without it, the blow, even if it hits the enemy visually, most likely will not cause damage.
2) Beats with a keystroke. For many types of strokes, you need to hold down 2-3 keys and press the hit button. But in 1/3 of the cases, the game considers one of the buttons not pressed and does not strike the blow you intended. This usually leads to instant death. Actual for the keyboard and for the gamepad.
3) A lot of bugs. The camera lives on its own. Either follows the character, then looks into the void, then looks at the enemy, then simply does nothing and does not respond to control. Departures sometimes happen (one for 30 hours).
4) The absence of long-range weapons, despite the fact that enemies are constantly equipped with it, and it is far from always possible to block / dodge it. Plus, constant flying enemies that can not be reached.
5) Sensible input-lag. Often a character simply does not respond to control until it is too late.
6) Lack of explanation of the combo system. There are combos, they are very powerful, but nowhere is it explained how to carry them out and what they generally are. Usually, a message “out of time” appears on a successfully completed combo, even if everything is in order and the combo is executed.
7) Oddities of control. For example, during a block, you cannot change the target. This results in a jump / squat. (???)

Features:
1) Add-ons ruin the game balance. In no case do you need to pass them before the first walkthrough (well, or before it becomes boring), otherwise the difficulty drops to zero. Their walkthrough gives simply a huge amount of resources and great modifications in unlimited quantities.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely loved this game. It is definitely a one of a kind game. Yes, it does take core aspects from other games, but everything else about it is unique. The story line (what there is) is unique, the environment is unique, the enemies and the way that you gather your resources are one of a kind. I highly recommend to anyone that likes a challenging, but extremely rewarding game. 8/10

Bugs:
I did not come across a single bug in the game. It worked flawless. 10/10

Graphics:
OMGoodness the graphics were amazing!!!! I spent so much time looking around at the environment. The attention to detail and amount of items scattered in the world really brought the atmosphere/world to life. The lighting in the game was fantastic as well....very realistic. 10/10

Level Design:
The developers created a mechanical universe that was in shambles....and they did an incredible job. You can definitely see the industrial/futuristic world that you are going through. There are some levels that becomes mazes but you will eventually figure out how the shortcuts work and can go to point A to B in no time. I love looking at an area and seeing that was the area I just went through. Brings depth. 10/10

Story:
The story line is incredibly vague. I mean you really have to pay attention to audio logs, TV screens, and what not to know what is going on. In the end I understood it but it did take a long time for me to figure it out. I wish the developers would not have made the story line this vague. Pay attention to all logs......5/10

Game play:
I hate to compare but this is a mixture of Darksouls and Bloodborne. You can tank, be light on your feet in and out attacks, or a mixture of both. There are tons of weapons and armors to pick from. Anyone's play style is welcome (love the selection). The way you have to dismember your enemies to collect their weapons/armors is awesome. It does get extremely hard in areas and you will die A LOT. Prepare yourself for this.....learn the move sets 10/10

Upgrades/Skills Tree:
There is no skill tree. You simply collect items and upgrade your armor, RIG, and weapons. Doing so increases the defense and damage you have. Upgrading your RIG does allow you to insert "implants". Some are manually used (health, strength, speed boost, slow time, etc.), while others are passive items. You find better items as you play. Very simple but very effective. 10/10

Enemies:
You will fight the same enemies again, and again, and again. This is mostly due to the farming aspect of the game. This personally did not bother me as I knew it would happen. It happens in all games like this....you farm. Going to new areas always did include multiple new variations of enemies and I was always on my toes preparing for what was around the corner next. 9/10

Replayibility:
Since there is NG+ in The Surge you are able to replay the game as much as you want. You will keep finding better and better items to upgrade all your equipment to the maximum. I did not personally go into NG+ but I am sure there are "new" items that were not in the original game. 10/10

Complaints:
- Some parts of the game are pitch black. You cannot see anything. The light on your armor does not light an enormous area. So it is hard to see. Especially if you are fighting an enemy.....in a tunnel. Wish they made the armor lights brighter.........PERSONAL COMPLAINT
- Spent lots of time in technician tunnels which are small and the camera angles are not the best. I understand why they are in there but dern......you spend a bunch of time in these..........PERSONAL COMPLAINT
- Just like in any FromSoftware game.... the fact that an enemy can start an attack...I dodge to the side....they spin during the attack and hit me is STUPID!!! They should not be able to rotate 90 degrees after they start the swing.....STOP DOING THIS!!!!

If you are into Sci-Fi at all, and enjoy a challenging but extremely rewarding game then I again highly recommend The Surge. I could not stop thinking about this game once I started to play it. GREAT GAME!!! 10/10

**Looking forward to The Surge II**\t\t\t\t\t
