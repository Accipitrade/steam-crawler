\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI beat this game once and after ~40h I can make a my 2c of it.
First of all, it's a souls-like game. This will either attract or push away players immediately.
For me it was the Dark Souls + SciFi that made it so great to play. I loved every second of this game and every death of my character. The story was kind of shadowed by the gameplay mechanics and sadly I couldn't really enjoy it that much.
It plays great, at a fluent 60fps, with pretty industrial / scifi environments and models. I literally had 0 technical problems.
Overall the difficulty of the game drops as you are approaching the final area. Probably because you die so many times that you are inclined to memorize and react to enemy patterns which are not that diverse. Still, attacking 2+ enemies will probably get you killed in the early phases. Also the RPG segment was done nicely, as you can affect your characteristics by equipping different gear and implants.
I think the game is pretty well balanced to satisfy that hunger for beating the odds. The effort you put in is properly rewarded.
This game is must-play for any fan of the genre.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is an industrial soulslike game where you gain weapons, armor, and upgrades by chopping off enemies' limbs, a combat feature which never gets old. It also gives the player agency over what materials they farm, which is a fantastic idea to implement in future games. The graphics are good, the art design is solid, and the soulslike inspiration feels earned.

There are plenty of problems holding it back though. Keyboard and mouse is a nightmare. It's a short game with just 5 bosses, and only 2 of those are memorable. Combat is fun, but trying to target specific parts is clunky and annoying. Environments and enemies are so repetitive. Finally, Deck 13 doesn't seem to grasp the fine line between difficult, challenging gameplay and unfair, punishing gameplay. They want every enemy to feel like a boss encounter, and instead it just makes the game a slog.

Finally, the DLC is pure garbage.

Hopefully Deck 13 continues to refine and balance their gameplay, and not fall into that elitist mindset which tips some soulslike franchises into meme territory.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter finishing Dark Souls I was looking for a "Souls like" experience and scored The Surge and The Surge 2 on a Steam sale. And I don't regret the purchase.

The Surge is not really a Dark Souls mainly because it isn't as difficult. A better way to put it is it is not as frustrating as Dark Souls and is very reasonable with its level design. It does not have the variety of enemies Souls has though I did not mind that because it is a much shorter game and one can end it in about 35 hours of game play. The story of the game is interesting, told mostly through a number of audio logs that you find around the game and hence is a lot more lore friendly in defining the game world and establishing the story that went down well with me.

At the core of the game play is exploration. Basically there is a base called the "OP" centre and once has to keep finding different shortcuts to the base as one progresses through the level. There are a few weapons, but there are only about 4-5 weapon types. Each weapon type has its own combos. Combat is different from Souls because its all melee combat. There are no ranged weapons, though you do have a drone that can attack from a range.

The graphics are very nice. They were perfect coming from dark, drab world of Dark Souls, as there is a lot of colours and the artwork is really nice. There are tons and tons of mangled, twisted metal in this game that has been beautifully rendered. The industrial complex is dark and disturbing in the mess that is left behind, yet beautiful.

I liked the game probably because how much less frustrating it is from Dark Souls and gives one the feeling of achievement while never having had the urge to smash the controller against my monitor. I have read the bad reviews for this game and a lot of them are about being one hit to death. They are by people who have never played a souls like probably and are just whining. I say jump right in and play this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has echoes of Dead Space 1, which was a very special game to me when it came out. Third-person action horror is a great genre when done right. While this doesn't achieve the heights of Dead Space, it was familiar enough to grip me. Besides the atmosphere, the game is actually pretty flawed though.

My biggest gripe is that enemy attack animations magnetize to the player, making dodging and sprinting past them annoying. The second thing is that the combat is designed for 1 vs. 1 however it's extremely common in the latter half of the game to be surrounded and stun-locked to death by multiple enemies at once. The solution to that problem is rather funny: run past all of the enemies.

Because of that, the game becomes a horror game again, where I was running for my life through a large portion of the final third of the game, trying to find which way to go to complete objectives. When it was time to fight the final boss, I picked out a nice grinding spot where I could manage the enemies and prepared for the final encounter. There's new game plus but I think that this game is mostly worth it to play once if you buy it on sale. Everything gets harder in NG+ so if you were bored it would be fun to grind through it for something to do, but it's likely that you have a big enough backlog to make that unnecessary. The pseudo-horror vibe carries the game and made it worthwhile for me.\t\t\t\t\t
