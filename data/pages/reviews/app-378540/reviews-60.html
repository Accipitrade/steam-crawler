\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Weirdly enough, my biggest gripe is with the story, background, and voice acting. This game is asking me to grind through hours and hours of sci fi robot street brawls, it could at least make it interesting. The setting is boring, it's another shady mega-corporation. The rooms are either too dark to be lit by your pathetic flashlight, giant grey squares with nothing in them, or generic outside sandy area. The side characters and their missions are all poorly written. For a game with a surprisingly large amount of NPC's, you feel incredibly alone. And instead of cultivating a sense of mystique, a "what happened to these people, are they weird because of what's going on, are they robots, is this a trick?" you're left feeling "wow what a bunch of weirdos, I hope the monsters get you." The voice acting is very poor and never conveys emotion, which is a shame since the story is pretty much "piece it together yourself through these audio logs!" And despite that intent, there's a lot of "tell, don't show". An audio log that amounts to "oh no, I'm so startled, what's happening is so startling, this is why I am startled" is just silly. Don't tell me "the earth is messed up", make that clear! This story had a tremendous amount of potential and just does not deliver. A grind is fine if it's fun. Otherwise it's just wasted time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA Step In The Right Direction

Both Surge and Nioh are games that have replicated the Soulsborne series well, and with their owns unique spins.

Major Differences From Soulsborne:

-Visceral dismemberment system

-Unique weapon and armor acquistion system via dismemberment

-Eerie, dark corporate atmosphere

-A Metroidvania-lite back tracking system for items

-Perk-like customization system to change consumables or permanent stats

-Combat energy mechanic to use drone/consumables/executes on enemies

-Faster paced combat, closer to DS3, rather than DS1/DS2.

My completion time was 36ish hours with most exploration done. But without most back tracking.

Overall, keeping it short, and concise if you like Soulsborne games, you will most likely enjoy this game, if not refund, not much to lose especially on sale, this game is great.\t\t\t\t\t
