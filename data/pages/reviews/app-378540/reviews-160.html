\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a big fan of the Souls series I was excited for this game. I played through and had a decent time with Lords of the Fallen so I had come to expect a Souls like that might be a bit slower and rougher around the edges. However, that is not this game.

The most critical failure of the game is the combat. It feels slow and sometimes downright unresponsive. Slower weapons have extremely long wind-ups and consume laughably large amounts of stamina. Fast weapons have decent attack speed and stamina consumption but have no stagger and will allow enemies to power through your attacks.

The previous issues could be ignored were it not for the addition of forced combos and poor hitboxes. I would often find myself trying to hit an enemy twice only to find my character swinging for a third time and taking an enemy attack that I had intended to dodge away from. Couple that with the poor hitboxes where often swinging at an enemy directly ahead will result in a miss and recieve an attack from an enemy that you would've otherwise staggered.

In short the acceptance of death from your own failures in a Souls title gives way to frusteration of death from poor mechanics in this title. Instead of being hard but rewarding, this game leaves you feeling like you only won because things happened to fall into place.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot recommend this title in its current state. There are some changes that could be made to improve it, and if that ever happens it will become a recommendation from me. The game captures the dark souls experience in a crude way, but misses all the nuances that soulslike will have to really recapture the feelings that games like dark souls 1 captured.

The balancing and improvement system for weapons and armor is really skewed. The weapon classes lack advantages/disadvantages for each class, and there are some that are just head and shoulders better than others in every way. Armor bonuses are simple "x% better/worse stat with y protection stat" and don't seem to make a noticeable difference.

Progression in terms of improvement of gear is very simple, requiring you to simply have enough scrap and spare parts to upgrade gear. However, this is gated extremely hard by the fact that the spare parts required don't drop until you are in the right area. Ex: To upgrade sword mk1 to sword mk2 you need upgrade part mk2. This continues with mk3 and mk4. These higher mk parts cannot be acquired until a certain area of the game, meaning your gear is essentially locked to a certain upgrade mk until you can progress. This wouldn't be a problem if the enemies weren't simply the same types with higher scaled numbers. This means that instead of feeling better with your upgrades, you feel like you are just on par. Not upgrading means you will just die faster/not kill as fast. The titanite system in souls allows you to focus your improvements, and the enemy types are varied and can involve timing and outplay to win fights. This is just simply not the case in this.

Overall, this is a very rough-around-the-edges soulslike. There are simple balance changes that could be made revolving around weapons and armors (upgrading, advantages/disadvantages of classes) to improve the experience overall and give a more rewarding feeling to playing. However, the lack of enemy diversity is a big problem, especially with how it comes down to simply more hp/damage instead of varied attack patterns and learning. As it stands right now, you go into a new area and get devastated simply because your gear is scaled for the last area and have to grind until you are back on par. This is not enjoyable once you fall into this routine and does NOT capture the core feeling one gets from a soulslike.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI should probably point out that, despite my eventual dismay about this game, I actually enjoyed the first 5-10 hours of The Surge. However, in the end, the game delivers merely an idea, and fails to live up to most of the sums of its parts.

The idea within the combat system of targetting body parts, and your moveset being different depending on what you are targetting is pretty cool. It was very well realized in the first two levels with the normal enemies. However, as the game goes on you begin to realize barely any of the combos and attack tactics will ever work. Most enemies later on can only be taken on with cheesy methods of dash stabbing, slide tackling, and general hit and run tedium. There is no robust parry and evasion (you can dash, but i-frames are terrible, and blocking is so clunky and unresponsive, with a terrible add-on of having a jump and duck mechanic which is borderline impossible to utilize) system, especially on the enemy's part. Instead, the developers just load them up with adamantine poise levels, and the magic ability to hit way faster than you ever can, with the force of Thor's hammer, staggering you to kingdom come. Later on you get enemies firing flamethrowers and grenades, while you hopelessly try to close the gap with your melee weapons, and they insta kick you down chunking half your health... bleh.

Character progression is so horrible it's soul destroying. Upgrading is tragically linear and gated, there aren't enough weapons, and even if there were, there is a terrible weapon proficiency mechanic (presumably added when the devs realized there would be no motivation to actually kill mobs anymore i.e: the only part of the game worth a damn) that more or less limits you to one weapon type.

Level design I found very interesting with the shortcut unlocking, but the design is so uniform and indistinctive that from the second level onward you will have no notion of where you are going, and out of frustration will probably miss areas. There really should have been a metroidvania style map here.

All in all, there is just no drive for the player to keep coming back once he or she hits the brick wall of tedium. The combat system needs reworking, and I just wish Deck 13 would hire someone to make a more robust and responsive sense of melee combat that works within some semblence of balance. Even if you could find your way to enjoying the stilted rules of combat here, there is nothing else driving you forward other than just plodding your way through the environment to get to the end of the game.

The Surge is a really good idea on paper, with a pretty tragic implementation.

Edit 26/5 -

Apparently changes have been made to the combat so I'm going to give the game another run in due time.

Meanwhile, I have stopped looking at review comments and won't be engaging any further. Thanks for anyone who has had an inch of common sense in their replies. For the git gud crew, I'll just copy and paste an earlier reply here --

"My frustration doesn't come from the difficulty, it comes from the methods to which you succeed, which is by circumventing the majority of the combat system as intended. Enemies are designed as such that you can never utilize it. I don't like it because there was clearly a robust and nuanced system here, completely thrown away by bad design, making it far less enjoyable. And that's what I want to do - enjoy the game while succeeding, not be bored while succeeding. Being bad doesn't come into it."\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who hasn't played the from software games for more than a couple of hours (mostly because of the horrid pc port of the first dark souls and because of how clunky keyboard controls were in those) I find this to be a nice point of entry in the genre. Unlike the games it was inspired from, the pc port is good and the game is perfectly playable with keyboard and mouse.

the good:
- the combat feels... right. The character moves as you'd expect and there is quite a bit of depth to weapon, armor and movement interaction.
- I personally enjoy the theme, it has a nice sci fi horror vibe and you get a friggin exoskeleton.
- YOU GET AN EXOSKELETON
- the level design is complex and ingenious, with plenty to explore and different ways to approach certain encounters
- the enemies are quite varied and the bosses are intimidating

the meh:
- I encountered some bugs, nothing gamebreaking but in one occasion I got stuck in an area that I could access but not escape. Fortunately quitting and loading the game placed me very close to where I got stuck but outside the dreaded area.
- occasionally the framerate dips, I have yet to understand what causes this but I believe it has something to do with the loading of some areas. It generally doesn't happen during combat.
- the music in the OPS centers (bonfires) gets annoying after a while
- the ingame crafting and equipment menus are a bit clunky, it's not always clear what the advantages of certain weapons and pieces of armor are until you wear them and check the full stats. You can also craft things that are completely useless (for example a second chest piece identical to the one you have), the game will not warn you and you'll have wasted resources.

Most of these are pet peeves and minor problems in my opinion so the game gets a solid recommendation from me.

-post-patch update-

The update fixed a lot of issues (none gamebreaking but still) and in particular the block mechanic is much more usable now. This change reduces much of the frustration some of the more challenging enemies offered while still not making the game trivial. Now it's perfectly viable to stand still and parry an attack rather than just having to dodge everything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a solid game, it has it's faults but it is well worth a playthrough.

Combat is great, the weapons each have their pros and cons.
Story is interesting if you take the time to listen to the audio logs and hunt down the bits of lore that are throughout the levels.
The enemies may at certain points seem overwhelming or even unfair at times but if you take the time to learn their movesets, there is ways to deal with any and every enemy efficiently. This type of thing is why I love the souls series and games like them.

My only real gripes about this game are as follows,
At certain points in the game (I can think of 2 specifically) that how you progress through the level is really confusing and gets to the point of frustration (at least for me). The level design in general is solid, shortcuts are plentiful, its just these certain places it seems a bit non-sensical as to how they expect you to navigate.
The loot system is unique and I enjoyed collecting the sets of exo suits and weapons.

The only other complaint I have is lack of bosses, I enjoyed the boss fights that were there, I just wanted more of them.

7.5/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like dark souls for the combat, DO NOT buy this game, it is horrible.
Enemies have no sense of consistency, Especially when it comes to stagger. You can stagger an enemy, then 1 hit after that, he insta attacks without any windup attack.
Another bad thing about the combat is the combo system. You have 2 attack modes, vertical and horezontal, does the same amount of damage buf if you combo them you can do different attack animation. The only problem being every attack past the first press, is insanely dangerous and there is no benifit. This mean to play effectivly and without risking everything on the enemy actually being staggered, you get to kill the enemy slightly faster.
Otherwise the best tactic is to jump attack an enemy then move back, again and again until the fight is over.
To further emphesise how ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up the combo system is, ill give the example of the duel wield weapons... ALL OF THEM, if you press Right trigger 3 times you do 3 pretty quick attacks, good, easy to dodge the almost instant enemy attacks. However if you press 1 more time, just 1 more time, you are then locked into a 4 or 5 hit combo, that have no stagger on enemiers. Couple this with the fact that with the agile exo suit, you can take about 3-4 hits from weak enemies and stronger enemies 2 shot you.
Most of the enemies are the same dude with either dual wield or hammer, but those that arent are just frustrating to fight against. One of these is a robot scrapper, it has a huge shield infront and has perfect tracking when not attacking. This means the only way to do more thant 5 dmg is to wait for 2 of around 6 attacks, and hope to god it isent standing somewhere you ant get behind it. Then try and dodge, run or shimmy your way behind it to get an attack or 2, Repeat 5-6 times, then fight the next one. No incentivy to try and out play it, there is no other way to defeat it.
Most of the enemies also have tracking when attacking, even enemies that jump at you will move mid air and hit you if you dodge a bit too soon. And since your dodge is about 5 cm and dosent show any indication of i-frames, its a pure luck if you actually dodge something or not.
This coupled with the massive input delay, since it seems like you have to start your animation before the next input is registered, you might change direction and dodge just to have your guy dodge in the direction you were previously moving. I even experienced my guy walking foward while holding back and dodgeing, makeing me take extra dmg from the second boss.
This all makes the combat a chore.

The rest of the game is a mess too. There is almost no story, and im 5 hours in. You might say thats like DarkSouls, but where the characters you meet there might reveal just a bit of the world, none of the 4 characters ive met does so. All of them are like "Cant remember anything plz help me".
The levels are appawling, everything in the same areas look the same, there is no way to tell if you are closer or further away from your "bonfire". This mean its a complete gamble if you spot a shortcut or not. This combined with the stale and unintuitive combat, makes getting back once dead a complete chore. They also had the bright idea to have a timer on your corpse, so if take too long its gone. This makes you just wanna run past all the enemies, especially since the combat is so bad.
Furthermore, the levels are chuck full of holes. you walk somewhere and there is a grate that isent there? boom you are dead. Since its all industrail and there is seethrough grates everywhere you cant see it. Most of the shortcuts also have a TINY lift that you cant see is up or not, if it isent you are almost sucked into the hole underneath it and usually die instantly since there is a lot of fall damage. And then you have to run allllll the way back.

All in all this game just took way too many things from darksouls and ruined them with stupid decicions that makes the game one big fight against the controls, and just hoping to god that your character dosent do some stupid ♥♥♥♥ that gets you instantly killed so you have to go thought the same ♥♥♥♥ again.

And if someone is still reading, and wondering, i have played all 3 dark souls, and even beat the first one useing mouse and keyboard, so i have experience with the genre, and ♥♥♥♥♥♥ controls, but even then DS1 with Keyboard and mouse was better than this garbageæ.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't like the Souls games a lot. I only played the Third because I wanted to try it.
A: It didn't hook me on stories
B: Missions were too complex to complete
C: Farming for souls got too repetitive. Borderlands did a better job.

Not many people may agree with me, but that is how I felt.
NOW ONTO THE THE SURGE!

Pros:
-Fluid combat
-Pace depends on playstyle of a variety of people
-Graphics are great on PC max settings
-Difficult bosses (I even upgraded myself more than I thought I needed but still had some trouble on the first boss)
-Variety of enemies and locations
-Deep crafting system
-High risk, high reward with Tech Scrap
-Limb combat is great
-Dogging has more depth to it that DS
-Optional "Cutscenes" give more freedom to a player

Cons:
-Stamina is used while blocking
-Not as many bosses as I hoped like in Bloodborne
-No character customization (But I don't mind to be honest)
-The "secret finding implant"s sound is very annoying and doesn't help when trying to enjoy the game

I personally love this game more than DS and I would recommend it to anyone who likes RPGs.\t\t\t\t\t
