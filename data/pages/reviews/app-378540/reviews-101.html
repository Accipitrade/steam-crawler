\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan, I really like this game. Yes, it's like Dark Souls, but better in some ways, IMO.

The most accurate games you can compare it to are Bloodborne and Monster Hunter.

Bloodborne in that dodging is more important than blocking and Monster Hunter because of how you farm equipment upgrades (which I love...see next paragraph).

In this game you can target individual enemy limbs for either more damage or to build armor sets and it adds a really fun layer to the tactics of the game.

Unlike Bloodborne and Dark Souls, in The Surge, you can fully customize your character/build with mods and implants that go into you "rig". Strategizing your build to maximize your power level is a lot of fun.

Personally, I go for speed in my offense and a larger health bar, but you could easily build a great number of nuanced rig sets, but for me, speed is key.

Unlike DS and BB, in The Surge, you load into individual levels with ONLY ONE "safe area". You won't be finding more of them in each level.

Also unlike DS and BB, when you die and drop your tech scrap (souls), you have a limited amount of time to get to them before they disappear forever so that's an added layer of challenge.

Good times, bruh!

UPDATE - 7/12/18

I just finished the game and all of the above still rings true, BUT want to throw out 1 negative of the game that got annoying....the maps (specifically the last 2) get crazy complex.

All in all, to me, this was a good bit harder than Bloodborne which, again to me, got easy after Father Gascoigne which is in the first 1/4 of the game.

Good game from a small studio and will move on to NG+ soon!\t\t\t\t\t

Small design details like that really help flesh out the narrative, which makes it kind of strange that things get so confusing and some times frustrating.

One major plus are the Exo Suits and the custom options/selection available. You'll get new parts by combating other exo suits, the trick is you have to not only defeat them... but focus on a part to get said part. there is an decent cat and mouse game you play as you start to look for parts to enhance your Exo-suit or like the collector I am.... going back for the full set and weapons. (man I miss Armored Core soooo much).

ooh yeah, DEATH is something you will see a lot. When it eventually happens, you'll have a timer to get back to that location and collect the scrap (the currency in this game) you've dropped. Failure to do so means a loss. The combat is actually really good in this game. it does not get boring. Every encounter keeps you on your toes and proceeding with caution. Over time you'll get confident... but don't get cocky!

I'm about half way through this so I'll add more to this review when I get to the end. Also... you are in for a treat at the beginning of the game. (You will see it as soon as you have control of your character) I have yet to see a lead protagonist with this much detail before the game starts. So far it's a Solid 8, bonus points for the colorful display and the great finishers. (each specific to the part of the body you are focused on).

I was just in formed that there is a sequel coming... that's great news. I hope they remedy the story pitfalls and lack of a MAP feature in the new entry. This could be a great new series if they button up a few things. That's it for now, see you at the end.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one hell of a good game, plain and simple. As a massive fan of souls-like games, this is definitely up there as probably my third or fourth favorite I've played. Though comparable to Dark Souls, this game does plenty on its own to set itself apart from what it derives so much from.

The combat is a touch faster than the Souls series, with plenty of combos to try out, the standard light and heavy attacks being replaced with horizontal and vertical swipes of similar speeds, a dash attack that's wildly fun to use, and a quickstep dodge that keeps the action nice and fast paced. It's almost more of a hack n' slash than a souls-like at times, and though it isn't brutally hard, combat is still extremely satisfying and a blast in general.

Stats in the game are dictated entirely by equipment, with levels only acting to increase your equip load. This allows for a lot of variety and flexibility in how you want to play. The gathering of equipment is also, of course, very interesting as well. Armor, with the exception of the very first two pieces you get, is not simply collected. You need to craft it yourself, with materials and schematics coming from literally chopping the armor off of the enemies you fight. This also gives combat an interesting dynamic, as armored parts of the body have more solid defense than unarmored parts, but unarmored parts give no reward for being chopped off. Weapons and their upgrades are similar, but a bit more simplistic, as weapons are directly collected from random items and chopped limbs, but they still need upgrades. The upgrade system as a whole is rather straightforward, but effective.

What makes this game really shine is its level design. Though the game only has about 6 areas, and only five bosses in total, the game makes up for it by making each boss pretty good and every area massive and complex. You can spend hours searching in one area, exploring every branching path, opening every locked door, and generally just trying to get to the end. Though the game sometimes struggles to give you a proper sense of direction, chances are just about anything can be found with enough exploration, which is already fun to do. The biggest downsides are that it can be borderline confusing at times, and the game sort of overdoes the metallic and cold factory look. However, this only slightly interferes with the overall experience, and the game remains fun.

The story is all over the place, and I'm not sure what to say about it. Having audio logs tell most of the story seems cool and all, but needing to stop and listen to something every now and then can get really tiresome. The story itself is vague beyond belief, and there's a lot of grey area on who caused what and what the meaning behind everything is. Hell, the reason the world is the way it is now is never even explained, and neither is the global impact of what's happening. The final boss is especially confusing, and though it's all cool, it'll leave you questioning a lot of things. I'm not sure if this is good storytelling or just plain lazy, and I honestly don't know how to feel about it, so I really won't take a stance on it. I guess if Bloodborne got away with it, so can this.

Overall, this game was a great addition to my souls-like collection. It was fun to play from beginning to end, and the most remarkable part is that Deck13 developed it all. I can't believe the people who made the somewhat fun but still hugely mediocre Lords of the Fallen went on to make something this good. They've really matured a lot as developers, taking what worked from their rather uninspired and poorly executed ideas and removing what didn't to make something much more original and better overall. A fantastic game, and a huge boost in quality from the developer's previous outing with this sub-genre of sorts.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI thoroughly enjoyed it. It has some minor flaws but if you're looking for a unique, difficult, and atmospheric sci-fi game then this one will scratch the itch.

You can over-level and just powerbomb your way through the game if it gets too difficult as well. The best tip for combat is to simply slide-attack your way in to hit, back out, and repeat. That mechanic almost trivializes some boss fights and encounters so you never really feel truly stuck. If you are getting one-hit killed by a boss then it means you need to level up or swap your mods to increase your health. One-hit KOs are not normal.

It's quite the fun game and I don't know why I loved it so much. The combat isn't as polished as Dark Souls but it controls much more smoothly than Witcher 3. Its plot amount is basically Dark Souls with about double the NPC dialogue.

I also loved the CREO World DLC. My first playthrough had the DLC in it already and it really was a lot of fun. Since it's available about 20% of the way through the game you can sneak in there and get some powerful stuff fairly early on. It's meant to be completed within the base game and not at the very end of it. Which is a little weird for DLC... But awesome if it's your first playthrough and you have it.

Bottom Line: I recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
