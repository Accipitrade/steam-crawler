\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is an interesting and engaging game. It has the exploration and item management that make the player want to see the world they're are currently in. It is mostly about the visceral, melee combat and strategic use of blocking and dodging. I liked the armour and weapon sets. However after a time many of the weapons seemed to feel a bit similar to each other in their class. I did like the variety of weapons and combat styles too. Also the armour variants are good, but much like the weapons, after a time you will never use certain pieces.
The story is pretty good for a game of this genre. It is told through a minimalist manner, with the player finding audio logs and seeing small infomercial type broadcasts about the levels. It adds a great deal to the world building and lore. I liked the attention to detail that deck13 put into their world.

It can have a steep learning curve and it will surly frustrate new players having to return to the med station after a death in the level. However, it is a game that rewards and encourages exploration.

The player customisation system is a bit lacking as well, it is definitely improved in the sequel.

All in all, if you enjoy a 3rd person extreme combat game with diverse weapons and armor as well as a pretty pessimistic story about the folly of unchecked scientific ambition, then The Surge is for you.\t\t\t\t\t
