\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game. Don't dismiss it at first just because you hear "It's just dark souls with power armor."

I don't like Dark Souls. I get lost easily in the large open world, the game is rather unforgiving with the perma -HP each time you die and become more undead unless you use items to become human again. Also not fond of how weighted the combat is. You have to commit to every swing, if you make a mistake those combat animations are very long and you're gonna get face raped by that mistake.

The Surge has a more fluid and less weighted combat system that feels slightly more forgiving than Dark Souls because it gives you shorter combat animations so you get a slight ammount of accident forgiveness and a slightly more increased time to react to enemy punishment. Also the game doesn't overly punish you for dying other than losing all your "Scrap" (EXP) which you have a timer to get back to get. The Game is challenging, but fair. It punishes you, but doesn't feel overwhelming.

If you like Salt and Sanctuary, but for some reason you're not a fan of Dark Souls (like me), this might be up your alley. Strap on your power suits lads, it's time for a fight!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPS: Play this like any game out of the Souls franchise and prepare to be infuriated. This isn't a game where enemies can be ignored or AI quirks abused. Every enemy can do meaningful damage, and the game almost feels designed to punish some styles of play common to the Souls franchise.

The tactical playstyle is fun, as it emphasizes your options for taking apart an oponent (literally, in many cases) and allows you to create strategies to deal with each opponent you encounter. The enemies you deal with are surprisingly varied for the setting and circumstances, but not nearly as wide ranging as the franchise this game takes it's pointers from.

Levels are laid out in surprisingly intuitive loops connected by criss-crossing paths that make excellent use of the vertical space - all connected by shortcuts and accessways that make the sprawling industrial complex feel positively enormous.

The story is interesting, and while it is somewhat standard sci-fi fare, it is interesting in the way it's presented.

TL;DR: It's a game that takes a lot from the Souls franchise - but playing it like a Souls game will garner quick rage-quits and innacturate reviews: Because you played the game wrong.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is that of a cyberpunk Dark Soul Fan like myself has been kinda wanting.

Please note I have more experience in Bloodborne than Dark Souls

This game misses it by just a inch of being a VERY good Souls-Vania (Yes, I named the genre that). Yes does it have the difficulty and the risk of losing your cash, yes it has complicated bosses and a vast array of secrets and exploration, but still some of it seems off, yet It's quite hard to explain what is.

The game graphicly is quite stunning, but quite demanding. I am running a somewhat low-end PC (GTX 645) at the writing of this review.

I am a big cyberpunk fan, and the art style of this game is right up my alley, and tickles all my sweet spots. With a lack of guns, it still has a Dark Souls feel of hack'n'slash fighting.

I truly am still having fun with this game, and still deciding to buy the DLC and if to preorder the sequal.

If you love Dark Souls/Bloodborne, at least give this a shot. it might just make your week!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been thinking about this game a lot lately since I beat it last week, and also heard the wonderful news of a The Surge 2.

I see a lot of criticisms that the combat is "clunky". That may be true, I don't really know what "clunky" means. The attacks do not occur instantly when you press the button which is what I think a lot of people expect. You have to compensate for that. I like the way it feels, it gives your attacks a sense of weight, and you have to make them count. This game is not a hack and slash.

I honestly find the combat addicting. The limb targeting system is great, gives you a ton of control. And I think it's cool that there's no guarantee that you'll hit the limb you're targeting, that your success depends on the type of attack you throw. Finishing moves are incredibly satisfying, especially if you toss on Aggression Amplifier and get a little health boost from tearing the head off a zombot.

The story is arguably flimsy, but I love Don Hackett and CREO. It cracks me up. Ironmaus is great. The voice acting is weak too but in a charming way. And I love the OPS song, it grows on you. If you start to hate it there are instructions on how to change it, and there's an option for turning it off.

That's another thing, the options menu is next level. Extremely descriptive and not cryptic, and there's an option for just about anything.

Another criticism I see is the lack of weapon diversity. Your stock of weapons actually starts to pile up quite a bit, to be honest, but like a lot of people have said the movesets don't change much if at all within the classes, and you often get pigeonholed into using the most powerful weapon in the class you choose to stick with. I don't really mind that too much, it opens it up for like one playthrough per weapon class, or lets you choose just a couple to mess around with. Doesn't have to be Dark Souls level of diversity. The same goes for armor, there are only two starting options, but you're able to steal the armor off of later enemies which I think is fun.

The game does get compared to Dark Souls, and for good reason, but I think to expect this game to provide everything that Dark Souls provides is unrealistic and unfair. The Surge expands on what Dark Souls created, but it doesn't feel like it's trying to be Dark Souls, or ride on its coattails. It is its own independent game and I think it executes itself really well.

I do hate most of the bosses, except for the Cerberus when he isn't throwing out PAX units (the first boss of the game) and watching from behind the safety of a glass pane and the second form of the final boss. A complaint I saw as well was that there weren't enough bosses, but I thought there were too many. I enjoyed fighting the normal enemies so much that I honestly didn't wanna try to solve those Rubik's Cubes that can one-shot you.

There are also complaints about the need to grind in the game. I get that. But I found grinding in this game to relatively quick, and still fresh despite going the same routes over and over. I think my favorite part of the game was getting my character up to 105 core power in the Executive Forum, unlocking all the implant slots, and messing around with different implant configurations.

I think this game deserves more praise, and I look forward to the sequel come 2019.\t\t\t\t\t

But you'll have to make it back to an Ops centre with your tech scrapsif you want to keep upgrading! Well, beating a boss that you have been combating for hours is pretty thrilling as well.

I would say that the only really weak area for me was the ending... after 30 hours of hard grinding (I'm sure people beat it in less, but remember I'm a newb to this style of game) I would have liked a bit more of a reward. Still, I give this a big thumbs up and think you should give this game your considration!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThese deck 13 games just rub me the wrong way. They try to emulate the souls like gameplay but I think they just fail at it. It’s not just the combat and the mechanics built around the combat like losing your souls when you die that makes Dark Souls appealing, it’s also navigating the beautiful environments and the amazing level design that keeps it feeling fresh and special. It’s world is intriguing and worth investing your time into. This game doesn’t offer much in terms of story or world building, it feels underdeveloped. The level design isn’t great it all has a samy feeling to it. It’s just linear corridors in an industrial setting that never changes things up, that’s not fun to play.

The combat is an improvement over Lords of the Fallen and the game in general is an improvement over that game but that’s not saying much because I thought that game was genuinely bad. The combat in this game when fighting normal enemies feels pretty decent. The attacks feel weighty and impactful and the dodging feels pretty good, but the difficulty feels artificial, it’s trying to be difficult on purpose and it just comes across as obnoxious. The game doesn’t do anything to make me want to overcome its challenges because I don’t feel like the challenges are genuine, it just comes across as forced, especially in boss fights which I thought were awful. It’s customization is very limited, it doesn’t really offer different playstyles or builds, more like singular progression by acquiring new gear so you can’t really experiment. The game feels very hollow and shallow for me, it comes across as wanting to copy the success of a much better game.
5/10 (Average)\t\t\t\t\t
