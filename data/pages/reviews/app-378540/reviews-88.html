\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is solid as hell. If you're a Soulsborne fan and need something to hold you over until Sekiro, I absolutely recommend The Surge. It puts a new spin on the formula and the combat is stellar. The game is gorgeous and it runs extremely smooth. It is without a doubt one of the most beautiful and most smooth games I have ever seen.

It is a little difficult to get the hang of the game at first with the unique system of combat and the armor upgrade system. There's also a new spin on the "estus flask" system which can be tricky to figure out as well. I'm pretty sure I ended up fighting the 1st boss while decently underpowered and made it all the way to the second area before the armor and weapon upgrades system clicked. The sharp increase in difficulty in area 2 led me believe I had to be missing something and I most certainly was. I had not been taking advantage of all that the armor system had to offer so it's important to remember to stay on top of that.

Pros:
+Amazing graphics and unlocked FPS
+Great level/character/weapon/enemy design
+Fluid combat (all melee but a variety of weapons fast and slow, heavy and light)
+Captivating and dark story
+Full support for PS4 Dualshock controller including on-screen button prompts. I've never seen that before

Cons:
-Minor bugs when using a controller. The mouse drifts and the game can lose focus (same exact thing happened in DS3).
-Actually can be kind of scary. There are some pitch black corridors and the human enemies are zombie-esque (story will explain that).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHeres the short and sweet of it. Darksouls except with mechs. what more do you need to know? buy it. its great.

Those that have played darksouls: this game IS NOT AS HARD as darksouls. but is very similar. You can "bank" scrap (scrap is currency used to level up) so the punishment for death is not nearly as big of a deal as dark souls. there is no health penality for dying mulitiple times.

the only con I have for this game is that it feels fluid like dark souls...but it just feels "a bit off" the mechanics of combat arent as fluid as dark souls and at times it feels "a bit clunky" the sticky targeting leaves a lot to be desired when trying to target a specific body part (especially when they are running at you) and i find myself having to focus more on dodging (which is very clunky even in light builds) while i try to target the body part i need to attack (or want to attack)

so far im passed the tutorial level as well as the second level and the story seems pretty underwelming but i suppose its not a deal breaker for me. i personally would not label this an RPG. please dont buy this if you are looking for a great story. (ill update this if my oppion changes as i get further.)

TL:DR= this game is darksouls but futuristic, combat is a bit clunky (compared to dark souls combat) and the story isnt great. I recomend this game. (especially if its on sale)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn adequate "Cyberpunk Souls" and that is not meant as derision. The limb targeting system is well implemented, the environments are substantially more vibrant than most of the maps From puts out, and the story is a decent twist on the "evil megacorp". That said, it can definitely overstay its welcome, especially for players who adapt to enemy patterns, since enemy placement is much less of a focus here than it is in stronger Souls-likes, leading to a very jagged difficulty curve.

That said, its shortcomings only stand out because it's compared to other really good games, and for the current price it is a nice sci-fi flavoured Dark Souls.\t\t\t\t\t
