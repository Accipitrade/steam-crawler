\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell this is s**t; I heard it wasn't great but it did well enough for a second game, so I gave it a go.

I really don't know how but the movement is odd; you go from slow walking to basically teleporting with the dodge. The dodge is also not reliable sometimes it works, sometimes does nothing and sometimes gets you stuck in terrain. Also due to the dodge having almost no invun and the large arch of some enemies the only good dodge is a double dodge backwards.

There are a few nice mechanics; the equipment and how you get it is kinda nice but quickly gets repetitive. Managing the enhancements are nice but a tier 3 adding 20+ health doesn't really matter when enemies hit for 30+ with quick attacks. The drone is rather nice but it misses most of the time-wasting energy and it doesn't do much; mainly been using it to try and get one enemy from a group.

While I like the equipment system and how it works [its probably due to [Morrowind nestalga] the execution feels wrong. Why am I going to equip an item when its states are increase the stamina cost of dodging by +10% for a tiny defense boost. It seems better to find what you like get the set and have the set bonus.

Weapon proficiencies; slowly improve the type of weapon you are using. I really like this your weapon will constantly improve slowly over time from this so you even if you stick to a weapon it will still improve.

Fighting in the game feels bland; you dodge you wait for them to finish their several wide attacks and then you stagger lock them until they die. So far the bosses I have seen are all gimmick fights; they can only be hurt at certain places or times and if they are doing certain actions they get invun because why not.

While in this game you can die rather quickly don't worry; there are shortcuts everywhere and there is a bank for your money so as long as you have some patients you won't be running risking everything all the time. However the money doesn't have massive value; instead of leveling you upgrade capacity. Upgrading some mods scale with capacity and you will need this to equip your armour and mods but that kinda it. The other main reason to improve your capacity is unlocking some doors as they will require you to have a high capacity to unlock them.

Not sure why but there are areas and some you need to walk through where there is environmental damage spots on the ground; you can't avoid all of them and some you can't really see until you notice the numbers coming off you telling you how much damage your taking.

There are also some glitches with the game; been hit/ rolled into walls causing the physics to be odd. And also noticed some audio desync at times. Have also had some random latency spikes which I cannot figure out. Set the game from very high to high/medium and still get latency at times (although it is rather rare at least).

While I dislike this game I will finish them through the headaches and annoyances. Just pray it gets better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS. GAME. IS. AWESOME.
I bought this with The Surge 2 (which I have yet to Play)
even for a severe lack of story, as a souls-like this game is AWESOME
while I am waiting for The Elden Ring to release (since the Demon Souls Remake is not coming to PC, cause some marketing team workers are just real stupid) I bought this game, as it was highly rated as a souls-like.

This is basically Sci-Fi Dark Souls, it is awesome, atmospheric, good music, not as good as Dark Souls 3, the only real downside, is sometimes the camera is too close to the character, or I just cannot see and I walk off a ledge that wasn't there. I have not encountered any game breaking glitches, or any glitches as a matter of fact.. but this game takes combat into a cool area where you can target specific areas (Legs, arms, head or chest) and do finishers, which are ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ AWESOME, If dark souls had finishers, god, that would be awesome, id love to decapitate my foes in Dark Souls like I can in this game.
The locations are nice too, and some of the friendly NPC's you come across are kinda interesting, though I wish there was more to them, but, eh, not a big deal for me, less talk means more action and exploration!

I am expecting the next one to be a better game, The Surge 2 that is, but I have not played it yet.
This game, as its own entity, is worth getting. It isn't as confusing as dark souls is, until later, the lack of story is not that big of a deal, and if there is story, it really doesn't display it proudly, but the combat is so good, and the fact you basically are a mech-human is so cool. the weapons are cool, the armors are cool, you can basically make your own Bionicle Human. The bosses are good, though, not as good as Dark Souls, and not as many as Dark Souls, but This game is a solid 9.5/10 from me. BUY IT.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust like Creo the surge was rushed to release and deck 12 went for quick and easy solutions. Many small shortcomings that could easily be overlooked if it were one or two here and there, but all of them combined left a bad taste in my mouth and i hate quitting games so late in. Several core features were frustrating such as enemy mechanics, camera work, and general control schemes for weapons.
"Levels too easy for the QA testers? just throw in some cheap shots from the enemy! Need a feature that rivals similar games? just copy what looks the closest! No one cares about actual functionality."
The map design while intricate and fun the first time through, was a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ headache looking for specific things to which the maps in the game were absolutely of no use navigation wise.
Speaking of navigation, near the end any attempt to guide the player completely fell off and i had to circle back on a hunch that they left some direction out. i ended up talking to an npc telling me to look for a person only to have already found them dead and that i already did what was asked of me next.

Out of an honest rating id give the game in total id say a 5/10.
Graphics and stories are great but not everyone wants to watch a video about a very pretty, useless, turd.
Glad the writers threw some sucker punches at elon though, it was the only thing that really kept me going.\t\t\t\t\t
