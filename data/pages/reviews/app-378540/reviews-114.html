\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI truly don't get all the hate around this game, its great. If you grew up playing Ratchet and Clank(the ps2 ones) I really think you'll like this game. The weapons are so well designed, and have great combos. I am not one to ask about how difficult the game is, but to me coming from Monster Hunter its pretty easy, the enemys broadcast their attacks very early on, and thus they are easy to avoid. The combos and dodging the attacks mixed with the awesome enviromental design and atmosphere make it a really really enjoyable game. IMO the company has a really "megacorp" feel.

One of the first games I've played in a while that I have wanted to keep playing and I have gotten excited about while playing. The targeting part system is very inovative, essentially who you fight have armour and some of their body parts don't have armour, ie legs head etc, and you can target those weak spots with a lock on thing. You can also target specific body parts to get certain weapons and stuff. Its very much like monster hunter too in the sense that you fight enemys, get certain parts, then upgrade the parts.

The player is very fluid, and there was no studdering etc. The game looks beautiful. TBH I'm not really one for horror games, and the intro kinda threw me off, it was kinda unnessecary, but once I started playing it was a joy.

Good Job Devs, can't wait for the second one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbove all other things, this game is satisfying. I'm a huge sucker for weighty combat systems and camer/post/sound effects that add to that weight. The Surge really pulls that off well.

I would argue the level design in here is more solid--or at least more consistent, than Dark-Souls. Everything had that "inter-connected" feel that people praise the first few areas in Dark-Souls for. This game is also much shorter, sure, but that also means the team can keep up that consistency of maze-like level design I love.

Lots and lots of weapons in here to enjoy and play around with. Sadly it looks like most of them share very similar attack animations, making the only choice "What looks cooler" (Though this can also be considered a good thing)

Bosses where all tough but nothing was as hard to get through as some of the hardest bosses in Souls games. They all still feel really satisfying to finally defeat. They're all amazingly intimidating and this fact is only amplified by this game's really really good sound-design work. There's a very narrow dynamic-range (Range between the loudest and quietest sounds) on sounds but in this case it serves the game well (Bosses who make a lot of noise will often drown out everything else, which gets pretty un-nverving to say the least). I have around 28 hours in game, and haven't beaten the DLC yet. I'm not bad at souls games but not particularly good at them either. For referance I beat the first boss on my second try, but had to try upwards of 10 or so times to beat the second. I'm saying this because--as is to be expected with these games, your time out of it will change drastically depending on how good you are.

Graphics are great and I could run the game on max settings with a 960, I'm sure you could run this on lowest with a solid APU if need be.

Some things in the story felt strange or looked over. I personally wanted more reaction out of Warren, be it from getting his legs back to slowly finding out story bits. It just felt entirely underwhelming. The voice work was great and I have no complaints there however.

The music is almost non-existant and the Medbay music is so infuriatingly catchy you'll either hate it and turn it off after 2 visits or set it to always play and max out the volume on it like I did.

Overall this game is hard to outright reccomend because I'm sure some people would just get mad at it and attack the game itself for their own failing. If you're into this kind of game however, go for it. I really enjoyed myself.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a massive soulsborne fan I was hesitant to play this game after playing a few hours of Lords of the Fallen. I wasn't a big fan of that game at all. Surge, however, is so different. It's still a soulsborne clone but they seem to be figuring out their own style. Where LotF was a medieval game of the same genre and mechanics as Dark Souls, this game sets itself apart in theme, story telling, and even a few really neat mechanics. On top of that, there's a ton of gear to pick from, lots of progression on multiple fronts and so many neat enemies. Not the normal fantasy spider or suit of armor but some genuinely neat enemies. It's definitely easier than Dark Souls or Bloodborne but that isn't a bad thing. This game was so much fun all the way through and it managed to hold an interesting story very well. Not the most original story but they did it in a cool way. I cannot wait for Surge 2. For those that care, I bought the game for $20 and then the DLC for $7.50. I just over 25 hours into my first playthrough. I wholeheartedly recommend it at that price and if you can't wait, I'd get it for the full $40 too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDa Surge.

What's good about it?
It's like Dark Souls.

What's bad about it?
It's not Dark Souls.

The similarities between Souls games and The Surge are extremely easy to notice pretty early into the game. However, a seasoned veteran of getting their anuses violently (and apparently pleasently) enlarged by Souls games, will immediately realize a distinct difference between the two games.

This game's easy.
(I feel like it's important to note at this time that there are two types of gamers. Those gamers who feel Souls games are too hard... and those of us ♥♥♥♥♥ who got gud. I got gud.)

That's the first thing I noticed, anyway. It felt a lot like being in the mid to late game of a Souls game and deciding to go back to earlier areas just for a change of pace while you dominate and take revenge on those enemies you thought were so hard before. I loved doing that - just going back to the beginning of the game and playing my way back to wherever I was - it was almost therepeutic.

Most of the time, The Surge felt just like that.

Well, what about the rest of the time? The rest of the time the game felt like only the utmost ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ moments of Souls games. You know those moments... when you're fighting a dude and some random undead just RKOs you outta nowhere. It's frustrating, it's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, and most of the time, there was nothing you can do about that. The Surge does this - A LOT - and it felt very irritating.

An example, if I may: I approach a very dark hallway. I notice some LEDs floating in the darkness very vaguely in the shape of a humanoid and hear the familiar zombie-like sounds of the common humanoid enemies. "Aha!" I might exclaim. "I have spotted you, villanous cur!" and then proceed to lock, choose a juicy body part to focus my attacks on, and have at it. EXCEPT I CAN'T SEE WHAT'S HAPPENING. THE LEDS STRAPPED TO THE DUDE'S BODY DON'T HELP AND THE LITTLE LAMPS STUCK TO MY SHOULDERS DEFINITELY DON'T HELP EITHER. So, I naturally take several hits from a combo I can't see and just simply die. Even when you can see the enemies, the animations can be very difficult to read and you'll take damage for absolutely no reason. FRUSTRATING.

I'd say about 50% of the way through this game, I felt it offensive to call it a Soul-Like. The Surge doesn't have anywhere near the scope that Souls games have. Souls games have little swords, big swords, really big swords, sticks, hammers, knives, daggers, scythes, bows, crossbows, idols, wands, staffs, magic stuff, fire, poison, shields, parrying, riposte, numbers, and things we don't even know what they mean! WTF is poise?! I know what poise is, but damn if I didn't go a really long time without knowing.

The Surge just has like five weapon types, a few armors, and mostly pointless ranged attacks that probably do more harm than good. It's so limited in scope that the game appears a lot simpler. So even at the very best of times, The Surge ends up getting pretty repetitive. I had no interest in starting a New Game + because I was already bored of the game.

I suppose, however, the most important thing about The Surge is that though it's much simpler in scope than Souls games, with its handful of different enemy types, its very few gameplay mechanics, etc., it is still not polished! Souls games weren't exactly perfect, but though they were more complicated, they were still more polished than The Surge. The Surge felt rough around the edges with game mechanics that felt pointless. As I mentioned before, only like two of the ranged attacks available to you in this game are useful. One is simply for pulling agro on one enemy at a time while the other is really only good for ONE enemy type. You can't even try different weapon types because of the weapon proficiency system. I never even bothered with the harder duck'n'hop dodging because it wasn't necessary. Most of the attacks are avoidable by simply maneuvering around them.

Anyway, I'd say that the game feels pretty mediocre. Compared to Souls games, it's dead last. No bueno.
Compared to games in general though.... I'd say it's on the better side of the bell curve, but still near the middle. You can thank all the similarities to Souls games for being on the right side of the bell curve, by the way. (So like C+ for those of you who don't speak goober.)

So I guess compared to the loads of other garbage I've played, this one is at least worth a try if it's on sale or you're just made of money or summin'.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is like Dark Souls with some awesome sci-fi setting and lots of eye candy.

What does that mean?

Dark Souls formula has a couple of specific rules:

1. EVERY enemy in the game can kill you in seconds. Even the easiest peon can get you if you aren't paying attention. As you progress and gain power, these enemies get faster and easier to kill but nothing is ever safe in this game.

2. When you kill stuff and collect XP/Souls/Tech you carry them on your person. If you die, they are lying on the floor where you were killed. This means you need to fight your way back to your body to get that XP back. The Surge has an additional twist, giving you an XP "bonus" for the more you are holding on your person - a risk/reward system for those daring enough to try it.

When you combine parts 1 and 2 you can reach the "difficult" part of this game. Say you get pretty far into a new area and there is a really tough enemy that jumps out and kills you - then you get back to that spot only to die a 2nd time. Now you start to get flustered and start skipping enemies and just trying to get back for that XP cache because it's 3 levels worth at this point, then some dumb monster you've killed 50 times takes a swipe at you and another one jumps and you are dead. 3 levels gone and your controller is through the window.

The levels themselves are long and elaborate, with lots of branching paths, locked doors, and secret passages. These are used as shortcuts to some areas of the level. Typically you will have a "hub" you start in, and you have to make your way though a section until you've unlocked the door/elevator/power grid that enables a shortcut back to base. From then on, you can start at this shortcut and move on making progress through the level unlocking more of these until you've reached the end boss and finished the area.

Combat uses a lock-on mechanism with lots of swipes, feints, jumping in and out. Each enemy has it's own behaviors and one of the tricks of this game is learning how to deal with each type effectively with your chosen weapon. The weapons themselves all have their own flavor of damage, stun, speed and combos and it's worth trying out the various types until you find the one you like. I settled on the single-rigged style of weapons for my first playthrough and enjoy them immensely. One thing I like here is that EVERY weapon is viable and can be used to the end of the game. The first boss drops a nice weapon that I used for about 90% of my playthrough.

The game is VERY pretty. Lots of glowing, sparking weapons, condiuts. There is distortion from gas and various weapons. The metaliic surfaces all reflect the light and just look gorgeous. If you have played any of the Dark Souls and are tired of the various shades of brown and death this is a refreshing look for the genre.

I've just finished my 1st playthrough at about 45 hours and I feel there is a lot of value here. I have not purchased the DLC but I'm thinking about it. I haven't decided yet whether to jump right into new game+ or to give it a break and come back.

Overall this was a great surprise when I found it and I'm very pleased I did.\t\t\t\t\t
