\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeck13 doesn't appear to have learned much of anything from Lords of the Fallen.

The Surge has a competently designed world and story. Exploring and learning what's going on in the game is engaging and entertaining. Most of the environments nail the interconnected, shortcut littered style that Dark Souls fans enjoy. Some of them are a bit samey and mazelike in construction, which can make exploring a chore, but overall I liked them.

The combat is another story. Most enemies have unreasonable amounts of poise, and most encounters are single humanoid enemies at a time. You will do your forward dodge attack, then dodge back, repeatedly, to kill most enemies. Enemy attacks typically have too much horizontal tracking, ambiguous hurtboxes, and low startup to do much of anything else. The forward dodge attack for most weapon types does significant damage, has a fast startup, and a fast recovery. Many enemies utilize the same kinds of weapons and attacks, including some bosses, making combat pretty samey overall throughout the game, with a few exceptions.
Compounding this, there are only five weapon types in the game. Though there are many weapons of each individual type, they're more or less just superior or inferior versions of one-another. Due to the way damage scaling occurs, you're disincentivized from trying different weapon types, because your proficiency will be low and require grinding to get back up to par.
The armor selection is pretty varied, but most pieces offer little additional protection in exchange for significantly hampering your character. There are some locations where the Liquidator (basically a biohazard suit) set is helpful to negate environmental damage, but otherwise I saw no reason to use anything other than Lynx for the majority of the experience, aside from swapping out the arm pieces for parts of a better set towards the end of the game.

Overall I had fun with The Surge, but my time with the game was marred by several frustrating moments, including an instance where my progress was not saved upon exiting the game from the menu. I was ready for it to be done when I killed the final boss.
If it goes on sale for less than twenty dollars, I'd encourage Souls fans looking for something to do to pick it up. Otherwise, pass.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm surprised by how much I've enjoyed this game. I don't actually like the Dark Souls series, so seeing everyone say it was Souls-like (to put it politely) almost put me off. I'm glad it didn't.

+ Neat futuristic, sci-fi setting
+ Looks great and runs even better
+ Addictive, weighty gameplay
+ A good challenge without being too difficult
+ I like the upgrade systems
+ Limb-focused combat is cool
+ The risk/reward nature or staying away from base for too long, and feeling very isolated and at risk when venturing away from it for the first few times on each level
+ Can progress at your own pace, and there's always the fall-back option of grinding slightly better stats or gear if you're struggling with an area (usually a boss)

= Levels can feel quite maze-like at first and it's possible to get lost, but you gradually memorise them
= The bosses aren't the highlight I was hoping/expecting them to be after hearing all the Souls comparisons

- It does become repetitive towards the end for two key reasons: 1) enemy variety is quite limited, and 2) you have to revisit one of the levels (and you do so not once but twice)
- Dis-incentivised to trying out new weapons (you level up your gear class so once you commit to a certain class of gear you're unlikely to ever try out the alternatives in that playthrough)
- Sometimes you may get stuck not knowing where to go or what to do next. I ended up clearing levels entirely of enemies a couple of times, trying to work out what to do next, before resorting to a guide that told me exactly where I needed to go

So I'd definitely recommend it, but ironically probably not to Souls fans, who seem as likely to rip into this as being inferior as they are to say they enjoy it because of the similarities. It stands really well in its own right and I'm glad I decided to give it a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a style of play I have not played before. I was expecting it to be more of a fighting game. There is fighting, but it definitely takes patience. The enemies respawn every time you visit the medical center. You die, a lot. If you die, all the collected material you were carrying lies back at the spot you died. You have a few minutes to go get it, but if you die while trying to get back to it, it is all gone.

Not a gripe, just a frustrating part of the game. It reminds me of Doom, but instead of weapons that shoot, now you have a mech suit and it is hand to hand combat.

I find playing this with a mouse and keyboard to be satisfying. I have not tried the controller yet.

The map is very interesting - there are many ways to get back to the starting point by unlocking doors as you proceed.

There are implants and armor - things you can upgrade. You can customize your load out as you see fit.

It is a game of trial and error. Lots of error. Every time you are killed or go back to the med bay, all the enemies have spawned again. There are shortcuts and ways around them, but an enemy you dispatched quickly in the last round my not go so easily the next time you see them. It is a combination of finding the right implants along with the right weapon for your fighting style. Do you want to fight with a big, slow, heavy weapon that deals maximum damage per hit, or do you want a quick light weapon that will require more skill in dodging and blocking? Do you want to have max health or max power? Do you want to replenish your health via health injections or use the power gained from putting your enemy down? You get farther and farther along, only to turn a corner and be ambushed and put down with two hits. Then, you have to start from the beginning of the level ... again.

My way of playing this is to start out fresh and see how far I can get, getting killed multiple times, learning the layout of the level and unlocking all the doors I can, collecting all the secrets. Once it becomes a grind, I'll exit the game - it will be waiting for me later.

This is a game where you may find yourself playing multiple sessions to try to perfect the right strategy. If you rush, you will die. No matter where you are at in the level, if you die, it is like starting over each time you die or visit the med lab. Each level requires you to be able to maneuver it with a "do not die once" strategy in order to proceed to the next level. I have also come across machines that I cannot kill yet or shortcuts I cannot unlock without a significant portion of grinding to level up. It's a trade off between banking your collected resources and starting again, or pushing it a bit further each time.

Made it to the third level after 40 hours of playing. Finally getting past the second level boss was a challenge. I find energy management to be my weakest part of my strategy. You gain energy from attacks, but it drains quickly. Right now it makes more sense for me to focus on my stamina and health because energy just drops so quickly if you do not use it.\t\t\t\t\t

A refreshing combat system with a unique twist delivering more than just dramatic finishers.

Clever, atmospheric level design with no hand-holding.

Challenging bosses that don't feel punishing for the sake of difficulty (once you learn the mechanics, of course).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnlike other souls-like games, The Surge focuses more on the average enemy rather than the bosses. I've died more to the common enemy by far than the bosses when trying to learn their movesets. The combat in The Surge is unique from other games in souls-like genre due to you being able to target various apendages and whether those apendages have armor or not covering them. After you damage an enemy enough you can have the chance to execute them and possibly cut off the apendage you targetted. After you cut off an apendage you can gain various loot from a schematic for a new part, a new weapon, or crafting material after the first time. Last thing to mention is that the level design is maze like especially in the last level, with many shortcuts interwoven throughout the map.

TL;DR
It's a souls-like game where you fight in exo-suits rather than medieval armor.\t\t\t\t\t
