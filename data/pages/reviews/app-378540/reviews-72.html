\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is initially quite fun. Then it gets terribly monotonous. There is a great deal of fight-grinding to be able to upgrade your gear so you are powerful enough to progress. You will fight the same sequence of enemies in the same locations over and over. And the boss fights are extremely frustrating—not because of how difficult they are. I mean, they are difficult. But after each death, you spawn at the ops center and have to get all the way back to the boss fight area to start over. Ugh... why can't I just spawn back at the boss area?

The game is quite difficult. I liked this at first. It was fun trying to master the numerous combos that are possible. However, as you get deeper into the game, you start to notice that the controls are not very responsive. For example, there is a lag between the time when you finish a combo attack and when you are then able to dodge or block. This leaves you vulnerable momentarily and there is nothing you can do about it. A similar situation is that when you start a combo, you are pretty much locked into it. You can't break out of the combo to dodge or block. You aren't able to respond to the enemy's actions in many cases. So in order to get really good at this game, you have to memorize all the different behaviors of the various enemies and be careful not to commit to a combo that will leave you open to attack. I prefer gameplay that is a little more flexible and dynamic. It gets too frustrating dealing with the limitations of the control inputs. It feels a little like playing a fast-paced game of chess rather than an action RPG after a while.

Overall the game engine is solid and the levels are polished. It's definitely a quality game. But it's just too boring grinding through the same areas time after time and it gets too frustrating dealing with the clunky controls when you start taking on more difficult enemies.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe second soulslike from Deck13 that I've played. A /million/ times better than Lords of the Fallen, but still very far from the standard set by FromSoftware.

Completed the base game in about 24 hours. Not played any DLC. Accidentally left running in menus so Steam hour count is off.

The good:
+ Graphics are very nice and performance is good.
+ Atmosphere is grim and unsettling.
+ Melee combat with regular mobs is generally okay.
+ Most areas are fair in the number and density of mobs.
+ The limb targeting system is pretty neat.
+ The implant system is neat. Similar to the plug-in chips in NieR Automata.

The bad:
- General area design is very poor. Bland and unmemorable, samey, copied and pasted. Gets progressively worse throughout the game. Here's hoping we won't see cable tunnels with exo-lifts repeated over and over in The Surge 2.
- Area scripting is mostly poorly designed. You're often not given a clue as to what changed when pressing a button or overloading a circuit.
- Crafting and upgrading uses resources that do not drop directly (gained by automatically dismantling other loot), adding an unnecessary layer of indirection/confusion.
- Only five bosses.
- The bosses are poor and unmemorable. That said, I'll remember one of them for giving me the feeling that Deck13 saw "The Bed of Chaos" from DS1 and thought "we can do worse!"

5.5/10 - There's probably a better soulslike to play. Would have set a "neutral" recommendation if there was such a thing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the game, overall positive experience and reminiscent of darksouls, however the level design feels too mazey and its wayyy to easy to get lost running around in circles trying to figure out what little thing you need to trigger to continue on your journey. Theres no map, there are "some" maps on objects in game but its not really helpful. Theres also lots of information that could be useful to the player that is just not visible in game. All in all id give the game a 7 out of 10. I got it pretty cheap so no complaints there, ill probably checkout the second game when its on a big discount.

I do however wish the artwork was a bit more intuitive. Its really unrealistic how your exo suits with armor on them, look. They dont cover your entire body and even your hands are uncovered at times, when the weight of what you are holding would crush you, its just very unrealistic. If you want realism, make a frame that supports the weight of the things this person is doing, because the artstyle doesnt show that, its just like random pieces on the guys body that make no sense realistically. You need a frame to support the weight so the person isnt crushed. He needs to be IN the suit, not carrying the suit. The suit needs to be carrying him.\t\t\t\t\t
