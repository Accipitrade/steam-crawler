\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn the year of 2019, there are nearly hundreds of games that do their best to emulate the success, and most importantly, the fun of Dark Souls. Souls-like is a term that gets thrown around often now due to just how many games have tried to capture that feeling right, but most tend to miss the mark. Be it from a lack of proper mechanics, bland worlds or doing all they can to pad the game out as much as possible, few ever actual feel as satisfying as the ever famous Dark Souls franchise.

The Surge, however, is a rare gem unlike much else.

This game, Souls-like as it may be, is unique in ways all of its own that make for a truly amazing experience. Some large changes immediately noticeable come in the form of a limb removal system, giving a whole new meaning to critical hits, and even executions that will never fail to leave you ecstatic...if you're a fan of brutal kills and gore.

On top of this, the way you modify yourself is vastly different than that of your typical Souls-like, coming in the form of mods to your suit itself. Though the progression of some of these feels rather lacking, or at the least, paced strangely, it's nothing that hinders the flow of the game.

In the few negative reviews that you can find for this game, many point out a lack of story telling, or new features to the Souls-like genre. As for the story telling, it is, at the least, what you'd expect from a Souls-like game, while still having moments of far more head on storytelling. I can only assume those harping on the story telling aren't familiar with the genre itself.

As for features, if the the Limb system, executions and suit modding aren't enough to warrant adding anything "new", you also gain a drone. This acts both as a weapon, and a way to gain access to secret areas when used correctly. I can only assume those complaining about a lack of new features didn't get very far.

I'm just starting my third playthrough of this game after having beaten it twice within the first 2 weeks of its release, and immediately, I'm feeling the exact same excitement that I felt my very first time. Noticing small things I didn't catch before, and of course, enjoying the change of pace where you start off in a drastically different way than most of this genre, it's all deeply refreshing.

This is a game that I'd recommend to anyone looking for that fun mix of challenge and rewarding exploration, adding in of course some highly enjoyable gameplay, and an always welcomed, but optional grind for more power.

Obviously, if Souls-like games aren't your cup of tea, this likely won't be either, but despite sharing such close relations, The Surge is one of few games of its kind that manages to set itself apart in a whole new way. This game is most definitely worth the price, full price or otherwise, and knowing that this game is getting a well deserved sequel warms my heart.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first played The Surge when it released in 2017, I very much had a love/hate relationship with it. Now, two years later, having played through a second time I can say for certain that there is no more hate-- this game is great!

Pros
  • The combat is reminiscent of Bloodborne's fast paced action, with, of course, some unique additions. Every weapon class has a whole set of combos to master-- and some special weapons have extra moves for specific combos. Many of these feel fantastic to pull off, and help keep combat from feeling dull despite most enemies being similar. Instead of waiting for enemies' one percent drop chance armor to fall-- you can go right for whatever you want. Like their helmet? Aim and chop. They have a cool looking hammer? Break their arm and slice it off to take it for yourself. Drones add some utility, ranging from things like reducing all damage taken for a small amount of time, to shooting enemies from far away.
  • The world, despite criticism, wonderfully sets the game's tone. The industrial locales aren't full of dull grays and pure concrete, no-- they're all colorful. Each area is accented by a color theme. The biolabs are full of greens, R&D has reds, service tunnels are orange, lights and signs everywhere pepper in other colors-- this is a singular facility, and it feels like it. All roads lead to Central Production B, you'll constantly find yourself winding back here from a new angle, uncovering more as you progress through the game-- aiding more in creating the feeling of a cohesive world.
  • World building from characters you meet along your journey, to audiologs you pop into your onboard ipods-- everything helps build up an entertaining world you'll get sucked into-- wanting to learn more.
  • Implants allow for a variety of builds while also allowing you to switch up your playstyle whenever you want. Scared of dying? Just equip only healing items, nothing's stopping you! Confident? Play a glass cannon; use damage boosting injections, stamina boosts, energy gains, and drone damage increases-- whatever your playstyle you're likely to be able to find something for you.

Cons
  • This game is unfair at times-- sometimes you'll be pitted against an enemy while you're standing on a two yard wide pathway with no guard rails to prevent you from dodging to your doom. Some areas are a bit too dark-- despite your rig's built in flashlight which is only as effective as a phone screen and you'll find yourself walking into danger. An enemy with a OHKO combo may be placed behind a corner that you can't peak behind, only to discover them by the time you round it with your face firmly plastered into the floor. There are particularly difficult late game enemies which constantly heal themselves, while also packing enough of a punch to OHKO. Luckily you can just run by most of them.
  • The free DLC enemies that were added between the first time I played and now are a bit very difficult to fight. The new gear they came with are all welcome, for sure-- but these enemies are equipped with thorough knowledge of the game's combos and pack a wallop. That's not a problem on its own, however-- several of these enemies are placed directly along the game's required pathways-- including some early game ones. This makes the game effectively more difficult than it was at launch. I killed each of them, sure, but I did not much enjoy my encounters with them.

Despite the game's flaws, I still had a good time playing it. I'd recommend it to anyone with cravings for games similar to Bloodborne or looking for a fun dark sci-fi action game.\t\t\t\t\t
