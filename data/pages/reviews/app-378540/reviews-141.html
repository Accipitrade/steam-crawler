\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't get me wrong. I had fun with The Surge. But that fun was very limited and was only had *in spite* of the game design.

The levels are just silly. They're insanely long, but that length is created by repetition. Same kinds of areas, same textures, same enemies. The first level is somewhat inventive, and feels good. But the second and beyond are just labyrinthine, mostly due to the repeated everything.

And damn. I love Dark Souls gameplay and design, but this is just not very well done. The combat is stilted and SLOW. Going from playing Dark Souls 3 to Bloodborne to this was a very jarring experience. The weapon variety is lacking, and the movesets feel incredibly limited. It's possible that I'm just bad at the game, or assuming too much, but given the amount of inspiration this obviously takes from Fromsoft titles, I feel that my experience with similar games gives me the right to reasonably criticize this title.

If you really want to try this sci-fi mech suit Souls-like, get it on sale and don't expect much. The plot is thin and faux-mysterious, the gameplay is repetitive, and the difficulty is all over the place. Oh, and there's no music; just a sparse series of electronic ambient tracks that could be made in five minutes on Ableton Live... which is just sad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*SPOILER FREE REVIEW*

This is one of the first games I've bought in a while that had JUST come out (I typically wait for a steam sale or whatever), and I did so despite some super negative reviews that I read and decided were just salty people who couldn't handle the difficulty. I'm SO thrilled with this game, it is absolutely awesome and delivers on every level as advertised.

I found it to be very difficult but definitely not quite Dark Souls' level of throw-your-controller-wtf-is-going-on. You aren't going to get lost going the wrong way for hours or end up somewhere without a crucial item that you need as long as you explore everywhere you go thoroughly. It was a very nice challenge, and I was able to complete my run-through without looking up anything at all - never got lost very badly, the bosses are all intuitive to fight (and amazing in design!). The gear system is quite brilliant, with how you dismember enemies for their blueprints and then parts to make the armors you see in the world - it too is quite intuitive. Its also worth mentioning that this gear collection system removes the classic sort of "rolling for your loot each time you kill this guy and then respawning him like a billion times to try to get lucky" RNG bullcrap that has permeated action RPGs for too long now. Also, you see a guy, you think his armor is cool? You can go take it. No BS armor sets or weapons that you can't get for yourself or are "badguy only".

I read some reviews that claimed that there was no story - that must be a joke! I mean even if you weren't meticulously exploring everything 100% to get all the audio logs (which pretty clearly spell out what's going on) the environment you are in and the main progression through the game shows and tells the story brilliantly. The settings are beautiful and not dull at all - yes for part of it you are in a factory, but it's huge and awesome and complex! Take some time to look around as you play through and you will find a lot of interesting tidbits, i.e. things scrawled on walls, etc. The story is frankly one of the coolest and most interesting plotlines I've gotten to play through in a long time!

Overall the game is quite fun, due to the layout of the areas you will definitely feel like you are making progress and when you make a mistake and die you are never too far away from your "Tech Scrap" to make it back to pick it up. Once you get in the groove with the combat system you will find yourself flying around fighting multiple enemies at the same time. Again from bad reviews I've read that the combat is "clunky"... well let me be clear, it is NOT clunky at ALL! All the moves are a bit like a fighting game, you need some timing and you might get animation locked if you mash a button too many times but each different weapon has a completely different set of moves, speed and animation so really you just need to find one that fits your playstyle groove. Maybe some fast dagger like weapons, or maybe a big heavy hammer are your style? It won't be too far into the game that you can find what you prefer and get into the deeper combos with your favorite weapon.

Just a really great game. Challenging and hard, but not really quite "WTF IS GOING ON" Dark Souls at any point, you definitely have just enough of an idea of where to go or what to do, but it definitely doesn't baby you or hand hold. The combat and crafting/gear systems are really tight and intuitive and fun. The bosses are incredible. I can't recommend this game hard enough - give it a try, it's amazing!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to love this game. I'm a huge Souls fan and when I saw this gameplay, I was excited to get more gameplay that follows the souls formula. The start of the game was a blast, the story was actually interesting, and it LOOKED really nice. The combat was also exciting and VERY rewarding, and the cut/loot system is fresh and well done.

But oh boy does this game lack polish. The rocket assembly boss is horrendously bugged. It is challenging, and the idea is well implemented, but the execution is awful. Once your target reticle switches to the upper torso, you'll more than likely shoot up and clip into his center torso, making that block/attack trade worthless as you won't do any damage. If you choose to NOT lock him, you'll clip into his legs and have 100 damage done every ~second until you die. And when you DO get him to fall forward for the attack phase, more likely than not, he'll be facing the wall and you won't be able to attack the proper areas to do any damage.

Like I said, I want to love this game. It's a new story that uses a proven combat system with several innovations of its own, and it's great to look at. But don't buy it until you see reviews saying it is completely fixed. Not "runs fine for me" but "yeah, they patched that, doesn't happen anymore"\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall a good Souls-like and a safe-ish followup to Lords of the Fallen with some cool innovation in combat.

My few biggest gripes were the inconsistency in enemy staggertimes and instakills. If you hit most enemies while they are in their stagger animation, the instantly get back up. And some of the hard-hitting enemies do a lot of damage even with lategame (NG) armour and implants. There were a couple of ragequits because of this.

Story and atmosphere vise the Surge is a bit lacking, but you can't do much to differentiate the levels too much because then it wouldn't feel like a highly efficient factory. Main story is quite interesting, side NPC quests are quite meh.

Definetly a buy if you enjoy difficult character action RPG games like Dark Souls series, Nioh & Lord of the fallen. It's a slower paced game with less bosses and more focus on traversing the different levels.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonally, I hate to say this. I don't like this game. It gets really repetitive really fast. But you're not reading this to hear me whine. So here's pros and cons instead.

Pros: Level design is something to behold. The areas are beautiful and expansive, for the most part. The enemies are hard to, but that's what makes it fun. It'll keep you on your toes and keep your wits sharp. The scenes are fantastic and the combat is... interesting. Selecting body parts for weak points is a neat concept. The characters are well diverse and, in my opinion, voiced rather well.

Cons: Gameplay gets repetitive along with combat. There is no clear indication of what to do. While that is sort of what these types of games are about, I feel at the very least there should be a map so the player could at least figure out where they are. Difficulty will spike randomly without warning. While that could be fun, it's mostly VERY annoying. The first boss could either be very easy, or extremely hard to figure out. Hit boxes are off, you could get hit by a weapon landing a foot away from you (however, that could just be my game or lag)

Overall, I could go on, but I would not recommend this game. While it has it's good moments, be it that they're far and few between, there are too many bad moments or just really long, drawn out moments where I'm trying to figure out what to do next. I could just be incompetent though. But whatever. Do what you want, I'm not your mother.\t\t\t\t\t
