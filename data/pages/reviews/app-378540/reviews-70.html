Dark Souls meets Dead Space with Fallout VATS



The Surge, for PC

Rating: 8.0/10 ; Great

Played: 2019

Highly recommended for any fan of the Souls series. It is also a fair entry point to the Souls like genre.

The Surge is a sci fi third person action RPG which is essentially a Dark Souls clone. Anyone familiar the the Souls series will know what to expect in this game though it does have its own unique mechanics and refinements. It excels at creating an interesting and creepy atmosphere with the highly skill based combat and overall gameplay of Dark Souls. Its unique body part targeting and character progression systems make this game more than a simple clone, instead allowing it a worthy place in the genre.

Pro
  • Good story, setting and characters
  • Newgame+
  • Challenging skill based combat
  • Variety of weapons, armor and equipment, many of which have unique effects
  • Unique character progression system that is not based on locking the player into choices and is fun to tinker around with builds
  • Good keybinding options
  • Many mechanics of Dark Souls have interesting new takes on the concepts (such as the bonfire and estus flask)
  • No inventory limit
  • Only tech scrap is dropped upon death, and it can be banked
  • Body part targeting and dismemberment for loot is an excellent risk vs reward system
  • Some backtracking after acquiring new abilities to get optional content
  • Some areas with good atmospheric horror

Con
  • Third person camera that can sometimes have awkward angles, or enemies or your character blocking your view
  • Lack of in game maps
  • Time limit for corpse runs before your scrap is lost
  • Cannot use scrap or higher level components to get lower level components, so you will have to farm weak enemies
  • Can only jump while sprinting, which makes the little bit of platforming harder
  • The arc welder robots are too unpredictable due to having too many different attacks
  • Too easy and tedious to kill enemies with basic ranged drone attacks. Would have been better to up the damage and make each shot cost scrap, like arrows in Dark Souls
  • Lack of multiplayer
  • Implant interface is somewhat unintuitive and user unfriendly

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother tragically underrated game from Deck 13. Like Lords of the Fallen before it, this game's Steam rating suffers from a combination of (a) numpties who complain vaguely about the combat being somehow bad and (b) snobbery ("it's just a cheap Dark Souls knock-off"). It's true that it is derivative of Dark Souls etc., but it brings enough innovations of its own to be more than a mere clone.

The game takes place in a gritty future scientific/industrial setting inhabited by lunatics in exoskeletons wielding power tools as weapons (this includes the player), various kinds of robots and other semi-sentient things. Late in the game, things tend towards more hard sci-fi themes, but I won't say more than that to avoid spoilers.

I found combat to be extremely satisfying, and I can see even at my modest skill level I can still improve further. Enemies telegraph their attacks "fairly", and I rarely felt that I died through no fault of my own. The progression system consists of upgrading your gear using parts torn from fallen foes, and is highly addictive. A variety of gear exists to suit various playstyles; for example, you can be agile but relatively vulnerable, or slower but relatively resistant to damage. Similarly, there is a weapon to suit all kinds of players, and you will need to switch weapons and gear to suit a given situation.

A legitimate criticism of the game is that there aren't actually many levels as such, although they are pretty big and very well-designed. The difficulty level is pretty high and mere mortals like myself can spend 40 hours on a playthrough, although admittedly, I am a completionist. So, personally, I don't find the small scale of the game to be a problem.

Technically, the game was mostly stable for me, although I encountered about 3 CTDs in about 40 hours. I can't put the blame definitively on The Surge, because apart from the obvious explanation of a bug in the game itself, such crashes can have complex causes such as bugs in drivers and middleware.

Graphically, the game is very impressive, with sharp textures and excellent lighting. Sound and voice acting are good. In terms of production values, I'd say that it lives in the "AA" space, and that's a good place to be, considering that an "AAA" game nowadays is typically a cynical, microtransaction-riddled cash-grab.

All in all, I give this game 5 severed limbs out of 5.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is probably the third most underrated game I've played after Darksiders 1 and 2. The combat is so good and so are the animations. Makes it feel like your character has weight. I've played *and* beat Dark Souls 1 and 3, and while they were okay, I didn't find myself having much fun playing them. I found The Surge to be much easier than Dark Souls 3 personally, and I found myself having fun with this game as opposed to the Dark Souls games.

Wouldn't say the game is easy however, because it is challenging, but never to the point where I wanted to throw my controller at the monitor. Dark Souls made me wanna do that. The boss battles are few but each one is challenging. They all want you to carefully understand its attack patterns and adapt and *git gud*. Each battle against a boss felt like an epic duel. And the sound plays a big role in making it great. The music that plays in the bg while fighting a boss, and the clash when metal hits metal- all sound exaggerated and epic.

The animations are hands down the best in any Souls-like game to date. I see a few people saying it's clunky and I'm like "what?". The game has a free demo, you should check it out yourself to determine who's right. From sprinting, to swooshing a weapon, they all have a sense of weight and are much better than in Dark Souls.

Another thing I can't wrap my head around is what people say about level design. They say it's confusing and lazy. Let me tell you, that while it *is* confusing at times, it is NOT lazy. People who don't like the level design in The Surge are people who want a pointer in their games telling them where exactly to go and what exactly to do. While there are some shared assets in each environment, the layout of these areas are definitely not the same, and are easily distinguishable as long as you don't have a brain the size of a pea. Think of it as Doom 2 / Duke Nukem 3d's later levels if you will. Those games have some of the best level design in gaming still, and The Surge is no different.

The environments are still varied enough to keep things afresh, though they are not as varied as Dark Souls which takes you through completely different environments. It's all industrial environments here, but each area does manage to look as unique as it can possibly be. Other reviews telling you they are the same from beginning to end are outright lying. There are only 5 big areas in The Surge, and you will be able to instantly tell which one is which if you're randomly thrown into the game.

Last but not least, I like this format of games far more than Dark Souls. In Dark Souls it feels like bosses make up 70% of the game and the minions make up for only 30% of it. The Surge is 90% minions and 10% boss fights. That way each boss fight feels like what it is - a boss - at the end of each area.

Tldr - the game imo is a masterpiece, and I urge anyone put off by the Souls games for its difficulty to at least give this a try. While challenging, The Surge never gets infuriating. The demo should be a good starting point of what to expect from the whole game. 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's such a mixed bag.

Progression:
+ Really fun way to gain gear by severing limbs
+ Scrap multiplier challenges you to keep going
- Timer on scrap recovery makes you rush and just isn't really fun

Combat:
+ Weapon variety (Although I found only the very fast weapon speed playable for me, anything else feels incredibly slow compared to the combat speed)
+ SOME enemies have good tells and fun to learn patterns
- SOME enemies have no decent tells and are extremely annoying (Especially towards the end)
Note: Had some real issues in the final stage, enemies just negate stagger, can't be blocked decently and have annoying tells

Environment:
+ Complex with a lot of shortcuts
- Very samey, and the complexity of the levels makes it hard to figure out routes (Mostly at the final stage again)
- A lot of backtracking opportunities would be a positive, but no fast travel makes it annoying to reach far away stages.

I'm very persistent and played through to the end, but honestly; I don't look back on my time with fond memories, but rather with fustration. I'm a massive dark souls fan, and even though some bosses or areas are tough, I always have fond memories of the things I conquered; However, I recoil at the idea of playing this game again (or in ng+), as there's just to much frustration with badly designed areas or enemies.\t\t\t\t\t
