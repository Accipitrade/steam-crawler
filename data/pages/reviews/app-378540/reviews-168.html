\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game handles like a mix between Dark Souls and Warhammer 40K Space Marine. Very fun!
Aesthetics wise, it's a post apocalyptic industrial landscape with sand and abandonment. It feels like a Nier Automata made for heterosexual males. No grand "Ar Tonelico" Style soundtrack like the one in Nier, in fact, there isn't much music in it, not including some hillbilly folk. Also, the only "Wow!" factor you will get in this game will be from the dismembering execution animations, like the ones in "For Honor".
Killing Skeletons, Orcs or endless hordes of the Google Android Mascot is fun, but choosing which body part of a human being to rip asunder through the use of heavy industrial equipment that exert massive amounts of shear force or blunt trauma, while blood spills everywhere, never gets old and I'd bet it probably never will.
Game runs great too! I have everything at max, with motion blur turned off, in 100% resolution (no multisampling).
In summary, I am very happy with my purchase! I definitely recommend it if you enjoyed reading the above! Cheers!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI initially recommended this game but 15 hours in my opinion has changed. This game is attempting to be a sci-fi Dark Souls sort of experience.

The combat system at first was very satisfying. The first two levels are very well done and every fight I had felt fair. Level design throughout is really cool with unlocking different shortcuts and seeing how everything interconnects. I enjoyed the sound design with the music being very foreboding and atmospheric. Overall I really enjoy the theme and my main drive in the game was wanting to learn more about the world and story.

I eventually had to give up though. Enemies in later levels are just broken, and they really point out the flaws in the combat system. Blocking is useless, as MANY attacks simply go through your block and there's no way to tell which attacks will do this. Using hit-and-run tactics with your dodge is the only way to make some progress with the later enemies which is not only extremely boring, it still doesn't work half the time because the enemies behave so inconsistently with their ability to be staggered and how often they stagger you and two-shot you before you can react.

The combat, which is the most important part of the game, totally breaks down about 10-15 hours in. You'll suddenly be dying every minute or two and it will not feel fair.

Character progression is super basic, and your armor choice barely seems to matter, as even with heavy armor you can be one-shot by certain enemies. Maybe I misunderstood something but it appears that leveling up does not increase your stats at all unless you equip certain implants, and even those implants have limits on how much they can boost your stats based on level. I wanted to try and grind to get more powerful to maybe offset the broken enemies but it didn't seem to make a difference.

I'm super bummed out because I really wanted to like this game and see where it went, but it's just too boring and frustrating in the later levels. Hopefully some of this can be addressed in a balance patch and I can return to the game, as there is a great game hidden under an ugly outer layer here.\t\t\t\t\t
