\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to recommend this game but I'm not sure that I should. I'm going to begrudgingly give it a thumbs up.

I like the aesthetic and the gear system. Both cool ideas that are fun to play around with. Unfortunately the gear system is very shallow so it takes all of two minutes to figure out exactly what you want and then never look at another piece of gear again.

The combat is overall ok. I think the animations are fine and fighting most enemies reminds of souls games. A few simple attacks that you need to learn and then they are easy.

The bosses are my biggest complaint. They are actually really good boss fights. They are technically well designed and are fun to puzzle out how you are supposed to avoid attacks and when the boss is actually vulnerable.
BUT
2 major problems:
- The animations don't match the hitboxes. I tested the first 3 bosses extensively and the hitboxes are actually fine and totally fair. But you will never know where they are without trial and error because the animations mean nothing. That is really bad for a game like this.
- The camera, my God the camera. The camera is the hardest enemy in this game. I was killed by the camera more than anything else. Honestly the camera is the hardest thing to get right in soulslike games, but it is super important. This game missed on the camera front.

If you don't mind redoing bosses until you can learn the invisible hitboxes and you aren't going to get tilted by the camera glitching into the wall effectively blinding you, then the rest of the game is actually great. Those are just 2 huge issues that are holding it back.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI fell in love with the surge when I saw it: the sci-fi world and the ORIGINALITY af all his weapons, maps and enemies. The idea and the concept behind the story is amazing, well thought and constantly evolving while playing, making you think about earth and his future, the responsability of mankind and ethic.
However, a lot of time I felt frustrated, even more in the end of the game, with some enemy damage being
overwhelming, or the number of foes being really difficult to manage while they keep attacking together.
The game GIVES you ways to exploit this, and you CAN play whatever setup you love, but sometimes it really feels like you have to adopt an intended approach, which may be not very appreciated, anyway it feels AMAZING discovering how much a different mindset can BREAK the game and, honestly, you probably would expect this by this genre of game.
The DLCs really showed me the love that DECK13 developers put in the game, I am impressed by how they introduced new challenges with different themes; it feels like a developer said "I love western films and amusements parks, and I will fit them in the game making everyone like them" and...it works...making even the effort to contextualize them.
The Surge is my little gem and I highly suggest everyone to give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t
