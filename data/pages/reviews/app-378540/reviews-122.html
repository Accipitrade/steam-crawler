\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeeing as this is on sale, it's an appropriate time to post a review for this game. Very simply, I really, really enjoy this game. Yes, it's a Soulsborn-style game (and I'll be comparing it to other games like it as appropriate), but with the "fresh twist" of modern technology. It sets out to do what it wants to, and it does a solid job of it. Is it perfect? No, but that's okay. Here's a very lengthy explanation why. TLDR is at the bottom.

The Story

Sadly, there isn't too much to write about here. You can avoid most of the overall story in the game and just kinda blunder through enemies and bosses. However, if you look into it, and pay attention to the cutscenes, dialog, and character interactions, it's actually pretty interesting to follow. It kind of falls into the average "tragic hero" trope in the beginning, but there's a fair bit more under the hood that the player can explore if they choose. Personally, I thought the story was particularly interesting and complemented what the game had to offer. It isn't something you're spoon-fed though, and it's not as intriguing as other Soulsborn games where you might pause the game for a bit to go look up the lore. But it's there, and it should be given credit where credit is due.

The Gameplay

You've got your stamina bar, you've got your dodge, you've got your whack button, you've got your harder whack button, and you've got your lock on. It's pretty simple to pick up and learn this game, and that's not a bad thing. Anyone who has played a game like this will be able to pick it up relatively easily. I would even go so far as to say this game is easier to pick up then other Soulsborn games, as something about the combat feels a bit easier to get used to. Early on, the enemies aren't nearly as punishing, meaning that you can really get a feel for wind-ups, learn telegraphed moves, and find a playstyle that suits you. Sure, if you're looking for the "punch me in the nether regions as soon as I start" style of game, it might not fit that, but it actually feels like a good entry point into this style of combat. As someone who isn't particularly good at Soulsborn combat, it was actually really pleasant to "learn" this game, really. That said, it doesn't just "hold your hand" throughout the later encounters, as you'll still have to work for your boss kills and such (unless you're like, a no-damage Souls runner, in which case, why are you reading my review anyways).

The Music

Now normally this is a point I'd ignore, as I'm usually the type to just put on some background music that doesn't drown out audio queues and play blissfully through the game. But oddly enough, this one struck home. All of the music in the game is decent. But there's a particularly atmospheric song in the OST, which plays in every medbay in the game (unless you turn it off, which props to the devs for considering that for those who might not want to hear it too much). There's something oddly haunting about the melody, and it feels at home in this game. The song is Prisoner by Stumfol. There's a beautiful melancholy to hearing this melody every time you revive, and it feels like it has two meanings to you: the medbay is a sanctuary and you are safe here, and that you feel alone in this world and need to trudge along to sort out what happened. Very simply, that's a beautiful atmospheric touch to add to the game, and I even found myself smiling as I revived and heard the song.

The rest of the soundtrack is enjoyable to listen to and adheres to the theme well. Oftentimes you'll be too caught up in the heat of the battle to really give it a listen, but I encourage you to give it a listen sometime.

The Content

If there's anything you can fault The Surge for, it's for not having too much content. You don't have too many zones, there's a limited roster of bosses, and it's not nearly as open-world as other Soulsborn games. It's not meant to be your "put in hundreds of hours" kind of game. That said, it introduces new concepts to make the most out of what it can. Hard-mode kills on bosses are something to work for and be proud of (and have the show-off potential through weapons), and smelter kills are progressive towards bragging-rights-worthy content. The "hack off what you want" style of gameplay is super cool, in my opinion, and really allows the player to farm or ignore enemies when necessary to suit their playstyle. See an enemy with a piece of armor you like? Target that limb and hack it off of them. Congratulations, you most likely just got a new schematic to make that piece of armor you wanted! To me, this was a really cool feature to have, and I enjoyed it greatly. This mechanic is a lot of the draw that The Surge has, and though ultimately there aren't TOO many weapons and armor sets, it's a really cool mechanic and I enjoyed it a lot.

The Theme

Alright. Take your favorite Soulsborn, be it DS 1-3, Bloodborn, S&S, what have you. Now, throw it far into the future. Suddenly it's post-apocalyptic and driven by technology. Does that sound appealing? If it does, then this game is definitely for you. If it doesn't, then that's okay, I'm just being honest here. The apocalyptic machine-ridden style isn't for everyone, and I feel like that could be a deal-breaker here if you're adamant about your fantasy style, so it's necessary to state. Personally, I enjoy this theme greatly, and The Surge does it well. The environments are detailed and beautiful, and the wreckage lying around is a good look for the game which adds to the initial feelings of being left alone in a world you don't fully understand. It's a simple theme, but The Surge does a good job with it.

The Verdict

If a Soulsborn game beset with rampant machines and literal hack-and-slash gameplay sounds fun to you, I highly recommend you pick up this game. It flew under the radar in a lot of aspects because it didn't do anything perfectly, but it did a lot of things well. The new things that it brought to the table were done well, so if you can get past a smaller-length game, you're sure to have fun with it. It's a solid pick-up to satisfy your Soulsborn craving.

TL;DR: It doesn't revolutionize Soulsborn games, but it does a good job at innovating where it can. It's a bit short, but still worth the time.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI greatly enjoyed The Surge when it first released and played a good 10+ hours into the game in the first week alone. There did come a point when the game died for me. While the enemies continued to change and become more difficult, (always a good thing in a game with repetitive combat), I felt too alone. I enjoyed the co-op of the dark souls series too much and felt at a loss in The Surge's world without that bit of community. So, my rating is based on the game, 9/10, not my with for a multi-player mode. Even a simple peer-to-peer system so my friends can join would have helped it last longer in my library. Thank you to the developers for making such a fleshed out game. I look forward to playing what you create next.

Rating: 9/10
-1 point: A large group of the enemies were not so much difficult as damage sponges. Complex moves > Giant Health pools.\t\t\t\t\t
