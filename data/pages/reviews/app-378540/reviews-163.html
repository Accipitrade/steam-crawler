\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm really enjoying this game so far! The limb targeting for new gear and for materials to level your gear is a really cool touch to the tried and true Souls formula. Like Lords of the Fallen, there is a timer to get your Tech (souls), but each kill you get on your way to retrieve your souls will increase the timer creating a really cool risk/reward. There is an energy meter that is seperate from your stamina that lets you do really cool things like hacking off limbs in spectacular fashion or heal yourself to allow to fight longet before needing to replenish your health potions. Mixing and matchin armor on your arms and legs allows flexibility in what you want to do in the game as far as fat rolling, etc. All your stats are on the gear you use. You level your rig that allows you to equip more/heavier gear and equip more mods that increase your health or energy. You aren't putting anything into specific stats. You can even bank your Tech so you won't lose them if you die if you just have a lot and know a boss is coming up soon. The only concern I have is the length of the game. After six hours I beat two out of the five bosses. But there were a lot of doors in the two areas I have completed that I couldn't get into because my level wasn't high enough so there is a reason to go back to earlier areas to explore some more even once you beat the boss. Also of note is there is no multiplayer or online functionality at all. That's fine with me as I never engaged with anything multiplayer related in any souls game and it kind of adds to the isolation feel of the world that you're trying to survive in, so it fits in a way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not really an authority on Souls games. I've puttered around in DSII a bit, and after several years have only recently gotten anywhere close to the end (I think?)

So rather than comparing this to a Souls game, or referring to it as a "souls-like", or whatever, I'll just say: I really like this game. It does what it set out to do and it does it well.

The combat is really fun and instantly addicting. It's super satisfying when you get it, and then it gets harder, in a good, not-arbitrary way (so far, I'm not all that deep into the game yet).

It's hard. It's not "I want to kill everyone and then myself" hard, but it's hard enough to make you think twice before getting greedy.

The incentive to fight the hard way is really cool: attack armored bits to earn more scrap and maybe a blueprint for a forklift-tine-turned-arm-stabby that would impress Ripley.

I've read some lambasting of the setting and of the story...well, the setting is neat as hell, and the level design is thoughtful and cool. It's very satisfying to take a risk and go down the spooky corridor with all the spooky dudes to find you've made a shortcut all the way back to the medbay and can now cash in all those sweet scraps.

As for story--and I know the souls series is supposed to be superduper deep lore-wise, etc--I don't think it's all that lacking when compared to a souls game. The latter are already intentionally vague, and Surge relies on this too. So what? I'm not in it for the story in the end man. I want to chop up fellow denziens of the dystopic cyberpunk future and probably go through a bunch of ♥♥♥♥ just to still find myself under the heel of the big ole sci-fi corporation.

Hopefully reality will bcome similar to this game someday, and I can become a corporate shill / scab turned mindless cybernetic murder fiend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tsoulslike formula but feels new

I will list my cons first as I personally feel they provide a bigger impact on if someone will buy it as 'Soulslike' is a big enough seller for me usually

CONS:
- Moves can be buffered meaning if you spam a button Warren will continue to attack for a while as you buffered an input, this can get frustrating if you spam the attack button too much during a fight.
- enemy 'poise' if you are familiar with the term from dark souls is extremely high when hitting an armored part but low when attacking an unarmoured part, whilst this seems obvious when said it does not come across like that when playing
- levelling doesn't really feel useful except allowing you to equip better gear and access some areas that are gated by levels, not useful unless you already have the better gear to equip.
- improved boss weapons when completing a special condition, this wouldn't be an issue if the boss was replayable before NG+
- lack of a co-operative multiplayer, this was one of my favourite parts about dark souls is that although the game had multiplayer and other people it felt lonely


PROS:

- I like the weapon proficiency system, it feels like you get some progression and stronger weapons improve the rate at which proficiency increases
- targetting specific parts, not a huge change to combat but still feels like its adding something to the gameplay
- combat feels fun and has a HIGH risk vs reward, enemies do a lot of damage but have obvious anticipation. this means you can tell or bait out attacks easily, then targetting the unarmored parts can lead into large combos, targetting the wrong part could mean death
- I LOVE the aesthetic of the game, the Sci-fi world looks great
- If you cut a part off of an enemy you get that part, they don't randomly drop their gear

overall I would highly reccomend this game to anyone who enjoys the soulslike formula but I don't feel it is as friendly as dark souls is for a newbie to the genre, in my opinion it requires some of the transferable skills from games like dark souls demon souls and bloodborne as basic combat with normal enemies is more challenging than in other similar games

Highlights:

Creepy setting and creepy enemies - dark factory, enemies that were once like you, now seemingly zombies/slaves - yet in agony.

That - I'm so far from the bonfire feeling - its here!

New mechanics - upgrade/gear/implant and "leveling" system - takes a tad bit getting used to - but its cool and well thought out.

Combos and weapons - you know how in Dark Souls some weapons made you feel like a ninja? Well here its a mecha ninja warrior.



--

For those posting negative reviews here's my response:

Difficulty - like in Dark Souls 1/2/3 - this is partially up to you - if you are getting killed often - stack life boost implants. Focus on unarmed parts, try faster weapons, run away :)

Setting - yeah its not the epic mastery of Dark Souls - but not everyone can be Mozart, enjoy the creepyness.

Not - exactly - like Dark Souls - Dark Souls is there for you and always will be - this is something new, enjoy the mystery/discovery.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:

- A return to Dark Souls 1-type of exploration. Relies less on connected smaller areas like Dark Souls 2 and 3 but instead you have a very large enviroment to explore in which you unlock shortcuts to make traversing it quicker as you go. I very much prefer this style, so it is a big plus.

- Enjoyable and meaty melee combat. I daresay it is even better than Dark Souls in some regards, especially when it comes to the bigger and heavier stuff which feel properly impactful and while still slow they flow a lot better than the Greatweapons of Dark Souls.

- Controls perfectly fine with mouse and keyboard. We all know how notoriously bad the Dark Souls-series is on this point, but The Surge definitely pulls it off flawlessly.

- Runs perfectly fine, even on my gaming laptop which is in the middle-ground between "Minimum" and "Recommended" specs.

- POISE! PRAISE THE SUN! The type of exo-skeleton rig you are wearing has stat akin to Poise in Dark Souls, only it actually matters - a lot! When you are wearing the lighter and more agile rigs, you are often sent flying into the air if you are unfortunate enough to get hit by an enemy with a heavy weapon. Wearing the beefiest rigs will not only reduce the damage taken, but keep your feet firmly planted on the ground!

Cons:

- No character creation whatsoever. Now this could be excused if the main character was written in such a way that his set of characteristics were central to the plot, but not as much. There is one spoilerific characteristic he has at the start of the game, but that could easily be given to anyone else. The role he plays could be filled by a myriad of different people.

- No online component whatsoever. Whether you loved it or hated it, the multiplayer aspect is a huge part of the Soulslike-experience and it is definitely noticable in it's absence.

- Retains the dumbest gimmick from Lords of The Fallen: You won't get the "proper" boss weapon unless you defeat the boss while meeting a specific condition. There is no way to know what that condition is in-game as you are fighting them, forcing you to either spoil the game for yourself by Googling all the bosses and what to do or to miss out on the good version of their weapon. While there is NG+ to let you fight them again, the bosses can't be re-fought in your current NG.

Overall I'd still recommend the game because I feel the positives heavily, heaaavily outweights the negatives.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is Deck 13's second attempt to make a Souls-style game, and their second failure. It's clear that the developers tried to copy the key elements of the souls games, and also that they completely misunderstood how to execute any of those elements.

The first thing most people think of when they think of the Souls games is the difficulty, and, while it's certainly true that they're difficult games, it's a means to an end. The difficulty in the Souls games is there, not for the sake of making a hard game, but to give the player a sense of accomplishment whenever they make progress. When you beat a Souls boss for the first time, or find a bonfire in a new area, you feel a big sense of relief and accomplishment. You think "oh man, I did it!" The Souls games also have pretty solid, smooth difficulty curves that make that sense of accomplishment a consistent thing, without ever pushing the player TOO hard. That's not true in The Surge.

The difficulty in The Surge is more accurately described as a series of increasingly steep cliffs, rather than a smooth incline. Within any given area, the difficulty rarely changes outside of a few encounters here or there (basically whenever you have to fight multiple enemies at once), but jumping from one area to the next is ALWAYS a big difficulty spike, and it very quickly (as in, by the third area), got to the point that the game stopped being fun, and that sense of accomplishment disappeared and was replaced with the ever present thought of "what garbage are they going to throw at me next?" Not only was that the case, but Deck 13 seems to have misunderstood how the Souls games even create challenges in the first place. It's incredibly rare that there will be a difficult encounter in the souls games simply because the enemies have big numbers (health and damage). The vast, vast majority of the time, it's a matter of figuring out the enemy's moveset in Souls, but in The Surge the later game enemies are made difficult not by having interesting, challenging to counter attacks, but simply by having large health bars and 1-2 shotting the player regardless of loadout.

Speaking of encounter design, that's another major draw to the Souls games. Specifically, the boss designs are largely fantastic, but the majority of normal enemies are also fine to great. Incredibly rarely will there be a fight that's simply bad, and lacks any redeeming qualities though they do exist (*cough*BedofChaos*cough*). Nearly every fight in the Souls games is at least tolerable, with a few HUGE standouts in each game that are a joy to fight every single time. This is also not true in The Surge (there's a trend here).

There wasn't a single enemy I enjoyed fighting in The Surge. Not one. Normal enemies ranged from minor nuissances to walking dumpster fires I did everything in my power to avoid. The "best" enemies were the ones that fought most like the player, and the worst were the ones that played by entirely separate rules, but none of them were good. They were either too simple and mindless to fight and as a result were simply boring, or they were jam-packed with obnoxious attacks that, while not necessarily difficult to deal with, were very frustrating for bad reasons. I don't mean to imply that bosses are any better, of course. Every boss was incredibly easy relative to the area they were in (barring the first boss, of course, since the first area is basically a tutorial), and none of them were fun to fight. Even the two humanoid bosses, which should have been the best fights in the game, were simply not fun as a result of the devs not knowing how to make a difficult, interesting humanoid enemy. Rather than those fights being straight fights against another human, following similar rules and using similar tools (like the best fights in the Souls games), they're basically bulkier normal enemies that go away for a bit so you can fight a giant, easy enemy instead for a while.

I'd say that the third major draw to the Souls games is their replayability. There's a good variety of weapons and builds in the Souls games, such that you can play through each multiple times, while getting a different experience each run through. In The Surge, however, there's a whopping 5 weapon types, with no variation in moveset in any given type, and there's no build variety because there are no dedicated builds of any sort in The Surge. Rather than getting a point to invest in a stat of your choice on level up, levelling in The Surge just increases your "Core Capacity" which basically functions as equip burden. Your health doesn't increase, your damage doesn't increase, nothing besides "core capacity" increases on a level up. Naturally, you'd assume that Deck 13 included a wide variety of interesting effects on gear so that you can still get an amount of variety that way, and you'd be wrong. Gear has minor percentage changes on some stats (like a 5% attack speed increase, or a -10% dodge stamina cost) that is hardly noticable, and the Implants that function as something similar to rings offer equally uninteresting changes to your character. Implants can either be boring passive stat increases (the only way to increase your max HP, Stamina and Energy), or equally boring consumables (healing items and damage buffs). The Surge doesn't even have the benefit of a long critical path with lots of side content like Nioh (the only non-Fromsoft game to successfully execute on the Souls formula), as I finished it in almost exactly 11 hours and there's basically no side content to speak of beyond backtracking to open some locked chests from early areas.

The Surge tried to copy the Souls games, and that's a bold effort in its own right. The Souls games set an incredibly high bar for their own sub-genre of ARPG, but The Surge falls so incredibly short that it can't even see the bar it's trying to meet. I would just recommend playing the Souls games instead, or, if you have a PS4, Bloodborne and Nioh.

At least The Surge is better than Lords of the Fallen I guess.\t\t\t\t\t
