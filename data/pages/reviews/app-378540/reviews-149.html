\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've enjoyed this game quite a lot. If you like the Dark Souls games, then you will likely enjoy this game. I've beaten the game once so far and I'm about a 3rd of the way into new game plus. Below is the pros and cons I've thought of while playing this game.

Pros:
*Amazing weapons/armor
*Interesting map design
*Unique update mechanic
*Will likely feel rewarding when challenging sections/bosses are finally defeated
*Excellent graphics
*Atmospheric music/sounds (especially in dark sections)
*Really engaging and entertaining battle system
*Can quickly switch between a heavy tank and a nimble fighter

Cons:
*Difficulty may cause some to rage quit
*Lack of explaination in game can sometimes be frustrating
*Repetativeness of some parts of the game may turn some away (e.g. farming parts for upgrades)
*Some may not like the very repetative music/computerized voice when leaving the Operations Station (where you heal/upgrade)
*Some bugs still occur, but not many (some already fixed since release)

Overall, I think this is an excellent game and well worth the cost. I really hope they make either DLC or a sequel in the future. I highly recommend this game if you like the dark souls games and a sci-fi setting.\t\t\t\t\t

Get it at full price if you really want to play the game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti have far from beaten this, but the amount of time i spent playing it gave me quite an impression of how things go, i'd say.

even tho it is clearly influenced by the souls series i'm not gonna compare it too much to those games. it has enough new ideas and mechanics and a completely different setting, that i think it's a beast of it's own.

surely the sci-fi setting is the biggest difference and it changes the atmosphere quite a lot. it is ominous and oppressing but in a different way. in souls you still had some depressing kind of hope to the world and most npcs still were encouraging you to go and succeed (except in that bastard child that DS2 was). here everything went to ♥♥♥♥ and you don't really know why or how.
besides that, the game gives you a few more hints where to go or what to do, but still: no real hand holding.

the fighting mechanics initially seem like souls, but only at first glance. sure, there is a stamina meter and punishing melee combat, but that's all it has in common. having played souls games will help you nontheless, since it feels familiar.
but in the surge you can specifically target limbs to chop them offf (for weapons, schematics and materials). that makes combat a tad more tactical, because you can chose to target weak points -to dispose of the enemy quickly- or take the risk of having a longer fight but maybe get valueable stuff doing a finishing move in the end. risk and reward.
in addition to live and stamina you also have to take care of your energy-bar. you build up energy during fighting and it can help you with using injectables, drones or other upgrades to your exo-skeleton; or rather your implants to it.
these implants are comparable to rings from souls and can give you health, stamina boosts, help you salvage more tech etc etc. you get the gist. they take up a certain amount of energy on your rig so you have to manage what you (can) use.

tech is basically the currency and xp in this game (if you want you can compare it to souls). you need it to craft things, upgrade your exo-rig or equipment. should you die -which you probably will do a lot- you have about 2 1/2 half minutes to get your tech back; it being dropped where you died. of course the enemies will be back, but defeating an enemy adds a few precious extra seconds to your timer. if you don't make it in time the tech is gone. so no pressure at all, right? the good thing: you can "bank" your tech in your 'base'. so if you don't have enough to level up just yet, you can explore a bit more relaxed without risking a huge amount or tech doing so.

the levels are very interconnected and cramped full of cut backs, secrets and shortcuts. you want to explore and find stuff. some of the areas are sealed off and can only be opened once you reach a certain (energy-)level. unlocking them is essential for progress and if not i pays off most of the time anyways. since all the areas of the game are industrial based in their design, there is a lot of weird ways to have plattforms and tunnels and so on to connect and tie together areas. thus they are more compact and complex and not quite as sprawling in since.


tl;dr: if you are into tactical 3rd person action adventures and are fond of scifi as well, then you will probably love it.


pros:
+ nice graphics and good performance so far
+ great feeling to it
+ tactical and very challenging fighting
+ nice and complex leveldesign
+ neat ways of upgrading and crafting

cons:
- not too much variety in the world
- some rather annoying enemies or placements of them
- that. one. single. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. country. song. in. OPS!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played the souls games for 100s of hours. I also played the developers previous games Lords of the fallen.

I like it. It has a lot of what makes the souls games good and a few fresh ideas.

Good:

- DS1 style level design, compact with shortcuts, consistent theme.

-Combat is fluid and well done. It has a backstabs which rewards good positioning.

- The limb cutting for resources is a good idea well executed.

- The implant and rig system is a quite nice allowing more customization than is normally available in a souls game.

Not so good:

- While the soundtrack is fine I find the use of songs annoying, there aren't very many and the country song playing in ops is driving me nuts. I'd pay money for a DLC disabling it.

- I find some of the mobs a little unfair. Many of them have leap or combo attacks that come out super fast which will 1 shot you with little chance to dodge. Also I think a few of the mobs have a little too much HP. I don't mind lethality but damage sink design is bad in my opinion.

- Your guy does not seem to have much in the way of iframes during dodging meaning that you'll often have to dodge away from enemies. This leads to drawn out fights which is a little boring but necessary since enemies are so lethal and hard to stunlock. I prefer the souls games in this area.

- Emphasis on thrash mobs over bosses. It's alright, but I like boss fights more. The ones i've seen so far have been OK though.

- Weapon/armor variety. I'd like a little more diversity in styles, but I guess the implant system sort of makes up for it.

Overall I like it quite a bit, it's better than Lords of the Fallen. It still does not rank among the souls games but it's getting closer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDark Souls + Monster Hunter + Horizon: Zero Dawn = The Surge

It isn't perfect, but it is enjoyable.
Very nice combat system, nice moves, cool weapons and armor, interesting lore (but unnecessarily obtuse), excellent level design, interesting and unusual leveling system, very cool boss fights (except Black Cerberus, which is a little annoying).

However, putting a lot of enemies in cramped spaces is a bad move to ramp up the "difficulty" near the end. It becomes somewhat irritating and boring. That's the biggest flaw.
The scenery doesn't vary too much, so don't expect nice views like the Souls games have. The fun here is in the combat and the maze-like levels.

(EDIT)
Played the whole game with the "light" armor variant (LYNX, changed to Chrysalis in the last area). Rarely got one-shotted, like people say it happens. If you try to understand how the system works, you can play however you like. The implant system needs a little tunning, but it is VERY adaptable to your every need.

I just wish you didn't have a timer to recover the scrap dropped when you die.\t\t\t\t\t

- Challenging gameplay

Cons:
- The game is hard as F***! Harder than DS3!

- Only melee weapons are available *spoilers* while enemies can shoot you with rocket launchers

Suggestion:
- Ease the game a little bit please. I’m getting PTSD from this game
- Add range weapons like guns, rifles and rocket launchers
- Add more weapons and armors as well as NPC vendors in which I can buy stuff from them
- Add the ability to travel from a Medbay to another
- Make me feel that I’m playing with a level 40 character. I don’t feel it at all! All the enemies can hurt me badly with a single hit.
- Make the blocking system a blocking system not just a useless move.

However you need to keep in mind the level of challenge between the beginning/middle of the game and the end game is exponentially different, and i dont know if this is on purpose or not. And some of the environment's falling will get you killed more than the enemy, but that just requires you to be careful and not accidently fall off a ledge or into poison gas.

But this game does a few things very well, and beautifully. The combat system is amazing! Very fun, and the bosses first group of bosses were a solid challenge, and pretty enjoyable. The combos and weapons are very easy to get used to and the executions are very enjoyable to watch. The farm system was good until end game (more on that soon), but of my 24 some odd hours played, i can honestly say i enjoyed almost all the game.

The bad part is the end game: This is where it divides those looking for an enjoyable challenge, to the extreme hard core that demand to conquer the near impossible. As you reach the end of the game, near everything can kill you in 1 - 3 hits, on top of the fact that 80% of enemies in the end game are faster than you, and have means of ensuring you dont get a full combo. due note that they are still beatable. The problem is it makes fighting a group of enemies extremely challenging and a major resource drain if you survive. The damage you take is increased the point where there is no point of farming for better gear because it feels like a waste of time.

I have every intention on completing the game 100% but the huge spike in challenge is borderline game breaking, and now im playing because of pride, not actual enjoyment. If you consider yourself a casual gamer, i would stay away from this game. If you are looking for a something very challenging and dont mind a game that needs just a little bit more polish, look no further because this game will treat you well.

You like enemies following you 360 degrees with their heavy attacks? Here you go!
You like stunattacks that can hit trough walls and containers? Sure!

7/10 if you like Soulsborne games.
6/10 if this is your first entry into the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
