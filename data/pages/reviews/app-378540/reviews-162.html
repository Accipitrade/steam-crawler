\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a solid game overall. Unfortuately relies VERY heavily on backtracking though the same levels, obtuse progression and a very lackluster ending. The total boss count is 5, which seems low but they are challenging and have good variety. Combat is good, weapons are cool, my only real gripe with the game is the level design and its frankly god-awful progression system.
You navigate through a portion of a level, killing enemies and grabbing stuff, occasionally hitting buttons or blowing circuits which have completely random or unknown effects. You'll hit a dead end, and then realize you hit a button 20 minutes back that opened a door you passed in a different area 40 minutes before THAT without it telling you anything at all. No map, no objectives, just aimless ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ wandering. Besides that, the game runs great, I RUSHED through this game, but if you played like a normal human I am sure it would be at least a 20 hour game.

If you have the drive, it's a worthwhile experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis game feels cheaply made. the entire time i was playing it i wished it had like 10x the budget to really fill it out.

you kill mobs repeatedly and are forced to target arms, legs, head, body to gather 1st the recipe, then the mats to make the item, then the mats to upgrade the item. and it's a mystery exactly how an item will upgrade or what the set bonuses will be. which, after all that work, shouldn't be the case.

dark souls grind had you plowing through mobs to farm which could be fun. this has you being specific with the targeting (which is awful with mousewheel) and when you're high level farming lower level stuff, you end up killing it before you can farm the loot.

Not a good game mechanic. Something like Nioh or DS gets it right with random drops, which allows you to plow through and farm up. This literally has you targeting the harder parts to "get" them, and avoid killing as efficiently as you can. Zzz.


it ends up feeling very tedious. the special "kill the boss a certain way to get a certain item" thing was awful in dark souls and it is awful here: it's a vague mechanic that doesn't make sense. I'm ripping apart a huge robot but cannot loot the thing that's right there on my screen? zzz.

Dark Souls had very different playthroughs depending on your class. This feels like there's one way to play, and it is centered around whatever weapon type you've levelled up. You can get some amazing item late game and completely suck at the proficiency, grats.

the difference between that and dark souls is that in dark souls you were a different class and there were enough items and bosses to make missing 1 loot or getting a specific loot from a boss not painful, like this is.

8.5/10

Pros
- Great, fluid combat
- Very useful armor and implant systems
- Extremely difficult at first
- Interesting and fun boss fights
- Good length
- I personally loved the story

Cons
- The difficulty kinda drops off at the very end of the first play through because you get very skilled and very upgraded. I am looking forward to a new game plus though.
- Though I loved the story, they could have developed the main character a little more.
- Hardcore dark souls fans will not like that it only takes about 40 hours to beat, but I really liked that. I have too much to do to spend 100 hours in a gruelingly difficult game
- Could have changed up the music more. though I loved the song at first, the prisoner song got old after hearing it for 40 hours.

This is a solid game and I enjoyed it more and more as I played it. I definitely would like to see a second one and think that I had more fun in this than I did Dark Souls 1. I am more of a sci-fi guy than fantasy though too.





Original Review
This is what you are getting and you should have heard it by now; this game is very very similar to Dark Souls. I didn't really know that going in, but I enjoy Dark Souls to a degree, so I have been enjoying this one.

The combat feels fresh. You have to build a whole rig suited to weapons and play style and each piece can make a huge difference. However, this game woops you so hard. Even Dark Souls 1 didn't woop me this hard. Not really a good thing imo, but I really can't bust it for that either. Just know, the game is very very difficult. It punishes you every chance you give it.

There is a story, for those of you who parrot paid, bs, reviewers like IGN (Ignorant, Greedy, Ninnies). Is it the best story, no. Has it got me wondering what happened and driving me forward? Yes. Does it have intrigue? Yes. The only issue here is this; the game is so difficult and so punishing and it takes so long to progress, that what would be a pretty good story can get lost if you aren't paying attention. Especially to the videos, audio logs, and conversations in the world.

Is this better than Dark Souls? No probably not, but that just depends if you like sci-fi or fantasy better. It's different enough to make it good enough for a buy, but ultimately it boils down to a very similar experience to the brutal Dark Souls games.\t\t\t\t\t

1) Very difficult and unforgiving (in a very good way)
2) The combat is vaguely similar (It is the same type of combat but very much in its own element)

If you want something very different and unique yet worth the risk this is your game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR Even if you don't like souls games you might really enjoy this one.

I am loving this game, and this is coming from someone who hated the souls series, and all the clones.

PROS
-It looks amazing
-Combat is fluid but still feels weighty.
-Combo system is fun once you get used to it.
-Attack angles matter for staggering enemies.
-Robot Zombies
-I love the aesthetic and the map design.

CONS
-Voice acting is bland, not good, not bad, just meh. The CREO hype man is probably the best VA I've heard so far.
-Combo system can be a pain in the ass before you get used to it.
-Parry system explanation needs a bit of work.
-Seems a bit grindy. No complaints yet just get the feeling it could be painful later.

The combo system is a little hinky IMO. At first glance it seems like a great idea but on certain weapon types it can get you into alot of trouble if you overcommit at the wrong times. It almost feels like Prince of Persia where you could click the combo in really fast and then the character executes the moves as you put them in. Some you can back out of with a dodge, while others you have to go all the way through the animation, and that's where the system feels hinky. at least to me. Good news though, you can use other combos for that weapon that are shorter and faster and only use that move when you know you can.

The parry system is really poorly explained. Yes this game doesn't want to handhold and I appreciate that, but some form of tutorial would have been very nice rather than dying 20 times trying to understand how it actually works.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary- I loved playing the game, but then the flaws became more and more apparent. 6/10

This game is the easy mofe for Dark Souls that i have been looking for. You can store your currency at any time to avoid loosing it and there is a quick save/load feature with F5/F8. That's all i ever wanted. Going without healing at ops or dying can increase your scrap multiplyer to 3x to decrease grinding too.

Combat, OK. Weapons feel appropriately weighty. Movement is deliberat and controls are tight. Every time i died i knew it was my fault (Except for the last boss). There were points in the game where i died multiple times, but at no point did i feel angry at the game. I lost 20k scrap (little over 2 levels at the time) one time and didn't even feel bad. By the end of the game i was running around with 80k before even concidering going back. The final boss is too big, you target it's limbs but the camera stays in the same place. This makes all jump and sliding attacks very finiky. I counted well over 50 missed attacks because a slide was never executed. I was fighting the camera the entire time. Early game enemies also do way too much damage, this needs to be scaled back.

A more contraversial move The Surge makes though is that it removes all skills and massively simplifies things. You have a core power level and your armor and implants take power. Nothing stops you from changing your loadout (at the medbay) at any point. This removes a large part of the difficulty in Dark Souls. I appreciate the ability to customize my loadout to he situation i find myself in though, so i see this as a plus. Then again, at one point your health just stops going up. What point is there to a heal that does 320 when your health never naturally goes above 200?

Map- I love how it folds in on itself. While the map itself is huge, shortcuts make it feel small at the same time. Signs are all over the map to help you know where you are at any time, but more needed to be done. I had no idea where the 4th area was, and the 5th and 7th areas were complete mazes. Even with 4 hours of trying, I never even got to the 3rd floor of the final area.

Inplants. Im going to be honest, not a lot of variety here. I would have liked to see more varriation here. There's injectables to power up attacks and another ones that increase weapon proficiency but that's pretty much it for weapon focused inplants. There are energy based implants, energy which can be used to restore health, execute damaged enemies, use an injectable, or to attack with your drone. Other than that, you can have 24 healing items if you find them and want to do so, or increase your maximum health/power core level. There needs to be more variety here.

Weapons classes each have a unique moveset that you either like or don't like. I was disapointed to see that all three of my single-rig weapons have the exact same moveset. Weapon streangth scales with proficiency, and as you find a move set that you like, you will naturally gravitate towards one weapon. This means that the weapon class you choose in the first area will likely out perform all other weapons. It's dissapointing to get a new weapon, only to have your current weapon be better. Single-rigged weapons are the only ones that can counter enemies without being damaged, but even trash mobs in the first area of NG+ will stagger you, so go for literally any other class proficiency if NG+ is your goal.

Story- takes too long to get going and doesn't wrap up in any meaningful way. A waste of time if this is why you are here. I killed multiple NPC and had no side effects or bearing on the story.\t\t\t\t\t
