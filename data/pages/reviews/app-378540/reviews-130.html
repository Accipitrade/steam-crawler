\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is a souls-like difficult sci-fi third person action rpg. You play as Warren, a paraplegic thats starts work at a megacorporation called CREO. You wake up in a junk yard part of the company with no memory of what happened, just a hologram in a medbay telling you where to go and do. I actually loved the story, way more direct than Dark Souls and the ending the best. I'll be honest, I didn't like this at the start. It felt harder than Dark Souls, because all weapons are melee without shields and I died more times in the beginning than any start of the Dark Souls games. But after you get use to the combat pacing it gets better. The combat is faster paced than Dark Souls with different types of melee weapons like 1 hand, heavy, single and twin rig, and staffs. Also with 3 types of damage types: Slashing, Blunt, and Element. They also added a lim targeting system, that you can cut off enemies' lims for upgrades. The map is well designed, thought the last area of the game is very confusing, but other than that really good. The armor and weapons are very beautifully designed as well. And I give this a 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game isn't Dark Souls and it really wants to be... but the designers really failed to capture the right gameplay elements to pull it off.

Armor in the game is straight up broken in a way that makes playing the game unnecessarily difficult, unintuitive, and unfun. The Lynx armor set can probably carry you through the vast majority of the game.

Attacks occasionally have combo strings from a single button press and while I'm sure that that seemed like a cool idea, they're actually quite detrimental to gameplay. You don't have the ability to cancel out of most attacks, so getting stuck in long combo animation means death against a lot of enemies.

Mobility in the game is pretty decent and feels good to use.

Level design is labyrinthian but not in a way that's interesting or makes sense. Meandering around service corridors that all look the same and are also filled with toxic gas is not fun or good.

Visually the game is fine. The UI is fine.

Anyhow, The Surge sucks, don't play it, thanks for reading.\t\t\t\t\t

From core mechanics taken directly from the souls games, The Surge manage to get its own identity by adding some more and removing the fat, while getting everything to fit the theme. The game can seem a bit unfair at some point, but if you insist you'll never get blocked indefinitely.

If you liked the souls, this is a good alternative. If those games attract you, but you fear you won't be up to the task, maybe try this one first.

It's good. Play it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought it because I thought it was an industrial sci-fi Souls game. It is, sorta. They copied a few systems, but they did a real bad job.

Movement is a nightmare. It feels both slow and incredibly loose at the same time, like you're controlling the camera rather than the character directly.

Combat is awful. It's slow, which I actually like, but it's also bad. One-on-one fights are alright for the most part, but the combat is not tight enough to support anything beyond that. Multi-part attack animations will leave you far more vulnerable than they should. Dodging sounds great until you do it and realize just how disorienting it is because of the bad camera.

The graphics are not half bad.

The implant system is interesting (but you'll probably just want to slot in +health implants because of the extremely high damage output of enemies coupled with the very poor dodge system)

It's really too bad, because under all the poor execution of such a critical part of an action game, it does seem like they had some cool ideas.

If the developer is reading this: if you're going to make a Souls-like game (which you clearly tried to) please have someone on staff actually play one rather than just reading about how it works. Combat tightness is key. If the combat doesn't feel polished, making the enemies extremely unforgiving/powerful isn't going to make it feel like a challenge, it's just going to be infuriating.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the record, I've played all 3 Souls titles, the first two on PS3/PS4, and the 3rd on PC.

The Surge isn't a Souls game. It's not "a souls-clone". It's more than that.

The similarities are there, but if anything The Surge takes a subtly different approach to the HARPG (Hard Action Role-Playing Game). Combat feels more streamlined, heavier, and a bit more real. There is a definite story with a hard-set character, and there isn't much room to alter what's happening, just how you go about doing what you do in game. The world is dystopian, but not unbelievable.

In short, where the Souls titles craft a superhuman character capable of going toe-to-toe with demigods, The Surge puts you in control of a guy who REALLY needs a break and to file for L&I. It's grittier, bleak, and wholly solid.

I recommend this game. It's been worth it's investment thus far, and I look forward to more from this team.\t\t\t\t\t
