\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun but frustrating.

The game looks nice, controls pretty well though not perfect but the real issue is how cheap the fights are.

For instance enemies will push through your attacks that should stagger them, which then staggers you and you get hit by follow up attacks, enemies attacks home in on you to the point they teleport which is clearly seen fighting the enemy behind the first boss, enemies are placed around corners to rush in and one hit you, etc..

This game is the absolute most frustrating game I've ever played and the story (at least in the beginning) makes no sense, the VA just sucks, theres zero world building, but its still fun.

To me its exactly like DS3 except runs well but just as cheap and agrivating and doesnt strike any of the points in combat that made DS1 a fair and rewarding challenge.

So if pick it up on the cheap its not a waste or if youre a masochist in denial that things DS3 was a good game then you'll get your jollies here otherwise if not and its not on sale/cheap dont bother.

---------------------------

Above was my first impressions of the game.

I've continued to play thegame because again, its fun but now as I take a break after having broken my controller I feel I can give clearer insight into exactly whats wrong with this game.

Before that I'll briefly touch on the things I enjoy about the game.
  • The graphical design is great. I liken it to sci-fi porn.
  • The combat, while confusing at first has more depth than most similar games. You have different types of weapons and then at least one subtype there-in, and they all have their own attack patterns and combos.
  • The levels are massive and the detail inside them is very nice. They never feel too big and are some what awe inspiring.
  • Theres plenty of interesting gear and they all have just the right amount of stats to give you a really enjoyable RPG and experience and there never feels like a "go-to" build.
  • In general the attacks from enemies, be them mooks or bosses, are pretty varied and theres just enough randomness to make enemies just unpredictable enough to keep you on your toes.
  • The game is quite rewarding. You likely wont feel like the next upgrade or new shiney is too far away, and if you get comfortable enough with the game you can reliably play without using the medical bay to retain your multiplier and even "bank" your scrap (XP) without losing it either. Lessening the risk.
  • The difficulty is curve is fairly consistent with the game throwing new enemies with their own unique attack patterns for you to master.
  • In general the whole system of cutting limbs is great. I could go into more detail but suffice to say it adds to the combat, looting and glorious violence of the game.

Now for what keeps this game from getting my recommendation...
  • Foremost, like I originally said, you will learn to become paranoid of every corner purely because there is a rediculously high over-use of enemies being placed there to gank you and with how easily you can be stun locked or 1-hit it stops being about any sort of 'challenge' quickly.
  • Shortcuts to bosses are usually filled with enemies.

    This is honestly the most infuriating part of the game, to fight a boss, get one-hit by an attack you hadn't seen yet, run back towards the boss fighting enemies along the way, possibly having to start the boss fight without full health (conversely potentially having the luxury of starting the fight with a reserve of energy), rinse and repeat untill you learn the boss...

    God help your controller if you happened to forget to bank your scrap before the fight and then have to rush to the boss before it despawns (a stupid ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ idea in itself), only to be killed along the way, removing the possibility to recollect your scrap... R.I.P. my controller...
  • The game desperately needed to have a map. While its nice to have these huge and interesting levels, when they loop in on themselves so much and include so much verticality you cant help but get lost and the rare times there are in game maps they are completely useless for more than looking the part.
  • A lot of the 'unique' enemies you find will teleport around.
  • Hit boxes can be larger than whats attacking you.
  • Enemies seem to have access to attacks you dont. The easiest example being the standing cross slash from the butterflies.
  • Theres an over reliance on one-hit kills and being stun locked to death.
  • Aside from the concept and the world building through the level design, everything else about the story in the game is crap, absolute garbage.

    The VA sounds like the devs done it themselves, the dialogue sounds like it was fan translated several times, major plot elements happen with zero context and just in general it feels like no one cared about the story in the slightest.
  • The camera sucks in a fight where you have multiple enemies or a boss with several elements you must destroy. Every time you kill an enemy you lose target lock and the camera starts refocusing away from the target you had (or some other weird shinanigans) and the developers conception of "auto-lock" is to lock onto an enemy that you hit.
  • Rarely there are enemies that can stop you regaining stamina or removing your entire pool of it which leads to you moving very slowly, not being able to dodge, block or attack, leaving nothing really to do except stand there or try to slowly move away from an incoming attack or out of an area effect.

Doesn't change my opinion of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLots of potential as a Dark Souls-like game but misses the mark at the most basic level (ie: the combat).

Dark Souls combat is based almost entirely on pattern recognition and timing. The player is expected to leverage their superior agility to exploit their knowlege of enemies movement and attacks to overcome them (most highly skilled players run around essentially naked because they do not get hit). While your character in Dark Souls does definitely become more powerful as you progress through the game, it is mostly the player knowledge and skill that increases during a playthrough, leading to a very satisfying experience. Simply put, whether you succeed or fail in Dark Souls it feels like you did it under your own merit either way. I still remember grinding Ornstein & Smough (the hard way, killing Ornstein last) for probably 4 or 5 hours straight until I succeeded. Having a player do this, without getting frustrated, is extremely difficult but Dark Souls pulls it off because of the feeling that I failed because I made mistakes NOT because the game was cheap or unfair. Surge feels like it was made by a group of people that were BIG fans of Dark Souls and played a lot of Dark Souls but ultimately didn't UNDERSTAND what made it such a great game.

Combat feels clunky at the best of time. At its worst, you'll see enemies sliding along the floor to retain proximity to your character in the midst of combos, performing high damaging near-instantaneous attacks with next-to no telegraphing beforehand, performing intant kill attacks with a attack arc larger than the distance of your dodge and the stun-locks... don't get me started on the stun locks. While there is aspects of the Dark Souls experience of getting owned by a newly encountered enemy until you figuring out what its strengths and weaknesses are, in Surge, even after that knowledge is gained these enemies still have attacks that feel cheap or unfair.

On the flip side, character attacks and movement feel like you are stuck in tar. Dodging has no i-frames, severely hampering its effectiveness compared to Dark Souls. Blocking eats up significant portion of your stamina and, frustratingly, there is a sizeable window after a blocking where attacking does not trigger standard combos but alternate ineffectual counter combos. This means you can immediatly attack but you're not gonna do much damage or hit hard enough to stun the enemy, draining your stamina to levels that make it difficult to deal with subsequent attacks OR you can stand there like a chump right next to the enemy and not block for a bit untill you can perform your standard combos again (I hope I don't need to explain why that might be a bad idea). Ultimately neither playing and agile ninja or a tank feels that effective of a strategy. Combos are slow and cannot be cancelled leaving you mashing the dodge or block key in vain while an enemy readies a devastating attack. In at least one instance I found that button presses did not consistently trigger the same attack patterns; double-rigged weapons vertical attacks would seemingly randomly initiate with either a left or a right handed swing, the first or second attack in a combo respectively.

Surge has some cool ideas like targeting and severing specific body parts to obtain said body part as loot, but the core mechanics of the game are inherently flawed making it feel more like a slog than a fun experience.\t\t\t\t\t
