\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt was starting well... But it goes downhill rapidly. Overall, the game feels unfinished, completely unrealistic, and at least at the beginning, the story is non-existent.
I am only in the second zone. But god does this game want me to stop playing.

First off, every PNJ out there roughly does 3 to 5 times your damages when you have "same level" stuff. Probably because they don't have to deal with the stupid controls. And evidently don't care if somebody is smashing blades through their heads or their chests (please hold my bear, put blades in my chest, while I rotate this 200kg mass, no don't worry I can handle it!).
As a consequence, most enemies will 2-shot you at best, and can usually combo you since interrupting them is unreliable. It's also funny to try and understand how they detect you, and decide to attack you. Sometimes you can shout into their hear, and they won't care. Other times they will see you behind obstacles. Obstacles that can, sometimes, be destroyed. For no good reason beside finding some passages, and sometimes resources. They are glorified doors and chests. Your weapons will go through everything else, not making any noise.

Upgrading stuff is uninteresting. You just farm the parts in the zone corresponding to the mark you want to upgrade to. And that's it.

Fun facts, enemies will continue to attack you even after you are dead. Because you know... 2017

Controls are mediocre. The camera is weird. Dodging is unreliable since sometimes your character goes into a frenzy combo, you don't know how or why, and you can't interrupt it to dodge. While your opponent will waive his hammer like nothing is happening, killing you in the process. Or while a second enemy you couldn't possibly see will just charge from across the whole level.

Also, can somebody explain to me how when you go back to base, if only to build stuff or change some implants, all the enemies come back to life? What the ♥♥♥♥ is happening in this universe?...

Long story short: if you want to go through the same level 200 times, sometimes being killed by the first enemy you see because the game wouldn't let you dodge, and you don't mind seeing the same 5 enemies all the time. I guess that the surge is an ok game.\t\t\t\t\t

DarkSouls VS DeadSpace = DankSi-fi

Mashup style of both games, yet not much like ither game at the same time.

I see many negitive reviews, and it feels like most of them wanted this game to be 100% like dark souls and it is not.

Deck13 has been brave enough to venture into a style of game that has been popular for the last few years
with their first instalment Lords of the fallen that had created some really well rounded game mechanics, but not without flaws here and there.
The surge is what you expect from people. you make mistakes and learn from them.
it is clear that Deck13 has listened to the public as to why lords of the fallen was lacking. Then took the effort to fix thoes problems in their next game. [the surge]

This is a review for The surge, not a review for why the surge is not dark souls.

-great atmosphere. the game has great cues with music for intense moments.
- combat feels sharp and dynamic - with little i-frames on dodge you are ment to use it to get out of the way of attacks, not jump into them as if you were going to phase through them.
- each weapon type has a very distinct feel to them
-game looks fantastic and the level design is fantastic. many times i open up a shortcut and i had no idea i was so close to a previous area [on failed attempts to reach/open said shortcut] the game feels like a maze, but in a good way. the levels feel grand.
looking at this game as a standalone game, the game is fantastic, while the game is 'short' in lengh. the production value is there with fantastic mechanics and great level design.
I know i will be comming back to this game even after i beat it for some NG+ runs

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not one to often do reviews at all, if anything this is my first one. I'm a little bit ways into the game and I've just to say, to whoever developed this game, good job.

I see a lot of people comparing this game to Dark Souls, and quite frankly, yes, it has a lot of elements similar to Dark Souls, but with its own little twist. One of my most enjoyable things to do in the Souls series was to collect weapons and armor sets to be a fashionable undead knight.

And whilst some armor sets are cryptic, which I found rewarding due to how they were hidden, The Surge's system of systematically ripping the armor straight from your enemies limbs is just such fan service for me. It's great. Being able to mix and match what you want, and yank a weapon from the enemy just to beat the snot out of his friend with it moments later is truly a blast.

Breaking away from this whole "Dark Souls" comparison, in all honesty, the setting, the story and the environment is giving me the most nostalgic chills back to Bioshock 1 and 2. I can't explain it without you going through the game and finding all the little audio logs like I am.

I don't want to droll on for too much longer so I'll just conclude it here. The setting and the look of everything, and how the enemies function is very appealing to me. And the combat is crisp enough to keep me engaged.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm approaching this as someone who'se never played a proper 3D Souls-like before, although I played enough 2D Souls-Likes to know what I'm getting into. I'll keep this short.

*Combat mechanics are good early, but fall apart towards the end as everyone describes. Every button hit after the first attack sends the character into a (painfully slow) combo animation that can't be cancelled. Uncancellable combos suck hard at the end game, where enemies attack harder than the player and are often capable of one hit kills if not blocked or dodged.

UPDATE: The game has been patched and the endgame is alot more forgiving now. Instead of 1-shotting the player, later enemies more reasonably 3 or 4 shot the player. The hard hitting attacks are now telegraphed better, and blocking is much more reliable.

Animations are also somewhat cancellable with blocks and are more responsive to dodges, now.

*No ranged weapons in the game, end game enemies come with healing/strength increasing drones that absolutely must be taken out first. Player has a Drone that can attack at range, but it's powered by a guage that only fills up while you're fighting, so more often than not you find yourself needing to get beaten down just to try and take out the enemies buff drone. Overall, the drones hiding behind mobs, and the flamethrower guys are the bane of endgame corridor fighting.

*Story sucks. It wants to be System Shock, but nothing makes sense and audio logs and convos talk in generalities that don't go anywhere. An entire in-game area is basically a plot thread that comes to a dead end.

Despite all that, I don't really regret the purchase, it's a decent gear grindy game where you get power armor and smack sh*t down (EXTREMELY satisfying and weighty smacking sh*t down, the combat feels right.) Not having played other Souls games, I probably don't know any better though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbout halfway through the game so far, and I'm really enjoying it. My thoughts so far:

+Combat is challenging and feels hefty.
+Limb cutting mechanic is an interesting approach to collecting upgrade materials.
+Great level design thus far. Areas feel large and twist back onto themselves in a very DSouls manner.
+Setting is interesting. I havent arrived at any major plot twists/explanations yet, so I can't comment on the overall story.
+Good amount of polish to the game.

-Some mechanics are unexplained or explained far too late. Why am I halfway through the second area before I get a tutorial prompt letting me know I can dodge and counter-attack high or low attacks? How much benefit am I getting out of weapon proficiency scaling? How can I tell which attacks are blockable? Seems really inconsistent.
-The high contrast industrial lighting in most places looks great and adds atmosphere, but at times it can make it hard to see your character and close enemies when standing in shadows. This is especially problematic as you need to be able to see the enemies initiate their attacks. I raised the gamma level but it still is hard. Some more ambient lighting would be nice.
-Rig-mounted lights are terrible for all but one suit I have found. The scarab suit gives an omnidirectional glow which is handy in the dark. The rest are spotlights that can only point directly ahead. I would have prefered to use the drone for light - it could hover above and behind the player to illuminate a larger area in exchange for not having access to its attacks while the lights were on.\t\t\t\t\t
