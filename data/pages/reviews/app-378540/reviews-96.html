\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot a Soulslike fan, but I enjoyed The Surge. I like it because it makes more sense to a western person (unlike Japanese Souls games). The combat is also pretty fun and rewarding. Loot and crafting are great because they aren't random. Level design wasn't great, I got stuck many times. The designers don't really use things like sounds or lights to guide the player. I was playing this one night, quit after reaching an objective and the next day I couldn't remember where I was supposed to go. The game doesn't give you a map or any kind of a log, so I actually had to look up a walkthrough just to get my bearings on my next objective. I also disliked how little story you're given. I remember thinking to myself as I loaded up a save, "Wow, 12 hours already? I still have no idea what is going on story wise." Weapon / stat balance really needs work for Surge 2 because in this game, there are stats like stability that are pointless and weapons like the twin rigged ones that you won't ever want to use over a spear for example.

All in all, having gotten this game on a sale, I definitely felt it punched above its price point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst saw the game on release and from looks at the gameplay thought it wasn't worth the 50 quid price tag.

Finally decided to buy it on sale at 10. would say it's a fair price for what you get, it's no where near as good as dark souls and the gameplay is NOTHING like dark souls so bare that in mind when buying it.

The only thing that this game shares with dark souls is the differculty and my god this game is hard. you'll start by fighting enemies with predictable patterns and easy attacks to dodge but by the second area it just becomes unbearable. from my first 5 hours of gameplay i kinda figured out how to properly approch the mobs. some will have what i'll call 'sweet spots' areas where if hit they'll stagger or even get knocked down, and then some... won't. and these tend to be the mobs duel-weilding or using staffs which in this game are the fastest weapons... and from the gear avalible to me they are just unstaggable which means they ust swing wildly at me stagging me endlessly while i futilely attempt to dodge attacks... which brings me onto the biggest problem with this game so far... dodging...

so... you come accross a mob who runs at you like a madman and then lunges straight at you at about neck height... in dark souls you'd roll under it, or dodge into it expecting I-frames. now i get i-frames don;t make sense in some situations, but in this game i feel they are needed. you're only option is to not dodge into the attack but rather to dodge out the way, which brings you miles away from your target so when you're ready to go at him, he's already ready to lunge again... and remember i said the duel-weilding and staff mobs have no sweet spots... well they're the ones running at you like madmen.

so to summerise for 10 quid defo worth it, don't get me wrong i love punishing games... but i prefer them to be punishing when i make mistakes... not make the right call.

i wouldn;t recermend if you are after a 'dark souls-like' game because this really isn't, neither is lords of the fallen which is made by the same company.

5/10 and that's generous.\t\t\t\t\t
