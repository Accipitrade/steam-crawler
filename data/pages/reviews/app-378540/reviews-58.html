\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanna like this game, I really do. I do like it in some aspects, but there are some things that I just can’t get over. Things I love about this game are stealing equipment, combat (sometimes), it’s concept, and the upgrading system. Now for things I really really don’t like. First off combat at times is atrocious, they’ll jam you into small areas with 1 or 2 enemies and expect you to be able to maneuver when the camera is just willy nilly. Another thing, I don’t think this game is made for combat where you’re facing more then one person. It’s incredibly grating to fight two people at once, or drones for that matter. There are multiple parts where you’ll fight a melee enemy, and you’ll fight a drone that can shoot you from 10 feet safely away. Everytime you get shot you’ll stagger as well, and some of these melee enemies are incredibly aggressive. Twin rigged enemies, I don’t feel are balanced well, they’ll break your guard easily using their weapons. When you use their weapons however staggering someone is insanely difficult. The one huge thing I detest in this game is ♥♥♥♥♥♥ combos. I don’t want them. I really do not think they’re a good idea for a game like this not at all. Pressing the attack button once and having it produce two subsequent hits on an enemy is just incredibly off putting and lots of the time you’ll get hit and lose health in the trade. Some combos you can’t even dodge out of, which is incredibly unfair to the player if you can see an attack coming you should be able to evade it granted you press the button in time. Besides those big things my main gripes with the game stem from getting caught on geometry, unreliable dodge animation, (I don’t recall) the parry mechanic ever being explained and stamina use as well. There are times when I’ll run out of stamina and my character will not move at all. Not being able to dodge, run, and swing while stamina is gone is completely understandable, but not being able to move thats way to harsh. Maybe it’s another thing that’s happening to me, but my character coincidentally will stop moving when I’m out of stamina and there’s an enemy in my face ready to wind up and saw me in half. Lastly, the Fire & Ice DLC hamfists several enemies into an already difficult enough game. They’ve been placed around the map, and they have incredibly volatile weapons. I think they’re introduced way to early on, and represent a huge challenge that the rest of the area doesn’t present at all. They’re like mini bosses that can heal full up. The one that comes to mind mostly is the zombie that wields MG Ignis, he has killed me more then any boss in this games. He flies around the screen or teleports if you wanna get technical and performs combos that last forever and you cannot block the fire damage that comes along. His attack patterns are floaty long and almost seemingly random. MG vigiles also comes to mind as it’s owner is surrounded by 8 enemies? They’re are 8 single rigged heavy enemies in a small closed space, and Vigiles is sitting in the middle of them. It’s incredibly daunting, and I feel that was a poor design choice. There was one more huge thing I wanted to say about this game, but I just can’t seem to remember. I think the checkpoints and shortcuts are pretty generous, but it lacks a map that I’d enjoy in a game like this. In a game like dark souls, I could do without a map, but for this I’d like one. I don’t know if I like this game. Boss fights are fine. They’re just fine, they’re not bad and they’re not good. If you’re craving more dark souls like I was, this game may not be for you, like it wasn’t for me. I think it’s interesting, and I’ll definitely finish it, but I just think it’s incredibly unrefined. Please don’t think of this review as someone crying because they weren’t good enough to beat the game, I’m going to beat this game I very much intend too. I just think this game could be done way better. I’ve beaten all the souls game, so I’m used to feeling the learning curve, but I’ve never thought of dark souls as unfair for this game though, I certainly have. I recommend this game because so hope people can find my review helpful, and maybe you’ll enjoy it much more then I do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeck 13 seems to improve with every release of their fighting focused games and while it isn't until The Surge 2 that they really knock it out of the park, the first game is still an excellent take on the Soulslike formula and absolutely unmissable for any fans of the genre. Unlike Lords of the Fallen it manages to find an identity of its own and though certain mechanical implementations do have the ability to frustrate (the slow to disengage lock on camera after a klll, cycling between enemies with LT being more difficult than necessary etc.) if you perservere it will likely end up feeling as a great addition to the genre. Do be warned that the game is decently challenging, but mostly fair once you get used to its tendency to implement assasination spots with the enemies and learn to master spacing, which is the key to fighting and when used properly can goad most enemies to do the attacks you want, when you want them to.

Both DLCs are also worth it, though one of them is quite different being a mini roguelike of its own.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore starting I want to point out that the only souls-like game I have played before The Surge was Dark Souls 3.

The Surge is a futuristic version of Dark Souls. It lacks the lore and unique locations of Dark Souls, but it more than makes up for it in gameplay. Surge has crazy good, responsive controls, making combat real smooth and fluid in turn making fights really enjoyable and engaging. There isn't a whole lot of variety when it comes to basic enemy types, but those that exist are interesting enough and give quite a challenge. I found Surge to be more challenging than Dark Souls 3 for the most part. The Surge only has a few bosses throughout the whole game though and they aren't too special, however I still enjoyed fighting all of them.

Most of the game takes place inside a facility. Meaning you will be indoors more often than not. Interiors look more or less the same in every area, though developers did a good job of mixing dark claustrophobic corridors with bright open arenas. There is no map, no mini map, no flashing arrows guiding you towards the next objective. You as a player will spend majority of your time exploring the facility searching for ways to advance towards your goal. CREO facility is a massive maze with many paths and shortcuts intertwining and looping back on each other.

Visually Surge looks good, but its setting doesn't allow for anything breathtaking since it is essentially just a big, often dark industrial facility. It does, however, have outstanding lightning, particle and other effects. I won't dive into story and voice too much because there isn't much to say. Story is lacking, voice acting is acceptable considering it isn't a big deal without a decent story anyways.

If you're looking for deep, rich lore I suggest you to pass, but if you're just looking for excellent souls-like gameplay this is the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel miserable saying this is Sci-Fi Darksouls 3. Because it really transcends that game's limitations, puts a new take on the weapon system, and quite creatively reinvents some of the mechanics.

This game is not easy. Do not expect someone to hold your hand and ask you where the "jump-scare enemy" touched you on the dolly...

The levels are complex three dimensional landscapes with twists and turns. However the designers have done an incredible job of making circular paths that constantly fold in short-cuts back to the med-bay (bonfire). Other quality of life improvements are plentiful, compared to DS3.
- Bank your scrap (souls) so any extra you have aren't being risks on your next adventure.
- Weapon combo system gives you creative ways to avoid the enemy, or punish them for blocking.
- Don't like your build? Completely redu it by switching out your weapons, armor and implants.

If you're a fragile, delicate, dainty flower and die... Hit Alt+F4 fast. When you load back in, the save state will be from an earlier time. It doesn't auto-save like DS3 on death.

However cutting off heads, arms and legs can't carry an entire game on its own, you can do more combos in almost ancient now Jedi Academy. Also it's nice that player can get stuck if his favourite weapon is a "sword", a lance, or double-bladed weapon. Some weapons work well on all levels while these ones often are too weak, the result is that you end up as a dancer, not a warrior.

The main story is a standard Souls-like dark theme, humans playing god - bad, nanites - bad. Very basic seen in other games or films. More "just to be there story" than an intersting take on what makes us human.
The thing is, DS games are atrocious at telling the story, but the world and characters are intersting and deeper than a spoon.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has some very strong points over other souls likes as well as points that make it not able to leave a very good impression with me after beating the game.

1. Atmosphere.
-This game has an incredible atmosphere. You can pick up audios while exploring levels and listen to them while you fight enemies. They are mostly very well written and a part I really love about this game. There's even section that have a horror-like atmosphere and very dark parts, which can be annoying but serve that tension-rich atmosphere very well.

2. Game play.
-The philosophy of the game play is pretty hard to grasp on as a souls player, as the principles differ quite a lot from a souls game. In a souls game you tend to be able to fight in a certain way and are able to overcome any enemy if you are good enough at it. In The Surge you very much NEED to fight a certain way against enemies with different weapon classes. Good thing is: Every enemy has some way and tricks to make them defeatable in a smooth way. You just need to find it out, which can be VERY frustrating as enemies tend to one shot or stun lock-instant kill you, as they do so much damage.
Elseway, the game play feels pretty nice, you just need to be careful about combos, because at some point they won't cancel anymore for you to dodge. Enemies jump at you very precisely and fastly, so it's hard to just run past them, yet very possible. If you want to play this game well, you would take each enemy on one by one and take a lot of time. Haste is the reason for defeat here. I often didn't like that.

3. Level Design.
-The level design is one of the weaker points of the game in my opinion. You spend a lot of time tracing your steps back, being confused and running around cluelessly until you finally find shortcuts or a hint of where you are. The last level is the worst of all there. I hate the architecture of it. A good thing about it though, is that if you feel like you found everything and finish the level, the satisfaction is great. But yeah, most of the times, there's just too many branches and ways and everything looks similar.

4. The Story.
-The story of The Surge is pretty cool, but the best thing about the story is not the progression of it, but getting knowledge about everything that happened previously by having it presented to you with promotional videos from the big company at reign, audios and environment. It's awesome to puzzle that together. Apart from that, the way your story goes and the little side stories of NPCs hit just the right spot. I was quite impressed by all the storytelling, world building and such for a Souls like. Dialogues are all well written and very well spoken as well. A strong point of the game.

5. Bosses.
-They one or two hit you often. They can stunlock you. But It's awesome to find out what their weaknesses are and fight them, and apart from the ridiculous damage they are well designed and very cool to look at. They are more strong than weak, but you feel that the gameplay is less focused around fighting bosses than enemies at your height. I enjoyed them anyway.

So if you read all that, I think it goes to show that the game makes a rather strong impression. It has awesome graphics, is very well designed, the music is passable and it has even more depth to it with all the weapon mechanics, specialities and even lengthy descriptions for every tool you get. Yet in the game play and game experience itself it has some of its biggest flaws, which harms the experience a little.

Yet if you want to play a high quality souls like with a nice story and unique environment (for souls likes) you will find a very strong game here.\t\t\t\t\t
