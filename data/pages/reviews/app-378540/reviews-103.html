\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is a game that I keep trying to learn to enjoy, because the first like 10 hours really are great. In the beginning it's nice learning the different mechanics such as being able to target limbs to obtain the loot items you want, which cuts down on the grinding that you find in other games, such as the souls series. However as you progress the difficulty curves at an unsual rate. When I say the diffficulty curves I mean they just give certain enemies weapons that do 10x more damage in their hands than it'll ever do in your own,*cough cough* security guards *cough*. As you progress through the game you find these enemies and your first instinct is to use your combos you've learned to use for the first half of the game, but all that gets you is a quick death. Eventually the game strays away from doing cool combos to simply waiting for the enemy to do a certain attack, jabbing them once, and hoping they don't nip you with a quick attack. This, along with the lack of directions the game gives you, really wears down your drive to continue playing the game. I didn't even know you could block and it's actually viable until the fourth boss in the game. I'm in the final area and I constantly find myself begging for the next doorway to just be the final boss so I can just wrap up the story, which is actually really well made. Where as game like the souls series you can figure out where to go and you really don't need to look things up I have no idea how anyone beat this game without looking up at least one tip to point you in the right direction or searching to see if the spider enemies are really this hard or if I'm just bad at the game.

There are a few points that work both in favor and work against The Surge, one being that armor from a defense perspective is virtually useless, I wear one the heavier sets yet still get tossed around because eventually every enemy hits harder than a truck and leaves you wondering why you even bother wearing armor. The pro to this is that it gives the player a choice to wear whatever they want. You want to wear the first armor set? That's completely viable because every set has a set bonus and in the end that bonus is what you wear the armor for.

All in all The Surge is a game that I really do want to love and put hundreds of hours into but it just doesn't allow me to fully enjoy the game. I would recommend this game to anyone who likes a challenge and finding out how to do stuff on their own but I must warn you that at times this game is harder than the souls series and it doesn't always leave you feeling accomplished when you finally beat that boss or unlock a new location.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is a good game that wasn't handled too well, there are a lot of design flaws that hold it back from being a really great game.
The combat is repetitive and tedious at times, the world design is linear and relies on vents too much, and the story might as well not exist for all it adds.
Despite all this and a few more nitpicks it can still be a fun experience, the first three out of five bosses are challenging and creative, the idea of hardcore challenge kills for boss weapons is fantastic, the looting aspect for smaller enemies is creative if a little botched.
It's a very short game with a lot of headaches to deal with, but if you enjoy 3rd person combat, buy it when it's on sale, it's worth a try in my honest opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really really wanted to like this game. I'm a Souls veteran, having played through all three Dark Souls, Demons Souls, and Bloodborne multiple times. I also love sci-fi, so the combination of a Souls-like style of game in that context sounded like it would be a perfect game.

I am what I imagine is probably about 2/3 of the way through the game and I think I'm finally giving up. I made fairly good progress up until now but at this point I am just not enjoying myself anymore. I find myself dying constantly, possibly due to being underleveled, but I think the biggest problem is that I just feel like I'm forcing myself to keep going. With the proper Souls games, even at their most challenging, there is still this feeling like I want to push on, improve my skills, persevere. Here, I just have stopped caring. The enemies are mostly challenging in the sense that they are super annoying. The combat system is somewhat lost on me. I get some aspects of it but it just doesn't seem interesting or fun enough to make me want to get good at it and learn its intricacies. Everything is super repetitive, from the level design to the enemy design. Half the time I have no idea where I'm even going because it all looks the same to me. Another corridor with an exolift at the end of it. Another guy carrying a big club. Another guy carrying some sort of saw. Another guy carrying a staff.

In a sense, I think it's unfair to compare this game to the Souls games because this is certainly trying to be its own thing. It does some interesting things differently. The gear system and the ability to chop off components from enemies and craft new gear is a nice touch. It's just a shame that the combat itself is clunky as hell. Half the time when I take hits from enemies I don't even understand what hit me or how I could have avoided it. The comparison to Souls games is less about this specifically trying to be like them and just a worthwhile comparison of how to do basic gameplay of any kind well and how to do it badly. Souls, for all its imperfections, has great gameplay. This, not so much. I hate not finishing games that I put a fair amount of time and some money into, but I'm afraid this might just be one of those times.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I went ahead and picked up The Surge after I saw it on sale. I really wanted to play it since release but the reviews were always mixed feelings on it, so I patiently waited until it went on sale.

It took me roughly 13 hours to complete the main game and I am currently playing it through on NG+. That's relatively short for an action RPG of this type, I'll explain why later. If the game had been 10 euros cheaper at full price, I'd probably have given it a pass for being short, considering NG+ does offer replay value, if for no other reason than to locate all the collectibles, audio logs, comic books, armor sets, weapons etc., as well as backtracking a bit more during the story to get more conversations out of the NPC's.

I've seen a lot of people complaining that the envirronment and the enemies are a bit repetitive with no real variation - which is mostly correct, at least when it comes to the base experience. In NG+ the enemies are a bit more varied, mostly with new NPC variations in weapons, armor and spawn places early on, to give you the true NG+ experience of throwing a major spanner in the works to keep you on your feet, now that you already know what you're going to be facing.

However, I'd argue that the level design is crazy cool from an objective point of view, almost to the level of Bloodborne when it comes to shortcut porn you're going to witness. The level design DOES get confusing at times, specially if you're like me and have trouble navigating rather lookalike corridors that snake and bend non-stop. Add in a little spice in the form of different floors and it gets hard to keep track at times of exactly where you are and where you're going, but you do get used to it after some time, so don't be discouraged.

The combat is hands down what this game does so well that it made me want to do NG+ at least once. The tiny addition of forcing the player to choose if they want upgrade materials and a longer fight with little stagger potential OR they want an easy kill for faster tech scrap (XP/currency) grinding is honestly great. They also had the great foresight to add the mechanic from Lords of the Fallen (yikes) - a game I would rather forget honestly - that I really want in more action RPG's. The longer you go without returning to your level-up/crafting point, the more XP you obtain from enemies, meaning if you don't mind the grinding, it's relatively easy to go out and just grind out a bunch of enemies to give you that extra bit of materials to upgrade your weapons/armor and the extra XP to get your core power increased to allow more/better implants to be utilized.

Overall or TL;DR, I do want to give this game my recommendation, specially if you can snatch it up on sale, even if it's only 10% off. The core game is great, solid replay value, the story can be confusing and rather mediocre, but let's be honest, the "plot" in an action RPG like this is not what most people play it for, enemy design, at least for bosses is cool, although there's certain design choices that are downright strange (I think we can all agree that any kind of "boss gauntlet" is lazy design), but despite that, the game kept making me want to see what's around the next corner. There was even downright times where I thought for sure that the game was gonna end in the next door, but kept going for a solid 2-3 more hours before coming to the conclusion. If anything I'd say just play the game and enjoy it for what it is. Plus, Deck13 has done a great job of adding new weapons and cool armor sets to the game FOR FREE, so go support them! Super excited for a sequel if it comes around.\t\t\t\t\t
