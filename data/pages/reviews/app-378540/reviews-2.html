\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge Rating - 8.0 out of 10.0

This is strictly my own personal rating system/opinion. Ever game starts with a perfect 10.0, then I deduct -0.50 for every issue I run into:

- Timer to get your scrap back isn't needed. The game is brutal and having a timer is added stress to our already stress filled lives.
- The run back through areas cause you forgot one key item or to talk to someone gets very repetitive.
- Not very many weapon play-styles. Some of the weapons are cool but just wish there was more.
- The overall game is very linear. There's not much deviation, Dark Soul games suffer the same fate sadly.

I highly recommend this game to anyone that likes a Half-Life 2, post apocalyptic setting. The game is fun and brutally challenging. It seems to be better to use a controller but isn't necessary. Expect to die Warren... and good luck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, 40 hours in and completed the game + both DLCs (Frontier Raiders and CREO Park)

The Surge is like a Dark Souls but with a Deus Ex Machina skin, so aside from that "Techno Skin" and setup, what's new? The combat.

It has something in its combat style that other souls game don't: in adition to what we are already used in a souls game (dodge, parry, attacks, etc.) you can focus different body parts (head, torso, limbs) not only to cut em off and farm the armor the enemy is wearing, but also to focus on unarmored parts to make some fights incredible easy for a souls game.

Now on the down side: There is a mechanic that, for the life of me, i never could include in my rotation of attacks when facing different enemies: The jump and Duck while you are blocking. Personally i could never really put that to good use in combat, it felt super clunky and the pay off is not something worth. Thankfully, all enemies have different approachs for you to decimate em once you learned their moves, so kudos to the devs for not forcing you to master said mechanic.

Also there's not a parry button, you just have to use the normal block right before an attack hits you to make the enemy lose balance. Which makes a bit of sense since you have a killing animation for each limb you cut from the enemies, so adding parry animations could felt a bit too much.

The weapons: There's really not much variety on em, and i don't mean the quantity, but the quality. At most you will change your main weapon 3 times in your entire playthrough because of stats. That's not good in my opinion. Not because you won't aquire new ones, but they all have the same attack pattern depending on the class of the weapon. And most of em have the same exact values for stats.

For example, you use a Single-Rigged weapon and that's it, you already saw the whole thing for every Single-Rigged weapon, they all have the same combos, with the exception of some Boss drop weapons that have an additional flashy attack. And even those ones are super hard to get because in order to obtain em you need to kill the bosses in a specific way, which of course you won't be able to know how to do it in your first blind playthrough (bosses do not respawn and theres no hints in the game about those special kills).

Now on the other hand, even with the negative points i said, the combat is SOLID. Once you practice the basics and get used to it, its an amazing experience.

Also keep in mind one important thing the game does not tell you: There's no stats re-spec.... because theres no stats to assign to begin with, you level your proficiency with a weapon class just by equiping it and using it, so if half-way the game you decide you now want to use other class of weapon... you gonna have a hard time, because you won't do any damage, so you need to go back to lower level areas and farm that proficiency with the new class of weapon you choosed. It's not good but also not bad, is just a different system so thats a discresion of each player taste.

The story: ....meh, good, nice. Nothing that other games with a set-up in the future of technology did not touch in a more profound way. Props to the opening scene of the game in the train station tho, very well done. Not much in terms of visuals, but how it makes you feel.

The mechas/robots: On point, you face different mecha enemies (mostly bosses) and their visual design and fight mechanics are very cool, nothing to call home about tho.

Enemy variety: For the lenght of the game, the variety of the enemies you will face it's on point too.



So to close this review i give the game a solid 9/10. Totally worth it and i had fun the full 40+ hours i put on it.

If you compare it to other games or souls game The Surge may not be the top dog, but its humble on what it does, because what they do they do it right and in the right ammount.

By the way... long live IRON MAUS!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI Finally beat this game after several years of playing it on and off. This may be the most 7/10 game I have ever played, which I mean mostly as a compliment, but also slightly derogatorily. It has a million ideas and it really goes for all of them, and in many ways it actually does succeed! But it can also feel sloppy and unfair nearly as often.

The combat system, where you can target specific limbs and chop them off after doing enough damage to acquire those gear parts from enemies, is legitimately very cool. Once you get the hang of it, the combat feels pretty good and has an interesting pace and rhythm and set of tactical considerations to it. When you are fighting humans. They put so much work into making this system feel good with humans, the most common enemy type, that fighting anything else can feel very janky and vague. The limb targeting still works on robots and such but only kind of, and their designs can make reading and reacting to their animations very difficult. And of course non-human enemies tend to be the ones that do the most damage and can often kill you in one or two hits. Many of the deaths in this game did not feel like they were my fault, and were instead due to jank, vague animations, weird hitboxes, and things that do way more damage than they look like they should.

The level design is also highly mixed. On the one hand the environment art and attention to detail is fantastic, and I respect how ambitious and complex they can be. On the other hand, they are often incredibly labyrinthine, far more so than anything Fromsoft ever made, and because they are primarily indoors there is a lack of landmarks to aid navigation. Many times I unlocked a shortcut and was extremely confused about if it had actually helped cause I could not for the life of me tell where the i even was in relation to the OPS room. I did eventually learn my way around, sort of, but there was a lot of me wandering around going huh??? and I would say that I am pretty good at navigating and internalizing complex 3D environments.

So the game is a mixed bag, but where it excels, other than the combat against humans, is in its setting, presentation, and worldbuilding. The whole industrial dungeon + techbro horror vibe is super well done and the constant PR interviews and marketing bits playing on screens throughout the CREO facility are pitch perfect. The writing was sharper than I was expecting too. This game has a pretty clear viewpoint on silicon valley, climate change, the exploitation of workers, and it ties it all into the setting and level design pretty well. The NPCs are some dark souls ass NPCs to be sure, but that style of writing transplanted into this sort of setting is surprisingly novel, and they do a good job making them feel distinct. The sad, vaguely inspirational country song that plays in the OPS rooms is also an absolutely inspired choice, its exactly the kind of thing a corporation like CREO would pipe in to breakrooms to try and connect with and motivate its workers. And like, it literally works! I'm humming it to myself right now! I felt relieved every time i heard that song! You are so right The Surge, I was always as free as I wanted to be, always as free, as a bird.... It is true that you cannot miss what you have never seen before. And weren't we all born in a prison? The prison of this modern tech obsessed society???

I think what is the true mark of this game as a 7/10 game (complimentary) though is that I am still thinking about it a week after finishing it. Its a genuinely weird and ambitious little game that truly does go for it in all respects. Its got gumption and you know what I gotta respect that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed it before and wrote it off because I thought it was too difficult.
Gave it another shot recently and It's now one of my favorite souls-like games.

Granted I still have a few gripes about the game, such as:
- Most of the later maps are a bit labyrinthine in structure and it's really easy to get lost when trying to traverse to new areas
- Plenty of shortcuts made for the medbay (this game's bonfire), but good luck remembering them
- Block/parry system is abit jank, sometimes your blocks will register and you can counter-attack and sometimes the enemies' attacks will go through your block like a knife through butter.
- Difficulty scaling for enemies is a bit ridiculous come later levels (looking at you bloodhound robots).

Other than that, the setting, the combat, the various weapon types to choose from and the move sets that they have for each I pretty much enjoyed. The story is not much to write home about, but there's much worse stories out there. The game did it's job, and I more than recommend this game for anyone looking to scratch that souls-like itch.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game with the caveat you are going in with the expectations that The Surge is not Dark Souls.

The similarities are heavily influenced by Dark Souls in the sense that regular enemies kill you in a couple hits, rest areas that are checkpoints throughout levels, interconnected shortcuts, and can lose your level up material if you die before recovering it. I personally think the emphasis is more on the levels and normal mobs, not necessarily the bosses. I say that because I somehow beat the game in 26ish hours and there is only 5 bosses. I probably have several hours that are AFK, but still. For reference, I beat dark souls 1 with all DLC in 22ish hours. What is there, 21 bosses in DS1 if you include DLC?

The one thing that really got me (for better or worse) was the interconnected shortcuts. I love that you will have 4+ shortcuts back to the same rest spot that gives you that "Aha" moment when you figure it out. That being said, the other edge of the sword is that the interconnected shortcuts can be very disorienting at times when you are running through dark narrow tunnels that have multiple branching paths that also have their own multiple branching paths that can look samey.

(Purely anecdotal, but I was literally lost for an hour and a half trying to figure out that I had to backtrack. I blame the booze and smoking, but still...) I even looked up videos to show me exactly where to go but the videos on Youtube were let's plays that you have to sift through 30+ minutes of samey locations with numerous shortcuts and still get lost. (Skill issue)

Some last thoughts: It definitely has it's own spin on the soulslike genre.
-You can target body parts to get crafting material and schematics to make gear.
-There is not much for build variances. You are a guy in a wheelchair who wears an exosuit a la-Avatar so you can walk again. (I literally wore the first levels gear the entire game)
-You have fast, medium, and slow weapons with their own scaling. You don't level up stats; you level your overall ability to wear gear and attachments.

In my opinion, It's a fun game that scratches that Souls itch while having it's own spin.
