\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonally i always have a open mind for new hardcore ARPG games, partially because DS's combat system is kinda plain for me, but i do enjoy challenging gameplays. The Surge managed to construct a new style of combat system, including new move sets, damaging system and targeting system. The combat system itself i would say it is a huge success, since i am dodge main i do have a pleasent time using light armors taking down human enemies within seconds, the block does not work well, however i actually never tried to block so i will skip on that.
Yes the combat experience is not perfect, since i hate dealing with robots, they are either too meat or requires too much repetititve work such as trying to get to the back and backstabs them, and they do not move like humans, they basically make 360 turns according to how they like, and the movesets are sometimes too fancy that makes your can't dodge in time, still overall speaking i think TS does have a fluent combat experience, with fancy movesets that gives player a more refreshing look comparing to DS.
The level design might be the main problem, since every level looks the same and feels the same, basically factory or ruin, and unlike Nioh or DS or BB, which all 3 of them managed to make every level looks different and refreshing even though it follows the same theme or age or style, but TS failed to do so, which is why i was not really motivated to play constantly, in DS i might explore a little bit of the new level after the boss fight even i decided to go play other games or go to sleep, well TS, i just takes a quick look, knowing it looks same, bank all my scrap and go take a break, especially i am really not a fan of factories and underground.
I always appreciate small and young studios because they are always dare to make changes, in TS they added bank system, added limits to recover scraps and added body dismemberment, and a lot of new small stuff. For all the small stuffs, i don't think they made the game easier or harder, it's just about how much effort should i put in or how should i put it, neither other hardcore ARPG have to take the small stuffs, i mean i definetly hope that you can bank souls in DS but still that's how small stuffs made games different from one another, and i certainly not hoping that all the game uses the same stuff.
Overall, i do recommend Hardcore ARPG lovers to try out TS, just to feel the new stuff. Hardcore ARPG is a small circle and i do truly thank TS for bringing new stuff to it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay so when Lords of the Fallen was released in 2014, I knew straight away it was a game I wouldn't have bothered to complete as it lacked many things. It was a variation of it's own hardcore action RPG content yet an alternative to others such as Dark Souls. Anyway when I heard about the Surge I looked into the gameplay trailers and what I saw was beyond Dark Souls content, in fact I personally wouldn't say it is a sci-fi Dark Souls. The Surge is a unique hardcore action RPG like no other. You can focus your attacks on a specific body part, your attacks are based on vertical and horizontal directions which allows you to mix up your combos in a much more flexible way and that makes a difference when you're using a weapon against different enemies, you can adapt. The game is perfectly balanced in combat, you will die at some point every once in a while but every attempt you make, you don't have to change your role. you eventually discover how your enemy works and his weakness regardless what gear you're using. This is one of the best games I have ever played out all the years since I was a kid and I'm excited to discover more about the back story. I definitely recommend this game 110%, well done Deck 13 for doing a great job and thank you. :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHow do I even start? Well this game is definitely similar to Dark Soul's system, borrowing the hp, stam bars. There is another bar added called energy, and you can use it to summon a drone, heal, get stam, do finishers, etc.
So... let's get into what's awesome:
1. The bosses are cool as hell
2. Your character gets customized into a badass
3. Mob farming gets fun unless you didn't upgrade at all
4. You feel really good about finishing this !A@!@# game.
5. The tactical limb attacking gets really strategic (i.e. get weps and armor or farm for currency)
6. You can definitely call yourself a reflex lord if you beat the game
7. Gameplay is fast and exciting enough to not be stagnant for at least 24 hours.
8. Gitting gud and having the right equips make farming a ton easier.
9. Very realistic moveset if you actually had a rig and lifting equip
10. 2 ways to kill each boss(different loot for that)
11. I like the exo-lifts
12. System isn't overly complicated, you get used to it really quick

What sucks imo:
1. Tracking can be way too good(i.e. security bots hitting you with anything within range or bosses hitting you way too many times :P aka not much of a delay)
2. Farming the literal crap out of everything
3. Sprawling map can be really hard to remember or traverse
4. Enemy line of sight is ridiculous sometimes.
5. Why are there manholes everywhere?!
6. No character customization
7. No pvp?
8. Too few bosses for the map that is listed in loading screen
9. Kinda meh story
10. New area? Oh damn I just got shafted by security dude, bot, etc. (Methinks a patch for damage reduc is good)
11. Range weps don't exist, only melee
12. Can't change color on armor...maybe that's going to change?

Neutral about:
1. Stamina....I think its almost to the point of DS2's stam system, but I don't recall
2. The whole boring factory thing? eh...I'm more offended by terrible lighting in most areas.
3. Timing on loot is kinda meh, later on in the game you're less affected by losing it.
4. The counterattacks and the attack animation precedence makes me happy and sad in some cases(hits Cerberus with hammer, regret forever).
5. Late game enemies...kinda too scary to effectively enjoy fighting

Honestly this game's pretty good if you like being a fast aggressive player for diving in and out. You can block, just that bosses probably aren't blockable in most cases.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have roughly 35 hrs on this game atm.
I think the game was pretty fun and refreshing up to the first boss. Farming mats and gear with the limb-cut-off method gave a fresh feeling about it. At least if something dropped you know what part that would be. The sci-fi feeling also a plus.
The level design is terrible especially because everything looks the same (that is why the game is only 10GBs) and 3 out of 5 bosses are basically almost in the same area but you have to find your way there. One could say that is a good thing but honestly there are so many service routes and "hidden" paths that you will spend more time finding your way back where you died or to the right path to the next boss than fighting against them. Let's take Dark Souls 3 as an example here, basically if you beat the game at least once and you see a screen shot of an area you can tell where the screen shot was taken, not so much in this game so good luck with that.

The lock-on system and the camera are just pain in the butt. Every freaking boss has many legs or arms in this game and locking on the right one will be a challenge. I don't know if the devs actually ever played this game because that is one of the most frustrating things in the game.

If you want to combo just make sure you are ready to be punished by your enemy. When you think you stun-locked a mob with your fancy looking combo the next thing is you find yourself locked in an animation while the mob will gain super armor and take 2/3 of your health down of even one shot you. The only viable move I found is the dash attack.
No eye-frames for dodging most boss moves, most attacks happen so fast you can't properly react to them.

Since this is a souls-alike-wannabe game I have to mention that in Dark Souls or Bloodborne if you kill a boss you feel some satisfaction after the boss is down. Let's say you kill Midir after 50 tries, 3 controllers and 10 rage quits and you may still say that yes it was a challenging and epic fight. If you look for that feeling this is not the game you are looking for.

Haven't completed the game in its totality, but so far I am very happy with my purchase.

Like most people say, this is a Soul's kind of game, but I honestly feels they have improved in areas that the souls game have not, such as the fighting mechanics, and dodging mechanics. Where instead of just simply rolling yourself out of danger, the game forces you to be more precise with your dodges. The weaponry have a more realistic feel to them, for example; heavy looking weapon feels heavy not sluggish, or a faster pace weapon feels exactly like it looks (fast Paced).

The story so far is nothing to brag about, but I mean, Soul's story isn't either to be honest. But if any of u are looking for a Cyberpunk-ish Soul's game this is it!!!!

Be warned!!!! You are going to get your♥♥♥♥♥handed to you the first few hours. Upgrading armor takes a while, and enemies usually carry heavy weapons, so the damage you take (no matter how strong you make your armor) will never compare to a bullet sponge (Unlike Dark Souls).
