\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA nice soulslike with a fun dismembering mechanic to grind gear.
The different sets give you bonuses and most of the weapons are kinda different from each other. The mechanics are smooth most of the time and the combat in general is a lot of fun.
The map design is cool and really reminds of Dark Souls 1. You unlock a massive amount of shortcuts which makes progressing really fun.
The story is okayish I guess. It's your first day at work, but everybody but you is in machine-zombie state, or an aggressive robot, so you hack and slash through all of those bad guys, while listening to mediocre audio-recordings, that tell you, that something isn't going the way it should at your new job.

But there are two big issues that really ruined the game for me in the first play through:

1 The bosses kinda suck. Plus there are only really few different enemy types.

2 At some point, the enemy-placement really starts to ruin the experience. I just didn't want to play anymore.
You hated the stupid little corners and narrow passageways in Dark Souls 2, where they just placed the enemies in, so you get ambushed? You hated it, when you can't get through by running, have no space to dodge and just have to go back and forth only going for one hit after another, because you can't dodge to the sides?
And theres not even a parry mechanic to shorten the pain? Sounds great, doesn't it?
And it's terribly annoying when those passages are on the way to a boss, you gotta try a couple of times and you always have to kill this ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ robot, that sits in this stupid narrow air vent, which is the only way to the boss arena.
Well in the last third of the game, you got a lot of this stupid sh**.

I didn't play NG+ but there are some doors, that are locked behind a lvl-cap in the first play-through and I was too lazy to backtrack and check everything. Maybe i come back a bit later and open those in NG+ to get some more of the shiny loot.

TLDR:

Good game with okay story, satisfying combat, nice loot mechanics, but it has some issues with boring bosses, enemy variety and enemy placement.
Buy on sale and get ~ 15-25 h of a good Soulslike depending on your skill and how much you want to explore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is a very good 'souls-like', perhaps the best I've played so far. Its combat is much faster, with the ability to dodge around instead of roll, and the way they handled equipment and upgrades is unique. Instead of finding things laying round the world, you actually have to sever the limbs of the enemy wearing the armor you want. Want that helmet? Decapitate enemies that wear that helmet. Want that weapon? Cut off the arm that holds it. It's pretty awesome, and makes farming for gear more interesting (as opposed to, say, hoping for a random material drop from a particular type of enemy). And instead of a typical stat system, you have implants that have a variety of different effects, enabling the creation of many different builds, regardless of armor choice or weapon choice. It's very challenging, even to those used to these kinds of games, and it has a good pacing with how much of the story it gives you as you go along (just enough that you get some idea, but also want to learn more so you continue pushing forward). It's a solid game, with a lot of potential to get even better, and I'd recommend it.


Below are all the problems I had with the game, but I still recommend it nonetheless:
Bosses are a little uninspired, with one boss being a strong version of a regular enemy, and another late game boss involving multiple battles with another boss from earlier in the game...very lame, especially when there are only 6 bosses in the game. The combat system, although cool with the limb severing and combos, becomes bland because there are not many enemy types. The game is stuffed to the brim with the same enemies, and it gets boring to kill them. And if you're rolling with a light armor/low health build, all of them can 2 or 3 shot you no matter what point of the game you're in (your character doesn't necessarily progress in health or damage like a traditional rpg, so you don't get that satisfaction of crushing enemies that used to trouble you but now can barely damage you). Shortcuts alleviate the monotony a bit, but the level design doesn't inspire exploration since they look very similar throughout the game. So you got similar enemies in similar crammed metallic corridors throughout the entire thing. It's like they wanted you to speed run their game.

You also have a lack of weapons and character customization. In rpgs, some of the fun is with making your character look badass, or at least the way you want them to look. In this game, I used the same armor set throughout the entire thing thus far (except for one section of one area, because of toxins, but I didn't really need to do that) and the same 3 weapons. Honestly, I'm only using the staff at all to try and introduce more variety into the combat. There's not enough variety in the weapons, and once you play it for a little you'll realize that there's really only one weapon worth using (which you get from the very first boss). Finally, I am at a part of the game where the option for a huge amount of backtracking is introduced...and I just don't care to do it. Dark Souls has a recursive nature, you repeat but progress at the same time, like a spiral...this game feels more circular, you are going around in circles trying to find the end. But in no way is this a 'bad' game, I've just seen all this game had to offer hours ago, and the whole thing is just variations on that theme. For me, there's not enough incentive to struggle through to the end. I enjoyed my time, but at about 30 hours I was getting bored, just speed running until I unlocked the shortcuts (if I could get that far). It's interesting to think about why Dark Souls is so much more compelling than this game...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll try to keep this as short as possible.

I'm really passionated about this game, and canno't recommend it enough if you're in search of a challenging Hardcore-ARPG.

Let's not beat around the bush, the comparison with the Dark Souls franchise is inevitable. So let me begin by saying that if Dark Souls hasn't been developed to be hard, The Surge definitly is.
Wether you're a so-called "souls veteran" or a newbi to the genre, you'll get your ass handed to you.

That been said, the game isn't unfair. If there is few design choice that are questionable, you have all the tools to overcome them.

There is a reason why the first boss has been beaten by 54,1% of Steam owners and only 32,2% have beaten the second boss. (05/04/2019)
The second area will make you feel like the tutorial is over. I always say to new player : "If you can beat the second area, you can finish the game."


Most encounters in The Surge are 1vs1, and each and everyone of those encounter are meant to kill you. Needless to say that exploration is pretty intense. But as I said earlier, the game isn't unfair.

The combat system is faster and more complex than Dark Souls. A video is worth a thousand words, so I encourage you to look at this one showcasing some of my gameplay : https://youtu.be/LAXe0mFFEPo?list=PLQO3V6CuABJQcnGO3Q0OmFSmYzUJ0jSMG&t=362

I can't go over the combat system without talking about the limb targeting and execution system.
To get new schematics or farm crafting materials, you need to perform an execution while aiming at the limb you want to cut (head, body, arms & legs).
To be sure to get the "cut", you need to deal more damage to the limb you want to cut. For that, it's required that you learn the different combos of your weapon (RB, RT ; LB, LT). Because some combos might do more damage to other limbs than the one you are targeting.


Builds depends on your set of gear. When you equip a full set of gear, you are granted a bonus set. There is 15 different set in the base game (+free DLC ). So if you want to try a lot of different things, you definitly can.
I made some exemple of builds for the wiki.


All areas have one Operation (OPS). During your exploration, you will discover a all bunch of shortcuts leading to it.
The level design is very intricate and can be consufing at time. But there is sign almost everywhere, indicating you what is near. Pay attention to the name of the areas, use the maps speard around CREO and put the informations together to know where you are and what is surrounding you.


My playtime might make me sound like a fanboy, but don't worry. I am well aware of the cons of the game and am the first one to point them out :
- drone is almost useless
- serious lack of diversity in the ennemy department
- not enough boss
- level design often uninspired with those narrow corridors
- forgettable NPCs (sidequest)
- forgettable OST
- no waifu material

Some would point out that the proficiency scalling doesn't encourage trying other weapons, and I would tend to agree. But in practice, it's just not true. As long as your weapons doesn't have at least ten level of difference, the balancing is still very good.

If there is one thing that I could be reproching directly to the developers ; it is their bad habit to "hide" stats from the player. Indeed, the way stats or set bonus works are pretty obscure...
Obviously, the more you know and understand the mechanics of the combat system, the more enjoyable the game become.
That's why I tried to give a maximum of information about stats on the wiki.


If you read that review, you should have a clear idea of what The Surge is about, and at the same time having all the tools to enjoy your experience.

If you decide to buy the game, I wish you the best !

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've seen many compare this to Dark Souls. Big no-no.

There are similarities, yes, but this game's combat is much more dynamic and free-flowing. You are more agile and stamina is much more forgiving once you start picking up modules that enhance their speed and capacity, allowing you to be more flexible in your fighting styles. Healing is also very forgiving once you gain modules that let you heal off of energy generated from striking enemies, provided you are agile enough to not take too many blows in the process.

Lore and storytelling is somewhat similar to the Souls series, except for the fact that you are experiencing the fall instead being placed a distant future from it.

Cons:
-My biggest complaint would be the heavy usage of maintenance corridors, which can get rather repetitive and could even easily turn into a maze for confused first-timers to get lost in at the last level.
-Shortly after that is a small gripe that melee is the only build you can work on; there are no equivalents to archery/magic builds.
-Lastly is the small number of bosses - although the non-damaged weapons they drop (provided you defeat them in a particular manner that makes the fight harder) is completely worth it as those weapons trump their ilk and scale well into late-game.

I wouldn't say this game is better than the Souls series by any stretch, but as it stands on its own it's still certainly no slouch. Pick this up if you want a more adrenaline-fueled and brutal experience than the more careful and tactical approach of the Souls series.\t\t\t\t\t
