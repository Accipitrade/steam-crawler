\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game itself is unique in the way you build your character and obtain loot. Combat is fun and satisfying. The way you build and upgrade your armor is interesting and motivates you to collect the pieces you want from enemies.

Unfortunately after a while it really felt like it outstayed its welcome. Except for the first environment the entire game feels like you're running around in factory doing the same thing over and over again.

I felt like it got repetitive and the enemies became more of an annoyance as a single hit is enough to take half your life bar away, a small mistake is enough to make you have to go all the way back and do everything over again in an environment you've had to look at the entire game.

Eventually near the end I lost interest to keep going and even close to the end I just dreaded those last parts of the game that I stopped playing.

I recommend this game as it's still a fun experience for a great number of hours but at some point for me it felt like it became too much of chore to actually see it through to the end.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game with a decent story and combat system. The graphics are top-tier, and lighting is done incredibly well. Combat is enjoyable and difficult. Light weapons kill slowly but are easier to hit with, while heavier weapons present the challenge in that with enough knowledge of patterns, enemies are quickly defeated in a few seconds. The implant system and leveling are also great. Since leveling is based on core power, for difficult early segments, it is possible to overcome them by grinding for power for more implants while getting to know the combos and patterns. The maps are intricate, but present many shortcuts to prevent having to redo segments every time you need an upgrade. Enemies also act to jump-scare you on multiple occasions, leaving you peaking corners every time you pass one. The only complaint I have is the combat system and terrain synergy. It's terrible. At times you find yourself fighting on the edge of a platform, and since attacks auto-lock and combos often carry you forwards (also of note is that the second attack of 3 move combos will trigger the third, and can't be canceled), you're often forced off the edge when the enemy dodges or walks in the direction mid-attack.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7/10 - A wonderful Sci-fi ride despite flaws

Gave up first time through at 2 hours; after running through DS trilogy came back and was satisfied.

+++ Scratches that souls-like itch well
+++ Nice Sci-fi setting; lots of original ideas put together in a tight package
+++ Excellent difficulty curve with circling back to old foes to highlight your progress
+++ Could be a clone of Dead Space with different mechanics & much higher difficulty
++ Fantastic world design. Truly DS1 inspired to connect new areas to old ones. Extensive interconnected 'world'
++ Combat is satisfying; Finishes slightly slower than ideal in repetition
++ Small dev team; from that perspective its great what the game achieves
++ Exit game from menu & fast forward credits is very much appreciated unlike FROM games -_-
++ Items & weapons limited but well thought through. Sufficient variety without burdening the player with choice.
++ You can overlevel to breeze through difficult parts
++ Fast farming is excellent +XP items, don't heal to increase gain rate and use workshop to reset enemies, +Gain life on finisher to eliminate healing reliance, +profit
++ Some gorgeous art and designs really bring you into the world
++ Unique bosses and tough enemies to really brings highlights to the experience
++ Nice intro & tutorials
++ Game is quite epic though runs out of ideas as its winding down and does the generic approach seen time and again
++ Finished the game without blocking

--- Voice on entering/exiting 'bonfire' becomes a drag; TV voices pour raindrops of annoyance daggers at your skull
-- Game tone undefined. Serious, sarcastic, funny, ridiculous - all over the place. Voice actors performance is so poor it brought me out of the experience regularly
-- Glitchy death falls all over the place. Hard to tell what dead zones are. Finishes sometimes cause you to fall.
-- Have to figure out what to do next time and again. Game expects quite a lot from you throughout.
-- Finished the game without blocking

A good experience with some annoyances. Deaths - bosses: 10, enemies: 50, falls: 80. I took advantage of over-leveling so would say this is easier than DS in some ways\t\t\t\t\t
