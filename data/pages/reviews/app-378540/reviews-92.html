\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I love this game. It flows nicely, the graphics look great (though "high" does not always mean realistically gorgeous detail , and I enjoy the story. The mechanics are interesting and new. I recommend you buy this game at full price just because it really pays offf in potential playtime. Anyway, here's a little list:

PROS:
  • Plenty of play time; should last a while.
  • Really cool gameplay mechanics! You damage specific body parts and chop them off to unlock weapons or armor, depending on the part. It's pretty satisfying to both have the option of chopping an enemy to bits or slicing their whole arm off, and the knowledge that in so doing you'll get a sweet new weapon or the blueprint for new armor.
  • Very diverse options for playstyle and decent enemy scaling.
  • Interesting story with various characters found through environmental elements, item pickups, and gameplay events.
  • You can add .wav files to the "songs" folder in the game's files to easily make your own playlist of songs for the Base Camp to play.

    NUETRAL (pro or con depending on personal preference):
  • A lot of grind. This game will expect you to leave base camp, do a thing, and return to base camp. Again, and again, and again. Even though I enjoy idly repeating actions and feeling my character get stronger every time, this may not be for everyone. If you like Monster Hunter, Warframe, Dark Souls, etc. you may like this game as well.

    CONS:
  • Graphics are, under close scrutiny, not really as "high quality" as they may initially seem. However, setting to high graphics quality does seem to create an undue amount of stress (I'm on an Alienware 15 laptop that's approx. 2.5 years old, no notable issues) and causes my computer to heat up a bit. In other words, I think optimization could be a fair bit better. Overall, though, I do think the game looks pretty good. Just don't stare at anything and you should be fine.
  • No ability to reset past saves. While not a problem for most people, I find this a little annoying when I screw up really bad and lose stuff. Not a huge issue, but I couldn't see this really being an attractive element.

    Overall, I really love this game. It's definitely "more than a sci-fi Dark Souls," but it has a lot of what I initially wanted from Dark Souls without the need for a controller and with some sweet new mechanics.
