\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was debating whether or not to buy this game and ended up picking it up the last day of the steam sale.

First of all for all you moose knuckles, I'm not going to compare it to dark souls. Yes it lifted the souls template, but it's still very different.

Very fun game so far. The combat is fabulous. I also really like the level design. It's very interconnected and the game doesn't hold your hand by giving you a map or anything of that nature. Simply awesome.

There are some interesting mechanics - the implants allow you to have a dynamic character. I've seen complaints about the weapon proficiency mechanic, and I get it, but at the same time I've been switching between 3 types of weapons regularly depending on my whim and it hasn't hindered me at all. In fact I'm equal on proficiency in staves, twin rigged, and single rigged.

Negatives:
The story is nothing great, but still serviceable.
I've had a few crashes here and there, but never lost more than a few minutes of progress knock on wood.
Bosses aren't all magnificent.

I have no qualms recommending this to anyone who even thinks they might like to sniff a game of this genre. In fact, I'll be a witches moose knuckle if this isn't one of the most fun games I've played in months.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough I have played a little bit of Nioh and Lords of the Fallen, I have never played any Dark Souls title before, so, this review is only about this game!

Imho, everything about this game is great: graphics, sfx, environment, gameplay, well, everything indeed. So I will only talk a little bit about the gameplay. Although I love it, some other people might not fancy it that much.

The first thing I would like to pinpoint is that the game is very hard in the beginning. Then, you start to learn how the game and the enemies work, and it becomes a little bit easier, and you become more powerful with tons of weapons and armor to choose from, and you think you got the games number, and the game slaps you in the face while you enter the next level. It's an amazing experience.

Your character can be anything between a ninja and an "unstoppable" terminator, but be advised, stick with your choice from the beginning or the game might get a bit harder.

The thing I like the most about it: the leveling up. Your character doesn't level up, your rig, weapons and armor do. The more you use some type of weapon, the better you become using it.

Anything else I might say, might spoil the fun of it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview in Deutsch / English.
Eine neutrale Wertung wäre mir lieber, da das Spiel in gewissen Punkten tatsächlich glänzen kann.
Beispielsweise die Möglichkeit Ausrüstung und Waffen von den Gegnern zu erbeuten indem man die Gliedmaßen abtrennt, Modfikationen in Form "Implantaten" am Charakter vorzunehmen ( Mehr Gesundheit, mehr Geld usw.) und einen recht hohen Schwierigkeitsgrad, wobei wir dort zu den negativen Aspekten kommen.
Die meisten Gegner sind zwar oftmals langsam und nach dem 4 aufeinandertreffen mit demselben Gegner weiß man wie dieser sich für gewöhnlich verhält, dafür sind die Schläge so hart dass man spätestens nach dem dritten Treffer tot ist.
Hinzu kommt, dass besagte Gegner oft durch die Gegend glitchen und der Kampf unausgeglichen ist.
Einfach gesagt: man erkundet ein neues Gebiet, stirbt immer wieder bis man kein Bock hat, macht weiter und stellt fest das man auf dem zurückgelegten Weg nur eine Abkürzung nach der anderen geöffnet hat bis man dem Boss entgegentritt und in ein neues Gebiet startet.

I would rather give a neutral rating because there are several aspects where the game can really shine. For example the possibillity to get the gear and weapons of your enemys by cutting their limbs, or the modification of your character with "implants" to raise your health bar or increased money gain. The enemys are strong and there is the turning point.
In most cases the opponent is slow and after fighting the same enemy 4 times he gets predictable. Nevertheless it is useless to know what kind of attacks your opponent is using because at least you die after the third hit or they even glitching around.
So after exploring a new area and fighting the same enemys a dozen of times you are pissed, get on, beat the boss, enter new area and get pissed again because you die again. And even if you explore a map without dying you get pissed because there are only shortcuts on your way to the boss that lead back to the starting point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally enjoyed this one, had low expectations after Lords of the Fallen but got it and the DLC on sale for $20. I haven't really truly enjoyed a souls-like since the original Dark Souls. The change to a sci-fi world in The Surge and the interconnected maps was enough of a change to bring me back. The DLC map was a bright spot overall, fighting mascots from a theme park had a sick twist to it and kept things fresh. The combat is solid, the different weapons/move sets are varied enough and the abilities you can stack can make for some really interesting builds. If you are looking for a breath of fresh air from the same dank dungeons, mystical cities and pitch blackness in the Souls series this may be for you.

Downsides are the repetitive hallways and lack of enemy variety. It could have been longer as well but I may go back for a NG+.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was pleasantly surprised by the quality & fun factor of this game and enjoyed it far more than most AAA titles. I finished Assassins Creed Origins prior to playing this and that was an absolute bore in comparison. The difficulty can be quite high but there are many tips & tricks you can find online to help out such as a few basic moves that the game never really tells you.

This game encourages paying close attention to signs, markers and other hints in the enviroment. Much like the Souls games there are no maps or waypoints and you really have to find your own way which is what I prefer.

The levels can be quite maze-like and confusing but I really enjoyed the design. The combat can seem slow and annoying until you find the right weapon for your play style and learn how to manipulate things properly then it becomes very fun.

I recommend playing with the DLC right from the start because you can access it on the 2nd level. It really adds some much needed variety and it smooths out the difficulty curve.\t\t\t\t\t
