\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA solid 8.5/10 in my opinion. Had way more fun than I expected and immediately went for NG+ and finishd it, and that's something that I hadn't done in years.

PROS:
+ Addictive loop of looting, crafting and upgrading. If an enemy has a weapon or armor, you can have it as well.
+ Good damage system based on body parts and armored vs. unarmored.
+ Nice industrial setting and atmosphere that offer something different than we are used to in the genre, combined with a metroidvania type of exploration. It is even pretty gory in some places, which reminded me of Dead Space.
+ The graphics and performance was really good for me (played it in 4k).
+ Quite a nice amount of weapons, armor and implants to suit your preferred build (fast, mid or heavy). The sets all looked different, detailed and cyberpunky.
+ Flexibility to change your build on the go. You may have to grind for upgrading materials if your armor or weapon had not been upgraded, but other than that it is totally flexible.
+ The story is nothing to write home about and is provided mostly through dialogues and audio logs, but at least it is decent and interesting to keep you intrigued.
+ The loading times were amazingly fast, just a few seconds, and that was on an HDD.

CONS:
- The maze-like areas and labyrinthian corridors make you very prone to getting lost, and it can be frustrating a lot of times, especially when having to retrieve your lost tech scrap (which btw is on a timer). I liked the metroidvania influence, but the game could have used a little bit more of distinctive elements to help with orientation.
- A lot of backtracking and not so many ways to go back directly to earlier areas. The shorcuts help but good luck remembering them.
- The targeting system was the worst. You can only change targets by deselecting them and selecting the one you want and in order to do that you also have to stop moving for some reason.
- Some junk here and there in terms of textures and bugs.

In conclusion, the game might have some flaws, but I absolutely recommend it to anyone who enjoys souls-like games but also want a change of setting.\t\t\t\t\t
