\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I had heard some mixed things about this game before playing it and having played it I can sort of see why but I still enjoyed it overall.

The core gameplay is good. It is a slightly simpler souls like with a few twists that keep it fresh.

If you’re not really looking for anything too in depth here are a couple basic pros and cons:

PROS:
Combat is satisfying
Unique leveling system
Cool weapon and armor designs
Challenging boss fights
Nice atmosphere especially in the scarier moments
Cool universe with some decent world building

MEH:
Level design
NPCs
Not many weapon varieties
Not many enemy varieties
Story has its ups and downs

CONS:
Game is buggy and not so polished
Main character's voice acting sucks
Story doesn't stick the landing
Pasing issues near the end

Ok, so here are all my thoughts:
I call this a simpler Souls like for three main reasons.
1. There aren't that many types of weapons and they don't all play as differently as you might expect. So, at the end of the day there are only really 3 types of weapons. heavy, fast and staffs (which are also somewhat fast but do play differently enough).
2. The second reason is that you don't encounter that many types of enemies and as with the weapons some of those are essentially the same as others but only take and / or deal more damage.
3. The last reason is simply that it is a shorter game and there aren't that many distinct levels or environments.

These three things didn't bother me all that much personally, but it is something to keep in mind so as too not be disappointed if you plan on buying the game. In my opinion what variety the game has does a good enough job at not making things get stale and it complements the overall world building and story fine.
I've also heard people don't really dig the level design and I get it, they are very mase like and places are not very distinct, but I liked it for the most part. The fact that there is no map sort of forces you to create a mental image and shortcuts are still very satisfying when you find them even if you don’t always remember what led where. What I personally didn't get at all were all the useless maps scattered around the world that were as helpful as asking a 4 year old for directions.
There are two twists on the Souls formula that make this game unique:
1. The mechanic to chop enemies limbs off to get their gear for yourself which works as beautifully as it sounds, it also makes harvesting for gear very nice as you won’t be grinding for hours trying to get a random drop.
2. Leveling up doesn't give you better stats, instead it raise your core power which you use to equip mods you can change whenever you're in your base, which means you can basically respec depending on the situation and you have great freedom to mess around and see what playstyles you like more, especially when you pair this with the armor set buffs.
As for the story, it has its highs and lows.
At the start you are just thrown in to this world and the bits of story you get don't really do much to get you interested in what is going on or why you are doing what you do. Most of the NPCs you meet are pretty bland and pretty soon you’ll get tired of people telling you they don’t know what is going on.
As the game moves towards its third act and you’ve collected more audio logs things start to get more ominous, sci-fi and far more interesting. For a while there is some very nice build up but unfortunately there is some awkward pasing in the final moments and the game just sort of ends with a lack luster and easy boss fight and cliff hanging cut scene which just left me thinking “oh, it’s done”.

As for some of the game’s mayor cons, I would have to say its mostly a matter of polish. I found the game to be buggier and glitchier than I expected. This ranged from enemies getting stuck in certain places, wonky camera movements, the freezes that last a second that were pretty frequent, finishing animations would often feel off like something didn’t happen the way it was supposed to, sometimes I would get weird startling sounds when actions didn't go through as expected, camera got fixed on a random spot once and I couldn't move it or use the lock on feature so I died, once my character got stuck in the slow walking animation and I had to restart the game, etc. There were quite a few of these which took me out of the game.
That’s it. I recommend it for what it does at its core. Kept me entertained for a quite a while.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood game and I would recommend. I'd say if you want a easier, scifi souls-like experience this is a good, cheap game. Picked this up on sale awhile back, and played/beat it whilst on a recent souls-like gaming kick. Already bought The Surge 2 for my next game.

Combat is solid. Atmosphere is good. Plot is nonsensical and told via exposition-boxes laying on the ground, but that type of "storytelling" is a genre standard. Also doesn't overstay its welcome, with a relatively brisk 20-hour campaign. Difficulty is right-on (basically every level has only 1 "rest/save" point so everything is out-and-backs or opening shortcuts back to this singular location). As the game is short, it has the standard difficulty ramp-up from easy zombies to fast moving terminator lookalikes to insta-kill nano deathballs.

Biggest complaints is that though weapons are nice and varied, armor is useless. It is one of those games that "if you get hit, you are in big trouble", so armor has no impact outside of aesthetics. You could do the game naked and outside of the armor-set bonuses it would have little-to-no game impact. Other complaint is that the healing is way to generous... by the 3rd or 4th level I never died due to lack of healing for the rest of the game (I still died... just not from lack of healing). So you had almost no resource management issues.\t\t\t\t\t

the bosses are interesting and fun, even the infamous one that suddenly makes you unlearn the way you are trained to play.

the level design is where this game shines. the designers really set a mood with this game, and you really feel like you're moving around in a giant futuristic factory. you hit a lot of walls as you explore, but you don't ever feel like you are on rails. this game takes the souls series' knot-like level design to entirely new realms. some of the really tough parts of the game are made rewarding by suddenly turning a corner, and opening a door back into safety, or a place you saw but couldn't previously get to.

i'm sure this is nothing new in a review for this game. it's solid, it's its own thing, and it's worth a playthrough for the story\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBottom line - excellent sci-fi souls-like, recommend!

Combat is meaty and visceral, and throughout the entire game almost every enemy encounter can be fatal, not to mention having to fight 2 or more enemies at once. Every weapons has a set of combos. There's also a bunch of advanced combat moves that aren't explained that good in the game but add to combat diversity.

Nice build variety allowing you to play with different rigs (sets) and their set bonuses, as well as implants (talents) appreciating different play styles. It all boils down you having an amount of Points (basically player level) which you can distribute freely between Rigs and the stats they provide - heavier and protective rigs are more expensive on the points - as well as healing items. Esthus-style flasks also cost points, along with healing-over-time and "convert-rage-meter-to-health" type of items and finisher healing, so players can select to either carry lots of healing, or invest points in combat capabilities and rely on finishers. Really like this take on resource management and the risk-reward it allows.

Adding to risk is no system to return to previous "bonfire" or any kind of teleportation between levels - you have to walk the entire way, risking dropping your scrap from defeated enemies (XP) which you have to regain in under 3 minutes if you don't want to lose it.

Levels feel much bigger then they are thanks to excellent design. A lot of shortcuts and openings, often so complicated it's hard to keep a mental image of the level.

There's not a lot of bosses in the base game but they're well-designed with the exception of that one fight. They're quite challenging, have multiple phases and never feel unfair (again, not talking about the 4th boss).

Sci-fi scapes are dark, and the game has nice ambiance to back the grim atmosphere. Graphically the game is alsolovely.

The story is never fully explained and relies on player collecting and listening to audio logs, and I really enjoyed the story behind The Surge.

Overall great game, enjoyed it a lot.\t\t\t\t\t
