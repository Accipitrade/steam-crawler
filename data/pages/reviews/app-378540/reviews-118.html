\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt was billed as the Dark Souls of the future, but better. I was no fan of Dark Souls but I thought that this would be worth a shot. I was wrong. This is not Dark Souls of the future, it is Lords of the Fallen (Dark Souls, except you know, horrible) with a thin viniere of tech and voice acting. It still has all of the same problems that those games have but without the feel of being new or the excuse of medieval combat for lack of ranged. In this you are stuck in a repetitive hack and slash with a dearth of skills or abilities, a dearth of any real customization, and a dearth of depth. My biggest complaint of Dark Souls-like games is that enemies respawn and it turns into deadly Farm Simulator. This made the same crucial mistake. Once something is dead, leave it dead so the story can progress. After a while this grind will settle in and you will get bored very quickly. I had hoped that the pace would pick up after the first stage, but it just didn't. The only thing that changed was the color of the bad guys and the difficulty got ramped up. Now I don't want to hear "learn to play", I can do all the combos just fine, but that is the problem. You spend all of your time coming up with some, lets be honest, dull combos but no plot development or memorable characters. I just played this a couple of days ago and I can't even come up with the main character's name. Add all this together and you end up just doing the same thing over and over to win and that is what is wrong with games of this type. One combo with one weapon class is all that you need to rip things to pieces. What I was hoping to see here was a hack and slash with some abilities from the engineer in mass effect. I wanted to freeze, burn, zap and smash my way through a legion of robots and beat some bosses, all while swept along in a cyberpunk dystopian novel. What I got was well, Dark Souls, we already have a Dark Souls, we don't need any more. Please Focus, come up with an original idea for once in your lives, please.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is a game that clearly took inspiration in the "Souls" formula (just like their previous title, Lord of the Fallen) and did a lot of improvement over LotF (I think they use the same engine).

  • GRAPHICS - The have stunning graphics with the exception of AA, which is atrocious.

  • PERFORMANCE - Stable 60fps most of the time with i5 4670, GTX 970 16gb RAM 1866, dropping to 50 in outdoors (which is fairly rare btw). Volumetric Lights OFF and Shadows on MEDIUM, otherwise I would experience much more dips in FPS.

  • SOUND - Sound effect of metal clashing they nailed it. It feels heavy, as it should, since we use pieces of heavy machinery as weapons. The music is serviceable i'd say. Can't say I even remembered any song (with the exception of the medbay song) even though I played through NG+3.

  • STORY - Maybe spoilers ahead!!! The story is told via few NPCs, audiologs scattered throughout the levels, item descriptions and in some small ways, banners in the walls, consoles and AD videos. I played through NG+3 and I don't know if I find all the audiologs, but I found it a bit vague.

    You can have a general idea of what happened. You enlist for a job in a big company that is trying to save the world called CREO, and after some time, after they installed your rig (the exoskeleton that enables you to walk, since you are a cripple), something goes wrong and you wake up in some kind of junkyard with a robot trying to cut your leg, the whole company is in shambles overriden with killer robots and zombie-like workers trying to kill you.

    I guess that vagueness was also inspired in Dark Souls, which left bits of the story vague for players to speculate.

  • GAMEPLAY - The main focus of the game IMO. They improved dodge A THOUSAND TIMES over LotF. In LotF, the dodge was TOO DAMN SLOW/CLUNKY even with lighter equipments. Now the dodge is fast with any equipment. The only problem I saw with dodge was when the enemy moved very fast and you try to dodge to the left but the camera changed position and you dodge forward for some reason (happens very rarely, but you get punished really hard in this game).

    There's no block like in the souls serie, where you can block while moving. You can only block while stationary and consumes stamina overtime. To get a counter-attack, you have to press block in exact same time (don't know, or in a very close frame) the attack would connect. It's different but I found not particularly effective (I would rather dodge, since dodge feels awesome in this game :) )

    Your "level" is determined by the core level of your rig. This "level" determines what equipment you can use and what implants (Like rings in DS) you can equip. Every piece of armor and implant has a core cost in them, so it can't surpass your core level. There are 3 types of implant: Injectable (heal, damage buffer etc.), Hardwired (can only be changed at medbay) and Hot swaps (can be changed anywhere). It's a bit different which make the game stand out a bit :P

    As you hit your enemy, you charge your energy bar that can be used to perform various actions like, activating your drone, use certain injectables and cutting limbs. it's like a "rage" meter and when out of combat, it stats to deplete.

    Cutting limbs is where the game stand out. When in combat, you can target a part of the body (head, torso or limbs). These parts can have armor in them, if they have you can choose to target that part e attack it. When the enemy is lower enough, and you have the energy to cut, the game prompt you to cut it. The more you damage it, the more likely you will get that armor part. Targeting a armored part deals less damage, but unarmored part doesn't drop loot, so the player has a choice of a bit easier combat or going for the loot. Overall I find the gameplay very solid.

Overall I enjoyed The Surge big time, I only found very short (I think it has only 5 boss counting with the final boss) and plan to get the DLC soon. Totally recomended it (Para os brasileiros, recomendo, mas com desconto, porque acho caro pagar 129,90 nesse jogo :P)\t\t\t\t\t

The game does have some balancing issues. Most fights are unforgiving, and with skill you can get through. But the moment you fight more than one person at a time, you're screwed. Also, the bosses are quite tough. I found myself having to grind a couple times to level up enough to make the boss fights easier, and at least survive long enough not to get one-hit killed.
The odd balancing aside, fanastic game, with a bit of a horror vibe. 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLots of fun. The level design does a nice job of looping back on itself with hidden areas and shortcuts to open up. The combat is satisfying and has some unique mechanics. The story is surprisingly solid and told very atmospherically (think Bioshock styles games with lots of audio logs to find). This game aspires to be dark souls meets dead space as far as combat and atmosphere and while it is nowhere near as epic as the souls games nor as pure horror as dead space it has its moments in both regards. I'd heartily recommend this to anyone who enjoys a challenging action rpg with tough combat and solid character progression customization options. I got about 35-40 hours out of the core campaign and plan to do ng+.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge feels like a game made by people who played Dark Souls but didn't pay close enough attention.

It has all the basics -- similar controls, corpse runs, punishing gameplay, and so on -- and it even has a few twists on the formula that feel like interesting ideas worth pursuing!

But the core components of the game that even Souls at its worst tends to get right, The Surge gets wrong. Souls combos are simple, but satisfying. Predictable by design. You know the range of your swing, you know where it starts and ends and what it leads into. Nearly every swing in a basic string feels useful -- a wide swing, a stab, an uppercut or a downward strike. The Surge includes things like short jabs, elbow strikes, pommel hits, in the middle of combos that also include wide swings or vertical strikes, and it just feels BAD. Animation priority is a fine enough thing, but when attacks feel unresponsive, and certain parts of combo strings feel completely useless? It stops being fine. It actively hurts the core combat when your own movements are both hard to predict (depending on which body part you're targeting, which direction you're moving, etc) and feel bad to use. When you're trying desperately to learn when to STOP specific combos because you don't want to enter into a long animation of your character delivering an anemic elbow strike with an absurdly short range, you've done a bad job.

This would be bad enough, but the level design compounds the frustration. Early on, facing the Soulslike staple of slow zombie-esque enemies, it's easy enough, but the levels do a terrible job of directing you towards the places you're SUPPOSED to go to advance, so you end up doing loop after loop looking for the tiny doorway you missed in the labyrinthine industrial wasteland and factories the game takes place in.

The enemy placement, too, feels bad; one-on-one duels are difficult enough using the combat system, but the game often likes to throw two or three fast-moving enemies at you. You lack the crowd control of a Souls-style greatsword (the larger weapons are so absurdly slow they feel completely useless, leaving you massively open to attack) or any real ranged attacks, outside of a drone you take a while to unlock and which functions more as a device to pull enemies than do damage.

Oh, and you take tremendous amounts of damage as well. A few hours in, even decked out in the heaviest armor you can manage, enemies will kill you in two or three hits. Similar to a Souls game, perhaps, but given how fast the enemies move, the poor combos and too-heavy animation priority, and how your health doesn't level up outside of optional implants you can equip... there are enemies that can one-shot you, from full-health, if you make one mistake. And it's not necessarily a mistake you can learn from!

The Surge has a lot of cool ideas. It's got a great look to it, and the basic movement and dodging feels good. Even the slow and awkward animations might be acceptable, if the enemies and the levels were designed around them; a slower pace of combat might be better, in fact, to ease in new players to the genre. It's such a shame that all the things around its strengths are profound weaknesses. Like I said at the top, their inspirations are obvious, but it feels like they didn't pay enough attention to them.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely in the "Souls" genre, it does enough of it's own thing that I definitely wouldn't write it off as a "Dark Souls Clone". It has a few systems that are its own and keep things feeling different (dismembering enemies with finishers for parts, the core system, etc). The setting is also fairly interesting with a few story threads and some nice hidden "touches" in the story you can find if you take the time to explore.

It's biggest pitfall is that the setting is very similar throughout. The game is the factory. There are PARTS of the factory, but that is going to be your environment for the whole experience. The DLC did try to directly address that by adding a more varied area and it is an interesting area, but it's DLC. Didn't encounter any major bugs or performance issues, and mechanically it all felt pretty sound. It starts out really tough, as most games in this genre do, but as you get more options via the core system (you can get things like health boosts, cores that charge health by doing combos, etc) it gets less brutal as you go in. Definitely worth playing if you're looking for another Souls-like game.\t\t\t\t\t
