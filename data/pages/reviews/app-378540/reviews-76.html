\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: A game that is sometimes very good, with some frustrating lows, occasionally very frustrating bugs.

This review is being written part-way through the game, as of the Black Cerberus fight. If my opinion changes, I will update this.

I originally played this game during a free weekend, and what I saw of it (mostly the first level) convinced me to buy it, at about half-off. I don't regret this purchase, but I think I might if I had paid full retail.

At its core, this is a take on the Dark Souls formula. (Maybe we need a genre name for that, so we can stop calling these things Souls-like.)

Combat can feel very good. At its highest moments, you feel like a man wearing a forklift. Try the Vert-Vert-Horizontal combo with the Firebug V2, for a good example of how badass the combat can make you feel.

Combat can feel very frustrating. Some trash mobs have one hit kill mechanics, as does every boss. I thought maybe I was under-leveled or under-armored, so I went and did the Park DLC (A very good time, by the way.) and came back in upgraded Sentinel (read: Medium) armor with a good set of injectables and passives and... Nope, one shot kill. These are avoidable in general, but it can turn combat in to a stressful situation of avoiding the enemy while it does whatever it does, and then getting a couple punches on it, and so forth. This becomes more problematic when enemies find themselves trapped in corners, and even more problematic at the times where I encountered glitches where the enemy clipped through the floor, and then reappeared behind me, mid-attack. I've also had attacks visually not land, and then .5-1second later, my health bar just wipes out while I'm walking around. Considering the sequel is out, I doubt they'll be coming back to clean up these bugs.

The levels are all very much the same visually, apart from the two DLCs, which did a good job of taking the sameyness of the industrial corridors and slapping a theme on top of them. Progression feels a bit stunted by the fact that you're constantly working on ways to link whatever hallway you're in back to the level's only Operations Room (this games take on the Bonfire).

The gear is interesting, but there's way too much of it and the amount of things you'd have to grind to try even a good % of it out is insane. I found myself locked in to my set once I had my armor pieces and weapon on Mk 3 because I just never farmed up enough mats to change to anything else.

Boss design is mediocre. I don't know how else to put that. Generally the bosses aren't doing much of anything interesting, and it's just you dodging one-hit kill mechanics and taking a couple swipes. Why this game is afraid of partially damaging you and allowing you to properly duel these bosses, I don't understand. It's worth noting that I do not know if you still get one-hit in the heaviest class of armor, but at this point there's no way I'm going back and farming enough materials to find out.\t\t\t\t\t

A couple of things I think would've needed improvement (consider everything else good):

- Too few bosses. Not even every level has a boss. There's only 5 bosses here.
- The level design is very convoluted and it's hard to keep orientation because every section of each level looks nearly identical as the last. If you have bad orientation, don't buy this game.
- Some more enemy variety would've been great. You'll fight the same 5-6 type of enemies but with different weapons.

Overall I'd say get it at a discount and definitely buy the Walk in the Park DLC before you start the game, as the DLC is weaved into the main game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay...where to start with this game.

So, I'm going to do my best to be brutally objectively honest about all aspects of this game. I'm also going to assume that anyone interested in buying it is someone who's played the Souls series and is a fan of those games. (Also, for simplicity's sake, I'm going to use the PS4 controller as a reference point)

Right off the bat, this game has similar feels to Dark Souls, in the sense that you attack with R1/R2, dodge with circle. You have running, dodging attacks, just like the Souls series. You have things to worry about such as stamina management, consumable management. You have a "bonfire" of sorts that is a Medbay, where you rest and upgrade your gear, craft new weapons and armor.

The things this game does well:
-Combat feels smooth and responsive
-Combo attacks are flashy and neat
-You can target specific body parts to sever their armor/weapons and acquire those blueprints for crafting as well as resource materials
-Certain upgrades are cool and effective, but are acquired too late into the game.
-Fights are challenging....maybe too challenging.
-Combat required a strategic approach at ALL times, not a rush in and kill approach.
-Endgame weapons and rewards are really cool, especially the final secret weapon of the game.
-You can equip multiple weapons and cycle between them on the fly.




Things this game does HORRIBLY:
-Early parts of the game are very challenging, and this challenge persists throughout the game.
-Upgrade system is only through getting new gear and weapons. You CANNOT increase individual stats like you can in Dark Souls. This means that if you are not crafting or upgrading weapons, you will never get stronger and you will never survive.
-Enemies have bizarre attack patterns and there seems to be no consistent way of knowing when to dodge/parry/block. This is quite the opposite of the souls games where you can easily learn an enemies or bosses patterns and learn to beat them effectively, knowing that it was your knowledge and skill that got you through a fight. This game is more about sheer luck than actual skill.
-Too many enemies can gank you when you are trying to learn the fight of just one enemy.
-Combat seems too unfair and unbalanced. (You try to dodge but you get hit anyway) I-frames do not seem to exist, and enemy hit boxes are too wide.
-A low level trash mob and a level boss do the same damage to you, meaning you can get 1 - 2 shot pretty much anywhere throughout the game.
-Upgrades for some reason don't seem to really make you stronger as enemies continue to scale and get tougher throughout the game, always getting just as strong as you. Basically, you never feel at an advantage at any point. You just feel like you're keeping up with the game instead of surpassing the challenge.
-At certain points, the game will literally F you over by throwing multiple tough enemies at once in your face at endgame
-Weapons don't feel unique enough to make you think certain weapons are better for certain fights. Anything can be used at any point if you like (which can be seen as a pro instead of a con)
-Dismemberment system doesn't work 100% which can be very frustrating when tackling tougher rarer enemies that drop rare armor parts.
-Level progression and design is ABYSMAL, easily the worst part of this game. Yes, the game gives you many shortcuts to get back to your Medbay (Checkpoint), but that's the problem, there's TOO many. In the Souls games, you always felt like you were making progress, something I call "Forward Progression", where you got to a bonfire, went through the level, found another bonfire, and so on and so forth. You'd find the occasional shortcut, that would link all these areas and that always felt great. However, this game's level designs are so disgusting and so narrow, they litter you with hallways and new pathways over and over until your mind will just twist on itself. You'll never remember which path leads where because the maps are so convoluted. Especially during the last level of the game. You'll want to pull your hair out because you'll have NO IDEA which way leads where anymore. Too many shortcuts and too convoluted and mindbending to try and figure out where to go next. This game follows a "Roundabout Progression" system, where you're stuck in a zone around just 1 Medbay, going in circles unlocking multiple shortcuts(anywhere between 5-7) throughout the area until you find something that will take you to another zone, doing the same thing over again.
-Certain enemies are literally designed to be unbeatable, even at endgame. (I'm talking about you, scorpions). These little pricks will literally leap across a map and one shot you, even when you're trying to sneak past them. Looking online to find a strategy to beat them, they'll just tell you to run and avoid them. That doesn't bode well for a game's overall design philosophy because the point is you should be able to master every encounter. In this case, no. They designed this game to make you feel that some encounters should just be avoided all together, especially since those certain enemies offer no apparent reward of any kind.
-Most enemies are the same old, boring generic humanoids with new armor pieces to dismember. Not much creativity.

All in all, if you like cyberpunk settings, and you like the Souls games enough to try this game out, try and get it on sale. The challenge is too unfair and ridiculous, and this is coming from someone who's beaten ever single Souls/Bourne game, including the new Sekiro and gotten every single achievement in those games. Those games were thought of as challenging, but to me they are easy now becauseI've mastered all encounters. This game is not like that at all. You'll always feel weak and struggling throughout every SINGLE encounter in the game.

I give this game a 2/5
