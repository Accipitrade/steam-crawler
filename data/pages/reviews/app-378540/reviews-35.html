I never managed to get it done before (bought it in 2017) but was always very entertained by it. Grim setting and storytelling which is underlined by the grim gameplay. It can be really punishing, but so satisfying at the same time.

Think of CREO Technologies, the company which hires you at the beginning and at which factory the whole game takes place, as a persiflage on Google. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finally got to play The Surge in 2021 and I definitely do regret not playing it sooner! To summarize, this is a very good game in its own right regardless of the Dark Souls inspiration behind it's mechanics.

There are two main pillars of the game which really stand out. The world design and the combat loop.

The other aspects like the story, voice acting, the score are functional and in no way elevate the game.
The visual design is a mixed bag but when it shines, it shines so so bright. Walking down a dark corridor with a fantastic lighting system was reminiscent of the way games like F.E.A.R, Doom 3 or Dead Space used ambient lighting to accentuate tension of navigation. Additionally, the industrial stylistic choice and giving each of the weapons and armor sets an in-universe explanation for why they exist makes it a coherent experience and massively accentuates the strength of the coherence of the setting.
The animation work while visually impressive does impact the flow of combat so just like the world of the surge, I'll have to loop around back to this.

The game world is designed as a set of 5 hub worlds with one of them acting as a central point to which the other hubs are connected. Each hub world is equipped with an OPS center which acts as your base of operations for character and equipment upgrades.
Each individual hub consists of a layout of rooms, corridors and deadly enemies where the paths consistently diverge, twist and turn, over and under until they loop back into your base of operations.
Central Production B, the central hub world which I mentioned above, is hands down one of the best level designs I have encountered in gaming. Period.
However, to contrast that there are some areas in the latter half of the game that do have some interesting ideas which are let down by its execution.

A lot of complaints with the game I've seen tend to be about the repetitive and lack of distinguishing features among the environments. I can see how someone who relies on visual landmarks to navigate around the world will suffer and that is primarily down to the same corridor preset fabrications used for the hallways connecting areas. If you can look past this, then you will definitely get a sense of enjoyment from exploration in The Surge.

The elevator pitch for The Surge's gameplay can be summarized in one word - "dismemberment". It is a bloody and brutal world, where limbs with armor / weapons attached onto them get hacked off by a cavalcade of weaponized industrial tools.

This allows for a brilliant take on the regular grind fest a loot based game can have as you get to choose what armor parts or weapons you want to chop off for schematics and upgrade parts.
I appreciate that the game respects my time and would rather make the game engaging than pad it out with repetitious runs of grinding for upgrade currency.

Your engagement with the combat is going to be dependent on the weapons you decide to use. There are 6 classes of weapons and each one of them comes with its own very unique style of disposing of enemies.
What dictates whether you will like a weapon comes down to whether you "click" with the weapon's animation timings.
If you do not like the feel of the weapons you start out with, keep persevering till you find a weapon class which suits you.

Do give this game a try. It does occasionally go on sale and is well worth the money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game that I find myself playing more often than I would think I would. I've played about 12 runs between the PC version and the PS4 version. It is a Souls like that's set in a future style company/research facility with a unique combat ability to target different body parts. This alone makes it neat and worth looking at at least. The story is kinda cool. You play as a guy crippled from an accident and bound to a wheelchair. He has been given the chance to work for the company CREO, who will supply him with his own "RIG". RIGs in this game are exo suits that are drilled into your skeleton and brain and allow you to lift massive things effortlessly, also allowing you to walk again. While undergoing the surgery the titular "SURGE" happens and everyone but you has their brain fried. This creates almost all of the enemies you fight in the game. Which sadly I now have to talk about the combat. The actual combat is pretty rough and hard to get used too because there is many things the game flat out does not tell you. The game has a bad tendency to lock you into combos (both ones you are doing and ones enemies are doing) that make it really difficult to engage with the enemies properly. I eventually learned this is because I was hitting the attack button too many times and it would save up to I think two button presses after the first. This wouldn't be so bad I the enemies reacted to getting hit like Warren (MC) does. When the enemies hit you you will stagger unless you are using the heaviest of armors and they can and will stun lock you to death. You could block but that takes up most of your stamina and will still end with you in the enemies combo. The key thing that makes the game different is the targeting system that lets you target limbs of pretty much everything you fight. In almost every case this doesn't really do much aside from letting you get armor pieces and weapons from enemies. With non-human enemies you can occasionally break something off them and when it comes to bosses this is almost needed to ensure you don't die. It really should have been implemented better but it is what it is. The weapons are pretty unique and cool to look at but almost all of them follow the same kind of flow in their combos. It then goes down to what can hit the hardest the fastest. This leads to most going to the single rig line of weapons because if you do hit and run tactics of dash hits and sprint hits there is no other weapon that hits harder for the speed you can do it in. I couldn't tell you why I've played this as much as I have (I say that about many games) but there is something special here that I can't just put into words. I played through this after a few years to get a refresh in my brain and getting 100% achievements, and I would say I like it and would recommend it but it doesn't feel like you should pay more then $15 for the augmented edition.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's really difficult for me to review this game positive or negative, but since I'd give it a "mixed" option if one existed, and the current rating is positive, I'll give it a negative. That said, understand that the game does have some great aspects to it.

Pros:
- Atmosphere is pretty strong. It definitely gives off the corporation gone wrong vibe, and the mechanical nature of a lot of things looks creepy and neat.

- Limb targeting is neat and very unique for a souls like.

- DLCs are pretty good. Walk in the Park is pretty well done, and even the simple DLC where you do trials of fighting enemies is enjoyable. You can put plenty of extra time into them.

- Areas have lots of shortcuts and connect back into themselves often. However, this is usually done in a way that doesn't feel organic to me, as you'll see from the first point in the cons section.

- Implants are cool, even though I think they could use rebalancing.

- I just have to point this out. You know bonfires from Dark Souls? Imagine if you could move a single bonfire around to different parts of a level. This game does that in the final level and it's SICK.

Cons:

- With the exception of the DLCs, the areas almost always look very similar to one another. One thing that I really, really disliked is that all throughout the game, you have these tunnels that connect areas to one another. They almost always act as shortcuts. Each and every one of these tunnels looks identical, and have very little to help you navigate. I got lost A LOT in this game, and had to look up guides to help me. This specific point takes the game wayyy down for me.

- Balancing feels weird. It's not terrible, but I will say that in NG+ it gets really dumb with some bosses that can just instant kill you in ways that don't feel good.

- While there are many weapons and the classes are distinct, there isn't enough variation in my opinion.

- Not many bosses, and while the ones that are there are okay... I didn't love any of them.\t\t\t\t\t
