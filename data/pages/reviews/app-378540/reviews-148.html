\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, this game has no right being as good as it is. There are quite a few faults, as it's not perfect, of course, but it's a really great attempt at taking the Souls/BB formula and adding on to it while taking it to a SciFi setting. Combat is faster than Bloodborne, believe it or not, and there are actually way more animations and combos per weapon than in any of the Souls games. The animations are mostly fluid and some weapons are ridiculously awesome. There are plenty of new mechanics to make it stand out, such as cutting off body parts of enemies for weapons and armor pieces, and how the more tech scrap (this games version of souls/blood echoes) you carry around the higher a multiplier you get to earn more from kills. I've only fought two bosses so far but damn they were good fights that were completely different from each other and really required me to put some thought into them.

I know that the devs made Lords of the Fallen, which I never played because I heard mostly bad things, but this game is definitely up to par with FROM's games in my book. I can't recommend this more as this is the kind of design that I want to encourage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've gotten far enough in I feel like it's time to review this ♥♥♥♥. This game is stressful. It's not as easy as the Souls games mainly because of how slow it can be. Enemies do a ♥♥♥♥ load of damage and some can just straight up one hit you. Once you get the hang of blocking and dodging, which isn't too unfamiliar, the game starts to come together. The combat can suddenly be very swift once you understand everything, but that can be said about the souls games too I suppose.

You only get one 'bonfire' save room per area, with many, MANY shortcuts that loop back, sometimes to other shortcuts. This makes getting around a little confusing without a minimap of anysorts, leaving you to try and remember if where you died was up this exo lift or those stairs... Speaking of dying, you drop all your scrap souls in one place as per Souls clone, but you get usually about 2 minutes and 30 seconds to get back to them before you just loose them all. It doesn't start till you leave the safe room, killing enemies adds more time to it, sitting in elevators will pause the timer, though waiting for them will not. But if you can make it back to the safe room with a bunch of scrap, you can bank it for later use, and so far, I haven't found a downside to banking them.

My main complaint is that finishing move invincibility frames aren't long enough, sometimes when doing a finishing move to cut a limb off, at the end as you back away, it leaves you completely open. This can ♥♥♥♥ up fights with large amounts of enemies. As soon as the limb comes off, you usually can get hit if something is nearby, though enemies usually just straight up miss. The same thing can happen when chaining attacks. Some chains force you to do more than one swing, leaving you stuck in an attack animation as they become unstaggered from the extended combo. (Edit) Before I said that the crafting system can be a pain for having to go farm the starting enemies, but what I didn't realize is that you can change what teir you craft the armor at. This means if you're in a pinch for example, you can craft a pair of legs in teir 1 and the other in teir 2. Weapons however are most often given in teir one unless they're from a boss, so you'll still have to go look for specific upgrade materials if you want to boost another weapon to try out.

Over all, I recomend this to people who are sad the souls games are done. The story isn't that amazing, but I haven't finished it yet either. The bosses (I've only fought the first 2) are very Metroid/Castlevania, they each have a stages and specific hitboxes and little things you have to do in order for them to die. Honestly, the game was a little pricy at $50. I don't really expect to come back to this unless they add more on or co-op, just some sort of online would make it a bit better. Really $35 - $25 would have been a better price for this game. At where I'm at, I expect about to be only half way through. So unless you're really sad that Dark Souls is done and need something else, get it. Otherwise just go make a Thrall Axe character in DS3, So much fun and describes the dodging of this game pretty well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game but the flaws of the Surge far outweigh it's good bits and it becomes more apparent the further you venture through the game.

It starts of quite well, easing into the brutal combat system and game mechanics with a tutorial built into the level, a mix of open spaces and tight corridors, and a brilliant level design that opens up numerous short cuts back to the single OPS room in each level (the equivelent of Dark Soul's bonfire).

However the game suffers from several bad design decisions - regular enemies hit incrediably hard and can kill you in two quick hits. There is a blocking function but it's quite useless and doesn't seem to offer much more than to lock you in place so the enemies can knock off another huge chunk of your health (enemies can be found in narrow corridors that your only defense is to dodge backwards and hope you don't get trapped - there is literary an instance in one level where you take a lift up to a narrow corridor with a fast, hard enemy a few steps away that you have no way of avoiding at all).

Dodging is a far safer tactic although due to level design there have been a few times when my character has lept to his death beacuse some enemies have been placed on bridges and platforms with parts that have no railings or have an opening in the floor to make way from some scenery. It may sound picky but when your running back to the spot you died previously and have to confront a quick, deadly enemy on a narrow bridge that is very difficult to run past, it starts to feel a cheap tactic on the developers part to make the game "hardcore".

The camera struggles with combat in tight spaces and the locking on system can sometimes get unresponsive if trying to disengage or switch enemies. Coupled with the sheer deadliness of the regular enemies, encounters become frustrating with the player dying often in a split second and having to fight their way back through the level to pick up the scrap currency they dropped on death.

Also of note the plot is non existant, the world lore and characters not terribly interesting or engaging. Except for a couple of parts the art of the levels are uninspiring factories and futuraristic labs that all look the same and on several occassions have left me lost because it all looks the same. The character the player plays as is middle age white guy number #2 with forgetable voice acting and personality, and the only one unique feature is revealed at the intro of the game and is then completely moot once you get strapped into the exoskeleton.

If your really got an itch for a dark souls-alike game, I can only recommend this game if it's on sale. At it's full price it is just not worth the frustrating enemies, wonky camera, and unengaging plot and world building.

By the way, I haven't actually finished this game. I gave up after reaching the 4th boss (there are five in total). The first two bosses are actually quite interesting the fight, the third one is an example of how bad the camera can get, and the last one I reached was uninspiring with a ranged stun that left me completely defenseless and frozen on the spot to his follow up attack which one shotted me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is an enjoyable RPG, taking influence from the Souls series of games but providing enough unique mechanics and structure to make it stand well on its own. You don't have to have played the Souls series of games to have fun in this one! It brings a unique aesthetic to this kind of melee-based gameplay of repurposed industrial tools and mechanical exoskeletons, alongside a kind of junkyard transhumanism as you swap different bits in and out.

Pros:
+ The keyboard and mouse controls actually work quite well, so while there is controller support, you don't have to use it
+ While the game can initially be punishing, learning the moveset and tells of the different enemy types can allow you to beat them reliably
+ Improving your character by upgrading armor, weapons, core power (essentially your character level) and implants (healing items and stat boosts) actually ramps up your capability nicely
+ Weapons can be favorited, and favorited weapons can be swapped between on the fly (G-key by default). You can essentially mark your entire listing and swap to the best tool for the current job.
+ changing the stats of your character is achieved through swappable implants, or what pieces of armor you wear- you never really need to respec to rebuild your character, just reequip yourself.
+ better weapon and armor upgrade items drop from later-game opponents, allowing you to freely upgrade pretty much everything if you're willing to farm their bits.
+ Scrap (the game's XP) can be banked at each level's central base location, preventing you from losing it on death if you're farming it up.
+ The areas of the game are a rat's warren of shortcuts and backpaths, where a significant part of progressing is unlocking shortcuts back to the central base. Finding and unlocking each of these can be quite satisfying, especially when it allows you to bypass areas you had trouble with on the way through initially.
+ the humanoid opponents are fun to outmaneuver and pick apart, and the finishing moves allow you to literally take them to pieces! Getting good against a subtype that used to give you trouble absolutely feels like sweet, sweet revenge, like a fly that has learned to pull legs off a spider.

Cons:
- Bosses can be killed only once. Once beaten, you won't see that boss again until NG+.
- Every boss drops a boss weapon, but you have to kill them in a special way to get a more powerful version- the game mentions this in exactly one loading hint, but gives no clues as to how to determine what that 'special method' will be for any of the bosses.
- The game doesn't do a good job telling you about many of the background mechanics. For example, sometimes you'll get extra healing items out of the blue, or recover a bit of health, with no real explanation as to why. There is a root mechanic around this based on your current core power and how much scrap you've picked up since the last time you visited the central base, or the total currently carried on your person.
- The player is a fixed character, a man named Warren, but he has little initial background or character development.
- There will be difficulty ramps in certain areas that you'll mostly overcome by grinding.
- There's a LOT of grind if you want to upgrade yourself, your kit, and your weapons as much as possible.
- In addition to dropping your carried 'scrap' when you die, you have 2:30 to get it back once you leave the base/respawn area. Killing enemies can add a little time back, but you have to be quick or you'll spend more time than you get from doing it. If you die while such a pile is on the ground, regardless of remaining time, it's gone for good as in the souls games.
-Some of the robotic enemies have absurd levels of armor and hit like a truck, making fights against them long, slow, and exceedingly dangerous.

I like the elements at play here. It's recognizable as being influenced by the Souls series, much like Torchlight was recognizably influenced by Diablo, but it brings enough unique elements that it is unquestionably its own thing. Combat feels meaty, and the finishing moves you can pull off on wounded enemies are satisfyingly brutal. The mechanics around targeting individual limbs to determine what loot you want from the kill, or to hit weak points, makes for interesting tactical decisions in the heat of a fight. Most importantly, the base combat (at least against the humanoid opponents) is just fun, making the grinding you do actually enjoyable: It provides the perfect excuse to keep bludgeoning foes into submission! Though I admit, there were a few rage-quits here and there, my general experience is that it's hard to convince myself to stop playing!

TL:DR- Take the bones of a Dark Souls game, put some Dead Space on it, throw in a little System Shock and Deus Ex, and you've got something like The Surge!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think it's a solid game with a few flaws.

Very similar gameplay to Dark Souls. The player will run around killing enemies and collecting scrap. You will use that scrap to create and/or upgrade weapons and armor. The player will also use the scrap to upgrade your power core, which enables you to wear heavier, more stable armor sets, and wield stronger, more power consuming weapons. Having a higher power core also allows you to equip more implants that do multiple things like increase your overall health and quicker stamina regeneration.

The game ran almost flawlessly. With all settings maxed, I stayed around 100 - 120 FPS 98% of the time. The game did crash three times.

There aren't too many bosses or enemies, but they all have a slightly different learning curve, so it makes things stay somewhat fresh. There are also limited areas to visit, but it's not really a deal breaker. The major negative thing I experienced was the camera. It always seemed to get stuck at the worst times. It makes fighting enemies in tight hallways very frustrating.

Definitely a recommendation if you don't mind getting slightly pissed off while playing a single player game.\t\t\t\t\t
