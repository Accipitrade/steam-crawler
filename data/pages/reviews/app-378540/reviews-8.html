There is also no way to aggressively angle attacks to get in an advantageous position to riposte even though you can target body parts. Dodging isn't really an option because it uses so much stamina and the enemy recovers rather quickly, so you'll dodge twice for every attack which makes the game even slower. Refusing to play with this much reservation because you prefer to attack in a flurry often results in another enemy coming out of the blue to backstab you. The stamina and stability rules which apply to you don't seem to apply to the enemy at all.

This is a classic game where ranged weapons and traps would be extremely helpful to watch your back, but the game offers none which makes no sense since it's a science-fiction future game. Yes, you get a companion drone with a ranged attack, but its pepper damage isn't very helpful.

The best case scenario is you play incredibly defensively with high speed weapons since low speed weapons don't deal much damage even when upgraded, but even then, your high speed weapons will endure a low speed game pace which is just frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I don't understand the hate about this game. I really enjoyed it and I don't usually play Dark Soul style games. I played Lords of the Fallen, which is game from the same developer and I must say that The Surge is improved in every way.

Story is mediocre but mystery around what happend is kinda engaging. I love that scifi postapo atmosfere. Characters and dialogs are boring. Story is mainly told through game world and audio logs.

Level design is great. Every area feels like a maze at first, but as you play, you will find more and more shortcuts to medbay (safe heaven) and the whole level design will start make sense. But I have to admit, that in some areas I felt lost and didnt know which path to take.

Combat feels ok and has a lot of variety so you can experiment between weapon types and find your playstyle. Through killing enemies you can get new armor and weapons in a unique way. In order to get it you need to target specific bodypart, do enough damage and then cut it off.

Through the game, you will obtain techscrap, implants and blueprints that you can put in use in medbay. Implants are very important, because they determine your health, stamina, energy, injectables and many more bonuses you can have. With techscrap you can level up your rigs powercore, craft new armor sets and level up weapons and armor.

Graphics and sound is ok. Nothing special but I didn't encountered any bugs, which is nice.

As for the difficulty, I didn't find game to be very hard, as some people say, but if you are not cautious, you can die in one or two hits. If you want more challange after you finish the game, there is new game +.

I also played both story DLCs. A Walk in the Park DLC was great. New location, enemies, weapons, armors, implants, boss and it ads to the story of main game. The Good, The Bad and The Augmented is more like a challange arena style dlc with little story and new items to get. It was kinda ok, repetive though.

If you are new to Dark Souls genre, this is a great game to start with if you don't want to be very frustrated. Veteran DS players may not enjoy this game that much. Overall, I can recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
