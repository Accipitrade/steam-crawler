\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFive hours of gameplay.

TLDR - Combat feels very solid. I love the heavy industrial theme.

Been spending most of the time exploring a very well crafted, and what feels like a very huge industrial facility. There are many, many shortcuts that you'll discover that loop around back all over the place. You'll be fighting crazed exosuit employees, industrial machines/robots that feel like they do belong in their environment.

For equipment, the player will be using what feels like industrial tools as weapons, drones that support you in combat, installing hardware onto your exosuit that give you buffs (+HP, extra healing, +damage, +energy gain, blah blah blah).

Weapons and armor are obtained mosly from chopping limbs off of enemies that are carrying it. See that guy with giant hammer? Go cut his arm off and take it. Oh that worker gal has some fancy looking armor, go chop her head off and take her fancy hat. Because that is what's important, hats.

The combat is fast and has a decent amount of challenge to it that has felt very fair thus far. Different weapons have their own movesets. Different armor give different trade offs, less stamina consumption for dodging but less armor and stability, etc.

During combat you will be building up suit energy, which can be used for dismembering enemy limbs, injections that grant buffs, or using your drones abilities. Energy gained during combat will slowly start to drain as you exit combat. So it provides another layer of resource management during combat to use extra abilities to aid you.

Medbays act as a hub area for a location. You'll spend your time here upgrading your equipment and possibly interacting with some other characters stuck inside the facility.

Something I thought was interesting is that leveling up your character only increases your maximum exosuit power and unlocks additional upgrade slots for injections. Your suit power is used to determine how much equipment you can equip on your exosuit. Armor and injections require a certain amount of suit power in order to install on your suit. Your suit power is also used as a way to gate content. Some doors and devices can only be activated if you meet the requried amount of power to activate said device. So backtracking to unlock that door that requries 75 power from the beginning of the game is definitely a thing.


So if you're interested in applying for a job at Creo, I'd recommend it. It might be slightly painful though, but just think of all the vacation time you'll get every month.

And no, there isnt any character customization or gender options.

Annoying enemies that pop out of somewhere? Check.

I really hope there are expansions / DLCs coming for this though.

Audio: I have come very close to releasing many chocolate hostages playing this game and I wouldn't change it for the world. It's intense, atmospheric and makes you wonder what is behind every corner. Ultimately, this is my favourite part of The Surge.

If you're a die hard dark souls fan this may not live up to your expectations but then again what will. But, if you're looking for a solid follow up to LOTF and a game similar to Souls then I definitely recommend it. I have enjoyed 13+ hours of bliss and I can't wait to get back on tomorrow.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a game that is seen as "Dark Souls style" it does things rather differently.

The combat is far more hack and slash, and you will be using combos and finishers to win fights. Targeting specific points on the body will return different rewards, if you want leg parts attack the leg, see a weapon you want? go for the arms etc. with the ability to target body parts you will be making quick decisions and going for the weak spots.

Level design is very miuch set in the same roots as Dark Souls, as you open shortcuts and snake around the environment with no set path. on initial thoughts this game does feel more layered with multiple levels within the same area.

Trash mobs are far tougher and can often put you down in 2-3 hits. swinging wildly may work at times, but this will be your end against mobs who are faster than you. strategy is needed for each fight, switch up playstyle depending who/where and how many enemies you are fighting.

Tech trash are your new souls, obviously these are dropped on your inevitable death. Slight difference here though is that you have to reclaim them within 2mins and 30 seconds, failure to reach them in time means they are lost. It means there is a frantic rush to get them back, instead of the Dark Souls calm approach of slow and steady.

Overall a great game if you want a Dark Souls game but dont want Dark Souls.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR Review:

Souls-like game with enough different to really stand out on its own. Fluid, fun combat makes playing this game very enjoyable while claustrophobic and dark level design keeps you on your toes the whole way through. Graphics are up to par for my standards aswell as sound and music being very good. Level/Crafting/Upgrade/Implant system is all very good and weapon/armor design is a blast and is alot of fun to make your guy look like a banged up 2000 Honda Civic. When you progress further and look like some crazy futeristic robot fighter you'll be very pleased. Weapon categories include 1-Hand (slow, medium and fast speeds) Duel Wield, and 2-Handers so the game gives plenty of options on how to play.

Overall very fun twist to the souls-like franchise and is definitly worth a play

Okay... maybe it isnt a TLDR but I couldnt help it :P
