\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game. Runs well and a good selection of graphics options to increase/decrease. Full controller support and mouse/keyboard, though I find controller seems a better choice for this type of game but that's just preference. Story so far is "meh". Voice acting is good enough though not amazing. Nothing wrong with either, but the main draw I find is the fun combat, which can be difficult. Enemies hit hard, and if you don't pay attention you can die easily at which point it pulls from games similiar to Dark Souls, where you respawn back at Operations, a small base with facilities to upgrade gear and weapons. Upon death you lose all scrap you were carrying (used for upgrading gear), but if you manage to get back to where you died without dying again you can pick it back up. And you can "bank" the scrap at the base to store it, but doing so resets a multiplier you accumalate by killing enemies which rewards more scrap per kill at the cost of losing it if you die.

While the levels seem mostly linear, there are side paths that reward items and unlock shortcuts back to the base to make things quicker if you die. Enemies respawn as well (except bosses), so you always have access to more scrap and materials as needed.

The neatest feature incorporated in combat is severing body parts via finishing moves, which results in that part (arm, body, leg, head) possibly dropping the schematic/weapon for use in crafting parts for your ExoSuit. To sever parts you just have to damage it enough while locked on to it (you can swap the lock-on to each part), and then press the button mapped to "Finishing move". At which point you get a nice little execution animation that sometimes adds a slow-mo effect to the kill cam. You can toggle the frequency of said effects in options menu.

So far, I'm thoroughly enjoying the game and will report any additional thoughts I may have as I progress.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really fun Soulsborne esque game, but it's unique in its own right.

The game is fun to play, it's combat mechanics are much deeper than at first glance. There are weapons that are better against armored, vs flesh. If you want to upgrade your weapons you need to cut off the respective limb from enemies (hitting armored pieces). If you want to upgrade your experience with a specific weapon you need to target flesh to kill enemies faster. It's a great balance and keeps the combat fresh and strategic.

Controls are really tight. This ain't Yooka-Laylee, your character never moves where you don't want it to and that's a great thing for a Soulsborne game.

Give this game a shot and it will not disappoint you.

Runs perfectly on my 4770K and GTX 1070\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that I completed the game, let me start by saying I still recommend the game, but will bring up a few issues that I experienced (This is NOT a review, just a list of problems others may face if they choose to purchase):

1) Level design is far too narrow for the combat style. In certain areas it was nearly impossible to not get stuck somewhere due to this, especially the latter half of the game this became even more obvious.

2) Enemies are more than just difficult, it is easy to be one, two or three-shotted by any enemy in the game. This causes a problem in that you can't make a mistake in any fight otherwise you will risk dying. Dark Souls enemies are tough but fair, The Surge just wants you to run through certain areas like a gauntlet match due to level design + enemy damage.

3) There are only five bosses, but due to my #2 issue, I am glad there are only 5. Outside of the first two bosses, the last three are amped up in difficulty to ridiculous measures. They are more frustrating than they are fun to fight, and although the last boss was invigorating to defeat, most of the time I just wanted these fights to be over with and move on.

4) Whilst I thought the storyline was good, I felt there were plot holes that were never filled, including side quests. One of the biggest anticlimactic letdowns you will experience will be near the end of the game.

5) Low amount of different builds available. All melee, no ranged or "magic" is here, which I knew when I got into the game. But the Drone you get early on is very useless throughout the game. I was hoping I could upgrade it, maybe if someone wanted to do a Drone-focused build that could have been the ranged option, but unfortunately even with the different abilities you find for it later, the energy cost is just never worth it when energy can be used for so many other important things.

5.2) Speaking of low amount of builds, the game forces you to stick to a specific weapon type due to the "proficiency" level. Initially I thought it was a good idea to help people who die often but still have some sense of progression, but really you won't use any other kind of weapon since proficiency takes quite a while to level up, and it does have some significant impact to how much damage you can do. I kept going through the game saying, "I'll level up one slow weapon and one fast weapon." but all I did was stick with Heavy-Duty throughout.

5.3) I know I keep going on about this, but it's important for replay value, which this game has little of unfortunately. So to top off the low build volume, you also have very few useful weapons. Boss weapons you get pretty much triumph over any other drop you will find in the game, except maybe for one weapon. So once again you will be limited to a handful of viable weapons, then one specific one due to proficiency.

Those were the biggest issues I discovered, but despite the negatives, it is still a pretty decent game and gave me about 25 hours out of my $45. I will revisit this if they ever add DLC or fix some of the build issues with the game.
