There are some cool moments early on the game that lay the groundwork some potentially interesting character development, but those are quickly forgotten.

PROS OF THE GAME
+ Highly detail environment and well optimized gameplay
+ Breaking enemy parts to unlock new gear, armor and weapons
+ Epic boss fight
+ Not grindy when it comes down to unlocking stuff
+ Short Cuts to unlock which make gameplay easy and faster
+ Rewards player for exploring the maps
+ Multiple set of weapons and armor
+ Enemy AI is brutal and smart
+ Combat always felt engaging

CONS OF THE GAME
- Only two major cut scene in the entire game and they hold the entire wait of story
- story was nonexistence and was an excuse to jump from one map to another
- no companion except for a Drone
- Big open maps with same enemies type
- only 6 types of enemies that include Heavy , Light , Drones , Aliens , Zombie and work machines

OPINION ON THE GAME
* Could have made into a linear experience and focused more on different types of enemies
* If wanted to make open linear maps could have added hud map or mini map atleast
* Didnot had to be an dark souls type game
* could had focused more on story and lore
* could focus more on character building

SCORE

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave you played any Souls like game before? Do you like being frustrated while gaming? Are you okay with losing progress after hours of grind? Do you have temperament issues?
If the answers to these are Yes, then you are a ♥♥♥♥♥ maniac.
Surge has a unique core mechanic which basically lets you rips off your enemy's limbs (not bosses) and collect the particular weapon/armour they had attached on that limb part. Its fun and satisfying honestly, its a very strong foundation for a mechanic and it fits really well with the story.
Although Souls players might not find this game too difficult. I myself have played Nioh, Dark Souls, Sekiro, and, I gotta be honest, Surge is nothing compared to these games. It aint too punishing. But nonetheless, I guarantee you, you will have fun.
Few RPG elements, such as choices for customizations, very few dialogue choices and... yea thats all. I personally did not feel connected to the character one bit. Which usually is a common thing in Souls like games.

But overall i'd say get it off when theres a discount. And a very important tip, use PAX as your main weapon, that ♥♥♥♥ is so powerful, when you upgrade it, it just tears apart enemies.

P.S. - I completely forget. If your answer is No for the questions above, just ignore this game unless you don't love your PC/Console/Controller.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - Portal setting, Dark Souls gameplay. Good fun and well worth it.

The Good

Combat feels weighty and big impacts are satisfying. Graphics are good and the gritty, hyper-modern factory settings are a pleasure to behold. Maps are confusing at first, but unlocking shortcuts feels rewarding and I never felt like the game made me trek too far away from the respawn point in any area. Voice acting is good and the story is engaging, revealed by audio recordings and conversations with NPCs. Weapon and armor types cater to certain playstyles, but implants allow for a great deal of flexibility, especially late-game. The 'cut off a weapon you like' mechanic is great fun and is well implemented.

The Bad

The game encourages you to stick with a single weapon type as you level up your 'proficiency' with it, but that means you're likely to stay with one weapon for the whole game. Enemies hiding around corners, ready to instantly attack you can be rather annoying, but it does keep you cautious and melds well with the claustrophobic, menacing atmosphere. Some enemies/environment combinations are obnoxious and you will feel like sometimes the game intentionally wants to hurt you on a personal level. There are things that must be done exactly right, before certain events, or you miss opportunities for weapons, achievements, and story elements due to the autosave feature. Some people may not mind this, but I find it irritating when I have to play the game all the way through again just to earn a weapon or a bit of the story. Bosses are a bit of a mixed bag; some are really unique and challenging (e.g. Big SISTR 1/3) and some are just a regular dude with more health/damage. The difficulty also varies wildly. I was able to one-shot a few bosses but then it took dozens of tries on others.

Note: this is an action game, but it's definitely dark. It's not a horror title like Dead Space, but it has some very depressing themes, imagery, and the videos/audio recordings take you to some pretty dark places. Most of the people you meet or learn about are either truly evil or have serious mental issues (even before the titular event takes place) and are exploited as a result. This fictional world is grim and they don't pull any punches showing you.

All in all, very enjoyable and highly recommended if you enjoy souls-like games. The Sci-Fi twist is worth the price of admission. Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t
