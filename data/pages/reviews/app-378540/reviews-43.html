\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExtremely late review for this game but I had just beat it for the first time and I feel like it was worth writing about. Borrowing many elements from the Dark Souls series and adding some elements of its own, Deck13 has made something very enjoyable. The mood in the game was very tense for the most part. I enjoyed the "sever the limb" mechanic. They have provided a very visceral experience when I can chop off a mob's leg.

The things they did right:
The mechanical bosses
Severing limbs mechanic
Gear progression

Things they did not get right:
The duck or jump mechanic in combat
Repetitive enemies
Annoying drones when they're just out of reach
One unique type of enemy's kill condition seems a bit buggy and unintuitive
Unresponsive jump
Overload circuits seem to be placed in random spots
Mix and match sets aren't worth missing out on a set bonus
Clever map design but very confusing since many areas look very similar to the next. Especially the sections where you run through the vents.

Overall fun game but I hope the ironed out the kinks in the sequel.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is a game that I have played on and off shortly after completing the Souls series-almost religiously. Like many before me, I am guilty of not giving games like this a fair chance initially, but having played this game and completing it, I feel confident enough to write a concise review discussing one or two key components that I think makes this game worth playing.

Firstly, I will start by saying that the combat feels great and the concept of the game is refreshing as well. I've spend about 80+ hours playing this game and I have enjoyed every second of it. There are moments that are rage-inducing, but I think that it comes naturally with the tag 'Souls-like.'

Secondly, the world of The Surge is very unique and it is one of the best game representations of what a future would be like should earth's ecology decline uncontrollably. The game emulates what could happen should humanity depend entirely on technology( mainly robotics) to sustain human existence, and I think that this is done really well.

Lastly, I will say that if you take into consideration the price of the game (DLCs included) it is bang for your buck. I bought this game when I was broke...because it was that cheap, and I was bored, but having completed it, I would definitely pay full price for knowing what I know now.

Furthermore, playing The Surge vs what I thought I knew about the game before playing it; I will simply say that it is heavily underrated. The most prominent misconception that people willingly adopt is that The Surge is a "Dark Souls copy" which seems to automatically breed an opinion that it is not worthy of being played free of comparison, but I beg to differ. Sure, there are many similarities to the souls series- I will not mention them- but The Surge is unique in that its story (though not perfect) is really refreshing considering that it is Si-Fi and not a fantasy type genre, and the mechanics of the exo-suit is really fluid.

The gratification you feel after defeating a boss is no less than that-gratifying, and I can safely say that the progression feels really good, too. There were definitely moments of frustration and annoyance (mostly related to camera angles and map design) but overall, the good certainly outweigh the bad, and I would 110% recommend playing this to the end as it is worth every cent considering the price.

To summarise... Buy the game, you wont regret it :)

P.S At the time of the posting of this review, I am about to start The Surge 2.

P.S After trying The Surge 2- Not a big fan yet.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great souls like experience with a lot of potential.

I can safely say, if you are a passionate fan of the hack & slasher, Rpg and especially the Souls experience this is MUST PLAY! At first you can easily describe it as Dark Souls with Sci-Fi and cybernetics. While this brings about an interesting and unique concept the Surge stands out different on its own but shows its influences. More brutal, punishing and less forgiving the Surge is all about being on your toes every second of the game. This can be overwhelming at times but it brings about an authentic challenge.

Downside would be a series of underdeveloped characters, including the protagonist, a less immersive world and story. In addition to that a few glitches and interactive issues with game play that can make a challenging game like this unnecessary frustrating.But looking past that is a something very fun and engaging with a lot of potential.

Despite delaying playing this with lack of interest, I m glad I finally did.\t\t\t\t\t
