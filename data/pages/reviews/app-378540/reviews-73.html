\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA really good souls-like game although it could still use some improvements in a lot of places.

Pros:
+ Great atmosphere
+ Interesting setting with a good premise
+ Plays well with a mouse and keyboard, even though I usually play souls-like games with a controller
+ Solid gameplay. If you like the gameplay of Dark Souls you'll most likely enjoy it here, although it's not as technical and varied
+ Has a few interesting twist on the souls-like genre. Like for example being able to target specific body parts of an enemy, being able to bank your tech scraps (aka your currency in the game, the equivalent of souls in Dark Souls games), that way you don't have to keep them on you if you don't have enough for a level up
+ Easy to understand and fairly simple crafting process. Like the head piece that an enemy is wearing? Just chop off their head to get the blueprint and then gather the materials to craft it for yourself
+ There are no stats that you can level up but instead implants, which have a variety of uses from giving you more health, more stamina, more energy, more injectables (to restore health or boost damage amongst other things), increase your damage, etc. The more you level up, the more implants you can equip at once
+ Wearing a full set of armor will grant you a bonus specific to the set you're wearing, giving you an incentive to grind for the whole armor
+ Lots of secrets to find throughout the levels, rewarding exploration
+ You can customise the music that plays in the medbay (the place where you heal up and upgrade youself), which is always a welcome option as there was only one default song and it was getting annoying after a while
+ Good variety of enemies, most of them being pretty interesting, although there could've been more variety
+ You have a time limit to recuperate your stuff after dying, which you can slightly increase by killing enemies
+ The energy system is pretty interesting and works well with the gameplay
+ Took me about 30 hours to get to the final boss, which is a pretty good duration. You can also keep going with NG+

+/- The armor set you begin with is honestly one of the best in the game because of the bonus that it gives you, which is kinda disappointing in my opinion because there's a lot of great looking set that i'll try for a bit but then switch back to the starting one because the bonus is much better
+/- Not enough bosses, even with the DLC. Although each bosses are very different
+/- Level design is pretty interesting for the most part with a lot of shortcuts but it's sometimes too confusing. The second level in the game especially has way too many paths and shortcuts and connects to pretty much all other levels in the game, making it hard sometimes to remember how to get from one level to another

Cons:
- Story is really confusing. I currently only have the last boss of the game left and i'm honestly still lost when it comes to the story. Sometimes I couldn't tell where to go next and had to look it up online
- Not enough variety in the weapons. Sure, a lot of them look nice but there's only 5 different categories of weapons and all the weapons in each categories all have the exact same movesets, which essentially makes it feel like there's only 5 different weapons with reskins and different damage stats
- NPCs weren't that interesting, didn't really care for most of them. There was one NPC that I actually cared about and progressed their storylines but the ending to their questline was pretty underwhelming
- Although the combat is pretty solid, there's not much variety to it
- No ranged options when it comes to combat aside from your drone which has a few different attacks but it doesn't do much damage. Most of the time I found myself using my drone to aggro enemies or stunning them but if you're the kind of person that likes ranged combat, you won't find it here
- Instant death if you fall off an edge, which would've been acceptable if the enemies could fall from the edges too but they can't except for one late game enemy
- No character customisation
- Main character (the one you play) isn't that interesting. You barely learn anything about him during the main game, you do learn a bit more about him during the Walk in the Park DLC but not that much still
- Camera can be a bit annoying sometimes during combat, especially during bosses. I've had a couple instances where i'd do some quick movement and the camera didn't follow me fast enough, causing me to not being to see where i'm at. Thankfully I've never died or gotten hit during those moments
- Blocking and parrying in this game is pretty useless. You can parry but it's really hard to do and not really worth it. Gameplay feels a bit more like Bloodborne than Dark Souls in the sense that you have to be much more aggressive with the combat


Overall I think this game is a great improvement on their previous game, Lords of the Fallen (which I personally wasn't able to get into) and a great game overall despite the flaws it has. I'm excited for The Surge 2 and look forward to playing it and hopefully they've fixed most of the problems that I have with this one.
As I said in the beginning, if you like souls-like, I definitely recommend The Surge although i'd say wait for it to go on sale!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is a mixed bag. Some elements are great while others are awful. In general, If you love Dark Souls combat, you will enjoy this game. But best purchase while its on sale.

To be quick: the combat is generally fun. It keeps you entertained through most of the game. The story is... meh. It's poorly told and confusing, but you'll understand some basic tones. Where it _really_ stumbles is in level design.

Dark Souls has wonderful vistas and memorable areas. You generally know where you are because everything is unique. The Surge is the opposite. There's really no memorable areas in the entire game. It's almost all metal rooms filled with robo monsters. The final 10% of the game embodies this failure to a completely frustrating degree. The last level before the big baddy is a loop-filled maze of annoyance. I absolutely *hated* it. It leaves a sour taste after a generally enjoyable experience.

I only give this a "recommend" because reviews are a binary system. It's more good than bad, but... barely.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEnjoyed my time with this game and would recommend to anyone who enjoys a 'Darksouls' style game progression and learning curve. The combat feels very different to the Souls games, but once you get used to it, it is a lot of fun, especially enemy dismemberment.

Good points:
- Meaty combat, you can feel the weight of the weapons
- Looks and runs great
- No overly long, unskippable tutorial
- No crashes in almost 40 hours of game play
- Reasonable learning curve (for this type of game)
- Varied boss fights
- Satisfying secrets to discover
- Enemy dismemberment

Bad points:
- Game is a bit short
- New game + doesn't really offer many new challenges to the game. I found just making a new character more fun.
- Very few boss fights
- Limited enemy types, they get a bit repetitive quickly

**I do not own any of the DLC for this game. This review is based of the base game only.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll go through positives and negatives first, then delve deeper into it and even comparing it to DS3.
Here we go:

Pros:
-Combat system is amazing, fluid, fast-paced, quite complex and damn satisfying.
-The game looks great, even without the grandoise of vast open spaces of DS3
-Acquiring blueprints, weapons and crafting materials is genius in it's simplicity - just cut them off an enemy to get them
-Armors look cool and have set bonuses, so it's heaven for completionists
-The main character has a voice.
-The game's map is a set of hubs with a lot of shortcuts you can unlock to make traversing the game way faster (especially if you want to cross several hubs to get some crafting materials of weaker enemies)
-The bosses are hard, but way less intimidating than those in DS series so more casual players can have fun too.
-Multiple weapon types available, and each of them is satisfying in it's own way and playstyle.
-No multiplayer (if you hate being invaded by those red ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ in DS)

Cons:
-No multiplayer (if you love being one of those red ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ in DS)
-It's easy to get lost on the maps, even after completing NG+ I was still running around a lot of the time
-the bosses don't have the same impact as those in Sekiro or the DS series - the game focuses mostly on fighting the smaller mobs that will wreck your sh*t in 2 hits if you're not careful
-The story is there, and it's quite a cookie-cutter one about an experiment gone wrong, nothing to write home about or create YT channels dedicated to

Summary:
I've completed DS3 the day before installing the Surge and boy oh boy am I happy I did that. I'm aware this may be impossible to grasp for some but the Surge gave me WAY MORE FUN than DS3. Ok I know it's heresy for some but hear me out - Dark Souls series is deep (if you REALLY care about the story that doesn't care about you in return), but all in all it's not GOOD at all. It's a typical Japanese over-the-top grotesque soap opera that makes no sense when you honestly think about it. The whole Soulsborne series tries to hide it's imperfections by using a grim tone and making everything depressing, because for some reason EMO culture is still strong in Japan in 2019. The Surge doesn't try being what it's not - it's simply a story that motivates a player to carve through enemies one by one until it's one of 2 possible endings. It keeps it simple and to the point. Level design is on a whole different scale from the DS series - since it's a production complex here you mostly operate in closed spaces which can get a bit suffocating at times, but seeing how DS3 could suffocate you with it's atmosphere even when you should be getting agoraphobia I'd say it's not that bad. The music is nothing to write home about, except for the song playing near your med-station every time you enter it. I'm downloading that.

COMBAT:
It's fast. It's fluid. It's damn satisfying. Even though I often got lost in multiple corridors of the complex I didn't really mind, because just fighting was so much fun. Single enemies can be deadly, and their difficulty level progresses smoothly. I've seen comments regarding the "scorpions" being impossible to kill and not rewarding at all and that the enemies through the game progress with you so you never feel any sense of progression yourself - It's a lie Cutting off Scorpions' tails gives you one of the most essential implants in the game and they're easy to kill for anyone with IQ over 60 as they have only 2 attack patterns. Grunts don't progress with your level at all - sometimes they progress with the story though, so you can meet stronger enemies in previous areas when you happen to have to backtrack a little - something that will take you no time at all if you remember the shortcuts you've unlocked earlier.
Diversity of weapons and combos is great - it's not DS where only some weapons are "valid" - you can do amazing things with any weapon here and feel damn good about it. Finishing an enemy is ALWAYS satisfying as hell. Every single time. Even if you've been farming enemies for an hour because you want materials for every single piece of craftable equipment like me - you'll do it with a wide grin on your face. Buy this game just for the combat.

Additional info:
Overall I had way more fun with this game than with self-absorbed and soapy DS3. Is DS3 better objectively? YES. Is it more fun? HELL NO . If you expect depth go watch The Seventh Seal. You'll die more in the Surge than in DS, but you'll do it faster, quicker, and you'll get this "oh well, it's on my head" feeling because of it.

DS3 is like the best pair of shoes on the market - stylish, popular, probably overpriced and not as good as everyone thinks but still great.
The Surge is like passing by the guy in the best shoes in the world in an old Mustang - sure his shoes may be great and all, but that V8 is cool in it's own way. It's way faster too.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile promising, The Surge is let down by a lack of direction in its general design.

The earl-middle of the game is by far the best part. The areas are more varied and interesting than any other section.
The beginning of the games is slow, but enjoyable in its exploration and finding of shortcuts and paths, further improving in the midgame. However by the end there are many branching paths interconnected by the EXACT same architecture and enemies every step of the way. Dozens of generic maintinence shafts holding nondescript exo-lifts leading to bland hallway upon bland hallway.
The bosses are boring and tedious or laughably simple. Big things with lings to swing and be cut. The only really enjoyable boss was the 2nd one. It took a few times to beat, but wasn't as easy as the third boss or as obtuse as the 1st one. The perfect middle between them.
When I play any game with lots of exploration and pathways I make sure to explore absolutely everything. Near the end of the game I found numerous additional paths with generic crystal monsters and thought, "I think I'll just head to the exit."
I fought what I assumed was the final boss a single time having taken half its health before dying and quit. I figured I'd seen all I wanted to see. There was no value in fighting through another boring boss fight where I slide attack -> dodge -> slide attack -> dodge -> slide attack -> dodge. You get the point.
Having played about 18 hours, the middle 9-ish were very fun, the surrounding 9 were annoying and boring. Recommending this game would be like recommending someone eat the top of an Oreo to get into the filling, then throw the bottom of it into the garbage since it isn't worth eating.\t\t\t\t\t
