The Surge on the other side got me hooked for a variety of reasons:
+ Great combat/Loot system
+ Difficulty throughout the game is consistent (mobs become more difficult gradually and you won't get stuck at a single boss for hours while dashing through the area mobs)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlease reconsider buying this game..

It may seem fun but trust me, it's not.
Gameplay and story are fun in concept, but with a badly optimized combat system, where lock-in animations will force you to fall out of the environment and lose your progress. At some points your finishing ability and other simple functions won't work, forcing you to take unnecessary damage, or at worst kill you.. Have this happen a certain amount of times, and trust me, you'll be putting the controller down with a sense of defeat. And in a fast-pace action-combat game, these key abilities are essencial.
The graphics are beautiful, but very similar as you progress, and at one point becomes boring. The armor and wepon gathering/building concept is interesting and should be a new and rewarding way to enjoy progression, but sadly they have low differences (in the long run) and becomes dull through-out the game.

If you've already decided to buy the game; i wish you luck and enjoy! but if you're still on the fence about the whole game, then trust me when i say you're paying full price for a game that is not optimized and still feels early access.\t\t\t\t\t

It's very obvious they got their inspiration from those games. I like that it feels like it expands on the combat system in it's own way. Not just with the limb targeting and finisher move system that allows you to rip body parts and exo-suit pieces off your enemies-but also with the blocking/evading system that feels much better than the souls games ever did. Not only is combat faster paced, but it's more varied given that you can't just block enemy attacks by holding the block button and facing them-you actually have to anticipate where your enemy will strike and either block or evade there. But even beyond that, the counterattack system lets me feel like even more a badass and even more immersed in the combat by letting me counterattack immediately after blocking/evading. The better enemies use it too and it makes it almost feel like playing against a human player-especially given their unpredictable AI.
I've been playing for many hours now and haven't found an enemy yet that doesn't seem to have multiple methods of attack. from lying in wait to ambush me mid-combat with someone else, to attacking aggressively the moment they see me, to waiting for me to attack to dodge my moves and attack me mid-combo like i try to do to them. the zombified enemies in the first level seemed dumb in comparison to the zombified and more humanlike enemies i've found since then.
The game also seems to have some side quests scattered throughout the maps. The maps themselves, despite being blandly factory-like in color scheme and layout, have been fairly large in my opinion. I've just explored the first 2 quite extensively so far in an attempt to familiarize myself with the layouts and landmarks i use to navigate my way around this facility. They both have a fair amount of shortcuts that I've come to expect and appreciate from games like these-even more so given that there is no real way to quick travel back to base to save your tech scraps.
That's another thing-this game's main currency appears to be tech scrap. nothing wrong with that-in fact, i even like it. especially when i can store it at the safe zone in each level so i don't lose it if i die. Doing so resets the combat multiplier that give more tech per kill, but also resets all the enemies, allowing you to practice and farm. When you die with tech on you, you have a certain amount of time from when you leave the base to get it. killing enemies adds to that time, or you can try to sprint past all of them to get it. personally, i like to kill along the way, adding more to my combat multiplier as well as my overall tech amount. To help you out even further, there are various tech scrap items you can use from your inventory that sometimes drop from enemies or have a chance of spawning around the zones. You can stockpile them seemingly indefinitely, or use them and bank them right there at base like i like to.
Ripping armor off of enemies yields an initial schematic for that armor piece so you can build it later, and later will yield gear parts to help upgrade those specific pieces of armor. Meaning torso pieces upgrade torso pieces, arms upgrade arms, and so on. It looks like every weapon and armor piece can be upgraded 4 levels and enemies with higher level gear will drop higher level loot to help keep upgrading your exo-suit. meaning it's not very helpful to grind in areas you've already been to if you're looking to upgrade your current gear further. you have to keep pressing on and unlocking the new areas. from what i've seen-i could end up being proven wrong if i get my power cell upgraded enough.
The exo-suit itself can be upgraded to allow you to do more with it. Beyond just unlocking new areas or side quests outside of combat, levelling up the power cell allows you to wear heavier armor, use better implants, and unlock more implant slots. With a wide array of implants to choose from-and the better armor obviously locked away behind higher power cell costs, you'll want to keep upgrading your rig. Some of the implants can only be swapped at base and other can be swapped out in the field-allowing for instant customization depending on what situation you're walking into or what weapon you're planning to use for the upcoming fight. the game encourages you to use different weapons for different enemies (there's no single weapon that is going to be great against every enemy.) and i think that's part of why they have the weapon proficiency mechanic in the game at all. to reward players to who keep using different weapons and penalizing players who try to do it all with just one and then realize late game that they need a different weapon against this new foe or cluster of enemies.
Some of the injectable implants can be stacked for more uses-such as the various healing ones-which is much appreciated in new areas when you don't know what you're going to come up against or how to survive fighting it. Because that leads me to my main reason for recommending this game-it's brutal. BRUTAL. Pretty much any enemy can kill you in just a few hits-if not in just a single combo. That sense of fear and requirement of near perfection is something i feel the souls games lost towards the end in an attempt to make it too accessible. this game is not for everyone. If you do not learn to fully utilize the advanced combat system, you're gonna have a bad time. This game plunks you down in a merciless world, but gives you all the tools you need to survive in it. Use them and abuse them or it will use and abuse you.

The CREO security guards at the start and throughout the second area don't seem to respawn, so if the armor piece you need isn't on them, keep going back to base and refreshing. i made the mistake of killing the last 2 in the same fight and missing out on the torso piece. don't make my same mistake.

Ignore that-they DO respawn later in the game and you run into more with that armor as well. Still, it's a good chance to get it early. Also, the better rigs with the extra implant slots really add to the depth of the combat. a wide range of stat effects are possible with 16 slots and the many implants.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would love to recommend this game to people. I like it's ideas and the setting is what made me buy it. I do not regret buying it since it hits a very niche place for me which means I can justify the purchase. However, I don't think this game will ever be a big hit and there are a lot of things that make me scratch my head while I play it. I never raged as much as I do at this game which takes me back to when I was young playing video games, not for good reasons.

The Good:
- Sci-fi mecha setting:
This for me is the whole reason I bought this game. I am a HUGE sci-fi fan and one of the reasons why I play Fallout so much is because of the Power Armor. To me this was a major win but is very niche as well seeing as that is probably not why most players will come to this game.

- Modular armor system:
I was worried when I first saw the game that it would not have a modular armor system built in. What I mean by that is, if you equipped the Rhino set it would be the entire rig not just a section of armor. Now that is not without it's faults. I will go on much like others have.

The Mediocre:
-The Combat:
This is a huge problem. While the actual system of combat is fun, fluid, and enjoyable to watch; the enemy design wasted all of it's potential. The art design of the enemies is fantastic but the only things that makes the enemies scary is their overwhelming power scale compared to you. They all 1-3 hit you, humans are acceptable but every robot I run away from. If the more...nuianced mechanics of the game in the dodging, blocking, and parry worked the robots would be enjoyable to fight. However, the dodge mechanics only work on a simplistic level when combined with human reaction and control scheme. There is no way that their jump up or crouch mechanic would be easy to do with their current implemitation. They use these hard to use features to substitute high and low block which means block does not work against 90% of the enemies you will want to block and you can't even block like they show in their trailer when warren stops the rhino from killing him. If Warren were to try and block that attack in-game he would have died.

-Level up, gear progression, and character customization:
Simply put there is very little. So much so that I will not be playing this game after I beat it unless they change a lot or add something worth coming back for. The "way" you play your character will be dictated by your early decisions and due to the profeciency system you will not be using other weapon types unless you are willing to grind. You will find due to enemy difficulty that you will not be using any of the unique traits of your weapons and armor might as well not have bonus's and be purely cosmetic. Weapons are the only thing that make a noticable difference in your survivability. I find I only pick armor based on the set bonus. Such as the Liquidator armor makes the enemies in R&D, Biolab, and first visit to the waste disposal area much easier to deal with. The armor therefore has situational value not overall gameplay value. I just do not see how my first character will play differently from my next if I decide to play again.

The Bad:
-Enemy Stat Theme and placement:
I think how Souls (since that is the comparison game) does it is fantastic. You have intimidating enemies that are easy to fight (most of the time) once you get past the face value of the enemy. Then you have a few like the classic black knights who are meant to keep a permanent challenge in the mobs so you stay on your toes. However the way The Surge handles the enemies is like every enemy is a black knight that you encountered before you are supposed to. As stated before every enemy 1-3 shots you with robot enemies being mostly immune to your damage unless you hit them where they are vulnurable. So for example the generic security bought you encounter there is only it's eye otherwise even with leveled up gear it only takes 5-8 damage from you hitting it's outside. While this makes you feel like you are truly trying to kill a tank with a power tool this does not make for compelling combat when you have this security bought tacked on top of 3-4 security guards who flip around and 2-3 shot you. It is nigh impossible to fight multiple enemies without getting ganked by one you can't see off camera. I just entered the production facility for the third time and now there are security guards stacked on each with these robots eveywhere. I no longer feel any desire to play the game since this makes it night impossible to do any of the compelling parts of the combat system without dying. When it came to me this is the future enemy standard I just gave up, not out of rage but despair that the game was no longer enjoyable for all I will be doing is dodging away until I can do one stab and then dodge again.

-Level Layout:
This is the part that makes me feel like I am playing an old school 3rd person adventure game. Often when you are in cramped corridors or on platforms with no guard rails you feel like you are more fighting the camera then the enemy. Too many times did I run off the edge or fell off due to prolonged animations for attacks or dodges or just not seeing it due to the camera tweaking out. This does not happen much any more and games so this is why I think of it as a bad. I will put as a disclaimer though that as I have grown I have narrowed my taste in games and therefore may not have just been encountering these games.

FINAL VERDICT
I would only recommend this game to people like me, who are really into Sci-fi/Robot games and want a decent adventure game in this niche setting. If it didn't reisde in a narrow setting I would say this game was not worth the price however given it's uniqueness I will say it is up to the buyers progitive, which is how it is always anyway. This game is not for everyone which is why I do not recommend it in this review.\t\t\t\t\t

The first 5~ish hours of this game were very enjoyable, however as i progressed further into the game it became glaringly obvious where the issues where, its trying so hard to be another souls game but it falls flat on its face.

Pros~

..:: Combat ::..
The combat in the surge feels fantastic, weapons feel very different and actualy feel like they have weight and impact to them, ive seen a plethora of people moan about slow weapon being useless, but they are wrong, i have been using a single riged heavy weapon its sure slow, but if you use the full lynx set it has a very good attack speed, and considering as of right now, my damage on it is over 100 i can smash most enemies in the area i am located in seconds. If i switch to a smaller weapon of even dual the difference is huge.


..:: Perfromance ::..
This game runs fantastic im running with 12gb of ram, a i7-3770 ( not OC'd) and a gtx 960 even at max setting im getting 60fps in nearly every area. Im yet to have any crashes though ive heard of others having them.


..:: Graphics ::..
Visually this game is very pretty while it may not be the most advanced graphicaly it still looks damn good for its setting and the animations on the player and enemies and bosses are brilliant.


..:: Gameplay ::..
You can store your tech-scrap( Souls ) in the medbay so you dont have to carry them around.


Cons~

..:: Story ::..
Good luck with that, the story if very flat none of the NPC's are really that memorable, and neither are any of the bosses


..:: Bosses ::..
The bosses are in any way interesting or mechanicly fun, they are tedious and boring, hit Limb X, stagger boss, Rinse repeate ( having only killed 3 bosses, and currently on the fourth ) Oh and you have to hit the boss on the specific area when you stagger them else you do 0 damage.


..:: Standard mobs ::..
Early on the mobs are quite tricky but manageable, however as soon as you get further into the game it changes from manageable to pointless, the mobs in this game only serve to either be used to farm for upgrades ( which are useless to some degree) or to make pointless fights with, if you want to actually progress in the mid to late game its much easier and faster to just speed run strat and ignore every mobs and press the buttons, also pressing buttons and entering the animations for that makes you IMMUNE to all damage.

Add to this that most if not all mobs have hyper armour and can just smash you to bits even if you are attacking them, and with the fact some of them have weapons you cannot obtain because they are ranged. you can quickly turn a trade for scraps into death.

Want to kill mobs and progress to the boss to get Scrap, dont bother, they can literaly 1-2 shot you even in tank gear, you will end up wasting most if not all of you healing before you even reach the boss even if you have been through the area several times. Its just not worth the time unless you are farming limbs.


..:: Upgrading Gear ::..
The concept is great, remove X limb to find that limb and use it to improve your own parts. However this can take several hours, espcially if you are farming for specific gear because the mobs dont always have the armour on them you want/need, and if you dont lop off X limb, good luck getting upgraded!

Upgrading your gear, makes you very marginally more stronger, now were talking small ammount you gain almsot no health only what i assume is akin to armour. if you upgrade your weapon sure you will do more damage but that aint going to stop the mob smashing you to bits in seconds.


..:: Combat ::..

Blocking, there is literaly 0 reason to ever block, heck you can hold block and your R-stick up or down to jump or duck from an attack but mobs attack and dart around so fast and the fact blocking makes you stand still, it has 0 use, its far easier and far safer to dodge.


..:: Misc ::..

There are only 5, yes FIVE bosses in the entire game, ive seen people beat this game in 16 hours.. for a 40 game that ammount of content is shocking, that price tag is far to high considering the replayability for this game is almost 0, sure there is NG+ but aside from maybe new mob placment and being able to upgrade your gear higher there is nothing new.



::VERDICT::

5/10

Whilst the game has so many glaring problems, the combat is fun, if this game was 20 it would be worth the price tag, right now this game is far to expensive for what you get, and has far to much artifical difficulty, and lack luster bosses.\t\t\t\t\t

This is a shame because The Surge is a truly unique experience, legitimate criticisms can be made, the frequency of one-hit-kill attacks is disappointing and the length is not ideal. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tLevel design is fantastic. One of the biggest things I loved about Dark Souls was the Interconnectivity of the level design. This is something I feel was done poorly in Dark Souls 2 and 3. The Surge has it. It's VERY satisfying to unlock these shortcuts that makes the world feel like it's 1 big place rather than small levels.

The combat feels great. There's a learning curve but once you get a feel for the weapon type you prefer, it's very fluid and fun.

The gear / upgrade system is a lot of fun as well. Targeting some dudes arms because you need to upgrade your own is cool.

Definitely a fun game worth playing especially considering it sells for $10 less than most new releases.

Protip: You CAN parry by blocking at the exact moment you'd be hit by the attack (perfect blocking). I mention this here because most people missed that tutorial popup or weren't paying attention.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is interesting and brings a neat feature that have not really seen in other games in the past. The ability to take parts of NPC's. So not quite loot drops(although they are) but say you see a weapon, helmet, or arm you like, you can choose to chop off said arm and add it to your frame.

Best way I can quickly describe the game is a futuristic Dark Souls. I say this because the game is quite difficult. There really isn't much to the fighting excep dodge, and jump in to hit then dodge some more all whilst keeping an eye on the stamina and holding x for a finisher when it pops up.

Now coming to this from playing For honor (300 + hours) It is a bit confusing as you use the right stick to select body part you want to attack. Instead I keep changing it to block.

The game is fun, if you like dark souls, sci-fi, and an incredibly hard challenge(as you can get rail roaded if you get in too deep with multiple mobs) then pick this up.
