\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge wears its Dark Souls influence on its sleeve, but manages to make something new and interesting with the formula. It's a bit of a mixed bag, to be fair, and a lot of the criticism is warranted. That said, I found that the more I played, the more drawn-in I became. I ended up addicted to the meaty combat and the sense of power you eventually attain, and finished it in about a week. I thought the time spent was well worth it, and I had a lot of fun.

The game looks very good, with nice realistic graphics and a unique techno-apocalypse style. There are, however, a lot of samey-looking hallways and you can end up getting pretty lost in all the twists and turns. I found that tension of danger and exploration very compelling, and it kept me coming back looking for that next shortcut. Music fits the moods well, and now that song "Prisoner" from the med-bay is going to be stuck in my head forever. The story is told in large part through the environments themselves, log messages, and a few other human characters. It's more straightforward than Dark Souls in storytelling, but it keeps a bit of that good mystery around what is going on.

The combat here is of course the highlight, and it feels great when it's working in your favor. Attacks have a lot of weight behind them, and the mechanic of severing body parts is brilliant. However, it can be pretty easy to get stunlocked by just two enemies. There are lots of hidden guys that jump at you immediately, or attack animations which have almost no forewarning. I fell to my death after a combo went on too long... more than a few times. So, while it's not always the best, it's eventually manageable and can be a lot of fun when you're in control of the situation.

One thing I have to mention is that when I started NG+ after beating the game, in the first scene where I get control of my character, I fell through the terrain and died. Then I respawn nearby, and die again, in an infinite loop. This renders my built up character save file totally unusable, and from a Google search I see someone else had the same thing happen. It's not really cool to let bugs that bad slide without fixing them.

So, in conclusion, while it starts a little slow and has plenty of nits I could pick, I had a lot of fun playing this game. If you're a souls fan, or just want a challenging action game to conquer, then this might scratch your itch. I look forward to trying the sequel, which I've heard improves on a lot of issues with this game. This game goes for pretty cheap while on sale, so I'd say it's definitely worth a shot!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge starts of very fun. Beating up enemies and then slicing their limbs off was awesome at the start and I found that this lasted throughout the game. I enjoyed the maps. Learning how areas related to each other was nice. Maybe a few too many areas that looked kinda similar.

A smaller reason for not recommending it is the enemies. Most enemies are in exoskeletons and though they can have different exoskeletons and some different abilities overall once you get the knack of killing them it's not much of an issue unless you try to run through a bunch of baddies and end up getting lynched.

The main issue I had with this game is that the combat is so simple. Once you get the hang of slicing and dodging it's pretty easy. I was (and still am) confused with reviews that talk about this games high difficulty.
Basically, everything you do is slow. Being slow means you die. Be fast and win. It became apparent early on that the fastest armour was the very first armour one can get. I had to stop playing in the final area (the Nucleus) because I got a weapon that did seriously high damage and was super quick. I imagine that finishing the game from where I stopped would take maybe an hour or two, but I really cannot be bothered to go back and just mow down enemies.

All in all, I can see no real reason to recommend this over something like Dark Souls. I like how you get armour pieces but that's about all it's got going for it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo call this game a Dark Souls clone with robots would be too kind to it. What this game is though is very punishing in almost all aspects without the balance that the Dark Souls games have. The basic premise to The Surge is simple. You as Warren a person that was bound to a wheelchair that enrolled with Creo to both get back to work as well as regain the use of his legs. Unfortunatly something ends up going wrong and he wakes up after a very brutal procedure to find that something has gone wrong with the facility. From there its simple Dark Souls teritory. Kill everything that stands in your way on route to the end.

Despite the fact that this game will come off as a Dark Souls clone with robots it does not use any of the same principles. The big plus with this game is that you can pause it anywhere anytime. That unfortunatly is where the plus's end I'm afraid. You see this game is highly focused on skill. To understand what I mean let me break it down for you. You get no gear and equipment from foes by chopping off their armoured or weaponised limbs. From there you can craft and upgrade them provided you have the nesscesary parts to do so. Along the way you'll get scrap which is the games currency. Unfortunatly the points you put into your power core DO NOT translate into upgraded stats which would help act as a way of ballancing the game out. As well as ellevating some of the frustration that comes with such a punishing game. Which is where skill comes into play. Because you cannot upgrade your stats to compensate for the high difficulty and your chosen play style you need to think smart in the long term as you'll need to be able to survive various foes with various weapons and ways of attack. Of course you can upgrade your skill with a type of weapon but its hard to see how it really benefits you in the long run as your core stats can't be upgraded aside from using chips to help with that.

What you get out of this game depends on what you want. If you want a Dark Souls like experince but with robots then this is as close as you'll get. If your looking for balance and really want to set your character the way you want to you won't find it here. This is the sort of game where having a middle option for reviews would be helpful as to me it really feels like one of those types of games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is by far the worst game I was ever forced to finish. To clarify I say forced because I am a completionist, once I start something I have to finish it. I initially purchased the game because I was looking for a good solo story line. I came across this one on sale and thought this would do. The story is not really that bad but the game play is where this one loses it for me. I started playing the game, died a few times and then started to notice that the npc's are hitting rather hard. It was increasingly difficult to navigate and clear areas. I figured it was because I was very weak and began to farm for scrap in order to start upgrading some gear. It did not make much of a difference. After doing some research I found that this game was intended to be really difficult therefore not for the casual gamer IMO. The npc's hit exaggeratedly hard and it just gets worse as you get farther into the game. The maps are designed to counter the play style. Meaning that you have to use a lot of dashes, side stepping, and such causing you fall off the map hundreds of times. Some boss fights are ok, the others are just absurd. So to make a long story short I went into this with the mindset that I would spend 8 - at the most 16 hours on this and have a good time. I finished it at a little over 30 hours and did not have fun at all. It was more like having to wake up and go to that job I hated so much but still had to go. I had no intention on becoming a professional The Surge player in order to finish the story line. Unless your looking for frustration more than anything else, stay away from this game.\t\t\t\t\t
