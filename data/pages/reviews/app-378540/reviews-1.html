\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like Dark Souls but are looking for something other than the medieval motif this is the one. It blends souls-like combat with a real Dead Space vibe. The niche combat mechanic in this one is good too, you get to select the part of the body you want to attack then after a few hits are prompted to perform a finishing move the severs the targeted part from the body in true graphic form. This is also the way you get the parts you need for upgrading... need to upgrade your helmet? Chop some heads off. The upgrading and leveling systems are very dynamic but easy to learn. You collect scrap instead of souls which is the universal upgrade material. Attributes are replaced by interchangeable mod slots that give you everything from healing (like flask from DS which you can choose not to use) to stamina, to elemental damage on your weapon. I'm loving it so much I got the second one before I even finished this one because I know I'll want to go right into it.\t\t\t\t\t

The visuals and the sounds are very good, the map design can be confusing specially in the last area, with no clear plot or objectives you could be running around just searching for something to overcharge or for a door to open in order to progress, this is the only negative point.

Overall pretty fun and cheap on sale. If you like soulslike games give it a try, if you don't maybe try something more polish first like Dark Souls or Elden Ring.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is a very competent Souls style game with a relatively simple story. If you have a souls itch that needs scratching, this can do it for you.

There are two things that make or break Souls-like games: the combat, and the level design. The Surge does a good job with both of these, but you will need to get used to it.

The gameplay is relatively simple, but there are several nuances that are never explained to you. The biggest one that is really never explained is the weapon proficiency levels. When your proficiency with a weapon increases, it will increase the damage you do with that type of weapon. How much extra damage depends on the specific weapons scaling (from Low to Very High) These numbers are added with no additional UI to each weapon you choose, so whatever weapon type you are using will always appear to have higher damage potential than other weapon types. However, if you spend some time fighting with a different weapon, you will quickly catch up in proficiency and see the attack numbers normalize.

In terms of combat, there are also several tools that you will rarely use. You are introduced to a dodge mechanic in the second area of the game (though you can use it from the start). The dodge will allow you to interrupt enemy attacks if you time it properly, but there is only one enemy type in the game that it's useful to use it on. Perhaps other enemies can be dodged to make them easier, but I never found it to be so. Defending in general is pointless when dodging and attacking do such a good job of protecting you. The game offers a pretty low threshold for staggering enemies, so if you just continue to attack rather than defend, you'll likely stun lock enemies, even with the lowest stagger weapons.

As for the level design, most of the levels are very intricate with several levels on top of eachother. It is extremely easy to get lost in these levels, and there are times where the path forward isn't obvious. However, there are always shortcuts that you unlock back to the "bonfire" medical stations. If you want a game that makes you explore to find the way forward, this is it. As great as the Souls games are, they more often than not have a very obvious path forward that you have to follow to reach a boss or new area. In The Surge, I don't believe there is a single level you'll find that is straightforward. If you don't want a winding, difficult to navigate game, you'll hate this one.

As far as the story goes, it's solid, but very simple. You may finish the game and wonder if you missed something or if there's more to it, and there really isn't (so long as you pay attention to what people are saying) If you want deep lore with lots of room for interpretation, this does not provide that, but sometimes it's nice to know exactly what a games is trying to say.

all in all, this isn't the greatest Souls-like game I've ever played, but I did enjoy it and think it's a very solid game. This game has lots of armor and weapons to choose from, has good VA performances, it nails levels and combat, and it has a pretty steep difficulty curve, but not one that feels unfair.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is a pretty fun souls like experience, though it's not quite up there with the FromSoftware offerings.

Solid combat (with a fun at first limb cutting gimmick for getting gear which unfortunately does get old eventually) with responsive controls and (for the most part) good balancing is supported by an interesting, if cliche story, decent graphics which are not the best from technical standpoint, but the design of items and locations is pretty good. On the other hand, the game can be a bit too grindy by the end, the platforming sections are a pain as the physics system is definitely not built with any extensive jumping around in mind and the technical state of the game is pretty poor - framedrops, physics glitches, AI pathing issues, etc. The sound is squarely in the meh category - weapon sounds and ambience are pretty solid, but music and voiceacting are inconsistent, but mostly bad.

The DLC - A Walk in the Park is a pretty solid pick, it's more of the main game with some new gear and enemies with a couple pretty solid boss fights. It also ties in well with the base game and provides some additional lore, characters, etc. The weapon/skin packs are okay on deep discount. The last DLC, the frontier something something is not really worth it. It's basically a rogue-lite mode that's not very good, does not fit with the rest of the game's narrative and it provides gear that's on the level of dying korean MMO - random flashy looking garbage that looks out of place, designed to target dumbasses with low impulse control.

Conclusion: The base game is pretty solid with a couple annoying bits, of the DLC only the Park one is really worth getting, the gear packs are okay when discounted and the last DLC isn't worth it at all.\t\t\t\t\t
