\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverybody is saying this is Dark Souls with robots. They aren't wrong. This is literally Sci-Fi Dark Souls, even down to the controls being identical. The level design is the same. The world is built around the same concept (a vast, decaying environment filled with shambling undead), except it's a sci-fi factory instead of a fantasy castle.

If the design pitch for this game wasn't literally "Dark Souls, but sci-fi", I'd be shocked.

And it's really good. If you liked Dark Souls, get this. If you haven't played Dark Souls, probably get that first, and then play this if you want more.\t\t\t\t\t

Original Review:
So this is my first peek into the world of "Dark Souls" games and all though i cant compare it to its competition i must say i have enjoyed this game alot.

At the point of writing this i am half way through the thrid zone, and i have taken down 2 bosses.

So the bad newz.

* The story is allmost non - exsistent and the voice over is barely servicable
* As info the game has crashed once in 16 hrs of play. No biggie really
* The help you get (as in pop up info) is allmost too much and take abit out of the emersion.
But i suspect doing without them as a newbie is not good either.
* I guess the game is a bit well a lot violent and prob. not for the faint of heart

The good stuff
* The game looks good and runs at a very stable framrate om my GTX 970 @ 1080p
* The game really can pull you into the world
*The game is well made, and have great combat
* Even though your just fighting the same mobs all the time sort off it really havent become boring yet at all
* The game is really great at teaching through mistakes you make. I can feel that everytime i play i get better
* And the difficulty atm. for me is just spot on, the bosses have taken some trys ( alot of trys :-) but defeating them never seemed impossible.

All in all im really having fun with this. Got it for 35€ on the sale and i think it was well worth it.
Is it worth the full price? Hmm weell mabey but... it is steam after all ^^\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely a Dark Souls like game its mechanics. I found myself enjoying it and its unique combat system where you target specific parts of your enemy. It is challenging and I kept dying a lot only to go back for more punishment.

However it didn't keep my interest for too long. I found that it only benefits to use the same weapon for a majority of the time and I had the same armor for a while too. It also does not have a truly engaging story. I haven't gone back to this game because it just simply started to bore me after a while.

That being said, I know many of you would find it more interesting that I did. And I think the developers did a fantastic job for creating a souls like game that wasn't Dark Souls.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPrice: 50€
Duration: less than 20 hours (hardly more than 15 hours, if you don't count all the falling in holes, running in circles for nothing or being forced to grind items)

That already speaks for itself.
But the game is lacking content in any regard. The game has a thin story with few NPCs that are not memorable. The graphics look all the same and there are only 6 small areas to explore with only 5 bosses and less than 10 different types of enemies.

Just judging by quantity, the 50€ price tag feels like an insult to the players. There are many other games out there that offer more (and better) entertainment for less.


But let's also look at the quality of the content instead of just quantity:

Graphics: 6/10
Looks great and runs great and smooth on first look. But soon it becomes clear that there are very few different graphics and levels and everything looks the same. Worse, the lighting effects, which play an important part in the game's dark tunnels, are hideous and unfunctional. It only lights exactly the cone of the lamp and the light doesn't reflect of other surfaces. Add to that, that the lamps always point to the floor while walking, it makes it almost impossible to see anything in the dark areas and result in the player constantly falling into dark holes.
The animations are also all extremely similar.
Then there are a lot of physics bugs - or rather the lack of physics - causing weird lumped up objects to float around the game.
So behind the nice graphics are serious issues and just plain lazy (or rather penny-pinching) design.

Story: 2/10
Plot is interesting, but the way it's told couldn't be more boring and obvious.
Setting is very subjective. Main issue is the lack of ranged weapons in a setting that obviously screams for them. Also setting is too close to modern day life and for that it's not nearly realistic enough like for example GTA.
There are hardly any NPCs in the game. The few NPCs are unengaging and one-dimensional and most of all redundant fellows. Without them, the game wouldn't have changed a bit.
The main character has the personality of a brick wall - yet there is no character customization...
There is hardly any storytelling and the ubiquitous audio logs are just a lazy way of trying to add some mystery and saving making extra NPCs. Often you don't even get to listen to the audio logs because they are either not interesting or the game already continues while you are supposed to listen.
All in all, the game has almost no story and it often is unclear where the player is supposed to go.

Sound: 5/10
Not much to say. Almost no music, not memorable at all. Soundeffects are fitting, but badly mixed.

Gameplay: 4/10
Too much to write here, so I just talk about the main issues in no particular order:

1. Level Design
Small, cramped, confusing, repetitive. Kind of fits melee only, but still a nightmare to navigate, especially with the little to no direction from the game. Too many dark holes that are hardly visible. Too many places the player accidentally falls off to while fighting. Too many places that look like the player can go there but instead dies.

2. Limb Targetting
Nice in theory, but in practice just a choice between more damage or more loot. It doesn't even matter much which limb you target as it doesn't change anything but the armor values which are either too much to stagger the enemy or not enough to stop you from spamming the enemy to death.
It also makes no sense to only get the stuff you cut off while the rest just... burns?
Why does the game even have multiple armor parts? It's just pointless grind and the set bonus makes it so that nobody will ever want to mix...
Most of all, the limb targetting takes forever and forces the player to scroll through limbs for every enemy - especially annoying if said enemies stand up again or if the player has to try and switch targets while fighting.
All in all, the limb targetting is redundant and a chore.

3. Horizontal / Vertical Swings
Supposedly that's for hitting the targetted limbs. In reality the player will use the same attack the whole time, regardless of what he is targetting.
Worse, every weapon has those attacks, so the all feel like the same weapon with different speed.

4. Difficulty and balancing
All in all, the game is way too easy.
Most deaths come from falling in holes. 2nd most come from gettin one shotted or insta-comboed by enemies. Never died because I actually ran out of health packs.
Health packs are instant and super casual to use.
Enemy placement is boring - 10 samey enemies in a row.
Enemy placement is unfair - often hidden and then onehitting the player from behind or while in combat.
Holes are hard to see.
Yet the game features no actual death traps - just bad level design.
Enemies easily combo the player, without being able to retaliate.
Yet enemies have a hard time hitting the player at all because of the ridiculous player mobility and the extremely low endurance cost of evasion and the extremely high amount of possible endurance.
Finishers are not only extremely annoying to watch, interrupting the gameplay, promoting grinding, but also make things even more easy as you can easily just skip the last 25% of combat by pressing E.
Armor is almost useless.
Damage calculations make no sense.
Enemy humanoids have moves the player doesn't.
The list is endless and I can't be bothered to continue anymore, also apparently there is a limit to reviews here.

5. Backtracking
The backtracking is just a really, really bad idea. Nobody wants to go back to the first level to pick up some pointless item he doesn't need to finish the game. Why not make it available the moment the player sees it? The mechanic itself is already bad, since all that stops the player from getting is is some weird button with a number on it - it's neither fun nor engaging and mostly just a chore.

6. RPG System / Character Development
There is almost no character development. Core power is just a number to open stuff and forcing backtracking. Implants are not permanent but yet almost predetermined since you will want to use health and endurance implants that fill most of the slots anyway - so the player doesn't really have a choice.
Overall, there is no sense of character development whatsoever.

7. weapon combos
Use a combination of different vertical and horizontal swings to do a different animation. Doesn't do more damage and locks the player in the animation for longer.... pointless and actually detrimental since just spamming the same attack does more damage and faster.

8. weapon types
5 weapon types. Heavy duty and single rigged feel almost the same. The rest is samey because of the vertical / horizontal swings. The weapon proficiency scaling makes it almost impossible to switch weapon unless you want to grind 5 hours extra...

9. general combat
The combat basically boils down to either staggering and spamming the enemy to death or the enemy having too much armor and forcing the player to jump --> attack --> jump away --> repeat. That goes for every enemy in the game. Also there is nothing else to do in the game. Just the same boring combat the whole time. It doesn't even feature ranged combat - what a joke.

10. camera
Camera in a 3rd person game should be easy right? Keep the player char always centered right in the middle and maybe move the cam upwards at a wall right?
Not in the Surge!
Here the camera often just floats around randomly, changing angles and interrupting the game.
Worst part is that in the 3rd boss fight, suddenly, out of nowhere, the camera intendedly zooms out so far that it's almost impossible to see the player character anymore, killing the player for sure.


Overall: 4/10


I would like to write more, but there is a limit to comments here. But in general you can see the game is lacking in both quantity and quality.
Ultimately it's worth maybe 5€, but even then I would feel like I wasted my time compared to other games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA rip off of Dark Souls? Yes, and for me, that's a great thing. "More Dark Souls is always welcome, if not, is revolutionary." -Yahtzee (Zero Punctuation)

I've only made it to the second area but I am loving it already, it's different enough from Dark Souls that it has more than just its own Sci Fi skin to it. For me, this setting is more interesting. While it would be blasphemy to claim that this game has more beautifully themed areas with scenic views, but I do enjoy the story more. There seems to be much more of a story in this game. The main character, Warren, was a new employee in a futuristic tech industry called "Creo" and when he was receiving his tech implants, something went horribly wrong, and he wakes up outside the company's fascility with no knowledge of what has happened. Unlike Dark Souls, this story seems to have a suspense factor to it. I'm only four hours in but unlike Dark Souls I have that suspense factor driving me forward, because of the urge to find out what has happened, why all these armored monsters have appeared and what went wrong enough to cause all the helper robots to go rogue.

Unlike Dark Souls, which could boast of gameplay that was almost completely absent of RNG, this game actually is. Instead of the mindless grind to farm certain items and equipment that you would innevitably have to endure at some point in Dark Souls, this game lets you deliberately target certain body parts on an enemy to get the equipment that you see on them. (i.e. attack his right arm if that weapon looks like it's for you :D) in order to receive it you have to perform the right hit combos so as not to kill him and generate enough energy (which is different from stamina) so that you can perform the right finisher attack, then bam, the weapon is yours.

Like Dark Souls, each weapon has a dozen differerent ways to attack and combine moves, making this very dynamic and engaging gameplay. The boss of the tutorial area was much more challenging than the Asylum Demon, for me. There was a certain pattern you had to follow in order to even damage it, I hope for more extremely aggressive puzzle bosses, they are the best!

Note: There's a complete lack of multiplayer mechanics in this game, so that might be a real turn off for you jolly-cooperation fans, but I didn't care. I always enjoyed Dark Souls for the single player experience, and The Surge is just more of that.

Pros

Gameplay 10/10
-Everything that made Dark Souls good and more, I love the new targetting mechanics they've added, and on top of that there's plenty of crafting, equipment management, aggressive enemies, puzzle bosses, catwalks, jump scares, shortcuts, and probably plenty of secrets I haven't discovered yet.

Level Design 8/10
-Maybe the level design is better, but I innevitably have to compare it to Dark Souls and I would give Dark Souls level design an 11/10, the areas are brilliantly and artistically layed out and connected to eachother, and one should never expect a game to ever do that so well as Dark Souls did, but this game comes reasonably close.

Atmosphere 8/10
-I know people say Dark Souls has better atmosphere with its lack of music, but I will always disagree, and my same complaint applies here. The immersion could be enhanced and each area had its own themed, ethereal and ambiant noises. The pop song that plays in the Medbay/Bonfire is pretty lit but that's it. Still, the dystopian future feel is just as strong with this game as the Dark Fantasy feel was with Dark Souls. It's good fun to run around getting chased by bloodthirsty rogue-bots while the Creo lady reminds you over intercom "Safety at work is our highest priority."

Story 7/10
-You would never say this game has a great story, it's just not the reason to play it, but I really enjoy the suspense factor this game has in contrast with Dark Souls. For a game with focus on gameplay, it's a nice added feature that is driving me to keep playing.


Cons

Polish
-Hardcore gameplay like this is ruinned by glitches, and I've encountered a few annoying ones that temporarily hindered my progress, due to the inability to save, like Dark Souls. Still, none of them were game breaking, but certainly annoying.

Conclusion

The areas just aren't as masterpieceful as Dark Souls, and while the dynamics are there, wielding some piece of sci-fi junk just isn't the same as a classic Dark Fantasy adventurer with a halberd and platemail. No, it's not as good as Dark Souls, expecting anything to be would be unreasonable and pretty much gaurantee your disapppointment, but it's good, really good, and better than Dark Souls in certain ways. I certainly hope we keep getting Dark Souls rip-offs like this.

8.5/10 (might get higher if later areas impress me more)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, the surge is basically dark souls 1 in a futuristic environement.

Besides the obvious similar gameplay due to demon souls inspiring a hole videogame genre, both games share the same characteristics:

+ Huge interconected world with a lot of shortcuts
+ Punishing yet rewarding game experience
+ A very very high skill cap
+ Gear is important but won't carry you through the game
+ Both games don't hold your hand and tell you what is happening and where you have to go. You must figure it out yourself!

Even if they are similiar, some differences can also be noted between dark souls 1 and the surge:

=> Environement variation: Dark Souls had way more environement diversity than the Surge, although it isn't that bad in the latter
=> Looting: The Surge looting system is much more rewarding, as loot depends on your capacity to cut down parts of the enemies
=> Crafting and character customisation: The Surge feels way more in deep that Dark Souls one's, with implants and different armor configuration that matters for other things than just poise and protection
=> Weapon diversity: Dark Souls 1 has a massive advantage over The Surge, although weapons from the latter seems to have much more diverse moveset. Also it seems to me that The Surge has way less useless weapons that Dark Souls.


If you are a souls-like fan you should go for the Surge without hesitation!\t\t\t\t\t
