\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a Sci-Fi dark souls that fails in combining it's multiple aspects well. I wouldn't recomment buying it unless you're a huge souls fan. If not then get it on sale. 50 dollars is honestly too much for this game.

There are multiple good parts of this game that i'll list.
-Good graphics and well optimized
-Large array of menu options
-First area was amazing
-Combat mechanics are amazing on their own that could have an entire review writen about them
-You can cut off enemy body parts in an execution and use it yourself
-Has a lot of unique ideas, most notable ones being the implant system that works as your stats and items with some increasing health and some giving you an extra heal, and how the enemies are more like minibosses with only 5 bosses in the game (although i've heard 1 is repeated)
-Probably a few more small ones that i've missed

Cons on the otherhand
-The game gets worse area after area. The first one was amazing but afterwards it got too cramped and maze-like
-The combat mechanics that are amazing are hardly ever used properly by anything in this game
-You feel stagnant compared to how fast the enemies progress
-The very similar levels along with a massive amount of back tracking and confusing cramped layouts and shortcuts in these decently small areas makes it boring and tedious to go through and sometimes gives you a feeling of no real progression
-The enemeis go from fun to fight yet still being difficult. to just leaping out at you, all easily taking out a good chunk of your health with no issues (I am using full heavy armor with 2 health upgrades and 6 different heals) taking the miniboss aspect to an obnoxious level when added with the other features in this game
-One example of a shortcut in the 3rd level that really made me decide how I felt about the game was one where not only is it hard to reach, but on both sides of you there are enemeis waiting to gank you, the direction you want to go in has 2-3, the other direction has 1 enemy but you have to fight like 2 more enemies, and you don't really avoid fighting multiple enemies either. This shortcut is also surrounded by poison. Some of the enemies that gank you are very difficult to fight alone, with hardly any openings as their constant jump attacks go too far to attack them afterwards and attacking them when they face you, no matter what, will hurt you as they jump back to do their follow up which is the forwards leap that's difficult to punish and sometimes hard to just dodge. It's a terrible level
-Executions used to steal enemy parts are an animation that moves you around. So if you're attacking an enemy next to a hole in the ground or a cliff, you have a good chance of falling. Not to mention that there are multiple examples of ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ holes in the ground.
-The weapon progression system doesn't encourage using multiple weapon types with your damage in certain weapon types going up the more you use the specific weapon, this makes weapon types you never use early on way too difficult to ever use later on.
-Other smaller issues I probably missed

Overall this game isn't bad. But it definitely isn't good. It has too many issues with it's level design to it's enemies. All of these issues ruin the otherwise great game that could of been made. I'd love a sequel to this game honestly, one that delivers a better story with better voice actors with better level design and more variety in how it's played. It felt too difficult with the best (and arguably only) way to progress is by running through levels. If you get good enough you can kill every enemy but, just run though. You'll be weaker, but you will also enjoy it more. ♥♥♥♥ the enemies in this game. They ruin it along with the level design. You really cannot trust a general review of this game, aka most of the positive ones. It's a good idea on paper that was poorly executed and most of them talk how it looks like on paper. Like they never really played it. It almost feels like payed reviews with how simple and optimistic they are. Do what you want but be warned about how little the good reviews mean about the issues of this game. As the issues cancel out a lot of the good parts.\t\t\t\t\t

First of all you have to know i've played all dark souls (a lot of hours) and also played LoTF (only 1 time, the game was bad imo, to slow...)

Good points of the Surge :
+ Environments (area's are beautifull and according well with the universe, you really feel the industry robotic atmosphere all the time)
+ Executions sytem (you can finish enemies with an execution when they are low hp, and those executions are cool and violent)
+ Looting/upgrading system (you can dismember your opponents by executions and loot the part you need to upgrade your gear. You got 7 parts of armor that you can upgrade while killing ennemies)
+ Money/souls system (you earn "souls" each time you kill an enemy or exploring the world. You can upgrade your level (core) while spending souls at 'bonfire' (safe zone here).
+ Respawning mobs (they only respawn when you go again in safe zone so you can farm some items and souls if needed)
+ Quick fight (each fight is pretty quick and your character is fast when he move/dodge)
+ You can choose to attack diffrents parts of the ennemies (non armored = ennemy die quickly but you will not loot item, armored = ennemy more resistant but you can loot and upgrade your gear :))

And now the cons :
- Combat sytem is a little to simple. Only vertical and horizontal attack, block is useless so you will spend all your time to dodge
- Music are not that much good
- Sometimes the camera is pretty obvious, especially when there is lot of ennemies/big ennemies
- The lock system is slow and not really optimized (you need to push R then push LT and switch with your pad yo switch ennemy..While dodging /moving camera it becomes hard to do all things at a time)
- Upgrade system is decent but you cant spend "points" in vitality, stamina, dmg etc...Only your core lvl change all of this. Upgrading armor/weapons is also decent but not efficient as it needs to be imo.
- no coop ? :D

So globally The Surge is a very good game. The unique futurist style of this game make him good and the "dark souls like" is still present. A great game for player that likes difficulty. I will do the game 3 times i think.

The Surge In my Opinion\t

The Surge is a souls like action RPG that focuses on dynamic combat dodging every enemy attack possible and fast paced action slowly fighting them one at a time. The level design in this game is great rather repeditive and by repeditive i mean they re-use the same level literally 3 times over and just change the enemy placement/loot locations. Ontop of this as you progress further and further into the level it becomes more evident that instead of creating challenging enemies the developers instead just made them unfair. Enemies can, for example be in the same gear as you, with the same weapon, and 2 or 1 hit you. Enemies later on in the game just outright end your existance before you can even get an attack off.

Character progression is another area in which The Surge fails. Unlike a true souls like game dying while holding a heap of 'scrap' doesn't really matter that much since your core power doesn't equate to anything other than slightly more health/stamina or more injectables. Not to mention you can just bank it if you don't currently have anything to upgrade. All your characters power will come from his gear and his proficiency with your preffered weapon type. Which is a huge game design flaw since it forces you to stick with a single weapon type throughout your gameplay. So good luck when you kill that boss you have been trying to kill for the past 2 hours and get a weapon that is drastically worse than the one you got from the starting zone. All because you never used staves.

Most of the issues with this game could be adressed with balance changes, and bug fixes. The developers could give players a reason to upgrade their core level outside of what doing so already offers. Like better scaling on injectables, base weapon damage increases, and perhaps armor increases. They could also balance some of the later game enemies and completly remove the proficiency scaling and just bake those stat increases into upgrading core power level.

In its current state I would say don't purchase this game. You will be better off waiting for the developers to patch and balance their game. Then pick it up in a sale.

For speedrunning (which is what I personally spent most of my time doing in this game) it isn't a bad game to try. This is mainly based on my experience with the un-patched version of the game where the devs left in some tools you can abuse to get strange things to happen. Simple tech that is easy to learn/use makes it a beginner friendly speedrun game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like this game, but the devs need to think about balancing.

-Armor is pointless when most of the enemies are capable of 2 shotting you.

-Your weapons do very little damage compared to the mobs you're fighting. This wouldn't be a bad thing if the enemies weren't capable of destroying you just by sneezing in your general direction.

-Why the ♥♥♥♥ would you fill 80% of a level with ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ shooting fire and gas at you? Those enemies are painfully boring to fight; and giving them a melee attack that can 1 shot you isn't challenging, its just tedious.

There are a lot of good things about this game. Its gorgeous and has a great art style. The combat feels weighty and I'm a big fan of the targetting and execution system. I really like the crafting and upgrading. I like the story and, despite it being a little cliche, I also like that you have to pay attention to all the little details to figure out how the story progresses.

I just wish the devs take some of the feedback on board, because I'm not the only person who is annoyed by the weird balancing in this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr 9/10, I hope this company makes more games like this.

Loved this game from start to finish. Being a fan of all four Souls games, I felt right at home with the fast and difficult combat. Gear and implant progression is simple, but satisfying. And with no stats to worry about, you can change your playstyle relatively simply.

I see a lot of reviews complaining that the game becomes "unfair" towards the end, and I can honestly say they're absolutely full of it. I hate to be the guy to say they need to "git gud".. but it's true. I recall three points in the game were I thought it was becoming too difficult to continue. But rather than quit and write a negative review, I took another look at my gear and implants. Each time I modified my playstyle to be a little better, my loadout to be a little more cohesive. The end result was my character could tear through opposition, with some careful planning and tactics.

All that being said, there are a few cons. The story, while mildly engaging, wasn't ground breaking. Some dialogue is downright bad. There's also a quirk where you can't cancel an attack combo, which can be very frustrating in the wrong situation. (my weapon launches into a 3 hit, 3 second long combo after tapping the mouse button twice, if something goes wrong mid combo I was basically dead with no way to react) Also there's some crystal enemies towards the end, which are beatable, but arent fun to fight (with very little pay off in regards to tech scrap gained) There are only about 5 bosses, and no online play.

All that being said, fantastic game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a huge fan of the "Souls" games. I've played them all. I really liked Lords of the Fallen, and I was so looking forward to The Surge. But I am so unbelievable disappointed in this game. I am to the third area, but have stopped playing. And here's why.

1. No purpose in life: "Souls" games aren't heavy on clear stories, but The Surge has taken vagueness to a whole new level. You're an employee of a company where something has went wrong. Then a hologram tells you to go into the factory. That's the story so far threw 2 areas. You'll have no idea of why you're doing what you're doing, where you're going, or why you should even go there if you did.

2. The body part targting system: Great in theory, but horrible in practice. We you have two or more enemies on you, you don't have time to target a specifc spot. You just want them gone. But you have to fiddle around with targeting while just tring to survive.

3. Some enemy attacks just can't be dodged. There are these four legged machines (don't know what they are called) that have an attack that just can't be dodged (at least I haven't figured out how). They leap from some ways away, but no matter when or how far you move, you can't get out of their way. This wouldn't be so bad if it didn't cause massive amounts of damage, and the fact that they can take a beating. So you'll get in close, then jump back to dodge a close attack. Then here comes the leap.

4. Attacking: This is another thing that is great in theory, but horrible in practice. You can either choose to attack slowly vertically or horizontally. That's it. Even the fast attack weapons are quite slow compaired to other games. The light/heavy attack offence wasn't broken, so why replace it with something subpar Deck 13?

I wanted so desperatly to like this game because I was craving a new "Souls" game. But Deck 13 missed the mark with this one. I hope some of the things are resolved with patches, but the fundementals of the game are just flawed.\t\t\t\t\t

So you will spend lot of time finding the right was to the next area.

I think it is safe to say: this is a wasted opportunity of a good idea of the exo suite hand to hand combat and good basic story also. Probably lack of founding, and too much grandiose goals of developers not properly realized.\t\t\t\t\t

-Great new enviroment
-Innovative way to get items
-"That" reveal
-Bosses are fun and feel like the good ol' days
-Shortcuts Dark Souls could be proud of

What I don't like:
-Hits still don't have that feel to it
-Most weapons are boring to use
-Enemies get repetitive

TL;DR: Buy it if you want to have a fun souls-like experience and you're ready for a challenge. The only problem that might be a turn off to you, if you enjoy Souls-Like games, is I don't feel it when I hit people in this game like i did in any Souls game.\t\t\t\t\t
