\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Surge is not a bad game, but it's souls-like aspect, however, could do some work.

Pros:
The gameplay's pretty solid, the story is interesting enough for you to still want to discover the truth even after grinding for hours. It's visuals are pretty nice, and the combat mechanics are the type that just *feel* right. The execution animations are pretty dope too.

Cons:
- It takes more than a couple of hours to get from one zone to another, and while they are fun to explore for the first time, it quickly becomes an annoying grinding routine to at least have a chance to survive in the next one. So if grinding's not your thing I would suggest skipping this game.

- I don't know if it's only my case, but the equipment upgrading system gets unusable for me after reaching armor/weapon level 2 (The "Mk.IV" materials are damn hard to find), which forces you to face enemies while underleveled.


The main conflict I have with this game it's that no matter how much you grind (which is all the time) your character still feels like it's at level 1, while all the other enemies keep getting harder to kill, and can kill you in less than 3 hits, no matter which class you pick. The armor sets you can aquire only feel like cosmetics since the damage you receive is still the same. It doesn't have much of a sense of progression.

Good game, just needs some polishing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've tried to write this review many times and keep deleting what I wrote, so I've decided to go with a pros and cons list for simplicities sake (NOTE: I have not beaten the game and do not intend to due to my complaints listed below) Lets start with the positives:

PROS:
-Runs very well in terms of FPS and general performance (Obviously this differs from PC to PC, but the game seems to be optimzed well)
-animations are fluid and are satisfying
-combat, for the first few hours of the game, is fun and enjoyable and you can really see what the developers were going for with the limb targeting.
-the overall aestetic of everything is fleshed out well. The industrial mecha vibe is strong throughout and the weapons and armor designs are great
-The intropart of the game, before you get your powersuit, felt really natural and enjoyable and i wish other games had interesting opening like this. It made me wish the story was better.
-Despite what i'm about to say in the cons, this is a (mostly) fun game. It feels unique in its combat and deserves and purchase when i goes on sale.

CONS:(Where to start...)
-Characters are dull and meaningless and voice acting is meh. None of the NPC were very interesting at all and really had no bearing on the part of the story i played.(Coming from Dark Souls where the community is spoiled with wonderfull charcters and voice acting, I guess it was foolish of me to expect that level of quality right off the bat)
-Hit dettection felt wonky at times. To be fair, most of the time it was ok, but i would have liked a bit more consistancy.
-Most weapons in the game felt unuseable. The combat in this games is fast, which is a good thing. However, several of the weapon classes felt as if you were moving through a swamp while the enemies were spinning circle around you pummeling you to death. Dont get me wrong, the moves sets for each weapon were interesting and fun to mess around with, but some were just too slow. Its a shame because the single rig, which has you strapped with one huge blade one your arm had some of the coolest designed weapons, but had and abysmal moveset.
-There is no satisfying feeling of progression. Leveling up your rig lets you equip more plug-ins sure, but they really dont feel very impactful due to reasons ill get to later. So if leveling up is lame, how about upgrading gear? Is that satisfying?
-Gear Upgrades feel pointless. While the weapons upgrades did allow you to progress easier thorugh a level due to the dmg increase, the armor upgrades had not effect on gameplay at all. The heavy armor felt the same as the light armor and only a few set bonuses were actually worth anything. Id say about 2 out of (i think)6 armor sets in the game felt useful. Not only that, but the materials used to upgrade gear needs to be grinded off of your enemies via the limb system. Its not too bad, but it turns the limb targeting into more of a chore than I think it should be. Enough about armor.
-Most enemies, outside of the first few hours, will at most 3 shot you. This is what really killed my fun. I would spend an hour or so trying to grind to level up my gear only to go back to a troublesome enemy and still get oneshot. The worst offender of this is the security gaurds. You meet them releativly early in the game and they push your ♥♥♥♥ in, but you think "oh ok, this must be an enemy I come back and fight later". You'd be half right. The games wants you to fight them when you first meet them for a side quest and they destroy you until you learn to cheese them, BUT when you come back later, several more hours into the game with better gear mind you, they will still 1 or 2 shot you. Most of the enemies you come across can do this and it just gets old after you die to a random jobber for the millionth time.
-Enemy and check point placement is brutal. Enemies first. Im used to cheeky placement of enemies from Dark Souls, however in this game, when the lock on system is horedous, fighting more than one enemy seems literally inpossible. Dodging and managing one enemy who can 1 shot you is hard enough, but 2 or 3 ( or at this one part 5) thats crazy. Each enemy can have hyper armor so even if you connect with one enemy in a group the others could just push right through you. To touch on shortcut placement really quick, the 2nd area, might be the WORST haul through an area with the least amount of short cuts ive ever played in my life.
-Combat becomes boring and unfun. In order to deal with some of these outrageous enemies you have to resort to BS methods of damaging them and abandon the not-so-useful, but still cool, combos the game trys to getyou to use. Too many enemies are armored, which essentially means, they will ignore your combos and attack you anyway.
-The game feels like it has conflicting ideas. The combat seems to work best in medium sized spaces with 1 or 2 enemies to deal with, and the first few hours feels good, cause thats mostly where youre fighting the enemies. After that tho, the game has you funnling down literally pitch black air vents where it demands you fight an enemy that is as wide as the pathway so you can get past. Most of the later sections of the game have these small corridors and mobs of enemies to deal with that fly in the face of making the game feel good.
-"Soul Drop" Much in the same way DS does it, when you die you drop your tech scrap and you have to go get it before you die again. However, what this game didnt figure out is how to place that care package in a reachable spot and not in the middle of nowhere. In this game, the scrap drops exactly where you died, not a few seconds before, not on the platform you were on right before you fell, but exactly at the point where you die. I had a death marker that was just floating in mid air because the ground dropped out and dropped me into a bottemless pit.
A few Minor complaints:
-The dialouge would play over itself or an audio log would cut out important plot dialouge.
-If you're gonna have an NPC in the world, dont have enemies stay agro'd when talking to them.
-in my 20 hours playing i only ran into 3 boss fights. On fight was just a guy who after taking a bit of dmg would just run away and call in a previous boss to fight. It happens 3 times in that fight alone.
-If youre gonna have a dodge move, put a fair amount of i-frames in.
-if youre gonna have a block, make it so it works and not so every enemy can just hit you throught it.
-I would have liked more weapon or armor variaty. The discovery of new armor and weapons was really cool, i just wish it didnt become obsolete so quickly.
I could go on probably for a bit and im sure i had more complaints, but its 3 o'clock at night and my saltiness over this game is wearing out. Overall, I find my self severely dissapointed in this game. I wanted something that could take up my time like DS did. I honestly wish i could return it. I probably wont beat it, and if i do beat it, I certainly will never play it again. This game had several ideas it was playing with and many of them just didnt pan out.

Final Verdict: 3/5\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game on Xbox, and it was horrendous. I had it refunded within 3 minutes. 30FPS is a dead joke at this point. That said, I immediately purchased it on Steam, and within 15 minutes I was in a wheelchair! Exciting stuff, I know! 60FPS lock, also a dead joke... Anyway, looks beautiful at 60FPS! Feels great, no matter how bad I am with WASD, and it feels very fluid. Not fluid as in fast though, it's very heavy. Every movement and physical action, so far, makes sense. Based on the intro, I really feel like the main guy is both exhausted, but excited. That may just be my imagination, my preference, but I love it. It's a 'this sucks, but I can walk so I feel like it was worth it!' kind of vibe for me, even in the combat. I know I haven't played much, but what I'm concluding is in the first few hours, I've enjoyed the intro to the game, the wonders of where the story will go, the movement and combat, and of course the difficulty. I've died to the boss many times, then randomly I realized I was ignoring the hint that I read several times, that was literally right in front of me! It felt great to realize that the environment wasn't simply a landscape, but kind of a living place. I felt like someone was studying this boss, and decided it was a good idea to take it down, and let everyone know how, in case someone like me came by. It's fun, it looks great, it feels great, it's tough, it's worth.\t\t\t\t\t

Edit.
I did not mention at first the good things about this game due initial disappointment so I mightaswell put them here aswell to be fair.

Annoying, but once you get hang of it its manageable. Combos are easily learned and quite nice in one sense thou at some point they may start feel bit repetitive.

Way how you collect pieces for armor upgrades is smart. Instead of hacking random pieces you choose what you need and cut that, or you aim for unarmored parts which does extra damage but gives no loot besides scrap metal chunks you might get from any part.

Levels infact do look quite nice at first.. though you will be running some parts so often, because of constantly dying, they mightaswell be white endless corridor after a while.\t\t\t\t\t
