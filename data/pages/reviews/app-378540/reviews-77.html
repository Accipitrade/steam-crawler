\r\n\t\t\t\t\tActually a very good, methodical action RPG with a great setting, intelligent level design, interesting mechanics and good combat.

Personally, I really liked the movement and combat animations: Attacks had the weight I expect from swinging a 3 meter long power hammer, and movement felt as if my character really had a heavy exo suit screwed onto him. The gameplay loop of chopping body parts off enemies to get their armour or weapons was also very interesting.
The only real con is that I never felt the need to use a different armour set or weapon, once I found a set that fits my playsytyle.

All in all: Yes, it is different than Dark Souls, but that is fine. Definitely an improvement from Lords of the Fallen (which was also a good game in my book) and well worth a buy, if you are into "Soulslike" games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA gem in the rough.

This game does not have the best story, the voice acting is kinda meh, and you never really understand what is going on. But you have so much fun beating up zombies, robots, evil guards and others with various industrial equipment, that it doesn't matter. It is so gorgeous, pleasing and satisfying to play, that you don't care that it's basically a carbon copy of Dark Souls. Which it most certainly is. Only in the future.

Every single hit feels weighty, all the environments are beautiful, and the gear you get all looks great. I could not stop playing until I finished, and now I excitedly await the next installment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn underappreciated game that kinda flew under the radar, The Surge is a souls-like game with grindy progression. The surge is not for everybody, in the sense that the gameplay is hard and unforgiving, just a couple mistakes (sometimes only one) and you're back to square one. While you can't customize your character, there is a plethora of armor and weapons for you to use and upgrade, each with their own categories and quirks. I would recommend The Surge to anyone looking for a good twist on a souls game with some challenge. If you come into this game expecting a laid back casual experience and in-depth story to be engrossed in, you will be sorely disappointed. In my opinion the combat system is fun and engaging, and as for the grind it can be brutal in some areas but I still found it enjoyable. Plenty of upgrades and pickups are scattered for you to find while exploring the maze-like maps, which is where it can also start to get frustrating. You will find a lot of locked doors and entrances that can't be unlocked until later, some enemies will catch you offguard jumping to attack you from a doorway or direction out of sight not giving you enough time to react,some poorly placed jumps will lead to your doom, and most of the sidequests are kinda bs "help me find this" with zero indication on what or where you're supposed to go. Overall though a great experience with enough replay value to keep me hype for The Surge 2, the nitpicks and frustrations I experienced can be overlooked as learning experiences for when I had to run back through an area after death lol.

TLDR: solid graphics, solid combat, solid aesthetic, poor side quests, and it couldve been a little longer/had a couple more bosses

OH and there's a fair amount of backtracking but don't worry there are shortcuts you can discover\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter the announcement of The Surge 2 I picked up this game on the sale and was pleasantly surprised, because it wasn't as bad as some claim it to be. And clearly better than Lords Of The Fallen that Deck13 were co-developer of. They clearly learned a thing or two from the LoTF experience and who knows how much room to grow they still have.

The character design may be a bit silly at times (especially when I found the Angel armor that looks almost comical), but when you sink into the game and stop comparing it to DS and just play the game it becomes engaging and makes sence lore wise. There are a bit too many vent shafts to my liking, because of them the world doesn't feel open enough, but at the same time shortcuts all over the map actually make it feel pretty vast. There are several "hubs" that load separately as different levels of the map which ruins the immersion a bit. Gameplay itself is pretty specific at what it wants to achieve and how it achieves it.

Overall I'd call it a good example of Focus Interactive game. The games they publish aren't inherently bad or boring. I'd even call their games fun, but at the same time they are not something that you would remember on your deathbead. Sure there are some exceptional games they published (like recent Plague Tail for instance). But let's just be real here - they are not in the big league and it is perfectly fine. That said I'm looking forward to The Surge 2.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: A sci-fi soulsborne game

The Surge is essentially a sci-fi soulsborne game with a clearer story and it's own strengths and weaknesses. Objectively speaking, there's little wrong with the game apart from a few dodgy camera angles. As such, it deserves a thumbs up.

Personally, I didn't like the game that much, but most of my reasons for disliking it are too subjective for me to consider it as a strike against the game.

What I thought about it:
  • Story isn't brilliant, but it's good enough. The intro was short and on-point, however, to my dismay, most of the game's story is told through audiologs found through the game, many of which are easy to miss
  • Gameplay is great. It's hefty, and it really feels like you're giving the enemy a solid whack when you hit them. The Surge also has a very unique system of upgrading your equipment, where you target an enemy's limbs, lopping off their respective gear pieces to claim for yourself
  • The level design is equally genius and abhorrent, both obscenely intricate and labyrinthian. Personally, I found it cumbersome to navigate, and the very repetitive theme made it dull
  • Boss fights were brilliant, but the generic enemies were lacking in variety
  • Decent length considering the price, and supports New Game +
  • Well optimised

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was very disappointed in this game.
I've played through Dark Souls 1, DS2 more times than I can count, DS3 and Bloodborne are not alien to me. This game felt most like BB, but with DS2's DLC levels of scaling. The difficulty from one enemy to the next varied greatly. I could practically oneshot one enemy, then the next would stunlock and kill me.
I wanted to like this game, I really did, but this inconsistent difficulty ruined it for me. The ENTIRE game felt cheapened by this one misstep.
Admittedly I enjoyed the design of the characters, the armor, the weapons, etc. The bosses (save for the last two) were fun to fight and different from any other fight in the game.
If you like the Souls format, you like the setting, and you're willing to put up with the inconsistencies, I guess I can recommend the game.

My biggest gripe- and perhaps I should write this after I cool down from the experience- is the final boss. Throughout the game I used a high-energy low health build. It was dynamic, it was great. I built up speed with one enemy, then tore through the rest. It was fun. The final boss, however, did not allow for this build. The energy production from the final boss is so minimal, and they deal so much damage, that I was being one-shot with no opportunity to recover or even get started. After a few hours of slamming my head against a wall (call me stubborn), I threw up my hands, went back to Ops, and redid my implant plan.

Instead of playing what I wanted to for the final boss, I cooked up a milque-toast tank/instahealth build and beat the boss in one go.

Buy it on sale. It's not worth 30 bucks. It felt way too short, and poorly balanced to boot. Don't let it be your first entry into the Souls series.\t\t\t\t\t
