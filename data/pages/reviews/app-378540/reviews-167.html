Qualms.

Cannot turn off country music at save areas.

With SPC controlling a PS3 controller, while moving the left joystick up (moving forward), if a button is pressed, it can wig out resetting the input to center. This is annoying because you will be trying to run or move forward, and sometimes the software just won't let you. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tCombat : The combat is very tight and responsive , the mechanic of targeting limbs works very well. You get horizontal and vertical attacks, which can be mixed up for different results. Mostly you find yourself trying to combo up your energy meter to do the awesome looking excution attacks - which trigger a cool slo-mo camera and are used to cut off parts, but you can disable the slow motion if you like. The dodging makes combat extremly fun and fast paced if you choose to be more aggressive, dashing is incredibly important and I suggest using it more than blocking. The dash attack is incredibly fun and makes a ton of badass dodges possible.

Equipment : The EXO suits look really cool and fitting for the "sci-fi construction" theme the game has. There's a decent variety in armor and the stats (attack speed, stamina consumption on dodging/running etc) make it so that you can build your character in a way that fits your playstyle perfectly. The weapons look AMAZING and feel badass as hell to use - ranging from a pair of forklift prongs as "claws" to a gigantic Hammer/rivet gun hybrid- there's a weapon for everyone!

Difficulty : Merciless, if you make one mistake you pay for it heavily. Engaging multiple enemies makes this even more true, and usually results in your death if you don't play smart. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tA newbie's Journey into the Dark Souls genre...


The Surge is in no doubt inspired by Dark souls but that's not a bad thing. You see this an emerging genre that's now exploring new a creative idea such as The Surge. Now what The Surge does right is it combat, while others say the combats is boring and repetitive, I feel it nails it perfectly for those who've never played a Dar Souls like game such as myself. Combat also involves the use of cutting off limbs to acquire new gear and weapons in extremely satisfying finishing moves. Now for the enemies, yes, they're difficult but I don't feel that this is a bad thing. You see having this high skill cap for some of the enemies makes it so that when you do kill them you have this huge wave a satisfaction, and you do get well rewarded for doing so as well. Now the only biggest down side is the surge has too few bosses. But other than that, I feel that The Surge is a great tribute to the Dark Souls genre and great place for those who didn't like the setting of Dark Souls.


8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried so hard to like it, I really did.

I am a huge fan of the Souls games, and I was so excited about The Surge to fill the Souls hole in my heart. Since Dark Souls will be the main comparison here, then I will begin with the first boss fight.

If you die to a boss in Dark Souls, 95% of the time it's your own fault. You screwed up somehow. You didn't dodge at the right time, you miscalculated a hit, you didn't read an incoming attack correctly. In some way, it was your error, and the boss just capitalised on it.

Let's talk about the first Boss in The Surge -

The boss itself was fairly easy to predict, however it's sword and stomp attacks were able to damage me even though I was not in range of them. Sometimes I had dodged under the boss and was behind it when I got hit by it's frontal sword attack. Sometimes when it stomped it's right leg, I was on the outside of it's left leg and would still get damaged. Other times this was not the case, it was random.

I also didn't even know how to kill it.. Until I realised that when I hit it, an orange bar underneath it's health bar would fill up. Once that orange bar was full, the boss would fall to the ground to "Recover", opening it up to attack. This was the only sequence of the fight where you could actually deal damage to the boss.

I died the first two times learning how this worked, so the third time I thought that I had it sussed. Except, this time once the orange bar was filled, it didn't do anything. The boss continued to attack me as if nothing happened. No matter how much more damage I inflicted, it just kept going. I was dodging around, waiting for the sequence to happen, but it never did. I spent hours dying over and over to trying beat this boss, without even actually inflicting any damage TO the boss.. Because the only sequence of the fight that allowed me to damage the boss wouldn't happen.

I turned my PC off, had a few hours break (I was seething), and came back.. Beat him the first time. The sequence magically worked as soon as that orange bar filled up.. And I was able to defeat it.

Wait until they fix the bugs, and wait until it's on sale to buy this - It's truly infuriated being killed so unfairly over and over.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf your hesitant to pick up this game, let me first dispel a common misconception, this game is obviously INSPIRED by Dark Souls but it PLAYS much more like a better, way more polished sci-fi Lords of the Fallen than it does DS. Combat is incredibly smooth (the dodge is much more Bloodborne than it is LotF or DS) and weighty, but despite what it looks like in videos, it doesn't feel clunky in the slightest. If you're like me, who picked it up unsure because I wasn't certain if the combat was up to snuff, then rest assured it plays well from the gate. I can't speak for the story, the loot seems kind of limited, the design is preeeetty bland for sci-fi - think Fallout without the rougish, western charm - and the music seems ok so far, but honestly, it's shaping up to be a well designed, intense, fun hack and slash, so if thats what you're looking for, look no further.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't let some of the negative reviews fool you. Saw some that said the game had no story, or no cutscene's or some BS like that. There's a story (planet is in dismal shape, corporation steps in to save the planet with shady methods), and cutscenes that explain it along with audiologs/etc. that fill in more details. Combat is okay, but after playing DS's, it feels a little slugish by comparison. It doesn't feel as visceral either, except when you do the finisher attack. Audio seems a little wierd also. The distance for when a sound starts fading out is very short. You get off a train in the beginning and there's some music playing somewhere, which plays loudly up close, but fades out abrubtly when you move away from it.

Only played an hour, but it seems ok. Certainly very difficult and you can very easily die in two or three hits. Not for the casual player. I'm personally going to wait for a sale.\t\t\t\t\t
