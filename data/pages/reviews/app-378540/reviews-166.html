READ: THIS IS WHAT HAPPENS WHEN YOU PEOPLE CONSTANTLY NAG ABOUT DIFFICULTY ABOVE ALL ELSE. THE GAME FORGETS TO BE A GAME.

I recommend this because, at the end of the day, i dont regret buying it, and i dont wish i could refund it (which in all seriousness is what matters) BUT I cant just leave it at that.

The Surge is in all intents and purposes, much what Lords of the Fallen was (these devs previous game), which was a reasonably competent, but otherwise unremarkable Souslike fanfic. What i mean by that, is that its a game that clearly looks at what a soulslike does, where its complexities lie, and where its charm comes from.... before completely failing to recreate it in its same fashion.

Make no mistake, its not a terrible game. But if it cost anymore, this review would change to a thumbs down mighty quick.

Combat- 7/10: While its by all means a functional combat system with complexity and a risk/reward system, it ends up being pretty shallow when taken at the end of the day. Some points are thus:
-Some weapons are clearly better suited for most situations than others.
-This game is another example of why Poise was such an important thing in Souls. (a stat which would calculate how much impact it would take to stagger you. The way of balacing the huge weight increase of heavy armor)
-It has a lot of different additions, like being able to high dodge and low dodge, block, do different combos and such, but at the end of the day you dont really use the more complex additions... pretty much at all. As a matter of fact the game completely fails to mention you CAN do combos which drastically alter the way the attacks come out (as far as i saw, anyway). So while there is all this fancy stuff, it tends to fall by the wayside when you can be just as effective from simply slapping and stepping back.
-Drones are useful additions, but barely more than a small extra attack and/or a means of pulling an enemy from a distance. Melee drone is helpful for a bit of crowd control, but so far ive found a simple bit of cat and mouse is just as effective...

Map- 8/10:
The map is pretty standard fare. Twisty windy side-paths up-paths down-paths, ladders, holes, secrets, destructibles, shortcuts, etc etc. It does it reasonably well, but theres a few too many dead ends with no value, and a bit much twistiness for it to be a superly competent map design. Half the time i spent trying to work out where the hell i was, in order to find a shortcut to open, simply because the corridors were so damn similar to each other.
Does the job, nothing special.

Graphics- 9/10: Aint nothing too bad. Aint nothing too great. Reasonably detailed, and doesnt lack in a bit of asthetics. Doesnt feel that unique though. Spacey scifi standard corridors for the most part. Does the job, and well, but nothing to write home about.

Characters- 7/10: In all seriousness, the characters in this feel like they play very little actual role. Possibly at one point they were more in depth, but in the end most of them seem to exist more as a person to help and then ask "whats going on" to, before becoming another npc for little more than fetch quests or minor (but useful) rewards. Protagonist bland. Just like LOTF, no real reason for one gender or the other, and extra effort is the only real excuse for not having a choice.
Possibly better off to have just had a faceless Protagonist. He basically is faceless 99% of the time anyway.

Technicals- 7/10: Performance is quite good, despite an odd bit of dip here and there (or so it feels. 970, 16gb ram and 4690k, running on SSD), the load times are fine, no serious graphical oddities or artifacts, and the bare minimum of crashes or stalls, bugs or lockups.
HOWEVER. the hitboxes in this game... are barely passable. Easily the games worst aspect. A number of times youll be hit by attacks youre 100% didnt land according to the models, and other times attacks will land before they should seem to.
Its not a deal breaker, as with a bit of extra caution you can pretty much work around it. But it certainly takes you out of the moment when an attack lands you think shouldnt have. Some people might think this insignificant, but these sort of games (Soulslikes) are all about punishing your mistakes. If your only mistake is assuming the models mean anything, then thats not your fault at all.

Overall- 7.5/10.
Not a bad experience to sink some time into, and for the price you could do far worse (as i recently discovered, thinking "Hey, Homefront:TR cant be THAT bad, and its on special!"), but... you could do far better too. Honestly, id put LOTF above this, but barely. Competent Soulslikes that dont really encourage a second playthrough.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVerdict tldr at bottom

No stat system, just implants to increase hp, stam, and energy. Increase rig core power with currency to increase the amount of implants you can have (both numerical value and the energy they need to work). Also need those slots though if you want hp pots or extra dmg ect. Suppose it's something different, would of prefered a seperate stat system personally. That's just me though, you may like it.

You use weapons more, the proficiency for that weapon type increases (single handed, heavy, single rigged ect). Problem is this discourages experimentation later on. You have been using a weapon you like for first two areas, you find a different one in 3rd. Problem is your preficiency is 1 for the new one and damage for it is ♥♥♥♥, meanwhile profiency is like 16 for the one you usually use. So basicall you have to gimp your damage to bring it on par with the old weap.

Love the targetting specific body parts for upgrades, weapons, and shematics.
Love the setting, levels seem cool and not too tough to work out once you explore a little.
Game is like 5gb or something to dowlnoad, so well done on the compression.

Combat isn't that great.
Find smaller enemies harder to deal with than bosses. Bosses once you figure out their deal can be fun and intesne. Smaller enemies can be annoying, like cyber men. Track like f**k, sometimes randomly go through guard (horizontal or vertical swings). Although should be said that this was whilst trying to farm body parts, otherwise counter kills with the weapon I used just mercd them.
Doesn't feel like stabillity and Impact (stamina drain defense defense and sort of poise mechanics) make much of a difference, as opposed to the actual defense values on armour.

Certain NPC in very stupid spots where they can be harmed by enemies, then dissapear, ruining that questline. (Irina by the way, this pissed me off so much, stopped playing for a bit).
TLDR/Verdict.
I mean, it's a good game, just wait for summer sale though. Muvh prefered DS1/3 as far as action rpg's of this sort go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is honestly a pleasant surprise. I haven't had this much fun in a while! I picked it up today, and I don't want to put it down. I've read some posts in the discussions forum that bother me, though. The dark areas are particularly engaging. You're never sure what you'll come across when you can barely see, even with your rig lights on. It makes me tense, and I love it. I watched 10 videos on The Surge before deciding to buy it. Worth every cent so far. I want to go into my own review now, because this has been an awesome experience so far.

GRAPHICS: Ooooooh man... this game is pretty in terms of the fidelity. WOW. 10/10

GAMEPLAY: I love the manner in which you obtain gear and materials. VERY awesome, gruesome and satisfying. Accessing the additional functions of your exoskeleton is actually an enjoyable undertaking, gathering enough items and currency to upgrade yourself to the point that you get a new toy to play with. Very well done. My only gripe is the timer on your "corpse" to retrieve your Tech Scrap. That's really not okay. 9/10

COMBAT: Oh man. A welcome change to the standard flow of Souls combat. Targeting specific parts to get gear, or targeting unarmoured sections to deal more damage. Dealing more damage has saved my life multiple times so far, and while it doesn't yield much in the way of mats for me, it is kind of sastisfying to drop multiple enemies in a row before decapitating the last one. Very well done and extremely entertaining. 10/10

MOVEMENT: Doesn't even feel clunky. It's actually very fluid and manageable. The dodging feels strange at first, having played the hell out of Souls games prior to this. However, you eventually get used to it and even find new ways to approach enemies using the dodge system and the slide system. Lots of fun to be had. 10/10

STORY: I don't really have much to go on right now, as I've just started. But I more or less get the gist of it. I'll add a score and change this once I've advanced further.

CUSTOMIZATION: While it lacks a custom character creation system, the way you manage gear on your character is actually pretty cool! A weapon slot, head slot, 2 arm slots, 2 leg slots and a body slot make up the system in which you customize your gear/looks (let's face it, this will become the new FashionFrame in no time). I'm not entirely sure if you can get additional exoskeletons, or if those can even BE changed, but so far, I like what I see. Still, the lack of a custom character denies it a full score. 8/10

REPLAYABILITY: I've come across enough information and even asked the forums about NG+, and I was told it was a thing, allowing you to upgrade further as you advance through each NG+. Automatic awesome. 10/10

SOUNDS: The sounds of your rig as you move, the enemies and their moans/battlecries, the clang of weapons on armour, the ripping and tearing of weapons through flesh and the pulsing of electricity are all very enjoyable to listen to. Even the visceral ripping, tearing and snapping as you dismember bodyparts is completely satisfying. Now, I don't know if there are weapons with additional sounds (Chainsaw swords, welding torches or the like), but for now it gets a great score! Subject to change in the future if I find some REALLY cool stuff with awesome sounds. 9/10

MUSIC: There isn't really any music so far. A lot of ambience and a few "triggered" sounds to tell you that you're going into a dangerous area, but hey. I guess I'll find out when I fight a boss. For now, no score here until I hear some actual music.

OVERALL: 9/10

PROS:
-Great, responsive combat controls
-Dark and gritty futuristic setting
-Plenty of weapon types
-Fast-paced combat
-Execution system
-Dismemberment (HELL YEAH!)

CONS:
-No Multiplayer/Co-Op features
-No character creation (male/female)
-Timer on Tech Scrap drop (not cool)
-No range weapons (I get it, non-military facility, but still... guns man...)

WOULD I RECOMMEND THIS GAME?
Hell yes I would. And that little piece of DLC? Worth it. A great extra with a bit of an assist early on. The noise the implant makes is annoying as hell, but it serves its purpose. At least it's not Navi the fairy... Pick this game up if you can. It's awesome!\t\t\t\t\t
