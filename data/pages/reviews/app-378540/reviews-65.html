\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm writing this after finishing the story

Pros
-Fluid combat
-Good graphics
-Good story
-Lot of viable builds
-Just like dark souls, you can replay it and backtrack to areas you couldn't access before.

Cons
-Navigation and backtracking can be pretty annoying without any map, only small prints of maps at random points.

Neutral
-Fairly short game
-To see enemy's health and stability, you need an implant, which takes up a slot from something useful. I personally didn't even bother to use it anyway, because it's only a quality of life thing, but I would have rather it by default rather than as an item.

So as you know The Surge is souls-like game, but set in a different era. I didn't enjoy dark souls as much, because it had much slower type combat, but The Surge felt much more natural to me. Combat is intuitive, enganging and just fun in general. You can try various combat styles and builds, which I find very interesting in this type of games.
On top of that, you have this interesting story you follow along.

You can bank you tech-scraps (like souls) at medbays, so you don't have to carry huge amounts if you need to save up. It's optional though if you want the challenge.
The game is not very long, personally long-enough for me. Took me about 30 hours, but I grinded and backtracked a lot so take it with a grain of salt.

Overall, i definitely recommend play this, even if you aren't souls fan. Game is so much fun to play and I'm trying to speedrun the game again now that I finished it properly.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore any of this, I will say this game was worth playing. It's oddly fun and not fun at the same time.

It's not fun because, is tedious; but I played enough to get all the achievments. I come from a Dark Souls background where I have 1,000s of hours on Console and a little more than a few hundred hours on PC. I enjoy the mechanics in Souls but I don't in this game and I'll chalk it down to one major thing; There's not much variety in how you can counter the enemy.

From people that know Souls, the main way to counter enemies would be to Parry, Block, or Dodge their attacks and have a counter attack ready. You can of course attack first if you don't always want to wait for the enemy to make a move.

However, in Surge, enemies often have an almost instant attack animation among their move-set which can make it difficult to counter unless you simply dodge when you think an attack is coming. Souls has those instant attacks too but you can remain mobile while blocking if you don't want to dodge. In Surge, you have to stand still to block which drains your stamina and can also get you killed depending on the length of the combo so you end up having to dodge anyway.
(Blocking is very situational, dodging is the way to go)

There is no parry mechanic in Surge so that's out of the way.

The most effective methods I found while playing this game was this:
Bosses and Enemies with a lot of health
- Slide attack with One-handed or Twin Rigged weapons
- Use strong combo or swing 1-3 times

Enemies with low health, low stability, or slower attacks
- Slide attack with a Heavy weapon to the head and go to town until the execute

I restricted myself to those attacks on enemies that can 1-3 shot me because you make a mistake and you can end up dying. That being said, this game becomes easy because you just cheese the enemies.

It only remains difficult in any way because there will be no point in the game that you can avoid enemies that don't have at least 1 attack that can 1-3 hit you. That's the only time you'll really die if you have a lot of experience in Souls at least. (Can't speak for other souls-like games)

TLDR;
If you don't mind killing enemies with simplistic combos and dying from occasional 1-3 hit combos then this game is fun. Executes are fun, farming for items isn't based on luck, weapons are fun enough, game looks good, level design is fine (no complaints for me), DLCs have the same pros/cons.\t\t\t\t\t
