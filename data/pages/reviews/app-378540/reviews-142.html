\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat can I say about the surge so far? I like it. I'll be making some comparisons to both Dark Souls and Lords of the Fallen here (The devlopers previous game)

First off let's talk about what they improved over Lords of the Fallen (The developers first push forward in to souls-lke territory) they improved pretty much everything over the community dubbed 'Clunky Souls'. It's no longer clunky that's for sure, combat and just movement in general is smooth and quite fast paced.

Combat has some interesting mechanics. You can't parry like you can in dark souls and you can't move while blocking like you can in Dark Souls and LoTF. When you block you are stationary BUT they did add in a neat little counter mechanic in the form of ducking and jumping. While blocking you can duck under an enemy attack and then counter or you can jump over the enemy attack and counter that way. It still requires the same sort of timing as say a parry in the DS series. Not being able to move while blocking would usually annoy the hell out of me but with those little added mechanics I can deffinately give it a pass.

Rather than rolling like DS/LoTF you have a Bloodborne style of dodging which again, is a wonderful addition and helps combat feel a lot faster (You'll be using this dodging a lot) You can combine these dodges with attacks too that create good combat openers that effectively stagger your enemy, allowing you to get a good couple of hits off before you back away.

Combat is smooth, it's fluid and such a HUGE improvement over their previous entry. It's also pretty damn difficult too. Unlike LoTF, the enemies in 'The Surge' do not screw around and will severely punish you if you make a mistake (This isn't a negative, this is exactly what us souls-like fans want) and that's just the regular enemies, the bosses are tough as nails. In LoTF 90% of the bosses were a joke to be honest but in 'The Surge' the two bosses I have fought so far have given me some real challenge.

For somebody like me who has put a ridiculous amount of hours in to the soulsborne series it's easy to get to grips with the mechanics, you go in to a boss fight knowing what you should look for, what you should pay attention to and such since you're so used to fighting bosses in a souls-like game but even then for me it was still a challenge, I imagine for those not used to souls-like games it would be an even bigger challenge.

The way gear is handled in this game in my opinion is a HUGE improvement over not only LoTF but the souls games as well (Yes that's right, I said it) In the Souls games you can kill up to 50 maybe a little less of the same enemy and cross every part of your body to just -hope- you get an armour or weapon drop, it becomes tedious and crushes your soul (No pun intended)

In The Surge however with the way you can target enemy limbs you are 100% promised at the very least a schamtic to craft that portion of armour and once you have that schematic you can cut off that same limb of the same enemy in order to get the componants to craft/upgrade said piece. Now it is not lost on me how grindy that feels and I don't really like grinding but it is still less of a grind that farming armour/weapon drops in the souls series. It's not based off pure luck in The Surge and you -always- know what you have to work toward.

So how about the world design? It stacks up to the souls series of games in layout but not in design. It's all inter-conected, full of shortcuts to unlock, secrets to find, hidden weapons and implants to find etc.. like you'd expect of any souls-like game but the world doesn't tell a story in design the way that the souls games do. To their credit they gave it one hell of a try and while the world and story elements, NPC that you meet etc.. are quite interesting, it doesn't grip me the way Lordran/Drangleic/Lothric did.

That's not really a bad thing, those are hard world designs to beat.

People have been calling this game a "Cheap Dark Souls" or a "Ripoff" or a "Cash in" but it's really none of those. It takes the souls formula but then does enough of its own thing to make it stand out from the rest in new and interesting ways which is what game design is all about really, improving ideas and concepts for others to then improve in the future and improve our quality of entertainment overall.

This game also runs flawlessly. I have yet to encounter any graphical glitches, any FPS slowdowns or any crashes. It's a very stable game on my system at least.

Now that the praise is out of the way however, I will talk about the issues the game does seem to have. Firstly it does have a few bugs such as not being able to talk to NPC's which will require you to restart the game, not being able to pick up items which again, requires a restart of the game and also buggy enemy AI that sometimes (Although rarely) notices you WAY before the point they should notice you, we're talking through walls and up stairwells here, that's not acceptable.

There was a certain point where I was fighting an enemy at the top of a stairwell, killed him, started walking down the stairwell only to be greeted by five enemies at the bottom all trying to get up the stairs (We're talking a stairwell that goes down six levels here) so I manage to pull agro from five enemies while being six levels above them, that's not acceptable ever.

One other downer is the lack of a fast travel system. Now usually I don't require fast travel, more often than not in my souls runs I will not bother with fast travel but this game severely needs it. It's really difficult to find your way to past areas, often you have to jump through a ♥♥♥♥ load of hoops and if you want to get to a specific area you'll find yourself having to manually travel to and through the previous areas in order to get to where you actually want to be and then when you're done? You have to go back through all the areas you just came. It's very tedious and one of the worst flaws the game has.

All in all though I'd give the game a solid thumbs up. I'm really enjoying it so far and I can see myself enjoying it for a while to come yet. Highly reccomend you pick this up whether you're a fan of souls-like games or just a fan of action/RPG type games in general.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDark Souls gameplay meets Dead Space aesthetic. Intelligent level design. Practically spotless performance. Hopelessly addictive mix-and-match gear system. Combat is fluid and fast-paced but not so much so that it loses its tactical edge.

There are a few minor faux pas; the crucial damage types are needlessly obscured, and the environment is occasionally unreasonable, like when the slashing arm of the robots whose attacks you have to jump and duck are concealed in walls. But these flaws are ultimately minor and didn't injure my enjoyment.

The Surge is a solid action RPG that scratches the Souls-like itch, and while it doesn't reinvent the wheel, its refreshing flair, polish, and pacing earn it a spot near the top of the genre's heap.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hate to say the old "This+This=This", but it really is a great representation of the Dark Souls formula. With Maze like exploration, bonfire-like safe places called Operations/OPS, losing Tech Scrap (souls) when you die, and having to retrieve said scraps, you can easily see the resemblances...BUT NOW YOU HAVE AN EXO SUIT!

Obviously a difficult game, but it does it well. You can only take a handful of hits (maybe less) before you need to inject yourself with some drugs. Par for the course with these types of games. Seemingly more shortcuts back to safety than Darksouls' bonfires, but with less safe places overall, means you can learn the layout of each area pretty easily. Being away from OPS for about 20 minutes and losing your way, only to find a door that leads you right back, is an awesome feeling.

The Combat/Gameplay is great. Trying to dismember body parts to get blueprints or crafting materials is a great way to give more control to the player. Want the weapon or armor this guy has? Focus attacks on these body parts until you can cut it off with a slowmotion, bloody, cinematic camera. Just don't forget to block or dodge enemy attacks, they tend to hurt.

It looks and feels incredible! You can't understand just by looking at screenshots or watching videos, you have to experience it.

Oh! Once you've beaten the game, you can restart with NG+ all the way up to NG+++...I think. 4(?) playthroughs total really adds to the replayability; Or you can just start over from scratch again...and again...and again...

Probably my favorite game of the last few years. If you are on the fence about getting it, and are a fan of Dark Souls, or difficult games in general, do it up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA gem of a game

The Surge is a rather interesting addition into the "BRPG" (Brutal RPG) genre. "Souls-like" is too limiting a term for this new style of ARPG created and made famous by FROM Software's "Souls" series. While The Surge has some familiar mechanics (stamina based combat, no map, a central hub for saving, and a single currency for leveling both your character and gear), it goes beyond with a more subtle combat system which allows for specific targeting and more in depth dodging.

I have bought this game twice... on Steam and for the PS4, and have achieved 100% trophies on both. This speaks volumes for how fun the combat actually is. Were this game yet another random RPG doing the same-old, same-old... I would not have finished it now... 9 times?

Your character's gear is completely fluid... meaning that you may choose any type of armor your wish to wear, and specializing in any type of weapon you choose. You have the ability to install "Implants" which grant you various abilities , such as increased health, health injections (think health pot, or estus flask), health on brutality, increased energy, treasure sense, toxic gas immunity, GF, etc etc etc. Any weapon you find can be leveled up, along with armor.

The setting is a Sci-Fi industrial dystopia... the industrial theme is prevalent throughout as you are working your way through the heart of a multi-national corporation's scientific and technical launch facility.

You play Warren, and Warren is anything you wish he or she to be. I, personally, like to think of my Warren as a STUNNING AND BRAVE transwoman who is also transracial while being hadicapable. So, you want to play as a beautiful woman? Warren identifies as a beautiful woman. You want to play as a wise cracking, 1940's film noir detective? Warren can identify as such. Warren is the tabula rasa of your imagination.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t
