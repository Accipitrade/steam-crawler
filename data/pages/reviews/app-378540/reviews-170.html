\r\n\t\t\t\t\t--EARLY IMPRESSIONS--

Game gets you hooked (or at the very least interested) right from the start. Combat feels good once you get the hang of it, there's a couple different weapons in the beginning area so it lets you try them out and choose what you like. Inclusion of easy-to-use executions (with mods that can alter their effect) has been a great addition. Level design is pretty good so far with a decent use of verticality, but the jump system is a little odd. Seems like one of those great B-moves that gain cult followings, and I'm guessing this will too.

-PROS-
Fluid Combat
Runs well on my PC (dual-core / GTX 1060)
Interesting story so far, hoping it goes somewhere
Crafting & Suit mods add enough variety

-CONS-
Graphics are...dated. Really nice visual effects, but textures, models and geometry are from 2012.
Jumping is very odd (you must sprint+jump to jump vertically)
Seems like finding crafting mats is a crapshoot so far

Recommended? Yes, price point is just right at $45

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeems okay so far. Already beat the first boss on my first try, so it's not exactly as ridiculously difficult as the souls games (at least out the gate). It has difficulty with camera control in cramped areas, which there are PLENTY of so far. Not a huge fan of how linear the stages seem to be. Not a lot of real exploration can be done yet.

The limb ripping mechanic is fun.

The dodge/block system seems iffy at best. Dodging feels awkward in either of the two starting mech suits. Blocking seems mostly useless since even with the biggest - baddest frame, you still lose a metric boatload of stamina from blocking. So it seems exclusively better to always be dodging. Which unfortunately, doesn't feel good. It jerks your screen around and just feels awkward in the limited space where half the time you end up smashing into a wall and losing control of the camera.

Attacks have a satisfying amount of weight to them, but the body-part targeting is nightmarishly annoying to do mid-combat. If you want to switch body targets, you basically have to tap through all of them furiously trying to get to the part you want to target. (Though hitting the head is easy, always seems to be UP. But, hitting any other part is an exercise in frustration. If I'm trying to target the body/chest area, it has an annoying habit of continuing from arm to leg to arm to leg to arm to head to leg to arm. You get the idea.)

So far, I'm a big fan of the premise, but not a big fan of the execution. The environments leave a lot to be desired, in that they're all SUPER TINY and you're in a HUGE MECH SUIT (depending on starting choices). Which is infuriating when you're forced to run past 5 enemies just so you can get a good feel for the tiny amount of space you have to fight them in.

CRAMPED COORIDORES AREN'T SCARY OR HARD, THEY'RE JUST INFURATING. KILL THEM LIKE ESCORT QUESTS, NOBODY LIKES THEM.

The game's sound design suffers from the same problems I had with PREY. Lots of LOUD NOISES FOR SEEMINGLY NO REASON. It's a jump scare tactic, and it's annoying. It's not scary, it's startling. Which I guess is fine, but it's definitely not my cup of tea.

I'm disappointed that the very good combat is being hampered by the very bland environments.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know the constant comparrison to dark souls is annoying, but really its the best way to describe it.

The combat is meaty and tactical, just swinging blindly will get you killed.

You explore the limited open world in great detail because hidden treasure and loot is all over place, or even a shortcut or a audiolog, explaining the world around you.

Ypou level up your character using scrap you find and collect off your enemies, these upgrades allow you to improve your overall capabilities, as well as unlock new modules, and the upgrades also allow you to access new areas of the game as well.

Weapons are different in the way they handle, and can be upgraded and improved, armor can as well, and there is a piece armor system, so you can have different armors on different parts of your body.

There is a proficency system that scales up the more you use certain weapons, this proficency changes the amount of damage you can do, as well as your speed and handling with that class of weapon.

You can get implants that change the way you play, or gives you short boosts, or changes your play style entirely.

Crafting is done by collecting scrap from your enemies and the world around you, and unique crafting components are found on the bodies of your enemies, which you can obtain by weakening a certain body part through combat. If you need a headpiece you need to weaken the head, then you will need to do a execution move so you can cut off that body part, and collect the pieces you need. This is also how you obtain new weapons, by cutting them off of your enemies, or of course finding them in the world.

There are collectable items you can find, so far I have found two, but you have to find them yourself, and usually they come equipped with a challenge before you can collect them.

The difficulty is in learning the combat and the moves your enemies do, if you are a seasoned Souls player you will pick up on it right away, if you are not you are in for a bit of a learning curve.

The theme and atmosphere of the game is pretty great, sometimes things can be predictable, and other times they cant.

Overall its a great Souls-like game, and I am looking forward to more play time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHEY DONE ♥♥♥♥♥♥ IT UP!

Have you ever played Dark Souls? Then The Surge will seem familiar at first. You've got a stamina bar, you've got your light swings, heavy swings, dodgerolling, collecting stuff to upgrade yourself, losing the stuff at death and having to get it later. But as you fight, first signs of trouble start to rear its ugly head. And then that trouble knees you in the gut at first boss.

This game's combat is absolute ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. It does not work as it was intended. The formula of Dark Souls is not that complex - your enemies swing and hit hard, but they are predictable. You dance around them, finding an opening to strike, because should you attack too early or too late - you get hit, you have to commit to your attack. The Surge completely fails at this formula because enemies adjust their attacks perfectly according to your position. The first drones you encounter are not easy to avoid - when they rear up for a charge, they'll track your position till the very end. This is bearable, yet extremely irritating, forcing you to take damage when otherwise it'd be easy to avoid.

And then there's the first boss. I haven't seen anything beyond the first boss, and if all the bosses are like him, I'm not going to. Instead of slowly turning around, which he has an animation for, he just instantly does a half U turn to hit you in the face for half your health if you don't charge right between his legs. His mechanic is also totally busted. You can't damage his health right away - you have to fill up his 'danger' bar or some ♥♥♥♥, after which he'll shoot a barrage of rockets at you. I guess you're supposed to stand under him so he gets hit by his own rockets, and then you can damage his health directly, but this doesn't always work. You can stand right beneath him when he shoots, and he'll still hit you without stunning himself. Then you're just stuck running around and whacking him for zero damage. The most likely outcome is that he'll kill you after you mistime your charge, or get hit where his attack is not supposed to hit you.

This is NOT how you do Dark Souls combat! Dark Souls is difficult due to well-designed enemies, not broken animations and ♥♥♥♥♥♥ hitbox. I advise you skip this one and get Dark Souls 3 instead. Save yourself some brain cells.
