\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this on a whim after reading the few but nice reviews on Steam that said it wasn't what I expected and, indeed, I didn't expect it to be this...weird.

I said 'weird' because there were other things I liked, such as the menu title (it's comparable to PS/Switch), the VA, the character sprites, the BGM, and so on. Even if the background is derived from a real photograph, it is OK as long as the plot is compelling. But the plot is quite dubious in my opinion. The world-building and concept are intriguing, but the story's flow is absurd. The events just...happened one after the other, with no explanation, link, or build-up at all. I ended up on the demon god's route without even realizing it; I assumed I was still in the prologue because how the story was still so linear at the time.

If the romance is there, I cannot feel it because how poor and illogical the writing is. Sadly, my request for a refund is turned down since the playing time exceeds 2 hours. So here I am, writing this review, to give whoever reading this a different perspective to consider before buying it blindly. If you want to try it out, I suggest waiting for a sale or trying out the demo beforehand, because it's not worth it, the art may make it seem so, but the story is seriously just worth comparing with the Otome constantly released on Android/ios.

*Demon god's route - Spoiler ahead*

So, the first time we met, he claimed to have abducted me and insisted on marrying me because he liked me, and I felt like sunshine without ever meeting. Okay, I thought, maybe he'll explain more when he opens up to me. But no, he said the same thing even when I was nearing the end of his story. There's nothing wrong with that reason; it only feels wrong because there's no backup for it. We just met, and all of our exchanges along the way are the MC attempting to figure out his background in order to escape, and even when he does open up, it's as if we're performing a job interview instead of confiding in each other. And throughout that period, everything the MC feels (at least, that's what she thinks) is sympathy for him. If you choose not to kill him, she will suddenly declare "I love you," be willing to die for him, and abandon her former life to run away with him. Yay, what an empty happy ending, without solving or feeling anything, even the Koshimpuk has more personality than the main couple.

*Final thoughts*

I guess I'll just wait for Pewrep's route for the time being, as he has a history with the MC, and I'm hoping that their development will be better than the game imposing events and feelings on us like it did with the demon god's route.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really loved this. The art style is beautiful and the music amazing. The story is well written except a few mistakes in grammar and translation. The yoice actors did a tremendous job here and I loved that this game is fully voiced. It makes it so much better when you can hear the emotions conveyed to the written text.
I can't wait for the full release so I can play through the missing routs.
I new excatly who caused a few bad ends I encountered and decided to go for the demon lord first. I loved him dearly but more like a mother even though I enjoyed his realionship with the MC.
Then it was time for Mr. Fox and man that story had me sobbing. Of course I made a few bad choices on the way, as you do but eventually I reached the best part of this story. The cute foxes living in a shrine of course. And with it the first name I recognized popped up too. Enomoto Takeaki.
At first I was like: "Hey I know this name!", and then Hakuōki came back to me and with it all the names and story endings.
That was my downfall.
I fell deeply in love with the fox god. After wittnessing a fox marriage I was in strange modd, shifting between happy, sad, full of thoughts and kind of moody. I stayed up until 4 in the morning and wrote an essay about the story I wittnessed just to clear my head.
This is not sponsored but I want to give a big thank you to LocaGames for developing such a good game for us to experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been a while since I've felt compelled enough to sit down and write a review, but after finishing the first route in Yukar from the Abyss, I really think more people need to check this baby out. I went in with absolutely no expectations and am now eagerly anticipating the release of the second half of the game next year. It's true that I usually complete a visual novel in its entirety before writing about it, but I feel it's a travesty there's not a single review on the Steam page right now, in ANY LANGUAGE!

First of all, whatever you're expecting this game to be, throw that idea right into the garbage, because you're wrong. Drawing on the myths and legends of Hokkaido, Japan, the story is way stranger than it appears. Sure, there are your stereotypical animal gods, hot anime boys, steamy romantic tension, and a cute little birb companion for good measure, things go right from weird to outright crazy before we start getting flashbacks to the Boshin War. And that's just the first character's route.

Once you're comfortably settled in to the madness, you might notice that the love interests are all fully Japanese voice acted, something we don't get very often with indie titles. The acting itself is pretty good, although I wouldn't consider myself an expert on seiyū. I do wish the other characters were voiced as well, but I imagine a good portion of the game budget went toward the four LI's voice work, since it feels like some of the other areas of the game are a little lean.

Outside of the soundtrack which recycles the same half dozen tunes, the most noticeably deficient aspect of the game would be the character sprites. As it is, only the love interests and a couple of side characters have them, and I feel like there could definitely be a few more for some of the minor named characters. But I feel like I'm nitpicking here. I can't even really complain about the translation, which reads fairly well with only a handful of noticeable typos and misspellings. Considering the game is in Early Access, I'm hopeful some of these issues will be remedied for the final release.

Honestly, I just feel like more eyes need to be on this game. I'll likely update my review once I've finished all of the routes, but for an early access title, I am incredibly impressed. Impressed enough to write my first review in over a year. Impressed enough to actually stream it. If you don't at least have Yukar from the Abyss on your wishlist, you should. And if you already have it wishlisted, buy it!
