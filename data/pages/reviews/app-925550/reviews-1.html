Great physics and many small annoyances

CRC Extreme shines in what matters most: the driving is fun!
It hits a sweet spot between simulation and arcade. In comparison to so many contemporary racing games with a subpar driving feeling, the physics of CRC are the reason why I recommend this game.

But, beware: There are no licensed cars (the reason why this game can still be bought), there are very few tracks and - what is the most bizarre oversight of the developers - you can't create custom races: There is a quickrace option, but that allows just to replay races from the career, including the same restrictions regarding tuning. So, for example, if you want to play a race on a certain track with your fully tuned buggy, this is most likely not possible.

Additionally, the game misses to tell you what the settings of the car parts actually do. the last races of the career are on tarmac race tracks and accordingly, I changed the settings to "race track" preset - because that seemed to be the right thing to do. Actually this makes the handling extremely stiff and sets your gears to very long - that leads to slow acceleration. Basically, the driving is more fun, the closer you get to the "offroad" settings. At least for me.

Anyway, if you have a soft spot for offroad racing and great suspension physics - give this game a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom the same great devs that brought us the "Street Legal" games, comes another excellent racer that racing game fans should play. This was a game I always wanted to try as a kid, especially after seeing the screenshots for it in a computer magazine that my older brother had. Finally playing it now, I was blown away on how it still holds up even after 17 years on the market. This game combines different types of racing disciplines (circuit and off-road), with the off-road disciplines taking center stage and offering you to drive multiple types of vehicles, ranging from rally cars, buggies, racing trucks, sports cars and many more on different types of environments.

PROS
+ To begin with, this is the definitive version to play on modern hardware. The steam release comes with lots of improvements and patches to make it playable on a PC running Windows 10. The game performs excellent, and it looks insanely good when playing it on 1080p.
+ Environments are varied and huge, featuring lots of different tracks for each one. Another great feature is the ability to drive around in each environment with the free ride mode giving you lots of freedom to cruise, or goof around if you feel like it.
+ Excellent handling and physics model. Each vehicle type feels great to control, even with a keyboard. Each vehicle is responsive and fun to drive, and the environments and the road surfaces affect each vehicle type differently. Things like jumps and most notably collisions feel impactful with proper weight.
+ Varied and well-detailed car models. Each car model is based off of a different racing discipline, featuring trucks, rally cars, sports cars, touring cars, buggies, track day cars and much more..., each complete with a very detailed interior, which feels great to drive in. All vehicles come with different upgrades, like aero and body kits which look great when applied on a vehicle.
+ Detailed damage modelling. The damage model not only affects how your car looks, but it also impacts your car's performance. Each vehicle has a great deformation model, so when you smash your car too much, you can see parts bending and flying off the vehicles, engines smoking depending on how many hits they took and wheels flying off depending on how much you've put them through.
+ Everything in the game can be customized to suit your preferences.
+ A very simple, but very well made upgrade system for your vehicle, with each part giving you enough options to tune your vehicle for whatever type of race you want to do.
+ Fun career mode, which will give you lots of hours of enjoyment. While some races may be long in the beginning and even later on, they aren't too long to make them feel like a chore to do.
+ A neat little feature is the ability to add your own music in the game (must be an MP3 format tho). Simply drag the files into the necessary folders and you're set.


CONS
- The AI can be huge c*nts when you are playing on the harder difficulties, with the most ♥♥♥♥ move being them trying to spin you out the moment you make the slightest contact with their cars.
- Resets can sometimes set you back really far away from where you activated them which may become annoying the more you use it.

10/10 - Would turn my car into a crushed cola can when trying to smash buggies again\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start by saying that I absolutely LOVE this game. I've played this game quite a few times through the years but never fully finished it. Played it with a keyboard, Xbox controller and more recently my Fanatec wheel. This game is amazing with a wheel, the FFB effects are spot on, it just feels right and I love it.

My two biggest complaints with this game are:
Balls hard cheating AI on tougher difficulties.
Lack of content.

Career mode looks daunting, but in all honestly it's not. This game can be VERY punishing, one small slip up and the AI will pound you into the ground without mercy or lube and that's where the length comes from. It's a pretty short game but mastering the vehicles, hitting the perfect tune for each track and mastering every style of each track is what keeps me coming back, aside from the amazing handling system.

There are only a handful of tracks and cars. The tracks will be split up into different lap routes and those same routes but in reverse. This is a bummer, I really wish there was more content as II absolutely LOVE the way this game plays and am left wanting more when I've blasted through all the tracks again and again.

CRC also has one of the best physics systems period for vehicle handling and dynamics, even the way the gear boxes work, how LSD's vs Open Diffs work, when driving the truck or buggy, if one of your drive wheels comes off the ground the wheel in the air will spin much faster and allow your RPM to come up, something you will experience in real life with an open diff but something very rarely explored in video games except for some off-road games where locking, welded and LS diffs are extremely important, but very rarely seen in a racing game. FFB effects like rough terrain, landing from a jump, bumps in the road and even velocity tightening where your steering gets tighter at higher speeds when you're trying to turn it beyond a certain point, ie. your tires start losing traction because you're turning too tight for the given conditions.

There's just something about Invictus games that always feels great, from 1NSANE and CRC to LASR and PT.

PT is coming to Steam soon and I can't wait. We need 1NSANE and maybe a follow up to CRC or 1NSANE.

CRC gets a big fat recommendation from me.\t\t\t\t\t
