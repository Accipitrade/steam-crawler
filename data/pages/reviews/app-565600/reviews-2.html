Short version: 80%
The first Christmas Stories has plenty of great hidden object scenes and an interesting take on the old Nutcracker story. There are some pacing issues, but the wonderful scenery and an interesting bonus chapter hide most of these problems.

Long version:
Hoffman’s classic Christmas tale, the Nutcracker has popped up in various forms over the past 200 years, so this hidden object game version was just a question of time. It is a rather loose adaptation though, where we play as a nameless stranger who tries to save both the Nutcracker and his princess from the evil Mouse King and his minions, because… I guess he had nothing else to do.

The game has an interesting mechanic in its helper system. Unlike in most HOGs, where we sometimes keep a pet, here we meet several critters who end up in our inventory as reusable handy “objects”, giving a new kind of depth to some of the adventure game bits, often to the game’s advantage.

Other than that, the first half offers a nice and tight pace with beautiful hidden object scenes and a comfortable level of point ‘n’ click elements. The second half is more problematic, as backtracking starts to become an issue, but at least the ending and the bonus chapter make up for it.\t\t\t\t\t
