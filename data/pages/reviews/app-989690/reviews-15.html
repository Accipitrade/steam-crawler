\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review will be updated as I play the game more.
Currently I'm still going through the mini campaign. Which is more or less a tutorial for the main mode of the game 'unconquered'.

The game for the current price point is okay to good.
The flavor of Conan is present though not omnipresent.
The game feels like a mixture between horde mode / tower defence and RTS combat in Warcraft 3 / Age of Empires.
For the sake of quickness labourers etc are not present in the game.
A necessary sacrifice for the gamestyle I think. But a definite minus.

The hero one controls levels, unlocks an ability can go out to loot resources, kill monsters/monster dens and find artifacts.
The hero plays a big role especially early on in the defence of the settlement.
As a solitaire experience the current state of the game is 'okay'.
Entertaining for people that like Conan and RTS's but nothing that shocking.

HOWEVER... from let's plays and just playing the game I deduce for now that Co-Op the big selling point of the game will be where the main fun is at.
It removes the stress of multitasking allowing more interesting micromanagement and more focussed atention on an exploring hero.

So far I get the impression the game could use some 'scale' modifications.
I'd like a bigger baseline build up much faster for the player but more late game waves.
Up the scale of the warfare and allow the player to field more goodies. The game seems to push one at lingering with low tier units and buildings far too long due to the scarcity of gold. Definitely something needing a change.

This is the kind of game I play casually so it will be a while before I return to updating the review.
at a sale definitely worth picking up.
If you're a big Conan/RTS fan and need some co-op certainly worth picking up full price.

I suspect the game does need one or two extra major patch to get where it needs to be content wise.
The latest patch was well appreciated, adding more missions, an extra hero (Belit, THE quintessential female counterpart for Conan)
If the devs don't give up on this one this could become a very very good game!
I'm giving it currently a down mark purely due to the need for finetuning and patching for it to pleasant.\t\t\t\t\t

Conan Unconquered breathes new life to the genre!

Having played a lot of They are Billions (TAB), I saw this upon release, and had decided it was a little to pricey for me, but the recent sale for Australian $19.56 was a steal and well worth it, so a friend and I buy it and install it. We started our first match on standard, only some of our experience from TAB in mind and we begin playing and were none the less overwhelmed by the different unit types in the game (although playing Conan sure helped relate with the environment and unit types anyway) and we lost on wave 17/25, not a bad attempt right.
I play the campaign the next day and slowly learn things from fresh, how each unit works, how the poisons, fire, etc will cause you to lose an army if you don't watch them and so on and learnt some tricks myself.
So my friend and I play again on the third day, our first attempt I expanded way to far looking for choke points and I let open the 13th wave into our base and it was all down hill from there, so we learnt to target catapults and battering rams at all costs, and we behind our third attempt, which was a two hour match of both communicating how to balance our resources and why we both must be building houses and one of us makes troops while the other focuses on research, and by the 20th wave we were booming, our walls were double layered and towers all over, we learnt where the choke points were strongest and weakest and we had a solid strategy and some really exciting moments during waves, this game really captured everything I wanted to see in a game hybrid between a tower defence and a survival and I highly recommend it to others!

The best features:
  • You get to choose from distinct heroes to accompany your playstyle, I love the wizard Kalanthes as his attacks do AoE splash and he has an ability to reduce damage and remove disease, fire, poison. This also means you can decide if you want early troops productions or not and you can run the hero around fighting creeps on the map like in Warcraft 3 to level up
  • Guardians on the map are elite units that provide for a challenge and drop relics which can provide your hero with passive or activated spells for the match.
  • The developers are fantastic and have a really good record behind them, so you can trust them to keep dropping excellent content
  • Graphics are really good and immersive to the Conan universe, again, this was really well done and deserves gratitude
  • Fully Co-Op game that anyone who has played TAB, would agree is what gamer's always want and need in these types of games, the game scales down the waves for SOLO play too, so don't stress about that
  • Feats provide permanent game progression, for example for having built a total of 20 javelin units across all my matches, online and campaign included; my units now have 10% more maximum HP, and these bonuses go for every unit and even building in the game, and is based on playing the game, such as having more starting gold every match based on surviving waves in the game, so at 200 waves overall you will have +150 gold from now on to start with, amazing idea.

What needs more work:
  • The game needs optimisation for the later waves, as there is a small evident amount of the engine slowing down when the waves start coming in; keep in mind that is on ULTRA for both my friend and I playing with really good PCs so this might be worse off if you are not running recommended hardware
  • Hopefully more content for co-op and even more heroes; maybe some sort of progression for your hero that can slowly add traits as you play them more and level them up permanently to start with different skills or something

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary: This game is ridiculously hard, frustrating as hell, but somehow manages to be fun. Most players will likely never win a game. Ultimately I'm giving negative because this unpolished but neat game lacks workshop capability which is sorely needed to smooth over the many rough edges and expand on underdeveloped mechanics. Unrelated, but the screenshots of this game are completely fantistical, your bases will not and cannot have housing spread out prettily and 50 archers guarding the walls on foot. They are artist mockups and nothing more.


I beat my first coop game on normal difficulty setting after 18 hours (each game takes 2 hours if you win, often 1-1.5 if you die). And after that journey I feel it's time to weigh in on what I went through.

The coop element is extremely well done, it's not just two people doing their RTS stuff side by side, it takes constant communication to plan and execute base design due to how resources get spread and locked to each player. Double edged sword, you can't cover for a weaker player at all. They will get you killed. That said, the quality of the coop experience is 100% the best feature of this game.

The controls are a bit clunky. Mostly lacking polish. The two big frustrations are exiting towers with heros in general, or exiting coop controlled towers with your units you put in there.

Here's the doozy though, how the difficulty works. I'm a man in favour of difficult games, a good old toilet sandpapering as it were. And this game is undeniably brutal. I don't think it's physically possible to win on higher difficulty settings. The big problem with it though is that the difficulty feels like ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. The biggest threats to your base are just ones the game doesn't give you any real means of dealing with.

Economy:

While this is difficult, I don't feel it's particularly unfair. It requires a lot of multi tasking to keep an eye on your gold income vs expenditures on defense. We had survived to wave 24 with nothing special, but in our winning game we looted a specific trinket that just produces absolutely ridiculous amounts of gold compared to what your base can generate otherwise. It's so unbalanced it's likely to be basically mandatory for a successful game.

Fire:

The low level fire javelin enemy ai are hilariously too good. They will set one building on fire per shot, and pick a new building in range the next shot. Not noticing one for a mere 10 seconds will have your entire wall ablaze. An enterprising young conan might spy stone walls and this this is the solution to their woes. But that doesn't stop a determined pointy boy, and your proud stone walls will also merrily be set ablaze shortly after. A very high end wall upgrade supposedly fixes this, but it's so far down the tech tree that you will be dealing with your stone walls burning down for a long time. And although units can fight fire, their AI is far more scatterbrained than the enemy setting things alight. They will run after the smallest excuse of an enemy, through flame and disease, at the slightest provocation as your base burns down. Fire also spreads quite rapidly, and trying to put it out can feel like the world's worst game of musical chairs. A firefighting dedicated unit would be much appreciated, but the best the game has to offer is a rare trinket that is basically a magical fire extinguisher.

Siege Equipment:

You can't outrange their catapults, they devastate your walls. IF they come in range, they have such a beefy health pool that you're still going to suffer badly. Battering rams are even worse, they one shot most buildings, and rarely two shot something particularly beefy. And yet again, so much health it's basically impossible to stop them.

Disease:

Honestly this mechanic is just annoying. Corpses emit clouds that slow your units to a crawl unless they get healed. This starts literally the first wave you get, and the answer (if it even works sufficiently, the descriptions don't give me hope) lies at the end of the magic tech tree.

Bosses:

Bosses seem like a cool mechanic, but again it just feels... unfair to the spirit of the game. The final wave, expecting an epic siege, was just a bunch of catapults and rams. Quickly and thoroughly dispatched, our outer walls still looked like swiss cheese. And then the final boss strolled in one shotting towers. We were only able to pull off a victory because he happily chewed on our housing district for a solid two minutes while we beat on him. The biggest threat wasn't epic waves of soldiers, or even the pile of anti-structure machinery, but it was an old dude with a staff who wouldn't die despite 2 heros and 15 archers beating the snot out of him for 3 minutes. The earlier bosses were a much scaled down but similar thing. Blatently ignoring/nuking defenses while being a punching bag with no particular answer to them. This is a defense RTS with no real way to deal with bosses other than killing off his soldiers and then beating on him while hoping he doesn't kill too many important buildings.

Conclusion:

The game offers many challenges, but never gives you satisfying solutions that are within realistic reach. Base design is hideous due to simply stacking 2, 3, 4 layers of walls after each fight just to slow the enemy down. Building flags need ugly mini walls around them to prevent getting instantly popped if a single melee gets through your walls (these must be rebuilt before you can start patching up the walls, which eats precious time from the seconds you have until the next wave). Much of the higher tiers of tech is unreachable in most games due to resources simply not being within realistic building range.

This leads to the game feeling very unfulfilling. The feats, a progress benefit of sorts between games, are also incredibly uninspired and practically useless. 90% of them are bonus 10-30% health on buildings/units which just doesn't answer the problems the game presents.

Without supporting modding to fix the gaping flaws, I just can't recommend it despite the very reasonable price tag.\t\t\t\t\t
