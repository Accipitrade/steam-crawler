\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game has the Conan art style, and unfortunately some game crashing/breaking bugs... with it being a read the controls fest to figure out how to repair structures... Lots of potential... despite still receiving updates in 2023 according to steamdb... a game breaking bug on wave 5 of the first mission is very disappointing and makes me consider returning the game over continuing to play it... as if singleplayer has a game breaking bug this early... imagine how bad it might be co-op.

I want to recommended it and do as I suspect I can look past the bug singleplayer... the game autosaves often enough but I'd image if one I bought it for co-op and experienced such game breaking bugs, I would grow tired of it and recommend against it.

This plays like a CompStomp/Art-of-Defense (AOD)... ironically there's Riftbreaker and another game playing homage to AOD with its title Age of Darkness. This is not so Brutal as that on the first level at least... but it's the same concept. Riftbreaker does it best but the co-op option here is enticing and the human vs bug enemies, such as spiders scaling down otherwise impassable terrain brings some welcome strategy and per some gameplay videos it looks like it ramps up and gets intense...

As I type this I realize I'd rather just go play some more of those other games than this as I haven't crashed in those games... so I guess even at ~$7 I cannot warrant this game... condolences to the Westwood veterans whom are with the developer... please make the controls tighter and on extended hover of the buttons to perform said actions show what the buttons are. I didn't check, but overlapping bindings would be nice... I did check and being able too have multiple bindings on a single command would be nice too. You all defined modern RTS controls. I'm going to chalk this one up as a quick keep the studio afloat money grab, and give it a pass for your games to come unless this game gets bundled or ends up in my library some other way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHm, it is an RTS game, sure enough. But why does everything take so long?

I like the Conan Universe, which I sadly do not know nearly enough about. Sadly this game, as special as it is with its economy system, it does not tell enough it.

This is, of course, moved to a nicely made comic in the main menu, but is it enough?

No cutscenes, no dialogs, every mission is the same formula: Build a base, raise some walls, and defend against waves of attackers. The attackers thereby only consisting of... maybe 12 different kinds of units. Not much and since you have a lot of walls, towers and choke points, each unit does not need anything as big strategy. Just tank it with your walls. And shoot it with arrows.

I tried different kinds of units, but only archers, of any kind really made a difference. The melee fighters are basically dead meat and cavalry only matters in larger numbers and on large rhinos. The main reason why you are not encouraged to is the amount of gold you are getting. You really need a big city to sustain it all and that takes time. And the time is not given, since you are only getting ressources every 24 seconds or so.

Well I could go on. But I did get to enjoy some of it since I love RTS and hero based combat. It was fun to explore the build up and find out how things worked.
But since a single mission takes over an hour and you rarely do anything it can be quite boring after a while.

The graphics and music are fine and exciting. I did not encounter any bugs or glitches and the models are nicely designed, fitting for the universe.

Finished the main game and the Belit's Fury DLC in a little over 20 hours on easy mode, since hard is really that. Hard.\t\t\t\t\t
