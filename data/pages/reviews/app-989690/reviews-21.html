Great base idea but it feels like its in early access, could have done with going through it actually.

Lots of small little issues end up making it more annoying then fun in single player but there is a bit of fun to be had in co-op which is something TAB doesn't offer (yet?).

Some of the issues are the pathing of units is beyond bad, overall graphics are pretty awful but i can forgive this with good gameplay. Balancing is wack, too many units and buildings cost way to much upkeep so you end up just using loads of low tier units and buildings instead because they're not really that much different. Only 2 Heroes unless you pay for the deluxe edition to unlock a third. Heroes are very basic, as in one active ability and a few more that can be picked up if you're lucky enough to get an artifact (probably not going to happen for most players).

If you love defence RTS games you'll probably get some fun out of this, if you have a buddy then it's potentially worth it but look at some gameplay first. Otherwise wait for patches if they come or a deep sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBLUF: This is a resource-management tower defense game with SOME RTS and RPG elements. DO NOT buy this game if you are expecting anything like Star Wars: Empire at War, Age of Empires, Total War, or any Conan game. Poorly balanced and lacking of any real RTS features.
I pre-ordered the deluxe edition of this game just before launch having heard about only by chance in an add on Facebook. I purchased because I am and have always been a huge fan of Robert E Howard and the Conan universe as a whole. Going into this game I was expecting something similar to Star Wars: Empire at War and Age of Empires. I also did not expect any spectacular visuals. What I got was not only disappointing but downright sad.
This game is VERY poorly balanced, as if they made little or no attempt to playtest it before release. Gathering resources is near impossible and the upkeep and initial costs of all units and buildings are outrageous in comparison. The game encourages you to explore the map for resources but you won't have time to do it with your 1 hero unit between waves because this actually a real-time tower defense game NOT a true RTS.
I have not tried the multiplayer yet as I cannot convince ANY of my friends or colleagues to invest in this game in its current state. But it SEEMS is as if the game gets a bit easier and balanced when you have at least one other friend to play with.
Personally, I do regret spending full-price for this game and wish I had waited till it was polished or to see it flop (which if they don't balance it and add some additional features soon, it will).
I will come back to this review and game periodically to see if it improves but honestly, I am not expecting great things from this poor excuse for a game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReview from a non-shill, someone who bought a 2-pack with no one to play with.

At first I really loved this game, reminds me of Age of Empires when you go full defensive and wait for the NPC's to attack.

Here are the dealbreakers for me.
-$10 DLC for the ranged hero on day 1.
-Farms don't give food per round, but rather a 1 time sum that you may use. Rebuilding destroyed farms does not provide food. What is on the map is all of the food you will get, and it's outside of your base.
-There's a 'pylon-esque' style of expansion similar to the Protoss in SC2 that utterly tanks the pace and play of the game; a weak and expensive structure that dies in a few hits.
-The construction paths are a mess when you take into account the upkeep costs on building pretty much anything. Everything has a gold upkeep which deducts gold every income interval. Want to build another structure that generates gold? you'll need food. It's easy to understand why building leaves a lot to be desired when there's a finite amount of food.
-The hero skills felt like an after-thought to help differentiate them from standard units with little to no real benefit in a strategy defense but rather meant to kill creeps on the map, which is arguably not worth the time and effort.
-I've had to restart the levels enough to know that there is no random spawn for anything. Maybe the resource drops from creep spawn locations is random, but that's about it.

To sum up why I don't recommend this game, imagine this;
Your mine generates 5 gold per tick, yet building it costs 2 food to build.
You have 10 food.
You build 4 mines (60g each) to generate a combined 20 gold per tick
You build another structure that costs 350 gold and some more food, with an upkeep 20 gold per tick.
That structure really cost you 10 food, 350g, and 20g per tick.
This is 1 structure.
Go to build something else, need to find a farm for that 1 time food surplus
Find the farm
Build your building
Little food left for troops.

This is essentially wave 2, after you've used up the food they start you with.

I really wanted to like this game but I feel like the version of this that was laid out on a storyboard or game model didn't translate very well to the actual game. My rating would easily be a 9/10 if there was a PVP element where you increased your income by sending creeps to your opponent, I realize that's a different game but this one felt pretty shallow with a tedious resource system meant to murk up the shallow pool so we don't see the bottom as quick.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played enough by now to call this a slow-burn, good game. I'm convinced that if you put 10+ hours into the game, most people will like it. And I'd venture to say people who don't like it, mostly spent far less time in this game which makes sense. Games are more fun when you feel you are doing them well. This game simply takes longer to get to that point than most other games.

My first 5 or so hours in this game were okay but it did not hook me. I knew the game was hard based on reviews. I wasn't scared of that. But thankfully there's a difficulty meter. Play this game on easy at first. 1/2 the fun is exploring the skill tree to see what all you can build. Well if you're playing this game on normal and not experienced at it yet, you won't have the opportunity to explore any of the cool stuff.

Pros:
- Music
- Graphics
- Gameplay
- In-depth tech tree makes the game deep
- Coop is very cool
- The Challange code feature is pretty great also
- It's an RTS that isn't multiplayer dependant (which is so many RTSs that are dead a month after release and nobody is playing it anymore) being largely a single player game. This is a huge plus for me as this game will be fun now or a year or two down the road should I take a break from it and want to come back.

Cons:
- No campaign story. I think it would have added a lot to this game
- Each Hero, as far as I can tell, only has 1 special ability after you level them up. I think each hero should at least have 2 or more to keep things interesting.
- Tech Tree and/or Resources (in short learning the game) has a bit of a learning curve. I listed that as a positive but it can also be a negative given it will take time to learn this game. Impatient people will be turned off by this.

But I really like this game and think it's well worth someone's time.

Quick Beginner Tips:
- Play on easy. No shame in that.
- Check the skill tree. Work your way to Marketplace ASAP. You can sell off your excess resources.
- Your 1st few waves your hero can take care of themselves. Eco up during that time vs spending it on an army. Early on you should build only hunter shacks (for food) and Hovels (for money. Which also cost food. Thus the need for hunter shacks). You want to keep building Hovels until you're at least +200 in money.
- As you advance to later levels, the key to victory is not hiding behind your walls. Battering rams will go through those like a hot knife through butter. You need to have powerful units (Calvery on Rhinos for example) in several small teams, because you'll be attacked from several locations, to go target those wall destroying units and fight them out in the field. Then you can retreat behind the walls once you've taken those out, heal up and let your towers go to work on the rest. Spacebar (pause time) is your friend to coordinate all this. #Victory\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt it's core, Conan unconquered is a fun stronghold-like defense oriented RTS.

The good: It does a lot of the core mechanics right. The maps are fun and varied. Graphics, music, and production quality are on par with other games from Petroglyph, such as Grey Goo. The economy encourages base expansion and ensures that there's always plenty to keep you busy between waves.

The bad: The campaign is very short for a AAA game. With only 5 missions, many players will complete it in 8-12 hours or less. The campaign makes no effort to tell a story. While the game is augmented with a (fantastic) comic, the presentation is disjointed. The campaign is so short, in fact, that I didn't have an opportunity to try out much of the late game tech tree, which feels unsatisfying.\t\t\t\t\t
