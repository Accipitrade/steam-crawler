\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally good for the price.

Visual quality makes the world look very alive, the soundtrack fits the theme very well (though a lot of it is recycled from Age Of Conan and Conan Exiles, still good stuff though!), and the gameplay is EXTREMELY challenging. A single wrong decision when building your fort can be the difference between victory or defeat, and every move you make has some weight behind it.

So far I've only encountered one bug that disconnected me from my own game after reloading a save (rather odd that one), but other than that the game runs very smoothly.

Would certainly recommend to fans of Conan, or those who enjoy They Are Billions that want something with more challenge.\t\t\t\t\t

You would think that the godfathers from Westwood could not fail. But... Conan Unconquered has satisfying and challenging combat, an interesting tech tree, nice character and art design, a very good soundtrack, voices, and ambient sounds... but all of it is ruined by a too short single campaign, and a poorly designed economy that requires insane amounts of gold to unlock even the simplest tech. Also, this world of Conan feels empty. It needs people wandering around working and living, as that would make the game more immersive; and I would feel more heroic protecting people and not just resources. My advice: the developers should unlock Conan Unconquered for the modders to fix the issues and expand the game experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe people giving bad reviews don't seem to be putting any time into it, so I really don't understand where they're coming from. I'm really enjoying it, and have only had one crash, at the very beginning, otherwise totally stable. There is a performance drop once a lot of buildings/units are down, but only seems to happen when I'm in build mode; combat has been smooth regardless of objects in the world.

I just really like wave/hero defense stuff, and even though there's not a lot to choose from in the genre, I think Conan Unconquered has a lot to offer. The upkeep system really makes you consider what's most important, and the tech tree has some good depth; there seem to be plenty of interesting strategies, and micro isn't 100% required, but definitely rewarded.

Also, the in-game comic is really good so far, btw (only on Chapter 4). The price is not too much to ask -- if you're patient, by all means pick it up on sale, but I'm happy with launch price/game all around!

Update: Devs have continued supporting / patching / updating the game, and it's made things even better. Still very pleased with this purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

  • Challenging
  • Pause Action
  • 0 bug issues (so far)
  • Complex and a lot involved
  • Great survival RTS
  • Feat system to give you something to strive for (helps with long term longevity)
  • Comic is pretty fun
  • Runs great on my system (which is high end, for what that's worth to you)

Cons:
  • Hero system isn't fleshed out, would really like to see this expanded

    If they had even half of something like what you see in the Spellforce series, I would be thrilled.

  • No mod support (major con for me)

    Longevity of the game is hampered without community support.

  • Feels a bit..."sluggish" to me. Would like to see unit speed moved to 1.10x or even 1.25x

  • Unsure of longevity overall.

    As always, I hope the developers (Petroglyph) have some future plans for additional content, but no plans at all have been revealed. Just that some things are sales dependent (makes sense).
