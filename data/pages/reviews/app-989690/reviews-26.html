\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like They Are Billions (TAB) and I'm a sucker for the Conan universe. The concept of a survival RTS resonates with me. So, when I found out that the devs from my all time favorite RTS, C&C, were making a Conan survival RTS inspired by TAB I was super excited. However, I'm sad to say this game has some rough edges. It just doesn't feel right. The unit animations and movements are oddly slow, the scale of units to buildings is way off, the graphics are dated (especially the units). I'd like to see more options for the pace of the waves and better randomization/variation on the maps.

There are some great new concepts and mechanics; like disease, fire, healing shrines, titans, mobs that can climb over or around walls. The use of the Conan age technology/lore for buildings, upgrades, units, and skills are well thought out. These all add new mechanics and challenges to the genre.

I find the economy of this game very difficult and slow mostly because of the limited resources close by when starting a new match. By the time you get your economy going it's already several waves in and you're still using basic units and constantly playing catch up. Maybe this is by design and maybe I just need more practice.

I've seen people complain that the some of the music, sounds, and art are from Conan Exiles and AoC which I don't mind at all. I think this adds continuity to the universe.

I'm not one to complain about DLC and I typically buy "deluxe" versions of games etc. because I don't mind spending a little extra money to support the devs of the games I like. Games haven't changed a lot in price since the 80s and they are far and away more complicated and cost much more to make. So, spending $10 more for some extra stuff doesn't bother me; especially when the game is only $30 to start with.

All-in-all it's a good game, it needs some work, has great potential, an RTS in the Conan universe is great. I hope they have a relatively long road of tweaks, patches, and content to deliver to turn this good game into a great game. If you like RTS games and you like Conan then this is a must try.

For whatever reason the Conan franchise has been under appreciated in the world of fantasy. I'm very happy to see more games and media breathing new life into it.

7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm really enjoying the game. To me it's like They Are Billions, but with more variety to it. There's quite a few different enemies, more different units to build, there are heroes and a bigger research tree than in TAB as well as more different mechanics like plague, necromancy, sandstorms and fire. The setting appeals to me much more and the animations are brutal.

Replayability is pretty good as well with randomly generated maps based on your preferences. It also allows you to set highscores that you can send to your friends to try and beat. The only gripe I have so far is that there are only 2 default heroes; Conan and a pirate lady that I had never heard of. If the devs are reading this, know that Red Sonja will be an insta-buy for me!

Additional random things I like:
* Your units are cheering when you select them, despite being in the Conan world, which generally means a quick and brutal death for anyone wielding any kind of weapon
* Music from Conan Exiles, brings me back
* There's a comic you can unlock by advancing through the campaign
* Waves are varied
* The units (both the enemy and your own) are all drawn form distinct Conan cultures.\t\t\t\t\t

This game is NOT worth the price as of right now and its even disgusting to see them putting 1 hero behind a paid wall of 10 euro when the came already is not worth the full price.

What I expected from this game was managing workers (thralls), give them difference tasks of resource collection, a decent campaign with story, a map with regions you conquer one by one untill you reach the end (Red Alert 1 map progression)


Wait untill more content comes that can justify the price, wait for 50%+ off sales or stay away.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game, very enjoyable coop. A ton of hours put into it already and still barely scratched the surface of all the things you can build in the tech tree. Challenging but possible if you have any RTS experience.

Few things that could improve; The repair cost to repair walls is significantly more then just "Razing" and re-building them. Seems that should be altered.. The Gold upkeep seems very high for a lot of the buildings, making it difficult to get to the highest Tier or tech, while enjoying any of the late game stuff more then just the last 2 waves. Ai is a little faulty, especially with controlled ranged units always taking the longest path to kill something over walls; instead of running up to the wall to shoot over it...

Just a few things that could improve the game. Maybe some level of Hero progression outside games to have something to work towards. Like leveling the hero to unlock more usable or passive abilities for future games. I feel the game will get repetitive quick without some kind of progression aspect to keep people engaged. Like 3 skill trees for Building, Hero, Units. As you level up you can distribute points to improve a specific areas.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCurrently 25 waves easy mode is my upper limit.

The game is pretty unforgiving of mistakes in resource allocations, carelessly lost units and inappropriate timing on research and new buildings.

Never underestimate the need for gold so keep your food and thrall housing up. You need to constantly monitor your upkeep fees to make sure you are not eating too heavily into your gold base as you progress through the game. Its very easy to find you are not producing enough gold for more advanced buildings and units because you over invested early in the game. Even command flags cost -3 gold upkeep.

Don't get too mad with the research and advanced buildings early into the game and when you do go down that path, make sure you have enough timber mills, quarries initially, then mines as you move into more advanced infantry units. You also eventually want at least 2 stockyards.

Command centres are your friend, particularly if you don't want to overstretch yourself by expanding your territory too much. and unnecessarily.

You will want to get 3 the artifacts from the 3 boss mobs quickly. As soon as you have one of your hero's and say 8 to 12 javelin units, you need to start attacking them. There is significant differences in difficultly between the 3.

When a dust storm (or whatever they are) comes through, there are gold and resource deposits scattered around your territory for you to collect. That's free money so get that and try to have a market built by the time the first one arrives so you can sell excess resources.

You should research economics and engineering before anything else and your first advanced buildings need to be the market and stockyard. You will want engineering reasonably quickly so you can get stone defenses. Without it, you will be forever putting out fires on your wooden wall structures and even with it, putting out fires and micromanaging that after invasion is a pain. Sometimes its easier to destroy sections of wall and rebuild.

You may want to consider leaving inner walls as you expand our territory, particularly any towers, even wooden ones so you can potentially bring down flying critters attacking your advanced buildings which can be very, very costly.

Its relatively easy to defend against infantry and cavalry units in this game. What does damage are catapults, those damn ramming machines can destroy wooden walls instantly, along with flying bats and stuff like spiders crawling around your walls. Monitor the makeup of invasions. If they include any of those units, that needs to be your focus and you want to use your hero to attack the advanced ground units.

heavy archers are great, heavy elephant cavalry units are great, sorcerers are great.

You will finish the main campaigns fairly quickly and beyond the map generator, there isn't a lot of content at this stage.

Don't forget Avatar Of Mitra. Bit of a slog to get him up, but if you do, its a game changer obviously.\t\t\t\t\t
