\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSurvial RTS
Pure and simple, this is a game where the focus is survival. It follows a similar premise to "They Are Billions" as much other people have stated. But there are some different aspects to this game. That said, this game is unique enough to be its own, as you control a hero who levels up.

Story
There is a small story(mini campaign if you will) that the game provides. Theres really no tutorial, so you'll have to do some reading and lots of trial and error. The setting takes place in Khoraja, when Conan was put in charge to stop a crazy sorcerer whos desert legions are bent on conquering and destroying. I do wish there was more campaign related missions. They felt kinda lacking the depth that the Coanan universe has.

Gameplay
Gather resources, build up defenses and train units. Then level them up. One thing you must do is explore as much of the map as possible. You need to do this for the following reasons:

1. You need to find resources. Occasionally there are some maps where resources are scarce . I understand that you are in the deserts of Khoraja, and there is not much there, but for gameplay sake, i found it sometimes troubling that I was lacking a particular resource.

2. Point 2 touches on 1, as you need to expand to construct buildings .
You'll need space to construct a fair amount of buildings if you are to grow your economy, tech tree. A small spot wont do, you will need an area as large as the screen can see for thralls.

3. Finding a defensible choke point
Its not always advisable that you just build in the open. You will need to carefull funnel enemies into killzones to maximize you defense and reduce overall operating costs.

4. Level Up you Units
Especially the main hero unit, who has one unique ability. ( Wish they had at least two, felt kinda lacking)

5. Look for artifacts
They are special items which your hero can use, or it gives a passive ability boost. Sadly, your hero can only carry two.

Economy Management
This is one of the most important aspects of the game. You must be aware of your economy. How much you produce vs how much you consume. Having more units requires more resources, but if you dont have the resource or only produce a bit, you wont be training much of certain units. On some maps, I had to rely on early tech units, as resources can be so rare you can't create higher tier ones. Its a bit disappointing. But after playing for a bit, there's certain strategies you must follow if you are out to create units. You must expand. and you must do a lot of micro managing of selling excess resources. The game does not do it for you sadly. I found myself constantly pausing to sell, as I could always use the extra gold to train or build something. The Pause button will be your lifesaver , especially when things get hectic late in game.

Heroes
They play a fairly big part early game. You must level them up to use their ability. They also kill some enemies in one shot, so its good to have them with your units when exploring. They can also tank most enemy units by them self, except the stronger guardian units and hero enemy units. The will be ressurected upon death, but there s a timer. Its key that you explore early to get resources and gold , it will make building your base and economy much easier.

Graphics and System
It was not bad. My game was set on high-preset at 1080P, and the game looked pretty good to me. Structures looked convincing of the Khorajan architecture and so did many units.

For the most part the game ran smoothly, but thats after a bit of tinkering. The game had automatically set V-Sync on for my computer, which heavily reduced my FPS. Turning it off helped keep the game usually around 55-60. Some larger battles with many units and fire effects would sometimes bring it down to 40.

I can't help but notice the game could be better optimized, as my system while old, surpassed the requirements.

Replayability and Value
I put these two in the same area. The game for sure has almost unlimited replay. But with the limited campaign and modding, I'll probably only play the game for a few more times. I do wish they opened up the possibility for community created content, it would have extended the game substantially. I'm only a light modder, but I would have loved to create scenarios to share. Something like a timed base defense, or gathering a certain number of resources.

Final Thoughts
The game itself , along with the update Belits Fury, took me roughly 30 hours to complete. For the 16$ CAN that I paid, I'd say the game is great for a sale price. At first, I had to play easy, but as I learned the tech tree and what I should construct first, the game became easier.

It would have been cool to also be the attacker. Being always on defense is cool, but there could have been more missions added in this respect.
