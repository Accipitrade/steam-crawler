\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is hard. But you learn with each attempt, and as you finish feats you become stronger. Definitely, do the story missions first if you can instead of jumping right into the multiplayer which is sort of a horde mode. If you do, put everything on easy except the strength of spawner camps since you will need those to level up your hero between rounds and early on you aren't building sprawling bases. In fact, build small and tight and maybe have two or so mobile forces. Walls and towers help but get that tech going too to upgrade the economic portions (thralls, command, food, gold) asap. As hard as it can be, that first Easy setting win my friend and I got was cheered exuberantly.

Game looks good with assets or design elements straight out of Conan Exiles, which is fun if you played that game.

Regarding DLC it is pricey for what you get and yet the magic user hero is much stronger than the female character, who feels under-powered. Kind of lame that the DLC guy is one of the best and costs so much but there you go.

I am recommending this game only if you are willing to accept how limited the gameplay experience is, and how challenge is built into that experience. It is meant to be lost at a bunch till you start winning, then you up the challenge and keep going. All you do is build and defend a base against ever harder waves. I think it, at launch, is a bit pricey for all that, but what is there is pretty well executed, so if you like that sort of game then get it, or if it's on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
