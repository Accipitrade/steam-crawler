\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll preface and say that I rarely write reviews, but I felt like I needed to share my experience with potential buyers.

I bought this to scratch my itch at "base building" with tower defense. Other similar genre games like They Are Billions and Factorio lost its flavor for me and I wanted some variety. I love the Conan universe, but I just can't recommend this game. The difficulty is too steep with the horrible resource dependency mechanic for every building. You basically have to play a perfect game without losing structures in the early waves to get your economy up and running all before the first unique boss unit comes.

My main gripe is that after hours of playing and multiple games, I still haven't gotten to the end game. I don't know what end game buildings are because it's so expensive to do anything and juggling expenses with base defense/wave management is too much. I'll admit, at first I didn't know what I was doing and gold income was low. When I discovered Engineering Guild Corpse Pickers, that really helped. Yes, you can sell resources at the Marketplace, but it's far too much micro and I wish it would auto-sell when you hit max storage.

Maybe I'm just bad at the game, but I've really tried to get into it and figure it out. For an "RTS"-like game, I don't think I should be needing to pause often in order to make my commands and buildings - that would make it more of a turn-base game.

My games go something like this:
1) Build up lumber and gold income, try to maintain 150+ gold income for rest of game (research Farming, Economics, Engineering Guild)
2) Survive first few waves soloing with my "p2w" hero Kalanthes
3) Get walls and towers up and staff with javelin throwers
4) Expand and get stone to get Engineer Guild up and research Corpse Pickers and Follower Tent.
5) Upgrade to stone walls and better towers
5) Get pummeled by fire units and siege units
6) Die to unique hero boss wave

The few times I didn't die to the hero boss, I had to micro my units/hero to slowly wittle him down. But while I was doing that, my base building suffered and I subsequently fell behind and died to later waves.

There should maybe be an option to have a higher base gold income so you can get your initial base up and running faster. Most of the early game, I'm just waiting for gold income to tick in order to build things. Yes, I explore and kill things with my hero during this stage of the game.

I wanted so badly for this to be my go-to base builder tower defense game.

Edit: The game leaves me frustrated and that's not fun. If you want a solo wave defense game go play They Are Billions. If you want a better base management/resource management survival game play Factorio.\t\t\t\t\t
