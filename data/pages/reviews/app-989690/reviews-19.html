The beginning is difficult because there is no real tutorial, you are thrown into the game with only gaining tips after you complete a task which will in turn explain the building or army properties of an action you just completed. The game timer is a little slow and over all can become boring if you are wanting some real action at a faster pace. But from my understanding their is different waves you can set for faster style game play that can be more difficult.

The game is a great concept and I am really enjoying it now. I hope they put more updates and time into the game to make it a cool Strategy/Survival game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I wanted to give Funcom a fair shake. I do love the Conan series and have purchased a few of the games under their banner in the past (Conan Exiles prior to this one was the better of the releases), so I've had my ups and downs with the company itself as far as Customer Support issues, Betas, ETC. I wasn't too excited about the release on this one, just because it was just another RTS skin etc. etc. etc. Boy was I wrong. Yes. this game has bugs, Yes this game is EXTREMELY difficult even on the easiest of settings, however it's been a long time since a game has made me this frustrated that I had to give it another go, and another go, until I defeated the last wave of that level. Is the tech tree bloated.. Yes.. is the game user friendly with it's Demo.. Not at all. Each Single Player level IS your Demo. It's to show you what you need to build in order to be comfortable in the level. Are they achievable.. Yes. Hard.. Very. But at the end of the day when I put the game down, I want to play it more and enjoy it. I haven't had a game frustrate me to the levels of wanting to play since Starcraft Broodwar. Despite the guys who are not recommending this game on 4-5 hours of game play I give this one 2 thumbs way up. It's just indeed fun, and thats why I buy video games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly? Kinda feels like a detailed mod developed for a somewhat dated, existing RTS as opposed to a fully-developed, standalone game. Some good concepts that don't feel completely developed (probably rushed to launch) and, while it is fun to play in several respects, I doubt will hold up well against TAB once that game launches its campaign with all of its associated upgrades.

If the Devs do plan on supporting Conan, it could end up as a solid game. Hopefully they do. Here are some more specific comments for anyone who is interested.

Difficulty. I happen to like difficult games, especially when it comes to tower defense/survival style so I really don't mind that this game can be tough. In fact, I see the difficulty as an asset. I've read a lot of complaints about difficulty but can only assume those people haven't played TAB on some of the higher level difficulty settings.

Questing. I like the concept of sending your hero and troops out to find additional resources, artifacts and increase XP. However, I think this area would benefit from enhancement in terms of rewards. I'd like to see potential tech tree upgrades or maybe even some unique tech rewards that cannot be researched (only discovered through questing). As it is, early questing can feel a little unrewarding when you keep finding resources like stone and haven't yet expanded your stone storage capacity. I think some more variety of map-based mobs would be good

Hero powers. Unlocking powers, enhancing stats as you level is good but seriously? Conan just gets a whirlwind power when his basic attacks already cleave? Come on Devs, put a bit more thought into this please. Sure, give us whirlwind but add a few others (on major cool downs if required for balance) as he and the other hero levels. Like I said, good concept for this type of game but not developed properly at this point.

Hero DLC. This has already been commented on but I'm going to add my own thoughts which won't be a surprise. Considering the high price tag for the basic game, it's an absolute disgrace that the Devs (or most likely the publisher) has locked up the third hero as DLC. I would urge whoever made that foolish decision to reconsider. Players rarely forget when they get screwed by a company and they will happily warn everyone about it. Do the right thing, unlock the hero.

Campaign. Also been commented on by many others. Feels like an extended tutorial in many ways. Needs expansion (thoughtful expansion) with unique objectives, unique bosses, unique map features, you get the idea.

Battles. These are more enjoyable than TAB in my opinion, however, the pathfinding is terrible. This issue possibly explains why sometimes the last few guys from an incoming wave never seem to turn up. Have had to save/quit/load to resolve it. Not really an acceptable issue these days.

Overall, if you like wave survival games like this, I'd wait until on sale and/or the Devs improve it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's an alright game. If you are a fan of wave defence RTS and turtling, like me, you'll enjoy it. It think I prefer They Are Billions, if I am completely honest, so if you are trying to decide between the two I'd start there. I personally prefer its art style and the number of units it can throw at you. I'm currently about 10 hours in, at a guess. I have had a lot of offline play due to being in the process of moving house (and the country I live in).

Honestly, Conan Unconquered is a fairly solid game. Stable frame rates, graphics are reasonable, I have only had chance to play on my old laptop, which only has a 965 in it, but it manages 60 FPS at medium settings and 1920 x 1080 resolution. Sound effects are the usual battle sounds and gruff voice overs, music is inoffensive. Nothing amazing, but nothing wrong with it either.

No bugs encountered so far, and the difficulty seems to be just about in the right spot.

I'm not really sure what else to say about it, it's not a bad game - it's also not amazing either. It won't set your world on fire, it'll just allow you to pass 2 or 3 hours in a single sitting without noticing where the time has gone.

Acceptable pricing, not pissed off that I bought it. I can imagine in a couple of years with the kind of sporadic time I am expecting to spend playing it, I think I will end up playing a hundred hours or so.

Edit: Looking at the other reviews, people are complaining about the economy side of things, I'm not finding that side of things particularly difficult. Much like They Are Billions, if you don't rush into upgrading your basic housing or building a large army too quickly, the economy is fairly plentiful. It forces you to expand in search of further resources and prevents you from building extremely OP armies. The basic units you have access to from the start are much stronger than the equivalent AI units, so allowing you to build a lot of them would be a bit silly, it is similar for the base defences, a ballista tower with a brazier in its firing arc is enough to defend a wall from everything up to wave 10, as far as I can tell so far.

So, absolutely recommended for anybody that enjoys tower defence games or the RTS of old where you could sit in a well defended base and watch the AI throw everything it has at you.

Not recommended it you get irritated by DLC that requires further payment. I personally don't give a about that sort of thing, just don't buy it if you don't want/can't afford it, or don't buy the game it you don't agree with it - it's not like they hide it from you, the deluxe edition content is clearly shown on the store page. Apparently a lot of people are wound up by the third hero unit sitting behind a pay wall. I haven't paid for the deluxe content but I may do in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
