\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like Conan, then I really love Tower Defense/RTS hybrids. Dislike day-1 DLC for the best Hero in the game, for 33% of the cost of the game. Automatically makes me score this less, even though I like it.

Game works for me, some frame rate hiccups I expect they will fix. But the game itself lacks some form of polish. It literally looks like the Dev's are still in the late 90's to mid 00's. Camera zoom makes game feel like I'm playing at 800x600. When you load a game, it tells you nothing like the game froze, but it is indeed loading. Fire currently in this game is highly mundane and forces micro management for its buggy at the moment. AI is pretty brain dead, no stance formations.

I don't consider myself some pro RTS player, infact I build slow and wrecklessly. Then I still beat the entire campaign in 14hours. Game lacks story and depth, the story is comics you unlock basically. Its not intertwined like C&C was. After you're basically left with trying coop, endless waves, or fan made maps(saw this in the game, dunno much about it, looks like a good feature though).

They did balance it pretty well for a first day release. Least for me. Last mission I almost lost 3 times, then prevailed. Dunno if they're doing some number switchero magic, but it works.

I only recommend this to Tower Defense lovers. Some RTS guys might like it, but will probably yearn for more depth. If you like both like me, then this is a shoe in. Since not many games play like this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI came in really wanting to like this game. I like survival RTS games in general, was a huge fan of They are Billions, and was hoping for a good co-op survival RTS. Unfortunately, I don't think this is it. Others have said that it feels like it should just be in the first round of EA, and I think I'd agree.

One general issue is the upkeep - as you build up your army/structures your upkeep increases at a high enough rate that you don't really feel like your income is actually growing. It seems like they want you to research advanced buildings/tech to more efficiently counter future units, but it's really hard to actually get to the point that you can do so. Instead you seem to hit a certain, kind of low, income in the midgame and then kind of stall out there.

This is compounded by the number of resources you have -- there's command, thralls, and food, all of which you need to spend for army upkeep/buildings, as well as gold, lumber, stone, metal, iron and so on, each of which gets eaten up by upkeep and is often used unevenly so you end up with big excesses of niche resources and a surfeit of one or two basic ones. It feels like the resource system belongs in an Anno game/traditional city builder, but in this context feels regressive and unwieldy.

I was hoping the co-op would emulate AOE 2's shared control of one empire, or SC2's team melee/archon mode, but instead they have kind of arbitrary divisions of units/resources that make it so you can't really just have one player specialize, or break up responsibilities, both people have to basically build the same units/structures they would in single player, just in a base with two people.

On top of that, there are mechanics that just feel clunky/unwieldy. I.e., your buildings light on fire when damaged. You have to individually click on each building with your units for them to take time and put it out. By itself, fine, I guess, but in practice you have to work through awkward pathfinding and spend a lot of time micro'ing your soldiers around fighting against the game to deal with it. The same sort of issue comes with disease - when a lot of characters die in one place, future soldiers fighting there get a DoT. You can solve this with tech - priests - but they're hard to access because of the aforementioned issue with your income plateauing, and as it is it basically just makes your mazes/static defense awkward to defend.

It really feels like it could have used a few passes to get rid of some resources/mechanics, tighten up the pathfinding, add utility like a toggle to have units automatically fight fires (and things like that), and work on the tech/income progression. I also don't understand why they decided to make resources etc split like they did in co-op -- at least give the option to transfer resources/units.

I like the core idea of this game, but it really feels like you're kind of fighting against all of this awkwardness to have fun. I'm sure that some repeated playthroughs would help me figure out proper tech order and resource gathering balance, but as it is it's too easy to get into a death spiral, and it's too difficult to get your units etc. to do what you want. I really hope that they do some overhauls, or someone puts out some mods. I really wanted to like this game, and I love the idea of a co-op survival rts (as soon as this game was announced I was ready to buy it on release) but this just isn't quite there.

I'm going to try it some more, and see if these issues are more with me understanding it, but we'll see. So far I've only managed 20/25 waves, and it's felt kind of hopeless, but we'll see if teching differently helps.\t\t\t\t\t
