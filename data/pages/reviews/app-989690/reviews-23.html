\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe pacing of the game is really poor. The amount of upkeep every building requires is insane considering the low amount of income you acquire of each type. If less of the buildings require resource income it could be a lot better, but right now it's actually unpleasant to really play.

I've seen a few people are reading this, and while I don't expect the devs to read it, I still hope that some updates will come to smooth things out. It's clear that this is *cough* inspired *cough* by They are Billions. That's not really a problem. Conan has a lot of lore and unit variety that could make things quite flavorful, at least you aren't fighting the same set of zombies, and there are bosses.

But turning a profit, and really fighting for growth in this game is a bit of a hassle. Even simple things like houses (which your economy is based on) require both flat resources *and* upkeep. The sheer amount of houses you need to make an important building like the Market profitable is insane, and you swiftly find yourself running out of wood (again for housing upkeep).

If you had to *fight* tooth and nail for progress, I'd like it better. But you aren't fighting, you are micromanaging resources in a pitiful balancing act that keeps you in pretty much the same place (economically speaking) the whole game. I've beaten the easy basic maps, but it did not feel satisfying to do so, and the bosses laughing their way through whatever walls, industry and or housing you happen to have in the way was really unsatisfying.

Conan Unconquered has a vast amount of potential. Smooth out the balance of resources and pacing, and there's a lot of room to add even more challenge. When they do that, I'll happily change this to a recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
