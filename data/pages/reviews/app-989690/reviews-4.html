\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall can't say that I would recommend it to any but people who enjoy forced encounter RTS games. If you don't like that, there are other more controlled RTS games that are out there that will likely be more favored by the larger number of people.

You get only minimal time to try and build up supplies during what would be a "campaign" mode, all the while trying to manage your very meager supplies. The progression of the rounds goes from so simple you don't need to invest in defenses to the next round needing all the defenses and an iron dome around your main base.

Before it is asked, no, there isn't an option to take the attack waves off or increase the length between them in a freemode. The most that you ever get is the ability to delay a wave by a couple of minutes.

As it stands even now a fair length of time after it has been released I cannot recommend it unless additional options were put in place for it. One small disclaimer on that, if you have a few friends that are into RTS games and you want to play together and get destroyed, then you might enjoy this.

Otherwise your general strategy is going to be: BUILD! Build stuff for them to destroy, then build more stuff to replace it. You will find yourself in most cases waiting for gold to build the next thing to get destroyed by a random trash mob while you're running from one end of your huge land claim for resources to the other.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is all about learning to balance your available resources to survive the waves of attacks. You need some foresight and plan ahead. Simply training a lot of units and building many structures won't work. Understanding the right time and place to build, do research, and go out to explore/hunt is all equally important. The User Interface may look a little unpolished but the other visual elements are fine in my eyes for this type of game. I can understand that many folks can get easily frustrated with the game's difficulty. But when you come to study and understand how a particular level ticks, just persevere and you will survive it.

I'm still early into the game and will add more play time to it in the coming days. It seems the "Single Player" mode lacks enough story cut scenes. But there is a digital "Comic Book" accessible in the Main Menu which you try to build and complete by finishing levels.

Overall, I can say I like the game enough and can recommend it if on a decent sale. You may like it especially if you're into the "Conan The Barbarian" universe. By the way, aside from "Command & Conquer", "Grey Goo" is another good sci-fi RTS game also made by the developers Petroglyph. That one has an excellent amount of great cut scenes.\t\t\t\t\t
