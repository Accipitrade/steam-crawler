\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter a few hours, I think I'm ready to give it a review.

Long Story short, here's the highlights. 8 pros - 3 pars - 2 fails. Recommended.

First, let's get the obvious out of the way. Conan unconquered is a RTS style game remenescant of Age of Empires or, IMHO, more like the original Warcraft titles with it's units synergy and hero class's experience and artifacts. A tower defense base building a la Conan franchise with population and resource management with great lore. Time is short and waves can be brutal to an unprepared army. There is a pause for those of you that are desperate. Some comics and readings if you find it boring. Seriously. There's also a coop mode.

I have based my review on my experience with RTS and the Conan franchise. I do not have 1000s hrs in it. I am simply sharing my opinions of my first play through of the campaign on default settings and haven't tested all the options of the game.


My pros
  • Steam support, built in achievements, Cloud saves, community challenges, good graphics and great music all supported by a good performance and great stability gave me a satisfying experience. (Although I did find the loadings to be long.)
  • Lore, comics, storytelling. Authenticity in short.
  • Tech tree and research advancements which includes a visual flowchart which is great.
  • Buffs, debuffs, achievement bonuses. (That's right)
  • Awesome patrol feature in unit command list.
  • Healing at a well is refreshing. ;)
  • Performance and stability.
  • Intuitive grouping keys and good UI.

My pars
  • Missed first impression.
    Was a missed opportunity at the CGI intro showing a God, which destroys everything friend or foe, walk out of a busted defense wall in pieces. Movie builds up great, god walks out of busted wall, wouldn't it have been more epic to have the wall smashed to pieces leaving pvpers with wishful thinking? Basically, I SMDH at the intro movie. Not a great start.

  • Costs balancement.
    Keeping in mind that a simple hovel(house) earns you 5 coins a tick(wtv that is, a minute or something), a watch tower cost 175 coins and has 300hp while an actual guard tower that has 1200hp which scales with research tiers and can be mounted with 4 ranged guards cost 120 coins. In what universe does that makes sense? The upkeep of some units can also be too expensive. You basically need a village to support a few archers and a wall.

  • Waves don't stop ticking.
    By the time you are done fighting a wave, an even stronger army is plowing thru the survivors without catching a breath, eventually. Add in fire, disease and the wandering thieves emptying your resource pools and you're in for a rough first play through. A grace period would give time to regroup, repair and would pace the waves. At this pace, growth is hard to achieve, recovery is next to impossible. Forget about exploring or going more than a throwing distance from your base to kill a nest and loot like the old Warcraft days. It's more like a black ops zombie's inevitable fall. Lock the doors and plank the windows. You're in for a rough ride. That's on default. The fix is really simple. Next wave? is the last one done? True/false. Simple.

NB; The reason I have these Pars is because I have the hopes that it will be tweaked in the future.


My fails
  • No follow order in the unit command list. Self explanatory. I miss it. Great key mapping alleviates it but micro managing every little move when you have multiple support groups makes it tedious overall.

  • Camera doesn't seem to rotate. Having an isometric 2D view base building game that doesn't seem to have any rotation or pivotal view features in 2019 gets an F even from me. At least, it has top down view option. Only negates to a E, sorry.


Overall, I recommend the game to anyone that likes being challenged at base building tower defense type gameplay because this is what game it is. Odds are, if your favorite game is Fortnite, you probably won't be impressed with a 2d tower defense game built in a fantasy RP universe in the first place.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been a Conan and Command and Conquer fan since I was a kid. I have read the Conan stories and books, and you can tell that there is a love for the source material here. Nostalgia aside, one can see the direction the designers are trying to go, and it is a fascinating direction. Sure there are some unpolished areas, but with the release cycles of games nowadays, it should just be accepted that games are going to get patched, upgraded and DLC. I understand people's concerns about paying for the 3rd hero, but hopefully the silver lining is that there are more heroes, lands, and factions lined up for release in the future, as was the case with Conan Exiles. Even Conan Exiles has come a long way from where it started, and I am sure with C&C designers at the helm (and no EA in sight) this game wont be abandoned to Pay for Play obscurity.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played for over a month and I think I have seen enough to give a review. The Good, the Bad and the Ugly.
The Good: The map is random which creates different maps but they are very similar. There is a large tech tree, the sounds/music are well done, the units seem to have a balance. Generally play flows well.
The Bad: I know I will not cover everything.. Autosave interrupts play to the point that it knocks you off your game, some improvement on this has occurred since release. There are no tutorials that help you learn the tech tree. The tree is extensive and by the time you reach the upper tech, I get lost on trying to get to a tech. The game is not rewarding... I mean that the game ends when failure occurs instead of reaching some end point.
The Ugly: Paying $10 extra for an additional hero is wrong.
I think this game disappoints as a rewarding game with replayability.\t\t\t\t\t
