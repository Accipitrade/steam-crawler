\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fun game so far, and has depth to it that will take a long while to master. Co-op wise it is just what was needed and is different than the usual mix. Yes, there are currently a few bugs and things to iron out, but its a new game and most games need a little time to iron things out. They have already released patches to address issues, so clearly they are listening and responding which is fantastic. I could care less about having perfect graphics as long as the game play is fun and my friend sand I can play together. I guess it all depends on what you value out of a game. For me and my friends, we just want something fun, and so far this is showing plenty of potential for fun.

For those who complain about lack of story and lack of solo campaign, I fail to understand what the issue is. It's not that kind of game, and never claimed to be. The random maps provide infinite replay without the need for pre-made forced stories. This is not an RPG, and is not a MMO, nor is it a "traditional" RTS. It's different, and in a good way if you accept it for what it is and not what you wished it would be instead.\t\t\t\t\t

The Bad:
AI = retarded

I cannot stress how rotten the AI is in this game, it is boarderline unplayable.

Building Mechanics = broken
The game often does not join adjacent walls or terrain, allowing enemies to simply pass by

Laggy Co-OP
Even with fast internet, I've had matches freeze up due to lag, then unfreeze and we're all dead. This is after quite sometime in a match. Super frustrating.

Day-one DLC
I mean, really?

Overall, the game has potential, its just badly needs some TLC

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far I enjoy the game for the most part. I purchased the 2 - pack ( one for my wife ) and Deluxe Edition content ( only for myself, wife didn't care)

We are enjoying the game, and see that as time goes on, hope that it's only going to get better.

The game is a mix between an RTS and a Tower Defense game. You don't have to worry about workers gathering a particular type of resource because the buildings will either generate it, or produce a type of non renewable resource until used and you need to construct more. ( It would be nice, but neither make or break it.) Could be I'm just use to it as I frequent WarCraft and StarCraft style games Age of Empires etc for decades.

I would like attack moving AI to prioritize attacking above healing, or putting out fires etc. I would much rather micro manage the clicking to heal, or put out the fires.

I would also like an option to create maps, select from at least a small pool of pre generated "permanent" maps. Keep the random generated tile sets as well.

I think with some optimization, bug squishes and general maintenance that this game will really shine, not that it can't be enjoyed as is.

I notice some people complain about a price. There have been worse games with a greater price tag. We, people, invest in what we enjoy. You will not go wrong here. Business is business, and if you're a fan of RTS, Tower Defense, and Conan then you will enjoy it.

I personally have not played another game like this to compare. Some claim "They are Billions" etc but I personally have not played that or Frost Punk.

So other than the occasional pathing issues I've experienced, and The Hero Kalanthes not attacking when attack moving. He will Heal or put out fires instead, very annoying to me, but not enough to make me unable to enjoy the game. I am not even that good at it. Learning how to get resources faster and manage / tech up faster, but I haven't figured it out yet.

I recommend this game and believe each person on the fence should give it a shot before listening to any positive OR negative reviews. I know I did, and am glad I did. We miss out on many experiences, as well as truths, when we fail to look into something for ourselves.

EDIT - I remembered a game I could compare the resource gathering to, Supreme Commander, if anyone is familiar with that. And Maybe some Star Wars Galactic Battlegrounds Saga.
