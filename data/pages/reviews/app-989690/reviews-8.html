\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun game.It's a mix of Majesty's survival and kingdom building
Structure build like C&C 3 Tiberium Wars
Resource variety and management like Rise of Nations
I will list the pros and cons of the game

Cons
-The co-op is only a customizable map, which kinda sucks.
-The Power and utility difference between the four heroes is unbalanced
-Steep learning curve in the beginning.
-Most trainable units are pointless considering you really only need ranged units.
-Expanding your base to new resource areas can be time consuming and a gamble if enemies spawn nearby

Pros
-Variety of enemy types and waves that spawn makes the constant defending fun and/or challenging
-Niche appeal to RTS players that like defending and survival
-Each of the four heroes has their own unique playstyle

Tips for new players
*Valeria is the most versatile and has the highest dps of all the heroes. I played 90% of the missions with her and I recommend you try her for venturing out for crates, guardians and defending your base.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a decent enough Survival RTS. Like you've seen from other reviews, it borrows a lot from They are Billions. With the most different feature being Hero availability in your base building sections. But its your standard TaB gameplay.

If you haven't tried either of these games, I'd push you towards They are Billions as I see it as more defined and polished.

If you're coming from They are Billions and would like to try this one, by all means, it's a decent game.

The highest praise I can give this game goes to its core mechanics, its polished and integrated well that should keep you invested for hours. I have no complaints on this regard.

What I would say pulls the quality of the game down is mostly to its presentation. The UI and some audio feel like it was rushed or they just decided to maintain the placeholder assets. It really makes it feel lesser than it could be.

There are about 10 levels in total, with an option to procedurally generate a map for replayability. Its a pretty short list, but after the first stage, each map takes quite a while to finish.

2. The building size is just terrible. It's so rigid based on square tiles and so small compared to the units.

3. There's no sandbox or some kind of survival mode. A Tower Defense game is most interesting if it has unlimited waves of horde coming to attack. But this game doesn't have that aspect.

5. Buildings have lack of realism. It's just a static 3D object with no interesting animation in it.
