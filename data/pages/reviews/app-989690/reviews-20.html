\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you have fond memories of classic RTS games this is a nice way to relax for an evening. This is a wave based RTS based on the Black Colossus by Robert E. Howard. Hero units mix up the combat as any respectable one should. The inclusion naturally occurring enemies wandering the environment also gives the game a more natural feel as units can encounter scorpions, spiders, and ostriches (vicious creatures) while gathering resources.

For those of you who have been holding off because of the "Day 1 DLC" complaints I would encourage you to inspect what the DLC actually is. It is a $10 dollar bundle that includes an additional hero, a new skin for one of the existing heroes, an EBook, and the game soundtrack. Consider the price of individual skins or soundtracks for other games and then consider the price of the bundle. While I personally would have preferred the third hero be available in the base game he is by no means an overpowered character.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - This game is a solid base to what could eventually become a great RTS - but it isn't there yet. The lack of content really holds it back.

The GOOD
--------------------
-The gameplay is very solid. Units are all interesting and there is a solid C&C style tech tree.
-The game looks really good for an RTS. Corpses pile up, the interface is easy + intuitive to use, and almost all effects have a clear visual indicator
-Coop is a huge plus

The BAD
--------------------
-There's a huge lack of content. There's essentially only one game mode (wave defense) that you're meant to play endlessly as a sort of score attack. There's a 5 mission "campaign" that's mostly just a tutorial for main mode. The base building/economy could easily slide right into a traditional RTS, so it's sort of baffling there isn't at least a skirmish mode.

The UGLY
--------------------
-Units get stuck and stop moving all the time, the pathfinding is almost as bad as OG starcrafts
-There are a lot of minor bugs that have yet to be ironed out, like units spawning inside buildings, towers becoming ungarrisonable, etc
-The economy is a bit punishing for higher research units, often it was more effective to spam low tier units instead of the higher tier ones and I would often find myself destroying my own buildings because the upkeep was so high
-There's only one type of map environment
