\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI watched lots of people play this. Saw lots of stuff about it that I liked, and wanted to play it myself. I like it as a whole, but it does have lots of things that I'm sure lots of people hate.

Pros:
- Breathtaking Unreal Engine 5 visuals. Great atmosphere.
- Meticulously handcrafted world map. It may seem like a bunch of junk assets thrown together, but I'm sure it took a long time to place and test. There are plenty of areas with themes that use relevant set pieces, too.
- Game is much longer/world is bigger than you'd expect.
- Some areas have philosophical/psychological meaning.
- Many different paths and shortcuts to find, encouraging exploration.
- Interesting speed run skip/shortcut methods.
- Relatively simple controls. I've seen many streamers say that "anybody can beat this game, unlike Getting Over It which has extremely difficult controls". I disagree with the latter (Getting Over It is pretty easy to me). I think the controls in both games require some thought, understanding and mastering.

Cons:
- No controller support (last I checked, anyway). Holding WASD and run for a long time hurts my fingers and wrist.
- The starting area lacks route variation and is rather lengthy and boring. This gets worse because you will fall back to the start many times. Worst part of the game.
- There are some areas where it feels like nothing is happening (eg. running a long time with no real challenge, or sitting on a vehicle/platform waiting for it to move from point A to B).
- No save, and a single playthrough can take many hours, so it's painful for people who don't have time or can't sit and play for long.
- A half-way point elevator was added, but I personally prefer a "resume from last known position" like Getting Over It. I did not use the save elevator.
- There are a few areas with misleading invisible walls, floors with no collision, etc.
- Climbing controls are kinda annoying. The way it works is you need to push against an object as it passes the character's chest area while falling. If you happen to stop pushing towards the object for that brief window, you will fail to grab the object. So you MUST COMMIT.
- The problem with committing is if you happen to land atop an object instead of falling short, you may accidentally run yourself off the top of the object before you have a chance to react, especially if the object is small. You can overcome this by learning, having fast reaction time, or abusing slow motion.
- Some cruel traps that can send you back to the start point without mercy. You should expect to fall back to the literal start.
- Player character is an ugly boy. You can customize his headgear, though.
- Story not translated for non-English countries. I liked the story, even though it's minimal. It adds to the atmosphere.

Conclusion:
I've seen streamers say they love it, and others say they hate it. So it's very much that kind of game. I personally liked it, but it's definitely rough around the edges and has janky bits. I think it's especially punishing for people like me who like to rush.

Anyway, if you're interested, you should try it and see if you like it. Refund if you don't. As always.

I recall seeing the developer say there will be a night mode and a multiplayer mode? So those are some things to look forward to. Even without, I feel like I may play this game again when I have spare time.

This was probably one of the most interesting games of 2023 for me, but there really aren't many games in recent years that I like.At all.

I also want to say that I'm generally not a fan of 3d platformers, because they often don't convey the jumping feeling anywhere near as satisfyingly as 2d platformers. This game's okay though. Much better than games like Street Fighter 6 and King of Fighters 15 that both have horrible jumping/gravity/falling, etc. Which is especially sad because they are 2d games (camera and gameplay-wise).\t\t\t\t\t
