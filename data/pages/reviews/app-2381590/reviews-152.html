\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGenerally I wouldn't bother writing reviews for bad games, but I feel like this game just has something that makes it that special.
The mantling and jumping are difficult to master in the sense that the hitboxes of the subjects are badly designed and hard to see what you should be jumping onto. It feels extremely clunky. The animation of the mantling/walking/running also feel unpolished and seriously rough.
The text to speech narration feels like it's been auto generated and the music feels like it plays when you reach certain points, not when multiple events happen, which continues to make the game feel unfinished.
As well as this, the game is terribly optimized, requiring me to turn all my settings (except for view distance, which I'd had to leave on Epic in order to see the rest of the map) to Low in order to even have a playable experience (100FPS barely) on a PC running 1440p, a 5800x3D, 3070Ti and 16GB of RAM.
Furthermore, the game will not save your progress if you exit to the main menu, this is just simply awful design and was the deciding factor of whether or not I would refund the game or not.

Iverall, for the hour that I played this game, I was seriously disappointed.
The game just feels like a bad, cheap 3D copy of Bennett Foddy's "Getting Over It" that's designed to attract attention for a week or two and make a quick dollar for the publisher. I will be refunding this game and will download it elsewhere to complete.
It is absolutely not worth the 15NZD that it is being sold for.
As well as this, I would've expected the game to be in an early-access stage, It is seriously unacceptable and outright disappointing for a game like this to be sold as a full release.
Maybe with a few updates to improve hitboxes and animations, I could see the game actually being a fun and challenging platformer, but in it's current state, I would not recommend anyone to purchase this game, regardless if it goes on sale or not.

Overall score: 2/10
