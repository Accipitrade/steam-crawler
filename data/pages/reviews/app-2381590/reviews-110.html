\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn its current state, I cannot recommend this game because it has one glaring flaw. It's not a bad game fundamentally, I actually felt quite compelled to keep climbing whenever I fell because unlike Getting Over It, the controls aren't complete ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. There were times where I thought the colliders were a bit ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ and the character didn't grab a ledge when he should've or something, but that's whatever in the grand scheme of things, you just climb back up and get better at it. The issue here is that the game doesn't ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ save.

The level is huge. I feel like I got pretty damn high in my time playing, but based on what I've seen from other people playing, I probably got maybe 1/3rd in my best run, which took about 2 or 3 hours? It's absurd how high this game goes (and thus how much progress you can potentially lose), which would be a fantastic thing to know that I'm getting so much content out of my purchase, but combined with the lack of saving, I have zero motivation to keep playing this game at the moment.

You missed a jump and lost 2 hours of progress? Fine, start climbing back up, that's the point of a game like this.
Your game crashed, which happened to me twice? ♥♥♥♥ you, I guess.
You've made a lot of progress but want to take a break and maybe play some other games for a bit? ♥♥♥♥ you, you have to finish the entire thing in one sitting.
You want to eat, handle a real world emergency, go to sleep, etc? Enjoy leaving your computer on all day and pray that the game doesn't crash while you're away, which even if it doesn't and you go back to playing it, you're just back to "you can't do anything else until you beat it."

I really don't understand why this is a feature. I looked it up and there's actually a post from the developers about it that says, "We understand that some resent the lack of opportunities to save progress, but the concept of the game is not to 'save game' - the point is that each successive level raises the stakes in the game, and the danger of falling down would be more significant." Saving progress is not the same thing as a checkpoint. I fail to see how saving progress would make the jumps any less dangerous, you just save the falls as well. People don't resent the lack of saving because we want an easy mode, it's because we want to be able to actually play the game without having to dedicate an entire ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ day to this game where we may or may not even beat it. Even Getting Over It saves your progress. If they ever add saving I might come back to this game and change the review, otherwise I doubt I'll ever touch it again.\t\t\t\t\t
