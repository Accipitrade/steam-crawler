\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love parkour, and while the controls are really weird, the gameplay was a lot of fun to me.

However that's where the positives end. The entire game feels like a cheap collection of default assets glued together in Unreal Engine or something, with little to no effort. There seems to be no obvious way to remap keys, the sound slider doesn't affect all sounds (wind volume is fixed apparently), UI is a collection of uninspired buttons and low quality png images, and the sound my pc made leads me to believe this game is very poorly optimised.

In a world where the competitors for this type of game are Getting Over It (with its inspired and witty voice over, unusual gameplay, original environments from the first 20 minutes, etc) and Jump King (also with unusual gameplay, genuine pixel art, etc), this game feels like a cheap copy of Getting Over It's concept made with no effort at all, using default and stolen 3D models, and with an uninspired and unemotional TTS voice over that makes it feel like the devs didn't care enough to hire a voice actor, or even voice it themselves, or even write a good script.

Once the TTS started telling parts of the story in the most monotone way, I couldn't go on anymore. The effort put into this game is in no way worth 7, let alone 10, when Getting Over It is barely a fifth of the price for an amount of effort orders of magnitude greater..\t\t\t\t\t
