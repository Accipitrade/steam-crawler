\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe most recent update makes the game playable. With a centered camera and less buggy collisions on rounded surfaces, most of these reviews have been made obsolete (press 1 to get a sane camera where forward is forward btw).

This is a Getting Over It style game through and through (A Foddian game?), though its a more genuine take on the genre ala Jump King. Lots of insane microplatforming challenges obsoleted by more daring but easier trampoline sections; it really is a game of risk versus reward where not getting tilted is the main goal.

My only gripe is that the game does not have autosaving, or any kind of saving to disc for that matter. I know the devs say that "not saving is part of the game", but I dont want checkpoints, I want a way to rage close the game and not lose all my progress. Dark Souls has a way to let you quit out without letting you save scum; the feature exists. Also save the settings to disc so I dont need to set it every time I open the game, and either mix the audio better or let me turn off your music while keeping your (interesting, if comically translated) voice content. The fact that the bottom of the slider isnt mute is annoying.

Overall, I wouldnt have recommended before the update today. Now that the game is fundamentally fair though, it's been an addicting and challenging game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOutstanding experience. If you like jump king, you might see this game and think its similar. While jump king has precise jumps down the the last pixel, this game is filled with risk assessment and is a test on your patience. If you just walk the whole game, and take safe jumps, you could win first try, but doing things fast and going for risky skips is very fun. People complain about the controls, but really i think they are quite good, the running speed feels fluid, there is good air strafing speed, but the "climb up" mechanic is somewhat hard to get used to. The character has a range to where he can clamber to, and to get good at the game is to feel out this range, to build up confidence in making it onto the next platform. Aside from the gameplay and mechanics, the visuals are really quite great in my opinion. Its sort of a collage of many assets and common game objects, that create a beautiful landscape that had me excited to continue the climb. To be honest, i was hesitant to use skips that a found early into my play through, just because i wanted to experience this handcrafted environment. My only real criticism of the game, is that i wish there was less bed skips. i think its fun to optimize your route through taking a riskier farther jump, but the beds(or other boosting objects) let you skip so much of the game, it would have taken me much longer to beat the game without them, and i plan to do a "no bed" run on my further replays of this game. All in all, 10/10 game for me, if you enjoyed Getting over it with Bennet Foddy, or similar games, pick this game up.\t\t\t\t\t
