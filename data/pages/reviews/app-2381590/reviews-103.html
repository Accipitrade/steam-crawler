In a few months, it might be polished enough to recommend
and even today, you can manage to enjoy it for sure. But there's a couple glaring issues that make the experience harder to enjoy:



- Invisible Walls:

In a game that is all about exploring and jumping around freely, you shouldn't have any invisible walls, let alone that many; there's a lot of scenery, but even high up in the sky, you might jump somewhere that the game doesn't want you to go to, you'll hit an invisible wall and fall down.
It's like closed doors in GTA, I understand why they're there, but I really wish it wasn't.

- Movement:

In a game about movement, I would have liked the movement to be more refined. Anytime you stray from the intended path and try to walk under an object you can't actually walk under, you'll start to get weird wiggles, your character floating in the falling animation, or your character getting pushed away. Even though it looks like there's plenty of space to go past that object.
Grabbing ledges is so slow and wonky, sometimes grabbing ledges you didn't want to grab.
Again, those issues are to be expected in most games, but not in a game about movement and exploration!


- Overall Lack of Polish:

Dialogue, if you can call it that, is a robotic AI voice with no subtitles that is hard to understand.
Changing the game volume does nothing (?)
The sun WILL blind you. It's annoying as heck.
The menus look terrible

Conclusion:

Despite this review being very negative overral, I do think that those issues can be ironed out if we give them more time, the core of the game is still enjoyable and I think eventually I'll switch this to a positive review. In fact even now I was considering a positive review\t\t\t\t\t
