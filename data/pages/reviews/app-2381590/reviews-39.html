\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"Getting over it" but better.
I'm actually someone who likes "Getting Over it with Bennet Foddy", and I can proudly say I like this game more. There are a lot of differences between these games, but the short version is this:
-this game gives you a character who moves phenomenally, as long as you take the time to learn what that movement is like. Putting this character in another platformer would be very fun indeed.
-to make the game hard, there are two factors working against this excellent movement. 1: you're doing a lot of PRECISION platforming, not necessarily speed or distance-based platforming. and 2: this game goes WAYYYYYYYYYYYYYYY higher than GOIT. Like...♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, I kept adapting to the vertigo and kept getting it again. So if you fall...you lose WAY more progress. Exponentially more.
-The last factor in your favor is that you can slow down time as much as you want. It's absurd. This creates a feeling that "you have EVERYTHING you need to get up this obstacle course...but if you fail ONCE, the consequences are dire"
-beds and trampolines are great. The game invites you to explore the environments, which are very cool. it rewards you with Easter eggs, a bit of environmental storytelling, and frequently, a bouncy-bed or trampoline which will often be a MAJOR shortcut. You'll have to deduce where the bounce will take you in order to line up your jump, though...risk-reward. Love it.
-Whether you liked what Bennet Foddy was trying to do artistically or not, this game is worth your time. It has a commentator that makes small talk, and as someone who liked Foddy's comments, these are pretty good too.
-trust your ledge-grabbing ability. It's way better than you think.
-you can double-tap crouch to do a dodge roll for no reason. 10/10.
-You can roll off of a ledge to ragdoll yourself off a ledge. 11/10.\t\t\t\t\t
