\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hated the game at first, because of the super clunky physics, movement, and hit-box. I swear there were so many unfair falls that I shouldn't have fell. But I just wanted to beat this damn annoying game once in for all, and I did. To be honest, I consider this game as testing your patience more, rather than testing your skill, like Getting over it or Jump King. I think if you don't give up, you are most likely going to beat the game no matter what. It just takes a long, LONG time to.

I also really liked the world view of the game. This dystopian city and a little bit of dark backstory were really interesting. Maybe it's because I commonly like B-games such as Getting over it and Sexy Hiking.

I know this sounds exaggerated and ridiculous, but maybe beating this game kind of encouraged me to never give up, even though it might seem impossible, just like when I looked up and saw the endless-looking way to go, and when i tried speedrunning a couple times, it actually gradually became easier and easier to beat it, everytime a bit faster than before (final time, 19:32)

I'd maybe recommend this game if you legitimately want to beat the game, but not really if you just want to casually run around without the intention of beating it. I think the true satisfaction and charm of this game hides behind after beating it, or else it will just bring you frustration.
