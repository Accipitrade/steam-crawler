\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a$$. I've heard people say it's like Getting Over It, but I strongly disagree. Getting Over It was a frustrating game, sure, but it was very polished and filled to the brim with intentional design choices. The controls were awkward, but once you mastered them, they felt surprisingly consistent and responsive. The physics and ledge detection were also very precise. The graphics and assets looked nice, and they were placed very deliberately to create certain types of terrain that posed new challenges to the player. Getting Over It is frustrating, but that is by immaculate design. Bennett Foddy clearly knew what he was doing, and he did it with a lot of care and attention.

Only Up, on the other hand, is a clumsy mishmash of ideas that seems great at first glance but is really janky in practice. It's frustrating, but not for the reasons the devs intended. In Getting Over It, you fell down because the terrain Benett Foddy intentionally created ♥♥♥♥♥♥ you over. In Only Up, you fell down because your character clipped through the ground or didn't grab onto a ledge he was supposed to grab. The ledge detection and mantling mechanics are inconsistent: your character will grab onto a ledge when you don't want them to, or not grab onto anything when you want them to. The controls are awful: your character feels very sluggish and stiff. It's like you're playing a platformer but with third-person shooter movement. The graphics are all over the place: the devs dumped every single Unity asset they could find into this game. While some locations looked nice and surreal, others looked messy and lazy. The game looks nothing like the screenshots, by the way. In-game, the textures look nowhere near as good, and everything that is not literally in front of your character will look like an object from Cruelty Squad. Oh, and there is dialogue in this game, just like, you know, Getting Over It. The difference is that in Getting Over It, not only did Bennett Foddy personally voice every line of dialogue, they also had a very clear message that tied in to the gameplay loop he created. In Only Up, the dialogue is voiced by a cringy text-to-speech program and tries to tell snippets of some asinine story no one cares about. There's no option to mute just the dialogue either; you can only turn down the volume of the entire game, which is the cherry on top of the sh!t cake.

TLDR: Only Up is yucky, no good, very bad. The entire game has this unfinished, asset-flip feel to it. It's what you would call a streamer game, designed to get funny haha rage moments out of your favorite neon-haired streamer. Beyond that, there's nothing about it that's good beyond its premise of going really high up into the sky. It's not even a good speedrunning game nor can it be compared to Getting Over It, which was actually quite polished and consistent in its frustrating design. 1/10.\t\t\t\t\t
