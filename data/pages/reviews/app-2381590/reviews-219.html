\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI picked this game up out of pure curiosity. In terms of actual gameplay, it's your basic parkour challenge. The controls are pretty squirrely. Definitely don't expect anything highly polished or professional, the game was clearly either made by a single developer or a very small group of people. All that being said the game isn't necessarily bad. Bottom line, parkour is addicting, and if that's the kind of thing that keeps you entertained, then Only Up has you covered. I will say though, that the price is a little bit high for the level of complexity offered.

What took my by surprise, though, is that there is a story being narrated to you as progress. I could be wrong, but from what I've heard so far, it's almost as if the game itself is autobiographical. That in itself is very interesting to me as I've never thought of games as a medium having the capability take that narrative form. Even though the voice lines are delivered to you through what I assume is a recording the dictate button on google translate, it comes across as an earnest attempt to tell a life story through the scenery as well as the actually dictation. Again, I could be completely wrong and it could just be a made up story, but that's not the impression that I've gotten so far.

Anyways those are my thoughts about this game 0.6 hours in. I just fell all the way back to the beginning. Probably gonna give it another try. The elephant is an ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.\t\t\t\t\t
