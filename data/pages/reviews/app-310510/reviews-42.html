Deathtrap plays pretty much as it looks: a cross between Action RPGs like Torchlight and tower defense titles such as Orcs Must Die, with a co-op campaign playable solo or up to 4-players.

Played shortly after release, it could have done with a touch more testing to fix a few minor bugs and glitches, but otherwise the gameplay is solid.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a Deathtrap of personal time, social activity, and personal hygiene regiments. With more DLC planned including additional characters, this game still has a lot of things to look forward to atop an already complete and truly satisfying package. Very much recommended for Tower Defense-hybrid fans of games like Dungeon Defenders as well as anyone who enjoyed the defense of the base portion of The Incredible Adventures of Van Helsing 1 & 2.

\t
\t\t
🟦 What I like
\t\t
🟥 What I dislike
\t
\t
\t\t
+ Solid TD game taken from the devs' own Van Helsing games with RNG loot, extensive skill trees, and extensive trap upgrades
\t\t
- Some maps are way tougher than others (because of trap nodes you have to use)
\t
\t
\t\t
+ 3 classes, 15+ maps + 4 Campaign stages of difficulty for each (not including variable Endless and Scenarios) and optional side quests.
\t\t
- Some of the character skill options seem frivolous (it's overwhelming at first)
\t
\t
\t\t
+ Workshop support for user generated maps, Multiplayer Co-Op and Versus modes
\t\t
- A few technical issues still being reported (I didn't experience any myself)
\t

On the off chance you may have skipped the NeoCore Van Helsing games, the foundations for this game can be found in some of the base defense levels there - this game takes that formula and not only inverts it, but improves upon it - building a new game with tower defense as the focus and ARPG as the secondary objective. The tone of these games is a bit grimdark and snarky, and this carries over into the graphics and the fixed areas towers can be placed. Generally, you'll be finding gaps in your defense and fighting those mobs off manually during rounds before shoring up for the next round and doing again when new types of monsters, or elites, come through. I still feel like some of the options for upgrades are a bit much and could be condensed some, but workshop support can help users adjust their own experience down the road. I had a lot of fun with this one for a while, if it sounds like your thing, check it out.

\t
\t\t
Content/Replay
\t\t
High - There's a lot of traps, maps, characters and modifiers for just about everything that could reasonably have math attached to it. Each map also has a star-level grade influenced by your efficiency and the level clear time (and maybe other things). To reach the end, you'll probably have to do some grinding.
\t
\t
\t\t
Pass, Sale, Buy?
\t\t
Sale - As a tower defense game with some ARPG elements, it largely succeeds, but can feel like it needs some polish, difficulty tweaks, and RNG to be in your favor.
\t
\t
\t\t
Overall
\t\t
Score: 4.0 / 5.0 - Above Average, Deathtrap is a solid spin-off game from what was once side quest content itself, with plenty of content on offer.

What does this game play like?
Kingdom Rush

Do you know what Kingdom Rush is?
It's a basic Tower Defense game with a Hero.

However, Deathtrap expands upon Kingdom Rush.

Deathtrap makes your Hero more connected to your Towers or Traps.

Furthermore, there is a loot system that gives you more customization over your hero and an intuitive challenge in what to build your hero out of.

I recommend Deathtrap but for the price of $20.... is pretty steep. I think it's a $10 worthy game until they optimize the reward system more and the sluggish waves.\t\t\t\t\t
