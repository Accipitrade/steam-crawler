\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI spent like 200+ hrs on the GOG version and now about 50 hrs on the Steam version. This is one of my personal favourites along with Borderlands and Doom series. If you enjoy Diablo style games and Tower Defense games this is a must have. It has a mixture of gothic/cyberpunk atmosphere which is awesome. People complain about Deathtrap being grindy. Yes it can be but it doesn't have to if you follow my advice:

- To level up your character very fast play the first map (Darkmoor) in endless mode (you will earn an abundance of skill points by doing so). But you should do this after you are like halfway through Tier 1, Tier 2, etc. after you upgraded your traps and character to a respectable level or when you are stuck with one particular map.

- Don't try to level up all traps in your inventory equally but stick to only a few traps (one fully leveled up trap per enemy type e.g. Frost Pillar for Giant monsters should suffice).

- If one trap doesn't prove effective against monsters, don't hesitate to reset and redristribute your research points. Same applies for your characters skill points. It's a nice touch that the game allows you to do this. It is strongly advised that you readapt your character and traps in order to succeed in a particular map with flying colours.

- In order to earn max number of research points for your traps you have to finish the map/mission flawlessly that means without dying and a without a single gate breach. You can earn extra research points if you also finish the side quest before the last attack wave ends.

- Study monster types thoroughly on the mini map before each attack wave and setup your traps accordingly.

- Inbetween attack waves you should first upgrade the traps which are farthermost. You will not be able to upgrade them during attack waves since you will be near your gate defending it fiercely 90% of your time.

- The more essence you have the more traps you can built and upgrade during or inbetween waves. There are various methods to gather an abundance of essence e.g. essence traps. Make use of them !

- Salvage items you don't need from the beginning DON'T SELL THEM. You will get valuable gems in return. Those you will need later on in Tier 4 when you start receiving Godlike items. You can upgrade those Godlike items up to 10 times with those gems.

- Sure everbody has his/her own personal favourites but for me following combination of traps proved most effective: Frost Pillar, Icicle Field, Essence Trap, Voltaron Coil, Razor Launcher, Monster Cage & Winter Queen Sigil.

- I have not tried but I DON'T recommend playing this game with a controller, you should definitely use a mouse + keyboard combination.

- Last but not least. Always keep an eye on the Minimap during gameplay !

Hope it helps !

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen papa Diablo meets mama TD...

I was confused when I started the first map. Everything was unclear. Mechanic of building/upgrading tower was strange. My first 20 minutes of game time was negative. But I continued playing. And.. I liked it..

It reminds Dungeon Defenders with only difference that you control hero not by arrows, but by cursor. You have 3 hero for choosing: Archer, Warrior and Mage. Classic. I picked Sorcerer.

Your hero has HP and Mana, money for traps(towers) and some abilities. You start with autoattack (ice bolt), active skill (Lightning), kinda of ultimate with a long cooldown (attack all unit on the map) and 1 skill which buffs trap.
So, you place some traps, wave starts, enemies come, you are walking around and casting spell trying to not die. Pretty funny, actually.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=1176152079

When a level finishes you get some items for the hero and appear in the "city". There are some menus:
1) Skills. You get SP with each level where you can spend them. You can improve all skills. Some skills has 3 avaialble upgrades and some only 1. Each per can be learnt several times. So, customization is huge.
2) Traps. There are 15 traps at total. Each has 5 perks. First 3 unlock upgrade in the game. It means, if you learn upgrade in the city it gives nothing to the tower until you spend some gold to upgrade it in the battle. Each such upgrade has 3 levels. So, 9 upgrades for a tower at total. There are also 2 upgrade which increases characterstic for traps. They can be researched serveral times
3) Store and Inventory. Your hero has Weapon, Necklace, Ring, Belt, Armor and 3 rune slots. You can find items in the battle, craft them or buy in the store. There is a random generating item system like in the Diablo.

There are about 15 levels. Each has 4 stages. To start the next stage you need to pass all levels at the 1 stage. I spent 3-4 hours for passing 6 stages, so i suppose there is a lot of game time.

There are different types of monsters: usual, fast, flying, strong and slow, archers. There are also improved version of monsters with abilities (like in the diablo) and there is a boss in the end of each level.
Design of levels is different. So it's cool

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=1176653193

I really liked the game even after 5h of playing. But there is a huge flaw. The game crashes constanly and developers are not willing to do anything about it. I played 6th stage 5 attempts and it crashed 5 times. The last one was on the fight with the boss at the last level. I was tired of trying and deleted the game. I read reviews, there is A LOT of threads in the forum and reviews which told about it. And.... no replies from developers. The game is abandoned. I also read, achievements are not working and the game crashes on level 9 for AMD videocard holders. So, be aware. save your money

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=1176653298

Resume
I would give this game a positive rating because it's fun game, but I can't because the technical part of this game is terrible. I couldn't pass 6th mission, because it crashed 5 times in 5 attempts. So... I can't recommend it. Stay away.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr Tower Defense meets Diablo 3 meets 'meh'.

Deathtrap is not a horrible game. It isn't a particularly good one either.

+ challenging, but not unfair
+ skill has a real impact
+ huge variety in terms of character and trap development
- huuuuuuuge variety in terms of character and trap development. There are so many friggin traps and skills around, it is absolutely overwhelming. As is almost always the case in situations like this, it leads to...
- balance issues. Some combos and traps seem borderline gamebreaking, like the essence trap which lets you build and upgrade so many 'towers' it becomes boring
- very grindy. Items drop randomly and can have a huge impact.
- absolutely zero comma zero zero story

Considering the price tag of 20 Euros and the store full of amazing games, I cannot recommend this.\t\t\t\t\t

You can only place specific traps in specific limited locations (So your only decision is when to place a trap - not where or what type). Much of the "challenge" (if you call it that) is as a run-n-gun type game. And that run-n-gun has a wierd mechanic where you have "essence" that fuels your shots. When you run out you have to run and hide to allow it to regenerate. So you are never really in a fight very long. And your "towers" are all pretty much decided for you. There is a skill tree - but honestly didn't see it add anything to the game.\t\t\t\t\t
