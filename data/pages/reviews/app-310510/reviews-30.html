\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game. It's not orcs must die, but it's good. I'm more into "get in there and headshot my enemies" than "diablo-style clicking around", but after putting 50 hours into this I am only now starting to get bored (so it managed to keep my attention for this long!).
The good:
Decent trap ideas.
Fun challenges on higher levels of difficulty, such as "you can only build this one kind of trap! and we will send a ton of very specific enemies at you. Good luck."
Fun inventory management: collecting/buying items to boost hero skills and trap levels.
A ton of options for upgrades: every trap has 4 things to level up.
Amusing voice acting (that sorceress was so flustered and yet so driven!).
Side quests on missions.
Availability of PVP.
Level editor!
Good soundtrack.
Scenario mode allows players to increase map difficulty in specific ways for higher rewards, (i.e. "enemies have a 30% chance of respawning! but you also get a bonus to XP. Your choice.").

The not-so-good:
I prefer being able to choose which traps i place and where, but this game restricts you to a few pre-defined slots, and the slots are restriced to a specific type of trap (so you have to choose 1 out of 5). It's fun later on to be restricted, as it's a greater challenge, but it was kind of boring on the first playthrough. I wish there was more freedom, at least in the beginning.
Endless mode isn't much fun (this is where Orcs Must Die did much better). Here they use that stupid idea that to make a game more difficult one must exponentially increase the enemy life bar. So once you get to higher waves - the traps do pretty much nothing, and i personally got really bored sitting there clicking on the same little nobody (the guy that should die in one hit), waiting for him to die for like 5 minutes.
Gets repetitive as there are only 13 campaign maps and then you just replay them 4 times on higher difficulties. (Yes, there are also 5 more maps that open up after 1st playthrough, but you can only do them in Scenario-mode.)

Anyway, overall it's a good buy if you're into tower defense and don't mind Diablo combat.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGet it if you enjoy tower defense games and diablo playstyle. Even though there are only 15ish maps. There's tiers of those maps along with survival and scenario with plenty of rewards to challenge you. The game itself is like dungeon defenders 1 & 2. however the enemies don't attack your towers (a few bosses do but they only temporarily disable them). So for me this is a great break from it. But because the enemies dont attack your towers. that means you have more micromanagement to deal with the enemies that are going to get through your defenses.

My complaints on the game are pretty minor and only a few. the music gets pretty repetitive. So you might find yourself turning it off. The controls feel right but every so often I know I hit a hotkey to use an ability but it wont work until I slap it again. Sometimes the enemies will cut corners on a turn making your floor traps ineffective.

Overall i'm enjoying it thoroughly so far and would definitely recommend it to others.

EDIT: I should give fair warning. at tier 3 stage 4 you will encounter a MASSIVE difficulty increase. Other players have been complaining about this issue for over a year now but Neocore refuses to change it. All other maps prior to tier 3 are fun, smooth and gradual difficulty that push you just right. the other maps after tier 3 stage 4 and on feel like "here's a ridiculous challenge now beat it" scenario. You end up cussing at the game more than you have fun. buy deathtrap but only if it's on sale.

Edit2: After not playing it for ~3.5 years and playing through it again. Stage 4 tier 3 is still too difficult. You are given medium level and giant enemies (both of which have lots of hp), you can only play on hard difficulty at tier 3 and you are given very little essence even with all character upgrades that increase starting essence and lower tower costs. There are still players complaining about the difficulty of the stage after all this time. Changing this review to negative due to the developer's unwillingness to fix it. I've also realized that you only get to play about 50% of the game up to this point. Still buy this only if its on sale. Because I am changing it to negative steam is clearing the number of people that have found this review helpful (which is 6 people).

Rather than just complain about the issue here are 3 potential fixes for stage 4 tier 3.

1. Starting essence needs a MASSIVE buff. This is probably the easiest and laziest fix.

2. Heavily cut down on the 1st wave's enemies. Either by removing the giants or halving the total enemies altogether. I think this solution is the most reasonable change. It will allow the stage to be beaten but keep it a challenge.

3. Remove the flying enemy's alternate routes and remove all 4 gates being opened on the first wave. This is probably the least favorable. The issue of there still being too many enemies and not enough starting essence still exist but at least you don't have to worry about 4 separate directions.

Also some enemies still try to cut corners still making some floor traps useless. This issue still exists ~3.5 years later further justifying this change to a negative review. It makes it feel like abandonware.\t\t\t\t\t
