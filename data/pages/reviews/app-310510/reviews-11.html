\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn the one hand, this game is better than the total reviews might indicate (while some much more superficial TD games have 98%'s)... On the other hand, it's much worse than some of the individual reviews might indicate...

It's good, overall - I'll spend some time on it, because it does have a wide range of various skills, playstyles, etc.

Really, the pain point for this is the huge missed opportunity. I do hope that a more competent developer takes some notes and takes this to the next level. I can't see this team fleshing out the concept properly in a sequel. There's just too much that they clearly didn't dig into and should have. Most skills are very superficial, most trap upgrades are obvious. It's such a cool idea, and great to see someone doing an ARPG/TD hybrid like this, but it fails as either and only succeeds at both by default.

The traps are not particularly well-balanced (certainly no worse than DG1, though), but the classes are terribly poorly-balanced. My first playthrough, I banged my head against a map I couldn't beat, and I'm a TD fanatic. Finally, I tried switching from the sorceress to the warrior, and went from hard mode to easy mode, just by playing a tank. Like with, say, Orcs May Die, the mobs will stop their path to destruction and engage the character if it's nearby, so having a tank, you just stand on a damage-over-time trap, near a gun, and tank mobs while the towers destroy them. Easy mode. But I'm enjoying it and will keep playing it. It's no DG2, but mostly because it just doesn't have the breadth of options. Who's ever heard of a TD game where you can't set the mode for tower targeting (e.g. Strongest, closest, farthest, etc.)? Cool that they have loot drops and items and all, but the item diversity is severely lacking, so you can't really create a build, you just pick up the highest level of each item and move on. It's a great idea with a lot of missed opportunities, but a solid recommendation for TD fans who can get over the disappointment.

Also, holy wow do the voices and the scripting suck bad. I've never felt the need to complain about this before, even in hastily-translated games and low-budget titles. Really, truly awful voice-overs. Turn 'em off...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought Red Dead Redemption 2 and this around at the same time. While one is a masterfully crafted piece of art, the other is a fun game. This one is the fun game as you might have suspected. While RDR2 looks amazing, i will come back to it from time to time reluctantly. I finished the first round (Tier1) of the campaign of Deathtrap at hard difficulty in about 12 hours and never had to think twice. I think i only took 2 breaks. The graphics are fine, and dont distract in any way from the addicting and linear gameplay. This is how a tower defense game should be. You can choose your skill and trap path yourself, and the various difficulty options let you play like you want WHILE giving a clearcut 'standard' way of play (see above). You don't have to change anything and can start right away. It's easy to understand and hella fun. 0 Bugs, 0 problems, nice explanation of enemies and mechanics in game (encyclopedia and tooltips). With all that said, the 'story' gives a frame that is exactly of the scope it should be. It makes you feel immersed while not being overly dramatic.

It's not an epic masterpiece like RDR 2 and many other games want to be. It's a limited but immensely fun game. This. is. perfect.\t\t\t\t\t
