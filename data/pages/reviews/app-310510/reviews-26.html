\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeathtrap is a tower defense ARPG based on the tower defense part of the Van Helsing series. If you liked that part of Van Helsing, you'll probably like this. The difference is that there is no open world here; the game is played in progressive stages where you get a set amount of essence (the main resource) at the beginning of each to build and upgrade towers, and you get more within each stage from killing enemies. Also like Van Helsing, you still play a character of one of three classes, get gold, loot, experience at the end of each stage, and upgrade points for completing each stage depending on the difficulty and how well you did, as well as for completing quests within each stage. The character development is fairly similar to Van Helsing; you get three skill points per level to spend on class skills. The upgrade points are separate and upgrade your traps individually. Both can be respecced for gold, and there is also a store in between levels.


Pros:

+There is a lot of content (13 stages x 4 tiers, the same map on a different tier isn't just higher level versions of the same stuff, but a completely different layout of traps, enemies, and quest objectives.) There are also optional scenario-only maps and player-made maps. Scenarios are stages where you are able to set certain difficulty modifiers in exchange for increased gold, exp, and loot.

+Decently interesting skill synergy between the character skill upgrade and trap upgrade points system.

+Relatively unique game; there aren't many tower defense games that have as much focus on character growth. Dungeon defenders is the only similar game I can think of.

Cons:

-The experience system. Any map more than a level or two below your current level gives no experience at all. This is particularly annoying given that players will probably be going back to several stages because they were unable to complete it on hard, complete a quest, or otherwise get all the upgrade points initially. Scenario mode gives almost no experience, even with the +90% exp difficulty modifiers on, regardless of which map you use. Endless mode gives fair experience for the first few waves, but then exponentially too much experience after that. Playing to wave 8 gave me about 60k xp. To wave 25, gave me 700k exp (leveled from 81 to 90). To wave 36, 1.8 million exp (leveled from 50 to 80). The maximum level is 100.

-Some stages are just too long. Earlier stages only take a few minutes, some later ones take 25-40+. Endless mode should have an option to start at any wave you've reached before, as it takes several hours (even at x2 speed) of just waiting to reach the higher waves otherwise, or alternatively have a much higher speedup function. A high level character can get all the essence they need to fully build out a map in one or two waves, and then do nothing for 20-30+ waves until the enemies get strong enough that they need to play.

TL;DR overall, I would rate 8/10. Tweak the exp system so that lower level maps have maybe a penalty but not a strict 0% cutoff, make endless mode exp linear and not exponential, and make scenarios give a lot more exp (in line with doing a current level map + modifier), and it would be better.\t\t\t\t\t
