\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't often write reviews but this game might seem enticing to some but I'll just tell you outright: Don't buy it unless it's on sale. It isn't a $20 game. It's not even finished really. I play it with my girlfriend but that's kinda what carries a lot of the fun.

The actual review:

First couple noticeable things when you start the game: 3 classes with no customization, not much variety there. And a big giant DLC button that's grayed out because they gauged market interest to figure out if they'll actually finish it or not and they said "nah". It's a bad sign when you slap a DLC button on your home screen then never make DLC for it. The RPG elements are okay. The lack of build variety kinda kills it though. After about level 15 you notice you have a choice of one of two builds per character. Tower focus or DPS focus. Towers are alright. About half of them do have interchangeability and have synergy with other towers so that's fun for planning. There's a bit of trend of most systems being a bit "half baked". Lots of time padding. Levels have the usual survival modes, custom difficulty scenarios and what not, as well as being able to play the game 4 times over with new challenges. But that's not really making a game longer to me, that's making up for things you didn't have the time or money to make so you changed a few parameters to squeeze a little more juice out of your lemon. And most of these things could have been saved by a more broad modding API or custom maps. "There are custom maps though!" I hear you yelling. Yes, except they removed any and all incentive to play them which I will address next.

The biggest sin is that they gutted enemies dropping loot and experience, really killing a major ARPG feel of the game. It could have really allowed for a Diablo feel but it just isn't there anymore. People made Workshop maps with first party tools that allowed for easy experience and item farming. One of my biggest pet peeves is when a developer makes their game, and people play it a way that they didn't intend, so they just gut their own game and say "You aren't playing the game the way -I- wanted you to play, so we're taking away the system entirely so you behave". It really frustrates me. Take the L and let people have their fun. There's just no reason to play Workshop maps then. The game is so shallow the gameplay itself isn't enough to keep it afloat.

In summary great idea, however it's a poor, unfinished execution. Buy it for $5 or less and play it with your friends.\t\t\t\t\t

In my opinion 20€ is a bit too much for this, especially after 6 years of release. Get it on sale only.\t\t\t\t\t
