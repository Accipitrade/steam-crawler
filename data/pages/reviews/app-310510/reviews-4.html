\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDeathtrap

Van Helsing movie mixed in a Orcs Must Die Tower defense game



Pros -

#1.3 characters to pick from

The all are very different so try them all

#2.Many traps

#3.Multiple difficulty levels

#4.Cooperative


Cons -

#1.It is very GRINDY

You have to replay levels you already beat to get the money and experience

#2.You need a computer and monitor that is newer than 2015 and the higher end the better

I had to get a new monitor and update my graph card or drivers to get it to run properly and before I did that it would crash.

#3.Some levels are designed for cooperative and will not be that fun solo unless you character is so OP or the difficulty is set low enough

#4.There is no barricade type trap to force them to change paths/direction so that is unique for a TD game

#5.There is limits on what type of traps and where you can use them



The Bottom Line -


Deathtrap is an ok TD game.I would give it a 6.5 or 7 out of 10.I would say it is worth about $5.


If you like Deathtrap I recommend


https://store.steampowered.com/app/201790/Orcs_Must_Die_2/

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother case of Steam needing a "maybe" thumb.

The game is a tower defense game that employ's an interesting mesh of TD and top down dungeon crawling play style. The towers/traps themselves are fun in concept and in most executions, The levels are simple in design and graphics. The gameplay is interesting and fun on the easy and normal difficulty's, and the UI is simple enough to get the basics down though mastery will take awhile.

I found the campaign to be entertaining and the character combat to be fun...ish, The "Traps" are kinda simplistic and get a little repetitive after a while. I was not expecting much having gotten it for 90% off and I was suprised at the amount of idea's and content here, even if it is a little bare bones and unpolished.



Now the bad:
The difficulty spike in going from normal to hard on the campaign isn't bad but the spikes going from campaign to any of the other play modes is absolutely insane. Part of this is due to an actual difficulty increase and another part is due to the utter LACK of resources needed to build and build up your traps, and if your traps aren't good enough, AND plentiful enough you will lose because your character is absolutely a wet paper bag in a hurricane. ALSO the rewards given for completeling levels/campaign etc are EXTREMELY low, you will find yourself choosing between EITHER leveling all traps sorta equally OR maxing a FEW towers.

The above being said the play-modes OTHER then campaign levels are NOT intended for anything less then a MAXED character and trap set, not sure how you get there without playing those levels but yeah. The "Character creation" is you selecting wizard, knight, or archer... but that's it, you cant change anything about your character not even the color.

This last point is more a pet peeve of mine then a real negative. The control style annoys the ever living hell out of me! even changing the key binds for movement my mouse will still move the Character unless I hold shift and that's just really annoying. I'm trying to kill something and suddenly my character is running into a group of enemies and dying. I HATE clicked based moving in anything other then a Myst style game. ALSO the freaking cursor is the size of a ant...seriously I've lost COUNTLESS lightening strikes due to this damn thing and the movement style. If you're going to make a TINY golden arrow that looks steampunkish then it should go with out saying that half the textures used in half the levels SHOULD NOT be the same color/texture, especially when you've no auto-lock, or aim assistance at ALL and it is critical to aim your shots! losing a perfect round at the last second because I lost the cursor against the same colored level texture really SHOULDN'T have been a thing...that's what beta testing is for.

In conclusion, this game is fun but it needs to be re-balanced and optimized, there's no reason for graphics this basic my 2070 super should be maxing out at a resolution of 2160 x 1440. Also you may want to increase the rewards for the levels as you can't even fill a map with basic towers on what you make from the waves, meaning there is no way to diversify game play because there is only ONE strategy that works.
