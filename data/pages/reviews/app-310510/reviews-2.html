\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBased on The Incredible Adventures of Van Helsing Franchise of games, those having played these can look forward to an expanded version of the Tower Defence Mini Game. If you into Action RPG Character Progression (which fundamentally effects games play), as well as comprehensive Tower Defence (including upgrade/buff progression), then this is a must play. The game also has a sizeable map pool - I am 40.3 hours in and no map has yet become boring. The music is good, especially the Battle Symphony, though it would be nice to have included a few more tracks.

The game is not without its flaws - Some redundant/broken menu options (I use Controller), but none of these are game breaking in anyway. More a case of quality of life effecting, but simple work arounds exist. I did have 2 game ending crashes (screen freezes, while game runs in the background - PC restart was required), but 2 crashes in 40+ hours of game play is really not a big deal in my books.

Note to the Developers: A similar game with next gen graphics and refined games systems (including more Hero Based content), set in the Warhammer 40K Universe would do well I am sure ;)\t\t\t\t\t

NOT BAD, NOT AWESOME EITHER

Disclaimer: This is my first proper tower defense game I played, so can't compare to other titles. But I had fun with it.

Neocore had a really good idea of mixing ARPG with Tower Defense. I guess mostly because they already had assets ready. Sadly, they did not go through with it all the way.

You have 3 classes of heroes to choose from (melee fighter, ranged marksman or sorceress), about 20 towers/traps and 13 levels, each with 4 difficulties while each adds a little twist to the same old map, to keep it fresh. I tried mercenary first but found one level impossible to beat. So I picked up sorceress with global damage spell and game was ez pez from there.

You level up your traps and characters ARPG style which is very nice, and if you need some extra levels, you have scenario or endless modes for that. You can increase different difficulty stats for better EXP gain, more gold or better item drops which is fantastic.

Where the game falls short is the stuff that devs clearly ran out of time or money for. Shop in this game is absolutely useless and never sells anything useful. Item drops are poor and so random it is hard to find good gear piece. You also have crafting here, but it is very basic, also heavily RNG oriented and worst part is that you can drop schematics for crafting that you already have - or which are for different class!

Enemy variety is kinda small, but at least you can quickly pick up what unit it is and what kind of trap will counter it.

Overall I sank 66 hours into it so it clearly did a lot of things right. If you ever see it on sale and like tower defense games, I would recommend grabbing it.\t\t\t\t\t
