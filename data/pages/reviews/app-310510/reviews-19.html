\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, FYI, this is a first impression review, I have only played for a little while. That said, I have high hopes, it looks pretty good.
For starters, I bought the game because I have a fondness for the tower defense genre. As a straight up tower defense game, this seems pretty weak. The locations towers can be placed are pre-determined, though as you unlock more towers you sometimes get a choice of tower types at a location. In general however, there is little strategy and no tactical depth to the the towers. There is also no pathing, and the tower upgrades seem less than exciting.
On the plus side, there is still a strong tactical component in the game, as you get to play one of three character classes and you are on the map during the waves, fighting them yourself alongside the towers. This part seems quite fun, which is good, because it is the bulk of what the game seems to be about.
The up-grades seem good for the characters though, with a store and a basic crafting. I like that the character in game changes with the armor and items I have equipt myself with. This is perhaps the strongest part of the game.
Again, on the negative side, there was originally a way to play with other people, this appears to be gone, from what I have read in the other comments.
On the plus side, the sound is excellent, the voice work solid, the visuals are really good and the animation is pretty smooth.
That said, I find the controls confusing (hold down the left button to move?) Oddly enough some very basic control information comes very late in the tutorial, by which I mean several levels in. Do yourself a favor and check the control options. Not only can you change the movement controls to include WASD, but there is a lot more in there.
I think the biggest confusion is the sense of atmosphere. There was clearly a lot of effort put into the world building and backstory. Each combat area comes with the flavor text, but it doesn't seen to transfer into adding anything to the game play. The encyclopedia fails to give any real depth to the monsters, which seems a major faux pas, which adds to this lack of immersion. They crate some really interesting monsters, which seem to have deep backstories, but to date there is no way to see them.

I will be honest, I got this on sale, and while it is well worth the few bucks I paid, I wouldn't think even five bucks was a fair price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA surprisingly good game, Deathtrap's story isn't going to rock your world, but the gritty environments, wretched creatures, and explosions definitely will. The soundtrack is very limited, but also very excellent; the voice acting is mostly good; the monsters range from mythological beasts to disgusting creations of flesh and metal.

More than just a tower defense game, the player has the ability to choose between the classic A-RPG sort of ranged/melee/magic classes in order to augment the defenses. Further, each character has access to trap skills, class skills, a market, and crafting. Points and loot are awarded at the end of each game, which you will use to improve your character's capabilities. These can be reset as needed, and gear is lootable (at the end of the game), purchasable and craftable with gold being a standard currency, and diamond, emerald, topaz, and ruby being used in crafting as well. Bonus rewards are available for defeating certain wild NPCs in a game.

As for the towers, there are various tower bases, each with a host of unlock and upgrade options that behave in certain ways and effect certain enemies, (hordes, armored, etc.). For instance, spawn traps can be unlocked to release werewolves, archers, suicide attackers, spawn ghouls from the dead, etc. And, of course, these traps reward the player with lots of little chunks of monsters, giving the game a very Diablo-eque feel. The traps are purchased using in-match currency known as essense, which is slowly replenished by killing the invaders.

To make things short and simple, what is the moral of the story? Do you like Tower Defense games? Do you like dark fantasy? Are you okay with some Action RPG gameplay to augment the tower defense? If the answers are mostly "Yes", then welcome home. Just be warned, this is not a casual "sit back and listen to some music/talk radio" tower defense game- it can get intense, so don't expect this to be a game to relax to.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn interesting merge of tower defense and RPG game, although the latter is quite simplistic.

The campaign is rather short - 13 maps doable in under 10h - however there is a lot of replayability. You can play the campaign 3 more times on increased difficulty (with different enemies, paths, towers available - only the base map is the same). Furthermore, after completing the campaign 6 extra maps become available. And all of them can be played in custom mode (where you can increase difficulty for extra rewards) and in endless mode.

There are two types of skill points:
  • skill points for your character's skill - you always gain experience and level up like in any RPG game
  • research points for improving traps - these you get only after completing the campaign maps, depending on the number of stars you acquired and occassional side quests

The latter is the part that I dislike the most, as it seems to me that I won't be able to max out all the traps. I am using only 8 out of 35 available traps, and to use others, I would have to spend a lot of gold to reassign the research points.

On top of these 35 researchable traps, there are also plenty of special towers that can be sometimes built on some maps.

There are occasional freezes and crashes (about once in 5 hours) and some bugs (such as non-working borderless window mode) remain unfixed for years.\t\t\t\t\t
