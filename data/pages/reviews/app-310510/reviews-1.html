\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYeah a negative is probably too harsh given you can get this real cheap in either a bundle or on a sale these days but can't say it amazed me, certainly not long term.
It's a strange but interesting mesh of a tower defense and a diablo-like with also alike aesthetics. It's certainly unique but can't say I found its design ideas as positives.
We can set traps only in very specific places and only specific types those spots dictate limiting our options. Outside of the missions we also can put in upgrade points into specific traps narrowing what is worth using even further. Thus the bulk of the killing we will do on our own in an ARPG fashion where we do have abilities, items to equip, stuff to craft but then given we're clearing mindless hordes just charging onward it more feels like sweeping. Perhaps it would feel much better if the progression wasn't so dearly slow. While the max level of a character is 100 getting there will require beating every mission at least 4 times...and this is what the game wants us to do. The campaign wasn't very thrilling and asks for redoing it multiple times over so you can hit some kind of character build at last. Given the grim setting it's shocking there is almost no story in here, our character might say something there and there but it just feels like doing same few levels for arcade vibe with only implied stakes making everything feel shallow and fails to captivate. By the time I dropped this game 3 years ago in 2nd campaign run I was thoroughly bored with the loop and little options as was my level was just 30 and didn't look back until now. Trying to come back to old save is a massive wall of systems that serve little practical purpose in the experience and sense of pointlessness as there was no plot to rekindle either.

Perhaps if the game had some plot to not waste the potential of the setting and level design, allowed more freedom with traps and rebalanced skill/upgrade systems so it takes less points to max something out or simply allow faster levelling then it could become something more engaging and memorable. As it is it's a good impression quickly turning into a meaningless slog.
Maybe it's more fun in coop but game seems rather unpopular. It's also severely completion-unfriendly with online achievements, forever taking milestones and even containing broken ones.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a role-playing hero, flattening hordes of monsters and letting the rest run into our masterfully constructed tower defense - Deathtrap takes the entertaining defensive interludes from The Incredible Adventures of Van Helsing and knits an entire game out of them.
You don't have to have played Van Helsing to have fun with Deathtrap, though. It takes place in the same scenario, but there are no story references. Especially because Deathtrap does without any plot after the short intro anyway. Monsters want to push out of a parallel world into reality, we have to keep them away from a portal, and that's it.
It's actually a pity, the charming main characters were one of Van Helsing's strengths - in Deathtrap, on the other hand, we control uninspired standard grimaces. But in terms of gameplay, the mixture of trap building and "hack & slay" fights has lost none of its appeal: As a warrior, marksman or sorceress, we lard our labyrinth with death traps and jump into the breach ourselves when the enemy threatens to break through to the portal.\t\t\t\t\t
