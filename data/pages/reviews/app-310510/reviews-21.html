\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could recommend this. I really wish I could. But as it stands, eh.

So, Deathtrap is an Isometric ARPG Tower Defense hybrid where you get to play a hero and also build towers and traps to stop monsters from entering your base. This reminds me a lot of Warcraft 3 custom maps which I adored and thus I had to give this a try.

And it's really fun! Lots of traps and towers to choose from, a good bit of customization for your 3 classes through skills n gear, and solid core gameplay. It also looks and sounds alright, so that's cool.

Sadly, this is where the whole thing takes a turn for the worse. The game has bugs. A lot of them. Enemies get stuck in walls, the game crashes for a set of specific graphics cards on a few select maps unless you start RIGHT AWAY with no build time, and there's a good bunch more I won't bother to list here.

And the worst part, the game has been abandoned by the developers who jumped ship to the next game. I suppose Deathtrap wasn't profitable but from what I can gather they used to listen to all the comments they got, fixed stuff etc. and one day later stopped completly and just made more games.

I dislike supporting devs that don't deliver a finished and polished product but I can suck that up if the devs promise to fix that issue. Sadly, this probably won't happen and as such I can not recommend Deathtrap.

If the game does work for you, cool. It's really fun and I wish the devs would care more about it. But with it not working for a buddy and additional glitches popping up here and there it's hard to recommend this in fear that it'll just not work for you, either.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLots of potential but two main flaws ruined it for me.

1. Poor controls. Click to move games should always run towards the mouse cursor when you hold down the mouse button, not try to path to the location the mouse cursor is over. For example if you're holding down mouse1 to run upward and your mouse cursor passes over a wall or chasm, you suddenly change direction and start trying to find a path to the location on the other side of that wall. In a level with lots of obstacles this is infuriating. And it is impossible to run and shoot at the same time.

2. You can only place towers in a few predetermined locations. There are maybe 3 or 4 places on each map where you can place each type of tower. And towers that have synergy such as towers that weaken against physical damage almost never come right before towers that do physical damage so the synergy can never be realized. Severely limiting tower locations in a tower defense game is such a bizarre design choice. It removes all the creativity and fun from the TD genere for no apparent reason.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a fun little tower defense game. This game is easiest 2p. The maps change when there are 3+ players in-game, making 3p the most challenging, and 4p rather easy.

The randomly-generated loot is ridiculously frustrating, because so much of what drops is complete trash, and the odds of getting a rune that fits your build are incredibly slim.

The game moves at a glacial pace, and some enemies even force the game to slow down!

Many of the Steam Achievements are broken in this game, which can be frustrating. Reaching out to the developers got a "We're working on it!" response, but it has been over three months since then.

If the Steam Achievements were fixed, and the in-game store were tweaked a bit such that it could be refreshed/re-rolled, I would recommend this game. As it is, I can't recommend it strongly. If you're into Tower Defense, this can be a nice co-op experience, but definitely pick it up on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
