\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a combination of ARP (torchlight, diablo, van helsing game people made this one) & tower defense gameplay. It's pretty fun, and allows you to create your own personal maps which can also grant you more xp and gold helping you to build your character between campaign levels.

There are two main progression areas for each character. The character's personal skills and skill levels for combat and buffs, and the character's trap points to invest in different kinds of traps. The trap points are earned by beating official maps at different difficulty levels. Character levels and skill points can also be earned in workshop maps, at least when I played last about a month and a half or so ago (in early access).

Creating your own map seems intimidating at first but you get the hang of the wave & group creator pretty quickly and placing trap tiles and shaping out the map itself is very easy. Some very cool shapes and types of waves and defenses are possible from the map maker and you can upload/download maps with the workshop easily. I want to see some maps shaped like the subscreen maps in the original legend of zelda peoples. Please do dis.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttldr; The game concept would be fun, but the controls just make it so damned frustrating to play.

The game concept is nice, similar to dungeon defenders, really. The problem, however, is that its leveling goals are so lofty that each individual powerup you place doesn't feel all that useful. The biggest issue, though, by far is the controls. The keyboard controls involve, what I suspect, is having 3 hands. While you can rebind the keys it doesn't feel much better. By default movement is the arrow keys and skills are the number keys, and, strange enough, some are bound to the mouse as well. The controler buttons are not spelled out in the tutorials at all, so it took me a hell of a long time to figure out which gamepad control did what when navigating the menus, not everything is selectable via the joystick, but rather a combination of left and right bumpers and triggers both, a very odd setup to be sure. Now, when you get all of that down, the targeting is even still much worse. The melee fighter isn't so bad, but the mage has a hell of a time facing the way you want her to, and her lightning spell, which eminates out towards her facing and hits multiple targets, tends to be ever so slightly off center and instead of getting a whole group, you wind up hitting just a couple monsters. Her tower buff spell, as well, seems to activate on nothing at all, and feels wasted, or it will activate on a tower i didn't think I had selected (selection happening by running near the towers).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is just a temporary 1st impression using Marksman up to lv 16/100.

So far, for me at least, this is the best TD game I have ever played. An insane amount of character and trap customization. Char customization comes from exp levels and loot whereas trap customization comes from gaining trap points from completing new stages, completing quests, passives from character skill points, and char gear. There could be more than I know about because I am only lv 16/100 on Marksman. Multiple Marksman can be spec'd completely differently so it adds a lot to co-op even if players are the same class. There is also trap synergizing going on. Lots of it! I will write a more detailed review once i have experienced more of the game.

Bottomline.....if you like Tower Defence games then there is a lot to love here. More than I currently can comprehend.\t\t\t\t\t

+ Flexible difficulty

Cons:

~ Lots of upgrades (thus lots of grinding)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is actually brilliant. Being a fan of tower defence games, and Games like Sanctum/Sanctum 2, i have to say that this game is right up there with the other games.

Gamplay
8-10
The gameply is fun, and addicting, each match can last from 10 minutes to half an hour and probably even longer in the harder levels. the gamplay is quite simple. just defend a portal that leads to the real world. waves of enemy are trying to invade the real world using portals and the only way to stop them is by setting up traps and towers, along with firghting yourself. there are currently 3 classes the sorceress who is able to use magic to defend all incoming lanes at once or is able to quickly take down/defend one lane really quickly, the ranger who uses guns and distance weapons * havn't played much of him yet) and a heavily armoured character(can't remember what class he is and again havn't played him much) Each of these classes have differeent skills that you level as you progress. Deathtrap alos has an rpg element as you can roam around the levels as the defending stage is going on and defeat map bosses and gain keys to chest littered throuout the stage.

Graphics
7/10
truthfully, i enjoy the graphics at first glance i did not, and thought they were not that good, but in game the look decent. it complements the game completely. The blood and effects are all quite nice and allow for the gamplay to look better.

Story
2/10
So far from what i know the story is simplistic in nature. the gist is that you are in these ancient ruins ( or something like that, again i havn't played through the begining cinematic/information more then once) to which the humans defended agains monsters centuries beforehand. and as you are in these ruins the monsters apear again and it is up to you to stop them from reaching the portals. ( again i will probably update this review the more i play)

Overall
7/10
So far it is a brilliant game, i am yet to try the co op experiance but i am sure it will make it even better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: Combine a classic tower defense game with a version of Diablo, where there are less item drops. Already runs great in Early Access!


I read about this game in a german news-magazine for video games. It sounded interesting, so I decided to give it a try. This is my short resumee after playing for roughly 8 hours, so this review is subject to change, just like the game aswell!

Gameplay:
Imagine a mixture between classic tower defense games and Diablo. Now imagine only 5% of the loot drops and a lighter character menu. Additionally imagine upgradeable towers, where you first have to unlock these upgrades by spending skill points on them, then you have to buy the upgrades on each map again when placing the tower. There're set places where to build the traps. The gameplay is really well working, insanely fluently and feels great, charging by it this game wouldn't need to call itself "Early Access".

Maps:
The maps all look different and you need certain tactics if playing on hard in order to beat them. The variety is not that big at the moment (like 10 maps or so), but there're more to come. Additionally, there's modding!

Items:
Works like Diablo, with smaller drop chances. Also, mainly bosses and giant enemies will drop items.

Characters:
There are three different classes, a melee-fighter, a sorceress and an unreleased one. You can only customise them by finding different looking gear.

What I didn't check out by now:
-Modded maps
-Coop-mode
-Versus-mode

I hope this short review will help you decide wether to buy Deathtrap or not.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\texcellent tower defence with lots of options for difficulty that makes replays both refreshing and genuinely fun. as well as having a really rich rpg element.

PROS
+ many different towers to choose from and ways to unlock more
+ rpg aspects, loot, items, character skills/stats, and crafting. the items and crafting are really weak right now in early access but the ground work they have is excellent. as soon as they implement more stuff its really going to shine
+ 2 different classes of characters (3 total at launch) all able to use all types of towers
+ single player co-op and pvp, the pvp is a very neat and exciting mode something i think i will enjoy a lot more once the full game launches and things become more polished
+ the level pacing vs difficulty, really fantastic how well the dev's have paced your character progess to match with the levels and tower implementation (when you unlock certain types of towers) ive felt at times i was too over powered for a certain level but there was very rarely a time that i moved on to either the next level or next difficulty and felt that it was no challenge at all
+ the diversity of enemies. at first i found the types of mobs to be a bit bland, but as more mini bosses and bosses started to appear with special traits the diversity really exploded. your going to run into some unexpected outcomes from the way some of them interact with each other.

CONS
i really only have one complaint with the game so far and it seems to be a running issue with this dev's games.. that would be the multiplayer, granted its early access so i assume its going to get a lot better when full launch comes around. but even so i can see a lot of the same troubles van helsing 2 had with its multi in that its a little convoluted not stream lined in the least ya kinda gotta jump through some hoops. if they can do a thing or two to optimise its multiplayer id have to say this is my hands down favorite tower defense.\t\t\t\t\t
