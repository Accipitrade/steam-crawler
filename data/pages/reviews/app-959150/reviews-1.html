\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is really quite terrible. Nothing is intuitive. You can balance your economy, hit end turn, and suddenly your coal goes from +5 a turn to -20. I didn't build anything, no trades were canceled, war isn't even going so my trade routes aren't being raided. Every resource fluctuates wildly like that. I played the first 100 hours of the game and more of a logic puzzle, trying to figure out what makes things do what. Even when it lists it: 10 coal and 10 metal make 10 steel, at least half the time there's another resource it needs that isn't listed. The war itself is pretty easy, just build endless artillery and be invincible. Almost no strategy involved.

Your allies will always hate you. The enemy is taking their territories and you want to help, but they will refuse to give you military access to their death. So you take back a province, liberate it, then have to vacate cause they just get pissed you're on their land. Why can't allies have military access? That seems like the simplest ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ thing to program in, and it would eliminate so many headaches.

On the bright side, I have an achievement for making it to turn 10 (ten!) and only 7% of players have it. All of my achievements have gold borders with 1 and 2% achievement rates, so it makes me feel like I'm super great at this.

In the end, I recommend this game. It is really quite terrible, that's true, but it's still one of my favorites. Just a great little laid back WWI strategy game, that requires heavy managing of the economy. If nothing else, you can get hours out of it as a very complex logic problem. At 200 hours I've only figured out how things work just barely enough to play the game as intended.\t\t\t\t\t

1. a Russo- Japanese War Scenario and a Boer War Scenario would round out the period nicely.
2. 4. An option to negotiate peace or an alliance with the exchange of specific territories.
5. I wish regions could have a majority and minority nationality and ethnic group and an expansion of the options that can be set up as new nations or protectorates.
