\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played a lot of Grand Strategy games previously, mostly Paradox Games, but this is my first of the Making History games.

The good:
- Industry is a lot of fun to manage. The resource, factory, infrastructure systems interlock well.
- Combat
- Events

The bad:
- minor inconvenience with diplomacy. Allies not letting giving me land access. Playing as Austria-Hungary I was conquering Greece land. The land was given to the Ottomans so I was in Ottoman land yet they wouldn't give me access. Created a situation where i had to leave my artillery in occupied Bulgaria and press Greece with infantry. Fixed it by loading in as Ottomans and giving Aust-Hung access, saving, than reloading.

The Ugly:
- Both campaigns i have been playing have ended with "Out of Memory" errors occurring every turn sometime in 1915.

I still recommend it as the bugs are bringing the game down but what do you expect in early access? So long as the bugs are left behind in early access.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the time being, thumbs up, awaiting fixes.

Well the early access definitely has some bugs. I'm hoping that they get fixed in short order. Let me describe how I got to them.

I played about 80 turns as the United States. Found that while the in game tutorial tries to explain a lot, but it is sorely lacking in explaining just how to do things you want to do, like producing units, or how to load them onto transports. The GUI is not very intuitive, but I did find that the looking up the same questions asked about their previous iteration "Making History: The Great War" answered the questions, as both games share the same GUI mechanics. Got semi-blindsided by Pancho Villa as I found that you really have to outnumber your opponent by 7 or more to one in order resolve a battle in less than six weeks with few casualties.

So I tried again, this time, knowing how to do much of what I wanted to do in advance, and not repeating the same mistakes I made the first time around. Had most of my troops massed on the Mexican border when Villa pulled his raid, so I was able to move en-mass to quash the threat before the Germans could exacerbate the situation. This time the Constitutionalist did not stand by with such a large punitive force on their territory and ordered me to leave before the job was done. This would simply not do! Told the to forget it and attacked them as well, pouring across the border. So now, along with the Zapatistas I rolled them up in short order while the Federales stood by and toasted marshmallows. Finally after I defeated the last vestiges on the tip of Baja and annexed all their territory only to have the game crash and not be able to continue. Sigh!

The debug log is useless, only stating that the client could not connect to the server (maybe because it locked up)!?!

UPDATE: Finally replayed past the Mexican Intervention and the game is still going. This go round Villa's raid took place a good 6 months earlier than last time. Also things have gone sideways in Europe. The Germans took Paris, then lost it as well as most of France. Belgium is still alive, but the Netherlands is gone. Germany is for the most part, contained. Poland is a puppet state of Germany. The Russian Revolution kicked off in October ... of 1915! And with all this going on, the Brits and French don't want my help, though their colonies don't seem to mind my green flowing in. Weird.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe sad piano is what brings me back...

In all seriousness, I think this game and the whole in the series need is a way to make production go by a lot faster. I can't tell you how many times my friends and I would either sit around and wait for someone to finish microing their factories, infra, etc, and we'd just sit around, OR like in making history the Second world war, the game would crash due to memory on any of our computers, and we'd have to restart the game because it wouldn't save in MP. I hope that isn't the case for this in the future.

The single player part of the game is alright now, but the AI does seem limited still in it's function to attack and find exploits in the enemy's lines. It's fun to test out and it 10 dollars cheaper than previous installations at the moment. If you're a fan, try it now or get it in case it goes up to the usual 30 dollars, or wait for the promised improvements.\t\t\t\t\t
