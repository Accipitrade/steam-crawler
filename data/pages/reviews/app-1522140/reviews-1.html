\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDemon Skin truely deserves the term outstanding! I was convinced already, when the intro played, because the devs managed to conjure a great, action-oriented dark fantasy atmosphere. When the game started, I was like WOW, because Demon Skin simply looks awesome! The graphics are full of details, and being only in the second-or-so level now, I already saw a lot of different scenery, every one of them packed with lovely details like ice-frozen yetis.

Coming to the gameplay, Demon Skin is very linear, but highly rewarding to explore the levels, may it be with new weapons or shards of a gem that increase your combat-ability and change your appearance. If you beat enough spiders, skeletons, dwarfs, mechs and many more different enemies, you increase in level, being able to use skill points for health, endurance, or strength.

I cannot wait to continue on my quest. Full recommendation for this epic hack and slash adventure!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am going to recommend this game with the caveat that you do not expect it to be a simple hack and slash side scroller. There is an element of puzzle combat, matching the stances of your enemies to block. The system is a bit clunky at first till you begin to understand the intended game play. I was a bit confused, I thought I had to use R stick to switch stances, but just hitting the corresponding attack button (Y for upper, X for middle, and A for lower) it automatically switches the stance for you.
It's a slow steady paced game that draws you in with consistent progress and learning.
You will die a lot finding the right jumps but it is never so punishing or difficult that you don't learn from your mistakes or get stuck and can't move forward.
It keeps track of every death even if you go back to a previous load just to experiment with a section...so keep that in mind...I racked up an extra 15 deaths thinking there might be a way to kill the werewolf you run into at the end of the level after racing an avalanche behind you....It might be possible if you put all of your level points into attack before you get there.... Just a heads up. I did not do that.
Full price, I would say it's a bit of a niche recommendation, but on sale, I would recommend anyone try it out for a change of pace from the usual hack and slash fare.
