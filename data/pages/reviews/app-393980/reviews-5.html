\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty Girls Mahjong Solitaire is the epitome of games which have a high Quality:Price ratio. I bought this game and enjoyed it so much, I bought two more copies for two associates of mine, and they love it too.

The gameplay is compelling, but simplistic. This is part of the reason Pretty Girls Mahjong Solitaire is a quality product. It might seem like Mahjong Solitaire on the surface, but it has a true, deeper, meaning.

The graphics are fantastic. The tiles are HD. I want to shake the hand of the brilliant person(s) who designed the tiles so perfectly. There's also anime girls, too, if you care about that.

The sound design is perfect. The music is perfect, and the voice work is phenomenal. My only complaint is that it is pretty loud upon initial start-up.

Overall, if you have the money, this is a must buy. This game has the potential to become the next big thing - it just needs help getting there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood news, bad news, time.

Good news: This actually isn't a bad Mahjong game and it's certainly worth playing if you don't just get a free one from either Windows, which comes with a free version until Windows 8 or 10, or one of a multitude of them online.

I liked the clear, clean versions of the tiles, however. To be honest, I play this game more for that reason than anything else, simply because they're so much easier to differentiate and take in at a glance, which is really nice for my next like.

All rounds are timed. You have "x" amount of time to finish a level and as you make a match, you get a portion of time back, making this a fun difficulty factor.

Similarly, each level/girl in the progression means the timer goes down faster, so the difficulty goes up. Each girl has a few levels with the final one being the strip-down version (bar going from top to bottom to reveal the outfit beneath.)

If you're interested in the anime girls aspect, they're pretty enough for digital art and they have various outfits with the final level for each girl being an undressing game (no nudity unless there's a patch out there somewhere - I've not looked.) They end up in swimsuits, lingerie and similar attire with minor changes in facial expressions.

Bad news: The premise of this game is winning the levels to get the models into less clothes like a creepy version of strip mohjong. Not really a totally bad thing if that's your interest. No nudity, again (unless there's a patch.)

The worst part of this game are the very few levels you actually get (or girls, depending on your focus.) It's actually a fun game, but it doesn't take you long to get the hang of it and beat it fairly quickly as there also aren't very many tiles per round/level.

If you get this, do make sure it's no more than a dollar or two. Anything more than that, and you've confirmed yourself desperate and, to be fully honest, getting ripped off.

PS: The girls never get fully nude - That should prevent a few more sales. Maybe the developers will add more females and clothing variations (perhaps something random) for each play-through. *Shrugs* Until then, avoid this.
