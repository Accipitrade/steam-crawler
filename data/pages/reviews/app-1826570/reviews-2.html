\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHead in the Clouds is my personal favorite of 0 Deer's escape rooms so far. The larger environment compared to The Train of Thought and Mansion Heist encourage more critical thinking and remembering key details, which is an important skill up until the very last puzzle of the game. The amount of puzzle boxes available at a time when entering a new area was also handled well, preventing players from stepping on each others toes. Players will still end up working on the same puzzle sometimes, especially near the end, but the rooms are kept at a good size and the information spread out enough that it feels like you're working together instead of half the group staring at pieces of paper dictating what the others should do.

The largest problem with the game is a problem with the series, only being able to carry one item at a time. I assume this is done to prevent one player hoarding all the items, but some puzzles, like the fuses puzzle or the knights puzzle, are made tedious by this feature.

I played this game and the rest of the series with one other friend, which seemed to me like the perfect amount. While you can go up to five, that seems like severe overkill. I wouldn't recommend going beyond three total players.\t\t\t\t\t
