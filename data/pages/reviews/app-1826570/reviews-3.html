\r\n\t\t\t\t\t My Experience

While I've never been much for single-player puzzle games, I tend to love playing these cooperative escape rooms with a friend. We've played the We Were Here series, Last Will, Escape from Kyoto House, The Timeless Child, Escape from School, and all of the developer OnSkull's offerings, among others. We've enjoyed the vast majority of them, and new releases in the genre always pique our interest. First Class Escape 2 represents our first experience with developer 0 Deer Soft.

We were very impressed with the game visually - the airship truly appears luxurious, and all surfaces in the game appear to have a sheen. We also each possessed an actual character model, as opposed to the disembodied torsos or spirits that we have controlled in other games of this type.

However, I found the actual puzzles in the game to be rather tedious. In many of these escape room games, the difficulty lies in locating hidden or cryptic solutions in the environment. There might be something you haven't noticed yet, or a solution that perhaps you aren't sure how to decipher. In First Class Escape 2, the difficulty lies in the puzzles themselves. There's nothing else that you're intended to notice in the environment, you're just fiddling with what you already have - switching capacitors (I don't know enough about electricity to know if this is the correct nomenclature) in and out to create particular ampere values, exchanging spools in a music box to create a specific song, moving pegs to create various values in rows and columns, or flipping an enormous line of switches until certain criteria are met.

Even once you have all the information available, all of the puzzles involve a fair bit of fiddling, which was problematic for two reasons. One, it completely slowed the momentum of the game to a halt, and two, these puzzles were difficult to complete cooperatively. One person knew what they were trying to accomplish and what they'd tried already, and another player trying to help would simply be in the way.

We decided to call it quits with the game when we reached a puzzle that involved shields, weapons, and suits of armor. We instead went to play something that was less tedious and more enjoyable, and haven't been tempted to return. If you're the type of player that finds most escape room games too easy, perhaps First Class Escape 2 will be exactly what you're looking for. But personally, I found it exhausting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played both First Class Escape games and LOVED THEM BOTH! They let you take your time to find things and solve the thought-provoking puzzles, all wrapped up in beautifully done scenery with a smattering of clues. I always take my time playing games like these so I can look around and see what there is to see. There aren't an annoying amount of useless objects or actions that do nothing to advance the game or help you, which is very refreshing -- and relaxing as you won't spend a lot of wasted time worrying about trivial things.

My only complaint is that you can only carry one object at a time. I suffer from visually induced motion sickness if I have to run back and forth/up and down/round and round, over and over in order to move multiple items to the same place to solve a puzzle and advance...being able to carry more than one, or to put several items in something like a wastebasket that happens to be there, or a laundry cart, whatever, in order to get them all where you want them (with the carrier counting as the requisite 'can only carry one item'), would be a huge plus.

Overall, these are top notch games! I hope there will yet another Chapter! :)
