\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great sequel! Unique, challenging puzzles and a bit of a story to go with it. The co-op works seamlessly, even with multiple disconnects you can just rejoin your friend and it's fine. The game runs well and the main menu has plenty of settings to adjust to your liking(seems like a small thing but many indie games lack these basic QoL features). Also, it's impressive how nice it is to interact with a puzzle, door, or drawer, etc. In many other puzzle games it's clumsy/annoying to interact with your environment but in the First Class Escape titles it's great. You can even click on a puzzle your friend is already solving to watch/help them! The hidden collectibles are an appreciated touch.

If I could critique something I'd say the environment could use a little more variation/personality/detail to look more natural. Also, the majority of the drawers you open tend to be empty or have no use, which is a shame because it feels like a missed opportunity to make the game even more enjoyable & immersive. Everyone likes rummaging through drawers for items/clues!

Looking forward to more games by these devs!\t\t\t\t\t
