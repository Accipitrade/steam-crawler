\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVIVE REVIEW :

This game is very promising, I've followed the development and everything so i was pretty damn hyped for this game. It brings alot to the table that makes you go wow and makes you excited for the future of VR. That aside its really obvious this game was designed with index controllers in mind. The only good thing i can say is throwing feels better than other games since you actually release your hand (well, sorta). Its still not precise but heaps better than, for example, SUPERHOTVR's "press to release while in throwing motion"-control scheme.

Other than that you'll be infuriated. If you want to walk (only allowed on right hand btw) while holding (squeezing and pressing the trigger) something? Tough luck pall, unless your thumb is able to twist in cirque de soleil kind of ways you're absolutely â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥. Running isnt really something you do on a whim either. You have to doubletap the top of the trackpad which is never instant. You'll find yourself standing like a dumb child that wet itself while some orange â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥ is flailing at you.

Oh, and dont get me started on pulling yourself up the ledge. Just forget about doing that. Its very VERY awkward and 90% of the time it doesnt, and when it does youll have quite the chance youll fall down right away. The way they describe you have to do it is : Grab ledge, pull up, crouch, EASY! Right? â™¥â™¥â™¥â™¥ no, you have to grap the ledge, pull up, crouch in real life, crouch in game (which had the same problem as running while holding something, cirque du thumbu) move your head forward in some kind of bootleg waterjumping technic. If that doesnt work with all the struggle you have within you your final resort is to do the same and as final manouvre try to lay on your back. That worked for me, once. Goodluck!

And as final negative (I only have 5 hours of playtime or so) Checkpoints. There are close to none. You died? have fun running all the way back past the guys youve already killed. Did a puzzle? Hope you remembered how and liked it enough to do it again pal.

I really REALLY want to love this game. the rest is so promising. the gunplay, the environment the weapons, the freedom, tackle anything your way. Its great, but its being held back by this old tech of mine. Unless youre the lucky index few, cant speak for oculus and wmr tho, save this game for when you are one of the lucky few or hope they adapt the controls. Because now Boneworks is more like CarpalTunnelSyndromeworks

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: I have problems with this game, but the review is intended to be positive. I decided to make it negative temporarily, until changes are made by the dev team.

While I do recommend this game as a unique VR title, it is very clear during playthough that it was made by amateurs.

First of all, let's not forget that the game did not have any checkpoints at launch. Why you ask? Because it took the developers 30 minutes for each level. It's pretty obvious that if you're the one who designed the game, you'll finish everything easily. Did they not have any play testers? ( I know they added the feature, but it did take them three months after release).

Vive controllers feel awful in this game. This game was made mostly using the index controllers, which allow you to pick everything up just by gripping the controller gently. Meaning if you don't keep your hands gripped, your item will fall. ( i.e items don't lock into your hands) Sounds cool, but when this translates into the vive controllers, it becomes a terrible experience. To hold an item using the vive controllers, you need to constantly put pressure on the grip buttons, which in turn hurts your fingers after a while. Also, when it doesn't strain your hand, it's just generally uncomfortable. The devs have yet to add a grip lock option to fix this.

Levels are full of clipping bugs. I have yet to have a session without an item, or my hands, clipping through something, forcing me to restart the game. It's very irritating.

Overall, the level design is pretty disappointing. Each level takes about an hour to two hours (depending on how you play it), and 75% of the time you're just trying to move items or figure out how to solve the unintuitive puzzles. The actual fun part of the game, shooting and killing enemies, lasts for a few minutes at a time. The pacing is very lame. Don't get me started on the puzzles. The puzzle bits are so boring and I wish they were never there. They're either recycled from half life/portal, or are really random and - again - unintuitive. The game has a bunch of buttons that make it hard to understand what they supposed to do.

And just in general, small things really make it frustrating to play. The inventory is system is inconsistent. Some of the slots don't work half of the time.


Although it sounds like I hate the game, I don't. It just annoys me how unprofessional (from a development perspective) it feels sometimes. The game is pretty cool, and very unique at times. I do recommend it for any VR user, but I will keep the review negative until the vive controller settings change.\t\t\t\t\t
