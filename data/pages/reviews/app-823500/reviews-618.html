Original Review.
Played on a Rift S.

I can see why people like this game, I really can, but I should never be asking more than once in a VR title "Where the ♥♥♥♥ are my hands?" or "Am I pulling this down or am I climbing it?" This is the issue that you have when you decide to lean super hard not only into physics but also into giving your player a full body that collides with the world. The game feels clunky and, like I said, there are many times where you just lose your control. Multiple times I wanted to pull a plank off a wall and every single time I ended up trying to climb it instead of pulling it. I also hit my face a solid 3 times trying to climb things because the game always insisted on pulling me in towards the thing I was climbing, forcing me to pull my hands in close to un-♥♥♥♥♥ myself. I would consider it a rougher around the edges Half-Life Alyx and say that if you can, buy HL instead.

Re-Review
Played on a Rift S, but with more VR experience.

I bought the game a second time, months after originally getting a headset, buying the game, and refunding it. I got it for $10 off and with ~200 hours more of VR experience under my belt, making it much more enjoyable. But I wouldn't say the extra experience helped me play the game better, rather helping to deal with un-♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ the game when it gets ♥♥♥♥♥♥. For $20 instead of $30, the game is much more enjoyable but still a rough time. Most of what I said still applies. Hands getting stuck, frustrating climbing mechanics, and some odd (but still able to be enjoyed) open level design make it a hard recommend at $30. However, for $20, yeah why not? At least for the zombie warehouse mode it's good.

More Specific Stuff
The guns are frustratingly basic. Ammo is only consumed when firing it, not when taking out a magazine. There's no mag release button, so your options for reloading are to use your hand to unload the mag and put another one in, or grabbing a mag and bringing it near the current mag which automatically pulls it out for you. A dedicated mag release would have been nice. There's not a fire mode select either, which also means there's no button for chambering around after running a weapon dry. Either pull back the slide/bolt or press trigger. Also, grabbing the gun as a physics object rather than a weapon is uncommon but frustrating still. Even more frustrating is that it's difficult to grab it as a physics object on purpose to use as a melee weapon. In H3VR, if I wanna hold my gun and use it as a melee weapon, I can. In this though, it's super finnicky and limited. You can basically pistol whip or stock butt, that's about it. I like being able to hold my weapon vertically to hit things. Also for some reason that's no vibration in the controllers when doing most actions with the guns?

Climbing is still a gamble whether or not you'll actually climb. And if you do start climbing, it's very difficult to get yourself up over the ledge when you get there. Other VR games sacrifice a bit of immersion by making it so that when you get to the top of a ladder or ledge and try pulling yourself up, the game hoists you upwards to make it easier. While a tiny bit less immersive in itself, it makes the rest of the action way more immersive since I don't have to take myself out of the game to start fighting the controls.

Again, at $30 I would steer clear especially with little VR experience. At $20 it's a serviceable experience.\t\t\t\t\t

The best and worst part of the game is the physics. The physics, when they work, are great. but the developers decided to put physics into everything and it leaves you fighting against the physics and it just is incredibly frustrating. I don't see the benefit to your body have such strict physics, it makes climbing up onto platforms a pain in the ass. dumb things happen like you try to reload while crouching but your arms are colliding with the ground so you can. The guns are by far the worst feeling in any VR game I've ever played, it feels like you're aimming in water.

If they had just left the body collision and physics similar to other games, and had normal satisfying gun mechanics, then this would probably be amazing. I want to like it so bad.

I'm sure some will say it's my fault for not playing the game "right" or something but I'm just giving my honest opinion of my experience. I'm glad other people like it, I wish I was one of them.\t\t\t\t\t
