\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is quite possibly the clunkiest, most infuriating, glitchy mess with a high rating.

The combat is fine, it's incredibly unsatisfying to hit someone in melee, grappling enemies is made impossible as they flail around spasticly chipping away at your health bar while all you wanted to do was pull off a cool stunt. Guns are cool, but they make all melee weapons completely unviable, it's cool to swing a sledgehammer at a dude's head, but if you've played blade and sorcery you know it's just infinitely more satisfying to kill dudes in that game.

The graphics are poor, you'd think with a unfinished, beta video game style they could focus more on the look, however everything just feels plain, unfiltered, dry. Empty, i understand where it's going but some of the textures just downright look ugly.

The atmosphere is little to none, it's there but god does it never feel impressive not once.

Loading times are atrocious.

Everything in the game is too heavy, yourself included.
Jumping feels like you have a collection of weights taped to your body, you leap up into the air for a pathetic little hop and then crash down and your hands probably will get stuck in a box or something along the lines of that.

The puzzles are in my opinion, the worst part of this game, this is the only thing on this review which i feel is arguable, it makes you use a lot of the climbing mechanic.

.
.
.

That was a moment of silence for everyone who has broken an item or a wall because of the TERRIBLE climbing, it's by far the worst thing about this game, so far not a single VR game i've played has been able to figure out how to pull yourself up a ledge properly. I spent about 30 mins trying to understand what to do with this dumb puzzle, until i figured out you just had to pull a lever and line up these platforms correctly, it didn't help that there was a bunch of graffiti put there for i assume, brainlits, who don't know how to look up or down, i spent ages trying to figure out if it had deeper meaning other than "Look at the platform up there that you've already been too."

I enjoy how instead of you getting some stupid video or monologue about the story and what happens in the game, you instead just get to explore and piece everything together through graffiti and set pieces. (now that I think of it my friends and I switched around a lot for the first part so I may have missed some form of large text explaining the games entire story line)

Speaking of set pieces, the levels in this game are terrible, there is never anywhere clear to go, sometimes i'll find myself sprawling over a fence that has an invisible wall on the top instead of going to this specific gate and ramming into it three times?

The enemies, while their designs cool and unique, are just boring to fight against, I've only played this game for around 5 hours, and hopefully there are more enemies yet to be introduced but the weird hologram ball guys are the ones I'm focused on. The only way to kill them properly is to point a gun and shoot at them, meanwhile in Blade and sorcery, i'm swinging around a double bladed spear sword at a storm trooper with a lightsaber, (While these are from mods, mods are basically essential to blade as it's only in development, and who cares they don't charge you 25 pounds for it?)
The only threat the basic zombie like enemy holds is if you drop a controller and have to pick it up, however, in doing that you snap your leg and are unable to move, then maybe he'll kill you. In TWD S&S every Zombie poses a massive threat if you're not prepared. If you run out of stamina and are caught out of no where, you can say goodbye to all your loot, it makes the game tense and rewards you for being more careful and paranoid. There was one cool enemy I seen early which was a robot half life headcrab, it scared the outta me and was an awesome encounter, more enemies like that please.

Satisfaction.
It's why VR is so fun, in no other form of entertainment am I going to get to slice a guy's head of without consequence, if i could change one thing about Boneworks, I'd make it more satisfying. Hitting enemies, solving puzzles, unique combat strategies, exploration and being rewarded; these are things that I want from VR games, and games in general. Games are nothing without satisfaction. Blade understands this and doesn't you with anything and you can just go in, pick up a sword and start killing dudes, The Walking Dead : Saints & Sinners, it understands that enemies must be a threat, exploration should be rewarded (I don't think twd s&s is perfect, just good), getting out of a sticky situation having only a dribble of health left, Deadlock, you go in, and shoot some guys. Beat Saber, if you haven't perfected a tricky song yet then you won't understand the immense satisfaction of mastery you feel. These are VR games are satisfying, Boneworks is too clunky, too ugly, too ruff around the edges to be satisfying, fun maybe, but not satisfying.

25, it's unfinished.
It should not be out for release in the glitchy mess of a state it's in. This can all be fixed with a new, thick coat of paint.
Boneworks has a lot of potential but if it doesn't get it's together soon it's going to be crushed by Half-life : Alex.

I'll be back to this review once i've fully completed the game.\t\t\t\t\t
