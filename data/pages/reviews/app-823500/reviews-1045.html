\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played this with a WMR headset (specifically the Dell Visor) and with that setup I can't recommend this game.

While I notice a lot of reviews here praising how the player model responds to the physics in the game, I found this to be the biggest source of nausea, simply due to your in game body not always doing what your real body is. I get that they want you to feel the weight of heavy objects, but I feel that's negated by you still having inhuman grip strength on those objects, so you can just grab and walk (with a joystick) to limply drag it wherever, plus with the game's story taking place in vr I can't help but think "why was this necessary?" as it just feels like a vomit inducing annoyance.

Oh, and the saving thing others have mentioned, I might've kept playing if taking more frequent breaks was an option (some would argue it's a necessity in vr) but there's no saving and no pausing. Each time you play you must start at the beginning of a level, and must get to the end of that level in one sitting, and each of these levels are roughly an hour or more of gameplay.

After working my way through 2 levels (not counting the fairly long tutorial) and feeling physically unable to continue without vomiting, I requested a refund.

I may repurchase in the future if saving and/or pausing gets added, or if I get a better headset (anyone got $500?) as my gripes with the physics might be fixed with better motion tracking, but until then I can not stomach playing anymore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"...Now what?"

Those were the first words I said when I completed this beautiful game.

Which really hurts me to say that I am giving this game a negative review, why? Because I LOVED this game, the trailer really did do it good, the puzzles it had gave me a half-life 2 feel to it, it literally was like you took half life 2, and threw it in a new universe, and put it in VR, and I loved it.

So why the negative review?

Well... A couple reasons:

1) The ending...Was pretty sudden, and sucked.
2) The lack of guns

I didn't get the ending, I had no idea the ending was about to happen until I got there and then the credits started rolling, hence why I said "...Now what?", because that's what I said, probably more akin to 'That's it?' because, you know when something is so good, it's like a train, it gets faster and faster, you're having more and more fun, and then it's like the train went from 200...to 0, in an instant, you flew into the window of the train, crashed out of it, and you now have serious concussion and several questions.

It's not what it was like, but it may as well have been with how sudden it was, especially considering the moments before it were...Mostly fun, like getting suction cups! Having a 1 v 1 fisticuffs fight with a king of a virtual kingdom! It was just getting good! Why, why stop there?! And the slap in the face to this, was that there were tons more weapons you could've got...If you do a second play-through, requiring you to use 0 of your ammo, no offence, i'm not keen on doing that considering this game took me 8 hours over a good few days to do.

Which nicely walks onto my 2nd point: There is a shocking lack of guns in this game...Seriously, the trailer gave me the feel I could blow people apart with a shotgun, or have a western duel with a revolver, or even snipe people with a hunting rifle! But instead, all I got were MP5K's, glocks, beretta's, M4's and an okay-ish amount of melee weapons (Crowbars, sledgehammers, swords and shields, there are more), which, actually kind of upset me! I know this sounds like a petty review, but you have to understand that this is a finished (Or at least, I was under the impression it was) game! I only just saw that apparently they're adding the rest of the guns in, but...The damage has been done at this point, for me at least, I just simply can't bring myself to go to my vr set in another room, turn it on, and get everything ready for another week of VR.

Now, now that I've gotten all the bad points out of the way, this game does have lots of lovely good stuff in it:

- The music
My god, this music really gives an atmosphere to this game, I loved it.

- The puzzles
I'd say a good majority of the puzzles are really great, and make sense, there were a couple (optionally, thank god) that made me go 'What the heck am I meant to do?' and I could've googled it, but didn't. But the rest of the puzzles were really solid, and I loved theorizing about what I had to do, like 'Maybe I could do X!' and if it worked, I was happy, if it didn't, i'd try 'Y', and so on, it truly was fun.

- The scares
This is not a horror game, I want to put that out there now, but there are some elements that can give you a good scare if you're not ready for it, I loved it! It kept me on edge until I was reassured that everything was fine...For now.

Sadly, that's it, but don't let this review keep you from getting this game, I really DO like it, but I know for a fact that people will care more for what the negatives are than the positives, trust me, the game has good gameplay, and gunplay (There's even a second chance feature ala borderlands which I didn't know about until I found it by accident late into the game!), but it's just those two points which really dragged it down for me, and sadly prevented it from becoming, as a certain youtuber would call it: D R E A M G A M E status, which really sucks, because I know this is a game that has taken years of development (See: Node on youtube) and perfection to get it right, which I can only applaud.

Thanks for reading.
