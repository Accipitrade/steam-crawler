\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA must-have for VR.

The controls and physics are better than Half-Life Alyx, and melee combat is satisfying.
I love how objects in this game feel to have a weight to them, even down to small things like a moving hanging basket breifly slows as it sways back and forth.

On a GTX 1660 ti I found some parts of the game unplayable. However, I found that using AMD FidelityFX modified openvr helped a great deal. Another performance must is enabling dynamic resolution in the settings menu. I wish more games had this feature.
You can find the FidelityFX OpenVR here. Massive thanks to fholger and the project contributors.

Using these 2 tricks/options, I found the game completely playable. The only time I ran into some issues was later in the game in a large open level (not going to spoil anything)

While the story was a bit lack-luster and incoherent, it didn't need to be flashy with how well the game itself is designed. The post-game game-modes are also a nice touch that I know will keep me coming back for more.

If you have VR, and you haven't already, give this game a go. It's worth your while.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is an incredible VR game that can best be described as a meta surrealist action-adventure game.

The gameplay is heavily tied to its physics engine, which is absolutely phenomenal with half life 2 levels of interactability, most objects are able to be interacted with in ways as unique as you can come up with. Your limbs and head are physics objects, and it's not out of the question to use that to your advantage, the tutorial even telling you how to exploit the game's physics engine to get some extra jump height and it's not out of the question to sequence break levels if you wanted to. (Crowbars make great climbing tools.) Both the game's design and the engine are very malleable, allowing for flexibility with how you approach levels and come up with ways to get from point A to point B, but naturally there are things you must do to get through, but there's lots to be found if you stick around and explore, and that concept goes way beyond the campaign.

It wears its self awareness with pride, weaving layers of virtual reality and degrees of separation of the game into the plot (And the universe if you're paying attention) and even the gameplay, creating a game out of what would seem to be a very fleshed out engine and a bunch of massive levels for you to explore. That does, however, mean that the game is not flush with features, but rather maps. What it does offer within that scope though, is varied and very, very solid, drawing from its physics-based roots and running with it extensively.

My only complaints are that the inventory system is a little difficult to utilize without opening a menu and vive players are reporting that gripping objects is very tough for them. Having used a vive before myself (I've since switched to the quest 2) I am inclined to agree. If you don't have a vive, though, this game is absolutely glorious. 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t
