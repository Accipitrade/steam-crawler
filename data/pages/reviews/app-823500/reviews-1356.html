\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy thoughts after finishing the Story mode:

+ 8 - 10+ hour campaign and 2 extra modes
+ Interesting and well designed levels that don't overstay their welcome (for the most part)
+ Different and distinct enemy types, all of which are fun to fight
+ Collectibles/secrets and optional areas add replay value.
+ Great soundtrack
+ Overall just great to look around and take in the details.

- Wonky/Sticky object manipulation
- Unsatisfying 2 handed melee
- Simulated body that seems to get stuck on everything
- Low firearm variety
- Unsatisfying weapon interaction
- Weak story

Worth your 30$ for sure. Doesn't quite do anything perfect, but the things it does do right are worth seeing. The oddly bouncy and sticky object manipulation gets frustrating when trying to do puzzles, and the simulated body looks bad, feels bad, and often gets in the way, and at times I questioned why certain decisions are made. The gunplay is hit or miss, I've seen 2 handed weapons done better in many other games, but the handguns feel just fine. However there were moments i felt that the game had truly been worth all of this buzz, and those moments are truly outstanding for what they are.\t\t\t\t\t

Everything has physics and this is a puzzle game experience.

As of current review: Each map is a phase of the story. You have to beat the entire phase to save progress. Some can take a long time. Longer than I've been able to play for. Sandbox mode and all other extras are locked. I assume you unlock them after story mode. The game is rough but fun. It can be frustrating trying to move your new VR body around, do the puzzles, mess with objects. I think as feedback comes in and time moves forward, the devs will address concerns.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely incredible. Best VR game out by far, as i thought it would be after seeing gameplay videos. the only thing that even comes close is blade and sorcery.

This is actually a full game unlike many many vr experiences, most games for vr are more like demos. Im so so grateful for stress level zero taking the time to make a full vr game, not just a quick demo or experience. I want to buy the whole studio lunch, seriously, its that good. Im also so happy that someone finally made a game that really shows the full potential of vr. After seeing the mechanics in this game, i feel like other developers will see the true potential of vr and make similar games. I feel like this is the first game that truly shows what vr is capable of. Its only up from here.

My ONLY conplaint is the melee weapon gripping system, it sucks to have to hold two buttons at once just to firmly grip a melee weapon, its very tiring on the original vive controllers. Im sure its way better with the index controllers but i dont have those

Also a disclaimer: this game has lots of puzzles, if you want pure action all the time, better stick to something like pavlov or blade and sorcery\t\t\t\t\t
