\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is absolutely worth it, although you should do your research before buying and playing it.

This game is definitely not for those very prone to motion sickness. I get it to some degree but have slowly adjusted to the smooth locomotion the game provides. A big help in the most recent update is that of the save points scattered throughout the campaign, offering greater chances to rest and play each level in chunks rather than 30 minute - 1 hour long sessions. Prior to this update I was always fighting my nausea deep into levels where I felt almost rushed to finish with the fear of losing my progress due to WMR going to sleep and closing the game if I were to take a break. Now this pressure is mostly alleviated.

With regards to Windows Mixed Reality users, I have been using the Odyssey + without much issue, although hand tracking has been slightly bothersome when maneuvering on ledges and manipulating objects. This is inherent in any inside-out tracking headset but is noticeable here.

The visual and audio design are both excellent, and indicative of a quality campaign rather than a series of shooting galleries and demo-like set pieces that are still too common in many VR titles. Consequently, it is necessary to have a decent rig to run everything. I was having stuttering on an EGPU setup with a 7700hq and a 2060S, so CPU performance is a big bottleneck when running this game. With a 2700x I've seen much less in the way of stutters but that is to be expected.

This game shows the potential of the VR platform better than any other game I've played so far, and I hope that more developers take notice of what is possible when using inverse kinematics and full body simulation to immerse the player. We'll soon see how HL Alyx pans out with similar design sensibilities and goals but for now this is the VR killer app to get on PC for those with an itch to play a dense, immersive single player campaign.\t\t\t\t\t
