\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've got your VR legs, this is a must-have. It's the jack of all trades, second best at everything, VR game.

The pros:
*Everything feels legit - sledges and axes feel weighty, but can still be more or less one-handed if you grab them right under the head, guns feel good and technical and the slight offness due to the laser/sights running parallel to the barrel at point blank is even there (aim your sniping rifle dead center at a padlock that's 20 inches from the barrel and you'll miss because the scope is a few inches above where the bullet comes out and aiming parallel to the barrel)
*Gameplay is simple and forgiving beyond the physics - dying is just get back in there, and saving is just grabbing a thing. You can level select, once you've reached any particular level in the story. Or just jump in the arena and beat stuff up and die or not.

Cons:
*It's too good - you want to keep coming back even after you've played through it's campaign, but there's no "randomize the enemies" or "new game +" or anything. I've played through it several times, but mostly to hunt for unlockables.
*Most of the unlockables are disappointing, considering the difficulty of reaching some - oh boy, a padding wedge added to sandbox.
*Fighting the king of VR felt like a chore, rather than a challenge.\t\t\t\t\t
