"Mind-Bending Action and Immersion: A Review of 'BONEWORKS'"
I'm Beatrice Braunwitz, and as an experienced game reviewer, I'm excited to share my thoughts on a game that left a lasting impression on me as a true trendsetter in the Virtual Reality industry: 'BONEWORKS'

'BONEWORKS' is a virtual reality game that was released in 2019 by the game development company, Stress Level Zero. The game is known for its innovative mechanics, immersive gameplay, and Illimitable possibilities. What makes 'BONEWORKS' stand out is its unique physics engine that allows for realistic interactions with the virtual environment. This engine provides players with the ability to grab and manipulate objects in a way that feels authentic and natural.

'BONEWORKS' began as an experimental game that was initially intended to be a tech demo for VR physics. However, as development progressed, the team realized that they were creating something that could be much more significant. The game's physics engine was so groundbreaking that it caught the attention of Valve Corporation, the company behind the popular Half-Life series.

Valve Corporation was so impressed by the game that they invited the team to their offices to show it off. This meeting led to the team working with Valve to develop 'BONEWORKS' further, and their collaboration even had an impact on the development of Half-Life Alyx. This partnership allowed the game to reach a wider audience and contributed to the growth of the VR industry as a whole.

The gameplay in 'BONEWORKS' revolves around puzzle-solving, exploration, and combat. Players must navigate through levels, completing objectives and battling enemies. The combat system is fast-paced and intense, requiring quick reflexes and strategic thinking. Players can utilize a wide range of weapons and tools to take down their enemies, including guns, melee weapons, and explosives.

The story of 'BONEWORKS' revolves around MythOS and Monogon Industries. Monogon Industries is a company specializing in advanced virtual reality technology, and they conduct experiments on the human brain through MythOS. The protagonist, Arthur Ford, is a Monogon employee who enters MythOS in search of immortality in the void. As the game progresses, Monogon Industries becomes aware of Ford's plan to achieve immortality in the void, and they launch a search to find and kill his physical body before he can succeed. This raises the stakes and adds tension to the storyline.

As a long-time user of virtual reality technology, I can confidently say that BONEWORKS is one of the most impressive VR games I have ever played. However, like any game, it has its strengths and weaknesses. In this section, I'll break down what BONEWORKS does well, where it falls short, and its limitations.

The Good
  • Limitless Possibilities—BONEWORKS offers a vast array of possibilities between story, arena, and sandbox modes, as well as an extensive community-made modding toolkit. This allows for a highly personalized experience that is rarely found in other VR games.
  • Immersive Gameplay—The game's smooth locomotion, expeditious-paced combat, and experimental physics engine provide a highly immersive gameplay experience that makes you feel like you're really there.
  • Innovative Mechanics—The game's reloading and slow-motion mechanics add a layer of realism and immersion to the gameplay. It's satisfying to be able to reload your weapon mid-air or slow down time to land a perfect hit on your enemy.
  • Puzzle Gameplay—The game's puzzle mechanics take inspiration from popular titles like Half-Life and Portal. It offers a challenging yet rewarding experience that keeps you coming back for more.
  • Aesthetically Pleasing—The game's inspiration from science-fiction and fantasy properties is evident in its graphics and design. It's a geek's fantasy come true, and the attention to detail in every aspect of the game is impressive.
  • Mysterious Storyline—The game's storyline is intentionally vague, leaving much to the interpretation of the player. While this is a divisive aspect of the game, players who enjoy unraveling hidden lore and piecing together clues, similar to games like Five Night's at Freddy's, will find themselves deeply invested in the narrative.

The Bad
  • Not Beginner-Friendly—As the official documentation states, BONEWORKS is intended for advanced VR users only. Its practice of smooth locomotion and fast-paced combat may cause motion sickness in some users, and it can be challenging to get used to the controls for those unfamiliar with VR.
  • Limited Replay Value—While the game offers a vast array of possibilities, it may not have enough content to keep players coming back once they've completed the story mode and explored the different gameplay modes.
  • Steep Learning Curve—The game's physics engine can be challenging to get used to, and it may take some time before players can master the game's mechanics and controls.

The Ugly
  • Soft-Locking—One major issue with Boneworks is the physics engine glitches that can sometimes cause you to get soft-locked in the game. For instance, you may accidentally drop a key item in a location that is impossible to reach, or find yourself stuck in an object that you cannot escape from. These glitches can be frustrating and may require you to restart from a previous checkpoint.

In conclusion, 'BONEWORKS' is a must-play game for anyone who wants a truly immersive Virtual Reality experience. The game's physics engine on its own was engaging enough to keep me coming back for three entire years. Although the game has a steep learning curve, I highly recommend it to anyone who wants to develop their VR legs and push the boundaries of what's possible in virtual reality. After playing 'BONEWORKS', I give it a perfect 10/10 rating and eagerly await what Stress Level Zero has in store for the future of Virtual Reality gaming with their next story-driven title, 98.6 DEAD FM.\t\t\t\t\t
