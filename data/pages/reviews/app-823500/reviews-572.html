\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I had a Rift S I wanted this game so bad. Now that I got one this was the 2nd game I bought.

Right out of the box, this game is great. You begin to learn how to interact with the world and objects and learn the physics before the main part of the story begins. The physics are incredible. Period. Interaction with the world is fun and you need to be very involved with it. If you are a new user to VR it may be difficult to grasp how to interact with the world. But with practice and gameplay it becomes much easier.
The audio and music is excellent and it changes based upon where you are in the game (if you want to listen to the music look up "Bonetones" online).
You need to solve lots of puzzles and difficult tasks in this game. It gave me the same feeling when playing Portal 2, having to think heavily and really stretch my brain to find a solution. Sometimes it's best to take a short break and clear your mind if it feels overwhelming.
This game performed really well on my computer (RTX 2060, i5 4590). I worried I would be unable to run the game due to the lower end cpu, but it ran beautifully. I set shadows to low, 2x MSAA, half textures, and disabled Ambient Oclusion.
In terms of motion sickness, I did not feel sick during gameplay. If you have a not good PC the game could lag and make you feel nausous. But in my case I felt fine playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore i got VR i was certain that when i do, Boneworks will be a must play/buy experience.

Now that i have gotten into VR, and tried quite a few VR titles already, i must say that Boneworks in particular heavily disappointed me and I eventually dropped the game without finishing it.

I wanted to experience the famous physics with the melee/gun combat the game was praised for, but instead i was constantly battling the janky controls in endless sections of climbing, jumping, block moving and weight balancing.
So many pointless and even frustrating puzzles.
Character randomly losing grip and falling from long climbing sections.
Legs getting stuck against things when jumping.
And many more annoying predicaments.

There were barely any combat scenarios, at least up until the sections i have reached.

The inventory system felt odd for me, the way the ammo and some side arms were placed felt unnatural and different to all other VR games i played.

The saving is also annoying. You can only manually save in certain chechpoints, so you can lose a lot of progress if you quit mid way before reaching a new one.
A game of this sort without a save and quit feature is simply tone deaf.

The game just didn't do it for me at all.

Maybe i would of enjoyed playing around in the Sandbox mode, but i haven't unlocked it yet. Apparantly there's a secret method to unlock it early but i doubt i'll ever launch the game again after such a negative experience.

My advice is not to get this at all.

If you want to do some shooting, play Pavlov.
If you want to do some stabbing, play Blade and Sorcery.

Wish i realized this sooner when i could still refund it.\t\t\t\t\t
