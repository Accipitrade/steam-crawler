\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tested VR by buying the Vive when it was on pre-order. I sold it days later as I was extremely unimpressed with the tech and games.

Years later I thought I'd try again, as this game's trailers and tech vlogs had me intrigued.
I got a Samsung Odyssey + (much better than the Vive I had originally) and got the game. I played it for an hour and forty five minutes the first session.

I am extremely impressed with the mobility and interactive physical world. I have a tremor and something this game nails is the 'smoothing' (although that isn't really what it is) of hand movements. the game is given weight and objects feel real because they don't simply have a 1:1 jittery motion in your hand like every other VR game.

This is the first time I've felt truly immersed in a VR game and have wanted to play constantly.

My only gripes are the lack of saving (I have a life and the enormously wide save points is really not acceptible, as I sometimes just have to stop playing and lose all my progress)
Also, the game is challenging, which is good, but some puzzles are really punishingly hard. not in their mechanics, but in the penalty for failure (I fell trying to make a jump and was punished by being returned to the beginning of the map (again, with no savegames to load)
This really needs to be addressed as it will definitely affect my ability or motivation to keep playing if I can't just jump in and out where I want to.

Overall an extremely impressive demonstration of the tech, and a very immersive and interesting world with its own unique character.

Floating hands and janky 1:1 object movement is now completely archaic, and Valve's entry with Alyx is already put to shame before it even releases. This game will surely set the standard going forward.\t\t\t\t\t
