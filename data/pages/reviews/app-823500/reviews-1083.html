\r\n\t\t\t\t\t2 hrs in. I have to write a review because this is completely revolutionary IMO. That being said ... I CAN understand why people are getting frustrated due to the advanced physics and realistic object manipulation. I would say that is NOT for everyone for these reasons.

BUT if you are a fan of complete control in video games (which I am), appreciate full range of motion movement in VR (instead of teleporting) (which I do), and want to rely on your own accuracy of picking up objects (I love games like Rocket League and COD for the level of accuracy/precision you are given), then this is for you!

It has a decent amount of puzzles in it, mixed with action of killing enemies (the guns feel nice to shoot, although they have a LOT of recoil).

The developers deserve some serious accolades for this. Honestly 1000% the best experience I've EVER had in VR.

I've also pre-ordered the new Half Life: Alyx, which I'm really looking forward to in March.

Congratulations for implementing so many different weapons and creating environments/puzzles that leverage the advances physics perfectly. Only slight critique I would have is MORE save points so that ppl don't get quite as frustrated if they get stuck or can't figure something out.

Amazing job.\t\t\t\t\t

That being said, this is the first VR game I have ever played that has made me nausious. It could be is that it's too focused on total immersion. Your character is limited in what actions they can do in-world. Not being able to move 1:1 in a virtual game for once is messing with my brain. I have yet to go beyond the courtyard level because I get too dizzy before completing the level. Hopefully it might be something as silly as my CPU not being fast enough for it, and if that's the case, I'll update this review when I have better hardware. If you are someone who is susceptible to getting dizzy in VR, this game is not for you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is pretty much arizona sunshine crossed with superhot crossed with onward crossed with half life 2 with a little bit of mirrors edge sprinkled on top. It really is a sandbox and does very little to hold your hand while giving you many ways to complete whatever you determine your objective to be. Even though this is a sandbox i would probably consider it the least casual of all the VR games I've played, i would not recommend it at all as a "first experience" you definitely want to have your "VR legs" before you play boneworks.
If you like these games i mentioned and you're looking for your next VR title to pick up before half life alyx (or after if that is when you're reading this review) THIS IS THE ONE, you do not need to wait for a sale either, it's priced extremely fair for a brand new VR game with this level of polish and amount of content, the developer seems to be on the level too! Enjoy!\t\t\t\t\t
