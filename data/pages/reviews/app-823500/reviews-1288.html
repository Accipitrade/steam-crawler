\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this, but with a strong caveat.

The good:

This game nails Inverse Kinematics and placing you in the world in extremely engaging and realistic ways. I grabbed an animated VR headset (think headcrab) when it jumped at me and held it off... literally. I held it. Then I picked up a brick with my other hand and bashed it while it tried to claw me. It was scary, adrenaline pumping, and fun. Similarly I grappled with a glowing zombie until I could beat it to death with a hammer. Later I killed several zombies with a pistol, and my practice with a real world handgun felt useful and relevant because it handled very similarly. Everything in the game interacted in ways that felt comfortable and immersive, and the game is probably the best available for fully immersive VR (that doesn't treat you like a ghost that can pass through everything).

The bad:

Everything about the level design screams 'inexperienced'. The game feels like a well done Garry's Mod level. The physics in the game are not there because they make sense in the level... they are there to be a physics object to play with in VR. Ladders that go nowhere, metal that turns into a ramp for no reason but to give you something to push because it's cool... it all feels like a Garry's Mod level, playing with physics because physics are fun with little to no cohesion or theme.

I have not gotten far into the game, so it's possible that design improves later, but usually it's the early levels that have the polish and later ones that feel rough. If that holds true for this game, then later levels are going to be VERY rough.

Recommended if you want to see how interaction in VR games made two years from now will work. Not recommended if you want a coherent plot or interesting world to explore.\t\t\t\t\t

This is a new standard for immersion in VR games! other devs need to take notice because this just set the bar high.

Pros:
-Physics based EVERYTHING
-Some interesting puzzles
-Guns feel nice and sound meaty
-Tons of fun physics toys to mess around with
-Great sound design

Cons:
-Physics based EVERYTHING including your body which can get stuck on things and be a bit frustrating sometimes
-Quality of level design seems a bit thrown together (some of it can be explained by the game lore) there seems to be a lot of props or places that seem like they might be important but don't seem to have any purpose beyond wasting curious gamers time.
-No manual save or checkpoints within the levels, if you die at the end of a level they might have taken you an hour to get through. you have to start over.. so dont die.

TLDR; Boneworks is a fantastically crafted physics playground with some problems that could make it frustrating at times

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the defining step in VR.
I will measure every VR game I play, by the Boneworks standard.
Graphical Integrity had Crysis, story-driven gameplay had Half-Life, Virtual Reality now has Boneworks.

The gunplay in this game is the smoothest I've experienced, I've played a good amount of Pavlov, and Contagion, and minimal Onward. After adjusting to HOW to holster and unholster objects, and where to place them on my body, everything was extremely natural to get my hands on and manipulate objects as I have been hoping for from a VR game. You can't just run over bodies like it's flat ground, your body realistically interacts with your surroundings and you can just use your hands to block things jumping or flying at you, the ability to actually just swat a jumping robot away from your face while you are out of ammo is an amazing experience.

Melee is overall great, but "Damage" seems to be finicky, or I am doing something wrong, there are some weapons that just seem to require more precision to hit with. The crowbar seems to need to pretty much hit on either of the pointed ends, as a "glancing blow" from the hook of the crowbar doesn't seem to do basically anything at all sometimes, I could just need some practice, but this is at the same level as Blade & Sorcery I would say.

The story seems to take a lot of elements from one of my childhood favorite games, "Dr Ford", Anomalous materials, simulation, the headcrab type robots and zombie-esque enemies, and even the random messages littered throughout.
The amount of detail is amazing, simple things like helmets being left around that you can put on that have various affects, as well as the little headcrab robot.

Everyone at Stress Level Zero should be proud, this is a phenomenal VR experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall:
Boneworks is okay, but it isn't anything special. The story mode doesn't save progress in each level. The physics aren't much better than other games. The puzzles can be annoying, and I can't play the game for more than 20 minutes without feeling sick. The game may be interesting for some people, but it doesn't have anything more interesting than current VR games. I'd at least wait until a sale to buy the game, then it might be worth it.

Pros:
- Enemies are fun to fight
- Interacting with objects is very easy
- Good length of gameplay
- Story levels are fun to explore

Cons:
- Nausea
- Low amounts of Progress Saving
- Puzzles become an annoyance
- Not much difficulty, rare to die


Nausea:
Let me be clear, I have played a good amount of VR games. I have a pretty good tolerance to VR nausea, but I still get sick while playing Boneworks. I can play Beat Saber, Blade and Sorcery, or Pavlov for hours at a time without feeling sick. I have the usual movement settings the I use in other games, but I still can't play this game for more than 20 minutes at a time.

Gameplay:
The story mode is a combo of puzzles, exploring each level, and fighting enemies. The puzzles aren't difficult, but there is a heavy lack of direction. There is usually a simple solution, but the puzzles end up being more of an annoyance than a fun part of the game. Even though there is a lack of direction, it does make exploring each level pretty fun. The enemies are not difficult to fight. It is very rare to die to an enemy, but that doesn't make it bad. The enemies are fun to play with, and it is fun to get creative.

Progress Saving:
In story mode, your progress is saved when you complete a level. Some levels can be pretty long, so if you want to stop playing for a bit, you will have to restart the level. While this isn't horrible on its own, this combined with feeling sick after playing for 20 minutes makes to annoying to beat a level.

Physics:
Boneworks is advertised to have "advanced physics". While physics are an important part of gameplay, it doesn't feel much different than other VR games. The weight of objects are done fairly well and you can grip object very well, but otherwise that is it. There are many ways to interact with the world, (like different tools and ways to touch/grip on objects), but it isn't too much better than other games.



I know I don't have many hours in the game, but that is because it is difficult to play for long periods of time. Also, I still want to refund the game.\t\t\t\t\t
