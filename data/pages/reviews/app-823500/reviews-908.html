\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo preface, if you do not have your VR legs (you get nauseous quickly, cannot gauge your room in VR or just can't use locomotion.) do not play this game. It is made clear that this game is not for you.

Boneworks is the best VR title I have played.

The Physics Engine in this game is a first in VR and will be unrivaled for quite some time. Like most games when you see a problem that needs to be solved, you come up with a solution. A first for VR, whatever solution you have is correct and can be done.

All in all, for about 15 hours of Story Content (If you want to get the developer gadgets) and endless time spent sandboxing the beautifully crafted levels, Boneworks is a MUST buy for Headset Owners (As long as you have your VR Legs) especially considering its price tag.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo preface I played this with an HTC Vive with Index controllers. I refunded the game and bought again after I got the Index controllers, if you are using the Vive Wands, this is not your game, the controls just do not feel comfortable for more than 5 minutes. Now to the actual review.

This game is fantastic,from the gameplay to the "story" (more on this later), but comes with some jank. Don't trust anyone who compares this game to Alyx because in practice they are completely different games with completely different experiences.

Physics
This is where this game really shines. While yes the physics could be more polished and could be without some random body issues, there isn't really a game that shows off physics to the extent of BONEWORKS.

Gameplay
The guns feel great. Everything works like it should (the two handed guns are reported to glitch out with the body of some people but that never really happened with me). Melee works as it should and generally works great aside from some jank with 2 handed weapons and your body (notice a pattern yet). Enemies are pretty standard and nothing really too remarkable. Levels are really the only gripe I really have with the game being some are really big and you can get really lost not knowing where you can really go and if you are going the right direction or down a random extra path, but I seem to be in the minority on this so maybe I just suck at levels lol. Puzzles are really fall in the physics section, most were never super difficult just took some time to actually pull off.

Story
The Story is well, not much there if you aren't searching for it. First playthrough I pretty much ignored the TV's and beat the game and had a swell time until the ending confused me and I searched it up. And holy crap once you actually go looking for the story written all over the walls this game gets deep with connections to SL0's other games. So if you want a easy rich story, this ain't it chief.

Conclusion
I don't know why I wrote this review, buy the game. You will experience some jank, this game is not Alyx level of polish but that is fine because it is attempting way more. But if you own a vr headset you probably already know of this game so what am I even doing here ok bye.\t\t\t\t\t

The physics speak for themselves. They are awkward and take a while to get used to. They make the game feel alive, and physical.

Story is bad, its just bad. Gameplay makes up for it though.
