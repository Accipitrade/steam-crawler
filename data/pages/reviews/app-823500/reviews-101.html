\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game-changing VR experience, though the launch of Bonelab may overshadow this game I assure you it is worth every penny. There are hiccups and moments of frustration but that goes for most anything and with just a bit of time and effort you'll have your problem fixed. As most people may agree, climbing is one of these hiccups occasionally. The combat is fun and the game is beautiful. The last thing I'd like to talk about for this review is that, in Boneworks, you are given two modes, sandbox and story, in order to unlock items for sandbox you have to find and deposit those items into reclamation bins at the end of every level. For some this may be annoying knowing you are gonna have to replay some of the longer sections because you couldn't carry it all and to some extent, I agree however this also adds a layer of replayability to the game, allowing you to come back and discover more secrets you couldn't possibly have found in one run, make of this mechanic what you will. I sincerely recommend this game to any VR fans who want a thrilling experience, filled with brutal combat, absolute spectacles of environments and enjoyable puzzles.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't like this game and I wouldn't recommend it but I can understand why some people would like it.

I like that stress level zero doesn't do or support crunch, I think it's good this game was made in a more ethical environment.
I also respect stress level zero for trying new things, and pushing vr limits and standards.


I think boneworks is a pretty good sandbox game, but it sells itself as a singleplayer story shooter.
I would consider the story mode boneworks weakest aspect, yet you have to play through it to unlock anything in the other modes.

boneworks lacks in enemy variety (only 6 different enemies + slight remodels with slightly higher/lower health/damage, and two of those enemies only show up in the last level),
lacks in level variety, and weapons within the same category feel samey.
The puzzles are forgettable.
The story telling is about as interesting as videos of people expositioning being played on a crt.
This leaves nothing in the campaign that's interesting, yet it goes on for like 10 hours.

For some reason I didn't have any directional audio whilst playing, whether that's a bug or intended idk.
There's no barking, or choreographing except for the headcrabs.
The material emancipation grills could've also reclaimed things like how the reclamation bins do.
When holding an object it doesn't feel like I'm holding the object itself but like I'm holding a semi-stiff spring attached to the object.


I think they should've made it so that every few levels there'd be section of level (or the entirety of a level) that'd be based around the player using one of the physics weapons that exist.
The enemy encounters should've been more diverse in my opinion (just replacing SOME of the zombies/nullbodies in most encounters with omniprojectors).


I find the world interesting, and a lot of the physics weapons interesting, too bad the game doesn't explore either.
