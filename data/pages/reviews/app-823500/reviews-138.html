\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHeads up: I played Boneworks after Half Life: Alyx, so my judgement will be going back and forth between this one and HLA.

So, Boneworks. I remember watching Brandon making videos about a new hit game he was developing in VR, which had pure physics interactions and a story somewhat inspired by the games he'd played (mostly Half Life, but there are others), and boy was I excited, both to see Brandon's work and to try it at last. Several years after, I finally got myself a Quest 2, and bought this one, together with a plethora of other VR games, including Half Life Alyx, The Walking Dead Saints and Sinners, Blade and Sorcery, and many, many more.

Boneworks is.... very unique, in several ways. It is one of the few, if not the only one that holds no bars when it comes to player comfort. Do you want to climb that 100ft ladder, grab a box, throw it on the ground, then grab a gun and fire from that ladder, only to jump/slip afterwards? You can. Just don't expect any accomodations when it comes to VR sickness. If you are not used to VR, and I do mean REALLY used to it, don't look at this one just yet, it will rock you around from the very first level.

The same can be said for its graphics. Being very CPU bound, several levels will forcibly run at 45fps (and lower) and reproject the image, which is extremely annoying, thus pushing VR sickness even more. This is all running on a Ryzen 3900x @ 4.2ghz, 32gb of ram and an RTX 2080 Super. It wasn't until I got several graphics mods (which add foveated rendering and several other scaling options) that I managed to maintain at least 90fps. Still, they do look great when the art aims for a realistic look (flashlights and fog are absolutely amazing) which, sadly, doesn't occur too often. It is not a game that will make you say "WOW" too often IMO.
While the stylistic choice of the game is definitely odd, what will make you say "wow" out loud is the sheer sense of scale that this game tends to have. At first you'd be walking/running around, but as soon as you look up, you'll see just how large the level is, and as you progress further, you will also see just how FAST you can traverse it, like way faster than you expect.

Gameplay wise, Boneworks holds up nicely, and while I would've loved to see more gun options (seriously? Rifles, SMGs and Pistols only?) they do handle really really well, though there are some artistic choices that are.... questionable (like hitting the magazine with another one on every weapon to take it out). Melee weapons feel really odd, maybe due to the physical nature, but I never felt comfortable using them, so I mainly resorted to either rifles or pistols. Enemies, which are very limited, are sadly, also affected by the physical nature of the engine. Often times you will see them tripping on each other or any other object they have in front of them, will hit them and move forwards. They have nearly no AI, and that can break the sense of immersion, only increasing the difficulty by numbers, rather than by careful positioning or strategy.

Gameplay is not only relegated to just guns though, and this may very well be the best aspect of the game. Having a body that physically interacts with the environment is also a huge plus, as it allows you to truly think outside the box. See a section of a level you want to get through? You could get a battery to open up the main gate, traverse through all of that, kill the enemies and finish it up, or you could just break the glass window, break through the building and skip a few beats, or you could climb up through the pipes, and skip the entire level altogether. It is through these interactions where Boneworks truly shines, and it does so swimmingly.

Story wise, the game is a mixed bag. While there are some absolute genius moments, many of those breaking the fourth wall constantly, others (mainly the story bits) feel rather detached from each other, with only a few of them tying up the story through "cutscenes", which feel out of place considering the enormous potential of VR. In the end, it feels as though the game is a series of levels that exploit the physics engine and the VR medium, rather than tell a solid story.

With that said, I would still recommend Boneworks to pretty much any owner of a VR headset. For a first time developer, this was a huge undertaking which works flawlessly. While it may have its ups and downs, I would still place this one right next to the best VR titles around, and that's saying a lot.

Here's hoping for the next game!\t\t\t\t\t
