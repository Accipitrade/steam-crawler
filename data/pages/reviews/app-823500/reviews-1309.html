\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. It is made for VR enthusiasts, and you can tell that from the opening cutscene which takes place in Valve's original VR room with the simplified QR codes pasted on the wall (filmed on location!) using a late prototype headset. The fact that this takes place in a virtual reality world gives it a really cool atmosphere and allows for a lot of interesting meta commentary. The physics based nature of the game is unique and fun, and makes puzzles a treat...most of the time. It's not a flawless game howerver, I would not recommend this for people who have sensitivities to motion sickness, and it's got some structural design flaws I'll outline below.

Pros:
-Amazing physics simulations
-detailed, interesting world with a lot of great atmospheric touches.
-Cool concept
-Great puzzles
-Fun combat with good gunplay
- Cool accessibility options like stick controlled crouching, which could potentially be useful for people in wheelchairs or with bad knees.

Neutral:
-Everything is a physics simulation, including yourself and weapons. Swinging weapons doesn't sync 1:1 with movement. This is fine, but it's a different style of combat than many are used to. Similar to blade and sorcery, though a little less refined.
-Ammo is currency, which is an interesting dynamic. I've found that I don't blow through ammo fast enough to notice it being an issue, so it hasn't really affected my gameplay
- The levels are large, with some seemingly pointless areas. Not a big deal, but weird to kill a bunch of enemies and then find yourself at a dead end.

Negative:
-Your body is simulated, and what you do with your hands affects your head. Some people will find this disorienting, though it doesn't bother me.
- Graphics are a bit simplistic. This is likely to make the game playable, and I think it's an acceptable sacrifice for a game to run well.
- Because it's physics based, sometimes puzzles can get a bit awkward because you can't seem to flop a board in the right place or something like that
- Climbing feels bad, you're way too heavy and it feels cumbersome. It's also hard to get over ledges.
- Rarely your hand will get stuck in a door or wall and it can be very difficult to get out. This is VERY disorienting and annoying, but rare.
- Checkpoints are very spread out, which means that you better be prepared to go through a whole level in one sitting or you'll have to start over next time you boot up.
- This is minor, but annoys me. Melee weapons will develop floating scuff marks that aren't properly placed on the body of the weapon, and it looks terrible.

Overall, I really enjoy the game despite its flaws. Probably the best VR game I've played to date, this is definitely not "just a tech demo".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been following this games development for a very long time, so the hype was real

*Playing on an htc vive with index controllers*

This game is truly magical it gives you a vibe that you're actually in this world most of the time, feeling like you're being watched and you're not supposed to be where you are. There are some minor physics and collision glitches every once in a while but nothing to really take away from the game.

This game kinda messes with you using the VR medium. In the main there is an exit door after playing for a few hours i head inside of it to quit out of the game. i was waiting... the door is just a space for you to take off your headset and realize you're in VR. So many little details that make you think.

The one thing a lot of people are griping on is the ability not to save, but i feel like it adds to the flair of the game letting you discover different ways around the game.

Overall this game is well worth the hype. especially for only $30! SL0 knocked it out of the park, hopefully this becomes the golden standard for VR games of the future\t\t\t\t\t
