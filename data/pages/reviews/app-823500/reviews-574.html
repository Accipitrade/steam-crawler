\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was immensely disappointing. The "physics" part of the game (nearly the whole game itself) is the most broken and deceiving marketing tool I have ever experienced. Your whole VR body can interact with everything in the game meaning there is no automatic climbing mechanic that is so much easier to use in other games. This makes jumping from ledge to ledge in some levels a pain in the behind. Not only are the physics underwhelming, but the gameplay is about as interesting as playing a demo that overstayed it's welcome. Levels feel empty and puzzles are more confusing than actually intuitive. You are also forced to play through these long climbing sections where the penalty is either falling 200 feet and having to restart (from the area you fell on, as there is no fall damage.) or falling into a death pit and restarting even further backwards. Shooting enemies is fun but can also get really annoying and repetitive as level design allows for no easy way to traverse behind cover from bullets being shot at you. And the most damning thing of this whole mess is that the ending to the game is something of a slap to the face. It is like watching a TV show that hints towards a final climax when there really is no climax. I will not go into spoilers (even though there is barely anything for me to spoil) but once again, it's a definite let down.

A solid 3/10 in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t
