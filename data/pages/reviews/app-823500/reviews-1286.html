\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing 7 hours of this game and thoroughly enjoying every minute of it, I can wholeheartedly recommend this game. It runs smooth on 980Ti, i5-6600K, 16Gb DDR4 using Oculus Rift S. The physics are something to get used to, because your hands do not clip through anything at any point, so you gotta be aware of your hand positioning etc. And apparently this game is a vomit comet as I've heard quite a many ppl getting VR sick in this, even though they usually don't. But if you've never had any issues with that, I highly doubt you do now either.

Few issues that I've found are:
1) At least when using the virtual crouch, the holsters are still located in the standing height, so getting your guns and ammo out when crouching becomes pretty damn hard.
2) You can't grab most of the stuff by using Oculus Touch grip buttons, but you have to use the trigger, which sometimes creates misfiring of your guns. This is a wee bit annoying.
3) Scaling up ledges is a task of it's own... not sure if you're meant to be able to do that, but learning it took me a while. You basically have to grab the ledge, pull yourself up, crouch to the max, lean forward and keep holding the run forward and then release your grip.
4) Even though the levels have checkpoints, I haven't been able to continue from one after I've shut the game down or after a crash (my compu's fault, not the game). And few of the levels are pretty long, so having to start the level from the beginning might be a big bummer. This also means that I usually have to play for few hours straight to get to the end of the level, as I do not dare to quit and take a break, because I might have to start all over again...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t-The gunplay and melee feel weighty and smooth.
Only complaints being: there's not enough weapons in both the melee and firearms pool, and that some of the melee weapons don't even get used in regular play.

-The physics system is incredibly satisfying in VR, albeit a little buggy at times.
Pretty much everything in the game having this level of interaction available to it, makes for very natural feeling encounters, both in and out of combat.

-The freeclimbing system is so much fun and allows for some very creative ways to traverse the level and solve puzzles, although lifting yourself up onto a ledge could do with some improved input. (the climbing system feels like it goes under-utilized in the mid to late sections of the game)

-The story falls a bit short. It's interesting, but doesn't gel too much with the gameplay or universe. With how little we get from the story, it feels as if they're leaving room for expansions. (which would be amazing)

Some things I'd hope for in the future, is any form of Level Editor and Workshop Support. This game would heavily benefit from user-made content. (weapons, levels, enemies, ect.) Plus extra content in any form will help the longevity of the game. Because whilst the story and main levels are fun and all, they can only be fun so many times in a row.

TLDR
Very fun game. Physics and freeclimbing allow for big-brain puzzle solving and exploration of levels. Story is okay, could've been better.
