\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs if Portal and Half-Life had a child that had a Garry's Mod education.

A game with an extraordinary well crafted physics interaction handling and a decently long single player player campaign. It has solid melee and shooting that invites you to experiment. The thing that everything (including player and NPCs) is physics with no prebaked animations, renders this game to a first of its class title. Sandbox and arena gamemodes are also included. No multiplayer so far though.

The textures may look like these are from some bad unfinished Garry's Mod map on monitor, but that is accually by design and looks much much better in VR. The performance is quite decent on a medium powered PCs (AMD R5 1600 + GTX 1060), which is not very easy, but also very important for VR.

In addition smashing crates, melee combat and climbing in-game is surprisingly a quite exhausting workout that makes you fit. It makes your bones work again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompleted on: Oculus Quest 128gb model.

Boneworks is in my opinion one of the best VR games you can get right now. IF, you have strong VR legs.
If you wan't to get this game as your first VR game, atleast try out joystick locomotion before you shill out 25€. Its not gonna be fun if you get nauseous all the time. When you have strong VR legs then you are ready to experience this fantastic game.

Overall movement and gameplay is very nice in this game. Beware, your movement is affected by physics, and as a result you do not clip through objects/ragdolls, which means you can easily get stuck in basically anything thats big enough to block your leg moving forward. Picking up objects and interacting with them is good, and they behave very realistically.

The story can be confusing if you don't pay attention it much, but otherwise its a cool story. The length of the campaign is i would say about 6-10 hours depending on how you wanna approach the levels. It took me 8 hours to complete.

This game requires a pretty nice sized playspace, but i personally have about a 1x2m space and played standing and had the seated mode turned on, and i encountered minimal collisions with objects in real life.
As for the hardware requirements? I personally have 16gb of RAM, i7-9700k and a RTX 2060 Super OC'd, and encountered stutters in melee combat very upclose, even when turning down settings a tad bit. I personally don't get motion sick from lower than HMD Hz framerate, so it only bothered me in the looks department.

Combat in this game is fantastic. Really. Its very good. Enemies are affected by physics, so if you run out of ammo, you could for example tackle the enemy and reload while the enemy is getting up. Almost anything you try to do with the enemy you think you could in real life, you most likely can.

Problems i find annoying in this game is, that your head is a physics object too, so if it gets stuck in a ceiling, it starts moving on its own, and also the grabbing system needs a toggle button, my hand gets tired when its holding the grip button for hours on end. But those are my own gripes with this game.

Overall i would give this a solid 9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t
