\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Great game, most replayable VR game I own (gotta reclaim 'em all).

I bought this game at launch and by the time I was half-way through the Museum level I was hooked. I'm an adult and few games have made me feel that sense of wonder that the first hour of Boneworks did. Of course that sense of wonder was quickly replaced with nausea, but over the updates the developers have done a lot to mitigate motion sickness and improve performance (assuming you have decent VR legs built up to begin with).

I'm on my 3rd play-through of the game and have yet to have found and reclaimed (through the use of the game's reclamation bins) all of the collectibles sprinkled along the levels. Every play-through I've seen things and areas I've missed or found additional easter eggs. The amount of time developers must have spent hiding things is mind boggling. I'm not normally a "collector" but the way the developers hid some of the items in places where most developers would think "they could never get here" makes getting them so much more rewarding.

Some of the mechanics do occasionally feel a bit "janky" (especially climbing where my arms would often get stuck on things I didn't anticipate), but the gun-play and the sheer number of times you'll think to yourself "that really worked!" well make up for those moments.

My only real complaint would be that after getting decent weapons there are seldom times where taking on enemies is actually challenging (even in Arena challenge modes). Combat can still be very fun as you find creative or cinematic ways to take out enemies, but the problem with being John Wick in VR is that you already know how every encounter is going to end... alone with a pile of bodies and no dog to keep you company.

All in all I think this is a must buy IF you are a fan of games like Half Life 2, great physics engines, or feeling like a badass in VR. The developers appear to be updating this game after launch so this great game is only going to improve.\t\t\t\t\t
