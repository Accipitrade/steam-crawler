\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game a few months ago and was very put off by the lack of a save feature. I couldn't play the game because I would be too nauseated after about 40 minutes. Fortunately that has been mostly resolved now with the introduction of check points. If you are making reasonable progress then you can find a checkpoint every 20 to 30 minutes; this is much better than only getting to save every hour or two.

The gun-play is very fun, and I think most people would find this to be the highlight of Boneworks, especially when combined with the amazing physics. That said, the physics are brought down a peg or two by the fully physical body. A lot of the puzzles can be very tedious with the physical body, and so I often see the physical body as an obstacle to overcome, rather than a feature of the game. Also, climbing is extremely nauseating. There is a section towards the end with some climbing and its just really janky. I made it to the top and fell and just quit because my stomach wouldn't let me do it a second time. I think with more work, a physical body could be made more enjoyable, but as of right now I don't find it very amusing. This is especially made apparent when I try to bring a gun out from my shoulder, and it gets caught on the body, or when I'm holding on the handles of a lift, and then the arm gets stuck inside the grip.

Overall, I'm having a great time with the game, and I highly recommend it to anyone with a vr headset. The game-play is very fun outside of what I mentioned above.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBar none the best VR game I have played. Satisfying gunplay and incredible melee combat with great use of physics. There are guns ranging from pistols to rifles (strangely no shotguns). You can fight barehanded to pull out an enemy's leg from under them then pick them up off the floor, hold both their arms then headbutt them, or just box them until they fall. Melee combat lets you attack with every part of the weapon. Jab a baseball bat into an enemy before doing a full swing, attack with the cross guard of a claymore, or smash the shaft of a shovel to push an enemy back before stabbing the end into them. The main story has plenty of exploration and physics based puzzles like something you'd see in a Half-Life game.

There are only two "but"s. The first is that this game is not interested at all in slowly introducing VR to players. You should be quite comfortable with VR in general before playing this game. There is only smooth locomotion (walking around) and your entire body is represented in-game. That means you have to be able to recognize where your body is in real life versus what your in-game body is doing in general in addition to not getting motionsick. Its not that big of an issue unless you start climbing or get too close to objects but it can be disorienting. The second is that the game has a limited number of enemy types and feels like a proof of concept in general though it does have a roughly 10 hour singleplayer without counting the post-game combat arena or the sandbox. I'd still highly recommend it to anyone who feels like they have got a good grip on VR games.\t\t\t\t\t
