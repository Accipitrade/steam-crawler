\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- Feels like a real game and not an unfinished demo
- Physics are cool

Cons:
- Motion Sickness 2: Electric Boogaloo
- No saves
- The physics aren't foolproof, but the whole game revolves around them

First game to make me motion sick in like three years. Some of the first experiments when VR went mainstream were bad, but then developers figured out how to not make people vomit and locomotion hasn't been an issue since then... until now. I think it's because all of your movements have a brief acceleration period because eVeRyThInG mUsT bE pHySiCs BaSeD, even though everyone figured out three years ago that it's the acceleration that makes artificial locomotion so vomit-inducing.

I could almost forgive the game for taking the physics too far because the physics are genuinely unlike anything I've seen before. It also feels like a proper video game unlike most VR games that feel just like unfinished demos. But then again the game makes you stack and climb on wobbly boxes and swing on chandeliers just to show off the physics and to be like HL2, with no regard for player comfort.

You could always take a break and try to fight the motion sickness, but oh wait... The game doesn't have saves within levels. So you must play for an hour or two without breaks or you lose your progress. That doesn't combine well with the motion sickness.

And even though the physics are mostly great, they still get in the way more often than not. Imagine trying to fight zombies while covered in wooden crates and you have no sensation in your hands or arms, and your legs are paralyzed. That's how this game feels. Your arms get stuck in/behind/around objects, you randomly grab stuff like concrete walls because you didn't keep your fingers completely extended (Index controllers), you can't vault over any obstacle
- even though your character can lift human-sized enemies no problem - just because your legs get caught on it. And if you try to grab something from a shelf you'll most likely just grab the whole shelf through the object and whip yourself airborne for some extra motion sickness. Then you break open a crate with a key in it, and the key falls through the floor. Oh well.\t\t\t\t\t
