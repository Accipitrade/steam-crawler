The only real advantage of this game is that guns are fun to shoot. That's it. The plot is absent, there are literally 3 types of enemies, physics "puzzles" (that have been outdated for like 15 years. I'd list other things that the game does bad, but there's literally nothing else to this game other than that. You go through repetitive levels, shooting repetitive enemies with 4 (yes, there are only 4 guns in this game - pistol, uzi, mp5, m4a1) guns, doing glitched puzzles until you get sick of it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that Bonelab is out, it's getting all the love for being VR Gmod, but never forget those who paved the way.

After finishing the game, Boneworks has a physics engine Blade And Sorcery only wishes it could have. The physical presence in the game and the terrifyingly close to reality world you explore makes this game one of the most immersive I've ever played. I kept myself entertained for hours with things as simple as a mug and a crowbar, simply appreciating how every last polygon in this infinitely detailed world has a hitbox. The story itself is a bit difficult to follow along, but it is still fascinating and keeps you going just to see what happens next. Now, I may or may not have downloaded a 100% save file afterwards just so that I could enjoy all the fun sandbox stuff after I was done, but the progression system for the sandbox will add hours of replay value for more patient players.

This is not bone and sorcery. This is so much more. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t
