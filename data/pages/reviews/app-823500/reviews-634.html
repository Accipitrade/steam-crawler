\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI gotta say, this is definitely a solid VR game. I enjoyed my time with it fairly well, and playing it felt like playing a VR version of Portal and Half-life. The puzzles felt satisfying, the gunplay was decent, and story concept was pretty neat as well. However, be warned that there is a LOT of jankiness in this game. Everything in game has physics interactions, including your entire body. This leads to awkward movement and handling of items, not to mention atrocious climbing. Also, the enemies are very easy, and very hard at the same time. They don't do a lot of damage, and die quickly, but somehow found ways to shoot me through cover. All of this may sound bad, but if you're willing to accept that the game won't be the smoothest VR experience, it's definitely worth a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a MUST-PLAY for anyone with a VR headset and half-decent PC. The physics based gameplay feels amazing, with everything being interactable, and your hands can't just phase through things. The enemies are terrifying (mainly the headcrabs), and they got me to jump every time I saw one. The story has more depth to it than meets the eye (which most people don't seem to see), and it links to another stress level zero game (duck season). The story mode has about 7-8 hours of gameplay. And even after that, you still have the sandbox levels and arena modes (which i enjoyed quite a lot).
Your PC doesn't have to be amazing to play it, either. I played it on the quest 2 virtual desktop app on a PC with an I5-3470 and GTX 1060 3GB combo, and it played just fine (Granted, there were a few hiccups here and there)
All in all, this game is a must play if you have a VR headset. It's my favorite VR game and is worth the $30 it costs. I would recommend to any VR user.

Edit: I forgot to mention the music. Ohhhh, the music! Michael Wyckoff knocked it out of the park with the boneworks ost. Give it a listen, it's definitely going on my normal playlist.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou know that Hollywood depiction of VR, as a virtual world you're teleported into, where you have the same control over your body that you do in real-life? That's Boneworks. You're able to climb basically anything that you should be able to in real-life and go where you please, to the point where you can easily sequence-break the game. Objects respond as you would expect (i.e plywood will bend when you stand on it, but will break if you repeatedly jump on it, a basketball will bounce to the expected height when you hit it etc). Special mention should go to the detailed collision meshes, there's basically no invisible walls on objects, so you're able to do things like shoot between the tiny gap in a fence or hook a crowbar into the handle of a locker to open it.

The game obviously is all about simulating as much as it can with physics, sometimes to the detriment of the player, like getting the crowbar stuck on your neck when you try to take it off your back, or the player's body getting in the way. If this sounds like it might annoy you, Boneworks might not be for you. But if you're keen on the idea of a technically-advanced VR game, you'll probably be able to look past these issues.

The biggest issue with the game's physics system is that opening doors and drawers is very glitchy compared to Alyx. In Alyx, door and drawers open and smoothly, but in Boneworks, they shoot open with a lot of force and often drag the object they're attached to with them, even if it should be something heavy like a desk or filing cabinet. And when you try to close them, they often bounce back open again. As I understand it, Valve spent a LOT of time on this part of Alyx, so I assume it's difficult to get right..?

The game is very polished, one of the handful of early VR games that feels like a full and complete game, despite it's low price. It has a decent-length single-player campaign (around as long as Half-Life 2). The gunplay also feels excellent, though it's missing some of the technical features that Alyx has, like being able to release magazines (you use your hand to just pull them out) or dropping the slide (you can only pull it back). The two-handed weapons feel great, they feel solid in your hands, they don't jitter or wobble when you don't have your hands in exactly the right place like in some games.

The first-person model also looks great, the limbs don't freak-out too often and the positions of your elbows aren't too far off where your actual elbows are. The run animation does look a little odd though, like more of a fast walk, probably in an effort to avoid using an animation that would result in the player's vision being bounced around.

The game also makes excellent use of the Index controllers. You can change your grip on melee weapons by slightly loosing your grip on the controller and the game even tracks your index finger when you're holding a gun to show if you have your finger on the trigger or not (Alyx doesn't do that!).

In short, if you're looking for a high-quality, polished single-player VR game with a good amount of content, you NEED to pickup Boneworks! Just keep in mind it's intended for advanced VR players and is a bit experimental.\t\t\t\t\t
