\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely a must-play. One of the best games to take advantage of the capabilities of VR.

One thing worth noting is the ammo system. It's not exactly realistic, but that's not necessarily a bad thing. Ammo count is decreased only when you fire a bullet. Ammo in magazines doesn't actually count. That means you can reload before your gun is empty and not waste ammo, but it also means you can run out of ammo, grab another gun that you had preloaded, and find it empty.

Pros:
- Crouching/jumping in game can be done via controls or by doing so IRL
- Large weapon variety - both guns and melee/throwable weapons
- All melee weapons can be thrown
- All weapons can be wielded one or two handed with varying degrees of success, e.g. two hands on an assault rifle doesn't work well, but two hands on a handgun reduces recoil
- Reloading guns is done physically
- Decent enemy variety
- Great sound effects
- Good variety of challenges and other things to do in the various arenas
- If you go to put another magazine in your gun the old one will just fall out. It took me way too long to realize this.
- Due to how the ammo system works you won't waste ammo by reloading a half-empty magazine without picking up the old one

Cons:
- While there is a huge variety of fantasy melee weapons, very few of these are available through the story
- Supposedly there was some sort of story, but a lot of it went over my head, it wasn't remotely clear what was going on beyond the general theme of being in VR (in the story) and the 'VR world' getting taken over by AI or something like that.
- Due to how the ammo system works, if you run out of ammo and pull out a different gun thinking 'this has a full magazine'... no it doesn't, you ran out of ammo.

Verdict: 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
