\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo people who get motion sick: IT GETS BETTER

I get severely carsick and was pretty upset when I noticed Boneworks doesn't offer teleportation movement. Being new to VR I bought this game and was sick before I even made it to the first lever in the tutorial room. I put it down for some time but eventually played in small sessions, taking a break whenever I got sick (DON'T push through it, it won't work). After a handful of sessions I could play for longer, eventually being able to play Boneworks for 5 hours straight without even a hint of sickness and without having to compromise how I move.

As far as gameplay goes, now that I have some time in VR (400 hours in VR overall) Boneworks is the crème de la crème of VR action games, on tier with Half Life: Alyx and H3VR as far as polish and quality, though I think those belong to a different category. What really sets Boneworks apart from others is the physics engine, it leads to some serious montage moments that I just haven't gotten in other games. From parkour, to crazy pistol shots or clutch melee, its honestly a lot of fun.

The main story itself has replayability because of how diverse the whole game is, but even if that's not your style, there's tons of different sandbox and side modes that are available. I'm excited to see more stuff coming out of Stress Level Zero and for other devs to catch up in this genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can enjoy a good physics based game, but this one takes it to the next level. Now, granted, that's not necessarily a good thing. I can appreciate what they're trying to do in making it so that your virtual body interacts with everything in its path, considering you're not just a floating pair of hands in real life. However, sometimes this can truly get in the way of the obstacles you're trying so desperately to circumvent. It's interesting how the physics can work in your favor sometimes, as the developers clearly state that the solution to every issue isn't so cut in dry in that you can occasionally cheat the game and take advantage of its mechanics in order to solve the increasingly difficult puzzles you're presented with. Overall, Boneworks plays with some cool ideas and offers a unique challenge that is as fun tackle as it is overwhelmingly frustrating.

On a side note, and mostly irrelevant, I had no idea at first that this was made, in part, by one of the guys from NODE. I haven't watched much of their YouTube content, but I've watched a lot of Freddy Wong videos. So when I learned that Brandon from Freddy's original social/video-making circle was one of the developers, I thought that was pretty cool.\t\t\t\t\t
