\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a really interesting step forward for VR in many ways, but don't expect everything to be leagues better than your current favorite VR game.

My personal opinion is games like Onward, Pavlov, H3VR all have better gun handling and overall feel to them.

Melee is very similar feeling to Blade & Sorcery. In some ways Boneworks is better, in some ways B&S is better at melee handling.

The main game is essentially levels filled with mini-puzzles, weapons and enemies to torture. Roughly 10-15 hours to complete the first time. Basic story is present. A sandbox arena is unlockable to play with physics and weapons to your heart's content.

What really sets BW apart from other games is obviously the physics. Every item can be interacted with. Going forward into the future of VR, all games will feel like they're lacking something if they do not replicate what Boneworks has done. It's quite obviously essential once you've experienced it.

If BW can pick up the slack on their weapons, I'd give the game a 9/10 for VR standards. But as the product is RIGHT now, I can only give a 7/10 strictly because I expected more from how the weapons handled. If you have zero expectations of how a gun should function, then you may not care at all.\t\t\t\t\t
