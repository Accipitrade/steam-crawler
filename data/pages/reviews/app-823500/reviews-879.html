\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game barely functions.

Boneworks bombards you with a constant stream of broken physics puzzles. The physics-based player movement, an unresponsive jump button, and climbing (more liable to get your body snagged on something rather than actually get you anywhere) make the simple task of moving around a chore. Your hands frequently get stuck inside physics objects. Shooting is average to mediocre, with the bulk of gun-toting enemies giving very little feedback when they are shot. Melee weapons slide off enemies and generally feel loose and unsatisfying.

I constantly accidentally remove the magazine from my gun instead of holding my gun in both hands, pull out ammo from my hip instead of retrieving my weapon from my left armpit holster, and get a melee weapon stuck in my shoulder (!) while trying to draw it from my shoulder holster.

I have softlocked myself several times or found physics puzzles stop working entirely, forcing me to reload from a checkpoint -- checkpoints which did not exist before an update added them to the game.

I am not impressed. On paper, a fully physically simulated virtual space sounds like the fantastical dream game we were waiting for -- it sounds like the future VR needs! Instead, Boneworks suggests that a fully physically simulated VR game was a mistake.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMaybe I have been spoiled by HL: Alyx (even though I bought it almost at release, refunded and bought it now again), but I just do not enjoy this. It just feels really clunky to play and maybe even a bit unfinished. I can appreciate the idea of having your whole body be physical in the game, but I feel it gets in the way more than it helps and it gets reeeally frustrating (like with the puzzles...). The sandbox part of the game is pretty cool, but I feel that you buy this for the campaign because it was marketed that way.

I do not enjoy the look of the game pretty much at all. I get it that it is the style of the game, to look like it is unfinished, but that is what it looks like now. They have a game that looks unfinished, which is the point and I get that but still, it is still what it looks like. Original but not a good look.

I will give it alot of credit because it is pretty much the first of its kind: a true VR game and more than just a tech demo. There is a story and I have heard that is is good, but I dunno. Just was not for me, but because of what it is compared to most VR titles around I will recommend it. But I would still hugely recommend HL: Alyx over this if you are looking for a "real" VR game.

5/10 (7 on a VERY good day)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tboneworks is an excellent game, and i think by itself it's advanced the vr market years ahead of where it would be otherwise, because it shows future devs a ton of things that do work and a ton of things that don't work.

because of this, it's inconsistent. it has great moments, but they feel buried in a slog of frustrating, monotonous moments. the absolute worst part of boneworks by far is the physics. which sucks, because they lean heavily into physics puzzles; you can tell the devs are in love with valve.

manipulating heavy physics objects is bothersome, heavy melee combat is inconsistent, and worst of all, climbing is frustrating, nauseating, and half finished. i understand what's trying to be done with it, but even though h3vr's climbing is less "realistic", it's leagues ahead of boneworks in fun, intuitiveness, and accessibility. platforming puzzles in boneworks (which are abundant) usually involve trying to cross some sort of bridge or ladder, falling off several times, and taking a full couple minutes each time to walk back to the starting point. gets old quick.

also, as for the story: there's none to speak of. the level design is good enough that it doesn't matter, but this means most of the game is spent traversing a linear gauntlet without actually having any clue of why other than it has something to do with a corporation and that you spent $24 on this game. this ties into boneworks' absolute biggest flaw, and it's one that's prolific among current vr games, and that's that it isn't a cohesive experience.

that being said, what boneworks gets right, it gets right. melee combat is the most satisfying i've seen when it actually works properly, the gunplay is pulled off well, and some of the puzzles are fun. the virtue of being a full length singleplayer campaign on its own is a title it only shares with alyx. there isn't a game out there that can be described as "boneworks but better", and things will probably stay that way for a while.

boneworks is a must play for anyone that is considering getting into vr development, and that's something i can say confidently. you can tell the developers put a ton of work into the game; it's about as polished as it can be, but the concepts that are inherently fun and the concepts that are inherently frustrating are important to keep in mind when translating your ideas into a vr environment.

like many other games i reviewed, it's really deserves an "ehh" more than a "yes" or "no", but i'm going to give it a "no" because if you expect something that lives up to the hype that surrounded boneworks, that isn't what you're going to get.\t\t\t\t\t
