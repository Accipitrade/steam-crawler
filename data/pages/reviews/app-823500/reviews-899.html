\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRight...

...Making every asset/model/item a physics object, and promoting it on Youtube across various channels for 2 years, does NOT automatically mean that this is top VR game, or that this is the "next generation" of VR. First of all, if you have anything other than the Valve index controllers, dont come near this game. To hold something in this game you have to permanently be holding the grip button. People who love the game will tell you "you just need strong hands theres nothing wrong with that", when all the devs need to do is make it toggle grip once youve pressed it like every single other "previous gen" game on the market when not using the index. Then theres the mechanics, your characters hands arms and body are all made out of rubber, you will flop about the whole time and jumping is so janky its a joke. Melee and climbing in blade and sorcery is way better, so much so that i wasnt impressed at all. The gun play is cool, but nowhere near the feeling raw data gives you with the double pistols, or super hot, i never felt like "wow this is so cool". The guns themselves are impressive models, and as are the tutorial levels (for the first 5 mintues), but the main "story" is painfully boring, gameplay and narrative wise.

This game DOES have a lot (not all) the good features from other VR games, but it does them all to a basic, unimpressive standard. All the features they have borrowed to try and make the complete VR experience has ended up making a stale and boring experience where you can do (almost) everything, just not very well.

BUT, if you are someone who is going to get huge enjoyment from being able to knock everything over, or pick up everything you see, then get this game, but only if you cant afford half life alex, because again, half life will do everything that boneworks does well, but better.\t\t\t\t\t
