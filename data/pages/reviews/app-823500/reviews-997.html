\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCool idea, but really bad story, some missions are super short, others are super long, no interesting or fun weapons during the story like in half life, little enemy variety, and all around the hyper-focus on physics above all else honestly makes this a much worse shooter than it could be.

My biggest gripe is the hyper focus on physics. One issue I have is that the game does not let me use my arms to their real-life length and cuts like 2-3 inches off for some absurd reason. Another is that the weight and balance combined with the no-dismemberment ancient simulation of gunshot damage for melee weapons makes any and all melee weapons very awkward and hard to use. Two handed weapons especially suck, they are way too heavy, easily get stuck on things, and dont really do enough damage to justify their awkwardness.

If your hands ever glitch through an object, sucks to be you, your whole body is a physics object and you cant stop it and have to spend a few minutes trying to un-stuck yourself. if your VR pinkie finger gets caught on something that your IRL body completely avoided, sucks to be you, your hand is now useless for a while in the middle of a fight, despite using your hands IRL properly and trying to avoid getting caught on stuff.

Guns are fine, but lack variety. My biggest gripe about guns is that you literally start from scratch on weapons every single level. Many levels have a ton of guns literally right at the end of the level and only like 2 from the start until then. Being totally stripped of your weapons every single level gets old fast, and it just seems lazy to me.

Overall, it's not a BAD game, but it's really not that great. It's novel, what with the fully-physics-based everything, but as soon as HL:Alyx or other VR games with more substance come out this will be forgotten. They took the physics interactability too far IMHO, especially without full body tracking, and it ends up being far more frustrating than fun to have a physical body in VR.

Also, for oculus at least, the grip controls are whack. Many objects require both trigger and handle buttons to pick up, including guns and stuff that are activated by the trigger. Some guns, mainly MP-5s, also have a lot of trouble cocking or holding the front grip because the game decides that you really want to unload your gun instead.

The whole game isn't bad, but it's honestly so unpolished on so many levels that I can't recommend it even if I enjoyed it marginally more than I disliked it\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs I'm writing this BONEWORKS is the peak of VR gaming for me so far. Games like this are what VR is meant for.

I believe this is the first game to be designed around the Index controllers, and it works really well. Seeing your individual fingers move, grabbing things, pushing objects around without pressing any buttons makes for a better and more realistic VR experience than anything else I've played.

The game gives off pretty big Half-Life vibes, which is a good thing. There's barely any hand-holding, and a lot of items and secrets are subtly hidden in the levels, which makes exploration feel very rewarding at times. When you pull of some cool combat trick or find a clever way to complete a puzzle you actually feel like you did something cool that the game didn't tell you to do. There's a lot of freedom to just try stuff and see if it works, and if it does the game doesn't mind, since it was designed that way.
Combat is solid, gunplay feels great, slow-mo is a neat addition, the puzzles are fun, movement works nicely. It just all feels and works good.
If Half-Life Alyx wasn't about to release, this really would've been the closest to Half-Life 3 we're gonna get.

I consider this game a must-have for VR owners who own the Index-controllers.\t\t\t\t\t
