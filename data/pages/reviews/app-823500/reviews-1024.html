\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks sells itself as many things: a VR game that's for experienced VR players only, one for those comfortable with having the VR training wheels off, and a game not for the faint of heart or those with a weak stomach. These marketing points, seemingly embedded within the design of the game itself are deflecting from the truth. Boneworks isn't a good or well made game, and its problems start from its fundamentals.

It has an abundance of glaring weaknesses stemming from its physics engine, which dictates the behaviour of all objects including the player avatar. Every interaction, from locomotion to melee combat is hampered to the frustration of the player. Objects wobble and seize within your hands or against the environment, or cause your first person perspective to lurch violently in unexpected directions. An early interaction had me use a hammer to destroy planks of wood barring a gate, and occasionally the hammer would get stuck inside the wood planks, launching my own player avatar upwards towards the ceiling. Another common interaction, climbing, causes the player's head to wobble around. Simply having your in game hands collide with objects in the environment at all can propel your head in the opposite direction.

Your player character will unexpectedly slow down or cease to move, or suddenly accelerate when traversing because of some completely unintuitive and unrealistic interaction with the environment, such as a collision between a held object and your own body moving forward, or a part of your simulated body colliding with the environment, despite your own body not matching up to the simulation.

In combat, enemies fall over themselves like tangled marionettes before you've even approached them, completely nonthreatening as they struggle to stand up straight, let alone do much to harm you. Your simulated hands and arms wobble when using melee weapons, even when your own body remains still, making melee combat especially feel less like you're inhabiting a space and more like your movements are translated to manipulating a prosthetic body comprised of rubber limbs and slinky toys held together by elastic ties.

The quality of the physics engine makes or breaks a game like this, as it's so heavily dependent on it for its physics based puzzles, exploration and combat. And though at times the game shows promise with puzzles and combat scenarios that are able to tackled in creative fashion, most of the time its interactions only highlight how much better the simulated physics within the game needs to be to have its physics gameplay work. The fight against its physics engine is the biggest challenge to the player, rather than any part of its gameplay design. As it is, Boneworks feels like a frustrating simulation of what it might be like to be an avatar in a physics enabled videogame like Gang Beasts or Mount Your Friends, populated by NPCs of a similar nature.

Boneworks shows a glimpse of what the future of a heavily physics based game in VR might hold, but its lack of a solid foundation and physics engine makes it more frustrating than fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI do like the idea of this game alot but I really don't like how empty and wonky everything feels in the game. Due to the wonkiness and the awkwardness of the IK body they give you, I cant play the game for more than 25 odd minutes without feeling like im going to barf. The nausea i get with this game is overbearing and Its a shame because I never had that problem with any other title. If they can improve gunplay to onward standards, ill gladly join back in but for now, the game feels empty and unpolished. Also, if you don't have any time in day where you can hop on for a few hours, don't play this game as there are no checkpoints for each level meaning if you leave during one, you're going to have to start all over again. Iv'e gotten STUCK on a level because of this mechanic making it impossible for me to even see the rest of the game.

(edit) - They fixed the saving problem by adding in a saving terminal between major points of each level. Still though the physics feel kinda wonky and unrealistic. Perhaps if they gave the option to turn off the IK body it would feel better (as half life alyx did, sacrificing the IK body for a more enjoyable experience). Still feels pretty empty.\t\t\t\t\t
