\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCannot recommend for the current price of $30. The game plays sort of like a proof of concept for Half-Life: Alyx. The problems are numerous, but the most fatal issue is that all but one of the gun sights are not aligned with the trajectory of projectiles, which is fatal and, frankly, unforgivable. The performance is lousy. There are inexplicable stutters and reprojection, even though my frame draws are occurring between 6-9 ms for GPU and about half that for CPU, with no missing or late frames coinciding with the stuttering. The graphics are elementary, suitable maybe for pre 10-series GTX cards, which I think is too low a bar. Most of the sounds aren't right for the materials, and are too loud, which draws me from my suspension of disbelief. Finally, at about 6 hours in and I still have no idea who I am, why I'm there, and why I should care. That is to say, there is no plot to speak of.

In terms of good things, the human interface portion of this game (menus, controls, customization of the same) was made by a mad genius that could see the future. It's been my experience that the puzzles are pretty good so far, nicely achieving just the right difficulty to be rewarding but not frustrating. The way the player movement accelerates and decelerates feels nice (but makes jumping with precision frustrating). The rate of accelerating for falling takes a lot of the sting out of mistimed jumps, and helps people like me navigate high places while mitigating our rational fear of falling. I like that there is physics-based everything, which was forward thinking. The doors swing ladders (and more) grab, and there is realistic-ish weapon handling that is easy to use. I'd say most of the development of this game was getting the controls just right.

I'd recommend this game if it were half the price. Get it on a sale.\t\t\t\t\t
