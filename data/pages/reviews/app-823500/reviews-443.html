\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPremise:
In the highly immersive and soon-to-release VR workspace, MythOS city, time has suddenly stopped at 3:14, dropping the game into hard lockdown and kicking out all the game's developers - except for you. Journey to the clock tower to reset the game's clock, avoid and fight back against the rogue AI attempting to purge the system, and unravel the mystery of why you're the only one left.

Gameplay: 9/10 (Almost Perfect)
The gameplay of Boneworks is finely-tuned to give you the feeling of controlling a physical body in VR. It requires you to play along in a sense - for example, trying to quickly swing a heavy object like a sledgehammer will result in your character freaking out and will look like a bug, but if you mime the action of handling the weight of the object, it moves perfectly with your real-world actions and feels great. Guns, especially two-handed rifles, also feel great. Melee weapons lack a bit of punch, making guns almost always the better option as opposed to repeatedly whacking enemies over the head and not doing any discernible damage. My only complaint here is that some mechanics just don't consistently cooperate. For example, storing objects on your back only works about 60% of the time, causing you to endlessly drop objects on the floor behind your back. Climbing up ledges is also an inconsistent nightmare and seemingly works only when it chooses to.

Story: 8/10 (Superb)
Haunting, mysterious, and complex. You play the villain-protagonist seeking to claim immortality for yourself by any means necessary, an unusual role, and one not typically explored in media. My only complaint here is that the story is so non-obvious on first playthrough that some of the intentional story choices (intentionally unfinished levels, for example) feel like actual game oversights and can detract from the experience. If I only realize how cool the game was after looking up the story once the game is over, there might be room for improvement in how the story is communicated.

Soundtrack: 10/10 (Perfect)
An epic, beautiful soundtrack that perfectly accentuates the experience.

Puzzles: 5/10 (Average)
Many of the puzzles in the game boil down to pulling or pushing a single lever or handle in the environment - however, most of the time, you will be dealing with geometry that is unlike anything you would see in real life, and thus you might feel like you're stuck for a long time before finally finding the lever you need to pull. The puzzles are fun enough once you know how to interact with them, but finding the lever or handle you need to interact with is usually a toss-up that requires a keen eye.

Overall: 8/10 (Superb)
One of the first VR masterpieces. Highly recommended to anyone interested in seeing what advanced VR has to offer. Know your rig before buying - some levels are extremely performance intensive and may cause some PCs that are not at the top end of performance to chug.\t\t\t\t\t
