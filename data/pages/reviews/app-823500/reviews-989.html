Best VR game to date

Seriously, I don't ever really use my VR Headset and its very seldom I do.
And I have been playing this game long periods of time.

I hope Half-Life Alyx keeps me this engaged.

I own the HTC VIVE Headset just to let others know.

So I thought I'd grab a metal Drum that was empty and hold it in front of me and walk forward. I was able to shield myself as the turret did fire but I was able to get around it.

Final Verdict. Some will love it and others may hate it. But I enjoyed it more than I thought I would.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tso the idea of this game is great. a lot of potential, and im sure that for the right person youll love it. but what upsets me the most is having a sandbox mode you have to jump through numerous hoops to get to and enjoy. why would you not create a game that people can enjoy how they want to? not only do you have to jump through hoops to OBTAIN sandbox mode, you have to play through the story, which in my opinion is tedious and less than entertaining, to even amass guns/materials to BRING to said sandbox. and these locations to warp these items to that place are few and far between. so even if you happen to find a glock/ AR/ what have you, it cant be sent there unless you happen to come across one of these drop zones. its ridiculous. i regret spending my money on this if not just for the inconsideration. let me play the game how i want. it was marketed as a hyper realistic gun simulator and here we are finding out its a tedious and boring puzzler/story game. and you HAVE to play it that way to have free use of the product you paid for. Id rather just play hot dogs horseshoes and hand grenades.

Edit: to be clear, I have not actually played 500+ hours of this game. maybe 10 hours tops, I was just irresponsible with my previous rig and would frequently just set the headset down and not touch my pc for weeks at a time while games were still running. Even with the 100% sandbox save, its entertainment value does not last that long.\t\t\t\t\t
