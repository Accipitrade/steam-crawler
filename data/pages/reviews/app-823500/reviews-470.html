The best VR game ever.

I'm writing this review after BONELAB because I didn't really give it a proper review before. BONELAB was good and all, but BONEWORKS... Literally AMAZING. You may think that i'm joking that this is the best vr game, right? No. I know Half-Life Alyx exists, and it's another Half-Life game after 16 years, but BONEWORKS was the most innovative VR game to ever hit this platform. Everything about this game changed the standard of VR forever.

-- GAMEPLAY --
The gameplay of BONEWORKS is its best attribute. It is an addictive, fun, puzzle/action/sandbox physics based game. Everything feels responsive and amazing. Now lets talk about the weapons. The melee weapons are ok, but they could use some work. HOWEVER, the guns feel AMAZING to use and are so satisfying! Also, the puzzles add a sense of "protection" or "mystery" to the game. These all make the game have a good balance of "I'm gonna cry," "This is the most insane thing I have ever done," and "I'm definitely getting put on an FBI watch list."

-- STORY --
The story of BONEWORKS seems small at first. You might not even think there's a story! Finding lore in this game is hidden in clipboards around the enviornments, with subtle additions. However, the story the game does present once you figure it out is wonderful. I don't know if I have it fully correct, but to sum it up with what I know (without spoiling the whole game,) you're name is Arthur Ford and you work for the company MONOGON which has its own virtual city named Myth OS. However, Myth OS is hiding something grand. They have this virtual place called "The Void" where its some connection in the middle from the virtual world and the real world. But, if you reach this place, you can become immortal and can do anything you want. Ford (his first name is Arthur but everyone calls him his last name, Ford) knowing this information, tries to sabotage MONOGON and make it go on lockdown so only he can access Myth OS. He does this by putting a USB from Gammon (MONOGON's rival) into MONOGON's database. Once Ford reaches Myth OS, he traverses the landscapes while fighting for his life with weapons found around the virtual space. I'm not saying anymore, as you should learn the rest yourself!

-- GRAPHICS --
"Oh it's a Unity game. The graphics must be terrible." Nope. Nuh uh. Nada. This game was released in 2019, but it still holds up in looks in 2023. In fact, BONEWORKS' graphics look better than BONELAB's graphics when BONELAB was released in late 2022. (This is mostly because BONEWORKS was a PC only release while BONELAB was a PC and Quest release, but still.) The attentions to detail in this game are subtle, but look pretty good. Infact, my pc still dies on max graphics to this day. (I can run BONELAB at max graphics fine.)

-- MUSIC --
OH MY GOD THE MUSIC. GHJUIDOSHGF*UOidsuhgiupofshjdgiufdhgoiudfh
The music in this game is spectacular. It always gives you the right emotion in the right places. Take a level like the Streets. The music is calm, and mysterious. It gives you the right feeling for the first level of the game where you can be attacked. But then, take a level like Tower or Warehouse. The music adjusts from mystery and fear to action and energy as you are fighting your enemies. (Also the full soundtrack goes SO HARD. I will just casually listen to it on Spotify.) To sum it up, the music is phenominal.


-- End Thoughts --
This game was amazing to play through, and it still never got old with the sandbox capabilites Everything about this game amazed me in some way. This game is a must buy if you are experienced with VR.

Final Conclusion:
Gameplay: 10/10
Story: 8/10
Music: 10/10
Sandbox/Minigames/etc: 9/10
Enemy AI: 8/10
Physics: 10/10
Final Rating: 9.5/10
