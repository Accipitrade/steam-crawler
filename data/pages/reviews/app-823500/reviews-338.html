\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS feels like the first steps towards the future of VR. I thoroughly enjoyed it throughout my playthrough and the subsequent scouring for easter eggs and collectibles. I always appreciate a VR game with the length of a normal game, something that so far only this and Half-Life Alyx have delivered.

Physics is pretty great. I noticed some issues with legs clipping through tables, or my hand getting stuck in a wall, but those are outliers. I love how the developers encourage you to explore every nook and cranny. I spent 45 minutes attempting to clamber onto an out-of-the-way vent system with a crowbar, dangling from a rope as I did so. Eventually, I managed to get onto it, and found a little milkcrate with a collectible in it. The devs are encouraging us to let go of our primitive ways of virtual reality (standing stiffly, tapping to rotate our view, etc). You can climb onto anything you can get your hands on.

Graphics are pretty standard. Definitely middle-range for VR, now that Alyx has set the bar so high. Still, it's definetely serviceable, and maybe the super low-res textures are intentional since it's set in a Matrix-like world.

Gameplay is exhilarating. Definitely top three combat systems. I always find something new when fighting. For instance, take out a zombie's kneecaps with your bat and they can't walk. You can divert mindless monsters into fighting each other by throwing one on top of the other. You can trip them over with a crowbar. The weapons are a lot of fun too. Of course, the best fight was (no spoilers) in the rave room. You'll know it when you see it.

Overall, tied for best VR game ever with Alyx. They've both got strengths and weaknesses. Boneworks gets an edge because I love full body VR, and it succeeds in its goal of leading VR software forward, while Alyx is designed more to introduce VR to a larger market.

TLDR: 9/10
