\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOut of the nearly 1400 Steam games I own, this is the only one I've ever refunded.

First of all, this game made me very motion sick, and I don't even get motion sickness irl. I have no problems with smooth walking in VR games. I use it all the time. But this game has a tendency to move your body around when you're trying to move an object around, and I think that's the cause.

You'll spend more than half of the first two hours walking through a museum, looking at exhibits that explain the controls. This game prides itself on being as realistic as possible from a physics standpoint. The lesson I learned is this: when it comes to making a fun game, a little less realistic and a lot more forgiving is preferable.

Let me compare it to Half-Life: Alyx for example. In Alyx, if you want to open a filing cabinet to search it, you grab the handle and pull. It works perfectly. In Boneworks, when you grab the handle and pull, your entire body jerks to the side, you move the entire cabinet on accident, and all the drawers flop open. In Alyx, if you want to grab something or release something you don't really have to grab or release with all of your fingers. The game is smart enough to know what you're trying to do. In Boneworks, you MUST grab with all fingers and release all fingers to climb, carry, or drop things. This causes weird problems like accidentally making a fist with one hand when you're picking something up with the other and suddenly realizing you're dragging a table behind you.

On top of that, much of the gameplay (at least in the part I was able to play in the first two hours) consists of dragging/sliding/pushing puzzles that aren't the least bit enjoyable.

To me, it's like a cheap Garry's Mod without the construction aspect that makes you sick and isn't very fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game! I was slightly new to VR and chose this as one of my first new VR games (I got lucky and didn't get motion sick easily). There's so much that goes into this game that makes it worth every penny:

---Pros---
-High Replayability (Arena w/ endless mode, sandbox, replaying story for unlocks and Easter-eggs)
-Amazing Physics (The climbing, grabbing, interaction, etc)
-Gun Physics (Extremely realistic yet easy to use)
-Rewarding puzzles (Numerous ways to solve puzzles and good level of difficulty)
-Unique enemies
-Lengthy Story Mode (Over 10 hours)
-Good Story (While not really explained, it had a nice flow to it)
-Amazing Price (I got it on sale for about $24 but would definitely pay full price)
-Great Developers (I've watched numerous videos of the developers creating/commenting on the game and it shows that they truly care about it.

---Cons---
-Motion Sickness (Easy to get motion sick if you're not experienced. I was easily able to overcome this)
-Saving is a bit interesting but by no means a deal breaker
-*Minor repetitiveness *(I've heard this from others but personally I disagree & enjoy every bit of the game)

(Note: as of the time of this review I have completed about half of the story, played for about 11 hrs, and completed numerous sandbox unlocks like Tuscany)

-Before hand, the climbing was a little bit janky, you basically needed to push yourself against the thing to be able to climb it. But now it's MUCH easier. It feels slick and nice and I actually would love to do it some more instead of trying to avoid it. Please make a climbing map <3

- The story is surprisingly good and I am really engaged with it once you do some digging. I mean a lot of digging....we'll get to that later

- This is more of a personal thing, but this game basically solidified my iron stomach. Before hand I would get a little bit dizzy but not much when playing VR, but this game ultimately just put it to the test and now, nothing bothers me.

- Slow motion was a great addition.

- The survival modes are great fun, I play them on a daily basis

- The environment is incredibly detailed and has little details in every corner. You can spend HOURS on one level just looking around. This game is made for people who love to look around in games, especially in VR.

What I don't like:

- While I have great fun with this game, you really got to LOVE VR in order to like this game. This game has no motion sickness settings, so you better have good VR legs or ready to be sick because this game gets REALLY intense sometimes.

- The physics is a bit jank. I know it's the main selling point, but the fact that I can't walk over bodies kinda makes it really annoying to deal with.

- Melee combat suuuucks. It's fun sometimes yea, but it doesn't feel right. Yea it feels good when you're stabbing things, but when hitting or slicing, it feels weak and pathetic.

- While story is good, the way it's told is not great. Stress Level Zero LOVES to hide a lot of details in the levels, which is perfectly fine, FNAF did that for years. But I would say that it is too well hidden, to the point where A LOT of people think that this game has no story at all. You really have to go digging to know about this game's story, so I'll start you off. This game is connected to Duck Season. Have fun

- Hope you love physics puzzles because this game has A LOT of them. It is cool to hold onto a trash thrasher and be carried onto the other side, it isn't fun carrying a dozen barrels to a specific place to make a bridge.


Final Thoughts: While this game does have it's problems, and the fact that it appeals to a very small market in an already small market, once you start liking this game, it becomes an absolute blast playing it. In summary, while Half Life Alyx is a lot more engaging, I would say Boneworks is a lot more fun\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game claims to be for "Advanced VR users only". What they actually mean, is it's only for people who have superhuman levels of immunity to VR sickness. The game has no teleport movement, only smooth movement via the joystick. Plenty of other games have implemented things to help reduce the motion sickness associated with that sort of movement, with features such as shrinking the field of view when moving. These options help to varying degrees, but they unquestionably do help.

This game has no such features. Just raw joystick movement. On top of that, physics is a little janky, so that interacting with objects that move me (climbing a rope, accidentally pushing off of a wall, etc) doesn't just smoothly move my perspective relative to what I'm doing with my hand, but instead it's just a little bit off and jittery, which ups the nausea by an order of magnitude.

This game is the fastest and sickest any VR game has ever made me feel. I can play games with teleport for hours with zero drawbacks. I can play other games with smooth movement for 30-45 mins, feel nauseous but recover shortly after I stop. I played this game for about 20 minutes, felt the worst I have ever felt, and in fact continued to feel unpleasant for a few hours after.

Honestly, I can't comment on the actual game, as I was unable to make it past the tutorial area. If any VR games give you even the slightest hint of VR sickness, do not bother even trying this one.\t\t\t\t\t
