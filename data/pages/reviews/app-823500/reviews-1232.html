\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy thoughts after the first hour or two of the game was that it was interesting but not a great first impression because the physics kept getting in the way of how i wanted to traverse the world, which lead to a lot of motion sickness. I spent the majority of that time in the tutorial area because I wanted to explore things, but kept kind of clipping the wall, which lead to unexpected feelings of motion sickness.

However, I kept with it, took breaks if I started feeling uncomfortable, and ended up feeling more immersed in this game than I have with any other VR title. Once you accept the rules of the world and that your body occupies physical space and things behave how you expect, it becomes insanely fun. There's something rewarding about learning how to finally climb in this game and it totally making sense, or seeing an object and your mind just accepts that it's heavy. The guns feel great too, there's tons of secrets to find, it looks just right. The only complaint I have is really the save system and if I had to nitpick, most of the times I get stuck end up requiring me to just break a lot of objects or stack crates, which makes me wish there was more variety. Still, definitely recommended to check out, just pace yourself and maybe try not to overhype the experience for yourself. Accept how this game works instead of trying to force it to behave like other VR First Person games and you're in for an amazing treat\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like this game... i really do, it's my first real campaign VR game, and it was fun and interesting...but I personally don't call this "the future of VR". And I will mainly just give my oppion on why don’t think this is the way to go for all VR games.
To start: I like the "real body" and the mechanics of heavy objects. but...in the end it felt like a limitation of my real movements, and when punching with bare fists I offend felt like: “Why can’t i just move my hands as fast as i could in real life, I mean I just did, so it’s not like Im exploiting anything” And the amount of times my hands would get _in_ on something, while trying to move objects for puzzles, was way too much.


Climbing walls and ladders was extremely frustrating, and maybe that was because im playing with OG rift controllers, but more times than not i would just fall, because my hands were not fully gripping the ladder, but indest holding on to them with the pinky finger.

Firing weapons was fun, and the only issue i ran into, was the stock of some weapons would get stuck on my head, but I guess i'm just too used to having no body in VR.

I say it's worth the price, and replayability seems strong - BUT i do not think this is the way all VR games should go, and I definitely would not call this "the Half Life Valve Wished they had" and I am happy that HL: Alyx goes with the floating hands.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI use to have a negative review, a TL:DR on why is this game use to be sickening and frustrating as hell for all the wrong, unhealthy reasons. Use to be a 4/10, for an example you couldn't even save in this game back then. Was a nightmare to play.
Now it's a positive review, as you can see. 7/10.

The game was inspiration for HL:Alyx and you can really see it, but be warned this is a more advanced VR game and can make someone new to VR incredibly, viciously ill.

The world has an ominous beta-tested PS1 feel to it which is quite nice, the enemies are fun to kill and the weapons are all cool as well. The secrets, sound design, and level design is also really well done, and it's extremely satisfying to cook up your own playstyle in VR, and for it to work in this game. Be a solid-snake sneak guy with a knife and pistol? It works. Be a run and gun idiot with assault rifle? Hard, but it's there.

But what I don't like about this game, is it is JANK. The epitome of the word Jank embodied into a Video game. For some reason the devs think human beings are made out of rubber, you will VIOLENTLY shake around when you climb stuff, stand on ♥♥♥♥, grab enemies, etc. And I truly mean it's a violent shake, like extremely rapid, all over the place, it's like bones simply don't exist inside your body. If you came from Alyx and want to try this, be warned that the feeling in this game isn't even half as solid, and it is genuinely frustrating, and exhausting. I'd 100% reccommend mods that turn you into a functioning human, it makes the game so much better.\t\t\t\t\t
