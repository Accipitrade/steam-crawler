\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBonewerkz is a very good game.

I would ordinarily call this an FPS but since it's VR, FP seems redundant. Bonewerkz is a shooter game where you play a guy who puts on a VR headset in order to enter a digital world. This in itself is fairly meta, but there's an extra layer of meta as you can find VR headsets inside the VR world of Bonewerks, which you can wear to transcend the reality of the VR world and enter a sadly limited panorama photograph, and nothing more. In short, it's just for fun, but it's cute the first time you see it.

This sets up the world, beyond that there's not much to say about it. You traverse mysterious levels that don't really connect with any real rhyme or reason, though there are one or two occasions where you are actually given the opportunity to see your target in the distance, and learn visually what the actual goal of the game even is - something not readily apparent.

So the story doesn't matter. This is okay. There's plenty of really good shooters especially where the story is a sideshow you partake in only because you are enjoying the shooting aspects of the video game you're playing. Fortunately, Bonewerkz is one of these shooters where the shooting, bashing, slicing, stabbing and general murder is entertaining enough, and the traversal of the levels, the puzzles, the endless parkour and climbing all make the gameplay experience a fun, thrilling adventure in getting your arms to move in just the right way, and your bullets to move along the correct trajectory.

Predominantly this is a shooting game. Bonewerkz gives you many different firearms over the course of the game (primarily AR-15 pattern rifles) to shoot enemies with. Some enemies shoot, some shuffle. Both enemy types rarely intersect, but when they do they are actually often at odds, which can be fun to watch, and useful as the distraction of infighting nets you some easy kills. There are also melee weapons of various sorts (knives, swords, but primarily blunt instruments such as hammers, sledgehammers, baseball bats and more) but generally, Bonewerkz is a shooting game, and it will remind you of this by ensuring almost every scenario you encounter is better solved with a firearm over anything else.

Partially, this is a result of the level design. While generally very good, there are many odd decisions and missed opportunities, I thought, which led to me spending much more time shooting than doing anything else. The game does not have melee levels and shooting levels, every level eventually becomes a shooting level because they inevitably grant you a gun.

Shuffling enemies are a natural fit for murder via melee weapon, you meet the enemy on a relatively even playing field. Shuffling enemies are a poor fit for shooting, because you're essentially just removing something from the playing field that has no way to combat you, and you really feel like you're just bullying a defenceless enemy more often than not. In this way, I was hoping there would be more levels were you are exclusively afforded melee weapons to deal with melee enemies, at least one linear level, and there really wasn't.

Shooting enemies on the other hand, are very difficult to remove via melee weapon, partially because they are much more mobile than shuffling enemies, but also because in your attempt to close the distance, you might take a few hits as you make yourself inadvertently a target. No question it's possible to be victorious in such an engagement, but you're essentially playing at a disadvantage for no real gain. Thus, a firearm is a much better choice, and basically on any occasion you're fighting shooting enemies the game is working perfectly. Much less of an issue because you will always have a gun when you need to shoot at something that can shoot back at you.

Gameplay aside, the overall aesthetic of the game can only really be described as 'Half-Life 2 adjacent'. The game is full of noisy, gritty textures, rusted out debris, graffiti of all kinds, developer textures that will call to mind the Source SDK, and there's even a merry-go-round, just like the introduction to City 17. I like it a lot, it's comfortable. The developers also I think took a lot of inspiration in terms of puzzles and the way areas flow into one another, and the way encounters, puzzle rooms and simple exploration are handled as well.

Overall, despite the minor issues I have with the game, I can't really imagine this leaving anyone dissatisfied. Bonewerkz is undoubtedly a quality VR video game - it is not a series of minigames, or one big minigame, it is a linear, singleplayer shooter
and I'd guess it's probably one of the best Virtual Reality games on Steam. Just don't expect much from the story, and you'll have a good time.\t\t\t\t\t
