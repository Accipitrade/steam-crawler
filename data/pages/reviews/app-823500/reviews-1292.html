\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinally a VR game that really makes me feel present in the world. I don't just mean that I have a presence, but rather, I feel like I am actually there.

The Good.
Excellent physics simulations.
Graphics are well suited to the game play.
Sounds are well designed and provide both ambient and active sense of surroundings.
Controls are very customizable, allowing for almost any play style and preference.

The Bad.
If you are the kind of person to experience motion sickness, this game will probably trigger you.

The Odd.
The physics system is very nice, but can lead to some very odd moments, like your hand getting stuck in the box you were using for cover, so you have to break said box to free yourself.

This game has me enthralled, and I feel like I'm playing a vr version of Human Fall Flat sometimes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is a good game in here, there really is, but it is currently buried under a general lack of polish that makes the experience frustrating. While the opening of the game showcases a bunch of cool features and physics interaction, the majority of the game that I have experienced so far just revolves around platforming puzzles. This would be fine if the platforming was polished and reliable, but it just isn't. Jumping is wonky at the best of times, and my hands consistently got stuck in the geometry while climbing, forcing me to restart.

Restarting is enough of a problem in its own right because of the current state of save mechanics. Simply put, there are none. Once you reach a level, you can load that level at any time from the menu. However, if you quit the game during a level, your progress will not be saved. If you die during a level (or get stuck in the geometry and have to shoot yourself) then there are a couple of sparse soft save points that it will load you to, but you will still have to redo the tedious platforming puzzles.

There is so much potential here because of how good the item physics are, but the player physics just don't match up unfortunately. Fighting enemies feels good, but when you have to do three twenty minute platforming sections before you get to the next set of two enemies, it hardly feels rewarding. When I finish a puzzle, I do not feel satisfied, I feel relieved and exhausted. That's about the summary of how I feel after turning off the game as well. I hope things get straightened out in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlready felt sold within the first few minutes of messing around on the menu. Suffice to say I was blown away. Probably the best VR game I've ever played.

I have two problems with the game though.

Firstly the idea of your character being a physics object works pretty well for the majority of the game, getting over bundles of objects can be a bit of an annoyance but I've never found my self stuck anywhere in the game which is good. The majority of my problems come from the climbing and vaulting. Mainly the climbing, you can find your self getting stuck when climbing ladders due to your feet getting stuck on part of the ladder below. This is usually easy to fix by just crouching or fidgeting a bit and happens rarely, just happens enough to make note of it though.

The other problem I have is quite a biggy and it's the save situation. Levels can take upwards of 40 minutes to an hour to complete with seemingly no checkpoints other than the very start of the level. I don't think manual saves are necessary, just a checkpoint maybe halfway through the level so you don't have to go through a pain in the ass parkour section again due to a crash.

Other than that game is absolutely solid, in love with it. Blew my expectations away.\t\t\t\t\t
