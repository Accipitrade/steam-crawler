\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a great game, but you cant write off some of the glaring issues it has.

Lets start with the good stuff first. The game has an awesome combat system. The way all the guns and weapons interact with the world and it's enemies is truly something only boneworks can offer. The arena mode and sandbox mode? Both extremely fun modes. It's really fun to mess around in the sandbox mode with all the items you unlocked throughout the story mode (we'll get to this later.) and the arena mode is a blast. It highlights the amazing combat system and that's when it shows it's true colors.

The story mode though?
That's a different story. (No pun intended.)

The story mode is *painfully* mediocre. At first, it's not that bad. The streets level is a very fun intro to the game, but that's as far as it goes for compliments for the story mode with this game.
After the first initial levels, they get tedious and boring. The levels are very long with not a lot of variety in enemies and looks. Most of the levels look the same. (The sewer level and the last level are exceptions) There are really only 3 enemy types in the game. (Maybe 4 if you count the zombies that throw stuff at you.) This isn't enough to sustain a 7 hour long story mode. You have the headset crabs, the Nullbodies/Zombies which behave almost exactly the same as each other, and then the Hazmat suit guys with the guns. Because of this, the story mode gets boring not to long after it starts.

And I also forgot to mention the biggest gripe I have with this game.

Everything in the sandbox mode has to be unlocked by putting things in "reclamation bins" at the end of each story mode level *before* you can use them. This would make sense in a game with not such a limiting inventory, but boneworks has a pretty limited one. You have one space for general items, two for handguns and small items, and another two for SMG's, AR's,and large melee weapons.This makes getting all the sandbox items *extremely* tedious. Most collectibles are hidden in "bone boxes" which are hidden throughout the story mode. The puzzles aren't exactly thrilling, either.

This game tries it's best at physics puzzles a few times, but it doesn't go well for the most part. Most puzzles in the game are really simple, but take a decent chunk of time to do for one reason: The way your body interacts with the world. In the game, You have an entire body. In my opinion, this isn't ideal at all. Your arms constantly get stuck in things and there's nothing you can do about it. I feel like having a body is less immersive and more annoying than just having floating hands in the long run. What Iv'e tried to do in the game multiple times is move my arms, but then realize that I can't, because I can only move my hands. This creates a conflict that gets in the way of *everything*. Climbing, running around, trying to climb over boxes and other physics items, you name it. It's like a plague that spreads it's filth throughout the crevices and hallways of the game. It's what really makes me have second thoughts about recommending the game to other people.

But overall, It's pretty good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf Half-Life:Alyx was like taking a lift up a mountain; Boneworks would be more like climbing it without a harness. Judging these two games from their cover, Half-Life:Alyx would be the obvious choice. It has better visuals, storytelling, etc. But Boneworks gives you the freedom Half-Life:Alyx won't. Want to grab a headcrab and inflict 28 stab wounds? Do physics puzzles sound enticing to you? How about being able to use an assault rifle? Great! All 3 listed are things that are only possible in Boneworks.

Admittedly, its not all perfect. Keep in mind that I've only played this on the OG HTC Vive, so I can't speak on the behalf of the other Headsets/Controllers. Boneworks suffers from what I would call an inconsistency in their control schemes. When grabbing most objects, the buttons function as a hold (which means when you let go of the buttons, you let go of the object in the virtual world). When you grab a gun from the floor, it now functions as a toggle (To drop the object in the virtual world, you now have to click the button again.) This is VERY annoying, as this again changes when you grab a gun from your holster. From there it acts as a hold again. This inconsistency has led to multiple times where I would accidentally drop my weapon on the floor during combat scenes.

To Stress Level 0: Please fix this inconsistency and add an options pannel for us to decide if we want our controllers to act as a toggle or a hold. Also give us the option to just use the trigger instead of having to death grip our controllers just to hold a stuffed monkey.\t\t\t\t\t
