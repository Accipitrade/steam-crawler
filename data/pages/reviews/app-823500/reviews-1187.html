Level design looks like something from a Unity beginners tutorial. All the "puzzles" are very predictable. On a keyboard + mouse game you just quickly click trough it but here you actually have to do it, if you fall then you have to do the long and boring (no autosave) level all over again. It's not challenging, it's exhausting.
It's like "playng" a very realistic vacuuming simulator - same room, no textures. - Very realistic but just a boring chore.

Enemy AI - comparable to Doom 2

The physics is nothing new. Hlaf-Life 2 did about the same (15 years ago).

And you have to do the "Story Mode" a.k.a 7 hours long climbing simulator before you unlock the only interesting part of the game - the sandbox.

Boneworks is way more interesting to watch on YouTube than to play. The whole thing is like a beta version of the Pavlov VR\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would not recommend BONEWORKS. I have been excitedly awaiting it's release, as it was supposed to be the next big step in VR gaming. Instead of delivering an immersive experience with astoundingly good controls and good 'feel' to it, we instead are given a very clunky and unenjoyable game.

Allow me to explain:

BONEWORKS boasts an advanced physics engine that allows players to confidently and creatively interact with the environment around them. They did this by making every single part of the players simulated body interact with the environment. There is a reason why other VR games don't do that; It doesn't work. You will constantly be getting caught on corners, ladders, doorways, you name it, because your arms and SIMULATED legs will get caught on them. Your arms will randomly just go through walls and doorways, getting stuck in them, sometimes requiring you to reboot the game. Heavy objects you need to carry around the map either are too heavy to move easily, which then require a pallet jack to move. The Pallet jack, and all moving platforms for that matter, handle terribly and glitch out constantly. All of the melee weapons just feel light and goofy, and don't seem to ever line up to where they should be.

The combat in BONEWORKS is laughably bad. I will admit, the gun play is cool, but it's nothing new. Go boot up Pavlov or Onward and you'll get the same experience. The Melee weapons just do not handle well, for the same reasons as was listed above for why the physics are not good. There are about 6 enemies in the game; Orange guy, Green Guy, Zombie looking guy, Zombie looking guy who throws things, Guy with gun on a ball, and robo face hugger spider. The issue is is that Orange guy, Green guy, and zombie looking guy all do the exact same thing: Walk towards you, and jerkily swing their arms at you. They trip over themselves, objects, eachother, and anything else you can think of. So that really only leaves you with 3 different enemies in the game, and one has 3 different skins to it. They all handle poorly. I dislike all of them, with the exception of the Robo Spider. I hate that one. Not because it's poorly made, but because seeing a spiderlike thing jump at your face is absolutely terrifying and they do very well. I applaud the creation of them.

BONEWORKS doesn't have a story. It attempts to tell one, it fails miserably. As a player you have hardly any idea what you're doing or why you're doing it. Aside from a total of maybe 5 cutscene/videos that are narrative, the game doesn't really key you in on what's going on. I won't spoil the end of the game for those of you who do want to play it, but it makes even less sense. I guess I'll just say when you get to the part where you find a crown, the game is officially making ZERO sense.

Now all of this could be forgiven, if the game had a good sandbox mode. There are three modes in BONEWORKS: Story, Sandbox, and Arena. You unlock Arena by completing the game, but it's just a melee arena against enemies that just walk towards you, with bad weapons. Just play Blade and Sorcery. The Sandbox can only be unlocked if in the tutorial mission you throw all of the items into the blue bin instead of the ones it tells you to, and then to open a hidden Archive room after the weight room tutorial. This is the ONLY way to unlock Sandbox mode. The issue is; Sandbox mode has NO items to play with. You need to collect them throughout the game in order to add them. So if you don't like the story and just want to play around with spawning enemies and shooting guns, the only halfway decent features of the game, you need to play through the story, finding unlockables, and then throwing them into blue bins in order to unlock them in the sandbox.




TLDR; All in all, BONEWORKS aspires to be the next best VR title to release. In reality, the dev's looked at every popular thing on the VR market, said "I can make that, but worse" and delivered on that promise.

ALSO THE GAME DOESN'T HAVE ANY SAVE FEATURE, SO IF IT CRASHES OR GLITCHES, YOU NEED TO RESTART THE 45 MIN-HOUR LONG LEVEL\t\t\t\t\t
