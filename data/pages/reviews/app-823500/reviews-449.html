\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is the best VR game on the market at the moment. (October 2021) Essentially, Boneworks is a VR Immersive Sim. If you're not familiar, an Immersive Sim is a game design philosophy which looks to give players the tools and abilities to solve problems the same way they would in reality, hence providing a more immersive experience.

For example--imagine you have to enter a building. In a typical game, you may have a scripted goal "Pick the lock and enter", for instance. You pick the lock and enter.
In an immersive sim, you might grab a brick off the ground and attempt to smash in a window, or collect crates to climb up and enter through the building's roof.

Boneworks was designed with this same philosophy. Players are afforded a massive amount of freedom in how they would like to approach every problem, which allows for much more satisfying problem solving within the virtual world. Use crates to skip lowering a gate, use a crowbar as a climbing tool to pull yourself up over a ledge you can't quite reach, et cetera.

The freedom applies to more than just exploration, however. You're also free to use your head and get creative during combat--use a brick as a bludgeon, throw an enemy corpse at another enemy, headbutt a combatant, or even simply throw your empty gun at them.

Boneworks is a masterpiece of game design. Immersive Sim + VR = the most engaging game i've played in a long time.

HOWEVER: If you are easily motion sick, avoid this game. Due to the physics-based gameplay experience, there is no option for teleporting locomotion. I am lucky enough to not suffer motion sickess, and thus was able to enjoy Boneworks as my first ever VR game.

But if you don't suffer motion sickness, this is truly a wonderful experience to go through in VR.\t\t\t\t\t
