\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is ambitious, especially the implementation of physics is fascinating and in depth. The first level proudly shows what it can add to the experience. Sadly, for me it did not fully work out, it was just too jank.
My weapons constantly got stuck on my vr-body, making it hard to aim. My hands sometimes would clip throw terrain, taking me like 2-3 minutes to free myself. The weapon slots on my vr-body would often not accept the weapons.
In addition is the level design is unintuitive and mazelike with far too few checkpoints. For someone without perfect VR legs, replaying long sections is just aching.
And there are many other smaller things: the hard to follow story, the too robust supply boxes and the useless turrets.
I really want to like this game, but for me it's just too frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t

After some odd hours I acquired my VR legs and finally finished first real level in one sitting. They have since added checkpoints which are a GOD SEND for people getting used to smooth locomotion in VR. At first upon beating the game I had no clue what the story was. Arena mode is the first mode that kept me coming back for more and it became easier and easier over time. The fact that your whole body is a physical object sells it the best. Any other current VR game feels incomplete after I've played this game and that says a lot.

9/10 (would have been ten but nine is a nod to the lore if you're that deep hehe)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Highly recommended!!!! Anyone worried about motion sickness with this titles? Well I am new to VR and some smooth locomotion games make/made me sick. The forest is unplayable for me, or at least it was when I first got my HMD(Samsung Odyssey +) and I'd get sick in 5-10 minutes. Games like No mans sky made me a little sick after 30-60 minutes at first. Now No Mans Sky doesnt make me sick at all. So I gave this one a whirl and its not to bad at all. Maybe after 1 hour when I first started out I would get mild motion sickness. I just took breaks when I first felt the first signs of it coming on. Now a few days later and I've been playing off and on all day with no nausea. It was only now 8+ hours later that I feel a little off. Nothing serious though. So if you do get some motion sickness, and the problem isnt low frame rates. Then just ease yourself into it, take 20 minute breaks after the first signs show up before the nausea sets in. Then boot it back up. Eventually you wont have the problem. :) This is hands down one of the best currently available VR titles.\t\t\t\t\t
