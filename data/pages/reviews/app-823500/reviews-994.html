\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow this is terrible. I'm very much a VR veteran coming from games such as Gorn, Onward, and Pavlov all of which are miles ahead of this game and leaving this as probably the most infuriating VR game I've encountered. Everyone keeps praising the physics but they’re simulated to such an extent that it just comes off as clunky and immersion breaking. VR legs are talked about so much in this game and ironically are also the game’s weakest element. Climbing is completely broken because of them. You can’t pull yourself onto ledges because you character doesn’t know how to use his own ligaments. You’ll be carefully walking along a ledge at a cautious peace when for no reason the character starts sprinting. There is an actual joystick click for sprinting so I’m not sure why the game feels the need to take random guesses at when I want to move faster. Performance is not spectacular either and I’ve been getting a variety of different frame rates but frequently low when it matters. I’m about an hour into the story now and have encountered maybe 2 or 3 enemies that were taken out with less than satisfying melee combat. Objects have weight and feel terrible as objects with weight in VR tend to. I spent 30 minutes on one section of one puzzle trying to lift “heavy” boxes onto a platform that needed to be weighted down that was about neck level. That’s when I stopped to write this review. Nothing special to see here and certainly nothing worth your time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tit's an impressive game, but it's waaaay too wobbly.
let me explain

when i pick up a trashcan in real life, the can lifts and moves with my arm, 1:1.

when i pick up a trashcan in this game, the arms act like a puppet on strings. i do not so much move my arms, as i do give them suggestions for where to go.

you are supposed to be able to climb ledges in this game, but that is also taken under advisement.

"grab the ledge"
right, close my hand near the ledge
"no, grab the ledge"
make a fist near the ledge?
"NO, GRAB THE LEDGE"
OOOOOooooh, OK, now i get it. you want me to let go with the other hand.

i've noticed this problem in games that try to be too realistic, and especially those that try to render full arms and legs.

items dont really have weight, its more like they move slower and take longer to stop. i realize that sounds like what heavier tends to mean, but it is done in a way that is more floaty than anything. rather than having a satisfying heft, the item moves as though it was going through jello, and the way the arms move with it makes it feel more like my brain is lagging.

i go to push something, and my hands to with wibbly wobbly movement, and my arms (as i said before) consider moving in the direction of my real arms, rather than having 1:1 movement.

realistic is impressive, but unless you have some kind of vr suit with gizmos and gadgets that can stop and slow my arms during movement, you wind up feeling less real, because your real arms have already passed through the wall and are off to the other side of the room while your virtual arms are trying to figure out if you are pushing the wall or feeling it up.

Would i recommend this game? kiiiiiinda? i mean, it is impressive, but the wobblyness makes it difficult for me to personally enjoy.
and i don't see valve changing the movement/interaction systems any century soon soooo, im gonna give it a "yes", but only because there is no kinda/maybe option.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI completed this game but found the experience to be lackluster and frustrating. The story is bland and was lost on me during the experience simply as it faded into the background. The characters arms seem to be rubber bands when climbing and jerk your perspective around. As the game progresses I found my characters hands getting stuck inside objects to an increasing degree. My computer had difficulty processing the game in later levels.

Gunplay was simplistic, with bullet accuracy being in question. Ammo is plentiful as are weapons. The default button to grab weapons off of the shoulder holster is the trigger while it is the grips in every other application. If you want gunplay it is done much better in other titles.

Puzzles were simple and usually required shooting parts. Some puzzles were incredibly frustrating but could be bypassed with some effort.

Climbing was nauseating due to the noodle/rubberband arms swinging you randomly. Climbing is required for gameplay and especially the ending.

Huge shift in the game occurred half way though that attempted to show off its melee and swordplay mechanics. This is done better in other titles.

Various things need to be unlocked by finding various objects in the game. These things include a firearms range so practice is impossible before you start the campaign.

In short, this is a glorified tech demo that combines a bunch of different elements. All of these elements with the exception of the physics have been done better in other titles. Save your money.\t\t\t\t\t
