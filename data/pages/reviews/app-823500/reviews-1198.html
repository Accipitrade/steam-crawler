\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks has one huge flaw.
Once you've played it, every other VR game will look terrible in comparison.

Played on a Valve Index with a high-end rig. No performance or hardware issues.

Quick disclaimer: as stated on the store page, Boneworks has some complex VR movements like sliding locomotion without comfort options, virtual jumping, turning, and climbing. If you're not used to that, the game might make you feel motion sick for the first few hours. Just play in short bursts, stop when you feel uncomfortable, take a break, and get back to it when you feel ready. In time, you'll be able to play uninterruptedly.
Second disclaimer: The game does NOT have a mid-level checkpoint system, so if you want to take a break, maybe don't close the game as you will lose your progress in the current level. While it is a pretty glaring omission, I don't feel it detracts from the experience and it definitely doesn't warrant a "do not recommend" review.

Nothing short of a masterpiece, Boneworks is a revolution thanks to SLZ's clear vision of bringing true interactability to VR. VR is no longer just about seeing a virtual world around you; it's about actually being there and having an effect on it. Everything can be touched, opened, manipulated, thrown, climbed, shot at, struck, broken. That is the philosophy surrounding Boneworks and it results in an intoxicating experience.

The game's physics engine is so advanced and lifelike that you can approach every situation your own way. The freedom of operation in Boneworks is astounding. Every object in the game can be used in some capacity; some pesky bad guys firing at you? PIck up that trash can lid and use it as a shield. Too many weapons for you to carry? Shove them in a shopping cart and bring em with you, only to throw the cart at a surprising enemy. The same principle applies to traversing terrain or solving puzzles, whether by climbing the environment (you can climb most fixed structures in the game, and that's a LOT) or finding a solution for the very open puzzles; as long as you can make it work, there is no wrong answer.

However, a game isn't solely defined by its physics engine and interactability. If the game world is uninteresting or doesn't give you the opportunity to exploit the engine, what's the point?

Boneworks' game design, while not exactly flawless, is still remarkable and enjoyable. The world strongly encourages you to approach problems your own way, without any clear path shown as "the right one". This is further reinforced by plenty of alternative routes and secrets, encouraging exploration and generally messing around with stuff. This also brings a very healthy chunk of replayability, as areas become available as you learn new techniques (you as in the player, you will learn how to climb certain objects etc.) throughout your experience. Boneworks is very open, and that is what VR needed; true freedom for true immersion. And immersion in Boneworks is fantastic. Graphical fidelity is on point, with good-looking textures and accurate physical behavior, the sound design is absolutely delightful (love the gun sounds), and the world in general feels tangible. It's a shame the firefight sections don't exploit the physics engine as much, as they are mostly corridor and cover-based. They're still fun, but they definitely could've gained from some more thought-out areas.

The story is slightly confusing and not always presented to the player in a clear way. This might read as "oh you need to put effort in finding out the story", and while that's sort of true, the story is obfuscated not by leaving the environment to tell it for you but simply by omitting elements. That's not enjoyable. Where games like the Dark Souls series present their story indirectly through environmental cues or item descriptions, Boneworks has writings on the walls and some direct conversations with co-workers, but unlike Dark Souls, the player is always missing some key elements to truly understand what's going on. Plenty of questions essential to understanding the story is left to the imagination, which leaves it feeling lacking. It also makes the ending very weightless and inconsequential, which is a shame, as the few indications of a story there are and the original setting point to an interesting narrative that could've led to something quite enjoyable.

Boneworks lives up to the hype, and that is no small feat. Its highlights easily outshine the areas where it is lacking, and the overall experience is just a damn blast. I could not put the controllers down and finished the game in a day, and I still want to come back to it thanks to the great replayability. If you're interested in VR at all, you owe it to yourself to play this gem.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, here's the problem with steam reviews, I don't want to say no to this game, but I also don't want to say yes either. Ultimately, I have to put down a no for this game, even though I also have been following it for a few years and was very very hyped about it.

I'll start with the goods with this game. Combat is lots of fun*. The slow motion mechanics allow you to have many ways to take down the enemies of the game and also in creative ways. Wanna have a dramatic knife fight with the zombie enemies in the game? You can totally do that. Or how about grapple one enemy, throw your knife in slow motion at the other, whip out your sidearm and take out the first one. It feels very awesome to accomplish, especially the dancefloor part in the last mission (you'll see what I mean if you get there)

The graphics of the game are also great (although a somewhat bland color scheme) My hardware is a 1080ti w/i7-8700k using the valve index. I haven't had any weird performance issues, stutters, etc.

The story* presentation is also pretty neat, having a mix of real-life fmv components.

Now I'll go over the bad. Combat is jank at times, more specifically with two-handed guns. For some reason, the guns half the time do not line up properly when you try to aim down sights. (and no, I'm not braindead with aiming, I've been playing many other vr shooters, h3 included which I would say has the best gun mechanics in any vr game so far) The guns jiggle on your shoulder, making longer distance shots harder to line up (I think the reason for this is because you have a physical "body" in game, so the way that you interact with those weapons gets messed up against the model). This is especially apparent when you use slowmo. It feels like it gets worse in slowmo, even though it should be better since you're literally slowing everything down to help you aim. The gun variety is also very weak (2 pistols, 2 smgs, 2 ar with very slight variations)

The lighting in the game feels very whack. The dark areas in the game are very dark, to the point where it's almost unnaturally dark. The sewer area in particular is just oof (at least if you explore well enough, you'll find a nightvision mask fairly early)

The story is also very lack-lustered. You don't get a clear motivation as to why you're doing whatever it is you're doing, but that's not the main reason you play this game so that's a small gripe.

The physics. Holy ♥♥♥♥ I have never hated physics in a game more than this one. I usually love having the freedom to move or interact with anything, especially environments or enemies, but ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. Any time you need to have ANY climbing sections, it instantly becomes the biggest slog/torture session. Now, I understand as to why it's like that, since you always have a physical body, it simulates how it would sorta react when you try to climb, but it's so tedious and so unnatural that it's not fun. Any time you try to move around on ledges with your arms, your head just keeps bopping up and down dramatically (anyone who doesn't have the best vr legs, I would avoid this like the plague, you will feel nauseous and definitely throw up.) Any time you try to pull yourself up, you have to go VERY slowly along with not even making much progress. Your legs are pretty much useless for any climbing sections, (even ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ladders, which is HOW YOU WOULD USE THEM) The only single time I've had an ok experience slogging through the climbing is literally the end, where halfway up a HUGE ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ BUILDING, you get magnetic suction cups which are kinda cool. Everything else is just miserable. If I wanted to simulate actual climbing or parkour, I would go outside and do that myself, even if I couldn't do half the ♥♥♥♥ that you can in game. Some people will disagree with this and I get it, there really isn't any game out there (that I can think of or played) that has this sort of interaction or "freedom" but just because you're the first to do it doesn't mean you're always the best at it.

At the end of the day, I'll go back to this game. It has its fun moments for sure, but the slog of exploration makes it miserable. The arena mode is probably the most fun to have since you don't need to worry about climbing, just fighting. The most fun I've had in a climbing based environment in vr is in h3. Yes it's unrealistic with how fast you can move around with the environment, pulling yourself up with ease and whatnot, but creating a game with the idea of physics and interaction, but then making it miserable is not fun. I would like to see future content updates like more weapon variety, enemy variety, new story dlc, etc. But as it stands, there isn't much to do and the very cool, very hidden things to do are tedious to get to.

TL;DR: The combat is fun, but bland and has small variety in weapon selection and enemy variety. The story is ok, the physics is cool for combat, but ♥♥♥♥ in any vertical traversal. The game has potential to become more interesting with dlc, but as it stands, I'm on the fence on it, leaning towards no. I will update my review if the game ever gets any content updates.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is great, HOWEVER it is also a NIGHTMARE, here's why.

The physics of the game are both it's greatest strength and also it's weakness, which I feel can be fixed with a few tweeks. For example, you hands will often get stuck inside of objects and walls, it's happened to me 3 times in 8 hours of gameplay, which a lot of that time was spent trying to get the purple key....

Another glaring issue is that the stock of rifles and smgs will collide with your body and face, making it extremely difficult to aim down iron sights properly.

The game will often have you leaping gaps, which can some times be very clunky and frustrating to pull off properly.

Another major issue I had was managing my inventory while crouching irl, which you will have to do often to take cover from enemy fire. Luckily you can access inventory via a pop up menu as well, but in the heat of battle that can be quite a nightmare to deal with.

So yes, in spite of the glaring issues that need patching and tweaking, I'd still say this is a bench mark title, great for vr pros, new people to vr who don't have their vr legs yet, I'd recommend titles that are good for easing into vr before this title.

PROs:
-Can interact with almost anything
-Combat feels great and lets you be creative (except riffles and smg's)
-Puzzles are generally pretty fun and fun to solve
-Weapons have weight and pros and cons you can feel

CONs:
-Physics can make certain actions frustrating
-Platforming is janky
-Inventory management can be quite frustrating\t\t\t\t\t
