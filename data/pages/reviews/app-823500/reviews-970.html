\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey say that Boneworks is a game that will only be completed by those very experienced and competent with VR mechanics.

That's mostly because the only people who will slog through this game until the end are those who have already experienced the worst and jankiest the medium has to offer. The final boss is a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ladder. It is excruciatingly long, there are no checkpoints, and halfway up there is a platform that is literally rigged to drop you back to the bottom if you step on it more than once. I'm not exaggerating, it's just a really obnoxious, really tall ladder, with no enemies or anything to complicate it. And by the top, you will wonder exactly why you've even bothered to get this far.

In terms of content, there is less than the average Unity prototype. One rifle with several attachments (half of which make no functional gameplay difference), two SMGs (with so much muzzle flare they're less useful than a pistol), and one pistol. There are exactly three enemy types, the headcrab, zombies, and soldiers. You have an effectively bottomless ammo pool, so much health you can facetank three or four soldiers long enough to kill them at leisure, and an infinite slo-mo button if you ever feel like you don't even want to try. The coolest thing in the game was a vehicle that referenced a previous game, but to unlock the vehicle you had to complete the entire level, backtrack the entire level to the beginning, and plug batteries in the vehicle. However the vehicle was in a small glass box, and the vehicle broke entirely the first time it touched the glass. What a waste of time.\t\t\t\t\t
