It still has some flaws tho. Sometimes you will be kicked out of the level and fall into your never ending death (sometimes it checks that you are falling tho and restarts the level). It happens if you standing too near to the vending machine and putting the mags in too quick. Nothing gamebreaking.

The weapon design is nice, but if you played gun club vr they feel a bit ... at least not as the best vr weapons ever made. but still very nice to handle.

For all the people who are crying over no save stations: You should be told not to play this game with your eyes closed. After every level it saves automatically and WITHIN the level you do not need saves because there are a lot of shortcuts that lead you almost instantly in your last location. YOU just have to FIND them. And unlock them, by unlocking doors or destroiying/re-position things.
In Bigger level you have indeed a checkpoint location. like in the tower level. after the disco is one checkpoint. as example.

Don't play the levels too quick. Pay attention to everything around you and explore a bit. you will find a lot of usefull and funny stuff.\t\t\t\t\t

(Played on Rift S)

It's fine.

Purely on the technical side, there's a lot to love here. Sandbox-y physics gameplay with decent shooting. But you definitely need to go into the game with the right expectations, because that is all this game really is.

People might say it's not a tech demo, because it does have a playable campaign. But what campaign there is is absolutely barebones, which goes for both the narrative and the actual level design. Straight forward levels, enemies that serve just as an excuse for you to abuse them and padding so you don't just run through the non-puzzle parts, and puzzles that often made me wish for the sometimes jokingly suggested "I get it"-button. Said puzzles can be clever, but rarely fail to be tedious in executing their solutions.

Frustrations in puzzle solutions are due to there still being numerous rough edges around parts of the gameplay. Sometimes literally (player getting stuck on corners or small objects), sometimes in other technical aspects.

Another thing I'll throw in here is the following warning: The devs are not kidding when they say customers should be accustomed to VR. I'd consider myself fairly experienced in the field, but even I had to adjust a little to the movement in this game. It's not exactly easy on the stomach.
And for what it's worth, the developers have been absolutely terrible in addressing topics like alternative locomotion options. I've read several responses from them justifying their design choice as a technical necessity. And as if that wasn't bad enough, they did it quite condescendingly.
But: I won't hold this against the game. In my eyes a product shouldn't be tainted by the attitude of its creator(s).And the game does contain multiple warnings both on this product page and in-game. I just thought it might be worth mentioning.

In the end, it's still more of a tech demo, though I don't consider that term as negative as many others. It's absolutely fine for what it is. Personally, I would have paid the price for the sandbox mode alone, but not everyone will share that view.

The negative verdict on this review is simply for the still rough edges of the gameplay, that tips the scales for me. Should they be smoothed over, which I expect they will, I will adjust the review verdict accordingly.\t\t\t\t\t
