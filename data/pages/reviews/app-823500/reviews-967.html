\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTwo words: ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.
This game is truly something special, and it is absolutely a must play for anyone who owns a VR headset. It is one of the coolest experiences I've ever had with gaming as a whole, and if anyone asked me if they should buy it, I would answer yes without hesitation. That being said, there are quite a few pros as well as cons to this game that people should be aware of before purchasing.

-----Pros-----

-Weapons Gameplay and Mechanics: The best thing about this game by far is how the weapons in this game feel. Everything from the assault rifles to the medieval swords to the various physics guns is absolutely phenomenal. The guns each feel decently different and get very close to uncanny valley, as Boneworks makes you actually reload the gun completely yourself, as well as aim. It is a very cool experience. The melee weapons also feel excellent two handed. If any of you have played Blade and Sorcery, Boneworks' two handed melee system is superior and feels great.

-General Gameplay: Overall, the general gameplay is pretty good. There is no teleport feature, which I personally view as a pro because its what I've always imagined VR to be like since I was a kid. Understandably this could be viewed as a con to some, as it does get nauseating after a while (more on that later), but it varies person to person. Climbing can be a little wonky in the sense that there are a few bugs, but its never enough to make me want to stop playing.

-Level Design: This is probably the weakest part of the game, and that's saying something considering that the level flow and difficulty ramping is still really good. The puzzles feel natural and the solutions aren't super impossible to find, most of the time I found it to be just the right amount of difficulty. I especially enjoy the hidden weapons and items that you can "recycle" in order to use them in the playground mode, as it encourages the player to fully explore the level.

-Replayability: The story's replayability is massively improved due to the "collect them all" system that encourages the player to hunt down and find all the super interesting weapons and physics guns scattered through every level so that you can keep ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ around in playground mode. So replayability that encourages replayability is a win in my book, especially for showing friends and family the game.

-Story: The story of the game is also kind of weak, in the sense that its much more passive storytelling. That being said, it's super easy to follow and still very cool and also pretty meta, as there are sections where you can put on a VR headset, making "VR-ception". That and the whole game is about how everything is a simulation, so all in all its very cool, just nothing spectacular.

-Price: The price of the game at $30 is definitely very fair, considering the replayability and excellent mechanics that come with it. Being able to mow down hordes of simulated figures and reloading truly does make you feel like a badass, there's nothing quite like it.

-----Cons-----

-Gameplay: VR sickness is very real with this game. While it does vary from person to person, I found myself becoming quite nauseous in my first few hours of the game (and I've played quite a few VR games and never gotten nauseus), so it will take you some tinkering with the settings to find what's comfortable with you. Not everyone will experience this, so this is more of a warning.

-Controls: The game's controls can be complicated at times, but the game does warn you repeatedly that its meant for people who have played other VR games before, as it utilizes advanced physics in regards to its mechanics. Again more of another warning rather than a bad thing, but its not super accessible to the casual audience.

-BUGS: There are quite a few bugs with climbing. By far the most irritating part of this game, it can be very challenging (and NOT in a good way) to climb on top of things. This is mostly due to the fact that the game would probably be better with a full body suit or something, but still. It's much easier to climb in Blade and Sorcery, and it doesn't feel nearly as clunky. Additionally, there were several instances where my arms would get stuck inside of walls, which is just frustrating.

HOWEVER! These are all forgivable. The game is still great and wonderful and truly an innovation forward in an industry saturated with remakes and remasters and the same franchises over and over. These developers are actually incredible and you can really see the amount of love and effort that was put into making this game a (virtual) reality. Everything from the smallest details like the stuff written on the chip bags and pizza boxes to the mechanics themselves is just straight up awesome. If you have a VR headset and have not played this game, you're doing yourself a disservice. Yes, the game's pacing could be better and the story could be stronger. But ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥'s just such a cool experience, I can't wait to see what the devs do next.

Overall, 9.3/10, would balloon gun the platform I'm standing on to bypass the entire ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ level again.

Edit: This game totally deserves to win VR Game of the Year. It's got some tough competition with Half Life: Alyx also being considered, but there's just something really special that this game brings to the table in both its difficulty and mysterious story telling style.\t\t\t\t\t
