\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will not recommend this game in the current state, especially for new VR players. I know this is a small team and they worked hard on this but I guess my expectations were too high for Boneworks because I was immediately disappointed with the game even at the main menu room. I regret that I have already played more than 2 hours and cannot get a refund for it.
The two most annoying things I noticed immediately was the terrible climbing system and the fact that the player cannot step over short obstacles. I never get motion sickness in VR but Boneworks pushes the limit of my VR legs with the way how the player moves in random directions while climbing like if he's drunk. If this is the first game that new VR players try, it will probably leave a really bad first impression of VR. The player is also so strong that you can pull yourself up with one arm and keep your body suspended away from what you are grabbing onto. What I expected was that the player body would hang from the grab point and only be able to pull up with both arms. You also have to look for a tutorial to figure out how to pull yourself up onto objects because it is not intuitive at all. You are blocked from moving by a concrete brick and you can't get onto a lift that is 8 inches above the floor without jumping. Imagine trying to back away from an enemy only to get stuck on a brick. Don't even bother trying to vault over railings or other short obstacles.
I expected realistic physics interactions and even though the interactions are much better than other VR games, it is still pretty bad. Objects are either too light or the player is too strong since I can grab a heavy CRT monitor or PC with one hand and wave it around. They try to simulate weight by causing the character movements to lag behind your real world movements if you try to move a heavy object too fast but just being able to even lift some objects with one hand should not be possible. If you try to open a filing cabinet drawer, you can end up pulling the whole cabinet down. Just walking into a desk or shelving unit can knock it over.
Texture and model quality are very inconsistent with some objects having high detail while others look like they belong in a 90's game. I can understand cutting polygons for VR performance but some of the textures on objects are super low resolution. There are doors that are too large for the opening in the wall so they clip through walls and numerous places where the bad texture mapping causes stretched textures. There is also a glitch where black impact decals remain suspended in mid air after object are moved.
This one is a minor annoyance but the grab area for tools/weapons on your back is very small and positioned on the right side of your head. I don't believe there's any haptic feedback for when you can grab the weapon either.
I haven't experienced much combat as yet so I can't review that part of the gameplay but so far most of the weapon interactions seems great. The heavy weapons like the sledge hammer are difficult to use because of the weight simulation and the fact that there is no indication if you are swinging too fast other than the input lag.
The game does not seem as polished as a number of other indie VR games. The strange thing is that, at least from what I remember, Duck Season from Stress Level Zero is one of those polished games. I'm hoping that as I progress further in the game, my impression will change and I will update this review. If they even just fix the jittery climbing and make it possible to walk over small objects I would consider changing this review to positive because those things are really annoying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHE FUTURE IS HERE,

One of the best games to have ever come to VR. The physics are accurate(mostly), the puzzles are fun, the combat is immensely satisfying and the level design is wonderful. There is so much to do just in the campaign and definitely allows for and necessitates multiple play-throughs. The warning in the beginning that this game is for veteran VR players is definitely no joke. Because your avatars body in the game is also a physics object, it will interact with the environment causing the camera to move even though your head is still. This can cause even a fairly veteran VR player like myself to feel uneasy or get motion sick. But if that happens, just take the headset off for a few minutes its not that big of a deal. After playing through the first couple of levels, you'll get used to it. Overall my favorite non-multiplayer VR game 9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an essential step forward for VR.

BUT-

Essential technical progress doesn't make for a fantastic experience. Sure, all the reviews for this seem to be glowing. However, I'd say most of these gamers are young, and haven't lived through titles of the 90's like Jurassic Park tresspasser (used similar physics puzzles and felt like a VR title even though home VR didn't exist at the time) or Half Life 2, again, using the same physics mechanics, only with a lot more action (and fun) in between puzzles. It literally feels like Half-Life 2.

If I didn't know better, I'd say this is a full-length game made out of a tech-demo testing gameplay mechanics for a VR half-life game.

The game puts you in the virtual shoes of an administrator in a Virtual Operating system, and you must work your way through the artificial system, climbing walls, using counterweights to lower suspended walkways, destroying null beings who are literal NPCS.

It's fun.

It's the necessary level of physics for VR games, that shouldn't cheat physics the way 2D games do.

But I'm looking for the real game that uses this engine and gameplay mechanic. This doesn't feel like it.

Thumbs up for the technological advancement however.

Worth buying? Yes. If Half Life Alyx hasn't come out at the time of reading. If it has, I'm sure that's what this should have been.
