\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn general, this game is incredible.

Before I begin, boneworks was absolutely nothing like I thought it was. I had the impression that it was a basic physics game with no real story with the purpose of just showing off the physics(which are incredibly realistic in my opinion.


Pros:
The ost is part of my top 3 best Osts of all time

The physics are incredibly realistic

The game doesnt hold your hand and youre free to do whatever, yet it doesnt feel bland and you know where to go

You can spend an hour in a single room messing around with the stuff

Gameplay: The game isnt just point A to point be and hidden bone boxes(boxes with things for sandbox and such) and other secret areas are pretty rewarding to find and dont seem like the game gave those things to you. You can watch play through s from 5 different people and they all may have different things.

Immersion: let me explain as vr in general is immersive. In boneworks, its sort of vr-ception as youre in vr simulating a different virtual world. This makes the game self aware and as a result, is literally tailored for the player. The ambient music and blocky, rendered visuals really make it seem like you are simulating somebody in a headset...while wearing a headset. Hard to explain unless you play which you shouldnt need to think over, just buy the game!!

Sandbox: fun to do random crap


...And a lot of other pros I cant name off the top of my head

Cons: (yes, this game has cons)

Gameplay(let me explain): gameplay is good overall, but one big con from the gameplay stems from movement. If youve played games like population one or the climb and tried vaulting over things in boneworks, you will be disappointed. The climbing resembles that of breath of the wild where you can climb in places you couldnt irl, but pulling yourself up takes a certain technique that, when mastered, makes vaulting possible, but is still sub optimal to do.

sort of janky: Now I understand that no games are perfect and this game is no exception. This ties in with climbing because this game estimates where your limbs are using info from your head and controllers and at times its akward as you get caught on items. With jankyness, I mean that grabbing things might be weird because your hand might be there, but in the game your hand is further back because something is in the way. Again, not something too bad, but very noticeable when playing

Conclusion: boneworks is one of those games that you experience once in a blue moon that really impacts you. Instantly, the Ost songs seemed to get out exactly the correct emotion for the situation I was in. Listening to the OST alone makes this game so impactful. In addition, the added ambience of the game where it is so simple, yet the meaning is so deep only adds to the greatness of this game. I see this game being seen in the future when vr advances as a super nostalgic game that resembles super Mario on the SNES and the likes. The game is incredible and in the past 3 days, I have added a good 6-7 hours in the game as I get lost in it. Out of any vr game I have played, and really any game I have played for that matter, boneworks is going to be remembered as a super simple, geometric, physics, sort of entry level, vr game that was somehow able to become one of my favorite games of all time. There is no reason not to get boneworks
