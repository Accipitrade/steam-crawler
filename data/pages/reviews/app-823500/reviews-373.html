\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is one of the 5 VR titles that have ruined every other VR game for me. (those others being, H3VR, Pavlov, Onward, HL: Alyx)

Despite this however, Boneworks is a game I can only recommend to the hardcore, It's in some ways close to the Gmod of VR and also in some other ways throughout it's campaign has immersive sim elements primarily within it's level design and gameplay. Boneworks is very much so a game that gives you the tools and expects you to experiment and figure out things on your own.

Boneworks focuses primarily around it's physics based design, Boneworks was almost one of the first of it's kind to have a full tangible body with physics and mass behind it. The only game I can think of which came prior is Blade and Sorcery.

For a few examples, firearms now no longer have unnatural artificial recoil, weapon recoil is completely physics based, you can for example, brace your gun on something, or block the weapon's path of recoil with your arm, melee weapons have mass and if you try to swing too fast you'll lose damage as your arms give out from trying to swing, or how firearms are now relatively effective beating sticks. Enemies are also completely physics based and have a variety of responses, shooting an enemy in the legs will cause it to trip, shoot it in the legs enough and it will be crippled and maybe even in-able to stand up. And due to the player physics, you can now hoist yourself up ledges or slide down a zipline with a crowbar.

Or, you can grab that enemy and piledrive em into the floor, or even use that enemy as an improv melee weapon. or even use em as a meat shield.

However, this comes with downsides. The player physics is relatively janky, especially when you manage to trap your hands on the other side of something or how sometimes weapons can have some issues with your shoulder. or many other smaller or larger issues.

The game can also be quite difficult to those who haven't gotten VR legs, there is NO teleportation movement, there is only smooth locomotion. This can cause the stomach to turn if you're less acclimated to VR.

In terms of other issues, the game is very demanding and requires a decent PC to even run without having the game be barf-land

The story mode's level design is also a big highlight, Each level is practically it's own sandbox and silent tutorial, it takes from the Valve school of level and game design, It doesn't verbally tell you how to play and each level teaches you everything silently.

The museum gets you used to basic puzzling and interaction and movement in a low stress environment, Streets get's you accustomed to combat against early enemies and advanced puzzling, aswell as how to navigate.
Run-off pits you against even more dangerous fights with some new enemy types, most notably armed omni-projectors and teaches you further mechanics such as climbing and allows you to use a larger variety of weapons.

Each level has more than just one path aswell, puzzles can be avoided with clever thinking and some finesse, some key doors can be skipped entirely and there are loads of secrets and alternative ways to move about in each level. Most notably the sewer level gives you two different path ways early on.\t\t\t\t\t
