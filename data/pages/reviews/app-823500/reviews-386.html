\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is such a great game.
I have been wanting to get it for months now, and waited until Christmas to buy it and I regret not buying it sooner. This game is one of the best gaming experiences I've ever had (VR and PC gaming). The music, the graphics, the game-play, the puzzles, everything is so polished and pleasant in this game.
Whenever you are doing a puzzle in Boneworks, you will never feel that it becomes repetitive or annoying, that is just how well crafted these puzzles are. Not only that, but you have a very distinct sense of accomplishment when you complete a puzzle or do something well.
Another thing, this game can run on potato hardware.
My PC's specs:
- Intel core i5 4460
- AMD radeon rx 460
- 16 gb RAM 1600 mhz
Obviously, my pc isnt up to spec for most VR games, however Boneworks runs surprisingly well on my hunk of junk. I have my graphics in game set to half texture resolution, 2xx anti-aliasing, no shadows, and I have steam vr set to 100% resolution and the game runs fine. Ill get and average of about 60 fps in most areas, with it sometimes dipping into the 30 fps range in areas that are more complicated.
I would also recommend anyone with low end hardware to use the VR mod for "AMD FidelityFX Super Resolution" as it REALLY helps my computer to put out more FPS in this game, and it works for AMD and nVidea GPUs.
I would definitely recommend this game. This game is by far, my favorite game I own.\t\t\t\t\t

PLEASE READ THE FULL REVIEW

Boneworks is one of the best games I've ever played. It's innovative, it's fun, there are tons of secrets and collectibles, and for $30 it's half as expensive as it should be. The soundtrack is fantastic, the visuals are beautiful, the environments are incredibly well designed and imaginative, each section feels unique from the last, and it's overall a fantastic game. I give this a negative review for one reason: The ending is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ doodoo fart ♥♥♥♥ bad.

A piece of media can be the best thing in the world, but if the ending sucks, that can ruin the entire experience. I find myself drawing comparisons to my favorite game, Bioshock. It's a fantastic game, but the final boss is kind of ♥♥♥♥. I'm sure many people would agree. Now take the ending of Bioshock and make it even worse and you have the ending of this game.

Imagine if Bioshock was building up a fight with Andrew Ryan for the entire game, but at the end you don't even fight a boss and instead have to fight off normal splicers while trying to do the same pipe puzzle four times. Then after a weird and confusing what can only be called a dream sequence, you're placed into the game again and it literally shows you Andrew Ryan as if you're about to fight him. Then you fight your way through a bunch of enemies and climb a ladder that's basically the same length as the MGS 3 ladder except you have to physically climb it with your arms. Then when you get to the top, you enter a small arena and just as you're about to get excited, you get shown a cutscene where your character dies and you can in no way stop that from happening. Then roll credits.

♥♥♥♥ the ending to this game 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t
