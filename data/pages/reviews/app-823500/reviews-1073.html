Gore Time in Garrys Mod

The primary way to take drones out early on is just to hit them with stuff. And that means big old earnest swings aiming for the head for the most part. It's kinda sickeningly kinetic. And worse still, your main starter weapon is a claw hammer. Which is both a nasty instrument, and has an unnerving tendency to get its claw stuck in their armpit during a wild backswing, or some other inconvenient place. The next thing you know you're de-armed, and being tracked by a disgruntled fleshy avatar with a hammer jauntily sticking out of their buttock...

This was genuinely horrific...


Tellytubby with a Hard Hat

My reaction after that session was totally: Not my thing. I'm no gore king. This is like being trapped inside a reinforced cardboard box with a crooning serial killer.

But I had another go. In my first round I'd realised I could use a bin lid as a defensive shield. This had given me time to take events in, to toy with a big floating physics puzzle, and to stay alive long enough to walk into a gun turret trap....

And leaning into that side of the physics turned out to be the key to getting glee out of this :D

Once you realise that you can place the whole bin over your crimson nemesis (mainly causing them to die in an unfortunate physics accident), things get a lot more playful :). Suddenly you're hurling bricks to slow them down, piling barriers in their way, kiting their (terrible) pathing into these jumbles of physics or down flights of stairs. Tripping them up and running away giggling. It made it a lot less oppressive ;)


Tin Man vs The Wheelie Men

Gotta stress that this is very much an indie title. A humanoid throws a gun under a lowering door, and looks like he’s powered by clockwork. The incidental audio is so thin that your legs sound like two sheets of tin when they knock into something.

But when the bespoke story audio is scraping and clanking down the other side of a wall, when you hit a grand set piece, or mess up a puzzle and drop 3 stories into a nest of ‘Combine wheelie men’ (and survive via some emergency dual pistol duck and dive), it is alllll good :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGonna title this little review as The Wobbling.

THE WOBBLING

Summary (TLDR);

Cons:

> Wobbly, limp wrists cause melee swings to feel weak and cumbersome.

> Reloading while Duel Wielding is annoying.

> Using physics to animate everything isn't always a good idea, walking feels weird, animations are odd.

> Using ammo as currency is a terrible idea, earning money for killing stuff and finding secrets would be much better.

> Smowmo could probably be improved by making the opposite controller (the one you don't use for walking) control the slowmo so atleast we can walk at the same time.

> Sound design needs a lot of work in some areas where the ambience is too loud to hear anything, music continues to play inappropriately which seems more like a bug.

Pros:

> The vastness of the maps and multiple choice routes is fantastic for adventure. Being rewarded for scrutinising the maps feels great.

> Climbing possibilities seem limitless and give you the freedom to overcome many difficult challenges if you do so wish.

> The graphics are lovely to look at. The screens showing the outside world are very cool to see, gives me an eerie feeling but the scenery is always nice, and is a nice break from all the darkness.

> The museum was very fun, learning about everything. The humour was quite witty.

> VR body way ahead of its time. It's very accurate barring some physics issues but for the most part, this game confuses my brain outside the game making me think my limbs aren't mine yet haha

Although I'm not 100% certain, I believe the developers are using physics to animate everything in this game which leaves a sour taste in my mouth. This game that I've experienced so far seems like a beautiful mess. The systems are way ahead of anyone else in the VR game but for comfort and accessibility I'd have to say Pavlov wins out here for the smoothness of gun-play, reloading and meeting general expectations of just being able to check your ammo. Boneworks doesn't show what's left in your clip or mag, but Boneworks also uses ammo as currency, but Boneworks also doesn't let you use the ammo currency of guns you don't have equipped.

Regarding the physics animations: It means everything walks weird, like they're shuffling. It means you walk weird, hearing several tiny footsteps instead of the expected one of two when walking in real life. I suppose it adds to the playful aesthetic of the mechanics but with a game seemingly coming off as sinister, dark and mysterious, this walking seems incredibly out of place and could benefit from good old fashioned pre-animated walk cycles combined with natural motion to link different animations together seamlessly. The AI seems alright, but the physics calling the shots means these guys will just attempt to walk over each other, fall over, walk into walls and boxes. I suppose this seems nit picky since they're meant to be drone/zombie-like npc's which is fair enough, I just don't get on with physics animations, they're always off.

The next part is regarding my title, everything is wobbly. Your guns make your hands bend back when you move them anywhere as if this was realistic somehow. Perhaps if you're holding a gun with a limp wrist then sure, anything with weight will make your wrists move when your arm moves. However this becomes particularly irritating when using melee weapons to do anything. I want to use the crowbar and smash boxes, yet I swing my arm down in real life with hopefully enough force to cause damage but the box in the game is met with a tap, a tickle, and my wrist seemingly snapping itself backwards as it catches on the box, waiting for my arm in real life to come back and meet up with it's virtual skeleton. I tested the physics of holding these weapons, ofcourse some are much heavier than others and probably could do with your other arm for support. It just feels like there's no force behind my swings. Everything is soft, I hit something and my arm breaks. I hold a weapon (crowbar) and my wrist wobbles as if I don't have a grip or control or any strength in my arm whatsoever. A lot of the times, my crowbar hits do no damage to the box or the enemy and when I do cause damage it's pretty unsatisfying because you know you didn't do damage from the force so much as you did damage because the tip of the crowbar connected to the right part of the target.

Slowmotion: It's incredibly annoying that slowmo is on the same controller that you use for walking, meaning you press slowmo, go to walk and boom slowmo turns off. I suggest making slowmo a button on the right hand controller instead since atleast you can turn your body physically if you need to turn. You can't walk physically if you're playing standing or sat down in a small room.

Besides these points, I don't have any other issues with this game, I can see it's more of a test than a game but that's fine, I enjoy the puzzles so far and the verticality of the map design. I love exploring these places, nearly 6 hours I spent on my feet yesterday just playing, I need a foot massage!\t\t\t\t\t
