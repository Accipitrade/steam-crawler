\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf there was an option between "yes" and "no" when asked if I would recommend the game, I would choose something in between, because I have very mixed feelings about this game.

Pros:
+ It's really fun to play with physics and to be able to interact with nearly everything
+ Shooting weapons feels great
+ I like the creepy "Hey!" from the enemies when you approach them, as well as the headsets jumping at you.
+ Speaking of the jumping headsets, it's really fun how it actually works putting your hands up as a reflex when you are being jumped and thus shielding yourself against the enemy
+ Various melee weapons to have fun with
+ Reloading weapons manually instead of with a simple button press like in many other VR Games is surprisingly fun and totally adds to the experience
+ Locomotion is good
+ I like the settings, being a VR destination halfway through development

Cons:
- The biggest con I have with this game: it's really easy to get in a situation, where the game moves your head but you don't move your head in real life, which gets really nauseating really fast. Some examples:
~ The game wants you to explore some tight spaces to get ammo. If you bump your head against the ceiling of such a tight space, it moves your head back, so that you don't clip through solid objects. Sound good on paper, but messes up your brain ingame really badly.
~ The virtual arms in this game were in my way CONSTANTLY. Climbing something sideways is aggrevating, because I try to reach over my other arm, but somehow my arms glitch inside each other, spasming out, also moving my head wildly again, causing me to fall eventually and doing that whole nauseating section again.
~ Jumping and climbing up ledges also pushed my head back and forwards a few times, which didn't make much sense to me, only making me a little more sick.
~ Jumping puzzles also suffer greatly from this. It really makes me not bothering about collectibles at all.
~ It also prevented me from getting creative in this physics based game, for example I couldn't use a plank to cross a gap because it instantly glitched when walking on it, moving my head to all sides

- The main weapons are largely SMGs or Rifles. Wish there were also shotguns or grenade launchers (Or did I just miss them entirely? In this case I guess they should be easier to get!)
- I hated the enemies which just should glowing orbs from their hands at you.
- They voicelines when you are about to die sound odd "I think I'm dying!", but maybe it's some lore thing
- I didn't get that you could survive when your view is fading by killing an enemy, it was never explained. I just found it strange that I always take so long to actually die that I googled if I can still survive, finding out this mechanic.
- In the beginning, you could use a garbage can lid to shield yourself against bullets from a turret. Later in the game I tried the same with way bigger barrels or other items, but the bullets went right through. Was a bit inconsistent.


So yeah, it seems there are more cons then pros, but for me it feels like there are as many pros as cons. If you got no problem with getting your head moved sometimes, it's a really great game. But as the developers said themselves, only play it if you got experience with VR already.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat an odd game. BONEWORKS has absolutely no shortage of jank; limbs and weapons and enemies are regularly clipping into each other, and often times the melee weapons hit like wet noodles until you get used to the combat system. I think the physics-based body just looks weird, and despite all the hype it gets, I don't think smooth movement is really that more immersive than teleportation.

Fans are hyping this game up as the future of VR and... I agree. Despite its flaws, when this game gets going it kills! Having to physically reload guns mid firefight is fantastic, and being able to physically push around props and open doors makes older vr games feel like they were made in the Jurassic era. The jank of the physics kind of even works in the games favor sometimes, as you are able to manipulate it to force puzzle solutions that wouldn't be possible in a more heavily scripted game.

I suspect that in a few years, as more polished and focused games using this kind of physics interactivity come out, BONEWORKS is going to feel pretty dated. (I wouldn't be surprised if some of those games will be made by Stress Level Zero themselves). But right now, this game feels like the cutting edge in what VR games can pull off, and I heavily recommend it to any experienced VR user.

Even if you end up hating BONEWORKS, dealing with headcrabs here is great practice for not dying of a heart attack once Half-Life: Alyx comes out in a few weeks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is the reason I decided to make the plunge into VR.
I had been sitting on the idea of purchasing a headset for a solid few years and this is the game where I decided it was worth the investment. Despite that, let me explain to you why this game ISN'T for someone who has just done what I did. The game description clearly explains that it's for "Advanced" VR players. In my mind I thought "Oh it's just for people who have played VR before at a friend's place and know how where the controls are"
I was entirely wrong.
No, this game is for "Advanced" VR players as in "If you don't have your VR Legs, prepare to have them swept underneath you to take a one-way trip to Motion Sickness-ville" Jumping, climbing, crouching, running, and the general movement can create some serious nausea inducing effects. It doesn't help that climbing sometimes causes the camera to shake like I'm T-10 in a SpaceX rocket about to hurtle towards Elon Musk's top secret Lunar Sex Dungeon.
So, why did I give it a positive review?
Because I can PUSH my hands towards a DOOR and it swings OPEN like it's SUPPOSED to do. The gun play is nowhere near the levels of H3VR but it's still a hell of a lot of fun because you can jump out of a window, grab a hanging pipe, draw your handgun from the shoulder holster, and blast some baddies. Something that feels amazing when you get the hang of it. Despite the lack of enemy variety surmising to Arachnophobia, Low Poly Night of the Living Dead, Higher Poly Night of the Living Dead, Droideka Hologram, and slightly larger Arachnophobia, I still have an incredible amount of fun finding new ways to engage these enemies.
tl;dr Good game, gooder physics\t\t\t\t\t
