\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is something special. Three hours in, and I've had my jaw hanging wide open several times already. Interesting story and spooky foreshadowing aside, this might be the most technically impressive VR game I've tried so far, with the only thing coming close being Lone Echo.

It takes a little while to get used to the completely physics based system, what with everything having collision and whatnot, but I've experienced very few issues with this system so far. The only thing that's really happened is I got my hand stuck in a wall a couple of times, and it might take a few moments to yank it back out.

The physical body looks and plays good. It doesn't feel like the typical disembodied ragdoll hanging underneath you; the calculations for elbows and the way your legs move feel really genuine, and the way your body interacts with the world is something I haven't seen in VR until now. I very much recommend this game to anyone who is excited about VR!\t\t\t\t\t
