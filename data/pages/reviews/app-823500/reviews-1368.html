\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to start by saying I think this is a very cool idea and is a very well made game for a lot of people. However, this game is definitely going to disappoint many others as it did me.

I only played for about one hour so I'm not sure if my issues might get better or worse but for the first few levels up until you reach the human from the trailer, I noticed some common themes.

Firstly I want to talk about the performance. I have an intel 17 7800k processor and a gtx 1060 3gb and only 8 gb of RAM. Whenever I was in an open space where the game had to render further distances I was forced to remove my headset and give it time because of how bad the stuttering is. I have my setting on the lowest they could be and I even tried turning the resolution changer option on. None of this helped at all and the game was still a laggy mess for 2 minutes.



My second issue is that although the puzzles are well made and had me thinking. I died twice in the SUPER long tutorial and got respawned about a 30 second walk away. Why is it that the first AI threat doesn't have any sort of checkpoint nearby?

My third and last point is that the marketed "Physics" aren't really anything special or unique in VR. I think that the idea of a full virtual body is cool but in reality if you can't control your legs how are you supposed to used half of the cool physics that's supposed to be in this game. I can't stress how many times I just wanted to climb something , but because of my stupid legs getting stuck on the side or the wall my VR body won't be able to make it. In my opinion the climbing in a Blade and Sorcery, although not realistic, is better.

All in all I hope to continue to play this game but it feels to me like I'm fighting a losing battle. On all the videos and trailers everything is so smooth and is marketed as "not janky" and as a "completely new experience." To me this game feels like blade and sorcery or pavlov meets puzzles but with way more lag. There really wasn't anything new or any more cool physics. I think that if you had a valve index and a really good computer that this would be an amazing experience, but for the average player might be a little lackluster.\t\t\t\t\t
