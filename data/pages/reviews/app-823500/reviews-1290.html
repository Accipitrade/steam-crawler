\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is really what I'm looking for in a VR game. By far the best VR game out there right now.

TLDR : Game is good.

Pros:
-Physics.
-Weapons are fun to use.
-Solving puzzles your own way.
-I'm a fan of the story, I'm only 3 hrs deep right now and its starting to pick up.

Neutrals:
-Sometimes the physics and climbing do not go as planned I get pulled or pushed in ways I'm not trying to go and hands get stuck in or around objects but that's part of the fun? Might just have to get used to it tho.

Cons:
-I think the start should be an overview of the controller controls, I use an index and at the start I must've used the right analog to try and move and its setup to stand/crouch and at the time I didn't know, so I spent 10 minutes trying to re-calibrate my height thinking my settings were wrong because I was crouched to the floor. After a reboot I finally made it far enough to get to the part that tells you what it does. (maybe I could've looked at the settings or something not sure)

-I would've really liked a proper saving, I hate checkpoint saving. I was doing a level for a solid 30-45 min and the game crashed and had to restart it. Even if the game did not crash sometimes I have to leave before finishing a level.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9 / 10 - Indie tech demo.
6 / 10 - HalfLife-like linear narrative / phys-puzzle shooter.
Boneworks is basically a cross between two of my favorite VR games: "Hotdogs, Horseshoes, and Handgrenades" + "Sairento" and also sets the first (low) bar for a new theory of physically simulated player. I'm not sure how to frame this...The game is deeply flawed, but it's also REALLY good. It's a tech demo trying like hell to be a narrative-driven VR-shooter breakthrough and doing a so-so job at it.

Cons first (you really should read the pros after, though):

The phys-body idea they're showcasing is interesting and has a LOT of potential but isn't polished enough. This is my first time getting a tad motion sick in about 500 hours of VR and it's entirely due to how your body "bounces" when you monkey about on ladders and phys puzzles. They should have added more damping to that bounce so your VR head doesn't rebound so much.

I've come to the conclusion that Story mode is a somewhat flawed/rushed sparse narrative acting as a giant 4 - 8 hour tutorial for Sandbox and Arena, which are SO good.

Pros (!!!)
If you like H3 and / or Sairento, this game really rocks as a wave shooter. I've played a lot of VR games and the only one that got me this hooked was Sairento. I just came out of a 2 hour-long Arena-mode binge and it was amazing. Gunplay is snappy and satisfying. Sandbox makes you feel like a kid again.

Good replay value in Arena and Sandbox modes. Now that I've learned the ropes, I don't think I'll ever play through the campaign again.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDont buy this game yet. Wait for a sale or something if you really want it

The game crashed multiple times on me, the physics got my hands stuck in a wall more times than i can count, forcing me to restart the game several times.
The story sucks balls, the ending makes no sense, has no buildup, no final boss, and doesn't explain anything. Better yet, to actually unlock things like sandbox you have to beat the games beyond mediocre story mode multiple times and search every nook and cranny in the map. (Which at this point, im not even gonna bother) Normally i would like this, if the worlds weren't so cluttered with random stuff and the horrible physics wasnt so restraining.
The gunplay is not all that great. The rifles collide with your head, so leaning in to use iron sights is nearly impossible sometimes. The only rifles that can be properly used are ones with scopes or lasers on them, And even then its difficult to aim.
You fight the same 3 enemies throughout the entire game, the zombies having 3 different versions which i dont count because there is little to no difference between them other than one has a chance of spawning with some weird projectile weapon. The headcrabs are fun to kill, but get boring after you fight the 5th horde of them halfway through the game. The soldiers AI is dumb as , they often just run into walls or forget you exist. They have a weird internal exoskeleton thing that is physics based, so if they bump into anything, it gets pushed out of their holographic body. This usually leads to you shooting at nothing until you spot the exoskeleton flopping around. It got incredibly boring shooting at the exact same enemies for 7 hours straight.
The physics puzzles in this game are just boring. Alot of them, mostly due to the extremely janky physics, tend to make you question if you are even doing it right. The amount of fumbling about until you happen to stumble into the solution makes it so irritating.
The non-physics "puzzles" Just involve finding a key and putting it into something. I thought we moved away from all FPS games using the same DOOM formula, but i guess i was wrong.
You dont get to touch the physics weapons at all until the very end of the whole game. You find one on a table, solve one puzzle, then the game just ends.
The ONE cool thing that i was excited about, the flying ship thing in the plaza area? I tried to fly it and it sent me flying, breaking the machine and getting me stuck in a wall.
The one HUGE issue that i know everyone is having with the game is the complete lack of a save feature. Just spent 2 hours in a level and you want to save your progress because youre tired and want to sleep? Nope, you better complete that level or loose ALL of that progress. Its 2019 and they dont have a basic save feature. What even is this.
The things i liked about the game? The atmosphere and aesthetics were pretty good and i liked the OST.

TL:DR

Pros:
Cool atmosphere, great OST
The headcrabs are fun for a few times

Cons:
Horrible AI
Tedious and boring puzzles
A grand total of 3 enemy types
The worst story ive experienced in a while
An ending to rival Mass effect 3
Crashes and bugs galore
Rifles are terrible due to head physics getting in the way
All the worthwhile modes are locked behind completing horrible story mode multiple times
COMPLETE AND UTTER LACK OF A SAVE FEATURE

And to think they had the GALL to repeat "What if its just a tech demo?" Over and over just to try and make fun of what everyone was saying. This is just a really long, extended tech demo\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe locomotion in this game is really nauseating, which exacerbated with all the locomotion based puzzles that you have to solve and then combine that with not having checkpoints produces a game that I just can't recommend at this stage.

After 5-15 minutes I start getting a headache, feeling sick and need to put the headset down.
- 5 minutes if I've been doing jumping/crouching.
- 15 minutes if I've been running around.

On the other hand, the guns are very fun to use. I love that your ammo is your money and that you can purchase items at intervals. The let down is that it feels like I've see all the guns available in the game already.

The game could benefit by having a proper progression on getting new weapons in the game. I picked up the SMG's before I even acquired a pistol (and a lot of ammo to spare), so there was never really a situation that made me want to use the pistol.\t\t\t\t\t

This game is absolutely amazing!
-The physics and technology that is in this game is superb
-The story mode is awesome with mini puzzles and challenges
-Easter eggs hidden all over
-Easily one of my favorite VR Games and I haven't even finished the campaign.
(The Tutorial or "The Museum" Was awesome just in itself - Watch some videos on this for more info w/o spoilers)

Some key points to understand before playing:
You need to move your body as if you are holding said weighted object;
For example - If you quickly whip out your gun from behind your head it flops all over the place.
But If you grab the gun and bring it down at a reasonable speed everything looks normal.
The Guns are meant to be more cinematic and easy to use than real life.
You can not change the fire mode on guns, but you can find guns that are set to different fire modes
Reloading is more of a "Look cool kinda reload" instead of "Reload exactly how this gun would in real life"

So as long as you understand how the physics work and how to interact with objects correctly then
the immersion and presence is like no other.\t\t\t\t\t
