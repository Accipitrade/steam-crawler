\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll put this at the top: The game is very good, and the combat is stellar. The middle chapters are fantastic fun, and it was truly one of the first BIG campaigns I played for VR.

Now with that out of the way, I want to talk about the interesting circumstances of the game's development. It was sort of a canary for Valve and their Half Life Alyx they were developing at the time. Stress Level Zero got some help and endorsement from Valve, along with getting to be the closest thing to a VR half life for a while, while Valve got to monitor its reception very carefully to see where the game succeeded and failed, to make tweaks to Alyx. I think this is a really cool relationship between indie and AAA devs. Indies take big risks and feel out a new niche, then the AAA devs come in to refine that formula with experience and budget.

That all being said, this game is a risk taker, and I think some of them absolutely flopped. Movement on anything except flat ground sucks, especially and situation where you need to climb. It feels like your torso and legs are getting caught on things. I still can't for the life of me climb up the top of a ladder. All of the physics puzzles also just aren't fun to solve in VR. Jankily dragging a heavy box somewhere is slow and unwieldy, and I think it works a lot better in a PC game like portal.

The early chapters are a bit meh, the middle chapters are amazing, and the final chapters just flop, at least for me. Everything after and including the dungeon, I just had no fun with. The "final boss" was incredibly clunky and prolonged. And I won't say what exactly, but they give you a REALLY fun and interesting toy in literally the final area, use it for like one puzzle, and then you have to climb a giant ladder and the game's over. Side note, on OG vive wands, holding onto something for a long period of time (i.e. holding grip button) just hurts. You *can* hold onto things a bit with just trigger, but they will slide around your hand, as you're basically doing an OK hand around it. It works better with index controls, but alas.

It's easier to find things to complain about than to praise, so consider the above paragraph just some constructive criticism. I still think the game is overall a great package, and worth the price when on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

Boneworks is one of the best and most innovative VR games to date. The advanced physics and large array of mechanics make this game awesome. You are constantly learning new things about the game's mechanics, such as the climbing, which can let you skip entire portions of levels. This encourages you to replay the game and take your time with it, changing up your playstyle and observing the world in more detail.

Stress Level Zero have made a fantastic game with Boneworks, and I can't wait to see what they do next.\t\t\t\t\t
