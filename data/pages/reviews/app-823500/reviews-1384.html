\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I am recommending this game, you should really only but it if you are very comfortable and familiar in intense VR experiences like H3VR.

The game now works with WMR, and I can say that it is a good experience for someone who is used to VR. When I played it, it took me about a half hour to get used to the controls, which isn't too bad. If you're new to VR, though, I would stay away from this game until you get the grasps of your controls with something simpler like superhot or the lab.

Also, if you have motion sickness this game will make you sick, I can guarantee it. There is lots of (in game) jumping, falling, and weird movement. I would also not recommend this for anyone who is afraid of heights.

Lastly, keep in mind that this is experimental. While there is a full physics simulation (which is really cool), it is a bit wonky in some areas.

All in all I do recommend this game for the people who know they're able to handle it. If not, however, hold off on this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot the Index Knuckles for this one and hasn't been disappointed one bit. I'm a pretty experienced VR guy, but I feel the need to take breaks once per hour or two.
This game is too meta for me, spent an hour just exploring the tutorial section. It was fun!
The most memorable experience was trying to get on a ledge with jumping and simultaneously crawling; that resultred in hitting my chandelier in Real Life.
Inventory and movement systems are really good and well-though out, guns are simplified but enjoyable.

Funny thing, I am getting some trippy moments now: hands feel weird, feels like fingers are automatically moving themselves without having to press anything.

The experience is solid, but you really need decent VR legs and some acclimation.\t\t\t\t\t
