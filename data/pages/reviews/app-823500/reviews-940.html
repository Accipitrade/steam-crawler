\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmbitious VR title with Half Life-inspired gameplay (especially HL2 because of all the physics puzzles).

You explore your way through a large mostly-linear level, fighting zombies, headcrabs and armed dudes. Combat is broken up with the occasional physics puzzle. Loot is strewn generously throughout each area, with even more for the observant or those willing to climb for it.

It even has a Gmod-esque sandbox mode, which isn't what I'm looking for right now but it's cool to have.

As of writing this, I'd like to see more enemy variety (there are pretty much only 3 types as it is), as well as more ranged weapons (grenade, shotgun, crossbow, etc). Would also be cool if you could cut limbs off or split heads with bladed weapons, but that's a big ask and I get why it's not in.

There's quite a bit of jank, but I still enjoyed the game and I'm impressed with what they've achieved. If you like Half-Life games and you like VR games, then you'll probably enjoy this.\t\t\t\t\t

Once you get the hang of the gunplay and get in the groove it really feels great to run and gun and swap mags on the fly. Some of the physics stuff is a little janky and climbing is annoying, but that isn't a huge part of the game. I'm playing on the OG Vive and wands and it plays great without having to tweak the controls at all. I'm fortunate enough to not experience motion sickness, but imagine this game would not be great if you do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first game that has genuinely left me with a lot to say, and there is a LOT to say.

Boneworks prides itself in its realistic and high quality physics engine, and for good reason. Boneworks has great physics, with large items having weight in the way you would assume they would. For example, if you try to swing a sledgehammer using only one hand, it isn't going to be as strong as you swinging it with two hands. Almost everything in the game can be interacted with, whether its picking it up or climbing a wall. However, there are some times where because your arms and head aren't given the restrictions that your virtual body has, there are many times that your virtual body just glitches out and you get stuck.

However, the realism doesn't fix the games many flaws. The pacing of the game is very odd. You will sometimes spend a lot of time solving a puzzle in a no stress, calm situation, and then 5 seconds later you're in a shootout running for your life. And, speaking of the puzzles, some of them are just lackluster. Many "puzzles" are just busywork, and a lot of them can just be skipped using a superjump glitch. This causes around half of the gameplay to be tedious and boring, while the rest is intense and action packed. The combat, however, is great. You can have meaningful combat and close and long ranges, with the guns being fun to use, and the melee weapons being very satisfying.

The plot of Boneworks is very confusing, and I along with many other players didn't understand the plot on our first playthrough. The story mode only took me around 10 hours to complete my first time through, however there is some amount of replayability with the arena and sandbox modes.

Overall, i think the strengths of this game outweigh the flaws, and its still a solid game to pick up and play. Solid 7/10\t\t\t\t\t
