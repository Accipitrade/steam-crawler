\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is the most DERIVATIVE game I've ever played. Period.

It's derivative to such an EMBARRASSING level that I doubt Brandon will be as free to enter Valve HQ as he was prior to the launch of his game.

When I first saw the NODE video of Boneworks "Testing The BEST VR GUNS Ever Made!" I wanted to pay for that demo level, and essentially I just have. Only, I haven't even got that because the save points are so far away from each other that to unlock the game demo section I need to finish The Streets level, which takes an hour each time to get to the next save point and is simply too tedious to do. Boneworks is sold as a game, but it really isn't. It's a tech demo that resembles a game. Compared to Stormland, Boneworks looks like it didn't employ any game designers at all. The result of all this looks as though it was just a string of ideas from the coders as to what might be cool in a game, but nothing more.

Let's do a thought experiment... What would have happened if Boneworks came out AFTER Valve releases HL:A with their tools for modding? If you put Boneworks up against whatever comes out of the new VR modding community (after HL:A is released) then Boneworks would come across as a bland experience with no new ideas. Just like it does right now. You can't even excuse it in terms of something that was rushed out before Christmas as a cynical money grab because these game ideas have been worked on for a while. No, this is just a poor single player campaign attached to a good tech demo. And that's lazy.

Hire better game designers. They really are worth it.

If you haven't bought this game, wait for Half-Life: Alyx.

If you have already bought this game, then wait for them to patch better save points, but don't hold your breath. Stress Level Zero don't respond to consumer demands. They didn't give the owners of Hover Junkers a single player campaign until way, waaaaaay too late. I never bought that game because of that. I doubt Boneworks will be much different.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI launched the game, started up the cute little tutorial area in a museum which is a fun way to demonstrate game mechanics. I found a side room with a garbage shoot in it. Decided what's the worst that could happen? Jumped down and wound up in a cityscape. Nothing was accessible likely due to it being a later area I can't access yet. Found a gate that was barred on the other side. Well.. Everything is a physics object including you... So I dragged a giant trash bin in front of the gate, hopped over, broke the boards blocking it and hit another roadblock. There were two metal bay doors both closed with no obvious way to open them and one specifically said do not open. Looks like they only open from the inside. Noticed a pallet jack a little ways away. Wheels started turning. Jammed the pallet jack under the door and lifted. 6 inches of clearance opened up, but not enough to squeeze through and the door was too heavy to lift by hand. Another roadblock... Until I noticed a SECOND PALLET JACK. More wheels turning, I jammed that pallet jack under the first and lifted. The gap was now wide enough to squeeze through. Two hours later of shooting enemies I was never introduced to, puzzle solving, following down whatever paths were open, I found a teleporter to section 5 of the game... And I never left the tutorial let alone going through any of the other four prior sections.

TL:DR - Best VR gaming experience this year.

As a review of the game mechanics themselves.. The physics based system is a bit clunky. You catch your arms on things walking by and get stuck, push yourself off high platforms unintentionally, only certain items are force grabbable forcing you to smash your fist and controllers in the ground trying to grab something, heavy objects are a nightmare to manipulate as your arms in game don't match up with your actual arm positions so for example, trying to use the sledgehammer is god awful because by the time your swing in game finishes, your actual arms are in a different position which causes you to miss the hit or makes it move in an odd way to try to catch up. Only way to fix this is to try to match your arms to the speed and position of the in game ones... Not a great feeling as it feels more like you're the game model trying to match up with the input while it's doing the same. The guns are NOT the best implemented in VR though they are fairly solid. That title will likely forever belong to H3VR though its an unfair comparison with it being built around gun simulation. They have the usual cheesy VR gun clunky feel but aim nicely and are at least enjoyable to shoot. I can't complain about not being told what buttons do what and how certain interactions are meant to work since... Well... I skipped half of the game by being a curious and determined idiot.\t\t\t\t\t

UPDATE: still an 8/10
This is an incredible game for VR. If you have VR you should do yourself a favor and grab it. The main story took me 12hrs and there is probably the same amount in replay considering the amount of hidden rooms and symbols I have yet to figure out. There are also unlockable modes that I'm interested in. However, the combat in Blade in Sorcery for melee weapons is still superior and I'm also not entirely amazed by the guns either. The physics throughout the world are what are dazzling at first, but the flaws in combat and guns really start to stand out. I wonder if some of this may have been masked by improved AI. The AI are really bad. In fact I had some levels require a restart because of how dumb they are (one idiot kept tripping over himself in a room I couldn't reach and I had to clear the wave of enemies - forced restart). The enemies in Fallout 4 VR are light years better than these AI. These AI are almost like the zombies in the new Pavlov update. The interactions with the environment, puzzles, and somewhat intriguing/creepy story had me enjoying myself. There are definitely some big wow moments and memorable scenes. One in particular will stick with me, it was a really well done and the immersion was high for me. I enjoyed it and do recommend but if you're at all on the fence, wait for a sale.

This is an awesome single player game in virtual reality. I strongly recommend this for people who recently picked up VR and have their VR legs. Dont start with this game! It will make you sick. It actually made me sick the first time I picked it up because I was in there for 3 hours straight for the first time in about a month. Ill be honest, VR has felt dull for me lately because of a lack of content. Ive had VR for a long time so Boneworks is a welcomed addition to my library.
I am compelled by the story. I find myself thinking about it (like right now on my lunch break at work) and Im excited to jump back in. The puzzles are complex and the environments appear to have many secrets. Im anticipating unlocks at the end of the game that I can come back through these levels with to find things Ill have to wait and see (refusing to google spoilers atm).

Graphics are perfect for virtual reality. The performance is top notch and I have zero complaints. This game is demanding as hell for sure. If you dont meet the recommended system specs then you may have problems and will need to lower settings most likely. My system is especially taxing with the wireless adapter and yet I still have zero performance issues which cant be said for many first day release VR games so bravo developers!!

This game is well worth the money. I anticipate another 3-4 hrs of story left for me and then I will be revisiting with unlocks and secrets because the ones I have found have felt rewarding. Honestly, just completing some of these puzzles in unique ways (at least they feel that way to me) makes me feel really accomplished. I imagine my total hr count will end up around 30hrs by the time this game gets old/something new comes along (thats 1$/hr for entertainment, great deal!). DLC will obviously change this and Im really really really hoping thats in the works..

Gunplay is great. The virtual stock is weird at first and not something I would ever want to see in FPS multiplayer but then again, after my second time coming back to it last night, I feel like I got used to it pretty quickly, although I still hate that the M4 doesnt have a way to change to single fire.

Physics are phenomenal. This is the new standard. In fact, this game is going to ruin a lot of other games when I try to go back to them, I can feel it. I think this level of physics is essentially required now, thanks to Boneworks (bravo devs!). I have to say that I strongly disagree with a lot of the reviews Ive been reading about body collision. While yes, my hand has gotten stuck IN in an object a couple times, Ive learned why its happening and can easily avoid it now. In fact, I think what they have accomplished with this level of detail is actual Virtual Proprioception. I never had proprioception like this in VR. Because there are consequences for my flailing arms and body, it ups the level of immersion for me by folds. I imagine that for the more Arcadey/Fast paced player this might be off putting, but I really hope this vote doesnt win out because I love how much more aware I am of my presence in VR because of these physics. Sure they need some tweaking, but I for one am a huge fan. I no longer feel like a floating body, thanks Devs!

Finally, I think this game did itself a disservice associating itself so closely with the Half Life universe. I constantly keep looking for ways to draw connections to that universe and sort of feel disappointed when I remember I shouldnt do that. I guess that just means Im really looking forward to Half Life Alyx which is a good thing. So if you are going into this game with THAT level of expectation, tame those flames a bit you wont get that level of storyline here. This game pokes fun at itself for possibly just being a tech demo and I think it is fair to say it most certainly is not. However, to VR veterans, there was that sinking feeling in the first few levels (no spoilers) that had me worried but the game does open up nicely later on.

TLDR Score: 8/10
