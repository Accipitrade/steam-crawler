\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGunplay is decent enough although I expected more from corridor digital.
While the game is fully playable on the vive I would really recommend this to someone with oculus or steam controllers as the touchpad movement is not ideal.
Yes I have tried different presets for the touchpad but they do still have their issues with going into a full run when that is not what I want.
Additionally the grip system for the vive needs to be a toggle grab. The presets allow for this but firearms will fly out of your hand if the grip button is not constantly held which can be a pain, to your hands and to your fun.
Lastly, The climbing mechanics take alot to figure out, I'm 9.2 hours in and I still cant figure out how to pull myself up to a ledge, probably going to have to look up a tutorial how to climb which feels like something I shouldnt have to do.

While these issues do impact the game, if an update for vive wand users is released then I would recommend it 100%. Until this happens I would only recommend it to those with the other controller styles.

I would recommend this game to those looking to see what the next step in VR games are going to be. Although be mindful that the experience can sometimes be soured by poor controls.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Note, this was one of my first VR Experiences* When I first got my VR headset (WMR Oddessy Plus) I purchased this game because I watched the development on NODE the youtube channel that promoted the game. I thought that the mechanics of the game and the combat looked amazing and I wanted my first VR experience to be amazing. The game was simply amazing. It all felt natural to me, the physics of the game, quite janky at some points in time but when they work, they work great. The combat felt smooth. during some battles, I even managed to break a sweat due to the intensity. The story (if you want to call it that) was mediocre at best. There was no real story, just move from place to place solving puzzles and fighting enemies along the way. The levels were open but felt bland in the cities, it was way to barren with little to no trace of damage or destruction. This effect did work in the game's favor at the start when you presented with an abandoned city, bodies laid in the street, no sound of cars or and person in sight, just eerie silence. After the "Story" you are presented with game modes afterword that can keep you occupied for hours, with challenges and new updates and game modes on the way, I can see myself playing this game for a while. I'm gonna break the game down into categories to rate. Combat- Easy 10/10 it felt amazing to shoot and use melee weapons on enemies. This needs to be the new standard for VR combat. Mechanics- 8/10 The physics is amazing and is definitely groundbreaking but there is a lot of jank that can break immersion, but nothing to cry over. Story- 3/10, There was barely even a story and the one that was presented was bland and felt extremely forced. Graphics- 6/10 Nothing special, but there are some bad textures on some of the assets but the game overall looks nice. Immersion 8/10 The Atmosphere along with the combat and physics makes the game very immersive and it really sucks you into the experience. But like I said the jank of the physics can sometimes kill the immersion. Overall I would give the game an 8/10, maybe a 7.5/10. If you are looking for a story buy Half Life Alyx, But if you are looking for a great combat physics game with puzzles. I can't recommend any other game. Hope this helps\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame feels unfinished:

Levels are large with generally very little put into that large space, making travelling around very tedious even with run enabled. Enabling said run is too ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ easy (double push analog stick), resulting in sudden sprints because you're simply trying to change your direction. This game desperately needs a hybrid walk/teleport system like Alyx.

Weapon sights weren't designed for people using a oled headset, they've ended up being so small and low res making aiming extremely difficult and finicky.

Body physics results in the camera going wild when trying to interact with the simplest of objects (♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ DOORS) resulting in headaches.

Enemy ai has no concept of pathing, resulting in enemies becoming stuck on walls trying to go at you in a straight line.

Hands get stuck in everything, having to spend more time getting them unstuck rather than actually interact with anything.

This is really just a prototype rather than anything actually finished. A prime example of a team that wanted to make a game that did everything, but ends up doing nothing well whatsoever.

Update:

Decided to try this game once more, this time with a different headset. Got to the medieval arena level for what it's worth. New points of comparison will come from Half Life Alyx, S&S WD, and Into the Radius

+ Using a higher res headset does largely solve the issue of weapon aiming. However, this should not be the only solution to poor weapon sights for earlier headsets.

- My issues with the levels have become worse. All levels are simply too large for their own good. The aesthetic of the game compounds this issue by making everything seem even more empty. This problem is shared with Into the Radius.

- The full physics body continues to ruin any sense of immersion by refusing to cooperate when trying to do simple actions such as climb up a ledge/box, climbing ladders, or becoming stuck on objects that you can't see. Walking Dead handles world climbing far better than this without inducing vomit.

- Why in the hell is grab item from inventory trigger and not grip. I cannot find a setting to change this.

- I'm amazed they didn't just crib the gravity glove mechanic from Alyx given the devs had early access to Alyx when they visited Valve. The gravity gloves would have solved a huge number of the issues in regards to world interact-ables.

- Enemy AI remains incompetent as previously mentioned.

I cannot finish this game no matter how much I try. Boneworks remains a tech demo that grew far too large for its own good, compounded with a dev team that is extremely resistant to criticism. 6/10\t\t\t\t\t

The second issue is that I wanted to test the arena mode, but it is locked as long as the campaign is not finished.

So, I cannot recommend it. That's a shame because there is probably a lot of work behind.

For the dev:
There is room for improvement: maybe to have a virtual neck more elastic to avoid head jerk, or to make smaller halls and to have less repetitive textures?\t\t\t\t\t
