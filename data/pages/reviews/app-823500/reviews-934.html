- The game jokes about being a "tech demo", but it actually feels like one. Not much of a story going on.
- Forces you to unlock things like the sandbox.
- Somewhat improved recently, but still doesn't allow you to save anywhere.
- Few different enemy types, none of which pose a big challenge.
- Physics puzzles can be more tedious than interesting.
- Seeing your avatar's arms and legs in game is distracting and disorienting.
- Awkward to interact with the inventory. Get ready to drop stuff on the ground.

Still recommending because it does a lot of things right. It feels like future VR games will crib a lot from this.\t\t\t\t\t

So uhm, obviously this was meant to be some sort of tech demo that got out of the hands of the devs, and they won't take any criticism. Wouldn't recommend at full price, maybe pick it up for free at greenman or is there any deal.

Art design is phenomenal, physics engine and logic is brilliant, but, that doesn't make a good game.

First off, it's incredibly unstable. Make sure you have nothing else running besides this, or the game will somersault you out of your VR experience through some vomit-inducing rave techno visuals. Don't touch the steam home button, and if you are on WMR, for the love of god, shut off your desktop visuals.

The hype for this game was not only around the physics, but also the use of the valve index to this game.

The valve index, adds, NOTHING, of value to this game.

Not a single thing.

Aside from single finger movement.

I've tried every controller and headset, and I'd even say the Oculus touch is the better controller for this game, or the WMR contollers if you don't mind losing tracking every time you hold a firearm or try to throw an object. Do NOT use the vive wands. Any function in the game on that is barely functional, especially climbing.





Level design is wonky, incoherent, pretty much feels unfinished and rushed out the door with no real thought behind it give or take one or two set pieces. You can practically bust out of any level with no effort, and circumvent 90% of the set scripted scenes by just taking a mildy alternate route. Stuff just kind of pops in and out of reality depending on if you pass the right trigger lines in a level, so it's pretty easy to miss a lot of the stuff going on if you don't stick to this incredibly thinly designed linear pathway that is just so easy to walk off of and break everything. It's also incredibly inconsistent. One level, you'll constantly find ways to get back to the old points you were at, like a sandbox level. Some other time, you'll be completely cut off with no real way back to any of the items or unexplored areas you left behind because you weren't sure if ' this ' was the way of progression or another branching dead end. To top it off, the game relies on the use of ' secret dev tools ' hidden around the map for you to ' find as an easter egg and fun time ' to be able to go back to some spots, which just reeks of bad design choices to me.

In a previous update, they added save support.
What this means is, there are some ' save stations' where you have to put your gear into a box and hold onto this object for the game to ' save your state'.
This means every time you reload this level, you will start from this exact spot.
Not only does the item box sometimes not even save the items (which can be a if you are trying to collect all the collectibles), when you load it in, get ready to start looking for your gear as it just gets blown out of it like meteors exploding over the night sky, as it all just kind of... spawns inside of each other and blows up.

Adding to that, are you a completionist? For the love of christ, and for your own sanity, please skip this game. Else, be prepared to play the same level from start to finish at least 4 to 5 times to get:
Unlocking the next level (Just play the game, right?)
All of the currency (forcing you to use melee only, as your currency is also your ammo, so, probably add 3/5 reloads of savestates to that)
All of the item unlocks (If you have the foresight to carry the stuff to the right points so you don't lose them, or betting on the wonky save system to properly save the items you placed down, so you'll have to restart again, in which most cases means having to go through the level entirely to be able to restart it, as restarting it from the menu reloads your save state!)
Doing all of the above again whenever the devs decide to update a level and remove/replace all the items.
Fun times!

There's pretty much guns and ammo laying all over the place, you've got these vend-o-mats that you slap ammo in to to pay for objects you can take out of them, which is really neat, actually. There's also a load of hidden items all over the place that sometimes you need to solve puzzles for or break out of bounds to find (again making me wonder if the out of bounds thing was by design or some developer cheat easter egg).
... Except, then, sometimes, you take 3 steps and there's a weapon-strip field which removes all of your items in your inventory, and there doesn't really seem to be a way around half of them...

Not only is that just an absolute waste of effort, but it just screams bad game design if you find guns in one room, and then in between levels get stripped off of your equipment to go back to a pistol start. What does that even say about progression? Is there even any sense of progression in a design like that?




Sound design is mediocre, but on par for a sub standard hollywood movie.
Same can be said about the music, which does add to the whole 'VR inside of VR' technology vibe, which in of itself isn't bad, but, gets fairly jarring at some points, especially when playing ad infinitum because some bug decided you're not out of a scripted event yet.

Graphics look amazing at first, until you look too close, in which case you will come across a lot of odd, out of place, sometimes even broken models, textures etc. which may or may not be placed there on purpose, but, I got banned for asking, so, I'll go ahead and suggest they weren't. You'll come across untextured walls, sometimes low-res models and textures just in plain sight or hidden around corners that you'd think would be some sort of LOD models, but no, they're just chilling there, for whatever reason.

Combat is ' alright ', if you enjoy tapping the slowmotion button to make everything ' epic '. Otherwise, it's mediocre, lackluster at best. Melee is too easily broken and doesn't really carry any impact aside from frustration. (Object penetration with sharp weapons is pretty neat, though, and the physical impact on your victim is pretty interesting to study.) Don't bother with two handed melee weapons unless you have razor sharp tracking, it simply won't work as intended or won't feel satisfying at the very least.

Weapons are alright, but incredibly simplified. You have your standard set of AR platform weaponry and some action movie favourites like the glock, colt and usp, M16, MK18 and MP5, all potentially with varying modifications. Be aware though, the modifications are baked into the weapons, and you can't modify weaponry yourself.
If you played any other VR game, don't expect much innovation here. Handling weapons has been streamlined to a near fault. Pick up the gun, do an extensive reload at first (insert mag, rack slide/bolt), fire until empty, next, lift magazine to gun, gun auto reloads, press fire, repeat.
No clearing jams, no trackpad/touchpad interactivity, nothing fancy for the index, just, slap and go.

The story is pretty much nonexistent, and just seems like an easy spin to add the youtube crew into their own cutscenes for extra brownie points. It's like FNAF, pretty much just waiting for fan input to write the story for them, with some ' lol random' humor thrown in to be extra zany. The plot twist is literally the thumbnail image of the game, so, uhm... good luck with that, I guess.


Again, the physics engine (and the added puzzles), and the gmod-style arena are probably the main draws for the game, and they're pretty neat gimmicks. It just feels like this was a passion project that ran out of steam halfway through production and shouldn't have been sold as a fully integral game, but more like a tech demo or presentation to show what the future of VR could hold.

Granted, none of that is going to make me stop playing it, I've already waited so damn long on this to come out.

Now to wait for a studio to pick up the proprietary tech and make it into an actual game.\t\t\t\t\t

You'll get about 11 hours out of the main story mode if you don't around with sub puzzles and collectables. The story is a bit murky, but still a fun ride, and leaves a lot for you to interpret yourself.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile it was clearly made with the Index in mind, I've spent over 24 hours in-game playing with the vive and the vive wand controllers, and have thoroughly enjoyed nearly all of it. It takes a little bit to get used to, and the clunky controls can be frustrating, but once you get the hang of it, it's loads of fun. Besides the steep learning curve, a big drawback of the game is its plot. Much of the story and its context is not told to you in a way that is easy to understand for the casual player. Unlike some people say, there IS a story here, but major aspects of it is found in SLZs other game, Duck Season, which makes it very hard to understand unless you look online or have dove deep into other SLZ games. However, despite these drawbacks, Boneworks provides an incredibly satisfying experience, especially for the size of the team that made it. If nothing else, I would recommend supporting the game purely based on the innovation that this indie dev have brought to the table for VR, and we need more devs like this, who are willing to take risks and innovate.

TLDR: Extremely fun once you get the hang of it, but controls can be clunky at times and the story isn't well fleshed out in front of the player.\t\t\t\t\t
