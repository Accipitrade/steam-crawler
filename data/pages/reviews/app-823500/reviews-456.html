\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMechanics feel a little clunky at first due to the weight and force system, but once you learn the controls, rules of the world and physical limits of your in-game body, the increased possibilities of intuitive physical interaction really enhances the immersion compared to Half Life Alyx. The belt/pocket inventory system feels intuitive as well. Basically, it's a trade-off of more clunkiness for more gameplay depth, and it's worth it. I think it won't get much better than this for virtual reality.

Gunplay feels good, and melee combat feels pretty visceral and violent, especially the first times you encounter enemies it can be even scary.

I really like the atmosphere of sneaking through places you shouldn't be in, like climbing over fences or breaking through windows with whatever object you have at hand. Level design-wise it's the type of game that you are supposed to break to proceed, It creates an experience that you truly own and remember. Especially due to the physicality, it makes you feel like you really were there and did those things.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t\t\t
$30 + 6-8 hours of physical labor to earn player freedom in a tech demo..? Err...

For a experimental VR mechanics/physics demo, why force players to play through a campaign to unlock the sandbox/arena modes and weapons, when the campaign isnt really the selling point to begin with?

I just wanted to jump right in and try the combat that was advertised using the impressive physics (credit where its due) & tools/weapons at my instant disposal, but instead I was asked to climb my way though big bland rooms of obstacle courses as my in-game legs awkwardly bent up into my body anytime I climbed... Not really what I got the game for and after an hour, I wasnt having much fun being forced to go through these rooms just to unlock the modes I actually bought the game for hours/days later.

You also have to manually collect the items you want to use in the other modes as you progress through the campaign. Quite literally, you have to physically work to unlock these modes if you didn't buy it for the campaign. That concept alone is just funny to me.

Dont get me wrong, its nice to have some sort of objective mode and the physics WERE great But I would much rather have the option of experiencing them freely.

If we isolate the game to just the physics, it's good.. I can appreciate that and Im glad it exists as a point of reference or inspiration for more innovative physics in future VR games.. But if Im to be forced to play any campaign, Id much rather invest my time and money towards a game with more substance and an actual story; games like TWD Saints and Sinners or HL Alyx (they also have decent physics).

Had this tech demo allowed me to experiment with the physics in my own way, which I feel should be naturally granted for a title like this, I wouldn't have refunded it... But I think it's fully the intention of the developers to keep it this way.\t\t\t\t\t
