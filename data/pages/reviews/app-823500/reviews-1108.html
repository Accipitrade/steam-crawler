\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is the most innovative VR game out there. It is SO much fun. It pushes boundaries of what we expect from VR and is the rare game to boldly redefine what games are. Don't buy it.

The game runs great on my crappy hardware and WMR headset, so it's not quite the same level as 2017 PUBG or DayZ for the past whole five years, but... This game is clearly unfinished. Your arms glitch all over the place, you spawn far from your body sometimes, climbable surfaces are horribly inconsistent, there seems to be no way to easily climb over a ledge you're hanging from, the physics is glitchy as hell, locking you out of some puzzles.

But if all of that sounds bad, check out the game's cardinal sin: no saving. No checkpoints, no quick save, no auto save, zero, zip, nada. The closest thing is respawning at the beginning of the level without the gun you were holding at the time. You have to play the entirety of every single level before you can make it to the next one. And these aren't short levels, some can go in excess of 90 minutes.

Clearly there was some thought into cross level progression because of the game's currency system, but they failed to implement even the most basic save mechanic.

I am going to refrain from anything positive about the game outside of my first paragraph until they fix this. The save mechanic especially. And I do have a LOT of positive things to say.

EDIT (21/02/2020): The saves update has come out and I can't wait to jump back in and check it out.

EDIT (26/11/2020): Game is in a much better state now. Definitely recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

Neat body presence wrapped in a pretty ♥♥♥♥ videogame

I think this game simulates a full body really well, considering it's only working with your hand and head positions. The interactions with the environment, your weapons and tools are very enjoyable.
Every other part of this game feels really amature though:

- Visually the game looks like it came straight out of 2004, which makes it baffling why the game chugs sometimes, making melee combat especially a complete mess.
- Artistically it's a mess, none of the enemy types have any visual design relation to each other or the world they're in. The levels look like someone messed around in Source for a weekend.
- The story is trite, barely relates to what you're doing and the most important plot points are conveyed to you via live action video on a virtual movie screen, instead of in the game world
- Level design is scattershot, badly paced, environments are way too big and tedious to traverse most of the time. Puzzle concepts don't organically evolve, the environments the puzzles take place in barely make any sense.

This really just feels like a tech demo that you pay 25 bucks for. The in game jokes to that effect only add insult to injury.

Only buy this if you want an excuse to mess around in VR for extended periods of time, as a game, it's just kind of a bore for long stretches of its campaign \t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have now edited my old review to replace with this belated review. I did end up buying this game again after the save system was fixed. The hours played can be attributed mostly to my son playing this game so much.

I give this a thumbs up with some caveats. Mostly due to the generic single player campaign.

1. Level design is bland and boring. Whole rooms with bland boring textures, but pulling off a generic Half Life vibe is something I guess.

2. The execution of the plot is b tier movie bad. The real life people on the monitors to get plot points across is too over the top and 4th wall breaking but in a cheesy uncool way.

3. The climbing/lifting yourself up sucks. Yeah, I get that real physics are at work here, but pulling yourself up to a higher platform is a chore here and I much prefer Half Life Alyx style movements.

4. Jumping should not be in this game. This is a VR game not a 90's platformer. I get that in real life you may have to jump up to a higher position to get somewhere but the way this game handles it is immersion breaking to me. Again I much prefer Half Life Alyx way of doing things.

5. There is not a fair system set up to get some of the most cool gadgets or weapons unlocked for sandbox mode This is not a Rare developed collectathon game from the 90's.

The developers here do not respect the time and energy it takes to get all these things legitimately unlocked.

I say this because after so many hours of playtime, my son still didn't unlock everything so I found a save file that had everything unlocked and used it so he could fully enjoy the sandbox mode without unrealistic requirements.

Now with all the caveats out of the way, the real reason we all play this game is because of all the cool gadgets and weapons you can use in the sandbox area.

My son has spent hours setting up games/scenerios in the sandbox area trying out different gadgets and weapons and spawning different enemies in each time. It is well worth the price of this game for the sandbox mode alone This is due to the developers attention to detail when it comes to handling weapons and how enemies react to the physics model used in this game. It's a sight to behold in vr, and even I get a kick out of watching my son on the flatscreen while he tries to come up with something new to do in the sandbox area.

I will be buying BoneLab on day 1 for my son's Steam account and if I like what I see after trying it for myself then I will buy it again for my account too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't help but compare this game to Half-Life 2. Boneworks shows what you can really do with VR like no other game does at the moment, and I really hope it becomes an example to other developers. Everything in the game feels stunningly natural, the way you interact with objects, the way you climb obstacles and the way you fight enemies. The immersion is truly hard to describe.
I'm not terribly prone to motion sickness, but I do get it in some games after I play for a while. In some games my eyes also start hurting. Neither happens in Boneworks. Yesterday I played it for three hours straight and didn't even realize it.
Definitely the best VR game so far. I cannot praise the developer enough.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE: I didn't play this game for almost three weeks, came back, and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ all but one of my complaints have been fixed. That being that the game has extremely long levels with no way to resume from a save later on. I STILL haven't finished streets because I rarely have more than two hours of free time to play a game, but I have gotten much further than before and enjoyed myself a lot. Changing this to a recommendation now. Once the devs add a save/load feature this will be a solid game.

Played through the tutorial, had some fun, then I got to the first real level, "Streets". Played for about an hour wrestling with the extremely clunky movement, inventory system and physics puzzles that don't even really seem to have any correct answer beyond "just ♥♥♥♥ around until it works" until I reached a puzzle that I couldn't figure out that involved moving a platform across a room with a deathpit at the bottom. I quit the game and came back to it a few days later only to have to redo the entire level. On my second playthrough I realized that the puzzle I got stuck on was unsolvable the first time because it was bugged and the platform couldn't move due to some ♥♥♥♥♥ physics object that was obstructing it. This time I solved the puzzle in about a minute and proceeded. The first batch of real enemies comes out at this point. I reached for my side-holstered pistol about five times while a singular enemy lumbered towards me and grabbed my axe each time. I said "♥♥♥♥ this" and just hit the dude with the axe. After laboriously clearing out the remaining enemies while literally fighting with my own inventory I moved onto another room with some climbable boxes. At this point I was already well aware that anything involving climbing in this game is just a ragefest so I avoided them as much as possible and just shot wooden box at the top of the stack. A key fell down, I inserted that key into a nearby door and entered the room. I picked up a VR headset laying on the ground and my hand did a 360 in-game. I let go and tried again. Same thing, except I held on this time. Then I was teleported out of the map and fell to my death. The screen faded in and I was placed back at the beginning of the level with none of my weapons.

You know how in Skyrim or HL2 you would sometimes walk on a physics object and it would fling you across the map? That's this game, except its VR and the whole game is simulated physics, even your limbs. I really wanted to like it, but I just don't. I'm having a hard time understanding the score it has on Steam because I can't be the only one finding it frustrating just existing in this game. Even something as simple as putting a weapon away results in it being dropped on the ground 9/10 times.

EDIT: Once you actually have a gun in your hand and have managed to successfully insert a magazine into it, though, the shooting mechanics are pretty good. The guns feel akin to Pavlov but slightly snappier which is a good thing.\t\t\t\t\t
