\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS IS THE VR GAME OF THE COMING YEAR! A MUST BUY FOR ANYONE WITH A VR SYSTEM!
But alas, this game has it's draw backs.

It's a combat game first, story game second, so you have to keep that in mind. There are a lot of things that could be made better, and it would make the gameplay 10x as good as it currently is.

Sandbox being locked behind Story Mode is a negative for me. I know you worked hard on the story, but locking the Sandbox and forcing us to play your Story isn't how you should go about it. Let people play the game they want to play it, not the way you want us to play it.

If you die, you reset to the beginning of the scene, and these scenes are LARGE. Don't expect to get any checkpoints during your run.

The Camera is VERY jumpy. You have a physics based body, that means a lot of wiggle. Attaching the camera to the head of basically a moving rag doll makes for some motion sickness inducing moments.

Climbing is too hard, we as VR Players control the Head and Arms of the player, and you make sure we know that. When we climb, it's all arm strength. Realistically, we would use our legs, so making the climbing just the slightest bit easier, including the mantling, would be a strong suit for the gameplay.

On top of the climbing being too hard, the hands and arms are very janky sometimes. They can go through objects on the occasion and get stuck, completely ruining your current run.

The Quickslots are too small. You have to be VERY precise to put things in the slots and to take them out. This leads to a lot of fumbling and throwing and time taken away from playing. Making them slightly bigger would be very helpful, and as well as allowing us to maybe move them slightly. Maybe a calibration where we can fine tune the locations to a more natural position would be nice.

I will continue to edit/comment on this as time goes on. I just finished the campaign and am eagerly awaiting the next update!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed it for 3 hours, definetely a great game. Have some concerns though which I'm sure will be fixed.

The physics are awesome but there are a load of bugs when it comes to placing things. When you walk with objects it flounces around.

The Gunplay is very nice and I love the animations. I feel in power when someone or something attacks me. Blunt weapons need a fix but except from that I like it. With sharp weapons I would love to see some more power behind it. When I'm waving around my hatchet I don't feel comfortable or like I'm in power. I would love to see melee changed.

Puzzles get tedious in the story. It isn't so forgiving, I like that and I don't like that. Sometimes I feel annoyed during a puzzle. Not because of the puzzle but that the game sometimes breaks it, and I lose all my progress.

And for god sakes the non existing checkpoints. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. I came so far yesterday and I lost all my progress cause there wasn't a checkpoint. It doesn't save and when you load your save you'll start at the beginning of your scene and it's very very very annoying. So make sure that if you're playing Boneworks you are ready to end the scene cause otherwise you have to restart.

All and all, a great game but in desperate need of changes. I'm gonna keep on playing it and I wish all the best to the Stress Level Zero team. You have changed VR forever. Definetely!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: Now that I've played a bit more, the game's gotten better (less parkour more fighting) + I got the hang of it. Still, this was my experience during the first couple of hours in the game.

Game mechanics are good, it's the level design that needs improvement.Game mechanics are good, it's the level design that very much needs improvement.

I was quick to buy this game because I've been following it for a long time and I am invested in the developments of vr technology. I don't regret my purchase for these reasons but I wouldn't recommend it to someone else based on the 2 hours I spent on the campaign (got to the "streets" scene where you push the lever to balance the tethered platforms.)

Pros:
Amazing never seen before physics which add a real sense of immersion.
Good humor built into the story and bits of text found around the world.

Cons:
While the physics are the best I've seen, the weight aspect does not work well with the parkour levels.
The significant lack of fail safes and check points are sure to have some players throwing their controllers down as each level requires an amount of speed, precision, and carefulness that I find very difficult in vr. It sometimes takes minutes just to get back to retry a certain difficult jump.

When you die, you spawn back at the beginning of the scene. Though all of the objects in the scene remain as they were before you died, I find it annoying to have to run through the level again.

Also the game didn't seem to have a save & quit option when I had stopped playing. If the game didn't save my progress in the scene when I come back to it, I'll report back here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere to start.

FIRST AND FOREMOST IF YOU ARE NOT VERY COMFORTABLE IN VR DO NOT GET THIS GAME. Besides your standard smooth locomotion there are a few more things that will throw newer VR players for a loop

I will keep this review spoiler free so some things may be vague but you can read it if you want.

First and foremost I beat the campaign in ~ 7.5 hours..... 5-6 is probably minimum 10-15 if you are the stop and smell the flowers kind of person.

Story: 6/10 - honestly its just okay, It felt shallow, and I felt like the story was an afterthought.
Tutorial 10/10 - great tutorial, and well integrated to the game, never once did I want to skip it, or felt like it was a waste of time.
Puzzles: 8/10 - most weren't too difficult, and the ones that are difficult it is more related to poor direction, and the finnicky physics system.
GunPlay: 7/10 - no frills, could be useful to have a virtual stock to steady.... and There is not much reason to use anything besides pistol outside of a few occasions.
Melee: 8/10 - The "weight" mechanic felt like it got in the way or was too slow, could be improved..... or maybe it just takes some getting used to..... though impaling enemies 10/10
Easter Eggs/References 10/10 - The game is FULL of them..... keep your eyes open.


Level Design - 9/10 - large levels sell the atmosphere/situation, But they are relatively sparse, and some of the "hidden" paths can be fairly obvious, it was probably a requirement for performance/optimization, and for the most part

Enemies: Design 10/10 spot on. AI 5/10, honestly dissapointing..... especially the shooting AI. Enemy Diversity: 6.5/10 - there are very few enemy types, and while they all feel unique...... it is VERY limited. I would have liked to see 2-3 more types... or variations.


Physics:

Interactables: 10/10 omg...... it will be hard to go back to games like 95% of stuff you can interact with.......very nice

Full Body physics body: 8/10.... its not perfect it is good, but sometimes, especially when moving I found that I would get things stuck..... having either haptic feedback when my hands/arms are hitting things or some sort of "help" system to prevent some of these snags would greatly improve this.

Weight System: 6/10 - just felt off.... part of that is dealing with massive amounts of colliders and is probably more the fault of the physics engine, but it could use some work.



The game was very enjoyable, and solid length.... I expect its a 20-40ish hour game once you are dealing with more unlockables etc. The game is innovative, but its not perfect and has its issues..... but its very interesting and innovative.\t\t\t\t\t
