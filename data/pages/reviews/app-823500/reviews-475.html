\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou know how games like Doom, Half Life 1, Halo, Half Life 2, etc. were revolutionary and changed games forever?

This is the next one in that line.

There is a level about halfway through the game called Warehouse. To get past the first part of the level, the intended path is to make your way around the perimeter of the warehouse, get a battery, put it in a socket to open a door, and make your way through the warehouse. And there's nothing wrong with doing it like that.

But, you could also vault over a fence and get the battery without going around the warehouse. Or you could put a crate underneath one of the front windows, climb on that, and make your way inside. Or you could climb the warehouse itself, skipping the entire section. There are even ammo crates on the roof, which confirms that the devs noticed these alternative solutions and purposely left them in.

Sometimes I don't even think they have a "intended" solution at all when creating a challenge. You might spot an ammo crate out of reach on top of a shelf. You climb the shelf, or get a chair and stand on that. or you can knock the shelf over, or use a long melee weapon to push the ammo crate off. You could even shoot the crate open and force grab the ammo.

Instead of thinking "what do you do in most video games," or "what is the game trying to get me to do," you think "what would I do in real life?"

Other games have done this non-linear and creativity-encouraging style before, sure. Breath of the Wild comes to mind for me. But the fact that this is in vr means that the number of possibilities for solving problems is exponentially higher. They don't have to code and animate every possible solution, because you ARE the animation.

Playing this was a mind-blowing experience, and I experienced it on the worst, cheapest headset on the market. The headset was about £150 ($200) and I have heard some people have gotten it for less. I may have had a worse experience than someone playing on a Valve Index, but the point is that YOU DO NOT NEED 1000+ POUNDS TO ENJOY VR.

However, there are two major flaws.

The first is that the story, although pretty good, is EXTREMELY hard to understand. I had to watch like an hour of youtube videos from varying sources to get a good idea of what the story is.

The second flaw is the Jankiness. The game is quite unpolished, mostly in the physics area. The physics are great when they work, but that is only like 80% of the time. Clipping and vibrating objects are abundant.

If the story was more clearly shown, It would be an 8.5/10. If the physics were polished as well, it would be a 9.5/10 and probably my favourite game of all time, vr or not.

Buggy as it is, I can still easily give it an 8/10. Quite frankly it is the best vr game on the market right now (yes that includes HLA). If you have vr, buying this is an absolute no-brainer. This is THE vr game.\t\t\t\t\t
