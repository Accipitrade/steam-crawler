\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was my most anticipated VR game of the year, so it makes me sad to make this review... The story and the enemy/level design looks fantastic and full of depth, but the mechanics that I hoped would make this game a huge success are actually it's downsides in my opinion.

Physical Presence
While I feel that being able to interact with the world in it's entirety is great for a VR game, having it for your entire body can cause a lot of unnecessary problems. Without also being able to feel the world, my limbs will get caught all the time and I wont know right off. This can make reacting to key moments fairly unreliable. This can also make climbing and general traversal incredibly frustrating and finicky, which is a huge part of this game. Climbing up onto ledges is especially difficult from time to time.

Object Weight
I do think objects need some weight both for immersion and balance, but a lot of objects in this game seem a bit too heavy. This combined with the way your characters body exists in the game can cause a lot of separation between what your character does and what you intended to do, which also adds to the previously mentioned issue of reacting to things being unreliable and sometimes frustrating because of it. Not to mention needing to hold buttons to hold objects leading to plenty of hand cramps when using anything other than the Index controllers.

Controls & Inventory
I cant say for the Index controllers, however I noticed that the default controls for the other controllers didnt match to what was explained during the tutorials, so I had to mostly figure them out for myself. The inventory slots (especially the hip slot) also seem to be very picky about when something actually goes into the slot. Putting an item away usually takes a couple tries. I also find myself accidentally pulling out items while trying to climb or do puzzles, and in turn sometimes wasting ammo on automatic weapons.\t\t\t\t\t
