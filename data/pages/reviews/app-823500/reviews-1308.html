\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCurrently, the game is still rough, but better than launch. The save points are nice, but really should've been there from the start.

Boneworks is a tech demo that largely fails when it comes to level design and just general game design. For as detailed as the physics framework holding everything together is, you never get too comfortable with any of it. You would think that by putting Half-Life on a pedestal, that the devs would have taken better notes, but virtually nothing that Half-Life is good at is represented in this game. Remember CROWBARS? Remember HEADCRABS? It's a homage in a purely visual sense; none of the craft is here.

In a Post-Alyx world, Boneworks does have merit in its controls, but the game you play with these controls is painfully amateur. If Stress Level Zero completely scrapped the campaign and built an entirely new one using the lessons they learned, much how Valve did with the original Half-Life in 1997, then Boneworks would probably be something I can recommend.

For now, I can safely say that whoever made me climb that giant ladder in order to complete this game, this thumbs down is for you. I'm not usually one to get hung up on a bad level here and there, but every stupid decision you make in the Unity Editor, I had to experience it in VR, making it 20x worse, so this thumbs down comes from the heart.

Boneworks is valuable as a cautionary tale, but I cannot genuinely recommend it considering how highly the game clearly thinks of itself. Nobody would play these levels if they were ported to HL2, that's all I'm saying.\t\t\t\t\t
