Controls.

The controls are a mess on the Vive, every item you pick up slides around, unless you press 3 buttons at once.
Story is dry, enemies uninteresting to me personally. Just very... bland.

Jumping has to be done by the press of a button, since the calibration for your height messes up actually jumping in real life. It says to redo your measurements of your height, and probably the floor measurements found next to it, but no matter how much playing around I did, it thought I jumped about 5 cms.

Movement is done by using your touchpad, which is horribly awkward.

The full-body system is not only awkward, but also gets in the way with the tedium of a cat chasing a fly. That is exactly how it feels to climb, or in general touch anything, since you cannot push your hands through walls, therefore it gets stuck in everything. Your hand in real life can be above you, on the right, but in-game, it's on the left, below you... Stuck on the very pipe you are trying to climb.

Gameplay

It's loading screens take way too long. Way, way too long compared to how long it should take on a mid-range system.

Dying puts you tens of minutes of gameplay back.

Hitting and stabbing is not only near useless, but, the experience of using weapons that aren't guns is a mixture of disturbance, surprise, annoyance, and disappointment. You can try hitting things like the wall, or an actual enemy, or you can hit a box, but every movement is slowed down, snaps into place, and does not comply with your actual movements. It's horrible, and I don't understand why I have to accord to the game's rules of physics, instead of just using our real ones.

Feels like it's all a rushed mess, be it for just the Vive controllers, or gameplay-wise. One that's trying to be different, and do things differently, and more realistically, but honestly just feels awkward, off, and just like an off-setting experience.

Graphics

Nothing groundbreaking, just the average for a higher budget studio, as far as visuals go. If anything, it's lacking, just for the sake of running good enough on VR.

Performance

Quite jumpy. At least on an AMD GPU, easily goes from 4 ms to above limit on occasions, coming from a higher-end OC RX 580. I played around with the resolution to try and remedy this, initially staring off higher, since most games can be put up some hundred or so.

Overall

Both immersion and fun is ruined by the annoying elements of this game, all by what is supposed to be it's highlighted and defining elements. Personally, I think it falls flat on it's ass.

There is truly nothing I can say about this game to make it sound good. It'd barely, if at all, pass as a non-VR title, and the only fun thing I found in it is stabbing through things.
If the only reason your game gets a pass is because I can say that "Oh, it's good for a VR game," then I'd rather not.

The full-body system works when all you do is press buttons, so you can look at those nice animations, and the polish that this game lacks at every aspect that actually matters for actually playing as a VR title.

From what I've seen, people are giving positive reviews mostly because of the game's "physics," graphics, and performance. But not a single one of those aspects make for a good game. Especially when number one is awkward and bleh, which is why it's not found in other VR titles, second is as basic as can be, and third is inconsistent.\t\t\t\t\t
