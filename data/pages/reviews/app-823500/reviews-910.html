\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is the most nausea inducing experience I have ever felt; I can't go more than 20 minutes a session without wishing I could puke.

1. Locomotion is the only way to play the game (ie, no teleport or hybrid). They actively brag about this in the intro (by breaking the 4th wall).

2. The locomotion is acceleration based. Not a simple on/off, it's like you're on wheels with a motor. Also, if you hit the thumbstick wrong, it will put you into an accelerated sprint (THE VOMIT).

3. There is no "blink" turning, it's not even blurred, it's only "HEY HERE'S 45 DEGREES OF INFO" IN .01 SECONDS! It causes mental overload and disorientation when in VR. To be fair, you can close your eyes for the duration of it, but I can also just go play HL:Alyx instead and not have to do that.

4. This game prides itself on physical interactions colliding and not clipping. The problem is when you touch ANYTHING, it has direct motion feedback to your head. Let's say you reach out for a door: when you touch the door your camera is ever so slightly pulled/pushed/wiggled along in direct proportion to your hand's physical interaction with that door; keep in mind your physical head isn't moving, while in game your head is being jerked around. So every time you pick something up your frame of reference/existence is jiggled back and forth. It's the most sickening thing I have ever experienced.

Nothing has ever in my life made me this sick. Rollercoasters, no problem. I've taken martial arts and been flipped around blindfolded, no problem. I've watched a goat get mercy-shot in the head after getting mauled by coyotes and its organs ripped out and still breathing, no problem.
But 20 minutes of Boneworks? You'll be laying on the ground for 30 minutes, too sick to move, wondering what you're doing with your life.

Do a web search for "Boneworks motion sickness", none of the suggestions worked for me but they give you a glimpse at the scale of the issue. At best try it for under 2 hours and return it if you feel sick at any point. I have tried playing this game about 10 times and get sick every.single.time.

P.S. HL-Alyx gave me 0% motion sickness.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I really get into it, let me just say that Boneworks is definitely worth it at $30, even more so if it's on sale, especially compared to a lot of other VR games that are around the same price.

That being said, the game has a lot of problems, some of which have been tweaked, but from my experience the game still suffers from a lot of the same issues. The reason the cons seem so much larger is because I'm trying to include some actual feedback. The pros are why you should buy it, the cons are just the issues I had with it.

Pros:
+ Offers a wide variety of weapons, from knives to rifles, plus a few original weapons.
+ A ton of physics objects. If it looks like you can pick something up and move it, you probably can.
+ Several unique levels that go for quite a while.
+ Open-ended level design with a ton of secrets and replay value.
+ An extra sandbox mode with levels you unlock by finding secrets in the main game.
+ Having a body in VR.
+ Some nice music and overall sound design.
+ The story presents some interesting ideas.

Cons/Critiques:
- Motion. Sickness. I'm pretty well-acquainted with VR and even I was struggling to get through the game at points. It's a bit out there and I'm sure it'd take a lot of work, but I think possibly implementing a locomotion system like Half-Life: Alyx where you always have the option to teleport could work well, though that might be better/more easily implemented in a sequel so levels and enemies can be designed around it.
- A lot of the early environments feel a bit dull and the music in the shooting range especially gets incredibly grating due to how quickly it loops.
- Lack of enemy variety. There's a grand total of about 4 enemies if you exclude the enemies that are just reskins.
- Physics can and will be very janky, so can climbing. It's understandable but in VR it's only gonna make motion sickness worse.
- I wouldn't say the story itself is bad, in fact I found it interesting, just the way the story is presented to you is bad. There's some subtle stuff like writing on walls, but for the most part it's just people telling you what you're doing or literally just sitting in a room watching a cutscene that doesn't make an effort to clarify anything. Chances are, unless you're writing stuff down you probably won't know what's going on for most of your playtime.
- Having a body in VR (tends to make motion sickness worse and your arms and legs frequently get stuck in/on stuff).
- The unlock system for sandbox could be improved quite a bit. I assume this is to encourage replayability, but it teeters on the line between that and just not making it intuitive. Like unlocking weapons by holding onto them until the end of a level is cool, but there's some stuff you unlock by carrying around a useless item for an entire level. I personally think that it would've worked better if the unlocks for enemies and various items were more hidden away but would instantly unlock upon finding them, instead of having to sacrifice a weapon slot or be forced to hold an item throughout an entire level.

I'm sure it looks like I just ripped the game to shreds, but I genuinely love this game and hope that a future patch or sequel makes it even better. If you've got VR, buy it. If you don't, it's worth considering.
