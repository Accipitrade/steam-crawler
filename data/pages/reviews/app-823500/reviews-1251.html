\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI followed the development of this game for a while, so maybe it was over-hyped for me. I did go in with a critical eye as well. I don't spend $30 on a game very often.
Playing on the Rift S I feel the controls aren't intuitive, and difficult to interact with objects. I'm sure the vive index would be much better. Things feel very springy in the game, hardly any of the movements seem natural, making me lose the feeling of immersion. It makes me realize there is a reason why VR players aren't physically tied to an avatar.
There is no saving in the game, which is annoying for people like me who don't get to play hours in one sitting. I reached nearly the end of the Museum, but had to quit. So I had to redo the entire 20+ minutes to get back to where I was.
The loading screens are very long. Long to the point where I think it crashes. I ended up having to place the headset down and do something else until it loaded.
I also meet the requirements with a 1060 and i5 7400, but it struggled to provide smooth frame-rate.
I am sadly disappointed in this game.\t\t\t\t\t
