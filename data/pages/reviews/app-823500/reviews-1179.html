\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTakes VR interaction to the place we all hoped it would go in 2016. They've done it, it's here. It's genuinely disconcerting feeling like you're IN a virtual world with a presence instead of just being hands floating around a disjointed head like most other VR titles, it does almost every single interaction we were all disappointed didn't work in early VR titles so, so well. The OG Vive and wands are still perfect for playing, though the Index experience must be lots easier and more natural.

The tutorial feels long as per the other reviewers but phases so smoothly into the real game you don't really notice it end. I agree with that other guy though, this game definitely needs mid-level saves. It took me 1h 30m to get through one of the first proper stages and you can't quit otherwise you're back at the start of the level and they are _big_ levels. If you die, back to the start! The only saving grace is the enemies stay dead and you keep your items, so you just have to traverse the way back to where you were if you die.

I love the climbing mechanics, as a RL climber it almost feels natural to me and the way the game handles your weight hanging off singular grab points is very nice, even climbing objects you can't grab is fully possible as long as you use your arms to lever properly and haul yourself up properly and use the crouch and d-pad movement to shift your legs over the edge, it's way too much fun to climb up structures and shimmy across girders!

Haven't seen too much of the story yet even after 3 hours but I've been enjoying really poking around in the engine and taking a look at every alternate path and little hidey hole and I have been having a lot of fun with it.

Boneworks has rekindled my enjoyment of VR after my HMD has sat gathering dust for well over a year which is a miraculous feat in itself. I was cautious at first but it really does showcase the almost full potential of what's possible in VR and it feels very good and natural to interact with the world. Heartily recommend.

"The stealth Half-Life: VR we didn't know we needed." - Me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow. Just wow...
This is the first fully fledged VR game I have played. I've played Skyrim VR, Fallout VR, Minecraft VR, and a couple of other games ported to VR. As you can expect, their VR support, regardless of the quality, pales in comparison to a game made from the ground up for VR.

Pros
-Runs smooth on my non-oc'd Ryzen 5 1600 3.2gz. 100% utilization in plenty of areas, but never got choppy.
-Looks great. The art style is fitting for the proposed setting. I have a 1070 and was stunned with the draw distances and clarity in some areas.
-The physics are IMPRESSIVE. Catching falling objects and having them jerk my digital body down with them, or pressing my waist to a table to reach something far away was a pleasant surprise. Only one physics-based puzzle felt flawed, but I'll get to that in cons.
-Decent gunplay. If this was primarily a shooter, it would pale in the face of games like Pavlov or Onward, but with the entire package considered? Fuggedabout it.
-Story took a little too long to get going, but when it did it served its purpose. It's no Valve game, but the environmental story telling was top notch.
-Much more, but I don't want to keep gushing...

Cons
-One puzzle was poopoo. I won't spoil anything, but it involved carrying heavy objects over planks, and said heavy objects had an annoying tendency to freak out when carried for too long. Tedious and poorly made, especially for so late in the game.
-The ending was meh. I liked the implications it left the player, but I was relatively dissatisfied with it. Hopefully it is simply setting up future stories.

Random thoughts
Played a little under 12 hours to complete the story. I def missed plenty of easter eggs and hidden areas. I know this because I found so many, and I usually don't have an eagle eye when it comes to those types of things.
I played on the Oculus Rift CV1. The controllers have been amazing, and this game's utilization of them left little to be desired. No interactions felt lacking simply because I couldn't use my last three digits individually. Except for maybe flipping an enemy or two off.
This is definitely worth the full price. I haven't even messed around in the arena or sandbox modes. VR owners need to vote with their wallets from now on. No more paying top dollar for lacking ports of old AAA games, we need to support games made for VR by devs that are bettering VR.

This game is amazing, and I can't wait to get back into it.\t\t\t\t\t

If like me, you have been watching this game since it first burst onto the scene over a year ago, then you will have had high hopes. I honestly cannot fault what I have seen so far. My only gripe is that with a Rift S, a GTX1080 and an i7 5820k 6-core processor, I struggle to get this game to run without stuttering, even with settings set to low and adaptive resolution switched on!
