\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can recommend Boneworks with a few caveats. If you only have moderately good VR legs (like me), then yes you will be getting sick. I still think the game is worth it though. There is a certain ladder in this game that is the most difficult boss battle I have ever faced (you will know it when you see it).

The good: the gameplay, atmosphere, puzzles, inventory management, and traversal all come together very nicely. Graphics are top notch, and there are only a few fps slowdowns in the whole game.
The bad: Climbing could have used a redesign. Allowing your viewport to be bounced around by physics objects in game is ok for normal gameplay; however, for climbing it just doesn’t work. I don’t know what the workaround would have been, maybe decoupling from the physics system while climbing could have been a good compromise? Thankfully there actually isn’t that much climbing in the game. If you can make it through the tutorial section, you should be fine.

The Excellent: We’ve all seen these mechanics in VR before; however, no one has really brought the positional inventory, IK body, and physics-based interactions together into a full game before. Very nice to see an independent Dev pull it off first 😊

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fantastic, the campaign itself takes at least 12 hours to complete, and it's simply amazing, the combat, exploration possibilities and ways to solve puzzles or climb stuff make you extremely immersed in the world and the weight and general physic engine of the game is probably the best aspect of this game, allowing you to use everything as a weapon with enough force behind the movements you do. This game is what a half life game in VR should be, this puts a really high bar on what Half Life Alyx will have to present with itself to compete with this game.

The only weak aspects of this game of which i could think of, is probably the story itself and lack of creative puzzles - there is a few here and there, but looking at how many things you can do with the physics in this game I expected a bit more of that.

Really recommending the game for what it is right now, hoping for new content maybe hitting the game down the line or seeing this engine being used in another game by this studio, if you ever want to show off VR to someone this game is something YOU MUST show to people when introducing them to VR\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou can find all the positives about this game in a thousand other places, so I'll just serve you up the negatives here:

-Loading times. 5, even closer to 10 minute loading times. This is insane. We're not rendering a black hole here.
-Your physics-based body. This blessing is also a curse. It leads to parts of your body getting stuck in things (heh), disorientation even for someone like me who's had their "VR Legs" for a while now, and weapon interactions that are not only goofy-looking but frustrating as well. Good luck trying to aim as well as you can in H3VR or Pavlov.
-Lack of autosaving. Each level or "scene" takes a good while to complete, maybe around 40-ish minutes on a blind run. If you have to stop playing at all in that time, be prepared to slog through from the beginning of that scene.

That's all I can think of for now. I'd say get this on sale, but it's not really worth full price. I'm still giving a "don't recommend" score because I don't think it deserves its current "Very Positive" score.

EDIT: From the comments, installing this on an SDD might help load times.\t\t\t\t\t
