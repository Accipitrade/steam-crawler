\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLived up to the hype for me been waiting a good while and here it is the ultimate VR physics experience. This is easily the best VR game on the market right now until Half-Life Alyx (maybe?).
Good -
- Mostly everything can be interacted with
- PHYSICS
- Combat is great
- Fun puzzles (maybe just because it's VR but to me they're really fun)
- Loads of secrets
- Huge levels

Bad -
- Arms can glitch out and get stuck on objects. (Can be avoided by holding hands somewhere in view)
- NO SAVES! You have to complete the entire level if you want to save, no mid game saving at all. (30 - 60 min levels make this just that much worse)
- Controls are all fine except gripping, there is no way to toggle grip so you have to hold trigger and the grab button which feels terrible. Hurts your hands after a while especially when you are in a fire fight and have to hold those buttons for long periods of time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly Recommend it. The gameplay is amazing and I am really big fan of the mechanics such as the weapons. The environment really suits the music and the atmosphere that the scenes have to offer. The puzzles are fun to solve and the climbing mechanic + the physics makes BONEWORKS really standout from most other adventure/shooter/climbing VR games. The feeling of being in a strange unknown world gave me a sense of wanting explore my suroundings and also made me feel like a bad ass in more action packed moments. After I knew all my mechanics all sorts of questions came into my mind: can I climb this? Can I open that? Can I do this and that and so on when I was progressing through the story. Being able to use the physics to interact with your world really makes it interesting and fresh.

After finishing the game I was really exited about the Sandbox mode that I unlocked but here comes a bit of a let down. When I entered the Sandbox mode I was hoping for a mode where you can play around with everything preunlocked: All weapons, All enemys and especially big structures like bridges and so on to play with for as an example making cool slow mo scenes or battles. Sadly there was only the stuff that you have unlocked through throwing weapons into the bin. Which sounds cool in the beginning but kinda ruins the meaning of a sandbox mode and also my fun I wish would've had with it. No Music, No unlimited ammo and sort of a blant environment filled with a small space to play with some sort of foam pieces which are indeed climbable but fall over the second you climb or stand on it.

(UPDATE: SLVLZ has announced about 1 month ago that they will be adding a lot of stuff that was requested by people such as - some also mentioned in this review - New Sandbox environments, the test chambers from the trailers, new guns and meele weapons, and more. For more information you can read the Post Launch Update announcement. This will change my opinion about the Sandbox mode that I talked about above to a more positive change though I'll have to wait and see it for myself after the updates are released.)
- Written on 01 / 22 / 2020

But all of that shouldn't fear anybody away from what the story mode has to offer. Infact I would go as far as saying that BONEWORKS is the best VR singleplayer titel as of the time of making this review. So if you are looking for a really good singleplayer VR game that sucks you into their world then BONEWORKS is your awnser. Oh and the OST's are a masterpiece especially if you are into synthwave.
My overall rating for BONEWORKS: 9,5/10\t\t\t\t\t
