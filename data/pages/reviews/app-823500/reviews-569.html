\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic VR experience!

I don't have a super expensive gaming rig; I used an RX 570 to play this game and performance was good, only getting noticeable frame drops near the 3rd half of the game. I played through on an Rift S, and the controls were great, and was over all very enjoyable:

Mechanically, I don't think this game gets enough credit; Only taking up 10 GB of storage, while having a clean and slick graphics design, while running smoothly on my computer is technically impressive, well done there! The in-depth physics system is also very impressive, with simulated weight and mass in every object, everything can be touched, grabbed or held by your in-game body and hands, with not a single object feeling 'unrealistic' in the sense that is is either too heavy or too light. Clearly a lot of work has gone into the interactivity of this game, and it definitely shows here.

Gameplay is admittedly straightforward; Explore each map from point A to point B while collecting materials, in this case weapons and ammo, while trying to get to your destination which is 'Time tower'. While this doesn't sounds very intuitive, it's not really, but it is a first for VR, and the plot is actually interesting if you play through a few times as I did. The combat is very satisfying and really fun, but I heavily recommend that you use the environment to your advantage, not just using weapons like guns. Use stuff like office chairs, computer monitors, etc, and it makes the combat really fun to mess around with.

The 'expert VR users only' tag is not lying; If you get motion sick in any way, or are not comfortable with climbing, full locomotion, and the like, this will make you very sick, so be aware.

Sound design is also great! Especially the music, which sets the tone for the whole game with upbeat synth beats. As well as good music, there is also an eerie atmosphere in this game that I can't shake, especially in the first few levels like the Streets, with a City 17 like noise echoing through the city space, knowing your the only 'human' thing there, similar to joining an empty multiplayer lobby.

This game also has a surprising amount of end game content and replay value; With TONS of collectables and secrets to find, as well as fun little mini-games to unlock, like zombie mode, and sandbox.

I cannot stress (no pun intended) enough how awesome this game is for a VR only title. I'm excited to see what Stress Level Zero is going to put out next with this innovative physics system that is used here, and if you have a VR headset, and are comfortable with full locomotion, buy this game!\t\t\t\t\t
