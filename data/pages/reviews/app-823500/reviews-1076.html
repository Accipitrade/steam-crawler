I've been waiting for Boneworks for a LONG time! As release date got closer, I began to get skeptical. I have a Windows Mixed Reality headset (Lenovo Explorer) and with the limitations the headset presented I was afraid of whether I'd enjoy the game or not.

Unfortunately with the limitations of WMR, it wasn't an experience I entirely enjoyed. The gun-play was fantastic, the graphics were great, but the climbing and physics crippled the game on WMR. I wanted to give the game a fair shot, but with the hardware I had, i can't recommend it.

I'm assuming the game works well on other VR headsets and that's fantastic! I hope others have a blast with the game, but until I can justify purchasing another VR headset, I'll watch from the sidelines.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ guys we're here.

If you have a VR system, and are accustom to motion without motion, this is an absolute must get. This is the first VR experience that has the polished feel of a typical single-player story game. Nothing else has come close so far. I had so much fun in just the tutorial of the game, I spent a good 3-4 hours throwing boxes at each other before I started to progress the story.

I had the privilege of working on the HTC Vive before the commercial version was fully developed, and I remember trying to get the physics in engine, to match what was happening in real life. It was next to impossible. Since then, I've watched games develop, from mostly things like job simulator, or Accounting VR, where moving stuff around was the main mechanic, to games like Onward, or Superhot. They were all amazing, but none so far have gotten the physics of the player's physical body over to the game in quite this way.

They've done it. This is the game you fantasized about since video games became a thing.

This is Real Life - The Video Game.\t\t\t\t\t
