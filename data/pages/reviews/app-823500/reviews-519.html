\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the time of writing this i have only played this masterpiece for almost 7 hours, and i am already 100% sure that this is the most mind blowing and entertaining VR experience you could ever get your hands on.

Everything you do in this game has such good feedback, you can sort of feel the weight of light and heavy objects, when you hit something with your fists or a melee weapon you'll feel that you hit depending on how much force you exert, when you pull the trigger on a gun, the gun would have a very realistic kick pushing you hands backward, mix that with a slight vibration in the controllers and outstanding audio and you got yourself the most realistic gun you could ever fire in a VR game.

The audio is also very good, not just for the guns but for also the impact or stabbing with melee weapons. The music and ambiance is god but its nothing like the dragon born theme from skyrim that would make you spend 3 minutes in the menu just to listen to the entire song.

The story is good . . . for a tech demo. If you play it normally it'll take you about 15 hours, if you play it and look in every nook and cranny for collectibles, ammo, and secrets then it'll last about 20 hours.

Bugs? Almost none. Very few times, your arm is gonna get stuck on a pipe, or if you hit something too hard it might clip through thin doors, but thats it. And these kind of things rarely happen.

Play time? This is a sandbox with next level physics, as long as you own this game and you are still interested you could have 100's on 100's of hours if you'd like.

Over all, great game, 10/10, knowing and playing such a game as this excites me for what the future of VR has in store for us.

PS: I got all the information of play time and storyline length from other community reviews, so if you were wondering how a guy with 7 hours of playtime knows how long the story and over all play time is, there's your answer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVR never has been and most likely never will be this good ever again without Stress Level 0 and their amazing team. It's The Half Life of VR, More Half Life than The actual VR Half Life game and its 30 Dollars less. Its innovative, Entertaining, and in strange ways beautiful. I would give up all my progress in the game just to feel what it was like playing through it for the first time again.

Amazing gameplay heavily focused on your environment. The world around you is your weapon, Things like a trash can will be surprisingly useful if you get creative.

Open level design that lets you choose your way around every situation. Take the front door or sneak in through a window? Maybe just jump the fence and bypass the entire house? Its up to you!

Engaging soundtrack that immerses you and has a firm identity that fits with the game. (The lyrics of the song "Opera De Ossium" Foreshadow the events of the game in latin! how neat!)

Hours of free post game content to keep you in the game and interested, really use every cent of the 30 Dollar price tag.

A surprisingly active modding Community that has even more hours of fan made content waiting to be tested! The forum website and discord server the "Bone Tome" Is a great place to get started!

Overall the Game is a solid 9/10. Sometimes the physics are a little buggy but it is an absolute Must Buy for VR, its engaging and creative, has a strong identity, and is rumored to be getting a sequel so catch up! With the release of the oculus quest 2 VR has never been easier to get straight into! So get to playing! I promise it delivers.\t\t\t\t\t
