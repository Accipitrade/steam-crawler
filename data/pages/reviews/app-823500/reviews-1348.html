\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTruly a next gen VR game. Pushes the boundaries in every way and is the most immersive VR game I've played to date.

Physics is awesome in any game, but doubly awesome when you're actually part of it. Guns are beautiful and slowmo is a real treat. Spent a couple hours just exploring the tutorial museum and just got into the game itself.

Regarding motion sickness: You will get it if this is the first VR game you've played. You will get it if you have not played smooth locomotion games before. That's not the fault of the devs, that's the human body telling you something's wrong. You can get over it the same way you get over car sickness or sea sickness - keep trying (with MANY breaks!!) and don't let yourself get overly sick. Took me about a week to get over it (a year ago), I have no problems with boneworks locomotion.

Music: Holy hell this is a good soundtrack.
Story: Interesting plot, not far enough along to say whether it holds up or lasts a long time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOriginal Review 2 hours in.

There is certainly something here. I'm gonna focus more on my negatives than just reiterating all the positives others mention.

The game starts really slow focusing more on teaching you the fundamentals and controls which is nice but it could really of used some funny dialog or an NPC babysitter of some sort to comment on what you are doing. You are just on your own and it feels a bit lonely and void of life during the tutorial.

To me the overall vibe seems like VR portal, but without the portals, and without the campy charm of portal.

So far the two major weaknesses is that the story is a bit lacking, it comes off more creepy fun house than funny like a portal game would be. Secondly the sounds are really not satisfying. Smacking anything with a item is wonderful because of the physics reaction and the vibration of the controllers but its all off put because most of the sounds are just all dead and flat.

Pros
- physics playground, controls are well made
- seems to be multiple ways to solve problems, (possible spoiler) example, At one point I used a garbage can lid as a shield to approach a turret when the markings seemed to indicate i was supposed to climb a wall to get around it.

Cons
-sound design not where I would like it to be
-story lacking so far

Most of the issues can be forgiven for a small studio and small price point of entry, Highly recommend you give it a shot.



Update - few more hours in

- Story has picked up a little, but still super minor, totally irrelevant to how fun the game is though
-The game got a lot creepier, I'm a big puss (absolutely hate horror) and can handle it, there hasn't been any intentional jump scares which I appreciate, though in VR anything is a possible jump scare
-Getting used to the controls has really elevated the fun I am having, you really have to practice getting used to your arms and hands clipping on things and have to move more realistically and its very rewarding.

Highlights of my game play (possible spoilers)

- Putting a traffic cone on a bad guys head.
- Getting jumped by a bunch of head crabs and barricading myself in an office in order to escape.
- I got into a wrestling match with a bad guy and ended up sodomizing the poor guy, very funny once I realised what position I had him in. I was trying to body slam him due to lack of weapons at the time.
- I actually used a crowbar and busted a lock using it as a crowbar, was very cool and I didn't think it was going to work at all.


The good
-Seated mode is great and fully allows you to play the game without fear of falling over or extreme motion sickness issues. (I have had very few instances of motion sickness with VR but I have fallen over once or twice)

The bad
- Sound design is still really disappointing
- I very much dislike the look of the bad guys so far. The guys with guns are robots inside of projected holograms which makes hitting them a bit of a pain especially in a medium where aiming can already be kind of difficult especially over distances.

The ugly
- No saving is a pretty big one, VR especially a game like this often requires shorter sessions and not being able to fully progress through the level and having to redo certain puzzles is very frustrating.


Minor tweaks this game could use
- The ammo pouch is located terribly at times, I would almost prefer it to be on my butt so I don't accidentally grab ammo instead of my left holster. Really an option to move it around would be amazing. In seated mode it cab be almost impossible to grab at time maybe I need to play around with my height settings more and its just a problem I am having.

-Dismemberment would be a very good idea for enemies. Kind of like Robo Recall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is revolutionary for VR and honestly its meta story that not many people know about is just as amazing. This game has the puzzles like portal, the style of half life, and the comedy of portal 2. The game is full of secrets and replay-ability and I freaking love that this game actually has a campaign longer than 3-4 hours like most VR games these days.

This game does come with its fair share of issues though. As nice as the kinematic body is the are times where my hands and my body get all janky with other objects when you are climbing or grabbing large objects. At least thats what happened to me. Also there are huge load times that are 5-7 minutes between levels.

Besides some physics problems and load times this game is amazing and I congratulate Stress Level Zero for their game that they have been working on for almost 3 years. I'm excited to learn more about this meta story as I play on and I hope when modding becomes a thing this game can become the next VR Garry's mod.

If you guys want to know what meta story this game has, just look up the connection it has with Stress Level Zero's game Duck Season and their hidden secrets. This story is way bigger than you think.

Also I miss Clipee the clipboard and I hope to see him again.\t\t\t\t\t

This is by far one of the most immersive vr games you can play. The launch was a little rocky from the WMR problems but the patch the team released only 13 hours after launch works perfectly. Just wish there was maybe a check point system for the campaign; this can be problematic if you need to take a break when you use a WMR headset as it will go to sleep, closing the game as well, after 15 minutes. Guess I got some good VR legs seeing as a lot of people are complaining about it. If you're finding that you are feeling sick, I recommend also getting Jet Island.
