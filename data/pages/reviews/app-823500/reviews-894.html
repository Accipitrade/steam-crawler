\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is with sadness that I leave this negative review. Boneworks has, hands down, the most immersive VR controls and world interactions of any game on the market. Yes, they can be janky, but you learn to deal with its eccentricities.
Unfortunately, the incredible movement, gunplay, and interaction mechanics are paired with a campaign that actively punishes you for wanting to enjoy those mechanics.

The 6-8ish hour campaign has a handful of cool physics puzzles, a few really memorable combat encounters, and some interesting set pieces for you to maneuver through and explore.

However. The devs don't want you to just have fun with this. They want you to avoid as much of it as possible. You see, the ammunition you're feeding into your weapons to mow down the enemy? That's also your currency. There is a finite amount across the entire game, and you need 90% of it (!) to unlock the best items for the sandbox mode, one of the biggest draws of the game. I'm not talking fun skins or easter eggs, I'm talking ♥♥♥♥ that's in the launch trailer that you can't earn any other way.

How do you get enough ammo/currency to unlock everything?

1. Don't buy ANY items during the campaign (no matter how fun they look)
2. Shoot absolutely as few bullets as possible (you can only expend a handful of clips over the entire campaign before damning yourself to too low of a score)
3. Crawl through every nook, corner, destructable object, etc. in every single level to Hoover up as much ammo as physically possible. These are not fun hidden zones, they're just random closets and storage rooms that are sometimes difficult to reach.

That's the game they actually want you to play. Don't shoot things, don't move through the levels in a smooth, brisk pace, crawl around in random corners of the map picking ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ up. You know, like chores. Oh, also, while you're doing this, look for capsule and objects you haven't seen before, which you must clog up your very small inventory with until you can find a drop off point that unlocks them in sandbox mode.

This process can take hours per level if you want to be thorough, and if you miss one ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ thing, you have to do the whole level over, because your progress is based on your best run of the level. In other words, if you find a piece of ammo the first time, but miss it in your second run, you have to do it again to re-earn that ammo. Oh, and while all of this is happening you're being shot at by hitscan enemies who you cannot return fire on, because, you know, the ammo is money.

Why am I so salty about a mechanic designed to encourage extra replay value?
1. Using incredible VR shooting and parkour mechanics to squat around supply closets on a stupid easter egg hunt is not what I paid for. I paid to do the cool ♥♥♥♥ in the trailer.
2. If I don't do this, I don't get the very best items to have fun with in sandbox mode, you know, the thing where you can use all your shooting and parkour mechanics.
3. Let me re-iterate, there are very meaningful gameplay mechanics (the suction cups, the gravity gun) that are locked behind the scavenger hunt mechanics. So I have to do this ♥♥♥♥ or cheat to experience the parts of the game I actually want to play.

In short, this is a VR FPS that punishes you for shooting and an exploration game that wants you to spend the lion's share of your time in the least interesting parts of the game world. Can you bypass this by downloading a save? Absolutely. But let's be clear, that's asking me to download a file that let's me skip the game.

I hope Stress Level Zero will find a better way to do unlockables in their future projects, because somewhere buried in Boneworks is a great game. Unfortunately, they just really ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hate you playing it.
