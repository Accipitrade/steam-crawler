\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I think this is my favorite first person shooter, even compared to any non vr games, nothing else really comes close in terms of ways you can approach the game, besides something like prey or other immersive sims, and even then vr gives you so much more freedom that even with just the pretty simple mechanics that boneworks has, you can just go crazy with what you want to do. I have a lot of issues with this game, the ammo system is sort of messed up, you never really run out of rifle ammo and rifles really just make the game super boring in my opinion, all you have to do is stand back and shoot stuff until it dies, and you never get any of the super cool moments that you can only achieve by limiting how powerful the player is, I've been playing the warehouse encounter with all the headcrabs over and over and over again with just a pistol and a knife playing it like I was solid snake or something and its really been some of the most fun I've had in a video game, there are pretty much endless ways to approach that encounter, and just using a rifle the whole game just makes it really boring, there is a mod that sort of acts as a hard mode where, with a bunch of other options, you can set your ammo level to zero at the beginning of a level and I highly recommend that, at least to me it seems like they were somewhat designed to be played individually where you pretty much start over with nothing at the beginning of one, but having the stores at the beginning of a level sort of doesn't make sense with that thinking and obviously I didn't make the game so I have no idea what I'm talking about lol. Another issue I have is with the enemy variety, there are pretty much only like three enemies, the crablets, nullmen or zombies, and the omni projectors, and even then i dont find the omni projectors that fun to fight because you can't really throw them around or physically interact with them like with the nullmen or crablets. Another problem I have is with the story, I know a bunch of the hidden lore and the connections between this game and duck season and everything and all of that is really really cool, I love when games try to have more complicated and crazy stories, but my issue is that the base story of boneworks is completely incomprehensible, there is pretty much no story if you don't do any digging or watch an hour long youtube video to try to figure out what the hell is going on, which isn't actually that big of an issue because the gameplay is just so ridiculously fun and there are only like three cutscenes, but I love stories in games and how uniquely they can tell them but when I first played this game I was just completely confused at everything that happened. That being said, even though I have a lot of problems with this game, the commitment the game has to freedom and player expression through the gameplay is incredible, even a game from a gigantic company like half life alyx or games like medal of honor, skyrim or fallout 4 vr just completely fall flat on their face in terms of the way that they harness vr's greatest strengths like boneworks does. I thought half life alyx in the gameplay department was a big disappointment, you are never really going to see crazy clips of people playing half life alyx because all you can do is shoot stuff, there isn't any melee or grabbing enemies or anything, and while I love half life alyx, boneworks is just on a completely different level when it comes to the core gameplay and what you can do with the insane sandbox on offer here. It is also crazy to me that stress level zero has put out a bunch of great updates for this game, I put like twenty hours into the hover junkers map alone back when that came out and had in incredible amount of fun trying to recreate a bunch of the crazy shots from the trailer, which whoever recorded made it look crazy easy to set up all the insane ♥♥♥♥ they pulled off in that trailer. I am incredibly excited for any further updates to boneworks if they still have any planned, but honestly I'd rather them just keep working on whatever crazy new game they have in the works, all of the node videos where they showed off boneworks were one of the main things that convinced me to get a vr headset, and I'm sure I'm going to get super hyped whenever and however they show off their new game. Boneworks may not be the best way to showcase vr to a new player, but it is the best way to showcase what vr can bring to video games, and shows how independent game developers can triumph over larger companies and create a game that others with giant budgets and giant teams can't even hold a candle to.


Also, please make like a merch store or something where I can support these developers more lol\t\t\t\t\t
