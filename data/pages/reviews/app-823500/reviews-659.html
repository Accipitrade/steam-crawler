\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA bit of a mixed bag: some genuinely cool puzzles, mixed in with great gun play and some clever mechanics, like climbing, which take the fully simulated body into account. But ultimately let down by ugly art direction, rubbish story, and bland level design which add up to make it a pretty unappealing virtual world to spend time in.
I would also suggest you need to be a well seasoned VR player to tolerate the motion in this - especially anything involving the constant swaying induced by
climbing or platforming (of which there is a lot).

The simulated IK body is both what makes this game unique, and what ruins it. The climbing is great fun to begin with (the sense of freedom to explore is unparalleled in any other game) but it's over-used and soon wears thin.
They did a great job of simulating weight/density of objects in the game world and conveying that through how easily you manipulate them. But at the same time, it makes certain parts of the game so painful to play: if there's a gap in the floor somewhere, and your foot goes through it, you're going with it. You'll get stuck in the floor and have to try and pull yourself back out with your arms. ALL of the platforming elements suffer from this, making those parts of the game a complete chore.
Your body feels like it is made entirely of compressed bungee cords - move your arms too fast while climbing, and you'll be bouncing back and forth, up and down for ages.

A lot of thought, clever ideas, and hard work went into making this game. But for some reason, it doesn't click.

If you enjoy physics sandboxes in VR you will get a kick out of trying this, but other than that I don't think I'd recommend this.\t\t\t\t\t
