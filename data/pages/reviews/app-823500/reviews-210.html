\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is absolutely incredible. Not one of my favorite experiences ever or anything but easily worth recommending. It's a game that like Half Life Alyx proves VR isn't a gimmick. But I'm getting somewhat ahead of myself

It's funny I bring up Half Life because Boneworks unapologeticly wears the Half Life influence on its sleeve particularly Half Life 1. Mongon soft is basically Black Mesa, the game takes place throughout a facility, like Half Life 1, the protagonist is silent (aside from a few grunts) serving the exact same purpose Gordon Freeman served in Half Life 1. The game tells its story through gameplay and subtext, like Half Life 1. There's a lot of parallels you can draw between Half Life 1 and this game. That's not a bad thing at all though, because Boneworks is not some mere Half Life 1 but in VR clone. It brings plenty of its own ideas to the table. I really like it, while Half Life 1 isn't like a game I super like or anything it's one I got my fun out of and respect a lot so Boneworks being what it is makes it cool.

Similar to Half Life Alyx I adore the combat in this game. On the one hand it's better than Half Life Alyx because you get so many weapons to toy around with from Melees, Assault Rifles, SMGs, shotguns, and so much more. But on the other I find Half Life Alyx's combat to be much more polished on the whole compared to Boneworks. I found Melee's really fun but also just finicky enough as to get me into slightly annoying situations sometimes. Aiming guns was fine on the whole but I had an easier time aiming in Half Life Alyx than I did in Boneworks. I'm not exactly sure why, maybe I'd have to chock it up to HLA on the whole having a more polished physics system than Boneworks. Which to get it out of the way that is the one major flaw holding Boneworks back for me, the physics can be really frustrating to deal with sometimes. Most of the time they work well and I have no issues, but the 2 areas that frustrate me the most are moving heavy objects and climbing. Climbing in particular is dreadful unless it's one of those walls specifically made for climbing. I'd often find my hand or arm getting caught and I'd be stuck flailing about trying to fix it and it actually made me sick on one occasion. Moving heavy objects is annoying because It's tough to get to react the exact way I want it to react but I can at least cheese it somewhat by just hanging onto said object and moving backwards to get it where I need it. Other than that though Boneworks is absolutely fantastic. The levels are just the right length, they all have memorable set-pieces, the whole game is full of great environmental storytelling that makes you question what's going on. I'll reiterate that I like the variety of weapons, the music whenever it plays is always fantastic. The game gives you a great sense of freedom to tackle obstacles. Boneworks is just an all around fantastic game. I still ultimately prefer Half Life Alyx to this game but Half Life Alyx got me to go back to this game and re-evaluate it in the first place and I had a blast playing this game. If you're a VR user but somehow haven't played it yet get on it because this is easily worth recommending. If you're like me, you've played Half Life Alyx and are curious to check out Boneworks, do it. I don't think you'll be disappointed\t\t\t\t\t
