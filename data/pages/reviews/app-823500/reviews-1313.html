SLZ you guys did an amazing job with this, VR just got its first flagship title. 20/10 would physics again!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven though I had to wait a day to get supported controls for WMR, it was absolutely worth the wait. Of course, in reality and idealistically this should've been working the day it released (which was when I bought it), but I don't ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ care now. At least it works and by God is this game a beauty.

Why do I recommend this game after the devs pretty much made WMR support an after thought?

Well it's for the same reason as everyone else, it's for the crazy level of interactability that no other VR game has achieved on this level.

I'm amazed at how this game runs on my specs:
CPU: i5-3550S @ 3.0GH/z
GPU: Nvdia GTX 1050Ti (4GB)
Ram: 16GB DDR3 Ram
OS: Windows 10 (Duh or else I couldn't use WMR)

While it does stutter a little and it looks somewhat jaggedy (sometimes) I can live with it. This game is totally playable for me.

*Something important to note about the "streets" level in particular in terms of loading it. If you find that when you attempt to load the "Streets" level and that you always crash. According to one of the developers named "CFish" if you do not have a large enough page file in windows (or if you do not have a page file at all) the streets level will always crash upon loading. If you have an SSD and purposely disabled the pagefile or set it to a low value, all you have to do is create a page file on another drive that it is not your SSD. If you want to know how to do that, google is your friend.*

This game does crash a bit for me (so far it crashed a grand total of 20+ times). All the crashing aside, the game is still absolutely fantastic. I think part of the crashing was because of the small page file I had on my SSD.

You might say that your character has "noodley" arms, but once you get used to it and the controls, it feels pretty natural. I get stupidly lost in the immersion of this game and I legitimately lose track of time. (When the game and tracking are both not failing on me).

So far:
The laggyest level is "Tower" due to how much has to be rendered at once within that level by the GPU
The scariest level is tied between the "Sewers" level and "Central Station"
The shortest level is "Dungeon"
The longest level is the streets level which took me 5 hours to complete.
The safest level is also the "streets" level, if you don't count the "museum", the "break room" or the "dungeon".
The most frustrating level was the time tower.
The coolest level was the museum because of how beautifully the game demonstrates its mechanics to the player.

Also for those who claim to get motion sickness in this game, I think are people who have not played enough VR games. I personally feel no motion sickness at all when playing this game. Aircar (which is a free to play VR flying car simulator on steam) was waaaaaaaaaay worse in terms of motion sickness, whereas this game caused no motion sickness for me at all.

This game probably feels the most authentic with the $1.4k Valve index, but at a $200 WMR headset, this game is just as good and for me I think it was $33 well spent.

Also the game recommends to play it sitting down, but I find that playing it standing is much more immersive. If I wanted to play this game sitting down, I wouldn't be playing a VR game.

Overall, 10/10 would yell: "SUCK BRICK KID!" again.
____________________________
Post-campaign thoughts...

Finally after 33 hours, I completed the game's campaign and it was a really fun one at that. It had its cool, fun, frustrating and scary moments, but again, for $33 this is an amazing VR title and I'm still not done with it, because I want to explore different areas of the maps using the physics tools while also unlocking those in sandbox.

I will avoid the arena mode though, because I hate the crablets... I avoided them at every chance possible in the game. Luckily though, they added a brawl mode, which allows you to disable certain enemies as you wish, so now I will be able to play arena mode crablet free. Woo!\t\t\t\t\t
