\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks was the first ever VR game that I played. Since editing this review, I've played dozens of other VR games, but Boneworks still manages to hold a special place in my heart. Yes, I've played games that I enjoyed more, games that had better gunplay, games that had a better singleplayer, etc. but Boneworks is still in my top 5. It may be because of some bias that I have since this was my first ever VR experience, but the mechanics in this game were just extremely fun to use.

  The story in Boneworks is negligible to the point where there seems to be none. This honestly isn't a con for me since the singleplayer makes up for it with entertaining but not frustrating puzzles. Boneworks is also one of the few games with a sandbox mode that I actually enjoyed. Usually, sandboxes bore me since I have to use my own imagination (which is virtually non-existent), but Bonework's sandbox somehow works in the game's favour. The enemies in this game are a blast to fight against since they're vastly different; from the Headsets (which are highly inspired by Half Life's Headcrabs) to the rudimentary Fords, fighting these enemies makes for a great experience. Killing said enemies in this game also awakened some sort of primal instinct in me and made my brain go into 'kill mode'. Only after murdering a man will my actions finally hit me with a feeling comparable to post-nut clarity. Finally, a game that made me feel remorse for murdering something, 8/10.
TL;DR VR Game That Gives You A Feeling Similar To Post Nut Clarity After Murdering A Man, 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
