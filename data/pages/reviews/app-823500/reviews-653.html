\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor being a physics based sandbox story in VR, I'd have to say it was money well earned.
Use caution as BONEWORKS is repeatedly told that it's more active in terms of movement, so people who get very VR-sick (is that what it's called?) may have to take short sessions if they choose to buy.

With the player playing as the main character, Arthur Ford, you plunge within MythOS looking for something, even if the only acquaintances that follow you want to shoot or slap you silly. or is there? the VR world in this universe isn't exactly powered off of the acres and acres of VHS tapes. it's like taking a bath in the universe's antimatter.

The story doesn't hamfist you with boring dialogue or context clues that stop you in your tracks. If you find the cutscenes boring, there's a skip button to get back in. In-game dialogue consists of combat lines, but some story scenes are interactive. That means you could blaze through the gate without caring about what's happening, or you can sit there and watch your coworkers say stuff about how wack the fictional VR world is going bonkers. Or, just smash the TV with a brick you found off the street. BONEWORKS doesn't mind.

And if you do mind the story, then Stress Level Zero has this funky cross-reference universe they have in their games. Not that they tell you that, it's up to you to snoop around and connect the dots.

Combat is fun, yet barebones in some types of places. There's 7 gun bases, with 4 of those having certain premade modifications added to it. A fun mechanic is the bullets-as-money system, where every shop within the game runs on your bullets. Will you choose to run your bullets dry in a haze of brass and lead? Or sneak around with a crowbar to conserve precious ammo? Heck, punch everything, be creative in how you solve problems!

After the story is done, collectables, arena, and sandboxes are there to poke out any extra things you could tinker with. Poke around story levels to find nice items that reward players with looking at every nook and cranny, run for your life against zombies that can climb, or claim your place in the arena with trials and challenges. If you did get them, you can just go creative or bonkers with unlocked physics items/toys in the sandbox.

After you enjoy your time collecting everything, neutralizing everything, messing everything up, it's kinda finished. For the ultra bored, a data wipe is available to start clean and collect-em-all. For the patient, new modules that have extra content might be released by the devs over at SLZ. Or if you're really desperate for some BONEWORKS content, there are some mods out there...

Overall, this game is great for getting your feet wet within VR. One can say it's a tech demo if they want to, and I'm inclined to agree. I can't imagine developing a VR game with all these physics interactions and paths, nevertheless creating a VR shooter that slowly and slowly turns into a tin can plinking simulator.

I may have spent too much time plugging into boneworks.\t\t\t\t\t
