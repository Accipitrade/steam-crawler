\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy most transformative moment in VR was had in the first hour of Boneworks. In the museum, there’s a statue with some buttons on the walls near its top. I wanted to see what they did, so I climbed up the statue. When I reached the button, I stretched with one hand while gripping the statue with the other. I couldn’t reach the button, so I pulled a sledgehammer from my back, but that still couldn’t quite reach as I was holding too close to the middle. I loosened my grip on the hammer and pressed the butt into my stomach, sliding my hand down to the base and increasing my reach. This was something that came intuitively as a beginner to the game and was possible only because everything I described was a physics object. The statue, the button, the hammer, my body, and my hand. Without any of them, this deceptively simple trick wouldn’t have worked.

Massive congratulations to the dev team on shipping this amazing game!\t\t\t\t\t
