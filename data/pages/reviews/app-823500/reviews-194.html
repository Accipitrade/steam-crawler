\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter first getting into VR, my friends all relentlessly recommended this game to me. I ended up buying it, playing it and regretting my purchase.
My first issue with this game also happens to be the biggest selling point. Hyper-realism. This game is (concerning physics and control) far too realistic. Full arm playermodels with collisions make things just that bit more tedious and difficult to control. VR is meant to feel like you are actually in the game, not piloting a sluggish humanoid robot. I will compare this game and its control to Half Life: Alyx. This game is the most immersive game I've ever played because the controls are so simple. After a few minutes of learning the controls, you can easily forget you are playing a game and lose yourself in the game. Alyx does not have any body models besides hands, making it as simple as possible rather than overcomplicating for realism's sake.
Another major issue i have with this game is the firearm operation. The guns are just plain not fun to use. Reloading is a struggle and firing a gun feels bland. Even the enemies are uninteresting. There is no gun in this game that hasn't been used billions of times before. Let's compare the guns of Boneworks to the guns of Hot Dogs, Horseshoes and Hand Grenades. The guns in H3 work prefectly. Lining up sights feels like firing a real gun. Reloads are natural and easy.
My final gripe is the general story. To me, Boneworks shouldn't have a campaign. It feels like a cheap way to stretch out playtime to acquire items for the sandbox. The story is just, for lack of better term, dumb. To summarize, all events in the story are due to the player's search for immortality. There is no pursuit of good, moral to learn, adventure worth having, boss to kill and/or reason to bother. Ultimately, the story is barely a story. As for the example of a VR game with good story, just look around a bit. Story VR games aren't particularly in my wheelhouse, but it is not hard to find examples all over gaming magazines.
To conclude, this game is far from the worst I've played. It serves as a pretty good proof of concept for extremely realistic VR games, which will inevitably be more commonplace as VR becomes more accessible. It is no doubt a well made game. The attention to detail with the physics and level design is amazing. But, the issues with it are still glaring and make the game hard to enjoy.

Overall, 2/10\t\t\t\t\t
