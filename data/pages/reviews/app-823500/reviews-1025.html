\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTake my recommendation with a grain of salt.

I like this game a lot, the physics are well done, the movement and controls are perfect, and the game play is interesting.

However, for some odd reason, this is literally the only game in my entire VR library that gives me severe motion sickness... I play a ton of vr games, many of which have smooth locomotion and interaction as Boneworks, but no matter how much I try to play this game, I can only play it for about half an hour before I have to set it aside and sit down before i vomit.

I thought I had a great tolerance for movement in VR, but I think I have an idea as to why this game gives me motion sickness.

Some vr games are designed to work against motion sickness by blurring the edges of your vision while you move. Others, however, incorporate your movement into the game so that way it somewhat feels like you're actually moving around and as such don't get motion sickness. The best example of this I can find is H3VR, where you have multiple movement options, but one in particular allows you to move by swinging your arms as if you were running. I think if Boneworks incorporated other movement options that didn't rely on the twin stick functionality that it currently has, it may provide a more comfortable way to play the game.\t\t\t\t\t
