But RIGHT when the games about to end, right when you ask yourself "Hey, where were those fancy and fun looking gadgets i saw the player using in the trailer?" You find an ammo vending machine, that sells all 6 completely unique physics tools for very very expensive amounts of ammunition that only now you discover (My playthrough that spans 10 hours got me around 7000 ammunition, each costs around 12 to 25 thousand EACH, now idk if there is a much faster way to get them that slipped past me but that seems like a long time to grind) A gun that works just like the phys-gun in garrys mod? Locked behind a vending machine, Magnetic climbing gloves that let you climb any surface in the game that would have been extremely fun to use in earlier levels? Behind a vending machine. Jet pack gauntlets to fly like iron man? BEHIND THE F*@&ING VENDING MACHINES. Yes the levels up to this point (except for the medieval part, that was probably the lowest the game got) were enjoyable but they all use the same mechanics and I had myself thinking that they would eventually give you a new tool to use rather than the stock standard axe, crowbar or sledge hammer and the small assortment of guns you find. Before getting hit with this massive frying pan that has "Oh yeah we had all of this stuff too that could have made really enjoyable level material" written on the back. No way in hell can i be bothered to grind all of this ammo just to get these tools in a mode where you get everything so theres really gonna be like 10 minutes of fun for each before you get bored. These tools would have been so fun to get throughout the campaign and have like 2-3 puzzles for each. So yeah that turned me off for this game but you may have a different view on it so let me know
