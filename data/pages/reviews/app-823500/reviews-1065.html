\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a great foundation that I feel falls short in some areas but makes up for those failings in, well, everything else. Boneworks has one of the best physics systems I've ever seen in a VR game. When you start to become more attuned to the mechanics and the controls you'll often find yourself trying to raise you legs on top of things in other games because it feels so natural or grab random objects to hurl them at enemies. The gun mechanics are also very solid, although not as amazing as I would have hoped after all the hype. Boneworks also exceeds in it's visual style; the visuals for environments, enemies and objects are phenomenal.

I also want to briefly comment on the soundtrack: It is great. One of the best. It does a fantastic job of setting the mood and getting you hyped in some situations.

Unfortunately, I do have some major issues with Boneworks, first being the Story. Without spoiling anything, the story felt quite "hollow" A lot of things were setup and left you with wild theories and questions but were ultimately not resolved by the end. The story is very confusing and the "big picture" only begins to make sense if you know about Duck Season's story and with a hint of speculation, leaving you still questioning what you where even doing during the story. I really hope that the story for Boneworks as it's own game is expanded upon in the future and in fact I would not mind seeing and paying for some DLC that adds new levels and goes more into the specifics of the story.

Boneworks also has a glaring lack of enemy variety. There are (from memory) 7 enemy types that you naturally encounter and 3 of them are basically just reskined and higher health versions of some of the previous ones. I really hope that if there is DLC we get to see some more interesting enemies that require unique approaches to take out, rather than simply shooting or hitting them in the head till they die, as a game with mechanics like this is just begging for enemies that require use of the physics systems to dispatch (One example I though up was a drone that the player must grab onto and shut down while it tries to fly away, but I digress.)

Overall, Boneworks is a solid and well worth experience that, with expansion, I would have no trouble recommending. I have heard somewhere that expansions and updates will be coming and I am hopeful for that if it is true but even without Boneworks is worth the current price, if a tad disappointing in some areas.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has blown my mind a few times.
It's hard. The climbing is tough to get a handle on, some of the limitations of having a physical body can be pretty weird and immersion-breaking, and the lack of save functionality is pretty annoying.
that said, it's fun. It's intriguing. The story is there for people who seek it out. It's told kind of like how Portal was told in the later chapters, with the Rat Man's scrawlings and an air of mystery.
You expect to feel like a total badass when you play this game, but it's tough to get to that point. It's not like Super Hot where you can easily pull off John Wick-esque moves with ease. You have to be a lot more careful and be able to think on your feet in certain situations.
The first badass moment I had was in the Sewers level. That's several hours into the game. I was ganged up on by multiple adversaries who were all coming towards me, and one of them hurled an energy blast at me. I'd run out of ammo, and it was the first ever time that I reloaded the gun, charged it, then killed an enemy to bring myself out of near-death. It was a cathartic moment. Because ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, is the reloading ever hard to get used to!
I'm not even done the game and I'd recommend it to just about anyone who's played any of the Half Life games or either Portal. It's a lot of puzzle solving and some pretty crazy combat, though most of the time the combat is fairly slow. You definitely don't feel overpowered, at least in the early stages.
8/10, would recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't think even checkpoints can make this game fun. There is serious level design flaws and the puzzles are a joke. They appear easy, which they are, but what makes them infuriating is the physics. Getting a hook to connect with a movable platform is WAY harder than it should be, platforms that dangle move vigorously while on them (even worse if you have to carry a heavy cube to place on a button), getting your arm constantly stuck inside of things, etc. You get the idea, you will be fighting physics constantly in this game and it completely ruins the experience. The level design also is just baffling. You can fall off a ledge and be set back a good 2 minutes, this will happen over and over and it adds up. I understand this was a small team working with nearly no budget, but I can't suggest this game to anyone because of these issues. I was really looking forward to the game because we finally got a innovative vr game, but it's halfbaked and every time I decided to play this game it was because I wanted to get my moneys worth.\t\t\t\t\t
