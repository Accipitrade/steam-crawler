\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is obviously worth recommending and many people have pointed out the positives. I am going to point out the negatives.

The game feels dead somehow. There is no real interaction or something to keep you engaged. Just mindless and boring enemies that shoot and track you through walls.

[Minor Spoiler]
At one point I was just walking through some tunnels and it just felt like such a void experience. No engaging or creepy sound effects, no creepy ambient sound. The graphics also really lost their touch in this somewhat darker area. I can imagine the flashlight adding to the ambiance in other games, but even with graphics high - it all just looked flat.

Hard to explain... But the game just feels like a massive alpha game due to the void feeling, dead sounds and unpolished enemies that shoot and track through walls. Also - enjoy climbing up ledges or slipping on slippery ♥♥♥♥ only to try and fail 5 times to get out and sliding right back to the start.

Game is cheap, so I'd say it's worth exactly its current price and can therefore recommend it. However, do NOT go in with Source-level hype. It's nothing like Portal or anything Valve would make, for that matter.\t\t\t\t\t

Started off wanting to puke and then somehow, almost unbelievably, I got to the first bit where you climb and jump and do all sorts of ♥♥♥♥ that should be vomit inducing but THAT actually fixed my brain and I could proceed in comfort. Weird huh!

Only downside for me is the sound design. The score is awesome but the sound design feels like either a total afterthought or was a misjudged style choice. For example the 3D spacial to stereo audio doesn't have enough cross-over bleed between your ears (as in, if you turn your left ear to something it's almost totally gone from your right ear which is super jarring) and there are tons of sound effects that are just totally misplaced. Like hitting a barrel with a hatchet sounds like slashing a cabbage.

I don't say this to be mean, more because I know what awesome stuff the Node / Corridor / SLZ guys are capable of - they are super talented and completely understand the role and importance of audio in film and it's for that reason I hold them to this standard.

Other than that I can see myself spending a ♥♥♥♥ ton of time in this game - it really is the new bench mark of how gameplay mechanics should work in VR (just please consider working on that audio dudes) :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: If you like the idea of physics puzzles, fantastic combat mechanics, and an overall polished VR experience, buy this game.

PROS:
- This is the most immersive virtual reality experience that exists today.
- The world is full of details.
- Most interactions with the various entities in the game feel _amazing_.
- Even though the game is not trying to be super scary, it can be terrifying sometimes. The surprise I felt when an enemy unexpectedly came at me through a door...

CONS:
- The controls and interactions in this game can be awkward sometimes. This game is not for beginners (it even says so after you boot it up).
- No save system yet. This means you have to start and finish a level in one sitting. These levels are long when you don't know what you're doing. After you figure out what to do, you can quickly zip back through previous levels.
- I slam my controllers into my Index headset sometimes because I forget I'm wearing one and bring my hands too close to my face.\t\t\t\t\t
