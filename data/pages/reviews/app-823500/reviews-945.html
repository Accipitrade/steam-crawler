\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a one of a kind VR game. Now I'm not a VR connoisseur or anything as I got my headset in the Summer of 2019 but this game was a fantastic experience. The physics implemented in the game is something new that I haven't seen in a VR game before and I was excited to try it out after they teased the game last year. The physics engine handles really well and handles as it would in real life, though for me since I have a Windows Mixed Reality, things like climbing and reaching out to my pouch to reload were somewhat finicky. I'll let that slide though as Mixed Reality is using inside-out tracking and is the worst when it comes to the VR market. The gameplay with weapons is excellent. Guns handle as they should and even holding one makes you feel more immersed as you aim in to take long-range shots and shoot your way into combat. The inventory system is really great too. The ability to holster your gun/items behind a certain area like under your arms or over your shoulder and take it out by reaching said area feels really nice. Environments are beautiful with some of the best graphics I’ve seen yet in a VR game. The game itself is really well optimized too, though at times when I shoot really close when an enemy comes by, I lag massively but that’s probably due to the particle effects being intensive as ever. Story-wise… it was a hit or miss for me. I felt like I was understanding the story a bit in the beginning but just got clustered up when I kept progressing. I’ll watch a video that’ll explain the story though since I AM interested in it. And to finish off (since I’m not really a reviewer, I wanted to make this review short, but still wanted to make it as I fell in love with this game) the soundtrack. Oh my God Michael Wyckoff, (producer of the OST) I applaud you since you captured that 80-90’s vibe the game is set in. Now I don’t listen to a lot of synthwave but I immediately loved every track that the game had. If you’re thinking about getting a VR headset and want a triple-A experience, this is a must-have! I’ve been wanted this game since the very first trailer came out and I finished it today. Stress Level Zero, I can’t wait to see what you come up with next!\t\t\t\t\t

This is a must have title for anyone who's serious about VR. I bought this game for it's gameplay, but also for how technically it was executed. Everything is physics-based, which can result in very unique scenarios and selutions nobody would expect.

The guns feel great. Melee weapons seemed like they should have felt good, but actually swinging at the enemies I couldn't really hit them consistently (my hits were often not "hard" enough to hurt). Though, the problem with the melee weapons might have been the not-great WMR tracking I was using.

For the enemies, there were basically two types of enemies: headset-crabs and humanoids. Although the game tried to make the humanoids different, they really just felt as the same enemy, regardless of what "type" they were.

The physical body had issues when trying to adjust to large masses (holding a very heavy object, the player hanging on one arm). This is part of the reason why this game can induce motion sickness when climbing, or why holding heavy objects (like a longsword or a heavy box) feels so "springy". But the game is more than playable, because these issues don't really come often. This is a pretty complicated problem to solve though, and the developers did a pretty great job trying to minimize these issues. Keep in mind that there aren't many (if any) VR games with such an intricate physics interaction system, which is a perfect fit for a VR game.

The most dissapointing part of the game was the story. There wasn't really any suspense or a very clear goal on what you're supposed to do as a protagonist. I didn't really catch any explanation about exactly what I was trying to do (there kind of was an explanation, but it was rather a speculation by another character), and getting to the end didn't really make it clear on what I actually achieved. While the mistery of all the story seemed to be intentionally not explained (maybe a sequel is planned? Or an extension to the story?) I really wanted to know a lot more about everything that's going on.\t\t\t\t\t
