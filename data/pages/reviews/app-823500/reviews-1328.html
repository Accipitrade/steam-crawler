\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs Boneworks a good game? Sure. Is Boneworks INFURIATING as hell? Absolutely.

Boneworks prides itself on its advanced physics engine and shows it off every chance it gets. Phys Puzzles are the most common element to even out the pace of the game and to act as a lull between combat. Unfortunately Boneworks has a terrible problem with its physics.

The player's HANDS, as you might imagine, are physical things in the world of this game. You have a great realm of freedom in how you interact with every single object in the game because everything WILL react and because of this some problems do occur. A little more details here, while your hands are physical your "arms" are most certainly NOT. The game obviously cannot know at all times where your arms are or what angle you have them at so it of course guesses. It is from this guessing that every single problem occurs.

There's not a real easy way to describe it but your hands just get stuck. You're trying to climb up a platform and you're hanging on for dear life and you go to grip with your opposite hand annndd... you dont see it? Where is it? Oh... it's stuck... it's caught between the wall and one of the rings of a ladder... How?? IDK beats me but its stuck. Can you unstuck it? Sure! Is it stupid to get out? Of course. The hand will spasm the crap out as it tries to get where your controller is all the while you're just hoping that you dont screw something else up and fall a thousand feet and ruin all effort you put in to climbing. There comes a point in time where you've played the game almost all the way through. You've realized that these problems are a thing you have to deal with and so you try to stay calm when they happen. But the fact of the matter is that sometimes when this problem occurs it is at a point where you are invested in the game and when it happens it COMPLETELY removes you from the game and causes a small amount of rage as it happens more and more. I honestly think your hands are the worst part about this game.... that being said there is still a lot this game does RIGHT.

Again, despite the hands being a problem ,the physics engine is incredible! You feel the weight of every action you do and every gun feels good to fire. Gunplay feels natural. The combat isn't too difficult but it remains fun. Puzzles offer a nice break between combat although they're sometimes annoying to deal with and come at times when you'd rather not care.

I experienced 0 crashes during this game. And virtually no performance issues except in one location near the end of the game (R9 3900x, GTX 1080, 16GB 3200, SSD). I cant think of anything more so I'll wrap this review up.

IS BONEWORKS FUN?... YES... IS BONEWORKS ANNOYING?... YES...

Everything boils down to full body physics being an absolute ass to deal with. Your hands should never get in the way of how you play the game. You, when you move through the Virtual space, are interacting with the space as you would if you were really there AND YET your virtual hands are not following suit. Your mind has told you that your action is correct but the game has said "not so fast" and while not a deal breaker it is very annoying.

I will recommend this game but hope in the future we can stray away from such intense full body physics.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic VR experience. Here's what I think after 3 hours straight.
Playing on Valve Index

#PROs#
*everything is physically based (arms cant pass through objects, everything is interact-able). I cant stress how immersive this makes the gameplay.
*Controls are mostly intuitive
*Puzzles feel original
*Theres a strange plot that's unraveling a mystery and makes me want to figure out what's going on.
*If you die you respawn at the beginning of the level, but all progress is saved (enemies stay dead, puzzles remained solved).

#CONs#
*Guns dont feel punchy against robot headcrabs and ranged enemies. I want more impact affect on each hit even if it's not realistic.
*Some actions require index finger "clicking" (to pull out weapon or magazine). Which doesnt feel good when you're pulling out a weapon with the same button you use to fire. And you have to remember which button for which action. I would prefer grab action for everything if possible.
*reloading while moving is really hard

All around a step forward for VR, the tech is a new standard for immersive gameplay and even though theres some occasional frustration with controls or getting stuck on/in things, the game feels fresh and well worth the price tag.
