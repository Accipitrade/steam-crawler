\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlways admired this game from YouTube perspectives, but after getting into VR, playing some games and then getting into boneworks I found myself very underwhelmed by the game. I personally found this game like playing QWOP in VR. The mechanics in theory are really fun, but in practice are extremely frustrating and exhausting to the eyes. This game will test your VR motion sickness with every play-through. I never thought a game could have too much interactivity, but I found my character to constantly get stuck on random objects which would jolt around your character in awkward ways, and attempting to do some of the physics puzzles would take several tries which is extremely punishing in VR. I found that the combat on all fronts was not fluid or intuitive whatsoever. Swinging a melee weapon had such a strange feel in comparison to other melee based VR titles. I also found that the guns were fairly inaccurate to shoot, even with smooth trigger pulls. It always seemed like my shots would veer one way or another despite knowing how to aim and shoot real guns. This game gives off a real half life vibe, but unlike half-life I found the overall level design to be confusing to navigate. The story is also very cryptic and uninteresting. The most fun part of the game is playing through all of the sandbox modes, which requires you to play levels multiple times and complete puzzles.\t\t\t\t\t
