\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEveryone is talking about the physics, and yes they are really awesome.

I wanna mention something else, that is partially facilitated by the physics but is it's own thing.

The level design.
Wow. These guys really hit that Portal-esque exploring a facility type feeling. I have spent FAR too long just ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ around in the levels rather than actually progressing through them. Hours in and I'm only on like the third level?
Besides just the mysterious environments, they've really allowed you to approach different problems and puzzles from a myriad of ways. For example, there is a gun turret set up at a sort of check point area. Now, you can either run from cover to cover to get up to it, or you can skirt around and hop the wall nearby to get behind it. OR, you can also use the very well placed metal trash cans nearby. You could use the easily noticeable lid as a shield (they purposely made it to be grabbed by the handle in this fashion), or you could even put the trash can on like you're Solid Snake/Frank Reynolds. Plus, there's plenty of little extra bits in corners, etc. that are completely unnecessary to the goal but are incentive to explore and check things out. And a lot of this integrates the gameplay design and physics INTO the level.
Absolutely excellent design and execution, Brandon and team. SLZ really are, as another reviewer said, the start of a VR renaissance.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS is great and you should get it if you own the hardware for it. Graphics, gameplay and sound design are excellent, there’s a decent chunk of game here and a lot to see and do. Physics are revolutionary for VR and I hope more games come along and take use of the foundation here. The games level design, puzzles and check points needed more work. The biggest thing holding this game back is the lack of actual checkpoints or saving during missions. The average mission is about an hour long and can be agonizing to die and have to back track a ridiculous amount of space after dying. Maybe in a flat screen game that wouldn’t be a problem, but in VR it can be literally tiring to have to retread and perform physical movement over and over again.

My biggest hope for BONEWORKS is continuous support after launch. Adding mod support would be the cherry on top for this game. As of now it’s a must buy for VR owners, but it could very well be a VR system seller if Stress Level Zero continues support for this game after launch.\t\t\t\t\t
