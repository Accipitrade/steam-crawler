Overview
I can see why many people like it, but personally it's not something I get a lot of enjoyment out of. For most people, the game is a blast, but for people like myself, it's not that fun. I don't particularly enjoy games that include long puzzles and long periods of "wtf am I doing?" Although usually the lack of a specific direction is a welcome change in a gaming market full of "quest markers" and arrows pointing you where exactly to go. The campaign is reasonably long, too, which is a plus... *cough cough* Superhot VR *cough cough*

Don't expect instant action or long, challenging combat. There are VERY long periods of silence, walking, and puzzle solving between battles. The game is best for its relatively intuitive physics... at least when you're not standing on medium-to-small props (more on that later).

The pros:

- The game contains a plethora of physics puzzles and has well-polished mechanics; for instance, the guns are very satisfying to use. Nothing feels quite as awesome as unloading on one opponent only to very rapidly reload, rack the gun, and promptly magdump someone else.

- Probably one of the best physics systems in VR. Objects actually feel like they have a bit of weight to them, and it's cool being able to deal light damage to opponents using random objects you find lying around.

The cons:

- Without full-body tracking, grabbing a mag during combat or grabbing a small firearm holstered on your waist can be *extremely* difficult or frustrating, especially when under the stress of being shot at and attacked by several armed enemies. This is because the body's position rarely reflects that of your actual body when playing (at least when crouched or taking cover).

- The AI is kinda just... stupid... or at least is not always engaging to fight. They'll frequently get stuck on an object or just stand in a corner to wait for you. Most of the time though this isn't a very large issue.

- Due to its nature as a mostly story-driven game (it might be called a "sandbox" but I'd bet that 90% of people never do anything more than the campaign), its mileage is considerably lower than it is for a game like Blade and Sorcery.

- Melee combat feels almost meaningless sometimes. Using most melees feel like you're doing little more than punching them. But it's important to note that the game's strong-suit was never supposed to be melee, so that shouldn't be held against the game too much.

- Somehow I've seen people tout that this game has one of the best climbing systems in VR, which very much feels untrue from my experience. Climbing has been nothing but agitating and boring. Not because it's difficult or my controllers are bad (I use Valve Index controllers), but because climbing in VR is just stupid. (KINDA SPOILERS? BUT NOT REALLY) There's this one part where you grab on to a giant wheel which is rotating, but it feels FAR from intuitive to let go of and use your inertia to drop onto the surrounding platforms. On many occasions will you find yourself either falling through or off of an unstable platform and trying to use the poor climbing system to push yourself back up. There's a section where you're supposed to cross a bridge that has huge holes in it, almost like a horizontal ladder. Which is VERY unintuitive in VR, and you will find yourself repeating a large, relatively empty section of the map to get back, should you make a mistake. On another occasion will you find yourself cleverly trying to make a stack of props to climb atop of, only for it to repeatedly come crashing down because the physics system isn't very great at accommodating players standing on props.

- I've played 5 hours but I feel like I've played the entire campaign. It just kinda gets repetitive after a while for me. If you're the type that likes arena combat, instant action, or simply dislikes puzzle games, then this really isn't gonna be the game for you. This was my first true VR game that I've played, and it left me honestly a little disappointed.

- The game removes your weapons from your inventory every now and then. Which, in my opinion, kinda ruins the scavenging and the 'I'm slowly improving' parts of the game for me. Why should I grab the rifle with the laser and reflex sight if it's just going to vanish in a couple of hours of playtime? It's like playing an RPG but your base stats get reset every time you start getting powerful. At least the game lets you keep your bullets I guess?\t\t\t\t\t
