\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a great take on VR gameplay, and it works as well as you'd expect for relying ENTIRELY on physics. It's a very surreal experience and an enjoyable one.

My only gripe (so far) is that I kinda expected more from the physics end of things. I came away feeling like all I really did was run around desperately looking for keys to doors (sometimes for a bit too long, mostly my fault as I am a moron) stacking boxes to reach ledges with occasionally sliding platforms around. I was expecting some Portal-tier physics based puzzles, some real head scratchers. This lead me to overthink a LOT of puzzles.

I was disappointed that the sandbox is kinda hard to unlock, I thought it would be either open from the beginning or after beating the story, but instead I had to look up what to do and I really never would've unlocked it otherwise. And the sandbox isn't really much of a sandbox. Yes, you can spawn in items at will with a spawn gun, but only if you managed to put that weapon in a Reclamation Bin at some point in the story. So if you want to fully play around in a true sandbox, you need to do some collecting first.

Another problem I had was that the game doesn't explain a LOT of things. I had no idea what the Reclamation Bins were for, I thought it allowed you to carry items over to the next level. Had I known what they were for an I would've thrown everything but the kitchen sink in there.
Last gripe, I NEVER used the slow-mo because you have to hold the top button (on Vive) on the left controller, the same controller that my thumb is using to control my movement. So my choice is to either awkwardly press forward and the slow-mo button, or just stand still and use it.

Despite these little issues, it's an overall fun experience, it's wacky and weird and fun to play. I look forward to see what content might be added in the future. It is early days, so maybe all of my gripes will be fixed at some point.
