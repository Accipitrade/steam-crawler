\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have actively sought out high-intensity experiences since I was a child, and do not experience motion sickness in any form of simulator, including VR. This game lacks any form of comfort options for those who do. This review is from someone who is comfortable playing Boneworks for extended periods of time.

With that said, Boneworks is perhaps the best VR game I have ever played. It's the first that has genuinely gotten me addicted to high-action VR gameplay. Although the physics engine has very likely been duplicated by many other VR titles in the past, this is the first full title (I count a "full" title as anything with a 5+ hour length) I've seen that not only makes use of it, but does it extremely well. Everything has weight and acts as you expect it to in reality. I've found myself surprised time and time again as I ask "will the game let me do this?", only to discover that yes, it does.

Gunplay is very similar to H3VR, in a good way. Although the guns in my opinion handle very similar to that game, SLZ haven't included aspects that would otherwise hinder the high-action gameplay. For example, although manual reloading is available, it's not required - simply bringing a new cartridge to your weapon will eject the old one, and a single press of the trigger will prep the weapon to fire again. Dual-wielding is also possible, although hard to reload.

As mentioned earlier, the game lacks any form of comfort options, going as far as openly mocking the teleportation mechanics many VR titles utilise in the game's tutorial. While I had a laugh out of it, this is probably because I'm very used to manual locomotion in VR. Many people aren't, sadly, and I've noticed that a lot of the game's negative reviews are due to the lack of comfort options.

In addition, the game lacks the ability to continue from your previous checkpoint. Checkpoints actually exist in the game, as evidenced when you die, but the game only saves when you clear an individual section, which typically takes up to an hour and a half. If you need to take a break, don't close the game or exit to the menu. You WILL lose progress. With that said, a patch is due in the following weeks to fix this. SLZ have mentioned that any major updates will occur in January, and that only minor bug fixes are being distributed.

You also have to mention the soundtrack. Problem is, I have no words to describe how amazing Michael Wychoff's compositions are. I'm unable to appreciate it in the game so I've found myself listening to it over and over on Spotify. It's the best soundtrack to a VR title I've ever heard. Listen to it - it's called Bonetones.

Boneworks is said to have heavily inspired mechanics in Half-Life: Alyx. If this is the case, and Boneworks is only the beginning, I'm very, very excited to see how Valve takes these already great mechanics and turns it into the most anticipated Half-Life ever.

Now, if you'll excuse me, I'm off to play more Boneworks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Good:
Boneworks is really the first great story based VR game and probably the most fun I've had in VR. The levels feel nice, the combat feels nice and solving the puzzles and just traversing the levels is fun.
The Bad:
The game has a few technical issues such as: when climbing over thin walls or fences sometimes when I fall back my arms phase through it as the arms don't seem to have collisions and this can cause softlocks, while Boneworks has the best stick based movement I've experienced it would be nice to have options such as armswinger and just as a nit-pick the M1911 seems to have misaligned sights. Also as an issue that I believe is exclusive to the Oculus Rift, when grabbing weapons from the inventory or using the item pull you need to press the trigger whilst grabbing which in every other case causes the gun to immediately fire.

Overall:
Boneworks is really a great game for anyone who doesn't get easily motion sick in VR and really the game's issues aren't that big and are mostly infrequent annoyances.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT Apr 21st 2020: It seems with the recent physics update the weapon handling as well as climbing has been improved. Using Long bladed weapons that aren't daggers still seems kind of janky as slashes arent as effective as just regular blunt trauma or repeated short stabs.

The firearm handling is not as realistic as I would like it to be as slide releasing or chambering a round without using the charging handle is performed with a pull of the controller trigger, whereas other games like H3VR have an actual bolt catch for M4 styled rifles and the HK slap in boneworks can only be performed with a closed fist from my experience. This use of the controller trigger is also true for handguns as there is no slide release bound to the buttons on the controller like in HL Alyx.

I would also like to see a thumb and index finger animation of pulling back the charging handle instead of the index and middle finger animation currently in the game. I would like to not unshoulder the rifle to chamber a round thru the charging handle as well as having a functioning bolt catch button.

Original Review:

I would say BW gave me at least a decent or satisfactory experience. It's quite hard to find the replay value in it or something that pulls me back to the game besides hunting for secret boxes and saving the items inside.

Pros:
Semi well done physics vr game lets you interact mostly well with your environment
Creative Level Design with a lot of secrets
Good Soundtrack
Graphics aren't bad

Cons:
Janky physics
Janky physics might cause vr sickness
Not fun and clunky to use two handed guns
Melee combat lacks impact and feels clunky
Lack of engaging story
Lack of explained game mechanics like hp, ammo scoring system
Lack of content that was shown on YouTube Channels
Lack of checkpoints
--------------------

While the first playthrough I had fun and enjoyed it, I have to force myself to keep coming back to this game with excuses like unlocking new items for sandbox or maybe seeing if two handed guns are fun to use.

The hype surrounding this game through the Node channel AFAIK focused mainly on the combat of BW. This is where I feel the game could use some more polish. Melee combat feels kind of floaty and dangerous. If you dont kill the enemy with one good first strike then melee combat becomes worthless as trying to wrestle enemies is pointless. Bladed/pointy weapons feel like jelly to use and aren't effective.

Guns are ok I guess. I do not like the gun sounds currently in the game as they sound as if they're not unique (might not be). Guns with stocks will collide with your ingame body and cause sight alignment issues as well as jiggly guns when you first draw them.

We were shown a lot more on Node and some stuff isn't in the game.

Climbing ladders, pipes, etc creates a very jiggly camera that might bleed over to irl and cause discomfort.

ATM I would say you can buy this game for the one playthrough if these problems seems like nothing to you. Otherwise stay away until fixed\t\t\t\t\t

Played on: HTC Vive

The game is amazing, very well made. Worth my 25 euro. Smashing enemies with a sledgehammer or just taking aim and shooting them in the face is very entertaining, since it all feels real with the amazing physics that are at play. While climbing is a little difficult, I can get used to it.

Some puzzles however really need you to use your brain, and the climbing, as mentioned before is pretty difficult.

The only real bad thing I find about it is not part of the game itself, but rather the fact the Vive wands are not really great for this game, and I would rather use the Index controllers instead.

UPDATE: Well, there are a few more problems that you might bump into. For example, arms getting in the way or getting stuck. Or weapons getting stuck. But other than that, it is great.

4/5 If this is a tech demo, then it is the best tech demo I ever played.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're skeptical because you don't own the knuckle controllers and think you won't get the best experience, then don't worry because I play on an Oculus Rift and I had a really fun experience and was blown away from this experience so far. I made a list of the pros and cons to make it easier to read.

Pros:
-You can see your arms and whole body (except for the head of course)
-You can hold melee weapons almost however you want
-Realistic physics (for the most part)
-Good combat system
-Good gun mechanics
-Fun puzzles and parkour
---------
Cons:

-Ammo is plentiful, which adds little challenge to the game
-The way you grip the MP5 is like how a crab spins a fidget spinner
-Sometimes my hands get stuck in walls
-Sometimes when I attempt to retrieve a weapon from my back I won't equip it (Probably just a height calculation error)

Overall rating: 9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is amazing, best VR experience to date, got it right when it was released. I got slightly motion sick for the first hour or so then everything after felt natural and smooth. For people who get motion-sick pretty easily make sure to take regular breaks and soon there will be mid-level checkpoint. For me I built up a tolerance pretty fast and can play the game for more than 4 hours straight without any problems. I was so immersed in the game once that when I took the headset off I tried to force grab my phone. But I think this is a revolutionary game, the mechanics are phenomenal, you can interact with everything, everything has physics to it, just like a VR should have. Valve take notes for Half-Life Alyx. All of this for $30 dollars, amazing!! Also the soundtrack, perfect. The game is also replayable, there are many hidden things around the map to collect for the sandbox mode, and replaying the story is surprisingly not boring.

"What if this is just a tech demo?!"\t\t\t\t\t
