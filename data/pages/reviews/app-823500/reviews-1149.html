\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe short version:
The physics they're trying to implement in this game completely ruin the VR experience.

The long version:
The so-called "physics" that are supposed to be so great in this game are mostly just the fact that the player model doesn't clip through walls like in other VR games. Sounds good in theory, but this means the game gets very difficult to control very quickly. It's easy to lose track of where my hands are because they often get stuck behind an object or wall. Likewise, the on-screen player hands are almost never in the same location as the controllers because the game insists on letting them snag on every physical object. It just makes things frustrating and ruins the immersion.
This game also only has smooth locomotion. In my opinion, no VR game should ever rely on smooth locomotion. I'm not usually prone to motion sickness, but this game gets overwhelmingly nauseating. The fact that your player model (including your head/view) gets stuck on everything you run into just makes it worse. In other words, your view is regularly and forcefully moved.

Regardless of the issues, I hope this game can be used as a learning experience for all VR developers. It's intended to be experimental, after all. As odd as it sounds, the player's hands must be allowed to clip through the world, and the player's view should NEVER be forcefully moved, either via smooth locomotion, jumping, etc. VR does not work unless these rules are followed.\t\t\t\t\t

The flow of this game is amazing.
Get from point A to point B however you want; fight some goons and VR headsetcrabs, then either solve a puzzle or climb up or across something, perhaps finding a secret crate in a high up corner making you wonder, how the hell do I get up there? This engine has some real kick to it, but imagining what could be made from it...endless possibilities.

This is also a long shot, I know, but how about a co op DLC? Having to combine strength to lift something, synchronized button presses, mechanisms that require one person to traverse a path that the other is helping to unblock from afar...

Really though, a level editor akin to Halo's Forge would be unbelievably epic, I would pay for the game a second time for that.\t\t\t\t\t
