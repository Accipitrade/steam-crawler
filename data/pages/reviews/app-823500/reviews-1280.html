\r\n\t\t\t\t\t7/10 but it's a VR Must-Have. It should be included in a bundle with Pavlov VR, The Lab (and all Valve VR derivatives), Google Earth VR, and Virtual Virtual Reality.

This game is a combination of Half Life, Portal, Virtual Virtual Reality, Super Hot and Climbey. It tries to have a deep backstory, but it fails in the end and is not quite as good at story execution. The physics are awesome and my mind and body are still lagging IRL. However, the physics sometimes feel more like Human: Fall Flat rather than a physics simulation.

On the level of suspense this game gave me, I'd rate it a solid 7.5/10, where 10 would be Virtual Virtual Reality (I avoid scary games). I was genuinely cautious moving through the game, until the end, when the narrative kinda skewed. The story should be redone. It's trying too hard to be like VVR and should have focused more on Slow-Motion in my opinion.

I spent over 8 hours in several sessions doing the campaign including exploration, backtracking, and puzzle solving. I have another hour in the game experimenting and playing the game, but also dealing with a bug.

The ending of the game is fun, but not satisfying. I've seen other players criticize the live-action segments, and I see their point. I think the live-action videos should have been done from a surveillance camera rather than cinematic.

There is 1 particularly super satisfying effect that occurs throughout the game, and it has to do with textures.

The price is not justified yet, but there seems to be more content coming. The game is worth $20, but it is innovative, and is just a taste of what's to come.

The developers state that they plan on making other games with this engine. This game is simply a tech demo. A very long and enjoyable one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot recommend Boneworks right now. While It is fun, innovative, and might just be the future of gaming, it lacks the important feature of saving.

The levels are large and interconnected. I once was captured and taken to a room that I had been to earlier. Watching the level move below me as I was taken back was an incredible experience.

The biggest flaw in the game is that there is no saving. There is a checkpoint function, but if you quit in the middle of a level you start over at the beginning instead of one of the checkpoints. The levels are pretty long and this gets frustrating if you need to quit in the middle of an hour long level.

When you die changes to the world are persistent. At times this is really helpful, puzzles will remain solved and won't need to be repeated. At other times this is an issue. Once i needed to get past a turret and used a loader as cover, but I peeked out and was shot to death. When I respawned the loader remained halfway between where it stared and where I had died, forcing me to use a more risky method to bypass the turret. Another time I was climbing and accidentally grabbed my pistol by mistake which caused me to fall off the wall and my pistol to disappear into the void. I have lost three different weapons to clipping issues similar to this and could not get them back them because of the persistent changes.

Boneworks is a really fun game and I think it is a going to start a really terrific era in gaming.There are a few big problems with the game, but all of them would be solved with a saving feature.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tedit: I take back my non-chalantness in the first paragraph, and any caveats i may have listed on twitter. I am in romantic love with this game.

Here it is! Boneworks! I kept up with the hype enough to know I had to buy this day 1. First things first, I'm not SUPER TOTALLY AMAZED by the how physics objects in your grasp and your hands and all that are now first class physics objects in the game. I've seen it before in many little escape room games. Sometimes I'd even play something with particularly good physical object interaction. None of them however, approached the LEVEL of Boneworks physical fidelity. These must be some detailed collision models. It's like Valve turning Havoc physics into the robust thing that it is today. I hope and expect more titles to attempt this. In fact, there is a (now that I think of it) an eearily Boneworks-like gun+physics test on the SideQuest store.

I have VR legs of steel. I almost never get even the slightest bit queasy. I wish I got queasy more often, and even lose some of that VR vertigo that made my early games so much fun. That being said, I could see how this game could make even VR veterans have to take breaks. I played the whole thing for 2 hours and felt great, but this game does some rapid jiggling of your VR body that

I'm not sure how realistic everything is SUPPOSED to be. I gotta assume they could spend a few more months tweaking everything in the physics/movement, but overall, I very much appreciate their no-holes-barred, full experience intended for the VR soldiers first and foremost. Still though, I've noticed things like, say, a huge dumpster full of trash move (after bumping into it) at similar velocity to when I was trying to pick up a milk crate full of bottles, so, not everything is perfectly simulated.

Getting used to your VR body having weight is hard, but its what this whole game is about. I've played plenty of games with VR bodies, but they were all cosmetic. It's fun, but since it takes this concept so seriously, you will need to practice well. I kinda ran through the tutorial and got stuck on certain actions, but I get it all know. I LOVE climbing in this game. You wouldn't want to scale a huge Climbey style mountain this way, but for climbing up a Aperture science testing chamber wall by its piping, awww, its so cool. I wish we could add more force feedback though, so I'd be more reactive to things my hand bumps into in the world.

I like the shoes the protagonist wears.

The puzzling is...hard...for me..because puzzles are hard for me, and the style (and scale) is all new. THey all turn out to be simpler in the end, and I've starting to see how some of it is optional, and I can now find myself climbing around and fudging my way through, which is pretty cool and is encouraged by the game. That being said, like many others I got stuck on the first garage door opening puzzle. It's not a puzzle at all, it's just a switch in the other room many people will miss. However, I spent like an hour trying to force that door open with the pallets and the jack and stuff. I could pry it open a good 8 inchs with that stuff, then kept trying to bend down and pull it out, but it would fly out of my hands. Killed my knees! A patch should make that process of opening the door work. A random twitter user had the same experience as me.

After you do a puzzle you get to shoot something, but that fun is usually over quick and it's back to puzzling. There might be less gun play in this game than we might have thought. I don't really mind, I very much see this as an adventure game, with points a and b. The enemies, however, at last the ones without guns, are basically just cannon fodder, because they (like you sometimes) cannot walk up stairs properly. It's whatever I quess, because they are scary, and the later enemies with the guns do pose a signicant threat. I wonder what the SuperHot enemies would sound like if they spoke.

Disregard the naysayers that say we need a save system. Don't give in to their demands. A save game system would ruin the sense of danger, and when you die, you just have to run back to were you were, not actually restart anything. During this process of return, I imagine many others like me will explore side areas they did not before and find new stuff. Clearly this is how its supposed to work, people.

What's up with the hand model? It's very unique, and the photo realistic texture style looks great in VR. I think that style created an uncanny valley on PC, but should be investigated for VR. He has a very angled thumb joint, (like Arnold Swarzenegger) I wonder if this was down for a technical reason.

I play on Index, which is why this game is fun. WindowsMR had trouble at launch but that should be sorted, and I hear the Oculus touch controllers work well. I will try this with my old Vive wands, and while I love them dearly (and am a touchpad man) I expect their poor grip button might have finally met it's match with this game. Those VR accessory people should fix it with some plastic.

Boneworks and is priced fantastically well. Given the hype train and that, they might have made less money by charging 40 dollars for it, and not 30, but its clearly worthy of that high price. I think Stress Level Zero, like me, believe that, for some reason, good VR games should be priced at 30 bucks. Most of the games that cost more than that arent worth the price, and given that it's VR, are huge gambles. Paranormal Activity was a 40$ game at launch, but it was clearly a 30 dollar affair. 2d pancake players should continue to play 60$ for their games they mostly just complain about on youtube anyway, while saying VR is too expensive. Burn.

I might have run out of thoughts, it doesnt matter. This game will frustrate you a bit at first, but its just so damn cool, and having your VR body become a first class object in the game, while cumbersome at times, or even annoying, is just so wild and cool and adds to the immersion like I've never seen. I found my self doing such weird body actions in VR like Ive never done. Dragging things behind me, and looking around for its clearance, noticing every option in the game for traversal etc. Not since I played through Alien Isolation in VR have I felt so immersed. I easily clocked two hours with the headset on my first play. While I consider alot of the points made on Road To Vr's review to be valid, I was jarred by their 3/5 score, and didn't think their want for more guns or a narrative were relavant. Ergo, this review is the best review of the game.

Loving the look of this game. People say it emulated off Portal or Half-Life 2. They are both wrong! The look of this game is actually based off the Portal+Half-Life 2 noir puzzling hybrid mod called The Trap 2 Mindlock, available on Steam for free. Learn your scholors VR people! This game does look like all that stuff and its great, and the music is proper too....except for the one that plays out of the radios scattered everywhere. It's out of place, and too jovial. It's like if all the radios in portal played the ending cake song, instead of that lovely muffled jazz bit. I don't think you can change the song on those things, I'll double check. You also can't make the vending machines dispense soda, which you know I tried a thousand times to be sure. This is a signifcant oversight.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played a lot more of this game since my original review, and some updates have come out that have smoothed out a lot of the experience, so I'm updating my review to talk about what's changed, and why I can now safely recommend it.

Some of the following is un-updated, showing my initial big gripes with the game. When I do have an update, youll see *UPDATE* heading up and trailing after my new thoughts on each subject.

----

4 Big Gripes, otherwise a very solid VR game:

1. Climbing doesn't match your arms 1:1, which makes the character wobble all over the place in-game, making for a very dizzying experience. Taking off the headset after beating the final level and I am definitely nauseous. And I've owned VR since the CV1 days so I have great VR legs. Definitely need to fix climbing.

*UPDATE* So a recent patch came out that "says" to have stabilized the climbing. I've played with it a little bit more and honestly it still feels really springy, at least on Rift. Maybe for other headsets it's changed and feels more solid, but here I still feel pretty nauseous climbing. Maybe not quite as much but it still feels like a major chore.
*UPDATE*

2. Inventory slots are too small (and not general purpose enough). In my playthrough, and in playthroughs I've seen on youtube, people are constantly dropping objects that they intended to put in an inventory slot (anywhere, pistol, rifle, item slot, etc) and then they have to pick it up and try again. I think part of the problem is the slots are actually on the back, not up near the shoulder like all other VR games. Just reposition and make the area slightly bigger (and on devices that support it, make them vibrate more) and it should solve that problem. Also, trying to grab the crowbar from the right shoulder slot with the left arm gets the crowbar instantly stuck around your neck. Would be useful to sort that out.

*UPDATE*
So this has pretty much been mitigated as I've played it more. I don't know if any changes have been made, but I've grown accustomed to the slot placements and find myself flinging objects instead of storing them much, much less often. However, walking forwards while trying to grab a magazine from across your hips is still difficult. I can feel like I'm in the area to grab a mag and half the time it just won't work unless I stand still (or walk backwards). Could use a slightly bigger collider there.
*UPDATE*

3. SANDBOX MODE IS LOCKED AT START. NOTE: IF YOU'RE BUYING THIS GAME JUST FOR THE SANDBOX, DONT. I've beaten the game, and played some of the arena mode, and I still have no idea how to unlock sandbox mode, which is what I was so excited for! A major disappointment that the vertical slice wasn't available right at the start, I know a lot of people are going to get turned away by that fact. Poor game design choice.

*UPDATE*
So it's been covered online now, but the only way to unlock sandbox mode is through a test being answered completely correctly at the beginning of the game. I still think it's a bad design choice, but it doesn't take too long to unlock once you know what to do.
*UPDATE*

4. Dual-hand gun holding and the mag pul grab points are too close to each other on all the weapons, making it almost impossible to do the one you want to do. I can't tell you how many times on all the weapons I've tried to grab the second handle only to pull the mag out of the gun, often dropping it on the ground in the process. It's especially noticeable on the pistols.

*UPDATE*
I've gotten a bit better at this as time goes on, but I think it's still a bit of an issue. They have the amazing magazine-to-magazine pull method implimented which I think works perfectly, I think it'd be great to be able to disable manual magazine grabbing to make dual holding easier.
*UPDATE*

and I guess a 5th. This game was being sold as a narrative story-driven adventure built upon the Boneworks physics engine... But this is basically a tech demo. Sure, it's long, and sure, it has some cool moments near the end, but all in all, 90% of the game was boilerplate. The story didn't include any npc's of any kind until the last like 30 minutes, save for the real life humans on televisions telling you what to do, which you can't interact with aside from breaking the monitors to shut them up. The story is thin teetering on the point of non-existent, which if the gameplay made up for I'd be fine with, but the climbing, gun problems and inventory management issues combined made for a sore shooter experience. EXPERIENCE. Not a story-driven campaign.

*UPDATE*
Still am not impressed by the lack of story, but the Arena mode has really grown on me. Being able to try out doing different things for different waves of enemies is really fun, even if none of them can do anything except shoot me or slap me with their blob hands. Not quite as mad about the lack of story anymore. Besides, Game Theory has made up for it with their awesome videos on the game. As I talked about the meta down below. ^^
*UPDATE*

Not to say the game doesn't have lore oozing out all sides! It's incredibly meta, includes bits and pieces from SL0's previous games that are supposed to "tie" both this and Duck Season together... but honestly I don't care. that's out of VR research, not VR gameplay, and certainly not a VR story-driven game. Just sad to see something so simple put in place for all the wonderful graphics strewn throughout the game.

Anyway, all in all, I did still have a blast, so if anyone wants a quirky, yet lovable and ultimately passable VR shooter, then go on ahead. For those of us who were expecting either A) A great story, or B) And incredible sandbox with guns, then I have to say no, I can't recommend it for now. The bugs, plus sandbox still being locked after defeating the entire campaign, just leaves me in a sore spot that I don't know if SL0 can, or would, fix.

*UPDATE*
Overall, I still don't think this game is VR's saving grace, but especially with the save point system now added in and more tweaks coming in the future, as well as new content coming in free updates, SL0 is on the path to having an exceptional shooter on their hands, and is well worth the money spent on it. (Please Brandon add enemies with melee weapons PLEASE)
