\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you have VR and a good set of "VR legs," you're going to want to try this game. It pushes the envelope when it comes to interactivity in gaming, and it's worth seeing through the occasional physics-driven jankiness to experience this peak at the true potential of VR. The campaign took me around 12 hours (steam crashed during one play session so it says 10) and kept things nice and fresh throughout. I was driven to complete it, looking forward to seeing each new level (which is fairly rare for VR.) The last level teases you with some incredible tools, which I've read will now be accessible in the rest of the game upon replaying, so there's some replay value. I haven't touched the arena mode yet either, so I can't say how valuable that will be.

There's minor issues found throughout the game, with the most notable being its lack of mid-level saving. The 2nd level of the game is really long and I found out the hard way that progress wasn't being saved. Holstering guns/melee weapons is off and you'll grab the wrong thing (or nothing) have the time, or drop your gun to the floor when you mean to pocket it. The physics will bug out, you'll randomly be flung into a wall, or your arm will get stuck in an object (which is surprisingly distressing.) Climbing is slow and wobbly, prone to making you nauseous. A lot of these stem from the full body physics system, so I can accept them as growing pains of progress. They're the furthest things on my mind when I'm jumping through the air in slow motion, firing twin micro-uzis at waves of clumsy null-bodies.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is not for everyone. It can make you sick and it has some jank due to its heavy reliance on physics. If you get into it, Boneworks is a great game focused on exploration, action and story telling. Its strong points are a very detailed physics sandbox, great gunplay and mechanics, fantastic soundtrack and overall sound design.
However, the levels can be a bit long and there are no checkpoints to save and come back to if you have to stop playing during a level. If you forgive its small issues like occasional bug and sometimes tedious item collecting, Boneworks is a new milestone in VR interactions and physics and a killer value for its price.\t\t\t\t\t

The highs:
Combat feels really nice!
The atmosphere is a very unique one to ones I am usually playing around in.

Definately good if you have the valve index controllers.

The next thing speaking of reasons i had to replay, glitches with the vive, im not sure what happened but sometimes my arm would just...not move? I'm not sure if this is the games fault or if its something involving my set up, but i never had this happen on other games so I'm gonna place it as the game? But i cant say for sure

But otherwise i think it is a really fun experience, makes me wish i was a little more fit xD\t\t\t\t\t

BONEWORKS is honestly worth every penny and more. I would suggest purchasing the BONEBUNDLE to show some love to Michael Wyckoff for the AMAZING soundtrack and the entire team at Stress Level Zero.\t\t\t\t\t
