The fact that literally everything in the world can be interacted with in a way that you would expect it to be should be enough of a selling point alone. A MUST HAVE FOR ANY VR HEADSET OWNER!
Warning- You will need to ease yourself into the full controller / body locomotion to build your tolerance.
Close your eyes or squint when falling, sprinting, going upstairs or downstairs, or any fast motion.
Eventually you will become used to it and it will be comfortable.

Full review:

This is the first true VR "game".
I can tell the developers know this because they flaunt it and taunt other games by having "What if this is just a tech demo" scribbled on the walls in a dark souls esq previous user fashion.

As of writing this review I have played the game for about 12 and a half hours (with 30 mins of afk time) and I STILL have not finished the campaign. I believe I am near the end.
Not only is the game lengthy like a single player game should be, but it is an absolute joy to play with a ton of replayability due to the infinite possibilites in some situations with the physics.

Some of the funnest things I have done in this game other than playing through it include:
1) Grabbing a headcrab (I hate them and you will too!), and trying to shove it into a trash bin. When I realized it wouldn't fit, I took a sledgehammer, placed the crab on top of the bin and hammered it into the trash bin, then continued to hammer the bin itself so that thing never gets out again.
2) Doing pull-ups while hanging above a drop that would surely kill me.
3) skipping small pieces of the puzzle areas by climbing and using the full body physics system
4) Getting jumpscared by head crabs so hard my gun literally disapeared as far as I could tell
00) I can't list all of the fun things you can do with the physics. Play the game with an imagination and you will do something noone else has.

The gunplay is amazing and so is the melee combat. The graphics are okay, however; they are carried by the fantastic world scaling, clarity, art work, and smooth vr motion. When you are in the VR world the graphics themselves actually come in 2nd to these factors when it comes to visuals.

cons- save points are sometimes a full hour apart, it doesn't have multiplayer, various small bugs sprinkled around. That's literally it.

Anyway, if you own a VR headset you have to buy it, you have no choice in the matter :p\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn amazing VR game through and through.

Feature-length campaign (took me 12hrs). Amazing physics and interactivity. Immersive firearms. Simulated weights. This game stands out as fun and immersive for 95% of your time within it.

The biggest snag with this game is the general lack of difficulty. Most enemies pose an absolutely minor threat, ammunition is so plentiful that it may as well be infinite, and some of the puzzles prove to be little more than a slow down on your progression through the level. Also, I can't forget to mention the lack of a save feature. The only way to save is to complete the level. Each level averages around 1 hour to 1 hour 15 mins. If you start feeling sick, tired, or otherwise have to stop playing, you lose all your progress in the level, and have to start from the beginning, which can feel like an absolute drag, having to backtrack.

Overall though, if you own a VR system, you should own this game, hands down. Sure it has a few minor snags, but ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ this game is impressive.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA VR noob's review:


I got an Oculus Rift S literally 3 days ago and have spent ~24 total hours in VR. Thankfully, I found that I do not get motion sick while playing, but I completely understand what others have been saying about this game and how trippy this physics-based movement is. The first time I looked down and walked forwards, I almost fell backwards since my brain tried to correct for the animating legs moving away from me. That went away after about 10 minutes of walking through the tutorial level though.


I've seen reviews saying that this game isn't fun without the Index Controllers. Since this is my first foray into VR, I can only speak for the Oculus inside-out tracking controllers and I think this game works great with them. Use the wrist straps though. Since parts of some levels require throwing things (or at least make it more fun if you do throw things) and the Oculus controllers require you to grip the palm of the controller, the controllers can sometimes get away from you. Besides that, the rest of the mechanics work flawlessly without the fancy finger tracking, so don't worry about that.


In terms of game play itself, I find I can't get enough. It's not a perfectly polished high-budget title, but it gets the job done. The guns, specifically the assault rifles and guns with a stock can be hard to aim since the stock can bump against your in-game body's shoulder and wobble the barrel. Hitting hollow objects like a trash can with other objects such as an axe or sword can often make them glitch together, requiring you to jump around and flail your arms to separate them from each other. Trying to climb over a ledge makes you wish the game tracked your feet since your legs dangle helplessly while your arms scramble around. But with all those weird mechanics, I can't get enough. The level of immersion the physical in-game body gives you is unparalleled and it makes other highly-rated puzzly combat games I've tried like Budget Cuts and Arizona Sunshine feel like last-gen concepts.

My only real complaint is the lack of custom checkpoints, but Stress Level Zero said that is coming, so that reservation will soon be gone.


So if you're on the fence about getting this game since you don't have an Index or have read some of the other reviews that slagged off the glitchier aspects of the game, I say try it for yourself and you'll be hooked. It really is that good.\t\t\t\t\t
