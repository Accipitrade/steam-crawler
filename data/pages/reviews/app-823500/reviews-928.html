\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best games I've played in VR, truly. They tweaked the physics a bit to reduce the wobbliness of two-handed weapons and generally heavy items. The game has a decent length to it, as long as you're searching for all of the collectibles meant for the sandbox rooms and ammo for the vending machine at the end of the game (however I used a save config editor so i could get ammo for those because I can't be bothered to grind for ammo in a sandbox game). It's also tedious trying to manage your inventory so that, when you get to the end of the level, you can throw all of the new things you didn't have before into the reclamation bin. Wish there was an actual inventory system meant for stuff like that.

The biggest downside for this game, is that there is no real modding support. No steam workshop. There's reskin mods that change the way weapons and character models look, but that's about it. This game would benefit SO much if there was modding support and would extend it's life beyond the games expiration.

Also, if the devs read this, please add a manual eject bind for guns. Not the menu-then-eject, or place the magazine next to the magwell for the empty mag to fall out, or pull the mag out first. I can't tell if there's an actual wrist-flick eject when i twist my wrist with an empty gun, sometimes the mag will actually fly out. But its a 6 in 10 chance that it happens. Not reliable in combat.

Otherwise, this game has the best physics in VR currently. The gunplay is satisfying, the slow-mo feature makes it better. The plot is... there, if you look for it (Game Theory has videos on it), and the physical presence you have in the game feels the most accurate compared to other VR titles.

Would highly recommend.\t\t\t\t\t
