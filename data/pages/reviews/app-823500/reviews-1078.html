\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a single player Valve game on an Indie budget, basically. Hell yes I recommend it, this is one of "*The* games" that comes out for a platform that's basically an obligation because of what it represents as a showcase for a platform's capabilities.

This is a game full of "The little things", like being able to climb on basically anything that looks climbable, all of the moment to moment mechanics that reward high skilled players, etc. If I listed them all here this review would be way longer than it needs to be but I'll just say I hope that mechanics in this game become VR gold standards in other games, and I look forward to seeing how speedrunners completely break this game apart in the future. There's secret ammo boxes in places you would never expect the devs intended you to go with climbing for example, which makes me wonder about things I missed, making me want to replay the campaign sometime.

Now, the highly polished feeling campaign clocks in at about 6 hours and leaves off on a decidedly unsatisfying ending unless we're getting a Boneworks 2 or a DLC in the near future, but wait, don't go, there's more to a game than it's campaign's length, it's got replayability in it and post-game challenges that are actually really fun. It's got a survival mode and specific challenges, a sandbox mode, and MAYBE unofficial modding coming in the future.

All in all, I wanna see more games like this for VR.

My only complaints with the game are sometimes the AI kinda bugs out and grabbing onto ledges feels needlessly wobbly for such an otherwise intuitive feeling parkour system.

Another thing, Stress Level Zero is NOT "Merely making a suggestion" when they say, unless you have your "VR Legs", hold off on playing this game. It has a few really fun setpieces in the campaign that involve breaking several conventional rules of motion sickness prevention and I can't recommend this to VR newcomers. Luckily, I never got motion sick in VR past week 2 of using it, so all those were to me were super fun like a roller coaster ride.

Aside from that, this is definitely worth $30 IMO.\t\t\t\t\t
