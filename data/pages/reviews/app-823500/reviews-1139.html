\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is the best VR game you have ever played, if you never played any other VR title in your life.

Boneworks is an experimental physics driven story based VR experience which tackles multiple VR functions, such as full body simulation, physics based puzzles, physics based melee combat, and firearm systems.

Now, while I see this game as a passion project in itself, with a story that has been put a lot of care and love into, I must admit that it fails to deliver the things it promised, or at least the things it hyped up its crowd for.

The systems mentioned above is all done on a mediocre level, and are outdone by quite a lot of other games, where they nailed these systems flawlessly. Blade and Sorcery wins over the melee here with lighyears. The climbing and traversing is atrocious compared to the one mentioned above, or Onward. Pavlov and Zero Caliber pretty much wins over the fight over firearms and shooting as well, leaving only the story [which's flaws are being patched right at this moment] as the only part of that game that can save itself.

At the end, I'm part disappointed, part satisfied. The game might be a huge mess, but it still is a good experience. It's constalt issues like yanky physics and performance issues can be looked over, which makes this game good enough for me to recommend it. I'm patiently waiting for the devs to gradually patch all the flaws and end up somewhat on a No Man's Sky level progression.

On the flipside though, don't get this game if you have played the ones mentioned above; you are in for a series of disappointments, if the mechanics matter to you as much as they do for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's pretty cool, but honestly all the physics-based stuff is more frustrating than fun. The combat is GREAT though, I absolutely love it. It's so visceral to nail a rigidbody in the head and kill him in one hit, or headshot him from a reasonable distance.

Movement, platforming, and puzzles in general are janky as ♥♥♥♥ though. Feels like the only way to perform a jump is to just keep trying until the stupid physics stop being jank and do what you tell them to. It's not that they're HARD, far from it, but like... moving the pallet jack is so frustrating. I have very little play space, so walking around to maneuver it properly isn't really an option.

There also seems to be a lot of.. "useless" areas. For example, at the end of the Streets level, past the reclamation bin, there's about 4 rigidbodies (the enemies) standing around. I kill one, and a few more pop out from a container. I kill them all and enter the building they were in front of, only to find... nothing. Just a couple of boxes, which I all broke, and all of them were empty. What's even the point of this space? Feels useless, and downright confusing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are many things that I like and don't like about this game. a little background I have been playing VR games since early 2017, I have Roughly 300 hours ( Not all on steam many hours on Oculus back when I had one before I got the Index not sure exactly how many hours so ball parking about 300 hours) of shooter / gun games in VR.

Pros:
I enjoyed the story, it was easy at times and difficult at others so there was a good mix of challenge and how to overcome each obstacle.

I had fun being creative when going against opponents I had many ways of fighting them if it was with a brick to using a sword all the way to going and just shooing them.

The physics in this game were really cool to play around with. With these physics you can be very creative on how you go around and complete each challenge.

Cons:
As cool as I think the guns are I wish there were more options to choose from along with some customization for them.

I would like to see a Fire Selector for some of the guns (I like to use M4/ M16 platform rifles but I don't want to use an M4 in exclusively full auto sometimes it is not always necessary though. (Personally I prefer Semi-auto so i have slightly more control over the gun as well as it helps make my shots a bit more accurate.

I thought the Sandbox mode is a bit under whelmed content wise. I understand that you can add items from the campaign with the save for later scan bit thing but I wish there was more added to it from the start.

I can't stand the fence climbing and regular climbing, it was difficult at time to get over obstacles making the campaign more difficult than it needed to be at times.

I have had many instances of items that I picked up to glitch out or get stuck in other objects but I think that is a known problem that I believe is going to be fixed.

Overall the game is playable and you can have fun playing it but I wish that the Devs would add more content overall and could fix some of the bugs with the game.

Personally I think it is a good tech demo but needs some work before I play it again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCurrently my favorite game at the moment in VR and have gotten about half way through. Being in this game just gave me one of the best feelings of immersion due to the ability to interact with assets and physics in such a way that other games have fallen short. Though not perfect in all situations, for instance climbing over walls still felt clumsy with your legs and feet getting in the way, and the way the first person view would wobble erratically while moving around and scaling walls and pipes, but you get used to it and after awhile, it gets a little easier.

The scenes and maps are not richly detailed yet the atmosphere, sound FX, and analog synth music made up for it somewhat. Little details, messages left on walls, sound effects reminded me of the feeling I first had when playing the Portal series, the robotic head crabs, and machine guns with laser sweeps, and of course the crowbar, all added to that eerie feeling of familiarity, yet you knew it was a completely different world and you were all alone in it to fend for yourself. The null men, zombies and other enemies were challenging enough to dispatch, with various weapons, guns, and melee physics. This is where the game shined in how you were able to interact with the environment and had to solve various physics puzzles within the scenes. The game almost encourages you to find cheats and hacks within your environment so it allows you to explore around, climbing over walls and barricades, moving objects around and using them in unorthodox ways. There were a few times that I wound up falling through the map and having to restart the game.

I didn't like that save points aren't built in and you have to redo the entire map over if you die or quit the game especially after having gotten near the end after a long session. Hopefully this is something that the developers can add in future updates. I'd say this game belongs in everyone's library who has a VR system, despite its minor flaws and imperfections. The feeling you get after you stop and the anticipation of wanting to go back into this mysterious world to see where you wind up next, the enigmatic twists and turns, the guns, physics and weapons handling are the best in class at the moment.

I'm playing using the Vive Pro and Index Controllers running with an i7-9700k, 32gb ram, a 2080ti, and had no issues with performance, as graphics aren't particularly demanding. This and Stormland are my two favorite VR games in 2019. I'm looking forward to Half Life: Alyx in 2020!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a game that I (among many) have been anticipating for quite some time. After my first 4 or so hours I said something along the lines of "...I'm whelmed by it. It hasn't disappointed, but hasn't exceeded my expectations." I stand by that quote as why it is true that Boneworks is fundamentally brilliant. The existence of more specialized games does drag the experience down. The story...well it never really pulled me; it's mostly up to interpretation and not really put forth to the best of it's ability. It wasn't really a deciding factor for me in the first place though as I have never really cared for Stress Level Zero's overarching storytelling. The story also suffers from some odd tonal changed near the end. It felt like someone said "Our level design is quite bland, lets do something really different." and while it is a nice idea; thinking back on it, it really destroyed the borderline horror aspect of the story.

Gameplay is...uh mixed. Lets start with the puzzles. Outside the large lack of them (or at least forced puzzles), they just felt kinda meh. The mechanics of the game feel as if you're breaking these puzzles rather than outsmarting the developers. There's a fine line between these two and I felt Boneworks missed that mark. But what about the combat? Well...it's okay. It's not brilliant as it took too much from several different styles and never really perfected them. Guns are the most fun thing to use in the game, but there is a serious lack of variety within these weapons; as well as a serious lack of true control over them. HHH is a great example of what I believe guns should be like in VR. Being able to lock the slide and eject magazines fluidly and without much thought. This felt like a video game gun in that you could only really reload and shoot, rather than feeling like a true firearm. They do feel really nice though, nice sounds and kickback, using them feels almost spot on in terms of point, shoot and don't miss. Melee combat is lackluster; there's no two ways to put it. Weapons that are supposed to feel heavy, feel that they hit without impact. Bladed weapons don't have satisfying ways to use them as they slide in your hand, with cuts being infinitely less effective than they should feel. Maybe it's my 10x more hours in Blade & Sorcery that make this feel cheap, but it just doesn't come close. Hand to hand combat is...well annoying. As someone who is used to feeling a good bit stronger in VR. Feeling useless with my bare hands felt bad. Not being able to push over enemies very easily without slamming them into the ground like a wrestler was more annoying than fun. Movement is excellent. Easily some of the best climbing in a VR game I've ever seen. Again only really compared to B&S; but this is different and in my opinion: suits the gameplay better than B&S style climbing would. Being able to crouch and stand using the joystick (on index controllers) is a godsend. Jumping, walking, running and climbing all feel great and is a strong suit of the game.

Animations are gorgeous. Like holy crap does this game just feel and look right. Being able to grab items and weapons in so many different ways feels like a big improvement. Enemies walking looks realistic as they trip and fall over objects (to be fair they are basically zombies). Though this has a serious caveat; when you have so many different ways to hold things, when you can't hold something the way you want...it feels cheap and annoying. You have so many options that when you feel limited; it's very noticeable. I also have to admit. I find the enemy design boring and lazy. These enemies are not all that intimidating or fun to look at. Yes they're scary when you first meet them, and a certain small enemy will scare you to the core when you encounter them. But I never had that: "I NEED to murder that RIGHT NOW" feeling that I find synonymous with good enemy design.

I know I just complained a lot about the game, but that's because the negatives really stick out to me. It's a great game; easily up there with the likes of B&S, Gorn & Beat Saber, but I can see why this game may disappoint some. The game feels (meme alert) like a tech demo for something that will be utterly amazing: the Half-Life/Portal of VR (mind you that Alyx exists now). I should also note that the game is for experienced VR users and those who get sick in VR may find this game difficult to play even when sitting down. And if you: the person reading this, have a set of Index Controllers, then I'd recommend this even more. It's what I played the game with and while they can be finicky; they improve the experience considerably as you rarely fight the controls like you may with the Vive wands. I also cannot forget (outside the fact that I did) about they abhorrent save system. This game requires you to finish a level in one go; if you die, you respawn at a random point in the level which is generally the start. You don't even replay the section; the level only resets if you leave the level entirely. Dying makes you run through all the bits you've already done. And because you can't save the game; if you cannot complete the level (which can be upwards of an hour and half long on your first playthrough) then you will restart from the beginning. It is unacceptable.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs a good game, but actually hard to recommend. I love a lot of what it does and would want everyone to experience it, but not as their introductory game to vr.

The biggest issue is the climbing. Smooth locomotion never bothered me, "parkour" never bothered me, falling never bothered me, heck even stuff like "Ultimate Booster Experience" I'm fine with... but this climbing man... I think its an excellent example of "just because you can doesnt mean you should", or to put another way, I feel its an example of an amazing physics system getting in the way of game play. Non ladder climbing feels downright awful due to the head bobbing and your vr hands constantly getting stuck on things because "muh physics interactions". I've been slowly getting used to it, but good god I wish they'd tone down the physics while climbing a notch or three.

I'm still not fully sold on inverse kinematics versus floating hands, but it at least doesnt bother me so much anymore. Maybe with full body tracking it would be really good? Anyway, I do wish we had a body type adjustment much like height adjustment though, the quick menu can be a bit wonky at times due to the virtual body being slimmer than myself.
