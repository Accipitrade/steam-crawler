\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is definitely one of the most innovative VR games out there, it's dare I say "ultramersive". However it's not exactly a seamless experience and you have to learn about the games eccentricities and limitations. When storing weapons behind my back for example, it's natural for me to want to use my dominant hand no matter if it's the left or right side, but since tracking isn't that great behind your back on the Quest/2, it would usually use the right slot no matter what. So if I wanted to stow or retrieve something from the left side, I would use my left hand. Obviously this isn't a problem with the game itself, but its one of the few things that remind you that the game wasn't designed for the mid tier setup. I feel like this is a fair criticism, especially given that the Valve Index has under 2% market share.

Then there is the sandbox aspect of the game. God, I love that kind of stuff, and I thought there would be a lot more of it. Apparently the people over at Stress Level Zero are very proud of their story (and rightfully so, but I will get to that), because they really want to lock you down into their campaign. I've modded it and illegitimately unlocked everything since beating the campaign, and that was a great choice.

Coming in from a place of knowing absolutely nothing about the meta going into the game, not playing SLZ's previous game Duck Season or really even expecting to have to search for the lore, the campaign got incredibly confusing yet still enjoyable. I managed to piece together the main ideas from the game's blunt story telling but a lot of it was lost on me. It's too bad that the developers couldn't manage to make the whole game a standalone experience to anyone whose last name isn't Holmes, because then it would be even more captivating.

Anyways, even though I don't have fancy haptic gloves, 256 sqft of play area, an RTX or a 10gbps link, I still had a ton of fun with the game and continue to, when I have time.

9.99/10, docking 0.01 because the first "whaddup son" is funny and the 173rd is still somehow funny and that frustrates me\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn my experience, Boneworks is a very fun and very intense game when it works, however there may be times where you could experience what I call "Jank". Physics in the game being weird and floaty, grabbing things feeling snappy and sticky (and that goes for anything, such as weapons, walls, enemies etc.).

Other than that, there's a lot of replayability in this game. There's items and objects for you to collect for later use in sandbox levels where you can truly do whatever you want, arena modes that are quite intense and fully use the combat, which is 10/10 for me, and a campaign that is pretty good imo, although very convoluted and at times a bit hard to follow.

Overall tho? I find this game to be an 8/10. Would be a 9/10 if it wasn't for the weird jank you might experience and the other cons I mentioned. I recommend this game for those who want a pretty good VR experiences in terms of physics, combat, climbing and more!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is honestly a mess of a game that I regret buying and further regret keeping. The physics engine is weighty and clunky which sounds like a good thing for some but there is so much lag and wiggle on weapons its hard to use them, Trying to hit something well with an axe or anything sucks. Bladed weapons are hard to use because its impossible to get good edge alignment, Blunt weapons are slow and unrealistic.(The worst offender to me of the melee weapons is the longsword which is SUPPOSED to be an agile light weapon to quickly dispatch enemies with thrusts cuts and bashes but its so heavy and slow you might as well not even bother except for collection) So the only real good strategy for melee combat is a brick or a knife for stabbing. the guns themselves are meh but the game basically insists that you duel wield pistol and smgs(by constantly putting them in pairs together) and even though its a bad tactic and reduces your efficiency I still really like duel wielding so its not a bad thing that they recommend it its just unimmersive how they did it, HOWEVER this game implements it very poorly as its difficult to reload both weapons in any form of agile manner. Thats because you cant reload while weapons are holstered and even if you could (because who knows maybe you can but the game hates me or something) EVEN THEN if you could do it your body is so unstable and works against you that it would be almost impossible to do quickly or consistently. Plus this game is REALLY not 1 arm friendly you need 2 arms to play this game without struggle(Personally I have 2 arms but I test 1 arm capability in every game I play for both left only and right only). In contrast look at a game like gun club VR. A game with fantastic 1 arm capability(for both left and right) and fantastic duel wielding potential, Why? because it has a consistent stable platform to place your guns in the center of your chest with an easy to reach ammo pouch so not only can you quickly or at the very least consistently reload 2 weapons at a solid pace but you can do it easily. That is a SIMPLE immersive consistent way to more easily allow your game access to 1 armed people and duel wielding in the game. "But Blood you don't own Gun Club VR" I played it on the Oculus Quest. And the rifles have 3 static grip points for your second hand. Infront of the mag on the forgrip and the barrel. However the one closest to the mag is unyieldy and the one on the barrel is unusable(who knows maybe my arms are just too short) on top of that the one on the forgrip is a static position which honestly isnt that big of a deal I just figured I would state that. Another big issue I have with the rifles is that when holding the forgrip you cant pull the charging handle to load the chamber you HAVE to be holding the main-grip Plus the rifles are the best melee weapons in the game(Long and light weapons can break ammo boxes in one hit killing most nullbodies in 1 hit) so why bother with the normal melee weapons other than the knives or the fire axe(which despite what I said earlier is one of the better ones for some reason) Story sucks and is boring but hey that's personal preference but hey that's my opinion on the story. OH YEAH and the game forces you to climb at some points and its a very significant part of the game... BUT ITS AWEFUL your body gets stuck on everything and you need to constantly switch between crouched and standing(Which luckily is just something you do with the right controller) but its so weighty slow and difficult to do, the only thing it did do right is because of using the right controller to crouch the body it is "easy" to get on top of things when climbing... you know when it wants to that is and doesn't decide to slide you backwards. Plus **((REALLY MINOR SPOILER))** at the end of the game you have to climb a MASSIVE ladder which is guaranteed to put a sharp pain in your neck. On top of that there is like 10 crowbars around the place leading up to the ladder 1 on the ladder and one at the top of the ladder **((SPOILER OVER))** Another thing is the enemy types. There are 2 enemy types which pose 0 threat 1 type that poses some threat but are overall really easy and weak just annoying. a semi balanced enemy type that provides a challenge but isnt unfair. and then 1 BS enemy who can kill you easily and is just a version the 0 threat enemies except now their hand glows and they shoot digital fireballs and they are super annoying.

Overall rating is 2/10 It has its moments of fun but overall is a janky annoying mess that isn't immersive. I don't recommend it at all. Not even if it was free... which it isn't.\t\t\t\t\t
