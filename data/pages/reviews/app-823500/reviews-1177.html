\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHands down the best VR experience out there. In terms of game-play I really have nothing negative to say. There are some cons to the game however. My biggest complaint is that there are basically no checkpoints. You complete a level in one go or you start from the beginning. It's easier each time around, but some of these levels are insanely long. I'm on the Streets and by the time I'm an hour in my OG Vive is melting my face. I just want to beat the game so I have access to the other game modes.

I also want to add that you should have plenty of room. I play in about a 6x10 and it's tough. The enemies in this game will sneak up on you and they will scare you. Keep in mind in those moments you are likely to punch a wall or a monitor while trying to push these rapey zombies off of you. My controllers and hands have taken a beating to the point I now I need to buy new controllers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat VR-game/sandbox, well worth the 25 bucks.

Some people complain about it being short and storming through it in 2 hours. I guess that's technically possible if you speedrun, only go for the portals/level-transitions and bypass all the enemies/puzzles/extras/creative solutions, which ,yeah, would make this game short and unimpressive.

The story is kinda out there and has a kinda "Show don't tell"-philosophy to it, so it's easily missed if you don't pay attention to it.

What it does have in droves is the sense of "This is the new standard of VR-interaction in games", and for that it receives my wholehearted recommendation. The recurring joke ingame is the sentence "Is this a tech-demo?" as it does have the trappings one such in terms of the artificial environment, polygonized enemies and on-the-nose physics-puzzles. Although it manages to make this aesthetic its own, I personally can't wait for somebody else to take this physics-model and run with it in a big way with a grand real-world narrative, organic surroundings/enemies, and situations that make you fear for your virtual life and not your virtual-virtual life, looking at you Half-Life Alyx.

The only comparable physics-model in VR right now is the one from Blade and Sorcery, and that's saying something, because it's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ brilliant.\t\t\t\t\t

there are some pretty lame faults that make this close to being unplayable.

The arms are wildly unnatural and distract a lot from game play. Hands are constantly getting "stuck" on stuff, for example if you reach past something and a pipe, box or valve is in the way your hand gets tuck behind it, constantly. Moving anything has to be done slooowwwwllllyyyyyy or you will have problems, this sucks, because I want to play the damn game not pretend I am still IRL
this isn's the holodeck. Some of the early attempts at adding to the IRL immersion is failing hard.
The arms waving around like spaghetti noodles does not help

The worst part for me is one I was really hoping would be the best, the crowbar. Swinging this thing around is like trying to hit something with a wet noodle. There is no explanation of "suspending reality and pretending it has weight" until later in the first level. Even with that explanation, NO, I am not going to "pretend" something has weight. That does not improve my being immersed in the game, it ruins it. If I am swing a crow bar at a box in real life it is stiff arm and wrist and not some weird mess of spaghetti noodle arms and broken wrists... the lack of realism KILLED having fun breaking objects. Also all the boxes were empty soooo... sad.
The sledge hammer two handed wasn't as bad, but i can swing a crowbar fast in real life, I don't need to pretend it has weight and swing it slow. VERY BAD. and considering this is THE CROWBAR seriously wtf here.

I dislike that there are all of these awesome running legs around but when your avatar runs it is clearly not the same mechanic at all. just those awkward feet shuffling even faster than before

Minor complaint similar to Arizona Sunshine, though this was fixed with a knuckles remap. In AS you could not pick up a gun with grip and then let go with out dropping it, this meant you had to drop the gun to do anything and the holsters for spares were useless because you had to drop the gun to grab a dif from holsters. Same crap problem here, I do not want to have to hold on to the crow bar with a death grip for hours! Nor do I want to set it down to open drawers. But that is down to the level of realism someone is wanting, and I am hoping it can be changed with a knuckles remap.

The game is interesting, and has some cool mechanics, and I have arguably not played much. I have only got as far as the first puzzle, past all the intros. I will say that more than anything the dis-function of the avatar, the noodle arms and the broken wrists when swinging the crowbar killed it for me though. I hope that gets fixed :/

not being able to grab ledges and pull up and climb over them is too bad as well

Not being able to save sucks hard

The locomotion being only fluid forward movement takes getting used to. I had thought teleport was pretty basic as a backup for those that dislike the vomit inducing locomotion of IRL walking? I have played Mage's Tale that used a stutter step to imitate actual walking that helped to alleviate getting dizzy from a room flowing past while you stand still and it worked fairly well, not a sweeping victory for movement but...\t\t\t\t\t

I'll give the developpers some time, and buy the game again in a few months if stuff has changed. I've refunded for now - This could have been so, so much better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, I hurt all over. Second, this game is not meant to be played with a low ceiling. Third, doing everything wrong is the best way to do it ... and this game allows you to do everything very, very wrong.

I'd recommend for anyone who doesn't get motion sick and wants a great physics simulation for a virtual body. Climbing over obstacles. Grabbing, dragging, punching, stabbing, shooting, and *gasp* climbing. (Sure, I've played Zero Caliber, I don't like feeling like I'm weightless.) I loved every second of that. The humor was goofy, but... why not? It's got it's own charm. It kinda wants to be Portal, but with how short those credits were. It's incredible that it has the polish it does. :D

I will say... this is the most accessible of the VR games. Others take a long time to introduce new VR players to. This? The tutorial is built right in.\t\t\t\t\t
