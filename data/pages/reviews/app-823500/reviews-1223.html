\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had an HTC Vive since my pre-ordered headset came in years ago and my first ever upgrade is this brand new pair of Valve Index controllers, which prompted me to get Boneworks. I LOVE this game. Love love love it. Immersion isn't about grand gestures and wide vistas... ok, well it is, but it's not JUST about those things, it's also about the little things. Like how you can get a sense of weight simply by being unable to move a thing rapidly. Like how when you release your grip on a thing you are holding up, by opening your actual fingers, it falls down. Like how when you release your grip on a thing that is holding YOU up, you fall down. It's about stabbing a ... whatever the Hades those things are ... with a sword and watching it go all the way through, then pulling that sword back out and watching the limp thing fall to the floor with the best physics to date. How weird is that, Mal?

On Motion Sickness: VR legs...it's like sea legs. If you spend a lot of time on a boat, you get used to it. When you spend a lot of time in VR, you get used to it. Gone are the days when I had to lay on the floor and squeeze my eyes shut and promise never to go up a spiral staircase in Fallout again, if only the gods would please just make it stop.

But maybe you aren't there yet and that dire warning about how you better be amazing at VR before you even attempt this game worries you. If so, my advice is to get it and play it til you feel the edge of the uggies, then take it off and do something in the real world for a few minutes. Rinse, repeat. Eventually you'll get so good at it you could virtually hop up and down on a virtual boat in a virtual storm with your feet firmly planted in the real world and you won't feel a thing. Train your brain. This is the game to do it because they give you nothing in the way of motion sickness easements. If you've been crutching along with fuzzed out edges and teleports, this game will force you past it. Just don't try to keep going when you feel it. Stop, walk it off, and then come back.

(For reference, because I used to have a GTX970, the supposed minimum, and I while I could play vr games with it, my experience did not resemble the ones I read about in reviews, so full disclosure: This review experience is based on an RTX2080tiGPU, 5th gen core i7CPU, 16GB DDR4, Vive headset (ancient pre-order version that has some slight differences despite being technically the same model as the currently sold headsets) with a wireless adapter, and a shiny new pair of Valve Index controllers. Your mileage may vary.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is NOT a tech demo!

This is a new staple of gaming just like Half Life was back in the day, the new century of gaming has come with THIS game
and It revolutionizes VR gaming completely.

Story-
It gets as convoluted as it gets, leaving the devs alot left to flesh out (Dark Souls vibes)
Which totally keeps me wanting from more.

-Gameplay-

THIS will be the milestone that future titles will be compared to in terms of combat, puzzles and general movement.

Every thing is physics based which leave the very good designed puzzles with multiple choices.
An automatic turret is keeping you from progressing?

Throw a trashcan over your head and walk past it
or you can grab a large metal create an use it as cover
or go ahead and throw some large bricks at it to tip it over
just let your imagination go wild!

-Weapon handling-

Is just right for the gun nut in me, i know it's not completely realistic but just standing there in VR, reloading and throwing the slide back on a 1911 is the best feeling ever (even tho it's just VR) and having different sight options (Iron, Holo and a Scope) makes you really aware of each advantages and disadvantages.
Melee people get their fair share too, with an entire segment (lategame) dedicated to it
but it doesn't mean you can't just go full berserk from the beginning... remeber this is FULLY phsysics based.

-Soundtrack-

Just phenomenal... every track fits the setting and prepares the mood for what is to come
I am person who truly believes music can make or break a game.
seriously all of my favourite games are based on their soundtracks
and this game absoluteley nailed it.

I SALUTE TO YOU MR. Michael Wyckoff YOU ABSOLUTE LEGEND!

-And sadly some bad stuff-

I yearn for more wapon variety like a shotgun, supershotgun, rocket launcher... Some obscure stuff, like come on man! (This is the Doom&Quake vet speaking)
They got melee weapons for days (Katana, Shortswords, Longswords, Machetes, Cleavers, etc...) but the guns are a bit lacking. (M4, Glock 17, 1911, MP5/MP5K, Micro UZI and i guess thats it)

-Checkpoints-

A point i have seen and experienced alot.
You NEED to finish a level to save progress.
which left me restarting a whole level completely due to crashes (regarding my own PC which i have fixed now).
The game has retry points, so if you die don't worry, death is not a complete reset
but they could have just made these save points as well.

TL;DGAS

Mark my words...
Stress Level Zero will be a major game dev in the future just like id, Valve and Blizzard were back in the day.
This game is a MILESTONE in gaming history.
A MUST GET. (If you have any kind of VR equipment)

10/10
