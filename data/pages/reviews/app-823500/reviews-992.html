\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've had some time to stew on this one after finishing it up late last night. On the whole, I am more than capable of recommending this.

HOWEVER.

Be aware that this is a game made by a team of less than a dozen people. It is not going to be even remotely close to the level of polish that will be seen with HL:A. But this is the stepping stone to that title. I've seen this described this way: if *Narbacular Drop* is the proto-*Portal*, *Boneworks* is the Proto-Half-Life Alyx. This is an apt description. This game marks a brand new era in not just VR Gaming, but Gaming as a whole. This game, along with Half-Life Alyx, will drag the world kicking and screaming into a brand new VR world.

I may talk a big talk, but I do have my fair share of criticisms. The ending to this game was ***exhausting.*** It felt dragged out and the "puzzle" at the end was downright tedious. The game was light on story, but that can be forgiven because this game is gameplay first. The physics systems, revolutionary as they may be, are most certainly rough around the edges. My hand got stuck in the walls numerous times. I also took issue with the inventory system. Pocket / holster location was tough for me to nail down, and this could very easily be solved with some kind of feedback that indicates your hands are in the correct place, maybe like a vibration in the controllers.

Despite all of this, I can give this game a glowing recommendation. It delivers on what it promises and does it well. As the description of the game says, it's ***Experimental***. Don't forget that. It is still an adventure worth undertaking, despite it being a bit rough in places. If you want a truly visceral VR experience and haven't already bought it, do it ASAP. Get on it.

At the very least, it'll tide you over until Half-Life: Alyx,\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo the game can be a little jank sometimes because everything is a physics simulated object but overall I feel like this is the game to really sell VR for me. Good cryptic plot (reminds me a lot of David Cronenberg's Existenz and I am a Cronenberg fanboy so that goes for a lot in my mind) that requires the player to engage in order to figure out but does not get in the way of game play. incredible soundtrack, so good that I went out and bought it after I finished the game. The most impressive use of physics in a game since... well since HL2 I guess. Good forward moving pacing that keeps you moving forward. Unique aesthetic and visual design. Physics focused gameplay, from the combat to the puzzles that, I feel could only have been done in VR. I felt that I was able to look over a lot of the jank (which comes from how experimental the game is in the first place) because this game really offers you the full package. It's the real deal I think. A true Half-Life successor that takes game-play and design concepts from the series and expands on them to the extreme. I feel though that I should point out again though that it is very experimental so if you have a low tolerance for a little jank you might dislike it, but for me I just am really appreciative of how far these guys are trying to advance VR games as a whole. It will probably be overshadowed by Half-Life: Alyx in a bit but this game should get some props too.\t\t\t\t\t
