\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a Dad to twins with a really busy life and I rarely get to REALLY get into a game, I've found myself setting an alarm to get half an hour in with Boneworks before the kids wake up, which to anyone else in the same position is review enough in itself!

For everyone else, this is an absolutely brilliant game, one of the few on VR that I have been excited to get back into every day and the first time I've been genuinely wowed by physics puzzles since firing up HL2. In short, in Boneworks' world, if you think it could be possible, it usually is.

The obvious comparison product is Half Life Alyx, which is a smooth, polished VR experience BUT, despite its brilliance, Alyx still feels like it's on rails and designed to move you from moment to moment as a tech demo with the illusion of freedom. Boneworks has more of the old school Half Life ethos where you see a distant door and how you get there is up to you and breaking the rules and taking a different path to the obvious is part of the experience (and often rewarded too). Beyond the 15 hour or so story line, there are sandboxes, arenas, challenges and much more to check out that make this exceptional value for money.

There are places where Boneworks falls down, the first is the use of both hands at once; climbing and manipulating larger objects (like crates and barrels), both feel like you're fighting the engine, rather than working with it, though this is likely down to the limitations of the controller tracking through the Oculus Quest I used to play.

The second is the inventory system, which relies on placing items into predefined holsters about your body by letting go of them in the right location. Great idea, but again, poor tracking occasionally leads to a prized weapon being dropped during a fight, or worse, falling into one of the games many abysses, never to be seen again. One especially frustrating moment involved dropping a flashlight during a dark tunnel sequence and having to load an earlier save.

These minor gripes aside, Boneworks is really worth a play and represents one of the few complete VR single player experiences, and one with good replay value, on several occasions having completed a tricky puzzle, I realised that the devs had hidden a short cut in plain view (like a climbable pipe or hidden walkway) and I'm already curious as to where they all lead!

The continued addition of extra content is appreciated too and I look forward to checking out the Zombie Arena that dropped this week.

An absolute gem, at a reasonable price and well worth a look.\t\t\t\t\t
