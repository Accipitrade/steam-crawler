\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt takes a lot for me to downvote a VR game, typically it is easy to impress. But on the Vive, this game is incredibly frustrating to play due to controls. Let me start by saying I am extremely experienced, I have had VR for several years, and have likely a thousand hours in over 100 titles.

You cannot custom map the controls for this game if you're using a vive, even using the steam workshop custom controller settings. If you change trackpad settings from press, to touch, jump will not work. Jump is absolutely essential for for progress in this game. This game also needs a permanent TOGGLE RUN ON. FFS I cannot tell you how frustrating double-tapping up is every time you want to move. ESPECIALLY a couple hours in when you get to jump and climb challenges. They all involve running and jumping with very specific timings and the control setup for this is so horrendous it's super frustrating.

I would have refunded, but unfortunately passed the time window. Also, do you like puzzle games? Because I dont. It isn't clear where or how you have to progress at many points, and you will find yourself exploring in circles if you missed something necessary to progress. I am currently stuck atop some roofs, can push some heavy track-cart thing, but it isnt clear where I am supposed to go or what I am supposed to do next. And this was after a very frustrating time climbing the friggen thing in the first place through the run-jump button issues.

Perhaps on other devices, such as Index or Oculus controllers, this isn't straight cancer. The physics is really cool, the graphics seem really good. But I cannot recommend this for any Vive users.

There is also a sandbox mode, I have not dabbled with it yet. Perhaps uer-generated content will make this purchase worth my while, or less cancerous if it isn't all jump and climb problems. Is there a game underneath? Maybe?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't like Boneworks at first. It's not exactly like the other VR games I've played. In fact it was only after 6 hours of play time that I finally decided to check what other people were saying about it. And this was the video that I watched.

That's when something finally clicked in my head and I realized what Boneworks is all about. It's not about the protagonist, but you, the player! Boneworks is designed for you to be able to express yourself and do the most wicked stuff in it. Everything from parkouring your way around the level, to taking down multiple enemies at the same time in slow motion, to shooting yourself in the foot and dying. I did all of those things, and a lot more.

Boneworks isn't the type of game you take seriously or try to "play it by the rules". This is a simulation, and you are Neo, there are no rules. In fact even the tutorial part of the game mentions that you can complete a level any way you want, even by cheating your way to the end.

The story and characters don't matter here, it's not that kind of game. Instead Boneworks offers the best interactive sandbox VR experience. Not all the levels are equally fun to play, but they're all unique. And they serve to introduce the player to new gameplay mechanics, and challenge their creativity.

As mentioned before, there are no rules in Boneworks. Any object can be used as a weapon or a shield. Even your dead enemies' lifeless corpses can be picked up and swung or thrown or used as barriers against incoming bullets. You can scale practically any building, and later on you'll be able to stick to walls and even fly. There's tons of secrets and loot everywhere, so exploration is highly recommended and rewarding.

Gunplay, and especially the slow-motion shootouts are immensely satisfying. Here's me Max Payne-ing some poor bastard. Tons of guns, attachments and weapon combinations. You can dual wield, you can grab enemies and throw them around, you can barricade doors or crack their skulls with a watermelon.

The game itself isn't very hard, so you're never in any real danger. Which in turn allows you to be as creative and as entertaining as you want to be. But that's not to say that it's a game that I would recommend to VR newbies. If you just got a VR headset and you're considering buying Boneworks because it's on sale - buy it. But play something else first, like Beat Saber or Until You Fall or Super Hot. And then come back to this when you're ready. Because Boneworks can be a little bit overwhelming to a new player. It's not a 'party game' that you let your friends take turns playing. It takes some getting used to, and some elements like navigation and parkour will take a longer time to master, at least in my experience.

Visually, Boneworks looks good! It's a game that chose clarity over fluff, and it's still a very pleasant experience. In no small part thanks to it's amazing physics simulation and sound design. And did I mention that Boneworks has a killer soundtrack? I did not expect that at all, especially from a first person VR shooter game. Sometimes I would dread the thought of leaving a level because the music was so good.

[here's another short clip of me messing around]

Things that I didn't really like? I guess I wish the save system worked differently. I was hoping that once I finished the game, I would be able to go back to any level with all my resources and gear unlocked. Because I worked really hard to collect everything and get all the guns. But instead, if you load an earlier level, you don't 'travel' there, you just load a save game from 20 hours ago. Where you don't have anything again. It was just a bit disappointing, it's no big deal. And maybe most players actually like this idea.

In conclusion, I think that Boneworks is a must-have title for any VR enthusiast. It's got advanced VR mechanics, satisfying gameplay, great soundtrack and universal physical interactivity, as well as a bunch of additional free content. The only thing that would make this game better would be some kind of coop or multiplayer mode. (This can actually be achieved with mods now!)

Stress Level Zero did a phenomenal job creating one of my favorite VR games, and I can't wait to see what they come up with next!

The audio is all over the place, and the voice acting is some of the worst I've heard in any game. Its painful to listen to. Most other games I don't care about voice acting because most other games have a "voice" slider, so i can just turn that off. Not this game though, too indie for that ♥♥♥♥. Finally the movement makes me sick, and no other VR game has ever made me sick. There's a spot early in the game where they make fun of the "old and dumb" way of teleport movement, like you do in Alyx. The game is literally bragging about how it's "new" mechanics for movement are better when I'm sitting here thinking "Yeah I've played tens of hours of Alyx and never once felt nauseous, and I've played a few minutes of this and I feel like I'm carsick."

So yeah, I don't like this game. It's whole vibe is kinda ♥♥♥♥♥♥ and the mechanics aren't novel enough anymore to be worth suffering the awkward/poorly realized parts.\t\t\t\t\t
