\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's better than Half-Life: Alyx in terms of gameplay and immersion **IF, AND ONLY IF** you can get around the MOTION SICKNESS and LEARN THE MECHANICS***(it takes practice).

Personally, it took me *months* to beat this game because I kept getting sick while playing and couldn't find the right way to play comfortably until very recently.

--- Graphics settings.
I recommend the very minimum graphics settings because you NEED!!! a SOLID 90 FPS while playing.

--- Standing vs. seated.
I ended up preferring to play the game while seated. I placed a stool in the middle of my tracked area and played the final three or so levels like this. You mostly use snap-turn for wide rotations and turn IRL for more precise adjustments. You will feel your VR-cable getting wrapped around the stool if you turn IRL too much, so it is easy to know when to do a turning reset (pause and rotate IRL to get the cable back to a good position)

In the beginning I played while standing, and while it is probably more immersive to play this way, it ends up just being difficult and annoying to have to worry about the cable and I find it easier to get sick like this.

--- SAVING.
Oh my god, the save stations are absolutely amazing. It's really hard to believe that the game launched without these. They are pretty frequent and mean that the levels don't feel anywhere near as long anymore. You can use these to save and quit, and then resume gameplay where you left off. Really crucial to use.

--- Comfort.
There are settings to tune the game's movement so that it feels more comfortable. I had my locomotion set to "linear" and I felt that it helped to make the game's movement feel more comfortable.

--- Virtual crouching.
It works, and it works well in this game. You use the right stick up-and-down to change your height in-game. So long as you maintain the same height IRL you probably won't get sick like this.

--- Climbing.
It takes quite a bit of practice. You need to use a combination of your virtual crouching and your arms to do the more advanced stuff. It mostly feels annoying to do in this game. It is a good thing the game rarely forces you to do it.

---Controllers
Index controllers are probably essential for this game. I cannot imagine having fun playing with Vive wands, not sure about Oculus controllers as I have never used.

--- Overall.
This game has a very high barrier of entry and enjoyment. It is way harder to play and to complete than Half-Life: Alyx (probably intentional), but if you can get through the initial struggle you will really enjoy it and probably want to replay some of the levels every now and again.

In conclusion, I give it 8/10. The final levels of the game are a bit weak in my opinion. The mechanics and level of interaction is top tier right now. It has very very good melee combat and gun combat. The levels are quite complex and have lots of hidden areas and things to find.

--- tl;dr
Try it, but don't be disappointed if you find it very hard to play.\t\t\t\t\t
