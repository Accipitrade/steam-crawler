\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI been eyeing this game since I got my Oculus 2 and finally just went and got it. I was a bit hesitant since it kind of has mediocre reviews but after recently finishing it I can say its worth buying, but I do understand some complaints.

Pros:

The gun play is very solid and realistic, best part of the game I would say. All the guns for the most part operate like the real thing and its alot of fun just shooting.

A few puzzles require you to climb and its surprisingly fun as well.

Graphics and setting are pretty good. Its kind of a wannabe Half life/ Portal kind of setting. Everything looks fairly real.

Cons:

Puzzles are a bit simple. Im not very good at puzzles and I was flying through this game. Using the climbing ability you can skip some segments, I wasnt actively looking to do that it just happened.

Story is completely uninteresting. Its a extremely simple anti-corporation story, which I would like but its just so bland and on the nose at times. The only real hints of bad things going on comes from stale graffiti on the walls. "Company bad" "Dont trust Company!" "We were wrong" stuff along those lines. There something about the enemys being alive I think? but its not explained at all. Story was a complete flatline.

Fighting was good, enemy variety wasn't, Headcrab robots, zombies and wierd guys with machine guns riding a orb. Thats about it. You dont really get to shoot anything that bleeds till the end. The guys that actually shoot back just look silly and its kills it a bit for me.

Guns are great, melee combat is crap. I may just be spoiled after 50 or so hours of "Blade&Sorcery" which is a masterpiece of swordplay gaming. Its hard to explain, you take a sword to these guys and its just not satisfying at all, you just wack em. No real cutting or anything. I stuck with the guns the whole game.

A little too easy overall. I only died a handfull of times, once because I was testing if you can shoot yourself in the head and die (you can) and the other times were when I thought I paused took off my headset and came back later dead.


So in all I did enjoy the game alot and I think its worth buying and playing. Its got some issues though. I hope theres a sequel someday.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll update this review once I've finished the game, however, I've restarted it about 5 times due to selling VR headsets and showing friends VR with this game. Every time I start this game it feels special though, and this is biased because this is the game that made me buy a VR headset. It just amazes me. Its one of those games where you look at something and ask "can I use this?" and you can.

The fact that your whole body is in VR, in a physics based world, is the key to a fantastic VR experience. Nothing is more immersion breaking than a floating hand or putting your hand through an object. After playing this, every other VR game will feel cheap. Everything just feels right in this game. If realism and physics tickle your fancy then you need this game. Everything feels so polished. And the soundtrack oh my god. I 100% know that in 10 years when I hear some of these sounds they will trigger some amazing nostalgia.

If you havent, go and watch Stress Level Zeros development diaries on Youtube. These guys truly care about what they make. I want more games like this.\t\t\t\t\t
