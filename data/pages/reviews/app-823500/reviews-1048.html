\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter extensively testing the game out, I realized something. BONEWORKS, works as a tech demo to VR. I don't deny it being highly experimental with movement, gunplay and puzzles, but there are things that work and don't work in VR and I personally feel that this game communicates that effectively.

In my opinion:
Movement
- Very fluid, feels like Half Life.
- Doesn't always work well, can be cumbersome. (irony isn't it?)
- Full body IK can give you nausea as the illusion can break sometimes.
- Running, jumping and crouching don't necessarily convert well from an FPS into VR.

Gunplay
- Fantastic
- Game needs more parts with Gunplay.
- More guns!

Puzzles
- This is really hit/miss.
- Some puzzles often leave you wondering if you did something wrong.
- Not everything is communicated well.
- The lack of QA can be felt.

I recommend it because its an experience in itself. Worth the entry price. You have to try it to understand what works for you in VR and what doesn't.

"For your testing convenience, all tests are available and all safety precautions within testing chambers have been deactivated." - Aperture Labs

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery disappointed after all the hype. Whilst it has some cool physics (albeit very wonky at times), the game just feels so barebones, more of a tech demo than anything. The first level or so with all the tutorial stuff was very cool and showed a lot of promise, but once got past that it really fell apart for me. The level and character designs feel very bland and empty, story is almost non-existent (from what I've played thus far), the combat can be okay at times, but melee just feels very clunky. It's also for me been very nausea inducing, the only VR game to make me feel this way...which wasn't helped by the fact that there were mid-level checkpoints, so I'd quit when I feel sick, then find myself having to run through the same monotonous corridors and areas, solving the awkward same puzzles, in order to reach where I left off. The potential is there, but it all feels very unpolished and rushed, as if they just quickly pushed it out before the release of Half-Life.

Some of the physics are definitely a lot of fun and should be applauded and there's definitely potential there, but to me it just felt so barebones as a game, lost interest very quick. Despite some advancements, it doesn't compare to other VR games with maybe less interactivity and less advanced physics, but much better design, story, comfort etc. Be interested in seeing what the developer does next.\t\t\t\t\t
