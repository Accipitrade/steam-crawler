\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a VR shooter, with some puzzle sections and a genuinely clever physics engine which doesn't take itself too seriously.
To look at games from the past it would be similar to how half life 2 and episodes played however boneworks doesn't have a particularly big story from what i have played so far.
I would say, common to all VR shooters, that you need to be comfortable with movement in VR, i don't get Motion sickness in most games now i'm comfortable using VR but i did get it with this game. I believe the puzzles that require you to jump from heights and or to climb are to blame for it. on the positive side i would say the gun play and the puzzle sections are rather well thought out and a lot of fun. plus the fact that some sections you can approach 2 or 3 different ways make every different playthrough quite different.
Pros: Great shooting, Puzzles and physics. Very natural feeling controls and decent enough story.
Cons: Motion sickness to those prone to it (Not fault of this game, most VR games can have this) and inventory management that is a little clunky left me a bit frustrated at times.
Overall, if you like VR shooters, this is a solid choice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely amazing vr game, best one I have played so far. The first impression this game gives is absolutely insane. What I thought would be my least favorite part of the game (climbing) turned out to be one of the coolest parts of it. Guns work great, reloading is great, physics are great.
My only complaints would be the floor in the loading screen is like on the tippy top layer of everything, leading it to obscure my chaperone bound walls and also my room view.
AI is also kind of bad, most of the non shooting enemies cant even go up stairs, null bodies and other zombie type enemies will continue to fall over or get stuck on doors. The shooting guys and headset crabs work fine though, headset crabs would be my favorite enemy.
Having a VR body is really cool and smooth locomotion is proven to be superior to teleport movement in this game. My only complaint with the vr body is sometimes it has a little trouble predicting which way you are facing (leading to me grabbing a clip of ammo instead of a gun on accident) and also sometimes my vr arms would get stuck or snagged on something leaving me stuck for a couple moments.
This game shows that vr has huge potential.
My one complaint is this game could be so much better than it already is in the places it lacks (story, some level design, mostly just story)
