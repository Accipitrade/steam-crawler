\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLook, this game got super over-hyped. You can't just not recommend it because it IS a cool VR experience and overall it's better than the majority of other VR titles by indie studios that we have to choose from, but temper your expectations. It's not a "secret half life game". It's not going to revolutionize VR.

VR is NOT ready for full body presence in my opinion, but even still, blade and sorcery does it better than Boneworks. The way your hands arms and fingers move makes you feel like you're in a cerebral palsy simulator, and your feet just kind of dangle from your torso in an unnatural way. It hurts the immersion big time, I'd rather have nothing but there's no options for it. The physics are unwieldy and the game requires you to manipulate huge things that are irritatingly laborious to transport. The puzzles are hard, not because they require advanced problem solving skills, but because even though you know the solution you have to try over and over again because of the unintuitive movement and mechanics. There's like 3 different types of enemies when you account for the fact that the majority of them are just reskins of something else.


Anyway you should get it, it's a fresh new VR experience that's worth playing, but don't expect miracles.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is like a chocolate cake with ♥♥♥♥ sprinkles. The overall game is pretty fun, but holy hell are some of the missions just terrible. First person platforming in VR is miserable when your character model bonks his head on every ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ obstacle. The climbing mechanic is fine, but my god, you did not need to put the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ladder from mgs3 in the game. The physics engine and the guns feel fresh, but the punishment for failing a precise platforming section is infuriating. I found myself putting the game down after falling 500 feet off a building that I spent the past 5 minutes climbing up due to an unclear system.
The other frustrating thing is that boneworks has a tendency to give you the really interesting and cool weapons after the level is over. Secret weapons and special physics weapons are handed out to you often after all the enemies are dead and you just need to walk through the end gate of the level.
TLDR: Core mechanics are fantastic, but the game suffers heavily from certain levels that are a downright chore to play through.\t\t\t\t\t
