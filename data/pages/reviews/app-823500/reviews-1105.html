\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am playing on a Vive with wands. I do think a lot of the issues come with how the game controls with these.

With that said, I enjoyed parts of the game however, I ended up refunding it. I plan on purchasing this game again in several months when I can get my Index Controllers.

The physics are a bit inconsistent or annoying. Some items which should have weight don't and ones you would expect to not, do. This can be a bit of a pain at times.

I do enjoy how the player models interact with everything with collision however, combined with how cumbersome the wands can be in this game it became a fight to get things in the right position.

Gripping was another issue, again most likely due to the wands however, this is impacted as well by the weight inconsistencies in the physics. With the wands you end up... pinching a lot in game. Carrying a lot of things with the index finger and thumb. Most of the time this works however, sometimes you appear to need to grip the rest of it which involves using the grip buttons on the wand which are just not ergonomic.

Those issues are the primary ones that caused me to refund.

This game also tries very heavily to mimic the charm and characteristics of half life and portal. Sometimes it hits and a lot of times it doesn't. Combined with the checkpoint system (which is being addressed) it just... is a meh point for me. I can see it going either way for others. I do believe part of it was lost on me again due to the controls but I'm not sure.

The game can be very tense and immersive and at those points it shines. There is a level of interactivity that I hope inspires other developers to take it even further.

Again, I hope to try this again after more patches and I get myself a set of Index Controllers.\t\t\t\t\t

My ex-wife and I have a daughter together, and adopted our son together. They are now both 4 years old.

When we were going through our separation, I found myself lost and miserable. I was self destructive. I got so mad one day from everything spiraling out of my control that I punched some concrete in a moment of overwhelming emotion. That caused me to break my 5th metacarpal in my right hand... my working hand... my games hand.. the hand that I held and carried my children to bed with.. The hand I desperately needed to make sure I could continue to provide.

After learning of the severity of my self-inflicted damage, I was borderline suicidal. Keep in mind that just a few months before this, I was the happiest man with no history of depression or anxiety. I have never had fits of rage, or been one to break down and cry, but I was in a low spot that just really buried me from being able to see the light on the other side.

I was able to experience other peoples joy and happiness. I was able to see the fruits of my "labor". I relaxed for 5 minutes to this music long enough to realize that I was going to be okay.

Today, I am close friend with my kids mother. We don't fight, or argue, or say hurtful things to each other. We are parents, and friends.

I now have 3 kids. My third child is, wait for it, ALSO 4 YEARS OLD. The woman I am with was going through a very similar situation at the time of my own separation, and we just stumbled in to each others life unexpectedly. We have been in a relationship for a year now, and are very happy together.

Moral of the story, you never know what life holds in store for you, and if I would have given up when all the odds were stacked against me, I wouldn't be where I am today. This silly little game helped me realize that.

The story was meh but the limitless fun of going through the levels definitely made up for the story. I'm glad that there was a story just for some insight into the why's of MythOS.

All in all, there is pure, limitless fun for hours on end. The story wasn't bad but it isn't the best. Definitely deserves the praise it has been getting from the VR community.\t\t\t\t\t

Puzzles feel limitless in the ways to solve them and gun play, although difficult, feels good to fire off. Do note: Your entire body in this game is a physics object and it takes some getting use to. Specifically your arms can interact with things so I find it easy to just pretend your arms are tracked as well as your hands and move about.

The game, unlike B&S does have playablity though. Not just goofing off in closed places, but actually good environments with progress. You're rewarded for investigating areas... and the game is about 14 hours long, but I'm explorative. Maybe shave off an hour. Enjoy VR to the fullest extent in Boneworks.\t\t\t\t\t
