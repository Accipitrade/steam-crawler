\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game, ironically because I got HL: Alyx for free due to buying Index gear on pre-order. I haven't played any of this type game or any FPS in general. Most of my VR has been social, Sansar and VRChat or games like BeatSaber. In Alyx, I was deeply upset that I could not hit the thing trying to eat my face with an iron bar. A friend suggested Boneworks, I read the reviews of it's legendary nausea and thought, 'Sure, why not! I can do full flips and combat rolls in VR (thanks Vive Wireless!!) Nothing makes me ill. This sounds great!

I made it about an hour before I had to lay down and wait for the room to stop heaving.
The next day I made it two hours, then four. It's like crossing the bar on a boat going out to see. Very choppy rough water. It gave me serious sick until I adapted.

Some of the physical puzzles are really hard and if you mess them up, you might have to reset an entire level and start again but most are pretty okay. The physics are weird but I like how they work. I practice tai-chi so the work of moving slow and smooth with heavy objects works pretty well for me and gives a surprisingly convincing experience of weight and balance. When you're really in the groove of it, it can be pretty fun. If on the other hand, you do something bad like get 2 metal fences hopelessly tangled, it can be nearly impossible to progress and fiddling with physical objects can get very sickening very quick.

I feel like a lot of the puzzles would be easier to deal with in full body. The character's knees kept colliding with objects in ways they wouldn't if it could see my feet and hips. Also kicking stuff (Doors for instance) would add to the immersion. Understandably, full-body is a small market in a small market so I'm not holding my breath on it, but it'd be super cool.

The story is a little on the lacking side at least at first. I wasn't even clear on whether or not there was a story or if it was just a big open environment, also, I think the textures are too regular (particularly on the walls) and a minor change in textures would probably help a lot with the nausea.

I'm going to 'Recommend' this game because I think it's an amazing tech demo and I love the physical interaction but only for people who are really into experiencing the new expression VR allows us to explore. It's frustrating and sickening to play but also thoroughly enjoyable when you hit the good parts. I wish it had the same level of polish as Alyx.\t\t\t\t\t
