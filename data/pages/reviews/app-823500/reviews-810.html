\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBneworks is exactly what the game description says it is. An experimental physics VR adventure. If you just bought a VR setup and have no games to play, this should be the first one you buy. Why? Because it helps give you a good grasp on how a lot of VR games operate. It's kind of a jack of all trades but the master of none. The shooting mechanics are solid, the puzzles are pretty easy to cheat your way through, the "story" is basically an afterthought, and there are games that definitely look better. As a Beginners game/ sandbox VR experience, I think it's wonderful, but playing enough VR before purchasing the game kind of ruined the experience, as other games have allowed me to experience the individual aspects the game offers at greater heights. VR I don't think is at a point where trying to simulate your body is a viable game feature only because you get stuck on so many objects that it becomes quite annoying at times, and climbing up some surfaces just becomes tedious. But the props goes to the devs for trying to squeeze a platforming shooter - puzzle/ physics based bullet time sandbox. If you're trying to get the most of your $30 and are new to VR, this is the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t
