\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is some of the most fun I've had in VR in a while. The shooting is fun, the puzzles are fun, circumventing the puzzles by taking alternate routes is fun, the balloon gun is fun...

To be frank, the game is by no means perfect. My biggest complaint about this game is that item persistence is lacking; on one particular level there were two yellow keys -- except, there's only one, but two opportunities to spawn it. And if you find the second location, the original key you found (and put in the lock) disappears, and the door you unlocked is locked again... yeah. Item persistence. Also, if you save your game and then come back, the stuff you were carrying may or may not be in your inventory, and the stuff you put in the pad to 'save' it might not be there, either. You pretty much have to complete the levels in one sitting of you don't want to lose stuff (maybe there was a rhyme or reason to what stayed and what disappeared, but it seemed pretty random to me...)

Second big complaint: tracking seems a bit janky in the parkour; sometimes holding onto a ledge and reaching for another one yanks you around; I guess they wanted to limit what kind of climbing you could do to simulate strength limitations (unlike another game, "Climbey", where you are basically a hydraulic climb-bot), but it makes some climbing puzzles frustrating... however, this could be due to the fact that I'm not exactly short, and my Index lighthouses only go yea-so-high, and I may have had them pointed down a little too much, so take that with a grain of salt.

THAT BEING SAID: even in spite of the above, the game is good fun. I've been thoroughly enjoying it, to the point of losing entire Saturdays to it, where I stop because my legs are tired of standing, and I take the headset off and the sun has stopped shining, all the lights are out, and my cats are giving me that look they give me when they've been watching me stand around waving these plastic things around all day, judging me as cats always do... the adorable, fuzzy little b*stards.

Anyway, if you have VR, like shooting stuff, and like mildly disturbing content (the entire game has a mild horror vibe to it, like Portal, but without the humor), and like puzzles (or even the hidden puzzle of skipping said puzzles), get this game. You can feel like a badass walking around with an automatic firearm in one hand, a superball in the other, bouncing the ball to shoot a nullman, retrieving the ball, and then continuing on with panache. Okay, maybe that was a weird example. But, it's the little things that count.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those moment when I wish Steam would have a "yes... but..." option for "do you recommend this game?".
Also I should point out that I might be biased because I played this after playing Half-life: Alyx, and to be honest, it kinda raised the bar for VR physics to a place that no one matches at the moment.

I played this after playing their previous VR title - Duck Season - which is related to this one. And I played on the Valve Index.

I do like the game, I like the creepy weird atmosphere, the vague story you have to kinda piece together yourself, I like that they are building their own universe with their games, I like that they are trying to mix it up with both puzzles and a shooter (like Alyx) and I know that at the time when it came out it had the best physics in VR.

But the bad is that the puzzles can get a bit frustrating, mainly because of the physics. At one point I wanted to place two planks over a gap so I can cross it. That took me almost 40 minutes. I kept falling and had to find my way back to where I was. Your guy will not always jump onto things like you expect, getting on top of things is a skill you need to learn - it is not intuitive and there is a specific way the game expects you to do that.
Objects in the world do not act like real objects. Close, but no cookie... They don't feel like they have weight, they don't always follow your hands properly. They collide into each other at unproportional force. When you try to move a large object, it just moves clumsily, it doesn't feel like it's heavy or anything like that.
Usually I wouldn't be nitpicking about stuff like that, but this game expect the player to use objects together, to build pathways or stack stuff together, so things need to behave correctly for this to be enjoyable.

Also it would have been nice to have a little more diversity in enemies. It's pretty limited to weird looking puppet guys that say "hey!", zombie guys and soldiers on balls. I met a spider-bot thing near the start, but never encountered another one... shame.

EDIT: I spoke too soon. Later in the game they throw those spider bots on you quite a lot. (actually I think they are more their version of the head-crabs from Half-life to be honest).
But still, from about 60% or 70% completion, there isn't much variety in enemies...

Also also I feel like the vague story is a bit TOO vague. I got to a point where I lost interest in finding out what it is. Maybe throw a little more our direction?
I mean, I get that it has to do with some kind of AI and that's probably what the demon dog from the last game was, and I do love it that they don't spoon-feed us the story, but they may have taken it to the other extreme. A bit more would have been nice.

But those complaints aside, the graphics are good, the world is well designed, a lot of the puzzles are actually great fun, the weapons are fun to handle and to use and combat is great even if you are a lousy shot like me, even melee combat is fun, if somewhat repetitive (go for the heads!), and I really love the weird trippy-creepy atmosphere. There's ton to explore, many secrets, you have hours and hours of gameplay here, and potential to go back and have fun with the story levels, as well as the sandbox arena.
This is a quality VR game well worth its' low price.
You know what? Price it 10 quid more, and it's still worth the price!
So yea, I do recommend it - absolutely - but I do think this company can do better, and I can't wait to see what they do next!\t\t\t\t\t
