\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinally a VR game with a reasonable price-tag. A 6 hour campaign and a bit more fun in Arena/Sandbox mode makes 25 euro's an OK price. The physics are unique, but weapon handling doesn't work that well. Pulling the... how do you call those things when the mag is empty??... is sometimes very annoying because the physics-engine gets in the way. I also don't quite understand why there is no fall damage or penalty.

A thing I really do not like about this game is it's sounddesign. It is very basic and I hear a lot of the same sounds during the campaign. Also, the story really isn't a story, it's more like random levels which follow-up. I have no clue why the orange people are orange and the little story telling things on the monitors were so boring that I threw the monitor on the ground fairly quickly. Climbing a huge ladder simply feels like buying time to let the game last longer in a very sloppy way. I think VR could be THE place for a good story that really gives you the feel that you're there. Well, maybe for a follow-up I guess.

When I see the 88% positive and convert that to an 8.8 mark on scale 1 to 10, then I think 88% is too high. For me it's a 7, maybe 7.5 because of the friendly price. Do I recommend this game? Not sure. Maybe later.\t\t\t\t\t
