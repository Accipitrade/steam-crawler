\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaven't even finished it yet and I can recommend it very very much. Probably the best VR game to this moment. Many speculations say that Boneworks was meant to be Half-Life VR and I can't disagree. Enemies, weapons and atmosphere make it feel so much Half-Life'y.

Basically first game (except Blade and Sorcery) to have full body with arms and legs that are world objects and have collisions with every object. You can even headbutt enemies with your forehead. Story is engaging, but as I said - I haven't finished it yet. Guns are much more arcade than in H3VR, but we shouldn't expect any game to get to this level of realism. Actually melee is much more fun than shooting, although using firepower can be pleasant too.

As for cons: This game probably won't be good for people with motion sickness. From my personal experience - I can play H3VR with smooth locomotion using Perma Sprint mode and I've never lost my balance. Here, because of how forces work, I get nausious after leaving Boneworks and looking at everyday objects. I guess for this kind of games you need VR legs and also VR Head. There are also many high obstacles on your path where jumping from 10m is neccessary. If you can't bear it than there is no way you will finish that game. Personally, I love it. There is no other way around it other than just working your VR legs out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS is the first long single-player game, that truly showcases the potential of immersive storytelling in VR.
The detailed physics system allows for more freedom in approaching a gameplay situation, than any game before.
However, unfortunately the game lacks a well thought out checkpoint/save system which makes dying an infuriating experience. On top of that, currently it's riddled with game breaking bugs (falling through the level, getting stuck in geometry etc.) that make restarting from a far away checkpoint even worse.

Regardless, it's a superb experience that no VR player should miss out on.


EDIT: A new checkpoint system is in the works, and many bugs have already been fixed, so by February probably all of my major complaints will be resolved. :)

EDIT 2: Since my last edit, the game has grown significantly. There are new levels, modes, weapons etc. The fact that the developers keep evolving the game even though it was great to begin with, is admirable. I'm looking forward to more updates, and eagerly wait for their next project.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, playing this game you can see the vision the creators had and the vision is cool. But the problem is in the execution.

The biggest problem with this game is the physics. The physics in this game is supposed to be some kind of cool feature, but what it is instead is incredibly awkward. It would be fine if the physics were just there as a nice feature to watch things blow up in interesting ways. But instead, we are expected to move objects around to solve puzzles.

I feel like I have some kind of degenerative muscular disease as I attempt to climb, jump, and move objects around and the physics makes no sense. I have to hit a cardboard box several times to break it up if it will break at all, and then others that are indestructible. Then the pallet jack that barely moves, then suddenly is so light I'm practically throwing it across the room.

These sudden changes between agonizingly heavy and suddenly weightless do not make it easy for anything remotely like fine control. There are places where we are expected to put two boxes on top of each other, or move a table to the side of a container and use the table to jump on the roof. These situations are not playable. There is a light table, but I can't move it without flipping it over on its side, on its back, when I get it close, but a little bit off, I have to start all over from the beginning. For some reason, I can't push things. So many times the puzzle would be almost solvable if I could just push the table instead of having to pick it up and move it. There are jumping puzzles, but it is so hard to jump consistently because it uses the joystick instead of a button. Usually, I find myself crouching mid-run instead of jumping. Climbing puzzles are constantly jerking my face around and slamming it into the wall instead of letting me smoothly slide down the wall, my arms awkwardly grabbing and yanking my body and it isn't even remotely intuitive. It would really help if the hand animations fit what they were doing, but they don't so it is all just off-putting.

Maybe if the physics worked in some intuitive way and if the controls actually worked consistently, this could be a good game. But instead, Boneworks comes off as nothing but a proof-of-concept to show what is possible, but not as an example of a good game.\t\t\t\t\t
