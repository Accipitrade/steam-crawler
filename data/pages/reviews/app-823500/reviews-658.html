\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame engine:
Boneworks is an incredible game just based off of gameplay value alone. The game engine is very physics-based and emulates many physics props with astonishing accuracy. Something looks heavy? Guess what, it IS heavy and is harder to toss around! Blade looks sharp? It is, and you can stick it into soft objects and let it stay there! You can also slow down time and see the simulation accurately slow as well! Then, there is the gunplay. In short, the gunplay is fantastic. Each gun functions similarly to how it would in real life, minus the jams. You actually have to shoulder the rifle and look through iron sights or the scope, all while holding the weapon straight. Pistols are easier to use but not nearly as fun. Slowing down time also affects gun behavior, too. Honestly, it's just fun to be John Wick in VR.

Story:
Boneworks has probably one of the longest stories for any VR game out there, and plenty of secrets to discover along the way. You can "reclaim" weapons and "capsules" which will allow you to spawn those things in sandbox later. It is pretty difficult to get all the secrets on the first runthrough, so you can go back and redo each level to your heart's content and unlock all the secrets MythOS has for you. However, I couldn't make sense of the story's narrative and while the levels were fun, they didn't really seem to follow a plot or story. If I had to sum it up? Inception but in VR, and that's really all I can say.

Extra levels:
There is a ton of extra content for you to go through. Fantasy Arena, hidden levels, even a zombie arena for you to explore in or simply try to survive! There is a load of filler content which can keep you busy for a while.

Soundtrack:
I thoroughly enjoyed the soundtrack, although I did have to turn it down in a few levels as it was just too loud. However, there is a Radio buddy on a few levels which features the song "Pick It Up". I picked it up and carried it on my back the whole time, like a true Boneworks enthusiast. You can do that, by the way. It's how I survived the sewers.

Overall rating: 9/10
This is one of the best VR games I have played and is extremely satisfying, only hampered by the lack of grace for newer VR users (motion sickness, anyone?) and a pretty confusing story. This was a groundbreaking and game for VR, and it shows.\t\t\t\t\t
