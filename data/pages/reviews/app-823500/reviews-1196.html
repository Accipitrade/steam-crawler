\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got to the final story mission, and the "game" is good. But in the end it doesn't feel like a game, felt like they finished every level with a "meh, good enough" attitude. I liked the game and I loved the soundtrack, but it just doesn't give me enough to want to hop back on and play. There are no plans for mod support, and the story is super bland, some other modes allow you to play around but it all only feels "okay". While BONEWORKS can showcase what VR can really do, it also showcases why people do not want to buy into VR. Every title is 20+ dollars, less than 10 hours long, and does not give enough content to make me personally want to boot up the game anymore. Once you finish the "story" you have seen almost everything the game has to offer, they even showed you the solution to like half the puzzles in the trailer.


EDIT/UPDATE:
The game has been out for a few months now with minimal updates that fix irritating bugs and errors. At this point in time it should be a 10 dollar early access title. I said at the beginning of the review that the game doesn't feel like a game, it's more on an experience. An experience that was so bad that I would like to forget it.\t\t\t\t\t

Show me those Bone-tones!

Boneworks is what I consider to be the VR staple, the nail in the coffin, the final bullet for those who choose to stay ignorant of Virtual Reality and it's advantages. While I don't think VR will outright replace pancake gaming, Boneworks right now is giving a lot of them a run for their money.

The physics - It's been hammered in to every nook and cranny, including into those who are confused by boneworks' relevance. You are no longer a floating interaction sphere anymore, your actions and body have weight. It feels incredibly natural to move, push and fight to the point where a seasoned VR user like myself had an actual issue adjusting to having a proper body. It was quickly adapted.

Guns - They're not H3 standard and some people became butthurt that they challenged H3VR's title of gun simulation king. That wasn't the objective. These firearms feel real. Your IRL Gun skill, if any, is actually going to play a huge role here. Pointing, switching mags and round counting are all a big part of this deal. (I also like how they're an in-universe gag)

CQC - Extending from Guns and Physics, CQC or Close Quarters Combat is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ludicrous in this title. The ability to dodge an enemies attack, grab their ankles and just outright bodyslam them into the floor is something I haven't experienced until now. It feels amazing. When you're stuck in melee, or hand to hand, you need velocity or a solid weight. You're able to grab enemies and use the environment to hurt them.

Atmosphere - MythOS is a place I never want to fully leave. I've routinely spend 4 hour sessions in this game and forgotten that I've been stood up the entire time. You're always engrossed, always piecing something together, always trying to find something that just might click everything to place. It's a wonderful feeling.

Soundtrack - My god, nothing compares. It's up there with DOOM 2016 for it's usage. You'll occasionally hear it in the background as you progress, it'll naturally swell as you search and fight enemies. Soon enough you've been absorbed by this feeling of adventure. It's a master crafted trackset that truly gives you the feeling that you're in an OS. There have been literal points where I've stopped to just take in the feeling, and it's something I hold dear to me now.

Story - It's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ WACK. In the good way. I will not say a word. You're left to interpret a little, and there's things you'll need to seek out but holy ♥♥♥♥. A condensed, mid-length easily 12 hour experience. Double it if you're going for a 100% run.

Thank you Stress Level Zero, you've given us a game that will define a whole new generation of VR. THIS is our standard folks. The game that (Allegedly) proved Valve wrong at their own VR Design. The title that redefined Half-Life: Alyx's physics based system and the game that just won my heart in 2019.

Absolutely 10/10.\t\t\t\t\t

I wanted to recommend this game. I wholeheartedly started this game wanting to love it. I remember seeing the first bit of gameplay over two years ago and getting overly hyped for it and I was hyped until release day. Upon playing it, I can't say that I recommend it.

First, having an actual BODY in VR is wonky, impossible to control, and so infuriatingly difficult to control that it makes the game borderline unplayable at times. There's a section where you have to cross a bunch of steel bars with a 3-inch gap between the bars and I fell through them about 15 times in a row. I almost threw my headset off in a fit of rage because it shouldn't be that hard and wouldn't be that hard if it wasn't for the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ body controls. Not to mention, about half the time you're trying to complete one of the ridiculous amount of physics puzzles (we'll get to that), your hands will suddenly morph into whatever you're trying to grab and you'll either fall and have to restart everything or it will kill you and you have to start the level over again. Yeah, THE LEVEL. BECAUSE THERE'S NO AUTOSAVE. IT'S 2019, WHY IS THERE NO AUTOSAVE??? THE PS2 FIGURED THAT OUT OVER A DECADE AGO.

Second, the sandbox mode is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. It's a nearly empty room where you spawn 2 types of enemy upon completion of the game and try to fight them. It's boring, it's stupid, and it doesn't contain half the weapons from the game itself. Before anyone jumps down my throat, I know you can use the reclamation bins to get the weapons to the sandbox mode, but I'll get to that gripe in a second. I don't see how anyone can defend the game's blatant disregard to throw away the physics gun that I FINALLY OBTAINED IN THE LAST LEVEL only to NOT GIVE ME A RECLAMATION BIN BEFORE ENDING THE GAME ABRUPTLY AND PUSHING ME BACK TO THE MAIN MENU. That was actually the straw that broke the camel's back for me and made me go to not recommended. (Also, you have to go out of your way and discover the module to actually start Sandbox mode. The devs locked a game mode behind a hidden collectible. What kind of nonsense is that?)

Third, the sights on guns handle like ass (primarily because you have an actual body to fight). Every time I'd mount my rifle to my shoulder like I'd do in real life, the player's body would always push the sight just a little off and I'd miss my shot. The sights on the ACOG M16 were also off. I positioned myself on my desk to balance, fired 3 shots at one of the weakest enemies in the game, had the sights directly on his head, and missed all three shots about an inch high. I finally just gave up and hipfired while praying the bullets would hit their target.

Fourth, ♥♥♥♥ MELEE WEAPONS. It's like trying to sling a sack of dead kittens to kill your enemy. I can't tell you how many times I died trying to swat someone away with a crowbar or a hammer or a ball bat or EVEN A ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ SWORD and it either wouldn't connect, wouldn't kill/stun, or would get the weapon trapped behind my body BECAUSE MY ARMS WOULD JUST SUDDENLY MORPH OUT OF MY BACK LIKE I BECAME PART ARACHNID OR SOMETHING. They were nothing short of mind-boggling in their incompetence.

Fifth, I do not understand why devs can't understand and follow this simple ideology; PHYSICS PUZZLES AND VR DO NOT GO TOGETHER. VR is about as easy to control as a bull with its nuts in a vice and physics puzzles require cautious, careful movements to go correctly. They compliment each other about as well as the final 2 candidates in the 2016 presidential election. Boneworks is littered with physics puzzles, something that was strangely omitted in the gameplays and trailers. The initial puzzle IN THE TUTORIAL took me almost 40 minutes to complete because of how terrible the jump mechanics are (who uses that tiny little hop? It looks like the player farted too hard and got about an inch off the ground). They have no place in VR games and anyone who says they work well should be tested for "medicinal mental enhancement".

Finally, don't play this game if you have the VIVE wands. My god, this game hates VIVE wands. The game peaks your vibrate motors out so they squeal like a lit pig. The game forces you to hold the grip and trigger buttons to hold anything and keep it held down the entire time, making using a melee weapon a test in grip strength and climbing a ladder ridiculous. There's also no way to disable vibrate or grip functions in game so you're stuck with getting hand cramps for 3 days after a couple hours with the game.

TL;DR, there's a good game in here somewhere, and the devs certainly put a lot of time and effort into this and should be rewarded for that. However, with the terrible use of VIVE wands, the wonky/glitchy body mechanics that make me want to cave my own skull in, the combat being eh at best, and not getting the guns we see in the trailers (balloon gun, physics gun, nowhere to be seen in the sandbox mode or near a reclamation bin), I can't recommend this game. I've waited two years and tried so hard to love this game, only to be disappointed and left feeling a little ripped off.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my second review of BONEWORKS. First review was positive, and this one will be as well. I do want to talk about the game's shortcomings this time around though (its strengths are well known.)

- In comparison to Blade and Sorcery the melee combat in BONEWORKS is too springy. Gripping and moving your hand up/down the weapon could also be better. B&S is sort of the gold standard at this point for good reason, The combat in BONEWORKS is still spectacular, so A- here

- In comparison to H3VR the shooting mechanics are too rigid. gripping certain sections of the gun feels clunky. The gun can rest against the players body like in real life (sounds like a good thing, but can lead to wonky behavior.) Aiming down sight with your left eye requires holding the gun out far. I ended up mostly using pistols as they were easy to handle. I reluctantly give this area a B-.

- Climbing is amazing in some ways, but can certainly improve. The head bobbing never made me sick, but it certainly makes it much more frustrating to climb than it should be. Certain objects that seem like the should be climbable don't grip. Climbing walls can lead to your hands going behind the wall. I'd say the gold standard for climbing is Climbey (minus the part where you can throw yourself around the map) Here I'd give BW an B+

- Story is good, but nothing to really write home about. It is a VR game where you are inside of a virtual world. We've seen that before.

- Level design is great! lots of hidden areas to explore, environmental storytelling, and secrets to collect. But good lord if you get tired in VR quick wait until they add the save feature before you start playing.

- Game length is great for a VR title. It took me 10-12 hours to complete.

BONEWORKS: A game that probably could have used a little more polish to iron out the wonkiness in some of its physics interactions, but is overall excellent and a highly recommended title. 9/10 game.\t\t\t\t\t
