\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, like many VR games, it seems like Boneworks has problems with working for non-typical player heights. My girlfriend and I both tried out the game, but could barely manage about twenty minutes each before motion sickness got the better of us. The firearms handled alright, but melee weapons and climbing did not feel remotely right.
We played on Oculus, so it may be that the game performs better on Index, as it seems to have been designed for that headset instead, but I definitely wouldn't say that it's worth the price tag.


Edit 1:

After the response from the Developer, I'm glad to hear about the save points, and striving to improve upon the game. That said, the initial point I was making has been missed.
Heavy locomotion in VR has never been an issue for either myself, or my girlfriend, and we play other VR games such as Blade and Sorcery frequently. The issue that we had was in not being calibrated to our height, and instead being stretched, or shrank into a match for the player character giving severe motion sickness. Imagine rolling around standing on top of a computer chair with grabber claws in each hand, and you will have the picture of the sensation for someone of my height. For my girlfriend on the other hand, it's a deceptive depth perception, and feeling as though she's almost down on her knees at all times, not being able to see things she would normally be able to.

The game shows promise in being a VR sandbox-like game akin to some others, but with significantly more diverse content than most. The core of the issue that we have had with this game, is the height calibration, which is usually the problem that we've run into when playing in VR.
Locomotion has not been the issue we ran into, though it might have been a problem for some people.

Edit 2:

So, the developers show blatant hatred for critical opinions evidently. I come to edit this post now after asking toward the community if the calibration problems experienced previously had been fixed, or if there were plans to do so. In a single day however, I have been banned from said forum for attempting to make the difficulty known. My first thread was locked, second was responding without any attempt to comprehend the very concept of what was being said, then locked as well.
After the locking of the second thread in question, came the ban.

Beyond simply disregarding problems with their games, and ignoring criticism, they now opt to silence voices on the platform that don't suit them. Both of my threads have since been deleted already.

TL:DR - Developers ignore the actual problem claiming to understand. When asked directly, confirm that calibration problem is intended experience, continue to ignore actual problem, attempt to silence the player. Continue trying to convey misunderstanding, receive multiple responses still ignoring complaint, then silencing the player again by deleting threads, and banning user from forums.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe best way to experience this game is to make your own fun. That's not to say the game is lacking in content or effort; Boneworks gives you control over your body and the environment in never-before-seen ways, but it's up to you to take advantage of that in a way that feels fun, rather than simply "point-and-shoot"ing your way through the entire game. Melee combat is overall more fun than guns (Although it does suck pretty bad against the "Omni-Turret" Hazmat guys.) It's worth noting that the death and respawn system in this game is very lenient, with a "Fight-For-Your-Life" borderlands style second chance, so you have lots of freedom to just mess around. The only reason you really have to conserve ammo is to unlock god-tools for sandbox, but if you really want that it's possible to cheat for it.

Months later I'm still regularly hopping into Boneworks to fool around in the sandbox mode, so I thought I'd modify this review to express my love for both this game and its creators. Every now and then, the developers release content updates to the game, all of which are completely free for those who already own the game. Not only do they add new weapons, but they add new levels, and entire mechanics if necessary.

When I first reviewed the game, many people, me included, noticed the game lacked a checkpoint-saving system, which meant that the game only saved between levels, which can last over an hour. In response, the dev team added a checkpoint saving system. I want to make it very clear how absolutely fantastic it is that they listened and fixed the problem.

Then, in praising one content update (The Redacted Chamber), I noted how much I loved being pushed to use both the environment around me and strategy to defeat larger amounts of enemies. Shortly after, Boneworks received a "COD:Zombies" esque gamemode, which fills this craving better than any idea I could have come up with.

I wish steam had more than a yes/no rating system, because Boneworks would be a definite 5/5 stars. I can't wait to see what else Stress Level Zero has up its sleeve, both for Boneworks and VR in general.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWarning: This will be a VERY in-depth review. Going from gun-play, parkour, enemies, etc...

Start: The gun-play, the parkour, how you move and pick up objects, it was great. Some things were hard, (Like pulling yourself up something while in the air and trying to aim down sights without your head pulling the gun down) but it was a neat challenge. The learning curve was great. Goes a bit slow, getting you into things, then medium, then fast since you've found out how to play.

The enemies were diverse starting from basic geometry with "fire-wire" like stuff to go around the body to resemble a "human." The enemies then went to small spider-like drones, then balls with guns (They were my most hated enemy in the game, easy to kill but damaging.), then zombies and yourself. They were great enemies with different ways to be killed.

The parkour was my second-hated thing in the game. It's hard to pull yourself up things while in the air, but I think that's the point. It's easy to vault over things WHEN YOU'RE ON THE GROUND and jump. Here are all the different jump ways to...jump? [Insert deep inhale here] You can tap the jump button, hold the jump button, crouch and jump in real life while holding down the jump button in the game, you can go from the top of the touchpad to the bottom making you go to a "crawl" then jump super high, you can jump then go forward/backwards/left/right, you can move WSAD and THEN jump, you can jump and grab something, and finally jump and knock down enemies. [Long exhale]

The gun play was great. Being able to take your magazine out with another magazine, use your hands, etc, is great. Everything on this was great...Eeeexcept your head getting in the way. Besides that, great.

The atmosphere was also great. The rain with the clocktower and the breakroom. The emptiness of the streets, like everyone has decided to stay inside or has either moved was also mysterious and great. Who built this box that can pick things up with no gravity? Who brought spray paint with them to go in the sewers and spray paint the ladders, Why is there all these things when they were only developing a city simulation?

It's all cool, mysterious, awesome, very easy to get used to, and more. It's one of the greatest VR titles I've played... 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs literally any promotion of this game will let you know, fantastic physics. They can get clunky and annoying to deal with at times, especially with clambering up things (for this it's best to have virtual crouching on) but overall, they definitely added a ton to how one would experience VR.
Confusing story and features though, still have no idea what the disposal and vending machine type thing are for, nor how money works either (I think it might have been just me who missed those explanations though) But the story still is hard to follow, what most people will see are simplistic type puzzles that utilizes this game's fantastic physics, and a bunch of shooting and whacking.

Decent length, took me 5 some hours to complete on my first run, with also a sandbox and arena mode, though neither being particularly enjoyable. As of this review's date, there isn't much to the sandbox yet. mainly just a couple guns and shapes to mess around with, and that's about it.\t\t\t\t\t
