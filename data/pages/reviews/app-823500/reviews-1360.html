\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been anticipating this game for a while now, the trailers and tech demo's on Youtube defiantly hyped me up as it showed many levels of detail that no other VR game has performed. I jumped into this game and i will tell you it is amazing, the physics and general mechanics are fantastic. I haven't completed the tutorial phase at this point and was unable to, due to being so overwhelmed with the greats of detail that was beyond my virtual experience.

From what i have seen so far, there are MANY weapons. The one feature i love in this game is how your enemies react so realistically to your attacks, if you shoot someone's leg they will collapse and crawl or if you simply smash an opponent in the head with a brick they will violently thump to the floor. Extra +++'s go to their addition to a slo-mo button which can be configured with taps of a button on how slowed down you want the game enabling you to pull off some great manoeuvres.

The game is very fluent and i encountered no issues despite using a Oculus Rift S set-up, i was concerned that my experience was not going to be as good as the trailers and videos due to not using the Index controllers however my concern was proved wrong from my time playing today.

Overall, this singular VR game has a bit of everything in it, so i highly recommend the purchase as you wont find anything as mighty detailed as this.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game's physics is what's on display here, and they are impressive, but also kind of quirky in ways that make the beginning sections difficult. It's possible to lock up some of the physics puzzles. Some of the map design isn't very straightforward. The holstering and reloading "grab regions" don't always seem to be consistent.
But despite all that I still want to finish this game because it's doing something very right. Something about the exploratory feeling of traversing the maps, the satisfying physics of smashing boxes and enemies, and the guns. It is an experience best suited for "VR experts" and people who have their "VR legs" as the game says at the beginning. But if you have a VR headset and you're looking for some quality content this one is worth picking up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly anticipated VR game for me. I've been hyped for this game ever since it was first shown on the "Node" YouTube channel.

The game features extensive physical interaction with objects, weapons, and the environment. The Stress Level Zero team also took extra care to make sure holding and using weapons (blunt and ballistic) is intuitive and easy to pick up, even for those who don't use a gun frame/mounts. Guns in this game handle better then Pavlov and Contractors, and I'd wager its right at or slightly above the level of onward.

However, as a left handed shooter, sometimes I end up grabbing a mag instead of my pistol, when reaching down my left side. I usually haft to grab my left butt cheek, to grab the weapon. Which is fine, and can be learned, but slightly annoying when it comes down to a fire fight.

The only downside to majority of the movement, and maybe its just me being a noob, is I cant seem to "lift" myself onto a conex container, or over a wall. Even when trying to crouch to bring my legs up, I can't manipulate my legs in a way to make them latch up, or allow me to vault over.

Overall, I highly recommend this game to anyone with a VR headset, as this is one of the games people will point to when talking about what VR and VR Games can achieve.

Good luck to the dev team and I cant wait to see what they build next!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI believe that Boneworks is the best VR game created to this date.
There's too much to say about this game, so I'll keep it short:
The physics simulations are incredibly realistic and satisfying.
The inverse kinematics are very precise and accurate, nearly perfectly matching your real world body except obviously when swinging heavy weapons, using slow motion, etc. Having a physical body really helps immersion and reduces the feeling of motion sickness.
I haven't finished the game as of writing this, so I can't comment on the story, enemy variety, weapon variety, etc because I haven't seen it all yet.
I think one of the best things about this game is just how well it runs on my computer. I had thought it would run terribly because of how good it looks and how advanced the physics are, but it runs at a smooth 90 FPS on my 1070 and i7 without needing to turn any of the graphics down from the defaults. I can even slightly supersample it.\t\t\t\t\t
