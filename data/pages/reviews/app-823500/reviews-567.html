\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo be honest, this game is a little bit of a cluster♥♥♥♥. If you're not careful, your arms will clip through fences like a hot knife through butter, I found most of the enemies to be hilariously weak what with the whole Second Wind mechanic, and the story felt like an exercise in futility that turned the game from the folly of an immortality seeker into a journey through a VR wonderland that has a one-way rabbit hole.

But thankfully, this is a game about, well, the game.
The physics make experimentation fun and exciting and make hand-to-hand worth doing, the guns are 𝘧𝘪𝘯𝘦 but lack variety that would make them more than a tool (and the game gives you waaay too much ammo in my opinion(and before you say "But the Big Prize machine in the Time Tower!" I wanted those to be challenges like the New Game+ items in RE7,(beat the game in under 4 hours, only use melee and pistols, etc.) not a collectathon, but that's beside the point)), and the melee? For a game as focused on guns as it is, the melee is not only fun, but practical as well. Not all enemies are worth spending ammo on, or perhaps it's just way too fun to skewer a Headset with a medieval dagger, or, say, slow time and try out a three-stroke combo you've been practicing on a hapless zombie.

I should wrap up. Levels are pretty good, but one or two will make your computer chug if it isn't up to snuff (I have a 1660 and a Ryzen 3 2200G and I did 72 FPS regularly except in the giant Marketplace near the end). OST is nice, but a few of the songs sound a bit pretentious. Like, it just might be, but a few of the songs think this game is the coolest ♥♥♥♥ 𝐞𝐯𝐞𝐫. Personal favorites are Ambient Travel for being such a chill song, perfect for screwing around with mods or just practicing melee combos on training dummies, Opera de Ossium for being a great song for just about any climax, but especially for this game's climax, and Constant Rising for being the song right after Opera de Ossium that sounds exactly like what it says on the tin.
One more thing: The game says "EXPERT VR USERS ONLY" and you've probably read the Store Page description that headlines with "advanced VR mechanics and concepts". If you decide to install and play the Melon Vault campaign mod, you'll know that's a white lie, but it isn't kidding on strongly recommending previous VR experience. If this is your first VR experience, you 𝘸𝘪𝘭𝘭 be knocked on your ass by one of the set pieces at least once. This isn't a strict requirement and I can't make you do what I think is right, but some lighter fare of the same ilk I can suggest is Arizona Sunshine or The Walking Dead: Saints and Sinners, depending on how light or dark you want the tone of your combination VR sickness and CQB training to be.

Final verdict: BONEWORKS... is a game. And for a while that was (and still is) a compliment. In a world that was (and still is) full of VR "experiences" and applications and... whatever VRChat and Rec Room falls into, BONEWORKS is a complete game, with all the fixings. A story, an arena with multiple modes, a set of sandboxes, and mod support to boot. If you're still getting sick by just using locomotion, you should probably wait until you have more experience with the headset until you hardly experience a thing. But if you're ready for the next step up in VR gaming (and/or spot it on sale), give BONEWORKS a try.\t\t\t\t\t
