\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is, if not, one of the best single-player VR games I have ever played in my list so far. It takes a lot for me to actually get immersed into a game, and for the first time in awhile, Boneworks does that. The amount of interactions you can have between the props, levels, weapons, enemies and yourself is indefinite. Feel the need to absurdly stab the hell out of someone? Do it against the enemy nords or null bodies. Want to have the feeling of those head crabs from Half Life jumping onto your head? Well you have come to the right place. Some of the puzzles will have you think a bit harder than you need to. The game doesn't need gimmicky visuals to pull you in... it does it with the subtle, easy to follow and interesting story. Even without the need of special effects or amazing visuals, the graphics and levels will make you look around and enhance that feeling of "Being in the Matrix". Boneworks is not a $60 overpriced single player game that's short lived. $30 for this title is a mere steal, especially if it's on sale. I absolutely recommend this game. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

Oh man, where do start? I have heard people only praising this game, but for me, I dont feel it. This just feels like tech demo and the content here is really weird.

So whats so bad about this game? It really tries to sell itself as this great VR game for "experts", but it falls short on its promises. Yes, really. When you start the game its shows warning that its for "expert" VR players. What it means is that there is only one locomotion and no teleport.

The game itself is not anything new nor does it do anything new. Gun mechanics in VR games have always been quite the same. Here its even more simpler since magazine auto eject if you put new in. Dont have anything here more to say. Guns are like 1911, glock, MP5, AR15 etc.

But the content for this game really is lacking. The level design is really weird. Levels are huge and they are quite empty. They feel like somebody was doing a tutorial on map editor first map, since they are so bad. Items are just duplicated next to each other and really weird and dont have any unified style to them. Texture are awful and seems like default ones.

Enemies are not varied. There are three types of them. AI than usually attacks by punching, if you have gun then they are no threat. Enemies that shoot back with guns. Third is somekind of crab(crablet) who jump at you. So those spider are quite fast, and compared to Half Life Alyx where jumping is quite slow then here they are quite fast, like too fast for VR game.

Physics puzzle are not really puzzle, but more of an hassle/chore. Solving them is more like fighting with the physics engine than thinking about the solution. I think developers should have thought more about if they should have put them in the first place, but this game really is like tech demo, where they tried to put stuff so it would have more features.

Also single player campaign is quite short, Levels are dull, Ending levels are more varies but still we dont see any good level design there.

Verdict: I have hard time recommending this game. For me its too raw and looks like unfinished game. There are also some minigames here like sandbox, etc. that might keep you interested.\t\t\t\t\t
