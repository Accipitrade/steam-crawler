\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMost of my VR time is on Pavlov VR and Beat saber so when I played this game I thought the physics were very strange, very gimmicky as I thought "why would you add faux virtual weight, this won't work".

I played the game through some more and I got used to the physics and it became surprisingly immersive. Getting used to adding mental weight onto things you're using is pretty strange, but it just works once you're used to it. You can really get into the combat side of things when you're used to it as you don't just have floating hands, you have an actual BODY which you can use; one of my favourite things to do is to headbutt enemies. The 'story' in my opinion at least is just more of an experience than it is anything else, but it kept me going well enough, the puzzles were a real highlight for the story. Sandbox is great of course plus the other extras, can have plenty of fun even after finishing the main game.

My biggest gripe is that this game is TOO GOOD. Going back to playing pavlov [in my experience] or any other similar VR game leaves me wishing i was just playing Boneworks again. At first the gunplay seems a bit odd in Boneworks, the shooting especially seemed OVERLY snappy, but again when I was used to it I found myself preferring it. The only way any other VR game competes with Boneworks is through sheer difference in content + multiplayer + modding support, if this game had those it would be unbeatable in every department. They have a great base game here if they make anymore games.

TL;DR Game so good it ruins other VR games.\t\t\t\t\t
