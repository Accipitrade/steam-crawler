\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh boy where do I even start with this game.. Boneworks is a fantastic title but also mixture of emotions.

Firstly, this game gonna be honest is downright terrible on vive wands, and it didn't help that mine where partially broken at the time I had first booted this game. So, as i had been planning for awhile was to replace them with Index Controllers and well.. my soured opinion of this game was completely turned around.

Boneworks is a great game that demonstrates what can, could, and maybe should be done for VR games to come. I would go as much to say this game is far more mechanically pleasing than Half-Life Alyx due to this games sheer amount of environmental interactivity. Plenty of boxes, crates and items to toss around and mess with, handling guns as the way it should be, and a physical inventory. (ex. grabbing gun from holster) Even being able to just even touch my enemies, like pushing them over with my bare hands is such a huge immersion boost and gives the player plenty of options to work with when controlling a crowd too. Being able to physically grab ledges and pipes to allow me to climb around where I please was such a great feeling unlike any other VR game I've played.

This game also addresses the various issues that bothered me specifically with HL:A, such as ammo grabbing from over the shoulder or not being able to jump or climb, propkilling, and just not IK rigged arms coming from my own view, and not having a physical body inventory. these are all things boneworks has and actually does.

Now this game isn't perfect there is most certainly some wonkiness at times with how the game handles physics on your full collision character but it's not the worst thing ever either. The plot of this game is very slim and well, I'm not really sure if its much of a plot to begin with, but I hardly think that was the point of this game as I played through its strange levels and puzzles.

Ultimately, I believe many other VR games should follow some of things that boneworks did to immerse players, because Interactivity is almost the entire game when it comes to VR, and that's exactly what boneworks is.. the ability to interact with everything in your vicinity.\t\t\t\t\t
