!!MAJOR STORY SPOILERS FOR BONEWORKS AHEAD; PROCEED EITH CAUTION!!

Oh man, where do I begin?

Boneworks, before I got it, was a game I had heard about from my friend. He was pretty hyped about it, so—feeling generous—I gifted him the game and decided to play it myself since he and I shared libraries between us both.

Now, a lot of the games I played in VR were either ♥♥♥♥♥♥ horror games or mediocre first-person shooters, so I didn't really expect much from it. Before playing it, I got Duck Season, something I can (unfortunately and embarrassingly) blame Game Theory on. I had no idea it was made by Stress Level Zero, a partner of Corridor Digital due to Jake's legal affiliation with both Corridor and SLZ (at least I think he's the legal for Corridor).

Duck Season itself was really fun and intriguing going through on my own, but when I found out they made Boneworks as well, I felt the need to play it. Oh my god, was I not disappointed.

The gameplay was absolutely something else, the gunplay and melee combat being some of the most fun parts of the game. The environments were also really nice to traverse and climb my way through (when given the opportunity). The enemy AI is a bit janky when it comes to the nullbodies, the zombies, and their variants. The 'human' AI was slightly better when it came to combat, but that's only because they were competent enough to throw a punch one after the other instead of throwing a slap every now and then. The Crablets and OmniProjectors don't ♥♥♥♥ around though, you'll have the most trouble with them, especially when they're in groups.

The story and aesthetic is something else, though. From what I could tell, you're a working employee of Monogon, the main story antagonist, under false pretenses. With the risk of being caught, you use a virus to get yourself into MythOS, Monogon's own virtual reality open-world hub, and into the VoidWay (that of which MythOS is built upon). Along the way, you get small hints that you really aren't meant to be there and aren't quite doing what Mike Diva's character and the other woman suspect you to be doing, the hints at their peak when they mention how you're being attacked or how there's activity from the OmniProjectors when there shouldn't be. The woman (who I just now realized is named Alora B.) exposes to you that she had figured out everything you were doing was a ruse, showing off the USB Gammon—a Nintendo equivalent entertainment company in the Stress Level Zero Universe and competitor to Monogon—had given you to inject the virus into the system and get into the VoidWay. It basically ends with you climbing a ladder, that of which rivals Metal Gear Solid 3: Snake Eater, after defeating a handful of Ford clones and walk into a large room, where you hopelessly watch yourself get shot and killed by Monogon personnel. However, because you supposedly managed to gain immortality because of the VoidWay, you're now stuck in MythOS, helplessly watching yourself die.

Oh, and the music ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ slaps, like it's so good when it kicks in during combat. The arena survival and other challenges are also a lot of fun, especially if you've managed to save the weapons you find during the main story with the reclamation bins. Any weapon you claim on your journey will be taken with you to the arena, where you can select whatever gun or melee weapon you've found along the way and use them to full effect in the arena.

I think my only gripe is that, depending on how well you are with the game, it might take between an hour-and-a-half and 10+ hours. The difficulty might also fluctuate HEAVILY depending on whatever part of the game you're on, especially in areas with lots of OmniProjectors.

Overall? 9/10

TL;DR

PROS
  • Fun and Satisfying Gunplay and Melee Combat
  • Engaging Story
  • Great Music
  • Lots Replay Value

CONS
  • Frustrating Difficulty Spikes
  • Fairly Short

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood VR game and utilizes The Virtual Reality aspect very well. It has a lot of content and the game is good at having you just mess around.

But...
The game is very annoying with its overemphasis on its physics. You are battling against the physics A LOT. It frustrated me so much just trying to do something that is suppoused to be simple.

One thing that I learned playing VR games is that VR games needs to help and automate for you in most cases. Navigating in a VR world is NOT like navigating in real life, and thus a game has to service a lot of simple actions for you in order to attain control, or atleast feel like you have control. BoneWorks does almost the direct opposite, but... its fine. The game is not bad enough for me to lampool it, its just this one aspect of being so physical also consequently making simply playing it more difficult. I mean, the physics dont really make the game that much more fun. I love the ambitious system that it provides but I still only interact with this system with the certain tools I am given.

The only times the physics really satisfied me was: Reaching a higher edge with a hook of some sort, opening a gate by pulling it with an axe (another hook). Certain ragdolls flailing due to my actions.

But it frustrated me more than it satisfied me :(


Level Design!
I also do not like the level design of the main story. The lead up to the gun range had a much more satisfying, although linear path. That is because most of its design is readable, while in the main story, so many puzzles cant be seen as a puzzle just yet because you dont see the problem yet. The levels are also HUGE for no apparent reason, making me explore and walk around big empty venues and the game just absolutely feels like an alpha many times.

You would think that this is an aesthetic of "playing through an unfinished game" but it came as a bad taste in it, rather than a good one. I could clearly feel the levels getting better the closer the ending I got. The sewer level can absolutely F off. Such a terrible level. I am sorry.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is very experimental and buggy. It has amazing potential. It wants to do everything, one can do in VR but fails at every single one. Reloading is a huge hassle. Climbing is a huge hassle. Just moving around is a huge hassle. Top it all of, everything is physics based. OH JOY. It makes doing the simplistic tasks big challenge.

The level design is obtuse, weird and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ long. And I really do mean, they're ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ long. You can easily waste a hour beating a single level. I had to look up walkthroughs multiple times just to beat the first three levels because you can easily get distracted what seems like a puzzle but it's just set dressing.

The level design is really that bad. Apparently, the devs thought players would beat the levels within 20 minutes. But they realized it took players on average a hour to play a single section. So they added saving points because THE PLAYERS COULDN'T FINISH THE LEVEL within a reasonable amount of time. And even then, the save points are still far apart. So expect playing a hour or two before you see your first save point. Unless you want to speedrun the parts you already playthrough.

This game would be suited for a sandbox, but the devs locked away sandbox mode behind their half baked story mode. You have to beat the entire story mode just to play around with the game's many toys.

Seriously, stay away from this game unless you're willing to torture yourself with the dev's idea of fun. (Throwing everything at the wall and see what sticks.) I would only recommend this game if you enjoy the idea of being beta tester, but you're the one paying for the experience.

Only buy this if you're want more VR titles to rot your library.\t\t\t\t\t
