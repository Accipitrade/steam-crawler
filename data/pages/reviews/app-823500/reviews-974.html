\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStress Level Zero does great work and this was a pretty ambitious project. What they accomplished is pretty impressive and fun in a lot of ways but it could be improved in some massive ways, as well.

For starters, be warned that this game is not for newbies to VR at all. The only locomotion mode is smooth loco and the physics cause you to be pushed and pulled around constantly. It's only recommended for VR veterans and the game itself warns as much.

It's also really janky. Your limbs get stuck on all kinds of crap, you sometimes pull a large melee weapon of a slot and find it's caught around your neck or something, you trip over corpses and items littered about, mantling boxes is a pain as they tend to wobble all over the place... I could go on and on.

The story is pretty barebones and has some issues I can't really get into without spoiling anything. For the majority of the game, though, you get a couple videos on TVs urging you toward the clock and then in the final act things get weird. The world building is great but the story doesn't really do enough with it, I feel.

Overall, it's worth experiencing just don't expect a flawless game.\t\t\t\t\t
