\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor not even 30€ it's a great deal. It's fun to play an to watch friends play.

But fist my critique:
- Only at the end of the game I stared to develop the feeling of really knowing what I'm doing. The tutorial (museum) felt rather annoying for someone who already played some time in VR and just really forced on me.
- The puzzles often are annoying. Usually I like puzzles but here they often feel out of place, are slow in executing the solution and generally slowing the gameplay down to a degree which becomes annoying.
- You can't take objects/tools/weapons from one level to another. This makes sense from a developer perspective since that makes it much easier to plan how a level can be handled. But it feels like the easy way out. For me as the player it feels like losing progress.
- Sometimes it's not clear where you have to go. In two levels I was just annoyed running around trying to find the right path. In one instance I accidentally pushed a hard-to-reach button twice which closed the path again, but I didn't noticed and due to a lot of useless doors/buttons in the game I figured it was just another troll.
- Climbing feels good until you reach the ledge. Getting over a ledge and pulling yourself up was always some fidgeting around until I actually stood ontop of the thing I just climbed.
- Every time I died I dropped everything I had in my hands right where I died. This seems unfair because now I have to get to where I died but without any weapons.

What I find great:
- You can solve problems your way. I played, explored and found a gun. Later I found a fence with a locked door, so I shot the lock and went though. A friend didn't found the gun and encountered the fence, so he just climbs over it. I didn't even saw that as a possibility.
- Everything interacts with everything physically. You want the loot on the top shelve? Climb the rack! Or just tilt the whole rack and bring the top shelve to ground level. Or maybe you want to get something to jump on to reach the loot.
- The levels are big enough to go explore and find ... melons? Only few loading times!

In the game you can, in a lot of places, find the writing on the wall "what if this is just a tech demo!?". This describes the game the best way. It feels unpolished in a lot of ways, like this "game" is just the core mechanics of something bigger. Similar to a picture where the frame and context are missing.\t\t\t\t\t
