\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel like this game is what VR will be in the years to come, but I get incredibly ill playing it. I'm unable to play for longer than an hour at a time without feeling extremely nauseated.

I would think that I'd be a "VR Expert" after roughly 2,000 hours accumulated in various VR games, but games with smooth motion have always been too much for me. I'm glad I bought the game to support the dev, even if I can't enjoy it for very long before trying not to vom.

The only thing aside from extreme motion sickness that I found unacceptable was getting halfway through "Streets" , only to suddenly clip between two objects and fall through the world, killing me.

I'll likely use a save editor to unlock arena and sandbox because I don't know if I can beat the game without making myself ill, and honestly that just isn't enjoyable.

The design is cool, there are neat Easter eggs, the physics are buttery smooth, and the massive amount of love the devs put into the game make it worth at least trying.

Although I doubt I'll ever be able to fully enjoy the game without something like a VR treadmill, or a different motion system, I'm pleased to see the direction VR games as a whole are heading, and I'm hopeful for the future.

Thank you stress level zero for pushing the industry in the right direction.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not that far in to it, I'll be honest. I just got to the part of the tutorial where you start with firearms. Unfortunately, for a game which prides itself in physics realism, the firearms in the game are laughable. Gripping a pistol with two hands starts slow-motion without my asking, which is annoying. There is no magazine-release button for any firearm, you must reach up and force it out of the weapon. Holstering and un-holstering is awkward as they force under-arm holsters upon you for some reason.
Weapons clip through objects and characters, and stocks on rifles are not usable as they also just clip through the player.
Lastly, the game prides itself that everything is a physics object. Unfortunately, there's a reason not many games do this: Objects when handled by the player love to glitch around horribly and clip through things in the most laughable way possible.
This is just a trashy tech-demo. Not worth $30. Maybe $10 at most. Pass!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor now, I have to give this a "not recommended".

Pros:
The game launched without issues.

Cons:
The entire experience consisted of being locked in a room with a coffee mug being told by a robotic voice to "reach out with your hand and grab the object in front of you", repeated at ~3 second intervals.

Try as I might, there was no way to grab anything. I reached out with my hand. I waited for any kind of indicator. I tried every possible angle I could think of. Every possible position of my fingers. I tried *not* waiting for an indicator. I tried emulating the animated hands to either side of the object. Eventually, I even tried climbing a pillar in the room, which didn't work. The whole time, that message on fast repeat like I was locked in some kind of reeducation camp.

No information about this online. I search and get nothing but promotional material.

Nope, sorry. Not for me. I'll give this one more try some other time. If picking up an object is still beyond me--a seasoned gamer with nearly 30 years of combined experience in every genre known to man--then it's refund time.\t\t\t\t\t
