\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is hands doen my favorite VR game. I feel confident in saying that even when compaired to games like half life alyx. This game does have its fair share of flaws, however. The story in this game is incomprehendable. I dont understand what I am doing or why I am doing it. I dont understand why the enemies are there, I dont understand the ending. That is by far the games biggest drawback. I feel like there was alot more potential for story in this game, but it was told so badly that I have no intrest in hearing it anymore.
One of the biggest gameplay flaws at launch was the save situation, and even though they have made it better, they should have taken the time to implement a better system. one where it would save everything in the level rather then savepoints.
There are many things I could nitpick about this game, like ammo locations, and framerate drops on certian levels, but I feel like those can be looked past.
But, now onto the reasons I love this game so much. This game has amazing combat. You have so much freedom to do whatever you want. Because enemies are physics based, you can move them around. Throw them off buildings, smash them into walls, drag them around the whole level. You can make your own play style however you want.
The transportation in this game is astonishing. The climbing has some issues, but more is good about it then not. The walking and running feels great at most times.

Overall, this game raised the bar for vr games. This game still blows me away when i play it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've never reviewed a game here but I genuinely think this game could be great one day so here are my 2 cents.

PROS
- The guns feel great. I only had the chance to try 4 of them but they all felt solid and accurate.
- The animations are great. Stabbing a mannequin with a sword never gets old.
- The physics are some of the best in VR, even before Half Life came along. I enjoyed shooting the one gun I had access to during my 90 min session.
- The guns sound great.

Cons
- The menu design. The very first menu, where you select levels and story. I'm not an idiot, but I've been exposed to so many great interfaces that when I see a ♥♥♥♥ one, it stumps me. Why is the selection for Sandbox modes hidden and only accessible by CLICKING THE MENU THAT SAYS 'STORY'. No indication whatsoever. Small things like this bug me, especially in this day and age where a monkey could design a better UI.
- The long winded tutorial sequence, called the MUSEUM, is poorly designed and absolutely unecessary. ​ Do people need help with VR controls, absolutely, but please do so in an interesting and intuitive way. I don't need literal wall to wall instructions and a repetitive tut that is unskippable. Half Life does this as part of its level progression, and it feels natural and not forced.
- Level design is ugly. This game was clearly designed by technical nerds and not actual level designers.
- None of the weapons are available. It actually makes you play the story mode to unlock weapons for the sandbox arena. This is stupid, as the entire game was never promoted to be a story driven one. Why not have them unlocked for the other half of your customers who just want to mess around? Small gripe, but i have a feeling a lot of people will agree with me.
- The controls are clunky for HTC users like myself. It was clearly designed for the google index.

To be fair, the only reason i bought this game was to play sandbox mode, spawn things and shoot them silly. Games like Blade and Sorcery does this well, and it doesnt make you go through hoops to get what you want.

VERDICT
Worth a shot, if you absolutely positively love VR and don't agree with anything I say. But personally it was absolutely disappointing. There's a lot of potential here, but unfortunately the devs all sat on it way too long and Half Life came and karate kid swept the ideas from under your feet and packaged it into a nice story. Next time hire an actual creative team and do more than a glorified tech demo.

Difficulty : moderate, if your used to how guns in vr work.
Graphics : pretty good, no rendering issues, runs well.
Physics: Amazing, you can grab pretty much anything and no objects go through each other.
