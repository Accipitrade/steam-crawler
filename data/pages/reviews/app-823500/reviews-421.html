\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: The best VR Experience I've played to date. Well optimized, well written, and well worth your time.

Hardware: Samsung Odyssey, with a 1080gtx (great experience) and a 3080gtx (even better experience)
Ryzen 3900x
32gb 3600mhz ram

My experience with VR: been playing around since the Oculus DK2 (TUSCANY!!!!) days. More time in VORPx than in good full blown VR titles with shooters, admittedly. I have an iron stomach with motion sickness.

Boneworks is special. Prior to playing it, I could never wholeheartedly recommend VR as a platform to my friends and family. Titles like Arizona Sunshine and Beat Saber can catch your attention for some time, but no other titles to date have created such a tailored narrative experience in with cutting edge VR mechanics with mostly HUGE success.

While playing on a Samsung Odyssey, I only had occasional 'VR' hiccups; such as my virtual body's head getting stuck between monkeybars. These moments were few and far between, however. Shakiness with weapons and awkward above-head tracking can be attributed to my Odyssey and still, weren't too much of a hindrance. I was able to move through the obstacles, physics puzzles, and even fly through the air with ease after an hour or two of practice.

Story-wise, I strongly urge you to play Duck Season prior to playing Boneworks. I won't spoil anything, but Boneworks is gentle in its narrative delivery (like Half-life....little exposition). You'll miss a lot of context if you aren't prepared. I personally loved the mysterious and 'meta' delivery of the narrative; although it may tick off less patient players.

The game performs like a dream. I'd run it originally on a 1080GTX with great native results on my Odyssey. After upgrading to a 3080RTX, I was able to scale the resolution 33% further with SteamVR to AMAZING results! I'd highly recommend trying to upscale if your rig will allow you.

That said, you will want to be pushing at least 90FPS in Boneworks to get the most out of the amazing PHYSICS in the game. Many puzzle and gameplay elements are directly married to the physics engine. I can't think of a game with more believable physics.... from stabbing, to throwing, to bullet ballistics, to real-world interactivity...Boneworks does it best.

This game has redefined how I see VR as a storytelling medium. It has also changed how I see physical movement limitations. My stomach has learned to not TURN when grabbing onto a massive windmill's arm to be thrown into the air to reach to the top of a nearby cliff. All the while, I had an amazing time doing it and am now obsessed with Stress Level Zero's developing metaverse.

10/10. Sad that I finished it and am out of content. There's a big void in my life :/\t\t\t\t\t
