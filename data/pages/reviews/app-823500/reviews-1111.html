Climbing takes some getting used to but works. Melee combat is excellent! The puzzles are interesting so far, nothing super hard. Aiming for pistols is excellent, I love being able to steady a pistol with my secondary hand. Assault rifles aiming seems a tiny bit off but works after playing around with it.

Once the null bodies went from mesh outlines to zombie-ish dudes it was a whole different game! I cant wait to see where the devs take this game. They already have been cranking out updates at what I would consider a furious pace. A true must play for VR as it shows the future of being able to interact with any object in a game world.

I wish they had mid level saves and I understand they're adding that to the game. I understand where they're going with no saves mid level but for someone with kids and a busy schedule being able to at least mid level save will be very helpful to get through the game

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was played using the Oculus Rift S.

I really, really wanted to like this game, but the controls and the no-clipping mechanics of the game are what killed it for me. I dislike the weight mechanic because it makes the arm movents sluggish, falling behind where my hands are. I found my head positioning relative to the floor kept going wonky in certain situations (something I haven't experienced in other games) and caused a lot of dizziness. I think this game is ambitious and it does a lot of things that aren't often done in VR. I don't think this type game is for me, as the movement feels less like it's designed for VR and more for a controller, but given the limited number of buttons the controls feel unnatural. I'm not saying that other games have done it better (I haven't played any other games like this), but I really feel VR in its current state just isn't quite ready for running, climbing, and platforming.

If VR makes you motion sick, this game is absolutely no exception.\t\t\t\t\t
