\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR
When the physics works, this game is a geniunely fun title worth replaying. When it doesn't, it can create really frustrating things like your arm being stuck inbetween a ladder or your melee weapon being somehow stuck to the enemy you just killed.

Just try it out honestly, it's flawed but the physics engine is leaps ahead of other vr games.

- The Good
Having a physical presence instead of being a floating heads adds alot more to making you feel immersed in the world it creates. Even the simple act of reloading a rifle takes several steps compared to just simply pressing the 'R' button. It brings a level of catharsis when you get more used to doing certain tasks more quickly like shooting and timing your attacks perfectly. I don't feel any sickness with this game but honestly I don't think any VR has given me Nausea. Impacts of guns and hitting something feels real because instead of a canned animation where they generically fall, you smash them to the floor. you can even grab certain objects to help you and your journey and even from the second level, this game makes you think on your feet.

- The Bad
Having to emulate weight in heavy objects is honestly frustrating aswell. Since you don't feel the impact of the object, its hard to judge what game makes it out to be. One thing I would like to see is more enemy variety, maybe with more complicated logic than just simply slapping you. maybe holding a melee weapon. Also the story is really nothing, its good you can smash the cutscenes because I found more entertainment in that than just listening to them. The difficulty does ramp up but I feel the ai don't scale with it. They never take alternative paths or try to sneak up to you. They mostly barrel through whatever is in their way.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks has the most advanced physics of any VR shooter to date, and it truly shines for it. As a general rule, if you can see it, you can interact with it in some manner, and it will have collision physics with other objects. This includes enemies, and I cannot put into words how satisfying it can be to butt-stroke an enemy before double-tapping them on the ground to finish them off. The story is secondary to the gameplay mechanics, and I feel like the game is better for it. It's not a AAA title by any means, but it is the most fun I've ever had in a "tech demo".\t\t\t\t\t
