\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredible game mechanics tied in with physics.

Although gameplay does sometimes get very clunky with the physics not behaving how you want it to and your arms and hands getting stuck inside of things often, this game is really pushing the medium of VR as a whole a bunch of steps further and really starts to show the potential of where we are going with gameplay.

However to my disappointment this title does not supply the option to play in the Native Oculus SDK..it's purely Steam VR. Also, it does not currently save your progress. It only lets you select levels from the beginning.

Still haven't finished the game yet and am about 7.4 hours in. I've done tons of exploring every crevice and toying with the mechanics and trying the "hard routes" to solve puzzles when I didn't realize there was an easier way.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBiggest issue in this game is the lack of a save function, especially in a VR game. I know for a fact that my hands, fingers and legs get tired after a couple hours of continuous play.

Guns are great. Melee combat feels fine. Physics are great. The whole atmosphere in the game is pretty damn cool.

Your hands getting stuck on odd things is a bit annoying, especially since your arms seems perfectly fine with clipping through fences having your hand and body on either side halting your movement. However I find this to be easily avoided.

From 4 hours of playing the game I find it to be a lot of fun to mess around with all the physics objects and environment. Can't say much to the quality of the story yet, but even if it's the worst thing ever, the game is still a great VR physics playground.\t\t\t\t\t
