\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game, I really do. It isn't a bad game by all means at all, it's pushed standards of implementing locomotion into VR games because teleporting sucks, but not every one is able to because locomotion makes them feel sick, but for those who can it's great.

I don't have issues with the locomotion at all, I have to give it a thumbs down because I genuinely am unable to experience the whole game. The game runs fine, my PC is a 2k priced PC and that's mainly because some parts were overpriced, my PC is able to handle this game just fine. The issue is because it has some jank issues with SteamVR on my end.

It will just decide with some loading screen or even just starting the game up, that it wants to rapidly phase in and out of SteamVR to the game, maybe this is a low PC spec issue, but when I first played the game through the tutorial I've never had this problem, and there was a time I was able to play through some of the first level before I decided to quit out of the game because I wanted to record it. I'd love to fix this issue but idk how, and I haven't seen any one else having this issue. But what I have played it was a great experience, and I really want to play more.

What I have experienced, I don't enjoy inventory management at all in VR, I own an index set and a lot of games use the grip feature for a user to hold onto something, which makes complete sense but I also feel the need to hold onto the controller which causes me to accidentally grab things like a weapon in my pocket and then I'll accidentally drop it, and some VR games if you arent gripping hard enough, you'll just drop what you're holding. I havent checked if there's just a toggleable gripping or not but I hope there is one.

The physics-based interaction and locomotion is great for being the first game to do it. But there are some things that feel off such as trying to climb when underneath something (such as the monkey bars in the tutorial). The guns feel good to use too, even the ones that require two hands to use which I'm not a big fan of in VR because it feels weird to be holding thin air.

Other than those issues what I was able to play of the game felt great.\t\t\t\t\t

tl;dr You get one of the best combat, and okay puzzle sandboxes for VR. There's mod support, so if you don't feel like spending the usual 60$, get this on sale or just for 30$. It's worth the price. It says it's for advanced VR users, but I would argue that starting off with this title isn't a bad idea.

Comparisons to HLA (Half-Life: Alyx) are bound to happen, and to go over my thoughts briefly on the topic, HLA is an average VR title compared to Boneworks. Narrative, characters, and world building are done far better than in Boneworks, but it doesn't feel like a showcase of what is possible in VR. Half-Life 2 is only half as fun as it is because, you can fling toilets and have an improvised explosive launcher every couple of levels. Boneworks is fun because I can skip half the puzzles by being a ballooned up boy. Boneworks has visceral, satisfying melee combat with a bunch of cool weapons, along with guns that will fit a plethora of play styles. The levels a mix of indoor, or expansive indoor/outdoor environments that allow you to mess with the game constantly. HLA has some cool upgrades for a few guns (no melee only guns), but nothing close to Boneworks. Both games have mod support, HLA mod installs being more streamlined with workshop support, but that doesn't matter much as both games are easy to tweak.

So get yourself some Melon Belly, and burn it all to the ground.
