\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBetter than HL: Alyx.
I started off vr playing a lot of boneworks at first, then it flew off my radar for a while. In that time I played a few different titles, like Half Life: Alyx, and Pavlov. Valve should have taken this game and reskinned it for HLA. It does what HL2 did for physics back in the day in my opinion.
When I picked the game back up after playing Pavlov, it took me a few minutes to get used to the weighty feeling of reloading guns. But, that's because Boneworks tries very hard to be realistic, where Pavlov allows you to get "close enough" for a more gamey feeling.
With the gun stuff out of the way, the rest of the physics are great. Picking up heavy objects is reflected in the game pretty well, even if it can't match your intention because of it. Using crowbars to climb up poles that are normally out of reach feels pretty cool too. It's an overall good experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGives you a really interactive experience. It shines best in its satisfying shooting and open ended puzzles. Climbing around the levels searching for unlockable items for the sandbox mode will certainly have me coming back for more.

Story is a little too vague to be engaging but the general tone of the whole experience and the set pieces feel appropriately uncanny and some times creepy. It riffs off of the feel of Valve titles for sure, but manages to be its own thing.

I'll note I played this on a Rift S and had no problems at all the whole experience with framerate or tracking. I do have a pretty new and beefed up PC (3060ti & 5800x) but I've seen forums where folks with a 2080ti complain about not being able to run this well on Oculus headsets. I wanna imagine that it's a weaker lower core CPU that's keeping some Oculus users from having a good experience playing in Steam VR. Anyhoo, I'm glad I took the risk of getting it here on Steam instead of Oculus Store.\t\t\t\t\t

Get if you've vr legs and are after some good vr action.

Being able to climb, jump, crouch and what-not to brute-force puzzles, get high ground, go to secrets, etc is super nice. It makes the experience of playing feel heaps immersive. The full vr body also helps this hugely.

Shooting is fun. The guns are done quite well, and being able to shoot something is kind of just enjoyable.
While AI is super simple and basic, the combat is thankfully still fun.
The enemies, excluding the gun dudes, are pretty awful. They're like a sausage on wheels - no rhythm to motion or attacking. Fighting them melee therefore is clunky and kind of chaotic. A quality I sadly have to use for every melee vr game so far. However, that's not to say melee is bad: It's really good as well. It's not in-depth as b/s, but the functionality is there, and the impact and what-not is nice to see.
Physics works, but climbing, swinging, jumping is kind of janky. Although it still functions well so gameplay isn't hindered.
Story pretty much isn't there when you first play. However, the lore goes super in-depth and is incredibly interesting to think about. I believe the game would be so much more fun if you understood some of the story/lore. So spoilers for some of that:


You play as the security programmer dude for this weird "portal" for real life and this ai-generated landscape. You enter the land (in vr) to investigate and fix a security problem.
The land uses pretty much dark matter to make the place work.
There's a flaw with some structure in the vr land, where this dark matter is leaking. So you want to fix that.
However, you notice that you can abuse this flaw in the place you're in: If you die in the black matter, you will be invincible.
So that's the goal: Breach security, hop into this dark matter stuff, and then be immortal.

More spoilers:


The notes you see in-game are left by you. Ones helping you reach your goal and from people that tried the objective before you, but failed. They leave a note to help the next you.
The non-helpful messages are from you, who achieved the objective but figured the immortality is not worth it.
The humans you see in the game are you. This includes people with gray jumpsuit, and purple entities.\t\t\t\t\t
