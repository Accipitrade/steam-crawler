\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really don't get why this game is considered the gold star for VR. It understand the potential of VR, but completely misses what makes VR fun.

Physics are good, appreciate the attempt to push VR forward, but this is a deeply unfun game. The guns are great, possibly the best I've experienced in VR.

But it has massive design flaws that hinder enjoyment of what the game does well:
- Levels are long and checkpoints are far apart, and if you quit you lose all progress since the last one. This means that you need to play hour long sessions or more to make progress, especially if you keep dying or are hunting for collectibles. This would be frustrating in a flatscreen game, it's unforgivable for VR, where playsessions are shorter due to possible motion sickness and physical exhaustion. Hell, the (long and boring) tutorial even says to stop and take breaks, but if you quit at that point you'll lose all progress and have to replay the first half of the tutorial.
- Too much physics on large objects. Pull a filing cabinet drawer in Half-Life Alyx and the drawer opens smoothly, open one in Boneworks and the entire cabinet falls over and everything on it scatters to the wind, along with your immersion.
- You unlock items in Sandbox mode (plus other modes like Arena) by finding hidden stuff in the campaign. This means you can't just screw around and have fun outside of the awful campaign without first playing the campaign. Highly recommend just downloading a 100% save.
- The stores require you to put ammo in one magazine at a time, which is just tedious. It's basically impossible to buy an expensive item if you're under attack, which happens often in the zombie mode

The final nail in the coffin is that the game has no art style or sense of environemnt. It has the aesthetic of your generic My First Source Map, completely missing the main draw of VR, giving you a sense of place.

Hopefully Bonelab will polish and iterate and find the fun, because the framework is solid enough.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game that, as much as I want to respect and enjoy it, simply can't recommend to anyone as it's too realistic for it's own good.
Is it a well-made game? Yes. Is it a fun game? No.
You constantly have to fumble around with the shakiest weapons ever, and by the time you spent 10 seconds trying to grab ammo from your belt instead of your other weapon or some collectible hogging up your inventory and actually manage to cram it in the gun like the spastic you are, you've either been swarmed on or shot at to the point where, instead of dying immediately, you just spend 10 seconds closing your eyes until you respawn without the gun you were just holding. Joy.
This game is so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ uncomfortable, too. I sadly use a Quest 2 with Virtual Desktop to play this game, but the awful controls can be attributed to the game itself. So yeah, holding stuff. You need to physically hold down the grip button in order to hold anything. This would be fine for props like boxes, but for weapons and keys, this gets super uncomfortable fast. Why isn't there even an option to have it toggle?
I wholeheartedly expect gun-nuts and technerds to come at me saying "ohhh its your fault" or "oohhhh the realism's the whole point", and I guess I'm sorry for not having infinite money to buy the best headset with the best tracking system with the best computer and best internet, because my experience with Boneworks has been nothing but uncomfortable. My forehead heats up like it's the deepest pit of Hell, internet issues keep tracking my hands and everything 200 minutes after I actually make an input, and my disappointment starts to show because I really DO want to like this game.
I absolutely adore how this game looks. Not so much the texture quality, but the setting, aesthetics, the music (except for that guy singing on the radio, but ESPECIALLY the Museum's theme). But when Doom VFR, a game widely mixed "for it's awful control" I can say plays MUCH better than Boneworks and I've had more comfort with, that really says something.
Unless you have top of the line hardware and internet, playing space the size of a football field, and the physical skill of a war veteran, OR somehow can get by everything and appreciate how the game looks to the eye, avoid it.\t\t\t\t\t
