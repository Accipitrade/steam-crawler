\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely love this game, it's got a great gun play in it, and the physics are outstanding. It's the best out of the VR shooters out there, everything is crisp, and almost makes it overly satisfying. There are a different missions in the campaign which all feel different and diverse with a mixture of different obstacles, with different weapons that you can find throughout the campaign. Another fun part of the game is the sandbox mode, it will still keep you entertained for hours, and honestly, my problem with this game lies with none of these, not the campaign or the sandbox mode. This just seems like a "project" more than a game at times, seeing as the developers just stranded this with no updates, and that's the problem. It would be cool to get some new weapons, perhaps even new maps, weapons, enemies, or even just a small sub-campaign. I won't stray away from recommending this, however, if you are new to the VR world, and stumble upon this, be prepared to receive a game that doesn't get any updates anymore. It hasn't been updated since almost the very end of 2020. Overall, I think if you want a one time play game with great physics, then this is it, this is the shining gem in a pile of other games. This game does have a descent campaign, with a good length to it, and to me, it feels almost like a portal-half life hybrid, as it has puzzles that are very reminiscent of portal, even with an atmosphere like it, with enemies that remind one of half life. To recap everything, great game, I would recommend, even though the game doesn't receive any support (which bug wise is fine, I haven't even encountered that many, only one instance where dead bodies would literally get up in sandbox), nor any updates. Perfect game, and concept, that I hope is picked up in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, keep in mind that i am biased towards this game, and i dont really review things much, but i firmly believe that this is still the best VR game on the market even in 2023. (Yes, i think its better than Bonelab). Still worth it now if you havent played, even if you've played Bonelab first, as i think this game houses a truly profound experience as long as VR games are your thing. Firstly, This game houses an absolutely amazing soundtrack that builds the atmosphere of the world of mythOS perfectly, and enhances your immersion even more (I would highly recommend giving it a listen). Secondly, The jank of the game never really got in my way too much and once i got used to it it was fine, and didn't hinder my experience enough to be considered a monumental negative. Third, I would 100% recommend this over Bonelab any day. Bonelab felt like it was trying to be the next Boneworks so hard with its "Revolutionary mod support and engine" that it lost a lot of the creative vision that the devs originally had with Boneworks, so they made Bonelab feel like a cheap sequel that was made out of the prospect of popularity and praise instead of wanting to make a truly amazing product like its predecessor. The core game of Bonelab feels like a shell that the developers quickly glued together to house its new features, while this game feels like a complete experience that will leave you coming back for its unique atmosphere and unrivalled VR immersion. In conclusion, play this game, lol. 4/5\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFully simulated player body is a great concept that is insanely difficult to pull off. In that sense, this game has an extremely cool gimmick ̶t̶h̶a̶t̶ ̶I̶ ̶d̶o̶n̶'̶t̶ ̶t̶h̶i̶n̶k̶ ̶a̶n̶y̶ ̶o̶t̶h̶e̶r̶ ̶g̶a̶m̶e̶s̶ ̶h̶a̶v̶e̶ ̶q̶u̶i̶t̶e̶ ̶r̶e̶p̶l̶i̶c̶a̶t̶e̶d̶ ̶y̶e̶t̶ it turns out Blade and Sorcery did the exact same thing a year earlier and did a far better job, wtf. The leg controls they ̶u̶s̶e̶d̶ ripped off from B&S make sense from the start and actually start to feel good after a few hours of acclimation. The bones are here for an actual good product, but that's where its good qualities end. The amount of praise I've heard for this game is more puzzling than anything I've encountered so far while playing.

The only type of "puzzle" is climbing up some kind of weird terrain, finding a key, and then climbing back to where you started. Optionally, you might shoot some hilariously braindead AI along the way, but you can just run past them if you'd like. The only challenge is wrestling with insane design decisions made about how much the playermodel should ragdoll when it comes into contact with literally any object. Even trying to walk forward in a straight line is impossible if there is any object in the way, no matter how small.
Climbing, which again is the solution to every single puzzle in this game, is unbelievably bad. There's an incredibly awkward weight system that never should have been written to begin with that keeps you from moving heavy objects quickly (who knows why; if it's too heavy to move I shouldn't be able to move it). As a result if you grab on to a ledge and just hang there, even if you're staying perfectly still, your playermodel will jitter around and bounce up and down, stopping suddenly as it hits the wall before bouncing straight back out again, making it as sickening as possible. Picture that while you wave your other hand around desperately trying to grab on to the next ledge above you. After anywhere between 2-15 seconds the hand you're hanging on with decides to let go for no reason and you fall all the way down to the start. I'm extremely lucky that so far I've been completely immune to motion sickness, because this thing is completely out of control.

The control problems stack on top of themselves to create instant frustration. I'm currently at this part where you have to load cannons to fire at a sphere in the middle of a big square room while enemies endlessly spawn literally right behind you. For some incomprehensible reason the devs made the tool that you have to use to load the cannons weigh about 600 pounds, so it's never anywhere near your physical real hands while you use it to load these balls into the aperture that's a good 5-6 feet off the ground. And since there are endlessly spawning enemies right next to you, you just have to wait until you get lucky enough to have the 15 seconds necessary to load the ball in, readjust the cannon because obviously it moves around as you try to load the cannonball in while shooting enemies and if you don't aim at the exact right part of the target it doesn't work.

I'm cutting this short, but I have so much more to complain about. Every song is a 5 second loop that becomes grating immediately and plays nonstop while you try to explore. Unsettling sound design in general. I absolutely hate how gray and lifeless everything is. The story is laughable, and the way it's presented even more so. Everything comes together to create the most uninspired, boring, least pleasant uncanny valley effect imaginable. Good god.

I highly recommend you play this game if you can get it for free or close to it. Otherwise, don't waste your money. You will have experienced the best it has to offer within 2 hours.\t\t\t\t\t
