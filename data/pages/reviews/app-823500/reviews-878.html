\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best VR games I've ever played, except for one glaring issue which completely ruins the entire game...

On the Vive, to hold objects you need to hold the grip buttons down for as long as you hold an object. The grip buttons are designed to be held for very short amount of times or simply clicked once to hold an item; then clicked again to drop the item. Boneworks completely throws away this concept and requires you to be holding down the buttons at all times to hold an object in your hands. This causes cramping after a few seconds of holding any item, constant dropping of items, and difficulty in playing the game.

Please, please, please fix this issue! If this was issue is fixed then the game is near perfect! But until this glaring issue is resolved I can not recommend this game. It makes the game too difficult to play and should be something which can be easily adjusted in the game's settings. :(\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a good exhibition for what strong physics can do in a VR game, but I don't see why VR needs these kinds of physics. The extreme level of "physics" in this game creates a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ up translation between my intended movements and the limitations of the in-game character's movements and abilities. When I need to move an obstacle, why do I need to feel like i'm picking up a 100lb box and dragging it with limp ♥♥♥♥ hands when I can move my hands freely in the real world. Most of the intended interactivity in this game (moving objects, carrying things in your hands, climbing, and punching or pushing) just doesn't transition well to the experience of manipulating things in a 3D environment, I really ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hated it. Additionally, the controls for the Valve Index were not that great, you have to squeeze trigger and grip by default to grab and hold onto an object, why can't it be one or the other?

HL Alyx does strong physics to a lesser extent but does them SO MUCH BETTER and the limits placed on physics in HL work much better for the VR experience. In HL, you are not constantly getting your arms trapped inside railings, bumping your legs on ledges, flailing stupidly trying to execute a punch, and just generally feeling like you have limp ♥♥♥♥ weak arms. Finally, the weapons, guns, and object destruction are all lack luster compared to some much cheaper and lesser funded VR titles. Guns feel janky to hold and reload, and punching always feels like an RNG roll. I get that this is a exhibition of new technology and it's a great example of what strong physics is like in VR, but it's not for me. I mean, give it a try but be ready to want your money back.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, this game is my favourite VR game to date. The physics alone are worth it. Reviewed on a Valve Index

Story: The story is obscure and some times you might have trouble following along, It requires some digging outside of the game to grasp fully.

Graphics: Looks great on the Index! The art has an unfinished feel to it, but to me it appears intentional based on story context.

Mechanics: Physics are the meat and potatoes that makes this game special. I was spoiled rotten by this game. Everything collides with everything else, objects react in mostly sensible and predictable ways, and most importantly the way you interact with the world is intuitive. I cannot play any other VR game without testing its physics and comparing that with Boneworks, only to end up disappointed.

HOWEVER, the game does not have that many accessibility options for those who get sick playing VR. There is only the continuous movement option, and you will be thrown around and bashed up many times. I would strongly recommend getting a solid pair of VR legs before trying this game.

Conclusion: This game is beyond fun, with a 6 - 8 hour long story mode, plus many other modes like the Arena and Sandbox. the game is getting semi-frequent updates, that only add more to the game and fine tune the mechanics even further. A must have for VR, when you are ready.\t\t\t\t\t
