\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo this is not the future of VR, because VR is just in it's very early stages. This is however by far the best game I've played in VR, and that is by no means only thanks to the awesome physics engine. The music is amaazing and the world is beautifully made and even though I've completed the story I feel like I've barely scratched the surface. I've never been so immersed (and scared) by a VR game and I just wan't to play more and more. The physics engine makes just traversing through a level or picking up ammo fun but it would be worthless without this incredible world and I can't believe there's no large studio behind this. There could be a more advanced storyline but trust me this is not just a tech demo. But let's adress the core engine:

This game really makes no compromise for player comfort, personally I have no issues at all but a lot of people are reporting getting a lot of motion sickness from this game. The joystick locomotion might be a dealbreaker for some people, but personally I think that is basically the only way to do fast paced games like this and people are gonna have to get used to it for games like this. There is of course also a lot of large scale motions and climbing, but your body also moves around from things like pushing on a wall. And your in game body doesn't always match up with your real body, which might be disorienting for some people.

But I don't think this game would be as incredible without this, and I absolutely love it. You can interact with your environment exactly how you expect and want to. This is true to some degree for all VR, you no longer press buttons and have set animations to for example pick up ammo. But this game takes it to the next level. I'm still in awe everytime I break an ammo box and just effortlessly push a few planks aside with my crowbar. If something is out of reach you can just drag the whole shelf down, or move some boxes to climb onto and use your axe to get some extra reach and pull yourself up over a ledge. All the puzzles and interactions are so satisfying with this system. Fighting is super fun and never the same.

But as I said at the start, I don't know that this is the future of VR. This level of realistic locomotion might be too advanced and daunting for the standard of VR, they state that this is for advanced user and that is true. There's also a slight lack of polish in the engine but that is to be expected this early on in VR development. And it's nothing that pulls me out of enjoying the game at all. I'm super glad that they went this route not only because I personally enjoy it a lot but it's also great that we get a variety of games. Half-life:Alyx will no doubt be a game that's easier for everyone to enjoy since it's built around teleportation. But there also has to be games like this.

This is not only the most impressive VR interaction I've seen to date, but it is also the best gaming experience I've had in years. And that is thanks to an amazing studio putting hard work into every little detail of the game, the audio and visual design is phenomenal and the gameplay is the most fun I've had with a singeplayer experience for a very long time. You are doing yourself a huge disfavor if you're not at least trying this out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGlorified tech demo. Combat is almost engaging, but with only 3 or 4 enemy types, the most enjoyable part is reloading. Many levels are confusing as to where I should be going next, and optional sections usually make me feel like it was a waste of time. Climbing is clunky, as your arms collide with the things you are usually grabbing. I really wanted to enjoy this game, and looked forwards to it, but with arena and sandbox locked from the start, having to go through the story and unlock them doesn't seem worth it. VR games should be about instant immersive experiences, like Beatsaber or Blade and Sorcery. Load up the game and go. This is a mess of design errors and poor level design, with a vague goal of physics simulation as its main attraction. Really should have started in early access.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt tries a lot of interesting things, but ultimately this just doesn't live up to the hype. Some call this the best VR thing yet, but IMO I find it the most absolute immersion breaking VR experience I've had. (The best VR immersion for me, is still Lone Echo)

Ignoring any sort of progress made with transversal methods in the last 2 years, this game forces you to do locomotive without a vignette (to fight motion sickness). On top of that, it adds a very bouncy jump ability. Even as a seasoned VR user & developer this all gets pretty uncomfortable to me after some time and honestly breaks immersion quite a bit.

The hyped physics simulation interaction really just sort of ends up being uncanny-valley janky Skyrim physics, where my hands/body would get randomly stuck in things or moving large objects never felt any form of natural. It became easier/funnier to just walk into doors than try to open them in any sort of natural way. Doing stuff with a Rift controller meant always accidentally opening menus and never quiiite feeling natural with the way you interact. The Vive is worse, due to having to grip the wands a lot of the time.

The campaign mode has a very long jokey introduction tutorial with no save points, and mostly feels like a bunch of physics demos they stapled together. I gave up ultimately in the Streets section afterwards, when after 30 minutes or so I could not for the life of me figure out how to progress in the game.

Anyways, I recommend trying this later down the line after some updates and/or when on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
