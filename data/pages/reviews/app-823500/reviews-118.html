\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI do recommend this game, and with Bonelab coming out tomorrow, there's no better time than now to play through Boneworks.

Graphics- 7/10: decent and quite detailed overall, but only a bit above average even for its time.
Gameplay- 10/10: Excellent physics model and very creative use of the game mechanics to make fun levels.
Side note, player friendliness- 2/10: Boneworks pulls no punches with the gameplay and can make some less experienced or less fortuitous VR users' experience much worse. This was my second VR game and I was fine, however, if you struggle with motion sickness in VR, you should probably pass on Boneworks until it goes away.
Lore- 10/10: Very deep and interesting lore that interacts with other SLZ games, however...
Story(telling)- 4/10: the story is quite easy to find, except that you find it by spending time looking at screens or at writing on the wall. While the story is amazing, the storytelling could use some work.
Post-game type content- 9/10: All of the post-game content is amazing, and were it not for the horribly annoying way of getting it, it would be 10/10. You can always just download a completed save file though.
Overall- 10/10: in spite of the flaws, Boneworks is among the best VR games out there, with Half-Life: Alyx, being the only arguably better game at the time of writing. However, the sequel, Bonelab, might be substantially better than Boneworks, and that comes out 24 hours and 21 minutes after I post this review.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is not your regular game. Boneworks feels more like an experience that shows you where VR is headed in the future. Now before you go buy this game make sure you get used to VR games and don't get too motion sick this game is recommended for experienced VR players for a good reason. Boneworks gameplay is phisycs based, your character is physically present in the game world and can interact with almost everything and the game makes good use of it. There's a lot of puzzles, climbing, bashing and gunning, some of those things are more fun then others but they all feel great. The climbing takes some getting used to, the puzzles can be tedious at times and the bashing frustrating, you have to bash wooden boxes to get ammo/currency and those boxes are really robust if you don't have the right tools to bash them open. There's not much gun variation but they all feel great, the only problem I had is that the enemy types felt kind of boring. The game leaves it up to you how you want to play it, there's no one way to play the game or solve the puzzles which gives it a lot of replay value. There is a story mode that is roughly 5 hours long, a sandbox where you have to find the items in the story mode (I recommend downloading a 100% save file) and arena modes. All modes are fun combine it with a few mods and you'll be entertained for quite a while. So if you're confident that your stomach can handle it and don't mind or like physics based puzzles go ahead and buy it or wait until Thursday for bonelabs to come out.

Score:
8.8/10\t\t\t\t\t
