\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks - Highly anticipated physics puzzle adventure

Boneworks gained a lot of attention before its highly anticipated release on December 10th.
An employee of Stress Level Zero and a creator of YouTube channel called "Node", Brandon Laatsch managed to gain enormous amount of hype for their upcoming title: Boneworks.
Now that the game has finally arrived, i can wholeheartedly say:
it did live up to its expectations.

In the story you are an employee of Monogon Industries and you have to enter corrupted artificial intelligence operating system called MythOs and try to solve what is wrong with it. Your coworkers seem to be long gone and you must proceed alone towards the clock tower of MythOs to debug the system.

Everything in Boneworks is based on physics and you must interact with objects like you would in real life. If object is really heavy, lets say a huge metal shelf, you must act like its heavy and move your arms slower. Great example of this kind of slower movement is using the sledgehammer; you must use your both hands and put some force behind it with big/slowish arcing movement. It's really hard to describe but once you try it yourself you will get it.

Core gameplay consists of semi complex puzzles, looting for ammo (which is also used as currency besides it's obvious use case), fighting against nullbodies, cleanup crews and vr-headset-looking headcrabs while adventuring through the corrupted operating system.

Anything you find in the world of Boneworks can in theory be used as a weapon; rip planks off from boarded door, take a chair from office or should you prefer a more classic melee weapon like a crowbar? Game has collection of knives, swords, hammers and all kinds of fun things to flail at your enemies. Same goes for the firearms; pistols, submachine guns, rifles and even some physics based weapons.

Graphically Boneworks has its own simple yet pleasing style. You are inside an operating system and most of the time it feels and looks like it too. Everything has this industrial-like dark style to it, which fits the game very well.

Sound design of this game is on another level. The feeling of space around you, as you hear the sounds is immersive and very well executed. Guns have oomph and melee gives a satisfying smack when you hit your target. Even before the release of the game, i loved the soundtrack of this game; dark synthwave goodness. I can't praise enough how well it fits the theme of this game. There are quieter more calm songs for times like puzzle solving or traversing the level, but then there are the songs that get your blood flowing when intense combat sequence starts.

Overall the game is a dream come true for me. Decent story, great gameplay, replay value (sandbox mode and secrets), good length for a vr-game priced like this (i completed the campaign in about 8.5 hours). I highly recommend this game for anyone who owns vr-headset and has some experience with smooth locomotion.

This is my Game of the Year. Thank you SL0.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am gonna explain why this is my favorite vr game of all time and why i think it even tops half life alyx.

By the way dont get the wrong idea, i also love half life alyx and think its the best vr game for not expirienced vr players. So, there are 3 main points about why i love this game:

1) The soundtrack:
The soundtrack slaps. The first time i passed over the radio i didnt think much of it but the second time it started to grow on me and suddenly i would carry it everywhere with me (that was the first in a game that i listened to the radio,excluding gtav) the soundtrack being memorable and kind of nostalgic is something i least expected from a vr game and a shooter at that.The music perfectly blends with the enviroment you are placed in and really gives you the vibe of not a real world but seemingly living one.

2)The Freedom:
Boneworks isnt an open world game of course but the majority of the levels have many ways of finishing them, for example in the Warehouse which is my favorite level in the game (that and streets) give you a variety of ways to enter the actual warehouse. I dont think there is another game that can rival the ways you interact with the enemies, i really like the idea of me being able to grab anything near me and use that to injure the enemy (for example breking a chair on them like WWE).Also the levels in boneworks encourage exploration and indulge your curiosity by awarding you with collectibles.

3) The atmosphere and the sense of scale:
The first time i booted the game i only knew about a segment of a level with some combat but nothing else. With that in mind the game starts you of in a calm and joyful tutorial that sets up expectations about the game which are then shattered by the streets in which i was always waiting for life to spring out ,someone talk or see someone else in that world but nothing, nothing appeared and the only thing you could hear was eary sound of machines and sometimes a very void like music that all together makes up the question: what the hell happened here where is everyone and what is going to happen to me?
One of my favorite moments was when i saw the first ever enemy who i tried to avoid at first as i was very scared of him (that was because of all the atmosphere at the beggining) but the he attacked me and my first instict was grab the brick and throw it and it worked (for a second) and i shouted in victory ,but then he got up and i just panicked grabed a hammer and started bashing his head like there is no tommorow. Also one of my favorite things to do is jump of real high places, this gives a better persepective of the world and a realisation of the scale of everything around you.

To summarise, BONEWORKS gives of a vibe that no game has ever managed to achieve, it is a mysterious game that makes you wonder what has happened here? and the game manages to make you feel tiny with everything you come across .

BONEWORKS is the foundation of future vr games and i believe that we will look back at it and get nostalgic . Thank you Stress Level Zero for your amazing work <3\t\t\t\t\t
