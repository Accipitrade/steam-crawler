\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: You're buying an overpriced tech demo.

If I had to describe Boneworks in one word, it would be janky.
Hows the physics? Janky.
Hows the gunplay? Janky.
How is walking from point A to B? Janky.

Everything about Boneworks sounds amazing on paper, but in practice it just didn't feel right. The physics engine tends to flail your head around wildly every time you try to interact with any object in the world. The guns, well.. The actually shooting felt pretty good, but every other interaction with the guns felt, well, janky.

The story is, well... There was a story? To be honest I couldn't tell you if there was or wasn't one. Something about watermelons? If you buy this, understand you're playing through some loosely related physics based puzzles (If you've played HL2 you've basically seen them all).

I have many, many hours in both Oculus and SteamVR since 2016 (and DK1 before that), and can easily spend all day in VR on a weekend. In Boneworks, I could barely make it 2 hours with taking gravol. "For VR Veterans" doesn't excuse being lazy and creating a (subjectively) bad product.

All that being said, this game had it's moments, but they were unfortunately few and far between. The last few hours especially were not because I enjoyed it, but because I paid for the damn thing and I was going to beat it. I think the 'v2' of this tech could be very promising, and I hope SLZ is working away at it, but until then, this tech demo is not for everyone.\t\t\t\t\t
