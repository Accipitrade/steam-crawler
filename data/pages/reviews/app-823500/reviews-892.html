I still do have to say the physics can be annoying to deal with at times but still keeps the game interesting and fun. ORIGNAL REVIEW

Physics are fun but everything else isn't. The story is uninteresting and boring, each level feels like a chore to go through, reloading guns never works out the way you want it to. If this game was nothing more than a 4 level tech demo I would be happy.

Edit: After spending about an hour in Arena mode, I have to say its quite fun. I enjoy feeling like a badass killer. But as that is only a side mode I still don't recomend this to someone looking for a quality full campaign VR game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis may-be the very best VR game I've ever played. Although games like Half Life Alyx may have better visuals/story and games like B&S may have more satisfying melee combat, this game is the only VR game that uses everything done well in VR games so far and puts them together.

I will start with the soundtrack, but for good reason. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ IT'S GOOD. I listened to the soundtrack before I even got the game, it is very memorable and is put in so well into the game. I can recall certain moments of the game where I just completely froze for the game music is so beautifully used to introduce some of the more grand sections. The music is probably one of if not my favorite soundtrack of any game. If you decide to go and beat the secret final ending of the game, the main menu music even changes to something so beautiful, I almost cry every-time I hear it, there have been zero other game soundtracks that have been able to do that to me.

The game's story is not very clear at start which gives all the more reason to replay the game, because it is not an easy story to follow. It's so genius what they have done, I don't know if it is on purpose or not, but I replayed the game and looked all around for secrets because I just wanted to know more about this world, and the secret areas the game provides are amazing. In fact, there is an entire series of secrets that you can follow to get a final ending to the game which includes one of my favorite parts of the whole game (the hand gun trail, just perfect in how it combines every good aspect of the combat in one timed section). Because of this, the amount of replayability of this game defiantly beats that of many other VR games.

The combat and game-play loop overall is fun. The strict inventory limit gives you the constant choice of what do you want to bring along with you. The physics allow for very creative things to happen to work around limitations, whether that be sticking a bunch of your melee weapons into a bat to make a very powerful weapon, carrying a trashcan filled with weapons, or trying to balance a bunch of weapons in your arms. It is really fun, and since your whole body is simulated, you can do some crazy things. The guns are also really fun to shoot, although it is rather sad that most guns are just repeats of pistols and rifles which a slight quirk. Ammo also is currency at stores which is an interesting concept, but what ends up happening is that the game is forced to give you enough ammo to be able to kill enemies and buy things at the store. If prices in stores didn't go up as you played through the game and ammo was a lot less common, I think that the stores would have worked better but a campaign overall like that probably isn't happening ever.

The environments although all taking place in a city (for the most part), the game still makes you travel through very different parts so it still feels like an adventure. Because of this common environment, when it does change to something completely different, it is very grand and epic.

Overall, this game will go down as one of the games I remember very well. The new updates that have introduced new content are very cool and replayable, but the game does suffer from the game-play loop being a bit repetitive even with the new sandbox tools given. I give this game a 95%, I really hope this becomes a shining example of what VR games should be like.\t\t\t\t\t
