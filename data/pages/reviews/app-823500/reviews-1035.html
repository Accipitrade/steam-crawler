This is as of February 7th, 2020
No spoilers in this review


PC and VR specs:
- Played on Valve Index @ 90hz.
- I haven't changed any graphic settings in Boneworks or SteamVR (default settings), except changing the rendering resolution to 100%.
- GPU: 1060 6gb (not overclocked).
- CPU: i7-6700 3.40 Ghz (not overclocked).
- RAM: 16 gb.

TLDR; Game is good and worth the money (CDN$ 33.99). Its well optimised (might be because of the Valve Index or SteamVR) and still is fun even after you beat the campaign.

Here's a list of the main pros about this game: (in my personal opinion and based on my specs)
  • Good and realistic physics to some degree (on rare occasions objects glitch out and get stuck).
  • Smooth gun mechanics. Shooting is very satisfying.
  • An actual VR campaign and gamemodes (my favourite is arena).
  • Good optimisation and graphics? Could be because of Valve Index/SteamVR

Here's the list for the main cons about this game: (in my personal opinion and based on my specs)
  • (my main complaint) Not being able to directly change the UI interaction keys from the settings menu. I mainly didn't like how you have to click the index trigger all the way to pull a magazine out and such but not have to do the same when interacting with the actual weapon. This kind of inconsistency with the controls takes me out of the immersion when I'm reloading/ pulling a gun out. There might be a way to adjusted it through the settings in SteamVR, but I don't like how there isn't a direct settings option for it ingame.
  • Pulling yourself up onto the surface you're climbing to is very frustrating most of the time. Climbing by itself is good.
  • Melee and fist fighting feels too "loose" and lacks the realistic force you should be delivering. Punching feels very weak even when throwing a fast and direct punch (this also applies to pushing, particularly with AI enemies).
  • Story isn't super easy to follow as gameplay doesn't seem to complement it very well. It feels like you are running through levels without a purpose.
  • Level design is amateur compared to other games.


I didn't mention the missing saving option in the cons list because I personally wasn't bothered by that problem since I can stand for long periods of time. As for motion sickness caused by the gameplay, most specifically the climbing mechanism, It didn't bother me either. This is considering the fact that I'm new to VR but again, it could be because of the motion smoothing feature SteamVR/ The Valve Index has. Another thing that some have a problem with is unholstering and holstering items. This was a problem to me at first but after a while you start to develop the muscle memory for the holstering locations (assuming your height is set up properly ingame). After beating the campaign, I just play the arena mode because I don't want to bother grinding to get all the items and spawnables for sandbox. The most surprising thing about this game is how optimised it is. Initially I thought my GPU/CPU wont be able to run the game smoothly but it did exceptionally well except for one or two scenes (even then it was still playable). I'm not completely sure if its because of the game or because of some optimisation feature on the Valve Index. Overall, this game is pretty good and worth the CDN$ 33.99 compared to whats currently available on the market for VR games.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game, a real achievement in VR. I recommend it highly, with some caveats.

Lack of checkpoints in levels that can take over an hour to complete in a standard paced play-through is pretty frustrating. I've had to quit early a few times, and was forced to restart levels I was close to completing.

Grabbing objects with Vive wands require holding the grip and trigger buttons, a really terrible design choice. Being able to toggle the grip would be ideal.

Other than that, this is one of the most immersive experiences I've played.

Edit: Seriously the lack of checkpoints is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ atrocious.

2nd Edit: Wow the last mission is terrible as well. Physics puzzles are not fun when everything is floaty and impossible to keep from moving. Trying to aim a cannon with wheel handles is soooo bad. And to have to them four times while endless waves of enemies come at you? No thanks.

Do yourself a favor and download a save that has 100% complete after beating all but the last level.\t\t\t\t\t
