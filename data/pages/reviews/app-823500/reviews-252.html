\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGotta be honest here. This game... Stress Level Zero you need to give yourselves a pat on the back for this one, this is maybe the best VR game I have ever touched, first off the story is extremely good although there is one massive issue many have already mentioned, it is the hardest story to understand. The normal player will not even realise the game has a story until they do a bit of research but once you understand it the games world and gameplay becomes so much bigger. I won't spoil anything but it's definitely worth the effort. The gameplay is extremely creative, it combines the physics based gameplay from games such as half life and portal and places it into VR. For many, this is too much for a VR game but SLZ didn't care whatsoever and pushed this game to the limit. It's hard to explain everything but literally just imagine half life 2 in VR and boom you have boneworks (Now that I think about it that's just Half Life Alyx but trust me it is a completely different experience). There is also a sandbox mode where you can have fun with the games physics and just to basically whatever you want and then there's mods. Yes, this game has a modding community, I was surprised too but it adds a whole pile of content to this $25 game. This game is probably one of the best experieneces with VR I've had and you should definitely buy it now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played through the "campaign." It was fine even with the bare-bones story, and sure the physics system is neat. If this were a tech demo, I'd say it's absolutely fantastic. If I were to rate this in terms of a video game on the other hand...

Let me just say that I didn't have trouble progressing through the game; I've played through multiple VR games so I was able to get used to the movement and the physics systems with relative ease. I think my issue lies with the design philosophy they and some other developers are going for here. I think that applying the same physics system used by the rest of the environment to the player character is a swing and a miss for the future of VR. It just doesn't feel quite right. The stickiness of manipulating your character detracts from the experience, in my opinion. There is a disconnect between the player and the character you are controlling. I feel that you should be immersed in the world around you, but the subtle need to contour how you move in real life just to simulate a specific amount of force or position is not an intuitive concept for VR games with our current technology. This is probably why Valve didn't use a full body system in Half-Life: Alyx. There are still clearly physics in that game; for example, sometimes you need to use two hands to move something heavy. Notice how the object itself drags when you try to move it, but you aren't truly attached/stuck to it. Half-Life: Alyx is fun, it feels great to play, you feel grounded in that world.

BONEWORKS is definitely a pioneer in the VR industry. Ignoring the lack of a good narrative (the story is kind of interesting? that is, if you search it up after playing), I'd say buy the game if you are truly interested in experiencing the physics system. The way the game handles player movement is very focused, but it is ultimately not what I believe to be an intuitive system to implement in future big VR titles. Maybe this will work down the road when VR tech is more advanced, but not now.\t\t\t\t\t
