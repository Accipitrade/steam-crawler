\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of, if not the best VR game out!

I have not played Half Life yet but this is a great game to get while waiting for Half Life to get cheaper. I rarely pay full price on games over $30.00 because I don't feel they should be worth more then that and lose interest on most games quickly. However, Boneworks is an amazing game for the money. Tons of stuff to collect. Easy to play story mode multiple times. They seem to always update the game to bring in new features or item. Its a weird game in a lot of ways but I love the style of it and all just blends so well. My fav thing about this game is if you can think it you can probably execute it in some manner. Want to stuff a zombie in a locker or trashcan?... You can. Put one of the jumping crabs in a desk drawer for a quick clean up? Yup. Just about anything in the game can be used how you want to use it,

Honestly the best, most in depth VR game you can get into right not. The physics make it fun and in a weird way slightly realistic.

THIS IS THE FUTURE OF GAMES.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to recommend this game as a must play, simply because it's a milestone for VR. Namely, full body models and physics on everything. That said there were a number of things I felt wern't so good. To be fair, I did just play HL:A before this. So, I'm being critical.

I liked:
- The full body interaction and IK system. Guns were great. When you get good at reloading it feels rad. Climbing was one of the best parts for me.
- Some of the puzzles
- Intense combat moments.

I didn't like:
- The story, which made no sense. It just felt half-assed.
- The pacing. It had good parts, then would lull. (Instead of a steady increase in plot and action.)
- The music. I'm sorry. It sucked.
- Some of the environments went from Portal-esc low poly to just plain cheap feeling.

But yeah, if they can make a sequel, hire some good writers for a decent story, get better music and craft some AAA level environments, I'd buy that for sure.\t\t\t\t\t
