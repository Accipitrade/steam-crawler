\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow. This is something I've never experienced before, and I really like it.

A lot of VR games are great. It's truly a revolutionary new way to experience video games. But playing this game especially does feel like being transported into a different world. It should be experienced all the way through by anyone who is resistant to VR motion sickness.

One thing I don't see mentioned enough is the soundtrack. I was pleasantly surprised with the mix of 80s synthwave music and some unique atmospheric tracks. They definitely put some thought into which tracks to play at specific times to enhance the mood too.

The game does have some issues though. They mostly come down to the physics being janky, especially anything related to climbing, and the storytelling being pretty weak. I don't think either of those things is a deal breaker, but I will say that there were a few climbing sections where I had to take a break out of frustration.

The story mode game length for me was about 13 hours. I really took my time and explored though, so I'd imagine someone could blaze through in 8-10 hours on a first playthrough if they wanted to. I'll probably end up playing through most of it again for the unlockables, which can be used in the sandbox mode. I'm also looking forward to playing the new zombie mode.

This game is great, and definitely worth every penny of the $30 price tag.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery fun, a great experiment as to how physics and VR can coexist. Absolutely a must have for new and veteran VR gamers.

I've been following the developer of this game since the FreddyW days when he was known as BrandonJLA. It's really cool to watch his progression from fun youtube videos to full blown game development. (I know it isn't just one person creating the game, Stress Level Zero has a full dev team.)

The story of this game is good, but if you're not "aware" of what it is, you might miss a lot. It's comparable to Dark Souls, where the backround story is hidden in contex clues, or obscure references, but maybe not quite as bad as Dark Souls.

Gameplay is very fun, story mode took me a LONG time to finish, witch was really cool for a VR title, most games can be completed in around an hour or so. This one took me around 10-15 hours to complete. I was so engrossed in exploring my environment, that the main level completion was kind of secondary. And when you are done with the story mode, there is a plethora of mini-games and extras that you can play around with. Plus, to unlock more extras, you have to explore various levels, or complete challenges to unlock them. While this can be seen as a way to just "pad the runtime" of the game, I see it as a fun incentive to play the game more, and explore every nook and cranny of the VR space. If that is a deal breaker for you, I'm sure there are guides you can follow to unlock everything.

Gameplay is a mix of "realistic" and "video game" physics, with a focus on making the world fun to be in, rather than a 1 to 1 replication of real life. For example, your characters physical abilities is basically limitless, being able to lift heavy objects and do 1000000 pull-ups without breaking a sweat, however secondary object interaction follows real to life standards. Another example is the gunplay. Guns focus more on the fun and easy to use example, rather than a comparable games (Hotdogs, Horseshoes, and Hand Grenades for example) example of 1 to 1 realistic guns. Making them very fun to play with, and easy to reload and use with minimal training.

TLDR; Very fun VR game, and I would say is a requirement for any new or veteran VR player. Excellent example of a "realistic but video game" in VR, following real to life physics, but retaining a very fun gaming experience.\t\t\t\t\t

Some examples could be Super Mario Bros, and how it popularized console gaming, or Sonic the Hedgehog and how it showed that other companies could stand up to nintendo, or maybe the counter strike series one of the pioneers for spray pattern based first person shooters, the list goes on and on, until we reach December 10th 2019, and boneworks is released. And I say this with no hyperbole, boneworks is the most important VR game ever released to this day. Now I know that is a very strong claim but i support it 100%. We've had some other very important VR games, such as half life alyx and Vrchat. But these games despite being incredible do not set the standard for vr games like boneworks does. Everything about this game is absolutely enthralling, The visuals are distinct and great, the level design is immaculate, the soundtrack is so underrated that its a crime, the physics are the best out of any vr game out right now, and the story stretches across the entire stress level zero metaverse, allowing for awesome lore.
