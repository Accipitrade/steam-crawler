\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was so excited to buy this game but sadly it's not very playable for me.
I'm currently running the Original Vive with Vive wands.
That makes any object interactions very difficult. Even the first little grab/throw tutorial.
Luckily I have wrist bands because it literally wants you to throw your controller.
You have to use the grip button AND the trigger button to grab stuff. And also release both to throw. Leaving you with maybe just your pinky holding on to the controller.
Second..
I've been playing VR for over 2 years and have no motion sickness issues except in roller coasters or racing games. I have over 1000 hours in Onward and never feel sick.
15min in this game and I'm not feeling very well. The game literally pushes you away when you are near solid objects or walls. If you crouch a little too low, the game pushes you upwards. It's very disorienting and unnatural.
Finally.. The lower body animation really breaks the immersion for me. Specially when my legs jiggle and jump forward clipping into objects or furniture. For some reason my legs always seem bent at the knee.. even though my height is properly adjusted.
I'll give it another go but I have a feeling that I will be requesting a refund.
At the moment I do not recommend this game for anyone other than Index controller users.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter finishing Boneworks and goofing around in the sandbox, I can safely say this game is my new favorite VR game of all time. #1. Easily. It's made me worse at other games because I keep trying to interact with the world in a way only possible in this game. I've been spoiled. Thanks, Stress Level Zero.

Objectively, this game is pretty cool but it has a couple flaws. No mid level checkpoints, odd button-mappings, and some janky physics quirks here and there.
Though a small price to pay for the incredible physics at play here. This game is as innovative as Half Life 2 was when it first blew people away with it's incredible physics interactions.
The gunplay is fantastic (although a drop clip button would be nice) the melee is incredibly fun but the most important takeaway that makes Boneworks so absolutely fantastic, It actually has a story. Story is what separates tech demo's from games. The soundtrack, atmosphere, and story are all told in a (semi) diagetic way that incorperates it into the game world.

Boneworks is the most immersive and engaging singleplayer game I've played in a long time and I can't wait to see where Stress Level Zero takes us next.

(Micheal Wyckoff kicked ass with the amazing synthwave soundtrack. My favorites are "Who Am I" and "Analogue Sunrise")

Edit: 80 hours in now(got all the bullets and "big prizes") and still love this game. It's my go to when Im taking breaks from work! Zombie mode never gets boring and i hear whispers of yet another content update!\t\t\t\t\t
