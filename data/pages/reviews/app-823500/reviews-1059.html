\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing. This was the first game I played after I bought my Oculus Rift S (literally started playing this within an hour of setting up my VR) and I was fully expecting to get motionsick and experience a steep learning curve-- but it worked beautifully.
I may just be lucky but I experienced no motion sickness whatsover, no pain from wearing the headset for 7 hours during my first VR session (even though I didn't use my glasses while using the headset) and the game itself took about 2 hours to fully learn, and after that it was just bliss.

The gunplay is slick and oh-so-satisfying. Pulling out two glocks from your holsters and lighting up a room full of enemies in slowmotion-- the simulated body really fools your mind into thinking you are actually there, and the physics are AMAZING. Basically everything is interactable and thus can be used as a weapon-- in a room with a nullbody? Pick up a computer monitor and chuck it at him, or just grab him by the neck and push him against a wall. However you want to play it, you pretty much can. And the melee weapons are so much fun to use aswell.

As a first VR game this was literally heaven-- I realize not everyone will have such a pleasant experience, if this is their first game. I was just probably just really lucky I didn't get sick. But for VR-experts and newbies like me, this game is a MUST HAVE and a literal steal at 25 euros (I would easily pay 40-45 euros for this).

My only negatives are that there are no mid level saves (which are coming in the form of checkpoints) and I wouldn't mind a few more guns (bigger variety of pistols, shotguns, maybe some sniper rifles?) but these are minor issues. This game is amazing. And the soundtrack is amazing too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS is genuinely the best VR game I have ever experienced. It's so good in fact that it has literally ruined pretty much every other VR game because it doesn't look or handel as well as Boneworks does. This game is the best example of the exciting future of VR. If a small team of people can pull of this just wonder what that new Halflife is gonna be like.
Guns: The guns in this game is unmatched. While Pavlov does a good job with the gun looks and accuracy the reloading and shooting award definitely goes to Boneworks. Its so enjoyable to slow down time and watch individual bullet cases fly out of your gun.

The physics in this game is incredible, almost everything is interactable and can be used as a weapon (Yes even a stuffed monkey can become a killing machine). The puzzles arent based on buttons and switches. They rely on puzzles that make work of of systems that Boneworks has set up like anti gravity, weight of objects and other stuff. Very similar to Portal but without the portals.

Enemies are just wonderful. They are smart and put up good fights and there is a good variety as well. Beating down hordes of zombies with a frying pan then swapping to slow mo and whipping out a pistol to finish them off feels so cool and cinematic. The length of the game is also pretty long. Not the Witcher long but i got at least 10 hours out of the campaign and for a VR game that's pretty darn good. But after the game content is truly great. There are tons of secrets and items to collect for the SANDBOX. I truly believe that the Sandbox could have been a separate game and it still would have sold as good as it did. Playing god with NPCs, sending bad guys flying with a gravity gun, setting up a scenario where you're surrounded by Nullbodies and you take them all down John Wick style is just some of the fun you have in Sandbox. And on top of all of this great game. The only part of this game that isn't great would be the story. There is little to none. Usually I would dock major points because I think story and narrative is a huge part of a good game but it's everything else is so good that the lack of story is forgivable. The creme de la crumb of this game is that the amazing developers are still planning large updates for the game. They said more guns, more environments for sandbox and more arena modes.
BONEWORKS, is truly a masterpiece. I think this game will be the new high bar for VR. We will look back at this as a building block for true immersion in a virtual world. If you own VR it would be a crime to not buy this game. 9/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay so now that I finally have the time to write this updated review for Boneworks, here's what I have to say about it.

This game is incredible, everything down to the core of this game is amazing. This game took VR to the next level. The environment, the combat, the unique puzzles. Now the climbing does feel a bit clunky at some points, but that could just be me sucking at the game.

Before I made this updated review, I had a very big issue with the lack of save points in the middle of levels, but now that has been fixed and now people who don't have a bunch of free time (like me) can actually progress through the game without having to restart the entire level.

This game is very much worth getting. In fact I consider this a must have if you have a VR headset. Its just that good.

TLDR: This game is an amazing VR experience and is a must have. Buy it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCurious but great experience !

Boneworks combines puzzles, gunfights, melee, all this with a complexe physics !

Campaign is long enough and worth the 25. Level design is like a demo but extremely polished.
Gameplay mechanics are great but sometimes pretty rough (especially when you want to climb on something, your legs no longer exist), and often your body members are stuck in objects (you do not step over objects on the ground, for example).

Soundtrack is fantastic !

Despite the few faults that I mentioned, it is true that Boneworks is one of the VR games that bring a standard in VR and deserves the success it meets.

So, it's a big YES for me !\t\t\t\t\t
