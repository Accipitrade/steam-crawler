\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[Played on Oculus Rift S]

Pros:
Amazing gun mechanics
Great physics
Sick melee combat
Tons of weapons
Fun enemies
Cool level design
The combat pace picks up significantly and you feel like a badass popping heads left and right with your own gun.
The game RARELY forces you to use weapons that you dislike, and in big action sequences hides a variant of almost every gun in the area, so you can find the ones you're most comfortable with.
The vending machine system works great, and I like how they adjust the prices of items to fit with the theme of the level.
Sandbox mode looks extremely fun, haven't actually played it much yet.

Cons:
The physics puzzles are short enough to not be too annoying, but they are annoying
The (very limited) non-optional climbing is, to me, not fun. The climbing in general is ok, but having no easy way to climb on top of things should honestly just be changed and I wouldn't be so down on it.
Physics jank is less funny and more annoying when doing a vr physics puzzle.
Before the addition of save points, I wouldn't have even recommended the game. With them it's significantly better.
Some save locations are right next to enemies, so you spawn in with no gear (it spawns in a pile next to you if you saved some, mags unloaded) and you have to quickly grab all your ♥♥♥♥ before you get killed.

Overall I'd give boneworks a must buy if you own a VR headset. Fight through the slow beginning levels and some annoyances and you'll be greatly rewarded in the later levels, trust me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes, it took me exactly 38 minutes to realize this game is not for me. The biggest issue being that it would seem Stress Level Zero only ever intended the game to work with the Valve Knuckles. Trying to play the game with standard vive controllers is laughably clunky. Force grabbing objects orients them in random and usually useless directions (I.e. grabbing a hammer by the head), you have to use both the grip button and trigger at the same time, resulting in almost instant joint fatigue, and you have to maintain your death grip on the object until you're ready to let go of it, which is also clunky as all hell.

While we're talking about negatives, the gunplay is so cheesy and stale that its obvious the hype generated by Node's gameplay videos was just that. Hype. If you actually look at the weapons while you're firing them... the slide doesn't even move. The trigger never makes contact with your finger.. Again, if you're using the Vive controllers, your index finger in game hangs 6cm off the side of the handgun, coming all the way in to "pull" the trigger each time you fire the gun. Its laughable. The recoil is also goofy as hell, and I'm sorry Corridor and Node fans, but "Realistic" gigantic muzzle flashes look aweful in VR, especially when they are obviously explosion textures that just flash on screen for a frame or two.

I think the concept of the game is interesting, and having a fully rigged 3D body was very immersive. However, the game is, in my opinion, entirely inaccessible to anyone who doesn't own the Valve Knuckles. Its a clunky mess with some of the worst controls I've seen. I had hoped the gunplay would be it's saving grace, but sadly I was wrong.

Maybe when I have enough money to wipe with dollars instead of TP I'll buy the knuckles and try it again. Until then I'd say steer clear of this game unless you've got too much money to throw in to your VR gear. I would implore SLZ to consider better optimizing their games for people who can't afford state of the art finger tracking, but I imagine that's not really what they're after.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm fairly new to VR, but I have to say this is the most immersed I've ever been in a game. After getting through the "tutorial" the game pretty much cuts you loose to figure things out on your own, which is probably my favorite part. Being able to come up with real-world solutions to in-game problems is where this game really shines. Weapons and other items work as you'd expect them to, although I occasionally have problems holstering/retrieving things. The only downsides I could think of are due to the current limitations of VR, things like your body/arms not being where you expect to see them and weight/inertia physics (ex: objects you can barely pick up with both hands can be dragged/pushed across the ground with remarkable ease). Even with all the realism, it is still a game and has its quirks to get around, but that too can be fun sometimes!\t\t\t\t\t
