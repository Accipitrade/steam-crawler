\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have mostly positive feelings about this game.
It's the best VR game made so far, so let's get that out of the way.
This game's main purpose is to open the doors to next-generation VR and set a new bar that other developers should strive to live up to. That said, this game is not perfect.

I'll start with the bad (maybe some of these could be worked on in the future?):
It seems to me that there are way too many guns/ammunition in the main story; you could opt for melee weapons, but when you have 2 pistols, 2 ARs, and an smg, what's the point?
There are also some questionable design choices along the way, like putting mobs right next to a save point, or having a clearly never-ending stream of mobs come one after the other as you kill the prior mob out. Or having the odd bit of climbable geometry that doesn't have any surprise in store at the top. Or mystery boxes that cost 800 bullets and contain nothing. It's clear that these devs want to mess with you, and they're not going to give you an easy time about it.
The gun-play and mechanics are also kind of sloppy and weak at times, comparing to games like Pavlov/Onward.
The story is kind of lacking and there isn't a whole lot to it.
It would be nice if we knew what reclamation bins did beforehand.
This game can be hard to run on a GTX 970 at times.
Some of the puzzles are going to make you hate your life.
And they have sold themselves short; they have created many of the tools to create a next-generation Garry's mod type of experience but A. They have hidden the sandboxes, and B. The game is not multiplayer.

Those minor peeves out the way, I want to talk about some things the game did right:
The movement/interaction/melee systems of the game are very well done, and these are pretty well the whole point of the game.
There are very many stimulating moments to say the least.
MP5 slap.
The intense feelings especially when you have to deal with several enemies closing in on you at the same time.
The world feels so good to touch, use, walk on, and knock over etc.
My girlfriend enjoys this game too (but unfortunately can't play it for more than 10 minutes yet due to motion sickness).
There are many pleasant (and sometimes unpleasant) surprises.
This game is really a thinking man's game, but you have to be a thinking man with some brawn too, because this game will take the piss out of you some times.

I loved this game overall. I rate it a solid 7.6/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm rating this down from only really a gameplay/performance perspective.

I was somewhat led to believe this was an action/half-life esque title, but I was immediately met with physics puzzles and near immersion-breaking gameplay when it came to climbing, interacting with objects, and fighting enemies with melee weapons. I was totally okay with physics puzzles and combat, but just how everything meshed together, terrible optimization for the Rift S (and other headsets from what my friends have experienced), lack of adjustable controls, severe framedrops at points, and movement so clunky that it actually interfered with solving puzzles, I really have no choice but to rate this down.

I'm honestly severely disappointed due to how highly spoken this game was but I'm glad everyone else loves it! It's honestly a super impressive game and props to the devs for making a great technical stride in VR, but this is too messy for me to play and it's disheartening to play in its current state. If you have a Valve Index, I'd say go ahead and try it because the game is HEAVILY suited for it, but if you don't and you're a Oculus, HTC, or Windows Mixed Reality user I'd suggest waiting until the game is better suited for other headsets.

--EDIT--

After visiting this game a year later, unfortunately it seems that nothing changes as the developers never really intended to fix some of the game's glaring issues with mobility and puzzles. I just downloaded a save from online to actually enjoy the full game instead of playing through the broken story. It is still mind boggling how they locked separate game modes from the start behind the story and other niche challenges. Although I cannot refund the game, I will not be supporting their next title if it is released and I honestly suggest not buying this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought a VR headset and have yet to feel totally immersed in one of the games you've bought? Thought you would get your headset and would lose yourself in your games but have to experience the joy of smacking a hole through your drywall with your controller? Look no further. Boneworks is the VR game I was waiting for, for so very very long. I've owned VR headsets since around the launch of the HTC Vive and no game has immersed me quite like this one. I have to remind myself constantly that this isn't real, so I don't run into my wall or try climbing up a nonexistant grab point. Is it perfect? No. It definitely has some rough edges, but it offers immersion like nothing else I've played. This feels like the first full fledged, built for VR game that made me go WOW I'm glad I bought a headset. Boneworks devs, if you're reading this, please think about leasing out your physics and locomotion systems out to big game engines (Unreal, Unity, etc.) Having the skeleton, physics systems, etc. that you guys have built could serve to be a fantastic standard for the future of VR games to be built upon. Much love Stress Level Zero, thank you for your hard work!\t\t\t\t\t
