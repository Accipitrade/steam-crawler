\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the first top tier VR games I ever played, it lived up to everything I could have expected and more. The campaign is by far the longest in any VR game I've ever played, I was relatively new to VR when I did it so it took me about 25 hours, it can be beaten much faster if you know what you're doing but I didn't at the time. The guns feel perfect, the maps are laid out well, melee weapons are an option (Unlike Half-Life), and most importantly they didn't compromise on pushing VR to its limits. Having a full body that that has collision in a game where you can jump, crouch, sprint, and climb all while standing straight up, great for small playspaces. This and Half-Life Alyx are probably the two best experiences you can get for showing everything VR can do, you can really get creative with how you played this.

A few small details I love:

You can pull yourself up onto things without having to actually grab them, because your whole body has collision and you
can pull your legs up with digital crouch.

Everything in the game has weight to it, including you. If you jump off with something it'll slip out from under you if it's not supported well, and it's something heavy you really feel like it's difficult to move, a lot of games don't do this.

You can interact with almost everything with items you hold in your hand, like turning a valve with the barrel of your gun (Unlike Half-Life). You can even climb using melee weapons like hooks. This seems like it would be obvious but a lot of games don't let you do this.

This one really blew my mind, in dark rooms if you point your flashlight at the ceiling it illuminates the room evenly as if it was real, I instinctually did it and I didn't expect it to work, fantastic attention to detail.

You can grab the gun in so many different ways, a lot of shooters in VR seem to forget this. You can hold it by the barrel, a different part of the barrel, or the mag well, or a few other places too. And you can knockout a mag with another mag.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me just say that this review is going to focus mostly on what I don't like about the game, so here we go. First of all, what I do like: it has amazing physics (Sometimes), the gameplay is great, it is super immersive, and a game to easily spend hours at a time playing without knowing any time has passed. Now on to the bad parts, for one, there is no haptic feedback on the controllers (for quest 2 at least), this does kinda ruin immersion but is certainly no deal breaker. Now my other complaint, remember how I said the physics were great SOMETIMES? Well, when they are not great, BOY ARE THEY BAD. I'm talking teleporting hands, (or sometimes even your whole body) noodle arms when you grab something or touch something at the wrong angle, you get it. I know that is all normal vr things but they happen very frequently in this game due to always interacting with things, so please, PLEASE pay attention to the "expert vr users only" warning because it is not there for no reason. I would recommend this game to pretty much anyone who likes shooters, sandbox games, and puzzle solving games, because that's what this is.


Final rating: 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
