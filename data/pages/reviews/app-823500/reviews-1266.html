\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood:
- Physics systems
- Guns and small blunt weapons
- soundtrack (sometimes)
- Story, although that is very subjective
- most enemies are physics based and are really satisfying to kill

Bad:
- Heavy weapons are tickle machines and are almost useless basing most of its force by how fast the weapon is swung rather than the overall impact of said weapon. In addition swords feel as bad as they do in blades and sorcery, as they are more a kin to dull weighted sticks then to swords.
- Climbing and platforming are not really that much fun in a game that has so much effort put into it's physics systems and world. If anything should be fixed in this game, less climbing and platforming!!
- That being said the puzzles are mostly climbing and platforming, with the punishment for falling off said puzzle as you guessed it, more climbing and platforming. Honestly it was kind of disappointing as the few puzzles that require you to use the physics and world to do some thinking are pretty amazing!
- The game has a limited soundtrack that while good earlier in the game begins to grate on the ears in the late game (especially in survival)
- One enemy which I can only describe as rolling auto aim holograms are some of the most tedious and annoying to fight, as they have 100% accuracy at all times, roll faster than you can run, and must be hit in precisely the right areas to kill. That being said if they were something a kin to their more zombie like counterparts where they are physical entities and would be bound to the physics systems would really make a lot of the gameplay way more interesting. In addition if they also added tactics to these new enemies would be the icing on the cake.
- Hand to hand fights are fun at first, but like B&S even if your are punching your opponent with the might of Zues, in the face, while you have his head gripped and forced down, a few mere slaps to your knees will be enough to kill you in a couple seconds.
- no toggle to grip settings so using a melee weapon is almost a forearm workout on anything but the index
- on a similar note the m4 has a particularly annoying feature where you can grip the mag well... but wait isn't that a feature you ask? yes, but to grip the mag well requires constant pressure while the barrel does not, so you will often grab your magwell then let go thinking you grabbed the barrel, only for your wrist to do a 180 the second you start firing wasting the few bullets you have.

Bugs:
- No option to remove the m4 stock from colliding with your body
- On top of that some weapons will get stuck on your virtual neck
- Climbing is a mess as your arms will clip into objects and often get stuck
- Your ammo and left sidearm holster are inches apart and near you armpit so you will sometimes grab your ammo instead of your sidearm
- In addition to that sometimes while climbing you will draw your sidearm or ammo, and good luck getting that back in
- 25-50% of the times you draw m4 mags will get stuck midair and become immovable. (There was also an instance while fighting where I trapped myself between 4 mags and had to restart the level
- no saving system, so if you have to leave, or say get stuck between m4 mags, to bad

I like this game, and if your in the mood for some VR physics based hijinks then I say send it, but if not wait couple months and see where the game is then.
