\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm using a classic Vive mostly. This game does deserve its aplomb and status. The devs have worked hard to make a fun and creative game. The level design is very cool and I do feel completely immersed in this frightening world.
However, this game is so frustrating!
Part of the hype surrounding this game is the "advanced experimental physics mechanics" and "hyper realistic interaction". It's a fun idea but this later claim is completely ridiculous. This is a fundamental part of this game obviously. Don't kill me, but I'm not sure it wouldn't have been better without it.
The only reason this game is so difficult, is because of the physics. Nothing works like it does in reality!
I can't grab or pickup anything that I want to grab or pickup. I always grab the wrong thing or my belt pack ammo. I usually cannot grab any of my holstered weapons except the behind-the-shoulder weapons. And often when I do that, I will get the weapon from the wrong shoulder.
I can't ♥♥♥♥ weapons without grabbing the weapon instead.
I'm constantly dropping items then I can't grab them back again without 2 attempts.
The virtual body in the game is very cool and well designed, one of the best! (although in this day and age I find it amazing that the only option is a white male lead). But strangely, if I ever look at my hands, the fingers are always weird when holding items. I've played this with the Vive wands and the Index controllers and one or more fingers are often flailed open even when gripping a handled weapon like a knife or bat. On the Vive, if you don't hold the trigger finger while gripping, the index finger will not be wrapped around the handle like the other fingers. I guess this is supposed to be 'realistic' since the the trigger operates the index finger, but surely while holding a weapon I don't have to grip and squeeze the trigger. It just seems like this would be a case where they could ease up on the 'realism' just so that the hands don't look so wonky.
Whenever I hold an ax, knife or hammer, the weapon will turn sideways in my hand. I'm not sure it matters for combat but why not just fix this super weird side effect caused by gaming "physics". I find myself constantly re-grabbing an item to twist it to face forward.
Jumping and climbing are so unrealistic. Somehow in this game I feel very powerful, but for some reason I feel like I can only jump about 1 foot off the ground. I can climb straight up walls and poles with near impossible strength, but I can't climb over a 3 foot barrier because I can't get my legs over anything.
This game is about fighting. Fighting the controls!
After all my complaining, this is one of my favorite VR games. I love it! ha ha.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright alright.. I know people are going to hate this but im a tough critic. Let's see.. where to start . Hm. OK. I played Boneworks for an hour at the most which was followed by a refund. This is what happens when you play a perfect game like Half Life Alyx and then goto Boneworks. The very first thing I noticed was the sensitive movement controls with random running. There are elements to the intro that just didn't come off as interesting while it's obvious the devs tried hard to implement a little humor. The audio blows ass completely and was a complete turn off. There is no sense of environment with the audio and it does not take advantage of the touchless headphones on the Index for that realistic atmosphere feel. The Colors are very bland along with some very unpleasant lighting. Not to mention the textures throughout the game are horrible kinda like back in the 3Dfx era. The first puzzle that they present is annoying for me (personally). So i finally get to the part where you shoot the guns.. I'd rather much only see floating hands at that point because of how ragdoll the arms look while loading mags. The animation from the kickback of shots throws off the whole idea of a physics based game. The melee weapons can't be used with real speed swings so that was a bummer. After watching this game being developed... it was just a let down when finally played and what i feel like they could have done different was make it less of a puzzle game and more of an adventure game kinda like Half life Alyx. Boneworks would have really taken off. The game needs better lighting, less bland colors, higher quality textures, and better audio that isn't so centrally panned where it sounds mono.

Even with all of this said.. I still give the game a thumbs up because it's a complete level upwards for VR in terms of physics and the abilities achieved. I would like to see more of a story mode in realistic environments with better lighting that shows off the great physics of this game.\t\t\t\t\t
