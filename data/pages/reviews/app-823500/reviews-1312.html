\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the few games that I've played that has not only lived up to the hype, but has exceeded my expectations. It definitely has a Half-life vibe going, which is a good thing. The VR is definitely immersive (to the point I've managed to break a couple of things in my game room by getting a little too vigorous with my movements), and the gameplay and gunplay is great.

PROs:
- VR Movement. This is a hard one for games to get right, and BW has done an excellent job. Using a Rift controller, movement is accomplished by the left thumbstick, but forward is wherever you're looking. The right thumbstick allows you to crouch or stand on tip-toes. Neat.
- VR Grip. Different grips (two fingers, full fist, two fists) allow you varying degrees of fine motor control or stability. I like it.
- Price. This is an excellent bargain for the price.
- Immersion. I haven't gotten too far into the story, but there's clearly a sub-plot within the game (think Portal) that's eerie, disconcerting, and draws you in to find out more.

CONs:
- Control wonkiness. The controls, particularly for jumping and gripping, can be a bit wonky. I haven't quite figured out the ideal way to climb, throw things, or mantle myself up onto a ledge. I know it can be done, but only because I keep trying and manage to do it somehow without ever knowing quite how.
- Save. Having multiple save points would be nice, so that two people can play the game at different speeds, or you can go back to a previous point and fiddle with something.

All in all, it's excellent implementation of a lot of tricky concepts - story, VR immersion, controls. BW should be a benchmark of what an excellent physics-based adventure/FPS game should be like. Only needs some minor polish, but doubtless that will come with time.

I give it 85% - SOLID BUY.\t\t\t\t\t
