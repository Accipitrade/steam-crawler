\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's essentially unfathomable that anyone would not recommend this game. This is the most refined, most immersive and highest overall quality VR experience to date.

And the best thing is that it's actually a full game, and a surprisingly intelligent one. It's full of weirdness, remarks, notes, graffiti, puns, etc. which seem to indicate that a lot of care went into creating a unique and engaging atmosphere.

Of course you can't expect triple A length and complexity from the story, and the fact that the levels are completely separate makes it feel somewhat simple. But the levels are actually quite sizable and some of them can take 2 hours to complete.

The worst aspect of Boneworks is definitely the lazy ass annoying saving system; or actually the total bloody lack of any saving system. It's very annoying that you're forced to complete a whole level in one session, because otherwise your progress is lost. And if you're playing through Oculus Link on your Quest (like me), and it disconnects... well, you're f*cked.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic, if flawed, VR experience from start to finish. Keep in mind that I played the game on an OG HTC Vive setup which will definitely come into play when I discuss some of its flaws.

The game has a balance of exhilarating gunplay, weighty (if wonky) melee combat, and enjoyable physics puzzles; but all of these moments are isolated from one another. Guns in the game are very detailed, and reloading is incredibly satisfying when the wands aren't getting in the way of each other. If you encounter an enemy, regardless of what enemy it is, you simply point and shoot. Luckily, this act is very enjoyable with some of the best gunplay to date in a vr title, but I left the game feeling a little disappointed with the lack of creative combat. Melee weapons are cool and weighty, but require the player to constantly hold down the grip button to hold them which can quickly cause finger cramps on the HTC Vive wands. On the other hand, throwing objects is the smoothest its ever been in a vr title due to it's hold and release mechanics. The physics puzzles are simple and approachable, but never terribly difficult. Despite this, it still feels satisfying to solve them thanks to the open-ended nature of the games physics and the fantastic climbing.

The campaign took me 14 hours to complete, though a large portion of that time was spent on exploring various nooks and crannies/messing with the physics. If you just wanted to proceed through the story it'd probably take closer to 10 hours for the average player. The plot here is nothing to be in awe of, most of it is relayed to you through tv screens that you can completely ignore without hurting the experience at all. All in all, an amazing leap forward for interactivity and Ultra-mersion in VR that just leaves me wishing they hand spent as much time & creativity on designing combat encounters and plot as they did on the physics simulations.\t\t\t\t\t
