\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a experimental physics heavy puzzle game that to be honest isn't that good. Far from it actually.
The graphics are comparable to Goat Simulator (with the exception of the models), climbing is absolute jank, the story is quite rubbish (interesting concept though), and there are many more issues I have not listed.

But at the same time, it does the unexpected: It actually pushes the limits of VR tech.

Your body is no longer a severed head with floaty hands: You are Arthur Ford, a double agent on a adventure to achieve immortality. And ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ does the game make you feel this.

Your body has weight, collision, legs, a torso, mass, basically anything a human should have. You can't just stick your head through walls anymore, your head will actually get stuck on the wall, and will not pass through.

It is astonishing that yet again, indie devs actually dared to do something AAA devs are either too lazy or too afraid to do. All VR games before Boneworks were just tech demo after tech demo, or game that really isn't a game, but REALLY tries to convince you that it is (i am looking at you, Accounting)

Through the jank and admittatdly garbage graphics, what you get is a solid puzzle shooter that is essentially the Half-Life of VR games.

The only problem I really do have is the fact that combat ranges from smooth like butter to clunky mess, which is really noticable in melee combat. Oh yeah, and make sure to NEVER USE the small SMG, it does no damage and is responsible for ruining my arena run because I thought "oh maybe i could use this lol". That gun deserves to burn in hell.

Overall, I'll give this game a 9/10, just due to the fact that it took this long to make an ACTUAL game for VR.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS is fantastic and is, in my opinion, the best VR game currently on the market. Other games do not have the same immersive effect that BONEWORKS has on me, the gameplay is done so well, everything is interactive, the gunplay is addictive, the puzzles are fun, and the world building is really cool. The atmosphere paired with the level design and soundtrack just makes the experience awesome. The storytelling could be better, as you really have to look deep into the game to fully understand what is even happening, but the actual story behind this game is fascinating. I wont go into detail about what the story is about, but it just so happens to be tied into Stress Level Zero’s past and future games which makes learning about the story so much more interesting, and makes me really excited to see what else Stress Level Zero has up their sleeves. Fun game, looking forward to the next few games in this crazy universe.\t\t\t\t\t
