This was supposed to be the game to really break in my Index controls, and I fully expected to be wholly addicted and lose myself in this game. But, that's not what happened. Every time I finish a session, I leave frustrated and any sense of achievement I might have felt while playing is lost just like my progress because there's no save states.

This game really goes all in with its physics, I mean all in all the time. It doesn't matter if aspects like climbing, getting off ladders, or reliably grabbing or placing something in your inventory does not work well. This game is going to do it 100%. That level of enthusiasm and confidence, unfortunately does not pay off. The physics in this game are incredible, and will no doubt be an innovation and foundation for future games but I would not go so far as to say they are realistic enough to make me feel confident in navigating or manipulating environments.

I think this is a glimpse of the future, but that so far is all it is. A stumble, and not a leap. I would recommend this for people who are really into VR, but I would not recommend this for anyone else.

I could nit pick all day with how things in Boneworks handle, how I've fallen through the environment, how my arms get stuck all the time because there is no full body tracking (not the games fault). VR has some growing to do, and I hope Boneworks can still be the trailblazer I hoped it could be after more updates.\t\t\t\t\t
