\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is 100% a AAA experience VR title

I remember when the game got announced people thought this would just be some sort of tech demo showing off new features for VR and even the devs themselves have added that as an easter egg in the game saying stuff like "WHAT IF THIS IS JUST A TECH DEMO?"

But this is anything but a tech demo, if you're new to VR and can only do 30 min -1hr sessions you're going to have one long ass game on your hands. for me it took around 15 hours to beat and that concluded with going around the levels and messing around with everything.

The story is really interesting and original, taking big inspirations from half-life and portal and merging them perfectly.
Both the art style and soundtrack are amazing and if like me you have been longing for something close to a valve game you'll fall in love with boneworks.

Performance wise it runs fairly decent for me, im on a 970 setup and still got fairly consecutive 90hz but of course some areas did dip due to there being more assets being rendered in or whatever, but as long as ur PC isnt 5+ years old like mine you shouldnt have anything to worry about.

The physics system is fairly similar to that of Blade & Sorcery but i feel i prefer the execution in this game more.
Otherwise if you're looking for a complete action/adventure for VR this is THE game for it

Gotta say it again tho, the soundtrack is amazing
Also the devs are saying they're adding even more content in 2020\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR:
Good:
- Out of body experience
- Crazy good mechanics
- Well implemented across large ranges of hardware
- Good campaign of puzzles and combat

Bad:
- No mid-level saves (Should be coming soon)
- No motion-sickness prevention features

I've owned VR for around two years now and this is the best game I've played by a long shot. One of the biggest things in this game is the amazing reverse kinematics (The body simulated in game). It's really good at fooling your brain into thinking that you are actually a part of the game. There's also plenty of attention to detail when it comes to the aspects that matter. The gun reloads aren't finicky and don't result in your controllers slamming together, inside out tracking (I use the HP WMR headset) still handles reloads behind the back *really* well (Which was one of my primary concerns), and the puzzle construction brings me back to the moments of playing portal. Personally I got around 17 hours of game play out of the campaign and I enjoyed every minute. Even after the campaign I still go back to it for the experience and the area modes. Amazing work from the developers here at Stress Level Zero. This is the kickoff of VR experiences that I would call serious out-of-life experiences.

Every good game has it's downsides, and here it's the lack of mid-level saves, which I have no doubt they'll fix in upcoming updates. They do leave a disclaimer stating that this game is for experienced VR users and I do have to say this is true to some extent. The biggest downside I experienced personally was the motion sickness, but it only lasted the first two days or so of playing the game. (It only happened for me after a few hours of game play) Being one in the crowd of those who experienced the support issues with the WMR headsets on launch day I can definitely vouch for their attention to fixing issues quickly and effectively while keeping those who are affected extremely well updated.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is not the masterpiece I hoped it would be. It's too buggy to be that, though time will tell how much the devs polish it. Boneworks is like Skate 3: A buggy magnum opus, but not buggy to a frustrating extent like Budget Cuts was at release. The much hyped IK body turned out pretty accurate aside from possibly being thinner than you. The real problem is it's prone to getting snagged on things, among other physics bugs like the gun you paid for (with in-game currency, not real money) falling through the floor Skyrim-style. The variety of weapons are fun and feel great. The levels are sometimes repetitive and need more checkpoints, which the devs are working on. Towards the end I saw some extremely low res textures and a railing my hand could clip through. This game could've used a delay. The 2nd plot twist at the end doesn't make sense unless you pay special attention in the tutorial. Plot twists are not supposed to be forseen, but they are supposed to be understandable. Despite that, the enemy design is iconic. The nullbodies are familiar and alien at the same time, like the red guys from Superhot but unique. The mechanical headcrabs are straight out of Half-Life, but have cute sinister sound effects that set them apart.

WMR support was broken at first but the devs have fixed all the platform-specific bugs I ran into now. Boneworks is CPU-heavy but runs great on my GTX 1650, it probably would work on worse GPUs. Despite my Core i5 3470 being quad-core 3 GHz like the system requirements say, the level with the clippy railing (Tower) froze multiple times and dropped to 30 FPS until I killed 15 or so enemies. Boneworks is a must-have in spite of its flaws, and I hope it gets the polish it deserves by the time you purchase it.

Did I mention the soundtrack is worthy of being in a Shin Megami Tensei game? This is some pure electronic goodness extract, and several tracks could be lead singles on a solo album, no game necessary. There's even a dancefloor near the end of the game filled with grunts you can dance-kill with knives. This is the best motion control usage since Zelda: Skyward Sword.

Update: Patch 1.4 has made your hands steady when climbing. Arms can still get stuck in wall though.\t\t\t\t\t
