\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one I was VERY excited for. I had heard it was a milestone for VR interactivity and physics. Then I played it myself. There is very little that is special about boneworks. The gunplay is absolutely amazing, yet everything else is bland and diluted. The level design of the campaign can either be complex and interesting, or just big empty warehouse rooms, and unfortunately it's almost always the latter. As for content, there is almost none. The physics puzzles which were very hyped up were exceedingly sub-par, you move the platform to bring the box over to the other side, this is almost every puzzle there is, and whoever decided that having a virtual body was something you should do in VR is a total nutcase. You try to pick up a box that is maybe 2 feet tall but you have no idea where you are grabbing because your knees spasm and clip into the object you are trying to pick up or examine. The physics are certainly not as revolutionary as anyone claims. This is nothing new; games have been using mediocre physics engines for 25 years, and this one is no better than that of half life 2. The melee combat is wimpy and dull, with you just slapping what might as well be a wet noodle at a red wire-frame enemy until they crumple. Buy this if you are fine with walking through empty room after empty room, then doing an out of place and very amateur puzzle.\t\t\t\t\t
