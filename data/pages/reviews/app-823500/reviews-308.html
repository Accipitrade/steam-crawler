\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe only VR game I get motion sick in.

Has gunplay that feels extremely scuffed. I wouldn't mind too much but some levels rely on longer range guns. Maybe its just on Quest 2 controllers but my hands aren't even where the grip is on the gun, it takes awkward maneuvering to properly aim, and quickly aiming is impossible because I have to figure out where my hand in real life is compared to in game, it's such a ♥♥♥♥ show and really turns me off to gunplay. A lot of the time, I have to move my hand to the center of my chest to get the gun on my shoulder so I can properly aim. AND BEST OF ALL, it doesn't even go where the sight is, maybe I'm just bad at shooting but there has been so many occurrences where a holographic sight reticle was aimed right someone and it doesn't make contact. And don't even get me started on the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ off hand grip, it just isn't where my hand is. On the M4 with angled grip and Holo sight, I have to grip it at the magazine rather than the actual grip because where my hand is IRL is nowhere near where it is in game. The guns themselves feel pretty weak too, the recoil is scuffed. but if I wanted to play a shooter, i'll just play Pavlov.

Also the gunner robots? idk what they're called, they are super annoying and unsatisfying to kill. Unlike the regular polygon zombie men, which spurt out blood, has large "hitmarker" bullet pen spots, that are easy to tell where you can shoot or not to land a shot, and make a neat sound when they die. Often time, the gunner robots are pushed up against the wall and I can't see their actual exoskeleton so I shoot the holograph wondering why they aren't dying, then am forced to either get closer or just shoot randomly until i kill them.

The game is really short too, there's a lot of side stuff, but honestly none of it has the same "feel" as the story. I can beat the entire story before my Quest 2 dies. There's mods but, they never will feel like the quality of the base game that has been nurtured by the devs.

Stabbing polygons is fun, and the movement feels so good once you master it. One of the biggest complaints I see for the game is the arms and movement. I love it so much, actually being able to physically open doors with your guns, prop your gun against a wall or like a bipod, and be able to climb and jump is really neat and I wish games like Pavlov would do it. It's so immersive it's insane. When I first found the sword in the mission with all of the headcrabs, I spent at least an hour just breaking the boxes with the headcrabs just so I could pretend to be a fuggin swordsman. It's beyond satisfying stabbing random ♥♥♥♥ in the game.


This game sits in between good and bad for me.
If the gunplay was reworked, as well as new enemies were added, and a co-op with more levels or multiplayer thing was added, I 100% think this would be the best VR game period. I think it's still 100% worthwhile to get, it's an experience worth going through, and even with all the flaws, this game sets the ground for perfect balance between "realism" and "playability".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a VR title that is built for pure physics fun. The game has great puzzles that take a while, but not too long to solve. Once you're done with the puzzle solving, you get to the shooting. God is the gun mechanics good. It's pretty realistic with the guns, and they feel impactful to shoot.

Performance wise, it runs very good. I'm using a quest 2 with a 20 dollar link cable off of amazon that uses USB 3, not even USB C. The game still runs flawlessly. I use a 1660 Super, which is pretty average, and an i5-10400F. Again, pretty average. I know that reviews from the past few years say that the game doesn't run too great, but i believe that the performance issues are mostly fixed. Just make sure you have an okay CPU.

on a scale out of ten, I'd rate the game an 8, probably a 9 if the weird bugs get fixed.\t\t\t\t\t
