\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is not the perfect vr game, very far from it. This games physics are well made for a game that took 3 years to make but the story and puzzles where very skuffed. As for the story it is the most cryptic story i've seen in any game. I knew almost as much of this vr world at the start of the game that i did at the end of it. As for the puzzles, thought was made of them, but i feel like they needed more testing. Most of the time i knew what i had to do with a puzzle, but couldn't finish it because the platforms would be shaking to much, or the floor would freak out because of physics.

After rewatching all the cutscenes and monitor interactions along with diving into other fan theories i can say that i confendtly understand most of the story. (Besides the headcrabs) The whole story is more on the simplier side, but whats worse is the plot pacing they barely tell you anything thats happening. there is a massive connection made at the end of the game to the very first cutscene, but it isnt explained so after it you are thinking "so... what just happened."

TLDR if you like playing with physics and guns but aren't looking for a massive story to chew on, and have the patience to withstand the buggy puzzles, its a buy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo ...and yes.

Single player game hidden as a tech demo.

Pros:
- Good gun mechanics
- Levels look nice
- Some cool puzzles
- Physics are somewhat cool

Cons:
- Janky. Arms/body can get caught on something and makes it difficult to interact with the world.
The vr body doesn't bend at the waist, so trying to lean over and grab something is impossible. The game world will just shift further away from you which is weird.
- Can't climb onto or over objects unless devs intended to, which means not everything can be grabbed.
- Puzzle after puzzle after puzzle. Gets repetitive and boring.
- Although gun mechanics are good, can be difficult to aim.
- Inventory gesture system isn't responsive enough. I often spend more time dropping an item than putting it in an inventory slot. Most of the times I just go into the option wheel to pull up inventory.
- Cannot save. Instead, you have to reach a checkpoint. So that means when you play again, you have to redo some, if not all, the stuff just to get back to where you left off.

Overall, the interaction is cool, but the body/hands gets in the way of everything and makes interacting annoying, cumbersome, and tedious. The puzzles are interesting for the first hour, but gets boring.

With this new tech (vr) and how it is saturated with glorified tech demo's, it would have been better if Stress Level 0 just went the story driven single player route rather than just room after room of physics based puzzles that aren't much of a challenge, but instead a tedious obstacle to get around until you reach the next tedious obstacle.

I just hope Half-Life: Alyx doesn't become the same. I just don't understand why make something based around physics when the physics is still clunky.\t\t\t\t\t
