\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a brilliant example of what VR games are capable of. Its heavy focus on physics and puzzles, as well as physics-based combat, makes for one of the most immersive games I have ever played. The sandbox nature of its campaign also encourages creativity, with the way in which you complete an objective being down to your own choices and abilities as a player. The amount of post-game content, plus replay value, ensures that I can keep coming back to it for a fun experience over and over again. It is not necessarily perfect, though, and sometimes the physics and combat can be a bit clunky, especially with melee weapons, and it takes some getting used to the climbing and mantling required at various points in the game. For what it has accomplished, however, I am willing to overlook the clunkiness as an example of VR still being in its infancy. It is brilliant, and if you are someone who has been playing VR for a while (and is no longer prone to motion sickness), I would highly recommend this game.

8.7/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlmost everything about this game is amazing. It is the most interactive VR game you can pretty much find on the market as of now. The melee combat is not perfect, stabbing feels good if not a little to easy while chopping and slicing does not feel natural though with chopping you get a few really good ones like the Fireman axe or the cleaver. Guns feel great to use and you really feel like John Wick sometimes. I chipped a tooth while I was a little too immersed and forgot to not clamp down on my teeth in fury that only the Null Bodies could truly feel.
Immersion is also a great way to describe this game. It really respects the environment that you're put in, and really lets you use it if you want. Climbing feels good, not very floaty and somewhat annoying but I suppose thats how climbing actually is. If you plan to 100% the game you need to get used to climbing.
If you are not used to being in VR then I recommend starting with something less movement heavy. Walking Dead Saints and Sinners is a good way to slowly get into BONEWORKS as they have a large part of gun and melee combat with a focus on inventory. Walking dead is a bit slower and less movement and climbing heavy. If you play Blade and Sorcery then you will be perfectly fine with this game.
Physics are your friend in this game. They just make the game so much more enjoyable. Actually touching enemies and environments and playing your own way is so essential. You dont feel like a character you feel like you.
Lastly, story. Many people say it has a weak story, which I can understand. The fact is that two cutscenes and a talking head on a monitor or two vaguely telling you into what the protagonists intentions are is not the most interactive story, and probably wont suck you in if you just pay attention to those things. The truth is that there are messages all over the place if you know what they mean. How would you know? Well, that is complicated. The true strength in BONEWORKS' story is that it is apart of a meta narrative between the Stress Level Zero games. To get the most out of this play Duck Season and if you get a chance Hover Junkers though it is lacking players. This narrative will continue to their future titles so I recommend if you enjoy this game to prepare in that.\t\t\t\t\t
