\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI gotta admit, so far, I love this game. The physics are incredible and I haven't seen anything similar in any other VR game so far. I liked pretty much everything the game offered up to this point, though while it was great and I would recommend it to someone that recently purchased a headset, it still lacks in a few places that I think should be looked into for future updates.

Here's just a quick list of somethings that didn't set well with me:

-Using literally anything other than a knuckle controller (Okay so I know that was what this game was more focused on, BUT I MIGHT PUT MY HEAD THROUGH A DRYWALL. Using a VIVE wand in this game is so incredibly uncomfortable and difficult, I'm not gonna ask for much here, just make the controls a tad more friendly so people without knuckles can use it without killing their hands.)

-The environments can feel big and confusing (This isn't too bad of a problem when you get more into the movement, but when you have to do strange and awkward movements over and over again just to figure out where you need to be it begins to become a bit of a fun killer for me, I really like fighting the enemies in the game, so it's kinda boring when I'm in a big quiet area with no one to fight. Honestly just like a waypoint or something to mark a objective would do wonders so I can at least get a idea on where I'm going.)

-Not having access to the other gamemodes at the start (This is a bit of a smaller grievance because while I dislike not having the ability to play these modes from the start I can understand and respect why you'd do this and the way you have to find them is kinda interesting too. But I would like it if you could get sandbox from the start, It's nice to have a mode to have some mindless fun in and play around with the physics of the game.)

I find myself climbing puzzles 3-4 times before I either end up quitting or magically teleport onto a ledge. For me, it was simply a frustrating game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a good game, just not a great one.

Good:
The physics are great.
The puzzles are quite fun.
Going through the game itself is nice.
The beginning of the game is really fun, as you mess around with everything and whatnot.
Interacting with the world is by far the highlight of these physics, it's really fun. Being able to use a trash can to block bullets, or pushing over a shelf all feels amazing.

Bad:
Heavy weapons are too floppy. I'm supposed to be holding a RIGID gun, not an elastic tube.
The combat is either boring, annoying, or both (Excluding gun holograms).
Enemies never really pose a threat. The gun holograms are the only real threat in this game (so far)
Holding melee weapons is a nightmare with Vive Wands.
The level design is a bit lackluster. The best level imo was the start of the tower after central station.
Somethings are straight up lacking any textures. Yes, it's supposed to be a virtual OS, but surprise surprise it doesn't look good having just grey grid walls all over the place.
Unlocking stuff for sandbox is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.

Trying to break ammo boxes is often really annoying. Obviously irl they aren't made of glass, but in a game, they should be easy to break. If I slam them on the floor, they should break. Instead, they kinda 'float' with velocity to the ground and don't break. I've even dropped one from well over 15-20 meters and had it completely fine. Hitting the boxes to break them is also unsatisfying as they usually take 2 hits to break. Once again, irl they would take 2+ hits to destroy. But this is a GAME. I don't wanna just stand there slamming at 3 boxes for 30 seconds.

The "Physical Body" in game sucks. It does what it does, but since it doesn't match up to your actual body, it ends up glitching into walls and such even if your actual body doesn't. (Obviously). More importantly, if you're climbing, your "arms" will sometime go places your arms don't actually go, which can make you fall or get stuck. Not a good feature.

By far the worst aspect:
You literally can't save. You either finish the level or restart it.
When something in the game glitches &/ breaks, you have to do literally the whole level to that point again. You also lose all your weapons & ammo (currency) that you collected in the level. Who on earth greenlit this?

Now sure, there are many more bads than goods for this game, however It's still a good VR experience and just messing with everything is cool. Bashing open doors and whatnot is quite fun. I'm recommending the game because if a new player wanted to buy and play this, there's no way I could say not to, it's still a cool game. However as a pretty experienced VR player, the game both immerses and breaks my immersion. It's a mixed bag. I wish I could recommend it "maybe" instead of just "yes".\t\t\t\t\t
