\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will admit, I had my initial wave of gripes playing this game on my Rift S, but the game has gotten many improvements over its lifetime. I used to have an issue grabbing items in quick motion, which lead to shooting some enemies with finger guns (it didn't work very well). I'm happy to say that grabbing objects feels much nicer with my controllers than it previously felt. Gun play works well, but the closer combat took some getting used to. My virtual arms feel rather weak and clunky with certain melee weapons, but I deliver a satisfying blow now and then regardless. In all honesty, the updates are the only reason I came back. These updates brought improvements to my initial issues while fleshing out various aspects of the gameplay. Many details have changed with said updates, and although this isn't the perfect game, it's worth checking out for how different this experience is. The adventure with its occasional puzzle has an interesting - and sometimes wacky - feel which kept me on "the edge of my seat" to see the next surprise awaiting my arrival. The occasional secret is fun to unfold as well, adding a reason to explore possibilities that would typically be ignored. Even if none of that suited me, the physics entertained me in many situations. I nearly died of laughter over some of the quirky mistakes my enemies made both in and out of battle, though these enemies aren't always such pushovers. I haven't seen everything this game has to offer, but for the reasons described above, I'm able to have a far better experience thanks to the development team's persistence and hard work. Thank you dearly for improving this game, and I wish the best of luck on any future plans.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I look at this game analytically, I usually end up landing on about a 6/10 for this game. The story feels practically non-existent and I didn't really have any idea what was going on. I've looked at discussions about the story and people are talking characters and companies that I never knew about in the game. I understand that some people like stories where you really have to dig into the world to uncover the bigger picture and piece things together but the fact that I can play this game from start to finish and have absolutely no idea what the story even was is poor storytelling.

The melee combat is kind of fun but sharp objects particularly don't feel impactful. The enemies feel like they're made of butter and all my weapons just slide into them with zero effort (you can just stand still, hold out a sword in front of you and watch the enemy walk into it and impale themselves with no effort on your part). There's an argument to be made that this is done with the purpose of making the combat less finicky but after playing Saints & Sinners where both the sound design and force required to use sharp weapons makes them feel much more satisfying to use, going back to Boneworks really makes you notice how weak they can feel.

The movement can be janky at times, parts of your simulated body will just get caught on things in the environment causing you to get stuck for a moment. Or maybe your arms will phase through a wall, meaning you're just stood there trying to yank your virtual arms free until the game eventually grants your wish. I appreciate the effort that went into creating this simulated body and it feels great at times, but can also feel like more of a detriment at others.

Gunplay is where this game truly shines. Guns feel satisfying to shoot and reload and when combined with slow motion, this game becomes the prime VR candidate for living out your John Wick fantasies. You have three types of pistols (each with a different balance of magazine size to damage per bullet), an SMG and an assault rifle with different variants for things like holosights and laser sights. Sadly there are no shotguns which is a shame since they seem perfect for sending enemies flying with this game's advanced VR physics, so hopefully we could get that in a later patch.

But despite all that, I really think this game is greater than the sum of its parts. Despite the fact that I believe both Half-Life: Alyx and Saints & Sinners are better VR experiences and just better games, I always find myself coming back to Boneworks when I want my fix of fun VR combat. And although I do think this game suffers from a bit of innovation imperative, valuing new and interesting ideas above all else even when they don't work as well in practice as they sound on paper, I will absolutely respect this game for trying to push VR gaming forward as a medium because even when these kinds of games make missteps they are important for pushing the platform forward and encouraging other developers to experiment as well.\t\t\t\t\t
