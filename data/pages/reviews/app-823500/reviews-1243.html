\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is fun to play around with the world, interact with things.
But so far the gameplay has been lacking (to be fair, I only have 1.6h in it). Melee feels powerless, even if you smash a cinder block against their head with full force.

Gunplay seems unrealistic and gun manipulation is on bare minimum. Even Pavlov has more gun manipulation (magazine eject and bolt catch should be standard).

The physics are janky, but still fun to play around with. But it also seems weirdly unrealistic at times when the objects just swing after your hand or bounce just too perfectly.
It feels unpolished currently, but it definitely has potential if they fix the bugs like crashing on certain parts (not my own experience but seen in a video) and a controller suddenly not working anymore (still worked in the SteamVR overlay, so it wasn't the controller itself).
With the sparse checkpoints, it can be really frustrating to experience such things, even so, more checkpoints would also be nice, must not really be manual saves too, just more. Frustrating to spend multiple minutes running because you died to a turret you didn't see.

I'd say to follow it for now and come back in 2-3 months and see if they improved the physics / fixed the bugs.\t\t\t\t\t

I am very much enjoying the game. Players can play with whichever playstyle they prefer. Puzzles are a little too annoying, in my opinion, as it can take a considerable amount of time to execute the puzzle once you figured out exactly what to do. However, I very much enjoy finding and collecting ammo crates: Many of these crates are placed in hidden or out-of-reach places, which encourages the player to think creatively about how they might reach them. I really enjoy this part of the game, as they are fun to solve while the reward is small enough that disinterested players can ignore them.

If you own a VR headset, and don't have too many issues with sickness, this game is a must-have.


OLD REVIEW:
I want to enjoy the game, but I keep encountering a gamebreaking bug which prevents me from doing so.

I play with the vive wands.
The bug makes my hand get stuck stretched out in front of my face. It will follow the camera and the hand will turn with your controller, but it becomes unusable (no grabbing and no physics). Buttons such as movement and menu still work.
The only way I found to fix this was to reset the scene or load a new one, which is very frustrating particularly with puzzles in the way. Some puzzles are impossible to do unless you have both hands available. This bug happens very frequently (I was stuck in the tutorial for ages, and I can't get past the street level) and can happen with either hand. Not sure if this is vive related or game related.

This video kinda shows the issue with the hand, although for me I was not able to pick up any objects at all.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8auKiGZSUQQ

A fix would be to allow a reload of the character model maybe?

I will change this review once the bug has been fixed.\t\t\t\t\t
