\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished the game and I really enjoyed it. The game was really fun and great but there were some flaws. Lets start with the positive, the games physics engine is very impressive. Sometimes it can be glitchy but overall it is an amazing engine. The gameplay is really enjoyable and I love the hand to hand combat. The guns feel great to shoot like really good. I thought the soundtrack was amazing and add to the game. Now lets go over some issues. One walking is not great. I think this is a problem with vr overall but sometimes the walking just doesn't feel right. Like for example sometimes you will have to jump over bodies because you cant walk over them which feels unnatraul. Some of the puzzles were really cool and used the physics enviorment in a really cool way. Others were mediocre at best and awful at worst. The game doesn't give really good hints so some of the puzzles are just you trying out things and praying they work which isn't fun and is bad level design. The game has some bugs like my hands clipping into objects so I have to turn off the controller to get them out. Glitches like these really take you out of the immersion that comes with vr games like these. My biggest issue with this game is the parkour sections. Its a vr game so jumping is always gonna be akward, thats why most vr games don't have jumping sections. BUT ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ this game for some reason has two or three jumping sections that made me want to stop playing the game. One example i'm gonna use is towards the end of the game were you have to move a heavy box across 4 platforms. This part took me 30 minutes until I gave up and just piled a bunch of lighter things on the button that was suppose to be helt down (the fact that you can do this is actually really cool but still WHY would you have this parkour section). My final complaint with this game is the story. Not to go to much into detail but it leaves a little bit to be desired but you aren't really playing the game for the story so thats ok.
VERDICT: I really had a good time with this game. The physics world is amazing and the soundtrack is spectacular (like who ever composed this ♥♥♥♥ needs a raise). But the game is buggy and some puzzles and jumping sections are not enjoyable or fun at all. There really is no other gaming expirence like Boneworks and I really respect Brandon and From Software for pushing the limits of vr games. I would say this is probably one of the best vr games and a most own for any vr users. 30 dollars might seem like a lot but the sandbox and arena modes will keep you coming back.
8/10 would play again\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts easy and hard to recommend this game so ill give it a go...

PROS (why i bought the game)
-the physics, needs no introduction
-half life vibe
-guns
-the tutorial is amazing
and uh, yeah, its a cool game
-immersion

CONS (like these are the things that made me refund, not nitpicking)

-everything scales down to your size and im a midget and like everything to be normaL size and not cater for me.
this means the enemies look like midgets and only give me the satisfaction that i just killed a child :(.
- the physics problems.....first one is like "omfg this is so fun" then the rest are like "ffs can i just kill some midget nullmen now".
-it registers me as left handed so i cant swing a right handed bat around as it wont let the leading hand well, lead.
-i cant f***ing use SLO-MO while moving on the oculus touch controllers so i never use it unless shooting, and even then....
-i own an i5 9400f and it says its not recommended, which is sad...but evident as i lag when approaching high physics areas. and trust me the rift s only has 80hz so when i drop its the equivalent of a stroke.

EDIT: i bought the game again and now i'm enjoying it much more, to fix these issues i first downloaded a binding on steam for press to slo- mo. which is absolutely amazing.
NEXT: I changed my height from 1.55m to 1.65m and although my character is bending down, the enemies and everything are a lot bigger.
LASTLY: The fact that physic puzzles were chores was kind of a misconception as after i passed that one devious puzzle everything was a lot more immersive and more fun. so goes to show just to soldier on. :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLook, straight from the beginning here, this is the best VR game I have ever played hands down. This game establishes a high, high bar for future VR titles that I hope to see challenged. Do yourself a favor and figure out a way you can play this game, it is truly an experience worth chasing. I do hold some gripes, though minor, but they should be mentioned. First of all, this game is EXTREMELY demanding on hardware. The first quarter of the game runs pretty much flawlessly, but the later levels get to a level of complexity that my Ryzen 2600 + 1080 TI + Index combo dropped frames down to the 30s. These sections were VERY nauseating and I beat them as quickly as possible to get out of that mess. Some levels, ESPECIALLY the sewer level (honestly, why do devs still create these?) are simple run and gun affairs that don't feel great. Don't get me wrong, shooting in this game feels excellent, but most other levels offer much more variety.\t\t\t\t\t
