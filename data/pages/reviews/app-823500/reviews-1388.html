\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat game on Vive Pro wireless with index controllers. The environment is a blast so far, I've only made it to the second level of the training area. The level of detail and physics are really good, coupled with all sorts of tasks and possible body movements this game is A+. Very impressed so far, will be playing more soon and will update.

Game also seems very well optimised. On my rig I got 88.9 average FPS in the first 45 min or so, so far.

Going to try some SS, game was running at 50-60% GPU use on default settings. The shadows defaulted to medium for me and I left it, there is a note beside this setting that says not all environments use dynamic shadows.

The only bummer for me is no real left handed option, though for me it isn't a deal breaker but I would love to use locomotion on the left stick. I just received my index controllers yesterday, this is the perfect game for them.

i7 6950x OC 4.4Ghz
32GB DDR4 3200Mhz
1080Ti OC

Not what I had hoped...

Starting off on Day 1 without support for WMR controllers was the first issue I had with the game, this was a huge let down after following the development for so long. I had such high hopes, but they were quickly dashed with that.

I saw that they realized that there actually ARE people who use WMR (According to the Steam Survey, more people use WMR than use the Index, last I checked at least) and decided to try to fix the issue. The fact that they tried their best to fix the issue once they realized there was an issue does mean something. That something, however, is not enough. Going through the tutorial world and getting to the zone that showed all the amazing controllers that the in-game company used was wonderful!... Except for that fact that it showed Index, Vive, and Rift controllers, cementing the fact that they forgot about WMR totally until after launch.

Once they fixed the issue, I changed my review to a positive one to show that I wasn't just trying to review bomb or some crap, giving them the benefit of the doubt.

Then I got into the game.

Horrible loading times aside, it seemed to be pretty decent. I saw something and went to pick up the item. My hand bent backwards in one of the most immersive breaking ways possible. That's saying something considering my time in VRChat.

The IK issues didn't stop there, if you try to reach for something, there is a good chance your arm will get stuck inside of the item, or door, or whatever it may be. For a product with such good hitboxes, this was another major let down.

After 2 hours of 'playing' (with a decent bit sitting outside of the headset to rest due to my own motion sickness (Which I can not blame them for, many people play it just fine, so that is just a personal issue of my own, not an issue from them) since I couldn't close the game unless I wanted to restart the zone as saving is not yet added) I finally got done with the tutorial and was ready to start the game and play in the first level; The Streets!

I went to the loading zone and... Steam Crashed, bringing the game with it.

Well, nothing is perfect, let's try again, annnnd... the game crashed. This time by itself, Steam was still going along strong.

I decided on waiting a day to give my head some time to rest from the sickness. I tried twice to load the level up today; both times resulted in the game loading on my Desktop monitor, but just showing a black screen in my headset. The controls worked fine, it's just... I couldn't see. Anything.

Once this game becomes actually playable, I will give it another fair chance to be what they promised. Until then, I can not recommend this product.

I want this to do well, but I simply can not bring myself to like it...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately the game is specifically designed for the index controllers. I use the Vive wands and these have grip buttons on the sides for grabbing. The game contains an enormous amount of grabbing objects (and specifically holding them) and this takes an incredible toll on your hands. If only they would have made some sort of grab toggle option.

I understand that the game was designed as part of a tech demo for the relatively new index controllers and they want to incentivize people to buy them. But you are leaving behind the majority of vr players. The vive wand is still the most used controller and there definitely should be better support for that.

I do NOT RECOMMEND the game if you use the VIVE WANDS as controllers!

Edit: Also the controllers vibration mapping is completely off. Every time you so as touch the wall, the vibrations go haywire, making very loud sounds. I'm worried my controllers are gonna break :(.

Edit: For the people saying it's no a tech demo and Valve is not involved;
- Headcrabs
- Big boxes on buttons
- Gravity gun
- Signature crowbar

Kinda sounds like Valve right? Now listen, I'm not saying that this is a bad thing. I absolutely love Valve and Brandon is frikkin amazing. The game is the first that features full use and possibilities of the new knuckles controllers and thats absolutely awesome. I'm just saying that for the people that do not wanna spend another 350eu for a second pair of controllers that can be fully used in literally one game, should also have the possibility to play this game.

Please Brandon, if you're listening, improve vive wand support!!!\t\t\t\t\t
