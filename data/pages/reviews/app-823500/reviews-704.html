\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis isn't a general "I don't think anyone will like this" review, but more of a "I didn't like this and if you're like me, this is why you might not like it."

I suspect that, for the time of its release, Boneworks was a really solid game and a pioneer on many fronts. There was clearly a lot of innovation and love put into this game, including many things I wish more recently release games had.

However, as someone who jumped onto the VR train a bit later, I just couldn't get into it. The core gameplay is fine, but you're constantly battling against the game in odd ways. Mounting is a struggle and asked of you constantly, you'll lose grip on objects while climbing randomly, your arms will get stuck in objects or between poles with no feedback to let you know what's happening. It's... very janky; I think I just prefer a well polished experience with less to offer when it comes to VR.

It's a cool idea, and when it works it can be nice, but it just wasn't for me and I ended up dropping it before finishing it. I suspect for players who really enjoy a good sandbox game where they can mess around, Boneworks will be right up their alley, but given the hype surrounding this game I was surprised by how disappointed by it I ended up being.

It's probably worth a try for VR enthusiasts but if it doesn't click right away, it probably won't more than a few hours in.
