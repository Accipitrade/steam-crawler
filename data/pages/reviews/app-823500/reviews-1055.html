The Golden Standard that VR games should aim for!

Definitely sets the bar high for VR games to come and is a next gen VR experience.

If you've ever played the Half Life series or Gmod and wondered in your silly life "Huh whats it like on VR?" this is it my friends.

(With Half Life: Alyx around the corner this game still has alot to offer)

Physics are phenomenal and combat is both satisfying and immersive and the bones just work.

+Puzzles are uniquely crafted. Theres a lot to do/use in the sandbox like environments/levels.

+There are various weapons and enemies you encounter.

+And to finally being able to slap your foes booty and shove those fools into a locker is very gratifying.

+Vastly interact with objects and your environment to solve puzzles with however way you see fit!


The Sand Box and Arena levels are both so fun, if you want to just hop in and mess around (granted you unlock the stuff in the main story)

-Downside would be mid-level saves (this has been addressed and will be implemented late January)


Overall, give this game a go. Fun awaits you.

Thanks guys @ Stress Level Zero and Node

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn it's current state, I wouldn't recommend anyone buy this game.

Most issues with it would be excusable, if not for the lack of any sort of save system beyond completing levels. There is no excuse for being unable to save and quit in a modern game, especially one trying to prove to itself that it isn't a tech demo.

The game is very janky, what with being a physics based VR game. When it works, it's amazing. However, it doesn't always work. Climbing is slightly ridiculous, with your body flying all over the place.

It's possible to clip your hands through objects and walls, and it happens very often. Often, you can manage to extricate yourself by moving your hand until you find a gap in the geometry, or by destroying the object it's stuck in, but you too often need to just kill yourself and restart the level. If saving were an option it wouldn't be such a big issue.

The levels are often too big to complete in under an hour, unless you already know the level and speed through without taking time to explore, and so I can only play when I have at least a couple of hours spare. If saving were an option it wouldn't be such a big issue.

If you ever lose tracking, which unfortunately isn't an uncommon occurrence with current VR, Boneworks is prone to flinging you through walls, either getting you inescapably and nauseatingly stuck, or you end up plummeting to your death outside of the level geometry. Falling to your death this way starts you at the beginning of the level with all of your progress reset, after a minute or two of falling. If saving were an option it wouldn't be such a big issue.

I am currently unable to finish one level, as something invariably happens for me to clip outside of the level geometry and lose an hour of progress. It has been enjoyable, but everything it does other games do better (Blade and Sorcery, Pavlov, H3VR). It would be worth the £20 for actually having a campaign and a story, unlike many other VR games, if only it had something so basic as saving.

Edit: The save-point update has essentially fixed all the problems I had. Some of the physics puzzles can still feel frustrating but that's to be expected, and the frustration is massively reduced now they don't have to repeated so often. Heartily recommend now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game starts really slow to roll out VR mechanics in a way that tries not to be overwhelming, a couple of 1 on 1 melee fights and some very tedious physics puzzles. However the game really hits its stride around the sewers level, where you're given some fun guns to play with (love that orange pistol) and a claustrophobic pipe crawl with enemies falling on you, sneaking up behind you, and giving you lots of fun combat situations that involve juggling your flashlight and weapons. The game is unapologetic about nursing people who are easily motion sick, they have you jumping off of large drops, climbing sheer heights, even wearing a VR headset while in a VR game. This bold, boundary pushing style of design is where this game gets my respect. People are going to look back on this game as the Mario 64 of VR. So many mechanics are genius, my favorite being the right analog stick being used for crouching, making seated play feel as if you aren't missing anything. This game has influenced half life Alyx, which has incorporated the crouch mechanic as well as adding smooth locomotion in response to the games tongue in cheek diss on teleport movement. Definitely a VR title to pick up. However, I'd recommend trying some more "primitive" VR games before playing this. Partly because after playing this they are going to be hitting different, but also as a way to appreciate the progress they've made for VR with their high risk ideas.\t\t\t\t\t
