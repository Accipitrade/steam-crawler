They're great, and should be a gold standard for what VR games should be trying to achieve. They can be finicky at times, but that's the result of being the only VR game in its time to even try and apply physics to everything. There's a physicality that Boneworks manages to achieve that no other VR experience does so far, and developers still don't seem to even be trying to reach this level of interactivity.

Supposedly the story has a lot of little secrets and implied lore, but it's honestly hard to follow and the setting feels oddly uninspired despite "game that looks like it's being built" being an interesting idea. It definitely won't catch your eye as a narrative, but it's cute and silly a lot of the time and sometimes that's enough.

Great sandbox game, and a great game to just spend a bit of time beating stuff up in. The interactivity is still top-notch with virtually no competitors.
Also, remember to steal all the weapons from the museum.\t\t\t\t\t
