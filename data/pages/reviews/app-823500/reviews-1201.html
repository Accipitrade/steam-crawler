\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay so. Just finished the main campaign of the game. I can say this is some of the most consistent fun I've had playing a VR game. The physics is amazing, the gunplay feels solid, the story is decent for a small studio, especially considering they spent a _lot_ more effort on the mechanics than the story. Every time I got into a loop of using the same gun or weapon, all I had to do was put that one away and use something new, and bam, brand new ways to approach each level.

Takeaways:
Physics driven interaction is the future of VR platforming and gun/sword play. I can't see myself enjoying teleport or clipping heavy games nearly as much now, especially those that try to emulate the Half Life style of gunplay and platforming. The time dilation mechanic is also very useful if you're having trouble with timing in a puzzle or fight.

The game could use some extra optimization on the later levels, I only had worse framerate right up until the very last level, and that one was only okay because it was small.

All in all, best VR game I've played yet, and a mighty symbol of what's to come.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tafter playing through the game a few times and getting a good portion of collectibles, I have come to the conclusion that this game is pretty good, but has some flaws. I played this game day one, before HL:A came out, but have played more of it recently after a year of updates and it really has improved a lot since it came out.

but thats not important. the question is SHOULD YOU BUY THIS GAME? yeah. but only if you have a good amount of hours in VR and please do not get this game if you have motion sickness. this game will rip you apart if you have motion sickness. the campaign is pretty good for what it is, but lacks in feeling unique and different. it doesnt feel like you are going through a real place or at least some kind of world like most games, it just feels like a bunch of connected rooms that just so happen to be where the player goes. the physics are pretty good but are a double edged sword, as they can get in the way sometimes. however i do enjoy the physics more often than not, so it is not a big problem in the game. enemy variety is pretty poor looking back. there are really just four enemies in the entire game. there are the null bodies that can also be zombies that have more health but are basically the same. theres the zombie that can shoot this weird light ball thing, there are the crablets, which are pretty fun to fight, especially with a melee weapon. the clean up crew are pretty lackluster to say the least. i do not find much enjoyment out of fighting them, especially in melee runs. i feel like theres a lot of missed opportunities for cool gameplay or just being able to get rid of the more cumbersome parts of the game, mainly the earlier physics puzzles. theres so much that they missed out on, for example the trash cleaner is used in the first level once for a twenty second "puzzle" where you walk on the sidewalk and nothing happens. the weighted cube on button thing is only used three times in the entire game, and the last time is actually the worst thing in the entire game ever. story literally does not exist and you need to read external stuff to understand whats actually going on.

TL;DR, its a nice game 8.5/10 could do better but overall pretty good. highly recommend you get it if you have VR and can handle the physics\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCan't get enough of playing this! I am pretty well accustomed to VR but the intensity of the motion was enough to make me feel a little uncomfortable the first day. After that I have had no issues, with one exception; my body is honestly aching from playing so much, but I kind of think that's great!

I've played a fair number of different VR games which offer different types of locomotion, interaction, shooting etc. and I've been pleased with the execution in Boneworks. First of all they adeptly hit a baseline of comfort and functionality, such as acceleration and snap turn. After that there are some differences which require getting used to, like the feel of jumping and vaulting, or using grab exclusively for weapon handling (no button to release a mag or release bolt/slide as far as I can tell). The weapon handling in general is good, I'd say somewhere between Onward (IMO a little cumbersome and awkward) and Pavlov (my current favorite, IMO the most consistent and functional, though a little more arcade-y). I think it depends on the person's expectations and which controllers they're using, so what can you do.

Physical collisions, grabbing objects, etc. can be somewhat unpredictable and wobbly, as you would expect, however; it would be pretty unfair to expect anyone to overcome such issues which are typical in most games, in VR no less. One thing I did not have high hopes for was melee, I've always felt that swinging objects around at enemies is a lost cause in VR, but I've actually been somewhat impressed. It's still pretty chaotic and floaty, and I tend to smash my controllers together a lot, but it is surprisingly satisfying to haphazardly grapple with cyber zombies and then land a big hit approximately where you were aiming.

Visually, the game is kind of all over the place, but it's actually sort of contextualized in an interesting way by establishing the setting as a sort of meta virtual world, and then mixing textures and materials ranging from realistic, to pixelated, to graybox (flat grid styled textures). Some areas can be a little jarring at times, like excessively dark areas punctuated by blinding lights or neon graffiti. It's worth adding a caveat that my PC barely meets the min reqs so my settings are all at absolute minimum.

On that note, performance has been mostly good with a few areas causing poor but manageable perf, up until recently reaching a large level which is almost unplayable. It's possible I'll have to upgrade before being able to proceed comfortably. If you are at or near min spec, you can still expect hours of stable gameplay.

The audio has been great, it's one of those things that is exceptionally important in VR, because it accommodates for the lack of physical feedback. I don't know what kind of work went into creating sound sources which are spatial or positional or whatever, but i can easily tell where a noise came from with the Index headphones. I usually play with music turned down a lot, but from what I heard it sounded great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust the list of pros and cons that come to my mind right away

Pros
- Items actually have proper physics and weight
- Not (much) clipping

Cons
- Main trigger is the "Grip" button for holstered items on Rift S controllers (Rift S controllers already have a dedicated Grip Trigger)
- Only Oculus Touch controls are listed in the controller showcase (sure, it's similar to the Rift S, but quite confusing at first notice)
- "Story" mode seems to be just a prolonged tutorial which...
- ...keeps you locked out of Arena and Sandbox on the very first playthrough for... some reason. Still don't know how to unlock it as of yet
- "Nullbody" enemies don't seem to react to some things (trash can lid, getting shoved with both hands only earns the player more damage)
- Some loading times feel to be about 3-5 minutes
- Checkpoints are very barren, thus having to run through the same section over and over after dying
- No manual save points
- Some items seem a bit too heavy (wooden baseball bat being held with two hands seemed a bit less heavier compared to the sledgehammer)

Personally, I'm going to hold off on playing and refund. Going to wait a few updates come out and hopefully some of these things are addressed. It's not a terrible game by any means, this is a great step for VR game standards, but it just seems like it needs a bit more polish in some areas.\t\t\t\t\t

Why Boneworks made my first 10/10

When I first played Boneworks I took it for granted. Then again, I agree with almost everything that I said in my first review, although things like combat with blunt weapons and optimization have been greatly improved. Even then I still thought it was the best VR game at the time, but then I thought Half Life: Alyx would make me ditch the game.

Then Half Life: Alyx comes around. I played it. I enjoyed it. But there was always something missing when I was playing. The story was nice and the gunplay was fun, but the environments around me felt static. I could pick things up and play with them, like using a marker on a pane of glass, but I couldn't climb, run, shove enemies down, or even hit them with a crowbar. In Boneworks, you could do all those things as long as you were okay with the jank that came with it.

I still consider Half Life: Alyx to be the best VR game out there for everyone, but it wasn't the best game for me. I wanted something more. I wanted to do more. Boneworks was there to give me more. Half Life: Alyx only contained a static landscape that was limited in exploration, while Boneworks lets you break the game for fun if you tried hard enough. By sacrificing polish and comfort, Boneworks gave you freedom.

Its not perfect. I would say Half Life: Alyx is closer to perfect. But the experience that Boneworks provides you cannot find in any VR game out there.

10/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS is a must have for any VR player, It is a fantastic game with smooth game play and mechanics not seen in other tittles. It is a super smooth game but there are a couple of problems. Sometimes you simply get stuck and lose immersion after you run into a wall to fast and clip your hands through it, and once in a while I encountered some bugs that made levels unplayable. A couple of times I had to restart a level because buttons and keys stopped working. This would result in me over complicating puzzles and giving up, then coming back the next day only for the puzzle to work how it was intended to. The same goes with keys, I restarted a couple levels because Keys would not work. These bugs will hopefully be patched soon. My only other complaint is level design. There are so many corners and turns that most of the time you are tangled up like your apple earbuds. I wish that the levels had been designed more so that this would be prevented. Every right turn, there is an opposite turn up ahead so you don't get tangled. Besides that, This game is amazing and I strongly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
