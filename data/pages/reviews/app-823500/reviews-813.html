\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe short review:
As one of my first VR experiences, I can say I got extremely spoiled by it. You can interact with almost everything in a physical way, which is missing in many VR games. It feels way more immersive but even more just fun to be able to stick your hand into something and instead of it clipping through the object it stops and if you use enough force you can push yourself away from things just by pushing them. I have very good tolerance for motion sickness, so that wasn't a problem for me, altough I don't recommend it for your first VR experience (learned that the hard way). The things to love about this game is its's physics, engine, physics engine, sandbox, enemies, weapons (melee and ranged) and movement (and mods but that isn't something you get in the game by itself).

The long review where I ramble about the issues:
This is an amazing game, but I really had to do a big decision between recommending and not recommending, because here:
It is awesome. I have my headset for like what, 2 weeks now and 80% of what Im playing up to this point is Boneworks. I know that the game is recommended for people who have some VR experience, but heck it. The physics, movement and just all these things are very fun and great, I can spend an hour in sandbox mode by just impaling enemies on a claymore or smacking the out of them with a bat, but heres the thing, the sandbox.

The story mode of the game, is not the best. The levels, yeah sure, theyre ok, I like the combat scenarios in them but I just dont like the puzzles sometimes, I just stand there looking at the giant obviously physics based puzzle with no clue what to do, and only ideas on how to cheese the puzzles, which ultimately leads me to just watch a walkthrough (tho this was more of a problem in the first levels of the game where there were more puzzles), but my biggest problem is the sandbox, and how it connects with the story mode.

To unlock things in sandbox, you have to find them in story mode and reclaim them in these reclamation bins which are in each level about once (every time you go to a next level your items disappear), and that is actually kind of ok for collecting weapons scattered throughout the map, which is fun to find them all and later save them, but you can also unlock props (basically things like crates, blocks etc.) which you dont just throw into the bin, but you have to find these small balls that contain a model of the prop, which when you throw in the reclamation bin you can use in the sandbox. This at the beggining caused a lot of confusion for me, as I didnt know you have to find the balls with the props in them instead of just throwing the props themselves in the bin, so there were many moments where I dragged an entire table to the end of the level only to see that I cant save it.

The thing is that the prop balls are often hidden in very hard-to-get areas and you cant simply get them all unlocked at the end of story mode without searching up a few of their locations online at least once, since theres always some in a level youll never find on your own, unless you like spending 3 hours on a level. There is also no way of finding out if you got all collectibles in a level, so you have to cope with often outdated online guides and your already collected things to extrapolate which ones are missing.

This prop ball idea also applies to NPCs and enemies, where you have to find these pills with the NPC inside of them at again hard-to-reach or just very rudely hidden at the top of the map, except that theyre way rarer than props and they spawn in these Boneworks crates, which is just a way to signify that something valuable is in the crate (other wooden crates dont contain anything). There are many NPCs which youll never find unless youre very eagle-eyed (which not many people are when youre supposed to be having fun beating enemies) or look into every nook and cranny of a level or follow a tutorial.

This always really makes me mad, especially after playing a level for 2 hours, finding every collectible only to look at an online guide to see theres another one I missed, so I have to replay the whole dang level with every puzzle and enemy just to find that 1 thing I missed to mess around with in sandbox mode. Theres also a display that shows how many stuff youve collected and how many youre missing, which isnt all that useful, only makes me more mad when I look at it to see how much stuff Im missing.

And one of the worst parts? Ammo. In this game, you can find ammo in a lot of locations, and youll have plenty most of the time, but you see, ammo is also currency in this game. You can buy some stuff with it in these vending machines, which are fun with reasonable prices. But theres this one vending machine at the end with very powerful and fun sandbox tools, like a spawn gun, noclip gun or super-punch weapon, which cost ridiculous ammounts of ammo, and to get them you have to go through each level finding at least 90% of all ammo boxes in that level (which are sometimes very hidden), not use the ammo (only if you really have no other option) and just keep it with you while using melee weapons all the way to the end to spend it on a very fun tool you should have gotten in sandbox for free after completing the story mode in my opinion, which is already quite challenging at its own. I know that the mechanics are there for extra replay value, which would be fine if they just toned it down a little, and that also the tools are quite OP, but I think you should get them after finishing the story without going back to grind finding ammo.

Summary: So yeah, as I said, the weapons, the way they work, the movement, the physics, the enemies are all fun and great, which just guarantee a recommendation on their own, but I just seriously, and I mean really hate the way you get sandbox stuff. Im not a person that likes grinding in games. Overall I would give it a 7,5/10 which would be 9,5/10 if it werent for some horrible level and progression design decisions.

What can you take away from it? Buy it. Its a great game to have some fun in, and see what kind of technology and experiences can VR bring us, but if youre either a completionist or just want to enjoy the game to the max, prepare for some boring grinding for collectibles and ammo.\t\t\t\t\t
