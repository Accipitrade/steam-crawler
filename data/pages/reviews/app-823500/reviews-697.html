\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't get all the hype about this game, because it feels (at least nowadays) way behind other realistic VR game.

First, it is the first time I have motion sickness in a VR game. The way the camera move and the way you interact with the environment feel pretty bad.

Then, the guns feel pretty cheap and unrealistic. I have 100 times better experience when shooting with a game like Onward or Half Life Alyx.
Similarly, fighting with melee weapons is quite disturbing. The enemies move weirdly, you don't always understand the impact (or absence of impact) of your weapon, and the whole thing is unnatural. I have 100 times better experience with a game like Blade & Sorcery.

Finally, the lack of checkpoints in the main game is pretty annoying, especially when going through platforms seem to be the main goal...

I'll try again this game to be sure that I am not mistaken, but for now, it feels like it lagging behind its ambition and other VR games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSuper underrated, fantastic use of physics in VR and a wild ride of a story mode.

For newcomers to VR be cautioned: this game can make you feel nauseous at first as it uses full locomotion - your characters walks without teleport, you grab all of the things and you climb heights.
After the sickness though you are treated to an intriguing world, clearly inspired by Valve games like Half Life 2 (crowbar included) with a surprisingly detailed story and world.

Best part of this game is the satisfaction of using the vast arsenal of weapons - an incredibly immersive experience and a tonne of fun.

Low part is it isn't as aesthetically pleasing compared to some other huge VR games (ie Alyx) though its a great game nonetheless and my favourite VR experience. Story clocked in for about 8 hours for me - with more entertainment to be had in the sandbox and additional modes.\t\t\t\t\t
