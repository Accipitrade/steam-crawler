\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the best VR game I've played since buying my Vive at its launch over 3 years ago.


The Half Life and Portal inspirations are very apparent both in aesthetics and gameplay. Others have said it but its worth repeating: This is the new standard for VR games. Not being able to interact with most objects in the world like in older VR games feels so dated now. I'm only about 7 hours or so in, and still haven't finished the main storyline. Apparently there;s a sandbox mode, arena mode, hidden developer tools to unlock, and hidden collectibles to go back and find in the campaign for replayability. I imagine I'll find many more hours of fun before calling this one done.


It's not perfect though:
My biggest complaint is the performance issues (system specs below). This is probably the most demanding built-for-VR game available. The adaptive resolution is a nice compromise between frame rate and visual clarity, but it gets so bad that iron sights and various optics become so blurry they're useless. This only happens to me at certain points and is well reported at this point so I'm hopeful Stress Level Zero can make some optimizations soon.


The only other complaint I have is how wobbly climbing is. For such an important mechanic in the game, it can be really disorienting here. I've had my headset for years now and am pretty much immune to VR sickness, but those who are more susceptible to to it are gonna have a bad time. The warning at the beginning of the game that this is a game for expert VR users is no joke. I know the mantra for the game is everything must be physically simulated, but I wish the climbing could be smoothed out some. Hotdogs, Horseshoes, and Hand Grenades has a pretty good climbing mechanic, although its like the opposite of Boneworks in that the player is weightless and has 100% control of how they move once anchored to a point. I think the ultimate climbing experience would be somewhere in between these two games.


Despite these issues I can't recommend this game enough! It's worth every penny!


System Specs:
- HTC Vive original with Wireless Adapter
- i5-7600k
- GTX 1070
- 16GB RAM
- SATA SSD\t\t\t\t\t
