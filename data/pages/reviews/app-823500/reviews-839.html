\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has some good core gameplay. But to be honest, that's where it kind of ends. It has no cohesion between levels, the art style depresses me, and after the 20th zombie I just get a bit tired of killing these textureless creatures. That's not to say I wish I never bought this game. It has its fun moments, and I'll take what I can get for vr games since there's not too many high budget ones. But go in with an open mind and low expectations. I gotta specifically call out climbing though. I hate it. Every time I see a vertical puzzle I have to sit on the floor for a moment and prepare myself mentally for the slog that is climbing. It feels janky as hell, my camera is always flying all over the place, I glitch into the pipe/wall, and it's incredibly slow. God forbid you grip at the wrong moment and you fall to the bottom. Ladders in this game give me nightmares, which is a shame since many of the physics 'puzzles' have them.


EDIT: I decided to finish the game. Wow, it's like they customized the game to make it the most painful for you as possible. My comments about the climbing? They quadrupled down on it for the final level. You literally have to climb up not 1, but 2 buildings! Then right after you climb up the second building (which literally took me 10 minutes because I fell off a single time), the game just ends. Credits. Wow.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSUMMARY: Recommended if motion sickness is not an issue for you.

My first VR title was Alyx and I really liked it. I read some reviews and saw Boneworks as the next logical choice. Boneworks definitely has more interaction. I really liked that I could walk up to a low wall, jump, grab and then pull myself up and over. It actually worked quite well. Recoil when firing a weapon with one hand is worse than if you used two, like it is in reality. Interacting with objects, even when they have no real significance to completing the game, just adds to the game experience. Boneworks offers plenty of this.

There are a couple of things I do not like, one not such a big deal, but the other is quite significant depending on the player.

1. Swapping magazines in weapons. Alyx used a button on the controller to eject a mag. This felt natural because that is how it actually works. In Boneworks, unless there is another way, you motion like you are inserting a new mag and it automatically ejects the spent mag. I did not like this. Not a big deal but its the small details that add up.

2. Walking / Movement. This is a big issue for me. I was able to play Alyx for an hour, maybe even more, due to the teleport movement method. In Boneworks you travel every nauseating step. It is difficult for me to play more than 15 to 20 minutes, depending on how much I move around. I have 2 hours into this game after owning it for a few days. I had twice that in the first day of owning Alyx.

If it were not for the lack of having the option to move around like you do in Alyx, I would not have any issues with this game.
