\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI see a lot of positive reviews for this game and I I wish I could be as happy with this game as others. This is, afaik, the first refund I ever requested on steam. I will write out exactly why I gave it a thumbs down, this was a personal decision and my not apply to others. This will be lengthy because I want to point out exactly why I didn't like it when others clearly did.

There's plenty good in the game, so let me start with thas. It has a great, half life 2-like atmosphere. At first, I was surprised at the environments and how the gameplay was, it's mysterious and has a sense of impending doom hanging in the air, even though you seem to be wandering around what seems to be a deserted area. I like the puzzles and how the devs use the physics in the game to think outside conventional gameplay mechanics. I like the realistic gun play which I experienced during the first few "chambers" which double as a tutorial. The physical body you are dragging along interacts with the world, and this does give a sense of real-ness to the experience.

This experimental physics part of the game is to be the big selling point. And here's where things go sour for me.

The first few "chambers" - I wouldn't call them missions per se - are somewhat ok. The physics based puzzles take a bit of time to figure out, but the way your body acts in the game is really offputting for me.

I get the constant feeling that my character has arms made of jelly.

It was really weird when swinging hammer for the first time. When hitting something, the hammer hits the surface ok but when swinging back, I see my wrist folding as if it has no strenght at all. Compare it to having your hand attached to your arm with a single spring instead of tendons and muscle. When you stop your movement, the hand keeps going back and forth in a very weird way.

When handling heavy objects, the same thing occurs. Often you need to drag heavy objects, and when you grab a heavy object to drag it away, it suddenly seems very light as it bounces left and right, but your arm and wrist flop in all directions as if they were not made of bone and muscle at all. This makes "steering" the dragged object, well, a real drag. The fact that there is no option in the game to just move left, right and forward using the left stick, but only move where your headset is facing and move in the direction of your controller, makes it additionally hard to move around with a heavy object in tow when solving puzzles.

Some items seem impossibly heavy but can be lifted with relative ease. When grabbing one of these "lighter" items often times one hand starts turning in weird ways and screws up your handing of the object, or the hand suddenly lets go entirely. As your hands are not always in full view when playing, this can be frustrating.

You also interact with objects you stand on, especially when jumping. For some reason there seems to be an exceptionally large force exerted on items you jump from. At one point, a cube I was standing on, simply toppled over on its side when I jumped straight up. This seemed very odd to me and broke my immersion in the game entirely.

Handling objects doesn't seem to be consistent. When grabbing an oject in order to topple it or slide it sideways, you often can't get the object to move. let go of your grip and slap or even gently push the object, and suddenly it moves like you wanted it to.

At one point I had to handle a plank like object, and set it upright against a ledge. You'd think it would be fairly straight forward, if it weren't for the jelly like wrists I was talking about. My hands got stuck on the other side of the plank somehow, the plank started glitching against my body and between my arms, and when holding it still in front of me, it was toppling over and back, as if it was attached to two springs instead of my hands.

All of these combined, make it really hard for me to complete more than one puzzle in 30-45 minutes.

And to top it off, these puzzles are bundled in a large map which has to be played through in one go. There are no saves in this game. If you can't continue for any reason and close off the game, you have to start all over again from the start of the map. This really was the straw for me.

So here's my opinion on the game. Others may enjoy it, but I didn't, not really. It felt unfinished to me. The physics are too buggy to be enjoyed thouroughly. Take this as you will. If you enjoyed the game more than I did, then good! This was just for those that are in doubt if this game would be something they like.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLots of fun to play. I love that you can basically use the force to pull items to you and the melee/shooting gameplay feels solid, quite well done. I find I am actually able to shoot things at a distance in this game unlike most VR games with guns I've played, haha. You seem to be able to play somewhat stealthily if you want, or just run straight into battle if you prefer, and the different weapons actually have different damage values and methods to use. I do wish it was a little easier to move bodies around, considering the heavy crap you can throw around like the hulk otherwise, just so you can sort of roleplay infiltration a little better, hiding bodies, but you cant really lift them off the ground, mostly just drag, so you cant chuck them in dumpsters or anything like that.
There are some very very fun to use weapons. I found the balloon gun and there's lots of entertaining things you can do with that despite it not really being a weapon per se. I usually end up using melee though, and most of the time use trashcan lids like shields, which are very effective for the purpose. (and possibly what they are actually meant for, considering)
Other than that I do wish you could keep at least one weapon between levels. even if it was only a single melee weapon. Having to start from scratch every level (aside from ammo, which is the only thing you keep between levels, which also functions as a form of currency) does get a little old, but honestly doesn't detract from the game too much. It is just that I'd like to get my favorite weapon and be able to use it more often.

Also, It would be nice to have a mantling function. It is extremely difficult to climb on top of things and I feel like that would greatly improve the parkour in the game. Granted most things you would want to climb up on that don't have an overhanging climbable surface are probably places you shouldn't be, but still. I like freedom to explore, haha.

The most recent drivers for Vega 56 (at the time of writing this both the most recent and the one right before it, 19.12.2 and 19.12.3 both cause a sort of "double vision" effect, mostly with text, and a few textures, so if you have that card, go back to 19.12.1, which works perfectly with it. I don't know when they will actually fix that, but hopefully when 19.12.4 comes out it will be fixed. That isn't a problem with the game itself, however, but I just felt I should mention it as sort of a PSA for people with that card.

Overall I highly recommend this game if you enjoy VR games.\t\t\t\t\t
