\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks was an interesting case to me because I expected the fully modeled physics body to be a step forward for immersion and while I enjoyed the mechanic it ended up breaking immersion for me a lot more. There's a learning curve in piloting a physics body driven entirely by the position of your hands and head while it's colliding with in game objects and you're trying to mime out your actions with your controllers. It became a lot easier when I started thinking of the body as a complicated character controller more than my in game body, but once you figure out movement the liberties afforded by the control scheme make for really creative gameplay. If you're like me things are gonna feel clunky at first but as you learn to control your character more precisely you can get really expressive.

The story is progresses with very little dialogue or context. Stress Level Zero are very into cryptic stories and environments littered with clues. It's definitely going to be more appealing to people who like watching lore videos and making theories based on ripped assets. The game can get really atmospheric though and while I was sometimes confused about my character's motivations I always felt like I was progressing towards something.

I'm a pretty big fan of this game overall but it defied my expectations a little when it came out. I was expecting a less polished version of Half Life Alyx with more ambitious physics but it's really its own thing. There are a lot more climbing and physics problems and a lot of the deeper fun will come with exploration or experiments in the sandbox mode. If you're looking for games to play on your fancy VR headset though I think this is a staple.\t\t\t\t\t
