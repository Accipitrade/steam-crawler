I will recommend to avoid melee combat whenever you can, though. Some melee weapons have a nasty habit of getting stuck, most melee weapons have absolutely no "stopping power" and enemies will continue to advance on you if you don't manage to kill them outright, and trying to fight unarmed is kind of an exercise in frustration. A lot of melee weapons also can make your character's hands shift position when you don't want them to which can really screw up your spacing and swing arc. Stick with the guns whenever possible, trust me.

The story is... odd. It's barely there and gets very confusing at the end. The game has a distinct lack of enemy variety as well, which ends up making things rather repetitive - you will mostly be fighting the same three enemies. Zombies that walk towards you and try to punch you, cleanup bots that have rifles, and Not Headcrabs(TM). There is a variation on the zombies that can launch projectiles at you, but they are otherwise the same enemy.

But thankfully the sandbox-style levels and your ability to approach most of the game's levels freely does manage to keep things interesting. There's plenty to unlock for completionists as well, if that's your thing.\t\t\t\t\t
