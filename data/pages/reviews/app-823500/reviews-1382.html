\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far it's amazing. The full body does work pretty well, even though I am not a fan of full bodies in VR. Physics are awesome, and the gunplay is great. Only thing I don't like is that on Index you need to fully press in the trigger when gripping stuff which is kind of weird, because I guess I'm used to Pavlov. This game was one of the reasons I upgraded from my Vive to Index, and so far it's definitely worth it. Obviously a no brainer to buy if you own VR.

I did end up having to take a break though. I have hundreds and hundreds of hours in VR but I did get the feeling in my stomach where I need to take a break. Not full on nauseous or anything, just an unpleasant feeling. Saw people leave negative reviews because of that which is complete ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.

Another thing is the physics get buggy when you start stacking things, which is to be expected I guess. No doubt these are the BEST physics hands down for any VR game, but you really can't stack things in order to get over a wall. I tried doing that because I'm retarded and missed something obvious, so I doubt you ever need to actually do that.

I also saw reviews of people saying the game sucks with Vive wands, which is honestly unfortunate. I have high hopes for them updating that though because that's just plain not fair. Surprising that people seriously gripe about that stuff on DAY ONE, but I guess I would too if I still had my Vive.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom the little i've played, i've enjoyed it. Can't play for more than an hour before getting motion sickness. Only complaint is it doesn't play very well with the original vive controllers, seems built for index.

Edit 2 days post launch, just completed sewers:

About 6 hours into the game now, I struggle to play even an hour before getting motion sickness, so I try to play in small intervals. Some of the levels are really good, but quite long. I have run into the issue where I cannot complete levels in one sitting and there is no quick save, so if you leave the game you need to start that level over again. Streets was the worst for this, because it's a very long level with multiple puzzles and no quick save points. Took me about 3 times to try to complete it.

I find hand movement can be clunky, for example if youre close to a box or move able object, your hand will often bump the box, instead of clipping through it. Anytime my character falls, I need to close my eyes so I don't get sick as quickly. Hopefully the motion sickness goes away after I get used to the game.

Overall, I'm enjoying the game.\t\t\t\t\t
