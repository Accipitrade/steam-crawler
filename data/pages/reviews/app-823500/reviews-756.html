\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere to begin.. Boneworks is such a phenomenal example of a great VR game. It has puzzles, amazing visuals, and superb physics. Yes the game can be wonky and does have its moments where it can glitch out. However looking past the minor physics bugs this game brings me back to the wonder and awe I felt when I first played Portal and Half Life. The puzzles are mostly manageable with a few tougher ones; nothing too hard. I may have not played it too long due to time limitations but all the hours I have spent has been surreal.

After playing Boneworks I can't really play other VR games due to how complex the fidelity and physics are. If you think you are able to do something in this game 9/10 you can do it. Want to stack up a bunch of barrels and jump over the walls on the street level? You can do that. It is amazing how everything just kind of works and how easy it is to learn. It has a nice subtle way of telling you where to go and how to do things without holding your hand and being annoying with hints or tips. Sandbox mode is super fun to mess around with everything. Survival mode is surprisingly challenging and fun. I personally like to "wall-lick" and explore every inch and look at every detail. The amount of hidden things is super fun like the buttons in the museum. This game knows what it is and shows what VR can do without feeling gimmicky. I wish I could come up with more ways to express how much I love this game, but alas I cannot.\t\t\t\t\t
