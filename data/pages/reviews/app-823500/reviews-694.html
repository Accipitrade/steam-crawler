My review on BONEWORKS
A game which I, story-wise, kinda don't like.
I played this with the Valve Index; I encountered no bugs while playing.

BONEWORKS is a game whose developers have introduced some revolutionary VR mechanics and for that reason I am labelling this review as a "yes" recommendation. Even though I may not like the game for the most part, I simply don't feel right not recommending this to people.

VR enthusiasts love this game
BONEWORKS introduces many new features into a Virtual world. You explore the world of BONEWORKS together with these mechanics and I must say it's very lifelike. Weapons handle realistically, you move around realistically. If us humans were controlled by joysticks... . Jump realistically- like this game does so many things good on the mechanics side.
Hell, I would've bought this even if it was just a simulator-sandbox without the story attached to it.

The... story?
I love myself a good story with clear plot points and entering the world of BONEWORKS was a bit of a disappointment.
I have no idea who I am, nor what I am supposed to be doing. Something to do with Monogon.

VR games are tiring. That's just a fact.
So, giving the player an engaging story/game feels like they're not wasting time in their room with a cube strapped to their face.
BONEWORKS makes me feel like the above. Whenever I get excited to play BONEWORKS I am quickly discouraged to play something else or just stop playing a VR game. The story builds around you going through many puzzles and battle arenas. The latter being very entertaining and scary.
The puzzles sometimes feel forced into the levels and are often quite boring. They make me feel detached from the world I am in. I cannot immerse myself into the story. I don't feel like I am in MythOS, I feel like I am in PuzzleOS.

I'd rather just have the option to skip the story and unlock everything without achievements triggering, but we know this isn't possible.
I really want to do the story of this game justice but I cannot stand being in the game for more than around 2 hours at a time which is low compared to the 5 hours I can play VTOL VR or Beat Saber which I will often play for 3 to 4 hours at a time.
So, yes, I have not yet finished BONEWORKS' main story and if you want to sum it up in the comments I really don't mind.
As of March 1st 2021 at 5:05pm I have finished BONEWORKS' story and I will stick to my opinion of it. There was little immersion into the story and I found certain plot-points very weird and illogical. Again, it's very tiring for my human body to keep up with so many puzzles hour after hour and honestly I had the tendency to switch to something more 'active' so to speak. I also unlocked Hover Junkers somewhere along the way, which seems fun but not something I can see myself playing.

Final words
If you read the above you would expect me to leave a negative review for this game, I will not.
While BONEWORKS' story is largely disappointing to me I cannot sleep at night if I would leave this game with a negative review. For the amount of time went into developing its physics, models, mechanics and so much more, I can't do anything else but give this game major props. They took a step into making VR games realistic and immersive. After toying around a bit in the one sandbox I had unlocked I could immerse myself.

If you like VR simulation games, this may be the game for you if you're capable and willing to play through a quite boring and tiring story filled with puzzles that annoy even more.
I rate this game a 7/10 with story.
I rate this game a 9.5/10 without story.\t\t\t\t\t
