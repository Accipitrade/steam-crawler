\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, what a game. I have just finished the campaign and found it much more enjoyable than what some reviewers, such as IGN led me to believe. To be honest, though, there are many unexplained plotholes that pop up throughout the game. The campaign it's self took around 10 hours for me, but the time may very between 6 hours and 12 hours for others. Like I hinted at slightly before, the story is a bit of a mess, but it is still pretty rich and enjoyable if you look closely at some of the minor details that pop up throughout the levels, but they still don't explain many things that the characters talk about and the game ends pretty abruptly with an ending that, let's just say, was unexpected. The physics are generally amazing, but there's a few annoying bugs that pop up here and there that are pretty inconvenient, such as a hand clipping through a thin wall or trying to climb a platform, but then you just fall off for no reason. Luckily it's pretty easy to recover from these. Also, you need to have some SERIOUS vr-legs(resilience to motion sickness) to enjoy this game to it's full potential. Don't get me wrong, the game is still very enjoyable without very good vr-legs, but you will need to take a lot of breaks frequently. The arena mode adds an infinite amount of replayability, but if fighting continuous waves of enemies isn't exactly your style, there's also a sandbox mode which, as I have heard, lets you build contraptions with objects you find laying around the campaign and such, which you can through into one of the many archive bins that you find through each level. To get sandbox mode, you must complete a few steps in the first levels of the game and look for a hidden area. If you want the specifics, you can look it up. Overall, I'd give this a 9.5/10.

TL;DR: Fun game, good campaign, mess of a story, infinite replayability, and fun for most types of people. 9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA wonderfully fantastic mess.

Now that I've finally been able to beat the game, I can say that I'll recommend it for other people because everyone needs to experience a fully physics simulated VR game, but there are definitely some caveats that come with it.

If you're just playing through the game and you don't care about exploring and looking for secrets, it's not too bad, but just about every time I found a secret I tried repeatedly to get it until I would get physically exhausted and extremely frustrated to the point where I didn't care how far into the level I was, I had it and would have to quit for a while, this is compounded by the lack of mid-level save, and early on in the game I had to redo streets like 3 times because of this or dying or trying to get a secret just to find my only way out was to get sucked up by the trash collector, just to get dumped way earlier in the level and killed. Once you figure the game out better it's not so bad and you learn to be more cautious and they are adding a checkpoint system so I'm not going to fault them for that. Streets is my least favourite level because it's where I did all my learning and had the most frustrating time.

Climbing absolutely sucks, and is probably my biggest gripe with this game, because your player body is a simulated physics body just like all the enemies, climbing is super floppy and extremely disorienting and causes you to put a lot of unnatural balance on your feet, my feet just kill after playing this game for a long session (because there's lots of floppy climbing), and I have pretty solid VR legs. Someone else pointed out it's because it moves your perspective in a way you aren't expecting because your VR arms are supporting the weight of a body while you are standing in place supporting the weight of just your arms, which breaks the biggest fundamental of VR, you know how games ported to VR early in its life would move your view or your head when you weren't expecting during cutscenes or something which makes you want to puke instantly, boneworks isnt that bad, but it's enough to give you a constant uneasy feeling while climbing, and there's a lot of climbing if you want to go for the secrets and unlocks, and this was a conscious decision and why there is a warning at the start of the game that it's for experienced VR users, and I'm about as experienced as they get.

Also because of the rigid player body, holding things is also super floppy. I never was a huge fan of the floppy physics in Gorn, and this is basically that. Sometimes I'll be caught off guard and I pull out a gun real fast and it's flopping all over the place while im trying to grab it with the other hand to shoot the dude. It works for me in games like Blade & Sorcery, but in those games I'm never usually caught off guard as much, but even just playing with physics objects sometimes, they don't respond how you would expect sometimes and it breaks the immersion.

Several times I've gotten my hands caught in walls or pipes and things and it just makes for a really clunky time at moment. Or trying to maneuver your way over a pile of rubble you just created, or trying to climb over ledges and the only control you have with your legs are to bend them, and try to work your body onto a ledge with your floppy bent physics legs, you get good at it by about mid game because you'll end up doing it a lot, but it can be quite frustrating at times. Some of the puzzles, while satisfying, are for the most part frustrating. I was really stoked originally after hearing about all the hidden area's and stuff in the game and finding all the secrets, but after playing all the way through, I can't really see myself going back to try to unlock things, it's just too frustrating to me because most of the secrets require climbing which moves your perspective too much.

Your experience may vary. Over all, it's a good game and it does things VR games have never done, some great, but some things just could have been executed better, if your player model wasn't a physics rigid body, 90% of the issues I have with this game probably wouldn't exist. I love that you can jump though, I wish more FPS games had a jump button, there's some moments in the game where you can do some epic jumps and it feels cool.

After re-reading my review, I decided to make some edits, it sounds awfully negative considering I recommend it for other people. Although having a full physics body in the game is what makes it as clunky as it does and as uncomfortable in some situations as it does, it's also part of what makes this game unique and there are certain moments where you do things or you figure something out, or you do some cool parkour move, or slaughtered some virtual zombies in a cool way in slo-mo that just make you feel fantastic and it's those moments that make the game feel fantastic, but it's also those moments that make the cons stick out more. Despite how negative my review sounds though, I'm having a lot of fun with the game, I definitely do recommend people play it, at least for the price it's not a huge risk, it's more value than basically any other VR game out, and the devs deserve it.

It truly is a fantastic mess, but I believe something like this was needed to push the VR scene forward because until this year, most games have been more or less the same.\t\t\t\t\t
