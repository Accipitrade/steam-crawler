\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me preface all of this by saying this game really is for expert VR users, as someone who has had the VIVE for 4 years, I had no problem doing anything in this game. This game is currently my favorite VR game ever made. The physics are amazing, giving you all sorts of freedom I never imagined possible. The gun-play and melee weapons feel amazing and are a blast to use on a wide variety of enemies. The immersion I felt was unreal. I currently own the old HTC VIVE and the old VIVE wands yet the experience was still amazing. I forgot about the whole screen-door effect after mere minutes of playing due to how immersive it was. After beating the campaign, I had multiple moments in real life where I felt as if I was in the game still. The graphics are really good for what its worth, however I wouldn't let it impact your purchase. The story telling might seem lackluster at first (and even throughout the whole campaign), but after watching some videos about it and replaying parts of the campaign, I noticed the story is really good and interesting. The campaign took me a few days to complete and was very fun the whole way through although I will say (slight spoiler ahead) I wish there where more levels with the gravity gun and other physics tools than just the ending. There are almost no bugs to speak of in my experience, and when I did find one, it was patched the next day. The soundtrack for this game is one of the best I've ever heard in my life, it is so energetic and fits the theme of the game. I have a playlist of around 20 songs from the game that I listen to whenever I can, whether it be doing dishes or driving to the store. The amount of updates added to this game is amazing. They are put out very fast and introduce amazing gameplay modes that would normally be sold for full price in other VR titles. The zombie update is a load of fun and I can't wait to see what they add next. For $30, you can't go wrong with this incredible game, it's incredible that this game has completely stomped any other VR game made by a third party. Overall 10/10 game, please buy this or you're missing out.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first VR game I've picked up in a while and I had been pretty excited for it after seeing some dev footage a year or so ago. The first fifteen minutes or so I was really into it, just walking around manipulating the environment in such a uniquely rich way is compelling. The game nails that.

Unfortunately though, I found my character's body constantly getting in the way of getting around in the world. The climbing area in the tutorial was frustrating, my character's head and body kept extending up into the monkey bars causing my arms to bend out in weird directions and I would get stuck unless I crouched and wiggled myself around a bunch every couple of rungs. That primed me to be especially frustrated by the jumping puzzle that came a couple rooms later. For the life of me I can't get the character to jump reliably which meant a lot of falling down and starting over. Pulling myself along the wall meant my character got pulled in strange directions which was disorienting, more limbs in weird places and not lined up with the controller position. I wish I could tell you how the gun play and fighting was, I never got there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Played using oculus quest, side-loaded with virtual desktop

I'll start by not comparing this to the other obvious title that it keeps being referenced against,because they are two totally different games. One showcases the beauty of vr and this is a creative physics based environment.

This game is a MUST if you have a VR system!
It plays really well to the idea of a VR world and is a constant reminder and keeps you grounded with the fact that things (the majority of the time) respond as they would in the real world. The physics based puzzles work great, there's never really one direct way to solve a puzzle and you can get as creative as you like using scenery to advance in ridiculous ways. Sometimes it can be a bit glitchy but never enough to ruin the experience as I was happy enough to keep trying.

The combat is fun, again using anything in the environment you can get your hands on. You can also purchase more entertaining weapons from vending machines by picking up currency as you go. The stab/slash physics are really fun to play around with and everything reacts as it should do.#My only gripe with combat using the oculus is that you have to keep 'X' Pressed down for slo-mo in battles but this then means you can't move around whilst in slow-mo.

There's also a heap of sandbox modes to get lost in and just d**k around with.
