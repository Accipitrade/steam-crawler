\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's clear the devs spent all their time on the game engine and as little time possible making the campaign interesting or coherent, which would not necessarily be a problem since it also has other modes, except you have to play the campaign a lot to unlock stuff, and it's both exhausting to play, and not very fun the vast majority of the time. Doesn't help that it looks like a beginner's half-life mod from start-to-finish with a bizarre smattering of medieval bits and pieces thrown in seemingly at random (I'm sure the story has an explanation, but it's delivered so obtusely, that you have to look it up online). Also, the involvement of youtubers both in the game and surrounding the game's development makes me skeptical towards the super positive overall impressions towards the game when actual critics gave it more of a 7/10.

What makes me genuinely baffled is how many people call this game better than Half-Life: Alyx.

Half Life: Alyx feels polished and finished. This game feels like an alpha build. Not by comparison, just in general. Every bit of interaction this game has over Alyx feels buggy, and it makes sense why Valve didn't include them. I'm a firm believer in the idea of "If you can't make the game mechanic feel good, then do not include it at all", and that's exactly what they did with Alyx.

Now back to Boneworks.

You have a body in this game, but it often works against you and gets stuck in things constantly. Jumping is undermined by the physics if you're standing on something that isn't 100% solid, which causes discomfort, confusion, and frustration. You try to walk through a doorway, and your hand gets caught in it. Punching, and blunt-force melee nearly always feel weak, inconsistent, and unsatisfying. The only melee worth doing is with blades, and even then, the interactions only feel right about 60-70% of the time. It's not really any worse than other Physics based VR games (except maybe Gorn. That game did melee quite well), but it still feels bad more often than not, and it's why Half-Life: Alyx doesn't have melee weapons (which they state in interviews and the commentary).

And lordy, do not get me STARTED on the voice acting for both your character and the enemies. It sounds like someone doing an impression, but is scared of fully committing to the bit. Very obvious the devs just did it themselves. Really hope they hire professional voice actors for the next game, and not just their youtube friends or just themselves again. Also, there's quite a few hyper-low-budget-looking live action scenes that really makes the game feel cheap. Breaks immersion every time.

Do I have anything positive to say about this game at all?
Yes.

1. You get a lot of content for what you pay for. Especially among VR games. I don't like most of it, but I appreciate that about the game at least. Would have rather they went for "Quality" over "Quantity", but at least they delivered on one of those.

2. The guns feel good. Genuinely. The pistols especially. It's also cool that you can throw the gun, or punch with it and it actually does damage. The enemies you shoot at aren't the most fun for the most part, but that's besides the point.

3. The rip-off headcrab enemy is actually done better than Valve's real one in my opinion. You can damage their legs, and grab them while they jump at you. They also actually hug your face, which Valve decided not to commit to in Alyx sadly.

4. While unlocking it requires an online guide and I hate that, the sandbox mode was a very smart inclusion, and I'm happy to see it. I also like the idea that you can carry things to the end of a level and send them into a portal to put into the sandbox mode, however I do NOT like that you can only do this at the very, VERY end of each level as they are absurdly long, and aggressively tedious. Your inventory is very limited, so the only way to get everything you want turned in is to either play each level multiple times (which can take up to 90 mins per play), or very slowly toss everything a few feet ahead of you throughout the level.

Now to say you shouldn't buy this game is silly. If you have a VR headset and don't get nauseous easily, then of course you should try this game out. It demonstrates everything that feels good and bad about VR. That said, I don't personally think it's a very good game when viewed as a whole, and now that the trailer for the next game is out and just looks like more of the same, as if anything about the theme of this game was worth bringing back, it's very upsetting. I wanted them to take this engine and go for something far better, not just work on making the engine less buggy and reuse basically all the same assets.

In short: This game is a mess, but you should try it once because it tries to have the most variety of interactions a VR game can have, and it's worth seeing what works and what doesn't. Also, a lot of people seem to really like it for some reason, so... maybe you will too?

Giving it a "don't recommend" because I don't really like it, and I feel the collective user rating percentage, as of now, gives a false impression of the game's quality.\t\t\t\t\t
