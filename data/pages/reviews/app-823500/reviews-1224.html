\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLiterally the best game I've ever played. Some people say the campaign isn't that long, but I'm about 10hrs in and only half way done. I explore the levels for secrets a lot, so that might be why. Looking forward to playing the sandbox and replaying the levels to unlock stuff.

Do not play this game unless you are very comfortable with VR and don't get motion sick. If you EVER start feeling motion sick in VR, please take a break. You do not want VR to be associated with motion sickness. This game has smooth locomotion, climbing, long drops, and other extreme motion that will definitely induce motion sickness on inexperienced VR users. Also, be warned if you get scared easily. This game is extremely immersive and will likely make you fear for your life at some points.

My only issue with this game is how the campaign saves your progress. Your progress is saved between levels. There are eight levels, some of which I've spent 3hrs on. That being said, do not start a level unless you have several hours to burn, because you do not want to have to quit in the middle of a level and have to start over.

The guns are extremely realistic. When you shoot a gun, the shells fly out of the gun as individual physics objects on the ground. Loading guns is very realistic with how you chamber a round, and like in real life, removing the magazine doesn't necessarily mean the gun is not loaded. There may still be a round in the chamber. The larger guns can often be held in multiple different ways, such as by the barrel, if you want more stability. Scopes and laser sights are very functional.

Killing enemies is very satisfying, both with guns and melee.

This is the best VR game ever made.

Edit: Turns out this game is longer than I thought. I am 16hrs in and not even half way done with the campaign. I do tend to take games like this slowly so I can look for secrets.\t\t\t\t\t
