\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was simultaneously the most annoyed and most excited I've been in a VR game. The excitement was initial - being able to interact on a different scale than what I was used to in a VR game was incredible at first. The annoyance came from the realization that the game is just like every other Stress Level Zero game - a very enticing shell of a game stretched over as many hours as possible to give the illusion of content. I played Hover Junkers at a friends' house when it first game out, often. I bought Duck Season and played it enough to know it wasn't for me. I bought Boneworks and realized after a small stretch that this would be a trend in this developers' works.

Stress Level Zero - the dev studio and some of the YouTube channels associated with - is an ADHD fever dream of a studio. The game is good, I'm not saying it isn't, but I'm saying that it is very much a product of that studio's ethic. Initial thoughts are, "WOW, everything is physics-based." "WOW, I really have to do everything as I would in real life." "WOW, this is not how it would go in real life." And "WOW, this is really all there is, isn't it?"

Just finishing the game has left me sour because for the entirety of my playthrough I kept thinking, "There must be a new (insert piece of content) after this." I kept thinking there would be a more involved enemy that wasn't there to make a bee-line to you and either shoot or punch you with no real use for the physics engine in either cases aside from the creative falling they would experience at the end of each exchange. I kept thinking the calibration was wrong because the movements I was attempting shouldn't be so difficult, but realized quickly the dedication to physics-based gameplay was taken so far as to take a lot of the potential joy out of the games' hand to hand combat. I realized the game was attempting to do something with the story that would be involving and unique. And then it ended. And the last level, for those who have finished the game, is spitting in the face of the gamers who thought that this would be more than just a tech-demo due to the length of the game and the devs repeatedly stating it would not be.

The last level of the game is literally one long ladder climb - and I'm sure the devs were well aware of the climbing being the literal worst aspect of the game, by far.

In short, this game was so disappointing to me because of how much potential it showed. If the physics was scaled back so that the climbing was even slightly enjoyable, the game could have been amazing. If the player-character wasn't so sluggish in comparison with the person controlling it, the hand to hand combat would've been worthwhile. If the game gave you even the slightest indication that it wasn't just the same thing stretched out over 10 hours of gameplay, the story could've been worthwhile. But for me, especially because of how it ended, the game was an exercise in fighting a games' mechanics to achieve your goals much the same way as a fixed-camera survival horror game was in the playstation 1 era.

Stress Level Zero will never get another dollar from me - they make tech demos. Astoundingly good tech demos with 10 hours of story to string you along, but tech demos nonetheless.
