\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks as a game is really its own thing entirely. The insane physics and the presence of a full body in vr is something few vr developers have implemented. And now I understand why. See, having a real body and the player being a physics object is a really cool idea, however the limitations on the player is enough to justify this being something you don't put in every single vr game. It certainly has it's place in the VR space, but should be used sparingly. Half LIfe Alyx is the perfect example of how physics and the autonomy of a player don't have to be 1:1 with real life for the game to be immersive. That said, I don't think either game's approach is necessarily 'better' or 'worse', they're just different.
Outside of Boneworks' main gimic, the game can stand on its own. The narrative was alright but not perfect. It did feel like it was somewhat forced onto a game that never had a story in mind, rather an idea of the implementation of hyper-real physics into VR. The visuals are very impressive for the scale of the dev team.
I would only recommend the game to people with a pc powerful enough to handle it. Even with a RTX 2060 and a Ryzen 5 2600 the game would frequently drop frames or lose resolution to such a significant extent that entire sections were too blurred to maintain realism with the adaptive resolution setting turned on. It's playable, but not great. It also induces a lot of motion sickness at times, so keep that in mind.\t\t\t\t\t
