\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood:
- Gun combat feels fantastic, nullbodies, zombies, and headcrabs give you a ton of feedback when they get shot. I can't express enough how satisfying it is to shove a nullbody down a flight of stairs, slow-mo Max Payne jump down after it, and shoot the ♥♥♥♥ out of it on its way down.

- Physical interaction. It has its quirks (climbing can be a little iffy and you sometimes get your arms stuck in/on things or inexplicably lose your grip on something, but overall it's solid. Your environment has a believable sense of weight and heft that other games often fail to simulate. Using the crowbar to open/pull yourself onto things is extremely satisfying.

- The game is kinda funny. It leans into having a weird, occasionally creepy atmosphere in the same way that the first Portal game does. At no point did I feel like it I was playing a Borderlands game, thank god.

Bad:
- Optimization. I've got an RX 580 8GB, i7 2600K, and 32GB of RAM. Right around min spec to play the game. The game runs beautifully at low settings even in very detailed indoor environments that are dense with textures, enemies, and physics objects. However, the second I get into a wide-open outdoor area, even the most simplistic of environments with few textures/physics objects, the adaptive resolution smears vasoline on my eyes and the game REALLY starts to chug. SteamVR seems to be causing some issues for Rift users as well.

- Progression. I haven't beaten the game yet, but the progression system is dumb as hell. Having to archive everything you find just isn't a fun incentive to replay the game. I like the idea of ammo as currency - that's honestly where progression should be focused. Let us unlock things for the sandbox by buying them from those vending machines. There are any number of progression systems that would be better than what we have right now.

- No checkpoints. Tons of people have said it better than me already but there's no way to save and quit, and the longer levels can easily take over an hour if you're spending time exploring. Really sucks to have to run back an entire level because there's no option to save and quit.


The good outweighs the bad here. Absolutely a major VR breakthrough and I can't wait to see more games like this. I'm excited to see what Stress Level Zero does next.\t\t\t\t\t
