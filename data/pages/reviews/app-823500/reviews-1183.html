\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn its current state, it's not 100% worth $30 and has some major QOL problems.
* Saves are pretty nonexistant. You can only select individual levels (and only ones which you have gotten to). If you have to do something in the middle of a level - gg. If you're going to play it then you need at least a solid 1-2 hours without any interruptions.
* There's no way to enable always-sprinting. If you're on the index you have to press down the stick, which gets pretty painful after a while especially if your index controller has a crappy stick.
* The game can randomly crash due to some sort of memory leak. I have 16GB of ram and it's still managed to crash itself multiple times even with nothing else open.
* The hands and anything you pickup has an extremely floaty feeling. I have a pretty small area so it's not exactly ideal to throw or do anything without checking my bounds first.

Edit:
* On the Index it's pretty hard to not grab onto random things due to the way running works (I have the hands calibrated correctly).
* Your simulated body will collide with things you pick up and it's overly large. I constantly have problems with melee weapons and hammers not working because they're stuck on my back, or trying to move a prop and it getting stuck on my legs. It's disorienting to have your hand in front of yourself in real life but to actually have it stuck behind you.
* Throwing things and using melee always feels like it has a lower amount of force than you're actually using in real life. I'm not sure if this is related to the previous thing though.
* I'm not 100% positive, but this game seems to have the typical default Unity stutter problem. Be default Unity only updates the game 50 times per second. To devs: Edit -> Project Settings -> Time, make sure it's as high as is reasonable.\t\t\t\t\t
