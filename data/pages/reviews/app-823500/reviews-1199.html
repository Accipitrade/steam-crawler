\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ game. Is it the second coming of christ no, is it really fun and a glimpse at vr games to come. Hell yes

The story
Not the games best feature. Its somewhat bare bones and feels like not much is explained. Though I liked the what they were trying to go for.

The gameplay
This games levels offer you a good a amount of freedom. As an example, in one level I was able to enter a building by moving a box next to a window so I could brake it with a pipe wrench, I then was able to climb through the window. This game will let you play how you want to play and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ its awesome.

The melee
You get a lot of freedom here you can sweep an opponents legs, cripple their limbs, push them over, grab them by the waist and throw them over your shoulder, etc. There's plenty of toys to play with too so you wont be bored. The baseball bat is a personal favorite.

The guns
There's a dedicated bullet time button with 3 different speeds. Oh yeah it's also infinite because the dev's are amazing. You can do the hk slap on the mp5 because bad ass. The gunplay feels decent and allows room for flair. If you've watched any freddy wong or corridor digital videos just play like that.

The meh
the physics can be alittle janky at some points. It's not that bad though.

I feel like the climbing could have used some more freedom.

This game is definitely best played with an index. I was playing with a first gen oculus rift and well I played through the game just fine I could tell that the touch controllers have some limitations. More of a me problem but thought I should list it.

The worst thing imo is the sandbox mode. The mode itself is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ awesome but the problem is getting stuff to play with. You have to unlock all the stuff you want to play around with by finding them in the story mode. so that's pretty annoying.

Overall I would give this game an 8 out of 10. It's a fun game that combines many elements into an enjoyable experience. I liked the idea of the story but I found myself wanting more.
Definitely worth $30

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI beat it recently after putting about 7-ish hours into it. I felt it was just "Okay"? Like, it does a lot of cool things, but doesn't do many of the things particularly well, and I feel this could be partially attributed to your whole character being simulated (instead of just you having some disembodied hands like any other VR game).

Climbing stuff felt awful, your characters arms constantly get caught on the enviornment as you climb, and then some objects, which are supposed to be climbable, my guys hands would just sometimes slide off of? Like I'd grab the top of a pipe, and his hand would still hold a grip, but would slide down the pipe. There were times where I'd see a clear path to climb to like a secret area or something and I was just like "I don't even want to ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ bother". On top of that, anything you climb or grab onto kinda ends up pulling your character in on it, there's were many times I would be trying to climb and my characters face is in the wall, so I have to lean back awkwardly to look around, and because my characters whole body is accounted for, I have my arms getting caught on ♥♥♥♥ as I climb. Because of this, exploring feels like a hassle, but if you're a completionist or want to unlock the extra content you're gonna have to do a whole ♥♥♥♥ load of running around and exploring.

Speaking of movement mechanics, while I am fairly new to VR, I've largely had a pleasant experience with things like smooth locomotion in other games like H3VR and Arizona Sunshine. Boneworks is the only game to make me feel kinda crappy after a couple hours of playing. The developers felt it was a good idea to basically ignore everything other VR devs learned to not do in order to avoid motion sickness. A user on ResetERA put it more succinctly than I can:

"Boneworks uses IK, and applies a physics model to them. So your arm, which is just inferred by your hand, might brush up against a wall, but the arm will physically move your VR body out of the way, so that your view in game sways back like you were stepping away from the wall, even though your IRL body didn't do any of that motion. The end result is that the game is essentially constantly swaying you around, pushing you against walls, through motions you did NOT do. This is the cardinal sin of VR, you NEVER wrestle movement of the camera away from the user. It makes people VERY sick. This was one of the very first most fundamental lessons learned about VR design."

There are no comfort options in Boneworks. Hell, the game mocks teleportation based movement right off the bat. You're gonna need an iron stomach to play this game.

All the cool melee combat that they touted just doesn't feel good or even as effective as using firearms, like I found most of the time when I did try beating up enemies with melee weapons it just felt like I was bopping them in the head a bunch until they died. Swinging swords didn't really feel any different or anymore satisfying than beating an enemy with a pipe, the only benefit of the swords is running it through the enemies which is fun at first but gets kinda tiring, it's no Blade and Sorcery, basically. You have to really aggressively swing your arms to do any damage to enemies too, which I don't feel super safe doing in my little office.

Firearms, speaking of, feel okay. Though I think H3VR may have ruined VR guns for me because H3VR's guns feel so good. The recoil patterns of the various full auto weapons feel all over the place, like first shot will go point of aim, and then the muzzle will dip way down and the shot will go lower than point of aim, then bounce up and go above, it just feels kinda weird. One of the first guns you get, the 1911, has an unusual front sight, and I never quite nailed down where to properly hold to get consistent headshots, the front sight seems to be lower than the rear sight (the front and rear sight should be the same height), so I naturally wanna tip the muzzle up a bit to get it to line up, but then I'm aiming a little high, so I try to hold low and still have issues hitting stuff. It works well enough in close range, but not at further ranges, which is something I didn't have issues with when using the other pistol, a Glock, which has proper sights. The weapon variety is kinda lacking, speaking of. You get two pistols that are functionally the same, the aforementioned 1911 and Glock, several kinds of AR which are mostly functionally the same, some come with different attachments like a laser, foregrip, ACOG scope, EOTech holo sight, etc. I used the ACOP scoped AR like once, then used most of the others whenever I could get my hands on them, but only really preferred the holo sight AR. Then there's the MP5 which comes in different colors, I guess, and a little Uzi thing.

Lots of samey feeling guns, but I'm guessing they also wanted you to rely on environmental stuff, but as was mentioned, the melee stuff didn't really feel amazing, and felt like more work than just shooting the enemies, and ammo is abundant enough that I never really felt the need to use anything but guns. There's also a variety of little gadgets but you really have to search to find them, which I didn't feel particularly enthused about doing because of how much of a slog exploring is, and even then any weapons you find you only use for the level you're currently in, you lose all weapons when you enter the next level.

The level of interactivity was really cool, but gets old quite fast, and part of this could be due to how jank it all feels. Like some objects have "weight" to them, but what this basically means is you get these awkward moments where you're holding your real arms out in front to carry something and your guy is dragging his arms because the object is heavy. A lot of it felt kinda under utilized, for most of the game I barely picked up random objects unless I had to for a puzzle, it was fun like I said to beat a bad guy up with random objects once or twice, but I mainly stuck to firearms. Speaking of puzzles, if you liked the physics puzzles of Half Life 2, then you'll love the puzzles of Boneworks, because it's all very half life esque physics puzzles.

One final grip, you have a slow mo button. Should be cool right? Well you have to hold to trigger it, on the same controller that has the thumbstick for you to move with. So you have stop moving and hold a button to go slow mo. I dunno if it's possible to remap that, haven't gone back to actually check. But to be honest, not sure I'll even bother going back. I've had a lot more fun playing the same little modes of Hot dogs, Horseshoes, and Hand Grenades over and over because a lot of the mechanics in that feel more satisfying and streamlined than Boneworks. Boneworks does some cool stuff, don't get me wrong, but it all feels like stuff that's waiting to be put to better use in a better game.


This is one of those cases where I wish there was something like a "Maybe" or middle option apart from the Yes or No on Steam, I don't think the game is bad enough to say no to it, but I find it hard to say "Yes" as well. For now I'll say "Yes", but it's more of a "Yes, but wait for a sale".\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is probably one of the very few VR games that feels like a full game at launch, rather than a tech demo or a game that solely exists to throw waves of enemies at you. The campaign is great, puzzles are fun with multiple solution answers and the weapons all feel great to use (with the exception of some melee weapons).

The only grudge I hold against this game is the enemy variety. Nulls are boring as hell to fight against, but make up the majority of enemies in the game. Later they throw hadoukens at you, but this doesn't make them anymore interesting.

I find the android/zombies quite fun, even though I assume they share the same basic AI as the nulls. I would say this is because of their much more interesting design and fact they actually have feedback other than an orange circle when they're attacked.

The enemies with guns are fun to get into firefights with, but they can only be hit in certain "hardspots" on their body and sometimes the game will glitch out and these won't exist or will be in another postcode because the physics engine bugged when they spawned. This has only happened to me twice but it was annoying both times. Other than that they're an interesting enemy type, you will often find yourself getting pinned down by one as others push to flank you, which is always exciting.

The gunplay feels sweet and weapons all have weight and great feedback. Even if your mag runs empty you can charge an enemy and KO them with the butt of your rifle or pistol whip their head.

This is a really great glimpse of the future VR has to offer, and I hope this will become the benchmark for VR games moving forward.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is worth a buy for anyone who owns VR, and doesn't mind a few hand cramps with a non-index controller. It definitely lives up to and surpasses all the hype it had around it before launch.

Everything is interactable and physics simulated. It isn't offputting when your body isn't synced with your controllers. It is truly nothing like other games. Firearms, while not having accurate operations (as things like the slide release and mag release cannot be pressed separately) are very fun. They feel real and the enemies react to being hit by them well. Mele left some to be desired or I just didn't know how to operate the weapons.

The level design is great. Keep in mind it is low on detail to improve frame rates. Feels like what an AI would kitbash together after given real-life assets and a default dev environment. Great mixture of Sudo combat arenas, puzzles, and areas based around a cool concept. Textures were noticeable great too.

This game is meant for the index controllers. The default controllers get very tedious as to pick up an object you are required to constantly hold down the grip and trigger buttons. Also, my trackpads are broken, due to a controller problem, so locomotion was very hard.\t\t\t\t\t
