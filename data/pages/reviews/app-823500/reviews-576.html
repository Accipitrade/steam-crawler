\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the best VR games I've had the pleasure of experiencing. It's got a deep, intricately woven story that shares a universe with Duck Season and even allows you to touch and interact with anything in the game environment, even allowing you to jump, vault, and climb whatever your heart may desire (Within reason. You can't scale a vertical wall...not without some rare Developer's Gravity Weapons.) It's also rich with some fantastic gun play, physics puzzles, and even includes Gravity Manipulation and a Time Slowing Mechanic. My few gripes would have to start with the sheer amount of collectibles to be found in the game, which, while I adore collectathons, always has so much very far out of reach in the game, requiring you to climb a LOT to get everything. And, on top of that, everything you wish to collect must be carried to a "Reclaimation Bin", which is always either out of the way, requiring more climbing, environment manipulation, or is at the end of the level, and you only have 5 slots total on your body to work with. Granted, once reclaimed, you never have to refind the object again unless you reset your collectibles, but it's quite a lot to do. Another issue is the somewhat annoying body physics, which, while fluid and great for interaction, can find your arms, head, or even legs trapped in objects while trying to climb, resulting in a fall and reset progress. However, it was still fantastically enjoyable, and left me utterly speechless. 8/10, not a masterpiece but is absolutely going down in history as one of the greatest VR games, even if people rarely discuss it in the mainstream.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I first played this game it made me sick, so I stopped playing. I just came back to the game and either I have a better tolerance for VR or they did something to make it better, either way it took me about 14 hours to finish. For me, Alyx half life is the standard, this game comes close. The environment is spectacular, the puzzles fairly easy but fun and the fighting is pretty decent.

Some of the negative, the physics can be wonky sometimes. Large melee weapons will cause your arm to bend in weird ways, I frequently dropped items and weapons and would have to go back and search for them, its too easy to grab the wrong weapon or ammo off of your belt by accident.
