Keep in mind, i play using a Valve Index, which i recon is the best possible experience.

This is the first VR game where after more than an hour of continious gameplay, i dont feel dizzy after i take off my headset. I dont know if this is because of the full body precence in game, or the realistic controls, but it's nice.
Visuals are great, not up to par with AAA non-VR game, but that's fine, because otherwise it would be to much for any pc available today, so it's as good as it gets.

A couple of notes though;
The playtime between save points is a bit too long in my opinion, because sometimes i just want to take a quick break, and in that case i need to keep the game running, to not loose my progress.
I should also note, that i've had 1 crash, but haven't had any issues since, so it's not a big problem.\t\t\t\t\t

2 Hours in, first experience review.

This looked and felt even before it came out - Brandon & his team did a great job just at showcasing the possibilites. I expected a completely different game after the first video of "Gun physics", but I'm not disappointed. The idea, atmosphere, setting - are just great. Found the first easter egg, nice legs btw.

The physics are just as presented - amazing. Revolutionary, I'd say. Heavy objects ARE heavy and you gotta remember that when dealing with them.

Maybe it's me or just Oculus things, but I couldn't really grab a ledge and push myself onto it, so I guess no climbing in that way? Had to find different methods to do a simple thing, like putting boxes or finding another, higher spot to grab.

Pros:

- Excellent physics
- Very well made... well, everything
- Interesting story (yes, 2 hours is what I need to see if it's gonna be nice)
- Some things just keep coming into gameplay that make my jaw drop, like the "Prepare thyself" moment. That was very nice of you, SL0.

There are more pros that I'm currently not ready to list, so wait for a second review.

Cons:

- Janky locomotion and player movement, but blame that on realistic physics.
- Ledge grabbing. Gotta research that.
- Heavy CPU usage? Can't say for sure, im getting bottlenecked anyway, but there is some occasional stuttering. Maybe it's a problem on my side.
- Can't really throw stuff with as much force as I intend to. Can't say if it's the VR restriction of speed, but either way it's not that tragic.

Another review coming after game completion.

GTX 2070 SUPER
Intel i5-9600K
