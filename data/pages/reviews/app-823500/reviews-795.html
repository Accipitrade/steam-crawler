\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's hard for me to write this review but I honestly, I just cannot recommend this game. The game has a quite boring story around it, that in my opinion is really pretentious and ominous for what it really is. I wasn't interested on it at all, which is quite a shame because the whole ambience of the game looks interesting-ish, although also the levels really do look like unfinished levels with development textures from the Source engine, which kinda makes me think if they were lazy to actually make something that would fit in a "MMO VR game" or wherever the hell the game is supposed to be located at. But honestly, this is definetly, by far not the worst thing from the game, because what is worse, is definetly the gameplay itself.

Look, the body simulation is impressive, the world interactions are amazing and quite realistic, the fact that you can skip most puzzles by clever climbing and/or clever world interaction is really cool, but this is really not meant for VR. This is, by far, the most nauseating VR experience I've had by far. And, before anyone rushes to this review's comment section screaming at me "but you need to have played more VR experiences with direct movement!" and similar things, let me tell you that you can shut the hell up, I have gone through HL Alyx with lineal movement without issue, I have played Vivecraft for a long while, with the Aether mod, I have ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ JUMPED OFF THE AETHER IN VR. And I have gotten used to direct movement in VR. But this game truly made me see I was completely wrong... or maybe it's just not a good idea to, without even letting me be ready, fling me from one side of the map to the other one, and my poor body and brain sending contradictory sensations, to a point to actually have fallen off to the floor or need to grab to SOMETHING because I really thought I was going to pass out in the spot. The body, that is fully simulated in this game as previously stated, will move sometimes on its own if you just move your hands around and happen to hit your virtual body, climbing becomes awful with your body in the way, ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ PRAY that you wont miss an input to grab whatever you're climbing or else be ready to grab onto something while you fall from a building or smth. Also with the fact that your body is completely there, there are many moments in the game where my hand gets completely stuck behind something, or stuck in a melee weapon that is stuck in an enemy's body... leaving you essentially screwed while in combat, or while climbing.

You know, it is actually quite funny how, when you start this game and you go through the "tutorial" (which is some kind of museum about VR technologies, actually), they mock other VR experiences of using teleportation as a locomotion method, by literally displaying cavemen using said method. And now thinking back to it, I really think that the developers of this game really are very full of themselves, when they're mocking the thing they should probably be offering too, so the game is accessible for more people. But in that, they definetly are making a fool of themselves.

I do have to mention though that the gunplay can be pretty fun most of the times, the gun interactions are really cool, and they overall feel awesome to use, no buts.

But, in summary, this is what they say in the description of the game. An experimental VR physics adventure. And I can certainly say, that this experiment was a complete, and utter failure. Not recommended.\t\t\t\t\t
