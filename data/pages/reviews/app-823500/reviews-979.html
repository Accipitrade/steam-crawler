\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven though it frustrated me at first, its a better game than my first impression led me to believe. I would wholly recommend it if you run an Index. Unfortunately I would not advise it for everyone using standard Vive wands. While there are some control options, they only offer a "push to move" on the touchpad and you have to hold the menu button above that same touchpad to use the slow motion...So no slo-mo while moving. Also, any melee weapon you use requires holding the grip buttons to keep it gripped (pretty sure these were only designed to be used as toggles - they can get tiring to keep squeezed and I have a little arthritis in the last 3 digits of one of my hands that makes it impossible after 5 minutes of playing, so maybe its me...then again most VR games moved away from holding that vive grip button to keep an item gripped long ago).
So, be aware that you might have to resort to custom control mappings to make things comfortable on Vive wands - this is stated in the control options so its not like they aren't aware. I've tried several custom mappings to mixed results (decent movement settings but triple click the grip button to release guns for example) and will likely have to just make my own. Seems ridiculous to me to not have the standard "onward style" movement using touch to walk and click to sprint. Also, it seems the grab and drop system could be similar to H3VR for the Vive wands without loosing any real nuance. I will go through the available custom mappings when I have regained some patience, lol (had to stop playing because I was getting a little frustrated with looking through and trying controller mappings) and will post my own if necessary.

FYI: I knew this was hinky for the wands before I bought it. Its just a bit worse than I anticipated, and it actually baffles me because I know Stress Level Zero is a good dev house who cares...but this seems like they couldn't be bothered to make it comfortable for us with the older hardware (because its by no means impossible).

Edit on 4/8: I tried around 10 custom control setups plus spent a while tweaking my own and just couldn't find anything that felt transparent enough; things just never felt fluid. I wasn't able to setup a touch to move control scheme that felt consistent and predictable (I will keep working on that, I might've missed something in the input settings or maybe its a sign my controller needs that rubber piece reset *the rubber bumper is now off center on both controllers so that is causing some of the issue - edited in on 4/9*). The grip and release for firearms works fine in the game without change and I'm not sure there's a way to change grip and release for melee only, so changing the grip button to "toggle" in steam input settings borks most of the game's interactions overall. I went back to the normal Boneworks settings and just stuck to firearms mainly and it really didn't take me much longer to get used to the way the movement feels. Its not about VR legs, I'm fine there...for me there doesn't feel like there's enough fine grain control over slower "walking" speed. The high acceleration in the input curve combined with a fairly long double click window make, for example, trying to place large "heavy" objects with precision a bit difficult (more difficult than intended) if you can't rely on real world space to physically step around in. I'm better with it now, plus it is a little bit of the fun of the game to overshoot and burst through some crates or hear your VR body slap into the wall.
Hopefully this next update addresses some or any of the Vive wand stuff because I'd love to spend more time in this game using a pistol/knife combo or something. Either way, I was less impressed at first but I have really come to enjoy the game even if I'm missing out on the melee side of things.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall Boneworks is the best vr experience out there right now (at least untill Half Life Alyx come out). It's not perfect, but it is still incredibly fun and immersive.

The biggest disappointment for me is the lack of enemy variety. Combat as a whole, seems to be kinda ignored most of the time. Most of the games is spent you walking around in a physics enviorment. There are only 3.5 enemy types. The nullbodys (unarmed orange people and their variants), the head crabs (which are the most interesting and fun to fight), and the cleanup crew (holograms with guns). There is also a nullbody variant that can shoot you as well, but they aren't that much more threatening, and there are also turrets but they are more of an enviormental hazzard than an enemy. All of them are really easy to kill and don't provide much of a challenge making combat kinda pointless. It still is kinda fun sweeping areas of enemies but it can get boring. I suppose there isn't much they can do to improve it since your body doesn't always move the way you want it to in close quarters.

Another annoyance is the collectable system. While I do like the idea of collecting weapons and objects for sandbox, the problem is that to collect them you have to bring them to the end of the level and toss them in a reclamation bin. The issue with that is that there may be 5-10 collectables per level, but you only have 5 inventory slots. Meaning you have to either slog a bunch of items through a level, or do a bunch a back tracking. Thankfully the levels are made so you can always backtrack (with a few exceptions).

So what is actually fun in the game? The physics puzzles and exploration. The simulated body is a game changer and is going to make it hard to play games without it. Climbing, jumping, and throwing all feel really accurate to real life. There is a tutorial level dedicated just to get you used to your new body. Each level is distinct and interesting with new weapons to have fun with. As you progress you slowly learn more about the story and the end you learn a bit more and you can choose to learn more of the lore online. My only gripe with the physics is that climbing ledges (floors and walls) is never properly explained, and you kinda just have to develop your own technique for it. It seems that they fixed the climbing. Making it something you can naturally do now without much fuss. There is also some good post game unlockables for people who want to find everything.

My biggest hope is that the devs release these tools for other game devs to use. It would be great to see other games with the full body simulation to be used for other genres and just to have a body that doesn't clip through everything. I would also like the so the Boneworks get some more updates to add more levels and enemies, and for them to not constrain themselves to stuff that fits into the lore (the game is a vr game, so they could do more fantasy and syfy stuff).

Edit: After playing Half Life: Alyx, I don't think every vr game needs this kind of interactivity. That is because, while high interactivity is very important, it doesn't need this flavor of interactivity. Although not having melee is really weird, in Half Life: Alex case, but throwing works for some reason. Also the weapon selector in HLA is way more convienent than trying to fiddle with body inventory, but not as fun.\t\t\t\t\t
