\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrustrating.

With sandbox and arena modes locked, all you can do is play through the storymode. It's a bit clunky, and unintuitive in places. Adding that you can't seem to move by teleporting, you can often want to take a break due to feeling frustrated, tired, or just outright sick. This was the only game so far where I felt significantly sick afterwards, so it was disappointing that there are no savegames.

All you can do is load the start of the last level you were on, meaning you have to redo a lot of gameplay. If there's no sandbox, and you're left feeling that frustration or tiredness or sickness, then why would you want to go through much of a level again just to try making some more progress?

The story is interesting, a lot of the interactivity is cool, but then why would they put all that effort in if they didn't even offer savegames or a relaxing sandbox mode?

I think being able to teleport short distances could cut out a lot of potential motion sickness, and being able to save the game closer to where you left off would be a godsend. Failing that, why not just let the customers use sandbox mode if they didn't have fun in the story?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinished Game in 8 hours (Just the Story mode, with a little extra exploring)
Combat is a lot of fun and puzzles can be solved in practically any way that utilises physics, I'm pretty sure I solved some in unintended ways (But that's part of the fun)

The story is a little nonsensical and just serves to give opportunities to mess around in the game in a host of creative enviroments.

Whilst the physics are absolutely fantastic, there were several times in which my hands would catch into a fence or forcibly disconnect while climbing (End section, I'm looking at you.) Otherwise, the physics allow for an incredible amount of approaches to any situation.
Taking fire from a turret? You could roll several barrels across the ground and duck behind them for cover, alternatively you could cover yourself with a trashcan and walk right up to it and knock it to the ground in a 'Metal-Bin-Gear' style.

Really nice glimpse into the future of what VR could look like in gaming, more a feature length tech-demo then a game, but an absolutely fantastic one at that.

Worth the retail price.
