This game is amazing, don't get me wrong. But the constant jittering from performance lag is purely vomit inducing. I like to consider myself someone with a strong stomach, but this game has at times made me stop playing due to the lag triggering motion sickness. This is, I might add, the only VR game that has ever done this to me.

Other than that, this game is without a doubt one of the best VR games I have played thus far. Everything is physics based which makes for a really fun game, however because of that, there are countless bugs that need ironing out. It's still early days yet so I'll give them a pass for now.

7/10

**Would easily be a 9/10 but the constant jittering and frequent bugs lower the score, which is a shame as when it occasionally runs smoothly I had an absolute blast.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a great improvement of VR, making you (the player) a complete rigidbody with physics interaction, and almost all objects in the world are physics objects and intractable.
This is how VR games should be, and not the stuff we've gotten before where you 'peak' into a VR world as some kind of phaseshifting god.
This is the necessary step to take VR to the next level, and I assume Half Life: Alyx will do this as well.
That said there's quite a few issue I have with the game past what I've mention previously.

First, there's still a few quirks left with where you have to allow the player to phase through material since you can get stuck in stuff pretty easily, or shove yourself away. It's very well done, but a few more steps are needed to make it really enjoyable (it'll be hard to dicern the difference between intent and accident though).

Secondly, there's no save-points or checkpoints of any kind. Just a few dark-souls shortcuts to unlock in a level, and if you quit the game you'll have to do everything again. Every level can take between 1-3 hours too ,the first time you play them.

Lastly, this game reminds me a lot of half-life 1 (or 2?) , in the way that there's a lot of physics puzzles, but they can be a bit confusing and unforgiving. I like the old-school feel, but I do not like the frustration that comes with it. Especially in a VR game where actual gaming actually takes quite some effort out of you due to physical activity and nausea.


But all in all , this is a great step into the future of VR and there's quite a lot to love in it.
Gunplay is the best I've seen, sound and feel is amazing and some enemies/scenarios makes you duck for cover and find simple strategies to take them out without the risk of dying (such as moving covers to get a better angle and not getting hit).

If Half Life Alyx hasn't come out or isn't the new sliced bread of VR. Get this, this is a milestone and should get the praise it deserves.
If you're reading this after 2020 and a lot of games are out and similar to this, they are probably better in every way and you needn't bother with this, this was just the prelude to the greatness you're experiencing now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an absolute mess...but it's the good sort of mess where someone's upended a box of lego on the floor. You'll rare find the right bit you want, but you'll have enough to improvise.

There's a reason people thought this was a half life game at first, it's trying to invoke some new senses of gameplay by tying some context together with some old fashioned mechanics and a sprinkling of something new. The downside is that whilst this is well polished, it all falls under the backup comment of 'For an Indie game'. So the story is lack luster, the combat is limited to a few enemy types within simple environments...and the sprinkling of newness is a potential mixed bag...

...But this isn't to say Boneworks is bad. Attempting to climb a shelf only for it to fall down under my own weight is as hilarious as it is motion sickness triggering. Still on the climbing there's an immersive nature to climbing, using a crowbar to hook around a pole to give you that extra few foot of reach to climb better.

This game will take most players about six hours, give or take two depending on how much you want to mess around with the environment. I enjoyed it and I'm sure most people will too.

There's more content here than VR game's I've paid more for...it's also probably going to be a good warm up to HL:A, give it a go!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat I liked:

-Amazing physics engine
-Interesting world
-Balance of puzzles and combat
-Weight in VR that works
-Physics based combat

What I did not like

-Gripping of melee weapons is weird, it is not like Blade and sorcery in that the grip is automatic in a sense, but whenever I used them I had to keep re-adjusting my grip constantly
-Sharp weapons don't have any vibration/haptics, which feels strange and takes me out of it (probs will be fixed mind you)
-Ending was a bit weird, unclear, and anti-climatic
-Valve index default slow mo control is wrongly place in my opinion, its on the movement hand, so I cant slow time and move at the same time, I did not use it in the entire game because of this, its a hold to use kind of thing, if it was a toggle with a timer sort of thing or mana, this would work but a hold down is not viable.

Overall its setting a new mainstream standard for VR gaming, collision combat like skyrim vr will be obsolete.

Thankyou to stress level zero for setting a new course in VR gaming!\t\t\t\t\t

Fantastic job Brandon or whoever the project lead is and all the devs there at Stress Level Zero. For me at least being new to VR it was easy to pick up and get used too. Being that this game takes a lot of its inspiration from Half Life I was surprised to not see a quick save feature. Flat screen shooters seem so bland now.
