\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know why people talk about this game like it's the greatest thing that ever happened to VR. It's OK at best, and it certainly is not even in the same solar system as Half Life Alex. It does have potential, but unless they redesign the controls entirely, it's never going to improve in any meaningful way.
Boneworks is trying to be Portal, but substituting the portal gun for firearms. The gun play is actually half decent. It doesn't require two hands for a gun like a rifle, but if you don't hold it two handed it sprays all over the place. I think that should be an option you can turn off. This is one of those studios who try to create a game in a very specific way and if you don't want to play their game the way it was "intended to be played" then too bad for you, because we're too high and mighty to give you the option for how you want to play the game you paid for. Another irritating issue with the controls is how weight effects objects. The heavier objects are slow moving when you try to either wield them or move them. For example, there's sledge hammers you can use as a melee weapon. I prefer my melee weapons to all move at the same speed in VR. I like how Bethesda implemented their melee system. The heavier weapons will only register swings so quickly to account for their weight. In this game, they move super slow. They try to warn you early on in the game by telling you to basically, "pretend you're swinging a heavy sledge hammer in real life". I hate that. There should be an option to change that. The same issue is problematic with other heavy things you have to move and interact with in the world like boxes. The heavy ones move super slow and you can't lift them above chest height, which is disorienting, and just comes across as irritating instead of clever. It makes moving anything a chore that gets on your nerves when things don't go well, which is frequent. Another issue with controls is the locomotion, it's crap. When you start this game, they have a warning that tells you "this game is for expert VR players". Well I definitely fall into that category. I have hundreds, if not thousands of hours in VR. So I am very experienced in VR games spanning back to the beginning of VR to the latest games. Moving around is a pain. There are several options for how you move, which is good, but it doesn't help when you character either moves at a crawl or, when running, feels like you're floating instead of running and you can get hung up on even tiny bits of debris on the ground. There's literal trash littering the ground all over the place in game and if you bump into it, it slows you down to barely moving. So maneuvering around all this trash is just annoying. Then there's jumping and crouching, OMG! It's terrible! Instead of a simple jump function, you have to hold in the button until your character "charges up" his jump and then you can get a full height jump. Otherwise you'll only get a partial jump. So jumping quickly between multiple platforms is virtually impossible, because you need time to charge your jump. There is a slow motion button. Maybe you can upgrade it later, but early on you have like 2 seconds of slow motion time and it's basically useless. Crouching is even worse than jumping. You press down on the analog stick to crouch, which would make sense, but instead of simply crouching, there's like a variable amount of how far you can crouch. Which, in theory, sounds good, but in practice, it's just makes it more difficult. And the controls are just clunky, they're not smooth, they're not even remotely good. Like an amateur who never played a VR game before designed the controls. Interacting with the lighter objects in game that move at the same speed as your physical body works pretty well. But you have any inventory that you can either bring up by using a button, which is what I prefer, or you can try to store and retrieve items from your inventory by placing them in "pockets" placed along side your body. You also get ammo in this same way. Well, the problem is it doesn't work reliably. When you start the game, they recommend that the game is intended, or should be played, seated. But can be played standing if you prefer. So I'm playing seated, but the problem with that is that retrieving items from your inventory while seated is unreliable. I go to grab a clip when I'm in a fire fight, and nothing. I have to try repeatedly before it allows me to get a clip, or I can pull up my inventory and get one that way, which does work, but just takes a lot longer. The same issue also causes you to drop your stuff a lot. Which ordinarily really isn't a problem, but you can drop stuff in a spot that makes it impossible to retrieve.
The way the save system works you have to toss the items you want to keep in a bin and then save, otherwise if you have to revert to your save, your inventory will be empty aside from ammo, which also acts as a currency. It's just tedious having to empty out, and then pick back up, my inventory every time I save.
There are some clever aspects to the game play, most of which have been ripped off from other games, especially Portal. Or drawn inspiration from if you're being polite. I mean, getting a VR version of Portal would be amazing. But this game isn't that.
This is basically a navigation puzzle game. Some of the puzzles are interesting and challenging, but the terrible controls ruin the experience. If the controls were as refined as some other VR games, this would be a great game.
The graphics are ok, nothing special. The game seems to be decently optimized and stable. I haven't experienced any crashing or stability issues.
If the clunky controls wouldn't bother you, then sure, this game is worth checking out. I intend to finish it. But I definitely wouldn't buy this game at full price. Given the option, I would still keep the game versus getting a refund. The studio is still working on the game, which is good as long as that lasts. If they either improved the controls or made some of the more irritating aspects of them optional, that would satisfy most of my complaints.\t\t\t\t\t
