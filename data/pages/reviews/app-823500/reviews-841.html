Ahem, anyway, onto an actual serious review. So, is the game fun? Extremely. I have somehow managed to get 18 hours played into the game in the span of no more than 3 days, even with a low-end (ish) computer and VR headset (Oculus Quest/Link), so it's only fair to say I really like the game. The physics are great, the graphics, even for low quality, are good, but its main problem is the sandbox mode and its story.
The sandbox mode.. isn't what you would expect. You actually have to unlock each individual map and item, by playing through the story. Now thankfully, there are save files out there that have most of, if not all, the items and maps unlocked, but then there are the achievements which you don't get.
And the story. For what it is right now (and I doubt it'll change later on), the ingame levels and physics puzzles are great for showing what Boneworks is capable of, but the actual story.. you know, the plotline and stuff.. It's pathetic. The cutscenes are fine, but most of the time I ended up skipping them.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI saw the hype around this game on YouTube long before its release, so it was on my list of games to buy for a while. It was on sale during the summer sale so I picked it up, but I didn't have a chance to play it until today. And I have to say, I'm not impressed.

Issue 1: pacing. I get that a game with so many mechanics needs to give you a tutorial, but my God was it boring. I almost quit before even making it through the museum. I thought maybe I had made a wrong selection and chose tutorial instead of new game or something. But no, thats just the start of the game.

Issue 2: physics. I know it is supposed to be a big revolutionary thing that this VR game is all physics based, and they went out of their way to make heavy objects feel heavy. But as the end user it just comes across as feeling laggy. Trying to hit an enemy with a sledge hammer feels awful, and even with lighter objects (golf club) it just doesn't feel like there is any power behind the swing because the swing is so much slower in game than irl. Moving objects around isn't bad, but interacting with them can be a pain in the butt. I positioned a box with no problem, but then spent a good 5 minutes trying to jump onto it, and then off of it and onto a ledge. The box kept trying to tip over and i'd slide off and not get the height i needed from the jump to make it onto the ledge. The ledge was reachable, so theoretically I could have just climbed my way up using my hands, but when I can actually manage to get that to work it just feels like a glitchy mess.

Issue 3: grip buttons. If i had index controllers, then holding objects would feel natural im sure. But having to squeeze the grip buttons on my Vive wands the entire time i want to hold onto any object besides a gun is ridiculous. I looked in the options and didn't see any way to change this. It seems like it wouldn't be a big deal to either allow you to just click them once to grab and click them again to drop, or even just use the triggers for the whole hand with melee weapons, instead of trigger for index finger and grip button for the rest. And again, this is for the Vive wands. It might not be that bad with the other controller types.

Issue 4: AI. I only had very limited experience with the AI in the game. I had the null bodies (i think they were called) stumble towards me and attack, and right before I quit, I saw some kind of armed soldier bots come out and attack the null bodies. That battle was, to be frank, pathetic. Multiple entities with automatic long guns against shuffling unarmed entities. And yet several of the soldier bots died. I watched one of them turn around and just ignore all the null bodies until one smacked him in the back of the head.

Issue 5: gunplay. This is my biggest issue with the game. The pre-launch videos I saw back in the day primarily were about the gunplay of the game, since shooters are by far my most played genre. But even just from the one room in the museum, I could tell how underwhelming it was. For reference, I play a lot of H3VR and also quite enjoyed HLA. Compared to the gun handling in those games, Boneworks is just dull. Having to tear the empty magazine out of every gun simply doesn't make sense. That's not how most guns work. Same with requiring the use of the charging handle/slide instead of having a bolt release. No one who knows what they're doing uses the charging handle on an AR15 while doing a reload. And racking the slide on the pistols usually results in smacking my controllers together. If there was a way to drop the mag or release the bolt/slide that didn't involve just grabbing it with my off hand, I couldn't figure it out. All the directions on the thumb pad are for movement, so i can't imagine what it could have been. Part of me wants to complain about having the ammo for both pistols and both SMGs be counted as the same, despite all using different mags, but i can let that one slide in the name of streamlined gameplay. Though I did often times end up grabbing (and dropping) a mag when I tried to grab the gun tucked under my left arm. Which BTW, typically when you're using shoulder holsters, you are doing a cross draw. You grab the gun on your left with your right hand and vice-versa. But iirc you couldn't actually do that. But maybe I just wasn't grabbing in just the right place.

But the unforgivable sin is their representation of the MP5. The charging handle on the HK MP5 is non-reciprocating. It doesn't move unless you are manually moving it. But In Boneworks, not only does it fly back and forth while you're firing it, but it even pops up into the locked open position when the mag is empty. The smallest amount of research, a *single* youtube video, would have showed them how the gun actually operates. I saw this issue in one of the pre-launch videos, and hoped it would be fixed before launch. I even left a comment pointing it out in hopes that they would be made aware of the issue. But here we are 7 months after launch and its still there. To a lot of people that might seem like not a big deal. But to me is just comes across as being lazy. If you want to make up your own guns like HLA and make them operate however you want then fine. But if you're going to put real world guns in your game, then you better do the research to find out how they operate. Anton (maker of H3VR) has shot just about every single gun he can get his hands on through various rental places like Battlefield Vegas in order to get first hand experience with how the guns function and handle. And for guns that he can't find he does copious amounts of research on them before putting it in the game. Now, I can't expect every dev to do that amount of exhaustive research for their game. What Anton does is above and beyond for sure. But according the the Boneworks wiki, there are 7 guns in the game. 2 of which are just variations of 2 other guns (M16/MK18, MP5/MP5K) so in terms of how the operate, just 5. Would it have been that hard to watch 5 youtube videos before releasing the game? To me, having such a glaring issue make it to the release just speaks volumes about the priorities that the devs had.

Its a shame because this was supposed to be the first big game that everyone could point to and say "this is the kind of game VR is capable of" but to me it is just a mess. Which is why I couldn't help but laugh when I saw the second tag on the game's steam page was "masterpiece." There is nothing about the game that makes me want to continue playing. The melee combat feels sludgy, the gunplay is clunky and unrealistic, and locomotion for anything other than flat ground just feels like a chore. I requested a refund since I've only played for just over an hour, but since I bought it so long ago they refused to issue it to me. In any case, I won't be revisiting it. Maybe I'll watch a let's play to see what that guy in the intro was doing.\t\t\t\t\t
