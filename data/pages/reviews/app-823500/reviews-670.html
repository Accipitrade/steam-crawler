\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay so this was one of the first proper VR games I played I got it not long after I got my VR pc and didn't touch it for a while after I first played it. The main reason for this is that I had not yet grown my VR legs and wasn't used to the movement in VR which made me quite sick. Now here I am with 1,000 something hours in Steam VR and I've been able to play Boneworks but I kind of held off on playing it because other VR games got in the way. That was kind of a mistake, as of writing this review it's been about an hour since I've beaten it and the longer I go without playing it the more I think about the game. So I'll start with the things that I liked about this game. The singleplayer story mode is very interesting. It doesn't have a lot in terms of cutscenes and voice acting but this is good as it adds to the mysterious tone that the game carries throughout the campaign. The movement is very immersive and well done to the point where I would like turn and move in real life only to eventually remove my Index and then be totally confused as to why I'm facing where I'm facing. The gun play is great I don't think there's another game where it's so satisfying to reload or pull out your mag before a gunfight to see how many bullets you have left. The sound design is fantastic from the guns to the music and enemy sounds it's all very well done and lends itself very well to immersing you into the game. The puzzles are challenging enough to make you think, but not so challenging that it frustrates you and makes you want to put the game down. Also with the puzzles there are a lot of ways to solve them leaving most of the puzzle solving to your imagination which is just great. I don't have a lot of bad things to say about this game really the only one I can think of is that at some points I noticed my rifle swayed sometimes even though I wasn't moving my hands. I'm not sure if this is an issue with my base stations, controllers, or the game itself so it doesn't even feel like it is worth mentioning but yea.


TL;DR: All in all I'd say in my opinion this is probably my 3rd favorite VR game. If you've got your VR legs then I'd heavily recommend this game even if you're new to VR get it and give it a shot it is a lot of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
