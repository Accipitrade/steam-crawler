-Go back through the campaign and try to get all ammo possible (to unlock the Utility Gun and Nimbus Gun on the time tower)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really loved Boneworks on my initial playthrough but coming back to it multiple times later, trying to go back and find the new content and unlockables and replaying the game after coming from so many other VR polished titles, its just not worth the headache.

The biggest pull for this game is the full body VR physics and while great on paper, it will fight you every step of the way. Every puzzle, every climb, every vault or jump, your body will fight against you and you will get stuck in walls, stuck in doors, pushed off things you shouldnt, move wrong from incorrect IK, fail to grab ledges, feet slip off ledges, etc.

It never ends and the body breaks immersion more than it gives from the constant fighting against the world makes movement a huge chore.

There's other gripes in the game such as:
- the poor gun controls that has no mag eject and you can either a) mod it in b) manually pull the mag and slap a new one in or c) use the weird system where bringing a mag close to the inserted one will eject it and put a new one in

- the best sandbox weapons, the developer ones shown in the videos on Steam with the physgun and such, require you to horde ammo in a playthrough to purchase it from a vending machine at the end of the game because ammo is currency for buying guns and the dev weapons are so extraordinarily expensive, you cannot get them when using guns constantly.

- the clunky melee. If you dont have full range of motion to swing a melee weapon with some considerable force behind it (because damage is applied via impact velocity of the weapon hitting the target) then melee simply will not work for you and you will constantly just be love tapping enemies with crowbars

- wonky save system where you grab pre-placed save stations with your hands and dump all your items you wish to save (generally yknow, your entire loadout) onto the platform next to it. Saving isnt just click a button and go, you now need to dump all your items, grap the handles, activate, and regrab all your equipment and rearm yourself to continue, making it an annoying ritual to perform everytime you come across a save station

- more of a nitpicky opinion but the preconfigured attachments on the weapons acting as "variations" is really off putting and honestly I'd rather just have attachments be working items i can attach to the gun, letting me build out a nice M4 instead of tossing one with a laser to get one with an ACOG\t\t\t\t\t

I WANT SEPARATE BODY SLOTS TO PUT THE STUPID SANDBOX UNLOCKABLES!!!!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs of right now, BONEWORKS is probably one of the better VR experiences out there. This game shows you why VR is the future and that the experience that we've always dreamt of is finally here. This game will make you excited about what is coming to VR, and it definitely holds its own with respect to half life alyx. Its main strength is definitely the combat, for it is very satisfying and dynamic. Whether you're in a firefight or fighting off zombies with your bare hands, the combat is always a unique and exciting experience and I would recommend this game for that alone. It is just a blast! The game's story has a nice variety of levels and scenarios that do a great job showing off the capabilities of VR. It almost feels like a demo that is the length of a full game because it seems to constantly try to innovate and test out new VR experiences. There are also different modes aside from the story such as sandbox and arena, so if you're the kind of person who likes to spend hours messing around, this is the perfect game for you.

While this is overall a great experience, there are certain parts of the game that I wasn't so thrilled with. Sometimes I was downright frustrated. My main complaint is the levels can seem flat out poorly designed at times. The game will have you backtrack and it is full of long, tedious climbing sections. Sometimes the climbing can be fun and elaborate, but it often feels like an annoyance. I also wasn't impressed with most of the game's puzzles, which is quite disappointing because physics puzzles have so much potential in VR. The puzzles lacked creativity and I was left feeling unsatisfied after solving them.

Despite its flaws this is a must own for VR.\t\t\t\t\t
