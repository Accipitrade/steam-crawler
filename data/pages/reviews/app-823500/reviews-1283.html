\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS IS WHAT VR DESPERATELY NEEDED!

So the main problems I've had with VR games, are that they are far too short (tech demos), or are not thorough enough in their design. In Boneworks there is so much to do... you go down one path, and you see 2 more paths, you start down that path and think, wait... I could have gone up there, and it always seems like there's 2+ paths for the same objective, which makes the game feels excellently non-linear. Most of the time you don't know if you're going in the right direction, but that's what's so fun. You're so immersed in exploring that it doesn't matter where the right way to go is, you're just going and don't worry about it. So much to interact with and so many ways to do the same thing. And oh my god the PHYSICS. The physics are like the icing on the cake for me. Every object has weight to it, which makes melee weapons a welcome challenge to learn. I absolutely love being able to pick up a lid-less trash can and just put it over an enemy's head and simultaneously grab them by the feet so I can hold them sideways and spank them for being bad xD

So now on to the minor bad things. The controls for the Oculus Touch controllers aren't the best they could be. The slo-mo button can only be pressed when standing still, since you have to let go of the thumbstick to press it, and the slo-mo only stays on when it's held. When making a fist, you have to lay your thumb on the button for your thumb not to stick out, it's hard not to press the buttons (not that it affects your ability to make a fist, but you just look like Elaine Benes from Seinfeld when she dances lol). That could be easily fixed by setting the so far unused touchpads to curl your thumbs.

TL;DR 10/10 Must-have VR game. You know how everyone says that Half-Life 2 is a must-have PC game? This is one of those games but for VR. I suggest checking the system requirements carefully.

EDIT: This edit does NOT contain spoilers for Boneworks, but it talks about a property of the story mode, read at your own risk. I found that the length of Boneworks wasn't quite enough. I was expecting Half-Life 2 level length, considering that it resembles Half-Life in many ways. I believe that Boneworks would do well with some DLC. New levels, new puzzles, new weapons, more enemy variety, etc.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo two hours into BONEWORKS
+Everything is interactable, and it feels like some secret sauce to make a VR game feel really good
+This is the first full game that feels like it was designed for the Index controllers. The finger tracking and grip sensor is very natural. I don't know how the hell you'd play it on Vive wands. This is also the first game that uses the analog-ness of the grip sensor in a way that doesn't feel forced. For example, if you grab a bat and want to choke down on the grip, you just loosen your grip without releasing it and the object will slide in your hand the way you want it to. It's the dumbest thing but if feels ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ magic
+It feels like there's some meat on it's campaign. There's top many 'experiences' in vr, so it's nice to get a thing you can sink your teeth into.
+It's very much a Half Life game
-Movement feels like you're leading your character around on an elastic band. I get why the hands are that way but the jumping feels real crappy.
-Holy ♥♥♥♥ this game eats CPU for breakfast, my i7-9750H is below min spec to the point that they advise you in game that it might cause problems. I've only gotten a chance to play it on my laptop, so I will have to try my desktop (Ryzen 5 2600X) to see if it's more of a clock speed thing (2.6 vs 4)
-I've had weird stutter with the FMV videos that play in the background. The intro cutscene completely crapped out, and the odd thing was that the game was running perfectly fine. It's also running on an NVMe drive so I don't think disk bandwidth would have been a problem either.
-The load times are BAD. It takes 2-3 minutes to load a level and that's off a NVMe drive. These loads are also while you're fully strapped in, so it's not like you can alt tab out to do something else while it loads.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR In 5 hours this took the title of my favorite VR game away from Blade & Sorcery (Sorry KospY). It is also the only VR game that I have played for a solid 4 hours straight, give or take, with a few short breaks.

The Index controllers are taken full advantage of and makes the game feel much better than with the Vive wands, (I own both). As other people have said, the leg mechanics are a bit odd, with your feet often clipping into surfaces when trying to climb them. I find the virtual crouch feature is only helpful when climbing and makes me nauseous in any other scenario. I will admit the game can feel "dead" at some times with lack of sound or even ambient music. Sound Design in general seems poor giving an unsatisfying thunk when hitting a enemy though this doesn't bother me all that much.

The puzzles feel great and there feels like an infinite amount of solutions to them. There was one puzzle that I worked through and it took me a few minutes but wasn't hard. Right as I got to the end I found a blue pipe, grabbed on and climbed to the end expecting a secret but instead it was where I started. Needless to say it allows you to completely skip the puzzle. This was not the only skip I've seen and I am curious to see if the speedrunning community comes to Boneworks.

At this point I have just started rambling on, so I will just stop to say 5/5 and glad this game lived up to my 9 months of hype.
Congrats to Brandon and SL0 for the amazing game they produced for having a small development team.\t\t\t\t\t
