\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor context, I played this on a Quest 2 over AirLink to SteamVR. I made it about 5 minutes into the story mode before getting super frustrated by the physics and how slow rolled the tutorial is. I made it into the break room and a shelf fell on me. This locked my player model inside it, unable to climb out. "Ah, a hammer. Maybe this is destructible." Nope. Walking through the game's locomotion or in real life just moves the view model with you since your character is unable to pass through objects. I spent 10 minutes button smashing my quest controllers trying to free myself or find a menu for resetting.

Eventually I was able to free myself without finding a menu, but my character was stuck crouching. Bending down to touch my actual floor, the view model would again follow, but couldn't recognize the floor was 2 feet from where it's supposed to be and would stay crouched when I stood up. I tried resetting view from Oculus and Steam: no dice. "Guess I'll just crouch walk through this for now..."

Shortly after, I am told how to run, and how to open my settings menu finally. It's unclear if the option was simply locked or if the button mashing was too erratic to trigger the menu. I would consider this still super early in the story mode and the game has begun to dump exposition through museum exhibits before even fully explaining the controls to me. At this point, the locomotion is really annoying me and I am skipping right past the "story" to try and learn how to actually play this thing.

The two options for joystick orientation are called something like head and fixed. Fixed creates cardinal directions and your joystick always points to them. For example, you enter a room and north is ahead of you. Pressing forward on the joystick will move you towards north. If you physically turn around or quick turn with the other joystick, your directions are now reversed so pressing forward on the joystick steps you backwards to North even though you are facing South. The other option is fixed to the view model instead of the cardinal directions. Stick forward moves you towards exactly where you're looking. Joystick backward moves you exactly away from where you're looking. This is simple to conceptualize but it makes strafing while looking around extremely difficult because you would have to compensate which direction the joystick is pointing while you look around to maintain the same walking path. Trying to walk down a straight hallway and I had to have my head turned to the side or else I'd walk at an angle into a wall. Small but annoying. Ideally it would be a mixture of both options. I don't remember which game specifically but someone has it so you start with the view model as "North" and the joystick is locked to those cardinal directions so you are free to look around with the relationship of "joystick forward is this direction" still fresh. And every time you let go of the joystick, those cardinal directions are reset to the current view model.

Also the physics and handling of objects felt super bad for a game with this much praise of physics and handling. Trying to pull a door open almost always resulted in it slingshoting forward but without the momentum to keep going and stay open so I could walk through. Throwing objects was very inconsistent. Climbing up the wall intended to be climbed took way more tries than it should have. "I've pressed the 'grab wall trigger' while my fist is smashed against it so why not grab the wall?" "Best I can do is fall all the way down, chief"

I've spent four times as long writing this review than playing so maybe I gave up too early. I'm starting to feel like the games journalist that couldn't grasp dash jumping in cuphead. Regardless, I think my experience would be considered an extreme edge case but it was the most exhausting 30 minutes I've ever spent in VR.
