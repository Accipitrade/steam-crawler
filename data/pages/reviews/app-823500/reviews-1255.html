\r\n\t\t\t\t\t( - Boneworks and its saving issue - )


The graphics are extremely good, the feeling of guns are great, the enemies are fun and sometime challenging to deal with.

With other words the game has a lot of great stuff, but there's also a few problems. The biggest problem, there's no way to save, you have to complete the whole level to "save". You're technically not even saving, you're just unlocking the next level. Which makes it so you can play that level again next time you open the game. Made me extremely annoyed when I played 1 hour and 30 min on one level, and wanted to take a break. And then released it doesn't save, guess what, all my progress was gone. All guns, all weapons, all ammo that I used time to find, and all the puzzles I solved. When I click continue game, I would love to just spawn back were I was in a level, with all my guns, weapons and ammo. Please add a save button, that would make the game a lot better, and that alone would be enough for me to update this to a "yes" I recommend this game.

The save problem is biggest one in my opinion, but I have a few small problems I would like to talk about as well. I can't really say anything about the experience with index and oculus. But at least the vive I had a few annoying problems with, you can't toggle for holding a weapon. So you have to constantly hold the grip button, which get's annoying when you're playing a level that can take 30 min - 2 hours. Climbing feels weird, and is pretty annoying to do, but again not the that bad, just nitpicking right now. Another really weird thing on vive, is that I wasn't able to crouch without jumping after I released the button. The crouch button was the same button that you use for charging for a jump. So every time I crouched I would jump afterwards, felt weird and I don't think it's supposed to work like that.

I actually think Boneworks is great game, with a lot of potential, it's just that losing over an hour of progress isn't fun. Do a few fixes and updated, and I'm sure this game be amazing :D.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never leave reviews, but I really felt like these guys deserve some praise.

There is so much to unpack here. The game is amazing. It definitely has its own set of flaws such as the "weight" of certain objects or certain geometries that could be refined or the "story"; it is by no means a perfect game. This is something other than a game though. It will be talked about for many years to come being as it is what is pushing the industry forward. The height at which Stress Level Zero has set the bar is so incredible that this will likely become a foundation for many VR titles for decades. It is easy to see this formula being carried over in any number of future titles. Want a gritty, immersive cop/detective game? This formula will work. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe hype is real. This is the only full "feature length" VR game I've ever played, and I've played a few that claimed to be just that.

Do NOT purchase this game if this is your first time experiencing VR. I imagine that the concepts introduced in this game may cause a beginner to fall or become ill. Personally, I don't experience ANY ill effects (although I am what you would call a "seasoned" VR user), and I have a feeling that has a lot to do with how well the game was made.

No it's not perfect, but I doubt any deity of your choosing couldn't do a much better job. Anything that you could logically interact with, is interactable and follows a fairly accurate representation of physics. Throwing mechanics, combat, climbing, bullet physics, puzzle solving, and pure exploration blend together almost flawlessly as you wander about this vast and mysterious world with childlike wonder. Want to smash a coffee cup like you are hitting a baseball? Go for it. Need to move a heavy box, act like it's heavy and you'll have a much better time. Not my words, but the creators of the game have done 99% of the hard work in creating a realistic and reactive world. All we have to do is work the 1% and pantomime like you really are lifting a heavy box. DON'T GET FIXATED ON THE FACT THAT IF YOU MOVE FASTER THAN YOU LOGICALLY SHOULD BE ALLOWED TO, IT LOOKS A LITTLE FUNKY.

I would say that this game would not be for someone who has very little video game experience in general. There are concept riddle throughout the game that make a LOT of sense for "gamers" but mean nothing to the average individual. While it may seem self-explanatory, not everyone is familiar with the idea that they will have to search for a key before the door will unlock. Some people may not understand what objects need batteries and what the batteries even look like. In other words, there are very little prompts when you have found something important for the game and its progress, and only (very) subtle clues for general guidance.

TL;DR
Again, lives up to the hype. Childlike wonder, amazement, curiosity, and epiphanies all come included. Under-priced, in my opinion. Only complete game for VR I've seen.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a good game that is enjoyable and a good experience. Although the game is good, there are some pretty severe problems.
I have been playing this game on the Oculus Quest which already means i'm not going to get an amazing and wonderful experience. Also, my PC is not the greatest thing ever, but its good enough to run most games like PUBG or Ark: Survival Evolved on good graphical settings. My PC's specs barely met the minimum requirements for this game and I was told that I shouldn't buy it as I couldn't run it. Well I took a chance and it runs decent. Its not blurry, the FPS stays good for the most part. Main issue is that whenever I go to a new place, a new district, there is extreme lag. Horrible lag that lasts for about 5-10 minutes. This is unfortunately my computers fault but I just want to point that out. Also, there is a pretty bad glitch where whenever I grabbed something, like a crowbar, my hands would glitch in front of me and were stuck there no matter where I moved the controller. I wasn't able to grab stuff either when this happened. Fortunately, I resolved this issue by uninstalling and then reinstalling the game.

Next thing I want to point out is that the game is good. The physics are awesome and fun. But, the story if very lackluster. I find myself wandering for 30 minutes wandering having no where to go, and other people have also pointed this out. I was hoping that the story for this game would be something sick, where you are like John Wick and gotta assassinate the people that tried to mess with you. Instead you are going against enemies that don't even move and die in like 2 shots. I feel bad for the people that hyped up this game a lot, I was honestly really excited for this game always watching the videos on Node to see what was going on with it. It looked amazing. I feel bad for the developers that they spent like 3 years developing a boring story. Everyone was expecting something amazing, and we all got something that was just good. I would say that I am new to VR and I haven't played to many games so this is one of my better experiences that i've had. But, I have played games like Superhot and Beat Saber and honestly, I've had just as much fun if not more fun on those games than I have had on Boneworks so far.

So, what i'm trying to say is that the game is just good. It will give you a good experience and an okay 6 hours of story. I guess my rating on this game is diminished by the fact that it does sometimes lag for me. My review probably doesn't matter in the long run because this game is being flooded with reviews saying "Wow. Just wow. This is the best game i've played in 10 years!!!11! I have devoted my life to this game and already have 40 hours on it! Can't wait to see what they do in the future!!!!!"
We are all in the honeymoon phase right now. Soon however, we will truly see that this game has its issues just like every other game that has ever existed.

I rate this game a 7/10
I believe that it can get better.\t\t\t\t\t
