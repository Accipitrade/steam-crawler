This was my first VR game and I'm so glad it was, it's convinced me that VR is a medium of game experience delivery that's worth the massive amount of effort it probably takes to develop. I really enjoy that this game allows for fluid movement of your character with a joystick instead of the teleportation-based movement that many VR games feel the need to implement (not that that's always a bad thing). It's a bit lacking in story though, this game feels mostly like it's showing off how excellently the physics engine is implemented, with the cherry on top being the fact that your character is actually a physical object in-universe instead of a pair of floating hands and eyeballs. This emphasis on the importance of physics leads to the creation of many puzzles and obstacles that wouldn't be possible in most VR settings, and makes this game really stand out.\t\t\t\t\t
