\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBasically:
A physics based puzzle game with some shooty bits and some stabby or whacky bits.

The good:

Physical interactions between enemies and the player allow for immense creativity when killing stuff and is insanely satisfying.

The game makes objects heavy using some kind of magic. I don't actually know how it works, but if you don't treat a heavy object in game like it's actually heavy, you'll just drop it.

You can climb a lot of surfaces which is really fun and takes just the right amount of effort (although that's probably a matter of opinion)

The puzzles are hard but not too hard - I didn't have to look anything up.

Gun play is pretty well done and you forget about the fact there aren't many guns quite easily, and don't forget, thanks to the physics your trusty rifle can make a good substitute for a hammer if needs be.

The AI isn't too hard, isn't too easy.

There are often several ways to progress.

The Dystopian art style fits and is pretty well done.

It runs well (Solid 90fps at 1440x1440 for the most part with gtx1060 3gb and i7-4770s) - Although if you do have a GPU with less than 4GB of VRAM, I'd drop it to 1200x1200 just to be sure, as it can struggle in some particularly large rooms. I only had issues in one room near-ish the end of the game.
-EDIT: A texture resolution setting has been added, allowing you to drop the resolution to decrease VRAM usage. Half runs perfectly everywhere on my 3gb 1060 now. I can imagine the lowest setting (Quarter) Potentially running on even the 2gb 1050 (At a push)

Loading screens are very infrequent.

The balance of puzzles and combat is pretty good - the combat acts sorta like breaks for your brain.

The bad:

No mid level saves meaning you have to play probably around an hour to reach the next level, possibly longer (Although I've heard this is going to be added quite soon)
-EDIT Saving has now been added, and you can ignore this point.

The gun variety is limited, and some guns are just the same with different attachments - although as i said earlier, this is easy to forget.

Sometimes my hands get stuck inside objects and are difficult to remove.

Sandbox mode is a little on the bland side. (Although judging by the wall were you insert a key to unlock it, it looks like more will be added, as there are more sockets for keys you can't get yet)

Story is a little bland, although it almost doesn't matter.

Conclusion:
All in all, this game is so good that it's few hiccups end up just being ignored by me when playing. The only thing that does actually have an impact on the overall experience for me is the lack of proper saving, although like i say, I've heard that's being added towards the end of this month.

Thanks to it's incredible physics puzzles and combat, this is one of the first games to me that isn't unique just because it's VR. Games like pavlov for example, only really have the fact they're VR going for it. But this has more. It has a genuinly unique combat system that I've never seen before.
10/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t- Played on Oculus Rift S

Definitely best VR game out there so far. (Waiting for Half Life: Alyx).

This for me is the first "real" VR game, whereas other VR games feels more like incomplete experiences (keeping in mind I've played VR for about 3 months prior to playing this). It is the most complete and immersive game that I've played in VR. Also probably the most fun I've had in any game (Both VR and not) for a long time.

Soundtrack, atmosphere, gun-play, and physics are all amazing. Story a bit lacking, but I hope they release some future content to make the game even more worth while. Puzzles was also easy enough for me to understand, but hard enough for a decent challenge. Progression in difficulty is also very good, starting out learning the game, to where you blast through some levels like John Wick.

You can really feel the inspiration from Half-Life, and Portal, but the game still differentiates itself enough to be its very own interesting game. The inspiration, for me, only adds to the game.

The warning they have put about this not being for players just getting into VR, is something you definitely have to take into account. Not only because of the motion sickness, but also for the overall enjoyment of the game. Because if you don't fully understand where VR games comes from, you can't really appreciate how fun this game is.

TLDR - You can carry radios, playing music, on your back. Best game - 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR This game is the future of VR - or could be, once haptic feedback is a common thing. Very fun, very high quality, and has an actual storyline! Also the tutorial is amazing, for VR as a whole and for the game itself!
Just make sure your computer can run it well or it will make you ill (just like any VR game).

---------------------
Full rundown, because I do have quarrels with it:

In most VR games, you can swing your weapon as fast as you can swing your hand. This can break the game (Skyrim for example) or just make the game feel unrealistic for obvious reasons.

At the time of this writing, imo this game is a tech demo for the future possibilities of VR as a whole. For anyone unfamiliar, this is because Boneworks uses a "realistic" system of weight and object collision and item grasping.

When you swing a sledgehammer, you actually swing it slowly and pick up speed as if it has weight behind it.

Want to swing faster? Hold it near the head. Want to swing it harder? Hold it near the end. Or, as is the correct way to swing a sledgehammer, hold the end firmly and let your hand near the head slide with the swing. That option is actually available!

Your arms and body will touch and be stopped by items. No more swinging your fists through walls, they are actually there. This is an incredible idea, and one I'm surprised didn't exist before this.

However, there are downsides that probably explain why. Without true physical feedback, it's hard for someone to judge how heavy an item is. In particular, there are items (sledgehammers for example) that I know for a fact I can swing faster and stronger than the game allows. It feels like I'm a young teenager trying to swing this thing. It is very disorienting until you figure out what each item "weighs" in game.

Generally speaking, because of this, I am much more comfortable using the same low and middle-weight objects I can swing with relative ease for the whole game, and have to ignore the badass two-handers because they won't do what I want them to do.

While the developers have a haptic system in place, as far as I'm aware it only affects feeling in your hands when gripping (correct me if I'm wrong). In fact I don't see any possible way to affect weight/swing difficulty other than adding a "strength" slider into the game to adjust for each person's uniqueness.

That said, it's still an incredible piece of work, and well worth playing if you are trying to stay up to date on VR technology.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed through on the OG Vive + wands (which aren't too bad once you learn to not destroy your hands via trial and error, but Index would be a breeze).

There's a definite lack of story and not to forget mid-level saves; you either dedicate 30m - 1h to complete a level or just don't bother (till saves are implemented). Aiming with rifles is clunky given how physics interact with the stock + player model.

But the way you can interact with the world with physics, climbing, beating puzzles by completing them in multiple ingenious ways and more really makes the game. Want to push enemies over and execute them? do that. Want to slowly peek doors with one hand, gun in the other? do that. I also spent a lot of time just going through the levels postponing objectives to explore and find new stuff.

I don't have a lot of VR experience at all, so I can't say this is the best VR game to exist but I'd like to view it as where they are going, and that this is just a start. I for sure loved the hell out of this game, it's definitely one of my favourites.

Though I will say whoever put the last ladder in the game deserves to be shot (yes, wands I know).

8/10.\t\t\t\t\t
