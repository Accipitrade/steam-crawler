\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust real quick I wanna make it clear that I have played Half Life Alyx, but not very much so I wont compare Boneworks to that. Anyways, onto the review. Boneworks is a physics based sandbox game, and its really really fun. You can play the game sitting or standing, sitting for comfort standing brings out the maximum experience in the game. I would recommend standing if possible. Like I mentioned earlier the game is incredibly physics based, you could probably call the game physics instead of Boneworks and it wouldn't any difference at all. That said, though, the physics aren't the best. In the very first are you could pick up a bat, swing it into a crate, and the bat is now somehow glitching inside of the crate. You can pick almost anything in the game, even enemies. There are also a couple of platforming bits in the game, sort of, but they honestly aren't very good. I won't go any further into them, though. But how about the puzzles? For some people the puzzles are just an annoying part of the game that you have to get past if you want to play the rest of the game. For others, they're really fun bits of the game that are super rewarding to do. For me, I'm kinda mixed but I usually like the puzzles. What I don't think anyone could disagree with, though, is the music. It's amazing, really. My favorite is "Who Am I." Now onto the enemies. The enemies really aren't that hard usually. For many of them you can just walk past them, and there isn't a single thing in the game the can walk up stairs except for you. If something is blocking their way to get to you, they don't try to take another way they just keep walking at you. There's only two ranged enemies (three if you include the turret) and one of them will often kill themselves with their own ball of energy because of splash damage, and the other ones go-to tactic whenever you get to cover is to walk up to a nearby wall and just stay there until you shoot them. Oh, and the second one I mentioned is usually in groups but one shot to the head with the 1911 is a one shot. actually for some encounters why would you even use your gun the correct way when you could just smack them with it as they run by you? There is also very little enemy variety. An orange, low health zombie, an actual zombie with about medium health. It also has a version that throws balls of energy at you. There's one that's basically a headcrab but its called a headset, and the Ford, which is the only NPC in the game that's on your side. All of them have their strengths and weaknesses, all of them are more powerful in a group. If you want a challenge, I'd recommend zombie warehouse or the arena. When an enemy kills you, you don't actually die however. You have about ten seconds to kill an enemy, and if you can do so during that time span then you'll be fine. The weapons are very fun to mess around with, especially in sandbox mode. Most of them are balanced, and you would have no idea how fun it is to make up your own stealth kill in VR. The gunplay is also amazing and so realistic, I just wish they had added more gun types than just pistols and rifles. Although I don't think any VR gun can be as complex and realistic as H3VR (Hot Dogs, Horseshoes, and Hand Grenades) The game does have lore (and a LOT), but you really have to look up a lore video if you want to know. I hoped that after you beat story mode then everything would just be unlocked for sandbox mode, but I was very very wrong. I won't go into detail about it, but will surely take a while to do it all. As bad as I have talked about this game, I really love it. It was the one of the first actual big VR games, and the story mode isn't just an hour of story mode on a first playthrough. It's more like 4, and Stress Level Zero has put so much hard work into this game and I respect it so much. But what about the price? To be honest it's under priced. They made a $60 game and decided it would be $30. Heck, the trailer for the game is literally just someone messing around in the game. There isn't anything in the trailer you can't actually do yourself. If you have any sort of VR headset, please please buy this game it is so much fun.\t\t\t\t\t
