\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot exactly experienced with VR, but I grasp the concept well. Simply put it, this game pushed the boundaries of VR for its time. I can tell a lot of time was put into making every system and mechanic. Almost everything you see in each level can be interacted with. You see a box? Well there a multiple different ways you can interact with it: Break it, hold onto it, use it to get to higher platforms, shield yourself from attacks, throw it as an attack (Probably not the best idea), put it on a button to open something, block doors from closing, barricade a door with a bunch of enemies right on your tail, etc. One box can have so many interactions. That leads me to another reason why this game is so unique. You see, there is not just one way to beat a puzzle, or entire level, but there are multiple different ways you can beat it because of the physics and mechanics. Let's take for example a random warehouse with a locked door. The door needs a battery to be opened, so you find a battery somewhere around the warehouse, and plug it in. Go into the warehouse, fight some enemies, look for non-existent lore clipboards, and then go through the magic level shifting portal. If you hate boring battery plug in puzzles, then why not just break a fragile window, and climb in the warehouse. No battery finding needed. Or you can be a speedrunner and climb the warehouse into the next level. A game like BONEWORKS doesn't need you to find one, intended solution to beat a level, because there are multiple different ways you can beat a level. As to not make this review too long, I will start the conclusion. In conclusion, this game has less bone working, and more mind working. With many different choices of what to do when you see a wooden box, or when you come across a giant warehouse with a battery puzzle, your mind is at work trying to figure out what to do. Definitely 10/10 for a tech demo-ish story based-ish type game. I recommended it, unless you have motion sickness problems, or any health condition that may be triggered by this game. Always remember to read reviews, and learn about anything in the game that might cause bad health problems to occur. Hope this helps with your decision making.\t\t\t\t\t
