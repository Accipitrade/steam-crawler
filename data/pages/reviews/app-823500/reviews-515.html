\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti could only tolerate this game's story mode for two hours. i learned pretty early on that the phsyics--while they can feel pretty good--is ruined by this developer's eagerness to make that almost the entire aspect of the singleplayer story. it's weird, because ive seen videos and interviews of the team lead saying that his inspiration was half-life 2, and listening to the way he speaks about the Boneworks, I started to get the idea his vision was to make the "HL2 of VR." it's kind of like half-life, but if valve kicked the weakest part of the game into overdrive: the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ physics puzzles.

half-life 2 and its sequels are incredible. not because of the physics puzzles almost shoehorned in, but because of the story, worldbuilding, and pacing. boneworks does have a "story" but you'll forget what it is by the time you reach the fourth area because each level will have you stumbling around for the better part of an hour, and that's IF you want to unlock sandbox content, the only place where i found myself having fun. the most story i think i got was some discount let's play youtuber looking dude talking to me from outside the matrix on an old TV monitor, only after being thrown through a couple gauntlets of physics puzzles that are so simple yet so awkward and cumbersome to solve because this game does a really good job of making beginners and veterans alike sick with its jank.

what is this "jank" i'm talking about? well, it's none other than the body physics. the novelty of having actual arms in a VR game wears off really quick because it will make you realize why most VR games don't have that feature: VR tech is young, for one, and the average VR consumer is NOT READY for it at all. the only way you can pull this off so that feels good is with the aid of full body tracking, a pretty big, empty room with a high ceiling, and finally, minimal glitches. I am fortunate enough to have an appropriate space for VR--and even then--with all this space and a valve index, my arms glitched through each other, got stuck on objects, and when i looked down? i was 30 feet above ground attached to a wall doing a slav squat. your character model is locked in this slav squat whenever you climb something and by god if it isn't the freakiest thing ever. it makes even climbing two feet to get on a ledge look painful, and there are SO many sections you need to traverse vertically.

what you need to do if you have this game and are getting frustrated with the story mode, go download a 100% save and pop that bad boy into your save folder.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried to like this game so much, but it honestly felt like a chore to play. From what I heard, BONEWORKS was an outstanding and fun VR game. Yet to me it seemed soulless and real wonky.

First off I only got 2 hours in, whereas this is my second attempt to try and play Boneworks, as the first play through attempt I got tired of it as well. Though there was nothing to hook me. The game felt empty, traversing through levels had nothing interesting to offer yet the same janky physics puzzles over and over. There was no sense of weapon progression, as every weapon is instantly handed to you from the start, which some people may like but I however do not. There's only three enemies with not so well AI. Where as fighting them gets old incredibly fast. Melee weapons feel like rubber as they bounce around and don't feel weighted in a good way. There's no way to rebind controls, as you have to go into the steam vr controller settings to change it from there, so if you are wanting to move when in slow motion, you can't. Which is honestly incredibly lazy as people have complained about the lack of key bindings, and no change has happened. When attempting to purchase items from the shops, it uses ammo as a currency. You have two types of ammo, assault rife and pistol. Though when you finish putting in your current ammo, you can't switch the ammo type unless you pick up the other ammo type or weapon that uses that ammo. So you are stuck with ammo you are unable to spend. Movement is not fluid at all, it just seems really clumsy.

I don't usually post reviews in general, especially ranting and posting a negative review. But this game takes the cake for making me irritated. For a $30 price point, this is absolutely not worth it. There are way better VR games out there.\t\t\t\t\t
