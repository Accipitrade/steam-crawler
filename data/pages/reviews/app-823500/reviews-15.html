BONEWORKS Review

First off, I love this game to my heart's content
I can't believe this AAA style game was made
by a small team, but not just any team, a
passionate and amazing one.

Stress Level Zero is the best video game studio
yet, it's unbelievable that a small studio ran by
a VFX artist (Brandon J Laatsch, an amazing man
and former VFX artist for "freddiew" or now known
as "RocketJump") could be so damn well made.

This experience was made by 18 people that
put sweat and tears to make this amazing
game. at the time nothing like it could bring
it down, nothing was better than Boneworks.
It's still like that to this day, Bonelab just
doesn't live up to Boneworks, but that makes
total sense. Boneworks is a story and physics
driven journey as you play as Arthur Ford,
the security director and double agent of
Monogon Industries, and Gammon.
You traverse through MythOS, an AI
generated and simulated operating
system created by or powered by
the Boneworks Engine and The Void
or void energy.

Whilst Bonelab takes place in Fantasy Land,
a simulated world where 'peasants' live
their lives in a closed and sad environment
cutoff by the bounds of pure dread and desolateness.
While in this world you play as another peasant
living in the empty world. You suddenly break the
rules of the Fantasy Land rules and restrictions
created by groups or cults formed by other
'peasants'.

They are very different games and are trying to achieve
different goals in what they do.

Yet Boneworks is perfect because it makes sense from
the start that you're playing a very great and unique
experience.

All I'm trying to say is just buy the game and it's
amazing soundtrack for only $40 today.
It's unlike any other game you've played before.\t\t\t\t\t
