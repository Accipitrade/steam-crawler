\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an amazing game, and I had the money in my Steam wallet ever since I learned the exact release date. However, in this review I'm going to give some positives and negatives. So lets start off with the good things.

1. The game itself is a beautiful game, the story is told more through atmosphere than dialogue.
2. The guns feel absolutely amazing, to the point where I would say they're nearly, if not already, on the level of H3VR guns.
3. The physics are great, and hand-to-hand combat is immersive and satisfying.
4. The game is not hard on hardware
5. In-game slow-mo is difficult to pull off. This is good because you can't abuse it and makes it that much more satisfying to hit headshots.
6. The story is a good length at about 7.5-9 hours average.
7. The OST is amazing.

Now, before I get to the bad, I'm going to go over things that aren't necessarily bad, but just kinda.. inconvenient.

1. Holsters are a bit weird. After over 200 hours in Blade & Sorcery and over 72 hours in Pavlov it's a bit confusing, the holsters feel weird and sometimes don't work the way you want. However after a bit it gets fairly easy.
2. The game is fairly slow to start. This is good for new VR users but a bit of a pain for veteran VR players.
3. Climbing feels a little clunky.

Now for the bad.

1. Melee weapons feel really weird. I play on Oculus, but the two-handed melee weapons, specifically the bat and sledgehammer, feel hard to hold. The knives feel awkward too along with the swords.
2. The story. It really hurts to say this but the story is very hard to follow and is confusing. However the amazing gameplay makes up for this.

I have very few bad things to say about this game. It's definitely one of my favorite VR games in awhile and I'm glad I decided to get it.\t\t\t\t\t

Playing a fully physics based VR simulation is unlike any other VR game I've played. Its a sandbox of fun ideas and creative gameplay potential which only get's more fun as you play. It is really like experiencing VR for the first time again, you start to re-learn how to move and interact with a physical(virtual) world that reacts to your movements. See a bad guy? Every other VR game: shoot him or stab him from a distance. Boneworks: grab a trash can, pull the lid off, flip it upside down and slow-mo drop onto the dude stuffing him inside the bin. Then proceed to bash his knees in with a pop can. VR, baby!\t\t\t\t\t
