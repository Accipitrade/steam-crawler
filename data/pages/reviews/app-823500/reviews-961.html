\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI finally went ahead and picked up Boneworks seeing it was all the craze in VR recently. Having been a regular VR player since the rebirth of the tech in 2013, I can now safely say I'm utterly disappointed with and put off by the overall experience.

Don't get me wrong there are a lot of good things in Boneworks: You'll see a lot of physical interactions throughout the tutorial. On their own they're nothing we haven't seen before, but they're put together in a way that remains unique to this game. It has a "militaristic" feel and explores a variety of mechanics ranging from climbing, to physically interactable objects with an impression of weight to them, all the way to a fairly impressive yet imperfect IK body model and of course, traditional VR gun play. Many of the things you'll see through the long tutorial section are individually impressive. It's also clear this game has been made with heart and a lot of love for VR.

While I can't take away what this game brought to VR, for me, once all the mechanic demonstrations were done and all the components came together to try and form a game: it simply didn't work for me. Only after a short bit of gameplay did I start getting annoyed with it. Fidgetty remote grab mechanic, finnicky ammo/weapon holster/belt system, a slow mail-order-speed jump, terrible uncompelling VR melee and an insane over reliance on physical interactions with absolutely dreadful "parkour" like puzzles... playing Boneworks for long enough started feeling like doing VR chores, or more like being a rat being experimented on in a prototype maze. It never came together to feel like a game, at least not to me.

The biggest problem with Boneworks may not even be the chore-like gameplay, but rather the fact that there's a set of design choices that can easily lead the player to nausea: Most importantly there is phsyical push-back in this game. Walls and objects can "push back" thus causing uncommanded movement in your character. This happens very often especially in the puzzles where you graze walls, ledges and mechanisms. The uncommanded movement is almost instantly nauseating. Furthermore, the IK body model, while sometimes impressive, at most times feels like it's invading your personal space, and it can contribute to this nauseating feeling. This becomes especially true when you're using the "wall grab" mechanic which causes the IK body's arms to clip through your face. Last but not least the jump in this game has to be the single worst implementation of jumping in a VR game... it's slow, it's got a sort of sickening sway to it, and it's just overall undesirable. I've got some practically invincible VR legs and it's unusual for me to experience motion sickness, and in this case I'm not just saying "tHiS gAMe mAdE ME diZzY" with no basis; I listed above what I believe are the technical issues that lead to the discomfort I experienced.

Bottom line is I definitely did not enjoy Boneworks. I appreciated the heart that clearly went into making this game, and the things it had to show me early on, but ultimately it just doesn't amount to something I find fun, short of demonstrating a set of novel mechanics that are impressive on the short term. I'll admit despite my negative opinion I didn't mind giving the game a chance well beyond the maximum refund time because I do think the developers deserve support as they're clearly capable of making excellent VR games in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed on a Pimax 5K+ with index controllers, on the technicall side everything ran great.

First of giant motion sickness warning. I purchased this on release and when I first played it couldn't even get through the tutorial without getting motion sick. This was despite having dozens of hours in other smooth locomotion games such as Onward without getting motion sick. I gave it another try today since I noticed that playing a lot of HL:Alyx seems to have made my motion sickness vanish completely and now I could play this without getting sick.


The "physicality" of this Game means that if you physically collide with something in game the game instead of fading out your view simply stops your head from moving, which is super jarring. Also hand interaction with solid objects causes the headset to move around a bit. E.g. if you stand in front of a virtual wall and push your hand against the wall it will push you back instead of just stopping your virtual hand, again super jarring. This even happens during basic interactions such as opening doors so it is impossible to avoid.

So unless you are 100% unaffected by motion sickness don't get this. Even then the controls are super janky and you constantly get stuck on ♥♥♥♥ due to your "physical body".

The weapons that others seem to praise as realistic, are super basic. Weapons that should have select fire don't, there is no mag release button(you can either physically pull the mag out or eject it through a menu, nor a dedicated slide/bolt catch button(again either pull the slide back or just pull the trigger). Onward, Pavlov, H3, Half Life Alyx all have more realistic weapon controls.

The Inventory slots are also super janky, you often accidentally drop your weapons or need multiple tries to take them out of your inventory.

There is no free save function you can only save at checkpoints(or completed levels) and the game makes you take of all your gear and drop it into a basket if you want to keep it, which frankly is just annoying.

Even after just the tutorial I was sick of the music and there was nothing really that in anyway drew me into the story.

So in the end yes you can do a lot of stuff, but everything is janky so I definetly wouldn't recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
