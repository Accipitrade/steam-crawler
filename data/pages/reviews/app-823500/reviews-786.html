\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of my most favorite VR games for a few reasons.

One, the game is physics based. This is rad because the physics engine affects just about everything in the game making it a fantastic sandbox. If you wanted, you could gather damn near all the items in a level and stack them on top of each other to make a fort. You can fist fight enemies and throw them into walls. you can pick up a chair and that can be your weapon if you want.

and speaking of weapons, while there isn't a MASSIVE number of weapons you can use, there's still a good amount. Numerous guns ranging from assault rifles to 1911s. There's a bunch of melee weapons like the spear, frying pan, crowbar and others. All these weapons are found in the world either in weapon crates, lying around, or in the weapon vending machine that uses ammunition for currency. You can also unlock all those weapons and more for the various sandbox modes by exploring the levels and finding these little pods that contain a mini version of the item.

the story mode has a pretty non existent plot that I paid no mind. What kept my coming back was the gameplay and the freedom that is present in every level. You can handle these encounters with enemies however you want. If you dont want to, you never have to fire a single shot, which would be a sin because shooting in this game is so fun! the physical reaction enemies have to being shot is very satisfying. shooting an enemy in the leg a few times renders that leg useless and they wont be able to stand on it. things like this make this game almost infinitely replay-able, and modes like the wave based zombies and the arena keep things challenging and fun.

Whenever i play vr, I always jump on boneworks. Its a super fun game with quite a bit of replayability. give it a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
