\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Note this review is based on mostly the main story and only a couple of hours on the other modes.

This game. Just amazing. I'm exceedingly impressed by it. It has definitely set the bar high for physics-based VR gameplay.

Whilst it lacks a certain shine in a small handful of the mechanics as opposed to games like HL: Alyx. BONEWORKS has a much wider variety of play styles and things to do.

The engine BONEWORKS is built on can easily be used to make the 'Garry's Mod' of VR, and I for one would love to see it.

I hope for the studios next game, they revamp the melee weapon system to be more like games like Blade and Sorcery. Whilst it is in enjoyable to a degree; it feels very... floaty? Always found when using two handed weapon; I would just hold the weapon out in front of me and sprint into an enemy. Using it properly and swinging was pretty unintuitive. One handed melee weapons were fine, but that's as much as I can say with it. They're not rewarding so to speak. 6/10.

The gunplay is perfect. I've seen so many people online saying 'OH BUT RELOADING WEAPONS SOMETIMES DOESN'T WORK, ESPECIALLY ON THE SMGS' I can tell this is because those sort of plays rely on just flailing their hands about to reload guns in other VR shooters. 10/10.

I very much enjoyed the overall puzzle solving of the game, whilst some of it could use more refinement as some of them felt quite janky. However, solving one inside of a physics-based VR game where 'anything goes' is very satisfying. 9/10

Can't say much for story as it doesn't really have one; whilst there is a theme and underlying story it's not interesting - I understand the goal and it's great fun to work towards, thankfully the goal is the only thing the game needs. The gameplay makes up for the lack of story. I hope this studios next game works towards providing a great story to accompany fantastic gameplay. 6/10.

Overall this game is a solid 8/10. I look forward to the studios next endeavour.\t\t\t\t\t
