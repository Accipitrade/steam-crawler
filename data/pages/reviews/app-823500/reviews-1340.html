\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm about two hours in at the time of this writing. I've completed what you might consider the "tutorial" (which only took that long because I spent a lot of time screwing around with the game engine) and gotten into some actual melee combat. So far, I'm very impressed. There is a strong effort here to built a physics system that rivals Half-Life 2 in the way it pushes the boundaries of what you can do in a game, and it does that.

At one point I got my crowbar stuck in a wooden box, so I lifted it up and smacked the box with the baseball bat in my other hand to get it free and watched the box go flying. I climbed up a scaffolding and lifted myself up onto the roof of a structure, and watched the scaffolding collapse below me because I shoved it away and it tipped over. Then I dropped down, grabbed a 12-foot steel pipe that broke off the scaffolding with both hands and dragged it around with me. I tried to swing it around, but it was too heavy to move very quickly.

Note that there *are* some compromises that take getting used to. You have a physical body in-game, and while it does its best, it usually doesn't match your real body 1-to-1. Surprisingly, it didn't take much for me to get the hang of manipulating my VR-body as if it were another separate appendage. I still have some minor problems with getting melee weapons caught on my own head... I hope no one ever hands me a sword and expects me to use it in real life. In general, since your hands in-game are physical objects, they can get caught on things, they can get mangled, and without feedback beyond the usual rumble, it takes a little extra effort to be mindful of your appendages' position in space.

All these things said, there's one thing that needs to be very, very clear: That warning on the game page that this is an advanced VR game are there for a reason, if you don't have your VR legs or are susceptible to getting sick in VR, this game will almost certainly make you sick. Kinda like some people can't play FPS games without getting sick, some people won't be able to play VR games like this.

So far, though, this is a solid experience, and I would highly recommend it to anyone itching for a good VR experience that's clearly heavily inspired by Half Life 2.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbout 2 hours in and...I'm a little disappointed in a few areas. I'm hoping it's just my rig not being powerful enough with a 2070 and Ryzen 3600, but I get horrible clipping any time I try to climb something, aiming two handed weapons is awkward and takes a long time to get used to. No magazine release on the gun or slide release is pretty lame, that along with snap locking reloads takes away the realism feel. I may just be spoiled from playing H3VR so much, but the gunplay just doesn't feel very good. Maybe all these things exist and I just haven't figured out how to to them, but gunplay seems extremely simple besides the grabbing mechanism.

The melee however, is pretty awesome. I love being able to push and pull and grab enemies then smash them with pretty much anything in the world. Everything has weight and it's honestly creepy FEELING things have mass in a virtual game. It's unreal how well the physics work (when they're not clipping through everything).

The actual story and game is amazing. It's like Portal meets Half Life. If you've followed SLO from the beginning you'll recognize a ton of familiar faces and voices from other YouTubers and channels. The music is fantastic and the visuals of the glitches in the game are beautiful.

The difficulty of the puzzles is way harder than I would have thought and it's giving me major Portal nostalgia in a really good way.

I'll write some more in my review once I'm further in.

Edit 1: Another half hour in. So the biggest issue with the game is a lack of save points. I spent an hour trying to get through a level then took a break, logged back in and had to start the ENTIRE LEVEL over again. Be prepared to spend at least an hour or two each session or all of your progress is lost.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe people saying this game is too short are stupid. The main story is easily 12-15 hours in length, and has tons of replay value when looking for secrets and such. Even if it only offered the main story, that would still be more than enough, but on top of the story you can unlock sandbox and arena modes. Sure, 12-15 hours doesn't sound too long for a regular pancake game, but for VR, this is far longer than almost all of the competitors.

The story isn't too gripping but it doesn't really need to be. You can dive deeper into the lore if you'd like but for me, the story just leads the gameplay. And the gameplay is very good but frustrating at times. I had to die so many times in Streets before I figured out that the enemies don't infinitely spawn, and instead just respawn to a certain amount. I just assumed it would be infinite so I always tried to run past, which of course, doesn't work.
The game is only as challenging as you make it. Sure, it's really easy to take out a horde of zombies with a gun at a distance, but that's not fun or challenging. You might, instead, try using your knives and pistol at close range.

Arena mode isn't as complex as Blade and Sorcery, but I can't really go back to that game because it just doesn't feel as good to play. In Boneworks, when you jump, your acceleration curve is very akin to reality. In Blade And Sorcery, you slowly take off like a rocket, and then slowly turn back around like a balloon. It feels weightless and airy.
In Boneworks, when you fall a good height you stick the landing. In Blade and Sorcery, if the ground is on even the slightest slant, which it usually is, you go flying in some arbitrary direction.

Sandbox mode is good fun. It's fun to spawn a bunch of enemies and have them fight eachother, or to see how many you can take. I've only unlocked one sandbox level as of yet, though.

All in all, while the game isn't flawless, as far as VR goes this game is the most advanced game to this point. If you want an unpolished glimpse at the future, and some fun along the way, Boneworks is a must buy.\t\t\t\t\t
