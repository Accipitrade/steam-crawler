\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is simply awesome. Remember all those things you couldn't interact with in other VR games? How you can't just simply push something aside or grab it? Yeah, not here! You can interact with everything in really intuitive and also fasinating ways. You'll be expected to solve puzzles with physics objects, combat (often w/ melee) various enemies, and traverse complicated areas.

The AI is really fun to play with, it's super interactive and fluid. They're not locked to animations, they're actually really convincing and interacting/fighting with them is just unlike anything else I do in vr. It's not just "grab them and they stop moving", they have mass and strength and pull away but you can influence how they move and they try to maintain their balance. It's just pretty cool and insanely fun to play with. Also there's these headcrab things so that's fun.

The only thing I'd call lackluster is the guns, BUT THEY STILL FEEL PRETTY DECENT. If you played H3VR though, it's not as good as that. It's still detailed enough to make you take mags out, put new ones in, and chamber it though. Just no fire-selector and other interactions which I find kind of disapointing. Most the fun I end up having is gunless though, but when they're necessary they still feel and sound pretty good.

In general, lots of VR games can *look* immersive, but this one *feels* immersive. Just all the world interaction, *and* ai interaction. It's superb. I play 'til my battery dies.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far i think this is one of the best VR titles you can get!

it has a lot of content and replayability.

There is a story to explore and do note that exploring each map rewards you like trying to find and get the balloon gun in single player for example. though i don't quite understand the story i did still enjoy playing through the campaign, though it did feel empty at times i do wish there were like human enemies like the Ford clone level type thing, but then again i guess i just like the gore of red blood splashing around.

then there is the Sandbox and arena mode which are really nice once u finished the story and discovered and collected every collectible in all the story maps.

i do love the gun play too the way it reloads the way it shoots its very satisfying.

but then the melee needs some work but is still doable.

Here are some of my pros and cons

Pros:
- Awesome physics
- Good sound design
- Rewards exploration
- has replayability
- interactive environment

Cons:
- Lack of enemy variety
- Just a few good soundtracks
- melee combat can be improved
- game feels a little empty not many NPC's to talk to just a few screens ( Could be cuz of the story?)
- Ford does not really talk to the people on screen (would have been awesome)

overall despite all the cons this is a really great game and i hope more developer follow in its footsteps.

Comparing this game to Half Life Alyx i would almost say i like this game a little more because of the creativity on how to kill enemies and that you are able to holster your weapon by physicly moving it to the holster and letting go.
Small things like that i do think matter but then again, i think this game is not made for new comers.\t\t\t\t\t

The save point mechanics have made it much easier for players who get motion sick easily to get through levels smaller portions at a time. The enemies generally aren't too tough, but they're fun to beat nonetheless. Finding creative ways to kill enemies is very fun in and of itself. The story mode has a lot of secrets to unlock, and conserving ammo to get cool items can create interesting challenges. There is a good amount of gun variety with smgs, uzis, pistols, and assault rifles as well as semi auto rifles. My biggest complaint is the confusing story which takes a second playthrough or outside videos to understand fully. Other than that, this is a great game that I'd reccomend to anyone with a VR setup.\t\t\t\t\t
