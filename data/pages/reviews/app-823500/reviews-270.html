\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPositive:
-Great physics: Incorporates mass, picking up large objects, etc really well, and the fact that your body will clip on literally anything makes it feel more real
-Spatial audio was very well done, I often took my headset off thinking something was actually falling in the other room
-Puzzles are fun if sometimes too straightforward
-Combat is fun

Neutral:
-Story plays out in a fairly fun way, but its really not clear whats going on and it does at times feel like they just found a way to stitch some levels together and call it a campaign
-Not a real variety of enemies and the behavior is really basic/too easy to exploit

Negative:
-By far the worst part of Boneworks is the super amateurish level design. I know its a small studio but it feels like I'm playing an amateur quake 3 map in VR- objects tend to have simple geometries and minimum possible polygons, the same exact objects are reused over and over, most textures are significantly lower resolution than the headset and feel like they were just pulled off of the internet and thrown on, some have visible repeat clips, many large things literally don't have a texture, etc. People will call it a stylistic choice but it felt lazy and was immersion breaking
-Still pretty buggy (but not enough to ruin it at all)
-Many random annoying things, like the difficulty of chambering weapons with the switch on the side without accidentally grabbing the ammo, difficulty of picking up things to your lower left because you always end up pulling ammo from your belt, etc

Overall: Boneworks is a really cool sandbox for VR physics and is definitely worth buying for any enthusiast, its fun for sure. It's definitely not a polished product though.

Ratings:
Graphics: 4/10
Music: 5/10 (sound effects 8/10)
Bugs: 5/10
Story: 5/10
Gameplay: 8.5/10
Duration (story): ~10hrs
Overall: 6.5/10
