\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort version:
Everything is great besides the check points & saving spots And the level design
Loading a saving spot will reset some if not all the enemies and your guns will be empty

Long version:
Okay so first things first i played this on Linux And a little bit on Windows 10 Using Valve Index
My specs NVIDIA GeForce RTX 2080 32gb ram Intel i7-8700K SteamVR on 144hz and on auto resolution

This was my first VR game and i got it as soon as i got Valve Index keep in mind that i havent played anything on vr before (aside from the lab and the index demo) so when i started playing this game gave me motion sickness (and i never had one before) so i stopped playing on the first level.
I tried after some time to get back in the game after i played some other titles and i had better configuration on my vr so i never had any motion sickness after that but the levels where too big and without saving i didnt really have fun coz i had to push through fast coz i had no idea how big the level was and even now that they added the save spots i find it very stupid that they havent done it on release!
And the save spots are stupid...
The enemies you killed will be back if u load the game again and your guns will be empty...
Well at least its better then nothing...
The physics work great, the enemies respond well when u hit them, the level design is very bad some levels require you to do some stupid puzzles the last level has no saving spots and no check points and you will have to climb a lot...
Some puzzles arent even puzzles you just have to move a box or you will have to move an object in order to walk to the next area...
When you beat the game you will be able to play the in the arena and its fun.
I had a ton of fun on sandox mode but i only have pistols i wish you had all the weapons after beating the game...

So over all i wouldnt recommend the game to a new VR player you need to "get used with your vr first"
But if u have played some games with your vr found the right configs then you can get this game and have fun

It works out of the box on Linux using proton 4.11 and 5.03\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI cannot recommend this game to anyone who does not have the Valve Index controllers. It was very clearly designed with them in mind with very little consideration for any other controllers. You have to hold both the grip and trigger buttons on the Vive wands to hold objects or items that you pick up, with the exception of guns. It gets uncomfortable very quickly and I took a probably a dozen accidental screenshots in the first hour.

I now have the Index controllers and have not changed my mind about the game. They give you a warning "For expert VR users only". I have lots of time in other VR games and have no issue with them like I do Boneworks. This isn't just because its a full motion game but because its incredibly nauseating. The main cause of motion sickness (In VR or otherwise) is movement that you are not expecting or don't have control over. Boneworks has a fully rendered body that collides with the environment. Not only does it collide with the environment but it moves you when it does. Very early in the game you have to pry boards off a gate with a hammer. If you don't brace yourself against the gate you will just pull yourself towards it and "hit" your head on the gate. It feels terrible and nauseating. The game features A LOT of climbing and that feels even worse. Your arms will get caught on things even though you only really control your hands. The game feels like a constant battle against the fact that it is VR.\t\t\t\t\t
