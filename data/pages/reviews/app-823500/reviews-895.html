\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a must-have. There is only more content being applied every so often too!

This game is heavily physics-based, and heavily aware that it itself is a game. This game is like exploring the recesses of your PC but in VR. "SysAdmin", "MS DOS", "MythOS". Incredible stuff.

There was only a single issue I had throughout the entire campaign. A box's physics with what I said in mind glitched out once. My left hand was grabbing said box, I couldn't let go but luckily the level was pretty quick and easy so I got back to where I was regardless of what happened. Which I think is a really rare occurrence in the first place. But I figure it's worth noting.

The closest thing to this level of accuracy in VR is Blade and Sorcery, which is still quite accurate but isn't a game that has a dedicated campaign. So if you are trying to decided which game to get, I would recommend this one. Physics in combat make every encounter a decent amount more difficult. Blade and Sorcery is good for mods, but this game's community has gotten more mods lately. Multiplayer for either is unknown. But is available through modding-- not the best but not the worst.

The longest Campaign in a VR game by a tremendous amount. Stress Level Zero is working to innovate VR as a platform one title at a time! Keep on going, SL0!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is the best VR game. It shows all other game developers what the baseline should be for VR FPS gameplay. Boneworks teaches us something about VR, it teaches us that the player needs to be part of the environment. So many VR games out there have the player as a static object that isn't moved by physics at all, and that is a massive problem. I see reviews describing Boneworks' physics as "janky" and I can't help but laugh, throughout the entire game we see the phrase "What if this is just a tech demo?" spray-painted on walls. The game is a test, meant to show us what is possible for the future of VR. I once saw an IGN article where the writer complained that swinging a sledgehammer was difficult in Boneworks, the writer of this article didn't pause to think that it's difficult to swing a sledgehammer in the game because the game's physics try to mirror real life, and in the real world it is difficult to swing a sledgehammer. In order for VR to become truly immersive we don't need haptic suits or full body tracking, we just need our actions in the game to feel realistic. Boneworks has perfect continuity because the physics applies to everything. You get completely immersed in the game because you have arms that constrain where your hands can be, racking the slide of a pistol feels AMAZING in Boneworks because your hand moves on the gun ingame like your hand is moving in real life. Your finger lifts off of objects if you aren't holding the trigger. If I had to sum up the Boneworks experience in one word it would be Terror. You empathize with your ingame body and your monkey brain begins to fear for its life. It's a surreal experience that somehow feels more real than any other game I've ever played.

It's time to ditch floating hands, non-physics gun models that can be put through walls, and melee combat that feels like playing with friction-less ghost pencils. Some people say a physics based multiplayer VR game would be bad, I say it would be better than anything we currently have.\t\t\t\t\t
