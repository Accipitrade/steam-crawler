\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompetent physics engine with a shooting/physics puzzle game begrudgingly crowbar-ed into it.

The good:

Has the best gun mechanics and handling I have used in VR guns feel responsive and act as I would expect a real one to.

world design is excellent and packed full of little details that add impressively to the story they're try to tell.

This game runs well and is very visually impressive - good graphics

The puzzles (other than climbing ones) are well designed punching the buttons in VR is far more fun than I thought it would be


The bad:
I don't think any of the devs have played the game though from start to finish. There are several boxes through out the game that you pull a handle on and it opens giving you a gun or ammo etc...

I am 172cm so I have to bend down to open the box, for some reason when you bend down your character stands up straighter so that your head high in game is the same as standing normally, if you keep going the game realises you are bending down then starts to move down faster this is both annoying and sickness inducing just not having that system would be so much better. There are several puzzles the require jumping to and from physics objects and they do not work well at all well as more time was spent with the fighting the physics engine to try and stand on a barrel and then climb onto a ledge becomes a time consuming and frustrating venture,

The inventory systems just inst explained and I don't think I have successfully stored a gun correctly first time, I drop it and have to try again, would be nice to have the ability to change to inventory body slot locations or have any from of visual indicator of where they are other than the side holsters.

The game is physics based which is cool, your characters is physics based which is not, if you reach out to open a locker door the act of reaching out will push you away from the locker very annoying this does not happen when pushing buttons thankfully as they're my favourite part of the game.

Exiting the game, is done by walking though a door in the main menu room and putting a VR headset on your characters head, I liked that, the first time as it was arty, clever and pretty meta however having to do that every time to just close the program is super annoying, especially if you haven't played for a while and are annoyed at the weird design choices and just want the motion sickness fest to stop, to try repeatedly to put the sci looking vr headset on just to exit is annoying, GIVE ME AN EXIT BUTTON YOUR BUTTONS ARE GREAT USE THEM, exit buttons have been good enough for every other game ever made why not here.


The VR aspects are well done but the DEVs seem to have forgotten a lot of the QoL game design choices developed over the last 30 years, If stacking physics objects and climbing them is the answer to a puzzle then why does the physics engine boot me off them, when i climb them why is it impossible to pull yourself up onto a ledge.\t\t\t\t\t
