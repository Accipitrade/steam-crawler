\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBottom Line: This game is really, really good.

The following is a list of everything the devs totally nailed:
- The melee weapons feel incredible to use. Get a knife or cleaver asap and have a great time stabbing stuff.
- The levels feature clever puzzles that really make you think and combat scenes that are more intense and nerve-wracking than they have any right to be.
- The physics are astounding. Smashing things has never felt so good. Taking pipe wrench to glass is a magical experience.
- For once a game has an option to crouch without physically crouching. My knees live another day.
- There are vending machines where you can buy items with ammo you don't need.
- There are only 2 kinds of ammo you have to manage.

This is a list of the few issues this game has:
- Climbing can feel quite strange, and it's near impossible to pull yourself up onto a ledge.
- It can be jarring to see your in-game arms acting out of sync with your real ones, but his can be mitigated by treating heavy objects like they have weight.
- Prone to crashing, but then again my graphics card is only just up to this game's demanding specs.
I've thoroughly enjoyed what little time I've spent playing this game. There are few VR videogames I could recommend more than BONEWORKS.\t\t\t\t\t
