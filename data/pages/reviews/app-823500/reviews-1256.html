\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[UPDATE] Checkpoint System is in the Works
https://steamcommunity.com/app/823500/discussions/0/3963662189543240419/

[Original Review]
Boneworks desperately needs to have more checkpoints in its levels. For a game that encourages people to play, it punishes you for doing so.

If you explore thoroughly, stop to play around, or try to get to a secret, the levels can take upwards of two hours to complete. Like many others, I've lost hours of progress due to saves only being at the beginning of levels, so I've started just sprinting through the levels because I don't want to lose my progress. If you die, you start over at the beginning of the level; thankfully, puzzles stay solved when you die, but that almost makes it worse, because you have to sprint through areas that are empty of enemies and all the puzzles are solved just to get back to where you are.

I really love this game, it is to VR what Half Life was to PC gaming. Once it fixes the save issue, I'll give Boneworks the glowing recommendation it deserves.\t\t\t\t\t

Besides rambling about my experiences, I just wanted to give this a positive rate as I saw a bunch of dorks whining about it not working for their sets. To address the biggest complaint here: Yes, it might not save a lot.. Not even sure when/if it saves. I get around this by completing the level. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo this game does have FANTASTIC features, there is no like about it. If you're really craving a story game for VR then have at this one. It's one of the better blended VR experiences I've experience gameplay wise.

Now for the big part, I think the hype that I heard surrounding this game may have gave me a warped perception on what to expect, (I had only seen one trailer otherwise) but I was very frustrated through a majority of this game. I played using the original Oculus Rift and let me tell you, I don't think the controllers were play tested. It's not terrible, I've seen far worse out there, but the slowdown ability (WHICH IS ALMOST MANDATORY IN SOME SECTIONS) is placed awkwardly under the left thumbstick which is also your movement. Just an option to remap keys would be a mass improvement.

The Physics are great when they work, but alot of times I found myself just getting frustrated with them. Maybe it's not for me, but I did find it fun to climb *for the most part*.

I'll be very blunt, the story was jaded and forgettable to me, I didn't skip any of the cutscenes but it felt very bland. It also seemed to be very off pacing wise, it was REALLY stretched out in the beginning and the last few sections I beat in a jiffy.

One complaint I have seen is motion sickness, I personally haven't felt motion sick in any VR games to date and this one is no different. I can see where some sections can disorient you but overall not bad. (If you are someone who does get motion sick, turn a fan on in your room. It will keep you grounded on where you are and help your brain from doing an anger.

One mechanic that I despise is the fact that if you die *to ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ or not* you are sent ALL the way back to the beginning. Yeah it keeps track on what you killed and the puzzles you solved, but boy did it cause me to take off the headset and go chill with another game.

There are also bugs, two very common ones for me were that when I was attacked, there was around a 1/5 chance one of my hands would be stuck and unable to move from wherever it was last. The other is with ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ LADDERS. Like ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ when I grab the railing I don't want to then fall from said rail half a second later. Not letting go or anything, commonly with the left hand. Idk why but it made the last level hella aids.

So overall I probably wouldn't play through the entire game again. A few stand out levels may help me reboot it some time. Also maybe if I find out how to unlock the sandbox mode. Either way, I would rate this a 6/10 Average if not a little more so, but probably not worth $30 IMO. If you can get it on sale for around $15~ I would say go for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo be frank, the game is good for what it is. It combines a good middleground of gunplay ala Pavlov (unfortunately not like H3) and adventure.

I'm going to completely dismiss bugs here because the ones that I did run into were generally of my own doing.

The BIGGEST issue this game has, and I mean COLOSSAL... is the lack of checkpoints.

Even as someone with his VR legs and is very familiar with the apparatus, the levels are both deliciously and sickeningly long. A death will set you back to the beginning, but with enemies and obstacles already cleared.

God forbid you grow tired and can't go on, or you develop hardware syncing issues mid-level and need to restart, it's ALL the way from the beginning.

Here's the thing to bare in mind, and I hope the devs read this:

VR, while slowly becoming more commercial, is still very intensive and very blunt in it's presentation.

When you have an expertly crafted level, especially from a studio that is known to hide easter eggs, people WANT to explore and look around before even considering the other activities.

Unless you're speedrunning or can pop one-tap headshots on the move, each level will take you about an hour and a half.

And even as someone who can run H3 gauntlets for 3 or 4 hours before getting tired, with Boneworks being an in-depth game that requires your attention, it is EXHAUSTING, and it's to it's own massive detriment.

I say that, because I've had two occasions where I've spent 45 minutes to get through half the level considering all combat, puzzles, and exploring and trying to break things, and then my hardware runs into a sync issue where the headset is constantly resetting itself and my arms won't cooperate as they fly off screen. While the hardware is my own technical issue, the EXPERIENCE is ruined for me because I have to make the choice of doing it all again after restarting the game, or pushing through the final 'stretch' and finishing the level so I can save.

Both times, I selected the latter because the thought of playing the same level again for another hour is terrifying, and because of that, the second half of the level, I was just hoping and AUDIBLY begging to the open-air in my apartment, that the level would be over. I end up stopping to look for secrets or trying to climb something crazy. Combat became less clean and appealing. And I generally just didn't want to play anymore. I was over it.

So what's my verdict:

With what is otherwise a spectacular game and one of the FEW AAA titles available for VR; with VR still budding in the minds of many and emerging as a new experience for those who have recently received their new hardware, and even for veterans of the hardware - the attention and dexterity-demanding ceiling set by this game is WAY too high and will likely turn off a lot of folks.

I myself will be taking a break for a few days because of how frustrating and tiring it is.\t\t\t\t\t
