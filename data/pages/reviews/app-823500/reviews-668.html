TLDR at the bottom.

Before I go into many points about this game, I'll go ahead and say that I really enjoyed Boneworks. I played many hours, enough to complete everything that is, and not one of them did I not enjoy. There were some things at times that happened to be frustrating but honestly I think it was due to how i handled the vr controls probably. So here goes...

Story : Honestly, I bought this game alongside many other games and my vr during a sale and at first i wasn't expecting much. From what I could tell from the store page, this looked more like an "experimental" game where the devs tried to push vr physics and mechanics. Once you start playing though, you start to notice there is more to it than just that. Of course its one of those stories that if you don't pay attention to details nothing will make sense, so its important to keep your eyes open to everything around you. I admit I only half paid attention so some things really slipped by me, but I got the big picture in the end, and although not the most thrilling story, there is more to it than you'd expect. I suggest reading up on the lore if you end up confused after the ending, lots of dedicated people out there to clear things up.

Graphics : Not much to say here honestly. While the graphics are meant to resemble simulation and bare bones, they are pretty nice and crisp in their own way. Nothing to brag about but nothing to whine about either.

Gameplay : Tons of fun to be had here. Of course vr is very dependent on the user, the more you immerse yourself into the vr world, the more it feels "real" and you start to let your imagination go wild. I often found myself crouching behind boxes like they were really there. The physics are pretty advanced for vr and some might even cause some slight nausea, they have for me in some parts of the game. I really suggest immersing yourself into the game and letting your imagination run wild, you'll get the most out of the experience that way.

Difficulty : To be quite frank, the game to me was pretty easy, maybe even a bit too easy. The difficulty really strongly depends and the player's own abilities. If you're great at reaction times and aiming in vr, the game will be easy, but if you're horrible at it, the game will have some difficulty to it. The game does offer a "last stand" of the likes of borderlands. If you kill an enemy while in "dying phase" the game will revive you with minimal health. So it ain't over til its over.

Achievements : Though there arn't many, they really give you an all around coverage of the game. Don't be fooled by the small count, they can take some time to finish. Some are easy and quick to finish and some are a real grind and will require some dedication to achieve, but that's not much of an issue when you're having fun is it?

Bugs : Honestly, I would say there really isn't much to be said here, or atleast i haven't encountered too much. There are some instances where some bugs would happen but it wasn't gamebreaking or didn't break the immersion too much, most of them just gave me a good laugh. The only really breaking bug i encountered was while i was wielding an m4, sometimes my arms would start spazzing out for no apparent reason which made it impossible to aim at anything.

TLDR : Decent story, good gameplay, decent graphics, good physics, no groundbreaking bugs, not too difficult. Worth your every penny, on sale or even full price.\t\t\t\t\t

"What up, son?" a devilishly handsome passerby says to me. It isn't until I slap him with a beer bottle, turn a chair upside down, and start bashing his head into one of the legs until something dawned on me: "Why do I have an erection?"

BONEWORKS is a physics-oriented virtual reality adventure game. I wanted to call it a shooter at first, and it's true - you certainly shoot. But you can also use various other methods to defeat your enemies which challenge your creativity. But at its core, BONEWORKS is my favorite sandbox environment to just play around with the awesome physics.

BONEWORKS comes with a variety of game modes. There's a more traditional "campaign" mode, that you need to complete to unlock certain sandbox modes. The narrative is a tad weak, but certainly not the strong point. The campaign includes a number of levels, filled with enemies and some puzzles, and some dialogue that strings the story along and at the very least, makes sense.

I really want to emphasize on the physics with this title. A majority of my hours in this game have been in the sandbox modes, which include some light game modes. You have your classic "enemy waves" gametype, where you can modify the types of enemies and the available weapons each time. There's also more objective oriented sandbox levels, with one that is reminiscent of COD Zombies. Oh, and they literally just added another sandbox level with a functioning hovercraft.

It really is such a delight to just immerse yourself in BONEWORKS' impressive physics systems. Catching an enemy leaping at you in midair, and turning to bash it into a wall until it's dead. Or pistol whipping another enemy, toppling him over. Just make sure you double tap. The systems aren't perfect, and you may get your arms or some item stuck every now and then, but I've found these times to be rare.

My main criticism really comes from the campaign. I loved it for what it was, but found myself wishing for more Half-Life style physics puzzles that require us to be creative with the systems and items we're given to solve them. But despite its short campaign, there are many secrets to be unlocked that encourage multiple playthroughs. And the sandbox levels alone were more than enough to get me another 15-20 hours of play.

BONEWORKS is what I wanted out of VR. I can pick something off the ground and bash enemies with it. I can interact with lots of the environment, and use it to my advantage when dealing with enemies. And there's some neat physics-oriented puzzles to boot that really let you see the potential VR can have.

Verdict & TLDR

Must Have
Recommended
Wait For Sale
Pass On It

BONEWORKS is a must have for anyone who owns VR. It pushes the boundaries of what is possible in VR, in ways that not even Half-Life Alyx is capable of. Despite having a campaign, I keep coming back to the sandbox modes just to have more time immersing myself with the physics systems.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the physics and concept of this game and I really looked forward to being able to experience the first realistic vr game. The problem seems to come from the fact that video games are made to make you feel POWERFUL, and when your character experiences weight and resistance and you don't, it makes you feel weaker and clunkier than you are in real life,

Combat is fun and interesting because the enemies all have simulated muscles and joints so damage feels real. The guns in this game feel so much snappier and real than in any other game thanks to their effects being noticeable with physics. But with melee I often get tangled up and stuck especially when my hands don't go where they're supposed to thanks to the physics of real life not matching up with those in the game. I also feel like I take damage often when I am hardly hit at all, or i obviously have an enemy restrained or blocked. The enemies all seem like they're a couple times heavier than a real person so doing things like hitting them with a heavy object leads to them just powering straight through, bending your limbs instead of theirs.

The puzzles are where I have my real gripe with this game. They're sometimes clever, but never fun. They always take way too long even for a simple puzzle because you have to do things like fenagle a really heavy object up some stairs or repeat a climbing puzzle multiple times, and climbing puzzles are the worst of the worst. Because your vr limbs are based off of physics and not your real hand positions. This is cool if you're simulating something heavy, but in this case it means your hands are a couple feet higher than you have your real hands, making climbing super clunky and awkward.

I really want to love this game. I love the idea of a fully simulated world and realistic physics for vr, but this just doesn't deliver on any other aspect of the game.\t\t\t\t\t

Welcome, to the Desert of the Real...


Ah yes, Boneworks. A game with great ambition, that somehow, hits its mark.
Having evolved away from phone based VR, and to a land of dedicated, feature-rich headsets, what better way to show off the current state of technology than to over do the F*** out of it. Boneworks aims to do just that, and gosh darn-it, it delivers.

Did somebody say "immersion"?
The first thing you will be greeted with is a message stating this game is not for newcomers to VR. Comfort settings like teleport movement are no where to be seen. A full virtual avatar, legs, body and all gives players a sense of truly being in the game. The players arms are proportioned correctly, and the way the joints move to match your hands are typically pretty close to being 1 to 1. The icing on the cake comes in the form of full finger tracking, courtesy of the Index's wonderful Knuckles controllers. The player can grab and interact with just about every object in the game world. An old CRT monitor makes for an excellent weapon to bash an enemy's head in, after flipping them the ol' one finger salute that is.

"You walk real good..."
The meat and potatoes of the game-play is in the way the player transverses the virtual environment. Often, objectives will be out of reach, and there are multiple ways to achieve your goals. This is platforming done right. Be it stacking objects to attempt to climb up, or making a long shot jump, there are few VR games that make such great use of the environment. Spending a while trying to reach a hidden box is rewarding, and frustrating at the same time. Normally I dislike platforming in pancake games, but here in Boneworks its great fun.

The Combat
Gun play is truly where Boneworks shines. There are several varieties of pistol, as well as many SMG variants, and assault rifles. All of which fell natural to use, and feature functional iron sights, and the occasional red dot, scope and laser sight. Reloading is performed smoothly and realistically, and with practice, a bit of flair.
Melee weapons, while present and in great number, require a lot of practice to be used efficiently. I was ready to write them off completely until I realized that I could grab an enemy by the leg and hip, slam it into the ground, then stab it in the head with extreme style.

The Story
Not much can be said about the story in Boneworks, you'll either love it or hate it. It's mostly told through clipboards found in game, writing on walls, and a few video messages on monitors. Personally, I feel the "less is more" approach works well, and had me digging deeper trying to figure out what exactly is going on in MythOS. This may be the most subjective part of the game for many players, but it sucked me in. You can expect around 10+ hours to finish the main campaign, if you stop and smell the roses.

The Jank
With more freedom, comes more jank. There are occasions where your arm my glitch through an object, or you might get temporally stuck in something, but given the amount of freedom to move and do what you want, it wasn't ever an issue for me.

Can you place a bucket on your head?
Yes.

Conclusion
Boneworks has been so far, my best experience in VR. After receiving my Valve Index, I find myself going back to Boneworks, even after my first playthough. On paper, HL:A is a better game, but to me, Boneworks is simply more fun. I hope Stress Level Zero will build on this concept, and I look foreword to seeing more from them.
I give Boneworks an absolutely solid 9 out of 10.\t\t\t\t\t
