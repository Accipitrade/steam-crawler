\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite honestly my favorite VR game to date. I intended to play it for a bit and spent 4.7 hours in it my first time. The longest i've spent in VR in one session since Skyrim.

I found myself using up 20-30 minutes just lifting various heavy objects into place at one point in an attempt to get into a hidden area. It didn't pan out but it was incredibly rewarding to look at all of the work I had done and feel like I actually accomplished something. I have had several moments like this where I would take my time and master that area and learn all I could about it before moving on.

I had one bug where I tried a lever one too many times and it got stuck and had to reload the checkpoint (beginning of the area, there are no save spots), but having mastered the area I breezed through all of it to get back upto the same point in minutes. That is when I realized the game was feeling second nature for me and I was comfortable enough to do cool tricks.

Despite the lack of saves I am thoroughly enjoying it.\t\t\t\t\t

VR Revolutionised? Not quite but very close!

About The Game:
Boneworks is developed by and published by Stress Level Zero, the developers behind some other high quality VR titles you might be familiar with such as Duck Season.

The game is a VR Shooter, with a full fledged story mode, arena mode and also a Sandbox mode for you to mess around in if that's your thing.

Gameplay:
Boneworks is a shooter at heart and a fairly competent one at that, it's fluid, gun play is fun and works well when the physics allow it to, but not only that there's a fairly nice feeling melee combat system mixed in there as well that functions pretty well too.

Melee combat is a bit hit and miss with the system not always registering hits and functioning off fluid motion rather than physical speed and movement, (this isn't H3VR melee combat unfortunately.) However when the melee combat works it really does shine, it feels nice to stab an enemy with a sword and have it get stuck in their body of swing a hammer and watch their body physics recoil from the hit.

Gun combat functions fairly nicely and feels good, the gun audio sounds punchy and they handle excellently with accurate recoil to the calibre of firearm you're using, 9MM handguns have minimal recoil and handle exceptionally well in akimbo situations, where 5.56/7.62 assault rifles recoil heavier both in single handed and two handed operation, however combine the super cool slow-motion feature in the game with all firearms and have a fantastic time mitigating and working with the recoil to make the most of the amazing combat. With this being said once again this isn't H3VR firearms and sadly they feel slightly arcady, I was hoping for more realistic operation of firearms but alas it was not meant to be. (What we got is still great though!)

Finally let's talk about the physics system in this game that was a huge feature the game was sold on, is this system good?

Short answer: Yes, but it has problems.

Long answer: The physics system while very good and really helps add to the realism feeling and makes you feel (artificially) like you are part of the game world has a lot of problems that need to be resolved. For instance when you're trying to climb things and you physically cannot get to places you want to because your physics body clips with the environment or objects around you really gets frustrating, trying to platform in this game can feel like a damn chore because of it and really, REALLY discourages me from attempting to do it in a lot of situations.

Walking around with the physical body can also be a pain when your limbs get caught on everything and insist on flinging you around or not allowing you to progress due to getting stuck on a body on the floor or object in the world, or when your hands clip through an object or wall and get stuck on the other side, this causes you to have to scale your way back up the climbable surface so you can free your stuck limb or reload the map, this leads into another complaint I'll get to later.

The Good:
  • A nearly fully interactive world and fully physics based world adds to the atmosphere and immersion.
  • Gunplay feels satisfying and the melee combat (when it works) feels great!
  • Slow-motion mechanics at the press of a button makes combat feel even greater and allows you to look awesome doing it!
  • Revolutionary VR technology that can only help other developers improve in the future and build off of what makes Boneworks successful!
  • It was once going to be a Half Life game so it looks, feels and plays a lot like Half Life but in VR!

The Bad:
  • Physics based objects can feel janky and often don't work as you intend them to work. This causes a lot of problems.
  • Vive Users Beware!!! it's clear this game was designed with Valve Index controllers in mind, you're gonna have a bad time with Vive Wands. (trust me!)
  • Movement options in this game are very limited, you have 1 option (at the time of reviewing) and I've heard it can cause motion sickness (however I have not experienced this feeling.)
  • There's currently no saving system in place for the campaign. This means if you leave the campaign you're playing you lose all progress, campaign levels take about an hour or more to complete so keep this in mind.
  • The platforming in this game can feel like a chore a lot of the time due to the physics based body, you're constantly going to have your hands and body clipping into things that will impede your ability to climb obstacles.
  • Sandbox mode is disabled in the beginning of the game... Why? Sandbox mode would allow players the ability to get in and start familiarising themselves with the game and it's mechanics, this is especially useful for demoing to your friends.

The Verdict:
With ALL this being said the game is still an 8/10 experience, there's plenty of enjoyment to be had here and much replay value as well with the ability to go back through the campaign and unlock extra stuff, the arena mode and sandbox mode adding even more value and endless replayability for those who really love the game.

However I must say that people who experience motion sickness should be cautious about picking this title up, perhaps watch somebody play it on Youtube or a live stream before picking this game up yourself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the idea of this game, love the physics, and love the work that got put into details that will make sandboxing an amazing experience. I'm a big VR fanboy, and this is a dream come true.

For this, I'm giving it a positive review, but only because there's no middle ground. There are still a couple of MAJOR problems with the gameplay, specifically the campaign, that are so obnoxious it's hard to recommend this yet to anyone looking to play VR casually.

1. No manual save. This flaw makes the campaign kind of a slog to get through because you know you HAVE to beat the whole level (which can be an hour or so long) or you lose all progress. This feels bad, especially if life happens and you have to go. There's some checkpointing if you die, but you don't really know when you get to a checkpoint and if you leave the level, your checkpoints disappear and you have to start from the beginning. This is the major Achilles' heel of this game, and it seems so easy to fix!

2. Motion sickness when attempting physics puzzles. This is the first game I've really felt it in, and I play all my other games outside of comfort mode with the joystick and smooth rotation with no problem, which leads me to believe I can take quite a bit. Physics puzzles can be REALLY frustrating when you have finicky objects that are either really heavy or involve balancing weight. Placing heavy objects where you need them is a pain since they don't really go where you want, and you usually end up knocking a bunch of stuff over and jittering all over the place since EVERYTHING is a collider. This may just be what we get with realistic physics, but it makes me hate some of the puzzles just because I know it'll make me sick. And this bad feeling is magnified when you know you can't take a break and play something else while you bounce back BECAUSE THERE'S NO SAVE. So you either have to keep the game open while you take a break or re-play the entire level later because you don't deserve happiness.

I know I left a positive review and spent most of it complaining but that's only because I believe in this project and think this could easily be my favorite VR title. The puzzle stuff making me sick is more of a personal gripe, but it's made so much worse by the fact that you cant save. I absolutely back up the other positive reviews that speak to all the things the game is doing right.

Fix the lack of a mid-level save and you've got yourself a winner. It's about time a studio took the time to really maximize the potential of VR. This SHOULD be the new normal of how a VR game plays.\t\t\t\t\t

I'm playing on Vive, and it just feels clunky. To me it actually makes you realize how unrealistic it is, in the awkward movement and control. The physics are cool, but over the top. E.g picking up something from a table often end up picking up the whole table and throwing it across the room!

Don't get me wrong though it's still a great step forward in vr! It's an enjoyable game and I highly recommend it, but it's not without flawes.

I think it's a shame the sandbox has to be unlocked, pulling yourself up on a ledge is a pain, and having to constantly press the grip when using melee weapon is uncomfortable, to name a few problems.

Buy ii I'm sure you won't regret it! To me it's a solid 7 outta 10 and they ain't kidding when they say it's for people with vr experience!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks might honestly be one of my favourite games, and by far the best VR game i have ever played. I love B&S and other great VR titles, but this has to be one of the most technologically impressive and interesting games i have played. The story isn't 10/10 for multiple reasons, but with how immersed in the title i was, it made me step back every now and again and just go "wow". The puzzles made me think, and the combat, while lacking some more weapon options, is just amazing.

The fact that almost every puzzle relies on something physical, and the fact that with multiple different puzzles have multiple different ways to complete them is very satisfying. For example, on the level 'Streets' I lifted a garbage can over my head and used it to save myself from a very upset enemy.

I have completed the story mode, but i have a lot of secrets to discover. The story gave me a very classical feeling with the fact that you often have to retrace your steps to find or unlock something new to progress. I have owned VR for a little over a year, and there are a few moments in Boneworks that honestly gave me shivers, which i had not experienced since the first two weeks of using VR.

The physics, oh boy, do they satisfy. While there were a few moments here and there that made the physics or your character not work as intended, it honestly just heightened my sense of fun for everything. The combat is satisfying beyond belief, i completely loved fighting with melee weapons and guns.

If you have the money, i would say it's an experience that you cannot miss.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate: Finished the game. Figured out what made me nauseous. It's the climbing mechanics. Your head swings around so much left and right when you're climbing it's incredibly disorienting.

Melee Physics still need improvement because they are incredibly jank if you're not using a short bladed weapon or a stabby thing. I had more consistent results holding an NPC and punching them to death than I did by using any two handed weapon.

Overall, needs more polish but still has the best single player VR campaign you can play. Some of the levels are incredibly immersive.

Total times crashed during finishing the campaign: 2

Update: Feb 19th. They have finally added manual save points and checkpoints to the game.

Meaning when you start feeling sick, you can actually stop the mission and not lose all your progress.


**Some Slight Spoilers**
For context: I have an Index

There are three main problems with the game in the current state:

1. SAVING (THIS HAS BEEN ADDED) - There is no auto-save feature or a manual save. If you have to turn off the game to do something. Have fun, you have to restart the entire scene. Oddly enough, if you die you won't always be respawned back in the beginning of the scene, sometimes you'll respawn in the middle of the level. That respawn location should act as the last save point of the scene if you quit, but at the current time it doesn't. Each scene takes roughly 30 minutes to 1 hour to complete and some have finicky puzzles that are tedious to redo which adds more pain to replaying the entire scene over again.

2. CLIMBING AND PHYSICS (STILL NEEDS WORK)- It sounds great and in many respects it is. But dear god, during this climbing/ bridge setup mechanic at scene 4. If you were to step on a panel (that you could use to make a bridge) it'd slide around like a MOFO. If you do choose the climbing option (which acts an alternate path) the ledge that you're supposed to fall onto is so small that even if you hug the fluffing wall, YOU JUST BOUNCE OFF MOST OF THE TIME. Pull yourself closer to the wall, let go, and you'll bounce off the wall and miss the ledge! That segment alone took me 30 minutes because of these janky mechanics. The amount an object slides when you step on it needs to be reduced.


3. Comfort (GOOD ENOUGH....)- Each scene is really long and the fact that there is no vignetting comfort feature adds to that pain. Over the course of a long play session you can get incredibly motion sick due to the amount of jumping and virtual movement in the game. Please... For the love of god... Add vignetting. I play a lot of VR but this is one of the few games that has actually made me motion sick.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is probably the best VR game yet. Very fun and immersive.
The only things that bother me (on Oculus Rift S) are:

I have to press the grip button AND the trigger to pull a weapon out of a quickslot. Doing that disrupts my immersion a little, as I usually don't put my finger on the trigger when unholstering real weapons (actually quite dangerous).
Speaking of fingers: If I have my real finger on the trigger of my touch controller, I expect the character in the game to do that with his gun as well. It just feels a little quirky that every time when I pull the trigger the character lets go of the trigger completely.

After finishing the story I found myself wanting more and starting a new playthrough to unlock everything I can and I still have lots of fun.

The sandbox and arena modes also have quite some potential.

I wish for more maps or a workshop or anything related to more stuff to do. I'm not saying that there isn't enough already, I just want even more of it.\t\t\t\t\t
