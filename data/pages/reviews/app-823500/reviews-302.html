My time in Blade&Sorcery made handling the sledgehammer so much easier too. If you intend to purchase this game then I would have to say it is definitely a must for any VR library.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would like to give this a neutral rating but I can't.
the game isn't straight up bad but it has many annoying features, that make it hard for me to enjoy it.

PROS:
- has a nice tutorial (usually forced tutorials in games are very boring)
- puzzles are okay
- I like the main menu
- campaign has an interesting setting
- short loading times

CONS:
- you often get stuck on small things on the ground, like curbs, wooden boards, bodies. stuff that you would normally just walk over
- the holsters often don't work and you're accidentally dropping your weapon mid fight, instead of holstering
- enemies often go right into your face, which feels very uncomfortable
- melee weapons are held very awkwardly, when you pull them out of the holster
- climbing is horrible. it feels like you weight a ton
- lifting yourself ontop of something is a chore
- some surfaces are not climbable/you can't grip some edges (why?)
- campaign has at least one completely dark level, which is annoying. there is night vision and flashlights but it still sucks and I was happy, when I finished it
- performance is mostly okay but the game freezes for a second basicly once a minute

I haven't looked into mods, yet, so I don't know if you can fix some of these issues
but these should have never been there in the first place.
it feels like they only tested the game for bugs and not for user friendliness.

it's not a horrible game but it's not good either.\t\t\t\t\t
