\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is just straight up one of the worst VR games out there.

Here's why:
-The sandbox engine is entirely unstable, so good luck trying to interact with literally anything.

-It is INCREDIBLY easy to clip out of bounds by mistake, which results in an instant death, which results in losing whatever item you had in your hand with no way to recover it.

-Every single item you hold in this game is bouncy, it's like your character is trying to make what you hold swing around and be wavy.

-Highly nauseating movement with an emphasis on platforming with a system not designed around such actions.

-Every gun in this game feels awful, I don't know how to describe it, it's the exact opposite of Pavlov

-A really messed up FOV where it's smaller than your actual headset FOV. Imagine if you zoomed in your eyes, that's the entire game.

(If you can recall how sickening it is to play normal FPS shooters with super low FOV, you can imagine how AWFUL it is in VR)

-Every melee weapon has different "weights" but they all do roughly the same damage so picking the smaller options is better, leaving cool weapons like the sledgehammer to be thrown aside as they're literally useless.

This game desperately wanted to be the next Half-Life or Portal but it also couldn't be bothered to put in the kind of effort that would be necessary to be the next Half-Life or Portal.

Not worth buying, even on sale. There isn't a single pleasant or fun experience in the entire game.\t\t\t\t\t
