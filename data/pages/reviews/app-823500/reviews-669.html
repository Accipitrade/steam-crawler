\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic foundation for a VR exploration game, but a rather lackluster combination of mediocre level design and very little story development leaves Boneworks far from the perfection that it has the potential to achieve. The story mode can be completed in a little over 2 hours, and is very easy. While gathering footage for an upcoming video essay I plan on writing, I played through the entire game in one sitting without dying.

I am excited to see Stress Level Zero's next game, as it will surely use the frameworks put in place by Boneworks and be a fantastic game- provided they put more effort, time, and resources into making an engaging environment and moment-to-moment storyline.

In short, If you own a VR rig and do not own Boneworks, it is worth a buy. But be aware that the game could be exponentially better with a little bit more care and attention put into the game environment itself, to the same extent they put into the fantastic physical interaction system.

To answer the question "Is this just a tech demo?" posed by the graffiti inside the game itself- No, but it certainly feels like one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeing one of the first VR games (besides VRChat) that I've played extensively, BoneWorks is a good intro into new VR mechanics, and a good intro into VR gaming in general.

The gameplay is superb. The game plays well, it looks good, and it feels very well made. And contains lots of functions that're fun and innovative as well. It's very remniscent of Portal and Half Life, with lots of fun weapons and greatly made puzzles and gameplay mechanics.

As for the Story mode plot, that's where I got confused. I guess you're trying to escape a virtual world? I think? I don't know. It didn't make much sense, at all. But the puzzles and atmosphere was very well done.

One peeve I have is that the developers felt that throwing a bunch of boxes into a space and calling it good would suffice for level design. Not really. Some of the levels felt really repetitive, and the sheer amount of physics objects in the space made it hard to move around easily.

Overall, I'd recommend BoneWorks if you're new to VR, need something new to play, or simply just want to screw around.

EDIT: After playing for many more hours, and doing some research, the lore and story are extremely well-constructed, and quite intriguing. But that still doesn't excuse the fact the in-game plot is quite confusing. Still, very good story when you want to learn about it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere is a list of my thoughts on the game BONEWORKS, if you've played the game yourself then maybe you will agree with me on some of the complaints I have made. Well anyways here is what I liked and disliked about the game.
(Notice the amount of dislikes)

Pros:
- Runs good
- Physics can be fun to mess around with
- Gun Play Feels nice, reloading and shooting weapons feels good. Physical gun recoil is awesome! Weapons also
function like their real life counter parts
- Good enemy Types, enemies are varied though I would have liked being shot at more with less zombie A.I.
- Environment is well detailed, plenty of stuff to go explore and see such as trash in alleys, food on tables, vending
machines, and etc.

Cons:
- Color palette gets a bit drab making not wanting to come back.
- Melee still feels clunky or to soft, my fist feel like they're in slow motion when trying to throat punch zombies with a
empty handgun. Its like my character doesn't shift his weight into the punch or something.
- Very disorientating and nausea inducing, this is the only VR game that makes me feel sick.
- Music adds to the sickening because its not that great. I remember trying to solve the first puzzle in the tutorial and
feeling nauseous every time I heard the beat coupled with how hands get stuck on everything.
Short Campaign, can be beat in 4 1/2 hours if you rush.
- Content hidden from the player that the player must go out of their way and find. No I do not want to go find
zombie mode (I have all the modes in the game FYI). When new content is added let the player play, don't hide it
because that's tiring especially when you've already beat the game several times.
- Would like to see a tool that I can use to make levels for the sandbox mode. While having the control to spawn in a
bunch of enemies is fun when you're in VR. Having full control over the environment would be much greater and
bettered controlled through some window application. Kinda like halo forge or Garrys Mod.

Overall: The game can have its cool moments that I will not spoil, but other than that it gets boring real fast and toward the end of the campaign I felt myself feeling tired of the nausea that this game gave me and I just wanted to finish the campaign.

I don't see myself playing this game again unless the developers add more vibrant maps to play on when it comes to their hidden content / arena game modes and I would also like to see some level editor for the sandbox where we could possibly upload maps to the steam workshop for others to play. For these reasons I'm going to have to not recommend this game. Unless you are only into the gun play and physics that this game incorporates then knock yourself out I guess.\t\t\t\t\t
