\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: It has some good ideas, but executes them poorly. The level design is absolutely atrocious, which is a crime in a game like this.



I really want to give this a thumbs up, but there are a few nagging issues that make it problematic. I really hope they fix these problems moving forward...

This game ought to be called Quarter Life, because it's trying to be the Half-Life of VR, but the developers don't seem to have a full understanding of what made HL1&2 great. Will you smash ammo boxes with a crowbar? Most assuredly. But what truly elevated Half Life, and made it great start-to-Xen, was the level design. And, while they definitely take some cues from there (but more, seemingly, from Portal), they miss a couple core concepts. The levels are intricate, and include physics-based puzzles, some optional, some required to progress. But the levels are big, open, and mostly lifeless. The biggest crime was the number of empty dead-ends. Games like this encourage exploration, but VR encourages short play sessions. So, when I explore to somewhere I'm clearly meant to be able to go (like through an interactable door), and there's just empty space with no puzzles, no stuff hidden away, nothing interactable, just an empty blah, I really feel like my time is being wasted. There are even places like this where the developers threw in "SORRY!" graffiti, seemingly to rub salt in the wound. On top of that, there are hidden areas with a tantalizing crate that should have some great reward in it, and then you spend 20 minutes figuring out and platforming and falling and you get there and smash the crate and it's empty. That's it. No reward, just a stupid empty crate. Half-Life, in contrast, had something interesting in every corner. Even if it wasn't useful, or interactive, it was at least something interesting to look at. Not so here. It's supposed to be a virtual world (oooh, meta), but for some reason this world is full of garbage (why? an obtuse allegory to managed programming language garbage collection routines?) and little else. Maybe they were going for brutalist architecture, but it just comes off as uninspired and very, very bland.

But, speaking of wasting my time in the preferably short sessions of VR, the checkpoint/save system is an absolute joke. The game forces you to go through a 6 or 7 step setup EVERY TIME YOU LAUNCH IT, and then the only options are to start the various levels. Want to save part-way through a level? Too bad. Get frustrated or a headache and need a break from VR? Well, you better suck it up and finish the level, or you're losing all your progress and have to start it over in the next session. And the levels vary wildly in length. Some take almost two hours, while others can be beaten in 10-15 minutes.

My final big gripe is the physics. It's disappointing they weren't able to polish this better before release. After playing Blade & Sorcery, I've got a pretty good understanding of what physics-based VR games should entail. But this one feels rubbery, inconsistent, and sometimes buggy. If you do any amount of climbing in this game (which you will), expect your handhold to randomly break or your arm to decide it wants to go around a pipe the wrong way and start glitching out your body. God forbid you leave your hand behind a fence and it becomes an anchor while your wrist phases through, keeping you from walking anywhere. Laser sights and scopes on weapons don't seem to mean a damn thing. You can have enemies dead in your sights, but when you pull the trigger, it doesn't register a hit. I don't know if that's poor barrel alignment, or what, but it's annoying. Guns only seem to really work reliably at close range, regardless of type or loadout.
After playing games like Pavlov, I expect tight gunplay. This is not it. Using the equip slots is inconsistent at best, and impossible at worst. After a while, I just relied more on the pop-up menu. Using a 2-handed gun feels like it's full of sloshing water the way it wobbles and waves.

Even the very beginning of the game has issues. When you turn it on and you have to enter your height settings, it seems to only work with the left hand for me. The right hand trigger doesn't register. One time, neither trigger registered until I rebooted the game.

Update: I've now beaten the game, and the last level was the worst of the bunch. A fistfight with an AI that doesn't know how to fistfight, followed by shooting down waves of enemies while forced to use the most basic guns in the game, followed by a pointlessly tall and frustrating climb.

After that, you get to watch the final "cutscene" of the game on another giant monitor in a dark room. You might notice this completely defeats the purpose of VR. You'll definitely notice that the story was paper-thin and nonsensical. Like, who was that one human you saw at the beginning of that one level that tossed you a rifle? There were no other humans in the VR space, so what the hell was that?

Really, most of this game can be summed up as "what the hell was that?"\t\t\t\t\t

Finally a new VR game worth my time and money !

Yes BONEWORKS is really, REALLY GOOD and Stress Level Zero absolutely nailed it.
My new favorite VR game for sure !

I've been following this project for years and so far i'm not disappointed, it's even better than i expected tbh.
Great graphics, amazing physics engine, interesting level design and puzzles, nice story and universe, tons of secrets, sweet soundtrack, list goes on...

This game is simply one of the best VR games ever made if not the best.
I advise to not watch any playthrough or streams so you don't get anything spoiled.

The only thing i will complain about the game is that we cannot quit and continue from our last checkpoint within a level and have to restart from the beginning if we exit the game.

Edit 15/12/2019: The devs just announced they will add a new save system into the game around january, so this won't be an issue pretty soon.

From the first time i saw the headcrabs like creatures and the first revealed environments for this project, i immediately thought this game was related to Half-Life...
Looks like i was right, as explained in the Valve News Network video titled "BONEWORKS is a Secret Half-Life Game".

https://youtu.be/anY-RBVbtzw

Anyway i absolutely recommend BONEWORKS to anyone who owns a vr headset.
This one gonna keep us busy until Half-Life: Alyx.

Rating 9/10.
