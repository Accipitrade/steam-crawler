\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I could sum up Boneworks in one word, it would be "Clunky." Boneworks does a lot of things right, but even more things wrong. TL:DR at the bottom.

1. The shooting is sub-par at best. Its passable, but when compared to Pavlov or H3VR this game's shooting feels awful. Its apparent that the devs really wanted to give guns that satisfying feel to them, but its overdone to the point of annoyance. Charging handles feel stiff, and the clicks of the mechanisms are way too loud. They don't feel like guns.

2. If you dont have a melee weapon with a sharp edge on it, god help you because blunt weapons in this game are next to useless. Why stabbing a crate with a combat knife to break it is more effective that beating the ♥♥♥♥ out of it with a sledgehammer is beyond me.

3. Having arms and a body in a VR game already isn't good for immersion to begin with, but Boneworks takes it to the next level by giving your entire body a hitbox. Doesnt sound bad on paper, but have fun constantly getting your hands and elbows stuck on the smallest things. Its part of the reason movement in this game isn't fun, especially in tight spaces.

4. There needs to be more movement options. Moving with the joystick is really boring. This is a VR game, that should be utilized. Have the player swing their arms to move or something. Have the player do something physical.

5. There needs to be a save system. I wasn't very happy when I decided to take a quick break, only to relaunch the game to find all my progress for that level had been reset. The levels arent short, most are quite long which only makes this more annoying.

6. The physics for the most part are good, but when interacting with heavy objects things get finicky, to say the least. There were numerous times where the game just decided to freak out and launch objects around. I had to reset a level because of this and dont forget, that made me have to redo the entire thing.

7. These are some more minor things that I was expecting going in, but still should be fixed. Walking/standing on top of props causes whatever you're standing on to just topple over 80% of the time. The jump height is too short. Jumping on top of a standard crate shouldn't be this difficult. Pistols feel more powerful that assault rifles. I was able to drop a guy with 3-4 pistol shots, yet it took like 5-8 with a rifle.

TL;DR The shooting sucks, most melee weapons are useless, the movement is clunky and boring. Levels are too long with no checkpoints, the physics tend to break, and the story doesn't make much sense.

If you want a good VR shooting game, pick up H3VR or Pavlov. If you want something story based, wait for Half Life: Alyx to come out. Boneworks doesn't live up to the hype.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI looked forward to this game hoping VR would finally have an actual GAME and not another Tech demo. Unfortunately once again, we got a Tech demo. So much so that they even wrote it on the walls everywhere in-game.

The gunplay is way worse than H3VR, the melee combat is no better than Blade&Sorcery, the puzzles are extremely simple, and the entire thing feels like a team of devs just played Half Life 2 and Portal and decided they needed to make a game loosely based on them, ear-worm radio song playing everywhere and all.

Every map feels like it was made in Hammer, some worse than others. Every enemy encounter feels the same and the AI are extremely simplistic. They either walk (slowly) or leap directly toward you. There are only 3 or so instances I remember even seeing the Turret enemies.

And on top of all this, the story is so embarassingly barebones and nonsensical that I actually started getting annoyed when I got a dose of dialogue. The ending is the worst. [SPOILERS] The game goes from Portal-lite to a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ Medieval Castle setting. It makes no sense and it's as boring as it sounds. The King challenges you to fisticuffs, you beat him, and that's just about it. It was embarrassing.

Overall, it was a gigantic disapointment. It was fun messing with physics sometimes and the music was pretty good.\t\t\t\t\t
