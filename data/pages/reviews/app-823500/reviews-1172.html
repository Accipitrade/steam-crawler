\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe hype is pretty accurate. Physics are amazing, if a bit janky in spots.

Probably best for someone with a large room scale playspace, but I had no trouble doing it standing in one spot and seated. Performance is fine, no complaints from me (mid-high end system). Only had a single crash so far, but the lack of save/checkpoint makes even that a little annoying.

The melee combat is very B&S like (though I think I prefer bonework's physics more). The guns are sorta pavlov like, with a tinge of onward.

If you're new to VR, maybe skip this till you've got a bit more under your belt, or take frequent breaks if needed. At this point though, I've not experienced any motion sickness. If you're the kind of person to play a lot of VR with smooth locomotion and snap/smooth turn, you'll likely be fine.

The aesthetic is also very HL and Portal inspired. You can see the influences for sure. I know there's a video called something like "boneworks is a secret half life game", and I can see why people think so (even without the behind the scenes stuff). It does elicit some of that nostalgia calling back to earlier source titles.

There's a bit of jank with the physics. Not much, primarily with your players noodle arms. It's not really frustrating, outside of climbing when you suddenly turn into some kind of possessed spinning puppet. Even then, it's not /that/ bad.

Overall, I highly recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a half life lookalike in VR and it shows, but that is far from a bad thing.

This game often requires intense shooting of objects and knowledge of how to solve puzzles using physics and creativity. Oddly enough, the puzzles never truly confused me except for one time when I didn't know what the jump button was. Many people I have seen often overthink the puzzles and are left stuck for several minutes or forget certain things in their environment are there to assist.

The game is amazing with gun physics. It has great recoil mechanics and you can even rest your gun on the tables and such. Melee seemed to be very fun up until you start using blunt weapons. They don't feel like they truly hurt the AI and I often wonder if I even delt damage. Other times it felt very fun to use a sword and battle several enemies.

The combination of both combat and unique puzzle design makes this a fun and revolutionary game. I feel like this has prepared me for Half Life Alyx and other games willing to follow this genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is what VR was MEANT TO BE BABYYY.

I seriously cannot express how cool this game is. This is every well-executed mechanic that has been implemented in a VR game in recent memory all rolled up into one tight package. Admittedly, the game does have some jank, and the story could be a bit less hinting at a story and more, y'know... story. Despite that, what this game set out to do was be a golden standard for story-driven VR experiences to come, and by golly has it achieved that goal.

This game (ironically, considering the ACTUAL VR continuation of this series is coming out soon), reminds me a lot of HL2. like HL2, it's got some sections where it's obvious the dev team was gleefully showing off their technical accomplishments. However, I'd say that the developers have really earned that right in this case, as did Valve all the way back in 2004.

If I had one complaint, I really wish the developers made more fun physics objects immediately accesible in Sandbox mode. I think this game has an INSANE amount of potential as a Gmod style physics sandbox, and I truly hope the devs explore that avenue by greatly expanding sandbox mode's options and acessibility from the getgo. It would really be nice if I could access more Sandbox content without quitting of of frustration from trying to complete an extremely janky parkour puzzle (something that I truly think that this game needs less of). Again, I would really love to see this part of the game fleshed out more, as it has a whole crapton of potential.


tl;dr: This game is pretty freakin' great. If you have a VR system, it's worth every penny.\t\t\t\t\t
