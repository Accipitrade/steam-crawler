Finally, a long awaited, highly anticipated, and seriously hyped up game is released that actually managed to live up to the hype that was built up around it. It was released on time without major game-breaking bugs (amazing for a small studio). The WMR controllers weren't working on launch, but that issue was fixed within hours. For the most part, the game as a whole works great.

Make no mistake, this game is a game-changer in VR. Objects have mass and they act as if they are heavy and cumbersome, even though you can't feel the weight physically. Walls and objects are solid and have collision (vs being able to move through them). Melee weapons have those same physics, so no longer are we doing "wiggle swordplay" to kill enemies. This is a game that changes the way VR games will be made. It raises the bar significantly, and anyone else hoping to follow have some pretty big shoes to fill.

I had buttery smooth frame rates (Vive + Ryzen 2700X + RTX2080). I was quite amazed that despite the fast action and smooth locomotion, I experienced no nausea at all. Everything was as it should be.

One really big game-breaker for me at the time of writing this (the day after launch) is the lack of any real game saving. It only saves as you finish the VERY large levels. If you quit at any point before completing the level, you have to start it over from scratch again. Seeing how this is VR and not many people can wear a HDM for extended periods of time without getting nauseous (something that the game even warns you about at the start), this seems to be a horrible oversight in an otherwise incredible game. Hopefully they can add something to fix this one glaring oversight in the game.

Another issue that needs to be considered is that you need a fairly high end system to run this at 90Hz or higher, especially if you're running a Vive Pro, Pimax or Index with their higher resolution screens (and to take advantage of the higher refresh rate of the Index). The minimum specs to run it are sorely understated IMHO, at least by the reactions of the community so far. As of right now, unless you have at least the recommended system specs on the Steam Store page, you will experience stutter and frame drops, leading to nausea causing you to have to take a break. And that brings us back to the former point of no meaningful game save option.

So I wholeheartedly recommend this game. It's awesome. There's nothing else like it out there, and HL: Alyx is likely to be the only game anywhere near this caliber in the forseeable future. But as a HUGE caveat, I would recommend that you upgrade to at least a GTX 1080 or RTX2060 Super before getting the game. Anything less and you'll be having a poor experience that will prevent you from being able to play through the entire game.\t\t\t\t\t
