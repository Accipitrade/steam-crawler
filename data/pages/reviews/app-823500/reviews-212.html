\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA frustrating example of how not to make a VR game. The height calibration, which other VR FPSes like Alyx and Arizona Sunshine don't need or can do as a quick "arms to sides, pull trigger", is a manual number entry nightmare and no matter what I do I seem to be constantly either unable to reach the ground, crouch enough (a control you have to find through random guessing, since the game is too up its own "ha ha meta-VR!" narrative to document it), or stop my character walking around on tiptoes. The auto button is a joke and concludes things like my sitting height is somehow both barely below my standing height but also that the floor is under my chin.

What game world I *can* get into is *incredibly* tedious "omg you're in VR?!?! in the game?!?!" deal with wall-graffiti out in the open going "what if die in VR, die in real?!?!". You exit the game by putting on a VR headset. Or, you know, just calling up the Steam menu and hitting exit there.

The only reason I haven't refunded this is because I've gone back to give it another chance too many times to be eligible any more.\t\t\t\t\t
