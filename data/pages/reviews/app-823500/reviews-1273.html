The game is awesome and I have been following it's creation before I even had a vr headset.
I love the art style and the holographic enemies. The wacky physics make the game all the better.

But every great game has a few short comings.
1. Melee weapons do little damage. I feel as if they could do a bit more damage.
2. No checkpoints or saves. It doesn't bother me as much as some other people, but having to replay the level when coming back to the game is a bit annoying but not infuriating.
3. Hands sometimes go through objects and get stuck.
4. Some occasional texture bugs, graphity disappears when looked at a certain angle.

Other than these 4 minor inconveniences the game is awesome and I can't wait to play it when I am at school or away from home.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI completed the game's story in about 9 and a half hours. The rest of my play time was either replaying levels or playing around in the sandbox and arena modes. I completed my playthrough using the Oculus Rift CV1.

☐ Casual Gamer
☑ Normal Gamer
☑ Expert

If you are used to smooth locomotion in VR in games like Pavlov and Blade & Sorcery, you won’t have many issues with the locomotion in Boneworks. The climbing can be awkward with your hands getting stuck to the environment, but I found after an adjustment period I didn’t run into as many issues. If you experience motion sickness while playing VR games, especially those with smooth locomotion, I can’t recommend Boneworks. Until a quick save or save-in-level is added to the game playing in short bursts is not easy with the game only saving on ‘scene’ transitions. Taking breaks is therefore harder, and if you're struggling with the locomotion you WILL need to take breaks.

The music of the game is fitting to the theme and I have no complaints about anything with any songs. My only complaint is the heavy repetition of songs throughout the game, with seemingly only 5 key tracks playing throughout the story. Be prepared to hear the same song many times if you get stuck on a puzzle or area.

☑ Average

Your standard fare for VR shooters. Gunplay is quick and easy to learn with no major issues. Enemies are rarely an issue with the near-death mechanic like that of Gorn where whilst dying getting a kill will bring you back from the edge. Puzzles aren’t amazingly hard, but I found myself overthinking some leading to me spending more time than necessary in some areas.

Bugs

☐ Lots of bugs
☑ Few Bugs
☐ You can use them for speedrun

As other reviews have stated, the hands having collision with everything is a problem with the IK arms bugging out at times. Climbing would benefit from some changes to the hands and would resolve my issues with it. A couple of times throughout my run objects I dropped seemingly phased through the ground, or outright despawned. Losing guns halfway through a level can be an issue and I am fortunate it wasn’t a key or battery that I dropped.

Story
☐ There is none
☐ Bad
☑ Alright
☐ Good
☐ Fantastic

The trailer includes the final cutscene of the game. There’s not much to the story beyond the surface and it makes me feel like SL0 had something grander in mind, but it had to be taken out for one reason or another. The game also ends rather abruptly in my mind, thinking I’d missed something when the credits started rolling at what felt like the third act’s opener.

Gameplay
☐ Frustrating
☐ Sleepy
☐ Boring
☑ Fun
☐ Challenging

If motion sickness wasn’t a problem, I would say this game is a must have for everyone who owns a VR headset. Shooting didn’t get old and the puzzles were varied enough to keep me engaged for the whole story and even wanting more to do. The sandbox mode is a nice break from the story though is entirely based on you placing items in the reclamation bin at the end of scenes or buying the collectibles from vending machines. The arena mode could extend this game’s playtime for you but after playing 7 rounds I felt like I’d seen all it had to offer.

Game time / Length
☐ Really short (0 - 3 hours)
☑ Short (4 - 8 hours)
☑ Few hours (10 - 20 hours)
☐ Long (40-60 hours)

VR Games often include some form of endless game mode to extend themselves if you want to keep playing. The story for Boneworks will take around 8 hours total to beat if you explore and get stuck on puzzles etc. It’ll take longer if you ‘accidentally’ quit a scene mid-game as there is no mid-level save option available. Sandbox and Arena offer more to do in the game and going back through every level to unlock everything in Sandbox mode will stretch out your time with the game. The story being quite bare in terms of its depth makes replaying it easy enough, plus a scene selector to quickly hop to areas you liked the most and wanted to play again means not having to slog through early tutorial type areas such as the museum.

Price / Quality
☑ Full price
☐ Wait for Sale
☐ Don't buy
☐ Refund it if you can

If you want one of the most rounded VR experiences with a story and good mechanics, full price shouldn’t be questioned for Boneworks. I personally am happy with what I got for that amount and can see myself come back to play more of Boneworks than my 11 hours total currently. The premium you pay for VR games in general extends to Boneworks, as in a typical format I’d say the game is slightly overpriced, while in VR it stands as one of the best on the market.

Final comments/thoughts
Boneworks would seriously benefit from a mid-level save feature as many have said. Longer scenes can take up to an hour to beat and if you are only used to quick bursts of VR it is asking a lot from you to dedicate that time. The game tells you to take breaks in the museum level, as I would advise doing so but without quitting the scene or game till you beat the scene. If a mid-level save feature is implemented post-launch I can happily recommend the game to more casual VR players as they won’t have to fight with motion sickness or other factors just for the sake of actually saving their progress.
