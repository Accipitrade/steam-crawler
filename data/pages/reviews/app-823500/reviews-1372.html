\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Game is good, real good, but it has some striking flaws imo.

For one, the story is barely there. Like it isn't even like other games where there's something happening in the background, it's more just like window dressing. You kind of just follow the path and explore here and there. It's nice, but the pacing is all over the place in the level design.

Two, there really isn't that much gun-based weapon variety in the game. There isn't even a shotgun in the main campaign, only pistols and light-medium automatic weapons. This is mostly mitigated by the creativity allowed by the level design though, I find the gunplay with the pistols very tight and using anything as a melee weapon is awesome.

The enemy variety is very lacking as well, with only a handful of enemies in the main campaign, probably around half a dozen enemies if i'm not mistaken, and that's being generous.

The climax of the game is also very forced and strange, you'll know what I mean when you get there, but it really isn't satisfying, although the buildup is great to it. The music is a great highlight, but it'll really give your shoulders a burn, lets just say that much.

I'm mentioning all this because in spite of all this negative ♥♥♥♥, I wholeheartedly recommend the game. The gameplay is so damn tight, and so satisfying, exploring the world is just a treat, even if in most cases you don't get too much of a reward for doing so (through the main campaign at least). It really makes me excited for Half Life Alyx, because this game feels like a stepping stone to a true AAA game in vr, with millions of dollars in budget and a full team of employees working on it, which is damn good for a 30 dollar indie game.\t\t\t\t\t

This creates the effect that the virtual world is moving up and down... it is very disconcerting and may cause sever motions sickness to sensitive people ... this happens only when in game, when in the game loading area, it's ok..... i assume this issue occurs because the virtual body the player has, has it's own inertia which on top of the inertia of the player real body, creates this lag where the up and down movement lags behind... very strange... i wander is this was a technical limitation.

Devs, you are aware of this, right ?

2. The campaign is ok, but nothing to right home about. and it does feel like a tech demo at times.

Grabbing stuff it's not that great... your hands kind of stick to things when you close your fingers on it... the movement of your fingers is gradual, but the grab it's binary ... hand to explain.

Good things about the game :

- The engine and physics are revolutionary. the game runs great for what is on screen.

- Weapons handing is great most of the time.

- There are moments of horror in this game which are great.

- i don't know how i'm going to be able to go back and play other games which don't have such incredible physics O.o


Over all the game is worth the money if you are able to stomach the up and down head motion lag the game has. i will switch this review to recommended in a few weeks or when the devs reply to this review .\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review will be updated as I play through more of it.

This game is a massive disappointment thus far, and I'm not even going to talk about the lack of WMR support or left handed modes at launch since I fit into neither category, but this game just doesn't work out.

The IK body they got sounds nice, even looks nice, but in gameplay it is the worst thing imaginable. Climbing is flat out impossible at times as your body gets stuck just still when grabbing onto objects (like what) and there is no momentum to help you over ledges and the like. You may counter this by crouching or even using virtual crouching but the problem is still present. Compare this to a VR game which accomplished climbing WAAAAY better, The Climb which was released nearly 4 years ago and STILL does it better, and this game in comparison is horrific in implementation. Interactions with objects are also finnicky (playing on a CV1 Oculus), and not very intuitive unlike other VR titles I've played.

The puzzles are inane and easily one of the worst aspects about this game so far, like one where your faced with a turret where you must pass. If the turret catches you for a second you're dead, so you would need to get some cover right? The forklift that was next was clue enough, so I use it and DEAD, my character utters a death grunt reminiscent of constipation "AAA IM DOYING" and I get sent back to the beginning of the level. While this may work for a game like Half-Life, the controls and flexibility to manipulate your environment is just not good enough and feels lackluster at best. Later I got around this, but still bad game design at it's finest.

Also load times on every level are infinite unless I reboot the game and manually select the level which is a massive drawback. The devs honestly should've spent more time honing out the accessibility options before releasing this in it's current state as while I do in fact like some aspects (shooting in general is solid), (gravity section was interesting), it lacks heavily in making this an enjoyable experience to say the least.\t\t\t\t\t
