\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't get sick easily in VR so I can't say much to that end but this game delivered exactly what I wanted it to. Everything is a little wobbly but to be expected in a completely physics based game like this.

Gunplay was simple to get used to if you've used guns in any other vr game and just the right amount of weird to use to still be fun while also making the enemies (who for the most part just walk at you) seem threatening.

Best part was the physics for sure. It can be weird when your hand isn't where you want it to be if it happened to get stuck on something but you get used to it and overall it added a huge amount of immersion compared to other games.

Worst part was some of the puzzle design. The best puzzles were obvious from the get go what to do and the enjoyment was trying to actually do it. e.g. seeing a button you need to press but you have to climb a wall and drag some platforms in the way then platform across. Some puzzles though were either just vast rooms with a lock on a door somewhere and you have to go hunt around for a key or there are just 3 unlabeled switches and buttons that don't seem to do anything but you know you have to get through the room somehow.

All in all, a very very enjoyable game. Took me about 8 hours to complete over 6 or 7 play sessions because VR itself is exhausting but that felt like a lot of gameplay as I went through it. This is also the first game I've ever reviewed just because I like it so much and fear people skipping out on it. This feels like the Portal of VR games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS REVIEW


Played on a ALIENWARE 15R3 with a HTC VIVE and INDEX controllers Have an INDEX but thats for a custom build and couldnt wait to play.

First off...IF YOU HAVE NOT YET DEVELOPED YOUR VR LEGS YOU MIGHT WANT TO WAIT. It can cause a bit of nausea and Ive been in VR for about 2 1/2 years now and still felt ill a few times when I started. About fell over a time or two while running and something caught my eye and I turned my head to look...while moving the headset determines the direction you are moving, and to suddenly took to look at something in another direction that you are moving sends you off that direction. Your brain says WTF your body is facing this way and now were moving that way and you may get a bit disoriented and sick. If you havent experienced VR sickness its like car sickness, sea sickness and air sickness all rolled into one (thats my opinion anyway) and BONEWORKS (BW) will push that. That said BW is FUk!NG AMAZING. I have never before wanted to jump back into a VR game as I have with this. Yeah Ive got BEAT SABER and its the go-to when weve got people over and they just love it, and its got the most time played in VR but deep down I want to tell them all to leave so I can play BW.

BW is not without its flaws. Some of the climbing can be a bit jittery and if you dont pull your hands close to your body while trying to climb they can get stuck at times, and make sure that no fingers are touching the controllers (INDEX) or you will probably be holding on to something. There isnt a cloud save that I have seen yet, which is why I am playing it on my ALIENWARE and not my custom build...I started it there, and yes I know I can move the save file, but come on do I really need to take the extra steps. Some of the physics seem a bit off. At times I find myself thinking I would be able to lift something like this over my head, but the game wont let you.
