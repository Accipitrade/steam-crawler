\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed on Oculus Rift S:

At times playing Boneworks feels like a revolution, a true vision into the what the future for VR gaming holds and that feeling is a magical thing that most games aren't even close to being capable of. While yes it isn’t perfect and has an amount of issues larger to another major VR title like Half-Life: Alyx, the true ambition of this project is really something to behold. Easily the best aspect is the physics, the world is completely driven by them and with the inclusion of actually having a physical body no matter how janky it is really connects and immerses you in the world that basically no other VR game has. Boneworks has the best instance of locomotion in any VR title I've played so far with smooth and responsive movement that doesn't make me motion sick in the slightlest. Almost every object, enemy, and prop is weighted to have some sort of calculable effect on the world around and coupled with some non-linear level design and a robust inclusion of traversal mechanics, the use of creativity in the virtual space has never felt so cool whether you’re jumping in real life and in the game to leap over insurmountable gaps or simply scrambling over a fence. The way Boneworks uses the 3D and your physical actions in the gameplay is poggers.

The combat is generally also fantastic with what there is but I can’t help but feel that the arsenal is lacking. There are FPS standards like assault rifles, pistols, and smgs but thats about it with the lack of any shotguns, grenades, explosive weapons, and gimmick weapons feeling strangely absent in a world that could so clearly be shaped by it. It could be so empowered to get an RPG and shoot down a wall that was blocking your entrance or having a harpoon gun to launch enemies into the distance and impale them on geometry. However, the weapons provided still feel very satisfying and Boneworks has some of the best reloading and inventory mechanics I’ve played in VR thus far. On the other hand, the melee combat is only alright with the two-handed weapons feeling janky and weightless and the one-handed/bladed weapons feeling like the only good ones to use.

Easily my biggest issue with Boneworks is the lack of variety found in both aesthetic and enemy variety. Every level is styled with the same grayish corporate aesthetic and while the actual scale and set dressing changed, I wish that the levels had gotten more atmospheric or varied with the farthest that the game strays from that previously mentioned look is an extra dark sewer level with night vision goggles and tons of zombies. The lack of enemy variety is an equally big issue with there really only being three enemies: soldiers, zombies, and crablets. The lack of any growth or variation beyond the opening three levels means that the difficulty plateaus halfway through and I would’ve at least like to see the challenge mount up for all of the post-game content.

Despite those gripes, Boneworks is a truly fantastic game and one of the best VR experiences out there that truly shows what the future of the medium can hold. The emergent systems and mechanics were brilliant and Bonelab has quickly become my most anticipated VR game for the future.\t\t\t\t\t
