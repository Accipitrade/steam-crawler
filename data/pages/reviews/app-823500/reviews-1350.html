\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really don't understand people ripping on this game. It is INCREDIBLE. I'm just a few hours in, and I've barely progressed I think because of how much fun it is to just explore and see what's possible. The environment is so cool, the "training" museum was jaw dropping. Yeah, things can get janky, it's a video game. With freedom of movement comes the freedom to mess things up. In games where you can't jump there's little chance of you clipping into some wall or something. In a game where you can literally climb half the surfaces...yeah there's going to be some clipping into things. Don't go off the main path and you'll have almost nothing glitchy.

And for anyone saying this isn't a "real game," or has no story, or is just a tech demo...this is as much of a real game as the portal games. So if you enjoyed the Portals, you'll probably enjoy this. If you think Portal had no story and was just a tech demo, then yeah you'll think that about this as well. The story is woven into the game. There's no chatty companion so maybe it's less noticeable, but the world is really rich.

As of now, for me this is the best VR game ever made, full stop. Play a few hours before going after it.\t\t\t\t\t

The combat is one of my favorite parts, and smashing the "null bodies" to bits with a hammer is a good time. The vibe is very cool, a very delicate balance of different elements to really give that dirty digital laboratory-esc game that no other game has. I do wish that you could somehow save your game in the menu, and boot up the game to that exact location with your weapons/tools. The only large complaint i have is that holding things is a chore. If you have any other controller than the oculus touch, you have to hold down the grip button every time you want to grab something, which isn't fun. Clearing a building with a gun makes your middle finger hurt. Overall, i would give this game a really solid 8.5 out of ten with an outstanding story mode and interactivity factor, brought down only by a simple binding, some gun-nut nitpicking and slight saving annoyances. I highly reccomend this game to anyone with a VR headset, and with a price of only 30 dollars, it's a must-have staple in everyone's VR library, up with Beat Saber and H3VR.\t\t\t\t\t
