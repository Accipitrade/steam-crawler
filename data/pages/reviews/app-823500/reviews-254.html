\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the most complete game experience on VR at the moment. I want to give this a glowing review but honestly the game is a little short after you cut out all the puzzles and opening museum segment. Near the end though you get 4-5 levels of pure combat goodness that is just great to eat up. I wish the whole game was like that but I've seen streamers give up after the opening tutorial because it took so long.

Also I hate the climbing tutorial, It was fun at first but jumping from one platform to another in VR in the first person is impossible. Then falling and having to go through it again is worse. Why would you put a jumping puzzle in this game? I think you can beat this entire game without jumping if you want. It feels unnecessary and aggravating to gate the rest of the game off unless you do the jumping puzzle.

gun play, level design, combat, everything else works great. I just don't see the point in adding puzzles to VR, they aren't enhanced much by requiring me to bend and twist and stretch just to grab a puzzle item. They feel more annoying than anything else and they aren't optional.

The 50% of this game that's combat I can recommend, the 50% of pushing carts and physics objects around like it's half life 2 is terrible but worth slogging through just to reach the combat. They don't put the cart pushing in the trailer or screenshots I'll tell ya that much.\t\t\t\t\t
