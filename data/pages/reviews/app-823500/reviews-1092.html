\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe closest thing to an actual "VR game" I've seen yet, but still disappointing. It has its moments, and it's not bad, but it's not great either.

Main issues:
1. The physics simulation can be satisfying in some areas, and frustratingly unreliable in others (especially when using Vive controllers).
2. The story is forgettable and vague and ultimately unnecessary.
3. There is a distinct lack of direction in some areas. Often the puzzles appear particularly difficult because the game leaves you no indication of what you're supposed to do. Other times you may become stuck because there's a grate or other obstacle that you didn't know you could push open.
4. The game seems to fall short of its potential. By the time I finished my playthrough, I had never felt truly challenged or impressed.
5. There's a real lack of style and substance, leaving the gameplay pretty bland. In the end it kind of felt like an ammo box-opening simulator with some puzzles and shooting lumped in.\t\t\t\t\t
