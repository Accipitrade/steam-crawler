\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is one of the best VR games that you can play right now, but it's not one without its flaws.

  • Climbing ledges is awkward, there should just be a button that pulls you up once you reach a certain point.
  • Climbing in general also feels very wobbly, the player moves like a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ helium balloon in the wind while climbing.
  • Combat is incredibly fun and melee is satisfying when the weapons don't get stuck indefinitely inside enemies.
  • Using any object in the environment as a weapon or a shield is brilliant.
  • Snap turning with a long weapon is a mistake (it's wobble time).
  • Arms don't always sync with your actions and often get in the way, and it only made me realize why they have floating hands in other VR games.
  • The headset crabs work really well as an enemy in VR combat.
  • Puzzles are... there.
  • Having the really long ladder as the final boss is really underwhelming. Maybe put the King there for a rematch.
  • Despite the intentional tech-demo aesthetic, level design and visuals leave much to be desired.

Don't let these criticisms to take away from my overall positive experience with the game, I want it and future projects by these devs to be better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHmmm. There is a lot to like about this game - but there are some problems.

Well, firstly it is slick, it is polished and the game play is (mostly) good fun.

Right - now onto the problems...

Okay. I don't get nauseous playing VR games, but I do with this - a bit. I can play about half an hour feeling okay and then need a break. I spend the time during my break wandering about dizzily mis-judging door frames and looking at my hands. If you've played it you will know why. This may not sound like much of a problem. Half an hour chunks is probably fine. Well it would be if the levels didn't take longer than half an hour to complete with no check point system. This is a HUGE problem and renders the game more or less unplayable for me. "Ah the museum level again. How I've missed it."

Towards the end of the level (or at least, what I hope will be the end - how do I know?) I find myself rushing past things that look interesting, and trying to short cut anything to get to the check point before the next dry heave. This is as much fun as an anorexia party.

I use HTC Vive. Not the best VR system on the market but not bad for entry level. The controllers are a little chunky and while the tracking is excellent they can get in the way a bit. When reloading a weapon you need to move your hands rapidly, one holding the gun and one pulling a little slidy thing. I'm sure gun nuts will tell me the little slidy thing is called a ♥♥♥♥-rearm-actuator or something. Well whatever - you have to pull your ♥♥♥♥-rearm-actuator to re-load. Not as much fun as it sounds. Especially when it always results in the controllers banging into each other.

I have a similar issue with climbing which seems to result in me hitting myself in the face with the controller.

Oh - the climbing. The level design can be quite annoying and often results in fairly tedious busy-work climbing and re-climbing because of a mid-judged jump. It's annoying. Especially when you've punched yourself in the face a few times.

In short the game is a must for anyone seeking to lose weight rapidly or with an interest in smashing their controllers into their face - or each other.\t\t\t\t\t
