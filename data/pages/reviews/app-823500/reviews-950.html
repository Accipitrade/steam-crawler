Cons
-Weak plot

Half-Life + Portal + Garrys Mod, in VR
Minus the excellent storytelling, BONEWORKS could easily pass as a Valve-made game. It clearly has the fingerprints of both Half-Life, Portal, Garrys Mod all over it. Half-Life and Portal for the atmosphere; trapped all alone in a bleak, mysterious world, progressing through the map little by little. A mix of all three for the gameplay: equipped with a realistic physics engine; and the manipulation of said engine for combat, solving physics puzzles, or simply just messing around in its sandbox world.

Setting and Style
BONEWORKS is set in itself, a fictional engine also called Boneworks, running an operating system called MythOS which is a virtual city. You travel through the different levels set in MythOS, solving different types of puzzles to progress.

The levels clearly have a game under development or work-in-progress aesthetic with its chaotic artstyle, generic gridlike floors, and wireframe / robotic models for NPCs. Because of this unique aesthetic and virtual reality setting, BONEWORKS clearly got away from the hassle of creating fully finished and lifelike set pieces, and also makes all the genuine in-game bugs and glitches quite bearable.

The realism and physics engine
Despite the work-in-progress aesthetic of its virtual world, BONEWORKS is quite high on the realism scale of video games. Almost everything can be picked up, thrown, and manipulated. Objects like boxes, glasswares, and electronics are destructible. Various types of weapons to use: Sledgehammers, Crowbars, Baseball Bats, Knives, Swords, Automatic Guns, Pistols, and many more. Combat is just as realistic. Weapon handling and melee combat is on par or even better than other VR contemporaries. There is gun recoil, manual reloading (where I find quite inconsistent), and believable haptics. Melee combat requires actual swings and stabs to inflict damage.

All objects have different weights and are well simulated by the game for VR gameplay, both in its physical characteristics and in handling. It is as authentic as it can be, and pushes the VR platform to another level when it comes to interaction with the virtual world. This results in a fun, unscripted, and spontaneous sandbox gameplay experience. Its not perfect though, as sometimes you will encounter physics bugs: body parts getting stuck to walls, objects clipping and getting tangled with each other, among other examples.

Going for realism, they naturally decided to implement fully rendered arms, feet, and bodies, and they work fine to an extent. To provide that sense of realism that the developers wanted to convey. Visually its very realistic. In practice, its not the most immersive experience; your arms get clunky all the time as only the hands have actual tracking.

Both your virtual arms and body constantly block your view and it messes with the immersion when you see them not in sync with your actual arms and body. This is also the case when using moving around and you notice your virtual feet walking / running while youre actually just standing still or seating. I wish there was an option to hide them and render just the hands like in Half-Life: Alyx.

The inventory system is typical. Two slots for each shoulder, three slots around your body plus an ammo pouch. Its not as accurate as I wanted it to be, sometimes I get the wrong things out of my body and I fail to pick up/slot objects on my shoulders, but its fine for the most part. You can also access an exploded view of the inventory by using the quick menu for a more convenient operation, a fine little neat feature that all VR games should game.

Puzzle-Platformer
The main gameplay element of BONEWORKS is its puzzle solving mechanics combined with platformer gameplay to progress through the levels. Most of the levels themselves are just one big physics playground that you need to solve through the use of mainly the physics engine: manipulation, pushing, pulling, throwing, running, and jumping around the world.

The puzzles are clearly inspired by Half-Life and Portal. From stacking up boxes to reach the next area, or putting some heavy objects to human-sized floor buttons to keep doors open. Thanks to VR, you are really immersed in the scale of the levels. Falling from great heights seems more terrifying now, jumping through obstacles are more tense than ever.

Niche in a Niche - extreme comfort levels
With VR gaming already as niche as it is right now, promoting it as a VR experts only game makes it even more niche, and rightfully so. It certainly requires previous VR experience to quickly familiarize with the game mechanics and develop resistance to VR sickness. It is definitely not for everyone.

Combined with the aforementioned fully rendered arms, feet, and body, there is no teleportation movement, only full locomotion movement. Buyer beware for those who cannot handle full locomotion. There is a jump mechanic in the game and works just as any other game. You just press a button to jump, though there is a slight delay. All these along with the already platformer and jumpy nature of the game, adds up and results in a very uncomfortable experience for me. I had my own share of VR sickness while playing, as opposed to other games like Half-Life: Alyx, or The Walking Dead: Saints and Sinners, where I could play for hours at a time without discomfort.

Story and Characters
The weakest part of BONEWORKS. It almost has no story in it and does little to entice players or give them reason to advance the game from a story standpoint. The plot itself is very vague and ambiguous, maybe to save the writers the trouble of having to write an actual plot.

Theres hardly any characters in BONEWORKS, and they are all forgettable, unessential, and have zero personality. You probably won't even remember their names while playing throughout the game, including the main character. Enemies are also as generic as they can be.

Technical Performance
The game runs smoothly even with the resolution set to 120% on my rig: i7-8700k, 32GB RAM, and GTX 1080. No crashes, game-breaking bugs, or major glitches I've encountered. Using an Oculus Quest with ALVR, I was able to play wirelessly and use the movement scheme to its fullest, without analog turning. Graphics are beautiful for a VR game although the textures are noticeable a bit low quality resolution wise.

Conclusion
BONEWORKS pushes the VR platform to another level when it comes to interaction with the virtual world. A fun and spontaneous sandbox VR platformer. I recommend this game, only if you can handle its extreme comfort levels and already have substantial experience with VR games.
