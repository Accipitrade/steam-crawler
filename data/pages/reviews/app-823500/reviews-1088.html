\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou'll see a lot of reviews saying this is the best VR game ever, which can brush over your head.

But let me tell you why, in my opinion this is currently one of the most revolutionary VR experiences you can get your hands on.

<<<The Good>>>
Boneworks has one of the most passionate, talented, and skilled VR indie devs behind it. Stress Level Zero have aimed to innovate and make VR better, rather than sticking to what is safe and known already. This game is a result of that ambition, and it really shows in the quality of the entire experience.

The physics in this game feel spectacular, even if you don't have any actual haptic feedback, you still "feel" the weight of objects. They've had a good balance of where you hands ARE and where your hands WOULD be if you actually felt the weight, and it tricks your brain in all the best ways. They've taken the time to fine-tune the strength of your character, the strength of materials and objects, and the strength of enemies into account, and it all feels real. I think the best example of this is I didn't even notice how good they were, because it all felt so intuitive initially.

The level designs are intricately detailed, full of secrets, and well paced. Each level is unique and interesting to go through, and the amount of replayability is really nice (especially in later Story levels, as there are several corridors to potentially explore). I never got bored on my first run through, and still don't as I look for all the secrets I can (and some are really well hidden!) and I'm always discovering new little things about the game and the levels I go through.
To add on this, the Story of this game is told through the levels, things written on the walls, and the way things function. There are displays, things written by things before you, and just a few other things that keep the mystery of the world going, and it creates an answer in everyone's head differently, without the game necessarily telling you what's happening 100%. It's nice, and it's good to have levels that aren't there for flavor text. The journey you go on has a reason and everything that exists within MythOS assembles itself in a story that starts making sense when you take the time to thoroughly look through the levels.

As previously mentioned, what encourages you to find secrets is the fun to be had in the Sandbox mode, which you can unlock different NPCs and objects by finding them in secret boxes hidden around levels, and thrown into reclamation bins. It's fun trying to figure out things, especially since there are very few guides or videos on it, so finding something feels like a "first-time" experience because you haven't heard about it from anywhere else.

The Arena mode is a great touch to really go against the AI in a focused setting and the challenges are different enough and make sense given the game's use of physics. It feels satisfying to complete your first Hard difficulty challenge, and even if the spawns are the same, each fight feels unique and engaging.

When you hit something, it HITS and it feels satisfying every time you nail something in the head. The shooting as well feels satisfying and doesn't feel too punishing with recoil at all. Both of these are enhanced by the Slow Motion button you have on your left controller, which tapped (once - x2 slowed, twice x4 slowed, thrice x8 slowed) feels absolutely amazing and makes you feel like an action hero at times. This can also aid in tight situations in arena or the Story, as it can give you a second to think about what to do without necessarily having to pause (which you can't anyway) but it's a satisfying and welcome feature that I miss in other games of a similar scope and style.

The Sound Design and Soundtrack are masterful. There's not much else to say other than it fully immerses you and grounds you in the world. The ambiance can make an area feel comfortable or intimidating and the soundtrack really helps the mood of it. This is with any soundtrack though, so gushing about how amazing it is, is sorta pointless :)

<<<The 'Bad'>>>
As with any video game real time physics, you WILL have some inconsistencies. Things get stuck in awkward ways, your arms go through objects more than you may like, and stuff can get caught on your character, leading to some awkward moments. This isn't a dealbreaker, but it's noticeable enough to mention. But to me, that adds to the charm and fun of the game. If EVERYTHING did how I wanted, it wouldnt be Boneworks, because I'm using logic from other games that let you flail everything like a paperweight and pass through walls and objects, and your person with ease. It MAKES you think about how you're handling something, just like you would IRL.

That's it really, no other complaints with this game.

<<<Conclusion>>>
"But Ki" I hear you say. "This sounds like any standard game with physics, what's so special about Boneworks?"
VR my friend, VR is what makes a physics game fully click and immersive. This is one of the first times I genuinely got fully immersed given all the reasons previously stated. It's as unforgettable as Half Life, and if you were awed by the first time you took the tram through the facility, this game will give you that same feeling ten-fold.

Few bugs, great physics, soundtrack, gameplay, pacing, AI, etc.
All make for a game I simply have to keep playing, and I'm excited to see where SL0 takes this game further.

9.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tArena mode is fine. I would give it a thumbs up if the Arena didn't require story completion.

However, every time I come back to it and try to mess with the campaign I am reminded of how horribly designed it is. Replaying some games makes you appreciate them more. Boneworks is a prime example of the opposite. This IS a glorified tech demo and a failure as a game. A lot of cool stuff on the tech side, but Stress Level Zero seriously needs to hire a level designer. These are some of the most unenjoyable campaign maps I have ever played. The VR immersion doesn't fix the sheer amount of nonsense in layout and pacing. Every time there's a genuinely enjoyable moment in the campaign, it lasts for about 45 seconds and then you're back to wandering around trying to figure out where these ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ put keys and collectables.

An incredible amount of wasted potential in these cool moments. I'm so glad you guys decided to give us a fun to drive segway in the very last segment of the game. A vehicle that lets you do drive bys with an uzi. This item is implemented in the last moments of the game instead of on say level 2. On a large flat plane with no real gameplay. No obstacle course, no track, no puzzle. Just a flat plane with lights on it guiding you to the end. That's it. Real good application of the assets you made. Great work.

This review would turn into 20 paragraphs if I were to list all my complaints about the campaign.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe reason I got into VR.
But, that doesn't mean it's perfect.

Pros:
Full physics modelling! Your body is a physics object so collisions are a thing. Want to headbutt a bad guy? Grab him and go to town! Punch a boarded up door? Let rip!

Slow motion; Want to be John Wick/Neo? At the press of a button (multiple presses slows it down further) And you too can dodge bullets, reload and shoot like Baba Yaga and The One had some mad, immaculate baby.

Graphics: One of the better looking VR games in my small experience. The world design is fantastic with some heavy nods to Half Life and the Aperture Science/Black Mesa style.

Brett Driver; Okay, bit of a man-crush here but Brett as the protagonist is great. Famed for saying 'what up son' I thoroughly enjoyed his voicework


Cons:
The first issue I came across is this is NOT a game for people that suffer from motion sickness in VR. I found this out the hard way in the first few minutes, but I may be one of the lucky people that becomes accustomed quickly as I no longer have issues.

Issue two is the lack of checkpoints/saving, currently the game only saves at the end of a level. A big problem if you enjoy exploring as a level can but I have heard this is being addressed in a future update.

There are also some physics glitches, I've found myself welded to geometry on a couple of occasions, but some mad flailing generally fixes that.\t\t\t\t\t
