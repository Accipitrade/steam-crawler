Alyx certainly tops Boneworks in visuals, audio, and polish, but I argue that Boneworks pushes VR gameplay much farther than Alyx does. Boneworks redefined the gameplay possibilities of a VR game with a physics based environment, full physical body, full locomotion, and more. This game ignores all of the "don't"s of VR and actually feels like a video game in VR compared to a more standard teleport shooter.
I love Alyx, but it has different goals compared to Boneworks. Alyx's goal is to create a AAA, smooth, and polished VR experience to the masses, and it's exceeds tremendously. I'd even say it's a better game than Boneworks. But it would be a crime to pass up Boneworks, and even [i[ if [/i]you've played Alyx, this game will blow your mind. Trust me.\t\t\t\t\t

My personal thoughts
I have not completed the game fully but from what I have played this is one of my personal favorite Vr games. I am going to start with this; this game is stupidly immersive and I love it. I have always dreamed of being so immersed in a game that when I am out of it I can barely tell the difference between realities. This game has achieved my dream for me. Yes, this game has its problems with having a mid range computer but it rarely ruins the immersion for myself. I can not wait until I am able to upgrade and just lose myself in VR.

Gameplay experiences
Now on from my personal feelings and dreams. This game is fun as hell, I love just smashing and throwing things, the realistic physics really give the game the unique aspect of what every "immersive" VR game is trying to achieve and is done so well. I love how pretty much every object you can interact with, still a sorta unique thing about this game, but definite others have achieved.
Another aspect of the game I adore is the movement and how well it is done, yes there are some problems with it but every game has to have some kind of flaw. Just the feeling of crouching and jumping across a platform is so satisfying to me in a way I can not explain. With that, it comes along with the sense that I am actually strong. I am going to admit, I am not one of the strongest of people in the world, I can not pull myself up a platform, but in this game I get to mimic that ability and strength. My body is seemingly weightless in the game, I can jump, climb and run without feeling any sort of fatigue besides maybe my arms hurting from reaching up and down so much from climbing,

Problems that I experienced
For me there was a sort of big learning curve. I have played a lot of vr and have never had problems with controls but for some reason this game kept messing me up. I would try to jump or climb but find myself pressing a different button from others messing me up in a part that I have worked so hard to get to but have to restart. After around 2 hours I started to get used to the controls, though sometimes I still mess up, I would still call it a learning curve for myself but I am not sure about others' experiences. Another problem I had experienced was pulling myself up things, I had never done this in this sort of way in any other VR game which is why I think I had such a problem getting used to having to do a variety of things to make sure myself would not fall.

Summary
Overall this game is amazing, from the immersion, to my first experiences and problems in the game I am still left satisfied overall. This game is %100 worth the price in my opinion, so much work has gone into perfecting this game and I am still left smiling after I beat to death every glass bottle I have found watching the glass shards fly everywhere. Get this game if you want immersion or just a great time, you'll find yourself playing around with objects and smashing bottles just like me.
