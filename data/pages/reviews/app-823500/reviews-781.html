\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis physics-based VR FPS title is a great game that I'd highly recommend for most people with a VR headset, although there are a few flaws with this game that may be deal-breakers for some.

The gameplay is a lot of fun and is very solid for the most part. The mechanics for picking up weapons and ammo is very satisfying and so is using guns to dispatch enemies. Melee weapons also work well for the most part, although I sometimes found fighting enemies in close quarters to be a bit disorienting and frustrating. Climbing was definitely the mechanic in this game that was the most difficult and it took some getting used to, but after some experimentation I eventually was able to learn how to climb fairly well such that I was able to overcome most obstacles with relative ease. The only element of gameplay that I really wasn't a fan of was the collectables system. The game doesn't tell you which items you've already obtained outside of the main menu, you can only save items at pre-determined points in a level, and you can only carry a limited number of items at one time, which led to a lot of frustrating and unnecessary inventory management.

The visuals of this game are fairly good, although they start to fall apart a little bit as you explore more. On my first playthrough I noticed almost no issues with visuals and found the environments to be very immersive, but as I began to explore a lot more on later playthroughs, I began to notice that many areas that were more difficult to reach were often much lower quality and there are a number of areas where textures were missing or broken. Given that you usually don't see most of these areas unless you do a lot of extra exploration though, I didn't find that this substantially detracted from my experience.

For me personally, the story in this game was fairly weak. While the game does have very intriguing environments and story elements, I didn't find that the story was presented well, and I was extremely confused when completing the game for the first time. I ended up figuring out most of the story from online research, although even the information available online is incomplete and full of unanswered questions. The story that does exist is very intriguing though, so I wish it was presented better. The story has a lot of potential, but fell a bit short for me personally.

This game was fairly stable as well, although it had some issues here too. This game does suffer from the "jank" that is common in many physics-based titles and I ran into a variety of physics-based issues fairly frequently, often finding myself getting stuck inside objects, having items clips through walls/floors, etc. I also occasionally had some performance issues with some levels running very poorly, and sometimes had to restart the game to clear these up. For the most part I was able to overlook these issues, but they definitely caused me some frustration more than once.

TL;DR This is a good VR title that's a lot of fun to play, despite some very obvious and sometimes frustrating flaws. Although some of the mechanics are difficult to learn/use, inventory management is a chore, the story is fairly confusing, and the game doesn't always run the greatest, there isn't much out there for VR that is similar to this game, and if you're willing to overlook these flaws, it's a game that you can have a lot of fun with.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the better VR games out there. The rigging and physics is amongst the best and is maybe only beat by Alyx. There are a lot of weapons and the level design complements the physics engine and the platforming. The sound design is atmospheric and at times even spooky although the music can get repetitive. The story can be confusing but it is strangely compelling and has a surprising amount of depth that I was not expecting for such a cheesy "VR within VR" plot.

My major issue that is a deal breaker comes down to the awful saving/loading and the collectibles. Save points were recently added in specific places but they are finicky. Sometimes my inventory items were eaten and placing items on the box area for storage sometimes doesn't save them either. Loading a game resets the level so all enemies and certain puzzles reset. I really wish they had just added a modern saving system instead of these weird checkpoints.

If you are like me and love finding collectibles, this game will fill you with dread if you try to get all the collectibles. There are a whole ton of collectibles including weapons, npcs, and misc objects. The collectibles usually involve some type of physics puzzle or parkour in order to get. I really enjoyed these puzzles.

However, once you find a collectible, you have to take them to a reclamation bin to "cash in." The problem with this is that most reclamation bins can only be found at the end of the level. You have 5 inventory spaces on your body that are shared between weapons and collectibles. The limited space coupled with the aforementioned saving issues means you will have to finish a level in one go without exiting, taking excess items in both your hands and placing items in areas that you can backtrack to take them with you (except for areas where you can't backtrack so you are out of luck).

I love finding collectibles and I enjoyed the puzzles, but I was so frustrated when I found out my effort was for nothing and I had to leave the rewards because either the saving didn't save the items, I couldn't physically take the goodies to be reclaimed, or I accidentally reached the end of the level and lost all of my items.

So if you want to find collectibles, you have to memorize the level or play it multiple times just to find a bunch of random doodads to fill your collection. This could be fixed by just making collectibles something you keep once you find them, or allowing you cash them in anywhere, or adding a pouch with unlimited space that only has collectibles.

The saving issues coupled with the hassle of getting collectibles that does not respect my time makes me hesitant to recommend this if you like collectibles or don't have a couple of hours to play uninterrupted. Which is a shame, because I really enjoyed the rest of the game. But when I look back to what I played, I only remember feeling very frustrated.\t\t\t\t\t

To start,
I use the Oculus Quest 2 to run this game. The performance from the Quest is great, I play wirelessly via Virtual Desktop so its a really immersive experience, cables always ruin the fun...

The game
Is incredible, with a story full of collectables and places to explore keeping you busy, or sandbox arenas and a shooting range. Boneworks is a lot of fun, imagine a really basic and pretty different Gmod type vr game.... well in the Sandbox maybe more than the story.

I would love
To see more updates in the future with more content. The game seems to have a lot of room for additions, while it also currently has a lot. 2 dozen melee weapons, a dozen guns, all sorts of physics related nonesense, and what can beat knee capping a zombie, and then blowing his brains out in slow motion while jamming to intense house!

Graphics
: 8 / 10 - I've seen worse, but I also feel like it could be better. It could be my Quest, or how I am running it, but over all at 90 fps it's right near crystal clear.

Story
: 6 / 10 - Don't let this point bring your idea of the game down, it's good. I personally only half paid any attention the first time through and it really didn't make much sense to me, just a guy in a world with a reason to kill. Maybe I missed something, maybe that's that, honestly though a good 12ish hours to beat the campaign once for me.

Combat
: 9 / 10 - Phenomenal, I absolutely love it. The only real times problems occur is when the enemies end up face pressed to your cheek. Other than that it's smooth and fairly practical.

Soundtrack
: 10 / 10 - Fantastic, almost every track is a banger, a few are slow but equally bang worthy, on a more chill and calm note.

Physics
:9 / 10 - Just like the combat its relatively realistic, the developers intended you to use the world to help you maneuver. By that I mean, they expect you to try and mess with and climb everything in the world. You will find notes out of bounds that show that they knew you might eventually find a way.

I'm no expert but I'd give it a 9 / 10! It's fantastic, quirky, and tons of fun!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo be honest, I thought this game was gonna be crap, I mean, who wants a you arms to get caught on stuff. Well, I was wrong. It's extremely fun to play this game, though I have some problems with it, so I'll list them here.
The Bad:
* Not everything is unlocked nor can be unlocked easily, you have to put weapons in the reclaimation bin to use later. Why? It's such a waste of time trying to get all these weapons, the best way to avid this is after you've played the game, just replace the save file with one that has everything in it.

* Climbing sucks, to be honest they should have just disabled physics with climbing because your arms get in the way.

* Weapons feel weird to load. To eject a mag you have to smack the current mag (inside the gun) to eject it, I feels weird coming from Pavlov VR. Racking guns also feel weird, I can't tell you how many times I've hit myself in the face racking guns because it feels like it requires so much more effort than necessary.

* Some stuff isn't very obvious. I will admit, I am very dumb, and the puzzles stumped me because I couldn't figure them out. For example, the vending machines with guns don't tell you the currency is ammo, I just thought it was money or something.

*Blunt weapons suck, don't bother using them.

The Good:
* The soundtrack makes you feel badass when you shoot ♥♥♥♥, it's so fun!

* Physics are an awesome feature, you actually have to put a magazine into a gun, what a feature!

* The story is amazing, I'll admit I don't usually play campaigns (Because I usually don't have too) but it's great.

* Slow Motion is something I never knew I needed.

* You feel like a Jedi when you use the force to pull stuff towards you. Not gonna lie I did this in real life with my phone, then I realized that it's real life. lol.

* The feeling when you complete a puzzle. Oh yeah.

*The devs actually have a sense of humor.

* The guns are so satisfying to use, popping a cap into a person is SO SATISFYING.

* There's a stuffed animal in the main menu, what more could you ask for?

Should you buy this game? Absolutely. Oh yeah, one thing. This game is for EXPERENCED VR USERS ONLY!!!! That means that you should be used to walking in VR games because, you know, you have to use walking.\t\t\t\t\t
