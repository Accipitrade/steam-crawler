\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: This review is only about the main campaign, because I haven't played any of the extra content outside of the zombies mode.

This game gets kind of boring after the novelty wears off. Melee combat is hit and miss. It's fun to bully Nullmen in hand to hand combat by catching their hand when they swing at you and dragging them around while you beat them in the face. But using two handed weapons is pretty hard for no reason. You have to move your arms in a completely unintuitive way to do damage with them. Sometimes you don't even have to do anything for two handed weapons to register damage - sometimes all you have to do is walk towards enemies with your arms raised while holding the analog stick forward.

Gun combat is gun combat - it works the same like in every other VR game. Not much to say here other than it works as expected.

I didn't care much about the story.

This game is the kind of game where it has highly exploitable mechanics that break the game. For example: Climbing. Puzzles don't need any thought put into them when solving them if you can just climb everything. There are some advanced game breaking mechanics like slowing down time and using it to bug out the momentum mechanics to propel yourself high in the air - this breaks the game even more.

It definitely feels like a tech demo. Like a full priced tech demo.

The graphics aren't particularly impressive. They certainly look as if they could be easily ported to the Oculus Quest 2 with no issues.\t\t\t\t\t
