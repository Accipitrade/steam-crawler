\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRealistic physics seems like it would be a perfect fit in VR. Instead, it's more of an annoyance that makes movement cumbersome.
The introductory levels (after the main tutorial in the museum) are unreasonably difficult. It took me way longer than I want to admit on finding a key which was hidden in a stack of boxes on a stage. I had walked through the boxes to test out the physics and had busted a majority of them to see what was inside. I went to the next area and needed the key. Because I didn't hit that one box which contained the key I couldn't progress.
Gunplay is fine but the physical motion of cocking the mp5 had my virtual hands stuck because I didn't do a "jerking" motion. The game constantly would register my actions as "not hard enough" so I'd be stuck at a fence trying to break the wood for way longer than I would like.
The physics when they work are great but they just don't seem to lead to a fun immersive experience.
I got the game on release and still have yet to actually want to complete the campaign because it's just annoying to fiddle with the physics.
Also the Jumping and climbing up just outright does not work the way I want it to.
I bought the game on release and stopped playing in hopes that a new update would make things better but they just havn't for me personally.

I have not gotten far in this game and I just don't see myself coming back to it again. I really tried to like this game, I really really tried.\t\t\t\t\t
