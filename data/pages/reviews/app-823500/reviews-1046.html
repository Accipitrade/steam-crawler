\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS, is a VR physics masterpiece that in my opinion is held back by its complete reliance on playing the story for any other type of play as both the arena and sandbox areas are locked behind finding secret things in levels. I unlocked the sandbox after quite some effort so i could test and play around with the items, enemies and physics only to discover that in order to enjoy the "sandbox" i had to play the story and find the items and enemies.

Another issue with the game is the controls for non-index controllers, i have a Vive and im forced to hold down 2 buttons constantly if i want to fight or interact with things for an extended period which can get uncomfortable very fast. While i understand why they decided to do it like that it feels like something that wasnt tested as the game is very clearly meant to be played with the Index controllers.

My final issue is motion sickness, and before people go off on me for "not being VR experienced" i very much am. I have spent many hours in VR running around, fighting, jumping, falling, ect ect with it only really causing issues after an hour or two on constant play. Yet BONEWORKS makes me motion sick within 20-30 min of playing it, im not sure why thats the case but something about it just messes me up.

in conclusion, i believe that BONEWORKS is a game held back by the focus on its story with too high a focus on use of the Index controller at the expense of others that can cause motion sickness even in experienced VR players.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI understand why this game is getting a hugely positive reaction. I really wanted to like it, but to me it feels like a very early version of the VR games we're going to see.

The gunplay should have been good. Grabbing the gun and reloading it felt snappy and good, but he animations involved in it felt very janky, with my in game hand bending into weird shapes the whole time. This hand issue leads into one of my biggest gripes with the game.

I ended up returning the game because of two reasons. The first being the physical body. While a lot of people have talked about this like its a positive thing. I found it to be the most frustrating experience I've ever had in a game. I flat out couldn't use the assault rifle, because it would get caught in my shoulder and I couldn't see where it was supposed to be aiming. And my hands kept getting caught in my body when I was trying to fight. I barely managed to kill the first enemy because of these issues.

But the worst of all is the saving system. I don't know how they could develop a VR game like this without including a quick save system, or at the very least putting the save points closer together. The developers claimed to have done so much work testing it, and making sure the mechanics felt good, and forgot to bring in playtesters, or some kind of QA that would have told them a huge section of the market for the game wouldn't be able to get to the next save point without wanting to throw up. There is no reason for the save points to be so far apart. On top of being potentially nauseating, it was frustrating going through the same level over and over because I didn't know where it was going to save.

There are parts that are absolutely where things are going to move forward, but there is just as much that should be left behind in these early days of VR.\t\t\t\t\t
