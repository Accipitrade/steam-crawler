Purchased it December 10th 2019 Seconds after it released. One of the best purchases I've made, no regrets.

Another thing I'd like to note, because this deserves to be in the review, is the soundtrack. THE SOUNDTRACK, IT'S AMAZING. Michael Wyckoff really outdid himself on this one. I even listen to it on spotify on a regular basis. While Boneworks didn't nail every single part of itself, It's really something that's never been done before. It's great that we have developers like Stress Level Zero, showing us what can be done, pushing the boundries. Someone has to do it first for it to be proven worth it or not. While I still find the story to be a bit, meh. It'd still get me hyped while playing the game.

I know no other game that gives you so much freedom in combat. You can use guns as melee weapons or as tools to hit doors open, can grab enemies and throw them around and against walls, etc. You can use any object or even your head and fists as weapons. The strength of your hits affects damage and has a believable physical impact on enemies. I played Half Life Alyx after this, which felt limited in comparison. Alyx has no melee and you have to use a gun from your inventory at all times, you cannot climb, etc.

I'm not too big a fan of the level design though, mostly a bit monotone to me. Atmosphere is dense and slightly creepy. Would have loved to see a few more "beautiful" or "fantastical" levels.

Also there are only a hand full different enemies which get boring after a

I wish it was technically better optimized, often get uncomfortable frame drops despite low GPU/CPU utilization. Playing on Rift S.\t\t\t\t\t
