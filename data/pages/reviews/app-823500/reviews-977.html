Grab a lad, use him as a human shield, and then chuck him at your attackers as you fumble to reload. Level of interactivity in the environment is second to none - though, actually, since this review is being posted the day before HL:A, it's likely the most interactive up to that point. Just phenomenal.

Note that there are quite a few points where you enter zero gravity, slide, climb, and generally are thrown around. If you are a VR newbie or get motion sick easily, be very careful to take breaks as soon as you feel a little queasy.

Don't let that scare you off from what is a truly amazing VR experience though! Just know that it's absolutely unflinching in its commitment to physicality - for better or for worse.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow. I've been holding off on reviewing this game, but I guess now's the time.

If you have an index, right now, this is a game you should probably play. With that said, it's also the most motion sick I've ever been in VR. So outlined in a hopefully coherent way are some of the things I love about this game, and some of the things that pain me to think about.

So first, the bad (sorry):
This game is all physics. Amazing right? Well no. If you're not careful when solving puzzles, you'll fall off platforms, and possibly get motion sick. Which I guess is my biggest problem with this game. The various forms of cart you have to move around to solve certain puzzles made me so sick that I had to remove my HMD after only a few minutes.

The good:
This game does things no other VR game does right now (HL:Alyx is releasing tomorrow so this could all be in vain). It runs on a physics engine, and is basically HL:VR. I love it. Everything just works, and with the Valve Index (knuckles too) it's just incredible. The puzzles are fun, the plot is OK, and the gameplay is amazing, providing almost infinite possibilities. There is even a sandbox mode (which you unlock by playing the campaign). It's really a full-fledged VR game. It's a lot of fun, and if you're a VR veteran, and don't mind dealing with minute amounts of motion sickness, it's worth picking up for it's modest price. It implements the knuckles (Index Controllers) very well, the best I've seen in a game to date (again, could change with Alyx's release).

TL;DR: Amazing physics-based VR game, sandbox mode, decently long story, crazy experimental mechanics, with a drawback of some motion sickness. Implements knuckles very well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really really want to recommend it because it has really solid gunplay, the way your arms work is pretty darn cool, pretty good sound design and music plus the physics are pretty neat... though they can also be absolutely infuriating...

The game starts off pretty solid but after an hour or two it gets extremely repetetive. It becomes an absolute chore. Having to move a ton of cubes from one side of the room to the other was really boring, the very fidgety "inventory belt" leads to dropping many mags and weapons, and absolutely infuriating climbing. After climbing the castle tower about 6 times; 2 times because of physics cubes throwing me off for being too light, about 2 times by slipping off even though I apparently had a solid grip (both of them when I was almost at the top). Plus not knowing when to let go and not to fall all the way back down. And to top it all off 2 more times after losing a fight to the king and it resetting me back outside of the castle (having to scale it yet again) and one more time after passing it, going to the area beneath it, then dying and having to scale it another time... I just gave up. Not to mention that some of the melee combat can be really cool yet also very bulky, flimsy and just buggy.

I know this review is kind of all over the place but that's just how I felt after playing. It sure has its moments but a lot of it is just boring and repetetive.
