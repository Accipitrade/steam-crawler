\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I say anything about this game, I just wanna say it has ups and downs, just like anything.

First, I'll name the opinionated pros.
+the radio song it's so good and catchy i just wanna carry that radio through the entire game omfg

+slo-mo combat yeaaaaaaaaaaaaaah booooooooooi

+The combat feels manageable and overall fun with the physics.

+Immersive as hell. Almost falling down a pit and only surviving because you grabbed a fence with only one hand, and looking down to see what could've you is TERRIFYING and so immersive.

+This might be a con for some people, but I like how you lose everything except ammo. It makes you adapt, and think what you'll only need. Overspending, you won't have any space for other good items, and you'll waste ammo.

+Exploration. Half-Life: Alyx and Boneworks do this incredibly well. Hiding ammo underneath a staircase, or even a key for an old armory in a garbage can.

+Attention to detail. Oh my god. Where do I start? The pixels when you get too close to a TV, the trail lasers leave when you move them fast, little things like that that just make the game feel so much more immersive.

+melon


Unfortunately, there are some things I must say this game could've done far better at.

-Some puzzles make you feel like you cheated (Lining up boxes to climb to your next area, barely making a jump somehow, etc.)

-The climbing can be a bit janky. You need to crouch to climb, and if you don't have virtual crouch on, it's a pain in this ass sometimes. Even if it can be immersive other times.

-Some of the physics with guns and magazines can just kinda break some immersion, along with how you can't just press one button to eject a mag. You have to open your mini-menu, and press eject.

Overall, 9.6/10 Kromer.\t\t\t\t\t
