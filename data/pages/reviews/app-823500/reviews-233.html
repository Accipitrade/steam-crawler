\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPicked this as a way to introduce myself to VR concepts since I'm a first time VR user. Damn... this game doesn't disappoint. Most fun I've had in a game in basically forever. Killing a humanoid up close with a brick or small hammer is genuinely a frightening experience. After being attacked by one for the first time, and finally managing to subdue it, I was genuinely so freaked out that I had to take a moment to regain myself... I can honestly say that no other game has ever had that effect on me.

Being able to pick up virtually anything in the game is kind of a surreal experience. Shooting the guns is one of my favourite things to do though. To fire the gun you actually need to pull out a clip from your belt and then put it in the gun and pull the slide of the gun and then only can you aim and fire. As a result of this, I did land myself in a situation today where I was shooting the MP5, ran out of bullets while being attacked by 3 or more humanoids ('nullmen') and I panicked and couldn't reload the bloody thing. xD So I decided to just hit them with it instead... it was still shockingly, and thankfully, effective. Another moment that I really enjoyed was sticking the barrel through double doors and shooting two humanoids through the tiny opening between the two doors... damn! That felt epic >,<

The controls overall are a bit jank, but I'm using an Oculus Quest 2 that doesn't have external sensors so... I guess some of it may be as a result of that.

10/10 if you have VR you must play this and if you're wondering whether or not to get VR this game will definitely convince you. *wink*

----------------------------------------

I'm adding this section post having completed the story. Most of what I mentioned holds true and I thoroughly enjoyed my time with the game, however, the game did start to wear on me a little near the end. I personally couldn't fully piece together any sort of coherent plot from what I saw and the ending left me with a lot of questions and few answers. Many of the puzzles seem incredibly complicated at first glance but are usually very simple to solve or can almost be solved 'on the go' without much problem solving. Not a huge deal but definitely a point worth mentioning.

My biggest issue with the game is that most of the enemies just walk after you and are not particularly threatening once the initial 'VR' scare factor wears off. As you progress through the game it tries to simply throw more and more enemies at you and because there isn't a large variety it quickly becomes stale. The enemies that were intimidating at first entirely lose what made them so scary in the first place and that goes for pretty much all of them. They're also generally very dumb. Even the ones with guns are brainless and don't really react to anything you do.
