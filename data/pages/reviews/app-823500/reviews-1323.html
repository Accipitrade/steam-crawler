Actual review:
Boneworks is an experimental VR physics based puzzle/shooter. It has a narrative, but its hidden pretty deeply and you'll probably need to play SL0's other game (Duck Season) in order to follow the plot.

You play as Ford, a Monogon employee who has just entered MythOS, an AI built VR system. Upon entering MythOS, you'll find that you have a fully physical body with legs and arms just like any normal person. You'll also find that everything interacts with everything exactly how it should. You can jump, run, crouch (both virtually and physically) and pretty much everything in between. You can also climb very well, despite what people may say. Learning to crouchjump is imperative. https://giant.gfycat.com/FamiliarRewardingHedgehog.webm

Weapons (Pistols, rifles and melee weapons) are all great. Since everything has weight, you'll have to "act" like you're carrying actually does weigh what it would weigh IRL. It sounds silly, but once you fall into it, it's very immersive. Whether you want to shoot nullbois from a distance or get up close with a sledgehammer, it all feels perfect. Slapping a magazine out of my MP5 and slamming another one in there feels so damn intuitive its not even funny.

So overall, Boneworks leaves me very, VERY hopeful for the future of VR, at least when it comes to titles from Stress Level Zero.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks Review

Just finished the game and Steam is saying I logged in 16.8 hours! It sure didn’t feel that long. Felt like 10-12. I did take my time and explored quite a bit. The game isn’t hard but combat does get pretty intense toward the latter half. Through the entire game I probably died only a handful of times.

Pros:

Good Physics

They’re the star of the show and what sets this game apart

Visuals and Level design

If you liked portal you will like this. Lighting is fantastic and branching paths, hidden rooms, puzzles, environmental story telling and verticality keep things interesting although you kind of feel like you’re stuck in a Las Vegas hotel.

Gameplay

Combat feels great. The highlight for me is breaking things and shooting things

Story

Environmental story telling is well done and reminiscent of portal, although not quite at the same level. There are interesting things happening with melons and the dubious character you meet toward the end is so damn creepy which reminded me a lot of ex machina. I did enjoy the ambiguity and dystopian weirdness of it all, however I can’t say I felt any real attachment to any of the characters.

Sound

The music is hypnotic and cool at times although it gets a bit repetitive and certain sound effects feel pretty generic with different surfaces producing similar sounds. Overall it’s pretty good but please let me break that radio with the cheezy music... it’s literally the only thing in the game you can’t break!


Cons:

Janky Physics

The star of the show is also the game’s worst enemy because when they fail, they fail miserably.

The Climbing

I strongly dislike the climbing in this game. Some people like it. I just can’t stand the involuntary head motion. It makes me really motion sick. That’s coming from someone with pretty good VR legs, having been a VR gamer for the past 3 years. I rarely feel this way and think it’s an unfortunate misstep and frankly a terrible design decision by the devs. The game also discourages taking breaks as there are no checkpoints. To top it all off, pulling yourself up from a ledge is nearly impossible and causes you to fall, so you must repeat the process all over again. That aside, you can avoid the climbing (for most of the game).

Gameplay (Traversal)

Running, jumping, crouching, climbing... everything feels a bit off. Running is unpredictable, jumping onto stuff usually doesn’t end well as things topple over too easily and you can’t reload if crouched too low. There are also too many crouch positions which affect run speed. I wish it were simpler had better precision and better responsiveness.


Conclusion

I really liked the game and was impressed by the physics, although they definitely need some polish. The motion sickness issue when climbing makes it hard to recommend- especially to newcomers... and it gets frustrating really quick when things don’t work the way they should. I’m satisfied with the game but probably wouldn’t do another play through unless they fix the climbing. I might mess around with the sandbox mode, which I haven’t unlocked. Overall it’s a really fun game and a step in the right direction for VR. Can’t wait to see where things go from here.

Score 7.5
