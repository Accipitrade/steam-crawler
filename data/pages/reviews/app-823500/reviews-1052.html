\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's genuinely what I want out of a VR game:
-Realistic guns with reloads
-Full body shown (and not just floating hands)
-Some form of movement outside of teleporting
-A VR game that feels like a full game

Game has it all, and it took me significantly longer to beat it over other VR games. Movements and how you interact with the environment are on point, and you can interact with everything in the game.
My biggest headache with this game is the checkpoint system. Some levels are fine, but the last one is a pain. For this game, if you need to put VR down for a break, best to beat the level first because if not you're back in the beginning.
Story is something you really have to pay attention to, which is something I overlooked because I was too into the gameplay to pay attention to it.

Game is worth it, I certainly recommend it. Especially if there are plans to add DLC\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are times in the campaign that feel a bit harsh on how much progress you lose due to a relatively minor mistake, like losing your grip on very tall ladders. There are respawn points mid-level in case you die, but if you run out of time to play or the game crashes (which fortunately doesn't happen very often), you lose all progress and have to start at the beginning of the level. These levels are not short, either. For what it's worth, they are working on improving that aspect by adding manual checkpoints into the levels for you to save progress on your inventory, but it will still necessitate starting at the beginning of the level with enemies respawned. It's a half measure, but it's better than nothing. I hope they revisit it yet again to add full save support.

So yes, there's a bit of jank to the game. Its movement is fairly hardcore. Even VR veterans might get sick at first. VR newcomers are practically guaranteed to get sick. Some people may never be able to get used to it, but it's a risk they took and it paid off: This sets a new standard for VR interaction. I used to be a naysayer of smooth locomotion, but this game showcases the possibilities it opens. It justifies stepping out of your comfort zone to experience something new and wonderful. The mechanics of the game set a standard that future VR games will be beholden to.

I look forward both to the games that are heavily influenced by this and the future work of Stress Level Zero. In the meantime, this is a mainstay that I'll continue to come back to.\t\t\t\t\t
