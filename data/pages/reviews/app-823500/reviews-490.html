\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite what some may say, Boneworks is an absolute fantastic game if you're willing to invest some time to get used to it. It's a game intended for experienced VR users and for good reason. Most people upon starting to play Boneworks WILL get motion sick, this is mostly due to your body being a physical object in game leading to you moving around when you don't always intend to. None the less, if you can get acclimated to this, the motion sickness goes away and you begin to realize the potential of having a full body in VR. Anything from using your arms as a weight on top of your gun to mitigate recoil to headbutting zombies and shoulder bashing them. There is so much you can do in this game that I've never seen in another VR game. The entire world is interactable as well. Break glass with chairs, smash computers, climb pipes up to the rooftops, throw a box onto an enemy and crush them, put an empty garbage bin on a zombies head to make them stumble and fall, smash a bottle open to use the broken glass as a sharp object, flip tables, pull over and ruin shelves, make towers and forts of boxes, add balloons to anything and watch it fly away (including yourself), work out with some dumbbells etc etc etc. The options in this game are limitless and there's a good amount of replayability. Even within the main campaign, there's never one way to solve something, even puzzles! You can solve puzzles and deal with enemies in the way that YOU want. There is the main story (usually around 10 hours), multiple arena modes including a zombie wave defense type mode, and giant sandboxes that allow you to use any weapon, fight any enemy, or use any prop that you've found throughout the game.

Do you as please, but I urge you to give this game a chance and not write it off as a "tech demo", its much more than that and is a game that you can feel the passion it was made with. A lot of people will say that this game is no good because Half Life: Alyx exists, however, depending on the person you may actually find Boneworks to be the better of the two experiences. I have played HLA aswell and while its a marvelous game in many regards like graphics and story, it misses out on a lot of the things that i think would be much better if it took from Boneworks.

TL:DR buy it\t\t\t\t\t
