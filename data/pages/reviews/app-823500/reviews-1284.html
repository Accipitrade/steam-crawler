\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun fighting/exploring/puzzling "Half-Life"-ish game that encourages thoughtful exploration.
Physics engine is cool and works well most of the time.
Sliding motion only.
Needs an improved save system.

As someone who generally succumbs to VR sickness, I was tickled that I could play this for a while without too much discomfort. Yes, it's sliding only, but I guess I'm finally getting some VR legs because I played for close to 2 hours tonight before needing to quit. There's a great sense of exploration and finding your way around. I'm still on the street level (1st level past tutorial) and I finally made it past the counterbalanced platforms when I succumbed to a giant picker-upper and ended up dropped in the grinder. I was reaching the limits of being able to playing without getting sick and was focused on finding the end of the level so I wouldn't have to repeat too much when next time. Oh, well, didn't quite make it.

I'm really enjoying myself, though. Can't wait to play again.\t\t\t\t\t

Things That Are Fun
Guns are very fun to use, but you barely get to use them until near the end of the "Streets" level

Enemies are fun to fight by using the environment to your advantage

Attention to detail in seemingly the strangest of places which is cool ( but makes me feel like attention should have gone elsewhere maybe?)

Things That Are Not Fun
Been getting motion sickness every 15 minutes and then having to put it down for several hours to event attempt playing it again.

Full body plus arms ruins the platforming as well as puzzles due to the weight of objects, they constantly clip into you or a body part is "left behind" which is very annoying because it happens during every platforming and puzzle segment constantly. Your own body is constantly getting in the way,

Constantly forced to jump to get up a slightly higher object which furthers the motion sickness when in other games you can walk and automatically ramp up to those slightly higher surfaces.\t\t\t\t\t

Multiple ways to solve puzzles and pathways to approach the levels.
