\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was looking forward to this game since it was announced on NODE. It always showed promise, and here we are today where the game has released and I completed it in a few days. (Of course back when it released as this is a rather late review.) The game has absolutely spoiled me in terms of VR. I look forward to melee combat games using the same physics this game uses. The creativity this game allows is absurd, anything you can do in real life, you can do in this game. If a Nullbody lunges at you, you can reach your hands out to grab his arms and push him past you like some Japanese Aikido master. You can strike enemies with your rifle, pistol whip, and go full John Wick on anything in your way. You can grab headcrabs VR Headsets out of the air and stab them with knives. The story felt lacking but it was made up completely with the gameplay and puzzles introduced. Everything is top notch, and I can't find the words to explain it in detail. It is truly an experience worth the price point (If you are already a VR enthusiast.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:
-Immersive
-Cool soundtrack
-Awesome combat
-Lots of secrets and exploration
-Mysterious narrative

The bad
-Crap checkpoint and save system. You have to commit to playing each level in one sitting as it won't save your progress until you finish a level. Also dying and having to re-do certain bits is irritating.
-Unresponsive holstering which caused me to drop my weapons on the floor most of the time. Holsters could do with being a tiny bit more sensitive!
-Some more advanced movement mechanics not explained
-Limbs getting stuck in objects is infuriating. One of the levels had me crawling through vents and trying to pull/push the vent covers off was a pain.

Overall a very good game. The narrative is quite interesting but some may find it underwhelming as there isn't much of it, but it's quite mysterious and gets you thinking. I'll be going in for a second playthrough to find all the secrets I missed!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA physics based game is a nice idea but Boneworks isn't ready for prime time. Everything you do is so messy and janky it makes every puzzle a chore to get past. I'm muddling through the game but it's often disappointing and frustrating.

Most systems are inconsistent and underdeveloped. The player has no weight, making leavering objects impossible and moving objects a nightmare (Theres a point in the game with a boarded up entrance with a picture of a crowbar next to it and a nearby crowbar, if you attempt to leaver the planks off it doesn't work, the player just lifts themselves off the ground. How am I not supposed to be disappointed by that? The devs don't even know how there own game works). Surfaces are either grabable or not with no logical consistency. Climbing makes your view swing backwards and forwards for no good reason. You can't even rebind buttons to the opposite controller making jumping for left handed players very awkward despite claims by SL0 that one of there devs is left handed.

Gun play is ok, but the A.I. is poor and breaks often. Level design feels random and the story is non existant.

It really feels like they've thrown every idea they could at the game without considering whether it works or if it's fun. Ironically, despite the in game mocking of "maybe it's just a tech demo" talk, the game really does feel like an unfinished tech demo, albeit one with a lot of content hastily slapped on.

The physics tech needs a lot of work. Perhaps if they ever make Boneworks 2 they will have time to polish every system into something fun.\t\t\t\t\t
