\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI get what Boneworks was going for and because it's quite unique in that attempt, I think it is worth playing to see what's up. However, uniqueness is not automatically good, and I think Boneworks missed alot of opportunities.

The aim of making everything physics based is great in theory, however, giving the player physical feet, arms and torso that can bump into or get caught on things and then asking that player to carefully platform or solve physics puzzles by hand is an exercise in frustration.

This problem could have been largely fixed by providing the player with a Half Life style gravity gun or physics tool from the start. The game does give you a gravity staff for the final physics puzzle of the game, and it's by far the best one! Before this mission you are also given the option to buy a number of interesting looking physics weapons (with in-game currency), but they are so insanely expensive there's no way to buy them unless you knew they were coming and saved up all game!

The environments and enemies are interesting in that they deliberately evoke a 'VR test environment' aesthetic. This could have been very cool if you slowly progressed to a more 'finished' environment and enemy set. I thought this was happening at one point only for the game to jump back to the 'test' aesthetic and abruptly end.

I could go into more detail about the disappointing weapon variety, hit or miss inventory system and only serviceable story but in conclusion I understand what Boneworks was trying to do, I just think it doesn't quite live up to the potential of it's ideas or build a world/gameplay that is as enveloping and exciting as it could be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSome VR games just wouldn’t be the same if they were on a flat screen. Boneworks is one of those. Unlike anything else out, this physics based title from Stress Level Zero is dazzling, and it is made all the better through VR. Playing as Arthur Ford, you must navigate your way through the virtual city of MythOS, fighting enemies and solving environmental puzzles along the way. The physics are excellent and combat is truly stellar. The battles never got old, as I always found a new way to dispose of my foes.

The environments where these battles take place are equally great. The amount of secrets and interactable objects is incredible, and I was always excited to find a hidden collectible. The only place where the game truly falls flat is the story. Stress Level Zero has done some excellent world building, but unlike games like Alyx, the story is very bare. Unless you read some lore guides, you will likely have a lesser understanding of what’s going on.

Boneworks is an excellent VR game and one I would definitely recommend. Despite a subpar story, the moment to moment gameplay is just so good, it became the main motivator to seeing the game to the end. 9/10\t\t\t\t\t
