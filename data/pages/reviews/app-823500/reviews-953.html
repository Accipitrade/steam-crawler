\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGot this game after getting an index, my first VR headset. Unless you count the 2009 samsung VR device that you put your phone into and maybe sometimes played minecraft on from time to time if you could deal with the screen blurring from heat and sweat after ten minutes.

At first, i couldn't get the hang of the VR movement. Being so new to VR, the way the character moved around made me feel quite sick and would have to break every thirty minutes to an hour or so. I ended up playing a lot of the beginning sitting down and it really did not take away from the experience. Over time i adjusted and now can spend much longer amounts of time in game messing around. I am COMPLETELY SPOILED by the physics system, the locomotion system, the way each item has its own unique look and effects. The game blew my mind and i didnt even really realize how much it did until i tried to play a different VR title that had positive reviews and came out a bit AFTER Boneworks. Man did that game not compare at all. Going for weapons, the inventory system, all i could think was Boneworks.

Enjoying the game to this day, excited for more content, excited for Stress Level Zero to come out with more awesome titles. Loved the voice acting, and the way the game grabs my attention and immersion. The game has a bunch of replayability, aside from the main game there is an arena mode that has challenges and an endless type of situation. Also available is a sandbox 'box' module that allows to spawn different items and NPCs you've unlocked to mess around with.

Playing a bit of devil's advocate, i would say that some levels seem to randomly have poor performance regardless of specs or system requirements. Im sure the team is working on patching and fixing issues as time moves forward. Otherwise, its all good.

Thanks for reading!\t\t\t\t\t
