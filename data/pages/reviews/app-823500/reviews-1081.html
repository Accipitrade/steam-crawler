You should 100% get this game in you own a vr headset. If you don't you are truly missing out. In this review I try to look at the negatives only because I don't see anyone talking about them. This game is very fun and an amazing experience.

I'm recommending this because this is an amazing effort. This game shows off unprecedented gameplay with its amazing physics and full body. This games only downside, the story, I see review after review saying the story is great this couldn't be farther from the truth. The story seems nonexistent and I've played the game twice. I understand what the story is but if i didn't watch the node videos I would be lost. The story is there but there is no story telling which leaves ALOT to be desired. I find this game to be very repetitive. The checkpoints in this game are so spread out its outrageous. Multiple times i found myself getting motion sick or just bored but I cant quit because it will backtrack to the last checkpoint 40 minutes back. Then after the first 3/4s of the game which is basically a torture test on vr users you encounter fully modeled enemies and its gets very fun... and ends abruptly. These are just my honest criticisms for the game and maybe I just missed out on the story but it feels like a tech demo. They acknowledge this but it doesn't feel like anything else.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA ridiculously fun VR game if you like seeing fully fun and realized physics and how you can mess with it. Grab the not-headcrab and stab it with a knife? You can do that. Jumping puzzle? Skip it and just vault over like an actual human being. Going through actual neat ass gun reloading and aiming. And so many other things I can't list I've had a ton of fun with, like doing MGS style CQC with a combat knife and a pistol, which actually worked super well.

The weak? Jesus Christ, the story is pure nonsense. DOOM, the original, has a better story. Not to say what's there doesn't seem neat, but its told through easily missable FMV videos on monitors in the game world. I'm still not done the game and I have literally no idea what is going on in the story. It's so confusing.

In general game stuff, the enemies are fun to fight, the levels are massive and sprawling with tons of exploration to have and not linear corridors if you're into that.

So, the TL;DR: if you're up for seeing some really impressive VR physics tech and a pretty neat game, you'll probably like this. If story is a driving factor for you? Stay away.

EDIT: OH, also don't buy this game if you don't have VR legs because this is super intense ♥♥♥♥.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo the Devs,

I LOVED Duck season, AMAZING story and some of the best handling of a gun in all the VR I have played which is A LOT. I was following/eagerly anticipating the Release of boneworks since the first reveal vid.

There are some GREAT aspects to this game which individually set a few bars higher for VR overall just like Duck season. I am sorry to say I am very disappointed in a few things. I feel too much focus has been placed on physics over fun so when it doesn't behave in a plausible and engaging way or is couched in humour it doesnt leave much enjoyment just frustration.

1. Story, lack of. WHO am I?, WHY am I there?. WHY do I care?. I know there are some small monitors with very brief message from other people. They didn't say a whole lot. Duck season was a simple game with an awesome unfolding scenario with multiple endings. WTF happened? did your writer quit?.

2. I got to what I assume is the last level after 14 hours, I was determined to finish wrestling with Octodad's Palsy stricken cousin until a problem which had persisted throughout the game came to a MAJOR head. I am not going to finish this game for this one singular reason

"WHAT THE FLYING *&%^$*&^ IS WRONG WITH YOUR BOXES!!!!!. Now I would imagine in the complex world of physics simulation 101 that how a box behaves is somewhere in the first class before lunch. I am assuming that you aim to recreate what happens in the real world so allow me to furnish you with a cursory observation in my short 40+ years of dealing with cubes of various weights and sizes that may help.

GIANT CONCRETE BLOCKS TEND NOT TO ROLL LIKE ^%^&%&! BOWLING BALLS.

Seriously EVERY time I went to interact with a Cube of whatever, Stone, wood or sponge it behaved like a greased pig on crack. Standing on, jumping onto, lifting, oh and try stacking them? seriously... I give up,

I might come back to this if the above issues are revisited.

I wish I could be more constructive but i am not going to pretend to fathom the technical challenges involved but with such a glaringly infuriating behaviour from an end users perspective which has denied me the cathartic completion a 14 hour journey I had to vent.

Thanks for pushing the boundaries of VR, but the Ooohs and Aaaahs of total immersion are waning fast as VR matures. I for one need more actual game to outweigh its limitations and shortcomings.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best VR game to date. It's obviously not perfect though.

Pros:
- Amazing physics
- You can interact with almost everything
- Soundtrack is good and really adds to the atmosphere
- Visuals are fine with quite some variety
- Level design is interesting and filled with secrets and details
- Puzzles and levels aren't too confusing


Cons:
- Occasional frame drops
- Can't save mid level (got implemented in January or Feburary)
- The game could use a few more levels (Finished the game in around 13 hours but I took it slow)
- Very few enemy types and many of them are boring
- The most interesting weapons can be bought close to the end of the game and are so expensive that you have to do a second playthrough to afford them
- Climbing is kinda bad
- Can cause motion sickness (especially climbing)
- Physics can get kinda wonky sometimes
- The ending could be better
- It's not really possible to ADS with the M4 using the left eye

Most of the issues are minor and the pros greatly outweigh the cons.

Edit: As expected, Half-Life: Alyx took the spot as the best VR game. Boneworks is still recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the best and most realistic (physics) VR game available right now, and is one of my all time favorite games - VR and non-VR.

The good - Very interesting/fun/realistic physics to mess with, many different weapons, enemies and objects to play with, compelling storyline. A must own for any VR fan.

The bad - For a game that is very adamant about taking breaks and not over-playing if you feel sick, it comes as a surprise that there is no way to pause/save your progress in the levels as they take some time to complete (30-90mins). However, the team has communicated that they are working on a checkpoint system.

You can tell the campaign was very much an afterthought, but it is still very detailed and creative. It allows you to learn about the game and what it has to offer, and forces you to explore and unlock the various items you will have in the sandbox. I really like the way they did this because it makes you want to play. If they just gave you sandbox right out of the gate with everything unlocked, it would get stale very quickly.

In conclusion, I'm excited to see what they add to the game. I think there's a lot of room for more weapons, attachments (and the way you can equip/remove attachments, maybe a system similar to Pavlov), as well as game modes, and maybe eventually a multiplayer sandbox.

Also GRENADES . That is the only item this game is missing and I think with these physics/slow-motion abilities it would be wild amounts of fun to mess with grenades.

THIS GAME IS AWESOME ABSOLUTELY PLAY IT\t\t\t\t\t
