\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHE GAME IS NOT GOOD

hear me out...the story mode is full of bugs, its confusing, jank often making the experience some of the most frustrating gameplay you'll ever experience, its almost unplayable without looking up a youtube video at some parts, the checkpoints are terrible, story is nothing but strange and confusing without watching any type of lore video or doing research yourself. Honestly, some of the puzzles made me take month breaks from this game. Unless you love puzzles that are full of glitches that make you unable to solve the puzzle, making it frustrating as all hell...you're gonna have a hard time.

ALL OF THAT being said, I still recommend you play this game. I recommend for one thing and one thing only: the physics. The physics are the most realistic and the most advanced out of any VR game even after 3 years. Just playing around with the guns or simply moving objects are a joy. That alone makes the 30 dollar price tag worth it. The story can be loads of fun up until any type of puzzle starts. The museum level which is only the tutorial could still beat every other VR game you have ever played. The sandbox mode, while being a pain to unlock, could be worth hundreds of hours of playtime. The physics are truly revolutionary.

The game wouldn't be worth the play if it didn't have the amazing physics it does. This game would fail heavily if it was a flat screen game. That would go to show you how much the VR and the physics really make the game shine.

Could be some of the most fun you ever have, but keep in mind, as it is experimental, the jank along with the confusing puzzles may also be the most frustrated you'll ever be.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game but I've tried to play it like 4 different times and quit after about an hour each time. I think this game is technically impressive but its in an uncanny valley type state. The physics and good for walking around and fighting but when you start trying to do weird or creative stuff they felt very annoying. My main gripe is that manipulating boxes feels very annoying compared to other video games. If I wanted to stack boxes in a game like half life or an immersive sim I could pick it up with E, carry it perfectly in front of me and press E again to place it down. In Boneworks this simple task can be very frustrating as I have to think about where to grab the box, how to hoist it and then worry about it bumping into other things. Another major gripe is the ledge grabbing, in a modern FPS game this is simple, press space near a ledge to be placed on top of it. In Boneworks there is ledge grabbing and climbing but you have to do it with their phyiscs system "realisitically" so it is bery finnicky and inconsistent. Also the weapon selection is done very poorly as you have to swap between trigger presses and grips in order to pull different items off of you vest and reload.

My main take away from this game is that realism and complexity != fun. The fun of a game like this is being able to perform wierd complex platforming and explore a 3d environment in interesting ways but I felt the constant need for realism made a lot of things that are simple in other games inconsistent or annoying in this one.\t\t\t\t\t
