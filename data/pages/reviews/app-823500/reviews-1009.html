\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI do not recommend this game for people that have the HTC Vive. It seems like it was not designed with the Vive in mind (I believe it was probably designed for the Index). They should have a warning that says something like, "Index is strongly recommended" or something. I spent the whole time fighting with the clunky controls for the Vive wands which really pull you out of the VR immersion (which defeats the entire purpose of playing VR). It is very hard to do simple things like jump in this game with the Vive. I felt like the game would have been much easier to control with a standard Xbox controller and monitor then trying to press all these buttons on the wands at the same time and in sequence. If you use the HTC Vive skip this game and stick to games that are designed with the Vive in mind; such as games that the play space is in a room scale with simple intuitive controls that allow you to just enjoy the VR experience and not fight with controls. Game like Job simulator, Beat Saber, or budget cuts. All of which anyone can jump in and enjoy right away and be immersed in the experience without fighting with clunky hard to use controls. Not recommended for casual VR gamers just looking to be immersed in a virtual world for a little bit.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is an okay game, which says a lot for vr standards.
Big pluses:
1. Physics for your body and the environment. This should be the standard for VR games. Blades and Sorcery has it too, which are the only two games I can think of who went the extra mile. In most other games you can just put your hands through the wall. Total immersion break. This game did it right.
2. It's an actual finished game. Yes, that's a big plus. We are still at vr standards here and having actual finished games with actual story is a huge thing as far as I am concerned.
3. The music. It's really good and makes otherwise frustrating challenges more than bearable (I am looking at you, long ladder I fell down 3 times from).

The negatives:
1. Old school. And not the good kind. You have a sewer level. A warehouse level. An underground tram level. There are keys all over the place and you have to backtrack plenty. It's like the dev deliberately tried to use every cliché of old video games. I can see plenty of creativity in even these levels too, but it's just not enough.
2. The physics. While having physics is great, these physics feel...off. My guns jerk around, my hands jerk around, my body jerks around. Look at people reloading guns or climbing structures and you will see what I mean. Blades and Sorcery is not perfect either, but they did it way better. The dev should try to improve his physics in this direction. Also make your character lift more. He feels weak with the way he can't even lift heavy objects above his head or swing a sledgehammer properly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall: 9/10
Gameplay: 9/10
Story: 5/10

This game is incredibly fun and I'd highly recommend everyone play it (although definitely not as their first VR game). The shooting is amazing and if you've read anything about Boneworks you've probably already read dozens of times how it makes you feel like John Wick. It would probably take the average person who is experienced with VR about 5 hours to complete, though I've put in over 16 hours not only because I've gone back to replay levels multiple times but also because this was my first VR game and there's a steep learning curve for someone who has never played before (100% of my friends I've had come over to play this tell me "I don't get motion sick" and then get motion sick 15 minutes into the game). Additionally, everything is physics based which makes for gameplay mechanics only possible in VR.

CONS:
- The story provided within the game is simple and not fleshed out. To get the whole story you have to play other Stress Level Zero games and try and figure out the lore yourself or watch YouTube videos from others who already have. The story that is there has a lot of potential and could have been much better had they put more effort into it.
- Enemy AI. I also found it very annoying how the enemies would randomly trip over stairs or anything on the ground rather than step over it. There was one part where I literally stood on a set of stairs and shot the enemies like fish in a barrel because they couldn't figure out how to walk up them.
- While the physics is one of the major positives of this game, it also has detrimental effects (although much much much less than the positive effects). I found it annoying that sometimes I wouldn't be able to run up stairs, I was getting stuck as if i was running into a wall and had to jump over it and only then could I run up the stairs. Additionally I found it frustrating having my arm get stuck in something and not being able to tell what it was stuck on without investigation. Usually this is because your hands and feet are physics based but the rest of your body is not. For example, your hand wont go through a wall but your arm will. I stuck my hand through a window trying to climb through only to have my arm go through the wall with my hand acting as an anchor on the other side. I couldn't get out and had to restart the whole level.
- Lack of a boss fight. I felt like this game was leading up to an epic showdown but in the end was let down. Technically there was a boss fight but it's a stretch to even call it that.
- Climbing. While having climbing be an option for exploration and finding hidden objects is great, the climbing sections where you were forced to climb to proceed in the story were cumbersome especially if you died and then had to redo it all over again.
- The gravity core level. There is a difference between difficult and frustrating and this level was just frustrating.

Overall I loved this game and recommend everyone play it. I know I've listed a lot of cons, but these are so insignificant compared to the pros that I'm only listing them in case someone at Stress Level Zero actually reads this and makes improvements.\t\t\t\t\t
