\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn most games, every object has a specific set of "verbs" that they can perform. The gun shoots. The trash can can be opened. The crowbar breaks wooden boxes. In Boneworks, you're not limited to these simple verbs. Turn the gun around and its a club you can use to smash boxes or enemies. You can use it to reach out and hit buttons that are just out of reach. Use it to block enemies or push them away before turning it towards them and shooting them. Pick up the lid of the trashcan and its a shield. Throw it at an enemy and its a weapon. Pick up the can itself and use it to cover your entire body ala cardboard boxes in Metal Gear Solid. And that crowbar? Hook it onto things and you can use it to climb things, pull things closer to you, etc.

It is here where Boneworks shines the most. As a sandbox of toys that you can use to break through all of the limitations that most games put you under. Most objects can be used in exactly the sort of way you'd expect something to work in the real world. You can climb on things, move things, throw things, etc. It encourages and allows an absolutely unprecedented level of creativity. It's such a stunning and great example of what VR can and should be.

Unfortunately, the game itself is somewhat lackluster. Though the interactivity is absolutely mindblowing, the game itself, enemy design, and level design are mediocre reimplementations of games like Half-Life and Portal. Were this on "pancake" screens, it'd be a forgettable game whose gameplay is a shadow of far greater games. The save system is absolutely awful in how it prevents players from having multiple user profiles or mid-level saves. The pacing is really off: the tutorial is far too long and the action really doesn't start until several hours into the game. The acting is also really rough among the characters that appear on video screens.

Even with those negatives, it's an absolute blast, and one of the best VR games out there to date. VR is a new medium, and very few other games have been this ambitious with making objects so utterly interactive. Of the few that have, none have built out such an extensive and full game. It's core may be somewhat mediocre, but the interaction you can have with it makes it so much fun to play in and play with. I can't wait to go back in and play some more!\t\t\t\t\t
