\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate- 11/12/2020. I still cannot recommend this enough for people that want a next level experience with VR. I still have yet to play a game that compares to this games level of freedom and immersion. Everytime I go back to this game I smile.

Update- Reached end of story in a bit over 9 hours.
Absolutley fun from start to finish. The physics interactions are amazing and I thought never got old. The arena mode for after you complete the main story is very fun and I look forward to see what content the devs add for that and sandbox as well as additional scenes. I highly recommend this to anyone looking for the next step in VR and VR interactions.

Absolutely the best VR game I have ever played. The level of interaction is insane. The amount of options you are given to solve some puzzles makes you feel clever every one you come to. Then when the game turns up the action it delivers. Running through an area John Wick style shooting from the hip and the vivid orange burst of your bullets hitting feels so good.

I do recommend though you gauge your level of VR legs. I personally have not had much of an issue with motion sickness. Only really making myself feel a bit off when attempting a side puzzle for about half an hour that involved a lot of jumping and navigating uneven surfaces. From the main story of the game I have not had issues yet.

This truly does feel like the largest step for a VR game. I was hyped going in and it did not disappoint. I am currently just out of the first scene that was after the tutorial and that took almost 4 hours for me. On top of that I can already tell that I will want to come back through this game many times. Performance is also very good. I am running it on MSA 4x and Medium shadows with a GTX 1080, 16gb and an i7 7700k. Everything has felt smooth. I am really impressed.

Played on an Oculus Rift CV1 with 3 sensors.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are good physics in this game. There are also very bad physics in this game. You will often find yourself moving things where you don't want them and flailing around quite a bit while throwing up cryptic gang signs. The game is filled with a ton of platforming puzzles and the jumping and climbing are very janky. I have found myself quite frustrated with it.

I get that it was a bit of an experiment and a push to really try some new things in VR and for that I applaud it. It isn't easy to solve these issues and they've done a great job considering all they have taken on, but some of it simply doesn't work that well and unfortunately affects the gameplay.

Right now I'm sort of 50/50 on recommending it or not. I'll definitely play more and update my review if this changes.

EDIT: playing this on a CV1 in seated mode

UPDATED REVIEW:
I played a bit more today and somehow...everything just felt way better. I think I just got completely used to how it all works and I'm having a great time with it now. Strange!

I take back most of the criticisms I had above and can fully recommend the game now. It's a must have for VR owners.\t\t\t\t\t
