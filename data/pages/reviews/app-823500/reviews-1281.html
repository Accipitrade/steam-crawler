TLDR
Physics based games have a tendancy to be a sandbox tech demo without much gameplay. While Boneworks has an actual game, it's not very well polished in that aspect. But what it does provide is cake, icing, and a kitchen sink. It's a lot packed into a package that's rough around the edges but meaty, oh so meaty. This is definitely not for everyone, but for those who wanted a more meat in their physics based game, this is a very good entry.

The Long Bits

Cons
  • Your limbs have their own physics. This means they will get stuck in things, sometimes clip through a ladder or kick a door you're trying to open. Your little legs are like two noodles with minds of their own. Your arms are like noodles that you have some control over but would like feel up that sweet sweet grab point on the other side of what you were aiming for.
  • Climbing/jumping puzzles. This is a physics game and the devs would like you to know feel that in your noodley human flesh arms. Want to climb up the edge of a wall? You can! If your spaghetti legs would only lift a teensy bit higher. Also ladders. Your hands and legs get stuck in them a lot. They can all burn.
  • Checkpoints. Spend a good 5-10mins climbing up the side of a building and need a short break? Too bad, there's no checkpoint there. There -are- checkpoints in the game, they're just not very well spread out.
  • Level design. Let's hide keys in hidden, out of the way places that are needed to progress. Let's reward the player for climbing weird puzzles and getting to out of the way places with... ammo! Or... NOTHING! Some of the physics puzzles are also a bit too tight (looking at you left cannon one of the levels. Why won't you aim higher *kick*)
  • Story. I honestly can't tell what the pooper is going on with the story. Some are told through these little computer screens that can be missed if you're looking around the room or something else is going on. Having a HUD pop up with the screen playing or something akin to Bioshock's tape recorder things would've been great. The levels give off a Portal vibe with hidden messages and strange areas spread throughout the game but the main narrative is not strong enough that I personally cared to dive deeper into the lore.
  • Sound. The gun sounds are anemic. All the pew pews have the wet noodle equivalent of sound and lack a bassy punch. Melee weapons are in the same boat with impacts feeling like they gently bounce off the skull of a target if it doesn't hit it -just- right. Stabby weapons feel a little better.
  • Inability to move the location of the body points. I kept pulling out a fresh mag when I wanted to pull out my left weapon and really wished I could move things around to where I'd like it
  • Weapon variety. It's pretty bare bones outside of a few unique ones..

Pros
  • Your limbs have their own physics. This means you can push things without grabbing them, including yourself. Like in the non-virtual world, you can't grab the floor so climbing up a ledge involves pushing yourself up from the floor, lifting up the legs, and leveraging yourself over the edge. When this is working, it feels like parkour and it's also pretty thrilling falling off the side of a building and catching an edge mid-fall to recover.
  • Gunplay. It's no Pavlov or Zero Caliber but those games focus on guns while this one is on physics. The feeling of reloading the guns are on par with the ones that focus on the guns outside of a way to eject the magazine with a button press. In Boneworks, you can only pull out the empty mag or smack it with a new mag to release the empty mag. Each gun has a pulling point to ready the gun if it was emptied.
  • Equipment slots on the body. They're pretty well placed outside of the ammo pouch being too close to the left armpit holster. It has just enough slots to carry around a good variety of weapons and feels very 'actiony' in the middle of a fight switching weapons on the fly and trying to reload frantically.
  • Gravity gun and baloon gun. These are fantastic and very creative additions that make use of the unique physics in this game.
  • It's an actual game. There are quite a few levels, there's replayability in finding secret areas and potentially speed running levels, and all of this is on top of a sandbox mode for an experience similar to games like Gorn and Blade and Sorcery.

The Final Ramble

Better than most games of this type (physics based games). It actually has a decent amount of levels that make use of the physics engine they've built. Gunplay is fun, though lacking in variety and polish (especially in sound). Melee is a bit iffy with weapons and arms locking with the enemy's in weird places at times and feeling like a paper noodle at others. Took about 10-12ish hours of casually going through the story mode and probably more re-exploring levels and sandbox mode. People with weak VR legs or a fear of VR heights beware, climbing is mandatory in parts and it's finnicky as all heck. Story is... uh... not the best. I'm sure there's lore in there but it's not presented in a way well enough for me to know what the poop happened. I personally enjoyed myself and hope other physics based games will have a go at what Boneworks tried to do with a campaign and actual gameplay in addition to having a sandbox mode. Iz funzies, but not for everyone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is, without a doubt, the greatest VR game to grace the market. The visuals are incredible, the mechanics are fluid, and the story is fun. (No spoilers here don't worry)

I should note, this is a seriously advanced VR game. If you're fresh to using VR, hold off on this game. The movement can make you unbalanced if you haven't adjusted to VR locomotion yet. I have hundreds if not thousands of hours in all types of VR, and this is the only game I've played in a long time that makes me lose my balance occasionally.

The mechanics are one of the strongest aspects of this game. Every puzzle can be solved hundreds of ways, and there's always another route you didn't find. Gun-play is fantastic, its not quite H3VR levels of complex but you still need to ♥♥♥♥ and load your guns like real life. The functions mirror real life depending on if you're loading from uncocked and empty, loading with one in the chamber, or loading after you fired the last shot. You can run dual wield or 2 hand it for extra grip, which feels fantastic either way. Everything feels smooth as butter after you get used to positioning for all of your items.

The enemies aren't the biggest threat but good lord will they scare you sometimes. You round a corner and BAM you have two guys in your face smacking away. It keeps you on your toes, that's for sure.

The story is fun, you need to experience it for yourself. Don't watch any youtube videos on the story if you plan on buying it. Its best experienced with your own eyes.

This is a major moment in VR history, an indie VR game broke top 100 concurrent players. Imagine what Stress Level Zero could've done with 50 million in funding and a team of 150. Half Life Alyx is certainly going to make some waves, but this will be the one that gets everyone's attention.\t\t\t\t\t

Melee, Climbing and Physics aren't quite as good as Blade & Sorcery

But hey, it's got all of those things plus a 8-10 hour campaign.

Unfortunately since the game is an experimental physics game, puzzle segments are sprinkled generously throughout the Campaign. They're simple enough, but it's hard to do them after an awesome combat segment and you're battle boner is raging; the pacing is terrible because of it. I could've probably finished the game in one sitting, but after two hours straight and walking into a room to see a physics puzzle, I have to call it quits.

The combats fun enough, I favour pistols because two handed guns are wonky.

Overall: This game is definitely a step forward for VR games in terms of the games length and feature set, but it doesn't set any standards when it comes to those features. Hopefully future content will be more Combat Focused because that's where this game shines.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t--EDIT:-- Completed the game in 7.5 hours. I took my time, but I didn't stick around for too long in an area if I wasn't finding anything. Rarely felt challenged by the game - most of the enemies slowly walk towards you, so they're easy to kite around and shoot. More limited resources would have been welcome - I didn't feel like I had to improvise too much, which is disappointing given how much crazy stuff happens in the launch trailer. I also wish they made more use of the cool stuff towards the end of the game, I felt like I was just getting started with the gravity gun. The story also barely registered with me.
--
Had a Vive since 2016, and I haven't played a game this much at once since I first got it. No other VR game right now has as much interactivity and has as much story content to go through. There's puzzle games with more interesting minutiae, but those last for 2 to 3 hours. There's games like Asgard's Wrath, but those are a lot less interactive.

There are games that do guns better, there are games that have better enemy AI, there are games which last longer, and there are games with more complex, interesting puzzles - but there are no games that combine them all in a satisfying way.

Of course, it's got problems. Any VR physics sandbox is going to be janky. It's about as polished as I can expect from an indie team like SLZ. But it's a blessing playing a game with (relatively) detailed firearms that isn't made by a developer who has never heard the words "feature creep" in his life.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHoly cow. This game really does live up to the hype. It's not perfect by any means and it definitely has a tech demo element to it, but what it does that only few have successfully done, is give a tech demo a fully-fleshed out storyline and compelling gameplay. This is exactly what the HL-series was all about. This game being a HL-inspired game built to prove specific VR mechanics to the VR development industry really is going to shake everything up. Brandon and his StreeLevelZero team have made a system seller, and it's shocking the amount of polish and how well this concept actually works in real practice. If you buy VR, this is the game to end on once you are advanced enough to play. Even as a VR veteran that started with the DK-series, I had to learn a lot about these new mechanics. I'm still seeing a lot of people that have not figured out some of the mechanics like using irl jumps and virtual jumps for boosts, using the tuck-leg feature in the right joystick to climb onto objects, using the locomotion stick to shift weight when hanging from things to jump to other things, ect. There game really has opened the door to so many more possibilities for VR and I think the entire gaming industry needs to keep a close eye on this game. I feel like this game has set a very high bar for VR games and I can’t see myself feeling as satisfied as I am with Boneworks. Again, congratulations SLZ, you guys have absolutely hit it out of the park!\t\t\t\t\t
