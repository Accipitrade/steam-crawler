\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHalf-Life: Alyx is significantly better than Boneworks. It's not close. At all.

I know that's a click-bait intro, but I had to do it for a two reasons:

1) I've read WAAAAY too many reviews for Boneworks saying the opposite (which feels willfully ignorant to the many issues Boneworks has).

2) For the first couple hours or so of playing Boneworks, I seriously considered giving it a negative review.

Here's the thing - I guess I can understand why some think Boneworks is better than HLA. Boneworks does try to do "more" than HLA. For example, Boneworks has melee combat. HLA does not. Boneworks maps your entire body into the game (arms, legs, body, etc.), which causes those body parts to interact with the environment. HLA does not. Boneworks also has more weapons than HLA.

But here's the thing:

Boneworks does everything...and I mean everything...significantly worse than HLA. And those things that Boneworks does that HLA doesn't - I now know why Valve didn't include those mechanics in HLA. Because they sucked in Boneworks.

Melee combat sounds cool. And the crowbar did feel like it was missing from HLA. But the melee combat doesn't feel good AT ALL in Boneworks. There's zero impact. Some melee weapons don't feel like they work like they should. Some melee weapons feel like I'm swinging a balloon. I ended up completely avoiding melee combat whenever possible because of how unenjoyable and unintuitive it felt.

Well, mapping your entire body into the game sounds like a great idea, right? No more floaty hands? Yea, it sounds great until you realize how insanely annoying it is. At the beginning of the game, I was constantly bending over to pick something up and then banging it against my VR knees. I obviously never have that problem IRL, so why is it such a constant problem in this game? I had to start picking up objects in an awkward way so that my VR knees weren't in the way.

Also, climbing over objects to get onto a platform above you is an absolute nightmare. I constantly had issues where I would climb above an object but actually getting my feet onto the platform was insanely difficult. My VR legs/feet were constantly getting in the way.

This comes back around to the praise this game gets for physics. The physics in this game suck. Really bad. Besides all the issues I mentioned above with melee combat and my VR body, there would be simple issues, such as throwing a box trying to break it. Ammo is kept in boxes that you need to break open and one way to do that is to throw it. Yet every time I did, it felt like I was throwing a balloon no matter how hard I'd throw IRL. I swear, if someone IRL was in front of my hand as I was throwing some of these boxes, I would have knocked them out. But in the game, the box just very gently floats to the ground and doesn't break.

Or there were times where I'd try to jump onto a platform and my body would start bouncing around like a bouncy ball. And then there were times where my VR body would get stuck in an object, causing the screen to stutter. Oh yea, and all of the pockets in this game are poorly implemented. I was constantly 1) accidentally pulling a weapon out that I didn't want to pull out, 2) not pulling the weapon out that I wanted to pull out, or 3) the weapon wouldn't go into the pocket and just fall on the floor. I started using the inventory menu most of the time because it was just way easier. The fact that they put an inventory menu in the game is basically the devs admitting that they knew the pockets weren't well implemented.

And are there more weapons in Boneworks compared to HLA? Yes, definitely. But a lot of them don't feel distinct at all. Each weapon felt distinct in HLA and felt like it served a specific purpose. That's not true for Boneworks.

And while the story in Boneworks was intriguing, it obviously doesn't come anywhere close to HLA.

So after all this negativity, why am I giving Boneworks a thumbs up? Because ultimately, it is a good VR game. While the beginning starts off way too slow, the later levels do pick up and are very intense and fun. While a lot of the weapons feel similar, the shooting is fantastic. The soundtrack is fantastic. The atmosphere of the game is genuinely creepy and interesting. Some of the puzzles were well done (even though the crappy physics engine actively made them worse). The graphics are good. And frankly, there are very few VR games that are like Boneworks and HLA, so I commend all devs trying to make a real AAA experience in VR.

Just don't go in expecting Boneworks to be on the same level as HLA. Don't believe the Boneworks fanboys that proclaim Boneworks is as good or better than HLA. Because it's not even close. HLA is a 10/10 all-time great experience. Boneworks is a thrilling 8/10 with some major jank and other issues that will drive you crazy.\t\t\t\t\t
