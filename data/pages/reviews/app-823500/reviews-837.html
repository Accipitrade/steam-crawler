\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is still worth the money, but it can be very frustrating and immersion breaking. It would be perfect, however, if Stress Level Zero implement these changes/fixes:

• I applaud Boneworks on the way they handle different materials like wood boxes and cardboard, so I don't understand why Omni Projector NPC's can BOTH TRACK AND SHOOT me through 1/2 meter thick concrete walls (I have found that they can practically track and shoot through any wall type except bullet proof glass).

• An indicator outline of the character's feet that is projected directly underneath the character when climbing. This would help make climbing more enjoyable, because in real life you wouldn't have to guess where your feet hitbox is when you try to stand on a ledge.

• Add a lean feature that allows the head camera to lean/stick out to one side when the player's head is tilted. This prevents the entire body from sticking out when leaning out of cover and would allow the player to look around when standing on a thin ledge because any head movement would move the character off of the ledge.

• Please just add a manual save function. What I like about the collectibles in Boneworks is how we have to be very thorough and creative when looking for collectibles/ammo. It takes a lot of work so it would be very nice to be able to properly save that work. Not all collectibles can be saved at save points, nor can all collectibles be put in the inventory. So please add a save option in the menu that saves all item locations, solved puzzles, and dead NPC's.

• Lastly, I've been having consistent glitches with the full-auto guns. I tap the trigger to fire a single round, but it instead fires full auto without stopping until I pull the trigger again or until the magazine is empty. During this full auto barrage, the gun also loses control and swivels out of my hand.

*Note: My opinions come from the perspective of someone who regularly trains Parkour/Freerunning and shooting (Glock 19, AR-15, and Maverick 88 are my regulars).\t\t\t\t\t
