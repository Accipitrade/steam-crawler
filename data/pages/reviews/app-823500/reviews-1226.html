\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS is probably one of the best vr games I have ever played. I have owned a vr headset for almost 2 years at this point and have had this nagging feeling that, this is really cool and I love it but this feels like a waste of money. There are no GAMES just these multiplayer only games, short experiences, or one note, arcadey games like robo recall that you can buy for a stupidly high price tag. This game removed that feeling from the back of my mind. I wholeheartedly enjoyed this game, but that being said its not perfect.

BONEWORKS gets a lot right in terms of gameplay which I will touch on more in a bit, for now I will talk about the issues I have with the game. Weapons of all varieties feel satisfying to use but there is a shocking lack of interesting guns for a majority of the game, they only get interesting at the very end and most players aren’t going to be able to use these weapons because they didn't spend hours scouring the map for every last piece of ammo. The melee weapons while having many options all with their own strengths and weaknesses there are no stand out exotic weapons like there are with the guns. Also I did not run out of ammo once in my entire playthrough they throw a stupid ammount of ammo at you. I had a collective 10k ammo by the end of the game.

The game has a pacing issue. While every set piece be it puzzle or combat is great there is too much dead space between each different encounter and I left the game feeling unsatisfied with the amount of combat encounters. I would have preferred more mini puzzles or more player opened gates (ie: Plank barricades, boxes in a doorway) to make you interact more with the world in this downtime. The sewer level tackled this problem very well for the first half of its runtime.

The enemies are a bit boring if I’m being honest, the only interesting one is the guy that shoots the electric projectiles and even that is trivial to dodge. The headcrab headsets are a nice addition. This leads back into my complaint about more weapons but I would have liked to see more shooting enemies with different guns. Even if they only added a shotgun enemy it would be enough of an addition.

What BONEWORKS does right:

Combat: Despite all the lacking parts of combat it is just too fun to hit people over the head with a sledgehammer or spray down a crowd.


Exploration: You are rewarded for going of the beaten path with more ammo or maybe even a gun.


Puzzles: There is at least one major puzzle on every level and they are all interesting (minus one with a weighted box and a hook toward the beginning.)


Player Movement: Finally a game that understands what locomotion should be, there is a surprising amount of depth in the movement. You can even crouch jump!


Physics Interactions: The way your body interacts with the environment is very believable for the most part but its not perfect in niche situations.


Unique setpieces: every level has its own unique feel to it, and there is even a lot of differentiation in single levels.


Atmosphere: I felt fully immersed in the rich world that they created here, it takes a bit but once you’re in, you’re in.


Music: Buy the ost dammit, it’s really good


Level Design: The environments facilitated the gameplay well in most situations.

On a side note, there is a shockingly high amount of player expression in everything you do, from breaking a box to every single combat encounter. I may just be praising vr for being vr but they really let you play this game however you want.

TL:DR: Great game, lack of cool weapons too much dead space, everything else is fantastic though.\t\t\t\t\t

As of writing this review i have NOT finished the game, future content could both change my opinion for the better or worse.

Damn, BONEWORKS, an amazing experience, i cannot tell you how much im enjoying this game.

I feel like for future game awards, this HAS to be nominated, it's probably the MOST revolutionary VR game of the decade.

While the physics can be clunky and messy sometimes, they mainly hold up amazing for today's standards, being able to interact with the entire world adds a new level of immersion never seen before in VR.

Pros:

- INCREDIBLE Synthwave/Techno Soundtrack

- Amazing physics and world interaction that manage to immerse you 100%

- Original story and character design

- Fully ridged body for even more immersion

- More gamemodes to keep you playing after you finish story mode

- Decent optimization for the more "meh" PC setups

Cons:

- Insane loading times

- Save system is trash

- Tower level lags A LOT

- Sometimes the game tends to freeze

I'd give the game a 8.7/10

Really recommend buying it, but if your a newcomer to VR, i say you get used to the experience before buying BONEWORKS.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game will arbitrarily change you POV as you are trying to perform tasks like lift heavy objects or climb. This will give most people motion sickness. I have a lot of hours in VR and I can't stomach it.

In games like Climbey your POV is always the same distance from your hands as IRL. This is good VR game design. In Boneworks who knows where your head will end up while climbing something. Your brain can't anticipate the motion. This is bad VR game design. Taking control away from the player like that is nauseating.

I don't have any problems with games like Elite Dangerous and Sairento where you can jump, do flips, and move around a lot because the player can ACTUALLY CONTROL WHERE HE IS LOOKING! Other VR games do not take away the players ability to orient themselves the way Boneworks does.

Sickness has gotten a little better with time but I still can't play for too long without feeling sick. It's a shame because I was really excited for this game. To add insult to injury the checkpoints are like an hour apart so it's hard to take breaks and come back to the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI adore this game. Definitely took a bit of getting used to compared to the games I usually play. The design is fantastic, the enemies despite being fairly simple are very cool. The cleanup crew is one of the coolest enemy designs I've ever seen. Crablets work fantastically and when they get you the first time it's a real treat.

Music in the game is fantastic, really compliments the settings and adds and emotional throughline that probably would haven't been preset given the lack of explicit narrative.

Weapons are a joy to use, although I mostly stick to pistols as they're super effective. Was a little put off at first about the slightly simplified weapon controls but it actually works super well and feels great.

The levels are great and do an awesome job is illustrating the world you're in. From the big open feel of the streets to the dark and cramped sewers the world feels very big and it really feels like you've gone quite a ways when you're done. The ending I think compliments this perfectly.

Puzzles are great, the ones you have to do are fairly straight forward and shouldn't take very long to solve. There are also most complex and tricky ones that aren't required to beat the game but are to unlock everything. This has provided a lot of replay for me, not to mention that many of the levels have several different paths through them.

The only people to whom I wouldn't recommend this game are those that don't have have VR legs, there's a lot of motion in all directions and it's not always completely under your control so if you suffer from motion sickness just be aware you may run into trouble. Fortunately for me I was able to run though the whole game in one sitting (~6 hours) without issue. Keep in mind this game can take FAR longer than that if you want to explore everything.

I've said this before but I'm not sure Half-Life is going to be the first game coming to mind anymore when I see a crowbar. This one is really setting the bar that I hope other games can reach.\t\t\t\t\t
