\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy initial impressions of this game were negative, first off, the player character's body physics kept breaking and getting stuck in stuff, second, the puzzles broke often and I had to break myself in order to get them to work, or just glitch my way onto the next area.

After the first 2 hours though, the game starts to get good, really, really good. The combat segments pick up and it becomes more of an explorer type game rather than a puzzle game (for the better).

I'm in the mindset that if you have to "get used to it", that's bad game design. And for the first two hours, there was a lot of bad game design (and bugs). But, once you "get used to it", it becomes so, so much fun. I just wish there were more "breach and clear" style moments, but I haven't tried out sandbox yet, since everything is locked behind a tediously boring grind. The story is great, but sandbox leaves a lot to be desired (why isn't everything unlocked from the start? why doesn't everything just unlock automatically once you finish the story? it's madness!)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is currently the best VR game to date. This game attempts to do a lot of what other games do with various VR controls and combines them all into one package. Its a bit of a jack-of-all-trades/master-of-none situation but Boneworks is 90% there.

Here are the games with comparable features to boneworks:
- kinematic VR body (contractors/zero caliber)
- physics based weapons and interactions (H3VR, Blade and Sworcery)
- Climbing (climbery, blade and sworcery)
- artificial smooth locomotion (Onward, Pavlov)
- Manual Reloading (Onward, H3VR)
- Virtual stock for two handed guns (H3VR)

Now Boneworks does a commendable job doing all of these various interactions but with a little more jank than the comparable games ive listed. Boneworks doesnt do VR guns as good as H3VR. The reloading mechanic isnt at realistic, you cant adjust the fire selector or boot release as intuitively as h3vr, and the guns dont handle or sound as good as in h3vr. Climbing on boneworks is serviceable but not as good as climbey. Lastly, the sound design is not very good, the various interactions in VR dont give off a good 3d sound like in games like H3vr or onward, where the sound changes bawed on the environment, with realistic echoes that give a real sense of presence.

All that being said, although Boneworks isnt better than any one of those other games with the individual features, but when you combine them all together you get a package that is really more than just the sum of its parts. Boneworks feels like the next step for VR gaming and should be the new standard for VR. In that regard, I am somewhat disaapointed that valve went with floating hands for half life alyx considering what boenworks has been able to achieve. It might be a couple years from now before we see anything surpass boneworks, and for that reason I highly recommend everyone with a vr set to play this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks was a game that I was incredibly excited for if I'm honest. And after finishing the game, I have to admit, I'm very disappointed. It's a love/hate relationship, and I'll explain what I mean.

I'll start with what I liked. Overall, I liked the aesthetic and general design that the game had going for it. Hell, even loading up and having MythOS display my computer's graphics card and CPU was a nice touch. The visuals are quite good, especially for a VR game and the music isn't anything to complain about either. The sound design is overall quite impressive. I specifically really liked how the guns sounded in slowmo. You could almost hear the reaction of the bullet going off in order. The initial combustion, the air rushing out of the barrel, etc.

The gunplay is also quite good, although, I find it odd that they didn't map a specific button to drop the magazines. You either grab a mag and bring it close to the gun to have it swap themselves out, or grab the used mag out and put a new one in. They feel good to control, although, I do much prefer the handguns, as the weapon weight can make the long guns wobble around in your hand as you frantically try to reload. Gunplay is definitely where it's at for Boneworks and I'll definitely be coming back to play the Arena mode from time to time.

Sadly though, that's where the good parts end for me.

The story for Boneworks is very vague with very little actually explained. What you're doing, why you're doing it, and what the consequences are for doing said things just aren't ever explained. And that's my biggest gripe with Boneworks. It almost feels like I'm supposed to already know the protagonist and the background lore before playing. And the ending is incredibly disappointing. It wraps up nothing, it explains nothing, and it's a total slap to the face.

Fighting barehanded is a nightmare. Enemies will get right up in your face and just flail their arms, aimlessly slapping you and draining your health as you try to punch, push, whatever you can to get them off. Never fight bare handed, as you'll only be wasting your time and energy. They barely flinch when you punch them, and pushing is pointless as they'll be slapping you the entire time you're trying.

Melee weapons are only a bit better. They hit much harder, but the weight connected to them is too much. I'm all for weapons having some weight in VR games, but when even a simple hammer can't be swung around effectively with one hand without it wobbling around like crazy, there's something wrong. Also, weapons can be held from any angle. So you'll quickly try to grab your axe while in a fight and end up hitting enemies with the side of it. That, combined with the weapon weight makes fighting melee just troublesome and not worth your time.

I really wanted to love this game. It's probably my most hyped game of the year. And it's from a company that I have great respect for. But the reasons I listed above just really soured the experience. That all being said, this game is a huge leap for VR. It takes risks and is the first to be a full fledged experience, rather than a simple arcade game or tech demo. The campaign took me roughly 8 hours to complete and I'd be lying if, overall, I didn't enjoy myself. If you're the type of person who doesn't really care about the story and just want to shoot things and solve (sometimes buggy) physics puzzles, this is for you. Otherwise, well, wait for Alyx I suppose.\t\t\t\t\t
