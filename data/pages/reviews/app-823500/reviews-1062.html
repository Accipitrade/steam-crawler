8/10 some bugs lower it

Living in the future of VR
Boneworks is a glimpse into what the future of VR holds for the world. This game is not meant to be comfortable like a VR game that anyone could play, it's meant to push the boundaries of what VR can and will be. Extremely interactive environments and objects, backed by an impressive physics system, make this game far ahead in any technical field VR currently has.

The Good

Physics
Almost everything in the game can be interacted with. Bricks and bottles on the ground, windows, and a trashcan lid are just a very few examples of things that can be interacted with. Also, a very unique thing about the physics system is the weight of objects. Boneworks tell you that if you lift a heavy object in-game as if it were a heavy object in real life, it becomes easier to move. This applies to situations such as moving a large box for a puzzle or picking up a body of an enemy.
VR Immersion

Level Design / Influence
Boneworks takes heavy influence from Valve games, specifically the Half-Life series. Enemies such as a VR headset crab are designed like head crabs from Half-Life. Level design flows in every direction with secerts to find and puzzles to solve, as did Valve games from the glory days. Without going into spoilers, the game follows a chapter like structure with a linear, yet immersive, story to tell. The Valve influence shows and makes the game even stronger.

Weapons
Guns work almost as they do in real life. Magazines need to be fed to guns in order to keep them firing. Cocking a pistol is needed to reload. Recoil applies to every weapon and can be altered to help you aim. Their are also a plethora of melee type weapons to use. Axes. crowbars, sledgehammers, and many more can be used to bash enemies up.

The Bad

Save points
The length of some levels is quite long which I do like about the game, however, the game only saves your progress when you reach the end of a chapter. It took me nearly 90 minutes to complete the level streets, and a break was needed with that. A save point system will be added soon but until then this hurts the game for now.

Bugs
Your hands and weapons can often get stuck for a moment with some objects. Climbing can be clunky when this comes into play, as when you're reaching for the next ledge or place to grip your hand is far behind you and can become stuck.

Overall - 8/10
Boneworks, with its flaws, has become my favorite VR game ever. It is not a tech demo of any sort, but a full fledged game with many features to play with. I'm very excited to see what the future holds for Stress Level Zero and Boneworks and just how Boneworks will impact the future of VR.\t\t\t\t\t

If you, like me, are prone to motion sickness in these types of games, you won't have much fun after about an hour of playtime. The levels, fortunately, are roughly one hour long each making the game far more accessible.

My biggest gripe with the game is it's save mechanics. Your game saves only after the end of each level. If you quit midway through a section due to being sick, tough luck. You have to start from the beginning. The devs are apparently adding a save checkpoint feature sometime in January, so here's hoping this can become the perfect game in my eyes relatively soon.

(UPDATE) Yes siree, they fixed it right up. As promised, there are now checkpoints in the middle of each level, sometimes more than one. This perfectly fixes the sickness issue that was once the biggest issue. Besides, after quite a few more hours of both this game and Pavlov VR, I've gotten over my motion sickness rather quickly. It's now a perfect game to me.
