\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI get that everyone loves this game. I didn't play the entire thing so take that into consideration.

But, it felt like little more than a tech demo when I was playing it. Very little in the way of actual combat and out of that even less that was even that interesting or challenging. Almost everything was a set-up to show off a concept or a feature. Even down to the format of groups of enemies just after picking up a specific weapon or finding cinderblocks on a catwalk above conveniently placed enemies.

Also, if you don't like boring, forced minigames in VR then this DEFINITELY is not the game for you. I would also say the climbing was bad. I wouldn't say it's the fault of Boneworks, but I'm not sure climbing will ever really be "good" in VR.

At any rate, it's a decent game and worth a look. I just didn't like it that much.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tQuite a VR must-have right now. As of current, it's not perfect and has areas for improvement. However, the physics are undeniably something to give credit to and games like this are what will pave the way for the future of VR. My motion sickness isn't a fan of joystick movement (this isn't the games' fault, I'm still developing my "VR legs"), but movement types like teleportation would not serve this game justice for the crazy level of immersion it's provided for me (armswinger locomotion would be a VERY welcome addition though). Gunplay is serviceable, melee combat is decent, level exploration is fun, story could use some work (still gave me goosebumps a few times though because I really get into this stuff), music is top-notch. Graphics are not anything crazy but aid in the artstyle/design/aesthetic the game is going for, and the performance of the game engine is good enough (recommend 4GHz, 4-core or better and GTX 1070 equivalent or better).\t\t\t\t\t
