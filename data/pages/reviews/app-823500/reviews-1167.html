\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Most interactive and immersive VR experience I've played. If you can play this, PLAY THIS. Twice as good with Index Controllers versus Vive Wands.

Bought this because I absolutely love Stress Level Zero. They're great devs, and I once had a really good chat with Brandon Laatsch at Magic Mountain while waiting for the tram to the parking lot, so even though he wouldn't remember me, I still feel personally connected to their games. Stress Level Zero understands what VR is currently and what it should be in the future. These guys "get it". Every object in BONEWORKS is completely interactable, with proper weight and physics. The gun interactions are pretty much exactly accurate to real life, and to aim you actually have to know how to aim. In one level that is really dark, you get a flashlight right off the start, and you can mount it on your shoulder. I was fairly annoyed at first when it wasn't pointing in the direction I wanted when mounted, but eventually I realized that I was aiming like I was playing a game, and not aiming like I would in real life. Once I sorted that out, it was amazing. PEBCAK, amirite?

I started playing this game with the OG Vive and wands right when it came out, and it happened to be the same time my Index Controllers shipped. While waiting for the Index Controllers to be delivered, I continued to play the game every night after work, and I fully enjoyed myself. When the Index Controllers finally arrived, the first thing I did was start up BONEWORKS, and after 30 seconds the difference was so immense that I climbed up on the notification monitor and deleted my save and started the whole experience over. If you're playing with Vive Wands or any input method other than the Index Controllers, you're still going to have a great time. I did. But the immersion added by the inclusion of the Index Controllers makes this game twice as fun.

Overall, BONEWORKS is a triumph, the best VR game of 2019, and Stress Level Zero's crowning achievement. Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIv tried many VR games with the time iv owned the CV1 rift ( Pre-ordered ) Never once did i ever feel compelled to finish a game in VR because it was always immensely repetitive.

HOWEVER.

Boneworks : By far is the most impressive physics based campaign VR title to date. I have truly never wanted to hurry home and finish a VR game ever. I have thousands of hours in elite dangerous probably about 700-800 of those hours are in VR. This sucked me in just like elite dangerous but not for its true to life scale but its pure complexity and comprehensive puzzles using every day mechanics that we take for granted in our daily lives.

There were moments in the game i had to take a step back, soak in the scenery, just to think long and hard about how to solve the next riddle. Iv completed some levels backwards because it was easier then completing them to what i assumed was the forward way. At one point had to go up these stairs which were next to 2 giant gas containers with some metal bars nearby that lead to a platform. I had to get very creative in getting that slightly extra high so my fingers could grab the ledge and pull my self up onto the edge only to then knock down the pathway so it was easier next time. Then once i managed to get my self up high enough i had to run across this very narrow support structure jump! grab this pipe ... that i missed and looked down as i fell straight down to my utter cringe worthy crash. * Hint use slow motion to make it 100x worse... D:

I enjoyed this game way too much.


I do have some cons though however.

while climbing for me felt ok, i found my self having to constantly fix my arms in game so i could grab the next area.

Some areas of the map if i walked in the right way my screen would go black unless i backed up. If i let it sit too long i had to restart the game. I could still see on my pc screen so i think this is some kind of bug. * maybe me? Dunno.

Why cant i grab the bullets in slow mo?

When using the .13 slow motion the rifles were impossible to use do to wobble. Works perfect with one hand.

The beginning of the game the audio on the rift CV1 sounded muffled and really loud.

Lifting some boxes even though i tried to grab the box like i would in real life left me struggling to complete the final area climbing the *( no spoilers )* so ill say final large door.

Other than that? Why arent you playing this game yet? Dont make it this far and think im going to solve any riddles for you ;) However if you think you can get there you can. I found about 15 Bonework boxes that i didnt understand was and im hoping they were the unlocks. I had to climb some VERY high places to get a couple too ;)z

Enjoy Boneworks because i know you going to buy it :)

P.S Devs, NODE is Dope :) and the only reason i heard about your game before steam.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore you look at it, let me tell you first! Its great! Its brilliant, and fun.
I it runs well, look really half life ish which is a good thing.
My system (Htc Vive, Standing, 8x8 room)

From the start it was quite fun, and I really fell in love with knives/swords and climbing. Which for me was really fun, most times I didnt even follow how the levels path was ment to be, I tried to find other ways, see if I can climb it etc.

Smaller SMGs and Pistols are great, the first bummer for me was rifles.
Now before I say anything, I play Pavlov VR so it could be my handling on rifles was just really adjusted from that game.

When holding rifles with two hands, it just really feels off, personally I found myself getting disoriented most of the times, It felt like my hands were getting janked all the time, like if I am nothing holding them right.
It really frustrated me, cause I really want to get the hold if it, I was double checking my tracking, calibraiting again, checking for blind spots for the base stations etc. My calibration was all fine. It just really feels off for me personally, maybe more suited something like the Index.

Checkpoints, I bet im not the only one who logged off after a good session, came back in and saw I got to do a WHOLE SECTION again.. which is frustrating, like you except something like checkpoints etc not that whole chapters are checkpoints especially in a platform like VR, and especially if you are playing standing, you might get tired, wanting to take a break, or jump back later. Quite frustrating.

VIVE :
The game itself feels more adjusted for something like the Valve Index Controllers (Aka Knuckles) as holding knife (with all fingers), which on vive means holding side-buttons and trigger. If holding only trigger you are like barely holding it and it sways quite a lot (like in irl). Where on index you just need to rest your fingers on it, trying at a friends house, felt much more right!
I found myself putting knives etc in my inventory all the time cause it was tiresome, sometimes hurting to hold around for longer times.

Closing to the end, you enter this quite dark level. And this level dark! I get it though, like for immersion(also there is a flash lights, when holding with trigger and turning around with the pad sways it.. and I personally use that a lot, so im better off holding with all fingers.. which again does not feel good). I would say its a good level, but the darkness really unmotivated me, cause of the disorientation, rather take a break and do the whole section over again when my eyes rested.

Jumping was a little weird for me.. I got kind of hang of it.
Incase someone having same issues, I am 1.83m. I had some issues, I even went back and double checked my height haha.. but if you are 1.80 try putting yourself on 175 - 179 it feels off, but jumping is much better.

This points added togheter really makes it annoying to play this sometimes, it gets your quite uncomfortable and disoriented. Thats the reason im not giving this a thumbs up as of right now..

BUT overall great game! It many times had me immersed and waking up the like "Wow" moments, you know when you got home with you Vive the first time, and was amazed with the stuff and played around like for hours since it was such a fresh thing, it really brought that back to me, which I loved so much!\t\t\t\t\t
