\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"BONEWORKS" is a great physics based VR shooter.

The game gives me a similar feeling like the first Half-Life did during the tram ride.

It's the feeling you get when, you know this changes things for the platform and pushes it in a good direction.

It pushed in such a way, that even through I love Half-Life: Alyx, makes me really disappointed about some of the design choices Valve made, especially when it comes to melee attacks, which "BONEWORKS" has and Half-Life: Alyx doesn't for some reason and makes it hard to play at times, especially when you have low ammo.

Other then that, hats off to the team at "Stress Level Zero" for taking a risk since many warned about making a game where you can walk, not only that you can climb, jump, slide and if my headset was wireless do a backflip while killing enemys. You feel like John Wick, a bad ass killing machine!

The story is fun, a bit short and the updates offer great replay value.

I do wish the enemies would have been harder, since it becomes easy once you get the aim of things.

Also I would love multiplayer, both in the story mode and sandbox mode because that would be hella fun messing around with the physics or having a John Wick buddy.


9/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really, really, really want to like Boneworks. It is clearly a labor of love by the devs. But as-is, it is just too plagued with problems to receive a positive review.

The physics are clunky and buggy. You will frequently clip through objects and get stuck inside level geometry, which causes nausea-inducing camera shudder. The full-body simulation is impressive from a technical standpoint, but suffers massively from having to interpolate your position from the headset and controllers. The result is an awkward, bumpy experience where it feels like you are actively fighting the controls just to navigate the levels. I have repeatedly gotten physically stuck because my in-game position was so far off my real-world position that I effectively lost control of my character.

I hate saying this because it's one of the main selling points of the game, but I really wish I could turn down or even turn OFF the physics and go back to a much more basic movement system. Anything to get rid of the terrible, terrible camera shudder.

The jumpy, clunky, physics-based movement is literally *the* most nausea-inducing thing I've ever experienced. The forums and reddit are singularly unhelpful, only mentioning that if you put up with it "for a few weeks" it will get better. The disclaimer at the start indicates that this is an "Expert" VR experience. I don't think that's the case. It's not expert VR, it's broken VR.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game's a bold exploration of what can be done with VR shooters / physics puzzles, and I commend it for that! As a result, it's incredibly janky, and very openly focused on physics engine experiments over good pacing. I won't comment on the story, because the game doesn't, either.

Coming off of something like Half Life: Alyx, you'll immediately miss the streamlined interactions and solid level design. Here, you're stuck to a ragdoll that you're only mostly in control of. Most of the progression is tied to physics puzzles, which can be a problem when the required objects glitch into walls and just kind of vibrate around in there for a while.

A surprising amount of the time, you'll find yourself hanging off a ledge, trying to wrangle your virtual feet (which you have very little control over) so the game'll accept you're trying to climb up. Boxes containing ammunition and the like are everywhere, but they take several hits with a melee weapon to break, and then you need a free hand to pick them up, meaning you're constantly juggling inventory items around for what should be a pretty straightforward interaction.

There's a lot more where that came from - this game is mostly a big sandboxy tech demo, and if that's all you're looking for & you can deal with the constant physics glitches throwing your camera around, that's not necessarily a bad thing! I just wouldn't necessarily recommend it, if you're looking for some kind of high-production VR blockbuster or the like.\t\t\t\t\t
