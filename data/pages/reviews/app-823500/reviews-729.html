\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a must buy for VR. It's very immersive and genuinely utilizes many of the unique features of VR. It's not just one of those games that could be played on anything that has goggles and virtual hands. If you are looking for a reason to get into VR, or want a break from VR chat, beat saber, or some other popular games, I strongly recommend this one.

The story is kinda weak, and you can see where some corners were cut. The "cinematic" scenes are nothing special. Sound implementation is a bit inconsistent, like when you smack a wood box with a hammer, sometimes you get a metal sound. The voice acting is also kinda odd. A normal jump sounds like you are making your last desperate attempt at a ledge. But what matters, the core gameplay is very good.

I didn't find most of the game very difficult or stressful (haven't quite finished yet, though) but it's a very fun ride. Having to reload and ♥♥♥♥ your weapon, and having no clue how many bullets are left in the magazine may sound annoying, but it adds to the suspense and fear. I do warn those brand new to VR to take it easy, it can get disorienting, and a larger play space is definetly a plus.

Overall, it's a no brainer for $30 (or less). It really does feel like a peak into what the future of VR holds.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS is what you get when developers combine comprehensive physics simulation with absolutely no concern for player comfort. You absolutely need to acquire your "VR sea legs" before jumping in, otherwise the movement in this game will be nauseating.

That being said, the combat is this game is incredibly expressive. The gun fights make you feel like a total badass, and the melee brawls are up close and personal. You'll be ducking behind cover as you frantically reload, grabbing a "null-body" by the head as you stab it in the neck, and catching a headcrab before unloading a magazine into it.

What holds BONEWORKS back is the fact that it holds itself back. The combat is by far the best part of the game, yet the game forces you to meander through levels and solve cliche physics puzzles before it drip feeds you some combat. The epic battles are spaced out way too far.

There's also some questionable quality of life choices throughout. You can only save at designated points throughout a level. You can't pause the game. Ammo is almost always tucked away in a box that is surprisingly tedious to break open. Grabbing a fresh magazine when your avatar is crouched is annoying because the ammo bag on their hips is further back than you expect.

BONEWORKS is also pretty ugly, in my opinion, and the levels are often sparse and uninspiring. The story was completely ignorable.

After reading all that, you may be surprised that I still clicked the "recommended" button. In the end, BONEWORKS is an innovative VR experience that pushes the genre forward. It's a VR game in the fullest sense, rather than a tech demo or minigame that so many other VR "games" really are. Despite its flaws, it's still a fun game that generates moments I won't soon forget. If you have a VR headet, it's definitely worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
