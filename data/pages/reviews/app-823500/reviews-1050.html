\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a bad game. I am sorry to write it so crudely. Do you remember the old platform games? There were magnificent and difficult ones, where you could master your character perfectly after many attempts. And there were bad ones: games in which the levels were simply frustrating, difficult, boring and sad. Boneworks is like the platform clunkies and frustrating. The music is repetitive. The disturbing sound effects are mixed with the controls in such a way that emphasizes states of nausea. The physics of the game, its "flagship", falls on the player like an anvil from the sixth floor. The interaction with the virtual body is realistic as long as you have tried at least once the state that precedes the ethyl coma, when your body does what it wants, always awkwardly, and the clouded mind tries to direct it with difficulty, holding back vomit. Today I tried to play boneworks after a month and, at the end of a two hour session, I turned off the game, the PC and decided to write this review. The platforming parts of the game are simply sadistic. Sometimes huge gummy objects have to be transported to create constructions useful for reaching inaccessible points. But the huge, rubbery objects get stuck everywhere, making the player skid, annoyingly dislocating their virtual joints. Nothing is positioned as you would expect and when you jump on a robbery and colorful object your body collapses everything, making futile tens of minutes of positioning work. Climbing is a realistic experience as long as you have rubber bands instead of arms and instead of hands two jellies that are sometimes sticky and sometimes slimy and slippery. Knives and swords are thrown as if they were dead octopuses: they smashes against enemies in all ways but never cutting. The enemies are few types, disturbing. Do you want to break the many wooden boxes in the game? Sometimes you will succeed with a few strikes, other times you will have to rage against them, risking to cause, if someone is watching you, the worry and discomfort of fixing a dangerous and bloody misfit, because you will have to convulsively beat the inanimate wooden boards, burning and sweating, then, most of the time, you will find nothing inside. The game mechanics are all painful. The platforms suspended with colored ethereal elastic threads do not support the weight of the character and collapse like rotten barges in a stormy sea. Pushing the various machines into the rails often produces the opposite effect to that desired: they will move you. As if that wasn't enough the levels are very long and you have to finish them if you don't want to repeat them. The overall feeling is that the developer are too sadistic.
