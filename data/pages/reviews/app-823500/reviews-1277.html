Pros:
-A really fun and interesting game.
-Physics are easy to understand.
-An overwhelming amount of content.
Cons:
-Levels are really long.
-Easy to get lost.
-Storing stuff in inventory via stashing it near your hips is extremely inconsistent. 8 out of 10 times I find myself dropping the item rather than storing it. Stashing items on your back is a lot more consistent.

Conclusion

Overall it really is a great game that I have enjoyed multiple hours playing. Absolutely one of the most immersive and entertaining VR games I have played. Though it is important to note some of the annoying and frustrating issues of no saving paired with extremely long levels I might not necessarily have time for. I will change my review if this gets fixed. Otherwise, I would give it a 7.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

First, I want to preface that this game is absolutely amazing. It is probably the best VR game out right now. BUT. Because it is so experimental, there are alot of technical issues. The biggest and most glaring issue is that
YOU CANNOT SAVE MID LEVEL. Each level can easily last 2.5 to 3 hours if your really enjoying yourself or have trouble with puzzles. So if you need to take a break because VR is strenuous on the eyes and mind, you better be ready to restart the level you were on from the beginning, because if you quit, or if you crash (and you will crash), you have to start the level completely over.

DO NOT buy this game if you get easily motion sick, cant stay in vr for longer than two hours, have a busy life that makes it so you cant dedicate hours to a gaming session, or hold modern games to a standard and thing every game should have the most basic functionality

Edit: they added support for vive cosmos and WMR, so the black void issue has been resolved. The problem was happening because you opened up the steam menu to set custom bindings and that wigged something out in the game, but now that you dont have to open the menu anymore, the bug doesn't trigger

I still cant recommend this game until the get a save feature implemented which they have announced that they will be adding one some time in january, which is really nice that they are communicating. As soon as that gets implemented, I will change my review\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisembodied. This game feels like the days when 3d games was just coming on the scene and developers had yet to grasp locomotion mechanics. I'm talking pre Duke Nukem/Quake days. Grabbing stuff is janky as hell. It's either super touchy or laggy. I understand it's suppose to simulate physics, but without haptics it just doesn't translate and feels disembodied. You really feel this during puzzles. Getting stuck in the cracks in the floor is not that fun either.

I was really looking forward to this title and I really tried to like it. I thought about refunding it after about an hour. But I stuck with it. I'm at the street level after around 3 hours and it's largely been boring and frustrating. And now I regret that because I can't refund it. There's really not much to do except figure out how to play the game. Level designs are boxy, again a throw back to the beginning of 3d games. There's just not enough action.

I wouldn't recommend this game. It's just not fun.
