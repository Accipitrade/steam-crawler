\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to recommend this game, but from my experience it just isn't worth the buy.

Negatives
- Awful controls for Vive controllers; movement was really weird and grabbing objects while having to constantly hold in buttons that make using other buttons harder to press was very frustrating.
- Very clunky when near walls; I have lost so many weapons just because my hands would get stuck inside of walls or through doors.
- Physics are a hit or miss; some of the uses of physics in the game are really cool, one of my favorite experiences being the gravity gun, but most of the time when doing puzzles it feels like I have to fight the game to move objects where I want them.

The gun-play was fun but it doesn't make up for all the bad experiences I had in the game. The story felt really short and ended so soon, and I was spending a good amount of time on the levels and it was over in 6 hours. I have spent the rest of the time messing around in the arena and sandbox.

One thing that will definitely change my mind about this game is mod support. I see a lot of potential for mods in this game, so I can only hope that mods will be available in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS is, simply put, the most VR game i've played in a long time. Does that mean it's the BEST VR game? No, it's heavily flawed. It's pacing is weird, it's story is nonexistent, enemy design is lacking and most enemies really simple to fight, it's gun variety is weak and it's level design and overall campaign structure leaves a lot to be desired.
It's also a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ blast. Combat feels great, level exploration is awesome, the soundtrack is great, it's constantly introducing new ideas and rewards you heavily for exploration, it's physics work shockingly well and the atmosphere is incredible. It's a technical achievement unlike any other in VR, though the barely-there story and the lack of content in many areas make it kind of shallow. Couple this with a lackluster ending and some questionable design decisions, and you have a flawed masterpiece on your hands.
It wouldn't take much to improve BONEWORKS in many respects. It's setting is just begging to be explored and the nonexistent story could be easily fleshed out more, and the lack of many established FPS weapon types like shotguns or explosives is sorely missed, but what is here are the foundations for something incredible. And it's a pretty good game in it's own right.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy Review of Boneworks, AKA: Melon Worship Simulator VR

The GOOD:

-Blade and Sorcery nailed Melee, H3VR nailed gunplay, Boneworks nails Physics.

-The only VR game to date that makes me actually FEEL like i'm in the environment, smashing(or wearing) boxes has never felt more satisfying. It's truly remarkable.

-You've got a Campaign to go through, 2 extra modes to unlock, dozens of weapons and toys to find, and hundreds of secrets and easter eggs to uncover. This ain't a tech demo guys.

-MELONS

The MEH:

-Now while i love how tangible everything is, the hands you use to grab the things? A little iffy with stuff getting stuck on you or your hands fall through objects and twitch wildly. Funny, but iffy.

-Climbing. Other games do climbing where you're weightless and you swing around as fast as you can move the controllers(BnS for example). Boneworks on the other hand? As with dealing with heavy objects, you kinda have to PRETEND you're climbing and exerting yourself, because your in-game body won't keep up because it has simulated weight to it. For most people, this and the camera bobbing while you climb, is what causes the most nausea out of the whole game, which brings me to...

-Zero comfort options(besides sitting), you're stuck with just smooth locomotion, while for me it was fine, others with weaker VR legs will have a TOUGH time.

THE BAAAAD:

-Yeah, the save system. Figured it out after getting stuck in the Museum RIGHT AT THE END OF THE LEVEL, i had to restart. It made me almost refund the game, many MANY times where it would be FAR into a level and something would come up and i'd either have to quit the game to take a break or a bug would break the level and force me to respawn at the start. But damn it, i kept coming back because the gameplay works SO well it(mostly) made me forgive the lack of checkpoints.

Overall, if the save system is a deal breaker, i get it. They're gonna fix it in January if you wanna wait, but if you can ignore that, you have one of the most satisfying VR experiences we're gonna get until maybe....Half-Life: Alyx.\t\t\t\t\t
