\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished the campaign, and I can honestly say this is the game I've been waiting for on the VR platform. Highly interactive environments, fun gunplay, and a meaty 10-11 hour campaign full of exploration, puzzles and secrets.

A big feature of Boneworks was having your full body presence be given physics of its own, allowing for interesting interactions with the various structures and enemies in the game. However, your arms and legs may get stuck in level geometry and props, usually when trying to climb over a ledge and not getting good leverage. That and the longer melee options tend to get stuck in your torso or shoulder when unholestering. With that said, it doesn't happen enough to take away from the fun of being able to shoulder check an enemy, slap a Matrix zombie in the face, charge straight through a set of doors, or crush a supply crate with your mighty feet.

Combat overall is fairly good. It's totally not gun sim levels of detail, but the interaction is there and you can slap the MP5s so that's a plus. Melee combat is a good alternative due to ammo being a currency as well as it just being fun to swing for the fences with a blunt object or get all stabby with something pointy. The ability to use bullet time/slow mo makes difficult or high pressure shots easy breezy, or just to add a bit of spiciness to your work flow.

I highly recommend the game if you're hungry for a proper full VR game, but with a word of caution. A lot of the mechanics and gameplay come at the cost of comfort. If you're not a fan of smooth movement or get motion sick from VR easily, then this game may not be for you. However, if you're seasoned with VR and may want to a new shooter with potential physics based hijinks, then this Bone will Work for you.\t\t\t\t\t
