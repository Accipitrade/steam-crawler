\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIncredible VR game 10/10. I have a confession to make. I like physics....... and ragdolls...... and i love VR. And being honest, I didn't like the story itself. I liked the environments i was placed in, the puzzles, but the story itself was hard to understand. The physics on the other hand, completely make up for it. Half of the time, I wasn't completely paying attention to the story line but rather solving a puzzle or shooting enemies with a crisp sounding SMG. I love simulated games. The type of game that makes you wonder if you could do this or that, try it, and do it as opposed to a game just telling you what to do or how to do it. Pressing a button to reload a gun vs throwing a magazine in the air and catching it in the mag slot of the gun. Boneworks's story line showcases incredible physics mechanics that make it worth every last penny. And then there's the updates introducing loads of post game content. Update 1.6 for an example: Hover Junkers featuring a new fully physical hover plane based off another game created by Stress Level Zero called Hover Junkers. Post game content is always a blast and I think that even after this game is discontinued, you will be far from bored for a long time playing this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn interesting experience to be sure. My foray into Boneworks is a varied story.
When I first bought the game at release I couldn't get into it. Already a VR veteran at the time, everything just felt off.
After watching a ton of videos about the development I thought I'd give it another shot; Now its somehow become my "go-to" game.
My second attempt didn't start any better but I'd read another review that helped a lot. You are not your character, you are controlling your character. "You," the character, are actually in a VR as well so the movements match that, giving you this feeling that you're controlling a puppet of yourself. Once you breaking that wall, the game becomes a sensory massage for your brain.

*Being a VR veteran seems almost mandatory for this game. If your VR game list only holds a few titles, this may not be the right choice right now. It has a lot in common with Alyx but without the guards that help you stay on track, whether or not you find that good immersion or bad mission design is up to you.\t\t\t\t\t
