\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to start this review off by saying something negative real quick, I've ONLY completed the tutorial level as i havent had much time to invest, Now with that being said I should also add I play on a Vive with the traditional Vive wands. I am NOT a fan of the Vive wands for this game, the grip mechanics are very cumbersome and hard to get used to. Not saying my hands are getting tired or my fingers hurt, but its a lot of effort to grab objects efficiently. Also having to press and hold the touch pad to move isnt great. Needless to say, its not ruining the experience for me, its just something id like to point out for future vive users.

Now, to begin.

This is hands down the most IMMERSIVE VR game I have ever played, purely due to the fact that everything is physics based and reacts realistically to my "body" and hands//arms. Everything feels SO good! The gun play is nice (would be nicer without the vive wands because they collide IRL for some actions) The melee feels really good, like a big swing actually feels like a big swing. I love the jump mechanic although when i jumped in game and IRL at the same time it caused me to do a super jump which almost knocked me off balance, and dont let me forget about the easter eggs and little things scattered throughout everywhere. There was so much attention to detail and effort placed in the development of this game, im VERY excited to play through the story, but now im looking to buy knuckles controllers because i really want the immersion factor that was intended in the development of the game.

TL;DR

Vive wands kinda suck but it is still very much enjoyable, the physics are unmatched and this game just feels so good. I never had any issues with sickness or dizzyness, the fact you can see your body and it almost mirrors your IRL movement perfectly is really helpful with the locomotion. Love the attention to detail and realistic physics interactions and puzzles. All in All this game gets a 10/10 from me (minus the vive wand controls)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to say, I was a little bit iffy on buying this at first, because I wasn't sure how long the story mode was. I'm still not, to be entirely honest, I haven't beaten it yet.
However, after playing 7 hours of this game, I've only reached the 4th level. It takes me on average about an hour and a half to beat each area, haha.
Don't let that confuse you though, it's not because the game is hard, or the stages are super long. I just have too much fun exploring every corner of the environment, and completely wreaking havoc on EVERYTHING in the stage.
If you like destroying room by flipping desks and smashing computer monitors in between shooting strange zombie like digital people, this is definitely a game for you.

The physics are very fun to play with, but do leave a little bit to be desired. My biggest complaint about the game is that everything feels "springy." Yes, everything feels physical, and I appreciate the devs efforts to add weight to objects, but even something as simple as trying to climb by grabbing ahold of things causes the player's body to bounce and swing all over the place. Real physics is a big more rigid that what happens in this game most of the time.
Gunplay could also use a bit of work. I have actually come to detest using large rifles, because the stock will pass through your body and hang up on things behind you, rather than being able to brace the stock against your body. Or maybe the stock is getting snagged under my arm. I'm not certain. But it makes it hard to aim the things, so I tend to stick with pistols most of the time. Easier to handle.

All in all, I definitely recommend this game though. The issues I've found with it are not exactly minor, but they're also not game breaking in most cases. For what the game offers, it is ABSOLUTELY worth the price.
Though I do hope the devs see this and consider tweaking the player physics just a bit, to alleviate the springiness.\t\t\t\t\t

Very good game for 25€
If you like to explore, learn new stuff in VR, solving puzzles with physic, then this game is for you.
However it is hard to learn climbing as the mechanics are a bit complicated but more realistic than in other VR titles. I needed to use google first to manage it. The tutorial in the beginning is long and fun but doesn't show you some important stuff.
I really like how you can sneak on your enemys and eliminate them in different ways. And I don't need to mention how much fun it is to shoot in slowmotion. :D

A big negative point is that you can not save the game and it also has no savepoints. At least this makes you find new and quicker ways to reach the end of a level. Still the levels are large and if you want to continue another day, you have to start from the beginning again.

There are so many VR games which offer you much less for a higher price. I have no regrets supporting a small studio releasing this game for a very fair price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gun combat feels solid but the melee is way too janky (almost nonexistential reaction from the enemies on being hit with a heavy object, no dismemberment), wish it got adressed in the updates. The campaign took me about 10h to finish, which in my opinion was a little too lengthy. I would prefer a more fast paced experience with an estimated time of completion within 6h. The checkpoints are definetely way too far apart. While the game isn't hard I would often die due to the janky physics during the puzzles and would have to run for about 2 mins to get back on track. On the topic of physics- often times objects feel just way too springy making it incredibly difficult to perform accurate platforming. Also throughout my playthrough I have not managed to climb myself up to standing position. I saw some guides telling me to perform a crouching motion while trying to get up but none of that works. I have no idea if it was intentional but if it was then that sucks, since it's the biggest thing that took me out of the experience.

A good game that needs some polish, definetely underpriced for how much content is in there.
6/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun, but with some janky inventory controls that'll trip you up. A nice "tech demo" in some senses but really the first decent Half-Life 2 / Portal knock-off in VR.

Using the Oculus Rift S.

INVENTORY
You have 2 over the shoulder slots for big things, 2 smaller slots on our sides that only really hold pistols, along with an additional lower side slot. You access them either by pressing B and choosing inventory, at which point you can see what you've got and easily select it. Alternatively, you can reach for either shoulder or either side to pull out whatever you've stashed there - but this is where things get janky.

Especially when you're in a fire fight, figuring out exactly where "in space" you're supposed to hold your hands to select a weapon from your inventory can be hit and miss. Going for your pistol and pulling out some random junk instead, or your melee weapon, only to then die as a result can be frustrating as hell.

What SHOULD happen: Is when your hands are in the requisite spot, a 'holographic' image or icon of some nature showing whatever you've got there should appear so you know you can go ahead and pull it out - if that's what you want. Likewise if the slot is empty, and you're trying to put something away, when you've got your hand in the right spot, it should show a 'Right Shoulder Holster" indicator, or a "Left Side Holster" indicator icon etc.. depending on the position.

Selecting weapons was also frustrating because on the Rift S you need to:
1. Find the right magic space without any indicators to guide you
2. Use only the TRIGGER to select a weapon (no other button works).
3. Then while holding the trigger, also hold the weapon with the side button of your controller.
4. Then release the trigger, so you can FIRE the weapon with the trigger...

It's an unnecessary double jank. It really should use the side button to select and pull out the weapon, so that you can then immediately start firing. Maybe this is configurable, I haven't checked. But by default it means you hold down the trigger to pull out a gun, then have to hold down the side button to "hold" the gun, so that you can then release the trigger and then pull the trigger again to fire it. Because if you let go of the trigger before holding the gun, you drop the gun. Again, this just feels unnatural and janky - and is a PIA during a firefight.

At the moment, you will also drop weapons you're trying to holster because you missed the magic space. And you'll pull out the wrong weapon, even ammunition, at really bad times. Having to press B and manually choose to open your inventory just to check that you stashed your weapon properly is annoying, but I found it necessary. The sound effect played when you holster something also plays if you drop it on the floor because you missed the magic space... Meaning there's no real clear clue that you've holstered something vs dropping it on the floor. Many times I've put away a crowbar or a gun, only to try and pull it out later, realise it's not there, and have to back track through the level to find where I dropped it on the floor back when I was trying to holster it.

Also frustrating is B opens up a "scene select" option, which allows you to jump between parts of the game you've unlocked. Really great when you hit the wrong key, open the B menu, press trigger because you're trying to shoot something, end up selecting an option in the scene menu, and then have it start loading a previous part of the game - subsequently losing all of your current progress and having to start the level you were just on all over again from the start.

What should happen: A confirmation. If you're in-game, and you want to change scenes. Pause the frigging game and have an "Are you sure? Yes / No" pop-up.

SAVE / LOAD
The game has no save / load, just a respawn system. When you die, you respawn at some random point (it's the same point, but you won't know where it is until you die) previously in the level - with everything still as it was (EG: You killed 3 Roller Ball Holodudes and dropped your gun fighting inventory jank only to die to a 4th one, and the guys you killed are still dead, with your gun where you dropped it). There's no health bar, but your health quickly regens if you crouch behind a barrier / hide before dieing.

This helps give it that tech demo feel because death doesn't really matter, you're just having a bit of fun. Which is cool and all, but I was looking forward to something with a bit more game in it. Enemies also feel a lot like a re-skinned Half-Life, with the requisite head crabs and crowbars. In fact the whole game feels like Half-Life 2. Which is a pity, because it would've been nice to really see something new.

Still, it's a lot more fun then yet another arena based sword fighting game, and so far there's enough game there to keep it more interesting than the usual simple VR gimmicks. The physics are also relatively well done. There's some jank with moving boxes, especially if you're trying to twist something to place it a bit more specifically but it's a lot better than other VR games. Puzzles so far are mostly simple but even then you can see a few ways to "break the game" and get through - which feels like a deliberate choice on the developers part and works well. EG: Instead of pressing buttons to bring a floor up, just use some planks you can move around to place in such a way that you can walk across the gap instead.

Inventory janky issues aside, I definitely don't regret buying it on day one. For roughly $40 Australian dollary-doos I think it's well priced compared to the competition.\t\t\t\t\t
