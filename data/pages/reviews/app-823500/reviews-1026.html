Pros
+ Unique physics engine (everything is an interactable object, including your own body; everything has weight; guns have recoil)
+ Interesting story
+ Great soundtrack
+ Great attention to detail, lots of secrets and unlockables for the game's unlockable sandbox
+ Guns feel real (well designed, have recoil that can be reduced by using two hands, have to be manually reloaded)
+ All in all very immersive

Cons
- Puzzles are too easy
- Enemies are dumb
- No saves when in a level, only inbetween They added save spots!
- Definitely a nogo if you are prone to VR sickness
-> Physics engine can cause your body and thus your view to have lots of unwanted micromovements
-> Only smooth locomotion is available (although anything else just wouldn't make sense)

Summary: Definitely a must play if you don't get VR sickness, or want to try easing into it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\tBefore using my review to determine whether you should get the game, do keep in mind I used an Oculus Quest with a link cable.
\tSeeing footage of this game before release made me so exited for it, because it looked like it could potentially be a system seller due to the unique physics based gameplay, and interesting story. When I bought this game, I was beyond exited, but when I first played I was beyond disappointed. My first complaints start with the movement. Moving on anything but a completely flat surface is a chore, and don’t even get me started on jumping or navigating complex geometry.
\tMy next complaint lies with the puzzles. Maybe I really am just an idiot, but some (not all, but some) puzzles feel completely broken, and the solutions usually just involve trial and error, or just clumsily smashing objects into each other until they work. The fact that picking up objects, especially heavy ones, feels so unintuitive, doesn’t help either. When picking up a heavy box, for example, it will be either be impossible to move, or take up all of your field of vision, leaving you stumbling around and blind.
\tI could go on and on about my minor issues with Boneworks, but if you have the money to spend, I recommend giving it a try at least. Maybe its leagues better on the Vive or Index, and I was just playing it wrong.\t\t\t\t\t
