\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been a fan of Stress Level Zero since back in the early Corridor digital days. I remember seeing their first stuff about Boneworks far before i even dreamed of eventually bothering to get a VR system (since nothing really ever piqued my interest as i had tried it before and decided having a massive hunk of plastic strapped to my face did nothing for my immersion) and was really impressed by their work and have been looking forward to this game for a while.

After having finally bought an OG HTC Vive and beaten this game, i can safely say i once again wish Steam had an in-between for recommendations as i can't really say you should or shouldn't buy this.

When it comes to the core gameplay the most fun thing is certainly that first time you load up the game and become enamored by the deep interactivity of the physical environment. Using a crowbar to grab onto a pipe and hoist yourself up, using one finger to open the door of a locker, using two knives to stab into soft walls and climb, etc. It's really impressive what they did with the game and it feels like back when people bought HL:2 for the first time but times three.

Also, the music is pretty good. Some of the ambient tracks are ok. The combat stuff is pretty good. A little overly dubstep-y sometimes, but overall good.

Unfortunately, the game simply falls slightly short of expectations in pretty much every other area of design from there.

Combat just doesn't feel very satisfying. For one the health system is atrocious. The way it works is, if you get hit a couple times, your screen starts to fade out, and you have to kill and enemy (to gain an adrenaline surge i guess) to stop it, which returns you to lowish health immediately. Why they didn't just go with a health bar is beyond me.

Firearms feel quite nice to hold and shoot, but my main problem is reloading. There are only really 5 guns in the game: a glock, an m1911, a MAC10, an MP5 (stockless and with stock, no difference though) and a couple variants of an AR-15, colored, long barreled, etc (again, no difference.)

None of them reload like you'd expect if you're at all familiar with firearms. The MAC10 has the charging handle on the side, not the top like in real life, so screw lefthanders. ARs and pistols have bolt releases but to use them you just pull the trigger. The MP5's bolt will lock back but you cannot do the HK slap, you have to physically grab the handle and pull down

Actually switching the magazines is really stupid. You can simply remove them manually, or you can eject them, but to do that you have to go into the context menu (where options and inventory is) and click Eject which is really slow and boring. Fortunately you can also just take the new mag and slap the handle/mag/magwell of any gun and the empty magazine will magically fall out, occasionally magically re-inserting itself further wasting time.

Don't worry though because the enemies themselves are next to harmless.

There are 5 enemies in the game: Nullbodies and zombies (which are the same except zombies have slightly more HP, there are a couple different types of each but all are the same as their standard counterpart), zombie throwers, which toss balls of energy at you that 2-3 shot you, Crablets, which are basically headcrabs except they can actually latch onto your face, (and, since they're made of a VR helmet, obscure your vision by showing a VR Landscape, which is really funny but also effectively disorienting.), Omni-soldiers which are holograms of combine hazmat soldiers directed by a rolling ball that deal a lot of damage but are one/two shotted, and turrets, of which there are maybe 4 in the whole game, which kill you in like 4 shots and are really annoying.

All the enemies are stupidly weak and die in a few smacks/shots. The NBs and zombs act like walkers from saints and sinners, except plain annoying.

I expected them to be similar to S&S, where you can grab their face, shoot them a couple times, then blow their brains out. But the NBs and zombies don't get stunned and don't reel when they take damage or get grabbed, so they're just constantly flailing their arms at you dealing damage. You can't push them away or stagger them or anything. Easily the best way to kill them is to just take an MP5 and mag dump them.

Oh and did i mention that you easily get thousands of bullets within a couple hours of gameplay? It's physically impossible to run out of ammo, which is funny because ammo is the currency in this game. (which makes no sense lore-wise, why were ordinary people carrying around ammo as money before the bad stuff happened?)

However i have heard people complain that to get a lot of the interesting sandbox stuff unlocked, you need to save almost every bullet and spend it at the very end of the game at this vending machine that has everything cost 10-20k bullets.

By the way, you know that little head dude that says "Whattup, son?" you pick up in the trailer? Doesn't exist. Period. How cool would it have been to have a sarcastic, slightly annoying partner for a section of the game IE GlaDOS potato? Nah, that's too interesting and memorable.

And the lore, i have to say, without spoiling anything, the story was severely disappointing. I'm even inclined to say it was downright awful. Nothing is explained, you're just expected to figure it out yourself.

Plenty of games and narratives do this, and it can be extremely effective when done right. But to do it right you need to have notes, books, billboards and signs showing the culture and way of people, have people that talk in this certain way and about certain things, and have environmental signs and signatures of this theme. Think Bioshock, Deus Ex, etc.

But in boneworks there are 3 cutscenes and no NPCs, i think 5 times an unnamed and honestly somewhat poorly acted dude/dudette appears on a screen and says/does some things, and that's it.

There are no characters. And i think literally anyone can tell you that what makes a good story is good characters. Think Undertale, Mario RPGs, Half-Life.

There are none in this game. Zero. There are nameless faces that appear to give you a vague idea of what your character in-universe is maybe supposed to be doing while you dink around for 15 hours and that's it.

The story, once pieced together, is boring and uninspired, unnecessarily meta, very short and apparently ties into some Stress Level Zero cinematic universe because haha everybody loved infinity war right? There's only 1 boss, plus the final boss, which was actual garbage and could barely be called a boss, and the ending was stupid.



Minor spoilers below.

Within the game are a few sub stories, but none are explored or explained.

Apparently the humans were selling melons to the Nullbodies which they "fermented using void energy", and drank, to become melonbellies, which are no different.

The ones who taught them to ferment were the Lava Gang. You see 1 note from them and that's it. They have no reason to exist.

Like i said there's no explanation to who are, what are, or why the Crablets exist, nor how they're made or who is doing it.

They mention the Nullbodies are being controlled once but never mention it again and you never figure it out.



Minor spoilers end.

The only critical bug i found is that there is no way to switch your currently eqipped ammo type. It switches automatically when you equip a gun. So, if you wanna buy a 300 bullet AR and only have a pistol, and have 30 pistol bullets and 500 AR bullets, you can only use the pistol bullet to buy things, and so cannot buy the AR. You have to wait until the game throws an AR at you for free.

Overall, worth 30 bucks? Sure, if you like the physics and want to just goof around in sandbox.

Looking for a cool and interesting narrative use for VR to make a good story?

Not a thing, but buy Alyx instead anyway.\t\t\t\t\t
