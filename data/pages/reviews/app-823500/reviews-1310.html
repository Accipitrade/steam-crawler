The puzzle are hard, to the point were you might end up cheating your way through one just trying to figure it out, which is a good thing. Combat with the rifle and heavier weapons is a bit janky but i imagine they can improve, small arms and pistols are great. It could use aim assist for throwing. The physics in relation to your body are hard to get used to but once you learn how to lift your legs and climb up surfaces it starts to feel more and more natural.
There are small tweaks it could use across the board and while it doesnt go with the feel of the game so far I would love for some big combat scenes/ Heavy enemies or vehicles. Hoping they show up later or with some expansions. The game is a for sure MUST BUY vr title however and a good contender for VR game of the year\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is hands down the most fun I have had in VR. The game is well polished and more functional that any other title I have played.
Pros:
-Very accurate and intuitive controls.
-Most satisfying gun play I have experienced.
-Lots of content. I don't know if I have played a single player VR game with levels with this kind of depth and replayability. Lots of little secrets to find, and hidden rooms to explore.
-They use some sort of detail scaling engine that makes the game run significantly more smoothly on my computer then other titles specifically: even large environments with multiple enemies does not hit my GPU to hard. (Oculus Rift S, Ryzen 7 2700x, GTX 1070, 32GB ram 2400) Runs the game like a champ
-Almost anything you see you can interact with. This is so immersive. being able to grab a pole and swing from it is awesome.

Cons:
-There are only a handful of guns. more nitpicking at this point than an actual con
-I have experience one crash. Otherwise rock steady reliable.
-No ability to save mid level, that I can find. The level's being fairly long so be ready to play through the whole level in one session.

In summary. This game has my highest recommendation. The people over at Stress Level Zero really put out a great game here. That's all I got. I really enjoyed this game and will be replaying it several times!\t\t\t\t\t

I didn't like the fact that I couldn't immediately jump into "arena" and "sandbox" modes, I'm guessing they were locked because I hadn't completed the main story mode. Overall cool game, but definitely not for me, if you like games for their story, don't have really bad motion sickness, and own an index, get the game.\t\t\t\t\t

Definitively the best VR game ever made for the moment

Pros :

  • Shockingly well integrated new innovative and very realistic physics system.
  • Great realistic (not all the way but I'll talk about it in the cons) guns system!
  • AT LAST, a well integrated melee weapons system.
  • Great storyline, and at last a real storyline (Game reviews evaluated over 10 hours of gameplay, incredible for a Vr game!)
  • There's a mix of what made Portal and Half-Life great in this game! A great mix between puzzles (And usage of physics items in them) and combat!
  • Awesome soundtrack! It really gets you immersed into this bizarre, yet awesome storyline!

Cons :

  • Guns don't mimic everything that a real one does (Dropping a mag by the press of a button, slide/bolt release, fire-selector, etc...) But that's not a big deal considering everything else they've done!
  • Maybe a little bit too long for a VR game. It sure is cool to have a long storyline in a VR game, but it also means you have to wear your headset for long periods of time, wich is not always super comfy.

In conclusion :
This game is a definitively must have if you have a VR headset. It's a whole new kind of VR experience!\t\t\t\t\t
