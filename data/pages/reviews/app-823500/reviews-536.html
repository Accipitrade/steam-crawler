\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game during the summer sale for 20% off and I absolutely cannot recommend it, and can't understand what all the hype is about
my complaints, in no particular order:

- the gunplay is shallow at best, far outdone by other titles in the genre
- there is no option to just grab from your inventory with the index controllers, you have to click the trigger, which really breaks immersion
- the location of the inventory is incredibly inconsistent, it borders on impossible to grab the correct things while crouching
- lifting anything heavier than a pistol (including a rifle) will cause your hands to start wobbling, as if your wrists just turned into rubber
- the story feels half baked at best
- for a game with several extended climbing sections, climbing is a terrible experience. not only will you wobble around as if your forearms have become springs, but getting up onto a ledge at the end of a climb requires some of the most uncomfortable movements I've ever made in a VR game
- the VR body is constantly getting in the way of things
- hitting enemies with melee feels like using blocks of styrofoam to attack ghosts
- the physics glitched out several times when trying to complete the campaign, with objects vibrating and sliding across the floor, or shooting off into the distance for no discernible reason
- the gameplay simply doesn't feel fun or engaging\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPhysics are JANKY AS HELL.

Yes, they advertise that you can interact with everything in whatever violent manner you prefer,. Their words.
"Physics based enemies that can be interacted with and dispatched in a multitude of violent ways based on player's actions."

The problem is the physics objects will also react violently as well.

The puzzle where you're supposed to jump from platform to platform, each held up by cables in what is a play room? More often then not I was dropped from the platforms because the game failed to realize I was grabbing the edge of the next platform. Nope, ain't happening.

In one of the follow up puzzles where you're supposed to build a bridge as one way across all of those beams, I was very violently ejected off of the beam when I was trying to gently maneuver one of the panels into place, one end pushed under the previous bridge segment.

And as for how they handle friction.. put it this way, there will be random places that are as slick as ice and have no right to be, even for rough textures.

I had fun with this game before when it was new, but having come back to it after some time, I now realize it STILL needs a lot of polishing. I would recommend to the developers to look at how other games handle movement and handling of physical objects, how smooth and accurate it is, and use that as a gold standard to reach for.

As it stands now, they're are far short of reaching what I now consider playable, unless your only intent is to run and gun the entire thing. That it does decently well. I cant' say the same for the rest of it.

The non physics puzzles are also interesting and worth it.. but again, nothing spectacular.. the handling of the large blocks you should be able to move around did not meet expectations.\t\t\t\t\t
