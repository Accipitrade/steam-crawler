\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSolid 8/10.
In terms of content and overall design, this is easily one of the richest VR games we've had yet. Weapons are fun, the universe (?) is interesting, and the puzzles make you think. It is all quite satisfying. Compared to the early VR titles - Arizona Sunshine, Lab VR, etc - this is a far better showcase of general capability of VR as a technology.
The only negative points are in its execution. True that VR is still a fledgling concept and that many things aren't fully worked out yet across the entire technology, but I feel that this game tries to do just a little too much. The tutorial makes it very clear to you have hard it is trying to become the standard, but unfortunately, many things are so fiddly that it can rapidly become irritating, spoiling the fun of the actual game. Arms getting caught on things, "physics" simulation of boxes that you of course can't actually feel the weight of (leading to erratic behaviour) - the have made a good attempt at finding a balance between complexity and capability, but I think they have overshot it slightly, and the game suffers for it.\t\t\t\t\t

There is tons of content and sandboxes that you can play with to your heart's content. You can interact with anything and everything, almost anything you think of you will be able to do. I'd have to say this is the most immersive VR I have ever played, yes even above Half Life Alyx. You should definitely be accustomed to VR before you play this though, not a good game for new users. But I say this is a must play for anyone with a VR headset.\t\t\t\t\t
