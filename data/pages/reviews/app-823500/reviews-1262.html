Since my initial review, I have continued to play the game despite controls being less than superb. As such, I have changed my review to positive because I now believe the pros outweigh the cons. I learned to have fun in spite of the janky control design for Vive. I think there's a lot that shines about Boneworks, but I still think players should consider the headset they will be playing on. This game was first and foremost designed with the Index in mind, all other control schemes are afterthoughts, with the Vive arguably having the worst. You'll have to be quite dexterous with your fingers to do certain actions together, such as running, using slow-mo, and crouch jumping. The lack of configurable controls is a disappointment. But when you can move past that, Boneworks is a VR game unlike any other. A pure physics playground for you to experiment and explore.


---------

My review is "negative" but my real feeling towards the game is more in the middle.

The game is cool and innovative in a lot of ways but if I could describe it one word it would be: Janky.

Much of what makes this game cool also makes it frustrating to play. It's a physics based puzzle game, but a lot of the time the physics work against the player. Even if you think outside of the box to come up with a solution, you may be at the mercy at the so-so physics engine. One example is that walking on items causes them to move backwards. There are several sections of the game where you may place a box down to help you get up somewhere high, but as soon as you start walking on it, the box starts tumbling backwards. Unless our hero weighs a lot and has been doing barbell squats since he was in the womb, I doubt he could be moving metal boxes like an Olympian log roller. There is also a secret area you can access by platforming across dangling ceiling lights. It's also plagued by this walking forwards problem. You can't really jump at the right moment, you have to swing yourself and awkwardly try to climb up each one.

Which brings me to my next issue with the game: the climbing.
The climbing in this game is tricky to do and seems like a big oversight. There's guides out there that show how to pull yourself up high ledges, but even so it's not a guarantee that it will work. There is very little in the way of scripting for your guy to lift himself up and over things; something Blade & Sorcery does near flawlessly. You have to awkwardly push your arms down, crouch in real life, and *then* use the crouch button in game and sort of finagle your way and hope it works. The game strives for realism, but the problem is that very few people own trackers for legs, so VR games need to rely on scripting the player model's legs to assist with climbing, something Boneworks doesn't do at all. Realism is good, but not when it interferes with fun. A game should first and foremost be fun.

The clunkiness of the game could be almost borderline charming, but it comes off as frustrating more often than not. You are constantly fighting with the game even if you were clever enough to get a correct solution. A certain level of frustration can be forgiven if the rest of the game is entertaining enough, but unfortunately most of Boneworks content consists of frustrating to navigate puzzles. A game like Human Fall Flat is janky on purpose and is part of the fun and appeal. But it's immediately apparent the clunky controls are deliberate and are fine-tuned to be amusing, not completely hindering the player. But Boneworks comes off like it was designed for hardware solutions that just don't exist yet to the wide commercial market.

It's ambitious and paves the way for a lot of fun concepts in VR, and it's very detailed, but I don't feel the hardware we currently have is up to snuff to make Boneworks a smooth and enjoyable ride.

Tag on some somewhat uninteresting enemies, and no ability to save in the middle of missions and you find yourself irritated and perhaps bored at times. Thus forcing yourself to complete a level even if you would like to stop.

I definitely support Brandon and the rest of SL0 and think what they're doing for VR is great. It's a niche that all of us want to be expanded and improved upon. I think Boneworks is a good concept and pushes some boundaries for what we can expect out of a VR game, it just falls somewhat short in the execution of it all.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLike many here, I was unbelievably excited for this game. Unfortunately, I have very few positive things to say about it.

This is the only VR game I've played that makes me nauseous. Your avatar is totally controlled by physics, so certain movements will make your head bob like it's on a spring, which is instantly vom inducing. Climbing is the worst offender.

The actual game is really bare bones. No checkpoints or saves at all, and the barren world is filled with inscrutable puzzles that really aren't any fun to complete. Also bindings on Oculus are broken, you can't move and slow down time at the same time.

I take no pleasure in typing this, I heralded this game as the future of VR to all my friends, but it's just not there yet. If I was the developer, I'd have sold or licensed this game to a bigger studio who could put more effort into the game itself.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo keep it concise:

1) You will need a beefy CPU for this to run smoothly without reprojection in most of the game.

2) The physics get weird sometimes, and this is a game that makes even many VR vets motion sick. Though for me its literally just climbing. I wish the camera didnt jerk around so much when climbing, would be fine otherwise for me since Im used to smooth locomotion.

3) Gunplay is good, melee is decent.

4) Story is mediocre. I wish they had put more into the story and lore, even just in the form of notes you find or more of those video messages you come across.

5) Game is good overall, but mostly Im excited to see what this studio does next. I think this developer has the potential to become one of the VR greats as we move into the era of VR gaming.\t\t\t\t\t
