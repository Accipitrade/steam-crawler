\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game gets my "dissapointment of the year" award.

- Not at all what it's advertised as, the trailer has been fleshed out with lots of acting, to make it look more action packed, in reality its nowhere near as polished or exciting when you actually play.
- No noteable storyline or incentive, you don't get any introduction, nor background story, apart from the random people that pop up on screens from time to time, and even they just end up leaving you as clueless as before.
- Boring gameplay, most of the time you wander through lifeless halls, streets and corridors, without a purpose or even any clue as to why.
- Wonky physics simulation, that you have to wrestle into doing what you want, all the time.
- Immersion breaking "weight" simulation, your hands feel like they are made of rubber, they just keep flopping around and get stuck on just about everything, even behind walls if you turn the corner too tight.
- Lacking graphics, that look like they have just been thrown together from a variety of asset sites, without playing attention to whether they even look good together, most of the graphics look very dated, whilst others look like they are straight out of Minecraft. (I understand that the physics simulation takes up a lot of the processing power and therefore they needed to cut down on the graphics, but then this should be incorporated into the story and at least stick to one style. Oh wait, that's the problem; there is no story...)
- Annoying sound design, the same annoying song repeats over and over in a 10 second loop, which you can't turn off.

Verdict:
It's a hastily and unnecessarily streched out tech demo, for a hefty price
There is potential in the game, and I hope the guys at Stress Level Zero get back down to work and make this the game they advertised.
We know that the guys from Stress Level Zero can do better, Duck Season was the exact opposite of this game, a simple premise, with an unsuspected antagonist and a lot of incentive for replaying the game (multiple story lines and endings, lots of Easter eggs and secrets, etc.), you could really feel that they had fun making the game.

PS: I played this game with the Index and Index Controllers, and tried both sitting and standing, with all the calibration done exactly as instructed.
