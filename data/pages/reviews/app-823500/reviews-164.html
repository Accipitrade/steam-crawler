This isn't a sandbox by itself

if you are only downloading this for similar reasons you would get blade and sorcery, you may want to keep reading.
If you are buying this game for the sole purpose of having a sandbox area where you can summon entities and shoot them or something, you will need a mod. Sandbox mode is NOT unlocked at the beginning of the game and the first level must be beaten in order to unlock it. All guns and weapons in sandbox mode must be manually unlocked in the story mode. That goes for different sandbox maps, different entities that be summoned and misc items. There is an arena aspect of the game for those who enjoy blade and sorcery, but that also needs to be unlocked.

Another thing that NEEDS to be noted:

Do not play this game if you get scared easily, there are several entities which may come across as scary, if you are NEW to VR, you shouldn't play this game just yet. Not because it can make you dizzy from falling, but a lot of the entities jump at you, and it can startle pretty easily. Past hand experience. This isn't meant to be a horror game, but its... an experience for some people.

PS: if you are curious about the scary aspect, its simply small creatures that jump up at your headset and attack you, really unpleasant.

but overall its actually really good and worth the money, I personally got it on sale, but its actually worth $40. really immersive.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tit's alright, kind of overhyped in my opinion

it's fun, no doubt, but the physics and controls they constantly seem to base their advertising around being amazing.. is kind of overrated and lackluster.

the seamless inventory system should be a good idea, with you holstering and unholstering ammos and weapons from your waist and backpack, but it only works 20% of the time in my experience. most of the time you'll just drop your item on the ground 5 times before giving up and using the menu system instead (that they probably implemented for this very reason) - same case for taking items out of your inventory. and that's fine, but it ruins immersion and pacing.

i think i can honestly say throwing things is easier in almost every other vr game i have over this one (and i have like 20, including super cheap, low budget games)

climbing sucks, as it does in every other vr game that has it, not sure how people keep messing it up

destruction collision is inconsistent, you can hit a box with your fists or a weapon 10 times only for the last 2 to register and break it - also a problem for combat

parkour is pretty god awful because of the snapping camera, making lining up jumps super difficult

the lifting physics are just annoying because things you would usually be able to pick up with ease in real life just sort of awkwardly become super top heavy and tip over when you attempt picking it up (stuff like chairs and plastic tables)

i know the appeal of this game isnt the physics - its a VR garry's mod, but they're annoying enough for me to not want to recommend it unless it's on sale

not to mention the fact that they plaster this warning all over the game that "this is a game for vr experts because it includes advanced physics!", and i can't help but feel like that's just a coverup for flaws\t\t\t\t\t
