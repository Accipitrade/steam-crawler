\r\n\t\t\t\t\tConsider this a hesitant recommendation.

The engine is fantastic, the gunplay is alright, and the puzzles are pretty fun.
The only reason this recommendation is hesitant is because its biggest draw, the campaign, is too short. You could easily finish it in a day or two.

The replayability is supposed to come in the form of a few sandbox maps and a couple of game modes, like an arena and a zombie mode. Personally I don't see the entertainment in the sandbox mode, and the combat just isn't as good as in some other games. With that in mind, if you were to buy a game for the arena or zombie game modes, you'd be better off going for Blade & Sorcery, with mods if you really wanted guns.

To summarize, the game is very good, at least as long as you're playing the campaign. The game falls short once it's finished. $30 is a lot for just the campaign, but if you can get enjoyment out of the other game modes, it would be worth it guaranteed. Even without those though, it's a tough call. For me, it's still worth the price, but I don't believe that'll be the case for everyone.

One last thing to note, I've heard that some people who get motion sickness in VR have severe issues with this game. I don't get those issues, so take that into consideration when contemplating this review.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks feels like a real game as much as it does an experiment. It throws mechanics and ideas at you that try something new with VR and the result is an experience that you won’t get anywhere else. It pushes you to break the game and get as creative as possible and the campaign just throws scenarios at you and lets you mess around with them. It is a really good game. BUT,
The physics (especially climbing) vary from buttery smooth to wobbly and feeling drunk.
The game really lacks replay value after you’ve completed the excellent campaign.
The collectibles are sheer frustration to obtain and mostly worthless.
Combat is outdone by other games and even by itself can feel clunky and unsatisfying.
The game is at it’s best when it combines all of it’s elements together but you only really get those moments during the campaign. And the lack of incentive to replay it makes it a game that will blow you away during your first playthrough but exhaust itself afterwards.\t\t\t\t\t
