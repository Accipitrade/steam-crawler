\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm going to keep this first sentence short and sweet, Boneworks is personally the best VR title to date, I could end the review here, but I won't, the team and their game deserve more than that.
Despite a number of individuals experiencing motion sickness due to the "intense" maneuvers the game entails, a fair amount of bugs, and a story that at first glance is kinda random, the game has a lot of character and is a blast to play. As stated previously, motion sickness can be an issue, so I wouldn't recommend introducing someone to VR for the first time in this game, even though it demonstrates VR and its capabilities perfectly in my opinion. I personally don't struggle with motion sickness, like at all. I'm the kind of person that looks up when upside down on roller coasters and wave at the people beneath me, but for others that have tried the game under my supervision, well they kinda struggled after about thirty minutes of gameplay. This used to be more of an issue than it is now as they just released safe points, so don't let this be a game breaker for you unless you really struggle with nausea.
As far as bugs go they're being ironed out, but bugs have made some games what they are, and this game is no exception. The bugs aren't major anyway.
The story is unique, many have compared it to HL, and as someone who has played both major titles, I have to agree. It's immersive, I've found myself getting very put out with enemies who hurt me and my allies, choosing to utilize my blunt weapon instead a firearm to provide a more "Satisfying" experience. I've also found myself shaking the hands of human looking individuals more times than I would like to admit. There is a lot of replay-ability, lots of items to collect and use in the game's sandbox and arena and lots of easter eggs to discover.
The controls are pretty natural in my opinion, performance is decent, I still recommend a nice system and meeting the hardware requirements. Visually speaking its, well once again like HL.
To summarize and to tie my previous statement, we're living in the pioneering age of VR gaming, somewhat how the HL series came on the scene in the pioneering age of home computer gaming. Both games have come around during the period of time that their platforms become available and practical for the average consumer. The graphics aren't photo-realistic, and the game isn't AAA level polished, but there were time consuming, driven, researching, testing, data gathering individuals who sat behind a desk or stood behind a headset and poured time and thought into this game, and I haven't seen that for a long time. I'm not someone who reviews a game entirely on its performance, aesthetics, or controls, I often don't review games at all for that matter. However, I felt this game and its developers was worth more time than just the time I spend playing it. I greatly appreciate the Stress Level Zero team, and their goals for this game.
On a separate note, this is the first game I've seen in a while that disregarded political correctness, at least from what I've seen. The game is pretty obviously American made, I have nothing wrong with games that aren't, but this title wasn't ashamed of that. From the weapon selection to the fact that a mailbox said "U.S. Mail" on it. I want to reiterate, I have no problem with games made in other countries, but similar in nature to how HL was set in New Mexico, tested missiles that had the US flag and the NASA logo on them, and shot at you with an AH-64 Apache gunship, this game and its team didn't flaunt their country, but still displayed it. This is something that just isn't mentioned any in video gaming today. They also included references, jokes, and real weapon names, a small aspect but still worth crediting.
I can't wait to see what this team and their games have in store for the future. With titles like this and a game involving tubed deli meat, metal "U"s, and hand tossed explosives being the pioneers I can't wait to see what the future holds for the VR gaming genre as a whole. The future is about as bright as the inside of a headset crab after waltzing around in the sewers for a while, how exciting. Drink Ultra Mersion
-=9/10=-\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven though Stress Level Zero recommend that Boneworks be played by advanced VR users, I couldn't pass up the chance to experience this for myself after watching the early development videos on the Corridor Digital/Node YouTube Channels.

At launch, I was relatively new to proper PCVR, with only the experience of a couple of hours of Superhot VR among other VR games and experiences to guide me. I'm very glad to say that even though that they have that recommendation on the store page, I still greatly enjoyed my experience with the game.

I played the game in it's entirety seated due to the fact I'm 6'6'' tall and have low ceilings. Many of the game's mechanics involve full upper body movement across all axis, however being seated did not restrict my experience with the game whatsoever.

If you are looking for a singleplayer shooter experience to tide you over until Half-Life Alyx comes out, this is the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t
