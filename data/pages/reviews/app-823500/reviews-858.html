\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTHIS IS RAGDOLL SIMULATOR, AND RAGDOLL IS YOU

I passed the tutorial, a couple of levels in 3 hours and still have no idea when the actual fun starts. What I've seen doesn't worth spent money. Seriously.

Every single gameplay feature here does not stand out from total amount of other VR games, and attempt to cumulate most of VR experiences into single heap became a failure. You want meele combat physics? Go play Blade & Sourcery. Interested in atmospheric, tense adventure? HL Alyx is the first that come into mind. Wanna kill the boredom by shooting? There is already a plenty of VR games from A to AAA where weapons don't fall out of hands and reloading succeeds at first attempt.

Well-known "advanced physics" puts spokes in the wheels everywhere. Movement of your limbs is tied to physics stronger than expected for comfort gameplay. You can't simply climb walls and stairs, drag heavy items without jitter - virtual body is made of jelly. Hands position corresponds with position of controllers only if you hold nothing heavier than a pistol or MP5. Sightly throwing of bricks/balls/boxes is problematic - hands release a grip a fraction of second after you've released a controller button.

All I can say about local puzzles - they're uninteresting, counter-intuitive and over-engineered. They don't give an emotional reward from solving. You drag a heavy trolley for a hundred of meters, clear the path from numerous obstacles for just making a prop for passing a platform with a couple of steps. Another example: you climb a lengthy ladder, then realize in the end that you've been idle while the ladder moved downward with a piece of ceiling. That the heck was that? There's a lot of false doors in levels: they're functional, but there's a dead wall ahead. There could be a normal door near the fake which looks just the same. Amazing.

Audio FX deserve special mention. Game plays better in complete silence. Ambient sounds are made from dime sci-fi samples and are able to drive you mad very quickly by it's repeatability and unnaturalness. Zombie enemies (aka null men) make only 2 sounds - neutral HEY and infernal ARRRGH!!111. A few radio players I've run into played the same song, there was a constant desire to shut it up as quick as possible.

All the things we love VR for are brought to the point of absurdity here, turned upside down and left only frustration and twitch. It's unfinished techno demo, outstretched for several hours with utterly boring, faceless enemies, gaudy level-design concocted in sandbox and a atavistic just-to-be story. And they want money for all of it as if it's a complete game! Don't be misguided by ads. Don't waste your time.\t\t\t\t\t
