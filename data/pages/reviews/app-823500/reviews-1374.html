\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRewriting my review having now finished the game*

It's the future of VR in the sense that the world is amazingly interactive. I was consistently finding ways of progressing through levels which felt entirely unique to my play-through. For example I found a broken piece of wood to make a ramp to get a heavy object onto this ledge which the game wanted you to get there by crossing these awful swinging bridges.


However, the movement and climbing is very janky and the way you hold a gun is inexcusable.
If you're looking for a sandbox VR fighting game I'd recommend Blade and Sorcery or if you're looking for a shooter I'd say Pavlov. But if you're looking for an impressive experience which captures somewhat the things which make those previous genres attractive while also including some physics based puzzles Boneworks is great.
Storywise, the ending was quite abrupt and unsatisfying but the fun and spectacle really ramped up towards the end.
I'm recommending this game despite it's shortcomings because it's not something you'll find in any other VR game. Give it a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
