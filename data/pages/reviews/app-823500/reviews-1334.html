\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed on a Rift S, Radeon RX 480(4GB), i5-7400 CPU @ 3.00 GHz, 8 GB Ram

Valve should already be buying this company and the physics engine. This game is the VR version of Half-Life 2, physics-wise. Stress Level Zero obviously have a lot of love for HL2 and it shows, with robotic headcrabs and virtual zombies. The atmosphere, soundtrack, and puzzles are all top notch. The true star here is the physics engine, which is the best I've seen in any VR game thus far. Not to beat a dead horse, but I get the exact same feeling I got in 2004 after seeing what Valve could do with the Source engine. This is truly the next leap in VR gaming, but it isn't without drawbacks.

Obviously, Stress Level Zero is not Valve, even 2004 Valve. It's a much smaller team, with (if I had to assume) 1/1000th the budget they had. This Day-One version is well put together, but there are significant load times for weaker machines (as in 4+ minutes load times). I'm guessing some of this is an optimization issue coupled with my now lower than ultra PC specs. Once in the game everything is smooth, gunplay feels great and everything from boxes to the player have weight and momentum.

This team obviously has a lot of passion for this game, as well as a deep appreciation for what Half-Life 2 did for the gaming community at large. Building a game from the ground up is a daunting task, but these people have done a spectacular job. In a sea of Early Access fails, it's nice to see a company deliver a product that, for me, works great, with few hiccups and mostly me feeling dumb for over-complicating a simple physics-based puzzle. Great job Stress Level Zero, I'm looking forward to the bugfixes and patches to tidy up your already outstanding game. Do you have a decent VR rig? Buy this game!!!

TL:DR Half-Life 2's groundbreaking engine design + Portal's dark sense of humor + Great soundtrack, level design, atmosphere = Something that sets the bar for Half-Life: Alyx. VALVE: BUY THIS COMPANY, TREAT THEM RIGHT! HELP EVERYONE BRING VR INTO THE TRUE MAINSTREAM!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t-- I hyped this game a lot, but I have to be honest: I can't recommend this game until a few things are fixed, because this game was supposed to setup VR to be a legitimate platform

1) The hands NEED to clip through the world when not in view. Too many times do they get stuck on everything, like doors. Walking through doors can be a pain if you open them towards you. They get stuck climbing. They get stuck grabbing. They grab random things... and so on
2) Climbing is... janky. Again, hands get caught on everything and the legs just glitch everywhere
3) The inertia-mass physics for melee weapons is not the way to go. Mass should be conveyed by the effect it has on the world, NOT by slowing down my hands to the point the virtual representation is no where near where I have them in real life. Why are we going through SO much effort to get such precise tracking just to have developers ignore it? It feels like I have to swing my arms as fast/hard as possible to get any effect, and the enemies barely move at all when hit... but my hands are slowed down for some reason
4) STOP moving the camera/view outside of the player's movement. It's annoying, and, take a look at how much time I've spent in VR. This is the ONLY games that's every made me a bit sick. STOP moving the camera everytime I touch anything
