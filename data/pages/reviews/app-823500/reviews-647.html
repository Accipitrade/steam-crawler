even though the guns feel kind of wacky they are still fun, some enemies are really dumb and this makes for great game play from time to time, ammo is not the hardest to come by so when you get a gun you are set for life and the physics are great.

The bad:
controls on the quest 2 are a bit strange to say the least, Weapons don't want to go into there storage slots on the players body, guns like to aim in random directions if you don't old them just right, the story (or what i played so far) is boring, and not very well told, it might take 2-3 tries to get up onto a ledge and you can get through most puzzles just by climbing over everything.

Overall
This games is fun for a while, you start to notice a lot of issues if you play for a while, it doesn't feel like a $34 (Canadian) game, it feels clunky and the story feels disappointing, if its on sale then pick it up, if not wait until it is.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing the game for a decent while I can say with certainty it is a good game, the combat and puzzles are good (although when first approaching a puzzle it can look quite cluttered and confusing) and the physics adds a lot (although it can be quite finicky at times). I'll also reinforce a warning the game gives you at the beginning, this game is not meant for people who get motion sick easy and/or are new to VR. It contains climbing, unprotected ledges and large drops (some of which are required for progression), along with CQB in dimly lit spaces. You should also know that the combat will almost certainly be melee based at some point.

If you're alright with the above things i would highly recommend this game. It is a unique and fun experience that is definitely worth trying. If not i recommend you get some practice with less intense/demanding games and revisit the game later.\t\t\t\t\t
