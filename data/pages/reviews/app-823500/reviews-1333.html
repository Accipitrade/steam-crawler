\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've gotten to play much more of the game, here are my thoughts so far:
The game is extremely hard to play on anything that isn't an Index. In the game, you have to hold items by constantly holding down the grip. While on Index that works fine because of the finger tracking, on basically any other VR headset (mine is specifically WMR), it's painful having to squeeze down the grip button constantly. This results in items constantly being dropped at bad times.
Another issue with this may be with ladders. I don't know if anyone else has experienced this with ladders, but when you get to the top, it is impossible to pull yourself up. your arms are much shorter than the rest of your body so you can't get up all the way. They added 2 vertical rods you can grab onto and climb up, but thats impossible too cause in order to climb, you need to let go with one hand and move it up higher. But in this game, if you let go with one hand, the other hand will slide all the way down to the bottom as if it has no grip. There were multiple parts of the game I got stuck cause I couldn't climb the ladders. Physics are quite janky and have a lot of room for improvement. Can't count the number of times my arms phased through a fence or door or cabinet and took awhile to get out. Last thing I'd point out is the inventory system is hard to use. The rear pocket is impossible to put stuff in, 100% if seated. Grabbing ammo is also impossible seated, and you have to have your hand in a very small area to put away ammo, while you can have it in a huge area to grab some. The side gun holsters are very hard to grab from, you have to throw your elbows as far back as you can. The shoulder pockets work great, with little to no issues.

I'm giving it a positive review cause it's still fun in the mean time, and the devs have plenty of time to fix things and add new things. If they were to remove the sliding down ladders, add an option to just press the grip rather than hold it down, improve physics over time, and improve the inventory, the game would be 10x better. It would also be helpful if they added the ability to save, as each chapter has been about 1 hour each for me, and you basically have to play it to the end in order to keep your progress.

This was one of the worst games I feel like I'll ever play. I've had frustrating VR experiences, but this is the first time I've ever died, reset so far back (and this is after they implemented these AWFUL immersion breaking save points) in a game that I just had to stop and take a break because of how aggravating it is. There are absolutely some really cool things in it, but there is just so much janky sticky rubberyness that it ALWAYS feels like I'm fighting the game more than playing it.

And don't get me started on the story.

If they put some more resources into it and rethink how things feel in the game, I think a sequel could be great, but this game first try is just plain bad.

*End Edit*

Its ok, but not great. For every little thing it do in an interesting way, there are two other annoying things that get in the way of enjoying said thing.

I will play all the way through it, but go into it with lower expectations if you can. For 30 bucks, its worth it, if it was 60 id of refunded it.

Heres some random things that bugged me:
-NO MID LEVEL SAVES, I took a break mid way through a level and when I relaunched the game I had to start the level all over again. Not that it took me too long, but there is an incredible amount of TEDIUM involved with some of the puzzles and I really would prefer not to repeat them.
-You need to press the TRIGGER to pull out your gun, causing many accidental fires. Who thought that was a good design decision?!
-Your feet pitter-patter rapidly and loud enough to feel extremely cartoonish, constantly breaking immersion.
-Trying to grab something on a shelf, missing and accidentally grabbing the WHOLE SHELF, then knocking it over with everything on it. Frequently.
-Everything you do feels noodly and rubbery. Like the physics are there to do certain things, but they are not complex enough paired with your own movements to behave the way they would in reality. This feeling of being so close to real end up making you feel like you are often struggling to do easy tasks.


Things that I DO LIKE:
-The style and the world are interesting. Getting a bit of a Valve meets Remedy flavor, but you know, via youtube.
-There have been some small moments that caught me off guard and really gave me a moment of feeling pulled into the game.
-I was so mad at the game restarting a level on me that I had a genuinely good time speeding back through it with a brick and hammer absolutely clobbering the enemies I came across.\t\t\t\t\t

Everything in the world has weight, the boxes you can move, the melee weapons you swing, the enemies, and everything else. This is the future of how VR games will handle physics. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tCool conceptually but I can't really recommend it in its' current state.

Stress Level Zero has been hyping this game to hell and back on their YouTube channel, constantly showing off the melee combat, gunplay, etc. and what happens the moment you boot up the game? A menu, a brief cinematic, and then a literal HOUR of tutorials. I have no problem with tutorials but as someone who's been playing VR games for about a year and a half I REALLY wish we were given the option to skip the tutorial completely and just jump into the action. I've seen people breeze through it in 10-30 minutes but I spent about 50 minutes in it because I figured it was all important and I wanted to see any clever stuff they added in there, but it was pretty generic and I wish I just rushed through it.

I got to a part where you have to use balloons to lift up your platform so you can make a jump over a gap. I put too many balloons on the platform and it took me up too quickly before I could jump off, which jettisoned me into a spinning blade, killing me instantly. "Oh ok, I guess I'm dead. The checkpoint will probably be nearby."

NOPE. It took me all the way back to the start of the section. I thought "Wow, that's a bit punishing to put a one-hit-kill death mechanic in a tutorial section, but whatever, I'll walk back." I walk back, this time the balloon gun is nowhere to be seen, glitched into the aether. I restart the level to fix the bug and it takes me back to the VERY beginning of the tutorial. I have to redo the initial jumping puzzle (which I hear get more plentiful and tedious throughout the game), go through the obnoxious tutorial and try again, this time getting it. Once I was out of the tutorial area, I jump into a trash duct and head through an abandoned town, whereupon I'm shot at by a turret while defenseless. I try to run by him and I die, and the game starts me all the way at the beginning again. I find out later I'm supposed to use a trashcan lid to block his bullets but at this point I'm too fatigued to keep going.

On top of this, the physics-based gameplay feels kind of inconsistent. Some things you can force grab and it'll teleport to your hand. No problem. Other items like boxes, coffee cups or clipboards require you to physically lean down and pick them up, and with the way my lighthouses are situated, that means I lose calibration pretty easily.

For 32 bucks, I'm simply not impressed enough to keep playing after spending a little over an hour trying it out. The gameplay is nothing special after playing Blade & Sorcery. The Stress Level Zero guys want us to know that they're all about making a difficult game in VR, but you can't front-load all of the tedious stuff right off the bat. Ration it through the opening stage like a sensible designer might. I get that they were trying to contextualize the gameplay in the story setting but I really could have gone without the lengthy museum introduction. I wouldn't even be surprised if they made a lengthy tutorial solely to pad out your playtime so that if you decided the game wasn't right for you you couldn't refund it because you spent such a long time messing around in the museum. I wouldn't put it past this company which has made Hover Junkers back in 2016, a multiplayer game with zero playerbase, no options to play with bots, still charging 20 bucks for it. Or Duck Season, a 3D version of Duck Hunt with a "spooky" twist and tedious gameplay that they also charge 20 bucks for.

Don't show off a bunch of cool guns and freeplay mechanics to hype up your game and then lock freeplay behind a campaign, that's just annoying. Refunded it for now, I'll probably pick it up again when it's on sale and maybe they've had the chance to refine some of the gameplay because moving and jumping feels like your character is made of noodles. At this price point I think I'll just wait, but I'll definitely be back, it's just a big slog at this point.\t\t\t\t\t
