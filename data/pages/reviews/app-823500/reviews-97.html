\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an incredible game. It really sets the bar in terms of movement & mechanics and is absolutely a must-play for any VR user. In fact, if you're toying with whether to get Boneworks or Bonelab -- GET Boneworks. Having gone through both, I honestly think this title is better and is my #1 recommendation when it comes to suggesting something that really showcases the mechanics and potential of VR.

That said, I feel like the meat of the game is all within the first few levels. Getting past the "museum" and stuff and the training course is really the eye-opening "woah, this is incredible". Past that, it got very repetitive for me; toward the end it was mostly a slog to just get through the levels so I could say I finished the game. Of course, I'm not one for first-person shooters, so I'm not the target demographic, but the fact that the physics and mechanics were still enough to wow me speaks volumes.

I'd love to see these kind of physics/mechanics but in one of those simulator games, like Job Sim or something that's geared more towards sandbox/adventure/exploration rather than shooting everything that moves and scary spiders jumping out of boxes.

Oh, I will say that the story was horrible. I mean, maybe it was just too over my head; but the plot did not appeal to me at all and was way too confusing in a TV Show Lost kind of way; like mysterious and cryptic just for the sake of being mysterious and cryptic.\t\t\t\t\t
