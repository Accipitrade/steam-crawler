\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off the ragdoll physics are really raggedy, pushing things into place and moving things around that would be simple in real life are an effort of frustration in this game, everything jiggles in this game worse than anime boob physics. On the flip side the physics are really sluggish too, it's easier to throw bricks than it is to throw kunai, which is very off putting because I'm a actually a decent knife thrower irl. The sensation here is kinda like trying to throw a knife under water.

Your character, although capable of climbing vertically if there are enough handholds, also seems to have the arm strength of a drunk toddler. You can't hold a sledgehammer or sword without wobbling everywhere and making very weak seeming ineffectual swings. Trying to climb over walls is also really annoying, even though you can lift yourself over the edge, your body just hangs there dumbly preventing you from getting over unless you do some weird crouching ape walk. Sometimes when you fall one of your arms will get stuck in the wall and you have to spaz out to get free.

The trailer makes it seems like there is an action packed story driven adventure, but I am 4 levels in and there is no story whatsoever so far, just you wandering around trying to figure out how to go from one end to another with the occasional lurking zombie or gun turret in the way. The closest thing to a story so far is the random graffiti on the walls.

Finally and probably the worst offender of all is there are no checkpoints or ways to save progress, so if your game crashes or you have to do something else there is a lot of ground you have to recover. I would take my time and try to find all the collectibles, then steamvr would randomly crash and it only lets you start at the beginning of each level, so its very discouraging and I wind up just rushing to get to the end. Or if you need to wrap up playing for a while for whatever reason, its dreadful knowing you have to go through everything all over again. It just takes all the fun away.

If the game wasn't so annoying with its shaky clunky wobbly physics and lack of saves I would probably still recommend it despite its lack of story just for the novelty of it, but after the really satisfying climbing from Stormland and much better throwing mechanics from Asgard's Wrath, this game makes you feel like everything is even harder to do in the game world than it is in real life.\t\t\t\t\t
