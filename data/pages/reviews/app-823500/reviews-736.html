\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hope in the future that Boneworks will be a footnote in the history of VR, earning the title as the most boundary pushing game to come out of the platforms early years. No other game seems to be brave enough in the current niche market to prioritise the physics and world interaction above player comfort, if the experienced VR player can fare with that being lost, the game will be a far more richer experience than other games can currently offer. Somewhat close to an antithesis of Half-Life: Alyx in approach, the training wheels are completely off, everything in the game has full weight and game world will have more control of your physical movement than you over it.
From a gameplay perspective it isn't perfect. I haven't pushed myself to complete it as of just yet, but I wouldn't let this be any sort of reason to label the game as a tech demo, it is more than worthwhile in terms of value. In the amount of time that I spent with it, it was enough to get the incredibly fun experience that I was expecting and completely change my thinking in what VR games should aim to achieve.
Near essential for any enthusiast.\t\t\t\t\t
