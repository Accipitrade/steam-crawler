\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is probably my favorite vr games at the moment and just by playing it for a little you'll definitely find out why.

The physics are just outstanding and it really gives half-life fans the fill they need until HLA comes out.

The combat is really smooth and has some of the most satisfying reload systems where you don't need to do a combination of actions just to reload a clip, but still feels real enough to keep you engaged.

One of my favorite parts of this game are all the little secrets and alternate routes you can take making the game go as fast, or as slow as you like.

The Sandbox mode is lacking a bit at the moment, but i'm sure in time they'll continue to add more content and my hope is that it gives off a GMod vibe.

The physics gun are a big part of the post first run as you need to redo the game and get every bit of ammo just to purchase one, but after getting the utility gun and null gun, i'm worried that any future levels/content they may add won't have the same flow and excitement as the past levels as i can just fly through walls and get to the end, but this is easily fixed by just fighting the temptation or by locking those features until you've beaten the level for a first time. But at the same note, the null gun and utility gun make it very easy to find some of those secrets you probably missed your first time around and makes completing the sandbox item list very easy without the hassle of going back through loads of enemies/puzzles.

Overall, this is definitely a must have game for any person with a little experience in vr, and gives you much more then what you payed for. 10/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGold standard for gun realism: Hot Dogs, Horseshoes & Hand Grenades, although too much realism can sometimes hinder fun.

Gold standard for locomotion: RecRoom, best run, jump, ledge grab, and climbing dynamics hands down.

Best physics and hand manipulation: Many other games do it better, even SteamVR home.

However, Boneworks is the only one out there (for now) that combines a good version of all these elements, which makes gameplay interesting.

I can't recommend it in its current state because:
- Level design looks alright and seems to be inspired by Half-Life and Portal but there's too much filler, lots of useless areas, and weird spawn placement

- Inventory is wonky and frustrating in situations where you need to react quickly

- Blue bins at the end of a level to have your weapons follow you to the next area feels like a cheap workaround to having a real save state between levels

- No checkpoints or saves, if you die you restart the whole scene

- Having a collidable body and arms could be a good idea if the physics were more polished and VR headsets had a better field of view, but it gets annoying and you always get stuck on random crap

- Physic puzzles could've been fun but once again, physics reacts weirdly and it becomes a chore

Bottom line: this feels like something you release to beta testers, or an early access version, not as a finished product. Wait a bit or get it on sale if you want to try it out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHoly Hell, this reeks with pretentiousness. You can't take a single step in the (very lengthy) tutorial without an accompanying onslaught of along the lines of; "THESE IN-DEPTH MECHANICS ARE COMPLETELY GROUND BREAKING, AND AS SUCH MAY BE DIFFICULT TO COMPREHEND !! TAKE BREAKS IN ORDER TO MAKE SURE YOU DON'T OVERLOAD YOURSELF WITH SUCH MIND-BLOWING PHYSICS !!1"

The devs are quick to assume that making a bowling ball heavy constitutes rocket surgery, and thus does a lot of superfluous baby-ing. From what I've seen, this extends into the campaign a fair bit as well. When they're not tooting their own horn, this looks like a decently well put together game, if you can accept the fact that it tries to do everything at once. The proverb "jack of all trades, master of none" really rings true here. The gunplay is mediocre, the puzzles uninspired. The best thing that can be said about the climbing is that it's possible. The level design is alright, but takes huge liberties from the "surreal" nature of the setting by having almost zero organization. The story has all the charm of a jittery mother at a wedding and is lucky to be only half as annoying. The actors make for very unfortunate mediums for telling it, too.

Maybe with time I'll warm up to it more. It isn't a BAD game by any means, but it's hard to recommend to anyone that isn't holistically easy to please.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game spends a lot of time with its weak points, but its strong parts are pretty good.

I think probably about half of the game is boring or frustrating. The climbing is a cool idea, but you just get stuck on things too often. I think I got my arm stuck through a wall 3 times, and the game respawned me halfway through the floor near the end of a later level. The combat is usually quite fun - it requires more technique and creativity than most other combat systems I've used. But there is not much variety in enemies, so once you have a method, you can just stick with it. The music is passable, nothing special. The story is very dull. The art direction is occasionally okay, but mostly devastatingly awful. The graphical detail, however, is usually really high.
The most exciting part of the game is the anticipation of it changing into something better - because it has so much potential. The physics system seems unique and interesting, but by the end I'm just disappointed they didn't put it to better use. Even so, I'm glad they tried making a long vr game.

Definitely a mixed rating from me, maybe 6/10.\t\t\t\t\t
