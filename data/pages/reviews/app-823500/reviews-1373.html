\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far so good. This game definitely feels like a general advancement in the field of Virtual Reality. The Graphics are very good - very Half Life/Portal (it's really no surprise, the game started out as some form of Half Life prototype by all accounts).

I own an Oculus Rift, so I was not able to access the advanced hand movement of the Index. However, the controls seem to work adequately, barring some strange oddities.

The finger tracking is perhaps the most sensitive I have ever seen in any game with the Rift; too sensitive in fact. You literally have to brush your finger imperceptibly against the trigger to have your hands twitch out like he has tourettes syndrome. Secondly, the crouch mechanics feel very VERY odd when mapped to the down and up movements of the right stick. Just gradually moving around with have your character lurch downwards in a Spiderman pose. It gets annoying. Finally, the hand grip tracking for the Rift just doesn't seem to be up to par with the more expensive models. More often than not, you will see your hand just snap to a surface likes it's magnetic and your hand will twist and warp as you move it. It could really do with an update.

But that's all I really have to gripe about. The game is absolutely phenominal. I can't wait to play more.\t\t\t\t\t

Post game review:

Overall game was great, ending was kinda lacking. I feel like there were tons of really cool puzzles and game mechanics they could've used but got cut short. That being said, it did take me about 12 hours to beat the game, so not short by any means. I just think the end game introduced a lot of cool stuff but didn't have time to build on it. Examples: Physgun, magnet climbing things, and the part when you have to use one of the guys that mimics your movement to open the cell (there could've been cool co-op puzzles with that mechanic)

Honestly one of my favourite vr games so far, will be going back to do sandbox and arena mode. Worth the full price. SL0 has done a fantasic job again and I'm eager to see what they'll put out next!

Now I'm ready for Half-Life: Alyx!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat up, son? Highly recommend this game!

Original Review:
Have not completed the story mode, so I can't yet comment on campaign length. What I can say is that--especially for $30--this is the best VR game you can buy at the moment. The story so far feels like a fun combination of some of my favorite games (Portal, The Stanley Parable, and Half Life). I expect we'll see some comparisons to those games, but Monogon as an environment is really fun to exist in.

The music fits the game very well, and the physics are beyond anything I've had the chance to play in VR. Gravity manipulation, incredible guns and melee weapons, and the feeling of knowing that everything you touch in-game combine to give an incredible amount of depth to this world. One last note until I've finished the game; I'm in awe of the graphics. While the general game looks great, the moments where shields/glass objects stand in your way have made me pause and take in the lighting effects. A lot of thought went into making these textures look beautiful to look at, and until you've seen them for yourself, I'm not sure I can describe them any better than that.

I'll write more once I've finished the story.

Updated review:
Finished the story. Took me roughly 13 hours, taking everything slow and soaking in as much of the environments as I could. I have sold non-believers in VR with this game, and I will keep coming back to it. Chances are, if you bought a VR headset, this is the game you were hoping to play. The physics engine creates a sense of presence that I haven't felt in any other VR game. If they sold the engine, I would not be upset seeing what other developers could make with it.

I really hope they make more games, and that Boneworks sees some updates. That said, the sandbox/arena modes are fantastic to have as extra content and will likely be the main source of joy from this game now that I've finished the story.

Having seen several friends approach the levels in different ways, I will be replaying the story to see how differently/quickly I can move through it. Thank you for the amazing game, SL0.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImpressions from first 3 hours.

It's pretty well polished, menus feel really good, and it feels like a real "game", unlike Hover Junkers. The story is a super basic cliche "corporation bad, die in vr die in real life" that's been overdone to hell, but who cares, it's the gameplay that matters, right? Well, too bad 90% of the gameplay is super basic, but incredible frustrating (due to the jankyness of your character causing everything to spaz out and go flying) puzzles that are so unenjoyable I don't even want to continue the story mode anymore (not to mention, there is no mention of save locations, and I now have to re-do an hour worth of "puzzles" that are going to make me pull my hair out due to the character spazzing out.)

The gunplay is okay, back when they were first showing off the gun videos years ago it looked pretty cool and innovative, but compared to what we have now they are pretty lacking in terms of features and feel. Although, when actually shooting enemies it's pretty fun, too bad there is barely any combat (there was 4-8 fights involving guns in the first 3 hours, and those only lasted a minute or two at most). Honestly, this feels like a rushed release, I do want to say, it is a pretty cool game overall, The city scenes are pretty nice looking, and I'm sure a lot of people will enjoy this game, but it seems 90% of the game content is just poorly designed puzzles that are nothing other than a means to open a door, which I cannot stand.

I would give this a middle thumb if possible, I'm sure plenty of people (particularly those new to VR) will love this game.\t\t\t\t\t
