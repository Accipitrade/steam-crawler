\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed with the full Valve Index kit:
I think there are a lot of innovative ideas behind this game, but unfortunately the final product is no fun to play at all (at least not for me). I bought this game short after its release and I was kind of blown away by the immersive feeling you have in the starting levels. Due to the very long levels and the missing save system I stopped playing the game until the recent update.
Now, I tried again for a couple of hours but still did not finished the street level as the controls and the total physics is way too clunky to have fun with. In real life it would be easy to put a box in front of you and then climb on it. In the game this is nearly impossible without failing a lot of times. The worst thing that can happen is that your in-game hands are completely out of sync with your real-life hand and you have no idea what is happening. For me this was totally immersion breaking. But I think the worst part is the level design. It makes no sense at all. The levels do not feel natural in any sense.

These physics puzzles might be a neat idea, but why is it so easy to move a whole working platform, but holding a hammer with one hand is nearly impossible? They use completely different scalings of "weight", which makes no sense at all.
I really want to enjoy this game and I bought it in preparation for Half Life: Alyx. And actually it really helped me to get rid of any motion sickness. But beside this, it is really no fun at all.
I would recommend everyone to skip this and simply wait for Half Life: Alyx. I am pretty confident that this game will have set new standards.\t\t\t\t\t
