\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo boneworks is weird. It's physics fight itself way too often.

Melee feels more or less on par with blade and sorcery, with one glaring issue. The body you are in has no clear physical strength level. Some times he has adult levels of power when it comes to things like climbing, or throwing a hammer. Then he turns into a 6 year old when it comes to flipping a couch, using a sledge hammer, or shooting a gun, that is clearly a glock, which should not kick nearly as hard.

The second big issue is the body itself. It has a weird weight to it. At first its neat to look down and see yourself. After a while your body just feels like a puppet. You are still just a headset with hands, but then the rest of you has no consistency. It way too bouncy and wobbly. I have no idea who thought thats how the human body feels, but they need to see a doctor.

And the final issue i have is the gun play. It preforms similar to zero caliber. Main issue is, the guns kick way too hard or too little. Then the guys hands end up being, in weird places and animations. It just combines to a weird, off putting feel. Then they make the words seem so empty and tense with a beautifully done atmosphere, but give you enough bullets to make it through a serious sam campaign.

It's melee is a saving grace, the phyics work great for the world and objects in puzzles. The main issue is, it feels good to see those physics, but the act of interacting with most objects usually are not worth it. Things like sledgehammers are top heavy, but with one hand, leverage, and gravity, you don't need two hands to swing it hard. I won't judge anyone for loving this game, it does some things really good. I think the real issue is, it did too much. Stop calling it the best VR game in existence, cause really, its just... not. Not compared to competition that are still evolving and growing, doing specialized things. On top of pushing the communities with modding, trying to help them, furthering content amount, and improving quality by helping said modders works stuff out\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to love this game, because when it works it is INCREDIBLY fun. The guns feel wonderful, the combat is exciting, there was clearly a LOT of work put into this game.

But I just can't recommend it as it is. I had to play through the 'Streets' level MULTIPLE times because there is NO CHECKPOINT SYSTEM, it's either beat the entire level in one go or lose all your progress. Now that I'm in 'Runoff' my guns have started dropping from my hand when I reach for a mag, they won't reholster correctly, the game freezes and crashes, and the physics puzzles have become insanely frustrating. I've probably spent 30 minutes over the past two days trying to put this heavy box on a hook before it starts shaking too violently.

Granted some of the issues are probably due to the fact that I'm using a Windows MR headset, but my god is this game frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't really recommend it, although I want to. Whilst it's clearly a labour of love and it aims high, it just isn't fun to play. It exposes the limitations of VR rather than pushes the boundaries. Sometimes it's a total mess and drifts close to being VR Trespasser.

The level design is awful and has you wandering round and round through bland and repetitive environments often with little signalling of where you're supposed to go.
Enemies are incredibly boring and just walk straight at you until you kill them.
The guns basically all feel the same, it's just pistol and rifle in slight variations.
The physics engine frequently has bits of your body getting stuck in the environment, for example in railings or on scaffolding. There's a reason Alyx is just going for hands.
Puzzles are tedious rather than clever and rewarding.

It's an engine in desperate need of a game.
