\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is probably my favorite VR title. I was very surprised you can control different fingers with the standard vive controllers. The physics are something special, and the combat is a blast. While these things are great, you've probably already heard of them, so I won't go into detail about those. This brings us to my two main issues with this game. The lack of saves and some puzzles.

The save problem is a big issue and many others have this same complaint. Many chapters last around an hour or more and do not have a single save feature. You can only put down the headset knowing your progress wont be deleted if you get the boneworks loading screen and you have a new scene in the quick menu. This is a quite annoying feature as you are forced to continue playing even if you dont want to. If stess level zero add a save feature this would be an 11/10.

My other issue is a smaller one. Some(Not all) of the puzzles are confusing. I feel there isn't much direction and you are left to guess as to what to do.

Besides these two problems, Boneworks is 100% a must get if buying a VR headset.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay is some of the most fun I have ever had gaming, not just in VR. The story has a bit of a slow start as it teaches you how to play but once you get to the later levels the action ramps up and the game really shines. The story is interesting but probably won't be why you keep going back to the game. The gameplay and physics are what will keep you coming back and are some of the best for a VR game I have ever played. Nearly everything in this game can be interacted with. It will likely take you a little bit to get the hang of climbing and jumping (crouch to climb over the edge of objects) but once you do it feels great traversing the map. It is very satisfying to leap for a ledge and snag it with your outstretched crowbar, then pull yourself up the crowbar and onto the ledge. This is the only game to almost make me fall over trying to reach something in game.

It does have some bugs/glitches and you need a solid block of time to get through some of the levels on your first go around. Some levels will take 1-2 hours if you are fully exploring and your progress will only be saved if you complete the level; so you have to finish a level if you don't want to restart it (there are some mid-level checkpoints for if you die during the level). Though it sounds like they are adding in checkpoints you can resume even if you exit. I did have to replay a few hours during my playthrough but it was a minor inconvenience since I enjoy the gameplay so much. There are some glitches like getting your hands stuck on the other side of a wall which is annoying but most of the time I have been able to unglitch them. I haven't had a single crash or the game locking up yet.

Overall I would highly recommend this game to anyone that enjoys games with incredible physics. Do be warned however, the developers did not make this game for people who get VR sick. They did not many (any?) comfort options and it is likely to make anyone that doesn't fully have their VR legs sick. I could only play for about a half hour my first time trying the game before I felt queasy. By my third session, that had gone pretty much completely away and I was able to play for multiple hours and still feel comfortable.\t\t\t\t\t
