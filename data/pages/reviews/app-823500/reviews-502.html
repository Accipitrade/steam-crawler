\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo in short, do I recommend this game? Absolutely.

In long, Boneworks is an action adventure VR game. It has a heavy focus on exploration in each level and has lots of weird and interesting secrets to find if you look around. One of the biggest things about this game that you don't see in other games is the fact that your character is a physics object. Which means you can collide and smash into things/enemies, this can lead to a number of interesting ways to fight including body checking and headbutting which is a very nice touch.

Some pros about this game,

The exploration is very cool, and you could probably spend hours in single levels trying to track down all the secrets.
Gun-play is really good and responsive. I have way too much fun just running and gunning through levels, and the ability to slow down time makes it an absolute joy.

Your character has the ability to climb or scale anything with the right tools, which ends up leading to a lot of really fun ways to avoid obstacles or puzzles if you're willing to get good at climbing. In addition, the game has some very clever puzzles using the fact that you are a physics object.

The game's atmosphere is amazing, it feels very desolate in its own way, and there are some very clever world building things. Things like having storage containers for variables because you're in a compute program. It's all very neat and feels very clever. The background music also does a very good job of this, as its very synthy(?) style really fits with the setting.

Some things that need improvement,

Melee combat feels.. clunky sometimes. Blunt weapons feel completely useless most of the time, and I ended up using my crowbar more like a spear than a blunt weapon.

Gun-play is really good, but there isn't a lot of variety in the firearms you get. You can 3 pistols which go from large magazine size, low damage, to low magazine size, high damage. Your rifles/sub-machine guns range from compact and lots of bullets, to a rifle that fires semi-automatically. There are really only 7 firearm types, all the other firearms are just variants/ have different attachments. If there is another game in this series, or in this style then I personally would like to see some more weapon types like revolvers or shotguns(Especially since you have a pump shotgun in one of the trailer videos that never shows up in the actual game). Also I would ask that attachments be removable and can be attached to different weapons.

I love finding all the secrets and unlocking all the items, but please put a secrets pouch or something for all the item orbs you find. I don't need more weapon space, but you run into the issue where you don't want to explore levels after a certain point because your inventory is full. Then you don't want to play the level again because you gotta trek all the way back to where you were while searching for stuff you may have missed. Just give me a pouch or something that I can put all the item orbs so I have a reason to explore the very cool levels.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tABSOLUTELY AMAZING. By far my favorite vr game. It easily beats out TWD:S&S. The story is unique and well done. Although it's hard to understand without watching a youtube video or 2.

Mechanics wise, its very polished and advanced. Some of the climbing can be a a bit jank and it's near impossible to force grab things through debris. The shooting is extremely well done and satisfying. Crouching can be done IRL or by pushing down the right stick. Jumping works well too. Grabbing and moving things is a joy, every object has it's own weight and feels like actually grabbing something.

The enemies are pretty same-y but the freedom of how you approach the situation makes up for it. There are a variety of melee, weapons, ranging from metal pipes to kunai. The guns are pretty standard, assault rifles, smg's, pistol's and some extra fun ones :)

Definitely one of the best vr games. Duck season is also worth a play. I'm so excited for what Stress Level 0 has planned!\t\t\t\t\t
