\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAwful game. Everything is physics based, which is cool at first, but it more often than not just ends up being a broken buggy mess. My hands don't go where I want them to half the time. Climbing feels cool and broken at the same time. There's barely any story to be had in the first few hours. Some puzzles are fine, but some are tedious and broken. There's one section where doors lock behind you for no reason, and you need to do a bunch of tedious backtracking. Even though it seems fairly linear, I still often found myself walking in circles wondering what the heck I'm supposed to be doing.

My biggest gripe though is the item "collection" system. In the tutorial, it says to deposit items into an archiver to unlock them for sandbox play. However, these stations are aparently very few in the story mode. You will come across dozens of potential and secret collectible items, but with pocket space for only two or three at a time, it would be mindless and tedious backtracking upon backtracking to get all the secrets. Not to mention, the items in your pocket that you meant to deposit can be destroyed if you walk into the wrong area. This means you need to re-find secrets, many of which require janky puzzle-solving/platforming to reach, if you couldn't find an archive spot. So whenever I find a secret, instead of saying "Oh Cool! I found a thing!", I instead say, "Oh great... another thing I can't collect because I don't have any pockets left."

Oh yea, I've also had a few enemies become invincible by hitting the walls at weird angles, but they can still shoot me. So that's not fun either. This feels more proof of concept than game. With more polish, and inventory space, it could be great, but as it stands now, it's just infuriating.\t\t\t\t\t
