\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe major problem with this game:

1) It's intense and the only VR game I have that makes me sick if I play for more than 30 minutes.
2) You're always bugging out trying to stash something in your belt but you'd have to move the controller inside of your own body or inside the chair you're sitting in to do so, so you end up smacking yourself and trying to get it to register what you want to do while you're being attacked and killed.
3) There are no checkpoints or saves, you have to complete an entire "scene" of the game if you want to actually continue where you left off.

As a result of these three, every time I play the game I spend 30 minutes or so playing but do not progress anywhere because I never reach a checkpoint before I have to take a motion sickness break. The next time I play I will have to play the same section from the start again, so I will never have enough continuous time playing the game to move any further.

UPDATE after 11 more hours of attempting to play the game: I've been able to progress somewhat after they added checkpoints. The biggest problem after playing more is that the controls require you to grip and hold onto whatever you want in your hands, and you're pretty much always holding something in your hands. On Touch controllers, this is incredibly painful on the hands and wrists and has led to actual injury playing the game. I have been diagnosed with tendinitis and experience incredible pain trying to play this title. The most regretted purchase in my library considering the cost compared to what I've been able to get out of it.\t\t\t\t\t
