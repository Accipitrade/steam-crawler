\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a VR game, it's an absolute blast to play. I made the mistake of making this one of my first VR experiences and got terribly motion sick on my first two sessions, so I'd recommend anyone new to VR to try and build their VR legs with other, simpler games first.

The only reason I brought myself to finish Story Mode was to unlock the other game modes which are so much more enjoyable. The Level Design isn't very good with puzzles just thrown at you with no context whatsoever, and there are times certain parts of maps take up quite a lot of space and intrigue you at first, but actually serve little to no purpose. The music is downright annoying and forgettable. I couldn't bring myself to like the story at all, and the characters on the monitor feeds had TERRIBLE acting and I always found myself just breaking the monitors before they could finish what they were saying... that brings me to my next point; It's the physics, the interactivity, and the combat that make this game still great despite its shortcomings on the game design side of things. It's definitely at the cutting edge of VR gaming. I only hope that VR games continue to get better and better from here on out.

7.5/10
