\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStress Level Zero has evidently learned many lessons from their own and other VR games. So many QoL additions are added to this game that really makes it clear these devs have played the crap out of this game and they've made it with love.

There are three main aspects of Boneworks: Combat, Puzzles, and Exploration. All three use the physics basis of the game elegantly. The combat system takes some getting used to, especially for the larger two handed guns, but the learning curve is worth it because the gunplay is just so satisfying, along with the melee. A really notable addition is the item vending machines scattered throughout levels. These types of things add lots of replayabillity.

The puzzles seem to be a good level of difficulty, unlike many other VR games with extremely easy puzzles, Boneworks has climbing, pulling, weight distributions and many more puzzle mechanics that combine with excellent VR puzzle design to deliver portal quality puzzles.

The length of the campaign seems highly variable depending on how much time you spend exploring every nook and cranny. That being said I spent hours on the first level just exploring the mechanics and hidden areas.

My specs are i5 6600k, gtx 1080, 16 GB RAM. The game has run extremely well so far. I was worried that my system wouldn't be able to handle this game because some games give bad reprojection (looking at you sairento) but Boneworks has been running at a solid 80 fps on my Rift S.

If you have VR, get this game. It is now up there with Beat Saber and Pavlov on the list of must play VR games.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCOMPLIMENTS:

Given that this is coming from a small team of game developers in a 3d game in vr being this innovative is nothing short of amazing.

It's impressive how little annoyances there are, but there some. There is no quick save, so if you get fed up or lose interest you'll have to start that level from the very beginning. Some levels drag on, some are quite short. Overall they are different enough to show all the potential BONEWORKS (as a platform/framework/engine) can bring to vr.


WISHES:

There should be an option to toggle-hold certain items, that goes for climbing too. My vive wands have seen a multitude of wear and tear because of this game. Some holding-interactions are just too awkward and certainly too uncomfortable. This should be limited to index controllers which are built to that end.

A "you are about to hit your rooms ceiling"-kinda warning system should there be too. All too often you have to reach over your head whilst your real world environment may not allow for this (sitting option didn't seem to solve that issue)

I tried different turn-rates, but even 10 isn't instant. Whatever Pavlov does here works better for me.

Although this probably isn't the core point of BONEWORKS, if this would have PAVLOV's gunplay in it, it'd be perfect! Doesn't feel as satisfying here...\t\t\t\t\t
