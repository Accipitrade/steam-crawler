\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthe enemies of this game are stupid, the hit boxes are small and weapon damage doesn't multiply evenly across the enemy types causing the headset crab to be the strongest in the game, while holding the smallest hit box. most humanoid moving objects take 4 shots to take down or one shot to the head (this doesn't always work for what ever reason but even still an head shot usually knocks down a standing opponent) the crablet on the other hand takes 5 shots on the outside or three to its sensitive inside, but still more shots than the humanoids with no access to increased damage, cants block their jumping damage with your hand. oh and if you have a melee weapon don't bother chasing crablets, their reverse speed is the same as the max movement for the player, so it can keep pace at distance until its ready to jump. and then jumps you might get one lucky shot inside the crab or spear it with a sword but you still need 3 more shots just as much as a standard enemy. it makes no sense why these headsets are so durable. if there had been a lore reason at the begining saying that "we've designed these next generation to with stand all the wear and tear of standard play" or somthing else cheeky i would understand, but theres no reason a single head crab should be taking as much damage and time as the physically scarier enemies. technically on the wiki it says the zombish body has 60 health making it take 1 more hit than the crablet to take down. but its got a larger hit box more accessable soft spot and a slower walking speed. in summery the crablet is the fastest, smallest, strongest enemy in the game with the most abilities, it can jump gaps, it can latch to your head obscure 100% vision. and theyre phisically stronger than you, spear one on a sword and it will dictate where the tip will go.

I absolutely love the climbing mechanic, most puzzles that have annoyed me I've instead found ways to climb around the obstetrical blocking my way. although the puzzles are fun they take too long for me to keep going back to an area and redoing them or make me feel ill as some of the puzzles put you on moving platforms that move your vision and make my stomach do spins.

unlocking things in the sandbox mode requires you to drop it into a specific receptacle, the issue is that there's only one per level and i often find my self stuck with these little balls clogging my inventory instead of the weapons i need to actually make it to any of these receptacles. these balls hold the unlocks for certain objects except the balls are super bouncy and I've mistakenly grabbed one instead of my weapon and ended up dropping it and having it bounce away which is the most frustrating thing when you find something cool and then combat forces you to prioritise the 5 inventory slots to weapons

gun play sucks,don't get me wrong at the shooting range its a lot of fun to go through the weapons but in combat there are too many grip points when trying to reload, pistols have the least grip points but when cocking the gun every time my controllers hit each other. i often end up with the gun pointed towards myself while trying to load the weapon. it is also often too dark to see down the sights of a weapon which ends up blind firing and wasting ammo. as well with the way the index grips work, every time i pick up a weapon i end up firing off a couple rounds. as well there is no mag release button.
swords and melee weapons have no bladed edge you can run the katana across and enemys throat and nothing, angle it to the tip and run the edge across and bam youve impalled the guy. so no blades just spikes, cool what about the weighted weapons, too slow to hit the crablets not enough damage to kill in one hit, too slow for larg groups. thrown weapons are a joke with the index controllers, i often just send the throwing knives into the ground or up into the air, same with spears 0 accuracy with throwing anything, so glad there arent grenades.

which brings me to my second point of the controls and grips with the valve index controllers. the grip on the middle finder are too sensitive with their grab which has made any door, cabinet, or drawer I've encountered to stick to the hand well after i have attempted to let go, which while gripping these things the game will then pull you as its trying to lift you up and climb forcing the perspective to twist and causes nausea. the control scheme on the index is poorly utilised the central pill is never used, left and right rotation being attached to the same stick that changes height has made it so that the turning is inconsistent requiring me to double and triple tap the left and right on the stick just to get the right angle and often go too far on way or the other. i would have much rather had the height adjust put to one of the pills.

movement is abysmal and makes me feel ill, i find myself resetting the movement style multiple times as it defaults to hand controls, which would work except when you are lifting anything above waste high the movement style inverts and makes it a pain to move and lift anything so much so that at one point it defaulted to the hand movement while i was lifting i got overwrought with nausea and started to fall and slammed my hands together cutting open my knuckle.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn short: Boneworks is one of my favourite games of all time and probably one of the best games for VR to date.

In detail:
The game isn't perfect, far from it actually. Interactions are often janky, performance isn't too great and collecting some things can be really tiresome. None of those can actually taint the experience though. Physics are incredibly immersive (my friends spend at least an hour each in the Breakroom before the actual tutorial). Weapons are distinct and feel natural after a short while. The inventory system is easy to use and doesn't let you take out your items periodically. While the story isn't as well-written as HL:A, it's definitely intriguing and unexpected.

On the subject of the campaign: it's around 9 hours, which isn't too great for a single player flatscreen game, but compared to other FPSs it's not too bad. Additionally, this only accounts playing through it once. On top of that you unlock the Arena mode, which lets you battle infinite waves of enemies or predetermined challenges with unlocked weapons. When it comes to collectables, there are a lot of them. Every level of the story mode holds at least a few weapon unlocks and many Sandbox mode unlocks.

Sandbox is akin to Garry's Mod. You get a utility gun and can spawn anything you've unlocked in the campaign. Redacted chamber and Hover Junkers don't have it, but instead offer you a smaller, standalone experience and let you unlock additional items.

Lastly we have the Zombie Warehouse - COD Zombies in VR. It's well made, melee weapons are scattered across the map. Utilities, guns and map expansions cost you ammo which you gain from kills. The mode also features the Board Gun which allows you to make barricades and play more tactically.

In conclusion: the game is a lot more than just a great campaign. If you want to unlock everything or at least the most interesting items, like the noclip gun, or the Utility Gun for the campaign, you'll play through it at least twice. If you add to that both arena gamemodes, sandbox and the promise of future updates, you get long, long fun.

BTW
I have NOT played Half-Life:Alyx yet, so I cannot fully compare them, but from my perspective Alyx is a lot more polished and gives you an incredible experience, but Boneworks is more unique and bold in the implemented mechanics (like the full, physics enabled body). Alyx however, has workshop support for both items and maps, while BW modding scene is barren. I might add a comparison section in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
