\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe tech is great and it's fun to mess around with physics and virtual guns, but once you get over the novelty and actually play the game, it feels like more of a tech demo. The levels are mechanically unnatural and bland in a way that one would design a test level for developer use only. It's the sort of world that would have been built by software engineers as opposed to designers and artists. There's no in-game navigation aid in the form of waypoints or a map so exploration is completely old-school, but the world poorly communicates what you're supposed to do or where to go. If you have to ask if you're going the right way, your level sucks and I never know if I'm going the right way.

The physics sandbox doesn't lend itself well to game play. Shouldering a long gun to try and aim down the sights never feels good with the rifle wiggin out as it collides with your virtual shoulder. I'm constantly losing track of my virtual hands because they got snagged on something so actions as simple as climbing never feel natural. I get that the devs were trying to simulate weight, but when we're expected to carry a particular box to place on a button why make it annoying and awkward to lug it around?

TLDR: The devs were more concerned with providing a realistic sandbox experience and forgot to make an actual game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the benchmark for Half Life: Alyx. If a team of indie devs can create over eight hours of top-level fun for someone who spends way too much time in VR like myself, the team at Valve needs to create a better, longer experience. BONEWORKS is:
-Immersive, so much so that I tried to set down my controllers on a virtual table, and sit on a virtual couch.
-High-Quality, to the point that I sat around like a chimp playing with the laser sights. And honestly, it's just
-Fun. Messing around with not only the enemies and guns, but things as small as how satisfying doors are to slam open and closed, is super fun.
However, it lacks replay value, but for how much fun it dishes out on the first playthrough, it doesn't matter in the slightest.
As for quality-of-life, I would suggest adopting the "grip button to grab/drop" system they use for guns for all Hero Items, as opposed to melee weapons and the like needing to be held by holding down the grip button. Playing a chapter at a time makes the holding fine, but binging the game and/or taking time exploring or using melee weapons or anything like that just makes your fingers ache. Also, the final level is BRUTAL on the fingers. Plz fix.
TL;DR - BONEWORKS is deserving of #1 VR title, and it has a lot of potential, and sets the bar for VR adventure, action, and puzzle shooters. 10/10, literally played again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKeep in mind while reading this review that at the time of writing this I have 13 hours playtime, enough for one full playthrough of the story and some messing around, albeit I know that this may not be enough playtime for some to go on a review weather to buy something or not, I'm also not super experienced in writing reviews so please correct me if I get my information wrong anywhere.

I played this game using an Oculus Rift S and the included Touch Controllers.

I'll start with the negatives so that I can praise the game later, as it really deserves praise for the positives.

First off, the story is hot garbage; I'm sure no-one came into this expecting something amazing and it's fine as a placeholder but I don't think I've seen a more boring story before, the concepts are super cool but the execution in writing and (for the short parts that contain it) acting just falls flat.

The enemy models aren't great, you can get used to them but they're just weird-looking, the null bodies especially show this, a generic zombieish enemy would have been way better than the wacky design we got.
On the flipside, the headcrab-type enemies are the best designed in the game I believe.

I feel that the level-design just falls so flat at times, especially the end which makes you climb up a massive structure which is just a completely vertical climb and is boring as hell especially when most of the game was super satisfying to complete, the end just falls so flat with the design, including the fight with the king being stupidly boring and not satisfying at all for an ending to a game where you do so much badass . some of the puzzles are okay but they don't make you feel satisfied that you've completed them.

now there's a lot of little things here and there such as putting away weapons at times can be a little janky but I understand that with a vr game this will never be perfect, and to be honest just the feature of pulling your weapons out is really satisfying.

Let's get onto the good of the game, the gunplay!
The guns are nothing short of incredible in this game, you feel so badass being able to mow down enemies and reload in cool ways with slowmo helping add to the C O O L A S P E C T this is something I've never seen done as well before in VR and this alone is just one of the coolest parts of the game.

The physics are great, at times yeah they can be wonky but overall it works so well and I absolutely love the ability to climb, I wish there were some more interactable objects just to add a little more immersion but what we have is amazing on it's own.

I love the amount of easter eggs in this game, it's super awesome and adds a ton of replay value to a quite short game.

So overall,

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is a mess. I am writing this form a perspective of a Vive user with Vive wand controllers.


Basically the user interface is atrocious with vive. To pick up items from a distance you have to press grip, trigger and place your thumb on the touchpad. and hold it until you want to let go. This includes melee weapons.


The melee weapons feel awful because there is a limit how fast you can swing your arm before your wrists fold over like cooked spaghetti, which makes having to use any melee weapons sluggish and frustrating as you wiggle your bendy spaghetti arms. Remember, this is while having to hold down grip and trigger and hold your thumb on touchpad on the wand or you drop the weapon. If you have a two-handed weapon you have to hold grip, trigger and holding a thumb on the touchpad.


Item interaction is frustrating as well. You have to pick up items with grip and trigger and thumb combo again. And hold them. Of course this becomes exponentially more annoying when the game decides your hand was not exactly in the right spot and magnetically sticks to the surface of a shelf or a table, which then ends up in a c**k-up cascade of epic proportions as the stuff you put on the table flies all over the room. Also, if your hand as much as brushes against a table, the table may end up flipping wildly, scattering all the items on it everywhere.

The only thing where
you don't have to hold the grip constantly are the firearms. I can't tell much about them because the game mercifully decided to end my suffering before I got a proper hang of them and crashed.


Climbing is like trying to slapping wet, stretchy noodles onto surfaces, hoping they stick and then wobbling like you were holding on to a rubber-band. Remember, you have to use grip and trigger button again to grab onto surfaces and keep squeezing or you fall off.


All in all this was a very frustrating experience. While they clearly tried to make something "cool" the end result reminds me more of a game called "Octodad" where you control a squid with limp tentacles.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks has some of the best VR gunplay and is really the first great VR title with a plot. It was hopefully going to be the Half Life 2 of VR. What really let's this game down however is that the level design is writing a check the physics engine can't cash. The extreme focus on physics based puzzles utilizing climbing and jumping is a novel concept except for the climbing and jumping mechanics are absolute ass. It's actually mind boggling, I followed the development of this game closely so I know that this is a project they really care about. So how they could release the game with the climbing and jumping mechanics, especially when they knew how much of a focus was on it, in this state is insane. Games with significantly less focus on climbing like Blade and Sorcery and even janky ass Standout VR do climbing much better.

Honestly this game feels like it's still in beta, SLZ really should have given this game another 2-4 months worth of dev time to iron this issue out. The problem is exasperated by the fact to unlock items in the sandbox mode, you are required to find hidden capsules in the campaign, which are often hidden at the end of these physics puzzle that are a frustrating chore to get through.

Seriously guys, smooth out physics and update this please, I so want to love this game but every play session ends with me walking away frustrated and pissed off. It's such a shame that such a minor this is genuinely ruining my experience.\t\t\t\t\t
