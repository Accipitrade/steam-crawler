\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI probably would have recommended the game when it was new and it still had some novelty factor to it, but as a late newcomer to the game I found it to be an inherently frustrating experience. I played using continuous movement. Hands keep getting stuck outside the view. If you try to grab items on a table it makes you grab the table instead. Any climbing sequence is a fight with the hands and body getting stuck in the environment (couldn't the friction/stickiness been zero when you didn't grip or something?). The combat however is quite good with most of the weapons being fun to engage with, except when the default grip point on any melee weapon is the worst grip point imaginable. Grabbing a sledge hammer? Then the game will always grab it by the center forcing you to readjust the grip to get any reach at all. It is even worse with one handed weapons like an axe or hammer. Why would I EVER want to grab it halfway on the handle? I lost count how many times I tried to grab something from the ground with my left hand just to have it pick up ammunition instead. When I instead wanted to pick ammunition in gun fights I seemingly did not grab ammunition half the time. Trying to equip/unequip items from each slot was also a hassle and I ended up trying to use the on-screen inventory instead. Then let's not get started with fighting the controls when you lift any big object. Oh and I barely know what the story was about as I couldn't stand watching the screens.

At least the gun play was engaging when it worked.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInsanely overrated.

The story is not interesting in the slightest and is pretty much just there.
Gunplay, enemies, climbing and melee combat has all been done better in other games.

The gunplay is probably the best of the mechanics in this game because it's not impacted very much by the infuriating physics, but there is a lack of weapon variety, there aren't even shotguns.

Climbing and movement is probably the most infuriating thing in this game, trying to move yourself around is annoying and limbs will get stuck on stuff constantly, it's very janky- and god forbid you try to climb over a ledge.

The melee combat is probably the most disappointing, hitting something with a weapon feels very unresponsive and the enemies barely react.

Play HL:A for an interesting world and story and better enemies, play Blade and Sorcery for good melee combat and climbing, play H3VR for better gunplay and weapon variety.
