What I like


Despite how negative of an opinion I seem to have of this game, I recommend it. I've been playing this a ton. The physics, sandboxes, and immersive gameplay make up for this game's flaws. It's seriously a fun ass game, especially when you use your creativity. You can use a trashcan's lid as a shield to get by a sentry turret or you can simply sneak by it. There are little robot spider VR headset things that jump on your head and you can slam them against ♥♥♥♥ if you want. That's what makes Boneworks so special. You can do pretty much anything you want with the environments you're thrown into. You have a lot of freedom to play how you want to and it's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ sick .

What I don't like


This game's story feels empty to me and I can't explain why. I feel like it's lacking direction at times. I get that I'm not supposed to know what's going on, but sometimes I really don't know what's going on. The story is tucked deep down in the shadow of the gameplay, if that makes any sense.

I never thought I'd say it, but it is pretty slow and repetitive after a while. I'm currently at the sewers scene. I'm not sure if this is just one slow area of the game, but I feel the game losing its energy. I have a feeling it's going to pick up again, though, so I'll edit this whenever I find out.

Also, the gameplay feels pretty clunky at times, but that's expected for any VR game that's trying to do as much as Boneworks is. Climbing objects is something I've had a lot of difficulty with. It took a long, long time to sort of understand how to properly do it. Aside from climbing objects, though, mechanics work smoothly and as they should. Most of the time.

Concluding thoughts


I think I'm just a little burnt out at the moment because I played five hours straight yesterday and two hours straight tonight. For a VR game, playing five hours straight is just something I don't do. I can't really do that. Something about Boneworks makes it hard to stop, though. It's addicting. If you're thinking about getting this game, but are unsure of whether or not your PC can handle it (as I was), I suggest you get it. You can always refund it if you play less than 2 hours of it (and submit a request within 2 weeks I think). If you feel yourself getting bored of the story like me, take breaks from it and try the MANY other things to do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is quite ironic that the game is developed by a studio called "Stress Level Zero". I was perpetually stressed playing this game.

Firstly, the atmosphere of this game is very intriguing. It has a certain mystique that leaves you wanting to know more about the world in which you are in. However, the story itself is lacking. There are clues to what has happened all throughout the game but it is not really developed outside of the occasional person talking to you on a monitor (which is few and far between).

Now, the physics. First off, everything feels weighted properly and moving things about feels real. The problem? Legs. When doing anything that requires you to climb, your legs will be stuck on something three miles away. I am not sure if this was an issue with my style of play but this is what caused the majority of stress. Perhaps I did not grasp something in the tutorial. Oh well.

Overall, I would say that BONEWORKS should be experienced by anyone wanting an enjoyable puzzle/shoot 'em up VR hybrid. Although my stress levels were certainly not zero, I would be interested to see what Stress Level Zero have up their sleeves next. Perhaps this proof of concept could be fleshed out into something far more substantial?\t\t\t\t\t
