\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the best VR games that I have ever played.

It literally feels like Stress Level Zero went over to Valve and saw Half-Life and said "Hey, can I copy your homework?", "Sure, just change it up though so the teacher doesn't realize."
The game's physics provide such a great feeling of immersion. The best example of this that I can think of is when you pick up something heavy in the game, the physics engine tricks your brain into thinking that the object is heavy and you struggle to lift it. It is something you have to experience to understand, but it's insane.

The main downside to this game is accessibility. The game has a warning right from the start that claims that it is intensive and not recommended to those unfamiliar with VR or prone to motion sickness. There are no teleport options or even basic comfort options, until they release the update for that, I would say you need to steer clear of this one.

This game is so interesting and fresh. I think everyone should try this one out. It is so insane.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game, I really do. But I can't recommend this game.
Where do I start?
The whole physics thing is just awful. I get idea what they we're aiming for and it looks good for most part, but when it comes to actually interacting with it, it's just bad. Like there's a spot where you have to build a "bridge" and good luck doing it. The best I can describe it is like playing with physgun in Gmod. However, you don't have physgun, instead you use your hands and again, I understand they trying to make physics more "realistic" but it just felt soo freaking awkward and bad to do so. I cannot stand the gun handling. It's just not responsive at all. I'm guessing it's because the butt of it is colliding with the body or something and it just goes haywire and I can't aim for ♥♥♥♥. I played hundreds of VR games and this is the first time I'm having these issues.
However, there's one fatal flaw with this game. One Flaw that ruins it. No mid-level saves/autosave. Its 2020, and they couldn't implement saves? I know the excuse why they did not do it, but you telling me you couldn't come up with lazy solution? (just reset physics object in level then). The biggest problem is that levels take hour or so to beat and this is intense VR game. At least you could have shorten/split one level into multiple levels, cause what happened and honestly what makes me quit this game until they solve the saves, is that you make huge progess into the level, you reach the point where you have to use the wonky physics, you fail many times resulting you to drop from big heights many times , now keep in mind, any normal/light VR user would already need a sick bag after 2 or so falls, I kept pressing on and even with my strong VR legs, I couldn't handle it anymore and got motion sick which made me quit the game and lose all of the progress that I made. What's worse is that they couldn't even implement that your inventory carries over between levels.
Further more, the game honestly just feels boring. I think I was finally reaching a part where there's story (the first 3 levels are glorified tutorials) but because no mid level saves , I won't bother with this until they actually implement it.

Honestly, it was not worth the hype. Walking dead Saints and sinners IMO implemented everything that boneworks aimed for but better. The physics are great, the whole climbing works way better in TWD ( it feels, again, wonky in boneworks) and so on. Maybe after few patches it will be better, but at this point, I just can't recommend Boneworks. Sad really\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere to begin with this game....

If you haven’t read up on this game, it was initially meant to be only a tech demo, that went the route of game. So any issues I give, take it with a grain of salt, as this wasn’t meant to be a AAA title. If at the very least, it’s basically meant to show you what VR is capable of, and developers what they can do/make with this technology.

The short version is this is Half Life VR. From the classic crowbar, to the pick up of mostly useless items(but fun, nevertheless). If breaking boxes hoping for loot and killing crab-like face eaters is your thing, then this is the game for you. If not, there are still a bunch of cool mechanics for most VR enthusiasts.

Pros:
Interesting motion mechanics
Climb-able objects, and ledges if you know how
Fun hack-and-slash physics with axes, knives and swords
Realistic guns
Simple body item-slots for weapons
Lots of hidden things to find
Definitely a workout when standing and hacking
A playground where you can fight to rekindle your childhood with seesaws and toy trucks.(Seriously. I was pretend driving that truck for like 5 minutes! Sound effects and all!..... *sob*)

Cons:
Not for people who get motion sick really easy
No drinkable or edible items. They’ve got cans and bottles(minor but it still bugs me)
Movement can be a bit wonky at times
Hands get stuck in stuff sometimes.(Rare but it happens)
Grabbing that hammer might mean knocking the whole shelf over
Added “weight” sometimes feels more cumbersome than immersive(wait...)
Enemies feel a bit re-used, but I’m still early enough on that this could change

P.S. If you find balloon gun hidden in the world like I did. Grab a chair and turn that thing into something that would make Mary Poppins proud!!!

P.S.S. If Garry’s Mod does this, I’m never seeing my family or friends again. Love you, but my life is colored with pixels now.\t\t\t\t\t
