\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnce you get used to the controls, this game feels fantastic to play!
Everything in this game is designed to be inteacted with it and it is the only VR game i have played with a full physics based IK body... this body is implemented really really well into the game, and allows the user to use any pick-up-able object as a weapon or a bullet sheild during combat, climb onto or accross any object, do fast paced movement such as vaulting, swinging and climbing!
The gunplay feels really good and in my opinion even feels better than the highly reveered Half-Life: Alyx. I very much enjoy using both melee and firearm weaponry in this game, love being able to reload fast with the pistols and rifles now I've gotten used to them. I love being able to use the heavier weapons such as the long sword or sledge hammer to pound enemies, knock them over of just keep them at an arms distance when i get overwhelmed.
My favourite weapon combination so far is a non-conventional one: 1911 for long/mid range, a metal bin lid for bullet cover and a brick for close range xD
The level design in this game works really well with the different methods of combat available and I love the levels with holes in them so i can use heavy melee weapons or my own weight to throw enemies down the holes.
For anyone who has found their "VR legs" I would highly recommend this game!
If you have not found your VR legs yet i would still recommend this game... but in shorter intervals... perhaps 20-60 minutes at a time untill you get used to the movement.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this after taking the plunge and buying an Oculus Rift S. This is my first VR headset so i was a little unsure if it was what it lives up to be. Immediately after getting into it i was amazed to say the least. The physics are incredible. Movement feels pin point accurate and every item in the game can be interacted with. Objects have a certain weight to them you soon forget you're in a virtual world. Hitting things with a hammer or axe feels punchy and the firearms feel great. The puzzles are creative and require thought and it gives the sense that any approach to a situation is sensible, the whole "There's no such thing as a stupid question" translated into There's no such thing as a stupid method. The only small gripe is that sometimes when an object or humanoid is in point blank range, ocasionally it suffers from buggy interactions, clipping or the motion controls not registering as intended but it's not a hugely problematic thing. I guess it's due to how VR works over all. This is such a huge step forward in gaming and you can tell the devs put their heart and soul into it. If you have a VR headset and a PC that can run this, it's definitely not one to miss.\t\t\t\t\t
