\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the time of writing this review, I only have 10 hours of gameplay and haven't yet beat the campaign. The TL;DR of this is that it's a great and innovative game, but it's not without its flaws, and it isn't for everyone.

Let's get into the positives

1. Of course, the physics. This is the game's signature aspect. Every damn object can be picked up, or broken, or interacted with in some way. No other VR game I've played has come CLOSE. I could and have spent a good chunk of time just messing around with objects in the game. The weight, sounds, and physics are tuned to near perfection.

2. The atmosphere. This ♥♥♥♥ is tense. No matter if I play for five hours straight or five minutes, I'm getting out of my headset sweaty. Not because of physical activity, though with my body resembling a stick figure more than anything else lends that some credence, I get scared. Full on HR at 120+ BPM. I don't want to spoil anything, but you're gonna be on edge nearly the entire time.

3. The radio. A very small detail, this, but I love it. There's radios around that all play one song on loop (at least in the beginning, I haven't found any this late in the campaign) and I love the song. I played a 5 hour session of the entire beginning with that radio on my back playing that song on repeat. I cried when it Disintegrated.

4. The exploration. This is sorta good sorta bad. There are tons of secret routes, rooms, tunnels and etc. Being vigilant of these will reward you with collectibles, ammo, guns, melee weapons, funny moments, and somethings I'm thinking are either world building lore or just atmospheric details. I'm too stupid to piece anything together if there even is something there. The bad part is I probably could have finished the story in half the time I've taken thus far (can't say for sure because I still haven't finished it) but I check every damn box, every ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hallway, empty every trash can, just looking for secrets or rewards for exploring, and it's quite rare for it to pay off. Like I said, when it does pay off, it pays off big, but sometimes I just get tired of smacking boxes against walls and stuff just to see.

5. The art and design. I could spend pages on each aspect of art and design in this game, but neither of us have that time, so I'll break it down. Sound design- ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ amazing. 15/10. Modeling and textures- pretty damn good for a small team. 9/10. Gameplay design- very convenient and ergonomic. Dropping or picking up things can be a little frusterating. 8.5/10. Level design and environment art- very clever, but puzzles tend to be quite punishing in terms of making you just spend time redoing things in the beginning. 8/10. Story- quite unique and interesting, but not too driven. 8/10.

Okay, now let's get into the cons.

1. The sound and music can get deafening in a fight. Sometimes there's just too much sound and it is very disorientating.

2. Dropping things in trash piles tends to result in said dropped item clipping through the trash.

3. Player clipping. I know this is a very complex and difficult problem to deal with, but sometimes if I'm climbing, my feet get stuck under something and my legs clip through the top, resulting in me falling, getting stuck, or 15 minutes of agony trying to free those pesky shoes.

4. Perhaps my biggest complaint, the saving. Progress is saved when you complete a level. Never any other time, except for dying which will spawn you a bit further back, however I encounter a problem of losing whatever item/weapon I was holding when I die. But whenever you want to put the game down for a bit or for the night, you either find a safe spot (because I don't think the game actually pauses) and leave the game running, or close the game and start the level over. This frustrates me to high hell sometimes, because as I stated before, I explore every nook and cranny. I spend nearly two hours on each level. Maybe I'm doing something wrong, because as I also stated before, I'm stupid, or there really is no way around it. If there isn't I don't understand why. Maybe it will be implemented in the future, maybe it's a design choice. I've learned to not start a level if I don't have time to properly explore it, but the first few times (yes, I was dumb enough to do it more than three times) I was pretty upset. But maybe the devs have done this for a reason. All I know is that you will go on long streams of on edge gaming without much of a chance to catch your breath, and it being this immersive of a VR game, it may be too much for some people. My friend seriously couldn't take it, but if you want to feel like a tactical badass who sometimes nearly ♥♥♥♥♥ themselves along with all of the anxiety and stress of feeling watched and hunted by something unknown, you'll have fun. Just go for a walk and cool off a bit while exploring the levels.

All in all, this fantastic, unique, and beautiful game gets 9 thumbs out of 10. Truly amazing.

Thank you for reading my super long review (longest one I've made) and I apologize for not proofreading it yet. I just finished a session of this game, it's quite late and I work tomorrow morning, but I felt a strong desire to write this review as I truly love the game. Thank you to the devs for creating an experience I know will stay with me for the rest of my life for building such a beautiful, immersive, and unique experience. You people rock, and if any dev actually reads all of this, thanks. I'm a 20y/o wannabe game dev who hasn't ever been on a team that's managed to finish anything even to an alpha state, but even a dumb kid like me can see that you people literally have an industry changing game right here. I know you must have put in love, passion, and care into this game that us players will never truly know, keep up the amazing work. ♥♥♥

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot without it's problems, I've gotta say, this is a cool game. There are many good points to make about it (and I'll probably miss some, but many other reviews have covered them anyway), such as the graphical feel, much of the level design and puzzles, the body and arm presence/inverse kinematics and cool jumpsuit your character has, down to the shoes. Great looking hands too. Also, the guns and the manual reloading, etc. I've put in 8 hours so far, so I've played quite a bit, but I have to say, this game has some wonky and janky problems.

The cool design and aesthetics of the game are right up there with AAA games in VR, but the frustration of the physics...oh man. I've never had a more annoying time with climbing mechanics in a VR game. I do have the Vive Wands I'm using with the Index HMD, so I know there are some issues there with lack of ideal controllers; but again, I have played multiple climbing games, including The Climb, Climbey, and the climbing portions in the Vader Immortal game. Those all are just straight forward and enjoyable, but I have no idea why I would want my body and hands to bounce around like overly active rubber pieces, catching on things all the time as has been my experience with Boneworks climbing and swinging melee weapons and tools. I've been regularly playing VR games since 2016, and this is one of the few top tier games that I can say I love and also get about as equally annoyed with at the same time. I have no problems with those who are loving Boneworks in every way, or at least most ways, as I can see the amazing aspects of the game that keep me playing as well, but trying to stand on or jump on a decent sized wooden crate/box or two that acts like the weight is more like cardboard and flips at the smallest offset of weight to one side or goes rolling/sliding off another box as I try to jump on top as my janky VR hanging legs and knees hit in to the box (since climbing on it like to hoist myself up will only cause it to roll and tip me off). It's immersion breaking, not immersive or enjoyable. Rubbery wrists, where the likes of crowbars, bounce and wiggle from side to side like your character has little to no strength to support such objects is just silly. Makes you seem like a wimp, not a bada** while in a virtual world. I'm still learning how to handle the physics as intended to create a more lifelike result, but I'm not "feeling" it. To be immersive, I would need more than some haptic feedback vibration to tell me my hand is caught on something or that I have a heavy object I'm swinging. Swinging a sledgehammer or even the crowbar is most of the time a task and it lags behind to create the effect of weight. I get that. I do. But it doesn't feel right and takes away the fun factor. I would need real resistance to my real arms and wrists to make it immersive. But nope, my virtual arms are going one or two ways (or flopping and bouncing around entangled in an object of the virtual world) and my real arms are going quite another way. So I could really do without all that jank that's immersive to some people, but not to me. Besides, if it was as realistic as it is expected to be, why does a box that I'm trying to rotate and push on top of another box...why does it's edges intersect into my virtual body instead of push off of it when I'm walking into it and trying to lift and rotate it? I'll mess around more with that mechanic of the physics, but if they could tone down some variables in the weight effects on some things and make other objects more stable, it should be doable.

I have not yet mentioned that in the last 2 play sessions, I've had Boneworks crash 3 times on me. Once was before the latest nVidia video driver update, and the other 2 were tonight (after having updated my video drivers the night before), where within about an hour of playtime between the 2 crashes, the game just locks up/freezes and I got kicked out to Steam VR Home and another time, locked in a jittery screen of Steam VR Home and yet Steam VR was still working. I have not had crashes in any other games recently either. I doubt much it's to do with anything other than the game, but I also don't think that it's likely the beta either that I'm using for the game, because it's more for just the save feature since that is available now. Thank goodness for that too, since the crashes would have caused me to restart such long levels.

I do want to add that the game runs very well almost all the time on my GTX 1070 card (also 8700k CPU and 16 GB 3200 MHZ RAM). Just not understanding why the random crashes /lockups of the game though.

If the game was more than $30. I'd be pretty upset right now because I'm past a refund window, but it's a great game overall for $30 despite the few issues I mentioned. I just wish there was a way to turn off (or more realistically >> Turn down) the hit detection on the arms, hands, and wrists, and to make climbing more reasonable...as well as make the character get some virtual protein and muscles going in that flimsy rubbery VR body I 've got in order to hold and swing things, and climb on things with more agility, stability and plain old ability. ...even if it is a Vive wand issue, which still would make it unique to Boneworks in my experience.

I'll keep playing these days to see if anything on the cons side changes for the betterment of the experience. Otherwise, I'll still enjoy many aspects of the game, and I'm looking forward to Half Life: Alyx next month.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, Boneworks doesn't have a save feature.

However, you can press a button labeled continue and choose from whichever scene you want to and pick up at the start of that level. The levels generally aren't too long, save a couple. As of now, I'm most of the way through the story, and it's great fun. The physics are great, and I often forget completely that I'm standing in my room. The atmosphere is great, combat is pretty hard at times with some of the more capable enemies, and the headcrabs are going to give me nightmares for a few weeks. I smashed a lot of stuff in my room by accident while flailing around trying to get them off my head.

Overall, the game is good. The negative reviews I've seen usually boil down to something like: Oh well the physics aren't that great, my arms wobble when I climb. Or: The game doesn't have a save function it sucks.

Really, if you don't have an hour to play anything in VR, just put your headset down. One hour is the longest it took me to beat a level, and that's because I was exploring the ♥♥♥♥ out of it. For the wobbly arm stuff, if you can hold yourself up with one hand while climbing for a solid few minutes without any wobble, I applaud you. The only instance I have had wobble is when I was one handed, trying to pull myself up a ledge.

It's worth the money, I highly recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
