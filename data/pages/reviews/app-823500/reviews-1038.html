\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreatest VR game I've ever played. It's by no means a perfect game, the flaws that bug me are the glitch of getting your hand stuck in objects, lacking diversity in weapons and enemies, and lacking a decent story. Aesthetically im not thrilled with most enemies either, but it's growing on me in general. The music is great. The sound effects are very half-life triggering. It definitely feels like playing what i'd imagine to be half-life VR. The "uncanny valley" physics are like nothing else. The weapon play feels so damn good. Reloading is incredibly satisfying. I'm also amazed at how it doesn't get me ill despite how much I play. If I put 4 straight hours into a Croteam VR title, I'd be coming out feeling sick but Boneworks, somehow it's like I'm wearing nothin' at all! nothin' at all! nothin' at all! nothin' at all!

Edit: I was wrong about the story. It's brilliant as hell. Deep lore here y'all, plenty of meat on this bone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks isn't quite the Half-Life 2 of VR gaming, but its an exciting and innovative game that has been and will be referenced as a milestone in VR interactivity. Its also a lot of fun in my opinion, with only a few flaws, one of them a glaring flaw.

This game has a bit of a learning curve despite fairly simple game mechanics. The physics are awesome; maybe too active when you are trying to stack some crates for climbing or whatever, but still awesome. The premise has been described as similar to a Valve game both in style and in experimental gameplay elements, one of those elements being the innovative use of physics, especially as they react with your body in game. Throwing objects is smoother than other similar games, climbing is intuitive once you get some reps in... but there are so many smaller examples of well integrated VR interaction for example: gun physics. The general feeling of firing and reloading guns is great in this game, requiring steady aim, and some practice with recoil control. Tapping the trigger at range is a must in my experience as full auto will kick your gun off target as you might expect in real battle. One reviewer experimented with using a wall to steady his gun against as he fired, reducing recoil as would happen in real life (maybe a small thing but the type of feature that VR gamers love because many of us got in to this for the increased realism).

The melee combat is also deeper than previous games; you can grab a melee weapon anywhere on the object (the head of the hammer, for example), or with both hands for increased control and speed. When you hit a robot enemy it reacts satisfyingly from the force and direction of your swing. Heavier objects have simulated weight meaning that your in-game arms may not be able to lift an object as high as your actual arms, and heavier objects may swing more slowly in game than they would if they followed your real arms at a strict 1:1 ratio as they usually did in previous VR games.

Story and setting are well done in the game, maybe a 7-8 out of 10. There's not a ton of story, but its done well and the setting hints at a deep and interesting world beyond the edges of the screen. No complaints here, except we wouldn't mind an expansion or two to add some more fun hours to the game.

So what flaws does Boneworks have? Well for me the only huge flaw is the one pointed out by countless reviewers: the save system. The game has only a handful of saves that can be reloaded after quitting the app. Yes if you die there are plenty of auto save spots to respawn at, but if you quit, you may be looking at redoing hours of progress before you understand just how sparse the checkpoints are. Not the end of the world but still... Otherwise its mostly just nitpicking, although I would say the Physics could use a slight toning down as they do the classic Havok stuff like refuse to settle once dropped, and kicking around more than real objects would. All in all not too many flaws from my point of view, although the game could throw in a little more action upfront but...

Fantastic and important game for VR gamers! 4.5 for me.
