\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly, this is the greatest VR game I've ever played. It does have it's flaws but I'd recommend it a thousand times over.

Pros:
Aesthetics, atmosphere, music, attention to detail, environmental world building, narrative, physics, the ability to do 'anything' within the physics. The varied landscapes surprised me and there are more hidden things to find than I expected. Bullet time is incredible, everything feels very polished and it is actually a full length game for once.

Cons:
While I love the story, it was hard to follow. I feel like there were a lot of great questions it raised and pulled me in, but never answered many of them. Or at least not in an obvious way.
There are really only 3 enemy types with some variations.
Limited guns, but hopefully more will be added.
No saving system, I believe Node is working on this, but it can be frustrating dying with no check points or needing to get off the game and forced to start the level over.

The cons are just nit picking at this point. Overall as a package, I give it a 9.5/10. A must buy IMO.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay so fist of all, I don't hate, hate[/], this game, but I'll focus on negatives, as you can read what is good about Boneworks in other places.
It's just broken, messy, janky, pile of good ideas in worst possible configuration.
I've played it on Rift S with modified Touch Controllers for "knuckles" like strap, and it was a pain to get trough. Inventory is on your body but grabbing anything from your inventory slots is a game of chance. Good luck getting what you need in a heat of battle.
Force grabbing using both trigger and grab button is a f*cking joke, I can't even count how many times I've shot in the air/myself by doing that.
EvErYtHiNg iS PhYsIcS is cool on paper, but in reality it's a chore. I've failed many times in simple things like climbing a ladder because my hand was in some kind of void outside time and space getting stuck. Don't get me started on "puzzles" everything is janky and not fun. The last box puzzle will be in my nightmares. My body was getting stuck on everything, and boxes were going inside my body, a lot.
If you are buying it for a story... don't. There is almost none, it's like last second addition like they realized that they need something to excuse poor level and graphic design.
Performance on my machine (Rtx 2080, Ryzen 7 2700X, 16GB RAM) was hit or miss, there were places where game ran like an absolute nightmare, but overall it was okay.
Also shooting is kinda okay, but I've mainly used glock for whole game - easiest to aim, and you can find it anywhere.

Don't get me wrong when everything works, it really works, I had tons of fun playing it at first, but when thrill of something new, and hype died down, I dreaded every play session, mainly for one last big issue. Levels can be long, but they don't let you save, you either complete whole chapter in one playtrough or you are starting from begging, it's not okay, and this with addition of everything above makes me think that developers only tested this in house, without input from outside.

All in all, buy on sale, play 1:59h and refund if you cannot stand jankyness of movement it won't get better\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great game so far, the atmosphere and level design is solid and the story, while sparse, is intriguing. The music and sound design is excellently done. Guns sound punchy, melee weapons (especially the frying pan) give satisfying audio and visual feedback, and the variety in both are quite diverse. Weapons and weapon slots could use some work though. Long guns (or any two-handed guns really) are clumsy and awkward to use to any effect, and weapon slots like to just kind of... not work occasionally. Longer melee weapons are quite easy to get caught up in level geometry, so short melee weapons tend to take the spotlight.
The physics puzzles are decently challenging, though failing one tends to lead to a lot of backtracking.
Overall, a solid experience that needs some tuning, but still well worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t
