\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's hard to overstate how groundbreaking this game is.

Sure, it's not as polished as HL:Alyx, but it accomplishes a number of things HL:Alyx -didn't-. Don't get me wrong, I think HL:Alyx is -also- a masterpiece, just for different reasons.

Boneworks manages to recapture the feeling of excitement I got playing the original Half-Life, complete with the gameplay-as-narrative method of storytelling. The story the game-play is telling is ambitious to the point of audacity. It's hard to put into words. You play a person who is logged in to a near-futuristic virtual reality workplace, an administrator who is in the VR after it went on lockdown and booted everyone else out.

While you're playing on your real-life VR headset, your character is also wearing a VR headset. In the VR simulation-within-a-simulation, you can pick a VR headset off of a table and put it on your head, being in VR in VR in VR. From here, if you look towards the bottom of your field of vision, you'll notice your feet and legs and floor from the VR in a VR rendered in-game, and if your mask is not well adjusted you might see your own real-life shoes and floor below that. Some of these VR headsets in the VR in the VR game sprout four blade-like legs and jump at your head to force themselves on your face (it's featured towards the end of the official trailer). Without giving too many details, the story has a nice transhumanist bent I like to see in modern sci-fi. In a way this is kind of the modern spiritual successor to Tron (I'll just pretend the Tron 2.0 movie doesn't exist but the Tron 2.0 FPS you can get on Steam for like 3 bucks is better story and experience-wise compared to the film sequel).

This game was a bargain at it's full release price of 30 dollars, considering the only other game even kind of similar to this one costs twice as much. This game makes me excited for the future of VR gaming, I feel like it's doing something completely different from the "AAA gaming experience as made-for-VR" and really exploring the possibilities of the medium directly - doing something new and compelling with it.

I think Boneworks will be an enduring classic, and be more influential on the future of VR gaming than anything that's come before it.\t\t\t\t\t
