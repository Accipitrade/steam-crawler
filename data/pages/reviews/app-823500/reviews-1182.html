\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am relatively inexperienced with VR games but this game took me right in. The physics based puzzles, the handling of the weapons and the drive to explore the levels really made for an amazing gaming experience. I have now completed the story (which was presented in a great way!) and I am just eager to get back into the game, explore the scenes more and figure out how to get to those secret areas I can see but wasn't able to access.

When I first started playing, it took me a couple of sessions to get used to the motion of the game. I felt a bit sick after playing for about an hour but with 14 hours in game now, I am quite comfortable running and jumping around.

Even with my meager GTX 1060, the game ran quite well and the visuals were really good. The sound was also excellent. The perception of direction based on sound just works as expected.

There is only one thing I don't like about the game and that is the save mechanism. You basically have to complete a full scene to save the game. From what I have read they are going to work on a system that will allow you to save mid-scene which would be a very welcome change.

I can't praise this game enough. It's a massive step in the right direction for VR games. As far as I am concerned, Boneworks is the game I will be comparing all VR game experiences to.\t\t\t\t\t
