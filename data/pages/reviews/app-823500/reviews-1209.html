This is the most amazing experience gaming I've probably ever had.

The gameplay is super fun and emergent and I think that's key. The world is really interesting and the possibilities seem greater than perceived.

Also, I've never gotten scared or tense really in a VR title and a couple times already I've had some panic oh-♥♥♥♥ moments and for me that's really freakin' cool.

I played last night for about .5 hr and started getting warm and a bit woozy in the stomach. I think its just because of the judder.
I use to get a bit motion sick so I took Dramamine for this first 3.5hrs and have to say I had no ill effects what-so-ever. When you take dramamine you can see the judder but it doesn't effect you. Its like seeing screen tearing on a flat monitor, its like... oh, there's judder. I had to quit to eat lunch and rest my feet a bit, but ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥... FINALLY... someone made an ACTUAL GAME.

I've watched NODE on YT and have anticipated this game but playing it is completely different than anything I've watched and that's a freakin' fantastic thing. These dudes took all the stops out and said F$CK IT! and it shows. This is the first VR title that wasn't an "experience" or "demo" IMO and its about time.

This game makes me look forward to HL Alyx and makes me look forward to the next time I play it. Its incredible.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, a massive thumbs up from me.

Pros:
-The best physics in a VR game to date. Really helps to add to the immersion
-Arena and Sandbox mode for when the story is complete to give you some more hours in-game.
-A reasonable price for what it is. Worth the £25.

Cons:
-The story is... confusing. From my experience, you need to do extra research into the game's plot in order to understand it. For me, this is a big con. I prefer jumping straight in.
-A few bugs, such as flying across and going under the map that break that game.

Overall:
I feel like the physics is the main selling point, which is all well and good until the novelty of the realistic physics wears off. However, with that said, the gameplay value is great, the combat is satisfying and you're free to apply your own, unique approach to many of the puzzles and combat situations you're presented with. The campaign, in my opinion, is a tad too short for my liking and, if you haven't done any extra research into what the game's plot is, you're left feeling a bit confused.

Overall, it's an amazing game that had a lot of opportunity that has certainly not been squandered - you can tell a lot of time and effort went into this game, and it certainly paid off.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed with OG Vive + Wands.

This game is great. It's a game about exploration and world interaction. It's not for new VR users, as new users will probably find the movement system to be nauseating. Even though this game may tempt you to buy a VR system, I wouldn't recommend playing it until you've got some VR experience.

What's so good about the game?

Mainly the physics system. Boneworks has done for VR what Half Life 2 did for regular games. If you're climbing something, and things are a bit out of reach - hook onto that ledge with a crowbar, and then climb the crowbar to reach the ledge. The crowbar is probably the most useful item you can carry. Climb up something, and whilst hanging from a ledge with one hand, use your other to draw your pistol and shoot something. Push enemies over. Push objects over. Find interesting physics-based solutions to puzzles. Boneworks has such a deep level of interaction that makes other VR games like Asgard's Wrath feel quite lacking.

What would you see if you watched me play?

You'd see periods of frustration from dealing with untold levels of jank, due to the physics. Falling through gaps you may not expect to fall through and trying to get back out. Trying to move a cinder block and getting mad that the game keeps putting my wrist through the holes in it, and having to pull it back off. That's part of the game that you need to deal with. After that, there's a lot of fun from interacting with the world, exploring for the secret Boneworks crates, solving puzzles and combat.

What will you enjoy about it?

Interacting with the world in a natural way that has not been seen before. Exploring. Figuring things out. Finding secret areas. Seriously - it's fun!

What won't you like about this game?

If you ran through the levels, you could probably complete them in half an hour. If you explore for ammo crates, weapons, secret crates, the levels will be more like 2 hours each. I've done the first three proper levels, where I consider Streets to be level 1, and they took me 1.5 to 2 hours each. As a completionist, the game is long.

Why is this a problem? The game has no way of saving mid-level. The first time I played, I got 90 minutes into Streets and had to go off. I passed several areas that seemed like obvious checkpoints. I quit to the main menu rather than closing the game, figuring it would trigger another save. I then loaded back in, and it put me back at the start of the level. If you play the game, you need enough time to complete any level that you start. They're working on a save system for January 2020. I was mad at the time of losing my progress, but I actually found a new secret on my next run through Streets, and it was quite fun.

Summary

Game is fun, has a few flaws. It's very good, surpassed its hype in my opinion, and is probably one of the best games that have ever been made. This level of world interaction has never been done before, and it's completely worth experiencing once you have some VR legs.\t\t\t\t\t
