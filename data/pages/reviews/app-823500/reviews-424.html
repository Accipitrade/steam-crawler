\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS is one of the only fantastic games on vr right now, right next to Half Life: Alyx. if you dont own this and you have a vr headset... what are you even playing??????
honest question tho comment what you have been playing instead of boneworks id love to hear

a lot of vr games are plagued with the "only 3 - 5 hours long" syndrome and it makes wanting to spend 30 dollars non-existent.

BONEWORKS is literally NOT that. it took me about 12 - 15 hours to beat the story mode, and the rest is all sandbox baybeee

the story mode is kind of just a sandbox with an end goal, though, and the game does not do a very great job at telling it's story. it also ends very abruptly, right after giving you a brand new tool to play with, then the credits roll. still, if this game only had its sandbox mode, it would still be worth 30 dollars. it's really fun to get creative and slap some absolutely goofy little bafoon off a building or hit the Yoinky Sploinky after murdering 10 people, and my personal favorite... sending a monkey to the atmosphere in a shopping cart via balloons

ive seen a lot of people criticize this game for it's physics and them being janky, and i get it, but it honestly is not that bad. ive not really had a game breaking issue from it... just a little climbing jank
btw the near-end-game "Tower" level is extremely unoptimized got like 30 fps on that level but oh well


WARNING: this game will make you sick if you are not used to slide movement in vr. dont give up on the game if you feel sick immediately. it's something you can get used to by playing other vr games with slide movement. in another game try turning it on, then turning it off when you get sick, and turning it back on for progressively more time until it feels great. if you decide to give up on the game, its your choice, but you're missing out on one of the best vr experiences.
