\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay so, i don't normally make serious reviews on games, most of my reviews are jokes and i never take them seriously. But when i finally upgraded my ♥♥♥♥♥♥ 1060, I5 pc into my current 3070, Ryzen 7 pc, i NEEDED to try boneworks. Boneworks is a game that i've been following since development and a game that i've wanted since release but never been able to play due to hardware limitations. As soon as my pc was finished being built the first thing i did was buy and install Boneworks and setup my VR so i could play it.

6 hours later, I have finished my first playthrough of boneworks. Now I will be playing through the game multiple times and i'll be replaying all of the bonus content afterwards but my initial thoughts after completing the main story.
Boneworks is the Greatest VR game ever made and possibly might be my favourite game of all time.
I adore every single thing about this game, the parkour, the combat, the music, the level design, the enemy design (♥♥♥♥ THE SPIDERS), everything.
Now Boneworks isnt a game for everyone, i know, but all of this is my personal opinion and i know that some people wont agree with me, and that is quite okay. Boneworks might not be the best game for people that are new to VR, i was able to try VR on the PSVR and at my friends house a few times, as the game is a physics based game and might make players motion sick a few times. But once you have adjusted to the Physics Based World of Boneworks then it is just stunning at how this game is made.


COMBAT
------------
The combat is essential to Boneworks being a great game, as every level has you fighting off a few enemies if you so wish. There is 2 main components to Boneworks' combat, Melee and Ranged.
Ranged combat is definitely the highlight of the combat in Boneworks. Each gun feels so realistic and the weight on the heavier guns adds to it, so it doesnt always just feel like you can wave every gun about with no gravity or weight to it. Each gun's recoil fits the gun itself and isn't too much or too little. The added attachments to some of the weapons make them even more fun to use, as you get scopes and laser sights for some of the assault rifles. Hell, even reloading guns in Boneworks is fun, as you can just swipe the mag away from the gun and reload, or just perform the reload normally.
Now the Melee combat is probably one of Boneworks' "Weak points". There are 2 types of melee weapons in Boneworks, light and heavy melee weapons. You are more likely to use the heavy melee weapons in boneworks as the light weapons are quite scarce. The weight to the heavy weapons just feel amazing in the game and they feel really realistic, however the weapons aren't really great to use as the enemies also use the physics engine, so attempting to whack a nullbody on the head with a sledgehammer might be difficult as he will be wobbling about. Light weapons are much easier to use, however slashing an enemy with say a knife or a small hand axe is quite ineffective, compared to stabbing them with it.

PARKOUR and PHYSICS
---------------------------

Now the parkour and physics in boneworks are probably the most impressive parts of the game (especially the physics). We'll start off with the parkour, it's amazing, that's all i need to say, its amazing. You can grab onto basically anything and use it to pull yourself up, you can even just rest your hands onto surfaces and push yourself onto them, as your own character has a body that applies to the physics in the game. You can do pretty much everything while grabbing onto something and aslong as one of your hands are grabbed onto the surface, you wont fall.
Now the physics part of boneworks is the most impressive part of the game, as Stress Level Zero have managed to make a game where EVERYTHING is using physics, even your own player! You can pick up heavy things in Boneworks and they will have weight to them, you can throw light things and they will glide through the air, you can collide things with other things and watch them tumble about. Hell you can even just push your hand into things and watch them collide with everything. Even the enemies abide by physics in the game, the enemies will collide with other things and react in a realistic way, the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ spidercrab things will tumble across the ground if they fly into anything, and you can grab and pick up the enemies and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ slam them into anything.

The Music
-------------
I can't describe the music at all, just go ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ listen to the soundtrack of the game, it's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ incredible, every single track on it is amazing.

The Enemy and Level Design
-------------------------------
Finally we are at the Enemy and Level design of boneworks. In Boneworks you only really have around 5-6 enemy types and some can seem quite "repetitive" and similar to eachother but they all work in different ways. The Nullbodies are the first enemy you encounter in Boneworks and are the easiest to kill. They are only activated by bullets and if you walk close to them, and they will try and swipe at you to deal damage. Their looks cannot really be describe as much more than a orange body with white lines all over, outlining it.
Next we have the "Zombish" enemies. These enemies are pretty much, like zombies, they look like rotting humans and they attempt to run at you and attack you, similar to the nullbodies but are much stronger and faster, and some can even ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ shoot cube blasts at you. Next we have the Omniprojectors, which are enemies that stroll about on little balls with assault rifles trying to kill everything they see. They are the hardest enemies in the game, as they are the only ones with guns and can move decently fast. Finally we have Ford, i will not elaborate on who Ford is, you will just have to play the game for yourself. OH YEAH AND WE HAVE THE ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ SPIDER CRAB VR HEADSET BLADE ENEMIES THAT CAN SUCK MY ♥♥♥♥.

There you have it, my own personal review on Boneworks, a game which might possibly be my favourite game of all time, and in my opinion, is the best vr game of all time.\t\t\t\t\t
