\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game sets the bar for what physics based VR games should be. The story is interesting enough to play through however if you want to run some horde type game play or simply play around in sandbox mode, you must clear the story mode first (you can unlock zombie mode before playing the story if you do a little easter egg in the lobby). You must be used to VR already before playing this game. I let some friends and family who weren't accustomed to VR try this game and they've experience motion sickness.

Hardware:
I'm rocking the Rift S with a RTX 3080 (i7 8th gen cpu) which means I am not utilizing their knuckles support (on top of other things, like a lower resolution compared to the index etc), which I assume adds on another layer of immersion that I am missing out on with the rift s. This game works well with a weaker graphics card as well, previously I had used a GTX 1060 and it works fine, even though I believe they recommend a slightly stronger gpu?

Nevertheless, this should be one of the first games you buy when you purchase a VR headset (provided you can run it, but if you're buying a pc vr headset, you should be able to run most games anyway haha)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSome sort of strange VR version of Garry’s mod is …. a terrible … er … good idea 😉 … especially when you just started using VR, there is sickness inducing head bobbing, it takes you at least twice as long to complete a level, the game is in beta, and there are no save games yet.

Yeah, a terrible idea really, but in December ’19 I didn’t know what I was getting myself into. I found the game quite hard for a beginner, but now looking back it made it even more memorable. Now that some of the nauseating effects have been reduced and there are mid-level save points, it’s definitely better.

The game is still pushing what’s possible in VR though, and also what players can handle. It’s challenging, climbing and vertigo inducing puzzles are part of that, which I personally enjoy a lot; but I can understand that it is not everyone’s cup of tea. There are plenty of guns to have fun with too.


The atmosphere in this game is rather strange, if not to say disturbing in parts, but also rather funny. I can’t really get into too much detail about it, as I don’t want to spoil anything. I had to literally laugh out loud several times. The Nullmen are named that way because of their intelligence I presume.

The soundtrack is interesting and good and certainly adding to the strangeness of the whole experience, although I found some ambient sounds to be a bit too repetitive.

One thing is a bit of a bummer, the fact that Boneworks is lacking the great story and backdrop of Half Life: Alyx; which you cannot expect with a small budget, obviously.
Of course, HL Alyx on the other hand is lacking the fun melee system and the freedom to just climb almost anywhere. After having finished both games I longed for another VR game which has both, but I can totally understand Valves decision to not include a virtual body in Half Life: Alyx, as without proper full body tracking you do tend to feel like you are using a river dancing ragdoll in some situations. And the comedic effect isn’t really wanted in HL Alyx I guess …

I reckon if there will be a Boneworks 2, and VR is more advanced with Melons Tracker and all those frills, then the Nullmen will be very grateful. 😉\t\t\t\t\t
