\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt has potential. A lot of the time while playing it felt "meh".

People talk a lot about the physics. Yeah, they are great and should be standard in a lot of VR games but what bugs me are the following things for example.

Guns feel weird and there is not much to choose from. Both one- and twohanded guns change sensitivity when aiming and don't really point where you are expecting it. It feels as if your input isn't always 1 to 1 and that messes up your aim a lot. Not sure what it is but Pavlov VR for example feels really straight and precise when aiming. And why is there no shotgun? ;(

Melee weapons feel really slow and heavy, even the swords and they don't really do a lot of damage. It just feels unsatisfying to use melee weapons but sometimes you have to because the small headcrabs take sooo many bullets to kill. It's easier to just take a bladed weapon and let them jump into it... I'd rather shoot them and melee the humanoid enemies. A bit more visible damage with melee weapons would be nice too like taking apart the zombies with a long- or greatsword.

Another point is the lack of good storytelling and the leveldesign. It feels really depressing to run around in this world.

Boneworks tries to do a lot of thing well but only the physics really stand out. In the end it's really more of a tech demo and I think I got expected too much because of the hype.
I have much more fun in simpler games that nail what they are trying to do. I guess Boneworks nails the physics but having a physics focused game with riddles where you can get easily motionsick isn't fun for a long time. I often avoided climbing or riddles because I felt I wanted to see more of the story and possibilities in the game before I had to stop because of dizziness. The fact that there aren't enough checkpoints doesn't help with that. And all the fun weapons/tools cost sooo much in this game that I don't think it's worth it to play again and collect that amount of currency.

I will keep an eye on the game and maybe change the review when something big changes but for now it's just a "meh" feeling in total.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most fun games I have ever played.

It's longer than most other VR games are, so prepare yourself. Also quicker to feeling tired because of so much going on. But I won't let that make me give this game a bad rating. It's a ton of fun.

Feels a lot like Half Life and Portal along with some other games. The world is very detailed and most of everything is polished enough that I haven't experienced too many bugs. I'm also playing on a Windows Mixed Reality headset. I know there were issues when it first launched, but it is much better in today's state.

Only thing I want most of this game is a proper checkpoint/save progress system which has been promised for the new update.

I can't play for very long periods of time and still on the 3rd level Streets, but from what I've seen, I really love this game and can't wait to see more from this developer.\t\t\t\t\t
