\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is the next step in VR gaming and is the most advanced and ambitious game we've seen.
The game has plenty of content, especially for a VR one. Overall, it's great and one of the best VR that can be bought.
I will focus on the negative aspects but generally this is a pretty good game so don't let all my criticism scare you right away.


But the game has plenty of issues right now and some of them will most likely not be fixed:

- One of the main goals of this game is to push the boundaries of VR as much as possible and Boneworks does it pretty well. But in doing so Stress Level 0 seemingly forgot about the very concept of comfort features.
One of which is vignetting/FoV reduction which would help quite a bit with VR sickness while moving around while not compromising gameplay.
When climbing around the constant wiggling like your character's arms are made of rubber doesn't help either.

- Some of the game's levels are very long, and right now the game doesn't have checkpoints nor can you save mid-level, this is going to be added in the future but as for now it forces the player to play for more than he might want or feel comfortable to. Adding to the games already poor pacing.

- You probably have noticed the high CPU and GPU requirements of the game, and I'm happy to tell you that for most of the game you don't need such hardware for the game to run very well.
That is until the final levels… For the entirety of the game I had almost no performance issues but when in the level named Tower the game extremely often drops as low as less than 10fps. The end of the game urgently needs optimization.

- Currently enemy variety leave to be desired, you pretty much have 3 variety of zombies, headcrabs, armed men and the occasional turrets. The doesn't give a lot of variety in combat and the AI doesn't help much as they don't create very interesting encounters.

- The melee weapons selection is pretty okay, there's something for everyone. But I can't say the same for the gun selection, two pistols that feel almost the same, 3 SMGs and 4 or 5 variants of the same assault rifle. It's very disappointing to see that you don't get more variety of guns like revolvers, DMRs, shotguns, etc…
This leaves gunfights feeling pretty samey and you keep running into the same guns over and over during the game. The worst part is that SL0 has a working shotgun but did not made it available in the game…

- At first, I thought using ammo as currency was a smart idea, creating a system where you need to choose between always using guns/using guns earlier or saving ammo for better guns later on.
But when you arrive at the end you realize that the most interesting weapons and tools cost nearly as much as the maximum amount of ammo you can get in the entirety of the game. Effectively forcing the player to avoid using weapons and the Monomats (weapons stores).

- If you're like me and you use Vive Wands… You better have strong fingers because the game will ask you to squeeze those controllers a lot and for extended periods of time, your finger will most likely hurt after a while playing.
And if you're like me again waiting to get Index controllers then maybe wait until you get them before playing this game.

- The physics leave to be desired in multiple places, some physics puzzles are very frustrating and using blunt weapons to break crates can be very inconsistent.
Sometimes with the same you break crates on the first try without putting a lot of force and sometimes you need to hit them multiple times with putting as much force as you can while also using proper techniques to maximize your force. This unreliability can also be felt in combat with blunt weapons.
It also seems that hitting a wood piece on top of the crate you're trying to break will absorb all the energy, leaving both the wood piece and crate intact.


That's a lot of issues, but I still think that the game is one of the must have for experienced VR users.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate: If you're thinking of getting this or Half-Life: Alyx, get Half-Life 10000%. This game is not friendly for newcomers to VR, only has locomotion and HL:A has more replayability. Everything below I still stand by, but HL:A is definitely the game to get if you're deciding between the two (HL:A also has custom levels, so there's that to think about too).

Incredibly satisfying game, gun play is like no other, I'd love to play more and more of this game if I didn't sweat so much after finishing a level.

This game is fantastic, it's so satisfying just reloading the weapons, or using akimbo pistols or MP5's just mowing loads of enemies down, or catching one of those headcrab thingy's mid air and hitting it with the bottom of your pistol many of times until it dies.

The only thing to consider when purchasing this game is whether you get motion sickness in locomotion games or not. Everybody is different so it might be worth buying this game and see if you get motion sickness or not, and if so you can easily refund it as long as you're within the 2 hour playtime and 14 day mark.

You'd be an idiot if you miss the chance to purchase this game if you own a VR headset and have the money for it, the price is extremely cheap for what it offers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is, at the very least, groundbreaking. While the story is murky at best and the game is a little limited (there's not a ton of weapons overall) but I expect the feature set to get expanded in post-release updates.

It feels like the first game to really let loose with the possibilities of VR. Full IK bodies, smooth joystick locomotion, grip-based controls, a nearly full-physics world. It's VR without the training wheels.
It's not without it's flaws, of course. The full IK body is great until it starts getting stuck inside of things, the grip points on weapons don't seem to have any good system for focusing on what matters, and the grip controls on everything but the Index (and maybe the Oculus Touch?) are uncomfortable to manage for a long time.
Still, at least right now, this game feels historic, and is fun as hell despite the bugs and the minor muscle cramping from power-gripping your controllers. Easily worth the price, and I'm hoping that it sees further improvements as time goes on (and as my Oculus Rift S shows up in the mail.)\t\t\t\t\t
