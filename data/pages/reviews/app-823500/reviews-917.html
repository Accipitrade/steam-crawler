\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts a fun tech demo, but fails to do anything super interesting. Still, it feels like a needed step in VR games and I understand this was done by a small studio. All things considered, I enjoyed the game for what it was, but it does have its warts.

Ups: Usual VR fun of forgetting exactly how long youve had on your headset, which way youre facing and how much your ceiling lamp costs.

Distinct locations that did show creativity. If you like looking around, theres plenty to find. Not much in the way of story, in fact I hardly even noticed one. But that doesn't bother me and it isnt why i picked up the game.

Shooting feels pretty good, but more on that later.

Good sound design overall and swinging stuff does result in satisfying slaps.

Downs:
Locked content. Seriously? Most people are going to pick up this game for the sandbox element and much of its content is actually locked, you have to find it. I almost refunded the game right there. People cant play VR for hours on end like they can conventional games and honestly, locked content stopped be fun around the gamecube era. An absolute step backwards. People pay for this mind you, I want what I paid for when I paid for it.

Very little firearm variety currently. Two pistols, various AR types that shoot the same, and two MP5 variants. Not even shotguns. The weapon handling itself is ok, but I laughed when i went to slap the bolt release on the M4 and nothing happened. They modeled it, but you have to use the T handle every time. Not everyone is a gun nut like I am, but I actually shoot a lot so little stuff like that bothers me. And as usual using two handed weapons is a little weird, but every VR game has that issue, not just boneworks.

Enemies are boring, and you really can't manhandle them the way you can in say, Blade and sorcery. Speaking of blade and sorcery....

The act of swinging stuff was just done better there. Way better. Granted that was the focus of that game.

Overall, dont expect anything super groundbreaking, but Im 9 hours in and enjoying myself. Even if I do wish the combat was a little better.\t\t\t\t\t

The same can't be said about the single player and the performance.

Every level's design is the same:
- Big Boxes with grey developer texture
- High Quality Models sprinkled in
- Enemy's are placed in the world carelessly and do nothing until you hit them
- No Narrative, serious you really have no clue what's going on or what to do to progress (it's fine at the beginning where you learn the mechanic/enviroment but if you progress 4 hours and still feel like this then the game lost me)

Can't believe people actually rate this overhyped tech demo higher than Half-Life:Alyx.
What a shame.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely in my top 5 VR games. The only point that I dislike is that due to the physics my arms/hands are sometimes caught on objects when things get cluttered around me. This causes some disorientation when my physical hand location doesn't match my VR hand location. Aside from that I've been having a good time and I can tell I'll be replaying this at least once more which I almost never do for single player games since I've missed/skipped some optional puzzles and have bypassed some hallways/rooms I saw. Also the radio track is a banger.

Completion update: still lots to do after the story. I'm 14 hours in and I am still 1000% terrified of the crabs, my headset has taken 3-4 spooky uppercuts when I've reflexively tried to block their attacks. A really great game that you can tell the developers cared about. My only complaint now that I've completed the game is that the story seems unfinished and not by a small amount. I won't spoil it but I feel like I was halfway through a story and then it's over.\t\t\t\t\t
