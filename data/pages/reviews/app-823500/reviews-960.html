\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA fantastic, detailed, and highly enjoyable game. Reviewed in Oculus Rift.

This game is not for VR novices. For those that have many hours in VR and know how they respond to VR motion sickness, this IS the game for you.

A detailed, realistic, and exquisitely tested physics engine is the foundation of this game. Throughout the missions, you are tasked with discovering in your own way how the physics of the game work, similar to a HL2 model. There is no describing the joy and exhilaration of discovering a secret, or mastering a puzzle, or just thinking to yourself "how do I get up there"?

The guns are fun and well implemented. They are less "arcade" like than Pavlov, mostly because the guns are physical objects that have to interact with the world. Extremely rewarding to see the game rules play out, such as when you run out of ammo with a melee enemy inches from you, so you just slam him in the face with your empty gun!

The Arena and Sandbox modes add replay value, and the missions themselves have so many thousands of secrets, easter eggs, and fun little setpieces that will certainly have those interested in it for hours at a time.

Easy to recommend for anyone with experience in VR. This is not a "showcase" to show off VR to those who have never played.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJanky as hell but also a lot of fun!

Everything is physics in this game which causes a mess some times, but it's ok because I had some laughs.

Guns are great, probably best guns I've experienced in VR, melee is ok but doesn't feel as good though.

The enemy design is completely inspired by the Half Life series, which is a plus for me, but they don't seem very interesting apart from that, they are all electronics or holograms or kind of synthetics, which don't feel great when you compare to other games like Walking Dead Saints and Sinners or Half Life Alyx.

The game is set in a VR world (VR inside VR) which I find a bit cheesy and the story is pratically non existent and the little there is is silly.

Level design is ok but not great, there's some exploration also a bit of backtracking some times. Very simple levels usually without many different assets. This is where I felt the game was developed by an indie studio the most. Didn't bother me much though.

Puzzles are fun at times but may get a bit frustrating because of the physics simulation that gets in the way. They are physics puzzles which seems ironic, but since the outcome is not 100% predictable some times what you expect won't happen and you need to repeat steps until you get the puzzle right.

This game is great as a VR sandbox, that's where it excels and where the most fun is.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Updated April 4th 2020*

Boneworks has aged like a fine wine in VR. The hype was very high and the wait was long, but this game delivered. It's full physics are second to none in VR at the time of writing. The campaign is steeped in environmental storytelling and a lore that's pretty well hidden, leaving the storyline somewhat obscure to the casual player.

This is more than made up for by the addictive gameplay. I completed the game before I'd even really started to get good at some of the mechanics. Climbing and traversal can be tricky and sometimes feel a bit clunky, but the functionality is definitely there and reliable. The game is packed full of secrets. You won't even realise how many on your first play through. And you will play it again. I never usually play a linear game more than once, but Boneworks has the cutting edge of VR physics at the moment. It draws you back in. You'll finish a level and wonder if you could have done it differently, and the more you play, the more adept you become at the sometimes tricky gameplay.

Gunplay is great, melee combat is great. Nothing in gaming feels quite like charging an enemy, pushing them over with your own hands and then beating them with a brick. The possibilities are completely vast. A true VR playground.

The addition of a sandbox and arena mode not only adds some endgame content and something to showcase to friends, but it also adds depth to the campaign as things need to be Archived in the campaign to be used in Sandbox.

Exploration, mystery and physicality are the name of this game. And it is great.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game, but sometimes the physics are downright bad! I had a few puzzles become unsolvable on me because of physics objects clipping into each other, which broke a couple puzzles for me during my first play through. The only way to fix this issue is to back out to main menu and reload to reset the state of the environment. This is incredibly immersion breaking and a hassle in VR. In spite of these flaws, I still enjoyed myself.

Pros:
  • Incredible physics when they work
  • All loose objects can moved
  • Fully rendered Player Body
  • Good weapon variety
  • Really solid and fun gunplay
  • Anything can be a weapon
  • Player physically jumps and no teleport BS
  • Dual wielding any weapons you wish
  • Head Cra... I mean "Crablets" are really fun enemies
  • I can live just about any Matrix FPS fantasies and feel like I'm "The One"

Cons:
  • Inventory system feels hit or miss when holstering weapons/items (Lost a gun a time or two)
  • VR arms like to clip and get stuck in walls/objects
  • Many objects can clip into other objects causing important puzzle pieces or entire segments to break
  • Climbing onto surfaces can feel downright jank sometimes and doesn't seem to reliably work every time
  • Awful controls for first gen HTC Vive (Please get Valve Index Stock!)
  • Some puzzles just felt like busy work and slowed the pace of the game down too much
  • Enemy variety was a bit lacking (The "Omniprojector" was the only gun shooter enemy type and I hated their design)
  • Story was "meh," but I didn't play for story

I highly recommend getting this game if you've been dying to have an exceptional VR experience. I really hope to see more stuff like this in the future with more polish. This is pretty close to a Triple A experience, but not quite there yet. If you're a supporter of VR, this is one game to get behind as an early adopter.\t\t\t\t\t
