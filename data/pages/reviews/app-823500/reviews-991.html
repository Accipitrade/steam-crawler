\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is probably the first "Proper" VR game I've played and it is by far the best.

The graphics are hit and miss with some spectacular effects and some embarrassingly poor textures. The story is nothing special either but the quality of gameplay is where Boneworks really shines.

I had fears that VR would never evolve out of short-range teleporting but Boneworks has implemented a fairly traditional control scheme for movement, giving WASD style movement with one stick/pad and angled turning with another. free turning can be enable but this is just as vomit inducing as ever so I don't recommend it.

Most things in the world have solid boundaries so you get a very strong sense of boundaries and spacial awareness even without any kind of haptic feedback. Your entire body collides with most everything and if you aren't supposed to phase through something you (most of the time) simply don't. This affords some really interested physics puzzles as well as climbing, shimming, and more.

Gunplay and melee combat are both excellent with a very wide selection of handguns, rifles, blades, and tools to hunt for and experiment with, enemies react realistically to damage and while hammers and crowbars are a bit cumbersome, a good gunfight is endlessly satisfying.

I bought this to tide me over until Half-Life: Alyx releases later this month. If it is anywhere near the quality of Boneworks I will be very satisfied.\t\t\t\t\t
