\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh sweet, motion-sickened jebus. It took quite a bit of practice to get over the nausea waves with this game, but it was worth it. This game is definitely one I'd recommend to anyone looking for a VR title to pick up.

The combat is really fun, and interacting with the environment is awesome, if a bit wonky at times. Having physical interaction between your VR body and the world is great, but it also can lead to jank when I collide with something in an unusual way. Also, having no sensor for my feet leads to some awkward platforming. One note for those prone to motion sickness, the "Final Boss" in particular made me super sick, not to mention the part after (WHY, just WHY with all the climbing).

The game's world and visuals are stunning, I'm positive there's tons of stuff I missed simply because the levels are so detailed and massive. The story was kind of a flop, none of it made sense and apparently it connects to some other games by this developer, but at least one's a horror title so NOPE, to be honest there's just nothing enticing enough to keep me interested.

Good game, hope these guys make more stuff!\t\t\t\t\t
