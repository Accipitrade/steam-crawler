\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile the game is innovative and interesting, the lack of a real save function absolutely kills any and all interest you should have in playing this game.

The developers are out of touch with what you will be experiencing. You will be glitched on objects and die , you will fall to your death and die, you will wonk out due to the physics, or enemies, or something you've done and die. That would be absolutely fine. People play this game for the wonky physics engine.

But you can't save your game, and every death used to reset you to the beginning of the level which was in some cases 40-50 minutes of progress lost. Now the developers decided they were not clever enough to address this complaint in the same way every other game developer in the world seems to be able to. Instead adding an additional "Save" system they dug out of the dumpster of 1998 gaming manuals. This save system allows you to save at specific "checkpoints" which are still 10-15 minutes apart for most players. Failing entirely to fix the fact that YOU CANNOT SAVE YOUR GAME.


I don't think they understood the problem in the first place.\t\t\t\t\t
