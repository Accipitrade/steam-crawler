\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a pure treat. I don't think I'll get as much time off it as games like Gorn and Blade & Sorcery, but what I am getting is incredible.

Campaign is about 7-9 hours long, arena is endlessly re-playable, and sandbox gives you a reason to go back through levels to look for secrets to unlock items to use. Light on story, heavy on incredibly solid game play.

The physics are insane. This game has some of the most consistent physics that make sense and are believable. I keep finding new ways to deal with problems upon replaying levels.

Solid VR legs recommended. If you aren't extremely comfortable in VR, this game might be difficult to play. Some will complain about the climbing mechanic, it can feel a bit wobbly and janky, it's a bit odd to get used to. I personally didn't have too much of an issue. This game works best when you pantomime a bit and really believe you are the character.

Lastly, the soundtrack is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ amazing. I really, really love some of the music you get when solving puzzles in this game.

Great job on this game, Stress Level Zero. I really, really love the heavy Half-Life and Valve influence here. This game has ruined a lot of other VR games for me, just because it's so compelling. The feeling of presence is really exceptional to me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been on the hypetrain for this game ever since node first showcased it on their Youtube channel( 3 years ago ). Aspiring to be a future Game Dev. for myself, I was intrigued at the idea of everything being "physics based". After playing for about 40 hours so far, I can assure you that this game is great. If you are a VR enthusiast looking for a good GAME, not "experience" but a GAME with story, puzzles, a variety of enemies, and an unbelievable interactive environment, look no further. If you are someone new to VR, you may find this game a little annoying at times, but stick with it! Since this is a physics game as you explore the world you will learn the weight of each object and the best way to carry it, the best way to attack certain enemy types( distance is your best friend ), how to climb( very important for secrets ), and perhaps most importantly, how to efficiently move around your environment.
The first couple of puzzles you come across are not easy, and it can be frustrating when your character walks off of the edge and you have to restart or if the object you are trying to jump off of falls over but if you stick with this game for at least one thorough play through, you will learn that, since everything is physics based, it is predictable.
I have explored every level in the story heavily and have unlocked the gravity gun( 20,000 ammo to buy at the end of the game ), and after knowing how heavy everything is it is so satisfying to fling around everything as if it is weightless. This game relies on physics the entire time and once you unlock the gravity gun you get this god-like feeling, even though all it is doing is removing one of the aspects of objects that makes this game interesting( weight ).
I cannot wait to see where Brandon and Stress-Level-Zero takes VR next, this game is not perfect but that is the beauty of it because there are many aspects that they can improve and expand upon in future installments. This game seems to me like an amazing template that they can continue to use for another game. They could certainly go for a horror game, or a shooter, or a extremely narrative puzzle based game of some sort. No matter where they go I am absolutely thrilled with this game, fully believe it is worth the price, am looking forward to the small tweaks and updates they are planning, and can't wait to see the future of VR thanks to the doors that Boneworks opens. As the man said himself "We got ya Triple-A's". -Brandon Laatsch

P.S. This game is epic, the music is epic, and slow mo is epic.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt suffers from the same things most physics based VR games suffer from: Limbs getting caught and twisted in odd situations and places. It simulates where your body is, which gets caught in odd places at times.
Physics is also somewhat 'bouncy'.

But don't let that hurt your opinion of the game. It's fabulous fun. I wish the story was more fleshed out and was longer. I also wish there were more puzzles. I envisioned some Rube Goldberg type puzzles, but most were quite a bit simpler than that.

All in all, it's worth it. Not perfect, but easily worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
