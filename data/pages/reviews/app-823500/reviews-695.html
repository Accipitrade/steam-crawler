\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEasily my most favourite VR sandbox shooter (or VR title period). The campaign provides some decent puzzles, plenty shooting galleries and a ton of collectables to give you a reason to head back on through. I've heard a lot of reviewers say the game is glitchy and it can take you out of the experience, but I found that it holds up really well and never really had a frustrating moment with it. Movement is very fluid after a few updates from vaulting, jumping sprinting, climbing feel effortless with the legs being able to auto retract mid air.

Sandbox and arenas are what have been getting me to constantly come back, it is sheer stress relief, taking out evil ford and the hologram dudes with a knife like a commando is satisfying. To add the guns feel real good to handle, the recoil is enough to force you to control it with decent ability but not too difficult to suck the fun from using them (overall much easier than other gun focused VR games).

10/10 murder simulator, would recommend for anyone needing "a bit more" from their VR experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tForced myself to play most of campaign over several play sessions (had to stop within an hour each time as motion sickness accumulates) to see if it lives up to the hype. For my money, it did not. I appreciate these guys reaching and trying to innovate in the space, but I would have loved to see less game and more ideas.

The campaign is pretty long compared to a lot of other VR games but the content is a pretty forgettable showcase of sickness inducing interactions and Half Life 2 era physics puzzles but in VR. I was hoping to see some innovative use of their genuinely advanced VR interactions, but there just isn't much there worth talking about.

There's a handful of interesting weapons to play with that benefit from the simulated bodies, but if beating up ragdolls doesn't sound endlessly interesting to you I would go in with low expectations and a barf bag ready.\t\t\t\t\t
