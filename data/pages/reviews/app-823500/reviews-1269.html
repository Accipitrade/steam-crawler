\r\n\t\t\t\t\t****Edit for Physics Update*****
Physics work a lot better now. It's enough to get me playing it for another several hours session. However, as time progresses the things about this game that piss me off kept pissing me off. Unless you run straight through the levels, ignore most boxes, don't explore, etc you're quickly going to get bored and irritated. I don't know why, maybe it's because there's too much to break open with usually only ammo/currency rewards and yet you're barely running into interesting stuff that would warrant so much gathering. The most intelligent things I've come across so far are the headcrab like bots, honestly I mean that. The other AI are so slow and boring and predictable that I can pop a head shot off no problem.

Back to physics. As cool as they are, the clunkiness of it still gets in the way of itself more often than being cool. Having to awkwardly hold stuff away from your body simply because your own body might get stuck in the crate, wall, etc or worse knock all the ♥♥♥♥ out of the milk crate you just painstakingly stacked so you could take all the items you found to the next save point ( which I think you should be able to do that thing where you keep the items there too )

Always trying to figure out what way a thing wants to be grabbed. Is it trigger? is it grips? IDK seems to respond to both and neither work fluidly, but they are both different -_-.

Basically though, this game feels like the scene from ready player one (book) where the main character is trying to escape the "prison" he's put in. That in itself was super novel and interesting when I picked up this game and I still get flashes of that, but even after all this time and updates I can't give this game a thumbs up. There are a lot of design choices I can't agree with and bugs ( which may very well be core engine problems ) that probably won't be solved ever. Game almost feels like it's ready for release, but still not quite from me. Sorry SLO, I appreciate all the efforts you've done so far though. Keep it up.

****Original Review****
I'm not sure this game was ready for release. The physics are the main selling point and they are just complete jank from my first play through. From the videos I've been watching of the development process I can take a guess that something went wrong last second because this just doesn't seem right. I'm going to wait for an update to see if this gets fixed, but from first impression I can say that I'm not impressed.

Theres a lot of aha moments where you understand some jokes and or nods to valve titles, but that's really just not enough for me as that just means SL0 is riding on someone else's coat tails. They don't really hit the bar of being something like a valve game, like they seem to be trying to do, and I find it more like pandering than mutual fandom.

There's no denying though, BoneWorks is a step in the right direction for VR. It makes me both more excited and uneasy about the release of half life: alyx. I hope Stress Level 0 continues to innovate and push boundaries, but for now this is getting a thumbs down until the physics get fixed.\t\t\t\t\t
