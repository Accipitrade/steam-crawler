\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty cool game! It has its moments, both positive and negative, and they're all pretty memorable, that's for sure. Wandering around a cramped, dark sewer with nothing but a flashlight for a while, and letting your guard drop for just a second to be ambushed by a bunch of green nullbody dudes slappin' your ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ cow. Staring into a room full of lights and floating grey balls, and oggling at how your CPU is actually melting down and will soon be irradiating the Pripyat river.

This game is not perfect. There are some points where the optimisation seems to... not be there? Framerates simply drop from the sky and even with a good VR stomach it can get quite uncomfortable. On the Quest using Oculus Link, something about the tracking or refresh rate makes melee combat really, really difficult, and of course the inside-out tracking means holstering/unholstering over your shoulder is... problematic.

Though I know all that can be solved with an i9-9900k and an Index, so who am I kidding here. The upsides far outweigh the downsides.

This game is scary. It might not look it in Lets Plays, but there's plenty of times I found my clips empty, my shoulder missing my handy crowbar and eight 4-legged VR-headsets leaping at my face. The immersion you get from being a physical entity in this world only enhances that feeling. Enemies, whilst not the smartest things in the world of AI, are still challenging enough that a gunfight or a horde of nullmen gets the adrenaline pumping.

The physics puzzles and interactions with the world are fantastic, when they work properly. The physical world feels complete and fully interactive, which of course it is. I remember finding a way to skip half a level just trying to climb something.

The only issues I have coming out of this, is sometimes poor optimisation, and for HMDs other than the index and vive (pro), there will be other issues you have to deal with. Oh, and also, people are right about the story, it's nothing ground-breaking.

Tl;dr, buy the game, just do it, it's £23 and it's a really freaking good game. Just make sure your CPU is up to the task, and please make sure you've played some other games to get your VR legs first. The trip down the sewers WILL make you fall over and vomit otherwise.\t\t\t\t\t
