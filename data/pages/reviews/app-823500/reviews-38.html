\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I start, I have nothing but adoration for Brandon, Stress Level Zero and everyone behind this game. I love their content and I would've loved to give them a glowing review. But someone's gotta call out the elephant in the room.

They shouldn't have bothered with a campaign, because it's awful. I saw the smooth IK/VR physics and working ironsights in the trailer and thought, "woah, the campaign they're teasing must be pretty good!" and promptly slugged through HOURS and HOURS of the worst level design imaginable. Did you enjoy the story and flow of Half Life games? Take that out. Boneworks is a loose strand of random events where you never know WHY you're in any given place. Do you like meeting fun characters with unique personalities? Can't have that either; the faceless people you meet will randomly show up, talk gibberish at you for ten seconds and then vanish for the rest of the game. Now I know what it's like to have dementia.

AND THE LEVEL DESIGN. You'll find out real soon that the level designer liked headcrabs a whoooole lot. So much that they designed a much creepier version, no holds barred, and hid them behind every shelf and box in the later stages of the campaign. Wouldn't it be FUN GAMEPLAY to be sent into shock every five seconds? Wasn't that the funnest part of Half Life? The same goes for other enemies- it became predictably annoying to preaim behind every box and WOOPS!- found an enemy oh-so-cleverly hidden, waiting to ambush me. Again.

So what DOES the game do well? It has great music and, if you ignore the campaign, it looks like it would be a great sandbox. The physics and guns would certainly support that. But I'm exhausted and not willing to slog through another round to find out. I'm sorry. Please learn how to design levels.\t\t\t\t\t
