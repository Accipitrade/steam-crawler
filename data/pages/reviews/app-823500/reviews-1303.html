\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo Spoilers in this review
I play this on this on the Valve Index, so your experience might differ from mine due to control differences.

Short version:
This game really lives up to the hype, it is extraordinarily fun, and I highly recommend this to anyone who has had no problem with high intensity VR games in the past.

Long Version:
When I loaded up this game for the first time, it took a bit of adjusting to get used to. I won't deny the fact that is game is much more jarring at first than any other VR game I have played before. The presence of a body and arms as well as the much faster movement gave me a touch of motion sickness and a headache for the first hour or so. I took small breaks, and I adjusted to the game pretty quickly and the experience was amazing afterwards, any motion sickness and headache is no longer present while playing. This is honestly the first VR game that I would say feels like a full game, not just a tech demo. The gun play feels amazing, very smooth, with only a touch of jank that I think can be attributed to the nature of VR games. Now, it is not perfect, I ran into a couple of glitches while playing that forced me to replay a mission from the start, as they didn't allow me to proceed, however the game just released so I'm sure those will be ironed out as time goes on. If there is anything that I would complain about, it would be the lack of a true "save" system. For example, if you quit playing while in the middle or near the end of one of the missions, you have to replay the whole mission from the start, which can be frustrating, as some of the missions can be quite long, as there is a fair bit of exploration that you can do. Other than that, this truly feels like the "next-gen" of VR games, and if you are looking to buy Half Life: Alyx in March, this would be a great title to get you ready for the first AAA VR game. And if you are not planning on getting Half Life, still get this game, because it is amazing, and worth more than $30 in my honest opinion.\t\t\t\t\t
