\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Story sucks. Controls like that won't wipe. Updates break saves. Devs don't support.

Boneworks doesn't auto-save, it uses save points like a ' PS2 game, but worse. The dev's couldn't get Items to save properly, so you have to take each item out of your inventory, one-by--one, and put it onto a little platform at the save point. Then grab 2 handles, wait for a circle to fill up, and done. Then you can exit the game or load it all back up and keep playing until you upchuck on the cat.

At first launch, Boneworks warns you that it's intended for "Experienced VR users," and that it "Uses highly experimental features". You start in a museum filled with tutorial videos and weird displays. Most of the maps are untextured.
The game has a lot of interesting ideas (mostly in the form of references to better games and movies), and technical feats. Towards the end of the tutorial, as you walk closer towards a grassy meadow it becomes clearer that the meadow is in-fact a wall that's made of LEDs, you smash through the wall to leave the area.
This gave me major Truman Show vibes, and kinda reminded me of the ending of Portal. But Boneworks doesn't really explore any of those ideas, and frankly doesn't deserve anywhere near their level of praise, because there's no depth to it beyond its gimmicks.

The story sucks. At the end you kill a few of the same NPC you've already fought 1000 times, now with more health. Do a couple more physics puzzles. Then climb up a Metal Gear Solid ladder (that's a long one boy), press a button, watch another poorly acted cutscene, and that's it. ' Garbage. Like blue-balls for your brain.

The physics look cool in trailers, but playing with them is literally sickening.
You control a full-body, rather than just hands and a head. The body also has it's own simulated physics, and Inverse-Kinematics for every joint, and bone.
The in-game arm has it's own weight, resistance, and joints that don't match yours in the real-world.
It feels laggy, unresponsive, and becomes exponentially worse if you carry anything. Melee weapons all feel like slinging around a sack of potatoes. The heavier it is, the worse it feels to control. Guns aren't better, your hand will snap to places on the gun when you grip it, causing riffles to wobble in your arms like a dying baby.\t\t\t\t\t
