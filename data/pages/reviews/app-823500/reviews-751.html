\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredible, one of the very few games I've played where I truly felt that it was worth every single penny of its price. It's done (in my opinion) for VR what Half Life 2 did for PC gaming: It revolutionized physics in the virtual space, and showed people its possibilities. While Half Life Alyx is a close second, BONEWORKS pulls ahead both because of its incredible OST, and because of the freedom and variety it offers in combat and gameplay.

However, I want to make one thing very clear. This game isn't for everyone. When they say experienced VR users only, they mean it. The game has no comfort options, only smooth locomotion. So if you've never played VR before and still want to get this game, I'd recommend playing seated for the first couple hours until your mind and body get used to it. There are also a few quirks of this game that can make or break the experience for someone.

First, there's the nature of its sandbox mode. To unlock items for use in the sandbox, you must first find the item either in a little gatchapon-style ball or just laying around, and then "reclaim" it at the end of the level. Personally, I love this sort of thing. It adds a ton of replayability to the game, and you get to go back and find all of the secrets you may have missed before. However, to someone else, this could seem like a boring chore and a waste of time. Basically, what I'm trying to say is, if you enjoy games like Super Mario Odyssey, Banjo-Kazooie, or you just really enjoy hunting for secrets and collectibles, you'll really appreciate this game's sandbox mode.

On a related note, there's the Dev weapons. At the end of the game, there's an opportunity to purchase powerful (and fun!) physics weapons, such as a noclip gun, an item spawner, and a Gravity Gun-style object manipulator. However, they're very expensive, and your ammo is your currency. So if you want to buy these weapons, you will need to do a melee-only run for each stage, searching high and low for ammo boxes. Luckily, the melee physics have been improved drastically since launch, so this is much less of a chore than it used to be.

Because of all of this, if you want to really enjoy yourself during your initial playthrough, I strongly recommend just ignoring all of the ammo requirements and collectibles and just playing how you want to. Enjoy it.

Third, there's the story. It's not told through long winded exposition or narration. Instead, the way the story is told in this game is akin to games like (as much as I hate to make this comparison) FNAF, where much of it is told through clipboards and context clues in the environment. Unlike FNAF however, you can actually make sense of it (YouTube channels like SuperHorrorBro and GreasedScotsman are immensely helpful with that). Another really cool aspect of this game is that it lives within a connected universe of all previous SLZ games (and those to come), building upon its lore with each new game.

TL;DR:

10/10: The replayability in this game is absolutely insane: over 100 hours and I'm still not bored with it. The freedom of movement and quality of gameplay of BONEWORKS simply cannot be overstated, with an incredible OST that adds to the experience. Though it isn't for everyone, this game is a must-have for experienced VR users with PCVR.\t\t\t\t\t
