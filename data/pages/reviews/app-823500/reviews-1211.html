\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite this game review being put on positive there's still a lot of work to be done. This is an amazing achievement for Stress Level Zero, since it's the first actual serious full game with a physics enige that not even valve could make. The fact that Half Life: Alyx is gonna be using a similiar physics engine tells loads about the game. WARNING SPOILERS!

Pros:
Realistic Physics ever seen in any vr game. Everything you grab has weight and feels like you're carrying it in real life. The gravity has some actual acceleration and everything you touch that isn't nailed to anything can will move.
Realistic shooting mechanics and weapon model. Every gun in this game is mapped out almost perfectly and you can do a lot of cool things. (Try slapping the HK base SMG's bolt when reloading).
Cool story and amazing level design. This is Portal levels of level design with a lot of cool ideas that seem almost ripped from the game itself. The story is really fun, though it takes a bit for you to understand your objective.
(Mostly) Cool NPCs. The Nullbodies, Headsets are really cool, and the variations of them are amazing. Though some of the hostile NPCs later on feel like reskins with minimal changes.
Cons:
No save function. This is really irritating as many of the levels are giant. If you turn off the game you will be sent to the start, not the latest checkpoint, the start.
Not enough content. There are a lot of cool physics weapons and hostile NPCs that are rarely if ever used. Examples are the big Headsets and the balloon gun. It makes the game feel rushed.
Lack of level content. I get that it's supposed to be a VR world that is under development, but there are a lot of levels where unless you know explicitly where to go or read a walkthrough. You don't know what to do. The tower is an example of this. I spent about 30 mins walking from door to door not knowing what to do, after being hand led through the last level by the graffiti. When I finally found out where the key was. I couldn't stop thinking about how many of the levels could be better with a few tweaks.
Bugs and glitches. Oh lord there are a few. Playing with some melee weapons they can clip through objects and be impossible to use again. Objects will fly away due to you holding your hands closer than in game, and I had to reset levels due to some stupid glitch deleting objects meant to be used later on.
Lack of endgame content. The game's story is amazing all by itself and it's fun to replay. But the sandbox mode is next to none with some of the functions not even working. The arena mode has very little in terms of content when you've done everything and the ally system is rushed (you can't even give them weapons). It would be fun to have some sort of new game+ function with harder or more enemies or less health to add more tension. And maybe some community made level editor where you can make your own levels that can be put on the workshop.

TL;DR The game is a buy. The price is reasonable and you get a lot of bang for the buck, but it still has a lot of potential.\t\t\t\t\t
