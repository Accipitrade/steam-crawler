\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my first negative review for a game and before you scroll away from my measly 2 hour playtime, I promise there is a good reason. I'm usually a quite positive guy, preferring to be optimistic when playing and reviewing a game. Development takes time and a serious amount of effort that I myself would struggle to put into anything of this scale. I wanted to like Boneworks, the story seemed intriguing and I'd seen the demos done by the team over at Stress Level Zero and they looked incredible, the physics being one of the first things that truly impressed me upon starting the game. I spent my first hour just messing around in the museum right at the very beginning!

However, it couldn't last. I've tried a lot of VR experiences, I love the immersion of literally stepping into another world and I've never had that horrible feeling of motion sickness. Except in Boneworks. I'm not gonna pretend to understand the technical side of VR game design, I don't fully know what causes such a feeling, but my library is full of other VR games that do not cause this. An example would be Blade & Sorcery (cause I feel like comparing games to the absolute gem of HL:Alyx is impossible), a game I can happily jump, run and fall in without having even an inkling of motion sickness. I've tried sitting during a playthrough, I even lay down for one, but no such luck. That sick feeling continues to persist.

I felt the need to raise this issue somewhere other than in my own head. So on that note, I cannot recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of the 'Must have' games for anyone with a VR headset. The game itself can be a little clunky/buggy at times but it comes with the territory of such a innovating genre. The story is very meta but it mostly takes a back seat to the game play. There is a lot of replay value in exploring the levels and seeing the different ways you can beat it. Can you use just a melee weapon and try sneaking around? Or should you go with precision with duel pistols? Or just go gun blazing with rifles and hope you meet your mark? All of these are viable options and can be fun experimenting with. Some of the more intense parkour obstacles in game can be more tedious and frustrating to overcome than fun. However they are mostly reserved to more dedicated players as side routes for some hidden weapon or props. The game also has a very immersive world with lots of little Easter eggs hidden all around you. Even when you beat the main story there are sand box worlds you can play that are just as fun if not more so because of the things you can explore in them.
Despite its flaws it is one of my favorite VR games of all time, and I believe it should be a shining example of what VR games should be.\t\t\t\t\t
