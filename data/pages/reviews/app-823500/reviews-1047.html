\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had been eyeballing the vr-gaming landscape for years, to me it always seemed like an interresting, yet overpriced experience with little to offer in terms of actual content.
Boneworks was the first game to REALLY peak my interest, the combat mechanics looked more advanced and smooth than anything i had seen from vr before, and i remember thinking to myself "when the H#!! did vr get this advanced?" So i got pretty hyped, decided to say f*:k it, and finally purchased a vr headset.


My expectations were high, but i am happy to say that this met almost all of them, and totally exceeded a few. This game runs smoothly on my mid-tier gaming setup (Rift S, 1660 TI, I7, 16gb ram etc), some have reported that it feels janky on the rift s, but i have had no such issues.

Pros:

-Combat is smooth and responsive
-God-tier interractability, pretty much all items and entities can be interracted with and manipulated.
-Tons of cool weapons with plenty of variety
-Interresting physics-based puzzles (reminecent of hl2 and portal)
-Works fine on lower end vr hardware, you dont need a valve index to enjoy this!
-Convinced that vr has the potential to be more than just another doomed gaming fad.
-FRIGGIN´ force powers, make colllecting iems and ammo a whole lot less tedious, and make combat awesome.

Cons:

-Lack of enemy variety
-Climbing is tedious and can be buggy
-No mid-level saves
-Can make some people motion sick

If you own a vr headset and have not played this, you are missing out!
This was the first pcvr game i ever played, in retrospect i probably should have stared with something less intense. I recommend that vr newbies take it slow and get used to the movement system before going all-in.

TLDR: It´s pretty good, i killed a guy with a flashligt.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good example of cool on paper, bad in implementation. While the near-full body physical interaction is a neat idea, basing a whole game around it just falls flat. I've got a pretty strong stomach and seasoned vr legs, but one level in this game just makes me ill. If this game was interesting, I'd be willing to power through it, but it's really not. Uninteresting level and puzzle design, bland combat, dated visuals. Nothing in this game really sticks out to me as needing to be in a vr game. The physical body concept isn't used in any interesting ways, and very little would change if the game had a simple grab system like most others. If this wan't a vr game, I could easily see this game getting middling to negative reviews for just being interesting. Alot of the mechanics have been done better in other games, and don't make me want to through up using them. H3VR, The Climb, Budget Cuts, to name a few. This game should have been a short sandbox tech demo, not an elongated story mode.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an exciting innovative VR experience. But holy hell, it is so hard to play without any way to save between levels. I'm pretty tolerant of VR, but especially when using smooth motion I really only like to play about 30 minutes at a time before having a break. But in boneworks you can't even pause the game, let alone save if you want to put the headset down for a bit. To get through the first level it took me. about 4 tries. Each time I had to speed through the part I had played and then finally get to the new stuff until I was familiar enough to get all the way through in one go. I really wish I could at least mess around in the sandbox, but that looks to be locked until you get through the story mode. And I just don't have the patience to replay levels over and over. It's disappointing because the gameplay itself has been great so far, but I only get about 10 minutes of it after 20 minutes of having to retrace my steps before I feel like I need a break again. It's just surprising to me that not even a pause function was implemented. Please devs, make adding a real mid-level save or checkpoint system a priority because I'm sure I'm not the only one that can't play until its implemented.\t\t\t\t\t
