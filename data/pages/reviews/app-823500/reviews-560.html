\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really appreciate what the developers went for here. This game is ground breaking in a lot of ways, and it's absolutely an impressive achievement from a technical standpoint. There are some very, very cool VR mechanics in this game such as:

- Good gun handling. Hand positioning is excellent. Manual reloading is great. Guns are physics objects and so you can do all sorts of realistic and interesting things with them.

- Item interactions are the best I've seen in VR. Your hand positions on melee weapons can be placed anywhere just as you could in real life. You can slide your hand up and down crowbars and bill clubs and such to position your hands just where you want them. Absolutely well polished and great interactions with items around the world!

- Door mechanics. Yes, that's right, I find the door mechanics to be impressive. You can open them exactly like you would expect to be able to do. You can even open them with the tip of your gun. It's just how all VR games should handle doors, and yet many fail to do so correctly.

In general all of the VR mechanics in this game are among the best you'll find anywhere. But I have some very, very big complaints:

- I do not like your body being a physics object. I've tried to enjoy this, but it just constantly doesn't feel right. I have 30+ hours of boneworks (I have it on Oculus too) ... and I simply cannot stand my body being a physics object. It constantly gets in the way of things, and makes interactions difficult. Vaulting over / onto things is just super difficult and I think it comes down to the attempt to make that interaction as realistic as possible, but it just doesn't translate into VR well. An assisted vaulting mechanic like other games, is so much more satisfying. In general, I find the body being a physics object to add nothing to the game, and take much away from it. Yet, I recognize and appreciate what they were trying to do here.

Climbing is also impacted negatively by your body being a physics object. Rather than climbing being a fluid motion like any other game, its kind of bouncy and dissatisfying. Does it work, and is it realistic, are besides the point. It doesn't feel good, at all.

- Perhaps my biggest complaint, which overshadows everything is the world / story. It is just uninspiring and unimaginative. The game feels like almost all the effort was spent on polishing the mechanics for VR (which they did) ... and the world + story was sort of an after thought. Everything kinda looks the same, drab, boring style. Enemies are boring and uninteresting, and ultimately it's a hallway shooter. There's no real exploration here... it's linear and guided for the most part.

- The puzzles are cool, but lack purpose. Rather than being part of some interesting world, they feel randomly inserted into the level progression. They're just too canned. I don't feel interested in solving them because there's no story behind them. It's just another physics puzzle in my way of shooting enemies and using the guns (which is the best part of the game).

All in all I think what they have here is an excellent platform. I would love to see them re-use many of the core mechanics for a more serious game in an open world. My hope is that Boneworks was just the start but that we will see something in the future which is both technically amazing and beautiful, and interesting.

So at the end of the day I find it very hard to recommend the game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI just want to say, this game is underappreciated for what it is. I really think this is by far the best sandbox based vr game (and the story is great just not a lot of progression from level to level) but I feel like the first tutorial level really throws people off because it isn't instant kill everything you see. The story is very replayable because you can find different ways to get to the end of the level each time. Most of the time if you would think "That would be cool if..." you can actually do what you were thinking. The only real problems with the base game are the insane amount of grinding you need to accomplish in order to purchase the dev tools. Most of them you can get just from playing the game but they still required hours of playing central station over and over again to get just one. After I got the utility gun i just adjusted my files to have 100,000 ammunition. It took a lot of searching to find all the collectibles but I used a guide to find the last 6 or 7 items. I recommend if you really get tired of looking just downloading some file.

My favorite part of the game by far is the mods you can find online. Bonetome has most of the mods from an "improved spear" with a scope on it, to custom campaigns with new enemies and hours of play time. Overall, just try the game and don't quit after the first 30 minutes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't really say that this game is bad, because it isn't. But I just can't give a positive rating because there are so many quirks and problems that make me unable to recommend this game to anyone. The gameplay and physics are great and a lot of fun to play around with. The levels is where the game falls apart though, sometimes I just wandered around not knowing what to do which kills the flow of the game when you just wanna shoot some bad guys. The tutorial at the beginning is way to long. I know how video games work and I don't think that anybody would play a VR game as their first game. The tutorial could be way shorter. The UI and the Menu at the beginning are also really confusing, sometimes I didn't even know how I could play other gamemodes and had to google how to do that. You also have to unlock all the other features like sandbox and arena mode by carrying boxes to drop points in story mode. If you bought the game just to play arena mode or sandbox, you still will have to play the story mode, which as I said before, is my biggest gripe with the game.

If you can put up with all these problems, then go for it. Otherwise I can't recommend the game.\t\t\t\t\t
