\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter beating roughly 13 missions I believe I can certainly say that this game is good. Good, but absolutely not great.

This review is based off gameplay using the Oculus Rift S.

The game itself isn't bad at all. I had a good time, however, it took a lot, and I mean a LOT of getting over all the gripes I had with the game.

Pros:

Fun, intuitive puzzles that make you think about manipulating and using the environment to your advantage.

Decent gunplay, but not amazing. Definitely not the best compared to games like Pavlov and H3VR but that's a given since those games are 99% about guns, so I won't knock it for that.

Amazing physics. Again, a given. The environment is *mostly* easily manipulative, with some exceptions. Will discuss those later.

Cons (get ready for this one):

- ZERO SAVE FEATURE. You need to complete the entire level to save your progress. This is the biggest gripe I have with the game. I personally do not get motion sickness that easily but when I do I have to suck it up and finish the level because if I do not I have to redo it. Absolutely unacceptable in my opinion.

- NPCs and characters are just bland. I think the most interesting enemy is the headset. The headcrab-esque enemy that attempts to latch onto your head and kill you. But that's about as far as interesting NPCs go. Nullbodies are just orange zombies, and extremely easy to kill, and then there's the enemies that shoot at you. Basically on a ball and roll around. Their tracking is kinda iffy and usually you should have 0 problem taking them down as long as you use cover correctly when there's more than about 2 or 3. 1 on 1s with them are extremely easy and they aren't a threat at all alone. There's also these blue zombie lookin' enemies but they're basically reskinned nullbodies. Very rarely do you find a variant of the blue zombies that fire a very slow projectile at you. You only really have issues with them if you're in a narrow corridor or passageway, which is about 5% of the game. Other than that they're also super easy.

- The physics are good, but I personally find them more infuriating than not. Your arms are like paper and can't lift anything "heavy" above your stomach, which can be really infuriating when carrying them up stairs or putting them on a higher surface.

- As I said the gunplay is good, but it's not amazing. Pistols are AMAZING. I had so much fun using pistols. Super easy to use, reload, and get used to. However, ARs and SMGs were a pain in my rear. Not so much the SMGs rather than the ARs. I expected to be able to actually use the stock of a gun to reduce recoil, but no dice. You'd figure as much since your body is a physics object, but no. Felt super janky to use and fire ARs and found myself using the M4 with the laser attachment and strictly using the laser. Irons are just awful in my opinion.

- The inventory system is mediocre at best. I find myself strictly needing to use the menu inventory to put things on my hip because it's nearly impossible to place things on your hip. You'll just throw them on the ground over and over.

- The story is so hard to follow other than "You need to stop MythOS by resetting a clock" I could not find why you need to do this, any backstory at all, etc. The gameplay was fun but I had a hard time getting into the story at all. Along with this, the ONLY friendly NPCs you can really find in the story is two people that occasionally have a couple lines through the game via messages on a TV screen. Other than that, no dice. No friendly NPCs to follow, Nothing. You are alone through the game. There is one time where you see a couple "mimics" where they copy your arm movements and may follow you, but they do not hurt you and are friendly to you, but that's about it. When I say "friendly NPCs", I mean people like Alyx in HL2 and CPU teammates, for example in Gears of War. There is none of that.

- I've found my hands to get stuck in boxes, walls, doors, etc SO many times. I also found myself glitching through a wall in a late mission, and falling through the world. That was extremely nauseating, but obviously wasn't intended so don't think the game is meant to nauseate you.

- I could not for the life of me figure out how to put the night vision helmet on, or if it's even possible. The game gives you them in a mission, and gives you it in the sandbox, but how the hell do you even utilize them?

- You have to essentially do an easter egg to be able to PLAY the sandbox mode, and the sandbox mode isn't even that fun or worth the effort. If you want to unlock sandbox for yourself: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PKPV-mRrUCk <-- Here's a video I found to help with it.

All in all, it's not the *worst* VR game I've played. Far from it. I just feel like this is a giant tech demo with a mediocre story. Which honestly sucks cause I was so excited to play this game. Amazing concept, but fails to follow through on so much.

All in all, I give it a 4/10. It's physics engine is certainly revolutionary, but that's about it has going for it.

Hopefully Stress Level 0 can fix some of these issues because if they do this can be a much better game. There's so much potential with this title and engine.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game delivers on all of its selling points. The gun handling feels great, the physics work well enough, and it has a ton of content (by VR standards). The atmosphere and tone align perfectly with the gameplay. It also seems really well optimized, I have not experienced any frame dropping or lag in three hours of playtime (my specs are about the recommended ones).

It's definitely not without flaws, the big things I noticed in the first three levels:
- Loading takes a while, but it is very infrequent.
- Some of the physics puzzles are vague or tedious. There's plenty of clever and satisfying puzzles though.
- Holding melee weapons is uncomfortable on the Vive Wands. The overall control isn't too bad but given how long a lot of the levels are it's annoying when you're out of storage slots and you're trying to hold something. I haven't tried the game on other controllers but I would imagine it would be better on a Rift or Index controller.
- There's no notification when you reach a save point. You can easily exit a level and lose a lot of progress.
- Some of the areas, especially ones involving falling, cause motion sickness. This may not be a problem for everyone, my tolerance is reasonably high but over the course of the long levels it can become an issue for some. I appreciate their warning on the store page and loading page that the game is for "advanced VR users".

In spite of these issues this is an incredible game that I would highly recommend to anyone who owns a compatible HMD, as well as anyone who is looking to get in to VR.\t\t\t\t\t
