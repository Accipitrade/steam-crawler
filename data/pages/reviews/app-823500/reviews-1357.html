\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLots of cool interactions in a well made engine. A door that was supposed to open didn't and I became motion sick after an hour looking for what I was supposed to do. I quit and took a break and when I came back I found out that there is no check pointing, “continue game” opens a stage select. After replaying the city streets level for the second time I was no longer having fun.

The humanoid AI characters could use some work. I would like to see flinching after hits and more dynamic movement. At this time they just move to your position and hit you, there is plenty of room for improvement here.

If you are a gun nerd you are going to be disappointed by all aspects of the gunplay. I know they put a ton of time into r&d but the execution was not to my particular expectations. The recoil is weird, the guns don’t have all their functions mapped to the controller (like bolt release or magazine eject) and some of the functions are finicky. Maybe the handling makes up for the level of generalization but wouldn't know, There has been no gunplay yet in 2 hours of play. There hasn't been much fighting at all really. At this point, it seems like a physics puzzler with fighting mechanics.

The game opens with a massive tutorial instead of totorializing through gameplay. Other designers find ways to teach brand new concepts without devolving into many minutes of totorialization.

I’m going to play more but I cannot recommend this game because of the aforementioned checkpointing issue. Games should respect your time.

After 9 hours I can say: The game is not fun to play or thoughtfully designed for play. I know people have rudely called this game a tech demo but factually the game part is not good and the tech part is pretty good, so I don’t know what else to call it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\teven after less than an hour of gameplay, its already meeting my expectations, exceeding them in some areas.

for example: sitting mode. never before have i seen this mode so thought out, allowing you to crouch, jump, etc. while sitting. its amazing.

interacting with things feels pretty good, may take a minute to get used to how you 'snap' to things when grabbing, and adjusting your grip on melee weapons can feel/look slightly clunky at times, but its perfectly functional otherwise.

shooting guns, while it works very well especially with 2 handed weapons, can look a bit odd, your hands/weapon twitching pretty violently on each shot, and then snapping back to position. but this is a small visual gripe, the actual aiming/shooting works very well, despite that.

the only other gripe ive had is trying to climb ledges using the sitting mode. when i hold down on the right stick to 'crouch' and bring my feet up, it pushes me off the ledge and it lets go, dropping me. im not sure if im missing something there. this issue has been fixed.

i may edit this once ive had more experience with the game, but if it continues as it has, this will be a must buy for VR.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a prime example of why thumbs up thumbs down is stupid and we need a 5 or even 10 point system to assign games. I have to thumbs down a fairly good game because I can't recommend it in it's current state and that's pretty unfair.

I'm an index user, can't help but admit to being disappointed. The way this physics engine works is kinda cool but also pretty glitchy. I find i'm dragging around bits of the level because of the physical body, I pulled a shelving unit over because I walked to closely too it and my foot caught it. then there was the bug where my left arm folded in on it's self and locked that way and I couldn't move it. it would have been cool if it was like some sort of dart i had been shot with that caused it but it was completely game breaking as a permanent state. I was about 2 minutes into trying to figure out a way to ungimp my arm and the game crashed.

highly annoying.

Now that my complaints are out of the way on to the good stuff. there are moments in this so far that have been truly amazing. there is a LED panel wall that's displaying a 3d image of a grassy field you can push aside and see the "real world behind" it's a very trippy experience, one that takes being in vr to get the effect of at all. The visuals over all aren't amazing but they are pretty.

There is a good sense of humor to this game as well. The parts of the game that are talking about the history of VR are pretty funny. the interactive displays made me laugh out loud.

I'm not far in, but it's worth the price of admission. However I think a bit over hyped and not ready if i'm honest.\t\t\t\t\t

BONEWORKS is finally here!
VR is really here!

I've been dreaming of this game for a long time and it delivers. This is the ultimate VR sandbox full of immersive exploration, puzzling, platforming and shooting. It feels like the first proper VR locomotion and physically simulated real world translation into Virtual Reality. The movement, controls, physics and level of interaction is unprecedented. I can see myself playing for this for a long time and for many hours at a time. This is likely due to the full body physics, arms, legs, hands, it is all there.

My very first session on release day today was over 3 hours! I never felt any level of discomfort nor fatigue and I was standing the whole time moving around. I am playing with the Valve Index which is definitely the recommended HMD to play with. The Index controllers are the most important aspect of input control mechanism that really unlocks the full potential of BONEWORKS. The mystery, intrigue and curiosity of Monogon Industries, MythOS, and the rest of this universe deserves to be explored, investigated and assimilated.

Enjoy this trip into a VR adventure in another VR world.

Highly recommended!\t\t\t\t\t
