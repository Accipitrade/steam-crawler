\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR, Game is bad, not even worth half the price, requires you to UNLOCK gameplay. I pay for games to have full features not waste time doing stupid things like throwing things in a bucket at a certain place just so I can unlock something. Easy refund


This game had more money put into marketing than gameplay. Forces you into a long af unskippable tutorial on how to run jump and throw objects.

Blade and sorcery never did this and surprisingly people know how to swing objects, climb and throw things! WOAH!!! It's as if we're not braindead. Maybe rename the game to brainworks (EHEHEH) since it teaches you some basic functions a 3 year old could figure by themselves if they knew how to work an index controller. Hands don't cling well to things like they would in blade and sorcery (an indie early access game which is cheaper) so climbing is tedious. At some point in the tutorial there was some moving rope and it threw me out of map bounds as soon as I grabbed onto it, forcing me to restart the entire thing.
Feedback is horrible entirely, punching a wall or object has no feel to it at ALL (yeah it does in multiple other games where you can punch) and weapons are nowhere near as good as pavlov, for example. So why would I be forced to emulate your physics of a heavy object if there's no real feel to it? Also, the index controllers have great pressure sensors why haven't you asked valve to teach you how to use them since youre totally so close to them?

I just scrolled down a bit and it's recommending players to have previous experience with VR gameplay but I feel this thing (or "game") wants you to function in an entirely unique way, but making you go through that hugeass tutorial while attempting badly to give it a Portal feel.

I'd recommend this if it were early access or half the price but right now it leans more towards MEH-NOish on the rating scale primarily because it's trying so hard to be above the rest.

Contemplating a refund since it works like ♥♥♥♥ (all text is rendered as an extra object in this game an not a texture making things appear very strange, not sure why they did that) causing graphical errors in new AMD drivers. I'll give it another shot when it's 50%-75% off next year cause I'm not a screeching weirdo that can't wait to play a single player game for what it's really worth.

Then again, many of us have Alyx for free...

"2. Limited License allows the purchaser (you) to use any song, in its entirety, in content creation (example: youtube video, twitch streams, etc.) in conjunction exclusively with Boneworks footage."

They're actually making you pay extra if you want to use the in-game songs when streaming their game. That's the kind of >people< they are.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought the game after initially refunding it and finally gave it a proper try, this is my second review of this.

Initially I had the same issues as I had back then, severe motion sickness. I gave up on the game since I can play games like Jet Island, Blade & Sorcery, Pavlov and Climbey witthout any issue. Smooth turning and comfort settings off.

This time, I was determined to fight through the motion sickness and took more regular breaks than I was used to, my motion sickness ceased after getting about 5 hours into the game.

So, how is the gameplay when you are finally used to the game?
It's pretty good, not gonna lie. The levels are linear, but allow for exploration as secrets are hidden everywhere. The game also allows you to make shortcuts if you know how to abuse the games mechanics. The story is mostly told through exposition and world details, though that is not the focus of the game. This game is a Half-Life 2 inspired tech-demo disguised as a game and it kinda shows with the janky climbing system (major cause of motion sickness), hit or miss inventory system and okay-ish melee combat. The main focus when it comes to combat is the guns and they feel and sound really damn good. If you have to choose between a melee weapon and a gun, choose the gun, praise the gun, the gun wins every time.

The game being a tech-demo is not a bad thing, as Boneworks is one of the first VR-Titles that have combined multiple mechanics that was previously featured in individual games and eventually inspired the Half-Life: Alyx team.

Is this a game I would recommend a first time VR user? No, I would rather have them play Half-Life Alyx first. It's a polished, comfortable experience and a great introduction to VR.
Other than that, the game is a janky, experimental experience that is really fun, but you have to expect the jank going into the game.\t\t\t\t\t
