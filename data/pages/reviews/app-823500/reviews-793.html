See all that cool stuff in the trailers? I can almost guarantee you won't see even a fraction of that fast paced action. You see the devs don't want you to shoot guns or burn through mags holding off enemies, or get into any kind of combat that is not melee why is this might you ask? The ammo you are burning through? That is your currency, there is a finite amount as well what really burns me is you need every last bit of it you can find to unlock the best items for the sandbox mode. And no, I am not talking about cosmetics, or keys, hats anything like that it's the stuff in the launch trailer that you cannot earn any other way that is unless you want to painstakingly go through every nook and cranny to find that precious ammo. I bought the game because I wanted to do that cool fast paced shooting that was shown off, but the game will punish you for doing so. My advice? Do not buy ANYTHING in the campaign no matter how fun it might look. Use your bullets as the absolute last resort you can only shoot so many bullets over the campaign before you dam yourself with too low a score. And lastly find and break anything that can be destroyed for ammo, oh and don't think that it will always be a fun scavenger hunt every time - it is not even close just random closets and storage rooms that are sometimes difficult to reach. That's pretty much the game there is a story ( I think? ) but I cannot hear whoever is speaking through the intercoms half the time in the first couple levels so I have no idea what is going on, or why the levels look half rendered at times, why the ( zombies? ) look like some of the worst character models for them I have ever seen in a video game. "Experimental physics VR " This is all the game is, a glorified vr Physics tech demo with a frankenstein's monster of fps mechanics (HHH does this so much better ) and utterly boring puzzle games.\t\t\t\t\t
