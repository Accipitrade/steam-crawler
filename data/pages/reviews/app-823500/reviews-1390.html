\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the worst VR experience I ever had. The positive first: interaction with the enviroment is preatty neat.

Now... the bad:
The physics. That one thing that was hyped and advertised as gamechanging is on of the worst thing I ever experienced in VR. Remember when articles wrote that Rockstar was to fanatic with their realism in Red Dead Redemption 2 that it hinder fluid gameplay instead of supporting it? That's the same with Boneworks and his physics. More then often they are in the way making you play in pudding. All is just jank and rubbery. Your body is 99% of the game in your way since you can't control your body like you can in RL. Rifles? Forget it, your shoulder will stop you from aim down side and there is no realistic feeling to get it working. Meleeweapons feel like the purple dildo from Saints Row 3 and as said before, gameplay is one of the clunkiest I ever experienced in VR.

Levedesign... there is none. It is random "physicspuzzle" level after random level. No ryhme or reason behind it stopping the overall game to get some sort of momentum. It constantly stops you progress way to abruptly.

If you want to force you through this because you want to be engaged with a good story that keeps you going? Classic Doom had a better story.

This is a physics techdemo from start to end. Also the developers don't care for accessibility. It took over a year to implement some sort of left handed support or control options in general for players to set up their inputs to their liking. They were overall unfriendly with normal questions aboud said support, theatening with kicks and bans on their Discord. Even when asked years after this game on their new game Bonelab here on Steam for an options that should be standart in every VR game: "This ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ option is in there." The options that gives access to the game for more players that otherwise couldn't. Accessibility is not important for them.

I am drifting away. Back to the tech demo. If you want to try then use the 2h refund system and hope this physics system will never get the standart in VR. Other games do it way better with the right mix of physics and "playerclipping": Alyx, Hellsplit Arena, Blade & Sorcery, Walking Dead Saints and Sinners. But Boneworks does not.\t\t\t\t\t

I find some of the puzzles poorly executed without a clear indication that you are doing the right thing. This is generally witnessed in the gravity well puzzle in level 5(?)

The game has sometimes very harsh checkpoints and if you crash or choose to quit in the middle of a level you have to start all over

Underground tunnel level is the best level!

Update 2: Beat the game. Amazing boss music. Opera de Ossium (feat. Tim Na & Hadrian McQuaig)

It's best played standing with Valve Index and a lot of room. It's a full lenght game, not a tech demo like one of the hidden rooms hints.\t\t\t\t\t
