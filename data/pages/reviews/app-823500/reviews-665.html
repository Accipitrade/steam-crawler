\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm really not sure why this wasn't taken as the VR half life game alot of people wanted, nonetheless it is a fantastic game with a mysterious story, and stellar gameplay to follow. Everything from the freedom of movement, to innovative storage/toolbelt system, to interesting melee combat, and deep engaging puzzles that really make you think. Sure the game is rough around the edges and some of the bugs and glitches break immersion occasionally, but I'm happy that the devs put the time and effort into making the game run at a stable framerate and add as many fun quality of life things to the game like: Being able to grab Vrheadcrabs and beat the crap out of them; use nearby blunt objects to stun or kill enemies; letting all melee weapons have a pierce effect when stabbing enemies; being able to climb, platform, and progress by simply grabbing on to things; being able to see your physical body; having a physical weight limit. Things such as these really make you feel the boundaries of life and the game start to fade. Amazing immersion with soundtrack to follow was also an incredibly nice touch as well. I had myself scrolling through OST on youtube for a bit after I was done. The AI was a tad boring and I mostly would have liked to see enemies be a bit more interesting. The ending was a bit lack luster and didn't make much sense, but I'm not a person who really cares about the ending of a movie or video game as long as they show at least a hint of effort or send off and I believe that was done here, could have been better. In closing this game felt somewhere in between half life and portal with immersion in VR like no other game. I'd say it's a VR must play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has stuck with me for quite a while.
Any VR power user (i.e. not susceptible to motion sickness) has to try this game out, I'd say. It offers unparallelled freedom of movement, which is really liberating. Climb, stack, and clamber your way through the -- at times, regrettably, a bit too linear -- levels.

It's FUN, and it actually almost works! Remember, this is still game physics. It's not going to be flawless.

Combat is fun, feels heavy and physical, even when using firearms. No complaints there. Quite the opposite, in fact.

Graphics are okay. The environments are not all that memorable, but they do serve their purpose, and they do have their moments.

The story... it's weird! I didn't really understand it while playing, but somehow it crept inside my head and remained with me after finishing the game. It feels well thought-out, I can say, even if it may be hard to fully grasp. Paired with the excellent soundtrack, it makes for a memorable experience.

Summarized: A must-try for all who can stand the movement.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis may sound dramatic, but Boneworks is probably one of my favorite gaming experiences to date. Playing VR and being able to interact with practically every item is so cool.

Wanna grab a box and collect coffee mugs on your way through the level? Sure! Wanna flip a table for cover from enemy gunfire? Go ahead! Wanna use a trash can lid to protect your face from a sentry gun, and then turn it off? Righty-o!

The gameplay for this game in amazingly immersive, being able to see an actual virtual body is great. Having items and your body actually collide with walls is something I didn't think I was missing in VR. Being able to use the force to a degree is also pretty cool ngl.

This game is definitely not for VR users who are new to Vr or made VR sick easily. If you belong to one of those two groups and tried to play this game, it would not go well.
But when you are feeling more comfortable/experienced with VR, this game is a must have. I feel like the game could easily be sold for much more, and I would still find it worth it.


Also, the lore for this game is really great IMO. When playing through without much knowledge of the StressLevelZero lore may be confusing. I found myself super confused by what was going on story-wise (I was able to piece a couple things together) but if you want to understand how deep and extensive the lore for this game is, then I'd go and watch GreasyScotsman's videos on the lore for this game (they are phenomenal).

Jank aside, this game is almost the elusive 10/10 I would give. But just so I don't go too crazy with the praise, I'll give it a nice personal 9.0/10\t\t\t\t\t
