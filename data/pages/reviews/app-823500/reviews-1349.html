\r\n\t\t\t\t\t While this game is certainly impressive, it has downsides.

The whole beginning part felt like it went on way too long and stroked itself a bit much.

It could run a bit better, then again I don't have the best setup so I'll take that for the most part.

Heavy objects and climbing around has been done by Blade and sorcery and is far more versatile, although goofy at times, than Bone works. They show it off but, you can't climb on terrain, just the stuff they indicate.

The jumping is inane, it might take more time to master but, it breaks the flow of gameplay as you have to slowly crouch, walk to the edge then jump to make it. Physical crouching doesn't seem to be as effective or consistent which means you deal with accidentally crouching as you turn. Also not being able to move in air is annoying, it be one thing if movement was restricted but, it just doesn't happen at all if you weren't moving before the second you jumped.

I haven't done much combat yet, but melee feels about the same as B&S, sorry for this repeated comparison but they do some of the same ideas. B&S feels a bit better but, I haven't fought live targets and Bone works seems to be mode focused on gunplay, so that's fair enough.

Overall if you're new to these mechanics they are impressive and deserve a play. If you've long since earned your VR legs, then this game is still a fair buy if you enjoy these mechanics but, is clearly in need of tweaking and updating. Its got a lot of potential but, its muddled in either lack of experience with game development or new tech, which both are super understandable, however these things still exist as problems.

I'll continue to play and if my opinion is changed then so too will the review.\t\t\t\t\t
