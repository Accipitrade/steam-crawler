\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wanted this game to be great, since the moment I saw the first tech demo showcase on YouTube years ago I was looking forward to the game, at the time I didn't have a VR headset but after Half Life Alyx reveal I decided to pick up a Valve Index just before this came out and it was one of my first VR games, unfortunately I was disappointed in what I found, don't get me wrong, I was very impressed in the first hour, the tutorial level was fascinating but once I reached the streets, it all hit rock bottom, maybe it was because I had to replay the same level 3 times because there is no quick save.

The atmosphere of the game stinks, it just feels like a raw tech demo, based on how the game started this is essentially a game within a game so it kind of makes sense, I didn't feel great in that world, basic grid textures, enemy design, sound design just didn't make me feel well in that world, it just made me feel depressed, I just wasn't having fun.

The game physics are amazing, you can interact with everything however after a while it becomes a pain, as an example you see a box that you need to drag, so you grab it but it's not as easy, it's heavy in the game, but it just doesn't translate well to me on the other side, sledge hammer... it was the most frustrating thing to use, the weight made it feel like my actions were delayed, think of streaming a game on a bad connection, it was like that using it. Physics are incredibly realistic but that doesn't make them fun, I didn't find them fun.

Guns... it was the first thing that was shown all those years ago, and it's clearly the thing the game is designed around, they are simply amazing, if only there was a multiplayer mode, I would have just skipped single player and jumped online and spend hours in there, they way you interact with them, how they feel to shoot, sounds... it was perfect, I wouldn't change a thing.

And to the most frustrating part of the game, as I mentioned earlier I had to replay one level 3 times, there is no quick save, if you quit you'll have to replay the whole level from the start or maybe the checkpoint if they exist, I didn't get far enough to one, after replaying the same level 3 times I just had enough and it was the thing that pushed me to ask for a refund, if not for that, I would have kept it, played it at my own pace from time to time just to have fun with the guns, I read somewhere that they are looking to add quick save to the game, but it was a little too late for me, they shouldn't have launched the game without quick save, I will return to Boneworks eventually, but as i'm so new to VR and i'm so broke after buying valve index, i opted to buy some other game instead.

Overall for guns and how they sound it's a big thumbs up from me,
for single player, story, world, enemy design, environmental sounds, that's thumbs down from me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe story might be lackluster and very shallow but this game makes up for it in game play. You can handle many different situations with many different tools and props that are built into the level. This game for sure is worth the $30 dollar price tag it offers a sandbox and arena mode and brings a whole lot of replay opportunity. The development team at Stress Level Zero has put out an amazing game for how small of a team they have made a game that should be in everyone's steam library who has VR. I definitely would recommend this game a 9.5/10.

You NEED This Game In You're Life

Overview of Negative Reviews

I'm sure your looking for negative reviews, so look no further, I will simply tell you what they are, and why you should NOT listen to them. 90% of them are about "save features" missing which isn't that big of a deal at all, 45 minutes to the next checkpoint is not going to kill you, and you can AFK at any time in safe spots. And the rest of the people are complaining about the new physics engine and how the game is "janky". I'm not going to blow the reviews off, but the dev's are already planning to release full save features, so cross that off your list of reasons not to buy it. And about the jankyness, this is a new physics engine, there are bound to be problems, glitches, and quirks, sure, but that is not a reason for a bad review,

Why This Game Is Fantastic

The physics are amazing, climbing feels great, and once you get movement down and reloading smooth, your unstoppable. The amount of ways you can kill enemy's is absolutely brilliant, in fact I have compacted an entire list of ways for you.

1. Smash their skull against a wall.
2. Grab them and repeatably stab them mercilessly in the stomach.
3. grab the nearest blunt object and start hammering there face in until the only thing left is a bloody pulp.
4. Jump off the edge of a platform in slow motion onto an enemy bellow with a sledgehammer and pulverize them.
5. Grab a machete or long sharp object, stab a Headcrab and use the now stuck Headcrab on your weapon to beat the next nearest enemy to death with it.


I could go on but the list would not end, Furthermore the next most amazing thing about this game is the climbing, you can climb almost everything, this gives such an amazing sense of freedom its impossible to describe, the feeling of being able to walk up to a some boxes or shelves or pipes and being like "I wanna go up there" and simply grabbing and climbing, such a simple thing that does so much.

The last thing I will mention is the Slow Motion, such an amazing and simple feature that just adds so many more elements to the game, allowing you to do things that you would not be able to do with simple reaction time, it makes the game so much more fun. You can do anything you want, play how you want, go where you want, and most importantly, kill how you want.

Closing Statement

I hate essay reviews, so I'm going to stop here, buy this game, its $30 for the furthest advancement in VR technology the world has ever seen. And if I could not convince you to buy it, maybe SwaggerSouls can.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is both brilliant and a little janky, but overall delivers an experience unlike any other currently available for VR.
There are no comfort settings so take heed on the developers warning on this, you will need your VR legs already to enjoy this game to its fullest.
The world building is good, but the story is sparse you will have to fill in many blanks yourself.
Level design is a bit hit and miss, but offers many ways to overcome obstacles using the environment and your VR parkour skills very often.
After unlocking them, Arena and Sandbox mode are where you will likely spend most of your time, allowing you to battle hordes of enemies with whatever weapons and items you manage to gather in the singleplayer game.
Definitely worth the price of admission day 1, if it's on sale get it, if it's not get it anyway.\t\t\t\t\t
