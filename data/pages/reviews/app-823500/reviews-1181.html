\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is trying really hard to be revolutionary, but everything is so fiddly it is extremely frustrating. Climbing in particular is frustrating as your hands frequently get stuck on or inside of the surface you are climbing. I found the climbing mechanic in zero caliber to be much more consistent and fun.

The shooting and guns are fun, but the reloading is a pain. Sometimes the magazine goes in properly, sometimes it doesn't. This isn't much of an issue in other shooters, so I'm confused as to why it is such a pain in this game.

The completely physics based environment is amazing and tons of fun to explore. This is definitely a plus, however, the melee combat and crate destruction being entirely physics based is absolutely horrendous. It takes forever to kill any enemy with melee unless you are extremely lucky and breaking objects to see what is inside is tedious when it takes 5 or more hits per box sometimes.

Overall, the game is a good preview at where future VR games should aim to go, but none of the mechanics are truly fun in the state they exist here. If everything were more polished I feel like the experience would be vastly improved.

4/10 for the attempt, but poor execution.\t\t\t\t\t

I've had a VR set for about 3 years now, got the original oculus rift + touch bundle, so my review is based on my experience in boneworks with Oculus.

This is the best VR experience I've had so far , it reminds me of portal/half life/stanley parable.

PROS

+ The physics are amazing, i spent hours messing around in the first few levels trying to climb/throw/find things. It reminds me of messing around with the playground in half life 2.
+ Gunplay is solid and feels very tactile, the gun sounds and models are nice.
+ Melee combat feels good, the weapons have simulated "weight". Its really noticeable in the difference in handling between a huge sledgehammer and a small knife/sword.
+ Story is good, took me about 8-9 hours to beat while still trying to explore everything.
+ Entire body is a physical object, so your arms/legs even your head can get caught on things, really immersive, no clipping through things.
+ Seated mode works very smoothly, you can customize your distance from the ground and distance from standing height manually to make it as comfortable as possible.
+ The reclamation stations unlock anything you put in them for sandbox mode.

CONS

- Performance in larger areas is rather poor, I have a decent PC (check my profile for full specs) and it still struggled in some areas.
- On oculus you almost always have to grab things with the grip AND the trigger or else it either wont grab, or only grabs with the palm of your hand and your pointer finger stays extended.
- The 1911 style pistol seems to aim high, but the glock doesn't.


Overall I HIGHLY reccomend this game to anyone who has a VR headset, even if it isnt the Valve Index, its highly enjoyable and has good replay value for unlocking thing to use in sandbox mode. Also worth it at the price its currently at ($30)
