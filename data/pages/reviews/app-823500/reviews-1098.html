this game is just not made for tall people with above-average arm length and clunky vive wands.

I'm not able to jump properly at all, no matter what height and floor measurement combinations I use, my arms are way too short, and messing with the height to fix my arms just screws said jumping capabilities over.

The vive wands were probably the worst of the 3 compatible VR controllers for this game, and I've never been a fan their touch pads.

You wanna grab something? Have fun holding down the grip and trigger at all times if you don't want to drop it. (thank god it doesn't count for guns)
Again, with the wands, you're already holding something (whereas the index, you're not), and now your hands will be rewarded with cramps by sqeezing the controller buttons, mostly at all times.

And funnily enough, grabbing is 70% of the experience.

I sadly had to ask for a refund. Maybe these problems would fix themselves if I got the Index controllers, and now I have, so all I need now is to buy the game again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm really mixed on this game, but ultimately I'd recommend against it. The game likes to boast (and meme) that it's "Not just another tech demo," but in reality, the execution makes it feel that way even though the game has a complete campaign.

The assets and mechanics the game uses to create its levels and gameplay are mostly good. There isn't anything here I would call stand-out that other VR tech-games don't have, but almost everything here feels solid to play with. The major exception to this would be the weight system the developer's have introduced to the game. The game attempts to simulate the feeling of objects being heavy by making objects that should have weight more difficult to move. This is done by making the in-game input lag behind the controller input. This actually isn't too bad a system when it works; however, a majority of the time when attempting to manipulate a heavier object, it will fling around and be incredibly difficult to get it to do what you want. This is super frustrating and can bring about motion sickness, especially when your arm gets stuck in a wall for no reason and you start flailing around.

Mechanical issues aside, the level design is the major reason I can't recommend this game. The game may have a campaign, but in reality, it's just a bunch of randomly haphazard levels the each feel like they were just duct-taped together with random mechanics. One level will focus entirely on climbing things. Another will be a shooting gallery. Another will be a very long-puzzle filled trek (the puzzle design is usually terrible btw) with a random fire-fight in-between for no reason. Then they randomly throw in an arena level where they make you used their melee weapons which uses their irritating weight system when you've been using guns the entire game. The levels are all almost very long, and there are no checkpoints if you want to quit and take a break, which is especially annoying considering the game's tendency to do stuff that makes you motion sick. There was a point towards the back-half of the games where the levels were starting to get relatively decent, but then they went right back to feeling like they were designed by complete amateurs right towards the end. There was also no cohesion at all between individual levels and the rest of the game. I'm pretty sure the story is just that you're in a VR world and need to leave, which is probably why it feels completely random. The limited enemy variety didn't help either.

TL;DR: Mostly good mechanics, stupid weighty movement can make you feel sick, level design is awful.\t\t\t\t\t
