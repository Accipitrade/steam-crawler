\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is no ordinary Virtual Reality game nor is it simplistic. This is a 'one in a lifetime' game which is not based off of any other video game and is completely unique to satisfy your VR cravings. I'm Gangsta Shots and I will give you the satisfaction in knowing more about this game, and by the end you will have a more deeper understanding of how this game works inside & out.

Story:
Lets start with the story side, BONEWORKS has a very unique and compelling way to tell and perform it's story. At the start of the game you will find yourself feeling confused as to what lies ahead but the game gives clear direction in which way to go and how to progress. The game is set in a Dystopian world where robots and monsters hunt you down and want to force you to stay in this virtual world where you, Arthur Ford have no control over your real life body & mind. You are in a Virtual world where your mission is to stop the Nebulas Mega-Corporation that created MythOS from taking peoples souls into the Void. BONEWORKS specifies it's story in a matter of puzzles and mazes where you will feel overwhelmed on how to complete that said puzzle and often leaves you gasping for air on how much brain cells are being used in solving it. However the game rewards you in completing a puzzle with ammo and... That's about it. Although it does leave you feeling proud on how you used your brain to figure it out without having the game fully hold your hand every step of the way. By the end of the story you will know how and why you (your character) are there and what your mission is in this unsettling environment.

Characters & Community:
There are a variety of characters you will meet throughout BONEWORKS and most of them are just obstacles for you to get around. For instance the Zombies, Void monsters and robots main goals are to stop you from progressing further and uncovering what MythOS's plans are. Your protagonist Arthur Ford is apart of the Monogon company and are there to put an end to the Void created by a company called MythOS. You also have to escape from the virtual city you are stuck in. Each character have a similar AI like the zombies which come in four different forms but generally all enemies are easy to deal with. You can even punch your competitors out of your way and can grasp them in any part of their body as you please. There are ranged and surprise attack enemies like the headset and MythOS guards who aim to put you down no matter what. Not all characters are evil as there are others on your side of the fight too. Although they are not actually 'in' the fight they are on computer screens who help explain and give tips to you along your journey into the Void. Played by Mike Diva and Heldine Aguiluz they are also employees of Monogon and they contact you through TV's displayed throughout the world. You will be over come'd with the staggering amount of weapons and firearms you can use to your personal liking whether your a Samurai type of guy or a Marksman this game suites all types of players.

Sounds, Design & Specification:
The sound is extremely well put together in terms of the things you touch and characters around you. The gunshots from firearms you find are as close to it being in your hand as possible and the sounds of metal clashing against other objects are just as entertaining. Every weapon you find have their own sounds and each sound is played differently depending on the item and what you hit it with. You can hear distant roars of zombies and gunshots from the ranged enemies so you can tell how far they were without having to look. Designs in this game are truly captivating from the shadows of the things around you to the interiors, weapons, firearms, characters and pretty much everything is believable in a sense. Although to Battlefield graphics the game does a very great job in rendering all the pixels to a fine point. This leads us to the Specs of the game. Running this from a gaming PC with a Quad core CPU, Intel core I7 and GeForce GTX 1070 I could have expected better in performance runs. I found it difficult to render long distances and found that my PC's fan speed went into overdrive when battling in Melee combat. I use an Oculus Rift S headset and tried every step in the book to get the game to run better. But sadly could not stop the rendering and PC fan-frying issues.

Comparative and re-playable Gameplay:
This game is cannot be compared to any other VR or non-VR title. From the story, narrative and gameplay this game does not follow the footsteps of any other title out there. With it's compelling story telling and hard grips to keep you playing I really did find this game to be fun and enjoyable. After you complete the story you can either play Sandbox or Arena mode and both are just as fun as each other. Sandbox mode is playable after finishing the story and you can use every item you have found and thrown in the 'Sandbox Bin' at the end of each stage in the Story. Arena is self explanatory where you battle a bunch of enemies in a thrilling environment and get rewarded with more items and Steam achievements.

Additional comments:
Overall this game is an excellent piece of the VR industry and has a huge impact in how VR is suppose to be and hopefully will be made. From the excellent story to the thrilling game modes afterwards this game is definitely one to be enjoyed by every VR owner. The only wish I hope gets granted is the ability to play with another companion in a Co-Op mode. But as far I am concerned this is good enough and well deserved it's current price tag.\t\t\t\t\t

You will find yourself drawn to the virtual world of MythOS over and over again. There really hasn't been another game like this. Don't wait for a sale. This one is worth full price. This game is also a reason to buy a VR headset if you don't yet have one. Works great with Valve Index.

PROS:
Immersion
Hilarity when physics don't work as expected
Amazingly fun gameplay
One of the best video game soundtracks
Plenty of replay value with arena and sandbox mode and collectibles

CONS:
Good chance it will be difficult to get the hang of for people new to VR and also could make someone feel ill if they're not used to this style of gameplay yet. If that's you, then go slow and take breaks. You'll get used to it eventually most likely.
Physics sometimes janky. This does lead to some comical moments on occasion however. But then again it also leads to some real pain in the ass moments sometimes as well.
Not enough variety in the enemies.
Some people will say the story isn't very well fleshed out and is kinda thin but on closer inspection I think you'll find there's more to it than you first realize. Not really one for the con column but I think many people will think it is until they dig deeper.

There's a few games that are reasons to get into VR right now and this is one of them. You can get lost in this thing.\t\t\t\t\t
