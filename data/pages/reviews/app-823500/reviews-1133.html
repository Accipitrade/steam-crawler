\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who has been hyped for this game for months I honestly have no idea how nobody else doesn't seem to be extremely let down.

Pros:
+Fun concept/setting
+World interactivity
+Interesting world/character design

Cons:
-Locomotion. Wow does moving in this game feel WONKY. Given that you play AS a physics object, running, jumping and climbing are all nauseating; an issue I never face with other VR games featuring tons of movement and high falls.
-Controls: This game is built for the index controllers more than anything. Prepare for soreness from holding objects because there's no toggle option. Moving also doesn't allow trackpad moving and forces you to use tap and hold
-Object handling: This especially shows its cracks when using the slowmo feature. Objects bounce around in your hand like your arms are wet pool noodles. Moving just a little will result in your arms bouncing back and forth. Aiming down sights requires you to hold your arms as far away from your body as possible. Don't get me started on the sledgehammer...
-Combat. Melee weapons don't have the responsiveness I was expecting. Obviously every game can't be Blade and Sorcery and maybe it's just the bad controls for vive but I always feel so stiff and restricted attacking enemies.

Overall the game offers you the choice to become immersed in the world on your own terms but it never really forces you to. The interactivity with objects is cool but when you stop and take a second to look around, you realize that the levels just feel like a modded map from Gmod. It's so much easier to just play the game like any other game because so many of the neat systems just don't work as well as they could. For the time being I'm refunding this because $30 for what feels like early access just isn't worth it in my opinion.

All of you will jump on this with the "BUT THE PHYSICS" argument. The physics feel like a coverup for an empty world with not much going on. I can only throw so many coffee mugs before I start to question why I'm even bothering anymore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI picked up an Oculus Rift S shortly after this game released. I already owned a few games that had a VR mode, but I had heard a lot of buzz about this game. Bottom line, if you own a VR headset, you owe it to yourself to play this game. I have gone through a handful of other VR experiences and have watched videos about many more. This game is one of the absolute best VR experiences money can buy. It has an unprecedented sense of presence and interaction with the virtual world. No other VR game that I have seen has the level of interaction that this game has. Each object reacts to the world and to your virtual hands with such physical detail, it is astounding. See an object you want on top of a shelf? Try pulling the shelf down to get at it. Want to open a locker with a sword for whatever reason? The physics are accurate enough that you can carefully stick a sword through a small locker handle and open the locker. Don't have a weapon? Break a bottle and stab away!
This is the gold standard for VR world interaction and physics. I find myself disappointed when I go into older VR games and can't interact with things like I can in this game. If you get motion sick easily, you may not have a good time, but I took to the smooth FPS walking movement quite naturally and I hate when I have to go back to the old teleport method.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis will become the new par standard for future VR games. Everything in BONEWORKS feels smooth and natural, yet remains extremely fun. I played with the original HTC Vive, with the wands and all. It took some time getting used to holding onto things with the wands' strangely placed grip buttons, but after a while it felt natural, so don't worry if you don't have Index knuckles yet as the game is still very much playable to its full potential. Eventually, even you can beat people to death with a chair in the confines of your own home, safe from the prying eyes of your neighbors, as well as the law! Who'd have thought that it'd be so fun just to grab stuff and throw it around?
Aside from the gameplay, it has an interesting story and a striking High-tech/Industrial visual style reminiscent of a mixture of Portal and Half-Life. Engaging the enemy is fun both with firearms and with melee combat, so no matter your approach, you won't get bored of fighting (if you consider what the null-men do to be fighting). However, there isn't much for diversity in firearm selection, as there are only a few variants of the same 3 guns.
The quality of the puzzles vary, some being a complete tedious pain and others being just outright easy, though most you encounter will be at least somewhat fun and engaging, a lot of the times you can just exploit weird physics to circumvent actual critical thought. The biggest downside is that not that some levels are long, it's that they can be long with no manual saves, so you'd better be comfortable being strapped in a VR headset for that long because that's the only way you'll progress in this game.

In review, it's the only VR game I've played that felt like a true game, and I can't wait to see what Stress Level Zero can add to the experience, as well as whatever they're working on next.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI had already wrote a review for this game, and i simply said "Incredible game" and that was it...
After completing through the game now, seeing the ins and outs of the entire story mode along with the arena and sandbox and unlocking almost all the items, I feel like I can confidently say this is one of the greatest games I have ever played.

The introduction to physics and how the game eases you into new challenges is done extraordinarily well. The whole game gives off a strong half life valve kinda vibe. Not to mention the soundtrack is incredible and will definitely become nostalgic in a few months. At no point does the game feel boring or slow either. I promise you after playing this game, every other VR story based game will feel slow, uninspired, boring, and lackluster.

Seriously the fact that this game has a single bad review boggles my mind.\t\t\t\t\t
