\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks represents what I want the future of VR to look like: a proper physics engine, full length game, tons a replay value, and an arena and sandbox modes thrown in there for good measure. Having owned Vive since the month it was launched back in 2016, this game felt like the first proper full length game designed from the ground up for the medium. At the very least in the genre of VR shooters, you won't find anything better at least until, perhaps, when Half-Life: Alyx comes out.

If you have the means to experience this, consider it a no-brainer to purchase. Be wary, though, that if you are prone to motion sickness, you may want to space out your play sessions as the game has no teleportation (which totally makes sense in the type of game it is).\t\t\t\t\t

What if this is just a tech demo?


For a while, I was on the fence about VR and it's future. A lot of games I had seen were either mediocre ports of pre-existing games or "experiences" nothing more than walking simulators. I was worried VR would die within the year because of that, but with the release of Boneworks, my opinion has changed. Boneworks is just absolutely fantastic. It brings back fond memories of playing Half-Life 2 for the first time and seeing all that fancy pantsy physics on everything in the environment. The most prominent feature on display in Boneworks is the physics, and how you exist in the game. Your limbs have physical presence in the world and has weight to it; swinging a hefty weapon or lifting a large box often requires two hands and handling as you would in real life. A walking jump won't carry you far so you need a run-up first. Obviously the lack of physical feedback is still a plague on VR, though I found the arms in particular followed where my actual arms were very accurately. Even if you aren't won over by "COR PHYSICS INNIT" there is credit due to the effort in these systems. The lads over at Stress Level Zero put a lot of thought into the engine and, in my opinion, should become the standard for future VR titles.

Buuuuuut there's also a game behind this too. It isn't just a tech demo right? There's a story in this game, I think. Fairly flimsy, mostly just to frame things, really. The beginning levels are mostly just a showcase of the engine in place, and nearly everything afterwards is just you adapting to your new VR you. I mentioned Half-Life 2 before and it's because of how much the setting and maps reminded me of it. Plenty of grungy urban areas and sewers in Boneworks, lemme tell you. There's even the odd physics puzzle here and there and at one point nearly turns into a horror game. Now, I don't think it's a stretch to say Half-Life 2 is a very good game, but there was also more to HL2 than physics. Narrative segments in HL2 were important in order to keep the player hooked into something more and thus keep it sticking with them longer, and Boneworks simply doesn't have that. There is an intriguing over-arching story to be sure. However there's very little of it during the gameplay segments that it takes a backseat immensely. The world itself has nice aspects of visual storytelling and leaves the player to fill in the gaps but doesn't isn't a substitute for an actual story.

Boneworks is definitely worth checking out, but it isn't a perfect game. There's two major things I would have wanted from Boneworks. The first is a longer, more involved story with more characters, agency for the player, and meta commentary on the tech and engine. The second is a more interactive playground for messing around in. Spawning in enemies and playing around with each weapon and physics toy doesn't hold interest for me too long, unfortunately. Wishfully I'd want a map editor or some other kind of creation tool, but something as little as additional challenge maps would go a long way.\t\t\t\t\t
