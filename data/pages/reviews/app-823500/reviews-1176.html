This is my 1rst VR game, so don't know how the other will relate to this one...

I can't play much at a time because it give me motion sickness after some time (that's why i may have a pretty high play time but for some it just sit there while i do something else waiting for it to settle so other time i quit and come back later) but so far i've made each level in 2 play time, the 1rst is exploring around messing and discovering quite some of the secret it has to offer, and missing a passage that you know you can go but not have the tool yet to do so...
2nd time i'm much faster as i know where i gone the 1rst time and it allow me to not waste time looking behind every box for more ammo...

I've never had a gun in my life but it feel natural, so far i like pistol, the smg i'm not a fan of, the "M4 looking" (sorry i don't know gun except CS) feel heavy, the other one feels lighter...
To be honest i'm only at the Warehouse level and just really shoot the "M4" for the 1rst time, only shoot mostly pistol so far and headshots are quite easy to achieve... Sometime i even make shoot i didn't think would be possible, maybe they aren't and is the game engine that make them connect but who know...

Going around corner with a pistol, grabbing with both hands and leaning to see feels like i'm John Wick about to unleash hell...

So far no real issue with the game, only problems occur then my PC is complaining (100% CPU usage because of an internet tab/ GPU getting too hot as one of the fan seems to have problem) but it's local to me and shouldn't be a problem with anyone else.

This is my 1rst ever review of a game on steam but it is so awesome that it earned it! (If there are misspelling in this review, do know english isn't my language so feel free to point them out but don't be too harsh about it :p)

tldr: It's awesome, if you have a VR headset buy it and play it, you won't regret it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere should I start...

So after seing and hearing about this game since the end of 2016 it has finally released and I have to say I haven't been disappointed! First of all I played it on the Rift S with the AMVR Controller Grips for Touch, it comes so close to the Index controllers, you wont miss out on anything. SLZ really did a great job to fully utilize the touch controllers! (I have no experience on VIVE or WMR, but I think their controllers are really bad and I can imagine its not as fun to play with those)

The obvious star of the show are the physics, although the story is not great, it also isn't bad at all, however I found it a bit disappointing towards the end, I would have prefered to go out with a bang, instead of a neck pain lol the ones who already played it will know. Your creativity is the biggest story teller here. The more creativity you have, the more fun you'll have! Trying to push the boundaries in this game is hella fun!

Although I have to say it took 2-4 hours, till it really clicked for me. Boneworks gameplay is definetly something to get used to, even if you've spend a lot of time in the amazing Museum at the start, there is a lot more you'll find out down the road. For example, I have to replay the Story only for the fact that I fully understood the climbing mechanics towards the end, so I'm pretty shure there is lots I've missed along the way, replay value is huge! Secrets & little details everywhere!

Once you got used to the mechanics it is pretty hard to go back to anything else, at least imo. Playing around in the arena, feeling like a slow-mo super soldier after mastering the weapons and guns, doing crazy reloads, throwing knifes, smashing crabs out the sky with the stock of your M4 and smash a dude to death with headbutts. After that it just feels lame to go back into a round of pavlov, where I just have floaty hands, almost no physics whatsoever, just feels shallow after Boneworks...

This Game truely is a milestone for VR gaming, definetly not one for the newcomers, I can imagine some getting pretty Motion Sick with Boneworks, but one that definetly raised the bar, no going back after this one! There is so much more I missed out on mentioning, like the amazing OST, great audio in general, little bugs and good performance and much more...

In the end this was an amazing game to playthrough and one of the best I've played it the last 10 years!! I really had a blast and hope Brandon and his crew expand the game down the line and I'm eager to see their roadmap.\t\t\t\t\t
