\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks, in one form or another, has been showed off since early 2018, and consistently made to look more and more exciting from then up until its release. It would be easy to think that this means it wouldn't live up to the hype, but that sort of notion is also thankfully wrong. Boneworks lives up extremely well to the hype built up around the name, and while not everything works perfectly 100% of the time, it's an amazing game regardless.

What Boneworks sets out to do is to combine high-quality shooting, melee combat, physics, environmental design, puzzles, and graphics, and make a story-based game out of that. And while it doesn't do any one of these perfectly, except for perhaps puzzles, its jack-of-all-trades approach makes for a stunning experience. The sense of immersion, likely in part due to the high-quality virtual body, sets quite a high standard for VR.

Of course, no game is without flaws. The physics in Boneworks aren't perfect, the lack of checkpoints can be frustrating, and the puzzles can be irritating at first. There is, at the time of this review, a lack of proper WMR support, but the developers are already working on a fix for that. So despite sometimes showing that there's still room for improvement in VR, ultimately these flaws are not dealbreakers for me.

It's hard not to recommend Boneworks to any VR owner, honestly. It's such an impressively made game that it's worth giving it a try at the very least. If Boneworks is representative of the future of VR, then the entire medium has an extremely bright future.\t\t\t\t\t
