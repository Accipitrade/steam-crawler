\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI own over 100 VR games in my library. This is the only game that is objectively not fun. This is nothing more than a glorified tech demo at best. This is the only VR game that has made me scream out in rage because of just how frustrating it is to try to play. There are NO saves, NO checkpoints, and NO progression what-so-ever in the levels, meaning that an hour or more of progress can easily be completely undone due to needing to restart the game for any reason.

The puzzles are not fun, they are simply frustrating. They are not 'puzzles' in that they are so simple there is nothing to figure out. Instead, you fight both the controls of the game AND the physics engine to perform simple tasks.

Arms, hands, and any weapon you carry will constantly get stuck on both your body and the objects in the world. The control scheme with Vive Wands means you will always angle away from your intended direction EVERY time you try to jump. 2 inch gaps, such as a curb on the side of the road, will require you to jump over them instead of simply walk over them, BUT you can't simply jump over them as you have to back up and do a running jump to clear that 2 inch height EVERY TIME, since your body after all is a physics object and you have no control over your feet, making them catch on EVERY jump you try to make.


Save your money. This is nothing but a demonstration of VR physics. Buying this game is like buying Unreal Tournament because so many other games use the UE engine. Just wait for Alyx instead. I honestly feel conned out of my money since the tutorial level and initial level took just over the 2 hour refund window before the game simply became a waste of time.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneWorks is right up there with the top VR games. While a casual player might not be interested, if you want a deeper experience with your VR games, BoneWorks does a great job with the amount of stuff you can interact with.

I'm playing through the game on my channel, but if you just wanna take a quick peak at some gameplay, here's the tutorial level. It's pretty standard for most VR enthusiasts, but there's some unique mechanics and the gunplay is amazing!
https://youtu.be/9uEQFts-mxY

THE GOOD:
Primarily the game focuses on combat, with some melee weapons and gunplay. What BoneWorks does differently is how you interact with these items, you'll have to rotate and position your melee weapon so the pointy end is facing the enemy, and every time you reload a gun, you have to add a clip and reload the chamber.
It makes gunfights really intense while you try to keep calm to reload with enemies closing in on you.

On top of that there's TONS of secrets! You can unlock a sandbox map along with different items to use it in, and apparently cheat codes like no-clip (I haven't gotten that far yet) so there's a lot of replay value.

Most VR games involve quick pick-up-and-play levels while BoneWorks has a fleshed out story mode with some great self-aware humor.

THE BAD:
My biggest complaint about the game is that you don't get any checkpoints in the levels, because the levels are basically a giant playground like most VR games, just with a start and finish. So when you die, you restart at the beginning of the level, but everything you did in that level will stay the same, you just have to walk back to where you were.
The game tries to rectify this with shortcuts, since they stay unlocked even if you die, but it would be nice on some of the longer levels to have a proper checkpoint system.

I also had issues with controller tracking on the Streets level where my hands would start floating and I couldn't interact with anything unless I restarted.. not respawned, restarted the level from scratch! Thankfully I haven't had that issue since, but it was super annoying.

CONCLUSION:
I'm on the original HTC Vive and looking forward to playing this game again when Valve finally ships me my Index.
If you like Blade & Sorcery or other more "intense" VR games, then you NEED BoneWorks in your library!
It's not the best game to break out at parties (unless you've unlocked the sandbox levels) but it's worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo preface, if you do not have your VR legs (you get nauseous quickly, cannot gauge your room in VR or just can't use locomotion.) do not play this game. It is made clear that this game is not for you.

Boneworks is the best VR title I have played.

First let's start with the good things about this game:

-Physics Engine
The Physics Engine in this game is a first in VR and will be unrivaled for quite some time. Like most games when you see a problem that needs to be solved, you come up with a solution. A first for VR, whatever solution you have is correct and can be done.

-Weapons
The weapons in this game are on par with H3. They feel real and they feel good. The rifles especially feel good because of the weight they have compared to other weapons like pistols. The melee weapons are also enjoyable to use. My favorite has to be the half sword!

-Character Body
This is definitely a controversial thing to include but it really does feel right. Too many VR games just feature hands as your method of control. Comparing that to Boneworks, they full body really brings presence and immersion into the gameworld. You ask yourself, how can I use my arms and legs to solve this level.

Just one bad thing:

-Story
The plot of the game was a bit lacking leaving the player confused about their part in it. Although lacking, I am positive they will bring some kind of expansion to it in later updates.

All in all, for about 15 hours of Story Content (If you want to get the developer gadgets) and endless time spent sandboxing the beautifully crafted levels, Boneworks is a MUST buy for Headset Owners (As long as you have your VR Legs) especially considering its price tag of $30.
