Game so far is highly puzzle driven which is not necessarily a bad thing but feels tedious to me. Funny because I normally like flat screen puzzle games, something about VR I guess.
Physics feels a bit clunky at times, which was kind of expected. Not everything acts the way I expect it to and I feel weak.
Combat is okay, but it seems like my gun interacts with my body a little too much making aiming feel odd, as if the gun stock is slipping/getting caught on my virtual shoulder.
Definitely more improvement needed imo.
Overall, it the game seems well polished and definitely one of the better VR games out there. May not be my cup of tea but I recognize it as a good VR game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter spending from 1:30 to 5:12 in this game, I can safely say its a flawed masterpiece.
And no its not flawed in any major way, just a VR game nitpicks little whiny complaints about the game.
And please don't get me wrong, I am a huge fan of Stress Level Zero, and I've been hyped for Boneworks for the past three years. It is exactly what it was intended to be, and it will still probably be my favorite VR game to date by the time I finish playing.

Before we get into the bad I'm going to state the good!
The OST (Perfect)
The Graphics (New art style but overall good lighting and textures)
The Player Character Real Time Body (Jenky at times but extremely accurate)
The Combat (Fluid)
The Puzzles (Requires Critical Thinking)
The Physics (9/10 Perfect)
The Guns (So accurate and fun to use.)

Here is the bad.
The Jumping (Quite possibly the worst thing about the game)
The Virtual Crouch (Needed to get to some places which ruins immersion quick)
The Hand Strength (At points you feel like superman, others, you feel extremely weak)
The Poor Map Design (Most of the games maps are fantastic, but a lot of the time there are completely pointless areas that you take a long time to go to, and find out they're absolutely pointless)
The Loading Times (Not the worst out of VR games, but definitely painful)
The Random SteamVR Loading (A lot of the time when I do something physics or particle based it will boot me to the SteamVR loading page for a second or two, and doesn't happen in most higher fidelity graphics games)
The Story (Messy and not directed at some times)

EDIT:

After much more time with the game, and completing the story, I wanted to touch on a couple of the topics I have already spoken about. The physics get much better once you really get used to it. Climbing is MUCH better when using virtual crouch which I dislike, but I deal with it. The combat is very simple, and even though they add different and fresh enemy types throughout the game, it never presents much of a challenge. I loved exploring every nook and cranny, and finding all of the amazing secrets throughout the game, and could not have asked for a more entertaining and down to earth dev team <3. I feel like a short story or some kind of comic would benefit the story well, as you don't get that much detail about what seems to be a very detailed world.

In conclusion, Boneworks is damn near perfect, and an absolute work of art. The development team put a tremendous amount of work and love into this game, and I'm so happy I was able to see the game evolve through videos on Node, and finally get to play it myself.

Love to everyone at Stress Level Zero, you have once again kicked ass with this beautiful game. To the future of Boneworks!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been so long since the first time I launched Half Life 2 and started having fun with physics. I watched a documentary recently about HL2 and how revolutionary it felt and I tried thinking back to how it felt and couldn't really remember. About the time I realized I had spent five minutes crouch walking holding a cardboard box over my head looking through the handle slot feeling like solid snake, I remembered the feeling. As I spent about 15 minutes sweeping up bullet casings with a broom I for some reason decided was my friend that I wanted to carry for as long as possible I very much remembered the feeling again. I haven't played a game that really felt like a true game changer in a very long time, and now I remember what it feels like again.

I have no idea about the narrative yet, although it feels very Portal-esque for reasons that are obvious very soon into the tutorial, but the technology is so absolutely enthralling I cannot recommend the experience enough already, and I just got to the point where you actually get to shoot guns(which feel absolutely amazing by the way).

If you're the type of person who likes to make dumb fun for yourself in games, prepare to spend dozens more hours than whatever the story says it takes, because around every single corner is some idiotic fun to be had doing some real dumb stuff with physics.\t\t\t\t\t
