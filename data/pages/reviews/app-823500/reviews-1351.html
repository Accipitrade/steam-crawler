As of right now the game feels pretty clunky with the movements and interactions, I'd imagine this will be even more the case if you do not set up your height/floor parameters properly. It is very easy to get motion sickness in this game in comparison to other VR titles, if that is a big issue for you then you should probably hold off on getting this game. I find that actually doing the movements while moving along with the character helps a lot with the movement sickness problem.
(example: running in place while moving and not using the right Index Analog stick to do turns, strictly use head movement)

Do I recommend this for people who have large rooms to move around in?
Yes.

What about people who have smaller rooms and are forced to stand in place?
Big No.

What about people who just sit?
Haven't tested it out yet, but I'd imagine you would want to still turn your head around while turning.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a great VR title, with some minor exceptions.

First, I'll give you what I believe to be the "pros" of this game. The things that I like.

[Pro]
-Full Physics/Interactions
-Interesting & New gameplay mechanics
-Natural doors
-Smooth locomotion
-Walking around feels natural
-It's the first time I've been fully immersed; I want to explore every part of the world.
-Level design and graphics are a step above most VR games/titles.
-Variety of Objects
-Variety of Guns
-Variety of ways to complete levels
-Makes you think "wow I wish all games had this type of interaction"

Now, the neutral, not really "good" nor "bad".

[Neutral]
-Can get motion sickness (Unless you are used to VR)
-Game prefers you use smooth locomotion and lacks some comfort settings

and finally, the cons, the things I disliked about the game overall.

[Cons]
-Guns lack a realistic feel compared to other VR shooters; though it's not that bad.
-Janky movements at certain times, due to your character being a full physics object
-Slow start; game starts slow with few enemies, but ends up picking up later on.
-Not very clear on how to get saved guns/weapons
-Checkpoints not super clear
-Made for those who have heavily into VR already; and has some understanding in how physics works (could be a pro or con depending on how you see it; and you don't need to be a genius, but a child couldn't solve these puzzles)
-Contains lots of puzzles, so if you hate puzzles, stay away.

Overall, Boneworks is a intuitive game that uses physics to its advantage; to allow the player to solve puzzles and fight enemies along the way. There is no real set path for your character, as how you complete levels and solve issues is up to you in the end. The pro's 100 percent outweigh the current cons and I can see some of the "Jankyness" in movement being more fine tuned and fixed in the future. I only have about 3 hours in (2.8 currently) and I can tell you that you will want this game if you are a fan of VR in general.

I now have 12+ Hours in this game, and my opinion still stands.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell I've played for nearly 3 hours and I haven't even gotten through the tutorial yet! That is how fun the physics interactions are in this game.

I also just got the Valve Index and this game uses the controllers really well , I'm having a blast.

I can't wait to get back in and explore more.

Update - Now I've played for almost 8 hours and this game needs a save system badly. Keep getting stuck and needing to take breaks from VR only to come back and have to start from the start of the level again. At this rate I'll never finish the game. haha .

Update#2 - Now I've played the game for 10 hours and I have finally completed the first level! lol. Even though there is no saving mid-level , I don't even care anymore , because the physics are so fun that going through the levels again is no big deal for me. You can always try something new.

Update#3 - 22 hours in, and I have now completed the game's story mode. As you play this game more and more, the more comfortable you get with the controls / physics and it all becomes so damn immersive. You don't have to think of controls , you just do what you want and it works most of the time. Truly a step above the VR games out there today.\t\t\t\t\t
