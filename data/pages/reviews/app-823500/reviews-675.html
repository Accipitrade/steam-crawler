\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI read that some people think that this game is better than Half Life Alyx, so I had to check it out... I wouldn't say it is necessarily better or worse, but definitely a must play.

If you spend hours picking up and throwing bottles at pidgeons in Half Life Alyx this game will blow your mind. The physics system and interactivity with the environment is a couple of leagues above Alyx's (which itself was already impressive). In Boneworks you have a proper simulated body (in contrast to just hands) and you can push yourself up ledges, actually climb ladders and climb pretty much anything that you could in real life. Also you can jump! If you know the term "invisible walls"... this game is pretty much the opposite of that. You can use anything as a weapon... even your fists. It really has to be experienced in order to be understood, because as far as I know currently there doesn't exist any other game like this one.

With the good comes the bad tough: If you are prone to motion sickness, this game will be a challenge. At times the game feels janky, because your arm gets stuck at a door preventing you to move. Or instead of pushing a heavy object, you push yourself away from it. This works all as intended, but since you don't feel the forces on your body, it can be a very disorienting experience. There is no teleportation like in HL Alyx, but only walking via stick. In other words: If you take Boneworks and remove anything that could cause motion sickness, you end up with HL Alyx. I personally had to take a few brakes from the game, but after 2-3 sessions my body magically adjusted and from that point on I had a blast with this game.

The core game is similiar to HL Alyx. There is much less of a story, but much more physics based puzzles and parts where you have to climb. The gunplay feels great. Overall I liked the combat better than in Alyx, since you have so much more layers to it (e.g. pushing enemies, grabbing head crabs midair, beating them against the wall in order to kill them, etc.).

There are some additional modes you can play after the campaign, so there is quite some replayability.

Overall there are some pro's and con's when compared to HL Alyx, but if you can stomach the motion sickness, you should definitely play this one!\t\t\t\t\t
