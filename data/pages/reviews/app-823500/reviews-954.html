\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the best VR games I've ever seen, and should be the standard for all VR games in the future.

Things I dislike:
Walking with standard Vive controllers is pretty rubish, but you get used to it.
Melee combat can be a pain, but when done correctly it is loads of fun, as in, you want to stop a guy who's about to punch you, so you grab his hand in slo motion, but you still get hit, so you restrain both his hands, and somehow him rubbing up against you gets you hit.
The Zombie guy and Ford really aren't that satisfying to punch, shoot, or kill. When you kill a null body or a head crab, you can see your impact a lot more clearly, it has a nice smooth defining texture to it all, you can easily grab and manuever a head crab to beat it up. A zombie or a Ford really aren't that satisfying though, I really wish they got something funky fresh to spice things up.
Take-downs are a bit tricky on enemies, and I really wish I could pin an enemy down while I punch him in the face repeatedly, but when I try to hold someone to the ground, they just stand back up, and I get lifted up, which really is frustrating.

Things I like:
Guns shoot good
Reloading is satisfying and not too tricky
Slow motion is great
Great story
Amazing polish and detail to everything
Climbing works amazingly
♥♥♥♥♥♥ everything is awesome

Overall one of my favorite VR games out there, and I am really excited to see where it goes in the future, a must-have for anyone with access to a VR set.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent showcase of VR interactivity and physics at an extreme level, to a point where the physics can get janky (arms clipping into objects you're holding, etc.).

If you like HL: Alyx, and are curious how its gameplay/combat might be if the realism were taken up two notches, give Boneworks a try. You can sprint, jump manually, dynamically adjust your standing/crouching height, climb ledges, and all sorts of other movement features that HL: Alyx has simplified. Some of these mechanics take practice to nail down, but you'll be executing them with relative ease once you do.

As mentioned in the opening sentence, just be warned about the body physics not working perfectly. Also, the realism is right under the border of tedium/troublesome-ness, so it might not be for everyone.

EDIT: And new features being added continuously for free (including a few levels and game modes)! Very worth supporting this game.\t\t\t\t\t
