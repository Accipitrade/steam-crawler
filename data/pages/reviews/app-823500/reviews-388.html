\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe fact that you are a physical object in this game (and understanding this) is everything when it comes to enjoying Boneworks. You'll often find yourself noticing that Newton's laws of motion apply here to an even greater extent than in real life and if you're not willing to accept that you'll struggle to a degree. Whether or not you enjoy that struggle is what will determine if you'll enjoy your time in boneworks. Sadly, the game seems to overlook its own strengths, combat and climbing, which are not at the center of its design, rather, physics puzzles, which I don't miss and are the reason I haven't even attempted a second playthrough. There is an overarching story that you could entirely miss, even if you beat the game, but finding a "Boneworks Explained" video on YouTube should straighten you out. Sandbox is a lot of fun too. All in all, yes, this is a VR Must-Have, I'm just not sure that everything it proposes is to be accepted as the right call for most VR games to follow.\t\t\t\t\t
