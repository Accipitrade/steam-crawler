\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've player about 4 hours straight, my feet are killing me. Every second was worth it, and still have some more to go.
Graphics - 7/10* - Nothing mind blowing, but it goes well enough with the "a game within a game" feel.
Combat - 7/10 - While I still resort to arm flailing in melee, the gunplay is a little sloppy, but still fun, especially with the physics.
Puzzles - 8/10 - Some are very tricky, but with some imagination and a will to win, you can find a solution, even if it wasn't originally intended to be the solution.
Story - 7/10 - Certainly more of a background for the puzzles and adventure to take place in, but had me guessing and has so many secrets to find.
VR control- 9/10 - Handles VERY well, my only issue is that you have to HOLD the buttons down when holding a melee weapon. (unless I'm missing something)

Overall - 8/10 - A VERY fun experience, and something everyone with a VR headset should give a try.

* - I experienced something I've never noticed or seen before... YOUR HANDS SHOW UP IN SHADOWS OF YOUR FLASHLIGHT. That was possibly the coolest VR graphics feature I've seen in a long while. Not to mention how when the helmet-bots catch you, they create this cool effect, as if looking through a window into another world. It's hard to describe and simply must be experienced.

VR is the future of gaming, and this, is a step in the right direction.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Edited after beating the game*
The game really lives up to the hype and the promise of a fully physics-based game! Its absolutely stellar and I'm looking forward to playing it until I find the little secrets here and there!
Gives me major Half Life vibes with what they do with puzzles and all, so have fun playing it both standing and seated!

Some Cons:
- Hand motion feels like I'm puppeteering a pair or arms rather than actively involved in the game's actions. While not terrible, it causes my arms to feel rubbery and less 1:1 interaction, which creates many issues (Arms getting stuck in Walls, Doors, Random Geometry, on Guns, and sometimes tangled in enemies when hand to hand fighting).
- Swinging your weapon back causes it to hit your back sometimes, ruining all momentum for weapon swings. This is again due to the rubber wrists this game has, and there's no way to tighten the grip.
- Climbing on top of geometry is so inconsistent, I haven't been able to achieve it more than one time. It feels like there's a tiny sweet spot for how to climb onto things, but its a lot easier to find another way around than keep trying it ¯\\_(ツ)_/¯
- On the Index, the slow-motion button is same spot as the Movement stick, so no way to move forward while slow-motion running unless you rebind stuff in the SteamVR Menu. Wish it was a toggle instead.

Aside from that, this game feels like the best tech demo we have out there, that shows that you can combine several aspects of all these VR games into a great product. Rather than specialize in one single aspect, this game puts together the best features of VR to show how they can cohesively sit together in a nice package. It ain't perfect, but its extremely fun and worth the money!
(Not for new VR users though - mainly because this game doesn't really teach you much, and expects you to have prior experience in VR in other games that require, fighting, aiming, shooting, throwing, moving around, & being used to vertigo)

Playing this on the Index.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*** MAY CONTAIN LIGHT SPOILERS***

The physics are really very very good, and it's super fun to use them to your advantage... But the story and level design leaves a lot to be desired. Many of the puzzles were downright annoying, due to the physics. For example: There's a level where a bunch of hanging platforms are suspended over a ditch. On your side is a heavy cube, and on the other side is a door and a button on the floor. The solution is pretty obvious: Drag the cube across the hanging platforms and place it on the button. The problem arises when as you're dragging the cube across, it will easily fall through the gaps between the platforms. After 3 tries at this, I gave up and had to resort to other means of getting across with something weighing as much as my character, this still took a few more tries.

A simple puzzle such as this shouldn't take half an hour to complete. I had a similar problem on another similar bridge with bars forming a bridge. I thought you'd just be able to walk across, as the gaps were so small you could realistically step over them. But nope, I fell through and had to go all the way back around and bunny hop across.

The last couple levels of the game involved about 10 cumulative minutes of climbing ♥♥♥♥ at a 90 degree angle. It felt more like a chore than a game. It felt like the game should have just stuck to being a sandbox, (which even after finishing the campaign, is still locked. Absolutely baffling)

Despite all this, there were some good moments, but I just can't justify recommending it unless they make the sandbox unlocked from the start.\t\t\t\t\t
