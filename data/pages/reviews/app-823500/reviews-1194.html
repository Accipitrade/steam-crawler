\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks so far has been an overwhelmingly enjoyable experience. The physics engine is insane. The puzzles are interesting. The gun play and combat are also very good, near perfect immersion.

I only have 2 hours on this game but I feel i already got my money's worth. however I have 2 suggestions/complaints. In the concept of realism and the future of VR and all that the rifles have the butt stock that clips on your body if you hold the gun too close. This is an amazing detail. It feels like the weapons have weight to them. However I shoot right handed but I'm left eye dominant. My right eye is so weak I literally cannot aim with it. Since I'm left eye dominant the butt stock prevents me from aiming with the rifles. I find myself consistently resorting to using UZIs and pistols. It would be a nice touch to have an option to turn off the stock clipping.

secondly the reloading system. Its nice and it works and its fluid but after playing pavlov, It would be nice to be able to push a button to drop the magazine out of a gun, especially the pistol. a little more realistic. I use the Valve Index and each hand B button opens a menu. I would be happier with the right hand B button dropping the magazines out. just a suggestion/idea.

Overall amazing game. can't wait to play more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame played with Vive (Standing), then on Quest w/ Link (Seated)
Playing with the Quest seated was a much better experience, as the controls were better, and I could relax a bit more.
I'd recommend playing seated (not on VIve).

This is a VR must-buy. The physics and interactivity are second to none. There is a lot of jank and clunkiness, but that is obviously to be expected since everything is physics-based. I got 10.9 hours on the campaign alone (around 2 hours of that was just playing around with physics).

Pros:
- Incredible physics
- Great sound design and soundtrack
- Good controls on Oculus and Index
- The best interactions you will find in any VR game
- Movement is great, and requires skill to master
- Price is great for the content
- Combat is great
- Best VR guns
- Puzzles can be solved in many different ways
- Good inventory system
- Very good melee combat system (but games like Blade and Sorcery and Hellsplit: Arena are better since that is their main focus, and because they have decapitation and dismemberment. while Boneworks doesn't).
- Because of the game's design, you can defeat enemies in any way you can think of (for example, you can use your hands, a random brick on the ground, a sword, and even your head).

Cons:
- Janky physics are inevitable
- Climbing is sometimes awkward
- Motion sickness is inevitable
- Insane CPU requirements (You're gonna need a really, really strong processor to run this game smoothly).
- No saves/checkpoints (Devs are adding this early next year).
- Little variety in guns
- Controls on Vive are very bad
- Puzzles are tedious and slow

This is a great game, but it's flawed and far from perfect. But because the game is made by a small indie team who've spent countless hours making this game, I'm willing to forgive most of these flaws. Overall, I'd give it a 8.9/10.\t\t\t\t\t
