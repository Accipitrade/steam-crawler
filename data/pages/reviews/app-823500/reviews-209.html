\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll be honest, I hated this game at first. I played this after beating Half Life: Alyx, so I went into Boneworks with the expectation that it would reward a similar style of methodical cover-based shooting. Because of this, I found myself angry at the incredibly dumb AI and often janky controls. I also dealt with a LOT of nausea - this is the first and only VR game that has really messed with me like this. Even after slowly working through it, ANY amount of alcohol combined with this game immediately gives me the spins

But there was a singular moment that changed everything for me. I was walking down a hallway listening to the muffled sounds of thumping electronic music from behind closed doors. As soon as I entered, the beat dropped and I found myself in a horde of enemies all dancing to the music. Sprinting around, shooting everything in sight, all to the tune of this incredibly hype song was an absolutely unforgettable experience and my opinion of the game did an immediate 180

This is a power-fantasy playground that gives you the tools to pretend to be John Wick. Don't think about it too hard, just sprint around like a madman and enjoy the carnage

Boneworks is just a tech demo. But if you're willing to work through the jank, this game provides a completely unique and ridiculously fun experience. This will go down as a defining moment in VR history

Also Mike Diva wooooo\t\t\t\t\t
