\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was a bit skeptical prior to release despite being amazed by the physics. I decided to take the plunge when the game came out, and here's what I think;

All in all, fantastic! A big step up in the potential of VR games. The first day I played with Vive wands, and the very next day, my Index Controllers came in. Honestly, both control excellently, even if the Index Controllers just come out on top. It really feels like these guys learned a lot about what works and what doesn't work in VR through the last few years, and made one of the best experiences and engines we've seen yet. Some of the things I wound up doing gave me that feeling I got when I first got my Vive in 2016 where I crack some of the biggest smirks. When the game gets its groove on, it GETS its groove on. When it doesn't? Well, it can be frustrating.

Admittedly, given the busy day I had when getting the game, I sort of rushed through the tutorial, but some actions felt clunkier than they should. While the gunplay is fantastic and many of the melee weapons feel badass, some melee weapons/actions just don't feel right. Then again, I'm one of the few people who can't get into Blade & Sorcery and feel it's too clunky, while everyone else seems to love it. I also find myself often getting one of my arms caught on some geometry and not knowing what the hell happened. After all these days, I also still can't figure out how to climb over objects properly too. There is a bit of a learning curve to how you interact, but it seems like some players on Youtube were a lot smoother than myself. I'll also say that the final few stages weren't great, but it picks back up prior to ending. My initial startup with the game after the excellent tutorial was hit and miss, but I identified pretty soon that the big issue is that you should not be concerned with getting through the game. Experiment. Look around, and mess around with your abilities. I just finished today but I know there is a lot of weapons, items, and tools that I missed. I wish I knew how to help people who get motion sickness, but I haven't felt that in VR since I was new to it.

In conclusion, this is a great package that is worth the asking price of 30 bucks. I don't know if we'll ever get mod support, substantial improvements to the gameplay, more firearms (like shotguns) or if others will get to use the engine for other titles, but I'm sure at the very least, many talented devs will take notes. Now, let's see if Stress Level Zero can resurrect Hover Junkers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks? More like Bonerworks!

One of the best VR game I've played so far and I have over 100.
It's got some issues but overall it feels amazing to actually be able to physically interact with stuff in VR.
It's got melee weapon mechanics from B&S and gun mechanics of Onward/Pavlov and so much more.
I was expecting it to be mostly a tech demo but I'm 9 hours in so far of solid gameplay including FPS elements but also physics puzzles.

I might go into more detail eventually, and probably update my review once I've finished the game.

As a final note I'm also a game developer currently working on various VR projects. Games like these are inspiring.
