\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the most mixed feeling review for a game I have ever written

Positive:

Graphics 10/10: not the prettiest looking game I have ever seen, but it certainly looks great and doesn't destroy my computor!

Story 10/10: I almost thought this would just rip off half life 2, but they actually created a really cool universe here.

gameplay 8/10: rough start, but it gets good fairly quickly. This game also has some of the best gun mechanics I have seen in a vr game.

Soundtrack 10/10: Very well made! I don't think I've ever heard lofi being used in an action game before. Analog sunrise and Bone Theme are my personal favorites


Negative:

My biggest pet peeve with this entire game is the fact that you have to unlock things in order to use them in sandbox mode. I was so pissed when I figured that out. I had just seen all these videos of people doing cool stuff in sandbox mode only to learn that it has to be manually unlocked using the reclamation bins. The whole point of having a sandbox mode is to allow players who want to experience the game without going through all the hassle of a story mode to mess around without any rules. This wouldn't be that big of an issue if they didn't make it so difficult to carry things to the reclamation bins. There is only one per level to my knowledge and they are usually placed toward the end. I have to carry anything I want to save all the way over there. There are also some walls you need to pass through that DELETE YOUR ITEMS. Not only is that taken from portal, but it also makes getting items for sandbox mode a nightmare. I can get behind the collecting items system, but the fact that it is required in order to use those items in sandbox really sucks.

Why is my ammunition the currency? This is a game with temptingly fun gunplay and it decides to punish me for using guns... The best way to gain ammo is by destroying the little ammo crates that are hiding in every nook and cranny. also, The game did not even tell me that the ammo was what I used to buy things in the first place. I only learned that from reading other reviews.

For a physics-based game, Boneworks has some wonky physics. Just because the physics are complicated doesn't make them realistic. all the heavy objects don't feel heavy, just unstable. They are all wobbly and I usually need two hands on the object to control it properly. In real life, I could hold a sledgehammer with one hand without it wobbling around. Not saying the physics have to be accurate to the real world, but I feel like they should at least be easy to manage. Even some of the light objects like hammers can feel unstable. This leads to...

The melee combat varies is quality depending on the situation. Sometimes I can easily clobber and enemy over the head and knock them to the ground in one hit. Other times they just shake the blow off without taking damage while also attacking me. Close combat is not as satisfying as the trailers make it out to be and the weird object physics contributes to this. Also, punching and slapping does like no damage which means melee weapons are essential.

Climbing onto objects is annoying with these Bennett Foddy-ass grabbing controls.

The game has kind of a rough start. the museum and streets levels are very boring and even a little confusing at times. They should have made the first two levels the most interesting to get players to continue playing. I almost gave this game a negative review because of these two levels. I guess the museum was sort of a tutorial and didn't really need to be interesting, but the streets just felt empty and I got lost a few times because there are some parts that loop back to the beginning of the level.

there are some items that can't go in the inventory. I guess it's because they aren't weapons, but I don't want to carry an object across the entire map just to be able to collect it for sandbox mode. I just want my comically large pill!

Other notes:

Keep a sledgehammer in your inventory at all times. Not only are they amazing weapons, but I have also managed to scale tall ledges with them, allowing me to skip some parts of the level with ease. Yeah... I wasn't kidding when I said this game had Bennett Foddy climbing mechanics.

the enemies don't seem to understand how stairs work.

I have been jumpscared by nullbodies several times

I like the duck season references that are hidden throughout the game.

Not exactly the games fault, but my oculus quest 2 keeps having periodic lag spikes when connected to my computer. This makes the clunky melee combat even clunkier
_________________________________________________________
8/10
not anything like I hoped for from watching the trailers and gameplay videos, but still a decent and immersive Vr game

January 2023 edit: I gave the campaign mode a second chance, and I actually really liked the story. Bumping the score up to 8/10 from 7/10\t\t\t\t\t
