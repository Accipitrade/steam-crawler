\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is in a word... Awesome! It high lights why every VR game needs to be physics based!

There was this one puzzle where I was standing on a platform and there was a big button that you need to put a heavy box on to open a doorway that was high up. SO I dragged the box onto the button and the door opened... There was a rope but I couldn't drop the box and grab the rope fast enough and when I tried to run and jump up to the edge it was just out of reach. It was obvious that I needed another box to stand on but that was a problem that would have taken a long time to figure out.

So I looked at my inventory and I saw I had an axe and I thought I wonder so I pulled it out and my hand was half way up the axe handle. So I gently tossed it up without letting it go completely and slid my hand down the handle two or three times till I was at the bottom of the axe handle. (That alone blew my mind because it's the first time in any VR game that I could do something like that)

I setup, I ran and jumped towards the wall and swung the axe to hit on top of the floor to use it like a hook. To my surprise it worked. I was then hanging there from the axe which was hooked onto the floor above! I was then able to pull myself up and grab that rope and pull myself up the rest of the way! Now I'm sure this wasn't the way they envisioned this puzzle to be solved because what if I didn't have that axe? It's like though if it makes sense and it works with the physics then why not! This blew me away!

There are a couple things I think could be done differently or features I'd like to see like, the ability to pull yourself up when you get two hands on a ledge. You can pull yourself up to your waist but there's no way to getup the rest of the way and I would have like to seen that even if it was just a push button animation of you standing up! There are other areas where I fell through the cracks between the floor and again I was unable to pull myself back up even though I could pull up to the point where my feet were literally 2 inches below the floor and in reality I could easily just step back onto the floor but I managed to get out by going through the crack while holding onto pipes and getting out that way but not after trying to get out the logical way for 10 min which kinda made me sick a bit in the attempt! So that could be better. But all in all this is the best VR game I've played to date and I hope that all developers going forward understand the importance of physics in VR games. Passing your hand or body through walls just doesn't cut it anymore! Everything needs to be physics based!

So I highly recommend this game. If Stress Level Zero ever make a serious military shooter with those gun physics they will destroy games like Onward which I love but would be worlds better with physics!! I know I've died a few times from clipping through walls which at this point should be not possible in future VR games that are done right!

I've only made it through streets so far but I'm excited to tackle the next level and see what other unique ideas I can come up with to solve some of their puzzles! Awesome job guys and if HLA is as good oh boy I can't wait!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn my opinion this is by far the best VR game to date. Long campaign, cool subtle story and awesome physics gameplay with really fun puzzles, weapons and combat mechanics. My one major complaint about the game is the controls for the HTC vive wands. This game was clearly made with valve index controls in mind and rather than adjusting the controls for the vive wands they kept the same basic concept where you have to constantly press down on both the trigger and the side buttons to maintain your grip on an object. This is completely unintuitive and the vive wands were not made to do this as the side buttons are almost flush with the controller, they're also relatively small and require considerable force to press making it virtually impossible to maintain your grip on both the trigger and side buttons for an extended length of time especially while moving. This game really needs to allow you to maintain your grip with a single button press if you're using the vive wands.

I got to a point in the game where my only weapon choice was a two handed axe and I had to defend myself against very small agile jumping enemies and it was virtually impossible to keep both the trigger and the side bottons on the vive wands pressed and so I kept losing grip of my axe and I died over and over again. To give you an idea of how bad and frustrating the vive controls are for this game a couple weeks ago I ordered a valve index vr kit but my delivery has been delayed until January. Because I love this game so much rather than waiting an entire month before I can get good controls I actually ordered a second pair of valve index controllers which should be delivered in the next few days and this cost me over $400 canadian dollers. I spent an extra $400 on controllers that I'd already be getting a month later just so I don't have to deal with the god awful vive controls in this game for another month.

As for other more minor complaints two handed firearms are just a little bit awkward. Normally for vr shooters I use a gun stock but for this game the gun stock doesn't work perfectly. When I use the MP5 sub machine gun with the gun stock it floats and twitches around in a weird way, I get the same problem with the M4 style rifles but it's not nearly as bad and the MP5K works perfectly fine with a gun stock. I think this has something to do with how your body is an actual physics object in the game and the rifles in game make actual contact with your in game body. This is actually a good thing when you're not using a gun stock as this creates a "virtual" gun stock in the game that allows you to shoot more accurately with rifles but when you're using a real life gun stock it just makes your weapon in game line up not quit right with your body and it starts jittering around making it more difficult to aim. This isn't so bad but it's a little annoying as I prefer to use a gun stock as it makes shooting feel a little more real. Other games have implemented a similar "virtual" gun stock system to make it easier to aim rifles without using an actual gun stock and I think the system that the game Onward uses just works better than Boneworks.

My only other small complaint is the save system. Currently you can't save your progress within a level so if you leave the game mid mission you have to start all over again which is a little annoying. This isn't that big of a deal for some of the earlier missions but as the game goes on the levels become longer making it more annoying.

Despite some of these flaws the game is still fun and even if you can't afford to get valve index controllers I'd say the game is still worth playing, it's just that you will eventually get to sections of the game that are extremely frustrating due to the retarded vive controls. All the developers need to do is allow constant grip with a one click button press and the problem is solved for vive users and that's really the only major complaint I have with the game. Although I mostly only mentioned negative things in this review the game is incredibly fun, I'm just focusing on the negatives for constructive criticism. If you've seen any of the trailers or gameplay expect the game to be even more fun when you're actually playing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCombining fun mechanics from other games doesn't forgive the exceedingly poor gameplay, design, and engineering decisions that ruin this experience.

Jumping puzzle challenges with crappy frustrating locomotion control and no quicksave - FAIL
Trying to write off your inability to create a save system as a quirky fun thematic thing - FAIL
Moving forward in development without quicksaves, or saves of any kind, and then creating marathon levels that take an hour or more to complete - FAIL
Your character cannot traverse a 6 inch obstacle without a running start, and you can't jump in place over it without bouncing back because of lazy clunky physics that is ironically the selling point of the game - FAIL
NPCs also cannot traverse a 6 inch obstacle without falling flat on their face, standing up and tumbling over again repeatedly, until they die, and this made it through development and testing and release - FAIL
Let's mount the camera on a spring because we hate you - FAIL
1,000 lb crate can't be lifted but tips over with the slightest touch like a cardboard box - FAIL
Realistic weapons with amazing details like nonfunctioning fire selectors locked in the wrong position - FAIL
A virtual stock that puts the weapon's rear sight on your chin and prevents you from aiming the gun - FAIL
HK weapons but no HK slap - FAIL
Orange barrels that don't explode, I mean, are you new here? - FAIL
Ammo crates that take 2-3+ hits to break open, then still requires contact with the ammo but not just with a weapon, has to be your hand, 10 seconds of annoying interaction - FAIL
Insert 700 magazines one at a time to get the gun you just had in your hand, a practically impossible interaction, made it through design, development, testing, and release - FAIL
Terrible looping pop song from someone's friend's indie band playing forever on radio props that can't be destroyed - FAIL
Teasing you with a nice beautiful, bright, green, expansive, sunny outdoor scene (at least twice!) before the cold splash of water that is 15 year old level editor placeholder textures used as a design aesthetic for a game that already suffers from thin-pretext-for-your-tech-demo syndrome. Maybe you get there later in the game but I would guess for only another tease - FAIL
Weapons in your holsters/inventory don't remember your grip position, so have fun with 5 seconds of fumbling to make any melee weapon usable every single time you pick it up - FAIL
Even though you're running constantly in this game, there's no always-run option, and running requires an additional button press or time consuming double-tap thing, and you have to deal with this every single time you move in the game, forever - FAIL
Body inventory system for ammo that you can't always access while crouching, you know like in a firefight, without intersecting your real world body - FAIL
Climbing with no inertial flinging - FAIL
Use anything as a weapon! / Holster these 4 items only - FAIL
Recalibration that you have to confirm 2 or 3 times each time you start the game - FAIL
Even if that's useful for multiple players, the game doesn't really support multiple players because there's no saving - FAIL
Slowmo bullet time feature that is input-mapped in a way so that you can't use it while moving, meaning after 5 hours I used it one time during the tutorial to discover that it's useless. People say this is a balance feature to prevent slowmo from being overpowered, but if that's really their solution to balance instead of just metering it, then what else can be said - FAIL


Don't mean to be rough, but once I started listing things, it just kept coming. There are some things this game does well, and it's great to see full scale VR games coming out with such a response from the market. But I'm halfway through the game and faced with replaying over an hour, and realized, that actually doesn't sound like fun at all and really I just don't care to do that. Probably will play some more in the future, and I'll keep an eye on patches because I'm sure these are common complaints they're getting, but right now this game has enough minor annoyances to make it a chore to play. Maybe I'll unlock the sandbox mode, which requires a series of steps so obscure that as many people will guess it as will drop 700 magazines one at a time into an item dispenser, but without custom maps I think I've already exhausted that experience in the tutorial level.

Here's some stuff that wasn't so bad:

Pretty unique texture/displacement detail blending makes for some really great looking surfaces at times - NEAT
Gunplay can be fun and snappy, if you overlook the fact that you're shooting a currency - NEAT
Running and jumping can work well in some cases, and the climbing/crouch control also has a certain charm - NEAT
I did play it for several hours in a row despite these frustrations, I knowingly went past the window for returns - NEAT
Some nice references and easter eggs in there - NEAT
Realistic price - NEAT\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game that, even if the story turns out to be a little bland, is worth it just for the incredible physics & immersion.

As mentioned in other reviews the body does sometimes get in the way, and climbing is not very intuitive as your legs will get stuck behind some obstacle requiring you to crouch to climb on stuff which feels counter intuitive.
Though i would not consider this an issue bad enough to not recommend the game, and I feel like some minor tweaking could easily resolve this.

Despite having absolutely no issues with VR-sickness, due to various reasons I have difficulty enjoying VR for more than about 1-1.5hrs at a time. This is usually due to getting sore eyes (both standing and sitting).

With almost every game I have played this is no issue at all, and I can play for about 1hr, take a break for a few hours, and then continue just fine.
However, in the current state of the game, the only savepoints are at the end of each level/chapter, which, at my current (exploratory) pace, takes more than 1.5hrs to complete.

Currently this means that for me to progress in the game, i have to either rush through a level and not really enjoy myself, or play the final ~30min of a level with severe eyestrain. Not ideal.

Not to mention the fact that if for some reason you end up dying, you also get reset all the way back to the start of the level, which can be extremely frustrating, especially if you died due to some issue with the controls/your legs getting stuck somewhere.
(when playing seated you have to crouch with the same joystick/pad you use to rotate, so it is easily possible to accidentally stand up when looking around)

The game is pretty good when I can enjoy it, and the museum at the start is very cool, however in its current state I cannot progress in the game well enough to really enjoy it.

Please add quicksaving/more save points/some other way to save progress other than at the end of each chapter/level.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a first impressions review. I intend to update it when i can be bothered progressing more. I've played games where theres heavy handed tutorials agonisingly stretched over the start of the game. It's a turnoff for me when a game needs more than 5 minutes to explain the basics explicitly. I don't mind when its tooltips over the course of a normal level, but we're talking about training scenarios hand crafted simply to introduce boring, basic concepts. Things like "in order to crouch, crouch" wow! Thats legitimately something game teaches you. Theres even a part that the game reminds you that to turn around, you can turn around. Amazing. I havent reached the part where it instructs me to blink yet, but i'm sure its coming. At the same time it frequently reminds you of all the fat baby options. Snap turn, crouching, force grab and more are repeatedly hammered home just in case you were worried that you'd need to get off your fat ass for an hour and look around with your skull. I swear i am not joking when i say, that i was given a big warning when i chose standing play. Are you sure? As if i had chose nightmare mode in Doom. If i sound overly negative, it's because i'm anxious to start. I want to get past all of the boring basic rubbish (all of this should be skippable).

The tech is impressive at the start. I wanted to embrace it fully, but i found that for every awesome immersive thing, theres also a negative that takes you out again. Cool, you can brush aside a pizza box or pick it up, or destroy it. Can you open it? No, are you crazy? Who would try to open a pizza box? The melee has a certain weight to it, simulating the heft of a sledgehammer or large obstacle. The drawback (a large drawback) is that this causes *intentional* desync with your hands. As in, you try to swing the hammer fast, but it lags due to being heavy, which is fine except, your hands are no longer being tracked accurately. So it feels like a 2 way relationship. The game simulates physics accurately sometimes, and other times, you need to roleplay or mime that "oh gosh this is heavy". For it to work, there would need to be physical resistance that you fight against, which is obviously not currently possible. So why chose this thing that doesnt work? Climbing is rubbish too. It's no better at all than climbey, your in game wrists breaking and phasing through walls and handholds, makes it quite QWOP-like. Your hands slam into the wall and get stuck and you have to pull it off without letting go with the other hand. Etc. Etc.

Sound design was noticeable in that theres tweeting and chirping and bleeping of electronics and junk that really irritated me in the first section or so. I found myself rushing hoping that the next area would be quieter. the next area was filled with the most annoying music instead.

As i said, early review, but i observed all of this in less than half an hour before i went back to VR chat. I bet that it gets a lot more exciting later on. I am not sure if it will get less frustrating, but i'm sure it couldn't be any more boring than what i have seen so far.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe physics, gameplay, story, environment and everything in this game is amazing! It's worth getting for that.

I wish I could recommend it and would recommend it if there was more comfort settings.

I play games with full locomotion like Pavlov, Onward, etc. and don't get motion sick at all.

With Boneworks I regularly get motion sick after 20-30 min, been playing VR since it came out with original Vive so have developed a pretty good VR tolerance.

There is no way this game would work with teleport mode so I understand why that's not an option.

I think basic comfort settings like comfort vignette (especially when you are jumping or falling) would in no way ruin the game but make it a million times better. I don't see any reason why comfort vignetting isn't an option especially in such a major release like this.

I've tested this game on both Rift S and a Valve Index with the same results.

Also the game really needs more frequent save points or ability to save anywhere, still haven't passed first area and get motion sick before being able to complete and then have to start all over again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclosure: This game was reviewed with the Valve Index.

Boneworks is very impressive Virtual Reality game that delivers on the hype it generated but not without a small series of flaws. The level of freedom and creativity you have to solve problems and deal with enemies feels near limitless and could not be replicated on a keyboard and mouse. The game’s immersion and gameplay feels so good that it really makes flat screen shooters start to feel even more outdated than current quality VR titles already do, to the degree of what PS and N64 did to Genesis and SNES in the 90s.
 
Below are three categories that encapsulate my thoughts about BoneWorks. You will read what I loved about the game, my neutral thoughts that I think are worth sharing, and areas of the game that I think need further development and/or refinement. Make no mistake about it, Boneworks is a title that continues to push Virtual Reality boundaries and showcases the incredible potential VR gamers have been talking about for years.
 
What I loved:
- The near limitless ways to handle enemies. You can hold weaponless enemies back with one hand while you shoot them with the other. You can bash their skulls against the wall. You can catch and throw headcrabs against the floor, objects, walls, or against other enemies. You can take out an human enemy’s knee caps with a sledge hammer and then pound away at their head with your fists. You can hold an enemies head back with one hand and stab them with a knife in the other hand (like the Walking Dead!). Did your rifle run out of ammo and the enemy is near by? No worries, just grab the front of your rifle and swing it like a baseball bat as the butt stock smashes your enemy’s face. If you can think it, you can probably do it in Boneworks.
- The interactive environments and puzzles give players a number of different ways to progress forward. You can get super creative at times and there never seems to be a wrong answer.
- There are a ton of fun easter eggs, some of which are Half-Life related. There are also a handful of secret rooms. Props to SLZ for adding in Solid Snake’s cardboard box ;)
- I love the way SLZ added weight to objects. It works in most cases well but some refinement is needed for weapons that feel rubbery.
- Being able to move objects without grabbing them is a lot more fun than I ever thought it could be and is actually practical during combat.
- The soundtrack for the game is surprisingly good and fits the ambiance of the game environments perfectly.
- There are certain things we have been doing in video games for years that SLZ flipped the scrip on. For example, why smash through wooden boards blocking a doorway when you can pull them apart? Dozens of small details like this add to the overall experience and give players a sense of immersion that we haven’t seen to date.
 
Neutral Thoughts:
- The story is not spoonfed but rather uncovered through careful observation of the environment and clues carefully placed throughout the levels. The story didn’t feel very original but some may be attracted to “evil corporation did a bad thing and I must destroy them” which feels a bit overdone, IMO.
- The enemies are not much of a challenge and are instead there for your enjoyment. The AI is pretty stupid in most cases. It is not uncommon to see enemies trip over their fallen comrades or over objects on the floor which can be a bit disappointing for some.
- The way weight is implemented in the game requires some training and getting used to. If the IRL player perceives that they are stronger than the in-game character, it can result in some wonky melee and climbing mechanics. This can be very confusing for new players or those like me who can actually swing a hammer faster then the in-game character can.  
- The slow-motion button is placed on the left controller which means moving while in slow motion is near impossible. I’m not sure if this was a deliberate choice of the developer to avoid abuse of the feature…but it is what it is.
- Some environments are bland and boring while some are vibrant and fun to explore.
- Some puzzles are a challenge and some are just frustrating due to limitations and wonky physics mechanics. Other puzzles can be easily figured out in seconds.
 
Area’s for future Development:
- There a bit too much “bounciness” in some aspects of the physics engine, mostly when climbing objects, ladders and when navigating tight corridors and spaces. Though I didn’t experience motion sickness, I can easily see others having that issue. I think the dev team should explore allowing player clipping in some cases. You might want to replicate how climbing is done in H3VR.
- Progress is saved when the player completes a full level. The problem with this is that some levels took me up to an hour and there no option to quick-save progress. This decision seems a bit lazy.
- There is no pause button which again appears to be a lazy decision.
- Because of the weight feature, some weapons feel rubbery (like Gorn). I think the team may want to examine having a 1:1 ratio with some weapons, despite it not being completely realistic. I have held an M16 IRL and it does not bounce around in my hands. Snap movement also exacerbates the issue.
- Having to place magazine, after magazine, after magazine, after magazine in the vending machine to obtain a weapon is needlessly tedious. I would recommend having the player deposit one magazine and just deduct the full cost from the player’s account. This is a serious issue for late game items that cost thousands.
- Gun stocks shouldn’t get hung up on the shoulder or chest of the player. I know it adds to the realistic feel of holding a gun stock up against one’s shoulder, but I would prefer something that mimics Pavlov’s gun play. This is one of those cases where allowing clipping might make more sense.
- To release a weapons magazine, you have to hold the menu button down and point your controller slightly up to a menu option that says release mag. It is not very intuitive, a pain to deal with, and breaks immersion. This is easily one of the most baffling and poor design choices I’ve seen in any VR game to date. This has to change.
- I’d like to see a larger area in which weapons can be holstered and unholstered. I dropped way too many weapons and struggled to grab some in a time of panic. Holstering and grabbing guns while crouched is an absolute nightmare. Again, I would turn to Pavlov or H3VR as a source of inspiration.
 
Overall, I would rate this game as a solid 8.5/10. It’s a great single player experience worth coming back to after completing the main campaign. I do not think that this game is going to age very well through the years, in fact I can see us looking back in 5 years laughing about the wonkiness of the engine and how far we’ve come since Boneworks’ release. That being said, Boneworks lays down a solid foundation for the future of Virtual Reality and gives players an experience they cannot get anywhere else on the market.
 
If you don’t own VR, I wouldn’t recommend buying it just for Boneworks, however if you own VR, Boneworks is on the (currently) very short list of absolute must have VR games. Well done SLZ and hats off to Brandon Laatsch for your countless hours of work and dedication. I cannot wait to see what comes next.\t\t\t\t\t
