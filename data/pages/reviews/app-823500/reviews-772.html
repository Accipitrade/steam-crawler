\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest VR game ever made, even better than Half Life: Alyx. This game does everything you could ever want in a VR game, on a technical level at least. Everything has physics, even your own body, which makes the game honestly pretty unpredictable but at the same time really replayable. Once you have enough ammo to unlock the utility gun, you can basically turn every level in the campaign into a sandbox. Climbing is fun, shooting is realistic and feels really cool, and the movement is pretty fluid, I can't really relate to the people who get motion sickness from this game since this was my second VR game and I didn't feel anything. Lucky me, I guess! You can play this game really quickly and rush through hordes of enemies or you can take it slow and explore the world and find a lot, and I mean A LOT, of secrets, these are basically spawnables that you can later spawn with your utility gun, which basically turns the game into Garry's Mode at that point, definitely not a bad thing though. One thing I don't really like about this game is that there is a low amount of enemy variety, there are basically only three types. 1.) Headcrabs, 2.) humanoid NPCs and 3.) Omni Projectors. You could say that the Fords are their own type because they behave quite differently and aren't really an enemy, but whatever. All of the enemies, however, are very well designed and fun to fight against / play with, mainly due to the many ways you can kill or interact with them. That's also why i'm so sad that there are only this few enemies, because the engine they made has endless possibilities and I can think of a bunch of different enemy types they could introduce, but hey, SLZ is a studio of only 11 people (I think, I could be wrong) so it's still pretty impressive for what they've done with Unity. The story flies by your face most of the time and can only really be understood through looking at really hidden lore throughout the game, so you won't exactly know what the story is when you first play through it, and even when you get the story, it's really not all that interesting, in my opinion. It isn't a bad or cringeworthy story though, it's just mediocre, which I much prefer over a cringey one. I'm way more of a gameplay-oriented person anyways, so yeah, I don't care at all if the story isn't the best of best.

And yes, i'm gonna say it;

Stress Level Zero's modified Unity Engine > Source 2\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo this day, still my favorite VR experience of all time. Hands down. It might even be one of my top 10 games of all time.

BONEWORKS is a masterclass in VR innovation. Everything a VR game should be. It was clear from playing that the goal of this game was to bring the best parts of every standalone VR experience and bring them into one full game. There have been plenty of games that let you climb, plenty of games with realistic guns, plenty of games with a fully visible body, plenty of games with realistic physics. In BONEWORKS, everything is there and more. The selling point of this game by far is the physics, along with the fact that you (the player) have physics. Gone are the days of being accustomed to phasing through objects, as your hands, head, body, and legs all are physics objects that interact with the world. This means you can use your hands to shove an enemy away or even climb up objects you normally wouldn't be able to grip. Objects feel like they have real weight, combat feels impactful and satisfying, traversing the world is easy and every path is the right path. I could go on and on about how much the gameplay speaks to me, and that's just story mode! I still go back to arena mode and sandbox to this day, my friends are particularly fond of the zombie arena. I will say that this game is best enjoyed using the Valve Index controllers, as it was developed with those in mind.

The story of BONEWORKS is still rather in dispute, as it wasn't clear cut given to us. BONEWORKS chooses to tell its story in the background, while allowing us to focus on gameplay. This does mean, however, that your first playthrough might result in a "Huh?" at the end credits. Actually going through the game and paying close attention to the cutscenes, video messages, and text on the walls that explain an in depth story about a double agent, the dangers of void energy, and a search for immortality, but I won't say much more than that. The story manages to not only stand on its own, but also tie back to SLZ's previous game "Duck Season". Duck Season had a lot of mystery and lore that could not be explained until the community picked apart BONEWORK's lore and explained much of it. I am personally excited to see where the Duck Season/BONEWORKS universe goes next when SLZ releases their next game. Love the games story, but it does lose some points for being super missable on your first playthrough.

In my entire life, I have only bought a soundtrack after playing the game to it twice. The second time was "No Straight Roads" (which is unfortunately not on Steam yet), but the first was BONEWORKS. Michael Wyckoff, who also created the soundtrack for the other SLZ games, Hover Junkers and Duck Season, crushed the soundtrack for BONEWORKS. It oozes industrial synthwave and fits every single part of the game it's included in.

I just adore everything about this game, and it's not even made by a big studio! Stress Level Zero literally only contains less than 40 people and managed to put out a masterpiece. I wish I could nominate it for more than one steam award, because I wanna nominate it for nearly all of them. Am I gushing a little too much? Probably, but for the sake of finality, I will say this. If you own a VR headset and are acclimated to games that use locomotion movement and are not prone to VR sickness, this is a must buy game.\t\t\t\t\t
