\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTruly the best vr experience yet. Some may argue alyx because of the polish, but this is the most fleshed out game in terms of features. There is no BS menus to pick guns from. It is all physical interactions. It takes advantage of the medium and gives you a sense of world, if you catch my drift. I feel like it is the VR equivalent of show don't tell. Menu usage bogs down the user experience resulting in a dissatisfied customer. Having interactions to navigate most things like weapon selection and such is great. It also doesn't overdo the realism allowing for hardcore and casual users alike to enjoy the experience. The reloading isn't based on persistent mags that need refilled with bullets, but doing that in this experience would remove some of the fun. Not to be the guy comparing to Alyx, but the combat in this game is much more refined. Guns have a satisfying punch, and there is enough variety to keep the player engaged. Alyx there is three guns that get a bit repetitive and there is a boring menu selection process to change guns. The melee system is also arguably the most advanced seen in vr yet with cutting implements being capable of both slicing and stabbing. The player can feel the weight of the melee weapons given the physics system at play. Whereas in Alyx there is no melee. The enemies are the cherry on top. They pose a large challenge for those new to VR. For more experienced users, messing with the AI and grabbing them to toy around is hilariously fun. They react to each bullet put into them, and each enemy has its own counter. They arent bullet sponges like some aforementioned HL:A enemies. This all combines to make the smoothest VR combat in a singleplayer game to date. That doesn't mean that the whole game is peaches and roses though. Some really cool mechanics that are presented in the tutorial level(zipline and such) are never seen again in the game. This is pretty disappointing. The other thing is on occasion the physics will bet a bit weird and you can get your hand stuck in a weird spot. There is certainly a level of jank with climbing mechanics espescially. Thats not to forget that the inverse kinematics body is still the best ever seen in a VR game so far. Overall, the game is a solid must buy until the bar for physics and FPS combat is raised in VR. It isn't perfect, and those with less than 2-3 weeks of VR should completely avoid it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy favorite VR game so far. Its been the most immersive VR experience I've ever had. There are games that do guns a bit better (H3VR) and games that do melee a bit better (Blade and sorcery), but those are both essentially arena combat games. This has a proper campaign, although the actual story line is obfuscated, which did leave me a bit confused on my initial playthrough. Not only that but boneworks does both melee combat as well as shooting very well. I've yet to find a game that has both satisfying melee, and well functioning guns.

If youre willing to fully explore each environment, connect some dots, and if youre like me, read up on the lore a bit, the story is actually pretty interesting.

In my own personal opinion, HL:A has a better story line, but this game wins in immersion, and was more satisfying for me to play. that being said, that is not the experience a lot of other people have.

If youre going to play this game, keep in mind it's recommended that you already have your VR legs. When I first started playing, I had to take a 5-10 minute break every 20-30 minutes. If you start to feel nauseous, take a break. trying to "fight through it" will most likely leave you feeling ill and not enjoying VR.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn absolute VR masterpiece, but with many flaws.
As soon as you start the game it warns you it's for "experienced VR players only". A little bit strage, considering the tutrorial area of the game clocks in at over an hour and the concept is quite easy to grasp. Really it's just code for "you will get motion sickness really fast". I recommend "walking in place" to somewhat fool your body you are actually running - that way it won't be as bad and you will be able to play it for over 30 minutes. Also, if you play this during multiple sittings across multiple days it will get gradually better.
You are basically immidiatly thrown into the VR world and it's very easy to understand the gamplay basics.It's quite fun just to mess with certain elements in a level and try to archive something - even if you know it's not really what you are "meant" to do in a certain area. The game definitely encourages trying out those things.
At first, it seems like a mysterious game with lots of "hidden" puzzles and stuff to find. In reality though, as the game progresses there are basically no puzzles at all and it turns into a generic shooter really fast. At most the game awards you with collectibles or unique weapons in crates in areas that are tricky to reach. It's not necessary bad, but also not particulary unique or challenging. Enemies are dumb, but really they have to be. Reloading your weapons in VR and fighting can be really challenging. I think it manages to be perfectly balanced for a fun experience. It would probably be very frustrating if it would be any harder and you die just because your weapon got stuck on a corner wall or you dropped your axe or instead of grabing ammunition you grab another weapon out of your inventory.
There are a few things I really think they should have been adressed:
* Collectibles take up inventory slots. And you only have 2 for heavy weapons, 2 for hand guns and one back pocket. You also have to carry them through the whole level to "save" them. Just to the next save point isn't enough. So you can basiaclly only carry like 2 of them.
* Putting weapons in your back pocket has a 33% chance of you dropping it on the ground. You can perfectly position your hand/controller on your back but sometimes it will just not register. You only know this because the sound of a "zipper" doesn't play.
* How did climbing even get though quality assurance? It's beyound janky.
The ability to remote-grab objects like the "Accio" spell in Harry Potter really helps this game though. It makes it less frustrating and less physically demanding to play. It only works on competely uncovered items though, which can be quite annoying if an ammunition pack is just covered by a piece of wood from the crate it was in.
The game also has some sort of story. It tries to be somewhat mysterious and give you hints of what's happening through graffity and noteboards scattered around. Somewhat like "Portal". Really though it's not very deep or interesting and you aren't really able to "identify" with your character or it's goal. I'm inclined to believe it might be better of if it was just completely scratched.
I still really like this game and recommend it to any VR player to see what's possible. I really would have liked if it was either a completely quirky puzzle game / sandbox or a serious mystery game. It kinda does a little bit of both but not really.
I'm also not sure what to think of the physics. On one hand, you can do almost anything. You can grab objects, destory them, interlock them, jump on them and many things more. On the other hand, grabbing a table with one hand and dragging it along reminds me of Goat Simulator. Objects really don't have much weight. Or being able to just climb certain random objects or parts of the level. But on the other hand it's also really hard to do things that would be easy in real life, such as putting two crates on top of each other, climbing them and jumping to a higher level - an almost impossible task in Boneworks.
So sadly it isn't really a sanbox game, it isn't really a puzzle game, it isn't really a mystery game and it isn't really a shooter game. It's something inbetween.
Grab it on sale and see what's possible on your VR headset. Play it for fun but don't set your expectations too high.\t\t\t\t\t
