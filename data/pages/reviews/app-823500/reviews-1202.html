\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNeeds some work, but you can tell a ton of hard work and passion went into building this. I love the physics; They're amazing and are quite obviously the future for VR games. That said, with how similar this game is to Half-Life, I'm a little afraid for the developers. Half-Life: Alyx might prove this title to be redundant even though there will probably only be two games of this caliber on the market come March. We'll have to wait and see on that though, and I'd say it's definitely not worth waiting. This is still an incredibly fun sandbox worth your time and money. In my experience I saw these major issues:

1. Performance. In open areas my framerate nosedives unless I turn on adaptive resolution, but then I can't really see anything that's not directly in front of me. For reference I'm running a 1080Ti and i7.
2. Checkpoints. If you die anywhere on a level, you'll start all the way at the beginning, but nothing will have changed. Enemies remain dead, puzzles, remain solved. Just a boring walk back to where you were.
3. There aren't many (or any) comfort options for less accustomed VR players. At least include an option to decrease the FOV when moving for them. Teleporting could be an option alongside smooth movement as well (though you'd still need to be able to use free movement due to the game design)
4. Impact doesn't carry over to touching objects. For example if a piece of box debris is sitting on top of another box and you hit it with a sledgehammer, the box below the debris won't break when it obviously should. This sounds like a minor gripe but it actually gets to be pretty annoying.

Even with these issues the game was a blast, and I look forward to coming back to Sandbox mode.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is exactly what I was hoping to see since I got my HMD. Mobility is often restricted in VR games, but not in this. You can run, jump, climb, vault and explore to your heart's content. For this reason, people who get motion sickness might get uncomfortable, but it is definitely liberating if you don't have a problem with it.

The gunplay is pretty fun, though limited to a few different weapons. the inventory system feels pretty cool, even if at times a little picky about the position you have to put your weapons in. Reloading is snappy and responsive. you can get really good at it and I feel it adds a little spice to the gameplay. I wish the ammo box wasn't so close to the pistol holster, because I've very often reached for my gun only to point an ammo box at enemies.

This game allows you to interact with pretty much everything, in a half-life-esque physics system. Your arms sometimes feel like noodles when you try to punch or grab things, but I find it hard to think of another way to convey weight with physics.

one nitpick I have is that the game doesn't let you climb over ledges easily, you have to be in this very specific position where you're not grabbing the ledge but just laying your hands on it, then hold the jump button and crouch with your arms as extended as you can, it can be really tricky to get yourself in that spot and many times it just made me fall off.

Loading times are definitely a slog, sometimes making you wait minutes between maps. honestly though? I felt like I needed that break most of the time, as it can be a bit of a workout.

Story wise, I don't have much to say. I was often distracted with other things whenever info was thrown at me, but that's mostly my ADD.

All in all, it's definitely worth the 30$. The developers have worked hard making this game look and feel good, and it shows.\t\t\t\t\t
