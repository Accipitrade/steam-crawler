\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR; VERY good VR experience. Nice physics puzzler, a bit like Half Life. Only downside for me was some rough physics when handling heavier weapons and some slight nausea after an hour or so.

So. Cons- Didn't look hard enough to find the story plot (I think there was probably more written stuff to find) and the movement made me feel rough after about an hour to 2 hours play time. 8.5hrs made the story line (However, see pros).... Um pretty much it.

Pros- Nice graphics, amazing soundtrack, good (if a little frustrating at times) physics, good gun play, good melee although I ignored most of the heavy weapons.
Story line I managed in 8.5 hours. This was me mostly just hammering through it though. I didn't take the time to start digging through a lot of the hidden areas and only clocked how I'd get a lot of stuff to the end half way through the game.
There's collectables throughout each level that you need to get to the reclamation bin at the end of the map so there's a lot of replayability in the main story mode. Then there's the sandbox mode, which I've yet to dip into but reading previous comments is where a lot of the fun is to be had.

Meh's- Heavy weapon physics is a bit naff. I understand that heavier weapons you will move slower and that's a hard thing to imitate in VR but just felt ....meh. Personally only used the heavy melee when nothing else was on offer. And even then I'd use a broken chair leg over say the sledge hammer. Very few, if any from memory, of the puzzles required use of the heavy weapons so felt needless.
Also some of the smaller looking weapons don't fit in the hip slots and that bugged me for some reason. I'm looking at you hand axe...

Overall a very enjoyable experience and one I'm looking forward to finding the secrets in.

Still playing, still enjoying. Drop back in every now and then because it's just fun to mess around in.\t\t\t\t\t
