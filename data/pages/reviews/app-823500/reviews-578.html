\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, it does not support WMR or any controllers that aren't Oculus. So I had to spend about half an hour fiddling around in the Steam interface creating custom mappings that map my trigger to the trigger and my thumbstick to the thumbstick. Lovely!

Well, once my controllers actually began to work, I noticed that you HAVE to play the story levels to unlock the sandbox levels, which is stupid. I went into the first story level, where you have to force grab a mug from far away.

Literally could not do it no matter what I tried. When I held the hand over the mug, it showed a dot. Thought that meant something. Nope. Nothing works. Open palm of any orientation doesn't work. Grabbing and doing any sort of motion doesn't work. My friend said, oh it's like pulling a rope. Didn't work. Then they said oh make a gun recoil motion. Didn't work. Nothing works.

I cannot figure out how to grab that stupid mug and the voice in the background that keeps saying "Force grab the object from far away" just makes it even more infuriating.

This game has so many issues, if you open the steam menu ever while playing then the framerate suddenly becomes unplayable until you REBOOT YOUR COMPUTER (!). It says my hexa-core i7 is not strong enough to run the physics which is a stupid lie. The game forces itself into fullscreen so you can't use the desktop from the steam menu until you hit your physical windows key.

I really, really wanted to play this game. The trailer made the physics look enticing, I've seen videos of people playing around in sandbox, but I just can't get this hunk of junk to actually work. I don't know why there are so many issues, but I'm getting a refund.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I start writing the review, I have to state something first, I have only been doing VR for a month, and I have a Rift S. Experiences may vary for people used to VR or have worse/better headset. I also have a strong stomach.

I have never had more fun playing video games in my life. This goes for all of VR and says a lot since I've been gaming my entire 18 years of existing. This game was so much fun to playthrough. I have to agree with the statement that this was the first true start to VR physics. Unlike other games, I never felt a disconnect from VR Reality or anything that pulled me from the experience.

Not once did I experience motion sickness. But again, I have a strong stomach and never get motion sickness from anything. But this game does have heights, something I'm scared of. Yet the height factor never ruined my experience. It actually made it so much better.

The story does exist, but nobody cares to listen to it. Not even me. And it's because it's not interesting. I tried to listen, but just didn't care, so I just destroyed all the monitors every time someone attempted to speak to me. So, while the story does exist, it's clear they want you to not listen.

What actually excels in this game is the physics and gameplay. The way you can climb anything, to the gunplay and melee combat, it all just works. And so well. It's just fun. The way objects have weight feels real. You may not be able to actually feel the weight itself, but with your character having arms, you get a general idea of how heavy an item is without actually struggling to pick up and swing a sledge hammer with one hand. Just by your arm going limp.

Sandbox and Arena game modes are really fun. Not much to say. Just horde modes and spawn anything (given you find all the secrets) and have fun

But I do have one compliant. It is the lack of guns. There are so many melee weapons, but only a few firearms. 1911, Glock, a couple M4 variants, 2 MP5's, and an Uzi. Plus a P320 that you only get to use in Zombie mode. That's it. So only 6 weapons. There needs to be more. There aren't any shotguns, all weapons feel the same. Only difference is what pouches you can put them in and if they have attachments. It can make gunplay feel repetitive. But not really enough to make it unenjoyable.

All in all, I rate the game a 8/10. A definite buy, especially at this price. If you find it on sale, BUY IT! If you don't find it on sale, BUY IT! If you actually like VR, get this game.

If I had one wish for a VR game. Mix this game with H3VR, Blade And Sorcery, and Half Life: Alyx.
This games mechanics and Physics (Blade and Sorcery's is good, but I like this ones better), H3VR only for it's guns (Let's be honest, that's the only good that about that game, but that's why it's so good), Blade And Sorcery for it's Melee combat (I just like it more for some reason), and Half Life: Alyx for it's story (As in, a story just as good, not that story in general).

The only thing that can make this game better is more guns. That is all it needs.

JUST BUY THIS GAME!!!\t\t\t\t\t
