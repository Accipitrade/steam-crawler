\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't think I've ever left a review on a game before, but after finishing my first playthrough of this game (of which there will be many more), I felt compelled to.

This is the most immersive games I've ever played. Not one of the most, the most. Having a full body with IK is one thing, but Boneworks takes it to the next level. A full physics body, interacting with full physics objects that feel like they have weight, in VR. It's truly amazing, and it would be fantastic if it were a tech demo, but it's not. This is a full game, with an amazing soundtrack, immersive melee combat that rivals blade and sorcery, great gunplay, and beautiful visuals and art direction. The plot is a bit lacking in my opinion, but that's easily overshadowed by just about everything else. This game is groundbreaking, and I truly feel that it'll influence vr games as a whole in the same way that the half life games did for the fps genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEh... I loved the hype... but it's not nearly the scope of some other games out there currently for vr.

The first thing that stands out is a direct contradiction to what was said in one of their videos, that the player's body itself is a physics object. First scoped rifle I found I placed the butt on my shoulder to reduce the sway... then noticed that it passes right through the player. No shouldering guns is a big miss in my opinion,

Aside from that, has nobody pointed out that this is merely Gmod in VR... only they built the game without allowing mods cause "it's not within our current scope". You can break boxes in the game... which you can't do in most games... so here you go! lol. The weight physics in regard to the player are a joke as your arms become some weird springs in an attempt to not stretch. The guns are perfectly balanced (stupid lack of shouldering!) and shooting feels great... but the melee weapons are something to be desired.

Grips in the game are wanky. The sledge hammer, something you come across a lot in the game, is virtually un-swingable with any force behind it. I tried all kinds of ways to get a good swing, but that stupid arm springs mechanic they put in the game prevents it. Using telekinesis to grab a melee weapon puts the grip in the middle of the object every time, and attempting to toss the grip up your hand (remember weight physics?) even with things such as the monkey wrench causes you to toss the damn thing five feet away as if you intentionally threw it away.

Enemy AI is awful. The zombie guys flail their arms and demolish boxes to get to you... because slapping a box like you would your girlfriend's ass should be enough to send it into splinters (poor gf!). The McShooty guys are almost tactical, but they ride around on these little balls that suck at traversing the terrain, so eventually they all get stuck and you can just pick them off. If the ground is flat and free of clutter (it barely ever is) then I've seen them do some cool flanks and such... but it's so rare that you end up wishing they'd just kick your butt instead of succumbing to they're own wanky physics.

All in all, looks like a great framework for talented modders to fix for us all... oh...

There's spray paint around the levels, of which one says "what if this is all a tech demo"... and with how much the entire game looks like half-life/Gmod, I'm guessing that it is.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStrange how hyped this is for the finished product which seems really really clunky, at least on Index. The grip and trigger to take a gun from holster is awkward, climbing a complete pita, lot of the simulated weight and physics is so janky and off. Things like opening a filing cabinet leads to you dragging it halfway across the floor before can open a drawer for instance. Just so many things are way off despite the "physics are perfect hype". The firearms are simplistic and clunky too and melee can be clunky with the limp wristed weird physics. I am not refunding as the premise is promising and more indie studios makign stuff like this the better and hopefully the kinks get ironed out but not there yet imho.

Reason I mention the physics the most is there is very little else of substance to the game since one of the core points is the physics and gun play both of which I find unsatisfying although promising in theory. Just feels like a "not quite there yet" nice looking tech demo with a longer length to me. Fwiw I've seen very good physics in other things I play so albeit without simulated object weight and collision simulation this has. It is double edged as part the thing that makes it clunky and awkward is those two things being simulated but at same time avoids the skyrim/FO problem of dual wielding claymores and waving them around like they're weightless like some kind of beatsabrefest.

As for the gunplay I wasn't expecting H3VR but just seems a bit too shallow and meh. Speaking of that it also has realistic simulated bolt carrier for goodness sake and simulated a lot of physics itself including sound that travels at correct speed. If you can spare the cash and want to fund Brandon et al and support small dev teams like this then do it but you know who you are, as for those on the fence I'd give it a miss in current state personally.\t\t\t\t\t
