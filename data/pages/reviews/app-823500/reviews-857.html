\r\n\t\t\t\t\t- First game I ever needed to refund on Steam -

The reason? Motion sickness within 10-15 minutes and unfortunately nausea following that.

I will add that I -STILL- have nausea as well as “vertigo” effects impacting my vision 13 hours after playing and going to sleep in between which is a first for me with VR...

Seeing that there are other people impacted as much as I was, I did not want to push it further the 2 hours mark just to see if my nausea would reduce over time.

Maybe one day I will buy it again but for the moment if I see that things have changed but for the moment I will try other less intense games!

As background information I played quite a few VR games including indie titles and poorly made demos on multiple platforms over the course of the last years with my Oculus CV1. Recently I played "Half-Life: Alyx” twice in a row (23 hours total) and did not get a single moment of actual discomfort in that other game.

Also note that I play with a fan at head level and in a room with AC so the lack of fresh air was not an issue…


As a side note, within the hour of play I managed to get stuck 3 times in game textures of the initial introduction map and also managed to hit my VR equipment twice which almost never occurred in the hundreds of hours I spent in VR (so watch your environment – in particular your headset vs. your controllers).

I do appreciate the fact that the developer is constantly updating the game.

I will end by saying that the movement and point of view in game when getting stuck and seeing within items seem to be the major contributing factor of the motion sickness and trying to get out of objects added to the frustration.

********************************
My play tips for VR so far (2020-07):
********************************

- Play with a fan on. I found out empirically that my ideal play temperature is around 24C / 75F personally and that reaching 27C / 80F my headset become less and less bearable.

- Play on a carpet to reduce stress on foot and body in general.
- Take breaks from time to time if you can resist the urge to play!
- Stop playing whenever motion sickness kicks in or in presence of back pain and the like.

- If needed, for first sessions or after a long break, use ginger pills to reduce or inhibit any potential minimal motion sickness even if you don’t expect any.
- Stay hydrated before, during and after playing (and also you will likely find out that you will end up being hungry during and after some gameplay.

- In order to prevent lenses from fogging before playing, you can pre-heat your headset for 5 to 10 minutes by forcing it to stay activated (I use the silk cloth that I use to clean the lenses near the sensor of my Oculus CV1) to keep some heat near the lenses so that when you put the headset the lenses don’t get foggy from the difference in temperature between your body and the lenses.

- Enjoy your time in VR! – If you don’t go do something else to change your ideas a bit!\t\t\t\t\t
