\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to leave a positive review, and will edit this to positive if/when saves are added in some form and WMR issue is fully resolved.

Cons -
1) Devs listed WMR as supported under the assumption that it would work. Their response and updates on the issue make it clear that they never actually tested with a WMR setup prior to launch. Their rush to release a fix is admirable but obviously you can only do so much in a short period and it will be some time before the WMR experience is on par with others.

2) There is no way to save short of beating a level and unlocking the next level. This is fine in games with short 5 to 10-minute levels. It's not ok in a game where a level can take you over an hour, or even several hours depending on your playstyle. You're starting to feel nauseous and need to take a break? Busy person, work, kids, whatever, and can't play for more than 30 minutes at a time? You're out of luck. It doesn't need to be a full-blown save system, just something to at least save the current state of the game, leave, and resume.

3) They aren't kidding when they say this is for advanced VR users. They recommend sitting down and after playing standing I 100% see why. I've never had an issue with a previous VR game but got sick very easily playing this standing up. Have been doing better playing in smaller bursts sitting down to build up to the experience but see above - not a great solution if you can't save.

Pros -
1) Physics implementation, even with the jankiness that brings, is just super fun. The ways you can get past any particular puzzle, defeat enemies, and just mess around for fun are endless. My first wow moment was opening a door with one hand and then having to hold it open with the other so it didn't swing back and hit me as I walked through. Truly the stuff VR is meant to do.

2) Pushing VR games to the next level. Sets a new standard for immersion. Hands phasing through things are NOT good enough anymore after this game. This is not the status quo they are truly pushing boundaries here.\t\t\t\t\t

PROS ~ it is a physics game so it has a lot of unique and interesting puzzles, the guns are fun and the weapon system is very interesting and plentiful, everything from hammers to swords to an uzi! the atmosphere is awesome and gave me chills. the story seemed fun and interesting and something that could be along the lines of Bioshock even tho it feels like it needs more "Meat" to it.
CONS ~ The physics need a lot of tweaking, it is a struggle to try and jump between platforms, things that you have to move slowly can fling across the room when you stand on them which is very aggravating, the weapons in your hands can sometimes feel too loose, everything is trying hard to have physics which means swinging a sledgehammer is slow and kind of frustrating, I think it it got tweaked a little bit tighter it can still be real and have fun swinging weapons around. Some of the puzzles are really easy to understand once you get them but because the physics are so bad right now it makes something easy like climbing a horrible, frustrating challenge.
OVER-ALL ~ Besides being frustrating and needing some tweaks right now with the physics and some things, I enjoy the game a lot, I've had a BLAST the first few hours I played if I just focus on moving forward, and i know it is still early and they plan on adding a bunch into the game, and I am honestly excited to see this game grow and get polished!\t\t\t\t\t
