Boneworks challenges you to think outside the box in every interaction. Things you think probably aren't possible or "they wouldn't have done that" are worth trying, because a lot of the time, there is hidden detail around every corner. When Portal came out, it was, "Think with portals."

If all you know about Boneworks is the sandbox, I encourage you to give it a try and get sucked into the story mode.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's times when I hated this game and times where I absolutely loved it. I'd recommend it to anyone interested in the potential of VR - this game demonstrates where things COULD get to, but doesn't really reach those levels itself. It can be incredibly janky at times and, if you haven't played a continuous locomotion VR game before, it will likely take a few sessions to get used to and to stop getting nausea. The world gives me similar vibes to Portal in that you're exploring this vast mysterious place as seemingly the only 'person' there. It lends the game an atmosphere that I really like - not quite scary but certainly a bit unsettling especially with the humanoid enemies. Overall I'd say its worth a try for VR fanatics but for newer people I would look to the more polished titles like Half Life Alyx to get started.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am so divided about this game...the gunplay mechanics are great and it definitely tries to push the boundaries of VR. However, the main story mode is extremely bland, the art style is repugnant, and the heavy reliance on physics (while cool) behaves more erratically than it should. Many times, ideas I had to solve physics puzzles didn't align to what the developers had in mind, and I was stuck until I happened upon the exact method they called for. I know this game is made by the same guys behind Corridor Digital, and while you can definitely see their love for the medium in this game, their poor level design skills, poor campaign/story mode pacing, and lack of visually appealing art style really hurt the experience for me. Granted, the shooting/melee combat is quite good, the experience is marred by wonky physics and being just damned ugly to look at. I really am at a loss for how people think this game is so revolutionary - it tries really hard to be, but it just isn't polished enough to put it on the level of something like Alyx or The Walking Dead Saints and Sinners. The controls had me cursing and confused when they didn't behave as I thought they would. I really want to like this game, but in my experience, I spend more time frustrated than enjoying it. Maybe if I get further in it I will enjoy it more, but I doubt it.\t\t\t\t\t
