\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a physics puzzle and combat game built from the ground up for VR. It's CPU heavy due to the physics engine it runs.

I loved how complex the puzzles were. They were not easy. I had to stop and think and try various things before I started figuring out how I was going to make it work to move on.

You use your hands for everything. Climbing ropes and rung ladders. Cocking and loading your weapons. Utilizing various melee weapons, everything from knives, sledgehammers to swords. Turning wheels. Punching or grabbing enemies. Throwing stuff. Breaking open crates with axes. Pushing buttons. Stacking objects. Moving parts of the environment into the different places. You're always doing something with your hands and sometimes doing two different things at once is required.

It's got a great loadout system too. Your ammo and weapons can be reached for and grabbed from different spots on your body. You just reach, grab, and you got it. To put them away you just place them in that same area and let go.

It starts off slow with training in the "museum" but it gets really intense and creepy as you get further into the story. The NPC's can be incredibly stupid sometimes, tripping over themselves and not being able to climb stairs. Other times they are disturbingly freaky. The way they say "hey" and stalk you. They scared the ♥♥♥♥ out of me more than once.

The lore of the storyline is vague and to be honest I sorta stopped paying attention to it. I played the game for the physics puzzles and combat. The storyline is forgettable and comes off as half-assed but that doesn't retract from the experience of the game.

Every level is a "sandbox" where you start from scratch and forfeit the weapons you had before. You get to keep the ammo you found to buy more weapons though. When you get to the point the game starts asking for 2000-3000 ammo for a weapon DON'T DO IT. You can literally walk around the corner and find the exact same weapons lying around for free.

The music is a broad mixture of electronica genres ranging from downtempo to drum and bass and it's very nice. There's even acoustic blues on one of the sandbox levels. The only music that sucks is whatever's playing on the portable radios you can destroy and I think that was intentional. I destroyed every single one I came across.

The ending was so bizarre and had me sweating my ass off!

Boneworks has the reputation as being one of the premiere games for VR and it did not disappoint. Just make sure you have a good compatible CPU otherwise you're gonna have a bad time.\t\t\t\t\t
