\r\n\t\t\t\t\t16hrs in and I have just now completed the story mode. Boneworks is a fantastic single player experience that brings some of my favorite game mechanics to VR: Branching paths, different approaches to a single mission, collectibles.

The VR aspect is so well done that it makes most other games seem like mere tech demos by comparison. This is achieved through a fully modeled body that the player controls as opposed to the standard floating hands with the occasional torso. The movement of the player character also makes it feel closer to a standard 2d game with some extra spice. You have the standard walk/sprint/strafe that would normally be controlled with WASD combined with an adjustable crouch "slider" (you can crouch or stand up but this enables you to be somewhere in between for better access to stuff near the ground).

You also have the ability to climb on certain materials which adds another dimension to gameplay and opens up plenty of potential for hidden items or areas which the game is great at utilizing. Also parkour which was actually kind of fun.

The story was a neat concept but with the missions being so long and the story parts being so short that I forgot what I was doing for most of the game. "Lack of save points" was a complaint from some earlier reviewers but they have since patched in more save points to cater to those who cannot always finish out an hour long mission.

I love this game and intend to replay it multiple times. The devs teased that they are working on another project and I can't wait to play that to.\t\t\t\t\t
