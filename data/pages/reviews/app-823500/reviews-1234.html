\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMore, more, more!

You will absolutely get your money's worth with boneworks at full price, but I kinda wish I paid and got 60 dollars worth of Boneworks. The physics based backend of thid game is so crucial to VR that non-physical VR games are retroactively made less fun for me. Boneworks doesn't do any one thing better than a dedicated VR game does, but it's on par or damn close in ever single field from melee, to firearms, to climbing, to physics puzzles.

I was hoping for a little more from the story. The campaign is very entertaining, but not really for the writing. Without dpoiling anything, you don't meet any other real characters in the game. The actual plot is happening in thr real world while the protagonist is the sole human mind stuck in the game. It's fairly esoteric and I haven't fully figured out what was supposed to be happening.

Took me about 12 hours to beat the story, nowhere near 100% on collectibles snd secrets though.

Only things I think could have been improved upon directly:
- thrusting with melee weapons seems vastly superior to swinging
- I'd have liked a little more specific functionality with the guns like in H3. Fire selection, modular attachments, mag release button, etc
- Campaign would have benefitted from other human characters, and a more direct involvement in the "real" story.
- Story pacing was weird. The moment that felt like the final showdown ended up being a couple levels short of the end. Made ending feel a bit anticlimactic.

Other than that I just want MORE:
- More enemy types (started to get dull by the last few levels)
- More weapons (There are 6ish distinct guns and maybe a dozen melee weapons)
- More challenges (Arena mode is shallow compared ro the sandbox gameplay of story levels)

In any case, Boneworks is a rare fully realized VR experience. The most important thing is the backend work SL0 did to get this game working. I can't wait for more games based on the same physical engine, made by SL0 or others. I don't know how I'm going to go back to having Rayman hands after this.

Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst, let's go over some of the bad things. Overall I think it's a good game, but these are some things you should know before considering purchase. That being said, the game is new and I suspect maybe some things will improve with updates (although, some can't be improved at all by the nature of the game).

  • It's got some bugs, and not just the ones crawling around on the floor. Mostly having to do with physics jank. For example, my arm got clipped through a wall and I just ended up redoing the scene. On one occasion, the game started massive frame skips and crashed.
  • Saves are only at the end of a lengthy scene. Because I love the game so much, it's usually not an issue. But the aforementioned crash made me painfully aware of it. I also played enough that my controllers died mid-fight. You could imagine how that played out.
  • Climbing over ledges is irritating if not impossible. You have to do some mystic combination of grabbing, jumping, crouching, and moving forward. Also, jumping onto a platform away from a wall you're climbing on doesn't work well.
  • Some physics puzzles are impeded by jankiness in a way that's all frustration but no challenge.
  • Attempting to steady the gun can sometimes pull out a magazine instead.
  • Sometimes the body slots don't register, which means dropped weapons when slotting in and multiple attempts when grabbing a weapon out of the holster. Other times I grab the wrong slot. After some time, you end up finding the sweet spot, but it's not perfect. The tutorial show you looking down at your holster and sliding in the gun, but this doesn't actually work. If you look at your holster, your body will turn. I tried messing with locomotion options to see if that was it, but no dice.
  • Physics jank can affect your body since your in-game body is a physics object itself. This can result in situations where you're stuck between various objects which push you around in unpredictable ways. I don't get motion sick, but this makes me feel weird.
  • Lacking in comfort locomotion options like teleport, shift, or arm swaying. It's easy to see why this is the case when you consider the physics, but it also means this game isn't for those that don't have their VR legs or get motion sickness.

Now for the good things. As much as the physics causes issues, it's also its selling point. I personally love the physics puzzles, although sometimes prone to jankiness, and enjoy using physics to defeat enemies. The gun action is so cool in this game. Each gun is loaded and uniquely to the design of the gun. Steadying the gun with your hand and aiming down the sights will make your shots more accurate. One of the most heart-racing things I experienced in this game was dropping a magazine while a horde of zombish headed my direction. I ended up bashing the first zombish's head in with the butt of the gun so that I could pick up magazine and continue firing. Speaking of enemies, you can grab the legs of those crablets which is so satisfying because it makes me feel in control even though they're jumping at my face. I used this leg grabbing technique to throw one out a window and many other times to hold and pistol it or use it to whack other crablets.

Besides the awesome physics you feel, I also the think the story is fairly compelling. I don't want to leave any spoilers though. If you don't get motion sickness and have gotten your VR legs then I say go ahead and buy the game. If you get motion sick then I can't recommend it, but you should definitely watch a play through online to get the story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs much as it pains me, this review needs to stay negative for now

What Stress Level Zero have achieved here is a big heavy door kicked open into the future of VR gaming. Playing in a world where you are aware that every object is interactive makes it feel ever so real. The on-body weapon storage is slick (+1 for the inventory view) and the gun control is immersive without being stressful. Your ammo is always in the same pouch and reloading early will not waste the ammo in your clip. Walking room to room for the first time in a level with the pistol in hand feels great, you don't know what's ahead but you know one thing for sure, if an enemy appears it's gonna feel good.

The crippling downside is the save system combined with tedious physics puzzles and obstacles. I've had to play through the 'streets' level three times now which actually made me quit the game for a few hours when I accidentally quit during the second run. For the next level I had a friend waiting for me, told him I should be near the end of my current level, it ended up taking an extra FORTY FIVE MINUTES, some of which was a cool shootout which I really couldn't enjoy as I just needed the level to end, knowing I would loose an hours progress if I stopped. It's worth noting that an hour in VR feels much longer than a regular game! Towards the end of this level there are two climbing segments where if you don't see the initial solution when you enter the room and are looking for an exit at the bottom, you have to drag yourself all the way back out. This is not rewarding when your arms and body love to get snagged when climbing.

This game is very much in the right direction, but I can only shout praise once it feels like less of a chore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t- Best body presence and tracking yet to be seen in a VR game, and the most interactive and physically correct environments yet. I'm amazed.
- Melee weapons and props are handled great, the guns feel ok (but the Vive wands are getting in the way sometimes).
- The Vive wands are not the best option but they work and get the job done. But there's some bug with the haptic feedback on the controllers since they can rumble on a really high pitch noise, which is defenetly not normal.
- The optimization is amazing (Vive with 250% resolution - no hiccups at all with a 2070S)!
- One bigger problem is the save system, which is only saving at the end of a level. The devs are already working on a solution at the time of the review so this can be changed for the better.

Great game overall! Would recommend to anyone with a VR setup, but be aware that you have grown your "VR legs" for this! ;)
