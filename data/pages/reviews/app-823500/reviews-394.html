Is It Worth Buying?
Even if it was $5 more I'd still suggest that you buy and play this game

What Makes This Game Fun/Good
where to start....
I can pick up a hammer and throw it at an enemies heads
I can stab multiple enemies turning a Claymore into a Shish Kabob
guns that feel great to use and smack enemies with
the smacking noises
being able pull your self up to ledges in VR
buttons
head crabs that put you into another layer of VR
puzzles that wont make you look up a tutorials but are still challenging
the vacuum suck to pull objects towards you
post game content that includes a zombie survival mode and a arena mode
CROWBAR
being able to pick anything up and smack someone with it
the AXE
Disco
Smack sound (again)
stabbing an enemy to the wall
the gun range
flying a vehicle that should fly
opening every single locker and then slamming it shut
PLANT
being able to pull a Skyrim and put a trashcan on my enemies head and watch them fumble around
Flashlight and night-vision
smacking the mag out of your gun and reloading
Boxing an enemy
SMACK
can play seated or standing
IT'S TIME FOR CRAB
Balloon gun
throwing things
Destructible boxes
a store full of melee weapons for you to use
NOODLE
and so much more!
The overall experience was great
oh and did i mention the best part..... the shopping cart
oh and a bunch of very hard to find secrets if your into that


Cons
once you beat the story there is almost no reason to replay it besides finding every box of ammo you've missed, finding guns you missed, or finding the secret weapons that is very well hidden and will probably need a tutorial to find.

the controls are not for everyone.
this give me a bit of VR sickness at first for some reason (even though the entire game felt smooth and mostly natural) but it went away and never happened again after the first day.

sadly melee weapons take a lot of force to deal damage (so if you have a lamp near by kiss it goodbye).
a lot of the enemies you fight are of very similar variety.
the amount of ammo required to get the DEV weapons is absurd.
holding melee weapons at least with the Vive controllers is a nightmare if you plan on using it for a long time.
opening doors when you have a full body is a bit rough for some people (not me)
hands getting stuck in places
The metal gear ladder but in VR (truly a finial boss to face)
and other typical VR jank that is in other games.

What about Mods
I haven't modded this game in any way and I've gotten 29 hours out of it, however there seems to be some really good mods for this game.

Conclusion
This game is easily worth the $30 price tag. It has nice game-play and only a handful of bugs. If you haven't picked this game up and you play VR you need to pick this game up. Just playing with the environment was enough to distract me for hours. (looking at all the chairs I've picked up and thrown at enemies...). The story does happen but is easily ignored, but its a decent length. The side content is fun for a while and mods will probably extend the life of this game for anyone that wants to.

8/10
would smack my ceiling again.\t\t\t\t\t
