\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most intriguing concepts of virtual reality is the ability to achieve a realistic simulation of worlds which are entirely the product of the imagination. One example of this sense of freedom is evident in the way that virtual worlds do not have to behave according to the laws of physics which rule over our physical world. Theories contradictory to our common spatial experience (and common sense) are easily applied to a VR environment. Buildings do not have to respond to the laws of gravity or physical material characteristics; collisions can or cannot happen; you can walk through walls; the figure-ground relations can be inverted; you can be at two or more places at the same time. New spatial sensibilities are discovered from the interactive processes used by a design in a virtual environment. The directions of left and right, in front and behind, up and down, corresponding to the Cartesian interpretation of the physical world could collapse.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is incredible! I don't really do reviews on Steam but this game is on another level. Akin to the original Half-Life in structure, this game really does feel like it fits in with the Half-Life games. The climbing is absolutely incredible and adds a new level of verticality to combat and exploration never seen before in an FPS. The only reason I have only 7 hours so far is because my 1060 3GB is struggling to keep a steady framerate, so I bought a 2070 just to play this game at a better FPS + Resolution. Not without her negatives though, as there are oddly no checkpoint/save system. This means that levels have to be completed in just one sitting which is pretty annoying, but I'm sure they can patch this easily. I hope.

Edit (8.03.2020):

Game has me coming back even after months. It's incredibly far from perfect, but it's the most fun sandbox you could get in VR. They also fixed the checkpoint system a couple weeks after release, so I guess I needed to correct myself.

Edit (03.01.2023):

This game is just so much better than Bonelab. Bonelab runs worse, feels worse and has a much worse campaign. While you can mod Bonelab more, the mods present for Boneworks feel better. Don't get Bonelab, get Boneworks!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is the kind of game you probably haven't played before. If you're new to VR, it might take you some time to get used to the kind of intricate arcade-simulation style of gameplay that Boneworks provides.

First things first - I played this on a brand new Valve Index - if you're playing on an Oculus of any kind, Vive, or a WMR setup, I cannot guarantee you'll have the same quality of experience I did.

At its worst moments, when you're accidentally grabbing the MP5K magazine instead of foregrip - when you accidentally pull your 1911 out instead of a new magazine, it can feel a bit like playing QWOP.
At its best, you'll feel a bit like John Wick or Snake from the Metal Gear franchise.

This is a new kind of game - and you'll need to train your body and accumulate muscle memory before you can merc fools like a true operator. Apart from a few truly dreadful moments of your arms getting stuck in walls, when something goes wrong, it's because YOU don't know what the hell you're doing. Because YOU messed up.

The gameplay aims to be "better than reality" with its force grab and very forgiving gun reloads, and succeeds in every way, except for the arm-flailing disasters mentioned above. You must immerse yourself in the game, however, because you can absolutely cheese this game. Go into every fight with dual handguns or a rapid fire weapon like an M4 or MP5, double tap slow-motion, and then win immediately. You'll want to be truly creative with your kills if you want to enjoy yourself, and the game encourages you to "play along" and mimic Arthur when you're grabbing large objects like metal crates or barrels. This is a game you have to want to play. Do otherwise at your peril.

The soundtrack is among the best I've ever heard and has some of the most solid synthwave bangers of the year. Imagine Stranger Things meets Perturbator.

The story is a more than a bit vague and if you wondered why the developers never shared story details, it's mostly because there isn't a great deal to share. Basically every plot point is a loose end, and the conclusion of the game is left completely to player interpretation. This isn't a bad thing in my opinion, but I could clearly see some, or even the majority of players feeling disappointed in it.

All said, Boneworks is a wonderful experience and I implore you to try it. This game has single-handedly pioneered the future of Virtual Reality, and should you be interested in where gaming might go next, look no further.\t\t\t\t\t
