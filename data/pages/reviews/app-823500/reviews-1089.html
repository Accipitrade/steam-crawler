\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI echo pretty much everyone elses sentiments about this game. It's really fun. The physics based combat and puzzling is really well done, but it can take some time for the player to actually get used to that.

They've also built a game world that, while a tad but underdeveloped feeling, is genuinely terrifying at times. If the damn mechanical VR headset robot spiders horrified me this much, I can't even imagine what it'll be like once I Half-Life: Alyx gets here.

The physical puzzles come together for the most part really well. There were a couple of puzzles I felt were a bit clunky, but for the most part they were very rewarding to complete. I also loved that there was usually always a way to just bruteforce your way through the puzzle. That's a feature I ended up using embarassingly often.

The plot ranged anywhere from "Meh" to "Oh, that's mildly interesting". But I don't really fault the game for that, to be honest. It has its moments and there were some implications laid out there, but it's nothing new or unexplored.

Slo-moing the nullbodies never gets old, it's just so much fun. I hope Stress Level Zero expands on this framework in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been an active VR user as of 2017 when I got a Vive, and I upgraded to an Index this year to prepare for upcoming titles. I was absolutely pumped for this game, and it didn't disappoint at all.

I was a little concerned about the games content. I would say the biggest struggle in VR is finding a game that genuinely feels like it is worth the price. This is the best VR title I have played so far by a landslide.

The story campaign took me around 14-15 hours, and it was an absolute blast. The setting and tone of the game are absolutely perfect. It feels like a Valve love letter.

I think my only complaint with the story mode is some of the puzzles are just a disaster to figure out, and sometimes it becomes frustrating and you overthink it a lot, when the answer is extremely simple. That comes with the game being so physics based, and I will take that trade-off gladly.

The physics based movement can be extremely difficult for some users though. I've had 3 of my friends struggle to play the game for a full scene (roughly an hour long most of the time) because its too much. Some people got motion sick and some got really exhausted and worn out. I personally could play the game for a decent amount of time.

The extra gamemodes are about what most VR games have at a $30 price tag not including the story, and the fact that it is also included is insane. Arena mode is amazing and I love it so much. I had so much fun in arena mode in this game than any other arena mode in another game. (though I wish the slow motion button wasn't on the same controller as the movement analog stick, I wanna move in slow motion)

In conclusion, if you have a VR headset, get this game. If you are looking forward to HL:A and plan on getting a headset for that, get this game too. It is proof that VR games can be more than just an arcade game in VR.

Also the soundtrack is amazing

THE MUST HAVE VR GAME.

This game is amazing. Don't get me wrong, it has it's flaws, from poor campaign design to tedious unlockables. However, Boneworks absolutely makes up for it's shortcomings through it's innovative game mechanics, physics based enemy design, and an extremely interactive world.

Pros:
  • Amazing physics ahead of any other VR game to date
  • Very interactive and captivating world. Grab a desk and flip it over, or stuff an enemy in a locker
  • Extremely satisfying gunplay and melee combat, with excellent reloading systems and weapon handling
  • Innovative Inventory system, with items being equipped on the player's body, sidearms, lower back, over the shoulder, etc.
  • Completely physics based enemies which react very realistically to everything from gunshots to impalements. Laugh as you
  • impale 2 enemies on the same sword at the same time
  • A great amount of replayability in the Arena Mode (If you unlock it through beating story mode)
  • Surprisingly good music
  • Sandbox mode to ♥♥♥♥ around in (if unlocked from story. This ones really easy tho basically 2nd mission)

Cons:
  • Climbing is straight doo doo. Probably my biggest gripe with the game (although it's not THAT important)
  • Story is pretty short and lacks in content
  • Sandbox and Arena mode are not unlocked by default (Big mistake IMO)
  • Inventory system, although very innovative, is often clunky (Could absolutely be updated in a patch)
  • Story mode puzzles are tedious at times
  • Unlocking items for sandbox/arena mode feels like a chore
  • Very few enemies (9 I think, although, most are basically re-skins. Really only ~4 enemies, but they really are fun to fight)

Summary:
This game is for people who are looking for a great physics-based vr combat game where they can just ♥♥♥♥ around killing enemies and playing around with physics, weapons, and interesting AI. Story is really interesting, but the real meat of this game is unlocking arena mode and fighting waves on enemies endlessly with the weapons of your choosing. My personal favorite loadout: a 1911 handgun, with a combat knife, mp5 in-case ♥♥♥♥ gets dicey, and a claymore longsword to have fun impaling crabblets and sticking them on the wall, still squirming. Laugh as it attempts to pry itself off the wall. Has this game made me a psychopath? No, it just unleashed what I always had in me.

9/10
Fix some inventory bugs & other bugs, add more replayability, and maybe throw in a few more weapons, and it's an easy 10/10 for me\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's honestly crazy to me how immersive this game, and VR has come. I don't forget that I'm playing a game, but just walking around in game and interacting with the objects and breaking them, feels like I'm actually putting in the effort to do that, rather than just hit a button to break object or to loot. Needless to say, its very satisfying.

This game isn't perfect. Each level takes a fairly long time to beat for first run, I expect to spend up to 2-3 hours on each level. And if you play this standing up like I do, your feet might start hurting.
YOU CAN SWITCH BETWEEN STANDING AND SITTING MID GAME. Which is nice.

This game also has a very vague story to my understanding. All I really understand so far in my run is that you are resetting some sort of VR Server, and the people that are contacting you are unable to get into the MythOS place thing.

Good parts about that I like about this game are the grabbing system, climbing system, physics, and all of that stuff. The physics in this game feel so good. I only wish I had a Valve Index to be more immersed. The enemies are real fun to fight against. I like the limited inventory space, but I wish I had at least one more slot at all times.
Btw, you have a slot on your lower back, just in case you didn't know that.

TL;DR : I would say this game is definitely a must have for VR gamers, especially those who are waiting for HL: Alyx. This game has it's flaws, but so do all games.\t\t\t\t\t

Pros:
The physics engine is by far the best thing about it.
The story mode is absolutely thrilling.
The weapon mechanics are beautiful.
The enemies are creepy and awesome to play against. After the ending (which wasn't happy, btw. Prepare yourself ;) ) I spent probably an hour in the sandbox killing them in spite - but honestly, in the end, I am supper happy with this game and was happy to spend time in it.



Cons:
The physics engine~ Sometimes your hands get stuck behind fences, or the objects you are trying to move don't stay in place, but this is really what this game is. Problem solving with objects.

I would highly recommend this to anyone with a headset. Next to beat saber and Super Hot, this is up in the best VR games I have played.\t\t\t\t\t
