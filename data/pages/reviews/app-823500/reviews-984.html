\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a long time user of VR. No Boneworks didn't make me sick or have bad content, I think majoritively it was okay, but I also think that the concepts used for physics interaction was awful.

Your hand gets stuck somewhere so you cant grab your gun under your left tit and you die. Your M16 wobbles like a rubber toy everytime you unholster it as you desperately try to grab the front of the bouncing gun to shoot back in a quick firefight. The inventory system is really bad and unfun to navigate because of the way your body in the game works. Looking down and seeing my characters feet power walk across the ground isn't funny it's annoying and makes me feel like i'm controlling an inflatable tube man with arms.

The weapon handling doesn't have options like virtual stocks that games like Pavlov do. So your guns feel HEAVY, but they feel inverted, so the weight is at the shoulder not on your front hand to control aiming. The enemies and environments are okay, but they get boring super quick. There's not any ambience so it really feels like you're just in a room alone navigating this awful physics puzzle that you'll mess up but not on your own terms, but because your VR arm clipped through a wall and is shooting you out like a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ bazooka at mach speed.

The music is boring, the enemies are all the same and always in these gray open areas with nothing else to look at.

I think it's a cool CONCEPT, but a really unpolished game past the first 2 hours of experimenting with the systems.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was extremely pumped for this game when it released. I flew through the campaign and beat it. I enjoyed the story and gun play. I also enjoyed the physics and swinging from fixtures, etc. I cannot stress how awesome it is to see your own body in VR. Floaty hands are something I could understand a few years ago but it's about time more devs are putting more effort into realistic graphics and gameplay. The overall immersion of seeing and using your entire body in VR is incredible.

The downside for me is that I have no real urge to play through it again. I cannot put my finger on it other than this:
The enemies did not provide a real challenge during the campaign. I felt no sense of urgency or panic (other than "the" big fight, but even then its chaotic for a few short moments and once you realize you can rush through it and ignore everything, or take your time.. it's meh). The arena mode is a great concept but fighting mechanical headcrabs and zombie-like enemies gets old fast. I understand you can change stuff around and such but it feels too limited. It gets boring fast and every ounce of me does not want to say that!!

I still recommend it for the experience.\t\t\t\t\t
