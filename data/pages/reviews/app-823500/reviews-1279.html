I don't want to have to quit the game to take a break and then spend the first 10 minutes getting back to where i was before... and having to repeat all the things i did before either.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a disappointment.

Boneworks' biggest issue is it has all this impressive physics tech and nifty tools to go with it. But the level design almost never allows for anything interesting with it. Plus you literally never get any of those tools until the very end of the game, and you still only get like 2 of them. And in an environment where you can't really get much use out of them anyway.
The only way to get the others is to play the entire game barely ever using guns, then finding most of the ammo collectibles to be able to afford them in the 2nd-to-last level. And then you only get them for sandbox after doing that since the level you buy them in has basically nothing to use them with. Like just who would ever want to do that? It just baffles me how much of a flaw that is, how could anyone have thought that was good design?

They could have even had entire levels built around one of the gadgets since your inventory resets after each one. That would have been a lot more interesting than what instead feels like the usual bland FPS campaign from most other games, but just with only pistols and machine guns and in vr. Seriously why is the gun selection so poor? There's a ton of different melee stuff, but barely any reason to ever use them. Since once you get a gun the game becomes just absurdly easy because only 1 enemy type in the entire game also has a gun. The only other ranged enemy's projectiles are so slow and infrequent that they're just absolutely no threat. The other 2 human-like enemies just slowly walk towards you and then awkwardly flail around to try to hit you. That is of course if there isn't some tiny thing they happen to step on and then "trip" over. It's just trivial to fight anything in this game. The headcrab-like enemies are just exactly what you expect and don't really put up any challenge either.

The biggest issue I have with all of these problems is the game's attitude towards a couple things. You can find some graffiti left by someone ingame saying "Is this just a tech demo!?" and it's very clearly the devs taking a jab at how most VR games feel like tech demos. Just because your game has a "campaign" doesn't make it not a tech demo. It just makes it a more traditional game. The irony is that this actually feels very much like a tech demo. It's a bunch of bland maps loosely strung together with no narrative. (The game's story might as well not exist. It pretends to have one, but it doesn't. You never know why you're doing anything or why anything is happening, nothing is ever explained.) The only purpose is to show off the game's tech. Except it doesn't even manage to do that well for the reasons I listed at the beginning of this review.

Also if you have an AMD CPU then avoid this game until the devs fix the issues with it. Many people including myself have had framedrops in most levels. And one level near the end even dropped to around 12 FPS. (The Tower level) That's completely unplayable, especially for VR.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort version: Feels like a Half-Life 2 mod but VR and has a story its own game universe, if you have every played one you should know what to expect, frustrating physics puzzles, a sparse story, mixed level design quality, and a lot of cool moments along the way. I think itts overall a good game.

Long version:

Physics: can be frustrating or fun depending on how well weighted the object is and how well it is interacting at the time (when they don't work I have had the physics send me flying a random direction, instead of grabbing onto an object grabbing through it and phasing your arm through with you hand stuck on the wrong side with now way back, instead of walk forward to jump to the next thing send what I am standing on flying backwards without moving me even a little). The first hour or so most objects are too light,but later on most object felt better and didn't go flying if I bumped into them. the fork lift dolly in particular feels terrible until you get to the one you see in the trailer and then they sudden weighed more and felt better from that point forward (I even went back to check that I hadn't just gotten familiar with the game but nope they looked the same but felt completely different). Some of the physic puzzles can be super frustrating and tedious despite being super simple to solve.

guns: are somewhere between Pavlov and Arizona sunshine in how they feel, not good but not bad and get the job done.

Melee weapons: similar to Blade & Sorcery, stabbing weapons felt really good, but slashing or bludgeoning require slowed swinging and were either hit or miss for me, some worked well and the speed you needed to swing with them was easy to figure out (actually love the great sword), some took a bit to get used to (sledge hammer), and a couple I never could get to work how I felt they should (hammer)

Climbing: Also feels similar to Blade and Sorceries, but too weighty for my preference. Overall I think I would have enjoyed it more if it wasn't used so much for long periods where an accident means starting over.

Story: Sparse but interesting, would have liked to see more of it.

World: amazing, absolutely immersive and feels like no area is bare, the only issue I could give it is some things I felt like I should be able to grab I couldn't and a few things my hand phased straight through.

Overall I enjoyed the game but it has some issues, some more glaring than others. It doesn't live up to the hype and fanfare the internet put out for it (honestly that was probably impossible but it doesn't quite hit the level of expectations the devs tried setting either), but it meets reasonable expectations, is an enjoyable game and one of the better VR games I have played.\t\t\t\t\t

Next Gen VR Game
BONEWORKS gets so many things right when it comes to being next gen that it really feels like it's on another league compared to other available games. Most important things are:
  • Smooth movement - Smooth Locomotion done right. Movement gradually but quickly speeds up completely removing (for me, at least) any VR sickness.
  • Realistic Physics - The game's selling point and for a good reason. Physics feel extremely real making immersion extremely easy.
  • Guns - Ever played a shooting game and felt like your arms were shaking? That's pretty normal without a stock. This game gets it right even without one, the weapon does not shake as much as it would making shooting more consistent.
  • Melee - Albeit not as accurate as the guns system, melee feels quite satisfying and the array of weapons varies so much it's always fun to try a different weapon and figure out the best way to use it.

Personally, I feel extremely immersed when playing this game. So much that even if I stop every now and then to check calls or messages, getting back into it is super fast without feeling like I'm just "playing a game" but living it.

There really aren't any points I could consider negative about this game. Sure, there are tons of extra-things I would have loved to see in the game, such as multiplayer, but being a developer myself it's easy to understand why they didn't implement it.

In short
If you can run the game, get it. The price is perfectly fair, the game is in a ready and finished state (unlike most other VR games) and it is fun. There's only one reason why you wouldn't want this game, but even that can be easily defeated through playing.

When should you avoid this game
If you are either:
  • New to VR
  • Prone to VR sickness
  • Only seeking relaxing games
You might want to consider skipping on getting the game. If you're set on overcoming any of these points, this game will help you. The hard way.\t\t\t\t\t
