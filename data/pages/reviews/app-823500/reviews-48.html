\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is without a doubt one of THE BEST VR games out there.

The story while isn't told very well, it is quite intriguing and lets you find it yourself as you go through these areas with messages from other people and may even find some of those people in there somewhere who are trying to survive as best as they can and have either given up or took it to themselves to lead.

The gun gameplay is very fun, the guns handle very well and are satisfying to use and enemies react to being shot in certain limbs and their limbs can even be broken which stops the enemies from attacking you as well as they were able to before, the reloading is quite satisfying and I love that you can smack a mag with another mag and doing it in a fight feels so good, the only negative I can give the guns is that the line up is a bit limited but each gun feels so good to use and is better at certain things.

Melee weapons feel good to use and are decent overall, not really as good as something like Blade and Sorcery which is mainly built around using melee weapons, but they're still satisfying to use and have good feedback and enemies do react to being hit from limbs and such with melee weapons, I also do like that 2 handed weapons are a lot more inaccurate when you're only using one hand but overall the melee is decent and can be great.

The physics can be a bit broken sometimes but they're so good overall and can create interesting physics based puzzles and everything feels weighty as it should be so picking up a box moving it or destroying it with your hands won't be as easy as using a crowbar or a sledge hammer.

The climbing can be quite janky as you have to put your hands into a fist and move them close to you as you crouch down which takes a bit of time getting used to and your body can get stuck so you would have to keep in mind your ingame body and head while climbing, the game can be very motion sickening as there's feedback to everything you do even falling down and hitting the ground will make you bounce a bit, even as someone who barely gets motion sick, it did make me a bit sick the first time but I got used to it.

The levels are quite unique and stand out from each other and they're all enjoyable and are lengthy, they're also paced well and they can be very interesting.

The voice acting is great and memorable, there's not a lot of voice acting but the voice acting there is done pretty well and builds up the mystery of what is actually happening.

The collectibles feel a lot worse to get in Boneworks than Bonelab as you have to get rid of an inventory spot for the entire level to throw them into the bin and you can find multiple in each level so sometimes you would have your inventory full of collectibles while you're still holding onto one with your other hand which makes reloading harder and is just overall more tedious than the more simple way of just popping them and not having to carry them the entire time in Bonelab.

The extra game modes are pretty enjoyable from an arena where you fight against waves of enemies to doing stuff in a sandbox room or going through a gun range as you try and get a good time, they may even have secrets that are great.

The visuals are stunning and have their own unique mysterious look to them which makes the game stand out with it's art style and it kinda makes it look very liminal space-y sometimes, it's definitely not as good as Bonelab sometimes or obviously Half Life Alyx but the game still looks good and the art style can definitely stand on it's own.

The game is REALLY performance heavy so I wouldn't recommend putting the game on the highest settings even if you have a beast of a PC as my 3060 couldn't handle more than medium settings sometimes.

Overall, Boneworks is a masterpiece and is a good example of how advanced VR can be with the right people, there's a bit of jank but if you can get used to it, it's definitely worth checking out, specially with the campaign as it's quite lengthy which is something a lot of VR games don't have and is a solid 9.5 and is definitely worth 30 bucks.\t\t\t\t\t
