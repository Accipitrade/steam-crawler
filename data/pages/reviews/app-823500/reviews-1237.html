\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhatup son! I just beat the game after about 16 hours of guns blazing, but like other SL0 games you're gonna have to beat it a few more times. There were hidden items, a few doors I didn't go through with keys I couldn't find etc. The ending I got was pretty mysterious with lots of imagery from another game. This game takes much of the physics you'd find in other games like Blade and Sorcery, Climby, Hellsplit Arena, or Pavlov and combines them bringing VR to another level.

-My only major gripe is that... well you know how in HL2 you might try to stack barrels to get to a higher ledge and then your feet would get caught or you'd start clipping and the barrels would fall over then you gotta pick'em back up and try again? That sort of thing happens a lot and can be frustrating.- **(They fixed this in an update!!) This has been one of the coolest games I've ever played. Playing this at 120hz with k'nuckles controllers is just mindblowing. I mean... this game is a MUST if you have an Index. It pushes games forward with the kind of interactivity that people have always dreamed VR could be. I've played Alyx--and it's a great game--but my mind always wanders back to this game for how much is pushes the envelope. This has become one of my favorite games of all time and I cannot recommend it enough.\t\t\t\t\t

Pros:
Physics
Guns are attached to your hand, which is attached to your arm, the arm bone is connected to the chest bone, the chest bone is connected to the, leg bone
This means that if you are standing on something and start to shoot, ya boi Newton uses his 3rd law to propel you in the equal and opposite direction.
Magazines can be ejected if another magazine forces it out. Saves on reloading time.
I crushed a dude with a door.
I repeatedly curb stomped a king until his crown fell off.
I didn't have to pay for Getting Over It with Bennett Foddy because I can already do that in this game. (To be fair, I already did this half a year ago with Blade and Sorcery to climb that wooden shack)
Sound Design
Everything sounds like it would in real life.
The reloading looks and feels great, but what sells it is the individual sounds that each movement makes.
Music is a bop
Especially the radio.
Replayability
Each level is basically a physics sandbox with multiple ways to beat it.
Ammo
Ammo is only spent at vending machines, shooting bullets, or cocking the gun while there is one in the chamber.
See Pros-Physics-3
Comfort
Snap turning is the best I have used in a game.
Never felt even slightly motion sick. I feel better than that feeling you get after you stand up too fast
Game Feel
A mix between Half Life, and the Matrix, with a Duck Game aftertaste
NoodleDog™
Cons:
Physics
You can't aim guns properly because the stocks lean against your head and body. Sometimes the gun gets pushed by your left wrist as well.
Sound Design
When you are dying, your character literally says "I'm dying!" Bit on the nose.
Replayability
Sadly enough I already took all of the routes on my way to the exit.
Haptic Feedback
The game isn't worth the nerve damage
Obvious inspiration from other games
No spoiler (I don't think) but at the end of the game, you see Chamber 2, which sounds like portal's testing chamber naming system.
Stuff written on the walls repeats itself. Reminds me of "the cake is a lie"
No spoilers, half life stuff is still half life stuff
You die in the game, you die in real life. The Matrix.
Comfort
The game prides itself on having that force grab thing, but I find myself bending over 4 times every 20 seconds (nice).
Melee weapons and physics props require constant grip on vive, something that gets very tiring after a while due to the wands high actuation force.
Levels can last around an hour long. Sometimes a breather is needed, and you won't want to be standing for 4 hours at a time.

Overall: The game is a dystopian tech demo. I would have liked it better if it was a tech demo, and the writing on the wall (literally) mocks people for calling it a tech demo, but that's what it is. Don't get me wrong, it's a really good tech demo. I have ~15 hours in NVidia VR funhouse.
Upon further explanation of the plot and slow motion, I give this game a 9/10 The groundwork is here, but there is some definite room for improvement: community mods, leaderboards, custom maps, just something.\t\t\t\t\t

It's just there to throw some enemies and puzzles at you. I wish I could give this a medium review rather than a thumbs down. I beat the game, and I don't regret buying it, but I'm not a big fan of it.

Cons: little to no story, boring levels and enemies, janky physics at times, bad climbing, annoying quick slots, clumsy controls, rifles that have to be held far from your body to aim, a terrible save system where you must beat a level to save progress (this has been fixed), little challenge, lose your weapons at the start of each level, poor enemy AI

THE GOOD

I don't know how to say this without sounding like I'm just hyping this title up. But I genuinely believe that this is going to be a landmark historical title that we're going to look back on as the moment VR gaming started to shift towards what we hoped and imagined it would be. In the same way that Half Life 2 really brought physics into games, I feel like the groundwork that Stress Level Zero has laid here will develop into something absolutely incredible down the road. Every game that they have developed has pushed the industry and other developers forward and it's been really cool following their journey and seeing the little ripples move out from the splashes Brandon and his team have made. The attention to detail and fine tuning of all physical collisions is the best I have ever seen in all of gaming, If you are holding a hooked item and think "wouldn't it be cool if I could reach out and hook that xyz... you always can. The VR physics body takes a bit of mastery. But surely enough, as you adapt this game becomes one hell of an experience and the expectation for games moving forward. A new standard has been set.

The game itself does a FANTASTIC job of setting up a good atmospheric world to explore and the soundtrack that compliments everything is the kind of soundtrack that makes you search out the OST out of genuine interest. The world is interesting and fun to explore and play with and the attention to detail in the all the physical interactions makes exploring and fooling around rewarding enough to help find more of all the little hidden gems. The visuals are quite nicely polished as are the combat and gun play with the stabbing and shooting really shining.

Actually the interactions generally are just really damn enjoyable. Everyone I've thrown in here to show off the tech have been impressed by the sense of presence and immersion and ah screw it one more time I can not stress how cool it feels to be able to interact with everything in the world either with the controllers, your tools or your full IK enabled physics body. They've given you such an amazing amount of options and ways to play and it's super interesting to see how different people play with that freedom. The level of care that went into setting up all the object collisions and interactions really makes a difference. Hooking a crowbar on a locker handle? Breaching doors by slamming into them with your shoulder while holding an assault rifle? Half cAwKiNG a gun to check whether or not a bullet is still in the chamber? Grabbing an enemy and headbutting them to death? Using anything as a weapon, tool, or building block? The attention to detail REALLY pays off in VR when you can be so hands on with the world, it's just a joy to play with and goof around in, again, a new standard has been set here. VR games should be more like this.

THE COULD BE IMPROVED

I've run into some /minor/ frustrations during game play but some of them I learned require a certain technique or some minor technical tweaking.... the added gun wobble on time slowing and occasional little glitches with the getting stuck in physics objects can be a little silly to deal with from time to time.... and I had to adjust my floor to - 15 cm and use a sequence of planting my hands and crouching both in real life and in game to ledge climb or mantle properly. But that's really it for me and I honestly expect some minor shakiness with cutting edge physics and feature implementation. I think it would be nice if the game helped you just a little bit more with these climbs but, even still. I eventually learned to speed dash things so that's cool.

The atmosphere is great because how can you NOT be immersed, but I feel like there's a major opportunity missed here in respect to the lack of in game audio interaction with the player. That's the ONE thing I feel would really tip the balance for this title to go from a really impressive game to a great game. Portal and Bastion are both EXCELLENT examples of this kind of design. As you progress through the game, the game talks to you about what you're doing, when you're doing it. Neither of them really repeat lines to bother the player, but they have a ton of recorded interactions that reward the player with little bits of humour, intrigue or lore. Glados and Rux help further establish a tone for the games and help contribute to the story in a way that ends up being the more memorable part of the experience over the puzzles, as interesting as those are. It kind of gently weaves a light story in a way that feels more personal for the player and creates these little aha moments for the player. (Not that the game doesn't already have those lol) I appreciated the little contact videos from the outside world, but I feel like there could have been a little more going on to flesh out the audio interactions. Duck season had lots of little fun things sprinkled throughout it. Lots of things played out to weave the story and jokes in. More of that energy here would have been a joy to explore.

That said this game is totally my favourite experience with VR so far and I feel like I've barely really scratched the surface with it between showing it off to a few friends and playing up to the the games end and some light arena waves so far. I was still learning tricks into the last hour. It sounds like they're going to continue to add more features and content and things to explore and play with and by gods I hope they do.There's so much to do here and it's well worth 30$ to support this. I can't wait to see what comes out of what they've done here and again I suspect others...COUGH VALVE COUGH will be making use of the foundations built here to make more carefully crafted and fully interactive worlds. Hopefully the next couple worlds like ALYX or L4D....

Fully recommend! Tons of fun. Please support these devs!\t\t\t\t\t
