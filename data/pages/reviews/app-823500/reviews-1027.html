\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game. I really do. The concept, play, physics are all incredible and a lot of fun. I appreciate the variety and deep level of attention to detail that went into this game. But I cannot get past the oversight in not including a quicksave option, or at the very least, more frequent checkpoints. I spent nearly an hour and a half working through a stage until I got completely stumped at a puzzle (inb4 git gud scrub), and had to put my headset down. There's so much working in a good way for this game, especially as an experimental physics based VR title, but I can't help but have a sour taste in my mouth considering the lack of an ability to save as you go. Would highly recommend, but beware if you are unable to grind out long sessions in a VR headset.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBy far one of the best VR games out there right now. The experimental physics 100% make the game better, and make the experience so much more enjoyable. The campaign felt satisfying and gave a different challenge of loosing all your weapons each level.

You don't even need to look for secrets to enjoy this game. The added addition of the skull crates with different items in them for the sandbox mode was a great addition, and makes the game more rewarding for people who like to look for secrets. The reclamation bins at the end of the levels are also such a great addition, giving you more reason to actually explore and get more stuff.

Along with the game just being a fun game, the music is one of the best soundtracks I have ever heard from a VR title. The only other VR game I can think of that had a soundtrack near as good as this one is the budget cut.

All and all this game is great, and if you are thinking about buying it, and have 30 extra dollars, I would 100% recommend you purchase this game and play it for yourself!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoy this game. It's the first VR game that has the realistic hand physics that don't make me want to rip out my eyes. (keep in mind I have not yet finished the game, and the reason is in this review)

What this game did good.
1. Physics-phenomenal. The selling point for the game really did not disappoint. Having realistic yet enjoyable controls for traversing, opening crates, and fighting is amazing.

2. Puzzles- Okay. Some of the puzzles are cool and interesting while others are just annoying. The one where you have to use an antimatter thing on some barrels to raise an object wasn't difficult, nor was it fun.

3. Level Design- Not bad. Most of the levels are cool and fun to play. Granted, there are annoying places in most levels, but that's to be expected. The slip 'n' slide thing in the fifth or so mission sucked, and I have no idea why it's in the game. I really enjoyed the level where everything was dark and you had to traverse the place with a flashlight.

4. Enemies- Mixed. I like most of the enemies. The headsets are the most bulletproof things ever, but that makes it more challenging, which is fine with me. I like how you can stick them on an enemy's face. The zombie dudes are cool, but I feel like they were downgraded after the first 2 missions you see them in. When you pop out of the grate and land in a dumpster in mission 5(I think) and see those zombie dudes with blue cuts on their face, it's scary. Then you notice that there's twenty of them, and they shrug off rifle bullets to the face. Later they die by one pistol whip. Overall, the enemies are cool, but did the developers really have to make the untextured guys talk? That was legitimately terrifying the first time I heard it.

Now it's time to talk about the things I didn't like in the game.
1. There is really one big problem I have with the game. Sure, I can nitpick here and there, but those are surface level problems. The biggest problem I have with the game is the lag. This may not sound like much, and initially it wasn't much. I managed to get through the entire game without any lag spikes worth noting, then I got to the central terminal mission. I can't even play the game because the game refuses to catch up with what's happening. I once had to stand still for 15 minutes straight to walk forward 3 steps. That's just ridiculous. Initially I thought it was my PC being stupid, but upon replaying missions I realized this wasn't the case. Until this is fixed, I won't be able to make any progress in the game.

Despite that major problem. It's still a fun game and I feel like anyone who has a computer that can run this should definitely play this game.\t\t\t\t\t
