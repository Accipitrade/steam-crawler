\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought vr to play this game alone and I wasn't disappointed.

Pro's:
+ Lengthy campaign + Sandbox mode
+ Crazy in depth locomotion. It an unusual but great feeling to be scaling a wall with your hands or to rappel up to a ledge.
+ Plenty of possibilities in combat with the slomo mechanics, physics and plethora of weapons. Took a little bit of time to adjust to the gunplay, but once you get it, its unbelievably satisfying.
+ Great soundtrack. includes some great trance, outrun vibes and a bloody choral piece (Sunset Arthur is my personal favourite).
+ Force grab is so satisfying and helpful. Should be in every vr game.
+ It's got Brett. Whaddup, son?

Con's:
- Requires a fairly intensive system. That seems to be down to the intense af physics so thats fair enough. Got some frame dips on my 980ti and i7-6700k, will test with my 2080ti max-q laptop and post results in an edit.
- Not a massive fan of some of the puzzles. I didn't find myself getting motion sick in the game except for the puzzles. Made me bypass a lot of the optional ones and I found myself a tad frustrated at them.
- Ending may not be everyones cup of tea.
- Needs mid-level saves (but that's being added in the future.)

This game is transcendent and the killer app for vr. Bought it at full price it was worth every single penny times ten. May need you to warm up your vr legs a bit but I feel like everyone with a vr headset should own this.
