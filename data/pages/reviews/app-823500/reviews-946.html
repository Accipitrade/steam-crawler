\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is arguably the most revolutionary VR game at this point, whilst simultaneously providing a fluent shooter experience. I'd argue it's better than Half-Life Alyx, which only wins in terms of visuals and the inclusion of a shotgun, but loses in the sheer amount of interactivity that Boneworks' physics engine allows. Want to headbutt a discount headcrab? Do it. Want to throw a crate at an enemy? Do it. Want to blast enemies with a shotgun? They'll hopefully be one eventually. Another great thing about Boneworks is that it's developers support for it. They've added better mid-level saves in the form of checkpoints, added new sandbox items and locations (did I fail to mention there's a sandbox mode for experimentation?). They are also adding more weapons, sandboxes, etc and polishing the game almost continuously. There are only two cons I can really think of. One is the intensity of Boneworks, as it's more-so designed for people with VR experience but, if you have a VR headset I'd hope you wouldn't get it just for Boneworks, so the solution is to play Boneworks in short bursts and play other games for longer sessions before buying (as it allows you to build up to the motion sickness). The other is the physics engine, despite its greatness, still needs cleaning up (which is being done every major update). In conclusion? Boneworks is a masterpiece of game design that has the perfect blend of physics, spectacle and gameplay. Boneworks isn't just a tech demo, it's the beginning of the future for VR. Personally, I'm looking forward to Stress Level Zero's next game.\t\t\t\t\t
