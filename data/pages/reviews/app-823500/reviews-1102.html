Does YOUR Bone Work?

BONEWORKS is ultimately a little difficult to review. This game falls into the same category of "formative VR games" that many others like Job Simulator, GORN, and others are in too. These are games that I still find quality but have issues with, ones I ultimately forgive due to what they pulled off when they came out.

BONEWORKS falls into this category, but I thankfully can say, with some confidence, that most of the game will age well. With that said, I do have some not insignificant problems with it as a game, regardless of the genuine advancements made here.

This game is the first time I played a VR game and immediately felt a HUGE level of believable immersion based almost solely on the game's mechanics and the harmony between any one system's individual functionality and the way they interacted with everything else. Your body is fully rendered and collides with itself, something MUCH more common now due to how well this game pulled it off. Practically every single thing in the game has physics, is interactable, can be picked up, and has believably simulated weight and heft to it. Mechanics like climbing, jumping, running, crouching, punching, and much more actually consider the physics of your own body, sometimes to the detriment of the experience but almost always to the game's benefit. The guns in the game are more complex than I expected yet simple and intuitive, and they offer a great platform that modders absolutely ran off with.

BONEWORKS is split into Story, Sandbox, and Arena modes with an overall great amount of content in the vanilla game and a rich modding community for when it begins to turn stale. The campaign is what most are likely buying the game for, and thankfully, besides the dogsh*t story, it is a more than competent offering. The world of Monogon's weird VR city is, to me, reminiscent of the dregs of Aperture in Portal 2 with its parallel of the neat and clean modern test chambers with the messy grave of Old Aperture and its lemon bombs.

As I said, the story is stupid, but the real issue is how poorly told it is. I've owned this game since its week of release, and I only learned literally yesterday that the game is about "achieving immortality". Disconnects between what this game thinks is a story and the environments you play in are a problem, because nobody making this game felt the need to just f**king tell you anything. Ultimately this is not an issue, but I did have questions when I went from a techno-metaverse Clocktower to getting into a fistfight with myself as crown-king of a rejected Wizard of Oz sequel.

Get To The Point

Buy the game (or BONELABS, or both). Ultimately this is gonna be a crucial piece of the VR time capsule, one that shows a significant step in connecting the many experimental sandboxes and simulators that other VR devs were making into a coherent, fun, and worthwhile package. The modding community is great, and the developers are interested in seeing their projects mature. BONELABS comes out tomorrow as of writing, and so far, it shows promise, genuinely great steps forward, and a lot of forethought put into how we play these games.\t\t\t\t\t
