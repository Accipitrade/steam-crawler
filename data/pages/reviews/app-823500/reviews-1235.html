\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a game that almost makes use of the full potential of VR (Everything except full body tracking) plus the regional pricing is amazing (Got this for about 12 USD)

The game has a nice balance between combat and puzzles and both are equally fun thanks to the game's physics. Some of the puzzles can be completed in multiple ways plus the game is fully playable from start to finish and has an interesting story that most VR games are missing.

One of the cons (for now since the devs are already working on a solution) is the lack of a save system. You need to finish a map in order for your progress to be saved and each map takes about 80 minutes at leisure pace so if you don't have a lot of time, it's not a good idea to play this game.

However, I still think that this game is the pioneer of the next generation of VR games.
It's simply a must-have for anybody with a VR headset.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not going to lie, I was really hyped for this game. Now it just feels super underwhelming. I'm not sure if it's the fact that I'm playing with the standard controllers, but the controls feel wrong. The constant physical and audible buzzing coming from the controllers isn't helping either. I couldn't even get past the tutorial, not because it's hard, but because it's just too much. Simple mechanics like jumping feel way too complicated. The force grabbing feels really weird. I feel like I'm just nit-picking at this point, I'm going to stop now. I get that they worked really hard on this game, but I didn't really even have fun while playing it, it just felt like I was watching a video of someone playing it, if that makes sense, I don't know.

Update: I've beaten the game, It took about 6 hours, the constant buzzing never stopped. The last level of the game is about 90% climbing, which is the hardest thing to do in the game, it's just climbing, with short breaks, for about half an hour. I now understand the story of the game, I still feel like this game was made with the index controllers in mind, so the "wand controllers" are an absolute nightmare to use, after an hour of playtime, your hands and fingers are sore.

Update 2: This is a good game. I think the only problem with it was the hype it got before it came out. I felt like this game was going to be life changing or something. It isn't a bad at all, but there are certainly things I don't like about it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is quite practically one of the best VR games I have ever played. Let's start with the graphics,

GRAPHICS

Graphics are visually appealing from place to place, but rather basic. I suppose that the game's storyline and locations are partly to blame, as the game's locations are rather bland until (end of story areas). 1.5/2

SOUND DESIGN / MUSIC

This game has the best soundtrack out of any VR game I have ever played before. I mean, BONETONES is worth a buy in itself. Gun sounds sound great and melee combat clings and tings are very satisfying. 2/2

STORYLINE/BUGS

I originally thought that this would be where the game would fall based on the reviews. However, the story is very much so a MINDF$CK rather than some in-depth storyline. The story really gets going by the end of the game and I found myself relatively satisfied. Although, janky physics (at times) ruin some of the immersion in the world/story. 1.5/2

PACING

Pacing is done rather well for the first-half of the game as you are getting 1 or 2 new enemy types per level. However, this really starts to slow down as you get into the second half the story's campaign. New weapons can also be found throughout each level, but are very hidden. 1.5/2

GAMEPLAY/LENGTH

This is going to sound rather strange, but hear me out, this game does nothing perfectly, (maybe physics) but does almost every VR concept known to man very well. Climbing is fun, Combat is engaging, and Puzzles are well-thought-out and are able to be quite a challenge.The game took me just under 12 hours to complete for me. This game is a FULL-VR game, not "Just a Tech Demo" and it even has an additional ARENA mode with tons of challenges to complete. Now THIS, is a FULL VR game. 2/2

With all of that said, this game gets a 8.5/10 (GREAT) and I have to warrant this game a VR MUST-PLAY.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest game on vr at the moment in my opinion (with some annoying edges)

The shining feature in this game is the physics and the way you can interact with just about anything in the game. learning how to manipulate an enemy in the sandbox mode, john wick style, and take them out by first stabbing them in the leg and pulling a specific body part to see them come tumbling down, is endlessly entertaining and is something that you can only do in this game. figuring out what is possible (which is just about anything) is a big part of what makes this game special, as the physics based gameplay allows for you do basically anything you could in real life. however this isn't to say the movement is perfect. due to the fact that you have a body in this game, it has a very bad habit of getting in the way when youre climbing or completing other tasks. melee weapons feel too heavy in some cases, and others (particularly the swords) feel like they dont really swing much at all. when wielding spears and other two handed long weapons, your arms will get in the way of eachother meaning you cant really spin them round very smoothly, also the fact that your wrist feel like they're made of rubber does not help as the weapons no longer feel as sturdy or solid in your hands. this is not to say that the melee is worthless, its slower paced and just takes some getting used to (especially if you have gone into the game thinking its like blade and sorcery). ive spend so many hours repeatedly bashing people over the head with bricks and other random melee weapons. Thankfully the gunplay is near perfect, if you don't care about melee then this is the game for you, as every ranged weapon feels great to use. However if you're like me, you'll want to mix them up, and hit people with the butt of your gun, then turn and shoot the enemies that are out of your reach. the fact that you can use your gun as a melee weapon in a way true to life, screams innovation and creativity. Perhaps maybe a bit more ranged weapon variation would be nice but we shall see in the future.

The story mode is a god send of a mode, its like you're playing the story of portal 2 in the world of half life 2 with so many cool little easter eggs that feel like they came right out of portal. I also didn't expect the story to be as long as it did, taking me about 10-11 hours to complete, which by the way was an absolute blast. the storyline is meta as hell and kept me seriously gripped the entire time, which is most certainly something that no other vr title has done for me. there is a decent split between combat and puzzle platforming, however i found myself spending a lot of time just figuring out what i was supposed to do. The level design here is not great, leaving you with a lot of frustrating walking and wondering where you have to go. The biggest issue i had by far was platforming. As your body tends to get in the way and your hands like to phase through walls at times, means climbing can be a pain in the ass, especially when your arms like to move by themselves when trying to reach parallel ledges. The weight physics feel so overdone when all youre trying to do is lift a plank onto an elevator, or place it between two beams to get across a gap, which ends up taking like 30 minutes. again youre rubber wrists will screw with you while trying to move heavy objects, making you dread anytime you have to move something heavy. perhaps some sort of physics assist on weapons/objects just to make gameplay more enjoyable. I can see what they were going for in a total physics real world, but gets frustrating.

overall, this game is seriously like no other but takes a massive learning curve before you really get to grips with it. Spending a decent amount of time in the sand box mode to get a feel for the climbing and combat is seriously advised, and expect to spend about an hour or two of game time before you start to feel comfortable with the mechanics. Considering this game offers 3 modes, all of which are outstanding, I can say this game is worth every penny, if anything i feel undercharged. The sandbox mode offers endless hours of fun and really is one of the best parts of the game, it allows me to stretch my imagination and try things that are just simply not possible in other games at the moment. I expect other games to build upon boneworks and use it as the new bar for vr games, and any other new title that doesn't have these levels of complexities will instantly turn me off the game. im straight up addicted to this game XD. I would 100% recommend this game to any vr enthusiast, but 100% expect this game to annoy the hell out of you. By the way, the people who down vote this game for the reasons ive specified are the same people who only played the game fort half an hour.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, I absolutely love this game, however, I feel like a few points should be made.

Physics can be a little janky at times. Limbs will get stuck in objects, and sometimes objects are just too hard to move to make sense. For instance, there is a part where the player is required to move a large beam. Its absolutely impossible to manuver and once you do, there is a good chance itll fall.

Climbing on top of things is not fantastic. The thought of using your arms to climb is amazing, but the fact of having to hold the grip buttons, bend all the way down and touch the floor, and also press a button on my controller is mildly absurd. It takes way to long to climb things, and constantly makes me try to avoid getting on something.

Story is... there. Its not forced by any means, however, and is extremely easy to gloss over. You really have to look in order to realize what its about.

Enemies feel spongy. Its just strange. Pushing and interacting with the humanoid enemies feels... weird. The enemies also tend to feel kinda bullet spongy, which is fun for playing, however absolutely impossible to tell when you kill them.

Finally, its not player friendly controller and HMD wise. Ive played games for hours on end with VR and never had a bit of motion sickness, however this game gives me major motion sickness in about an hour. There is a lot of movement, as well as a ton of falling and climbing, which really disorients most players. I found that its bareable, however still not fantastic. Its impossible to use slow motion with the vive, as pressing the topmost button as well as the track pad feels clunky and typically you dont actually move. Ive also found my thumb to get extremely tired and sore because of attempting to sprint everywhere or using slow motion. The inventory system works, however holstering on the hip is terrible. You cant be looking at your hip or it drops to the ground, and its very dodgy to pick up from it as well. Picking out of any of the weapon slots is dodgy, there was a multitude of times where I found that grabbing a gun out of my armpit took a ton of attempts.

So, to finish, I absolutely loved this game. In my opinion it will absolutely change how VR is done in the future. Its not without its flaws, but it is nevertheless impressive at what was done. Amazing job, Stress Level Zero.\t\t\t\t\t
