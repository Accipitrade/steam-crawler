\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros
An amazing game if you have the system to play it

Cons
Buggy and causes a lot of motion sickness. Only buy if you can handle intense VR games

I really want to love this game. I really want to recommend it.
I watched Node religiously as they game was developed and it is the main reason that I shelled out on an index. I highly rate the game creators and really want to write a positive review. But I can't. I am not a hater.

The issue I have is the game is too buggy. My arms get stuck inside objects like bins. Text and textures flicker and distort (a known AMD issue) and the in-game adaptive resolution reduces the game down to a blurry mess. And for some reason it is the only game that triggers intense motion sickness after 10 minutes of play.

I am gutted I can't enjoy the game I most looked forward to. I can't progress past the 3rd level because the save points are too spaced out; I end up feeling sick and have to quit so I'm caught in a loop of trying to get through the same level over and over. I've tweaked every setting and even upgraded my rig to try and resolve the issue.

I know veteran VR users will say motion sickness isn't the developers fault, but I don't get sick playing Pavlov, Blade & Sorcery or Alyx, all with similar motion mechanics.

So in summary this is an amazing VR game, with bugs and flaws that seem to be related to AMD cards. I would only recommend buying it if you know you don't suffer from VR motion sickness, because it will break your heart if you can't play it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great purchase for experienced gamers with their first significant jump into VR.

Boneworks is a VR Shooter with vague motifs of Hardcore Henry, Half-Life and Tron.
Unlike some products, Boneworks has full body physics. Your full body is realised in the VR play space. It's surreal but almost immediately becomes familiar, particularly on the index controllers.

I would recommend standing play for the complete experience, seated just doesn't emulate it.

The game's emphasis on physics puzzles,climbing and generally trashing your environment to look for secrets never stops feeling engaging. Exhaustive at times but very engaging and very gratifying when you finally cheese the game and climb around areas you aren't meant to. Which the game admits in the opening puzzle tutorial, it's okay.

The other two main forms of gameplay are the gunplay and the melee combat. One felt great almost immediately, the other took a lot of getting used to but ultimately was incredibly gratifying. Guns function in a way that is isn't too complicated or simple. You're given enough freedom with the weapon to position it as you wish and reload as you please. The arsenal of weaponry does feel somewhat small but you'll always be happy to see certain weapons like the M1911 which was my handgun of choice or the MK18 which honestly is probably the best gun in the game.

The melee combat takes A LOT of getting use to, how to swing each of the weapons properly because they all have tangible weight to them. I had a lot more fun with the machete, axe, swords and the dagger which basically cheesed nearly a whole mission for me. Hand to Hand combat is probably the best one of them all, grabbing enemies and punching them in the gut or head. It gets even more interesting but i won't spoil it too much. Mix that up with gun combat and you're doing some gun fu. Chow Yun-Fat/John Woo style. It really does just take a lot of learning to master.

The game's difficulty always feels challenging up until a bit of a weird spike in the last encounter before the epilogue. If you're good at game and their conventions then you'll probably be okay but anyone more new to gaming mechanics and concepts might find this game hard and overwhelming at times, to the point where i don't know if i can recommend it.
Boneworks loves throwing everything at you sometimes both Combat and Physics wise.

The game's more narrative elements are difficult to judge for me, it's complicated with many threads but you are able to piece what is going on if you are paying attention. It's not FNAF levels of lore. It's tongue in cheek and not the clear focus of the game.

Graphics and Environments are probably the most clear and easy to interact with that i've currently tried in VR. Not sure how this stacks up to VR vets, drop a comment.

It's still an absolute blast that i can recommend to anyone that owns a index/knuckles VR setup.\t\t\t\t\t
