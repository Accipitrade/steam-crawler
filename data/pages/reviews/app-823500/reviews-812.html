RECOMMENDED

Graphics: 9/10 - Very good graphics. Consider they’re stylized to fit the environment and the story so there’s a lot of wireframe NPC’s. The environments amazing on the Vive Pro and that, alone, makes this game extremely immersive. More than many other VR games.
Sound: 10/10 - The little dialogue is delivered really well and at points in the story that matter. However the soundtrack, oof… Hands down the soundrack is absolutely FANTASTIC. I couldn’t get enough of Opera de Ossium, Bonetheme and the iconic Pick it up, a beautiful nod at Portal and Still Alive, executed beautifully.
Gameplay: 9/10 - The inverse kinematics implemented in this game and the actual mass given to the player puts this game at an entire different level, however those same features allow for some weird glitches to happen now and then (nothing game breaking though) The level of immersion and variety of playstyles allowed by granting the player a huge level of freedom to solve problems is superb.
Story: 8/10 - This game is part of Stress Level Zero’s game universe, most of the story is told by the environment and the clues you find in it. Some cut scenes give a little more exposition but it is up to the player to catch the gist of it all. For me it took me a while and I had to go online to find some “pieces of the puzzle” I was missing.

Overall Score: 9/10
Extra Notes: This game is the breath of fresh air VR needed. A lot of people call it “a glorified tech demo” and they might be right. But trying to pass that as an insult is absolute nonsense.
These Devs managed in VR what HL2 did for PC gaming way back when. Both games tried and succeded on stuff no one had before and set a bar really effin’ high for others to try and follow.
If you’re the proud owner of a VR headset and a capable computer, and haven’t tried this masterpiece, do yourself a favor and buy it. It’s worth every cent. Just bear in mind that it’s not for the faint of heart and one should already be used to the particular feeling of movement in virtual reality.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I get to the review, I want to say that I have only played the main story and have not yet played the other modes. Now onto the review:

This game is amazing. The physics puzzles, the fighting/shooting action, and even the suspense I got from wondering what I need to do next based on the somewhat vague plot were all super fun, immersive, and at times a bit scary for me (I really can't handle horror, so even the times in the story where you had to walk into dark places with or without enemies got my heart rate up).

This game is so far the best VR game that seems extremely realistic even if the environment and entities might not seem as such. The natural physics prevents me from getting motion sickness, and it also greatly increased my immersion. The only issues I really had with the physics simulation were that during melee fighting and using my hands to interact with the environment, my virtual hands wouldn't really follow my actual hands that well, but I'm pretty sure that it all boils down to the fact that I'm playing on a Rift S. Even so, I still found both of those activities to be extremely enjoyable because the interaction with weapons and the environment felt extremely natural.

The gun use was definitely my favorite part of the game, as it really made me feel like I was holding the guns in my hands, and the physical interaction between the gun and my body/the environment added to the experience. I loved every second I had a gun in my possession to fight off enemies because it gave me the opportunity to escape reality for a bit and just experience taking out tons of enemies with guns.

This is also definitely a game I'd come back to play again for the main story and especially to try the other arcade-style modes like zombies and the fantasy arena.

10/10 would recommend to any VR gamers or even to non-VR gamers just to see what VR can do right now\t\t\t\t\t

Overall opinion.
BoneWorks is a fantastic VR game that shows the potential of current VR kits like the Valve Index, with full index support the game can track individual fingers using the sensors in the controllers themselves.
This leads to the player being able to touch and grab almost everything and use those items for different purposes. 8/10

Game Theme
To say that BoneWorks has a specific theme is extremely hard, as the whole game is about exploring the different things that VR can accomplice. The game includes different game elements, but none that dominate others too much.

Pros:
Good optimization
Decent movement
Good gun mechanics.

Cons:
Minimal story (Almost forgot there even is one)
melee can feel clunky.

Info:
The game is made with the user strongly in mind, the game's optimization had been focused on heavily to give the user the best experience with the game.

Alongside the optimization, the game also has decent movement, it's not something overly complex, but it's a more reliable movement system compared to other games.

The gun mechanics in the game are based on how you would reload and champer a weapon in real life. While the recoil and stability are far from how it is, in reality, they still made the gun-mechanics some of the best and most thought through mechanics around.

The game is more of a demonstration of how VR can be used to make different scenes, so the very short story is easily forgotten throughout the game.

Where the game succeeds in ranged combat with guns, the game will in some cases feel weird when it comes around blunt and bladed weapons. There will be moments then the melee weapons will feel impactful as you would expect, but there will also be moments when it will feel like they just aren't hitting as they should.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRunning an gunning never felt so satisfying. (I don't believe there are any spoilers in this)

That being said, this game is better off as your third or fourth FPS VR title (that phrase sounds redundant). My recommendation to you for your entrance into this genre would be Super Hot (low skill level), Half-Life Alyx (low-mid skill level), then this; that way you can get accustomed to more realistic shooting and movement without vomiting (thanks to the movement). Train yourself to be able to move through the motion sickness when using thumbstick navigation if you haven't already, it'll be worth the pain. I'm sure there are other games you can use in place of either of my suggestions but these are the first that come to mind in order to better appreciate some of the features/mechanics this game has to offer it'd be worth while to have a bit of experience and a few hours under your belt. I enjoyed this game wholeheartedly but I did have to go lie down after torturing myself for the first hour with the controls. Once you beat the story I recommend the horde/survival modes. There are easy ways to cheese some of them with enough credits but if you reach that point the way I did it'll feel earned to just massacre your enemies with ease.

good shootin', have an iron stomach\t\t\t\t\t
