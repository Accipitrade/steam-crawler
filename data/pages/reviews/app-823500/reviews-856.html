\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter lots of consideration, I think the experience of playing though BONEWORKS was the first time VR felt worth it . It was the first VR game that didn't feel like a tech demo. And playing it is likely the best VR experience out right now.


Preface

However, it's not for everyone. I had very little problems with motion sickness, but I've read enough reviews of the game to know that I'm in the minority here. I've also seen many comparisons to HL:A. I haven't played it yet, but after seeing some gameplay, I can see the source of the comparisons. HL:A reaps the benefits of being connected to a much larger, more popular series. Despite everything I'm about to say in the next section, HL:A probably has a better story for that exact reason.


The Story

If you like the kinds of stories seen in movies (plentiful cutscenes, memorable characters, actual conversations between characters) in your games, this isn't the game for you. BONEWORKS has a story that's deeper than it seems, and it hides itself in sparse cutscenes, worldbuilding text blocks, and random writings on walls. You might not understand what the everlovin' ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• just happened after your first playthrough. And I love it.

I love it because it had tells a complex story without the support of a big studio like Valve or an already existing game universe.


The Gameplay

The physics are to die for. Being able to grab any place on a melee weapon short of a blade expands your combat options exponentially. Being able to use objects in the room to eliminate enemies is priceless. Wait, what am I talking about? Just watch the trailers if you want to know more about the physics. Especially the release date trailer.
EDIT- Looked at some more reviews, and a lot of people have problems with the physics, mostly due to the virtual body (especially the hands) getting stuck on objects. I'm not sure what to say about it, other than the fact that it was never a major problem for me.

I thought the environments were usually cool places to explore. They're not re-inventing anything new in that department, but they serve their purpose well enough for me.

The guns feel properly arcade-like. For manually reloaded guns, their operation is focused on the player having fun, rather than being accurately recreated firearms. The gunplay is very solid, and reloading is clean and crisp. I've had tons of fun just practicing reloading in Sandbox.

I commonly see complaints about how clunky the melee combat feels. I also found it to be clunky, until I practiced melee combat more in Zombie Warehouse. It feels like an actual fight, a no-rules, brutal, and dangerous struggle. You'll have to push, grab, headbutt, stab, slam, and lodge hammer claws into enemies only to get them stuck. But at the same time, it can be as simple as a good swing to the skull if you know what you're doing.

Things like Zombie Warehouse, Arena, and Sandbox add many hours of fun to the experience. Just today in the Sandbox, I used a device similar to a gravity gun to propel myself forward in a shopping cart towards a crowd of clones of the main character, and rammed both a spear I was holding and the shopping cart into the crowd. 'Nuff said. Plus hopping back into story mode after you've completed it gives you the opportunity to get more things to play around with in Arena/Sandbox.


Misc.

The soundtrack is iconic-sounding. It always feels perfect for the moment, whether you're ducking behind cover and hurriedly reloading, or exploring a quiet courtyard.

The game isn't very difficult, and I found myself only dying a couple of times during the Story mode.


Conclusion

It's 1 AM when I'm writing this, alright? Just buy it already. I don't think you'll be disappointed with a game with this much variety and opportunity.\t\t\t\t\t
