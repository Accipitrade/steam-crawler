\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs of today, this is the pinnacle of what VR is capable of. It truly captures every aspect perfectly; You can climb, shoot, throw, grab, and do puzzles that are intuitive and engaging. Combine this with a story, and you have a game that has as much impact on the VR scene that Half-life had on the gaming scene.

Sure, other VR games have their own specific things that make them good, but the rest of the mechanics seem unrealistic and heavily unfinished. This game combines all the right elements, and combines them to something that is perfect. You can't just pick up a sledgehammer and whack-a-mole away with one hand, things have weight, and you have to take that into consideration. You can't move that large boulder with little effort, you have to put your whole body into it and actually do what you do in real life; Find the right angle and increase power gradually. Shooting entails that you have to reload exactly how you would in real life, and aim correctly. No more crosshairs (what are crosshairs doing in a VR game??), no more "touch this button and we will reload for you".

It also includes puzzles with multiple solutions, and the world can be traversed through multiple ways. See the fence over there? Climb it and find an alternative route, or maybe a new weapon. What happens if you take the ladder, rather than walking straight ahead? These small things creates a better environment, where you don't feel limited at all.

Boneworks is fun, it is unique, and it has an incredible charm to it that other VR games need to take note of.\t\t\t\t\t
