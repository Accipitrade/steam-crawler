\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe bad:
You do not get to use guns in combat until after the tutorial area, which is incredibly boring. I did not like the first hour or so of gameplay at all. The story is bad, not because there isn't one, but it isnt explained at all. The ENTIRE time I played the game I had no idea why stuff was happening, and I don't think a good story should be incredibly vague to the point where you need to watch lore explanation videos on youtube. Enemy types are repetitive, and basically The Walking Dead zombies in terms of how they attack. Overall the physics felt okay, but I think other games feel way better to play in some ways (B&S swordplay is inexplicably better than in this game, for example.)

The good:
The game is most fun to play with guns to me, and in the later levels I felt like John Wick with how much fast paced combat I had to deal with. This has been the only VR game that has genuinely scared me, though, once you know the few enemy types in the game, you will know what to expect around every corner. This is also one of the most immersing VR games I have played since I was actually invested in the gameplay, and not just messing around in a no-stakes sandbox. At one point I tried jumping in real life to reach a ledge, instead just smacking my real hand into the ceiling. I did enjoy the soundtrack quite a bit, also.\t\t\t\t\t
