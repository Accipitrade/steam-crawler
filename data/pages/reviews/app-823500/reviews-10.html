\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off no hate to Boneworks (despite my review not to get it). However it's just my taste in video games and especially VR games.

I'm a big fan of the Portal series of Valve and when playing the first hour it screamed of Portal to me and I thought that this might be a huge fun VR game for me. However the feeling or rather the emotion wasn't there. Although Boneworks wasn't made to have a immersable campaign. It's main objective was to give the player the best physics and gun play for it's time and btw they f'ing nailed it. The fact you can climb with a pair of crowbars on a wall is insane and grabbing a mans leg and splating their head is fun.

However if you're like me, interested in a slightly open world interesting story campaign. Boneworks may not be for you. The levels are so open and bland sometimes (Some spots were really eye catching) and puzzles don't feel as satisfying to complete to me, more as frustrating in some way (might be skill issue). Also I really got no connection to the game neither. If anything I love most is characters in games and in Boneworks there are quite literally none. It's just you alone and by yourself against no personality ai's.

So if anything, if you're here for the story it may not be the best thing. But if you're interested in playing a game that's closer to touching grass than this, you might have a bang for your buck in this game. Maybe I can return to it and change my opinion but if anything it's more of because it wanted to do something that was far off from what I expected of it and that's why I didn't enjoy it. But still props to the devs for setting a new standard to VR games.\t\t\t\t\t
