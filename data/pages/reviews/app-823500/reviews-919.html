\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBONEWORKS has enough gameplay mechanics for at least 3 games. It's far from perfect, but it does melee, ranged combat and climbing pretty well.

Pros:
+ Most extensive physics simulation in current generation of VR. It really makes the world feel alive, adds a lot to immersion and provides tons awesome and unique challenges
+ Lengthy campaign with huge world to explore + additional levels in Sandbox mode like Redacted or Gun Range
+ Realistic weapon handling, both melee and ranged

Cons:
- It's quite janky, although that's expected from a hardcore physics-based game. Things get stuck And getting your virtual legs over ledges is huge pain in the ass
- Enemy AI is atrocious, human-like opponents can barely walk. That spoils the fun of combat quite a lot
- Slashing attacks in melee feel weird. Also there is no dismemberment/decapitation
- Body item slots are very unreliable. There is a big chance to drop item while storing it or pick wrong weapon from the holsters
- Item unlock system for Sandbox|Arena is too tedious. You need to carry everything to the storage bin. Why not just unlock items when they are encountered?
- Story is way too obscure. I had to watch Game Theorists videos to make any sense of it)\t\t\t\t\t
