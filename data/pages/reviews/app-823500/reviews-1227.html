\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo apply the potential of Boneworks raw systems to a shooter experience is to neglect the small liberties that must be taken in order to preserve flow, comfort, and consistency in a VR game. Throughout the campaign, Boneworks' showcased many moments of frustration and disengagement whose lingering taste outweighed any flashy or creative scenarios. Simple tasks such as reloading, holstering, turning wheels, saddling ledges, and snap-turning have all been made irksome due to this commitment to unscripted motion. High-stress scenarios and jumpscares are made all the more anxiety-inducing with how objects decide to act on their own. Physical body presence promised interesting applications, yet the inverse kinematics here leads your desynchronized jello arms to find their way into walls, onto shelves, and away from where they are needed. Furthermore, the way in which forces placed back onto the player character will relocate them may be realistic, but certain cases that unexpectedly move the player threaten can be a source of motion sickness.

With the game’s sandbox off-limits until the campaign’s completion, players must struggle through long, uncomfortable environments without access to saving. When glitches sent me out-of-bounds, levels and all their finicky puzzles, had to be started from scratch. The purpose of featuring a variety of guns and two-handed weapons is sadly hindered by the redundancy of each category’s internal properties. With only three enemy types to interact with and a conceptually narrow selection of combat tools, encounters quickly become predictable. The inability to own weapons between levels also removed the potential for satisfying story-shooter progression. Without permanence, shops and using ammo as a currency bring a lack of purpose to the numerous side-secrets that offer only excess ammo as a reward.

When applied in gameplay, the prestige of Boneworks' long-awaited physics system breaks down. It's in those first few stages of non-violent concept exploration that Boneworks resonates the most.
