\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been looking forward to this game for a while and was putting it aside because I was overwhelmed with other VR titles and wanted to get used to locomotion movement before I get into this. I was looking forward to play it, because it promised a fun realistic experience in a story driven gameplay. Unfortunately the experience didn't hold up to my expectations.

It took just few minutes for me to realise that it might not be what I was hoping for. Some aspects are pretty nice (like destruction physics or gravity play). Unfortunately the rive for realism made the game experience very annoying at times, especially when it comes to the players body. To put it simply, Boneworks made me fully realise why Half-Life Alyx (HLA) has just hands: body gets in the way in ways it doesn't in real life. You cannot bend over a table, you cant punch stuff near the ground, you cannot jump with your physical (real) body, etc. I tried this for an hour and I had enough. The immersion experience (putting graphics aside) in HLA is much stronger for me, often forgetting I'm in VR, because nothing gets in the way of the experience, while in Boneworks I'm being constantly reminded that this is just a game, because guns get stuck in my body, I bend over table and my body moves away, I crouch and my body doesn't go up again, and so on.

It goes against intuition, but game is a game and has to be treated that way in order to feel real. Boneworks fails that by trying to be too real and finding itself in the VR game equivalent of "uncanny valley", which is a pity :(\t\t\t\t\t
