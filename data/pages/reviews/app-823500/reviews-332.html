\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCan you have fun with Boneworks? Yes. Does it have its moments? Yes. Does it have some cool ideas? Yes.

OK, then why the negative review?

First, the game is visually fairly unappealing. It gets a few points for some bigger rooms which really feel big in VR, but those are fairly few and far between. Everything else feels generic, with the exception of the little spider bots.

Secondly the story is... nebulous at best. I appreciate that at least some work went into creating SOME story, but it really doesn't give much satisfaction when you play the final cutscene which itself is jarring and just feels tonally off.

And finally, possibly the game’s biggest sin - it discourages players expressing their preferred playstyle. See, the developers, for whatever bizarre reason, decided the game needs points - and those points are also the in-game currency... and ammo. To get the highest scores (and be able to unlock the coolest toys) the player must avoid shooting while looting every nook and cranny for ammo. There's no room here for a custom playstyle - want to use the guns (which are really fun to use I might add)? NO BAD GAMER, BAD. Want to get as fast as possible to the exit? NO BAD GAMER, BAD. At the end you'll get none of the cool toys to play in the sandbox, for you won't have enough credits to unlock them.

Some of the levels have multiple paths the player can take - which feels like a great idea until you realize you need to traverse them all anyway if you want to get those points. Or how the unlock mechanics have you trekking like a madman back and forth through the level, trying to carry as much trash in a very limited inventory space. Or how the game will have you climb a weird route for a "mystery box" - and just when you feel "hey, I did it, what do I get for my reward?" you find the reward is... the ability to spawn a box in the sandbox mode. A #$*! box... Oh, assuming you manage to bring the box-unlocking bauble all the way to the end of the level...

And don't you even THINK about saving the game. There are save stations which will preserve whatever items you have in your inventory as well as whatever items you place in the specially designated spot... but everything else about the level will get reset. If you cleared paths of enemies, unlocked closed doors or did anything else - all of that will be gone when you decide to continue. And the levels aren't small either, so forget about poppin' into Boneworks to play a quick session.

Finally - did I mention how there's no sense of progression? No matter the goodies you have on you at the end of a level - there's no point in keeping any of them, for you start every level with nothing but the "points" you acquired thus far. Found a cool weapon you like to use? Yeah, at best you can gain the ability to spawn it in sandbox, for you definitely can't bring it over to the next level.

I seriously wonder what was going on in the minds of the developers when they designed these systems. It's like they were deliberately trying to make their VR game as unfun as possible.\t\t\t\t\t
