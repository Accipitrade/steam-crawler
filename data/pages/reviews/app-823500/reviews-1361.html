\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIndex Controllers: very natural gameplay. Spent at least half an hour in main menu playing around with physics, and then proceeded to smash every box in the levels to find goodies.

I like the physics a lot, allows for a lot of creative approaches - like using big sledgehammer to grab a ledge just outside of reach and pull yourself up.

NPCs are kinda dumb, but don't let yourself swarmed by them, or you'll get pretty physical.

Guns are ok VR guns with manual reload, athough magazine release is only accesible through menus. You need to grab and pull the magazine out, or nudge it with your other magazine. Very flexible inventory approach - you can open a menu with your stuff or just grab it off your back, or side pockets. For some reason you need to use trigget though instead of grip, maybe not to grab stuff accidentally when interacting with world items.

I don't know how far I am yet but at 3 hours in feel like I'm in the middle or 1/3 of the game. Haven't found a lot of weapons and haven't seen all enemy types yet.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo far, so good. Boneworks delivers an experience that plays a lot like it looks. Interaction with common objects is a surprising joy that will make you wish every game put such attention to detail into everything. You can even sift through the debris from a broken box, or use a gun, hammer or any other object to do the same. It's a little thing, but it adds to the overall feel.

Warning: If you have index controllers that have the thumbstick click issue, you'll have to 'double tap' to run, which is annoying. I hope they can add an option to always be set to the 'run' state. I'm fine just feathering the joystick to move slowly if I need to, but full stick deflection should run IMO.

The weapons are fun to use, but there are a few little issues that don't conform well to how weapons actually work. For example, you can't currently hit the bolt release on the left side of the M-4/M-16. Anyone with that habit drilled into them will find themselves getting stuck during a reload that would otherwise be muscle memory. And on that same subject, there is no magazine eject button, meaning you have to yank out the empty mag by hand, or hit it with a new mag to magically force it to eject. But both of the above issues are tiny and unlikely to impact the average gamer. (and it's worth noting that yes, you can slap the bolt release with a closed fist on the MP5 and its variants, so that's nice!)

Holstering weapons in your holster slots seems to be hit or miss after a while. They offer a 3rd 'large' weapon slot on your right side, but I'm not able to use it without drilling down into the inventory screen.

Finally, even with their wonderful focus on physics, enemies getting up in your face are just as annoying as they always have been in VR. You can find yourself clipping through a badguy, and as you try to back away to get a grasp on WTF is going on, your own hands drag the guy with you.

But I'd still give this a solid 9.5/10, even with the above issues. Their choice to price it at $30 USD is great, as It really fits the product. It doesn't have the budget of a huge 3xA title, but it seems to be lovingly crafted.

If you own an Index or the controllers, this is a must-have title. I can't speak for other input options, but I'm guessing they won't be too far behind either, especially HMD's that have natural grip options like the Oculus.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t1.5 hrs into the game:
** I have giggled out loud multiple times in excitement for being able to do something in a video game such as hook my crowbar on a shelf and hurl it across the room.
Am having a bit of trouble with grabbing things, and letting go of them, i see how this is designed for the index knuckles (im using Rift S) and it would be much less awkward on that system, looking forward to trying it with the mamut grips i receive tomorrow, might make the experience less clunky, especially when being able to let go of the whole controller.

The big question, is the physics realistic, in short no, or at least i dont find it so, i actually find it comically clunky at times, for example being able to pickup a desk by just squeezing your palm against it...

BUT nevertheless its a really good attempt at a super interactive world, the writing is great, the game is fun, i wasted one and a half hour on just the tutorial part playing around with stuff, been playing VR for 4 years and this game was MANY firsts for me... I am super excited to jump back in and play later tonight, im even more excited to try this with the enhanced grips, and if i ever get my hands on and index with a pair of knuckles even for 30 mins, i know exactly what game im going straight for ...

KUDOS and congrats to the developers, definitely have an instant VR classic here! GREAT JOB!!

Did this game live up to all the hype and was worth the wait ... YOU BETCHA!!!

Was it AMAZING enough to make me write my first ever steam game review (694 games later)....?!\t\t\t\t\t
