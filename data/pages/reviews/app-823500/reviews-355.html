\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's alright when it works. Unfortunately it requires too much of my time to get it to work every time I launch the game. It touts itself as a "VR game for advanced VR users" but really this message is just a coverup for the fact that the game is pretty janky and you have to be willing to fix or deal with the jankiness in order to play it.

First and foremost the game fully relies on the "player height" setting in order for features and physics to actually work. Unfortunately this height setting doesn't seem to have anything to do with the player's actual height, as I have to mess with it every time I play and the sweet spot to get the game working is always some random height somewhere between 5'6" and 6'4". I'm 6'0" but for a game where player height literally determines whether the game works or not that doesn't seem to really matter when setting it. There is an option to automatically determine player height, unfortunately that option doesn't work. If I didn't have to spend 30m playing with the settings every time I launched the game I might start to enjoy it.

When I actually do manage to get the game to work it's generally pretty fun, but I still have some gripes with the gameplay, mainly pertaining to the VR body they give you and how you have all these holsters on your body but in order for them to work you have to be looking straight forward. If your head is aimed to the right or left and you try to grab something off your body it's not gonna happen. I don't know anything about game design but honestly I feel like the game should be making an assumption about the body's orientation based on the 2 elements it it's actually able to track (my head and my hands) and calculating using that but the only factor that seems to matter for body orientation is what way my head is turned, meaning I have to look straight forward to interact with any of the holsters on my body.
