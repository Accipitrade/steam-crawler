\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is incredible and I want more.

UPDATE:
After having played this game a bunch and having gotten some opinions from other people I thought I'd say some more than "gamegoodmore!"

This game definitely isn't for everyone.
If you get motion sick then forget about it, this game does not care it throws you around and there's no options to stop if from doing so.
The game is not pretty to look at. There are dev textures everywhere and not a lot of color.
Controls are hard to get used to and can feel very janky.
Melee combat kinda sucks.
And the story is hard to follow, and can be kind of stupid.

However the best part of the game is the freedom it gives you to tackle problems it throws at you. Don't want to pull a bunch of physics objects around to make a bridge to clear a gap? Just jump it and grab the ledge on the other side like a badass. Or be stupid and spend over an hour stacking a bunch of gym equipment and climbing it to get a secret item you're not even supposed to have yet. On the topic of collectible, there's a lot of them so if you like that sort of thing you got a lot of replay value. Most are found by different means but there's a lot of climbing.
As someone who doesn't get motion sick I really love how the game just doesn't care about throwing you around. No slowly floating to the ground or teleporting to jump, movement is just like normal FPS games, although combat is slowed down a little considering its in VR but not by much.
And last but not least the game has a KILLER soundtrack which is absolutely worth listening too.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my favorite VR game at the moment. I see lots of reviews saying the save system sucks or too many bugs with the game. I do not agree with those people. The levels are fairly long, maybe one hour each level. The game doesnt save unless you beat a level. This doesnt make the game bad, it does make you have to devote an hour at least to playing, if you want your progress saved. I beat this game after playing it for about 10 hours. Im not that good at video games. I think lots of people really suck at video games and thats why theyre giving it a bad review. This game is intense, difficult, puzzling and downright terrifying at times. Thats why I love it. Prepare to get your adrenaline pumping and your brain working to get through the story mode that is Boneworks. Also, the story behind this game is excellent. You basically transfer your consciousness into the void and your physical body is left behind. The lore is really deep for this game but most people wont get it. If youre up for a challenge and your PC is too, buy Boneworks.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh my god, I cannot describe how amazing this game is. The physics interactions are stellar, the gunplay feels great, the music sends chills down my spine, and the bones work. My only warning is that the game does not have many comfort settings (only smooth locomotion). If motion sickness absolutely kills your VR experience, I would recommend waiting on this one. The physics, as ground-breaking as they are, can occasionally break down, resulting in weapons and limbs getting stuck in physics objects, but the game has only been out for 2 weeks and the devs are already working to fix up the issues that spawned at launch.
Overall, Boneworks is a stellar game for such a small studio, and is a pioneer of VR story-driven adventures.

Definitely pick up Boneworks - 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t
