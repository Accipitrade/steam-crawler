I had no weapons and the nullbody zombie like creature was after me. I grabbed a trash can and put it on his head. I tried this on another one and he was smart enough to take it off.

Being able to climb a lot of things takes this game to new heights too and the game being sold for $30 (basically a steal) makes this purchase a no brainer. Adding this to my list of VR essential games with the likes of Beat Saber, Onward, and Jet Island.

I've played on Index and Quest w/link cable
This game took me 11 hours to complete.
NOT a tech demo
More than likely requires strong 'VR legs' otherwise, you're likely to get VR sickness within a short amount of time.

Quest users w/link cable: Note the height can auto-reset and hinder your ability to jump. Go into the settings to recalibrate the height of the floor.

As always, If you experience VR Sickness (nausea, light-headedness, dry mouth to name a few) STOP IMMEDIATELY. Let your body return to normal, hydrate with some H2O, and start playing again. (Don't play hungover either)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the only VR game I've played for 7 hours straight in one sitting, standing? Standing.
It basically combines all of the good mechanics of most VR games into one long singleplayer campaign following Arthur Ford, an employee of Monogon Industries. Your goal is to reset the clocktower of a virtual operating system, MythOS.

Of course you probably don't care about the story, you're here for the physics, which the game does extremely well. Practically anything you can carry can be used as a weapon, or as a tool to help you solve one of the game's many puzzles.

Boneworks is probably gonna be the game that'll be used as a benchmark for quality in VR, or even be used to sell the damn headsets themselves, and for 30 dollars the game is a steal, plus an additional 10 dollars if you'd like to buy the soundtrack and support the devs even more.

I'm very satisfied with my purchase and chances are you will be as well. Buy it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm about 5 hours into the game and it has been a wild ride. To start, I am using a Vive with Vive wands, GPU GTX 1070 and CPU i7-8700k, 16GB ram, installed on SSD. Game runs very well, but is CPU heavy for sure. The gameplay is as described, very physics driven leaving open a wide array of options for you to take enemies down and traverse the environment. My biggest problem so far is the controls. I feel like the game was designed with the Index controllers in mind, as to hold a melee weapon, or even interact with some elements of a firearm, you must hold the awful grip button. I am getting Index controllers soon, so I'll update on how those feel, but I imagine it will enhance the experience a lot.

Edit 12/14/2019: Beat Boneworks with Index controllers. Took me about 8.5 hours of a slower first playthrough to beat the game. The game was overall pretty good, the ending sequence had me really motion sick, and I normally can handle anything in VR, but your mileage may vary. The Index controllers really enhanced the experience. They made holding items less of a chore and feel more natural. There is some replayability in the story where you can unlock new scenes for sandbox and arena by finding easter eggs. If I remember correctly, the developers said there will be some post-launch content as well (in form of guns and new scenes). Overall a good product looking to set a standard for VR interaction.\t\t\t\t\t
