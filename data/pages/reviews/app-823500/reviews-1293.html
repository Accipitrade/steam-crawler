\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably the best true VR game to date. First I want to say I am playing on a Valve Index (with the index controllers) and a pretty good PC. The game itself is really awesome, the physics are truly amazing and it really makes the world feel real and tangible. With these awesome physics does come some jank, and there is a lot of it. Climbing makes me physically sick and my arm is getting caught in things constantly but even with that and some game breaking bugs I still feel like this is an amazing experience. This game can also get actually scary at times, some of the dark levels mixed with the ambiance and enemies makes this feel like a horror game at times. The weapons are all pretty good, I don't know if I'd say the best VR guns but they are not bad. Performance is also great. My biggest complaint is the save system, as of right now you can't really save mid level and its REALLY annoying. If they fix that than this game is A+ for me. I feel like some of the bugs and climbing issues are to be expected with physics as complex as this and I don't have a huge problem with it. Overall if you have VR this is a must have, you won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery good and immersive experience for experienced VR users. Newcomers, however, may find themselves thinking "why won't my hand go there I'm doing it in real life?" whilst holding a heavy object or climbing. It might not be as enjoyable to someone who has never put on a VR headset compared to someone with basic knowledge of VR mechanics and is familiar with their controllers.

Overall the story was deep and well detailed with many hidden meanings and messages and lasted around 6-7 hours for me but it will vary on person to person as some people may want to take a wander around the levels and see what is around every corner and under every box (good luck, there's a lot to discover!). Would've liked to see a tiny bit more character development and/or depth, however. Depends on what you are personally looking for in a narrative game.

The physics are incredibly accurate with the idea of "if you can do it in real life, you can do it in BONEWORKS" with a few exceptions such as force grab and some experimental weapons as after all, it is a video game and reality is there to be defied!

I had been following the development of BONEWORKS for about a year prior to its release. And I have to say, it certainly lived up to my expectations and all the hype the community had for it (in my opinion at least). And the fact that they held off on spoiling too much of the story made the release even better as players got to experience it all for themselves and listen to what might be some of the top VR game soundtracks ever released.

Overall, I would highly recommend this title to anyone who owns a VR headset and knows the basic layout of their chosen hardware (headset and controllers).

Gameplay: 10/10
Soundtrack: 10/10
Story: 7/10 (could've done with some more character development/depth, good on the whole though :) )\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those games I wish Steam had a point system instead of a binary "yay or nay".

TL&DR: It's a pretty decent game, especially considering how barren VR gaming scheme is. You can see that devs actually put the effort in to provide the best possible experience. IMHO has some core flaws that I'll be explaining but does it worth your money? Yes it does, get it and you mostly likely won't regret it. The game's like Blade and Sorcery and Arizona Sunshine having a baby.

Also mind that I played until the end of the second level. Haven't finished the level, I'll explain why in a bit, so I'll update this if my experience changes.

PROS:

Physics - The physics in the game are actually decent. If you played Blade and Sorcery (great VR game btw), this game has the physics system integrated to the gameplay just like that game, except a bit more ironed out. Most puzzles are physics-based as well and when you realize you found a solution that is not intended, it feels very rewarding.

Gameplay - It's pretty ironed out and fun. Many different ways to kill your enemies. Also, it uses your body's key points for the physics so you can do cool stuff like headbutting the enemy.

Level design - I liked the way they used simple geometry for the level design. For the most part, it's pretty clear where to navigate to and due to climbing and interaction with the environment, the game let's you to pick the way you want to move to your target point. Also, I liked the general state of the puzzles. They really put a lot of effort into them, so much so that they reminded me of HL2 physics puzzles.

Game quality - The game's pretty well done. I didn't have any frame drops or any problems with a GTX 1080 using the vanilla Vive headset. There are some minor bugs here and there but in general it's pretty sweet.
WARNING: In the tutorial area, you're given a balloon gun in an anti-gravity place. You can shoot yourself with the balloon gun which attaches balloons to your body. DON'T DO THIS.

I wanted to see if I can make myself float with enough balloons (You can btw) so I did that. Then I head into the anti-gravity area. To get down a bit, I popped the balloons and then entered the vent. I don't know if it was because of the balloons but the anti-gravity didn't wear off. As a result I couldn't get down from the ceiling and I was sorta stuck there. Thankfully I had a pistol so I shot myself in the head, which restarted the level.

CONS:

Controller - This game is an index game, through and through. I don't have an index headset (cause Valve is taking their sweet time restocking the inventory) therefore I don't have index controllers so I played with the initial Vive controllers. To date I never really found a problem with them, but ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ this game scales the ergonomic problems with the controllers at least threefold. I wish I could show you the wound I have on my thumb from gripping the controller, it literally started hurting after about 2 hours of gameplay. So beware if you have base vive controllers.

Level design - While it's a pretty clean cut level design, I have some problems with the backtracking. In the second level, you can access to one of the hidden crates in the level. To do that, you need to head to the street which I did. The problem is, there was no way back other than having the massive suction thingie to take me to all the way to the beginning of the level. I don't know if there are checkpoints but since I ended my gameplay without finishing the second level I guess we'll find out.

Physics - As I said, physics is the main gimmick of the game and is the main mechanic of most of the puzzles in the game. However, due to physics being...well physics, it's a pretty unreliable base to build the puzzles upon. One finger slip and all your progress goes to hell. If you just play the game normally without much exploration, this wouldn't bother you as much. However, once you start climbing places and swinging around, one finger slip really does mess up a huge amount of work. I was about to rage quit for one of the hidden boxes.

SO FAR I PLAYED UNTIL THE SECOND LEVEL, WILL UPDATE THE REVIEW AS I LEARN MORE ABOUT THE GAME.

UPDATE: I'm very sorry that I have to do this but I'm changing my recommendation to negative from positive. The game is still dope don't get me wrong, however I just found out that there's no save game in the middle of a level.

Imagine a puzzle game with levels taking longer than two hours (or more if you're stuck at a puzzle) and the game having no save game feature. I have to play this game more than two hours at a time and if I'm done with a level, I can't start the next level knowing that I may not be able to finish it. This is ridiculous honestly that the game gets to determine my playtime for me.

Update2: After finishing the game and actually re-playing it for a while, I can easily say that the game is worth the price now that we have a way to save progress. Haven't tried the new update (1.4) but I heard great things about it so don't think it'd affect my judgement in a bad way.\t\t\t\t\t

Note, the review does have some spoilers on what to expect in certain situations/levels of the game
Headset: Vive pro
Controllers: Vive wands
System specifications:
Ryzen 5 1600, GTX1060 6GB, 8Gigs of RAM, Running from SSD

Note: i'm an experienced VR player, i dont get motion sickness at all, your experience may vary from how much you can handle

I've been waiting for this game ever since i got my headset, and today i finally finished it, and i must say this sets a new standard for VR as a whole, the physics, while a bit messy on some occasions are something i have never seen in a VR game before personally, as for the game itself, it is not a tech demo, nor a small VR game, this is a true fully fledged game

The pros for me come from
-Original soundtrack that is pretty well designed

-The performance (aside the tower) is really good, this is one of the most optimized games i played

-The environments are beautiful, and distinct, every level has a different theme

-The way you interact with the world feels natural mostly, when its not glitching out, knowing you can pretty much manipulate almost everything with your hands

-the full body IK is pretty well done, arms never got in my way, i honestly prefer this over floating hands

-The weapons, while i wish there were more firearms, like shotguns or revolvers, as well as being able to swap out different scopes instead of always switching the same gun with different attachments, the gunplay itself is satisfying, getting headshots, reloading manually, the gun recoil, and the weight the guns have

-The enemy AI, while its not something too advanced, i honestly liked their animal-like behavior, as for the armed enemies, they were also pretty tough, though imo the player has quite a lot of health and if you kill an enemy while dying you will revive yourself

-The physics, obviously

As for the cons, there were quite some things i disliked, there were few occasions where i was lost for 30 minutes to an hour trying to figure out where to go, especially in the final level, a huge open area and not much clues as to where you have to go, i spent an hour there, and after i did end up finding what to do, i was stuck again cause i couldnt pick up the object i needed without knocking it first, despite being able to lock onto that object and attempt to attract it to my hand

The sound design, while the music is pretty good, the ambiance is almost always the same, and considering the amount of time you can end up in one part, it gets a bit annoying

Not much enemy variety, while this may not be such a big deal for a game like boneworks, and especially when it does have quite a few cool enemies, i do wish that there were more, and maybe even enemies armed with different weapons

I also didnt like how lackluster the options menu is, in the controls department especially, the vive wands are for sure not the best controllers you can play the game with, i wish you could customize your controls a bit more (ex. i'd prefer tap to move over press to move)

Overall, this game sets a new standard for VR in my eyes, a lot of times i forgot where i was in the real world, that means the immersion is for sure there, Good job, Stress level zero, im pretty impressed by this game, and will be looking forward to your future projects\t\t\t\t\t
