\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti've been following this game since launch, bought it day, etc, etc, you know the drill.
Upon loading in for the first time, I notice that my movement is incredibly imprecise, which I know for a fact is not due to my setup (Oculus Rift with Three Sensors, which has been tested in easily 90 games, worked great in all of them) and that my framerate was stuttery (Again, this is a custom-built gaming desktop specifically designed for futureproofed VR support, and I have every recommended spec met or exceeded.) Upon loading into the main menu, my game crashes immediately. I reboot the game, everything works properly this time, and the framerate has stabilized significantly. I head into the tutorial, learn the mechanics, spend some time solving a puzzle. By this point, I've gotten used to the hand movement not working properly. Specifically, every movement in game is jittery and snappy. If I swept my hand in an arc in front of me, the game would track it as my hand pulling back and effectively punching the end point. Obviously this isn't great, but I continue playing, hoping it self-stabilizes. Then I reach the gun range. This is where everything hit the fan. First of all, by this point I've been playing for about 1.8 hours, trying to get through the tutorial. I get to the gun range, grab a pistol, and go to load it. I assume, based on their claims that they have the most realistic guns in VR, that loading it will be similar to loading weapons in games like Pavlov or Onward. I was wrong. When I load the pistol, the magazine suddenly snaps in, and I go to ♥♥♥♥ the pistol. This process seems simple enough, yes, but try doing it when moving your in-game hands feels like you're playing surgeon simulator on a game console in Goat Simulator. In other words, its bloody impossible. I finally manage to load the gun, and shoot downrange. The bullet proceeds to fire 45 degrees to the side of where I am aiming. I realign, assuming I just missed my shot. Again, the bullet goes flying away from the target. Rinse, repeat. I went through 3 magazines trying to hit that target before realizing that no, I wasn't aiming at the wrong place, these guns just don't shoot properly, and the direction they fire mixed with my lack of control over my hands has absolutely obliterated my aim. I move on to a rifle hoping it will be better. Load the mag, go to ♥♥♥♥, and... my hand snaps to it. Why. Why is this how this works? I give up on the guns, and decide to try my hand at melee. After all, they claim they have a very accurate system for that. I grab a knife and go to stab a dummy, in much the same manner as I would in Blade & Sorcery. Lo and behold, the knife... phases through the dummy. At this point, I have spent half an hour in the weapons section, and have discovered a grand total of no decent weapons. Later, in an actual area, I try to stab an enemy. It does nothing, and I die because none of my weapons function like a real weapon. Try slashing with a knife in this game. I dare you. You can't, because the melee system doesn't work. Also, the story of the game is... well, it's effectively nonexistent. It really is just an overglorified tech demo. Oh, and the sandbox/arena modes? Practically inaccessible until you beat the campaign.

TL;DR:
Don't buy this overglorified tech demo. Its not even a good tech demo, considering how janky movement is.\t\t\t\t\t
