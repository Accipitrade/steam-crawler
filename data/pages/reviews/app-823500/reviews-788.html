\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMotion sickness simulator.

Boneworks seems to be a very cool sandbox vr physics shooter. However it seems it has been developed by VR enthusiasts with very strong VR legs.

The game works through locomotion movement with a physics simulated body thus it makes sense there is no teleport function. But unfortunely the developers haven't added any VR comfort options such as FoV decrease during vertical movement or running and there's no snap option (kinda makes sense again due to physics based movement, but maybe 0,5 sec blackout wouldnt be bad either).

As a result of this my game sessions can't be long (usually between 10-20 minutes) and that's the biggest problem for me there's no quicksave thus it's hard to stop when you're getting nauseated.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirstly, this is not a game for VR newcomers. If you're just starting out, you should start off with other games that don't rely on physics in the way that this game does. Get a sense of your limitations because this game can certainly test them at times.

But when you move past that- this is basically the largest physics based sandbox in gaming history. Everything that moves, from the scenery to the AI- and even the player themselves- is a physics based object. The story feels like a store brand Half Life, but the game isn't really about the story- it's about putting control of the digital environment in your hands, and offering you countless ways to manipulate it.

For some however, this can come with its drawbacks. This game is literally a first of its kind- a shooter/puzzle game whose #1 mechanic is its physics. I know you might think, "But that's been done before," and it has- but not in VR (at least to the scale Boneworks does it). So when you throw the player into a character whose entire surrounding area and even body relies on digital physics, it can be disorienting as holy hell. When I got the game when it originally came out, it could be very clunky, jarring, and even nauseating at times. And this is coming from a person who probably has about 500 hours logged into his Rift S, at the worst of times only suffering a mild headache from having the thing on for too long. I eventually wound up setting it aside on the shelf (in my Oculus Home) for awhile.

But from what I understand, much of these issues have been patched, and I'm excited to give it another go.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a VR title that is glorified for it's physics engine, full body interaction, and, what is the most appealing to the new players, sandbox. This hype you have for the game is quickly killed when you get first hand experience with its selling features and realize that they're terrible or have completely unnecessary and sometimes painstaking steps to find any amount of enjoyment. The story of the game relies on completing puzzles which a lot of the time integrate its physics and weights. These puzzles make a gruesome experience when these physics bug out or completely disorient you when a seemingly heavy box falls off a raised pallet jack for no reason and then swings you around. Your player's full body being physical sounds cool on paper, but is quickly realized to be wasted potential when your legs just dangle below you offering what I would even claim to be negative support as they refuse to climb up 2 inches from the ledge you're hanging on. Most climbing in the game you just want to get past and one hand placement the hand doesnt necessarily like and you're back at the bottom of the alley and the terrible "puzzle." Then there's sandbox, the most enjoyment you'll find from the game, but not only is it locked from the start, but you have to go out of your way in the story mode to unlock it. But if only all of that was my only issues - I would of given it a positive review. The sandbox starts bare bone only with maybe 10 items total to spawn; so to get items into sandbox you have to bring them from all throughout the story mode. If you want to have more weapons, you have to find the weapons you want and then strip yourself of them in the main game and essentially delete them in designated areas just to be able to use them in sandbox. And for props, you can find bouncy balls for each prop which are usually hidden in full on secret areas completely out of your way and usually not easy to get to. A lot of these issues are kicked to the side just because it's labeled for "experienced vr players," but with 500+ hours in steamvr and no problems with any other puzzle game, I can't recommend this game. Just buy Half-Life: Alyx or use a vr mod for Garry's Mod for a better time.\t\t\t\t\t
