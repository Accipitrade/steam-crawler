\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not going to go into pros, because that would take me too long. Its a very well thought out game, with advanced physics and great puzzles. But I want to give a proper review.

This games was meant to be challenging, fun, exhilarating and puzzling. The fun part is missing on some areas.
Sandbox mode you have to unlock, I'm fine with that but we were promised you could just play it without the story mode, it just felt a little rushed. Once watching a tutorial on how to get sandbox, I started to play.
I was so excited to play, but when i got in, nothing was there. I thought it was a bug so I looked it up. Turns out you need to play through the story mode *after* unlocking sandbox mode and picking up and using every weapon, and fighting every npc. This dissapointed me, so i went online and downloaded a save file with all of the tools. After that, I've had great fun. But more was missing. This game feels very unfinished, the sandbox is kinda empty, and i feel like the playrooms and quick download off a website and rushed into place.

I really don't want to be negative, but there is a few things I would fix about this game to make it *even more* enjoyable than it already is.

Story mode:
Have shorter waiting times between levels
Have more saves and checkpoints
Be more clear on how to progress
Fix minor physics issues that annoy me a lot

Sandbox mode:
Make it available immediately available from the start
Make it so that all the weapons and npc's are there from the start
Add more stuff! E.g. a punch bag, crash dummies, boxing ring, anti-gravity zone
Have music
Make that playroom nicer looking, with forts, climbing walls, swings, ropes, etc.

Overall this games is great, and you should definitely play it
It just feels a little unfinished, and has minor bugs
But if it is fixed, could be the best game I have ever played full stop.
Hope Stress Level Zero reads this
Love Corridor Crew and Node, great channels,

From CheezyChips04.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks: The pinnacle of interactivity in VR (for now, at least).

I'll admit I was skeptical about Boneworks whilst it was in development. It looked promising, but I still had this sense of doubt once I looked more into it...Until I watched the date reveal trailer and saw how a level played. I was beyond thrilled and picked it up a day or two after launch.

Boneworks is one of a kind for VR, in the sense that everything can be interacted with and it adds so much to the game. Unreachable area? Try latching on with a melee weapon and pull yourself up that way!
Puzzle giving you some hassle? There will be a way to bypass or make it easier if you look and plan hard enough. Just having everything be non-linear within a level is incredible and contributes so much despite how subtle it feels.

In terms of the story, it's rather unorthodox, but the gist is, you're Arthur Ford, security director of Project Boneworks. And you've been hearing about this area of MythOS called The Void. In wake of MythOS being locked down, you're the only remaining person there. You then set off to the Tower to cease the lockdown and save the day, as it were. It gets deeper and somewhat eerie, but I'll save you from spoilers.

All in all, Boneworks is a testament to the future of interactivity in VR games, leading some to call it a 'tech demo', which does get poked fun at by the devs with certain graffiti reading 'What if this is just a Tech Demo?' scattered around the levels. Details like this make the world all the more charming and I can see myself returning time and time again to this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverything is great. If you love physics based games this this is a good one. It can take some getting use to the full body presence and how it interacts with the environment but that only for a short period. The graphics aren't the best but they are good and don't take away from any immersion, the weapons models are detailed and functional and the melee weapons have great physics. There are creative physics based puzzles with freerunning in every level with also fun collectables. I am using the HTC Vive to play this game and it is pretty much perfect the only problem (which isn't Stress Level Zeros fault) is that holding the grip button for a long time can cramp and ache your hand. The game has lots of replayability with the collectables and extra modes+challenges and repeating the same level doesn't get boring because of all the creative and maybe not intended ways you can solve the puzzles and eliminate the enemies.\t\t\t\t\t
