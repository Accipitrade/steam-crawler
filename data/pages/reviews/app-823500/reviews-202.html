\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn a nutshell, Boneworks is good but overrated. I've just finished the Campaign, and only now found out through a Steam video that I could use ammo to use the weapon dispensers. I think this speaks to how unintuitive the game is to me.

Physics is more frustrating than satisfying. You're told that objects react better to you if you act as if they have weight, but the game offers you no feedback as to how "heavy" an object actually is. As a result, everything heavier than a gun just feels janky to play with (climbing in particular is the worst, as the game sways the camera when you're hanging off the ground). It's also baffling to me that a game that markets its physics this much would have me use a gun in most enemy encounters.

The story is bare bones and barely works to push me through the levels. Explanations (both story and mechanics) are often told through in-world technobabble that gets old quick. It's also difficult to tell if you're doing things correctly, because there often isn't enough feedback for it. For example, in the final boss battle, I had no idea if this was a fight or a puzzle - "fist fighting him took too long, so it must be a puzzle right?". I'm also extremely annoyed that the fun toys were left to the very last level in the game, and you only get to use it for a few minutes.

I'm only not recommending this game for the Campaign mode. I like the music, and I heard people buy this game for Sandbox mode.\t\t\t\t\t
