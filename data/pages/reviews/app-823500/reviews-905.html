\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor anyone comfortable with VR, this is a necessary experience.

This is a glance into the future of what gaming can be. Like Half-Life, Boneworks takes many of the established game mechanics (shooting, puzzles, melee, platforming) and brings them into the next generation and does a great job of balancing them.

What Stress Level Zero has done here isn't just mixing established mechanics though, no. They have taken the best examples of VR mechanics we have seen over the past 4 years, rebuilt and improved upon them, and made them all function in a fully physics-based world. Boneworks jokes about being just a tech demo, but in reality- it's reducing every VR game before it into a tech demo by being so insanely innovative.

Also, this game does have a story, a rather deep one, it just avoids getting in your face. Yes, it's obvious, the story was added in as a bit of an after thought, but if you check out their prior game Duck Season, you find it's a game that appears shallow at first, but then impresses later on with it's 7 endings and many, many, hidden secrets. Naturally, Boneworks follows this style. You have to look for the story in Boneworks, you have to actually read the text on the walls. There are no cutscenes, or obvious main villains. You have to think about where you are and why you're there, you have to fill in the blanks, you have to find the clues. In the end you find they are telling a story that might end up as deep in lore as FNAF is.

This game is awesome, it's a masterpiece. The future of gaming might not be on this exact engine, but this is likely to be the template others will work from. So if you want to see the true revolution that is VR gaming, I think Boneworks is a great place to start.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t[Played with Oculus Quest (using sideloaded Virtual Desktop and Oculus Link)]

Boneworks is a physics based action and puzzle game, with the most groundbreaking VR physics system that I've ever experienced.

My first time playing this game, I was blown away. I had owned VR headsets for a long time before this, and I thought that I had seen it all, but clearly, after playing this game, I realized there was so much more potential for the current-age VR platform.

This game is the definition of "Oh wait, you can DO that?", and it really shows. This game proves what our VR bodies are really capable of, while still staying in a very realistic setting physics-wise. It has a wide range of movement options, including crouching , running, walking, jumping, climbing, and likely many more that have slipped my mind. I especially appreciate the fact that crouching in this game doesn't require crouching in real life, as I can say without a doubt that if you play this game seriously, you'll be doing that a lot, whether it's to get cover from enemy fire, or to get that extra height in a jump.

One of the coolest mechanics that's in this game is the item-grabbing mechanic, in which you have the ability to grab any item around you that the game considers useful from a pretty decent distance from yourself. Despite this mechanic, when playing this game alone, with no outside distractions, I was fully immersed. It was to the point where after a particularly long Boneworks session, I actually tried to levitate household items to myself a few times in real life, which was both quite funny to me, but also a little concerning.

The soundtrack is one of the highlights of this game. I can't think of a single song on the lengthy soundtrack that isn't an absolute BOP. In many stages of the game, I would actually stick in the level longer than I should, just to keep hearing these songs, as I feel like some of the best songs were rather underutilized, like the Museum theme.

But, sadly, a game that’s this polished has to come with some downsides, and sadly, there are quite a lot.

For instance, the height settings are very strange, in how you commonly have to set your height in different ways all the time, depending on how often you play standing up or sitting down, although this issue is not a major pain. Also, in this game, you can grab items from your back for quick access, but when crouching, it's quite difficult to grab those items, which, in a dire situation, could be a life or death situation. Another issue is that doors in the game are very immersion-breaking. They are far too light, and your arms and hands can be caught on the opposite side of doors if you go through a door too sloppily. The issue where your arms and hands get caught on the other sides of objects is actually quite common, like when jumping a fence or wall in the game, which gets very irritating after a while, and is extremely immersion breaking. My fix for that issue is just to stretch your arms in the opposite direction and try to move your hands, and the issue sort of fixes itself after a few seconds. Though I think that there should just be a setting or button to recalibrate the location of your in-game hands, without any hassle.

The level design is usually pretty good, but usually only in levels where there is a defined end that you can get to without confusion. There have been a few times throughout the game where either the game just glitched, or, one or two of the puzzles were just very confusing, and I honestly can't tell which one.

There is no story in the game, or, at the very least, there is no immediate, memorable story in this game other than "Get to the next level to escape or something, I don't know". If there was any more than that, I simply don't remember as it has been a while since I have done a full "Story" playthrough.

The ending of the game felt very abrupt and anticlimactic, and it left me feeling very empty after the main portion of the game was done. The ending felt like "That's it? That's what I spent all these hours working towards?" Despite this, however, there are still many awesome side-modes that will keep most people occupied for a while, and they have been adding more content since release, so there's already plenty reason to play after beating the main portion of the game, because chances are, that there is a lot more to do.

The collectibles in this game are, I'm just gonna say it now, very dumb. It's not really hinted at how to unlock many of the things that the game offers, to the point where I doubt anyone could easily find them all without looking at a guide. Most of the really cool stuff in this game is hidden behind collectibles. Like, I had to replay the entire game just to unlock some essential stuff for the sandbox mode that I missed, and frankly, aside from the soundtrack, playing through the game again just for extra content wasn't fun, it was just tiring. Very tiring.

Also, the load times in this game are atrocious. I know my computer isn't the best in the world, but other VR games load extremely quickly, if not instantly, while this game takes several minutes, both when starting the game, and going to a new location. It takes so long to get into any part of this game, which is very, very boring, and also very unmotivating. And I doubt this is a hardware issue, as this game runs fine on my computer on decent settings when I'm actually playing it.

So, in summary, this is a really good game with a fantastic soundtrack and a revolutionary physics engine, but, it gets held back by its many flaws.\t\t\t\t\t
