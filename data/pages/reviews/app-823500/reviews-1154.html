\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA+, 98/100 score.

If you like GAMES, and Either have a Vr rig, or are wanting to buy a VR Rig, BUY BONEWORKS! The gameplay is like nothing that has existed before and should be considered THE benchmark for what a vr game should be going forward. I pray that the game's makers Create and offer story expansion DLC's because like me, you wont want the game to end. Hell of a job Brandon. Hell of a job.

My only critique is that The graphics don't push the boundries as much as they could have. But given the insane amount of computational power required for the physics, I can understand prioritizing the physics instead of graphics in this game's case. After all, not everyone is playing the game on an 8 core 16 thread machine or higher. I'm guessing the graphics were kept at the level they are at, to enable even slower 4 core cpus to play the game.

This game uses only more realistic movement options, so if you are a fan of teleport locomotion, you are out of luck. The game was made to prioritize the immersion of movement/locomotion. Since teleport locomotion is the least realistic and immersive method of vr movement. The intentional omission of teleport movement ensures maximum immersion with the amazingly coded full physics body avatar you inhabit in the game.

Due to just HOW immersive the game is, If you are the kind of person who throws your controllers, or who falls over/loses balance when you get really immersed in a game, you might want to play seated, or get your hands on knuckles controllers so you wont be unintentionally nailing people in your household with projectile controllers.

Those who are TOTAL VR Noobies IAs in those who have never playeda game where you interact with your environment alot more than a wave shooter, should perhaps start off with something like Windlands to get used to locomotion that is as much leg based, as hand based. That being said, Even noobs will fall in love with BoneWorks.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMost impressive and immersive VR physics of any game to date. Every action really feels like an extension of my body, being able to slide my hands up and down weapons and objects and just knock EVERYTHING around keeps me entertained for hours. I think I spent 30 minutes just flipping tables and benches and stacking boxes and trashcans and other random stuff and climbing on them. Loads of fun, and the feeling of "mass" when interacting with heavy objects feels very natural as well. Probably the most standout quality of the FPS aspect of the game was the scope on the sniper. It was the most natural and accurate implementation of a scope in VR for me yet. Played today for 4 hours straight on my rift cv1 after downloading and i didn't want to stop. I could easily spend hours just interacting with objects, not to mention the gameplay which is fantastic as well. Fun puzzles that aren't too difficult but not too straightforward and combat is fun although the AI is a bit potatoish, but fun nevertheless. The game feels like a cross between half-life and portal and I'm hooked. Best VR game in my opinion so far, next to titles like Elite Dangerous and Asgard's Wrath and will probably remain the best until Half-Life Alyx comes out. Great job devs, can't wait to get back in. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is the most polished and well though out vr game I have ever played and I have play a ton of vr games. All but one of the mechanics feel PERFECT! The inventory slot under the armpit wouldnt holster my weapon a lot of the time but it is still the best inventory system currently in any vr game. The physics amazing for example there is this jumping puzzle with suspended platform that you can swing on by leaning! It felt like I was actually swinging it was one of the best experiences I have had in a game. Weapons are done perfectly better in my opinion than in hotdog horseshoes and hangrenades. The movement is almost perfect I say almost because when crouching I would hit the snap turn a lot (may just be me) but it was very annoying since you need to use the crouch function to lift yourself up on a ledge (amazing feature btw) but the jumping, running and walking all feel perfect also you can stand on your tippy toes! YOUR TIPPY TOES what game can you do that in huh? This one! There are a ton of bladed weapons to choose from and let me tell you one of the most satisfying things to do in this game is slowly stab something and watch as it impales your enemy perfectly. This game gives you very little boundaries letting you do really whatever you want. Sound design is great think portal levels. Graphics are amazing lighting is good the artstyle is really what sells the graphics all the little details. Level design is where this game shines it has puzzle aspects that are perfectly placed into the world for you to find and hints if you look for them. Tons of little secrets to find hidden around. Lots of collectables. The buying of items using bullets is a stroke of genius in my opinion I want to see that system in more games. I havent finished the campaign yet but I hear there is an arena mode and sandbox I cant wait to sink many many hours into this one. If you havent gotten this yet and dont get motion sickness very easy the what the hell are you waiting for GET IT NOW! I promise this is the best purchase you will make this year. There is a ton more I could add that I love about this game but Ill leave those up to you to discover.\t\t\t\t\t
