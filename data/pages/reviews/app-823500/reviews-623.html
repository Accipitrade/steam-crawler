\r\n\t\t\t\t\tShort review: This game is honestly a great bundle of a lot of types of games, including first person shooters, climbing and melee.

A bit longer review review: This game is basically a lot of VR game genres combined, you can legit grab a enemy by their shoulders, go above them and kick them, then headshot them from the back. Aka this game is a first person shooter with actually good guns, and also has climbing, physics, everything has weight, and every enemy is a ragdoll so you can legit push them over, enemy's limbs also weaken when you damage them so you can shoot an enemy in the knee and they will fall over and not be able to stand up for a couple of seconds. Everything about this game is amazing, the soundtrack, the gameplay, the story, the sandbox, the fighting and everything else. Even though my computer is complete garbage and has below the minimal specs for this game and also a crappy Oculus Link cable that I bought for $7, it can still run it at like 25-30 fps.

Edit: So I just beat the story mode of this game and my opinion on it changed a LOT...

It's even better than I thought. I have been missing out on so much by just downloading a complete save file of this game and only playing sandbox mode, the story mode of this game is honestly good, even though I didn't pay much attention to the actual story because of my peanut sized brain, the levels were pretty good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is a VR-game that was marketed as the first truly complete VR experience, meaning that it wasn't going to be a tech demo, but rather a full-length campaign with well fleshed out mechanics. While it certainly manages to be a very good VR experience, I don't believe it to be the masterpiece that some people hyped it up to be, but I'll get to that. This review will basically just cover the negatives of the game, but it is worth playing. The gun mechanics are fine, the soundtrack's great, and the story's whatever, it's not too important.

The game's key component is "physics.". Stress Level Zero tried to incorporate physics into as many aspects of the game as possible. Almost every enemy functions more akin to an AI-possessed ragdoll rather than a traditional NPC and the player's body is also fully physically simulated. It does feel like they were trying to test the boundaries for what's possible in a heavily physics-simulated game in VR, and I think they've got a good foundation. The problems come in when you realize that some abstractions make for better gameplay, and going for realism isn't always a great idea.

For instance, when people saw the Half-Life Alyx announcement trailer they were skeptical. Floating hands and no visible body was initially seen as off-putting because it was believed it'd ruin the immersion. What this actually meant however was that you never got stuck on objects and you ended up not really noticing that you didn't have arms or a body. In Boneworks, I wish they'd gone for that approach, because not only will your body collide with objects and enemies (sometimes weapons too when you pull them out of your shoulder pockets.), but it has weight, meaning that trying to get Ford to pull his ass up a ledge is inconsistent and not very fun. The idea for VR platforming is great, but Ford as he stands is too weak, and his body gets stuck on things a lot.

Movement is easily the worst part of Boneworks, as you'll sometimes have to repeat a balancing puzzle because Ford carries momentum and takes an inconsistent amount of time to stop, meaning you'll overshoot ledges and fall off beams. Games like H3VR and Population: One already figured out that climbing should be weightless, and an even greater number of games figured out that having the character carry momentum when running is disorientating and annoying. This game is simply put unusually nauseating, and throwing out a "Veteran VR players only" warning can't excuse mechanics that are objectively flawed or obnoxious, such as the aforementioned problem of getting stuck on things, being too weak to climb things, or sliding around when you stop running.

Melee combat is a mess, in all honesty. Some weapons will reliably kill a null-body, others will seemingly do random damage (and of course they also have their own weight to them, meaning that if your grip on them is even slightly off balance, they'll handle like ass.). Your very own fists are the biggest offenders of this, since you'll sometimes 2-punch K.O. an enemy with them, and other times it can literally take like 20 punches to down somebody. You can grab enemies and push them around but since Ford's so weak the best you can do is to either sweep a leg with your hands, or push their chest so that they fall over. Enemies that are grabbed will also not stop attacking, so you'll actually just put yourself at risk by trying to do this. This is a shame because getting physical in a game where all enemies function like moving ragdolls is great! You can even break arms and legs to limit an enemy's ability to fight back. There are mods that allow you to increase your strength, and while that admittedly makes fisticuff combat a little more fantastical, it also becomes a lot more reliable and fun.

The level design in the game is hit or miss. The 'Streets' level is good, but many are mixed bags. The level that follows it for instance, 'Runoff', has a decent first half that culminates into tediously long canals with a ton of hitscan rifle enemies with bizarre hitboxes. The sewer level sucks and feels out of place for the setting, mostly because of how it looks. There are cool ideas and set pieces here but many levels don't take advantage of the game's ruleset. How about a level where you have to climb, jump, and swing your way to the top of a tower, all while fighting enemies? The maps feel strangely linear, even though there are opportunities to sequence break. It's almost like some of them are placeholder maps, even.

Finally, I need to talk about the balance. The game uses ammo as currency to buy weapons at shops. Ammo is absolutely everywhere, sometimes in plain sight, and sometimes very well hidden. The problem is that basically every enemy will go down in one or two shots, and a single ammo crate will give you maybe 15 or 30. Even with liberal use of guns, you'll easily end the game with thousands of rounds to spare, which completely trivializes the challenge and makes melee combat completely pointless. On top of this the AI is extremely basic. With a few exceptions, they will only walk toward you until they're close enough to flail an arm at you, and that's it. Climb a dumpster and they can no longer hit you. I had to force myself not to use guns in my playthrough to get some variety in, especially since the guns don't always handle all that great. The player also gets regenerating health and a slow-motion mode, so it's all over at that point. You'd think it'd be a great power fantasy but it's really not. I think I would've much preferred resources to be a lot more scarce, making guns a situational emergency weapon rather than the thing you use 100% of the time. Actually force the player to make use of the environment around him to defeat enemies, instead of just pointing and clicking with a pistol.

Boneworks is good. I'll even go so far as to say that it's very good, and that it's certainly a VR game people should play, but it needs refinement, or there needs to be a sequel that trims the fat.\t\t\t\t\t
