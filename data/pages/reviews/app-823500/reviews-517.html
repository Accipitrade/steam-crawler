\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is basically a tech demo that's been extended to a full-ish length campaign (about 7-8 hours to finish). And it works! There's only 3 types of enemies (small robot, human sized enemy that flails at you and a ghost human that shoots at you). The combat is pretty good. I spent most of the game punching the crap out of every enemy and not only was it pretty fun, it was quite a workout!

However there are some flaws, for example, most of the melee weapons feel pretty bad to use, they feel very awkward to use and most of your swings just end up missing more often then hitting. I understand there's supposed to be weight and lag behind them, but other games with weighted melee weapons such as Saints & Sinners or Until You Fall do it much better.

The puzzles in the beginning levels of the game were also a lot more tedious and boring then fun to go through, thankfully it's only in the start of the game and the game is mostly combat focused later on, which is where it shines.

There is also no gore or dismemberment which I feel like ruins the purpose of having a lot of weapons in the first place, it just feels better to use your fists or guns.

Overall, while this game wasn't the "Mind blowing, best vr experience" that so many people claimed it to be, it's a very solid, fun, if a little janky experience that's well worth the 25 bucks I got it on sale for, but even the base price of around 30 dollars is well worth it if you can't wait.
