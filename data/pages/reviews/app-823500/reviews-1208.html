\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEasily the best VR shooter available if you've got the VR legs for it.

If you've finally got the grip of smooth motion in VR give this game a shot, the most complete VR game out yet, doesn't feel like a level to level tech demo like a lot of other content does. Story is pretty out there, and the game makes no mistake taking itself too seriously, it's just a full out romp from beginning to end with a few portions that are more than a bit frustrating. The campaign has its highs and lows, with the lowest point coming right before the credits roll, coincidentally, you're pretty high for this.

The biggest negative for the experience currently is that story missions don't have saves, so although a death in the instance will respawn you relatively close, if you're 20 minutes into a one hour level and quit, congratulations, start over. Additionally, the hidden game modes imho, shouldn't be hidden, as they're not too much to shake at until workshop support or something comes to the game. Another gripe I had was the cool sci-fi weapons featured in trailers were just not present in normal gameplay, so you better save up your ammunition to get them in the campaign or sandbox mode.

Overall, I have no issue recommending this game to anyone whose gotten over motion sickness in VR that is looking for a cohesive story game with solid gameplay mechanics and puzzle solving. Throw on your dumpster and dash past the enemies to the endzone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou know, it's like if Portal and Half Life 2 had a janky VR game with no plot.
The physics are much improved over any other VR game, it's true. However, this only serves to make me feel LESS immersed since I can see my arm or object I'm holding bounce off a wall or crate in the game but I keep swinging away in real life. If anything, the realism makes it harder to get engaged. Like melee fighting: Most of the melee weapons basically suck and feel like they're made of rubber. I can personally swing the items around in real life better than in game. So I go to hit an enemy with an axe or something. The first problem is that your character's arms are quite short, so your range is not as long as you think it is. I kept giving them gentle breezes across the cheeks instead of braining them. The second is the velocity detection or whatever they use to calculate melee hit damage is screwed. I swing with all my might and it just taps them on the head. So I try little fast swing with just the wrists or elbows, and that sometimes does better. Stabbing weapons are much better about things, so get a knife soon as you can. And to top it all off, I found that hitting enemies bluntly with my SMG wound up being more effective since A) the hit detection was consistent and B) I had the option of shooting them if I wanted and C) it did about as much damage as hammers or other one-handed melee weapons. More, maybe, since one hit to the head usually took them out.
The gun combat is fine, good even. I think it's a bit unfair that the rolling gunner dudes seem to be able to spot you through walls once they've detected you, since I'd try ducking behind a wall to circle around and I can see their laser pointer bleeding through the wall, following me. The game also has a slow-motion feature, but I never used it. Why? That's because I was playing on the Oculus Rift S. The Slow-mo button is the X button on the left touch controller. So if I wanted to slow-mo in a gun fight, I'd have to move my thumb from the analog stick to the button and stop dead in my tracks to use it. I'd rather keep moving in a firefight than mess with it. It'd also be nice to see more guns like shotguns or something. As it is you've got pistols, an uzi, an SMG, and a few rifles. Plus some wackier weird ones I couldn't afford at the shops, so I couldn't see what they did.
Which brings me to the topic of shops. So you use bullets for currency to buy a random selection of items at seemingly random prices at the beginning of each level. This is because at the end of each level, you lose all your weapons. They disintegrate if you're holding them and they drop off you if you're carrying them when you walk through these green instance fields. The game makes a big point of labeling "Reclamation Bins" that you're meant to toss your weapons into before you exit a level. But why? As far as I know, you can never get them back anyway. You drop them in there, they get digitized, and poof they're gone. You never see them again, you have to scrounge or buy new weapons in the next level. So what's the point of having a Reclamation Bin if the game already forces you to drop them all and you don't get them back?
If you've read other reviews, you'll see that the save system is indeed crap. You can't quit in the middle of a level without resetting all your progress, and if you die the checkpoints of respawn are very far between. The devs have said they're working on fixing that though, so thanks devs.
I do like that the puzzles in the game usually have enough wiggle room for a frustrated player to find a way to cheese them in some way, an intentional design from the developers. Sick of trying to deal with some seesaw or platforms? You can try climbing up some pipes or wood and haul your way to victory. One thing that does bother me about the climbing mechanics is that there's no way to do a pull-up. So if you've climbed to the top of some building or big box and are hanging off the wall and get both arms over the top, you can't get up from there. I realize they're not simulating legs or anything but still , can't you have some contextual button or detection to let us pull ourselves up onto something? As it stands, you have to find some protruding pipe or wall or something to grab onto, then pull yourself on that, then hover your feet over ground to stand.
So then there's the plot. I don't know if the people who liked it were playing the same game I was, but you've got next to no idea what the hell you're doing throughout most of the game. It's some kind of abstract pseudo-meta thing going on where you're a guy in real life who hacks into some megacorp's server VR world thing to do... something. Then you get video messages from people in the real world as pretty much your only source of plot. You've got some guy who knows you trying to help you out and talking about real world stuff happening, then some lady who apparently is a rival or something who wants you to help others get into the server you're hacking, I don't bloody know. The whole game is just a series of puzzles and mazes and exploring to get to the next area or setpiece. It's got a lot of cool ♥♥♥♥, and it looks really nice, but I was just blindly wandering around and had no idea what I was doing, why I was doing it, what I was trying to accomplish, or even if I did accomplish anything. And without spoilers, the game has all this intrigue and buildup with hints and questions throughout the game in the form of scrawling writing on the walls from crazy people, then you get dropped into something completely random, fight some guys, climb a bunch, and then watch a cutscene that still doesn't explain anything. Yeah. So whoever says there's a great plot either has some meta knowledge of the game or is deluded.
I enjoyed the game, don't get me wrong. But I did not enjoy it about as often. Frustrating mazes, fiddly physics, unforgiving deaths, and no real story. As many of the writings on the wall read, "What if this is just a tech demo?!" and I think that's really all it is. Not worth $30.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo not buy this game if your looking for good constant action through exciting and interesting levels. I know that's what a lot of people were expecting. I was expecting some great physics and body interaction mixed with a lot of really gun combat. But what I got? Finicky climbing controls that required a player to climb around places like spiderman and place huge glitchy planks across gaps to get across unclear parkour levels. one after another.

Small puzzle spoilers bellow:

3rd level is called 'streets'. there is a level here that requires you to place the mentioned glitchy planks across gaps, or you can just climb across which i was thankful for, because the planks were way too huge to handle in vr. A normal pc game, sure. Looks fun. but VR? No, never. Very uncomfortable. not to mention this game is for extremely advanced VR users only. People whos minds have long since adjusted to high VR heights and extremely uncomfortable and wild situations involving lots and lots of parkour.

4th level is called runoff. This level has a whole area in the beginning involving one of those button elevator puzzles where the buttons are too far away from the elevator to press them and go up. So you're forced to spiderman this part by climbing around on the finicky poles and falling down over and over as you struggle and fight with the games nonexistant 'climb up onto ledge' system. You're forced to treat your VR body like a thing that hangs below you that you need to move your arms higher and higher in hopes that sometime soon they'll make contact with the edge you're trying to get up onto. Many VR titles have this issue, it's nothing new so I wont blame this game for it. But it's bad to have it be the most obvious was to get passed a level with a 'broken elevator' gimmick. At the end of this 4th level, through a lot of fun combat in a sewer, theres a tunnel suspended in the air. This tunnel drops down all the way and breaks the last part unless you wedge something under it to catch it. If you have nothing wedged under there before it falls? Good luck figuring out how to get to the door on the other side, most just restart the whole dang level and do the spiderman part and everything all over again. It's simple once you understand that you need to wedge something under it, but first timers usually ragequit from having to redo the frustrating spiderman bit again.

Spoiler bit over..

Please give us sandbox before the campaign so we don't have to struggle through all that parkour to get to the fun sandbox part. This game is obviously a fighting physics game first, and the parkour campaign was an afterthought to stick the new mechanics onto.

TLDR: Good showcase of VR physics mechanics in motion with combat. Poor showcase of climbing and parkour in VR\t\t\t\t\t
