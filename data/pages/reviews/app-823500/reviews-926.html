\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYeah... this game isn't good.

  • Slippery and inprecise movement. don't know how many times i've slipped/ran off ledges when i'm very clearly trying to stay away from said ledge(s)
  • Climbing mechanics are terrible and disorienting (glitched through/got stuck in objects more than once)
  • Combat isn't satisfying at all
  • Mediocre gunplay
  • Whenever i'm hitting some stationary objects with my hands (eg. buttons coming out of the ground), my height gets raised a few cm; this gets annoying and disorienting quick (changing height makes no difference).
  • Getting close to some large objects (boxes, tables) causes the characters legs to bend 90 degrees (have adjusted my height; makes no difference).
  • A lot of empty spaces in the environments/levels. No idea wtf is going on/where I am. all the environments feel like randomly thrown together industrial areas with 0 consistency.
  • physics when holding objects feel floaty and unrealistic

Overall, this is a really janky, floaty and disorienting vr game. Just play Alyx instead. It does everything this game does but a hundred times better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe developers of this game are clearly pioneers, and the knowledge gained from the experiments they've engaged with this glorified prototype, are without a doubt very valueable and useful for future projects.
That said, it has too many flaws and quality of life issues, and stops being fun and innovative -really- fast. Even after the big update i still cannot fathom why :
  • My body bounces so freaking much. When jumping against surfaces or enemies i bounce back just as hard. Also notable during climbing. My arm feels like a bungee cord when holding onto something.
  • Shooting still feels janky. Pistols feel great but aiming a rifle feels like i won't be able to hit anything beyond 20m.
  • The quickslot system still does not work comfortably, i still don't understand why i need to pull the trigger aswell to grab something out of a slot.
  • Reloading is still prone to so much error. Supporting with the other hand results in grabbing the mag 50% of the time and is utterly infuriating. Why am i still able to do this when the mag releases automatically anyway?
  • Why the slomo button is on the left controller, the need for two inventory buttons, and why we can't simply swap them out in the SteamVR remap tool ;
  • I should put effort into getting to special boxes. The contents are almost always useless and obscure. Edit : I've replayed the museum and noticed it has been reworked to explain some stuff. Great! But not really intuitive for a returning player.
  • Why the inventory system still hasn't dedicated slots for these 'obscure' collectibles. I'm not going to drop a weapon for some blue glowy thingie with a box in it. The game does not explain the value of these items.
  • I still have a hard time accessing sandbox content? I must be missing something.
  • You decided to fill the majority of content with tiresome environmental puzzles. Really. You show off all these fantastic guns and weapons, which i've used maybe 10-20% of the time and can't take along with me. Why? You even made ammo the core currency.
This game will, and shall frustrate you as soon you -think- you're getting the hang of it. You'll crave control you can't govern, and fumble the majority of your attacks because some object next to you pushed you away or trapped your hand or leg in a physics glitch mid axe-swing and forces you to brohug it out with mr blue man instead of cleaving his skull in a fashionable manner.

EDIT : I've managed to unlock some sandbox content and realized this is where the game shines. Managed to make the slowmo a bit more usable by downloading a custom bind. Am having fun. Changed my recommendation but i'd strongly suggest to be less obscure about unlockables. Explain more in the main menu. Especially for (impatient) returning players.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is fun if not chaotic in the best and worst ways,

The gameplay and feel of Boneworks is mostly solid and mechanically fun, if not having, while well done, slightly jumpy/janky physics (which was vastly improved in an recent update), and if from hearsay isn't enough, due to Bonework's having the player's body act as and like a physics object, having only locomotion-based movement, and being able to climb on certain surfaces to get secrets/etc, this can cause motion sickness very easily, I highly recommend you get your VR-Sealegs for locomotion in Half-Life: Alyx, H3VR, or hell even VRChat.

A few complaints I have are that I wish the melee weapons felt better, when a weapon hits a enemy it just never *felt* right, like stabbing was the only thing that felt responsive, Blade & Sorcery being an extremely good example of how to handle melee weapons in VR.

I'm also conflicted over having to go through past levels (once you beat the main story) to get every single item/some game modes just to mess with it later/in the sandbox, and otherwise you’d have to cheat by downloading a 100% save file, on one hand it rewards exploration and creates some replayability, on the other hand it would’ve been a wonderful gift to the player to have sandbox and arena mode unlocked once you beat the main story with all weapons available.

The story I’m just confused about, I felt like I missed something about the story, if I did that is, and I wish the ending led upto something more after climbing all the way up just for credits, though I did notice various parodies, nods, and references to the VR industry and development sprinkled in throughout the game, which I thought was nice. From what I heard from others, there is a lot more to Bonework’s overall story that is really easy to miss or is simply a part of a grander story for all of Stress Level Zero's games, which I might have to look into.

I feel like Bonework’s story isn’t really the reason you play Boneworks, you play it for its gameplay, it’s reckless abandon for comfort options, and to sweat like a pig, and if you want more out of the story, you just have to look for it.\t\t\t\t\t
