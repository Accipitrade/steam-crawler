\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a modified review after spending more time on it. I still stand by all my original complaints, I still feel like the controls are janky, but I want to give some positives too.

This game allows creative game play choices, which can make for some fun and interesting moments, but be ready to fight with alot of annoying BS to get to enjoy that fun stuff.

Pretty much every encounter with enemies has gone like this for me:

"Oh boy, there's some things to shoot over there,"
*reaches to holster to grab gun
*game inexplicably chooses to put ammo in my hand instead, or just grabs nothing at all, or randomly grabs the key I had stored in a different inventory slot, from the complete opposite side of my body (why!?).
*I stumble away from the now botched encounter, trying frantically to open the menu to put the key back where it goes, since you can't access that inventory slot without the menu.
*I finally get a gun in my hand, use it,
*i try to reload, but for some guns, my character's hand keeps snapping to the wrong part of the gun repeatedly so it takes several tries to make him grab the right part and rack it
*I try to put the empty gun back in my inventory and grab a different one, but the gun randomly decides not to go into the inventory, and instead drops on the ground.
*I scurry around pathetically trying to get my lost gun back

Basically, I spend most of the encounters fumbling, wondering why the game is doing what it is doing, and not really getting to enjoy the exciting gun battles the trailer promised.

see my original review for more technical problem related griping I guess.

But some positives of this game, and the reason that I have changed from "not recommended" to "recommended" is has a robust campaign for a VR title. And it feels like they designed it with the idea you will experiment with the environment, rather than do very specific things to face the game's challenges, which is great if you are the curious type of gamer who is always asking "will it let me do this?" when you play a game.

So I think people should try it. But be ready for many frustrating problems you will have to look past to enjoy what it has to offer.

Original review:
I feel like everyone hyped the quality of the physics interactions in this game, but I have had the opposite experience. All physics interactions (picking up things, carrying things etc) feel janky. It feels like your arms are those long thin wobbly balloons used to make balloon animals.

I don't get sick in VR easily, but this has given me some very uncomfortable moments. It is very easy to accidentally lift my avatar off the ground when grabbing or setting down large things, which feels like the whole world is moving oddly.

Grabbing things is sort of violent and abrupt, and so is letting go, so its easy to do something like, try to grab a weapon on a crate, accidentally grab the crate, which snaps the crate suddenly upward, throwing the weapon wildly away. Or god forbid you are doing a puzzle that requires you to gently set a plank down across a space you need to walk across. who knows where it will wobble off to when you let go.

It's also easy to accidentally grab ammo from your hip and drop it when you are trying to do other things. I am constantly struggling to get guns to go in my inventory instead of just fall on the ground behind my back.

I'm a nearly daily user of VR and I have played a variety of VR games, so it is not a matter of inexperience with VR interactions for me. I find this game's janky controls frustrating.\t\t\t\t\t
