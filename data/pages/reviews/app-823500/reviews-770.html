\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the best VR game I've played to date. The fully physical mechanics take a little bit of getting used to, but once you get it, it's endlessly fun and satisfying. It's got an interesting atmosphere, one that was particularly strong in the game's tutorial level which took place in the form of a museum. This is largely accentuated by the game's incredible score, one that took me off-guard by how much it exceeded my expectations.

The campaign is interesting and varied, if not a little disjointed, and will feel very familiar if you've played Half-Life 2. The level design and core game mechanics feel like VR physics-based realization of HL2's, from picking up ammo boxes to breaking boarded doors with your crowbar. The entire campaign is about 10 hours long altogether if my playtime is any indication. Nothing too difficult, most levels didn't kill me and the ones that did were mostly due to me making a dumb mistake or not understanding something. There are enough secrets and items to unlock to justify playing through more than once, though I've yet to see how fun that will be.

There are a few extra modes including an endless combat arena, zombie warehouse, and a collection of sandbox maps. The items you can spawn in the sandboxes are dependent on what you've collected in the story mode by tossing items into 'Reclamation Bins'. I hope to find much replayability in the sandbox.

Though I'd thoroughly recommend this game as it's more than worth the price, there are a few things you should consider before buying:
* Having a fully physical body means your movements in the game can differ from your real life movements and cause motion sickness. If VR makes you queasy, take a moment to build up a tolerance before playing.
* The core gameplay is combat-based. There are no jump-scares but if you're particularly easily frightened you might find a specific few of the levels difficult to complete (including one that requires the use of a flashlight to see).
* Unlike the aforementioned Half-Life 2 there is very little - if anything - to the plot. There are a few dialogue bits with some exposition, but they explain very little and are of no consequence. The only thing keeping me playing was the fun gameplay, not any hope of progressing the story. If I wasn't so enamored seeing Mike Diva and Heldine in their cameos I probably wouldn't have listened at all.

If none of those things bother you then you're in for a great time! Jack me in!\t\t\t\t\t
