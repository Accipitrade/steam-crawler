Boneworks Review: An important, yet flawed VR title.

This review is going to be in depth. After an intro, I am going to go over a few topics, then end it with things I feel would make the game much better.

I’ve been waiting for this game anxiously ever since the first video teasing it was uploaded to Node over a year ago. I even bought a Valve index for both this game, and the new Half Life in mind. Was it worth it? Yes, but it still came with a significant amount of flaws.

Story

To me, the story was kind of meh. Nothing overly exciting or engaging, but just interesting enough to keep you wanting to find out more the further you get into it. However, it really wasn’t interesting enough to get me to look for clues and secrets to find out more hidden details. I would much rather look for secret weapons and NPC’s to unlock for sandbox mode.

Music

The music fits this kind of game extremely well. I’d always find myself becoming more engaged with my surroundings whenever a new piece of music kicked in that sparked my curiosity and sense of wonder. From when you enter a stairway, to entering a courtyard filled with enemies. The music does it’s job, and does it very well, but it never really stuck with me outside the game. Even trying to recall a track now that I’m writing this is difficult. That shouldn’t take away from the fact that the music in this game is indeed well made, and a perfect fit.

Graphics/Performance

The graphics were exactly what I expected. Nothing sublime or amazingly over any other VR title, since most of the work and polish went into the physic’s engine. Perfectly expected, and the game still manages to look fantastic, on par with the other highly rated VR titles out there. However, what I was NOT expecting was the shoddy performance. I’m running a R7 2700x, 2080 TI with 32 gigs of ram, with no thermal issues whatsoever, which places me far above the recommended rig. However, even with this, I encountered several parts of the game that took my frames down from 120 to 30 or even less. The frame rate plummets, the quality becomes on par with minecraft, and it becomes near unplayable. Turning down the refresh rate to 90 seemed to help make it more bearable, but frame loss is still rampant in some parts of the game. I can’t even spawn more than 20 enemies in sandbox mode without performance taking a hit. I suspect that the devs didn’t optimize the game properly for AMD CPUs, since I’m seeing the majority of folk with comparable problems running with AMD chips. This severely impacts my enjoyment of the game, and sometimes forces me to quit.

Gameplay

THIS is the big one. This is where I find that the game shines, and falters the most. This category is a broad one, that I’m going to separate it into some bullet points of its own.

Guns: Obviously, the guns are the main part of the gameplay. Guns guns guns, and I’m happy to say that they did an exceptional job. Guns work like they should, the recoil feels nice, reloading is fun (when it works properly,) shooting accurately is actually a challenge, and it just works as it should. However, there are some problems that need to be highlighted. First and foremost, there should be an option to disable stock interaction with the player’s head. Despite being right handed, my right eye is naturally far weaker than my left, so I often find myself conflicted on how to shoot. It’s never been a problem in VR before, but because of the inability to move the gun’s sights in the middle of my viewpoint due to the stock, I have to pick the lesser of the two evils. Not an inherent flaw, but it’s something annoying that I have to deal with. There’s also the fact that reaching into your ammo pouch while crouching is an absolute nightmare. You almost have to reach into your bowels to get a magazine out, so I often have to stand out of cover to reload, which has caused more than one of my total deaths. Then there’s the armpit holsters, which sometimes don’t even work properly. It’s like I have to face forward perfectly to get a consistent point of pulling and returning weapons. Strangely, this is almost always the right side, and never left. Don’t know why. There’s another big problem I have with the guns, but I’ll get to that in a bit.

Melee: I honestly don’t know how to feel about it. Sometimes it works fantastic, other times it feels like my arms are pool noodles even with the one handed weapons. When melee combat works, it rivals Blade and Sorcery. I loved sneaking around the sewers with a knife and silently taking out those green guys with throat slits or stabs to the back of the head. However, when it DOESN’T work, it’s infuriating. When I unlocked the nordic axe, I went straight into sandbox mode to give it a whirl, only to my disappointment, it suffered from pool noodle syndrome. There’s very few weapons in the game that allow you to reliably use one hand, and that’s an immense let down.

Physics: This is what I believe is the game’s biggest shining point, and it’s biggest downfall. Physics makes the combat more interesting, makes interacting with the environment far more fun, and makes the game far more interactive and engaging. Being able to interact with everything in the world with a well crafted physics engine is something that VR desperately needed, and I believe that Boneworks is the first game to truly get it right. That much is obvious, and I don’t need to go into detail about it. However, the physics makes some certain things absolutely infuriating. Climbing? Kinda fun when it works. However, that ends pretty quick when your hand decides to get stuck on something that it shouldn’t. That’s when I have to shake my hand around and move around my room in a frustrated frenzy just to get my hand free, which usually ends with me falling. Have this happen over and over, and it’ll really wear you down. It also likes to mess with you when you’re moving around tight spaces. One second I’m moving past a barrel or box, and the next I find out my arms wrapped around it and decided to take it for a trip. I then have to struggle to get my hand free. This can happen quite often if you’re not careful, which is incredibly frustrating. Jumping on boxes? Good luck. You’re far more likely to roll whatever you’re jumping on way before you get a good foothold and can actually use it as a platform. Climbing ladders? Get ready to fall a good few times before you get the hang of it. Pro Tip: Use the virtual crouch feature to bring your legs up, then throw your hands back as far as possible to try to clear ladders easier.

To wrap up this review, here are some things that I feel should be added to the game to overall improve the quality.

More gun variety, like AKs, shotguns, grenades, and machine guns.

Optimization, since it’s clear that the game is not properly optimized.

More graphics options/gameplay tweaks

Re-adjust the weight/damage of some one handed weapons.

Easier way to re-bind controls. Time slow on the left hand is aggravating.

Even with all of its flaws, Boneworks is still absolutely worth the asking price. Was it worth the wait? As of right now, yes, but I’m still a little disappointed. Hopefully the game continues to grow and improve through patches and even DLC.

[AS OF 4/9/2020, I NO LONGER EXPERIENCE THE "GETTING STUCK IN HOLLOW OBJECTS" PROBLEM.]

Honestly, I don't even know where to start with this game. It is so amazing! The physics are the best of any VR game I have ever played, the combat is amazing and has endless opportunities, and the soundtrack is amazing! I feel as if any person that owns a VR headset should own this game.
If you want to feel like John Wick, or if you want pull off unusual stunts involving loaded guns or swords, this is the game for you! The soundtrack fits nicely with the game and will help you get immersed. Every song in the soundtrack conveys a different emotion, and if the right song is played at the right time, you will feel inclined to act pull off crazy moves.

I have 35 hours in this game and can say with all honesty that I still have things to do. The sheer amount of secrets and challenges the game presents to you is just fantastic and won't allow you to get bored!

This game is still flawed, though.
I really dislike the fact that you can't save the game, and the game only saves when you exit a loading screen. This encourages you to either complete the entire level that is 1-2 hours long, or to somehow pause the game and leave it running without volume (which rarely works).
Getting wedged in hollow objects is also a problem, but is not too bad to the point that the game is unplayable.
When climbing pipes horizontally, I've experienced a problem where it would teleport me really far or really behind on the pipe.
Another problem I ran into was VR sickness, but not severe. I play VR frequently, so it was only about a hour in until I got VR sick, but after I kept playing I was able to play with no problems, with most of my sessions around 2-3 hours long with a few short breaks after before returning. Many other people seem to get VR sick easily when playing this game so I thought this would be a good heads up.
I also use the Vive wands/controllers and many other people seem to have problems but for me it is pretty good. The only problem I would say I have with the controllers though is that it is a bit hard to do certain actions on your left hand while in slow-mo, but that is pretty much it.

So despite all this, the game is still fantastic. It is still a good game overall and offers an experience I have never had with any other VR game. This game is basically the Half-Life of VR, setting the standards for future VR games to follow. Valve is already following in Boneworks footsteps, so why don't you give it a try?\t\t\t\t\t

Overview:
This is tough, given how excited I was for this game, but unfortunately I have mixed feelings with Boneworks so far. There are moments that are fun but I mostly felt like I was fighting the game itself rather than being immersed within it. This game, at least so far, is mostly a box stacking simulator complete with annoying forklift. I'll break things down further below, but if you're on the fence about Boneworks one thing I’ll say this - it's more Garry’s Mod than Half-Life. If you love physics simulation and are down to move some boxes, this game is for you. If you’re looking for a good story or mind blowing combat, this one is a pass in my books.

Controls:
Things start off well, with lots of locomotion options. Unfortunately, your character gets caught up on the smallest of ledges which makes navigation around the world a real pain. The one of the first things you do is learn how to jump over an ankle height wall. I'd find myself spamming the jump button to try and make it over the little steps that should be simple for a character to step over. Then there is climbing. I can hang from a pipe with one hand forever, climb hundreds of feet easily, or hold myself out an iron cross pose for as long as I choose like a god-level gymnast but I can't climb over a ledge. You can (sometimes) grab a ledge and host yourself up to it waist height but then you're legs just dangle. Trying to move the legs “up” with the crouch joystick will sometimes work, but it’s very janky. Just automatically step up onto the ledge! Sadly, it just doesn't feel good to move around the world. I feel like I'm dragging my avatar around and constantly fighting with it to do even the most basic things. This ties into an overarching complaint I have with the game, a focus on realism at the cost of fun.

Puzzles:
Man, the designers REALLY were 'inspired' by Half-Life here and unfortunately they've learnt all the wrong lessons. If you hated the gravity gun and wishes you could stack boxes all day by hand this is the game for you. The first two hours of this game is stacking ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ boxes, dragging boxes, pushing over boxes, and forklifting boxes. Talk about sinking a hook in your players early. The puzzles are the worst kind of puzzle too, ones where you can SEE the solution right away but then struggle to EXECUTE the solution because your ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ physics avatar isn't strong enough to lift something or the box your dragging around gets stuck on something. It's not hard to solve the puzzles, they don't make you think at all, it's just a chore to complete. The funny thing is, if you did have a gravity gun, this actually might be fun. It feels like they build the game around a gravity gun then removed it because Valve wouldn't let them. The forklift is a nightmare. It's so annoying to move around and you NEED to use it to move heavy boxes. And if basic box stacking wasn't enough for you the other puzzle mechanic is finding the correct colour key to open the locked door. It's so uninspired. None of the puzzles so far have been fun to solve or new / unique / interesting. It's the most basic of physics puzzles but you need to complete them using the QWOP avatar. They should have taken some lessons from Gang Beasts here and made some fun physics puzzles instead of focusing on ‘realism’ as a UPS warehouse working.

Gunplay:
Overall fun but weirdly grounded in reality. We're in VR and this is a video game. It's even that trope of a 'video game' inside a video game. Despite that we still have standard real world weapons that you need to load and ♥♥♥♥ like real gun. Why not do something new and interesting? We're in a setting where you can do anything you want with a narrative framing that lets you explain away anything you want and the best they can come up with is simulate the same pistol / SMG / rifle you've played with a thousand times before in every other game. There are tons of 'realistic' gun sims in VR already, let's do something new. There is a little hidden side room early on that gives you a balloon gun and that was exciting, but at least so far, that’s the only thing I’ve found that I couldn’t get in a American Wall-Mart. The enemies AI leaves a lot to be desired here too.

Melee:
Meh, it doesn't do much for me. I feel like I'm fighting with the avatar to get it to swing an item. When it does work as you’ve planned it feels good, there is a very satisfying thunk when you sneak up behind one of the dummies and smack them with the HL crowbar. I personally don’t see the need to be so “realistic” here. I know they are going for a feeling of weight but it just feels like my avatar is a total weakling. It’s a video game, let me be better than a like school boy level strength avatar. It still doesn’t even make sense though, given my avatar can climb like a god and hold his whole body off a wall with a single arm but he can’t swing a hammer. Realism at the cost of fun.

General Gameplay / Design:
Let me force grip anything. You give players a fun mechanic then only allow it to work on some items? why? It's fun, just let me force grip whatever I want. This game doesn't know what it wants to be, a fun video game or a hardcore simulator. The hardcore simulator isn't very fun and they prevent me for using the fun stuff as much as I would like to. Lastly, sometimes force grab is the grip, sometimes it's trigger, and sometimes it's both. Just pick one and make it consistent.

Checkpointing is pretty rough in this title too. When you die you're dumped back at the start of the whole level that you need to run all the way through over and over.

Story:
Meh, cringe video on screens isn't how to tell a compelling story in VR. Its VERY inspired by Half-Life and Portal. There is like writing on the wall hinting at the 'something is not quite right' world like Portal everywhere. It just feels weirdly uninspired or like a fan game. Even the environment is an 'homage' to City 17, complete with playgrounds and teeter totters. Maybe it gets better but so far it's like a fan game that just retreads what's was in Half-Life / Portal to almost cringe levels. It's even got head crabs and crowbars for god's sake. It's funny, because now that we know there is a new Half-Life game coming to VR feels even MORE like an out of place fan game that assumed there would never be a new HL game. I wish they just told an original story. Or just gone the Garry’s Mod route (nothing wrong with that, everyone loves that game) and focused on more things to do in their sandbox. Lastly, speaking of sandboxes, it’s annoying I have to beat the game to unlock it.

Art:
Very bland. Doesn't really push the envelope in any way. It's all framed in the narrative context of it being a 'dev world' but like, that's a really thing excuse to just use plane ass art everywhere. Tron was 'in a simulation' and it had tons of style and personality. This is just kind of uninspired.

Take away:
This game is impressive, don’t get me wrong. If you love physics sandboxes and want an amazing one to play around in, this is it. They clearly spent a ton of time and effort on making everything physics based but never really stopped to think if that would make the game fun to play. The dedication to their own version of realism I think hurts the game overall. They already make a bunch of concessions to make the game playable I don’t see the point of just putting some convenience features that just make the players time with the game more enjoyable. Auto-step up onto ledges from climbing, make my avatar stronger so I can swing a cool hammer, give us some ‘only possible in a video game’ dope guns and toys. So many aspects of the game design so far feel uninspired while the tech they’ve made is impressive. I'm going to keep playing till the end and hope that it gets better. My experience so far has been disappointing, unfortunately.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: Boneworks feels half finished. From bizarre level design to progress blocking bugs, it felt like I was finding something new to annoy me every time I played. The game's great when it works, but mind numbing when it doesn't. I cannot recommend it unless you already have an Index and have a high personal tolerance for jank, and you really need a campaign experience.

Pros:
- Close quarters gun combat feels great, especially with pistols. Enemies are in general very responsive to damage and incredibly satisfying to kill, from visual effects to their ragdolling.
- The soundtrack is fantastic.
- The game finally delivers a lengthy campaign experience for VR (that isn't exclusive to the Facebook machine), something everyone's desperately needed for a long time. Granted, I think it's a lot longer than it needs to be for some poor reasons, but even without those issues in consideration it should be at least an 8 hour campaign. That's pretty solid.

Cons:
- This full body physics simulation does not work. I mean, at a literal level, it does function, but boy does it feel like ♥♥♥♥. From items wobbling in your hands, to one leg getting stuck on a ledge and killing your momentum platforming, to your entire body wobbling when trying to climb something, to your arm just getting caught on same random piece of map clutter and getting you stuck in a damn hallway, it feels like everything that happens is just an argument against full body physics. Or at least a full body physics implementation like this. Good, comfortable, and stable full body physics are definitely possible, but this ain't it chief.
- Basically every form of combat that involves two handed weapons is terrible. Namely anything with a stock, there is something very bizarre about how the weapons' collision physics interacts with the player's body which will cause it to frequently twitch out of position and send your shots way off where you wanted, assuming it doesn't just spaz right out of your hands first. Melee weapons are less spastic in general, though you'll probably still have body collision issues when you try to pull a weapon off your back and it gets stuck on the other weapon on your back and clips and clangs into your neck until you let it fall down.
- Speaking of weapon handling, a universal issue with firearms is for some reason every interaction is limited to grabbing. I guess there was some design perogative to minimize the use of buttons for item interactions, which sounds neat at first, but it honestly just holds the game back for no good reason. Magazines can only be ejected by grabbing at them with your free hand or using the radial menu, there is no eject button. Likewise, there is no slide release/bolt catch button at all, you have to grab the slide/charging handle and pull it, again using your free hand. There isn't even a physical bolt catch you can just slap on the M4 platforms. This is bizarrely limiting in a game that seems like it'd favor dual wielding so much otherwise: your rampage will always be interrupted by a lengthy and finnicky reload process.
- The excellent soundtrack is frequently at a bizarrely low bitrate in-game. I'd guess this is just a technical limitation, but it really compromises what I can tell are excellent compositions.
- The game is prone to random bugs. For example, there was a period of about two days where after a few minutes of play the player's arms would just become totally inoperable, preventing any play at all. Likewise, I've found that sometimes opening a weapon crate or even item from the store can just suddenly crash the game. Speaking of crashes:
- There is no saving during levels, period. This significantly worsens the importance of crashes when coupled with how incredibly long a lot of levels can be. I opened a weapon crate near the end of about an hour in one level and that just completely deflated my interest in playing for a couple of days. Hell, even without crashing, this means that starting the game is pretty much always committing to an hour or so playing, real life be damned, or you're doing it all over again. Thankfully, the developers have acknowledged this issue and stated they'll work on a solution but for now it's a major mark against the game. Hopefully they system the eventually implement can improve the game's playability.
- The cleaner enemy type sucks. Or whatever they're called. The projection guys in orange suits with guns. I mean, design wise, they're rad as hell and it was super cool to realize what their gimmick is. But fighting them is a pain in the ass beyond belief, especially in say the Arena mode where you can just end up with zero cover if you're unlucky. Their weapons are hitscan and they pretty much don't miss, so they're just guaranteed damage over time if you can't protect yourself and they're too far or you're too overwhelmed, so you might end up having to cheese the weird regenerating health system (killing enemies prevents you from dying) as you move towards them. They just aren't fun to fight.
- The level designs tend to range from boring to frustrating. It's not all bad, but it feels like it's more bad than good. Most of the puzzles in the game rely on the wonky physics interactions or awkward climbing with the wobbly ass player body. A stand out from the final few sequences of the game involves carrying a heavy cube across some dangling platforms, and it felt like it was all a matter of luck for the cube to not get stuck on something or for the platforms to not just totally go sideways and slide me off them. I guess this particular example mostly ties into the physics complaint, but this is just one of a number such "puzzles".
- The story is an afterthought. It's there? Kinda? There are awkward live action cutscenes (presented through an in-game theater screen, because yeah, what can you do I guess) and otherwise it's pretty much all environmental storytelling. I honestly feel like the game coulda done better without the cutscenes and just left it to the literal writings on the walls. Especially with the entire vague ending sequence's impact being totally ruined by you literally walking into a room with a big screen where the final cutscene plays out. That just felt like such a flaccid way to close out the game.
- The sandbox mode has a weird obtuse unlocking process that you're pretty much going to need a guide to figure out. I don't know who thought this was a good idea when a lot of people are probably getting the game primarily for the sandbox, but yeah, seriously. Thankfully the steps involved can all be taken care of within twenty or thirty minutes within the tutorial level, but it's still rather needlessly obtuse.\t\t\t\t\t

So those who do not have an hour or two to be in VR will find it hard to progress since they will have to replay parts of the game since they cannot save mid level.

UPDATE: I have recently finished the "warehouse" mission, and I had to complete it multiple times due to a bug. I have looked it up and this has happened to other people in the past and some need to do the level over 2 times because of this. I would greatly appreciate if SL0 did something about this, because it is very frustrating spending lots of time exploring every single inch of the level for it to all be reset on you unexpectedly.

UPDATE 2: I have just beat the game and I cannot describe how much I enjoyed this entire experience. I am now excited to revisit it and try to collect enough ammo for the end-game equipment and to explore sandbox and arena mode. I liked how some parts were tense and you could feel it and unlike other games, dying in this game can set you back so you really do not want to die because there are consequences. It took me almost 12 hours to do my first run-through of this game and I definitely think that it was worth the money spent, definitely one of the best VR games that I own. If you are on the fence about it, I suggest you get it, and if you end up not liking it, you can always return it within 2 weeks or 4 hours of playtime. I played the final levels (clock tower until the end) the day after they released mid-level save points, but I never saw one throughout these levels. Granted, these levels are on the shorter side, but I still feel like I should've seen a save point somewhere. This may be kind of fixed for some users, but still be weary if you do not have much time to put into the game in one session. This is definitely not a game you can play for 5-20 min and then move on or put down like you can with other games, this game takes some longer sessions.\t\t\t\t\t
