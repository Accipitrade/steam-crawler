\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat physics and melee combat, can be finicky and disorienting but, there is a warning for that in advance. The shooting is a little weird, I don't miss often but there is just something about it that throws me off, can't really place a finger on it. The main story has a lot of great moments, well placed cover, unique ways to kill enemies, and quite a few awesome weapons. The pacing is a lot like a half life game. Slowly getting introduced to the many mechanics and only beginning with a few light armaments and a crowbar. Hell my first kill was with a brick.

The physics puzzles. Oh god the physics puzzles. Hard to really enjoy some of them, and there are quite a few. I only used the internet for one of them, and the answer was blatantly obvious which infuriated me. The physics never glitched out for me, and I found a few ways to cheat some of the puzzles. This game rewards creativity.
The sandbox and arena are some nice additions, my favourite part of the campaign was finding the various weapons and tools you could find hidden around the levels that you had to collect. Once I found something cool I would drop it in a box at the end of the level to play around with it in sandbox later.

Overall good game. I liked it, but some might get frustrated with the mechanics and constant puzzle solving.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun but it has its issues. I give it a 7/10

Pros:
-Your VR body interacts with everything without having to actually grab them and you can grab everything which is unheard of in VR
-Gunplay is fun but its no H3VR
-The ability to use tools and weapons as you see fit is really cool even if it's not always practical
-The puzzles have a portal/half life kind of vibe to them and can be solved many ways
-You can headbutt stuff and enemies
-You can beat enemies to death with the bodies of their allies
-The slow mo is super cool but has issues listed in the cons
-Objects have weight and your character will struggle to lift heavy stuff

Cons:
-NO CHECKPOINTS or ability to save and come back to the game later
-Your VR arms are floppier than a dude with ED. Trying to wield a melee weapon is awkward sometimes because of this and trying to get a grip on a rifle can be difficult because its flopping around in your grip. Ive never had trouble holding an AR with one hand IRL but your character has a serious case of limp wrist
-Virtual stock doesnt seem to work makes the AR feel like more of a spray and pray weapon
-Your floppy arms get stuck inside of things like ledges and objects, especially when trying to jump on and climb things
-Objects have weight but youre also apparently the juggernaut. Trying to traverse ramps or steps youve made using boxes is stupidly difficult because you end up pushing everything out of the way
-Slow mo is cool BUT (and i dont remember seeing an option to change this) you have to hold the slow mo button on the same controller you use to move so its impossible to hold it and use the joystick to move kind of defeating the purpose of slow mo since now youre a standing target in slow mo. Also aiming in slow mo with a rifle is a pain since now your ridiculous recoil is in slow motion
-Devs kept talking about this story but Im 4 levels deep and havent encountered anything hinting at a story for the game

The cons seem way worse than the pros but it's still a pretty fun game despite its issues, recommend picking it up on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
