\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBest VR game of 2019 (until Alyx came next year)
For sure, it is the one advancing the standard of locomotion, physics and user interaction.
It features also good puzzles and story, with content for at least 8 hours.

The game has a lot of possibilities but also lacks polish... however, the devs are still updating it and it is the best sandboxy game, possibly second best FPA you can play today. Initially I could not recommend it as it has no save feature, but the devs have included checkpoints so now it is playable.

Be careful if you feel dizzy playing other VR games. This game is the worst as there is only joystick locomotion and there are a lot of puzzles that require climbing. However, once I get used to this game, I can play any VR game without issues... Just get out of the game once you feel dizzy to train those VR legs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks is an essential for anyone who owns a VR headset. It's a big leap to better VR games and physics.

After my 10 hours of game time I find myself walking around grabbing things like the corner of a door to see how it feels. It's weird.
The game is great. It looks like they were developing a physics demo to show what VR is capable of.

Pros:
Best physics in any VR game. Beats Blade & Sorcery which is 2nd.
Good graphics and gameplay.
Very intriguing story.
Has a few horror elements (or I'm just a pu$$y)
Good soundtrack. The first time I found the radio I spent >10min just jamming to the song. I need it on YouTube.
Can run on a mediocre CPU. I'm playing with an i5-6600 and 2080 S (sick combo, lmao) and have only experienced some lag when a lot of things need to be loaded. I.e objects and enemies. Though I will be upgrading my CPU to a 3700X or something.

Cons:
No save function as of now. If you quit in the middle of a level (they're pretty long) you'll need to start from the beginning. I think they are making a save function though.
Not a lot of graphical settings.
Nitpicky but important:
You can't grab the bolts on the big pipes since your hand will just pass through it.
Your weapons can get stuck through a fence.

Now, for the biggest con, Climbing. I either need a 1hr long tutorial by Brandon explaining how the this works or it needs optimization. If it works as intended, I feel like they could've done a much better job at explaining it in the tutorial area. I find myself needing to crouch all the way down (irl) and putting my hands to the ground while spamming the jump button just to get over a ledge.
I saw a guy on Reddit climbing with ease, so I think it is possible. Would've been nice if they taught you how to do it in the beginning.

As I said, this is one of the best, if not the best, VR game on the market. I hope they'll release their incredible physics engine to the world for a fee of course. Imagine if every VR game had these physics :-)

Also, a quick warning: While playing you'll forget everything about time. I put on my Rift at 6pm, and within what I thought was a couple hours, 7 hours had passed.\t\t\t\t\t
