\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have such mixed feelings about this game.
It has so much going for it, but feels unfinished...

I'm not docking points for being an "experienced users only" title... Personally I never get sick from continuous locomotion, and I can't really imagine playing this game any other way.

And while some people might hate it,
I honestly LOOOOOVE how falling from huge heights is a regular part of gameplay.
I used to love jumping out of trees, off of tall diving boards, etc, as a kid... this scratches that same itch.
Most games discourage long jumps with fall damage, but Boneworks pretty much requires it.
Honestly the rush gets me every single time! Such an exciting feeling. Frequently throughout the "campaign," I would go out of my way just to climb a tall section of the map and jump off it again.

However, falling is literally the most exciting part of the game.
Everything else feels pretty janky.

Shooting is clunky... certain objects (boxes, etc) seem to have slightly larger hitboxes than their displayed image. Frequently I would line up clear headshots on enemies whose foreheads were just barely peeking out over some cover... believe me I know how to shoot... but the bullets simply wouldn't have any effect until I moved completely around from cover (even though my sights/scope were visibly on target the whole time).
This seems like a glaring oversight to me. Also why is the MP5 so difficult to reload? If the game encourages shooting this much (I had 3000+ rounds on me at one point) they should make sure the guns work better. Pavlov does it better and they have a wider variety of guns in that game too.

Melee weapons are pretty unsatisfying due to their unpredictability...the way half of your hits will just bounce off the target. Other games (Walking Dead: Saints & Sinners, for example) have recently done a better job of this, where weapons still require some finesse and sensitivity to their simulated weight but have a higher success rate.

The WORST part about Boneworks, though, is that while it's hailed as a "groundbreaking" physics sandbox game... I feel like they crudely ripped off a bunch of Valve's concepts (physics and otherwise) and not necessarily for the benefit of the gamer.
Don't get me wrong, I love the IDEA of solving a bunch of physics-based puzzles and whatnot.
But when ridiculous movement glitches prevent the user from executing it smoothly, and failures are punished with long falls and ladder climbs, it gets really. fricking. annoying.
I respect what they tried to do here but there were SEVERAL times when I just said to myself "this stupid game is broken" because excessive wobbliness in objects and/or the character's body made certain puzzles exceedingly frustrating and disorienting. Like I said earlier I have strong VR legs, but this game pushed me, man. I didn't get nauseous but I sure got mad. I like a challenge, but not if it feels like the game is busted.

All of my complaints could be forgiven if this game were stronger in the content department.
If you told me this was a student project, I would believe you simply because I can't believe all the blatant Half-Life references. Like OKAY GUYS I get it, I love Gordon Freeman too, but you don't need to have all the crowbars and headcrabs? You could have done something else?
Enemies & environments don't have as much variety or detail as I would have liked, because this is "a tech demo" as the game itself seems to admit.
So why is the tech still so glitchy? I wouldn't want to build a game using this engine, not with the way it is now.
The ladder-climbing experience alone (SO SHAKY WTF) is enough to make me never want to play through the campaign again. Devs should check out Population One, climbing feels fluid in that game...

In summary,
I'd recommend this game only if it's on sale, and if you are a true VR enthusiast with great patience and a stomach of steel. Boneworks is an interesting piece but not one that I think I'll be returning to anytime soon, except whenever I crave that falling-off-a-tall-building rush again...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts Something.
Boneworks is really just an in-depth physics demo.
Its actually really impressive as a demonstration of how far VR games have come and I would rate it highly. Where it falls short is that progression through the game is nonsensical. The Plot has almost no narrative and is very confusing.
Do not buy this game if you are looking for a story. There is none. I feel like it almost told a story but was just not something I could grasp.
There are parts where the action and the environments are fun and there are others where you just want to get through it. Some mechanics frustrated me like climbing ladders and walls. Also how levels reset your gear and melee was a struggle at times if you didnt have a great melee weapon.
