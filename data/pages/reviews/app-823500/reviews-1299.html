\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was amazing. Took roughly 8 hours to complete (probably can beat it in 6 if you don't goof around with the physics like I did).

The physics was great throughout the game and it really surprised me how consistently good it was. The only times my character model got hung up was when I was trying to move in impossible ways (moving an arm through a solid objects like metal bars), but would fix itself once I moved my arm back. This only happened two or three times and was my fault when it did that; I much prefer this system over the traditional 'your hands can phase through any object' VR physics system.

I thought the story was very good throughout, although the end was kinda anticlimactic and left me wanting more. I really appreciate the non-linear level design in the majority of levels. It gave me the feeling of choice and the fact that I wasn't forced to do things in the exact order the developers wanted me to helped with the immersion.


Thoughts for the future of this game:
Workshop and multiplayer would be amazing in this game; it has the puzzle solving of Portal, amazing physics, and great gunplay. I could see anything from cooperative puzzle maps to zombie survival to pvp coming out of the workshop.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter my first 8 hours of experiencing Boneworks on the Oculus Rift, I decided to leave a review to inform the uncertain potential buyers about the so-far pros and cons I've experienced. Keep in mind that these are things I have experienced and therefore may be opinionated.

Pros:
- As of right now, the most advanced physics game on the VR market.
- As of right now, the first VR game to implement a really good fully-fledged melee as well as a firearms combat system.
- Good Environments (visually stunning, fun and interactive).
- Good level-design and good AI (many extra areas to explore).
- The longest somewhat interesting storyline so far (Have not finished the story yet).
- A realistic price for its content.
- Fun yet not too complicated puzzles.
- Love the "headcrap" effect. ;)

Cons:
- Boneworks.exe was detected as malware by AVG (may be patched soon).
- The game is not compatible with Windows Mixed Reality (currently being worked on).
- Average performance yet good considering my graphics card. On average the game runs 30-50fps with frequent drops to 20fps depending on level design (Runs worse than Pavlov & Blade & Sorcery).
- No manual saving (It will save from the most recent checkpoint and continue from thereon).
- Often struggle to grab my holstered weapon behind my shoulder (Maybe a me issue).
- No options to disable forced use of the stock on the firearms with a stock (Except by holding the gun really far away from you).
- Experienced clunky interactions on the Oculus Rift and a friend on the Vive (struggling to grab a hold of interactive items when there is more than 1 closeby and hand colliding trough objects causing it to be stuck).
- No option to bind custom controls.
- Locked sandbox till completion of the game. Sandbox is hidden in the first level.
- No Multiplayer (They said they will keep updating and improving the game so maybe this function will see light in the future).

There honestly is not a lot of bad to say about this game since you can tell the developers really put their hearts into this project.
The things mentioned under "BAD" are things that can be fixed and improved. I just suggest if you experience motion sickness fast please only get this game when your specs are close to the recommended maximum specs because those rapid movements combined with frame drops can really make you feel sick. Even I start to feel sick after about two hours of playing and I rarely get motion sick, so I truly recommend taking breaks often to recover from your playtime.
Regarding the controllers, it is obvious this game is best experienced on the new Valve Index controllers since they mimic your finger movements accurately making it more immersive.

Anyway, I think this game truly did live up to its hype and is so far my favorite VR game yet. If you are willing to taste a bit of what the future of Virtual Reality may bring us this is a really good game to start with since as of right now no other VR title is as far beyond its time as this one. Rate it 9/10.

I hope this helped some people! :)

GPU: Nvidia GTX 970 (with a defect HDMI boomer)
RAM: 16GBs
SSD: 420GBs
System: Windows 10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe physics interactions, firearms, stabbing, enemies, melee combat, and anomalous items are all extremely satisfying and wrapped in a solid campaign. I don't care what anyone says I love the environmental design. I wasn't sure about the inventory system but it quickly grew on me.

Ammo management is very well done. Only firing depletes your ammo. You can reload your gun a million times with out firing or throw a million mags on the ground and your ammo count will be the same. It's very freeing.

Story is a little lacking. Physics puzzles are neat but nothing to write home about. Melee hits feels very light. Checkpoints are very far away from each other. Your gun collides with your body and makes it very difficult to aim in some situations. Definitely room for improvement / rough edges but overall a great game to introduce fully physical worlds.

Motion sickness warning. If you're more sensitive stay away. I never get motion sickness in any other game but in Boneworks I have to take a 5 to 10 minute break after every level (~1hr). Hands move the character around a little too easy. I realize this is necessary for physics but even resting your hand on a button moves your view around a bit. Jumping causes me the most discomfort mostly because your character crouches down in anticipation of the jump. I usually close my eyes when jumping and it seems to help a lot. Climbing is very wobbly. I feel like it has the most room for improvement. No options for blinders or fov adjustment which would be greatly appreciated.\t\t\t\t\t
