\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Disclaimer, I have not completed the game yet, these are first impressions. (Played on an Oculus Rift CV1.)

This is currently on my list of the best VR games I have played. I've already spent a good amount of time in just one session without realising it (3 hours). Gun-play/melee is satisfying and rewarding, including the overall use of physics. The puzzles are also good and well placed throughout the levels. The story is engaging and overall immersive, as I found myself lost in the world many times.

My only criticisms is that I have ran into one game breaking bug resulting in me having to restart the entire level. Often times the physics can play up due to the full physical nature of the body in VR space during climbing/falling/grabbing onto world props and items. Although this could be due to incorrect height/sensor settings, but not too sure yet.

In conclusion, it is definitely worth it's price tag and great fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, after playing this game, I can happily say that the boys over at Node and Stress Level Zero have lived up to the hype and made a masterpiece. If there was any time to buy a VR headset, now is the time. This game has some next level physics and in-game interactions that made me go "Whoa?" more than a few times. It's got a nice charm to it, that indie charm mixed with some slick and interesting mechanics that work well for what kind of game this is.

What game is this? This, my fellow steam users, is what I would call the "Half Life" of the modern, VR-ridden day. A quake for this kind of era. This game will propel VR headsets into the eye of those who probably didn't care about VR before, just like how Half Life and Quake both made it more of a worthwhile investment to get a 3D Graphics card. I don't have the Index, but I plan to buy a pair of the controllers soon, which I imagine will make this game even more immersive and deep. I use the Vive Wands, which have the worst grip buttons in existence, but that doesn't make this game unplayable. I highly recommend, if you have a VR headset, especially if you have an Index, you should buy this game.

EDIT: bought some Index controllers and played through the game again. It's a lot better with Index controllers. The Vive Wands make this game a chore\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played a majority of the primary story mode, and even with just the gameplay alone, it's worth it as an owner of a VR headset. The amount of creativity you could have with this game is unlimited. Everything being interactable in a physical world IS the next step for Virtual Reality. It has Portal and Half-Life vibes with how it tells the story and its level design with puzzles. There are a few issues with the full physics world, though. Some things are just awkward to grab, or it's too easy to fall of objects you'd think could be used as a walkway. Climbing on brown walls with the hand indicators feel unintiative and lead me to fall more than ten times due to it. This game is NOT for brand new VR players though, it can and will make new players motion sick. Boneworks is one of the few VR games to make me motion sick, and that isn't a slight to the game. I just haven't gained enough VR legs yet with full locomotion. With those warnings aside, this game is my favorite VR game I've ever bought. This is no tech demo; this is Boneworks.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow.

That's my first reaction to pretty much everything in this game. The way the tutorial is handled, the evolution of the levels and enemies, how insanely immersive and fun the physical world is... All of it

At the time of writing this review, I put about 7.3 hours into completing the campaign, and that's excluding any of the secret areas, achievements, arena mode or sandbox. So even if you try and blaze through the story like I did, you're still looking at 7 hours of story content.

If I had to list any gripes, it would be that in a couple of the levels it got pretty confusing trying to figure out where you were supposed to go, but I'm pretty sure that's on my part and not the developers.

Another semi-gripe is that I would have liked to see more graphics settings, as even on a well-prepared VR system (1018ti, 9900k, 32gb ram) I still experienced frame drops to 90 or less. If you're not running on an Index, you would be totally fine and wouldn't notice the difference, but otherwise the drop from 120+ to <90 is quit noticeable.


Brandon and his team at SL0 have done an overwhelmingly amazing job creating what I would say is the most impressive and advanced VR experience at the moment, and I think it'll be thanks to their pioneering in virtual interaction that future VR titles, especially Half Life: Alyx, will be pushing even further when it comes to physics and presence in VR games.

I've only just scratched the surface of this game, and can't wait to hop back in tomorrow. (:\t\t\t\t\t
