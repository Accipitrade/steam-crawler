\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMore of a tech demo than a game.

Since release they've fixed the seemingly obvious issue of the game not having save points - not sure how they thought they could release without that.

The story and gameplay leaves a lot to be desired. Boneworks is more about doing interesting things with physics - so it seems fair to judge it on that. Sadly they need a lot of work. The idea of making the entire player physical while sounds good in theory, simply doesn't add anything positive. Getting my limbs or entire body stuck inside doors or objects happened at least half a dozen times. This kind of thing wouldn't normally be a big deal, but with entire sections involving climbing complex geometry with your physically-simulated body flinging around, it's inexcusable.

The inverse kinematic arms are disorienting and certainly never look right. The physical weapon interactions are dreadful, with picking up heavy objects feels like they're in slow motion, not heavy. How an object will interact with the ragdoll enemies may as well be a dice roll.

It seems they couldn't decide between making a VR physics demo or a VR game, so they did half of both and released it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: Screw it. Despite what I think of the game itself, it's half the price of Half-Life: Alyx, and it has active developer support (who seem like some really chill guys, not going to lie)
If that alone sounds appealing, I recommend it. Read on to hear my full thoughts on the game.

BONEWORKS is much like the original DOOM, in that it's an amazing game in what it set out to do. The thing is, when Quake came along, DOOM got outdated with the huge leap of innovation that came from adding another axis of movement.

For me, Half-Life: Alyx is BONEWORK's Quake. From simple things like being able to aim a weapon properly because it's not colliding with your body [FIXED IN 1.4] that's a fully modeled, barely controlable physics object, to actually being able to pause the game. Does this mean BONEWORKS is bad? No, there are still things that just feel better in the original DOOM. From holstering weapons to being able to properly jump, there are some things that those who want to be fully immersed in VR will appreciate more here than in Alyx.

The Catch 22 for me though is story. Because I find the best way to enter a game's world, no matter the method of play, is through the story being told. And while it may be unfair to directly compare BONEWORK's story to Alyx's, I'll still complain about this because Stress Level Zero needs to work on storytelling. I say storytelling because there is a story and it is good, but I only understood half of the critical details of what was happening while I was playing.

Overall, I would say get it when it's on sale. Otherwise Half-Life: Alyx is a safer pick. That said, I still enjoy this game for what it is, that being a vital step forward for VR. Even if I feel like the devs were in too many layers deep into VR.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played majority of the story mode for Boneworks and I have to say it's really fun. Obviously it isn't the perfect game and I've had issues here and there but non to completely ruin the game. I had a lot of fun running around in story mode and have finished it several times.

The physics are great and really fun. I did have issues sometimes the character would grab wrong thing at the wrong time. For instance, I was in a middle of a gun fight and I needed to reload instead of grabbing the ammo it decided to take the entire gun. I've also had issues with the inventory. If I grab my shoulder item with the corresponding arm (left shoulder = left arm) it works flawlessly but if I use my other arm for some reason it has issues registering it and did find a good spot to put my arm but it sometimes doesn't register. Another issue with the inventory is if you are crouched you can't store ammo or weapons in hip inventory places but can still grab ammo, this isn't ideal when I'm in the middle of combat and if I stood up I would get clocked in the head.

Overall, great game, great physics, definitely not perfect. I do like the save spots. I do recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
