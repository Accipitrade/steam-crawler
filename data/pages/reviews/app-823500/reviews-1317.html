Doesn't matter if I push down on the edge or closer to the center of the pad, it just feels random when my character will run or slowly walk, which makes the jumping puzzles incredibly frustrating to the point of being impossible. Other than that the game felt great and I have no motion sickness complaints whatsoever. Some framedrops though, not really related to what was going on on screen. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe physics in Boneworks is very well done. Almost all of everything you see in Boneworks can be interacted with. Break a crate into pieces... and every splinter of wood can be moved with your hands, or things your holding. Your entire body reacts with your environment around you, including the smallest things like bullet casings. Weight of objects is considered and calculated as well. It's not perfect in this aspect, but it's probably the next best thing short of actually holding things in real life (the valve index controllers are great in this regard).

Now, the story is... okay I guess. If your a fan of the "Portal" games you'll be happy to know this kind of plays out in a similar fashion. Theres some enemies, lots of weapons, and where this game really shines.. the puzzles/environments. You basically walk into a room, look around, make a plan, and then... try to make it happen. There are so many different ways to accomplish things in this game and your rewarded for thinking outside the box. The sence of accomplishment you get when your crazy plan works out is amazing!

Boneworks seriously raised the bar for VR gaming and I can't wait to see what's in store for VR going forward.

Flawless performance on:

Ryzen 7 2700x
GTX 1080ti
32gb ram
1tb m.2 nvme ssd\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt just doesn't play well.
As a physics demo, it's not to bad (but nothing new from what I've seen so far)

They have tried a little too hard, if anything, to make you feel like your in virtual reality. This makes the whole game feel weird and janky.

The gunplay isn't good - Contractors does a better job, feels natural.
The melee feels overly reliant on "pretending" you have a heavy hammer in your hand - Gorn does this better and Blade and Sorcery feels fluid whilst retaining the weighted feel.
Climbing feels somehow numb? With the VR body getting mangled and stuck in weird ways - Even RecRoom managed to make climbing feel good, although their is no weight to it.

In short, it feels like the old HL2 physics engine reveal from around 2003 but you get to bumble around with in a VR body too.

I won't ask for a refund - purely out of curiosity of possible expansions or mods.\t\t\t\t\t
