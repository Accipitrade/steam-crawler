\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInitially impressive, ultimately disappointing.

The Good:
-Physics interactions are interesting and satisfying to experiment with.
-Decent campaign length and re-playability with arena and sandbox modes.
-Almost any object can be picked up, pushed, thrown, or used as a weapon.

The Bad:
-Like any game centered around physics interactions, they can be a bit glitchy and sometimes downright infuriating.
-Poor enemy variety
-Cutscenes in a VR game that play in a virtual monitor...seriously?

The Ugly:
-The "narrative" is almost non-existent.
There are implications left in the world and some limited dialogue that suggest something big is going on but it seems like a cop-out excuse to write anything actually meaningful. I didn't learn anything interesting about this "mysterious" AI.

I had a good level of fun with the various objects and weapons in the game but everything else feels like such a big let down. Some of the puzzles were just needlessly annoying because of how glitchy the physics were and the end of the game is just about as rushed as it could get.

I really wanted to thumb this up at least for the novelty of its mechanics but I'm pretty miffed about the lack of any direction in the story and the rather small variety of enemies.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe games a janky half decent shooter that barely functions. Don't expect the developers to do anything worth while because clearly they're either overwhelmed or have too much money to care about the issues there games have.
The melee system is strange and features unusually good weapons combined with . The firearms in the bone marrow engine games are usually extremely simple lacking attention to detail or realistic function in games that should be trying to create highly detailed objects.
The enemies are all pushovers once you get your hands on a gun. They try to encourage you to conserve ammo by treating it as a resource that you can use to buy items but if your trying to 100% this game your probably going to want to stop fighting enemies in the first place since they dont actually pose a threat in the first place. Your character is near invincible and can outrun basically anything they throw at you. The only ranged enemy in the game is extremely janky and has a tendency of either getting stuck on objects and walls or just missing you entirely. Alot of the jank in this game is because of the physics engine, Really there's no reason why half of the things in this game shouldn't function without it. The physics rarely seem to actually contribute anything to the game unless you count immersion as being important or useful in the games design.
the marrow engines physics are incredible but not impressive. If you have fun throwing random objects around in the world this might appeal to you but realistically that's just not going to cut it. The team decided to cram their game full of puzzles in order to try and impress people who didn't want to throw dead bodies at the wall for fun but as the game progresses you seem to find less puzzles and more shooting galleries. By the end of the game all your doing is walking and shooting.
In the end that's all it is: walking and shooting. You can go back and try and justify all the clunky but rather easy fights by going back through the game again and looking for all the collectible items but for what reason. All the collectibles do for you is either provide you with new weapons that you likely don't need or place able objects that you'll never use. If you do go through the effort you can buy tools that basically turn you into a all powerful god that can fly through walls and create things using a tool gun but whats the point. Why go through the effort of attempting to create something in this game when I know that it will either be a shooting gallery or some physics objects stacked on top of each other. Whats the point of being god in a broken world. You could talk about the issues of this game for days but I'm only human, I can only hope that one day someone of great following finally talks about the games issues instead of blindly praising it because valve decided to shove an index in their mailbox
I can only recommend this game to men who want to pay for their sins by entering a sterile virtual hellscape for 10-16 hours.
