\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBoneworks impresses me, but lets me down all the same.

I really like this game as an introduction to VR. The museum section at the start of the game allows players that are new to VR dive in without feeling too overwhelmed. I took my rig over to my parents' house to show them the latest in game tech with my Index.

My 60 year old mother was able to put on the headset (with some guidance from myself) and start the story-mode. It allowed her to wander around and get a feel for the movement while she found her VR legs. She fortunately has a strong stomach so she didn't experience and discomfort at all.

The physics makes this game quite impressive. It feels good to smash things and press buttons. The gunplay is smooth without getting trapped in the "realism" like other gun-heavy titles can be. Reloading is pretty easy, and I didn't find myself struggling to navigate my equipment belt like I do with games like Onward.

On the negative side, the melee feels like ♥♥♥♥ when you're fighting against enemies. If you are not able to fully immerse yourself into the mindset that you are swinging something with tangible weight and resist the urge to swing your weapon at full speed, it will feel bad most of the time. I found the heavy weapons nearly useless, like the sledge hammer. For such a cumbersome weapon, it seems to rarely do notable damage. The lighter weapons like the hatchet feel pretty good though, and feel full damage consistently.

Likewise, when I got to close to enemies, I found it really difficult to attack without tangling my physics body into the enemy's. I would often get stuck on them and pull them along with me as I try to retreat. Because of this, I basically ditch all my melee weapons as soon as I find a firearm because the game gives you endless amounts of ammo, so you can just spray through baddies.

Gunplay feels good, like I mentioned, with the caveat that some weapons don't seem to shoot where you're pointing them. I found it easier to point-fire weapons accurately than I did using the iron-sights, even after practicing.

Over-all I would still recommend. I definitely have fun while I am playing this game. The story is lack-luster, the environments can be hard to navigate, and after playing for a couple of hours the VR charm is lost and I find myself rushing through puzzles and cheesing my way around things when I can. All that being said, I definitely enjoy playing it whenever I do, and the VR museum is a great place to introduce VR newbies and friends to VR.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEhhhhhh. Get it if you want to see an immersive-full-body experience. It's impressive on that front. But for everything else... TBH it feels like an aggressively mediocre Half-Life 2 mod that loves physics puzzles, but instead of HL2's gameplay that would make everyone dismiss the mod for what it is, it has an overly immersive body/physics simulation instead that will impress some VR enthusiasts.... if they don't projectile vomit first, sine the game's level design is perfectly content to make you jump off massive heights, or have physics objects push you or have your game-body get stuck and freak out.

Okay maybe it won't be that bad, I never vomited or felt very sick, I think I have a high tolerance for what I've played of VR games so far. But everything about the game just kinda makes me feel sick to its stomach, even the bland grey artstyle makes it hard to look at. Plus the way levels and puzzles are designed are more frustrating than fun and are far too big. It has an annoying checkpoint/save system (that supposedly wasn't in the game at launch?!) to help with this but a lot of the time I was running around wondering where the hell I was meant to go. OR I'd have a physics puzzle break and I would have to load a checkpoint (thankfully this only happened once with a certain elevator, but GOD some of these puzzles are needlessly complicated in such long levels).

So over all, the pacing of the levels are bad and made me feel frustrated and fatigued. The AI is not very impressive either aside from the headcrab enemies which you can grab. The combat is almost trivial and the game is insanely generous with ammo that you will never run out. Melee weapons are fun except when your "elbows" get in the way of you hitting things, then it turns into a mess.

Then there's the "story" which I didn't understand what was going on exactly at all. It just felt like the sort of confusing, vague story you'd get from an old mediocre HL2 mod. I didn't really feel any motivation at all, which probably didn't help with my feelings. All I know is you play as McWhiteDude01132 in some virtual world that goes bad or something.

Playing Half-Life: Alyx after this shows how important tight level, presentation and game design decisions are for VR titles. Sure in youtube videos the gameplay looks amazing, but when you actually get to playing it... You might not be as impressed when you wonder around lost in ugly grey environments for ages and then get stuck on some annoying physics puzzle.\t\t\t\t\t
