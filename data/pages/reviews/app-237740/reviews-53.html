\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYoull get a better experience with watching someone else play the game. Youll get the references and humor from everyones favorite nerd in the same manner without the dull game. Let me explain WHY it's dull.

The people who made it clearly didnt spend very much time creating fun mechanics. I love difficult games. Got every achievement in dark souls, beaten super meat boy, and I even beat I wanna be the guy. But all those games have VARIETY. I go into a new stage or area and get killed by something different.

In the AVGN game, you die in 2 ways, falling to your death, or deathblocks. 90% of your deaths are to SPECIFICALLY those 2 things alone. You wont die to any cool enemies or a platforming puzzle, youll die to deathblocks and pits that ARE THE GAME. You could remove everything else and the game would be just as difficult. It is NOT imaginative when your deathblock "puzzle" is jump, jump, dont jump, jump, over and over again. Any mechanic in the game only serves the fact that you need to avoid deathblocks while avoiding them in a stage at some point. Fire and deathblocks? Check. Slippery floor and deathblocks? Check. Gravity and deathblocks? Check. Poorly lit rooms and deathblocks? Check. Spikes and deathblocks? Check. Rockets and deathblocks? Check. 2 silver surfer sections filled with deathblocks? Check. Enemies and deathblocks? Check. Unkillable THING that moves in a circular pattern around deathblocks? Check. Deathblocks and deathblocks? Check. You literally cant go 5 seconds of running in any stage without avoiding deathblocks. ITS BORING.

It's 8 stages of random mechanics and deathblocks, followed by all of them in a single 9th stage that has twice as many deathblocks.

Sure the bosses were a little neat, (except Fred ♥♥♥♥♥, he was just more annoying than anything considering you will NOT beat him the first time you get to him) but youd get to see them by watching someone else play the game. Even with 30 lives on normal, some of the spots in particular (the ones with deathblocks around the deathblocks around the other deathblocks) are literally designed to get you to piss away your lives anyways. Then youve got a small number for the boss and of course you lose them and you get to redo the whole stage... the one filled with deathblocks. So exciting. And when you finally do win, you dont feel on top of the world, you feel cheated. It was less skill and more about memorization and I could play Simon Says if I just wanted that.

I dont regret buying the game and supporting AVGN, but man I feel like I wasted my night more than anything.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a longtime fan of the Angry Video Game Nerd show, I was looking forward to this game when I had heard that it was in development. When I finally got my copy, I realized very quickly that my money was well spent.
This game was definitely made with fans of the show in mind, but, it can be enjoyed by anyone who has a love for old school 2D platformers. Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures is a challenging, 2D platformer where you take control of the Nerd himself (or one of the characters that show up on the show, even people like Mike Matei, that you can unlock.)
If you have played a platformer on the NES before, you can imagine what you can expect from this. The difference being that it has the AVGN humor, and tropes from the show. For instance, AVGN's main weapon is a NES Zapper. His life are bottles of Rolling Rock. Levels are based on games he has reviewed in the past, even things he has only talked about- Such as the most ridiculous trap room a game developer could ever make... and they made it in this game.
The game is a blast to play, and as I said, it is challenging! Although, I do think that there are a few too many checkpoints in the game, and this makes the challenge a little lower-the final levels of the game are madness!
So, if you are a fan of AVGN, you will love this. If you are a fan of challenging 2D platformers from the NES era, you will love this.
Forewarning, this game is DEFINITELY for the mature gamer. Lots of Swearing and potty humor, so if that isn't your thing, you may want to pass this up as there is quite a lot of it. But, otherwise, this game is a fun throwback to my childhood gaming days, with AVGN's brand of humor, and LOTS of references to the show, even very obscure references, these guys really fit a little bit of everything into this little gem.\t\t\t\t\t
