\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures is a 2D side scroller platform game that features the titular character by James Rolfe in a world that parodies old school games and the web show itself.

The various levels of Adventures feature a specific theme and a distinct boss each. You can beat them in any order you wish: once you cleared all the areas you can access the final stage and its boss. Along the levels you can find power-ups and collectables, cameos from internet celebrities and sometime other playable characters with different stats from the Nerd.

It's worth mentioning that the game is very difficult. The obstacles are either annoying or plain hard to overcome and a precise timing is often required. On Easy difficulty you have infinite lives and tries and an higher stack of health (as to say, beers). However, starting from Normal difficulty onwards you recive a certain number of lives and tries, culminating in some unlockable difficulties that require you to finish the entire game with three or four lifes or just plain without dying even once.

Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures is a challenging game in its own right and will surely please the long time fans of the show due to its numerous references\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAVGN has been apart of internet culture for quite some time now, so it was a no brainer that the nerd would finally get his own game.

The AVGN is notorious for playing bad games and being rather crude while presenting such games. What I find interesting about the AVGN game is that they actually incorporated these game design flaws and made them into something great. From impossible jumps to unfair design, AVGN Adventures takes these monstrosities of game design and turns it into something fresh, exciting and just fun.

The difficulty on this one is worthy of the title "NES Hard" and the actual game itself plays like a mixture between the original classic Mega Man games and Contra. The music is reminiscient of nostalgic 80s chip tune and brings gamers back to a time where the basics were all you needed. The sprite art is beautifully built from the ground up with some clear inspiration from other titles, again, such as Mega Man.

Many people seem to say that this game has hardly any replay value which I would like to say is complete bogus. There are three different characters to play as in AVGNA, two of which need to be unlocked by finding them hidden throughout the 8 different stages (All based on episodes of the AVGN.) Also, the game can be played in different difficulty settings, allowing for more than one gameplay experience. A true completionist will want to not only beat this game in more than one way, but master AVGNA, much like those of retro gaming tastes do.

The in game dialogue (in text boxes) is crude, filthy, disgusting, and downright hilarious. AVGNA incorporates many of the Nerds' iconic quotes as well as invent new terms that are definetly NSFW.

Overall, this game is definetly worth purchasing and playing through. The difficulty is extreme at times and I would recommend a controller rather than a keyboard. Those with a taste for retro gaming will find this a masterpiece. Even, if you're not into retro gaming, pick this one up when it goes on sale. You won't regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

Tight controls, vintage level design, and (of course) 'Nerd allusions define this game. With a gamepad, it feels like I'm playing a SNES game that's been inexplicably ported to XBLA, and that's a damn good thing. But if you're not accustomed to the kind of language and imagery that's an integral part of the show, it can seem a bit overwhelming. (here's a tip: don't die)

That said, it's a definite must-have for fans of ScrewAttack, AVGN, and the classics alike.\t\t\t\t\t
