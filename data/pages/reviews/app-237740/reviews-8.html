\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst things first, this game is probably the hardest I've ever played in my entire life. I died over 600 times during the full playthrough (no exaggeration) and had moments where I was seconds away from screaming and breaking my monitor. That is exactly what makes this game so amazing, it's unbearably difficult and yet strangely addicting in a way that makes you keep playing it on and on.

By the time I finished it I felt a wave of euphoria run over me, I had to finish it on easy because I was literally unable to do it on any other difficulty. Every level was a challenge of its own and the final level nearly broke my soul.

All in all? I'd give it a 9/10 and will now share both the positive and negative aspects of it below.


Positive (+)

1 - Stellar 8-bit soundtrack.

2 - The Nerd's quotes when he dies are oftentimes hilarious.

3 - Countless references to AVGN characters and games.

4 - Incredibly beautiful worlds despite being 8-bit.

5 - Hilarious Nerd quotes during gameplay.

6 - Being able to play as other characters each with their unique power-ups. (no spoilers)


Negative (-)

1 - You have to wait for the death quote to finish loading before you can start over. This can get extremely annoying due to some levels having extremely difficult sections where you die constantly.

2 - Some of the Nerd death quotes make no sense whatsoever such as mentioning actions that certain body parts aren't able to do.

3 - The final level boss is FAR too difficult. The game itself is very hard but he is practically impossible.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd like to start by saying the genre of this game is one of my favorites and this entire review is simply my humble opinion of the game, which means it's probably going to have at least a little bit of bias in it. So please be smart and read reviews from many different people with many different taste/backgrounds, okie dokie? Good. Great. Grand. Wonderfull. NO YELLING ON THE BUS!

CONTROLS:

The most precise and satisfying 2D platformer controls ever! No slippery bullcrap, lightspeed falling after walking off a ledge or slow movement either. You have infinite ammo and you can hold the aim button to stand still and shoot in 1 of 7 dirrections. To shoot downwards you need to jump and aiming downwards. You can change characters on the fly by simply pressing a button on your controller/keyboard. You can also duck by pressing down, and believe me, although the ducking movement is very subtle, there are some instances where you can avoid damage simply by ducking.

GAMEPLAY:

As you could already tell from the trailer, the game has a lot of focus on jumping and shooting, so it's basically Mega Man but with a lot of swearing and 3 extra characters instead of new weapons. It has 9 stages in total (8 main stages and 1 final stage) with 1 boss in each. There are 4 different temporary power ups you can pick up in the stages. The Rocks power up give you 25 rocks to throw in a arch. It's more of a running gag, although it might be useful at some very specific momemts. To be honest I never used it since I get the last extra character pretty early. The Super Scope greatly increases your damage, but you lose it if you get hit or change character. Game Graphic Glitch Gremlin stops time for a few seconds and you can use it twice. Super Mecha Death Christ destroyes everything on screen but you can only use once. You can repeat the stages as many times as you want to find secrets or farm extra lives. You can even repeat the bosses, but you don't need to do that to get the secrets of each stage.

The game is hard as hell but it requires nearly no "pixel-perfect" accuracy. Nowadays I can beat a stage without losing any lives, but not the entire game. The problem this game has is the same as the classic Mega Man titles, but maybe a little worse: cryptic level design.

This means your first time playing this game without a guide will be hell. You'll die very often. But playing it a second time WITH a guide and watching other youtube walkthroughs/tips videos will be a cake walk in comparison because you'll be familiar with the tricks for beating the stages with less hassle. The game is not extremely cryptic, but it doesn't have obvious/intuitive solutions to many obstacles either. The good news is that you start the game with 30 lives, the bosses have no invunerability frames and there are multiple nintoasters that serve as checkpoints in every stage. Also, just like Metal Slug does with the tank, this game rewards you for getting better at it with the Super Scope, which is a power up that disappears if you get hit but makes the bosses a complete joke.

Don't mind the number of hours I have with this game. It can be completed in your first time in about 2~3 hours. The only reason I have over 40 hours is just because I REALLY love replaying this game, because in every replay I discover something new that allows me to beat the game more smoothly. I'll refer to some tips for some of the worst moments in this game by the end of this review, but that might spoil the experience for you so read them at your own risk.

GRAPHICS:

As most run and gun/platformers with a lot of shooting, this game appeals to the artistic style graphics, but don't let this fool you. This game has excellent animations and a beautiful pallette. Being the psychopath that I am, I particularly like the explosions and all the blood splitting. Despite the fact that this game is pixelated, it's still a modern game which means there's no flickering and no slowdown, regardless of how many enemies are on the screen.

PRESENTATION:

This game is a homage to the Angry Video Game Nerd where you play as the Nerd using a NES Zapper in a world with pixelated graphics, "bullcrap" difficulty (read the GAMEPLAY part of the review) and chiptune soundtrack similar to the NES era. It has Mega Man/Contra gameplay and a lot of humorous references from the show. The Nerd even makes comments about some of them and he also does his classic "I rather have a buffalo take a diarrhea dump in my ear" lines whenever he dies, having a different line each time. By far, my favorite reference is the game over screen when you lose all your lives.

The menus are pretty simple. There are only 2 cutscenes in the game, one at the beggining and one after the final boss, but both are hilarious.

SOUND:

Phenomenal chiptune epicness. There are no songs in the game that I think are bad or unfitting. In fact I absolutely love almost all of them except the snow stage and the haunted stage. Those 2 are alright, just not phenomenal. The sound effects have good quality as well. There is no voice sound though.

STORY:

You got sucked into a crappy video game by a arm grabbing your balls. It's a AVGN video game. What the hell did you expect?

Conclusion:

HELL YEAH I recommend this game! At least for people that like Mega Man games, that is.

Pros:

- Great controls
- Good gameplay
- Great graphics
- Great sound
- Great presentation

Cons:

- Level design is cryptic (nothing too extreme though)

My rating:

8/10

TIPS:

- Never forget that many attacks/hazards can be avoided simply by ducking. Seriously, I can't stress enough how many people forget this...

- Try to find the secret areas in each stage (or look at a guide...I won't blame you). You'll need the extra character to access some areas and many of them have health, extra lives and the Super Scope.

- In the beggining of the hell stage there's a pixel-perfect jump with spikes right after you jump over a hole (that has a fireball going up and down), go all the way to the right, go down and then go left. But you don't need to pass over the spikes in the intended way. You can simply drop from above through the hole with the fireball by pressing left in your DPad on the right side of this hole (once the fireball goes up, of course). You'll always safely land exactly on the left side of the pit with spikes below. Preferably, use the Guitar Guy since he moves faster horizontally.

This stage is easy, so the best strategy against the boss is the Super Scope. Also, considering there's a secret area that has 3 extra lives right at the beggining of the stage, this is the best place to farm for extra lives.

- In the boss fight of the haunted stage finish the hand first and Jason afterwards or both at the same time.

- You can skip the death blocks section of the final stage by simply using that green bouncy platform that go up and down to go over this whole section off screen (kinda like finding the warp zone in Super Mario Bros).

- B.S. Man is perfect against the boss of the "inappropiate" stage since he moves slowly and his attack does heavy damage.

- In the final boss, first watch a few "easy way to beat the final boss" videos to understand a bit of his paterns. In the second phase when he has spikes around him, use the Guitar Guy to shoot him from the other side of the screen in the lowest platform when the boss comes back down. In the third phase he'll start dropping feces from above. Stay in the middle platform, observe the feces falling while shooting him holding the aim button and walking off to the lower platforms on each side to dodge. It shouldn't take too much pratice to do the aiming as second nature. Whenever he passes over you, if his projectile falls too close to you, you won't even have to move at all to dodge the next one. In his last phase stay next to the platform with spikes and jump his spike ball when necessary while shooting him using the aim button.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAVGN Adventures is an action game and "rage platformer" based on AVGN real game story, aha. Or does "rage platformer" description only apply to games where you get trolled with unforseeable traps? Then it wouldn't fit. But you get it.

It wasn't as bad as I expected. I would learn and pass challenges, music was fancy, it employs "it's easier than it looks" by having modern standards of forgiving collision boxes. Seems like fun but two features put it down.

First, lives system. You get like 30 of them on Normal. No, you don't restart a whole game on Game Over, just get booted into level select. Not the best thing in rage platformer. But I did come appreciate. If you die so much per level, then Game Over means that it's time for you to take a break. BUT they don't replenish lives between levels, so you are mostly wasting time if you barely beat one level and then go into another, not until you get Game Over to reset them.
Easy difficulty gives you double health point, which is a bit too much, and gives infinite lives yet problem number two remains.

Second, death blocks. Oh boy, so many death blocks. Touch one and you are instant turkey. They sure overuse them a lot, even Haunted House has ghost death blocks that you can see only when upclose. Which isn't too bad, as I have said it's easier than it looks with game's forgiving collision but unbalance is still there. And a sour taste.

And game seems to be a bit repetitive and plain. The stuf feeling of beating the death block challenges is pretty much what gets it going. Because otherwise there is a bit of lack of powerups or weapon changes. There are some but you will die instantly to death block to keep them so they don't count. Monsters are nearly all just obstacles that don't go after you. And bosses feel samey, designed by 80s amateur tech demo programmer who has just discovered the sin() function.

Amazing references tho.

Well, I still can't deny that game feel fun or rewarding. But problematic. Which would have led me to so much pain deciding on recommending or not.

Thankfully, AVGN 1&2 Deluxe is out, which has heavily remastered the previous two games. It has normal difficulty that has infinite lives and less death blocks. And it has "old-school" difficulty which does have these features back. Consider that one instead.\t\t\t\t\t
