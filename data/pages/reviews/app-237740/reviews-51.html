I think he's a great reason why gaming on the internet is such a popular thing, especially the whole "I'm pissed off so I'm going to make up words to coicide with what I think about this game" fad. But this game... Not even remotely worth your time.

First off, let me express my UNDYING HATE FOR SETTING UP THE CONTROLLER. I had a struggle just like everyone else, and it's almost a year past it's last patch. Every controller I plug into this PC is recognized by EVERY game I own. This game? Nope. I had to fidget with the advanced settings just to let the game recognize my controller. And as much as I wanted to use my Xbox One controller, I was limited to my 360 controller.Hell, even my PS4 controller worked, What a bunch of ♥♥♥♥.

Secondly, this game does a great job at making you FEEL like the ♥♥♥♥♥♥' nerd. I couldn't help but go through the game and say nearly the same kind of lines AVGN uses in his own reviews as I went through each stage just to cope with how ♥♥♥♥♥♥ the levels are. But that's the only good thing it has going for it. Just because you FEEL like the ♥♥♥♥♥♥' nerd doesn't justify a 9.99 dollar pricepoint and it's extrutiatingly difficult levels (especially the final level).

There's a difference between intelligent and clever level design that pushes the boundaries of difficulty (Kaizo Mario is a great example of this) by utilizing every single mechanic to to be frustratingly hard, but you envy it because the way the level is set up is extremely smart and well-thought. This game? This game is just a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ ♥♥♥♥. You like invisible blocks? How about death blocks that force you to die upon touching them. Hell, this game has INVISIBLE death blocks! Granted, it's tied to two specific levels, but the game is already hard enough. That's just a small sample of how the game forces you to cope with the ♥♥♥♥ it gives you. It isn't clever. Just a huge ♥♥♥♥.

You'll blaze through 30 lives like it's no one's business, but 30 lives is about enough to get through a single stage... Excluding the LJN stage. This is the only forgiving mechanic in the game, seeing as your only punishment is restarting the stage and not the entire game. Each live gives you three hits before you're done, but the game isn't very sparing of these magical ales. It's pretty ruthless. I've gotten to the final boss of the game and blaed through 75 lives. That's three seperate playthroughs of the level, by the way.

Final thoughts. The game does a great job emulating how The Nerd feels when reviewing games. That's it's biggest pro, and possibly the only pro. This game isn't clever or smart or forgiving or whatever. It isn't like Super Meat Boy where everything you do is your fault so you can't feel too upset about it. Nope. This game didn't justify it's original 14.99 dollar price point or it's 9.99 dollar price point. I'm more than glad I only paid 3.99 for this hunk of ♥♥♥♥.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst and foremost, if you are not a fan of the Nerd, blue humor, NES hard gameplay, and parody, turn around. This is not the game for you.

AVGN Adventures is a love letter to everything that is the Angry Video Game Nerd. You've got the Rolling Rock health display. You've got familiar Cinemassacre characters. You've got Super Mecha Death Christ. You've got Glitch Gremlin. You've got the NES Zapper as your main weapon. You've got hilariously vulgar lines coming from the Nerd at every new element in the game. And you've got homages to some of the Nerd's best moments.

As for gameplay, it's kind of like Mega Man in that you run and shoot similar to the Blue Bomber. You also have stage selects before each level and, after defeating the previous bosses, you fight in one final stage that throws everything at you. It also throws in a little bit of Super Mario 2 in having different playable characters with different mechanics.

The music is the absolute best part of the game. Each level has a driving theme that's also an homage to a certain game. While my personal favorite is the theme to Future ****balls 2010, the Laughin' Jokin' Numbnuts theme is just crazy.

It's not a perfect game at all, but it is more competent and has had more care given to it than most of the games James has played on the show. If you're a fan of the Nerd, you should've already gotten the game by now.\t\t\t\t\t
