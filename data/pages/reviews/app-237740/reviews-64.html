\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is a hidden genre of game known only among the hardcore: "platform hell." Among its top echelon is the classic, "I Wanna Be The Guy," which inspired Steam games Super Meat Boy and VVVVVV. Platform Hell games are identified by one simple trait: the game knows what you are about to do, and punishes you for doing something predictable. A cloud mighdisappear as you land on it, for example, or an apple may fall off a tree UPWARDS as you attempt to jump over it.

As a cataloguer of terrible video game tropes, it is natural that the Angry Video Game Nerds includes many, many, surprises in this game. However, AVGN-Adventures is nowhere near as difficult as these other platformers and is slightly less fun.

The Good:

* Awesome level variety harkening back to several classic video games.

* Dozens of allusions, cameos, and references to Angry Video Game Nerd episodes, classic video games, and video game tropes.

* Your character's light-gun changes color depending on the difficulty level. Pink for easy, orange for normal, and gray for "old school." (I was never able to reach one of the higher, unlockable difficulty levels).

* Like Nintendo games of yore, there are plenty of secrets to find.

* Great use of humor.

* Each level ends with a unique boss from nerd-culture.

* Fantastic pause music.

The Bad:

* The levels are a bit short, although they make up it with their difficulty.

* The "You've got Skill" Steam achievement is duplicated... there are two of them for some reason.

* Control Issue: It is hard to fire your gun to the right if you recently started moving left, and vice-versa. This becomes very annoying.

* Some enemies, like the reindeer on the snow level, have "magnetism" or something and stay right ON your sprite. since your gun can only fire outwards, it makes them lethal and unfair to fight.

* The game uses the enter key as a pause button, but if you shift+tab out of the game to talk to someone, anytime you hit enter to send your message, the game pauses/unpauses.

* The only level that is truly "really difficult" is the last one, which is only unlockable after completing all other levels.


The Ugly:

* At times, the game felt more like a chore than a game. I got bored after playing through the first level, but only stuck it out to write this review.

* The game only has a few special items: useless rocks, super scope, "glitch man," and "mecha Jesus." (I'm making up my own names for these things, I forgot what they're killed.) I was expecting to discover more items as the game continued, but no, there are just the four of them.


The Verdict:

* Pick this up if you like AVGN or platformers, but don't expect a rock-solid experience. I personally did not enjoy my time with it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn honour of the upcoming release of AVGN: The Movie, I decided to tackle this one.

I'll make it short, partially because the game is also rather short. The game will last you about 4-6 hours, depending on how good you are at platformers, This game is very similar to Megaman 3 when it comes to control, flow, and level design, but even more difficult. If you don't like to die a lot, then a game that is easy to get an achievement for dying 1000 times isn't for you.

The game lets you shoot in all directions, except down for some reason. This is a nit-pick worthy note to me, as he does claim to put elements of all his favourite side-scrollers together, which one of them is Super Castlevania 4.

All the great AVGN moments are referenced, with the Couch / Guitar guy, ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Man and even Mike Matei, James' best friend and co-worker, being playable. You will see halarious moments, such as the "Pitfall first ladder" rant being heavily enforced. You go down the first ladder in one of the levels, and instantly fall to your death into a chamber of lava, spikes, and flame-sharks (which, by the way, you get to ride later!).

The humour is exactly what you would expect from a game written and designed by James Rolfe. Sarcasm levels reach 9001, as the nerd himself will comment on the "♥♥♥♥-factor" of the game. When I was playing the "Beat it and Eat it" level, which pays omage his Atari Porn Games episode available only at his website, I hit a slippery spot on theground and fell to my death saying "♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, stupid ice... wait. That's not ice."

Level design is very good, but enemies lack real depth. While the poop jokes were kind of novel in 2007, the just aren't best served wth a dish of platformer.

Other minor issues I have found are that sometimes you don't get a chance to read the text. It took me quite a while to find out how to switch characters, as the only time it tells you without going to the option screen to my knowledge is when you meet your first alternate character. Another small problem I ran into was the music bugged out on Blizzard of Balls, looping indefinately until I closed the game entirely. This never reaccured, so it may have been fixed in one of the regular updates Mike and James roll out every few weeks.

Over-all, if you are an AVGN fan like me or retro-loving gamers, it's a must-buy at a respectable 8.5/10. For non-fans and platform haters, it's a non-impressing 6/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, I'm very surprised to see some hate for this game. Let me try to be as objective as possible here. AVGNA is a game that, let's face it, is designed around the problems that James Rolfe has pointed out in his over 100 videos for his popular web series, the Angry Video Game Nerd. AVGNA is mainly a tribute to his legacy of video reviews, but which manages to capture the spirit of that show while also providing humorous dialogue and some pretty fun levels. The control is very good (best played with a controller), the graphics are appealing, the health pickups and one-ups are relatively plentiful, and there are numerous checkpoints scattered generously across the levels. The music is very catchy and gives the game a strong sense of energy. I also enjoyed how you can play as different characters who have different weapons and abilities, if you could find them.

Now let's get to the major complaints. Yes, this game is full of cheap kills. That's part of the point. This game is full of meta jokes because you're playing as the Nerd who has to fight the summation of obstacles that have plagued so many of the terrible games from his youth. As I was playing, I was really thinking "Wow, this stuff really must've been horrendous" in reference to the games he had to beat for his show. But the beautiful thing about this game is that I feel it never exceeds the threshold. The "cheap" sections really aren't that long, and the difficult areas are always preceded by easier sections that are meant to teach you the tricks or gimmicks of each level.

For people who are debating whether or not to buy this game, I would say that if you're a fan of AVGN and you like platformers or played them as a kid, give this one a go. In terms of 2-D platformers that have come out recently, this is definitely one of the best. The level design actually had quite a bit of thought put in, and even though it can be frustrating, if you're smart enough to figure out basic patterns and patient enough to repeat certain sections, you won't regret getting this game. I've been a big fan of James for years and I think that this game stays true to the basic feel of the videos and the attitude he exudes as the Nerd. Plus I loved the humor and self-referential jokes.

To respond to negative reviews, here's what I have to say: There's no way I can understand complaints about this game being too difficult. Compared to some of the games people of James's generation played growing up, like Super Ghouls and Ghosts, Silver Surfer, and Battletoads, AVGNA is a walk in the park. AVGNA was made as both a parody of bad games and as a send-up of the games that defined "Nintendo-hard." Kids these days are coddled. This game isn't really that tough when you get down to it, nor is it unfair. As for complaints that the game is padded, you could say that for any platformer. The only platformer I've ever seen with a decent storyline was Braid, and this game is definitely not that. Is AVGNA worth $10? I suppose it's subjective, but I had more fun with this game than I did with several other more expensive games that may have technically better graphics and more of a story but fail at having an interesting character or unique feel. This game doesn't feel recycled, it feels new and different, in spite of its origins from past games. In other words, it synthesizes elements from both the AVGN show and from the games themselves, but I felt that when notoriously bad elements were included, it was always for the sake of humor, and they were treated in a relatively fair manner, even though some people find the game to be grinding.\t\t\t\t\t
