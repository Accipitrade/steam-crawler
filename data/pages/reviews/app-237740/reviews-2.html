The only way to get out is by completing all the worlds and defeat the end boss. You got seven worlds to complete. AVGN Adventures is itself a very solid throwback to NES-era games, complete with the ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô• difficulty and references to the absolute worst the library had to offer, as well as lots of references to actual episodes of AVGN on YouTube. That being said, I do have a few complaints. It seems like James and his team were a little TOO experienced in game development, and when they set out to make a hard game, they accomplished their goal tenfold. This game might be a bit too hard for casual players, and definitely outside of the difficulty range of half of the AVGN's fanbase. One achievement, the Halloween one, was being manually enabled by the devs, meaning the only way to get it now is to enter the Steam console and downgrade the version, since the devs no longer add it every Halloween. Another one essentially requires you to beat the entire game with 1 HP, 1 life, and no continues. It's just an unfun design decision for achievement hunters to feel forced to beat a mode that was obviously designed to be impossible.\t\t\t\t\t
