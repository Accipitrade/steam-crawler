\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven if you're not familiar with the AVGN, this game still impresses as a throwback to the 8-bit Megaman games. Yes, it's a lot harder, but they give you 30 lives and constant continue points on the normal difficulty, so it's not much of an issue. It's very doable even with a keyboard. Once you've memorized how to get past the hardest parts, and as long as you're taking your time, it's not even that bad. Of course, the game basically taunts you into rushing head first into the various obstacles, and by the tenth time you've died to one, you end up playing so recklessly and "on tilt" that you just keep dieing, with the Nerd character giving a hilarious randomly generated complaint after each death. The game is a bit short, like a traditional Megaman game (8 stages and an amazingly evil final stage) but for $10, it's totally worth the money. There are a few secrets to find, like unlocking some bonus characters, and there's always the added replay value of trying to complete the game on the hard difficulty, which limits the number of lives and continues you get.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a lot of potential. The mechanics are good, the pixel-graphics are wonderful, and the controls are tight. It would have been a great retro-styled platform game if it weren't so difficult.

It seems that the team who made this game decided that making an ultra-difficult game would either be funny, or a fitting homage to the older 8-bit games that many us us grew up with (many of which owe their own difficulty to the quarter-eating business plans of arcdaes).

As others have mentioned; death will occur often in this game, and it would have been nice to be placed at the last checkpoint instantly (a la Hotline Miami) instead of being forced to sit through a screen with a "funny" AVGN quote after each death. The "death block" (tiles in the environment which kill you upon touch) are pretty annoying, and do seem to stem from the developer's need to artificially increase the diffictulty.

If you enjoy ultra-difficult games, perhaps you will enjoy this. I found it to be much harder than Contra; maybe more akin to Ninja Gaiden or Battletoads. Overall, I was disappointed because I found this to be more of a test of patience than a proper game.\t\t\t\t\t
