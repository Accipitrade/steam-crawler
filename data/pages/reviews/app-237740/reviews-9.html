\r\n\t\t\t\t\ti see death blocks in my sleep, 10/10

But seriously, this game is a fun romp, but sadly a lot of the enjoyability is lost to very lazy level design, with death blocks scattered everywhere that pop out of nowhere. The mark of a good difficulty-based platformer is dying with a feeling of "Ah man, that was my fault". This game forgoes that game design philosophy for "Let's just throw a bunch of random garbage on the screen and make the player trial-and-error their way through it", which makes the game become a drag. Combine this with questionable enemy placement and some sections that seem engineered to be impassible on the first run, and you end up with a game that isn't really even that rewarding once you've passed terrible sections, because you don't feel like you've actually DONE anything, just that you've rammed into the wall of blocks enough that you've managed to slip through the cracks.

This problem is exemplified in the HORRIBLE haunted house level, which presents possibly the most misguided and baffling design choice - blocks that move along in a scripted pattern that are also invisible. This is a horrible idea, because the game is already filled with guesswork, so adding yet another element of the unknown is just plain terrible.

But despite it's flaws, I just can't find myself to dislike this game and often revisit it. While the level design is bad, the controls are responsive and feel great to play with, especially the aiming and gravity. Just don't play with a keyboard.
The other characters are an interesting idea, and they do add a fair bit of variation in the gameplay, and the non-linear structure makes finding secrets with them rewarding (although this comes with the drawback of all levels being designed around the nerd, which makes them only useful in a platforming sense in very specific situations).

The game is very charming, with lots of references and characters that are cameos to the AVGN videos, which avid fans will enjoy. It also allows for some truly awesome moments, like Riding the laser eyed shark in the hell level , which I will admit made me laugh. The randomly generated messages upon death are fun, but quickly lose their charm after dying so often.

While my opinions on this game are VERY mixed, I can still wholeheartedly recommend it - If you're willing to put up with the garbage it WILL lob at you.\t\t\t\t\t
