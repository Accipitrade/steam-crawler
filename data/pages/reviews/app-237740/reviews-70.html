\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGraphics: Game is visually designed to resemble classical 8bit games of Nintendo era. It succeeds in that very well. The graphics are simple, yet there are many little things mixed in the background.

Soundtrack: Each level has its own background music. They are all memorable and energetic. I would listen to them even outside of the game.

Game content: Game consists of several levels and a hidden final level, which appears after finishing every other level. Levels are mostly basic sidescroller which vary occasionally into faster sideshooters. There are also other playable characters hidden in the game, which you can unlock by finding them. The hidden characters have different abilities compared to each other.

Gameplay: I played the game with keyboard and had no difficulties, although it seems to be designed to be played with a controller. The controls are very fluent and you feel like you are in control of your character. There are some parts which are harder with some of the playable characters. Luckily you can swap between them freely in the middle of the game.

Difficulty: The game is notorious for its difficulty. Nothing in it is impossible of course and since you have unlimited continues, you can keep on playing. It is just frustrating enough to make you want to finish the stage you started, even if you have been stuck on a difficult part for some time.

Overall a decent and fun platformer game. I would recommend it to anyone who likes classic sidescrollers and/or The AVGN.\t\t\t\t\t
