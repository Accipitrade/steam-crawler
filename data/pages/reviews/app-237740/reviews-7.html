\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn short? Pretty disappointing. As most people who (I presume) buy it I'm an AVGN fan, I watch James since its beginning. The game itself does stay true to AVGN concept and character, but that's about it, it's a game that doesn't stand in its own two feet as a platformer. I would call it a "meme" game and a "one trick pony". As people have stated it is hard, but the thing is that it isn't hard in a good sense, it just aims to be frustrating for the joke of being frustrating. AVGN of course is about a gamer who is angry about crappy nes era games that have bad mechanics, bad controls etc. This game makes a joke of that by giving you useless powerups, jumps that instantly kill you, etc. It's funny the first couple of times, but after 50 times it's well...frustrating. What's the point of that? Celeste is hard, but it's fun, there is more to it, same Super Meat Boy, not this one, it's just a simple platformer full of show references. Sadly those references are not enough to keep your interest. Just no depth in the game to speak of. 2 stars out of 5.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell; it took me awhile but I finally beat "Old School". I am currently working on "Hard as Balls" and if I do somehow manage that, I may just give a shot at "Impossible" and "YOLO" mode.

This game is definitely a bit of an acquired taste at first, as the difficulty is quite high compared to the average platformer that comes out these days. I do play plenty of Castlevania, Ninja Gaiden, Mega Man, etc. but this game has some things that you really need to get used to before things will feel "smooth" or "fluid" (even though James always says 'fluent' when meaning 'fluid' for some reason... who knows?)

The controls are pretty damn solid, but there just seems to be an extra tiny bit of input lag than what I'm used to on any other game I play. I seriously have no idea why it seems more pronounced here than any other game I play... But again, you can get used to it (and maybe it's just an issue for me). This plays sort of like combining a SHMUP (Shoot 'em Up -- R-Type, Gradius, etc.) with Mega Man. Basically in the sense that there is a lot of pattern memorization, lots of button-mashing and a bunch of different weapon styles to use.

As you play, there are 3 other unlockable characters: 'Couch Guy' AKA Kyle Justin, the theme song creator for AVGN; 'M*therf*cker Mike' AKA Mike Matei who helped write episodes, played various goofy characters and mainly, his character in this was featured in Board James -- a sort of 'spin-off' of AVGN which delves into tabletop board games; and finally, 'B.S. Man' AKA B*llsh*t Man. B.S. Man is an even more obscure Cinemassacre character who would do extremely short anti-reviews of various objects, ideas and services. Each character comes with different traits; such as Cough Guy being able to shoot a waving projectile which can be extremely helpful on bosses as well as various areas where enemies clutter the screen. He also runs the fastest but jumps the shortest -- a la Super Mario Bros. 2 essentially with Toad. MF Mike plays a lot like Luigi with a high jump but slower run speed, and then B.S. Man who gets a special ability to double-jump, similar to how Peach can hover.

If you're familiar with AVGN, this game will have a bunch of cute/humourous references scattered throughout; from the "Where'd you learn to fly?" b*tch as a stage boss to the Cacadaemons (yes, Caca as in poo, duty, diarrhea, ass-juice, etc.), the Ghosts of Christmas Past, the giant death pit from the Super Pitfall review and finally, the coup de grace. Fred ♥♥♥♥(k)s himself. I'm guessing the spelling in the game is 'F, U, C, K' instead of 'F,U,C,H" to avoid possible issues using an actual guy's name, but who knows. Maybe the guy who built the game didn't even realize which episode it originated in and what the actual spelling was though.

Now we have to talk about the soundtrack. Oh boy, what a jam. This is one of those soundtracks I find myself getting caught in my head constantly, just like I do with all the great classics. So I've got to say, that alone bumped this game up massively as I already get that nostalgic feeling if I come back to the game after a month or two. Such a great feeling, and incredibly strange to feel from such a recent game.

Also, if you somehow made it this far, buy this version. This version is FAR superior to the remaster. Maybe I'm in the minority, but the terrible AVGN II engine, muddied graphics, ATROCIOUS audio mixing and generally unfavourable changes to songs/sound effects... It just doesn't compare to the original. Get this one and ignore AVGN II and the remaster. Honestly, part of me wonders if they remastered the first one so they could simply cut the original designer out of profits for the re-release, but that's me being my cynical self.

Seriously, get this if you are an AVGN and NES/SNES/Genesis fan. Get it now!\t\t\t\t\t
