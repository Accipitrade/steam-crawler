\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAVGN Adventures, as you probably figured looking at the screenshots, is a sidescrolling retro style platformer. What makes this one different from all the other retro style platformers is that it is based entirely around the concept and exploits of the Angry Video Game Nerd.

AVGN decides to play a pretty bad game, as he usually does. However, this game decides to pull him through the TV, sucking him into the world of terrible videogames and bad videogame tropes. The plot is very simple, and only really serves the purpose of giving you the setup to actually play through the adventure.

If you're not really a fan of the AVGN series of videos, then this isn't for you; the whole game is designed to be a playable homage to the Nerd and everything that the fans love about him and what he does. The whole game is jam-packed full of references that most people familiar with the series will get and appreciate, as well as a number of deliberately bad game design choices that make their way in as a result of his frequent complaining of said issues in many of his reviews. It all adds up to a game with themes that are very faithful to the titular hero.

The levels are deliberately very tricky, and oftentimes you'll find you die a lot in each one. Most of the levels often have instances of trial and error. You will most likely die not knowing how to navigate certain obstacles the very first time, or not react quickly enough not knowing what's about to come. To offset this style of difficulty, the player is given a total of 30 lives to work with. This is usually enough to make it from beginning to end of a level. But it's in this feature that lies my main criticism with the game.

The system of 30 lives to make it through any given level is fine, however my main issue with this is that upon completing a level, your remaining number of lives will carry over to the level you play next. This means that unavoidably you will always start subsequent levels with significantly fewer lives to work with, and you might find that you won't even have anywhere close enough to beat a level the first time. Get a Game Over, and you lose your checkpoint progress.

There is no way to reset your lives count except by getting a Game Over, either. I've tried to save, quit and come back into the existing save to see if that would work. Sadly the save does save your current lives count, meaning it was intentionally designed that way. So if you begin with a meager 5 lives, you will either want to just kill them off at the start or use them for practice in the early part of the level, because you're probably not going to get too far with them.

That issue aside, AVGN Adventures is a really fun game for fans with great graphics and awesome 8-bit music. Highly recommened if you love AVGN!\t\t\t\t\t

I wish there was a Mixed opinion for reviews, but since there isn't, I have to make it a negative.

When I was 12, I was very curious about this game when I saw it was rated M on the 3DS/Wii U. I was like, "what the heck? How can a pixelated game like this get a M rating? That's stupid." That was the time I never heard about the Angry Video Game Nerd. So when I found out the game is on steam, I bought it on sale to see what it's like. Well... since I was 12 I wasn't use to language, I stopped playing after 5 minutes, and never touched the game ever again.

Now after 2 years... I came back to this game since now I'm use to language, and it has been a while.

Now to the actual review:

Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures (AVGNA) is an action-platformer. Nerd and his friends were playing a sh!tty game, which caused Nerd's friends to be sucked into the TV. Nerd almost escapes, but then an arm comes out the TV, and grabs him by his... balls... and gets pulled inside the TV. There isn't much of a story, but it's an alright and appropriate opening for this game.

The gameplay at parts can be fun, but most of the game is filled with cheap deaths. The first level isn't all that bad, and the easiest out of the whole entire game. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ is more difficult, but yet still passable. Happy Fun Candy Time is even more difficult, to the point it can make you get quite frustrated. Future ****balls 2010 feels easier than Happy Fun Candy Time, but it's still quite hard. Blizzard of Balls is like Future ****balls 2010: It's easier than HFCT but it's still quite hard. Thy Farts Consumed has the coolest moment in the game, but the level is quite difficult, and needs some thinking.
Dungeons and ****holes has a part that is quite unexpected for a cheap death, but the level is quite hard. Beat It and Eat It is a... low graphic porno stage... that actually made me rage quit. It's mostly the second hardest stage in the game.

That's how far I got, but you know where I'm going: The game is hard as hell. The game does make fun of bad level design, but that doesn't make it any better.

The gameplay does go overboard with the death blocks. They're everywhere... and in every level. If you touch one, it's an automatic death. Since these are in every level, and everywhere, you will die a lot. To make things worse: there is different type of death blocks. There is a type that just stays in place, a type that disappears for 3 seconds and reappears for 3 seconds, a type that you need to be close to see where that death block is at, etc. etc.

When you die, you get AVGN references to the show. They're suppost to be funny, but for me, they just appear just childish and cringeworthy. If Nerd said it in the show, it's funny, but if you read it, it's not that funny and you just want to get back into the game (the death screen lasts for like 5 seconds each. No joke.).

The game does have positives, though.

The 8-bit graphics are the best I've seen in any game. Everything is very detailed, and you know what you are looking at.

The soundtrack kicks ass. They did a good job on making the soundtrack sound amazing, and fit appropriately into levels.

You can use a controller. That makes the game much more easier to control since using a keyboard doesn't really work well for platformers.

TL;DR: But even with its positives, Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures is just a game that is only made to piss you off. I know it's suppose to piss you off, but making a game way to overwhelmingly difficult just ruins the fun of the game, and it won't make you want to come back to the game.

I will try my best to finish the game, but I can't recommend it (really wish there was a Mixed option for reviews). 5/10.
