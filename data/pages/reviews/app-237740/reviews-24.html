The game is quite bad and thats a shame because I want to like this game but it's just bad. The main reason is the insta deaths. In good platformer shooters like the megaman series insta deaths are rare and well thought. They use the insta deaths as a extra challenge on top of the normal enemies. Many times this is done in a pit format. A good example is the air man stage on mega man 2. The deathblocks aren't a extra challenge on top of the normal enemies. The enemies are a challenge on top of the deathblocks. The enemys are also very weak and pointless. On my 40 min playthrough of the whole game I didn't die once to a normal enemy. I would say 70% of my deaths were to the deathblocks and 30% to the bosses. Overall the game is too short, has cheap deaths and is actually quite boring.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tASSS! ♥♥♥♥!! WHAT WERE THEY THINKING???

Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures is a platforming 2D side-scrolling video game whose mechanics revolve around a continuous trial and error. Pretty hard at the first glance, it requires mastery (or a deep knowledge of the traps beforehand) that comes hand in hand with practice. I would say that it is like Dark Souls, but this game has a lot of cheap tricks that becomes a sudden death even if you're an experienced player.

Talking about the graphics, don't expect an AAA game level. Pixel art is OK and it reminds me a lot the games shown in the Angry Video Game Nerd videos, just like a 16 bits game. Music is pretty awesome and it has been created with that retro style in mind.

A lot, and I really mean it, A LOT of this game has been created having the AVGN videos puns or characters included. So you won't get that references if you didn't watch that videos. It's not that bad and it's still a good platforming game overall, but maybe a little bit too short.

If you're still thinking about buying it or not, do me a favor: watch the very first Angry Video Game Nerd video on YouTube and then check out a little bit of the game in a walkthrough video. That should help you more than anything you can read here.

Should I recommend this game? If you liked the show, and a hard and good platforming game: HELL YES! If you only want a platforming game, there are a lot of them better than AVGN Adventures, but it still worth it if you can get it at a lower price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has great platforming, very good controls, and a comedic retro atmoshpere that pokes fun at NES games.

One of the first things I noticed was the controls. Running and jumping feels great in this game. Using a controller, when I missed a jump or ran into an obstacle, I completely felt like it was my fault and not the game or the controls.

I feel the challenge is good because of the variety of difficulty levels. On "normal" mode, the game auto-saves after every level, you get 30 lives and unlimited continues. There are also about 7 or 8 checkpoints on every level as well. I died 529 times going through the game on "normal" mode. Probably 100 of those deaths were on the boss of the game.

The atmosphere is what makes the game most memorable. All 10 levels (apart from the tutorial level) have a distinctive feel to them. I liked how the 2010 themed level starts with the Nerd saying: "Sure, this is what 2010 was like, puke green blocks and purple jellyfish." I also like the death blocks. They are blocks with skulls and crossbones on them that kill you instantly on contact, poking fun at how in old games everything seemed to kill you.

One issue with the game is the levels getting blurred out when you beat them, making it hard or impossible to read the level name. Another thing is you can only have one saved game file and if you select the "new game" option, you will erase your old game save. That almost wouldn't be a problem since you can do the levels in any order, but the last one is only unlocked by beating all the other levels first.

Overall this is a fun, challenging, memorable game that I am very glad I spent $4.99 on.\t\t\t\t\t
