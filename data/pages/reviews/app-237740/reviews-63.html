\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBased on the legendary online reviewer by the same name, created and played by James Rolfe, AVGN Adventures is a delightfully retro 2D romp. Clearly, this game was designed to appeal to casual gamers as well as AVGN fans. The controls are very responsive and easy to pick up (I would argue, for all its simplicity, it's some of the best controls I've ever used), and, despite what many have said, the difficulty is balanced fairly, sitting on that line between easy and frustratingly hard to provide healthy challenge. Compare this to say, Worms Revolution or Pokemon Battle Revolution (nominal similaraties purely coincidental), where much of the challenge comes from how unfairly rigged the game and the AI are against you, and you can appreciate how good a game this is. As a fan of the Nerd myself, I also enjoyed the sheer volume of references and characters crammed into the game, and the many cameos from other reviewers (though no JonTron, Spoony, Yahtzee or Nostalgia Critic? REALLY?). The music is also fun to listen to and has a lot of variety. The main complaint I have with this game is also a warning: if you miss a level-specific achievement, you can't replay a level you've already completed or start a new game to go back and do it without your old file being erased. This caused some frustration and anger with me when I started a new game to complete the "No, you listen!" achievement in the tutorial level, only to then realise that the game had replaced my old save file. Given the simplicity of the game, a better saving system really wouldn't have been difficult to do. As a final note, gamers who aren't followers of the Nerd won't get the references (then again, would you really buy this game is you weren't a fan?). A great game, and very addictive.\t\t\t\t\t
