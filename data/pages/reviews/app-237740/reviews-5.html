A game that is intentionally bad, which makes it bad

It seems that I had the wrong idea for this game for a long time. I have been following the AVGN and his internet adventures with sh*tty games. I always thought that this was supposed to be a hard but fair game. I was wrong. There are parts of it that are challenging without being annoying but the overall taste at the end of it is that it was made to piss the player off.

My playthrough
I played the game on Normal difficulty. I completed the 8 levels and unlocked the final one. Made it to the final boss and made it as far as the 3rd stage of the fight (I believe it’s the last one). I can’t be 100% sure but I think the last level took 2.5 out of the 6 hours I have played. The last level is cheaper than any of the games it makes fun of and a lot cheaper than it has any right to be. I would have completed the game if I played for another hour probably, but I decided that it’s not worth it. In retrospect, I would have probably skipped playing the last level altogether.

Gameplay
It’s a shooter 2D platformer game. You can basically move, jump and shoot. There are some additional weapons but to be honest they are mostly there for show. I remember using them to actually do something useful probably a couple of times. Like most of the things in the game, they are references to things from the AVGN show with no real purpose and correlation to the mechanics.
And that brings me to the biggest problem, which is at the core of the gameplay. It doesn’t focus on good mechanics and build the difficulty around them, but rather uses mechanics and level gimmicks from other games and create cheap deaths around them.

I want my beers back
Using a finite amount of beers as lives is another really bad idea design-wise. It has been an obsolete system for almost 15 years and for a good reason. The only thing it does is that it takes away the opportunity to try as many times as it takes. There are limitless lives in Easy mode, but I feel that you shouldn’t be punished for playing on Normal difficulty - usually how the developers intend you to play the game. Then again, when beating a level in Normal difficulty, it is still saved and you don’t have to play it again even after losing your 30 lives.

Of course, when you start a new level you’d prefer to start it with all the lives possible. The levels are hard and some of them are a lot harder than others. So I knew that having 10 lives when finishing the previous level, is most likely not enough and didn’t want to make it two-thirds into the level before starting all over again. Many times I had to die 10 - 15 times on purpose, before playing a level for real, because I wanted to reset my lives count to 30. So, the final result is to create this stupid logistics problem for absolutely no reason. There is no strategy here, just disrespect for your players’ time.

Presentation
The graphics and music are awesome and this is one of the main reasons why I kept playing the game. The nerd’s lines and interactions are also pretty funny.

Summary
Overall, it’s not a good game by any stretch of the word good. If you want to try something challenging, there are many games out there that have perfected the formula. Don’t waste your time with this one.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSurprise gift from my mother and wife plotting to ever distract me.

Very good game. This is the second game from the studio/individual who gave us the Manos: the Hands of Fate game and you really see the similarities between those two games as they share some stuff graphically, and he also brought over/recycled a boss from the Manos game. The game, and I assume Manos also, was made in Game Maker Studio so this is a fantastic advertisement for it because, well, it's a good game.

First of all, this game is so difficult brutal doesn't do it justice. This is 100% intentional as the difficulty is over the top and to the point where it's hilarious. In fact, there are so many points where the game will kill you in a totally unfair and unavoidable way where the only way to know that something means instant death is because you died.

There are of course countless jokes and references to the AVGN web series. So many of these are indirect, such as enemies or the above mentioned death traps I mentioned above.

This version is good, but I would recommend getting the AVGN 1 and 2 pack as it contains both games redone in Unity, but also a bonus level if you finish both games.

This version however is very good.\t\t\t\t\t
