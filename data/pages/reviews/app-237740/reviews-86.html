\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a huge fan of AVGN and have watch every single one of his videos several times. To see a game based on his outrages is such a brilliant idea. This game really takes everything that he hates about bad games, or they take some of his comments and make them an actual idea. (Especially the "Super Pitfall" reference was brilliant.)

If you've watched his videos you'll easily catch the references through out the game and the appearences of popular characters. Some of them are so awesome it brought a huge smile to my face (like the fireshark) and it's also nice being able to beat up the boss on the Atari stage. They are in no way overpowering a whole stage, just a small reference placed perfect in some parts of the game.

I liked that the stages had their own themes as well as that they tried to include his famous quotes in the game as a reference. I wouldn't have mind seeing more bats in the horror stage, as it always seems the old games have way too many bats than needed. I think my favorite stage was the Winter stage, just because of what happens after you defeat the first boss. Just brilliant and easily my favorite part of the game. Anytime you die, you get a randomly generated outrage from the nerd which is fun when there's a great mix up.

The graphics are well done and the music is very good. The controls for the most part feels very solid. I'm glad you can adjust the keyboard to your own liking.

So why don't I recommend this game?

Well, unfortunately whoever designed all these stages obviously ran out of ideas after taking the nerds outrages that don't fill a complete stage. So half the stages consist of instant death blocks. More than half of the deaths within the game will happen due to these blocks that are everywhere. Placed in hard-to-reach areas, walls of death blocks that changes between invisibility and jumping over them with a sketchy trampoline techinques. Did I mention they are literally everywhere in this game?

What would have been a brilliant platformer with great humor and references, ends up being a practice jump game to test your accuracy whether or not you're able to avoid these deathblocks or not. I guess the idea is making the game so utterly frustrating you will swear and scream like the Nerd, but even the games he played seem more fun than going through a stage of deathblocks. A lot of the games he did play featured some areas with frustrating jumps, but not to the extend that this game uses them.

If it wasn't for these tedious deathblocks, the game would honestly be very short. There isn't much variety to the stages other than being lucky enough to avoid deathblocks and occassionally shooting some enemies. It's like switching from one deathblock avoiding game to another. Just add some new sprites and backgrounds and voila, you are ready to make another test of frustrating accuracy. It feels quite sad that such a fantastic idea just ends up as a bland jumping game.

I can enjoy a game despite them being difficult. This isn't one of them though. Anytime I completed a stage I didn't get that rewarding feeling. In other difficult games, you complete the difficult part, then move onto another different, yet difficult task. It really was like playing the same stages over and over, with the deathblocks making up too much of my playtime than it was enjoyable. I enjoyed the parts of the game where these weren't present and quite frankly, I wouldn't have minded them being a bit here and there. It just feels that they are all over the game as a way to extend a very short game into something longer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a person who was watched the AVGN's how since the beginning, it amazes me to see how many (obscure) references have been put into this game. A 2D sidescroller that harkens back to the old SNES days, The Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures is a smorgasboard of James Rolfe's ongoing Youtube sensation and celebrates every second of it.

Graphically the game has an inspired look. Meaning that pretty much everything in the game is an homage to something from the show, and the music is probably better than it deserves. While the presentation is top-notch, the gameplay is where the game falters. The game has the tendency to mock the faults of other games: not being able to see oncoming enemies beyond the screen, checkpoints that are placed at sadistic spots and instakills are only the tip of the iceberg. Alot of trial and error is in place here when it shouldn't. If you want to point out the mistakes other games make by being unfair to the player, don't make the same mistakes aswell.

Frustration aside, I still think this is a game you should at least give a try. The presentation is great, the soundtrack is unbelievable and it makes for some fun moments. You could do a lot worse.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeing a huge AVGN fan I knew I had to own this game, having watched the show since at least 2008.
I first started playing it with a keyboard and it was quickly seeming utterly impossible to make it through the first stage I tried. After switching to controller the impossible became much more possible and suddenly I could easily fine-tune my jumps instantly making for a far more enjoyable experience. So I sincerely advise using one with this game.

Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures is a side-scrolling action game which offers you that pure sense of accomplishment and rewarding feeling harkening back to the simpler side-scrolling days of old, complete with punishing, somtimes unfair, hand-sweating level design. The controls are tight and responsive and won't be working against you in any way. The challenge will seem daunting at times but with patience, observation, familiarity and quick reaction you can make it through. You will fail MANY MANY times but each one is a step forward!

There's many references to the show and James Rolfe's content on Cinnamassacre in general.
The music is also kick-ass, retro, catchy bit-chip! You feel like quite the bad-ass as you play!

I recommend this game if you're looking for a hardy, worthy retro challenge!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAngry Video Game Nerd Adventures is a love letter to the 8 bit NES era as well as the era that coined and defined the term NES hard! The game puts you in the role of the easily angered, foul mouthed, ill tempered Nerd as he travels through 8 levels of "Game Land" inspired by "Video Game Land" from the late 80s cartoon "Captain N: The Game Master" while the gameplay is inspired by classic retro games like Castlevania, Contra, Mega Man, Ninja Gaiden and others.

Each level has various nods to games from the past as well as games that The Nerd has reviewed on his web series. For example, the level "Beat it And Eat It" is inspired by the Atari Porn games for the Atari 2600, and the level "Boo! Haunted House" inspired by the "A Nightmare On Elm Street" and "Friday The 13th" NES games!

The most satisfying thing about this game is the challenge! Yes, areas of certain levels can be difficult and will even get you to curse like The Nerd himself, but that is what makes this game fun! The process of trial and error while playing through the game and being rewarded for beating the level instead of having you're hand held with a useless guide that shows you how to beat the level or give you recharging health or anything else that current games do nowadays. The game is still very fun as well as rewarding to play and gives you just that right amount of challenge that makes you want to keep on playing til the end!

If you are a fan of classic "NES hard" games like Castlevania, Contra, Mega Man, or Ninja Gaiden or are a fan of the AVGN character and his web series, then you should definitely get this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRemember the days when you use to sit in front of a 20" CRT TV playing side scrollers for hours? You do? Well then, this game is for you!

Don't think for a second that this is another rip-off of Super Mario Bros, because it's not. Be ready to get your ass kicked while laughing about getting your ass kicked. Your left thumb may even feel that old but familiar feeling of rubbing the d-pad too much. Right Pointer Finger will take his turn shooting/jumping while his best friend Mr. Thumb is having a well-deserved break. All the stuff you remember and love from retro gaming is here!

I'd hate to see how much time I wasted as a kid playing this kind of game; Steam game tracking will certainly give me a good idea in the near future. The fond memories this game pulls from the back of my mind is what makes this game so special. I have not played a game like this in years!!!

Do yourself a favor... Take a break from the HD games and play this!

Two thumbs up!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great NES-style side-scrolling game even if you've never played an NES side-scroller (which I haven't). It also helps a lot if you're an AVGN fan, as there are a lot of comical references scattered throughout the game.

The game is ball-bustingly hard and cuss-inducingly irritating, but in a way that makes you want to keep playing it over and over again rather than making you want to stand up and throw your computer across the room (but you might do that too). Plus, there's also the comic relief you'll get from one of many of AVGN's trademark "I'd rather" lines, which you see every time you die.

Oh, and the music's awesome, too.\t\t\t\t\t
