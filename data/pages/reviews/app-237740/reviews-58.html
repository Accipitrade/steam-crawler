\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fan of the Nerd (granted much later than most, but I still watched all the episodes) I bought this game when it came out. The game is hard, and usually still doable with some rage to it.

However, I can't recommend this game to most.

The difficulty is actually the reason I gave up playing this game, as I never even beat it on Normal. All the bosses fly in a figure 8 pattern and many add projectiles into the mix for added dodging. I never did beat the final boss, even after 300 lives dedicated to him and his final level (After a while, I could usually reach the boss after burning half my lives on the level - the final boss probably killed about 120 of those lives).

The power ups are rare, so rare that I almost never bothered using them when I found one. The 'balls' powerup is terrible (as it is supposed to be), the glitch gremlin can freeze things in or out of your way (really depends on timing), and Mecha-Death Christ is usually out of the way (dangerously), and provides a screen nuke for enemies that I can gun down without assistance. The Super Scope is a nice boost of power, but goes away after just one hit from any hazard... you won't hold onto it for long.

Dont' get me started on the number of insta-death DEATH BLOCKS this game throws at you. You can't even try to count.

It's not the hardest game out there, but only the truly dedicated will beat this game. There are also way harder and more rewarding games to dedicate that time to. 5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor fans of the Nerd, definitely. The references alone will make it worth it.
Now, for those who are more interested in the gameplay than in the scatological humor, I think the platforming is pretty solid. It is ludicrously difficult, but it's not as cheap as you may expect from a game with a premise like this. Sure, there are some parts where you can't help but get hit (one of the witches in the last section of Beat'em and Eat'em springs to mind), but for the most part it's all about timing and pattern recognition. Some people might complain about the instant death blocks, but I didn't mind them.
The graphics are pure 16-bit goodness, but the real highlight here is no doubt the music. The theme songs for Thy Farts Be Consumed and Laughing Joking Numbnuts are just amazing.
My biggest complaint are perhaps the controls. The characters could use a bit more traction, and there are times when they don't turn around when you tell them to (this is mostly noticeable in boss battles). That being said, this doesn't completely ruin your experience like in other games.

So in short: yes, I would recommend this game to those looking for a challenge, though it's best enjoyed if you're familiar with the series.\t\t\t\t\t
