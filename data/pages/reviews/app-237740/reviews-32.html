\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHere's what it boils down to: if you enjoy the challenge and frustration/reward ratio of classic NES titles, you will probably enjoy this game. If you don't, you absolutely, positively will not enjoy this game. And if you've never played an NES game, or at the very least never played one of the first three Mega Man games... well, "I" can't "recommend" piracy, but you should probably try playing some of that.

The reason for this is that this game borrows a lot from the original Mega Man games. And Metroid and Castlevania and there's a little Ninja Gaiden in there and... well... look; basically this game is a pastiche of the best of the NES. And I've definitely seen criticism of AVGNA because it's 'using elements from games the Nerd reviewed' or something. Let me be frank. At least I can *beat* this game. Out of the 30+ NES games I own, I've completed less than a third. This game is a CAKEWALK comparatively speaking. You want impossibly difficult? Go sit down with Monster Party for a couple hours. I guarantee you'll be looking at this game differently.

This isn't to say that the game is easy. A term I hear thrown around a lot among nostalgic and retro gamers is "NES hard". Which is to say that games from that time period, as I've said before, tended to be highly aggravating in difficulty. However, there's just no feeling of satisfaction quite like finally managing to beat an area of a classic game you've been struggling with for hours. It's honestly a bit difficult to explain what makes it different from most modern games, but I think it has to do with how cinematic current games are. Even if you struggle with a section for hours, you've primarily done it to progress a story. 'NES hard' means it's just you and the game. If there's a story, it's bare bones at best. You struggle over every obstacle just to be able to say you did. Current gaming is generally like watching a movie. Retro gaming, at least in the 80's, was more like climbing a mountain- there's nothing up there and there's no logical reason to do it, but ♥♥♥♥ it- YOU DID IT.

Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures is one of the only modern games I've played that has managed to capture that feeling, albeit to a somewhat lesser degree. Like I said, the game isnt' as hard as some classic titles. And as much as I like James's work, the presence of all the reference jokes can be somewhat distracting. The soundtrack is pretty solid. I don't think it's something I'd listen to in my free time, but it works pretty well and it sounds nice.

Basically, if you're waffling, refer to my first paragraph. But for me, I recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fun 2D side scroller, but I'd likely suggest picking it up only if you're a fan of the AVGN series or a fan of really hard 2D side scrollers. There are a total of nine levels and you can play through the first eight in any order you like; completing those will unlock the final ninth level. With multiple checkpoints throughout each level, there's a decent amount of challenge to be had in getting through the game.

One thing to note here is that if you play the game on any difficulty above easy, you're going to be subject to a life system. So if you're playing through a level and run out of lives, you have to start back at the very beginning of that level, regardless of the latest checkpoint you got. This almost made me give up on the game, as the very last stage got to be a bit too challenging for me on normal difficulty with a finite amount of lives to work with. If you want to change the difficulty, you pretty much have to start over and complete each level again.

I ended up beating the first eight levels on normal difficulty, which I thought was a fair challenge, but like I said, the final level was hard enough to make me drop down to easy. Unfortunately, that meant replaying through the previous eight levels again, although they were much easier to maneuver through on a second run. The final level was still a challenge, but well worth getting to the end.

At the end of the day, if you're familiar with AVGN and the various characters that have appeared in the game, there's plenty of references and funny material to get you through to the end, as well as some really great music and the option to purchase the soundtrack separately. I do think some of the frustrations in a few areas of the game take the joke of this being a hard game a bit too far, but then again, that's kind of the point - this is supposed to be a very challenging side scroller reminiscent of past generations of video games.
