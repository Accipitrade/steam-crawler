\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAVGN Adventures is a game I have been waiting for a long time. I have heard mixed reviews on the game but I would like to interject my ideas and likes about this game. As well as my dislikes.

First of all, the game was designed to be a challenge, a hard game, designed from the AVGN's experiences with the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ games he had played on various consoles. As one would think, this game is more geared to those who are fans of the AVGN, to those who arent, they wont do so well with this game and will get angry fast.

I'm not saying just non-fans would get mad, I did too at times but I found it funny. I felt the Nerds pain with ♥♥♥♥♥♥ games and this was it. The game is unfair, very hard, challenging, and unforgiving on some accounts and that is why I like the game.

The inside jokes are awesome, if you are a fan of the AVGN like me, you would see all the jokes and laugh at them. Even the final level is a whole joke. Though to be fair that level is hard as balls.

I dont recommend this game to those who arent fans of the AVGN, and I would recommend it to AVGN fans but if you are fan, you most likely bought it already.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is among my favorite Indie games of all time, but it's also one of the hardest, and it's weird, but hey, I love weird games! Now, it's starring AVGN, who is one of my favorite reviewers of all time. For those of you who aren't familiar with him, he's a nerd who plays sh***y games and curses. He may not sound like much, but he's hilarious, and so is his game. The story is about the Nerd, the Guitar Guy Who Lives Under the Couch, the Bulls**it Man, and Mike Matei playing a game together, when suddenly it sucks them in, and grabs the Nerd by the crotch......did I mention this game is weird? The levels are based on AVGN episodes, and boy, they are really, REALLY difficult. It's difficult in an old-school style NES type of way, which is great. I also like it how the Nerd in the game says the lines he would say if he played that game, and wouldn't ya know, there's an episode where he plays this. There are a few Easter Eggs here and there, for example, if you stand still for a moment, he'll give you the middle finger. Now, 30 lives on Normal Mode may seem like a cakewalk, but far from it. You're going to find yourself on countless Game Over screens with dirty yet hilarious lines that the Nerd says. Overall, I'll sum up this game the same way one of the guys on E3 who played it did: "It's Fard; A combination of fun and hard". I love this game, but unless you know how hard old-school games are, do yourself a favor and don't play it.\t\t\t\t\t
