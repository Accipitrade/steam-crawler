\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt sucks. Fandom aside, I like The Nerd, but let's pretend that has nothing to do with this and just review this game for it being a game.

Pros
*Colorful
*Good Music
*Good Control
*Very funny

Cons
*Uncreative enemies The same enmies are just reskined for every level.
*Artifically extended levels. You can't just go forward, game makes you zigzag up and down stages.
*VERY SLOW. A speed runners nightmare. The constant hazards, instant deaths, and cheap tricks means you must constantly stop every few feet to watch your step.
*Very cheap death traps. Instant kills everywhere. Pretty much every cheap difficulty trick they used in early gaming history roled into one. No skill can perpare you, just sheer experience and memorization.

Overall, don't pat yourself on the back for finishing this game. Don't give it thumbs up for "being good and hard". It's hard in all the wrong ways. It's not Demon Souls hard. It's not hard in that way that is both cheap but skill based. Yes, there is a degree of skill required, but most of this game is just trial and error along with memorization. Due to the how cheap the trap placement is you have to play it multiple times just to figure what kills, when, and where. Your skill won't save you untill you die a few dozen cheap deaths.

Look, I get it. It's oure favorite Nerd and that Nerd humor is in here. Everyone is also, "It's just like the classic NES games" YES! It is and classic NES games SUCKED! They were frustrating, badly designed, and cheap as hell. THAT'S THE WHOLE POINT OF AVGN's show! There's a reason why we don't really see games like this anymore, because at it's core, it's a bad game. It's not unplayable. It's not impossible. Hell, its not even unfun sometimes, but it's still at it's core bad game design. I know, I know, "They wanted to make a bad game". Yeah, but just because a Cook burns your food on purpose doesn't mean you can't call the food bad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat retro-style game based on the Angry Video Game Nerd webseries. Fans of the hit internet show will get exactly what they want from this.

Over the years, the AVGN has provided a lot of laughs and now you get to play as the angriest game you have ever heard as you blast your way through a variety of levels. The game is rather challenging (even on Easy) as you are tasked with various enemies and obstacles, designed to test your gaming skills. All of the levels take inspiration from games reviewed on the show as well as the AVGN's comments about them. Each time you die, you get to see a quote from the AVGN.

People that are not familiar with the AVGN might not get the humour and they are unlikely to get the references but the gameplay is good enough to still make it worthwhile for people that like tough old-school type games. For fans of the AVGN, this is a must play.

Gameplay: 7.5/10
Graphics: 8/10 (for the retro style)
Controls: 8.5/10
Replay Value: 6.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

He's gonna take you back to the past...

In the early 2000s I discovered a new show on Youtube that rekindled my love for retro video games. The first time I saw the Angry Video Game Nerd, I immediately went to my attic and found my old gaming consoles. To this day, I am still keeping them around on stand-by (far away from the attic) just in case I feel like playing some old gaming goodness.

The Angry Video Game Nerd’s show mostly consists of the Nerd playing badly designed Nintendo games from the past, cursing at them and destroying them in a humorous way. It was a great show back in the day and it’s still going strong. Obviously, I am still a huge fan of the show.

A game starring The Nerd? Sign me up!

In 2013 an AVGN game was announced and almost instantly I bought it. It was advertised as a 2D 8 bit-like platformer starring the Nerd where he visits many different worlds reminiscent of his episodes and fights monsters and bosses like he usually does in his show. It sounded really good and many fans, such as myself, got excited and jumped on the hype train.

Fast forward to 2018 and only today did I finally force myself to beat this game for the first time ever.

Hard but fair? No!

I still remember when Stuttering Craig from Screwattack (Nerd’s publishers at the time) was doing a first ever preview of the game. He constantly kept saying how the game was hard but fair and how every death is the players own fault. The two most common descriptions were: NES hard and fair.

Only the former is correct in my opinion. NES hard, basically means that a game is made harder to make up for its length, which is absolutely true, since it only took me about an hour and a half to actually beat the whole game. “FAIR” is something that this game isn’t.

I don't mind hard games. I mind frustrating games

Please try to understand, I have no problem with hard game. Heck, some of my favorite games are really hard. Super Meat Boy https://store.steampowered.com/app/40800/Super_Meat_Boy/, Dark Souls https://store.steampowered.com/app/570940/DARK_SOULS_REMASTERED/, Castlevania https://store.steampowered.com/app/1018010/Castlevania_Anniversary_Collection/, Ninja Gaiden… many of these games are very hard where dying is very common. In these games, when you die, you are angry, sure, but you also know what to do next time in order to avoid death.
This is not the case of the AVGN adventures.

Good controls and amazing music

The game is, like it was advertised, a 2D platformer. The AVGN gets sucked into a bad video game and has to clear 9 worlds in order to escape. He can jump, he can shoot his NES zapper in many directions, he can find power ups and even unlock other characters.

The music in the game is amazing. It’s easily the best part of the game. If anything, it was the music that kept me going until the very end. The graphics are also appealing enough. They are clearly made to resemble 8-bit NES games that are AVGN’s favorite things to review.

Death blocks! Death blocks everywhere!

The one problem this game has that pretty much kills all fun out of it are its levels, or rather, the level design. Instead of having creative platform levels with difficult but passable obstacles, this game seems like it just throws everything at you and expects you to avoid it all. Unlike the NES games, that the nerd likes so much, this game can fit a lot of enemies on the screen at once and the game won’t slow down. Hordes and hordes of enemies will attack you in the most fiendish ways possible, trying to kill you and prevent you from reaching your goal. Luckily, the nerd has a couple of beer bottles as his health and he can even replenish it by finding more beer. His zapper is also a good weapon to deal with most of the enemies and even if that’s not enough, there are other characters with other weapons who can usually take care of everything the nerd can’t.

Unfortunately, the enemies and the bosses are not the main obstacle in this game. The biggest obstacles in this game are the little blocks with skulls on them that kill the nerd in one hit if he touches them. It wouldn’t be so bad if only a few sections of the game had them, but these blocks are everywhere. They are the most overused asset in this entire game. No matter how well you may be playing this game, killing, dodging enemies… one death block can kill you instantly, regardless of your health. Well at least these blocks are visible and are easy to spot and dodge right? No, not even close. Sometimes the blocks will fly at you. Other times they will block your path by appearing and disappearing and you will have to make through them. Then, they won’t even show themselves until you are right next to them. Let’s jump towards that platform there… oops, I just died because there was a death block that I didn’t know was there.

It ends up being a game of trial and error

What it boils down to in the end, is that the AVGN becomes a game of trial and error. There are no skills evolved, it doesn’t matter how much you played platform games in the 80s and 90s. Until a death block kills you once (or more than once) you won’t know it’s there. The game pretty much becomes a Cat Mario game. It really ruins your enjoyment of the game.

First I thought that maybe the game was made as a joke. Just a project to display everything the Nerd hates about old NES video games and put them in one game for a frustrating experience, but no, this game was clearly marketed as an actual video game with one of my favorite video game reviewer as the protagonist. Yes, when you look at the game as a whole, it looks like a mix of all the best AVGN jokes and tropes packed in a cute little game, but wouldn’t it be better if this game was actually good.

I would insult this game, but the game does it for me, as every time you die, the game displays a randomly generated AVGN curse.

I love AVGN but I don't love this game

This is not a good game. Not as a 2D platformer, not even as a Cat Mario / Dying simulator. If you want a good 2D platformer, there are plenty of other better platformers on Steam. If you want a good dying simulator, guess what? There are even better dying simulators than this one. Either way, as much as I love the AVGN and as much as I want to believe that a game about the AVGN is a good game, that’s just not true.

If you like the AVGN like I do, just go and watch his show.
