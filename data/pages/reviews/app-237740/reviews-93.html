Instant death-traps, tricky jumps, and trial-with-lots-of-error gameplay abound, and anyone looking for a quick fix might end up dissatisfied.

If you look closer, though, the game is quite self-aware, and isn't nearly as bad as it appears. Players start out with an insane amount of lives. There are unlimited continues. Stages that get beat stay beat. Players can replay beaten stages to find new secrets. A few runs through a level often yields a surprising amount of improvement. As the makers professed, it is tough, but fair.

For anyone looking to feel like the Nerd for a few hours, this game is a more than adequate tonic. Just expect the game to fight back.\t\t\t\t\t
