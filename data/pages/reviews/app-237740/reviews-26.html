\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAVGN Adventures is a very good game that brings back a lot of what made the old 8-bit classics so great. The gameplay mixes elements from various different platformers, with Mega Man perhaps the most notably source of inspiration. The game also looks great, with a cool aesthetic that emulates many different old-school games. Finally, the soundtrack is exceptional, featuring numerous callbacks to series like Castlevania and Metroid.

However, the game isn't perfect. The gameplay does take a while to get used to, as the characters' hitboxes are a bit larger than one might expect, and the platforming can feel a bit slippery at times. In addition, I think it was a mistake to include the life system in the game. Even though on normal you get 30 lives to complete a level, it's often not enough to get through it, which means you have to go through the entire level again. It may not have been such a huge issue if the game didn't have so many instant death blocks and traps, but some levels just go way overboard with them.

This wouldn't be an issue if the game over state wouldn't send you back to beginning of the level. I know that the easy mode has infinite lives, but I wish the game had a mix of these difficulty levels. The one comparison I could think of is if Super Meat Boy had a life system that would send you back to the beginning of the world if you lost them all. That would completely ruin the game.

The life system in AVGN Adventures makes the game needlessly frustrating and takes away a lot of the fun that it otherwise gives you. I ended up completing the game on easy as a result, which in turn made many bosses too easy. I wouldn't mind the countless retries in the event of death I would have to do on harder difficulties if I wouldn't have to worry about a possible game over during a boss fight forcing me to do the entire level all over again. Regardless, this is still a very good, and fun platformer where the difficulty is part of the enjoyment, it just has some issues that I wish weren't there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an 8-Bit platformer with shooting elements (like Mega Man). This game is, obviously, about the Angry Video Game Nerd and pretty much everyone on the internet knows him (unless you're living under a rock). This game has a total of 9 stages with their own music, level designs and boss battles.

Even thought the level design can sometimes be different, it feels like it's the same. There is no real variety to it. One level design that I can think of is the stage called "Thyfarts Consumed". The level design was in my opinion abysmal. There is one segment where you have to wait and go, wait, go, wait, go and so on. This can sometimes be extremely tedious.

The music in this game is surprisingly good. I especially like the music from "Happy Fun Candy Time". If you want to hear it for yourself than search for it on YouTube.

Of course the game is full of references (Mainly about AVGN). Each stage has a lot of references of older episodes and characters. A fan of AVGN will have a good time with it.

This game is also well-known for being difficult but "fair". I put the word fair in quotation marks because the game is not really that fair. This game has a lot of instant death blocks. These blocks are in every level and it feels like a cheap and lazy way to make the game hard which is a negative point for me.

There are also multiple characters that you can play like Mike, the B.S Man and the Couch Guy. Each character has their own strength and weakness.

Overall I would say that this game is "okay". It has some serious flaws but you can still enjoy it as a fan of platform gamesand as an AVGN fan. I would only recommend this game for the die-hard AVGN fans and also for people who like challenges.\t\t\t\t\t
