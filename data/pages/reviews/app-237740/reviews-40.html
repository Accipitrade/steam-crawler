\r\n\t\t\t\t\tREAD ENTIRE REVIEW BEFORE DECIDING UPON IT!

"He's gonna take you back to past..."

AVGN Adventures is a 2D sidescroller platformer inspired by alot of retro classics...and alot of retro disasters, but we'll talk about that later. Anyway, you should know who the Angry Video Game Nerd is. He single handedly started everything to do with gaming videos online. He's a foulmouth nerd who has dedicated his life to warning the world of bad games, whilst being absolutley hilarious at the same time. If you have never watched him, what are you waiting for> Go watch an episode right now 'cause you're really missing out.

Anyway, the game starts the Nerd being sucked into one of these terrible games and must get ou. Simple, right?
Ladies and Gentlemen, I now present to you my least favrioute thing about this game...

THE DIFFICULTY IS OFF THE CHARTS!!!

I never usually loose my temper with games. However this one really started bugging me within 5 mins of boot up. Not trying to spoil anything, but I was in sweat and tears during the final boss fight. I tried for an hour and couldn't do it. I managed to develop a stratergy for part of the fight, but died something like 400 times. I gave up all red and in tears. The next night I went back and my strategy still worked, but I was still struggling. So I used a walkthrough, combined it with my stratergy and I finnaly did it!

Anyway, the point is this is easily the hardest game I've ever played. Even on easy. I'm impressed just beating it once on easy only having to use a walkthrough once!

But, the real problem here...

If you watched AVGN for as long as I have, you probably will have memorised some of the things he complains about in games. And, without listing them, I can tell you this game has ALOT of these problems. Its like it took inspiration from bad games!

WHAT WERE THEY THINKING?!

(Sigh...)I usually decide whether I'll reccomend or not reccomend a game beofre reviewing, but this time I'm really struggling. This game almost ruined AVGN for me. I went through sweat and tears just to beat this game on the easiest difficulty! However, it has gogeous retre graphics, occasinally fun gameplay and, of course, the Nerd. Due to it taking too much inspiration from bad examples of games and not being entertaining 100% of the time, it is with great sorrow and regret that AVGN Adventures is the first game I dont reccomend on Steam. Still buy it, play it but not till the end. It is the most brutal and unforgiving game I've ever played.

Did I mention the feeling when you finnaly beat it...?

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game after watching a short lets play on Youtube. It seemed like a fun game based on the visuals and concept. Being a fan of the AVGN series I wanted to support that as well.
But boy do I regret spending those buck.

The controls are so stiff that jumping on a platform fails in most cases. It feels like they were intentionally made stiff to subtract from the ability to steer the nerd. The truth I however fear is that the guys who made this game are inexperienced game devs or simply have no skill in their trade whatsoever OR were so busy thinking of foul words that they didn't have time to test if the game was actually playable.
The good humor from the series is more or less absent in place of just swearing endlessly.
In regard to the indie AVGN fan games I've played (watch episode 115 if unsure of which I'm referring) this doens't present much original content.

The level design is really bad as well. I haven't played for that long because I more or less ragequit due to all these issues I'm adressing here. But I did play all stages. And they're the same: Unfair when they should instead be challenging.
I've played and beaten tons of platform games. Especially old and hard ones. But this simply isn't fun.

I wish there were more positive things to say about this. Unpolished, frustrating, insulting and unworthy of my time.

To sum it up: FreakZone Games did an awful job with this game, and why ScrewAttack would have them make a sequel is beyond my comprehension.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI came for the nerd, I stayed for the gameplay.

I've been a massive fan of AVGN for years, seen every episode multiple times etc etc etc. That was the main reason I bought the game, however after playing it for several hours, I almost forgot the connection between AVGN and the game, I was really enjoying the game as a game.

2d platformers are hard to do, it's such a simplistic style, which in my opinion makes it even harder to do right. This game does it right, a lot of fun, injected with good humour and very very challenging. But the nerd should be proud of his own game, this game may be challenging, but it's challenging for the right reasons, it's well made and the challenge lies in the difficulty of the game, not flaws in the mechanics.

It is however intensely frustrating at times, my housemate who lives on the third floor was texting me asking me why I was screaming (I was all the way downstairs). You can end up losing 30 lives by dying in the same place over and over again, but the satisfaction of when you finally get that bit right is fantastic.

I've played about 9 hours and i'm currently struggling on the final boss on normal mode, but once i'm finished i'm sure I will start it all over again, I think the replayability is reasonably high.

The only 'flaw' (if you consider it a flaw) is the fact that the game is a lot of trial and error, especially on the more challenging levels. Even if you are the greatest 2d platform player in the world, you will end up having to learn a thing or two about the upcoming sections before you can complete them. But for me this is fine and it didn't stop me having fun. Plus you feel like a total badass once you can do a previously 'impossible' section with zero deaths.

All in all would give this game a solid 7 out of 10. Fans and non-fans alike can enjoy this simple, enjoyable and difficult game.\t\t\t\t\t
