ANGRY VIDEO GAME NERD ADVENTURES

So, a game of the AVGN at last! I could say that I'm a fan of his work and videos which are ridiculously funny! To see a game made and based on his numerous rage quits is really a great idea! To put it bluntly, this game is like a nostalgic return to the past, playing some truly HARD NES games! There are a few references to his videos and the appearances of popular characters.

The stages have their own themes so you old school gamers, you're in known grounds! Anytime you die, you get a randomly generated outrage from AVGN which is fun when there's a great mix up.

The graphics are as you would expect from a generic platform game. The music is very well done! The controls are for the most part feels very solid although, when I played the game with the XBOX 360 gamepad, I had a hard time being precise with it.


POSITIVES:
+Ridiculously funny!
+Gameplay is as you would expect from an old school platformer.
+Music is well done and catchy!
+Keyboard controls are solid.


NEGATIVES:
-Incredibly and blasphemously hard! There is way too many death blocks everywhere. Prepare to retry many times and don't forget to reach that checkpoint!
-Level design is as bad as the games reviewed by the Nerd himself. (Death blocks, hard-to-reach items and areas, frustrating precise jumps).


OVERALL:
If it wasn't for these tedious deathblocks, the game would be short as there isn't much variety to the stages other than being lucky enough to avoid deathblocks and occasionally shooting some enemies. They might be the main reason to "make the game" feel longer but the overuses of the deathblocks was and still is very annoying to me.


RECOMMENDATION:
While I haven't finished the whole game yet, I would still recommend this game to old school platformer purists only. If you love AVGN, it is a big bonus and you gotta be prepared to die very often and scream like the Nerd himself!\t\t\t\t\t

The nerd's profanity and crude sense of humor are both included along with the charms of our old nintendo games. Many familiar NES games and AVGN episodes are referenced. The challenge is definitely there, but gets impossibly hard to the point of unplayability. I would definitely reccomend this game, but use a controller. It makes the game a bit more bearable and to my experience is the only way to beat the final boss (The controller buttons aid, but the keyboard ends of failing). Perefect game for any fan of the Nerd, NES games, or a good challenge.\t\t\t\t\t
