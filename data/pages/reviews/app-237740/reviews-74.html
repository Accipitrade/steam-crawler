\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is terrible! It is full of cheap kills and vulgar language via this "Nerd" character who is nothing more than a cheap megaman knockoff who proceeds to whine about every single thing this stupid game has to offer. There are not very many levels and all of them are crafted with the level design equivalent of something an 11 year old boy drew on a piece of paper when he was supposed to be paying attention to math class. Tons of one-hit kill blocks muck up what could have at least been a mediocre yet fun little romp through the somewhat clever parody levels of other much better games. But that's it there are only 6 or 7 levels! That's HALF of one megaman game. Also this nerd is not your only character you play as. He has friends - go figure - a nerd with friends! Doesn't seem very nerdy if you A) have friends and B) have a cool gun that you fire at everything. That's what cool people have! In fact the only thing nerdy about this stereotype is that hes got glasses and whines!

The other characters all suck and do not help you out in any way and the collectible "♥♥♥♥ pickles" add nothing to the game. There is no reason to collect anything in this game unless you are really so bored that you'd want to play this atrocity again. Oh, and despite the pretty colors and simple gameplay, this game is NOT for kids. Heck I don't think its for adults either as the humor is an uncanny blend of juvenile and adult (see: "♥♥♥♥ Pickle"). This is a game that I can only reccomend for sick twisted minds of 20-something stoners and drunks who want something stupid to laugh at for about 30 minutes before getting tired of dying and need to go play GTA while they get high and blow up cars and run people over.

Depraved, dawdling and unfunny. I give AVGN Adventures a 9/10 and you should definitely watch the guy's videos and actually find them funny before trying this game otherwise you will believe every word in the review I just wrote.\t\t\t\t\t

If I say "I like the feel of this game", than why do I not recommend it? This can be answered with a simple two words: Death Blocks. They just ruin the game completely. A game should not have to challenge their players with instant death on contact with a block. And I know, without the blocks, this game would at most take two hours to complete. These death blocks are the reason why the game is ruined.
Honestly, I would only ever recommend this if you are truly a hardcore AVGN fan. General platformer fans will not be happy with this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6/10

I hesitate to “recommend” the game but I can’t let myself give it a bad review. If you enjoyed the platformers of old (NES specifically) you will find some familiar tropes and humor. I’ve never really watched anything this game is based on – I was more interested in the throwback style and references to old games. The game itself is fun but challenging to a fault – it’s nearly unplayable except with the unlimited lives option. The levels are designed to be a throwback to the challenging platformers of old but they go too far and create something that is borderline unplayable. I’ve actually beaten Battletoads and Ghost and Goblins on their respective systems and this game is harder than either of those combined.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe most frustrating platformer of the century but maybe also one of the most fun. I played it with a keyboard instead of a controler off which EVERYONE says you shouldn't do but I managed to beat the game up till the final boss any way so it wasn't that bad. Never beat him though, sadly.
The graphics are traditional, the animations and controls are smooth and the humor in the game can give you a chuckle or two, though the jokes might get old after a while.

It's hard, it's cheap and sometimes downright ♥♥♥♥♥ in your face with the difficulty (I played on normal) but there is both an easy and a hard mode for people who want more or less of a challenge. You have instant-death blocks and other cheap tricks that will make you scream just like the Nerd: "What were they thinking!?". But hey, that's a part of the entire conecpt of the game so it's hard to give it criticism for that.

The one completely perfect thing about this game? THE MUSIC.
Every stage has fantastic music that keeps you energetic and burned to keep playing, no matter how hard a certain part gets. but strangely, it's still not distracting at all as I never had the feeling I died because the soundtrack distracting me, so it seriously one of the best examples of video game music I can think off. If it were a sauce, I'd call it finger-licking good!

Intense all the way trough. Recommended for any one who enjoys side-scrollers and a tough challenge, even if you're not a fan of the AVGN!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis may be the hardest game I've ever played, and though it's fun, I have a hard time recomending it. Mechanically, it has a lot in common with classic NES Mega Man, but the dificulty curve is through the roof. Its designed to be nearly unbeatable, except through reptition and learning to beat a level the hard way, much like super meat boy. The art and music are great if you appriciate 8/16 bit era looks and sounds and there are lots of nods to both nerdy culture and classic games that any retro game fan is sure to love. Sadly, the game is too lude and crude for its own good... It doesnt' try to be witty and instead hopes that you'll find tastless nonsince amuseing, but I didn't. I'm sure the humor will appeal to someone, but not me, and it will likely appal the average person. If you love dificult, retro stylized games and can enjoy or ignore course and disguesting content, you'll love this game, especially considering its low price point! Sadly, for the average gamer looking for old school platformer thrills, i'd have to suggest you instead look to polished, longer, less offensive games like cave story+, super meat boy or rayman.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, let me start off by saying that this is a fun game. It's pretty much all I've been playing recently; the controls are nice, the levels have variety, and you get a great feeling of accomplishment when you get past hard parts of the game or beat a boss. However, there are a few things that have really annoyed me, and one of them was enough to make me stop playing the game entirely.

My biggest complaint is that there are no save points. There are checkpoints that you respawn at, and the game saves every time you beat a world, but if you quit in the middle of a level, you won't be able to go back to where you were. This really ♥♥♥♥ed me off, because I was fighting the final boss at the end of the hardest stage in the game (which took me 20 minutes to beat) when I had to leave. When I came back, I couldn't resume the fight. Frankly, there's not much of a chance of me going back to playing it anytime soon, simply because of this.

As for my smaller complaints, some of them may be by design, but that doesn't stop them from being annoying as hell. Whenever you get hit by an enemy, you lose all momentum. Normally this wouldn't be too bad, but the amount of enemies and hazards the game designers put before or in the middle of big jumps is just ridiculous at times.
The standard short invincibility period when you are damaged seems to be present in this game, but it doesn't protect you from most enviromental hazards. This one is really a tiny annoyance, and it becomes less bothersome as you get used to it, but I still feel that potential buyers should know.
As well as losing momentum when the enemies hit you, you sometimes move slightly in a random direction. There's one part of the game where this is especially annoying, where even the slightest hit from an enemy will send you a pixel to the left straight into a skull block.

Another larger complaint is that you take too long to respawn. In other extremely hard, death-comes-in-an-instant games (Hotline Miami, Super Meat Boy), you respawn instantly when you die. In AVGN Adventures, you have to wait at the game over screen so that the game can show you its novel at first, but eventually just annoying randomly generated nerd quotes. This can be very annoying in the parts of the game that are filled with the skull blocks that instakill you when you touch them.

Another warning to potential buyers: This game is mostly fanservice. If you're not into the Angry Video Game Nerd, then this won't be very fun for you. Unfortunately, even the fan service aspect of the game leaves something to be desired. It's filled with funny references, but other than that, not much of the dialogue and level design really reflects the show all that much.

Overall, it's a fun but extremely flawed game, and definitely worth it if you're an AVGN fan looking for a couple hours of (extremely frustrating) fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're into challenging platformers (e.g. Megaman, Castlevania and Mario), this is one to look out for. The graphics are pretty sweet and the music is awesome. The control is good and unique in its way, comparable to Mario, but slightly stiffer. Be warned for very frustrating level design. Fortunately, there are many many checkpoints on the way and you get 30 lives to start with. That should give you a good idea of how this game is designed and I like it a lot.

The reason for not recommending this game is because of technical issues. This game does not play well on a keyboard so I highly recommend using a controller, but besides the Xbox 360-controller, getting standard Windows controllers up and running is way too hard to do and required the use of third-party software, plus there's a bug that introduces 8 seconds load time after every death when using the controller, which is horendous. These are issues that were mentioned a lot in the forums, so I am not the only one. As such, even though the game is fun and creative, I cannot recommend a game that is technically underdeveloped. I have tested version 1.7.1 from february 2014.

Other than that, this game begs to be played on a tv with a controller in your hand, rather than on a PC. I would like to encourage the developers to make it run natively under SteamOS. This is one of those games that would benefit from an open console platform.\t\t\t\t\t
