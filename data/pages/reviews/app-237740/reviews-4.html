\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a long time fan of the AVGN since he first started I managed to beat this reasonably difficult game after 2 hours. The AVGN game looks great in the trailer, but this game is a pretty basic by-the-numbers platformer. Sometimes however it can slip into lazy flash game territory(Looking at you Satan boss.) Overall I'd say this game is a fairly good time, but beware if you don't know or care who the Angry Video Game Nerd is you could be left frustrated and underwhelmed. There's tons of fun in game references to episodes of the AVGN, and lots of cameos. While the game is mostly fun and reasonably challenging there is a decent amount of hard to ignore issues that can hamper your enjoyment. The main game is very short. Enemies have extremely basic patterns. The one weapon upgrade vanishes when you get hit which happens almost instantly since it's always placed in a place where you will just lose it right away so you never get to enjoy using it. Some boss fights aren't always interesting or enjoyable. The final level has a huge difficulty spike that could take you longer than all previous 8 levels to beat. There are a lot of pluses however: The art and sound effects are solid and nostalgic. The level themes are very fun, and utilize the themes well. The music isn't really memorable, but it's not offensive either. You'll either love or hate the death screen(which I wish you could turn off personally once you've seen it a few times.) The final boss fight is pretty solid which is good since not every boss fight is interesting.

Overall I'd say it's a 6/10 or 7/10 depending on how much of a fan you are of these kinds of games and how big of a fan you are of the AVGN. I had a good time, but I kind of wish there was more. I feel if you pick it up for $5 to $10 you should feel you got some bang for your buck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt feels like yesterday when I watched the 'AVGN Games' episode that endorsed this game but really, it was 8 years ago. I didn't grow up with the games reviewed in the AVGN series but I did grow up with the show. Playing it for the first time now, I think it holds up as an excellent platformer and a soaring homage to the series and the games covered in it.

It's a game that feels like it was made today: the art style is great, the music is banging and the controls feel precise. As a run and gun platformer, it nails the Megaman formula while adding in the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ that the Nerd complains about each episode like lasers that kill you in one hit, death blocks, floating heads among others. It's pretty funny and adds that signature AVGN charm. Yet the game never feels too overwhelming: playing on Normal, there are checkpoints everywhere and with a large pool of 30 lives, you have lots of time to learn whilst still adding in stakes. Like most good platformers, this is tough but fair.

As a Megaman type game, each level features around 2-3 minutes of tough platforming before finishing off with a boss fight. Each level is chockfull of references to the series and retro gaming. For example, the 'Beat Eat it and Eat it' stage mocks the unregulated Atari Porn games (which were reviewed in a now-deleted episode on the series) before climaxing to a crude parody of 'Custer's Revenge' as the boss. Other references I particularly enjoyed were the inclusion of Super Mecha Death Christ, the cheesy 'FREDDY'S(tm) COMING!' splash text, the brutal game over screen inspired by Friday the 13th, the 'Where'd You Learn to Fly?' head and the Earthbound leitmotif in the boss theme. The game truly is a love letter to AVGN fans and gaming fans as a whole.

That said, the difficulty of this game seems all over the place. I died around 512 times during my initial playthrough but for about 90% of the game I thought it was actually quite straightforward despite it's difficulty. For example, I died a combined total of less than 75 times for five levels, around 100 times for the remaining three (♥♥♥♥ you 'Boo! Haunted House') and then over 300 times for the final level, with the vast majority of those during the final boss. I must've been drunk or something because even though I got the pattern early, I was just dying left and right to Mr. Fred ♥♥♥ as I spent a good 2 hours (half my initial playthrough) to beat him. But yeah, prepare for some weird difficulty spikes.

Even if you don't know anything about the series, you should give this a go: it is a victory for the 'Nintendo Hard' games of the past offering a great platforming challenge for anyone to enjoy. Basically, the game doesn't ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ suck.\t\t\t\t\t

Let's start with the good:

The graphics and soundtrack are awesome. The crude humour is funny, plus the boss fights and character cameos really show that the design team knew the source material well, it really does have the AVGN feel to it.

Now for the bad:
Which is unfortunately, the gameplay.

Take a well-designed platformer like Celeste. Its a very challenging game, yet I enjoy playing it and have the drive to beat it. This is because in addition to having amazing soundtrack, graphics and storyline, the gameplay is absolutely solid. New mechanics are introduced throughout stages, the difficulty curve generally ramps up smoothly and most importantly, the game never feels unfair or tedious.

Now on the other hand, the obstacles and enemies in AVGN adventures are just mostly reskins of each other stages, and so there doesn't seem to be much variety in terms of level design (other than the boss fights). The game sometimes gets too challenging to the point where it becomes unfair.

You have invisible tiles, falling trap tiles, and the insta-death skull blocks with some of the worst hit boxes ever in a video game. And those skull blocks are in EVERY stage, usually in tight corners or difficult sections to add artificial difficulty. Oh and spoilers, there is a 9th and final stage that is basically a troll level, just intentional bad level design and is just unfair and not fun at all to play.

Not to mention, every time you die, you are forced to see a death screen with AVGN rage quips, which you can't skip. And you will die a ton of times in this game. It's funny the first few times, but afterwards, it gets really frustrating and impedes your progress. In Celeste, you respawn immediately after you die so you can quickly try again and gain the muscle memory needed to beat tough sections. Having an unskippable death screen is a time waster and a big distraction.

Overall, even though I’m a fan of AVGN, their first official video game is just whatever in terms of fun factor, there are definitely better platformers out there.
