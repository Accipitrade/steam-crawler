And most of all, I think that's what one's looking for. They want content based on their interest in AVGN; which this game well delivers. Almost all of the classic content is alluded to, either in game design of the levels, or in specific callbacks to the humour and world. Since James Rolfe was consulted, it also isn't dishonest or half-assed, and though the action sequences are over the top, I think it could pass for a "genuine" game rather than just a parody.

My favourite aspects of the game were the challenges, music, and the excellent use of switchable characters. You can play as the Nerd, Kyle Justin, Mike Matei, and Rolfe's character The B.S. Man. They all have strengths and weaknesses, and each level is so varied that it makes switching between them all really rewarding, so you can craft a unique gameplay experience. Since you can do the levels in any order, that also helps a lot for fresh content, or finding something new to do if you get stuck or frustrated. I spent about four hours of my playtime on the last level alone, trying to get through and finish the final boss; and it was ludicrously hard, but also well designed and the perfect example of how each character and technique could be called on for an obstacle. On top of all this, just like Rolfe says, the game has awesome music, specialized for the levels.

My only complaints few and far inbetween. It's a shame that the bonus characters have to be unlocked in a specific order, so it's possible to finish a lot of the game before you grab them all, and they could potentially be missed entirely. Likewise, a lot of the levels rely on the 'deathblocks" that just instantly kill your character, and though they could be similar to pits or one-hit deaths in general, their visual design can make some sections of levels feel similar to others; and aren't particularly referential to any games Rolfe has played. Furthermore, the Christmas level is awful. Not from a standpoint of gameplay, but its entire design makes no sense. I suppose they were trying to "get in the Christmas episodes of AVGN" but they weren't actually Christmas themed for the most part. They're often Bible-game themed, or Winter-themed; and even the original wasn't all that Christmas-y. I'm glad they fit in the 3 Ghosts, which was great to see, but the rest of the episode didn't feel like an AVGN game at all, it was just generic Christmas imagery (whereas, for example, the Haunted House level is a reoccuring joke in the series for having bats, zombies, ghosts, etc., based on Rolfe's love for horror franchises)

This game may not be the best if you aren't a fan of AVGN, but if you have any fond memories of the web series, it's an excellent experience that's fun enough to play more than once.\t\t\t\t\t
