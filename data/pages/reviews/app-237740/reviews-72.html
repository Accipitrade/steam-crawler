\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fan of this game, I'd give it a 8/10.
7/10 for the game itself.

The Good: Controls are fluient. Music is in 8-bit and in it's awesome glory. The game's graphics is all in classic 8-bit style that can reflect on the Mega Man games on the NES. The game has hidden cameos of other known internet celeberties and easter eggs. A lot of references reflected to the show. You even have the freedom of choosing which world you want to start off and complete in any order. It maintains the retro feel of playing a NES game that offers the challenge. Higher difficulties offer more challange to those who want to beat the game and make a name for themselves. Just like in past challenging games such as limited lives, removing the save feature, and the use of limited continues to replace the save feature if you plan to full play the game in the harder difficulties. So it has replayability.

The Bad: This game is hard to recomend if you're not a fan or never even heard of who the AVGN is. By playing the harder difficulties and the save feature turned off, you can't quit out the game and lose all your game progress. The game is short and can be finished in about 2 hours. The bosses feel somewhat repeatative and can be predictable when most of them move around the screen in a figure 8 pattern.

I don't have a lot to say for this game. But the game will urge you to play it again time to time. You may have a lot of lives. But they will start to run out fast if you're not too careful. The game has to do with a lot of time and patience if you plan to get through it alive. If you try to speed run the game with no thought, you will die so fast before you can say game over. The hidden characters you find and play as can be useful sometimes to get from a point you can't reach, a hidden wall or passage, and help you out getting a few extra lives and power ups. Although you will be using The Nerd more than any other character when it comes to killing mostt to all enemies and bosses. The game maybe hard. But can make you come back just to beat the harder difficulties to prove you can beat them since the game is short, but sweet. The game recomends you get the full experience by playing it using a controler. The game is really unfriendly to those who use a keyboard. You can rebind the keys, but the jump timings can be hindered just by a bit when it comes to key timing. I don't recomend buying this game at $15 full price. Wait for the game to go on sale. Still, it's a fun game not to pass up. A good game to play for yourself. But the game is much better when The Nerd plays it. Just like in the words of The Nerd said, "It's all my fault! $@%! this game!"

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoyed this game, more or less. Its pays homage to classic platformers like Mega Man and Castlevania really well. The music is pretty sweet and the controls are fluent. Lots of cameos from Cinnemassacre and AVGN episodes as well. So what is wrong with this game? Repetition. The levels are themed very differently but the enemies look different but do the exact same thing. there are indestructible ones that go in circles, ones that bounce up and down, ones that walk back and forth, and ones that fly across the screen dropping ♥♥♥♥. The death blocks seemed like a good excuse to not add variety to the enemies. Overall not bad but I would say ait until it goes on sale to buy.\t\t\t\t\t
