\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's start with context: Overall, I'm a big fan of the Angry Video Game Nerd series and James Rolfe in general.

With that said, I personally didn't enjoy this game much. It kills you all the time as a joke - you can take three hits before dying, but instant death from falls or the omnipresent "death blocks" is usually right around the corner. Then you get a random-generated text snippet of the Nerd's response, which will get old long before your first playthrough is over. And if you lose all your lives (you get 30 lives per continue on Normal, I think, which sounds like a lot until you try the game)... then you get the extra mean ending screen from the Friday the 13th episode, i.e. "You are dead, all your friends are dead, your pets are being skinned alive," etc. Again, just three to five seconds of staring at this gets old before long.

"Why not just stop sucking at the game?" You may ask. Well, it all comes down to what you feel a video game is:
1. A cruel, convoluted obstacle course for you to suffer your way past through endless repetition of the process of "look, guess, die, guess, memorize, repeat" in regard to exact movement patterns, for bragging rights?
2. A way to relax and enjoy yourself after your actual job or other responsibilities are taken care of for the day?

For me, option two makes more sense. You may disagree, and if you do, you may very well enjoy this... "game". However, any non-ultra-hardcore-macho-fnrrrg players reading this should be aware of the fun-to-bullcrap ratio. It's about 0.1 to 15. References are plentiful, but a lot of the time they're just sort of... there. "Oh, that's the thing from that one episode. Hehe." is about what you'll get out of them; expect no cascades of laughter. It's the old "comedy game" problem; if you go in expecting it to be funny, it'll be harder for it to make you laugh. If you expect a serious game, and that turns out to have a sense of humor on the side, laughter comes easier.

I hear the sequel is better. Maybe skip right to that. Or if all you want is a challenging Nintendo-style platformer, Hollow Knight will torment you just fine while also being beautiful and reinforced with a compelling narrative, told in glimpses. I still find it too difficult to fully enjoy, but there is considerable artistry involved, at least.

This game is... mostly just a mess of death blocks and frustration. Sometimes they're invisible until you get close. Sometimes they blink in and out of existence and there are a few specific pockets you have to run between. There are death beams, death plunges, things that can and will knock you into the death plunges, platforms you'll slide off of and lose lives...

I found myself at the final boss fight, trying to find the right pattern of where to stand, when to jump, how to avoid the damage areas, which of course is the challenge. Memorizing the appropriate motions and then repeating them with a few shots thrown in here and there until the fight is over.
I found myself running out of lives and replaying the final level, again and again, trying to get good enough to have enough lives left to practice fighting the boss until I can beat him. And then I just found myself thinking, "Why am I doing this? Is this fun? I'm not going to feel satisfied after this. I'm going to feel like a chump who just wasted a couple of hours learning how to beat this one guy in this crappy game that kills you constantly for the heck of it.

Maybe I'll go back and beat it some time. Maybe I'll get the sequel on 90% sale and skip to that. But I can't recommend this game to anyone not specifically looking to play a game that is difficult for the sake of it.

Oh, and if you want to support James Rolfe, you can always buy the AVGN movie, DVDs, Blu-rays... and if you're curious of this game, just watch his episode about AVGN games.\t\t\t\t\t
