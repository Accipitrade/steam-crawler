\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA game based off of a youtuber sounds risky, even more so when the game is $15, but surprisingly, AVGN Adventures is pretty good despite some issues. The games control is silky smooth (although I reccomend a controller), and the graphics are colorful and full of charecter, and the music is fitting and memorable. The games difficulty is high, but MOSTLY fair. I say mostly because there are points where the game doesn't quite live up to the name, and on higher difficulty modes where lives are few and far between, It's a real hastle when an unexpected instant death block comes on screen, leaving you less than a second to react. Another issue I had with the game is that alot of the games variety of enemies are just clones. I noticed on several occations that I would find an enemy in a stage based off of horror designed like a ghost, and then go to the hell level and find that exact same enemy with a new sprite and same sound effects. Yet another issue I had was that the highly mature and rauchy humor throughout the game is really not very funny. Even when the game quotes the youtube sensation himself, without any context, the jokes just come off as immature and childlike. But even with all of these issues, the game is still really fun, warrenting another playthrough from me. Just be careful with the unlockable difficulties unless you're a masichist.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs an AVGN fan for years, I really wish I could recommend this game. I really do. The game was clearly created with AVGN fans in mind, and is so filled with references to episodes that you'll really like how many cool touches they are to the game. From The Nerd's death trap from Super Pitfall, to the three spirits of Christmas from the Home Alone episode, you really wouldn't believe the amount of in jokes have been added to the game.

But I have to look at this from a gamer perspective, and honestly? It's one of my least favourite 2D Platformers to date. The developers fell into the Mega Man 9 pittrap of flanderizing Nintendo Difficulty by thinking "throwing down as much ♥♥♥♥ as possible" is all fair game. Unfortunately, it isn't. It isn't good level design, it isn't good difficulty balancing, it's simply fustrating, simple as.

And I know the joke of the game is meant to parody the silly things in bad games, but that's the problem right there. The game can lampshade the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ design, and cheap layouts as much as they want, but it doesn't change the fact they're present in the game, and they're unbelievably annoying to deal with. From rooms filled with Death Blocks, that are in unbelivably idiotic layouts and locations, to the point where it feels like flat out luck to get past them.

And if you have to play this game on a keyboard, get ready for even more pain, because the keyboard controls are even more of a pain to deal with, and unusally glitchy. For example, I tried to switch the layout to use W,A,S,D as the arrow controls, but as soon as I started the level Future F**kballs, The A button wasn't letting me walk left, instead just turning the character in that direction.

There are also four playable characters in the game, these including AVGN himself, Couch Guy, Mike, and Bullsh*t Man. Unfortunately, I only found myself playing as Mike, and Couch Guy for most of the game.

AVGN isn't a bad character, and he's useful for situations where you need balanced jumping, but Mike has a very powerful ranged attack, and an extremely high jump, which makes platforming slightly easier, while Couch Guy has a very good long range weapon that arcs, and goes through walls, and he has the fastest speed of the characters in the game.

The worst character by far however is Bullsh*t Man. He has a double jump, but his speed is so terrible, his weapon is so useless because it isn't long range or very powerful, and his normal jump is terrible, with his double jump just giving him a very slight boost to make him have the normal jump of maybe Couch Guy (The third worst jumper in the game).

The cameos in this game are also kind of cool as well. Seeing Angry Joe, The Completionist, and Jim Sterling in the game was certainly a cool little surprise. That said however, there was some really glaring obmissions, and I'll give you AVGN fans one guess as to who it is.

THE NOSTALIGA CRITIC THATS WHO.

I mean seriously, even if Nostalgia Critic hasn't done many game reviews, he's still done Blues Brothers, and Babe's Kids, and he's done a ton of video game movies/tv shows, and that doesn't even talk about the fact that NC is Angry Video Game Nerd's rival. They had a battle that the internet still remembers to this day.

James Rolfe himself has cameoed in NC's movies, as well as Doug Walker (The person behind The NC) cameoing in the AVGN Movie, so I was incredibly disappointed that he didn't even get a mention in the game, when other Youtubers like Egoraptor did get a cameo (Personally, I'm not a fan of Game Grumps, but that's more personal opinion more than anything.

On top of that, the extremely fustrating level design of the game also ends up ruining some of the jokes. For example, it seems very clear they created the game with a death toll in mind, like Super Meat Boy did, but where Super Meat Boy was a fast paced platformer with extremely short levels, AVGN Adventures takes more of a Mega Man style, which really doesn't suit the style. Mega Man levels are generally very long for a death toll kind of idea, and when the player is getting killed over and over by cheap layouts, rather than their own fault, then it only ends up fustrating them.

It doesn't help that when the player dies, they get a random quote from the AVGN talking about how the game is a pile of ♥♥♥♥. The problem is there's only about 10 to 20 of these in the game, and so, by the time you hit maybe the end of the first level, depending on which level you start on, you'll be so sick of these quotes. What makes it even worse is you can't skip past them until you've seen the text written out, meaning you have to wait about 5 to 10 seconds each time before you respawn.

If there's any positives to the game, the graphics are pretty good, and inspired by SNES games, and the music is surprisingly really awesome, and as I said, the injokes, references, and cameos are pretty cool to see, but in general, AVGN Adventures is a completely disappointing mess of a 2D platformer that falls into almost every pitfall that the Nerd has pointed out in bad games over the last 9 years.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn their zeal to make a game that features all the things the AVGN has complained about, they managed to make a game that is only slightly below average. One would expect that the novelty would make up for it--having useless power-ups, cheap deaths, and all that were intentional design choices, after all, so you know that you're buying into that when you buy the game. In addition to this, though, AVGN Adventures has clear problems that should have been fixed.

Serious Problems:

Movement. The characters move with all the precision of Meat Boy. This makes playing the level "Beat Em and Eat Em", which features a ton of slippery surfaces, even more frustating. The imprecise controls are one thing, but there is something else that combines with them that, honestly, threaten to make AVGN Adventures unplayable. And that "something" is:

The camera. Rather than centering the AVGN, AVGN Adventures has him just slightly to the left of the center when you're moving to the right, and just slightly to the right of the camera when you're moving left. Changing directions on the fly becomes borderline impossible, because the camera shifts, too, which can and will totally screw up whatever difficult platforming section you were trying to get through. And that's a problem because of:

Knockback. The knockback in this game is weird. I'd tentatively say that the knockback consists primarily of you getting hit UP, not backward or forward. In good games, knockback is predictable, and can even be taken advantage of. There's none of that here, and the unusual knockback will be a source of many deaths, simply because it's non-standard and very difficult to get used to. And, naturally, there aren't very many platforming sections that don't feature enemies coming at you, because:

The level design in AVGN Adventures is horrendous. It's dull, uninspired, and boring. As ProJared rightly pointed out, there are actually only a few enemy types in the game, and what they look like simply varies based on what stage you're in.

And, as ProJared also points out, the developers substituted instant death blocks instead of creative level design. Predictable disappearing/reappearing Deathblocks appear in almost every stage, and they're not really difficult to get past; it just requires you to wait. There's a lot of waiting in this game. Something ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ is the most obvious offender: half of the stage is filled with instant death beams that are ridiculously easy to avoid and accomplish little more than making you wait for 3 seconds every 15 steps. This will almost certainly be the first level that you beat without dying at all.

And you'll be glad to not die often, because dying is tedious. Even though there's an obscene number of checkpoints, even on Normal mode, death is a pain in the ass because the developers thought it would be funny to have a random AVGN statement about what he'd rather do than continue playing--every. single. time. you. die.

And you die a lot. That's kinda the point of the game, supposedly being Nintendo Hard. But it's not. This is AVGN Adventure's main flaw: it PRETENDS to be Nintendo Hard by throwing a lot of cheap deaths at the player, but it's actually a very easy game. This is no I Wanna Be the Guy, which throws up a lot of cheap deaths AND is Nintendo Hard. I can't quantify what the difference is.

It's a short game. It can be beaten in just a few hours, which makes that $9.99 price tag WAY too high. For $2.99 or $3.99, I could recommend this. But at $9.99? You're paying more than $1 per hour for an experience that... just isn't that good.

People often say that the music in AVGN Adventures is awesome. But that's not true. The only level in the game that has good music is Something ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, and I'll give it that--that level's music is incredibly awesome. But that's only one-ninth of the game; what about the other 8 levels? They're nothing special, and a few are actually annoying. One stage has really awesome music, but 8/9 of the stages have boring, uninteresting music, yet the game is still cited as having awesome music. It doesn't. It has one stage that has awesome music. The rest of the music kinda sucks.

Almost all of the bosses are the same, too, including the final boss. There are one or two exceptions, but most of the bosses just move around in a figure 8 while shooting things.

So here you have a game that would have quickly faded into obscurity if it didn't have the AVGN's name attached to it. There's a sequel coming out relatively soon, and my sincerest hope is that the developers took a few lessons in creative level design before then, because I don't consider filling a stage with death blocks, using only 5 or 6 enemies, and making players wait constantly to be "creative". Even the final boss is just a rehash of several other bosses in the game, with nothing unique or new going on anywhere at all. Take that $9.99 and buy the Mega Man Legacy Collection instead. You'll get way more entertainment out of Mega Man than AVGN Adventures.

To that, you're probably saying, "Well, duh!"

And that's my point EXACTLY. They're both $9.99.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHoly butts, this game is awesome!

I've been a fan of the AVGN since he started, and I've met him a handful of times. He's a really cool guy, so I try to support him in any way that I can. When I heard he had his own game, I jumped at the chance to get it.

Simply put, this game is a blast. It's a tough as nails action platformer that perfectly captures the spirit of older gen titles. Kickin' music? Yes. Tight controls? Yes. Colorful, varied stages each with their own theme and challenges? Yes. A play experience that makes me feel like I spent my money well? Yes.

If you remember playing games like Contra, Journey to Silius, Mega Man, and Super Turrican, this game is for you. It's a simple premise: shoot and jump your way through each stage, all while collecting power ups and dodging an insane amount of obstacles. That's it. And it works. It's the high level of difficulty that makes this game so good though: a very balanced difficulty system allows players of all skill levels to get what they want from this game.

Aside from the great gameplay, this is also a fantastic low-brow homage to the games of yesteryear. The incessant profanity made me laugh, too.

Buy this game. Expect a torrent of swear words and colorful metaphors to explode from your mouth, but it's a gratifying experience when you finally beat it.\t\t\t\t\t
