\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI intially went into this game with low expectations I have been dissapointed with licensed product before (having played a myriad of movie tie-in shovelware titles in my youth.) However I was very impressed with AVGN Adventures, a game that will appeal to both long-time fans of The Nerd and fans of hardcore retro platformers.

This title was released in 2013, developed by FreakZone Games and published by ScrewAttack. As shown by The Nerd in his episode "AVGN Games", games about The Nerd and his angry world have existed long before this game came out. Many of those titles were homebrew titles often created by one person and released on the Internet for free. AVGN Adventures is the first commercial AVGN game and certainly the title with the highest production value. Visually, the game features rich and colorful pixel art in additional to some smoothly animated effects (The Nerd exploding into several bits and pieces a la Fallout 3 is a highlight.) The soundtrack hearkens back to the video game days of yore, featuring an engrossing score that fits right at home with the games overall difficulty as well as sound effects that further draw you in.

The gameplay is almost perfect for a game of this genre, it's hard but not unfair, and it's levels are fun and all vastly different from eachother. My only true gripe with the game is the finicky keyboard controls which unfortunately makes jumping and landing with precision challenging. This problem is of course erased when you plug in an Xbox 360 PC controller, however it does not erase the games overall difficulty. This game is not recommended for anyone who's not familiar with NES titles like Mega Man, Ninja Gaiden, and Castlevania as the difficulty of this game mimicks that of those classic titles. Also, the game doesn't have much replay value after you complete it for the first time which is acceptable for a $10 game but it would have been a nice feature to unlock additional levels or harder difficulty levels after you complete the game.

It's definitely worth your money if you like hard-as-nails, old-school platformers!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all: This game will definitely make sense only to fans of the Angry Video Game Nerd alias James Rolfe (duh...).

All in all it is a sweet, hard platformer that spoofs not only a lot of old, bad games of the Atari and NES era but also the Angry Video Game Nerd "culture" (like him collecting beer bottles for health and his constant cursing).
It is challenging and funny (already if you play in easy mode), colourful and full of detail.
The graphics are fine, the music is okay but also a little bit forgettable (with the exception of the AVGN theme song in 8-bit). The sound effects fit but can be annoying sometimes (but I guess that was even on purpose, like: Annoying sh*tty game reference = annoying sound effect).
A handful of different levels, a lots of secrets to find and a couple of Steam achievements additionally keep the player going.

As I said: A good game and tribute to this angry internet reviewer but mostly recommended to players who at least know who that is and what he does. To others...I don't know, it could appear extremely random and needlessly hard ;)\t\t\t\t\t
