\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
+ Fun and addicting gameplay which is familiar to anyone who has played platformers on the NES
+ Awesome soundtrack. Great mixture of chiptune tracks (bought the soundtrack straight after hearing for the first time)
+ A must for the AVGN fan; playable characters, power-ups and more reference the AVGN videos.

Cons:
- Difficulty at times can come across as unfair, especially if playing on normal difficulty.
- Fairly short. There are 9 not very long stages and the entire game can be beat under an hour by an experienced gamer

Verdict: 9/10

Comments:
Overall, Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures is a fantastic game that pays homage to classic 8-bit platformers and is a must for AVGN fans. The difficulty may be a turn off for some people but is perfect for people who enjoy a challenge. The energetic soundtrack is the cherry on the cake which makes the whole experience a really enjoyable one. Can't recommend enough.

P.S. I highly recommend the soundtrack as a separate purchase to anyone who enjoys chiptune music

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery hard to recommend.

Utilizing copy-paste death blocks, cheap death designs, and many of the worst tropes of video game design does not make this a great game. Ironically poking fun at the flaws of yesteryear doesn't elevate your game above those same flaws. This, overall, leads to a frustrating experience. The entire game I felt like I was gritting my teeth and telling myself: "But, it's AVGN, it's ScrewAttack, they're all the best, this can't be bad..."

The namebrand is what sells this. If you're a fan of the series, there's plenty to love and discover from the show. You may die a dozen times along the way, but it's still exciting to point out all the AVGN moments. Also, the music is absolutely top notch and the highlight of this game.

I'm sure there's enough blind fan ambition to rate this up, but I cannot recommend it to anybody but the most dedicated of fans.
