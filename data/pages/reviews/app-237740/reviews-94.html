\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvery gamer has heard of AVGN, and almost definitely seen at least one episode. Excessively vulgar, harsh, and, well, angry, the Nerd debuts in his first official video game, "The Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures". This is just going to be a brief overview of the game.

The game consists of a total of 10 stages à la Mega Man style. There is a brief intro stage, followed by 8 stages that can be done in any order, and a final stage available after the previous 8 have been completed. Each stage parodies one or more games/themes, with countless references to AVGN videos. Said references range from in-your-face things like lava sharks, to things as subtle as unremarkable enemy patterns that were only featured in a single video of the 100+ episodes. There are also several secret characters from all of Cinemassacre that can be unlocked once located, and switched to at any time during gameplay. In addition to all of this, there are plenty of cameos that are strewn throughout the game featuring internet personalities like Egoraptor, Angry Joe, and The Completionist.

The graphics are a fantastic recreation of the classic 8-bit era, and the overall design of the characters and levels is just incredible. The music though... wow. The game really shines in this department. Every song is absolutely amazing, and there's even a kick ass little beat that plays whenever you pause the game (a reference to the Battletoads episode). "Future ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ 2010" is probably my favorite track. The controls are very tight and about as perfect as a platformer's controls can be. It's really difficult to describe, but as soon as you start playing, you'll understand. The one thing that took some getting used to is the positioning of the Nerd. The Nerd sticks his gun out, and it's completely to the side of his sprite. The area where the gun is takes no damage, but the character sprite does, but because of the size of the gun I naturally see the point at which they connect as the middle, which caused a lot of misjumps and deaths early on.

One thing has to be made clear... this game is hard. Very, very hard. Some of the difficulty stems from complaints AVGN has made about games in his episodes, and were seemingly intentionally put in the game to cause anger and frustration. A relatively skilled gamer can get through it on Easy with some patience, but difficulties beyond that require much more skill, patience, and dedication. This game is truly challenging, and will seriously piss you off at times... but strangely in a good way. Like, just enough to make you want to beat it, so as to prove to it your superiority. There are several difficulty levels that are unlocked by beating the game on the previous highest difficulty setting. To beat the game on the very highest difficulty would probably take the caliber of a demigod.

Overall, the game is a kick ass platformer, but damn is it hard. At it's current price, it's hard to recommend it to someone who isn't a AVGN fan. I beat it on Normal in around 5 hours, but I died about 500 times and I'm pretty skilled at old school platformers. To me, there's pretty high replayability, as I want to try and beat it on the highest difficulty settings, but to someone who isn't into old school "♥♥♥♥ you" hard platformers or aren't a fan of AVGN, A single run through (if that) is probably enough. If you like AVGN, you should pick the game up. If you don't, but you like extremely challenging old school platformers, you might want to give it a shot, but all of the show references will go unnoticed, and there's an enormous amount of humor and nostalgia there, as this game is a hate-filled love letter to AVGN fans. If you're casual... stay away. Far, far away.\t\t\t\t\t
