\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really enjoy my time with this game!

While I am not a fan of games that try too hard to 'bring back the past' by featuring 8-bit graphics and old-school mechanics borrowed from NES games, I do enjoy hard 2D platforming games that require patience and memorization.

AVGN Adventures is definitely it's own thing and does not rely on you having any sort of nostalgia for the genre. It has a distinct visual style, very well implemented mechanics that are both old-school and new, and is just generally one of the few examples of a modern 2D platformer done right. Sure, there is a little bit of trial and error, but I would say that patient players will not find themselves frustrated with the level design and the death traps. The level design is devilish, but not unfair! Really, all of your skill depends on whether or not you can remain calm and on how fast you pick up on shortcuts through levels and boss fights. Secrets are hard to find (I would say impossible) and I would suggest looking at a guide to find all the cameos and characters to help you on your way through any of the higher difficulties.

If you are a AVGN fan, you'll definitely get more of a kick out of the game as there's lots of references. I can imagine the 'dialogue' in the game being grating to non-fans as you can't turn it off. That quickly becomes a problem for anyone that wants to replay the game multiple times, as the dialogue still remains there.

The game can be beaten in an hour on lower difficulties, but the game banks on you replaying it on higher unlockable difficulties. If you like earning achievements, this game makes it really satisfying to get them (for the most part). I played it for 8 hours according to my timer, and I have only beaten the game on 2 difficulties so far (Normal and Old-School). All that changes is that you have more limited amounts of lives and harder to beat enemies to face (as well as other small things).

All in all, AVGN Adventures is definitely a memorable 2D Platformer and definitely an instant-classic for AVGN fans. For everyone else, though, this will just be a fun experience for a few hours. I recommend it to anyone who likes a simple game with challenging level design and a lust for earning achievements.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy Review for Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures

Postives
-Has you play as james (AVGN) and you must beat every level to survive and have a chance of escaping the game
-Jokes on death/game over
- has three modes , Easy , normal and hard
-60 fps
-v-sync option
-Can rebind keyboard keys and controller buttons
-Has options for fullscreen / windowed / audio sliders
-Has keyboard and xbox controller support
-snes styled levels
-Good varity of levels totally 8 ( this is a short but hard game)
-Snes styled graphics look decent and look like they could of been runing on a snes


Negatives
-Some unfair death spots so expect to rage and get use to seeing game over if playing over anything above easy.
-Has some issues on death at least on my rig where the death text would speed up for no reason
- Had some display driver issues with my 6870 would disconnect then reconnect but found out it was due to using radeon pro(disable it for this)
-No graphic options (though I couldn't imagine what much you would need for them with graphics this low. If you can't run this it's time to upgrade your toster and retire it to making pop tarts

Overall game is ok, good to play maybe once or twice maybe three if you're looking to say play once on easy to learn levels then try normal and then try hard ect.
Keeps true to the AVGN videos and if you like AVGN then you might like it unless you hate platformers with death blocks.

7.5/10

I recomend geting this on sale at 3.99$ at 60% off because of how short a game it's though if you would like to pay 9.99$ to support james better then feel free to do so since he really does put in alot of work into his videos and this a source of income for him besides donations / buying his movie/ buying his merc.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAngry Video Game Nerd Adventures is like a trip down memory lane for AVGN fans. If you really like The AVGN then this game is for you. It has alot of the same humor that is found in the videos and many charactors and settings are in the game that are direct references to certain episodes of AVGN. If your not familier or a fan of AVGN you may like this game less however if your a fan of older video games such as side scrolling action shooter platformers I would give this game a try. It is however insanely difficult and that is the way it is meant to be. Also what I found really interesting and fun was the ability to play not just as AVGN but Mike which is pretty cool among others. This game was a bit short however but I guess the real challenge is seeing how much better you can do going thru it again and dieing a little less.\t\t\t\t\t
