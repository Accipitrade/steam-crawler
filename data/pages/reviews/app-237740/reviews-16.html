\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonally, I love this game, but I don’t know how readily I would recommend it to the average Steam gamer out there.

A large portion of my love for this game comes from the fact that I have watched almost all of the Angry Video Game Nerd’s videos and I have played (and occasionally own) many of the poorly conceived 80s and 90s games he rips on. A lot of the things that are going on in this game are direct references to gags from his YouTube series and most of the rest are inside jokes for retro gamers... hence my enjoyment.

Essentially, this game is an ironic callback to the days where every TV-show, film, and occasionally junk-food franchise got a lame, side-scrolling, overly difficult, platformer based on it, and that is how the game plays. Of course, I grew up on that stuff and the game does this stereotype rather well. As the description states, it does feel like a cross between the Mega Man X series and Castlevania 3: it is primarily a shooter, it has a stage select mechanic, and features additional characters you can find and switch between for dynamic game-play along with plenty of secrets. Although it does get frustrating at times, it has an easy mode for more casual gamers and several re-spawn points in the levels. I like the style of the game (SNES graphics and NES sound), the themed levels, and its replay value (try to find all the other YouTubers that cameo in the game). There is also something really rewarding about how massively the enemies explode when defeated.

Ultimately, I think the game-play is decent and holds up on its own, but I feel those not familiar with James Rolfe’s titular character might be a little confused by some of the sarcastic lines, vile imagery, and bizarre situations that appear in the game. If you have not watched his videos yet, you should check him out on the Cinemassacre channel--he was one of the early video-game vlogers who influenced the gaming culture therein. If you can grok him, this game is definitely for you!

Also, BS-Man farting is the most convincing double-jump physics I have seen thus far! Kudos to the devs!
