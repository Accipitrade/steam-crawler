\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn the barest of descriptions, this game is a piece of interactive fan-art. It distills 8+ years of content from it’s legacy, The Angry Video Game Nerd (produced by James Rolfe, who is a pretty awesome guy) into a Nintendo-length and Nintendo-hard adventure. For those who have watched the web-series, it’s a short walk down memory lane, a challenge to find all the references and easter eggs, and a way to experience the trials The Nerd has experienced over the lifespan of the series. It was lovingly produced to be an intentional experience, and for fans, it’s delightful.

It’s important to point out that this game is not for everyone. Like most fan-art, you can appreciate the aesthetics if you approach it unaware of the original work, but a lot of the heart, depth and meaning will be lost. Yes, the “retro” graphics are charming and the game handles well, and you may even see inspirations in the level design from games like Castlevania if that’s your generation. However, with no other perspective, you will soon be frustrated by the games seemingly poor level design. The game constantly cheats and frustrates you, killing you time and time again at no real fault of your own. Things appear skewed against you in some very unfair and unfun ways, never relenting.

For fans of the series, however, this is expected. This game emulates the experiences of The Nerd throughout the video series, constantly subjecting himself to terrible games from the genesis of home gaming under the premise of warning others against their trespasses. This game takes a sampling of those horrors and concentrates them into one experience, each level its own little snapshot of gaming hell. The joy of playing this game is largely derived from nostalgia, remembering the Olde Games and the despicable things that they did to you, that they made you do to beat them. We remember afternoons and evenings spent dedicated to conquering just one more level, despite the awful controls, the contrived traps and the straight-up horrendous level design. It’s all we had, and we didn't know better. This was just the way games were. We hated them, but we kept coming back, learning to love them like digital Stockholme-suffering prisoners.

Conceptually, the game helps us remember simpler times, and for that I do recommend it, but with a caveat: If you aren't from the Nintendo generation, or you haven’t seen the Angry Video Game Nerd web series, then I highly suggest you experience those things first.

A movie thats out today and a video game thats right here on steam. But will this be the game you will want to play ? We play as the angry game nerd who gets trapped in Game Land. So he has to conquer all the levels to get out. So how is this game ? Personally i really enjoyed it. This game is a shooter platformer in the style of contra. The game also is quite difficult and one of the hardest games i have played. The game has a few problems. The level desing is pretty bad. The controls are kinda stiff at moments cause the game requires a split second reaction time. The game is also quite buggy.The game has this really annoying thing called Death Blocks which seems like all levels are made. If you touch em thats it game over and its quite annoying that youre jumps need to be very persise to not get killed by em. Overall Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures is a really fun platforming game with an exceptional soundtrack and its also really fun to play. Its also quite short and i suggest you get this game on sale. I give Angry Video Game Nerd Adventures a 7/10
