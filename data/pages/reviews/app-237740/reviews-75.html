\r\n\t\t\t\t\t$15 maybe a bit much, maybe $10 or less is a better price for what it is.

The game should really appeal to anyone who likes the AVGN videos and maybe only them. People who don't like the comedy style of AVGN or don't know him will probably just get annoyed with the game and the story as a whole. There is swearing everywhere but its just text, there is no voice in this game and the story is told via text boxes with drawn art cards depicting scenes at the start of the game and the end. Honestly, story is negligible and really isn't to the concern of the player in these sorts of games. Its all about gameplay.

You play as the nerd or one of four characters you can find. You can hot swap between these characters and some may make situations easier but in all honesty all you need to beat the game is the Nerd and Guitar Guy. The nerd being perfectly fine with all situations aside from those the Guitar Guy is required. Each character has their own special stats/move so you can have some choice about how you accomplish a goal. But for the most part their is really only one way to get by most obstructions. Jump a second to soon or late and you die or get hit. This is to be expected with this type of game but it does get annoying as you progress and insta death blocks become more and more common.

Oh yeah, insta death blocks. Some of the biggest bs to ever grace this game and they are everywhere. I mean EVERYWHERE. They are just plain overused, like the dev couldn't figure out how to give a fair new challenge to the player so they just tossed insta death blocks in everywhere they could which is especially painfull as you get more and more stuff on screen and you have to pay attention to all of it to keep from getting hit. Once you figure out when to jump, run, or whatever it stops being such a challenge unless a glitch or so messes you up.

What I found is the game has really strange hit detection. Sometimes things that clip into your character don't hit you (enemy attacks or insta death stuff) but sometimes you can be not even near it seemingly and it kills you. Exactly how close you can be to something before it kills you is something I still havn't figured out. It just seems like luck that you live or die, especially in the last insta death dissappearing and reappearing block puzzle. You just have to keep trying over and over until it lets you through. Of course skill does come into play but I can't tell you how many times I thought I was in the clear only to have the blocks appear and instakill me although I know I was clear of them. Its complete bull. This same problem can happen with platforms but it seems to be limited to falling platforms I think. You can jump and get stuck on the corner and then the platform falls.

Bouncing is a game of luck aswell. You can try and get good at it but even when you think you have it down your character desides to take a suicide dive straight into insta death. Its like playing with fire while soaked in gas, you have to have the patience of a saint and a surgeon in one to do it right.

Controls are solid aside from the bouncing mechanic which is like the wheel of misfortune. Your character controls very smoothly and solidly aside from times when you need to do jumps perfectly on a single block with an instadeath right beside your intended jump spot. At times like that its easy to overjump and die or under and fall to your demise but this maybe also something to do with the weird hit detection. I really wish I would have recorded it in slow motion to see exactly what was up.

Bosses are a pleasant challenge and once you figure out there pattern they are pretty easy, but thats after you die a good number of times though. Fred ♥♥♥♥♥, the final boss, is an absolute pain in the rear as he is right after the most horrible level in the game. Once you get past beginners traps and turtle your way to the end you will have no lives, but even with knowing the level well you might only get to him with 20 lives and let me tell you that you'll probably need them. What I did was crouch on the lowest right platform and when I could I would jump up and shoot him as he flew up until he brought out his balls. When hes got his balls out (hehe) crouch on the left side of the platform when hes flying to the left side of the screen, and when he flies to the right side of the screen get on the left sideof the middle platform and crouch. When he starts crapping on you you have to pause the game to see if he is going to ♥♥♥♥ above you, then you have time to react and dodge him. I would advise shooting at him while hes above you then pausing fast as you can when under him. It seems like cheating, but honestly since the crap is random its fair in my opinion. When he brings out his wrecking balls or chain balls (whatever the heck you call them) get on the two platforms farthest to the left.

There you go, Freds dead baby!

Powerups are fairly rare and and theres only 4 types, beer for health, the SNES gun, mecha jesus, and the glitch gremlin. The gun sucks because in one hit you lose it, and the gremlin is mostly useless as it only stops enemies and fire balls and similiar dangers like that from moving. It dousn't make them stop damaging you so its pretty useless aside from points where you get lucky and you line everything up so you can run through an area without getting hit at all. Mecha jesus kills everything on screen and is only really used around where he can be picked up.

The game is pretty goofy, just like AVGNs style but people who arn't familiar with this may become offput by that comedic style. But its all taken from the show and I even chuckled out loud when I saw some of the enemies, text, and levels in general. They nailed AVGN, maybe a bit to well.

The graphics are farily nice and colorfull. Not as good looking as Bastion, but still good looking. The music is also good stuff. I can't remember if its the same song throughout the game as I was to focused on playing the game. Which I think goes to show the music is good if it didn't annoy me and helped me keep my head in the game.

Another bad point is the acheivements, I don't think its possible to get them all. YOLO seems impossible and impossible seems masochistic. I died over a 1,000 times but I had to play through the game twice. Oh yeah, twice! Not even on purpose. I wanted to see other difficulties and without thinking and half on accident I pressed new game but I didn't select a difficulty. The stupid game deleted my save. THIS WAS IN A NERD VIDEO! This was one of the most basic things and they screwed it up. You have one save file and nothing else but you can go in and edit he save file I think in order to skip parts in the game. I didn't do this because I didn't feel like cheating and possibly disabling acheivments since I just don't know what it would do to them.

In summary and TL:DR

AVGNA is a rather good game with alot of throwbacks to the old games featured in AVGN. Unfortunately it is somewhat flawed in some very annoying ways and takes to many of the things that just piss gamers and AVGN off and puts them in this game. Its much better though than most 2d Platformer games around these days as instead of trying to be someing silly like art it just tries to be a fun video game and succeeds for the most part. But technical hitches here and there do creep up at times and can be very annoying when they do but its hardly a constant problem.

It can be very challenging, but also very rewarding when you do finally beat a level and eventually the game. The only really big issues are hit detection (because when it does creep up and become a problem you can waste several lives with it) and some of the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ jumps or puzzles that are just stupidly difficult. The game is pretty goofy, just like AVGNs style but people who arn't familiar with this may become offput by that comedic style. For better or worse they took everything from the show.\t\t\t\t\t
