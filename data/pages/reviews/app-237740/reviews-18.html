\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: It's fine. Just a really hard Megaman game without the weapons you get at the end of each level.

I mean there's not a whole lot else to say besides what's in my TLDR to be honest. It's got tons of instant death blocks and some ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ enemy placement and knockback. It's not Ghosts and Goblins hard or anything (assuming you don't play on the oldschool difficulty setting that is, which tbh I don't recommend. Like yeah it's a challenge but you'd have to really really love this game or achievement hunting to suffer through beating this game on anything harder than the "hard" difficulty), but it'll definitely give you a run for your money. Honestly, it's just fine. Nothing really that special beyond being an AVGN game, but certainly a good time if you're into hard retro games. I feel like it's sort of pointless for me to even write this review as I feel like those who would be interested in the game will probably have already played it or at least know about it.

Oh and instead of weapons you get like in megaman, you can unlock hidden characters in the levels which have different attacks. It's a neat idea, but not executed that great as the nerd just kinda remains the most versatile overall. That said you can switch characters on the fly with the press of a button, so it's not like you're locked into any one character, which is nice, but it probably would have been nice to have one fully fleshed out character with all sorts of different options as opposed to 4 different characters that all feel rather meh.

Honestly, just go play the sequel. It's definitely easier, but it's so much better and more fleshed out as an overall experience. Still, it's not a bad purchase for 5 bucks if you like this sort of game.\t\t\t\t\t
