\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is actually genius, but I don't reccomend it unless you're a big AVGN Fan. The game is intentionally bad, using various tropes from so called "♥♥♥♥♥♥ Games" as the nerd calls them. Such as death traps without warning, higher difficulty settings with generally unrealistic expectations.

The game is quite short with only nine levels. Nine very hard levels (Especially Laughin' Jokin' Numbnuts and Boo Haunted House.) There's only nine, the description says ten, but it's counting the tutorial stage, which I really wouldn't as it's that short and without challenges.

A big part of the game is memorization, once you know where all the sudden death traps are and the trick to them. You can pretty much do it every time with the boss (There are a few with no rules outside of "Kill them before they kill you, ♥♥♥♥ dodging, just hope they die before you do) so it's more for the Nerd Inside Jokes. So as I said, if you're an AVGN Fan, pick it up when it's on sale (This game isn't worth 15, but 5? definitely.)

You'll laugh hard, so hard you'd rather have a buffalo take a diarrehia dump in your ear! In this game where ya die, die, die, die, die, die, die, die, die, die, die, die. Because the difficulty is LEGENDARY!

Oh and for those who do buy, play the game in this order, Future ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ 2010, Dungeons and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, and Beat It & Eat It. This will help you unlock the hidden characters. Guitar Guy will be needed for his speed, Mike for his jumping, and BS Man for when you need to kill enemies fast.

Anyway, yeah, like I said, if you've seen AVGN, buy it when it's on sale, if you haven't give it a pass. I'd reccomend it at full price if it didn't use artificial difficulty (I get that it's part of the joke, but still), and was longer.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe production values here are pretty impressive. The graphics and sounds are all glorious recreations of our 8-bit past. And while the gameplay is enjoyable and intense, the levels are sloppily developed. For example, the ♥♥♥♥ demons that you fight in "Thy Farts Consumed" can easily be dispatched from a safe spot without any effort. Most of the enemies are simply blocking your path and provide no challenge to get past.

However, the strength of this game is the straight up platforming. This is the kind of game that truly makes you feel badass when you get past a series of spike traps and death blocks without dying once. Speedrunning would suit this game well for that reason, which the game promotes by showing clear time and number of times died upon completion of a level.

While I think the game is far too expensive at $14.99, the novelty of it being The Angry Videogame Nerd, awesome music, hidden characters, and challenging platforming still make it a worthwhile purchase for fans.
