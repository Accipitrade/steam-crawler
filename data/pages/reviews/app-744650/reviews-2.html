\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been a big fan of Tower Defense games my entire life. Everything from Bloons to Defense Grid: The Awakening to StarCraft and StarCraft: Brood War UMS to Plants vs. Zombies and whatever else I'm forgetting. I'm also a huge fan of MOBA-style games as the many hours of Dota 2 on my profile would suggest.

Order of the Gatekeepers has been able to combine aspects of both genres to create a very challenging and very re-playable experience. Anybody who buys this game can dive right in and have fun, but it also has a higher-skill ceiling for those that want to play around and experiment with different tower/item combinations.

Last, but not least, I understand the developer to be very active in updating and maintaining the game-play and mechanics, so it's nice to know that he's passionate about the project.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOn the surface this game appears to be another generic TD game with relatively rough graphics and an otherwise off-putting price tag of 19.99. Normally I would never think to play a game like this but I thought what the heck; obscure games deserve some attention too. And after playing I can say...

Something is here...

Although my perspective on the graphics did not change, they're rough and that's that, but if one can overlook that, you'll realize that this game is in no understatement one of the most interesting perspectives on the TD genre. The remarkable efforts put into developing this game by the creator are reflected in a uniquely deep and lengthy system with extensive tower variety, and a sub-system which allows the equipping of said towers with an even larger variety of items that boast a wide array of effects. During my play time, this amount of information was admittedly overwhelming, and it will probably take the average person at least several failures and losses to begin grasping it. Nonetheless, this game is absolutely worth a look. Even if you don't end up liking it and refund it, this is a creator who is most definitely worth the follow, especially based off this promising start. The most intriguing developments and takes on bland and unremarkable genres come from independent creators like this one, and I can honestly say he has accomplished bringing new life to an otherwise dead genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeems very fun, but as of right now the Campaign does not let you go past the first level. You finish all the waves up til the last wave, which says "Victory!", but the game never actually goes to that last Victory wave, so you just sit there until you decide to quit. You can choose levels by hand rather than playing the campaign, but I would like to go in order. So until the devs see this and push out what I hope is a small fix, I'd hold off on it.

Other than that issue, the game seems extremely fun, especially if you're someone who loves theorycrafting and very in depth systems in games.

EDIT: The Dev fixed this issue within 1 hour and that was really my only issue with it so far. It seems extremely fun and looks like it has a whole lot of potential for hours of fun and trying new strategies. Big thumbs up from me.\t\t\t\t\t
