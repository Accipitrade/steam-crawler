\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is visible from the first second, that this game is a mobile port. The detail level of theBlu is way better, BUT I have to admit that I found it really fun to dive through the ocean, explore the environment and watch all the differnt creatures.

Visuals:
The models and their animations look good, if you do not get too close. Quite low polygon count for some of them, but the biggest issue are the damn low resolution textures without any bumpmaps or some advanced shaders. The water in some environments is very muddy, so that you cannot see more than few meters. This makes it sometimes a bit hard to find the creatures you want to see, but on the other hand, it challenges to explore and swim around. No graphic settings in game. Hey, VR users have powerful machines! :-)
The in game menus are well done, self explanatory and quite intuitive.

Locomotion:
Swimming around is possible by either use the Vive/Rift controllers to perform swim movements with your arms or, which is more convenient, to use the propeller feature of the contollers. Very positive: The movement direction by propeller is steered with the orientation of the Vive controllers and is independent from the view direction. Depending if you dive with one or two propellers (on left and right controller), the speed changes accordingly. I played around 30 minutes in the first session without any nausea.

Variance:
Here the game has to offer much more than theBlu! Several scenes with different creatures and enviroments. It is kind of included search game to find the fully narrated info points. The variance and locomotion possibilities are the biggest pro-arguments for the game.

Interaction:
Ok, here we are at the point with the biggest potential to improve. There is NO physical interraction between the Vive controllers and any animal! This is really the biggest immersion killer in the game, forget about the low-res textures, but that no animal reacts on touches with the controllers and that there is no collision detection at all, really breaks it. The animals just swim through the contollers and as well sometimes though the headset. I mean, it is not needed for exotic animals, but at least sea lions, dolphins and other sweeties shoud react and interact with the player. The white shark does it as well (in an unfriendly way). I think all this comes from the gameÂ´s mobile origin, but on the PC it would have the potential to be a "theBlu killer" with some better textures and interactions.
Oh, the game claims to have some intractions with Orcas, sea lions and dolphins, but this is limited to drop some fishes or rings at random positions into the water, where the animals interact with. So the "interaction" is to press a button to drop the objects.

Conclusion:
So this review sounds quite negative, why do I give a thumbs up? Well, as said in the beginning, it is fun to explore and see the animals and I see a big potential for improvements. Children will have fun for sure. The game is not in early access, but still I hope the devs will improve one or the other mentioned point in future patches. At least the interaction with animals, like in theBlu should be doable and would bring the game to the next level. Overall, I hope and recommend.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor $US9.99, this isn't too bad. Ocean Rift is compared with theBlu a lot. Where theBlu has you in a small area where you can move around, Ocean Rift allows you to move around a much larger area in all directions.

theBlue gives you three set experiences on a set timer, whereas Ocean Rift gives you a larger number of dedicated experiences with no time limit.

Suggestions for updates:
Improved skin detail. All the animal families are identical. For example, the pod of dolphins all look the same - no scratches or slight imperfections to give them individuality. The whales are barnacle-free.
Clipping. You can move inside all of the animals. This is an intense immersion breaker. You can be swimming with some [identical] whales, when suddenly you'll swim inside their models.
Clipping is also present in the floor and wall rocks where boundries are not present, as well as seaweed and other decorations. The seaweed could be animated to sway to a side when the player interacts with it.
Resolution. Most of the animals and decorations look too bland and cartoony. This is where theBlu dominates.\t\t\t\t\t

Throw rubber rings for the Dolphins etc to swim through using your trackpads or use side buttons to propel yourself around (use both controllers for faster speed). The voice commentary for each animal is very nice giving you some info on habits etc. Area's to swim around are really quite spacious. If you go real deep your right controller becomes a torch. Very good atmosphere and highly recommended if you liked 'theBlu'. Spider crabs, Snakes, Sharks, Dolphins, Whales and many more are here. The Great White startled me bigtime while I was in the cage. Now I know how Hooper felt lol. Great price too. Comes highly recommended!\t\t\t\t\t
