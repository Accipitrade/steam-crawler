\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, to be fair, I will start with some gripes - as I am absolutely in LOVE with the game. I gotta be critical of some things that bother me, before I sound off about how great being a wrecking ball in the shape of a ninja feels.

i5-4690K, GTX 1070, 8GB RAM, SSD install path.

I can play some of the more detailed levels, like Cemetery, with .2% Reprojection. Then, I get into some compound level that is nothing but hallways, and I'm reprojecting 50% of the time, and occasionally dropping frames. There is optimization work ahead of the team, but we are talking about Early Access, and those issues come with the turf.

Sometimes grabbing for a specific weapon doesn't work right. Mostly the shoulders in my experience. This can be refined.

Other than being able to crank SS wayyyy up on some levels, only to be left in Async Reprojection land, with the same settings, on a more basic / less detailed instance, and the weapon grabbing inconsistency of the shoulders, I am absolutely taken by this game.

I got a Vive for Black Friday, played through some Arizona Sunshine, wanted something with more RGB LEDs in it. Love Warframe, and got that kind of vibe from this titles promotional materials. I decided to buy it, expected to refund it. That said, when this game is locked to 90, it feels AMAZING! Totally VR Warframe! I went from tele locomotion, to jumping from wall to wall, following up wall runs / bounces with VR Backflips, landing on the ground and doing a groundpound. Every time I see a group of enemies, my mind FLOODS with ways I could dispatch them. Maybe I take a knee, dash forward, and use my momentum to SLIDE ON THE ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ FLOOR WITH PLASMA SWORDS CROSSED IN FRONT OF MY TORSO. I AM THE CUTTER OF TENDONS~! Maybe I strind teleports together with my a pistol in hand, triggering slomo, and living a moment in Tracer's shoes, while I pop up immediately behind an enemy, blow their brains out of their teeth before they can turn around, and teleport away to the next unfortunate goon! Maybe I could do a wall run, into a wall bounce off of a pillar, and do a backflip off of it, while drawing my bow, and firing an Arrow straight down an opponents spine! It's usually some kind of mix, to keep it fresh.

The game makes you feel like a god, until you crank up the difficulty in tandem with your growth as a player. It can become soul-crushingly relentless.

I started with little experience in VR, and the jumping, bouncing, sliding, and all of that funn stuff is easy to get used to with bullet time. I no longer need the time distortion to do any of that, however.

I am happy with my $30 spent. Some of the enemies are just... not animated well. The Sumo opponents, Assassination bots, and all of that come to mind right away. Some concepts aren't explained to you. What is that cone on the Sumo when he charges, does it mean anything? Are the bosses just anmnoyingly sponge-y, or is there something I am supposed to be doing to kill them faster? Do I care? Nah, just finna kill some more sentient Corsair Keysboards with swords, thanks. Looking forward to the Campaign, and other future updates. Cheers!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE 18/03/2018

Figured I should update this review. I've started playing VR again and since my last review the game feels so much more polished now. I can't really mention anything specific.. it is so many small things that add up to it feeling like a really nice game. I'm not sure if the AI has been tweaked but I'm struggling to pull off the same 'cheesing' as before. I'm really pleased with the progress that has been made with Sairento since EA and I would still recommend it thoroughly. Especially if you have that free oculus credit to spend.

PREVIOUS REVIEW

This is a brilliant game. I felt VR was under a bit of a lull for a while but this has reengaged my passion for it.

My only real complaint would be the AIs simplicity. It becomes easy to predict and is often incredibly stupid. I've been able to complete missions just by picking them off with a bow from a distance while they watch their comrades die and do nothing about it. Sometimes they like to stare at walls. Often you can cheese them a bit too easily by rushing to one side of the map, running them down, then rushing to the other.

That said, this is one of the best VR experiences out there right now. It can provide a true adrenaline rush in the higher difficulties, its a good workout, its challenging and theres so much fun to be had with your approach to missions.

The publishers here really need to step up their marketing because and I only heard of it a week ago. A few of my friends who have had VR for months haven't heard of it until now either... But that might be because it's early access. I think once major streamers and critics get their hands on this it will boom in popularity.

Unfortunately it's being buried under a flock of other lower quality VR games and demos right now. Make no mistake people, you need this game. The locomotion in this game is excellent, the weapons are immense fun to use and theres plenty of replay value.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAbsolutely excellent. Despite this game not having a story, I'm not going to knock it for that because it's EA.
So far, outside of BAM VR, this is one of the most visceral and entertaining VR games out there.
The combat is fluid, the enemies are varied enough to keep things fresh and everything comes together wonderfully.
Do keep in mind that I've got VR legs like no other, so if your a bit squeamish with some VR games, you might want to look elsewhere. This game involved LOTS of jumping and high speed movement, but that is what gives it appeal.
Oh, and... The melee. One of the best moments I've experienced in VR was with this game. I was blasting Waking the Cadaver and mimicking basic punches, elbows and neck snaps with the melee function. While it's not perfect, it takes this game to another level.
9.5/10.\t\t\t\t\t

-price

-maybe a bit more weapons would be cool
