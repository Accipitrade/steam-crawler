\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, I was looking for a decent shooter and basically heard about all VR shooters being bland and repetitive.

Thankfully, as I soon found out after purchasing this game, that it isn't true for this title.

The Good:
- The controls are incredibly intuitive and the jumping/movement system well-thought out.
- The action is fun, it shows that the game lives off a kind of coolness factor that you achieve jumping in front of an enemy and shooting him almost indifferently *POW* "you're dead".
- The skill system is pretty good, as well as the relic system to power up your guns.

But with all nice things, there are also downsides.
The Bad:
- After almost completing a wave, with one or two enemies left, it might get tedious to find the last bad guys. I'd recommend a visual highlighter once you reach 3 enemies left or similar.
- Sometimes, the jump path isn't perfect, leading to clipping through objects, stairs and floors.
- The mission rewards aren't given out automatically but locked in one of the chests around the level that opens upon completion. Please highlight those as well or at least make it known to the player that they might miss their loot.
- I reached level 25, and there aren't a lot of guns as of now. I hope that will change in the near future.

The Ugly:
- When guns collide with the other gun or with the environment, it might happen that they completely bug out and fly over the place, breaking the otherwise extremely well-executed immersion.

Despite its flaws, it was definitely worth purchasing and I'm looking forward to whatever might come in 2017.\t\t\t\t\t

The unique mobility usage (jumping), and the ability to equip and use a blade and gun simultaneously makes the combat more pleasing and varied than Raw Data, though it lacks a story, and the enemy types are currently very limited. Mixed Realms is headed in the right direction here, and for me, this game is well worth the asking price. Addressing the difficulty level, I'm not the best at FPS combat, and I am already playing on 3/4 difficulty, but there is certainly more room for balance. The bullet speed, enemy spawn rate, and enemy type variation all are changing factors, and stage difficulty increses with each level. I'm excited to see where this goes and how other combat VR games are influenced in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is an excellent example of what's possible with VR, and it's also fairly fleshed out for an EA game.

It uses a modified blink system that makes you realize you're actually locomoting through the world, but doesn't induce motion sickness (There was a brief feeling of it, but it went away after two minutes of using the controls).

It has a good array of weapons that I've seen so far (Unlocked 4 weapons so far after 3 hrs)

Even better, it really makes you feel like a badass cyberninja.


Currently, it's too easy, but I think I saw somewhere they lowered difficulty temporarily for reviewers(?)

Other issues:
Bullet time seems finicky (or at least poorly explained) to me. I squeeze the grip buttons while jumping, before jumping, before activating jump, etc, and I get it to fire maybe 10% of the time.

Killing with the sword requires no force. You just touch an enemy with it and they go down. Vanishing Realms VR has instituted this kind of mechanic where the controller needs to move a certain distance before actually inflicting damage.
I think the current mechanics can be improved via the same mechanic, as well as using the blink for momentum/force.

Conclusion:
It's a fantastic game already that is only going to get better. The soundtrack, the visual, the controls (with the exception of bullet time, I've found) are crisp, responsive, and intuitive. The gunplay is solid, but as with almost every VR game out there, the gun sights could be a little bigger/better\t\t\t\t\t
