\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou have real good vr legs, nothing upsets your balance or stomach in vr? You wanna know how it feels to be a sword and guns blazing ninja? Try this.

The gameplay is pretty fast, the enemies are deadly, but so are you. A first person hack and shoot fest. Jump and leap, bounce off walls, do some wall running, slice and dice enemies or pop a bullet in their head in slow motion. Like I said : you best have some good vr legs, but man, this can be a blast to play if you are into these things. Unlock different swords, guns and ninja your way though the maps. The game can be disorientating sometimes, but pretty decent in all the possibilities given.

Although not a game I have hundreds of hours of playtime with, I enjoyed it. Nothing really like it came out since.

There is a good amount of different enemies, each with their own attacks and weakness, although you will see a lot of them since there are so many.

The comfort settings are there, although you have the biggest freedom when you let go of the comfort IF you can stand it.

I would say, give it a try. Or check YouTube if you want a better picture on what to expect before purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is awesome. Simply put, it's one of the best VR games I've played. I was initially hesitant due to the games' slightly older age it might not feel as good as some other releases but I needn't have worried. The game is incredibly responsive and intuitive. The gameplay is highly enjoyable and there's a very compelling 'just one more mission' feel to the game.

Let's start with some positives:
+The game is HIGHLY customizable. You can tweak settings to your hearts content, including locomotion style (snapping or free move) choose automatic or manual reloads, 'sticky' weapons or manually gripped weapons, and oh yeah, the ability to backflip.
+Good variety of weapons including SMGs, swords, rifles, bows, and grenade launchers
+Level up/Relic System let's you increase weapon ability/general survivability as well as customize each weapon with a unique colored emission. There are perks and you can craft your own relics with resources gathered from missions.
+Missions are largely the same in terms moment to moment gameplay, mostly involving killing a certain amount of enemies, but have modifiers, different environments and reframings (wave survival, vs room clearing vs endless enemies +boss) that keep them fresh.
+Movement feels really good. There's jumping, teleporting, sliding, wall running and backflipping and everything feels responsive. Combined with bullet time, you just feel so, so cool while playing this game.
+Environments are pretty and enemies are distinct. There's a good number of maps.
+The team is still offering technical support for the game and responds quickly.

There are a few small negatives:
-Sometimes a hand (usually right for me) will get briefly stuck, either unable to grip a weapon or let go. It happens rarely and always fixes itself pretty fast (though quickly turning the controller on and off fixes the issue as well). However, this might be a personal controller issue.
-Character model height is a little bit off even after calibration. It's not by much and is a very minor issue that doesn't affect gameplay at all. Again, might be personal hardware issue.
-I got too into it during a session and smashed my finger against my closet door :(

Not negatives, but would have been awesome to have
+Environment climbing in a similar fashion as games like Blade and Sorcery. With all the traversal options, it would have been neat to grab ledges or swing off of things...but few games have such mechanics and with all the movement options in the game is basically not needed.
+Weight to melee. Not an issue for many enemies, but if an enemy takes multiple hits, melee loses a bit of wow factor.
+Cosmetic options (clothing colors) for player avatar, though the avatar model is nice as is. Def not essential, I just like customization :)

To conclude, some of the awesome I've done and you can do in this game
+Flip over a charging enemy, kill them in slow motion, land, slide, and gun down another enemy
+No look headshot an enemy with a bow
+Dash and slice an enemy akin to Overwatch's Genji
+Deflect bullets back at enemies
+Jump over an enemies attack and kill them.
+Hot swap weapons to get an opportune
+Many more to discover!

All in all, the game is currently a strong 9 to 9.5/10. With some more time with it. I can definitely see it becoming a full 10. If you've got VR hardware, this is one of the must plays.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought it on sale and haven't regretted a moment.

The good - dynamic, fast-paced, fairly intuitive, supports melee, ranged, semi-stealth, guns-blazing playstyles - and everything in-between. Easy to pick up, challenging to master, especially if you try trick kills and are using two weapons with different dynamics at once (like a pistol and a sword). Rather repetitive, but not boring, due to a lot of weapons and strategies available to you. The campaign story is nice, but awfully short, yet the replayability of random missions makes up for this.

The bad - outdated graphics, models, and animations. The city streets, textures, enemies - reminds me of early/mid 2000s games, not 2015+ games. Cannot stress this enough - lack of CONTROLLER STABILIZATION. Weapons don't feel like real guns and swords - more like weightless cardboard/cheap plastic toys - the shake makes it difficult to aim at large distances, esp.with sniper rifles, and takes away from immersion. Blade and Sorcery VR was also released in 2018 (I think), and it has a much better "feel" of weapons, both melee and ranged.

P.S. Hiro's legs kind of bend inwards when you try to duck - as if he desperately needs to use the bathroom.

P.P.S. Plasma sword's "V-R-R" sound is rather annoying - plays even if you're not swinging it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo Sairento had been sitting in my Steam library for awhile. When I first loaded it up, it felt clunky and unfinished, presenting itself more as a VR experiment than something I'd set out to play. I played a few randomized missions, tried the initial story mission or two, and then set it down for other offerings for a couple years.

... only to come back and give it another try when I specifically wanted a VR exercise game that I could use to get my heart rate up without plugging into Beat Saber for the hundredth time or leaving myself a sweating mess like Thrill of the Fight. Sairento fills that niche nicely, offering scalable intensity and a pretty good workout without absolutely destroying me on low energy days.

After reading some of the more recent reviews, I also realized I needed to turn the difficulty way, WAY up and turn on some of the more interesting features (smooth locomotion and backflips) to really get the intensity I was looking for. This will still not be for everyone, and for those who still get motion sick in VR, keeping teleporting on will be necessary here. But with these features on and in full force, the game gives a surprisingly satisfying workout and all of the good brain juices in a Superhot-meets-Warframe way.

That said, the experience is still pretty bare bones, and the lack of Workshop support means there are limits to how much fun you can have without serious investments in modding. This is also very clearly a horde AI model, so you're not going to find many intense fights against single opponents here. But the fun still shines through in the sheer quantity of enemies and action, and the game handles VR bugs, space dragging constraints, and Index controls tolerably well while I twist and turn in the air spraying bullets at virtual baddies.

Which is enough for me to toss in a recommend. There are a few extra weapons available as DLC too, and they're fun, but the base game should be all you need.\t\t\t\t\t
