\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe long and short of it is that it's a gameplay kind of game; story's pretty weak, but loads of fun to screw around in singleplayer.
Sairento VR lets you fight like a modern-ish ninja, with all kinds of guns, throwing weapons, and melee weapons that you can slot into five holsters and utilize on the fly. The combat and movement is a joy to take part in; Dash across the room into a knee slide and slash enemies as you swing by, then leap into the sky and front flip in slow-mo while shooting the enemies behind you, go right into a wall run and fire at the guys under you, all before leaping down and decapitation the remaining foe. It's just a lot of fun to pick a random mission on the map, go in and annihilate everything that moves with as much grace and fluidity and blood as you can.
That said, the game feels like it really has trouble running smoothly for more than thirty minutes at a time. I've got a gaming rig and by the time I'm an hour in, the frames get quite choppy and you can be firing an SMG while strafing and your bullets are firing out of air two feet away from you because the game is slowing down so much. It makes it hard to get a good ninja groove going when you feel like you have to turn it off after a short playtime just so it can stop having a panic attack and work properly again.
The story? Bland, boring, and barely there; used just as a framing device to somewhat explain why massacring tons of cyborg ninjas is in any way rational or believable. Weak in both character and narrative, it's something you plod through just to get to the end bit where you're free to pick missions on the city map and screw around.
Another important aspect of the game is the RPG Skill Trees, where you can specialize in different weapon types and character traits like Loot collecting, Chi, mobility, or others. Though I'll caution you; prospective buyer, if you plan to play on the higher and higher difficulties, ignore the Health skill tree as it is a noob trap. On the highest difficulty (Shinobi), all hits are one-hit kills. Doesn't matter if you have 100 health or 1000, you're dead in one hit. Need to focus on dodging and leaping around to avoid damage entirely. There are certain skills that let you negate a death once per minute, which is very useful and a must-have for higher tier levels. Another issue is you only get so many normal skill points, then you get Prestige Points after level 100 to tweak numbers, like adding .25% damage to pistols. So you can't get even half of the skill trees maxed out, forcing you to commit to a rough strategy.
Combined with the weapons and armor is an upgrade system, using Relics. Each weapon has three slots to put in enhancers to your guns, with all the basic kinds like fire rate, magazine size, damage, etc. However, there are also interesting unique modifiers that can change how you use weapons. Like an infinite ammo mod for a pistol, or one that makes your sword dual-bladed, or silencing a rifle shot for stealth killing, or ricochet arrows, or explosive arrows. Armor has less slots depending on which body part, and there's no way to pick up new armor or appearance mods, just gameplay enhancements. Like changing your wall running and bouncing into wall grabbing, letting you sneak high above your enemy and pick them off from above, or quadruple jumps for your boots. All these modifications can drastically change your gameplay style and offer loads of fun.
Maps are fairly cool and different, with procedural tile-based level design for the non-story missions. A surprising variety in fact. A graveyard here, a train there, a city district and more. You'll soon be encountering them over and over when you play late game, but it's still more than most may expect.
I haven't touched multiplayer, mostly because it's dead. I haven't seen any parties or lobbies or anything whenever I pop on it. So I can't say how co-op ninja-ing is.
Like I said at the top, it's loads of fun for gameplay (if in small bursts due to game processing problems), and boring if you're here for plot. Definitely buy it on sale to have something really cool to mess around in on VR.\t\t\t\t\t
