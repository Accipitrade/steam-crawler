\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn my humble opinion this game had always a great gameplay since starters: the idea of a CyberneticNinja.

You have full locomotion option and you could jump very easily by aiming where you want to land (of course you have also the safe teleport option for players that might get sick).
The thing is that by jumping, while you are in the air you start to handle other movements, like jumping to a wall and start running through walls, or you could crouch in the real life and you will land and start sliding.
You could level up your character to add additional jumps, or other skills. One of those skills is the ACCELERATION ability. It slows the time and this mixed with all the previous acrobatics will make you faster than JET LI kicking ass in action movies .....and IT FREAKING WORKS, it is really fun.

From starters you could choose whatever weapon you want. If you prefer a silent approach you have kunais (similar to a small knife), shurikens, a silent bow, and of course some swords hehehe...
If you prefer to unleash bullets you have revolvers, rifles, smg, snipers, shotguns.
And you could mix your load using different slots in your body so you could swap easily in the middle of many kills hehehe
(all those weapons have unique ways to handle them using the VR controllers)

You evolve your player gaining experience and random loot that you invest to improve certain values of the previous weapons and your skills.
Right now I'm enjoying a lot the ACCELERATION abilities so I'm evolving that skill tree but also I enjoy a mixed gameplay between sword, the bow, and smgs...so I'm also placing some points on those weapons.

This is a FULL game and has a main campaign (I'm on level 6 now) but also has a way to generate random missions that you could play in the middle of the campaign too if you prefer to gain more experience and improve certain things....but besids that...the missions are fun because of the kills you start using and mastering (I really tried some locations just for the fun and keep coming back)
Also let me tell you that you will end up testing all the weapons to test different gameplay styles.

Now this game is not perfect, but is getting better and better. In the middle it had some bad reviews, because the game initial visuals were not the best, but those were updated and improved (a lot). Also the game had some bugs during some of these updates but so far the devs are following the game closely and they fixed all those gradually.

Right now I have to recommend it because the game gives you a fun gameplay and you enjoy it while you start learning....

Still the game could be improved a little more, and DEVS are doing this. Today while I write this they added a new level location already that is being tested.

Also the game has multiplayer support but I have to be honest and tell that I didn't tested it because I want to finish the campaign first.
Also played several random missionsI testing all the weapons on different builds....just for the euphoric moments.

For example: I learned to do a jump against the wall and raise both hands, the ninja does a Backflip.
So, at that instant, I activated the ACCELERATION and when everything is in slow motion upside down, I aimed my bow to a close enemy and made an inverted shoot with an arrow to his head (awesome effect!). Just before landing, I crouched myself to land sliding and pull my sword to cut another enemy in the middle during the slide .... wooooooooooooooow.

For me Sairento is an 8/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSariento tries hard, has a lot of fun ideas, but is another proof that today's VR title tend to lack polish. After 45 minutes of playing the game, I had to go back to the store page and confirm that I hadn't actually bought an early access title.

It itself, the interface isn't the most user friendly, which could be saved by the gameplay if it weren't ridden by bugs: teleported out of the map at the end of missions, enemies sometimes spawning while I'm being brieffed and movement locked (unskipable), but sometimes freezing in place mid or end movement...

I could look beyond the clunky ground locomotion system to focus on jumping, but even then the poor detection of my holster and sabers, coupled to the conter intuitive way guns are reloaded actually add to the general clunk. I know there aren't control standards for VR in the way they are for other games, but still... clunk.

After having played several hours of Robo Recall, which seems to get mostly everything right and is free with the oculus touch, having to pay for a title which still feels like in early beta is disappointing.

Not recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFantastic first person shooter with jumping, wall-running, sliding and bullet time. There's a cyberpunk atmosphere, Ghost in the Shell-like, enhanced by some nice music.
Beside the story campaign are modes with randomly generated missions and you can host tailored games with drop in/out play. Chapters can be replayed in any order and on several difficulties.
Levelling up and finding loot for mod slots keeps you coming back for more. There's even some basic crafting. You can save gear loadouts.
I have had some clipping through walls but you just respawn and don't lose progress. Funnily enough you can move around outside and eventually find a way to jump back inside.
After the first car which looks like it was drawn with a crayon the visuals really pick up. You get to see your arms, legs and shadow, too.
Gameplay becomes increasingly rewarding.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy new favorite Vive game. Got some hours in on it so far and am impressed. Not unnecessarily difficult, not ridiculously easy. The tech trees and item augmentation systems are nice, the loot system is pretty decent, and the game is a lot of fun to play. The controls are pretty intuitive and easy to use, too.

It does have some technical glitches, however. I have a decent gaming PC (water cooled dual 1080 Ti, i7 extreme, etc.). However, this game stutters a lot in certain situations involving some of the bosses.

There's also some wall clipping issues that have forced me to restart a level a couple of times because I either get stuck or end up outside the level.

All of that said, these issues don’t take away from the experience, which is one of the best so far.

Suggestion to devs: Make auto reload on by default. Highlight the tabs in the bottom of the loadout screen… I didn’t realize they were there until after an hour or so into playing. Probably would be helpful if there were some guidance on the loadout/item mods for a short period instead of throwing people right into it.\t\t\t\t\t
