This is a very good game for the price it have.
It`s been a long time that I don`t play fps games. In computer, to play with mouse it is too limited. Shooting with your hands while looking to the direction that you need to see is a very good sensation.
The strongest point in Sairento is the quantity of available weapons, summed with the upgrades that you can use. You have access to all weapons since the begining of the game, but with time you can upgrade and even change the hability of weapons. The only thing that lacks in the game is an explosive weapon, for damage or for status for enemies. But considering the variety of weapons, it is a minor detail.
The enemies are extreme different for each other. You have to fight since the basic enemy with pistol until a flying woman with area damage. Not forgetting the sumo guy, running to you to break your bones. My favourite enemy.
The bad thing is some glitches (you cant teleport with the left hand in the same way with the right, sometimes you go out of the scenario, etc) and the poor variety of scenarios.\t\t\t\t\t

If you don't like all of that stuff, you can grab an assault rifle, 2 pistols and an smg and shoot your enemies. There are relics which grant all kinds of awesome abilities and that you continue to get as you level up. One is subspace ammo, for the pistol which grants infinite ammo, another is ghost blade which makes it so each consecutive strike makes your blade reach longer. It can increase 5x. Then you have defraction that turns the plasmablade into a dual sided darth maul light saber. Or singular will that lets you control your throwable glaive(like a giant shield thing) weapon in mid air so that you really go mad with it. I only mention these because as you customize your character, they become so much more powerful and it is so much more satisfying when you are doing off the wall crazy crap like running along walls deflecting shotgun shots into people's faces or running along walls headshotting all of the guys along the ground. In short, this game is worth it and is a must have if you have VR.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis long rambling review split in two for your perusing convenience.

~~~The Important Stuff~~~

The Gameplay: Sairento is a game that provides an excellent example for having one trick that you do really well. There's not a lot of different types of gameplay on offer here, it's all combat. But the combat is EXCELLENT, the movement is fluid and allows you to pull off some really awesome acrobatics without it ever causing nausea. I'm honestly amazed at how well implemented it is, I think every other VR game I've played so far has had a movement system that was both simpler and more disorienting, so bravo. The weapons are all really fun and satisfying to use, they all feel unique and some of the more distinct weapons like the glaive (chakram-like throwing disc), bow, and the kusarigama from the DLC have some really cool functionality. This game really excels in making you feel like a cyberpunk ninja. It also has a nice progression system that alows you to augment your skills (wall-run longer, slide further, jump an absurd number of times in the air) and between it and the healthy armoury there are plenty of ways to approach combat, so it never gets too repetitive.

The Campaign: Sairento tells a short story of conspiracy in the Sairento organization, the cyber-ninja elite protectors of Neo-Japan. It is fully voice acted in English (that might be obvious, but it's something I was a little concerned about since the developers are from Singapore) and I thought the cast did a really solid job, especially the actors for the two main characters; the PC Chieko and her mentor Broke. I'd say the same goes for the storytelling, not masterclass, but very very solid. I did see the "big twist" coming pretty early in, and I think anyone familiar with cyberpunk fiction probably will too, but it wasn't a case of "this is cliche" so much as "I wonder if they're going in this story direction? that would be neat" and they were, and it was. The campaign is quite short, not counting the interludes where you complete a few arcade-style missions before doing the next story mission, I'd say the campaign took me about four hours. It ends a little abruptly as well, you finally meet the big bad face to face, and it seems like it's setting up a third act, but that's actually just a framing device for continuing the arcade missions. It works, it's just a little underwhelming. All in all, I'd say if you go in expecting the length and quality of a good cyberpunk short story (not novel or novella) you'll be perfectly satisfied.

~~~Thoughts on Other Aspects of the Game~~~

The Maps: I would put the maps n Sairento in to two categories; the indoor ones (observatory, lab, etc.) and the outdoor ones (temple, streets, etc.). The former are a little bland, just a bunch of hallways, they provide good play spaces, lots of things to run along and backflip off of (hold your hands over your head as you jump off a wall, it's pretty awesome) but not terribly interesting to look at. The latter are quite a bit better, they do a really nice job of capturing that cyberpunk "high-tech low-life" aesthetic, with lots of holographic advertisements for fictional companies all over the place, and even a few references and in-jokes. All in all I'd say there is a good balance and variety. My biggest complaint is that all the maps are kind of closed in, even the ones that are outside are mostly narrow streets, so you never get a sense of scale of the city, and there's no point where you get a really good view of the neon skyline of Neo-Tokyo, it's just glimpsed through windows. Far from a deal breaker, but I was a little disappointed by that.

The Art and Visual Design (UPDATED): I'm a sucker for the cyberpunk aesthetic, and I really like the art direction for Sairento. As I mentioned above, the indoor maps are a little underwhelming, but the outdoor ones are cool. The enemy and weapons designs are all really interesting, there's a lot of detail to cybernetics on enemies and everything is highlighted with neon colours. The enemies and weapon designs are all visually distinct and have a lot of personality. UPDATE: There was a recent update that overhauled the visual design of the Street and Alley maps, which were previously a tad underwhelming, and now they're both gorgeous, plus there have been two new maps added both of which I really like. So far the devs have been doing a really good job communicating with the community, fixing bugs, and improving the games features (there are even MORE acrobatics options now then when I first wrote this review).

The Setting: Again, I'm a sucker for cyberpunk, and I am intrigued by the setting of Sairento. Unfortunately there are no points where you can explore and dig through e-mails for lore, but there are some hints at a wider world worked into the dialogue. Cybernetics regulation laws, wars past and present, it's not much, but I really appreciated what was there, and it makes me want to know more about this world.

Nitpicks (UPDATED): Just a few things that annoyed me a little bit. First of all, unlike most VR games, in Sairento you can't pick anything up that isn't one of the weapons you have on your person, or really interact with the world in those intuitive ways that VR permits (outside of combat). It's certainly far from a deal breaker, but it just feels to me like it isn't taking full advantage of the technology. For example, in your dojo (the hub/menu) there is a tea set that I keep trying to pick up, but I can't, and it's a little disappointing. Building from that, the dojo seems a little superfluous, it has a bridge and a second room, and a few decorations, but all in all it's pretty bare, and nothing does anything except the holographic screen where you adjust your settings, equipment, and choose missions. It's still neat that it's there, and it help with immersion, it just feels kind of... empty. Finally, my last nitpick; if you turn on subtitles (which I do so I don't miss any dialogue) instead of fixing the subtitles in space, the game treats it like you're on a computer monitor and has them at the bottom of your vision. However because they move with your head, the subtitles are always on the edge of your vision, and very hard to focus on, it's actually quite headache inducing. I've seen this in other VR games as well, and I can't imagine how anyone thought it was a good idea to implement subtitles this way. Why not use speech bubbles? Is that more difficult to program than I think? Probably. Anyway, I did find a workaround, which is that if you look down, the subtitles kind of collide with the ground, and move up in your field of vision, so that's what I did, whenever someone was speaking, I looked down (or you could just not turn subtitles on). UPDATE: A recent update fixed the subtitle issue by adding the option to display subtitles on your wrist-mounted computer, so make sure you do that, and thank you devs.

~~~Wrap it Up You Long Winded So-and-So~~~

So that's my long rambling thoughts on this game. All in all I would absolutely recommend it to anyone who enjoys action games and considers themselves any kind of fan of cyberpunk or martial arts fiction. If it's on sale at all, definitely jump on it. Even if it isn't on sale I'd say still go ahead and buy it, you'll definitely get your moneys worth, and I'd say the devs have definitely earned the support. While your at it, I'd recommend the weapons pack DLC as well. I just bought it and tried it out, and the kusarigama alone is worth the just under three quid. It introduces new mechanics, working differently from any other weapon in the game, and it's an absolute blast to swing around. The kigiri is fun as well, I was a bit colder on the Slugger, but hey, another SMG.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the type of game I bought VR for, leaping around dual weilding swords, running along walls and slicing enemies legs off.
Its an amazing concept, but very poorly executed and suffers from poor game design. Right off the bat you find yourself leaping to slash at enemies only to find that they immediately back away. Realistic reaction sure, but it kills the game play.

The first tip off was the splash screen for the game. Where the studio title was a 2D video.

Grabbing guns from your waist and back, and swords from your back are implimented in the most basic of ways. Having played Arizona Sunshine and Robo Recall for the occulus comparing the eqiuping in this game, feels laggy and forced and slow like dragging a block of wood through sand. The former games are very smooth, with equiping and you can feel when you have equiped something. With Robo Recall the bullet deflecting is challenging but also fluid and easy to execute. Moving a gun to your hip in some cases will reload the weapon. which is anoying if you are trying to put it way to grab something else.

But the main downside for me was the feeling of it all. Leaping across a map landing next to an enemy slashing with your sword only to have that enemy take one step back to be out of range. Realistic reaction? Yeah probably, but I came here to feel like a powerful ninja cutting dudes left and right. This is less of a challenge and more of an annoyance.

You also find yourself being surrounded by enemies early on, and most of the time its just fodder. Slicing through someone does not feel like slicing through someone.
And thats the point of VR to feel like you are somewhere.

All in all this game is not worth the price tag.


Edit Response to Developer Response:

It's still not worth the price point. This is a $10 at most. And I am not going to buy it again to give it another shot.its buggy, the multiplayer is laggy and desolate of players, and gameplay is not smooth. Level design is aweful from what I have seen. The most interesting level design was the tutorial.

The AI is extremely easy and unchallenging. I never said it was too difficult. I just said the way the AI reacts to some situations is against what the core gameplay is. ITs bad game designs. I don't know why you would have melee enemies cosntantly walking backward swinging their sword. while guys with shot guns just stand there next to you to keep shooting and have no self preservation. It should be the other way around.


2021 Update:

I got this game in a humble Bundle, and decided to give it a try with my new hardware. Everything above still stands. It's bad game design and just another wave shooting with mediocre level design.\t\t\t\t\t
