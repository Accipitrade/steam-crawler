The game can get repetitive if you don't mix it up abit yourself (Increase difficulty, try new weapons / talents etc). There also needs to be some sort of boss fight(s). The Daimyo feels more like a mini boss as it stands.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis wasn't the first VR game I purchased, but it was one of the first ones I thought REALLY showed promise as great example of what a VR game that wasn't afraid to let you "move around" could do. It got away from the early "wave shooter shovelware" issue that was (and honsetly still kind of does) plague the current offering of VR games, and I liked that a lot.

I hadn't played for a while, and I know the game is still in early access but I let the devs spend some time cooking the idea, adding some vairety, etc. and I fired this back up today to see how things are going.

Initially, I felt that it went from perhaps a bit on the easy side to just too far in the other direction, and was a bit too overwhelmning. I did feel the earlier builds let you take a bit too much damage, it wasn't really realistic. But now, you just get bombarded with enemies on all sides and killed without any real chance to understand what is happening before you die.

After a few minutes, I was able to figure out that you CAN just ninja hop everywhere, liek a crazy person, to try and avoid death, fire off a shot or two, dash away again... lather rinse repeat. I Had figured out how to (mostly) not die, but realized very quickly the game was a terrible experience when played this way.

I'm not one to knock a game just for being difficult, I did find the early builds a bit easy but this didn't feel any better in terms of actually having fun and felt like it would be a bad initial experience.

The movement controls are still great and enjoyable. It just feels like, instead of really trying to figure out how to make the existing game a bit more challenging, they just bombard you with more enemies.

EDIT: After some time to consider feedback from others, I went back in and gave it another shot, I was able to get much further and I figured out how to get back into a groove with this game. I Have updated my review and my recommendation of the game. The devs have made some very slight tweaks I think do make a difference without nerfing the enemies too much. And I'm sure the higher difficulties are still challenging. I'm looking forward to the campaign mode!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing this game for nearly a year and as my collection of VR games grows bigger I come back to Sairento everytime. There are quite a few quality VR action games out there that have sword play and gunplay in them, but none of them give me the level of satisfaction I get from playing Sairento. The action is so good. The wall running and jump sliding makes you feel super agile and when you start feeling confident you can even start playing around with back flips and cartwheels off the walls for fun. No other game I've played gives me that level of freedom. Most games don't let you jump at all, let alone soar through the air like a super ninja. Zipping around in bullet time blocking bullets and slashing enemies is so much fun. Even the gunplay with revolvers is solid and satisfying. I feel like a gunslinging super ninja playing this. I can't recommend this game enough. My current favorite VR action game. Probably to eventually be replaced by the next Mixed Realms game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - buy buy buy

Longer version: This game is what room-scale VR is all about. Satisfying movement, bullet-time slowdowns, open environments, great weapons, variety... it's terrific. This is the game that you would have designed as a 10 year old, running around with your nerf guns and your buddies pretending to be ninjas.

The weapons are incredibly varied, from pistols and SMGs that you'd expect to explosive kunai and bouncing shuriken. Don't like how a gun plays? Get some mods and change it.

There is not enough end-game content to call this game finished, but even as it stands, it is a hard buy if you have a VR headset. There are a ton of shooters out there, but none of them are this fun. FUN. Seriously, this game is fun. Great job, devs, keep up the good work!\t\t\t\t\t
