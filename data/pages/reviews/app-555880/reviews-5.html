\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI know a lot of people will disagree with me. But I think the state of the game / the movement reminds of the first VR games out there. It's just aiming and teleporting to the point where you want to go, with two options: aiming straight to "dash" or aiming up to jump. Please correct me if I'm wrong... but I also don't see an option to change the movement.

EDIT: Thanks for the correction and therefore: Yes, there is a way to change the movement which makes this game feel way better to play due to the "movement flow". As I now tried it with the other movement, I feel much more comfortable.

The story reminds me of a demo, or a try to implement a complete storyline in a fighting game...which I don't think really works with these kinda games. The only thing you do is get missions / told to kill some guys in your way; for me this does not make a full story. But there are a lot of games like this one that work the same when it comes to the "story". My advice is looking it up and getting a viewpoint from someone who played it.

It is a cool and fun game that really feels good when fighting. I would recommend it for those who like to slash some ai and want to have a good time feeling like a ninja. BUT I would wait until there is a sale for -at least- 20 bucks or so. Nevertheless you can give it a try and find out for yourself :)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's mission-based VR Borderlands.

You get equipment of varying types and damage values, there's upgrades to your abilities, and SMGS dominate everything, just like in Borderlands 2. Melee combat is occasionally hit or miss. One-hit-kills always feel good, but when a melee attack doesn't kill an enemy they kinda just... freak out? You can swing a melee weapon faster than they can react. I don't know if it's possible to balance this.

The slowmo mechanic is what makes the game, and elevates it to a level almost beyond anything else I think I've every touched in VR. It's incredibly close to SUPERHOT VR. Weirdly enough, I think the complexity with weapons turned me off from coming back over and over again. Haven't played in a long time (over a year now), for some reason Steam says I've barely played the game after ~200 hours.\t\t\t\t\t
