\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is really fun. While it's not perfect, it's extremely engaging and there's always something fun to do.

-Cons
Throwing weapons extremely difficult to aim. There IS an AIM assist stat that you can increase with gear and maybe some high level gear or skill dramatically changes things, but for the most part I've found these weapons to be wholly ineffective. Your best strategy is always going to be pistols/smgs or swords.

There are stealth mechanics but no real way to engage in stealth beyond the first few enemies. I would think that if you could leap directly to an enemy with your sword drawn it should initiate a stealth kill sequence. Murdering people in one room alerts enemies in another room which is also kind of annoying since it essentially means once you alert anyone the entire map is on alert. You can run away but the missions are timed and taking too long detracts from your score.

Sometimes the AI stops responding for some enemies, this is particularly an issue when you've killed everyone else and a guy is tucked in a corner aware like he's playing hide the pylon in StarCraft and you have to hunt for him for l ike 5 minutes. There are skills to help you detect enemies from farther away but these shouldn't be necessary. Once the number of enemies dwindles to a small number they should just be permanently visible.

-Pros
Action is intense, lots of difficulty options, lots of loot to collect and skills to level up. Honestly aside from the issues I mentioned above everything else about this game is fantastic and it's definitely a great game for your library even if you're a total novice at VR the easy mode difficulty makes it possible for anyone to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe controls are decent and movement feels fluid and mostly natural (though if you're prone to motion sickness, you should probably pass). However that's about all this game has going for it.

Hit detection is very lacking - there were a number of enemies in the first level where I had to swing my sword through them three or four times before it finally registered a hit, and since each enemy takes two or more hits to die, they had a ton of extra time to shoot me dead. This problem also made the one-on-one sword fights with other samurai enemies very frustrating, since I would move to block a slash and yet they hit me anyways. Aiming your guns is similarly annoying, as they feel wildly inaccurate unless you're in the slo-mo mode. And about 3 minutes into my first mission I literally fell through the floor as if I had noclip on. Hit detection needs a lot of work.

And about the theme of the game - you play as a cool, future ninja lady. Problem is, I didn't feel like a ninja at all. If you aggro one enemy, every single other enemy in the room starts chasing you, regardless of whether they were feasibly in earshot or not. Maybe once you've learned the game's controls a bit better, this type of fight is cool - but for a beginner, fighting 10 enemies at the same time is frustrating and confusing. I guess if I play a ninja-based game, I expect at least a little bit of stealth being a viable option.

Overall, I do not recommend this game unless you're already pretty good at this genre in VR to begin with.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a pretty good game. I reccomend getting i while it is on sale though since there are certainly flaws with it.

I will start with the pros. First off the movement and combat is fun. If you are in to cyberpunk/anime you will absolutely love this game. It feels like I am a soul reaper from the anime Bleach flash stepping. As someone who practices HEMA this combat is not realistic at all, but it is not meant to be. Even for me as a martial artist it is fun to play like GORN.

However there are flaws with the game. The biggest one I could find was that the combat was not as immersive as GORN. It still felt like I was holding a controller in my hand I did not get the impression that I was holding anything beyond visuals. The stabbing is also kind of weird but this game is more of a hack and slash /shooter anyway so I don't mind that.

This game has potential without a doubt. If it just felt more immersive I would say it is worth every dime. Also the developer of this game has been great with helping me fix a major bug I came across which prevented me from playing the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGreat VR game with some gear/level progression and uniquely fun locomotion. Of all the VR experiences I’ve tried in the last few months, this one made me feel the most badass I’d say, It is also a great way to burn calories as it gets pretty intense at times. Not coincidentally, it is also the game that made me decide to get some rubber covers for my Vive wands because I wind up smacking things fairly often in my cramped space when using melee weapons.

The gameplay itself in this game is pretty fantastic. If you ever wanted to get a taste of what it would be like to be in the Matrix, this is probably a decent analog. The ability to wall run, triple jump and slow time at will while wielding weapons of your choosing makes for a crazy ninja experience. Once you get a feel for it, you’ll be bouncing all over the place, dodging bullets and decapitating stuff in no time. I haven’t finished the story yet but it seemed passable enough, but you’re not really going into this game for a gripping plot, it is more about the gameplay experience.

While killing stuff, enemies will drop relics and other useful resources that can be used to upgrade and specialize your weapons or improve your chances at loot on missions. Aside from just stat boosts like more damage or life on hit, you can also find legendary relics that add unique and powerful perks to the weapon or armor they are applied to. You’ll find things like explosive tipped or homing arrowheads, gloves that let you hang onto the walls or give free bullet time when doing a ground slam, sword relics that improve damage while sliding, things like that to upgrade your gear to fit your preferred playstyle.

You’ll also be gaining experience the whole time and leveling up. Leveling gives skill points that you can use to further enhance your method of play. You can be a stealthy, no guns sort of cyber assassin or and insane acrobatic guns blazing, akimbo style madman, it is really up to your preference. You can even carry enough weapons (5 slots total) to have mix up your gameplay within a single mission if you want.

I have had some performance issues with it, mostly centering around the DLC kusarigama and the sumo wrestler enemy type. In one case it seemed like the weapon didn’t retract to my hand and was stuck in the map causing the physics engine to grind itself to a halt nearly causing me to hurl. But also any time the sumo’s armor blocks attacks the game really seems to struggle on my RX480. Admittedly, my rig is 7 years old at this point so this might not be a factor on higher end systems. It hasn’t been enough of a problem to keep me from enjoying the game either way.

I’ve barely played 1 round of the multiplayer co-op, so I can’t comment much there. I did get a glimpse of the slow-time system in place for multiplayer though. Any time you use the bullet time, it creates a bubble around you in which time is slowed for everything in the bubble. Meanwhile, outside that bubble other people/enemies/projectiles are still moving around at normal speed. It seemed to work well enough in my limited experience.

Definitely worth picking this one up if you have a bit of room to swing your arms around like a madman and want to feel like the ultimate cybernetically enhanced badass.
