\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is seriously the greatest VR game that I've ever played. You play as a genetically and cyber-enhanced assassin in a dystopian future, fighting against terrorism and power abusers. You have tons of weapons at your disposal, swords, assault rifles, pistols, bows, giant shurieken, and so much more. Combat is exciting, you can choose to dodge or deflect bullets with your sword. You can counterattack with your variety of weapons, cutting enemies up, mowing them down in a hail of bullets...

You have abilties to super jump and slow time, but enemies can teleport and super jump, too, so be careful. It feels like you're out of an action movie when you leap through the air, slow down time and cut down enemies as you sail. There's a variety of gameplay modes, Purge where you fight from room to room, Assassination where you have to take down one 'boss' and all its minions, Wave Survival (self-explanatory), and there's a pretty intriguing story to go along with it.

There's even multiplayer, although I haven't tried it yet, because I think I'd get completely slaughtered in it. I'm sure it would be even more exciting, as you're not fighting against a slow computer, but the active minds of human beings.

This is totally worth every dollar I paid for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first discovered the pre-release demo version of this via my Viveport subscription and it was by far the most fun game I tried out in this manner. Naturally I had to buy the full product and I wasn't disapointed. Though the gameplay is harder and a little more complicated than the demo it has a lot more variety and longievity.

This is basically an arena/room clearing combat game. It utilises various guns, swords, thrown weapons and bows all of which have unique enhancements and provide for a variety of play styles. You can also slow time and do a variety of stunts such as sliding and wall running. These last two aspects are something added to what is essentially the teleport mechanic of the game. Instead of directly teleporting you leap and slide at high speed in such a way that seems to cause absolutely no nausea and maintains immersion. When you target somewhere to jump on occasion you are shown that you will bounce off in a certain direction or initiate a wall run. You can also squat down as you land to initiate a slide at the end, so if you ever wanted to John Woo yourself across a room on your knees firing off dual SMG's and laying waste a group of enemies, here is your chance!

This game is just so much fun. One problem I had though is that whenever I tried to play multi-player there was no one to play with. This is likely a mixture of being in the UK (timezone issues) and there just not being that many VR players in general. However, that's fine as I generally prefer single player anyway. But if you wanted advice on iff the MP aspect is any good I'm afraid I can't give that.

VR Ratings
-----------
Atmosphere = 3 - Generally looks good, the music and the locations feel authentic but that's about it.
Interaction = 5 - Simply amazing and one of the best combat VR games I've played.
Content = 3 - Multiple modes, multi-player, stores and power ups. Actually pretty solid for an arena game.
Replay = 4 - I expect the game will eventually get old, but I can see it being my go-to combat game for a long time.

Total = 4/5 - One of the best VR games on the market.\t\t\t\t\t
