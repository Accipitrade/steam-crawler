\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow...so freaking cool. I can't even beat the first level, and I don't care. Leaping, shooting, wall running, panic reloads, OH NO, A GREEN SAMURAI! It's a really fun game, that will tax your abilities.

Raw Data blew my mind, Arizona Sunshine held my attention, the grind of Dead Effect 2 kept me coming back for more (and more, and more). I think Sairento is going to be a perfect meld of all 3 with it's own incredible (and surprisingly easy to pick up) platforming on top.

Another must have for Vive owners.

UPDATE: I have never added on to a review before, but must tell you, this is the single coolest game on Vive right now, IMHO. I don't know how the devs, have done it, but they have made, wall running, triple jumping, sliding, shooting, reloading on the fly, anime-style "fly-by" melee, and much more all accessible to a middle aged gamer.

The game is still early access, some glitching happens (clipping through walls has happened a lot). AI is simplistic. There is no campaign, yet, but none of this detracts.

IF you have your VR legs, you MUST buy this game, this is what we all wanted from video games from day one.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot for nothing, and it is probably a nitpick most people will simply bash me for, but the reason I refunded this game was because of the model. Not that having a model is bad; but the fact is, I have long arms.

I could not find a single option to increase arm length on the model, which led to me misjudging jumps, shots and slices for the swords. This caused me to end up looking more like the cyber ninja with poor depth perception than a badass warrior.

Also, due to the fact that VR is still in that stage of figuring out most of what it wants to do, the melee leaves much to be asked for. Not that its hard to figure out, the sharp end goes in the bad man, but its got that issue almost all melee VR titles have; which is basically that the most effective and destructive move you can do with your swords is to poke them with it and jiggle the handle really fast to kill them.

This, again, makes it hard to suspend your disbelief when you basically become the sonic tickle monster who slips into enemy compounds and makes the enemies giggle to death.

In all, I bet this game would be great some. Moving felt fluid and neat, which if you don't get motion sick easy is super satisfying to master. But for me, its a no; even if its for a fairly nitpicking reason. But unlike non VR games; I have to connect a bit with what I'm supposed to be. And if my arms stop almost a foot before my real arms do, it really messes with me.

Solid 7.5/10 for people without giant mungo arms.

Edit to positive: Sorry for the late edit here but even though I've not repurchased this game yet I'd like to thank the devs for adding a long arms mode to their game. Stuff like that is what makes good devs, great devs. Will certainly be repurchasing this in the future!\t\t\t\t\t
