This is simply my favorite VR game so far. (HTC Vive)

Good :\t
+ Feels rewarding to play
+ Weapons variety (from dual pistols to shurikens, even a godamn bow with a multi-arrow mode)
+ Rich & smooth gameplay (slide, run on walls, backflips, all of that in slowmo)
+ The progression system makes you want to play again and again
+ Armor and Weapons upgrades (you can loot relics to fit on your stuff !)
+ Difficulty settings : the more difficult, the more XP and Loots you get, truly satisfying
+ Very good graphics in game, you don't 'feel' the pixels


Bad :\t
- big FPS drop on big maps, but most of the time it's perfectly fine (i7 8700k, GTX 1080, 16Go DDR4, SSD)
- that's it

Can't wait for the 18th's final release, campaigns incoming !
GOOD JOB DEVS !
This game alone makes my Vive totally worth it.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI’ve been buying lots of VR games since I got my vive. Many games seemed to be “the best yet” to me... and now it’s Sairento VR.

People kept talking bout the game and said how it’s totally cool and fun to play... I then checked videos and screenshots and decided to ignore it as it looked bad and boring. Finally, when FO4VR, Doom VFR came and went on I now decided to give Sairento a try. I really wish I would have tried this earlier (a demo would help).

Even though u use teleportation the movement in this game is insane and very smooth. I am at about 9 hours of game time now and I really love the game. I can do 3 jumps in mid-air, slow down time and just slash everything in my way. I like the music too, but decided to listen to my own music and I am having a total blast with the game. Can’t wait for the next game session. I highly recommend this game and I hope there will be more maps and enemies soon. Later this month the campaign mode will be released and I hope it will be as good as the missions we can currently do.

Awesome game. Get it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of my VR titles I keep coming back to. The movement is fluid and fast and it does what all best practices for VR tell you shouldn't be possible. Jumps, dashes, slides, double jumps, back flips. Go to bullet time, skip along a wall, do a backflip and shoot a cyber ninja. Finish off his colleague by sliding past him, sword extended. This is definitely a skill based game, the more you play it, the better you get at stringing cool moves together.

Loot and upgrade path for your character are very extensive. Weapons feel satisfying and diverse.

Devs are very active, seems like every time I boot it up, they've added some new cool features. In the past there have been some bugs for certain updates, but it is shaping up to be a very good title. Absolutely playable and enjoyable at the moment.

If you want to see how far current VR can be pushed gameplay wise; check it out. Graphics are rather more functional than gorgeous, but that's not the reason to play; feeling like a cyber ninja is.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't say enough about how awesome this game is, but sadly I had to return it... and not because of the game itself, but because of my VR layout.

The variety of weapons, enemies, progression systems, and other gameplay aspects were phenomenal. Some of the mechanics in the game were things I never even imagined possible in VR from a controls perspective. Now granted I ran into one or two tiny EA issues like an FPS drop here and there or a very minor controller glitch, but in the time I played (about an hour or two) I was hooked until I ran into one thing.... literally....

The game uses a very close perspective for melee combat which is a huge part of the game. My space for VR is only about 13' by 20'. The problem is a lot of the enemies stray so far outside of that distance that I end up hitting things around the room outside of the boundaries (even with the chaperone on high).

So that's why I sadly had to return it in an effort to not break other things in my VR space. Once I get a bigger space I'll be back in the game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll get right into it.
I'd never heard of this title but I was looking for VR games (during the sales) and this was part of a bundle which graphically looked nice, but I figured there would be a trade off... and there is, I'll come back to that.
I've been burned by Early Access before too but I figured VR is still finding its ground, so took the chance - I'm glad I did!

The graphics, are stunning especially for a VR title, most developers seem to think the allure of VR means that we don't mind travelling back to the 80's for graphics... This game presents like pretty much any recent FPS out there but then immerses you in the action.

The controls are natural and slick, You can have weapons on your shoulders (reach back and grab), on your waist (grab at belt holster level) and then one more weapon on your lower back (reach to the small of your back and grab)
Reloading is done by simply flicking the gun up and down - this seems pretty responsive though and I haven't accidentally reloaded... yet!
Although sometimes when I'm in a panic fight, I have accidentally put away a sword or swapped to a gun - this is more me than the game though, I think!

Movement is done in a really nice way, its the normal press, select, release but it doesn't continue or freeze the action, it slows it down - Which means enemies moving at high speed are still coming for you, as are projectiles etc. There are plenty of configurable options for the movement too but if you go for the full movement option, jumps and bouncing off walls can be a bit disorientating at first but rapidly you get used to it and again it becomes natural.

Gameplay is great, you have various guns, projectiles, swords and a bow to choose from so you'll find something that suits your personal way of playing. There is character progression and weapon/armour progression too - so the more you play you can activate new traits and upgrade your weapons and armour.

The trade off...
Its Early Access, so sometimes things can glitch, I've seen a Sumo lose all interest in me and ram a wall, and stay doing that until I put him out of his misery... I've fell through the scenery and made it back into the game... I've also fell through the scenery and watched it disappear into the abyss above while I plunge never endingly into the cityscape.
I'm sure with a bit of effort you could break your game too and there doesn't seem to be a clean way to get back to the menu system, so its just a case of crashing out and starting again.
Ocassionally I've seen my sword be the wrong way around blade facing in, handle facing out, which renders it quite useless, but this is remedied by resheathing and unsheathing it.

Even with those flaws (which I'm sure will be ironed out) I'd highly recommend this game, I've only played single player so far, but I'm looking forward to Co-oping it with friends and of course the forthcoming campaigns

As a bit of background..
I've been waiting for games to come that peaked my interest in VR.
When the Vive came with Fallout VR and Doom VR (Two of my fave series) it became a must have for me...
Sadly both of those games didn't quite do it for me - They're great but I think movement in VR is something that is still Very much under development whereas these guys seem to have gotten it just right for this game.

Hope that helps! :) Happy Hunting!\t\t\t\t\t
