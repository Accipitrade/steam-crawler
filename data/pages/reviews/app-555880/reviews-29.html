2018-10-01

The graphics and engine update dropped. I guess no more of that gritty look and going for that gorgeous inside a movie art style. Seriously the game was doing real fine and the devs did a remake / overhaul that nobody complained about - one of the things that you don't know you want until you got it, and as a result the immersion is now even better.

I'm all for it.

This is just the right amount of depth combined with stright-forward fun. Except getting tired I dont really see a reason I wanna stop playing.

8.5/10, double that if you can convince your friend to join you and cyber-ninja together

--------------
original review in March 2018

Keep in mind that VR is currently no way profitable for devs to make full-fledged games.

Once you have your expectations parameters set correctly, being a cyber ninja in this game is stupidly fun.

Some negative reviews DO actually addresses some mechanic problems that hinder the game from going even further, such as repeating assets/rooms/areas, melee weapons does not have a "weight" feel with them, aiming-down-sight and holding weapons with two hand still needs work. However for such a VR-oriented game we have here, I really have not much to complain about.

The momentum of the game really picks up when you acquired some weapon"augs" or "powerups" and combine them to tweak the weapon u like, also creating weapon loadouts that suits your playstyle and taste. Kinda sad to see some people didnt gave that a chance as seen in their playtime, as its quite lacking at the first one or half hour unfortunately.

The price for this game isnt very impressive, but still very justified at all;
The game updates constantly and the fixes are on point.

If you are looking for a typical $60 dollar Triple-A gaming experience, you could do that, and better: some of them did an alright job converting into the VR space like Skyrim, Fallout 4, Doom, and recently the LA Noire.

But if you want a cyberpunk themed game designed from the ground up for the capabilities of VR?
This is your poison.

7.5/10 recommended, +1.5 for cyberpunk fans and x2 with friends\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was very excited to play a game that looked like it had great melee combat. Turns out you can just point the sword forward and wiggle your hand around and kill someone. I hate that most VR games with melee combat have this problem. The only solution I've seen was GORN. After you hit an enemy your melee weapon would bend, which I thought was just a gimmicky art style but after trying many games for around 2 months now, it's the only game that has amazing feeling melee combat, simply because you can't just wiggle your hand inside of someone and watch them fall over.. But I'm not here for a review on GORN, I'm here for this. It's not entertaining enough gunplay to justify $30 to shoot things and have a nice locomotion system. Melee is an integral part of this game. Even something like deflecting a bullet just didn't feel to work. I like actually hitting the bullet away, but as I sit here and watch the trailer it appears you're supposed to just keep your sword in place? That wouldn't feel very satisfying by the looks of things, and enemies had such a huge spread on their bullets that if you tried to deflect one, multiple would still hit you. Story wasn't really interesting, I honestly can't even remember what it was about. Level design was from what I could understand procedurally generated, so depends on the person whether you like that or not, I do. I'm sure some of the upgrades are cool but I didn't want to get my playtime to a point where I couldn't refund it just to see if those few things are cool. Overall I just feel very disappointed in the melee combat system.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is great. The movement controls are a bit jarring at first, but pretty quickly you'll be chaining wallruns and slides like a champ. To those that find it too hard know that bullets move slow and you move fast, so as long as you're giving new move commands the AI has a really hard time hitting you. That was my FOO strat (First order optimal strategy, a strategy avaliable early that you can use effectively to the end).

That said, after a few hours of play and some sleep you'll be wallrunning down a hall emptying your pistol clips and switching to blades as you slide through the remaining enemies blades outstretched. Most movie action scenes are usually a series of 'combat poses' with gunfire and blades and this is literally just that. There is a small variety of weapons, but honestly the heaviest caliber pistols and the swords are all I've ended up using past a short experimentation period. (the high caliber pistols because killing basic enemies in a single shot feels great) The thrown weapons are really hard to be accurate with given the amount you're moving around, so it's hard to compete with the point and click of the guns. Plus the swords can do crazy damage because you can waggle them like an idiot and be a little ninja woodchipper.

The bad parts are as follows: The story is bland. It's not so bad its funny, but it has nothing interesting to offer. Luckily you're here for the gameplay itself, and the story doesnt get in its way. The level design could really use some work, particularly gauling are the levels with the same room copy-pasted repeatedly. Given how much your character uses the environment having varied rooms makes for a varied game. That said many of the environs are well designed and very fun... But it's clearly not the result of a design philosophy, more just the 'shotgun approach' there's at least one that's just a big open platform with some spokes, and it's boring because there's no 3d environment to bounce around in.

Bottom line the game is great, it'll satisfy my VR action itch for the forseable future. 5 stars. such adrenaline, much wow.\t\t\t\t\t
