\r\n\t\t\t\t\tin theory this game should be great. from what i've seen so far there's tons of customization/crafting, a large array of settings to customize your gaming experience and the general premise of being a cyber ninja killing stuff with a gun in one hand and a sword in the other.

however...
there's a few things that hold the game back quite a bit (for me personally at least)

1. the locomotion system is weirdly contradictory to the idea of being a cyber ninja. you can either teleport or walk for direct locomotion + jumping. so to quickly go from point a to b you either spam the teleport or try to "jump-move" to your destination, which is unnecessarily complicated and with enemies around you also very annoying at times, given that it would be quicker and less motion sickness inducing if the game would just allow the player to sprint. (good examples are gorn or blade and sorcery where you have to move your arms to run/sprint)

2. melee combat with the swords in this game is just terrible. no need to actually swing your swords, just wiggling your controllers a little bit will spam hits on your enemies and pretty much insta-kill anything. in fact just teleporting into an enemy with your swords in hand will damage them. this is a very lazy way of implementing melee combat (just like in skyrim vr...)
some physics based combat would probably help a ton. i mean this is vr, so i'd want to actually have to swing my blade to inflict damage.

3. this is a minor thing compared to the other two but the levels feel weirdly empty, reminding me of the level design of multiplayer shooter arenas of the early 2000s. putting a bit more love into the details of the maps would help a lot. I know it's vr and not some AAA graphics monster, but arizona sunshine for example features fantastic and detailed level design without melting your hardware, so it's definitely possible to make the levels feel a little more like real environments.


tldr; can't recommend the game in it's current state, but it shows a lot of potential, so i'm looking forward to what the developers are coming up with.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have the minimum specs and an oculus rift s. It's probably partially my fault for the problems I've encountered with this game but besides that. This game very quickly becomes not fun to play. It's fun to jump, slide around, and slash someone with a sword. It's not fun that the throwing weapons just straight up don't work and the handguns seem to jam for no reason.

The throwing weapon controls are abysmal. Hold to prime it and then release while swinging forward to throw it. Except that half the time, when you swing forward and release, nothing happens. This has nothing to do with frame drops. Even in the base area where there are no enemies you can barely throw these things so you don't have a snowball's chance in hell to actually use them in a firefight.

Second of all, when you finally do manage to throw it, it shoots straight into the ground. The game is never clear on where you're supposed to let go of the throwing weapon. The only time I ever got it to shoot straight was when I was bending my wrist back so far as to be uncomfortable and even then it was inconsistent.

Inconsistency defines this game. I noticed during a particularly intense battle that I was point my guns at the enemy, and pressing the trigger but nothing was happening. I checked and I still had full ammo. (A brief aside but reloading in this game is the worst. You're supposed to shake the controller down to do it, but the sensitivity is so low you have to practically shake your headset off for it to work.) I tried to replicate this jamming after the fight was over and it still happened but to a lesser degree. The guns have a rate of fire that is slower than you can press the trigger of course but it's inconsistent. The rate of fire varied based on the game's temperment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t+ Possibility to do all kinds of supernatural parkour-type moves, and you truly feel like a badass and most of all - have fun.
+ Despite the mobility, it does not really cause movement nausea very easily unlike many other games. I personally only use teleportation (if possible) in all VR games, and this was a good hybrid solution.
+ Allows for multiple play styles: Ranged, melee, shooting from afar, shooting close to enemies...

± Some replayability in the mission mode and through item progression although the campaign was not very long nor anything special.

− Enemy variation is a bit low: You'll gradually be introduced to new enemy types but especially while repeating missions, you wish you could encounter slightly different ones.
− Some guns feel barely impactful while others 1-shot nearly everything.

All in all, despite its minor shortcomings, so far this has been the most fun VR game I've played gameplay-wise.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter being disappointed with over 30 VR titles, I've finally found a proper game made for VR.
The addictive action feels exciting and natural, thanks to things like air jumping, wall running and time slowing, criticals that cut enemies in half and pop their heads off, and a great implementation of the controls, which can also be configured to an amazing degree. But where it really shines is the skill tree and gear system, allowing simple but game-changing customization and power.

The skill tree is well thought out, covering proficiency with different weapon types as well as tons of character stats like mobility, health/chakra management and how you interact with loot drops.

The gear system centers around Relics, which can be looted, rewarded and even made. They come in 4 rarities, from common to legendary, and the legendaries are packing unique mods that drastically affect play style when combined in certain ways, such as using rifle with the silencer and headshot bonuses, or homing arrows that explode, or swords with life stealing paired with a bonus to all healing in your armor. Relics roll for specific weapons and armor pieces, and drop with stats at your current level, so you're always wanting more.

Story mode is short, but there's plenty of challenge missions, co-op and multiplayer to feed your addiction. Cyber ninja-ing to a synthwave soundtrack through beautiful maps, leaping through the air in slow motion while headshotting one dude and cutting another's head off when you land, trying to stay alive longer to get more legendary rewards... It's a real good game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of my favourite VR games and probably one of the best action VR games out there right now. It's a little rough around the edges but the core gameplay mechanics are extremely fun and unlike anything else I've seen in a VR game. The movement options like wall running, sliding, flipping, etc, are really cool and feel pretty fluid and intuitive once you get the hang of it.

You can customize your loadout from a decent arsenal of weapons that even features some very unique things like swords, kunai, shiruken, and a bow. Melee weapons essentially function like lightsabers and melee combat isn't nearly as robust as melee focused VR games where you can block and parry and your swings have windup, but it works well for the game's movement focused style as generally you'll be using your swords while doing something like dashing past an enemy. There is also heavy use of bullet time allowing you to pull off some pretty stylish kills and maneuvers.

There's not much to the campaign and the graphics are very arcade-like but personally I like the cyberpunk aesthetic the game offers and the real meat of the game is simply slashing and shooting up bad guys in the procedurally generated missions and challenges. There are plenty of RPG elements to keep you grinding such as character level, skill points, and augments of varying classes of rarity for your armor and weapons, the rarest of which offering unique "diablo style" traits such as returning a bullet to your magazine with each headshot.

If you have a VR kit and are looking for more action games to try I would highly recommend this game. It plays like an action scene from the matrix movies.\t\t\t\t\t
