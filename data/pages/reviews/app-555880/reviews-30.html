\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI read many reviews, even saw a few video reviews. I was so hyped to play this game. Really excited to see this game coming out of EA.

I tried the first level of "campaign", and died after a few enemies. Totally fine, i'm newbie. But then I quickly catch up the game mechanics and realized the same campaign level that I was going to retry was actually a total randomly generated level. You just go from one "cubic" area to another mindless "cubic" area by opening the same door over and over. This is not a campaign. This game has no soul like most other premium VR games.

I don't want to spend too much time describing what I didn't like about the game because its mostly about missing pieces /poor content and polishing, i'm just sooooo disappointed about the overall experience. I was looking for an immersive ninja action-oriented experience. It's not. It might have been a blast during the the first year of VR for its ninja theme and the fact you sweat a bit, but with so many other good games out there now, Sairento VR is clearly overrated to me. Most games now priced at this level are offering a much more complete experience.

It makes me sad to say this as I'm aware the dev team is small and hard workers dedicated to this title.

Unfortunately, I cannot recommend this game in early 2018, unless you really want to just swing your ninja swords minlessly. You've been warned!\t\t\t\t\t

For instance, there's a button to press to activate Acceleration, or slow time. When you hold the jump button to plan your jump, it also slows time until you release and launch into the air. If you hit Acceleration, then plan and execute a jump, it reverts to normal time even if you should still have "left over" slow time from the first Acceleration hit.

Playing further and unlocking more skills and gear mods definitely improves the experience. Experimenting with the different weapons you'll probably land on your favorite loadout and ignore everything else. I'm not sure how a human could actually use the zoomed scope on the sniper rifle, but it's there. The later experience is definitely better than the early experience. However, the game becomes very repetitive very quickly and the growth rate for both skills and loot quickly makes it feel like you're on a treadmill going nowhere. If you love the style and play of the game that may not matter. The core of a good game is here, but the execution still needs work.


Original Review:

Playing room scale makes the game more difficult because the areas are so wide open you can't make use of the real physical space. So you end up standing in place "jumping" around. In the first three levels I clipped through MANY walls and then the game finally crashed on me. Definitely not ready for prime time.

Perhaps after you've leveled a lot and increased the duration of Acceleration, but early on it's not too useful. Even without the crashes and glitches I'm not sure I'd be able to recommend this game. It just wasn't fun. It might have done better as a straight desktop game, but then the levels are out of a game from 1998.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI purchased Sairento on sale after having been on the fence about it for a while. It turned out to be one of the best VR game purchases I have made.

The Bad: The graphics will not stun or amaze you (particularly if youre going into VR from a 4K res monitor), but what VR games graphics will? There is a brief but seemingly substandard tutorial considering the myriad of options and gameplay variances available. The tutorial teaches you to do some basic jumping, wall running, and weapon use. It does not teach you to do any of the fancier flips (if you decide to turn them on in the options menu). Furthermore, it doesnt tell you about basic game mechanics such as assigning skill points, equipping different gear, equipping relics to gear, selling and buying, etc. All these things are certainly easy to figure out autonomously if you take the time to dig around the various option menus. But, I had played this game for a couple hours before even realizing that my character level meant something, or that I was collecting those little item icons during missions for a reason.

The Good: Other than the standard graphics you get with every VR game, and the need to do a lot of self-motivated digging around to find the games true potential; pretty much everything about this game is great. Although I only own about 20 to 25 VR game titles, Sairento has the best gameplay I have experienced in VR yet and has an uncanny ability to make you feel like a real badass. The minor RPG elements make you feel like youre actually achieving something as you drive forward, instead of remaining permanently stagnant like in most action or shooter games. There are tons of options for controls and gameplay mechanics that make it customizable to a players preferences, more so than almost all the VR games Ive seen.

Great game, I would definitely recommend it for anyone considering it. You wont regret it.
