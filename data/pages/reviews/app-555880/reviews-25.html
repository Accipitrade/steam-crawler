\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:
+Really versatile movement system, with double/triple/quadra jumps, wall jumps, wall runs, slides, backflips, gound smashes, dashes, and whatnot. And roomscale movement still works, so you can move and dodge yourself. I don't recommend backflipping yourself in the living room with the vive on, though.
+Plenty of options to enable or disable these features in case you get motion sick. I myself only get a bit sick with backflips.
+Well controlled bullet time acceleration, that can be combined with the movements.
+Good variety of weapons. Katanas, different pistols, submachine guns, assault rifles, sniper rifles, bow, kunais, shurikens.
+The weapon change sytem is pretty good. You have two holsters on your hip, one behind it, and two on your back. You decide what you put on them, though the big weapons like rifles or bows can only be put on the back. Overall feels very intuitive. Being able to unseath your katana and behead someone in the same motion is badass. You can also just punch people.

The bad:
-The story is really predictable, and the final confrontation feels tacked on. Some mechanics like the laser walls are used once and never again. Does a good job for chaining missions with increasing variety of enemies at least.
-Some enemies are very bullet spongy. Though the ones you'd expect to be fragile like the invisible shinobis or the gunmen still go usually on one good headshot.
-Locations feel a bit uninspired, all of them are your typical cybertokyo, and a bit samey.
-On elimination/purge missions, sometimes the last enemy appears on who knows where and you have to look for them, killing the momentum.
-Stealth is limited to "kill guys without being seen, with melee or bow". Is good to get a few cheeky kills before going back to ham town, but not much else.

Overall a pretty good game. Just set your chaperone with enough margin so you don't end up unseathing a katana into your tv.
Also, do a pistols + rifle loadout and play with John Wick OST. Just do it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not trying to discredit the negative reviews - everyone has their opinion but to say it's a terrible VR game is a real disservice to the hard work of the development team here. I think the DLC is a lousy cashgrab on a fairly high priced VR Title but the game itself is awesome.

I personally compare it to games like Robo Recall, Raw Data (I have the PSVR version so keep in mind I have an inferior version compared to PC), and Aeon. It may not have the production values of Robo Recall (or humor) but it definitely has more longetivity and is the same price. It's actually cheaper than Raw Data (doesn't again look as good) and makes you feel even more like a one man/woman killing machine. Finally it has a lot more game mechanics than Aeon but still gives you that feeling that you're taking on the world (and slicing limbs off too).

Gameplay is a bit repetitive but there are a fair amount of environments and enemy types. You will generally speakiing see the same enemies for each mission/challenge but since you're either trying to complete the story, try out new weapons, or get upgrades (it has a loot system to upgrade your weapons) you really won't care. You're too busy figuring out your next move by bouncing off the wall or wall running to worry about how many enemies look the same.

This game has a lot going for it. You have coop (I haven't tried it), great controls that work well for a novice to a VR veteran, and just the overall feeling of being unstoppable. If you like games like Superhot, Aeon, Raw Data, or Robo Recall you have to try this. It's one of the best VR experiences in my opinion. It may not be a game you come back to everyday but you will want to come back to it now and again for a long time!\t\t\t\t\t

Experienced on the Oculus Rift with Touch Controllers

I can't believe I paid $23.99 for this game after putting my faith in and believing the Very Positive Steam reviews. This is not a good game. Don't get me wrong, if it was normally listed at a lower price, I could recommend it. But it's just not worth a $29.99 experience or even a $23.99 experience. It just isn't. Most games I buy at that range have better production values or polish. This game has none of that.

The whole game looks and plays like it's still in Early Access and needs refinement. Everything is clunky. You're supposed to be this ninja moving either stealthily or acrobatically across the world. It feels like you're a handicapped ninja. Your holstered handguns feel weak. Your swords feel overpowered. The best strategy is to jump around like a jack rabbit in heat at a bunny ranch. You jump and wildly swing away at enemies that you can slide past and swing your sword wildly and then jump or double jump to the next enemy. And if that wasn't enough, you can momentarily slow down time.

The chapter stories were just laughably bad. The icons and text would go just beyond your FOV. Not that it mattered because it was just go kill all these bad guys who are actually our good guys who were hacked. Does it matter? If it moves either shoot it or swing wildly with your swords. The game's arcade missions were very similar to the chapter stories minus the babbling dialogue at the start.

The graphics and sounds are all rather mediocre and lack anything close to resembling AAA quality. The environments were bland and uninspired (this is supposedly future cyber punk Japan, how can you not do this world better?) I wouldn't mind any of that if the combat were smooth and satisfying or if the enemy AI weren't so dumb. But it's a combination of both that leaves me pondering how this game has Very Positive review status at this price point. Don't you all expect more at this price? I do.

I get it. It is kind of fun to double jump and wall run and then land and slide. But it gets old fast. There isn't much more to do. I was excited when I saw the lasers and having to turn them off. Yet the execution of that was pedestrian. And I love all the play options available. But options and some nice available movement styles is not enough at this price range. Enemy AI is not there. The story is not there. Combat is not there. And even the movement is too clunky. I don't understand the positive reviews.

Several times I got stuck while traversing stairs! Does that sound like a ninja? Several times I got stuck in the game world after a jump (one time I flew off the map). There's even a menu button to reset your position (which restarts the level as well). Does that sound like polish? And I tried different loadouts and none of them felt as good as dual swords or one sword and a pistol. The bow was especially disappointing as it is not effective in this type of game. To progress the chapters, I had to do missions I didn't want to do. The missions types were uneven in difficulty (of the same tier level). Even the shuriken didn't feel right when throwing!

Rate 4.5/10. I see the appeal, but it's very shallow and the production values are very low.\t\t\t\t\t
