\r\n\t\t\t\t\tControls are woeful, at least on Oculus.
Rotate left/right doesn't feel responsive at all, and having it share a stick with reverse look and teleport is a nightmare.
Hand rotation seems off with some of the melee weapons. The sword itself follows the angle of my hand, but the in-game hand is tilted which confuses the brain. Thrown weapons shoot out of my hand 45 degrees in the wrong direction. They are unusable.

Movement is fun, but it's way too easy to get stuck on terrain or other objects, especially railings overlooking dropdowns. I'm a ninja assassin, I should be able to jump off a catwalk without getting caught on the railing.
Also unless you turn on "long limb mode" the length of your in-game arms is limited for some reason. Echo Arena does in-game arms without this problem. But no, you have to tell the game you're a gorilla in order to have proper tracking the full length of your arm.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game sounds great in concept, but the execution is lackluster.

Firstly, the movement is just terrible. Bouncing off walls is awkward and often the character misses the mark on the higher jumps due to getting stuck on the edge or losing momentum. The easiest part about the movement is getting stuck in a wall or falling out of bounds, and I've gotten stuck on slopes and stairs, as well as getting stuck while trying to jump down while I'm on a ledge, more times than I can count in my short time of playing, everything that isn't just moving straight forward has been a consistently frusterated experience.

Secondly, the combat, which I found had amazing ideas but they all fell short due to gameplay. The build options on the weapons and armor are amazing, and when I equip relics I can actually feel a difference in the weapons, but it doesn't fix the problems.
Hit boxes on the swords falls short, I can stick enemies with the tip of the sword and it doesn't actually do any damage unless I'm basically hitting them with the hilt. Wiggle waggling the swords is honestly the best way to combat most enemies, whether you're dealing with one enemy or like twelve.
The guns seem fine from what I've played and there are several fun relics that make guns either well rounded, or very specific and situational.
Throwing weapons as well are something I just didn't understand. In games like Robo Recall or Blade and Sorcery the throwing is easy and feels natural. In Sairento VR its like everything is off by at least a 30 degree downward angle. I was really excited for using things like the glaive but throwing in this game is so unnatural and the only way I found any consistency, if you could call it consistency, was by flicking your wrist just enough to register a throw and letting go which feels like way too soon, and even then I'd be aiming too low. After attempting to use them for several missions and then practicing in the Dojo I just had to unequip them.

Lastly, is the slow motion concept, which is what I feel like should tie the movement and combat together for a fluid experience. The only problem is slow motion doesn't help with the chaos of everything going on around you, most of the time combat gets to the point where there is just so much going on at all angles that slow motions does nothing and its better to just run away and swoop back around. It doesn't help with movement when the movement is just so janky and unnatural.

I love that the developers are passionate about this game and listen to positive and negative feedback, and because of that I really really tried to enjoy this game, attempting to stealth with full ninja set up, a Deadpool-like build, and just straight up guns blazing, and no build felt proper for the sheer amount of discord going on in this game, the consistently buggy movement being the worst perpetrator in this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was the first game I played in VR and it unfortunately set a VERY HIGH BAR in terms of what I expect from every other game now.

Every other game in VR feels like an absolute joke now compared to this. I don't really think it's possible for me to enjoy anything more than Sairento on my vive anymore.

It's like that netflix commercial with the famous people saying stuff like "OH DON'T FLY FIRST CLASS IT RUINS YOUR LIFE!!!!"


Don't buy Sairento VR because it will ruin every other game on the market for you.



PS: If you're super crafty and hyper reactive, you can slow time right when the kunoichi teleport to you, put your weapon away, and slap them for not bringing you a sandwich. Technically, you have to hold the trigger and punch them, but it's usually a 1 hit KO on standard difficulty and it's hilarious to pull off.


PSS: If you're dense, the review is saying that it is easily my favorite game. I can deflect bullets with a sword. I can use a throwing glaive as a pseudo-shield to block bullets, I can use a bow to bounce arrows and use a bow to block bullets and use a bow to friggen slash people in half????? Yes, that is a thing. The bow can slash people in half at close range and it can block bullets if you're good enough.
I might try to shoot bullets with my own bullets and see if I can bounce them matrix style in a few minutes here. That'd be badass. The progression system is fun and you can build your character in a multitude of ways. You get to respec your skill points whenever you want and remove weapon enhancements on the fly in the dojo. The weapon upgrade system is basically like how the weapon randomizaion in Boarderlands worked. you get upgrade chips for weapon categories and specific weapons based on your level and you can "re roll" some of the stats if you don't like them, sell them, or dismantle for resources.

I can't say a ton about weapon balance right now. Some weapons are MUCH harder than others to use. I hear that some people have trouble throwing the glaive, but for me it's probably one of my favorite throwing weapons. The katana is really fun for dashing around through your enemies while you hold it extended for a "slash dash" attack. You can combine your locomotion and weapons for stylized attacks, so it's not as linear as "teleport somewhere and slash stuff." The possibilities for combos and attack styles are literally endless. Especially with backflips and side flips. If you enable that in the options, you are literally in the matrix. The game becomes the matrix, no doubt at all. (Especially with triple jump. triple back flip...just try it trust me).

The level of depth in combat is unparalleled (did I mention you can deflect bullets?). Not only can you deflect bullets, but you can also block close range sword slashes. My favorite thing to do is have a katana 1v1 when there's 1 katana wielding enemy left. You...can...actively...block...their...slashes!!! Seriously..just stand in 1 spot and practice with an enemy. I don't think it's possible to block blade waves or missiles, but everything else is game.

The kunai and shuriken are expert tier weapons. I have good aim with the bow, sniper rifle, glaive, and can poke eyes out with the DLC claws....but omg the kunai and shuriken are so hard to aim. I haven't tried any aim assist builds, but I might. Their damage is INSANE but they're incredibly difficult to aim. I throw knives IRL and the mechanic for throwing them in game is pretty spot on, but..... I've never thrown knives at the distances you have to throw them in game. They might be more of a point blank weapon that I've been using entirely wrong, but it's still do-able.


There's also an infinite ammo mechanic in the dojo. if you run out of ammo, just sheath and pull out again and ammo is full. Super handy if you need to practice your aim.


The sniper rilfe is aids. I used to no-life Overwatch and I have amazing aim with everything...but that scope is so jittery. I notice 0 difference while 2 handed. I would honestly say the sniper rilfe is the worst weapon in the game, only saving grace is that it can basically 1 shot everything on ez mode. 0/10 weapon, would not bang.


I keep going on and on. The game is amazing.
Buy it.

DLC weapons are fun AF and worth it too IMO.
