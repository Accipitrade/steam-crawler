These controls feel clumsy at first, but they enable experienced players to perform complex jumps and movements to get the edge in battle. There are very few VR experiences more satisfying than jumping 20 feet in the air and simultaneously headshotting two enemies, then pulling out your sword and slicing the enemy you land on.
The story is acceptable I guess, not super interesting but its okay. Throwing weapons are extremely difficult to aim and I wouldn't recommend wasting your time practicing them, it's different on each throw no matter how hard you try to throw identically. Throwing weapons don't do much damage anyways, so no real loss.
All things considered, this is probably my third favorite VR game, behind Beat Saber and The Forest. Solid 7.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdited after posting since Steam hates long reviews.
Disclaimer: I have not played Multiplayer so this will be a single-player only review.

First, my one disappointment. There used to be an in-game recording option but it is no longer there. It was a really great way to show off the capabilities to other prospective VR peeps without using external software. It was super-easy to use and I'm bummed it's gone.

Sairento is an amazing game; I can't believe it's suddenly getting mixed reviews. Not only did the developers just announce they're providing a free update coming with more weapons and maps, but they've consistently shown a willingness to listen to feedback and implement it, 1.5 years after launch and counting. In VR-land, that's a good sign and unfortunately not as common as it should be.

Don't expect:
- a fencing or sword fighting game. This is not Sword Fighter VR or Blade&Sorcery; don't come looking for parrying or lunging. If you are standing still while fighting, you are doing it wrong. (Unless you're practicing your Gun Kata, in which case: yes, buy this game.) When using swords in Sairento, you want to be moving, like sliding past and slicing off legs or jumping over an enemy and taking their head off.

Expect:
-you, but a ninja
-wall walking
-wall jumping
-backflipping (if you want, more on that later)
-jumping multiple times in mid-air to change your trajectory, dodge, and aim
-crouching IRL to slide after a jump
-sliding with a sword in each hand, taking out kneecaps like the good Lordninja intended
-using one sword to deflect/reflect bullets while charging an energy wave or firing one of dozens of guns with the other
-carrying a railgun on your shoulder, a shotgun on your other shoulder, two swords at your hips, and an SMG tucked down the back of your ninja pants
-wielding any two of those weapons at the same time
-play stealthy and quiet, hiding above a door way to drop down and ninja chop a neck or scissor slice with two swords
-play like a "true" ninja with swords, shuriken, glaive (think Turok's Razorwind, yeah, that one!) bow, and kunai
-play like Neo and kick in the front door with dual-wielding pistols, SMGs, and a shotgun on your back
-play like a monk and Ip Man your way through (recommend LOTS of room around you for this one or RIP your surroundings)
-skill trees for each type of weapon as well as a general skill tree where you can FEEL the difference as you level
-post-campaign generated missions that include adjustable difficulty for wave-based fighting, advancing to clear a level of enemies, boss-killing (and multiple bosses if you ramp up the difficulty) using any of the above methods
-prestige skill bonuses after max level reached
-more that I've probably forgotten because there's SO MUCH HERE

The sheer number of options you can select to customize your experience is fantastic. Did you want to backflip off a wall and ACTUALLY have your vision move like you backflipped, shooting in bullet time while upside down? Yeah, they thought of that. Did you want to do that, but NOT have your vision go upside down? Just tick that box; they thought of that too. Different styles of movement? Mhm. Bullet time vs no bullet time when aiming your next jump? Why not? How about different custom loadouts for the sheer number of combinations of weapons? Sure, as many as you'd like. (It saves your skill loudout too!) Do you want to switch weapons without holstering one? How about having to holster first before switching? Pick whichever you like. Did you love Superhot and now feel like having a life bar would be too easy? Get the Synaptic Overload relic and turn up mission difficulty. What's a relic, you ask? In addition to the leveling system with actual skill trees, you can "slot" different relics into different weapons. These relics drop during a mission or can be bought and sold, and you also receive different boxes for leveling or as rewards for missions. You can reforge parts of those relics to customize your loadout even further, allowing you to prioritize a combination of: reload time, magazine size, damage (raw), damage (percentage), sword deflect size, sword reflect chance, shotgun bullet spread, XP gained, different types of drops gained, etc.

And then there are the equivalent of Legendary relics that offer even MORE options. There's a relic to decrease your railgun body damage but drastically increase your railgun headshot damage. There's a relic that boosts your damage a huge amount when dual wielding SMGs. There's a relic to fire three energy blade waves instead of one. Relics that change how you play, how your guns work, and how your skills work are all present.

Seriously, the amount of customization and work that went into this game is staggering. It is absolutely worth the price and deserves the same level of appreciation as Superhot, Beat Saber, I Expect You to Die, and similar. Please buy it. The campaign is fun but the real joy comes from the ability to play the game as a ninja, as easy or as hard as you want to, in almost any way that you want to.\t\t\t\t\t
