\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have way more hours in this game than Steam shows. I played it first at a VR Arcade and then through Viveport Infinity before purchasing it on Steam.

This game is the 2nd VR game I ever played on the first day that I ever tried VR. It was the game that made me decide to buy my own VR setup and, despite finding many other great VR experiences since, it is still my all time favorite VR game. To explain why I love this game so much let me paste here what I texted to a friend right after playing it for the first time:

"I found a VR arcade nearby and tried out a Vive Pro last night and it wAs AWESOME!!! I played some generic space defense shooter and was like: "Huh, that's kinda cool I guess." ...and then I played Sairento... The guy told me at first that it was a teleport-to-move game and I thought: 'Lame! But cyber-ninjas and customizable sword and/or gun loadouts, so I'll give it a shot.' Turns out it's not really teleport-to-move though. It's a use-a-teleport-like-pointing-system-to-FREAKING-LEAP-through-the-air-like-a-cybernetically-enhanced-ninja,-then-jump-again-in-mid-air-to-change-direction-or-double-jump,-then-jump-to-a-wall-and-keep-running-along-the-wall-as-long-as-you-want,-then-jump-down-on-top-of-an-enemy-as-you-plunge-your-sword-through-his-face-and-use-your-other-hand-to-blow-away-the-next-two-with-your-gun,-then-turn-and-slash-the-next-one-down-with-your-sword-after-you've-deflected-his-bullets-with-said-sword-to-move-system. I'm definitely getting a Vive. And Sairento."

I'll also add here that although I did later discover that walking movement in many VR games made me motion sick (which Natural Locomotion fixed for me), Sairento's movement system, despite being an exhilarating rush, never once made me sick. Of course, these things can vary from person to person, but that was my experience.\t\t\t\t\t

*there's not really any variety in enemy types - there's 2 melee types - regular katana wielding bots and a bit stronger katana bots, 3 long range shooty bots - one with a rifle, a shotgun and a sniper - sounds like a lot but they all look the same and just run towards you.

I haven't tried the multiplayer or the crafting and all of that because I was struggling just navigating and playing the game so I don't know about those

the upcoming game they are going to release(says 2022 q4 which means really soon) looks like the upgraded version of this game so I hope they will deliver because the game could be a 10/10 if not the things i mentioned above\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is not the "AAA tittle for a budget price" I was led to believe by certain publications. If you are looking for a game with a good campaign, I burned through it fairly quickly and was not impressed by the story much other than the fact they payed for a decent voiceover performance. Not a whole lot was explained to me in the game, like what all the fabricators and intel tokens I was grabbing are for. I seem to have currency but could not find any shop in the menus to spend it on. There is an EXP/level up system, multiplayer (which I did not touch) and a zone to jump in for quick missions outside of the campaign itself. I get the impression that the game is designed to offer long term play, and the campaign is just a quick backstory on the setting rather than the main attraction.

Despite not being labeled as Windows Mixed Reality, it played fine on my HP Reverb G2, although I had to grab a community made control scheme, which was not hard to do. Graphics were decent, although some environments clearly looked better than others. I often felt like I was running around in a map made from the Duke Nukem 3D engine, with some areas being tweaked with artistic tricks to look more like a modern VR game, and others feeling more like they were built out of cardboard standees. Even the cardboard zones were artistically interesting though, and the novelty of running around a 90's style game environment was not detrimental to my experience. An added advantage to this is levels loaded very quickly, quicker than most games even with my SSD, although loading into the game itself was about average speed. If you want a game to get some quick basic exercise in, the loading speed will let you quickly jump from level to level.

Combat was fun, though sometimes not as satisfying as I would have liked. I only played on normal and I think I may have benefited from turning it up a level or two (which also grants exp bonuses) Many enemies die almost instantly from the touch of the swords, while others required you to shake the sword(s) on them like a miraka. I mostly stuck with the blades, and found the simple act of dashing or jumping to them as quickly as possible to be entertaining enough. I had very little trouble with motion sickness while jumping, wall running etc, although I have a higher motion sickness resistance than most people and can overcome some problematic motions with acclimation. Either the game simply did not cause it to begin with, or my own high tolerance adapted to it easily, even though my headset runs at 90 hz which is on the low end for a headset.

I got it on sale for 15 bucks, and for that price I can't say I have any regrets despite what I found disappointing.\t\t\t\t\t
