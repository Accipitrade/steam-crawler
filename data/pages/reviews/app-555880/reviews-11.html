I typically walk away blown away/pleased or ready to learn the ins and outs of it, with pretty much all VR titles I have played so far. Not so with this game. I will be refunding this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery fun combat, though IMO Steam is a bit misleading when it says this game can be played seated. When I tried playing seated, it would make me crouch 100% of the time which in reality was okay because I feel like you want to be crouching in game like 80% of the time anyways. However there is supposed to be a calibration setting so that you can determine at what height you would be considered crouching, but this calibration didn't seem to do anything past a certain height.

Also a bit tough to play seated because you'll want to be turning at high speeds to attack at all angles, so in reality while it can be played seated with 90% functionality, the game suffers if you play it seated. At least it did for me.

As for the game itself, it's very fun and very cool. However there are 4 different buttons that all jump, which was weird for me considering that crouching can only be done by physically crouching. If you're out of shape like I am, the crouching irl and twisting constantly is extremely taxing and I could only play for about 20 minutes standing before I was aching too much to continue.

This is a good and bad thing. Bad because it means that certain body types will absolutely struggle to play this game, good because if you are determined while being out of shape then this game will most likely help you improve your form.

Only con so far is I wish the devs would replace one of the 4 jump buttons with a crouch button so I can keep playing after I'm worn out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an excellent VR title that highlights the limits of tethered headsets and small play areas. (Played with Valve Index)

Pros
1. You get a workout--those quads are going to hurt from squatting and twisting.
2. A wide array of customizable weapons (using "Relics" which can be dropped during missions or purchased with in-game currency generated by mission objectives, etc.)
3. Different mission types, from guns blazin' to stealth objectives
4. Campaign/Story mode + Arcade mode
5. Top-notch movement and mechanics (the double jump/wall run is both fun and a life saver)
6. Cyberpunk aesthetics

Cons
1. With the Valve Index controllers you have to be mindful of the grip, since the slightest pressure around the waist area will pull the side/waist weapons into your hand, swapping out your main weapons if you had those.

Not-really-a-con-just-an-observation
1. The world is a bit empty, though in all fairness, this is an action game, not an exploration one. Plus, the futuristic art style of the maps and fast-paced gameplay compensate for the lack of "stuff" typical of a more fully realized world.

It took me a bit to get used to the mechanics--for instance, the swords and throwables are not the easiest to use at first (at least not with my current VR setup)--but once I got a hang of it, Sairento became one of my top VR games. Kudos to the developers!
