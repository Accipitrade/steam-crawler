\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot really sure why this is "best of VR". Gameplay is clunky at best:

- throwing weapons simply don't work unless my wrists are bent backwards and even then it's almost impossible to aim them where I want them to go
- reloading weapons is garbage and doesn't even work half the time requiring too much downward force (would be better if it was like Robo Recall where you just throw them away and grab new ones)
- melee weapons are silly, you just wiggle it in front of you and do damage since there's no account for momentum.
- running up a wall in training didn't work at all, I ended up having to go side to side to get up the wall and complete it
- the game doesn't feel good when having a smallish play area available so that spinning 360 degrees is not really viable. Means I had to keep moving forward and then turning around and the game doesn't seem to want me to do this at least in the campaign mode (enemies coming from all angles around me)
- I couldn't get the slow-time to work in campaign mode AT ALL. In the tutorial it worked fine on the boxes, but in the campaign I pressed B over and over and nothing was happening so the combat got very frustrating quickly.

The only fun I had with this in the time I tried to like it was with the bow, but I'd just play 'In Death' if I want that experience.

Not going to refund because I got it cheap and hopefully the devs can address some of these annoying gameplay issues, but for now I wouldn't recommend this game - there are loads of better VR 'gun' experiences to make you feel badass out there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: About graphics, I've looked through the menus and turned everything up a bit and it's reasonably better, but still not amazing.

In this game you can lift your arms upwards at the end of a wall run to initiate a side flip, and this is not always registered so it can be irritating. However, looking further into the menus I've found SO MANY options you can customise. Now, when jumping you can lift your arms up to initiate a flip forward, backward or sideways and activate slo-mo. What used to be temperamental fun from a wall run is now on tap for use and ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ is it fun. - best to have VR legs, I can handle this stuff for quite some time but after activating this even I started to feel wobbly.

Sairento, where to start..

This game sports bad graphics, a laughable story and other irritable problems.

The story is horrific, portrayed mainly through phone calls with subtitles that clip through the floor and wall. On the couple of occasions you do meet characters their voices are playing directly in your ears and they are just staring forward talking akin to a PS1 or PS2 game. No stereo audio to be heard in this department and no sense of self in them talking forward and through you.

The locomotion will make you throw your guts up over and over again, unless you have your VR legs. You will clip on things you can't see, you will sometimes not wall-run but instead bounce.

The weapons have no balls to them, whilst they are mostly fun to use, I just use the pistols. The sword feels too flimsy whilst the rifles and smg's feel like pea shooters.

There is no indication what the collectables are for or that they can be used for anything.

So why is this review positive?

It's really good fun!

Diving, jumping and sliding through enemies on progressively harder levels can be incredibly satisfying. It turns out through the awkward main menu you can find character levelling up, weapon customisation and completely free weapon load out choice. Back and side-flipping (although stomach turning to begin with) off walls is great fun along with freedom to drop into slo-mo to refine shots, dodge incoming attacks and get out of hot spots.

Where this game shines is in the endless mission runs where the difficulty ramps up, as you perfect your ninja abilities and try to stay alive for as long as possible.

You need to go into the game settings to look at all the changes available to you. Aside from different controller support there's fine tuning of bow and arrow aim, when to activate slo-mo, crouching, virtually everything to customise your cyber ninja experience.

After looking in here, I no longer play "jump around, snap 90 degrees everywhere" but jump around, flip, turn smoothly etc, and it's made the game 10x mroe enjoyable.

To trophy hunters: some of the achievements are a bit wanky, and others are downright tedious.

I'd recommend this game 100% on sale, full price if you're prepared to power through some tedious missions and a few setup changes regarding which options are on/off. I picked it up a while ago for <£10 and felt it was adequate.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is FUN. When it works.
It's stable enough to run most of the time... But I've run into some hitches and even some pretty serious bugs ranging from inconveniently being stuck in the ground when I land (which is easily fixed, just leap elsewhere), to being unable to pick up ammo or being in a permanently "glitched vision" state (a status condition that's part of the game). One time I even was unable to extract and complete the mission because the indicator was always pointing straight ahead. Most of these bugs are serious buzzkills and make me put down the game for the day.

*But when it does work*, it's a super fun jump around flip everywhere arcade style shooter where movement is the game and bullet time is dealt to you in spades. I genuinely feel badass doing backflips taking out someone behind me with my submachine guns as I land into a slide, and cleave past a couple of enemies with my katana afterwards. The movement is easy to understand and execute and you're in for a good time.

While the game works.\t\t\t\t\t

It doesn't feel polished, it doesn't work how you expect, the UI is bad, all animations are awful (especially trying to use both hands to aim a rifle or shotgun), and a plethora of other negatives.

And yet...I don't care. It's so much fun. It took me a good two hours or more to really internalize what the game was expecting me to do. I was standing still and only moving when I needed to get to the next area. The "dash" feels like your standard teleport mechanic from countless other VR titles, but you can't think of it that way. You should never be standing still in this. I'm only five hours in and I'm doing wallruns and slides and backflips and I feel like the biggest badass ninja there ever was.

TL;DR - it takes some getting used to, but at some point it will click, and you'll love it.\t\t\t\t\t
