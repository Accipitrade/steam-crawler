\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has developed quite a bit since I first bought it. Finally got around to playing it. It has a story now, though perhaps not a robust one. Story is delivered all through comm dialogue for the most part. Gameplay is fun. I really enjoyed the sword play, being able to swap grip to reverse and shooting charged blade projectiles. I like the range of weapons I can bring into each level, it kept it from getting too boring and repetitive. Weapon mods are kind of clunky and could benefit from an overhaul. Simplification of some sort. Maybe a way to compare mods more intuitively. Overall an enjoyable game. I'm sure some will see the value of replaying this game, I do not. So for me, the price point is a bit steep at $30. I'd pick it up for $15 probably.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave I only just bought this game and then worked out how to do back flips whilst duel wielding shotguns just so I can shoot dudes in the face but in style. Yes. Yes I have.
Nicely presented Ninja simulator set in a cyberpunk world. Story is there but multiplayer isn't. I accidentally clicked the block and it tried to put me in an Australian server, the opposite side of the world. Hopefully this is something the devs are working on.
Various number of guns and settings with relics that mod your weapons to improve them. There's a progression system in place so you level your ninja up to take on more difficult enemies, learning different moves etc.
If I had any complaint its the lack of training in combat. Sure it tells you how to equip swords and stab people, and how to pull out your guns and turn on bullet time, but it doesn't explain if you can deflect sword blows or bullets. I'm guessing you can but the actual mechanics of it are lost on me at the moment. I've only managed to block the thrown fans so far.
It's a good game for some mindless murdering but it's not a true physics based sword play like Blade and Sorcery looks to be so if that's what you're after then you're in the wrong place.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty fun game but frustrating bugs that shouldnt exist in a full release game.

There are only two bugs I encountered and those are the SMG ammo counter disappears and I will occasionally clip the ground.
Free motion was clearly not the original method of movement.

The swords are unsatisfying and the guns feel like they have no feedback with whats going on.

I have played games with no UI that make me more aware of when to reload than this game.

Something just feels off.

Turn off free motion and it feels almost like a Time Crisis game where you choose where to walk.

Not the best game, but a decent shooter to go to every now and again. Wait for a sale though.\t\t\t\t\t
