\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game!

I'm surprised by how robust of a robot creator this game is. If I could imagine it, I could find a way to build it and make it happen. The control programming and ability to change the motors and pistons and other construction elements to exactly what I needed them to be is the best part of the game to my engineering based brain. I want to make it customized, and I can! I could see a real robot behave like any robot I've designed in the game (minus the strength of any joint I make and wiring headaches) but I love that I can create with the right freedom but realistic limitations.

The challenges are amusing and I love that there's secrets to each level and challenge. It's the perfect thing to try and find the basic solution to beat the challenge and then to return to it and re-engineer the robot to get the secrets or every level of completion. It teaches iterative engineering and making improvements to your design.

This game reminds me of BattleBots, but also way more creative and thoughtful than building a robot that destroys something else faster than itself. I can see this game in the hands of a budding engineer or robotics hobbyist and learning so much about robotics before having to buy a single thing other than the game. If enjoyment in this game leads to a machine shop and thousands of dollars worth of robotics components, I would thank the game for making the first step.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLackluster and unsatisfying campaign. Basically no progression at all; there is a single unlockable item that isnt literally worthless, which is an upgraded piston you get after the first level. There is no finance either, you have unlimited parts from the first level.

All levels in the game consist of a single objective, with 2 sub-objectives that are just minor additions to the main objective. Like delivering a sandwich, but making sure it doesnt fall over. The levels are pretty varied, but all very basic and poorly balanced. Most them can be completed in less than 2 minutes after you have made your robot.
There is no incentive to replay a level other than getting a better score by building a cheaper robot and completing the level faster.

There are some weird camera bugs while in build mode that will teleport your robot out of bounds and make the camera continously flout upwards. And then there is the usual physics-jank where things will wobble out of control and fly across the whole map.\t\t\t\t\t
