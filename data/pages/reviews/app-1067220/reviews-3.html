\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI honestly can't get enough of this game. There's something incredibly satisfying about the moment where your kooky robot idea/design works *exactly* as you'd hoped and you feel like a genius inventor.

The building mechanics are easy to pick up but also incredibly deep and powerful - not to mention the coloring system, which is practically a paint program unto itself - so you can basically build anything you can imagine (my only gripe on this front is that I wish there wasn't a bounding box in sandbox mode).

IMO there are 3 key things that set this game apart from other sandbox builders out there:

1) The challenges. Each challenge level has a main objective, 2 sub-objectives, and a bunch of secret objectives (which aren't actually all that secret). I know some people invent their own objectives in games like this but I really appreciate that the devs gave me tons of interesting - and often whimsical - goals and reasons to build different types of robots. Sometimes you can reuse robots with minor tweaks but for the most part the objective diversity keeps you on your toes and requires you to design totally different robots.

2) The humans! Besides being charming, the humans add a dynamic quality to the environments that's uncommon for the genre. Watching the humans react to my robo-shenanigans is just awesome and never gets old. As much as it's gratifying when things go according to plan, it's often more fun when they don't, thanks in large part to the derpy humans.

3) Sort of an extension of #2, but there's a unique comedic tone that permeates the game. IDK how to describe it except to say that other sandbox games are often funny/lighthearted while RoboCo is, like, gut-laugh hilarious.\t\t\t\t\t
