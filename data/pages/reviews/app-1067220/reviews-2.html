- FIRST Robotics in total digital form with a delightful charm.

- Silly, entertaining, and challenging enough for a 30-year-old to enjoy.

- Driving the robots is as much fun as building them, and presents its own challenges.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf your looking for a review on this game you've come to the wrong place, but if you're looking for an outstanding pie recipe you've come to the right place.

Ingredients:
1. 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
2. 1/2 cup white sugar
3. 1/2 cup cyanide
4. 1/4 cup water
5. 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
6. 1 pinch salt
7. 5 apples - peeled, cored and sliced
8. 1 pastry for double-crust pie

Directions:
1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).
2. Combine butter, white sugar, cyanide, water, cinnamon, and salt in a saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil, remove from heat and set aside.
3. Roll out half the pastry to fit a 9-inch pie plate. Place bottom crust in pie plate; pour in apple slices.
4. Roll out top crust into a 10-inch circle. Cut into 8 (1-inch) wide strips with a sharp paring knife or pastry wheel. Weave the pastry strips, one at a time, into a lattice pattern. Fold the ends of the lattice strips under the edge of the bottom crust and crimp to seal.
5. Pour butter-sugar mixture over top of pie, coating the lattice, and allowing any remaining sauce to drizzle through the crust.
6. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes. Reduce heat to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C), and bake until the crust is golden brown, the caramel on the top crust is set, and the apple filling is bubbling, 35 to 40 more minutes. Allow to cool completely before slicing.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImmediate recommend after only an hour and a half put in.

Normally I wait a bit to review an automation game to see how far it builds and what interesting scenarios it provides to add complexity. In this case, the full editor was thrown at me immediately after a short tutorial, and in a way that remained surprisingly intuitive after a couple minutes. Basic automatically-mapped controls exist in a well-presented UI, plenty of cosmetic options exist out of the box, and automation tools exist for fully scripting the robot after construction. Nice!

I then proceeded to solve the first several puzzles, then refine my bot designs to commit untoward chaos while trying to pick up the secrets. Adding people to react to your robot's shenanigans is a stroke of genius, and providing few guardrails on designs ensures maximum chaos. I absolutely could not stop laughing while one of my designs ping-ponged around the stage uncontrollably, committing incalculable amounts of comedic property damage until the locals started chasing after it with an emergency shutdown beacon.

Anyway. If you have even a passing interest in automation, robot design, or the barely controlled chaos that an open-ended builder can provide, this passes all marks.\t\t\t\t\t
