Volume and graphics are not the only criteria to decide whether a game is good or not. Ronin is such an imaginative indie game, although its graphics are not very excellent, the combination of horizontal action gameplay and the turn-based system is a very creative and bold attempt. The bullet time pause gives the player time to think about the next move, and thus can carry out many ordinary action games often difficult to achieve the operation.

Ronin's shortcomings mainly lie in the process being too short, difficulty in quickly starting the operating system, as well as the occasional game bugs. part of the level design is not very reasonable, at the same time, turn-based combat is often difficult to bring the player a sense of feedback to kill the enemy.

No doubt, as an independent game with novel gameplay, Ronin is well worth a try. After all, its price is not very expensive, the quality is also quite good, certainly worth supporting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I had first started this game I found myself really enjoying the premise. A turn based side scrolling stealth game is such a unique idea and sounded really interesting and learning that the creator of this game also worked on games like Immortal Planet and the artist of this game worked on Helltaker, all signs were pointing to beat the game. Ronin itself has simple story that perhaps could have been fleshed out more but works for what it is. It has a unique control scheme that takes some getting used to but is super rewarding to learn. You'll find yourself replaying the same level over and over again at some points and honestly that was the biggest downside at points. The difficulty ramps up very early and having to restart an entire area because you are bombarded by enemies or the lock down counter hit zero or simply because of a glitch on the game (I cannot count how many times I grappled and was sucked under the map) was tedious to say the least. However I'd like to say the wash of pride and feeling of reward you get after wiping out all the enemies and making your way to your motorcycle is worth it. Not to mention the soundtrack at points is super fun especially on your way to kill the second boss. Good game glad I spent the time to 100% it : )\t\t\t\t\t
