\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI REALLY want to love this game. It's an AWESOME concept with a quality aesthetic and great gameplay.
...to a point.

This game's single biggest problem is inconsistency.
Inconsistent jumps. Platforms that suddenly decide to be solid when the jump arc indicator says otherwise. Playing mother-may-I and HOPING that the engine will let you use one of your abilities that you're relying on to get through a combat.
This is fine throughout the earlier levels, where nothing is really hectic enough for it to bug you. But the moment the difficulty begins to ramp up, it gets INFURIATING.

This game is VERY good at finding ways to piss you off, and that's not just through inconsistency, but mobility. What's that, a foe across the map wants to trigger the alarm? But it's combat mode, so you're not allowed to manually move along walls like you usually are i guess and suddenly there goes half your mobility.

I really, REALLY want to like this game! I really, really do! It's such a cool concept! But I just plain can't -- the execution falls flat in the gameplay so hard it hurts.

It's definitely worth checking out, but I can't honestly recommend you purchase it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this. I really did. I mean I have an extra fondness for indie games and I can understand and appreciate the effort of smaller teams.
Ronin is visually a very stylish platformer with turn-based combat where your goal is to gather information on and assassinate 5 foes. So far so good. And occasionally it's quite fun. Occasionally. But mostly it is just extremely frustrating. So frustrating it makes me swear in nine languages (and I only speak 5). The main reason for this is the combat system. When it works it can be brilliant. The problem is that it rarely works as it is supposed to do. Because, you see, the only way you can move during combat is not by walking, or running but by jumping. Imagine a bunny-rabbit samurai on Red Bull (I think that is why the character wears a helmet, to hide the long ears). Which in itself isn't necessarily bad. Your character can with her samurai (read: jedi) mind-powers predict the enemy bullet trajectories which are represented by red lines. Similarly your jump arc is represented with a white arc which you can move. And in theory the end of the arc indicates where you'll land. If you jump higher the white arc will partially change to red where the latter color indicates the beginning of a second turn meaning your first turn ends in air. However, when it's coming to predict where she'll actually land her mind-powers are very off more often than not (which makes me believe the Red Bull is actually infused with sake). Which means, you'll probably land somewhere else than intended. And that two turn jump is actually a one turn jump and you'll end up landing in front of a 9mm projectile. And you die. All the time. Or instead of that beautiful long jump which you had perfectly planned for 3 minutes end up in a short jump. And you die. Again. And again. And again. Not because your planning was off but because your long eared samurai can only jump and she cannot even do that right. Which is really FUBAR. Where F stands for Frustrating. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tSadly, another game with a cool concept, bugged down by the sloppy execution. Its a turn-based game, so you have all the time you need, to pick precisely where you want to move, yet the game often does not do what you ask it to do. Since it requires a lot of precision and planning ahead, one mistake on the game's part often costs your life, and with the difficulty and length of some of the latter stages, it becomes extremely frustrating. It's a shame that such a cool and simple concept simple doesn’t work on the most fundamental level. It's like if in a shooter, sometimes when you shoot someone, nothing happens. Or an RTS where you units won’t listen to what you want then to do.

It has other flaws, like the extra objectives on each stage being the same and character progression being tided exclusively to them. Also, I like a cryptic, simple story and all, but it needs at least some character. There is nothing about the plot or theme and stands out, because no, ignoring the two doesn't make the game unique anymore I'm afraid.

It's quite common for debut indi devs to overshoot and fail in delivering overly ambitious and complex games, these failures - while still hurt - don't live up to the pain of, seeing someone come up with a perfectly minimalistic and small, yet original (yeah yeah I've played gunpoint and sure it looks similar but that and the jump is where the similarities basically end) idea, seemingly perfectly suited for a one man dev team, and still fail. With the jumping fixed it would probably be a very fun title, but as it is, I can't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this game, it does a lot right (the turn based combat in a side scroller is a cool concept). The difficulty is really inconsistent, and half the time it's only difficult because of the dodgy broken movement where you jump, it shows where you're going to jump to only to just not move at all and get stuck on a ledge and instantly die because that counts as your turn. Grappling works fine on the other hand, so I dunno how the jump function is so off

I don't know if it was just me but I found going undetected was pretty much a write off after the first 2-3 levels, so I never got any skill points to upgrade abilities. I thought that was why the game felt as hard as it did. Then once I finished it and started new game+ and had all the abilities did I realise it made pretty much ♥♥♥♥ all difference and I didn't miss out on anything aside from stunning enemies, and the teleport that didn't work half the time and some enemies just ignored

only 2-3 negative aspects ruined this for me, I love the game concept, art style and with some better mechanical execution it would have been a solid game. but no.\t\t\t\t\t
