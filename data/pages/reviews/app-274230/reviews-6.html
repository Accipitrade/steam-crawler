\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI see a lot of recent reviews are mixed, which gives me mixed feelings considering the game is essentially as-advertised. Most issues I see complained about are just a matter of opinion or most people not being good at the game, which is fine, but not something to have your entire review orbit around. I'll be honest, I suspect a bunch of the new attention the game received might be because Helltaker's creator, Vanripper is the artist for this game. It's not something noticeable but something you start to see in the style when you're told about it. You can also find early demo animations on the artist's Youtube channel.

It might say I've only played a little bit of the game, but that's just Steam's counter. I played the game at initial release and have played it on and off when I initially pirated it as a kid, buying it recently since I've wanted to support the developers for a long time. Since buying it I've beaten the game and completed it on NG+ within a day or so of light playing, so that should give you an idea of length.

The strongest part of this game is easily the soundtrack. Swordpoint and This Is The End are easily my favorite tracks and honestly the soundtrack makes for good recycling to use for running with other sci-fi tabletop games like Shadowrun, as I've found use for.

As for a review of the game itself. Gameplay is solid but is rough around the edges at times, but not to the point where it proves frustrating, at least in my case. Biggest issue are the Jump indicator sometimes not working for when you're on a platform, with you wasting a combat turn on a jump attempt to go down but being caught by the platform. Another small issue is the sometimes unclear system for detection and triggers for making an enemy begin the alert countdown. Though this only really is an issue for one level.

I've heard issues by other people for soft-locks, though it isn't really an issue, and again, is only really a problem for one or two levels. And that is if you're playing like a dunce.

The Story is minimal and practically nonexistent, serving as a pretense to the levels and progression in the game. The comic gives some backstory to the character but honestly it's not needed for anything more than a quick and pleasant read.

Main issue one might find with the game is price-to-length. Depending on how you value your time and enjoy the game, people might find the current price debatable, since the game is very short, around 15 or so levels, and the game can easily be completed in one sitting, not counting NG+. So probably pick it up during a sale or something if you're hesitant.

Honestly this is a game I would recommend. Short, fun, and the soundtrack is a pleasant bop, not much more to say.\t\t\t\t\t

The Good:
Nice style, I actually like the corporate & geometric look everything has

The Bad:
The jump trajectory is buggy, sometimes you don't go where you want
There's a lot of things that are never mentioned, as if the creator expected you to know them like he did
When you get your ability meter full, you're forced to use the final ability even when lower ones are better
The level design is decent at best and very frustrating at worst. On one level, I was able to softlock myself
The game often puts you at bad checkpoints forcing you to restart the entire level
The enemies aim is pretty inconsistent so you have to hope that they don't make an inescapable scenario
(by the way, all enemy aiming is red lines, so it quickly becomes very confusing especially when there's multiple attack types)
Frustrating side objectives that make the level design even worse if you choose to go for them
