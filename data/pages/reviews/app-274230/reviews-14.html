\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love the unique combat mechanics utilized in this game, they force you to think ahead a few turns and try to manipulate the actions of the enemies as you slowly whittle down their numbers. That said, at some points the combat itself can be kinda janky/gimmicky. You can do almost exactly the same thing twice and have completely varied results. This in and of itself is not problem, it's nice to not be doing the same thing every time you enter a fight. Unfortunately though, this can mean you are repeating the same fight several times just to get the perfect run, especially when some of the inconsistencies a things like: will he slash at me and kill me this time or not? Now this isn't really a problem if you aren't a completionist. For me though, this resulted in spending a decent amount of time focusing on one or two different scenarios just to get it right. Overall though, I really enjoy this game and the experience that came with it.

TL;DR: Good game with some unique mechanics slightly hindered by some jank. Try it out to see some unique (as far as I know) combat and some fun ninja/assassin style gameplay light on sneaking.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll the good things and all the complaints in the reviews ring true. RONIN is a great game concept hampered by lack luster execution. Controlling the jump arc with the mouse works with a painful slider where the jump arc doesn't always show your true path, as your character might just decide to grab the next meter of ceiling because the jump arc was sort of close to it. I hate the combo system, since all the more useful abilities are locked behind it. I get it that it's part of the difficulty of the game, but it's a design choice I don't like. The game also doesn't do a good job of explaining its basic systems, you might want to read a guide to see how the game works.

That said, when you get a good groove on and clean out a room in a cool succession, it's pretty neat. But when you fail for the 15th time because the controls aren't as accurate as you need them to be you feel like tearing out your own cheeks out in frustration. If you have a bit of a masochistic streak when it comes to games, consider getting it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tControls are clunky even when they're working like you'd expect. Once you're in battle there's little you can do to climb like you normally would so your option is to jump around all over the place until you can find a point that you can jump to when the enemies are above you, and most of the time the reticule lies and you miss the platform anyway and have to start over. You can stab a guy when he's 1 turn from sounding the alarm and it still sounds the alarm anyway, which is a pain when most of them only give you 2 turns before they set off the alarm. Stopping time when you're not in battle mode is not intuitive nor helpful. The grappling hook is the same button as jump, and there's very little indication on when you're going to use the hook or jump -- sometimes the hook and the jump notification are on screen at the same time so it really doesn't make sense what's about to happen. Overall the concept is good and with a little polish this could be a good game but it just isn't in its current state.\t\t\t\t\t
