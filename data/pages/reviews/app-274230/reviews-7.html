\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust finished getting every achievement (finished new game+).
Ronin is an alright action/turn-based platformer, but not fantastic. To be great, there'd need to be either a greater focus on stealth (the real time movement), or some fixes to the turn-based fighting.
Progression through the game feels okay, after you finish a mission meeting all the conditions you unlock skill points. All but one of these are combat related, you can only use the other one in stealth. You can move left/right, and have a jump and a grappling hook with your mouse. In combat, you can only jump, use your grappling hook, and attack/use your abilities. Once someone notices you (you enter the light) combat becomes "turn" based. All the enemies do something (usually shoots) at the same time, but your action has priority. Not being able to move left or right is a pain since you can't go up corners (you can only jump off the wall), and you can't dodge bullets (if there's a trap under you, you can only try jumping left, right, or stay still for the round). There are a few enemy types, but mostly you are trying to dodge the red lines by the time you're done moving. The alarm system keeps the fight moving (stops you from hiding) but can get annoying when you can't get to the person calling the alarm.
There's not much of a story, which is a strength and a weakness for this fairly simple game. I get the point, but there's nothing besides a photograph to flesh out the details.
Maybe 14$CAD was fair in 2015 when this came out, but if you like action/turn based platformers, consider picking this up on sale.\t\t\t\t\t
