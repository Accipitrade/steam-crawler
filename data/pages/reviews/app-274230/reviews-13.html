\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn Ronin, you play as a modern ninja Inigo Montoya. While the gameplay might be very reminiscent of Gunpoint (which the game humorously points out through one of its hints), the main character has made peace with killing everyone involved, so go nuts.

During battle, gameplay becomes a mix of turn-based and real-time. Time flows normally for a second but then pauses for you to plan your next step. It feels really great to jump around, throwing your sword at people. The game has a satisfying tactical element, as you have to manage enemies and plan each move according to their next shots. It has really cool visual and sound effects and a great art style.

However, it can be incredibly frustrating when your character acts differently from her predicted movement. Sometimes the UI tells you that your jump will stop in midair, but then you land and get shot for it; or you want to jump down from a platform, but you end up just tap-dancing in place, wasting valuable openings that come only rarely when there's 15 people shooting you every turn.

It feels really great to mow down an army by yourself little by little, but only when the game does what you tell it to. The gameplay leaves little room for error, and buggy controls make for a bittersweet experience that relies a bit more on luck than on skill, and it's upsetting when that happens. Most mistakes send you back to the beginning of the battle, and after cutting through most of the enemies in a fight, the amount of retries due to faulty jumps really takes a toll on your patience.

Overall 6/10; it's a great game with some replay value, but would be better with more responsive controls.\t\t\t\t\t
