\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis one was rough. I'll be the first to admit I have a relatively short fuse with games like these so I try to be as rational as possible when ascertaining whether or not my shortcomings in a game are the faults of myself or the game.

RONIN has really neat ideas and clearly takes inspiration from Kill Bill which is an excellent film. However, the impact of these ideas are lost in the game's shoddy execution. For example, the atmosphere is ruined several times due to bad checkpointing and needing to wait about 5-7 seconds for the game to re-load after you die. In games like Super Meat Boy or Hotline Miami, these problems never came to be due to the games relying on instantly dropping you back into the action from a reasonable point every time.

That's not even mentioning RONIN's annoying waste of cool abilities. In order to use these abilities, you need to string a combo together. Yet, if you play the game as it tries to suggest you do, the chance to engage in these combos rarely occurs as you'll be picking off individual guys one-by-one. I honestly think just setting each ability on a cooldown that lasts a specific number of turns would have been a far better way of going about this.

Lastly, and most egregiously, RONIN's controls do not work half the time. The jumping is incredibly awkward to pull off occasionally and the grappling hook just sometimes doesn't grapple and effectively wastes your turn. Not to mention the asinine decision to not provide the ability to climb up a ledge when you're hanging off of it?

Not even the music is all that great, half of it is completely forgettable. Skip this one folks\t\t\t\t\t

4/10
What makes a good game? In my opinion it should have either interesting story, great atmosphere or good gameplay. RONIN has little to no story and no atmosphere as it bets on good gameplay, and its not a bad bet... gameplay has some good ideas and I would have really enjoyed it... if it werent completely ruined by horrific controls...
- Aiming of the jump is very weird (jump is most used move in the game)
- Attaching of the rope is not consistent
- Having two enemies downed on the same spot? - which kill button to press (it makes great deal of difference, as you can only kill one per turn... and you have to guess)
- Switching between turn mode and live mode should not be done automatically - how many times the game turned off the turn mode and alarm was raised while I was thinking?
It does get better, but not because you learn the moves, it is because you learn what not to do (there is a difference).

I am a fan of this genre, but I would not recommend this one. Joyful feeling of "job well done", when figuring out a strategy or when making a good combo, gets overrun by having to fight with the game whole time.

RONIN is not necessarily a bad game but first go take a look at other titles like Gunpoint or Mark of the Ninja.\t\t\t\t\t
