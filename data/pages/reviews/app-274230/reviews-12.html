The RNG.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile this game has many similarities to "Mark of the Ninja", it fails to deliver the same fun and depth, which is kind of unfortunate, because it clearly has potential. But the lackluster implementation of both, the combat system and the level design, makes this a boring, annoying and repetitve experience.

As far as I've played, the levels look all the same and have all the same theme: Activate computers to gain intelligence, jump around to kill enemies, kill the "bad guy / girl", get to the motorbike. Rinse and repeat.

The combat system is fun at the first few encounters, but quickly becomes annoying, because YOU CAN'T FRIGGING WALK! All you can do is jump, even if the enemy is just an arm length away, you have to jump! Or if you are hanging on an edge and the enemy is right above you, you have to jump. Also the two-stick control is weird, because it simply is unprecise. You have to release the right stick to activate the jump / rope. But while doing so, you distort the target location again.

Second, the "lockdown" system is stupid and inconsistent. You have a fight with 3 enemies in the far left side of the building and suddenly you see the red count down at the top of the screen and that "cell phone" icon at the far right side of the screen. So you jump and try to get there only to find out that between you and the alarming enemy are two solid walls and a gap. So you can't even get there (in time) directly, because you would have to get out of the building and climb (sorry, jump) all the way around. The alarming enemy can't see or shoot or interact with you, yet it still somehow knows that it has to trigger the alarm.

Third, the game says itself: This is no stealth game, just kill everyone. In my opinion, stealth elements would have made this game way more interesting and fun.\t\t\t\t\t
