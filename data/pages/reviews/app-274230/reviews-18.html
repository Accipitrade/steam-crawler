\r\n\t\t\t\t\t+ Unique game mechanics.
+ Interesting variation of puzzle combinations.
+ Optional sub-objectives.
+ Useful skill tree.
+ New game plus.
+ Very satisfying to complete.

- Game mechanic's unpredictability will get you killed often.
- Feels like you need to "cheat" the controls at times.
- Full price.

Ronin is an interesting combination of a stealth/action/turn-based game, allowing a player to pull off massive, room-clearing combos (albeit slow, and meticulously planned).

The skill tree unlocks abilities that greatly improve game-play, while giving the player a reason to fully complete missions.

Difficulty increases after first completion of the story, allowing you to start again with the completed skill tree.

You will spend a lot of time learning the game mechanics, which could use some improvement. I played on keyboard/mouse, where a combination of the mouse and space bar are used to move frame-by-frame to execute perfect combos. Unfortunately, it feels like you're "cheating" the controls at times, rather than being satisfyingly fluent.

You'd be wise to wait for it on a good sale.\t\t\t\t\t

Ah, Ronin. I have a love-hate relationship with this game, but at least it never lies to me.

THE CONTROLS

The controls, while difficult to get used to at first, are always consistent and usually people saying the controls are inconsistent just aren't used to the controls and are blaming the game instead of their own actions. It's possible to do the exact same actions in the exact same order and get the exact same result. I never had huge problems with the controls of the game when I got used to them, although occasionally i would end up targeting the wrong person because two attack icons were right next to each other and I wasn't paying enough attention to know which one was which.

THE PHYSICS

The physics of this game are interesting, and sometimes you can break the game by having a character (player or enemy) go into a wall, or have your sword go into a wall after you attempt to recall it from certain spots (like from outside the building it's in while you're hanging on the outside wall of said building.) Other than a few flaws, the physics engine is really good, and pretty consistent as well once you figure out how it works.

THE STORY

WARNING: SPOILERS BEYOND THIS POINT.

The story of this game is very minimalistic, but it's possible to figure out what it all means. What I've gathered from it is that, the photograph that appears on the loading screen is an old photo of a bunch of coworkers. The little girl in the photograph appears to be the main character as a child, and the man next to her is her father, whom is also the owner of a big corporation. The five people the main character wishes to slay have seemingly conspired against the main character's father, killing her father to take control of the company. A little bit of each character's personality can be deciphered by not only how their levels are set up, but also by what the little story cutscenes say about them.

The Old Man is accepting of his death, as he knows he deserves it. This is proven by how he reacts to the main character entering his room; just standing there and waiting for his death to come.

The Wisegal believes she is untouchable, and prepared ahead of time for her old friend's arrival (by having multiple gunmen waiting behind a door, weapons ready to fire. She also has the nightclub she's hiding in filled to the brim with gunmen to protect her, and she even has a gun of her own. She clearly has a lot of trust in the effectiveness of firearms, seeing as how she doesn't have a single samurai in her nightclub.

The Doctor is a very intelligent man, and designed the main character's devices. He lacked compassion, which might be why he agreed to help with conspiring against the main charcter's father. He knows he can't defeat her, so he decides to attempt an escape instead. He uses one of the decoy devices to trick the main character, and attempt his escape. If the main character manages to kill him before he escapes, that's the end of him. If he manages to get far away, he'll attempt to jump through a window and make it to another building that's filled with even more guards, but misses the jump and falls to his death. He clearly had a good plan to stay alive, but couldn't execute it properly.

The Officer is clearly a selfish man, likely even a little psychotic. He was trained by the same person that trained the main character, so they are evenly matched. To counteract this, The Officer has the area filled with gunmen to keep the main character on her toes, and to sway the fight in his favour. He doesn't believe that anyone deserves to be saved, and this is proven by the fact that there is multiple civilians in his stage that have been executed. His level is one of the two hardest levels in the game.

Finally; The Boss. He is the one who took ownership of the company after the main character's father had been murdered, and he also knows the main character is coming. His level is the other one of the two hardest levels in the game, especially so if you are playing on new game plus (aka hard mode). He was presumably one of the main character's close friends, which is probably why he had gotten ownership of the company after the original owner (aka the main character's father) had died.

The Main Character. She's the daughter of the original owner of the company, and a skilled swordswoman. She wishes for vengance for her father, and is hunting down every person that conspired against him, to give them the fate she believes they deserve. On The Boss's level, her thirst for vengance becomes so powerful that bullets don't even kill her instantly, and instead start a timer, counting down how long until she dies, with the timer resetting every kill she gets. If she kills The Boss, she has completed her objective, and her new objective is not "Get to the motorcycle" if she has been shot, but instead, is "Accept your fate." Once she has accepted her fate, the game ends, giving you the sad ending. The loading screen photograph appears with the middle burned out, and credits roll.

NO MORE SPOILERS BEYOND THIS POINT

THE GAMEPLAY

Ronin feels like two different games in one when it comes to the gameplay. Normally it feels like a really interesting 2D Stealth game, but once you go into combat, it turns into a turn-based strategy game where everything you do during your turn decides whether or not you live or die. I personally really enjoyed the combat system, but it's not for everyone. There's many different things you can do in this game. You can go on a no mercy run and kill everyone, including civilians, you can go with a full stealth run where you attempt to be spotted as little as possible, etc etc. You can try completing levels in different ways and see how many different routes have been created. I'll probably be coming to this game every time my social anxiety starts acting up, since it's one of the few single-player games I enjoy.

THE END

Overall, this game is a great one. It has it's flaws, but I like to deem it's flaws as a form of personality for the game. I'd rate this game as a 9.5 out of 10. Not perfect, but it feels as if it is.\t\t\t\t\t

Enjoyed the game; it reminded me of Gunpoint, which I later found out it had been inspired by. Wasn't sure why some recent reviews were mixed, so I read through them. Many complain about controls, which does take some getting used to. Some complain about jump arc and bullet line accuracy, but I did not have an issue with these (and please note my opening statement; I have all achievements, so I played plenty).

Sounds like using a controller is a terrible idea on this game. I suggest keyboard and mouse.

The game doesn't go through detailed controls, so here are some control tips...
Try holding the spacebar WHILE OUT OF COMBAT (it lets you play frame-by-frame and to some ridiculous sword throw combos as long as you keep out of sight, and seems necessary for completing new game plus mode ie. hard mode).

When paused, hold left click to see current jump arc. If aiming a jump and you want to cancel it, press right click.

If you want the game to unpause and spacebar isn't unpausing it, left click the play button on the bottom right (I think this is a rare glitch that can trigger sometimes when you enter combat while in the middle of certain actions)

Mark of the Ninja
Gunpoint
Aragami
Deadbolt

Iji (not on steam, but free afaik...bad graphics, fun game. Google this: Iji Remar)

Tenchu (Playstation, not PC. I only played the first one a long time ago; not sure if newer titles are still stealthy)
