\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn hindsight, I should've asked for a refund before hitting the two hour mark. It's not a horrible game, but it's not a good one either. First, there's seemingly no plot. Or rather, it's as if there's a plot in the heads of the programmers that didn't quite make it onto the screen. Or maybe it's in there but I never saw it because the controls are so fussy â€” which brings me to the next qualm. The controls aren't reliable nor intuitive. Perhaps it's an issue with the UI, but there are often times I try to use the grappling hook and can clearly see the UI indicate it should connect, but it does not. This is impacted further by a single poor control scheme choice â€” once you have moved the right stick, you MUST use the grappling hook. If you've changed your mind, you will have to hold the stick in whatever direction you thought might work out while you push another button. If it re-centers, you'll end up trying to launch instead of doing something else. If you try to rotate back but your hand slips, you'll probably end up missing the shot that way too. Finally, the save points take too long to load, and many just don't make sense. Sometimes they're right at the beginning of a combat. Other times, they're down the hall. In any case, this is the type of game that demands constant reloading. Were it an instant action without load times, that would be fine. However, this is not the case. It grates on the nerves when you have to redo the same single room and it takes as long to load as your last failed attempt.

That said, the game could've been pretty awesome without these frustrations.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst the obvious - this game looks like Gunpoint. Unfortunately it's nothing like it - not just in game mechanics but in actual polish as well. The story is throwaway, but whatever. The combat if it works as it should would actually be interesting & that brings me to my point - almost every system in this game (that I managed to encounter before refunding) is utterly broken. So first off the jump - you have very limited angles at which you can jump & even if you find a good one it's a dice toss whether the character actually jumps that angle or jumps forward 2cm. Yes, really - quite regularly too. Same story with the grapple - dice toss whether it actually connects at all & even if it does you can't predict the angle you'll swing so you don't know whether you will swing away from danger or into it. Also in combat you can only move by jumping.. This includes trying to get through a window opening. Outside of combat you can simply climb through but not in combat - and you may need to move through one - which can take 5 turns sometimes to get the jump and grapple just right. And you frequently have to deal with the threat of alarm. Now this is not a stealth game (as the game constantly interrupts to tell you) but that isn't really true, you die in a single hit & most importantly one of the 3 optional objectives each mission is for no alarm to be raised so if you want to do that you can't allow that to happen. I can go on and on. Also when you don't move the character moves incredibly sluggishly which is just weird. There's a core of a really good idea here - if it was polished to the degree Gunpoint was it would probably have been pretty good - but it isn't. I can go on but you get the point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a great game. 11 hours in and ive 100%'d it
It took me 7 hours to beat it the first time and by the 11 hour mark i had beaten the newgame+ so its not super long but about as long or longer than most Call of duty or battlefield campaigns. and although those games do have multiplayers they are also 6 times the price. So overall, its a fine length. It would certainly benefit from some kind of steam workshop integration to download, play, and rate user-made maps to extend what can be done with the game.
Its worth the price but has some issues. For one when you are in combat you cant move like out of combat. For example: jumping, putting down a grapple, then ziplining to it would take three full turns but out of combat you could jump a half inch off the ground, grapple, and zipline to it within the span of half a second. though this does make not being noticed in combat very very powerful. If you sneakily take out loads of guards by hanging them then take out as many as you can by throwing and recalling your sword, it makes a lot of combat a lot easier. another movement issue is that you cant run or climb up and jumping past a corner or off of a ledge can sometime just straight up *not* work. i quickly learned to just not try to do it and it wasnt an issue anymore but when i was trying it, it was annoying.
This game is not hard, sorta. It can be very hard if you dont allow yourself tolerances in the movement, dont do the optional objectives to get skill points, and dont pick off guards before flinging yourself into combat. But if you pick off the ones that might be annoying, take advantage of windows, and use the sword throwing/recalling mechanic it becomes much easier.\t\t\t\t\t
