\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is almost good, however it is ultimately let down by it's controls. I have lost track of the number of times I've accidently killed a civilian trying to go up stairs that they were standing next to (normally it would be A to go up stairs, but a civillian being next to the stairs would mean it's now B).

Trying to jump with an analog stick is problematic: as soon as soon as you release the stick you jump, however it is not always easy to cleanly release an analog stick, so your jump is slightly off and you die. They could have easily fixed the jumping on a controller simply by having a button that commits the jump rather than having it jump on release.

Also, there is no way to cancel a jump once you've started moving the analog stick which is frustrating, as you have to hold the analog stick at an angle once you've started to move it even if you don't plan on jumping and are instead trying to detirmine the best action.

The checkpoint system is also inconsistent and it's triggered many times straight after I've accidently triggered the alarm or killed a civillian, causing me to redo the entire level to get a perfect.

So, yeah. These may sound like trivial issues, but it really starts getting on your nerves when you consider the difficulty of some of the puzzles and getting to the last or second last enemy only to have the controls get you killed and having to start over again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRONIN is a two-dimensional turn-based stealth game, where you play as a mysterious ninja warrior attempting to wipe out the five heads of a (presumably) evil corporation.

RONIN aims for a Super Meat Boy / Hotline Miami style of game, where you constantly die and repeat short clips of each level, treating every "battle" as a puzzle. However it fails spectacularly, and what could've been a great game quickly turns into an catastrophe. To help explain *why* RONIN is so bad, I'll be referencing Hotline Miami and Super Meat Boy a lot from here on out.

The biggest issue is the controls. Other than out of combat movement, every action is controlled by the mouse. Attacking, jumping, placing decoys, all of it on a single left-click. Of course this leads into a lot of accidental movements, or wasted turns, which often leads to your death and a subsequent retry. With such limited options, it's not really possible to do any sort of fine movement or angle-perfect jumps. SMB and HM have very fluid controls, giving the player complete control, and thus ensuring when the player misses a jump or a shot, it's all the player's fault. RONIN feels like it's fighting you every step of the way.

The enemies are enragingly cheap for this sort of game. For the most part you're dealing with guards who fire once per turn, and gunners who fire for two turns straight. Simple rooms of 1-4 enemies aren't bad, but later on machine gunners will section off large chunks of the map (as their shots last into your turn) and you simply won't have the room to dodge or escape. In HM, every shot is an insta-kill, but you have control over the situation. You can run, strafe, hide, ambush, etc. RONIN tries this, but doesn't let you do anything but jump and kill.

And finally this game has no real explination of...well anything. The attack point system isn't explained at all, skill points are needlessly difficult to get (you have to perfect a mission for just one) and the story is just gone. Other reviews mentioned getting stuck in levels since they didn't have certain perks/skills, but to be fair I never encountered that issue.

Achievement hunters will find the achievements laughably easy, but they involve sitting through the game at least twice, and really is it worth it?

This game deserves a 4/10. It had potential, and if it were reworked I could imagine it being a blast. But as it stands, RONIN feels like a rushed iPad game that you'd get for $5 and forget about days later. Honestly just buy Gunpoint if you want to see this game's concept done right.\t\t\t\t\t

  • You enjoy this game's main aspects, and have watched some gameplay or at least read some reviews
Dont buy it if...
  • You don't like turn based combat
  • You don't like shallow lore and story
  • You don't enjoy simple graphics
  • You hate quick games and/or games with not that much of a challenge

Characteristics:

  • Turn Based
  • Side Scrolling 2D Cartoon
  • Blood splash and many flavors
  • Devolver Digital
  • Short playtime
  • Cool design and art

Review averages:

General:

  • Mechanics: 8/10. They do their job. And it's usually a hard category. Getting an eight it's a solid score. Sometimes the game will not respond to you, but it is rare. Usually it's your misreading or mistake. But it's not perfect. It's 80% close to.

  • Graphics: 8/10. They're simple, but they're great. They fit there. Although the font of the texts could be better. It's the real let down in this category.

  • Content/Surprises: 5/10. Average. Game introduces some new enemies and challenges, but most of the time you just be fighting, having the same problems. New game + has more enemies and more complex behaviour. But nothing really amazing.

  • Lore/Story: 5/10. Storytelling is really interesting, revealing almost nothing. But it feels too close to nothing. I don't know if it is the lack of cutscenes, log information in game, collectibles with descriptions unravel mysteries of the lore, but something was missing here. It's cool how every officer you kill kinda designs the overall look of the challenges and tasks of each mission. But nothing amazing.

  • Music/Sound: 5/10. Meh. Music, like most games, bores you quickly. It changes with the situation, which is a plus, but it stops there. Sounds are cool, I really enjoy the sound of the sword slashing through air or flash. Some other cool sounds are in the game, but again, nothing that was the main focus, it seems. Just feedbacks and flavors.

Plus and Cons:

  • - Only 9 Achievements: 4/10. Game has enough content for more. There could easily be achievements like "Kill x guards", "Clear a mission without killing anyone", "Finish a mission in less than X seconds", "Die X times", "Die every way possible", "Kill in every way possible", and so on... Yet you only receive achievements for obvious objectives like "Clear Mission 1" and two, and so on...

  • + Has NG+: 7/10. High score on here because of advanced behaviour of enemies. I was not expecting that. Plus, it has more enemies, so, if you enjoyed the game mechanics, you have some more challenges to try on. Which brings me to my next point...

  • - No workshop content: 4/10 Although I'm feeling kinda picky with this one, it is only fair. The game has a puzzle feeling, where you have to try and solve how you'll behave upon a specific challenge, and it would be awesome to let players create their own stages. I know it's hard to create a game with the possibility of community created content, but it would raise so much the score of this game.

The game has a lot of potential regarding replayability, with every level containing multiple ways to finish, diffrent approaches regarding combat and abilities you can unlock, there is a lot of options to explore.

The game takes about 8 hours to complete (including both normal game and game plus) , give or take, depending on the skill of the player. But, thanks to uniqe game mechanics, it's definitly a title worth coming back to after a while.

Unfortunately, game is not very active, when it comes to story telling aspect. There is very little story in the game itself, and the DLC digital comic, doesnt give much insight into the story as well.

The sound track on the other hand is very well done, and definitly worth listening to, even when not playing the game

Personally, the game is one of my favourite indie titles, and i'd reccomend it to anyone who's into platformers/action games\t\t\t\t\t
