\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I'd like to recommend it, I really would, but having spent a good portion of each playthrough on the last level gets old really damn fast. And all because of the checkpoint system.
How the checkpoint system works is, when you load your checkpoint, you will go to just before the area where the reset happened. That is great, it works really well in allowing you to try new strategies or reattempt a section.
But the last level adds a new mechanic upon taking a lethal hit. Instead of instantly dying, you will get a timer like the lockdown timer, except once it hits 0, things don't get harder, you just die. Sounds cool, but for the good ending, you can't get hit. Sounds fair and like quite a bit of fun actually. But in case you don't want the bad ending, you have to avoid said hit, no less with more guards of more variety. Okay, an honest challenge, yeah? Nope. When you decide to reset to last checkpoint, it brings you back to (nearly) the beginning of the level. Why? Maybe how the checkpoint system works, but in the end, what matters is that what becomes a game of tactical efficiency melts into a frustrating slog that turns a great game into a sour memory.
Sans the terrible last level, I'd say to maybe wait for a sale, try it out, and have a good time with the game. With said level, I don't think I could recommend it period. I keep coming back to the game with unknown anger towards it, only to get through it all and relive the last level that so cruelly ruins a solid game. But if you enjoy challenges like those, be my guest. I am not a fan.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRONIN is a fun and very different game that's hamstrung (at least on a controller) by some questionable design decisions. Are the sticks on your controller snappy and brand new? Great, the only thing you have to worry about is waiting for the weirdly glitchy menus. Is there the slightest bit of wear on your controller? Cool, you will never know where your character is about to go in the turn based combat. Where you want? Maybe! A random platform? Possibly! Directly up in the air? Could be that! Directly into the line of fire? Extremely likely!

This is because to input a jump, you press the stick in the direction you want to jump, and then you release the stick. If it snaps back perfectly, congrats! You did what you want. If the springs are a little old, whoops! It does something a little less predictable.

The interface itself isn't always accurate, either, even under ideal circumstances or when playing without a controller. The game hand-waves this on a loading screen (telling you the jump arc is a lie), and it can certainly be a bit irritating, but the arc being slightly off sometimes is far less of a problem than the arc changing drastically after you've already made your inputs, which has been my experience since I started playing with the slightly older controller I use for travel (it still works perfectly in every other game I've tried, so I'm sure it's just the weaker springs in the sticks).

I still recommend the game! It's fun and stylish. Just be braced for some input-related frustrations that will likely never be corrected.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRonin is a turn based action game with some stealth gameplay mixed in to it, as a concept the game is rather interesting but unfortunately the execution is passable at best.

Game features 15 levels, which severely suffer from lack of variety - while the levels themselves are decent and introduce mechanics such as environmental hazards, the lack of variety comes from having only 3 different types of enemies in the entire game. Combat sequences are more akin to puzzles that punish failure with an instant death, unfortunately the skill system severely limits the fun factor of the game and the way you can approach situations - thus making the action rather repetitive.

Which brings me to one of the biggest issues in the game - the lack of polish - while I can ignore technical flaws such as being forced to play in windowed mode because otherwise the entire game is unplayable due to misplaced action prompts at 1440p, bugs in the gameplay loop are much much more difficult to ignore.

Knocking enemies inside walls where they can trigger an alarm but you cannot kill them, enemies not dying after being hit, passing through enemies instead of stunning them, having your jumps blocked by an invisible collision and myriad of other issues made the gameplay a frustrating slog.

Combine the above flaws with the fact that the game has virtually no story beyond what you can read in the steam product description - I really can't think of any reason to recommend the game, you'd be simply better off spending your time with another game.\t\t\t\t\t
