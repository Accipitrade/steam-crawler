\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to recommend this game because when it works it's so much fun.

The blend of real-time sneaking and turn-based action I find extremely compelling, and I enjoy the way it makes each group of guards a little puzzle to solve. It took me a few levels to really figure out the controls, but once I did I found that they suited gameplay admirably. The steady addition of upgrades, hazards, and new enemies kept encounters from becoming too similar, yet things never felt overwhelming.

What, then, was the problem? In a word: Bugs.

Not “charmingly weird face animation” bugs either, “game crash that destroys your progress” bugs. I don’t think I played a single level without the game crashing in the middle at least once. Often it crashed many times. Progress within a level is not saved between runs, and some levels are quite large. I exited via the menu maybe twice during the time I’ve spent with this game; the other times I just gave up in frustration after yet another crash.

I have other miscellaneous gripes (sometimes you can get stuck in walls, checkpoints are unpredictably and sometimes inconveniently placed, load times after a failure are frustratingly long), but none of them really prevented me from enjoying the game. The crashes though, they did.


TL;DR:
The gameplay was exactly what I wanted, the game just crashes ALL THE TIME.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game plagued by a bad UI. The controlls can be frustrating at first, but after the first few levels you get the hang of it, and what unfolds is quite beautiful.

RONIN is at it's core, a turn based Ninja game. As soon as Enemies are present you can no longer move in real time, and are forced to jump, use a grapling hook, or attack an enemy. This mechanic turnes what could have been a mundane hack and slash title, into something more of a puzzle game with loads of style.

Skill points are essential for unlocking the full potential of RONIN, but you will not get them unless you meet certain requirements in each level, wich is not quite made clear to the player. Once you realize this, it is hard not to restart if you miss a task.

Definately worth a try. You will be able to decide in an hour if it is worth your time. If not, you can always get a refund.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen this game plays right, it makes you feel amazing. It has a great balance between stealth and action, making for some fantastic moments in gameplay. However, there are some significant issues that you should be aware of.

Pros:
- The core mechanics are flawless
- Turn-based combat feels awesome
- Great learning curve at the beginning
- First half of the game is extremely fun
- Cool animations
- Upgrades are pretty neat
- Totally and completely different from Gunpoint

Cons:
- There are points in the game where there are so many enemies on screen that it feels impossible to dodge all the bullets aimed at you
- Dying in one shot is fine, but when restarting puts you back at a checkpoint 5 minutes ago, and you get stuck... (it can get really frustrating)

While this game has an amazing concept, it only executes it well in the first half of the game. I found the second half so frustrating, that after completing it, I didn't feel like touching the "New Game Plus" mode.
I'm still glad I found this game though, and I recommend picking it up (especially if on sale). Just be aware that the second half of the game can get frustrating (or at least it did for me). If you can pretend the second half doesn't exist, then this is a short, but otherwise perfect game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have finished this game 3 times. Two on normal and one on New Game Plus. And I have to say, there is nothing quite like it BUT, it is also incredibly infuriating. New game plus is practially hard difficulty. The only thing it introduces is, assault rifle users flick towards you when they are shooting instead of canceling out a line and you lose automatically when alarms are triggered.

The infuriating parts are when game simply refuses to work. Sometime the game just says "no" when you are perfectly planning an jump. And believe it or not the hard mode is simply impossible to do with out frame pause spamming and as such is impossible to beat with a controller. I also felt quite a lot of infuriation before new gameplus with these issues.

If there was anything like it, this would suck as it can't even stand on its own two feet as it is. It is broken and at times down right impossible without cheesing. Some level designs are clearly not thought through much and I doubt developers even play tested the entire run on hard mode. It is a great game when all the pieces fit, but it very often does not.

I will still give it a thumbs up because it isn't as frustrating upon first runs. It just saddens me the developers really put so much effort into this game and making it wonderful but decided to say "♥♥♥♥ it, lets just be done with it" at the last point. I feel like there is an alternative reality where ronin is as polished and has a cult following similar to mark of the ninja or into the breach, but here we are. Still give it a try, it is dirt cheap and one of a type.\t\t\t\t\t
