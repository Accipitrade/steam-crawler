\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR go play Gunpoint or Deadbolt. You'd be doing yourself a favour.

Terrible, TERRIBLE gameplay. There are signs of deliberate choices in the level design, and you can tell that some thought
was put into each mechanic. The result however is just frustrating, as even people who like this game admit.

Just a few of my grievances. Bear in mind, the game is very hard , so you should take every chance you can get:
  1. The game switches into turn-based combat when you're spotted, but not quite. There are times when your enemies have noticed something going wrong (e.g. a broken window), or right when engaging your enemies, in which the game is still playing in real time. You should be able to pause, but often the game just doesn't want to, and you miss the opportunity to take out an enemy, and you start the fight with a weapon aimed at your head at point blank range.

  2. Vertical movement is just atrocious. If you're hanging from a wall, even if you're right below the floor, you can't climb up. You have to jump away from the wall and try to somehow grapple and land on that floor, which is not very lickely. This is not a problem when the game is in real time, but somehow you lose the ability to climb up when in combat.

  3. The jump controls are terrible (for mouse users at least). I've had to play a section with mines a few times, and I couldn't help but notice how wildly the range of your jump can vary. Sometimes I can't even jump over a mine without dying, and if you try to drag many times, you usually get less and less jump height and distance. If you insist for a while, you can usually find the right place to drag from to get your full jump distance, but why do I need to spend so much time just trying to control my character?

  4. Two more problems with jumping. First, if you want to go from a platform to a floor below, no problem, you just jump down. Do you want to jump down and forward? Well, the white aim shows that I can. Except no, you just jump forward and land on the same platform you were on. Even if you jump forward just a little bit, you'll just land on the same platform. This usually means death, and it constrains your choices for absolutely no good reason (except that the programmer is not good at polishing their game). The second problem is that jump height is laughable, and it's very hard to go up, as mentioned before. Some games offer some leeway by lifting your character just a bit when you reach a certain height, but in this game you just pray that you'll be in position to climb up.

There is some sort of tutorial, or rather, the game has ways to teach you some of its mechanics. At times, it baits you into trying something and die, and then you realize that a certain enemy works in a certain way. There are no problems with this, that's actually good game design, and the game does have checkpoints so that you don't lose too much progress in these cases. What the game can't teach you however is just how unpolished everything is. It's a tactical, turn-based 2D game, yet when you want to get creative and try to actually move around and dodge enemy attacks, the game will just laugh in your face.

Do you want to move up to this platform that is a bit above and in front of you? Tough luck.
Did you believe you could jump down and knock down an enemy just because the white aim showed you so? How naive. Now you're going to just move a step forward and die to enemy fire while looking like a fool. Good luck on your next attempt.

I played this game two times. The first time I quit during the first few levels. Then I thought nah, I couldn't have been that bad, it was just me not understanding the mechanics. I got to the second-to-last level and stopped there, not wanting to put up with the stress anymore. I watched the ending on youtube (nothing to write home about) and uninstalled this game for good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat if Katana Zero was bad?

It would look a lot like this game.

I realize that I am writing from 2022, with all the hindsight entitled to that, BUT having just 100% this game, I think I am fully qualified to talk about what a frustrating, broken, and tedious mess this game is.

Let's summarize: this game doesn't know what it wants to be. Hints throughout tell you that "this is not a stealth game" yet you are rewarded for taking things slow and methodical, hampered as you would be otherwise thanks to the broken combat system, as, especially in New Game Plus, you'll find that bursting through the window, sword in hand, gung-ho as can be, generally finds you full of bullet holes and reloading at the latest checkpoint. In fact, one of the earliest and most useful skills you can get is the stealth kill, as it helps you clear out enemies without alerting the rest, a subtle sign that this game's fundamental design WANTS you to be stealthy. The ability to eliminate enemies without alerting others is absolutely vital to clearly a lot of the later missions as well, given how broken the alert system is.

To put it in metaphor, imagine that you're sneaking into your girlfriend's house after dark, parents sleeping and all that, and as you climb through the window, the dog starts barking, AND THEN SUDDENLY EVERYONE IN THE ENTIRE NEIGHBORHOOD IS AWAKE, GUN IN HAND, AND INSTANTLY STARTS SHOOTING AT YOU AT ANGLES THAT YOU DIDN'T THINK POSSIBLE. Especially in New Game Plus, once you alert one person, it quickly degenerates into a game of "how much can i jump around before I have to reload the save". This wouldn't be a problem in theory, but unfortunately, the combat is so broken and your tools so situational that I often found it easier to abuse the ONE NON-COMBAT TOOL AT YOUR DISPOSAL: The Grappling Hook.

The Grappling Hook, despite how poorly it has been implemented, is the one thing that keeps this game winnable, and it's clearly not by design, as you're forced to constantly press and release the space bar in order to abuse its ability to pause in what is normally a very real time game. Abusing this mechanic, you can zip around the map like you're a crackhead STEALTH-KILLING people who, if you did it the way the game wanted you to, would just shoot you like Old Yeller behind the barn. What would otherwise be an incredibly difficult game, not by intention, but by poor game design, becomes simply a carpal tunnel-inducing lesson in tedium.

Speaking of difficulty, this game is difficult. I don't think it's meant to be quite as challenging as people say however: the evidence is in that often, it's just a matter of RNG whether a machine gun guy spawns or someone happens to aim slightly differently. Now the latter could be the fact that I do not have the autism to move in exactly the same way every time I try to get past a benchmark; but that just demonstrates that even if you do the movements like you see in a video, you're not likely to succeed unless you get lucky with your mouse movements. It just becomes a matter of doing it enough times to where, statistically, you have to succeed.

Did I mention the mouse movements, by the way? Very broken. You need that to jump, swing from the hook, etc, but generally, the guide it gives you is inaccurate or unhelpful. I have often had the situation where I thought I was jumping to a different platform, only for my character to just skip, and then get shot like a dog for it. Of course, after I learned to abuse the unintended features associated with the grappling hook, I cared less, but during combat, this unresponsiveness and lack of control makes it feel less like something rewarding on its own and more like a chore. It's absolutely broken.

Anyway, to conclude, this games sucks. A lot of people complain about the art and story, but ultimately, it's just the bad game design that makes this a bad game. The only reason I 100% it is because I was looking through my games for ones that I had almost cleared and saw this one and bad have I regretted putting in the additional 12 hours to do it.

Bad game, do not play.\t\t\t\t\t
