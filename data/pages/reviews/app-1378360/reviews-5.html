Synopsis:

White Hell is a fun romp through well designed levels in the vein of old school shooters like Duke Nukem and Half Life 1. Humour is on the front stage, being draped over a competent but simple fps, with very Finland specific jokes and setting. The game oozes charm and feels good to play. There is some jank inherent to the genre, but also some jank that isn't and is yet to be ironed out of the game at present. The game doesn't take too long to finish, but is truly trying its very best to entertain the whole way through.

Full Review:

White Hell is a old school "boomer shooter", which have had quite the renaissance in recent years. What sets it apart of the dozens of it's kind is the setting and style.

White Hell takes place in post apocalyptic Finland, split by two warring factions of both Nazis and Communists respectively. You wake as Jarmo, inebriated and disoriented. The objective is simple, get through the hordes and get some well deserved peace and quiet.

The two enemy factions are well represented by their roster of bad guys to shoot and maim, which are pleasantly different from each other, providing a sense of faction identity to each. They make you swap from a weapon to another, to best deal with whatever the game throws at you.

Mechanically, White Hell isn't that complex. You've got a decent selection of guns that all feel more or less rewarding to shoot and have their respective alternate modes, when using "Sisu"-mode. Sisu is gained with kills, and kills gained during usage of Sisu in turn provide armor. The shining paragon of your arsenal is your trusty boot, being able to block and parry any projectile if timed correctly. Learning how to do this will immensely help you, so you better learn to kick some ass sooner or later.

The OST is composed by Hakita of ULTRAKILL fame, and is, well, okay. It's very fitting for the theme and pleasant to listen to, but it isn't something I'd go out of my way to listen to outside of the game.

The best part of White hell is the level design. All of the levels feel fun to go through and lived in. They clearly want you to have a good time, with levels teeming with set pieces and locations that all feel new and memorable. There are dozens of well hidden secrets within the levels that give you not only an usable reward, but a feeling of satisfaction when found.
Humour is all over the place, some of which will sadly pass over peoples heads if they're not knowledgeable of finnish culture and specifically, finnish meme-culture. There is still plenty for everyone to enjoy, from nostalgic time era appropriate ham in voice acting to references for those in the "boomer shooter" subculture. Health pickups are also beer cans, so that's also a thing.

The biggest problems for me personally come down to some minor confusing elements on how to progress certain levels and some of the weaker sound design elements. The revolver especially is a bit, well, weaksauce when shot. The UI could use a second pass to make it less rough and the game menus are also quite grating, not in a charming way.

Early Access:

As far as things are now, I'd say the game is definitely fun and worth taking a look at. There is some jank (unintended, unlike some of it), that I'm sure will get ironed out over time during the Early Access process. Even if the game would stay as is, I'd be happy with what I got. If you've got feedback, the devs seems responsive and willing to hear you out over on the steam forums or on their discord server.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhite Hell is about an average day in Finland.

It's also one hell of a boomer shooter, taking full advantage of the early quakelike jank that seems to come with such a game. The weapon arsenal is satisfying and punchy, the SISU-mode creates a dynamic ebb and flow in combat, rewarding aggressive play with some of the most creative and powerful weapons I've ever had the pleasure to enjoy in a shooter.

If I would say anything is that the early enemy types (the fascists) are a bit basic and some parts of your arsenal start to feel like dead weight as you progress in the game. Case in point, the knife, revolver and SISU-sword. The knife and revolver feel like a sort of "traditional obligation", and if your goal is to harken back to old shooters and emphasize a growth of power during the game, then thats fine. However, playing some of the newer takes on the genre (DUSK, DOOM: Eternal and ULTRAKILL) makes me appreciate the luxury of making starter weapons important parts of your arsenal. The SISU-sword also feels a bit underwhelming, since every other SISU-weapons outpace it in terms of damage, area of effect and cost-effectiveness.

But besides that, White Hell is even in an unfinished state one of the more genuinly fun shooters I've played in a while, and I can't wait to see more progress on the game.

Also hakita sure knows how to make some banging tunes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReviewing the initial Early Access release of 9 levels, after playing the demo first, Early Access release is a huge improvement.

This isnt much of a movement shooter like ULTRAKILL(how I heard about this game) but it has some fun mechanics like parrying projectiles with kicks. Which is helpful for most if not all enemies.

There's a lot of attention to detail, it plays really fun and satisfyingly.
Level design is a big highlight, as well as secrets which are fair and very well-thoughtfully placed. Often good platforming required.

+Enemies are fun, almost a bit amusing with their voices and how they gib
+whole atmosphere is engaging and has a good design
+Weapons are simple but effective and feel fun.
-not much tech with guns, or any movement tech other than the original explosive jumping useful for speedrunning and routing, but this isnt very necessary with how the game feels
+Despite just running and crouch-jumping being the only real movement, it's pretty fun and fits the level design and some platforming.
+good level design and great implementation of secrets, involves clever platforming and searching, no unfair secrets

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t"White Hell" may not look like much, but it's quite a cleverly put together shooter. The stars of the show here are level design and the setting. The levels feel good, the enemy designs are fine, and the balancing is quite nice too, I never felt like I could rely on just one or two favorite weapons. The setting I like a lot, although I have to say, there was a ton of references and jokes that flew over my head since I know next to nothing about Finnish culture. I can at least smile when I see "perkele" sprayed on the wall, so there was enough to keep me going. Overall, "White Hell" is a good experience, and offers a decent number of levels even now, at the start of early access, amounting to at least 4-5 hours of gameplay. I would easily recommend it to the fans of the genre.\t\t\t\t\t
