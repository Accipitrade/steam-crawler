\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFinn-a shoot some nazis.

White Hell is a low-poly retro FPS set in post-apocalyptic, frozen-over Finland circa 20XX. Our hero, such as he is, is a dude named Jarmo who seems to have slept through the apocalypse in a drunken haze. On his way to figuring out what the hell is going on, he is immediately accosted by a gang of neo-nazis and later on, also neo-communists. Each believes Jarmo to be a member of the opposing faction, so his survival will depend on not only whatever firearms he can get his hands on, but his use of SISU- a mysterious green power that enables him to use more powerful weapons briefly that siphon armor from kills. At first you'll only have the "Sword of Destiny" which can project horizontal energy waves forward, but you'll find additional SISU weapons at specific intervals as you progress. Currently there are SISU versions of four weapons with a planned eight total.

Jarmo will also need to find more beer to keep his health up, but thankfully there's a lot of that lying around. He can also kick things. He can kick enemies off of ledges, kick projectiles back at enemies, kick down doors and instagib unsuspecting idiots behind them... in White Hell, if you can kick it, you should. Successfully reflecting projectiles in this manner will give you SISU energy, as will kills and damage taken.

Jarmo's journey will take him from his ruined apartment complex through the woods and across rivers into sewers, cities, bunkers, a train and an airport, to name some of the places seen in this early access build. The weapons are standard, but functional: A revolver, pump shotgun, machinegun, bolt-action rifle, grenade launcher and flamethrower, the role of the rocket launcher instead uniquely occupied by an anti-tank rifle. Secret areas and items abound, but I found only a fraction in my first playthrough.

In a similar fashion to HROT being rife with Czech cultural references, White Hell is, as you might've guessed, seemingly full of Finnish ones. I've had some explained to me on twitter but most are lost on an ignorant yank like myself. Hopefully someone will deign to write a guide about it as was done for HROT. I think one of the posters I saw might have been a Cruelty Squad reference though.

This early access build has 9 levels out of a planned 24, some of which will be secret levels. I'm not sure any secret levels are in the game yet, but I'm sure someone will find them before long and save me the trouble.

I do have some small complaints. There is some oddity with the aiming that makes me less precise then I ought to be, as if mouse acceleration or some other feature was active, though there is no such option in-game. There's also a sort of inertial wind-up to the movement- it takes a brief instant to get moving and to stop fully. Neither issue is game-breaking, just off-putting. I would appreciate knowing whether these are intended and if not, if they'd get looked at.

Honestly White Hell isn't the absolute best retro FPS out there, but it's funny, the weapons feel appropriately powerful, the level design is excellent and the theme is unique. I look forward to the full release.\t\t\t\t\t

Finnish man wakes up from drunken slumber, yells perkele, kills fascists and communists.

I finished what exists of the campaign in about 3 hours, maps are vast and look great within the aesthetic that it's going for. Being set in Finland there will be references to the country both past and present, varying from memes to a historical person well known by some.
Understanding of the Finnish language isn't required, but even a basic understanding may or may not enhance your experience with this game.

A lot of the guns are great and there is a good amount of variation of enemies from the Order of the Blue Eagle and the Red Tomorrow, both factions have enemies that are vastly different from each other, so it definitely did not get me bored.

Guns sound and are animated well given the deliberate design choice and they all sound great, the shotgun sounds extremely punchy and will gib enemies into a million pieces if they are low enough.

The first boss I find quite enjoyable, though I won't really say much more than that.

Music has been composed by Hakita, who also works on Ultrakill, I enjoy the music from playing the game and am hoping to see where it may go as the game goes on.

Overall I think this game has its own charm and has good gunplay and can wholly recommend it to someone who may enjoy this Finnish Wolfenstein-ish experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe date of writing (2021.08.15.)

This game grabbed my interest because of my past with playing My Summer Car, another game all around the topic of Finland. I actively was hoping for a nod or a reference to My Summer Car. Wasn't disappointed.
The game comes with a story where Finland was overrun the winter cold all country wide and the opposing parties of neo-nazis and hardcore communists are ruling the country. We wake up in our toilette, and the actions starts immediately. The game offers a wide range of enemies with huge maps, lot of weapons, and the special weapon of Sisu, with 4 firetypes. All fun, while the maps are getting bigger, we have to fight more enemies as well.
Oh, and after 7 hours, you sure will learn a lot of new Finnish swearwords or expressions, if you have never played anything from Finland before.
It's a retro shooter, with a story, many enemies, many references to creators, like Civvie11 or refernces to other games, most of the time to and about Blood.
Recommended without question\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLove letter to a bunch of old FPS games.

This retro shooter has references to old games, references to new retro games and a lot of environment story telling. At time of writing it's missing a few sounds and maybe just a little bit of polish in some aspects (some lights bleed through walls sometimes and other small things like that) but the variety in locales and enemies will have you going deeper.

The aesthetic reminds me a bit of old Wolfenstein but if they kept Wolfenstein 3d's weirdness. It's as if someone saw the old games and said, "I'm going to make a bad guy cult out of all of these".\t\t\t\t\t
