\r\n\t\t\t\t\tfun, humorous boomer shooter in the vein of quake, with a hilarious weapon bob animation and pretty satisfying weapons. on level 6/10 so far (i started over a couple times cause I wanted to find secrets). Levels have taken me between 5 and 40 minutes, but when one took me 40 minutes it was because i got lost and was compulsively secret hunting. There's a dukey nukey style kick but it also parries projectiles, funny enemy sounds, great meaty shotgun sound, nice midi soundtrack- the level 1 theme in particular slaps. Normal difficulty is a little on the easy side but still plenty engaging and ive still died a few times and there are two higher difficulties to choose. If you have any connection to finland you will probably like the humor a lot more than me, but even not getting half the jokes the game is pretty funny.

just a very solid game for retro fps fans\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt’s got some nice whimsy to it, which I found quite refreshing as a lot of good, contemporary Wolfenstein clones have fairly serious tones. The gunplay feels nice, the movement feels nice, the non-linear maps feel nice, it all just feels nice. A nice comedic take on the classic FPS genre, complete with Nazi robots, Communist robots, all sorts of robots. My only real complaint at the moment is that the AI can be a bit goofy at times, and while I’m sure many would argue that it adds to the charm, I think it just makes the game seem a little more janky than it should be.

All that said, something about it just didn’t grab me, and whilst I definitely enjoyed playing it, I don’t think I’ll be coming back in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a really good boomer shooter. It knows what it is, and has fun with it. The secrets usually aren't too hard to find, and the devs know you're gonna find a way out of bounds somehow, and usually reward you with at least a joke. "You're not supposed to be here Perkele" was a favorite of mine. I've only gotten to the area where you power the blue, green, and red wires, but it has been fun the whole time. I always know it is not the game's fault when I die, but my own. The only complaint I have of this game, is that the firing feels too slow. If it were just a bit faster it would feel just right. The story is also very simple, yet effective. It's funny to see Jarmo be classified a Communist by the Fascists and vice versa on the wanted posters. The enemy variety and bullet patterns are also very fun to get muscle memory too. My last complaint, probably bigger than fire rate, is that the game will refuse to let you shoot at times, it seems completely random, and is possibly a bug? Either way, hope that being able to shoot without it refusing to shoot gets fixed/changed. Thank you to the devs for giving me a FPS to get back into the genre.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBrief Summary:
-------------------------
OVERALL: 5/10, repetitive, amateurish, and not (quite) worth the money.
-------------------------
Music: 1/10
Visuals: 4/10
Gunplay: 7/10
Level Design: 8/10
Difficulty Curve/Balancing: 8/10
Systems Design: 3/10
-------------------------
This is clearly a nostalgia project, and I had low expectations going in. I actually enjoyed the game quite a bit for the first few levels, and thought I was going to give this game a thumbs up and a higher score. As the game goes on, a few good things are added here and there, but I mostly found that the longer I played, the more the flaws stopped being funny and took away from the experience. By the end, this felt less like a love letter and more like an amateur's attempt to make something acceptable while cutting corners here and there. Don't get me wrong though, I kind of like this game.

THE GOOD:
It's funny. The audio, props, and environments are all silly and made me chuckle, and this game has one of the silliest final bosses I've ever seen. The guns feel pretty good for what it is. Right off the bat, shooting someone and hearing a loud bang, then watching them explode into gibs is just too damn rare in shooters these days. Levels are large (almost to a fault) and actually feature some fun themes, layouts, and secrets. The game isn't too long, and (at least on the "tipsy" difficulty setting) never really felt too easy or too hard. There isn't really much of a story, which is a plus for a game like this.

THE BAD:
Enemy A.I. is non-existent. All too often enemies are placed on the map (sometimes even with their backs to the player) and activate when engaged/approached, only to walk in small circles, and get stuck on any wall that separates them from the player. Enemies often seem so helpless that I almost pity them (I never thought I'd feel guilty for shooting a bear with a laser gun strapped to it). Checkpoints are also few and fare between, which wouldn't be an issue if the levels were a tad shorter. I listed music first on the list above because the music in this game is SHOCKINGLY bad, turn it off and listen to your own tunes to enhance the experience. The enemy types are sort of lacking in variety as it is, and are repeated endlessly in the last few levels. The "you need the blue key" metaphor is leaned on a bit too much to progress through the levels. A weapon roster this size should probably incorporate a weapon wheel, or at least a HUD element showing us where in the list we are.

THE MIXED:
The game features a powerup/alt fire for some of the weapons, but it doesn't really pack a punch and may as well be ignored. Visuals don't really detract from the enjoyment, but are too ugly to lean on nostalgia alone. Exploring is fun, but it often results in backtracking.

White Hell is in 'early access' at the time of this review, but I would be surprised if much of this is addressed. In any case, at $15.00, your money is best spent elsewhere. It's dumb fun at first, but after a while it's just... dumb.\t\t\t\t\t
