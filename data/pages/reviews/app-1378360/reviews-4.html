-the powerup mode sisu is a great idea & execution but it really does trivialise the already easy combat even further. the game really doesnt have difficult enough, numerous enough or even bullet-spongy enough enemies to balance sisu out
-combining everything above the game lacks any seriously threatening enemies, there is one later on that you have to be careful around but nothing else comes close

but its not all bad, game has decent and sometimes even great level design despite the occasional huge area. some of the bigger levels took upwards of 30 minutes to complete and can be quite non-linear. secrets are all over the place and i probably didnt find even half of them. the rifle feels great to shoot with and is my favourite gun in the game, i only really have complaints about the shotgun (which is useless if you arent hugging the enemy) and the machinegun (sounds kinda bad and is very overpowered for the amount of ammo you get) but the rest are good. the only hitscan enemy in the game is a sniper which was really memorable, they are always far away blending into the snowy environment and you gotta watch out for a scope glint

as of 9/11 there are 9 maps in the game which took about 3.5h to complete, its not a huge amount but the more non-linear levels are probably the biggest selling point for this type of game. hopefully the game will get more of those in the future\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhite Hell is one of the most fun games I have played recently.

I'm not that interested in FPS games as I was in my younger years (In fact, the last time when an FPS game got my attention was when I played Enlisted, which was fun, though I don't play it anymore. There isn't any real reason why I stopped playing.), but when I saw the trailer for White Hell, I thought that I should get the game at some point. Before buying the game, I checked if it has any demos so I could try the game for myself before buying it, and after finding out that White Hell has a demo, I downloaded it and played for a few hours. It was good, so I decided to get myself the full game. It's awesome.

First of all, the weaponry the player is given feels balanced. Through the course of the entire game, you can use seven weapons (excluding your only melee option, that being the knife that you are given at the start of the game), all of which feel unique. For example, the rifle is a one-shot weapon against weaker enemies (the range doesn't matter, as opposed to the classic shotgun), but it's still a good tool to use when it comes to stronger foes. Those seven weapons, however, aren't the only part of your arsenal. If you manage to kill enough enemies, you can use the SISU mode, which grants you access to four otherworldly tools of destruction. SISU mode is perfect for crowd control, as the SISU weapons usually kill everything in one hit. Furthermore, each kill grants you 5 points of armor, so you can get yourself some protection. Finally, the kick parries are an interesting risk-return tradeoff mechanic. Failing the parry usually means taking damage, but if you manage to pull it off, it feels awesome and it makes your job easier to do. Heck, one of the levels even seem to encourage this mechanic, which I kinda appreciate.

The level design is good. While the first two levels may seem linear (at least they seemed rather linear for me), in reality all of the levels encourage the player to explore it, be it for key items, ammunition, healing items or just to find some secrets. The enemy placement is also fair. Speaking of enemies, both Order of the Blue Eagle and Red Tomorrow provide unique foes to battle with, although I have to say that the commies have better, much more fun enemies. On the other hand, I think that the fascists under the banner of the Blue Eagle have better levels. The last thing in this paragraph that I would like to say a few words about is the difficulty. I first played the demo on "Sober" difficulty (the game's equivalent of Normal difficulty mode), and when I completed it, I instantly switched to the highest difficulty setting in the game, that being "Wasted". The difficulty of this game is fair - I never felt like the game was artificially difficult and whenever I died, I thought that it was due to the use of incorrect tactics.

I like the game's atmosphere, even though I have no idea how to speak Finnish (except for "perkele", but that's probably the most well-known Finnish word to non-Finnish speakers). Some of the background details are funny, although I'm certain that I haven't discovered all of the easter eggs in the game, so it looks like I've got some loose ends to deal with. The music is just awesome - Hakita really did a great job with the soundtrack for the game. My favourite tracks are easily "Ending the New Reich", "Full Speed" and "Valkoisen Helvetin Humppa". Even the tracks that I liked the least, such as "Red Dawn", are quite good.

Now, onto the cons of the game, because this game isn't perfect. The keycards can cause confusion - I remember how I ended up walking through the third level of the game for minutes, being annoyed how I had the keycard, but the game noted how I still need one, before figuring out how to use it properly. The same problem appears in the eight level of the game, where even though I figured out the objective, I still wandered through the level like a fool, not knowing where to go. I also feel that there's too much revolver ammo thrown around the late game levels, and while the revolver ammo positioning is understandable if you start the level without playing the previous ones, in the case of playing through the whole game, you'll most likely use the revolver when the ammunition for other weapons has been depleted (Note: That's the issue I faced in "Wasted" difficulty, and I have used different tactics - your experience may differ from mine). Lastly, AI pathfinding has issues, as one of the reviewers has noted that the AI doesn't understand that in order to get to the player, they have to go through the doors instead of walking up to the wall.

However, the cons are too little to make the game not feel fun. The whole game was a great experience and I look forward to retrying the game multiple times - either to find secrets, kill all the enemies that are present on the map or beat the levels as fast as I can (heh, I feel like I could even speedrun the game in the future). I shall observe the game for future updates and see what new things have been brougth to the game.\t\t\t\t\t
