\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery well presented, but ultimately superficial Prostitution Sim made in RPGMaker. There's about 9hrs of gameplay to 100% it; although it could go on a fair bit longer if you were more focused on grinding out the JRPG combat; or quite a bit quicker if you were less interested in exploring every corner.

Uncensor patch available from the publisher's website for free (requires e-mail registration and login).

The game doesn't escape the shortcomings of the genre

Prostitution sims tend to be pretty uninspired and same-y, and this is no exception.

Same issue with having a female protagonist that you deliberately walk into moral hazards in order to corrupt her - which breaks ludonarrative synergy.

Same issue with the character having spontaneous amnesia the second an event ends - walking around as if they hadn't just been sexually assaulted.

Same issue with boring clichéd scenes - just a tickbox effort of content inclusion.

Same issue with reusing assets constantly - instantly destroying any narrative significance to what the player is doing.

The game is quite happy to race you through the JRPG combat

JRPG combat is almost always a rubbish element of eroge - fantasy combat has nothing to do with sex, and when you've played one RPGMaker JRPG you've played them all.
Fortunately this game abstracts out the combat - so while you're roaming through the thoroughly boring and barren dungeons, you can defeat enemies just by walking into them (at the cost of some HP).

Boss-fights do actually trigger typical JRPG combat where you've got the usual attack, guard, spells, items menu driven bunkum.
They're pretty brief, a couple of them have a few gimmicks to keep it fresh. They're fine, if boring and totally redundant. They still feel like padding, but fortunately it's not padding that is too cumbersome.

The metagame is mainly resource management

The game has a fundamental ticking-clock element (5 days until game over), which forces you to make trade-offs between your limited time; the gold you earn from doing quests and jobs; and your HP which depletes while doing various jobs as well as fighting monsters.

You can regain HP by resting - at the expense of time; or using potions - at the expense of gold.

So your playtime is spent rotating these resources trying to make net gains, whilst gaining the 50 victory points (towns people who will perform a ritual with you) needed not to lose automatically on the fifth day.

It's a tolerable game design, but not one I found particularly interesting.

The game has lots of varied minigames

There's a Pac-Man style maze game; there's two quirky dungeon-crawl events; there's a bemani minigame; there's a quick-draw game; there's a puzzle or two.

They break the game up a bit, and add a degree of polish, but they're not particularly fun or worthwhile on their own, and don't really make the game seem any less superficial.
Worse still, there's a couple of movement based puzzles which - as always - suck because RPGMaker's movement and hit-detection is fundamentally weak and ill-suited to these things.

All of them are fair, they all work fine, aren't too taxing, but they didn't really do anything to tackle the game's core deficiencies.

The sex scenes are boring, unoriginal, repetitive, skippable, and disposable

Don't get me wrong, the art's good, the main character's attractive enough, etc, etc. But as is typical for the genre; the publisher; and to a lesser extent, the medium - it's all rather weak and bland stuff.

Probably the biggest problem is how it constantly reuses art-assets non-stop, making about two thirds of its content entirely redundant. You can go to the recollection room at the end and see in the thumbnails "oh, it's identical pose with X random faceless NPC; now it's exactly the same scene even though it's happening with a completely different random faceless NPC".

Lazy, low-effort, low-value.

For a game with such a fundamental focus on slavery, and constant sexual assault, it's very tame and low-stakes. It leans into the "cutesy-oopsie" side of non-con tropes, which leads to some tonal issues.

It totally fails to make any meaningful use of the standard corruption tropes, so in that respect is very much inferior to competitors such as Tina: Swordswoman of the Scarlet Prison

The closest thing it has to narrative routes is that you can advance various prostitution jobs through increasing levels of kinkiness as you progress - but as there's typically only 4 events per job, and they're all open-ended, it falls completely flat.
So you get an unsatisfying combination of "ground hog day" repetition of events while you're waiting to progress; and then spontaneous amnesia of what has happened the second you leave the job and move on to something else.

The game isn't substantially better than the alternatives

The presentation is, as I said, slightly better than prostitution sims like President Yukino; Fallen ~Makina and the City of Ruins~; etc, etc It's roughly comparable to Treasure Hunter Claire - maybe a little tighter and more interesting.

But it isn't good enough to overcome the defects of the genre; it's not very good value for money; the content isn't particularly engaging; the sex scenes are bland.

If you love Prostitution Sims, then sure - this is another typical example, and it's way lighter in its non-con sensibility than most of the others (maybe with the exception of THClaire).

But I'd recommend skipping it for most people. Tina establishes a tolerable minimum standard for the genre, and if you've played that, you don't really need to play any others.

QUICK FACTS

Type: H-RPG
Developer: Milky Way
Publisher: Kagura Games
Price: €16,79
Est. playtime: 10-12 hours
Censorship: yes (patch available at publisher’s website)


THE GAME

MECHANICS
Straightforward RPG-Maker gameplay. In Sylphy and the Sleepless Island (SSI), you control a single MC, exploring various locations while interacting with NPC’s and fighting monsters. Combat is simple and fast-paced: monsters are defeated by running into them, which costs a certain amount of HP. Only bosses require turn-based combat. The gameplay is spiced up by various mini-games, many of which require a decent amount of dexterity.

While the monsters and mini-games may offer a bit of resistance, the underlying challenge of the game is getting sexual experience as possible. This is not as easy as it seems, since you’ll have to complete the game within 7 in-game days. As many in-game activities take time, you’ll need to make tactical decisions on what to do with your days and nights. At the end of the game, you’ll even get a nice benchmarked graph with your results in this area.

Controls: keyboard and mouse

STORY
You play as Syplhy, a poor girl who has resorted to thievery in order to pay for the treatment of her sick sister. During the intro, you take on a job to steal a magical artifact from Diezel Island. This goes wrong, and you accidentally release Lyla, the spirit of the night, from her magical prison. You are taking captive and bound with a slave collar, making you an involuntary servant of every inhabitant of the island.

Your only chance of getting free is catching Lyla again within 7 in-game days. This requires you to enlist the aid of as much other people as possible, in any way you can.

The story is light-hearted; while it features serious themes like sickness and slavery, none of that bothers Sylphy and her friends too much. Despite being a slave, Sylphy remains mostly in control of her own actions. The story has a number of different endings, which largely depend on the outcome of the final battle and the support you’ve gathered throughout the game.

QUALITY
SSI is one of the better game I’ve played in this genre. Most H-RPG’s struggle to find a good balance between actual RPG gameplay and hunting-for-lewd gameplay, and often fail at least one of those. SSI’s devs have made the clear choice to focus on the latter, which pays off. While it’s fairly easy to complete the game during your first playthrough, Syplhy will afterwards likely tell you that your performance was average at best. This offers a convincing incentive to play again, this time using your time management skills and knowledge of the game to the best of your ability.

The production quality of the game is pretty decent, too: sound, visual’s, menu’s and translations are all well done, and I didn’t encounter any bugs.

THE GOODS

SUBJECT
SSI’s lewd is mainly about Sylphy’s sexual experiences. She starts out shy and somewhat reluctant, but is quickly convinced by the people and circumstances around her to loosen up. In addition, there are three other NPC’s - Sylphy’s sister and two girls she meets along the way – that have a few lewd scenes. In most early-game scenes, the girls are tricked, coerced or sometimes forced into sexual activity; later on, they do it voluntarily.

CONTENT
102 CG-based scenes, and a few (hi-res) animations. Many scenes recycle material, but there is still plenty of variety. The game also includes 16 different outfits for Sylphy to unlock.

ACCESS
You’ll encounter lewd by either agreeing to do stuff for NPC’s or by losing in mini-games. These events are all clearly marked in advance; I think it may be possible to complete the game without seeing lewd, although that’s evidently not what this game is made for.

Once you’ve seen a scene, it remains available in a recollection room in the inn. After completing the game once, a switch appears in the recollection room that allows you to unlock all scenes. There’s also a NG+ option which allows you to carry over stats, gold and more.

The lewd in SSI is excellent. Artwork and text are of high quality, and most notably, original. The artist and writer are remarkably good at putting a new spin on many of the classic tropes in the genre.

VERRO’S VERDICT
I wholeheartedly recommend SSI to any H-RPG-fan. It’s a well-crafted game in almost every aspect, with fast-paced gameplay, very good lewd and above-average amount of replayability. Get it while it’s (or you’re) hot! (Sorry, had to make that pun once).
