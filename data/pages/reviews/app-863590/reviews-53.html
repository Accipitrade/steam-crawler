\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a gem of an indie game. There aren't too many games that can captivate me all the way to the end. If you love space exploration; a good, text based story; slowly building up your ship; gather resources and fight your way thru hordes of enemies or let your diplomacy choices decide the outcome; you can't go wrong with this title. There are twists and turns and you never now what awaits in the next system or behind the next gate.

It took me a while to finish the game, around 35hrs, there is quite a lot of content/universe to uncover. Sometimes I got lost and it was not easy to figure out what the next steps were or how to solve a particular story/puzzle.

The only gribe I had was, that when I finally got my super cool dreadnaught battle ship build, there was nothing left to do with it, except for finishing the final mission and story. The final techs are not easy to get as research points are finite, so by the time I got to the cool builds, I was already in the final end game. Getting there was a lot of fun though!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got this game thinking it sounded interesting. I am a huge fan of space RPG's and this looked like a good one. Sadly I cannot recommend it.
The good,
It does have an interesting story hook.


The Bad
For all its potential it looks and feels like a cross between asteroids from 1979 and galaga from 1981.
movement from place to place takes an eternity, there is really nothing to see while you travel for the most part, and combat consists of your ship shooting dots at other ships as you move around and just like the previously mentioned games spinning in a circle and button mashing seems to work just as much as trying to pursue a target.

For those who remember and enjoy playing the previously mentioned titles this is probably a game you will enjoy, however I cannot endorse it.\t\t\t\t\t
