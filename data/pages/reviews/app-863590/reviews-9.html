\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell worth the money. It has the feel of being a 4X game (Master of Orion, Sins of a Solar Empire, Endless Space, etc.) but narrowed in. You control one ship, and you're landing on/supporting one space station. The other alien races were distinct, believable, and fun. Exploration was compelling and rewarding. The story is interesting and has fun twists. The choices you make matter and are permanent (although you can still revert to earlier saves if you make a decision that goes too badly). At 13$ it was a steal. If I had known I would have liked it this much, I wouldn't have waited for it to go on sale.

The game does have flaws, but they aren't terrible. Here's my list of nit-picks for those who want them:
-In order to unlock every technology, you need to blow up ships of various factions and scavenge the wrecks. This put my diplomatic playstyle at odds with my desire to complete the whole tech tree.
-There is a main story quest that I find objectionable and I feel could really benefit from other conclusions in order to progress the story you need to provoke and commit genocide on a race of sentient bio-weapons that probably deserve it.
-Most of the time the limited guidance adds to the appeal of the exploration. As you get closer to endgame it can get frustrating because the next step is unclear.
-For those of you who care, there are some minor graphical issues from time to time. Flickering, etc. Nothing big.

Again, good game, lots of fun. Well worth the price

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a recommend with caveats. It is both parts inspired and deeply flawed. There are some great mechanics here though I think it leans more towards casual. Combat is not punishing nor is it challenging. If you die it is largely because you stepped away from your screen or rammed into something hard way too fast. MIght be nice if there was an arcade mode or a challenge mode that made combat more punishing.
The crux of the game is exploration and the game offers a lot of exploration, though some does get repetitive and sometimes you get hung up on what to do next. I'm not for games holding your hand, but I have to agree with others that the quest logs could use some improvement. There is a lot of frustration for some of the quests. Searching them is possible, but a chore even if you know what you are looking for.
I enjoyed my playthrough but I don't know if there is enough replayability or challenge to keep me coming back for more.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played through the game and solved most quests. The number of playing hours compares to Gothic I.

This is basically an open world 2D game, which runs on a 3D engine and has nice graphics. The mechanics and level design is very good and balanced: It is fun to play, and solve the puzzles. The dialogues are good, and the NPCs appear authentic. The main story line diverges at the end, so that two different endings are possible.

The game could have been even more addicitive, if it had more animations. For examples, the NPC portraits could be animated while talking, along with audio being played. Also, a 3D view of the player's starship would have been cool, because it is highly customizable and the player identifies with it. The level/story design is really good, but more nonlinearity would add re-play value (e.g. to choose a faction or a specalization).\t\t\t\t\t
