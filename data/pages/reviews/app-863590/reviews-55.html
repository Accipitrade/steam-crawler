\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, I got a little over 15 ours under my belt and the game is already starting to loose tempo. You scan the planets not really interested about the result. You fight the same ships over and over again and you try to expand the known star systems to do more of the same. There is no point in sight, no clear wish on how you want to develop.
The quests rack up in your Mission Log, but no quest pointers are visible. If you pick up the game couple days later you might not even know, from the scarce quest description, what were you supposed to do. And when you finish, the rewards are far from satisfying. Even if you get new tech you have to look manually in the whole research tree not knowing exactly what you opened up. Currencies are more of a hindrance, you eventually get as much as you want to build your ship the way you see fit.
Pros: love the modular ship design and the fact that you can focus on a specific gun system or another
Cons: not much to do. You have a beautiful spaceship that you move from system to system without a sense of purpose or fulfillment. Gets tedious really fast, not much point for replay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRecommended with (big) reservations.

I stopped playing before finishing up what I believe was probably the last lump of main story, because I got tired of the way the game is designed. I like puzzles, okay? I play lots of puzzle games. But I think the developer here is using "hey, it's a puzzle" to cover up the lack of information/guidance.

The way the game is structured right now:
- Explore everything you can find.
- Realize you seem to be unable to proceed.
- Explore everything AGAIN until you figure out what you're missing.
- This opens up a few more systems. Explore them.
- End up at "unable to proceed" again.
- Repeat. Several times.

I think the "well, have you checked everything" or "have you killed enough of this type of enemy to get the crucial drops you need to progress the story" aspects of this game are there to conceal that there isn't all that much else. I mean, exploration is fun but it runs out quickly, combat is fairly boring (there's theoretically positional damage but in practice you just make sure you have more armor and better weapons than the enemy, hold your fire button down, and wait), and without the "wander all over the galaxy hunting for some kind of clue how to proceed" aspect, the main story would be pretty short.

Why give it a thumbs-up? Well, it was compelling enough to play for sixteen or so hours (my time-played stat has a lot of idle time with the game paused) before I got tired of searching space over and over; visually the game is well-designed; I liked all the various little stories on the worlds and the rest of the writing. (I love the obnoxious science officer back on the base and the fact that everybody else hates him. I love how your science officer is so happy about everything new she finds.)

Basically, it's less deep than it would like to be, and it's fun for a while, but ultimately you're going to find there's less there than meets the eye.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat a great game! Combines mulitple genres, mostly action-adventure, RPG, and shoot 'em up. Very reminiscent of classics like Star Control or Sid Meier's Pirates. Fittingly, some of the design elements and game mechanics feel rather "old school": For example, there are no quest markers - you will have to find your own way. That does lead to one of the game's few downsides: At certain times you will propably wander around aimlessly, scratching your head, wondering where to go next. Additional gameplay elements like trading (only implemented rudimentarily) or factions and reputation might have helped to bridge these slight motivational depressions. But overall, I can wholeheartedly recommend Starcom: Nexus to anyone who loves Star Trek, classic adventures and arcade shooting games. A great experience with an engaging story, a dramatic finale and a rewarding ending! Keeping my fingers crossed for a follow up!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not terrible, but I really can't recommend it, especially not at this price. It's got a good idea, but execution is lacking, and overall the game is quite sparse.

Things I like: locational damage, controls feel nice, UI is functional, tries to rip off Star Control 2 (that's always good).

Things I don't like: Lots of backtracking, too much A to B travel time, repetitive gameplay loop, and planetary lander choices seem to boil down to 'have the lander upgrade tech or lose crew.'

Overall, I give the developer a pat on the back for effort and making a game that looks fine and plays fine. It's just not compelling or particularly good. If you're really interested, go for it, but there's nothing to get excited about here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPurchased Starcom after reading several reviews here favorably comparing it to the Escape Velocity series. Having played several hours, I'm not disappointed; it's the closest thing I've played to EV (plus a little Star Control II- not a bad thing!) since the original games. If you liked those, you'll almost certainly enjoy Starcom.

That said, areas to explore so far in the "open world" galaxy have thus far followed a fairly linear path, and there haven't been galaxy-spanning mission strings like in EV, so the game does feel a little shallow at times.

Also the graphics look better in-game than in the screenshots on the store page- they aren't particularly impressive, but not ugly either.\t\t\t\t\t
