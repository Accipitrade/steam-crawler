\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe whole premise is nothing new - you find yourself in a galaxy far far away and are trying to get home.

But... it's boring. You have randomly placed stars with planets near them, connected by wormholes. So you scan every planet and often pick up something, which prompts you to send an away team and usually find resources and/or "research point". From time to time you return to base to research new upgrades and improve your ship. Also from time to time you find someone who doesn't like you, so you shoot them. Rinse and repeat.
Also... it's boring. Everything is far apart, so you spend most time flying somewhere while hypnotizing the distance indicator. At some point, there are no wormholes, so you fly through empty space to the next star. Which is nowhere near as far as the lightyears of emptiness we know in real life, but it's still more staring at nothing and waiting till you get there.
Also also, it kind of sucks. every planet is dead, with nothing there but the remnant of a long-lost civ and the occasional tiny tiny community. You'll meet a few races' traders that are willing to talk instead of shoot, but no settled worlds. The only progress you make is via research points, which are awarded randomly when you see something interesting. No leveling up, no XP from anything, no idea why this is described as RPG.
Last nail in the coffin was autosaving - happens when you pop out of a wormhole. Not when you reach a new star. So after you've spent way too much time looking at the starry background while waiting to check out a bunch of planets, you meet a guys who doesn't like you, but can't defeat him with your lousy tiny Surveyor ship. You explode and find your last save was weeks ago. The only positive thing was that this happened before the refund deadline.\t\t\t\t\t

speed is key. Also If you have enough engines you can just run away from any fight you cant win. Speed also helps you catch comets and probes which can give tons of research points.
2) When you need more firepower research your turrets, really going to be your main weapon. if you do happen to get missles early still not worth it to use them at first since it takes alot of research points before they are actually good. Armor is mostly useless at first so don't waste research on it.
3) Don't forget to research larger ship sizes as you have the resources to make bigger ships.
4) Trade is important for early game advancement since your ship won't be able to take on any difficult enemies, make sure you look up who to trade what to. I never really did any specific trade routes, but anytime I saw a trader I would make any good trades I had available.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely worth the price I paid ($13 USD); on my first play through (normal difficulty spent 2 days during quarantine -> approx. ~ 30 hours for first play through.

Pros:
1. Decent story/plot- Without spoilers; you start off as the captain of a small cargo ship which experiences a spatial rift and get sent to a different universe (sort of like Star Trek Voyager). As the captain you try to explore the known making enemies/allies in the universe while making it back home. Score 4/5

2. Combat system - Enjoyable, and easy to understand for a 2-D game. If you find it too easy or difficult there are several different difficult settings (easier, easy, normal, hard, very hard, ultra) Score 5/5

3. Graphics - For the money you're paying this is amazing; especially the design and color scheme of planets. Score 5/5

4. Lack of troubleshooting/bugs - I was expecting to encounter game bugs/issues with a cheap game. However I experienced 0! While playing through this I found that the community/developer is pretty active on the steam community in addressing issues/bugs or difficulties progressing within the game. Score 5/5

Cons:
1. Ship customization - Since its an open world rpg some of the ship class advancements and weapon/system upgrades are unlocked via quests or combat progression which can decrease the amount of enjoyment while playing (for example many players have unlocked drones so their spaceship can be a "carrier" within my 30 hours I didn't get drone research unlocked.
While progressing through the game the number of equipment or module slots is only unlocked by upgrading your ship - starts at 8 module slots. However at endgame there's no difference between the ship classes since each ship class has 80 module slots. Score 2/5

2. Planet exploration - Becomes a repetitive and time consuming grind since engine upgrades become locked until endgame (hopper function). The exploration results in several two general categories (no anomaly or anomaly detected) when the latter occurs you are given giant text blocks which give you a choices of action which generate a reward or punishment. Score 3/5

3. Mission/quest log - It's almost impossible to distinguish what's a secondary or primary mission for plot progression or fun/resources. Furthermore, the user interface for the log is really basic so it becomes impossible to retrace what certain missions require you to do/where they originated since the game is open world and the mission log builds up. Score 2/5

4. Faction diplomacy / trading - This is probably the most frequent hurdle/difficulty players have with the game. The diplomacy works on a very basic scale with 3 levels: hostile, neutral, or friendly. Your interactions with ships/planets of these factions affect the diplomacy level (no progression/regression bar shown). Trading for the most part seems to be an unnecessary feature in the game (you could waste time and farm an asteroid belt for resources instead of trading). Score 2/5\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played both StarFlight and Star Control II in olden times when they were originally released, and the experiences shaped my expectations through the ages to this era. Those were heady days, when maps were printed and included in the box as a form of piracy protection, and hints were only available if you had a friend who had played the game, too.

Starcom: Nexus is a great game. The pacing and writing are fantastic: new ship upgrades feel empowering and new discoveries feel rewarding after trekking through the expanses between clusters. The narrative guides the player through a galaxy-spanning adventure, full of interesting species and harrowing events. Despite a few clunky mission briefs, I never felt stuck or stymied as there was always someplace else to explore right up to the end.

As an experienced and perhaps jaded gamer, I can't recommend this game more highly. What a refreshing endeavor! I sincerely hope the developer is working on a sequel.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA nifty little game, Starcom: Nexus is a fun top-down space combat game with plenty to do and explore.
The game loop mostly consists of exploring the star cluster and scanning planets, with a good dose of combat in between.
As you destroy enemy ships and send away teams to planets, you will gain resources and research points used to upgrade and customize your ship however you see fit. The shape of the ship doesn't affect how maneuverable the ship is (it's a spaceship, after all), but it does affect module performance and which hitzones you expose. It's a fairly powerful system, and gathering resources to build that epic gun you just researched is very compelling.
It also has a pretty good and engaging story that made me want to see it through to the end, which for me came in about 24h of playtime. Both the main and side stories also have bits of humor sprinkled in to liven up the atmosphere, keeping it from becoming too serious.
The ending itself, however, was a bit lackluster, but honestly I don't really mind for some reason. The journey getting there was pretty good, all throughout, and I don't regret playing it.

One last note: Be aware that you will probably end up in situations where you don't know where to go or what to do next to advance the story. Just keep it chill and explore all the nooks and crannies you haven't been to yet, and pop back to base every now and then too. Also, certain shopkeepers will get new stock of both quest info and artifacts that will unlock solutions as the main story progresses. Check back with them as well. If you're completely stuck, the steam forums probably have the info you're looking for.
Oh, and don't forget to put down text markers on the map for places you can send away teams to more than once. You'll want to be able to more easily find them later. Trust me.

Recommended to anyone who likes top-down space shooters. It won't blow you away by any means, but it's a fun run nonetheless.\t\t\t\t\t
