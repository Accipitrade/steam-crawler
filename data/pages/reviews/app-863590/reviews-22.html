\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter my initial play thru of Starcom I can safely say that I should have purchased this sooner. I only meant to check the game out for a min but it had other plans for me. After pulling myself away because my children needed me (blah blah blah) I realized just how strong Starcoms grip was and I havent felt this way about a game for quite a while. Said children still require my attention so I have to be brief.

Starcom is a very polished and refined game above and beyond other similar indie titles. It features a narrative that has engrossed me paired with gameplay mechanics that are simple but satisfying. It really reminds me of Star Trek more than anything, having you scan planets for anomalies and sending down an away team to gather resources and some story elements. Although I dont know how meaningful they are yet, many of these story elements present you with simple choices similar to FTLs encounters. There is also research for new ship tech which will improve ship speed, weapons and other things. The combat is simple and again satisfying. What the game does really well is mostly in the quality of the UI, the art design and it doesnt feel amateur like many other indie titles in the genre.

I cant wait to play more of this game which is a feeling I havent had in a long time.. I will make sure to update this review with more time played.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCompletely unexpectedly, this game is more about exploration and finding interesting phenomena on planets to discover rather than a trading simulator or combat. The combat is great fun, it has one of those module based ship builders so you can design your own, and the writing is really good. Most of the gameplay involves flying around the galaxy scanning planets, and many of them have a paragraph of text / event with choices and they're really interesting. Way longer than I was expecting, which I guess can be good or bad? Can definitely recommend it for 20 hours or so, but all the reports I'm reading say the end game is broken and the plot is never fully explained.

Pros
- Build your own ship out of blocks
- Big research tree
- Really captures that Star Trek exploration feel
- Excellent writing
- Lots of planets have anomalies which have short narrative choices to make, and can yield injuries or resources

Cons
- If I need to find a specific planet to complete a quest, there are no tools to do that at all. I have to zoom in on the map looking for a planet with an exclamation mark. Can't filter or search
- Some quest markers point to the wrong places
- Sometimes the map is wrong. There are a few planets which I've surveyed with no results. - - Sometimes when I open the map, those planets are marked as 'Unsurveyed Planet'
- There are several quests that just give you a description of a planet or an image. There are hundreds of planets in the game, so inspecting them all individually and comparing them to a screenshot isn't going to happen.
- By the endgame, you have a mission log full of quests, and no idea where to complete any of them
- Depending on which order you do things, it's impossible to research story-critical items late-game
- Some research doesn't function correctly
- Buggy\t\t\t\t\t
