\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a love letter to the original Star Control. There is very little procedural generation, so no replay value, but it is a beautifully written and executed space exploration RPG with plenty of content to enjoy.

* Around 30 hours of content (I think I pretty much explored it all).
* A few bugs still in the game, but the developer seems to still be releasing patches.
* a "no hand holding" approach to quests. Take notes as you go! I enjoyed solving the clues.

I wish the developer would add procedural generation for a sandbox mode for some replay value. As others have mentioned, a wave combat type game mode focused on combat and ship upgrades would also be amazing. I don't think either of those will ever happen, though, and the game is still worthwhile in its current state.\t\t\t\t\t

This is the game I wished (fantasised?) that the new Star Control(s) would have been. A brilliant experience with a strong old-school influence and great atmosphere. The story had just the right touch of whimsy and humour along the epic Sci-Fi theme, the battle visuals and mechanics were amazing, the planet artwork was lovely, and the music complemented the atmosphere perfectly. Only blemishes I can think of are a handful of small bugs and some missions whose difficulty "spiked" a bit, mostly due to non-obvious solutions, but the sum of the experience more than made up for that. Very well worth recommending!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan this game was fun. I am an old Sci-Fi fan and to see a lot of the good old Sci-Fi tropes coming up during exploration of the games galaxy was very entertaining. Also building your ship up from a pathetic little tug to a behemoth bristling with weapons and advanced technology was very satisfying.
The story was also quite good. It had me invested in the mystery which had me playing for hours on end.
The game could be considered to be repetitive by many, but I didn't find this to be a deterrent in this instance. The game was fun enough to play that it wasn't too noticeable by the end.
I will say that getting to the end I did find the ship progression ended a bit too soon. After you reach certain technological milestones the game gets quite easy. I would've also liked to make my ship even bigger as I found myself sitting on a pile of unused resources. That could very well be because I was quite thorough in exploring every nook and cranny of the galaxy, meaning the ship I ended up with was justifiably overpowered by the endgame.

Please make a sequel, Devs. You have a pre-purchase with me :)\t\t\t\t\t
