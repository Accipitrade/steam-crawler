\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very good space exploration game. Nails the feeling of awe, excitement and fear that comes from being pioneers out in a strange new environment. Gives you the confidence to try things and play while at the same time keeping things fairly tight and not letting you be overwhelmed. Succeeds by focusing on the things that you want to do the most, explore, land away teams and fight other ships/stations instead of making things overcomplicated and fiddly like so many other space games. The writting does a great job of setting the mood and immersing you in the world too. Some of the later puzzles and quest lines can be a tad obscure but I think for some people that might be a plus. Is a decent length for the game that it is, if it were longer I think it might outstay it's welcome. Having said that a sequel or followup with a little more depth to the interaction with other races would have some excellent potential. I had a great time with this and am looking forward to whatever this studio produces next.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great example of where indie gaming can be superior to AAA titles. Overall, Starcom: Nexus is fantastic. It has an interesting and creative story (albeit not wholly original, but some people like myself may never tire of this type of story - avoiding any further description to avoid spoilers) which evolves and is discovered throughout the gameplay. The Research/Ship Building concept is clean, concise and fun - offering a solid gameplay dynamic that ultimately could have been expanded even further with more options or even extended to the Station itself (expansion, perhaps?). And overall the gameplay is fun and entertaining while being light enough that you can step in and out of it quickly - it is easy to pause and save as needed.

What some may see (and have criticized) as failings - some quests too difficult or obscure, for example - I would say is an example of indie gaming greatness. It's not perfect, perhaps there are parts that are somewhat lumpy and inconsistent - but the exploration of that inconsistency provides great variety and the core of the game has been very well executed.

Recommend playing through on minimum of Hardcore difficulty until you have beaten the game.\t\t\t\t\t
