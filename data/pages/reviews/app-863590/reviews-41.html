\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs I have aged I have found that open world non-linear games have lost their appeal. I do not have time for a 120hr sink anymore. I want decent visuals, fun game play, and a decent story. This game provides these in spades. I love being able to switch up my ship all through, experimenting with different formats. The alien races are nothing special and the diplomacy could use a little complexity but it's a minor detail.

The story was unexpectedly good. Typically I don't like certain science fiction dramas featuring a certain type of questionable technology, that makes you do mental gymnastics, but I will let it slide. The mystery was excellent.

The audio/visuals were outstanding. Cool soundtrack, explosions, weapons visual effects. All of it.

Highly recommend this for anyone wanting to spend 50 hours or less.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt gets so many thing right that it's hard to give this a thumbs down. But I'm going to anyway, because this game does not respect your time. It tries to do the "thoughtful exploration of an interesting universe" in a way that just simply doesn't work for the kind of game it is.

The combat is okay, but only just good enough to not be a negative. Certainly isn't interesting. The storytelling is okay, though almost all of the story is just flat text with two options, Proceed or Come Back Later. Literally every planet has the option to "get stuff" or "not get stuff". And the 2nd option is almost never the right choice. Diplomacy is just "follow the correct path down this linear chat tree", with no interesting interaction or dialog.

It's like you design a game to do "everything", and then make everything 4/10 quality. It does those things passably, but nothing really well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall this is a fun adventure and well worth your time and money. The main story-line is engaging, with an excellent slow reveal of what's happening, which really pulls the game together and gives you a reason to progress. Many side vignettes add additional flavor and reward for exploration. The core gameplay and progression loop is tight and well-paced, at least through the late midgame; there's always just one more thing to do before you finish up for the night.

On the other hand, the game isn't without its flaws. Although gameplay mechanics are basically fun, I found them quite shallow. Combat is straightforward, with little diversity in available play-style. The complexity of the tech tree and ship design is largely superficial: if you look closely, it really boils down to deciding how you want to allocate your points between firepower, defense, and mobility. I also found that the progression mechanics fell apart a bit in the endgame: I landed in a bit of a "now what" situation where I've unlocked all marginally useful technologies, maxed out my ship size, explored every obvious next thing to explore, and it wasn't obvious what to do to progress the story. In theory I think I was expected to remember some vague hints encountered weeks (real world time) ago; in practice I asked the google what detail I'd missed, which doesn't make for the most satisfactory of conclusions. The difficulty curve is also a bit off, in that the game becomes trivial as you progress (but you can just increase the difficulty to compensate, so whatever).

Bottom line: you'll be too busy choosing which interesting thing to explore next to notice the imperfections. Just make sure you explore everything and note the locations of inconclusive investigations, because they will be on the final exam ;)\t\t\t\t\t
