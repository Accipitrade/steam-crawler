\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: The game is a bit less tedious with "reasonable" cheats - giving several thousand extra research points (toward thrust, energy, and utility tech) and at least 100+ of "rare" resources, and 500+ of common ones at or near start.

It's still a slog. The in-game quest system is a joke. There is no way to focus in on a quest (even through research) and I have unresolved quests and planets from 4 hours in game that I still have no clue how to solve at hour 16. Should I scan a random planet 10 jumps away? Maybe that unlocks things. But even if it does, the quest doesn't update. I need to remember someone was looking for something, and that I've found that something. And then scan 400 solar systems to see the ones with unresolved issues, and then hope the description matches up.

There is good content here, and it could be an engaging game, but the execution botches itself so many times.

This feels like half the game it was supposed to be. What's there could be entertaining, but balance and pacing is horribly off. Every. Thing. Is. A. Grind. And it's a pointless grind.

Resources, which the game gives out in small pieces, can't be acquired in meaningful quantity early-mid game through combat or exploration. Nope, you need to exploit the economy. Just look up a guide, there's no point trying to decipher it yourself. Note, this also involves a lot of tedious traveling between systems.

Research, used to make your piddling ship do anything even remotely well, is the least gated. It's still gated, because you need thousands of credits to get weapons, energy, utility functions, and engines. Oh boy do you need engines. 3 different kinds of engines. Oh, and each take a nice quantity of rare resources to build.

Travel: You expect from the early game that there will be a lot of connecting lanes (system to system) and warp gates (travel to any other discovered warp gate). An hour in you warp gates start to disappear. You're stuck doing 8 system-system jumps before you find another one. Meanwhile you can't change, upgrade, or research and enemies get progressively harder, but to backtrack means 20 minutes travel back and forth.

So you finally find a warp gate, return home, use your 1,000+ research credits to upgrade your ship and find out you can't build the new engine type, or weapon type due to resources. You've fully scanned every system not teeming with enemies, and you're still unable to access T2 components. Meanwhile the story plotlines are still a dozen system hops past your furterest exploration point.

What does the game want you to do?

VISIT SYSTEMS NOT CONNECTED BY STARLANES! How long will that take? 1-3 minutes depending on engines, tech, and energy. It's just you and setting auto thrust, and then managing your boost. Nothing else happens. There is nothing but the void. Finally reach the system, spend a minute or two scanning the planets, hope you get one of the rare scripted events for new tech, and spend 1-3 minutes going back. SUCH AMAZING GAMEPLAY!\t\t\t\t\t
