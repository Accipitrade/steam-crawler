\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStory? Good. Gameplay? Good. Graphic, sound and UI? ¯\\_(ツ)_/¯

In short: You're a young officer who's ship get immediately whisked away far from home. With the help of a space station which conveniently follow you there, you explore those new strange surroundings. Make friends or enemies. Explore a ton of planets/stars/derelicts/probes/comets/aliens/space seeds. Risk your crew to explore, do SCIENCE, forage and/or die in horrible situations.

Gameplay: You build your ship like a lego based on parts you start with and earn over time. It's prettier than many such systems for what that is worth. Everything you do, see, learn, trade, etc., can potentially get you points toward research and toward a detailed tech tree. A lot of it is partially hidden and unlock based on your in-game actions.

Example: Kill missile carrying ships, salvage some missile parts falling off from their exploding ships, repeat a few time and "BOOM, SCIENCE!" you unlock missile research. Then you go after comets and catch a few probe, maybe explore a few planet and trade your old tech with aliens... and before you know it you got enough SCIENCE(tm) to research and upgrade said missiles and get you started toward the many missile upgrade branches.

There's about ten currencies but it's all a lot simpler than it looks like at first. Simply upgrading your trading tech, mining a few thousands asteroids, killing and exploring will get you rich enough to buy and do everything requiring them.

You've got shields, you've got jump-drives, drones, a bunch of shooters and beams and whatnots, it's all there. A lot of the planets are randomized so there's no two runs exactly the same.

The rest: I like pretty graphics as much as the next guy so I had to do somewhat of a double-take with this one. At very, very first glance, there's not much to see with Starcom: Nexus and the first UI elements that pop-out are extremely basic. I find those useless screen buttons ugly and the armor and energy bars should be placed and detailed better. Anyways, it gets much better once you start playing and see all the pretty effects the game has to offer and expand your ship beyond the useless, tiny, slow peashooter you start with.

An instant classic as far as I'm concerned. Some rough edges here and there so far still early after release (review based on 1.09-preview) but on the flipside the dev is still actively working on patches at the moment. Normal difficulty is extremely easy and any veteran videogamer is strongly suggested to ramp the difficulty to the max.

A solid 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm having a lot of fun with this one. It kind of reminds me of old school space exploration games like starflight 1 + 2. You zoom around the game universe exploring and collecting resources while solving local problems. But as you explore, the larger story emerges organically from the encounters you have.

The game does a nice job of giving you new tools to play with as the game goes on, so you feel a sense of advancement. I love tinkering with the ship, and exploring the extensive tech tree.

I'm not finished with the main story, but it seems to be a pretty entertaining sci-fi tale, told mostly through text.. I've been impressed with how funny it is as well. There are a number of fairly subtle sci-fi in-jokes and some of the alien interactions are pretty amusing. Downsides would be that the pacing is a little weird given a lot of manual travel, but there are upgrades you can buy to take some of the sting out of that.

Well worth the price.\t\t\t\t\t
