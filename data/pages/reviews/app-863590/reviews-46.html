\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game - especially to kill some time. It requires little to no serious strategy and you'll spend hours cruising space 'treasure-hunting'. It has some serious downsides, but the sheer addictive nature kept me coming back.

Visually, it is a very simple and pretty game. Combat is almost a throwback to 80s arcade game and you pretty much just got to point and click. Your weapon choice and placement is not too crucial (im pretty sure you could beat this game with just the early weapons and upgrades). Customizing your ship is easy, and a lot of fun. You can create a fast ship or a behemoth bristling with guns.

The story is okay. You have very vague goals and most of your time your up looking for 'this or that' for another quest. The exploration panels are very well written and evolve as you progress through the game, remarking on similar past expeditions.

Roleplaying is very limited. You interact with the same talking heads who give back canned feedback. Your crew is silent and really only serve as a number to be replenished after they die in combat.

The main aspect of the game is exploration... and you will spend a lot of time flying through blackness. This part of the game is both a blessing and a curse. You can watch netflix / TV on the side as you wait for five minutes to the next planet. Upgrade engines as soon as possible.

If you are looking to fly around exploring lost worlds, get into epic battles, but don't wanna pull your hair out... then this is the game for you.\t\t\t\t\t

So what is Starcom Nexus?

Starcom Nexus is one of those top down space games where you fly a spaceship. Like the old Escape Velocity or Star Traders.

The game has a pretty simple gameplay loop. You have a ship, you explore the universe and survey planets in search of anomalies and answers. Doing this gives you resources and research points which you use to upgrade your ship, weapons, systems etc. so you can explore further and into more dangerous territory.

The game features a huge galaxy to explore with several races that are either friendly or not. The ship builder is based on blocks (armor, weapons, generator, engines) and you can assemble your ships like it's made of LEGO.

The quest/exploration design is pretty good and it's not always "follow the waypoint". You are gonna need to think, read hints and ultimately give up and look at a guide succeed. It's surprisingly well made even though some quests might be a little too obscure.

This is the extent of the gameplay. There is a bit of trading but it's really simple, don't look at becoming a space trucker, this is not the game. There is no fleet, you only have one ship and there are no random missions or bounty hunts that you can do for cash. There is no cash. The entire galaxy is hand made and everything is placed with a purpose.

This game has surpassed my expectations and is surreal to think it was made by a one-man dev team.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was completely blown away by this game, I picked up Starcom Nexus on sale and I wasn't really expected much of anything, but I ended up finding a real gem with this one. The gameplay is super simple on the surface, but as the story progresses and you unlock abilities/technologies it really starts to add layers and depth that keeps it fresh and interesting throughout. The real winner here is the ship customization system which lets you change things up and helps keep the game from getting too repetitive.

The story is absolutely outstanding. There are little bits of interesting lore all over the galaxy, some are important to the main plot and some are not but all of them add a ton of depth to the universe of Starcom Nexus. I was especially happy with the various alien races that you meet along the way, each of which has its own unique style.

If you get a chance, I highly recommend picking this game up. It's great for at least one playthrough, and I have a feeling I'll be back for another run at it in the future.\t\t\t\t\t
