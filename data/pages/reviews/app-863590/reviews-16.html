\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy initial feeling is this game evokes the feeling of a game like Star Control 2 without being a clone or a spiritual sequel or anything of that sort, really it's just a very fine example of a game in that 'you are in an unexplored sector of space, time to meet aliens, investigate mysteries, find resources, and upgrade your ship' genre.

It is more streamlined than a lot of entries in this genre, overall I felt I was able to do a lot more interesting things with my time, even gathering random resources or blindly exploring large empty areas of space wasn't nearly as time consuming as it could have been, fairly short diversions before getting back to more exciting things.

I did find normal difficulty wasn't very hard except for a few spots, and it didn't take long to power up and make those parts doable, but by the last half or last third of the game none of the normal encounters were any threat and no longer required tactics. There are many difficulty levels above normal so this isn't a big problem, and for this type of game I would much rather feel too strong rather than have a whole universe 'scale up' to make my progression meaningless. Overall I'd say the length of the game is perfect for what it is, and I found getting from start to finish was clear enough that I was able to keep momentum, which is rare for me.

If I had a foible it would be the map system. I wish there was a separate map level for system view, inter-system view and entire sector view, because the way the game frequently zooms your map out automatically and doesn't bring it back in to the system-view level when you arrive at a destination made it hard for me to get my bearings.

I also wish there was a way to set a custom target or waypoint that would appear as a pip on the edge of the main game window, currently you can set waypoints but they only appear when the map is open, and sure i can move around in map view but some things do not appear on the map. It may also help to have a better indicator of whether the direction my ship is traveling or pointing is actually intersecting with the point I want to go to or not, perhaps a dotted line that extends from the front of the ship icon on the map.

These are pretty minimal things but I guess I didn't have that many problems with the game really. Actually that reminds me, most baffling moment was before realizing that after entering a nexus my ship could coast on its momentum outside of the nexus, leaving me unable to choose a destination. It would make sense if the map would automatically close when it does this, or better yet if the ship would just stop moving when interacting with the nexus.\t\t\t\t\t
