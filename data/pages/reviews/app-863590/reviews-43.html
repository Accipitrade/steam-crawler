\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStarcom Nexus is an excellent little indie gem.

I went in with apprehensive expectations, I don't normally like hex-style building and I also don't enjoy games that look/feel arcadey in terms of combat.

However, Starcom was able to keep my glued to the screen with a mix of excellent world building and storytelling, aswell as a host of interesting little anomalies across what feels like a very expansive, but rich galaxy.

If you get a dopamine hit from researching anomalies in stellaris, this game will be dangerously addictive for you.

The only thing you shouldn't come here looking for is replay value, you can do just about everything you want to in one run.
It's an excellent game and is absolutely worth your money, especially when on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an incredible game, you should definitely buy it. It has a brilliant story, beautiful graphics, satisfying progression, exploration and resource mining. I would describe it as a combination of SPAZ and Planet's Edge, but better than any of the two. Here is why I love the game:

  • The story is incredibly cool. Not just the main story, but also the side stories are well researched, insightful and stick with you.
  • I've read somewhere that the game has a "Star Trek" vibe, and I totally agree. In the best sense possible, it's clear the author drew at least some inspiration from that.
  • Technology research is incredibly satisfying and so is shipbuilding.
  • Experimenting ship designs is free, i.e., you can try out cannon X and recycle it losslessly, which is AMAZING because it allows to play around without being penalized for not knowing how to build a good ship at the first try.
  • I love that quicksaving/loading is incredibly fast and can be done anywhere. While some people like the roguelike "haha sry something unexpected happened, now you're screwed" thing, that's a total turnoff for me and saving frequently avoids exactly that.
  • The author is a total nerd (again, best possible sense) and has so many funny references everywhere. E.g., to the Rockwell Retro Encabulator. I absolutely LOVE IT.
  • Progression is possible without a walkthrough and puzzles have the right difficulty to them. Although admittedly I'm now stuck very late game, I refuse to lookup help because I am SURE that I can figure it out.
  • Shooting and upgrading your ship is so FUN. Even mining is fun, oh geeze I could do that all day. It looks pretty and is just cool.
  • Enemies are "scary". And well-designed and nicely drawn. Just very cool vibe.
  • It runs incredibly well on Linux (even with multi-monitor setup, which even some AAA titles struggle with sometimes). Even though this point is last, this is REALLY important to me, but the story really is even better.

I have read on the website of the game that there is possibly a followup. You can believe that I'll buy it in a heartbeat, unseen.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is absolutely amazing - it has a nice story, pretty cool ship building mechanic and what I like the most, a really unique combat system (you can shoot off some parts of enemy ships without outright destroying them, leaving them either defenseless or immobile and you can come back to them later).

For what is at first sight a rather simple game with a single developer, it is a really rich game with an amazing open world galaxy (you have free will, you make the choice of going where you want, doing what you want and playing the game how you want) - you can choose to follow the main story, do some side quest, destroy some enemy base or just casually explore and gain resources.

Most of all, I was rather amazed by the fact that during my whole playthrough, the game didn't crash even a single time and I didn't meet any game-breaking bugs either (important to make note that even that it doesn't seem at first glance, the game is quite deep and complex with some of its mechanics which makes it even more impressive). Considering this game just recently relased and has ONLY ONE DEVELOPER, I say this is truly amazing and shows the work and love the developer put in this game.

I highly recommend this game, it offers a fair amount of hours of entertainment for its price, has some replayability value and is overall quite nice and relaxing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEnjoyed this game immensely. Thought there were some bits I wasn't as fond of purely due to the nature of how I broke up playing the game and the details I picked up on.

Make no mistake, this game can easily be considered a puzzle game, but its focus is on exploration. The combat wasn't the focus, but it certainly was fun. Fighting could be intense and satisfying, and by the end of the game your ship turns from barely-capable hauler to unbreakable fortress or unstoppable typhoon of dakka.

For the most part, however, I feel this plays more an entertaining background to the meat of the game; anomalies. A diligent captain should make note of everything he finds, as it just might be of use later to find hidden worlds and powerful friends. I, for one, loved immersing myself in the role of the captain trying to solve the mystery of the strange universe they may have stumbled into. Where were they? What happened? Was this the future? The past? An alternate reality? But also, how to defend the lives of my crew and of kite station, and to find a way home for those people.

Every planet scanned and every unsolved anomaly was a step into what felt like great intertwined mysteries. Not everything was significant, but most everything was certainly apart of the picture of the galaxy, for what it once was and for what it is now. History is evident, but it isn't simply spat into your face but something to be inferred from all the information you've found.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first I was a bit disappointed in it not liking the loose controls and some lack of polish and feel. But after giving it a bit longer I appreciate the breadth of exploration this game provides. It has a similar feel to one of my favorite games of yesteryear Star Saga One (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_Saga). Its more about the story and less about action.

Discovering and exploring space and revealing little short stories at most every new planet you find becomes quite interesting. So far have not run into any duplicate story points beyond just a few but they are handled well in that it realizes its a repeat and is written differently accordingly.

Overall a nice little game that has been entertaining me.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile it might look simple, this game will drag you in and keep you playing all night long.
It has constant new quest hooks that don't become overwhelming
It has a decent combat balance that together with the simple but good ship building and research tree makes you feel like you are growing and making a difference, without making the game to easy
It constantly gives the feeling that there is an overarching world & story in the background without forcing you through it, instead pulling you along with the minor quests and exploration.
Oh and the exploration... while on first look it seems like there is a definite path to follow with the gate network, you'll quickly learn that there's more on the unbeaten path than you think and that that enormous trip to the faraway start starts looking less and less daunting as you go.
Definitely an easily overlooked game that's worth a big recommendation!\t\t\t\t\t
