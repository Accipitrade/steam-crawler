\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you enjoyed the "flying around probing planets, collecting resources and reading interesting blurbs about them" portion of Mass Effect... well that's 75% of this game. It does a great job of simulating hopping around a galaxy exploring, collecting resources and clues, and fighting opponents.
The ship building and combat mechanics are really simple. On Normal difficulty it's only really a challenge in the early game.
Unfortunately, the later game turns into a bit of tedium as you try to put together the actual plot threads and figure out what you need to do to advance the story. There's a fair bit of riddle- and puzzle-solving, and trial-and-error deep space exploration hoping you stumble on to something. It can especially get frustrating when you know you've seen a planet or space object you need to visit before, but can't remember which, out of ht e hundreds you've done, it was.
Another quibble: solving things is pretty linear. There's not much to do but increase your ship's combat power and speed/jump range. There's no specialization in science or diplomacy or stealth or anything like that to give you different options.
But don't get me wrong, it has the bones of a great game, especially for an indie developer!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWe'll try be brief.

Pros

- The game is well animated with beautiful detail, and great effects that simulate space well (or at least the idea of space). It's very easy on the eyes while not being bland.
- The plot and story are well written AND engaging. The hard sci-fi has wonderful attention to detail while still managing to be funny.
- The soundtrack is aesthetically fitting, though I couldn't call it memorable. It's pleasant though, and never jarring.
- Ship design is completely modular with many researchable technologies allowing for great flexibility in ship layout. All physical changes to your ship are completely reversible at no cost from home base, so you never need to fear wasting resources or "ruining" your run.
- The core game loop is a tad linear but exploration is vast and generally rewarding. There's little hand-holding though and the game sometimes expects you to figure things out on your own. That is a pro to me, but may not be to others. YMMV.

Cons

- Combat is not very hard. Particularly in the late game, most fights were nuisances at best.
- 1st world problem alert Research points are exceedingly plentiful, especially later, so completing all research trees in one play is not only possible, but probable. You can try all technologies and builds in one play through, removing a lot of incentive to replay the game.
- The crew mechanic in the game is maybe too forgiving? As your ship takes damage and experiences various events your crew members can die. As your crew members go down, the overall effectiveness of your ship starts to lower. Weapons do less damage or fire more sluggishly, shields regenerate slower, and hull repairs take longer. You start with a maximum of 15, but this can be increased through research trees and ship modifications. The mechanic itself is a great idea that's executed well. However...

You can regain all lost crew by visiting your HQ, infinitely and at no cost. Like a Pokemon Centre.

This should probably not be that easy given the setting and player circumstances.
Spoiler Your entire crew as well as one space station have become stranded in another galaxy where there are no other humans and limited resources.

Overall, it's a great experience and any "cons" are quibbles at best. Definitely recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFrom a game mechanics perspective, this smacks of FTL, ATARI's Asteroids, and old top-down Star Trek space combat games I played as a kid, albeit with improved graphics. It's fairly simple space combat and exploration mechanics with a minor choose-your-own-adventure and/or RNG aspect to some away missions, but it feels polished and fun, even with an entire galaxy ahead of you to explore in similar fashion.

Where the game really shines is from a writing perspective. This is a love ballad to sci-fi enthusiasts, with references to classic books, some never brought into mainstream awareness through movies or episodic content, which have made myself and a friend chuckle repeatedly, with excited whispers of "Wait, was that a reference to suchandsuch from whicheverbook? Oh man, these people are *nerds* <3". What's more, you don't need to get the references to really enjoy the writing and feel like you're just catching glimpses of ancient civilizations and mysteries you'll never fully unravel.

If you want a fun space adventure story with enjoyable combat and a universe to explore that feels both expansive and well-populated, I highly recommend giving this one a try.\t\t\t\t\t
