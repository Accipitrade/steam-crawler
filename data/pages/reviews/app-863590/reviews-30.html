\r\n\t\t\t\t\tsimple (read: proven) mechanics + made with love = surprisingly entertaining

The game is kind of a jewel. There is ship designing, trading, factions, a lot of exploring and quests. All mechanics are rather simple but this fits the game over all.

While the combat is action, the overall tone of the game is slow. You travel around, speak to different species, collect clues to solve riddles and enjoy the small stories told.

Talking about the main story and the smaller stories: this is where the game sticks out. Many planets have their own tiny story, each species has their own history. While there are some repeating events (mostly around finding resources) the game never gets dull and one always want to fly to the next system to see if there is something new.

When it comes to quest, you have a log (of all received messages), a map (where you can stick notes on) and sometimes even a quest marker. When there is no quest marker, you'll actually need to use your brain to make sense of the clues given. Which is quiet refreshing compared to some other modern (AAA) games. Some riddle are even supposed to be solved on paper, for example, triangulating a position.

For project of a single person the game is amazing. Of course there are some minor discrepancies here and there but overall I've enjoyed the game a lot.\t\t\t\t\t

2. Fun combat.
3. Great writing and sense of mystery for the first 60-70% of the game.

The Bad:
1. The story turns into glorious, ridiculous nonsense at the end. All sense of wonder, dread and mystery is thrown out the window.

3. Ship combat is really fun in the beginning of the game, but by the end it kinda sucks. No tactics, no strategy, just blast away.

Summary:
Great game for the first two thirds. But the last third is a massive letdown. Such a shame. I really wanted to love this game!!\t\t\t\t\t
