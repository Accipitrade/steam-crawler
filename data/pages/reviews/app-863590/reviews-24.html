Where do I need to go? Am I there yet?

Starcom: Nexus is a space exploration, top-down space shooter game in which you try to find your way back home in an unknown galaxy using customizable ship designs and researchable technologies.

Initially I thought this game would be fun to play, and it was, but after getting closer to the end game the game felt nothing more than a directionless travelling simulator.

Pros

- The gameplay feels solid, visuals and performance are nice, and the combat is satisfying, as you are able to shoot off your opponent's blocks individually with neatly made particle effects.

- Researching technologies is quite impactful and worth paying your attention to. Your Plasma Cannons for example can shoot at double the range with the appropiate research.

- The "Choose you own Adventure" story segments in the planetary anomalies are often well written. Picking a choice in one of these mini-stories could give you resources or make you lose crew members, so read and plan carefully.

Cons

- One of the major issues and design flaws with this game is the travelling. While being able to fly anywhere you want sounds good on paper, you will soon realize that the galaxy is quite big. While multiple solar systems are connected through wormhole networks, certain more isolated solar systems require you to fly all the way to them, essentially taking 3 - 10 minutes of just holding Shift + W, depending on how fast your ship is.
This becomes much easier to manage with mid-to-late game technologies, but until then, you're gonna have to endure this slog, which already, can turn off certain people.

I highly recommend researching engine upgrades and using as many engines as possible, as that will save you hours in the long run.

- Besides the annoying travelling, the game often only makes vague hints on where you need to go and thus makes you travel around the galaxy without a clear directive. In my case I was flying around aimlessly throughout the galaxy in mid-game in the hopes to find more solar systems that either unlock new missions or add progress to the main story.

- The alien interactions in this game is very basic. Either you trade with them or fight them. That's generally the gist of it.

I already got my money's worth of playtime, the vast majority of which was just flying around in deep space. I do admit that the game was very enjoyable when I knew where to go and when I was in a solar system full of surprises. But I'm not planning on spending any more hours drifting around the galaxy again. But if that's your thing, all up to you.\t\t\t\t\t
