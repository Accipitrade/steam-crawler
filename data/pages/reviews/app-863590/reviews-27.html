\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHate to give this a negative, but it's really "meh" in my opinion. The game idea is right up my alley, the writing is nice, events are varied, graphics/music/etc. are all fine for what they are. It's almost like you're playing a version of Wayward Terran Frontier, with just more stuff implemented (but less depth).

That said, the game is super buggy, and the creators don't seem to have made much effort to handle this. It plays like an early access/beta-testing game. How so? Well aside from the game regularly locking up late game (acknowledged by devs, but > 10 months since last patch and not resolved, happened to me 4x in the past 2 hours before writing this review), the game invites you to play it "out of order" (by nature of just stumbling around trying to find what to do). The problem here is, the missions/etc. don't account for that, and you break your game by doing things out of order (IE: destroy that science station? great, you'll never finish that mission), this has happened to me repeatedly, finding stuff before I was meant to (because I had nothing left to do except randomly wander).

A lot of the game surrounds "exploring", but as others have said, distances are vast, you have no meaningful scanners, and unless you enjoy a game where you fly a slow ship (relatively speaking) in a grid pattern desperate to find that one planet/artifact/anomaly [black on black of course] which you missed (because even at maximum ship upgrades, your scanning "vision" is probably around 0.1% x 0.1% of the active game play space, if you tried to do a grid scan of the entire map (just driving across it, fastest possible ship, 1500au or whever scanners max out at, etc.), you'd probably need over a week to cover all of the space.

I tried pretty hard to finish this game, but eventually just got frustrated. I can't find anything new, game isn't providing any new prompts/hints/etc., I presume it's probably some event I did out of order breaking the story line, and only my desire to *complete* the game was driving my past 8+ hours of game-play.

Overall, a great idea, but it just lacks so much polish that it's not fun, and there's no hints it's going in that direction until pretty far into the game (>6 hours). It's a grind to find anything late game, hidden arbitrarily on an unreasonably large map, with zero hint about how to find some stuff aside from simply meandering around for hours upon hours hoping you'll stumble upon something. I should have cut bait a long time ago.\t\t\t\t\t
