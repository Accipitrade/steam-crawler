\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPicked this up early access, and despite the expected "bumps" the game sucked me in and wouldn't let go.

A 104 hrs play through for me. Not sure on the replay factor for me, so I took my time. My early access start and the nature of emerging content didn't make for great continuity for my experience (the nature of early access) but over all a very satisfying play through and smart overall storyline.

The good:
- The controls are simple but effective. I ran old school keyboard & mouse, but the Xbox controller layout looks doable.
- Just enough ship building, & tech research aspects of the game to allow you to both make it your own in the looks department but also enable your own play style without holding back the game flow.
- Resource management system is cool. The different resources are used to build your ship components and act as the currencies. The twist here for me is no common currency in game, which makes trading interesting. Resource scarcity was never an issue as they can be mined, scavenged, traded and gained through mission rewards.

The not so good:
- The graphics are pretty simplistic, (I like the style) so I didn't expect the display artifacts and occasional display lock ups throughout my play through. Again it's still not final so I assume it will become a non-issue as they approach final release.
- Game's save are automatic only. All save points are kept (you can go back) but you can't rename them, so bad decision recovery is tricky but possible.
- The story is great but the games execution of it is a bit too loose to carry you the whole way through. Simply put expect to get lost often, and don't expect the games help to get back on track. Use the forums....

Overall I really liked this game. Its was a bit bumpy but well worth my time for one run through and got me off my butt to write this.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame and engine have great potential but this game falls short in a single player platform....

*Main Issues*

*Story follows a linear path that you will routinely hit snags in and will have to back track and slog through a messy poorly organized journal to find what you missed to get back on track.
*There is no official wiki or other source to get help except the steam community board. If your data mining skills aren't up to par the game essentially ends for you. (44 hours for me and I still haven't found the clues to unlock the sphere or even turn off the sentinels)
*Combat is the best part of this game but once you reach a certain point of tech development it doesn't matter anymore.
*Interaction with other races is limited and they serve really no other purpose but the occasional trade or story hint.
*Music is ho-hum and gets repetitive quickly.
*Lots of ship design options but the variance boils down to shields vs armor and blasters vs lasers vs missiles or drones.
*$20 is a bit much for this title, perhaps if there were some kind of multiplayer option...

At this points I don't even care about seeing the single player endgame I just want to see this game re-made as an MMO with crafting and clan battles for territory etc.

The ships aren't premade, they're modular. Want another plasma cannon? Go ahead. Maybe another shield generator, or missile launcher? Have at. Whatever you like, within the size/research limits you have at the time. I found being able to customize my own ship down to the last module to be a lot of fun as I took it out to fight and explore. There are a lot of possibilities here, I barely touched on them to avoid spoiling things excessively.

There's a story to the game. No, really, it's not some flimsy pretense to get you into fights (though there's no shortage of fights!). Main mission story, side missions, a variety of races with different personalities and fighting styles. I consider this game to be money well spent, and look forward to coming back for more play with future updates.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSolid top down space sim with a surprisingly deep story featuring a few nice twists/revelations.

Gameplay can be a little slow to start but once you find the rhythm of exploration + research moving around and combat becomes much more fluid. Combat and ship building is simple but enjoyable and later game tech trees allow for a few interesting builds although one or two modules still feel like they need a bit more of a purpose.

The main and side questlines can sometimes become a little confusing and it can be a little frustrating to lose your way from time to time but generally the game provides most of the information you need, also the side quests are impactful and worth completing to acquire more resources and research opportunities.

I enjoyed my playthru as the game is in it's current state and look forward to future patches to see what they add.\t\t\t\t\t

Upsides:

-Practically bug-free (I have found none)
-Nonlinear gameplay, you can almost always dictate what to do and where to explore
-Neat graphics

Downsides:

-Controls take a time to get used to
-View is locked into a top-down view that can't be rotated either
-There are a few points at which you don't really know what you're supposed to do to advance

Suggestions:

-To really spice it up, the ability to command a whole fleet rather than just a single ship would be great
-There ought to be more customizable content, such as the appearance of interactable characters

Overall rating: 4.5/5\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the only game I've played that is feels like the spiritual successor to Star Control 2, and I've played a lot of space games over more than a decade.

For zoomers: Star Control 2 is the king of all 2D space simulations, thankfully Starcom is modern and feels right for this generation.

The universe/story is deep and immersive and pulls you in right away, combat is fluid and satisfying. It's like all the other recent half decent space games minus all the crappy stuff like running out of gas, uncontrollable rovers on planets, pointless micromanaging, the handcrafted map is a really nice change from the procedural blandness of today's games.

Graphics are also beautiful, nice music, it's the whole shebang you have been waiting for.

Still early access, but I hope this keeps improving, it's already worth the money I've spent.

Note to dev: I would like to be able to zoom in more to see my ship.\t\t\t\t\t
