\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis was a fantastic game. It evoked the memories and joy of playing Star Control 2 (one of my favorites) without being very much like Star Control 2 at all.

The storyline was really interesting to me, but discussing it would be pretty spoiler-y so I'll leave it alone. But essentially you're stuck somewhere without the resources and knowledge of your homeworld and need to find a way to survive and maybe get home. As any plucky space hero knows, that means going out to beg, borrow, steal, stripmine, salvage and, when all else fails, buy resources and technology from any and all sources to build a bigger ship with as many guns on it as possible.

Exploring planets feels risky, but not punishingly so, and you are often rewarded for - or at least can avoid the grisly death of yet another xenobiologist by - paying attention to details and clues found on other away missions (also, whenever you find something weird you can't do anything with yet you should probably leave yourself a note on the map). I really enjoyed the breadth of the lore found in my exploration, sometimes a thing would be related to the main story, sometimes it would just be finding out about some ancient tragedy or accident. It made the galaxy of this game feel like it was inhabited by others with their own stories and more than just a lonely sandbox for me to play in.

The ship building and research system were really cool, and worked together flawlessly. It was fun to design new specialty ships for speed or power or for specific tasks. Combat was satisfying, but I played on normal and at a certain level of tech and resource availability it became fairly trivial (Check it out. Independently targeting particle-beam phalanx. Fry half a city with this puppy. ... We got nukes, we got knives, sharp sticks...). To be fair that should be part of the reward for exploring every backwater planet for megatons of resources and weird alien tech. When I play through again in a few months it will definitely be at a higher combat difficulty.

And that leads me to the main downside. I've finished it and it'll be a while before I want to come back.

Oh, and research the deep space speed increases a soon as you can, getting to planets off the beaten path was kind of a grind until I did that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start by saying that you should get it on sale. I like the game design quite a bit.... but I find it to have two main problems that kinda kills my enthusiasm for the game which will ensure that I never actually complete it:

1. Moving around isn't very fun. travelling through the void basically means point in a direction and go afk for 5-10 minutes. Even moving around in a system feels sluggish (sure, you can dump research into making better engines to go faster - but even when you do that the travel isn't very interesting even if it's faster.)

2. It's kinda empty. Even when you enter a system that has a dozen celestial things to visit - it still feels empty. A large part of this is because you spend almost all of your time just moving from one celestial/place to another and the moving around in the game isn't very fun


What I'd recommend:
The first thing that comes to mind is that there should be asteroid belts all over the place to allow players to mine resources as an option. You run into a couple dozen asteroids in that first system, and then I haven't seen a rock since (visited about 30 systems maybe 40). This would give players something to do that isn't simply moving from point A to B to C to D and back to A. And the extra resources would give players more opportunities to be creative in ship designs without having to wait until the end game when I'm sure everyone will have more resources than they'll ever need - if they ever get to the end game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all this game is made by one person - I always try and give a shoutout for that.

This is an exploration game, and it doesn't try to hold your hand. After the initial events in first 2 minutes of the game, you are expected to go out and explore every corner to try and piece together what really happened. There's a lot of reading. Not quite as much as some other games, but if you're the kind of player who skips event texts and so on, you'd come away quite disappointed with the experience, I think.

The format of the game is quite similar to Sunless Seas / Sunless Skies, if you've played those, but there's less reading and it's more forgiving. The spirit of the game though, I think, reminds me a little of Infinite Space () on the Nintendo DS, although this is not a character-driven game. Speaking of characters, this game explores the galaxy and its inhabitants, but your crew or ship gets very little in the way of development or exposition, so this isn't Star Trek or a space opera. You can easily fill in the gaps with your imagination, however. You are the captain of a starship! Go wild. Make your own story.

The gameplay is cool, certainly much better than Sunless Seas / Skies in my opinion. You get to build your own ship, and all resources are renewable in one way or another. Basic plasma weapons have medium range but do constant damage, pushback, and shoots down drones and missiles easily. Beam weapons have long range but do more damage up close. Nuclear missiles don't use energy, have the longest range and absolutely wreck enemy shields, but could be shot down. Drones are essentially swarm missiles but have a resource cost per unit. Shields are like a luxury in this galaxy; only the most advanced ships use them, and you unlock it yourself in the mid-game. You get a superweapon - which space captain doesn't want a giant co-axial laser - and a micro-warp late in the game, and these are so much fun. The game certainly looks the part - the dynamic lighting system really works wonders.

One nitpick I have is that early game can be quite challenging (which is good), but in the late game, you can build your ship into an absolute fleet-wrecking monster, so you'd probably want to change difficulty at some point, but at time of writing, this is only possible in the Lua developer's console - check the forums - but I think the dev's working on the feature. You'd probably want to start at least on Very Hard, despite what it sounds like.

Give it a shot if you like what you've read.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhy does this cheapo $20 game have so much going on?

1. Nice graphics. Not outrageous, but nice. Explosions and ships all look very good. There are satisfying explosions and weapon effects for sure.
2. Interesting and extensive research and customization options. You can build your ship all kinds of ways from all kinds of parts, and more parts get unlocked as you progress.
3. Difficulty that feels good. Everytime I think my ship is OP and the game is too easy, I manage to find a new place that really ups the enemy power level and makes me have to go back and rethink/improve/upgrade my designs.
4. Exploration and combat. A good mix of the two, not too much of either. And most importantly if you want to go farm combat, you can visit certain places and enemy populations respawn. Blowing stuff up for fun and profit.
5. Enough mysteries and places to discover to keep you wanting to play a little more to find out just a little more. So many breadcrumbs.

Only negative is some travel times are excessive. Many systems are off the beaten path and don't have shortcut travel points. Nothing that can't be solved with a quick cheat engine speed cheat I suppose, but not everyone has access to 3rd party programs.

I can't believe how good this game is for it's price tag.
Comparing it to that recent Star Control puts Star Control to absolute shame. Everything here is better. Including not having to listen to comically stupid aliens be outrageously silly.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI probably came into this with the wrong expectations. I was picturing a game more like Starsector, Heat Signature or Elite Dangerous. Sort of an open-world scifi sandbox with a living galaxy that you can prod and interact with in interesting ways. This is more akin to a sort of twin-stick shooter in space. Don't expect to be making smuggling runs or pitting factions against one another or scanning the market for the best shipping routes to raid. It's an action game, not an RPG, and that's fine. However the design falters in a few areas:
- As others have pointed out, it's too slow paced for an action game. Elite Dangerous has tons of downtime but it goes to serve the fantasy of actually living in a real galaxy, and has tons of menus to fiddle around with between stops. Here the next goal is just another enemy to shoot. I kept wishing that the game would focus entirely on just sending enemies after me and letting me upgrade my ship between bouts, because that loop is quite fun and I think it would have been enough to entertain me for several more hours.
- The planet scanning and anomaly system is just filler, and gets tedious pretty fast. Basically, you need to scan every new planet by hitting X. Either the scan reveals an anomaly or it does not. If there's an anomaly you hit X again to bring up a short dialogue screen which concludes with a choice. This choice (From my playtime) falls into one of two categories: A. Either do something , or do nothing and leave. This isn't really engaging, as the reward for doing something is not revealed to you until you do it, so you might as well. By the time you arrive at a planet you will be parched for something interesting to happen, so of course you do something. The other type of choice I encountered was type B, where you either do something, or something else, neither of which give you a clue of what the result will be. I never experienced a choice like "Do you want the gold or the titanium?" which would force me to consider what I needed most at the moment. Just choose one and move on, it's a coin toss and the best you can do.

With that said I think the combat and ship improvement systems are solid, and the game is far from unsalvagable if a few tough choices are made with regards to its design. As of now I would not recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
