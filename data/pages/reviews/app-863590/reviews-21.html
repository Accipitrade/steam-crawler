\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is one massive issue that a lot of people are going to refund over - the terrible quest tracking/0 map updates. Essentially if you don't annotate literally everything on the map yourself you will be lost for hours trying to figure out what to do - and even if you do (I mostly did) you will then have to comb over your million notes to try to find the relevant area you should go to.

This includes trying to guess which part of space different races are in, important things that people say, once, in an un-repeatable snippet of dialogue (e.g. put plasma guns with as much space around them as possible to vent heat = higher dps, this is literally mentioned 1 time near the very beginning of the game and never again, there is no reference for stuff like this)etc.

I have over 30 hours in at this point and still haven't beaten the game yet because of wild goose chases. Most the game is awesome, but this one aspect is major turn off, and makes late game a slog.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Serious face SPAZ. Layered simple mechanics that work surprisingly well. Exploration required. Pro-tip: Stars have planets around them.

EDIT: I got this in a sale, and frankly, it's worth full price - in fact, it's cheap. The only bug I've seen in nearly 20 hours of play time is occasionally a planet you've surveyed will show that it hasn't been when you're not in that system - but it's minor and rare.

There is more here than in many $60 titles. If they put out DLC for this I will get it without hesitation.

I focused on missiles and plasma, reactor and the subsystems stuff which has worked extremely well. I recently got the deep space travel time boosts and that makes an enormous difference too. For missiles, you basically have to trade to get cobalt as there is not enough anywhere otherwise. Otherwise, make use of the notes system so you know where stuff is to go back to. The game does not hold your hand and figuring stuff out is part of the fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEarly game is great exploration and the first 10 hours are great fun. It then becomes very repetitive in the midgame scanning several hundred planets by pressing X, and late game turns into a terrible experience. It takes forever to get anywhere in your ship and you're constantly backtracking, flying all over the map in futile attempts to progress the story. It's a needle in a haystack simulator without telling you that a needle is what you're looking for in the first place, the haystack is literally the size of the universe, and you can barely move.

Early game any star system you bump into gets marked on the map. As the game goes on, more and more you will have to try and find specific planets and systems that you missed, in order to progress. Except you don't even know if you've missed any or not! All you know is the story doesn't seem to progress and you don't know why. It could be that you just need to talk to a specific alien to progress, or fly to a specific planet again that you already found. You don't know and you'll have to look it up on the web.

When you finally get to the end, the boss doesn't pose a threat. It's literally go to him and press this win button once on your keyboard. It's not a fight, so there is no need to time it or be specific about positioning. The outro then is text again, there's no video or voice acting in the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a difficult game to rate for me, because the reason I stopped playing, a lot of people really just don't care about but is very important to me, specifically.

To start, I admit that I didn't get very far. I explored maybe 50 star systems, and by that time I could outrun anything I didn't outgun within those systems. The plot was interesting, even through the "starter plot" was pretty predictable, but maybe I'm just genre savvy about those sorts of things, and I stress I'm referring ONLY to the very basic premise you start out with, I don't think I got far enough into the game to get to any other plotlines that weren't side-fetchquests.

So, why I stopped playing. The ships. I hated looking at them. I hated looking at enemy ships. I hated looking at my own ship. I am not a fan of the "pile of boxes" way ships are handled, and the aesthetics of the ships is something a game like this lives or dies on for me, and the ship design here was something I just could not ignore or look past. If that's not something that bothers you, you'll probably love the game and I hope you do.

Overall I don't regret buying the game just on the principle of how much effort and care was obviously put into it, but sadly it's not a game I'm going to be reinstalling either.\t\t\t\t\t
