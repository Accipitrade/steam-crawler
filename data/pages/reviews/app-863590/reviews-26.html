\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the outset, I'll say that I've had an itch for a top down single-ship space simulator for awhile, and nothing has really quite managed, within this sub-brand of the space sim genre, to do it quite like Freelancer. SPAZ, Drox Operative, and others got *close* but ... always fell short in some way for me. This game did the trick.

Without spoiling much, I'll just give the basics - you start off a fresh member of starcom with your first starship - a shuttle craft, basically. You are assigned to an unremarkable space station hauling some pottery. Things go wild, and you ended up stranded somewhere totally else with only this one space station (and its populace) and you and your shuttle. Your tech logs are corrupt, you have limited resources, so you have to build your way back up. Its up to you to explore this new galaxy and try to keep you, your crew* and Kite Station alive. (*Aside from managing morale, your Crew are mostly numerically represented red shirts...do with that what you will).

I beat the game in the time shown. Like other reviews, I'd say I have no intentions of replaying it. It does technically, as far as I can tell, have at least two "endings" but one of them is basically just "hey are you a xenophobic sociopath? here's an ending for you !" so... pass. All in all, replay is nil from my view.

As I said, it scratched an itch. It wasn't perfect, and I had to look up a couple things at the very end because a couple key things to find ended up being suuuper obscure. I also didn't do all the side quests because, again, obscure. Other than that, though, I found my way through this new galaxy on my own, which was satisfying.

The writing is good, the survey missions and random discoveries add enough spice alongside the (in a lot of cases, fairly limited and linear) interactions with the alien races of this galaxy, and the combat is good enough not to hate it for how often you have to do it. Some things could definitely have been done better, like the fact that the alien ships are all just kind of...stammering around in space, with no purpose. Also the fact that at a certain point you realize, relatedly, your enemies are basically just loot pinatas once you get strong enough. But overall, I definitely give it a thumbs up. It was a really fun 10 - 15 hours, for the last 5 it started to wind down in a big way (aside from a couple cool twists in the story) and I was ready to be done. Consider that when you decide what price you are willing to pay.

7.5 / 10
