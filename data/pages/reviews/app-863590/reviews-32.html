\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWriting this review after finishing the campaign.

I really had alot of fun with this game! And for me personally this is one of those games where I think "Thank you for making this", because in some aspects it was more than just entertainment.

For those, who want to find out if this is a game for you, let me answer two questions: What is this and how do I feel about those aspects?

What is this game
  • You build your ship based on (previously researched) modules. See below, but in short: Not so strategic as it seems like.
  • Big galaxy to explore, including different types of wormholes
  • "deep" characters: Various characters + races, which can change relation towards you throughout the campaign. Also you can influence relations (e.g. by killing them).
  • Expect at least 20h of playtime, if you consult walkthroughs frequently. If you don't, you can easily add another 5-10 hours.
  • Constant fighting of various types / sizes of enemies
  • Biggest feature: Really cool campaign / story. Most story lines are independent of each other, but in the end you will solve most of them to proceed.

My opinion (Pro / Con)
  • Ship Building: This is fun at start of the game, especially when low on ressources. But from mid game on most likely all ships turn out the same and it clearly lacks diversity. I was disappointed by the lack of importance. Some strategies (e.g. building a big and slow drone ship) are not compatible with the game requirements (e.g. you need speed for exploration).
  • Travelling / Exploring: It is a mixture of completely free movement in the galaxy, with streamlined path of planets / stars and movement shortcuts via controllable wormholes. Expect to visit alot of places at least twice. Also, expect to travel a lot into deep space (while doing nothing else but listening to music), which might demotivate you from time to time until you figure out how to speed this up significantly.
  • Difficulty: You can adjust enemy difficulty as you please. But the real question is: How hard are the quests? In my opinion you often do not get enough help from the buildin functionality (pings on map, questbook, history of conversations, etc.). Luckily, you can look up almost every obstacle, which you can run into, online / in steam dicussions. I personally had some issues with late game. So consider my playthrough time including external help.
  • Story: I try not to spoil. This is my biggest Pro of this game. There is a mixture of AI, aliens and history to be combined into a good overall story, which builds itself up piece by piece. (Maybe except for the very ending(s) itself) It is very consistent, logical, entertaining and more often than not makes you think about stuff that you never thought about before.
  • Gameplay: A bit shallow, but fine for me. You will fight, explore and solve quests. But you do not solve quests via complex interaction mechanics (like lock picking), but more with selecting the correct options in communication screens and executing the correct tasks (on the galaxy map) in the correct order.
In short: I came for the ship building, but stayed for the story. I had some WTF moments, which kept me thinking about this game long after closing it. Just like a very good movie with a big message (e.g. Interstellar).

I only have two words to the devs: Thank you!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love it. Been up too late too many nights in a row on this game...

Simple build your own ship, explore, solve a galactic mystery game.

Lots of great unique features - like being bale to leave notes in game on the star map.

Intuitive 2D interface. Love it. so easy.

Great writing & story. The different cultures and behaviors of the different alien ships are all entertaining.

Graphics are great for 2D

Music is solid.

The tech tree does give you options, and is not linear... You can completely skip some tech in favor of others.

Just enough weapon variety to make it interesting.

Wide variety of resources makes upgrading your ship an interesting challenge.

Multiple kinds of travel in game. You can just go long way like Start Trek, which takes time. Take worm holes scattered around between systems. Use the Star Gate network when you can. And there are a few different ship catapults between a few systems even.

The interactions with the many aliens species are cool. And actually useful and different.

You can tell this game was a labor of love for the people that collaborated on it.

Unlike say - FTL - Many of the planetary visit impact the story, and you find yourself checking you notes and going back to planets and star systems for strategic reasons.

It's just a good looking game, good sound, good music, with tons of freedom, open ended game play with a cool story line that you can follow or not. Build you own ship. Fly wherver you want, use whatever FTL you want. Make friends or enemies, make peace after war. engage in trade or don't.

I like freedom. I like driving through the peaceful black for a minutes or too and ebing rewarded with extra reseach points. I like being able to avoid heavily defended worm hole routes and Start Gates by going the long way through the black.

I like building a ship that has one small engine, and 20 plasma cannons. I like being able to build a ship that looks like a batman symbol or a hammer head.

It's fun, freedom, looks good, sounds good, and cool story.\t\t\t\t\t
