OLD POST - PRE-OVERSIGHT:

Steam needs something more than Yes/No for "would you recommend this".

I WANT to recommend it - it's a great space exploration game. Good ship building, and discovering all of the mysteries on various planets is a blast. Overall, alot of what I dig in a top-down space exploration game (Space Rangers is a long time favorite).

However...
There is no way to view your inventory. You collect piles of material, which is used for trading and building... but the only way to see how much you have, is to either trade with an NPC, or visit your home station.
I'm writing down everything I pick up, so I know what I need... but that's quickly become far too cumbersome.
Huge oversight for a game that has you collect so much.

This gets exacerbated by the fact that some alien races require you to pay them, or they become hostile. You have no way of knowing if you have their required currency, because there's no inventory list.
This will lead you to becoming hostile with them, without any chance to do anything about it.

Overall - the game is very good, it just needs some more work, and some basic features, so I can't recommend it.

If you can get by without any type of inventory system, grab it. If that's a dealbreaker for you, wait a bit and see if it gets patched in.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is basically a super simplified, top-down Elite Dangerous. It doesn't have nearly the depth of Elite Dangerous, but it's MUCH easier to pick up and play. It's easy to see this game has had influence from ED, The Expanse, and other space themed entertainment.

I love the modular 'build your own ship' customization that is similar to the ship building on Kingdom Hearts. I like the upgrades and research trees as well.

I realized I love this game when it gave me that certain kind of 'explore the unknown and find cool stuff' feeling that Elite Dangerous also gave me.

For example, I was putting around the galaxy and saw a really neat comet orbiting a star system which had a peculiar outgassing of a red color. Shortly after, I surveyed a planet with a civilization that appears to never have progressed passed the medieval stage. This was due to the planet's irregular growing cycles, that resulted in frequent wars fought over scarce resources. There were also tales of a bad omen where a 'red dragon' flew across the sky. To my satisfaction, this 'red-dragon' the inhabitants were speaking of, was the red comet I saw earlier. Sci-Fi nerds will enjoy that this game is full of neat little lore tid-bits like that.

The only thing I don't like about this game, is that it leaves me wanting more (which isn't necessarily a bad problem to have) -- More content, more weapons, more upgrades, more missions, etc. Even some DLC would be great. I'd love it if they added something similar to ED's Engineer stuff.

This game has great bones. I really hope they make a sequel that has more everything. If you like Elite Dangerous, or Nova Drift -- you will definitely like this game.\t\t\t\t\t
