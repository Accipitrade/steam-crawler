\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like this game. It feels a lot like Starflight (that ages me, most of you will never hard heard of Starflight, but it is one of the grand daddy's to all space exploration games).

There are a few frustrations here and there, some of the puzzles are reasonably obscure and some of the flavor text gets used a little too much, but the gameplay is excellent, exploring space is fun, decking out my ship is awesome and engaging, and there's a good explore/research/build/improve feedback loop that gets very engaging towards the end of the game.

Finishing the story took me about 25 hours. There was a few minor things I hadn't done but I think I had basically seen most of what there was to see. However, there are enough things I didn't research that I will certainly go back and replay the game on a harder difficulty to try them out (lasers and drones for me next time, I think).

I enjoyed the story thoroughly, and it kept me guessing. The payoff was fun and interesting and reasonably unique, good storytelling even if some of the dialog fell a bit flat at times.

Overall, it's an excellent game and I highly recommend it. The fact that it was written by one guy just makes it even more impressive. 10/10 would buy again. Can't wait for Starcom Nexus 2!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy 27.5 hours is deceptive. Many of those hours were boring-hours of flying around aimlessly because the game doesn't do a very good job at explaining what you should be doing. There are lots of hours of fun, but boy did the late game get boring.

Other reviewers mentioned that the late game quests are a mess, and I couldn't agree more. I eventually looked up a quest location in my save file just to finish the game. It was leave the game unfinished, or at least see the final 10 mins of the game. I appreciate games that don't hold your hand, but this much closer to not respecting the time of the player than fostering a sense of discovery.

There were lots of little bugs that hurt the experience overall. Typos in quest text, errors in the UI display, flickering when changing sectors, sound popping when changing sectors, artifacts around planets at max-zoom, certain elements not being generated in a sector, and maps suggesting something that isn't actually there, are just some of the bugs I ran into.

Giving a thumbs-up over a thumbs-down, but this is closer to 60-65% for me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is truly a hidden gem - as someone who sunk countless hours into Space Pirates and Zombies (the original one, not the sequel - hard not to make the comparison), this definitely scratches the exact same itch of surprisingly well-polished 2D space exploration, with above average combat and controls, and research and ship design in there for good measure.

Exploring pretty much everything I could think of (bar a single puzzle I never solved, and checking every single sector of the map), it has given me around 30 hours of entertainment, and I can see so much potential for expansion and/or modding - that is a great prospect for sure! The lack of save scumming (a single save only) also make for replayability - it can be frustrating (I regretted some mistakes later), but I think it's fitting.

The story is definitely ok, the lore and dialog interesting enough to catch the attention and the overall game is really worth getting! Gotta say, I'm impressed and hope to see more added in the future!\t\t\t\t\t
