It wasn't too bad though and if you trade properly you can get what you need in a reasonable amount of time.\t\t\t\t\t

I have two not so positive things to say but neither should keep you from getting the game.

1. It is very possible to accidentally blow up the wrong things and get locked out of content. So if you happen to accidentally blow up the zoar trader or whatever. Be sure to reload last save.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis one is hard…

On one hand this is a very well made “little” game. On the other hand, the gameplay is too repetitive for the long time it takes to solve the story.

To make it simple: You go around space, scan planets, take decisions, communicate with alien factions, solve mysteries, and fight hostile factions.

What I like:

- Graphics and audio are good and immersive.
- I like the gameplay idea: there are a lot of planets to scan and solve, and each have unique pictures and stories.
- It looks like you can influence how the story unfolds. Everything evolves so naturally around your decisions…

What I would like to see improved:

- The tech tree is too straightforward: It does not make your play-through unique, it is just here to peace your progress.
- After 10 hours, I became bored of doing again and again the exact same things.
- Combats are too simple and just become boring the same way.

All in all, this is an immersive and original take on space exploration. I like the general ideas and ambience. However, I became bored of the gameplay loop before reaching the end of story.

This is a shame because I would like to know what happens at the end and how good or bad I was at “solving” it...

(that said, I will probably play the sequel with the hope to see some improvements.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of very few top-down space games which are NOT bullet-hell shoot'em up clones.

This one is a more slow-paced (yet still with engaging combat), mostly about exploration and roaming around the galaxy.

The ship-building mechanic is great and really enjoyable (especially at the early and mid-game).

The main cons for me is a lot of bugs with Steamdeck controls - though almost all can be fixed by a custom layout (mostly binding keyboard hotkeys to the buttons you like).

Overall - I couldn't found another game with similar gameplay, really awesome one for those who like to explore and like to have less hand-holdong than usually in modern games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tExcellent Game. It did the most difficult thing, making a very sci-fi twisty mystery to solve where you don't know where to go and a big galaxy to search in, just picking hints as you go, and without a last boss or final big fight, because that is the spirit of these kind of games which are all about discovery and mysteries on a galactic scale. It does have space battles with Plasma Turrets, Laser Beams and Missiles, but the exploration is the main deal. And watch out for environmental hazards, I did get my ship destroyed by the clockwork-like pulse of a Neutron Star at one point. I didn't see it coming but that's the dangers of space exploration,

I played it as a fix for a game in the 80s called Starflight, I couldn't find anything that came close to that. But this game did the job in every aspect, except for the fact that you can't drive the lander on a planet's surface.
Many people here in the reviews talk about Star Control, but this game really is a tribute to Starflight, not so much Star Control.

Just be aware that it has a ship log that saves every conversation, every anomaly found, every discovery that doesn't solve anything at the moment, and that you'll need to search in it and reference it a lot, because there's no way you'll remember what you found several star systems ago that applies to your current situation. Some people will find it a slow business for sure, but that's what this is about.\t\t\t\t\t
