\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMeh... 30h for a full run on hard difficulty and explore every corner, do every mission. Wasn't difficult at all.

Goods:
-Variety of events on planets, don't repeat often.
-Nice, unexpected and well articulated story.
-Music not bad... there aren't many tunes but you won't quickly get sick of them.
-Seamless experience, no hiccups, no loading screens (well if you saturate the screen with 100 missiles you get occasional stutter)
-Not expensive.
-Seems there will be a sequel soon.

Bads:
-Tech tree very limited, they say 150 techs but they are mostly incremental. Only 3 or 4 weapon types that don't complement each other nor require particular skill to use. Shields are waaaaay too strong and make battles too easy.
-Little action overall.
-Boring frequent long travels between systems and vacuum searches... Sometimes frustrating and requiring google.
-Not much science to shipbuilding.

Summing up, "Meh" is an accurate rating. Do i regret the money spent? No, yet they won't easily convince me to buy the new version they're making. I will probably resort to piracy to try it first if i get curious.

If you like this kind of games you should take a look to:
-Space rangers HD (the best game in the class)
-Star wolves 3 (1 and 2 suck bad with infinite bugs)
-Star control saga (classics, but graphically didn't age very well)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile i am recommending it, its just barely so... I would say 51:49 split. Game is kind of slog at the beginning, very good mid-game, and frustrating when nearing the end with quite disappointing endings. Lets start with the good stuff: Fighting while simplistic is very enjoyable, just as shipbuilding part (although i wish for more variety in module choices)
Audio and visuals are solid, especially particle effects for engine jets, missile launches etc are well done, just like cosmic landscape in the background.
Game is not for everyone - gameplay is very slow, repetitive and whole plot/lore is explained through plain text (no voice acting). Its all enjoyable to certain extent, what really drags this game down is the way of task giving...
Quest details are too vague, or You are not getting any hints at all about possible direction of Your objective. Sometimes You are essentially told "find x" without ANY markers, tips, or indicators that somewhere - across dozens of planets You already checked - some event has been triggered and that particular planet will give You new dialogue options. That would be okay in more linear game, but with this kind of open world game it forces You to backtrack A LOT, which is incredibly time consuming. I found myself pointlessly wandering around wayy too often. Its not that bad when You are still in the "gearing up" phase, so You dont mind changing course for more enemy encounters, possible research point events, etc... But once You are perfectly fit, after visiting same system 4-5 times You reaaaaally feel like its just pure filler to inflate playtime numbers...
Another thing are the endings. One of them don't even tell You its actually an ending, so You just keep flying around looking for some follow up... Until You reload the game to try another option. Very poorly done. That aside... After very interesting build up and all that effort... Both endings just felt very rushed, to the point You just ask "and thats it?" afterwards. Not to even mention all unanswered questions. It may be intentionally open ended and sequel will pick up from that moment... But otherwise it left bad taste for me.
Maybe i'm just overly negative. But... i'm still leaving thumbs up as i believe pros outweight the cons! Game is definitely unique in a way and deserve some forgiveness, especially considering developer team size and price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStarcom gets right what a ton of its peers seem to always screw up: the sense that space is an exciting place full of unknowns and surprises just waiting to be discovered. Every solar system is just brimming with possibility. What might you find? Could it advance your knowledge? Will you be greeted as friend or treated as a threat?

Where other games fear you'll get bored if you don't go a minute without blowing away enemies left and right, Starcom has the confidence to keep you playing with mysteries to solve, stories to uncover and knowledge to be gained. These discoveries, which come in all kinds of surprising ways, award Knowledge you can put towards researching new technologies. It's so refreshing to find a levelling system that is keyed off exploration and not how many baddies you eliminate.

The story is excellent and the writing throughout is smart. In your journey you'll be treated to plenty of neat sci-fi ideas. The writer had the confidence to believe in the world of the story they were creating and for once didn't undercut the experience with a load of warmed over pop culture references and out of place humour.

Combat also feels good, with a variety of technological advances that add more options to your defenses and arsenal. Like all other elements of the game the mechanics are nicely balanced.

In fact, you'll likely never feel like anything in Starcom bogs the game down too much. It is a very polished experience. I've seen some people have issues with travel being a bit slow in some instances, but that's probably because they've forsaken some travel technology they should have invested in earlier, or neglected to rig their ship for high speed travel (reconfiguring your ship costs nothing, so if you need to do a trip to that star that's waaay the hell out there you can always strip it down and make it all engines).

I very highly recommend Starcom to anyone who loves sci-fi and exploration. It is a perfectly balanced, unconvoluted, fun and enthralling experience and is quite possibly the best game of its type to date.
