I grew up in the 80's and my first real space exploration game was star control and star control 2. Though, those didn't come out until 1991 and then 1992. These ran on a 486 DX 66, if anyone can remember those computers, lol. Though the graphics of these games, weren't anything, compared to todays standards, they were easy to play and kept me mezmorized in the fact that, I was in space meeting new races and collecting valuable resources to upgrade. I highly recommend purchasing this game for the price. Its only 17-18 bucks and its only on v0.1 something. But there is so much you can do already and there is a quite a bit of content. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is not ready for prime-time... Or even the late show.... As it stands of this review (02/14/2019)I find the game dull and many of the design decisions suspect.

Can't see stars until you 'discover' a warp gate but then can see them fine? Really? Gee, you'd never have thought that humans have been looking at stars, even those in nebulae, for centuries with technology fare more primitive than Starcom should have...

No missiles? An extremely ancient form of technology and we can't research it up from the get-go despite our Chief Science Officer being the 'smartest scientist in Starcom?' Yet they're required on multiple vessel upgrades? Really? You're going to gate us on 1950s technology? Yet we can make our fusion cannons better?

The economy is primitive and highly exploitable. Just go back-and-forth between two ships in the same system and trade platinum for Cobalt-60 for platinum for Cobalt-60 and you'll be rich beyond your wildest dreams.

Combat is, well, zero challenge. And I say that as a man with a partially crippled left hand who tends to find movement/real-time combat more challenging than average.

You're given a lot of meaningless detail that isn't explained. A class A3 planet? Why? What does that mean? Apparently nothing as far as I can tell. And there's certainly no 'encyclopedia' to tell you. And so it goes.


The biggest sin though is that it's a grind. Go to star system. Scan planets. Destroy some laughably weak enemies. Eventually go back to replace dead crew from the, apparently, infinite supply of crewman on the starbase. Upgrade ship.
Lather, rinse, repeat.

And so it goes.

All I can hope is that some significant changes to the scope and design decisions are made. Because right now.... It's just not a good game.\t\t\t\t\t
