\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery enjoyable game with a lot of potential as development continues. This game just feels good to play.

Pros:

* Solid ship combat, in line with what I'd expect for a game of this genre.
* Great writing across all the different races you encounter, and the various celestial body descriptions.
* Lots of fun little references to sci-fi pop culture which don't at all feel out of place.
* The puzzles are fun & satisfying to complete, while helping to break up the gameplay a bit as you explore the galaxy and add depth to the game.
* Exploration feels rewarding.
* Different weapon types available for ship & combat playstyle customization.
* Addictive - "just one more jump!"
* Core gameplay loop is solid - explore, shoot, kill, upgrade, repeat.

Cons:

* There is no variation or tiering for ship modules. E.G. You don't get to choose if you want 2 big plasma cannons or 10 small ones. So by end game you ship is most likely going to be largely composed of a bunch of whatever weapon modules you specialize in, each looking identical, and making your ship look pretty much like an undifferentiated blob. Having tiers or at least variations of each module type would really help to make ship customization more interesting.
* Faction interactions are pretty shallow at the moment, which is a shame given the good writing. I'd love to see more factions and things like faction missions.
* Because weapon types are locked behind not just research points but also story progression, early game feels very samey and you're not able to differentiate/customize your ship until much later in the game much beyond module placement.

Overall any things that I feel are shortcomings of the game amount to me wanting more of it, because it is already quite good. I'm very much looking forward to the future as development continues.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an EXCELLENT game with a huge amount of potential.

That said, I am the type to nit-pick, so here's my list for 0.14. Maybe the devs can address these items:

1) I think the gameplay (read: combat) gets a little repetative after mid-game. Just point and shoot, no real tactics involved. If your enemy is too powerful, then just come back with a bigger ship to straight-up overpower them. This could potentially be solved by adding special "boss" style enemies that could appear randomly.

2) Definitely a huge research bottleneck. You can only really research 2 weapons branches before finishing the current game content. Generally that will mean the basic Plasma-Cannon plus Missiles. By the time you discover the third weapon, you're already too invested in the other weapons branches to switch your focus over. Various other research branches are just novelties at that point - lasers, spline mounted railgun, drones, hop drive with an explosion at the end... all not worth weakening yourself to try to pursue. This probably could be solved by making research more plentiful and easier to obtain in the mid and late game.

3) Space is pretty big. At least while I was playing, I found myself spending a LOT of time with cruise control turned on with turbo locked on, and either browsing the web or watching a movie or tv show on the second monitor. ... don't really know how to solve this one. Maybe add an auto-travel function, where the first officer takes over and the captain gets some shut eye? Fade to black, and then fade back in in the target system or something. Maybe add random encounters where you get called to the bridge early.\t\t\t\t\t
