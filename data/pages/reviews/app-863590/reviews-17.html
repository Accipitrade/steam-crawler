\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA novel take on space opera with entertaining gameplay. As for what exactly that gameplay is... well, take your pick. Some ship design, classic top-down combat, and exploration elements that give it a rather Star Trek/Stargate feel. In addition to these aspects you also optionally engage in trade and puzzle-like elements, the latter of which can often improve your relations with any of the various alien factions you can meet as you progress through the game. (Except the bugs. The only good bug is a dead bug.)
The story was also gripping, and I couldn't wait to finish it. Discoveries lead to mysteries, which unravel to reveal new discoveries until you finally reach the exciting conclusion. There's even a degree of replayability due to the story choices, relations with alien species, and tech tree choices.

That said are a few areas that could be improved, or might put you off the game depending on your tastes.
There are a few systems you can only reach with a long cruise through interstellar space, and while these only take a minute or two and the game will always give you a quick way back shortly after you reach them, that is a minute or two of nothing in particular happening.
Some of the puzzles and quests are a bit irritating as well. One in particular which has been the topic of much discussion made me break out a graphing calculator, and while it's not difficult to find what you need online I'm not sure a game should require outside resources to succeed. Another quest simply gives you a rather vague locations that's actually fairly far from the actual objective, so that was a bit of a pain.
Lastly there's the controls. Don't get me wrong, the basics of movement, aiming, and shooting are perfectly fine, but selecting secondary weapons and making warp hops are a little clumsy. The only way to use secondary weapons is to swap through the list using a single button, and you don't get an indicator of which one is currently in use- you're only told when you swap. This is a problem especially in the later portion of the game, where there's something of an opportunity cost to not using at least three of the secondary weapons on your ship. Ideally I'd like if secondary weapons could each be bound to different keys entirely, but even being able to swap them with hotkeys would be a significant improvement. For one unlockable weapon in particular I'd like to be able to toggle the axial gun to charge rather than have to hold it down continuously. I also wish there was a better way to control the distance of warp hops, as it's easy to overshoot or undershoot a jump especially if you're trying to use warp shockwaves.

TL;DR:
Pros:
  • Engaging story
  • Varied but generally entertaining gameplay
  • Moderate replayability
Cons:
  • Occasional annoying missions
  • Controls for later game upgrades can be awkward
