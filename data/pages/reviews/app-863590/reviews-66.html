\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great little space exploration game, currently in early access. Its short and fun. I reached the end of the early access version after about 10h on my first try, making the game a bit easy. I expect the game to grow in length over time. The current version makes it clear you reached a end of the current main quest after about a third of the tech tree. The game is well polished but could do with a bit more interactivity. The ship editing is impactful and intuitive and allows many strategies. The game benefits greatly from being able to specialize, solve some type of task, then respecialize and solve another.

The storylines, of which there is at least three are decently written, if a bit linear. The game shines with its random exploration. I find myself curious about each new planet, despite the brief descriptions.


There is an upper limit to the gameplay/questline limiting top depth with current mechanics. Survival elements could expand this adding long term play focused on growing your base. As is I recommend this game for a good day or two of play and expect the game will last longer once it is completed.\t\t\t\t\t

It's good to keep it this way as when the battles starts, the sreen gets really busy.

In many ways this game did right is what StarControl reboot should have been, it's great to see a new indie game out do a well established franchise name like StarControl.

The only thing that this game is missing are just the voice actors for the missions and intro speeches, but not bad for an Early Access game.

The controls are very intuitive WASD to move and mouse to Aim and shoot, mouse wheel to zoom in and zoom out when flying around like Asteroid.

Very nice use of Neon lines to represent stars and other interstellar objects.
Blinding suns when you fly close by is also a very nice touch, similar to Elite.

I paid full price for this game, it's well worth it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to say for a game that literally came out only 8 days ago in early access, it has been one of the most fun experiences I’ve played though in a long time. Reminiscent of Reassembly, SPAZ, and Starsector but with an emphasis on chill exploration and discovery, Starcom really hits every aspect of what I like in a top-down space sim that can be played casually. The combat is never too easy or too difficult (most of the time,) and it feels absolutely amazing the way damage and enemy destruction works: The parts flying off and ending in a gratuitous movie-like multi-stage set of explosions, damn is that sexy! BUT of course the game isn’t without its issues, the big 2 for me are that you can put yourself in a research block where you’ve spent all your research points on stuff that isn’t meta in terms of combat and are stuck with an inferior ship. Late game the enemy stations and drone swarms in particular can spike the difficulty if this is the case, so you really do have to be careful about what you research even if you want to try something new and cool that might not necessarily be optimum for combat. My second issue is simply that the game is far from complete in terms of story and content, with only a small chunk of the map explored and only so many resources/research you can accumulate before there’s nothing else to discover and harvest. Of course with time as more content is added to the game this issue will be resolved, and with enough content maybe even the first issue of hitting a research block will disappear as there will be enough “stuff” that gives research to unlock almost all of the research tree and thus you’ll always be able to build a viable ship even if you didn’t invest in the more meta technologies at first. All-in-all, this is an amazing, casual, top-down space sim game that I’d recommend for all my friends who are into that genre of games. Hopefully the developer keeps up the good work and we get a 10/10 product at the end of its development, I’m definitely optimistic about it.\t\t\t\t\t
