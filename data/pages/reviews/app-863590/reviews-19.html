\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't expect much from this game when I installed it yesterday. Almost 20 hours later I'm happy to admit that I completely misjudged it. Starcom: Nexus has an amazingly deep and original story, quirky characters, interesting aliens, great gameplay, beautiful graphics, an atmospheric soundtrack, and tons of cool stuff to discover. It's basically a Star Trek game with the serial numbers filed off, but of a quality most actual Star Trek games could learn several lessons from. If you're looking for that "explore the
infinite beauty of the universe" experience but don't have the patience or nerves for a Stellaris campaign, this is the game for you.

For all the things this game does right, there's one thing I can't help but criticize. Starcom: Nexus is a puzzle-heavy game. Now there's nothing wrong with puzzles, but when a video game makes use of them I expect it to give me all the tools I need to solve them. Unfortunately, Starcom: Nexus does not do this and instead expects the player to keep notes and perform geometric calculations on a sheet of grid paper. My school days lie 20 years in the past. I'm not sure I even own a pair of compasses and a ruler anymore, and I don't have kids I could pilfer them from, which created a bit of a problem for numerous quests in this game. In the end I had no choice but to consult online guides for the map coordinates I needed to progress, something I normally try to avoid because it kinda defeats the point of poring over puzzles in the first place. That said, thanks to the wisdom of the internet, I managed to get past these roadblocks quickly and went right back to enjoying the rest of the game.

Final verdict: 9/10 - would buy again without hesitation.\t\t\t\t\t
