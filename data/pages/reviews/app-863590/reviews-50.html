\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is a somewhat depressing statement to say that I have been waiting almost three decades for a worthy successor to Star Control II. Does Starcom: Nexus fulfil that sadly vacant role? My assessment is: almost.

Don't get me wrong, this is high praise indeed.

I will admit that I had low expectations going in. I thought that this was going to be a pretty shallow exercise. Maybe play a couple hours, design a ship or two and then shelve it. A few of the other reviews led me to believe that this was going to be a “phone-app” level of disposability - reviews often hinted at game mechanics that were tedious or repetitive which is something a developer does when they're trying to artificially inflate the size of their game.

Instead what I found was that Starcom is a testament to the concept of building on very simple mechanics. It took the lander mini-game and resource collection system from Starcontrol and made it even simpler, and then layered a storyline on top that was just as epic and almost as detailed.

Yes, the game is basically going from planet to planet hoping for a positive anomaly scan, but the writing and pacing of the story really drives you to keep hoping that you’re about to turn over that next clue. Yes, travelling to un-gated stars takes a long time, but I felt that this was more an intentional game mechanic designed to provide “soft barriers”. It’s an open sand box, so these barriers guide you where you need to go whilst still technically allowing you freedom of movement. And for those concerned, mid to late game techs make crossing even large distances fairly trivial.

It’s really the storyline that makes this game though. The writing is top notch and it really was a compelling journey to try and figure out exactly what is going on.

So with all that good stuff, why is it only an “almost” worthy successor?

The game does have some flaws. There are some game mechanic flaws – I found the log invaluable but this only highlighted how clunky it was to use. There were also some frankly hilarious typos in late game dialogue. I found the secondary weapon system far too clunky. And there were a few points where the storyline faltered and I literally had no idea what I was meant to do next. Re-reading the logs got me back on track, but it could have been slightly more obvious.

But probably the biggest disappointment for me was that I felt the game could be “larger”. Each of the alien races basically had one, possibly two quests, but then became fairly unimportant until the very final endgame. It would have been great to give all races more dialogue, quests and interactions – more development over all. Starcom: Nexus doesn't ever approach the impact that you could have on race NPC's - in StarControl you could change the destinies of just about every race. Unfortunately this means that the races in Starcom don't have that much of an impact on the player. There was a lot of potential here that I felt was unexplored. Why exactly are we siding with the Saurid over the Chitik? Are there consequences of the Araona reunification? Why don't the Tyn Coeden ever mention the Phage despite being next door?

One a smaller, related scale, why aren't there more NPCs? The writing for Dr Pilman is excellent and that game-spanning punchline was a killer, but where are the other memorable characters?

Probably my most significant gripe was that the end game felt rushed. Here, the game falls victim to its basic nature, unfortunately. Trying to keep things spoiler free, at the very end you need to find a certain Paguroidea and some…objects. I knew instantly where they were because I’d already discovered them, so completing the endgame took all of about 15 minutes, which was…disappointing. I also sense that there was probably meant to be a big end battle but this turned out to be somewhat anticlimactic, possibly due to the difficulty I was playing on and my ship design.

And my biggest gripe is was that the very end is fairly unrewarding. If the developer actually ever reads this, my strongest advice is: invest in a decent end game cutscene or something – a one paragraph text blurb doesn’t cut it after that epic a storyline.

So to sum up, Starcom: Nexus - solid, basic mechanisms and a great storyline which is epic but I felt could be *more* epic.

All in all though, strongly recommended.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt has been a while since I played to some degree a solid space game. Although the armament seems to be somehow limited this is to some degree mitigated with upgrades.
It has been a long time when I wanted to read the story in the game and this little gem motivated me enough to do so. Don’t take me wrong, its not a diamond, but it makes you wonder.
So far I was able to find 1 downside to this game. Mission log management. For so many years now the games held my hand and this game doesn’t at all. You open the mission log and it may provide some info, but the starting location etc it’s something you need to figure out. Further to this, there are pretty no walk through or guides how to get the ball rolling on some of the missions which sucks as well. After 17 hours I have visited to what I believe all locations but still can’t progress more than 8 missions. I’m simply stuck. Initially I thought that exclamation mark on planets will sort things out but I visited every single one and came back to home base multiple times with no luck. This is not enough to thumb down the game as it still provides for what I’ve paid for. I’ll hold onto this one for a while and hopefully will complete it as some point.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmazing game and I was sad to reach the end because I wanted more, even though it took me 35 hours and I probably didn't even finish everything on offer.

After originally posting this and reading other reviews, I want to elaborate on my experiences with the game.

The story is spectacular and there are very rpg-esque elements as far as decision making goes, you can truly choose your morality. There's a lot of good character development as well and the same goes for the races you meet. Additionally, each story element is wonderfully unique and fresh, and all while being fairly reasonable from a scientific standpoint. From incredibly fast tectonic plate shifting to relativity to irradiated water, you can tell that the developer really did the research to make everything believable. Obviously, since you're traversing an entire galaxy, the speed of ships is extremely exaggerated but it balances beautifully to make the pace of the game just fast enough without rushing or becoming tedious.

And then there's the researching and resource-getting aspect of the game. I can see how some people may find it less engaging, but for me it was perfectly set up so that progression didn't feel like a grind, but was also rewarding. The one problem I have with it is that none of the enemies scale unless you make a very particular decision towards the end (hint hint), but by that time the most difficult enemies are either obsolete or soon to be anyway.

The combat itself is visually really good and intense, and certainly for the first 20 hours or so it is very challenging in a good way. Combined with the ship-building mechanics, you have a nice plethora of options of how to approach combat.

Essentially, it's like Stellaris except with an individual ship that you control and a way better experience overall.

For a single developer, this is one of the best games I've ever played. Scratch that. This is one of my favorite games of all time, full stop. Bought with medium expectations, left astounded by how fun it is and wishing I could play it for the first time again, and definitely will play it again many times anyway.

EDIT: The developer is also now donating sales from Ukraine, Russia, and CIS to support the Ukrainian people, including donations to doctors without borders. Just further cements my appreciation and admiration for what this solo developer has managed. Fantastic!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend picking this game up only if you are a big science/astronomy nerd but can suspend your disbelief when it comes to faster than light travel and some other things. Lots of care has been put into the textbox events, of which there are hundreds.

Do not get this game if you want a balanced space combat experience with a living open world. In this game you go from very underpowered to horrendously overpowered within hours and nothing in the game really ever offers a challenge beyond that point. I am at 20 hours and i can honestly say that 18 of those hours has been spent flying through empty space, scanning, and reading text boxes. This is not an alternative to space pirates and zombies or space rangers.

The game actually plays more like a point and click adventure disguised as an open world space sim. You will spend hours flying around and gathering things to open other things and repeating. The story is genuinely interesting to me so i enjoyed the experience despite being totally let down by the hold spacebar to win combat.

6/10 meh, hoping the ending will blow my mind\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed several hours offline, disregard the published 42 minutes.

Like Escape Velocity games, which I personally loved, with better graphics and bigger star map. A bit like Space Rangers as well which i'm also a fan of.
Travel can take too long early on or if you go off the grid, missions are often obtuse and unclear. Upgrades are deceptively deeper than initially might seem on the research tree,. which expands as you progress.

Variety of alien races with their own different backstory and disposition towards you which can change for better or worse depending on certain actions you take. Hint: read closely to avoid hostile diplomatic relations.

Planets look good from space, as do the visual picture of them once landed on surveys, great variety there too The story is interesting, the ship battles are fun and sometimes challenging. I'm not a fan of the blocky look of the ship tbh but it's functional enough. I do like the ship building aspect and on certain missions rebuilds must be done strategically.

I'd like to see unique npc alien ships/characters, with subplots that dovetail into the main story arc at some point, but overall this has been an addictive game if you like these type of games that I'd give a recommendation and hopefully further development would realise even greater potential from what is now a solid foundation.\t\t\t\t\t
