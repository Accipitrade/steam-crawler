\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood, enjoyable story based game. The early access version is somewhat linear in that regard so not terribly replayable other than to see what might happen if you chose different options at various points in the story. Not sure to what degree that might change in the release version so I might update this review then.

Beautiful graphics and sound. Features a modular starship that the player can build to fit their preferences which was something I really liked. The story itself was very enjoyable and well written. Recommended.

Full release update edit: Bug fixes and additional or more fleshed out stories essentially characterize the released version. Very strongly reminiscent of the old "Starflight" games of the '80s. Hopefully, the dev sees fit to continue adding to the game, as it is a very enjoyable change of pace from other games with similar subject matter. I should note that the game does include a few decidedly "old school" puzzles which may require some basic geometry + graph paper or the like to solve. While this was refreshing for an old bloke like me, it might be a turnoff for some folks. Highly recommended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's still in early development, so all of this is to be taken with that in mind. The game is pretty good about keeping a progressive pace, with all of the research options, ship building, and various story lines you get involved with. The action is pretty good. Just when I think my ship is able to whip anything, I get pummeled by something new and have to rethink my ship design and combat tactics.

What would I improve? I would create more options for combat. For instance, using the tractor beam to remove a section of a ship, at an energy cost. Or perhaps have a technology that can scramble their sensors and cause them to fire on each other.

I assume the story and side quests will be fleshed out more, but it currently starts of strong, and then starts feeling like it's dragging at a point. It can be challenging to really know what to make your next priority for exploration.

For some quests where you have to make hard decisions, there should be some options for investigating certain aspects of the situation, perhaps enhanced by researched technology, that allows you a better chance at making the optimal choices.

I would also consider making it so the higher the difficulty, the greater the rewards.

There's really nothing about this game I would consider "bad". Overall it's solidly enjoyable if you like games like this.\t\t\t\t\t
