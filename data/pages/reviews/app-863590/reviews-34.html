\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStarcom Nexus is a wonderful little exploration space combat game. You'll get at least ~20 hours on your first playthrough. The game really shines with the story and exploration aspect and relies on you paying attention to the text on the screen. Sometimes the map will flag locations, but you should really be leaving notes on the map to remind yourself what was there and what you found. You never know if it was important to the main storyline!

The ship combat is twin-stick. Point with your mouse and click while flying using the keyboard (or use a controller). Secondary weapon is spacebar. Towards the end combat becomes less of an issue because your ship becomes a juggernaught of destruction, but you can still get satisfaction out of completely wiping your enemy out. The variety of weapons leaves a little to be desired and there's room for improvement there. You'll be able to (eventually) max out the research tree so don't worry about dedicating to a particular tree.

Overall a very good game and I enjoyed the story. Took a little twist there that I wasn't expecting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are wondering whether to buy this game, the short answer is: GO BUY IT.

So, on to the details. There are very few good space exploration games like these. There is a new Homeworld in the making, but who knows when it will be released. Fear not though, don't jump out from the 3rd floor window out of boredom, because STARCOM NEXUS is one hell of a ride you must experience and it will definitely fill the void you are experiencing in the desert that is called gaming industry.

Why this game has so low sales numbers? Where are the suits to give this game a well deserved pat in the back and a gargantuan marketing budget? And how come I stumbled so late into this gem? Well, partly because Steam has terrible search and recommendations engine and game journalists are pampered idiots. Anyways, in Starcom Nexus you will encounter:

A vast universe filled with mesmerizing space phenomenons,
where you can go where ever you want,
do whatever you want
and all the while enjoy a great story,
build your ship akin to your liking,
laser barrage enemies to hell,
research things that pique a tech/science nerd's interest,
read perfect background info on things,
interact with all kinds of well thought out lifeforms.

Everything is perfectly laid out, well balanced, except the combat. Which needs some more love, because after a while you will become so powerful that everyone in your path will be demolished and driven through mud.

The only thing I missed are more weapons, even more weapons and related research, and of course more challenging adversaries so that you would have to retreat occasionally, recuperate, lick your wounds and to mount even more world shattering weapons to zero badasses.

PS: I hope planetary bombardment will be included in Starcom Nexus 2.

Perfect game, and from one human only. Nice, can't wait for the sequel.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSeemed like an interesting game. So i decided to take a chance after watching some gameplay on twitch.
In short:, it was a great ride.

I just kept wanting to know what was on the next planet, because 9 out of 10 times, it was something different every time. And each time you think you know the size of the galaxy, i'd discover some new story element, and it expands some more!

Its one of those games which underscore how story is pivotal to a make a good game. Its like reading a good sci-fi book. Only your in it.

There are some puzzles, but most are not mandatory. And if you really cant figure them out, the answers can be found online.

A hint, if you encounter something that seems worthwhile to remember, or seems like you would visit it sometime later. Use the map marker to make a note for yourself. It might save you some searching later on in the game ;)
Although for most stuff the ingame log search is good enough.

I truly enjoyed this game, and i would wholly recommend it.
