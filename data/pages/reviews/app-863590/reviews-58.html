\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game reminds me of another game I spent literally months playing when I was a kid called Escape Velocity. For anyone nostalgic about that game, I can't recommend Starcom: Nexus enough.

With only a few hours under my belt, there are 2 things I really enjoy about this game, exploration & shipbuilding.

Exploration sets in the minute you start. There are wormhole that transports your ship to different parts of a new galaxy. These jumps lead to new worlds and alien species that all can be interacted with in some way. The interactions are a bit superficial, but the game sets a light tone that allows you to make fun an interesting choices.

Ship building is really interesting as each ship is completely modular. You can design the shape and strengths of your ship however you want. Want a slow gunship, you can do that. Want a speedy ship that avoids conflicts, you can do that too.

All in all, I'm having a lot of fun. The only thing I'm not sure of is the replayability. If each galaxy is procedurally generated, then it could be a whole lot of fun exploring everything anew. If not, then it might be a bit boring.

For the price point, I think it is definitely a buy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've enjoyed these kinds of games since Starship Command in 1983. I have completed the story once or twice so far so i'm not claiming to know everything the game has to offer. I'd recommend it if you like these kinds of 2d action/rpg/space-opera type games.

Plus:
Good Visuals
Good Gameplay
Good Story elements
Good Shipbuilding
Good Tech-tree
Stable (tiny bugs if at all)
Accessible (easy to get into but not too shallow)

Minus:
Some spelling errors (Astral units instead of Astronomical Units)
Could do with more story strategy elements to flesh it out
Lacks Mod support so the game could be much bigger

Suggestions:
Implement modding (See endless sky)
or
Build structures (mining facilities to trickle in resources, gun platforms to guard facilities, maybe even space stations)
More research options and gradients (more but smaller upgrades could be better)
Rework factions so it's possible to take wildly different paths (see warpgate)
More variety of ship components (Emp burst, cloak, auto-turrets)
More space stations to interact with or take new missions from
Open ended universe mode (would require more variety and long-term gameplay opportunity)

Some of these ideas may be present and I just missed them so far, or the developer is focusing on a shorter and simpler game to make sure that everything is done right (not a bad thing). Basically I think the game is already excellent and hopefully can expand into a bigger version of itself.
