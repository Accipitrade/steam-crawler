\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo you remember Fallout 1 or 2? How weirdly exciting it was in the overworld to suddenly stumble on a quest or a town on the way to your location? That's how it feels like the devs got their ideas out of.

Even though you're being given a set route through worm holes that you can warp with from one system to another, the game never specifically told you that that's the way to go. You can, and are encouraged to go out of the set route into the deep, empty space. Sometimes you'll find something interesting along the way and get some extra research points or resources that can improve your ship. The whole vaccuum of space that stretches all across your map is for you to explore.

For the cons... Well, personally, the only thing i wish this game has is some kind of resource management for me to stress over, like fuel or food for my crews or something. And so far, 3 hours in, i've seen no risk in doing explorations into the deep space. You might suddenly got into a system full of Chitik swarms who'll shoot you on sight, but it's also really easy to just turn back and boost away if you don't wanna risk dying.

The only risk management you're gonna encounter are during events that only happens when you're investigating a planet/space object, or when you meet a new species and had to find out whether they're friendly or not through dialogue choices.

All in all it's a fun space exploration RPG with good writing. An easy 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can warmly recommend this little space rpg. Great exploration, story and combat works nice.

A few notes to help you avoid getting stuck (no spoilers):
1. I would strongly recommend using the notes you can make whenever you find something you can interact with more than once. Some are fairly easy to find again without, others can be a complete nightmare.
2. Note what aliens live where so you can find the one you're looking for easily again.
3. You can (and might need to depending on who you make enemines of) communicate with races hostile to you. Avoid killing them for long enough to get close enough to hail them!
4. Your log is searchable! You can find log entires related to whatever you're looking for to help you figure out the various quests\t\t\t\t\t
