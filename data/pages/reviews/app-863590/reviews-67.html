\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is the best of genre, easily and unequivocally, since the original Starflight. The mechanics are fantastic and elegant, the environment is beautifully rendered, and the interface, with one or two exceptions, is intuitive and responsive.

That's not what makes this game excellent. This developer remembers that this genre is about the story; and that story is discovery. The premise supports the game design, but it springs far beyond that. By the time I got to the end of the early-access story stub I was disappointed not because I had not completed the research tree or built the best ship, but because I will have to wait for the dev to find out what happens next. This is proof of interactive story telling; you're reading it like a good book. You want to explore the next planet and the next to drive the plot forward, not because you want loot or xp.

Do me a favor and buy this game to encourage the dev to finish the story. I need more of this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlthough it's not a bad game, I currently I can not recommend it, not for this price.

Short review:

The game has a lot of potential, but suffers immensly from lack of depth and content, and an overly simplistic gameplay. All you really do is jump through a systems wormhole to the next, shoot a few enemies, scan a few planets, collect resources, then go and jump to the next system, shoot, scan, ... and rinse and repeat until you reach the end.

The store-page describes it like a great space adventure, with interesting things to discover, and meaningful choices..
Sadly, in reality, the game is very linear, with very little to do or to explore. It's more like a grindy mobile game. For 5 dollars this would be fine, but not for 15.



Long review:

The game looks beautiful enough, has good enough music and so far I have only ran into a few graphical bugs. And it has great imagination regarding the "anomalies" you discover. Which sadly don't have any meaningful impact on your story whatsoever.

To list the main problems:
  • No real depth: The gameplay is overly simplistic, and everything feels the same. All star systems are the same with a little graphical variation, all enemies behave the same, the two friendly races are just generic ships you can trade with, and even the planetary discoveries are all the same except in writing (chose an option to get resources).

  • Combat is boring, and too easy. You aim at an enemy, shoot your weapons and wait till it dies. Mostly you can just sit still for this, because the AI is really stupid.

  • Ship building (on a grid) is a useless gimmick. It doesn't matter where you place parts, and it has no meaningful impact on combat. Furthermore, there is very little actual parts to use. Only one type of engine, one type of turret, one missile launcher, one type of reactor, ...
    The Dev could remove the Ship Builder and replace it with a simple text-selection list of parts, and it would function just the same.

  • Discoveries on planets don't feel meaningful at all. The writing is interesting, but after the first 5 I didn't bother reading it anymore, because it's always only a multiple choice option, that always only leads to some resources (or in rare cases to display another star system somewhere, but it won't tell you where).

  • Research doesn't feel like it makes any impact. I researched almost everything, and I couldn't really notice any difference in gameplay (except that now I have missiles to shoot enemies from safe distance with).

  • Camera control is bad, you can only zoom out a littlebit, and often enemies get hidden behind the edge of the screen above you - while others are visible to the side at the same range.
    This is because you can't move the camera-view, e.g. by edge-scrolling.

  • The map isn't very helpful. It doesn't show you the names of planets, where an enemy or friendly station is, or any mission targets. It's basically only there to show you where you jump to next.

  • There is only a few missions, and feels like it's only an incentive to keep you grinding.

  • Logs are a mess. You have to go through the discussion logs and read them again to see what you need for one of the missions. Why not an extra window that shows you directly your current mission in detail?

  • Also there is some minor annoyances, like when jumping through a Jump-Gate, the screen flashes bright white for a moment, which really hurts the eye.


So at this moment, this game is a foundation with potential (fine for Early-Access), but it really feels more like a grindy 5-dollar mobile game at the moment.
From the Developers reply, it seems that he doesn't want to change the Core-Gameplay-Loop (jump to next system, scan, shoot, .. and repeat).

So I think the problem is also that the game is wrongly marketed as a space adventure, when in reality it's more of a very simplistic top-down shooter in space.
It would probably be better for the Dev to change the description, and focus on that shooting aspect.



Verdict (tl;dr):

Don't buy this game if you're looking for a great space-adventure, with deep meaningful choices, a lot of customization and a diverse world to explore.
The gameplay is very arcady and linear, and at this stage sadly also very repetitive. It feels more like a mobile game right now.

From the Devs reply, this is what he wants his game to be like, so the description is kinda misleading.
However, if you like simplistic, arcady top-down shooters, then you may enjoy this game for a few hours or 20 minutes inbetween.

I would still suggest to wait at least some months to see where this goes, as of right now there is very little content and once you have visited your first star system, you have pretty much seen everything you'll do in the game.\t\t\t\t\t
