\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHighly recommended for space captains who want to explore and not get bogged down in a procedural space grind. This is a true non-procedural space exploration game. It plays more like Star Flight (from what I can remember) than Star Control. The exploration is self directed but the narrative keeps me wanting to play.

The writing is great. I laughed at a universal translator misunderstanding during the very first alien encounter. Almost every planetary survey mission is unique. They also frequently narratively build on each other as you progress.

Combat is twin stick shooter and very appropriate for the exploration vibe. The ship damage, explosions, and other effects make it fun even thought there isn't a lot of depth there.

There are some controller hick-ups. Navigating the starbase UI with a controller is glitchy. I sometimes couldn't fire missiles with my controller.

Overall, great game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBig, big fan of this game. Highly recommended.

Pros:
1. Travel is enjoyable through the star systems, especially once your ship's engines are upgraded for more speed. The fast travel system is well thought out - it encourages exploration and feels good.
2. The exploration system is enjoyable. Very reminiscent of Star Trek, scanning a planet and sending down an "away team". This part is through text only, after you send an away team, but it feels satisfying.
3. Ship building is tons of fun - lossless recycling of parts means infinite configuration. You are only limited by the size of your ship (which you can upgrade) and the materials you have on hand (you can get more by trading, exploring and fighting).
4. Very rich story, with several twists and turns. No planet I explored felt like a waste of time. A lot of smaller discoveries that seemed unrelated to the story turn out to be tiny puzzle pieces.
5. Combat is incredibly satisfying. Several different weapons are discover-able, and it's really dealer's choice as to which the player wants to use. No matter which weapons you use, it's incredibly fun to watch an enemy ship explode under concentrated fire.

Cons:
1. Travel between star systems (the dark empty nothing of space) is rather boring. Unfortunately, there's not much anyone can do about that - in fact, there are already some nice surprises waiting in the dark for players. The best thing I've found so far is just to watch netflix or a youtube video during long-term travel between star systems.
2. Sometimes, it would be nice to have a bit more hand-holding, mission-wise. I got lost in several parts of my journey, unsure where to go or what to do next. For example, you're supposed to destroy a certain number of objects scattered around the galaxy, and I had missed one. I spent hours roaming the entire galaxy, with no luck on finding it. I ended up emailing the developer and asking for the coordinates. It would be great if there was a way to help lost players get back on track after a certain amount of time has passed.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was a bit weary of this title at first, saw the videos on the store page and wasn't super interested to be honest. I did see that it was on sale however and I'm a sucker for sci-fi/space games, so I went ahead and purchased it.

First off, I would like to say that I am really glad I purchased this title, the devs have done a fantastic job in how they've put this title together. The story is intriguing, the gameplay is exciting with multiple difficulty levels, and the shipyard is pretty neat.

PROS:
- Good storyline
- Some interesting side quests and puzzles
- Shipyard is fun to play with for a bit
- Some of the commentary is pretty funny
- Interactions with the galaxy inhabitants is interesting
- Graphics are surprisingly good (seriously wasn't expecting much from the videos, very pleasantly surprised)
- Large universe to explore

CONS:
- Ship parts are fairly limited and ship design doesn't change much in late game.
- Once you're in endgame, some ships can't be destroyed apparently, which makes completing tasks difficult at times (possible bug?).
- Wish there was a way to repair standings with some of the races as one slip-up can lead to them hating you with no re-course to fix it.

I am about to run through my second play-through but using the beta build this time. I will update my review accordingly.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's really good. The best Space game I've played. 10/10
I'll list a few of my favorite aspects.

Combat
I played on "Ultra+" difficulty, currently the highest setting, for a decent challenge. A few ship encounters / enemy fleets were tough but fair. I never felt that the game was "too hard." I will say that one particular battle took around 45 minutes, but man was it satisfying to finally win.

Technological Progression
I went from running away from Destroyer-class ships that I could barely scratch, to flying into entire fleets of large Battlecruisers and obliterating them. Every technological advancement brings you closer to galactic supremacy. Ship design and research point allocation are very important, but you can build the way you want to and still make a solid spaceworthy vessel. The game gives you base ship designs to help you get started.

Story
The story is hard science fiction, which I am a HUGE fan of. If you've watched Star Trek or the Expanse, for example, some of the themes will be familiar to you. But the story is very good. Plenty of mysteries to keep you in suspense until the end. As of right now it offers a complete and satisfying story, with the potential for even more if the developers continue this project.

Exploration
There are hundreds of planets and dozens of star systems to explore. The galaxy is VERY BIG, but not overwhelming. After 35 hours of gameplay, I still get excited to visit each new planet.

This is a great game and the developers have done a fantastic job. Even if they never update it again, it's still worth the full price. I hope they extend the game even further.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t* Huge, Open map - you can use warp gates or fly anywhere, no loading in between systems. This is great for exploration. There is a lot to discover.

* There are enough gates and fast travel that it isn't a chore to get somewhere fast, unless you're exploring in uncharted sectors.

* Combat is satisfying and controls are solid.

* Research is straight forward - it's not too in depth, pretty linear but that's why I like it.
Sometimes I don't want a billion stats and choices to make, sometimes I just want to explore and blow stuff up.

* Progression has been good, I've actually felt like I'm getting stronger as I've progressed my research, quests and places traveled.

* The lossless build system is nice, you can experiment with different ship configurations without worry.

* There are many neat little things to find for research to kinda keep it interesting, but the game loop is simple. Your progression depends on exploration and planet scanning.

* It has a quicksave (f5), this is very convenient, easy to jump into, do something, save and come back later.

All in all, they did a good job. It scratches that space shooter/exploration itch, no bugs, and it's nice looking. Story isn't annoying and overbearing, or long-winded. Most of what you find out is in fragments anyway. There is a story, a few of them I think (I'm not done w/ the game yet and am avoiding spoilers) and they are interesting - along with your main goal.\t\t\t\t\t

Only complaints so far would be that the default difficulty is way too easy, I heavily suggest starting with it a bit higher than you normally would in other games. Worth buying.
