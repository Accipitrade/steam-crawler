\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt has been a long since I experienced a game as well rounded as this! I was worried the action part would go towards the difficult side while I enjoy exploration more, but turns out there was no need. The difficulty of the shooty bits is just perfect. If you are stuck you can easily get resources and upgrade your ship. Ultimately, the exploration and the story kept me glued to the screen like reading a great book can sometimes do. The short stories with a single visual image that describes each planet really paint a rich picture of the galaxy, and gives that "oh I must explore just one more system" feeling. 43 hours to complete the game, and I am sad it was this short! (Note: I haven't devoted 40 hours to a single game in many years)

The game contains some puzzly bits that you have to watch out what the logs are saying to figure what's meant to be done next. It's never annoyingly difficult and I haven't encountered anything that is stupid or illogical. Super cool!

Negatives? It's too easy to crash into other ships because you turn slowly, and then they turn hostile, but that's minor if you save frequently.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf your'e a fan of old space opera adventures like Starflight, Star Control 2, etc. this game will entirely scratch that itch. With an interesting plot and puzzles that force you to actually think on the clues and the modifications and discoveries available, this game delivers on everything it offers on the store page and more.
Combat is smooth and simple, with your Plasma Turrets tied to the left mouse button and all secondary weapons switched with R and fired with the spacebar. However, in the late game, you may use point defense and the various secondary weapons so much that the turrets become obsolete.
The game is surprisingly replayable. There's no "new game +" or anything, as the technologies researched unlock stages of the plot, but the combat is fun enough and you may wish to experiment with different playstyles in your fittings. I found myself spending time in dock just fiddling with the modules to squeeze out that right balance of power generation and firepower while fitting as many technologies as I could, and ended the game (there are two different endings) with a drone-launching carrier with multiple missile and laser banks. Next time I might prefer a slower ship drenched in turrets, or go all-in on the engines for blazing speed.
New players, I recommend the following strategies:
1. You can never have too many reactors. Ideally, the right balance will allow you to leave the "boost" thrusters on at all times (or at least outside of combat).
2. Don't skimp on shields when you get them. Again, balance your power so there's not much drain to charge them, and fit at least two shield generators. You'll rarely take damage to your armor after that. My final ship had 4 of them.
3. All races get a massive technological upgrade just before the endgame. Be prepared for it.
4. When in space with sentient tree ships around you, SLOW DOWN. These guys are needed for at least two quests, and collisions deal damage. They fly massive tree ships with all of the grace of a 90 year old trying to make it to the sunrise breakfast special at the local buffet, and you can accidentally make them hostile by running into them too hard.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am enjoying this game. I recently attempted to play Star Control Origins and after experiencing numerous crashes of the game, I purchased Starcom: Nexus because the combat appeared similar (I wanted a feeling of nostalgia from my youth playing Star Control 1 & 2). While the games are not a 1 for 1, Starcom gives me a more nostalgic feeling than Origins did, and without the technical issues.

Beyond the combat, I like the ship construction. You can follow a blue print, completely customize your build, or something in-between. Plus you are not penalized for experimenting with your build since you never lose resources if you change.

The story line is decent. Although I still not far enough into the game to know whether or not it will promote or dissuade replay of the game, since I purchased the game for less than $10, even if I only have a single play-though it will be a good value for the money.

Overall, its a well made game that is interesting and fun to play from a small developer (something the gaming industry is always in need of) and a definite thumb's up for me.\t\t\t\t\t
