\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(Warning: this review is very critical and very harsh. This is not a comment on the genre, the
demographic, or the developers, and should be taken as an honest and blunt critique of the game. Also, spoilers)

Puro is very widely praised as a well-designed character, although he lacks any dimension and does not change through out the course of the game, contrary to the title, which I also have a problem with (very difficult to look up). The game forces you to have an optimal (?) relationship with Puro (questionably, because a few of the dialogue choices will trick you into sounding better than the one that's actually preferred) in order to complete the "true ending" of the game. A single accidental mistake deviating from the walkthrough will require a full restart of the game, or a backup save if you are very lucky.

Luckily, after playing through sections hundreds of times, it becomes muscle memory. This game is unreasonably difficult and relies heavliy on RNG, which may cause sections of the game to occasionally be unbeatable without a reset. Save very carefully.

This game presents itself fairly sloppily, with very large spaces in between loops of background music, extremely out of place RPG Maker default sounds, and oversaturated art,. although the music is quite good, and very memorable. Unfortunately, the art and music clash violently with the atmosphere of the game. The most absurd example is the juxtaposition of the boss music and the boss section where you are avoiding the dragon's crystals (mind me, not really using the right terminology). A Spanish-style jig, to my mind, isn't really suiting background music for a slow paced puzzle section.

The music is genuine and pretty easy to listen to, but there is a bit of strange harmony, and I can tell that the Famitracker tempo controls were not used to their best, because it sometimes sounds a little unstable. Sometimes, two different channels play the same note, which creates a very dull and muted sound in the NES sound chip, so it can sometimes break the flow of sound.

The dialogue and narration of the game is originally in a dialect of Chinese. While I do not fault the game for simple translation mistakes, I do fault it for its continued redundancy. Between tedious box pushing segments which well overstay their welcome, the game can, at points, be seen as a button-masher in between very challenging sections (the true ending will probably last 10 minutes if you read the text, and 5 minutes if you mash consistently).

This game is very lacking in performance. Even on new, high-mid range computers, with no programs open, you are bound to experience occasional lag spikes, during which your inputs will be ignored by the program, leading to many frustrating, trivial deaths. This lag results from maps being too large, and having WAY TOO MANY events in them, mostly in challenging sections: like the cat chase, the library, water sections (especially the boss fights), and sometimes the white latex section.

The premise of the game could be interesting, but it is not used to its full potential. The game does make an attempt to seriously portray the dystopia in which it takes place; but during these scenes, the execution is cheesy, and they are drawn on for far too long to compensate for their scarcity. The true ending is spoiled by half of it overlaying a plain, black screen, with no music. I believe the story is too short, and underdeveloped. There isn't enough time in normal gameplay to become seriously attached to these characters, and when 'tragedy' occurs at their expense, it's bland and unsatisfying. A game shouldn't have to rely on a fandom to develop attachment to the characters, it should be able to speak for itself.

Some polish would be needed to improve this game. While the effort is there, it doesn't seem to be complete, possibly because the game may have been slightly rushed. This game's official development, if I recall correctly, only lasted about a year, which is a VERY short amount of time to develop a story-based game. Perhaps if it had been developed for longer, some of the issues would have sorted themselves out.

Art: 5/10 (uses a limited variety of colour, sprites are far too big and cause confusion, particularly in box pushing and chase segments, which are not exactly few and far between)
Music: 8/10 (music is memorable and takes advantage of a variety of styles, but does repeat itself in terms of harmonic language, and often clashes with the gameplay)
Story: 4/10 (the story had potential to be decent but ended up turning into lore dump and fandom fuel)
Dialogue: 2/10 (overly verbose and repetitive)
Gameplay: 3/10 (Stiff and four-directional RPG maker controls and poorly programmed RNG [courtesy of RPG Maker VX, sorry to complain about the engine], box pushing, puzzles with clues that are either horrendous or are so blantant that it makes you wonder why there is a puzzle at all)
Character(s): 4/10 (Puro has a memorable and simplistic design, but is verbally offensive to the player at meeting, talks far too much and is mostly used as an info dump, and the game forces you to love him in order to get a worthwhile ending)
Villain: 2/10 ("villain" is mostly two-dimensional, and has quite little to do with the story, other than to provide limited context for the player's situation, if you happen to get to the last quarter of the game. He is a villain only briefly, and for the remaining time, he practically begs you to cooperate with him.)

Overall, 40%

While the game does explore unique creatures, they serve mostly to generate fan art, and by the end of the game, nothing has truly Changed. I believe DragonSnow and ShiZi have the capacity to make good games in the future

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey should change the name of this game to "Pi$$ed," because that's how you'll feel playing it.

I love the concept: You wake up from suspended animation in a lab that at first seems abandoned, but you find that isn't the case, and that the current inhabitants have designs on your fate. If they catch you, you'll be changed forever. It's heavy on anthropomorphic animal (furry) transformation, which is awesome in my opinion (although your mileage may vary).

But lets get to the meat of the game... First off, the graphics aren't great, but it's more of a style than anything else (although the crudeness of the graphics does impact the game in other ways, like trying to figure out the placement of movable boxes). There can be a lot of screen tearing, both in windowed and full-screen modes (my Intel I7 with an nVidia GeForce 1050TI chugs along sometimes, and it shouldn't), which is irritating when the game requires you to make precise movements at high speed, which is already difficult because the controls are barely functional and imprecise (especially if you're trying to use a gamepad -- just opt for the keyboard for best results). The music, what there is of it, is actually pretty good if you're into chiptunes. But all of that is negated by the premise and not why I'm giving this game a negative review.

One irritating aspect of this game is its complete lack of polish. There are literally no graphics options to help alleviate some of the drawing problems. There's also only four save slots and no way to watch previously unlocked scenes without playing through the game again, and the game just isn't that fun.

By far the most annoying aspect of this game is the difficulty level; it's outright unfair, especially when there can be long stretches between checkpoints (you have to watch every cutscene every time unless you manage to save right afterward, which is annoying in and of itself). Not only are the controls a hindrance during most of the scenes that require you to move fast, but the computer's AI is relentless and also somewhat random meaning that even when you learn to navigate a level you can be randomly caught by an enemy for a "game over," so it won't always help even if you *ahem* "get gud."

And the game is built around it's "game overs:" They are literally where you get the payoff of seeing your character transformed, but a lot of them aren't very cinematic or particularly interesting, and considerably less so when you've seen them for the 30th time in a row. The game does let you skip them to immediately bring up the save state loading screen, but still...

I can't recommend this game, which is a shame, because with a few tweaks I think that it could've been a home run.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's start at the beginning, found this game in my steam queue because I played a few games that have a few tags in common. Looked at the description, seemed like a decent game and decided to buy it because of its low price.
I never played anything like this, which is why, at first, I found it the weirdest game I've ever played, but I quite liked it.

Before I continue, let me say the (for me) only negative point of this game, it's really hard at certain moments, and the controls take a lot of time to get used to, so be sure you have some spare keyboards laying around.

Most of the times I immediately quit when I keep failing at something in a game, but something kept me going, I somehow felt I had to finish the story. Even though I had a couple of "♥♥♥♥ my life" moments while playing, I started to really like this game, especially it's story, it's amazing and beautiful.
I love story driven games that have somewhat different endings, they're the only games that deserve to be in my top 3. This game definitely deserves a spot in my top 3!

There are only a couple of other games where I can somewhat relate to one of the characters, and this is definitely one of them! When I play game with an amazing story and a character which I can relate to, it becomes an experience I probably won't forget for the rest of my life.
My first ending wasn't that good, so I played it again because I had to get the good ending, couldn't live with one of the bad endings.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
+Well done sprites and music
+Interesting story and characters
+Unique enemies unlike any i've seen before
+Bosses are also very creative
+Anthropomorphic animals (ok maybe i'm a bit biased on this one)
+The True Ending is really touching

Cons:
-Controls and hitboxes can be weird at times, only made worse by the fact that everything is locked to a grid and you cannot cancel a move
-The difficulty will take a massive spike if you go off the "right" path

Information:
Every single enemy that is not a scripted event is a one-hit game over, and scripted events require key/button mashing or you get a game over
This game is VERY hard, it will make you frustrated at times and you'll need to take short breaks to recover, the difficulty curve is a straight line right at the top
The enemies, obstacles, friendlies, are all centered around latex, and any time you "die", you either get absorbed by the enemy or experience "transfurmation" into a clone of the enemy that attacked you
Save points are helpfully placed, but sometimes it's far enough away for you to not want to risk or explore anything
You can only save at pre-determined points and not wherever you like, which may seem like a negative, but it's to prevent being able to break some parts of the game I imagine
Some transfurmations will have sequences after or while they happen
Admittedly, it is pretty fetish-y, and does nothing to hide it

Overall, I do recommend this game, but keep in mind it's very hard, some things in it can be weird at times, including the controls

If you have the money, definitely get this game, it's worth every single cent

Other notes:
The True Ending is very hard and frustrating, but in the end, it's really wonderful and touching, i'm not going to lie, i cried a bit from joy too\t\t\t\t\t
