\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe reviews lied to me. I beat this out of principle and chose not to refund it. I'm not expecting perfection it's a furry game and I wanted an interesting story about the main character not wanting to be transformed. Instead I got an incredibly reptievely infuriating game. I wanted story and meaningful things to happen instead in the first 30 seconds you're likely going to die. Die as in transform and if that's your kink I guess it's a plus. There are so many cheap deaths that it's stupid. Just get ready to spam the F12 button to skip the death/transformation scene because you're going to seeing it a lot. The problem is the whole game has so much trial and error that I ask myself why am I continuing to play. The fact that all the gameplay is movement based is another real shame.

In this game you're going to encounter common monsters that transform you. Occasionally you encounter more unique monsters and in almost every case these creatures have no backstory or dialogue or anything. Why are they there besides offering a unique transforming? The few dialogue options that you do get are cool, I wanted a few more hours of that dialogue not 20 minutes.

I got the bad end on normal first play through no guide. It's unlikely that there's some magical conclusion to the story that will change my overall opinion on the game.\t\t\t\t\t

While I don't typically like RPG Maker games because the way the engine is and all, I'd have to say games like this are an exception. When they're deep and interesting and you're actually trying to run away from monsters, sort of like Ib (and this one)

While it is sort of odd and *cough* fetishy, it's really engaging. The art style is awesome, the music is on par, you end up loving Puro by the end, just heartwarming even for such a short story. Many thanks to both DragonSnow and Shizi for their awesome work.

The main thing I'd have to complain about though is, again, the movement system of RPGM. It makes it all the more frustrating when you're running from enemies or trying to navigate something without dying.
And that's another thing, the monsters are sometimes a bit too "smart" in their random movements, and there's way too many traps (monsters) you can fall into that it makes it awfully frustrating to run away from something without running into one. Usually needing to go back to the last save for dumb reasons. Very frustrating.

Overall though...I couldn't dislike this game. Perhaps I'm biased a bit to the whole kemono/furry thing, but it was just generally interesting and enjoyable. And come on, transformation is awesome, let's be real. Even if it's...into something made of latex goop, haha. (If you ignore that fact, it's not as bad.....)

I don't normally like losing to things but if it's these are the ones who want me to become one of them then I'd happily throw my humanity away just because why not? Also I enjoy the usual rpg jokes when you inspect items.

Cons: Difficult annoying chases, Player getting stuck or move delays during chase.
Puzzles? Understandable. The chase however? Irritating as hell! How am I supposed to know most of the time on where to go? Sometimes I just go to the walkthrough just to see how people even do this one. Let me tell you this, I kept getting caught on and on and on until I was out of things to think. Okay so maybe that wasn't the biggest problem, but the ai moves so freaking FAST and I have no idea what to do until I go through the walkthrough again.

So all in all, I recommend this game. Even if it's a "little bit" frustrating, I still love it and it's worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
