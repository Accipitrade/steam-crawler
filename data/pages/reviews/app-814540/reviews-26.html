\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, just to get this out of the way, this game is not a puzzle game. Not enough so that it could be classified as such. There are a few chase scenes as you run away from enemies, which are fine on their own; however, these chase scenes usually start with 1-2 seconds cut-scenes, which cannot be skipped. This sounds manageable, unless you are struggling immensely with certain paths (like the white tail chase) because of the hard-to-manage controls. This results in what feels like an eternity wasted just waiting to fail again. After the first fail, doesn't it make sense to either let the cut-scene be skippable or just remove it entirely?
While the music isn't bad, after a while, it can get very repetitive, yet that is partially my fault, for sucking at the game. HOWEVER, some of the areas just don't have music, which was very strange. The sound of silence is very loud.
The best part about this game (for me) was the slip-n-slides, which truly felt like puzzles, and not brute force.
Lastly, for anyone wondering what this game should rated age-wise, M, M for Mature (17+), because there is no reason that anyone should have to involuntarily view these inappropriate images without consent. [The game does not give a warning about lewd images.]

P.S. I can now never use the word "change" ever again.
P.S.S. The main character is Texan, the sidekick is Scottish, and the main antagonist is muffled in way that makes him near-unintelligible.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt was 10pm one april evening, and I wasn't tired, but I wanted to play a game in my backlog. Decided to finally give Changed a shot.

If you want the tl;dr:
~ This game can be VERY frustrating, with some puzzles being cryptic (You might need to pull out a guide), the chase sequences seemingly based on RNG. It might feel unfair at times.
~ But this has an intriguing, fun story, great art style, great music, it's funny, cute, and If you're even mildly curious, give it a shot!
~ If you don't like the look of slimy, wet latex look, then you might need to look elsewhere, or look at some other footage online to see if it's for you aesthetically.


Longer review:
The screenshots really sucked me in, the art looked just so enjoyable! I was curious by these "gooey creatures" there, and your character turning into said creatures. I then decided to go for it.

What I got in the end was an endearing adventure on friendship and sacrifice. And I want more! Even after I've 100% seen everything.

Waking up in your capsule, scared, alone and attacked immediately, sets the stage for a game that will not hold your hand.
From a moment that might be of respite, only to be turned around and actually hostile. There's no hope here, until you find a seemingly neutral monster that isn't going to attack you.


This game has really great artistic design. It feel's "ms paint like" old style flash game, but polished. I'm not sure if there's an actual name for this style, but when the game was trying to portray you feeling uneasy, I felt uneasy. When you felt scared? I felt scared. It's like this fear of the unknown. You don't know what's going to pop around every corner, or come out of the floor, walls or anything.
I'll be honest too. I don't normally like the wet latex look. The idea of sticky, slime blobs and slime and stuff like that creeps me out, makes me feel uneasy. Knowing that the creatures "assimilate" you made me feel incredibly uncomfortable at first. The thought of your player being only wearing shorts the whole game, no shoes or shirt, the thought of getting this latex on them just made me incredibly uneasy. But after a while, I dig it actually, and I grew to like it!

The music, sound design, I thought it fit well. Atmospheric, and the music really fitting the experience too! I enjoyed this aspect, and I found new favorite chip-tune music to play!


But I want to probably bring out 2 of the most decisive parts of this game I feel.
1: I had to pull out a guide on some parts because some of the puzzles felt too obtuse, cryptic to actually get through. Although I'm not the most proficient with puzzle games so your mileage may vary.

2: Some encounters feel they're rooted in RNG and may take multiple tries to get through which isn't the most fun. I found them entertaining though and I kept pushing through. To be fair I'm also very stubborn and will keep trying and trying until I either win or get frustrated and ragequit. I kept pushing through, never felt the urge to ragequit! I always learned what to look out for, and became a more proficient player from last time.



I really enjoy my time with this game. It was fun, it made me laugh, it made me feel things in my gut. I smiled, I was happy. This game made me happy, and I enjoyed every moment with it.

If you can have Patience for some of the more RNG aspects, difficult puzzles, and it's style of Furry TF related stuff, you got a fun game, and a great experience ahead! I recommend it.


Also yeah this game is very much rooted on Furry Transformation content!\t\t\t\t\t
