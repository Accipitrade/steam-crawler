\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRarely to I feel compelled to write a game review, but Changed (as weird of a game it is) really stood out to me. I originally bought it because I heard it was pretty good but was a very challenging puzzle game. It certainly lived up to the legend.

Story
I wasn't sure what to expect in terms of story going in. Changed follows a formula that many other games use today where the story is slowly hinted at but then later you receive proper information make it easier to understand. For games that have a story, this has become fairly common. Just because it's common doesn't mean it's bad. It's common because it works. The story feels overall understandable, characters have motivations, enemies have a reason to be there and attack you. Most things make sense. Plot twist(s) keep it interesting. The story feels fairly original. Five different endings makes the game last a bit longer.
1.8/2.0

Gameplay
Puzzle games can be tough to make. Often times constant puzzles can become boring quickly unless the mechanics get changed (haha changed) up every so often. However, this game manages to keep the player intrigued by changing the puzzle mechanics every couple of rooms. Hell, aside from a few outliers, the puzzles even slowly get harder as the game continues. As far as difficulty goes, this game is tough. Not too impossible but challenging enough to be fun for the average player. However, not every puzzle in the game was perfect. A few of the puzzles included some... unforgiving RNG. One of these sections (*cough cough* white latex territory *cough cough*) was so annoyingly painful that I took a peek at a walkthrough. No joke, the walkthroughs that I found basically just said "Good luck. You'll need it." Save points. Sometimes they're really close together, sometimes they are way too far apart. Did I mention QTEs? Yeah it's got those too, but they're not too close together where they feel repetitive, but instead provide a refresher from usual puzzle solving.
1.7/2.0

Graphics
Somehow, this game managed to have more CGs that any visual novel I've played. Almost every death has a unique animation, or an entire series of CGs accompanied by a monologue explaining what's happening in very graphic detail. Nice. The whole game featured a very neat, cartoony art style and the whole game was pleasant to look at. The important characters give off the proper auras just by their appearance. For example, the friend character looks real cute and friendly and the protagonist looks violently neutral the entire game, aside from a couple death animations. Some of the enemies seem a little too pleasant though. It's though to be afraid of an enemy when it looks like it's about to come over and hug you. Also the colors scheme felt a little inconsistent. There were times in which it felt like the game was going for a mostly white and black scheme with few colors to make important objects pop. Other times there were a lot of colors, most of which were on insignificant objects. Good art though, for being done by one person.
0.9/1.0

Music/SFX
The music hits me as kinda odd in this game. It was some good BGM, all made by one guy apparently, but it wasn't really mixed well into the game. There were times when music was noticeably absent, making the game feel really weird. Other times when the music was there it was fairly loud. I think the game could have included a more somber track to be played over the more emotional scenes. In terms of sound effects Changed is a bit lacking. Because of the game subject, the *glorp* sound effect is used a lot. This gets old. Very quickly. Maybe add a second glorp effect that randomly plays in it's place sometimes. Other than that the sound effects were almost not existant. Music was pretty good and matched the game style for sure.
0.4/1.0

Enjoyment
There's a lot to be said about a game that gives you depression simply because there's no more of it to play. I really enjoyed my time with this game. There were a couple times in which it felt like I was not going to be able to beat the game due to some hard puzzles, and I wasn't using my saves properly so I had to restart the very hard game from the beginning because I got a bad ending. ALSO CAN I MENTION THE DIALOGUE TREES?? There are only, like, 3 in the game but good lord are they annoying to navigate. Changed probably looks like a really horrid game because I'm just listing off problems upon problems. It's not. I had a lot of fun playing this game. Aside from the... less than pleasant death process, I thought Changed was a very good game that I might reccomend if you can handle seeing some odd themes. FUN. GAME.
1.7/2.0

Bonus Points!
+0.4 Drawn, developed, and written by one dude (+ translation help)
+0.5 Dev's first work
+0.2 All music made by one person
+0.3 Multiple endings!!!
+0.2 why the hell the are the characters written so perfectly
+0.3 Pleasant post game credits sequence

Conclusion
As much as this game is great, I cannot reccomend it to everyone. If you're interested, look at the steam page. If the game looks like... not your thing, forget it. But hey, if it looks like you can handle it and it's hard puzzles, give it a shot. I enjoyed a bit and found it challenging to keep myself from continuing to play it.

Final score:
8.4/10.0\t\t\t\t\t
