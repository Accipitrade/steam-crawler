\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSomebody get Dr. K some friends.

But in all honesty, despite this base game's MANY flaws when it comes to the limitations of its engine, it is at heart my favourite game ever. I've never had a game impact my life as heavily as this one, and made me, ironically enough, "Change" in many ways. The story is depressing while at the same time being optimistic at points, and every single ending pulled at my heart strings in different ways.

However, the main problem comes in whenever you have to go through the puzzles. Enemies will randomly react to the things that you do no matter what, making it impossible to strategize. It is almost like a falsely imposed difficulty, but it's not fair in any way. You can take the same path five times, and one of those times it will work, while the rest won't. There's a perfectly acceptable amount of RNG in games, but whenever your difficulty relies on RNG then there's a problem.

Dr. K gets introduced far too late into the game, without any introduction except a few books here and there, that most people miss. His introduction is far too sudden, and it doesn't really give him much character. I feel like he needs more time to develop as to why he wouldn't fight to stop you anymore, instead of hitting his head once and then giving up on the supposed future of humanity. He is a wonderful character, don't get me wrong, but he gets kind of shafted later on. There isn't any good endings that involve you being able to make his life better, despite the very obvious need for someone to rely on. He deserves better, honestly.

Puro, while easily being one of my favourite characters ever, also suffers from the flaw of character inconsistencies. He goes from acting all cute and stuff, to being much more dark. However, instead of rolling with that, suddenly he acts like you're his only friend in the universe and that he would do nothing to hurt you. The fact that you NEED to talk to him at random points in the game whenever it isn't even hinted that you should, or else you get two bad endings is partially annoying on my end. I lost my first two playthroughs because of the limited saves mixed with me not talking to Puro right. He's a wonderful character, but he's inconsistent.

However, everything that I've said, funnily enough, gets fixed in the Special Edition. K is introduced much further into the story as an antagonist bent on keeping you from escaping so that you don't spread the virus. He maintains himself as a constant threat looming over you, while also giving you more in-depth sight into his psychology and just how much he actually doesn't want to hurt you. He's a survivor who's been lonely for years, not some un-feeling monster who would kill without a second thought. He even stops you from stepping into the fans, showing genuine concern for your life.

Puro is in the same boat as Dr. K. With the new fixes to the gameplay you can see just how much Puro wants you to survive, and not just a few notes here and there. He's actively gone out of his way to save you with numerous plans, which makes you feel like he's actually a constant presence as well, all the way up until you meet him. K seems to imply that he isn't what he seems, and then the payoff comes whenever you find out just what he actually is, and whenever he shows his actually consistent personality.

The AI and level design are also significantly fixed in the Special Edition, making some levels that were notoriously difficult feel fair and balanced in between the monsters and the player, making some sort of tension that looms over a lot of the room. The AI may move randomly some times as well, but it's not RNG for every tick like it used to be.

Long story short, this game is amazing for its story, but it's a dredge and a half to actually get to the story. The Special Edition improves on the game in every single way, and if you aren't under the idea of playing a partially frustrating game then I suggest going and waiting until the Special Edition fully drops.\t\t\t\t\t
