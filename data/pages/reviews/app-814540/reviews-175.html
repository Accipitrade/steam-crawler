\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn short: I love the narrative and the story, it is super unusual and intriguing, a great mixture of science fiction and furry fantasy, there are not that many furry themed games with plots of such quality. I hate the puzzles and most of the game mechanics as they are broken and torturing.

In long: I will focus on the broken mechanics. First;y, there must not be procedurally generated solutions to puzzles because if you do the same thing twice it can be that the first time it worked and the second time it did not (sliding and dragon puzzles). Secondly, timings for many chases or escape dynamic scenes are absurdly tight, there is literally no room for mistake, and you must be perfect at every single movement (gassed ventilation, tail chase, kitty cat at the beginning, orange leopard at the end). Thirdly, if narrative elements such as notes and pictures are ued to explain the lore then DO NOT MAKE THE PLAYER SACRIFICE HIS LIFE IN ORDER TO READ IT (shark room, black latex sliding puzzle, second tail chase). Further, save point locations sometimes are inconvenient, it makes you travers a little tistance but the time to do it adds up to hours because the player dies a tremendous amount of times. Some minor points, the dialog tree with puro at the library has to have a backwards or comeback option because if you want to explore all his dialogue lines you must go over the same dialogue choices again and again at the moment.

Fortunately, the author develops an improved version of the original game where all of the above problems have been addressed and most of the improvements are neat and successful.

In conclusion, the story is awesome, sometimes it is tragic, sometimes it is hilarious, and the major advantage is that it is touching, and generates a whole spectrum of emotions. However, in order to experience it in the original game you have to be prepared to die a lot, to get annoyed a lot and be ready for your butt to explode a lot. I recommend that you use a cheat engine to slow the game down during intense moments and utilize this steam guide to make your journey less frustrating. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tAllow me to state that the gameplay of Changed is purely meh. The requirement for precise movement and fast reactions is hindered by the sheer unresponsiveness of the keys and awkward control scheme, which I found myself having to manually rebind. This isn't helped by the fact that the game leans much more to an artificially difficult, trial-and-error design philosophy. Even then, the gameplay feels rewarding and just easy enough to be fun.

Ignoring the gameplay, Changed has a fantastic story (although it may not be for everyone due to the themes present), with interesting characters, lore, world-building, etc.

It's certainly a shorter game, but even then it's quite a pain to replay it to get different endings, although abusing save files is perfectly accessible if you want to get that extra story without devoting hours of your life to replaying.

Overall, Changed is a great game to buy with your spare cash, and should give you quite a fair bit of entertainment.\t\t\t\t\t
