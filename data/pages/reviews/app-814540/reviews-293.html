Some puzzles, some chase sequences, a lot of traps, but nothing that's ever supremely hard to do, just, a few segments that are very RNG-heavy that don't feel great. It kinda fits with the kind of game this is trying to be though, so, ehh~ And it's fairly cheap & has a decent amount of content for its price so if that's a concern, it really shouldn't be. All-around pretty well-made for a game targeted specifically at furries, lol.\t\t\t\t\t

This game is not for everyone. I think there are two types of people who would enjoy this: People who like body morph horror, and furries who like TF/TG stuff (full disclosure, I'm the latter so I do have a bit of bias for the game).

TLDR: This game is a little rough on hitboxes and grammar, but It's Snowdragon's first project and English is not his primary language. The art style is simple but good and it's clear the dev team put a lot of effort into this game. It's a hard, unforgiving game. If you're a furry, like TF/TG stuff, and/or like body morph horror buy this game. Puro is #1 Husbando; I wish Snowdragon would sell a plushy of him so I can cuddle with it every night.

Things I like about this game:
I do like the art style; it's simple but it works. And clearly they put a lot of work into creating the visuals for the game. Also it was made by a very small dev team, and I believe this was SnowDragon's (the creator) first project. So I wasn't exactly expecting Skyrim remastered level graphics.

The Story is pretty interesting. I won't spoil anything, but make sure to look through documents, pictures, and pay attention to the dialogue if you want to get the full picture of what's going on.

Details. This game does not skimp on details during gameovers, and there are a lot of them. It does a great job describing exactly what happens to your body and mind as you change. So save often and don't be afraid to intentionally lose a few times to see what happens (although you probably won't have to try and lose for several of them. It's a hard game)

It doesn't hold your hand. There's no tutorial, it just drops you into the game and you have to figure things out. I'll give you one tip: save before you decide to touch anything. And if you're really having trouble, there's walkthoughs, let's plays, and guides out there.

It's really dangerous. Game overs are everywhere and they are merciless. The game does an excellent job of making you feel like you're in danger, you're mostly powerless, and that your odds of winning are very slim.

Variety of enemies. Pretty self-explanitory. Lots of baddies make for a more interesting experience. Also, most of them are pretty cute despite how dangerous they are.

Things I have mixed feelings about or dislike

The Grammar. I want to start off by saying the creator's primary language is not English, and he has done a lot to improve the grammar and writing in the English translation since the initial release. That being said, it's still a little rough and hard to understand at certain times. Just be prepared to be a little confused and meet the script halfway.

Hitboxes. It's a little unclear as to where the hitboxes are so avoiding game overs can be difficult at times.

Things I am head over heels in love with:
Puro. He is the cutest, most adorable wolfie monster. I love him, and if you play through the game so will you. Snowdragon should sell plushies of him because he would make a mint.\t\t\t\t\t
