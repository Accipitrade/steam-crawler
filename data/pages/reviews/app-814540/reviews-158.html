\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou wake up in a capsule after several years of sleep with nothing on you except a pair of black boxer shorts. You're cold, hungry and confused. You'll soon discover the danger lurks around every corner and it looks like the human civilization has collapsed. There are monsters! Puzzles! Frustration! And more! Have you got enough guts and luck to escape and survive the predicament you find yourself in?

Don't know!

Changed is a great little game about a "fetish" of sorts. I think it's a fetish? I don't know. Doesn't matter. What matters though, is that the game is pretty good. It's got this charm to it, from bad spelling to silly dialogue and nice, simple, artsy visuals. The story is surprisingly interesting as well.

It's also punishingly difficult. Some of it comes from the pure randomness (or it feels like randomness) of how the enemies behave in certain rooms and situations. Some of it is because of the controls. There's lots of trial and error involved. Even more so if you decide to go after the lore bits. Some of the more interesting snippets are located in the middle of quite difficult puzzle areas where you will end up losing the game if you stop to read anything. You will also run into situations where you lose the game if you go the wrong way and there's usually no way of knowing which way is the right way to go before you try again. Like I mentioned above. Lots of trial and error.

There's a nice variety of different enemies. Each one has their own unique animation for when they catch you. Some of them are really adorable as well and I found myself running into enemies and other dangers, letting the game beat me just to see all the different ways to lose. Certain areas also have unique situations which you will, in some cases, have to trigger yourself. Try to hang out for a bit on the balcony at the beginning of the game, for example.

There are also multiple endings. Depending on what you do in certain key moments during the story you will get a different ending. I highly advice to always save your game to a free slot, for this reason alone.

The game is not terribly lengthy. It depends on how long it takes for you to figure out your way through some of the more frustrating encounters and how much of the in game lore holds interest for you. The store page says, there's approximately 6 hours of game to experience and I find that to be a very accurate number.

Get the game. It's good. Not too expensive either.

Also, one final note: Be nice to Puro. He's a goober.\t\t\t\t\t
