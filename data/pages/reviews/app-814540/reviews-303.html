\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I enjoyed the concept of the game. The flow of the story is solid and the ideas for the bosses and characters were great. If you're a fan of this genre, there's also a bunch of artwork and accompanying text that makes it quite enjoyable.

Unfortunately, due to execution issues, it can be frustrating to play. The major fundamental things that need improving are controls and RNG - or at least helping the player compensate against those. There are times I've repeated certain segments upwards of a hundred times due to fighting against controls and/or RNG - especially in later segments in the game where there is little to no margin for error. This problem becomes even more obvious when you have to sit through forced dialogue/cutscenes repeatedly for a segment.

Control and RNG issues aside, there are several things in the game that are not obvious without a guide. Be it trying to find specific bad ends or even understanding what you're supposed to do in the game as it is not always intuitive.

That being said, if you're a fan of this genre, I'd give it a try anyway - there isn't anything else like it on the market.

I sincerely hope the developer creates more of these type of games. Heck, if possible, even NSFW-versions would be fitting for this genre. One other thing I'd liked to have seen is the ability to see the text events when implementing something like the "True End" credits area.

Pros:

- Great concept
- Some fun/interesting bad ends, especially when being corrupted (or in one instance, brainwashed)
- Lots of artwork (even if low-res)
- Plot is workable
- Varied gameplay and puzzles
- Cute 8-bit style music and sound effects

Cons:

- Controls are not responsive leading to failures
- Too much RNG
- Unclear direction for player at times
- English translation is awkwardly imperfect at times and breaks the immersiveness
- Needs quick save or better save points or ability to skip dialogue/cutscenes
- Wish there was more content! (Game itself can be completed within an hour if perfect playthrough.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a fan of the transformation genre, I really enjoyed this game. The transformations were varied enough to remain interesting and it was a nice touch to have different transformations occur even by the 'same' enemies. There are a lot of goo transformations but I find that they are appropriate for the setting and certainly allow for a great variety of scenarios to occur such as the goo rooms. Of course, there are enough other sorts of transformations that you don't feel restricted just by the goo stuff and if anything makes the game exciting enough to want to see what the next enemy or object does to you. My personal favorite forms of transformation came in the pictures scattered throughout the game.

Characters are nice to interact with and listen to because their motivations are far more than a simple curl of a bad guy's mustache. They have good reasons for doing what they do and so their working with or against the character does not feel awkward or merely as another obstacle. One issue, unfortunately, is that for some interactions with Puro, one has to completely restart the dialogue tree in order to get to asking new questions that are themselves part of other question branches. This is needlessly frustrating as the player has to waste a lot of time going through heard dialogue just to ask a new question and should be addressed by the developer in their future games.

Mechanically, the game is nothing special. The controls are a bit sticky and you only really have the option of move or interact. Still, the gameplay performs adequately and you should be able to make it through any area without having to worry about the character getting caught by their own mechanics. On a good note, the gamemaker also took the mechanics as they were given and changed up the gameplay enough to ensure they were kept fresh with some puzzles requiring interesting usage of the base movement and interact abilities.

Pacing is decent and there are certainly a lot of objects to interact with. An obvious downside of this game is that the environments look very similar to one another in some areas but it's obvious the creator tried to minimize this as much as possible. If I had a complaint about the environment is that there are various notes scattered through some running or puzzle sections that you simply can't interact with without getting caught and having to restart. I would recommend next time to have them placed in safer areas so a player would not have to feel the need to either complete the area or risk reading the material and starting over.

Finally, to address difficulty, this game is really not that difficult. All puzzles can be overcome with some thought. The issue of difficulty comes from two main elements. The stickiness of the controls which makes fine movement hard to acquire and the randomness of enemy pathing during chases. These can make certain sections frustrating but certainly not impossible. With a bit of smart thinking and luck a player can get through any area fine. This is further benefitted by the f12 escape feature that allows the player to almost immediately try again instead of having to watch the same transformation sequences.

For a first time game maker this is a solid game. For the price of 6 dollars, I certainly think it's well worth it for any fan of tf or individuals looking to get into the genre. For the casual puzzle player, this might be a bit frustrating but it is decently erected to provide them with a challenge while also preventing puzzle repeats that can bog down other games. All in all, a great game and I look forward to seeing what else this gamemaker can create in the future as they grow as a developer. I hope more transformation games, as the genre does not have much in that area, but I won't complain if they venture outside of that.

Lots of love to Puro and K.\t\t\t\t\t
