\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite knowing a review for an actual furry fetish game will appear on my profile, I feel obligated to make a review for this game. I know for a fact I'll get poked fun of for trying this game out.

This game is fantastic. Sure, the concept is absolutely depressing, and the feeling of helplessness fills the player with dread and misery, yet the game's endings are amazing. I managed to complete every single ending of the game, and I have to say that it's completely worth it to work to unlock the perfect ending. I wont say much due to the possibility of spoiling the game, but I will make a few notes:

This game is ported from Japanese to other languages, but unfortunately the language port was poorly done, leading to bad grammar in many places or the infamous "square text" in multiple situations, as the font has no way of even displaying said text.

The difficulty is incredibly frustrating. While, during my consecutive playthroughs I had less trouble getting through the game, I still found it difficult to play at times. Being made on the RPG engine, the controls aren't exactly refined either. While the game takes about 3 or 4 hours to complete on someone's first try, it would realistically take about 30 minutes to get through the whole game if the difficulty weren't so high.

But I'm almost happy that it was difficult in the end. Makes you feel like you conquered a harsh challenge, like you would feel when completing Bloodborne or a Dark Souls game all on your own. Every ending of this game makes you feel upset, knowing that there had to be SOMETHING you could do to get a happier ending.

Well, in the end, when given a choice to stay or leave, all you needed to do to be happy was just ESCape...

Tldr; this game is an easy 8/10. Would absolutely recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

Summary - Great Premise, Decent Writing (Even with how Engrish it is due to translation) Good Character Designs/Animations, God awful Gameplay and Design Choices. Worth your money? Kinda? Look. Up. A. Guide. Follow It to a T with otherwise you will get a bad ending and be locked out of the rest of the game w/o restarting. Use your saves intelligently, and save in all four slots if you are interested in getting the bad ending achivements.

Warning: Some unhidden minor spoilers.

Where do start. I absolutely love the premise and idea of it, a bit cliche at times, I'm also not into the fetish much at all, but I bought this because I can be ok with it and, and I kept seeing in reviews how good the story was, and that's what kept me playing, now it's a indie developers first game, not gonna expect the best, but oh man, the gameplay and design choices are bad. And dont get me wrong, I so far have actually enjoyed the game, It is well made overall with the Characters and Dialouge and once i get to finishing it I'm sure I'll have a more positive opinion of it, but there still has been multiple times where i went "I hate this game so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ much" Or "Why am i still playing this?" but either way.

Most chases are luck based/trial and error and you have to wait 6+ seconds unless you press F12 when you get caught, be aware. It's interesting to watch the animation the first time, grating the 10th.

and please, oh dear god, Don't force a player to get a bad ending over a difficulty, if you are going to have two difficulties (or more) make them both viable to play the whole game with. Not just make one the "Actual" option and the other one a gargantuan waste of your players time.

I've had to reset my game THREE TIMES and if i can muster the interest in fully finishing the game to see the storyline I paid 5.99$ for. a Fourth time. Four Resets. Because of bad design choices which are easy to miss storyline lockouts if you don't do EXACLTY what the developer wants you to do. If this was a f2p game or even like 2$, I wouldve let it pass and given this game a more positive review, ontop of a reccomendation, cause even with these problems i have with it, the story is actually pretty good from what i've seen, the worldbuilding is fantastic, and from what i've seen I do love some of the designs, but no, this is 5.99$ and 7.98$ if you count the OST.

For my first playthrough, I went through the game casually on Normal, and missed one dialouge option at the end of the ventilation section towards Puro where i was supposed to thank him for helping shoo away a black dog. Bam. Bad ending. Mk then guess i'll restart..

Second playthrough, I went through the game (Mistakenly on easy because i dared to see the story faster.), did all the correct dialouge options, and bam, bad ending for playing on easy.

ooooookay, Third playthrough, slogged on normal for the 2nd time, having 0 fun the entire time slamming through the dialouge ive already seen twice now, doing the correct dialouge options, Horray! No bad ending this time, I start going through the second portion. during it I apparently missed a interaction where i was supposed to wait for Puro to catch up with me, and got a bad ending because of it, and accidentally saved over my slot i made when i first got there just before i got the bad ending, not realizing in doing so i locked myself out of the rest of the game. Again.

Holy hell, Don't make your progression system for your story-based-game to be so tedious to deal with, having to do everything perfectly to see the whole game. I really want to like this game from what i've seen, but it's just so damn annoying, especially with the problems it already has. And no, telling a player to play the game over again is NOT good as a design choice unless you do it correctly, this is not Undertale, where resets are an actual genuine part of the game and you can see other dialouge options and make different choices to still get an ending you desire, (and even then theres no real "complete redo" resets in that game) this is just forcing you to go back and do the game again to fix any 'mistakes' you made that you didnt even know you made until it was too late.

Gameplay wise, some of the puzzles were quite fun to figure out, like the robot pressure pad puzzle in the Lab, the 1st area Floor Puzzle. Yet others, Not so much, as a few of them were a bit of a leap in logic (like doing 3002 as a password for MOON? Guess that kind of works.) and i actually had to look up a guide for when you enter the ventilation and have to type in 8020 (and i still haven't figured out where the hell it gets that number from, even with the hint, maybe i'm just dumb, but either way.)

Another gameplay issue I have, is this game, unlike what people Ive seen say in reviews, is the game is not "Hard"
It is Tedious. Fighting the shark boss near the end of the first half? It isnt Hard, It's just annoying, and once you figure out the strategy it's still 'difficult' cause the game can still just go "No." and screw you anyways, alot of other parts of the game will just game over you because you went the wrong way, forcing you to just slam your head into the wall until you figure out the path, aswell as sparse save points, requiring long walks just to get back to where you just got transformed. Parts of the game that ive done also require VERY specific movement to get through like When you're getting chased by the orange (cat/dog?) through the White Latex area and you have to stand in a specific spot in the top left corner and just barely not get touched to avoid the rest of the enemies to be able to get to a previous area where you can hide in a random convienent locker. The game has it's good, even great moments, but the bad outweights the good.

Edit: Finished the game earlier, my opinion doesnt change much. A plot convience ending which couldve come up earlier than K doing what he did. Having to press Escape during the final choice to get the "Actual" ending is just... Ok? Unless i looked up a guide, I would've never figured it out without clicking every key in that sequence (and still wouldnt realize i could do that. Both other choices are also bad endings... Nice... Atleast i don't have to restart the game to get to choose those options and experience them.) Maybe there's a hint that i didnt find along the way to get to the end that i didnt find, but either way.

Characters/Etc

Puro as a character was alright, I don't personally understand all the love he gets, maybe it's from me having bad experiences having to go through the 1st bad ending twice and restarting twice because of it, but the "Wolf Den" Scene in conjunction with what he does to you anyways gives me mixed signals. He is a lil munchkin though and i enjoyed alot of his dialouge post getting past the main generator.

"K" (i think his name is) Is a ok character, I absolutely hated him during his reveal, and still dont like him that much even after seeing all the endings, but the fact that he couldve just, yknow, Talked to you first, instead of knocking you out and going off to kill Puro.And let's not talk about the bad end where he transforms/brainwashes you. alot of mixed signals from what he does and what he says.

Expected dialouge dump near the ending was neccessary, but i still feel it could've been handled better, not gonna say much else as my storytelling criticisms don't go that deep typically.

If i could give a Lukewarm/Neutral review i would.

All in all, if you got the cash, go ahead and buy it I suppose, the developer definetly did a good job overall, just heed my warnings. There are huge flaws.

also I just spent 10+ hours of my life playing and writing a detailed review for a Furry Latex Fetish game. Help.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA short story game about a guy who does not want to become a latex furry who eventually meets a non hostile latex furry. The main focus of the game is about you and your partner trying to survive. The gameplay is alike to other top down horror titles like Ao Oni. This means there is heavy empasis on trial and error until the player figures out the correct movement patterns to escape their attackers. I do not reccomend this game as the trial and error aspects outweigh the story elements. The story elements are simple dialogue choices and actions that improve your relationship with your partner. There is no other major gameplay elements other than being nice to your partner and running away from attackers in a specific pattern.

TL-DR version : Trial and error gameplay outweighs the story elements thus I do not reccomend.

If you do plan to play, bring a walkthrough to help figuring out the movement pattern you need to follow and do not pick easy mode else you will miss out on 60% of the game.\t\t\t\t\t
