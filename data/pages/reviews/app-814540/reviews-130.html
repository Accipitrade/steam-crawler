Brilliant game halted by poor game design choices.

Warning: Review is based on the original game instead of Special edition. This is mainly because to my knowledge, Special edition is not fully finished yet. Might rewrite after Special edition will be considered finished.

Note: I did not enjoy fetish content presented here in any way. I played game mainly for characters and story (we will talk about that later).


Let's start from the first thing you see after watching game disclaimers.
1. Main menu.
Default Rpg Maker menu sucks without proper editing, it's common knowledge. Here dev added Language select, "Changed-Special" download option and Operation Guide.

> Language select could be added as a menu in game or be asked after pressing the "New Life" button.
> Special download option could be a downloadable content you can install since the game is already heavily integrated into Steam.
> Operation guide is virtually useless. By the time you get to press this button, you probably already figured things up. Not to mention that you can encounter this guide in-game for some reason.

2. Visuals
Game has a really simplistic visual style most of the time. It really adds some kind of charm to the game in my opinion. Most things in the game use less than 15 colors if not less. Despite me personally liking everything about artstyle, I wouldn't mind seeing improvements in that department.

3. Gameplay.
In this game, despite being made in RPG Maker VX, we don't fight or even have an in-game menu (there is technically 1 kill you get but you don’t get anything from it). This is due to the game being more puzzle and chase oriented. There is no death or game over either, here it's called 'Transfurmations'. Game is often compared with Dark Souls in terms of difficulty, which is completely incorrect (will tell why a bit later).

> ’Transfur’ is just a longer death animation. Not sure why you would need it unless you enjoy fetish content like that.
>Since the game doesn't have an in-game menu, you can't exit at any time. Dev forces you to use F12 to reset the game into the main menu.
> Saves are as chaotic as it can get. Sometimes it takes 20 seconds for the next point to appear, sometimes you can get past puzzles, chases, boss fights and still have to get past obstacles to get to save points. Not to mention fake save points.
> Most chasers are somewhat smart, the problem is that oftentimes they move at the same speed as you ON TOP of ability to dash in your direction with increased speed that enemies have later on. Game basically demands you to have REALLY precise movement. Having challenge is not bad per say but there is fine line between challenge and just straight torture. In this game, it's well above any enjoyment line.
> Some puzzles are absolutely insane. Won't spoil any of them but there are a few specific I don't understand how dev wanted us to solve them.

4. Story
Gameplay aside, this is THE reason you want to at least see a walkthrough of this game.
No matter how many times I watched the playthrough, I always enjoyed the emotions in dialogues but despite that I still think that writing could be improved. Plot is easy to understand and has a decent narrative flow.

> This problem comes from Gameplay. It’s hard to enjoy something when you have to replay the same moment around again and again.
> Colin is a name you won’t hear in-game (at least I haven’t found) but this is actually the name of the main character. I won’t call this a problem but rather a strange decision that probably happened after the game was released.



DON'T CONTINUE PAST THIS POINT IF YOU DON'T WANT TO BE SPOILED



5. Endings
This is where the game gets its meat. Game has 5 endings with 4 of them exclusive to ‘Normal’ mode.

> First bad ending named “Halted” is what you were hinted at up to this point. Puro does what you expect (or don’t expect idk). He transfurs you into his host. I personally don’t consider this Bad ending, rather just not a true ending. You unintentionally save humanity and continue to live with Puro. I consider this ending the second best you get.
> Second bad ending named “Too Late” is THE bad ending. Puro tries to save but he fails. Dr.K smashes his mask and he turns into a pile of goo. After you find out about this, Dr.K tells you that your resistance is futile. No matter your efforts, you can’t overpower him, even with a weapon you pick up. Afterwards he turns you into his loyal servant dog. He also mentions that Puro already went past Crystal phase and is now turned into a bunch of latex puppies. Not sure why he couldn’t just cure you like in the hidden ending from there. Still, It’s kinda wholesome that Puro transforms into a pack of puppies. This is the worst ending you can get.
> First good ending named “Sacrifice” is probably a neutral ending but still sad. In this ending you agree with Dr.K to stay and remain hidden from the virus. Puro however doesn’t want to stop his dream of getting out of this building, and so he leaves you. Due to the nature of Puro, this ending implies that Puro dies for his dream while you abandon him for the sake of humanity. Sacrifices must be made… This is a neutral ending but this doesn’t mean it’s not the saddest ending.
> Second good ending named “Terminated by Sunset” is another sad ending. This ending curses us with a similar outcome as previous but this time it’s much worse. You decided to leave. Both Colin and Puro enjoy the outside world but sadly it’s short lived enjoyment. Name says it all. You die at the sunset. In the hands of your only friend. Going back to Puro’s nature. He probably could infect you right before you died, but as shown in achievement, he buries you like a best friend would do. Heartbreaking considering that it means that he will die shortly after you. Second worst ending in my opinion.
> Hidden good ending named “Changed to Wolf” is the true and the best ending. I won’t reveal how you get it here but you can find plenty of guides on how to get it. Basically, Dr.K reveals that there is an option for you to transfur without losing your brain just like he did on himself. This means that Colin won’t die from the virus while being able to live with Puro. Of course you agree and Puro decides to come with you as a bodyguard in case Dr.K commits betrayal. Some time passes and you wake up to Puro near your face. Dr.K reveals that Puro got a bio-robot they wanted to test latex on, which means that Puro finally got a host. Now with Colin being immune to the virus and Puro having a host, you both leave the building with Dr.K ready to support you in the future. Events of the second good ending repeat but this time you live happily ever after. I rarely ever get emotional with games but this was enough to make grown man cry. I can’t express how happy I am for them. This is the best ending and you should get first!\t\t\t\t\t
