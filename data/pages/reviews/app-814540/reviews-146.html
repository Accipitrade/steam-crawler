The good guy is a prime example of when a developer lacks the writing capabilities to make a character that isn't a basic, bland, 2d and is generally unlikable. This very same character is meant to be the "good guy" Even though he prays upon your downfall, and even celebrates the loss of your humanity, yet is meant to be the good guy. You are meant to love this character, and when something bad happens to him, you're supposed to feel sad, but you most likely won't. This character is 2D, and lacks basic character development. There's tons of deus ex machinas where the good guy just shows up to save you from impending doom, this is explained with the "I'm watching over you!!!" excuse, very weak. Not to mention that this character is symbolism for the developers fetishes.

The bad guy just wants to transform you, and just like the good guy, he is also happy when this happens. In the story, he doesn't change one singular bit, there is no reason why he wants to kill you other than just because "infection disease!!!" The bad guy wants to kill the good guy so you he can't save you when he wants to transform you. You're meant to dislike this character, but you simply just see him as a minor inconvenience, instead of the big bad villain who wants you dead. This character gives some exposition that never gets expanded on, there's a couple pieces of dialogue that hint at some stuff, but you'll never hear these things mentioned again.

The ugly. The story itself is very basic, and doesn't have much substance. The story is "bad disease, we find cure, cure make us into the devs weird fetishes!!!" Although, the characters do receive some more depth in the remake, but it isn't much and is still bland and unoriginal. There are people who proclaim that the story is an excellent piece of fiction, but I disagree. There are many plot elements that don't ever get expanded on and there's so many instances of just "we need to prove the good guy is the good guy, so lets throw the player into an unwinnable and hopeless situation, and let the good guy save him." This is not a natural feeling way of having the good guy, be the good guy.

The game play is more tedious than fun. The puzzles aren't fun, and only lead to frustration and confusion. They don't feel logical at all, but there were some that were fun. Most of them don't make logical sense, such as one puzzle where the code to a door are the big tiles on the floor, these tiles spell out the numbers for the code. Why would someone put the numbers on the floor? Why not put the code on a note? I understand that it is supposed to teach you to read notes, but it doesn't make logical sense. The most fun part of this game is half way through where you have to fight a tiger shark, and a dog that has tentacles, which isn't the weirdest thing in the game.
