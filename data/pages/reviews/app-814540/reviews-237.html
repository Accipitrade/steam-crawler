\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow, I'm not one to judge those who like the sort of content this game contains, but even then I think it is in your best interest to skip this either way. While sure, there is some good to this game such as the charming art style and premise, it suffers pretty bad in the gameplay and story department. In terms of gameplay, it essentially boils down to copious amounts of trial and error disguised to be "puzzles", hindered even further by how jank the controls and enemy AI are due to engine choice (RPG Maker). Choices you make also matter pretty heavily and can cost you your playthrough should you do any choice wrong, which the game never tells you as far as I know. As for story, while I believe it works on paper, it feels pretty dull and void of the potential it could've had. I can never hate Puro, but I would be lying if I said he didn't felt like a pretty one-dimensional partner throughout the game. It doesn't help either that this game has a bad case of "Engrish" reading, which while I won't hold it against the devs heavily due to it not being their native language, it still irks me.

As I said at the start, suggestive content aside, you should probably just skip this game. Maybe get it on sale if it ever does, but still. I know there is a "special" upgraded edition coming out in the future, which from what I heard improves on the base of the game substantially, so if it ever comes out I may give this game another shot, but for now, just wait until then and look elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t
