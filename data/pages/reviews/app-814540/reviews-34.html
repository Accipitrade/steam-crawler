Notes
This is a review about the classic version that may be replaced by the special version anytime soon.

Overview
At first I was kinda unsure about playing this game, because most people say it's an eroge and it even has sexual content tag on Steam, and I wasn't in the mood to play NSFW. But in the end, even though there are some suggestive scenes, it doesn't really have anything that lewd, it's actually much softer than the ecchi you normally see in anime. There's nothing sexually explicit, it's actually pretty cute and wholesome and has some Undertale vibes to it. It's one of the best indie games I've played, just wish it was longer (but looks like the special-edition will fix that).

Gameplay
Quite simple 2D game where you basically just have to walk around and interact with things, you also have to run once in a while.

Graphics
The game is entirely in flat color pixel art, it has good quality and some cutscenes feature even more detailed pixel art.

Audio
The soundtrack is quite nice and it fits well with the atmosphere of the game. The sound effects are okay.

Price
The game has a fair price for the content it provides.

Difficulty
The game is mostly linear so it isn't hard to find everything and most of the puzzles are easy as well, although some may take a while to find out what you need to do. There are some parts where you need to be fast, which can be challenging, but nothing impossible.

Duration
The game is a bit short, even if you explore everything and want to get all endings, but the upcoming special edition will add a lot of content so it will be a little longer.

Replayability
You may want to play again to get an ending you lost or just to see some game over scenes you haven't seen in your previous run.

Achievements
They are fairly easy to get, you just need to get all endings, which are quite easy to achieve.

Problems
In the Portuguese version, there's a puzzle you can't normally solve because of a mistranslation, the only workaround is to search for the answer on the web.\t\t\t\t\t
