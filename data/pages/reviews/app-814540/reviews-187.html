\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game was one of the most weird ones I´ve played in a long time. It made me think of it´s themes for the following days, not limited to "cute" stuff, it gets existential. I am not good at writing reviews, but I´ll try my best to get my feelings about this game out in a way that may lead you to buying this game, as I totally recommend this for everyone, not just furries. This will be a long read.

(Some very mild Spoilers ahead, nothing you didnt read from others)

I myself received this game as a joke, commanded not to look at the storepage or any of it´s content at all before playing. The only thing I thought I recognized was the Deuteragonist of this game, Puro, as this character is fairly popular amongst various internet communities. In short: I went into this game completely blind.

It should probably also be noted that I - before starting the game - haven´t really been engaged in furry topics except for your standard anthropomorphic animecharacters. BNL, for example.

--- Puzzles and chases ---

You will get a pretty hard action puzzle game with a lot of chase sequences. There is easy puzzles solved in minutes, but sometimes I really got stuck for up to 30 minutes just to figure something out. This is not to say that these puzzles are unsolvable, but you should not be a smoothbrain like me when attempting some puzzles. It is often benificial to just focus on everything surrounding you, you WILL have to think hard from time to time.
Some puzzles might seem unfair, and there is a tiny bit of RNG in some of them, however most of the time you are the only one to be blamed for not figuring out something very simple for so long. I felt very dumb at times, especially when my pals, who I streamed this entire game to, figured it out WAYYYY before I did.
As for the chase sequences, you have to think very fast if you do not wish to "die" (more on that later). This ranges from taking the right route over pushing crates in the right combination to stopping dead in your tracks when enemies suddenly move in front of you.
To make it short: Some puzzles and chases are pretty straightforward, some are rather complex.

You will "die" a lot of times, there is some trial and error to some puzzles and chases. This however does not reach an annoying level if you are mild tempered. I for myself almost raged two times due to RNG and especially me being a smoothbrain. If that happens, it was best to pause the game and continue on the next day. Trust me, you can´t think straight on this when angry.

7/10 very hard, not unfair, tiny bit of rng, quite some trial and error.


--- From puzzles, on to you "dying" ---

As you probably noticed, I always put the word "dying" in quotation marks, as "dying" in this game is not like physically dying, but rather being transformed into an anthropomorphic animal like latex creature. Yes. You read that right. Latex creature.
I will be using the term "dying" for this, as this is a Game Over situation. One could say this game is a "try not to become a furry" simulator.
Dying was very uncomfortable for me in the first few hours. There are even some cutscenes dedicated to dying, often described in heavy detail. This is definately the "sexual" part of the game, one could say this makes it a fetish game.
It never gets too explicit tho. Very suggestive, yes, explicit, no.
To be perfectly honest, after around 2-3 hours of playing this game, I was fine with the deathscenes. In this game, pretty much everything is out to "kill" you, and thus you will also die a lot. There is a truckload of different ways and forms of dying, gotta appreciate the variety.
Many people state that this game gave them a transformation fetish, and I can very much understand where that´s coming from. Especially as no one should be blamed for his sexual preferences.

You will likely become used to those sequences, and if not, u have even more reasons to try and stay alive as much as possible.

8/10 for execution. (hehe, you got that joke, right?)


--- Story ---
(no spoilers, except Puro)
You are a nameless human who suddenly woke up in a laboratory environment. The world seems to be wiped from humans, as everything around you is in ruins. You will find out why in the game, if you keep interacting with your environment.
The story evolves around unusual friendships, self-preservation and finding your own identity, what defines a living, conscious being and can get a bit phylosophical about it, in a good way.

You will also meet Puro, your friendly(?) neighbourhood latex monster, who is the deuteragonist of this game.
Disliking Puro is impossible, no explanation needed.
In my opinion, the writing on his character is gorgeous. There is a sh*ttone of personality to Puro, which made me really attached to him over time. This game pulls off something a lot of bigger games did not achieve. You really become attached to those pixels that jump around on your screen.
I am quite an emotional person, however it has been many years since I´ve gotten extremely emotional about a video character in so many ways, except for maybe Arthur Morgan (yeeeah boi) and Charles from the Henry Stickmin Collection. (damn I´m cultured)

So, to make it short: The story, it´s depth and character development are one of the best I played in a long time. It is not a masterpiece by any means, but then, what is? Any story has it´s flaws. What´s more important is that the story of "Changed" isn´t bland, nor that foreseeable. It has some stereotypical elements, but those are executed very well.

9/10 Story about friendship, self-preservation and finding out who or what you really are. Also Puro, I love Puro.


--- Music ---

Oh yeah, I was waiting for this part!
The soundtrack of this game is an absolute banger, even more if you´re into 8-bit music. If not, you will be by the end.
Being able to pull off such an amazing soundtrack with nothing but some few Midi-sounds never ceases to amaze me.
The soundtrack has some Undertale vibes, but still carries it´s very unique own identity with it. It gets you going when you are being chased by latex monsters who want to get your ass.
There is a lot of different titles for various things in the game. Chase music that is gradually evolving over different chases, unique and fitting themes for the various areas in the game that will not get annoying.
I also have to mention Puro again. My boi has such a good theme, I sometimes just sat there, vibing.
Serious probs to the composer of these sounds!

9.5/10 8-bit music done right!


--- Endings ---
(no spoilers at all)
This is something you should experience for yourself, thus I will keep it short and informal.
This game has multiple endings, all off them fleshed out. What ending you reach is dependent on your choices throughout game, so I advise you to save your game before engaging in dialogue. You should keep those savefiles if you want to experience every ending without having to play through the entire game again. Do not overwrite them. The outcome is not decided by dialogue alone tho.
The endings were all pretty emotional to me personally, one more than the other. I won´t go into detail.

9/10 emotional rollercoaster hitting right in the chest.


--- QoL / Technical stuff ---

This is where I have to complain a bit. Quality of Life can get bad at some points. I.e. having to redo whole sections of a level because you lost to a boss with no prior savezone before it. It can get mildly annoying.

This game is made with RPGMaker, and you can definitely see and feel it sometimes. The controls (for me, personally) were a bit wonky, as in your character would not respond immidiately upon inputing sth. This is due to the tile based movement system. Most of the time, the controls are perfectly fine though. Music volume shifting between rooms is also a bit annoying.

Otherwise, nothing to complain. 6/10

--- Conclusion ---
- buy -
Limitation of characters unfortunately. Conclusion in my following comment.\t\t\t\t\t
