\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I just got this game earlier here are hte Pro's and cons:

Pros:
- Even if you do not interact with a lot of Characters during the game, the ones you meet are generally likable
- The art style is very simplistic, yet fitting.. That just shows you how a good art style doesnt have to be overly complicated
- The Transformation animations seem very fluid and are nice to watch. It was fun to see just how many of those are in the game
- There weren't any major plotholes in the story, at least none I saw. I like the way you slowly learn more about what happened that put the world in its current state. I actually rather enjoyed the story.
- There's a Sergal?
- Yup theres a Sergal!

Cons:
- It was just right (please read my comment down below to get some deeper insight)
- Getting the best ending requires you to do "some" rather cryptic things. Like at one point you have to wait for puro to SLOOOWWWWLLLY walk all the wayx through a room when theres no indication thats the case. challenging Puzzles and chase sections I understand, this I dont!
- Some cutscenes/dialog is LOOOOONG and after failing a sequence you might have to sit through all of it again.
- The translation is... funky but honestly, it does get the job done and considering where the dev is from, he should be cut some slack

All in All I would recommend that game to "certain" people. I had a few fun hours finishing it. However, there was one thing that instantly catched my attention. This game was made in RPGmaker. And while the RPGmaker is a very accessible tool it does have its backdraws. As someone who's had years over years of experience with RPG Maker games and the program itself I found the difficulty just right. It wasn't too easy, but I do understand where some people come from when they complain about unfair hitboxes. To understand the Hitboxes and the seemingly random times you get hit by enemies you need to understand that the maps and movement in RPGmaker games are laid out on a grid. So even if something looks like it might hit you, it doesn't. On the other side, if something looks it has no way of hitting you, it does. Its all about understanding how the movement works.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged is a beautiful game. It is a difficult RPG game, that really doesn't give you a lot of information to start with other than your own intuition. From beginning to end, you have to figure out puzzles, avoid goo monsters, and harness the POWER OF FRIENDSHIP!
You play as a human character, who remains nameless through the entire game (His name is Lin, but I digress)
You eventually meet a goo monster who isn't trying to kill you, named Puro. Puro is a sweet, innocent, adorable goo and you should be nice to him.
I fell in love with this game and it's characters. Mostly Puro. It has a lot of lovely art, and at every corner it gave you a little more info about the world. Another warning about what you might face. Every puzzle you complete engulfs you with a sense of accomplishment, because you had to figure it out. It's not easy. In fact, you will want to give up. A lot. It isn't a game for everyone; a fair portion of people might not enjoy the game. It's targeted towards the furry community, and at large people who enjoy transformation and latex creatures. If that's not what your all about, that's fine. The game is a masterpiece in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBasic Plot: You're a human who woke up in a lab. You want to escape. Don't touch the goo creatures, or they'll basically possess you.

The Good:
Undertale-esque (for lack of a better reference) pixel graphics
Some clever puzzles
Likeable characters
Pretty decent soundtrack
Decent pacing
Multiple endings
Simple controls
Almost all areas have different mechanics
Lore and hidden stuff

The Bad:
The enemy AI is kinda random and nonconsistant; This is bad because touching the baddies (except one) is instant-gameover.
If you don't do certain things, your playthrough will end prematurely; Worse, all the 'triggers' are easily missable.

'Your Mileage May Vary'
Furries (especially ones with a transformation kink) will probably get more enjoyment out of this game.
This game can be considered a rage game.
This game has some questionable scenes. While its not explicitly stated as sexual, its not hard to interpret them as such.
Don't be afraid to use a walkthrough.
This game was made in RPG Maker, however you feel about that series of applications may also relevant here.
The music can be a little repetitive (though I only really noticed it when I played the soundtrack outside of game).
The game's short; if you know what you're doing, a playthrough can be completed in 2 hours or less.

The Rating:
My Scale, from 0-10, whereas:
0=Hot Garbage
5=Average
10=Virtually Flawless

This game gets a 6.5 (6 for base game, .5 as it it the developer's first game).
I can see this game being a cult classic that never really spreads outside the furry fandom. Worth the price of admission.
