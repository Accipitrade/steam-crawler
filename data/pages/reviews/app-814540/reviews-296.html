\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged, how I see it, is a very interesting and highly reactive puzzle/strategy game. And, understandably a RPG through the fact that it runs through the RPGMaker Engine alone. It is a great experience for those who can put aside quite a bit of hesitation about the cover. The biggest and most obvious reason why I can recommend the game, as much as the English Version could use a small amount of re-scripting/cleaning up, is honestly the story to the game.

As much as I had a moderate difficulty trying to understand the script and story as we go, I gave it as much effort as I can and followed it through. Honesty, I think the big thing that I am worried about now is how the human and Puro will end up. I really hope they are alright and well.

The games puzzles are on par with providing some of the most challenging aspects, if not slightly irritating but in a good way! And, I believe that my only gripes with the puzzles is only limited to some of the limitations of RPGMaker that I am aware of from personal experience and having seen through playing around with the game. Obviously having to see the same cut-scenes after loading up from a game over can be infuriating to the modern gamer, but, I had the patience and determination to get past this flaw. Each puzzle area provided a unique amount of planning, strategics, and most definitely fast and dependable reflexes to avoid getting "transfurred" as it is referred to in the game.

Honestly, if the game lacks anything, it might just be filler or some extra fluff like maybe additional secrets (though I am not even sure if I got everything after the true ending) and some content in between the meetups with Puro and major events. Maybe I might be asking much since this is a first from the developer, but, honestly this is the best I have ever seen an independent group do in terms of creating a very good game. A game over screen and BGM would have been nice to have, but I can understand any hesitation with the way the engine is limited and if a much faster restart time was desired from getting transfurred or essentially any game over to loading back in time.

The one or two things I did remember being an actual problem was the configuration menu being in Chinese still, but, google translate was able to get me through that one pretty quick, Additionally the English version does not seem to give achievements, at least I don't think there is communication between the game and Steam is working.

To sum it up, even at the full price I believe this short, but, well crafted experience is highly recommended to all of my friends, but, mostly the fluffs out there in my friends lists. The game provides a challenging and rewarding experience that, given time or patience, can be fully appreciated in English. I really hope to see more out of this as a series, even if it moves to comics/illustration. I preferably would love to see another game and additional character development. I have to say I am a sucker for situations where a sole survivor or a single person is subject to either an destroyed world or one in danger. Games like this remind me of others like OneShot and Undertale. Explains why Splatoon's story/lore strikes me in a similar fashion. Either way this is a golden piece to me in the ever expanding world of Video Games, I can see the honest grit and hard work that was put into this game by its developers and I hope to see more in the near future.

You can count me as very much so a Puro & Human Supporter. >w<
~ Invert P.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst off, if you do not like the idea of getting transformed into anthropomorphic animals, or 'furries', then this game is not for you becasue it will happen a LOT.

As a an game, The difficulty is frustrating and challanging, the save station are in moderetly decent places, and the sudden chase moments are shocking at times. The game is all about trial and error, so saving is a must. there are a few places where the mechanics of the game seem to be a little off, mostly the stealth room.

I was not compleatly enthralled by the overall story of the outside world. if anything, I was more interested in Puro, the only sentiant companion you make. his story is quite well done, if the translation was cringe worthy. I spent many hours simply talking to puro, enjoying the odd wolf's company. and, as a note to everyone, there are a total of 3 true endings.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: This game is plagued with poor decisions, tedious gameplay, poor controls and more, but there are many good parts.

Edit: Upon reaching 100% completion, I have this to say: the game gets much, much better as it goes on. The problems from the beginning are mostly solved by the end, aside from those that are engine limitations. The developer has very clearly improved over the course of making this game, and I now feel inclined to reccommend it... just because it was more positive than negative. The bad parts are still bad, but the good (just barely) outweighs it.

There are a lot of issues, with some being so bad that I don’t think that I would recommend it to most people.

  1. No (good) fullscreen. It was made in a version of RPGMaker prior to VX, so stretched 4:3 is as close as it gets. To toggle into the incredibly ugly stretched out and blurry mode, press alt+enter. Some people say that it looks fine on their screen, but for me it stretches the screen out to fill my 16:9 aspect ratio. If it stayed 4:3, I would have no issues, but it doesn't.

  2. It’s hard. Expect to play the same 30 seconds of gameplay over and over again. This isn’t really a downside, until you hear why it’s hard.

  3. The reason why it’s hard is because of how luck reliant everything is. This is the main reason why I don’t give a recommendation for this game. The fullscreen can be overlooked, but this is a deal breaker. All of the enemies move erratically due to the randomness of their movements, which can be extremely frustrating. It makes some segments of the game near-impossible if you aren’t incredibly lucky.

  4. Grid-based movement + fast action = BAD. Another reason for the difficulty of CHANGED is the movement. While grid-based movement is fine for a slow paced JRPG, CHANGED is not that. A large portion of the game requires fast and accurate movements (and the AI to go the wrong way for a while) in order to succeed, and the grid based movement system does not work for this. The left and right directions are favored over the up and down, so moving along a long hallway while dodging goo is difficult, as you need to release previously held keys in a manner that isn’t present in most of the game.

  5. A minor one, but every menu except for the main menu and save menu is still in Chinese. Luckily, this doesn’t come up much, but using keybindings is hard because of it.

  6. There are seemingly insignificant choices that will ♥♥♥♥ up your game completely. This is a major issue. If you miss even ONE thing, you get locked out of half of the game. THE FIRST ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ CHOICE IN THE GAME CAN DESTROY YOUR ENTIRE PLAYTHROUGH. Well, it’s not even the first choice in the game. If you choose the “easy” difficulty, you loose the game... after a few hours. “Easy” is a trap for people who don’t want to look up a walkthrough. There are numerous decisions that will permanently ruin your game. THANK PURO. ONLY CHOOSE THIS OPTION. That isn’t the end of it though. You might think “but 999 will make you get a bad ending if you make one mistake!” and while it is true that some games that I love make similar mistakes, there’s a key difference. In this game, “choices” are not always shown as such. There is a section past the 50% mark that requires you to wait for Puro. This isn’t entirely obvious, and failing to do so will cause you to need to restart the game entirely. This is not Zero Escape, and this is not Undertale. The forced resets have zero purpose but to be tedious and annoying.

  7. The water area runs poorly.

  8. No controller support... even with Steam simulating a keyboard. Somehow, even a Steam controller pretending to be a keyboard cannot fool this game.

There’s plenty of good things though.

  1. The poorly translated story is fun, engaging, and relatively well told when compared to other RPGMaker games on Steam. Despite the fact that even the translators are obviously not fluent in English, the story is still interesting. If you think that you might fall for Puro, you will.

  2. Lots of transformation. If you like TF, CHANGED is great. It’s almost entirely latex TFs, so if you’re into that, that’s great. There are animated transformations whenever you lose, but after you’ve seen them 1-2 times, just know that you can press F12 to skip them. Also, on the wall are even more TF sequences in the form of still images. Played back-to-back, there’s over 1/2 an hour of TF animation not counting the sequences made up of still pictures.

  3. The art, while it was likely made in MS Paint, is fairly cohesive and has a distinct style that sets it apart from many other games. It is varied, creative, and interesting, but there are a few odd choices here and there, but they don’t detract from the experience.

  4. Despite being incredibly cute, this game is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ terrifying. Needing to act fast or else loose all of your save data back to the last save point (usually only 30-60 seconds of progress) is nerve wracking in a great way. Some specific segments are some of the most tense moments of gameplay in any game that I’ve ever played. While I wouldn’t call it a horror game, that random chance (that I hate so much!) really does serve a purpose in making you think that you will be able to be able to reach your goal... and then bam. The latex tail catches up to you and attaches itself to you. It’s game over, and you get to watch an animation.

  5. On the note of incredibly cute things... Puro! Puro is an interesting character that you will grow to appreciate as the game goes on. Unless you make even one mistake in your dialogue...

  6. The game sometimes has moments that make you think “Wow! They thought of _that?_” I won’t spoil anything, but hearing the reactions of Puro to some things the player can do is great.

  7. I’m only sort of leaning positive on this one, but the puzzles were fun. This isn’t Professor Layton, and the puzzles aren’t perfect, but when you get that “Oooh! That’s what it is!” feeling, it’s great. Unfortunately, many of the code puzzles are just plain bad. Here’s one:

    4 digit numerical code: XXXX The hint is MOON. The code is 3002. It makes sense after the fact, but without any more hints, this puzzle fails to be entertaining.

  8. Another “I’m not sure if this really was good, but it was more positive than negative and is worth noting” thing is the music. The music is... interesting. It is entirely deaf to the tone of the story and gameplay, but somehow... it works? The music is no masterpiece, and I have no desire to buy the soundtrack, but the happy tunes that sound straight out of a bright and colorful NES game somehow fit with the terrifying and dark sections of the game in an odd way that I can’t really describe.

  9. There are some great moments in this game. The very last choice reminds me of the Diabolical Box puzzle in the second Professor Layton game or the final 999 puzzle on the DS version, and in a great way. I love things like that.

If those sound good, just know that YOU WILL NOT GET TO SEE ALL OF IT IF YOU PLAY ON EASY MODE. You will only see about 50% of the game. Seriously. You get a bad ending at the 50% mark and need to start over from scratch... after a few hours of gameplay.

In conclusion, this game is deeply flawed, and I am incredibly hesitant to recommend it in the same way that the majority of other reviewers have. There are some great moments, but the gameplay is irrefutably bad. Progression is bad, the puzzles are hit and miss, the controls are horrendous, and the game relies heavily on luck over skill or strategy. There is a lot to like here in terms of story, but there is very little to enjoy about the gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
