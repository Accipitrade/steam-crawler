\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to like this game, but infuriating game design and a total lack of any logic completely ruins it for me. The 'puzzles' have no logic to them at all. It's all a dice roll. Let's take, for example, two rooms, one right next to the other.

In one room, you have an entity that can move two spaces every time the arrow keys are pressed. That makes them one space faster than you. This is easy to get around with certain tiles that will bounce you over obstacles. However, the entities have no logic to how they move. You cannot plan around them, as certain playthroughs they will leave a spot open for you to jump over and block their position if you get there in the right amount of moves. Other times they will form a wall of entities that will corner you in a room with no possibility for outsmarting them. I would say that these puzzles are trial and error, but there is no method. You just have to get lucky with how they move.

Likewise, in the next room, there is a boss that will spawn crystals under your feet, threatening you with an instant game over if you remain on any tiles with crystals after your next move. There are also slower versions of those same entities in the room as well, about 10 or so. Most times the crystals will force you to move in one direction, but after moving in that one direction, the only exit is then blocked by four or five entities. Other times, there will be a full cross of crystals, one on all sides of you, meaning there is no way to avoid a game over. There is a 50/50 chance that when the boss uses the crystal attack that you will have no way to avoid it and will suffer an unfair game over.

There is nothing in this world that is more of a turn off than unavoidable game overs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, I'm changing my review up a little. I've decided I would like to actually express my feelings about this game.
My main issue was the story. Honestly. You're forced down this one pathway the entire time, and if you stray in the slightest you get boned and have to restart. No warning. I have had to play two days total in order to complete the game, though one of those may have been me showing friends the game.
As somebody that has been playing games for years and years, I can definitely say this one was pretty out of the box. The lore is mildly interesting but it takes some insane searching in order to get a decent understanding of, and a few sacrifices.
Now, the reason I'm holding a grudge with the story is how controlling it is. It lets you express emotion, but only certain emotions. You can't tell Puro to go himself, you're forced to take him with you. You're forced to kiss his ass in order to get an ending worth your time. You're forced to love him.
And I hate it.
I hate stories like this. That let you have emotions but tell you which emotions to have. That let you do what you want but only give you a handful of things to do.
I'm not here to try and convince you that the game is horrible- you may actually like it. And I sort of did too. This is just how I felt about the game's story.

I want Puro to die in a hole and I honestly sometimes regret meeting the person that showed me this game.
Hope you enjoyed listening to my opinion and have a good day.\t\t\t\t\t
