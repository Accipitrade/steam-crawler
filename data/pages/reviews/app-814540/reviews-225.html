\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first thing I grew to like about Changed when I was hearing of it was the drama: The constant struggle against their mind, body, and risk of a new and unwanted new body. I ended up finding out how much a gem of this can be if you look past the animal-people aspect and people who hate it for such. And to any people who hate against furries, be it due to the somewhat weird ways people ASSUME most of them act, or due to the fear of the unknown, Changed is a good place to grow to understand, settle the differences of or even have you grow to like furries and their community (minus a few cursed scenes that were put in as a joke.) Just beware, though: transfurmation exists in the real world; I've had stories of people who chose to become a furry irl after playing Changed (but for me, it might not be too late...) which is the somewhat terrifying reality of Changed. I wonder if it was Dragonsnow's intention to have such happen though; Dragonsnow is most likely a furry himself, and he could've made Changed in order to have people like, understand or even be part of the furry community. And if so, he did a good job.\t\t\t\t\t
