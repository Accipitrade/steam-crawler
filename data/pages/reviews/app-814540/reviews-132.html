\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged, the notorious furry game everyone either loves or hates. Please note that +'s are positives and -'s are negatives.

So, the Original.
+ Puzzles weren't bad, some had a bit of trial and error to figure out what to do next. However, once you got past them you got a pretty good feeling of triumph, like you could conquer anything!
+ Game overs were a wild card, you didn't know if you were gonna get something that looked oddly sexual, weird, horrific, or just "oh, I'm a furry now."
+ Concept is super cool, it's a unique idea that isn't cliche or overdone. I mean, turning people into monsters to try and save humanity? Name a game or movie that's done that before.
+ Story was easy to understand, you don't need to be a game theorist to understand what the hell happened. You overall just found out about it by playing through and observing just about everything.
- Achievements were bugged at certain points, so I had to go back and get certain endings. Said endings were hidden behind long dialogue, so it was just pressing "Z" for a few minutes rather than actual gameplay. People said that the best way to try and avoid it is to open the game up via steam library rather than its icon on desktop.
- Game could get a little infuriating if you got stuck at a puzzle/chase sequence, but luckily thank the gods for the F12 feature they added. Definitely makes it less annoying to deal with, but not by much.

Now, Changed - Special.
+ Take everything from the previous game and amp it up a notch. There's more puzzles, more areas, more enemies, and even game overs. So much, in fact, that it takes almost two hours to complete the early access version.
+ With more game overs, there's a lot more potential for body horror stuff. In the previous game, all the unique arts for the game overs took place after the fact of being changed, some not even having unique arts. However, in this one there are unique game over screens for every transfur, some that take place mid-transfur. In these, you can see the look of utmost terror on the Protagonist's face as he realize what's happening to him... It definitely gives you that "Jesus Christ" feeling to it.
+ New Story too, so it doesn't feel like you're just playing the original again but with newer graphics, which is what a lot of remakes do nowadays.
+ Even if some of the new transfurs are very weird/kinky, it's still weirdly entertaining to see these.

Overall, I'd definitely suggest this game. It's only 6 bucks (in USD for me) and perhaps a 2-3 hour experience, that is, excluding game overs and what not. Plus, you get the remake for free! It's a melting pot of different genres, and if you're looking for a short(?) experience that'll weird you out, this is definitely for you.
