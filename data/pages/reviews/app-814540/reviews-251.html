\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNote: the below review is for the base version of the game. There has since been released a 'remaster' named 'Changed-special,' which you can download in the utilities section of your library if you own it. The new version of the game fixes most of the issues presented in this review, if only by removing the elements which cause the underlying systems to become problematic. Movement is still not responsive or good for precision but the AI has been reworked so most puzzles with the exception of one (the dragon puzzle) no longer rely on RNG elements, many art assets have been redone for better readability, and there's overall more polish to the game. The writing is more clear and there are more elements to lead the player to conclusions the game wants them to get to.

Unless you're into the whole fetish thing this game has, this would not even be worth playing if it was free. The game runs on a practically unmodified version of RPG maker so it automatically loses points for that alone, but aside from the art and music, the entire rest of the game is just poorly made and poorly coded. The environment design is incredibly confusing and painful to work with when you require very precisely timed and accurate movement and there's a wall someplace there clearly doesn't look like there is, and the player controller is unresponsive and awful, as is the case with all RPG maker games. I respect the fact that they tried to put in so many game aspects into an RPG maker thing but they did not at all improve the systems in place in creating that game and we're left with a painful experience that isn't worth the story it's written for. I should also mention many of the puzzle aspects rely on RNG to function, which is an immense problem and only serves to make the game seem unfair and inconsistent. Avoid unless you want a rage game or are really into latex furries.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAh, where to begin with Changed? Over the collective almost 15 hours I spent playing through this game and seeing every ending through, I can definitely say I enjoyed the time I spent with it, though with that being said, this game might not be for everyone.

I'll start off with the obvious; yes, this IS a furry game at the end of the day, though underneath that you'll find a far smarter game then you might think initially.

To kick off this analysis, I'll bring up the Gameplay first; this game is HARD. VERY, VERY HARD.
It's probably one of the hardest games I've played (and finished) though most of it is down to the types of enemies and environments you'll face throughout and what strategies you take to overcome them...just expect to "die"/get "transfurred" many, MANY times before you see the end. The few "bosses" there are in the game provide several unique challenges, too; puzzles also nicely work their way into the mix, with some requiring quite a bit of memorization and brainwork to overcome.

Unfortunately, there are some issues I have with it as well; the movement takes a bit of time to get used to, since not only is the movement grid/tile-based, but you can only move one direction at a time. The enemies' AI also have unpredictable, sometimes random behavior on occasion, which can lead to some moments feeling unfair/cheap at times. I also had major slowdown at a specific section near the halfway point of the game, especially at two back-to-back bossfights, ( these were the Tigershark & Squiddog bossfights for anyone wondering) though that's likely due to my (current) computer being very weak.

As for the overall Story, I can't really speak too much about it without getting into spoiler territory, so all I'll say about it is that the story is surprisingly dark...plus it rings eerily well in some regards with what's going on in the world today, even if it may be all coincidences since this game released a few years ago. My opinions on it? I thought it was very well put together and really gives the game a good atmosphere when you step back for a second, and may even put things into a whole new perspective. As a little sidenote though, this game does have some occasionally odd grammar due to it being translated from other languages, though these instances aren't a big issue ever since (apparently) a better translation came out recently.

Next up, the Characters; while the game only has a small handful that really show any sort-of personality, what's there is definitely effective. Colin, the character you play as through the adventure, doesn't necessarily speak much outside of some of the game's endings, (and through the player's own dialogue choices) though over time you learn to sympathize and really feel sorry for the situation he's going through, given the circumstances which I don't want to spoil since it's story-related.

Next on the character list is Puro, who acts as Colin's only true friend throughout the adventure after meeting him a little ways into it, even saving his life on multiple occasions. Personally, I think he's one of the best characters in the game because not only is his personality good, but he truly cares about Colin's well-being, gives several heart-warming moments between the two of them, and...well, look at him! Who doesn't think he's absolutely adorable!? also, REMEMBER TO ALWAYS BE NICE TO HIM OR- ok getting back on track now

And for the last character I'll discuss, we've got Dr. K, the mysterious scientist who (sort-of) acts as the game's primary antagonist starting halfway through the game, and while he doesn't appear too often, whenever he's around he's definitely a bit intimidating. His character really starts to shine a lot more at the very end of the game when a number of twists & important story choices come up, and these revelations really give you a different perspective on his character as well as his intentions.

Now onto the game's music, which was composed entirely (at least, I THINK it was, so take this with a grain of salt) by SHIZI; at times, the game's soundtrack is composed of ambient noise, but at others it can be exhilarating & fast, slow and tense, sorrowful & moody, or just something to fit the mood of whatever area you're in. Granted, hearing the same song over and over again if you're stuck on an area can get tiring, but as a whole, I think the soundtrack is amazing and way underrated; it even has elements of some old 8-bit soundtracks, though that could be down to the soundfont used.

And finally, I come to the graphics, which to me have a unique style of their own; imagine the simplicity of something out of the 8-bit days mixed with the large sprites of something on a 16-bit system, as well as anime/chibi and furry flavors mixed into it and you've got a style that's overall simple as stated above, but appealing, pleasing to look at most of the time and recognizable, which I think matters a lot when it comes to this new age of pixelated-style games. Some areas lack fine detail at times, and while these moments do stick out, there aren't very many of them thankfully.

-----
RATING:
Gameplay: 8/10
Story/Characters: 9/10
Music: 9/10
Graphics: 8/10
OVERALL RATING:
8.5/10

In summary, I think Changed is an excellent game that's definitely worth your time and money, especially since you'll also gain access to the game's currently W.I.P. remake which adds plenty of new content, updates basically all the graphics and is far more forgiving in terms of difficulty; on its' own though, I think it's an experience you're sure to enjoy if you give it a chance...just don't be surprised if you end up coming out of it as a furry.

(Notice: This review is meant for the original Changed, and not the currently work-in-progress remake "Changed Special". As such, some of my criticisms may not apply to it, though that remains to be seen since the game isn't finished as stated above. Thank you for reading my review, though! It took about 3-4 hours to write up!)\t\t\t\t\t

There will be trial and error, unpolished translation (I used the Spanish version) and some frustratation at times, and it will ultimately depend on each user's perspective whether the adventure itself, the interactions with Puro and the goal of reaching the end are worth pursuing. I was willing to go through it and enjoyed it, though not everyone may and that's alright. Those interested will want to make sure to save their progress from time to time just in case and pay attention to monster patterns if they find themselves stuck: if you are prepared to handle these aspects, Changed is a touching experience worth playing through.\t\t\t\t\t
