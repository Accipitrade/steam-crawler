I am absolutely blown away by the beauty of this! Of course, I haven't played anything like this before, so I don't exactly have too refined of a pallet... nonetheless!

When I bought Changed, I was most definitely expecting a super dirty game or buggy- things that would deter me. But no! The game is balanced... magnificantly! Sure, there's hinting, and many suggestive references, but nothing that would be immediately bothersome (Unless you count the fact that the enemies are Flex Seal monsters that want to steal your booty, as bothersome). The whole idea of "transfurmation" is really well thought-out; props to the imaginations behind the concepts. The lore, characters, and overall design inflicts upon me a unplaceable feeling of nostalgia... almost hypnosis. I'm STILL emotionally stunned. The "True Ending" was just about as happy of an ending as I can expect a game to have- I was most pleasantly surprised. Heck, I was crying tears of joy at the end! On the other hand, I wish there was more to it; however, I do not wish for more super-sketchy controls!
That's probably the only thing that made playing Changed less of a pleasant experience (And believe me, it's ineffable how pleased I am with it) would've been the many hours I spent redoing a puzzle because it's so friggin' hard! I understand that difficulty may be one of the unavoidable concequences of making a game in RPGMaker, but I wonder if it could've been a tad bit less nervewracking. Another thing is also the regular typos; something that's completely understandable for a first product- not to mention Changed was translated from a Chinese . These previous reasons are why it does not score a 10.

Nonetheless, Changed has successfully secured the pedistool of 1st place in my records, at least for now. This is surely not a game I will quickly forget; in fact, I'm considering buying the merchandise that's being sold because I loved the game so friggin' much. This game goes down in my gaming history as one of the best furry RPG video game I've ever played!
I doubt DragonSnow will see this, but I want to congradulate you on a job very well done, and I look forward to seeing more content from you in the future!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged is one of the strangest experiences I've ever had playing a video game. At first a friend recommended me this title because it was apparently furry cringe. So, I went in with very low expectations, and to say I'm surprised is an understatement. This is like ironically going to see the emoji movie and getting Wreck-It Ralph. I mean wow, the gameplay could easily be classified as a rage/puzzle game as you're going to be dying ALOT. It's basically "Try not to become a furry: The Game”, except there are some great characters and a creepy tone that this game manages to pull off. It executes body horror in a way few games have, and the enemy variety is sure to keep things engaging and spicy.

The music also ranges from good and atmospheric to annoying, especially when you have to repeat the same boss fight for the 32'nd time. The controls are a little wonky as well, but they aren't anything too terrible. I found myself very invested in the protagonist’s survival, and you really do feel that connection and triumph as the game moves on. Overall, I liked it, in a morbidly curious kind of way. :3\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged is a RPG game with a great Story in which you wake up from cryo-sleep in a tower (maybe a research Facility). You can barely remember anything like how you got there or what happened before you woke up in general.
As soon as you collect your bearings, some strange "creatures" with the intent of changing you into one of them start appearing and coming after you. You are now tasked to escape the Tower and to discover what has happened here.
But to achieve this you have to accomplish many puzzles accompanied by many story-telling elements like hidden newspapers, notes from scientists and photos of "test subjects".

I can really recommend this game to people who want to experience a great story and are up for a challenge, because this game is pretty hard!
No Health bars or anything like that, one touch from one of those creatures and you're one of them.

And don't get scared away by the screenshots, yes those are Furries. But does that really matter? Have an open - mind and try the game. Don't just dislike it or hate it because it has Furries in it, rate it for the Gameplay which i can only recommend!

Anyway, thanks for reading my Review!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me take a moment to explain my experience with this game.

This...game...is...fantastic.

To start off, I got this game for two reasons. One reason being that...well, I thought the art style was very cute. The other reason being is that I got a vibe from the game as I felt it looked similar to games like Undertale. It looked very interesting to say the least and I wanted to give it a shot.

Long story short, I'm very glad I did.

Changed is a game that I would describe as a challenging puzzle game; without spoiling the story, you will be faced with challenges that require quite a bit of precision and intellect to solve (a bit of an exaggeration though it helps to have those things). The "Difficult" tag for this game is no joke; there are moments that seem ridiculous, but if you put your mind to it, every puzzle in this game can be solved, and nothing is truly impossible.

Not only that, but I was also quite surprised to find that this game is actually pretty story intensive. It's nice to see a small game like this impact a player through the story, and I honestly didn't expect to fall so much in love with the characters and the storyline itself. Puro is literally one of the best characters ever and no one in the world is going to convince me otherwise. <3

Now onto the art style...if your first impression on this game was that it was a blatant furry game, well...you aren't wrong. At all. I'm not gonna lie, the game is very furry in and of itself. The game has a very big theme with transformation and latex monsters, and it's very very evident throughout the course of the game. Despite that though, it's nothing explicit at all, and it's very interesting to see the unique transformations the player can go through. Not only that, but it's incredible to see the effort that the developer put into each individual transformation. Each monster and obstacle you encounter inflicts a unique transformation on the player, and that's not even taking into account the lovely cutscenes and sprites throughout the rest of the game, some of which are also transformations.

Overall, if you are a furry and looking for a new game to play, here you go. Play it and you'll fall in love with the characters, story, and the themes, and this I can gurantee. However, if you aren't a furry and may be thinking of not buying this game, I urge you to reconsider. Yes, the stuff in this game can be a little uncomfortable to someone who isn't a furry, but given the chance, you'll be surprised to find a game with a lot of effort put into it, coupled with some enjoyably challenging gameplay and a wonderful story to boot.

To DragonSnow and to those who worked on this game, you now officially have a devoteed and very happy fan. X3\t\t\t\t\t
