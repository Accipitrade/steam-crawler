So, This game. This game is, interesting to say the least. Overall, I enjoyed it, and put quite a bit of hours into this game. When I wrote my last review for this game back in...2018 I believe, I didn't get really far into the game, but, a few years have passed and I've explored much of the base game. Now, I think I can write a actual review about it!

PROS: I really enjoy the Pixelated RPG type style the game has, as it reminds me of old retro RPGs. The setting I also really like, it's very unique and interesting, compared to some of the other games that I've played. The soundtrack is also spot on, It has that 8-Bit feel that is nostalgic to me. The story and characters are also pretty good, I like the twists that can happen, I also like how you can choose what ending you get by the decisions you made. It reminds me of games like The Walking Dead. I always loved that type of story and being able to change it intentionally or unintentionally, so seeing it in this game made me really happy.

CONS: This game is exceptionally difficult, I wouldn't say that it is Dark souls or Dwarf Fortress type of difficult, but It is very hard. This type of difficulty makes the game somewhat hard to enjoy, but this could also make people enjoy the game more, because it's challenging! But, to me, I think that this type of difficulty would make a few players give up, because maybe they are stuck in a boss fight (Like The Tiger Shark.) that they can't complete. The next thing wrong with this game is optimization. This game is somewhat poorly optimized, I run this game in most parts at 60 FPS, but there is some parts that my computer just can't handle for some reason. Let's take the Ming Cat room for example, When I'm outside of that room, I'm running at about 60 fps, when I enter it, my frames drop to around..20 - 25. Like I said, this might just be my computer being actual garbage, but I feel like the game could be a bit more optimized for lower end setups.

Do I recommend this to other players?: ......Yes, I do! Although this game does have it's issues, it's overall a very fun game to play! It has a unique setting and story, interesting character design, some nice story twists (If you get them, that is! X3), and challenging bosses and enemies to overcome! All, for six dollars on steam! (I think that the game is at least worth ten, but that's just me!)

Safe travels, Survivor!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is punishing. Any enemy that touches you immediately gives you a game over. Then you get a roughly 2-8 second long transformation scene (most of which is your character standing still afterwards. the transformation takes less than a second) and you get sent to the main menu. You can then reload the game and you spawn at the last checkpoint you reached. The game has many difficult sections that can take over a dozen tries to get through and reach the next checkpoint.

Frankly, my main issue with this game is that the number of times that you get a game over in normal play requires a faster respawn than is available.

Besides this, the game is pretty well-made, but not really outstanding.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay where do I start with this one. This game is.....rough, to say the best. Alright let me explain.

This game is adorable. DAMN cute. But that's just on a visual level, and after watching a video on youtube about it (Since youtube has apparently recommended this one particular video to everybody) it definitely piqued my interest. So I picked it up and played it. And did I like it?

Nnnnope! The gameplay is somewhat unintuitive, and you don't really know what exactly you can and can't do until you leave the first room. So at this moment in the game you're limited to whatever controls you would think you'd use in an RPG. There's a puzzle to escape the room that is a bit clever, but be warned, you can still die during it. If you do, then you have to go through the whole intro sequence again. Now most likely after dying a few times and working your way to the next room you'll probably be wondering "How do I save?". Well in the next room over on a little sticky note, you'll find a list of controls which unfortunately didn't translate properly. It'll give you standard controls and F12 for Kanji characters that didn't translate properly so instead they're unicode cubes.

So F12 must be the save key right? NOPE it restarts your game. After that I pretty much just gave the game a shot as much as I could but eventually got bored. After watching videos on the game though I have to say that some of the puzzles are either too abstract or would take a lot of time to figure out, more depending on when your last save was, with some serious trial and error. Not to mention the RPGMaker AI really has to be working with you, it could easily ruin a plan you may have depending on how it feels.

So how is everything else in the game? The story is kinda minimal, but suffers from a bad translation. Now in some cases that can kinda work. One of the only other characters in the game, Puro (Who is best husbando) it almost seems endearing, almost as if he's trying to teach himself to talk. The rest of the game though, the translation could really use some work. The music? Ehhhh, I like the music, but I don't feel like it fits. I give the developer credit for including his own music instead of taking stock RPGmaker music, but it just doesn't feel right. The music needs to have a bit of a lighter tone to really give the ambience the game seems to be going for. The sounds? Stock. It's just stock RPG maker sounds, not much to say.

Okay, so what about the reason that everyone came to this game, the TF's? Well depending on how you feel about it, most everything in the game, the enemies, as well as the story honestly gives the impression to the player of "WHY IS THIS A BAD THING?" if you happen to be into it. On the OTHER hand though, this game may be a survival horror game about the last human on earth not succumbing to a furry virus and trying to get out alive. It's really a matter of preference to the player.

Overall, the game is very cute and the story's decent. But the unintuitive and hard as nails gameplay, the latter being what the developer was going for, might make you want to just watch a playthrough of it on youtube. But if you really want to give the game a shot and see if you can brain your way through it, I'd recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
