\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged is an RPGMaker survival adventure game, albeit its MS Paint-esque, anime graphic style breaks the norm of the engine's appearance. Even more unusual is the premise of your character being put in, who finds himself in a desolate research facility filled with strange animal creatures consisting of organic latex. As there's barely any fighting mechanics, the game challenges you in avoiding and maneuvering around the creatures that hunger for a host to possess. As the game is built to be difficult in many places, I fell victim to animal transformations numerous times and had moments of frustration. However, the difficulty level is complemented by the imaginary "death" sequences as your character is caught in a cute interaction, which leaves a sort of satisfying result that softens the impact of loss throughout the game. Yes, this game is made for crowds who have a shred of fetish for above-mentioned premises. *clears throat*

This game is not all about furry service, as there is an actual plot and some background lore that brings in the latex foundation for a reason. The few, sentient characters you'll meet are very memorable and even a particular character is sure to win many players' hearts for the writing.

The sound effects are commonly stock assets from RPGMaker but the chiptune music are varied and fit pretty well with the peculiar and looming environment. There are multiple endings that depend on your choices and interactions, which had me replay the game with improved training and a new difficulty level required for true ending(s).

Be warned, there will be trials-and-errors strewn throughout the game, so players that fall outside the intended "transformation" appeal may have to check before buying this game. I still recommend it if you're into either the survival adventure genre or have no problems with the furry niche that's the game's premise runs on. Either way, Changed is a difficult yet satisfying game made by one developer (+ one composer) that has managed to carve a unique niche out of this indie game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged is a game about trying not to become a furry that is simultaneously adorable and horrifying.

Gameplay-wise it's nothing spectacular, but it does manage to present technically challenging puzzles despite being a walking simulator made in RPG Maker VX. While the challenge is enjoyable the first time, it becomes infuriating when you miss an obscure flag for an ending and have to repeat segments that suffer from excessive dependance on RNG.

Visuals are minimalistic but cute and get the job done. The audio design is good. Mostly silence or atmospheric noise, but intense chiptune sounding tracks kick in whenever there's a suspenseful moment (which is often).

While the content of the game doesn't bother me, I feel all direct references to "latex" in regards to the game's transformations could have been removed while keeping everything else intact. If you can look fast the blatantant fetishism that it presents you will find a solid narrative with the furry transformations being an intetesting story gimmick that like the game itself are both adorable and horrifying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged is a cute game with bucketloads of flaws.
all in all, most puzzles are nice but nothing special, the foreground-art is cute and the story is, as far as I progressed, good enough.

But the flaws really make me unable to recommend it.
It starts with the fact that it has no options. Or so you think. If you played games in the good old days, you may have thought of them beforehand, but if not, the only way to figure out how to fullscreen is a google search or finding a random piece of paper in-game (ctrl-enter). The same piece also tells you how to open the options (F1) but the dev didn't bother to translate them, I have no idea what any of it means.

I mentioned the art is cute - that is the enemies and the player. A nice very simplistic art direction. The backgrounds tho are ms paint copy paste. Sure, at some places they're actually changed (hehe) in an interesting way, but mostly they're bland and look like from babie's first game.

What really got on my nerves tho - you can't skip cutscenes (cutscenes in the sense of "camera scrolling" and text boxes, nothing really interesting). There are certain areas that are largely luck based so you have to go through them a ton of times. And everytime you do, you have to wait for the cutscene. And because of the luck-basedness, the cutscene is easily longer than most of your tries. Rebirth has a shortcut, why not a skip-scene one? That would have been a piece of cake to implement.
On top of that, the game has quite a few chase scenes where you have to run through an obstacle course at high speeds, which make the "brilliant" handling "shine".. The game decides at random if three key presses at once even work (for example, running left and then hitting down to evade something to the continue left - unless you let go of the left key, chances are he doesn't go down). Also, it's tile based, meaning you can't change direction in the middle of one, which make high speed pursuits really annoying. Jump and runs figured that one out 30 years ago.

Without that last two I could recommend it for its price, but with those I just can't. the luck-based levels are frustrating enough, but having to wait through that cutscene time and time again or have to die because the character didn't run where you told him to run is nothing but unnerving.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor this review, let's put aside the FF (Furry Factor).

What makes a good RPG maker game ? Good graphics, good music, good universe, good story, good dialogue, good puzzles and sometimes good action. This game only contains 1.

The music is the only acceptable aspect listed above, it has a personality and a entertaining melody, but that's about it.
The universe is a post-apocalyptic world that looks like the inside of an office covered with j*zz.
The entire story could be shortened to you escape a j*zz covered office .
The dialogues are few because of a lack of characters for an RPG.
The puzzles are also few and are only made of finding codes and pushing boxes. There's also a HORRIBLE puzzle where you find a secret door in a wall that took me 3 stupid days to find.
And the action... You know what, it deserves its own paragraph.

The action is basically consists of running away from stuff that one shots you and could be divided in two : Reflex escapes and puzzle escapes. That could sound fun if made right, eh ? Well, it's not.
Because it's a RPG maker game, your character takes a full second to move, so you need to be very precise when you change direction, otherwise the move is simply not registered and in a reflex game, it's one divided by fun. That's without the traps, confusing map design and stupid AI (I'll cover it later).
Yes, you could just become better at this and have good time, but what will really kill your fun are the puzzle escapes. You just like the enemy with the goal of escaping it. Alright, what's wrong ? Here lies the biggest problem of the game. The AI moves almost at random. It moves towards you, but in a random manner, which makes for truly frustrating experience. Pacman had this covered 39 freakin' years ago! You cannot know where the enemy will go and you cannot engage the enemy in any way, because you'll never know if you'll be able to escape or not. The best way to escape any danger is to find a way, try it, fail and repeat until you achieved it. Imagine that with the uncomfortably high FF.

So as a whole, the game only has a high FF. Everything else is missing from a good RPG maker game. But still, you should try it, who knows, you might actually enjoy it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've honestly been back and forth with recommending this game or not. It's really hard to choose. On the surface this game is obviously a latex furry fetish game where the entire premise of the game is centered around furry latex transformation. However beyond that the game is pretty interesting,

The pros:
Fetish stuff aside, with an interesting enough story and neat character designs.
There is bits and pieces of lore scattered about, although you're pretty well aware of what's happened without it, just adds a nice touch to the game
The music is banging, most specifically the boss fights.

The cons:
The entire game tends to purposely be bad game design and relying a ton of RNG.
You'll often find yourself with the right answer and knowing what to do but it could take you one try, it could take you twenty tries, it really depends on how the AI acts because it's never consistent. (An example of this would be the first boss where the entire room is movement based so everything only moves once you move allowing you to stop and think which way you want to go. The boss summons crystals that move randomly and then stop, making your next move triggers them and they all attack. Sometimes these crystals can and will just decide to randomly select all 4 movement options for you and you're just screwed, that's it. You're at the whim of random AI bull crap.)
A lot of the challenge of this game is that literally everything you touch is instant death, thankfully you can f12 to go back to the title instantly and reload but the fact that this key is needed because of how often you will lose on a first playthrough should say enough about how this game works.

A vast majority of it is, even with knowing what to do, trial and error and just hoping the AI is working in your favor. I really want to recommend this game but I often find it more frustrating than enjoyable when dealing with the RNG when I just want to progress and see the game through. If you don't like games built on trial and error tasks and constantly doing the same thing multiple times with one random time it just works, then you probably won't like this game.\t\t\t\t\t
