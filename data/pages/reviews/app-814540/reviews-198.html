\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI obtained all the achievements and played through all of the content.
While this is an interesting game with a rather generic mystery apocalypse scenario, I find it lacks commitment in what the story wishes to convey.
There's a lot of suggestive lewdness, and I find myself asking why it was included. It felt like it was appealing to a certain demographic, but didn't want to commit to it.
I would have enjoyed the game a lot better had it not had suggestive latex monsters plastered throughout.

The puzzles are interesting, and the bosses were rather hard to fight at times, which I enjoyed significantly.
It was challenging in a positive way, but I feel like the uncomfortable imagery outweighs my positive experiences.
The music was pretty good, but it was frustrating to have to repeat dialogue and hear the same music over and over after a failed puzzle/chase sequence.
The AI moves in a strange way, and I felt like I was relying on luck a lot more than I would have liked to.
The "True Ending" had me feeling exhausted, and the bad ends seemed to be more well-thought out.

I hope to see more from this developer in the future, but I feel like this game isn't one I could recommend to a friend.\t\t\t\t\t
