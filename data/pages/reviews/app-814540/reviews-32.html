\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have really enjoyed this game. I had already watched Neco the Sergal on a few episodes before playing but I found out it's quite a good puzzle game with an interesting story; I was really worried it would be super fetishy and stuff but it's fine. I do think anyone can enjoy this game but people who like anthromorphic animals will enjoy it more as the person who made it is a furry I think.

Some of the things I enjoyed: I think the game really nails the ambience, you truly feel you are in a apocalyptic world rather than just being in one especially when you go outside. I also really enjoy the retro styled art the developer used; and also DragonSnow's friend SHIZ I think made an excellent soundtrack to match it. The latex beasts are awesome too and the art they have.

I do think the most important aspect of Changed is its benefit on people's mental health. I think in this hard world, you need this kind of escapism to help you release all the pain you have and keep you going. I think mental health within the furry community is generally quite difficult because when going through stresses, you desire that certain someone who's a anthro to help you out. I know I wish there were literal anthros walking around but know that it ain't true. However, your relationship with Puro is that someone in Changed who is that anthro helping you in life. I know that was a bit of a tangent just to talk about Puro but it's the kind of escapism that helps me get all that stress off my chest and keep me going. I know this is a bit cheesy but maybe you have experienced or will experience the same thing while playing this game.

Regarding the endings, I'll try not to spoil anything but some of them are quite gut-wrenching especially with the context. However, a bit of a negative are the conditions to get some of the endings; you'll probably need to read up a guide after getting a bad ending to see where you went wrong. I recommend saving in different slots so you can choose the best point to try and get the other endings. The conditions are mainly to do with your interactions with Puro but with the best of intentions (and for someone like myself who loves Puro to bits) it's extremely difficult to get a good ending without looking up guides.

I think there are some sections of this game that were really difficult. I know some people talk about the difficulty of the squid boss but I thought the shark boss was harder. There was one puzzle to find a code that I would never have found out without a guide but majority of them I could work out. It takes a bit more thinking but quite satisfying when you work it out.

To summarise, I love this game and love the anthro aspect of it. I do recommend this to furries but also non-furries too who like a warm but tear wrenching game with puzzles and a bit of action. The game will only take you 2 hours to complete if you know what to do but it took me 12 hours as there's a lot of puzzles and difficult parts that take a number of times to do. I'm also looking forward to the special edition when DragonSnow releases it. The awesome thing is DragonSnow is very passionate about his game and I'm sure he'll improve on a already awesome game.

Also thank you so much for making a game that touches my heart. I am deeply grateful for this game and has helped me out in life so much. I wish all the best for the developer and hope he continues to find success in his game development career!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought this game because I need 2+ hours of playtime to buy the game's associated items in the points shop.
Just 5 bucks, no big deal. And I need those 2 hours, so might as well give it a shot, right?

Sadly, I do not recommend this game for a variety of reasons.

As for the menu:
No resolution control, no audio control, no changeable keybinds, no nothing.
There's really only two buttons you'll be using outside of your arrow keys: shift for run, z for interact.
Both of which I have an issue with.
1 - Forced to use z even on qwertz layout.
2 - Outside of sneaking areas you'll hardly ever let go of shift. All it does is tire your pinky.

As for gameplay:
There's two difficulty options, easy and normal.
The timings on chase sequences are very tight, even on easy difficulty, making me wonder why it exists in the first place.
Particularly since choosing easy will end the game at the halfway mark and lock you out of the true ending. Why?
The way to progress is not through skill, but trial and error, and some encounters are entirely RNG based.
You get a key - F12 - to skip death scenes, but you cannot skip the cutscenes ahead of chases, which you'll be rewatching a lot.
You will encounter button mash mechanics that are designed to f*** with you.
The abundance of insta-kill obstacles, particularly in safe zones, is more of a nuisance than anything.
Additionally it is very easy to accidentally brick your save file by saving in the wrong spot in the wrong moment.

Changed advertises itself as 'high difficulty' and is designed to test your patience. But even when compared to typical rage games in the I WANNA format, Changed does not hold up. All in all I've been mostly left with a feeling of frustration rather than accomplishment. Changed is not made to challenge you, but to p*** you off.
This is a crudely made game worth ~20 minutes of playtime, not accounting for the additional hours of trial and error, with little love put into it looking at the complete lack of basic game settings, and the various grammatical errors within the dialogue.

The only redeeming quality here are the stickers you can get from the points shop. That aside you can find better games for free.\t\t\t\t\t
