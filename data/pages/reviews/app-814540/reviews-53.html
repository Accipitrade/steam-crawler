\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a not-fun and awful fap-bait game, albeit with some redeeming qualities. A game not being fun is the most scathing insult I can make towards a game. Note that I didn't play the special edition, but from what I can tell the only notable differences would be that the game would be slightly less of an eye-sore and slightly easier.
Pros:
- excellent rockin' Gameboy-esque soundtrack
- if you're part of the 0.0000001% or whatever of the human population who is into various ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•-erotic furry scifi transformations, this game sure has a lot of that for you to touch yourself to
- Puro is an interesting, cute, and fun character
- it has somewhat interesting sci-fi universe and setting
- it is dirt cheap when on sale, so if you don't like it and can't refund it, you don't waste a lot of money, just time.
Cons:
- the game isn't challenging in a fun way, it's just designed to kill you a lot to force you to watch ‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•‚ô•-erotic "transfurs". Challenges are either based on going through an area a few times to learn what to do to not get insta-killed, RNG, QTEs, or exploiting bad enemy AI. It felt like a waste of time, unlike other challenging games. There was one especially poor fight, that based on RNG would make it impossible for you to win.
- the game gives you too much exposition telling you what you should be thinking and feeling. The game's "point-and-click" aspects could have been done a lot better, and would've been better with a more "show don't tell" design
- the game dialog and cutscenes and transformation scenes are unskippable, and to boot they're verbose and meander too much. This is a big pain-point, as if you're like me you'll die several times before getting though an area, and you'll be forced to go through dialog and transformation sequences over an over. After the first couple of times viewing dialogs or scenes, I just want to skip them so I can try the challenge again. Even though the game is generous with save points, they still don't feel like enough due to the unskippable dialogs.
- if you miss triggering several easily accidentally missable dialogs with Puro even once in the first half of the game, you essentially miss out on the second half of the game. I can understand triggering a different ending, but locking out half the game is overly cruel. If I actually liked the game, I would likely just play through it again "correctly" to unlock the second half, but the game sucks so I'm not doing that.
- the game's artstyle is garbage and looks like it was made in ms paint. It gets painful to look at after awhile.
- the game is chalk full of what appears to be shoved in OCs. Adding people's OCs is always a bad idea for a game, as the developer has to insert encounters in other than for good gameplay purposes. They ruin immersion, make the setting feel inconsistent, and are typically eye sores.

Pass on this game, watch a youtube playthrough, or visit e621 .\t\t\t\t\t
