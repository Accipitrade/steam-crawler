\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend watching the trailer, this game has a lot of furry transformation. If the trailer puts you off I recommend not getting the game. Getting transformed is the fail state and the death of your character. This game is hard, so you're going to see a lot of it. This review assumes you are playing on normal difficulty and that you are playing for survival. I am not into furry transformation so I don't really have authority to comment on this aspect of it for those who are into it. But do know that furry transformation is a big part of the game.

Expected play time 4-10 hours.

The game involves precision evasion, stealth and puzzle. You have no means of defending yourself and all you can do is contextually interact with the environment and run. This game is quite hard, punishing and often like to throw curve ball at you frequent. You are never allowed to be complacent as you won't know when and where the next threat will appear. There also a fair number of trial and error as not all option are obvious to which will result in game over or will result in progress. You will also need to develop a strategy for most of the level and chase sequence as running alone won't be enough to progress.

Unfortunately the game engine doesn't feel like it was meant to support the precision required for the game as it is quite easy to overshoot into the wall or a trap that will end your run. You'll be restarting and loading your previous save a lot, while the game has F12 function to skip straight to the main menu I wish there was a quick restart button and a way to skip lengthy dialogue before each difficult level. The AI can be quite erratic but not entirely unmanageable but it certainly can add to the frustration.

If you play RPG maker horror game, Changed game play will feel quite familiar albeit it lacks the usual horror atmosphere despite being set in a dilapidated bio research facility. You could see the assimilation as horror, although your character expression after assimilation doesn't really show this until you realise that your character mind is wiped and its actually the expression of the assimilating creature. Nevertheless the game still instil the feeling of helplessness and paranoia. After the first level you will learn to trust nothing.

Art, sound and music is ok. Its not bad but not outstanding either and there a low bar considering its a $6 indie game. The trading card art is of high quality at least.

There isn't much of a story at first until the 2nd half of the game. At first its just about your trying to escape the facility while you try peace together a puzzle about the current fate of humanity and the purpose of the research facility. There is a surprising amount of lore and world building that I was not expecting. The story get more interesting after you encounter Puro, the only living thing in the facility that doesn't want to assimilate you albeit for being too meek. More about him later.

Note* An update just came out with a 2nd english patch, so this paragraph is no longer accurate.
The English translation is less than sub par, I hope it gets another translation pass. Narration can be verbose, clumsy or just plain bad. I find the sub par English fine for Puro however as it make sense for that character and even adds to it. Puro dialogue may be clumsy at time but you can still perfectly understand him and the emotion behind them.

Puro is a significant character in changed and most likely the reason you're playing this game. From Puro you can learn a lot about the world, facility and creatures. How ever the dialogue tree involved has you going back to the beginning to see the other dialogue option. It would be nice if the dialogue didn't end and instead give you the option to go back to the other previous topic.

Without spoiling too much, Puro is quite expressive, enthusiastic and quite endearing. Depending on your action Puro will be supportive of you through out the rest of the game to his best ability. It is essential that you are supportive of Puro as it is necessary to progress through the rest of the game. When given the option always thank Puro, sit with Puro or wake when given the option. Its not that he is spoiled or demanding but that he lacks confidence. Otherwise your run will end halfway and you'll have to start from the beginning to make the right choice

Conclusion

The game certainly can be an exercise for frustration, the furry transformation can certainly be a turn off for many. But if you can brave the content, there is certainly a charming character and a story worth playing for.

  • Play offline so you are not seen playing it.
  • In your steam library you can right click Changed, click set category then tick the Hide this game in my library
  • You may also wish to hide you inventory because of the trading card.
I don't think this is necessary but I'm not your situation. I'll find out eventually if this result in social suicide since I'm pretty sure most of my friends aren't furry let alone into transformation.

This game has a really fitting name, most people who finish the game aren't going to be the same one who started it. Figuratively speaking.\t\t\t\t\t
