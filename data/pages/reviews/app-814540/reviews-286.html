\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged is the story about a human boy waking up from cryo-sleep in a strange tower; he has no recollection of how he got there. Hell, he can barely remember anything at all. As soon as he starts to find his bearings, strange creatures with the intent of changing him into one of them start appearing and coming after him. The player is tasked with escaping the tower with the human's ...well, humanity intact, and discovering what happened along the way.

The real selling point, as is obvious by now, is the whole transformation/furry thing. If you're into that, this game can actually be fun as you guide the human into situations where he gets transformed (and you watch the outcome, your bad, bad person.)

The story of Changed is alright. It might just be me being a bit more nitpicky since I do a lot of writing, but there are actually some really nice moments in the story that genuinely can make you smile or cry. The English is a little bit off due to the creator not speaking it too well and having to rely on people translating, but it's not unbearable and has gotten more refined. The story is not incredibly deep, but it serves it's purpose well for the game. I won't spoil it because it's such an integral part of the game.

The gameplay aspect boils down to light puzzle solving and running from the things trying to latch onto you and exploring and learning about the "lore". Simple four movement directions, shift to sprint, sometimes you have to interact or push something.

Honestly, the gameplay aspect is the weakest part of Changed. Made in RPG maker, it can feel very clunky at some points, and pretty much ALL of the gameplay revolves around trial and error; you will see the load-save screen, a LOT. What I mean by this is that Changed depends heavily on memorization or sometimes even luck to get you through some areas in the game.

There are no health bars or powerups here; one touch from a creature and you're gone. This can result in a lot of frustration as sometimes puzzles are not well explained, or you lose because of some cheap death because there was nothing to suggest you would die there in the first place. There's no way to defend yourself from enemies outside of running.

You will pretty much experience a sense of euphoria every time you find a new save point. Puzzles can often only be solved one way and one way only. And make sure to make multiple saves...because for some reason the developer thought it'd be funny to make the good endings unobtainable unless you do some very specific things...

Not a huge issue, but once you engage in a conversation, there's no way to back out of it. You simply have to keep mashing the space bar to progress through text. It can get slow and repetitive, especially in long sections without save points that you have to keep repeating cause of a death.

Is the game worth it at full price? I don't really think so. If you're into transformation and furries, it can be fun but nab it on sale and be prepared to progress slowly.

Game took me...I'd say maybe 6 hours to do, even with a guide (some of the puzzles/areas are pretty bad...I'm not sure if it's been fixed, but the final puzzle's hints were incorrectly formatted, leading to an absolute mess).

It's not a bad game, it's not the greatest thing ever, but it's nice if you can nab it for cheap and like this sort of stuff. Your enjoyment probably won't be as high if you don't.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I wasn't expecting this game to be as fun and challenging as it is when I picked it up on a whim. There were times where it was a bit frustrating, but it feels great to finally suceed. With many of the puzzles, once you figure out the solution it's all a matter of timing your movements correctly. There are a few sections that feel more difficult than they probably should, but that's a minor nitpick.

All in all, if you enjoy action-puzzle games with both fast-paced puzzles that make you think on your feet, and slower, more traditional think-your-way-through puzzles, I would definately give this game a try. Just remember to inspect/read everything for potential puzzle hints, and to always think outside the box.

Also, Puro is adorable. I really wish there had been more art of him interacting with the player. Thankfully I got the True ending on my first run, since I insisted on pressing the "unused" button at every opportunity.
