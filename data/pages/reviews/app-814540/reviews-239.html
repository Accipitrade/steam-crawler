\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA captivating game. It's not my most liked game ever, but as far as PC games go, this is a plus.

The graphics are well drawn. The atmospheres are pressing. The characters overall feel "fully implemented." The story had me buckled in for a good ten or so hours, and that was with my mess ups.

Now, the AI of the enemies can be rather annoying at times, but there's always a pattern to it. I even managed to get through some parts by doing things unintentionally. The puzzles are difficult, which is good. I like a challenge. If I may be so bold to say, the pacing is very similar to "The Witch's House."

I was so engrossed that on my first run, when I accidentally got Puro killed, I couldn't sit with that knowledge until I ran through it again with my objective being keeping him alive.

One thing I will complain about though, the QTEs are frustrating. My ability to mash the space bar isn't as good as it was three or four years ago. If the developer could make it more forgiving, that would do nicely.

So far, I've only talked about the original. I've played through the additional partial remake, "Changed-Special." I'm interested to see how DragonSnow remixes the story and game elements. What's there has far more attention to detail, and the new character quirks and styles further reinforce my thoughts on them.

Thanks for reading my review,
