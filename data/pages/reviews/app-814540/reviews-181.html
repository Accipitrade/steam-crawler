\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally disappointed, I was looking forward to this based on the other reviews. The premise and theme are solid, I like TF stuff, and I wanted something *different* then everything else I've been playing. But my mistake was thinking this is mainly a game for furry and TF fans, when I think its primary audience is masochists. I can't think of another explanation for why people put up with the awkward interface, frustrating chases mixed with terrible controls, lack of resolution options and volume control, and the inability to have a simple die/respawn feature.

Here's how it goes: Complete a simple puzzle - Get chased - die because your character moves like the robot from Short Circuit - smash F12 to end the animation - go back to the title screen - load your save - redo the puzzle - get chased - die - smash F12 - back to title screen - load save - redo puzzle - get chased....over and over.

The difficulty people crow about in the positive reviews and in the discussions is not, like, Dark Souls difficulty where it's based on your skill and learning new things. The difficulty is, "How many reloads are you willing to endure to see some vaguely ecchi furry art?" And to be blunt, the artwork style and TF scenes are really just not good enough to justify it. Changed-Special is also nothing, well, special. A very slight visual upgrade in a tinier window that is still a work-in-progress. It shouldn't be advertised on the main store page at all.

In an alternate universe this is a story rich point-and-click adventure or visual novel with dynamic choices, with absolutely no movement controls or quicktime events, and I like it a hell of a lot more.\t\t\t\t\t
