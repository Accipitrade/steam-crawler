\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPart I: Naivety

While browsing the Steam Points Shop, i stumbled upon multiple items from a small game called Changed. i enjoyed the aesthetic of the depicted creatures and so i delved deeper. i read the game's synopsis and an idea popped into my head: "This game would be perfect to stream to my friends." i thought. "It's short, its premise is wacky, and haha latex furry game." How wrong i was, that this game would only lead to a few hours of entertainment. That it would have no lasting effects upon myself.

Part II: The First Impression

The game's premise is mysterious but simple: leave this complex. So many questions are immediately raised: "Where am i? Why am i here? Why am i being chased by latex creatures?" But your initial thought is to simply ignore the questions. Answer naively that it doesn't matter, that it's just a fun furry puzzle game. You don't yet know what is in store.

Part III: The Challenge

Early on, you'll find that the game's difficulty is inconsistent, that it raises and lowers randomly. Many puzzles will lead to your death. You will die again and again and again. But something strange was happening. As i died over and over and over again, i still felt compelled to finish this game, to beat the challenge, to understand the mystery. The game told me making it out of this place would not be easy, there were times i had to go back several puzzles because i missed something i needed to progress the story. So why did i stand in defiance to it? i don't know. i don't know. But i could feel myself... changing as i played.

Part IV: The Change

In the military, recruits are beaten down until they are broken and then recreated in the image of a soldier. Changed is much the same. This game has broken me down again and again and again. My spirit has been crushed. And in the stead of that person, a new me was made. Forged in the fires of this hellish game. i have come to care for the creatures of this world. i have come to be... a furry.

Part V: Acceptance

i know now that i was no match for the game. Much like Lin, the player character, whom i have seen get covered in the oozing latex and furrified hundreds of times, i too have become furrified. THAT is the true nature of this game. And when it came time for the game to end, i nearly wept. For i had become a Puro truther. Puro's pain had become my pain. i was assimilated. The game told me to get all the achievements. The game told me to purchase all the trading cards, to forge a badge, a badge to mark the game's victory over me. But why does defeat feel so liberating? This question i shall ponder for hours longer, as i complete Changed: Special Edition.\t\t\t\t\t
