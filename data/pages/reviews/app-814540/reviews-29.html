\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has a certain... charm. The art style is cute, as are the characters and interactions. My heart was kept rather warm for the duration of the game (at least as far as I could make it.) And obviously the game will probably awaken... something, within you. The only issue is how unbelievably infuriating the actual gameplay can get.

The game was apparently made in RPG Maker XV. It utilizes an invisible grid system for movement, which is honestly the entire problem. There are multiple chases and puzzles to complete while being harangued by wandering enemies. The issue is you have to be absolutely *perfect* with your movements and placements, which isnt easy when the grid system isnt displayed and you constantly are catching on invisible grid corners or hitting enemies hit boxes without realizing it. It doesnt help that the movement controls seemingly cant be changed from the default awful arrow keys to WASD. Why I must use the arrow keys when I almost never do in any other game, I do not know.

I made it as far as probably 75% of the way through the normal difficulty version and got to a point where you have to simultaneously dodge floating enemies and stationary obstacles, all while a robot mimics your movements in a different area. You have to make the robot navigate a course with YOUR movements while UNABLE to see it half the time. So while my eyes are on it (or Im trying to get the camera to pan to it), I get hit with an enemy or blunder into a trap on my side. I gave up, as this was probably the fifth time I was filled with frustration just trying to get past one area. I was even following a walkthrough play by play.

Is it all a skill issue? Sure. I admit I sucked at this game. But I ran out of steam, and I bet many people will too. Until the next "special" version comes out that supposedly lessens the difficulty, I cannot recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
