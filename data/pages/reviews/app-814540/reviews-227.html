\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA rage game, but not in the sense that the gameplay is difficult, but in the way the gameplay is unfair. Everything is rigged against you and, unlike good rage games, which are difficult in the sense you have to blame yourself for mistakes, in Changed you realistically can't blame yourself for any mistakes you make. Didn't take me long to refund the game.

Gameplay consists of god-awful puzzles that require you painstakingly look at every tiny detail in every area, and fights are just running away and hoping you don't get caught on the tiny details that were given hitboxes for no reason. Truly a perfect example of bad game design, and a type of game that has been done much better countless times before. I'm sure the story is there but it is NOT worth the abysmal gameplay.

Furthermore, controls are delayed (something confirmed by a friend who 100%'d the game) and tile-based, which may be the worst way for any type of RPG to work. Nothing about controlling Lin is fun, and leaves you wishing it had any other control scheme.I have yet to play a paid-for game with controls this embarrassingly bad/

The fact that this was made in RPG Maker is even more mind-boggling. It must have taken mad skill to screw up RPG Maker this badly, and have it operate this horribly.
