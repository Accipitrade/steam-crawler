\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI tried to like this game. Oh, boy I really tried. But I must say that this is the most infuriating gameplay I have ever seen.

I'd like to start this by saying that I think the game is a good game that is held back by it's poor gameplay. The story and characters are interesting, and I want to learn more about them, but that's hard because progressing in the game is just unbearably frustrating. Here's why.

Most of the challenges in this game are either a chase sequence, a reaction related dodging sequence, or a combination of both. But this just can't be done with the most clunky, slow, just plain terrible movement I've ever seen. This isn't even the dev's fault, the engine the game runs on always has this terrible movement in all of the games that use it, but it especially doesn't work in a game like this. I can't tell you how many times I've died because my character didn't move when I clearly pressed the arrow key. Or how many times I died because an enemy (all of which kills you instantly and sends you back to the title screen) spawned in front/on top of me. Or how many times I died when I had quite literally PERFECT movement, all because I was just unlucky. 95% of my deaths in this game ended with me saying something like "I couldn't have done anything there." or with me getting mad and hitting my desk, screaming "That's ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥!". The air conditioning and white latex den in particular felt really just plain unfun.

But even then there's still some other problems, mainly with enemy spawning and overall level design. Having to go through the same area 6 times, only to die to some enemy that practically jumpscares you and then realizing you didn't save is just plain angering. Or when the thing that is usually safe isn't safe anymore. If your walkthrough says things like "Good luck on this section" or "This part's just trial and error" then there might be an issue.

I'm going to finish this game, I'm just about halfway through as of writing this, but I don't feel like I'm going to have fun doing it. If the movement just felt a little more responsive then I bet would totally enjoy it. I'm not asking for the dev to change anything, I know the (movement) issues I mentioned are out of their control. But, this game WILL have you dying over and over and over again, until you finally beat one part, just to do the same thing on the next part. There is an easy mode, but it only lets you get to "Dark Latex Decision", which is about halfway. I like the story, but the gameplay holds me back from truly liking the game. I might change my review after I beat it, but for now, it's a negative.

8/31/22 Edit: I beat the game shortly after posting the review, but just never got around to updating the review. Now I'm coming back 4 months later to state my opinions on the end of the game.

This next part will feature spoilers for the end of the game. View at your own risk.

The end of the game is something I am... mad about. The gameplay doesn't change at all; I came back after a while and died ~20 times on the last damn obstacle (Which, you guessed it, is another tight movement chase-style sequence that pulls no punches). But the thing I am most annoyed about is the actual ending. The very end of the game has two possible endings: let Puro go out into the world without you as you stay with Dr. K, or go out with Puro, get sick, and die almost immediately, quite literally in Puro's arms. But, there's actually a secret THIRD option. How do you access it, you ask?

Well, just say no!

Literally just press escape and exit out of the dialogue box. That's it. This option is never presented to you in any way, it is pretty much a secret option that you either learn by accident or by a walkthrough. If you get this ending, Dr. K essentially says "Oh yeah I forgot that I can just give you the same treatment I took, lol sorry". You get the treatment, become half animal, leave, and live a (presumably) happy life with Puro. My issue is not with the ending itself, but how the only good ending in the game is sort of hidden from you. The ending could have had you do something nice for Puro (more than you already do) and he somehow helps you in the end. But nope, it's just "press a button you normally can't press and get the good ending."

Now, maybe I'm being too harsh on the game. I will admit that there were some puzzles that were clever, such as the robot vacuum puzzle near the end of the game. I will again say that the game has charming characters, and I like to learn more about them. I heard that a remake is coming out, and I am excited to see how that turns out. But for now, the gameplay will make my review stay as a negative.\t\t\t\t\t
