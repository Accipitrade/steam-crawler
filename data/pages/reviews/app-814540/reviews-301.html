\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI saw a few images leading back to this game and was morbidly curious, hungry for a new experience untampered by anyone else's opinion. Now I don't usually pull all-nighters for games i've never heard of, but after the first couple minutes I was very enraptured by the world and the characters (While few) in it... And then the first transformation happened. Now I don't like to step on anyone's preferencial toes but this is clearly inspired by people with a certain fetish that I don't have. This is both a good and bad thing. For one I didn't think it was overbearing to the point of overshadowing the bleak and dramatic story for me, but at the same time I could see it scaring off a lot of people who aren't into that and missing out. Aside from that the gameplay was engaging but could be very frustrating. At times it seems like progression was based on whether the RPG maker pathfinding RNG was on your side or not... But having used the program myself I know that it's not easy to fix. I have to say the icing on the cake was the brilliantly bittersweet endings. Never did I feel like anything was a copout or didn't make sense, and all of them tugged at my heart in different ways. I recommend this game with a caveat to those who don't like furry material.

TL;DR
Good game with great story, furry dreck sprinked within\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only found out about this game a few days ago, since then I played it very frequently, Now having got almost every ending except one of the not true endings I feel like I can do a real analysis and review of this game. I'll break it down into seperate catagories.
Story: to give a small summarry you play as a lone human trying to survive a monster infested facility that you know nothing about. In the process you will make a friend and try to care for him and in return he cares for you. It's rather allegourious to actual human interaction try to show that appearance isn't really what defines us as human but more what we do. It's rather philosophical which I appreciate though it's not very subtle with most of it. I enjoyed this heart warming story even with having to stop to figure out some of the meanings behind the translation. The translation itself seems to be a text book translation, it's clunky seems strange but isn't to hard to figure the meaning behind if you think about it.
Gameplay: You will be changed a lot. This is one of the charming things this game has and I personally am a fan of. It's difficulty is very high I had several points were I felt like just quiting out of frustration of failing a puzzle most of the way through it, but the story managed to make me wait it out. It is frustrating, but it does not seem to be designed that way like other games such as "I want to be the Guy." It's more like this to show the real struggle survival is, which I can relate to very much having been destitute once a long time ago. The 'deaths' are around 50% figuring out the puzzle, and 50% of you had bad luck. It could have used a few of the more detailed changes in later stages of the game, but I understand how that also could become annoying. Luckily you have a quick button to get back to the load screen F12 which after failing the same puzzle the same way for the 5th or more time is a god send.
Visuals: The style is distinct and consistent, it's simplistic design helping it to convey changes much easier. It's a real treat for those that can appreciate most any level of graphics as long as they are consistent.
Sound: I do not know enough to really give any analysis on this, so I just say it's good.
Positives: I love this game having transformations as a major element in it's design. I've been working on figuring out a format for a game with transformations as well and it isn't easy to mix it in. I do appreciate how sincere this debut game is and I would like to see what they might develop in the future. Also to note if you get one of the true endings, or the best ending you get access to the credits screen where several of the changes can be viewed at any time and a save point is offered there as well, I used this after getting one of the not true endings to help easy the frustration some of the later puzzles presented. It's also relatively cheap and I believe worth it's value.
Negatives: Needed a bit more of either a random selection of change scenes in the more difficult puzzles, or to tone down the difficult one or two degrees. A couple requirements for endings can be obtuse but I am not stranger to that. Just a bit more of a heads up on two of them would be appreciated, like a subtle hint that your companion was having trouble moving through a room.

Overall:
It has flaws, many flaws. Though it's sincere effort and story shine help make up for it. It's not perfect, but it's promosing as a debut game as the developer put it. I would recomend to any who can suffer a extreme difficult section now and a again, or to those that enjoy a good story. (score is skewed due to how much the story made up for the frustration.)

For those shallow enough to not read anything else.

SCORE: 8 or 9/10 Interesting and should be checked out.\t\t\t\t\t

* Post joke review edit (I wish it was a joke anyway.)

So I've just completed the game and I have got to say, it is VERY good for what you pay for and go in expecting out of this.

I originally bought this game as a joke between a few friends and I intended to stream the entire thing, but about midway though, I realized the story wasn't as simple as some blatantly fetishy furry game might allude to, and I became thoroughly invested with the story and characters. I decided to stop streaming further after that, I wanted to avoid spoiling the story for any of the aforementioned friends who might pick it up.

I managed to complete the game in about two hours total play time between save points, HOWEVER, I actually completed it in ten hours steam play time, which DOES count backtracking, failing an area and getting a game over, or trying other routes for different endings, unlike the in game time tracker which does not. This isn't exactly what I expected from the eight hour estimate the dev gives on the page, and I do feel it is a bit misleading, but it's not a big issue. This is really not a lot of content, and honestly I wish it was longer, and at the same time I wish it wasn't. I just wish we had more story, and less trap filled hallways, which are what really filled out my steam playtime. ...Here's where we need to talk about the gameplay

I'll keep it short, it's not bad. It's not good either. At times the puzzles, monsters, areas, traps, and characters can work well and make you feel pretty clever once you figure it out, but in many other cases it'll leave you frustrated and in agony to get to the next save point.
Personally, I managed to stay up until 3am, shouting oddly creative swears and slurs and hitting my desk in frustration as I neared the end.

The gameplay though isn't too important to me, however. For me, it is the story and the characters, I fell in love with them and thoroughly enjoyed both for the little time the game was focused on them.

It's a bloody great game for the story, the characters, and even a bit of the lore. It was all very interesting and fun to watch, and it's not bad in other areas either, but that is nothing special, for me though, the story is. Definitely not what I expected from a game about such a subject matter, it's a pleasant surprise and I very much recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
