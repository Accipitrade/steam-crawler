\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game for me and my friends as a joke but now I can firmly say that I am a true believer. This is one of those special games that pop up maybe once a generation where the mind of an undaunted visionary pulls through with spectacular emotional storytelling and surprisingly fun gameplay to make a bizarre yet unforgettable experience full of soul and passion. It's not perfect by any means, but it's got a lot of heart, and easily lands in my top 10 of all time. Come at it with an open mind and I think you'll at least be able to see where I'm coming from.

The game itself is incredibly simple - you just move - but don't let that trick you into thinking this is some sort of walking sim. It's incredibly ruthless with its enemy AI and stage layout, and solutions to overcoming certain obstacles usually aren't immediately obvious. Every room you leave with your life is another monumental victory, and you should expect to "die" several dozen times (maybe even going into the triple digits) throughout your first playthrough. Fortunately, resetting and getting back to where you were is extremely brief, and the more drawn out death cutscenes can be easily skipped by hitting f12.

On the topic of enemies and death, despite the premise almost seeming like some sort of survival horror, it doesn't take a master psychologist to tell that the concept of being drowned and assimilated by cutesy anthro goo animals with wide child bearing hips is one that didn't strictly come from the developer's purest of headspaces. It's easily the biggest turn off the normal audiences have for the game, but I think it adds a lot to the game's charm, despite not getting the same reaction the target audience might from being turned into a white, sticky slime dog. It's truly a premise that would never ever be seen outside of this guy's little passion project and I'll take it any day over another AAA game about being part of "The Resistance™".

You'll also run into several chip tune tracks while dodging creatures and solving puzzles. A good number of these songs are only meant to be listened to for a few seconds while running from something and the rest are just serviceable. not terrible but nothing I would ever listen to outside of the context of the game. The only exception to this is track 4 on the OST. Crystal Zone goes insanely hard for absolutely no reason and it's been stuck in my head for the past few weeks.

Everything I've been talking about so far would have you believe that Changed is just another quirky fetish game with some challenging gameplay, but the story and characterization easily skyrockets it into one of my favorites of all time. This is one of only three games I've played that have made me feel something after finishing it. It could be that I'm a sucker for stories where the main character travels with one good friend, but I was extremely invested in every character in the short 5 hour time I got to spend with them. The best parts of the game are just the few times when you and your companion, Puro, sit down and talk for a minute, and I think the merciless difficulty does a lot to make these quiet moments more impactful. There are plenty of documents scattered around that help you piece together what happened to the outside world, and they do an excellent job of bolstering the setting and making it feel real. All of these elements fold in on themselves in the true ending, which I urge you to find for yourself.

As much as I like this game, there are a few objective flaws worth pointing out
-There are several unintentional spelling mistakes in the script as well as odd sounding lines of dialogue. The good far outweighs the bad but you can tell that English wasn't the developer's first language.
-Some of the later puzzles are incredibly cryptic, to the point where I know the answers to them but still don't know how you're supposed to solve them. There's a point where you literally have to guess a digit to a password which is a real slap in the face.
- The steps to getting the true ending are also very vague. They're not Cave Story levels of obscure but you most likely won't see a good ending without a walkthrough or flowchart. (Tip: if you want the TRUE true ending, one that's not gut wrenching, hit Esc when you're presented with a choice at the end)

I want to play it when it fully releases, but from what I can tell, the dev's remastering of this game, Changed - Special, fixes a lot of these issues along with adding more content and improving spritework. I know the version he has for download on his Patreon is further along in development than the Steam version, so check it out if you want to support him. I have no idea when Special will be complete, when the complete version is coming to steam, or even what's going on with the development seeing as I'm too poor and college aged to support his Patreon, but I know he's working on it alongside his second game - Silenced, so I doubt it's gonna be finished anytime soon. Regardless, I'm looking forward to whatever crazy ♥♥♥♥ DragonSnow puts out in the future, and I wish him the best of luck with the development process.

I'm not gonna score this game because it's one of those pieces that change entirely depending on your attitude and viewpoint. To many it's a creepy, frustrating gay furry game, but to me it will always be a work of art, and the starting off point to what could easily be one of gaming's greatest underground auteurs. Godspeed and God bless.\t\t\t\t\t
