\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs gud.


...To be a little more useful to people deciding whether you want to get this, though... This isn't entirely a game about fetishes, for people who have fetishes. This is a game that you don't even necessarily have to be a furry to enjoy. The "worst" it has are a few suggestive scenes that are easy to ignore. But if you have those fetishes... then I guess it's a bit extra good for you.

It's a little rough around the edges, due to a slight language barrier (the English translation is a LOT better than it was a while ago though), but the story is a pretty good one. And Puro is absolutely a gem of a character. Even the player character has that slight bit of characterization through sprites and a tiny bit of dialogue that makes him more than just a blank slate stand in for the player themselves.

Gameplay-wise, you will often hear that the controls are not very responsive for a game that is requires very precise movement. I disagree, though I see what they are referring to. The game IS difficult, and at a level that I consider to be exhilarating rather than frustrating. And when you finally do beat a section, it is very satisfying. The difficulty that is not quite so intuitive is that the game engine works on a grid-like system, not free movement. So adding an input before you are on a tile will make the game ignore that input (though there is a surprising amount of leeway here). It may take a while to get used to the rhythm of the controls, but I found no issue after a little time.

If you are looking for a genre for this game, I'd say it's a mix of action-based reflex gameplay and puzzle elements of varying types. I cannot recommend highly enough that if you enjoy puzzle games, that you do not look up any walkthroughs. Now that the game is better translated, it is NOT necessary, and ruins quite a lot of the fun.

However, if you are easily frustrated by not being successful after 20+ tries, this may not be an enjoyable experience for you.

Oh, and also, the soundtrack is a banger. If you like more retro-styled game music like I do, it will be a big plus. Though the only slight downside is that that soundtrack only exists in impactful moments, stressful, sad, and so on. The only improvement I can think of for this game is that a quiet ambient soundtrack would be appreciated. And perhaps some more puzzles, but I'm just a person who likes puzzles.\t\t\t\t\t
