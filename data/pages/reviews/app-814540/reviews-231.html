Intro

So I'll be honest, going into this game, for six bucks I didn't have high expectations, but it is surprisingly fun, challenging, and has a pretty good story to boot. Also just to clear this up, this a review for Changed, not Changed special.

Gameplay, 8.5/10

[] So the gameplay in this game, it isn't the best thing I've ever played, but it is still really enjoyable, every section for the most part feels different and puzzles are very smart in this game. As with most puzzle games, I would not recommend looking up any sort of guide for this game. It gives you everything you need to figure things out, although the solution to something may not be obvious at first. If you plan to get the true ending to this game I do recommend writing down the door codes so you don't have to figure them out every single time. Some sections in this game can feel like complete bull, I'll say, but it is rewarding to figure out how they work. This game is difficult, but difficult is good for a puzzle game in my opinion. There are a few quick-time events in this game where you have to mash spacebar and or the left and right arrow keys. I would recommend that you make a macro to spam spacebar and left and right arrow keys respectively, because for some of these, the rate at which it expects you to mash is too high for my liking.

Graphics, 5.5/10

So let's talk about the elephant in the room, the 'transfurmation' mechanic in this game. Yes, it is strange, and yes it is all throughout the game, it does hold some weight in the story. But the fetishization of some of these 'death' scenes is just too far for me. You will get used to seeing them though, and eventually you won't be thinking 'ew', you'll be screaming at your monitor because you died, again.

Story, 8.5/10

Keep in mind, the spoilered sections contain spoilers, if you care about the story of this game, don't hover over em. So the story in this game is quite good in my opinion, your companion throughout the game is well written, and likeable. The setting of this game is also pretty interesting. Puro, your companion is great, and he has a lot of cute moments with you in game. As well the lore of this game is actually pretty interesting, a virus has killed the majority of humanity, and the only way to stay safe is by being 'transfurred' and the scientists at a laboratory had been working away at a way to transfur people without them not being themselves anymore. But one day there was a riot and a lot of people broke into the lab demanding a cure immediately. That caused a lot of samples to spill and stuff to get broken, so this reduced the chance of normal people getting to live to zero. I assume that most that rioted in the building were transfurred into the monsters you run from in the game. The elites of the world were sealed away in a bunker where they would only come out when the virus was completely gone. And Dr. K, seemingly the only scientist left in the building with his brain intact. He had to transfur himself after the riot to prevent the virus from killing him. He has been working in the lab for around five ish years as far as I can tell. And he runs into you twice in the game, once, he kidnaps you and either gets beaten by Puro, or kills Puro, depending on your actions. And at the end of the game, where you can pick between three endings. His only goal seems to be to save humanity. That's about all I can think of for the story, of course it's more explained in the game itself, but that's kinda my shaky gist of things.

Music, 7/10
I like the music in this game, but after dying so many times and listening to it so many times, you start to notice it a lot more than you'd like to. And personally, repetitive music really grates on my nerves, it's just that you'll be hearing these tracks over and over again from the beginning, because you die in this game, a lot. If you're planning to buy the ost for this game though, I think that's a lot more tolerable and good than it is in game, because you don't have to hear the same track over and over.

Overall, 7.4/10

I think this game has its faults, certainly, but it also has its strong aspects, those being story and gameplay. I really enjoyed this game and I wholeheartedly think it is worth your six dollars. I hope that's obvious by how long this review is. If you can look past the transformation fetish stuff, which is difficult to do, this is a good game. This is this dev's first serious game, and this is actually my first serious review of a game, huh. Hope you all have an amazing day, or night, this is Twinni, going dark.\t\t\t\t\t
