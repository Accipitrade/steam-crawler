\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMan do I want to give this a positive review. I mean I really wish there was a mixed button here. I'm really quite split. Maybe it would be better if I presented this in a itemized fashion.

Short list: for quick viewing:

Pro:
Charactcters
Basic ideas of the world
Music
The idea of the puzzles (not the execution, except for the password puzzles)
The Art
Smartly scary
Puro
Price
Puro
Monster Design
Puro

Cons:
Horrible engine choice, makes the game 200x more frustrating
Terrible save system (but they were close)
Really Engrishy translation for no good reason
Horrible randomness in puzzles and enemy ai, in a game where defeating an enemy involves solving a kind of puzzle
Trial and error gameplay in the worst way
Horrible dialogue system
Contradictory writing, even to the end, for no reason
Horrible and numerous plot holes
Horrible EndingScumming on par with Shadow the Hedgehog
Terrible endings in general


Pro:
The main characters are mostly well done and I really feel for them, I even got emotional a few times

The world building at its most basic idea is interesting

The music is good

The IDEA of the puzzles is neat, and for most of them, figuring out the solution is interesting, but the execution isn't except for the password puzzles because they are real, deterministic puzzles.

The art is pretty good if you like the style

Its scary in a smart way (as in, it feels scary without going over the top or stupid)

I love Puro

The price is fair

I love Puro

All the monters have very interesting designs

I love Puro

Cons (and boy are there cons):
Why, oh why, did they decide to use RPG maker for a game that relies on precise hitboxes, quick movements, and puzzles where you have to step on certain parts of the floor and not others. 70% of my game overs were because I didn't understand where the hitboxes for the insta-lose spots were, but you have to slip past them, or because the movement system in the game is not meant for many quick, precise movements. Seriously if the Dev took the time to learn 2D Unity, the game would be 300x better.

The Dev tried with the save system, they really did, but not enough, this is a game that desperately needs a save point after every room, after all cutscenes. I was half tempted to hack the game and script some sort of save state system.

Somehow (and I feel sort of petty saying this), even though they outsourced the english translation to voluenteers, and even though I'm playing the game a month after the translation was released, it still reads like massive Engrish to the point where the dialogue is at times, a total enigma as to what they're trying to say. Also, theres a lot of missing characters, for some reason, even though the script in the option menu renders just fine.

When you make a game like this, a puzzle-dark souls hybrid, you have to remember what makes those genres playable. Determinism. If the solution to a puzzle is non-deterministic, it will be a frustrating adventure to try and solve it, you can't figure out all the rules because what set of rules they use is completely random, and switches while you're trying to solve the puzzle! With a souls game, the goal is to learn how to predict the actions of the enemies so you can exploit them and defeat them. Just like with puzzles, if the enemies behavior changes randomly every time you meet them, and even while you're facing them how are you supposed to get to the end goal of predicting their actions? Some of the enemy AI in this game violates these principles hard. I went through this game 3 times (more on this later) and got stuck at the same places, even though I knew the solution, because the enemy ai is so erratic and your only weapon is predicting what they're going to do, which is impossible, all you can do is try again and again and again until the dice rolls all come up in your favor and the solution to the room actually works.

This game is trial-and-error in a way that I haven't seen since the early Sierra adventure games. You will lose to something you had no idea was there, you restart, because the last save point was before 20 cutscenes you skip everthing again to do things different this time. Over and over and over again. Because of all of the above cons (ESPECIALLY the save system), this ends up being worse than those Sierra games! How did they think this was fun?

The dialogue system for branched dialogue trees is a horrific mess. When you get to the end of a branch, you either have to go through the entire tree again up to the place where the branch splits to choose a different option, or you don't even get to choose a different option, as you can't start dialogue again.

Okay now we're getting to the counterpoints to some of the pros from above, these are all cons that dilute or negate the success of the pros:

The world building is good yes, but the writing contradicts itself several times, to the point where even when they fully explain how the world in the game works, I have no idea how it works, I have no idea which of the 5 contradictory things they state matter-of-factly about how things work are true.

There are so many plot holes in this game that even swiss cheese would tell it to tone it down. I'm not going to even list them all here, but theres a lot of times where I said "wait a second, why don't they just do this", based on rules (contradictory rules) set in the world. Even the ending is one big plot hole.

This is a phenomenon that I have really only seen in Asian-developed games, I call it EndingScumming. You know it, you go through the game, then for reasons near impossible to know, you get the "bad" or "false" ending, and you have to figure out the inane logic required to get the "good" or "true" ending. Bad perpetrators will separate "bad" "false" "good" and "true" into seperate endings. Really rotten perpetrators will force you to re-do THE ENTIRE GAME to get other endings, and the ones that were made by the devil himself won't even tell you how to get the other endings. The game that will destroy civilization as we know it will combine all that, and not even let you skip most of the cutscenes and non-main gameplay while you go through the entire game for the 50th time. Well folks, this game isn't quite in hell, it does give you hints on how to get to the "true" endings except the "good true" ending, but those hints are very vague. And to get to the best true ending...well, that is a mix of massive plothole, inane logic that no one would ever know to do because you don't know the conditions that get to that ending, and really just awfulness that made me lose all remaining enjoyment of the game. So I would say on the EndingScumming-o-meter it rates a Shadow the Hedgehog, almost the one that will destroy life as we know it, but has one saving grace that keeps it out of that territory. Terrible.

Last, the endings just suck something fierce. There is absolutely no reason for the tragedies that happen in both "non-best" "true" endings to happen. And that's really the straw that broke the camels back here for me, that sucked out the last of my enjoyment of this game.

The TLDR is definitely going be though: tries to be UNDERTALE (minus the meta) in characterbuilding and making the player invested in the world, and succeeeds in that end, mostly, but ultimately, is brought down by the inexperience of the developer along with things that the developer just doesn't have a talent for (like writing anything that isn't a character). I think the only solid things that will come out of this is what the community will do with it, I mean I've already seen a little of that, I read a spinoff comic based on one of the weird endings in this that made my cry and was miles better than any ending for this game. But this game really really needs an extreme remaster, maybe by the dev once he gets more experience and gets a team to fill in his weaknesses.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Despite the somehow "google translated" translation, this is a perfect game that people are likely to spent time on it.
A lot of animated CGs, very, very furry, good story overall, and most importantly: A ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ awesome gameplay! It really reminds me of Undertale, but harder since the begining and requiring more patience. I mean, the game does sometimes frusturate you, but the ending will definetly worth the strives. I would be more happy if there are some rule 34 content, well... still a game for all ages
The game it self really gives the player a lot of surprise, like hidden content or chased by a monster(worst times ever).
Also, for the one who doesn't like RPG games should also try it. Ask me why? Well, this is unlike other common RPG where you need to collect items or what----- you don't need those things in Changed. You simply walk, run and press Z. It's a quite long game(10~15 hrs) for 5 bucks... and the experience is like 100 hours in R6S... not kidding. You got to be very careful, little psycho horror but not too crazy that makes you stop... all that you need is patience and time.
First time writing a review ever, it might sound really messy...... but at last and not least, give a thank you to
兽屋之龙 of bringing us such a great game. It will be even better if there's Spanish translation and a bit better English translation from Chinese...(oh, also this is the first time I think a chinese game really beats japanese RPG games... not kidding)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell. Move over, Dwarf Fortress, this is a game where losing is really fun.

It's very fetishy. If you like that sort of thing, look up the author's FA account, by the name of DrDragonTim. He posts rather less restrained material there. If you don't like it, F12 will reliably drop you into the main menu instantly.

The game's brutally difficult. I used a guide to avoid bad endings and cheat a bit on the most abstruse puzzles, and that left one - but only one - room in the game where the difficulty felt unfair to me. So the difficulty is probably fine if you do use a guide, but it looks like it could get really frustrating without one. Even struggling with the sticky RPGMaker controls feels appropriate when your character is wading through water or latex.

The translation is thick Engrish, but there's a cleaned up fan-made version available on the Steam forum, and rumors of an improved official translation, too.\t\t\t\t\t
