\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt a glance: An EXTREMELY strange, but surprisingly well-put together game, filled with hard puzzles and kickin' music. If you like interesting / unique games and pretty damn hard puzzles, you'll enjoy this. 8/10

First, let me state this: This game is easily going to be one of the more weirder games you'll ever play. There appears to be quite the sexual undertones to many of the "deaths", and the odd setting and characters will definitely make most people uncomfortable, but it actually doesn't effect the story or game-play of the game itself too bad. This part of the game seems to be attempting to lure in a specific audience, and that's alright if you don't really care for it.

That said, the game itself is VERY good. The game is somewhat short, but it makes up for the lack of hours with utterly stunning music (This is the composers first debut, btw!), mind-boggling but fair puzzles, and a genuinely interesting story. Seeing this is the developers first game, it's quite impressive, and I am glad I got to play this.

I DO want to share one of the biggest critiques, and it DOES include spoilers, so if you don't want the game spoiled / have already played, please skip this part.
One of the biggest problems I have with the game is the character Puro. While I do like Puro at the end, and his character arch is pretty neat, his introduction and forced friendship paints a negative view on Puro, and not in the way that makes sense to his personality, or the players instincts. The player is forced to become partners essentially with Puro in order to get the "true" endings, and it just seems a tad forced. This isn't really a problem with bad story telling as it is just a common mistake made by beginning developers. Honestly, this is more of a personal nit-pick, but lots of people feel the same way, so I believe it's important to state.

Well, that about it. For a very humble 6$, this game offers a lot of fun, and has easily one of the best soundtracks I've heard in a game. (Like, seriously. get the soundtrack edition, you won't regret it!)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou know, you aren't going to get much games like this around now. This game is themed like Undertale in the sense that you're on a square grid of movement, and the entire world around you is pixelated (despite being far ahead of that era of gaming). It also has emotional themes and really makes you feel like you've done something wrong to the characters.

I will admit though, it can get a little cryptic, but I guess that's why they put a disclaimer at the start of the game. It only *slightly* fetishizes the latex monsters, and the choice is entirely up to you. For many people though, the main subject is gonna be Puro; you're only friend throughout the game. He's a cute lovable character, and pretty much the icon of the game. It's highly underated though after such a game like Undertale came out. I like to think it would have done better if it was released before Undertale, but that's just my thoughts.\t\t\t\t\t
