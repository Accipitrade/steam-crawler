\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Game is one big loot box

I have been recommended this game time and time again. By friends, by friends of friends, even coworkers. I've heard nothing but it's praises, so when I booted up the game I was expecting challenging and rewarding gameplay with an immersive setting and story.

What I was met with was a system that had some challenging puzzles and an immersive setting with an intriguing hook - And one really, really buggy cat (The first one, if you know you know).

From here the menu selections started to make sense - All of the down and depressive stuff may be story related, but when I can follow a walk through to a T and still fail multiple times because RNG AI just didn't bless me that day I didn't think that coming back to the game was worth it. Upon looking into it, the game itself is relatively short but everything feels so long because of the "Challenge." The problem is, the challenge isn't real. It's a roll on a random table if the AI makes the right move. There's nothing you can do to save yourself, you're at the mercy of the game.

If you're a furry, and into goo & latex this game is just right for you because you'll be getting a lot of it.

If you're here for the story & gameplay, don't bother playing it and go watch someone else play the game. There's no challenge if there's nothing to combat a dice roll. At least in Dark Souls you can dodge.\t\t\t\t\t
