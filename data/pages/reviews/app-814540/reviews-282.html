\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged is... uh...

Okay, first off, yes. You can watch that trailer and rest secure in the knowledge that this is exactly what it looks like. I feel the need to defend this game as being more than that--there's a lot to it, it turns out!--but let's not kid ourselves.

Changed is an RPGMaker game, technically, though it's not an RPG. It's... you know the Ultimate Chimera bits from Mother 3? Where you're walking around in what is clearly an RPG engine, but instead of combat, you just straight up lose if the enemy catches you? Think that, but with fetishes. The intertwining of the plot and gameplay mechanics is almost comparable to a ROR, in that technically your goal is to escape the compound without being subjected to the latex goo TF section of Fur Affinity, but we all know why you're really interested in this game.

The graphics and especially music are outstanding (absolutely buy the soundtrack too if you're considering buying the game at all.) Gameplay-wise, there are some very impressive feats for a combat-free RPGMaker engine piece. On paper, "being touched by the enemies = TF bad end" is the game's one and only mechanic, but in practice, the author has creatively turned said mechanic into everything from stealth sections to puzzle rooms, intense all-out chase scenes, and even boss battles. Most of which you will be retrying a lot. Did I mention this game is brutally trial-and-error hard? It's a good thing that losing offers such a consolation prize.

Of all things--and this might not even be the developer's intention, but it is accidentally perfect regardless--Changed serves as a surprisingly good friendlified horror game. That is, if you are vaguely interested in how horror games work in terms of gameplay--the exploration of decrepit laboratories, the running and hiding from enemies that pop out--but are too young or squeamish for gore, too jumpy for jumpscares, or otherwise unable to handle horror, then Changed offers a fantastic way to experience that sensation in an environment where losing isn't quite as traumatic. It's like the difference between running for your life from an actual murderer and playing a friendly game of tag with a friend.

Which isn't to say that the plot of Changed isn't deadly serious and well-scripted once it gets going. The characters are endearing, the twists are emotionally gutting (the second bad ending in particular is brutal) and yes I cried over the goo TF game, shut up, it's legitimate, I'll fight you.

Fair warning: There are two points midway through the game where it Sierras you with sudden bad endings unless you remembered to do a succession of things previously. You'll want to maybe peek at a walkthrough to minimize backtracking and restarting. And given the aforementioned difficulty (especially since "You have to not be playing on Easy" is one of the checks for the first bad ending) you will want to minimize backtracking and restarting.

In conclusion: Puro is the goodest boy. Also this game is great on several levels. Please play it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t10/10 (minor spoilers)

The game is hard AF, but once you have gotten transfurred 100+ times by the same latex it gets easier. Story is good. Puro is adorable. Gameplay is a bit tight, but its easy once you get the hang of it.

The first run I did took about two days and it sucked cuz I got the bad ending and I raged because this game is so hard. I would get stuck on the same area or "boss fight" for hours and then find out I didnt do something right and had to start all over again. Every time I load up the game I look at File 1 with disgust cuz that was my first run.

The second time was much better, everything was easier cuz I did the hard parts hundreds of times before. This run had a longer story because I did EVERYTHING, but I completed it in a short time, about five and a half hours, non-stop. Turns out I missed a part where I had to sit down and let Puro vent and cry to me and that was it. I got through everything fine and got that perfect ending.

The main way to get the best ending in this game is to have patience and be AS NICE TO PURO AS POSSIBLE. Spend a lot of time with the fluffball and you will be fine, if you do it right then you should get through the game without him "transfurring" you. At the end when K. gives you a choice and the screen goes black and white, press ESC and a whole cut-scene will play and its great.

Overall, great game, story, gameplay and characters. 10/10, recommend to friends and all that . I wont play this again because I don't want to suffer the wrath of that white tail thing again-\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged was a game I didn't intend on finding. I hadn't been searching for games on the market for a while, but this one caught my eye.

Now I want to make this very clear, I am a HUGE . I do have a bias towards this game because of the furry motif. However, I will try to fairly assess this game without having too much of my furry love pour out (I hope uwu).

Changed is a story-based puzzle game. You progress through a large laboratory tower after waking up in a chamber. You don't remember anything but you have an urge to go home. Early on you realize you're not entirely alone; as you are met with latex creatures who's only goal is to assimilate with your body. Avoiding danger (and saving as much as you can, thank me later), you'll pass scattered documents, papers, and photos revealing more of the story. I won't go any further into the story than this, because this game is best experienced going in blind.

There are very few characters in this game, at least, sentient characters. The world seems very small and you have a sense of aloneness throughout much of the game.
Now, the actual gameplay itself. For some it might take a bit to get adjusted to how the game is angled. Sometimes it may look like your character should be able to pass through an opening but won't be able to. For me, it was not a major issue and I got used to it very early on. The game allows you to choose between two modes: Easy and Normal. Easy mode is, well, easier, but prevents you from progressing past a certain point fairly early on. Normal mode is harder than Easy mode (who would've known) and allows you to properly progress through the game to the very end. This game is no joke, there are genuinely tough sections. You may get frusturated, but as someone who generally gets mad at games easily, I was fairly tame in my temper throughout. However, there is a fair amount of luck and RNG in the game concerning enemy movement. It's not so terrible that it's unplayable, but it may get frusturating from time to time. The game is puzzle oriented, using clues and mechanics throughout to progress. Because of this, looking up walkthroughs or Let's Plays can easily ruin the experience, so unless you are REALLY stuck on an area and you think you've looked everywhere, I recommend again to play this game completely blind.

The music in this game is beautiful. It's a nice chiptune arrangement that reminds me a lot of Undertale's music. There are parts of the game that lack music, however, and it makes those parts seem really bland. Maybe this was intentional to make you seem more alone, but I have no idea. It is kind of disappointing, because I will take any chance I can get to listen to more of that soundtrack.

The art style of this game is charming. It has an obvious MS-Paint vibe to it and I personally adore it. I haven't seen many games use this style, which caught my eye and intriguied me when I first stumbled upon Changed. It's so simple, yet so beautiful.

There are multiple endings in this game, three to be exact. It can be kind of annoying, as certain decisions you make throughout the game can ruin your chances of getting a certain ending. If you don't plan on looking up walkthroughs to see how to get these endings, I recommend saving on multiple files (there are four, use them).

Now, many people do say this game does get fetishy with its use of latex monsters and "transfurring." I don't personally mind them (and it is kind of hot owo") but I undertstand it's not for everyone. Seeing how you do experience those sequences very often, I will say that if it bothers you to an extreme degree to avoid this game. But, it really shouldn't have to prevent you from experiencing this beautiful game.

I give this game a 9.5/10. This game was DragonSnow's first creation, and I look forward to more games from him in the future. My main flaws with this game were just the lack of music in certain areas and achieving the endings. One last thing though, there is a "true ending" and yes, it did make me cry. Regardless, I recommend anyone who's reading this and on the nose about buying this game to totally check it out, it's WORTH every single cent.\t\t\t\t\t
