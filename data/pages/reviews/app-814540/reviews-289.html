Changed is one of those very rough indie gems. I loved the game, but it's most certainly not without flaws.

Let me start with what sold me about this game. The fact that you're playing this post-apocalyptic survival horror scenario where almost every single death is interesting makes this game really appealing.

I love K's character. I think Puro has a very interesting development and premise. And I think that the fact that your own character grows with every single step they take along the way is incredible - specially since you're not even given a name, any sort of upgrade or more than a line of dialog throughout the game.

There's a miriad of different enemies that, as soon as they touch you, will end the game. But instead of killing you, you will get transformed into a similar creature. This makes every time you die a bit of a surprise, making you curious how you'll die this time. Even the guides tell you how to get all the death scenes.

Let me get to a few of the weak points of this game now. First of all, the game is incredibly hard. But not in a rewarding or interesting fashion. There's lots of bugs that make the game incredibly hard to play through without killing you because something just randomly spawned in front of your face, because the game glitches or because the crontols are as stiff as your grandma's hips.

Yes. The controls are awful. I assume it is because this is made with RPG maker and your average RPG is not about running through a tunnel like a madman fleeing from a mutant snow leopard that runs faster than the pokemon bicycle and will catch you if you don't time your keypresses perfectly.

These controls, paired with the fact that some enemies just move randomly across the screen is a recipe for disaster. You'll spend 20 minutes trying to escape a room that contains a single path and a monster chasing after you. And in the end you won't feel accomplished but terribly frustrated. The last level of the game, for example, takes an eternity to beat and contains no enemies at all, you just have to run through a room filled with spikes while controlling a character that seems to make slower turns than a semi.

I rag on the controls and the chase scenes a lot, because I feel that the important part for me gets buried under them. And that's the world behind the walls of the tower. I wanted to feel like I was roaming a tower full of monsters and medical records that told me what happened. And at some points it just feels like a game of "Simon says" with a lion that's chasing after you.

You'll get to the end of these scenes completely unnerved and with no patience left for Puro who wants your attention.

That brings me to the next issue I have here. The branching dialog. You're supposed to say certain things at certain points that just don't feel any different or right. You're supposed to thank Puro for everything he does. But all the options you're given feel encouraging.

At one point you'll have the option to choose between "You're great" and "Thank you" right after Puro gets into a fight with another creature to save you. I thought that telling him that he did great was encouraging. But doing so gets you turned into a dog 15 minutes later in the game. Didn't seem fair to me.

There's a level where you're supposed to wait for Puro to follow you, but if you happen to want to return to save the game, the game glitches, Puro disappears and you get turned into a dog 20 minutes later. For me, that made that super sweet moment when you get rewarded for slowing down for him super annoying, because I had to find an older save to recover the progress I had lost.

My last complaint would be the fact that the game has a hard time explaining it's own mechanics. Some level design is amazing. I feel like the octopus boss is an amazing example of a boss that's self explanatory and fun to beat. Meanwhile, finding out that Puro can throw you over enemy hordes without a warning is something you'll only know if you had read the guide.

I thought that a bit more stealth, more exploration, and a few more puzzles would have been a great addition. And I'm really hoping that the game gets enough traction for us to see a second iteration. I'd love to see K's story fleshed out, know where Puro came from, or how the world outside actually looks and feels.\t\t\t\t\t
