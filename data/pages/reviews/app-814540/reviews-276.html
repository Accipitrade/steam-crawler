This game does feature a lot of running, and it isn't very clear what you should do now--so not only you have to run, but also figure out where you should go next.

It's a game for 8-9 hours (all my trials) of running and trying to time your key presses ideally while running away from creatures (I think they still are cute though) and looking for places where you could save your game. Getting to the final of the story is quite rewarding and there are multiple endings!

Just be aware that this is on RPG Maker so this is kind of PITA when playing and trying to hit those keys. However, it's more of registering presses issue.\t\t\t\t\t

I wanted to know what happened here, and what is going on in this really odd world.

Now let me get onto the actual bad points of the game. This game is one of the worst controlling games I have played in a long while... I'm usually used to how horror games would control on RPG maker, but this is just downright atrocious. It could be a mix of the graphics or something along the lines of that, but... Either way, you're going to be fighting the controls a whole lot and a lot of deaths you're going to feel were not your fault at all. Also... The game feels horribly designed. Such as many puzzles and challenges the game throws at you being down to sometimes pure luck. There are going to be many moments where you want to absolutely tear your hair out.

Although the worst sin of all is how you get all of the endings... I played this game 100% blind the first time and I had to restart, over and over and over and over, because I didn't do something correct that I had no indication of doing. That means fighting the controls the whole way through again, and doing the same puzzles that even rely on luck many, many times again. This right here is just purely bad game design, and there's several endings that you need to get to... if you even make it that far. If you play on medium difficulty and or don't do specific things that the game has not told you about- you get a bad ending, and have to completely restart as the Dev, YES- The Dev is a self insert character- Tells you what you did wrong and wants you to reset the entire game from the very beginning... The Dev... Is talking to you directly as his OC...

Now this is his first game and project, and I can tell because he actually tells you that in the game when he speaks to you. Now I could be kinder, but you just don't... Do that... I have nothing against him, and good on him for following his dreams and making this game. Although personally talking to the player as your OC and punishing them cause they did something that was your fault is... Awkward...

Now for my final point to where I keep on seeing people say "OWO PURO HURR DURR" and that's the only thing they say in the positive review portion of the section... Puro is a flat and even one dimensional character who offers nothing. I even asked a few members of the CHANGED community to what his character and personality even is... There was no responses. It's weird as the game FORCES you to like him, as if you don't be nice to him, say goodbye to your save file... And be prepared for the game author being upset that you were not nice to his OC enough. (Although not all the characters are bad, I actually enjoyed the others presented in the game.) I will say at least Puro has a very appealing and simple design. So that is a major bonus on him... But character wise?... I think there should've been a lot more done, and forcing the player to like him is what kills it.

I rate this game on a Neutral stand-point, and I hope the Dev can make improvements on his next project and I hope you enjoyed reading my criticisms on the game. I'm always open for comments and discussion, and will revise this review if it calls for it.

I'd recommend this game for it's story and the special content it would provide for people who are into this sort of thing, but other than that? The gameplay holds it back so much that I have to give this a thumbs down. Loved the story, characters and setting... But hated the gameplay so much that it hurt to play.

(Also a slight negative is that I noticed that my achievements broke and they refused to work... I'm not too concerned about it, but it is something that happened even after completing everything. Although that could be on my end as I play most of my games offline as getting connected to the internet sometimes is a rarity.)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLife is too short to NOT play a furry/transformation/goo/latex fetish game.

Actual review – this game is kind of a mess. It was made in rpg maker and it shows in every default sound effect and janky event tile. The “difficult” tag on steam isn’t lying. If you step in the wrong place, you die instantly. If you try to explore and go down the wrong path, you die instantly. If the AI decides to be mean, you die instantly. The game doesn’t play fair, and it doesn’t hesitate to take cheap shots. Also, the translation is full of spelling and grammar errors, text goes outside the box, and the game’s options (f1) aren’t even in English, so if you don’t know rpg maker, good luck figuring that out.

So why even recommend this? Well the main draws of Changed for me were its charm and rewards. The art is simple and cute. I’m not into anything transformation/goo/latex (yeah, keep telling yourself that…) but the animations for these events are nice and fluid and oddly make death less frustrating. Another great addition is the f12 button, which lets you restart quickly and skip death animations. I played this game blind, and died a lot, but I think it feels rewarding enough when you get through a tough area to make up for the frustration. That being said, it could’ve been designed better. There’s one save point in a room full of enemies that straight up says: “don’t save here if you’re surrounded or you could get permanently stuck”. That save point shouldn’t have been there in the first place. Also like I mentioned before, random enemy AI can screw you over by no fault of your own.

I say all this, but one thing I genuinely loved here was Puro! Changed reminds me a bit of Ib – another rpg maker game. You explore a strange and dangerous place, and make a connection with the only real friend you find. Because of this character and the general theme of the game, Changed is more cute than anything else. It has the “horror” tag somewhere in that list, but there’s no real jumpscares, and no blood/gore at all - just goo. The atmosphere is chill when there’s no music, and more active in dangerous areas when the music kicks in. There’s even 5 endings, and it’s not too hard to access the final ones because I did it on my first try by just showing enough care to and thanking Puro. There’s one other important character: Dr. K, who explains more story and has good motivation to stop the human. He isn’t even a bad guy - he just doesn’t want to risk letting out the virus again.

Overall Changed is a messy excuse to make some money off your weird fetishes, but its charm and uniqueness somehow carry it through, and I don't regret all the time I've spent playing it. Changed-special is being worked on, so we'll see if that changes anything about the game when it's done.\t\t\t\t\t
