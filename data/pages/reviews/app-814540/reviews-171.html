\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStory-
The game has a simple plot. You wake up in what looks to be an abandoned lab where you have been asleep for who knows how long. You have to escape while different creatures try to transform ( or transfur ) you. You can find different things like old newspapers, diary's, notes, and other written things to help you progress. Changed has a very strong emotional feel. All the characters will have justifiable motivations. There are also a lot of choices that often lead to "Why did I pick that" or "I should of seen that coming, mainly towards the ending. With all of the different endings, most would say the best is the hidden one.

Gameplay-
The gameplay is a mix of puzzles and action bits. Once you've worked out how to play most of them become quite simple. If you have a failure it usually results in a transformation. I didn't like it when the game changed its puzzles for no reason, causing them to be harder. For example, at one point you must travel through a nest of dragons. Up until that point, you and the enemies moved in a pattern. In that area only they would move when you press a button and try to follow you.

Graphics and Sound-
The graphics and sound is exactly what I'd expect from a game like Changed. If you're trying to solve a puzzle, its nice and calm. But right when you are being chased a high paced music starts, all while keeping it simple. The unique music made just for the game is amazing. Its simple but also amazing.
Transformation is another main part of this game, whenever you fail thats what happens. The animation is a bit rough but it fits with the games style. Besides that its still amazing.

Difficulty-
Its difficult during the first bit. After you get into playing it more, it'll become much more easier to do the puzzles and running from creatures.

Price-
Great price for what you want, it also comes with Changed-Special which is a remastered version with different transformations and features.

Overall Score-
8.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

+ The story is GREAT , it fits all of the characters nicely, it is coherent and it has a lot of room for expansion if needed.

+ Lots of unique transfurmations, there's probably one for each kind of taste.

+ I personally really like the graphics, they do get quirky sometimes with inconsistency, or weird outlines but overall I enjoy them.

+ Quite a bit of replayability, not only for the multiple endings but also for perhaps trying to make it difficult for yourself with challenges.

+ The game is decently long, although it feels like we barely spend time in the ground floor.

+ The soundtrack is fantastic, the style of music fits perfectly with the game theme, although it doesn't have much interaction with the gameplay, rather than cooperating with the graphics it accompanies them.

Cons of the game

- The movement is pretty uncomfortable, I tried fixing it by plugging a controller and mapping it to the game, it did help a bit except in sections in which precise movement is required.

- Many action sequences are tedious, they require a lot retries and precise movement, I don't feel like this is really bad, but just annoying having to replay those scenes dozens of times.

- The dialogs become quite annoying when replaying or retrying a bossfight, you already know them but still have to wait, spamming the spacebar just to be transfurred 10 seconds into the fight, and have to redo the dialogs again.

- The (english) translation is... weird. It's understandable, but it has some moments where characters casually say things that feel like were translated with Google.

- The last password puzzle was terrible: I spent perhaps 15 to 20 minutes gathering the information of the 3 rooms, making a spreadhseet, searching if I missed something to interact, yet all you had to do is randomly guess the last digit. This does add nicely to the story, and how Puro sees humans, but it feels extremely underwhelming to try solving a puzzle just to realize all your effort was useless .



Special edition

If you buy the game, it comes bundled with a remake under development, I'll also list my issues with the current version:

+ It has LOADS more of content, I knew it added new rooms and mechanics but didn't expect this much!

+ Puro is made more obvious to be the one leaving the clues, and wanting to help you .

+ It is way more friendly towards unexperienced users, it gives saving prompts before important events and decreases the difficulty to make the game easier and less frustrating when retrying.

- It is way more friendly towards unexperienced users, but if you are already experienced you can be annoyed a lot by all the forced save prompts and feel a very big drop of difficulty compared to the original, I hope they'd add settings for this.

- They completely, utterly, outrageously, heckingly, massively messed up Dr. K. I was streaming my first playthrough of this game to some friends and we were very annoyed of how retarded Dr. K is in this version: he tries to transfur the human, even though these weren't his intentions in the original and he barely has any reason to; he called Puro a "bastard" way too many times to count, he lacks enough braincells to think that telling your enemy that "you only have 4 bullets so please don't move" is a bad idea. For some reason he traps the human, but then releases him??? Why can't he just, lock the doors (which were locked), walk to the other building, and transfur him himself? His vocabulary has also dropped to the one of a 7 year old since the original game, I don't know if this is part of the transfurmation drug but he seems even dumber than Puro. In this version he is just a classic cartoon village, rather than the "unintended bad guy" from the first game; honestly, horrible design in this version. I loved Dr. K in the original game, but this is simply too bad.

- Please please PLEASE FOR THE LOVE OF GOD ADD AN OPTION TO REMOVE THE WHITE OUTLINES, IT'S TOO MUCH FOR MY EYES.



Conclusion

Changed is an amazing price, especially for this price, if you like what you see, get it, if you don't like what you see, get it, it'll probably change all things.\t\t\t\t\t
