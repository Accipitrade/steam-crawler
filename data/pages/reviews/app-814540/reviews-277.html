\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is absolutely fascinating, the story is fed to you in adequate bits and pieces to keep you intrigued as it pieces itself together. You can tell the developer put a lot of heart into this creation of theirs.

The art is somewhat base but there is a LOT of it and nothing I'd pin as poor quality. It is as shown somewhat provocative and you bet it's furry. That was never much of a focal point for me however as the other elements and might I say marvelous sound design kept me involved and playing to the end.

Having said that the game is frustratingly difficult at certain intervals that may halt your journey for upwards of an hour if you're unlucky.

Is it worth a purchase? Absolutely, it's not adult and remains tasteful so even those without quite the same interests will definitely find this game enjoyable.

PROS:
-Stellar sound design
-Interesting characters and plot
-An excess of art and consistent compelling atmosphere
-Simple to play and learn, mild puzzle elements and frequent saves

CONS:
-Game design is at times clunky, unfair or difficult to an aggravating extreme
-Furries (or a Pro if that's your thing)

SCORE: For the price? 8/10

I absolutely look forward to more work from the designers and they can count on my purchase.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt this point you'll probably know that this game is notorious for being very hard and featuring very weird and suggestive images and animations.

First a word on mechanical soundness and difficulty: It's an RPG maker game (VX) with no combat system. Generally you touch something you lose or it's a QTE. The main challenges come from:

1. having to precisely navigate at high speed with VX controls that are not really made for this sort of thing, like traversing a maze with something chasing you that will kill you if you are 0.5seconds off with just typical RPG maker controls.
2. Often having to know about objects that you cannot see or barely see due to the perspective.
3. Playing against indeterministic enemy AIs on enemies that tend to have a physical advantage over you. One earlier encounter, the big tail would invariably defeat you if it moved efficiently as it is much faster than you, but it will often misstep. I am convinced that many of the encounters are impossible to win with bad RNG.

Save points are very frequent, especially before hard encounters, but NOT AFTER. You may beat a boss and then run into a death trap before finding another savepoint. The game can be cruel at times.

About the weird visuals: It's clear that the developer is into that sort of thing and people who are curious or also into such kinks(Transformation, slime, absorption, latex, fluffy things) will get something extra out of it, but if you think the game is just an excuse to expose people to these animations you are very mistaken.

What this game really means to people who have played it comes from the story and characters("Puro", as most reviews will tell you). If you have a tendency to be empathetic towards NPCs and be thorough, it becomes an extraordinarily intense experience and may, no pun intended, change you.

If you want to go through the game without spoilers, just please take this one advice:
Watch Episode 2 of the 2011 version of "Hunter x Hunter" and remember it thoroughly.
The game has nothing to do with the series, but a lesson from it is crucial.

EDIT: A remastered version is in production and will eventually become the default version of the game. The remastered version addresses many of the issues of the original game and expands on its content it should be done by the end of 2020 and will be available to all Changed players for free, as its development builds already are.\t\t\t\t\t

I'd definetely recommend using a guide for this one as there is no shame in using one in my opinion. ( the puzzles can get really cryptic at times and honestly kind of annoying)
