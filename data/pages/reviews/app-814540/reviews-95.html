\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:
+ OKish story
+ OKish character, although i wish Dr. K has bigger portion on the story
+ Common plague/apocalypse hollywood style world building, but i'm good with it
+ Nice soundtrack
+ Many puzzle are challenging with good game design

The bad:
- You only can get worst ending on easy difficulty, which happen halfway of the game. It's not really easy either, but only slightly less difficult.
- There's no auto-save point just before puzzle started. On some area, it means you need to see same scene, text box or preview of area which could take between 5 seconds to 1 minute. It's very annoying if you dying many times. At least "F12" exist to skip if you stuck/dying.

The ugly:
* Several areas suffer from poor game design where lots of trial-and-error required, must take shortest path (you're dead if you take 1-2 steps longer) or both.
* Not fan of rough artstyle, where you could off-by-one easily from intended position. The CG is fine though.
* It's easy to get locked from certain ending. It's annoying if you don't wish to repeat same puzzle more than once. You either need to load from certain save point or edit variable #24 using save editor.

While Changed is better than i expected, i have mixed feeling about this game. If you like difficult puzzle game who can tolerate some poor game design, you'll enjoy the game. But if you're casual player or play mainly for story/character, this game isn't for you. I'd rather choose "Neutral", but since Steam doesn't offer such option i'm going with "No".\t\t\t\t\t

CHANGED is a game made in RPG Maker about escaping a tower and avoiding physical contact with most of its inhabitants. This is achieved by quick reflexes, puzzle solving, being nice to your eventual companion Puro, and for many rooms—pure luck. There are key moments with Puro that will set flags which determine the availability of possible endings for that save file.

So what is the "fundamental flaw?" Simply put, it is the absolute necessity of continually re-loading saves to advance.

"Dying" (transforming) over and over and save-scumming will be necessary due to the difficulty and RNG in many rooms. I don't care how much of an elite pro gamer you think you are; unless you have already seen how the game is played in different areas, you WILL get countless game-overs. A limitation of RPG Maker shows here: each death takes the player back to the title menu to reload the save. This quickly becomes tiresome. In other games where the player is presented with a difficult room to overcome, a death can be rewound, or the room will be instantly re-loaded, and there is not much delay in trying again. In CHANGED, it's all about re-loading saves countless times to either learn the obstacles' quirks enough or encounter more forgiving RNG in order to get past a room. Sometimes, you must advance through unskippable game dialogue to be able to try again, though that can depend on when/where you last saved. The fact that dialogue is unskippable is a bit ridiculous.

Much of the time I only kept playing because I wanted to advance the story, not because I was having fun. The question is, are you the type of player who would feel bad for failing so much, or feel challenged to continue trying? I think there is a disconnect between presentation and gameplay. To me, the game initially presented itself as a casual story progression game, and I went in expecting such. What I actually encountered was a decent story gated behind a lot of cheap game-overs. I don't think many people would watch the game trailer on the store page and think "insane difficulty."

There are specific rooms that seem to be "at peace," but if you stand there too long, it's an instant game-over. There is no way to predict this, and one occurs after a boss fight, which in my opinion is unforgivable. Many times I was rolling my eyes at the latest death, opening a video to watch on the side as I re-loaded saves and completed the tedium of a room. I found myself mentally disengaged because being forced to do something over and over like that was boring. This was the case with many areas, but I have to specifically call out the "tail chase" and "squid dogs" areas for being exceptionally bad RNG experiences. For the tail chase, you need the tail to randomly path a certain way to delay itself rather than run straight toward you as you navigate obstacles. There are also "shadow" enemies that can RNG themselves into your only escape path, while the tail is in pursuit, resulting in game-over. For the squid dogs, after dipping your toes into the water about ten to twenty times to lure them to one area, you hope RNG keeps them in that area, and does not path them in a way that prevents your clean sprint to safety.

Furthermore, there are in-game areas that suffer from limitations of the RPG Maker game engine. For example, puddles of latex on the floor will transform the player if stepped on, resulting in a game over, and they look like they cover about three game tiles. You learn quickly that to step on a puddle means game-over. However, there are areas where it is necessary to step on them, leading to confusion as to how to advance. As it turns out, the left and right edges do not result in a game-over; only the center of the puddle has any "collision" with the player. From a story standpoint, that makes no sense, because any contact with the latex should result in transformation and thus game over. I was stuck on at least one room for a time due to this confusing inconsistency. This same problem applies to the latex pillars, where the player's character model will actually clip through the pillar but not result in a game over, and it is again necessary to do this (e.g., white latex nest, lion chase sequence).

Ultimately, I think the story does not justify the difficulty. I recommend watching a commentary-free longplay if available, and probably even skipping through it to get to the story parts.

Oh, and take it from me, it's totally a fetish game.\t\t\t\t\t
