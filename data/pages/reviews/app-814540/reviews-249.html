\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged is a game that I would say is both horrifying and relatable at the same time. Allow me to explain:

1. Story - The game takes place in a time where a global pandemic has eradicated most of intelligent life and only few remain. You are the last of the captured test subjects that were to be used for trial test for the "vaccine" for this deadly virus known only as the "pale" virus. Escape the research facility and survive.

2. Gameplay - Most of the game is centered around light puzzle solving and your ability to quickly navigate the tightly packed rooms to avoid being "changed" into the anthropomorphic creature that are hell-bent on capturing you. Most puzzles consist of pushing boxes around to clear a path and others will have you look at your surroundings to find hints to certain roadblocks. Unfortunately, this game's AI is incredibly quick in both speed and smart. To which case, you will need to make near perfect button presses to beat certain levels. So be prepared to press F12 more times than you would like. But you will be able to persevere and beat the level in due time.

3. Music - The music found in Changed is delightfully retro. Giving off the 8-bit vibes that reminds me of both the NES and some soundtracks from UnderTale. Shizi is the wonderful artist that is responsible for all the great songs that accurately complement the situations that they are needed the most. And other times, the sound is completely absent, only leaving a few sound FX to drive home the sense of dread in certain areas. For me personally, I wish that the sound FX were more 8-bit like the music, instead of being lifted straight from a typical RPG builder pack.

Regardless, the sound design is passable in my opinion.

4. Aesthetics - The artwork for the levels and characters are a delight to interact with as I played through this game. A style that reminds me of sprites made in MS Paint, but done professionally. Navigating the awkward level design left me a bit upset when it caused me to get captured and die as a result. This was only a problem in both the Library (when being chased by the gigantic tail) and the Ventilation room (when being chased by one of the leopard beasts).

5. Conclusion - To wrap things up, Changed is a game that is challenging and intense in both setting and character dialogue. At times, I felt depressed and saddened by the end of my first journey in how I identify with Puro in that I wasn't like most of the furry community, that I felt like an outcast for who I was.

My first run was on normal difficulty since I was warned that I wouldn't be able to get past the "dark decision" if I played on easy difficulty. Even then, I still was met with the bad ending even though I made sure to thank Puro (both at the Library and in the room right before the water level), sit next to him to comfort him, and wake him up in the water level. I was then criticized for not being nice enough to Puro throughout my adventure with him. I felt cheated and robbed of the other possible ending I would have experienced, but did not because of a possible oversight that is with the game's coding. I was then told to START OVER and "be a better friend". Hopefully this gets fixed in a future update.

As of the writing of this review, a special edition is available for early access to those who bought the base game. This version of this game MUST be played after completing the original game for plot reasons since the special version will spoil most information on the first level. This version also serves as a remaster of the original game with quality of life changes like: easier AI, levels are now easier to navigate with clearly marked floors and better save points, new rooms that weren't featured in the original, and a WAY better narrative fleshing out all of Changed's cast. Currently, you are only able to progress up to Puro's hidden room due to the current build of the special edition.

If you enjoyed this game like I did, head over to Dragonsnow's patreon and consider supporting him there.\t\t\t\t\t
