\r\n\t\t\t\t\tChanged is certainly... unique, to say the least. Bizarre is a much better term for it.

This is one of those games where I regret ever buying it, but the amount of perception and knowledge I gained as a result experiencing it is undeniably useful and has changed me as a person. The thinly veiled (or in most cases for this game glaringly obvious) fetishy content is discomforting to say the least. While I myself am willing to continue on despite discomfort or dislike, playing through certain areas was challenging (disgust inducing), and despite becoming rather desensitized to it all after a while some areas still have that effect. The common comparison "Furry Fetish: The game" is not out of place in regards to this game, but while that itself is a large focus point of the game, you cannot deny the other focus points of the game what is due.

The puzzles: are frequently quite challenging, the chases as well, but this is more from the way that the enemies behave rather than raw difficulty in a lot of cases. I'm not much of a puzzle person myself, but I was still able to get through these puzzles at a moderate pace.

The world/universe: is a lot more interesting than many give credit for. The bits and pieces of scattered lore tell a story that is a nice punch to the gut to read through. Which brings us to my next focus point.

The story: Well, if you aren't playing the fetish game for the fetishes, then you're probably here for this. Jokes and jabs aside, the story is a very large part of the game, and helps explain to you just what kind of universe you are in, and what your situation is. The story brings up all sorts of moral dilemmas, and invokes thoughts and feelings of just what exactly defines humanity, along with what is more import; The greater good? Or freedom, escape. Personal, objectively selfish desires. It's quite sobering to think about, and some of the endings cause some heartache. When the antagonist isn't so evil, and is looking out for the best interests of the whole world, it really makes you think.

Flaws:

While the game is okay enough, a lot of the AI is very RNG based, which means that for no reason at all, an enemy chase could be a breeze to walk through, while another chase scene could take 2 hours of retrying to finally get through. The enemies have a habit of breaking off in random directions for no reason, while other times they straight up lock on and drop kick you like a Cloaker.

My other gripe is that the controls are sort of... off, (Probably due to it being made in rpgmaker tbh) Now, I can't exactly explain what about them is wrong, but sometimes the controls will be very laggy or delayed for no reason at all, and in some cases, like chase scenes or puzzles that require vert specific and precise movement, they controls will completely stop responding, or make you continue to run in a certain direction without registering the other key you are pressing. Movement is a tad on the clunky side to put it simply, and sometimes is very broken feeling.

Final thoughts/Conclusions

Do I regret purchasing this game? I'm still not entirely sure. As I stated before, yes, I regret purchasing and owning this game, much less having played the game(34 hours I'll never get back), but I cannot deny how the experiences that resulted from this game given me a deeper understanding of things. For better or for worse I guess. For 6$, It's not the worst game, it's about 4 hours if you're good at it, more if you suck ass at puzzles and precise movements (the rpgmaker engine isn't helping the movement any).

Still though, I find myself wondering. Do I regret getting this game? There is no simple answer, aside from saying that I still don't know.

My advice? Leave this place. This is not something that you wish gain intimate knowledge of. Ignore this game, save your money for something more meaningful, and just go.
Yet even with these words of warning, the dreadfully curious will still carry on.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is fantastic, lore rich and simply .. well made

Alot of love has been put into this game, from the plot, character personalities, Transfur animations, artwork and chase/fight sequences,while yes the game is rough around the edges but its designed to be very difficult and i loved that.

Pretty sure this is one of the few games that i purposely died in many times just to appreciate the artwork alone, it is amazing to the core, despite it being an 8bit game, you dont need AAA graphics to make a masterpiece.

The game's environment is rich in lore and purely fascinating, it is HIGHLY interactable, this is one of the very few games that i was super engaged into it's lore because of how mysterious it is to the point where as i was playing i was theorizing to myself on what the the latex creatures are, how they feel like, how hosts feel in transfurmations all based on hints given throughout the world through notes, visuals, conversations with Puro, i've even went to investigate frame by frame the transfurs in the game to see how the body changes and its just phenomenal, so i've kept a close eye on the texts and visuals in this game DETAILS TELL ALOT.

This is one of the best games i've ever played in my life and by far one of my favorites.

Now.. the game isnt pristine, as i've said its rough around the edges, one frustrating thing i've gone through is how some sequence areas are reached after ALOT of conversation between the world characters and sometimes just the distance to reach SOME sequences after retrying, which leads to long wait times until you get to try and learn a chase/fight sequence, i wish they implemented a "skip all" option to players who have seen conversations before.

The other problem i had with the game is the movement, while yes its made by RPG maker but at times its just horrible, i on many occasions walked into enemies and obstacles unwillingly because of how "draggy" and "tanky" the movement is, in other words your movement isnt altered until your player passes one tile in the world which lead me to overshoot and undershoot movement, especially in high precision sequences like the vent chase, the two water level boss fights, white latex nests with the pillars, and the "exploding crystals" in the dark latex nest or walking into crystals accidentally because of the movement, however it didnt shoo me off the game, i kept trying and it felt rewarding winning a sequence after learning the patterns and outplaying or juking enemies.

OVERALL, its an amazing game that will have you hooked hard into it, wanting to search every room for every interactable object and getting to see the amazingly rich text included in the Transfur screens, and to witness every small detail in screens or just conversations that help the world and story building.

I give it an 8/10, the game is amazing but the gameplay if improved would make this a 10/10 from me\t\t\t\t\t
