\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs of now I have achieved 100% in this game. For the most part I liked the game, the story telling and the characters were good. While most games try to push really hard for you to like one of the side characters in their story to the point where it backfires and you end up hating them, I didn't get that effect with the character(s) they were pushing for in this game. It's artwork (mainly sprites and still images) are good for the aesthetics it was going for, paired with the 8 bit themed soundtrack and music which for what it is was done pretty well. This game is a "puzzle" game, while I found none of the puzzles in this game to be actually hard what I did find to be hard was the terrible controls of the game. Inputs appear to be buffered but will randomly get eaten and fall into the void which wouldn't be a massive issue if the game wasn't based off of 1 hit deaths (fail states) in this game. \r\n\t\t\t\t\ttl;dr: Interesting concept, terrible execution.

To begin with this game launched in a tiny box that was about 3 inches square. When I tried to access the menu on it the whole thing was written in Japanses. After googlein and digging through a few forums I figured out that you can enter fullscreen by hitting alt+enter, and was able to start the game.

You start off in a room, and a monster that hasn't even been introduced can come "kill" you while you're stuck in a unskippable text sequence. After you figure out how to defeat that monster (which was rather unintuitive, I just happened to do it by mistake) you are released into another room, which contains a door with japanese text on it (I found this door while being chased by another monster). After you escape from that room you are asked to outrun another monster that moves about twice as fast as you, and has a headstart. After a few fruitless minutes of that I googled the answer and apparently it exhausts itself after chassing you for a bit, and the returns to mach 3 a second later. This was never actually communicated to the player, and coulda taken me ages to figure out on my own. At this point I decided to refund the game beacuse in 30 minutes I had run into enough unexplained, unintuitive, and unavoidable brick walls.

Oh, and also song on the sound track is ~30 seconds long, and just loops after half a second of silence.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWow, when I saw this appear in my recommended I thought it was some weird furry trash, but I was surprised of how quality the game was. I picked the game up because of sheer curiousity and I do not regret my purchase. I've never hated furries so it didn't take me long to get used to the transformations, I even found some of them kinda cute. The story is very fleshed out and gives all the characters a reason. This game has great storytelling and if there ever is a sequel I'll be excited how the story would continue. The art is also really good. While some of the transformation scenes are... questionable, most of them at the very least not hard to look at. I even have one of the (currently as of writing this) characters as my profile pic. One problem I do have is the controls. For a game about outsmarting enemy AI, it doesn't do a great job in the control department. For the most part it's fine but the human's movement is stiff and unresponsive. Also this game has puzzles... and they suck. When you're not escaping enemies, you're solving puzzles. The puzzles are very cryptic and (I'm not joking) near impossible to figure out, but the parts where you outsmart enemies are enjoyable and difficult. I still recommend a guide for the puzzles. And the translations... you just got to see them to believe them.

All in all, this is a great game. I had a lot of fun getting through it. This game could've been a dumb flash game on Newgrounds, but no it's great quality. The game has problems but not servere enough to the point of ripping your hair out. It doesn't matter if you're a furry or not, I think this is a game anyone can enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t
