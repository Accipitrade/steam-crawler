\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, so this game is absolutely amazing! Like, it's fun, it has catchy music, it has a good story, good characters, good transformations x3 I first played through it with one of my good friends before doing a solo playthrough, and I just simply love Changed so much!!

You play as a handsome 21 year old guy named Colin (Though this will only be known from outside sources) and you have to basically try and escape this tall building without getting.. well.. changed! Latex styled furry creatures will try and change you, and you have to avoid them all on your way of discovering what happened in the world and see if you can meet anyone still around!

The furry creatures are cute yet intimidating enough to ensure a good balance of the serious lore of the game and the comical, whimsy and transformation based moments.

About the gameplay, while the movement is tile based, to the dismay of some, is still perfectly enjoyable, giving you plenty of save points to use along the way to go again and again at the tricky bits. Just make sure you don't mind a bit of repetition before you play. As some sections will try your patience!

The graphics are very cool aswell! Everything is designed so you can tell what it is, while being toony and plenty colourful, even with all the white due to the lab themes, it is still no issue seeing the wobbly latex creatures move about trying to get ya aswell!

I'd reccomend for anyone looking for a unique experience, furries will naturally love this i'm sure, and even a non fur could enjoy it, if you don't let the theme put you off any! Could also reccomend to anyone who, you know, has a thing for transformation!

Colin is best cute determined guy <3 Puro if the absolute best boy <3 And K is best scientist wolfy <3 some of my all time favourite characters! And sexy ones at that~

I'm sorry about that x3; that concludes this report!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will start this by stating that Changed is in no way a perfectly balanced game in terms of difficulty by American standards, but by Asian standards it delivers as expected. What this translates to is that the game is very strict when it comes to how much time the player has to succeed at a given task. Enemies are usually one or two steps behind, and everything kills you in one hit by design.
— This game doesn't mess around in terms of gameplay difficulty.

The controls are somewhat clunky. During my time playing it, I experienced many frustrating instances where the controls did not respond as I would have expected in a more polished game, but since this is the developer's first foray into game development, we shouldn't be expecting a great deal of polish in the control section.
— The game's control are unresponcive, but the player can adjust to the controls.

The story is fairly decent even if most of it is in the background. The supporting cast tend to speak quite a bit, which if you like lore and characters like I do, isn't a problem, and tend to flesh out the universe's rules. Much of the story is in interactable items such as books, computers, and other items in the universe. In addition to this, many characters can be spoken with and have decently sized dialogue trees. While the translation isn't the best in the world, it does get the point across.
— Overall, the story is well written and presented.

The characters in particular are quite fun. I won't say much beyond that due to spoilers, but they are decently written and have motivations that drive their actions. The emphasis with one of the characters is on building a friendship with him over the course of the game. If that isn't your thing, then you likely wont enjoy yourself.
— The game's characters are well presented.

The audience for this game is understandably niche: it is a furry game afterall. It is better if you go in not knowing anything about the game and explore it for yourself. However, if you find involuntary transformation and latex creatures particularly repugnant, you may not want to buy this game. There are plenty of Let's Plays on youtube, and I suggest you at least look at the first episode of any of those to see if this game is something you'll be interested in.
— It is a good game, but it isn't for everyone, and I suspect that those whom enjoy submission will get more mileage out of this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI HAVE NO WORDS, PLAY THIS RIGHT NOW
This game is awesome! One of the best singleplayer games I've ever played. It may look all furry, kinky, NSFW and stuff, but that's just judging a book by its cover! It's actually a really good game even if you're not into the furry fandom in fact. If you enjoyed games like Undertale, One Shot... this game is 100% for you.

First point: the story. It's really simple, but that doesn't mean it's a bad story. The narration is great and easy to understand, and the game simply says what it has to say without overcomplicating things. Kids would love this game as much as adults. So the story and narration is a 9/10 for me.

Next: the artstyle. This is 100% subjective, so feel free to skip this point if you want. I personally like the way the art and sprites of the game are drawn. Keep in mind this was made by one single guy (apart from a music composer) and this was the best way to get it all done without wasting too much time and efford. But it's really easy to like the artstyle. If you ever readed Homestuck or any Andrew Hussie's webcomics, this game is pretty similar: it tries to imitate Microsoft Paint. Just since I like that style so much I want to give it a 10/10 but to be fair there are games that do it better like Ori and the Blind Forest. So objectively it's like a 6 or 7/10, but remember it's not mandatory for a game to look great if you want it to be good. Just like many modern 3D games that look awesome but are actually pretty meh.

Characters: This is totally a 10/10. The enemies are just so memorable and unique you will be able to distinguish them without paying barely any attention. And I'll tell you there are A LOT. Not many games manage that.
Then there's the allies. I won't reveal much, but I really liked the fact this game didn't include many friendly NPCs. This way, you don't get too confused trying to remember who's who, who knows who, who hates who, who knows what, who does what... and the best part is it works! Undertale was a game that often included many annoying characters who were not relevant to anything, but this game didn't make that mistake. And I really liked these characters, might be the best I've seen in a while. Maybe better than Undertale's.

Gameplay: Really good! Not very original, but it doesn't have to be. It plays like any regular RPG without a combat mechanic. You have to figure out how every enemy works in order to find their flaws. You can't kill them, so you'll have to run. They become pretty easy once you know what you're doing, but it takes a slow crawl to get there. This trial and error system is similar to many old games, in order to keep playing you have to figure out everything without much help. This is not a bad thing but you may get annoyed by this if you don't like hard games (that is not the case for me, Dark Souls is one of my favourite games).
So yeah, gameplay is a 7/10. It's alright. You won't hate it, you'll like it but you won't love it.

Music: Music is an important part of any videogame. This soundtrack was made by a friend of the game developer and it's not very original, just some generic chiptune ambience. You can clearly see the guy's got talent, but maybe due to lack of experience or something like that the songs just... meh, they are OK. Could be way better, not memorable at all. But they fit, which is important.
5/10. Maybe you'll like it, that's subjective.

TL;DR: This is Undertale with better characters, a simpler plot (and maybe a better one too), OK music, hard dificulty and many, many memorable moments. If you have a busy life and can't play games normally play this one! It's very good! No matter if you're a kid or an adult. Let your child play this game! Let your girlfriend play this game! Let your grandma play this WORK OF ART!

Also, prepare to die like a lot.\t\t\t\t\t
