Review of: Baldur's Gate: Enhanced Edition Official Soundtrack

Well, it’s the official soundtrack for Baldur's Gate: Enhanced Edition, simple as that. The whole album consists of forty tracks; thirty-three of them were composed by Michael Hoenig as a part of the original game and the remaining seven by Sam Hulick for the remastered version. Honestly, I like the musical score for Baldur’s Gate because all tracks are atmospheric and blend in really well. Both gentlemen delivered the finest pieces of music.

Anyway, if you’re a soundtrack collector or simply like it, I’d suggest getting this DLC during a sale, especially as a part of the Infinity Collection bundle, because it’s definitely too expensive at the full price.

Soundtrack-Review
Baldurs Gate soundtrack was always something that stood out to me. Not because of the great action filled battle themes, actually those are the more worse parts. But because of the fantastic atmospheric exploring tracks.

Sound
The soundtrack itself nothing you would hear besides in the game. Except you are doing pen and paper or plan to do mods with them to include this. Well the whole tracklist is perfectly made for a pen and paper round as many of the atmospheric tracks are not too distracting to be able to listen to and play a round.

Now personally i think its atrocious to ask for 8 € which is basically half of the games asking price. The original author itself probably doesnt even see a penny of this and i doubt many will buy this. I rather wished they would have excluded the OST from the anthology and made it into a seperate pack as the anthology would have been otherwise so cheap that anyone would have bought it.

Conclusion/TL;DR
As much as i loved the atmospheric soundtrack in baldurs gate i dont really see a reason to buy this for this incredible asking price. The price might have been justified if they would have given the original author some cut of the sales.\t\t\t\t\t
