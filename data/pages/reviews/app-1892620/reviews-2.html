\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was enticed by the positive user reviews and bought this game, but to my disappointment, CEO is actually like a baby version of Capitalism that I doubt will ever grow up.

The most apparent limitation is that there can only be a maximum of 6 AI companies in a game. In Capitalism Lab, you could have as many as 40 AI companies and up to 58 by using a script for advanced players.

In Capitalism Lab, you could get a real sense of running a multinational company competing against a slew of AI competitors with diverse strategies focused on retail, production, real estate, media, stock investment and tech.

In CEO, you have none of these as it doesn't even allow you to set up media companies and the real estate simulation is extremely bare bone with no abilities to buy land plots or build apartments.

Other key components that are essential to a realistic simulation including city public buildings, inflation and macroeconomic cycles are also sorely missing.

In a nutshell, CEO is a much reduced version of Capitalism falling short in the core economic simulation with a severely limiting AI competition that offers no long-term replayability. If you want the best business sim game, get Capitalism Lab - the latest game from the original series, not this lackluster copy of it!

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCEO is basically the spiritual successor to Capitalism 2. If you have any interest in management, business, or tycoon games you should buy this. This game is an absolute steal even at full price considering what is on offer here.

If you're interested in getting a holistic understanding of business concepts like market share, margins, supply chains, etc. and how these fit together I can't think of a better game to simulate and teach you the fundamentals.

I've played Capitalism 2 and Capitalism Lab. Those are both great games, but the main reason why Capitalism Lab is good is because it's essentially just Capitalism 2 with some minor changes. The biggest problem I had with Capitalism Lab is that it's really expensive for something that didn't even improve on the graphics and still has some of the basic limitations of the original game.

CEO does everything that Capitalism 2 did, but it actually improves on the formula. Better graphics, a better interface, really intuitive tutorials, and more products and nuanced gameplay.

The only criticism I can offer is that the developer needs to do more work to get the word out about this game. I am a huge Capitalism 2 fan and I only realized this existed a month after release by using the interactive recommender on Steam. Once the dev feels the game is ready, he should focus more effort on marketing. Basically get some YouTubers to promote the game on their channels, post on any threads that may still be discussing the old capitalism games, etc.

This is a hidden gem on Steam, and I hope it gets the attention it deserves.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf asked whether this, an economic simulator, is better at what it does than the Capitalism series, especially Capitalism Lab then the answer is no. It is much simpler and offers only a skin-deep level of insight into the market. So, for veterans of the genre, I will not sing the praises of this, as the use is limited to offering something slightly different to try out for once.

That said, this game does what the others of the genre struggle to; it is accesible to newcomers.

It has enough content for a good bit of gameplay, the learning curve is quite low, and the interface is intuitive and simple. For people interested in this type of game but not wanting to be thrown into the deep end, I recommend this as a good starter.

Of course, this is early access, and everything is subject to change. Hopefully for the better, but who knows.\t\t\t\t\t
