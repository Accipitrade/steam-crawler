\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCapitalism 3 some say like it's a bad thing. Capitalism 3 ! I've been waiting...
For twenty years or more I would, every now and then nostalgically do a Google search for 'games like Capitalism II' and here it is! Whoop whoop. I bought it straightaway and don't regret it. CEO is terrific. But yes, there are some glitches as one would expect with the mechanics of something this complicated. Developers listen up; I'm playing head to head with my bud and we set the game to 1 region, no ports, 10 million start, with six AI competitors. It was thrilling. Many games were played with each of us getting the trophy. At that difficulty level, in that scenario, we had to start with a farm and sell meat and try for the pet food or timber or textiles etc, until I learned how to manipulate my stock. Here's how it's done: I build a small farm and medium store and get some income so the bank likes me. Then I borrow as much as possible regardless of the rate and buy up my stock while its cheap. Then I issue twenty thousand shares (only) and instead of my stock going down as one might expect in the real world, this game jumps it up! My share price goes from $24 to $32 the same day, and when I do it again with 20K more shares, it goes to to $48 and then to $56 and then to $80 and then i sell as much as possible at the top. I can raise about $50 million. THEN THE REALLY INTERESTING THING HAPPENS - there's a 50/50 chance the stock will crash to $.10 cents allowing you / competitors to buy it all up again for a few thousand bucks. So, as fun as something like this is to discover and prove, it ruined the game with my friend because now instead of enjoying CEO we are competing in a sad variation called 'Stock Fraud'. And worse - we can't even cheat equally. Just I'm playing Stock Fraud it seems because my pal claims to be unable to replicate my process. So there's that. But otherwise the game is fantastic!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to say that I wasn't a big fan of the capitalism games before I bought the game "CEO", although I remember playing them a long time ago.

Some people criticized this game for being a copy of Capitalism 2, yet I bought the latter to test it thinking it would be great and I didn't like it at all, the graphics were from another time and the gameplay as well as the choice of products was too limited.

In terms of graphics and gameplay, I really enjoyed playing CEO. The tutorials are very well done and allow us to understand the mechanics of the game to start our game.

The game offers a lot of raw material and it is quite possible that in the future new products will appear and if the developer (who works alone on the game) agrees to add a Steam Workshop feature then we will be entitled to many mods created by the community!

Well, it's true that the game needs to be completed on some points like a help to find more easily the possible combinations to create such or such products.
However, you have to know that the game is in Early Access and therefore, it is normal that it lacks some finishing touches especially since this game was created by a solo developer!

Anyway, I loved playing this game as it is and I'm really looking forward to seeing the improvements that will be made to the title once it comes out of its Early Access state.
Thank you for taking the time to read my review and I hope it helps you make your choice. In the worst case, you can always get your money back but I think it's a shame to miss out on this gem because there aren't many games of this kind...\t\t\t\t\t
