i didn't notice it at first and bought it thinking it was the collection, but that version isn't available on steam for some reason? i really just wanted to play 1602 again

anyway, after installing the Uplay launcher, you are greeted to a city builder with great depth and a focus on diplomacy and economy. it has a steep learning curve with little tutorial but feels very rewarding after learning it on your own. at least if there was any i wasn't sure where to find it.

you may go in wanting to conquer the world with warships, but i almost guarantee you will stick to trading. it feels like actual war and combat make up only 1-5% of the main gameplay. not that it wouldn't be fun, but there's many ways to win a game and diplomacy and learning trade is where the real struggle is at. out of the nearly 20 hours of gameplay so far, i've mostly stuck to building small trading villages and just trying to learn all the mechanics.

if you like deep, rewarding, city-building games with high difficulty and management systems, then this game is pretty awesome
