\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, you're looking at another Anno game. You're thinking, "Gosh I just want to run an industry and sell goods and manage a little economy and advance technology and enjoy a diet coke level of combat."

Yeah this Anno isn't really what you're looking for. Go play Anno 2070, its a better experience.

Anno 1404 first, is glitchy as hell. Ubisoft will suggest reinstalling the Uplay launcher, reinstalling the game, and dangerously modifying your Operating System. The game just wont launch half the time. I've found the only way to launch the game is to try to launch it from uplay. it will fail. then try to launch it from steam. it will fail. then restart uplay, completely close steam(yes, I mean open up your application manager and close steam.) then reopen uplay. Launch it in offline mode. IF uplay lets you run in offline mode and you have closed steam and you have tried to launch Anno1404 once and closed it, it seems to let you run Uplay in offline mode and play the game you purchased.

The game itself. It's exactly anno 2070 but less. Graphics are essentially the same. There is only two factions to build from which is okay but its kind of bizarre how one faction has most of the stuff and the other faction just kind of hooks in here and there to make one complete supply tree.

The combat in the game is kind of... muted? There's combat, but its not really clear which vessel is a war vessel and which isn't. Like my Oriental Warship had no combat ability? Might be another glitch though. It's a seriously glitchy game.

Resources are essentially infinite. You just pay to replenish a resource well. Theres also a Noria fertilization thing but its just another money sucker. It makes desert area farmable but requires money to replenish and its just kind of meh.

The User interface was also just, not very intuitive. Like to fast forward you have to open a special menu that plasters itself across the middle of the screen. But the menu can crash the game for one, but also is just intrusive for some essential features.

Look, at the end of the day, Anno 1404 is closer to a mobile game than a true Anno Sequel. It's Ubisoft, you know you're getting screwed somewhere, but with Anno 1404 its too much to deal with.

If you are considering buying this game, just please take my advice and go play Anno 2070. Its less glitchy, it has more content, and frankly, better graphics.

I'll be requesting a refund but I think I waited too long. I really thought I was just missing something but I played for 17 hours in 3 days and its just Anno 2070 with less stuff and more glitches.\t\t\t\t\t
