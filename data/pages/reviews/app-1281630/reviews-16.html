The game will be adored by you


Do you like creating trading routes?
You will get it. You can create one ship and set up his route so that he takes you from one of your islands the wood to another island. After which he goes to the island(bot's island, which there are several, even while playing online, with other players) and sold him the rest of the wood that did not fit into the warehouse of the second island in the route. After that, this ship will buy tools from the bot, bring them to your main island, and so on in a circle.

Musical accompaniment
I just don't have the words. Most of all, my camera stayed over Venice (one of the bot islands), because my favorite music was turned on over this island.

But
Replay value nearing at... once a year you will want to play a new session. One more but - it's going to be one long session. All my playing time at the time of writing the review is one long session (not considering the first, short, game sessions while I was dealing with the game mechanics).

The main difference from Anno 1800
Of course, only in my opinion. I noticed one main difference from Anno 1800, which I played quite a bit - you don't have to leave a certain number of residents of each class. Because in Anno 1404 businesses do not require residents of a certain class. Therefore, you can safely improve all residents to the maximum available level and not think about anything superfluous. And I don't think this is a negative to the evaluation of the game.


My rating is 7 points out of 10
