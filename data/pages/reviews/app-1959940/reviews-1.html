Updated for April Patch
***
Finally got a chance to pick this game up again...
The addition of the explorer class vessels definitely cuts down the end game slog. Also pleasantly surprised how much better the addition of the small loops made the maps. I also enjoyed the addition of fog of war since it makes it easier to avoid unnecessary combats. Being able to pick up the loot pods while in combat was a big plus, especially for the five-wave combat instances, since I don't have to agonize about running out of fuel as long as I've got an extra movement point available. While there's still quite a bit of back and forth grind, the improvements are definitely chipping away at it.

I still feel that the game could benefit from some additional things to discover, and some additional story line. I still want to know who blew up my station in the first place, and maybe even track them down for a final boss fight/fleet battle maybe?

With all that being said, I'm changing my review to a recommend, although it's a very tentative recommend. I'm very curious to see what further developments are in store.

Original review below.
***

This game has a lot of unrealized potential and if development continues then I'd be happy to switch to a recommend, but in it's current state it's fun for a few hours and then it's just endless grind. I picked it up hoping for a 2-D "lite" version of Wing Commander: Privateer, and while I had fun initially the game ran out of content way too quickly. I started out playing the huge map with 500 systems and after ~10 hours I had completed the "story" missions, I had fully upgraded my ship, I had successfully cleared 10-15 combat instances, and I had only visited ~150 systems. Took another 20+ hours of grinding to fully explore all 500 and finish off 250 combat instances. No new enemies, no new quests, just the same 5 side missions available at every station, and random trash enemies slowing down the pace of the game. I then tried out a small map with only 200 systems and with my previous experience managed to fully explore and complete in about 10 hours.

*** PROS ***
- Graphics are pretty decent. No real issues telling different enemies, or inventory items apart.
- Crafting is well developed. Logical steps to get from base materials to advanced items.
- Early game has tension and excitement where random combats pose a genuine threat and require some thoughtful maneuvering to succeed.

*** CONS ***
- Not enough content to make late game exploration anything other than a slog.
- Combat instances are nigh impossible without a fully upgraded elite fighter by which point the regular random combats are just annoying wastes of time.
- Combat rewards. The only reasons to kill enemies: you don't have a good enough ship to avoid them, or you're running out of fuel. None of them drop weapons that aren't obsolete by the time you can kill the enemy that drops it. No loot-exclusive gear, anything they drop can be purchased or manufactured easily.
- Timer for fast travel is egregious. Having to wait 15 minutes (minimum) in real time to use fast travel makes it almost pointless to include it.
- Maps are very simple branch plots requiring a lot of back and forth through the same systems. No loops, or one-way wormholes, just a lot of dead-ends.
- No real economy to speak of. Minuscule variations in prices between stations make trading pointless.
- Variety is sorely lacking. If your core gameplay loop is centered around exploration then there should be new stuff to discover beyond the first 30 minutes of gameplay.

*** NEUTRALS ***
- Sound/Music. It's okay. nothing really great and I can mute the parts that get annoying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, I have so much cool stuff to say about this game.

Once, you have got used to the controls, and used to the mechanics, This game is incredibly enjoyable to play.

Their is so much satisfaction when you build up enough resources to upgrade your ship. Even if its just upgrading your Cargo bay.

The combat is simple, but really fun. And it surprised me how much actual tactics were involved in the more complex fights.

The graphics, might not be ground breaking, but they don't need to be.

The Combat instances can sometimes be brutal, but I love it.

This is one of those games where you can easily lose track of time, and realise you've suddenly spent 10 hours on it.

I really hope, there are going to be more Weapons, more ships and more stuff in future. Maybe even a tougher difficulty setting. But I certainly can't complain, this has a very good "Cost" to "hours of enjoyment" ratio. Its very hard to put down when you're in the middle of a campaign.

Quick summary:

I would thoroughly recommend this game if you are up for a decent fun challenge. It will keep you occupied for hours.\t\t\t\t\t
