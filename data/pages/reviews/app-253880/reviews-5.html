\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think this game has a lot of problems, but I'm still putting it as recommended simply because I don't know any other games that have this dynamic slow death of the world and also because the game itself is still very interesting and at least decently enjoyable if you can get past the fact it's just one big gather mission.

So I'm going to assume you already know the premise to the game. The world is doomed and you need to get off this damned rock. Lets jump right into gameplay. It's pretty normal RTS where you select units and you command them to move and attack. It's also got a lot of industry standard things like band boxing, double click selection, adding units, grouping, patrols, etc. What sets it apart is that the whole game is persistent. You have a main base (that you don't really interact with much) and you go to missions. The game feels pretty fluid in this regard and also helps with a BIG FACTOR in why I recommend the game. We'll talk about that later though.

So there are three corporations. UCS (United states I think?) ED and LC (Lunar corp). UCS has walker mechs, ED has tanks and LC has futuristic hover cars. I don't know why, but when I first played this game it was around the time Phantasy Star had released and somehow I sorta associated the LC with the Newman's from PSO and so like I got it in my head that the LC was a buncha all female space elf nuns from the moon. They don't seem to talk much like a normal human would and seem more innocent and have voices you'd expect from an elf from space or whatever.

I didn't even favor them at first because their vehicles crumble under any kinda pressure, but once I played the campaign I fell in love with them. Their adorably innocent voice lines were great! They really give the vibe that they don't know anything about war and it feels great to guide them to survival. I kinda wish the main character had been a space elf chick too, but I forgive the game because the idea of a strong male from a foreign corporation leading them to victory was probably a more appealing idea to the demograph at the time.

Anyway, now that I'm older I realize that they're most likely just nuns who went to the moon or whatever. Far less satisfying, but whatever. I mean can you blame me? They're a super advanced organization from the moon that lands their structures on the planet by flying them in. How could I not assume they're not humans and then just kinda lead myself by the nose to the elf conclusion? This is probably where I started to develop my weird fetish for elves alongside PSO. Kinda irrelevant to the game though so lets move on!

As far as combat goes, there is a nice assortment of weapons, though maybe a few more per faction or some overlap would have made it more enjoyable. Rockets, plasma cannons, artillery, standard cannons. And as you research more your weapons upgrade both in looks and functionality. As an example, you can upgrade your cannon from one to two, then upgrade the ammo it uses to hit harder. Really I've never been a fan of upgrades that you can't visibly see an effect and this game really solves that. I'm also cool with upgrades like command and conquer red alert 2 where unit veterans get cooler weapons that do more damage. The point I'm getting is that as you upgrade and research you will see and feel the difference in your playstyles. Though once you have the game down you'll start to see ideal ways to play (example being to make a lot of rocket units to shell a base).

Really the combat is solid, but starts to get a little dull in the later missions and also some playstyles get favored a lot more later too. Like shelling a base with rockets is almost 100% neccessary because of some defensive emplacements. Fortunately the enemy doesn't have infinite money so you'll eventually win if you can endure.

Endurance is really the big feature of this game. And when I say that, I say so in a derogatory fashion. This game's pacing is horrible. Most missions consist of going to the mission zone, unloading your units, destroyed enemy bases and then sucking the land dry of resources. Because the end goal of the game really is to get as many resources as you can. You will win once you've mined 'X' amount of resources and delivered them to your colony ship in space. I like the way this feels in regards to the end goal being reachable at any time if it wasn't gated behind limited mission resources. I feel like if someone took this inspiration they could make a MUCH better game. Probably make a great RTS game with procedural generation and rogue-lite elements.

Moving on. Some of the music in this game is great, and some of it is just meh. Sound effects are good, but the voice overs can get a tad bit repetetive. I DO like the LC voices a lot. Something about their adorable innocent voices makes me happy. Then sad when they plead for help and then die. Also the fuzzy sound of 'low hp' units is neat. I don't know any other games that do that. Another feature that games should consider adding.

The environments, for their time, were pretty good too. Now the graphics can be an eyesore if you're not someone like me who can actually tolerate mediocre 3D and appreciate old school ♥♥♥♥♥♥ graphics. In conjunction with this one song the artic snowstorms are FANTASTIC feeling. Unfortunately they don't last.

Speaking of, lets talk about the whole timed campaign thing. Yeah you have a time limit to complete the campaign. For some this is gonna be a big turn off. For me I see something AMAZING marred by bad pacing. To give an example, your main base (for the lunar corporation at least) starts off in snow riddled mountains with snowstorms and such. As time passes, the snow fades to tundra, then to temperate with snow patches. Gradually it becomes temperate and slowly the tress die and the grass vanishes and it keeps getting worse and worse. This alone makes me recommend this game simply because of how amazingly fitting it is to the theme. And while games do sometimes have 'seasons' it's not quite the same.

Anyway, this game isn't amazing or anything, but I feel like it's a must have for anyone who collects and enjoys unique and different games.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDespite being old, this game can easily even compete with the most modern RTS games. Here's why.

-The mechanics are easy to learn and difficoult to master
-You can use 3 inscreens to play. Have one eye on your base, one on the battlefield and one at a strategic important point.
-You play above the ground and underground. Dig a tunnel behind the enemy and attack from there.
-You can build bridges and tunnels everywhere where it fits.
-You can combine all sorts of units with all sorts of weapons. A tank with lasers, rocket launchers and a tank cannon? No problem!
-Map editor with much stuff, you can even create multiple maps and link them together so you have warfare on multiple maps at the same time!
-AI fastly responds to incoming activities, also your own units tell you what's going on
-Wrecks of units and buildings do not vanish like in newer games. They remain until they are destroyed manually (which can be fun)
-Ammo consumption on units, make sure your guns can fire all the time
-You can shield and arm up buildings so you don't have to focus so much on leaving defensive units in your base
-3 fractions that are all quite different to play
-multiple interfaces to choose from and to fit into your playstyle, (don't) need a map or settings tab for units? One click and it pops up or down
-Dynamic weather such as sun, fog, rain and even snow that slowly starts to cover your buildings and not moving ground units
-Active day and night circle
-Upgrades do not affect units, either you produce units with the new tech or still fight with old tech
-Still a good graphic for it's age
