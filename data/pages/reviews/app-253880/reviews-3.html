\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt the time of its release, Earth 2150 has been one of the most revolutionary RTS games on the market. Even today, the level of complexity is unparalleled, while remaining an absolute joy to play.

Firstly, as with all the old games (and this one is nearly 20-years old), caution must be applied as to running it. Setting it up might take some time, not all resolutions are working and the game might refuse to work on some machines. That's what the refund button is for - but if you get it running, you won't be disappointed.

Earth 2150 features:
Unit customization from ground up. Choose the chassis and weapons, each combination is effective against different units.
Land units, planes, boats, hovercrafts.
Multiple layers of combat. Ammunition management, unit experience, various types of damage and defenses.
Land terraforming. Almost any part of the map can be raised or lowered. Bridges can be built. Maps feature an underground layer as well.
Different weather conditions. Night and day cycle that drastically affects gameplay.
Most importantly, clarity - this is not a game based on spreadsheets, most of these features are easy to understand and use.

Honestly, this game deserves a remake.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSHORT VERSION: You wanna play a nice, long and unique campaign? Play the base game of Earth 2150. Wanna play a decent set of expansion missions? Play the Moon Project. Wanna play the most optimized and content-complete version of the skirmish/multiplayer mode? Play The Lost Souls. Just be aware of the campaign on this last one. It was clearly designed for veteran players and will be hell for newbies to get acquainted with.




LONG VERSION: Earth 2150 and its Moon Project expansion were one of my most favorite RTS titles in the early 2000s - I must've played 100+ hours of this sucker, along with crafting my own maps. Never even had the chance to play it online, yet I still absolutely loved it. Because I never picked up The Lost Souls Expansion (most reviews back then said it was just a mission pack with little else... not exactly correct), I decided to grab the Earth 2150 Trilogy pack when it was on sale for the sake of nostalgia. Yeah, about that... turns out they actually did add in a couple of new units, along with rebalancing the factions. The base game is missing many features, such as a greater camera zoom-out distance, better compatibility with modern hardware, and more multiplayer features, including the ability to assign teams to the AI (in the original, the AI never attacks one another, only the player, even in FFA mode).


What makes this franchise unique is how you can research and design your own units with a variety of weaponry, each unique to their respective faction. For example, the ED Khrushchev heavy Tank can carry one main heavy weapon, a heavy energy shield, and one small weapon attached to the top of the main weapon. Said main weapon can either be a twin 120mm tank cannons, a set of lasers which cause enemy units and structures to overheat and explode, a large ion cannon that disables its targets, leaving them vulnerable to capture or destruction, a heavy rocket launcher which can target both ground and air units, or a ballistic missile launcher, which can be upgraded to fire tactical nukes. Some units are capable of carrying even more upgrades and weaponry, and be upgraded via research. Oh, and your units do have limited ammo: thus, you'll have to build supply transports and depots to prevent your units from running out.


An apt comparison would be the Warzone 2100 games, only this time you have more options than only choosing propulsion, chassis and turret.


Each faction has their own little gimmick: the ED (Eurasian Dynasty) primarily uses sturdy tanks along with a few amphibious units, and their weaponry mostly resembles modern day arsenals. The most sci-fi weapon in their arsenal is arguably the Ion Cannon, but it's essentially just an EMP weapon with a different name. The UCS (United Civilized States) uses mechs and a handful of tracked vehicles, with their Plasma cannons being well-rounded high-damage energy weapons, at the cost of many of their earlier chassis lacking shields or armor. The LCS (Lunar Corporation Society) exclusively use anti-grav tanks and have regenerating health, at the cost of less armor and overall HP.


Do expect to encounter a variety of graphical and engine bugs due to the game's age, especially when watching the video cinematics (which often simply refuse to play) or trying to fiddle around with the screen resolution. 1080p causes some hiccups, and the game will randomly crash on occasion so please do remember to manually save every now and then. Again, the base game is the most difficult to get running; you’ll probably have to look online for advice on how to keep it stable.


Sadly, it's a real shame nobody's tried rebooting or remastering this franchise. Lots of potential being wasted by leaving it to wither away, untouched.\t\t\t\t\t
