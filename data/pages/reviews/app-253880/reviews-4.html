\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst thing i would like to say is that this game is old..soo for those who cant deal with or tell units apart with old graphics this game might not be for you....

That being said this game has wonderful challanging missions....and for some people since this is a no hand holding RTS...will mean that they will call it "boring"or "bad". I personally love this game since with the constant base there is an actuall connection between one mission and the next...so not only do you have to plan to finish the mission, but you have to plan how to take all your units + resources from the mission area back to the base so you can use it to help kick start your base in the next mission.
(a few of the small missions i was able to steam roll over them with the mass of units i made in a previous mission allowing me to net a large amount of resources).

One major thing to be aware of...if you just do the bare bones for each mission you will fail the campaign.

All and all i would say that this is a hardcore type of RTS that you just dont find anymore and is a true gold mine for those that enjoy the challenge and dont need tutorials to hold you hand.

p.s. being able to also customize the weapons on all your units is well done in this game and makes it highly enjoyable to do replays with different weapon focus.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game still holds up 18 years after release.

The climate is distinctively dark and gloomy. You can tell that the world has been ravaged by decades of war and there isn't much left to fight over. The campaigns are non-linear, if you keep losing you will start getting more and more defense missions, up until having to hold your own capital. Downside is that in some cases it gets a bit grindy, as in a lot of missions your objective is to mine resources - meaning sometimes you just end up staring at the screen doing nothing even after all enemies have been eliminated.

The basic gameplay mechanics are surprisingly varied for early 2000s time period. Weapons have their own armour penetration values, there are energy weapons and force fields, weapons which overheat enemy vehicles, and guns that can disable enemy units, making them vulnerable for capture. The basebuilding in this game is pretty solid, maybe not Supreme Commander levels, but you still end up creating quite the large compound.

Music's pretty decent, and is also probably what helped me shape my current tastes for music back when I was a kid. Each nation has their own soundtrack, distinct for day, night and combat situations.

Overall, this game is a little gem. A little bit obscure, good for its time, and able to hold its own today.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUh... no. I played several RTS before. This game is mediocre at best. I know is old, and that's OK to me, but the visuals are rather primitive. There are not many units but still is hard to tell one from another. Pathfinding is terrible. Missions are repetitive and unnecessarily long. Story-wise is nothing to praise about. Even after playing the tutorial you still can't figure out how to do several things. There is no help in-game, no tooltips so the learning curve is a steep one. Combat is not rewarding, is sluggish and units have a hard time finding their way into battle. Research is expensive and takes a long time.

Found no bugs nor had any crashes. Runs fine on Win 7.

Overall, there are a few interesting features, like having a main base that can't be destroyed and some other minor things. This game feels like something that takes way too much time and gives very little in return. I won't say it's totally boring but lacks on the entertaining side of things. It becomes a chore rather quickly and begs for you to use cheats. That's not a good game to me.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA rather fun little game. The main campaign is interesting but incredibly exhausting; you're essentially running the entire campaign with the same pool of resources, and the goal is to gather enough minerals for a space fleet to escape the destruction of Earth within a time limit that spans the entire campaign. If you haven't completed the campaign within the time limit, Earth blows up and the game ends. If you've wasted all of your minerals on fighting or otherwise have done a poor job of managing the resources, you won't have enough minerals in time, the timer will run out and Earth will blow up and you'll lose the game.

Well worth diving into at some point if you have a weekend or so to spare. You probably won't complete the campaign, but the game will definitely be interesting. If 7€ is too much for you for a game that is as ancient as this one, pick it up at a sale at some point.\t\t\t\t\t
