\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIgnore hours played, this game does not like the Steam overlay and for whatever reasons hasn't recorded my game time.

So Earth 2150 is a fairly standard straightforward real time strategy game with some unique ideas, especially for it's time. First off, you can customize units over the course of the campaigns. Though your options are fairly limited in the beginning, you can upgrade your units with stronger weapons and defences, this goes for some of your structures as well. With that you have numerous playing fields which consist of your main base and the mission area. Both these features truly make this a unique experience. However upgrading building units does come at a cost of precious resources, as your main mission which spans the entire game is to collect enough resources to basically build something of an Ark to leave the dying Earth.

Now onto the technical side and the games age showing. This game does not support resolutions of modern computers properly. As far as I've seen, there aren't any real mods for the game to fix this, so know you will have to play at a much lower resolution. Now note the game does function at higher resolution, some of the in-game menus will cause crash to desktops to occur when you open them. As far as I've been able to see, there is no fix for this. Otherwise, the game runs like a charm and runs just fine on Windows 10 and Windows 7 OS.

Now when it comes to gameplay, this game will make you think and strategize early on. Resources are very limited, enemy units are not joking around, units (both owned and enemy) can run out of ammo on the field which requires constant supply deliveries in the mission area, view for units can be obstructed by time of day and weather. Earth 2150 was ahead of its time. But of course, it doesn't have it's drawbacks. AI in general isn't particularly smart and will require an immense amount of babysitting and careful cooridination, as units will tend act a bit silly and blindly roll into enemy fire when using large groups.

Pros:
-Excellent real time strategy
-Compelling story
-Gameplay has aged well

Cons:
-Technical limits due to age
-Dumb AI

Overall, this is a good game and worth buying if your up for a challening real time strategy which falls into the same vein as Homeworld. Just be prepared to deal with feel of working with a much smaller resolution. But trust me, this game is worth playing, even if it's a bit blocky.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Earth 2150 Trilogy is an excellent game series that remained true to real-time strategy conventions but still offered many interesting features, such as the ability to construct original units from component parts.


Those of you who haven't experienced the science fiction of Earth 2150 have missed some good gameplay, highly realistic environments, and an exciting plot. All of these features satisfied even the most demanding RTS fans. What we can say for sure is that the outstanding atmosphere presents the most appealing aspect of Earth 2150. Changes of night and day, weather conditions, and highly detailed landscapes proved that the guys at TopWare really take their work serious.

Each of the three organizations features more than 50 units, weapons, and structures in its arsenal, many with upgrade components, prerequisites,and specific properties. Weapons and technology range in power from simple chain guns and cannons to earthquake generators, weather control centers and shields. Structures run the gamut from energy supply facilities and mines to research centers and base defenses.

Single-player campaigns include a carryover resource system from mission to mission, so maintenance of a home base is mandatory and requires transportation of units and resources to and from specific mission areas. Skirmish mode offers access to campaigns and battles created with the built in map editor, and serves as a practice field for multiplayer training.

The Earth 2150 Trilogy is one of those games series that make you wonder why others in the genre don't have the same features. Then again, a RTS game with good graphics, good artificial intelligence, and good unit balance is quite uncommon. But the Earth 2150 Trilogy meets all these demands: The graphics are distinctive, the computer opponent is good enough to give even experienced players a tough time, and each side plays differently and has its own advantages.\t\t\t\t\t
