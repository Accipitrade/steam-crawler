Works without any issues on Linux with Proton (7.0-6).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk I like this game but there one or two issues that need fixing (which I don't think will be) and then I will 100% recommend.

1. Selecting a different puzzle.

The game starts with Vienna by default, I could not allocate any other location/puzzle, so I thought I had to complete the 40,80,200,400 with Vienna and the next location would unlock, however no new location unlocked at all.

As it turns out the puzzle selection is on a map which you will overlook as the markers are dark grey and the map is light grey, there is no indication whatsoever unless you hover over with your mouse then it shows the location names on the map which you can choose which location you want.

I would like to make the suggestion to the developer to maybe either make the current location marker flash/blink on the map or change the marker/pin colors to red, something to let us know a different location is available.

2. Adding a zoom (right click) on the piece you pick up would be very helpful especially with the 400 piece puzzles.

The music is ok at the start but I did find myself muting it, there is just not enough variety.

I have seen other reviews stating the same issues so I know I am not alone with these problems.\t\t\t\t\t

This simple mechanic is what makes a puzzle feel like you are doing it IRL, and not having it is just annoying.

I like that they max out the puzzles at 400 pc, because it just gets too tiny otherwise. I have a largeish monitor (32") but still hate doing 1000 pc puzzles on it because it is just too unwieldy. Do consider this as a viable option to the competition, or add it to your jigsaw games library along with the others and support casual game developers.

I got this on sale, as I prefer to do, but could argue it is worth the full price (US$4.99) as well.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wouldn't consider myself a jigsaw puzzle player, but Super Jigsaw Puzzle scratches an itch I didn't know I had (my 23 hours playtime tells me that).

It's very satisfying to play a piece that fits, and then to complete a puzzle. Of course it's not like playing a real jigsaw puzzle -- for one thing, all of the pieces are in the correct orientation to begin with. But (unlike some other digital jigsaws I've played) the pieces 'feel' solid and chunky. They click into place when you find some that fit together, and there's a pleasant 'pling' sound when you place them correctly on the board.

The music that comes with the game is pleasant enough. I'm no musician: I wish that the music were more 'circular' (you can easily tell when it restarts, which I find off-putting) and I would prefer to have more variety. Of course you could tell me to plug into my own choice of music, but the tunes that come with the game do match the mood.

A very satisfying and relaxing experience.\t\t\t\t\t

Just one more piece...
Beautiful pictures, Soothing Music, and addictring gameplay with challenge that runs the gamut from casual to infuriating. All the things you love about Jigsaw puzzles minus the lost pieces

I kid you not,; this game may well be a new guilty pleasure for me. I've always loved jigsaw puzzles but I am one of those people who some how always finds n-1 pieces in the box, where n is t the number of pieces you know you put back. But i digress. This game brings all that fun (and frustration) to your pc.

The Good
  • The pictures you're working on are gorgeous! There are also a goodly amount of them. A world tour of cities to be sure.
  • The music is soothing.
  • The challenge can be whatever you want it. Each picture comes in different puzzle configurations. 40, 80, 200, 400 pieces. 40 Is a walk in the park and 400 will have you teraring out your hair at times. All of them enjoyable.
  • Addictive gameplay. Maybe its just me, but the game is quite compelling. I keep coming back just to slot a few more pieces in and its always just one more piece. Not even kidding, this is not the game to play to get ready for bed. No. This is the game that will keep you up all night necause you started a 400 piece puzzle and you just want to fit in a couple more pieces...just a couple more

The Bad
  • Missing some key features. IThere is no ability to rotate the pieces, or zoom in on them. I can kinda understand the first one because well, it'd be a nightmare if you had to find the correct orientatrion and position of those pieces, especially on the 400 piece puzzles., but t the latter. There's no reason for it, especially on the 200 and 400 piece puzzles where the pieces are just so small it can be very difficult to figure what's what. Just sayingm could have easily had a zoom feature, I mean its not like the right mouse button is doing anything.
  • Minor gripes. the game provgides you with virtual voxes for sorting loose pieces, However once you snap two pieces together they automatically move to the puzzle board. It would have been nice if you could put small connected groups in the various boxes.

Conclusion

This game is good. I mean it scratched an itch I huaven't been able to reach in a looong time.. There are some little niggling problems but at the end of the day i can only give this a solid recommend. if you nlike puzzles you will love it and if you've never tried a jigsaw puzzle, , this is a good way t get into the hobby.\t\t\t\t\t
