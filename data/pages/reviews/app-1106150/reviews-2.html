\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWho would have thought it? This game is really good. Thumbs up. I bought it on a sale expecting it to be a bit of garbage but it proved to be rather pleasant.

A few points that I would like to mention.

- I would prefer if the game was full voiced but I still like the fact that even with limited voices, I have the voice of the characters in my head. Still, dear developers don't do that again.

-I am 100% with the devs for not including a highlight feature. Finding items is part of the game exploration and it's an integral part of the genre, in my opinion. I think that highlight feature BS started with the release of adventure games for tablets and phones (I'm not 100% sure about that) Personally, I don't like it all. It's plain lazy from the gamers part.

- There are a couple of cases where user has to perform the same action. Yep, that was not very cool. I had to experience something similar from the first Monkey Island and the feather puzzle at the boat. I didn't like it either back then.

- The game looks really nice but a few more animation frames would be a welcome addition (mainly in the cutscenes).

- Does the one-click for everything make the game easier? Maybe it does. I would have preferred having a Monkey Island 3 style of interface rather than this which is more similar to the Telltale's Sam n Max series.

-It's not very short but in the end, I felt that it should have an extra part (even small) Not a big complain though (I got the same feeling with Full Throttle)\t\t\t\t\t

TL;DR
Yet Another Hero Story is an average point-and-click adventure that doesn't really do anything wrong, but doesn't make a lasting impression either; for its price, it's a perfect time-killer if you have nothing else to play and want to satisfy your adventure-craving while waiting for something longer and more challenging.

Summary
Yet Another Hero Story puts you in the shoes of Martin McNish, an adventurer fresh from college and ready to save the princess and get filthy rich. The game is roughly divided into three acts, which takes Martin from joining the official adventurers guild, through the quest to get into the evil castle where the princess is being held captive, to eventually confronting the bad guy in an attempt to free the princess (with some twists here and there, of course).

The game itself is your regular run-off-the-mill adventure game; controls are standard for point & click, so just click on items or characters to interact with them. There's an inventory system, from which you can select items to use them with characters or combine them with other items. Yet Another Hero Story features nice, hand-drawn 2D graphics and animations, which depict nice scenes. According to the store page, it features 50 different areas, and this is about right. The game's areas are decently filled with mildly interesting characters, which don't feature a whole lot of dialogue, however. The dialogue that is there, will occasionally make you smile, but don't expect to be wiping the tears from your eyes from laughing. This is partially due to the voice acting, which is (for me, at least) the biggest negative of the game. The game is only voice-acted partially, and the voice acting that is there, is really cringy. IMHO, you either do voice acting the right way, or you don't do it at all, and in this case, I think the game would've been better off without it.

The puzzles in Yet Another Hero Story are basically ok; they aren't too difficult and will never seriously challenge you. The only thing I didn't like was the end-game, which basically boils down to progressing through a dialogue tree, requiring you to pick the right lines. Picking a wrong option results in having to do the entire dialogue over again from the start. The devs added some different animations and results to each dead-end path, but they're definitely not diverse nor funny enough to compel the player to explore all different outcomes. The ending throws in a little twist, but when the credits start to roll, you'll be left with a satisfied feeling.

Pros and cons
+ nice graphics, good scenery and backgrounds
+ decent value for money, making it a nice in-between game

- only partial voice acting, which is very cringy
- short and easy, it will never seriously challenge your intelligence
- annoying end-game sequence

Verdict
It's a decent game that really doesn't do anything wrong, but doesn't have anything that really stands out either. The mechanics are what you'd expect of a point & click adventure game, the scenes are nice but not outstanding, the puzzles are ok but not really challenging, humor is ok but not "rolling over the floor"-kind, music and sounds are ok, and the voice acting is terrible. You can easily finish the game in 4-5 hours, so it's ok in "value for money" terms. If you like adventure games and are looking for a time-killer in between two big games, Yet Another Hero Story might help to sate your adventure-game-hunger. In a sale, it's a no-brainer: pick it up.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, a good indie adventure game.

Good Stuff
---------------
- The graphics are really nice.
- Well written, although I would have like it better, if it was a bit more text heavy in some parts. (Maybe that's just me)
- It's funny at times without trying too hard.
- Logical puzzles.
- Simple interface
- Not too short, not too long

Not so good
-----------------
- Some of the dialogs feel a bit underdeveloped.
- The game doesn't include full voice over. Although, I guess that it is something to be expected at this price point.
- Some things will be too familiar for veterans of the genre.

Overall
----------
It's a pleasant game with high production value especially in the graphics area.

No, It's not as good as Monkey Island 3 or Day Of The Tentacle. Personally, I would have preferred if it was a bit more challenging (then again, I have finished almost all point & click adventures)

For an indie game, at this price, it's a good choice.\t\t\t\t\t
