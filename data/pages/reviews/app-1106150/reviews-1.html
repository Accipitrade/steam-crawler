\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is one of those games that I wish I could give a neutral rating as opposed to a "recommend" or "no recommend." When forced to choose I just feel it's negative points slightly outweigh it's positive. 'Yet Another Hero Story' exactly what you should expect, so forgettable and generic that it doesn't even have it's own real name. It's just another in a long line of hero stories. With a name like that I guess it was somewhat evocative of movie spoofs like 'Scary Movie' or 'Not Another Teen Movie,' but this game didn't manage to successfully spoof any other games with a hero story it simply recycled some common tropes of a hero's journey.

The writing was really blasé and the text was rife with grammatical and spelling errors, something I could overlook if the story was at least still engaging but it was just not a very exciting story and lacked any real depth. It tried to be humorous, but it mostly falls flat. The characters are okay, but slightly underdeveloped with little backstory and little to make them memorable or endear them to you.

The gameplay itself was frustrating. I grew up playing classic point & click adventures from Sierra and Lucas Arts. I still replay them and I love playing new games that are done in a retro style that's similar to those classics. I don't mind dealing with some of the issues that commonly arose in the genre but I found it to be a little too much in this specific game. The pixel hunting in this game was really terrible. I've enjoyed and played Torrin's passage where you've had to literally find single pieces of grass that would agree to let you cross them. I've played games where you have to find a specific spot on the wall for a secret button. This game just really epitomizes bad pixel hunting. "Bad pixel hunting?" you might be asking yourself. Yes, bad, because pixel hunting done well can still be fun and feel rewarding. Adding a glimmer to a tiny spot that draws your attention to it for example, but still requires you to notice it and closely examine it makes for good pixel hunting. At least in the examples from above I knew I was expected to hunt for something. This game plays almost like a hidden object game where you don't even know what you are supposed to be looking for. Without a highlight hotspot option it can be very difficult to find the objects you need to progress especially since some of the quest objectives/directions are too vague and so many of the objects are barely noticeable or blend in too much with their background.

Then there's the weird voicing. I don't mind playing games which aren't voiced. I don't even mind playing games with bad voice acting. What I find annoying about this game is that most of the conversations have the first line voiced but the rest of the text is not. Really they should have just picked one or the other, but instead you start a conversation with voice and then nothing after that. The voice acting itself and the quality of the recording is also all over the place with some of it good and some of it actually down right bad. On the positive side the solutions to the problems are pretty logical and easy to figure out even if finding the objects you would need are not.

This game is also a little disappointing in terms of achievements. There are a total of 20 achievements, but only 3 that are missable and require some additional action. That doesn't make for a very challenging gameplay trying to get them all. As long as you play through and finish the game you will unlock 17/20 achievements and the other three are pretty easy to get without really trying. Without looking at a guide I did get all 20 on my first (and only) play through.

It took me about 5.5 hours to get through the game and if you managed to find objects that eluded me for a while, I would guess you could play it in less. $4.99 full price is a relatively reasonable amount money for the amount of game time but it just doesn't feel rewarding to play. I guess if you really love the Point & Click genre and you find this on sale, it's probably still worth a try, but don't be surprised if you have to refer to a guide because you can't find something related to pixel hunting and don't expect a riveting story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- Good art style/animation
- Humorous dialogue
- Goofy/fun cast of characters
- Music

Cons:
- Odd/bizarre puzzle solutions, unfulfilling puzzles with unclear objectives
- Very hard to see items
- Abysmal and incomplete voice acting
- Commas in dialogue are used incorrectly, making some of it awkward to read

I hate to say this, but I didn't finish the game. I just couldn't do it anymore, I found it frustrating and not fun to play through. I wasn't even stuck when I decided I was done, I simply was just not having fun. There were just way too many instances of me getting stuck because I missed an item that I didn't see, or something I could click that I didn't see or notice, despite looking thoroughly. Some items were way too small and hard to see. I saw a review say "finding items is part of the game exploration and it's an integral part of the genre"... I think that's a pretty ridiculous opinion. This isn't supposed to be a hidden object game. I shouldn't need to put my face right up to the screen to see items. I never did that at any point and I have good vision, but I found that it was too much in this game. Of course there's a little bit of this kind of stuff in most point and click adventure games, but in the great or even good games, it's not an issue or even worth talking about, other than perhaps a brief mention of it. It's just too overbearing in this game. The voice acting is, quite frankly, really horrible and honestly, pretty useless. I'm not exactly sure the point of including voice acting in the game when 95% of the game is text.

It sucks because the game had some good things going for it, but I found that the flaws just completely overshadowed the entire experience. The game is so close to being good, but I just can't say that it is. Flaws aside, I think the majority of people who gave the game a positive review state that it's not an amazing game. Everyone seems pretty ho-hum about it. There's definitely a chance that fans of point and click adventure games will enjoy this game, but I personally just can't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

The humor is OK, dumb and corny, but not Monkey Island or Leisure Suit Larry level.

No option to run the game windowed instead of fullscreen.

This is a classic "try combos of items in your bag and clickable spots / NPCs until something semi-logical works". Many of the obvious combos don't work, but keep trying, some combo eventually will. There are a few items that seem like they might be important and you need to get them, but nope, they're red herrings.



MILD SPOILERS:

The hero has a donkey buddy. Who the hero completely ignores for most of the game. And who plays no real role in the plot past the first scene. Weird. Like the devs had plans for the donkey then dropped them, but left the donkey in. No Shrek jokes, sorry.

There's one cutscene where it might seem like the game is broken / frozen; keep looking for objects in the scene, one is there. On my Mac, given that the game's graphics were choppy / jumpy at times and the voices kept dropping out, the scene being a bug seemed reasonable. Haha, no, the weird frozen scene was intentional, haha.

There is a maze you have to get through, resetting back to the beginning if you take a wrong turn takes way too long. By the time I finally got through the maze, I was about ready to punt the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game isn't a must-play, but if you're a fan of the genre, you will like it. Although the game has the same plot of most hero stories, a few twists make it unique and funny. The characters all have unique and interesting personalities. I could nitpick the game, but since it's only a few bucks and was mostly made by one person, I'll applaud the effort. But there are a few issues.

I've never seen a game that only has partial voice acting. But the way they did it is the best way you could do it. Each character besides Martin has one or two voice lines before it's text only. This gives you just enough to know what they should sound like. It's weird, but it kind of works.

The donkey plays no role in the story except at the very beginning and end. Considering the effort you make to get it across the bridge, it's a letdown and a missed opportunity. We also never saw what happens with the lawsuit.

The game could use a double click feature to move you to areas quicker because you do a lot of backtracking. In certain spots, Martin won't move to where you click because of bugs. You'll need to move him elsewhere before you can get to where you want to go. Double-clicking would solve this problem.

My biggest complaint is most objects are challenging to find. 80% of the time I was stuck was due to not seeing an object I should have. The knife and feather were two of the hardest to see. I didn't even find the feather and only knew there should have been one from skimming a walkthrough. After not seeing it, I assumed it was for another part of the game. Only to read it was this part and I still couldn't find it. I had to go pixel to pixel to locate it. Most adventure games would benefit from a hotspot indicator, but this game would improve considerably.\t\t\t\t\t
