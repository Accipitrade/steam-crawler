\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you've played the likes of Dominion, Ascension or Star Realms you'll feel right at home with this game. If you've played/own Tanto Cuore, you'll be right at home with the mix of aesthetics and gameplay. If you aren't familiar with any of that... well good luck to you, the tutorial is pretty rubbish. However the game is pretty easy to pick up, just a little confusing to look at. 'Campaign' isn't any sort of gameplay, just a way to unlock cards. There's no story or development here, just a core game with an anime artstyle.

Dominion is very much what I'd compare it to, not only for the deckbuilding, but for the slightly flavourless feel to it... it's neither good or bad as such, and the more advanced cards add a bit more to it. Star Realms is a bit more fun. Ascension has more randomness, which makes the games more interesting.

But there's a reason that Dominion is the most widely played deck building game, and that's because it's easy to both pick up with the easier cards, and develop through to the more complicated ones, there's strategy in picking the right combinations, of knowing when to focus on building your engine, when to mess with other players, and when it's worth switching to going for the victory condition, and the princesses are a nice little touch with their special effects.

So all in all, I like the game well enough, but don't love it.\t\t\t\t\t

LONG VERSION

1. Game Mechanics : using only mouse in this game making this easy to control. About the gameplay, many fellow HoC gamers said it resembles Dominion so if you're familiar with it, this game might have the same system. As for me, these kind of games is pretty fresh and new, so the learning curve is quite gradual (practice makes faster to understand). The game offers 15-30 minutes of game each session (assumed the CPU think is set to fast and we as a player think as fast the CPU).

2. Visuals : anime- style waifus are pretty much eye candy. Too bad there are only 7 (yet) to choose with. The interface and layout of the game is neat and beginner- friendly. There is also a downside, that the game is better in windowed mode (not in fullscreen as it will be blurred by a bit).

3. Sounds : while yes, a digitalized version of a card game will be enhanced by matching sound effect, it's nothing too special. There is a less- known feature that is we could add custom BGM in the game local folder. Is there any possibilities for voice acting of the princesses? That will add up some fun.

4. Story : the only "story" you could get is via obtaining random card in Campaign Mode. Basically, we must earn points to unlock each random card. This system either makes me motivated to play more and sometimes makes me disappointed for the "story" unlocked content after trying so hard to obtain points is so brief.

5. Replayability Value : you could replay the game until you're bored. So good luck with achieving all the Steam Achievements before you got bored ^^.

6. Fun Factor : the fresh game mechanics of the game is still amuse me until now (even with so much losses to the Weak CPU). The nice graphic is very much entertaining all the time.

7. Value for Full Price : to support the great developers out there, why not? I buy this game because it was ranked pretty good for a card game but I have a hard time playing board game irl so digital version is the compensation for that. Imagine that you don't have to spend extra time to shuffle card nor to place each card based on their layout and especially not to have calculate the points ourselves. While yes, that's the charm of board game but I prefer something easier to have fun.

8. Others : This game will be pretty stressing if you expect for victory each time you play. Instead try to discover ways to obtain Succession Points with different card or different princesses.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEver wanted to play politics using money to buy everything for your backed princess so you can get her to the throne? This is literally that kind of game.

Jokes aside, this is a deck building game. All cards are available so there's no in-game transactions or anything like that. It's all about during the game you build your deck against other players or CPU. Several types of cards in order to screw with your opponents or benefit yourself while trying to win succession.

There are different win conditions which is always nice.

I'd say, definitely grab it, it's an entertaining card game even with friends. Sometimes it may feel basic since all you have to do is get to 20 points without anyone contesting you. There's ways for other players to screw you over though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, have I played a lot of the game? No, but I've played enough, and realistically this game deserves a middle thumb, a solid "Meh" because it's not bad, but it's also not good. Buuuuut a negative review might catch the eye of somebody looking for an honest review more than a positive one might, and 20$ is 20$ for somebody considering buying this.

Full Disclaimer: Have never played the card game, but I've played other card games released by the same company that ported them to the US.

This is not a bad game, and in all honesty, I kind of like the mechanics and playing against CPUs and I will probably do more of that to unlock the campaign bits. The art is fun, the UI is decent, the music has catchy bits, so overall it's not an unpleasant experience. Those are the positives.

The negatives.

Boy, the tutorial is just... not great. Given, if you take 5 minutes with the tutorial then you'll figure the game out, it's not *that* complex, but certain things (What are the alternate rules, why can't I seem to save custom decks, what *is* the campaign exactly?) are just not explained at all well. Or at all in some cases.

The multiplayer from what little I looked at it seemed to lack a chat option, which... not a huge fan of that. Also seemed pretty dead, though the game hasn't been out for that long. So really your options boil down to: A) Play against CPUs a bunch and have an okay time or B) Play against people who most likely have played the card game and are waaaaay better than you.

Overall, I'd get it on sale, the game itself seems to be put together rather well, the gameplay is decently fun against CPUs and if the multiplayer community picks up, I think there could be some interesting strategizing you might do to make the game pretty fun to play. But at full price? Right now I wouldn't buy it for that (even though... I bought it pretty much for that, but shut up!) and I hope that it gets a vibrant community and some patches in the future.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's kinda like Dominion, but a lot cuter and fun. Reminder that this is a deck-building card game, not a collectible card game. You don't have to buy packs, you already start with all the cards needed to play. You build your deck during the match.

Gameplay: Everyone starts with the same 10 cards in their deck, a couple of useless maids and some land cards. Lands give you gold which's used to buy more land or other stuff that can also give you gold or have a nice effect to annoy other people. New stuff you buy get added to your discard pile. Once your deck runs out of card, your discard pile containing the stuff you bought is shuffled and becomes your deck, this way you build your deck in-game by recycling cards, unless you banish them. Once you have enough money in your hand, you can back a princess, each with different skills, and then start giving her succession points with the backers you buy off the market (lol). You win the game by reaching 20 succession points, unless other people get to reach 20 points before your next turn. If that happens the first one to get to 30 wins.

Pros:
+ Simple and extremely fun game.
+ Short games, around 10 to 20 minutes.
+ Nice and cute, family-friendly, art.
+ Klam-Klam.
+ You can NTR other players.

Neutral: (mostly quality of life issues, nothing that makes the game bad imo)
. Can't (re)join a game after it's started (replacing cpu).
. There's no time out for turns (yet. it's being implemented), so you'll have to restart the match if someone dies while playing.
. You get immediately thrown back to the title menu after a match ends, so you can't easily replay with the same group.

Cons:
- No chat.
- It's easy to get addicted to HoC...
- You will get NTR'd by other players.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is surprisinggly addicting. The physical version itself it very fun, but to finally be able to play this game whenever I want is a blessing.

Gameplay: 8/10

The game is fairly simple, however, strategies do get more complex later on (just like a normal deck builder).

Sound: 7/10

Music is okay. It's nothing to write home about, but I have no cmplaints.

Graphics and UI: 8/10

Same artist that did Etrian Odyssey also does most of the art for this game. All the princesses are quite cute, which is alwasy a plus. Menu is easy to look at and swift. In game UI however is quite cluttered and you can sometimes miss what's happening if you aren't paying attention (luckily there is a log to track plays).

Overall: 8/10

The game is easy for anyone to get into, and at the same time, not too difficult to master. It's very enjoyable and fun. Of course there are some technical caveats here and there, but nothing toooo gamebreaking. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's Dominion with the Etrian Odyssey artist drawing cute young maidens for it.

Actually, not quite. It's a deck builder (If you are unfamiliar with the term, a card game with no collectible aspect. You all start with terrible decks and use the powers on your cards to buy more cards that get shuffled into your deck to give you more abilities.). Dominion is still the grandpappy but this isn't too bad, going a more Euro direction than Ascension, Puzzle Strike, or Star Realms, its competition on Steam.

You still get cash to buy big point scoring cards, but you must buy a princess candidate first. She will take the cash cards you used to buy her. With her at your table, you have access to her special ability, you can store cards on the cash cards she took to get them out of your deck and play them on one massive turn, and you can (really, you MUST) drop off your scoring cards with her to actually get your victory points.

This is very different from the elegance of Dominion, in both good ways and bad, but above all else it is an interesting twist and deserves play. It is closer to Dominion's elegance than the other games of this type on Steam. It requires more strategy than Star Realms and Ascension (Though it lacks Ascension's sometimes crazy combos) and is much less of an intense knife fight than Puzzle Strike.

All those VN segments you see in the screenshots? Those are bonuses. A side game. There's no story campaign here. Just vs AI or Online vs. The AI is decent from a couple of plays. The segments are part of the "Campaign" which is not a story so much as a tracker to see who is winning with each princess candidate, rolled up with the ability to buy skit scenes with the characters in the game using points you accumulate from playing.

The game is solid. The variety of cards is on point (But not as incredibly generous as Ascension). The english translation is strong. It has a neat art style. I recommend it if you like card games.

EDIT: I will add online takes FOREVER because the cards are more complex than Ascension or Star Realms, and there is no asynchronous play option and absolutely no compromise is made within game logic for async options (Any time you have to involve the other players, the game comes to a halt even when it does not impact your turn until that effect is resolved. Thbe game does not do ANYTHING automatically, even a brain dead "show this so bad thing doesn't happen" requires manual input.), so you're looking at a card game that can easily take twice as long as a PUBG match with ALMOST as much boring thumb twiddling. So if you're buying this and don't want to do vs AI play or play with close friends, you may want to reconsider.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving played Dominion and Tanto Cuore, I am absolutely excuberant in the final release of HOC! Many cards are familiar to the other games, so it was not hard to start. There are major differences that add much needed flavor and direction that the other games lack. These include limitless buying ability (I figured that out by accident!), the Coronation and Sudden Death, plus fantastic artwork (Tanto Cuore is pretty too, but is baseline hentai)! I simply love the theme of HOC. TC is more difficult to bring out to a group for having to explain that we "hire maids with our love!". It's not slavery! HOC is so much more approachable for all types of people, faster to play, and contains just enough expansions to keep playing without becoming dry like Dominion did (I only have the base game and Prosperity, that's all you need for a decent game). And the price is perfect! I purchased it on sale for $20, and am happy to see that I get a well written tutorial (which holds your hand just enough to get you started without being slow), several campaigns, and Ranked mode!
Final score is 11/10!\t\t\t\t\t
