\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAMAZING SOUNDTRACK AVAILABLE. seriously, it's not really advertised that you have a soundtrack you can listen to but if you go into the local files the OST is there zipped and all it needs is extraction and my goodness its amazing. I don't get tired of listening to it while playing so bonus! Who ever made the soundtrack, you did a job well done.

Art is amazing and beautiful and having the card game irl, it's wonderful to have this game online so it's not too difficult to find others to play the game with.

This game is a deck building game. Every player starts with the same amount of cards and as the game goes along you purchase cards and what not to build your deck up. Much more interesting compared to other card games where you need to make your own deck before the game and hope you can beat someone else. Love the concept. Hopefully Tanto Cuore makes it to steam.

Cons,
- Resolution
----Seriously who ever worked on the window really needs to patch this work. I would be happy if I could get it full screen but it isn't possible with the way the game was built. In short, it's always windowed. I'm sure some people like windowed but there are those who want full screen. Hopefully this changes.

- Tutorial is missable which isn't a good thing really. Granted it will be the very first thing you see but I know some people will miss it, I forgot it had a tutorial since I already knew how to play and just skipped it.

-Flexibility is at a minimal. No undo feature in this game so make sure you chose your actions wisely for the time being.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am really on the fence about this. On one hand, Heart of Crown is a decent deck building game and somewhat fun to play in its own right, but with no support for multiplayer unless you have up to 4 friends who have each purchased their own copy of the game and online battles don't have any built-in form of communication, so it's all too easy to end up with one guy in your game wasting most of the turn timer on every single one of his turns before finally choosing what to buy, so you might end up needing to keep a youtube video open on the side or something. That being said, the bots are decent at the game and are good for quick matches if you want.

My biggest disappointment is the "campaign." Really, all it is is a glorified skit viewer where you take points earned in normal games to draw random cards and see short skits associated with them. This lack of a full story campaign doesn't actually detract from the game itself, but it was disappointing to me since I had gotten a little hyped for some sort of story mode in this game, so it's souring my memories a bit.

However, if you like deck builder games, this one is pretty fun to play. I recommend this game if it is on a decent sale, but if you have anyone you think you would want to play it with, you might want to go for the physical game instead so that you can play that together.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHeart of Crown is probably most easily compared to Dominion, and you will grasp the basic concepts easily if you've played that beforehand. It is however not fair to say that it's just a copy, HoC does enough original stuff to stand on it's own two legs, and DBG's are a genre I would love to see more of on steam.

The artwork is nice and bright, the music is serviceable and it's one of those "Gotta like the genre to like the game" type of things. The biggest surprise is that the AI actually performs nicely. Most games like this barely provide "Drooling Moron-Bot" for their CPU Matches, HoC actually put some work into their Bots, and I still consistently enjoy playing against them. They play about ten times quicker than your average player, for starters.

As far as Bang for your Buck goes, card variety could def. be bigger. Counting my time played Offline I got about 10 hours in and I think the game could profit massively from some Expansions and greater variety. Playing with a random Lineup is still fun, but I wont be for long for anyone looking to really sink his teeth into it.

The Tutorial is also implemented badly, in my opinion. I've played a good few DBG's in my time, and thus adjusted quickly. But any new player unfamiliar with the genre is liable to get completely lost. Especially because the Setup is set to "Beginner" instead of "First Game" the first time you boot it up. Devs should probably take a look at that, maybe make it accessible without having to play on the side.

Another gripe, the "Campaign", which you will have to be generous to accept as such, consists of drawing random Scenarios presented Visual Novel-Style. That by itself is cute, and I was actually curious on getting some more background-Lore. But for some reason you are limited to drawing a card per day, even tough I usually get enough points for buying 4-5 just from playing an hour or two. There is zero reason to do that, unless it's just blatant padding of conent, in which case: Don't do that please.\t\t\t\t\t
