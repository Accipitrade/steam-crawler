\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game seemed like a really cool and fun game, so I bought it.

I was sad when I saw there was no single player option at all for the one who only wants to play online every now and then.
My biggest problem is that there is no way to easily learn the game because the in-game tutorial is not well made. If you go to the Discord server to ask for help there might be some nice guys that are willing to play with you to teach something. If not they link you to the board game manual and say that reading there will get you started. I shouldn't have to rely on outside sources to learn the game.

I think what could make the game better would be to add a real tutorial that better explains how to play the game, a single player mode, like a story mode where you can learn about the characters and clearer text on the cards.

All in all: If you know the board game and play with your friends, this would be a fun game, but not if you're playing alone and for the first time since it's hard to find people to play with.\t\t\t\t\t

Certain actions MUST be taken to progress through the action then you must push the "?" button to see if that triggered the script to tell you what you need to do for the next tutorial tip. Then push the arrows to read the text. once done you must attempt to do that action. once done push the "?" again and repeat until their is no more tutorial. Bad right? doing this will make you fail your first game since the AI is doing things in the back ground so fast and you have NO IDEA whats going on. So I will explain what I understand of the game so far.

1st you will spend your territory to get Coins. these are the farming villages you start with. Ignore the maids for now. Clicking on the villages grants you a single coin per village. this is your money each turn. you then use that Money to buy things on the "market" thats that mess at the top left and top middle. right click to read the description of the cards. double click to buy. each card has a cost. which is the number in the top left of the card. once your out of coins. you end your turn. thats just turn 1!!!!

Each turn follows you drawing from your deck and adding to it by "buying" from the market. City's and Large Cities give more gold and take the same slot as a village. so instead of getting one gold your getting two or more. You can buy action cards which do various action like drawing more cards to giving you more gold.

Then the game adds a mechanic called "Links" These are yellow arrows on the card either right or down. if their is no arrow then its End card. these cards are usually great to tie in to other cards that have dual links or a great way to finish a hand.

Now at this point your getting a large deck, getting money and im sure by now the AI has backed a princess. so lets talk about that. "backing"(IMO buying) a princess is down by having at least six gold and to the right of the "top middle" pile there is a pile of princesses. clicking on that gives a list of the princesses that are left/available. once you back a princess, your turn ENDS!!!! yep make sure its not a turn where you have twelve gold or something. This puts you into the race for the throne.

At this point you want to buy Points. those are the maids,senators,and dukes. the ones with crowns at the botton of the card. some cards on the market give those too (highly expensive though). Now its time for that stupid maid that keeps showing up in your hand the minus two points. Your given a choice now. Either spend gold to BUY at the market, or Turn in Points. You can only do one or the other. The thing is turning in points "banishes" the card. Poof its gone, no more is it added to your deck at the end of the turn, no longer do you have to see that card anymore. good bye minus two point maid. granted doing that makes your princess lose two points. buts thats one less card in your deck. at this point, you buy lots of point cards, and if your hand is mainly point cards you give them to the princess. if not go buy more points/cities or what ever else you want.

Now heres the clincher! the point score keeps going up until someone hits twenty. yep twenty, or at least twenty. once someone hits twenty its "Sudden Death" Every one gets ONE turn to hit that twenty mark. if NOT that princess is the new queen and the game is OVER!!!! On the other hand if other people Do get to that mark. it turns into a race till thirty. If you didn't make it to the first Coronation (sudden Death), the game skips over you since YOU dont matter anymore and you get to watch the AI or other players fight for the throne.

Thats the gist of the tutorial in a nutshell. theres things like Keeping and recalling action cards to do long links and such, but thats advanced techniques in my book. i just put a rampart on it to defend my territory forever :)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHeart of Crown is a lot like other deckbuilding games, which is a really cool and somewhat new type of tabletop game, where you and several other players buy from a pool of cards to add to your deck, and eventually win enough points to score a victory. What sets Heart of Crown apart from the others, is just how buggy and terrible the game is, even compared to other terrible and buggy deckbuilding games that are already out there, like Ascension's PC port.

Don't buy this game, but if it interests you, go to your local boardgame shop and buy it there. The PC version is completely useless. You would actually have a better time getting a rip off version and playing it in Tabletop Simulator it's that bad.

The main issues is how the game runs. It's very bright and the graphics will look terrible on 4K monitors. The game also will not work well if you go borderless and set it to a more sensible resolution.

For multiplayer, it won't work unless you know about port-forwarding and messing with your firewall enough that you could code your own multiplayer card game.

There is no real tutorial. Technically, there is a "help" menu that you can use, but it has a habit of overlapping the actual game. So the CPU will start playing cards, and sometimes you'll be trying to read the tutorial and you'll accidentally click on something you didn't intend to, or a graphical element you can't see.

The tutorial also has a habit of explaining rules that will affect your current action *after* you've made the action. For instance, the tutorial will tell you to use a succession card, but it won't tell you that playing a succession card prevents you from playing other cards. The game will *not* move forward unless you end your turn, so you'll play a succession card and wonder why you can't do anything, only if you then hit the little tutorial button will the tutorial continue and explain what happened.

Also, sometimes you can do actions on a card, and sometimes you can't. Princesses' have powers, and I'll be damned if I can tell when or if the powers went off or are relevant. Many action cards will sometimes work, and sometimes not. And it has nothing to do with the irrelevant arrow system that was never playtested.

In addition sometimes card costs will not register, or you won't be able to pay for cards you should be able to buy, or sometimes you can buy a card you normally couldn't. This is because the cash system is bugged.

Lastly, actions are incomprehensible and unintuitive. Sometimes actions require a right-click, a double-click, a drag and drop, and the game does nothing to tell you what's required when. You sorta have to randomly mash the mouse around the screen until something vaguely useful happens.

Just, don't buy the game. Even the creators seemingly know it's because they link the rules for the board game on the discussion board instead of the games for the PC port. It's great that I know when to shuffle the cards in real life for my video game.

Try again.\t\t\t\t\t

Don't get me wrong, it's a fun little card game with cute art but theres definitely some room for improvement.

First of all, I started the game in japanese and accidentally clicked away (what looked like) the tutorial. In-game I searched for a way to change the language just to find out, that I had to right-click the game in my steam library, go to "language" and select english.
So far so good.
The tutorial wasn't comming up anymore and I clulessly started some vs cpu battles. By accident, after like 5 battles, I clicked on the button with "?" in the right corner.
Suddenly this maid jumped in my face explaining how the game works.
....Soooooo maybe this can be implemented better?

Card-unlocking is one of the main reasons why I like to play card games like these. So I was super happy when I saw that there is a campaign available. I tought that you earn points, can absolve some sort of special battle and gain a new card if you win. But nope. All you get from spending your points are backstory-scenarios for the princesses. Nothing else. And it takes forever too, because the amount of points you have to spend increases everytime and it seems that you can only draw a limit amount of cards everyday as well(?).
And I dunno, for 22,99 EUR thats....a bit disspointing.

The thing with the resolution doesn't bother me that much, but yeah.


As I said earlier, the game itself is fun, but there just too little content and optimization for the price tag.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComing from someone that has never played a deck building card game and doesn't know what Dominion is, I've really enjoyed this card game. Reading the instructions before trying it was really intimidating but the in game tutorial is actually pretty good. At least enough to get your bearings so you can start learning strategies on your own.

The vs CPU offers a decent challenge. And it's a godsend since you won't have much luck getting an online match. That said, I haven't gotten tired of playing against the PC yet. The large selection of cards keep things fresh.

The "campaign" is really just VN style cutscenes where some characters talk for a bit. You don't really progress through a story, instead you play regular games against the CPU or online in Vs mode and that grants you points that you use to unlock more cutscene events. And they're not unlocked in any particular order so it doesn't give you a cohesive story and instead just gives you some insight into some of the princess' character and personality. I don't like that the cost to unlock scenes increases as you unlock them in a single day and if you close the client, you can't buy anymore until tomorrow. Seems like an artificial way to lengthen game time so people don't marathon through it and unlock everything in a week.

The effects of the cards moving around the table and sound effects are pretty smooth. The music is really charming and I can listen to it for hours. The card art however is a bit too low res for my taste. You have gorgeous card art and it's all displayed at a blurry 720p mess. It also makes reading the text on the cards harder on the eyes.

The game is really addictive. It's really easy to jump into a game for a few minutes and wonder where the day had gone after the 6th consecutive match. The rules, though intimidating at first is really easy to pick up once you've played a game and there are a lot of ways to use the various cards to achieve victory.

I definitely recommend it if you like the art and like strategy/card games.\t\t\t\t\t
