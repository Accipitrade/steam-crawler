\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR : Buy it if you're a deck building fan such as Ascension, Star Realms, Cthulhu Realms.

I am writing as someone who has spent hours in Ascension doing disgusting long e-peen stroking degenerate combos and felt good about it. This game is a labour of love from devs and I appreciate them for making an enjoyable software transition from the physical card game. Don't expect additional game modes though as this is a port of the card game through and through without any additional bells and whistles aside from the campaign mode, which are visual novel skits about the princesses.

The AI is quite aggressive even on weak so new players might want to do solo matches without AI first to gauge the synergy between cards before committing to an AI match. The tutorial is also non interactive so it's better to read up on the how to play documents if you're absolutely new and never touched a deck building game before. For seasoned deck builders, the learning curve is fairly easy as it is similar to other deck building games.

Good game, would buy additional expansions if released.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love card games and deck building games, and this one certainly sounded interesting. Unfortunately the quality is *drastically* less than it should be... if this were a $4.99 game? Sure. But for a $10+ price point it is reasonable to expect things like:

-Visual / graphic assets that are not low quality
-Graphics settings to allow the game to run full screen, rather than in an offset window
-A tutorial that follows a more logical order of explanation (the concept is not hard to learn, however the way it is
presented and the order of the concepts they show make it more tedious and initially confusing than it needs to be).

My main problem and reason for refunding is the atrocious blurriness and extreme lack of polish on the technical side. I'll consider giving it another shot if its on sale at least 70% off or if they do a drastic overhaul, which it seems unlikely they will.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs mentioned in other reviews, it is true that the tutorial in this game is very lacking, but this game is not hard to learn at all. Just help yourself a little and look on youtube for a tutorial video. I promise that you will learn the basics in 10 minutes.

As of the game itself, it is a deck building game. You gather resources (coins) and spend them to purchase extra cards from the market on the top of your starting deck. The variety of market decks is what makes the game fun and and renders it lots of replayability. Different sets of market decks require different strategies to build your deck in combination of the princess's ability of your choice.

This game also has a special design as the chain of card-play. It means you have to chain the next card you play with an already-played one, otherwise you cannot play one. This adds another layer to your deck building and card play, as the "chain-ability" of the cards and sequence you play them do matter. I have seen multiple board game reviewers praise that this design stands outs as a smart one among other deck builders.

Thank all the kickstarter backers and developers for making this game a great success.

I hope the developers would add more DLC if possible...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am very hesitant to recommend the PC version of this game. I love the physical version, it's probably my favorite board/card game and it's a lot of fun for all the reasons documented by other reviewers. However, I find the PC version a little frustrating. I can't figure out the networking so I've mostly been playing locally against the AI.

1) The gameplay is far too fast to follow, even when setting the AI to "think" slowly. By turn 10, when the AI is stringing together 5+ action cards in a row, the cards go by too quickly to comprehend what has happened. There is no option for click to continue, so that you can read the card that was played and understand what is happening. There is also no pause, so that you can go back and read the log and try to figure out what was played. Ultimately, the game will tell you what you need to do when an action is needed of you on an opponent's turn, so I guess if you're okay with following orders despite being confused, then it's fine.

2) Even the Weak AI is incredibly aggressive. I've played about 20 games and won maybe 4. At least one of the AI will back a princess before Turn 5, and it's almost always Bergamotte (and if not her, then the first will certainly be Klam-Klam). My last match saw an AI back a princess on Turn 3, which is literally the earliest turn possible. The AI will often accept the -2 penalties for using Farming Villages too. That all just seems way too aggressive for a "weak" AI. If I'm doing so poorly on Weak, I'd hate to see how badly I get destroyed on Strong.

3) The user interface is very clunky. Often dialogue boxes will remain for far too long, obscuring play. It is often unclear when you have to drag vs. click vs. double-click.

4) As mentioned by other users, the "campaign" seems to be just one-off scenes that you can only draw once a day. I guess it's better than microtransactions. I unlocked a couple of "titles" but I couldn't find where to set them.

On the plus side, playing the PC version did clarify a couple of rules that we had been doing wrong. (Notably, nerfing Lain&Shion so that the bonus turns can only be used once per round, and also stacking similar cards in the random market so you don't just end up with five copies of the same card that nobody wants)

So I guess my recommendation is to buy the physical game instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm awful with reviews. I have this board game physically with all the expansions, and have been playing it online with friends during the CORNA Pandemic.

Plus: Fast, fun Dominion-style build your own deck board game with the added bonus of waifu.
Decent Discord group to find people to play with.
Many cards and card sets, a lot of strategy.

Con: Every person needs a copy, which is fine for internet play, but really wish two people could use the same computer.
Kinda pricey?
Not all the expansions are in it, and I don't think they ever plan to have them.

Still, if you're now doing most of your tabletop gaming online and you want something interesting, I totally recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
