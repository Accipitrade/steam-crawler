Z, just as it used to be

Contrary to "Z steel soldiers" this is indeed the full, uncut, unbugged version of "Z".
This is game is funny and unconventional as far as RTSes go, even today. The robots do not fight for resources but for control points (flags) that bring all buildings in a given sector to the side holding it. Once a side controls the majority it becomes very hard very fast for the other side to catch up.

The cutscenes are darkly humorous.

That being said, Z is now around 25 years old, a quarter of a century have not been too kind to this game. People familiar with modern RTSes will miss many features such as
precise sound regulation,
key binding,
a sophisticated pathfinding AI,
intuitive production menues
...
Z is just a little between Dune 2 and C&C 1 in terms of usability.

Nonetheless, after activating the high-DPI compatibility mode the game runs perfectly even on my SurfaceBook2 with its 4k screen and detachable graphics card. More than what can be said about many games that were released after Z.\t\t\t\t\t
