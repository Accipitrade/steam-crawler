\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI used to have this game on PS1 back in the day. I never completed it back then, but when I found it on Steam I knew I had to have it! It's a slightly different RTS where the gameplay is more about taking over areas/buildings/vehicles rather than building them.

That's not to say there's no building of vehicles and training of units at all, though! But instead of how it's typically done in the genre, it's just more automated. The factories you control just continuously produce units for you, but you can choose which units you want from what's available.

I found the game to be just as fun now as back then, maybe even more so. A bit let down that the cinematics between levels are just the same ones that repeat over and over, though. But the game itself is a lot of fun!

A little more than 4 hours for 100%.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis remake is a technical mess, to say the least.

The pathfinding of the units is completely broken, they often get stuck in rocks and other things obstructing their way, which means you have to carefully watch each step they take (which is impossible due to the terrible controls) and sometimes they don't even respond to your orders. For example ordering your soldiers to shoot the enemy units often results in them running straight into the enemy soldiers, where they get totally annihilated. Or they sometimes ignore that the get shot to take a flag, even if you ordered them to kill the enemy units first, so they die without doing anything.

The controls are also pretty terrible - you cannot move the screen using your keyboard, it's almost impossible to select certain units if they are stacking and most ingame buttons feel unresponsive. The menu is total garbage and unintuitive, e.g. if you press escape during a mission you get into the pause menu, but if you press escape again the game doesn't resume (which you'd expect) but asks you to close itself.

What annoys me the most is that the developers seem to have ditched this game a few weeks or months after releasing it, knowing about the terrible state of the it (which is shown in the update log on steam). This is even more aggravating since a lot of the mentioned problems (like missing keyboard controls) could most likely be easily solved and would greatly improve the game.

So unless you really want to play Z because of nostalgic reasons I cannot recommend spending even as little as 6€ for this game. You will be disappointed and frustrated by it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a nice port of the original game but the controls have changes to not require the keyboard which leaves the game bugged. Robot units that can both destroy or capture an empty vehicle will often do neither and stand around. This make the APC practically useless because it will bug out and be rendered inoperable. There is no volume controls for audio and the AI path-finding is somehow even worse than the original game.

Still highly recommended because it's a real time strategy game that has many features in it that no other RTS game has ever had. Symmetrical maps, the AI starting with the same or better units than you, resource gathering replaced with capture the flag gameplay. Sneaking behind enemy lines, thinking outside the box and flanking are usable, functional game mechanics and smaller units can get lucky and take out larger ones especially if you micro-manage them. This is not a game of numbers but of tactics. Fast paced, as a veteran player I breezed through the whole campaign in under three hours but hard mode is hard. You will win or lose each round in 7-14 minutes and find yourself replaying the levels over and over hoping the AI makes a mistake.\t\t\t\t\t
