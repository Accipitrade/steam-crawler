\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL/DR:
Summary: This is an older game. 90's RTS (Command&Conquer, Dune 2000, etc). You can't build buildings or bases. You capture tiles and use prebuild structures to build units. Unit cap is 50.
Seems to be the first Z or Z95 version. (Missing higher resolution setting, Only has 20 levels instead of the 35).
Great nostalgia purchase.
Trailer tile is a little misleading. If you full screen tile video you can get a better idea of resolution.
Game is fairly short. You could probably beat and 100% it in <4.5 hours.


Long:
I played the original back when it first released. This was a fun play thru but is very short. I do remember the game being more difficult. There doesn't seem to be any remastered areas. I was hoping the resolution would at least be a little improved. Setting show it as 1080p, but its obviously not.
It played almost flawlessly and the only issue I noticed was soldiers wont enter vehicles some times. And single units from squads will just stay as the rest move as directed. They will fire if an enemy approaches so it isn't a graphic issue.
I do hope at some point they do a remastered. Adding the extra levels, higher resolution, multiplayer and bigger/better maps.
If you're purchasing and playing this, Im guessing its for the nostalgia. I was pretty happy when it was added to steam. There is an open source "The Zod Engine" that looks to be a little more refined.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: This game is great, this port is awful.

The original Z was an extremely engaging early-90's RTS that could have been as big as Command and Conquer if the release calendar had worked differently. It was well-balanced with a terrific and comedic plot and varied units with different strengths and weaknesses in a Rock-Paper-Scissors system. It had a campaign of satisfying length and just the right amount of strategic depth. I can't say enough good about it.

But then there's this port of the game, which isn't just bad, but irredeemably awful and shouldn't even be sold on Steam. A few years ago, some devs made a mobile port of this title for Android devices which was...well, nostalgic I suppose but without the fine controls of the original that you can only get with a mouse and keyboard. The pathfinding was also terrible and there was no way to perform otherwise simple actions like splitting units, which is important when fully leveraging a strategy. It was a rough port intended solely as a cash grab and would not have satisfied anyone who knew what they were trying to do in the game.

The worst part of the story I learned today - these devs have taken the terribly ported Android title, and ported it 'back' into Windows, and with nearly no changes from the mobile title at all. This game features NO hotkeys at all for unit selection or grouping or anything, everything is point-and-click (and it's chancy even then). The pathfinding for both enemy and friendly units is abysmal and fast vehicles will not capture flags right next to them if another robot in the same group is tasked to it, even if walking from the next zone over. The map has one size and the minimap takes up a lot of the UI screen. I could go on, but the true felony is the lack of hotkeys which makes the whole game a clicker of massive size and scope.

This is not how I wanted to see the Z franchise turn out. It was a terrific game and could have built upon the original title rather than becoming a old IP used only for jobbers to make crummy ports for nostalgia cash grabs and LOL's.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not gonna lie, I played this mostly for the nostalgia buzz. It isn't a perfect game, and if it released in 2020- even as an indie game- I'd review it far harsher than I am.

But for a game that came out in the 90s- a competitor with warcraft 2... It's different and fun. Normally in these kind of games you turtle around your defenses until your army is stacked with formations of late game units. But Z is fast. You fling your starting units outward in a desperate territory grab and every time a unit finishes you race it to the front. Immediatly. The main resource in this game is time. Elite units build slower and the most important strategic decision you make is when to change buildings to build better units.

If the game has a problem it's perhaps a little too fast. Usually you can tell if you've won a map or not in the first minute or two. Especially on the early maps. If one side gets an early lead or an early skirmish goes wrong it's often impossible to recover from.\t\t\t\t\t
