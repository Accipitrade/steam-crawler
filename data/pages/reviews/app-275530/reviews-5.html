“If you love something, let it go. If it comes back to you, it's yours. If it's run over by a car, you don't want it.”
~ALF

First of all, what is Z? Well, let's just say that when I was a kid, we didn't have Company of Heroes. We had Z. Aside from the fact that Z takes a humorous approach and cutscenes here are some sort of mix between Ninja Turtles, ALF and Starcrash, heavily focused on robots, Z is pretty much it – an early game in Company of Heroes genre. You capture the flags, you produce units and you destroy enemy stronghold (tower) to win the level. Pretty basic, but addicting stuff. And like Company of Heroes, Z always looked good. Even though it was released only one year after legendary Command & Conquer, it looked way, way superior. Vehicles used to leave traces on the ground, your troops used to shoot some fauna for fun (a lot of funny effects included), there were funny animated portraits and heck, there were even destructible landscape elements! In other words, like I said, this game is pretty much Company of Heroes from 90s. In a comedy-oriented settings. And I sure have a lot of good memories about it. I mean, let's just admit it – it was one of those games that every RTS fan had on his or her computer back in the days. So, what the heck with a thumb down review? Let me explain.

First of all, it's not the real Z from 90s. What we have here is a modern “remake”. Nothing's wrong with it and Command & Conquer pretty much proved that modern RTS remakes can be cool, but the thing is, Z didn't get proper treatment. What we have here is one of those quick and crappy ports from mobiles. You know, with crappy touchscreen-oriented UI and stuff like that. In other words, the good ol' DOS game was ported to mobiles and then mobile version was quickly ported back to PCs. Because money. I won't lie, it's not like this version is especially bad. Unlike many other ports of this kind, Z feels quite OK. There are no “easy win” features, no cutscenes were removed and even the new UI doesn't really feel that bad. And yet, this version doesn't feel authentic, you don't get the original DOS version as a bonus (like in System Shock case, or in the case of Earth 2140 on GOG) and there's close to no options to make this version feel either more old school or more comfortable for new players.

Another bad thing is that this version doesn't have the expansion kit missions. And yes, I know that most of you, guys, didn't even hear about the expansion kit. I mean, it was only released in Germany and there weren't that many copies made, which makes the expansion somewhat rare, but let's just admit it – modern remake was a perfect chance to finally make the expansion missions available for everybody. Sure, it was only 11 new levels. And all of them were pretty much reworked versions of the first 11 levels from the original game (just on the bigger maps and with all units available right away), but come on! You've made the remake and didn't include the expansion? Screw this. Just... screw this. Command & Conquer did it all right. Heck, it even included exclusive missions from console versions of the game! It was just that – an ultimate version of the good ol' game. Z? It blew it. It's, like, the same old thing. Only worse.

And it's not like the game aged that well either. I mean, sure, it still have small but dedicated community, but all in all, while being a pretty basic “capture the flag” RTS experience without having anything really special about it (cutscenes repeat themselves most of the time, AI is painfully dumb, pathfinding is hell, the maps don't have enough variety, etc), it's not like it's a “must play” title for today's kids. I mean, it's not like it was a good idea to make AI smarter or something like that (the game was balanced for the dumb AI and changing it will also change the entire experience), but it's just that – nowadays, Z is not an essential experience. When it's about games like Command & Conquer and WarCraft? Those still worth your time nowadays. No matter how old you are. Z? It's pretty much a nostalgia trip for old geezers like yours truly. And they totally blew it by ruining the authentic feeling. On mobiles? Sure, this version is worth it. Just because it's a game from your childhood in your pocket. But here, in Steam? It doesn't really feel good. Heck, there's that Russian guy who did way better job with patching Windows version of the game (the one that came with the expansion) for Windows XP. Unfortunately, his version won't work properly on Windows 10, but that's not the point. The point is – I just can't recommend the Steam version. It should have either kept more authentic feeling or go the opposite way and add more modern features like Command & Conquer: Remastered Collection did. Dixi.\t\t\t\t\t
