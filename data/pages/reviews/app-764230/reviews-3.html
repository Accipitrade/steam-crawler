\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't let the five hour play time fool you. I've racked up probably fifty or so hours on the Android version, and was super stoked to see it come to Steam.

I played the original Shoujo City on Android and - while I would hesitate to say my time spent playing was completely enjoyable - I enjoyed it enough to give SC3D a chance... and boy was I glad I did. I consider this a 'guilty pleasure' game - a pure yuri-themed dating sim that encompasses the finer points of Japanese culture - but I enjoy the hell out of it.

The addition of a story mode (Walkthrough Mode) was a welcome change to SC3D's otherwise stagnant gameplay, and adds a sense of accomplishment for sticking with it. This is very much a game where it will probably take you two or three restarts to get the hang of how the game functions; but with each restart you'll find yourself enjoying it more.

I've seen people say that the words feel a bit empty and... yeah, I can see that; but I don't necessarily think it's a bad thing. Too many NPCs may cause it to run slower on the Mobile Versions (even though I've never been a firm supporter of mobile gaming). I've noticed myself that the mobile version heats my phone up a bit after extensive play.

I have two major complaints about the game - and considering how minor they are, that should tell you how I feel about it. The first complain is the dialogue. It's good... but a bit... blunt in places. I think the script could use a once-over by native speakers of the languages (SH3D supports a dozen plus languages, not all fully translated yet) to give it a bit more life and flair. Again, it's not bad, but I think a once-over will give the dialogue - and those speaking it - some much needed realism and life. (I volunteer to look over the English script! :D)

The second major complain I have is the camera. The camera - time and again - proves to be my number one adversary. While the controls are simple and responsive (to an extent) I find it impossible to find a perfect middle-ground for control sensitivity (control sensitivity controls both movement controls and camera control sensitivity) where moving and looking feel good. I personally believe some method of locking the camera behind our character would improve upon this (especially in Rotational Control scheme, where the game controls like an old-school Resident Evil game, wherein A and D turn your character right and left.) or perhaps adding a secondary function to control camera sensitivity.

A minor complaint would be Endless Mode seems... a bit empty. Even past the low NPC count, there's just not a lot to do in the world.

Please don't let these 'complaints' jade your opinion. It's still a solid game with a lot of potential in the future. I don't know if it'll get more content in future updates, but just know I plan on keeping an eye on the development regardless. If you've got ten bucks to spare on a cute, fun little yuri-themed game, then please pick it up and support the developer.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFound out about it a few days ago, and just bought it a few hours ago. I know it's a work in progress and I have only experienced a little of it as of now, but I am so hyped. This game seems to be closer to what I was looking for out of the box than in sims and awkwardly modded to death games. Really excited for the promising future of Shoujo City. Nice to have a purely yuri game of this type for once.

If you are going to play now, it's important to remember that this is still being worked on. The controls and camera are reeeaaallly awkward right now, at least for me. BUT if you go in with the right expectations, it will be fun and cute. The final version is going to be really epic from what I can see already. Plus the dev seems to be really open to suggestions from what I can tell so far.

And in what other game can you casually ride a roller coaster with a neko maid dakimakura as a cute anime girl?

EDIT: I REALLY feel like this game should have Workshop and modding support by now. I still recommend it, but it's been loooong enough without it. If the whole sims team cannot cater to what everyone wants, then a single dev cannot cater to EVERY person's whims and that is where mods come in.\t\t\t\t\t
