\r\n\t\t\t\t\tArchangels Hellfire DLC is finally here. With it comes PvP skins, 1-4 player coop arena and most importantly... the PvP gameplay is not free to play for all!

https://youtu.be/VlTYji1YpQA

Ok, lets get the ugly stuff out of the way. The 1-4 player coop is basically just a coop wave shooter. It seems to be balanced for 4 players so if you make a private group with 2... Good luck. Video on that coming later.

PvP skins seem fairly simple atm. White vs Black. I would like more customization and more options in general for making my mech my own but skins are a good start. Selecting which skin you use could also use some love as each new skin appears to show up as an entirely new mech. Instead of picking a mech, then picking a skin you scroll through the light, medium and heavy of skin 1, then move on to the light, medium and heavy of skin 2. It's extremely minor but would be a nice QoL adjustment

I'd also like to see loadout customization options. It would make things harder to balance but I'd love to be able to choose how my light mech fights. I'd love to have melee weapons too.

Ok, with that out of the way. Archangel is a good mech game. It's nothing spectacular. It's not the be all, end all of mech games. However it's still good and in the current market with so few mech games it stands out.

One thing Archangel does really well is give you a sense of scale and presence inside the mech. From the main menu to the cockpit I absolutely felt the size of my mech. From stepping on baby choppers to climbing skyscrappers, gazing at the tiny pine trees that make up the scenery or even your own mechs arms through the cockpit, you feel like you're piloting a behemoth.

PvP has gone free for all! That's a business move I can get behind. With VR titles like this that depend on population I usually comment on how waiting for games will drive people away creating a catch 22 cycle that ends up killing the game. With the free to play PvP model that fear should hopefully be mitigated with a larger population that can pick up the game for nothing. You get extra incentive for purchasing in the form of the full story mode and coop arena plus some skins however no PvP gameplay element is blocked off from F2P PvPers. I personally think it's a great idea and hope it succeeds and sets an example for other VR devs suffering from population issues.

Overall the controls felt smooth. Point and click for the most part. I wish boost was more reactive. By that I mean, when I press the boost I want to boost and when I let go, I want to stop boosting. Currently it functions such that you tap the button and you get a set amount of boost time and you cant cancel it early. So if you need a quick hop, you have to do a full princess Peach jump and glide. It makes maneuvering in the air a bit clunky.

In the end, I had a good time playing. The price for PvP is perfect. I won't comment on the 19.99 overall price tag for the story as I haven't played through it yet but it seems reasonable from what I've played.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI only played this game for about an hour at the moment but so far it feels very polished. The graphics are good and look crisp inside my Vive. The voice acting is pretty good and there is a story attached to the gameplay, I know having a real story isn't something everyone cares about especially in a fun VR game but I am someone who needs a story to stay invested in a game. The controls feels comfortable and although I played standing up it seems like it will be enjoyed more if you sat down while playing, preferably in a nice computer chair or recliner so you can match up with your in mech position for extra immersion. The only thing that I have disliked so far is that it basically plays like an on the rail shooter. You do not actually move your mech around, you are automatically pulled along a set path while you look around and shoot the enemies around you. I will update this review after I have sank a few hours into it.
