\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAudio and visual as ever is great, but the narrative for this expansion really lets it down. The best thing about it was the cinematics.

For a main campaign, it really under-delivered, which Bungie have already admitted they AIM to underdeliver, but this is next (sub) level. No spoilers, but we're expected to care about X Y Z when we don't even know them/only just met them. Nimrod talking like they've known us/Osiris for years. The Maguffin being newly introduced NOW when we're nearly at Final Shape is absurd, there's been subtle allusions to it, but we've been given nothing to invest emotionally in. Just wham bam, this is a thing, you know, the thing, we all know the thing.

People are obviously comparing this to Witch Queen, which was a stellar campaign. But we've literally just come off of an emotionally compelling arc ending with SEASON of the Seraph, that delivered a quality story. Then we get a campaign that feels like a "filler" season story.


Other stuff:
The new UI is a step in the right direction, there's some clunkiness with it but mostly smooth.

Commendations system needs more thought, very limited window to actually give commendations. Being able to do it at any time from looking at carnage reports, in addition to the current would be way better.

Strand is fun, still early days to see how it stacks up but for the most part of the legendary campaign I didn't even bother using it unless railroaded.

The new patrol zone still has classic railroading and invisible walls... when we literally get given the ability to grapple thin air this is very disappointing. Not quite the strand playground we had been hyped for.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHuge disappointment in terms of the narrative, especially after riding off the positive high that Witch Queen provided. Came out with more questions than answers. The new zone is pretty but lifeless, makes me not really care about 'defending' it. Upon closer inspection you see the rather bland designs start to stick out, I've seen most compare to a quickly built Halo Forge map. The main NPC for the new destination is extremely unlikable and spews ear grating dialogue and really kills the tone during certain sequences.

The new subclass Strand stands out as pretty unique but the campaign spends too much time 'giving and taking' on it. Unsure of its place in the game as of the moment but looks to be a viable alternate super with the right aspects/fragments to come. Hopefully not a gimmick.

There's a lot of good gameplay changes with the addition of loadouts and a rework to armor mods, I'm not too keen on the changes to the latter as they might've 'oversimplified' it for newer players to build craft and try new playstyles out, however I worry the depth has been lost a bit with these changes.

New gear is subjective but looks well made, the weapons are just reskins unfortunately.

Overall I really do not recommend this XPAC one bit considering the rather sloppy story given and poor content for £39,99 alone is yike, let alone the full edition which is £79.99. Unhinged. The gameplay changes just could've been a patch alone.

I am now wary of future releases if this is now the norm for Bungie.\t\t\t\t\t
