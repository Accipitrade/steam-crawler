\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk where to begin.

The narrative was bad. Almost the entire ride through we hear about the veil, and the Witness, and how bad another colla[se will be. Ok so we jump to neomuna and meet 2 cloudstriders who looked ripped out of Cyberpunk. They never elaborate as to why the city never helped earth, never helps us really understand why the citizens decided to live in the vr world, never explains as to what this veil object is that they defend and the witness wants. Nimbus is the horrible comedic relief who has some very meh dialogue. Rohan well.. he's cool but not for long. Osiris acts like a complete douche till about the very end of the dlc,

So strand is also never really explained outside of Osiris and I vaguely quote "light requires the right devices/tools where as darkness powers are pyschic and memory based". That's it. Nothing else. Oh and you go through the whole campaign getting a BOOSTED type of strand that has much worse cool downs once unlocked after the campaign is finished. I also noticed to buy more fragments you need to earn a currency for Strand fragments but I can't say as to how grindy that is. I turned the game off.

Ok difficulty is subjective however, on legendary on solo I noticed two parts in particular that have an aggressively bad approach to the missions format. As they are essentially the same thing although slightly mixed. This is the Headlong mission; which becomes available again once the story is complete for you to do again for timed scores didn't see a reward though. And the final mission with Calus' fight.

Let me explain headlong's arena and then I'll go over Calus'. Headlong is against the vex in a cylindrical room with a rotating laser wall that never stops and has a slightly larger hit box and doesn't need to actually hit you to chunk half your health at 100 resistance. Now we have 4 squares above us that will have hobgoblins spawning a couple times before they move to the ground with the eventual minatour boss who also has a hobgoblin rifle. Now we have a lot of adds. Like so many adds it's somewhat absurd alone. Especially because you are forced to use strand so none of your gear will be beneficial to you. Not too mention headlong is really easy at the start just grapple through kill some vex voila. But they don't put a check point in the game near the boss because it's a timed mission later. So every failure sees you go through the 3 or 4 big yellow bar minatours before the actual boss plus a wyvern

Calus' is similar. Instead of a small cylindrical room it's a large circle but with a low center platform for where various cabal enemy types will spawn throughout the fight. The sides have two raised but fairly bare and not much cover catwalks. The entrance and what I call true north are areas where cabal flamethrower units will spawn. Chunky yellow bars with strand shields/tech packs and boop physics. My god the boop physics. I died at least 12 times to getting wall bopped or thrown out of my grapple and sent plunging to my death because of it. In my eyes that is not good difficulty design. The rest of the arena is missing parts so it's very easy to fall though, get suppressed by one of the two tormentors who will show up and fall, or just have a bad grapple placement.

Ok so those are my difficulty gripes. Take it as you will.

Ending; My god imma spoil it so leave meow or forever hold your peeps.

I'm warning you \\-__-/

It blows. It sucks so hard. The witness takes control of your ghost connects the veil, witness cuts a triangle out of the traveler and inside it is a pink/purple psychedelic trip. Witness' ships turn to pink mist and get sucked in and so does he. That's it. no explanation, no collapse, nothing. Everyone acts all worried but nothing really changed.

So over all I would wait for a very deep sale unless you are committed to Bungies wonderful FOMO design philosophy. Better to spend your money on something whole and complete like the upcoming RE4 remake or something. go buy a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ rc car idk.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think a lot of people are expecting this to be The Epic Conclusion, or grimdark cyberpunk fantasy, but that isn't what it is. It's basically a playable sci-fi 80's action movie, at least in regards to the campaign of Lightfall. There's a lot of interesting lore bits, too. I don't love everything in the expansion as a whole, but I think people went into this with expectations that were waaay higher than it should have been for something that pretty explicitly is the first half of a two-part finale, with the last part being The Final Shape next year.

The story really isn't anything to write home about, especially compared to what could have happened and there are a lot of missed opportunities with it. But it's fun, and I definitely think it was worth the wait. I'm pretty excited to see where they take the game next.

Strand as the new subclass is mega fun even without the entire kit of it available to me yet. I'm super curious about how it will pull together later.

The characters are pretty good - the Cloud Striders came off as a bit two dimensional and hammy, but, in an endearing way at worst.

Build crafting and QOL stuff is a bit mixed. Mods got weird this expansion, a bit barebones at the moment but it isn't *that* bad. More delicious Bungie content for my veins in between other games - it definitely got me back into the game after basically only doing seasonal story missions after last expansion (plus season).

TL;DR: Strand is fun and the story is fun, too. There's some hammy moments, it's all a bit over-the-top and silly, but Destiny has always been that way. Don't come looking for grimdark, or for something hyper-serious, and you'll have a good time. First half of the end of the Light v Dark saga was definitely Good Enough (tm). If I had to give a number, 7/10. Better than Beyond Light, at least!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA filler for an expansion, feels rushed, and lacking the bare minimum required for the asking price (most expensive expansion in D2 history) nothing meaningful at all has happened in Lightfall, in fact so little has happened this full expansion could have easily been a season. No joke, nothing worth while to do in this expansion, the focus is 80% on Strand, which as a subclass is inept.

Multiple times in game you will be forced to use Strand in the campaign .. each and every single time you become noticeably weaker, and this is with buffed regen on all abilities, 10x's faster regen .. and you feel powerless while being forced to use it. There was not a single moment in the campaign where I wanted to use strand, and when given the choice (press E to unlock inner power) I completely skipped it so I could use any other subclass.

Strand is simply inept as a subclass, and honestly they get you use to it with insanely fast cool downs, just grapling everywhere non stop, not horrible .. but once you unlock strand 90 second cool down per graple...

Really Strand was meant to be part of Witch Queen, it was taken from Witch Queen and put into Lightfall because Bungie knew LF had nothing to offer. Everything about this expansion is uninspiring, I had to force myself to play the campaign, even than it still too me over 2 weeks to complete 3-4 hours worth of content (on one class, no wonder they are charging you to skip content you paid for, and already beat).

Every character introduced is pointless, and annoying, they tried to pull another Cayde-6 moment and failed miserably. Nobody cared .. looking back from Witch Queen Lightfall is a massive step back, and easily up there with one of the worst expansions in Bungie history (D1, and D2).

Everything interesting that happens in LF happens during a cutscene that you're forced to watch, can't have you play a part in that story ... this is a big miss for Bungie, and it genuinely has me worried we might have gotten an early previous of Final Shape with what we received in LF. Because it is clear no effort was put forth for this expansion, I mean our new planet is a city ... cool lets explore ... wow calm down buddy, you 5 foot off the road .. out of bounds ... What, more than half the stuff you see is out of bounds, you see something 20 foot away, think cool I'll explore .. and instant kill barrier.

Than you look at the LF weapons, all reskins, what about season of Defiance? All reskins too, no effort at all was given, this is purely a cash grab, Bungie couldn't wait to pull the rug. This would be unacceptable for any developer how this got green lit for a long running franchise is beyond me.

There was a lot of changes going into LF that people (including myself) have wanted, however Bungie took the sword to too many things at once, the game feels broken at the moment, armor mods are in a worse state than ever, build are extremely limited now, and even simple stuff like strikes are worse. They took some of the old strikes and made them longer/more frustrating.

No one said "Well I've been playing these same strikes every day now for the last 7 years, I just wish they where longer though". Yet Bungie felt the need, in many ways the game has been improved, however you don't feel it, what you feel is the vast amount of stuff now broken. The game currently now feels in a worse state than it did a month ago, Bungie wanted to make a lot of changes, changes they where clearly not ready to make, and in the process made the experience worse for many players until the issues can be fixed months in the future.

I mean seriously, it feels like Bungie just took common complaints from the forums, said we will fix this, than once asked how they plan to fix it they simply shrugged. Said they will figure it out, yet still forced the clearly not ready changes down players throats. More than half the changes Bungie promised did not happen, simply not ready, yet Bungie is still going around swinging the hammer making the experience worse for players.

One thing is for certain, several people who approved these changes should lose their jobs over this fiasco.\t\t\t\t\t
