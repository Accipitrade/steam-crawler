\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR at the bottom (contains raid and story spoilers btw)
This is kind of a mixed bag for me, on one hand, I love the new mechanics they brought to the game with this dlc, strand, loadouts, reworked mod system, it's great. But on the other hand is the major flaws that this dlc has in it's campaign and raid.

Let me elaborate a little, this campaign was hyped up a lot by bungie. There was trailer on top of trailer, they even let some people play early builds of it. They were really making it seem like it was going to be an amazing dlc, but, well, it wasn't. The campaign starts off really strong, the visuals and cut scenes are incredible, not to mention the mission was just really fun to play. But after the first mission, it more feels like you are just helping out around neomuna than stopping the witness from literally erasing us from existence. At the end of the campaign you fight Calus at the veil (still have no idea what that is btw, because they never explain it). And you win, and then your ghost gets possessed by the witness and creates "the link" and you lose. And that's it, guess you gotta wait until final shape in a year and spend another $50 to find out what happens next.

The over all mission structure is good, I actually really enjoyed playing on legendary, but yeah no complaints there. As for the actual world of neomuna, it is breathtaking... for about 5 minutes and then you have seen all of it. This area is tiny, and lifeless. I was really hoping for a full on open world where we could traverse rooftops and stuff, but that clearly didn't happen. It's not quite as lifeless as europa but then again, europa is an icy moon orbiting jupiter, you'd expect it to be more empty than a cyberpunk city, right? Another thing I wanted to mention is that the main new character, Nimbus, is really annoying. Bungie did some weird editing on his voice and it just makes him sound incredibly annoying. I have kind of been hating on lightfall so let me talk about strand which I think is a very cool new addition. The grapple hook and being able to suspend enemies in the air is hands down the highlight of strand. It adds a totally new way of traversing. You can grapple on to a rocket, a thundercrashing titan, a wellskating warlock, the list goes on. There are so many cool things you can do now that you couldn't do before and I think that it is a much more unique subclass than stasis (although maybe not quite as good in some situations).

Alright back to the bad. The raid is just simply not that good. I have only done 3 raids before, so take this with a grain of salt (last wish, vog, and RoN). But the raid is just simply too easy. For 3 out of 4 encounters the max amount of people needing to do mechanics is 2, and the rest are on ad clear. You can literally get a team of 4 somewhat experienced players, 2 new lights with like 20 hours on the game, throw them in this raid and they'd be fine. Unlike the other raids I've done, just doing ad clear will always be an option for everyone, and some people in your team don't even need to know how the raid works, they can just clear ads. The 3rd encounter (planets) is incredible, in my opinion it's the best part of the raid. Plus, in this encounter, 4 people have to do mechanics, and the other 2 on ad clear. The boss its self isn't much special, just an ordinary cabal with some extra health really, but getting to that damage phase is really fun and cool. Now lets talk about Nezerac, the final boss, the "Final god of pain." If you haven't done the raid you probably think he is a really difficult boss, right? No, he is easily 1 phase able. Not to mention he is literally a re skin of a normal tormentor with extra health. The raid does have some very good loot though, so it is still worth doing, and even though I kind of hated on it here, I did have fun, except for the second encounter. Speaking of which, did you know that capping your fps at like 60 or 45 is a legitimate strategy in destiny now? Yeah, the things that send you across the map in the second encounter doesn't work as well for people with higher frame rates, and will often not launch them far enough, and kill them. It got so bad that my team found that well/shatter skating was more reliable than the lifts. Even though we would occasionally mess up and die, it was still better than the lifts that most people will be forced to use. Also some of Nezeracs attacks are also fps based.
