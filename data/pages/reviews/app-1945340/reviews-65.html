Bad News First
-The story is incredibly dull, feels sped up. The Gameplay in the campaign is fun but then it just ends out of nowhere.
-The Saved Mod Builds are semi nice, however it cannot pull weapons from other characters (it can pull from vault) and you can't properly label each loadout, my izi rocket build is literally labeled as "Gambit" its stupid
-Day 1 Bugs not patched for day 1 raid, this isn't a problem now but its stupid it ever was, the list of items disabled during the raid race is pretty large
-The Raid is a joke, the only reason they disabled collision damage is so the terrible launch pad mechanic wouldn't kill you 80% of the time, the boss is weak af, the boss wipe mechanic can be brute forced through. The fact that people were clearing the raid on contest flawlessly and with trio is a actual joke. There weren't any DPS checks unless you ran out of ammo. The only good encounter was 3rd encounter. 90% of the armor is ugly, the flawless shader looks like a terrible version of the one Guardian games shader.
-Being forced to use strand in the legend campaign, why
-Nightfalls and Lost Sectors aren't hard they are annoying
-Heavy GLs are still bad
-Soundtrack is forgettable

Now onto the good things about lightfall
+Way Better and easier mod system
+The exotics are pretty dope, especially the raid one
+Strand is pretty fun
+Legacy Focusing makes it so they don't have to bring back old weapons and armor that were already in d2
+Nightfall weapon focusing is so nice, if theres a easy nightfall but trash weapon just grind out mats and focus it when its featured
+LFR meta is gone
+MGs are relevant

overall 6/10 Get this when its on sale for half off\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCAMPAIGN
The story is downright awful, arguably the worst in the series. The missions are still fun on the legendary difficulty, but not as good as the witch queen's campaign. The strand segments are fun but they're sometimes used to distract you from the fact that a good handful of the missions are glorified patrols, similar to pre-WQ campaigns. The missions in the witch queen just flowed better, had more interesting and unique environments, and felt more polished. Tormentors, however, are way better than Lucent Hive. So much better, that I think Lucent Hive should be updated to match them. That being said, none of it matters and they are a waste of time if they don't appear ever again. Overall it's better than most campaigns in the series but falls short of the expectations last year's dlc set for the future, and the quality of the story has me very concerned going into the final year of the saga.

STRAND
The subclasses feel good to play with and kind of carry the campaign. I wish the grapple was more of a movement tool than a "melee grenade" but that's my only real complaint. Definitely in a much better spot than Stasis was when it launched.

POST-CAMPAIGN (NEOMUNA AND SEASON OF DEFIANCE)
Post-campaign content is generally better here than it was in witch queen. Neomuna is a much more rewarding place to spend time in than the throne world was. Terminal overload is absolutely clear of wellspring, but it's no EP (come on man at least have rotating bosses). The Partition missions are significantly better than preservation, which was arguably the worst pinnacle activity in the franchise's history. In fact, I'd say they're even better than some of the LF campaign missions. Season of Defiance is another battlegrounds season, and for what it is it's pretty good. Just 3 new strikes essentially and they're better than their heist/psi-op counterparts. The vexcalibur mission is pretty good, not as good as the other exotic missions though. HyperNet current, the new Neptune strike, is fine I guess. Decent strike. The arms dealer remake is good imo, just makes the strike slightly better. LAKE OF SHADOWS ON THE OTHER HAND IS NOW THE WORST STRIKE IN THE GAME THEY SHOULD NOT HAVE TOUCHED IT. All of that aside, this expansion has made me sick of the Vex Network stuff and I hope we don't see it again, at least for a while. I think the Vex should be taken in a more esoteric direction. Other people seem to like it though, and I'm sure it's a lot easier for bungie to make than most other content.

RAID
I don't think raids should be made for new players anymore, we have vog for that. This raid was already too easy during contest mode and once that was gone it just became an utter joke. Less than half of the team has to engage with any mechanics at any given time, and that's not where we should be 9 years in. This might be a little better if the combat was actually engaging but as with most recent raids, combat difficulty is simply not present in this raid except MAYBE Nezarec chasing you down during contest mode. You could run through this raid with gray weapons and there would be no tangible difference between that and an endgame build. When every other facet of the game got slightly harder in lightfall, for the raid to be THIS easy is insulting. Visually, the raid is stellar. I thought at first it looked way too similar to vow but the second half is absolutely stunning.

OVERALL
Lightfall feels like a dlc that would come out after Beyond Light, and if it had I think it would've been received significantly better, but as it stands it feels like a step back into the era of awful storytelling and mid campaigns. I can't recommend this expansion for $50, and definitely not the annual pass for $100 if the seasons are anything like they have been in the past.
