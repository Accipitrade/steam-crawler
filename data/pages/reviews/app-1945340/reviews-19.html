\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I had to describe the Destiny 2: Lightfall experience in as few words as possible, those words would be: a chore. As someone who has played Destiny as a main game way back in Crota's End (first ever expansion), I've seen how little content can be given out in an expansion that still retains a $20-$30 price tag. There have been plenty of times I felt I wanted more after finishing all there was to offer. After this DLC, I did not want more. I wanted it to be over. Even after finishing D1's campaign, as confused as I might have been, I still felt intrigued and excited to explore the universe.

The campaign overstays its welcome, and on legendary [as a solo player] is damn near unplayable. The modifiers at the bottom of the screen claim to scale difficulty based on party size, but the difference it makes is negligible. You aren't given the new subclass outside of the campaign until you finish the final story mission. This creates huge issues when you run into high octane, high difficulty fight sequences where you're forced to use abilities you've only touched for a total of 10 minutes. Maybe if you've been grinding D2 for every expansion (making sure to pick up every exotic along the way) and tailor your gear's mod setups to match the current meta you may do fine, maybe. As a new or returning player, you simply won't have that option.

And the difficulty is not THE PROBLEM. The problem is where the difficulty comes from. Every single time I had to wipe while playing the campaign it came from two issues; stuffed into a small room with hard-hitting enemies with little cover OR flung off the edge. And most of the respawn points require a good deal of back tracking to actually begin the encounter over, which is 100% a level design problem that should NEVER happen in Destiny. In other games, such as the Souls Franchise, this is an intended mechanic, but it does not work in this game. After dying 29 times and doing the run back to the room 29 times, a small jumping puzzle turns into frustrating nonsense pretty quick.

The story is pretty bland with severe pacing issues and forgettable characters. Even characters that have routinely been pretty cool or interesting in the Destiny story leading up to Lightfall just feel watered down. Every line out of Osiris' mouth for the last 5 story missions is basically the same phrase. Add a "Jar-Jar Binks" tier annoying, temporary side character and you've got the full picture

Finally, as someone who took a break from D2 after Forsaken, the returning player experience is absolutely awful. When I came back after a 4 YEAR hiatus, every single DLC was still full price. All the story missions required to get caught up on the story [if you're not a dirty lore nerd like me] are locked behind a $100+ price tag. Not to mention all the exotics from said expansions are also locked behind the same price tag. This is pretty hard to justify if the game stops receiving updates after the next expansion's life cycle comes to an end, as is speculated but not confirmed.

TLDR: Story is annoying and does not advance the plot. Gameplay for said story is aggravating at best. Returning player experience is dog water. Game could die soon after next expansion. If you're returning and want to play the end of Destiny's story, wait til' Final Shape expansion & watch 'My Name is Byf' on youtube to catch up on the story so far. If you're new and want to get into Destiny 2, you might be a little late and Lightfall certainly not the expansion you'd want to jump onto the sinking ship.\t\t\t\t\t
