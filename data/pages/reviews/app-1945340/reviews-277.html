\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy last review of Lightfall was very Impetuous so I thought I'd make an updated review after playing it for a few more weeks.

If you played the last expansion (The Witch Queen), well, the story is a major step down from Witch Queen. And boy, do I mean major step down. It felt like one big strand tutorial instead of the high stakes race to stop the second collaps like bungie claimed it would be. And even with Lightfall's story being one big strand tutorital, absoltuetly NOTHING is expained lore wise. And I mean nothing. One of the main topics being The Veil. I played through Lightfall twice and I can't tell you a single damn thing about the veil. Only thing I know is that it's somehow connected to the traveler? Even regarding strand, it just kinda pops up out of nowhere. We sort of just find it and then are like "oh wow, guess we have a new subclass!" One of the things I love about this game is the lore and story surrounding the Destiny universe. It's honestly one of the things that keep me coming back. I loved all the lore packed into expansions like Forsaken, Shadowkeep and Witch Queen. Lightfall unfortunately left me starving and craving more.

Strand is mediocre at best. It's so obvious that Strand was intended for Witch Queen instead of Lightfall. While the way you obtain and unlock strand itself is better than how you unlocked Stasis in Beyond Light, obtaining aspects and fragments is arguably worse. You have to spend colossal amounts of time (depending how much you play) to fully unlock Strand. It's a major grind to collect strand meditation to unlock all the other fragments for such a mediocre subclass. I will admit, it's a fun subclass. But if we're talking in terms of endgame or even survivability, compared to the other Four subclasses in the game, strand doesn't hold up very well. Even with all the fancy suspending builds. (at least in my opinion).

When Neomuna was first announced and showcased, I was beyond excited. A bright neon metropolis that looked like something ripped straight out of a retro junkies fantasy. As someone who is utterly obsessed with 80's culture, retro and synth wave, I was officially hyped. I gotta be honest, I was kinda looking forward to feeling like an 80's action hero while playing on Neomuna. But all my expectations and hopes quickly diminished once I first played on Neomuna for a little bit. The destination feels empty. It feels desolate. It feels like it's missing something. Well for starters, all the citizens in Neomuna have uploaded their consciences into the cloud ark. Meaning they live in this virtual version of Neomuna. In my opinion it's simply a lazy excuse so bungie doesn't have to make new models for NPC's and implement them into the game. Not to mention, all the enemies in Neomuna are quite literally bullet sponges. Safe to say my dreams of being this 80s action pack space hero have been butchered by the lazy developers over at bungie.

Overall, Lightfall failed to add anything crucial to the story and the content that it provided is neither fun nor does it provided anything that I want to chase after. As a avid destiny player, I'm extremely disappointed and terrified. Like Byf said, this is suppose to be the start of Destiny's endgame. If this is what we have to look forward to, then destiny's "light and dark saga" is utterly screwed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe campaign is short and completely underwhelming, I dislike almost every aspect of it. The new city and characters feel pointless, the concept of discovering an alien city is severly under-utilised and somehow worse than The Dreaming City which is god knows how old at this point. I felt much more engaged and intrigued by the Throne World of Savathun and the story of the Hive, so this step back is almost unbelievable.
The missions feel more in line with Beyond Light than the Witch Queen, but the story and characters make it worse than Beyond Light ever was. Tension is built through Osiris screaming *spoilers* which fails to bring any enthusiasm to me. This is then torpedoed by other characters in the game and their inane, 'fun' dialogue. I'm glad I didn't buy the deluxe edition for the first time, as I'll finally have a clean break from this franchise. The finale was just as confusing as the rest of the muddied plot.
Finally, settling back into the meat of the game post expansion feels like a chore and comes with the same headaches. If you're on the fence, do not buy this yet. Please. Or buy it off me so I can get my money back.\t\t\t\t\t
