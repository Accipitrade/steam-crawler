\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall is arguably more robust and wider than 2022's Witch Queen, but not larger.

The main pitfalls lie within the narrative and the choices made to deliver a new whimsical story filled with questions at a grave endgame where answers are needed.

Each character we interact with in our efforts to learn strand are hyperbolic in their personalities. The carefree young gun, the grizzled veteran, the grumpy old man. The few heroes we get to interact with lack depth, which is only worsened by them being the only people you will see on Neomuna. Besides a non-descript hologram which will deliver exposition on post-campaign fallout, Neomuna is a lit city with no warmth. Destiny is not Cyberpunk 2077, and a bustling metropolis was never to be expected, but even 2017's Forsaken was able to implement multiple NPCs to the Dreaming City. Creating a city to patrol then saying that all its citizens are hiding in cryopods is contrived, especially for Year 9 of a 10-year saga.
On the other side of the $50 coin, the quality-of-life improvements are massive and, as of right now, still not fully realized. The mod overhaul will take time to experiment with, and loadout will remove barriers from casual players from attempting endgame activities.

The story is done, it cannot be changed. And it’s not great. And the abovementioned QoL changes are free to all players. Should you buy Ligthfall? You probably already have. However, there is still 85% of Season of Defiance to see, a new raid to experience, and three seasons with two dungeons to play with the Deluxe Edition.

Is Lightfall worth $50? Not particularly.
When Included with Season of Defiance? Possibly.
Is the $100 Deluxe Edition worth it? Absolutely.
Without even knowing the other 75% of the Destiny year ahead.

Even with a city with no life, characters with no complexity, and a story with no answers, bungie has shown that they can make a good game while telling a compelling story. Lightfall is a stumble in a series of Ws.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDoes the story have issues so far? Big time. Is this expansion a step down from Witch Queen? Yup. But ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, the people acting like this is one of the worst DLCs in the game and comparing it to stuff like Curse of Osiris are absolutely mental.

Game play is great and the difficulty overhaul they have done has made the day to day content fun and challenging enough so that it is not just a absolute joke anymore. Strand is unique and tons of fun on all classes, although I am slightly skeptical how it will hold up in end game content with it being mobility based and having survivability issues. Campaign is fun and challenging on Legendary although the story is very underwhelming, new QOL features like loadouts are very welcome and useful, the loot pre raid release is decent and brings some interesting new perks, new mod system leaves a bit to be desired and definitely feels like a bit of a downgrade but overall is more streamlined and easier for new players to explore as well. Finally the Guardian ranks & commendation system feels like a huge miss and definitely could use some tweaking to actually feel meaningful.

Overall its more Destiny which has never been a problem. Game play wise the game has been refreshed and is tons of fun, but if you're really on the fence about it no one would question you waiting for a sale or major update to jump in.\t\t\t\t\t
