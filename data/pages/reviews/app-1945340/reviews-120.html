\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not sure if I've ever written a review. I'm pretty bad about it honestly, I consume content, expect it to be great and complain when its not. This time around I'm going to be better and actually define what is wrong with this expansion. This review will contain plenty of ***spoilers*** but I highly recommend reading it. Saving yourself from spoilers with this one and trying it for yourself could waste your money.

I've been a Destiny player since the Alpha of Destiny 1. There have been plenty of times where Bungie missed the mark. We've seen it with raids you can beat in about 20 minutes all the way to whatever they were thinking during the Osiris DLC. Maybe thats the problem, this story line revolves around interactions with Osiris as well. They just have trouble writing solid story lines with him. Even when we found out Savathun was impersonating him for awhile. It didnt make him any cooler, it just developed her story even more. Thats another reason this expansion was such a loss. Witch Queen was such a gain and met all the hype it was given. This was suppose to be even more hyped and most of the time I questioned what was even going on.

The main issue with this whole thing is the story. I'm going to list all of my issues with questions.

If the WItness is essentially the all-powerful "Darkness King" that can manipulate reality, be omnipresent, suppress anyone's power that opposes him, why is he gone, LITERALLY the entire story? He shows up, touches the Traveler, his eyes get real big (yes even bigger), then he tells Calus and his Shadow Legion to go do stuff for him.

Why does he have Calus doing everything for him when he SHOULD be a Thanos level threat that can ruin the galaxy on his own? I mean, sure, get some help for the places you aren't able to be, but pyramid ships should be everywhere. Main characters should be dying. This DLC doesn't feel like a threat once.

Why are we STILL fighting Calus years later and playing a campaign that feels just as impactful as the Red War? Where our light is at risk of being taken but then we fight cabal for 6 hours, fight their boss and then its over. Difference is, apparently we still lost and the Witness got the traveler, but we still have access to our Light supers and Ghosts? Confused yet? Me too but I'm not done.

Why on Earth were the Vex there and then "justified" with one line, "Oh they want the strand too". Oh umm okay cool got it?

Speaking of Strand, what even is a strand? Like okay, ripples of reality or something but you cant just casually bring up these wild topics and not explain them. Our three Light subclasses, given to us by our Machine God Traveler. Okay. Stasis, a Darkness subclass given to us as a gift from the Darkness. Okay, I guess. Strand, a subclass, chillin on a city in Neptune that we've never seen, inhabited by a race that Osiris kind of mentions a couple times and even they don't wield it or even know what it is. Theres a moment where they bring up some Kingdom Hearts type logic and say something about their conection makes the strand solidified to Neptune and there just kind of out said "what is this story?". Still not done.

Speaking of Strand x2, The Shadow Legion (10th "different" version of Cabal in case you're not following) is on this newly found city as well looking for power before we get to it. I'm thinking, okay, so kind of like the Beyond Light story where Eramis wants to use Stasis against us etc etc. Nah, you wrong, they lookin for the Veil. The Veil? Huh?

The Veil. Gun to my head, if I had to explain what The Veil is I'm gone. You know as much as I do. If you haven't played it, you probably know more than I do. Playing it made it harder to explain because they dont explain it. The best part, that makes me laugh while typing this, is the population of Neptune dont even know. They're just like "trust. its important".

More Strand stuff, you get a glimpse of Strand throughout the whole expansion. Why in the World would you not let your player base get it right away and use it the whole time? Theres one moment in a hallway where you use it for about 10 seconds and then you're "exhausted" again and it goes away. Thats what happens until you beat the entire Lightfall campaign, you get exhausted after using it for 30 seconds and then finally you get to use it and grind to unlock everything. It would've made way more sense, to get it right off the rip and unlock things as you're playing through Lightfall. It would've made it way more bearable.

My brain is fried at this point but in the end, The Witness creates the Final Shape, goes into the Traveler and everyone just kind of watches and says "Dang". On the theme of Thanos level threat, The Witness basically snapped his fingers but instead of literally anything happening, you just go back to doing your weekly missions until the raid drops and hopefully thatll answer some questions.

If anyone answers these questions with "DiD yOu ReAd ThE lOrE?!" I shouldn't have to. This is a major story expansion. I didn't have to read a thing to understand Witch Queen. Start to finish that DLC was solid. This one is a bag of mess with so many moments where you're left scratching your head.

Thanks for reading my first review. Considering I spent $100 for the annual pass version I felt pretty compelled to write it after what I just experienced. They cant miss the mark like this anymore. Especially with how many other solid games are dropping left and right this year. I HIGHLY recommend spending your money elsewhere. For those that already paid for it, lets hope the seasons keep us around and add better well explained content.\t\t\t\t\t

While shadowkeep certainly had it's issues - the story and feel of the expansion felt a lot better than this one.

Strand is cool and all, but not worth the price of entry imo.\t\t\t\t\t

I can understand some of the issues people have with the campaign story, (I mean seriously, even after seeing it, wtf is the “Veil”) it was not nearly as bad as some earlier reviews said. I thought the pacing of the overall story, although slightly rushed towards the end, was still something that mostly made sense from my perspective. If I had to change anything though, I would’ve preferred another mission or two to bridge the gap between learning more about strand and the final boss, but hindsight is 20/20.

The strangest thing to me, was how little the vanguard actually talked to the Guardian throughout the campaign, instead the majority of our contact was with Osiris and the Cloudstriders. Having Osiris to guide through our exploration and fighting on Neomuna was pretty cool. He’s certainly been humbled with the events of last season and has been a much warmer guy. Having a couple scenes and lines of dialogue on his well-being had me feeling a little sad for him, but he has people around to comfort him.

The Cloudstriders, and specifically Nimbus, did have me letting out a couple chuckles from the pure absurdity of their dialogue, but I understand why they were so light hearted. I can’t really remember the last time a character was purely or mostly humour based, but Nimbus’ personality and tendency for quips was a little out of place at worst, and usually capable of a chuckle at best, so I didn’t mind his character.

On the other side, the enemies present in the campaign, specifically the vex and shadow legion, were more of the same, with shadow legion getting a fresh coat of paint (and incendiors becoming infinitely more annoying because of their stupid boss level knock back stomp). However, as a small addition that they got on top of their new paint job, were some new darkness emitting shield generating backpacks, which were pretty cool I guess, but ultimately acted as just another small shield, like the moths in Witch Queen, just without the explosives. Aside from the purple turtles and brass robots, the witness released more of his own home brewed forces, the tormentors. Featuring a void based move set and multiple stages to their fights, they can put up a pretty good fight (but, whoever made the decision to have tormentors be susceptible to finishers deserves a raise). While not too terribly scary on their own, at multiple points in the campaign you’re stuffed into a small room with up to two tormentors and a bucket full of cabal, making each tormentor fight exciting and somewhat hard.

The small previews of Strand throughout the campaign were actually sometimes less so forced and more so a suggestion, as although sometimes you were locked in to only using strand, a lot of encounters have it to you more as an option rather than the go-to. Of course this doesn’t apply in any missions where you’re forced to use Strand the whole way through, but gameplay variety is always nice.

Strand itself was a lot of fun to mess around with during missions when there was increased ability regen, but otherwise, the cooldowns on abilities were a little too long for the minor effects they had. Prime example: the grapple was fine during missions where it would recharge basically immediately, but the one minute plus cooldown outside the campaign felt a little awkward, but it is made up for with a good couple ways to refund ability energy. On top of this, melee abilities with 3 charges are kind of fun, but good lord does it take a while to recharge.

Weapon wise, they were pretty cool, and Quicksilver Storm was my most used weapon for the first couple missions, but I quickly swapped back to other options from before Lightfall later on in the campaign. I feel like a couple Neomuna weapons have good potential, but I never had any I deemed worth using.

Ultimately, the fun factor of the campaign kind of made up for some of the story beats and the characters were nice, if some should’ve been featured more. The enemies did pose an actual threat, but the introduction of Strand and a decent array of interesting Neomuna weapons helped put massively, so my final score for the DLC and Neomuna would have to be 7/10.

P.S. Legendary campaign was absolutely some of the most fun I’ve ever had playing a game, period.\t\t\t\t\t
