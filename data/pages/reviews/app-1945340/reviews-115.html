\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing Lightfall and experiencing the new strand, weapons, and location, I wanted to provide some constructive criticism on the narrative. While the new additions are great, I found the storyline to be lacking and unoriginal. It felt like filler content, reminiscent of a generic Marvel movie, and I was surprised that Bungie would produce something so underwhelming in today's competitive storytelling landscape. The campaign was short, leaving me with more questions than answers. Now, I don't mind a game keeping some secrets to build suspense, as I am a fan of games like Death Stranding. However, in Lightfall, all we seem to be doing is protecting the veil, and despite the importance attached to it, we have no idea what it is. It appears everyone else knows, but the player is left in the dark. I am hoping that the raid will provide some much-needed clarity and serve as the saving grace of this DLC.

As for strand, new weapons, and other features, I will reserve my opinions until I have tested them thoroughly. However, at this point, I cannot recommend this DLC to someone new to the game. Even seasoned players like myself are left feeling confused and frustrated with the lack of story progression and excessive filler content. Overall, Lightfall has not met my expectations, and I hope that future DLCs will have a stronger focus on narrative development.

Update (April 18th, 2023):
I have revised my review of the Lightfall DLC and the current season of Destiny 2. Upon playing the DLC further, I must admit that it has a lot to offer. The weapons are unique and can be used in the meta, Strand for the Hunter is fun to use, and the changes made to the class building system have made each class feel different and unique. Although I still believe that dumbing down the armor perks was not a good idea, it does help players understand the perks better.

My previous review focused mainly on the campaign, which I found to be lacking in narrative depth and full of reused content. However, the end-game content is decent and offers players more to do than in previous seasons. I appreciate that Bungie has used characters like Asher Mir, who have been underutilized in the game's story, and I hope to see more of this in the future.

However, the current season is a different story. The death of Amanda Holliday, while tragic, felt unimpactful in terms of the game's plot. As for the current season, it is lackluster and unfortunate. While I appreciate the teaming up of characters like Mara Sov and the implementation of Devrim Kay into the story, the gameplay for the seasonal activities is mediocre at best. Playing with randoms can be a chore as they often do not know what they are doing, slowing down progress and causing frustration.

In conclusion, while there are a lot of things that Lightfall has to offer, there are also some glaring issues that Bungie needs to address. The Lightfall DLC has its strengths, but the campaign is lacking in narrative depth. The current season is mediocre at best. While my criticism of the DLC and the current season still stands, I recognize that there is a lot that the game has to offer, and I hope that Bungie can learn from their mistakes and improve in the future.

On a somber note, it's important to acknowledge the passing of Lance Reddick, who was a brilliant actor and voice actor. He had a significant impact on the game's narrative, voicing the character Zavala, who was an important figure in the game's story. Reddick brought a level of gravitas and nuance to the character that made him one of the most beloved characters in the game. As a fan of his work, I am deeply saddened by his passing, and I know that he will be missed by the Destiny community and beyond. His contributions to the game will not be forgotten, and he will always be remembered for his incredible talent and dedication to his craft. Rest in peace.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen I play this expansion, I think "something bad happened here."

I don't have proof, but it feels like this content was pulled out of its intended place in the development cycle to give the new subclass, 'Strand', an expansion to release with before The Final Shape comes in to close out the Light and Dark saga. As a result Lightfall suffers at almost every level. Lots of things we normally expect from these releases aren't coming out this year, from new gear and weapons, the ARG/raid puzzle and secret lore book, new maps for pvp modes, etc. The campaign is the biggest disappointment; tons of cutscenes and lines are being re-used and re-mixed from an entirely different development timeline and unfortunately those seams show Hard. Everything feels strangely out of place, off-tone, inappropriate and abrupt to the story we've been building up to for the last ten years. Neomuna as a location didn't even exist in lore until yesterday, and there simply isn't enough done to ingratiate it into Destiny's world, particularly when we're supposed to also be focusing on a "potential apocalypse" back on earth.

It's not all bad. I have enjoyed what I've played of the new subclass. If I tilt my head just right, I can glimpse some of the magic Lightfall might have had as a new setting to kick off a bombastic, post Light-and-Dark saga Destiny world. On an individual basis, the mission quality is sometimes quite higher than your average campaign mission from Beyond Light or Shadowkeep. Caiatl's presence in the story is the one time signal wins out over noise, and it further endeared me to her character. (Probably because we have actual context and investment re: what's going on with her.) I noticed an interesting thematic through-line about pain and control that, I think, proves someone cared about this story and these characters. However, much like a shattered vase even good materials can't hold water.

Some people may have reasons to buy and even enjoy Lightfall, but I can't imagine that being true for the average person. Since QoL and mechanical updates are free, the campaign and patrol zones are the bread and butter of an expansion purchase. Destiny 2 takes a lot of investment to really enjoy, and at least in my experience, invested players have too many reasons to feel burned by this release. I'm happy for the people that are getting something from this expansion, but for the rest of you, stay away-- at least until it goes on sale. For those lore/raid/worldbuilding fans looking for consolation, hopefully we'll be able to look back on this as Destiny 2's gas leak episode.\t\t\t\t\t
