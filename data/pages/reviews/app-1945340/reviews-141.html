Updated 3/17/23 (at the bottom)

Overall campaign:
Half baked story that adds a new destination. The story focuses more on the new subclass than anything else. Even Bungie knew how old it was getting with the strand stuff and added in a training montage to acquire this powers right before the final mission(s). But hey, we love those training montages am I right? (no). Definition of bad pacing right there. However, the usage of strand in the campaign was very limited but was also extremely fun with how they reduced the cool downs by 30000%. Throughout the 4 hours it took to beat the campaign, it explained nothing. The new characters were fine but added little to the overall story. One was extremely predictable and the other the standard lighthearted character. People complain way too much about Nimbus; they're fine. Big bad was big sad with how fast we clapped their cheeks. Turned someone who has been apart of Destiny into a literal joke. Where did all that power from the witness go? Into a gun? That's our thing, we use the guns. I was expecting to be slapped with answers harder than my dad slaps but instead left with more questions. This honestly felt like a worse seasonal story without explanations, and with a new destination spread out across multiple missions. One can only hope the seasons carry this expansion.

Season 20:
Season 20 so far is looking great. Its week 1 there is not much to add on top of that.

Strand? Its freaking amazing right out the gates. Hopefully bungo won't nerf it as they already nerfed it when you got it out of the campaign.

Overall Bungie has made SEVERAL improvements to the overall game but the expansion's campaign was extremely lackluster. Its mid but idk buy it if you want.

Week 2 updated opinion:
Bungie has done a PHENOMENAL job at improving the game. The new subclass, Strand, is extremely fun, balanced, and just overall great. The story for this expansion did suck but I can safely say after all that I am having an absolute blast.

3/17/23
Day one was too easy. Contest mode is for the hardcore players, that is why it is CONTEST mode. Other than that Raid super fun. After exploring a bit more into the lore that lightfall provides, I can say that this "expansion" is pretty alright. By no means does this mean that the story presented in the campaign is NOT doggy doo doo, but the lore given after it/in the surrounding missions/loot are substantial enough to satisfy me. Power levels feel weird on Neomuna because having an 1810 raid auto rifle tickle red bars is not good imo. Overall, this still feels like a season + new subclass instead of a genuine expansion. Still having a blast though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo... this is hard for me to do because I love this franchise, but I would have to say to avoid this for now. Sadly, while it still has its moments, the story is rushed, unexplained, and kinda short. The new subclass is neat, but it doesn't justify diverting from the BIG moment of the Witness showing up. The best way I can describe this story is a side quest you decide to do right before you have to go fight the big bag. Not to mention, the side quest has NO explanation, and the ONE character that should be asking all the important questions doesn't, has "character growth" off screen, and is just and upset angy grandpa the whole time, while the other character you deal with is annoying with toddleresque decision making and just makes the story feel like a joke. The ONLY cool character drops an important part of his own tale, and it instantly makes him an expendable character, and bungie does just that, making his sacrifice not feel important whatsoever. The whole story from opening to ending cutscene feels like it happens in a matter of maybe a couple hours, and at the end, nobody seems to give a ♥♥♥♥. They have the general sentiment of "well that happened. oh well" and on top of that, it feels like there are no repercussions to what happened at all. Byf put out a good video on all the bad parts of this story and I gotta say hes pretty damn spot on, and it influenced this review a bit, as his points there opened my eyes after playing this "expansion."

The subclass, its ok, but you have to grind out a new currency to unlock the aspects, and i get it, but it doesn't explain too well how to get said currency, and it feels like it takes a long time to earn enough to buy one. Theres also a lack of aspects right now, but that will change after the raid (hopefully.)

All that bad out of the way, they did do some things really good. Adding in hints of another character (though he will probably be the raid boss and sort of takes away that mystery entirely), the new zone is stunning along with the music, weapons and armor are well designed including exotics (though i wish the new destination based weapons had an auto or scout rifle but oh well). But this good doesn't out weigh the bad enough for me. Ill still play, because I want to feel like i got my moneys worth of play out of the game, but I honestly sort of dread playing through the story on my two other characters right now. Probably will just skip what I can and just power through it, and usually i watch them all a second time to see if I missed anything, but this left me so irritated that I don't want to do that with this story.

TL;DR - Story is such a disappointment that I'm not sure if I want to play through on other characters. Good parts don't out weigh the bad, and this is just not the best Bungie could have put forth. Not worth the money right now, get it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

Barely any questions answered and more questions formed (unless questions got answered in lore books/exotics which I haven't gotten to yet).

Hopefully the Seasons for the year make up for Lightfall and hopefully doesn't influence the practice for Final Shape.

Bungie, if you do actually read the reviews and see mine: thank you for bringing back Queen-themed weapons and gear. However, please do not do the same thing in Final Shape. Give us a epic and meaningful conclusion to the Light and Dark fight we been raging for about a decade: or I'll Strand you.\t\t\t\t\t
