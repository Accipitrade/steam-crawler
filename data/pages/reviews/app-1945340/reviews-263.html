\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnderwhelming.

For the "beginning of our End" it seemed pretty plane. The gameplay felt good, the campaign was not as good as witch queen but better than anything before that and it looked phenomenal. Nevertheless, it felt completely disconnected from the calamity everywhere else in the system now that the "darkness is at our doorsteps". The story finale underlined that. The moment the witness is there he is gone (i am aware that he will semingly be the final boss of final shape but meh).

Neomuna is cool, but also has the problem of detachment. Why Neomuna, yeah i has the veil but in the end it didnt really matter. It felt like Bungie just wanted to have a cool vaporwave sci-fi experience and therefore picked neptun and created/ designed strand (grapple cooldown should be reduced in pve) the way it is. I am not saying that that is not cool but how already said, it feels detached from the catastrophy on going in the background.

Bungie tried to create an infinity war for final shape as their end game, but utterly failed (sadly).

Additionally its form of wrapping the content hasnt changed a bit. 2 armor exos per class, not even a handful of new exo weapons and all of them only obtainable through quests. I was already salty that there were almost no new exos in the season of seraph and hoped that lightfall would close that gap but... To elaborate my critisism on the exo quests: On the one hand it makes the exos easier obtainable through a generic and scripted way to receive them, but on the other hand exactly that took the spirit from exo weapons. (Reminder: The last new exo, whether armor or not, we didnt get trough a scripted way i think should be only cloudstrike in beyondlight (ecxept raids and dungeons). Quests are not bad in general but if there is one i dont want it to be revealed earlier as i can do it (strand pistol/ stasis glaive) and then they should be depending on the exo at least very special. With easier access to new exos their quests turn just into something you do to have them in the collection and only use them if they are good or the weapon was buffed in some form.

The only hope is the raid and the seasons (which i dont lay much hope on).
Not worth the money, but if you really want it, wait for a discount.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t**LIGHTFALL CAMPAIGN SPOILERS**

Updated for Lightfall.

This is a preliminary review for Lightfall after completing the campaign. This review will only cover the campaign.

The Campaign

This is the second DLC campaign released with the option for legendary difficulty, something that was well received in the Witch Queen expansion as it added difficulty without requiring you to use specific loadouts.

Unfortunately, this isn't replicated in Lightfall. The difficulty is a cake walk for the first three missions, even heavily underleveled. The real frustration comes from the last two missions, the difficulty scaling is immense and the game just throws bullet sponge enemies at you. This wouldn't be so bad if the checkpoints were fair, but the checkpoints I encountered whilst playing always took me back about 15 - 20 minutes of gameplay. The last boss in particular has two phases, each phase solo takes about 15 minutes, and there's only one checkpoint at the beginning of the encounter and far too many ways for you to just randomly die or get booped off the map. I struggle to believe a QA tester sat through this mission solo on legendary and thought "Yeah that's a reasonable level of difficulty."

The Campaign as a whole is incredibly confusing, filled with macguffins that are never explained to you. I'm honestly sitting here fresh after finishing the campaign and I still have no idea what the campaign was even about, there's a lot of "we must destroy this device before it's too late" but it's never actually explained to us what anything actually is. Why should I destroy it? What would happen if I didn't? What the hell is even going on? None of this is really explained. Most of the campaign either boils down to a strand tutorial or 'macguffin A needs to be destroyed otherwise it'll affect macguffin B and that'll be bad because of reasons'. What?

Also what the hell is The Veil?

The only familiar face in this campaign is Osiris, who is particularly obnoxious in this and he seems to belittle you every chance he gets. The new Cloud Strider characters are cringe-worthy. It's the typical grizzled vet partnered with the overzealous rookie trope and it's done particularly poor here. The dialogue is completely tone deaf and cringe worthy, on the levels of current Marvel movies and is also totally out of place given the gravity of the situation we're facing.

Rohan, one of the Cloud Striders who is some sort of grizzled veteran is killed a couple missions into the campaign. I think I heard him speak a total of about 10 voice lines by the time he died, and when he did I wasn't torn up or bothered about it, it was simply a case of "Well, I guess that happened". Then there's Nimbus, who is the over-eager rookie Cloud Strider, who also doesn't seem too bothered about his mentor being killed either, as only a mission or so after it happens he's back to cracking Marvel-level one-liners.

For a expansion with a name as ominous as "Lightfall" given the connotations of "Light" in the context of the game, to amount to a strand tutorial and not much else is incredibly disappointing.

I would put this DLC, campaign-wise at least, on the same level as Curse of Osiris, and we all know how that was.

All this in conjunction with the gutted build system really feels like I've paid full price for a worse version of a game I already own, because of that I cannot recommend this.\t\t\t\t\t
