\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI say this with a heavy heart, but Lightfall is probably the biggest letdown in Destiny's history. This was meant to be the penultimate chapter of the Light vs Darkness Saga, but not only was nearly every bit of narrative potential dropped, we were not given a single answer to any of the questions that Bungie has been asking us for years, and worst of all it seems like most of the lessons learned from Witch Queen were tossed out the window. This was an absolutely critical point in Destiny's history and the narrative team should be ashamed because they couldn't have picked a worse time to fumble the ball as hard as they did.

On top of the mistreatment of the story we have seen a $10 increase to the base expansion and an additional $10 increase to the annual pass, and I just want to ask, where is this money going? Its certainly not going towards the writing staff, and its definitely not going towards new earnable cosmetics because the cash shop seems to be getting bigger season by season. In fact I believe that there is less content in this expansion than both Shadow Keep and Witch Queen. It has to be noted that Bungie is no longer an indie studio and I see no way in which they can justify a price hike when they now have Sony in their corner, and especially when they are offering less in this package than its predecessor. I am certain that Bungie have lost a lot of the good-will that they have been building with their customers since Beyond Light, because if this is what they're giving us leading up to Final Shape I would be very scared of what is to come.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIdk man. I enjoyed the campaign on the first playthrough. Sat my ass down for around 6-7 hours (with breaks) and got solo legend finished. I did enjoy it at the time, but hours after beating it...I kept thinking about some of the plot points and how it kinda felt forced since I didn't really understand what was going on.

"The Veil" Problem:
The Veil is an important story element. Its what we're fighting to get control of and the ultimate goal of the story...and its almost not explained like at all. Why does it matter? Once we find it how do we use it to our advantage? How will it stop the second collapse? The answer is: who knows? How are we supposed to care if we're not told WHAT it is and WHY its a big deal?

Mods Overhaul:
I like the new menu and its layout. Its confusing to get a hang of but im slowly getting it. like it or not i'll have to get used to it.

Strand:
I think its fun, but its also kinda pointless at least right now. I don't get the hate for the subclass besides the fact its weak.

The Witness and Calus
Oh man this is the best part of the whole DLC. WQ left us only knowing the witness on a surface level. We still dont know much but they feel like a real villain now. Any cutscene with TW and Calus was awesome.

If you haven't bought the dlc and are on the fence about it, then just wait for it to go on sale. You're only missing on some cool cutscenes (that are on youtube now). Lets hope the seasonal content at least makes this next year worth something.

First Impressions:
Honestly? This expansion was really disappointing. I wouldn't recommend any of my friends spend money on this one.
I expected this campaign to be a step down from Witch Queen, because of course it was going to be really hard to live up to an expansion as amazing as that.
But.. damn. It feels like this wasn't a step down - it was a complete fall.
So much hype went into this one for me - Destiny finally taking a darker tone. A new movement-based subclass that can be really fun to mess around with. Calus coming back. Some non-binary representation. Overall quality of life upgrades.
All of this, and it really felt like we got the worst of every one of these things.
The darker tone was completely botched and could only be felt in the opening and ending scenes. Strand is hardly even a viable option in comparison to the other subclasses.
We only get TWO scenes with Calus - two pretty decent ones that open up his character a considerable amount for what they were, but for an expansion's main antagonist, you'd expect more. Hell, I felt more for Eramis back in Beyond Light than I felt for Calus here - and we have had YEARS with Calus, only for all of that to basically go to waste (unless Bungie has more plans for him, which I doubtfully hope for).
Nimbus could have been better. Overall, kind of annoying, and completely contradicted the tone that the story was trying to set.
Finally, the quality of life upgrades: Great in some areas, very, VERY bad in others. Being able to save a loadout is fantastic, and it was something I was looking forward to for a while.
That being said, the new artifact system feels like I just watched my car get stolen. We had a great thing going here that was pretty good, but everything good about it was completely stripped away in favor of an over-simplified waste of time.
Overall, this dlc makes me feel one thing: Robbed.
I feel robbed.
I feel like there was so, so much potential here to do good. I feel like this could have been a great follow-up from the Witch Queen and a proper lead into the Final Shape... and we were robbed of it.

My last glimmer of hope for Destiny 2 moving forward is this thought: They are currently putting more effort into our next expansion, and couldn't afford to put as much care into this one as they would have liked.
Destiny is known to do this - Season of the Drifter (bad) was followed by Season of Opulence (pretty damn good). Season of the Worth (very, very bad) was followed by Season of the Arrivals (really good, personally my favorite season).
Maybe, just maybe, the same thing could be happening here. And maybe the Final Shape will be better, maybe even good.

No matter what, I know for a fact I'll still play. But it's with a heavy heart, and one less metaphorical car.\t\t\t\t\t
