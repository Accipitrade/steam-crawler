\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe one good thing I'll give to Lightfall is that the Strand subclasses are really good (for now). Most everything else about Lightfall comes across about as well as a wet fart on a Sunday morning at church.

The story narrative is very bad and disappointing (embodied by a meme of Lightfall's story line being 3 separate acts of "What the f is the Veil?"). Personally, I didn't have high expectations of the narrative after the narrative of one of the season's from 2 seasons ago had a guy wake up from a coma by making tea using the relics of a dead god (which is probably one of the dumbest things I've ever heard and that's considering that much of Destiny 2 can be explained as "space magic").
Much of the narrative is plagued with some weird choices, a point in particular where we supposedly "mastered" Strand and then immediately couldn't wield it properly anymore (anyone who's played the campaign knows the bit I'm talking about and how absolutely dumb and meaningless that entire "cutscene" is), and a similar sort of plague that stories like the Star Wars Prequels have where you *know* that everything ends in tragedy (the DLC is named "Lightfall." I hope I don't need to explain it).

The gameplay fares a little better than the story but arguably not by much. The best way I can describe it is that you don't need to be a chef to understand that if you take a simple steak and add 30 different types of spices or other such things at once, it'll be harder to figure out what specific things made the steak taste good or bad to the person eating it.

In Lightfall's case, they attempted to increase the difficulty across the game in order to try and "ease" people out of the current "gear score" type system we have with light level. The problem with this is many areas fell over-tuned and even your basic (chill out) type activities like patrols on Neomuna (the new area) feel annoying at times. The most infamous bug with Lightfall so far being a bug that has existed in Destiny 2 since Forsaken that is assumed to be tied to frame rate which makes certain enemies (the most infamous being Cabal gunships with their missiles) hit multiple times with a single attack and leading to you being 1 shot by a single missile that normal does 30 damage for your effective 200 hp (going based on PvP numbers for HP as a reference point).
Obviously, this results in 2 major issues with all of the Lightfall areas: Lower end players (I'm "mid-tier" in that I actually do raids sometimes but I don't raid often) who aren't specced out and optimized are put in an "adapt or die" situation while higher end players get the difficulty increase they want.
The irony in this regard is that some of the more "standard" activities you are required to do (like legend lost sectors) if you want to get into that "higher end" gameplay are now stuck at a power delta of -10 (effectively similar to being 5 levels lower than the enemies at all times) and the base light for enemies there are either at or above the max light you can attain via normal gear means, making even basic legend lost sectors a slog due to enemies doing massive amounts of damage to you for you even looking at them the wrong way.
The final statement I'll give to PvE gameplay in this regard with Lightfall is the Day 1 version of the Lightfall raid where everyone is light capped to 10 below at all times in a difficult race to see who finishes first was *easier* than a legend lost sector since Lightfall dropped. Do with that information as you will.

So far I've been going over the bad aspects of Lightfall but I do want to note that there are some good points Bungie gets with Lightfall:
Firstly, quality of life with the mods system has improved by making all of the mods utilize one singular system known as "Armor Charge," which is acquired from picking up orbs of light (power but I'm a D1 vet so I just call them by their original name) in normal gameplay anyways. The side effect of this is that a significant portion of those mods were nerfed in order to compensate (which you can feel the nerfs if you were one of the "higher end players" before Lightfall dropped but you have to juggle way less stuff now).
All in all a good change imo but Bungie still has a problem with their tutorials being worse or just as bad than their main "competitors" (i.e. Warframe) so your mileage may vary (YMMV).
Secondly, quality of life with acquiring specific weapons is way better in a bunch of ways. Most world drop exotics (or all the exotics you've previously acquired) can drop from a new event on Neptune that involves a Vex Strike Force. I've only seen a couple of these as they are extremely rare due to the variable time duration before the next one arrives but they drop guaranteed exotics (no idea if they drop any of the lost sector exotics or the new ones and I've not seen anyone be able to test it due to the event's rarity).
There's also a new system with each of the main vendors for the main playlist activities (strikes, gambit, PvP, and sweaty PvP) where they hold the earned engrams from their stuff for you and you can force your way into getting old weapons that aren't in the current loot pool and "force your way" to a roll you want as long as you've acquired it once before. Nice.

The third good thing I'd like to talk about with Lightfall is weekly objectives at each vendor have been revamped (alongside a difficulty increase to strikes) so some of them are easier to do now (sweaty PvE strikes known as nightfalls have an easier objective for their weekly than before).
The catch is that some of the stuff in the strikes playlist takes longer and Bungie f-ed up royally by adding Heist Battlegrounds (the playlist activity from last season) as nightfalls without accounting for the fact that said Battlegrounds have infinitely respawning enemies that get buffed from the nightfall modifiers in an activity that was never designed to be a nightfall. F-ing *oof.*

Now, there's a whole other section I *can* add here about all of the nerfs Bungie proceeded to give with Lightfall and the most recent nerfs post Lightfall launch but, all I'm gonna say is a few of the things nerfed on top of the most recent nerf to a build in the most recent hotfix (at the time of writing) have made me take a stance that Warlocks can complain about Titans and Hunters being OP when their Starfire Protocol builds that have been OP as all h* for the past 3 seasons finally get nerfed. And I can safely say at this point that it's more likely Starfire will get an ornament before Bungie nerfs it.

I'll finally conclude this massive ranting diatribe about how Lightfall sucks with this: Lightfall on the gameplay side has seriously convinced me (on top of the recent nerf to the Synthoceps Glaive build because of Winterbite being way too strong when it released) that Bungie either doesn't play test their own game or their play testers were ignored/not qualified enough.
Winterbite as an exotic weapon proves that to me because Bungie somehow didn't think a heavy weapon glaive that has synergy with an infamous melee exotic (Synthoceps) on top of being able to slow (and eventually freeze and shatter) targets on melee hit wouldn't be abused to whatever extent it could be abused honestly tells me Bungie not only has no idea who their playerbase is and how they play, it also tells me Bungie didn't have any type of foresight that a weapon like that would be super strong from the get-go on the melee alone.

Edit: I should add this tidbit as well: If Lightfall's story and the rumors/theory that Final Shape and Lightfall were both supposed to be 1 singular DLC originally, it explains Lightfall's story being as incoherent as it is sometimes and I honestly would've preferred Bungie delay it so both of them could release as 1 in order for people to understand everything better. Obviously, Bungie chose to rush Lightfall and go for money over waiting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I think the story was a bit cringe, I don't think that is the main issue with this expansion.

Bungie has made everything used to farm exotics unobtainable for casual players. Lost sectors, which already weren't much fun farming for a tiny chance at exotics, now require significantly higher light level than the soft cap. Even after soloing the legend campaign and doing all of the weekly quests, I will still need to farm for 30+ hours for minor upgrades in order to do lost sectors at the legend level.

While I believe they made steps in the right direction for the seasonal artifact, everything else is 3 steps back. They've made everything way more difficult, and requiring significantly more farming to even attempt at the level it is designed at. While hardcore players may not have a problem with this, and I myself enjoy a challenge, I do not like having to do busy work for weeks in order to be at a light level in order to do base activities.

Coming after Witch Queen was already a tough act to follow, but this game feels like it needed an additional 1+ year in development for it to even hold a candle. It feels rushed, and it feels completely against what the community has asked for. Whoever is in charge of these changes, I hope you take a look at how you do things. While this will likely satisfy the 4+ hour a day gamer, for us that are casual at maybe 8 hours a week, this is an embarrassment.

This is, in my opinion, worse than even than Curse of Osiris, which I thought was the worst expansion created for any game. Maybe they will be able to fix it, and I hope they do. But as of now, I don't think anybody should play this expansion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t5/10 rating. 6000 hours. I play alot. I've played since d1 beta days. First off, anyone who tries to defend this expansion with BUT STRAND IS GREAT AND SO ARE THE QOL's (quality of life), I want you to really take the time to think about what you're talking about. QOL is NOT part of the expansion, was it delivered with it YES, but you could be playing the base game with no lightfall purchase and you'd get those same QOLs. The seasonal content is NOT part of lightfall. You buy it separately! so the season pass, exotic seasonal missions, activity, and etc are not included.

Pros of lightfall:

1. Strand: strand is fun, it has introduced new mobility tech, its has its HOT moments, but it also has very mid moments that make you switch to a different subclass. but overall strand is fine and can totally be nasty.
2. Exotics: the new exotics weapons are cool but are very mid. LMG is a support weapon but no one used it Day1 on the raid so that should tell you that is NOT good enough to replace any other support option for endgame, maybe gms? who knows. Sidearm is very cool but not meta breaking. glaive is by far the best thing so far but has already been locked out once from being used in endgame and at the time that i'm typing this review, its still bugged and will most likely be locked out again so you cant even use it in endgame again. armor: i'm not sure if its locked to the dlc or if you could get it drop anyway but the armor does come with the lightfall campaign if you beat it on legendary so its been addressed in the review. The exotics are cool but should already be part of the subclass itself IMO and strand would feel much better but its almost like they took core features from the subclass (like fragments) and made exotics instead. but the exotics overall are are good, they are new and unique they achieved the base goal of an exotic. I may never use them but destiny sandbox changes all the time who knows.
3. raid is very cool thematically

Cons:
The entire campaign is TRASH.
We went from Witch queen and to this rushed and bare bones strand the tutorial side quest. Yes shadowkeep wasn't hot but it was 30 bucks and was before WQ came out. WQ shows that bungie can craft well addressed themes and stories.
This is the SECOND to LAST expansion in the light/dark saga. We should not be getting more questions but actual answers. The whole reply from bungie that the "veil" will be addressed throughout seasons is the stupidest thing i've ever heard. The world is ending and you've lost but hey tune back in for the next episode of dragon ball z! Seasons are supposed to be supplemental to fill in the gap, the CORE story is supposed be in the main expansions. The veil was the biggest macguffin i've ever seen in a video game. WE need to stop the Witness from getting the THING, so he can't do the THING, but the THING can't be destroyed because Neomuna need its, but we also can't allows links to the THING because THE THING will do The Thing THAT the witness wants the THING to do so that HE can do His THING and We'll have to stop him from the THING in the final shape. Thats lightfall in a very crude nutshell. Osiris is made to be an angry old man who wants to rush everything, he seems to know the importance of the veil but never tells us why outside the fact that witness wants it but it's never explained how osiris new the witness wants it, like is the witness broadcasting his words and thought with calus to everyone??? but magically osiris knows we need to follow calus and grab the "veil". strand is immediately jammed in your face out of no where. Beyond light made sense because the main villian uses strand, here its only WE used it, not even the people of neomuna have SEEN STRAND or tried using it. "but they aren't guardians" okay sure, but eramis wasn't either and could use stasis so it's all based on the writers of the story and thus shows that the story strand was trash. we had a few tutorial moments in Beyond light for stasis that was okay it wasn't bad, but Lightfall continues to give and take strand with tutorial moments that make the whole game seems like a strand simulator and takes away from the core threat that is the witness. BUT THEY'LL NEVER SEE STRAND COMING is the shoehorned reason we seek it out. The legend story was challenging in gameplay, but the core environment was sometimes stupid and annoying especially certain fights like the final strand training with the stupid vex wall moving around where it almost immediately killed you when you touched it, took me out of the story and made me hate playing that level. threshers are the new bosses in the game for some stupid reason. in fact, threshers are harder than calus was for the final boss. calus was made to be this monster who now has the power of the witness and all he does is shoot little darkness bolts and a darkness mini gun which split into cleavers for the second phase. he literally is just a massive colossus and gladiator. Even Savanthun was more interesting. Yes did she have the same abilities as hive guardians and even Ghaul from d2 Y1. But savanthun has other things you had to do on her second phase and she was very challenging if you were not prepared and in a group she was THICK. but calus can be cheesed by swinging around with strand or standing on one of the podiums near him and he cant touch you. I found the tormentors more of a threat than the final boss. nimbus and rohan, man if there was ever a moment that something should been saved for a season its gotta be that fact that rohan dies. I dont care. who is he? idk some dude we just met. save his death for the later seasons where we have grown attached to him. Also nimbus is annoying, but its not nearly as bad as other people say he is. overall story was a 3!!!!! a 3/10. yes it was worse than beyond light because beyond light introduced us to the darkness and explained to us that darkness is not an actual enemy but something that can be used. it has purpose. Lightfall is supposed to be the LAST road bump to the climax. It needed to be BIG and it was stinky. We little to no interaction from the vanguard, mara, mithrax, anna, the exo stranger, and caitl is barely involved in the game save a few moments. This should have been a big story with the team working together but instead we have osiris losing his mind and having a few come to jesus moments.

Guardian ranks: these have elements that are tied to the lightfall campaign so its been addressed. They are essentially pointless. They reset EVER season, NOT year. Who cores then? oh you took the time to knock out specific challenges, cool no one cares. especially when everyone starts out at rank 6. i have 6000 hours and i'm the same rank as someone who just started last season like WTF. ranks should be like call of duty prestige ranks, they keep going up each season adding to the rank you earned from last season. but no we reset them for some stupid reason.

pvp: LOL did lightfall add anything that isn't free?
Gambit: just content vault this mode already only 100 people care at this point.
trials: its trials nothing special.

the raid: listen, i'm totally an elitist, but i do not care how many people get the day1 emblem, and i was actually relieved it didn't take long to finish the raid. but bungie needs to figure out what contest mode is supposed to be! There is no shot a master lost sector or nightfall is HARDER than a day1 raid. They made everything incredibly harder but made day1 a cake walk LOL. The final shape has to step up with a bang. I personally tag the raid with the campaign because i don't think there is really anything that is meta in this raid. armor is cool thematically but i'm not giving or taking points away on the raid experience

literally strand is what gives this expansion a +2, ironically bungie sacrificed most of the story for strand and strand ended up being the only good thing about the expansion. this is a very low 5/10. i'm so disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI enjoy picking up Destiny for a few months once a year when the expansion rolls around. I catch up on the relevant story beats that occurred since the last expansion, ready to see what's gunna happen next, as when an expansion drops it's usually the biggest hit of Destiny storytelling you can get. Going in this time, there was a big-bad with a lot of intrigue, we were still yet to learn the motivation and goal. However the expansion explains nothing at all...

The expansion campaign did nothing to answer anything. Trying to build intrigue upon intrigue, with no revelations or answers, leads to apathy. An expansion should be where your questions start being answered, where twists are revealed or motivations explained, where the story advances and you get to be a part of that moving story. Lightfall does none of that. The front and centre plot continually threatens something happening, and then just ends... It might as well have ended with a message saying "Tune in next year, and bring £40 again to see what happens next!"

The story might get better explained by each passing season, but I don't like the seasonal model the game has, I'm not gunna stick around and buy each season to see it. I'll just follow it on Youtube, I guess.\t\t\t\t\t
