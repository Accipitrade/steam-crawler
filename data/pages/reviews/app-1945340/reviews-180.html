\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter the disappointment and frustration of Beyond Light (Sunsetting, basically being a $100 piece of marketing material for a new subclass, and a lackluster storyline with very little Gravitas.) The Witch Queen felt like Bungie had finally turned the page. The campaign was superb, the storyline felt weighted and consequential, and the new environment in the throne world was atmospheric and immersive. Fast forward to Lightfall... This feels like 1 step forward 3 steps back.. The campaign has very little narrative merit, once again the whole campaign feels like a $100 dollar piece of marketing for the latest darkness subclass (with the return of a drudging grind to actually be able to experience all that subclass has to offer in fragments), and the latest destination offering Neomuna holds very little pull for me to spend any time in that location. It feels lifeless and uninspiring. This is all without considering how the new build and mod system feels like a dumbed down plug and play version of the build-craft we had in previous years. All in all, this DLC will only potentially be redeemed by it's Raid and future changes and or it's internal seasonal content. Honestly, if you are looking for an expansion to purchase, purchase the legacy bundle. Save Lightfall for when it inevitably becomes part of a discounted DLC package.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall definitely has some story telling troubles within the campaign, but the campaign missions themselves were enjoyable. I understand others don't like some of the new characters (or their dialogue), but I can see why they made things the way they are.

Despite these two primary complaints, Lightfall brought some great things:
The campaign missions are fun
Strand is a pretty cool new subclass with new ways to interact with enemies
Awesome quality of life updates with new menus and UIs
The new expansion and seasonal weapons so far seem pretty great (especially the new exotic bow)
Seasonal missions are a lot of fun so far
A lot of curiosity about where the campaign is going (Despite what others may tell you, the story made enough sense, despite not everything being clear)
Great changes to playlists and overall game difficulty

The haters can hate, but at the time of writing, the game is barely 48 hours old. The biggest problems relate to the campaigns writing, but that's such a small fraction of the release. I am willing to bet that in a year's time people will look back and realize what a massive overreaction they had.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs far as Quality of life changes to the game and some cool new areas to explore in PVE content I would say buy it. What Bungie did to bring about the next expansion in terms of those PVE updates it is pretty good and fun to play. That's the only thing I can recommend this expansion for. If you are someone who enjoys and pays attention to the story this is about Beyond Light and Shadowkeep level bad. Actually I think they explained more in those expansions when it came to important plot developments than this expansion. Neomuna is cool and I get why the character Nimbus is the way he is - I just don't care for the character. There were heaps of missed opportunities and wasted potential in this expansion. Calus did not feel like he should of been the main bad guy of the story. He was much cooler in Season of Opulence and we got to see a lot more of his character in the Dungeon. He felt like a side villain to the story. The Neomuna folk pretty much live in cyberspace which made Neomuna seem devoid of all life. It made it really hard to "want" to protect this place other than the Veil. Which was BY FAR the largest fumble in directive story telling I've seen to date. Nobody knows what it was then everyone knew what it was but you. Never explained.

Now not everything was horrible. There is build up to Nezerac who already seems like he'd be a super cool and interesting character to further develop. We had some funny scenes with Osiris and we got to see the extent of fear that the Witness can produce. But overall I won't recommend this game for it's story which is sad to say after Witch Queen.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInitial impressions are not good.

After Witch Queen, the Lightfall campaign was set up to be a really important story with the arrival of the Witness. This is not what we got. The tone is way too comedic and filled with cringe one-liners that don't fit the mood of an imminent apocalypse. The stakes are supposed to be unbelievably high, but no characters seem to recognize that. Story is filled with Macguffins that don't even have a half-decent lore explanation given alongside their introduction. The characters speak like they know the answers to our questions, why can't we? All the marketing focused on the Witness, who is barely involved in the story at all. The few interactions between Calus and the Witness are a highlight amogst the generally poor story the campaign has to offer. Too much time spent focusing on Strand and not interesting side stories rather than Calus and the Witness which should have been the prime focus.

Gameplay wise, the campaign played fine on Legendary difficulty, maybe a little worse than the Witch Queen campaign. The Tormentors made for more engaging minibosses than the Lucent Hive though! Arriving at the same time as a balance patch and buildcrafting overhaul that greatly reduced overall ability uptime - kinda FeelsBad changing over to the new sandbox but it might be better for the game long-term, hopefully in time we will look back on this change favorably. Hoping the end-game of this expansion can redeem this flaming mess at least somewhat.

Overall, a disappointing release from Bungie. Lightfall feels like a filler expansion created to buy more time before The Final Shape releases.\t\t\t\t\t
