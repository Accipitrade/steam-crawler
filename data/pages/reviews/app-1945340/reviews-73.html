\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe short review: Some of the best gameplay, well worth the price of admission, with a healthy dose of copium for the upcoming 3 more seasons too, but lore fell flat on it's face in campaign.

The long review... Oh boy, here we go.

Destiny 2 Lightfall has a weird spot in my heart. The penultimate expansion in the Light and Dark saga didn't start off on the right foot - Clearly apparent by the "Mostly Negative" tag showing up for the first time since Curse of Osiris, I believe. A rating I wholeheartedly disagree with, yet understand where it came from. Anyway, here's my take on the Neon-shining, Strand-weaving, Bombastic 80's action movie-styled DLC which takes us to Neomuna.

======= The Story =======

(Soft spoilers ahead, avoiding any bombshells)

The campaign story casts us diving into Neomuna, the Hidden City on Neptune, with the sole task of stopping Emperor Calus from reaching the Veil, securing it and allowing The Witness to use it against the Traveler. We team up with the two Cloudstriders, Rohan and Nimbus, the protectors of Neomuna, and discover Strand - The threads that bind the universe together.
Sounds like a great premise, right?
Sadly, the story falls flat. The stakes are never fully explored, for example, "what is the Veil", "why does it hold the power it does", and "how our enemies can use it", the titular "Lightfall" fells more like a Light Hiccup, and with no real consequences for the story (save for a few characters), the campaign enclosed on it's own seems to be more of a filler episode of an action anime, than the full-scale action movie people expected after Witch Queen. That isn't to say the story is unforgivable and bad. It's just mediocre, and that's a bad spot to be in for the expansion leading up to The Final Shape.

The story after the campaign seems to suddenly pick back up again... IF You stick around and look for it. The patrols, the exotic quest (Secrets are back, baby!) and many more, all seem to breathe good storytelling back into the Expansion, but it might not be enough and I hope it's not too little too late.

As a small side note, I still haven't gotten into the story of the Raid, but from what I've heard from outside sources, it's amazing, so fingers crossed for that.

======= Gameplay =======

Lightfall brings two major game changes to the Destiny universe. A new subclass, and the build system reworked.

First off, we have the new Strand Subclass and element.
Acquisition: "Lightfall" Campaign completion | Upgrades: Strand Meditations (gained through using strand, generally)
Small note: I have only played the strand Hunter and Warlock. If You're a TItan player, You may want to look for better opinions elsewhere, but from what I've seen, the new Super is busted damage-wise.

Strand is pretty nuts.
We have
Weaving (Creating objects), Unraveling (killing enemies recently afflicted by Strand), Tangle (An orb left behind after an enemy has been killed WHILE affected with Strand), Suspend (CC which locks enemies and stuns Unstop Champions, in PVP a slow that lifts enemies off the ground and puts them in 3rd person), Sever (decreasing damage output of an enemy), Woven Mail (DR in PVE, no protection against melee or headshots in PVP though), and then finally, Threadlings, our newest minions.

On the Hunter side of things, the main takeaways are instant reloads, infinite grapples and insane Crowd Control. I personally use FR0ST-EE5's to constantly have my dodge loaded and available, which in turn allows me to Strand Dive and Suspend (Crowd Control) masses of enemies indefinitely, but I've seen people running The Sixth Coyote,

On the Warlock side of things, the two main schools seem to be either Necrotic Grips + Osteo Striga combo, which, if set up correctly, gives the user incredible ability regen, incredible CC and a really impressive Super regen, or the Threadling Swarmer, who with the insane ability regen, seeks to spawn swarms of green little explosive helpers who help dispatch any threats to their master.

From my experience so far, both classes have potential to be insane in endgame content PVE and have the benefit of being unreasonably fun to play and experiment with.

The strand weapons so far feel pretty underwhelming, not to dissimilar to The Witch Queen's first craftable weapons (the Father's Sins, for example). They're not bad, they're just very... Specific. They come with very obvious trade-offs, the only real exceptions being the Volta Bracket sniper rifle and the Circular Logic machine gun, these two feel really amazing and show the true potential of Strand for me.

The new and remastered modding system brings a lot of changes. Some good, some bad, and some that make me really hopeful for the future.

First off, You'll be pleased to hear - We have a dedicated "modding" screen, which is accessible to everyone who A) knows how to press an arrow > or B) knows how to navigate a menu with their mouse / game pad, where You can see and adjust every single mod currently on Your character and their weapons.
Secondly, You'll notice all Your favourite mods are gone. Powerful friends? Gone. High-Fire Energy? Decimated. Font of Knowledge? Well...

The current armor charge system really heavily favors people who were used to playing with the wells of light system, but sadly it also seems to really streamline the impact of Your modding on Your gameplay. I can't really say where I sit on the changes done here, but a lot of people do not appreciate this change, and would wish for allowing the players some of the creativity in their builds back. One thing I can say, however, is that I think this change is a good sign of what's yet to come. WIth new mods hopefully somewhere around the corner, the modding system rework really seems like a massive QoL change. I just wish we didn't have to pay for it in so many mods, forever lost to time.

It's also worth mentioning the changes to certain strikes and activities. Feel free to look up what happened to Proving Grounds, Lake of Shadows, Arms Dealer and the Inverted Spire, but generally, there seems to be a trend of "bringing difficulty back to Destiny", to quote the TWABs.

======= A short segment about the beeps and the boops =======

The Audio and Music teams both outshine themselves once again, creating such incredible bangers time after time after time again, it's getting really boring even mentioning this in a review. Every single time a new or reworked subclass or a new weapon show up in the game, the first thing most people notice isn't the sound... But with time, You notice the crispy explosions, You notice the incredible pieces of music the campaigns drop... And this DLC isn't any different.

As a side note for anyone who wants comparisons, "Tyrant Overthrown / Desparate Measures" is the new "Insurrection". Fight me.

======= Post-campaign activities, Season 20's first weeks, the Raid =======

After You wrap up the campaign, You have four major things to do;
- Grind out Neomuna rep, between exploration, the three exotic quests (Void MG, Strand Sidearm, Heavy Glaive) the new Terminal Overload Three Stage Public Event (on a daily, 3-part rotation as well), and the Partition Weekly activity...
- Dive into Season 20's weekly story and new Battlegrounds
- Take on a resurrected evil in the new Root of Nightmares raid
- Find the new AVALON hidden exotic quest

All of the above are absolutely amazing and give tons of content to have fun with.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall is a $75AUD Strand Subclass Tutorial with a little bit of story tacked on.

What little story the campaign has feels more Borderlands than Destiny. For example let's have an annoying surfer bro NPC/Vendor (whose writing/dialogue is all over the place) and even some of the patrol dialogue feels like it's from Borderlands. The one new character I actually liked gets killed off . Although I did also like the Osiris and Sagira wrap up/Osiris' chance to grieve but it also doesn't take place as part of the campaign and is instead part of an Exotic Misson.

The new subclass feels completely different from the campaign to actually getting to use it without the mission buffs to cooldown. Pretty fun to use in the campaign, but is a grind like stasis was to get a somewhat effective build out of it. I could forgive it if the cooldown for the one selling point (the Grappling hook) was significantly shorter at least in PvE content.

Neptune outside of the campaign is often barren and lacking any enemies at all.
The 1 new enemy was hyped up to be something intimidating but ended up being yet another tedious and annoying bulletsponge.
Lightfalls "New" weapons are reskins from Shadowkeep (The Moon) with neon glowy bits instead of feathers and beads.

This expansion goes nowhere, explains almost nothing and the pacing is all over the place. It feels like cutting room floor filler instead of an actual DLC.
Not worth $75AUD\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's talk about the space rhino in the room: Yeah, the narrative baked into the campaign does not live up to The Witch Queen or Beyond Light...or even ShadowKeep (mostly because spooky Bungie is still best Bungie). And the tone from the NPC we deal with the most is cringe incarnate, given the stakes and mood from the rest of the universe. Those were some odd choices.

But the game itself still feels amazing. The Quality of Life changes make build management and leveling improved experiences. The increased enemy density in most activities gives us room to flex our existing and new powers and weapons, and new enemies and activities added much-needed life. The sandbox is in great shape, even if certain activities <cough>Gambit<cough> were neglected. The Asher/Vexcalibur lore and quest are excellent, and the new raid is visually and thematically stunning, even if it left certain players wanting a greater challenge.

There were some misses, but I am still going to spend hundreds of hours on this across platforms, and so are most of the negative reviewers. It's probably (definitely) not the best point to jump into the universe, but it's still a hell of a ride. No ragrets purchasing. Not even the one.
