\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJust beat the campaign solo on legendary. It's a 4/10 for me, dawg.

You only interact with like 3 people the entire time, the trans-strider's California arrogance and downright awful dialog had me cringing the entire time. I think I only saw like 3 new weapons during the entire run, and that's with the legendary bonus chests. The story was so dumb and forced. I get that they were trying to inject some 80s vibes, but as someone raised in that era, they missed the mark by miles. This story was executed like a C-list movie from that era which is just bewildering to me. The whole 'gee golly will I master Strand now?' c*ck teases the *entire time* had me groaning more than trans-strider's dialog. Every moment of impact is punctuated like a modern Disney/Marvel film. Having an insightful, poignant moment? Well don't get too interested because HERE COMES THE LEVITY!

Did the devs ask the guys at Borderlands and Saints Row how, exactly, to nail that 'genuinely awful dialog' feel? It's so painful. The surrounding mechanics and QoL additions are welcomed. Loadouts are a god send. Neomuna feels like a mostly lifeless unity asset dump. Soloing legendary was difficult, but a satisfying challenge. I wouldn't have had nearly as much enjoyment of this vacuous campaign if it were on normal. Most missions can take up to an hour depending on your luck/loadout. Good luck on Calus.

In closing, the surrounding 'game' is mostly fine but for Bungie to drop the ball so hard on what has been, up to this point, a great story is such a colossal disappointment.\t\t\t\t\t

In short: utter disappointment.

The only reasonable explanation is that Bungie has to be diverting developmental resources elsewhere, because this is just not "it".. not by a long shot.

Maybe it's more flexible and easier to understand for new players? Not sure why this system needed overhauling.

Loadouts: This is mostly a welcome change, but why lock additional loadout slots behind "guardian rank" challenges?

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tApologies in advance for the novel. I've put 1800+ hours into this game and I've now decided to quit, so I feel like I should explain my grievances appropriately.

Some info on my history with this game first - I've played D2 since the first week it came to PC, which I suppose puts me in the "veteran" territory. Destiny 2 and WoW have been the only two games that I've put any serious long-term time into. I'm also a working 40yo with a family so I think perhaps that puts me into a different category of player these days vs. what I expect is probably the majority of players who are likely younger and have more time to play. That said, I've done every raid and dungeon that's been in this game and enjoyed that portion of it immensely.

Witch Queen was probably the best yearly DLC Bungie has ever produced - and correct me if I'm wrong - but for the most part it was built and finished during COVID, while their entire staff was working and communicating from their homes.

Lightfall - ironically titled - was built and finished after being bought by Sony for 2 billion dollars. Time and Resources should have been at an all time high but this might actually be the downfall of Destiny 2 unless they address some of the glaring concerns that have arisen. I would be remiss if I failed to mention what seems to be a wash of false advertising - but if you want to dive into that, I suggest looking into threads on Reddit about this.

The story an dialogue behind this years DLC is, in a word; childish. The flow of it all felt so disconnected that it actually made me angry having to listen to it. I can't fathom how the writing of this Xpac passed any kind of quality control - my only assumption when playing it was that this was very, very rushed - probably to hit a deadline mark without really considering the consequences. There has been data released to support this assumption now apparently.

I'll be honest and say that I didn't even finish the end fight - I ran the entire campaign on Legend, most of it solo, and it was agonizing to play through. The last fight sort of forced you into making use of the new subclass Strand - in which grappling through the air seemed to be the play, but even that couldn't solve for many of the unavoidable and seemingly random mechanics from the boss and horrible knock-back abilities of some of the mobs in the room. I ended up trying to use Void and Solar - which I knew well and had in the past got me through Solo Flawless runs of Prophecy and other difficult levels of content.

In the end, or what became my end (of play), It just wasn't fun. I probably could have finished it easily on Normal and carried on with the Seasonal content, but I just didn't see the point anymore given that S20 was going to be another copy/paste Seasonal format which we are all tired of.

On top of this - there are again NO new Crucible maps, only 1 new Strike and absolutely NOTHING new for Gambit - the game mode of Gambit wasn't even mentioned during the entire lead up to this expansion. Not. Once.

A rushed DLC, broken story narrative, lack of explanation for so many things, nothing new for the core playlist of the game, I could probably go on.

As a D2 veteran, I'm finished.

If you're new and making thinking about trying Destiny 2 with Lightfall - I would suggest waiting or skipping this one for now. If you've never played this game, I honestly believe that the campaign of Lightfall would make you hate this game.

Sad to say. I wish my feelings were different.

Onwards and upwards. Change is good, for me anyway.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall is an abysmal addition to the Destiny 2 collection of expansions. For a campaign to be as hyped as it was giving us a real threat to our existence it let down harder than any other game i've ever experienced. For once Bungie had given us Marvel level excitement as this was the Infinity War to the Endgame that is to come next year (the Final Shape). Which Queen did such a Beautiful of giving the players what they want; a brand new destination, revamp of the campaign for the better, and an actually engaging story that changed we view our enemies in the Destiny universe. For the first time we got to look at what we have been fighting this entire time, a look to the "Big Bad" of the Destiny franchise. Going into the campaign i was ready to pick apart the witness and figure his motives for the traveler. I can say on behalf of the entire community that we all had extremely high expectations, as we should coming off the Which Queen year. But all we got was a limp story with a character we have been dealing with since year one, no look into the Witness aside from a few small cut-scenes, a subclass no one wanted to be implemented into the game, and a limp cliffhanger that we are supposed to wait an entire year for. This DLC was essentially Calus's return and immediate demise. I could have told you when beyond light came out that Calus would have gotten merked during the Campaign. Which im fine with predictable but at least give me something in between not just "hey here's strand, oh while were at it lets actually go to Neomoona and do what we came to do". Bungie should have given us a Subclass of Sorrow like everyone wanted, would have tied to so many stories and given darkness a more toned look. Instead of the childish and weak Strand which is essentially Spiderman PS4 the subclass. All in all bungie has taken 3 gigantic steps back from Beyond Light and The Which Queens years of content. Unless Bungie pulls something magnificent out of their sleeves for the upcoming seasons I'd say were stuck back where we were in Shadowkeep.\t\t\t\t\t
