\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who was rather new into the series, hopping in around the end of beyond light, and who rather enjoyed witch queen, i was looking forward to this dlc, and sadly much to my disappointment and bittered expectations of this industry. this was yet another long line of disappointments from the gaming industry. i only really have myself to blame for getting "hyped" for the update and i accept that, but even minus that, this dlc feels very lackluster in comparison for all the "hype" that all the advertising and the story was bulit up to.
and, well. forgive my language, but i really cant phrase it any other way; They have ♥♥♥♥ the bed, maybe even harder than most other of these type of situations entail, and at a time where ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ the aforementioned bed was the worst possible time to happen.
Most of the balance changes are baffling and the few ideas that are good or, at least, sound good on paper (Loadouts and the Guardian rank) are hampered due to a bizarre focus on seasonal reset and other sorts of bizarre designs decisions that i cant imagine a sober mind would come up with or at least a sober mind with a idea of game design. and a lot of people have problems with the armor charge/well change, as well as butchering stasis crystals and just in general, which particularly hurts, as stasis is one of my favorite subclasses both in terms of theme and visual design.
which speaking of subclasses, Strand, while also very visually appealing to me, and enjoying the theme, and as a warlock main, seems to be inspired by one of my favorite roles in rpgs (summoner/beast masters) and i feel bad for titans, who deserve better than to just have yet another roaming melee super, and just, doesn't feel as strong or as good as the other classes, especially with how bad the cooldown times are, almost one or two minutes for one charge of a grappling hook doesn't feel good, even with stacking stats it doesn't feel fast enough. and took up far too much of the story, which is, as well a convenient segue into just how bad the story truly bad it is.
I wont spend too much time in this section, as many people have talked about the plot, so i will leave it brief and there will be spoilers so, if you are interested, watch a youtube video about it, cause, if i being honest, this is not worth 60$, the map feels empty, strand training takes up far too much of the plot, out of two of the new characters, only one of them is decent characters and the other one is a rather unbearable typical "marvel snark/sarcastic" character. the one who is a decent character dies, and the unbearable one ruins any drama or tension, if your characters cant take an in universe apocalyptic event happening seriously, then i see no reason why i or anyone else should either. Plus their designs don't really feel like they fit into the world of destiny, at all.
The veil and the ending are the most confusing parts, as no one explains what the veil is, but they all act like we know and have known what it is, and is baffling to be frank, and the ending just opens up far more questions than it answers, at a time when the community is desperate for answers, and despite the fact that we had lost, seems to not really affect anything at all, except the traveler is not in the sky anymore, and has a neon triangle on it. just extremely confusing all around.
I could go on and on for hours about how i feel destiny has fumbled this at a time when all eyes were on it, but i don't really need to, you can just read the other comments on this page and get a much better view on each individual flaw from people who have far more hours in the game than i do and know every inch of the lore. To be honest this feels more like venting my feelings at this point about this confusing update and just how disappointed i was. so i think my opinion about this update is fairly clear, and thats not even mentioning the bugs and how they messed up other aspects of the game, not relating to the update, somehow.

TLDR: Bungie tangled up the story, made weird balancing changes. gutted some features, the new subclass needs buffs, and seems like most of the time developing was used for marketing instead of making a functional, non buggy update. seems like destiny is ending, not with a bang, but with a brick on the accelerator and a hose in the gas pipe

An edit, since this is the first time I've made something like this; in the interest of being fair, i did still enjoy it, while most of it was laughing at the game, due to the bugs and poor story, i did genuinely enjoy using strand, and while it needs buffs and more variety for titans, i had fun with it and the weapons i have used so far i have enjoyed, that doesn't mean its good, or well made or done and there's always a chance that bungie learns from this and the seasons are written better and the next dlc could make up for it, but for now, and based on what I've heard on bungie and the way they patch the game, i'm not sure it will, though i do feel its better to remain optimistic than pessimistic.\t\t\t\t\t
