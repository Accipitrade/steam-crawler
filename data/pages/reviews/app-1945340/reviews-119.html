\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWrapped up the campaign missions. The new City is sprawling and lends itself, like the giant Cloud Striders, to a sense grandness of the game. The story itself felt like I, as the main character, was once again being used as a tool instead of the hero. This is not to discount the extra voice lines you get, it's just how I felt playing the campaign. Overall the global story arc of events are confusing as to what is going.

As far as gameplay goes, Bungie gets another win from me. The Magical Super Soldier feeling is back in full swing. With Strand the new Darkness Class, you gain some new abilities to craft around. With being a Hunter only main I can speak for that. The zip and whip feeling is a a great feeling, even though about 20% of time I yeet myself off a ledge. I look forward to mastering it's gameplay. During the campaign, like in Shadowkeep, you gain the ability for a battle and learn to use a little so by the end you have enough use for the final fights and rest of the game. The downside of this was it made your carefully crafted builds/loadouts useless. This coupled with the Legendary Campaign difficulty and going solo lent a few restarts to mission and some homebrew curses being screamed at developers.

I've enjoyed it overall so far and I look forward to the continuing story and growth and Byf's eventual fill ins.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a tentative recommendation.

There's plenty of very valid criticisms, particular with regards to the unsatisfying campaign storyline but in most respects I think there's a lot more bad than good. I'm hopeful that the raid might make good on some of the open story threads left by the end of the campaign, but the raid isn't out yet so I can't factor that into my opinion. The new Tormentor enemies are like mini-rhulks and a lot of fun to fight, much more interesting and challenging than Hive Lightbearers. I overal enjoyed the campaign on legendary, but if you play solo there is a STEEP difficulty spike at the end, in particular the final fight while solo just feels frustrating and unfair which is only worsened by some really long encounters without many checkpoints.

Neomuna has a patrol location also isn't especially interesting. In much the same way that I felt the Throne World was small and not all that interesting to explore, Neomuna has the same problems. But I think that also speaks to how patrols don't really feel like they have much of a place in current Destiny, you're there to grind the public events you need to do for the exotic quests, but once they're wrapped up there's little reason to go back.

Strand is excellent. I've only played as a Threadweaver, but I've already made a couple of builds with some really fun playstyles and this is only improved by some of the new weapon perks like hatchling. If you're playing strand warlock I highly recommend playing around with a necrotic grips build, especially since strandlocks have 3 melee charges now.

The new exotics really great so far, in particular the Quicksilver Storm is excellent for strand builds once you have the catalyst. I'm not crazy about a lot of the weapon designs themselves but there's some great perkpools and some of the new perks like envious assassin and keep away are top tier.

The systemic changes that Lightfall brings are overall a net positive. The Loadout system is a welcome feature despite some clunky UI decisions and I think the reworked mods system, while killing some old builds like Elemental Wells, Powerful Friends on hunters and Warmind Cells, it makes the mods system much more comprehensible and focused creating something that can easily be built upon. I'm not sure how I feel about most of the more powerful mods being part of the new seasonal artefact meaning most of the best builds will be invalidated between seasons but only time will tell there. Guardian Ranks overall seem like a good system and certainly more interesting than just grinding out levels but I'm not sure how things are going to feel when some of those ranks reset at the end of the season but that's something we won't really know until season 21.

In conclusion, Lightfall unfortunately does not reach the high bar set by Witch Queen and I think that's left a lot of people disappointed. But it's far from the worst expansion, for me that award still belongs to Shadowkeep and I would also rank it above Beyond Light. If you enjoy Destiny it's an easy recommend, but if you're a lapsed player or jumping in fresh I think you're better off going back and playing Witch Queen instead of you haven't already.\t\t\t\t\t
