\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
Strand Subclass is fun

Cons:
- Story is very lackluster which was essentially one of the only things that even kept me coming back to these DLC's
- Come up with an original villain to fight instead of bringing back a re-skinned version (Calus). The marketing even made it seem like the Witness was going to be the big bad, but instead you only see him in cut scenes.
- The Vaporwave/Neon theme is a very very cool concept, but was poorly executed. Even the trailer had more showing than the actual new area incorporated visually.
- Along with the Vaporwave/Neon theme, why not have some actual music that fits this atmosphere? Hugely missed opportunity there.
- The new area seems much smaller than areas of the past, I mean you only have a total of two instant travel points? That's pretty telling.
- The same repetitive grind after the campaign.. earn reputation, grind pinnacles from crucible/gambit/strikes, do patrols/public events, grind exotics. I thought this monotonous way of gear leveling was supposed to change at some point?
- Difficulty of legendary scales up so harshly when another person or two join that it almost encourages you to play solo. Is the point to encourage players to play on normal difficulty just to make them repeat the campaign on legendary for the exotic, then do it again twice on each alt character (different class) for a total of 6 times? That's like torture, especially with a lackluster campaign. Come on Bungie, you don't have to find tricky ways to pump up the hours played for everyone just to make it seem like you made a good DLC. How about actually making a good product (DLC) with a compelling story that people will want to play over again on their own accord?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts unfortunate that the delivery of the story in this Lightfall expansion wasn't on point to match their previous Witch Queen expansion entry. Destiny veterans will always tell you that there has never been a "good" campaign except Witch Queen though, but this one didn't live up to Witch Queen. The story follows our guardian in pursuit of Calus, a villain we've been chasing for nearly 6 years now, and introduces some new faces into a previous known world.

That said, this Expansion is incredibly well done. The gameplay, art, strand, themes, and system overhauls make this worth picking up. The story is even fleshed out with the aftercare quests included with your purchase, but the fact its not part of the 8 mission sequence really upset a ton of people. That said, the story flop shouldn't take away from all the amazing work the Bungie team has done on this expansion. Its not groundbreaking, but its been incredibly fun and will culminate in a wonderful raid experience next week where you will see community sentiment begin to shift positive again.

If you're a Destiny fan or have waited to get involved in the game, its not a bad time to start. This expansion is a B tier expansion, but its nowhere near as bad as the other reviews attempt to paint it. Gunplay in Destiny is alwasys wonderful and the entry bar is always getting easier for first timers.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start by asking you a question.

If you had to boil down why you play Destiny into only one specific aspect, what would that be?

If the answer to that question is the narrative, then unfortunately Lightfall is not for you. The writing is sloppy and tone deaf, the character development is non-existent and by the end of the campaign you'll know less about what's going on than when you started.

So why do I still recommend Lightfall?

Well if the answer to the former question was gameplay, then I am happy to report that, at least in that department, Bungie did not disappoint.

Lets take a second then to talk about what they got right.

Strand is a refreshing addition in terms of it's playstyle, providing more variety to how you can approach combat and contrary to popular belief, is a very viable subclass in endgame content The new Tormentor enemy type is well designed, unique and challenging. Not to mention when they show up, they genuinely make combat more tense and engaging, even more so than Lucent Hive in Witch Queen. Neomuna is also an interesting location to explore and a welcome departure from most of the ruined settlements found on other planets. The level design and AI tuning done in the campaign, makes for one of the more challenging DLC experiences I've played in Destiny, especially on Legendary. Also some of the changes Bungie has made to the build crafting system with the addition of Loadouts are things Destiny players have been asking for for years.

So then... Lets address the elephant in the room. How does the sloppy narrative effect the overall experience?

Well don't get me wrong... I would definitely prefer it if both aspects, gameplay and narrative were equally as strong in this new DLC. Especially with the successful balance Bungie had found in that regard with Witch Queen. But unfortunately that's not what we got here.

Was my enjoyment of Lightfall affected by this?

Sure! Absolutely! However not nearly enough to ruin my overall experience of what Lightfall had to offer.

Okay look... Let me level with you for a second.

I'm the kind of Destiny player that reads almost every Lore tab and has probably watched almost every My name is BYF video... Even the ones over 3 hours long. I am a big fan of the narrative universe that Bungie has created. This game definitely has great stories to tell and deserves better then what we got with Lightfall. That being said, at the end of the day, the narrative takes a back seat for me in favor of fun, challenging and engaging gameplay.

So in summary, if you are interested in a thought-provoking, well thought out narrative that answers the questions that previous DLCs established, then sorry to say, this isn't it chief. However if you're able to move past the cheesy dialogue and seemingly rushed story, Bungie's gameplay devs definitely have done something right here with Lightfall and it is clear that aspect was prioritized over everything else. As a result I had a blast and if you are anything like me, you most likely will too.\t\t\t\t\t

Obviously the story was a filler that was meant to buy time for what Lightfall was meant to be (Final Shape).It is clear that changing the original 3 year trilogy to add a one year gap has left the writer with little to no time to write a cohesive story. We have seen the writers do a fantastic job with story lines and Lightfall feels like a huge level below. I don't harbor any ill will to the devs, clearly the decision was made by higher ups at Bungie.

As of the time of Lightfalls release i wrote a review saying the story was completely pointless and had no conclusion. However with the release of Season of the Deep, we have gotten huge revelations within the story that NEEDED to be part of LF. With Witch Queen we had the entire expansion story all in one play through meaning if you paid the 60 bucks for that expansion you received the entire thing. The revelations that Deeps brought us are massive and can not be left out of LF. Most players who played on LF release will never get to know and will have to pay more for the story they should have received in the first place. Apparently 60 bucks for the DLC story isn't enough they want 120 bucks for it.

I am a player who has 2.5 k hours in D2 also 2.5 k in Warframe. I don't compare them one to one because of the massive differences however in terms of micro transactions it is insane how many revenue streams D2 has over Warframe yet releasing IMO worse quality content. Every year more story and the same activity with slightly mixed up mechanics. whereas games like Warframe create new game modes that implement new ways to play the game. The core game modes have been the same for years and only QoL changes have been made to them.

I am a PVE player strictly and don't interact with the PVP scene almost at all. however it would be rude of me not to mention how insanely disrespected the PVP scene is. Maps almost never get made and the community outrage is definitely there.

This expansion to me was a slap in the face with the marketing of the Witness as the main antagonist when in reality we saw him in one cut scene then went to Neomuna to catch a mcguffin. Using the strategy of having the crescendo event of the entire Destiny franchise to get people to buy LF has left a bad taste in everyone's mouth. However this was most likely done deliberately because Bungie knows that every Destiny fan will be buying Final Shape. It doesn't really matter, this is evident from how bad the community outrage has gotten yet still Bungie has not released any statements or even mentions about it. When the hype of Final Shape gets going no one will remember this dumpster fire and will definitely be buying into it. I just hope some will keep the outrage going enough to get Bungie working on it.\t\t\t\t\t

When the game is good, and the lore moments are good, this game is the best hit of dopamine that you will find outside of recreational narcotics. Witch Queen is only rivaled in terms of quality by Halo 2, or Destiny 1's Taken King.


The Lightfall campaign is a short, haphazard tutorial to how to play a subclass. It does nothing to tie story threads together. Characters that have had serious and dire implications on the lore are just...absent... Characters that are introduced with this ((("expansion"))) have no depth, there is no setup to how they fit into the story, are clumsily introduced, and leave no lasting impressions outside of "they exist" on those that play the game. There is no substance to this expansion. You would find more to do from Beyond Light's content drop. Shadowkeep had a better narrative. Curse of Osiris had a clearer threat, and a clearer objective. For those that know, that last one is incredibly sad.

I can't recommend this expansion in good conscious to anyone. Save your money, play a game developed by a studio that cares about your personal investment and playtime. Just leave this one alone.\t\t\t\t\t
