\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm sorry, I wish I could recommend it but...
For how great Strand is, how cool it is, this DLC and the changes it brought are two steps forward, and three back.

Step one: Strand
Strand is genuinely one of the greatest subclasses of all time. It takes a certain amount of skill and knowledge to use. It's fun to use, it's got an interesting aesthetic, I like it. Good job, Bungie.

Step two: Buildcrafting 2.0
Modding armor has never been so easy now - sure it takes a minute to get used to the new mods, but I am so very much happy that elemental mods have gone away. Loadout slots have made things also significantly easier - sure I wish I could give the individual loadouts their own custom name, but that's just me.

Regression one: Difficulty Tweak
So, I am a Titan main, right? And I always run resilience 100, meaning- maximum damage resistance. 40% damage reduction. Cool right? Now it's down 10%. Okay, no big deal. Suddenly, every single Enemy in Neomuna has tons of health, my weapons feel like they're made of rubber, and I just die over and over and OVER. You wouldn't know how upset I got when I tried to clear the Thrilladrome Lost Sector, only to get bulldozed by that stupid Hydra! It was a CHORE! Playing this game feels like a chore!

Regression two: Writing. (Spoiler warning)
The story of this expansion is... questionable. Ignoring the fact that Neptune is a gas giant and there is no solid ground to land on- why is the veil on Neptune? And... what is the Veil? Everyone acts as if they knew, but I don't know, I don't know how significant it is, and- there is this weird sense of urgency during the whole campaign, that leads ultimately to nothing!

Regression three: Bugz & Performance
Yeah it was inevitable - there are bugs littered all over this expansion, and in the base game too now. In a cutscene, Osiris' cloak was messing up a whole cutscene. Last night, there was this puzzling bug where everyone was like invisible, as well as the guns, and every gun sounded different... it is hard to describe what the hell it was, but it was super distracting during the grind. Also at one point I tried to switch out some armor mods- it.. didn't let me???
Also, what the hell happened to the performance of the game? I run a RTX 3070TI and a AMD R5 3600 - and in some scenes, where it should still be 60 frames rendered at seconds, and it would dip down to 15. FIFTEEN, because there were a couple of vex on screen.

TL;DR: Neomuna is cool and all, but the bugs, the performance and the ballbusting difficulty is just... no.
I cannot recommend anyone getting into Destiny 2 right now. Maybe, if Bungie reconsiders some of the decisions, I'll change this review too. Until then... I'll sit here on Pluto, and watch this mess from far, far away.

4/10 - needs to sit much longer in the oven

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo say the campaign story is as good a Witch Queen would be a lie. The campaign itself is fun and they did the Legendary difficulty again which brings a good setting to the campaign. Most of the missions are well made and challenging in a legitimate way. I think the Calus fight is a bit crazy, while possible, is just better to cheese it. The story does not explain much and this expansion, story-wise, just seems like a bridge towards the Final Shape.

Where I do recommend this expansion is where it really matters to me and that is gameplay. Strand is very much one of the most fun things they have added to this game. The movement and class fantasy with this subclass is great. I have played with both Hunter and Warlock and can say that they both feel really good and pretty balanced. The grapple is great but PvE cooldowns could use a little decreasing even after the fragments. The seasonal activity is also fairly engaging as of the first week and is something that I am cool with repeating. The terminal overload activity is engaging as well and the Vex Incursion Zone is a great addition to Neomuna. The raid has not released as of my review so I cannot speak on this.

Finally, there are a few QoL changes made with this expansion as well. Some crafting materials have been removed and simplified which helps with new players. Weapon and armor focusing is great and lets you get the gear you want with that bit of randomness MMO players still like. In-Game Loadouts are very nice and let you switch between builds quickly and efficiently. While it does need a slight tweak, overall it is a great addition.

Overall I have really been enjoying my time with the game, if you play for story this is not going to be your favorite expansion as the campaign aspect is 5/10. If you love the gameplay and gameplay loop of Destiny I highly recommend. Overall the expansion is an 8/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:
- Typical destiny expansion grind with new exotics, new missions, new subclass, and new events (as a current destiny player, more destiny is always good)
^ I will note that many of the new exotics are very cool but we will see how useful they are.
- strand is the most fun destiny subclass with high mobility and a unique play style.
- I typically do not care for new locations, however Neomuna is very cool and I definitely got some hours in exploring and completing new location events.
-changes to mod system are a great start. Many of the complaints i feel and see about the system can easily be fixed with the inclusion of new mods (which has been confirmed to come later)


Cons:
- Not a great new comer DLC. As a veteran to the series, the story was confusing. I could only imagine if this was my first expansion.
- the story is very short and feels more akin to destiny’s old style of expansions.
^The campaign took a backseat in importance while other aspects of the game received much needed attention.
-overall not as innovative as Witch queen felt.


Summary,
I think the feedback is very fair, however many of these reviews are focused solely on the campaigns disappointment. Beyond that there is a lot to enjoy in this dlc. Especially strand which has led to The most fun I’ve had in destiny in a while.

At the time of this writing many of the other destiny DLCs are on sales. I strongly recommend new or returning players jump in with Witch queen. Get some friends and experience that incredible legendary campaign, new strikes, some of the coolest exotics in the game. Then maybe pick up light fall.\t\t\t\t\t
