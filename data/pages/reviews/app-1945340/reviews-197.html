\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am actually going to disagree with the majority here and start by saying this review is not a completely accurate one of this expansion as it has been 3 days of the update being out, there is still story, gameplay, abilities, and content not in our hands yet. And yes all the other reviews you see are reviewing without waiting like myself, even IGN waits, its a joke.

Let's start with everyone's main gripe, the story. (spoiler heavy) I have never seen a group of people so uninterested in the story suddenly become so frustrated with it. I agree that Witch Queen was phenomenal, but I feel that many people who have been experiencing this expansion forgot how confusing the Witch Queen was before the raid dropped and the additional lore and content poured in. And while the story may not have lived up to lore expectations, when has it? The story has always been self contained, if a player wanted to go on YouTube and watch a compilation of all Destiny 1 and 2 cutscenes through each expansion and season they would find themselves a coherent enough story. And I will agree it can be better, but I do not agree that this fact alone means this game is awful and unplayable, new players want to have fun and I believe without a doubt there is a lot of fun to be had here. I will attempt to tackle some of the complaints I have seen with the story:

1. Caiatl and Calus never got their moment! Yes, they did.
I completely disagree with this, the only thing we didn't get was them fighting in a cutscene but I didn't even want that, I wanted to fight Calus again myself as we have fought him before. Caiatl leaves the fight on Earth to come deal with the Shadow Legion personally, we fight along side her and she stands against his army and psychic powers while we finish the job. How is that not the conclusion we wanted? The cutscene that explains she does not consider Calus her father anymore completed her arc for me.

2. Cloudstriders are bad. They aren't.
This one is laughable to be honest. They aren't bad, they are two sides of a coin that Osiris needed for his character development and they were both written well. I've seen people call Nimbus cringe but his jokes are straight out of Cayde-6's handbook. He made me and my friends laugh several times throughout, and his dialogue with Osiris is peak character interaction for me. Everyone is complaining about which characters would have been cooler in the story, but no one is bringing up the Exo Stranger (Elsie) when she clearly used to be fighting on Neomuna when she travels through time to help them against the Vex, she should have been involved in some way, BUT unlike everyone else I am willing to see how the seasons and year of content complete this arc in our story. Rohan's death was predictable, but I predicted that Nimbus would be reckless and get him killed which is pretty generic, however, we the player got Rohan killed. That's a big change of pace and tone. I felt powerless in the moment it happened, because as much as everyone here will tell you he was a boring character he was not. He was a wise, interesting addition to the game, and his loss hurts because of how much we could have accomplished with his help.

3. The Veil is not explained and leaves us confused. It is not explained, but it is not confusing.
Look, let's start with Destiny 1, our first question. Woah, what do you think the Traveler is? I've already got like 20 people going, the Traveler is a gardener, it's a terraformer, it brings life. How? Why? Does no one realize once this question is answered then the story is over? What is the nature of the Witness and the Traveler? This IS the question, yet everyone is annoyed that it wasn't answered and the story wasn't just ended here. Why are people annoyed there is more story, more build up towards this moment? Regarding the Veil, yes it is not explained, and no the other characters do not know more about it. We all know it is important to the Traveler and the Witness, so guess what, it is important. Just as the Witness's character is interesting for being unexplained and confusing, so is the Veil. It will be answered, it is another expansion of the story and the universe as a whole, and I am excited to see what happens. Even the ads said, "Our end begins" not "our story ends" so I am still confused at the communities insane reaction to this.

4. Strand ruins the gameplay and story. This is so far from the truth it hurts
The Veil causes distortions in both darkness and the light throughout Neomuna, the ghost states it many times. You do not need to know how to understand that it does. Ahem, the Traveler still exists. Anyway, this is when Strand is stumbled upon and everyone acts like this is bad writing. Yet, the Cloudstriders didn't know it existed, it took Osiris becoming a better mentor and friend to us to understand what it could do. That moment when everyone is excited to have seen the player master Strand was the climax for me. I am going to say something divisive, I do not think the vast majority of the player base can use Strand well, if at all. It is the most versatile subclass to this day and I don't think I struggled at all during the campaign using it. I just had a lot of fun. Learn the subclass, see how strong it can be, then let's talk more.

Overall, this is getting similar reviews to Shadowkeep, and no one here should disagree when I say that is criminal. Shadowkeep's story is about find a single pyramid ship, on the moon, a place we have been before. And most of the campaign is gathering armor with no cutscenes or dialogue. Yet somehow, people place this story with its incredible cutscenes and amazing dialogue and rich destination with Shadowkeep. This is how I know the community does not understand this expansion this time around. Maybe it deserves to sit at Mixed on steam, but to say 25% of people who are playing it enjoy it? That's crazy, because people still enjoyed and played Shadowkeep.

Where as the rest of the reviews will just say the story is mid and leave a negative, they will also say the gameplay is a ton of fun and still leave a negative. If you haven't been playing Destiny for the entire time, the story is likely not your main priority, Strand and the gameplay is. And the good news is, that it is awesome.

Gameplay is phenomenal and don't let anyone here tell you otherwise. Strand is fast paced flying around, in PVP and PVE. Most fun I've had in Destiny since Stasis. But I think Strand is quite balanced, and all the complaints Bungie is getting means they will probably buff it to make the extremely easy campaign even easier. The guns and mods on armor feel great when playing and managing your buff timers has never been so rewarding. And so if the gameplay is great, and several people agree, why all the negative reviews? The game is fun... don't play it? Well, my friends who don't play Destiny were asking me questions about Strand and the game changes for the last two days, first time the game has been interesting in a while.

We have always had patience for this franchise, so where has it gone now? Destiny has been a roller coaster of ups and downs, not that I still want that, but it is foolhardy to say this expansion is a huge step down. It is as if people haven't seen how the Crucible, Gambit, and Vanguard playlists are changing with Strand. It is as if people don't like having a loadout system, or commendations and easier to understand UI. It is as if people don't love amazing music and cutscenes with emotion and humor. If you want to hate something so bad, then hate it, but for those who are unsure, just know many people who play this game want it to fail and are not happy with nearly peak gaming experiences.

I know I come off of as a Bungie defender and lover. While I love Destiny, and want to love all aspects of it and its community, I don't. It can be better, this expansion too. I love to understand and discuss people's views, I have comments on, lets chat politely!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall. The marketing team deserve a raise. The hype train was insane until release.
Gameplay wise, it's good. The legendary campaign was challenging and rewarding, and tormenters are fun to fight.
Strand however, felt underwhelming. Grappling is fun, and I love flying through the air at mach 20. But it just felt underpowered. Every time I was forced to use Strand, it felt like a downgrade than anything else. Maybe its cuz we didin't have any build when we were using strand, but it sure as hell made me feel underpowered. Also the warlock super (my main), the missiles just went everywhere. I had no way to aim it. It felt very frustrating.

The new buildcrafting elevated my well build, but I feel like the armor charge/orbs system really limits player build diversity. now everyone's using orbs and armor charge, because there's literally nothing else to use. Before lightfall, there's players that'd use charged with light builds, well builds (me) and warmind cell builds. But now it's just, Armor charge/orb builds. I feel like it killed the diversity.

The story was awful. It was so rushed at the end, that we don't even know anything. What's the veil? They keep blabbering about it. What's the radial dial or whatever it's called? Why are neomuna people in cryosleep? Did they just get lazy for an excuse to not include people? Every time Nimbus spoke I wanted to claw out my eardrums. Their voice is so annoying, and the attitude they have is diabolical and unbearable.

By far, Neomuna's scenery is what carries it. It's an empty city with literally nothing in it. Unlike the other regions, that feels fitting. EDZ is a ruin, so is the cosmadrone and Europa. but Neomuna isn't a ruin. It's a cyberpunk city.

I think bungle really dropped the ball on this one. I thought they got this figured out after Witch queen, but I suppose not.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Lightfall DLC, while touted as a flagship for the grapple ability, falls short in many aspects. The patrol area is a mere fraction of Europa's size, with half of it being devoid of any enemies or public events, making exploration a lackluster experience. Adding insult to injury, most of Neptune is walled off with invisible barriers, preventing players from fully utilizing the grapple ability.

The campaign itself is a major disappointment, reusing assets from previous areas and missions, with only a handful of new content to justify its price tag. Even on the legendary difficulty, the campaign is a breeze, with the game artificially inflating the difficulty by restricting players' abilities and forcing them to stand still while enemies spawn.

The new strand super ability is also underwhelming, struggling to deal significant damage to bosses or clear out groups of enemies. Most of its abilities merely apply a slow ticking poison, making it one of the weakest elements in the game.

Adding insult to injury, there are numerous bugs in the DLC, causing quest issues that require players to replay the campaign if they weren't the fireteam leader.

While there are a few positives, such as the armor design and well-made music, they are expected of any AAA game. The story is interesting, but locked to 30fps, even though most cutscenes in the previous DLC were unlocked.

In conclusion, unless you can get this DLC at a steep discount, it's not worth your money. The few pros it offers are already included in the $10 seasonal pass or are free, making this DLC a major letdown for Destiny 2 fans.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe gameplay and feature updates to the game feels incredible to the point that, if you've never cared about the story, this is a fun and engaging expansion to go through, even if the final mission can be a letdown compared to Witch Queen's.

But, needless to say, it's disappointing for an expansion to be this hyped up by the marketing and setup well by the story preceding it, just for it to be a cookie cutter campaign with no character development, a heavy use of plot devices with no clear explanation to them, among other tropes that any player can see coming, and yet we're expected to be surprised by them.

The main aspect of Destiny is the universe and its story, the sense of discovery and being surprised with the unexpected through narrative and gameplay. Lightfall has failed at all these fronts to some extent, and it's hard to believe this is what followed up Witch Queen. In hindsight, I'd have much preferred a delayed release or just that they didn't split The Final Shape into two products.

Wait for a huge price drop, likely to happen prior to The Final Shape. If you need to pick an expansion to start Destiny 2 with, Witch Queen still by far the best and (as its stands compared to the other expansions to date) the only purchase really worth the players' time in 2023 Destiny 2.\t\t\t\t\t
