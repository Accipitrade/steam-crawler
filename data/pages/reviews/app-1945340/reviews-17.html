\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 Lightfall had a cool creative way in introducing strand. How they managed to give Osiris purpose again and let you create a connection with him was great and worth every second of playing. I wish we got something like that for Saint 14 in terms of story gameplay. This being said, for an expansion campaign this was not how they would have done things… When you think of an expansion worth $50, you think of a full story introducing new lore points and giving guidance of what is to come. Lightfall did not hit that mark in the slightest. A common theme we see with Destiny 2 is introducing new things and never expanding upon it. For example, The worm gods on Titan, The War Mind, Many other Fallen houses, the Awoken, The Vex. We seem to just go full into an expansion and finish only half way into the answers we are looking for as if it did not matter in the first place. Lightfall is seeming to fall into this problem with The Witness. Unless we read the grimoires (Destiny 1 had them, but not Destiny 2) where are we supposed to know about him. The campaign basically had us run around killing vex (who was drawn to neptune why?) with strand, which we were not compatible with for the whole campaign…why? Then we go into a raid to fight Nezarec who was the witnesses disciple (Do we even get told about how Savathun killed him once already and the witness had to revive him in a capsule? I don't think we did) The story like every other expansion has holes everywhere that don't show a clear end goal and as the missions every week continue we do not get any closer to understanding. Now we simply are listening to a whale which is what the hive worms used to be before the witness corrupted them into what we know as the hive worm gods. Ya i'm sure you didn't know that either, I sure didn't before looking this stuff up… THAT WOULD BE CRUCIAL TO KNOW AND AWESOME. It is a small context like that which is missing and no one will know unless they take the extra step to find it. This expansion I would rate a 2/10 and I'm not even going to include the endless bugs because at that point it's just a refund….\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst time doing a review so pardon my dumbassness. Also spoilers if you care much, but the story ain't worth much so meh.

I played through this dlc twice, 0.5 with friends and 1.5 without em both on legendary difficulty(finished later half of first on my own). I have to say, I get some of peoples concerns and think others are under represented.

Pros:
Neptune: Honestly, the setting and lore of Neptune seems really fun and interesting to me. The whole "Neomuna" thing, their culture and their lore seems really cool and fun overall. Might be a bit shallow and I'm not a lore buff for this game at all, but what was presented either made me look slightly into it or just seemed fascinating.

Characters: I don't have much experience with Osiris content wise and while I didn't like him much during this DLC, he did grow on me a bit by the end. Nimbus also seemed a bit too peppy at the start but I really warmed up to him and started to really like him, especially in his interactions with Osiris. Pretty generic, but fun and even a bit better than generic at times. They did try to do something with Riden or whatever the senior Cloud Strider was, but introducing a character and then killing him off straight away feels like a cheap jab that did nothing that they wanted it to.

Strand: Strand is ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ fun. That alone makes the DLC worth a good bit.

Neutral:
Story: From what I can tell (Haven't been through too many campaigns in Destiny 1 or 2 so I'm not the best opinion on this) it was par for the course of Destiny 2 storylines. I THINK i understand why people were dissapointed. Witchqueen was a step up from the norm and this is the DLC with the biggest baddie, supposedly, coming out to play so it was underwhelming in both regards.

Bad:
Level design: To be honest, i felt like the maps were generally pretty ♥♥♥♥♥♥ for strand. A lot of encounters where you can stumble off a ledge during really important fights and the grapple hook often can't save you. A skill issue on my part yes but I do find it odd that they decided to make so many maps with constant ledges and the grapple hook genuinely doesn't seem to synergize well with the core movment of titan and warlock (the ones i played the DLC with so far). A lot of the maps also felt like they had a lack of cover and while I understand you should be swining around, you don't get strand for a lot of missions and when you do there are ledges everywhere and its a no-respawn battle so it feels like it isn't worth it/its just as dangerous.

Bosses: Most of the bosses in the DLC are just Tormentors. This feels really ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ lazy, especially with how sometimes they are treated like elite enemies who can be stunned and executed, while others are treated like full on bosses. They aren't that hard once you learn how to cheese them but the game does force you into some annoying situations with them like locking you in a room with em or putting you against two. They are also honestly not fun to fight, with their silencing stopping even your super, near one shot grab, and overly specific weakpoints, they are more tedious than anything to fight. The few other bosses are bland and normal enemies like a generic Cabal commander and so on. Calus himself is honestly just annoying as well. Only difference is his fight is also really hard as well as annoying. His first phase can be dealt with pretty easily by staying in cover and shooting, but his second phase is really tedious and frustrating. The way the developers seem to want you to fight his second phase is by briefly shooting at him, grappling away, and then repeat until his entire second health bar is gone. I could only get his second health bar to about 25% before being booped off the ledge and using an exploit found online to beat him. That part is a skill issue, but his general design for the fight starts with a really bland first phase and a really tedious and unfun second phase. Then theres the "Parting the Veil" tormentor fight but thats only necessary if you want an ability for Strand. Really frustating boss fight where you get have to kill a ton of ADs while the tormentor pelts you, shoot him for a bit, he summons more, and repeat until he forces you into melee range like Calus.

In general:
I honestly feel like the main issue with this DLC is the actual gameplay itself and, in a video game, thats REALLY bad. The story, characters, and new subclass all are ok or even really good, but since the actual DLC itself is just downright unfun and just a slog to get through. as a final rating with those things considered:

5/10,
3 for strand
1 for the characters
1 for the lore introduced.
0 for the actual gamplay of the DLC itself.
