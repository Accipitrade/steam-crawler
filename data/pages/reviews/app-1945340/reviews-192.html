\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI usually don’t give actual reviews but given the mostly negative reviews as of right now I feel the need to give my opinions. [MINOR SPOILERS MARKED]

I’ve been playing since D1, so I have had my fair share of destiny (~500) hours and there are cons with this dlc, definitely, but in no way do I believe they outweight the pros enough for me to not reccomend it.

First, the planet. Neomuna is probably one of the most vibrant and cool locations we’ve been. The major gripe being the social space and areas near it are devoid of life and it is pretty noticeable. There is a lore explanation for this but I feel there could have been a better way around this. ——{Minor spoilers START} I think it would have been cool to see projections of citizens prior to what occurs a few missions in, in which they would then disappear and create a sense of dread and urgency for us to save the people of neomuna {Minor spoilers END}——.

Next, Strand. I’m a warlock main so I can only attest to that side but I think strand is fun as hell. It was a tad too forced during the campaign but it never really drew me out of the action. After not doing the legendary campaign for WQ I decided to throw myself into it and I’ve been enjoying the challenge, whether that be the fixed light level or the greater enemies. Strand was never really a hinderance during this but I would not at all mind if it wasn’t as forced as it is.

Nimbus. Cayde was hilarious and beloved, but now that we have a kid trying to fill the role of a hero the community thinks they are corny af? Of course they are. Nimbus is SUPPOSED to be written like that and I personally never had problem with their dialogue and it honestly adds to Nimbus’ charm as a young person trying to carry the weight of the world on their shoulders and finding an outlet in quippy humor.

Finally, the story telling. For me this was the biggest miss and it sucks because Im quite invested in the lore. —-{Minor Spoilers Start} I started the campaign wondering what the veil was and my fear of never knowing slowly came into fruition and the disappointing was I saw it coming from the start, it was a real slow burn. I can only hope this was a result of a possibly major change in the direction of lightfall and its relation to the final shape.

But all in all. Those are probably my only complaints and I don’t believe it constitutes as would not recommend. The art direction is so good in this expansion. The voice acting was amazing per usual but this expansion truly shines in its overhaul and advancements to the combat system. Although I think the initial stage of this new mod overhaul is not what it should be but it can really only go up from here. Though this isn’t even tied to lightfall directly. What is, is strand. Strand is mystifying and probably the coolest power us guardians have ever wielded. We can GRAPPLE, and any gamer knows that if your game has a grappling hook it is instantly better and that is no exception with destiny. Its 100% needs some tuning as to replace a grenade slot with a movement ability of comparable CD is lame. Other than that though, strand is sick. And the tormentors are truly terrifying. The hive guardians of WQ were neat but the tormentors blow them out of the water. When your screen turns red and you hear their shriek of terror you COWER.

All in all, my closing keynotes are this: If you are in this for the lore, you’re better off watching a byf video. If not I truly think you’ll enjoy yourself as I found myself pleasantly enjoying the game and I don’t think its deserving of mostly negative. Some negative reviews are def justified but not near this many. The overhauls and new mechanics will inevitably be a major net positive but only time will tell. I have faith in bungie and I hope they strive to make the single greatest story ever crafted in gaming with the final shape.\t\t\t\t\t
