\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me preface this by saying I love this game. 2k hours, been through thick and thin. But there are just some things that are inexcusable in this dlc.
1. The lack of payoff for the veil.
All throughout the campaign, this huge important thing, the veil, is built up. But in the final mission, it is just kind of some circles and branches, and the ghost says it feels like the traveler. What does this mean? What is the veil? Why is it so important?
2. The grapple cooldown.
For about the first half of using strand, everything has fast cooldowns. I expected this. But then in the latter half, the melee and super cooldowns are normal. So I thought, "Wow, if these are the normal cooldowns, I can't wait to use strand! The grapple having a low cooldown is SOO fun! I can't wait to beat this!" But then I beat it. and it's a 2 minute cooldown. I was worried pre Lightfall that it would have a 30 second cooldown (I thought that was outlandishly long) and my disappointment when I saw the little number was so big. The rest of strand is really cool and fun though.
3. The introduction of new things in the campaign.
Cloudstriders feel like we have been dunked into cold water when we are introduced. We just treat them like old friends, even though we know nothing about them. We focus wayyyy too much on strand in the campaign. We just kinda ignore the THREAT TO THE TRAVELER! Just to experiment with strand and stuff. Also the new weapons are literally just reskins.

==THE GOOD STUFF==
1. The campaign was really fun.
Legendary difficulty was very challenging and was super entertaining. I loved the tormenters and the final boss was incredibly difficult. But overall everything was super fun.
2. Strand (Other than the grapple cooldown)
I love the supers, the aspects fit the intentity perfectly, everything sounds really nice, and overall strand is just really fun.
3. The loadout system.
I don't have a whole ton to say about this, it's really nice to have.

Overall, Lighfall gets a 5/10. Hopefully Bungie can improve these shortcomings and make the upcoming content releases as good as the Witch Queen.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo preface this, this isn't a bad DLC in terms of gameplay. The gameplay is great. I mean, it's Destiny, after all.

The negative review is basically for how much this expansion falls flat on it's face in almost every way. The writing and dialogue is awful, no unique new weapons (They're all reskin of moon weapons), no ritual armor refresh, pointless veteran rank system, new mod system making some builds feeling absolutely gutted, the most lifeless city as a patrol zone, and some of the most interesting aspects of Strand (to warlocks, at least) being completely locked until next week or until the raid completion, so I can't even properly mess with the new subclass until that arbitrary check is fulfilled.

I would end my review there, but I just need to talk about the story, as it's absolutely one of the worst narratives we have gotten in Destiny. This is the penultimate expansion that is supposed to Final Shape, which would conclude the Light vs Darkness saga. So, there was a lot of hype around this expansion, since it seemed we were going to get a lot of our questions answered. Guess what? Absolutely NOTHING was answered. We still don't know what makes the Traveler so special. We still don't what the Witness wants with the Traveler. We don't know why The Witness even needed the Veil to do whatever he needed to do. What the hell even is the Veil? Nothing was explained, nor did any of the characters even question it.

And the writing is awful. We basically entered the apocalypse with the Black Feet entering our solar system, and yet we this whole expansion felt lighthearted due to the dialogue of one of the most annoying characters ever introduced: Nimbus. His surfer bro lines and attitude just felt as if this whole situation wasn't as big of a deal as it was. And it sucks too, cause I personally don't even hate the character like most people do. But he is way to centric in the story when he should have been given a more backseat role so his comic relief can feel appropriate, like Fynch in Witch Queen. Instead, we get a character that feels he overstays his welcome in the campaign, and some very terrible lines throughout.

Basically, all the hype and marketing for this expansion felt like a lie. It really feels like they phoned it in, and they probably did seeing as this technically was supposed to be the final expansion for the current saga, but they probably realize they didn't have enough time for everything so they decided to add Final Shape and use this as a stopgap to be able to have more time to flesh out whatever is gonna be in Final Shape.

Just buy the season if you want a good story. For some reason, it feels like the seasonal story is what the expansion should have been about, with the expansion story being the seasonal side story.\t\t\t\t\t
