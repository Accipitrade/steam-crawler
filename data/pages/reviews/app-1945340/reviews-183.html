This review pertains to the overall content model and the Lightfall expansion.

If there was ANY doubt in your mind that "the people who made Halo" have all left Bungie, Lightfall should make it painfully obvious. Not only do we get ANOTHER "season of sameness", it's another one with artificial difficulty. Artificial, because the design philosophy for "increased difficulty" seems to be bullet-sponginess, higher mob density and a new type of mini-boss - all stuffed into a closet-sized boss room. Which is EVERY boss room now. Even the mechanics feel designed to make encounters take as much time as they possibly can. For the difficulty of the legendary campaign, there are FAR too few checkpoints. No one wants to replay the same three platforms sixteen times. Especially not if the reason has nothing to do with skill and everything to do with glitchy abilities and cheap boss tactics. You could blame "bad RNG", but when these events line-up 7 times out of 10, I'm going to call you a liar and tell you it's DESIGNED to facilitate that type of situation. On top of that, class stats have been nerfed, so that you're less effective than you previously were. The armor mod system was also tweaked. Lots of mods are just gone...others work differently, so that you have to experiment to see what currently works, vs taking tried-and-true builds into the new content. Speaking of the new content... Neomuna feels empty. You'll get news feed audio and two - yes, just two - new NPCs.\nThe VA's are fine, but some of the dialogue sounds like it was written by feeding reddit posts to ChatGPT. The story felt very, very rushed and almost as if they farmed it out to someone who had only just watched a short video on Destiny lore and was instructed not to bother writing reveals or explanations for anything. What IS "the veil"? Why are there only two Cloudstriders? Why does this expansion suck "radial mast"? We're left to wonder. The one new NPC I thought was tolerable, they waste utterly. The other is the foulest type of cancer and should be lanced and biopsied immediately. There is no one to root for, no faction to really protect or commiserate with, nor does the ultimate antagonist even deign to try us. Instead, reaches waaay back, digs up a buried turd, paints it gold and throws it at us with a pair of his tormentors. "Hey, I thought we already killed him?", says my mate. Yeah. Me too.\nStrand, the new sub-class, is shoe-horned into every mission in such a way that it makes the content even more tedious. In fact, most of the story becomes "work with Osiris to investigate Strand", versus the epic war in which we're supposed to be engaged. That's bad enough, but worse, they consistently ask you to trade your carefully crafted build, with all it's synergies and forethought, for a grapple, a melee and a super...with no healing and no options. You _can_ avoid using it sometimes, but mostly, you're expected to bend over and take it. I might suffer this with a smirk, if Strand was any good. As a class, it feels like it was the pet project of the Warlock lead. For a Hunter, it's pitifully weak next to any other option. Titans' super is Wolverine melee? Seriously? You couldn't think of _anything_ else? You wanted a high apm sub-class with ultra-slick traversal and flashy skill synergies? ♥♥♥♥ you, here bomb.\nAll of this - every point of it - I could overlook or outright ignore...if the gameplay was FUN. It isn't. It just feels tedious. For the first time (mine, at least), I cannot find a single mission or area that makes this DLC worth having. I truly do_not_understand what the hell the point of Lightfall is. Are we being punished for something? Are you guys okay mentally? Blink twice if you need help...because this was a CRY for it. Normally, I'd devour new content for hours - but for the first time with D2, I'm actively putting-off what I could be doing to finish it. It's just going to be an aggravating slog that will eventually piss me off. If socials are any indication, I'm not alone in my appraisal. My advice to anyone looking at this DLC would be to set a timer for 1:45:00. Play through whatever you can until your alarm goes off. If, at that point, you haven't found a reason you love it...refund it and move on. I wouldn't expect it to get better with time. These devs may have a firm grasp on what actually makes a looter-shooter fun - but Lightfall just isn't.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere was a rumor floating around that the lead writer for Witch Queen was in charge of Lightfall. I can absolutely tell you either they produced a complete fluke with Witch Queen's fantastic story, or they were not actually allowed to make decisions for Lightfall and are in their position purely as a marketing gimmick.

This story introduces a bunch of concepts and never explains any of them, while Bungie says in blogposts that we're going to get explanations over the next year. Every season starts with the same amount of content as one quest in FFXIV, and adds another bit of that every week. It's a terrible model, one that Bungie will pretend they're better at then drop an entire expac with the same story formula. If you don't play Destiny, don't start now. Witch Queen was a great time, and in hindsight it was because you got to see the Destiny 2 that COULD be. Lightfall is Destiny 2 at a glance: it's all the INGREDIENTS of a good story, but in the wrong portions so when you try to put it all together it comes out an oozing mess that occasionally tastes good.\t\t\t\t\t
