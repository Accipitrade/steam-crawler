\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt breaks my heart to write this review, I love Destiny 2, but Lightfall leaves a lot to be desired.

TL;DR: Lightfall doesn't feel ready for prime time, if you're a patient player, wait for a patch or a sale, as of right now it's a season's worth of content with an expansion pack's price tag. Strand is fun if you like Strand and you're good at movement mechanics (I'm not), the build crafting simplifications will be useful to new players (I'm not), and the new UI is.... well, it exists. In terms of features, Lightfall has left me wanting.

Story notwithstanding (others can do a better job discussing the plot, of lack thereof) there are a few gripes that I have, personally. Cards on the table here: I'm lousy at traversal mechanics; I avoid vehicle combat in games, I got my butt whooped in the first level of the new Spider-Man because I couldn't get the feel for the motion and movement. Enter this season's biggest selling point: Strand.

Strand is introduced to us initially as a movement mechanic, it's webslinging and grappling hooks, and I'd say I spent a sold 30% of the three hour campaign missing jumps, missing swings, falling into pits, and lunging to my death over the edge of the arena. Admittedly the enjoyment and functionality of Strand will vary from person to person, but the game railroads players into using Strand to advance the campaign, which meant that I had a much more difficult and frustrating time than I would have had if I'd had more options.

If you are good at movement based mechanics and enjoy Strand then your experience of this expansion pack will be much more fun and fulfilling than mine was. Strand isn't for everybody, but everybody has to use it to progress the story, that's a decent way to ensure everybody knows how to use the system, it just makes some frustration for those of us who, well, suck at it.

Another big selling point is the simplification of the build crafting system. Almost all the nuance, complexity, and trade offs have been eliminated from the build crafting system; it's much more streamlined, and, in my opinion, much less fun. Part of the joy of the old build crafting was discovering how everything interacted together, a lot of this has been lost in the new streamlined system, it's all pretty straight forward now. Trade offs and interactions are minimal, the nuance and complexity aren't there anymore; it feels a bit like switching from a 200hp rear wheel drive sports car with a manual transmission to a 150hp front wheel drive econobox with an automatic, on the one hand that'll bring a lot more players into the build crafting game, on the other hand a lot of the fun is gone, and that lack of fun might end up pushing older players away.

Build crafting was a huge part of the fun (and frustration, yes) of Destiny 2, now it feels hollow.

I'm also sad to say that Bungie is moving away from the crafted weapon system; there are still plenty of weapons to unlock, but they're refocusing on encouraging the player to get top tier random drops, that's fine, I really liked weapon crafting, but maybe it'll be more prominent in future seasons. That's not really a thumbs-down worthy gripe, I'm just a little sore.

Finally there are the interface improvements, cleaner UI, easier access to information, stuff like that, most of which I haven't really used; having gotten accustomed to the old system I find the new one confusing and counter intuitive, I don't go into the "Journey" screen because it doesn't provide me with any especially useful information. Much like the changes to build crafting, I have to assume that the new UI features will make the game more accessible to new players, but as an old player I'm finding it obstructive.

Brass tacks: I LOVE Destiny 2, I enjoyed the heck out of Forsaken, Beyond Light was pretty okay, Witchqueen was brilliant, the Vanilla game is brilliant, there's a ton of fun to be had in Destiny 2, but I can't recommend Lightfall right now. The story feels like seasonal content, Strand is fun for people who will like Strand (but it's forced), the build crafting will open doors for new players (while closing doors for old ones), the new simplified UI is kinda' confusing.... the biggest objective benefit I can claim for Lightfall is that we can save our loadouts in game instead of having to use DIM, and that's nice, but otherwise? Otherwise I'd kind of like to go back to Season of the Seraph. To be fair to the game, once I'm used to all of these things my opinion might change, I don't know how I'll feel about the changes made in Lightfall when I'm six months into it, but my initial impressions aren't great; it could be that there's more depth to build crafting now than I realize, that Strand is much easier to use than I know, that the UI has information I haven't found yet, but right now I'm left feeling like I got a seasonal patch's worth of upgrades and changes, not an expansion pack.

If you're currently playing Destiny 2 you're probably going to buy this expansion pack, there's not much way around it, if you want to continue the game you gotta' pay to play. For folks like us, this review is a lost cause.

If you're an OLD player thinking about getting BACK into playing Destiny 2, wait for a patch or a sale. There's just not enough here to justify $49.99 in my opinion, Strand is a neat gimmick, but the story hasn't advanced very much and the under-the-hood upgrades offer debatable levels of improvement.

If you're a NEW player thinking about picking Destiny 2 up for the first time, DO IT! But don't rush to buy Lightfall. I can easily and strongly recommend the main game and expansion packs, except for Lightfall. My advice to you would be to play the free version, if you like it you can upgrade to the older expansion packs, and then after you finish those (hopefully) Bungie will have had time to square up Lightfall. Again, to reiterate: Vanilla, Shadow Keep, Beyond Light, and especially Witchqueen, are ALL worth your time and money (in my opinion) but Lightfall just isn't fully baked.

It breaks my heart to write this review, I love Destiny 2, I just don't like Lightfall very much. A lot of the improvements feel like setbacks, the new class isn't my cup of tea, the story is languishing, the gameplay is still as fun and as tight (and janky) as ever, but players can get that for free and for cheap; Lightfall simply doesn't add enough compelling content right now for me to give it a recommendation to anyone but current players (who will likely buy it anyway.)

(A note to any devs or engineers who might be reading this: I'm not criticizing your work, I'm criticizing the direction. I haven't run into any unexpected bugs, nothing is broken, the mechanics of the game are fine, and you guys did a good job developing what you were told to develop, I just don't like what you were told to develop. The technical points of Lightfall are exceptional, the talent and acting an execution are all on point, but it's not the technical points, or talent, or execution that are Lightfall's problem.)\t\t\t\t\t
