\r\n\t\t\t\t\t Lightfall was supposed to be our Infinity War. We were not supposed to win, and we didn't but the difference lies in the fact that there was no snap, half the universe did not get wiped out, there was nothing. We are still alive, the last city still stands, we still have our light, it did not feel like a loss. There were nine years of questions of the narrative that we anticipated would be answered and they weren't. There were many more questions than answers. What is the Veil, it's purpose, function? Why have we just discovered Strand, where does it come from? What is the Witness' end game, what did they do to the Traveller, why did they need the Veil?
If you're a new player I can't recommend this campaign and that hurts to say, even as someone who is invested in the lore, I didn't understand half of the things that were mentioned. The pacing was all over the place and Zavala and Ikora even had Mass Effect Andromeda level of weird facial animations while they were talking about defeat.
However, the gameplay was amazing. It was action packed, relatively fast paced, and blast. It feels like Bungie's narrative team just dropped the ball spectacularly. All I can hope for was that Witch Queen's level of story wasn't lightning in a bottle, never to be recaptured. Hopefully the seasonal stories to come may provide some clarity, but seasonal activites should not fix the short comings of the main story, they should push the marrative from the main campaign to the next narrative of the expansion to come. Overall, Lightfall was a spectacular gameplay experience but a giant flop in the narrative.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis story of this expansion is a huge disappointment and fails to deliver in any fashion to the buildup of the Witness or the coming war that Bungie has given and teased in the material leading up to release. The story has god awful writing in both the aspects of dialogue and direction with the addition of what could be the most disliked character to date and absolutely ZERO explanation to what anything is, why it’s happening or what it does.

SPOILERS AHEAD.


Cons:
1. Story is outright terrible. This is the second to last expansion (that we know of, they might decide to include another filler one at this point) and we know nothing about very important points.

The Radial Mast? No idea what that is, but they need it, we must destroy it.

The Veil? Teased since loooong before, right in front of us for 8 story missions. What is it? No clue. You fight the final boss under it, you still don't get any explanation during the main story about what it is.

Main villain? No clue about his intentions. Cuts a hole into our gods, enters it, leaves. Refuses to elaborate.

2. Despite that the poor narrative will still undercut every gameplay aspect. Mission goals feel confusing and repetitive as you waste time chasing McGuffins that are never explained with characters that are barely introduced that you're expected to care about. Origins of powers and locations are just swept past and the actions of even the main villain will fail to make sense and never get explained. If you feel like your missing out only the first and last cutscene have any significance to the overall story, nothing else mattered or even had to happen.

3. There are straight up ZERO new legendary weapons, outside of maybe the raid weapons. Every single "new" legendary weapon is just a reskin or reissue of an old gun, including the seasonal weapons. Even worse the perks on all of them are pretty bad, so I don't even feel like grinding for them, my old weapons are all better in pretty much every way.

4. The new exotics that we have so far are incredibly uninteresting. The new lmg is absolutely terrible. The heavy glaive basically just freezes things, which sounds cool but is basically useless when compared to the many other ways we have of freezing things and is a MASSIVE waste of your heavy slot. The stasis bow is pretty cool I guess, if more than a little predictable and uninteresting.

5. The two redone strikes are really bad. Lake of shadows went from being the fastest strike to one of the slowest, but not because they added in new interesting encounters, it's because they added in TWO seperate payload escort sections just to pad out the time. It's just joining the list of strikes that I instantly quit out of when grinding the strike playlist.

Overall, I would not recommend this expansion to anyone, unless you just really want what little content it offers. There is next to no replay-ability in any of it, no loot worth grinding anyway, and the vast majority of the changes (qol, new systems, etc) that bungie made are poorly thought out and poorly executed. 2/10.
