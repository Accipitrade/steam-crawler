\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't usually do negative reviews, but figured I'd say my issues with the current expansion (as, I have over 3000 hours in the game as of writing this, and the past two weeks at least, I have 0.0 hours in the game due to how much apathy I have for the game right now)

If you want to get into the game, I'd recommend picking this up on sale than full price.

I have no issue with Lightfall as far as how much content there is. Strand personally feels really fun to use. Neomuna IMO feels better to explore than the Throne World. (Witch Queen may have the story on lock down, but half of the Throne World felt like a swamp area, and I am just reminded of sewer levels in games, so Throne World did not feel very fun to "explore" to me, similar to how I hate desert levels in a lot of games as they feel "hot and miserable" to me) And I personally did not have any issues with the new characters (unlike some negative reviews... that's not a good reason IMO for the bad review)

My issues are that I feel like as this expansion came around, and even a bit before...

Well first, the story in this expansion didn't really answer any questions (something that was said would be a thing in the 2022 showcase) and their excuse was "the seasons will follow up with answers!" after that backlash.

Seasons are not part of the expansion. The expansion in the standard edition comes with ONE season, and once the new expansion arrives, the seasons featured in the previous expansions are shelved. (aka those "answers" pretty much with it, unless they do what they are doing this season for the cutscene they released a week or so ago and will have that as something to look back on... but even then, the content LEADING to that won't be playable unlike the campaign, which again doesn't answer questions)

The beginning cutscene and the end cutscene feel attachable almost like a lot that was inbetween could be phased out of story elements when looking back. (like, what was Zavala, Ikora and everyone doing the entire time while the Guardian and Osiris were on Neptune? Staring at the Witness staring at the Traveler?)

I could go even more in depth but I don't want to get too spoilery in the review.

Even before this expansion, Bungie really felt like they could not be happy with how certain Exotic weapons or armors could perform and disable them or outright nerf them to the ground if they feel it's too powerful, without a moments notice. Do I have a problem with nerfing things that are too powerful after being in the sandbox for a bit? No. Do I have a problem with Bungie shipping new armor/weapons and disabling/nerfing them right after because it looks like they didn't bother to test them? Kinda, yeah considering if they keep doing it, they might need to get more QA testers or figure something else out to not ship broken things on release of a new season/expansion. (hell, Quicksilver Storm, the preorder weapon for this before the expansion came out? It kept being disabled for WEEKS due to it not working like it should)

As for other things... Bungie's been really going monetization happy.

- Skip the campaign on other characters... for $20
- Changing season prices from $10 to $12... but you gotta buy it with Silver, and there is no $12 silver price so gotta spend $15 to get a new season (worst thing they've done IMO to cash in on new players)
- Dungeon keys. They used to ship with the expansion for free (buy the standard price expansion, get a dungeon too), once a year. Now there are 2 dungeons a year... but the price is, unless you get the Deluxe edition which comes with them, you have to pay $20 extra to get both. (second worse thing they've done, considering the dungeons are advertised with the season they are in, and can trick people into buying the season when they only had interest in the dungeons)
- Season events are pretty much there to sell more on Eververse, the only freebies you get are a new weapon and a sparrow/ghost shell. Meanwhile they added an event card, that costs $10 and you have 3 weeks to complete all the objectives or you can't claim everything on the card you paid $10 for.

Note: The only seasonal event I HIGHLY recommend playing would be Solstice, which is coming up as of this review. Unlike every other event, Solstice comes out with not only a new weapon each year, but a new armor set that if you complete the event, it glows too. The armor can then be transmogged (with currency in game) and you can then have an armor piece that IS from an event, that does have a white glow too. No other seasonal event gets this type of treatment, this is why I highly recommend playing during Solstice. (It won't have the glow that shows different colors depending on subclass choice, but that is something from Eververse, either purchasable for 6000 Bright Dust or 1500 ($15) Silver... while the white glow shows you put in some effort to get that armor... as 6000 Bright Dust on the spot is hard to get a lot of all at once)

But with how Bungie has been, I would not be surprised if they change this somehow too.

Between how they've handled their "all questions answered" expansion and their very greedy monetization plans, for a game that is "community driven" it hasn't felt very community friendly recently over the past several months, which has made me have less drive to play.

Bungie only hates it when players have apathy for the game?

Well I'm feeling pretty apathetic right now for Destiny 2.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall feels thrown together, like some of this should have been in Witch Queen (like Strand, and Osiris's struggle) but wasn't ready, so they delayed it and forced it into Lightfall. I really don't think I received my money's worth (especially with inflation) though seeing as how everything is walled off with time-gated and pay walled content I may change my tune as Bungie throws together more "value" of this DLC on a regular basis to expand the severe lacking story. The story in of itself gives more questions than answers as it pivots (again) it's story to add a tired trope of a MacGuffin to make the Light and Dark story more convoluted,and less fleshed out, than it really needs to be. From a character stand point I felt like phoning in the gay relationship between Osiris and Saint-14, would have more appeased the LGBTQIA+ community than pushed a story narrative in general. That's not to say I personally am against that way of life, but when it is thrown around so freely to cover any character traits or to make them more "interesting or filled out" it comes off more lazy than does any good from a story telling stand point. We see and hear through lore books and story more about the sexual relationships and proclivities about characters more than the nailing down actual story/narrative points (which have pivoted so many times during Destiny/Destiny 2. Personally where I feel they changed their tune was when we either sided with the Tower or The Drifter when choosing to be a "bad guy" or "good guy". Bungie panicked, and made the darkness a "it's however you wanna interpret it, dude" approach than actually extend their "10-year story telling" model by making a slight Civil-War within the tower and Destiny. No matter, rather than wish for what was, we get what is. What that is nothing short of a typical mediocre phoned in expansion that happens every so often. See, Bungie gets either lazy or has an overabundance of "bits and pieces" of content/ assets and cobbles together something resembling a few hours of playtime. Then the player base gets mad that they were sold this. A few silent weeks/months go by and Bungie makes an apology saying they will do better, listen to the player base, improve on delivering quality content. Lastly, Bungie puts something out that has the old spit and polish on it to lull the angry mob into a sense of satisfaction until the next ball is dropped and the cycle repeats itself (see: Destiny 1: The Dark Below, Destiny 2: Curse of Osiris, and "Vanilla" Destiny 2). I told a friend about all that's wrong, this time, with the story character traits and gameplay and he responded "Then, why don't you just stop playing?". Fair point, but with all this time put in to my character and witnessing all the story's twists, turns, and devolving I only responded "Well, with only one year left, I kinda just want to see how it ends...". In short, Bungie may have my wallet for one more year, but make no mistake, it's only because of the 10-year tale, certainly not because of their product which has been at their worst slap-dashed slop, and at their best, a life distracting looter shooter with potential.\t\t\t\t\t
