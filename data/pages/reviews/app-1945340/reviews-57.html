\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet me start off saying this: I don't believe Lightfall is the most awful, unplayable, boring thing in existence. I don't even think Lightfall is a "bad" piece of content. Just because I can't recommend something to others does not mean I hate it.

Now that the geeky and uncomfortably weird Bungie meatriders are gone, let me tell you why Lightfall isn't something I can recommend buying - especially for full-price.

Let me put it this way: You know when dads tell their kids "I'm not mad, son. I'm just disappointed." Of course you don't, Destiny 2 players have no idea what having a father is like. Every time I get hopeful for something in Destiny 2, I usually end up disappointed with what I receive. It could be a new season. It could be a new DLC/Expansion. It could be a simple hotfix or balance change that's been needed for a long time, but every single time we get something new, something goes wrong, and I and many others show the same sentiment of disappointment. This, of course, is my own fault - namely because I'm hoping for a different outcome when buying the same product for the same company that (sometimes) never fails to disappoint me. Again, I don't hate Lightfall. I really don't. I enjoyed quite a few things while playing through the campaign, and even some endgame content surprised me, but it has issues. Big issues. Big issues that make me want to slap myself for playing this game so much. (Don't even check my time on it, it gets worse on other platforms.)

*Spoilers ahead, but I'll try to negate anything major*

Going through this campaign left me feeling empty. And it's not just because I'm playing Destiny. Witch Queen was a mixed bag for me. The campaign was great, the narrative overall was great, the endgame content was fantastic, and weapon crafting is an interesting (but never settled in) idea, but I think you'd have more fun stapling your balls to a car on fire than playing any of the seasonal content. So, I don't think it's fair to say Witch Queen spoiled us, but it merely made us expect better from Bungie. We thought we were getting better. Lightfall proved that Bungie is either back to their old ways, or theyre smoking that Bhutanese shadow-garden-grown dark evil pack again. The story goes as follows: Calus and the Witness are here. The Witness is here for the Traveller. Calus needs a MacGuffin called "The Veil" in order to... well, do something with the Traveller. Calus invades Neomuna, a cybercity located on Neptune, which also happens to hold this "Veil" we're looking for. We meet two new characters on Neomuna: Rohan and Nimbus, two "Cloud Striders" or defenders of Neomuna and its people. We fight Calus and his army, yada yada yada, blah blah blah, etc. That's as far as I'll go to avoid spoilers.

Here's the thing, though: without spoiling the ending, that's as much explanation/exposition as we really get. We have no idea what's going on. Plain and simple. After completing the campaign, you'll be left with more questions than answers, which is what I THOUGHT Lightfall was going to remedy; I thought we were going to get some answers to the questions Destiny 2 players have had for years. But it didn't. Just like all of the story-related content we've received in past years, we have no idea what's happened. Sometimes, lore guys in the community such as My Name is Byf on YouTube will have videos up on what's happening behind the scenes with the story with every new season, raid, expansion, dungeon, etc. This is what's hilarious, though: even lore buffs have told me THEY have no idea as to what's going on - that's how you know things are really, really bad. We got more exposition on Nimbus's pronouns than we did on anything Darkness related. (joke, for the love of God, it's a joke.)

Speaking of Darkness - Strand. Strand is actually really fun. Pretty decent, too. Will it beat out the Light subclasses? Maybe. But how potent it is relies on your own buildcrafting, so it's hard to judge on its own. The fun factor is there, though. The problem with Strand lies within the Campaign, not Strand itself. The Lightfall campaign REVOLVES around Strand. To the point where the campaign feels like it's about strand, not about any of the things we - well, hear, not learn about in the narrative. While it can be fun, it has its weirdly cringe-y moments and completely ruins the story.

The weapons in Lightfall are... reskins. It doesn't necessarily make those weapons bad, but Neomuna's weapons are direct reskins of those on the moon, with all the personality of those on the moon removed from them. The new world drops are "new" weapons, but reskins of those of weapons that have existed in the past. I don't want to include season 20 with my review, but it's worth mentioning that the Defiant weapons are direct reskins of Season of the Lost's weapons. With Destiny being a game revolving around loot, especially the guns, reskins aren't the end of the world, but it leaves a sour taste in my mouth every time I see one. The new Root of Nightmares raid weapons are reskins of previous weapons, too. This is the first time we've had reskinned raid weapons since Crown of Sorrow, and Crown's weapons were reskins of Destiny 1's weapons, which weren't in Destiny 2 before. Again, it's really not the end of the world, but who wouldn't want to have new weapons for the new expansion you paid $50 for?

*Minor raid spoilers ahead, but nothing that wasn't in the cutscene everyone received once world's first was completed*

I wanted to wait for the new raid to review Lightfall as a whole. Root of Nightmares is... one of the raids ever. Not bad, but not great. The fight with Nezerac was disappointing, especially with Nezerac being an entity hyped up since vanilla Destiny 2. The mechanics throughout the entire were boring, especially because most of the people in the raid are on ad-clear, with the only exception being the third encounter, which I thought was really cool. Other than that, mechanics were boring, buggy, or... ad-clear. I think people will have fun playing the new raid, but it isn't a top contender for "best raid" for me, at least. Loot-wise, as I said, the weapons are reskins, but not terrible guns themselves, with the exception being a double-barrel shotgun, which is just hella cool. Thumbs up for the raid exotic, Bungie.

The future of Lightfall all depends on the seasons that come after, which is a whole debate in it itself. Whether the narrative gets better or worse, whether the content is good or not, and if good balance/QoL changes are made are the things that will make Lightfall a "recommend" or "not recommend" for me.

For now, I can't recommend Lightfall to anyone but the extremely dedicated and hardcore players of Destiny 2. They'll basically need the DLC to play anything relevant. If you care at all for Destiny's story, Lightfall won't do much for you. If you're a casual player, you'll probably give up the game trying to understand anything at the moment, and rightfully so. I don't blame you at all. And if you're a hardcore Destiny 2 player, well, don't BS me, you pre-ordered this DLC as soon as you saw that it was available to purchase.

If you've read this far, thank you, but you obviously are really frustrated with the game at the moment, or just care about your cash.

TL;DR: Wait for a sale if you really want to play this. Can't recommend it to most at the moment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are some great positives about this expansion, namely: armor mods, QoL features, a raid that doesn't require memorizing 3,719 symbols (Vow sucks, don't @ me). Strand (as Titan) is decent and the grind to unlock the fragments fits with the normal gameplay loop as opposed to how Stasis was in Beyond Light.

As for the bad: The story, for one. Witch Queen was its own story and the seasons for it expanded upon it. Bungie has jebaited people into having to play the story AND the seasons to get the full picture this time. The physics seem to be worse this expansion than any prior. They've recently made it to where physics impacts reduce you to 1 HP instead of outright killing you but everything seems to have that much more force behind it (phalanx boops, stomps, etc.) - all dialed up to 11.

The ugly: Power scaling. Gone are the days of power fantasy in my power fantasy game. Everything has a power handicap tacked on to it now, even Patrol...who thought this was a good idea? Coupled with the increase to health of enemies across the board, the combat experience is now reminiscent of OG The Division when everything was a bullet sponge. A wyvern now has more health than OG Valus Ta'aurc. Legendary primaries are almost entirely awful and the only way to get a primary to do damage is to use an exotic one (finally a justification for my dumb self who bought SUROS in week 1 of Destiny 1 over Gjallarhorn) due to their inherent damage bonus against the rank and file. The only activity I recall anyone asking for a more difficult time in is the strike playlist and it's funny because strikes and old patrol zones are the only places where the game doesn't feel like a sweatfest.

I wish I could get a prorated refund for the annual pass that I bought with the game. I can only hope they walk back the heavy-handed difficulty changes.\t\t\t\t\t
