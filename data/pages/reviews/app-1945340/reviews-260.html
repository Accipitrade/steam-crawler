Lightfall is a mixed bag. It was unbelievably hyped, by both Bungie and the Destiny community. It's the second last act to what is now a 9 year story that will be concluded next year in the final shape expansion, which will close out the Destiny saga. Strand looked amazing, with new movement capabilities that would completely change the way we approach combat in meaningful ways and introducing a whole host of new build opportunities. The revamps to difficulty, the introduction of legacy focusing, the mod manager, the introduction of load outs (♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ FINALLY), new pvp modes, a new destination in a neon cityscape, a brand new raid and the introduction of the Witness, the leader of the Darkness and the strongest enemy we have ever fought.


And yet.

I never bought into the hype, because after playing Destiny for 8 years and having been through every high and low this franchise has had I knew to never set high expectations for a Destiny release. Lightfall, in regards to it's campaign at the very least, is wholly disappointing.

Destiny's story has been made a priority over the last few years, becoming one of the core parts of the game after having been ignored and largely incomplete before. Bungie has delivered some actually great story beats, character moments and arcs, compelling villains and twists and turns into a game that once had a laughably terrible and incoherent story. Last years Witch Queen expansion had this story transformation on in full display, with the best writing and character moments Destiny has ever had wrapped up in a tight bundle of great mission design and as always exceptional gameplay. I highly recommend you purchase the Witch Queen to experience its story, because Lightfall is unfortunately a major step backwards from that.

Lightfall is about the arrival of the Witness, the leader of the Darkness that we have been hearing about since 2014. The Witness has arrived to initiate a second collapse, and along for the ride is former cabal emperor Calus, who we have frequently run into over the last 5 years of Destiny 2 (he was also the final boss of the Leviathan raid, or at least a robot version of him was) They are here for the Traveller, for...... some reason. That is the truly baffling part about this campaign. Even after beating the story, I cannot tell you anything about what the Witness wanted with the Traveller. I cannot tell you what the Veil is (a mysterious item we're sent after throughout the entire story), I cannot tell you anything about the Witness because he is not present at all during this story. We're mainly fighting with Calus, and Calus does little to nothing throughout the story as well, culminating in a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ garbage final boss encounter with him that is only difficult because of the ludicrous amount of tormentors the game throws at you. And once you defeat him, Bungie decided to have Lightfall end on an ambiguous cliffhanger where SPOILER ALERT

the Witness kills the traveller by cutting a portal into it and then he ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ leaves! He goes into the portal and disappears, with no explanation on what he just did or where he has gone. It's looking like he will just chill in the portal for the next year until we finally come face to face with him in the Final Shape. What a kick in the balls.

Lightfalls story in a vaccum when compared to every other destiny campaign besides the Witch Queen, is not bad. But it's how good the Witch Queen was that makes this campaign INSUFFERABLY bad and straight up LAZY. I can guarantee that Lightfall and the Final Shape were meant to be one connected story which were then split in half and released separately in order to make more money, and this has resulted in a story that feels incomplete as we wait another YEAR to see its conclusion.

I will be playing the rest of what this expansion has to offer, but so far, I am very disappointed even with my already low expectations.
