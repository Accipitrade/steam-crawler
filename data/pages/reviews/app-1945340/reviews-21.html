\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI don't know what to say about this to an extent.

The campaign's missions remind me woefully of the short and overly easy campaigns of Dark Below, Rise of Iron, and Shadowkeep. This is especially disappointing since the Witch Queen's campaign actually felt like a campaign. The levels were longer, and far better thought out than anything previous. Some parts in this feel somewhat thought out (the key section in First Contact), but it's clear this needed a delay, and I finished the damn thing on Legendary. The story generally feels barebone. We don't get insight into the Veil until the end, when it should have explained what these things are from the very beginning. Maybe I missed something since I didn't do most of the season stories (If so, please explain), and even then, most people aren't going to read the lore, so it should have been up to the writers to tie it in to make the story together. Strand is a bit of a ♥♥♥♥♥ to work with, also. Nimbus also reminds me too much of Pete Davidson. The Cloudstriders seem to lack depth and insight, and I wish we could have spent more time doing things with them until what happens to Rohan (Spoiler free btw, anyone who knows, knows).

For positives, there are things I like.
.Neomuna (though lacking in interaction, and bare feeling) is a stunning location. Whether it's the rising towers (That I wish could have been traversed more) or the neon lights, or Calus' fortress, there's quite a bit to look into.
.The Calus fight is legit challenging on Legendary. To be fair, I did cheese it, and I'm probably going to get shunned by either the community, or Bungie's moderators for this confession. Also, there's a scene with the Witness and Calus that was somewhat disturbing.
.Strand when working right can be a creative way of traversing (though it could be argued it needed a tweek with how it works).

I cannot say anything about the Root of Nightmares raid because I haven't done it. (Need to learn it since all the fireteams expect me to KWTD, should I research first?) But for the most part, this is not worth $50. The Season of Defiance story is ironically a bit better than this, though I could be wrong. If anything this is only really worth $30 at the most, but more importantly, it should have been rewritten, and delayed by a few months in order to get the story figured out.

Update as of July 15th, 2023: The Root of Nightmares is a gorgeous looking raid. Need to play it more since I mostly cleared ads. Pretty easy, but hate the platforming sections.\t\t\t\t\t

To top this off most of the campaign forces you and I mean literally forces you to use this subclass which is below par in every way to the subclasses we already have.
Examples: Ad clear void, arc, solar and even stasis significantly better. Champions Every subclass now has something for champions so eh. Mobility I mean top tree dawn before the nerf was faster and Titan with ramparts and sword can travel further. Survivability, Void beats strand hands down on every subclass thanks to devour solar also beats it due to resto.

Oh and need I mention that the big gimmick the thing everyone was excited for strand for is bound to your grenade so if you have a discipline build 1 it's practically wasted because the zip line thing comes back so quick and 2 it does mediocre damage at best even when you chain it with a melee.

A potential fix for strand I could see working. Unbound grapple from grenade rebind it to x like hunter dive. Replace Grapple with a grenade option maybe a threadling turret or something. Don't force people to play it for campaign missions that are required for progression. Finally, fix warlock jumps. Strand really does help warlock when it comes to jumping because it gives them recovery from bad jumps but we shouldn't need an entire subclass to fix this problem.\t\t\t\t\t
