\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: story bad, strand very good, rec'ing for the new zone, subclass, and (hopefully a good) raid. Would not rec for people who only play for story/lore.

Story:
Storyline was confusing and disjointed. No explanation of the McGuffins we're chasing, which wouldn't be an issue if every other character didn't act like they knew what the McGuffins were and were just refusing to share that info with us.

Strand was clearly designed for Witch Queen and due to Light 3.0 changes was moved to Lightfall. It shows, and tragically so, through the over-emphasis on Strand during the campaign.

Tone of the story is all over the place; again, see the above complaints about the McGuffin, but also that characters really don't know how to read a room. This is the end of the world, of humanity, of the Light, and possibly of existence, but the tone does not match those stakes at all. The season's tone (1 mission so far) much more appropriately matches the stakes.

Content:
1 strike, 1 subclass, 1 raid (to be released). No updates to Crucible maps, no changes to Gambit (no surprise there). Feels like not enough considering the increased cost/requirement to buy the first season alongside the expansion.

Gameplay:
Strand feels absolutely incredible during the campaign. The ViDoc mentioned wanting a high APM (actions per minute) gameplay style, and they hit the mark during the campaign with aplomb. I only wish there was a high APM mode I could go into that would reduce potency of Strand abilities but increase uptime massively as it is a very enjoyable playstyle.

Overall thoughts:
I wish they had taken some more time with this story. I've replayed the campaign on my alts to unlock Strand, and if it's approached with a view of "no one knows what the McGuffins are, but we know the Witness/Calus want them so we're gonna make sure they don't get them", it's more palatable. No one knows what the McGuffins really do; our side (Guardians and Co.) just found out about Neomuna's existence, and the two Neomuni we interact with don't know much about the McGuffins either because that's not their job or area of expertise. Making that clear was imperative, and Bungie did not make that clear which has led to so much backlash and confusion in the community.

If you wanna play with Strand, you're gonna have to get Lightfall. I think Strand, Neomuna, and the raid this next week will be worth the price, but at this point that's largely dependent on the raid. If you aren't planning to day 1 raid and aren't jonesing for Strand, I'd say hold off on getting this for now and get it on sale when Bungie drops the price to try to make up for the massive backlash happening now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnfortunately, I can't recommend this expansion.
I've been playing the franchise since the Destiny 1 beta and unfortunately this expansion is a huge step in the wrong direction. And that's after Witch Queen and the end of Season of Seraph, which both hinted at a bright future for Destiny.

For 50€, this expansion contains far too little new content:

- a story, which was probably radically changed in development (like Destiny 1) and raises even more questions than it answers (if answered at all)
- ONLY 1 new Strike (2 old ones were MINIMALLY "renewed")
- 0 PVP maps
- Gambit completely dropped
- no dungeon
- no new core vendor armor
- at least we still get a RAID (if it goes on like this, they won't be released with expansions anymore...)
- Timegated endcontent and not much to do in general
- Reskinned weapons from previous seasons
- public Events are the same from Destiny 2 Year 1 ...

All in all it's just too little for a big expansion, which should prepare us for the grand finale. Every year less content is delivered for more money.

Some positives:

- Art design of the new planet Neptune and the city Neumuna is top, as always.
- The soundtrack is a 10/10
- Strang is currently very fun and still seems quite Balanced
- the few new weapons are fun and have good perks

Furthermore there are many QoL improvements like loudouts, recommendations, etc.
But these improvements are not part of the expansion and available to everyone for free, so they do not improve the rating of the expansion.

Bungie, what happened after Witch Queen?
Just disappointing, once again....\t\t\t\t\t
