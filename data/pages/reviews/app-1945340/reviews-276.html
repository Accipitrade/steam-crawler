\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt started out with the narrative text talking about the new reputation vendor in the they/them pronouns which, whatever, but it made trying to read it hard to understand until it dawned on me what was actually going on in the words I was reading. It didn't help that this character's voiced dialog reminds me of every low IQ TikTok video I've had the displeasure of experiencing.

The story is fine. It's a serviceable video game story. Nothing mind-blowing. No wow's this time around, sadly. It's short, predictable and to the point. Enough to keep me on the same page with where the overall theme of Destiny is heading. It just isn't up to par with the last expansion, not even close.

The cyberpunk atmosphere is disappointing. I was hoping for something really eye-popping and atmospheric, but it's just a bunch of bland, empty areas with a lot of neon colors. What a shame, as I am really into the retro cyberpunk style and this is what really got me excited for Lightfall.

The combat is still solid, however. The new strand business is a great addition. Some people say it's under-powered, but I think it's safe to say it will be tuned over time. Aside from that, overall, it's essentially more Destiny 2; more of the same gameplay loops with a new coat of paint, new loot to grind, etc. The minimum expectations are met. What really shines are the QOL additions, which are really great for veteran try-hards, who need to collect everything and min-max their builds, swap things around on the fly for different activities. For everyone else, it may not be that substantial.

Devs are either still working from home, and you could argue that it shows, or too focused on Destiny 3 at this point to really keep the Witch Queen quality going. Either way, nothing we can do about it now. It is what it is until the final expansion.

It's fine. High-tier Meh from me, coming from a big fan of the franchise. I'll probably keep playing it. I wouldn't recommend anyone drop what they're doing to buy this at full price though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWorst Destiny 2 expansion in a very long time.

After pursuing a serious tone to the game for many seasons, we're back to some of the most cringe-worthy quippy dialogue ever written. The story itself is pretty weak for the first time in awhile as well. Feels like they had the b-team cooking on this one because it's not good. The mod system has been drastically simplified, which would be good if it hadn't also been completely gutted. The gameplay loop that most builds had prior to this just doesn't exist anymore. The new subclass has atrocious damage and survivability like everyone thought it would, but they also bamboozle you during the campaign with about a 20x faster cooldown on the only neat part, the grapple. Once you start playing the rest of the game it's on an insanely long CD and it feels like a huge bait and switch.

The new player experience is improved and the commitment to no per-season grind is nice, but this is a major step backwards for Destiny. I played several hundred hours last year and went into this season perhaps more hyped than I've ever been for an xpac and now I'm honestly questioning if I'll still be playing Lightfall in a week or two.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tone step forward

three steps back then tripping and falling down the stairs.

Every issue the community had with the game was being "resolved" in this expansion. This was a lie... With the success of the witch queen you would expect bungie to have learned their lessons from that success and improve for this release. There's so much that's wrong with this the best I can do is give a bullet point list.

before i begin let me give a caveat

most of the stockholm syndrome ridden community knows the truth that most critisim levied at bungie is usually accompanied with the asterisk of "things will be changed in the future" and that many of the problems with the game have been resolved. This is an excuse for poor content releases and copium for us to hope the game improves.

but lets begin anyway...

>Buildcrafting nuke and revamp: with this recent release, bungie has sunset or reworked a majority of the mods within the game, this should change alot, but when it comes down to the wire, nothing has changed, builds have just become less creative and more generic and streamlined, as the multiple playstyle mods have been all combined into a single playstyle, thus gutting the diversity of builds as they are all focused around a singular resource. This shouldnt be too awful in the long run, but the changes feel hollow and kinda redundant.

>Plot and characters are underwhelming: This is supposed to be an active apocalypse scenario, and the game cant keep a thematic tone. This is supposed to be a status quo changing event, but literally nothing happens. And a good portion of the cast have just kinda... vanished? It's also not helpful that the new characters are boring at best and annoying at worst.

>The more things change, the more they stay the same: Powerlevels need to be grinded out, but you will always be capped below the powerlevel of the mission, making them redundant, powerlevels are a feature that needs to be removed or reworked. Making enemies tankier and players squishier is not an increase in difficulty, it's annoying.

>Disrespectful of players time: I wanted to mess around with the new subclass and make some builds... too bad a majority of the fragments have been locked behind arbitrary time gates so I cannot even unlock them for a week and a half...

>all of these issues pail in comparison to the real issue. the community has repeadiately voiced their opinions on these issues, and pleaded that they not return for this expansion... but they returned reguardless.

>the biggest issue tho is the grapple hook has a nearly 2 min cooldown.\t\t\t\t\t
