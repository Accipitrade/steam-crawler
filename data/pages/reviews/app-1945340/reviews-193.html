\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe amount of negativity being thrown at this is insane. This has been out for literally 2 days and the only valid criticisms I've seen are some new bugs (which should honestly be expected at this point it's not easy to completely redo a massive part of the game like armor mods and add a completely new subclass) and quippy dialogue which can be annoying at times but doesn't ruin the entire campaign experience like some people think it does.

The legendary campaign missions were a blast, and as someone who went through most of them with someone who hasn't played in years, were actually challenging at some points. Loved the difficulty of the final mission and some of the halo-esque moments in some of the other missions.

Neomuna is sick and it's clear that a lot of time and effort was put into it. Also loving all of the little secrets hidden around the patrol zone like the action figures. Very detailed and it's a nice addition to the mostly desolate locations we've grown accustomed to.

I love Strand's utility, it might not be the most defense-oriented subclass but it rewards fast movement through tough combat areas. The grapple hook is nothing but fun and just builds upon the "go fast" dynamic that's been established with Arc 3.0 and Eager Edge. The way new Strand abilities/aspects/fragments get unlocked has been immensely improved upon compared to Stasis in Beyond Light.

Again, this has been out for 2 DAYS. ONLY 48 HOURS. The raid hasn't been released, the seasonal story is in WEEK 1, there's new exotics and craftable weapons that a lot of players don't even have yet. It's easy to focus only on the negatives, especially when there's glaring problems and not everything has been presented perfectly, but remember that this was made by people who care very deeply about the game they're creating and who have spent and will spend a lot of their time on this incredible world for people to continue to enjoy.

As someone who's been playing since the D1 beta, Lightfall is a mile ahead of what was presented in D1 vanilla and deserves way more credit than it's receiving. Bungie continues to listen to community feedback and implement it in the best way they can. It might not be perfect, but development takes more time than a lot of people realize. The bugs will be patched, systems will continue to be improved, and this will keep being one of the greatest games to ever be released. Just be patient and enjoy the ride.\t\t\t\t\t
