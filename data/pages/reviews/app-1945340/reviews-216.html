\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall should not have been released in its current state. It's a half hearted effort for the big clash which the game has been building towards for about a decade now.

The new characters introduced are extremely poorly written for the world the game is set in and more so for this DLC. The constant barrage of lame one liners is straight out of an 80s comedy action movies. It surprises me that it passed any kind of Quality Checks.

For a 50$ expansion, just 1 new strike is not acceptable. Most of the 'new' weapons are just reprised Shadowkeep weapons. Crucible and Gambit are nothing more than an after thought for Bungie.

The entire mod system has been refreshed into something which is lacking and should have been not releases unless they had more variety of mods ready at launch. This entire thing about making Destiny challenging is nothing but an effort to increase play times. Sure there were some outliers that needed to be reigned in but Bungie rarely strikes the right balance.

The new Darkness subclass, is fun to play with but the cool down times and the poor damage output on it make it more or less unappealing in anything higher than Legendary activities. Maybe the timegated aspects will change it.

At this point, I am convinced that Bungie wants to put Destiny on cruise control mode and milk the cow dry till their new IP releases. Just 1 new PvP map in the last year atleast is for me ample proof of Bungie's intentions. Bungie is not a small indie studio that doesn't have access to resources. This was akin to Game of Thrones season 8. It is so bad that I don't think I will be buying Final Shape at all. Very disappointing.

That said, as usual the music is pretty solid. The opening mission has some beautiful visuals. And strand when empowered is fun to use. The last fight of the DLC was amazing albeit it felt tailored for the new aubclasa.

Only buy the expansion if it goes on a sale and like me you love the game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter a bit more than 2000 hours on Steam in Destiny 2 comes my first review. Destiny has always been a hard game to recommend to new players, but this time especially. This year, we were supposedly getting the "penultimate chapter", the "penultimate experience", etc. but this has to be one of the most over-sold and over-hyped piece of games to release in a good while.

People, I hate to say it but Bungie dropped the ball with this one.

Positive stuff first.

-New subclass seems reasonably fun to play.
-New subclass looks cool.
-Season 20 story and gameplay seems sweet.

Negatives?

Humanity and its allies stand at the precipice of total annihilation. Who is to help us in this predicament? A campy and happy-go-lucky weirdly designed giant of a child. Story and writing worthy of a 8th grader lost in their delusions after reading too much modern Marvel comics. If you've played Borderlands 3 and watched the twin antagonists, well Bungie delivers an even cringier dialogue here, especially taking in mind that we are supposedly facing our doom in this expansion. BL3 is a comedy game from the get-go, Destiny 2 isn't.

Want the real review? Go on Reddit and watch the whole place united in unanimous disapproval of the story and writing.
