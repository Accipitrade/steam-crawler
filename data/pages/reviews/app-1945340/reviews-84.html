\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter spending a week with Lightfall I can confidently say that it's not as bad as some outlets are saying. It's not as good as Witch Queen or as transformative as Forsaken, but it's a damn sight better than both Shadowkeep and Beyond Light. It's definitely better than Curse of Osiris, Warmind, and even D2 Vanilla.

I've adapted to the changes to the armor mod system. I still think it's less robust and more restrictive than the old system, but I can still make powerful builds. I think these restrictions were put on as training wheels for newer players or players that were intimidated by the old system. I can sacrifice what I liked about the old system for the sake of those players. I think being able to engage with one of the cooler parts of Destiny is a net positive overall.

The story in the campaign isn't the best they've done, but also not the worst. The post campaign story has been excellent however, and that gives me a lot of hope for the rest of the year. If they can continue this narrative consistency, we'll look back on Lightfall's narrative fondly. If they instead decide to follow anachronistic storylines then this will be something that Final Shape has to make up for. I do think Nimbus is kind of a miss in the context of Destiny. If they were a little more serious, or had an arc that made them feel more adult then maybe... but as it stands right now... they're just annoying.

One thing I've always wanted in Destiny was to roam the streets of the Last City. Personally, I wanted more context for what I was fighting for. Families and friends huddled under a silent god to claw back from extinction was always compelling to me. I've long since accepted that Destiny was never going to have that because of how resource intensive it would be. Anytime we have gone in to the Last City, it has felt abandoned. So Neomuna feeling lifeless is par for the course. We were never going to get an actual wide open map of a city. So I'm less disappointed in that. I'm frustrated that Bungie even suggested that it would be a full city. As it stands, the location is actually very fun to explore, but its still a Destiny location. Understand that going in.

I really like the increases in difficulty across the board, and the commensurate reward increases. Even though each Nightfall completed is taking longer, I'm getting way more for my time spent. Plus, it's just satisfying to know that your build is actually good, and not just good in strikes. I think that's going to be off-putting to some players, especially new ones. I wish that there were more granularity in difficulty choice.

The guardian rank system and commendations need more work. There's a good foundation, but tying commendations to progress and pinnacle rewards only serves to undercut their purpose. Right now, I just randomly give them out without thought, and I'm sure others do too. A commendation effectively means nothing except that we're all helping each other out to reach a higher guardian rank.The rank system resetting is also a miss as it doesn't accomplish what guardian ranks sets out to accomplish. To let other players know your proficiency in the game. I still like the system though. It just needs to be refined.

Finally, Strand is crazy fun. Strand is worth the price of admission alone. The extra mobility, the utility, the new builds. All of it is very very impressive. Right now it's a little limiting because you only have two aspects to choose from. That's changing next season, and I can not wait.

All in all, I like this expansion despite its faults.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t9 years and 1500 hours have consistently proved that Bungie is out of it's depth with managing a game of this size and complexity. They simply do not have the technical or design skills necessary for Destiny to live up to it's potential, and are ok with this game hobbling from season to season barely holding together.

The worst part about this is that the moment to moment gameplay is great but the game that surrounds the 30 second loop has become arduous, frustrating and tedious. So many problems can be attributed to a lack of common sense when it comes to basic game design, such as difficulty correlating to rewards. Legend lost sectors are now an order of magnitude more difficult but have not had their rewards increased in any meaningful way. Player power has been dialed back, which while needed was done carelessly. Stasis builds are miles behind other subclasses due to the loss of elemental fragments. Non-Exotic Primary weapons feel like they are doing less damage than a rudely worded email to the enemies.

In short, if you have not been sucked into this black hole of time and happiness called Destiny, steer clear. If you have, please lose the sunk cost and FOMO fallacies and stop associating with this clown fiesta of a franchise, because the only thing you will miss out on is frustration.\t\t\t\t\t
