\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: This is an 8 out of 10 expansion, by Destiny standards. It's pretty good in a lot of respects and disappointing in a couple others. Narrative is a bit weak, but mostly just by comparison to Witch Queen- it's a fairly average Destiny plot. Legendary campaign feels a bit easier than the WQ one, which can be either good or bad depending on your perspective. Strand needs more development but initial impressions are that it's fun and has a solid niche in PvE as a sort of control/lockdown focused subclass (arguably better at it than non-Bleak Watcher Stasis builds).

General systems improvements across the board have been very positive. I think the armor overhaul was a success and am enjoying the new system more than the old one (also helped simplify inventory woes by removing armor elements, so that's another plus). The new engram/vendor rep systems feel more fun and rewarding than previous ones. Despite community bellyaching about increased difficulty in the week leading up to launch, I think the improved rewards from most playlist activities more than make up for it. The commendations system is fun, from my limited experience with it thus far. Feels good to give them out to people you enjoyed playing with, and to get them back in return.

Most of the points where it falls flat are areas I have also found other expansions lacking in the past, so I'm not judging it as harshly in those regards as some others are. Witch Queen set a high bar for campaign story and Lightfall definitely doesn't quite make the mark (unsurprising, it's the same writers who worked on Beyond Light, not the ones who did WQ). I found the end of the campaign fairly disappointing largely because I was hoping for some answers to holes in the narrative and just got more questions instead. Aside from the ending, though, the narrative was fine. The pacing was decent. The individual character writing was fairly good- I enjoyed the snippets from lore books and thought the banter with the NPCs was mostly on the better side. I actually liked Nimbus, which is probably a hot take- I thought their goofball attitude was endearing and helped keep the campaign from being "get yelled at by old men" simulator. (Seriously, between Rohan, Osiris, and Calus, there's a lot.)

Neomuna is a fun destination. I enjoyed my time there a lot more than either Europa or the Throne World, honestly, but that's probably a matter of aesthetic preference more than anything else. (Personally, I got a bit sick of the Throne World feeling like it was one third castle, one third swamp, one third scorn-infested cave, especially when the castle environments were the only one of the three I liked.) I liked exploring around and checking out the city. One of the new lost sectors is really fun (it's a vex-infested arcade full of really goofy props and vex goblins standing around playing arcade cabinets)).

Strand is a mixed bag. I'll admit that I was one of the Titan mains worried about Berserker being disappointing pre-release. After getting to use it a bit... I'm still not very fond of it, but I like it more than Stasis, ESPECIALLY aspect-less launch Stasis Titan. In general, Strand at launch is a lot like Stasis was at launch, which shouldn't be surprising. It's definitely better in several respects- unlocking the aspects and fragments is a lot less annoying. In terms of how "strong" it feels, "Suspend" is an incredibly strong debuff, I started building my kit around applying it to strong enemies to lock them down and it's honestly really fun. Haven't touched PvP yet so can't speak for that side, but I'd be surprised if it was nearly as strong as peak Shatterdive was or anything like that.

There's definitely a bit of feelsbad with the Strand kits right now- the Berserker aspects mostly feel disappointing and I really wish I had the option to put my grapple in my melee slot instead of my grenade. Bladefury as a super is yet another Titan roaming super, which is disappointing in and of itself- but I'll give them that it's the best feeling Titan roaming super I've ever used. Not a particularly high bar to clear, but it is something. Can't speak for the other two Strand subclasses as I haven't used them yet. Broodweaver looks like a lot of fun, excited to give that a try once I can get my hands on it.

Overall I genuinely think this is a mostly good expansion. Sucks that the plot is disappointing. Neomuna is cool, I enjoyed exploring it and I think it makes for some great combat arenas. Strand is fun and I hope it gets more polished in future updates (which is exactly what happened with Stasis). General gameplay flow changes are very good. Season 20 is not DIRECTLY related to the campaign, but I will say the changes to the seasonal model seem quite promising and the S20 exotic bow is a lot of fun to use, I used it for a lot of the campaign missions. My expectations for Lightfall plot-wise were not that high, especially after the writing felt a bit rocky in the past year of seasonal content, and they were more or less met. Have to agree that the campaign ends anticlimactically, but at this point, if you're only playing Destiny for the story... why even bother? Just get it all from Youtube videos, it's not really worth your time for plot alone. If you're mostly just here for the gameplay, you'll probably enjoy Lightfall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first "DLC/Expansion" I've reviewed for this game; that should say a lot.
- So far, I've completed the Legendary Campaign, have completed several of the new quests.
- This is not a review of seasonal content associated with this year.

This expansion just did not check any of the boxes for me. Developers stated that the object of this expansion and associated story was to provide the player the feeling of "being in their own action movie." Following Witch Queen, I had a lot of expectations story and game-play wise. Overall, the story had time for the "action" and no time for the "story" part.

Everything about this expansion was rushed. Characters express the least emotion over the death of a shipped character, who had "expendable character energy (thanks Byf for that description) about 20 minutes after meeting him. Character development for more permanent, new characters also was lackluster. After completing the story, I absolutely do not care about this new location, the inhabitants, or their way of life. I would not have the player sacrifice anything for them.

Overall, the story was a "strand tutorial" of a version of the new subclass which we as players have no access to. I understand there are some balancing requirements, but the play through had enhanced abilities and game-play which are not available to the player at the moment.

A lot of people are comparing this expansion to Warmind. Xol, our main enemy for Warmind, is I think comparable to Calus. On completing the final mission for the Witch Queen, I encountered a character that had depth, motivation, and powers I was afraid of encountering. Calus is a neutered character with a gun and an AoE attack. Honestly, the Tormentor unit is more scary that this bloated space rhino.

A few things need to happen before I edit this review, in any way.
1.) The raid needs to be both substantial, and provide answers to a lot of the plot holes and confusing narrative devices.
2.) Ditto on the seasonal content for this current season.
3.) A buff to PvE strand recharge rates. I don't want to be spider-man, but the subclass should be on par with what was introduced in the campaign.

As of right now, I cannot justify spending $50 US on content that is the equivalent of what I expect from a seasonal story.

Some nit-picky items:
- I do not want to be familiar with Nimbus. His character has demonstrated no value, nor power. The player character has killed gods. He is not the player character's peer.
- Nimbus' voice did not need the synth. Or rather, apply the same voice effects to Rohan as well.
- For newer players, explain the significance of the Ishtar Collective.
- For newer players, explain why everyone is freaking out over Nezarec's voice.
- I thought this game was past the "Chase the MacGuffin" phase.

I'll edit this review, if needed, after the raid release. We'll see how that goes.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThey really could've done better with... basically everything. This review is going to be a disorganized collection of thoughts and criticisms I have:

The story is just... god awful, really. They KEEP leaving us on yearlong cliffhangers, these DLCs hardly even constitute half a season if you measure this like a TV series.

Neomuna is a lifeless and boring location that completely underdelivers on what makes cyberpunk and futuristic style cities interesting to look at and explore. Full stop.

Reduce the grapple cooldown, seriously. A minute for one charge on 100 discipline is horrid. Strand feels way too much like a gimmick class that is pretty much outmatched by every other class in terms of deadliness. Knife/Grenade spam on Solar Hunter with Spine of Ahamkara is infinitely stronger and a much better room clearing device.

ALSO, PUT GRAPPLE POINTS ON ALL LOCATIONS, PLEASE.

They completely removed Charged With Light mods for seemingly no reason. Literally butchered all of the charged with light builds I used, which were ALL usable in all content. Between the Armor Charge system and weapon crafting, Bungie seems content to dumb down everything as much as possible. All of the Charged With Light builds Youtubers and common folk alike have put together are completely useless now.

Literally ALL of the legendary weapons from Neomuna AND Season of Defiance are reskins. ALL OF THEM. EVERY SINGLE ONE. That neat handcannon from the title art is nowhere to be seen ingame, and that just makes me sad. Hoping it's some secret exotic quest soon, otherwise I'll be really disappointed.

I'm very disappointed, again. I keep thinking you guys'll get better after a small bump in story quality in a previous season just to get ♥♥♥♥ on by the team's consistently bad writing, abysmal story direction, and inability to keep me engaged. If all of the good writing has to come from text in a lore book, then you're doing something wrong.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe first words that came out of my mouth after finishing the Lightfall campaign were: What the ♥♥♥♥ happened? Not in a good way.

Man was this really disappointing. The raid hasn't come out yet but what happened with this is already enough for me to not recommend it even if the raid hits hard.

The Lightfall campaign is fun to play through, but it makes little to no sense in how it is explained and progressed. It seems painfully obvious that this was a quick make up for the real Lightfall expansion and split up into two so they can make more money. They need to realize Witch Queen was their most financially sold expansion being released just days before Elden Ring was because they refined everything they've learned from their past mistakes to make a nearly perfect expansion. By releasing a inferior product to that not only makes people feel bummed, but it violates the trust established that a comprehensive campaign can be written and played out. Neomuna's patrol zones barely feel like a city with being able to travel between skyscrapers and stuff and battling it out with Cabal at road checkpoints.

Everything else was good in the game. (I'll update for the raid.) Strand is really fun and the post campaign quests are decent.

I do not recommend this expansion to anybody because it is overpriced at 50$. I think it would be fair enough for it to be lowered to 40 or 35 still including the season. If you're desperate for the Strand subclass with the grapple hook (Which was really fun to play around with.) then you can shell out 50$ at your own expense. Just don't expect much story wise from the campaign.

Although Final Shape is probably the one that'll pack more of a punch, I am not going to be ever buying an expansion again from Bungie unless they prove to me that Lightfall was a accidental fluke in planning and writing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall Review:
I'll just post this review now because I feel like sharing my opinion and disappoinment towards this campaign. This review solely covers only the main campaign experience of the lightfall and not the seasonal content because I just started playing the seasonal stuff.

I'll start with the pros first
- great combat encounters and set pieces. Very bombastic and action packed. I really enjoy the legendary difficulty the same as Witch Queen was last year. Please keep making legendary and high difficulties modes for campaigns and other activities.
- Tormentors are fun to fight
- Strand is a decent addition to the subclasses and quite fun to play with.

Now to the issues:
I can't express how disappointed I was when the campaign end so abruptly and with a weird cliff hanger. I thought this was supposed to be the moment we ramp up our fight aganst The Witness. Witch Queen was a great campaign and its seasons were so good leading up to lightfall narratively that you expect something more substantial to happen now. Instead, you have a campaign that barely explains & solve anything, makes you spend half of the campaign training using strand instead of doing important stuff, barely introduce the new characters you met & in my opinion failed to sell any sense of an ongoing life threatening conflict that is happening in Neomuna. The world building in Neomuna is so weak in my opinion. I honestly think they should have just package this together with Final Shape to make a more complete and satisfying narrative. I hope the story picks up during the seasonal content and i hope the next expansion does not disappoint.
