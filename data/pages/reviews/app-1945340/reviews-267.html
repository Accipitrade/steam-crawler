\r\n\t\t\t\t\tRevising my Review:

I firmly believe this DLC is a strong 7/10; the story is frustrating and the tone feels so insanely misplaced it makes my head spin. This doesn't feel like the time to set up key players and objects with the Final shape looming on the horizon - is the time inbetween going to be enough to tie off any loose ends??

Gameplay wise though, Lightfall knocks it out of the park. Loadouts are snappy and fast, lots of activities feel sufficiently challenging, and strand feels amazing. There are builds and times where it feels like just "Green stasis", but sets itself apart with its distinctively more aggressive playstyle I feel. QOL has improved a LOT with the update. Ada-1 becoming Shada-1 is fantastic.

I would like to hope that the reception to the story is a hard lesson for bungie, but that feels disingenuous after we had Witch Queen, which is one the best written stories D2 has had to date. Not sure what happened in development but its been really worrying to see such a shaky reception so close to the end of the light and dark saga,

Still, excited for the years to come, and I hope we get more expansion on what a lot of things are soon; In the meantime, strand is great, and I will zip around with it lots :)\t\t\t\t\t

Lightfall: the good and the bad (spoiler free)

The story
The good:
- As many have said, Destiny doesn't miss with its villains. Calus is great yet again in this expansion, and the Witness maintains that nebulous, mysterious presence it had in all its prior appearances.
- Some of the negatives below are somewhat alleviated in the post-campaign quests, which are in my opinion better than those of Witch Queen. If you are left with many questions after finishing the campaign (which you will be), do yourself a favor and go complete the two exotic quests that unlock (rewarding the machine gun and glaive).

The bad:
- Why should I care? Many of the key plot elements of this campaign are never satisfyingly explained. Who are the cloudstriders? (no, not just their names). What is the Veil? Why do we need to protect it? We went the whole campaign without ever once asking these questions, and the final cutscene answers basically none of these while instead asking even more new questions. I don't want to have to read the lorebooks to understand the narrative of the campaign I've just played.
- There are several plot holes throughout the campaign and missions I won't get into here, but were quite noticeable. It felt like we just glossed over some major details for the sake of advancing the plot.
- The tone of this expansion is all over the place. I want to take humanity's second Collapse seriously, but it seems like Bungie's narrative team doesn't want me to. Nimbus' quips are everywhere... it seems like they can't finish a sentence without a joke of some kind... 80s action movie writing taken to the extreme, and overall a significant step down from the previous expansion.
- The campaign seems to focus more on our mastery of Strand than on the impending doom of humanity. More on that in the Strand section.
I could say more, but other reviews have said much of this more succinctly anyways.

Neomuna
The good:
- the 'public events' (I forgot their actual name) are a neat twist on the traditional public event formula.
- ARCADE LOST SECTOR

The bad:
- Neomuna was meant to feel like an "active warzone"... yet another miss, and just another patrol zone you'll forget about in a month. Enemy density is exactly like in the throne world, this time complete with a lot of assets seemingly recolored and reused from Bray Exoscience and the Ishtar Collective.
- If you were expecting to be zipping around the city in between skyscrapers like Spider-man, well.... no. The skyscrapers in question are off in the distance, looking cool I guess. Neomuna doesn't feature a lot of verticality, which I imagine could be because the devs didn't want to make Strand feel mandatory for navigation.

Gameplay

Disclaimer: I played the campaign on legend with a friend (titan+warlock). I imagine the split aggro made it quite a bit easier. I will do it solo for my next character.
The good:
- The difficulty of the legendary campaign was solid and comparable to Witch Queen. A rewarding challenge. PS: the exotics you get for completing the legend campaign have a 67 roll, so they're actually usable from the get go!
- Tormentors successfully delivered on the promise of a dangerous, challenging, miniboss-tier enemy. Big thumbs up. I don't look forward to grandmaster.

The bad:
- Certain sections of the campaign force you to use Strand, which invalidates the build you had going (especially bad on legend). It is significantly harder to stay alive using Strand compared to a well-built solar/void setup you probably needed to clear legend solo. In others its use is optional: my friend and I opted NOT to use Strand in the final mission since we were struggling to stay alive and control adds effectively.
- One particular mission (hint: moving deathwall) seems very hard to do solo, especially since you are required to use Strand, which forfeits your survivability in exchange for better movement (which will still get you sniped and killed lol).

Strand
The good:
- Strand FEELS fun. It FEELS fun to zoom around and grapple melee stuff. It FEELS fun to have an army of threadlings kill stuff for you.
- The tangle system are equivalent to warmind cells, and fun to engage with. Try throwing a tangle and grappling to it.
- The three subclasses (yes, even titan, which I main) FEEL good to use and have their own niches and corresponding builds.

The bad:
- You spent the entire campaign using a grapple with basically no cooldown, only to find out it has a 1 min CD at tier 10 discipline once you properly unlock the subclass. FEELS bad. In endgame content, I don't see this being a substitute for a grenade under any circumstances, as pulling off a grapple melee is far too dangerous to be worthwhile.
- We are back to timegated fragments. Very few of them are available right now, and some of the best ones won't be here for a while. At least they did use the courtesy of telling us when they would be available in the pouka pond menu.


If steam gave me the option of leaving a mixed review, I would. I tried my best to avoid being reactionary and subjective, but it's hard to do when reviewing new content for a franchise you've been with for nearly a decade. I hope Bungie can course correct the story in future missions/seasons but for now it's a mixed bag.\t\t\t\t\t
