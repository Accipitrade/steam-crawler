\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is difficult to properly convey my frustrations with Lightfall as a whole. On one hand it does provide some great quality of life improvements, especially with regard to new player on-boarding. Lightfall as a whole feels like a great jumping on point for new players who want to get caught up. Where Lightfall fails is obviously with the story, but it is also a culmination of little things that add up to big problems. I've never played a Destiny expansions that more emphasized the game's underlying issues than this. The high framerate bug is a nightmare if you can run the game above 60FPS as you'll suffer untimely deaths simply because you can run the game well.

Then there are the usual (reoccurring) bugs where mechanics break or speed up to wipe you even when everything seems to be going fine otherwise. The servers have been stable since launch, which is a plus, but it seems like every few weeks the API has to be disabled due to a bug, or something gets disabled because of another weird interaction. It feels like Destiny is tearing apart at the seams and every new release does more harm than good. Normally I've been able to look past this and enjoy the game, but I am just so burned out on how often it happens. It feels like there has to be a better way. Combined with the fact that I find most of Lightfall's new additions (Tormentors, Root of Nightmares) underwhelming, I just can't recommend it as a long-time player. I hope things improve over the course of the year, but I am far less optimistic this time around.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, a little over 2 weeks into the DLC, and here are my opinions (spoilers for the raid and story overall) Please keep in mind, this is my opinion, if you disagree, that's great. It's great that we can have people with different opinions. But at the end of the day, this is a video game. If what I say makes you not able to sleep at night because of how different it is to your opinion, go outside, touch grass, and please find a better hobby, thanks!

The Bad:
-The story. Oh god, the story. We are at the tip of the light and darkness saga, if there was one expansion they needed to get right, it was this one. The stakes could never have been higher. First of all, the veil. The fact that quite literally everyone in the community is asking what the veil is is kinda sad. This crucial story element in which the entire story revolves around is never explained. Imagine if in forsaken, cayde never died, and we were just told to go get uldren sov. We would ask, well, what did uldren do, to which petra would say quick hurry, stop uldren, he's about to do a bad. That alone would've turned the DLC from the best to the worst (not literally the worst, but a bad DLC overall). Now take Lightfall, we know the veil is key to the witnesses plans, and if he links to it, the traveler is done for, but what does it do? Is it part of the traveler? Is it part of the witness? We know that Nezarec was originally carrying it on his ship before savathun killed him, but that doesn't really explain anything as to what it actually is. My other critique of the story is the whole thing is basically a strand tutorial. I get that new green ability fun, so give players new green ability so they have fun, but the whole thing feels like it's spoonfed to us. Beyond light kinda felt natural. You got it just a little bit, in important boss fights and in places to hone your darkness powers, but in lightfall, it's basically in every mission for just a little bit at a time. The whole thing just feels too forced, like this ability alone is the only way to stop calus, but in reality, you could (and should) fight calus with only your light abilities. The whole things feels way too forced, and it just takes away from the whole fun factor of it

-The raid. Without getting too controversial here, here are just some facts. The only other day 1 raid I've completed was DSC, which had around 5,000 teams beat it on day 1. Vow had around 6000ish in the two-day span that it was up. Root of Nightmares had over 40,000 teams clear it. WHAT? This is supposed to be the pinnacle endgame content, some of the hardest content that pve has to offer, and you're telling me that brand new people were able to complete it, and multiple teams were able to flawless it during contest, and some even trio'd the final encounter. All during contest. Worlds first completed it in 2 hours and 23 minutes. That's insane. Talk about the easiest day 1 ever. I for one am never putting on the day 1 emblem, because it isn't special. It wasn't a challenge. I spent so much time preparing, dps testing, specing loadouts, and took a friday and saturday off work just for 7 hours of raiding? Not worth it in my opinion.

Now you may be asking, why did you check you recommend this if you just spent all that time ♥♥♥♥ talking the game. Well, the day 1 raid is over, and for anyone on the fence of buying this, that probably won't apply to them. The story is the main content in this expansion, but the post campaign content will make you come back. Which I think bungie did a great job on

The good:

Terminal Override: I for one was quite sad when they took away mars. No I'm not an ana bray simp, but I just loved the destination for one key aspect. Escalation protocol. Not only was it one of my favorite things to do back in warmind, but even during arrivals it was so much fun to just hop on, and grind away at escalation. Why? Why not, killing an increasingly large amount of ads is fun. The enemy density was great, and even trying to solo it was a fun challenge until the final boss. I've been waiting for bungie to return a public event that was similar to ep, and in my opinion, they did with terminal override. An activity that throws ads at you, and has you defeat certain bosses. Along with that, the loot from it is quite good, mainly because of the whole leave instance and come back to get more. Overall, terminal override is just a fun way to relax and mindlessly kill ads while getting pretty decent neomuna loot and world drops, while having a blast.

Strand. While the campaign was basically a strand tutorial, strand after the campaign is a blast to use. I'm not that much of a pvp player, but from what I've experienced, it's not that bad in pvp. I was originally worried about the hunter super with it's super long duration, but the dr you get is the equivalent to putting a sheet of paper over your body, and calling that armor. But in pve, swinging around from hunter grapple points or tangles and just punching the living crap out of everything is so much fun. Endgame viable? Eh, if you're careful. Easy content viable. Absolutely, and super fun too.

Seasonal activity: Defiant battlegrounds are so much fun. It's the perfect mix of difficulty and ad density. Much better than the battlegrounds that we got last year. By the way, the focusing system, wow, amazing. Screw the whole currency where you need to play the activity, then strikes, then the activity, then more strikes, god I hated it. Just playing the game can get you the engrams, and hoarding them to just unload and get all the gear you want, sign me up.

Nimbus: The people that say nimbus is annoying is either grouped in one of two groups. One, they're just trans-phobic and solely because he uses they/them pronouns they hate him. And to those people, your opinion is just not valid. To the people who say he's just annoying because of his voice and character, ok, I get that. Sometimes he can get annoying, but having an extremely happy, bombastic character that has no care in the world that the universe as a whole is coming to an end is just what we need. He's always happy (besides when he's dealing with Rohan's death), always looking at the positive, and personally, I love that for him. I'm definitely in the minority when I say this (and that's ok, everyone's entitled to their own opinions), but Nimbus is actually a fun character that I like.

Overall: Narratively speaking, lightfall is a flop. They didn't explain the main story piece that we were chasing after, and because of that, we were left confused the entire time, instead of being intrigued. Gameplay speaking, i'd say it's great. You have great post-campaign content that you can dive into, a raid, I guess, and a good seasonal rotation that rewards your time and entertains you. So, should you get lightfall, it depends. If you don't give a damn about the story and want fun weapons to play with and fun activities to do as well, go for it, it's well worth it there. If you solely care for the story, don't even bother, just watch a My Name is Byf video summarizing the whole thing and save yourself some money. I'd say if you're on the fence about buying it, just know that the story isn't everything. Sure, it's the main selling point of the expansion, but the real thing that you're gonna keep coming back to in this game is the content, and in that department, they did pretty well in.\t\t\t\t\t
