\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEh, whatever. My review here really doesn't matter because most of the people reading this have already bought the expansion.

Not buying the latest expansion bars you from a lot in D2: The latest raids and dungeons, Trials, and Seasons. Everyone addicted to Destiny has already bought the expansion because they know that regardless of how good or bad this expansion is, they still need to buy it.

For people on the fence, whether you're new or considering getting back into the game, I dunno. It's a weird time to start playing with the overall Light v Dark story coming to a conclusion in a year. The story with this expansion is more analogous to a summer action blockbuster than something comparable to Witch Queen. I'd overall rate the expansion somewhere a bit better than Beyond Light. I still enjoyed the gameplay, and the new strand ability is fun. The QoL changes to the game are a mixed bag. I like the buildcrafting changes/simplification, but the guardian ranks are disappointing. I understand why they didn't make the Guardian ranks based on past content (would force new players to buy previous expansions to get high ranks), but seeing almost every player with at least rank 6 isn't great either. Being someone who really gets into grinding achievements, I'll still pour in time for the rank anyway even though it resets every season.

It's tough for me to form an opinion on this because I'm so used to seeing the community make a mountain out of a molehill that I instinctively go contrarian to them. In this case, I feel they have a point, even if they're more negative than I would go. People who think this expansion is worse than Shadowkeep are ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ idiots have the memory of a goldfish. Would it really have been better for the story to be "Look here's an interesting pyramid. Oh, you can't enter it yet, so go kill these old bosses to get armor pieces so you can enter the pyramid, fight more old bosses, then get a cryptic cliffhanger ending that doesn't make sense until 2 years later"? While Lightfall's story was detached from what we've been building up towards, Shadowkeep had no story at all between the intro mission and the final mission. God, I ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ hate this community.

The raid is absolutely beautiful and the music is good as always. Unfortunately, since only 2 people need to know the mechanics to actually complete the raid, it's really boring. At least it makes the pinnacle grind easier, I guess.\t\t\t\t\t

TL:DR
This DLC can be summarized by one singular word: Mid. Its not bad but its also not good either.

Neomuna:
Neomuna is an amazing looking area. However, I feel like it had something missing. This felt like their one chance to give us a legitimate city but all we got was the outskirts. Imagine standing on top of a skyscraper and grappling to and from. While guardians sparrow down below with the skyscrapers towering above them. It feels bad that what most of us probably wanted was just out of reach in the skybox.
Story:
We have no idea what the Veil is and we probably never will. However, the gameplay sections were fun (We played on the legendary Campaign.) Especially the section at the end right before the Veil where we fought through hoards of cabal and tanks and tormentors. Calus was done dirty by this dlc though. He was much easier than savathun and mostly reused the A.I from gladiators and Colossus. There was a chance that they could have brought back a few things from the leviathan boss (Like the clapping.) For the cloudstriders, I feel like it would have been better to make them allies throughout the next year then kill of Rohan in The final shape instead of in Lightfall. I knew from the moment I set eyes on him that he would be killed off instantly and of course it was correct. There was no emotional attachment there and my friends that I played the campaign with even joked that Rohan's core was a new momento. Also Nimbus' entire character is marvel writing. He literally tried to fist bump Caiatl right after we killed her father.
Strand:
Strand is fun. It has amazing Ad clear potential. However, the most advertised thing, which is the grapple, is the weakest part of strands kit (Except for hunters.) Which is honestly a shame because the grapple is fun. The best way to probably fix it is to refund the grapple if it is used as a relocation tool but not if it uses the melee or just make the grapple a movement tool, seperate from the grenade slot but in exchange remove the melee. I have no clue how they could make grapple work.
Root of Nightmares:
If I can summarize the raid in one sentence it is this:
"2 people do things while the rest ad clear."
1st encounter is one person running around and shooting glowing balls while the rest ad clear. 2nd encounter is two people running around shooting balls while the rest ad clear. 3rd encounter is the only interesting encounter with multiple people moving planets around and then doing dps. Then it goes back to 4th encounter which is two people shooting glowing balls while the rest ad clear and then shoot a pushover boss until death. Also not to mention that Nezarec can be stopped using basic geometry making him even easier and he could be 1 phased in contest mode. CONTEST MODE. This raid is not fun or challenging. It was honestly really boring since most of the time the same people have the same roles with no raid mechanics to force other people to get involved and even if it did there was always a work around. In my opinion compared to all the other raids in Destiny 2, it is probably the worst raid in the game. I mean people Day oned this raid more than all the other raids combined. If that doesn't say something about this raid then I don't know what does. I have no idea how this can be fixed either without me suggesting that the raid be completely reworked.
Conclusion:
All in All, Destiny 2: Lightfall wasn't good but it also wasn't bad. Which is why I am legitimately calling it Mid. It has glaring issues that need to be address to make this a better expansion and it makes me sad that this is supposed to be part 1 of the end of this light and dark thing that is going on and I'm sure that the hate wouldn't have been this bad if this wasn't advertised like this. However, I will say that the Music and graphic design team are carrying this game atm. That is all though.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall was a very polarizing expansion but I feel like it comes down to two main things.

The Story:
I admit, I was pretty disappointed in the storytelling this season, they kept dropping new things into the story that they didn't explain whatsoever, like the Radial Mast or The Veil. I get that the whole point of the Veil is that we are going to spend the next few seasons learning about it etc but I feel like that's lazy storytelling. Introducing something with this much of an impact so late into the story is an odd choice to me but I am optimistic for the year ahead.

The Gameplay:
While the story was lacking in a number a ways, I feel the gameplay truly shines this year. Everything about strand feels excellent, the mobility is extremely fun, the sound design is excellent, strand is genuinely one of the most fun subclasses I think in the game right now. The new destination Neptune is full of a lot of content similar to their other locations, but I feel like this place is unique if for nothing but the layout. I have yet to play the new raid so I will not comment on that.The campaign was really fun as well, I beat it on legendary with some friends and there were a few encounters that truly were challenging. Each level had a phenomenal mixture of gameplay and cinematography, it felt rewarding and fun to play each mission.

Overall, I really am having fun with this expansion, I wish the reviews weren't as negative as they are because I honestly think this was one of the more fun campaign's to play through.

I turned comments on so can anyone let me know if Beloved is still obtainable, I didn't play during the season where Calus's ship was back and really would love to know now that it's gone.
