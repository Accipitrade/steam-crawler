\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall somehow managed to be worse than RedWar and Shadowkeep, which is impressive in its own right. Despite the Pyramid invasion that put this expansion's events in motion, the gravitas that the new and returning characters bring to the table makes the entirety of the campaign feel like a filler arc. There is little to no clarity given in regards to new plot elements - it presents itself like the narrative team thinks you already know and understand concepts like the Veil, despite them only ever being namedropped on occasion before this point. New faces are forgettable, and Neomuna feels like a throwaway area with no soul. Without dropping spoilers, the finale falls flat, drawing out the same emotional response as vanilla D1, leaving me scratching my head and asking out loud, "that was it?"

As far as gameplay is concerned, gunplay is top-notch, and the campaign missions themselves manage to be engaging enough with high enemy density. The strand segments are enjoyable to a fault, yet the low cooldown abilities are sure to have spoiled me for normal post-game content.

I cannot recommend this in good faith. I'd say that I hope Bungie learns from the plethora of poor reviews, but if they knew how to take criticism, we wouldn't have gotten this pile of garbage in the first place.\t\t\t\t\t

For someone who has played ever since D1, this DLC was a letdown. The story falls flat and leaves you more confused than anything. To me it seems that those who made Witch Queen and the seasonal stories, were not working on Lightfall since a lot of old mistakes were made. Gives the impression that Bungie didnt learn from it's mistakes. If you havent been following the story of Destiny, this DLC aint the one to try get back on track with.

There is also a glaring lack of new things to do in Week 1. I ran out of new stuff to do in the first 2 days, being forced to wait till resets for some quests. I even dipped into the seasonal stuff and ran out of stuff there as well.

Overall a disappointing DLC. Sadly Can't recommend unless your a die hard Destiny fan.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall lightfall is a very mixed bag, and that's unfortunate coming off the excellent expansion that Witch Queen added. However, it's still an extremely decent expansion that added a lot to love, and I think the community reaction to some genuine problems is somewhat overblown.

The Good:
- Strand is a lot of fun! It's too early to say how good it'll be in endgame content, as we don't have all the fragments yet and don't have actual endgame content unlocked, but there's no denying that zipping around with the grapple is a blast. The verbs are all also very fun to play with, suspend in particular being a very powerful and flavorful way of controlling groups of enemies. Warlock threadlings are also incredible and extremely cute.

- The campaign's gameplay is fantastic. I haven't replayed it on my warlock or titan yet, but on my first play through at least I enjoyed the gameplay itself more than I enjoyed WQ's (or any other expansion).

- The interactions between the characters can sometimes be very good. Calus and The Witness in particular I thought had great interactions that characterized both of them well, it's the first time we really see The Witness as anything more than "Evil smoke guy" and I think they did a good job with it.

- People complain about Osiris being annoying, but I actually really like that he was - we were told in lore constantly about how he got exiled in part for being a nag and harassing everyone about not doing things right constantly, but this is the first time we've seen that facet of his personally. I also think it did a good job of portraying his frustration with not being able to do things himself anymore due to losing his light.

- I've enjoyed the season so far but it's too early to say anything more than the battleground was fun and the characters seemed fine enough.

- The new exotics (quicksilver and stasis bow in particular) are very fun to play with and both seem at least decent in high end content. QSS was the preorder bonus and I've already used it successfully in GMs, so it's also endgame viable in addition to being fun, which is always a plus.

- The new mod system is a much needed streamlining of the old one. While some people are complaining about their old builds not working, I've yet to see anything that's actually truly broken and doesn't just need a different approach to get the same result. The biggest exclusion is elemental shards, but stasis builds can compensate using the new orb system to get similar results still. The biggest benefactors of the new system is obviously new players, who don't have to wait months at ada-1 for every mod now.

- General UI changes are very positive, love the new endgame screen, the guardian ranks screen is very pretty, having reminders in inventory for the new champion stunning mechanisms is great, etc.

The Bad:

- The overall story writing for this expansion was a big step back from WQ. This mostly manifests in three areas: first, the dialogue writing, especially for the cloudstriders, feels... rough. In what's a very serious near-apocalypse moment, Nimbus sounds like they're trying to be Cayde-6, but not funny. It feels like a serious tonal mismatch that takes you out of it. Even when Rohan dies, Nimbus comments on it for like 2 sentences then goes back to cracking a joke every other sentence. There's an interesting read there where Nimbus is burying their emotions and sorrow but there's nothing in the text that suggests that's what's happening. Secondly, the veil - the *entire campaign* people talk about the veil like we're supposed to know what it is. Even osiris, who's supposed to have been searching for it ever since awakening, and should have more questions than anyone, is just like "wake up jesse it's time to get the ~veil~." Even at the end we don't learn what it is or why connecting it to the Traveler is a Bad Thing, we're just expected to chase the mcguffin and kill things in our way. Third, strand takes up wayyy too much of the campaign. This isn't Beyond Light - discovering a new power shouldn't be the focus of the campaign - instead, the drama between characters & the struggle for our survival should be taking up most of the narrative time. It's frustrating spending 90% of the time chasing after strand while everything important just kinda happens around us. It felt like we were back in the D2Y1 or Shadowkeep era or storytelling, which is particularly frustrating when WQ did storytelling so right.

- Guardian Ranks are kinda fundamentally broken. Level 6 is very easy to get in a couple of hours, but the jump to level 7 is a decent bit higher (and we don't know what you need for 8-11 yet). This wouldn't be a problem if guardian ranks didn't seemingly reset to 6 every season, making it impossible to tell the difference between a level 6 player (started 2 days ago) and a level 6 player (playing for 4 years). This is mostly an issue because the entire point of guardian ranks was supposed to be this.

- Commendations are a cool idea but so far mostly just are used to give the other 2 people in your strike a commendation as long as they weren't horrific. It'll probably work better in 6 player activities, but in strikes it just feels a bit silly to commendate literally everyone.

- Neomuna feels kinda empty. I'm hoping it's fleshed out over the course of the year, but WQ does not give me hope for that.


Overall, it's a massive shame that the writing is as troubled as it is, because it overshadows a lot of extremely good things in this expansion, both brand new things and improved systems from WQ. I've been having a lot of fun with it and expect to continue having fun with it for a long time more - ultimately, the campaign is only a small part of an expansion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love Destiny 2, but Bungie really phoned it in this time around. The story is not good at all, as all the reviews point out. I think the real issue is the actual game play changes, specifically with the Strand subclass.

In the campaign it has extremely short cooldowns, which is fun but still not the most effective. The problem with Strand is that it relies on your getting into melee distance, and flying through the air. These are fun but ineffective strategies in Destiny. Aerial combat just means that the enemies get to aimbot you to death while you have no counterplay.

Even in the campaign sections designed with Strand in mind, you're at a disadvantage due to being forced out of your existing subclass build. I'm not sure what the thought was there instead of just allowing someone to build a strand subclass from the start and plan their build that way.

This problem gets even worse after the campaign when the empowered buff for Strand doesn't exist outside of a few select locations. The grapple hook ability has over a minute long cooldown, for an ability that's going to get you killed anyway.

The new mod system is streamlined as well, but lacks variety. With a majority of mods being sunset your power level is dramatically reduced. Most builds revolve around generating orbs of light and then picking them up for a temporary buff that increases ability regen or damage reduction.

I don't really care about the story, it sucks whatever. There is still hope that with significant buffs strand could be a competitive choice but if you're going into Nightfalls on any difficulty using Strand, you're just setting yourself up for failure. I hope Bungie will quickly address these issue but I would not recommend the game in it's current state.
