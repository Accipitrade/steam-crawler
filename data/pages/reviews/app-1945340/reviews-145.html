\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI will try to make this Review as thorough as possible, at least in regards to the new Destination, Campaign and characters and gameplay changes/ additions (New Subclass for example). there may be potential spoilers inside the review but they are necessary to clarify why I believe this expansion cannot be recommended.

I will also state I have not completely finished the campaign, but I am currently on the final boss fight playing through on Legendary and cannot find the will to finish it at this point in time, if that is any indicator to how bad I think this expansion is.

Obligatory, "I've played this game and its prequel in their beta states, every expansion and completed almost all of the main content and am considered a veteran player, yadda yadda yadda."

Lets dive into the Good, the Bad, and the Ugly, in that order, for a couple major points every player will encounter if they decide to play this expansion.

1. Neomuna, the New destination.

The Good: While exceptionally small compared to other expansions respective destinations, Neomuna is actually very nice to look at. It has a solid zen/future city aesthetic which is exemplified in its artistic architecture and smooth constructions, soft neon glows, holographic flora and a variety of gardens such as rock, sand, and traditional. It genuinely feels like you are in a futuristic city that isn't just a Cyberpunk clone. It's underlying lore and existence is actually a very creative approach that, if handled better, could have been a major selling point for the expansion. The now electronically uploaded citizens maintain the citys defenses and have very nice (Patrol) dialogue which makes a point to show just how their virtual city operates.

The Bad: Beauty isn't everything, and while the sites are certainly good to look at, there aren't alot. There are no outstanding secrets to be found around the city like in Europa or in Savathuns Throne World, and the only hidden areas are either tied to Campaign missions or Lost sectors with no other purpose. Calus's Forces (Cabal) who have apparently occupied the city are sparse and their patrols are scattered, making for an otherwise empty city. The Vex are also in the city with the same issues, and practically no presence except for a few public events, patrols, and a Campaign Mission or two.

The Ugly: There are no signs of life, other than the enemy's you are fighting. the "Cloudstriders" are no where to be seen except in the Campaign and even then its only two of them. There are no civilian or Cloudstrider corpses even though this is a surprise invasion. There are no Vex fortifications or overnight Cabal fortifications outside a couple large ammo boxes strewn around the city, despite the overwhelming number of landing craft flying around, and the city itself, other than the third of the explorable map that was literally crushed by a giant spaceship, is effectively undamaged aside from a few minor building fires, and craters. I should also point out, that the addition of a Grapple had not swayed Bungie in the decision to include restrictively placed invisible walls, and soft and hard kill zones, effectively removing one of the more enjoyable aspects of having a grapple in the first place.


2. The Campaign

The Good: The campaign includes many nice set pieces and epic moments on-par with what Bungie has created in the past. Especially in some of the places in the second half of the Campaign. Reasonably sized areas make using the new Subclass, Strand, Easier and enjoyable. The Legendary setting is engaging and the changes to enemy scaling can be felt with effect, resulting in a satisfying play-though on most of the missions, and in the patrol zone. Solid Rewards for Legendary. you'll be at the power level Soft Cap by the end of it.

The Bad: The story is a mess. The premise is a novel one, and one I innately know could have been very interesting to dive into, however, the characters and their otherwise uninspired delivery and representation left a rather disinterested taste in my mouth. The two Cloudstriders you meet, named Nimbus and Rohan, are the archetypal "Rookie with a fiery spirit who wants to prove himself" and "The Disciplined veteran bound by duty, but has a soft spot for his comrades.", respectively. You basically learn all you need to on the first Campaign Mission, and that's it. There is no diving into their past or how they became what they are, and there is no genuine dialogue between the two to solidify their relationship or motivations, and what they really think of Guardians and the Light. Its just, "well we have the same enemy so I guess we are friends now." Mara Sov would be amused beyond compare. This is for all the characters too. Osiris is just Angry all the time, and then magically calms down and studies Strand, and Caital (The Cabal Empress) only ever talks about her Dad, Calus, and never about the place shes in. But this is just the Bad.

The Ugly: The overarching story makes no sense, and nothing is clarified to you. You never know what the Veil is or why it's important, even though you have the guys that know about it standing next to you in the patrol zone. You never get to know what the Radial mast is until it gets destroyed and even then you still don't know. No one ever talks about the flying robotic fish we saw in Beyond Light, even though they are everywhere. They really just tell you nothing and it leaves you disinterested and unsatisfied. It feels like there is no real point or drive to what you are doing in-game, other than; "Go here, shoot these guys, and kill this boss." The Cutscenes are also a mess. Very cut and dry with no real character put into them, and sort of thrown in rather than put there on purpose. The dialogue is mediocre at best and cringy at worst, and makes you almost regret finishing the mission on some of the more heinous ones (Looking at you Osiris and Nimbus)


3. Gameplay Changes and Additions:

The Good: Reworked enemy scaling has resulted in a fresher feel to enemy confrontations and it feels more rewarding to fight now. A reworked Build-craft system (Mods Rework) has streamlined the process and made it easier to create more dynamic builds, at the cost of some more excentric ones like "Warmind Cells" and "Wells of Light". An artifact rework has turned what would have been mod space into permanent passive upgrades (Better). Loadout and Mod manager help to manage inventory and gear more effectively. The inclusion of "Guardian Ranks" and commendations that help to more accurately identify just how skilled a guardian is and how helpful they are, while also providing players a way to keep track of progress, and important goals.

The Bad: the addition of Strand has been a rough one. Its ultimately under-powered and its most notable feature, The Grapple, is on a very long timer (outside of Campaign) since its tied to your grenade, making it almost pointless to equip unless you have a dedicated build. Add that to an underwhelming Fragment pool and subclasses that out-perform it in nearly every instance, it fell out quickly (Not sure if its updated at time of posting).

The Ugly: This is more to do with the campaign and Neomuna, but still relates to Gameplay. Strand is tied tightly to the campaign, and the only way to get it is to beat the campaign, at which point you are given a watered down version of it that doesn't work like you've been used to up until that point. It is also somewhat annoying to use in the campaign because you have no proper knowledge of what Fragments and Aspects you have equipped and how to effectively build around it, which is a problem if you are on legendary in some cases because you are REQUIRED to use it constantly. There are whole mission segments where you HAVE to use strand, and this detracts both from the Campaign and Strand, since it forces you to play in an uncomfortable or annoying way.

I would type more but I've run out of words. Final score 4.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall is an embarrassingly bad addition to the Destiny 2 universe, a titanic blunder, and a terrible experience.

You will notice from the hours I have spent on this game that I love it. I do love it. I love it very much. However, I cannot recommend it. Run fast, run far, and spend your money better than I did.

It is with some regret that I say that Lightfall is awful. It is Destiny's equivalent of the 2016 Suicide Squad - a poorly edited, showy mess that somehow feels vaguely embarrassing to interact with. I'm not choosing this comparison accidentally - Lightfall showcases a confused narrative full of tell rather than show storytelling, a stunning lack of direction, and dialogue that will make you physically cringe at least twice per mission.

Lightfall is, of this writing, a failure in every way. The campaign is stupid and nonsensical. Strand, the new subclass (and core new feature of the game) is underpowered, poorly designed, poorly marketed, and poorly sold. Neomuna, the new destination, is empty, barren, and quite literally lifeless. The few action-packed areas of it are a slog, stuffed full of bag-of-hp enemies and events plagued by bugs that incentivize players NOT TO PARTAKE IN THEM.

Lighfall's campaign contains a plot so unbelievably stupid that it left me and my wife, who have played every DLC and season since 2017, utterly confused about the overarching plot of the game (we made the mistake of giving it the benefit of the doubt - sadly, it turns out it is even dumber than we had feared).

It is so profoundly awful it has damaged the good that came before it - rising action built by the excellent Witch Queen DLC and a year of good seasonal storytelling foiled by the narrative equivalent of a belly flop instead of a climax.

Strand, meanwhile, contains some promise. Or, at least, it would, if it were not repeatedly shoved in players' faces like Martin's famous geode. The first time I played Strand I adored it, but then the campaign kept forcing me into it, over and over - without giving me the ability to pause and examine the subclass in the subclass screen!

Perhaps the greatest sin here is that Strand is incredibly powerful inside the campaign and woefully underpowered outside of it - low damage, long cooldowns, and an unbelievably irritating slog of a grind to unlock it (one that does not outdo the walk-across-broken-glass that is the Stasis unlock but is very nearly as irritating). I can tell now that my feelings when finally fully unlocking strand will not be elation, but excitement that I can finally take it off and put it away for good.

Not to be outdone however is Neomuna - Bungie's attempt at a futuristic cyberpunk city whose primary resemblance to other cyberpunk videogames is its failure to deliver on any of the promises it has made. I mentioned before it is empty - a common player complaint. Well, I have to say, I think I prefer empty to the alternative.

When Neomuna is not empty, it is clogged by enemies with basic AI and more health than a hospital. It is plagued by more than one buggy event that actively encourages players not to take part to speed their progression. NPCs appear in the form of amorphous clouds of light with about as much definition as the afore-mentioned narrative, except for the occasional appearance of a cackling villainous voice that for once in this expansion seems to want you to understand what is actually happening (too bad what is happening is thus far little more than cliche after cliche!).

Lightfall is bad. It is embarrassing. It is a layer cake of bad choices with a thick layer of what-the- frosting.

Do not buy this expansion.\t\t\t\t\t
