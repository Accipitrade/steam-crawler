\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPreface - I'm a hopeless Destiny fan that will play when noone else is on in clan or friend list because I like challenging myself to solo content just because, and will get countless hours up even if I'm not happy overall with the game. So here goes -
Well, lesson learnt I guess, I won't be going out all guns blazing and pre-ordering the next expansion if this is the standard being set.
This really just seems like a filler campaign, albeit with a new subclass (that you are forced to use in a the campaign mode to even progress), with absolutely no genuine substance.
Yes, there is a new raid, and exotic quest, but other than attempting for world first completion which always draws a crowd, I really don't think there is much more to keep players coming back that's game changing and wow factor.
The enemies are more of the same recycled content, with only tormentors adding something 'new'.
Bungie has been doing Destiny long enough to know what works and what doesn't, and I gotta say, it's a bit of a head scratch-er as to how they missed the mark completely with this expansion.
The story-line was flimsy at best, with an elderly man yelling and getting angry, and new planetary NPC's which I just found annoying. With no real insight or explanations at all, other than vague references about a veil and final shape. I know this was deliberate on Bungies part, because they LOVE time-gating everything to draw out the story-line as long as possible, but there comes a point where people won't bother and I think this is where hardcore Destiny fans may just spend more time in other games to fill in the time until a legitimate, engaging expansion comes along. Definitely watch the you-tubers if you don't want to waste money for any lore or new information you might want to know about destiny story-line progression.
It's much more of the same with the PvP, no new maps, same potato connections, with a few new trials weapons that are insanely overpowered and will induce the nerf hammer once again (immortal smg). Apparently the sandbox for PvP has/is changing in future (again)?, but sadly it's just not there at the moment, and yes, it's a well worn record - "We are implementing changes to make PvP better" which always inevitably ends in a big F. There is still a good reason people with "good" stats only ever go into the PvP with their sweaty team mates in tow and never solo. No-one likes getting kerb stomped by stacked teams, or potato router connections. I always hold out hope for the PvP, but year after year, expansion after expansion, I always get let down. Le sigh.
New planet Neptune, as well as the new raid (glorified dungeon) are very aesthetically pleasing, music is on point as always, these things Bungie generally always do well with.
New armour system takes a bit to get used to, but overall I guess it's ok? Bungie implemented a savable load-out system, it needs more flexibility to replace DIM as a viable system, but doing a quick save for a load out you have is fairly seamless, with no lag to change them out which is nice.
Also, a great change is we don't receive red-frame weapons which aren't craft-able, but other than that....
So yeah, overall I don't recommend this expansion based on Bungies history and known ability to do really good expansions (Kings Fall, Witch Queen), but this is almost Beyond Light level disappointment.
But if you want something to waste money and a bit of time on, then by all means go for it!!\t\t\t\t\t
