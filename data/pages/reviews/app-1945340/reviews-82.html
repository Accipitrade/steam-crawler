\r\n\t\t\t\t\thuge step-down storywise from the previous expansion, the witch queen. you know you've done something wrong when even the vaatividya equivalent of your franchise (my name is byf) can't even seem to explain its main plot device (what is the veil? what is the radial mast? what happens when the "link" occurs and how? why do everyone in this universe cares about this so important thing now when after 9 years of development, nobody has mentioned it in any way shape or form?). the new characters, rohan and nimbus, were respectively extremely forgetable and another attempt at creating a funny buddy to make up for the loss of the only character everyone cares about in this entire franchise, only to come up as annoying. the best moments were the cutscenes featuring the story's main antagonists, calus (who is not even a new character) and the witness (possibly the coolest thing that bungie has done, but feels extremely underutilized despite its massive teases at the end of the witch queen's campaign and promotional material for this expansion). rumors are that this expansion was not supposed to be "lightfall", and that piece of content will be released next year as "the final shape" and so they just decided to build this expansion with this name instead, and honestly, i wouldn't even be surprised if that was the case: if you cut all of the campaign and leave only the opening and ending cutscenes, you'd realize all your guardian does in the story seems practically irrelevant.

gameplay wise, it's still decent i guess. really enjoyed my time with the level design in most of the missions and the legendary difficulty was a tough but fair fight (except on the last boss). the musical score still remains an absolute highlight and here we have some of the most epic compositions this franchise has experienced yet. the strand subclass is fun to ♥♥♥♥ around with but since its locked behind this 40 dollar expansion, it's best to save that money buying an indie game featuring a grapple hook (buy ultrakill lol). what i didn't enjoy was how neomuna, despite being a vibrant retro-futuristic city, feels quite dead and they had to do lore explanation on how "the citizens actually live in the city's servers or whatever"

**Redeeming hope:**

They're just stretching the storyline we expected to get in lightfall over the course of season of defiance and maybe the rest of the year.

Neomuna is pretty cool, a little bigger than Europa.

Raid is AWESOME so far (*also good hard (so far)*)

ill give it a 7.5/10 since its not as bad as beyond light or shadowkeep but not as good as witch queen.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\r\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\t\r\n\t\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBungie has fallen far with this expansion. Even shadowkeep year was not as bad as this. There was only a single moment of the campaign gameplay that was fun and that was the tormentor fight. The level designs are clunky, unintuitive, and lack any real cover; a classic example of artists making the decisions for map layout rather than gameplay developers. Prettiness matters nothing if the experience makes you want to faceslam a desk. Bungie used to know this but not anymore. Bungie has released a lot of cool stuff the previous year to lightfall that escalated our power. But now they are overcompensating by multiplying enemy density, and having most munitions track to you so that cover means nothing and you have to out-heal in order to survive encounters. In other words they geared the campaign difficulty primarily for the top 5% of players using the most meta builds. So many times during the campaign I felt like I was being punished for not playing the exact way that the devs envisioned me to play.
Lightfall was supposed to be the last DLC years ago, but later Bungie pushed the last DLC to The final shape. Its painfully apparent how much content has been uprooted and dropped in other DLC's it wasn't meant for. To the point where I hesitate to say that they turned Lightfall into a cheap cash grab. The witch queen expansion was when strand was meant to be introduced as that's when the concept of darkness being a mental force with memory and threads was introduced. Instead, strand was deposited into Lightfall so it could fill up half of the focus of the campaign. The campaign when not obsessing about strand kept rushing us towards the unexplained veil which everyone in the story knows about except me. While simultaneously ripping us out of the campaign story in order to increase light level to get to the next mission. And ultimately, the effects of the campaign seem to have made no lasting impression on the destiny universe other than a new destination being dropped in, and the traveller being somehow dead but not dead.
Overall though the biggest shortcoming is the story. Bungie has teased us for years about the mysteries of light and dark and a supposedly fascinating dynamic of how those forces relate to our universe. But now I doubt that that story has any depth. As we reach the eve before the last expansion, there are so many critical plot points and questions that are teased with no satisfying development or answers. I fear it is now too late in the saga that by the time they do reveal the overall bigger story, it'll be so rushed that few will care about it.
I've been a fan of Bungie since Halo CE. I've followed through all the Halos, through the Destiny franchise, and I've backed them. This is the first time I've felt a need to write a review as I've now lost faith in the studio. And its sad, I was excited for this expansion to a story I love. Now I don't even care what this year will bring for Destiny.\t\t\t\t\t
