The main complaint I've seen is that the theme of this expansion diverges too far from the mainline narrative, something that was communicated long before release. In contrast to the standard dark mystery theme we have come to expect from Destiny, this one sports a campy action movie theme, something the equally disliked character Nimbus personifies. I believe this contrast to the main narrative springs from the delay of the Light and Dark sagas conclusion to Final Shape, which was facilitated to fit the Light subclasses 3.0 rework without conflicting with the new Dark subclasses these next expansions will introduce. This resulted in Strand being pushed from Witch Queen into Lightfall, which pushed the final Dark subclass into Final Shape. Where the story was originally supposed to end in Lightfall, it was instead pushed over to Final Shape, and Lightfall got a new narrative based around mastering Strand, which would place it as a filler arc where Bungie embraced a different direction because none of the Lighfall narrative really affect the rest of the story.
With this in mind, I do not feel Lightfall's story was disappointing or a letdown. We expected answers to mysteries that were revealed to us in Lightfall, but those answers aren't here. On it's own though, the Lightfall campaign presents a satisfying narrative our conflict with Calus, and our discovery of some things entirely new, namely Neomuna but also Strand. It plays into its campy vibe very well without being obnoxious, but it does seem silly when compared to the existential crisis ongoing in the mainline narrative.\t\t\t\t\t

Story
This looks like Bungie changed its player base target audience. The story of this DLC is poorly written with lots of plot holes and suffers from "Marvel" syndrome. Can somebody at Bungie explain what the Veil is? Why didn't we lose our Light when the Traveler was caught in the black fleet's force field, as happened during the Red War when Ghaul conquered our city and the Traveler? Why were Zavala and Ikora talking at the end of the campaign about the Traveler being dead or lost when we still have our Light? Did Zavala or Ikora know more than we experienced in the campaign? Overall, it feels like all the actors in the campaign know more than the player. Osiris talks about the Veil like he knows more about it. From the player's perspective, the Veil looks like a huge, shiny, neon flower. Overall, the story leaves you with tons of unanswered questions.

Characters
Is Nimbus supposed to be a replacement for Cayde-6? If so, it's just not good. I don't enjoy Nimbus' cringy jokes. And why did you have to kill off Rohan? He was such a badass character!

Gameplay
During the campaign, we were forced to use Strand abilities that were underpowered for a legendary campaign. I expected more action on Neomuna, such as the Vex and Shadow Legion fighting among each other in large groups.

When I saw Empress Caiatl personally come to help us fight the Shadow Legion during the campaign, I was amazed. Why did Bungie miss this potential in patrol zones? Also, why does Nimbus only talk during public events and not come to help us?

Locations
Neomuna is nice but I would like it less ping and more fluorescent orange.

Art
Music in this DLC is amazing.

If there is rating I would give story 4/10 at best.
Music 10/10.
