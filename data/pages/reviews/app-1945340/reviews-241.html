Overall: 5.7/10

Nearly finished this review, then my PC crashed, so I'll just do a summary xD

The story suffers from a severe case of irrelevancy. Without divulging any spoilers, the whole campaign feels like filler, like an excuse for Bungie to squeeze in an extra expansion before finishing the Light and Dark saga. If the first and last cutscenes were put one right after the other, we would hardly be confused and the story would move on as if nothing were missing. Dialogue, especially from one new character, isn't just awful, it's downright painful.

The gameplay is fantastic--especially the new subclass. My only complaint there is that we don't unlock the full thing until after the campaign is completed. The environment has issues with reused assets, but is still enjoyable.

My advice is to wait until this DLC goes on sale for at least 20% off before you buy it. The new weapons and Strand aren't OP, so you won't be crushed in PvP.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR if you really want it maybe wait for a sale, nothing new or interesting here

"bringing challenge back to destiny" is such a joke when the idea of challenge is just bullet sponge enemies. They only have one new unique enemy for the whole expansion which is beyond lazy, been going through legendary campaign with a friend the pacing feels very off, the missions are really bland compared to the Witchqueen campaign, overall the legendary campaign feels easier, yet more annoying then Witchqueens. So much for bringing back the challenge!

The open world area feels very unsatisfying to explore, you'll just be doing the same things you've been doing for the past expansions; like boring patrol missions, etc. where you run around for a bit and shoot enemies, or stand in one place and shoot enemies. I think they could really take a page out of other games, add big world event bosses, sparrow time trials, or even better strand time trials where you have to grapple using strand to get around the environment. Strand is another joke btw, for an expansion centered around it they sure didn't design anything around it. The open world scatters random grapple points around and there is never a moment in the game where you need to truly use strand outside of some mission gimmicks. You also have to play the entire campaign without it, imagine if WoW or Final Fantasy went oh yeah check out our new class this expansion aren't you guys excited to play it? Oh you actually have to complete the game before you can play it though, and then you have to spend hours leveling it up too, so hope you weren't too excited! Feels a bit backwards if you ask me.\t\t\t\t\t
