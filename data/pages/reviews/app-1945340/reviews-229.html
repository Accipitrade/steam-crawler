\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you were expecting Lightfall to be a turn around for the destiny 2 series, it's not. It might be the closest thing we've had to a complete nosedive in terms of content quality. Gameplay wise, strand is cool, yet completely broken or weak at the same time, but nearly every other part of the game is becoming unbearable. From the power creeping legendary weapons they make you re grind for the best version of, to useless exotics, to the painful focus on "buildcrafting". Lightfall falls short at bringing any meaningful change to Destiny 2's struggling content loop. Crucible once again was left with zero new content, networking updates, or rewards. Meanwhile the strike playlist only received one new strike, and a battleground which is painstakingly re issued content. Gambit... Yeah.

The story was supposed to be a great transition or addition to our building story which started in season of Arrivals. However, Lightfall's campaign gives absolutely zero meaningful impact to our over arching story progression and has zero impact on any of our actual gameplay or game world. It fails to explain any newly introduced elements or characters before immediately writing them off or shelving them for later. It feels as if nothing happened at all in the story. This living in the monster of narrative impact witch queen had, it seems Bungie's best writers were on the sidelines here. Don't even get me started on the corny Nimbus lines.

I do NOT recommend a purchase of Destiny 2 Lightfall OR the annual pass. This seems like a great annual pass up on a consumer end. Here's to hoping the raid is good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMostly Spoiler-Free review. Only hints at story development, nothing explicitly stated.

This was it. The penultimate expansion for the Light and Dark saga, a narrative that has been teased for almost a decade and heavily expanded on since Shadowkeep. Lightfall fails on almost every level when it comes to its narrative. It is unsatisfying and the stakes are always unclear. Indicative of the JJ Abrams style of the "Mystery Box" storytelling, Lightfall spins its wheels for too long expanding on Strand without tying the new subclass to the overall story or the meta-narrative of The Winnower and The Gardener, The Witness and The Traveler.

The first red flags were there. The need to have weird narrative hooks like how The Witch Queen expansion had this forced focus on you being a private eye, with the Title of the Expansion you can earn being "Gumshoe." While The Witch Queen expansion was the pinnacle of storytelling in Destiny, the theme was discordant with the narrative. That is fine though, that is flavoring. In Lightfall? No, the forced focus is all that Lightfall has. Since the unveiling(pun not intended) of the new logo for Lightfall, the signs that this expansion was not going to respect the weight of its narrative were present.

This is MCU phase 4 levels of writing, of a narrative that is embarrassed to be in the universe it finds itself in so it lampshades its stakes and undercuts any themes it attempts to have. Themes of Loss and Connection are mentioned in lazy, expository dialog, but these themes are not bore out in the actual story.

Rohan is 3 weeks from retirement and practically says as much and instead of subverting that trope, they pull it off earnestly which feels like a parody. The 1980s Rocky-esque training montage before the final mission feels like the game is mocking its own premise. It all feels cynical and disinterested in telling a compelling story and only cares about the bright neon lights. The focus on the, "We want you to feel like a 1980s action star," motif never actually gets off the ground but the motif hamstrings the narrative to undercut any stakes it may have.

I honestly need the Bungie Narrative Team to write up a blog post or contribute to a TWAB to explain what happened here. This expansion's narrative is so bad that it can lead to the death of the franchise. All the air was sucked out of my excitement for The Final Shape after the final cutscene ended and I had to talk to people in the tower to find out what happened. The Red War, the first Destiny 2 campaign, was more consequential than Lightfall. That is a narrative failure.

I get it, the Narrative team is trapped between wanting to tell a story while being constrained by the Destiny business model, but I feel there is a lot of compromises and workarounds that could feel more satisfying and keep the anticipation for The Final Shape high.

I needed Lightfall to be the expansion the upended everything, that changed what it meant to be a guardian and what it meant to be a lightbearer. Instead the expansion wastes too much time with Strand and despite Strand being literally about the strings and river of life that binds us all together, the story of Strand and the Story of Calus and the story of the Veil, and the story of The Traveler and The Witness never feel truly connected.

Sadly, even the mission to mission story never feels connected. It feels that they tore the expansion apart and put missions in different moments of the campaign and then rushed these weird Interludes to try to bridge the narrative gap.

Look, it's Destiny, the gunplay is fun. Strand is nice in the campaign(even if grinding for something like 6K Strand Meditation points on each character to unlock all the fragments and grenades is going to suck). There are good moments in the campaign, but overall this is a massive disappointment and makes me happy that the Season is only adding 4 weeks of story content and the Pinnacle Level is not going to raise over the year. It means I can finally walk away and do something else when I am done with each seasons' story. I have a 9 year backlog of games I need to get to.

This is The Boring Company Las Vegas tunnel. Something hyped up to extreme degrees but ends up being a straight short drive to a disappointing conclusion. But hey, at least it looks pretty.\t\t\t\t\t
