\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe average-player-facing narrative (i.e. the campaign missions and cutscenes) seem somewhat undercooked, like management tried to push in too many moving parts simultaneously and the writers just couldn't keep up. However, literally everything else is perfect. Dozens of QoL improvements, great visuals, stunning music, deeper lore, etc. are all here.

Discovering and using Strand is one of my favorite modern gameplay experiences. While it doesn't quite reach the heavenly echelons of Ultrakill, Rhythm Heaven Fever, and Beat Saber, it remains an absolute blast. Specifically, the grapple-melee mechanic is ridiculously satisfying. Bungie never misses in that respect.

Nimbus seems like some of the best mainstream nonbinary representation so far. The way they're integrated into the world gives off the impression that Neomunan society has truly normalized non-traditional gender stuff. It is refreshing, to say the least.

That being said, it's still $50 USD for the campaign and the season. If you only play destiny for the campaigns, this is probably not the wisest purchase you can make value-wise. If you play any decent amount beyond that, or have any interest in the lore beyond just the main campaigns, you'll probably be happy with it.\t\t\t\t\t

We get demoted to a side story on a planet that we aren't made to care about, with characters we aren't made to care about and plot lines that are important because Osiris said so. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs an avid player of the destiny series, since I loaded that 360 up to the D1 beta 9ish years ago, I have generally liked the major changes and felt like there were good intentions. However this is way different. The campaign doesn't answer many questions and does leave us with more than we gain with them however I understand it's only the first week. The weapon re-skins are fine because they have updated perk pools and a new twist. HOWEVER, the increase in difficulty has brought about the problem of legend lost sectors. These are very challenging and only got harder with the update, yet there are still exotics gated behind this activity. The grind got longer and exotic drops are just as scarce. This feels very unnecessary and it seems like with major exotics being gated behind it, it should at least be a tad easier. Not 3 minute runs easy but less damage output from the enemy AI. GUARDIAN RANKS are also terrible as this adds another meaningless level of grind to the game. The game already has grimoire, seasonal leveling, now commendations, seal gilding, and to top that off power level itself. The idea of having to do a raid to complete a crucial step if baffling as all players don't have the ability to hop in for multiple hours or do cheeses (locked behind items) that make it take less time. The overall guardian rank system makes no sense and I have not met a player yet that cares about it. There is no incentive to do it and even if there was could it possibly match that of putting time elsewhere? To summarize this review, legend lost sectors are much harder for the same loot drops and take much more time when it could take 3+ hours to get a single exotic. Lastly, guardian ranks feel forced and redundant to overall game play especially when there is no incentive, it resets per season, and there are many other ways to spend time on the game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: The gameplay is fun, the story is filler.

**SPOILERS INCLUDED**

The narrative to this story felt more like a tornado of ideas. There wasn't any narrative construction in which the main story made any sense. While the game play that has always been enjoyable and it is, I felt very disappointed with this new expansion. The majority of the story was either being forced to use the new subclass "Strand" (which no ones builds we're built for and upon release you couldn't unlock everything however you can now.) or chasing after this mysterious artifact called the "Veil". Which the Veil was never explained throughout the entire story and it's briefly mentioned in lore. . In between that, it was filled with cringe characters, a poor plot, and dialogue that was confusing and not engaging. The first cut-scene and last cut-scene could have been 1 cut-scene, it almost like this was pieced together quickly. There is a new character that dies and you are only forced to have 2 conversations with him, this doesn't build any relationship to him and thus I felt nothing for his death. Neither did his apprentice in the game, there was only 1 or 2 dialogues mentioning it, then it was back to cringe dialogue. I think for the most part Strand is good, however I feel this entire expansion and story is filler. Strand was originally slated to release with Witch Queen and that's why the Witch Queen "Veritas" armor has Strand logos all over it. It's hard to believe the same devs that are now back in studio, not at home, and now have massive funding from Sony made both Witch Queen and Lightfall. As for now, I am disappointed, I hope they can pull it together. The story felt very short, then there wasn't much to do afterwards. Having had Forsaken and Witch Queen in the past, the expectations are higher, especially if you want me to pay for it. Please don't give me a side quest.\t\t\t\t\t
