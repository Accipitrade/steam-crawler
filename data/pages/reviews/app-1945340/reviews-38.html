I would've liked a bit more time before jumping right into the pinnacle grind but hey, Thats MMOs for ya. If you're a returning Guardian on the fence or a New Light interested in the game, I'd Definitely give this one a shot *while its on sale*\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStory is horrible with a mcguffin that's never explained whatsoever, Osiris is horribly voiced and whines like a toddler a tenth his age, Nimbus is one of the most disgusting, weird, annoying characters ever made with the absolute worst visual design I've ever seen all in the hopes of pushing your typical trans psycho-babble. Rohan is dead before we can even know about the character enough to care, but he's far better of a character than Nimbus which makes his weirdly done death tragic for all the wrong reasons. The city of Neomuna, though very pretty and cool looking, is often flat and un-detailed, giving it this almost "white-box" or unfinished feel and look. This is compounded by it being entirely devoid of life except for four total npc's you talk to here and there and the enemies. They use this stupid "all the citizens turned into light when they went into their vr pods when we got attacked!" excuse and it feels so synthetic and fake. Its a horribly manufactured "game convenience" only there so they didn't have to like, ya know, try. No life, dead environment. Its soulless in Neomuna. Speaking of enemies, they're yet again the exact same enemies you've been fighting since the release of D1 but reskinned. Except for Tormentors, they're new and very cool but you only get to see a couple in the whole campaign. Because of course. The Witness does things we don't understand, in ways we don't understand, using an object we don't understand, to go to a place we don't understand. And that's the story. Exceptional writing. Nezarec is awesome, but he's stupid looking compared to what he could/should have been and was hyped up to be. Rhulk was a trillion times better looking. Strand is a fun class, good edition to the game, but is handled very badly. Throughout the whole campaign leading up to its completion where you permanently unlock it you use a suped up version of it that's better and more fun. Then your reward for completion is a worse version of it. WHAT??? Let me grapple around man, Jesus. All the designs for the classes are fine or good, but the concept art they show is literally just better. Somehow they chose their worst ideas to be the ones that got made into the game. Brilliant. The constant exposition on the nature of Strand, basically being the element of string theory, is stupid and so bad its cringe-inducing. They use constant whining and non-sensical jargon in an attempt to sound like philosophers or wise introspective thinkers but just end up spewing ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ with random buzzwords thrown in over and over again. YouTubers I watched covering the new lore explained it far better and more interestingly (and enjoyably) than the people who wrote said lore. ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ is it bad. Calus, oh Calus, my poor child. His final boss fight was just him going from one version of a REGULAR CABAL SOLDIER to another type, using a cannon and dual axes. That's it, he's just a cabal soldier but the size of a house. Dear God did they blow that fight, one that they had been hyping up and building towards with Calus since the literal beginning of D2. Almost impressive how bad that was done. But hey, the exotics in this expansion have been fun and the soundtrack is perfection. In short, DON'T buy this expansion until it is on a MASSIVE, and boy do I mean massive, sale. Then grab it for twenty bucks or less. Twenty at the absolute max, if you're REALLY itching for something to do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, Destiny is my favorite game of all time.
With that out of the way, I have to say that this DLC is definitely not worth its price. The story part is short and while the difficulty is sort of increased it can be completed very fast without much effort. The difficulty is another thing: artificially pumping up the health and damage of certain enemies in certain areas doesn't make the game more challenging or enjoyable. It makes the game tedious and frustrating.
Neomuna is a cool looking place, but that's about it. Not really a proper city, but rather a random area with some nice lights and colors.
The only fun thing this DLC offers is the Strand subclass, which has become my favorite subclass to use. It offers unique mechanics compared to earlier subclasses and is just fun to use.
All that said, I can't recommend Lightfall to anyone unless it is on heavy discount, I'd say under 15€ would be a proper sum for it.\t\t\t\t\t
