\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy friends and I stopped playing Destiny 2 at the end of Year 1. I had heard good things recently so I decided to buy back in on this large release. That is my bias for this review.

The first thing I noticed is just how difficult it is for a returning player to... begin again in this game. I had heard that all of our old items were removed(?) but that wasn't the case, I still had all of my old weapons. But the world seems so much smaller than I remember. When I logged in there was only one story location available to me, and when I arrived there were dev notes on the ground everywhere!! This lack of front-facing polish was very surprising to me. After some googling I learned that I needed to click "Lightfall" in the upper-left of the navigator to select where I *should* have been going. By then I had tried all the things that had blinking lights on them, but they were all just old, now-defunct quests and locations.

The old areas have been removed from the game. After a few hours of playing I can make an educated guess that this is due to the DRASTIC changes in level design from what I remember. The player's model is much, much larger than before, and enemies are scaled very far down. To the point that the largest rooms in the new areas feel like the smallest rooms from my memory. Enemy AI is different, now. Difficult to put my finger on what all has changed but I can say that the game feels more action-oriented, and yet the player feels much much weaker than I remember. The developers aren't afraid of putting you in a room with, say, 20 or 30 enemies. In year 1 that would have been unheard of. Level design is noticeably worse now, too. I'm breaking vents and navigating through dark ductways to make my way to... a circular room with one to three pillars for cover in them. This is *not* what I remember destiny being about. Much, much more platforming adventure stuff, too... in a first-person game? I just don't understand what they are going for here.

The new campaign is abysmal. Characters act and talk like pre-teens, yet they are humanity's last hope before our destruction. The only ally you bring with you can't remember what accent he's trying to use. No one knows what the big bad is up to, even after seeing it shown in the cutscenes between missions. Two missions of real plot spread over 16 - 24 hours of "shoot the vent to move to the next room." ugh.

$50 is just the beginning. To join other players in... anything outside of Neptune, I need to pay more (not going to happen). To play any of the things I remember doing I need to cough up another $60 (strikes, raids, new PvPvE thing). Every corner of this game is monetized now. I can't believe I couldn't just boot this up and play a strike like I remember.

The new weapon animations are straight from Fortnite. Your character can dance, your reloads are gimmicky and childish, I found myself intentionally under-leveling because I didn't like the animations that the weapons played. Also, how is it that we are 8 years into this franchise and the weapons still feel like they are from the 1900's?

This is a waste of $50 unless you are EYEBALLS DEEP in Destiny 2 already, and even at that, it's a cash grab to monetize what playerbase is already there. Do. Not. Recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros: Strand is cool, Neomuna is pretty

Cons: This is the most MID dlc I have played in a long ass time, and for destiny this is like top 3 most middling (and borderline trash) paid content I've ever seen. I was literally able to guess what was going to happen thru 4 of the 8 missions before even getting to the halfway point or sometimes even without hearing a word of dialogue. The grind is felt more than ever, with bungie reintroducing the massively outdated "stop for a minute and level" power grind right into the story. Neomuna is cool, but after almost 9 years of destiny, I don't want to have to do the same "look around, level up, come back when you git gud or get leveled" song and dance. TWQ gave better content and the amount of missions and drops meant you could more or less go through the whole story (on legendary no less) without having to stop and spend time leveling to continue and it's baffling to me they threw that lesson out despite massively positive player feedback.

Side note: the new mod system in conjunction with the combat changes really feel like artificial difficulty. Without much diversity of build crafting at the moment, the difficulty of enemies is so vast that it's difficult to even care about this system at the moment. It's a real shame we were forced to sacrifice the wealth of mods and builds for this lackluster and unfinished feeling system.

Buy if you're really really interested in strand, otherwise wait a few seasons to see if content there improves and makes a purchase like this worth.\t\t\t\t\t
