\r\n\t\t\t\t\t I just had to delete all my positive Destiny 2 reviews. I can easily say Destiny 2 has been the game I’ve played “the most” in my life. It’s unchallenged in it’s type of hybrid Looter-Shooter/sudo MMO form. It made for such an amazing experience but something has happened, and it’s awful.
All of my positive reviews we’re made when I had “1,118 Hours” in the game, and now I sit at roughly 3,500. It has amazing content and don’t get me wrong. If a friend catches me at the right time I might hop on and do a raid, or a dungeon with a friend but I’m not longer really in love with this game.
I’m simply wanting to put this review out there to tell anyone who sees this. Don’t buy any Bungie’s Destiny 2 content or Marathon in the future, unless there is some major changes. I don’t even want to use the energy I have for elaborating on what’s all gone wrong but I’ll try to just make a simple list…

-Server stability is awful, so awful they have to use every animal’s name in the animal kingdom to designate the errors!
-Hackers everywhere in PvP, no true acknowledgment of it
-Event Cards for copy and paste events costing $10
-Gambit is a meme
-Awful Story, and Awful Campaign game play wise
-Blaming the awful writing on them wanting to “extend” the story into seasons
-Blatant aggressive MACROtransactions pushing, they are not Micro!
-Free currency alternatives to buying armor removed
-PvP itself is just ignored, and I’m not a PvP player and it irks me
-”New” Iron Banner mode just ads three PVE Turrents to a Zone Control mode?
-Increased cost of expansions, Battle Passes just to have heavier monetized cosmetics, and seasonal events?


I know they’ve had awful issues for sure with interaction on social media “toxic” gamers and their community, even getting death threats. This is a freaking video game, there is no point in that. They do however deserve to be called out for running something that was great, and people just need to walk away.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you finished the witch queen campaign and went straight into lightfall, it would not be unreasonable to think that you had accidentally booted up an unfinished beta version of a seasonal story. My beloved 'Defend the Ghost' missions return in great numbers, and along with them they bring the least cohesive story since base destiny in 2014 (at least shadowkeep was straight to the point). Last year there was more detail put into a lore tab describing Saint-14 and Savathun banging whilst Osiris watched from the background like an elderly Sneako, than there was detail put into explaining the main premise of the campaign, the Veil. Given this was the season of cutscenes not playing correctly, I assumed I had missed some vital story beat explaining what the heck was going on in this story, but no - Bungie would rather spend their time clamping down on over delivery than ensuring that the finished product of their £40 DLC has a plot.
Had this been advertised as a quirky 80s action movie then by all means tell your silly strand story, but this in no way resembled the penultimate expansion in a series which has dragged itself through ten tumultuous years of some of the worst AND best storytelling in the games industry.
The gameplay is fun at least. can't fault them on that, but this is mainly a base game destiny feature, and not something you are paying for when you buy Lightfall. Mission design is up to the Witch Queen quality in terms of level design and challenge, which provides a really satisfying experience, unfortunately overshadowed by the crushing weight of having to carry a story which rushes main, side and background details, while also allowing absolutely no believable character development (cough cough Nimbus and Osiris).
Art, Music and Voice Acting are all up to Destiny's exceptional standard, which makes Neomuna at least bearable, if not my favourite destination, although it could have been much more pleasantly received had Bungie put civilians in this "living city", rather than settling with random yellow lights which are vaguely humanoid-shaped (An engine constraint possibly?).
It really is a shame that this DLC was shoe-horned in between WQ and TFS, i can only imagine how good this game could be if the bungie higher-ups allowed their brilliant staff to perform to the levels we all know they can (and have before).\t\t\t\t\t
