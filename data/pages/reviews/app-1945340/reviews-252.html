\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo spoiler review
After spending time running through the main story of the campaign and working on the extra end game content included, I have this to say.
The story quality is in the same realm as the witch queen, but with less exposition, and more focus on playing through the story than grinding out destination activities (bounties, public events, and all the grind that was extra heavy in beyond light)
There are far less cut scenes but that does not impact the weight each one has to the story.
Strand is a fun fresh way to interact with the world and is unique in the way that it is entirely based on interrupting the flow of combat through movement rather than restriction of it the way stasis does, suffice to say the two classes are opposites in the same field of concept.
If you are looking for game changing aspects of combat, grind, ect. You will only find a bit here, build crafting is easier, and there have definitely been sandbox improvements, but it is at its core still Destiny 2.
My rating for different things in the DLC are as follow, and this is subjective.
Story: 9/10 for the weight it has alone.
Gameplay: 7/10
Destination/environment design: 7/10
Sound Design: As always bungie hit a 10/10 for this one.

This review is pre-raid release and there is still a whole year of seasonal content ahead of us, so choose for yourself how you feel about it, things could change between now and when you read it, just remember that if you like Destiny, this will be something you most likely enjoy.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis might be the most disappointing release from Bungie ever, given the hype they built up for this and the level of storytelling we've had from the game over the last year this is a massive step back.

The Campaign is a glorified Strand tutorial that had me almost asleep in my chair, Neomuna is empty and small and the citizens being "in a virtual space" is just an excuse to not have to design a unique city population. They shouldn't have created this space if they weren't willing to put in the effort. Nimbus isn't unbearable but they are still just Bungie's version of an MCU comic relief character which ruins the whole vibe and i wish they would really lean more into the dark and serious tones that make Destiny feel like a strong narrative experience.

Strand is fun but it is underpowered and very poorly configured right out of the gate, grapple is entirely a movement utility like icarus dash etc. so why is the cooldown the same as a grenade cooldown when they function completely differently? And the "new" mod system is less intuitive and serves only to limit builds, a massive downgrade from the pre-lightfall system.

The guardian rank system is pointless and was entirely a waste of development time, commendations mean nothing as the game incentivises you to hand out commendations to anyone and everyone regardless of your experience with them to obtain powerful rewards from Hawthorne or increase your guardian rank (which is in no way indicative of a players actual experience or skill)

Only positives i will give this DLC are the servers were significantly more stable on launch than most DLC's before it and the Tormentors are a cool enemy type that with some small adjustments could really add a good layer of difficulty to the game.

3/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou know you f***ed up when My Name Is Byf makes a 26 minute video about how disappointed he is in you.

If you look at all elements of the story and all the raw material writers had to work with in a bulleted list, honestly I think it could have been a fantastic story. I love the overarching plot and I love the character concepts. It could have been so good. But then the narrative design team handed it off to the script writers and we got this. There are inconsistencies and plot holes everywhere. You can tell the dialogue is supposed to have stark, exciting contrast and flavour between the four voices you hear in your coms (Ghost, Osiris and two new characters introduced in the campaign). Instead it's annoying and cringey half the time. Characters communicate in unrealistic speech patterns and say things that don't feel true to their character concepts.

There's been so much missed opportunity for really deep insights and character development. The writers created the biggest, juiciest narrative pie and then they used a potato masher to cut and serve it. I blame 90% of the flaws in this expansion on the script writers. If I'd known you were going to butcher content with such awesome potential so badly I'd have offered my own dang services to write your scripts.

The actual gameplay and the new mechanics and QoL improvements to character customization UI have been so exciting and have, in my opinion elevated gameplay beyond what it has been so far (I do miss a few of the armor mods we lost in consolidation, but overall, I think it's a great improvement). Strand is real fun. It'll need tweaks for sure, but it's fundamentally satisfying and scratched an itch I didn't know I had. There are new combat modifiers that bring serious challenge to the legendary campaign and a couple of new enemies that put a certain sense of "Oh sh**" back into gameplay that we haven't had since the scorn were first introduced (in my opinion).

Visually, I LOVE the solar punk setting in Neomuna. Fondness of a setting is totally subjective so I get it's not for everyone. I personally love it and want one of those zen pond things in my bedroom.

Overall, Bungie, you had so much going for this expansion. It could have been so great. But you gave the story to some truly incompetent script writers and unfortunately, no matter how good the raw material is, there's not a lot you can do when some of the dialogue and narrative progression is genuinely painful to sit through.

10/10 Gameplay. 9/10 Strand 8/10 UI overhaul. 3/10 writing. I can't recommend it as long as I actually have to force myself through cut scenes and dialogue to get to the fun stuff.\t\t\t\t\t

I was excited after witch queen, but what goes up must come down, and bungie ended up digging themselves back into the pit WQ raised them out of.\t\t\t\t\t
