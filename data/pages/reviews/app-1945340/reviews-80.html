It's hard to give this even a decent score. This DLC has to be one of the worst in recent history, especially so after Witch Queen. The story is just all over the place, adding new plot points such as the veil and Neptune that have no connection to anything of destiny's history. Tormentors are interesting, but I wish they were explained better in the game. Also, strand being a post-campaign reward is one of the worst choices they could've done, but probably was only done because they wanted to make people play the campaign fully. All in all, only buy if you want strand and are willing to set through an annoyingly stupid campaign for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR - If you're up-to-date on DLCs and don't care about the campaign, it's a good expansion. If you're invested in the story/campaign or have missed one of the prior DLCs, it's a terrible purchase.

Pros:
- Stasis is a lot of fun
- New exotics are cool
- Seasonal activity is pretty good
- Raid is fantastic

Cons:
- Story is super disappointing. It feels like the campaign story is just there to justify why you have Stasis and is otherwise unfulfilling
- Legend campaign is borderline unplayable solo. The encounters were clearly balanced around having multiple players, and playing alone is a lot of kiting an enemy that has a ton of health until you finally chip it down.
- Multiple progression activities and Pinnacle drops are unavailable if you're missing the previous expansions. You're basically stuck paying the extra 30-60$ to get the missing expansions if you don't want to be way behind everyone else in terms of progression. At best, this is bad design. At worst, it's incredibly predatory.

Overall, as someone who plays almost exclusively with friends and doesn't care much about the story, I'm having fun with it. If you don't fit those criteria though, I wouldn't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEven for Destiny standards, this is some irredeemably bad storytelling. It's one great, big procession of unexplained contrivances, with the Guardians having difficulty with Strand but suddenly being masters just because Osiris told them they gotta believe or some nonsense like that - Bungie, this is Destiny, not PaRappa the Rapper.
Main story ends with a character who had all of 5 minutes of screentime, give or take a few seconds, sacrificing themselves, and the game plays it like some big, dramatic, emotional moment, as if we actually got to know this character at all.
My absolute favorite moment in the story [SARCASM INCOMING] was when the Witness took over the Ghost like it did in Shadowkeep, but did it only this one time, and it only amounted to spouting off exposition and reminding the audience about just how big and omnipresent the Witness is as a threat, since it has done a whopping NOTHING since it arrived in the Solar System in the opening cutscene. That was truly the moment of all time, and definitely wasn't a decision made out of complete creative bankruptcy or anything.

Gameplay-wise, Neomuna does not have anywhere near the verticality implied in the trailers, even the EDZ has more elevation change, and it is visibly smaller than almost every other patrol area in the game at current. It also feels and looks like an amalgamation of all the hackneyed tropes of vaporwave- and 80s synthpop-styled videos.

Strand had potential, but Titan mains already knew it was gonna be a load of (I can't say this word, but you know what it is) because the abilities Titans had almost all invariably revolved around using the fist, just like the Behemoth subclass did for Stasis Titans in Beyond Light. It's just Lime-flavored Striker except you can kinda fly with it, there's nothing special to it. The literal most creative thing you got with the Berserker subclass is that you can release a blast from the barricade that tangles enemies in Strand... which is just the exotic perk of Khepri's Horn with extra steps, and we've had Khepri's Horn since BEFORE DESTINY 2 WAS EVEN ON PC.

Everything, and I mean EVERYTHING, about Lightfall is underbaked and under-delivered, and yet no matter how much we complain, nothing's gonna change - this has been Bungie's M.O. since Destiny came out, and it's always made them money, because why put in any more effort than the literal bare minimum, when you can maximize profits by being deliberately misleading if not outright deceitful in the marketing, since we're still gonna get suckered in anyway?

The only thing that Bungie does right is the trailers with all their visual flair and making everything look awesome, all just boasting about how great the rehashed content's gonna be, just so they can underdeliver and then come up with all these excuses and apologies about how they're going to "do better in the future" only to make it an active effort to do even worse as they go on.

No more, Bungie. I think I've had my fill of the Destiny universe from this moment forward. Anyone who cares about their wallet had better do the same - but please, newcomers, take my heed and just uninstall this game. I'm gonna play through the seasonal content just to give that a fair shake, but I've already made my mind up that this is the last season and last expansion I'm getting from Bungie. I feel sorry for anyone who bought the annual pass - Witch Queen was such a good step in the right direction, but they screwed us all HARD with the follow-up.

And before ANYONE tries to tell me, "OH BUT THIS IS JUST THE FIRST PART OF THE END OF THE STORY, YOU CAN'T JUST JUDGE IT ON THAT!"
The first book and first movie of the Lord of the Rings is a fantastic start to a grand adventure, and I'm not even a LotR fan. I will accept no excuses.

Honestly, it all leaves me feeling extremely heartbroken. I made many good friends in this game, and this was THE game of choice my own father and I would always boot up, just to play together. I started from Destiny 1, Day One, and got my dad into it during The Taken King. We even wrote our own lore and headcanon, and had our own adventures, writing our own story for us to fill in the gaps Bungie left. It really felt like it was OUR game. We connected even more through this game, through high school and college and whatever nonsense came our ways. He chose Titan, just like me, and I made him a better Sunbreaker than I could ever have been. All just for Bungie to then prey upon us with weak expansion after weak expansion, monetizing everything just for the sake of taking all this money and all this time from us, all to repeatedly underdeliver. Witch Queen looked to be a step in the right direction, like Bungie had finally learned... only for Lightfall to be the single most unpleasant, unfulfilling, and genuinely miserable time we had with anything Destiny related - save only for our own shenanigans bringing in the humor, since not even the game would take itself seriously. We've both decided that this season is where it ends for us as Guardians, and once the next season starts, we're moving on to other games - Bungie has failed us for the last time.
