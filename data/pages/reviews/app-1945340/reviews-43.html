\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you are someone that is driven to play a game by narrative this expansion is not for you. That being said I've been a part of this community through pc and console off and on for 9 years and for the longest time the majority of the community gave zero ♥♥♥♥♥ about the story because narratively it was not great. This started to change in Season of the Chosen when bungie started caring about it's narrative not just the off screen lore. I say all of this to express that this is not a new thing for destiny. Over the past 2 years we've had off and on compelling stories which has made us expect more. I am in no way saying the narrative of lightfall is good, because it isn't, but it has been much worse in the past. The difference with this expansion is in shadowkeep and beyond light they left the game in a bad state, it was extremely buggy for console players especially and they were not generally much fun to play. The same cannot be said about Lightfall. Strand feels not only amazing but is balanced and is actually good EVEN in end game content which stasis lacks other than warlock. The grapple feels amazing and one could make a debate it is the best grapple in video games. Neomuna while feeling empty in some areas is beautiful and has great activities to compensate for the emptiness that is present when the specific area isn't featured for the rotating zone event. The suite of guns delivered are fun and exciting (as always) and the music is on par with other releases like witch queen. The raid while being a little easy is very fun and has amazing weapons to chase with a very good exotic shotgun. I am a fan of Lightfall, this was effectively the opposite of Witch Queen, which was, great expansion off the rip then no one playing after two weeks. Lightfall is bad expansion of the rip but here was are sitting at over 120,000 players on steam at 1030 pm on a Thursday over a month after the expansion came out. I will always play destiny so maybe i'm blind.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's start with the pain point; the campaign. This campaign is mediocre at best. More questions than answers are brought up, the Cloudstriders weren't developed nearly as much as they should've been, and Strand seemed forced in. Calus was an alright villain but wasn't nearly as menacing as he should have been. If you want a good story, this ain't it. As far as difficulty goes, I think the challenge in legendary mode was solid, especially solo. The way they applied the difficulty to the rest of the game, however, is something I can't get behind.
Campaign 4/10

Post campaign content like exotic missions and lore drops are much better than last year. Final Warning is a menace in the Crucible. Deterministic Chaos offers a solid amount of utility. And Winterbite is very broken. All jokes aside though, Winterbite is an exotic I can see still see being good without it's bugs. The lore implications after the campaign make me pretty excited for future seasons. (TITAN IS COMING HOME BABY!!!!). I had some fun cleaning up some of the quests after beating the campaign.
Post campaign content 7/10

The raid was an odd one. Having two raids in a row on a pyramid was something I wasn't hoping for not. Not having the raid of an expansion take place on the new destination of an expansion is something that hasn't been done since Shadowkeep. I personally think not having the raid on Neomuna was a bad call. But that's just my preference, the raid still looks nice. The first encounter (Cataclysm) is a nice introduction to the seed mechanics but not enough people are involved for it to be engaging. The second (Scission) can be so buggy it's not even funny. The launch pads can be so finicky it's just annoying to use them. At the very least it has ONE more person involved in mechanics but that's honestly still not enough. The third encounter (Explicator/Planets) is the best in the raid. It reminds me of the vault from last wish but with a boss, and I like that. The encounter itself looks really cool too. But it's kinda weird they don't bring the raid's central mechanic into it. The fourth and final encounter (Nezarec, Final God of Pain) would be a very interesting and engaging fight if it weren't for A. Nezarec's constant buggy behavior while in damage phase and B. Killing him during final stand doesn't interrupt his wipe animation, meaning that even if you kill him, you restart the encounter. But I like how much of an active threat he is, it keeps you on your toes.
Despite all of it's flaws, most of my problems with Root of Nightmares comes from the bugs it has, fix those and it's only issue is difficulty.
Overall: Root of Nightmares Raid 6.7/10. Solid raid if the bugs are moved out of the way.

Neomuna is visually pleasing, but the destination and it's local activity, Terminal Overload, are honestly a bit disappointing. TO ultimately just felt like a poor attempt at recreating Escalation Protocol from Mars that we had back in the day. The weapons that drop on Neomuna hardly have excited either. The pulse rifle is pretty alright, I personally just don't like 120 hand cannons so Round Robin didn't really do it for me, the Sniper is the same way, and the fusion rifle could be better. The Terminal Overload weapons are also ok, but the mode gets repetitive pretty quickly so getting good roles on them feels like a chore. The strike is going to be interesting to play on higher difficulties, I liked some parts much more than other.
Neomuna and it's activities 6/10

Strand is an interesting beast of a subclass. It actually launched pretty damn balanced in PvP. Buildcrafting doesn't feel limited for it in PvE. It's genuinely strong without being pay-to-win. I'm impressed. I'd say this is where most of the value of buying Lightfall comes from. But that's not exactly a good thing. I like the identities each version of the subclass has between Hunter, Titan, and Warlock. A very strong subclass while still giving Arc, Solar, Void, and Stasis room to breathe. The process of obtaining the aspects and fragments are also much better than Stasis and Beyond Light.
Strand 9/10

Season of Defiance was rough. The Battleground strikes were good, but not what I was expecting considering we had already had Battlegrounds last season. The story didn't get me to care enough about anything that happens, which is bad considering the theme of it is the cost we're suffering at home on Earth as a result of open war with the Shadow legion. The seasonal weapons are pretty solid all things considered. The exotic mission has interesting implications on the future story of year 6. Plus it's a fun time. As a whole this season was strong at it's core gameplay wise but didn't offer anything too exciting. I can say it isn't mediocre however.
Season of Defiance 7/10

Please note that this review was for Lightfall and not the entirety of year 6. We still got at least 11 months if not more until the release of The Final Shape so there is still much to experience. Considering Strand and the raid, I can say that if you were already planning on getting Lightfall, it isn't exactly a ripoff. I can see this expansion being a solid pickup in the future in the grand scheme of the story in terms of the campaign. The campaign is where most of the complaints for this expansion lie. But where it lacked in the campaign, I'd say it makes up for it in other areas. I can say $50 USD is worth it depending on your budget. I cannot say the Annual Pass is worth the money right now, but to be fair, more than half the content that comes with it isn't in the game yet. The gameplay improvements that came with this expansion were not discussed here, but my feelings towards most of those changes are positive save for the difficulty changes at large. I have mixed feelings about this DLC, but I think there are positives to be had here.

Overall score for Destiny 2: Lightfall: 7/10\t\t\t\t\t
