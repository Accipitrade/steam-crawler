\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review will only discuss Lightfall content, not the changes to the base game that came with its release.

First off, the $50 listing was because the season pass was included, but I didn't want the season pass. There was no other option in the Steam store, so I just went with it but have no plans to engage in the seasonal content extensively. I'm tired of the seasonal model that stretches out stories and side stories. The entirety of Destiny 2's Light vs Darkness saga has gone on for too long because of it.

Now on to Lightfall. The campaign overall gives the same vibe as the Red War, except the story is heavily downplayed by the lack of information around various story elements. The Veil, Strand's existence and why only we are harnessing it, witnessing what The Witness is capable of yet so little happens (which is expressed by Calus even). If this is to be addressed in the future seasons, I'd say forget it and move on from this game. It took three years to link events leading the The Witness coming, and now I have to wait possibly another year to maybe fight The Witness? And the power Calus was granted was a letdown given that Rhulk from the Vow of the Disciple raid took multiple Guardians to take down, but it takes one Guardian to defeat Calus. Strand's integration into the story felt very forced, much like when an overpowered character in a story is used to progress other peoples' stories (like our Guardian fighting these big names versus anyone else). The characters keep mentioning it as a means of progression or a way to fight back, and the reasoning seemed ridiculous.

Neomuna, the new destination, feels like a contradiction. There are people, but they aren't there. Their conscience is in the network, so you'll never see them. A pretty sad excuse to not have NPCs anywhere at all. What's the point of all the buildings then if they're just living their lives virually? It's so empty and lifeless. It felt like its creation was strictly for the campaign missions and nothing else.

The missions in the campaign were each unique, but for some reason, they didn't feel as memorable as The Witch Queen's missions and sometimes felt awkward or unnecessary. We had a unique new enemy, Tormentor, who gave a lot of players trouble in Legendary difficulty. We had a mission that involved using a Drake Tank and a mission with its own Hammer of Dawn from Gears of War. But then we had a recycled concept with the Cabal getting overshields the same way Hive got overshields in The Witch Queen. We were forced to use Strand in various circumstances, and it was forced as an obstacle or solution in the story. There were parts where one has to ask themselves "Did I really need Strand in all of that?"

The final boss was essentially a reskin, oversized Colossus and Gladiator. The fight overall felt like a great design for both Strand and non-Strand users, until I got to second phase. The Gladiator aspect of the fight screamed "Bungie didn't test this themselves". They ignored the existing fact that Gladiators and any other mob push a player when lunging at them. Now scale that up by x5 with the boss's size and lunge distance. Very thoughtless for all players. The developers in charge of designing the last boss fight were probably only thinking of pushing the need for boosting Airborne Effectiveness and the use of Strand.

Side note: A lot of the campaign and the new strike felt like they were ignoring the existence of melee/Sword/CQC players.

Strand doesn't look good on paper, but it's very good in practice after you get some Fragments. Unfortunately, the campaign fails to help players see this as the Fragments cannot be obtained until after the campaign, and even worse, Strand is only accessible during certain missions at certain points. This is probably where the inherent problem with integrating a new subclass lies. They want us to try it and use it in campaign, but not to its full potential.

Honestly, the only aspects I really liked were the Tormentors, the last stand against the Cabal in the final mission, and the soundtracks. The soundtracks were really good this time around, but I hear people play without music on.
