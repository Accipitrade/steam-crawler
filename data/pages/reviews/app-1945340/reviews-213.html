\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHot take, Lightfall isn't terrible.

Firstly, I took a break from destiny after beyond light came out and didn't pick it back up consistently until the end of season of the seraph, so like, January of this year. Now that you know where I stand lets address the issues the masses have thus far.

"Strand is weak." I would much rather a new subclass be weak at launch, and it be buffed into a good place than it coming out broken and being nerfed into the floor. To each their own I guess.

"Poor Performance on my PC." I can sympathize with this a little, I went from getting 130-160 fps to 90-120 with some drops even lower. Neomuna has some relatively intense lighting so that can explain it, but it is worth of note.

"Why does the expansion cost more than witch queen did at launch." The base version comes with season 20 of the seasonal content, more on this later.

Now lets get to the big stuff:
"Poor Writing/Nothing is explained." I will concede that the writing at times is a little off beat for destiny, but I never found it obtrusive. Many say that Osiris cant shut up or is constantly whining. I would argue that he is the only character in the dlc that is actually worried about the in game situation. Many say that Nimbus is annoying as-well, meh, his voice is weird but again, not terrible. I could see that the pacing is a little off, but in context I think it works *i will explain later*.

This dlc relies on a macguffin, as do most things, and the macguffin while isn't explained down to the finest details, the characters and player character are able to infer what the macguffin does especially with the context of the previous expansion, and the lore within. The main critisism that I agree with is that the campaign does force the new subclass down your throat and it can get annoying.

Back to the pacing/narrative issue, this dlc comes with season 20 "Season of Defiance" which will most likely tie up alot of the loose ends, and add to it. After the raid goes live and it is finished a cutscene will go live for everyone, most likely adding to the story, on top of the exotic quests and flavor text for items. Many people expected this DLC to be something that is isn't. It wasnt going to be just like Witch Queen, or follow the same beats. Lightfall is a setup for the Final Shape, the big dlc for next year. Much of the same how Beyond Light was a setup for Witch Queen. Lightfall was originally part of the final shape, and was later separated to bridge a year long content gap. As it stands without any of the seasonal content and the raid, Lightfall is a weaker narrative, but it is not a mostly negative expansion, as the majority of other people would like to suggest. I am having fun.

TL;DR
new subclass needs some work,
read the lore, listen to the dialogue,
seasonal content and the raid can add more context,
Narrative isn't the best, but it isnt shadowkeep levels of wack either.
I dont regret buying the dlc, I regret looking at the collective fandoms opinion and letting it sour my fun.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStory: Poorly explains anything and feels rushed. I would say things are spread thin, but the only story beats are us learning strand and Calus trying to get MacGuffin that we don't even get told why is important. The Witness is there, but I can't really recall him doing anything special outside of two major events. The only memorable new NPC is Nimbus, and for all the wrong reasons. If Cringe was a subclass, Nimbus would be the one who teaches us how to use it. Hearing their voice is annoying enough, but whoever wrote this dialogue needs to be moved to a lesser role. It sounds like zoomer dialogue written by a boomer that falls out of the chair laughing every time they see a Minions meme on Facebook. There was even an obvious chance to develop this character and have them grow to become more mature via the story, but that was completely ignored.

Strand: Fun, but not viable outside of higher rank activities in it's launch state. Grappling is cool but it has too long of a cool down for it a lot of times contributing more fun than actual utility. We don't have all the fragments to fit into our builds yet, but the subclass in general offers far less than the other 4 for PvE. Funny mobility might be okay in PvP, but I doubt it will be popular past this month.

New Mod System: The new mod menu is nice and while it is cool that armor element isn't a worry anymore, it all came at the cost of removing most of the mods that added complexity and intricacy to the game. It has been dumbed down to a less satisfying system that I feel has been accurately called "babies first buildcraft" for good reason. They did this to make it easier for new players, but ended up hurting the game for everyone. It is nice to be able to save things as loadouts now, but DIM is still better for people who know what they are doing.

Guardian Ranks: Awful for new and veteran players. If you are new, you will get basic info about the world and where to go, but it still leaves out a decent bit of basic tutorials for players to learn. Better than what is was before though, but that isn't saying much. All "veteran" players are put at the same level regardless of time/experience in the game previous to Lightfall. It is still too early to say at the moment, but currently the way it works feel like an arbitrary change that doesn't really do anything meaningful. Everyone just looks like they are the same experience, which is overall misleading for new players that are expecting these numbers to mean something. At least season pass level gave an idea of how many hours someone spent playing the game in the past 3 months.

Witch Queen was so good and I tried not to let it raise my expectations, but just the expansion story/campaign just feels so lazy. I would rather play Curse of Osiris again.\t\t\t\t\t

Story is okay but feels rushed and brushes over many spots it seems for times sake, like they knew they were going to make only 8 missions an a new area to explore with 3-4 hub spots to run around but they are all small slightly open areas (for use of the grapple mechanic) but leave little to actually explore and with the removal of planet resources both the random chests and resource nodes are pointless outside of trying to LVL up your faction XP.
