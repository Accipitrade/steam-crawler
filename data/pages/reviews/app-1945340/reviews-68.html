\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've thought long on this, I gave it a chance. I can't in good conscience recommend this chapter. The story makes no sense anymore, it's also way too brief for a DLC chapter. I feel cheated this time. Everything has been dumbed down which I can live with but it makes builds feel very flat now. The concept of fixed difficulty levels is one I like if it means we can do away with the pointless Light Level grind but instead we have both. The increased difficulty spoken of takes the form of enemies appearing out of nowhere to one shot you. You know you cleared that corner but as soon as you turn your back a big bad spawns into it and you die. Between that and the bugs every death just feels like you have been robbed. Strand is a lot of fun, I will give it that. It's just a shame nothing else is. I pre-purchased the season pass as I always do but this is the first time I can honestly say I regret it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFiller should never be acceptable in a video game, yet Bungie sold filler while lying every step of the way during the marketing campaign. Lightfall returns to darker times in the Destiny franchise with this massive step back: a phenomenal core idea tarnished by a studio scared about keeping their engagement metrics in the green more than delivering a satisfying experience. Power creep remains unchecked yet much of the sandbox languishes in a state of uneven balance. Many core primaries are terrible yet ability spam and an infinite ammo economy are insanely easy to achieve. The game has largely been made more difficult so players need to "earn their loot", but rewards are so threadbare you wonder if the activities are worth your time. Encounter design has reached it's worst state yet, as developers create themed encounters but fail to understand how to make an encounter satisfying or interesting. Bugs have reached an all time high: numerous fps bugs cause instant deaths and Main Story Elevators to break, fall damage intended to no longer kill kills constantly, character models become invisible every few matches impacting PVP until you restart.

Worst of all, when the experience landed and people raised their concerns, Bungie went silent. Not the 'nose to the grindstone' type of silence, but talking about all the love and ignoring all of the criticism. Bungie lied about their product and traded the trust they fought hard for the past few years for money to keep the train rolling. They could right this ship in a minute with simple changes, but never forget they set a price tag on what they think your trust was worth. 40/100$ is what you're love for destiny 2 was worth to them, and that feels pretty cheap.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverything about this expansion screams "unfinished." Maybe this expansion will be worthwhile once final shape comes out, but This expansion should be good on its own. After the campaign, I knew the raid needed to be amazing to save the expansion, but as a veteran and dedicated raider, it didn't meet that mark. I love to sherpa raids and this is the first time that I legitimately don't want to teach a raid because of how simple/boring it is. It feels like a dungeon that they added an encounter to so that more than 2 people are required for intended mechanics. I would also possibly like strand more if it weren't for the fact that as a warlock main, I am still essentially forced to use solar or void for the higher-end content I prefer. No matter what aspect of the game you prefer, I cannot recommend this expansion. Here's hoping for less cash-grab tactics in the future, and maybe some more attention given to the dedicated veteran player base. (that being said, if you're new, this is a great time to start with bungie catering to new players)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's really unfortunate. After The Witch Queen being so amazing everyone was really hoping Lightfall would be a continuation of that, especially from the amazing ending to Year 5 in Season of the Seraph.

What we got was disappointing at best.

Positives first: Strand is super fun. It started off weak as hell and everyone (including me) complained but once they gave us all the fragments to play with instead of locking it behind arbitrary dates, it was clear that it was powerful. It is an excellent addition to the sandbox and I cannot wait to see how it evolves over Year 6.

The gameplay and sandbox is probably the most fun it has ever been. I love blowing things up with any of the 3.0 subclass verbs, supers being amazing for DPS again, and the general gunplay improvements and changes to add density philosophy have made most activities chaotic fun.

The raid was...eh? No raid in Destiny is terrible for sure, but something just fell flat between Vow of the Disciple and Root of Nightmares. An extremely simple mechanic (connecting one dot to another dot several times) that only gets iterated on by...adding different colored dots to connect? Third encounter with moving the planets was a great spectacle fight and I was excited to see how that'd be iterated upon for the final boss and then it just...doesn't. It was disappointing to say the least.

As always though, the music (both in the overall expansion and the raid) was top tier as always and so is pretty much every vista. Destiny's art teams have been carrying the franchise for years and this release is no exception. The raid is stunningly unique and Nezarec is amazingly voice acted. Despite the mechanics and overall difficulty being disappointing, the spectacle of the raid was fantastic and will cement it as one of the best raids to rerun.

The main disappointment was the campaign. Holy hell, what happened? The Witch Queen's campaign was near universally praised, both for its narrative and its legendary difficulty. How did we go from that to this? The story is atrocious and riddled with holes. What's the Veil? We're not going to tell you, we'll just assume you know. What's the Radial Mast? The thing you haven't destroyed yet, get to it. What did the Witness do to the Traveler? No one knows but everyone is assuming it's dead.

Legendary was at least less of a disappointment but it was a step down from the last one. The difficulty was just...non-existent. Even with a full fireteam of three we mowed through each and every encounter with relative ease. I suspect this has to do with the fact that Strand was drip-fed to you throughout, meaning it couldn't be too difficult to account for the subclass missing 90% of its kit.

Despite that, there is one moment in the campaign that is probably the best moment in all of Destiny. During the final mission, you are fighting with Caiatl and her Cabal in front of the gate to the Veil. Narratively, still weak. But you are fighting wave after wave after wave after wave of Calus' cabal and it was chaotic and it was desperate and it was amazing and I wish it lasted longer and I want more of it.

This isn't the death-knell for Destiny like some people are claiming it to be. Nor is there nothing to be worried about.

Destiny 2 has hit an iceberg. Not a huge one, it hasn't ripped through the hull and caused it to start sinking. But it has caused damage. Enough that the crew really needs to take a look to see how much and what they can do to repair it. Its current path is unsustainable and brings us uncomfortably close to the uncertainty of Destiny's past. Much like Beyond Light, the seasons will have to do some serious carrying to The Final Shape.

I cannot recommend Lightfall in its current state. I don't think I can recommend it in any state. Even if a new season comes out that is so good that you're actively committing a crime by not playing it, I'd just say get the season and not the expansion.\t\t\t\t\t
