The story does not answer any multi-year questions, the campaign is rushed, and the new NPC allies are so "Try hard to be funny" in a bad way.

A $50 expansion gets you:
1 quick to forget campaign with a couple cool cutscenes
1 new strike (we used to get 3 for less money in early DLCs)
1 new PvP map (ONLY ONE!!!!!)

I love Bungie and hope they figure it out when the next Destiny 3.exe comes in 3-5 years. Until then, Destiny 2 is now just a "Play for a week and move on" type of game. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you play this game already and are invested, then there's no harm in getting Lightfall. That being said, I do not recommend this for new players when it comes to the story. There's some good, some bad, but overall as a die-hard fan of the franchise I am disapointed by the story. This will be as spoiler free as can be .

The Pros:
  • The gameplay is still one of Destiny's strongest features. Not much has changed in terms of the overall gunplay. The campaign was a very enjoyable experience when talking strictly about gameplay. The vision of being the leading charge into a war far from home was achieved and done very well, keeping the experience very engaging.
  • The new subclass (Strand) is a very fun addition to the game. While writing this I am not sure of how strong it is right now but is an incredibly fun addition and will hopefully be developed on throughout the year.
  • I've seen a lot of people not liking the new location and how the Cloud Striders don't fit into the universe but I will disagree and say they are a good addition (even though Nimbus can be a bit much I will agree). The new location is visually great and enjoyed the different areas in the campaign.

The Cons:
  • As mentioned from the start, the campaign was a big disappointment to most people I've seen talk about this and has been a letdown for me. The main thing you are searching for and trying to protect is not explained. Even content creators who look at all the different bits of story and lore also have no idea what it is. This leads to an ending where we have no idea what has actually happened and unsure how we should feel or even care about what happened.
  • Following that, the campaign itself feels rushed. A lot of the issues are about the campaign and the story. There are 8 missions total and are a good 25-30 minutes each on legendary difficulty which felt good. The issue is that this lead to a lot of events happening too quickly without any proper development and will leave the average player confused and still even confuses the more invested players.
  • The narrative seemed to do a complete 180 and went back to vanilla D2 marvel-esq dialogue. A big reason why I liked and engaged more with the story of Destiny was back in Forsaken when they killed Cayde. His death lead to a more serious tone from all the characters as it felt more and more like a dystopian universe while still being hopeful that there is still something that can be done. This issue is directed more to the way Nimbus is written as being a rebellious rookie but makes witty jokes during what is believed to be a universe ending threat while everyone else takes the situation seriously. I still like Nimbus. they're fine, but they're dialogue for the situation is SO bad

It is a massive disappointment to go from an experience like The Witch Queen, having a brilliant campaign experience and story to what is supposed to be "The beginning of the end" where we literally have no idea what even happened and will probably now have to wait an entire year to see what it leads to. This worries me as to how all of this will end with the final expansion coming which will end the entire Light and Dark saga.

If you are new to the game and want to have an enjoyable campaign experience, I would start with Witch Queen, especially with it going on sale now.

While I enjoyed the gameplay of Lightfall, the campaign was a big letdown and missed so many opportunities and you would probably agree after playing it. I cannot recommend this to new players who are not familiar with the story. For people currently playing the game and are still invested then it's fine as you will still enjoy everything you do in Destiny as nothing has changed, but this is an expansion you can miss out on, which is sad to say for someone who's enjoyed the game throughout the years despite the rough spots it's been in.\t\t\t\t\t
