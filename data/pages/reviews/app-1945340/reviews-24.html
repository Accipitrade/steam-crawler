\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter seeing all the negative comments I was hesitant to purchase Lightfall, but I did and I ended up enjoying it. While I generally agree the story isn't as rich as Witch Queen and it leaves more questions than answers, there are several other elements of Lightfall I really enjoyed:

- Strand - I liked the way the campaign was built around it, and I needed the tutorials to get used to it. It really opens up a lot of movement for guardians
- Legendary Campaign - I enjoyed the challenge of the legendary campaign. Each mission took me several attempts and the boss fights were fun
- The environment and level design - I enjoyed the new destinations on Neptune and the level design around it. Fresh take and art style compared to other expansions, and it was neat to dive into a golden age relic
- The music is always on point
- The destructible environment pieces in some places made battles more intense; I noticed more of than any previous expansions
- The characters I did not mind although I understand why people think Nimbus is cheesy. Rohan/Osiris are great
- The lore (that is available now) is rich and it was neat to learn about the population of Neomuna

Overall this felt like the beginning of a several season story. And while I don't necessarily agree with Bungie's pricing models, the studio does good work. Especially given the major flops from other game dev companies this year. I was ok with funding them to create more epic content\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly I liked it a lot. Nimbus is a nice change of pace compared to the super-serious cast of pretty much every dlc - i can see why people don't like them, they have "marvel" style dialogue, but IMO i think thats a good way to differentiate Neomuna citizens from every other race in Destiny. I mean, c'mon, these guys are living in the metaverse. As for story, overall, short as heck, vague, questionable, sure, but not as bad as people make it out to be. Strand has honestly become my new favourite subclass, and Neomuna feels like it has more secrets than most of the recent DLC releases. The arcade lost sector reminded me a lot of the disco fallen lost sector from Forsaken. I think the biggest reason why fans arent so keen on this DLC is how *different* it is. I, personally, find that difference refreshing - but as a lore fan, they did kinda spit in our faces and force us to read the wikis. But overall, not terrible.

Anyways, TL;DR : Is Lightfall great? no. Good? maybe, it depends on what you look for in story & worldbuilding. Bad? not by a longshot.\t\t\t\t\t
