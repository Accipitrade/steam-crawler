\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Story is Part 1 of 2, be prepared to be waiting for answers. Gameplay systems are massive upgrades almost across the board, and new subclass is very fun once you get the customizable version in postgame.

Decent campaign, albeit one that necessitates the player being ok with having questions posed that dont get answered neat and tidy by the end, and require waiting for the future plot to be explained. This DLC is very much "Part 1 of the finale", and in that lens its fine. Other reviews will harp on about "how all the characters talk about a McGuffin (The Veil) that they never explain even though every single character and their mother knows what it is", and after replaying with a careful ear for this particular pain point, I can say clearly that none of the player-aligned characters know very much about The Veil, and never even imply that they do. All they really ever talk about is that we know its important to the baddies, and therefore we must stop them from getting it. Every bit of what the characters know can be explained by plot points that are fairly obvious to someone who has been around for the past year or two, and if not, as long as you understand that the characters dont actually know *what* is is, rather just that it is important, you'll have a better time. Secondly, the postgame quests start to elaborate on what it is, in a round-about way, and I for one am looking forward to learning if I am correct in my hunches throughout the next year. Because for better or for worse, Destiny IS leaning into the "live game" aspect and telling a story over time, and this expac is just a part of the finale. If that's not for you, and I can't judge you for that, maybe steer clear of the game as a whole, because that's what Destiny is.

The new Subclass is a TON of fun, though unlocking the modular Fragments in the postgame is really needed to make the classes work. That said, each class has at least one very strong, fully fleshed out build, with many other options to take to make your Stand playtime a joy. Grapple is of course the flashy star of the show, but the non-movement focused aspects of the class are great too.

Raid is not out as of the time of review, but I dont have any doubts about what the Raids team can do.

While yes, many of the new destination and seasonal guns are based on older model bases, this is explained in lore for anyone with a brain bigger than 144p, and regardless, they have decent perk combos and element types that we haven't seen before, so I dont quite see the bone to pick here. Look at Witch Queens Throne World guns: They had new models for each one, but the actual gameplay impact was minimal, I dont think i've seen a single person using one of them since like the second week of WQ. I'd rather have good "recycled" content than trash "novel" content.

The new mod system is the old system, rolled into one and streamlined. No, all my favorite builds didn't make it through, and overall ability uptime got hit a bit, but the new system is very powerful in its own right and is better for the game. the loadout system is AMAZING, much better than I had even dared hope for. Incredible change, 11/10, bravo. Quality of Life for DAYS.

While I cant speak for future seasons, the current one that you get for free with any version of the expac continues directly following the plot of the expac and provides powerful and fun artifact mods to play with.If you are new to the game, please know that the seasonal content is almost always relevant the the plot nowadays and the Lightfall story will be very likely continued in meaningful ways throughout the next 12 months.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThough I do have gripes with the expansion, I still think this is a decent expansion.

I would not reccommend this to new players, but I would to your average Destiny enjoyer. The campaign narratively does have issues, especially following how good Witch Queen was. It can (and has) impeded on peoples views of the expansion.

That being said, the gameplay is fantastic. Strand as a subclass is a lot of fun on every class. The post campaign has a better narrative, better fleshing out Neomuna and the characters and the exotics are very enjoyable! Neomuna is a fantastic enviroment, reminding me a lot of Coruscant from Star Wars the Old Republic as well as some of the best and most unique lost sectors Ive seen. New guns/ perks are fun, and as I said before the exotics are proving to be fun as well!

After finishing the campaign I have been swept up in the QoL changes and gameplay and have been genuinely enjoying the game.

Heres hoping the raid is great, as it could balance out the issue of the campaign's story!\t\t\t\t\t
