\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright so many people are being really negative about this DLC. Some of the criticism is valid, others well not really. I'm going to give a critical review of this DLC as a Game Design and Development student and whether you should/should not get it. First off, narrative. This was done fairly well, but left me with a few more questions than answers. I feel as though the campaign focused a little too much on Strand(the new subclass) and could have maybe focused a bit more on Calus/the Witness/other Lightfall lore if we had unlocked Strand earlier. However, this does not mean the campaign is bad. I had quite a bit of fun playing and the music as always is top tier, hats off to Bungie for the killer soundtrack. Secondly, the new loadout and armour mod system. The loadout system is very nice, while there are limited slots, you can very easily fit your favourite builds into those slots. The armour mod change is quite refreshing, though it did make end game builds a bit less dynamic with the removal of elemental mods. I quite like this system even though I have seen some creators mald over the fact that it panders to the "casuals" too much. I have been playing this game and Destiny since 2014, and honestly I couldn't care less about what creators say, they are in a league of their own, as long as I'M the one having fun then it doesn't bother me, and yes, yes I am having fun.

So... should you get this DLC?

In my eyes... Yes. It is quite a bit of fun. If you are critical about the story then maybe this DLC isn't for you. If you want to be a green spider man flying across the map all the time then YES get it, Strand is a LOT of fun.

Overall a pretty good DLC. I wouldn't give it S tier status like the Witch Queen, but it is definitely solid.

Well there's my 2 cents, do with this information what you will, I will continue to enjoy the game and keep my reservations for this DLC critical but fair.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I really enjoyed this DLC, it isn't the best by far and it does have its flaws but it is certainly enjoyable.
Nimbus and Rohan are pretty enjoyable to listen to and Nimbus personally wasn't as annoying as some people have been complaining about - they have pretty entertaining dialogue where it occasionally balances on the line of 'cringe', but that isn't bad.
The story seems a little chaotic so to speak and that's coming from someone who doesn't always keep up to date with the lore, never heard of some of the things introduced and alot of people seem angry that some new words, objects and names are being tossed about.
The missions are all pretty enjoyable, some feel a bit slow here and there but most of the time I enjoyed every single mission - some people heavily disliked the final mission, but personally it was thrilling and extremely punishing on legendary difficulty.
Plus the Tormentors were an amazing addition to the game, honestly they should be more aggressive at times as they seem to just wait around for you to act first, plus most attacks are a tad slow - apart from that, I was on the edge of my seat fighting them!

Overall, entertaining dlc, nice story, a few new characters and just more Destiny 2.
7 / 10

Could've had some more information on some of the lore beforehand, more Osiris would've been nice and the new Mods system and the 'Journey' features seem just unusual, personally they don't seem overly useful with Bungie attempting to "Reinvent the wheel", but they might just have to grow on me and I may be proven wrong.\t\t\t\t\t
