Lightfall is the World's First Strand Type "DLC".

The Story:
Thought it was really good personally, until...the ending, which has several plotholes which are actually mind numbingly awful and infuriating! 9 Years of build up for "yeah we ignore the players basic agency to sort of drag the plot to where we want it to be" even though this DLC was the first one to really try and drive home the fact that We, the Player have agency. (The first time our Guardian ever takes control and gives orders to other NPC's happens here which is not a good precedent to set mid story then completely ignore) This is completely ignored in the end cutscene and we return to being the mute sociopath with about as much input as a falling grain of sand.
Terribly disappointed in the writer's for this one as the build up over the last year was actually phenomenal (for Bungie) and they really tripped and fell on their face at the finish line.
Bungie marketed this as "our Empire Strikes Back" and here it felt like the empire was not Calus' Shadow Legion but once again the true antagonist of our player Guardian... F.O.M.O. - "Suppressor Of Wise Purchasing Decisions"
and The Market-ing Team - "Hype Machine, seller of a long dead dream"

Gameplay and encounters:
I cannot believe I am saying this but... WOW!
Best campaign experience I have ever had in a Destiny DLC, levels were really well designed for combat encounters really taking advantage of both Strand and the other Supers for maximum use of space in rooms, Tormentors are the most interesting thing gameplay wise since Reckoning and Season Of Opulence (early 2019). Bungie really really knocked it out the park in terms of fun, frenetic encounter design.

New Subclass:
Strand... for the long and short, I've only played it through on my Hunter but Destiny ain't that complex and I know how the other classes will feel once I hop on them.
TBH I love strand so far, the rest of the fragments will be unlocked today which will benefit build crafting for it a LOT.
The grappling hook is probably what you'd expect if you've played Apex Legends/Titanfall 2 or Halo Infinite. Very very fun to use and the momentum carries very nice with a lot of directional control in the player's hand ;). Am excited to see what aspects and fragments are alloted to the classes in the year to come.

Mods, Buildcrafting and Endgame tier readiness.
Ability regen really does suffer here however as bungie have shifted the buildcrafting and mods system to be far far simpler and less rewarding imo. This means there will be a lot of time's unless you really build into ability regen (losing power as a result) where you will have NO ABILITIES ON HAND. This is a result of a Mods 3.0 system rework introduced with Lightfall making the buildcrafting system far easier for new or returning players whereas active players have had their builds gutted and dumbed down to the point where within 5 minutes you'll find the 5 or 6 mods you will more than likely throw on and keep on for the coming year unless there's some REAL innovation coming. The reason this is so disappointing is that for the last year we have been far far more powerful than we were in our past but not through "Light Level" but... and this may be hard to believe but... through GAMEPLAY!!! Yes, Destiny in the Witch Queen era felt more like Doom Eternal than Halo Reach but we've slipped back down the mountain once again falling to a more stiff, stationary, gunplay focused experience. Not saying that's inherently bad, just not as good as where we were and these new mod changes really drive that fact home.
As for difficulty, well... Bungie have changed the way difficulty works so we are now back to the "arbitrary power level" difficulty increase as opposed to genuinely more engaging gameplay. Lazy and a painful regress to accommodate a more casual causal audience.

Well folks. That's all I have to say for now as of 03/03/2023. Will update and amend as I see fit. Take care of your selfs!
Eyes Up! Per audacia ad astra!!!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI laugh to myself when I see the comments about the story, and not knowing what the veil or whatever is. Not knowing doesn't bother me because I couldn't tell you anything about any of the story, from when it was released. I skip what I can and don't bother to read any of the lore. Names are brought up often and other than the vanguard characters, I couldn't tell you who they are talking about.

I play Destiny to shoot things and do space magic, that's it. If I want something with a story, I'll play something else.

As for strand, I doubt I'll use it much. I didn't like being shoehorned into using the dumb grapple and not have a grenade in the campaign. If i want to grapple, I'll go play any of the Arkham games or Gotham Knights. I was glad to see after you gain access to strand, it does have options for things other than the grapple.

The city is good enough and at least it's colorful. New things to do, new things to shoot, and new things to wear. Once I clear all the campaign associated missions, i'll do the seasonal stuff and if it's like the last three or four seasons, I'll play it a lot (notice a lot is not one word, I hate that), and then play something else the last couple of weeks until the new season begins.

The ability to save loadouts is great. I need to get used to the mod system to figure out the best set ups for gear. They keep taking away or nerfing all the things I've used before. Warmind cells, then charged with light, and the last few seasons of using wells. I don't care about the vanguard rank or whatever it's called. I almost always play solo and I'm sure some of the steps for the next rank will require playing with others, so I doubt I'll ever advance past six, where I started.\t\t\t\t\t
