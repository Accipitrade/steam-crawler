\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter finishing the campaign on legend and doing a bit of post-campaign content, I'm a bit mixed on Lightfall. Off the bat, the gameplay of the story missions is some of the most fun I've had in the entire franchise, although a major part of that is likely due to how unique Strand is. I think my biggest problem with Lightfall, and where most others draw issues as well, is the narrative. In Witch Queen, it felt like Bungie was finally capable of crafting a compelling narrative that nicely concluded within the campaign, while still leaving room for expansion throughout the year. Lightfall's story actually started off fairly strong in my opinion but began to feel rushed towards the latter half of the campaign. By the end, we're left with more questions than answers and certain characters feel underdeveloped. I think most of the harsh feedback the DLC is getting is more from a place of disappointment and anger with Bungie as it seems they've forgotten what made WQ so amazing. Overall, I'd recommend Lightfall if you are a fan of Destiny, after all its more Destiny, but if you were a returning vet or new player waiting for the game to reach the next level, I'm afraid you'll have to keep waiting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros:

1) Strand is incredible. A fantastic new subclass. I definitely need to experiment with it more, but my goodness is it fun. Swinging out of thin air is always a fun time.

2) Neomuna is an amazing new location. It feels like a Destiny map, There's so much good stuff with exploring and feeling like Spider-Man swinging through it. It strangely feels also refreshed to have a location that isn't plagued by ruins of the pre-collapse era.

3) The new characters are a fantastic addition, including the cloud striders. They aren't guardians, so it makes them 10x more bad-ass when they do anything putting themselves in danger. Returning characters, driven by Osiris, maintain their role as a pivotal force in the story.

Cons:

1) The story, while a majority of it being a solid experience, is ALL OVER THE PLACE!!! They tried to cram way too much into 8 major missions. The story features strand, a new world, Calus's pivotal moment, the Traveler's pivotal moment, and setting up the end of the Light vs Dark saga. This would be hard to do for ANY dev team, but doing this all in 8 missions is just way too messy. It was fun, and I enjoyed the campaign on legendary, but it felt extremely rushed in places.

2) The thing that always irks me is that there is always story elements that are extremely important, yet NEVER explained. You just have to either guess or look it up. An example is the Veil. It's mentioned so many times, and it's even show off to us, but we never get a clear explanation of what it is or why it does what it does in the moment it does the thing (no spoilers). The final cutscene was also extremely confusing. I just wished more was presented in either cutscenes or narrated to us, rather than in these damn lore books.

3) The grapple on Strand, while fun, is VERY hard to control I figured it out eventually, but my goodness, I died so many times and my buddy and I had to wipe on legendary just because the grapple didn't work as intended.

4) The most disappointing feature is the hype. This is supposed to be the peak of the Light Vs Dark war. Other than the opening cutscene, our guardian feels completely disconnected from this "war". I wanted an entire campaign of massive space battles and huge epic showoffs between us and the Pyramids, and we don't get ANY of that. I really hope the last expansion of this saga, "The Final Shape" really delivers, or else people will feel very angry.

Overall: I would definitely recommend this expansion to any Destiny fan, but I think many of you should wait for this to go on sale. While I had a blast playing it, anyone who isn't a hardcore player is either going to be confused, or will feel like they are in an action movie that has no purpose. Playing the campaign on legendary is way more rewarding, and I recommend even casual players at least try it. It makes you stop and appreciate the missions and not just blow through them like the base game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor those of you in it for the Main Campaign and Story, play Witch Queen instead cause this isn't it.

Gameplay is fine, it's Destiny 2 you know what to expect. But the Story and Tone of the campaign just doesn't compare to former dlc's leaving a directionless narrative that almost feels like a long tutorial for Strand. Personally I would take Strand and the Campaign out of the equation when you look at the 50$ price tag, because both of those just are not worth factoring into the cost.

Neomuna itself is a very nice looking location, but there is a lack of verticality that was originally hoped for during trailers. Not to mention the lack of citizens, failing to make the city feel alive and lived in.

The strike just feels like something reskinned from Season of the Splicer and a few of the later levels in the campaign have some very questionable level designs that can lead to frustration when you are forced to use strand to traverse them. This is especially problematic on legendary when you have to sacrifice your current build for a barebones uncustomizable version of strand.

Oh and one more thing. For all you buildcrafters out there who like to fiddle around with builds and experiment with armor mods. Almost HALF of the armor mods are just gone. Some got merged together, but many of the more niche and interesting mods have been either over simplified or removed outright taking away much of the fun of buildcrafting.\t\t\t\t\t
