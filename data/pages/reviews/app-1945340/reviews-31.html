\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that this had been out for a bit, I can finally let out some concrete thoughts.

The new destination, Neomuna, is really amazing, much better (in my opinion) than Savuthun's Throne World. The aesthetics and details are all there, and its a wholly unique place compared to other destinations. I have spent countless hours just wandering around, exploring, completing patrols, as well as grinding the public events.
The new quality of life stuff--gameplay wise--has been super nice, finally allowing more flexibility with varying builds to handle tougher enemies instead of forcing to use a particular weapon has been a much needed feature. The new modding system is a big improvement, as well as item management becoming way more simple than past are more than welcome features. Not to mention the new subclass, Strand, is the most fun I have had in Destiny in years.

Where this DLC fumbles the bag completely, however, is the story. I can't even say that, because there was a massive lack of story for a DLC that is meant to set up next years big conclusion (Lightfall) to this ten year story all Destiny veterans and alike have been playing through. Now we are all worried how Lightfall will be handled and if it will even stick the landing. I can't recommend this DLC purely because of that. All the previous changes listed, besides Neomuna and Strand, are free to all players, so what you pay for is not worth it at all. Sure the new destination is great, but it is not necessary to enjoy the rest of the game at all. Strand is freakin awesome, but I don't think it switches up the game enough to become necessary to buy.

All in all, this DLC was not it at all and it sadly has put a poor taste in my mouth regarding Destiny. Which is not a great thing to say as the past few months of the game have left me dry of any motivation to keep logging on.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like Strand, I also like the things that came for free with Season 20's update. Unfortunately I'm not sure I can recommend this to both old or new players.

Good stuff:
Like I said, Strand is really fun and creative (if you unlock it anyway). It makes you play the game mostly differently, and Grapple gives the game much needed mobility - it's a grappling hook that's green. I really like the Threadlings from Warlocks and makes me feel like a green supervillain of some kind. It's also powerful outside of the campaign, allowing you to dish out massive damage and deal with huge crowds with relative ease.

Terminal Overload is kinda cool, and allows you to target farm some good guns from Neptune. It's like Escalation Protocol on Mars back in the Warmind era. It's also not a push over, so don't expect to solo this on your first or tenth attempt. Just don't forget to rank up your Neomuna standing to level 14, that's how you get non-generic drops from the final 2 chests.

The Campaign's final boss is fun. Definitely not as exhausting as Savathun last year with her whopping 4-phase boss fight. Some people dislike the final boss for being just a big dude with gun and swords, but it's the arena and grapple that make it work. Disclaimer though: I've only finished it on Warlock solo, on Legend. Not sure if it's as fun with the other 2 classes.

Lightfall's soundtrack is pretty good btw.

Bad Stuff:
It's already predictable at this point but the story is not great. It had flashes of something interesting, especially the setting. What is the Veil? Maybe we'll find out on the next season that I didn't buy anymore. Strand also feels pretty shoe-horned into the narrative, it's just kinda out there as a deus ex machina. There's a training montage which I guess is OK.

Neomuna kinda feels empty outside of Terminal Overload, and that one Vex public event with a thousand adds on screen. Grapple points just disappear when certain events start. I thought this was a play space that encouraged us to use Strand, why take out those grapple points?

I'm probably on the minority on this but have enemies in this game getting spongier? I don't like them to keel over in 1-2 shots but they have really made everything just a little bit too tanky this time around. Doesn't help that you fight Cabal for most of the campaign. Even the patrol zones share this trait, regardless of your current Power.

I really don't like Tormentors as an enemy. They're supposed to be a cool addition but they end up being just another dude that bee-lines to the player to insta-kill them (most of the time). They have weak spots that glow that you're supposed to shoot - which invalidates most splash-damage type weapons. The generic ones are weak to disorienting nades, but the named ones take way too long to die and are immune to being blinded. Certain stages of their health also make them immune to Swords. You'll be kiting at them for just a bit too long for my tastes.

This game engine is aging real hard and fast. Neomuna tanks my system, a system capable of running a lot of games with raytracing enabled at 140Hz or so. This one area beats out some truly amazing-looking benchmark titles. All it takes is for a few Warlocks to cast Needlestorm into a crowd of Vex to completely crash the game to desktop. It's slow, unstable, and a total mess from time to time. Both Radeon and Nvidia seem to be suffering a lot from this madness, so you can't avoid it.

Speaking of game engine, they did fix the physics-related deaths this time 'round. They didn't fix Swords and roaming Supers phasing you through enemies - and usually straight to a cliff, to your death. Certain projectiles will still 1-hit kill you if you're above 60 FPS, which you most likely are on PC.

Conclusion:
I glanced over a bunch of stuff because they're general updates to the game, rather than Lightfall-specific ones. It feels like I hate this expansion but I don't. I grabbed it on sale because I had a few friends still in this unique game, plus Strand is genuinely fun.

D2 is an old title and a lot of its weaknesses are attributed to its aging systems and the looming feeling that Bungie has already moved on from this game. It feels like it on a lot of occasions, especially on this expansion. But it's far from the worst, nor is it a great expansion. It's not a must-grab, nor an instant-avoid. But hey, I heard Lightfall's raid is cool, dunno, my old raid team is incomplete now, so I can't try it.

If Steam gave a mid rating, I would've chosen that. A 5/10 at most. Just don't even think about getting this at full price, it ain't worth that much.\t\t\t\t\t
