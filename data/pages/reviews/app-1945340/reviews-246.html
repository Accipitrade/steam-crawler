\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI genuinely love destiny 2. It breaks my heart to rediscover mediocrity in it. I'll try to keep it spoiler free. For context, I did solo legend campaign.

Don't get me wrong, The gameplay changes are amazing and strand is really fun.
The music might be my favorite from an expansion yet too,and neomuna is quite the eyecandy, in general, good things to say about most aspects not related to story.
Bungie does deliver on their aforementioned vision of a summer 80's blockbuster, with high octane arenas, thrilling set-pieces, sparrow escapes and even blasting through the windows of a building. Grappling with strand has been some of the most fun I have had and as you learn to master the sequence of actions for your class, you can have an amazing uptime on grappling and flying around, even on a Titan! On that note, Strand is pushed to you a lot, so much that it does take a bigger role in the story than what I would like.
Tormentors legit had me screaming "oh ♥♥♥♥ oh ♥♥♥♥",I love them and hope to see them in more places.
In other words, this is a high octane, fun, and a more lighthearted expansion (despite the implications of what is happening in Sol). Fighting with allied units in THAT mission was a huge win and I hope to have more chances to share a battlefield like that. I am sure anyone can have fun in new ways in Destiny.

That said, this was a critical narrative moment in the franchise that could not be handled as it was, I understand Bungie's vision but this execution, in my opinion, was not what we needed in this point in time. The 80's action hero vision CAN work, if there were lulls to actually take in the calamity, or actual insight during (not after) the campaign.
As unfair as it is for the other aspects, this makes me maybe harsher on the review in this specific expansion, again, because of the context of final shape being next and season of the seraph's end, our journey thus far and how Bungie has grown since Witch Queen.

How can we care when we understand nothing of the stakes and consequences; also the disservice to characters with years of buildup, the shallowness of the bonds with new characters... The utter lack of engagement from our vanguard! It felt out of character.

Predictability is not wrong in a story, it often is a sign of good writing. What they do with the cloudstriders though, felt like the safest, most bland decisions that have hurt the current narrative and what could have been; predictable beyond good measures. We did not get a chance to care or explore in a meaningful way the impact these characters can/could have on our older allies. There is more you can learn after the campaign, ( and a MASSIVE development in the strike that I can't believe did not get a peep from the Vanguard at least), but it can be missed by new players and old alike if they take too long to continue the post-campaign activities and it can have less weight to it. That should have happened during the campaign. Make the players and characters process the losses during the campaign, not the lore, not after.

I do not think Nimbus deserves as much negativity as they receive, I agree that they are too "okay" with the massacre on their city, their voice could be a little more clear and the dialogue can be very tone-deaf and take you out of the immersion. Players need to understand they are basically a teen learning the ropes and they do not know conflict as we do, so all this weight is new, too much, too fast. We can slightly see this at the end, but during the campaign they are hard to like. Nimbus needed more time to cook to better understand where they are coming from and grow to like this new cloudstrider, as I think the mayority of people will be put off by Nimbus' aloofness instead of appreciating and understanding them. That said, it's not the first character like this in destiny and it does feel like a tone deaf execution thus far. I still don't enjoy Nimbus too much but post campaign I see where they come from more and strongly believe Bungie will involve them into the narrative in either the next or following seasons or the middle of this one. I hope Nimbus does not stay, as I feel they are, one-note. It feels disrespectful to players and the narrative.

In short, Strong start, a middle with a little of an identity crisis and off-pacing, and an uneven end with great spikes of action (specially if you get the hang of strand by then) but poor storytelling (to what we KNOW Bungie is capable of and has shown in recent seasons and WQ).
It does not feel like the same people made witch queen. This all feels like it could have been a good season instead, to better spend time with characters.

As a mainline drop? An expansion that feels mediocre - and more as a promo to Final Shape-as it is weighed down by what we know Bungie can deliver in narrative, during the most critical turning point of the franchise yet. Despite the plethora of positives that if numbered would be more than the misses, such as all the fun that Strand brings to the sandbox and my favorite Destiny OST... Narrative just could not miss at this moment, it is THAT meaningful in my opinion.
How did we get here? How do we never repeat this?
I am sure the raid will be an amazing experience, but I don't know how the expansion might redeem its story.

TL;DR: I am torn between recommending or not; If you care deeply for the story like me, you might have a better time jumping in down the line as the season progresses (to have more / actual answers) or we dive into next season, season of the Deep, where I believe by the name alone that the story will be moving on more into what we expected. Right now, it feels like something is missing. (I know it is the first week, but remember Witch Queen? Immaculate ending and teasing of what's to come, DURING the campaign)

If you are not too put off by the vision and kind of story Bungie wanted to tell with the expansion or are not too invested in the narrative, I think you will enjoy this a lot , specially Strand, and can recommend it now!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a step down from Witch Queen but it's still a decent expansion. My main gripes are primarily the extremely forced character of Nimbus and the much weaker storytelling this time around.

Nimbus: As a character, they feel particularly forced. The character is totally ignorant to all of the death going on around them. People are dying, worlds are being besieged (including their own world), and Nimbus can't help but be a cheerleader the entire time. Also, Nimbus' voice acting is among the absolute worst I've ever encountered. I'm not sure if it's the voice actor themself or if its the effects that are attached to the voice, but I'd rather listen to nails on a chalkboard. I get that Bungie tried to give the character some kind of hybrid feminine/masculine non-binary voice to represent the character's identity, but it's just awful. Compare that to the Witness, who is presumably a genderless creature, who has amazing voice acting & effects, but also very effective at contributing to the story rather than destroying the mood. Nimbus, as a character, makes me dislike the new location just because I have to hear their voice constantly.

The storytelling: Destiny has a great story. The writing team definitely has some bumps in their track record but I think they've done a good job overall. Honestly, for this expansion to be called Lightfall, is strange. Where does the Light fall? All this story does is set up future events -- it doesn't actually tell a story by itself. Neomuna is cool, I guess, but I think you could've told a better, more personal story if you had simply made the expansion destination the Last City (IE, let us still have the same strand theme in a metropolis but in a location we actually care about). Neptune is a bit of a disappointment but it's fun nonetheless. I just don't feel attached to it, the only characters there are soulless and annoying. But ultimately, the destination itself is fun to travel around and I would say that I significantly enjoy Neomuna more than Savathun's Throne World, as far as exploration and general gameplay goes.

The things I do like:
-Neomuna: Neon cityscape. Much more easy to throw on some music and do events or grind out stuff here than Throne World. Plenty of stuff going on to do.
-Legendary Campaign: Campaign was fun as hell, even if the story wasn't as good as it was last year.

I am a bit hesitant on Strand. It seems like a bit of a gimmick and I don't see how it will be useful in some endgame settings. Hunter and Titan both have roaming supers, which I can't imagine will be too useful in GM's or Raiding. I'm sure they'll have some prospective strategies in PvP. HOWEVER: If they intend on Strand to be fun and useful in all content, the cooldowns need to be drastically reduced. A grappling hook that you can only use once every 45 seconds is pretty ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ useless. Outside of that, Strand is fun to use in general but unfortunately I can't see myself using it if it doesn't get something to buff it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHoo boy what a horrible night to have a curse (of Osiris)

As someone with over 3800 hours in this game i can easily say that my relationship with both games has been a roller coaster over the past 10 years. For me hopping into the wq solo legendary campaign was like biting into a nice juicy ($40) burger that melts into your mouth; its got a nice brioche bun and all the ingredients mesh well together leaving you satisfied and full and wanting to have another. They also followed your order and didn't put pickles on it when you asked for them to be removed.

Lightfall was like biting into a greasy ($60) mcdonalds double cheeseburger which leaves you bloated, gassy and a overall feeling that you should have cooked your own meal at home. Oh and they put pickles on it when you specifically asked for no ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ pickles. Strand should have been half unlocked midway through the campaign leaving you with 1 more aspect to unlock once beating the campaign. The story didn't just take a back seat it got locked in the trunk while on a 2 hour drive. At least Calus got a nice epic boss fight with two phases (should have been 3 imo), leading up to the fight was a weird bland mission with baffling dumb friendly ai which bungie has struggled with constantly outside the heavy frames from season of the worth/ seraph.

You know its bad when the biggest lore nerd Byf can't even understand what the veil is and said exactly what i thought while playing: "What went so Wrong?"

I will be grinding to get raid ready which is the only avenue that bungie never misses on. I just hope they don't do my boy nezerec dirty.

Overall do not buy this unless you get it for free or it goes on sale for like 12 bucks

The Winnower shows up at our doorstep along with its forces to mess up humanity

we explore this oddly silent ship as we follow a hive we'eve never seen before (savathun) drift across this creepy ship in our peripheral vision (very atmospheric horror like)
We come across lord shaxx who has been corrupted by savthuns mind control tricks
we fight him in a actual light bearer vs light bearer fight
we are forced to shoot his ghost severing savathuns mind control magic
we question our actions but we move onward to the winnower
we see the winnower in a more human like form as it tries to empathise with us and offers a bargain
we decline and choose to fight it and it shows us its true form
it pulls off the veil covering its fake form
its begins to morph into something like a mix between a centipede and bbq ribs ( i must have been hungry lol)
Our mind can't comprehend its true 4th dimensional form as we are frozen in fear
