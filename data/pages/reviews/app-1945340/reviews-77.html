\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR. Lightfall is a lot of fun, and there are a lot of good things that came from it. Some things are annoying and had me shaking my head. The only thing that stood out as legitimately bad, though, was the story line. Overall, I still recommend it for any returning or new players.

The Good

These things were done well for Lightfall, and I think they take a step forward for the franchise. Not necessarily perfect, but still good indicators of positive game development.

  • Strand is amazing. It's super powerful, and it's incredibly useful for high-end content. Not to mention, it's one of the most fun subclasses out of the box as well. The movement feels smooth, refined, and fast. Bungie nailed it with strand and I'm excited to see new aspects, fragments, and exotics in the future.
  • Story gameplay and new missions are much more engaging than WQ (not the actual story and lore itself, though). While being forced into strand can get slightly monotonous, I believe the constant fast-paced action was a breath of fresh air compared to the slower pacing and long dialogues presented in many previous expansions. Also, fighting Tormentors is significantly cooler than the light-bearing Hive in my opinion, and they added a perfect touch to the legendary campaign (wish we saw more of them, though). On top of this, the new strike, Partition missions, and "secret" exotic mission are some of the more creative and exciting things I've seen from a major expansion in a while. The one Partition even feels slightly reminiscent of a certain racing league in the original Destiny... (SRL is coming back with Guardian Games, trust me)
  • The raid is probably more beautiful than any other, imo. It did fall slightly short on the difficulty side of raids, though. However, having an easy raid every once in a while adds to the game I believe, as it makes it much more repeatable and not nearly as much of a burden to farm. The mechanics are easy and the bosses don't have much health, but that makes it perfect for new players coming to Destiny 2. On top of this, I'd say that some of my fondest memories probably coming from messing around in easy raids with friends, such as VoG or DSC. The raid exotic is also one of the most exciting ones that's been released in a while, and I'm excited to grind for it. Overall, the raid gets a B or B+ from me. Not the best, but certainly better than many.
  • The quality of life changes are great. Having in-game loadouts (even if they're limited and still feel like a beta), getting elemental boosts on any subclass, removing affinity-specific mods, and not having to equip artifacts mods all feel amazing. It feels like you can build any way you want to now without having to watch YouTube videos to figure out exactly which armor pieces, affinities, and mods you need. Great change overall.

The Bad

These are some of the smaller gripes I have with Lightfall. These are not make-or-break for me, but some annoying things that could've easily been changed should anyone have given some extra thought into them.

  • Strand is super-powered during the campaign, with lightning fast cooldowns and grapple points everywhere to utilize. As a result, when you finally acquire it, it just feels flat-out bad. You can build into it to make it strong, but the first impression post-campaign left a slightly sour taste in my mouth.
  • Weapon designs feel lazy. The Neomuna weapons are just reskinned Moon weapons and the raid weapons look like base models for weapons with weird plastic chunks put on them to make them feel "alien". I've heard that the Neomuna weapons actually are upgraded Moon weapons as stated in the lore (I'm not 100% sure on this, so forgive me if I'm wrong), but that just feels like an excuse rather than something that should actually be part of the game. The weapons themselves perform great, but it's just not as exciting to get a new weapon and think "oh wait I've seen this before".
  • "Bringing challenge back to Destiny" shouldn't have included lost sectors and exploring Neomuna. Lost sectors feel unbearable to farm now. Exotic drops were already abysmal enough, especially since there is not form of focusing which exotic you can get other than the slot it will fill. If you're lucky, you'll be able to get a better roll on an exotic you have within an hour. The other 90% of the time, you'll spend hours on end in some of these excrutiating lost sectors only to never get the exotic you want before quitting. The Neomuna lost sectors are especially painful. Just ran one of them today, thinking I was holding a good pace only to see that it took me 12 minutes to finish, and I didn't get an exotic. If you think I'm doing 12 minute runs on a lost sector for a 1 out of 4 chance to get ANY chest exotic, you're out of your mind. Another thing that was added, for some unknown reason, was a power cap against roaming enemies in Neomuna. I'm not sure how large the gap is, but I'm 1816 power and still seeing sword icons. It doesn't feel good to be roaming around the Vex Incursion Zone and get two-shotted by a hobgoblin headglitching on a platform that's two miles away when you're trying to farm chests and planetary materials. Weird decision Bungie.
  • Removing some of the more niche mods is kind of disappointing. Sometimes for a more specific build you needed to make use of some particular mods to really make them pop. For example, Argent Ordnance, Seeking Wells, and some of the rapid kill mods. It feels like there really was no great reason to get rid of these mods, and it sucks that there was no replacement for them.
  • Commendations and Guardian Rank are weird. While overall, the system was implemented well, it was still buggy. And the entire philosophy of commendations is kind of undermined by the fact that it's set as a requirement for Guardian Rank. In addition to this, Guardian rank is reset every season, so some of these ranks that likely won't even be reachable until close to the end of the season will be only available for a few weeks before all of your effort is wash away. I know some of the achievements carry over, but it's strange that everyone is set back to rank 6 at the beginning of a new season, despite some players solo flawless master dungeons and are 7 times gilded conquerors while others simply... acquired an enhancement prism once before? Maybe an elitist take, but it doesn't feel great, especially for veteran players.

The Ugly

This is REALLY bad. Two steps backwards kind of bad. Legitimately poor design.

  • *SPOILERS* The story and lore is awful. It's been said many times that this is "The Infinity War of Destiny". After completing the campaign, it felt like all we did was play with strand for a few hours and kill a couple baddies in the process. Almost nothing is explained, and every player is left more confused about what happened and what is currently happening than anything we've seen in a long time. Rohan dies mere hours after you meet them in a situation that felt very avoidable in the moment, Nimbus is the "lighthearted comic relief" with annoying dialogue that seems to have the completely opposite effect that Cayde used to have, Calus is built up to be incredibly powerful but just shoots at you when you fight him and chases you with swords like a gladiator when you annoy him enough, and the Traveler is "gone" in another series of events that just felt like they were completely avoidable, yet it's still sitting right there in the sky, only now it now has a Sweet Spicy Chili Dorito on the front of it. Ultimately, it felt like very little was actually at stake, and didn't have nearly the same mic drop that Witch Queen had a year ago. We know Bungie is capable of amazing story telling, but this feels like a story that was written last minute by an intern, and it certainly makes me question Bungie's judgement as we move towards the end of the Light/Dark saga.

This expansion is irredeemably ♥♥♥♥ and I do not recommend it at full price. The main praise I see for this expansion is that the gameplay is great, which is irrelevant as great gameplay was not magically added in Lightfall. Destiny 2 gameplay is great whether you own Lightfall or not. The true merit of an expansion should be what the name implies, how it expands on the already existing game. For what it's worth, strand is a great addition to the Destiny 2 sandbox. Even if I am quite annoyed by the grappling hook CD. It is a blast to shoot and throw tangles, watch threadlings attack enemies, and use all of the fragments and aspects to their full potential. The only new thing of worth is Strand and arguably the raid, so prepare your expectations accordingly if/when you buy this pathetic excuse for an expansion.\t\t\t\t\t
