\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe campaign of Lightfall falls flat on its face in so many ways. The story has a few cool cutscenes, nothing at all gets answered (both old questions and new), the writing for some characters was bad/out of place, and we're left at the end of the campaign even more clueless than when we started. Everything focuses way too hard on Strand, rather than addressing anything revealed to us in the opening cutscene. We learn nothing more of the Traveler or the Witness to any capacity, and it just feels like a waste of time.

Aside from the campaign, the other features introduced like loadouts, commendations, and the armor mod overhaul weren't as disappointing, but I still have some gripes.
- The loadouts overall are pretty fantastic to finally have in game. It also saves your guns to the loadout with no option to remove them, but there's a workaround for this by applying a weapon that you'll later delete (so it cant fill the slot)
- Commendations are a nice way to... commend people... but I feel like the approach is all wrong. You get limited commendation "cards" to give to players, and I feel like that restricts the way in which I want to let players know I appreciated their contribution to the team.
- Armor mods getting simplified into an easier to understand system at first glance, but after messing around with it a bit it feels like buildcrafting got oversimplified into a state that doesn't allow for synergies we used to have (particularly stasis shards RIP). I hope to see more mods introduced or something to address this, because I understand this may be in part to address power creep but regen rates were also adjusted on top of this system, which feels like too harsh of a knockback.

The campaign sucked, but the gameplay itself shows promise and feels pretty solid right now. I hope they continue to build on armor mods and tune Strand as we get more exposure to playing with it, and they really gotta figure out what to do about the story. I don't see a way to redeem this massive fumble, because any explanation as to what's going on at this rate is gonna be too late for it to actually make me care. It'll be more of a "oh finally, *that's* why they did that... okay I guess."

Overall, 4/10. Definitely sub par, story sucks, writing sucks, gameplay needs work but I like the adjustments made to the sandbox so far. Doesn't feel like it's worth $50.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter playing through the campaign I am left thinking... What the hell happened. If I could refund this DLC I would. But unfortunately I played well more than the 2 hour limit to refund. I played in hopes that it would get better. To my great disappointment, it did not. I am left wondering how this happened in the first place. How did the storytelling downgrade from what we saw in the Witch Queen to this?
I have seen lots of complaints in the same negative comments, "rushed", "low effort", "money grab" but for once I cant help but feel the same. I feel cheated. After playing, I genuinely feel that I got robbed of my $50. Where was the story? What was the point of this entire narrative? What the hell is the Veil? No answers. No conclusion. No beginning, middle, end story structure. What we did get was a huge mess.
Tone? Have you heard of a stories tone before? You nailed it with the Witch Queen, and other expansions. This felt like a mess of tone like a thriller writer joined up with a comic writer and pulled the story in two complete opposite directions. The seriousness that you were trying to implement in the story was completely offset with the terrible corny dialogue. Nimbus most of all felt like a copy and paste from the MCU. A franchise who also struggles to get tone right. Adding comedic cues at the worst times breaking all tension or sense of threat.
Strand. What the hell. It was advertised to be this really exciting new subclass. Which luckily still has the potential to be with future patches. But you cant tell me a single dev played the subclass and agreed "These cooldown times offer lots of fun and interesting opportunities for our player base." I mean what the hell...
In conclusion, I tried to keep it spoiler free. To anyone looking if they should spend money on this DLC, think very hard. If you just like Destiny for its gameplay. Sure. It's solid as always. If you want more out of your $50 though (almost the full price of a brand new game) like story, plot, engaging characters, sense of accomplishment. Don't waste your time... Or your money like I did\t\t\t\t\t
