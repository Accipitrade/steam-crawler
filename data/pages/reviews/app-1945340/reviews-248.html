\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor starters, I play destiny a lot.. i mean a lot. There is a lot wrong with this expansion. It is not all bad but definitely a giant step back in terms of what a good expansion should be. It is very confusing how we can go from the narrative marvel of Witch Queen to this..

Pros:
1. Like always, Destiny is a very pretty game aesthetically speaking. I think Neomuna looks incredible.
2. Strand is very fun (I play hunter), it will be interesting to see how it fairs in Harder content such as GMs or Master Raids.
3. Bungie nailed Calus, He looks very cool and in my opinion is the only decent character in the narrative.
4. The cutscenes are really cool even though they do not explain much of what is happening.
5. Aside from the campaign itself, the expansion bring with it many much needed quality of life updates (from loadouts to buildcrafting).
6. The expansion brings with it new weapons (strand weapons as well) and perks, all of which I have very much enjoyed.
7. The exotics I think are incredible this expansion and is something I always look forward to every expansion. The exotic missions are also very fun too.

Cons:
SPOILERS
1. The Narrative: This is a big big problem. The campaign starts off OK, but after the first mission is riddled with confusion and inconsistency. The reason we go to Neomuna is to protect the veil. Sounds interesting and cool, but you soon realize that we have no idea what it is and it is never explained to us. On the contrary, it seems like the Cloud Striders know what it is, as well as Osiris to some extent but none of these characters even explain what it is, why the witness wants it, and why it is so crucial that we get to it before Calus. What is (I assume) supposed to be the most high stakes campaign yet, does not feel like it is at all because we have no idea what we are doing. I am a lore nerd, i dont know everything, but I know a lot about Destiny lore, and I cant even explain anything. After the marvel that was the Witch Queen narrative it is such a let down that the Lightfall narrative falls completely flat. By the end of the campaign we fail to prevent some connection between the veil and the Traveler (again, we do not know what the consequences of this are), and the Witness opens a portal in front/on the Traveler and presumably is now somewhere else and has taken the Traveler with him. Something big has just happened but because NOTHING is explained, it feels like throughout the entire story we managed to do absolutely nothing, and we are no closer to answers than we were throughout the year of Witch Queen. If you are hoping for a similar narrative experience that you had with Witch Queen, you will be disappointed.

2. CloudStriders: They had the potential to be really cool, but there is little to no character development for either Rohan or Nimbus. Rohan especially could have been a really neat character. He is the older of the Cloudstriders and is sort of in charge between the two of them. Nimbus is just annoying and causes these confusing tonal shifts within the campaign. From what we gather the Witness is about to bring about the 2nd collapse and he just does stupid one liner jokes the entire time. What bothers me the most is that the death of Rohan was so obvious that it was going to happen and because we know literally nothing about him, theres no character development, his death is just stupid and hold little weight if any at all. To hammer this point down even further, soon after his death, Nimbus is still cracking annoying one liners.

3. Osiris is very annoying in this expansion, just as annoying as Nimbus. Most of what he does seems so out of character for him. What's more is that it seems like he has decent knowledge of what is happening, but never decides to tell us or queue us in on anything. It is like he sort of panics and goes off on his own and is insufferable to deal with.

4. Empty Planet: Neomuna looks really cool, but its kinda empty. To be fair, it is explained that the citizens of neomuna are alive, but have been "uploaded to the cloud", yes, quite literally. So we see these holograms standing around that are virtual citizens of Neomuna. I can see how this could have been a cool idea, but it makes the world feel so empty, and there is not an urgency to really save anyone.

Last thoughts, as someone who loves Destiny, I have struggled to convince people to pick up the game for as long as I can remember. The number 1 complaint I hear from people who refuse to stay with the game is because they are confused and have no idea where to start. Lightfall broke the consecutive number of players record for the game, and unfortunately, I am willing to bet most of those players will be gone within a month. Bungie with Witch Queen proved that they can create a multi-layered narrative rich with lore and excitement, and it really sucks that they were unable to deliver with Lightfall. To me, it seems like Lightfall was a sort of stop gap between Witch Queen and the Final Shape. A way for Bungie to spend more time working on the expansion that will finally unravel all of the questions and mysteries that have plagued the community for years. One can only hope. If you are looking for a good story, Lightfall is not it. As someone who loves the game, and has tried to get others to play it, I find it very difficult to recommend the Lightfall Expansion to new players.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is true that the Story of Lightfall was not as great as we wished it would be. The beginning of Lightfall was great. The opening cut scene set the stage for something we thought that was going to be the craziest moment in Destiny. In the end in turned into a training arc of learning a new subclass.

Starting with Neomuna
It looks great. The atmosphere is nice and it is clear that this space was meant to be used with Strand. There are multiple grapple points throughout the map where players can grapple around infinitely. Design team had fun here and its going to be fun to traverse.

Strand
I got the Hunter strand class and I enjoyed flying around Neomuna with the grapple hook. The super is also fun to use and it really brings out the mobility in hunters with the smooth animations and controls. While I do believe it is a fun super and ability to move around, my one concern is how it will work in end game content. It seems that (for Hunters) the only way to stay alive is to keep moving and get woven nail and there is not a way to sustain yourself on this class. Will have to play more to test this but first impressions have me thinking this way.

Story
The story of Lightfall did not hit the mark like it did with Witch Queen and in my opinion I feel Strand was part of the problem. Witch Queen did not have to deal with introducing a new subclass so they were able to branch out on the Witch Queen story and discuss what Savathun's plan was. The Lightfall story could be summarized like this:
Great Witness and Traveler Moment
Landing on Neptune
Finding Strand
Cannot use other abilities because of Cabal/Witness Tech
Strand is unaffected so we got to use strand
Fail at using Strand... A lot
O by the way the people of Neptune and the Veil are important you should protect that
Strand Training Arc... Im not kidding this happened
Final Boss
Witness cliffhanger, see you next year
The End
It is sorry to see that this was how the campaign was presented and I am hoping in The Final Shape that there is no new subclass and the campaign team gets to focus on the story.

Level Design/Enemies
The Tormentors were a great edition to this and they kept the difficulty up.
The boss fights were hard but not hard enough to be impossible and the new mechanics incorporating Strand were great.
There are no champions again... thank you keep doing this
Rewards were great throughout the campaign and upgrade modules were plentiful and the progression was smooth, went to the next mission on legendary with minimal extra grinding.

Quality of life changes are nice to see and the new build system is easier and builds can vary more.

Why I am giving it a Positive Review
I have been playing Destiny for a while, so naturally I will have biased towards anything that is Destiny. The story is not the only thing in Destiny... it also is not the worst story in the world; it just did not live up to Witch Queen and it felt rushed because of the new subclass. There are more things to do, new activities to grind, new quests to finish, and a new raid on March 10th.
Bungie keep making things fun and make The Final Shape a time to remember.\t\t\t\t\t
