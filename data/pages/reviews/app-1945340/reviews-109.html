\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2: Lightfall is, in almost all respects, a fantastic experience. I say almost, because its story is one of the worst Destiny main campaign stories in a long long time. I haven't felt this disappointed with a Destiny story or characters since Curse of Osiris.

I need to clarify one thing here before I get into the review; Yes, the story is a small part of the expansion, it is the first 4-8 hours depending on player skill and campaign difficulty. But first impressions really matter, and my first impression of Lightfall was "That was it? That didn't explain ANYTHING!"

But after playing for a week, trying out Strand, playing the post campaign quests, unlocking the new exotics, playing the seasonal activity, and toying around with multiple Strand builds on both Hunter and Warlock, I can safely say that in almost all regards aside from the story? Bungie nailed it.

Now, I can't speak for PvP, because I avoid Crucible like the plague, but in all the content I've played, the game has felt phenomenal. The balance changes they added to bring difficulty back have been nice, the almost complete removal of Match Game in hard content was a fantastic decision, and some new additions like the Quicksilver Storm catalyst actually had me using an exotic primary (Though Quicksilver Storm is currently bugged and dealing 40% less damage to red bars)

While the story is lacking, and the characters, or rather one character in particular, kill the tone, the actual missions themselves were almost all great! Bungie said in marketing that they wanted this expansion's missions to feel like you were the hero in an 80's action movie, and the campaign hits those notes perfectly.

However, as someone who cares equally about content and story, Bungie has lost my trust. I'm on the copium train of "Lightfall's original story got shifted to The Final Shape and current Lightfall is just a filler expansion" but we won't know if that's true until The Final Shape releases next year. Bungie are already promising that the coming seasons will answer the questions that the story failed to answer, but I fear that the damage has already been done.

If you're a veteran Destiny player, you likely already own Lightfall. If you're a New Light, start at Shadowkeep. If you bought Witch Queen and are on the fence about buying Lightfall, I genuinely don't know if I can recommend it. I've marked this review as a positive review, because I have enjoyed every minute of my time playing Lightfall after the campaign, and after they removed the time gate on the Strand Fragments.

In short, Lightfall from a sandbox, subclass, and gameplay standpoint, has been some of the most fun I've had in D2 for a while now. But its story falls flat in the worst kind of way, at the worst possible time for it to do so, and kicking off the year with such a failure kind of kills the momentum going forward.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR - you want to get into the game at this point? Tough luck, but if so, just get Beyond Light for Stasis and Witch qeen for 30$ and stay away from LightFail, not worth your time or money.

Disappointing honestly on many levels, especially after Witch Queen.
Campaign was... ok, gameplay-wise, but the sections where you are forced to use Strand (which is severely undercooked, at least for Hunter) were an absolute chore that drained any fun out of it. The story was pretty bad (What is the Veil for crying outloud?!). The characters... Rohan was cool, too bad we get so little of him. Nimbus is a walking personification of gender politics, especially in his design and his attitude is tone-deaf and full of Marvel-esque quips and puns in the "this is the end of the world" scenario. Entire campaign is padding shoved in the middle of what is essentially Final Shape intro cutscene. Hell, even Zavala and Ikora reactions after campaign are "Oh well, another day in the city, eh Guardian?"

Just an overall letdown. Did the writing team leave this to all one intern with no writing experience?\t\t\t\t\t
