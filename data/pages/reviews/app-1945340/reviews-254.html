The story is a travesty, everything else is mediocre


the most unlikable, unimaginative and cringy characters in a fifty dollar package. the story is a joke, they've been building up to this moment in the story for nearly a decade and somehow managed to f up every possible scenario and deliver one of the worst story lines in the history of the game. i could go forever about the details but i want to keep this review spoiler-free.
this should not be the narrative for a major expansion, they could've easily told this story as part of a season. the only interesting character in the whole story line is the witness and honestly that's it, every other character is either a cringy cyborg or a lifeless blob.
if you care about destiny's story do yourself a favor and skip this one.
and if you don't care about the story, the new weapons are fun, strand is creative and fresh, neomuna looks cool, the new quality of life updates are well implemented and easy to use.
all & all a 5/10

Except absolutely none of that happened.
(This is where I would put a spoiler alert, if there was anything that developed that could actually be learned from, or someone could find "spoiled"... sadly, you're going to know about as much going into Lightfall as you do coming out the other side)
Instead what we got was a really drawn out, McGuffin chase, which, is never explained as to what exactly it is, what it does, how it works, hell, why we're even so hell bent on obtaining it. The Veil just appears, something with little to no narrative exploration, it's just something that we know our enemy is going after, to do something, that will be bad, so we must get it, in order to do something, or stop something... the really annoying thing is everyone else just kind of acts like this is all common knowledge and that they're well versed in the entire subject, and acts like we are as well, even though this is one of the only times it's even been mentioned in the entire 8 years of Destiny's existence.
I know everyone thinks that Neomuna and The Cloud Striders are just the best thing ever, but honestly, I dig Neomuna, it's pretty cool, but these "Silver Surfer", carboard cut out, who gives a ♥♥♥♥ really, characters feel really out of place and out of sorts with the narrative so far. It's like someone at Bungie said, "let's take an all ready super convoluted narrative, with dozens of repcons and characters of mention who our ideas for eventually ran out, so they're just kind of there now... anyways, let's add some new characters, but make them really ridiculous, from their looks, to their speech.... oh yeah, and let's make them as similar to The Silver Surfer as we possibly can without getting hit with a lawsuit." I just don't understand how we went from Witch Queen back to something that hit home about like Curse of Osiris.
Game play wise, the reworking of some of the older strikes and other older content, the new Guardian Rank system, the new mod system... good systems and I like them all. Game play wise the game is maybe more solid than ever. The Commendation system is kind of stupid... as it stands in a strike I can give one person a card saying they were fun and the other one saying they were helpful, not 2 of the same card to both, so most the time I'm just trying to remember which one of my random fire team members danced that time we were one the elevator or something similar, so I can give them the fun one and the other by default gets the helpful one. Seems not a lot of thought (like the narrative) was put into that whole idea.
I don't know... this seems like a pretty common issue with Bungie... set things up for a knock out grand slam, literally just follow through with what they've lined out, instead they straight shoot off into Silver Surfer fantasy bs that helps nothings, complicates a lot, and leave me pissed off and kind of hopeless.
The only reason I am recommending this game is because if you have no investment, like 8 years or so like some of us poor bastards, this would be a great place to start getting kicked around by Bungie. The core game play is good if you like looter shooters, just don't expect anything much in the area of effective story telling... or logic... leave that with the other expectations you may have because if Bungie is good at one thing, its ruining expectations.
