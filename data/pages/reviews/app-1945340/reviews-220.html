\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played Destiny since the first game, and I've followed it to some extent since then. Gotten invested in the world, played through each and every DLC that the game had to offer. It was no different for Destiny 2, either. The first game wasn't great, but kept me occupied when I was younger, and for what it was worth, I enjoyed it. For awhile, I enjoyed D2, but eventually dropped it. Recently, I have found games less enjoyable, but I have had some more fun times when I picked this game up again. I was actively looking out for Lightfall-the content provided in the trailers and basically everything else surrounding the expansion actually gave me a sense of excitement. This was going to be the pre-cursor to the end of a story I have been following for almost 10 years. It has stumbled(a lot) in that time, but recently, I started to think it was getting better.

This made me doubt things. A lot.

For starters, the new stuff we have been given so far (this was written before the raid drop or whatever else may come out for this damn thing) has felt-to me-like very little. New subclass with strand, and a new location that had been built up quite a bit within the story itself. I'll touch up on that mess later. The subclass (strand) looked pretty fun, and maybe had the potential to shake up the gameplay loop quite a bit. The grapple hook is fun for sure, I already really enjoy movement mechanics in games, but everything else fell flat. Strand was hyped up to a fault, probably to the detriment of the whole experience. Moving on to the gameplay changes, some things were good to have, like presets for different loadouts or however else you'd like to put it. The upgrade modules were also reworked, alongside some other general quality of life changes-but that's all I can really say was "great" about this whole update.

The new location (Neomuna) was just as underwhelming as everything else, if not moreso. We were drawn in with the bright lights, a cityscape that appeared larger than those we had seen before, and a giant spaceship with the villain's head smacked right on the front of it looming over the horizon for us to wreck later on. But, what we got was a rather small area, with lots of places closed off by high walls of buildings or with large gaps for you to fall to your death. The place feels lifeless and empty, despite its good looks and buildup. Of course, it has its own patrols, lost sectors and a few public events sprinkled in for good measure, but those don't really measure up to anything. The most engagement I can say I got from the campaign was from the beginning of the first mission, which didn't even take place in the city itself.

Speaking of the campaign, all I can say is it is probably the least engaging of all the ones I have played so far. I wouldn't recommend playing it on Legend unless you're a masochist that likes the idea of being castrated or shot before you SO shatters your jaw. The final mission is a pain in the ass, and the added difficulty in the mode feels very artificial. Almost anything one shots you, no matter how high your resilience is, and the campaign itself is not lacking things that shoot at you. If you're a newcomer, do not even think about touching the legend difficulty for the campaign. Might be a good idea to not touch the campaign itself.

Next, I'm going to go on about some nerdy ♥♥♥♥ that most of you probably don't care about. Spoilers for basically the entire thing ahead. Read if you want to.

The story is the most disappointing thing about Lightfall for me. I am an artistic guy-I like creating and hearing stories, and it isn't too often you get to experience good ones, but the optimism that Witch Queen gave me was misguided. My standards aren't that high-a story doesn't have to be a mindblowing, bestselling, postmodern and expectation subverting masterpiece for me to enjoy it. Setting expectations too high leaves you wide open for disappointment-and that applies to all things. Regarding the story itself, we start off with The Witness trying to do something to the Traveler-to take it, destroy it, we don't know. But, in order to go through with its plan, The Witness needs the assistance of something called The Veil, which was revealed to be on Neptune in the city of Neomuna. So, you hitch a ride on a cabal ship, clear through the joint after encountering a Tormenter (the only new enemy introduced in the campaign), and launch out of a Cabal pod into the city. You get to use strand for the first time, and you meet up with two cloudstriders, Nimbus and Rohan, who will be important to the campaign. Nimbus is the young one full of energy-not too bright, but competent in ability. They're a relatively fun character, being witty in some aspects, but that's all that's really interesting about their character. Rohan is the older one, a disgruntled veteran who is trying to show Nimbus the ropes.

These two will help you on your quest to stop Calus, who has been given new power by The Witness. He is sent to acquire The Veil, but due to some new machine thing that his army is using, you cannot use the light around it. This pushes for us to learn more about Strand, and I ♥♥♥♥ you not, Strand is a bigger plot point than the actual conflict. Most of the things you have to do in this campaign revolve around it, and because of that, basically everything else suffers. The characters don't get much fleshing out, so we don't grow attached to them. The plot has built up Calus for years, starting with the original D2 and with The Leviathan raid, but he gets very little fleshing out here as well-and he is the main antagonist. The total cutscenes in this expansion are around 20 minutes-combined. 20 minutes to set up our story, our new characters, our antagonist, and whatever else. And oh, don't bother getting attached to Rohan, he dies before the final mission. It barely impacts Nimbus' character at all, and is generally inconsequential to the plot.

After getting a better hold of strand, you go to fight Calus with the assistance of Caiatl (who is also present during the story, but barely) and her army, to prevent the emperor from getting The Veil. She gets little interaction with Calus himself-who is her father, mind you-and what interaction they do have feels like basically nothing. No development on their familial bond-or their past familial bond-whenever they talk with each other. Another absolute waste of potential. You go deep underground in some type of facility after you fight through Calus' Shadow Legion, and eventually come face to face with Calus himself. His fight is very underwhelming, and so is his demise. Unless you fight him on legend, in which case, you are about to waste many minutes or hours of your life you're never getting back, lmao. Worst fight in the game to me. Moving on, after you kill him, your ghost is being pulled toward The Veil, being possessed by The Witness and establishing as a sort of conduit for The Witness to access The Veil. After Nimbus snaps Ghost back to his senses, it's already too late.

A cutscene plays, and we see The Witness cut a giant triangle into the traveler before going inside. We have no idea what any of this ♥♥♥♥ means, or what The Veil was even used for-or why it was even necessary-but with the epilogue of this whole thing, the point is pretty clear. You lose. And it all feels absolutely weightless. I'm fine with bad endings, but with how this entire campaign was hyped up to be the precursor to the end of this 10 year old story, I would've expected more. At the least, more cohesion and development, but what we got was an absolute disappointment. And I think that's worse than saying it was bad, because there is some fun to be had in making fun of ♥♥♥♥ stories. But something like this, for me, is much worse.

Bungie needs to get their ♥♥♥♥ together. This isn't ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, it's close to nothing. And you can get it for just 50 bucks. Don't waste your time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI mean, if you're already hooked on Destiny 2, you don't really have a choice, because lots of the bad parts of this expansion are changes to the sandbox as a whole.

The new campaign has fairly fun missions, a few can be a bit painful but it's not bad. However, the writing is atrocious and the integration of Strand into the story is very lacking. Honestly, I don't really care too much about this, though there were some design choices I found very questionable at times.

As for Strand, it's... ok. It has some real potential to be incredibly cool, but has some serious flaws and even more questionable design choices. Why is the grapple cooldown so long for an ability that is a detriment to use in difficult content? It's fun and helps with traversing maps, but there is no reason to ever use it over a normal grenade outside of being fun to swing around. No cooldown when grappling to a tangle helps, but there's many cases where that isn't an option. Depending on which class you play, Strand will either be very interactive and open up new options, or be fairly flat.

The mod changes are where I am most floored. While I am happy for the removal of armor element types, the reworked mods are a huge step back for build crafting. There are hardly any new options, and many many old ones that were completely gutted or outright removed. Many things that should be mods or fragments are also seasonal perks. Build crafting has taken a major hit for the worse with Lightfall, and it just feels less dynamic and interesting compared to Witch Queen.

The new UI changes are a mixed bag. Guardian rank is meaningless, commendations are silly but also ultimately meaningless.

At the end of the day, it's still Destiny 2. But it seems like somehow they have lost sight about what people actually want from the game, and this expansion's direction has taken the game's feel on a turn for the worse.\t\t\t\t\t
