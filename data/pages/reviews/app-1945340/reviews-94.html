\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis DLC was a failure. Bungie got the basics wrong in telling a story and just did not hit the marks for a AAA game title.

The lack of information on what it is you are trying to obtain, the consequence of losing and many wasted narrative opportunities with already known and new characters creates this lack of emotion between yourself as the player and the situation which you find yourself in. Due to this lack of emotion, you can't understand the magnitude of this story - you simply do not care about whats happening. And even at the end, when we see, but don't understand, the consequence, the narrative still does not answer those questions.

I understand that the years 2020 and 2021 impacted Bungie as well as everyone else, so it was clear they needed more effort in developing Witch Queen which would release in those years. This was evident and Witch Queen was a great success. However, it looks like this left Lightfall behind, under developed and behind scheduled. And instead of owning up and maybe delaying so its the same quality, they rushed it to get it out on time.

They even halved this DLC - originally Lightfall was going to have the Final Shape narrative in it as well however they completely cut it in half as it was not ready. Fair enough, but what they should have made sure was that Lightfall could uphold itself with the narrative it deserves and the questions we ask. It did not. I'd imagine that the answers we are looking for are in Final Shape, however that's at least another year.

The game play was good. A range of missions to complete, but the one thing that I was not a fan of was that the missions pushed you to use the new subclass Strand. The lack of being able to customise your approach to the challenges you faced made for a frustrating experience, leaving a sour taste about Strand. There was only one way to complete this story, unlike the sandbox which Destiny 2 is where you can use whatever, wherever and whenever.

This failure to captivate the player with an interesting and creative story creates a huge disappointment. In my opinion, for the 8 years of playing and the 2500+ hours on the destiny franchise across many platforms, the ending of this campaign was the most disappointed I have ever been. And when all eyes were on Destiny 2 for the ending of the "Light and Darkness Trilogy" and the first encounter towards an overpowering enemy we have been teased with for 5 years, Bungie fumbled ... fumbled hard.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBiggest nothing burger of the entire series... Literally Shadowkeep did it better and they didn't have a multi-billion dollar company backing them up.

Ironically, the Lightfall weapons are the Shadowkeep weapons reskinned. I didn't mind it since I thought it was related to the exodus program and the technology related to it, but nothing, literally nothing is explained at all.

As a D1 vanilla player until now, I can't help but feel gipped out of $100 for what seems like a heavily recycled game at this point.

I do not recommend this game at all. Just save your money or get something better.\t\t\t\t\t
