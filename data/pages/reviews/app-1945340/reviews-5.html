\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a review made for active destiny players. If you're not one of those, the short answer is no, don't buy this.

Lightfall is the latest and greatest entry into bungie's "don't overdeliver" policy and the last one we'll see in this saga of destiny (hopefully). Now, it isn't all bad, and we'll delve into that, but first I want to clear up something. Bungie is not stupid, they aren't deaf to player feedback, and they know exactly what you've been asking for. They take their time adding things for the genuine reason that it would raise expectations too much, and if you want proof of this, they gave a presentation at the game developer's conference (GDC) that you can find online, give it a watch.

Lightfall is a dlc that actively punishes you for paying attention. I'm not going to bother with spoiler warnings because nothing I talk about is explained in the dlc anyway. The first half of this campaign focuses on the radial mast. What is the radial mast? No clue, it's never brought up, only that it is bad and could destroy everything. Then you shift your focus back to the big bad at hand; The veil. What's the veil? Well, the witness, who you also don't know about, can use it to open up the traveler and do...something. Yeah, that's the full explanation by the end of the game. The campaign itself is just a tutorial for the new subclass, strand, where they take a full 12 seconds out of every mission to show you how to throw a grenade or make a jump and then you don't get to use it again because you're "not strong enough".

Strand itself is a pretty cool subclass but much like its brother stasis, struggles to find its place in the sandbox. Unlike stasis however, strand does have some applications, but to unlock more of it there's no objectives or anything. You just have to use strand. A LOT. What's interesting about strand however is just how much better it fits into witch queen than lightfall, even down the names like "broodweaver" and "threadrunner" both of which sound very hivelike. Strand fits perfectly into the deepsight sections during the witch queen campaign, and it all even has the same color scheme as the whole dlc.

The new subclass is not unlocked for you through the entire campaign solely because you're "not strong enough" but this campaign exists almost solely for you to learn how to use it. Storywise, this leaves you with...well, nothing. You go after targets that have no other explanations to them except that they're "big and evil and have to be stopped". Right before the final mission, you get a cutscene of a training montage for strand and now suddenly you're an expert in the matter. That is an incredibly frustrating method of storytelling and I can't put into words how upset this made me.

Just like they always do, the atmosphere on Neomuna, the new planet, is gorgeous and bungie's art team does a phenomenal job. The characters though...not so much. I don't even know what the cloudstriders are supposed to resemble, but they look very out of place. The entire planet is in contest mode at all times, meaning you're always underleveled for the enemies, which I find very funny after bungie developers' criticisms of blizzard for doing something very similar. There's no new race or anything, just two guys, one of which has about three whole lines in the entire story, one of which is him dying. The other, is Nimbus. Nimbus is about 50% of the reason people hate this dlc out the gate. He tries way too hard to be funny with very childish attempts at humor and never takes anything at all seriously until AFTER THE STORY (he tries to fist bump caiatl in victory mere minutes after killing her father. I didn't make that up).

And finally, the gameplay. Well, let me just run you through the exotic quest you receive after completing the story.
1. Pick up 5 planetary materials
2. Interact with conflux and kill stuff (not defend it)
3. Walk through big ass area that was part of the campaign and listen to nimbus and osiris talk
4. Complete a lost sector (which, admittedly, is a cool lost sector!)
5. Kill 100 enemies in one portion of the map
6. Walk through other giant area from the campaign and pick up item
7. Go back to the vendor area on the polar opposite end of the map via fast travel and listen to nimbus talk
8. Walk BACK to the end of that first big ass area from the campaign
9. Play through an actually unique mission in the garden of salvation raid area during which nimbus casually mentions that steps 3-8 were completely unnecessary
10. Listen to nimbus talk
11. Watch a cutscene where nimbus and osiris talk
If that sounds like fun and and engaging gameplay to you, then even still don't buy lightfall as this was singlehandedly the most interesting quest in the entire dlc to me and I logged off immediately after to write this review.
Now the guns are cool! The exotics are interesting, at least for the most part, but there just isn't very many of them and they don't fit very well into the sandbox. Getting most of them is a chore, though this is nothing new to destiny.

The raid? The raid is pretty cool! Uses some unique mechanics...or rather, one unique mechanic and then the third encounter which has nothing to do with the rest of the raid. The scenery is interesting, the boss is cool, and the gear is good. Not many complaints there, just wish the rest of the dlc was like this. The dungeon which is part of the deluxe edition is also cool, but it is very short and is extremely drawn out by giant sections of just walking and bosses that unironically have more health than any enemy or raid boss in the game. I'll update this to include the other dungeon when it releases.

Overall, much like this review, Lightfall is a very poorly paced and strewn out piece of story that clearly was not meant to exist in the first place. Bungie forgot they needed to make two dlcs after witch queen, which is evident by the shift in tone of lightfall to the synthwave-futuristic style it's in now. They made a quick, non-elaborated story so that it won't make any holes in the final shape story and bundled a subclass from a different dlc along with it. Destiny is a roller coaster of high points and low points, and it's designed that way. This is a low point, much like destiny 2's launch, and much like shadowkeep or even house of wolves if you remember that. It'll be followed by a high point, and I'd expect final shape to be at least as good as witch queen, but we're not here to talk about that. Don't buy lightfall. 4/10 for destiny standards, 1.5/10 for dlcs overall.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't say much that hasn't already been said. Bungie dropped the ball and let us down, plain and simple. As someone who paid $100 for the expansion and the following seasons, I feel cheated out of my money moreso than ever before by these devs.

The expansion left me with more questions than answers. While having a new (admittedly fun) subclass to ooh and ah over, Bungie doesn't seem to know what they're doing with their game. The single most important cutscene in the history of the franchise was locked behind week 6 of the season AFTER the expansion initially released. We spent the expansion chasing a Macguffin that we learned nothing about, other than it's connected (hastily and in a massive contradiction) to a semi-important faction from Destiny 1. They killed off a character that we didn't get the time to care about less than 3 hours of gameplay after his introduction, only for his underling to play it off as "eh, we all die at some point." Bungie, I'm talking directly to you here. Nimbus' attitude was a mistake. Making Nimbus the focus and main character of the expansion was a mistake. You had Calus' daughter RIGHT THERE and all she can do is show up to save our asses when we fail to take out the Radial Macguffin?

Along with the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ story, Bungie added next to no new content to the core playlists. They added a new raid, which happened to be the easiest day 1 raid in the history of the game (we're talking more clears than all the other day 1 raids combined) since the only way they can think of to artificially inflate difficulty anymore is to crank up enemy damage resistance and sprinkle in some Champions. They also added a new strike, that being ripped from one of the more pointless missions of the expansion.

Oh yeah I forgot to mention my main gripe with the expansion's story.

It progresses the same way it would have if we had never interfered in the first place. Go and watch the cutscenes from the beginning and end of the campaign, you can stitch them together so they flow flawlessly from one to the other. We lost by interfering in the same manner as we would have lost by sitting on our hands in the HELM the whole time. In the end, none of it mattered. All 5-8 hours (depending on difficulty) of the expansion's story results in nothing you accomplished during having mattered. Strand. Osiris. Rohan. Nimbus. Calus. None of it mattered.

this is a warning sign for Bungie to bring their A game in The Final Shape, we'll be watching.\t\t\t\t\t
