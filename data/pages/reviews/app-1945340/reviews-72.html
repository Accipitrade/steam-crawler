\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: not as awful as people are saying it is, but it is absolutely not worth the price that they are asking for. wait for a sale or get it on a key site. it feels like a filler dlc while they're banking HEAVILY on final shape next year.

story has some cool set pieces but the plot is pathetically bad and doesn't tell you anything, getting this after witch queen just makes me wonder what the hell happened at bungie. the whole give and take of strand during the campaign was quite annoying and the campaign felt more like a strand tutorial with insufferable writing than something to progress the plot we actually want to progress. and bungie saying 'oh we're gonna explain everything and more about the veil and the witness throughout the year. this is only the beginning of lightfall' is such a copout excuse. like yeah sure seasonal storytelling is fine but they could at least explain things in the MAIN CAMPAIGN of a YEARLY EXPANSION that costs 75 AUSTRALIAN DOLLARS AT BASE.

strand itself is really fun and the best addition to the sandbox in a long time. doesn't feel op in pvp and in pve is stupidly fun crowd control, very good build potential that will only get better as the year goes on with more weapons and aspects. post-campaign stuff is alright, just standard destiny affairs honestly.

neomuna is okay, don't really have much thoughts about it honestly. its just another destiny destination.

not gonna bother talking about pvp or gambit since those continue to be left to fester pretty much.

the raid (on day 1 contest specifically) was really easy in comparison to past raids, the combat challenge wasn't really there and the mechanics are so braindead that you only need 1 or 2 people doing them with most of the team on ad clear. i still like it cause you can just turn your brain off but i like that for AFTER contest is over. when contest is on i WANT to have my balls ripped off and forcefed into my mouth, but damage checks were non-existent as the current meta makes everything a joke and bosses shipped with so little health. however in terms of looks and sound the raid is easily the best looking one.

is lightfall awful? no. it has redeeming qualities and the game still feels fun to PLAY.
is lightfall a huge disappointment coming right off the heels of witch queen? yeah, definitely.
am i worried about how bungie is going to stick the landing for final shape? their LITERAL FINAL CHAPTER OF THE EPIC LIGHT AND DARK SAGA? yeah, i'm very concerned. i've been with this game since i was 16 years old, i'm 24 now. don't make the finale a flop bungie, you will quite literally NEVER recover if final shape bombs.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tCan you imagine spending 8 years building a story with lore so strong it developed a cult-like following on multiple media platforms, only to completely fumble the one opportunity you have to finally introduce the big baddie and cement how serious the conflict is?

How about telling people that the real story-telling will be in the paid content that you'll be rolling out over the course of the next two years?

How about reaming both new and old players with aggressively increased difficulty because you felt that players 'just weren't challenged enough' with your already shamelessly grindy light-level system?

It's always 2 ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ steps forward, 4 back with this company.

They introduced a great new subclass, but made it a giant pain in the ass to get.
They introduced a neatly packaged and refined modding system, but you're forced to play -only- the most meta builds to be useful in any of the more difficult content unless you spend -HOURS- grinding your light level.
They finally bring out one of the most interesting villains in fiction, only to give you whiplash with some of the most jarringly bad pacing I've ever experienced in a narrative.

Let's talk about the Cloud Striders. Both characters with potential - not that you'll ever find out. They go from 0-60 with zero exposition until one of them slams into a canned death while the other continues acting like a surfer meme. There's no character growth. There's no time for the player to understand them, let alone nurture a relationship with them.

It's never expanded that perhaps Nimbus is the way he is because he's only got 10 years to live due to his enhancements. Can you imagine the physiological ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ that would have on a pysche? You become a super human, but you sacrifice everything to keep your home safe.

That is a potential goldmine of character growth wasted on poor story-telling.

And let's look at Osiris - instead of the slow burn watching him become more and more crippled by the anxiety of losing his light and torture by the hands of the literal Queen of Deceit and Lies, we're forced to deal with a grumpy old man who's motivation gets completely derailed as soon as a new toy comes his way (strand).

I'll be honest, I'm only still here because of the magic of the Destiny 1 alpha. I felt something playing that I hadn't felt since I was a little kid. At this point, they've got one last opportunity to keep me around, and that's Final Shape. If that flops, I'm done. But I will say this - no more preordering. They don't ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ deserve it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's alright honestly, but Lightfall really made Destiny 2's age apparent. It doesn't look or feel like an expansion for a triple A game should in 2023. Neomuna as a location is really empty and barren. The environment looks flat and uninteresting with very little detail added to the city's buildings. Add to that a total lack of citizens which is hand-waved by the *Cloud Ark* and you have a location that feels totally unfinished. The worst offense, however, are the invisible barriers that prevent you from web-swinging onto rooftops with strand. This location that was touted as being designed around strand's grapple mechanic, and instead we get bounced off rooftops because the game designers didn't intend for you to go there.

Unfortunately the campaign is about as well developed as Neomuna. You'll fight the same enemies you've fought thousands of times over the course of 8 missions. Every so often the new "tormentor' enemy will show up and chase you around while you backpedal and shoot it's weak spots. You'll be gifted with strand-type gameplay often, but it actually makes the game more difficult because your normal class is likely better tuned and more useful to you. Legendary difficulty is here too, but it just gives the enemies fatter health bars and more damage. Overall it I only enjoyed the final 2 missions which had some interested set pieces and fights. The rest of the missions were forgettable. The story made no sense, but destiny's story has never made sense so it really didn't bother me as much as others.

Once you beat the campaign you are finally given full access to strand, which is the main gameplay addition that you will likely purchase the expansion for. It's OK. It's fun to swing around and you can setup some decent synergy once you spend 200 hours unlocking all the fragments. Some things are bugged, but they did a good job balancing this class compared to how stasis was when it released.

The final nail in the coffin is the light level mechanics. Light level has been around forever; not much has changed about how it works which is exactly the problem here. You buy the expansion and are hyped to jump into the new content and BAM: sorry guardian you can't do that yet, your light level is too low! Every enemy here will kill you instantly. So go do some old strikes you've done a million times already until you are ready for this content. There's a whisper thin amount of new content, so they have to stretch is out by forcing you to grind old content in between each drop of new stuff. It's an exhausting formula at this point and has me asking myself if the new content is even worth wasting so much time in between.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally wanted to like this DLC despite the extreme negative backlash, but this DLC is assuredly a massive disappointment.

- Neomuna is a neat concept for a patrol zone with interesting visual design, art direction, and and storytelling, but feels like it was barely designed for Strand. There are invisible walls on rooftops everywhere -- even where it looks like you could go. There is very little, if any sense of verticality as we were led to believe.

- Strand is, once again, a very cool idea and has its' moments where it feels amazing to use. But, this immediately ends after the campaign and Bungie suddenly gets realistic with how often you'll be getting to use your abilities. Your grapple is a 1:45 minute cooldown ability that replaces your grenade, ultimately removing your base crowd control capabilities in exchange for added mobility that you'll get to use once every 2 minutes, or twice every 2 minutes if you're a Hunter. This is at least alleviated with things like Tangles, the fragment that lets you regenerate grenade energy for dealing damage, but ultimately, the whole gimmick of 'extra mobility' is not worth the sacrifices and awkward playstyle adjustment you need for it.

Strand melee ability feels incredibly weak on Hunter and take too long to recharge on Warlock for what they do -- can't speak for Titan personally.

That said, Threadlings are awesome.

- The story of the campaign feels incredibly rushed, probably because of the rumor that Lightfall was at one point, The Final Shape before being split and turned into a filler DLC, and as a few friends have described, like Bungie is just tossing Destiny 2 buzzwords in your face hoping the equivalent of shaking keys in your face is enough to keep you satisfied. It's irritating. Nothing meaningful happens until the very end, and we could quite literally cut out the entire campaign and have the same result, just without Strand.

- Onto one of the biggest complaints of Lightfall; Nimbus. Nimbus is, frankly, not as bad as many people say, in my humble opinion. Their dialogue is incredibly tone-deaf, and at best, facepalm worthy, but not quite worth getting scalding angry over like some people have chosen to. The post campaign segments with Nimbus proves that Bungie does know how to give depth to characters, but it comes far too late.
...
...
But, I think that's enough negativity for now.
Lightfall DOES do some things right.


- Tormentors are by far one of the coolest additions to the enemy roster yet. It's very refreshing to have an enemy that requires your attention in a unique way. Tormentors feel like a Dark Souls enemy, almost, in that they telegraph what they'll do and hit like a truck if you don't bother to get out of the way, or in some cases, immediately kill you for sticking around too close to them.

I LOVE what Bungie has done with Tormentors and I am begging for them to add them to more content.

- As usual for Bungie, the music SLAPS. The composers have done very well with matching the story beats and segments of Lightfall with an excellent, unique soundtrack. Won't say much more, but as always, the music's great.

- Circling back to Strand, the concepts once again are very neat. Hunters have a very cohesive identity with what they have access to. The rope dart melee with the catch mechanic is an idea I want to see more of, even if it's absolutely awful at rewarding you unless you hit a small mob of adds. Warlocks have a very unique focus with their take on the Strand Subclass, being able to constantly output Threadlings which are simultaneously adorable and deadly, a perfect mix for Destiny 2.
I can't speak for Titans, as I only can suffer through Lightfall's campaign so much, but from what I hear of my fellow brothers and sisters in the way of the fist, generally speaking, Strand Titan seems to be well received if a bit uninspired.
Now if only the cooldowns weren't completely disproportionate to the effects of the abilities. I'm sure Strand will see a fair amount of use, but it won't be in the way Bungie intended, swinging around at breakneck speeds and unraveling the enemies of the Traveler. Which is fine, if not a shame.


Ultimately, Lightfall has left me with a lot of discontentment. This was an especially shocking failure after following Witch Queen, a DLC that completely reignited my love for Destiny 2. It's like some amateur band playing right after your favorite -- it's a slaughter and it isn't even a fair fight.

I wouldn't recommend this one, unless you genuinely dig the aesthetic of Strand and want to be fully in on whatever crazy development comes as a result of new content being released.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA little bit of a backstory, I got kind of tired of Destiny 2 near season of the seraph. Saw the lightfall trailer and got interested again and bought the DLC. So what are my thoughts about Lightfall.

Pros:
- Increadibly fun and challenging gameplay during the legendary campaign that I havent had in a long time.
- The new Strand subclass is really strong but breathed new life into both PvE and PvP. Havent had this much fun in the crucible.
- The addition of loadouts have made it so much easier to make new and fun builds.
- The new exotics, perks and guns have also made it more fun to use different weapons that arent the META.
- The revamp of subclasses, making them able to fight champions is a nice plus as it makes builds into higher lvl activities boring by only using the anti champ mods from the artifact.
- The raid might be easier than others but has a great premise, a fun boss, good loot and is extremely fun.

Cons:
- The biggest con is the storytelling of lightfall. Bungie tried to do to much to fast.
- Everything is extremely OP once you have a build that works.

Other:
- The new season (Season of defiance) isnt that much to scream about but it is only the first few weeks of the season.

So overall I think the hate towards Lightfall is kinda oversaturated and feels more like mob mentality. If you look past the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ storytelling and focus mainly on the gameplay, Lightfall does what it should.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is not a bad game as a "Mostly Negative" Steam review average suggests. The problem entirely stems from a bait-and-switch with the marketing and the campaign storyline, which suggested that major world changes were happening, that *Spoilers*...they did not.

The campaign narrative is also tone deaf, and the characters sometimes obnoxious.

That aside, this is DESTINY. It's a game that offers 1000s of hours of stuff to do. After the 5-10 hr campaign, the exploration of the world and sandbox open up and the enhancements present within the release come to light. The gameplay itself, as always, is the BEST shooting experience around. Strand, the new subclass that you can actually use after the campaign, is a fantastic refresh to interacting with the world and its enemies. The art and music are sublime, as always.

If you like a world where you build a character through a variety of activities in a world with deep (somewhat inaccessable in-game) lore, this is a great product that will give you MORE DESTINY. If you're looking for a FPS shooter campaign to try and drop after 10 hrs....this isn't for you.\t\t\t\t\t
