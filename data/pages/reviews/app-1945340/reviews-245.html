\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMechanically, the legendary campaign was one of the worst experiences I've ever had. There are three primary reasons I think this game is unforgivably bad and why Bungie have become directionless and complacent. Please note that I attempted legendary solo before I gave out on the 7th mission and just got sick of how bad the story, mechanics, and overall pacing is.

1. Boss Health and Bullet Sponges
Overall the boss fights are by far the worst part of this heap of dung. Heavy ammo does 1/10th to 1/20th of the bosses health. I think every single fight I was scraping by with nothing but Primary Ammo trying to move from cover to cover to get one shot in. Fights take a tremendous amount of time and give no checkpoints in between. Most of them have run ups to where if the game decides to surround you and kill you, you have to start not just the boss fight over but the entire fight leading up to it.

2. Unhalting Ads
What I mean by this is ads or basic enemies that never stop spawning. There are certain triggers that make another wave of ads spawn that the player, after fighting them over and over, learn to manipulate. You end up trying not to cause those triggers and begin to exploit the gameplay mechanics therein. Overall, nonstop, unhalting waves of ads is, in my opinion, the laziest and most boring way that you can add mechanics to a boss fight. Oftentimes these ads are spawning behind you, around you, constantly flanking you as you try to chip away at this boss' ungodly healthbar. It makes me think that nobody in Bungie even tried playing the campaign - how in the world would anyone play this and say "yeah, this is enjoyable enough to ship."

3. Terrible Puzzles
The best way I can exemplify this is an example. In the first mission they give you a tooltip that says that you should look out for and shoot vent openings to progress. In doing this they teach you the way they design levels non-linearly, if the doors are locked you should look for a vent. Then in about mission five, for the first time in my memory, they instead removed all vents from the room and made it to where you had to punch a door. They didn't make this door stand out particularly well, it looked like any other door. Every other door it looked like didn't open, didn't respond to punching, and triggered the player to look for another path. And then all the sudden they go against the entire philosophy of level design they've been doing since Destiny 1 and put in a punchable door. Not to mention the killer grav lifts that are bugged on release, unclear objectives, and inconsistent objective marker placement.

Overall this is the worst campaign I've ever seen. Even if I were to go along with the gaping plotholes and get into their "the darkness is the friends we made along the way" philosophy, it's writing that you might find in a middle school poetry book with dialogue to boot. While writing this I keep seeing videos of the devs talking about how exciting their campaign is. And I can't help but think that each and every one of them are delusional and tunnel visioned on how poorly designed their game has been recently, and how far into the the depths of failure they will sink into these coming years.\t\t\t\t\t

[Disclaimer]: I'm not going over any Seasonal content yet and will not be going over mechanics of the game other then Strand. This is because I haven't played Seasonal content yet (friends wanna do it together) and I'm not well versed on many of the mechanics that were changed, so I'm not a good person to give my opinion on them.

[TL:DR]: The story as a whole was just underwhelming and failed to explain anything to us, leaving us confused and lost at the end of it all. It feels like this could've just been Seasonal content and it would've made more sense, but instead we paid for a 60 dollar DLC for a new Subclass and just to be left on read by the story team.

For those that care for more detail, my more expanded thoughts on it all.

To start, Lightfall is a campaign that was extremely hyped up and rightfully so. Witch Queen had left us on a good note, the end of Season 19 cinematic was amazing and had us on the edge of our seats on what was about to transpire. Then the launch Trailer came out and folks were even MORE excited about it, as we got a glimpse of an enemy we'd have foreshadowed for a good bit and everything else going for us. All that was left was for Bungie to not fumble the ball.

And boy did they fumble it bad.

The campaign started off well, since it really is hard to mess up an introduction that had been amazingly done already by both the Season 19 final cinematic and Launch trailer. We got to hang out with Osiris, who understandably takes a large role in this after being absent because of Witch Queen, as well as got a cool short scene where we got to be inside the drop pods that the Cabal use. Which when we landed, we were introduced to the new planet and the new 'faction', if you can really call two people a faction.

Off the bat, I was aware Rohan was gonna just get thrown away during the campaign. Seeing as they specifically say "We generally only live 10 years and mine are almost up." Which.. great, already know not to get attached to this character and just focus on everybody else we were introduced to. Nimbus wasn't bad either, but I can't say I really liked them either, since they were very one dimensional overall. There's the new faction introduced who protect the city.

We're then introduced to the new subclass, the infamous touch gra- I mean, Strand abilities and honestly? Not really a fan of it. Almost every single mission of the campaign was more focused on how our Guardian interacted with it then actual story telling, which if that wasn't bad enough, it also forced you to use it in multiple situations where it wasn't really viable AT ALL, especially on Legendary. Overall, wouldn't be as big of an issue though, if the rest of the story didn't suffer because of their hyper focus on Strand.

Then, there's Calus and The Witness. They were already hyped through the Trailer, but honestly? Both were super underwhelming through the entire campaign. The only enemy that was a product of The Witness that I knew of, were the Tormentors, they were unique and definitely a pain in the ass to fight, but I liked them as mini-bosses. But overall, we didn't really see much of either big bad, next to a few cut scenes of Calus being salty we kept beating him and his floating head. Then, the only time we saw him in person, you kick his ass in a really, REALLY irritating fight. I'll admit, wasn't my favourite boss battle for sure, but it marked the end of the campaign and.. that was it.

Now, with all that in mind. A few of my main complaints with the story, if some of them weren't already obvious;

To summarise why I'm mad in short. The story just left us on a GIANT cliff hanger with basically no information about ANYTHING we were supposed to care about throughout the entire story. For example, the BIGGEST question that literally every review currently has.. what the ♥♥♥♥ is The Veil? Why was it so important?

The entire campaign felt like it was way too focused on the Strand and just completely failed to focus on actual important aspects of the story that would've kept us hooked to finish the campaign. Instead everything kinda became this mindless slog of just - killing. Nothing was ever really explained to us, we just were expected to ignore the fact we knew nothing and focus on learning Strand.

Then, once we kill Calus... that's it. The story just ends without explaining any of it. The Witness leaves after our Ghost makes the link, very anticlimactically and we're left wondering just -- what the ♥♥♥♥? What do we do now? What the hell just happened?

I'm not one to mind endings to stories that result in the players losing as long as it's a loss that explains more about the story - but this really didn't feel like it expanded on the story much or told us as players anything. We've just been left even more confused then before and really just.. unsatisfied overall.

Don't know how else to end this review, but that's basically it. It was really underwhelming for the hype that was built up with The Witness and everything, it just.. sucked, for a DLC that's supposed to be so important.\t\t\t\t\t
