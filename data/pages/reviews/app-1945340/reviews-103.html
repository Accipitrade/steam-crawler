\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll sum up my journey in Lightfall with three words: "I had fun."

Honestly seeing 'mostly negative' when i view this DLC is sad. People either are listening to content creators or barely play the game instantly complaining, rather then letting Destiny do what it does best: tell a slow unfolding story. Even if there are issues with this DLC, they are minor and Lightfall does not deserve to be called a dumpster fire, which is all I am seeing online and in this review section.
Alongside the quality of life changes Bungie has been making, this DLC is a masterpiece gameplay wise. The armour overhaul and the all the subclass reworks being done, gives me the freedom to use so many new combinations of loadouts. Strand is simple and refreshing, and when you get all the elements unlocked you can see how Bungie designed a subclass around chaining abilities for combos. Again I will use the word fun, loads of fun. People who say strand is lackluster are delusional or haven't put in the time to buildcraft.
As far as the story goes, is it perfect? No. There are a few things that could have been done differently, but i honestly think this a critique of personal preference when it comes to storytelling. Bungie told us we would be getting an action-packed story where we were an action hero, and this in my opinion is what we got and my goodness is it fun! I had the same level of fun playing this campaign as I did playing Halo 2 or 3. Seeing our Guardian struggle to master a subclass was refreshing as well, rather then just we can do it perfectly as with everything else. A complaint of Lightfall is that very little is explained in the campaign. This honestly confuses me because Destiny has always been like this. The campaign is new-light friendly while if you want to dig deeper into the story you can with lore books and tabs on certain gear.

I'll end my review with a message for new lights and veterans. To new lights, if you have never played this game before or are thinking of returning this DLC is a friendly entrance into Destiny 2. The story is simple and allows you to focus on buildcrafting. The legendary version of the campaign is a great balance of challenge and fun. The World is refreshing but deadly. You will have to get your power level up to fight the invading army, which adds to the immersion. Just a solid, fun campaign to keep you entertained for 5-10 hours where you are the action hero. The cost is alot but a reminder that you are paying for dungeons and raids and seasonal story content for the entire year (if you buy the annual pass).
To veterans my goodness I'm not mad, just disappointed. You barely spent 2 hours into the campaign and then throw the whole thing in a dumpster fire without letting the story unfold. The raid and world quests still need to be done. This is an MMO RPG, Not everything is explained on the surface level. We don't even know what the Traveler is after playing this franchise for a decade, calm down. If you hate the DLC that much, don't play it. If you think it's overpriced, don't buy it. But instead most of you still play the game (cause its amazing) and continue to add fuel to the fire of how much this DLC is trash. Not only are you and annoying child, you are setting a bad example to anyone wanting to check out this amazing universe. Most of you should have your light taken away; you are not worthy of it anymore, maybe you never were.

Was this DLC the best thing ever made, you can decide that for yourself but I can tell you one thing: it is a ton of fun, at least for me. What more can you ask from a video game?\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBingo has already been on a slippery slope since taking away the base campaign, the Curse of Osiris and Warmind expansions, and the Forsaken campaign. Sunsetting weapons also took place around this time, where Banjo took away weapons people played hours upon hours to acquire and then reintroduced the same weapons in paid content to be re-acquired. NOW, Bonko has charged over $50 for an expansion who's story is what I'd describe as "filler content" and re-skinned weapons. SUPPOSEDLY the new powers you get in Lightfall were originally going to release with the Witch Queen expansion, and the story campaign was going to be part of the Final Shape story.
The story of Lightfall itself is a let down, mainly due to it being nothing but a tease to an answer to a question that we've had since almost half a year ago. I'm not a fan of the story revolving around a macguffin, and all the side characters acting like they know what the macguffin is, and we (the main character) NEVER FIND OUT WHAT THE MACGUFFIN IS, WHAT IT DOES, OR WHY IT IS BAD TO LET THE BAD GUY GET IT. We also get two new characters named Rohan and Nimbus, who are the designated protectors of Neomuna. I don't think we get much of a chance to bond with them, and by the end of the story I don't care much for them. I didn't even feel sad when Rohan sacrifices himself to stop a second macguffin from messing with the first one. What did make me feel sad is realizing that the story, that I paid for, doesn't answer any questions I have, and I will instead have my questions answered in the coming FREE seasonal stories and the next expansion I'll have to pay for.
I do enjoy the Strand powers we get. They feel great to play with and I enjoy the new movement options we get with it. Neomuna looks great, and we still have yet to get the new raid from Bankai, so keep an eye out for reviews on that. The new exotic weapons we've gotten so far are kinda good, but nothing amazing (except for the new giant ice glaive). The new exotic armors are nice, too (nothing over-powered, but still good).

Now, while I will continue to play Destiny 2, I cannot in good faith recommend this DLC to anyone new or coming back into the game. I already bought the pre-order package where I get the base Lightfall, the four seasons for this year, two new dungeons and a reprised raid, and pre-order bonuses. All of that came out to over $110. Lightfall itself is $50, while Forsaken (an expansion with a much better story, two destinations, the game mode Gambit, a raid, more exotic weapons and armor than we get with Lightfall, etc.) was $39.99. Bangkok, if you see this review, please get some help.
