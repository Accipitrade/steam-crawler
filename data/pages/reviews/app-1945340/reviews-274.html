\r\n\t\t\t\t\tReally quite rough, sadly. An extremely lackluster story that felt like it was missing half its content. Combined with a dumbed down and more irritating mod system that coincides with a rather large global nerf to ability regen, this expansion has missed nearly all the marks.

The story was very isolated from what was supposed to be the big threat, the Witness finally arriving, and while there was one mission I really enjoyed, I really only enjoyed it because it was like a mini-horde mode mission. Without spoilers, the story basically ends with a "oh ♥♥♥♥, I can't believe that just happened" reaction from all the characters but...nothing really changed? Everything is as you expect it. If you thought this was going to be the Infinity War of Destiny, you'll be sorely disappointed. There was an event, sure, and it looked like it'll be interesting...in one year when the Final Shape comes out, with filler seasons before that.

And unlike Beyond Light, which was slightly redeemed by how fun Stasis was on launch (excluding PvP), Strand really feels sorta dead in the water.

Anything it can do, another sublcass can do better. Threadlings don't hit very hard, suspend is at best equal to freezing and at worst far worse due to the lack of bonus damage from a shatter. Unravel is scorch's poor cousin. Grappling, while extremely fun, manages to be the most disappointing element here. Throughout the whole story, you're treated to an extremely reduced grapple cooldown (3 or so seconds) that begins as soon as your feet are back on the ground. This cooldown persists like this even once the other cooldowns (super, melee, class ability) are normalized, leading you to believe the grapple might just be that accessible, considering you're giving up your grenade for it. Finishing the story you come to find that with all mods spec'd into grenade cooldown, you're looking at about a minute between grapples at best. Any less discipline and it's going to be much longer.

Now, why would they do that? I'm not a dev, but if I had to take a shot in the dark I would guess one reason and one reason in particular: PvP. Constant grappling would break PvP, maybe, probably. Yet another instance of Destiny's power fantasy floundering for a mode that Bungie barely pays attention to.

This one was just too much bad for me and not enough fun. I even defended Shadowkeep because at its core it brought back a nice location and had a cool story. But this...if this was the expansion they had released instead of Forsaken back in the day, I think Destiny would have had a decent chance of dying out.

Also, if you see anybody "criticizing" Nimbus for being non-binary or "woke" tell them to shove a surfboard up their ass and try criticizing Nimbus for an ACTUAL reason, such as them being an unbearably annoying character with about three idle lines that keep repeating and dialogue that is actually about as bad or worse as pre-Forsaken Destiny. Seriously they're unbearable, and the vocal quality is really strange with whatever audio effects they used. The VA is the same one that did Athena in Hades, so I'm sure they would've done a really good job with proper vocal direction and mixing.

The other characters outside of Nimbus are pretty much just as cringe. A lot post-MCU dialogue. And by characters, I do mean disembodied voices. I guess the engine couldn't handle any citizens actually roaming the streets of Neomuna, and they explain it away by saying all of them uploaded their minds into a virtual simulation. It's a barebones excuse to have another empty patrol zone. I'm not sure if it's just due to their engine or if it's from hanging on to last-gen consoles for this long but...jeez.\t\t\t\t\t
