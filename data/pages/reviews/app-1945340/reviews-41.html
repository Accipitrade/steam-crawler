\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has enough to keep you both busy and immersed, enough to not want to or need to play any other game.

The gameplay is fantastic, with synnergistic space wizardry powers and shooter action, satisfying movement, flexible skill and equipment builds. The armors are stylish and new ones are constantly added. The weapons all have their own language, and flavours. Nothing feels pedestrian when it comes to the actual gameplay. Arguably one of if not the best feeling shooter still out there today.

The activities are varried, and there is something for everyone, from PvE, to PvP, to PvPvE, from hardcore to casual, whether you have 15 minutes or 15 hours. There are quests ( or "Bounties") to reward pretty much anything you plan on doing.

Perhaps the best and worst part of the game, is its world building, narrative and lore. There is so much of it, you are entering a universe of its own, but a lot of the times, it gets revealed to you piece by piece and for newer players - it can feel a bit disorienting (but not impenetrable). If you like your lore, you will be easily immersed. If you don't care, you won't feel lost if you skip straight to the action.

Holding it all together, is a very very painfully passionate community, of players, of clans, of "content creators" (god we need a better name for that) that constantly make always up to date guides, provide advice, and hold each others hand through this epic space opera of space wizards with guns.

One piece of advice: join a clan, don't JUST use public LFG discord servers - it is a sad experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tlightfall is an enjoyable expansion but it falls short on some of the components destiny players have come to expect.
tldr if you enjoy genociding aliens and collecting cool loot with your friends this is a great expansion. if you are captivated by the worldbuilding and storytelling the world of destiny 2 has to offer, you will be sorely disappointed. but its still a live service grindfest
+ new patrol zone is pretty good, there's a lot going on in terms of secrets and public events and the lost sectors are some of the more interesting ones. it feels a bit lifeless due to the lack of flesh-and-blood inhabitants though. the featured weapons are decent and have some unique qualities but most of them don't stand out as remarkably powerful or interesting, which is par for early game patrol zone gear.
+ the mission-oriented campaign quest and missions are great. it follows the witch queen formula in that there's a lot of interesting enemy variety, including a brand new unit and some minor variations on familiar ones, and plenty of opportunities for new players to develop their characters and returning players to put their gear to use. recurring campaign missions offer some rewards, and almost no patrol zone busy work (much better than beyond light). the new subclass has a few jarring tutorial sections in the campaign which we could have done without, but doesnt help or hamper the experience much.
- the campaign story sucks to the point of frustration. the player is constantly strung along by other characters (some of whom are very annoying!) and virtually nothing about the stakes or the consequences of the story beats is communicated.
there's something very weird going on and everyone involved seems to either have a firm grasp on everything happening, or like the player, are completely clueless. the introduction of the new subclass in terms of worldbuilding and story was not compelling or gratifying, unfortunately in line with the rest of the story. compared to the introduction of stasis i consider this a downgrade. overall the story is the biggest flop in recent memory.
+ new subclass is very cool, fun, and powerful and has a lot going on but it needs a bit more attention (coming next season) but its still fun to play around with for now and undoubtedly has a place in every part of the game.
~ the raid is decently fun and very interesting to look at, but it's honestly quite easy and doesn't often engage the whole team in mechanics or puzzles. the stakes also seemed pretty weak and self-contained. definitely a downgrade from Vow and Crypt. still a worthy challenge for newer-to-midlevel players and a fun experience.
~ includes Season 20 content, which is somewhat overshadowed by the main expansion and on its own is noticeably less interesting than the previous few seasons. quest/mini-campaign is mid, but the exotic mission is really cool and the game just drowns you in the seasonal loot items, many of which are worth pursuing.
+ the exotic quests (i think theres 2 main ones plus a bonus exotic for completing the new subclass unlocks) are pretty engaging. they flesh out the world and characters and the weapons are enjoyable to use and fit in well with their associated subclass features (void/stasis/strand) which is always welcome but becoming the expected norm.
+ changes to the base game (which are technically free to play) include consolidation/simplification of difficulty tiers, a huge armor mod rework (which is mostly good but still needs a bit of work) and a handful of extremely welcome quality of life features like fully functional in-game loadouts (up to 10 per character) new engram focusing and storage options for mainstay ritual & seasonal gear, and nice touch-ups to the deepsight weapon shaping/crafting system

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny has a weird trend of following up a above average expansion, with a below average one, Lightfall is this point.
The story is once more a big ancient enemy from lore coming to get us, this time the biggest of them all, the Witness. His arrival however lacks any sort of tension. For something setup to be as big as the Reaper invasion in the Mass Effect trilogy, the attack of the Witness is small and sidetracked by the mission of stop his lackey to breach the Veil. What’s the Veil you ask, well it is this magical barrier that splits the living world and the … Wait this is another IP, in Destiny’s classic terrible worldbuilding, it is never explained. But the worst is the ending, or the lack thereof. With the least amount of spoilers, essentially the antagonist achieve a goal and nothing happens, the story just “ends”. The story clearly got cut at the midpoint ( My bet is in the first act, games often end the first act with a player defeat) and Bungie know that you’re buying the next DLC.
Gameplay wise, this is the worse designed campaign, with constant tutorials and actually annoying points, like the mission you have to run from the enemy forces on your unarmored transport that becomes a Russian roulette.
The seasonal story fairs better, heck Mara is by far the best character of this game, but the repetitive nature of the seasonal mission will always be a downsize.
In the end, this is beyond the light 2.0, very meh story, new subclass and that’s about it. It gets the negative not recommended because of cut ending, but in the end it’s just another destiny DLC.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t(50$ for half a dlc)
At first I was just disappointed in this dlc, I returned to destiny at the beginning of this season from an almost 3 year hiatus, thinking this dlc would be a good way to get back into the game. My general consensus on the dlc was, the campaign had fun enough gameplay, but as most people have heard had a story that was incredibly lacking. I don't want to repeat what everyone else has said about this. But after a month of playing the new dlc and season, I can surely say it still isn't worth it, and to a greater degree now that the season has continued. I also recently learned about the original timeline for the Destiny 2 dlc, lightfall was never meant to even exist really, or it was supposed to be something completely different. Instead we get another dlc to complete a story that is frankly disjointed now because of this dlc. I won't lie, I didn't see a point in this dlc coming out, I would've personally preferred if they kept the original plan and focused more on that dlc. Even if it would've been delayed for a even longer period of time. I'm sure Bungie could have come up with something to fill up this years seasons, without a new dlc.

Getting to the dlc itself, I felt like the balancing of this dlc also wasn't too great. After playing the past years dlcs I felt like there had been way too many enemies packed into the missions. And while strand was fun to play around with, because you were locked away from it for most of the dlc, you never get to truly use it against the hordes that the game throws at you. The new enemy type, tormentors, I found very boring to say the least. I'll give bungie that they have a cool design and attack style, but I ended up just running away and shooting at them. Fighting one was just playing a game of chase really, even their ranged attacks you could just outrun, with the rank and file enemies being more of threat during these types of fights. Next is the issues with neptune as a patrol area itself, it isn't a very interesting area to fight in. When I first saw that the dlc was going to take place in a city, I was hoping for more vertical and urban like combat. Instead we got a zone that didn't have much of that at all, its mostly open and flat. The neon city of Neomuna has a cool aesthetic, but its layout has one of the least interesting designs I've ever seen in the game. And the enemies within the city aren't fun to fight either, the vex and cabal platoons that litter the city are nothing new, we've seen these enemies since the first game. And while that's an issue on its own, what I found most tiresome fighting them was the fact they were just bullet sponges. Idk if it was like this when the other dlcs prior to this dropped, but I don't get why they have so much health. In the campaign missions they had a normal amount of health, and weren't at all hard to dispatch. The ones in the patrol zone you just have to sink bullets into to die. While my light level is now much higher, almost at the cap, they are a bit easier to fight. Which makes me question if the enemies were set to a level of 1800 or 1810? And why this was a decision they made? I don't really know, maybe it's to distract you from the lack of content. Recounting a point that I had said before, none of the enemies are really new. The tormentors being the new one off enemy type, are as I've stated not very good, their greatest attack is wasting your time really. I left at the end of the shadowkeep seasons, and seeing that we haven't had a new enemy type since forsaken is more than disappointing. I wasn't expecting lightfall to be a taken king sized dlc, but it had the chance to do something cool. Introduce some new species to fight, something related to the witnesses forces like the new tormentors or Rhulk. Going over the campaign/narrative seems kind of pointless especially with other reviews talking about it, but something about Osiris' presentation felt odd. His voice actor sounded tired, and at point performed like an emotionless robot. I know he's never been super emotional throughout the series, but it felt off.

What is actually good in this dlc though? Strand for the most part, strand is super fun to play with, it's refreshing use, and creates a unique play style centered around it. And that's what I will give bungie for this dlc, they definitely did make a great new subclass. The new sets of weapons have cool designs and are fun to use. And while this isn't dlc specific, the new defiant battlegrounds I did enjoy a lot. However they also bring another critique to the dlc itself. The story around this season specific game mode is far captivating than the dlcs. So is the gameplay provided within it, something I have genuinely enjoyed. Neomuna for all its fault, does have some cool background characters, the whole idea of the cloud arc and a virtual but still kind of living world is very interesting. I honestly didn't mind the mystery of the veil, other than that you never truly learn about what it was.

I feel as if this has gone on long enough. Concluding this I would have to say the dlc is most certainly not worth it. Whether you're a returning player like me, someone who has been playing the game consistently for most of its time, or someone new to the series. Priced at 50$ and during a time in many face the full force of a faltering economy, for half the content a normal dlc would offer. The only reason I see for this dlcs existence is for Bungie to further milk this series for all its worth. While pulling the wool over its own players eyes, making them think that they had returned to being a staple developer with the witch queen dlc. I can only hope that final shape is a return back to what we once had with dlcs like forsaken.\t\t\t\t\t
