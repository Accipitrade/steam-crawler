\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll start by saying this (as a Destiny Boomer):

For any person who has taken some time away from the franchise or simply hasn't touched it -- a warning is that the community is a hive mind. This isn't meant to flame or defame anyone, but most of the negative reviews are coming from people who are upset with stuff that happened halfway through this game and are missing the entire ACTUAL purpose of this expansion. Please approach the Vex Collective that is this community with care and caution.

So -- for the actual review portion! Spent the last three days running friends through the story on Legendary simply because this expansion is actually that fun. The new subclass has definitely been given plenty of love and Bungie seems to have put a lot of genuine care into the release, which is more than what can be said for half of the expansions, especially the precursor to this one. The story is actually really good, and there is finally some closure to the Red War from the very start of Destiny 2, which can't be said for the constant murder of an entire Hive hierarchy. (Thanks Eris -- you failed the task successfully.)

Some pros and cons of the expansion are in order though.

Pros:
- New VERY FUN subclass!
- Enjoyable story
- Really fun and interesting new exotic weapons
- Challenging content for hardcore players, fun casual content for casual gamers
- Content added explicitly from Lightfall is super fun to use
- New adjustments to gear and mods
- Pretty sure they ninja-adjusted the effects of mobility on jumps (been getting places I couldn't before without Strand)
- Strand makes stuff that used to take hours to complete only take 20 minutes minimum sometimes
- Good Soup

Cons:
- Story isn't perfect -- still a few things that could've been done better or tweaked
- Grav Lifts are the raid boss
- Due to the recent release, much of the potential for weapon/super damage is unhinged
- Osiris AND Crow are still alive
- Lots of back and forth for the story and quests associated to Lightfall
- Strand Grapple doesn't always track properly, so it gets used by accident a lot. (Should probably adjust how grenades work)
- Some casual content has gotten to be unmanageable without competent randoms or good friends
- Lots of Stasis, almost too much with the release of Strand (It's not that good -- please stop giving me ice-cube shooters...)
- Recent release updates may have bugged out older content from prior expansions

All in All: It's good. There are plenty of things that Bungie could do better, but it's definitely a step in the right direction. Getting to see this franchise through both Destiny's closed beta and Destiny 2's open beta, I think this is the best thing since Rise of Iron from Destiny. It's not perfect, but it is definitely better than Shadowkeep or Forsaken (which is a toss up, because they seemed to put a lot of love into Forsaken too, minus Cayde.) I can't wait to see how the dungeons and the raid work out. I'm definitely looking forward to this year in Destiny as whole thanks to Ligthfall alone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore I start, First, Spoilers. Second, I've been playing Destiny for 8 years. So I think that my review speaks for those of us that may be feeling the same way and also for those that have been playing Destiny for awhile. Third, this will be lengthy so I hope you enjoy reading. Lets do this.

Lightfall is, as I can explain it, eating your favorite dish at your favorite restaurant, taking a bite and realizing that your food has been cook by someone else. It's not made by the cook you know who puts love into that dish! Which is DEPRESSING! It's not what you expected at all. And you continue to eat this disappointing food in your mouth that you know is way better than this. (I hope that analogy helps.)

Don't get me wrong, the gameplay is fun. Strand is great, the legendary campaign was good (We will get into story soon), tormentors were, cool I guess, but besides that, it was disappointing. Bungie, we know you can release good expansions that cover every aspect! You've done it before with Witch Queen, Year one of Destiny 2 (went downhill after that), but you've done it! This expansion didn't though and I'll explain why.

1. Story
Wow. What a miss. We literally had no questions answered. And now have newer ones!
- Veil
- Witness
- Calus. Again.
- Plot

The Veil. All we know is that it's like the traveler? I always thought that the veil was a group or an organization that was super powerful, and it turns out to be this "LSD Trip" Type of root? I don't even know but apparently its very important! Because the Witness needed it and he got it. I can't really say more to this because I don't know more on this which is sad.

The Witness. Okay, we got to see this "other side" to him this expanison which Bungie should've given more of! We were introduced to him briefly at the end of Witch Queen and I think we barely got to actually see him! Most of the time he was using our ghost... Cop-out? I don't know but I wish we saw more!

Calus. This dude was legit a raid boss twice. And we beat him in a campaign story that didn't even feel like it was worth it. This dude was the biggest and baddest. His ship eats planets PEOPLE! Idk not much here but just disappointed.

Plot. This expansion, I believe, was nothing more than a dumpster fire as terms as plot. What did we do to progress this "Light/Dark" Saga?! All that happened in terms of light and dark is the Witness went into the Traveler and the traveler is none responsive? And has a cool triangle on it? For a title as Lightfall, the only light I saw fall was the sun going down from how long the campaign was (which was a good thing). It was just not there! Personally, Neptune and Neomuna are not plot altering! Sure, cool, but it didn't feel like we were doing anything productive except protecting the veil. Idk, idc for Neptune or Neomuna or the cloudstriders.

To wrap up the story, I really don't know what to say besides, "What just happened."

2. Reusing Old Content

Yay my favorite. Man, we don't want to do the same patrols since 2017. We want New! It's called an expansion for a reason! Right? Am I crazy for thinking that a expansion of $50 should have new things besides the obvious: exotics, weapons, armor, raids, etc.? Idk but the reusing of old content is really getting old (no pun intended).

3. Lack of Direction

The biggest fear for people is, "Where are we going." We want to know what direction we are heading in. And we are just left at stand still. I'm hoping that the seasons continue this "Light/Dark" Saga but for right now it seems like we are now just waiting. WHICH SUCKS! In Witch Queen, we knew the Witness was coming. We had something to look forward too! Bro, we got a stinking Triangle in front of the traveler and you want me to believe that I'm just supposed to watch it like paint drying on a wall?! But hey, that's what seasonal content is for right?

Closing thoughts:
Man. Really bummed. I wanted more! I wanted the detail! Not a cyberpunk expansion with cool colors. But don't get me wrong, gameplay is fun and I'm looking forward to the raid. The Narrative is what tanked the recommendation of this game. I'm just so lost and have so many questions which WILL NOT be answered soon, or I believe, will be answered in the next expansion. I hope someone from Bungie sees this because this is all I'm saying. I enjoy Destiny! I do! But it feels like we are being left in the dust and you keep letting us down! If the team at bungie is struggling, say it! I believe if you made a statement like "Hey, want you guys to know, we've been dropping the ball and we are changing.", we would be crying for joy because we are at least getting some sort of communication. We just want to enjoy the story of Destiny but it's hard to like it entirely.

Hoping this helped and let me know what you guys think <3\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tKicking myself for falling to the same trap Destiny/Bungie continues to lay out. I think I am finally going to put this game down for good.. but I know I've said this before in years past.

The campaign is pretty cool to start, but the honeymoon phase doesn't last long.. I quickly learned that pretty much every builds/guns I used two Season ago had been nerfed into oblivion and were no longer fun to use in anyway. Always fun to come back to a game after a small break to realize most of the guns and gear you worked for in past seasons is obsolete.

I beat it on solo Legendary and most of the missions they force you to play the "Strand" subclass are absolute cancer and make me think that the play testers/developers are stupid or just bad at their job.. probably both, idk.

that said the game is obviously beautiful..if you can deal with all the flashing vfx and seizure inducing lights spamming your screen at all times during battle, but unfortunately that doesnt make up for the lifeless game Bungie has created.. its all a façade.. they trick you with the promise of an "openworld" loot based rpg only to find out you have to shell out 100$'s to have access to the entire game and feel like you aren't at a disadvantage to those who've bought all the content.

I really want to love this game.. everything on paper about this game seems so promising.. then you actually play it.. and the energy it gives off just doesnt feel right at all.. I'm really not sure how to describe it. I know Bungie has a diehard fanbase that will always keep Destiny afloat for as long as bungie keeps creating, but those of us on the fringe of this game should really consider removing this game from the library, or atleast wait for reviews before ever making a Bungie related purchase again.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that I've had a few days to sit and ponder here is my review of Lightfall.
First we have the gameplay in which I have to mention the new super, Strand. Easily the most fun super that I've used. It's easy to traverse, very powerful, cool animations and changes the gameplay in so many ways. Gameplay wise I have always found Destiny fun, and I wasn't sure how it could get better, but Strand has definitely improved on one of the games best quality. Gameplay is a 10/10!
Next up is the location, Neomuna, Neptune. A cool unique location that may be one of my new favourites. HOWEVER, it lacks in size and while still have a bit of verticality, it could use with more. Once the campaign is finished, a new area is unlocked which is a sort of museum and it reminded me of the Arcadia on Titan. Overall Neomuna is a cool place and thankfully doesn't lean too much into the synthwave genre, but lacks in size and verticality. 5/10!
Lastly we have the campaign and it's story. Whilst I enjoyed seeing Calus back and his interaction with the Witness, the story and characters were rushed and felt more of an introduction of things with no explanation of what they are. Despite the story just giving us more questions than answers, the campaign was really fun. Each mission was really cool with each having stand out moments, one mission we're jumping through space, and the next we're using our sparrows to escape the enemy. Campaign is a 8/10. Story is a 5/10.

Overall, 7/10\t\t\t\t\t
