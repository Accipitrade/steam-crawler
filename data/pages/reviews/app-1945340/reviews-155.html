\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this, but after finishing the campaign and messing around in neomuna for a while it became very clear that this expansion was rushed and had a lot of cut content.

For starters, the campaign is really fun overall full of nice new mechanics and systems, but the narrative itself is very lackluster: Characters reference certain elements constantly but never explain them to the player, the actions of the main characters don't seem to make a lot of sense most of the time and by the end of the campaign it doesn't seem like the characters on earth did awhile you were on neomuna ( in fact the last cutscene is just the second half of the first cutscene of the campaign !).

On the other hand the gameplay was pretty satisfying, stasis is a bit barebones right now, but a really great addition to the game, however it suffers from the same problem stasis did, because you only unlock the damn subclass at the end of the campaign, and you need to ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ GRIND for all of the customization options for the subclass, it seems like they didn't learn anything with the other subclasses. It felt awful having to waste a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ of glimmer to fully unlock all of the light subclases, it felt specially horrible to grind to complete the stasis subclass, and it still feels awful to have to grind to fully unlock the strand subclass as well. Granted the grind is way shorter than these other subclasses, but why did i have to do it in the first place if the main campaign focuses so much on mastering this new power ? It all feels like a contrived reason to get you to grind eternally for basically no reward !

Speaking of the rewards you grind for, there is a very small amount of loot for you to actually obtain, and a substantial amount of the of the new weapons released in this expansion are reskins of old ones, the damn trailer had new unique weapons that aren't in the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ game, even dataminers didn't find them in the archivs of the game, how can they release so little for such a high price ?

Even if you disagree with me on all of these points or believe that the seasonal content (which i am actually enjoying a lot) makes it worth it, this rushed mess of a nonsensical of a confusing campaign and cut content is a slap in the face to anyone who was expecting the "infinity war of destiny", specially at such a steep price, and i cannot reccomend this expansion in the state it is right now to anyone interested in destiny 2.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll put as much time into this review as was done with the story and characters in Lightfall.

Story: Piecemeal at best. More questions than answers and at this point in time, the "Endgame" we find ourselves in, we should have been given answers. Instead of answers, we're left with the typical nebulous keywords and thus more questions. Hard fail.

Characters: Who cares about Nimbus and Rohan. No character development was ever made and with that, I could not care any less if both of them were killed off. All the main cast and company were shuffled in the background for the "seasonal" story... and even then, they only play very minor roles. Hard fail.

Loot: Another season, another set of 'x' weapons to chase after for the sake of simply one's lack of self control, aka: Oh my gawd new loot, I NEED to get them all! Reeee!" EPIC FAIL.

Season: Don't know and don't care. It's the exact same model we/ you all have played for the past 'x' years. It's a dead horse that should have been buried long ago. EPIC FAIL.

Sound: Typical

Music: It's good enough

Subclass: You are teasing with the new Strand throughout the boring and easily forgotten campaign. The penultimate mission has you running through a Metal Gear Solid VR mission to "hone" your skills, right before being tossed into the final fight. Epic fail. That should have been done long before the final mission. Long before. You don't spend a few days preparing for a battle, days before a battle. Utter nonsense.

Bugs: Typical mods + exotics disabled items, because no one could spare the time nor money to hire a quality assurance team.

Final verdict: There is no one, absolutely NO ONE left at that studio with vision. No one left who wants to try and be a pioneer in the industry, not anymore. There are a few shameless "Halo" style mission plugs and I am CERTAIN this was done to try and curry favor with whatever community members are left who still were rose-tinted glasses 24/7,. all year round, and never hold companies accountable for their egregious mistakes and errors.

This studio is tired, clearly,. Gone are the days of Bungie being willing to taking risks, trying something extraordinary, and telling compelling stories. How did we go from the likes of the With Queen to whatever drivel and sludge that is Lightfall? There are clearly people in positions of leadership who are positively NOT qualified to hold said positions, but I'm guessing they are there to help promote "The Message" that is being shoved down consumers throats at every turn in the media industry.

TL/ DR: For all you hold dear and true in this world, I beseech you, men and women of the world, SAVE YOUR MONEY.. This is positively the worst I've ever experienced since nearly the D1/ D2 vanilla experiences.\t\t\t\t\t

Cons:
* the story is terrible, lacks context in a lot of areas and the characters are bad

* the new area is "technically big" but mostly just linear and "on rails" -member the 80's guys... member?
