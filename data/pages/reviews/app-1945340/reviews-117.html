First and foremost, the campaign feels lackluster and uninspired. The story is predictable and unengaging, with few memorable moments or characters. Even worse, the campaign is incredibly short, clocking in at only a few hours of gameplay. It's clear that the developers prioritized quantity over quality, and the result is a rushed and unsatisfying experience.

The new activities and game modes don't fare much better. The new strikes and Crucible maps are forgettable, and the new seasonal event feels like a repetitive grind. The loot pool is also disappointing, with few exciting new weapons or armor sets to chase after.

Even the technical aspects of the DLC are problematic. Many players have reported glitches and bugs that make the game nearly unplayable at times. The performance is also inconsistent, with frequent lag and framerate drops.

Overall, the latest Destiny 2 DLC is a major letdown. It fails to deliver on its promises and offers little incentive for players to stick around. Unless you're a diehard Destiny 2 fan, it's best to skip this one and hope for better content in the future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAmidst a sea of people who for some reason only just now decided they need to know every single detail about everything in the Destiny universe, people who think misgendering characters purely on the basis they don't like them is based and funny (it isn't) and people who have some valid criticisms, I still recommend this DLC.

The Good:
1. Strand is extremely fun and opens up a lot of new gameplay elements to explore, more builds is always good, and the general fantasy of being super mobile and literally deleting people from existence is delivered well.

2. Neomuna is pretty, it also comes with solid new music, decent enemy variety with having the Taken, the Vex and the Cabal, and has a fair few new activities that are as of the current time of writing, extremely rewarding.

3. The Campaign is very fun on Legendary, with the possible only exception of Headlong if you aren't fully accustomed to Strand yet. The Witch Queen Campaign formula is truly the best thing that ever could have happened to Destiny campaigns.

4. New Mod system is streamlined very well for all players to understand, and the availability (read: not having to go to Ada every ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ week to hope you get the mod you want) is excellent. The lack of armour polarity is also very welcome.

5. Loadout System, UI updates and Guardian Ranks are all neat additions, in particular the ability to swap loadouts in activities is a godsend since DIM doesn't let you do that, the UI is smoother and generally nicer to look at, and Guardian Ranks just gives people something to do other than immediately leave to Orbit.

6. Exotics
New exotics are quite cool, I especially enjoy the class exotics, especially the Berserker Titan legs that turn it into an absolute CC monstrosity, as this is my current favourite class to play in the game right now.

The Mixed:

1. The Campaign story is... rushed, obviously, we knew that Covid is the primary reason Lightfall happened as The Final Shape ended up being greenlit to be the finale instead of this one, so we have ended up with a two-parter finale essentially. There are many issues as a result of this, stuff I personally don't really like going on is that Strand feels like something I should have been able to learn about and unlock separately from the Campaign, as I think it should have instead focused more on the actual conflict at hand. This would have given more time to develop characters, flesh out concepts that debatably need to be fleshed out, and would probably have led to a more consistently satisfactory experience. But, in my opinion, I do not NEED to know everything, I do not NEED to know exactly what the Veil is, I do not NEED to know why Strand exists and I do not need to know all of these things, I will FIND OUT eventually as this game has excelled at lore for a long time, but only recently has remembered how to have actual story. I will accept that it is not what everybody (including me) wanted or expected, but it is not CoO level, that is just total cope.

2. Build diversity took a hit with the new mod system, but I've been able to adjust and have fun with it, Stasis took a large hit in particular due to the removal of elemental shards which is a shame as I enjoyed Stasis a lot. I hear there are plans to add more mods so hopefully that addresses this issue.

The Bad:

1. No new models for legendary weapons is quite disappointing as a player who likes to use weapons purely based on aesthetic.

2. Neomuna itself is lifeless with no reason for me to care about the people in it at first glance, my opinion changed doing post-campaign content, but the fact all the regular citizenry is in the matrix basically means I have way less attachment to them than I do with the people of Earth, so my involvement feels weirder.

3. I don't like Bungie's monetization and wish this game didn't require you to basically own every xpac, it should be changed to work like WoW where all old xpacs basically are subsumed into the base game and the newest one is the only one you actually have to buy.
