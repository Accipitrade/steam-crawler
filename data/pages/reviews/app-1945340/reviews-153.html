\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's get this started with the campaign. The setting, sound design, and gameplay were all phenomenal, no surprises there. What truly shocked me was the narrative. Bungie had reached an all-time high with their narrative over the past year, with the Witch Queen campaign, and seasons like Haunted and Seraph having absolutely stellar stories. But Lightfall fell MASSIVELY short of the quality narrative I had come to expect. Bungie set the bar high, and their failure to meet that bar is one of the biggest fumbles in Destiny history.

I'll try to keep this spoiler free: The story focuses way too much on Strand and the Vex. When I initially heard Bungie say they wanted to avoid how stasis was released with Beyond Light, and wanted to incorporate the unlocking of Strand into the campaign, I took that to mean that we'd unlock the subclass early, with fragments and aspects unlocked as we play through the campaign. But really, it was just Beyond Light all over again, being teased with strand throughout, just with a far simpler method to unlock it fully after you complete the campaign. As a result of this, a lot of the campaigns focus is diverted from the Witness and the main story towards us learning Strand, with another huge part of the campaign focusing on the Vex, and let's be real, with what's going on in the main story, why should I care about the Vex right now?

Post-campaign content has been nothing short of great though. There's a lot to do, the activities are really fun, with only one real pain point for me, which is some of the quests requirements with patrols and public events. Do we really need to be focusing LITERAL HOURS of our time into doing patrols and public events in year 9 of Destiny? I get it, you want people to learn the patrol space and what it has to offer, but good lord, we don't need to have 3 quests all with significant patrol requirements involved to get a grasp of the new destination.

As for Strand itself, I think the overwhelming majority of people who are hating on it haven't fully unlocked Strand, and thus haven't begun buildcrafting and fully exploring all Strand has to offer. There is A LOT of potential with Strand, with a lot of that potential being end-game viability. I can understand the frustration from Titan mains, as it's yet another roaming melee-based super, but it's also (at least as of now), one of the better damage super options (when paired with Synthoceps). The other frustration I totally get (and share) is the grapple cooldown in PvE. I totally get having the cooldown be what it is for pvp, but in PvE, why was Bungie so conservative with the grapple frequency? We have to give up a grenade to use it. Sure, you don't consume the charge if you grapple to a tangle or a pre-made grapple point in the world, but I don't see the harm in significantly reducing the cooldown for grapple.

On the whole, I do recommend Lightfall. While the narrative for the lightfall story is severely lacking, the gameplay, art and sound, and loot is all top notch, and if you have enjoyed Destiny for the last 9 years, you'll enjoy it now.

7/10\t\t\t\t\t
