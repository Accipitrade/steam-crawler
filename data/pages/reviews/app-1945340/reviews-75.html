So first and foremost I am gonna list the negatives about this DLC that many. AND I MEAN MANY PEOPLE have a problem with. The story is meh. As in its fun to play but it still does not make up for the ♥♥♥♥ (i got censored...) storytelling. A lot of Destiny Vets are gonna be disappointed with it if they care about the story. Lost Sectors are 10x harder than they used to be. Mobs are deadlier in certain areas. And Cabal Ships can 1 shot dead you. (really weird but itll probably be fixed...i hope)

So yeah overall the DLC has made the game harder and has bad storytelling. Honestly The DLC itself is not worth 49.99. Cause it looks like a stop gap DLC to help give devs more time to work on the next DLC.

Pros: Rightfully the negatives can be a big turn off for some people. However I do have good news for new players and returning players. This is biased a little bit so its honestly up to you to agree or not.

Gameplay, even though it is harder in some areas it is REALLY FUN! Mechanics, Loadouts, Modifiers, and all that other good stuff is in a really good spot. The seasonal is also really good so far. Its only week 2 so it is subject to change. Strand is really good. PvP is at a decent spot as well. All I can say is if you know how it is then YA KNOW. For new people. It can be hard sometimes but its PvP so yeah. Its gonna be not so pleasant sometimes.

Overall: The DLC is not worth 49.99. Its just not. Especially compared to Witch Queen. Honestly the price should be 39.99 or lower. The story is hot dogwater that was left to evaporate into the sands of the sahara. The gameplay is incredible and the seasonal is looking to be good. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis may be short, but I am least putting in more effort to writing this review than bungie put into the story or design of this expansion.

- Strand grapple is gimmicky but in most cases worthless due to cd and taking up a grenade slot.
- Poor choice in design for light level and increasing difficulty.
- The strikes went from having multiple obvious difficulty levels in normal, hero, legend, master, grand master to basically taking legend out and giving us 2 master difficulties.
- Outdoor grind content is set to always be above your light level by around 10-20 levels regardless of your light level. This kills the core game play loop of do content > get light level increase > do content more easily.
- The overall difficulty of raids and strikes are about the same as before, however outdoor grind content is harder to kill and hits harder. You get dumped out of the LEGEND version of the campaign with 1770 gear into neomuna where the arbitrary trash for public events and random grind are all 1810+
- Threshers. Oh hey, I am all alone in a nice safe quiet spot trying to explore and then WHAM. Thresher missiles from across the world with pinpoint accuracy and instant 1 shot. If not the missiles, then the thresher turret with pinpoint accuracy and instant 1 shot.

I could go on about how poorly strand was incorporated into the campaign (oh look, you need to jump 2 feet in the air, better spend 30s in animations to get and lose strand for that jump that takes 2 seconds without it except you can't proceed until you grab the strand), how obnoxious the voice lines are, how empty the map is (of anything but overpowered trash), or how little seeming overall content there is so far but I have already spent more time and effort than this expansion deserves. I went from being overly hyped about the game to seriously doubting I will ever buy another expansion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNow that the raid is out I'm going to go completely against the grain here and recommend this DLC especially if you like Destiny. Mostly Negative is way too harsh in my opinion. If anything I think the best score for this is Mixed and I'll explain why. This review will have spoilers so if you're worried skip to the end for a TL;DR

Story:
Getting this out of the way immediately. The story is just simply not up to par with the story telling we have gotten since Beyond Light. They had such good momentum going into this expansion only for it to feel like filler. I was very excited to learn more about the Veil and The Witness and I still know nothing. There are some good parts though. The little bits we got of The Witness and Calus were excellent. If we had gotten more of that and less of learning Strand and Nimbus the story would be a lot better. Another bright spot is the post campaign story content. It was overall excellent and made me like Nimbus a lot more than the actual story did. Another point of contention I saw was Osiris. I think they did Osiris perfectly here. You may not like him or his character but he felt completely in character with how he was described in the lore and considering the circumstances he was under for the past 2 years.

Campaign itself:
The actual missions however? They were by far some of the most fun missions I've played in Destiny. I did the Legendary campaign with my 2 friends and we had an absolute blast. Getting and messing around with Strand during the campaign was so much fun and gave us a ton of laughs as we discovered all the fun quirks of the grappling hook. I would say playing though this campaign and ignoring the story is an overall better experience than Witch Queen. Even though I loved the Witch Queen campaign I have 0 desire to ever replay it on my other characters while with Lightfall I really wanted to go back and replay it just due to how much fun it was.

Neomuna:
Neomuna is one of the most enjoyable locations Bungie has put out in part to the difficulty changes. The enemy density on Neomuna is great and the new Terminal Overload activity is tons of fun. Partition is also another super fun new weekly activity and the new strike is one of the best ones in the game. The three new lost sectors are also some of the best in the game and Neomuna is just another beautiful destination overall. I hope more is added to it in the coming seasons.

Strand:
In my eyes Strand is a massive success. It is fun and powerful and I am still having a blast with it. I am a warlock main and I was worried about the threadlings and how good they would be. While their AI is lacking, with how many ways you can generate them I never felt hindered. Even with just two aspects it feels like there are so many different builds I can make use of and I am greatly enjoying using it. I have played a bit with the other 2 classes and cannot wait to dice into them more in the coming weeks. The one issue I have with Strand is the grapple itself. It really needs a cooldown reduction of some kind as I feel Bungie was a bit conservative here.

Exotics:
Bungie completely nailed them this year. Every exotic weapon feels strong and has a purpose. Counting Season of Defiance weapons, every single one feels powerful in its own way. Even the weakest of them, being the new LMG, has a purpose as a support tool in raids instead of using Divinity. Overall Bungie really improved on the exotics this expansion compared to the ones in the previous 2 expansions that have mostly been rotting in my vault.

Raid:
Bungie has once again delivered another banger raid. I have completed it in contest mode as I have for the last two expansions and this was by far the easiest contest mode. There was a lack of any sort of damage checks and instead relied on execution which was very refreshing. The raid loot is also pretty awesome with some of the most unique armor designs Bungie has ever produced. The weapons however are a noticeable downgrade from Vow's. They are still (almost) all really good, but nowhere near the power that Vow weapons had. The raid exotic (which I was lucky to get 2nd run) is extremely powerful though. It is genuinely a joy to use.

Season of Defiance:
It is only a few weeks into the season but so far it is really good. The new activity is another battleground, but it is really enjoyable still. The story is also very very good and I can't wait to see it unfold.

Mod system:
With this expansion Bungie completely overhauled the armor mod system to make it easier to understand at the cost of a lot of build diversity. Whether this is a good or bad thing varies, but I think it is better as it allows newer players to get a better understanding of how the system works. Even then we still did not lose much power and in some cases some builds got even stronger. In my eyes I think the system has more pros then cons and I hope they expand on it.

Difficulty:
Bungie did a massive difficulty overhaul with this expansion and it has been somewhat controversial to say the least. The main change is how power level is affected in certain difficulties. There are a lot of forced power deltas that don't allow you to overlevel an activity or set you to a certain power level below the activity no matter how high your power level is. I think this is a good change and is definitely needed after the insane power creep we got last year. Another great change is the changes to champions. Each element now has several ways on their own to deal with champs and have overall made them less of an annoyance and have opened up more builds. However, all of these changes has unveiled some issues. Mainly primary weapons feel pretty awful aside from SMGs and Sidearms. Bungie is already planning to address this later this season so hopefully they fix this soon. The other issue is Legend Lost Sectors. They are simply too hard for the average player now and take way more time than they used to. Bungie really needs to reexamine these as they are even worse now than they used to be.

Conclusion:
Overall I think this expansion has more hits than misses. The main pain point is obviously the story. This is now set and done and nothing can be done about it and we can only hope Bungie improves on it for The Final Shape. Aside from that this is another VERY solid expansion. It came with a great new destination, fantastic new exotics, another great raid, Strand, and a lot of good system changes that I hope Bungie can continue to improve with time. If you're at all a fan of Destiny you are really missing out by putting this campaign to the side because of a bad story. The story is the smallest part of an expansion and everything post campaign is great and makes me hopeful for The Final Shape.\t\t\t\t\t
