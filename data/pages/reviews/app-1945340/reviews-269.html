I noticed the reviews to be very mixed, and I agree with most of the bad reviews. I still rate this up though because I still enjoyed it whether it was because of the dlc itself or if it was playing with friends. You won't know wtf is going on in the story, with a lot of missing details about where anything came from and how it happened. There are also characters like Nimbus, who should def be replaced with a new one. I don't know what they were thinking making him. However, I found that the new strand mechanics are pretty good and fun to play with. Personally, I think they did a good job incorporating campaign missions that use these mechanics quite effectively to make it more fun to clear. The missions were pretty good, but it was way too short and some recycled content were used. And even if I want it longer, I don't mean more filler and more recycled content. I recommend that you play the campaign with two other people (the max #), since it made the game a lot harder and more enjoyable, especially if you have friends that are relatively new/not good, since it gives them quite a challenging experience. I had lots of fun messing around and I think even at this level of game level creation, you can work your way around to have fun. I do agree though that we expect more from a AAA game company, and its really sad that they do not respond well to what the community wants. In the end, I think if you have a group of people that play often and plan to do the upcoming raid day one, you should buy it now, but if you have time to think about it, I think it's a good idea to buy this dlc when it goes on sale for a much cheaper price. Hopefully, the upcoming content and raid will change our minds about this dlc.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDisclaimer, I have gone with recommending the game in this review as you have to either choose yes or no. I feel Strand is what made me decide on a thumbs up instead of a thumbs down, as it WILL bring great build crafting and entirely new ways to play and experience the game. I do however, really lean toward 50/50 and have made this review so you, the reader, can decide whether you may decide to make the purchase or not.

I have only played through the legendary campaign on Hunter, and haven't touched really any of the post campaign stuff yet.

Now, onto the review.

First and foremost, Strand is really cool. It feels nice, and is a breath of fresh air that we last saw with Stasis. Sure, the Light subclass reworks were great, they weren't exactly anything new. Being able to create a Tangle, and then Grapple to it without consuming your Grapple, and then pick it up, throw it through the air, and Grapple onto it AGAIN as it pulls you through the air is ever so satisfying!

However, realistically the Grapple is slightly useless, and 9 times out of 10 I will be using the Shackle Grenade, as you will get not only more combat effectiveness from that, you will also just get more build effectiveness from it. Why is this? The Grapple cooldown is just under 2 minutes, whereas the Shackle Grenade and Threadling Grenade are both just over 2 minutes. You are spending almost the same amount of time waiting for your Grapple to come back as you are waiting for your Shackle Grenade to come back. And because of this, I would much rather have something that can deal a slight amount of damage and Suspend enemies or create these cute little seeking Threadlings that deal a pretty decent amount of damage over just a pull in one direction.

The story, while I don't hate it as much as everyone else seems to, I do entirely agree with everyone's complaints. It started off great, with an incredible cutscene and and an incredible premise to follow. After the first 2 missions, however, it very quickly feels rushed and as there is no real pacing. You are just being forced into doing thing after thing with no real feeling that you are actually even doing anything whatsoever. Each mission almost just feels like a fetch quest, just without the fetching and more "go here and do this."

Nimbus, one of the new Cloudstrider NPC's is awfully annoying. Has an annoying voice, and feels like they were pulled directly out of a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ sitcom. They do nothing but throw out stupid, annoying and cringey quips every 10 seconds they are on screen. We never really "meet" them, we are just introduced to them and are immediately best buddies, no real connection is made, we never really learn about them nor do we do anything with them other than just see them work in cutscenes.

Neomuna, the new destination, while is pretty, is also a major disappointment. It is flat, has a lot of invisible barriers everywhere, and in many locations (almost always around "damaged" areas like rubble) has PS2 game textures. Which isn't a joke by the way, you can quite literally count all 256x256 pixels in the textures. It is empty, as this massive metropolis of as cyberpunk-esque city has no one around, and is instead replaced with these terrible holograms that just float statically and *vaguely* resemble a human head, shoulders and chest.

The new Legendary weapons added, which I have used very few of mind you, aren't bad. But they aren't good. They are just reskinned versions of the Moon weapons added in Shadowkeep. The Heavy Grenade Launcher is really the only redeeming one, as it is the first heavy Wave Frame style Grenade Launcher.

The Origin Trait that rolls on these weapons is similar to that of Quicksilver Storm, in that it creates these homing missiles that just automatically happen. You don't really notice these, and the few times you do, you just briefly see a little missile fly toward an enemy and do some damage. That's it, they aren't special. The new Strand energy type is GREAT to have on day 1, instead of waiting for an entire year to get it like we had to with Stasis, and the Hatchling perk is neat, and I can for sure see it getting used in builds, but that's all I can really say on the new weapons.

Overall Lightfall falls into the typical "overpromise, underdeliver" rhetoric that we are seeing a lot with games these days. Do I think it is worth buying? Maybe. I think Strand really is worth unlocking as it will open up new build crafting options. And this is only my perspective on one of the classes, in which is also the strongest. I hear and have seen Warlock looks strong, and Titan is as terrible as we all expected. Aside from Strand, I really think it's not entirely worth purchasing this DLC, as the story is terrible, explains nothing and is full of characters that make you wish they would stop talking.

It is on par with Beyond Light. Just imagine if Beyond Light had a Legendary Campaign option, and you pretty much get the general idea of how Lightfall feels.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNON SPOILER Review:
If you are looking foward to the begining of the end, well, here it is, a huge letdown. The build up from the last 10 years of lore focusing in the light and dark saga into, this? Witch queen was successful in its story and how it was written. What happened here? Now im very concerned for The final shape.
Anyway, this expansion comes with the usual strike, weapons to craft, a new location, future raid, the campaign and a new subclass. The whole package is pretty solid for people that enjoys the gameplay and legendary mode campaign. The new "strand" subclass is well made and very fun to use. New enemies, the tormentors, are a nice addition to the combat, they are challenging, not so annoying, or easy, or hard to kill (well sometimes).
So if you like destiny gameloop combat and grind, its a good expansion.
But why not recommending it?

SPOILER REVIEW:
I havent seen bad writing in tha game since year 1 d1 or curse of osiris. This expansion lore storywise was sold as the culmination of many seasons, a decade of content. The witness has come, the beginning cinematic was great but the arrival to Neomuna was lame (From here it goes downhill), we just get there, the reveal has no impact at all for how it was teased the season before as something important. So we need to find "the veil" a macguffin for the plot before the witness does, and Calus is the one stopping us this time as a disciple. Eventually we get new powers found in the way, that somehow counter Calus/witness machines that supresses known powers from guardians. So they cant counter strand because is new.

Before this expansion released they showed strand in the first trailer as the "new thing", i was worried they couldnt carry (an almost) closeure to the story, while exploring new things in between, adding this new subclass, a new location and new characters. Well now we know the final product is a mess.

Bungie has built this world with plenty of characters we care, and they decided to put these Cloudstriders in the Campaign. Rohan and Nimbus. Later in the story Rohan sacrifices himself for the plot, and i couldnt care less for a character i just met. Why not somone we already know? Also Nimbus is annoying as F.

Neomuna, is beautiful. But why do we need the plot here? Why not the last city? Maybe it was for marketing reasons giving destiny a fresh and hot image with something popular like "cyberpunk". But i couldnt care less again for a place i just arrived. We have protected the last city and earth for a decade and nothing happens. So again, no impact.

I felt the Campaign focus was more about knowing how to weild strand. I dont mind this, but i was looking foward to the "lightfall" as the title says and the witness intentions. Strand shouldnt be the focus of the story, and i felt like most of the campaign was. There was no tension, or desperation. It was predictible we will loose to the witness but the story presentation has a lot of "fun" and less serious tone as it should be in this stage of the story, a climax with tragedy hopefully, but nah. This is not infinity war levels of strugle, its rocky 2.

The campaign wasnt so long but it delivers fun for 5-6 hrs (after that theres more missions to do). Calus was wasted most of the story. But the boss fight wasnt bad.

Overall the content is ok. But is a huge letdown for the story perspective, it doesnt deliver of what they where teasing since 2013. A huge disconnection of what a climax should be. It felt rushed and tried to do a lot of things at once. The writers for the past seasons did a better job. Now im really worried for The final Shape, because this expansion didnt felt like a beginning for the closure in the light and dark saga, its like a 10% of what it does, and im getting rise of skywalker vives of mess for the next one.

6.5/10 [A Fun and Challenging Disappointment]

I hope bungie delivers during the year with the next seasons, so the story is more like a year thing and this dlc was just kind of a prologue. We will see.

Will i still play it? Of course, even if the story is a massive letdown its still fun, and there is much to do after. Usually the best parts of lore in the game are whats built around the campaign.\t\t\t\t\t
