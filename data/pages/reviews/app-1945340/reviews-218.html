\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOnce again the Destiny community is overreacting.... again. Time and time again the Destiny community does this, don't listen to them.

This expansion is great, not as good as Witch Queen, but still pretty great. Strand is fun as hell, the story is interesting and leaves a lot of mystery. Tormentors are scary, but were not as fun to fight as Lucent Hive. Still fun, just not as much. There's things left unanswered (which is good despite the communities crying about it). There's things to explore and discover in the lore that would have been just overwhelming with exposition dumping otherwise. There is somethings that could have been explained more, but its not too big a deal. Nimbus has a fun and energetic character that I've missed in this game. Despite the... colorful opinions on them by the community, the representation is very well done.
I would have liked to hear them refered to as they/them more earlier instead of it being mostly in text, but I can understanding Bungies hesitaion to put more forward considering the response to them so far. All the new guns feel great. And the final boss was genuinely fantastic. If it stopped there I'd give this expansion a 7.5/10.

However that being said the $20 character boost to skip the campaign after beating it once is completely unacceptable. Campaign skips should be free ESPECIALLY if you beaten the legendary campaign. Strand having to be unlocked individually for each class is hopefully a bug, if not is a misstep. Though the new way to unlock strand abilities is much better then the 3.0 reworks and Stasis. The lack of anchor points in Neomuna post campaign is strange. It really feels as those there shpuld be more yo allow you to use the grapple more. With that I'd drop it down to a 6/10.

Without those last three, which ultimately don't matter too much it's still a very good and fun. Strand is the most I've had in this game ever. It is insane how well strand turned out. I would still recommend this expansion. I would say wait for a sale though unless you're really into Destiny or Strand as at the end of the day it's still just more Destiny.

Separate but also connected is the season, which is included if you buy the expansion. It's still separate so I won't talk about it, but its something to consider if you're concerned about value.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have the Annual Pass edition but most reviews are going here so I'll go here too.

Spelling it out honestly, this DLC isn't that bad.

Does the story feel like a tacked on mess they made to push back The Final Shape? Yes. 100%. This is a real rival with Borderlands 3 in terms of disappointment on the story side. Nothing happens because nothing is explained, and nothing has impact for the same reason. Some characters are written well or have good moments, but there aren't a lot of good moments of dialogue. And yes, Neomuna also feels like a very lackluster world zone, but it's still fun to jump around and explore which is kind of all the other world zones are good for at the end of it all anyway so...meh?

But this is also still Destiny 2. If you liked the overall game before, you'll probably still like it now. Beyond the story, there's new missions, new exotics, an updated sandbox, a 100% positive change to the armor mod system once you hit the learning curve, good post-campaign content, and seasonal content to enjoy as well. This is still the Destiny a lot of people loved before, and at it's core it's still a good game.

Did Lightfall fumble the ball? Yes, absolutely. Story-wise and in small ways elsewhere it feels like we've taken two steps back. But does that mean it can't still provide the same hours of excitement, fresh experiences, and good times? Of course not! We don't know if Lightfall will go down as one of Destiny's weakest years, it isn't off to the best start, but at the end of the day it will still be the Destiny we love to play and love to hate.\t\t\t\t\t
