\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'd say that lightfall is about a 6/10.
For what it's worth, strand is fun and by far the best part of the expansion.
Where it really falls through is the campaign and post-campaign missions. While the missions themselves are fine and fun to play through, the storytelling is atrocious to the point that I refuse to believe this was written by the same people who turned out witch queen just over a year ago. Next to nothing is explained in the campaign, so while stakes are apparently high there's no reason for the player to get invested in the story. The entire reason you go to neptune is to stop the witness from getting a maguffin, but then you learn absolutely nothing about said maguffin over the course of the story. It's just "the witness wants the veil so we have to stop him" at the very start, and "the witness wants the veil so we have to stop him" at the end.

The other issue with the campaign is it's obsession with strand. A good 50% of the campaign is just you trying to master your strand powers because calus has a second (completely unexplained) maguffin that makes you not able to use your super. The origins of strand are never really explored and the necessity of it is never really driven home, and it just feels like it's tacked on to extend the length of the campaign.

As for the post-campaign, it's just region chests and patrols with one actual mission. Credit where credit is due, the implementaion of strand is good and was way better than stasis'post-campaign. After completing the campaign, you get your subclass, all of the strand grenades, both of your aspects, and one fragment. All you have to do to fully unlock strand from there is use it. As long as you do things with strand equipped, you'll get strand meditations that allow you to purchase more fragments.

I'm giving lightfall a recommend as I believe it doesn't deserve it's current rating of 33%, but make no mistake: this expansion isn't worth the listed price. If you want to get it, get it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTl;DR; Bungie missed the mark with a sub-par expansion (so far)

Off the bat, this is not Bungie's greatest work. It has problems but the changes made to the difficulty and build crafting have both brought some desperately needed freshness and along with some quality of life improvements this is so much better than it is being given credit for.

Yes the story side of things has somewhat fallen off the rollercoaster, the lore isn't adding up, the new characters introduced feel two dimensional, Osiris though is being proper Osiris - a pompous, disagreeable, argumentative, over opinionated a**e just as he's been described over the years without ever really living up to the rep. Amanda Holiday seems to be a bit all over the place and the dialog in activities just grates but frankly that has always been true, it is just which of the core characters is sounding terrible

One of the biggest disconnects is the difficulty, for casual players this is now a challenge. Even just aimlessly looking for targets to complete a patrol is dangerous in places. All of the prior build crafting has been shaken up thanks to the new armour mods and when you mix in the new strand powers it struggles. Not so much if you've got a mild build that doesn't lean heavily on stacked and aligned mods, but if you're all in on a power trip build then the moments when you're forced to use strand can feel like a major step back instead of the power trip that Bungie obviously intended

The hardest part of this is change, not just a beloved character dying or old familiar content being locked away. There are some fundamental changes that make this feel different and the problem with change is that it upsets people, in some cases quite badly judging by some other reviews. How opinion changes over the next few months will be telling\t\t\t\t\t
