\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 Lightfall is good from a gameplay perspective. However, it's a massive disappointment from all other angles after the phenomenal Witch Queen narrative and is one of the sole reasons why this is getting a Not Recommended.

TL;DR: Story is muddled with too many questions and pretty much no answers with Bungie saying that you need to spend $10 and wait 3 months for some answers to the big MacGuffin and the 2 guns shown in the preview image. The new raid feels more like a 6 player dungeon. Cannot recommend to new and returning players at full price. Strand is fun but needs tuning. Only buy if you're committed to Destiny 2 and don't want to be left behind.

The story (spoilers): The Witness finally makes its move and the Black Fleet arrives in the system to surround the Traveller. The Traveller lets out a beam in an attempt to destroy the Witness and fails, then becomes ensnared by Pyramid ships surrounding it. Amanda Holliday is MIA after her shuttle crashes and Calus is sent out after the Veil. Osiris and the Guardian tail the Cabal fleet to Neptune where a vibrant city was concealed while also running in to two things: an anomalous energy that gives the Guardian the power of Strand, and two Cloudstriders named Rohan and Nimbus. After regrouping near the Hall of Heroes, the Guardian, the Cloudstriders, and Osiris have to plan their next attack to stop Calus from getting to the Veil. After running into unknown suppressors that block the Guardian from using supers and hearing Calus is getting close to securing the veil, the Guardian hi-tails it to Calus's flagship to destroy the Radial Mast. However, Calus was expecting them and locks them into a fight they can't win, until Caiatl bails them out and flees from the ship before darkness consumes them. After this failure, Osiris berates them and they need to learn more about Strand. For now, there's an issue with the Vex attacking the CloudArk, so they have to blow through a ship to stop the Vex from stealing power from it before falling into a Shadow Legion trap and having to escape. The Hypernet Current strike occurs, where it's told there's Taken inside the Vex Network again, and this time Nezarec shows his hand that he lives. Then, we find where the Radial Mast is being transported and the Guardian goes with Rohan to intervene and clear out the Cabal defending it, until they become exhausted and can't use Strand to destroy the mast. Rohan then sacrifices himself to destroy the Mast. After a Pyrrhic Victory, Osiris pushes the Guardian harder than ever to learn Strand by defeating Vex. After this, they have to make a final stand as Calus launches an assault to secure the Veil and breaks through their defenses, leading to a final showdown with the Guardian vs Calus. In the end, Calus is killed by the Guardian but then Ghost is possessed by the Witness and links the Veil, causing the Witness to harvest its power to open up a portal on the Traveller, with it and the Black Fleet going inside.

Now for major criticisms about the story: It feels like a cut off prologue. With how many questions are brought up, we're given practically nothing to go on whatsoever yet the game keeps pushing the idea we should know something about the Veil. What is the Veil and the Radial Mast? What do they do? All we're told about the Veil and the Radial Mast is that the Veil powers the CloudArk and it gives Ghost the same feeling as the Traveller . The ending is still fine as it brings up a mystery with some parts known, but if you introduce two very important things with absolutely no background to what they do other than befuddling hints, they feel more like MacGuffins than powerful objects. Bungie has come out to say that season 21 will explain some things about the Veil, but why can't we have gotten at least SOME information now instead of a $10 promise?

The Character writing: Rohan and Nimbus are supposed to be buddy cops, the vet and the rookie. However, one is written well and feels like a character with some history and the other character is Nimbus. Especially how It's telegraphed heavily that Rohan dies, especially with an interlude from him saying "Cloud Striders die in 10 years and I'm about 9 years in, I feel like it's almost the end for me" it defeats the purpose of his character as a whole. Nimbus on the other hand feels extremely flat and a lot of line delivery sounds extremely wrong. While Nimbus being Non-Binary is not a bad thing, the design of the modulator to distort their voice that makes their previous gender ambiguous which makes their lines garbled, where Rohan has none of those issues whatsoever. Nimbus also tries to make a bunch of "heroic" one-liners and fails horribly due to poor writing, character direction, and the aforementioned garbled voice changer. Even their grief over Rohan's death is only apparent in two instances: right after Rohan's death, and in the exotic mission for Deterministic Chaos where Nimbus trips the Black Garden's alarms and decides to rush in on a hint Rohan left behind and almost got pulped by Vex, despite eulogizing about how "I'm doing this for Rohan" or when Osiris criticizes their rashness by responding to him with, "You don't know what it means to be a Cloud Strider". It makes Nimbus feel flat and annoying due to a lack of character growth occurring throughout the 7 hours or so you go through in the main story.

Visuals and Music: Bungie always delivers in these departments and knows what they're doing. Neomuna feels like an 80's rendition of the future and I'm all for it. The score for this is also very well done, especially with the new raid. New armor and shaders look compelling. However, as a massive criticism: The only new weapon models are the Raid weapons (and even then they're old models with an Alien barrel, akin to Borderlands 3) and there's still no new core game mode armor (Vanguard, Gambit, Crucible). This cheapens the experience as the lack of new models makes it feel like old guns with a new coat of paint rather than something unique. If you're looking at the main page and wondering about the Hand Cannon and the Grenade Launcher in the preview image: They're not in the game as Bungie confirmed they're coming next Season despite it being on the page for Lightfall itself, not a $10 season that requires the Deluxe edition or a separate purchase.

The Raid: It's both disappointing and too easy. World's First was 2.5 hours and had the most contest clears ever, even more than all previous Day Ones combined. The entire raid can be beaten with Thunderlord and minimal buildcrafting with Dungeon-level mechanics behind it. In essence, it still looks cool and has a great soundtrack and loot, but the raid is far too easy and it's another massive let down after one of the best raids of all time last year, Vow of the Disciple. If you play Destiny 2 for raiding, it's still fun, but it's far too easy to be considered "challenging".

Strand is fun but feels overtuned and will likely get nerfed in the future, but the idea of using an unknown force to ensnare enemies feels phenomenal.

As an overall: The story needed some things to be explained properly, or even partially, for it to not feel like a mess. Nimbus as a character is far too flat and has a confusing voice direction due to distortion and extremely odd line delivery. New Weapons being reskins of the Moon weapons feels creatively bankrupt, especially with Bungie promising previously that we'd get new armor and weapon models every year with yearly expansions. New Raid is far too easy and shows a further decline in difficulty despite Bungie's intentions of making content more engaging. Overall very disappointing after the success of Witch Queen.

5/10. Not bad, but is awfully disappointing compared to the Witch Queen. Buy on sale only.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall is okay, not great. Score: 3/10

Def a downgrade in Mod System and World from Witch Queen. The ending of Lightfall might be the worst in D2 history, then the characters after make no sense at all about what happened.

The campaign is made in a way to annoy you, not challenge you. Its meant to do this IMO so you buy the campaign skip on the other two characters "it worked for me" btw. Generally though the campaign is fun, Strand is cool and some cutscenes def make you want to keep playing, the last cut-scene of the campaign though is sponsoring this review since it was terrible. Like Bungie spent 10 years for this ending of Lightfall to be awful, seriously a 1/10 ending and the motivation to keep player after it has dropped significantly.

The mod system is awful, the old system from witch queen was better. Mod descriptions also don't match what they actually do lol. When a mod says "ALL" it should mean "ALL". It does not lol, only certain things are effected and it seems intended not a bug.

Making the game harder does not exist, like I said its just annoying not hard. You are not killed by enemies but rather by falling cabal pods, ammo that goes through walls, or random one hit attacks from other sources. The problem with this is that, its not avoidable. You can not react, you just die.

The positives of Lightfall are minimal, Destiny 2 is a great game but this is 100% a step back instead of a step forward.

Hopefully someone else gets some enjoyment out of it, as a long time fan I did not. Wasted 8 hours of my life for some ♥♥♥♥ ending\t\t\t\t\t
