\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhilst the story is EXTREMELY lacking and poses more questions than answers, and the pacing being all over the place, the pros outweigh the cons MASSIVELY. Most of the reviews on the dlc are first impressions of the story (which i do understand a lot of people buy the expansions specifically for the campaign) but considering we haven't even seen the raid yet is a bit too fast to jump the gun.

Overall this expansion is the most fun I've had in destiny period! Strand is an extremely fun subclass to play around with and is good enough that you can use it in raids aswell, unlike how stasis is very underwhelming in endgame activities. Suspend is an extremely strong ability versatile in almost every situation. Not to mention the (almost) Forsaken level of QoL improvements to the game and FINALLY the game becoming extremely accessable to new light players!

If you haven't gotten into destiny because its overwhelming, now's the time. With the guardian rank system the game will teach you and with ingame loadouts, simplified buildcrafting (which to some is worse but that's personal preferance) and, in the future, ingame LFG, never been a better time to get into or BACK into destiny.

The patrol space is BEAUTIFUL with a public event that feels like a new activity instead of just do an objective and get glimmer, and lost sectors that have alot of effort put into them (not just 2 rooms and a boss anymore) and the IMMENSE amount of hidden lore around its fantastic.

First impressions were not great for the expansion, but this is by far one of my favourite expansions not because of story, that still sits with Witch Queen, but because the game just became so much fun with the QoL changes and Strand.

EASILY recommend it to anyone who really enjoys movement and just having alot of fun!\t\t\t\t\t

Strand is very interesting and has a lot of capabilities for different builds and traversal like this as a Titan in my opinion hasn't been this fun since the Twilight Garrison days of Destiny 1. I do wish with Strand though we got a few more exotics armour pieces with perks designed to fit Strand as I believe there's only 1 per class at the minute, I guess future Seasons and DLCs will bring more. Will be interesting to see how this fairs next to the current Light subclasses in the long run.

Now onto Neomuna, quite like this destination in terms of colours and style, however I wish there was a little more height to it, even though its a city, it still feels quite grounded. The post story quests and content is definitely a good aspect to building more on Neomuna and its inhabitants. I'm hoping a future Season explores a little more into some of the story of Neptune.

Campaign: there's been a lot of comments on the campaign and the story and unfortunately this is by far one of the biggest negatives of this DLC. Going from Season of The Seraph and The Witch Queen I'm not sure where it went wrong, it definitely feels rushed and I even tried to make it last a few days so I could understand / enjoy it more, but even then I'm still left with so many questions, but I feel the campaign should have answered those before the end. By no means am I saying its terrible as it does have some highlights, but I just wish there was more explaining and spoiler alert, more Witness. I guess more will be explained in the future Seasons to come.

Ultimately I like the DLC in terms of everything else, as a long time Destiny player some really good quality of life updates. I just hope the next few Seasons tell a good story and make it all make more sense, despite I feel the campaign should have done that. The Final Shape is next, hoping Bungie takes most of the good feedback and makes it the greatest DLC yet as it seems to be the finale.\t\t\t\t\t

-It plays real good! Strand is a great time, the campaign missions are fun and varied, the new activities are entertaining and the open world activity has much higher QOL than D2's earlier attempts to do similar stuff.
-The music is great, but it always is.
-Bungie is finally taking steps to address quality of life and it feels good. The strike pool has been bolstered with battlegrounds from previous seasons so it's less mind-numbingly repetitive, the new mod system is fun and straightforward, artifacts give perks instead of mods so you don't need to waste slots to kill champions with legendaries, there's a lot of good ♥♥♥♥ here.

Now the stuff that's neither here nor there.
-For an 80s inspired tech metropolis, Neomuna is kinda bland. It starts you off looking out over the city from high above which briefly tricks you into thinking it might have some significant verticality but it doesn't. I like getting around it, I don't mind looking at it, but comparing it to the marketing, it feels like it's really missing something. Why is it so grey? Where's the colour and the life and the density of detail? Why don't we get to ambush enemy groups from rooftops in the slashing rain? Neomuna will occasionally hit you with a blast of one hue that fills the entire screen but that's not the same as being colourful, that just means it has colour. The only colour I can remember is the eye-searing orange-red in one part of the zone and it makes me sad.
-Destiny 2 is still missing a lot of key MMO infrastructure. They love their pretty minimalist UI but to keep it, they've refused to integrate basic social features that even more equivalent games like Warframe added years ago. This isn't a mark against Lightfall specifically but that the game lacks things as simple as a 'who have I shared zones with recently' list to this day is just a little bit insane.

Time to roast! Some spoilers ahead, but trust me, you don't care.
-Who wrote this ♥♥♥♥? I played Witch Queen first when I picked up the game again and was genuinely impressed by the quality of the character work on display but Lightfall is not just full of holes, but the things it does manage to execute are done with some truly hamfisted bluntness. Instead of taking things that have already been established and playing with them, like a good conclusion should, it introduces new things left and right (what the ♥♥♥♥ is a veil?) and only resolves half of them. And that's not even touching on the appalling dialogue. There is truly too much to cover here and it's all bad.
-The art design has gone down the toilet this expansion. I touched on this a bit earlier with how dull Neomuna looks, but all the new characters look like absolute trash. Rohan, Nimbus, even the much-vaunted Witness range from uncomfortable to behold to genuinely laughable. Looking at the Witness shouldn't be funny, but it has broccoli for a head. Something has gone very wrong.
-What the hell happened to the new legendary weapons? There are new weapons in the marketing, but the legendaries we actually got are just hilariously ugly reskins of old models.

And finally: Nimbus. Let's talk about Nimbus.
Nimbus is insulting, in every respect.
I'm non-binary, and was excited to get some canon representation in such a big IP. I would have been willing to look past a lot. But instead of a character, I got a bundle of surfer dude tropes stuffed into a body out of some artist's horny fantasy. Their face is closer to that of a porcelain sex doll than a real person, and it's attached to this bizarrely gigachad body, complete with an excessive number of far-too visible veins and a giant codpiece. It's like the art team were so determined to make Nimbus 'look non-binary' instead of look like a person that they took the two most extreme ideas they had and just mashed them together. It makes me feel like the design team sees people like me as sex objects and not as human beings.
But I can't rant about their appearance and (lack of a) character forever, because I need to talk about their voice.
Bungie hired an extremely talented non-binary voice artist to do Nimbus' lines, who's previously voiced Athena in Hades among other things. This was a classy call. But apparently, Bungie decided that Marin Miller's lines didn't sound 'non-binary' enough, because they've butchered Marin's delivery by very obviously pitching it down. Imagine having the audacity to get a non-binary actor to voice your non-binary character, then adjusting the pitch of their voice to sound more androgynous, and in a way that damages the quality of the lines to boot. Marin does quality work, but Nimbus is truly unpleasant to listen to, entirely as a consequence of Bungie's post-production tampering, and that's just sad.

I've heard people suggest that Lightfall was a rushed middle step to give Bungie more time to work on The Final Shape. Considering it has a story that mostly just left me confused, populated by characters I can barely tolerate, I truly hope that read is true. Lightfall is such a massive dive in writing quality from Witch Queen it's not even funny, and it leaves me deeply concerned about the state of Destiny 2's narrative going forward.\t\t\t\t\t
