\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny is and has been on a decline, for years. A chunk of the revenue is probably going to Marathon, which make sense from a business perspective, but it sucks as a player.

TLDR Bungie said "Ey yo we gotta make some more moneys, uuuh, Destiny is reaching the end of its lifespan, let's drive in-game purchases and artificially increase playtime, and keep using the model that already works. Hide it? Why would we hide that we're doing this? They've been playing for like 8 years, they'll keep buying it."


The current gameplay loop is:
- Spend $100 on the yearly expansion and 4 seasons
- Play through the campaign that doesn't actually tell you anything about what's going on, so you need to rely on Byf or Myelin or another lore channel to tell you what's actually going on. (If they can even figure it out)
- Play through a drip-fed story of talk to person, do activity, talk to person, do other activity, talk to person, talk to hologram of that person, wait a week. (Even though we clearly have instantaneous communications from Earth to Saturn, we still have to physically go to a place and talk between missions.)
- Realize that some parts of this grindy game were lengthened to clearly increase playtime (See Lake of Shadows rework)
- See that the seasonal model is the *exact same* as 3 years ago. THREE YEARS. (Season track, season exotic weapon, 6-man activity, alternate smaller activity, drip fed story, same size loot pool, 1-2 NPCs as the main focus/vendor. It's a fill in the blank model.)
- When an event comes around, if you want everything from it, spend $10 on a few extra goodies including a shell, shader, and I don't even know. And THEN spend more money getting the armor sets from Eververse.
- Eververse.
- And the freebie armor sets will always look like literal garbage, while the ones behind a paywall look pretty nice. We got our first symmetrical hunter arms in a WHILE and they're locked behind Season of the Deep.

(And yes I'm aware that this game is meant to be grindy, and in-game shops are a standard practice with these games, but still. The priority for new content creation resources are clearly going towards Eververse and *not* towards making the game better.)\t\t\t\t\t
