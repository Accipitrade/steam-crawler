\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall is an enjoyable and promising start to a new year of Destiny 2.

The new Strand-based subclasses are interesting and capable. The new patrol destination is more challenging, with resilient and more numerous enemies. It is a refreshing rebuttal of the inane notion that PVE 'landscape' play must be pushover gameplay, and I hope they continue with this stance going forward. Power-level caps in higher-end activities and increases in ability cooldowns mean more gunplay and less ability-spam.

The campaign is comparable to the previous Witch Queen expansion's story in size and progress: it's meant to be an introductory chapter to a narrative that will lay itself out as the Seasons progress. Story tangents follow from the campaign in the form of side quests, while the main beat continues with the Season of Defiance, which has already begun.

If you're willing to improve your game to enjoy the increased general difficulty, willing to forgive Bungie's politics seeping into the game (including their decision to portray a prominent new character as belonging at some stupefying band of the neurological spectrum) and willing to improve your game to enjoy the increased general difficulty, I think you'll find Lightfall a worthy expansion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOkay, so I am now writing this up; this is a complex review because I have come to Love and Hate this game, there are a few things I have found suitable for this expansion, but it does not at all outweigh the bad of it, so let's get this ball rolling.

Strand:
Well, where would you like me to begin? Well, for one, their focus here was implemented poorly, i am sure a lot of you have played the story by now, and they made too many missions regarding this power, yes it is enjoyable to play with, it could use some buffs to bring it in line with our current buffed light powers because at this point there is no reason for anyone to use this in raids and or anywhere else precisely. For crowd control, that is a stretch. That whole 80s-style training montage with Osiris or sounds like an old grumpy 90 year old who has lost his will to live and yells constantly. was done poorly, but what they should have done was just two missions or one dedicated to mastering strands, they played this sub-class up to be a significant factor but I found myself still using my light sub-classes.

While it is fun to use in patrol, I do not see myself making any builds around this. Needs work but fun.

The Veil: well, can anyone remotely close enough tell me what this is and why I should give a damn. throughout season 19, Osiris made it a huge deal but then came the time for it to be front and center, but then we get a tom and Jerry chase. Where I was left with no answers from season 19, hell, they dubbed this whole expansion our "Infinity war" to the light and dark saga. But it did not do that; from season 19 to lightfall, we still need to learn what the Veil is. And why I even cared. It was hard to focus on this story even with the horrid surfer dude talking to me; Nimbus is a pointless and bland character who, yes, I believe to be a rip off of our beloved cayde for the sake of lightening what was supposed to be a dark tone of apocalyptic events. But those events never occurred, and we were left with a light-hearted ending.

I felt cheated by this ending, the witness who at this point, I am 100% rooting for, won for purposes he won, and we lost, but did we lose? what a giant ball that didn't do ♥♥♥♥ to help us or the Eliksni or the Krill aka the Hive nor Cabal. So we lost what happened to the traveler? did it die? Why do we have our light powers still? shouldn't our ghosts have died? Why didn't Osiris or someone more critical die instead of Rohan? Coming from the Witch-Queen, which was so good, I could not help but feel as if a lot of those who worked on that expansion jumped ♥♥♥♥ and left that following year of it being out. This is the only logical conclusion. Could the Vex help me figure this out; why is Lightfall so horribly done while Witch-Queen is so good?

However, from its incoherent story writing, we did get some tremendous exotics and a fun seasonal mission for the Vexcalibur, which I don't quite understand was part of the seasonal pass. it had nothing to do with Mara and ♥♥♥♥. Hoping the raid is good at least, however now Bungo is aware of their poor storytelling and vows that in the season of the deep and future ones after that, they will explore more on what the veil is, well we should not have had to wait when this whole expansion was about that and did nothing to inform us the PLAYERS. Of what it is. 49.99 usd and I felt cheated. I cannot give this a good rating, or I cannot recommend a new player to invest, save their money, and watch youtube videos on this DLC and save for D3. If we ever get one, that is.

I give it a 4; QoL does not factor in, as everyone who owns this expansion or not gets access.

NIMBUS: ♥♥♥♥, just pure cringe ♥♥♥♥. Cayde-6 is rolling in his grave. Those like me have been on this journey since the beginning back in the beta era, hell, even the Halo era of Bungie has been on this journey, and now we must finish this story. But Final Shape will be my final destiny investment.

Any future investment will be made after releases so I can scout. To make sure it is worth buying or not. Bungie and Destiny 2, I Hate you, but I love you!
