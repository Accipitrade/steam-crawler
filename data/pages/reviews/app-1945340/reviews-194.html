\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI wish I could say that this expansion was one of the best Bungie released so far, but unfortunately reality is far from that.

Starting with the good and ending with the bad, some things that stood out for me:

1. Music was impeccable and so were the sound effects.
The members of the sound team proved over and over that they know what they're doing, giving us once again some of the best results.

2. The new environment/destination, more specifically its design.
They wanted to offer players a neon city, and that they delivered. It can feel a bit empty sometimes considering that this is a place where humanity supposedly thrives, but all in all the main goal was achieved.

3. UI/UX.
Having more info in the game and more options to work on our builds was a good addition.

And now the bad:

1. Story/campaign.
It heavily lacks depth. It feels rushed and it feels forced. You are indeed being given an objective right at the very beginning of the campaign, which is finding a "veil", but little to no context is provided regarding said objective throughout the whole campaign. On top of that, you learn nothing new about your antagonist (Witness) and his plans and nothing new about your protagonist (Traveler).

2. Strand (new subclass) and its forceful insertion into the campaign.
Though fun to use sometimes, the campaign focused more on the player learning how to use Strand than on the main story, making this expansion feel just like an overhyped tutorial.

3. The new destination vendor/NPCs.
If Bungie’s main goal was to create expendable characters having no tangible impact on the way you experience the game, or characters that look completely unreliable when it comes to addressing the story’s main conflict, they successfully achieved that.

This being said, you decide if this expansion is right for you or not.\t\t\t\t\t
