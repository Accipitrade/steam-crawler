\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo lets rate the expansion on more than just one aspect of it, rather than jumping on the bandwagon of, 'Story Bad so its all bad"

Story:

It is Undeniable how BAD the story really is. Cringey Character's like Nimbus, Strand tutorial for 7 hours, Confusing and unexplained idea's and reasoning's for why certain things happen, For someone like me who doesn't mind the Story, but who does still enjoy reasoning and logic, this element is 5/10. For those who REALLY enjoy the Story its probably a 2/10. The give and take of Strand combined with annoying, tedious, frustrating "conclusions" makes for a very lack-luster ending making you wonder was it all worth it.

Location:

NeoMuna is rich with amazing views, a destination that is MUCH more engaging than any other in the game's history, there is simply much more to do on the Planet of Neptune, and i think people allow their judgement of the story, to lead them to the premise of, "Why are we even here when we didn't get the answers, and story we needed". That alone does not take away form the fact that NeoMuna is the best location in the game right now in terms of engagement, activities, and secrets. Even the damn lost sectors are a step above (We can now game in-game in a arcade). While Witch Queen comes close, the changes to Enemy density, light level changes in certain area's, and overall CONTENT in those area's NeoMuna takes the cake from a, "there is literally more to do" perspective. It stands at a 8.5/10 for me (just because something is the best doesn't mean it cannot be improved).

Game-Play & Strand:

With the amazing changes to build crafting, and the simplicity of it being in-game, its a big feature and a massive quality of life improvement to the game. It enables more game-play, and less time making a build. The gameplay as a result is fun, rewarding, and currently the power fantasy is met. I would argue it is too easy at the moment with the introduction of Strand. Strand is pretty damn good, and has defined itself as one of the best sublcasses for all classes in the game. Again it does seem a tad too strong, but it is definitely had time and effort put into the design, to where you can make fun and interesting builds within it than are also going to be viable in the end-game. (PVP it is currently breaking which is nothing new since every new subclass and reworked subclass has broken crucible on release).

Final Section: Is This Rating Warranted?

It definitely stems down from, do you enjoy the story. If so, I would say maximum class this as a Mixed Review as the other elements discussed here, easily make up for it. The Raid that launches with this DLC is not released at this time - so hopefully some answers are resolved and discovered there for those who care about it that much. The same people who write these reviews about it being "Really Bad", still play the game, still grind the game, and won't ♥♥♥♥♥ about anything BUT the story because there is nothing else to complain at as all the other elements are good, if not great.

TLDR; Story is bad, absolutely lazy writing, bad characters, totally get the frustration in that one. Game-play is as good as it always has been, even better with the new Strand Subclass and in-game Loadout system. Location is content wise, the best, its well thought out, and optimised well for game-play. Again No raid at this time, so these negative reviews are exclusively only valid towards the story (or lack of) and the narrative. If it is something you care about, then don't play it.
But if you are a average D2 Player, you'll play it, either like it or dislike it, complain, and then still play the game because Seasonal Content carries this game anyway, which everyone seems to forget how Mid Witch Queen's Seasons were, with rehash on every seasonal vendor, and seasonal activity, and exotic missions, and it also brought Season of The Plunder - probably one of the worst seasons in the HISTORY of the game.

We are a week in, give it time, your nightmares about the bad narrative will fade away when the seasonal content is flowing and the raid is actually released. Jesus Christ

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who develops and supports software solutions for a living at a fortune top 100 company, I am sympathetic to the pains that developers go through. With that said, I could eat a can of alphabet soup and s#!t something better than this expansion. At my job if someone developed something this horid, they'd be fired on the spot.

The number of bugs I found in just 24 hours, the incoherent mess of a 'story' if you can even call it that, the lack of any length to the content, the largely empty world zones that were added, and the unenjoyable mechanics leaned on heavily throughout the expansion makes this an overwhelming disappointment.

IGN gave it a 5/10 and caught flak for being "harsh". Honestly for a $50 expansion this is a 2/10 product at BEST, and that's being generous because the quality of life changes that were added actually were a huge improvement and very well done. The game play in the missions had moments that felt glorious. There are areas of the zones that are gorgeous. The problem is that everything surrounding these little gems is a heaping pile of dog s#!t, and the quantity of the bad is such an overwhelming amount that it completely stifles the pieces that are actually good.

Shame on Bungie. I feel embarrassment on their behalf from how incredibly bad this is. I'm also angry that they squeezed out this turd and actually released it to allow their stench of failure waft across the world.

If you read this Bungie, you need to do significantly better. Witch Queen was pretty awesome. This was absolute garbage.\t\t\t\t\t
