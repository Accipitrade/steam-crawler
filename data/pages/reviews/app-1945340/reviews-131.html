\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLet's address the elephant in the room. The story was a mess. It introduced too much, raised more questions, and provided us with no context necessary to be able to understand what is going on. That being said, the content of the expansion outside the story has been astounding.

Neomuna: The location is very fun to explore, and interact with. The public events are challenging, however are incredibly fun once you have enough people in the location with you. The set pieces are really pretty, and navigating it has been a delight.

Strand: An amazing subclass. We're still in the early stages of it, and builds are a little one tone. That being said, it's such a fun time to unravel enemies, and send out threadlings to do your work. I hope it doesn't get nerfed too hard in the future, because I love how powerful it feals.

Build Crafting: The current iteration of the mod system is definitely an improvement over what we had. We finally have loadouts in game, and it's so simple go quickly reconfigure a build for niche purposes. The current selection of mods is a little lacking, however I expect Bungie to improve on, and iterate over this system. It's still new, and even so, it's enjoyable.

Bungie has said that the story will unfold through this year. I wish we had it unfold in the campaign, but I'm willing to be patient. After all, it's still a very fun game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo be clear, I was hyped for Lightfall ever since they announced it a few years ago with the marketing advertising a campaign that would wrap up or at least start bringing a saga that I and others have followed for 10 years to a close. After playing the story that was not the case, it just felt like filler for the next DLC, and if it were just that I wouldn't have too much of a problem with Lightfall as a whole. What broke it for me is repetitive and boring gameplay, a poorly designed subclass that doesn't serve much purpose in the game outside of a bug that lets you do a lot of damage which will likely be patched in a week once the raid comes out, and seasonal content that is a checklist of doing the same mission several times for a week, that has so far been my whole experience with Lightfall and given the 50 dollar price tag, I expected something I could at least enjoy but so far all I have done is play for a bit at a time, get bored, and then leave to play something else. If you expected this to be the a big expansive DLC that has a good story, this is not it. The only value I see in buying this DLC is the seasonal loot, the raid, the ability to participate in the broken crucible meta, and the new strike which is actually kind of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
