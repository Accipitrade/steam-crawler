\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, this was a big fat disappointment of a DLC. I've been playing Destiny as a franchise since the days of Destiny 1's open beta on the Xbox 360 which was back in the summer of 2014 and i've played every DLC they've ever released. Destiny as a franchise has had great expansions like the Taken King, House of Wolves, Forsaken & more recently, the Witch Queen but it's had it's fair share of bad DLCs like the Dark Below, Curse of Osiris and now, Lightfall.

Overall I can't recommend this to anyone who really cares about Destiny and hasn't got money to waste because that's exactly what you're doing if you buy this. It's hard for me to believe the same writers who did the Witch Queen did the writing for Lightfall. I'll go into the goods and bads below but TL;DR: Don't get Lightfall and save your money, you'll be thankful you did. If you really want to know the story (which is explained better in the cutscenes then missions lol), just look up some videos on Youtube. Atleast doing that is free.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Obvious DLC spoilers ahead - this is your spoiler warning:

GOOD

- Strand is awesome, one of the more complex subclasses they've added. It's fun to use and mechanically, is impressive and feels very OP. Only issue is how you unlock, explained a little in the bad section. Shame it's locked behind this joke of a DLC, should be free for everyone and if you got the DLC solely because of Strand, I understand why.

- Final boss fight in Lightfall feels difficult and for me, felt like a borderline raid boss which considering it's with Calus and he's been in Destiny for like 6 years, feels quite fitting that's he's difficult.

- QoL changes are great. New mod system is good and the preset/loadout system is fantastic QoL. New guardian rank system is somewhat confusing but acts as a good way to guide players on what they should do next.

- Raid will (most likely) be great - At the time of writing this review the new raid isn't out yet but Bungies raid designers are talented and their track record is phenomenal. Could always be a miss but most likely won't be.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Sadly that's all for the good parts lmao. Bad parts below:

BAD

- Writing bad - Nimbus cringe, that's all I have to say on that one.

- Voice acting was ok, didn't like nimbus' voice as it felt like it had too much autotune. Rohan's voice was better. Witness' voice sounds awesome just like it did at the end of the Witch Queen.

- Story was terrible, terribly written, rushed & predictable - Focused more on Calus than the witness so feels like a Calus DLC as a result. Doesn't conclude anything. Osiris is written to be unlikable and spends too much time being a grouch and shouts at the player for half the DLC which again shows bad writing. Characters like Zavala, Ikora, Exo stranger etc. left on the side lines when they're meant to be part of the war against the witness.

The Veil is seen in the last mission but not explained in the core story arc. It might be explained in post-DLC missions but I haven't done those yet and it shouldn't be explained there anyway. In the end, the witness connects with it and opens a portalb in the traveller and goes through it, I assume to the 'final shape' he keeps talking about but that's it?

- Some missions are way too long - in the final mission, you battle against a cabal siege outside the building of the veil and it just drags on too long.

- One new enemy type which is disappointing. Others are just ones we've seen for the last 10 years since Destiny 1 but with the word 'shadow' at the start.

- Have to complete entire DLC to get access to strand even though two missions are dedicated to training with it? What's the point of the training missions if we don't unlock the thing at the end of the training? Also how Strand is used within the core DLC story arc is used here and there and overall that's annoying. Just do what the taken king did - introduce the bad guy, find out you need a new power to defeat them, then travel to another location and do one big mission to unlock it fully early in the story and then use the rest of the DLC to power up the subclass further to be sufficient enough to defeat the end boss.

- For the 40 pounds price tag, the DLC simply doesn't offer much. 8 missions in total which, considering the DLC sort of focuses on the witness being the most evil and powerful being in the solar system it's not enough to fully flesh out the story the fans deserve. You can get the DLC done in a day lol.

- New POI looks cool but that's about it. Take away from all the flashy neon looks, it's your regular POI we get every DLC however Neomuna feels devoid of content. None of the verticality promised from the devs and from the spawn point, I can go a good 30 seconds without running into enemies as they're all situated in one area. Feels fun to travel around with Strand but again you have to do the entire DLC to experience this and the POI was built with strand in mind. Disappointing.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

More bad than good, really not worth picking the DLC up. 2/10 rating at best.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLiterally does not deserve its Extremely Negative review right now. And while I do recommend Lightfall, its current state is... complicated, and probably not for everyone.

The story is different to what we usually get in Destiny. It's 80s inspired, more light hearted and doesn't take itself as seriously as it perhaps should during these last moments of the Light and Dark. Ultimately, we're left with more questions than answers.
While most reviews more or less agree that Strand was this expansion's highlight and redeeming factor, the Story itself, while rushed and at times somewhat simplified, should get us thinking. Do we need to know everything about the Veil right now? Should we really expect a bustling metropolis in Neomuna instead of the apparitions of the CloudArk? Let Destiny 2 tell its story the way they want. We'll get our answers in due time.

Lightfall is fun. The gameplay is great, Strand is just what Destiny 2 needed right now, and if you don't mind a bit of chaos in a story, this is still worth the purchase. Just... just take parts of it with a grain of salt.\t\t\t\t\t

(this review is spoiler free)

Firstly, lets get the negatives out of the way, or more accurately, THE big issue.

The story for Lightfall had a lot of hype built up the marketing, and with the success of The Witch Queen, expectations were high.
Unfortunately, there are a few key issues with the stakes not being clearly conveyed, so if you're here for the story, temper your expectations a bit.

Gameplay wise, the campaign is incredible, with Strand being a fun, if not particularly powerful as of yet, new subclass.
The new planet is fun with events and patrols and all you would typically expect, with the addition that the difficulty is set significantly higher, with enemy vehicles (which previously were a minor nuisance at worst) now being major obstacles worth taking down as soon as possible.

Over all, I would recommend this if you're really excited for the gameplay, but if you're just here for the lore, I would be prepared to wait for the story to develop further.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs lightfall amazing? no, but it definitely is not as bad a many people in the community make it out to be. Yes the story is mediocre at best and does not explain much of what is happening in it, but most of the critiques seems to be coming from the fact a mystery still remains a mystery to us. Possibly it won't once the raid actually comes out but who knows if that'll be enough for those who are disappointed in this product.

The gameplay at least in my experience has been great, it's been fun to use strand and fling myself all over the place whilst also now actually having some challenge added in. The loadout system is nice and just generally useful as someone who doesn't want to remember every possible loadout I could be using in a given situation.

In closing, I'd say lightfall is worth it if you want fun gameplay and are fine with a meh story. Every single fellow D2 player I know who has played this has enjoyed the experience even with the lackluster story, and to be honest the story isn't going to get worse once you've played through it whilst the gameplay will only get better.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvery once in a while my close knit will hop on to play Destiny 2. We were hyped for Lightfall---until we found out that is 100% not worth the price it's being sold for. It's super sad. If you haven't purchased it yet, wait till it goes on sale or don't even bother with it at all. It was funny on certain occasions with my group, but overall, super saddening and very buggy. The plot wasn't properly written, characters say things that are HEAVILY poor on timing (or even repeat the same lines twice with the glitch). I'm very bummed and disappointed, was looking forward to having a lot of fun again on the game with my buddies. By the time of the boss, no one was really wanting to use that green strand stuff because it keeps glitching and just doesn't even work half the time (there's a bug where you straight up can't super, and it bugs legit everything else to that ability). Couldn't even beat the boss as everyone was having bugs through the entirety. It's very saddening. :"(((

Edit:
Okay, we were finally able to beat all the missions and luckily without any bugs this time around. You can only get full access to the subclass AFTER you beat the story, which honestly really sucks. What a lengthy tutorial for a subclass. Though, underrated opinion, I like strand when it doesn't bug out. The Threadlings, CUTEEEEEE!!! It's better once you can properly edit it and change stuff around to your liking. Not many bugs either with those newer features you can unlock. Though, it could be a lot better.\t\t\t\t\t
