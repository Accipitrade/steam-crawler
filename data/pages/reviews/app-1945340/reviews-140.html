\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSee the review My name is Byf put up regarding 24hour impressions.

Spoilers.

The story is dry, short, and written by a 5th grader.
What is the veil? We don't know. But that doesn't get asked. Somehow, we need to get it because the witness wants it and no other reason. The entire campaign is "chase the mcguffin" with /nothing/ explained.

The entire campaign is blue balled strand. We should have gotten it immediately and had the chance to learn and actually unlock fragments as we progressed through.

Callus is one of the greatest villains in destiny 2. He has been around forever and deserved a better writing team for finishing him off.

Caitil and Callus and their dynamic could have driven the story so well. A daughter disappointed by needing to defeat her father. Compelling. Missed opportunity.

Nimbus should never have happened. He behaves like a child who cannot read the room, and has no emotional reflection. How would that type of person be selected and allowed to become the neomuna version of a navy seal iron man?

He (Nimbus) seems to be written to be comic relief, in the worst moments. His mentor sacrifices himself, and there is no lasting tonal shift in nimbus. He should have changed. Interacting with him as a vendor should have a more serious tone, with a joke here or there. He should not be jovial. The literal worst thing to ever happen to their city is going on now. Discovery, Invasion, attack on "the veil" and how it powers the cloud ark, and his mentor died leaving him to defend the place alone.

He should not be wise cracking, and any character that would be wise cracking in that situation should never have narratively been selected for the cloud strider program.

Rohan, well, he was a good character, and then you wrote him off in the most predictibe way possible.

My feelings about Nimbus aside, you wrote him as a carefree adolescent who cannot read a room (nobody actually wants a main NPC to be on the spectrum).

It would have been compelling story if nimbus was the one who, when the chips are down, sacrifices himself to protect the city. A defining character moment of rising above his joking attitude and choosing the noble path.

Rohan then getting the story development of a "father" who lost their "child." Thereby meaning that Rohan can get council from Osiris and vice versa. Both have experienced great loss, and that can be explored in game.

The writing team dropped the ball so hard on this whole campaign, I cannot in good conscious recommend the campaign to new players or returning players.\t\t\t\t\t

Personally, I think the initial hate against this expansion is justified, but overblown. The Story missions themselves are way worse than witch queen, and leave you with a lot of open questions. Nimbus is a little annoying at first, but I warmed up to him after a while. The post-campaign is something else entirely though. The Deterministic Chaos quest is fun, Neomuna is a way better destination than the Throne World, and has something to do thats not just one Mission with slightly different dialogue.

The Strand Subclass is incredibly fun, and incredibly powerful. The cooldowns are almost nonexistent with the right build, and the damage output is extraordinary.

I cant speak on the raid yet, since it hasnt been released yet, but the buildup of mystery to Nezarec is well done.

All in all, it may not be worth the 50 bucks, but I get enjoyment out of the entire experience.\t\t\t\t\t
