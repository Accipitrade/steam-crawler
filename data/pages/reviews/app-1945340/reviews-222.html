\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHi everyone, I’ve been playing Destiny since House of Wolves. I have had great times such as with TTK, Forsaken, and Witch Queen, and bad times as with D1 Vanilla and Curse of Osiris and now Lightfall.

Lightfall can be fun. Strand is fun to use with your “spiderman” fantasies, but the grapple recharge is a bit slow even with max discipline. Neomuna cool place to explore. I like the kind of retro vibes it gives off. But sadly lightfall can be bad. The story, hurts, I mean it’s comparable to D1 Vanilla. We meet new characters just to know nothing about them. The Veil…. Jesus The Veil. During the final mission I asked my friend if he had any idea of what it was and he said no. I just learned that Byf doesn’t even know. And the ending with how the Ghost made a link and that’s how the Witness is able to enter the Traveler like, I really hope the lore gives me some more information. I know this is somewhat rambling but I just felt the need to post this to my fellow guardians. I love this community and I love this game in all of its flaws, but lightfall was a let down. I’m excited we lost for the first time ever, but I can’t explain to you how or why we lost. I really hope Bungie can redeem themselves, be it seasonal stories or final shape, but this needs to be a lesson that cannot happen again for Destiny’s sake.

My recommendation, if you want a fun time give lightfall a shot. Tormentors are scary as hell. Legendary mode is pretty difficult. Strand and being able to grapple onto people, ships, sparrows is awesome(wish there was more momentum behind it). If you are looking for the Bungie that made Witch Queen and want a story as brilliant as that. You wont find it in lightfall as of the current state. For now you can find me in the wilds, your fellow hunter signing out. Goodnight and good hunting.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tComplete step down from The Witch Queen, both narrative wise and gameplay wise. It really feels like this was just the product of the Bungie B-Team while the main team is hard at work on The Final Shape, at least I hope that's the case. It would explain a lot of the shortcomings and absolutely bizarre change in writing quality. Easily one of the worst stories in Destiny history and Destiny has had a lot of ♥♥♥♥♥♥ stories. It feels like the bare minimum was done at every facet, from level design, story, weapon design, and even music. It's normal for Destiny music to have callbacks to specific leitmotifs to convey some sort of emotion or invoke nostalgia, but there's usually way more original music. In this OST however, there's so many constant call backs to Beyond Light's themes for some reason, sometimes just outright taking chunks from that OST and morphing it into strange "new" song that doesn't fit the mood at all. It really just seems like they just had to scrounge for scraps to make this filler DLC so that they can bide more time for TFS. All the departments were seemingly in their own separate universes which makes every part of this DLC feel so disconnected. The environmental artists always do a great job but there's some areas of Neomuna that look like they spent way less time creating. The Cloudstriders are very clearly "The Authors Poorly Disguised Fetish", and the writing for those characters are just god awful. It's basically unanimously agreed that Nimbus is annoying, tone deaf, and horribly written. It's a shame because the voice behind Nimbus has shown in their past works that they are a competent voice actor. Rohan on the other hand never gets the chance to develop into an interesting character, so all I see him as is the boring "old soldier" trope. For some reason Osiris is also randomly rude and pissed off at times. I'm assuming the writers were trying to make Rohan have some sort of emotional moment that would calm him down and make him less of an ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ but the pacing was so awful that they never had the chance to fully flesh that out. The story basically takes place over the course of 3 hours and the Vangaurd do absolutely nothing other than sit in the H.E.L.M and stare at The Witness draw a triangle into The Traveler with an LSD crayon. They literally sat in the H.E.L.M the entire time while I was ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ around on Neomuna. They never explain at all what "The Veil" even is. I'm running around chasing after something that I don't even know nor care about because they never give me a reason to care. Apparently this whole time there was some war going on since Zavala mentioned the "human loss of this war". WHAT WAR?? I was ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ around in this ♥♥♥♥♥♥ 80's action movie planet with the silver surfers, idk what ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ war you're going on about. Now apparently The Traveler is gone?? But I see it right there on the destinations screen with its Travelerussy wide open just staring at me. Wtf is going on Bungie. How do you go from a brilliantly written story in The Witch Queen to this absolute garbage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe expansion is pretty okay. Like a decent 6-7/10. Granted the raid isn't out and this is day2.

Pros:
1. Art Direction is top notch, as usual.
2. Sound design is fantastic.
3. Score is really well done.
4. The cinematics? ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥.
5. Gameplay is solid. The increased difficulty across the board makes the game feel much better, and the legendary campaign (aside from being marginally tougher than Witch Queen) is still a blast to play through.
6. Tormentors are terrifying and I literally ♥♥♥♥ myself so hard it propelled across the room like my character when a phalanx hit me the first time one was chasing my slow ass like this is some horror game.
7. QoL system updates are mostly solid, especially the loadout system. It's not as comprehensive as DIM but it's really nice for a quick switch (my 4-6 builds x3 characters thanks you bungo).
8. Strand, while weaker than the other subclasses (cooldowns, endgame viability, locked fragments make judgement difficult), feels AMAZING, especially during the campaign. The animations, VFX, sound design, the whole package just feels fun, when of course you're not waiting for it's cooldowns.
9. The new strike is pretty cool.
10. I ♥♥♥♥♥ with the new gear and ornaments.
11. Rohan and Nimbus are cool. Much of the hate for Nimbus kinda comes off as a bleed effect for too much Marvel. Understandable but it's a bit much.
12. Calus got the mitties you been waiting for.
13. Lore is good, but then again, it always is.
14. ALL BLACK SHADER BLESS YOU BUNGO.
15. They brought back Trust.
16. You can honor people, it's nice.

Cons.
1. Story is mid. It's not the worst thing that's ever been made, but following Witch Queen, and the recent Season of the Seraph, it definitely comes off worse than it is. It's a serviceable "chase the McGuffin like an 80s hero" story. So if you play games mainly for the stories, this ain't the expansion for you. There are also quite a few narrative plotholes that should have been addressed. Those wouldn't have made it out of a meeting in one of my classes, so they shouldn't have been shipped. That being said, we're two days in, and the post-campaign missions do somewhat fill in blanks and issues people have with it. Somewhat. So hope remains.
2. New mod system is some ♥♥♥♥♥. Give me my +20s back. Also, RIP Stasis builds, you'll be missed.
3. I'm not the biggest fan of reskins, so the number of reskins this expansion is pretty upsetting. Would've have easily waited 6-8 months just to get custom Neomuni guns, non-Braytech reskin buildings, custom Season of Defiance weapons, and having the D1 classic cutscene armor appear in game. Don't get me wrong, the current guns are dope (especially the flame graphics) but c'mon bungo.
4. Map isn't very vertical. Disappointing to be sure, but expected.
5. Campaign felt oddly, short? I don't know, just don't remember Witch Queen feeling so short, despite spending similar amounts of time on it. Strange.
6. Crucible still needs love.
7. Community. Lack of honeymoon phase due to narrative backlash makes any venture into its Reddit subs that room-on-fire meme from Community.
8. They brought back Trust. Must return to the Gambit treadmill *sigh*.
9. Commendations resetting every 3 months is a massive disincentive to actually go for the max rank. Moreover, ranks 1-6 don't really matter because of how easy it is to get to rank 6. Those two factors kill what was essentially pitched as a way to differentiate different skill players, and turns it into another treadmill.

If you're wary after all the negative coverage, just grab it on sale. I enjoyed it, and don't regret a dime I spent. Nothing can be as bad as what happened to Anthem.\t\t\t\t\t
