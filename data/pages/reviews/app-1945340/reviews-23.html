\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall gets way too much hate for its own good, and you need to know why it's actually worth getting and playing:

I do agree that the story of the campaign is rushed, confusing, and lacks interest to be appealing, which is underwhelming in how it factors into Destiny's storytelling. But the gameplay of this campaign is so damn good. From the first mission to the very last, it's composed of lengthy missions that are structured with multiple forms of entertaining waves of enemies and fighting against them, as well as Neptune's breathtaking views. Aside from these missions, Strand is arguably the best subclass in the game, so learning to use it in the campaign and eventually unlocking it at the end is really beneficial to have in the game. But the campaign is also criticized for how easy and weak the missions are. Which is why I strongly emphasize that LIGHTFALL IS ONLY MEANT TO BE PLAYED ON LEGENDARY DIFFICULTY. Play legendary with a friend or two, and going against strong and intense enemies will actually feel like you're battling a military superpower. Plus the final boss fight will be one of the best boss fights you'll ever have, because it's close to great as fighting Savathun for me personally. What this campaign has differently from the other ones is that you won't have mandated bounties to feel bored from, because you're either doing well-structured and lengthy missions or doing strand runs, which is beneficial to do the more you learn and use it better.

In general, the Legendary experience of Lightfall is a 8.5/10 for me. As long as you play this difficulty only and have extra fun with friends, you're gonna have a great time. The storytelling sucks but it'll age better the more Bungie releases new stories and missions, but the entertaining gameplay from start to finish is what captures how good this campaign is. Playing this campaign on Classic difficulty only ignores the true quality of Lightfall, so don't listen to any generalization of this because it's simply not true. I hated Lightfall for all these exact reasons, but now this is one of my top 2 favorite campaigns with Witch Queen. My last note is to not buy Lightfall for the full price, but get it for discount. From my experience, getting it for $30 and having such an amazing time on Legendary difficulty was worth every penny. Best campaign gameplay-wise, unlocking Strand, great exotics, and cloud nine.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI like the DLC and the changes it came with but it's not my fave. I'm gonna list negative to positive. But everything more detailed down below.

I will say it the DLC (content and what changes it came with) is not bad but it's not good. Well, it's not WitchQueen level good. Sadly Bungie did the fatal mistake of hyping this DLC up and then absolutely under delivering this bad boy. To be fair this is typical for this game. The story is a strong point for the game, but not exactly making it into an experience. Bungie aren't really known for that. I guess WitchQueen and seasons of the seraph and some other stuff sprinkled in between drove the hope up for everyone but it sadly did not come to be with this one. This one is a bit of a dud when it comes to a couple things.

So what are the negatives for this DLC you might ask. Well let's try to list them...

//Story Content and encounters//

The story content and encounter for me are the biggest letdown...

1. Well very blunt put.. Again. Story telling. Every character and every situation in this campaign really tires to persuade you that: "Hey this is very important!!!". But it really doesn't come across. There are a couple events in the campaign that are interesting but nothing to really write about or worth mentioning. The characters you meet Nimbus and Rohan are nice characters but that's essentially it with new characters. And they don't really do anything tooooo memorable. Beside one of em doing something to save everyone (trying not to spoil).

2. The encounters aren't really that interesting either. I feel like compared with other destiny campaign encounters there is way many more enemies on the screen so running anything with addclear is a good idea especially if you're doing the legendary campaign. So that's fun.. However with that being said that gets kinda annoying once the tormentors enter the arena.
The new enemy (tormentors) even though fun to fight have been used in a very lazy way. Every encounter or mission to be fair goes a little like this: Addclear, addclear, addclear, maybe small puzzle, addclear, addclear, tormentor with adds in a tight room, add clear, die, die, come back after a short break, tormentor, addclear, die, tormentor dead, mission done. The tormentors are great! The design fantastic, what it does is super cool. etc. The way it's been used however is kinda lazy.
To be fair that bad boy can only do something to you when it is up close and personal. But putting 2 of them in a tight room feels like you're fighting in a UFC octagon two heavy weights with a bunch of aggressive dogs that keep on coming from some random dark hole on the wall.

These are the most glaring negative things. However there is some good stuff. For example:

// New Class, quality of life updates, good future building blocks //

These are some big positives you can enjoy with or without having the DLC. However since they came all together with the same update it's worth and deserves pinning these on the new expansion.

1. Strand is super fun. Very unique. It's fun to see how that class works in the universe and how it works with the overall gameplay. It is really nice playing with it once you have a good build going on and looks to be more useful than some of the other classes right now... Single target damage or big addclear is possible with it. As well as giving you a high degree of mobility is super nice.
It feels great and it's a great way of gradually making guardians more capable in the grander scheme of the lore, as they are slowly and steadily mastering new powers to defeat evil. Has lore value and gameplay value for sure.

2. Loadout manager and some needed changes like gear not having to be a certain element (either arc, solar, void or stasis) are good changes. With that now all mods are available that's a good way of having less gate keeping and more flexibility in general for new and older players.

3. The game now is new player friendlier and also with the current content in the game the lore makes more sense for new player. It's more interconnected as before it felt like you joined half way through. Also grind has been reduced it feels like, you can focus certain exotics, matchmaking is open for different harder content as it should have been a while ago, etc,etc,etc... You can read other reviews and watch videos for what I'm saying by people who are much more educated on this than I am. But in general the game feels friendlier.

There are numerous other good and bad things to this expansion. If I could give it a rating from 1 to 10 it is probably a 7.5 out of 10. If the lore and campaign would have been better and Bungie wouldn't have hyped lightfall so much I think it would have been a 8.5 out of 10. Just for the needed gameplay changes alone and things like loadout manager it is already pretty good what this expansion brought with. Don't let a bad campaign spoil this good time in the game.

I understand why people are upset with this DLC and Bungie really fumbled by hyping it up so much. If Bungie would have said: "Hey look the new DLC won't be WitchQueen but for sure will bring some interesting stuff." not exactly in this way but you guys know what I mean. I think people would have received it better. However with a 100 dollar price tag it sure is a bother for whoever bought it expecting again WitchQueen level content and world building...

Regardless I recommend the DLC and playing Destiny 2 right now.\t\t\t\t\t

the campaign, and more so the "legendary" campaign (hardmode) is riddled with so many imbecilic oneshot mechanics and instant death scenarios it has forever scarred me.

The enemies are of a managable difficulty, as you'd expect from "hard mode" but the mechanics are crap. Things like carry an orb that keeps you from defending yourself, while being shot by 20 guys. Or a boss fight where every hit is 1/2 of your health and he spawns x2 adds which are also bosses and can oneshot you without escape if they get in melee range.

(((minor spoilers for campaign gameplay:)))

Or the awful decision to force you to use the new class in certain missions, empowering it more than whats normal, only to remove that empower for the last 2 missions and make you play it without a build or even fully unlocked, against said boss.

(((end of spoilers)))

The entire campaign and many of the side quests are like this, where the difficulty does not lie in strong enemies and tough mechanics, but in idiotic oneshots and enemies simply being way to many, way to insta kill-y or simply have so much health you could go take a dump while fighting them and they would still be alive by the end. You on the other hand, will die in 2 hits, and start over from checkpoints far back.

The staggering fact is that most peoples advice on how to complete this is to do it in a group. Which would be ok if it was group content but this is the frikkin single player campaign!

The story was ok but did not at all live up to the hype/build up, and it is unclear on certain things like, who TF half of the characters/lore events are, cause, remember, they deleted half their prior story expansions lol. But standalone it is good. And has cool vibes.

But the way it is presented, in this awful endurance challenge of a campaign, where 90% of my deaths were not out-skilled but rather; literally yeeted of an arena due to unavoidable aoe knockbacks, or oneshot cause I got too close to the boss, or oneshot cause wyverns got purple laser weapons of mass destruction... it makes it hard to enjoy it.

Oh yeah and while you can tune it down "a bit", by playing normal campaign, the sidequests and later content is all tuned super high so frick you from the devs lol.

And while its easy to say just git gud; my experience is that the problem is not skill, its simply bad design. Gosh darn awful design actually.

When you finally finish and you have the new class for real, you lose all the campaign powers and have to grind to unlock it all. And again for every alt you have.

This campaign/sidequests etc was genuinely some of the worst piece of gaming I've ever had to do, and yes I did it solo on legendary.

I will recommend it if you like this game, which as a whole is actually quite good, if you don't mind grinding that is, and I will recommend it cause of the new subclass being cool, but I will review it negatively due to my before mentioned issues.

The sheer amount of pain I've suffered at the hands of broken design and unavoidable deaths here is as high as my dis-contempt for this expansion.

Do better Bungie...\t\t\t\t\t
