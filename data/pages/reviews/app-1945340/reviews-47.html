\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*DISCLAIMER: This review contains spoilers for the entirety of Lightfall*

How did we go from Witch Queen to this?

The Good:
- Loadouts
My only complaint is that loadouts should not be locked behind lost sector completions. What sense does that make? Does bungie literally have to make everything about a grind or money rather than just giving it to us?

- Strand:
As a class, it is good. The only part most people cared about, including me, was the grapple. My only complaints about it was the story focused too much on it and restricted us from using it too much.

- OST:
Bungie always kills with soundtracks so this is to be expected.

- New Raid:
While the difficulty was a MASSIVE issue, it was an overall really good raid if you can look past that.

- Exotics:
Love the exotics and the new toys we get to play with. Always fun. Especially the stasis glaive.

- Rohan:
One of the only original characters to come out of this expansion that actually felt enjoyable and interesting to listen to. He actually seemed like he knew what he was talking about and could have been a incredible mentor to Osiris during our stay. I knew he had character death written all over him the minute I met him. Bungie seems to love killing the only good characters in the series for shock factor or marketability.

The Bad:
- Commendations:
Abusable, farmable for ranks, and misses the entire point of why they were requested in the first place. They aren't even that descriptive. A person with leadership doesn't mean they can't still be an ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥. Even if you keep nerfing the requirements for the guardian ranks, it still only feels like a rushed idea compared to the commendations that we needed. Why would I give a commendation to someone that was an ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ the entire raid? I get what they were going for, but you raised a community of grinders, and made it the commendations into a grind. If they didn't see this coming, then Bungie does not understand their fanbase at all.

- Buildcrafting / Mods:
I would take back the old buildcrafting any day. We did not get a better system for buildcrafting. We got a worse system overall that came out half baked. All of the mods are either reused, simple, or not diverse enough compared to everything else. We got an incredibly small handful of them too. I like the clean format and the selection, but compared to what we had before, it was so much better and had so much more breathing room compared to this bland array of simplicity. Not to mention, loadouts being locked behind guardian ranks discourages buildcrafting or having a lot of builds ready to test. 6 to start out with is NOTHING compared to the amount of builds that I have seen coming out of the woodwork.

My suggestion would have been to just give everyone all of the mods in the game. Stop locking them behind Ada-1 and making us wait months - literal years for 1 mod that was meta that we didn't bother to grab at the time. At the very least, we don't have to rely on Ada to get good mods every week.

- Neomuna:
I love the general aesthetic of Neomuna, and how colorful it looks. But it feels so empty and repetitive. There is no sign of life anywhere. All of these buildings, structures, archaeology, design choices, are there with no people actually inhabiting them. It's just people inside of a cloud database, so the only character we get to see is Rohan and Nimbus. Neomuna is basically just a planet full of homes, random structures, power grids, and defense systems for Nimbus to wallow inside of for being alone after Rohan dies.

The entirety of Neomuna is just recycled content with a new coat of paint. I can, at the very most, understand recycling content for seasons, but why do I pay so much money for a new area that just feels like you copy pasted every asset and idea you could pull out of the sunset/still available dumpster. Every patrol, lost sector, "terminal overload" either takes something else from another event in the game, or is just entirely copied from another location. Nothing about this area is original or fresh. This area is garbage, and I unironically get more enjoyment from remembering Titan as a playable area.

- Nimbus:
Bungie trying their best to make a character that can be Cayde without bringing Cayde back. When Cayde was being funny, atleast Cayde could realize when a tense situation was happening and partake in it like a normal person. Nimbus is so dense and unaware at all times, that it is not even funny. This character never has anything interesting to add to the overall tone of the situation, the story of the expansion, the atmosphere of Neomuna, and makes the story so much more insufferable. Nimbus constantly blabbering they/their mouth all the time makes me wish I was Cayde during the start of Forsaken.

- The Shadow Legion:
I was so excited to hear that there would be new Cabal types and the Tormentor was being implemented. Too bad the Shadow Legion are arguably no different than every other cabal army we have already. The only thing they do differently is drop a piece of the pyramid if you don't shoot it. Even if you don't shoot it, it takes no effort at all to blow it away. Tormentors are still good, but they are buggy, inconsistent in aggression, and are just damage walls more than anything. I feel like bungie got too caught up in how cool they looked compared to how they performed.

- Story:
I get this was supposed to be an arc in between the final shape, but did it have to be the most uninteresting, least explanatory, and utterly pointless expansion story to exist? The whole point of this expansion was to finally kick off the events of ending of the Light and Dark saga, but (someone else mentioned this in their review) the first and last cutscenes of the expansion are the only ones that actually progress the overall plot.

We are hit with things like "The Veil", "The CloudArk", and other stuff that we are told we need to protect, collect, discover, locate, etc. But no one takes 2 seconds to explain what these things are, why are they important, how Osiris magically understands all of these concepts or things. It makes no sense. Even if they explain it later on, the whole idea was to start explaining in the story, not in the side content afterwards.

I am not saying you can't progress the story through the seasonal content, but the community has made it well known that we are sick of this method of storytelling, and that the seasonal story should push the plot in major ways. I would argue this expansions story would be a complete fumble if it didn't have Calus, build Caiatl's story and character, and show us more of the witness. Also, killing Rohan was one of the worst decisions ever.

All in all, if you are really wanting to participate in Lightfall, wait until it is on sale or don't buy it all together. There's really no point in this expansion existing other than Bungie getting the big bucks Sony was wanting so badly from them. I really hope Final Shape is better than this laughable mess of a DLC and Bungie can recapture the spark that was the Witch Queen.\t\t\t\t\t
