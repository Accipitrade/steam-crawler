\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGod....dang...it Bungie. Why? Why do you think we dont care about story or lore being fully fleshed out? Its clear im not the only person, not by a mile, who cares about the story and the lore of this universe. We are talking a decade of story building, of lore, of things to encounter and discover. And we are told to wait. We are given a campaign, without spoilers, that essentially spits in your face for all your effort and gives you some vague ending of how we 'lose'.
Bungie has literally said this is supposed to be Empire Strikes Back of the Destiny universe. Thats an insult to that movie frankly. There were no stakes we actually feel, no consequences that actually make us go 'oh no this bad', nothing.

I am genuinely worried for the future and especially next season. Historically, the next season is always a bit of a rougher one in terms of appeal to the playerbase, Season of Haunted as an example.

One of the few redeeming features is Strand is actually super fun to use in the open world. I will give full credit where credit is due and Strand's ability to grapple and get to places, I love it and its super fun. BUT, this is where we encounter yet another problem. Strand felt shoehorned in, to the point it was aggressively in your face about its importance, yet you are never given more details on this. Literally none, just that its cosmic strings that connect the mind.

Dont even get me started on the Veil.

Also the music, the music is usually gorgeous and no exception here.
It pains me though, that this is what we got. After so much hype, so much presentation, so much communication from Bungie(lets be fair theyve been doing better), and yet this is what we got. I dont know where we go from here.\t\t\t\t\t

In this current expansion the writing quality is awful in comparison, they have gutted the mod system which has completely destroyed build crafting, the only reason i consistently game back to destiny was the gameplay and sandbox and now it feels like they have undone all of their progress and turned it into a hollow shell of what it formally was. you gave us loadouts so we could run all these unique builds without out the complexity of re-slotting our mods every time for every build just to get every build we could make gutted, so now we dont even need loadouts because i never have to swap out my mods. strand feels designed with the last mod system in mind so the way the kit flows with melee cooldowns is horrible, stasis is beyond horrible now, and any really unique or fun elemental well builds are just gone and replaced with a boring cool down system. you shouldve removed charged with light and the damage boost but kept everything else and built upon it for more class diversity, maybe subclass specific mods for synergies with abilities. for example "when a target is suppressed with abilities generate a void well"

The writing and characters we got in lightfall was abysmal, we got nimbus who has marvel level humour (completely shoving humour into every scene when it doesnt match the tone and is executed horribly) who seems like doesnt understand any of the stakes and acts like a brain dead child. Rohan was a pointless character and his "moment" completely was hollow and bared no weight i was just waiting for the scene to end so i could get my loot, he needed more development if you wanted that moment to be impactful. neomuna has no personality, its not unique and feels so lifeless for a secret society that has been working independent for centuries. the only decent parts were with caitl, calus, and the witness and that was only two missions tbh.

this dlc felt closer to a season than an actual expansion and one of the worst ones we've had in a while (ive played since beta destiny 1 and only missed the pirate season with eramis). the writing is closer to destiny 2's launch than the witch queen, major step down in quality.

gameplay wise you gave us strand with super fast cooldowns the whole campaign and the second we're out of it it goes to stupidly long cooldowns that are just boring to engage with. every single boss is a tormentor, no variety, especially when last time we got 3 types of unique light bearing hive with the moth mechanic. shadow legion are just a reskin, they play no differently despite being imbued with darkness, all they get is a little overshield deployable. It makes sense why the vex are here but honestly they dont feel impactful to the story it feels like they just make the story spin its wheels so not alot is happening. it takes so long for the story to get going and then its just done, over. The only decent change they made was how the artefact works.

Overall i normally grind everything out the second the content comes out so i can day one raid and compete and currently i dont even want to open the game again. i would get a refund if i could\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeen playing for years and am disappointed.

Story was just not what it needed to be. The Witness arrived and we don't interact with it at all. We go to find and protect the Veil from Calus, which isn't inherently bad, but there are no stakes. What is the Veil? We don't know. Why does the Witness want the Veil? We don't know. What will happen if we fail? We did, and we don't know.
Strand was sold as a new power we create and refine, but instead we stumble upon it on the side of the road. During the campaign it feels good, but post campaign the recharge is too slow to make any impact.

I'll give credit where credit is due; the action sequences are good, fighting alongside Caiatl and her cabal, holding the line.
The tormentors are great. They appear on the field and change the objective. They cant be nuked upon arrival and need work to avoid while chipping them down. Music is, as always, fantastic. Also, the marketing team did a fantastic job of making a lackluster experience look incredibly exciting.

With the state of the campaign narratively, I can't in good faith recommend Lightfall to new players, which is a shame. It's left me with a bad aftertaste and feeling like the seasonal content and post-campaign are chores to complete. The lack of information created a lack of stakes, with things happening that don't mean anything to me.
What makes this worse is that I know the narrative team can write amazing stories, we've been getting them on and off for years, consistently since Season of the Chosen, and that is what makes this such big let down.
I have hope that this can be remedied going forward, but until then I'll be watching from social media, not through gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
