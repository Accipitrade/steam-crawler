I love Destiny, I want to give this a thumbs up. But I play for the story and I found it pretty underwhelming, especially compared to the Witch Queen or even Beyond Light.

Without spoiling too much, I found some rather important characters didn't get enough screen time or development. Also wasn't a fan of a new character who felt a little too silly/out-of-place with something that's suppose to be us losing. It's also meant to be a big-impacting story but the main story missions make the large conflict feel small.

I want to recommend this because Destiny is one of my favourite games, but for the price they are charging for this expansion it's not worth it at this price point.

The abilities are neat but you aren't missing out on something completely game changing, world is also cool but it is a lot of re-used assets.

If things change later on, I'll gladly change my review but until then, wait for a deep sale or hope the Final Shape is better.

Pros :

1. Strand is fun, and pretty effective in end-game content.

2. Calus and the Witness are great every time they’re on screen (sans ending).

3. Gameplay-wise, the campaign is pretty similar to Witch Queen, as opposed to previous campaigns such as Beyond Light and Shadowkeep.

4. Environment and sky box art (especially for the raid) did a great job.

Cons :

1. The overarching story was really bad, even for Destiny standards. An example is major plot devices (the Veil and the Radial Mast) never get explained and the entire story being left on a cliffhanger for another year. It's the equivalent to Thanos getting all the Infinity Stones and just leaving through a psychedelic portal, without a single character explaining why him getting the Infinity Stones are bad. None of the impact of the "second collapse" or "losing" is felt anywhere in the in-game world, except for the Traveler no longer being above the city.

2. Neomuna and the Cloud Striders are strange. They really don’t fit in Destiny, at least not in the current saga, especially for a campaign set around a second collapse. Not only that, but Neomuna is small and pretty lifeless, with it's only redeeming factor being that it looks pretty. Also Nimbus is so annoying, they make me physically uncomfortable

3. I'm pretty sure 90% of weapons outside of the raid are complete reskins, and large portions of Neomuna have been ripped from Deep Stone Crypt. I get that repurposing assets has it's place in game development, but if you're charging nearly the same amount as a full priced AAA game (God of War on Steam is the same price as Lightfall), I'd expect a little more effort.

4. The raid was disappointing. The gear seems alright, and the environment art is aesthetically unique, with a beautiful Tree of Silver Wings in the sky box. The mechanics however, are what I'd expect from a dungeon. Me and my team ran through the entire raid twice within the first 24 hours, and with easy damage checks, really simple mechanics, and some jank here and there, it simply doesn't hold a candle to the other raids of Destiny 2. Not to mention that Nezarec looks kinda goofy.

Overall, I am just a simple Destiny fan who hates the direction the game has been going, but continues to buy expansions because of a single sliver of hope that it will be amazing. My personal grievances aside, I can't justify spending $50 on this, just to end up with 6 hours of a mid tier campaign, followed by the same thing I've been doing for 3 years, and a really disappointing raid.\t\t\t\t\t
