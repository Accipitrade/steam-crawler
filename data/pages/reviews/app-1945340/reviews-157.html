\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSigh. Lightfall is unfortunately not very good.

The Witness has led the Pyramids on an attack against the Traveler. In order to commence the Final Shape, it requires the Veil. It sends the Disciple to its location on Neomuna, to use the Radial Mast to establish the Link. It's up to you and the Cloudstriders to stop them.

If those sentences looked like an incomprehensible pile of word salad garnished with proper nouns and vagueness, congratulations, welcome to Campaign Mode. That's all you get.

The campaign mode is equal parts chasing vague story beats and an extended tutorial for the new subclass, which you don't unlock until you finish the whole thing. Aside from the Big Bads, Osiris and Caitl are the only returning characters here. Osiris is as insufferable as ever, pretty much throwing away all the development he got over the past three years or so to turn into a font of spouted exposition. The two new characters are just about as unpleasant, looking and feeling like they escaped from a Saturday morning cartoon in the '90's called "Radical Patrol!" or something. One is barely a character, and the other has such cringey dialogue that they make me legitimately concerned for future representation in the series. Without giving anything away, the "twist" at the end feels incredibly cheap and unearned, and the resolution doesn't tie up any loose ends at all.

Strand is fun when you get to use it, but it needs some tweaking. The downtime between abilities is too long to make it appealing over the 3.0 subclasses. Grapple definitely needs some type of cooldown reduction out of combat, as traversal with it is rather frustrating otherwise. The tracking on all the abilities is a bit of a miss as well, and a lot of them don't interact properly with existing mods or exotics. A lot of it also cares about you using Strand weapons, but there just aren't enough of those in the game yet for that to be viable.

The new world is appealing visually, but feels just about as soulless as the other bombed-out worlds after a while. The new activity that was supposed to be a spiritual successor to the fondly-remembered Escalation Protocol just feels like an overly-long public event. The public events themselves are just copy-pasted from other worlds like Cabal Excavation or Vex Network Disruption. Very little about the world is memorable in the long term, which is unfortunate.

Lightfall is not a must-buy. The campaign is underwhelming and the story doesn't advance the overall narrative at all. Wait to pick it up when it either goes on sale or when they rework the Strand subclasses a little bit to make them worthwhile.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't think I've been this disappointed at Destiny ever before. I stick with this game, through thick and thin. Even at some of Destiny's lowest points, I still played. But now.

The lack of interest for the main story is appalling. They focus more on Neomuna and the Cloud Striders after the campaign instead of giving us answers to questions that arose after Witch Queen. If you are a new player who knows nothing about the story, join the club as a seasoned vet is in the same boat.

The new mod system replaced any signs of individuality. Before LF, you want a Warmind Cell build? Stasis shard/well build? Charged with Light? Heck, even whole builds based on 1 artifact mod. Now, it is so brain dead and simple that we are all basically the same. The only thing that changes is the weapons we use.

The legend campaign was stupid, the final boss wasn't even the hardest. Savathun had real mechanics, she had a gorgeous battle arena, it felt immersive. This final boss just felt like a strike, Legend NF at best.

I will say I enjoyed the first week of the season, it was nice to see old faces get some love and screentime. Even the seasonal activity is a good adaptation of the Battleground series we have grown to love.

I really hope that the Bungie writing team either A) can improve drastically on the story or B) have been following a model of good DLC, bad DLC, good. Destiny gets a lot of s*** from a lot of people I know, and it's times like this that I just can't defend some decisions. No game is perfect. But damn, it's heartbreaking to see this.

I love Destiny. I always will. The guns feel the best in any FPS I have ever played, you can legit be a space wizard who has guns and swords! It's the ultimate mash up. But, this ain't it. I always want more people to try Destiny, it gets a lot of unnecessary ♥♥♥♥, but if you are a new player, PLEASE don't buy this yet. Play the base game, understand the mechanics. Play other campaigns. Only buy this if you need to.\t\t\t\t\t
