\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpon release, I sided heavily with the crowds of folks that had proper ♥♥♥♥ to say about this. I've been playing Destiny since Forsaken, I am not a veteran and I do not pretend to be. Through most of my playtime and content consumption revolving around this game, I have not been involved much in the lore, nor did I understand most of what was happening in cutscenes or in-game dialogue. I just wasn't invested, I was here for the gunplay and addictive power level grinding.

Just about 4 or 5 weeks ago, I came back and ended up grinding more content than I ever had at one time, so my hype for Lightfall was gleaming. I took off work for it. I had become invested in the story, specifically the ending to the season in which Rasputin gets 404'd and the Traveler becomes "gone". I binged Byf videos and contemplated getting a physical Grimoire or two. I wanted to see the continuation of the The Witness and what type of mayhem would come with it.

So, imagine my shock.

In this sea of "Mostly Negative", I feel at home, because I'm definitely in agreement with the overall sentiment. If the cost of this was your main gripe, I can empathize. If you thought the campaign didn't answer enough questions, totally fair. The original roll-out of strand? Pretty bad. Think Nimbus is annoying? Very fair. But I can't sit here and tell you that I disliked this new chapter in Destiny, because I can't.

I'm waiting for what happens next. I paid 100 dollars for 4 seasons, not one 9 mission campaign. I'm aware that not everyone is thrilled about the model they use to feed story to us, but I think it's just dandy. Though the witness cutting a portal into the traveler brought upon a hundred new questions, I will continue to play through the year, wearing whatever helps me hear the secret Nezarec phone sex conversations, for just that extra bit of context.

Destiny is a good game, probably.\t\t\t\t\t

Do not confuse this with a general rating of the 6.5/10 for the entire Lightfall expansion itself.

Yes, the campaign is a major part, but still not everything. The new subclass Strand, the reworked Mods/Armor system 3.0 paired with the option to save Loadouts, new interface and design of tabs, introduction of guardian ranks, the change to crafting weapons, the increase to overall difficulty of activities and multiple options to stun champions with subclass abilities are a great addition to the general game.
Overall, all the quality of life changes are great and improve the general feel of the game and brings forth its strengths.

Sadly, I think that the campaign is letting all the veteran players down, which have been sticking to the game for a very long time and are invested into the development of the story and its characters.

Neomuna is a great planet, which offers a lot of interesting little events and does not come short in terms of size, content or secrets.
The Subclass is fun and brings a fresh wind into the sandbox with its need little tools to destroy your average space alien.
For everyone claiming, it is almost the same as stasis, please gather some more experience with the subclass, and you will find, that you will be able to do much more with stasis, and that stasis can do much more in some aspects than strand ever will. Every subclass has its strength and weakness in a constantly evolving sandbox.

In conclusion, the campaign is the most atrocious part of the expansion and is getting hated on for a reason. But let's be honest, how many times are we going to play it anyway?
But all the quality of life changes, adjusted difficulties, strand and the vibrant new destination improve the overall experience of the expansion.

Still with a bitter taste in my mouth from the campaign, I am heading in to the raid and a new year of destiny.

It can only get better from here on out!
