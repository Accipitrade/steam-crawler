\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who has completed the story, unlocked strand and obtained most of the post-campaign exotics on hunter I would recommended Lightfall.

First off I’ll start with the story. I’ll avoid spoiling anything that isn’t in the intro cutscene. To put it simply, the entire campaign is narratively lacking. It had you spend the entire time trying to defend an object called “The Veil” that (seemingly) everyone knows of but you. All the while the main villain is attacking the traveler head-on which is right in orbit of earth. The threat of extinction is right at our doorstep and we’ve gone all the way to another planet to dink around with strand. This would be absolutely nonsensical if it wasn’t for the wonderful and enigmatic “Veil” which for unexplained reasons the Witness needs to actually attack the traveler. There are many many more plot holes that I won’t bother to discuss but this is the main pain point I believe. The entire story hinges on something that we aren’t explained the significance of. That sucks and it makes the entire campaign feel less impactful.
What it does right though is it’s focus on strand and our learning it. It really does feel like we’re trying to master a new power that we don’t understand and that felt fun and engaging.

Now as far as gameplay goes, The Lightfall Campaign is great. It stuck with the format created in The Witch Queen with long, checkpointed missions and an extra hard mode for those of us that like a little extra loot, quicker leveling and a healthy dose of suffering. There are many tough encounters and a boss fight for every mission but also many Strand focused movement sections that were a ton of fun with me and my friends. They were a very fun way to learn our way around a subclass that functions very differently than others.

Now, as soon as you enter post-campaign and unlock the actual strand subclass you have a very sad realization: Grapples Cooldown is 100 seconds. For Warlocks and Titans this essentially means you’re stuck using it on tangles that will refund it and occasionally using it to save your life. As for hunters I think we’ve got it a bit better off with the two charges and being able to create permanent points to use repeatedly. Overall though I think strand is surprisingly effective in most content and a ton of fun to use. I’ve had to focus my entire build around grapple regen but after Bungie unlocked the final fragments I actually feel I’ve got my build in a place where I have as many grapples as I want.

Story: 5/10 it’s very lacking but I was still relatively engaged

Gameplay: 9/10 difficult but very fun with nice jump-puzzle breaks -epecially fun with friends

Strand: 8.5/10 it requires a lot of effort but it can be super fun and effective in most content

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, let's get one thing clear. The game play does make you feel like a badass. I actually had to take a break from the story to grind in strikes. It was something that I expected from a game like Destiny 2.

Pros:
The game play is good. (reason already given)
As a Titan main, I actually found Strand to be fun, despite backlash. There were times where the nanites from my Outbreak Perfected and Strand would just clean the floor of enemies that were in my site. Seeing health bars go down was insanely satisfying. But is also a double edge sword in this case. (See negative)
The story starts off strong in the first 2 missions. The cut scenes with Calus and The Witness are really good to make me hate Calus in a good way.
The quality of life improvements such as load outs make it easy and seamless to switch gear.

Negatives: (I've got a lot to say about this)
Nimbus... This dude sucks. Pandering to the most toxic part of the LGBT community is the least of this character's problems. One being that his voice may sound interesting at first, it gets very grating as you progress through the story. Design from the neck down, I have no idea what Bungie was thinking. Rohan looked fine to me, but Nimbus design makes me think that the people designing Nimbus didn't know what to do with him. His personality comes off like a really childish and unlikable version of Cayde-6. The not very serious vibe I get from Nimbus was the biggest buzzkill from one certain event with Rohan, all the way to the end of the story.
The story in this game. Started out strong with the 2 missions, only for it to get worse. Maybe I missed something, but I don't get why the Vex are on Neomuna. I don't recall if it was ever explained when Osiris and the Guardian met up with Rohan and Nimbus. But that's one of Lightfalls biggest problems. Stuff we had questions for, aren't answered, and we're left with more questions. What exactly is the Veil and why is Osiris acting like he knows what it is? Why are people minds in the Cloudark if it's at risk of being destroyed? Heck the whole premise of the Cloudark made absolutely no sense. Even the ending had me saying out loud, "What are you talking about?". You'd think Bungie would have learned and had experience with previous expansions and seasons. But man, This is Curse of Osiris bad.
Strand. You would think that you unlocked Strand towards the end of the story after a training mission, or even in the training montage with Osiris. But nope, you unlock Strand AFTER the story is done. There are times where I try to melee, and just misses its target. You could be looking at an enemy and you would lunge at them, right. Well if you play on controller, if you're within range and you try to strafe and melee, the Strand melee will go in the direction that you are moving in.
The commendations I found to be pretty unnecessary. I've played Vanguard Ops multiple times and I have not noticed any payoff for giving out commendations.
Armor Mod 2.0 I think is a massive downgrade. I had a build pertaining to Warmind Cell Mods that slapped, only to be sunsetted for a new system that is way too basic. It's serviceable for newcomers, I'll give it that. But a player like me who already put Thousands of hours on Steam and consoles altogether, this is a massive L for people that loved their builds.

Overall, If you're like me and spent money on the deluxe edition, you probably feel burned for spending so much when your set of expectations are in check. If you're just in it for the game play, you should be fine. But you'll really start to get burned if or when you do the same thing for your next character. Or just pay to skip the campaign. Seriously Bungie, I've done it once with one character. Why of all things have you kept that in, other than to monetize on a repetitive grind? I'm just in hopes that Season of Defiant and onward picks up the slack that Lightfall dropped the ball on.\t\t\t\t\t

The mostly negative reviews are misleading to the actual experience of the campaign.

Every facet of this expansion is amazing to explore and play through except...

the main story, which feels disconnected from itself in an extremely jarring way.
it isn't bad per se but was very obviously rewritten last minute. This causes it to feel disjointed and overall confusing and that makes me really sad because everything else about this expansion is really really good!
  • The new weapons are fun
  • Strand is a great subclass to play with and i'm personally having a ton of fun swinging around
  • The exotic quests and post campaign content are so much better than the actual main campaign confoundingly enough
  • and the lore is great and there are a lot of intriguing mysteries yet to be unraveled!
but the story as-is just feels like it shouldn't be like that.

Do I recommend it?
Of course, everything else about this expansion feels fresh, well polished, and put together. I just wish the main story was written better.

If you don't want to pay $50 for this, put in your wishlist and wait for it to go on sale.

and if you want a good main campaign story, buy the witch queen instead.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started playing Destiny 2 during Season of the Haunted and put about 550 hours into the game from July 2022 until now. I have really been enjoying the game. I found activities like Strikes, bounties, Crucible, and seasonal rituals to be engaging and semi-therapeutic for my Attention Deficit Disorder. I spent a lot of this time overcoming the deficiencies in Bungie's new player experience, watching countless videos about mods and buildcrafting, and looking for ways to compensate for my lack of a robust Vault of pub-crushing weaponry. Destiny 2 has some of the most fluid, dynamic gameplay I've ever experienced in a game. While many were burning out on the seasonal content delivery and re-use of assets, I was experiencing most of it for the first time.

Lightfall has been a wake up call. I was enjoying the game so much that I pre-ordered the Deluxe edition as soon as it was revealed. I was so excited for all of the QoL changes to resolve some of the issues that had been such a barrier to entry for me. What I got instead was a lackluster story in bizzarely barren new patrol zone. The QoL changes amounted to some nice User Interface improvements and a gross over-simplification of buildcrafting that effectively broke my understanding of the game while also providing no in-game tutorial for re-learning the ropes. The expansion introduced a single new Strike, nothing for Gambit or the Crucible, and weirdly undertuned versions of past seasonal content added to the Vanguard Ops playlist. Put simply, it feels like Bungie created a minimum viable product and released it to the masses without any regard for how it would be received. The infrastructure developer in me believes that they're attempting to coast Destiny 2 into the finish line while they develop Destiny 3 in the background, hopefully on a canned engine rather than their custom one. I really hope that's the case, but even if so, it doesn't justify the sort of treatment provided to Lightfall.

My feelings right now are that Destiny 2 is an excellent game that desperately needs a development team that cares about it. Players are being treated poorly, but more importantly, a great game is being neglected. It's a real sorry state.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith a single expansion, Bungie wiped away years of goodwill with an ill-advised return to their D1 ways. With Whedon-esque dialog ruining any attempt at seriousness and MacGuffins on top of MacGuffins with literally no explanation or payoff, this DLC easily ranks as one of the worst in the D2 era.

What's even more stunning is to compare this to The Witch Queen, Bungie's best outing of the D2 era. But when viewed in the context of the original Lightfall's delay, now known as "The Final Shape," it makes sense that this was nothing but a money grabbing stop-gap. Light fall is nothing but seasonal content priced as a full expansion: only one new strike, a repurposed seasonal activity, no truly new weapons, and a questionable new armor system.

Ultimately, a game marketed as a the penultimate chapter in The Light and Dark Saga instead lands the narrative equivalent of a pop gun. An outing so irrelevant that the opening and ending cutscene that appears to have just been cut in half and a dearth of content that should make even the most hardcore D2 fans wonder: What exactly is Bungie doing with all this money they keep charging us?\t\t\t\t\t
