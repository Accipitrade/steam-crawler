\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter giving it some time to mull over my feelings and try to decide how I really feel about this DLC, I'm going to give it a final verdict of 6/10. That's a lot lower than I initially felt that it deserved, as I was riding high after beating the campaign. Gameplay was as fun as ever, and Strand so far has been a total blast to play with. The new quality of life changes that have been added to game, on top of the new mod system in place have a been enjoyable, making build crafting a lot more accessible to newer players, and veterans alike. Music was great, and the visuals of the story and the new destination of Neomuna scream "Cyberpunk" but in all the right ways. I have yet to explore all of the new destination and do everything else that's on the planet, but where my biggest gripes lie now is in the story telling.
Spoilers: As far as the telling of this story, you can definitely tell that this was more like the first act in the ending of the Light vs Dark saga that Bungie has been telling for years, but the blatant lack of real narrative driving forward with who the Witness is and what it wants with the Traveler is really showing. The story took one step forward and 3 steps back, leaving not only so many more questions than we began with, but also just leaving too many things open ended. What the hell is the Veil and why does everyone know what it is, excpet us? Why does the Witness want it? Why do these Cloudstriders on Neomuna, this ancient race of Humans who only knew the Lightbearers as their title of "Warlords" just openly accept us? What happened with Osiris, going through so much personal character growth last season with realizing his head strong mentality is not a way to get things done, just completely get reversed on itself? It makes little sense, and these characters were all changed to just fit the narrative of a cheesy 80s action film? Not to mention a literal training montage, just to drive that point home. Lastly, the Calus boss fight. What did all of this hype and build up of this character that has been taunting us and driving us forward for years, since the beginning of Destiny 2, finish on? A generic boss fight of a Colossus shooting at us, just to have him turn into a Gladiator subtype enemy as well with just a huge health bar. At least with the Savathun boss fight, she had a plethora of unique abilities and animations that were specific to her, and her alone. It's a real bummer how much of a letdown that was. The new Tormentor enemy type is really great though, and honestly I really enjoyed fighting them and what they brought to the table. I hope to see more unique enemies being made, but again, where was that love for our boy? I have a lot of love for this game, and I would love to bring everyone back on board to play so we can have fun with the content this game has. There is a lot to do, raids, dungeons, and whatnot. If you're not fully invested in this saga like I am, perhaps these shortcomings of the story might not bug you. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am not a 10 year veteran but have close to 4000 hours in game combined between this platform and battlenet. This expansion is unfortunately nothing more than a seasonal story arc with a new subclass that isn't all that good. It was oversold but is underwhelming across the board, great job by the marketing team to be fair for the level of hype that was created. A lot of the "upgraded" systems are actually not an upgrade but more of a dumbing down of the game. We haven't gotten any new PvP maps and only are seeing one new strike, where did the dev time go to? Overall disappointed but still holding out hope that the developer can make it right, this was their worst effort out o the box yet.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt is quite comedic that lightfall was overhyped and underdelivered just like cyberpunk, the game they very likely took heavy inspiration from.

Strand is really fun though, especially the grapple. I had lots of fun swinging around on Neomuna during the campaign. I just wish they made it a shorter cooldown. The campaign kinda gives you a wrong idea on how much you can spam your grapple, since you are strand empowered all the time. Strand also feels a little bit more tame compared to how stasis launched. Which might be a good thing, considering the absolute audacity of stasis warlock getting nerfed only 9 days into Beyond Light. They better not pull something like this again!

The "new" legendary weapons in Lightfall aren't quite new either. The Neomuna weapons are reskins from the Shadowkeep DLC, the seasonal weapons are reskins from season of the lost and the random lootpool/ritual playlists are all weapons we've seen in one way or another already. The only new weapons we'll get will be the raid weapons. This means that 90% of Lightfall players will not get a single new legendary weapon in this DLC. Even shadowkeep had more unique new weapons. The legendary random loot pool is completely broken right now. It doesn't feel like the new guns have a higher drop rate than the old ones anymore. I've gotten so many dupes of witch queen weapons and still not a single copy of the new harsh language grenade launcher. Promotional images show weapons that aren't even in the game: Lightfall banner on Steam, ingame banner when selecting missions and many trailers and so forth. It seems like they scrapped the weapon set but couldn't be bothered to change the images. Bungie is a multi billion dollar company btw, not an indie studio, stuff like this is not excusable.

Story wise we are looking at a huge downgrade compared to Witch Queen and Forsaken. The campaign just feels like one long tutorial for strand. The destination feels empty and lifeless, which is weird because Bungie advertised Neomuna as a city brimming with life. The two new introduced characters both feel off. The old guy, who was actually an ok character, was just there to be killed off half way through the story. Meanwhile the young guy is the abomination of some zoomer-writer at Bungie trying to incorporate Marvel humor and weird Twitter antics into the Destiny universe. Also no idea why they had to slaughter Calus so much with this DLC. Neither did he deserve this, nor does it make any sense after setting up him and us working together for years. I guess now we will never get Menagerie 2.0.

The music, cutscene and sound design teams did a great job as usual.

TLDR: Strand is great, everything else not so much.\t\t\t\t\t
