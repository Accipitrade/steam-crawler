\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis will be sectioned off into 2 parts. Narrative and Gameplay. I will preface by saying the gameplay heavily outweighs the narrative in terms of recommending because the main story takes up a good 4-6 hours while the gameplay can span beyond 100+ hours. So take the narrative criticisms as only a small fraction of the overall product. But as someone heavily invested in the lore and universe of Destiny, I have some choice thoughts.

Narrative (***Spoilers***):
I completed the campaign on legendary, had some initial thoughts then decided to let it sit for a good 24hrs. What happened? The Witch Queen was peak Destiny. The writing was phenomenal, the tension and world building was immaculate. The following seasons further pushed the interest in lore and proposed many questions that intrigued. The build up rose and rose and rose just to... fizzle out? The writing here in Lightfall feels uninspired and leaves more questions than answers in a pivotal part of the story. The tone feels so disconnected from the situation at hand. This is the end, this is where light falls. These are the final chapters where doomsday is imminent.. yet it feels like nothing more than B plot. They say the stakes are high, but offer nothing to spike urgency. They said, "We lost." and my only response is, "if you say so I guess?" because there is no weight to those words here. The term, "we lost" is only as effective as the moments that precede it. The only loss I feel is the unfathomably wasted potential. Any attempt at striking emotion is met with a feeling of contempt. Shoehorning in the death of a character we met 20 minutes prior in a feeble attempt to invoke emotion feels greatly underwhelming and criminally cliche. And in what is supposed to be the climax of the light/dark saga, where once again the stakes are supposedly very high, this being the only "major" death feels like Bungie is playing it far too safe to their own detriment. It's a whole lot of whats and whys in a chapter that should be answering questions, not producing more. For a franchise that was previously heavily criticized for it's weak writing, had it's rising from the ashes moment in a fantastic comeback story, it feels like we've fallen back into the same old tropes Destiny was once infamous for. Show, don't just vaguely tell. There's no narrative substance here and overall I am left feeling confused in a terrible way and absolutely disappointed.

Gameplay:
This is where I think Bungie did a great job and where most of your time will be spent (obviously). Gameplay wise, it was intriguing and innovative for Destiny. Strand is a blast to use and has a pretty good variety with its build potential. I just wish the cooldowns outside of campaign were faster (grapple outside of campaign feels almost meaningless when on a multi-minute cooldown timer) but that's something that can be fixed with updates and build crafting. The missions were fun and had enough variety to keep me engaged. Solo legendary felt just right for what I was expecting to be a good challenge. The Tormentors are a fantastic addition and when they said they wanted them to be the most terrifying enemy we'd face, they were not wrong in that statement. They have rather smart AI, a solid set of moves and debuffs, and an execution that is *chefs kiss* Tormentors get a solid A+ here. I hope they get added into some other activities. Neomuna is a beautiful destination. Can feel barren but taking the lore into consideration, it makes sense and is pretty eerie. Has alot of environmental story telling and a few fun public events to battle through. The strike is also very neat and has an exciting character reveal. Overall, the gameplay is great and perhaps the best Destiny has ever been.

To finish here, I know the language here can make it seem like I HATE this DLC. I don't, there's still a lot to really like here. But the story, the main thing I am strongly invested in, crumbled. The story misses almost every mark while the gameplay further innovates on what we all know and love, but as Destiny DLCs are centered around the progression of the overarching story, that part is a no from me. But for all the QOL changes, new subclass, new weapons, strikes, world activities, upcoming raid and dungeon, I love it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI rarely review anything. But as someone who has dedicated a lot of time to this game I can't go without saying something.

I have never regretted buying game as much as I regret buying this DLC. It is an immense disappointment. Honestly I'm not even sure if that is descriptive enough.

There's a lot of specific things I could talk about but it would spoil a lot of the narrative and I don't want to do that. But I guess I can summarize it like this:

Very little in this DLC makes sense in the canon of Destiny. It feels like all of the lore has been completely retconned to the point of not even mattering any more. Characters with huge legacy are just ended in the worst most undignified way. The new subclass, which is honestly very underwhelming, being a huge part of the campaign was a monumental mistake and as a narrative, again, makes zero sense. As much as people ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ bout Beyond Light, the campaign was flawless in comparison.

And unfortunately, very little was answered, very little was clear the new characters and their interactions are just ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ cringey and off putting to the point of flat out ruining moments over and over and over. Like some of the NPC's I just flat out hate visiting they are so cringe.

A positive I guess is the art and sound teams, but the art and sound teams have been carrying the franchise since Forsaken honestly.

I thought Curse of Osiris was bad, this is worse. I'd play Curse of Osiris again just to make sure but.... hah! yeah.\t\t\t\t\t
