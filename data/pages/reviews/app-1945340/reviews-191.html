\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game play is fantastic, and I've really never had as much fun as I am with Destiny 2 as I am right now. Strand is fantastic and dynamic, making me change up how I've been playing for so long and allow me to think about certain abilities differently than I did before. The more challenging content is great and makes it a lot more engaging than the simple run through with a Grenade launcher kill fest.

The visuals as always are fantastic, the visual design of Neomuna are a fantastic change from the greys, greens, and blues we're so used to seeing used everywhere. With some breathtaking scenes like seeing Calus' ship for the first time on the surface, or the massive fleet of Cabal in orbit in front of the tower.


Now, for the elephant in the room, the story:


IMO, the campaign is great AND story where great.

A gripe I’ve heard a lot is Rohans death being so short into the story without getting to know him well. The point was that we didn’t get to know him, and didn’t need to get to know him FROM Rohan.

In the exotic quest for deterministic chaos and the interludes we get to hear how Rohan effected nimbus personally and since we spend a lot of time with nimbus, it was about how it affected NIMBUS not US.

As for posing a lot of questions that weren’t answered in the initial campaign, how is that a bad thing?

Spoiler Warning

And regarding the veil, if you played through that same exotic quest for deterministic chaos, we find out that the veil is a copy of the black heart, referred to as the “pale heart”, or the heart of the traveller. So by connection, making an inference based off of the witness going after it with Calus while keeping the traveller captured and protected by himself, that he needed the travellers heart (the veil) to ‘access’ the traveller.

And for the “karate kid” esk training mission, I saw it as us obtaining as much offensive power as we could to use against the shadow legion and try and stop Calus. It’s like Thor obtaining storm breaker to be able to defeat Thanos. Not a waste of time, but rather a necessary means to an end (the end being to defeat the shadow legion).

We where shown during gameplay why strand was so important since it was able to bring down some of the most formidable enemies that the shadow legion had. And since they presumably didn’t know we would have access to this power it was our best chance.

I don’t agree that the story was “chasing the McGuffin” since the story bears happened in this fashion: we tried a -> b (fighting them head on) and suffered major casualties. Then we tried to do a -> c by going around b (the witness) and focusing on Calus and trying to defeat him, but since we knew they would be too powerful for us (from seeing how easily they brought down the guardians that where sent out), we went to a sort of side path to C through our training and working with the cloudstriders.

This story is great, one of my favourites since it DOESNT just line it all up for us and leaves us wondering what will be happening next without a clear or even hint at what the witness will do, since we have practically no reason to know without him just telling us (which would be worse) or it being done.


Now certainly there are some things I had issues with, some minor visual bugs in one of the cutscenes, and some minor connection issues with the servers.

But some issues with the story I can agree with is seeing what the rest of the vanguard where doing. We can assume they where doing some sort of logistics/leading in the HELM, but we only see them twice in cutscenes and nowhere else. Personally this doesn't really take anything away from the overall story at this point in it, but definitely could've been improved.

Overall I'd give it a 8.5/10, TLDR: Some minor gripes with the story and some technical issues aside make this one of my favorite Destiny 2 DLC's yet.\t\t\t\t\t
