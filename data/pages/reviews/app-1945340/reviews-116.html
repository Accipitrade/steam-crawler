\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not as bad as you might think. If you compare this to witch queen which came before then Lightfall will come out as lacking. However on its own, it can hold up and is definitely better than most dlc expansions D2 has had.

Pros:
- Campaign gameplay and level design were all fun. Almost as good as WQ however not as long when compared. Nevertheless, the combat felt engaging and legendary difficulty (which is what I recommend for you to play in) felt more balanced as compared to WQ.
-Strand is fun. The new subclass feels really good and the best part about it is that it was not really released in such an OP broken state like Stasis was in PvP. Only gripe is how much it was pushed in the campaign which is another issue.
- The QoL updates brought with the DLC is just too good. No downsides here.
- The loot so far are all fanstastic. I haven't gotten all the God rolls and exotics but I haven't been disappointed with what I got so far.

Cons:
-Narrative: This probably the biggest let down from the whole DLC and is probably the biggest sour point people have. People have probably explained it more before so I'll just give some short pointers on this:
--Given that this should be Destiny's "Infinity War" the ending felt lacking and we were left with more questions rather than answers. I am aware that the story will be continued through the seasons but for me that shouldn't be the standard and that wouldn't be fair to people who didn't or will not buy the seasons.
SPOILERS:
--Another bad point from is the veil, a super important thing in the campaign. Which I have never heard of in my 2000 hours of playing Destiny 2. From the start of the campaign, characters talk about the veil and how important it is without explaining anything to you. The game assumes that you already know what it is from leading the lore books or lore cards or lore from armour or guns.
--Strand. Strand is fun and is actually good but the way they just shove it down your throat ruins the whole pacing and feel of the campaign.

To put this in summary, I'll go back to the Infinity War analogy, you are an Avenger, you are tasked to get the infinity stones (the veil) before Thanos (the witness) but they don't explain what an infinity stone is and what it does, you just go do it. Then you discover this new weapon (strand) so everyone is telling you to train and master it like it will beat Thanos with the stones. Fast forward to the end, you got the weapon, you use it but Thanos wins. But instead of snapping he leaves, you don't what he will do, you don't know what he can do, you're just left there with your weapon with no answers.

Overall:
If Witch Queen is a 9, then Lightfall is a 7 with the story holding it back the most. Which honestly is more of a feedback to how Destiny 2 tell its story and other important narrative points rather than the campaign itself.

Sidenote:
You'll probably see a lot of hate on Nimbus. Best way for me to describe them is that they are the Robin to Rohan's Batman. Once I look at them that way, I enjoyed the character more and it made more sense to me on how they act. The hate on the quips I would blame on "Marvel Fatigue" rather than bad character design or bad writing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an incredible addition to Destiny.

There is an issue with the story where it doesn't progress the way you would expect a penultimate expansion's story but everything else across the board is 10/10. I think they are saving the storytelling for the seasonal content which is not very fun as you would also have to buy the seasons separately or together if you got the deluxe

Great new subclass, great quality of life improvements (passive artifact perks, load outs, mod screen, champion stunning brought to menu). They also made the content more challenging which I like but I feel like the rewards can be a bit better.\t\t\t\t\t
