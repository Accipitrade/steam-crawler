\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is from a very, very supportive Destiny fan. Even when D2 was at its low in Warmind, I fond something positive. I could feel the bones of a great game and slowly but surely, Bungie improved upon it. Added and iterated it to become fun. I'm not even good at shooters. You can ask my clanmates and LFG mates. I'm terrible at shooters. I can't jump worth anything in Destiny. Falling death by architects is probably my number one reason to die. AND YET I STILL PLAY THIS GAME.

So, when Lightfall hit I was beyond excited. After the triumph that was Witch Queen, I was ready for the Lightfall to be Bungie's Infinity War moment. A cumulation of gameplay innovation and storytelling.

***NO SPOILERS IN THIS REVIEW*** Suffice to say, Lightfall is far, far lacking and a tremendous disappointment from Bungie. The narrative makes absolutely zero sense. The dialogue is cringe and makes no sense especially from the Cloud Striders whose civlization was removed from humanity hundreds and hundreds of years ago but talks like California surfer dude from 1995 complete with fist bumps. The ending is one basic gigantic cliffhanger except I don't know what the cliffhangar was about. Some event X happened but no one knows why X is a bad thing because nothing has really changed since X.

Strand being introduced is clumsy and again, makes no sense. It just existed randomly in the Neomuna and WE, the player, randomly found it?? On our first visit to the planet? Nevermind the hundreds of citizens and the Cloud Striders? I thought, at least, the Cloud Striders would know of its existence. The subclass is undercooked. Has potential but players still have to wait to unlock all fragments in order to assess its potential. Strand should have been a separate season instead of shoehorned into the expansion.

1 New Strike. No new Gambit map. No new Crucible map that I can see. 1 new Season which is separate from the Lightfall expansion. This whole expansion is a dud. Definitely do not recommend nor say it is worth the money Bungie is charging for it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter finishing the campaign of lightfall I'm rather mixed with my feelings and left unsatisfied.
For context I've LOVED witchqueen, I think that was peak D2 expansion so going into lightfall I was very excited and had rather high expactations.

Let's start with the pros:

-Calus & The Witness: I loved the interactions between Calus and the Witness, everytime they were on the screen I was deeply interested as to what they were saying and I really enjoyed the Witness in particualar.

-Gameplay: the gameplay, (the new subclass) strand feels amazing, swinging around with strand simply feels really good. as of writing this review most of the fragments are still locked but I think build crafting with strand will get really interesting and really fun.

-QoL updates: there are a lot of QoL updates to go over but if you've played destiny 2 before you'll appreciate them.

-buildcrafting: I've only looked a small amount into buidcrafting but I can already tell that I'm gonna have a really fun time mixing and matching my weapons and mods together

Now to my cons:

-The story: the story was rather.... rough. I was going into the story hoping for a Witchqueen level of writting but all I was left with was more questions and dissapointment. It feels as almost a lot of the story was cut out and the pacing was all over the place, it felt like, again, a lot of it was cut.

-The heavy focus on strand during the campaign: like I stated before I LOVE strand but during the campaign it just felt more annoying then fun to play with. mainly because there was a enourmous focus on strand during the campaign which felt really forced and not really all that fun, it's fun once you unlock it but not during the campaign.

-The difficulty and length of the campaign: Ok for this last point I have to mention that the campaign felt waaaayy shorter then Witchqueens, maybe it's just a feeling I had but Witchqueens campaign was around 8-9 hours whilst Lightfalls is maybe 4-5 hours.
The difficulty of the campaign is also rather too low, I finished the campaign on legend with some of my friends with almost no issues at all, compared to Witchqueen where I struggled a lot of times, maybe this is a result of the Subclass 3.0 update but the campaign itself didn't quite have that challenge as Witchqueens.

*Edit
It has been a couple of days since lightfalls release and overall I must admit it has been pretty enjoyable. Now of course that doesn't change the fact that the narrative is horrible but in all honesty the gameplay/ playing around with strand and building/ crafting loadouts has been a ton of fun. it's still too early to give me review of season of the defiance but from what I've played I enjoy the activity even if it is just a glorified strike.
I'm still very hesitant to recommend this dlc to anyone but if you're not someone who's gonna care for the narrative of this dlc and is more in for the new subclass/ loot I think you might find something enjoyable in here\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI understand the frustration with the expansion and i frankly can't disagree. The story of this expansion was a huge step down from witch queen, the expectations that were set by Bungie themselves were not met. That being said this does do a few things right. Strand is a breath of fresh air, feeling different then anything I've seen before. The campaign missions were fun and challenging, although they focused a bit too much on strand. Neomuna is breathtaking, probably the most interesting destination in the visual sense. The soundtrack is amazing to literally nobody's surprise. New guns and armor both look and perform well, and it has been a joy to grapple around previous destinations. Unfortunately that's where the praise ends. Although Neomuna is beautiful, it feels empty, with a handful of patrols and public events you've done a million times already. Expanding on the campaign, I personally enjoyed the addition of the cloudstriders. It's something new for destiny and i really wish more time was spent fleshing them out. Nimbus was enjoyable and I'm not entirely sure where the hate for them is coming from, especially after the post campaign exotic quest expanded on them more. One of the main critiques that has gone around is the lack of an explanation for anything going on in the campaign. As a D1 player who actively follows the lore, i'm just as lost as my friend who just started playing. That should not be how i feel after the "Beginning of the end of the light and dark saga".

In the end if you actively play destiny, you've already bought this expansion, left a negative review and then continued playing. Most D2 players tend to react strongly and i don't blame them. However the expansion isn't an affront to nature like some reviews would have you believe. Ultimately it's your call as to if you want to play or not. I hope that Bungie can have a strong season this year, but I'll admit my trust has wavered. If you can get this on a sale it's worth it for the green silly string, currently not worse then Shadowkeep in my humble opinion.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is not the quality we got in Witch Queen!
The entire story for the campaign felt poorly written.

This expansion feels like it was entirely built around the Strand subclass. That is not a good thing.
The campaign itself exists pretty much as a prolonged and painful Strand tutorial.

The new Strand subclass itself is a major let down if you are a Titan class player. For Titans Strand is built for close range fighting, but it lacks DPS capability to really be able to properly pull off close range fighting. Strand DPS is very lacking in a boss fight. The much vaunted grappling mechanic for the game is amusing but feels more like a gimmick thank a reliable weapon. Also, having the grappling counts as your grenade, so you are lacking any usable innate grenade ability. Oddly enough for strand this seems to be less of an issue though as the other two grenade options are Strandlings, and Bolos. The strandling grenade provides a wide area of coverage but is very lacking in its DPS. The bolo grenade provides crowd control, which the titan barrier also already does.

This expansion also pushed a lot of revamps live into the game. A core principle Bungie went after was to make the game harder. To do this, they changed how well armor quality can enhance player abilities. They smoothed out progression in armor quality a small bit but they also nerfed the cap. Tier 10 bonuses now acts like Tier 8 used to prior to this expansion. They also increased timers on many grenade and melee abilities.

The entire armor modification system was overhauled. Instead of 3 types of sockets on armor there are now 2 types. The combat style mods were mostly commissared. The maimed survivors were pushed into the general socket slots. While much simpler than the old system, the new system clearly feels truncated and limiting compared to what we had before.


Recommend avoiding this Expansion. If you MUST get it because you want the Season Pass stuff, consider just getting the Season Passes on their own, or buying this on a VERY steep discount at a later date.\t\t\t\t\t
