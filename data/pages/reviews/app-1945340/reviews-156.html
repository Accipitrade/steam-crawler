\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you don't carry about the story or characters then this is a positive review, the Gameplay is solid, the sections with Strand are a lot of fun, the Legend campaign is difficult without being too frustrating if you have friends. The Story falls flat on it's face and the Characters are... not good. More detail below by sections.

Story: Starting with the worst point, the story is terrible. It opens with a great cinematic which makes you think this really could be an apocalypse, that we have to survive not win, and all that drops as soon as you land on Neptune. The instant you do that it goes from a dark tale about survival to an 80's action movie with the tropes turned up to eleven. it's fun if you're looking for that but completely breaks with the tone of everything up to this moment. Nothing is serious and major plot points are never explained to the point the entire story is a hunt of the plot device. The few serious moments you get are usually interrupted by a new character throwing out a cheesy one-liner. Some people are hoping the Season and Raid will fix a lot of the issues and they might, eventually.

Gameplay: The new subclass Strand is fun and the segments of the campaign where you get it to use are a lot of fun, the final boss fight in particular is an excellent arena to demonstrate it's potential. However outside of these preset arenas for Strand it plays a bit differently, so you aren't getting an accurate experience of what it will be afterwards. That being said the community is finding out a lot of ways to use it very effectively, and it is still strong. I will warn you however going through the campaign on Legend while solo is a sometimes frustrating challenge, even though they've tried to make it easier for solo player one unlucky move or hit will send you back to a checkpoint.

Characters: The Cloud Striders are an interesting contrast to us as Guardians, while still fighting for similar ideals. My only complaint about them is that the story doesn't really give enough time and focus on them to get attached, and a lot of the time we do get with them is spent having them make corny jokes or imitating classic 80's action movie tropes. Osiris is a returning character for any player who's been around even for the past two to three weeks and the story does spend some time on his development, but a very small portion of it. The Witness and Calus serve as the main antagonists and some time is spent developing the two of them, but for an expansion which was hyped up as going more into why the Witness was hunting the Traveler and what it wanted you get none of that. Calus has some character development which is most of the few uninterrupted serious scenes you get in this story.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: If you play and enjoy Destiny you will enjoy Lightfall. I rate it 7.5/10 with Shadowkeep being 4/10 and Witch Queen being 9/10.

Most of the negative reviews revolve around the story. Sure it's not as fleshed out as other expansions have been but Lightfall is significantly better than Beyond Light or Shadowkeep. It's not as good from a narrative standpoint as Witch Queen but fundamentally this expansion is an amazing refresh if the game with excellent mechanics and interesting gameplay.

Strand *was* timegated but now all of the perks are unlocked and the subclass feels significantly better. It still needs some tweaking with cooldowns but will definitely come as it did with Stasis and the 3.0 subclasses.

The difficulty increase feels great across the board.

Commendations are a bit half baked but the core of a solid system is there.

The new location is fantastic and the new world event is action packed and very rewarding.

The new seasonal content is a cool take on the "battlegrounds" system we have had this far.

One of my only major complaints is it feels like there is a lack of new weapons in this expansion. I'm not saying "bad reskin of moon weapons" but rather that here is no real new frames. It would have been cool to see some new archetypes with the new strand damage type.

On the "moon reskin" weapons. People are insane to think that the guns are just reskins. All of them have a new damage type with new perks and new combinations of perks. To say the new guns are just reskins is a pathetic attempt at rage bait. Actually take a second and look at the weapon perks. Theybare what define the weapons not the 3d model.

Take it from someone who has +3800 hours in D2 on steam alone: All in all I like this expansion and I'm excited to see what this coming year of Destiny will bring.\t\t\t\t\t
