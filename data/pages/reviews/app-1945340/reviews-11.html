\r\n\t\t\t\t\tStarting with the negatives, Story content is horrific and I pray that whoever decided to ship an unfinished story knows that they are solely responsible for this blunder. Note that when I say "unfinished story," I don't mean the campaign is buggy and unplayable. I mean that the narrative is straight up unfinished. The story certainly reaches a conclusion, but characters, mcguffins, etc are not once ever expanded upon. Wanna know why Calus turned to the Witness? Can't help you. What exactly is a Cloudstrider? No clue. Who is the Witness? No idea. What in the world is a Veil??!?!!?! Sorry, pay us 20 dollars 4x a year so we can slowly expand on it. Excited to see the return of Nezarec? Dies immediately after trolling Neomuna for all of 4 weeks. This DLC has the absolute worst writing ever in Destiny, it is genuinely worse than D1 Vanilla.

For most people, the narrative won't be a deal-breaker. We all know that Destiny isn't known for its amazing narrative and it never has been. The game play/repeatable content was fantastic. Neomuna as a destination is one of the best ever, rivaling The Dreaming City in replay-ability and fun. Avalon is a fantastic secret quest with a compelling narrative contained within itself. Override on Neomuna is a very fun activity, and provides itself as a wonderful testing ground for builds, weapons, and exotics. The exotics and subclass add a lot of flavor and refresh the moment-to-moment game play which was already fantastic.

In my personal opinion, if you're new or returning to Destiny, skip Lightfall and keep waiting until Final Shape. For veterans and concurrent players, this is the same-ol' story as ever and we'll still play this spit-in-the-face narrative and gleam what good there is from it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe only good thing I can say for this expansion is that the gameplay in the story is fun at times.

The bad things are with the immensely disappointing story and mechanics. This has been a force teased for about a decade and this feels like a clobbered and mashed together mess that has worse writing than a Dungeon Master's first improvised D&D campaign. Strand does not fit in this story whatsoever and no amount of heavy lifting the lore writers (usually do) can convince me that this belongs back in Witch Queen and not in Lightfall. Lightfall was originally teased in 2020 and was given it the same kind of aesthetic and teases as The Final Shape did until last August when it had the cyberpunk vibe. I was initially on board for a new setting in Destiny 2 and wont mind it until it had style but is as shallow as a puddle. The Season of the Seraph ending cutscene had a better tone and story than this entire expansion with Neomuna having absolutely no substance or story behind anything you look at beside that ONE graffiti art of the cloudstriders in the past. The cloudstriders provide zero chemistry good or bad with the guardians or vanguard and never interact once even in the seasons (as of Deep) even though they could prove an interesting enemy or ally even with the Final Shape teaser I can't understand why they even introduced the cloudstriders because there is no way they can fit in a narrative like that without feeling somehow more intrusive than Lightfall. On a story perspective, this is far worse than Curse of Osiris because at least Curse of Osiris was a villain of the week while Lightfall ruins any credibility behind the Witness having no forces other than a few Tormentors and the mummified reanimated corpse of Nezarec in the raid not included in the story but in a raid in post. Neomuna's story is the most pointless and lackluster event I've ever seen in Destiny and lacks less story than most seasons other than the Witness. It is a decent end to the Caiatal arc but then again it could've just been a season with how inconsequential this story is. Strand is awful and the grapple, being the main selling point, was executed poorly with it taking up an important grenade slot with a very long regen time and having no useful perks for it other than the woven mail status.

Crucible is STILL in a disgusting state with damage still being dependent on frame rate which is disgustingly bad. Servers are in a worse state than I've seen with outages lasting several hours on random. Season of Defiance that came with this expansion was weak as well with the death of Amanda being predictable and poor. And somehow, Season of the Deep provided more unique gameplay and story than the expansion and starting season COMBINED.

All in all, this is just an awful expansion that was a massive letdown after something as great as Witch Queen and the only thing Lightfall did better than Witch Queen is that it was a bit more fun than Witch Queen with Tormentors sometimes. This feels like the filler in between Witch Queen and Final Shape and was all mushed together in Neomuna with some of the laziest writing I've ever seen with lore writers writing significantly better stories for someone like Rhulk back in Witch Queen which was optional as a boss and to read.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've loved Destiny since it came to PC back in 2017. Some of my best memories in gaming can be attributed to this game. I however cannot recommend this game in good conscious. Several years of very poor game design decisions have led bungie down a horrible path putting it in the worst state since vanilla D2.

Bungie has consistently catered to casuals that play the game for 1hr a week (if that) because they are the vocal majority unfortunately. Due to this, any time the game gets challenging things get immediately nerfed. Day 1 raids which used to be an achievement are like sub 2 hour events just so that bungie can make more money selling raid jackets and off twitch rivals viewership.

Loot is now meaningless. Crafting was introduced in witch queen and is one of the worst things bungie has done to the game. There is no loot pursuit anymore. Instead you login for 30mins a week, get your guaranteed red border, and log off until you hit 5/5. Did you get some loot along the way? Just shard it because you'll just craft it later.

Despite all the glaring problems and community unrest, Bungie will not acknowledge the issues. Instead they try to sell merch for the latest social issue they want to parrot while pretending the game is fine.

In catering to the casual base to an overwhelming degree, Bungie have forgotten who their core base is. Lightfall and the last two seasons have seen almost my entire friends list (mostly the hardcore crown consisting of Top 100 raid teams) quit the game entirely.

The only way things will get better is if we all stop playing. I was once VERY vocal and passionate about the issues facing the game over the years. Now I am at a place of apathy...I just dont care.

I hope the recent outcry from the community is a wake up call for bungie. I want the game to return to it's former self but I am doubtful that will ever happen again. Until then I recommend everyone else spend their time and money on other games.

STAY AWAY FROM DESTINY 2\t\t\t\t\t
