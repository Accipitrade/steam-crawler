\r\n\t\t\t\t\twith someone that has 1000+ hours in Destiny 2, I would not recommend Lightfall. At best, a 6/10 but there are still many issues.

Lightfall has WONDERFUL scenery and beautiful game play but when it comes to story, there are many blanks. First off, the Veil has yet to be explained, and even though they market Neomuna as a bright, vibrant city, but there are no citizen and just a bunch of random dialogue from the AI form of the citizens. As you go through the whole campaign, Osiris teaches you how to use Strand. The campaign is more like a play ground to understand and use strand then an actual story and game play driven campaign. Also, most of the strand sub-classes fit into the typical stereotype of each class. For example, the Titan stereotype, being a tank and close combat is exactly what is portrayed. There melee is a close up and they don't have another option along with there super. Also, they made excising content (Vanguard) much harder. While in vanguard, you will ALWAYS be below the power level. I like that they made there game hard, but when I run a master, or even legend lost sector, the new raid (Root of Nightmare) feels easier game play and mechanic wise then any of the lost sectors.

Now, strand viability. Strand is at best is mediocre subclass. To fully unlock the potential of strand, you must spend hours, or days depending on how much you play to fully unlock strand. Once you have collected enough strand meditation you get access to all the existing fragments and each 2 of the aspects. Every subclass also has one melee and one super. All guardians do have the three grenades. Comparing Strand to the other 4 existing subclasses, it just doesn't do as well compared to them. As a titan main, I feel that strand only has so much to offer compared to other subclasses like solar, void, or arc. While strand is fun, you don't have as much survivability as solar, or even void.

Lightfall is far from perfect, especially bug wise. The new exotic glaive (winterbite) has been disabled for a bug were it is doing to much damage. They also nerfed synthosteps, with glaives because synthosteps were buffing glaives to much. Also, there are random times when the strand grapple ability just doesn't work. Now to raid bugs. The raid, Root of Nightmare, was very fun, not so much challenging. What did make the raid challenging were the bugs. On the second encounter, scission, there are catapults that you must use to launch yourself across the chasm to activate the nodes. While going back and forth, there has been many times where I and my teammates have died. After scission, it is pretty straightforward until Nezarec. While fighting Nezarec, you will have to shoot his shoulders. What they are suppose to do is to light up black or white. There has been many times for my team and others team where there is no explosion coming from his shoulders. This will cause the team to wipe, unless they can brute force the timer. Overall, the raid is pretty simple and rather easy besides the bugs and glitches.

At best, lightfall was decent. Lightfall has its ups and downs. If you are more into game play and enjoy challenges, you might enjoy lightfall greatly. On the other hand, if you are more into understanding the story, you might not be so lucky with this one my fellow lore buddies. If you are just staring Destiny 2, your better bet would to purchase something like Witch Queen to help get you started into the game rather then something like Lightfall. Overall Lightfall was fun, but it still has its many downsides.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen it comes to Destiny, everything in gameplay is summed up to: What are you shooting at and with what do you shoot them. And this is solid as always. each DLC usually brings more tools to use on somewhat new targets. And although it seems enough. This is why I gives the Thumbs up. on the mechanical level, all works. Strand is neat, new weapons and new enemies ? The Shadow Legoin might look cool but they act the same as any other Cabal with only the small Darkness obelisk to give them more defence like the light moths. you might argue that they sould feel the same since they are the same. but then again, we saw in the past that altering a race alligment can change them drastically in behavior. just like the Taken. Bungie could have given them some new gimmicks beyond a tiny obelisk.

Ive waited for so long to post my review because I had hope for the raid the show somethig new in its aftermath. In Forsaken we got an entire new area, in Beyond Light the debris falling from the sky and Clovis. In WQ we got some nice effects and interactions. And here... some collectibles with no lore. The Raid itself was fun but somehow... lacking. like it could have been longer.

And the final part we all know was coming. The Veil and the story. If only we knew more about it or what is its supposed ot be, we would have been more stasified by protecting it. It is connected somehow to the Black Heart which we also know barely anything about. And the story ? I remember the devs talling how this time the new subcalss would be easier to unlock but you still need to finish the entire campagin to unlock it. The use of Strand as a device to further to plot was really not needed. Also I want to compare it to WQ. Last year we had interactions with the villain, massive lore drops from every mission. In Lightfall... none. Even in Beyond Light we had interactions with Eramis. despite not being great DLC, hearing Variks shouting at Eramis really felt as he felt boiling hatered. none this year came at Calus like that or even close to it.

Bungie, please. Make the Final shape Nerratively good. We know you can, you did it many times. next DLC is the true money shot.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWelp, here we are. Bungie has made a sweeping series of changes that have affected the game in some positive and some negative ways. With Lightfall came a set of UI updates and systems changes, core playlist changes, a complete overhaul of the armor mod system, and some champion reworks that take subclass verbs into consideration. This is of course on top of the Lightfall Expansion itself, which brings its own campaign missions, the new destination Neomuna, and the exciting new Strand subclass for all Guardians.

The things that Lightfall does well are areas where Bungie and Destiny on the whole have not really faltered in the past, the gameplay with Strand is fresh and exciting, with multiple new ways to fight enemies and engage with activities. The Grapple ability is an exciting addition to the Destiny sandbox that adds a lot of speed and verticality to its PVE combat. The complexity that Strand adds as the 'Action per minute' subclass as Bungie calls it, is a welcome change to some of Destiny's more static PVE combat in an era where most Warlocks are stuck using Well of Radiance in anything harder then a legend nightfall.

Speaking of game difficulty, one of the more divisive changes Bungie has made is a complete rebalancing of the games Player Versus Enemy content. As the game has gone on more and more Guardians are starting to experience the power creep that is inevitable in a game where you are constantly collecting gear that makes you stronger and stronger. This results in Neomunas Patrol zone feeling more like a fight for your life then a casual stroll through the Cosmodrome might feel. While some players are enjoying this new difficulty I worry that it has skewed parts of the game that are meant to be relaxed and low stress to become excercises in frustration. The shift in difficulty is hard to accurately gauge given that Bungie dealt a double nerf to Guardians by also completely overhauling the armor mod system. Gone are Elemental Wells, Charges of Light, and Warmind Cells (good riddence), replaced by the new Armor Charges. Armor Charges are a more universal system that attempts to offer the best of the three previous systems in a much more simplified package. I say attempts because not everything was given a 1:1 comparison. Certain mods that were centerpoints of many builds were not brought forward into this new system such as Elemental Shards for stasis, Seeking Wells and Bountiful Wells, and also the Quick, Swift and Rapid charge mods. These are only a handful of the mods that were Thanos snapped out of existence, but they are easily the most sorely missed.

This dramatic shift in difficulty may also be due in part to the age of the Tiger Engine showing. Destiny was originally played on seventh gen consoles that could only push 30 frames per second, now people are playing Destiny 2 on current gen consoles and PC, with framerates that are easily double if not triple what it was designed around. This results in strange behavior with enemy damage such as Psion snipers and Cabal flame throwers dealing more damage at higher frame rates. Whether this is tied to frame rate or the games internal tickrate and netcode is not definitively known outside of Bungie, but as a regular player I have definitely noticed a difference in damage between 30 and 60 FPS.

Without getting into heavy spoiler territory, the narrative for Lightfall is not Bungies best work. If you are coming from Witch Queen expecting a self contained narrative that poses and answers its own questions, you will not find it here. This is very troubling given that this is the penultimate chapter of Destinys 'Light and Dark' saga that started with Forsaken (or Destiny 1 if you want to be reallllllllly petty). Lightfall brings several new questions and few new answers, which normally is not a problem, but when you are this late in the game its somewhat tiresome and cliche to be asked to care about a mcguffin that you as a player know nothing about when every character around you seems to know every last detail about it, and just refuses to say anything about it. The campaign feels more like a vehicle to deliver the Strand tutorial then it does an actual conflict that our Guardians must resolve. This, combined with some very odd characterization of known characters, as well as some questionable writing for a new character, results in a narrative that is both frustrating to try to understand and ultimately unsatisfying to follow to a conclusion.

All that said, if you are a die hard Destiny fan, and you have already sacrificed thousands of your real life meat hours to Bungies nearly perfected Skinner box, then you are likely already playing Lightfall and this review will do nothing for you, but if you arent, and you like cheesy 80-90s action movies complete with training montages, and truly awful puns and dialog, then you might like Lightfall. I did not care for the narrative, its characters and characterization, or the vast changes Bungie made to the game as a whole. Quite simply, I am no longer having the fun I was before Lightfall, but there is still fun to be had.
