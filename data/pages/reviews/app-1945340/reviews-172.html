\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA chase-the-MacGuffin story without explaining the MacGuffin. I was not able to relate with the new characters during the campaign. I expected better writing from Bungie at this point. This was supposed to be the story that answered the looming questions from years' past. They sadly fell short. Additionally, this expansion was marketed as you saving a hidden human city from invasion. During the patrol sections, the new zones feel just the same as the other worlds in Destiny 2. It doesn't feel like an invasion. The city also feels very lifeless. No natives moving around. It feels like a ghost town almost. I felt like I overpaid for this expansion.

Gameplay is fun. Gunplay is still solid. While overly pushed in the campaign, the new subclass Strand is fun to play with.

If you've been consistently buying the expansions year after year to see the whole story through, then sure, I suppose I can recommend this expansion to you, preferably at a sale though. If you're new or a returning player, I'm sorry to say that this campaign will likely not be a good return for you.\t\t\t\t\t

still haven't moved to a new powerful game engine

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI saw someone say "Hot take: Lightfall isn't bad," and I fully agree. Are there some things I dislike about it? Absolutely. I never review games because it usually doesn't matter, but this one kind of bugs me.

TL;DR: I love the expansion because I love the game. I love the expansion because of the beauty and the graphics, even if the story content isn't what we all wanted it to be (thus far)

Today, 03.02.23, is the THIRD DAY that Lightfall has been out. THIRD DAY! There will be bugs, there will be things people won't like about the game, there will be tweaking and balancing and that is okay because it is literally the third day that the expansion has been released. This is about as far as you can get without getting a few spoilers, but I will try my best to redact them as much as I can.


My number one problem with this campaign is that this feels more of a story about how to use strand, not a story about the game, and it seems like most of us agree. I have no idea what The Veil is, I have no idea what The Witness is doing twiddling his thumbs inside the Traveller with all his lil ships, I also would love to know why the Traveller's attack just created weird red bushes, but at the end of the day, someway somehow I know that Bungie will turn it around to connect. That's exactly how I felt playing through the seasons during Seraph when I had missed 75% of the story line after I stopped playing when Risen ended. When the final cutscene of Seraph happened, things started to make sense. I still had questions of course, but these little random lore pieces that were seemingly useless in the beginning came together in the end.

Let me just add to this by saying the tormentors were horrifying on legend, so for anyone who says they weren't as hype as we thought I wholly disagree

Neomuna is so beautiful. It is everything I wanted it to be. There is so much to explore, tiny little spaces I didn't expect to be able to sneak into, gorgeous skies and amazing graphics. Like, wow. Sure, I'm still too low in light level to tear through the place like I own it, but man do I like the activities and lost sectors. I'm also just a fiend for cyberpunk-esque stuff, so that helps a lot.

All in all, if you like the game, I think you'll like the expansion. The devs put a lot of work into this and I intend on playing it to the best of my ability. I truthfully think that the first three days are nothing compared to the rest of the season or the rest of the expansion, whatever they may add. I don't regret spending $100 on the full thing, nor the $150 on the collectors edition.

Now, if you'll excuse me, I gotta go kick some booty on Neomuna.\t\t\t\t\t

My review on the Lightfall campaign

  • Playability
  • Graphics
  • Storyline
  • Characters
  • In-game changes

    Playability was good. I enjoyed the DLC but something was missing which I'll be summarizing in this review.

    Playability was good. You can move around and do stuff of course. The new subclass Strand is amazing, a very cool concept i'd have to say but it feels useless, the cool downs take forever for the grapple and the grenade which needs fixed asap.

    Graphics were good. I wish the planet Neomuna had more lively to it. It looks bland and some of the plants were copy and paste, they didn't sway around which also goes for trees swaying in the wind. What I expected was more ambience to the planet. More sounds to make it more appealing. I also noticed we didn't get anything new from public events; it also was a copy and paste. Why not push the payload on Savathuns throne world? I also wanna put out that i've hardly seen randoms on the planet at max i've seen 3.

    Storyline, well the storyline based on the DLC showed and it was amazing coming from a lore fanatic, but the campaign was lacking. While playing through it and watching the cut scenes I was lost on what was going on, I expected more of the campaign like The Witch Queen. Beating Calus was honestly easy in legendary. Me and 2 friends it took us 10 minutes to kill him only. Wish there was more in the campaign.

    Characters, cloud striders were pretty cool, I thought there would be more than one to be honest. Not much to say about this though, just wish there were more characters added that we could've seen.

    In-game changes, there were a lot of in-game changes from the tower they added a Cayde-6 memorial after like what 5-6 years?? They should've done that when he died. I like the up-voting/commendations in activities, the journey section to show the reputation/rank you earned throughout the years of playing. Loadouts is what we needed which is an amazing addon same thing with mod customization pretty cool addons. Lightfall brought 9/10 or 10/10 content apart from the campaign. Campaign is more like a 6.5/10, there are details that don't get shared with you that all the NPCs seem to know about which makes it harder to get emotionally invested. Very excited for the raid, I'll edit the review if the raid is bad but I sincerely doubt it.

If you need an incredible main campaign that hits all the emotional spots, give this a miss. If you are okay with a mediocre shooter campaign followed by tons of pretty good repeatable content and gameplay systems that feel amazing, pick it up! Destiny hasnt felt this good in forever.\t\t\t\t\t
