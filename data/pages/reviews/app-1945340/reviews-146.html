The intro mission preys upon you nostalgia. The only mission worthy of being called "legendary" was the final boss fight. Art work is amazing, as always. Neomuna completely lacks diversity in aesthetic from one region to another, although the lost sectors are actually very cool, but I didn't pay $50 for lost sectors.

Only some of the cut scenes were amazing, and most of the music was actually from the Red War Campaign (I remember only one truly distinct, new song for Lightfall). Most of the missions were short, boring arenas in public areas for playing with strand and made absolutely no contribution to the story.

This campaign is completely beneath Bungie's capability and is uninspired, low-effort garbage. Whoever in leadership made this call needs to be fired and should consider diverting his career path to burger flipping. But even burgers require care and a certain degree of attention to detail to be good.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think like a lot of other players I have been playing Destiny since D1 vanilla, back then Destiny was new, it was exciting, it was Bungie showing us what they could do after making Halo for almost 10 years. But I remember dropping off the game quickly with nothing new for me or my friends to do. Since then it's been a pretty bumpy road with lacklustre expansions, sunsetting of locations, weapons, and campaigns, and a few design decisions that left me scratching my head.But despite all that there were some real amazing moments that left me coming back for more, Witch-Queen being the most recent one as well as the amazing year of seasons in 2022. But now, almost 10 years later, the light and dark saga is almost at it's end. And this campaign was meant to kick start that ending. Do I think the campaign story fell short? 100%. But I think the overall package of Lightfall is incredible, Neomuna is an amazing place to explore, the weapons are fun and interesting to use, and Strand, geez where to even begin with Strand? This is the most fun I've had with Destiny in awhile, and I usually love most things Bungie brings to the game but Strand has been so much fun to master. I just can't get enough of grappling then swinging down and obliterating my enemies with a strand melee.

There is so much in this expansion that is amazing, and I didn't even touch on the quality of-life updates that Bungie added to the game, but it's being overshadowed by a lackluster story that leaves me asking more questions when I should be getting answers. And that's a damn shame. I hope that Bungie can answer some of these questions through-out the next year of seasons leading into The Final Shape, and they bring it home with a ending that leaves me satisfied and happy putting all this time into this franchise.\t\t\t\t\t
