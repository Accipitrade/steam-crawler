\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who's a big fan of the lore and has been enjoying where Destiny has been going since Witch Queen. Holy crap is this a massive letdown.

My opinion is mostly based in the story but there are many negatives having to do with buildcrafting and gameplay that others could better address than me (strand ability cooldowns being over a minute at 80+ stats, wtf were you thinking). My biggest gripe is that this expansion was more concerned with plot than narrative. The writers knew what they wanted to do, what needed to happen, but when it came to execution and the way everything "flows" its horrendous. This narrative suffers from some of the worst pacing in any story Bungie have made making it feel short even though its roughly just a tad shorter than Witch Queen.

Slight spoilers(if you even care, the story sucks so...):

The missions here are sudden with little to no explanation or dialogue to flesh out the characters or Neomuna itself. The cloudstriders are just suddenly there and your helping them. One to two sentences of the Cloudstiders saying "thought all guardians were bad, but I guess your good" is fine if you're a person who saw the lightfall reveal and trailers leading up to this and already knew cloudstriders would be in the game, but for Osiris and our guardian to just be cool with these random guys and only asking a sprinkling of questions throughout (none of which felt believable), it comes across as rushed. The cloudtriders personalities are stereotypical with Rohan being a generic tough mentor who you hardly get to know by the time the campaign is over and Nimbus. Nimbus.... Oh boy.

Slight tangent incoming-- While Nimbus as a character might be a clear social justice push to insert a nonbinary person into the game and world where no one was asking for it, I'd still be willing to look past it. But the way it was written with characters addressing Nimbus saying "they/them" 5 times in a single sentence comes off as forced and odd. Not to mention this decision to make Nimbus Non-binary has no relation to their character and nobody ever addresses it as if they all psychically knew what pronouns he preferred just by looking at Nimbus despite his clearly male body. It's never actually addressed by anyone other than characters saying "they/them" ad nauseam. Because of this I'm just gonna refer to Nimbus as a guy like many others are because maculine/femine physical traits are real things and our language used to reflect this exists.

All of this slight cringe still could have easily been forgiven if Nimbus was a fun likable character like the staff at Bungie seemed to think he was. Now I'm going to say without exaggeration that Nimbus is the worst character Bungie has ever written. The voice acting was bad, the dialogue was unfunny and felt like borderlands 3 level, and there was no emotional depth to his character. Sounded almost like a parody. You can tell the writers desperately wanted you to like him as a character so they inserted so many quips and Marvel-like dialogue that it hurt to listen to.

Calus and the witness have two cool cutscenes and I hope you savor them because that's all your getting, Other than that Calus laughs at you a couple times and then its over. No depth, no back and forth between him and your guardian, Bungie just made Calus who is one of the most fascinating characters with a huge history have the emotional depth of a random Hive knight. Compare that to Savathun who you spent so much time focusing on in witch queen. This could have been that but with Calus. Instead Bungie chose to deticate FAR TOO MUCH time on strand and Osiris spewing random BS with the Cloudstriders about it. Calus was underutilized and only present in two missions, one of which being the finale which feels so sudden. If you thought this would be the big Witness expansion, you'll have to wait a year to get any answers as to what is going on. Nothing that wasn't already known or assumed was explained regarding the Witness or the Traveler or the Veil.

This expansion is taking a thousand steps back from where Destiny was with Witch Queen's narrative.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis expansion sucks.

1. The Campaign is not a complete story, its an unfinished story that abruptly stops right when things are just getting started. You waste tons of time not really doing anything, then pause and wait for seasonal content to tell the rest. I wanted the full story now, I already waited since Destiny 1 for this moment...

2. Strand sucks. I could suspend all enemies on the entire map indefinitely and I still would not use it. It is weak and mediocre fragments are double time gated, based on a set date, and when ever people finally beat the raid. Using strand increases difficulty by taking away survivability from your class, and giving you mediocre utility. Stasis was better.

3. Enemies are tougher, you are weaker, and enemies will always be tougher. This makes tons of weapons unviable in current state as you will die using them. The nerfed important mods like Powerful Friends which no longer gives stat boosts to mobility, or the melee mod for strength. Mods are so generic now that build variety has diminished.

4. Pretty sure so many weapons, artifact mods, and class abilities are straight up bugged and not working correctly.

5. There is far less content and things to do in the game now. I am not a fan of paying for content I will not get access to until a later date. I bought this because I was expecting a full fledged complete campaign with an ending that finishes the Light vs Darkness Saga as we know it.

The marketing is straight up lies.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall in a nutshell
*Ive only been as far as completing the legendary campaign*
Pros:
Strand is really interesting and I think in the future it will be a very competitive class. Its fun, engaging, thematic, and competitively viable. Maybe except titans. Sorry titans. I feel like it hasn't filled out entirely yet but that's to be expected with a brand new subclass.
New stuff. Exotics, zones, enemies, etc.
Cons:
They have completely destroyed any value mods had. We used to be able to become charged with light with ability usage and now its only from weapon kills and finishers. On top of that its orbs and theres a time limit. Meaning you have to proc your multikill from bullets (incandescent, voltshock, dragonfly, etc wouldnt trigger orb generation for me) and choose to either run through the enemies to grab an orb of light that may have fallen off the map and use your ability IMMEDIATELY, wait until youve cleared the room and risk that it disappears then watch as your armor charge goes away before the next room anyways, or ignore armor charge entirely. They nerfed many of the mods percentages or functionality or just removed them entirely. Also mods that previously could be equiped to any armor piece like the former charged with light mods have been changed so they can only be placed on a specific piece of armor (grenade and melee mods are on arms for example).
The story feels like they rushed outcomes or cut pieces out of middle points to make all the events happen in the limited campaign time. The character interactions dont make sense when they act like they have known each other for years but just met 30 minute ago. *****MINOR SPOILER WARNING***** There's something bad that happens maybe half way though the campaign that I feel I should care about but I really don't because its all so new to me.
Tormentors feel over tuned because "you wanted them to be scary" (their words not mine). If they appear in my GM's I'll lose my mind.

TL;DR: Strand is cool, story is odd, the buildcrafting is dead if any of it was from your mods.
I rate this a 1/5. I paid $100 to get lightfall and I would pay $100 again to delete it entirely from the servers.\t\t\t\t\t
