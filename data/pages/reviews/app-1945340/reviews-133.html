\r\n\t\t\t\t\t *DISCLAIMER: This review contains spoilers for the campaign of Lightfall*
\tAs someone who has played this franchise since November of 2014, put over 6,000+ hours combined into both Destiny 1 & 2, I've been with it through the highs and the lows. To me, Lightfall has to be the most disappointing expansion that Bungie has ever released for Destiny.


STORY
The most underwhelming part of Lightfall is the story, it can only be described as genuinely abhorrent. The plot is extremely predictable, none of the characters (with exception of The Witness and Calus) are written in a likeable or interesting way, the dialogue is insufferable, and the narrative feels EXTREMELY rushed. The story is so bad in fact, you can literally play the opening cut-scene of the expansion and the ending cut-scene back to back, and the story makes just as much sense as if you played it. (someone actually did it here: https://youtu.be/XQFKhwnkok0)

\tLightfall is the penultimate chapter of what Bungie is referring to as "The Light and Dark Saga" within the Destiny universe. An apocalyptic event threatens all that we as guardians know, and the universe as a whole. Sounds dark right? Well for some reason, the entire thematic tone of this expansion is light hearted and fun. Any slightly dramatic moment is completely thwarted by some of the most laugable writing/dialogue I have ever seen.

\tA great example of this is in a cutscene after defeating the final boss of the campaign, Calus. Calus's daughter, Caiatl, is saying her farewells to her father we have just defeated. Instead of this being an emotional moment, a new character literally flys in, jokes about Calus being ugly, and tries to fistbump Caiatl, whose father LITERALLY JUST died in front of her. This level of ignorant writing is present throughout the entire campaign, and unfortunately, isn’t even the worst part.

\tWith this being such an important expansion in the saga, you would generally expect some lingering questions to be answered, especially since there are questions within Destiny's lore that have gone unanswered since 2014. Instead, Lightfall's story decides to leave you completely in the dark, the entire campaign. Every character, even your Ghost, talks about this mysterious device The Witness is pursuing known as the Veil, like they know exactly what it is. But not a single one cares to explain, or even hint at what the Veil actually is to the player. So the entire campaign you feel like you're a moron, and get frustrated as literally everyone talks like you should know what it is already. They spend so much time focusing on the new subclass Strand and injecting comedy into every line of dialogue, they forgot to actually create stakes for not only this expansion, but the upcoming Final Shape. They introduce more questions to remain unanswered that you have to wait a year to find the answer to. So as a result, you have almost no attachment to anything that happens in the story, because there is no tangible reason to care about what you're doing.

\tThe only positive regarding this story is the cut-scenes with Calus and The Witness, but even these fall flat as both characters aren't given enough time in the spotlight. We don’t know why The Witness wants the Veil, the story of Lightfall doesn’t give us any reason or context as to why we should fear The Witness, especially when we’ve trampled everything before it with ease.


STRAND
\tDestiny has always had incredible gameplay, and it's the primary reason it has survived so long despite the many missteps. Strand, the new subclass, is genuinely the only reason I'd argue this expansion is worth purchasing.
\t
\tStrand is RIDICULOUSLY fun, and absolutely the highlight of the expansion. In particular, traversing with the grappling hook makes it hard to use any other subclass now. Adding so many new layers to the combat, it weaves so naturally into the ability/gunplay already present in Destiny, it's genuinely so addicting my build entirely revolves around it. I play on Titan, and it has become my main subclass, the neutral game of strand is stupidly fun, especially with the new exotic leg armor they introduced, and the super feels great.

\tThe one disappointment I have with Strand is how it’s used within the campaign. Bungie implied that Strand would be integral to the campaign, which it very much is. But, for some reason, instead of giving us the subclass at the start of the campaign and us powering it up over the course of the 8 missions, they instead continually give you Strand and strip it away from you, and it’s infuriating. Some sections, they will legitimately give you Strand just to grapple over a pit for 5 seconds and then immediately rip you out of it again. Why? Why not just give us Strand and let us use it for the whole campaign. (Then we wouldn’t have gotten the cringey training montage I guess…)


NEOMUNA AND LOOT
The new destination Neomuna is a interesting, but very, very disappointing destination. Bungie made it seem that Neomuna would have lots of verticality, with its towering buildings and pristine, civilized environments, it was shaping up to be something different to what we've had in the past patrol zones. None of this is true. Neomuna is one of the smallest patrol spaces in the game, and has almost ZERO verticality, it is almost completely flat and feels nearly identical to every other patrol space we have gotten. Most shockingly, it feels completely dead.

\tThere are no people walking around Neomuna, and there's actually a "lore" reason for this. It’s because apparently everyone has uploaded their conscious to a metaverse-esq cloud thing? So instead of people, there are just weird, barely visible holograms everywhere representing people, but all it does is make the city feel even more barren and lifeless.

Neomuna is fun to traverse with Strand because it’s been specifically made with Strand navigation in mind, but other than that it’s a somewhat bland, flat area to run around and do patrols in. As well, a lot of elements of the city seem to be reused assets from Europa in Beyond Light, particularly Breytech architecture elements from DSC in a lot of the indoor spaces in Neomuna.

\tPart of being on Neomuna is leveling up the vendor, and gaining new weapons. This is also a huge let down, as all of the guns acquired from Neomuna are simply reskins of other weapons. There are NO new weapon models introduced, they even joke about the guns being re-skins on one of the lore tabs of the new guns which just feels like it adds even more insult to injury. So you don’t feel the need to even patrol Neomuna because the guns aren’t interesting enough to warrant wasting your time on them.


MISC.
I’ve ranted on for a long time, so for this section I’m just going to list a few more bullet points that I don’t have enough to make independent sections on:
- The raid isn’t out yet at the time of writing this, but I’m sure it will be phenomenal, as Bungie’s raid designers hardly miss
- The legendary campaign was great from a gameplay perspective, Tormentors are INCREDIBLY fun and scary to fight, no complaints there
- The QoL changes are great! (Loadouts, UI, Artifact mods, etc.)
- The new mod system has a lot of good ideas, but the build diversity of the game has been severely downgraded versus the old system
- The Guardian Ranks system, great in concept, absolutely horrible execution, the fact that a person with 2000 hours is the same rank as someone with 100 hours defeats the entire purpose of the system, and it resets every season? Yeah no thanks.


CONCLUSION/TL;DR
I do not recommend Lightfall. Despite having one of the best gameplay additions ever (Strand), it is a dissapointing expansion at possibly the most important moment in Destiny's narrative. As a fan, I can still find fun, but I'd recommend waiting for a sale or skipping it entirely.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, using strand is fun but without fragments it is unusable. The grappling grenade cooldown is way to long (fixed somewhat by fragments and the hunter aspect). If you are here for the story or any destiny lore this is not the DLC for you. The story featured osiris being a whiny child in a rush to find and protect "The Veil". We happen to magically find a new sort of guardian. Bungie made these two characters expendable and forgettable. The only fun part about the campaign is using "enhanced strand" so the cooldowns are non existant. Otherwise there is no story. We have no idea what the veil is. We don't know who the witness is. We don't know why the witness wants the veil. We don't know why strand exists. No questions are answered and only more pointless ones are created. Why did the witness choose calus? What is the veil? Why do we care about the veil? Why is our ghost acting strange?

TLDR-
BUY IF:
You enjoy farming a public event over 3 days for 8 hours a day to unlock all of the fragments. For one of your characters.
You will pay 60-100 USD to swing at a moderate speed
You want champions to be easy to deal with (strand is insane vs all 3)
You like summoning threadlings (little minions)
You want a heavy glaive that freezes stuff
You want a sidearm that has aimbot (sorta)
You want to fight a tormentor (actually a cool enemy)
DON'T BUY IF:
You care about a good/okay story
You care about quality control
You care about a polished experience
You like money more than a somewhat mediocre dlc
You think calus deserves a cool ending/story

Lightfall? More like Lightfail
I have been playing Destiny since D1, and am kind of used to... Questionable choices from developers. But this expansion is a whole compilation of failures. This effect doubles on the fact, that Lightfail was marketed as a breakthrough in gameplay and storytelling, a pivot point in Destiny world where all the forces gather up in an epic clash. It was marketed for long as Infinity War in the realities of Destiny.
So... What did we receive in the end?
Story: I know Destiny was never for genius plot lines, but oh my, this is just horrendous. Forget about tension and epic, there is just n o n e. The actual Witness plotline is touched in two-minute cutscene, that was CUT IN BETWEEN, and in this cut the actual gameplay is fed, having very limited connection to the cinematic. The Neomuna story instead is a pile of tech-space-magic gibberish, fed by two characters, actively competing for the role of the most annoying being ever. The story is not mysterious or progressively unravelling - instead it is a stitched together set of, what feels, concept ideas, without any comprehensive story line or actual explanation of what and why is happening. I refuse to believe that these are same people who created WQ.
Neomuna: much-hyped unique location, branded as a city under siege, fighting for its life. Indeed, some parts of it really look impressive and innovative... but "some" is the keypart here. Other than few points of interest, Neomuna looks and feels empty and soulless. Moreover, what siege are we talking about? There are two =>one active cloudstriders, hanging out in the tower - that's it! No one is fighting for this place, it is not a battlezone. Do not attack the Cabal and it will feel like it's a picture of their city they are just vibing in. I felt more atmosphere in Tangled Shore than here. The style is just not coherent and adequate, Striders are korean-mmo-level strippers, Muna is a discount cyberpunk, loot doesn't have a common theme with either of those. Moreover, half of the city is so blatantly reused from Braytech assets, that at times you wonder, why are you playing Beyond Light. And yes, the "reuse" part will be so, so much worse.
Strand: A very powerful subclass, they said. Giving up your grenade slot for grapple is totally worth it, they said. While Strand is indeed fun to use and adds more dynamic gameplay, its horrendous cooldowns and damage is messed so that I barely see it competing with Stasis or 3.0 classes. Grappling itself is fine, except the times it bugs out and tethers to wrong point / cuts down the thread. But hey, at least the progression is not as awful as Stasis was.
Gameplay: Well, Destiny is Destiny, that's it, not much changed here in the core. However, Lightfail brings a tendency of spawning enemies in LARGE quantities, whole platoons of these. And level designers have, seemingly, forgot how to make levels that are not small confined boxes with four symmetrical pillars for cover. In a summary, this makes legendary campaign a nice experience of being nonstop surrounded by bullet sponges in a square box with little to no cover. Spawn rates is definitely what I would appreciate, but you know, maybe level design should be a stage in development?
Overall quality: just every aspect of this "expansion" is a downgrade to the game. Horrendous incompetent story that feels and acts like a filler, level and asset design with very little effort into it, balance issues, poor gameplay decisions, reusal of everything everywhere to the point where 70% of DLC gives you deja vu feelings - all this product feels like something that was assembled last-minute from what was on table for a technical demo, but instead managed to get to producion and marketing. Even less does it manage to compare itself to previous ones
- all other expansions, while having flaws, were a breakthrough in at least one part of the experience. This one is refined, condensed disappointment from top to bottom. This DLC never lives up to hyped expectations about the next Collapse, breakthrough in Destiny story, innovative gameplay or immersive surroundings. This is a raw, unpolished pile of inconsistency, lazyness and lack of quality, and neither its aspect shows actually competent work. All of it while being the most expensive DLC for Destiny to this day.
Think twice before buying it, think twice before believing anything the Devs say in their videos, and don't think that it will get better if you didn't like Lightfall in a few first hours - it won't.\t\t\t\t\t
