\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLightfall Pro's & Con's:

Positive Aspects:

Stunning Visuals:
Bungie has always excelled at creating visually stunning environments, and Lightfall is no exception. The DLC introduces breathtaking new locations, each with its own unique aesthetics and atmospheric qualities. The attention to detail in the game's graphics enhances immersion and elevates the overall visual experience.

Expansive Content:
Lightfall offers a wealth of content for players to explore. From new missions and strikes to additional raid encounters, the DLC expands upon the game's existing activities. This extensive array of content ensures that players have ample opportunities to engage with the game and discover new challenges, keeping them entertained for hours on end.

Enhanced Gameplay Mechanics:
Bungie has introduced a few new exciting gameplay mechanics and features in Lightfall that add depth and variety to the gameplay. These additions include new weapons, abilities, and subclasses that allow for more strategic and dynamic combat encounters. The DLC revitalizes the core gameplay loop, providing players with fresh experiences and keeping the game engaging.

Negative Aspects:

"Engaging" Narrative:
Destiny boasts an intricate and immersive storyline. We as the players are taken on a thrilling journey through new worlds, encountering captivating characters (besides Nimbus) and uncovering deep lore. The DLC's emphasis on storytelling adds depth and meaning to the overall gameplay experience. This season we got a Strand tutorial coupled with sum dialogue and that was it. Bungie really?

Pricing Model:
One notable criticism surrounding Lightfall is its pricing model. Destiny 2 follows a "season pass" structure, requiring players to purchase each DLC separately. This approach can be burdensome for players who wish to fully experience the game's content. Some argue that this pricing model creates a divide within the player base, limiting access for those who cannot afford multiple DLCs.

Power Creep:
The introduction of new and powerful weapons, abilities, and subclasses can sometimes lead to an imbalance in gameplay. Lightfall has the potential to disrupt the game's delicate ecosystem, resulting in an uneven playing field for both new and existing players. The prevalence of power creep may alienate some players and create frustration within the community.

Lack of Innovation:
While Lightfall offers an abundance of new content, some players argue that it fails to introduce innovative gameplay mechanics or groundbreaking features. Instead, the DLC builds upon existing mechanics and concepts without pushing the boundaries of the game's design. This perceived lack of innovation may leave some players longing for more substantial and transformative updates.

Endgame Content:
For dedicated players who have reached the endgame, Lightfall's content may feel lacking or repetitive. The DLC focuses heavily on expanding mid-game activities, such as strikes and missions, but may not provide sufficient endgame content to keep veteran players engaged. This potential imbalance in content distribution may lead to a sense of dissatisfaction among long-term players.

Conclusion:
Overall, Destiny 2's Lightfall DLC brings a mix of positive and negative aspects to the game. Its engaging narrative (besides Nimbus), stunning visuals, expansive content, and enhanced gameplay mechanics contribute to an overall enjoyable experience for players. However, the continued pricing model, potential power creep, lack of innovation, and endgame content warrant attention.
