-The new characters in Nimbus and the older cloud strider (I legit forgot his name) are either amazingly annoying or boring. Playing too hard into the boring master and student trope. Nimbus is annoying for his dialogue that I skipped any cutscene he is in or try to grab his quests and run.

-The new menus are either glitchy or boring. Infusion can be done in 3 separate menus now? Which is either a bug or strange.

- The MacGuffin (strange item that drives the plot for some reason) in the story is called "The Veil" and I can admit I don't know what the hell it is. I used to study lore for this game and played last season. A lot of story beats seem very ham-fisted or treated as "oh you know this thing right? oh you don't well then we aren't gonna explain it".

- I barely played the new subclass but I can admit I am not impressed. The movement of it all, the swinging, etc. I feel this could be me but being a Titanfall 2 player and also a fan of the Spider-man games I feel these new subclasses were over marketed as this game changing new movement sub class when somehow it feels only slightly faster.

-The new darkness enemy is annoying to deal with. Its just grabs you drains you over your light and has the movement speed of a rabid chimp aka it will grab you and proceed to bum rush you over and over making a constant loop of annoyance and pain.

Do I recommend this? Nah just buy spider-man miles morales thats what I'm doing.
I'm currently trying to get a refund for this dlc.\t\t\t\t\t

Bungie has let us down with Lightfall;
The tone for this expansion is such a far cry from Witch Queen's somber story telling and Season of the Seraph building up the Witness as THE threat which promised a much awaited confrontation between the Traveler and the force that brought the last Calamity with them.
Instead the plot very rapidly devolves into a buddy cop show with the tired cliche of the grizzled veteran and happy-go-lucky rookie, with Osiris trying to keep things in order. Nimbus's jovial, blundering attitude confuses me regarding what the target audience was for this character; it would've been better at the start of Destiny to have this juxtaposition but this is the penultimate expansion. Year 6 of D2 and Year 9 overall. So to let Nimbus be the driving force behind a story that quickly amounts to "we're just winging it, here's Osiris being a grumpy old man" with no clear establishment of the true scale of the threat is bland and uninteresting.
The pacing of the story makes no sense; at some point we get a training montage (the best scene but the bar is BELOW the ground) after we establish that the invading force is only barely being kept at bay and the threat (never really specified) to the whole system is being actively targeted all the time.
You literally stumble into Strand; no build up comparable to the trailers "you discover this power's potential and grow it yourself!" plus the obstacle course mission is a crime against humanity.
Disciple Calus had the opportunity to be a perfect foil to Caiatl since he isn't rotting in a vat anymore with robot proxies; the potential to see him work his charisma, showmanship, and manipulations was completely wasted. Season of the Haunted built him up just so he could clown around.\t\t\t\t\t

Long story

Only good thing to come out of this is the class and armour overhauls, they are a definite improvement to the game. I would argue though that the grapples need a lower cooldown, like seriously? 100 Discipline and it's still a 53 second cooldown just to be able to be somewhat agile, even after sacrificing a grenade slot?

We see the "woke" and "representative" character being Nimbus, who is very clearly a male body with a feminized face and a female voice actor with AI/sound tools lowering the pitch to an uncomfortable degree. This would be completely fine had this been consistent. The only other Cloud Strider we are introduced to is that of Rohan, very clearly a male character. Why not just make them both non-binary as a special race or group of people? Not to mention Rohan, does very, very little for the story and dies extremely quickly with no real care for his well-being and no emotion by the player as we barely know him. Funniest part in this new group of people, is that their dressed like gimps, pointed that out to my friends with a photo and they loved the resemblance.

Spoiler-y complaints below

How can Destiny 2 begin us with a raid leading to the hype surrounding Calus; an ex-Cabal god-Emperor with millions of clones of himself aboard the Leviathan.

From his lore - " Under his rule, the Cabal Empire became a seat of all-encompassing hedonism and revelry. His accomplishments during that time included redistributing Praetorate wealth to the people, granting citizenship to the Imperial Legions, and putting down various alien threats such as the Sindu, the Clipse, and the Psions' OXA Machine. "

It makes no sense, we see the Witness in the introduction tanking constant and direct Solar energy from the traveler, we see him destroy life and control matter with the swaying of his hands and fingers. Why would this all-powerful being not just incinerate everyone with the snap of his fingers like Thanos?! Is it because the main Guardians have god-resistant plot armour? \t\t\t\t\t

Gameplay: Great as always, Strand is a blast. Been using it as a Warlock outside of the campaign and it feels super solid and fun. Viability as always is a question but games are meant to be fun!

Mod System: Sidegrade. Lost some mod functionality, but the overall freedom and power we gained is worth it IMO. Just need something to take the place of Seeking Wells and Elemental Shards and it'll be solid imo. Artifact mods are one of the biggest Ws we could possibly have gotten.

Loadouts: Fantastic.

Exotics: Really enjoy the new Warlock legs. New machine gun is cool. Overall exotics are really unique and inspired this time around IMO.

Story: Main story was slightly weak. A lot of it is fleshed out by side quests I've done after the main story, or by dialogue directly following it. Which kinda sucks but the depth is there if you look for it at least. Not nearly as bad as a lot of people are saying IMO. People are comparing it to Shadowkeep. Those people need to get real.

Characters: The mentor dying part was very telegraphed, but we didnt spend much time with him so I don't really mind. Just a backdrop to introduce Nimbus. Again further explored in a side mission after the main story. Nimbus is fine, don't get why so many people are hating on them. Seen a lot of complaints about the characters acting with too much levity, as if Osiris wasnt constantly ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ and screaming and crying the entire campaign (in a good way). Nimbus was the only one with levity and that is their entire character. Calus was also a great villain and a nice boss fight. Lots of subtlety to his performance showing how he, just like Eramis, is subservient to the Witness out of fear.

Destination: Another common point I've seen is how Neomuna is empty. Its empty in the campaign due to the missions but I've done a few post-campaign missions around the patrol zone and its very similar to other patrols, if not better. Throne World and Europa to me are beautiful but kinda flat, not much depth. Neomuna has a LOT of details and intricacies in its design.

Overall: Really enjoying the expansion as a whole. Weak points as with every expansion, but the highs are really high. The reworking of the customization and introduction of Strand IMO set up the game for a lot of future additions that in hindsight we can thank Lightfall for. So many purely background improvements will leave people with disappointment, its inevitable, but I really like all the content I've played, especially post-campaign.\t\t\t\t\t
