\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs this really the company that made the Halo trilogy and Halo: Reach? Is it the same company in name only?

I would rather wait another year for new content then experience this level of disappointment and loss of faith (read, again, and again).

The marketing campaign for the expansion was a more impressive and cohesive story than the DLC itself.

The drop in quality isn’t surprising given the pattern of Bungie’s previous releases but this one stings more because the presentation of the content for the release had more substance than the content itself.

I’d vote to have the marketing team start writing the dialogue and plot points, since the tone they sold seeker more in line with the universe of the game.

The gunplay will always be fun, but this game occasionally becomes more than that, but never for long enough. This Marvel-esque corny and cringy writing, A new and empty, hollow location. Plot holes and unanswered questions while the advertised story itself is left behind early on.

If you haven’t bought it yet, I wish I was you. I would have been content to sit this one out.\t\t\t\t\t
