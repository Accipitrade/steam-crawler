\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is the first time I have written a negative review for Destiny 2. This is mainly because I never had to experience the worst DLC Shadowkeep. I started playing D2 in Season of Arrivals and became a lore nerd over the last 2 years or so.

The Bad: In the most crucial point of D2's history where the storytelling and direction of the game needs to be shown clearly and questions answered Bungie did not deliver. Character death's being telegraphed, numerous plot holes, not showing gameplay of The Witness which IMO was the reason why everyone was even coming back to D2, unanswered questions about lore aspects that should have been answered in this campaign. Overall the campaign felt more like an intermediate Season focused on Strand 3.0 and Modding 3.0. If My Name is Byf has problems with the storytelling, you best bet its bad. Elemental wells got completely removed, stasis classes are gone for now since the shards do ♥♥♥♥ all, no new mods to replace wells.

The Good: Strand itself is fun to play and works properly. Sorry Titans you got shafted once again. The campaign difficulty was very good and IMO harder than WQ. Neomuna is nice destination with vibrant colors. New Loadout system is fantastic and the new modding system (minus what was talked about previously) allows for less confusion and is good for the longevity of the team.

All in All, Do NOT buy this DLC if you are new player wanting to jump back in and see The Witness. Do BUY this game if you hate this game and love this game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really wanted to like this DLC. The subclass is incredibly fun and the game play in itself is fun, but the story? The thing I'm personally here for? It wasn't there. Nothing was ever explained about anything; the lore behind strand is confusing, and we don't even know WHY we're going after the macguffin that we're sent after. I had a lot of expectations after the previous year's seasons and Witch Queen, and I was sorely let down. Only reason I don't regret buying the annual pass is that if this story were a season instead of a DLC, I think I would've enjoyed it a lot more, so I'm confident I'll like the seasons up until Final Shape. But as a whole, I expect so much more out of a DLC than what we got.

6/10 with game play, 1/10 not including game play.\t\t\t\t\t

If you set aside story entirely and simply focus on all of the new features such as Gaurdian ranks, commendations, strand which is a huge one, mod management, mod rework, and loadouts then Lightfall is an amazing expansion. Narratively speaking, it's clear that Lightfall and The Final Shape were the same expansion and ripped apart from eachother, but I don't mind that as it's only clear in the story. If the Narrative was only a bit different and explored some more plot points as well as explained some things better I'm sure the community would have labeled it as one of the best expansions to ever be released. It's an impossible balancing game of features and story. The community needs to be at least a bit more understanding when it comes to a game development point of view and Bungie needs to hit that balance or the expansion will "suck."
Lightfall doesn't exactly deserve many negatives reviews but Bungie needs to work on their expansions Narrative much more.\t\t\t\t\t

Strand grappling is fun albeit a long cooldown post campaign (they'll probably patch it later so no biggie there). The new guns seem cool perk wise and archetype wise but both main weapon pools from Neomuna and the season are reskins visually so it depends how you feel about old fashion with new function. The new exotics are interesting and unique - post campaign missions and quests are fun and feel smoother narratively compared to elements of the main campaign. So many 'Halo' moments to be had in this xpac that I love - particularly Reach and ODST. Overall i recommend it, there's alot of good here to combat the very bad in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t
