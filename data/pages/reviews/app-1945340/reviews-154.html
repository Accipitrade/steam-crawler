\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDefinitely falls flat with the narrative, but gameplay wise it was awesome. I'll post this as recommended because I don't think it deserves a mostly negative rating, I would say a neutral mixed would be good. Neomuna is a super cool environment, Strand is fun and fresh feeling, quality of life improvements are nice.

However, those good points do not outweigh the fact that the writing is terrible and feels like a huge letdown to those who have been invested in this franchise for a while. Major points of the plot are confusing and it feels like the characters are incredibly shallow, causing the player to have a hard time finding meaning in the story. Nothing is wrapped up in a satisfying way, it feels like filler. (This also makes it terrible for new players to get into, not that that wasn't an issue for awhile before this expansion, it just makes it worse.)

TLDR, Gameplay really fun, writing for story bad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've finished the Lightfall campaign and am currently doing the exotic missions post campaign.

In my personal opinion, this dlc is overly hated. It's nowhere as good as the Witch Queen obviously, many details like the Veil (which you have already heard millions of people complaining already) or Neomuna was not explained properly. Bungie did say they will explain more through the season pass but personally, I would prefer if the main DLC explains everything instead.

BUT, I do think the campaign built up a good foundation for the upcoming DLCs or season passes. They DID show us what the Veil looks like. And, spoiler alert: they did show us what the Witness do with the Veil, proven by the final cutscene where the Witness and the Traveler got sucked into the portal . It's a bit ambiguous but there's a lot of hypothesis for that.

Story aside, I just don't see why people are saying this campaign "shadowkeep" level of bad. This is significantly better than SK and BL, it even rivals Forsaken DLC dare I say. It has a campaign, 8 missions, legendary difficulty, 10 hours of playtime and it's actually hard. There is also a lot of stuffs to do after campaign, too. Multiple exotic quests to chase after which also at the same time helped explaining to us more about Neomuna (and hopefully the Veil too but I couldn't tell yet so far).

My feelings for this campaign is mixed, and unfortunately Steam does not have a mixed option for a review. I would recommend it after TWQ, but I also suggest people to just grab this DLC when it goes on sale later on.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou're fully allowed to enjoy this DLC, but let me give you one important tip:
Don't buy it for the story.

Buy it for the cool new destination.
Buy it so you can Strand all over your enemies.
Buy it for the new weapons and armour.
Buy it because Nimbus is some much-needed comic relief in what feels like a gameworld full of Soulsborne NPCs.
Buy it because the raid is f*cking sick.

But don't buy it for the story.
It's not as bad as so many other people claim (it beats CoO, Warmind and Shadowkeep by a country mile), but it's very vague, and it refuses to give context to what you're actually doing while treating you as though you already have all the context.

Hope this helps. And I hope that if you do buy it, you can appreciate it for what it is, and not what it should have been.

Edit: two additional points;
1. The raid is short, simple and mostly enemy killing. Visually beautiful but not very challenging.
2. As if typing this, there's currently an offer on the main game's page to buy the previous 3 DLCs as a Legacy Collection. If you're looking for story, buy that.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't get me wrong. I learned absolutely nothing about the story of Destiny during this campaign. I got cool spoderman powers now have a shoulder charge on warlock. But I don't hate this expansion; I had an ABSOLUTE BLAST playing it. Gameplay wise its probably some of the most fun I've had on this game. It's the first campaign I've beaten in one day and the first campaign of D2 I've gotten to experience with my brother, who's been beating Destiny campaigns without me since like 2017.

I loved playing this. And while I'm immensely disappointed with the story and kinda ticked off that this expansion cost like a LOT more for much less, I loved playing it and am going to keep playing it because I love how it plays. The patrol zone is really beautiful and cool, while not necessarily the most immersive place ever - but its a destiny patrol zone, I expect nothing.

Strand is a super cool subclass. It introduces an aspect of movement I've felt this game has always needed and all of its abilities synergize really nicely to achieve really cool and helpful things. I love that hunters can create grapple points. You can grapple thrown tangles and literally ride them into the nearest enemies face which you bash in with your shoulder, or you can literally just grapple to your friend and have them launch you if you want. Now that I have access to all of the fragments it feels how I wanted it to feel and does amazing things in game.

I know that this review is going to get hated on, but I really did enjoy playing this. I came to play Destiny, and I had a blast playing this expansion with my friends. If you are looking for a great story, play literally any other game. Game play wise however, its a blast. Shooting bad guys and unraveling reality and throwing it at them should always feel this good. I haven't felt this much like a bad*ss in a game since I played DOOM. Soundtrack is one of the best and accommodates the 80's action movie vibe incredibly.\t\t\t\t\t
