\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I would compare The Witch Queen or the seasons proceeding to The Mandalorian, Lightfall would be The Book of Bobafett.

While the teams responsible for art, music and game design have consistently knocked it out of the park at Bungie, everything else about this expansion feels aggressively shallow. With the expansion being increased in price this year, the story felt several missions too short, the depth expected from the lore and characters as we know them were disappointingly lacking, and the characters we meet... well there is a profoundly questionable disconnect in the writing and direction for the Cloudstrider, Nimbus.

I should be applauding Bungie for including Destiny's first non-binary character, but instead I'm cringing at every interaction and astonished they decided Destiny's first non-binary character should be portrayed as a socially inept and unlikable bolstering idiot. Nimbus was a good opportunity to teach inclusion and understanding, and his gender neutrality could have been an interesting part of understanding a nuanced character. Instead this clown did nothing for LGBTQ representation besides check the gender neutral box and hurt the image of LGBTQ individuals. A friend of mine told me they liked Nimbus, and proceeded to explain that their character made sense to him because Cloudstriders only live for 10 years. The narrative is so fast and loose in this expansion that he finished the entire campaign thinking Cloudstriders were actually children in giant adult techno-bodies and I like this misunderstanding so much better than the canon because It actually makes Nimbus more tolerable.

Basically every character in this expansion lacked meaningful motivation, development, and depth. I personally don't have any issues with how they handled the Veil and thought the final mission was actually pretty dope thanks to the visual storytelling at work there, but not narratively. You do learn, however nuanced and loose what the veil is, but judging from the reaction of most players, it was definitely too subtle for them. As for the gameplay, it's still a fun expansion, but it is now week two into the launch and I'm already bored. I like strand, but due to the strict limitations on cooldown I find myself barely using the subclass the way I envisioned I would, pre-launch, and I feel the new exotics are only mildly interesting to use.

Everything else I could say about the expansion, including it's quality of life enhancements fall under the same category of thought: Shallow. Commendations are a miss. The decision making window is too small and the rewards attached to giving them incentivizes quick and thoughtless giving, making any use of the system pointless. Guardian ranks are a miss. Whether you've been playing since launch or started playing last month, everyone is either a 6 or a 7 and the markers as they are today that place you in your rank lack any sense of depth that's meaningful to players logging in right now. Loudouts are good, but usability and function leaves some things to be desired. The UI/UX on this is both good, and atrocious at the same time. The fact that I have to thumb through 60 different names, icons and colors (20 each) in one direction to label each loadout can be infuriatingly tedious and was no doubt a rushed implementation. Sparrows, ghosts and ships should have been included in loadouts but is only an inconvenience

All in all my suggestion to Bungie is to start spending more of the money I gave them on Destiny 2. This constant minimum viable product garbage is really pushing me out the door as the quality of said product declines without more being value provided.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay wise, this is a good expansion but the gameplay was already good so I dont think the expansion really is to credit for that. Strand is dope as hell though and the new guns are tight.

Story wise, this is absolute F tier garbage. Characters have wildly inconsistent tones with the events that are happening around us. Practically no information is gained and only a million more questions are added. Pacing is a straight slog, leading the campaign to feel extra excruciating on your second or third characters. You'll also want to turn your dialogue volume all the way down anytime you might hear Nimbus talk as they almost immediately ruin every ounce of immersion you may have at any point. Laughably terrible character writing for both new characters.

Environment wise, Neptune is cool but they really oversold the verticality in the trailers and lead up to Lightfall. This is just another patrol zone with practically nothing differing it besides the color scheme. Huge missed opportunities all across the board that really make this expansion feel like Bungie has no idea what theyre doing. Kinda makes Witch Queen seem like a fluke and not the rule. If you look at all the expansions and the writing, it becomes quickly apparent that Witch Queen will not be the status quo.

They really pushed hard with their marketing and practically everything they said was oversold and underdelivered. Hoping Bungie really understands the player feedback regarding this and make some serious changes to their narrative leads. Whoever okayed the story for this expansion should absolutely be replaced.

They had so much momentum from the past year and this expansion was supposed to feel the beginning of the end or some crazy huge revelation, when in reality we just got chase the macguffin for 8 hours.

Also, to everyone saying the seasons will make the story better... what happens next year when those seasons are gone? Lightfall by itself, is without a doubt, the worst expansion we have had and I hope they can pivot off this.

Guess its time to hook back up to my copium supply and wait for the final shape.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a longtime Destiny player who religiously preordered all deluxe editions because there aren't any games quite like this, Lightfall has been a complete disappointment.

-Story is absolute and utter cr@p, crazy I know

-Post game content isn't too bad, exotic missions are decent albeit some of them are a tad grindy

-The overall difficulty overhaul has been great, it's great to see some challenge in the game, however I cannot for the love of me fathom why they had to apply the change to Legend lost sectors. being underleveled is one thing, but making it so that you have to run sub 10 minute runs for the exact same exotic drop rate? That's a TERRIBLE decision Bungie. Keep the difficulty in GMs where i actually enjoy being patient

-Strand has been pretty fun, the gameplay loop is fun if you can set things up well

-Seasonal story is already better than Lightfall's one LMAO

-SPOILER: the writing this time is so utterly sh1t that the people of the last city are being enslaved by the shadow legion in the seasonal storyline and the vanguard is doing NOTHING. they've been spectating from the H.E.L.M. since the start of LF and continue to daydream while the literal Awoken Queen is the one who's helping save the civilians. It's so laughably bad you'd think the writing team for LF and season of defiance are beefing with each other and are writing their own narratives

-Secret Exotic mission is good though, go look for it and youll be rewarded

Verdict? Don't buy if you have yet to buy, new or old player alike. Bungie needs to know that they cannot release absolute cr@p like this and expect people to stay. LF was marketed on a grand scale with multiple collaborations and was their chance to get more people into the game. I'd say they fumbled hard with this one. I'll be playing to get my money's worth but I don't think this review will change even with the release of the raid, I feel like this is a clear case of Bungie being so proud of the work they put in but never realized how bad the end product actually was. I can only hope and huff massive amounts of copium that they won't fumble The Final Shape as hard as they did LF.

Refund Lightfiller.\t\t\t\t\t
