\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a good expansion. For me, it's second only to The Witch Queen, which wins out only because of its story being the culmination of years and years of world building. Lightfall does not have that. But that's not Lightfall's fault.

The story of Lightfall is not the story of The Witness, who was introduced at the end of The Witch Queen as a replacement for the character who had, for years prior, been the big bad of the story. The story of Lightfall is the story of Neomuna, a city and people suddenly besieged by the forces of The Witness.

Lightfall was always going to be a brand new story. It had to be, because there was no foundation from which to build it in the same way as The Witch Queen's. Even though the ongoing saga of Light vs Dark concludes in the next expansion, Bungie have arguably reset the story in its final hours with the resolution of the prior largest enemy and the introduction of a newer bigger one.

I don't really know how you do that well. But I do know that I enjoyed Lightfall's story. A lot. Neomuna and its people are extremely cool and interesting. And they're all brand new, something we've not gotten in Destiny content in a long time.

But even more than the story, the missions themselves were the best campaign missions of the series to date. Some were less good, but most were solid, and some were incredible. Strand is immediately more engaging and fun for me than Stasis was with Beyond Light. And the post-campaign story and missions are continuing to be interesting and challenging.

Lightfall easily delivered the most enjoyment I've experienced from a destiny expansion since Forsaken.\t\t\t\t\t
