Lightfall launched and I felt like many, it was a huge disappointment and did little if nothing to evolve the franchise. I didn't even finish the campaign, and took a 2 week break from the game 2 weeks later, like someone in an abusive relationship, I decided to log back on and finish the campaign and other content I had paid 100 dollars for/ I was met with a message that said I was banned. Upon submitting an appeal all I got in response was that the ban would not be overturned. They did not provide me with any reason as to why I was banned ( because there wasn't one ) . Myself and many others had thousands of hours of their time and over a thousand dollars of content wrongly taken from them with no recourse and no explanation. I never thought i would say this but because of this i feel bungie is more predatory than activision/ blizzard in this manner because even they would not issue a permaband with no explanation.

TLDR
Do not play this game unless you wish to have thousands of hours and dollars wrongly taken from you with no explanation why.Any one who gives a dime to this company is setting a dangerous precedent that gaming developers do not have to be accountable to the players who support them. If you're looking for a good mmo experience there are plenty of much better mmos . The game had potential 5 years ago and it was squandered . Not only that but Bungies had decided to cannibalize their most loyal player in a lukewarm attempt to be perceived as doing something to fix the many broken aspects of the game. I don't thing disappointed even sums up how i and many other feel about bungie,\t\t\t\t\t

Strand, any game is better with a grapple hook...
The main focus of this expansion is the Strand subclasses. Being as the 2nd Darkness subclass, where Stasis came first in Beyond Light. For each class:
The Broodweaver fantasy is all about creating these seeking creatures called Threadlings that attack your foes. They can attack, and follow you (Perching) with their super being a one time FU-button that spawns a large amount of Threadlings.
The Threadrunner fantasy is all about movement, big surprise, with a heavy focus on the Grapple Hook. Their super is a roaming super in which you can swing around with a rope dart in hand capable of taking out foes at mid range.
The Berserker fantasy is all about aggression, another big surprise, their entire kit focuses on buffing themselves and constantly being up close with the enemy. Their super is a roaming super in which they wield two large blades in which they can slice and suspend foes.
Overall, Strand fails to deliver the promise/vision from Bungie as a "High APM" subclass, as it still plays like any other subclass; but it very closely reflects Stasis more than the light subclasses. Strand, like stasis, has a large amount of crowd control with the ability to string enemies up and Suspend them in mid air. This ability in itself is considerably over powered, and I expect many patches to tune it later. Also the Grapple hook, unlike most games with one, this one doesn't need 2 anchor points to use (including yourself). Instead this grapple creates grapple points on the fly, with the hunter getting the unique ability to create semi-permenant anchors. Unlike Stasis however, these subclasses feel more balanced and fleshed out (now that they dropped all the fragments for it), on top of that too there is no time-gated grind that you must do (...yet) to unlock all the pieces for Strand.

New Digs
Neomuna, is quite the interesting location. This location has been hinted at in a few seasons way back, but what or where it was, that was never revealed. Unlike most of Bungie's locations, this one has a single central theme, with almost no variation between area/zones. Most players believe that this focus makes the area feel bland in comparison to other locations Bungie has designed; this is most in part of the theme they chose. It is designed as a full fledged space for an area in Destiny 1 that was considerably mute in design, which doesn't help the location at all. Something frustrating about this zone however, is that they cap your level here and purposely make you weak; making the enemies much more dangerous than other areas. Not only that, this area is a complete and total retcon.

New Events
There are two new events, with one being a reprisal of an event that was tied to the Season of Undying, way back in Shadow Keep. One of them is unfortunately VERY tedious and not designed at all for solo play, which a majority of players will find frustrating. Both of these events need to be rebalanced still to make them less of a chore to complete. To put it in prospective, imagine fighting 6-12 bosses at a time who can 1-4 hit kill you and they all have the health of a normal boss.

Hypernet Current
As a strike, it is pretty good, very fun to play and replay. Bungie nailed this one on the head and in the future they should expand on designing Strikes to be of this caliber.

New Exotics
The exotics for this expansion are all really great. The Glaive is completely unique, the Machine gun fills a niche that no one saw coming, and the Side-arm is a unique take on the Smart Pistol from Titanfall 2.

A Real Nightmare...
Considered by many, the easiest raid in Destiny 2, which is partially true; it is by far the easiest raid to get into. Considering this was world firsted in under 4 hours which it currently holds the record for the fastest raid completion of all time, including Destiny 1. Simultaneously however, the player base has matured as an audience and our skill matches too, making this raid feel slightly disconnected to the majority of the 2%. Unfortunately however this is the Glitchiest raid, not counting server issues, to date. There are a large amount of inconsistent mechanics that rely on some really old technology in the game engine that should be reworked to be much more consistent. At the end of the day, the raid is great and it definitely is unique in both design and gameplay/mechanics design.

The worst, for last
Where do I start? I guess I will save the rant for Byf, as he said it best, but I will re-iterate what he stated in that this is one of the most poorly timed releases in Bungie history. This expansion feels not only a narrative st- no, leap backwards to the content Bungie has been producing now. I could compare this campaign's narrative to Destiny 1 Release, which is a massive leap backwards. This was so bad, Bungie had to spoil future content for the game just to keep the entire community from losing their minds about this narrative blunder.
To top it all off, gameplay/level balance is all over the place, which is unheard of for a Bungie game; except for maybe Halo 2's Legendary Jackals. There are missions that force you to Handicap yourself in playing a gimped version of Strand, and this veries heavily depending on the section. According to Bungie these parts of the campaign were bugged and that was causing the difficulty; but that doesn't matter it should work at release. Remember who you are [Bungie].
I will however note that they achieved their design goal for making a campaign that feels like an action movie, as it really does. Kudos for that I guess? Maybe if this was released as a pre-lude to Witch Queen, or as an add-on to it for a supplemental story might have worked better.

I can recommend this to new players, as this is a very new player friendly expansion in comparison to other expansions in Destiny, especially for the endgame content in this expansion being far more approachable. If you are looking for story however, I'd steer clear of this one, and never look back.\t\t\t\t\t
