\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDo not buy this! At least not yet.

The campaign for Lightfall is just a giant tutorial for a new subclass that you don't even have full access to. So you're learning something you will not use in any other content.

Everyone else has gone on ad nauseam to how terrible the storytelling and dialogue are, so I won't echo that, just affirm that those points are valid.

The only thing that could redeem this is a banger raid, but that's not worth $50. At least wait for a discount.

If you're a returning player, the free seasonal content is really solid. The narrative for the season is good, the activity is fun, and the loot is good enough.

You will have a better Destiny experience pretending this DLC does not exist. Just play the seasons until "The Final Shape" and enjoy the quality content from past years at a much lower price.

Definitely will not be purchasing the next DLC until AFTER reviews are out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can't tell whether there's a very devout sect of people dedicating their lives to convincing others that the game's bad, or they're playing a different game altogether.

Here's my review: it's more Destiny, with a new storyline, an amazing soundtrack, a few more things to chase and a new set of abilities. The story leaves on a cliffhanger regarding the Witness and the Traveler because it intends to build up on it with seasons... As it's been doing for a while now. I'm surprised people haven't noticed this, at all.

Strand is... Interesting. At first I thought that it wouldn't be something I could ever take in harder content, but then again, the game made me finish the legendary campaign while having a preselected fragment and aspect, and that's already part of the hardest pieces of content you could play, the "easy" part being that you don't have to deal with champions / match game elemental shields of 15 flavors with one loadout. Back to the topic: once you start unlocking some aspects, it actually becomes pretty decent. It's also able to stand on its own two feet, something which other subclasses don't seem as good at as they were before... Which brings me to my second point.

The mod system overhaul has murdered a lot of my builds, and left the gameplay loop feeling (from an ability standpoint) a whole lot clunker. Then again, people have asked for this precisely. However, it's more accessible for new players, and far more straightforward.

Finally, there are plenty of QoL improvements (those come regardless of buying the expansion, of course), and I personally prefer this new destination over both the Throne World and Europa. Exploring it doesn't feel like a chore, and I like the thematic, as well as the expectation of discovering more about it.

Overall, the game is still the best it's ever been, and I invite everyone to monitor the playtime of those who swore to never touch it again in their reviews for a little Easter Egg.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing Destiny 2 since release. I've played every expansion and experienced some of what most of the community would consider to be the lowest points of the game's lifetime both in terms of narrative and gameplay. I also consider myself to be relatively forgiving when it comes to this game and it's high and low points. Most of the expansions or general content drops have had at least something that made me say that I did not regret purchasing, and things that I would eventually come back to and enjoy for a little while before taking breaks.

With that being said, I don't think that I could recommend people to purchase this expansion. I legitimately don't think any other major content drop in this game has left such a sour taste in my mouth. At least with the other less well received content, it unfortunately was relatively expected or even just "meh". Lightfall feels almost insulting especially coming right after Witch Queen, which was a really great expansion. The tone of the campaign is so jarring considering the marketing emphasized heavily on the fact that this was "the beginning of the end". It felt like there was almost no stakes with the way characters acted most of the time, and the introduction of Strand was extremely sudden and made almost no sense. That's not even getting into other glaring issues, namely the complete and utter lack of new weapons and armor. There's maybe like 5 new weapons and one set of armor from Neomuna? No new blue armor for leveling or even armor for the ritual activities, like they promised they would do every expansion.

I could go on a much longer rant, but my point is that this expansion feels so weird in terms of how off it is that it makes me think something went incredibly wrong during the development. It almost feels like they shipped an earlier build of the expansion in certain ways. Like I said, I'm typically very forgiving of Bungie personally, and I never post reviews on Steam. Lightfall at face value is just that disappointing. As much as it pains me to say it, I strongly recommend that you just save your money and skip this expansion.\t\t\t\t\t
