i'm changing my review because the devs have addressed the issue that prevented my recommendation previously.

i will continue to return to add further insight to how the game can be improved. but for now, the 2 big issues i see that could vastly improve end-game satisfaction is having customized drones or ships, with landing pads, rec rooms, crew quarters, etc, that function like the space station but on a smaller scale. the ability to send those ships on missions or into battles. and also including a melding with galactic civ vision. have opposing factions, planets to discover and dominate, resources to extract. then eventually the ability to build manufacturing nodes to actually produce the ever important beam emitters and other components instead of only relying on trade.

then i'd also like to see this thing go big like minecraft. right now the station design is rather limited in scope. there's a limit to the number of sections you can add to each corridor, and they can only go one direction. i'd like to see the ability to add cross corridors, and to make it as big as i want. right now it feels rather small.

I crashed 3 times, the UI is intuitive and easy to work with but the upgrade system is utterly frustrating and repetitive for no reason. I believe that the game could benefit from introducing or better adapting relationships with enemies and conditions. Not to mention true means to specialize your station.

What if I want to become a pirate heaven? Or become a criminal syndicate? Or a space ecological habitat? Or ally myself with the aliens, catoring more and more to their needs and such? Or become involved with the federation or become an outpost ob their behalf?

There a lot of growth room here and while not complete yet, I have high hopes for the game if it decides to become a bit more complex.

As it stands, I give it a 7/10.\t\t\t\t\t

Now it has to be said that you will experience bugs, yet for the most part they appear to be only impacting visual indicators such as how much of a resource you need vs. debilitating game breaking bugs.

Overall, I would recommend this game as it is a relaxing experience into the pursuit of running a space station that shows a lot of promise for growth.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t11/28/2021 I'm going to give this a positive review even though I don't think it's for everyone. The developer seems active and I feel like with more time and more content it could be a great game. For me the issue is I feel like I've seen everything there is to see at only 10 hours played. Once you've figured out how to get a positive cash flow in the early game there isn't too much more that's interesting to do. 5-6 restarts and I was good to go. The trick is to upgrade what you have as much as possible, play with the pricing, get a cafe asap and wait for the better eneergy production buildings.

Research unlocks a couple of items but it's mostly % increases to what you already have for example buffing up the solar panels or increasing your fuel sales profit. I wish research gave more special items or was just generally more interesting.

You can send drones on missions and the only difference is the risk % where they might get damaged. What they bring back is artifacts which you can research for extra research points. I was hoping for unique buildings or items. This part needs to be fleshed out more.

You build relationships with factions over time by trading. You can then eventually get a stockbroker and buy stock in the companies. I did not do this and saw no point since I already had a positive cash flow. And then there are raider pirates that you can either pay off or defend against. Sometimes they attack without the option to pay off and can do a lot of damage. You need a good amount of lasers for the asteroids and then turrets and machine guns against the pirates. There is also biological weapons that are apparently used against the organic ships but I never found a need for them.

I wish there was a bit of story or lore or just really a little more to do outside of clicking on traders and hoping for more transparent aluminum.

Overall it's not a bad game and I had no crashes. There is a bug where if you raise the fuel price the warning says the docking price is too high even if you set the docking price to zero I'm sure that will get patched.

Personally I found the game a little too easy on "normal" but it would be a great intro to tycoon games for someone not familiar. I'll keep this review positive as it stands but I am hoping for a little more by release.\t\t\t\t\t
