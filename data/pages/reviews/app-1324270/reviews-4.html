Police scan once year Feb or March, Raiders will come every 5-7 years these you can't bribe and will require more firepower to get rid of. Pirates can be bribe but can drop cash and items. Aliens will only attack for your Research points so spend wisely and each time you can spend.

Early game this game is a real grind. Hard mode w/ events on. Don't build anything you can't afford to loose. The bathing house is one of the highest profitable building 20,000 a month in revenue when full upgrade but cost 15,000 to get your hands on 1. Everytime i try to rush R&G sends a asteroid to destroy it. GAMEOVER. So piece of advice start small and grow small. 2 docks are find early game. Then focus on BILLBOARDS 1 cafe, ___then swap this for a bar. Then you get into research .

Recommended research paths... HINT; don't research anything if its locked behind a level for example Shield Generator can be Research at lvl 2 Station but u need a lvl 3 to use the prototype. This goes as well for the Machine gun. I normally will aleternate between the Upkeep cost of building Upkeep cost of the Turrents, and Faster generation of Science. After this if you want to maximize profits on your Commercial side DRINK cost is a good one to get. Then Shield Generator then 2 bath facility to ramp your profits up.

The NPC pilots will come up w/ missions 5 typs:
Smuggler only triggers when security lvls are low. (hold a certain item for x amount of months) If caught by the police you will be fine for the contraband and fail the mission. Be Mindful of the time when the police will come check your station.

Repairs this requires a repair bay basically mission about repairing Pilots ships x amount of screws x amount of gold exc... But the catch you have to have these items on hand.

Commercial Mission you have aquire a cafe and this guy will pay you 3229 c for 2/m but during that time the cafe itself will not generate profit.

Trader mission: can you aquire x amount of this items and have it for me in 3 - 6 months . These are good if you have the capital to buy things in bulk.

Ship Mission ' prototype' these are unlock around level 3 or Level 4 you have to aquire the Drone Blueprint and build it with the parts require to go on these missions. you can usually artifacts from these missions. The ships will level up but i have seen any improvements over time.

This game is very complex tycoon w/ allot of stuff going in a MICRO and MACRO level. if you are good at managing finances, promoting your company then you'll enjoy it. Have fun when you achieve nightmare... 0% people have achieve the plat on this :D hope this helps

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's promise here but there are concerns.
This game is kind of like building a fire and trying to light it, eventually given enough time and swearing you pull it off. However once you do and you feel warm there's no point. The end game is building the rest of your station, lagging the hell out of the game, being the number 1 company, and going mad from hearing the same song on loop. There isn't a lot of player feedback (why are my employees unhappy?), there's a lot of bugs, 1 song on repeat, it lags late game, no multiplayer, and there is no end game. There is also this terrible mechanic where you can only do some things by manually trading with every ship and hoping they're selling a blueprint. Customers complain constantly if your prices are a cent too cheap?? There's no global control of prices, or turrets. All trading is done manually via a laggy point and click menu it isn't very interesting and you have barely any room for products and you have to sell them manually as well. There's no market and little point to do it.
I wish I could compete with my friends and see their stations, or use my success to buy another quadrant and build up an empire swapping between stations as needed. Build a shipyard, service even larger capital ships, buy a planet, create and maintain a robust trading market, so much could be done here.
I love the concept, the asthetic, the initial difficulty, getting that fire going is quite fun. But after you accomplish that and the elation wears off that's it.

I love that it possesses the spirit of Babylon 5 and Deep Space Nine in regards to a lonely station, surviving on Commerce and protecting itself and its inhabitants from evil foes, while also having the potential to venture out on some missions and do some extra-curricular activities.

Right now, once you've unlocked all the station modules and cracked your own financial formula, the game can almost run itself.. which is fine! because it's Early Access and I'm sure there's more to come!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn it's current state it's a clear thumbs down for the price you pay.
Having it played 10 hours, I've gotten every single achievement that is there to get. $15 for 10 hours is not sufficient for me for a positive review.
Overall it's not a bad tycoon game, considering it's still in early access. It does need quite some polishing though before making it worth its price.
A few annoying things:
- Save games do not have a timestamp, only a date stamp. Cumbersome when starting the game and not remembering the name of your last save game.
- Screen resolution keeps changing back to the default when starting the game. No biggie since it is EA but still an annoying function that should be a simple fix.
- Very easy, even on nightmare difficulty, once you've got the hang of how to make money. Almost impossible to fail once cash starts flowing.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs the game stands in this moment, no I cannot recommend it, and there is a strong reason why I am starting with this statement.

The game felt too much "off the shelf" in design and execution. I can't help but feel like what is out right now isn't going to be much different than what updates will provide. I could be wrong, and I truly hope I am because the game does have some potential.

The real frustration point for me is that once you get the hang of the game... it just feels like its going to be noting more than a f2p game mechanic where your only challenge is how much time you need to wait to add another brick into the wall. That's not to say that the game isn't challenging, as it certainly has that element, but only at the start. Once you hit that stride, the rest of the game becomes monotonous. By its current design, there are no functions that can bring back up the difficulty level without absolutely throwing your station into crisis and going into a fast nose-dive.

Ultimately, I don't hate the game either, and I'm content having spent my money to support an indie team. I wish them luck and hope for them to prove me wrong. I will come back to this game in time to check that. Probably in 6-12 months.\t\t\t\t\t
