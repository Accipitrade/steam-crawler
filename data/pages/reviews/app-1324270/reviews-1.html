\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI watched the Alpha Release playthroughs, and thought it looked pretty cool, but the actual release seems to be a huge step backwards.

The interface is LESS polished than the Alpha?
The Alpha also had kind of a tutorial, but the actual game just lets to randomly click around to figure things out. Even though I watched the playthroughs, I still didn’t know how to do certain things.
For instance, on the left it says to Docking buildings 2/2, Shipps Docked 6/10, Jumpgate Connections, wihtou telling you to get any ohose completed. Even the electric grid, you need to connect each solar panel to things you want them to power up.
they never tell you that.
If a module has a red icon of people over it, it means they need employees.
Why not make it so you can click on that symbol to open the hiring tab, or the employee roster tab?
Same with the electric symbol over the module.
You can’t just randomly click in space to deselect a choice. You need to click over on the build icon, to change your choices, but that opens the building menu, so you need to click twice to get back to just having a curser.
Or, you can right click to deselect.


Hiring employees, for instance should be a lot simpler. When you hire them, your roster does not change. It did in the Alpha.
In the actual game, there is no indication that you hired anyone.
I don’t think it’s bad luck, all my employees are awful. They are either 200+ credits per day, or 70 and dumb.
My list was Lazy, Lazy, Dumb, Loner, Introverted, Genius.
There is an icon, on the top left, and when you hover your mouse over it, it says “employees”, so you’d think if you click on it, it would bring up the employees panel. Nope.
There is a giant Red Block on the right, that says “Employees: Your employees don’t feel safe” Again, you’d think if you clicked on it, it would bring up the employees panel. Nope.
There is a block of stats on the top right, and if you hover your mouse over it, it says “employees”, so you’d think if you click on it, it would bring up the employees panel. Nope.
There is a tiny Magnify glass on the top center of the screen, which says “overview” if you click on it, you get 3 choices: Company, Station, Employees. Awesome. You’d think if you clicked on “employees”, it would bring up the employees panel. Nope. It opens a list of your current WORKING employees, not your whole roster.
The only way to see ALL of your people (I accidentally hired 10) is to click on a module, then click the “employees” tab, then Where you see what employee is working on that module, you need to click “change”. THEN…. THEN you can see your whole roster of people.
And.. again… The alpha did it better. In the alpha you can see multiple traits.
In the final version, you see the name, the bad trait, how much they cost you, a happy face, and where they are working (if they are assigned to some place). There is no way to see how they feel about different things. Are they hungry, tired, angry, etc. Like in EVERY other Tycoon game ever made.

There was an Upgrade button on the Modules in the Alpha, not in the Final game. why?
You have the option to adjust the price of services like you do in most Tycoon games, but here…. it has no affect on the customers.
WTH? Really?! lol. So I start off with a 40 cred docking fee and a 15 red fuel fee, and the customers are happy.
I bump it to 999cred for both docking and fuel cost, and they are still happy.
Rolling in money.

Research is a mystery. They don’t explain how to get research points at all.
there are buildings that give MORE research points, but you need to reach research level XXX before being able to buy them.
so… How do you get to research level XXX if you don’t know what gives research points?

Seems the only way to get research points is by achieving the goals set on the left of the screen.
Docking buildings 0/2
Ships Docked 0/10
Jumpgate Connections 0/2
Security 0/40
Each time one of these (or group of these) is complete, you get a screen that says +credits +research points, and what you unlocked.
So you are not slowly building research points like in every other game. They never tell you this.


One of the goals is to have Security at 40.
You have 2 choices for Security. 1) is an escape pod, the other is a shield.
The shield gives 4, and the escape pod, gives a whopping ZERO. So you need 10 shields on your base.
this is basically making your base a wall of shields, each using 15 energy (same as a landing pad).
So I have a single extension, that is just shields (6 shields), just to get the credit.

This means I have 3 stations, a massive amount of tiny shields, and 1,300,000 credits, with 13 employees, and just hit Level 2.
It’s 22/11/3000.



I really thought this was going to be like Deep Space 9 or maybe Babylon 5, where you need to make certain areas on the station fit certain life forms, but… no.

Honestly, I really enjoyed Rollercoaster Tycoon, and Zoo Tycoon, but this is far from those.

Devs…. please make this a better game. I REALLY want to play this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a fun little tycoon-style game which is changing regularly as the devs improve it. The game mechanics are not super complicated, but not too simple either, I found the latest version (0.9 as of writing) to be a fun challenge, especially after some re-balancing the devs did compared to previous versions.

In the latest version you can see a "galactic" map of nearby stars, and the idea is to find interesting spots where to build your space stations so they work as relays for travelers going around. You have to build jump gates in between the stars and your stations, and as you unlock higher levels you get access to better stations and things to build on them.

Overall I found the game to be fun, although there's a couple of glitches here and there (for example, clicking "don't show again" in the employee training confirmation dialog doesn't actually prevent it from showing, things like that) but I haven't found these to harm gameplay too much.

I'm looking forward to the future versions of this game, as this is a genre that hasn't been very popular among developers lately. Cheers!\t\t\t\t\t
