\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is Livings Legends #1, a classic hidden-object game brought up to modern standards. It's a straightforward expression of the genre with point-and-click adventuring, puzzle mini-games (easy to moderate difficulty), and of course hidden-object scenes. There are no groundbreaking mechanics, just solid gameplay. The story is not especially interesting but it serves the game well enough.

The original version is available on Steam in the Frozen Fear Collection and, if we're honest, this remastered version plays identically. If you've already completed the original then you don't really need to play this one, but I think it's a good enough game to warrant a replay, and so you might as well play this nicer version.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Living Legends is a collection of really nice puzzle games, based on old fairy tales, re-imagining and continuing their stories. Another similarity between them is that the antagonist always turns everything to winter, and you must defeat it, restoring normal weather.

This game is based on The Snow Queen by Andersen and it is my favourite of the three. The graphics are really nice; some people may say it's kitsch, but whatever, I loved the visuals, especially when a region turned from winter to autumn colours. The puzzles are good and challenging, but not too extreme. A bonus chapter is available, and in the end you can replay the puzzles or hidden object games of choice. Overall I loved it and I can recommend it to everyone.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great game to play during the cold days of autumn / winter. Players who like the cold seasons, the fantasy genre, the Rosicrucian societies and roses in general will be pleased with this point & click game. You play as a female protagonist who embarks on this wintery journey to find her beloved. As the adventure unfolds you will come to know the other characters' stories, especially the motifs behind the villains' actions and the surprising personal weaknesses that can lead to social disaster. A mere glimpse of curiosity to destroy a town? Hmm... It sounds like me.

PROS:
- Beautiful scenes that capture your eyes;
- Although repetitive, the music fits the game;
- Good voice-acting;
- Story is interesting;
- Journal entries feels like a diary;
- Fast-travelling map (not used, I prefer walking through those scenes);
- There's a mini-game mechanic that I haven't seen in other games: you have to hover the mouse above the tiles in order to put them in the correct place. Now this was superb!
- Main game + Bonus Chapter + Squirrel mini-game;
- Two sets of collectibles: ice squirrels and morphing objects.

CONS:
- No Steam achievements, nor trading cards;
- Wish the protagonist would interact with the villains;
- HO games are repetitive;
