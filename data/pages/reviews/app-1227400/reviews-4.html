\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is just utterly charming.
A work of interactive fiction taking a lot of inspiration from inkle's 80 Days, Over the Alps manages to provide a bit of quaint, almost genteel, spy-fi from the '30s (A very overlooked setting imho), with gorgeous postcard art and engaging writing.
In terms of the interface, it's arguably the most engaging and cute interface i've seen in interactive fiction in a long long time, supplying the player with both visual and written information, doing a lot with surprisingly simple elements.
Again, i found this game utterly charming, and if you are a fan of interactive fiction, like 80 Days or Wanderlust, I heartily recommend.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, where to start? I don't write many reviews but I felt this game really deserve it.

I bought it on sale because I'm not usually fond of visual novels, but the time era, graphics and the fact that I know the country where it takes place made me give in.

It's a visual novel, and oh boy am I not disappointed. I find the views and the drawn countryside simply elegant. I surprized myself sometimes just staring at the view. It's a simple style, but as I said, very elegant and enjoyable. The character portraits sometimes felt a bit "childish" in their style, but were overall very good.

The story is quite taking. The first chapter is very well written, I found the second a bit lacking but had a really good pace.

The soundtrack: mostly ambiant noise (inns, street, train...) and not much music. But when there is music, it is just excellent. Usually short tracks (about the time you'd need to read the text and make your next move), but always hits the right spot. To me, they nailed the music.

The game is still developping, and I cannot wait to see what they have in stock. I loved every minute I spent playing this game and am eager to discover upcoming new content!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPitch perfect: the writing reads like the best old adventure classic you've never read and manages to pack punch, warmth, humor, and pathos in short paragraphs and postcard-length mini-episodes. You're never too long before you're making a new choice or zipping to a new location. I haven't enjoyed a game's writing this much since 80 Days. Oh look! This came from some Inkle veterans. Makes sense.

The writing isn't the only thing that's pitch perfect. This game has a simple, great visual design that doesn't hold your hand. Navigation is subtle and never distracts from the vintage poster style art: flocks of birds are used to indicate buildings you haven't explored yet, a spinning compass tells you when it's time to choose the next path on the map, etcetera.

I've only been through one playthrough as of this review. I can easily see 2-3 different playthroughs depending on how successful you are in the main plot AND in roleplaying different types of spy characters. Will be playing again. And honestly, it's still worth playing through a similar playthrough for the writing alone. So I'll say an easy 4 playthroughs.

The only criticisms I have (and I think great games deserve criticisms, because then their devs can get better and better and, also, so the good stuff doesn't ring like empty praise) were the following:

*I was often caught by police when I'd actually successfully kept down my footprints, and then once I'd hit my footprint quota I would get a warning but no police raid
*The game keeps track of the spy's dominant personality; I just wish this was logged in the journal somehow. I also wasn't sure if you could earn multiple traits or just one dominant one. It's touched on in the final epilogue postcard but otherwise doesn't seem to affect the rest of the story too much.

I wish I'd taken screenshots of some of my favorite quotes, but I still hear snippets at the back of my mind: something about the sun setting like whiskey "and I couldn't drink a drop of it." Just wonderful. Never stop writing!

Very much looking forward to more from Stave Studios. <3

Edit: How did I forget to give kudos to the sound design? It felt like I was really racing cars, drinking coffee, and snuggling up to a crackling fire. Sound guys don't get enough warm and fuzzies, so here's my two cents: you did everything exactly right. (And I wish more games, especially games that lean so heavily on narrative and very few other elements, would spend more time on their sound).\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImpression of the game so far:

Since I played "80 Days" long ago, I was struggling to find a compelling "choose your adventure" game that was as expansive, as well written and visually appealing as that one. I was pleased to find that this game helped me scratch that itch. I haven't yet found anything on Steam that's as expansive as the aforementioned "80 Days", but I'm certainly enjoying my spy-adventure across Europe in this title!

Update after playing through the first case file twice:

The music, writing, and visual elements compliment each other so well in this text based game. It's not complex or heavy material, but it doesn't have to be; it's a fun spy vs. spy romp set in WWII with interesting, likable characters.

If you enjoy narrative driven games, I strongly encourage people to download the demo to at least get a taste of it. When you purchase the game, the save file from the demo carries over. :)\t\t\t\t\t
