\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really like Over the Alps.

This game is extremely relaxing and yet, it does a superb job involving the player into its interesting and unique story. Historically, Over the Alps is special for it’s the first game I’ve ever played that established world war two as its backdrop, yet decided to focus its physical location in switzerland(a persistently neutral nation). But come to find out,(and this might disappoint some), world war two is exactly that in this game, a backdrop. The conflict actually has little to do with the storyline, other than giving the conflicting spy agencies a reason to exist in the first place.Of course that’s not a bad thing, all it does is cement the fact that this game is very much a spy thriller, but not in the same way hitman is. Instead the entire game plays out like a series of letters you write to “Aubrey”. And the choices you make are disguised as individual pieces of the letter. I really think this storyteller's way of playing a choices matter game is interesting and the fact that there is no timer on how quickly you must make choices helps let the player decide the impact of certain decisions.

I can’t say much more without spoiling the game’s story, but I think you should definitely try it out.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good:

- The writing is really good. It touches a cliché or two (probably more than that), but they are the best clichés to have in a
interactive spy novel. Enough to say that writing is so good you will probably finish the game in one session.
- The graphics simple but very nice. I love the little animation that there is. It is so understated and yet so effective that it
is Swiss-like. ;)
- The music is sparse, but what there is is very well done 50's cinema style. Like the animations, the sound effects have a
Swiss quality to them.
- Translation is also very well done. I played the English and Dutch version and they are equally well done. This is coming
from someone who avoids Dutch translations like the plague.
- The game is very reasonably priced, no need to wait for a sale here.

The Meh

- - Stave Studios probably thought that the game and its interface was intuitive enough that it did not need a tutorial. I don't agree.

The Ugly

Can't think of anything.

Verdict

I love Over the Alps. It is the lighthearted bittersweet style of writing that is just perfect for my taste. And i love the graphics to death.

Download the demo and give it a go. And if you like it, don't be a Scrooge and buy it full price. We need more games like this.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t'Over the Alps' is a solid narrative game. It is well-written, appropriately bittersweet for a spy story set just before WWII, and has superb graphic and sound design.

As compared to other recent games based on immersive narration (to which the writers especially have contributed, the game feels really close to 80 Days), Over the Alps falls a bit on the lite side: Having played the first two adventures (a third will be added for free at some point), I can see there a re many ways to play through them, but only little that changes depending on your approach.\t\t\t\t\t
