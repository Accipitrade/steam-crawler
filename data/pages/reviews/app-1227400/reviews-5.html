\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOver the Alps is a text based narrative game, set during the build up to world War Two.
Like most games of its type, you are presented with situations and given a number of ways to react. Not much new there. However, what makes this game stand out are two things: a) outstanding writing and b) a unique art style.

The writing is fantastic. The plot is engaging and the choices given to your character make it feel like you are building a distinct personality with each play through. Though relatively short game, i have played through three or four times and still feel like I haven't seen every choice/path to be found.

The art style is inspired by travel posters of the period (such as LMS posters here in the UK), and is a joy to behold, and the text clear and legible. The sound is also top notch and suits each scene you are presented with.

This is a great game and the average play through time of around two hours feels perfect - long enough to tell its story, short enough not feel like a chore if you want to replay and discover all of its secrets.
Also, there are two more missions to come, in the future, included in the games price.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn interactive spy story, just a bit like 80 Days. Great concept, but rather poorly executed.

You chose what you want to do in the story by picking stamps, the pictures on which seem themed to determine the mood of what you say (or so it seems, any kind of tutorial is non-existent). Stamps like aggressive, nice, polite, secretive. Only, what you chose really results in a highly random event, consistently leaving you wondering how that matched the stamp at all. And that then leads to randomly good or bad results. It would have made more sense just to roll dice.

And whatever results you end up with hardly matter, because the writing and events are invariably dull in each location.

I love this type of game, but can't recommend this one.\t\t\t\t\t
