\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe T-62 is one of my favorite tanks. I enjoy the creative aesthetics of Russian equipment and the T-62 is no exception. Graviteam has once again stepped outside the well-used, but wonderful, box of WWII to present us with another interesting morsel of history: the Iran-Iraq War.

I remember as a teenager in 1980 hearing and reading about what was happening there. It even struck my young, tender mind as something horrible, yet fascinating. I appreciate Graviteam for highlighting this fairly unknown, and horrific, era with as much reality as a "war game" can likely portray.

The quality of the DLC with its new equipment and map included is well worth the price. It's a typical high quality Graviteam product.

I'd like to suggest their other game to complement this DLC, the Steel Armor: Blaze of War. A first person tank simulator, where you may take control of the T-62 or the M-60 on some excellent maps, while being able to add extra tanks and APC's, and give them orders, like you can in any other Graviteam game.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSolid DLC. A relatively short operation, (5-6 turns) Iranian attack on entrenched Iraqi armor in open ground, with a river serving as the MLR.

Approx. battalion size elements of Iraqi T-62's and BMPs vs. Iranian Chieftans and M113's. The Iranians also use BMPs, so it's interesting seeing NATO and Soviet vehicles integrated into the same force. Both sides have some off map artillery, as well as limited air assets in the form of F-5's and Cobras for the Iranians. From what I've seen so far the Iraqis have a few recon helos, and that's it. Might be more towards the last turns. (4/6)

The terrain is flat, with long sight lines and a few small rises that afford great views over most of the map. However, there are palm trees along roads and groves dotted over the map that tend to ricochet longer shots. The primary terrain features are the numerous irrigation canals that divide the landscape up. Crossing points are few enough to make advancing a dangerous business, especially against entrenched armor. Iran really needs to use their air support well, or they get bogged down very quickly.

Overall, the map feels very organic, and imo is one of the better maps. It's very open, and heavily favors the defender, but is varied enough to where using defilade and good tactical movement is rewarding.

However, while I love these modern releases and hope they do more, the engine is really starting to show its age. Semi-guided munitions, jets, and rotary wings do not play very well with the Graviteam formula. The helicopters in general can be very immersion-breaking, very floaty and flying way too close to the enemy. Jets also move kinda janky. It's not awful, but enough to be noticeable. ATGMs suffer from some of this as well, with inconsistent guiding and damage, so I suspect the engine doesn't handle post-WW2 speeds very well.

The audio is my biggest gripe with the DLC (and game). The Chieftan firing sounds like a cowboy action sound effect, bombs whistle like a teapot and small arms sounds and infantry voices are just bad. I'm not sure why this very important part of a combat environment is so far behind the rest of the game, but here we are.

The strafing, jet, and missile sounds on the other hand, are pretty decent, if poorly mixed.

Not too many bugs to speak of as of yet, (turn 4/6). Damage on the ATGMs is weird, as I hit a Chieftan's turret 9 times and he was still rotating and firing accurately, even if detracked. Perhaps there's some ERA or CM modeled that I didn't see, but 9 ATGMs to the face should be fatal. On that note, turret popping and cook offs are especially spectacular in this release, and ammo conservation is your friend. White the ATGMs struggle, regular old T-62's absolutely chew through the Chieftans at the time of this writing, (Feb 22) curious to see if that changes.

I've noticed several infantry using MG42's, I'm assuming this is just laziness on GT's part,
they didn't want to reskin essentially the same gun to the modern MG3. While Iran did and does indeed have contracts for the domestically-produced MGA3, it definitely broke my immersion to see an MG42 being carried around.

Overall, a great DLC and well worth the money. A fun, fresh, and cool mix of units in a new theatre, on a good new map. It's a manageable size, but still the largest modern op yet, certainly the most exiting. As I said, the audio leaves a lot to be desired, but that is GT as a whole. The engine also sometimes struggles to simulate the physics of late Cold War combat.

All that said, it's a fantastic DLC and I am enjoying it greatly. Hoping GT polishes the formula a little bit and continues to deliver more modern scenarios.\t\t\t\t\t
