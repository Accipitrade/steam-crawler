\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI was extremely excited for the way this game looked and after playing the intro and waking up on the island I was ready to go.
I went into my "survival game mode" went to find a stone and some sticks to make an ax (chose backpack and fishing rod from chest) figured could make tools quick. Got the ax and was like oh yeah it's go time.....Why can't I chop down the trees?.....why can't I smash the rocks to get stone...I mean come on guys this is survival game basics. Who wants to hit a button and turn a tree into logs I want the pleasure of doing it myself it's part of the fun of a survival game gathering resources. I was honestly bothered by it and as clean as the game looks and feels I'm debating refunding since it seems even though this is Early Access this isn't going to change and was developed this way on purpose. Can we also get a real block mechanic? Parrying as a main defense sucks. All that said this game is amazing and honestly I would sit here and play for hours on end like I did with Raft....I just can't play it with these 2 mechanics the way they are it doesn't feel right. Hit button turn tree to logs but it wasted time so it's night now and oh no you're no where near your house...that's annoying.. staying out a little late to gather seeing the sun start to go down as you're swinging away and going oh man gotta get back then trying to rush to safety is exciting. Please take this into serious consideration your game has what it takes to be one of the really good survival games and I want to see it get there. Leaving a recommend...because I believe this game has something special and that it's Devs care about feedback. Prove me right.\t\t\t\t\t

I like survival games a lot, I have 239 games with the tag survival. From resource management to simple zombie destruction. Green Hell is a game where you need to learn how the game works. How to heal yourself etc. This game is telling you all that when you discover the resource. Which makes the game less complex.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought it the other day and doing a completely blind play through; I'm a few hours into the hardest difficulty. I've played every survival game, and I have a sick addiction to horribly punishing games like Souls, anything with permadeath and other such titles.

What I can say about this is that it feels slightly different than the rest. They're honestly trying to incorporate realistic survival mechanics, and that you could compare to something like Green Hell. The game also gives you a cave to start, so I don't see any real need to build and I'm pretty tired of doing it on the 100th game anyway. However, I can see a need for outposts in the near future. I don't feel like I have to eat and drink every 30 seconds which is actually nice to see. These aren't negatives for me, because myself and many of you have done these same boring tasks countless times. That doesn't mean they don't matter, however, just that you aren't eating 20,000 calories a day.

Every action you do that is with a static object in the game (trees, bushes, corpses) or when crafting etc, it takes "time" in the form of moving the in-game time ahead forward when you perform the action. You can't just run out and harvest a forest on day one, and it's a clever way to implement a time sink without feeling forced. There is a good mix between slow skillful play and action based combat with the variations of creatures, status effects etc. There is a small story to follow as well, so it's not just an open sandbox.

It's not Long Dark because you aren't freezing to death, sitting in the shade in relatively safety is much simpler; however, I was a little bold and ran when I should have walked and died stupidly a few times at the start, so I'll applaud them for that!
For a reasonably skilled gamer, any of these 'survival' games always reach a point where you snowball beyond what the game can throw at you, and that is likely going to be no different here in the end. For the price I already feel like I've had the value however, and that is what really counts.

I'm not sure if the top reviewer is having some other issues when they wrote how awful it is, but it's not the game I'm playing. Sure, the final product could use some sliders to manage certain things for the ultra hardcore (myself included) - but that is a minor thing for an early access title, which ultimately is why we should all be here. Best thing to do is voice your opinion in a positive manner, and I understand how that can be frustrating when you don't feel heard.

Rant over, go buy the game. I spend more a day on weed.\t\t\t\t\t

One stick weighing 1 unit One Stone weighing 1 unit when combined to make a tool now collectively weighs 4 units? If you get wet on a tropical island where the sun is hot enough to the extent sunburn can kill you, you get the flu? unless that is you light a fire and dry off, on a tropical island where the sun is hot enough to the extent that sunburn can kill you? I am in, however, early accesses and no doubt the the devs will take time to ponder and improve. I shall keep playing and look for updates that will make this more of a game, and (for me) less an exercise in tedium. But for now I wouldn't recommend it.
