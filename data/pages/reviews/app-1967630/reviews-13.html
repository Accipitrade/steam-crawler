\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthings i liked:

-felt clumsy at first, the game seemed very slow paced but it quickly went away once you lvl some skills and spent your survival points.
-the storyline is fun and exploring the map is a good experience
-finding new recipes and books is rewarding as you know you will make use of your newly aquired talents
-the environment is great and immersive
- the gameplay is fun overall

things i didnt like:

-farming items feel like a chore sometimes. you usually have to wait a while before a mashine finishes something you need
-farming copper ore is quite annoying as it takes 21 (?) days for it to respawn. thats just way too long when there is only 4 copper mines
- my animals (boars, snakes and scorpions never respawned). could be a bug
-farming wood to build housees annoyed me as there are only a few trees you could chop to get logs. there should be way more palm trees for building.
- i had to look up a youtube video on how to find horseweed. the was no clear indication on where you can find it. at least tell the player that you need to go to the other islands to get it
- you can only find a small amount of clay on bird island, you have to plan the exact use of it precisely.
- to craft the mushroom soup, you need puffball mushrooms. i had already used all on the ones i found and they never respawned. so i could not get the survival point for that task
- i didnt die once but my overall health was decreased by 10 and i never got it back to full. im not sure if this is intented or a bug

suggestions:

- implement farming pots and seeds, so that you can farm your medicine at home
- make trees respawn or implement more for building houses
- reduce respawn time of boars and copper ore mines
- implement a small clay mine on bird island
- implement puffball mushroom spawns for cooking
- reduce the time for drying skins by a few %. i always had to wait fo them to be ready
- give indication on where to find horseweed
- indicate big leaves (bananaleaves) on the map. you always need those but you never really know where to find them or if they have respanwned already

i liked the game a lot and i cant wait for the next chapters to be released. for now i have finished the storyline and crafted almost every item at least once. i explored all the islands and farmed all kinds of resources. i would recommend this game to anyone who likes green hell, stranded deep or games that are similar to those.

it also has ships and sailing, and a story. it has the classic "the more you do of something, like running, swimming, gathering, cooking etc. the more you skill up that skill" which is always a great & satisfying mechanic. base building feels very good and to the point. all around a very solid and promising survival game.

only thing I kinda miss is being able to plant at your home base so that you don't have to travel far away to gather mats. I hope there's much more to come and can't wait for other biomes/regions and further development. recommended for any survival game enthusiast.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a good ol' fashioned SP survival game with an engaging story (ok, it won't win an Oscar, but it's good), a nice looking game world (e.g. better than ARK 1 but not Unity 5 photo-realistic) and mechanics that are different enough to be fresh and challenging but not so out there you are lost even if you're new to the genre. The game is still in EA and only 2 of 5 regions are currently available but there is already a lot to see and do while the realities of walking/sailing in and around Caribbean islands in the 15th century make the world seem quite large - and biome exploration is a basic requirement if you want to do more than sit on a beach drinking coconut milk and trying to spear a crab for lunch. There is a good amount (over 100?) recipes and techs to learn, upgrading gear, clothes, ships and workbenches as you go as well as 'learn by doing' character development system. First person only may be an issue for some, however, the difficulty settings provide options for hard core masochists as well as casual gamers looking for a less stressful way to die slower (jokes, "very easy" mode is just that). Building is solid if not amazing (want to build a polished marble Greek temple? Go play Conan) but Fountain of Youth allows you to build a base that looks and feels like it should for the Robinson Crusoe you essentially play. The wildlife and resources have been balanced since the demo released and the whole package is now just a blast and one where you are (thankfully) never really super resource rich and secure as things take a sensible amount of time to regrow/respawn. Technically, I have an intel i7 8700 with a GTX 1080Ti and the game runs silky smooth for me and I've had zero freezes, CTDs, save game problems or any other issues at all that I can think of. The devs are engaged in the Steam forum and more updates are planned in the months (July?) ahead. I've finished the story such as it is to date, been to all the islands and key locations (that I know of), built most everything there is to build, stocked up on a relatively comfortable amount of resource in my base (again, not enough to feel really relaxed about things) and it has taken me about 60+ hours (I really didn't rush through though) - I will almost certainly stretch that as playing it as a sand box is very possible. Multiple save slots also means I will be starting again shortly with new settings and base location.

Would I recommend as is, even in EA? Hell yes.
Would I buy it again knowing what I know now? Hell yes.
Is it good value for money? No - it's GREAT value for money.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've got to say this is probably the best EA crafting survival game right out of the gate that I've ever played. And I have played most of them, and for much longer than I would like to admit. I was concerned in the opening scene when it had janky controls in some kind of mini-challenge but once arriving on the beach and getting a feel for actual gameplay it turned out to be an awesome single player experience.

There are a lot of unique mechanics and I can't think of one that I dislike. The dramatic and level up sounds remind me fondly of Conan Exiles. The graphics are very well done for a Unity release. There is an overarching story that you can follow at your own pace or just ignore if you like.

There are a lot of building parts if you like building or take up residence in a POI like I did and set up shop there.

A massive array of crafted items are revealed only as you discover their components in the wild or craft other related items. Hint: craft almost everything at least once (whether you want it or not) because it may unlock something you really want or need.

Combat and survival seem fun and well balanced to me. And finally a game recognizes that you cool down in shade and heat up in direct sun instead of just overheating you in any hot biome. Like most similar games, there is a bit of grinding for resources and running around dealing with stamina gauge, hunger, thirst, and disease. But all of these challenges seem to progress at reasonable pace and give you adequate ways to remediate them.

It's an awesome game now and has clearly outlined expansion path that will undoubtedly provide new exploration and combat.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLoving the game so far - I have completed all existing quests, tasks, and recipes, mapped all current islands. eagerly awaiting the arrival of the remaining 3 island areas to continue the adventure.

Pros:
Compelling story line
Beautiful landscapes
Sailing and exploration is really well thought out and fun

Cons:
Housing and building pretty nerfed at moment - I tried to get a traditional roof with two opposing sloping roof tiles - but says "can't overlap tiles" so stuck with shed like buildings at present (i.e. slopes only one way or flat roofed)... Building levels pretty hard as you can only put a ladder on a platform level, rather than just up against existing wall
Some materials are really rare and in the case of puffball mushrooms only found them so far in one location - so means mushroom soup is off the menu as they had already turned to spoiled food before I got to the rest of the raw materials to make the soup... all other food recipes seemed pretty reasonable.
Lack of flint and pyrite (almost invisible against rock), are they even renewable - why not have these as additional rare slow to spawn at copper, stone or obsidian mining deposits? Seems to make more sense to have them there (then locked until you have a pick, so wouldn't cause a surge early on).
Can flexible branch be burned at some point in the future - it only currently has one use but can't be burnt, so is pretty much ignored as a resource post crafting that item... this is true also of tegu skins (I assume there will be more craftable objects from the leather at a future point?)
Mining resources - the refill is somewhat pathetic after first depletion - i.e. copper mine has around 17 units initially but only 2 units post refresh after 14 days of waiting to refill!!! - this is a bit silly as the requirement on copper is pretty high when upgrading buildings and weapons (and subsequently the need for repairing durability meant i actually regressed back to using bone knife and obsidian weapons/tools as copper became too rare for practical use for tools by later stage of game)
How about putting in some salvageable iron in shipwrecks (as there definitely would be some!) - this would mean the initial items from the starting chest could be repaired. and would be more useful .. there currently doesn't actually seem much of a benefit of any of the starting items as the durability on them is so low it is always more beneficial to craft something new (with only the pistol being the only item that doesn't have an equivalent item in game so can't be mended or crafted at present, so the only really useful item)
Could the concentration skill also outline /highlight resources or any items on the ground - this seems useful for mushrooms, or any dropped items (it does seem to currently give a map reference for items dropped in the sea)
A number of times I was jumping around on rocks in the mountains and fell into what i suspect is meant to be an inaccessible location as you can't jump out of it again - could there be an unstuck option on the escape button menu that means I can be moved to a safe areas with all my items intact... Dropped items in such locations could become potentially unreachable - which would be bad news for any unused items such as unique items like the skill books.

Definitely looking forward to future adventuring in the game.\t\t\t\t\t
