This is an amazing game already, my favourite survival game now! I'm not that far in this EA version yet, but I had played the demo for about 90 hours last year (several playthroughs). I think that alone says a lot.

It's a pretty realistic survival game (no weird monsters or fantasy elements), which presents you with a lot of the challenges you'd also face in reality. From the obvious unfriendly animals, over weather effects like sun, wind, rain and related illnesses, injuries e.g. when crafting in the dark, and of course your own needs for food and water etc.
There's a day/night-cycle and crafting actually costs time (it's fast in realtime, but could represent an hour or 2 in the game for example), which I like very much.
There are 5 different difficulties you can choose from, but I think if you're looking for a truly relaxing experience, you should look elsewhere. Although compared to the demo, they definitly made "normal" (the middle diff) easier, especially those killer birds :D Animals are less aggressive now. But you still need to deal with lots of different threats. Be prepared to figure many things out on your own. There is a tutorial with the very basics, but that's it. Exploration is encouraged, as you unlock new crafting/building recipes by discovering new resources, which are sometimes only available in very specific parts of the world.
There are a lot of things to craft and build, including boats to explore other islands. You can also explore the underwater world.
In addition to that, there's a story. I can't say much about the story yet, as there wasn't too much of it in the demo and I'm not far into this EA version, but the survival part alone is fun and challenging enough for me to recommend this game already.
It looks good (I love the lighting, especially sunrise + sunset look gorgeous!), the atmosphere is awesome, and I haven't encountered any bugs yet (there were also not many in the demo and rarely, if ever, game-breaking). I feel like there's some optimization still needed though.
You can also rebind your keys.

The devs are amazing, very involved with their community and receptive to feedback, which was already very visible during the time the demo was available and got new/tweaked features and fixes. It's also apparent that they already included some more of the demo feedback into this EA version of the game.
There's an EA roadmap, which looks very promising to me. Even more crafting. building, areas to explore, longer story, etc.

Update after 40 hours game time:
The first update is here and adressed some community feedback already, great! Especially that you can now harvest stuff while a snake is watching you from 10 meters away is awesome.
I finished the story part that's there until now. I like it and I'm looking forward to learn more about what happened to my crew and how our search for the fountain of youth is going. But having finished the story doesn't mean that I'm done exploring yet. I still have one big island and several small or tiny ones to explore.
The game is worth it's money for sure. I'm 40 hours in, haven't explored eveything yet, let alone built more than a very simple small hut with a dock (and the machines I needed so far of course). I also only play on normal right now, so I could just enjoy the story. I'm gonna switch to harder difficulties in my next playthrough(s?). Plus I feel like having played the demo helped a lot, because I knew right from the start, where to get my first resources and what I would need etc. So add another 5 hours or so to my playtime for that :) And this is only EA with 3 of the 5 regions still missing!\t\t\t\t\t
