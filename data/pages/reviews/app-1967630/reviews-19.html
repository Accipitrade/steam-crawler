\r\n\t\t\t\t\t So far I've been very addicted to this game since release because it strikes a balance of being a challenge without being overbearing. Survival elements are always on my mind but not terribly hard to manage just a matter of planning out my trips for resources. As with most survival games progression eases the difficulty for water/food etc. as you get better items. Then added onto their are perks added in as you do things repeatedly aka talents that kick in. Combat takes some skill after repeatedly fighting "honey badger" and the birds I haven't tired of it.

I would like to address some users on the forums making complaints that puzzle me. The fov of this game is fine... I have the worst motion sickness that's induced very easily, so far I've played for hours and not felt ill. Time passes as you craft/harvest etc. which is different but it makes time matter rather than just conjuring things into thin air like a wizard. Hence that's why mp/co-op is not really even possible because if that's added it would do away with the time mechanic altogether.

Overall I'm really surprised by this game when I purchased it I was expecting a janky typical survival game for the price but was dead wrong. It has a lot of content at 8hrs ingame so far I don't even have a boat constructed as i'm still exploring the first island. Gotta give credit to the developers for answering questions in the forum nice to see them interact with people rather than hiding like some devs do. Only con is the "threat nearby" cannot build anything... I would like to see that done away with as its annoying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t5/9/23 Edit: The game is a comprehensive survival experience with a diverse variety of plant and animal resources available to the player for use in crafting/building. The second cluster region of islands has new animal and plant species, but none of the islands are all that different from each other in terms of visual appearance or resources. In most instances, resources are in higher abundance on the main island where the player begins - so there is little reason to build multiple bases or production facilities.

Clean water seems very difficult to obtain, even on normal difficulty. The main island has 2 rain water collection sources and the ability to craft personal collectors or distillers, but even then you're playing the RNG game with weather. Water evaporation is too quick. It could rain an entire day and your collectors could fill completely. By the next sunny day, the natural rain water collectors could be at half capacity by the time the player makes the journey to the collection site. Distilling saltwater is useful, but tedious as the player has to refill the saltwater tank and light the fire each time. Solar distillers are most efficient, but unavailable until unlocking a particular resource. Most survival games often fail to nail freshwater resources and Fountain of Youth is no different. I'd normally push Coconuts here, but I've gained so many negative health effects like Mild Indigestion from consuming coconuts that it's just not worth it anymore. It's like a 2% chance to gain a negative health effect when consuming Coconuts and I always hit the jackpot. I'm hoping down the road we can plant our own crops and fruit trees.

I'm a bit conflicted on the story. It seems like the developers are still choosing between whether this is a survival game or a story driven game. It's clear to me there was almost no budget for the static cinematic stills. Having to sit through a forced cinematic as the camera pans around 3-D models performing basic actions makes me feel like I'm back in 2000.

Honestly, the game is good, but I'm finding more excuses not to log-in and play because of the animal re-spawn bug. I get it, early access, bugs are expected and ironed out prior to full release, but there has been 1 hotfix patch since launch. I'll pick this up again when the next region is added.

The Good:
1. Gorgeous environment
2. Vast landscape with numerous plant and animal species
3. Diverse blueprint and crafting system
4. Basic in-game tutorial (might need some tweaking)

Needs Work:
1. Mapping system - Drawing the map is as simple as climbing one of the larger trees or canvasing the area from a high altitude, such as the top of a rock. This action reveals every resource in a given radius and is available for inspection at any time, which really detracts from the overall immersion of a survival game. I'd rather have the ability to draw the map from a high point, but manually contribute my own markers as I discover them. This would also require a more diverse variety of custom markers available to the player.

2. Crafting/Harvesting - The current crafting/harvesting uses an automated menu system that displays projected resources, the time to craft/harvest, and the tools needed to perform the action. You are not swinging your ax to cut down trees, nor are you reeling in the fish at the end of your fishing rod. You are unable to harvest if you're being targeted by any predator. A HUGE downside to this automated menu system is that you are often attacked by roaming pests (scorpions/spiders) during an action and is completely unavoidable without a craft-able pest deterrent. If I use a certain skill to scan the area for pests and find nothing within a 200 ft radius, it's utter crap to be attacked by a scorpion 30 minutes into a tree harvest.

3. Skills - The majority of skill perks are fantastic. Ex. An additional 5% damage from thrown weapons, increased stamina, and a 5% increased maximum weight capacity as the player levels each skill category. Other perks reduce the chance for injury during cooking by an additional 1%. The XP needed to level skills early is fairly quick. Leveling skills past level 5 is tedious as you're still earning the same amount of XP for performing actions - despite supposedly raising your understanding in those fields. The current build discourages night time activities as the player has a greater chance for injury after sunset. I'd rather see perks that may increase the chance for a higher quality food item as you level your cooking skill or provide some benefit to night time activities. The Point Perks (PP) task system is a nice implementation, but is more story driven. The player has to explore ancient tablets and stone drawings to unlock and improve specialized skills.

4. Flora/Fauna Re-spawn - Every resource operates on a timer, with the average duration being about 30 in-game days (50 days for the tortoise). Days progress as real time. If a passive process (like drying hides) takes 12 hours, you can either wait 12 hours or complete an action to speed through time. Re-spawn timers are displayed on the map for animals, but not stone or plant resources. I will say it's pretty annoying to spend an ENTIRE day traversing the map from one side to the other in search of a stone deposit, only to be reminded of another 2 days remaining on the re-spawn timer. Currently, the re-spawn timers for animals are bugged. The days count down on some animals and reset once hitting zero days or sailing between island regions.

There's a few other observations such as locating the majority of stones only in dedicated stone piles, or identifying Liana used for making rope and other tools. I think the description for Liana reads "can be gathered from Banyans and some bushes." Like, hey idiot, go find the Banyan. I had to Google what a Banyan was. I think this is where we should actually have a flora/fauna encyclopedia or journal in the Journal tab, where every plant/tree and animal we interact with creates its own page.

The durability system is just horrible. I spend too much time repairing, gathering for repairs, and managing weight so I can repair my gear which is really heavy btw.\t\t\t\t\t
