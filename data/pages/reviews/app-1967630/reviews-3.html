\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a great game, even in its current state, July 2023. As it stands, the main starter area is done as well as one additional island group, with another slated for next month and two more to follow. The developers have been adding these areas as they approach full release on schedule, with added patching and bug fixing between the area additions. Even at it's current state, it's quite balanced in gameplay and difficulty, with additional difficulties for a challenge if you prefer that option, including a hardcore death mode. It's reminiscent of a combination of The Long Dark, with a more first person perspective similar to The Forest. It is single player, but don't let that dissuade you from a very good game that looks to be a keeper even now, and with additional areas before the full release, should be a mainstay in the survival genre.

This isn't a shooter, nor a co-op game and the struggle you will face to survive long term is real and an integrated part of the gameplay. You have to carefully manage food, drink, rest, and multiple health conditions, even as you attempt to gather resources for a foothold, and many upgrades of things along the way. But the story here also pushes you to advance and not get stagnant, with rewards for things along the way as well as points to spend to customize your character a bit. The maps are static, but quite large and as a guess should provide 80+ if not 100+ hours of game time by the full release.

I've played through to the temporary finish point on normal and about at the same point on hard mode (with an additional challenge mode after that should I want to play it.) The additional challenge is welcome and puts this game very close to the feeling of The Long Dark where you have many times during gameplay where you wonder if you'll make it, if you'll loose a precious life (called fainting here,) or if on hardcore death setting, if you'll end your game. This game requires a bit of strategy, a lot of preparation and some luck along the way to survive. As someone that loves survival games, I appreciate that planning, upgrading and building are rewarded during gameplay, and while luck is there, you can fight it with better planning and gear.

The graphics are good, utilizing the Unity engine they appear to have gone for a feel somewhere between painted as in The Long Dark and realistic, similar to The Forest. If you've played Green Hell, its a similar look and feel. There are multiple dark areas in the game as well as some stormy/rainy nights that can be VERY dark. Weather is handled well in the graphics engine and is also an integrated part of the gameplay, requiring rain for water collection, overcast days for better fishing, sunny days for better visibility to do mapping, etc.

The sounds are good, with a decent undertone of music, which changes as you approach danger, etc. There are also unique sounds to most of the wildlife in game, providing a way to hear when they are near, as well as which direction they may be in, even if you cannot see them yet.

The game loop slowly changes over time from basic survival, branching out to gaining a foothold with an encampment, and growing to sailing, traveling to new areas, creating small outposts to help process things you find there, and so on. Boating in this game is rather simplified, but allows fast travel once you do some initial exploration. The vast majority of this game is on foot, which highlights both the early time period of its setting as well as making it feel like more of a challenge for survival.

I'm still having a blast at 150 hours in on harder gameplay modes and I think that bodes well for the full release. If you are a survival game lover, go all in now at the early access price, I don't think you'll be disappointed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is in Early Release and review comment might change.
You are a crew member on the search for the Fountain of Youth. You become ship wrecked and have to survive, thrive, map and explore all while searching for surviving crew members.
You must hunt, eat, drink and gather resources to make the tools you need to expand your skills and move to other regions
For me I went into this early release game guessing it was similar to all other survival games. Though grindy , the goals you achieve and the story driving missions add a fun complexity to a exploration based survival game. After about 2 hours in I became fully invested in the adventure. As a result I was motivated to advance my skills and technology, Who knew that you can turn a coconut into a radio (Gilligan joke inserted).
I found my self frustrated when I finished the initial content, I just wanted to move forward. For me that was indication that the rest of the game will be a blast.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA survival game in a conquistador setting with a strong focus on exploration and some interesting mechanics like sunburn?

What's not to like, this sounds right up my alley.

About fifteen minutes into the game though, I started suspecting that the developer lacked understanding of what makes a survival game fun.

The things is, how you interact with the world is the possibly the main concept you need to get right in this the kind of game. At its best you experience a tactile engagement with a living world. Chopping that log, felling that tree or picking up those rocks needs to feel good and rewarding.

Or you could just make the process so tedious, I loose all enjoyment of it.

Which is what this game chose to do.

Here, when you engage with any object or "resource node" in the world, you press a button to interact, which brings up a menu. You then select how many resources out of a maximum you want to harvest from that node - let's say picking up three sticks out of five available from a small tree. The game then calculates the time per resource harvested to pass while you did that and advances the clock accordingly.

It feels like sitting in a beautiful nature location, but only focusing on the spreadsheet data printouts of it that you have brought along.

It feels inherently wrong.

It feels like I am playing a game, not interacting with a world.

While this is the one design choice that made me not want to play the game, it is by no means to only bad one.

In good survival design a resource node never feels like a resource node, but a part of the world you engage with. The opposite of that would be when you, for example, cannot pick up those nice looking stones over there, because they aren't part of a node or you can only harvest sticks from that specific kind of tree, never mind picking them off the ground like you would in real life.

Which, again, is what this game chooses to do.

Nevermind that those basic resource nodes are unnaturally scarce. I get that the developer wants to make survival challenging, but when you make picking up sticks, stones and leaves a tedious process with a lots, and I really mean lots, of running around, the fun gets lost along the way. I don't think I have ever seen it done quite as badly in any surival game.

Sadly, the world interaction through menus and time passing offscreen seems to be integral to the developer's vision for the project, which is why this ended up being the first time I actually refunded a game on Steam.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEvery once-in-a-while I find a gem of a survival game that just gets me hooked, in the case of this one I played it for 12 hours straight in one day on a long weekend. The gameplay loop, the crafting, resource gathering, hunting, exploration, RPG elements, and a bit of story all combined and done well make this a ton of fun. It's similar to Green Hell, but I found this much more fun and not so brutal in difficulty. Though on this you have a nice variety of options for difficulty, including an iron man mode. I've just played on the normal difficulty and settings, though I will say it may be a bit too easy on this mode since I have yet to die/faint and I've recently been to a more difficult island and killed a Jaguar. There are a few downsides to this game, it can sometimes be a bit of a grind and become tedious when having to carry so many heavy resources back to your base/camp to craft what you need. You can build a cart and a log cart to carry more stuff longer distances to mitigate this. Another minor issue is that I often find that I wish this had co-op multiplayer, because there are so many other games just like this that have that feature and this is a very fun game which I think would be even more fun to play with a friend or two.\t\t\t\t\t

Seriously.

Imagine a modern survival game that...when you swing an axe at a tree a menu comes up where you select how many branches you want. That's this game. The devs intended it this way. None of the screenshots show it (as of this review)

It's like playing a baseball game but when you go to swing the bat, a menu comes up with how you want to swing the bat then text shows up where the ball lands.

It's...dumb. Weird. Something that is really, really out of place in a modern 3d game. Something you'd expect to see in a text based game. Hopefully this is a one off thing and not a looming nightmare coming to all 1st person games.\t\t\t\t\t
