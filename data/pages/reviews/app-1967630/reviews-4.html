\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA good survival game with decent crafting and building. The main difficulty comes from there are many types of statuses and diseases to manage. Quite grindy. Able to sail around and explore islands freely on a ship is fun.

Didn't quite like the combat because it feels buggy eg. the leopard can often attack while having its back facing the player or tegu has longer attack range than its body hitbox when lungeing forward.

Another thing is the brutal equipment durability system, combine with the long resource respawn time is a disaster. It takes 8 ingame days to spawn one copper ore when 2 ores are needed for 1 copper ingot. For comparison it took me around 30hrs playtime to reach day 100 without constantly skipping time with sleep. Even after unlocking copper tools, I wasn't to use copper tool all the time because the copper mine supply from all regions simply cant keep up with the amount of copper used for repairing tools. Pretty much forced to use stone tools. What a disappointment.

The game is still rough in its current state but it is fun as it is.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI found this game as a result of a steam sale. Never heard of it before, but after reading some comments and looking at some pictures of the game I thought "That sounds/looks exactly like what I've been looking for, but where The Forest really let me down".

I am 2.5 hours into the game as of writing this review and I am absolutely hooked.
Conceptually speaking the game is very straight forwards, but it has so much more depth to the survival than you'd initially assume. I love everything about it so far and I couldn't be happier that steam suggested me this gem of a game I'd otherwise unlikely find. Worth every penny if you love "scavenge for resources, staying healthy is a task, survival-type" games like I do.

One time where I had just about set up a fire to cook some food to save my life, only for it to immediately start raining upon me actually managing to ignite the fire, that's the kinda things that makes you go "oh screw you!" (I literally said it out loud) but that is also the elements to a game like this that makes you really feel like you're "fighting for survival".
