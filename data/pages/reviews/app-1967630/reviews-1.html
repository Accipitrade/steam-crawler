\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn excellent take on the genre. The controls and UI are relatively easy to learn and do not hinder the immersive nature of this storied survival game. The graphics are sublime, especially the ocean and it's waves, and qualify this game for purchase by themselves. When you throw in the developing crafting system, meticulous environment, and a flowing story that doesn't feel like the usual plot on rails you can't avoid in other games of this type, you get a product that has plenty of replay value. Looking forward to future updates, hopefully one that will vitalize the poor ocean with only 2 types of fish you can hunt underwater. The only things sorely missing, IMHO, are an auto-run keybind and the ability to acquire a compass at some point. Overall 4/5, possibly 5/5 when we get to discover who the murderer was.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts a really great concept, when I 1st saw it I thought "a mix between Green Hell and Stranded Deep". While it may give stranded a run it doesn't come close to comparing with Green Hell. After giving it a solid 10 hours I ended up here for this review. As an early release, I wish I has waited awhile longer before trying it out. I spent 90% of my time playing, running around trying to find simple crafting items. Nothing is in the same area and if you run halfway across the map to get long sticks, make sure you have an axe. if not, you'll spend another 20 minutes trying to find the stones to make one. The map is extremely spread out and the animals are pretty much tied down where they are, no roamers. It has been a complete waste of time trying to build a camp beyond a bed and a fire. Even the long sticks that you can find take 18 to 20 game days to respawn and the next batch of trees to harvest is nowhere close. The survival aspect is not to bad but the entire game at this point is very repetitious and frustrating. The game looks decent but all in all, it is very boring at this early stage of its life. I do hope the material gathering and mostly barren, dead map receive a lot of attention.

Again, a lot of potential but I stopped enjoying it about 30 minutes in. The other 9.5 hours were spent running around aimlessly in search of the simplest crafting items, trying to find water and trying to stay alive. The map is just to big with everything separated and spread to far apart which didn't allow me focus on the story much. Some very good concepts, I think it will be great if they bring it all a little closer together\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR

Highly recommended, If you enjoy games like the long dark, Green Hell you should enjoy this.

Pros.

Has a great sense of immersion, exploring, crafting, base building, weather, music and sound.

Negatives.

The game uses menus with progress bars for a lot of the resource gathering like mining, and cutting wood. You don't actually cut the tree with an animation. As gathering takes time it is another resource for the player.

I would prefer to cut and mine myself and see cooking animations not just a menu with a progress bar.

You don't sail between regions yourself but can sail around in those regions, I'm sure this is done for gameplay reasons and maybe performance.

One more thing is the performance around some areas with a lot of foliage, still playable but not great.

Highly recommend Survival: Fountain of Youth, but remember it is early access.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has everything a survival addict wants. I've played The Long Dark, Green Hell, Subnautica, and a whole host of other survival games and this one is among the top 3 in my opinion. The crafting seems to never end as there are always new recipes that make life better and better. Even though it is possible to get "settled" in you never really feel like everything is fine. There is always something else you need to make survival easier. I love that they have the options separated for iron man setting as I always love playing permadeath but it's usually only available on harder setting that you cannot usually set it up separately but in this game you can. The wildlife is fun and the scenes are breathtaking. I only with they could add a paint feature to take "photos" of the world without the HUD. I haven't finished the game yet so I don't know if they have implemented this but one thing I would add would be perhaps random things washing up on shore maybe added nets that would catch drifting items or fish that could be checked daily. Traps would be an awesome addition.\t\t\t\t\t
