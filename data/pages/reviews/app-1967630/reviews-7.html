\r\n\t\t\t\t\tInteresting game so far. It's pretty tough to stay healthy. Lots of crafting of healing-type items to keep negative effects off. I like the survival difficulty of it.

The first person perspective, or field of view could use some settings. I'd love a FOV bar to zoom out a little (or third person, even better). Also the POV feels like you are 2 feet tall, or crawling around all the time. This game gives me a little motion sickness because of these things.

Outside of that, this is a heavy inventory management game. Lots of different items, inventory capped by slots and carry weight. So be prepared to make a bunch of containers if you are like me and pick up EVERYTHING. ;)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to admit that this is one of the best survival games out there (imagine if Green Hell and Raft had a baby and then add a great story line, challenging survival and good crafting/building aspects. There are a lot of things I love about this game (even in its early stages) with only a few cons: One of them is the copper re-spawn times. With the current rate of decay and needed repair of your copper items/armor, weapons, e.t.c, having to wait 14 in-game days ( I believe its around that number) for a full re-spawn of a copper node/resource, OR at least 9 in-game days for just one copper nugget to re-spawn at the node is pretty frustrating. Basically, your copper tool/weapon will break before there is enough copper to replace or repair them all. I only wish they would shorten the wait time a bit for the mining resource re-spawn ( I wouldn't expect them to shorten the decay/repair rate as this may take away some form the current challenging aspect of this game (you really have to plan your days and resources in this game :). Overall, though, I give this early access survival game a 8 1/2 out of 10 and huge thumbs up to the developers for a great, challengin and fun survival game that has many of my favorite things (open world, survival craft/building,sailing, ocean biome and tropical islands with dangerous wildlife! :) look forward to the updates and new content (more islands to explore!)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's a good game, but let down by a sailing mechanic that completely undermines its own lore.

There are contemporaries in the Survival Crafting genre, like Stranded Deep and Valheim that make their sailing a compelling part of the gameplay and world exploration. Their sailing has genuine hazards of various kinds, a combination of skill-floor and tech upgrades to improve the experience, and yet it plays no real part in either of their lore. The sea is just an environment to be traversed.

In Fountain of Youth there's a LOT of lore built up about how dangerous these waters are. These seas have claimed many ships, and you're a ragtag survivor of one of them, on the trail of other ragtag survivors from yours and previous shipwrecks.
This lore is built and built and built upon continually as you explore across the first island, locate the next one, then prepare to put to sea again to continue on your epic journey of adventure...

Aaaaannnnd then your boat turns out to be a Mario Cart that just scoots along the water, faces no dangers or hazards other than bumping into things and losing a sliver of health, and can be ignored entirely once you build a dock at the other end to fast-travel back and forth.

It's hard to overstate just how disappointing that was. It's boring, makes exploration between islands basically meaningless, and worst of all it DEMOLISHES the game's own lore about the dangers of the sea... I should have died. Preferably two or three times. Or survived and been forced to make a new boat... The waters around these islands should be giving me survival-horror levels of heart palpitations, so that I'm left thanking my lucky stars when I finally DO reach the next major narrative landmass, in one piece and with my boat intact...

But instead I just couldn't be bothered to play anymore. It really did take all the wind out of my sails. Pun intended.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn absolute fantastic game, i love it. It is a mixture and a combination of all good things out of Green Hell, Raft, Long Dark and Salt 2 Shores of Gold.
Of course it is early access and there are some minor bugs, but the game can be played perfectly until a point mid game.
The improvements which i wish:
1. Better UI, for example in the beginning you will drink a lot of coconuts. The actions needed to cut open en then to drink the coconut are getting after coconut 5 a bit much. It would be easier to make one action drink and accumulate the time and actions needed.
2. Being able to lift two logs after a certain strength
3. Being able to see the other animals with V after a certain skill upgrade
4. A bit more realistic weights of copper. One copper needed for one axe is not 5kg, but 1kg. Currently it takes the speed out of the game immensely when you have to farm copper for some items.
5. When you improve your skills like skinning or gathering you should be able to get more. now its only a time factor
6. The ocean floor looks like most tropical oceans of 2023. Coral bleaching emptyness, almost no life and no color. This needs a lot of improvement to get the game to feel like i am in the 16th century... please view Jacques Cousteau movies for inspiration
7. There is in some locations an overkill of harpies
8. The spawn rate of the komodos / lizards need to be reduced , now after 2 days there are new ones.
9. It would help when you skin or click an animal to automatically get the arrows back
10. It should be easier to upgrade all tool benches, work equipment and that it will take all recipes. Now sometimes you need the old work bench besides the new one just to make a base item. This should be integrated in the better version.

Please continue the great work and i cannot wait until the game is in a later stage of development!
