\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm changing the text of my review after playing it for a few more hours to say this.

This is the best survival game, full stop.

Not the best horror game, not the best action game, not the best base building game (though it is very good at those as well, yes even horror. You will be scared when a snake jumps outta nowhere to get you or when a jackal starts chasing you). The best SURVIVAL game.

SotF could learn a thing or two from this.

You will get cold at night. You will wander around frantically searching for a cure for the runny nose you caught during that same night, only to be ambushed by a snake that poisoned the crap out of you.
You will watch as the clothes on your back fall to tatters over fall damage and running through nettles like an a-hole... and you will do all this with a sunburn.

You will also notice that your hunger and thirst drain at a, for the first time in this genre, decent and appropriate rate. You can keep yourself from starving for days without eating if your activity is low, and this is something most games like this have struggled with. (gotta eat 10lbs of cooked flesh a day or I die!)

This game is hard, yes, but seriously if you just take your time... make each day have a singular task (today I get rocks, today I get food, today I try and make some workbenches, etc)the game is easily approachable.

Well that's my rant, have fun!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI strongly urge anyone who enjoys survival games to pick this game up and give it a try, AND BE PATIENT! When I bought this game I found myself not enjoying it for the 1st 2 hours of gameplay. I took a step back and actually played another game for a couple of hours, and once I was relaxed and ready to try this game again I went for a straight 12hr binge. What I didnt like at first impression was how stiff and slow everything is, coupled with the "Long Dark" style of time passing during certain actions you make. But I now very much appreciate how the physics of the player work in the game, as it feels more grounded in realism, and really isn't that bad at all. The time passing mechanic kinda grew on me as it actually keeps the pace of the game going from decision to decision and I have absolutely no complaints.

What keeps me hooked on playing the game is the immersion I feel in the world. The graphics are great , but not fascinating and they remain consistently polished to the point where every asset on the island and it's surrounding waters feels like it belongs. The map itself is very well made and I find myself keen on discovering everything if I have the stamina to do so. There are so many things to learn and build in this game that I can foresee myself putting hundreds of hours into this game, which brings me back around to my first point.

If you are a fan of Survival games, you don't want to miss out on this game!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am really liking this game, but I have some constructive feedback....

Pro's
- Visually great looking
- Challenging in terms of keeping healthy
- Large maps/ areas to explore
- lots to construct and things to build (work stations etc)
- Small elements of good things from other games (Crafting, exploring, sailing).

Con's / constructive

1) Basic things like sticks/stones can take 20 mins to go find (not in abundance) like the Forest
- When you need 4 sticks and need to run 15 mins to a tree on the other side of the island
- When you need a stone, the minimal 2-3 formations are empty and you need to find a random round stone
- Running past trees/bushes which can't be farmed (no animation) like on Raft is grindy

2) Away from the story, the game can feel bland like "Raft"
- Lots of random small islands with nothing on them
- It's great building kilns etc, but clay is very rare in most areas - which defeats wanting to build a base/items.
- Same animals all over, dog, snake, and scorpion placed near resources makes it a bit "MMO" feeling

3) Resources are spread far too wide, with 3 or 4 stone piles on a large island (when running past millions of rock
formations)
- Large Island - Great! Lots of trees and rocks - Awesome! Can you mine most of it - NO?
- Throw stuff like random big sticks, branches, and more sticks around, let us build and be creative once the story/ initial survival element is getting stale.
- No animations/time consumption for crafting - often contradicts the survival system

When you first spawn in, getting sunburned, thirsty, and naked...feels like the movie "Cast Away" which is AWESOME!

After a while, it starts feeling like the game "Raft" where it's nice and pretty, but so much missing and there could be so much more to do...please add this whilst it's in early access! :)\t\t\t\t\t
