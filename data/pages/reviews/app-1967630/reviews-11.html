\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like taking your time to harvest, craft and build, take a look at this Caribbean themed survival. However, the pacing is very slow so be aware of this if you like more action orientated gaming.

The game is very chill as far as survival goes, and I'm certainly enjoying the game. Learning the lore and building my home base.

It is very polished in terms of movement, graphics, interactions and the like. So the game-play is very good.

The major downside is that everything you do uses time. If you want a specific resource, which might be on the other side of the island, it can take an entire in game day to achieve. Travel time, harvesting takes time, travel back, use the resource takes time.

Great game, but needs options to modify the timing it takes to do things, or the amount of resources gathered.\t\t\t\t\t

For a game this is still in Early Access phase it is truly amazing - some of these reviews calling it boring just don't make sense to me unless they just straight up don't like survival games or have unrealistic expectations for a game in its development phases.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo start, this is a beautiful game. It is immersive and fun to play and explore.
I have logged 40+ hours in the game and am on day 140+ in game. I have completed the story line and have crafted everything but leather armor with copper plates. I am playing on a 1080Ti and have no lag issues at high settings.

Pros:

Graphics and lighting are beautiful.
Water effects are beautiful.

Weather matters:
Rain and heavy wind prevent you from starting fires.
Rain will cause a soaked condition that can lead to illness.
Overcast makes it harder to see under a forest canopy and in the high grass.
Sunlight can cause overheating. Keep to the shadows.
Wind causes high waves that damage your boat.

Animal encounters:
The snakes are terrifying. (It took me a while to figure out V = concentration vision)
Spider and Scorpion encounters while harvesting startle you!
Animal AI is pretty good. If you are at elevation they can't get to you at, they see you and run away. When you drop to their level, they come back for you.

Story is wonderful:
It pulls in elements of Central/South American native themes. While the story give you tasks, it doesn't hold your hand. It took me a while to figure out what I needed to do.

Survival crafting system is well thought out:
Once you start doing it, the time passing during crafting is actually to your benefit. It keeps resource regeneration moving. It also makes you think about your conditions before you decide to engage in a 10 hour activity.

Exploration matters:
You HAVE to explore to find enough resources. I thought this was a con at first, but between the regions, there are plenty of resources to find what you need.

Suggestions:
When a resource regen timer is done, have the full amount available. Or start regenerating 1 at a time until they have fully regenerated at the end of the existing timer.
Allow trees to grow back after X days. 180? 360? or something that makes sense.

Expand crafting recipes:
Aloe is already used for burns. Can just let people apply it without crafting. There are other examples of this in game.
Raw hide strips should be used as cordage in addition to only using tendons. A processed hide would create a lot of them.
Allow break down of big/strong/flexible branches into long/short sticks

Faster respawn of ground spawns like rocks and small sticks. You could eliminate the need for small stone nodes if this happens.

Don't put respawn timers on animals on the map - surprise me!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo I didn't read fully. I just picked the game because I was looking to explore and kill some time with a sense of achievement.

I didn't realize this was an early release until 20 something hours in. The few bugs I found were minor, the only thing I'm really disappointed in is that I can't progress any further without the full release.

Let me tell you why I like this game besides the fact that its beautiful. It's intuitive. Crafting is simple yet creative. You don't have to alt-tab to figure out how to make things work, the information you need to know is given to you without being simplified to a blunted scissors level.

The pace of movement, like physical run speed, is pleasing, not some dawdling drag where you see where you want to go and crave some auto-run feature. You can go vroom and not waste the 35 minutes of free gaming time in your busy adult life but the world is almost big enough to feel like it has a vast size to it.

The survival aspects, well I can't say much about this because I don't spend too much time in the genre but I like the thought put into sun burn and illness. I like having to think and account for my actions in a game and have solutions accessible.

The only downfall to this release is that I have to wait for more. Like a year of waiting for this makes me sad. I want more content now. Can you start on the sequels now? expansion projects? oh! Mods?
I want to play this game in a VAST jungle. You've nailed this is going to be a hit.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery immersive, challenging, and BIG! There's a lot to discover!

I played the demo a while ago, and was thrilled to see this came out EA. At first, I found early game difficult and unforgiving, so you eventually learn to play cautiously, but the game is flexible and gives you tools and methods to turn a bad situation around. That's part of the survival experience that makes it fun!

Some tips for new players: Palm juice collectors. Build a canoe early and build docks around to fast travel, Don't wait for the story prompt. Mapping is KEY, and you don't need to climb a tree to do it. Did I mention the canoe? Build one and upgrade it, it's almost like a mobile base that you can fast travel with.

For future improvements, I'd like to see farming and logs that can respawn. I'd also like to see copper ore being more abundant. I found the source for it, but it's on a long respawn, though I still have a couple little islands to explore that might have more. Also, I'd like to see the game be more forgiving of risk-taking, and have less of a "permanent" penalty of death that can only be healed by a limited item in the game. Also a better save system so you can prepare before a risky endeavor. Some sliders and game customisation would address this.

I also think gathering/crafting takes WAY too long, and fuel should last longer. It's part of the struggle, but it's too tedious.

I'm looking forward to seeing the rest of the islands come out!\t\t\t\t\t
