\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter having played for 60 hours, I am changing my former negative review to a positive one.
Fountain of Youth attempts to simulate a more realistic survival game, which I think it does successfully.

This game takes some getting used to, and its going to take most players time to get used to the game mechanics. This is not a game that players should attempt rushing through. You really have to look at the resources you need for your future crafts and plan accordingly.

One of the oddities of the game mechanics that players may or may not enjoy is the that nearly all resource gathering and crafting is menu driven. You won't be swinging your axe to chop down a tree. Instead, you will be taken to a menu which will tell you how long it will take for the action. Time in this game passes accordingly, and is an ever present struggle. For that reason, simply gathering everything you see is a poor strategy that will often leave you starving or dying of thirst.

I personally am not a fan of this mechanic. It is the least immersive way of experiencing a survival game. It feels like it should be a placeholder for future animations. But given that the leveling system is based on this time mechanic, I believe this is the direction the developers will continue forward on, for better or worse.

Graphically the game is average, and so is its music/sound. However, the maps are designed well and create an engaging player experience.
AI leaves a bit to be desired. Fighting in this game comes down to timing your attack so you don't take damage from creatures leaping on you.
Weather conditions and your overall character hunger/thirst/energy levels do play an important part in success, and are a contributing factor in whether you get wounded while engaging in nearly any activity. It also effects how likely you are to catch a cold, a stomach ache, fever, etc. There are a number of different conditions, all resolvable by creation various liniments and bandages. It does however ultimately come down to RNG, and can be very frustrating, especially in the early game.

One gripe I do have about the game is that the feeling of real progression is difficult to come by. Important resource nodes take a long time to replenish, so even though you may be able to craft better gear, the resource cost often means you'll still be crafting stone tools well into the late game. Similarly, the game leaves you feeling like you're just chasing your own tail most of the time. And though the game does get easier through your characters level progression and perks, you never truly feel like you've fully gotten out of the stone age. Larger ore nodes and/or faster replenishment time would help alleviate this issue, but it may be by design.

It is worth noting however that the game is far from complete. There may be more tiers that they will be implementing to get around the above issues. To the developers credit, I had no serious bugs during my 60 hours of playtime. The bugs I did experience were more of a slight inconvenience. The part of the game that is complete is well done and perfectly playable and enjoyable. It seems that the devs have more of a DLC style roadmap in mind, where they will release new islands through development. This is a smart idea, and I hope that players will not need to start completely over as they add to it.

Overall, the devs did a great job bringing everything together, and I recommend the game for anyone into this genre.

- You like Green Hell, The infected, 7 days to Die, The Forest, The Long dark and Sons of the Forest.
- You like hunting, gathering, crafting, base building.


Do Not Recommend * IF *
- You have a life outside gaming. The grind is brutal at times and you don't have the time to spend
- Want human enemies to combat
- You want to complete the game in a single run-through, or even two days.
- Hate snakes and spiders. and snakes and more snakes. ** DEVS: you could dial this back a tad, really.

There's no 3rd person view of your player, option.

The graphics are good. Not Sons of the Forest good. But better than The infected, Conan Exiles, and 7 days to die. Most games are better than that visual insult, The Long Dark.

Prices is fair for what you get at this time. Has a lot of potential for expansion and improvements.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is another case where I wish steam had something besides recommend/not recommend as options for reviews.

I fully understand that the game is in early access, and expected the issues that can come along with that. I did experience several crashes right after the intro and had to turn all of my graphic settings down to low to even get past it.

The game looks great, graphically and I was getting good frames after figuring out how to get through the crash-at-intro issue.

My issue with the game though is one of the most basic, core elements of any survival game - gathering resources. In every other survival game I've ever played, you actively chop wood, gather stones, mine iron, etc. But here, if you want to gather wood for instance, you just press E on a tree, choose how many sticks you want to gather, it tells you how much that will degrade your tool, then you press G to confirm, and it's done instantly.

This also means that you don't really interact with any of your tools, other than weapons. You just craft an axe/cutting tool/pick and it sits in your inventory giving you the ability to use the gather GUI for that resource.

Now, on some settings, even though the gather action is instant to the player, it will take in-game time. On the basic settings, it is just instant. This just feels to me like a cheap system all around.

I will probably come back and give this game another shot after it progresses a little further, but it just feels a little underbaked at the moment for me.


Edit after developer comment: I really don't understand that logic at all. Actively/interactively gathering resources (Green Hell, Grounded, Subnautica, Icarus, etc.) also burns time, energy, hunger & tool durability. This method just kind of seems like a way of doing it that doesn't involve creating animations, lol.

It's fine to make that design decision, but that logic being the reason just doesn't track.\t\t\t\t\t
