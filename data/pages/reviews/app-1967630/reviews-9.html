\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSummary: the game started as a hardcore realistic survival simulator focused on resource and time management, but then developers decided to target a different audience of casual players, so with each update all survival mechanics were dumbed down and discarded, until there was nothing left of the original concept.

Detailed review:
Demo version of Fountain of Youth, released back in 2022, had strong vibes of The Long Dark and Green Hell. Which was a good thing, because there aren't that many survival games focusing on balanced and realistic survival. Gathering food, water, treating wounds, hunting animals and struggling against hostile environment to survive until next day were your primary tasks. It was clear that original concept for the game was a realistic survival simulator where you had to carefully explore islands and plan your actions in advance, manage time and resources.

That's why people who played the demo reasonably expected the game to continue evolving in that direction. Unfortunately, developers didn't have a spine to stand up for their original vision. As soon as some kids from Minecraft/Roblox scene started to complain, because they had to actually spend their time surviving and could not just instantly win everything and build skyscrapers, developers took the game into a drastically different direction - towards a relaxed doing-nothing & fooling-around sandbox game.

Some recent changes the developers introduced compared to original Fountain of Youth from 2022:
  • Attack trigger distances for all animals were reduced by 500% or more (now it's less than 2 meters for some predators), so now you can freely walk everywhere without ever being attacked, unless you specifically look for encounters and literally bump into animals.
  • Inventory/carry capacity was pushed well beyond any realism (was ~20kg on hardest mode after all upgrades, now it's over 90kg), so managing your inventory and building carts to carry more items became irrelevant.
  • Crafting times for all blueprints were reduced to bare minimum.
  • Requirements for quantity of resources for crafting are also reduced in every update.
  • Amounts of resource spawns is increased in every update, while resource respawn times are decreased, so there is less and less incentive left exploring islands.
  • Fire starting time was decreased several times, so now you don't even need to use tinder (which was an important part of original gameplay).
  • Time to harvest resources is decreased in every update, so now all harvesting is almost instant (takes 1 minute, in the past it was from 5 to 10 minutes of in-game time).
  • By disassembling crafted objects you always get back 100% of resources, including from burned fires. Which means you never need fuel for fire, it's infinite. The whole concept of harvesting and stocking wood (integral part of original gameplay) is destroyed.
  • Original concept of exploring global map and travelling between distant islands was removed completely. Was replaced by instant-travel, where you just click on a button and next second you arrive to another island without any effort. Food dehydrator and smokehouse became meaningless for gameplay, since there is no feasible scenario any longer where you need any long-term storage food.
  • Mines with ore, which in the past required crafting pickaxe, now have pre-mined ore just lying in front of them, so you can pick up ore without any effort.
  • Cold temperature at night (required burning campfire), mosquito swamps (required malaria treatment), and many other features from demo were also removed completely.

This list can go on and on, every single aspect of gameplay was affected. The whole game was remade in a way so that you can successfully "survive" without any real effort and without ever being in danger. After all these changes, there is no survival simulator and no original FoY concept left to speak of.

Moreover, all of that fully applies even to hardest modes. While I and other people asked developers to differentiate between difficulty modes so that the game remains similar to demo at least on hardest settings, there was no response, and developers keep introducing sweeping changes across all difficulty modes. Every next patch note comes with row after row "more resources everywhere, less time to do everything, easier everything, etc." They call it "improving game balance", but somehow all that balance always goes only one way - by making everything x2, x5, or x10 easier, and discarding all original survival mechanics one by one.

Long story short, in hopes of attracting a wider audience of casual gamers who can't be bothered to actually survive and just constantly beg to make instant-craft, instant-harvest, auto-walk, auto-eat, infinite-resources, and basically instant/auto/infinite everything, developers completely butchered and destroyed their original vision of the project. The demo was brilliant and had a strong potential to become a cult classic survival simulator in its own right, but now all of that is gone.\t\t\t\t\t
