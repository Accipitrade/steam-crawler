\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 27 hours I can officially say I absolutely enjoy the game. I'm not the biggest survival gamer but I really like how they've done things here. Progression is not locked behind a grinding xp game loop that requires endless hours of doing the same thing over and over just so you can now craft a slightly better spear. You can craft the base, better, good spear all based on the resources you've found and resources themselves aren't locked behind an xp grind either. You want to use obsidian spears? OK, there's some upfront effort to put in but once you've located your obsidian node and have the stone pick you can craft your obsidian spears at the work bench. You can do that by day two if you prioritize it or you can wait, your call.

The story is there in the background and for what it is it's enjoyable enough. I actually forgot I even had a story line to follow for a while I was having so much fun discovering the island, new resources and being chased around by angry boars.

The best part of this game in my opinion is the way they handle building. One of the things that absolutely kills me in most games that have free building is the need to swing my hammer fifty times just to put two walls in. This game? Gather the supplies needed and the game automatically fast forwards time based on what you're doing. This isn't just for building it's for everything. I don't need to hit a tree fifteen times with my ax, I just say I want to cut down this tree and the game moves time forward. It really allows me to enjoy what I want to do vs. what I have to do.

The water/food system is good as well but could be better with some additional basic tweaks. At first it's brutal staying alive but once you figure out the tricks it's no real challenge. Until of course you fail to plan ahead and things snowball on you once Murphy and his damned law get involved then you find yourself starving and dying of dehydration while a jaguar paces around your dock as you slowly slip away into oblivion hiding underneath it ...\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is excellent. I've put thousands of hours into games like ARK, Green Hell, Conan: Exiles, and 7 Days to Die, and I like Survival: Fountain of Youth *at least* as much as I do any of those.

It's a full-fledged and already highly polished survival game. The regions implemented so far feel complete and robust. The mechanics implemented so far are mostly smooth and polished. There are a few minor bug fixes and adjustments that need doing, but based on what I've seen already I'm confident that these will get ironed out over the course of early access.

The devs have provided a roadmap for the next couple of regions and some notes on the remaining parts of the game beyond that, and I'm excited to see everything on it.

I don't expect to put as much raw time into S:FoY as I have other games like it, but my playtime in those games is driven in part by multiplayer playthroughs with friends. That said, I'm already planning at least one full, fresh-start playthrough for each new region released. This game doesn't - and won't - support multiplayer, but none of the Fallout series (that I care about anyway) supports multiplayer; multiplayer isn't right for those games and it isn't right for this one.

I'll be keeping a close eye on this game and Odinsoft's future releases.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is by far the best Survival Island game i've played.

And I've played alot of survival games on PS5,Xbox, And PC.

Now lets get into the cool stuff that you will love alot right away.

1. Controller support off the getgo... able to be played on Steam Link... on my Phone.
2. Carts... Yes carry all your items with you and don't think your in The Forest... wait wrong game... either way we got 2 of them so far.
3. Ships... yes full on exploration using ships... when you get it... its very fun to just drive around.
4. The game's early hard mode, goes away once you get to know what your doing... the difficulty was hard sure... but it was good... later updates might need to add more difficult enemies on newer islands... but for now its fine.
5. Stone,Flint,Obisidian,Copper, and plans for Iron... what? you want cool stuff. go get the resources... the wait is worth it when you finally get a copper spear and can kill all those annoying pesky uhh squirrls? waht are they again... to busy murdering these things.
6. Foundation base building similar to ARK,Forest, and alot of other games... at least its windows are interactable.
7. A Dock Piece... yes a Dock Buildable piece... why do games just not have any Prefabs nowadays? this prefab is one of my fav looking.
8. I Love the story.
9. I've never CTD Or Hard Crash, besides Lag Spikes here and there... game is running perfectly fine.
10. Im running out of ideas... i'll edit later and add more stuff.

The Bad.

1. FPS Drops here and there, nothing major... you won't notice it.... mostly when loading alot of stuff in... which you will be fine dealin with.
2. The Lack of more content... now this isn't a major thing... its just i want more. and its coming, so don't let that get you down.
3. The Walking/Movement... now it seems slow sure... but this was probably to slow the early game down... which is fine, but we need a faster movement later game.
4. Doesn't seem to be any Stone Building/Brick/Clay building parts, could be a future update.
5. Tools break fast... idk if its me... or i'm just using them to much.
6. Figuring out Hides... is a nightmare... and needs improved... abit more... the Jackel hide as far as i can tell does nothing... the only way to get hides is from Boar, and Tagues.
6. It doesn't seem to rain on other islands very much.
7. Custom settings... for resource respawns... world day/night cycle... monster amount counts... stuff like this is missing and should be a feature later on... for Custom Difficulty.
8. 3 Boats... at the time of writing this and playing it... I Was hoping more. then again still early access.
9. Mapping is kindof weird... since i can't auto map while walking? like i've seen this area... just map it already? do it in a more smaller reveal area... if you want it that way... but when the map says NOTHING SEEn... i'm confused.... since i remember the area... explorers would just remember the stuff... not map everything... most of the time... ish.
10. nothing really else... i can pull out of my head... game is fine, it just needs more updates.

Overall worth the money, good devs, needs more content, fun to screw around with.\t\t\t\t\t

I LOVE this so far. I absolutely love Ancient History and I love things like Mayan and Egyptian ruins = THIS TICKS THAT BOX!!!

The exploration is fun, totally at your pace. This isn't a hardcore game. You're supposed to, however, SURVIVE. Go kinda slow and enjoy the experience. Try your ass off to stay alive. I haven't "died" yet as of this review.

This is absolutely brilliant and a wonderful survival crafting game so far and it's only going to get way better. I really can't recc it enough.

If you like the idea of finding cool Mayan-like ruins and artifacts with a cool story and great mechanics this games for you. I loved this so much that it took me this long to look at other people's opinions because I didn't want it to spoil the fun I've truly having.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tholymoly it has been some time since i played a game with the amount of garbage and clunkyness this one here has.

The graphics are quite good so far, but thats it.

The gameplay and movement mechanics are complete garbage.
The way they put a "simulation-layer" behind every basic sort of action like "grab a hanging coconut from a palm taking 40 minutes time + ~40% chance to injure yourself while doing it --> the wound you recieve as a result is not just a simple HP-drain but a status-ailment" lets you limbo in micro-management loops way to often.

For example: - difficulty setting: Hard

Your Character starts with 100 points which slowly drain during a day.
Hunting a whole chicken --> harvesting its meat --> ignite a fire on your campfire --> cook the meat --> eat the meat (all of it with a small to mediocre chance to suffer a wound) takes around 2-3 hourse meanwhile where your hunger depletes by a far greater value then this could balance out.

Even without that you would need 11 chicken or 35 coconuts (whole! and not the "green" ones, as they only quench your thirst!) to eat to go through the day.

What happens when you get injured while crafting/cooking/harvesting or else?? --> you need to mitigate the effect with another loop of "gather leafes to make a bandage (which could take you hours as the tend to appear extremely scarce) --> craft a firestarting tool, ignite a fire (which will fail quite often --> the amount of time (60 minutes!) is still spent!), craft a bandage --> apply the bandage...then notice that the bandage only heals 1-2 stacks of wound --> craft another bandage (takes 30 minutes and you need a burning fire to do so).

Meanwhile you beginn to become exhausted.
You will need to build a sleeping bag for which of course you need other materials.
The crafting takes 45 minutes, with a 14% chance to hurt yourself.
You cannot sleep, when you are hurt, hungry or thursty.
You cannot craft when you are sleepy.
You cannot make food, for which will need "crafting" when you are sleepy.

Everything in this game is so damn boring and pointless beyond the point of stupidity and lack of logic.

Why ffs. can't you take a damn stone and throw it up to a palm to gain a coconut?

I hope steam lets me refund this even with allmost 3 hours spent ingame.\t\t\t\t\t
