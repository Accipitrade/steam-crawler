\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI stumbled across this game while watching a youtuber's stream before the early access release, and thought it looked interesting. It is interesting! I'm glad I bought it, and have really enjoyed my time playing through the two regions available so far. I've got a weakness for survival games, but I find many seem to be either too gruelling, too online multiplayer, too psych horror, or too bland. This game is tough but manageable, repetitive but dynamic, and has a story and skill-tree mechanics that really appeal to me as a 'singleplayer, story-driven game player' kind of person. Resources are scarce, the environment is a challenge, and the slightest mistake can and will attempt to kill you. Once you get your rhythm going and are surviving without much difficulty, you'll progress to the next region and be suitably punished for your hubris, ending up at the bottom of the food chain once again and needing to learn quickly before you're overwhelmed. 'Fainting' is a neat mechanic, and so is the panacea living water, and I've enjoyed the huge variation in necessary materials, tools, and buildings required to progress.

For a small studio in early access, this game is already a great achievement in my opinion, and I'm excited to see where the game goes as it develops. I didn't experience any bugs, only some very light wonkiness in the in-game text, and sometimes the voice acting - honestly all completely understandable and didn't affect gameplay at all. The only thing that did affect me was I haven't seen more than 3 shards of flint for the entire game across the two regions, meaning I'm a bit stuck when it comes to firestarter progress, but otherwise it's been an overall engrossing, vibrant, strategic little game. I hope the developers continue to show it their love and hard work, because if you like survival/crafting games, this is definitely one to watch and support.\t\t\t\t\t

All in all I’m extremely excited to play all the content, finds all the treasures and books.\t\t\t\t\t
