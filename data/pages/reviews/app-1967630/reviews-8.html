\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm not big on writing reviews, but I feel compelled to say that this is the most addictive survival/crafting game I've played in a long while. While many in this genre have no plot, this game does have a light and unobtrusive plot that lends purpose to everything you do and makes exploration more enjoyable. The health mechanic is unique, and will make you think seriously before rushing into any situation. Death does not mean a simple respawn. The skill tree progression makes sense and is appropriate to how new quests and areas open up, although it's hard to resist the urge to get every ability at the outset.

Be advised that the game is not yet complete, although 80 hours in I've just hit the end of the plot line as it exists so far. That does not stop me from exploring and crafting, however, and there are probably 100 or more hours of that in the regions I've opened up (I explore EVERYTHING) already.

One thing I'd like to see is the ability to place building pieces even where they intersect with other physical objects. For instance, if I build a platform over an open gap, but the last piece intersects with the ground, I can't place it. It would be nice if it would allow you to place it and just truncate it where it terminates into the ground the way Icarus does. Then I'd at least bridge the gap, even if I couldn't place walls on the platform afterward.

All in all, this is a buy. You're gong to love it!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI recommend this game to anyone that wants to be wowed with a fantastically immersive, intriguing, highly original content environment, story line, and a sandbox environment letting you progress through the story at your own pace, or just mull around and try not to perish. Following the story line does help you discover new ways to overcome challenges that are much more difficult to overcome in early stages, but as you grow and learn new skills, get new blueprints for better gear, build them, you find you can ease of the intensity without a constant grind. But you will not get there easy, and sometimes you have to be imaginative and creative and think about how to use newly learned recipes in a new place to overcome issues you face in the new place. There are puzzles to solve, items to find, materials you can only find a few times and then those have a limited lifetime, so learning alternatives will help you adapt. Sure, there are some bugs here and there, it is early access at the time I write this, but for the money spent, I already feel I derived a ton of value from the game, with moments of terror, thrill, accomplishment, fun, and awe were worth it! I have no history with the game, did not even get the demo, just came across it a few weeks earlier, tried it out, and then proceeded to spend over a hundred hours playing it, longing for it when I slept, thinking about it while working, just unable to not play when I faced a particular unsolved challenge. I even made up my own goals long after finishing the game, from building, to challenges in making one of every possible thing, like all armor sets, or maxing out food drying skill, figuring out which armor was best in which situations, learning which medicines would heal in the right way and how many I needed on hand when I adventure out, climbing structures I bypassed early game to find treats laying about I would never have found, swimming through underwater rocks, mapping other non-storyline critical areas of a map, and so much more. The last thing, I've been wanting to be on a beach for a long time, and this is the closest way for me to feel like I've really been there. Try it, you won't regret it if you like the things I mentioned above and get a kick out of playing a game solo, no one else is needed for me to really get the best from the game and survive on my own wits and work.\t\t\t\t\t
