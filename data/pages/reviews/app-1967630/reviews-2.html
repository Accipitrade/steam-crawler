\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought this game in mid-July '23

At this time, I experienced no bugs.. which is pretty amazing for EA. I daresay the smoothest EA I've experienced.

Solid survival game. If survival isn't your thing, then why are you even reading this?
Good concept, good story line (thus far), and with the Unity Engine being used, great graphics.
Dev's are in constant communication and updating often, so its not a "release EA and ditch it for the money grab" kind of thing.

Sure, there is one sticking point for survival genre players - gathering. In this game, you click most objects and there is a quantity to gather.. choose your quantity, click, and its a time/equipment wear penalty, not a time-sink. This makes it quicker, sure.. and its a little "immersion distrubing" but its nothing that A) you don't get used to and B) you really end up not minding because there is a certain amount of resource management at play here. If you had to "chop every stick" you would never get anything accomplished.. it would take you so much time, the days would have to -literally- be in Days (not about 5 seconds a minute like it is), then certain other aspects of the game would have to be re-worked for that kind of time frame.. Basically, you fire this up and when you experience it, your thought is "well, thats easy" but after region 2 it dawns on you why they did it this way.

Other than that, the craftable items are plentiful (more than I thought there would be if one stuck to the 16th Century bit.. which they don't Completely stick to - solar water distiller) but I get it.. Lots of travel time in the boat you make. Lots to explore..

There are a couple things I dislike, personally..

Fast travel being one. Once you've discovered a region, you Cannot Sail your boat to it anymore. You have to fast travel. The fast travel system should be optional, not forced.

Details. You're attracted to the smoke from a campfire.. Great! You get there, and the person that supposedly started the fire is a Skeleton. Fire would have been long gone. Make it a dead body, not a skeleton... also makes the player realize that they might not be "that far behind" who they're chasing.

2H weapons and Torches. What happens to your torch when you whip out a 2H weapon? Do you stick it in your pocket? Not likely. It's still working, so it has to go somewhere? It should drop to the ground if you have to fight in that situation. It would still work, and you have to pick it back up..

Crafting materials are all over the place. From one thing costing "next to nothing" to other things costing "wow.. really?" But all that is probably going to be ironed out during EA, so I'm not too worried about it.

Piles of scrap - system needs worked on. You can't tell me this Huge Pile of stuff is going to give me a couple of sticks... c'mon...

All in all, I really REALLY like the direction this game is going, and how polished it is for being released into EA mere months ago.

I have no problem recommending this game to gamers who enjoy the survival genre..\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game sits comfortably between The Long Dark and Green Hell with a couple unique twists.

It is NOT your usual "punch a tree get wood" survival, it is very much your "plan your time and resources" survival.
People complain that the menu based harvesting and crafting system is not immersive, why can't you just swing your pick at a rock and get some stone. This is simply not that kind of game (there is plenty of those around) and as a TLD player it makes me super happy.

Difficulty spikes between the different modes is significant and I strongly recommend starting at the lowest one, especially if you're new to management based survival.

It is a lot of fun.\t\t\t\t\t
