\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am a relative newcomer to survival games, but I've enjoyed the very well-developed "The Long Dark" and decided to give this one a try for the new survival elements to master and for the opportunity to play with its building mechanics. My only real gripe so far is that in the time I've been playing, the game has crashed 5 times over the space of about 24 hours of play. Fortunately, the game has a very generous autosave mechanism, so I've never found myself having significantly lost an enormous amount of carefully gathered resources. The faint and respawn mechanism is also very generous to the player; all your items carried remain at the spot where you fainted, in a neat little bag, ready to be picked up again.

The game has several different levels of difficulty, appealing to a broad range of players who can customise their survival experience to their satisfaction. Newly discovered resources unlock new blueprints, and over time you can build an interesting range of tool benches to craft everything from medicine and food to weapons, tools and buildings. I like that the appearance and function of your tools do evoke the absolute primitivism you're stepping back into. You definitely feel like a caveman when you're running around wielding a stone axe!

The flora and fauna are very diverse and add to the authenticity of the Bahamas setting. It looks, sounds, and feels just like a tropical island where the player is the unlucky castaway. The Bird Island starting point has quite mildly dangerous wildlife, but when you progress out into the next regions, there's a definite step up in what you encounter. I appreciated the warning about scorpions, spiders and snakes since I'm quite arachnophobic, but the spiders didn't detract from my enjoyment of the game in any way. I look forward to the future updates and seeing how the game develops. I hope the developers read this review, because their hard work is very much appreciated!\t\t\t\t\t
