\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHeilwald Loophole is a fairly exceptional gem. While billed as a stealth psychological horror, and excelling at horror when it wants to, it is overall a more casual and interesting atmospheric walkabout than it presents. Wielding the PSX aesthetic very well, every second inside the Heilwald Klinikum is unnerving thanks to excellent art direction.

The main conceit, that you cannot die, is used to good effect. "Deaths" are used to open up alternative and side paths, and a large portion of the game (and key component to 100% completion) is being curious and clever with how you end any given situation. While not a time loop per se, you are able to navigate to areas previously visited at will and begin most of them 'from the beginning', as if you were never there before.

At four hours for a first playthrough, it's well paced, unique, and with such strong direction that it's worth its price and its place on the shelf of both horror fans and those more casual and exploratory. A must buy if you can spare the ten-spot.

The following is what I dislike and is spoilered since it goes further in depth about several game specifics, though it tries not to actually spoil anything. Note that the below doesn't invalidate what I've said above and I'd purchase the game again despite my personal misgivings.
The ending was underwhelming to me. While providing excellent atmosphere, it leaves threads completely loose that I'd rather be tied up or at least hinted at. Additionally, I didn't like the only true "puzzle" as much as I wanted to. Asking you to judge the story figures you've seen so far based on how they've broken the hippocratic oath, the answers are either too obvious to really allow discussion, or vague enough that it is difficult to rebound if you inevitably get any wrong, since you are given no guidance on a failure. Being able to review the knowledge documents you've picked up may or may not have helped, but it would have made me feel I had more info at my disposal for the task. Also, a rather two-dimensional chef is included among them; it makes little sense and makes it harder to find his correct answer, since the average person would not consider him bound by such a medical oath. Also why even give me the option to kill the garbage collector!? They're the nicest guy!!\t\t\t\t\t

Its dirty, patients are neglected, the staff is openly hostile against you, going so far as mistreating you, and someting supernatural seems to go on, because... you cant really die.
You have to find a way out of this hellhole and maybe finding out which powers are at work here.
The story is a classic indie-horror game mystery, even though it explains not enough in my opinion to be satisfying. Still, if you want to buy this game for the story and the setting alone, I think you will not be disapointed.
By the way, the game isnt really voice-acted with the exception of one character and maybe some throw-away lines. Most NPCs speak in a kind of garbeld voice, similair to Simlish, while the subtitles convey what they are saying. I personally like this style of voice-acting, because it lets actual dialog stand out even more, but I think its a matter of taste.


Graphics
The whole games goes for a retro PS1 aesthetic. While it is of course debatable if this looks good, it has the great effect of obscuring details, adding to the mystery.
Still, most of the enviorments and models are clearly crafted with love. All important characters look distinct, down to the silhouette, and most important objects are distinct from the rest of the scene, not at least because they have this shine to it.
Bottomline is, I like the style. It adds to the mystery but isnt needlessly confusing.

Gameplay
As I said in the beginning, this game is a Adventure Game. Specifically a 3D one from the ego-perspective. So most of the time you will solve puzzles to procress, but the game has also stealth parts and chases to spice the whole thing up.
