\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst things first: I only did a first impression Lets Play on this because I realized that this is not the type of game for me (I am abysmal at run & hide-games), so I can’t say much about the entire game & plot but I played for an hour, which should suffice for some feedback.

The gameplay is smooth, I think especially for running & hiding, the movement is fast, personally, I found that running usually works best but there’s also a few parts where hiding is required. This part was surprisingly fun for me since I usually hate it.

The enemy design was my favourite part. They are both hilarious and terrifying, I loved the pixel weirdness and audio cues, the three enemies I encountered were a hoot, 5/5 can recommend.

The puzzles are mostly easy, although I personally thought that the first chapter was a bit hard to crack since the loop made it hard to try things out without getting a bit annoyed of the repetition.

Which brings me to my main criticism: I have nothing against the loop mechanic per se but there’s so many parts of the game and the pretty slow dialog that you can’t skip that it gets repetitive and ultimately a bit tedious since you HAVE to wait for the characters to finish jabbering before you can make a run for it. This gets old once you’ve heard the same dialog three times in a row, it’s like grandpa’s jokes at a family gathering.
There’s also certain twists that are connected to the loop that seem like a good idea at first but then only end up costing time because you have to move through them over and over again if you failed something. Yes, there’s no death in this game but the alternative plays pretty much like death, so it’s not necessarily an improvement. The main character doesn’t even remember former loops which is kind of weird because that’s not how Groundhog Day works, right?

Anyways, I think that for gamers who enjoy run & hide, fun enemies, and who don’t mind repeating some game steps, this can be a really cool experience. For anyone who has no patience for slow-talkers who say the same thing over and over again: run … and hide, lol.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA brilliantly weird puzzle/stealth horror game with a lot of meat on its bones for the short time you have it for. While the looping horror gameplay sounds like it'll be some boring PT clone, this game takes the concept in an entirely different angle, promoting you to try different strategies and techniques around the same areas and hostilities.

Going through each unique well-designed area was a lot of fun, each manned by a strange (and often hostile) overseer. Sound design is perfect with the music building very well combined with the wholly unique 'stalking' noises each threat would make. Visually the game's style is brilliant and a proper ode to old school graphics while still feeling fresh in the superb enemy and NPC designs. Some of the recorded dialogue as frenzied enemies chase you down has unsettled me in a way few other games have.

There isn't much to complain about here: just a few clipping/animation errors in the Library/towards the end, and the final encounter/area not feeling as fleshed out or complex as its predecessors. Other than that however, you can't go wrong with this brilliantly passionate and eerie title.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUse bizarre Timeloop shenanigans and Lynchian logic to enact karmic justice in a once prestigious medical establishment that has degenerated into a corrupt and horrific institution that actively harms the people its supposed to serve and care for.

This game is wide rather than long and has a tone of side and optional content by design. To beat the game just rushing through is easy but with the way the game is designed it encourages you to loop back to earlier areas over and over again to discover new areas, notes, items and even side quests and secrets. Certain events can even play out a bit differently depending on your approach each time. You can even continue the game after beating it to double back and try to get 100% which I still haven't gotten because some of the routes are less than obvious and more obscure than you would think. I like that though, I like the process of figuring out how deep I can go and coming up with "OK what happens if I do this or that?" its cool.\t\t\t\t\t
