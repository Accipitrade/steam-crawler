\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAn engaging little story and game.
Has great atmosphere, thanks to the graphics based on the PSX era.
Fantastic sound design and music help complete its style.

In terms of level of horror. It isn't extreme. Full disclosure for those who hate jumpscares, there are a few that can give a light startle, never anything to make you actually jump out your seat. Two are even played for laughs, we'll talk about that in a moment.

It does what any good horror game does and leaves you feeling unnerved and cautious as you explore the hospital searching for a way out and learning about the place and its staff along the way.

As mentioned earlier there are moments of humour sprinkled in that help lighten the atmosphere a bit. It doesn't take itself serious enough to not warrant a couple of laughs along the way. Humour is often a good addition to horror when done correctly as it lets you deescalate and let you catch a break from the tension before it builds again.

The main loop of the game is... well... looping.
Our protagonist can loop back to areas before they did anything in them, letting you play out scenarios again and see different outcomes. They can either loop from dying/being caught or travelling though holes in some areas.

The game encourages you to explore all outcomes in each area. You will learn more, unlock new areas and new interactions. Not everyone you encounter is an enemy, some characters can offer advice or help in return for something. Both friendlies and enemy alike are all colourful/quirky/downright disturbing. You'll find times where you can both be scared/creeped out and amused by those that chase you.

As usual with horror games there are puzzles or at least moments that require you to think on what to do. How to outsmart or avoid a pursuer or obstacle in your way.

A playthrough will take between 3-5 hours depending on your speed. Games don't all have to be 50-300 hours long to enjoy them.

Admittedly the ending felt a little lackluster, you'll make sense of it, it won't answer all your questions though.
It still makes my teeth tingle though. . _ .

If it intrigues you, take your time, explore, soak up the atmosphere and enjoy your stay.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love horror games with some gameplay heft to them. Not just running and hiding. There’s running and hiding in The Heilwald Loophole too. But instead of being the be-all-end-all, it’s one facet of a larger puzzle. And it is very much a puzzle game, in first-person. One where you visit different locations in a bizarre clinic -- the clinic where no one dies -- avoiding or confronting a staff that feels like they’ve been ripped out of Theme Hospital, if Theme Hospital was situated in Silent Hill.

Failing means a redo -- retaining whatever important stuff you managed to accumulate, like keys and information. It all fits together; the whole map serves as a web of locations you have to visit. Each one with different hidden outcomes leading to new places or puzzle pieces. But you need to figure out how to uncover them -- no matter how uncomfortable it may seem. Often it’s about going head-first into something.

Either way, it’s a great little gimmick that encourages exploration of all the weird nooks and crannies.

I love how off-center the whole experience feels. It has this weird pacing to it. The wavy, low-res textures and convulsing pixels add a sort of sea-sickness. It has a good mix of horror clichés too. Yet it feels distinct with loads of idiosyncrasies and bizarre details.

Recommended, if you like inventive and spooky PSX-style horror games.

Review
There is a huge influx of short, ps1 visual styled “horror” esque games that tend to be walking simulators or have some survival horror elements. I don’t mind, they are often short, unique experiences and never get stale for me as most have some unique twist in them. I often get bored with longer games and compared to the market this one is one of the longer ones, clocking in at around 3 hours for me.

Gameplay
While its more or less a walking simulator as you can interact with items, but the game never presents you really riddles, with one exception. If you played outlast, this one is quite familiar except that you NEVER know what comes next. Seemingly nice people who talk with you normally suddenly chase you or dislike it when you walk away from them and vice versa.

The gameplay mechanic of the loop is basically a system that wants you to experience various “routes” in the game that unlock either new lore, key cards or directly new areas. If you unlocked a key card, you can jump back to a previous sequence which restarts from the beginning therefore presenting a new path to go.
There are some side quests or alterations of outcomes that end technically the same but are different due to actions you took, albeit there aren’t that many.

Most levels are designed in this way: You meet directly or later a person that either talks to you or directly chases you, chases you after speaking or is always friendly. There are multiple outcomes, getting catched is sometimes even necessary to progress the story therefore its neat that they added an achievement for each area, so you know that you finished the task.

As some paths can be tricky and you aren’t sure which trigger will do what, there is a guide and most levels have only 2-3 ways it can end.

The game doesn’t have real complicated puzzles except if you count using a crowbar to destroy planks or using anaesthetics to sedate someone chasing you. There is a single really annoying puzzle at the end of the game, where you have to set each person a number. Each number represents a personality trait which can only be found in another room. Those are obviously quite cryptic. A lot of the people have traits that can easily be split between 1-2 traits, or you have no clue at all which ended up in me spending the most time on that puzzle, longer than for multiple levels which can be incredibly frustrating. I really like despise overly complicated puzzles like that.

Also I had a certain bug at the end that didn’t let me proceed so I had to restart the WHOLE campaign.

Story
From what I’ve got you are either a patient or an intruder who experiences the deterioration of the Heilwald clinic. They received a new head doctor who changed a lot of things that ended up in slowly destroying the established system inside the ward. Patients and Staff alike rotted away physically and mentally, as the title suggests not only the gameplay mechanic is a loophole but also can be defined some kind of inadequacy in a certain set of rules.

But the game does not really give you much to speculate with as the lore is mostly behind notes and some certain lines at the end of the game. The rest is more about experiencing a really freakish mental hospital. If you expect much more you will be disappointed.

Sound
The chasing sequences and all horror aspects are mostly due to the phenomenal soundtrack and sound effects. People have all different walking sound effects, and the game works mostly with gross sounding or loud noises to convey horror and I really liked it.

People usually talk gibberish, stuff you can see in Banjo Kazooie or Paratopic. Unless if you run away from them or during special triggers they talk in a very distorted English or German. Which I thought was odd, that some had German and other had English catchphrases. Taking one solely would make more sense.

Graphics
Either you love the style, or you don’t. Most games have some sort of setting where you can at least tweak the amount of pixelation or colour depth, this one doesn’t have it so if you don’t like the aesthetic, you won’t enjoy the game. I really liked it especially the designs of the various NPC´s. Especially the other patients look outright nightmarish.

Conclusion/TL;DR
If you are into PS1 styles horror games this one is a must. Especially the first few levels are amazing if you like horror that not only makes you not able to guess what will happen next. Albeit the story is too cryptic for me and there was that one single puzzle at the end that frustrated me, the amazing soundtrack with the sound effects of the “monsters” who chase you make for a memorable experience.

Good horror game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI found this game as a demo in itch.io back in 2021. Now, after experiencing the full release of the game, it was trippy, comical and unnerving. "Every action, every decision merely opens up a new pathway to venture down, leading you deeper into the Heilwald Klinikum", this quote delivers what it was intended for you to experience. The adding of the loop mechanism, encourages you to figure how move on to discover new places. Nevertheless, they were moment where you are left to wonder, what shall I do next? To die or to revisit? Take your time, explore and do not rush.
