This is a re-review

I waited until I had 1,000 hours to give this game a review. When I did that, roughly 2 years ago, I gave it a personal 10/10. Now with Witch Queen finished, Lightfall's release, and another 1,000 hours down, I'm re-reviewing Destiny 2.

Start with the Bad
- Destiny 2 has the worst new-player experience of any game similar to it. Easily. I have personally tried to get many of my friends into my favorite game and very few of them stick around. You know why? None of them have any clue what to do. The tutorial you are given is the equivalent of 6 or 7 pop-ups that show you where things are within D2's many, many confusing menus. If you are brand new at Destiny, it is essential to have a friend or two to help you out.
- There is no free starter campaign, or any starting campaign at all. Well, there is, but it's more just a quest. Then if you're interested enough in that poor mess to keep digging deeper, half of the story so far has been removed from the game. You quite literally have to either read or watch a video explaining everything. This is not good for new players.
- Seasonal model is tired. You know how this is if you've played any game that's been around for more than a couple years. However I will say Bungie has already addressed this and has been making pretty good progress shaking this aspect of the gameplay loop up.
- Filler and the nature of a live service game. There are things you can't escape when making a game like Destiny. Sometimes you're going to drop the ball on a season, make weird narrative choices in favor of better gameplay loop or layout, or even have to revert a few updates. It goes without saying that the game isn't perfect by any means. But its not bad.
- The community on Twitter and Reddit is some of the most toxic garbage I have ever had the misfortune of stumbling upon. Do yourself a favor and just form your own opinions as you'll have a much better time if you do. Such a hate-train of a "fan" base claiming that they love the game lmao.

On to the Good
- Best feeling gun-play in the industry. Insane power fantasy, unique weapon types and exotics that truly feel otherworldly. This game makes you feel like the ultimate badass magic space-wizard/marine/scout or what-have-you. Weapons vary from hand cannons that poison the opponent over time, to a "fusion rifle" made from the bones of a slain dragon, spewing fire left and right. Rockets that turn into more rockets or a black hole rail-gun, the possibilities truly feel endless.
- And you look good while doing it. Bungie has some of the best texture and model artists in the game these days and it always shows in the detail in the weapons, armor, and destinations. Take on the space-cowboy look or go for more of a world-ending-paracausal-being-hell-bent-on-humanity's-destruction type of deal. The destinations- vast sky boxes that look like they belong in a museum tied together with equally beautiful maps filled with secrets and easter eggs for all of the curious players to go wild hunting for. Every year they deliver some of the best patrol areas I've played around in, and a sandbox built on experimentation. With the inclusion of a grapple ability with Lightfall, the floodgates have been opened.
- Abilities and buildcrafting are the first step into the endgame. Once you have this complicated, yet recently streamlined aspect of the game figured out, you can pummel enemies faster and with more flash than you ever knew possible. Solar - fire, Arc - electricity, Void - gravity(?), Stasis - ice, and Strand - rope(?) are the powers you have at your disposal. Weapons that synergize with builds to make them more deadly add a new layer of depth to the customization. Play how you want - meta or not you'll be kicking major ass.
- A universe, characters, and lore on the level of Star Wars, Lord of the Rings, or Elder Scrolls. If you like to play a game for its story, Destiny isn't always super clear. However, if you like being immersed? This is the game for you. Digging deep in the lore to gain further context to this massive story being told is the best thing I ever did playing this game. A greater appreciation for the characters, writing, secrets and all can be gained from a little bit of learning.

Conclusion
In truth there are so many reasons that I, specifically, love Destiny 2. Everyone plays for different reasons. I love raids. Dungeons. The engame PvE. That is my bread and butter but maybe you'll find something that you love to do. Exploring destinations for the fun of it! Gambit! Dares of Eternity and the zany obstacle course! Maybe you're crazy and want to try your hand at the ever-shifting metascape that is the Crucible (PvP). The game is so huge and full of content that there really is an endless amount of stuff to do. I fell in love with this game and its universe when I was still in middle school. Almost ten years down the line now and it really has been such a wonderful journey that I still look forward to every day. My friends and I have grown closer through this game. The memories of day one raids, or getting absolutely crushed by my wife in the Crucible (she's cracked). This game is not flawless, not by a long shot, and sometimes it drives me insane. But nothing scratches that looter-shooter, sci-fi-fantasy, cosmically scaled itch like Destiny 2. If you're willing to put up with it - give it a try.

Final Score - 10/10\t\t\t\t\t
