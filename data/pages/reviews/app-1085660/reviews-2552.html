The kind that brings you flowers the next day after giving you a black eye and busted lip, then promises they'll change and that they could never be with anyone else, despite smelling like the fry oil of the fast food chain your best friend works at. Thankfully, I was smart enough to purchase this game before the move to free to play, making my voice near silent because no money is necessary, arguably, to play this game. Had they not made this move, my dissatisfaction would still have some impact, despite Bungie being stone deaf.

Want to play a game that gives you a 5 level xp boost at the start of the week that makes you play like a god? But then after that, the enemies, oddly enough, become near bulletproof and incredibly challenging? This is the game for you.

Want a game that is more of a digital shopping experience, like a second life, but as a space saga where the sparrows don't shoot and the ships are useless? You found a winner.

Want to spend hours reading lore? Go buy a good book. Or read the lore for this game because since day one this thing hasn't gotten it right.

Yeah, I'm bitter. I'm bitter because I'm gullible enough to believe that people can and do listen. That they can and do care about the opinions and suggestions of their fans. That telling stories that challenge the way we view the world is important. And if told well enough, people will pay for that instead of meaningless trinkets that only hold the attention of a parrot, at least until the following update anyways. But I guess it's all part of growing up in the big city.
Finally, I'd say I'm a fool, but that would mean I'd have keep playing.


Update 6-9-20

So now they're removing content that people paid for to play. Fantastic. And I can't say that I'm surprised at the idea of wielding Darkness in this game. The writing was on the wall for this feature and should have been dropped (added) a lot sooner.

Update 11/21/21

This is the worst psychologically manipulative game. Do not play this game if team work is something you appreciate. This game prides itself on being in a constant state of competition. If you spend all your effort working to defeat enemies, fellow "Teammates" can come in a steal all the work you've put in, and get rewarded for it too. This doesn't require skill. It's just petty.

That's part of the trick, it baits people with a false sense of ability in the open areas as solo players and then literally turns everything up to 50, basically making everything that you're working towards useless. You're never, ever satisfied. The meta constantly changes. There is no such thing as power in this game.

There are still no vehicles.

If you don't feel good playing this game, the store can give you the bump you're looking for.
It is not a free to play game. The semantics of this really needs to be examined much more closely. The phrase "Free to Play," needs to be very restrictive in it's use. You cannot access any of the important content unless you pay. That is not free to play.
