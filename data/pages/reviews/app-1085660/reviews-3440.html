\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the most soulless, quickly-hashed-together AAA games I've ever played.

Although the particle effects, music, and environments carry an extreme sense of exploration, wonder, and effort, practically every other department - writing, voice acting, activities to engage in, enemy variation, weapon variation, and even sometimes soundwork is abhorrent.

Bungie doesn't care about you. If they did, they would've fixed up the game (as promised!) when the Activision split came.

They didn't. Eververse is still there. No major changes to it. No major changes to the game, either... almost as if Activision was a scapegoat for Bungie's halfhearted attempts at creating a sequel.

The game is completely bankrupt and devoid of everything that could enlist it into the ranks of normal games. Guns consistently feel the same, the artificial difficulty and drops rob the game of any strategem or fun, and most - if not, all - activities can be completed with a heart BPM of 120.

Imagine going to a liquor store. You pay $35 for a bottle of whiskey.

Instead of being handed the bottle, you are told to report back each week to the same liquor store for one shot, over the course of the next three months. If you don't report back each week, you don't get that weeks shot, and must wait until next weeks.

That's Destiny. I paid $35 for something I hit the content ceiling for in 4 hours, and am expected to come back every few weeks to play more of it.

The incessant attempt to make me play player-versus-player gamemodes with people from entirely separate continents is also extremely sad. You're above that, Bungie. This is an FPS, not a moba. Ping actually matters, especially when every gun is hitscan.

Just release Halo already\t\t\t\t\t
