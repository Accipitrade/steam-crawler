The game can be imbalanced as all hell, like getting a bunch of down syndrome toddlers to build a lego set. But the music, the atmosphere, the story, its incredible.

Want to enjoy the game? don't play PvP, at least not a lot of it. PvP can be great, but is always unbalanced in one way or another. Because there are just so many factors at play, something will always be busted and meta.

The class rivalry is like none other I've seen in a video game. Titans have their crayons and need to punch everything. Warlocks have their floaty space magic and magnetism to falling off cliffs. Hunters have absolutely FRABJOUS capes(Its a thing) and crippling depression. RIP Cayde. My class advice? Become the majority, Hunter Master Race. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's not friendly to new players at all, and Bungie changes the rules after the fact, finds you in violation, and then will ban you because apparently people if you're a guy helping out your 60 year old father score some decent loot so he can go do patrols with nice shiny things (which wasn't an issue until the release of Witch Queen) and decides your the guilty party while they smugly get to read everyone's letters begging them for unban to his or her account, there is no recourse for you. But, they're never going to give hardly anyone one because that would deprive them of more money. It's a pretty game, few shooters are as equally satisfying. The PvP is a joke, and if you're not in the know, the learning curve is aggressively difficult. It's no where near D1 or what that used to be, and it wouldn't be so sad, if they weren't trying to promote themselves as if they're a game worthy of an MLG team in any meaningful way. They can't seem to get CBMM or SBMM right for the appropriate events, Trials is an absolute sweatfest, and anytime someone finds a decent armor combination, the ban hammer gets dropped because we can't have players with lives getting anything in any reasonable amount of time, and not to mention, they call this game a "looter shooter" but constantly blue-ball you on loot all the time. You can run solo lost sectors for days and have what, 50 enhancement cores, a vault full of trash blues and maybe a few exotics with trash rolls. Xur's only roll is to dangle in your face with what could be and instead, the game, rarely if ever, seems to give it to you. And even if you spend all the hours watching streamers, practicing with clanmates, working hard to get a good K/D, focus on collecting a vast array of God rolls, getting high rolled stat armor, and carefully analyzing your armor mods, you can still get smoked in PvP by the latest broken meta, or shotgun apes with aimbots, or streamers playing other people's accounts. So, rather than fix the glaring issues, Bungie decides to make everyone wait in the rain while they build an outhouse for lack of a better expression. Typical money-grubbing corporation gone free-to-play to score the big bucks, and constantly dunking on the die-hard fans.
>muh protect the integrity of the game experience for players that play the game organically
Here Bungie cannot help but to deeply, DEEPLY smell their own farts. I'm sorry but half the fun is being tired of getting smoked by the same boss, and learning a new OOB glitch with my mates to clap that sucker. But Bungie is increasingly focusing efforts on you having fun only in the way they want you to have fun, and anyone that falls outside of those expectations are reprehensible individuals whom deserve absolutely no mercy. But remember, EvErY BaN iS MaNuAL AND BuNgIe NeVeR mAkeS mIStAkEs... give me a break, if that were true, there would be no process to appeal a ban in the first place. Why there isn't a system of progressive escalation for infractions is beyond me. Probably money. Oh, and if you play a game of gambit, and a blueberry is cheating, your account can get wasted, too. Way to go, Bungie. Really top stuff there. I'm not trying to be bitter or resentful, I did highlight some reasons for why it is a good game. They improved the story immensely, there is plenty of build crafting opportunities if you like to min-max and nerd out, the game is aesthetically pleasing, the handling of the weapons feel authentic (generally), I'm simply tired of getting my account completely wiped out only to start over, and my ban not get overturned. Destiny 2 is a great game, the problem is that Bungie is the developer, and sole judge jury and executioner. If it was a bad game, I wouldn't buy the dlc again. But if you're a new player thinking of trying it out, steer clear. Video games aren't supposed to take up all your time, this one is greedy for every second of it, and when you think you've gotten something down or have a sure thing, it gets thrown back in your face.\t\t\t\t\t
