\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe introductory missions do a good enough job at explaining the game to newer players, although i wouldn't be surprised if you don't get pulled in by the game immediately considering I feel a lot of the enjoyment of this game comes from already being invested and caring about the loot you get. A good portion of this game is the grind to get better loot and if you do not care about that it can be very difficult to figure out what you should be doing next.

i also find it hard to see how people are to get engaged in a story that is 7 years going when half of the story content is inaccessible because of vaulting content, which is a shame considering the story is probably at the best its been in years. The best you can do is watch lore series on youtube which I don't think does it justice. It seems like Bungie wants to go for an evolving world where there is more of a "You had to be there" feeling. For example, one of the main story characters "Cayde-6" was killed off a few years ago and right now the intro missions go over how he died awhile ago and who killed him like its part of this worlds history that you werent around for. I think this is a fun idea in theory but it is difficult to get invested in some of the story if you haven't already been following it.

If you are looking to pick this game up because it is free, be ready to have a very small amount of content to play, or buy the season pass to have access to the current content. If you ONLY play free to play you have access to:

6 open world zones in which you can do public events, mini world dungeon type things called lost sectors, patrol missions, and other smaller things. By default I believe it is set up so initially only have access to one area but unlock more as you gain xp from playing. so in all you can visit these destinations:

- European Dead-zone on Earth
- The Cosmodrome on Earth
- The moon
- Nessus
- The Tangled Shore (edge of the solar system)
- The Dreaming City (The Awoken homeworld)
- Europa (cold place)

You can play the three core game modes without much restriction. This includes the Crucible which is the PVP mode, Strikes are similar to dungeons in other games where you run three people through a mission and kill a big boss at the end, some strikes contain mechanics that you have to do, and Gambit, which is a sort of PVPVE game mode where you kill PVE enemies to get motes that you bank and whichever team banks 75 motes first wins. You are allowed to invade the enemy team and kill them to stop them from banking their motes occasionally.

As of May 22nd, 2021 free players now have access to the raid "Vault of Glass". Raids are one of the main reasons i play Destiny 2 and they are so much fun. They take a bit of learning and require outside sources to find a group to play with, but it is 100% worth it and I would recommend trying it out as soon as you are a high enough level to do so.

All in all, if you are a free to play player, don't expect too much unless you are willing to buy the pass to play new content every 3 months or so and a large expansion every year, but if you are willing to do that and you can get invested in the loot grind, get ready for one of the best looter shooters out there.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a pretty good game. PvP is fast, intense, and fun. PvE can range from casual open-world mass murdering to skill-intense raiding mechanics. However, be prepared for grind and frustration.

This game has heavy amounts of RNG in its reward system, from getting specific guns and armor to drop to the rolls of stats on those pieces of equipment. You may be excited the first time you see a specific gear piece drop, only to realize the stats are worthless to you. After a few times of this, you just stop caring about most drops entirely. They're all just stuff to sort with Destiny Item Manager and you'll never even pay attention to individual gear pieces except exotics.

Quests for guns can cause you frustration even if you're not doing them. Someone else on your team probably will be and they will not switch off their hand cannon and play with the team in ranked PvP because their quest requires headshot kills with handcannon and a ranked PvP tier (real example.)

It's still a fun game, but know what you're getting into.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who never played Destiny 1, or Destiny 2 on console, I am thoroughly impressed with what Bungie was able to accomplish with this game. I originally played for free, but later bought both expansions when they went on sale. They were both worth it in my opinion, as they add new public game modes, single player scenerios and so many raids it will take many hours before I can finish all the content.

The story and voice acting are quite lacking, but lets be honest, this game is more about satisfying run-and-gun mechanics and team activities. No one cares the plot line is so tacky it's almost out of a YA novel.

The controls are fluid and intuitive. Although I've never played, the controls/physics are so similar to Halo it didn't take long for me to be competitive in multiplayer matches. Although the matchmaking sometimes doesn't do the best at balancing the teams, I still feel this would be enjoyable for anyone from professional to casuals.

Overall, I'd recommend trying the free mode -- it really is completely free. If you enjoy it, I'd grab the expansions while they're on sale. Some have strong opinions on the differences between the DLC, but I felt they were both worth their sale price.\t\t\t\t\t
