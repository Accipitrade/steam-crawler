\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's a lot to fit in and explain, so here's the verdict: don't waste your time unless you have friends who will play this with you and are willing to put no less than $40 into the game. Want to know why? Continue reading.



With the release of Forsaken for D2, people were hyped, with fanboys claiming that this was D2's TTK (The Taken King). They praised the story, the concepts, and the general layout, but the sad truth? It just kind of sucked. The entirety of the campaign is this: go directly hunt X bad guy, go through a needlessly tedious or difficult boss fight, mission's over. Multiply that by, what, six and you have the entire Forsaken campaign. So, what else did you get? Well, you got two new areas, gated exotic "quests", a raid, and a third tree for each of your subclasses that could be unlocked with a Mote of Light that required a ridiculous bit of tedium.

Why is that little bit of background important? Well, Shadowkeep is Forsaken's idea, but worse. A few cutscenes that were hardly important, a very basic plot than an intern could have drummed up, some items that were either 'meh' or themed reskins of basic weapon frames, a Vex raid, aaaaaaand not much else. The entire Moon map is just a reskin of the original map with a couple dungeon additions, the Vex are now covered in vegetation to go with your veggie-covered guns and ship, and the local Hive have red on them. Got a couple Morn-themed armor pieces and weapons, with the armor being the main goal of Shadowkeep since it apparently """allows you to bypass the energy field""".

But now, for the ending of Shadowkeep. The ending was so incredibly abrupt and cliche that it feels more like a commercial break than anything else. Seriously, it's so axed and incomplete that you actually have to ask yourself if that was really the end of the campaign, and you don't really get confirmation until you get your Good Boi exotic. Nothing is revealed, just kill a new version of the other bosses that you've faced with an annoying mechanic, get guided through the Pyramid, pick up an artifact, face an evil duplicate of yourself who claims to be your salvation in a cutscene, then cut to black and that's it. Seriously.


But enough of the very lackluster "campaign", let's talk about the game mechanics themselves and what they've changed. They made quite a few changes, so let me summarize a few of them: do you like Pay 2 Win mechanics? Do you enjoy unbalanced gameplay? Then this is for you. It used to be debatable as to whether or not the game was P2W, but not now. With their F2P overhaul, it's objectively P2W. Pay $10 and you get access to a hand cannon, Eriana's Vow, at level 1 of your Season Rank that not only pierces most shielding states with the game (neutralizing basically all Vex enemies, since they are this season's theme), but that has a higher range/accuracy rate and ratio than a scout rifle, and no, you read that correctly. If your aim is good, this gun obliterates almost everything on the PvE spectrum and can out-shoot almost every scout rifle in the game. If you refuse to pay $10, then you can totally do that. Just know that you're losing out on at least 50% of the rank-up rewards that you could be earning (including XP boosts), that OP hand cannon cannot be unlocked until Season Rank 35, and you will earn approximately 33% of the progress that you could earn on The Vow, which is the quest that you have to do to get the catalyst for Eriana's Vow. Even with the boosters, doing nothing but Strikes, it takes literally 100 STRIKES in order to finish The Vow. Sure, you can do Gambit and Gambit Prime alongside Crucible in order to earn progress, but MuLtIpLaYeR/PvP IsNt NeCeSsArY (a common BS argument shared by many fanboys, it seems).


But let's talk about the final nail in the coffin for me, personally. For all of those people out there who bought the game, the "DLC", the bundles, and who want to move to a new platform, probably PC? Yeah, you can't do that without having to re-buy Forsaken. That's right, your ability to play DLC does not transfer across platforms. Not only is this a huge problem for the very notion of Cross-Play, but this is a slap in the face to all fans and long-term players of the series, most notably because this can be done and corrected. When you enable Cross-Play, they not only link your root account to accounts on other platforms, but they see all of the "DLC" that you've paid for up to that point for each given platform. This is yet another game where all of the "DLC" is actually False Flag DLC, where you have all of the content downloaded; what you're paying for is a checkbox to be enabled that then allows you to use that content within the game. If you have money to spare, you can check this yourself on console and PC.



The point is very simple. The "new" content is very barebones and short, they implemented a legitimate P2W structure, the gunplay is still unbalanced, and this offers almost nothing unless you have people to play it with. The fact that they're trying to whittle away at their players' wallets with extra garbage, while delivering little to no fresh or enriching content, makes it all the worse.

But hey, at least they finally fixed that bug where Bows would count as Fusion Rifles for certain bounties, most notably the Calibrations from Banshee-44. Doesn't matter that it's been in since around the release of Forsaken, right?\t\t\t\t\t
