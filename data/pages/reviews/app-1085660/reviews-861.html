\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEverything about this game boils down to the fact that it should just be an expansion to D1. There is nothing new. We've had ships and sparrows for 10 years and we got to race sparrows for a season. Takes place in space and we haven't left the solar system (Idk nor really think it matters where The Tangled Shore/Dreaming City takes place).

The open world is like going to Earth and exploring your home town and that's it. That's every location.

The scope of the game is so small for a game with so much lore and potential. It's been however many years and I feel like the game has progressed a small patch worth.

The story is so lackluster. Which is a shame because you know they care about the lore/characters/setting, sinch they have volumes worth of lore, but I dont understand why they make you go to an external site to read it all.

PvP was terrible even before Stasis was introduced. They give Riot a run for their money with how imbalanced things are.

PvE is fun for 30 minutes. In that time you've played the same 3 strikes in a row, so you alt f4 and call it a day.

The world quests/random events are not too bad, at least they have somewhat engaging mechanics. However, because the world is so f-ing small, you just do them over and over without much in between. And it just serves as a means to and end: mats.

The bounties aren't fun or interesting. I'd rather do current WoW world quests... f*** me..

Bungie does Raids and Dungeons well. You just have to get there(specifically Raids) doing all the things described above, and you better not skip a day or hour friend.

Overall this game is the cliche'd very big but even more shallow pool. When I scroll through games to reinstall/play again, I always look at Destiny and think you know what maybe I should; I like the gunplay, the aesthetic, the world, the abilities...but then instantly like a punch to the face I'm reminded in 1 hours time I will have gotten painfully bored and uninstalled it once again. This thing could have been so much more. I'm not much of a Warframe player, but from the outside they seemed to have done more with less.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLeft this game for an entire year, and when I came back the game refuses to allow me to play long enough to attempt to enjoy it.

After years of work by the developers, hundreds, or even thousands, of hours spent playing across 4 different platforms(PS3, PS4, Xbox One, and now PC), and two different games, the only thing about this experience that I have reasonably found to be consistent is the near constant connection errors and the disingenuous error codes that refuse to do anything other than place the blame on MY hardware or internet connection. I once firmly believed, that due to where I live, these error codes on Console had some merit to them because it was not infrequent for me to have connection issues with my Playstation or Xbox, but having experienced this game on a personal computer where I have full control and can transparently monitor my internet connection constantly in real time, I have recently come under the impression that these 'connection errors' may be fabricated by Bungie in an effect to pad my playtime.

Destiny 2 is an unreliable, unstable, and at this point hardly innovative shooter experience. Mobile management apps no longer reliably function to aid in the quality of life of this game, and the game itself is virtually unplayable thanks to it almost constantly saying Steam servers are down despite me remaining fully able to voice-chat with my friends through them.

Playing this game feels like returning to an abusive relationship. The only emotions I can get from it are Anxiety, Frustration, and a sense of Dread that at some point every five minutes a lying, cash-hungry piece of software will blame me for it being the only thing on the internet I've lost any form of server connection to.\t\t\t\t\t
