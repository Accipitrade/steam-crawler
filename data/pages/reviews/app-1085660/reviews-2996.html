\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn almost every respect, Destiny 2 is vastly superior to Destiny. The gameplay is smoother, there is loads more to do, and the game is constantly being tweaked and updated, with more content being added every few months. But despite all of the major improvements, i can't really recommend it to most people.

Currently, the "new" content that is released roughly every 3 months is set up in a battle pass esque fashion, which i absolutely hate. You have to pay $10 for the new season every 3 months, and you only get until the new season to unlock the gear that you just paid for. And In order to unlock all 100 levels of the season to get all of the available loot, you have to sink massive amounts of time into the game farming XP, something most people just don't have time for. And Bungie knows this too, so as an extra way to squeeze more money out of your pocket, they give you the ability to pay even more money to level up the season pass that YOU ALREADY PAID FOR. It costs 100 silver per season rank, and there are 100 season ranks in each pass. You do the math, its a lot of money. But hey, don't worry! If you don't have enough time to unlock all of your season ranks and don't have the cash to buy the rest of them, you can always wait until the gear from that season becomes available in the Eververse store, where you can purchase a chance to get those items for more real money in the form of cosmetic loot boxes.

The seasons aren't just purely cosmetic though, over the course of each season, "new" content is released, most of the time being new 6 player game modes. My problem with these is that they are either not entertaining at all, a waste of time, recycled content from previous seasons, or a combination of all 3. Take the Vex Offensive content from the season of the Undying for example. The Vex Offensive was the main 6 player game mode introduced during the season. It involved going from map to map, and killing enemies with little to no mechanics. At the end, you would fight a painfully easy boss that is just a re-skin of a different boss! I'd probably hate it less if the rewards were anything decent, but every gun you could unlock from the Vex Offensive was objectively worse than the "pinnacle weapons" which i will get into next.

Pinnacle Weapons are new weapons introduced to the game every season that can only be unlocked from extremely long, often very difficult quests similar to exotic quests, and sometimes even more challenging. The reason for the quests being so difficult is that each pinnacle weapon is basically like a legendary version of an exotic, with a toned down perk. They are most of the time much better than any weapon you can unlock from any of the seasonal content, and there tends to be at least one pinnacle weapon every season that is completely broken. The problem with pinnacle weapons is that due to them being so much better than any other weapons in the game, players to never swap out their loadout for another one. And when everyone has the pinnacle weapons, PvP is filled to the brim with the same few uber powerful guns. And they also present the problem of ruining any other content in the game. Once you've unlocked all the good pinnacle weapons for the current season, what's the incentive to go out and try to get other gear? What is the point in pouring hours into farming for a good roll on a raid weapon when you can spend half as much time on a pinnacle quest for a gun that is loads better than your "god roll" anyway? The pinnacle weapons are a neat concept, but they make farming seem pointless, which is very dangerous for a franchise that has previously been very based on farming.

The only piece of reasonably enjoyable content since Destiny 2's launch on steam has been the Shadowkeep DLC. It introduced new weapons and armor, new exotics, a challenging and extremely fun new raid, And a new dungeon. Once i was done with Shadowkeep's content, the game began to feel very stale, and once i got all the pinnacles i wanted, i felt no need to keep playing because the end game is so completely boring and empty. Hopefully the game will get back on its feet with the reintroduction of Trials of The Nine next season, but my hopes aren't very high.

Don't get me wrong, its a super fun game, and i've got more hours in it than any other game in my steam library, but Destiny 2 is flawed in so many ways that I can't recommend it to the average player. And i understand that its a free to play game and they have to make money somehow. But time gated content and micro transactions/loot boxes are not the way to do it if you want to create a good game. In my opinion, Bungie should go back to their Destiny 1 system of releasing less frequent, massive DLC's that add large amount of good, new content.\t\t\t\t\t
