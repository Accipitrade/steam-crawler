\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is pretty fun... For the first couple of hours (30-40 hours). Maybe significantly more hours than that with friends. But after that you kinda start to see what the game is really.

Skyrim with less freedom & guns.

The game world is vast, but instanced-based (which isn't a problem). But like Skyrim, pretty shallow. Events replay on a set schedule, quests are straight forward and do not affect the game at all. You also rarely will see any improvement in your character as the enemies scale with you and your party. So all in all, the game plays exactly how it plays for you since day one. The different guns and armors you pick up along the way does nothing to change this. It kinda reminds me of guild wars 2 in that aspect. But more so in PvP.

PvP is fun, but from what I can see, there isn't a lot of competition and tons of imbalances due to how various passives and exotics interact in the pvp setting. It's mostly an afterthought it seems. Very similar in feel to guild wars 2 in that sense.

PvE is where the game really shines as there are TONS of content to go through. But like I said earlier. It's all shallow and there is no real satisfaction when doing any of them. The campaign is exceptional, but once that's over it makes you wish you never completed it in the first place. Reason being the game outside of the campaign is just filled with shallow and mindless activities that serve no real purpose but to increase your characters power level by a marginal amount... Only to realize that enemies scale with you as well... So what's the point Bungie?

This is even further exacerbated by how bad the loot drops are.

Loot is mostly trash and every once in a while they throw a new exotic weapon at you. I'm convinced if they didn't people would quit in droves simple due to how bad the loot is. This game would definitely benefit from having more robust loot drops like Borderlands or even Path of Exile. Which kinda ties in to my final point.

There is no end game. There is no early-game. Only a mid-game and all the in-game anxiety that comes with it as you run around with no real purpose. You can do those quests and you can do those bounties but at the end of the day you only feel punished for it. Both in time spent, and the fact that you now have less content to work with. There is no real trade-off as you gain no real in-game value from doing any objective.

It's a social game with a crosshair & bullet sponged attached.

Overall, I would recommend the game for what it is. A mindless shooter to play while in discord with friends. Don't expect any else. Outside of the campaign as the looting is ♥♥♥♥ and the pvp is ♥♥♥♥.

I wouldn't pay full price for it. Wait for a sale as you'll run through the most compelling content in a 2 weeks and sit there pissed you spent your money on it. Typical "Triple-A" gaming experience in 2020 though.\t\t\t\t\t
