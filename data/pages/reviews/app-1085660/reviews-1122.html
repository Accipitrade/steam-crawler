\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have a few thousand hours on PlayStation, couple hundred here. I'd say that compared to the first game, this one was created with PC in mind and it is a far nicer experience on PC than console.

Regarding the story, the content is quite rich if you know/want to commit to it. You'll have to do the side missions here and there, read the lore on the weapons and armor, read the entries you get from everthing you do if you want to understand the whole story. Once you get into it, the story and the universe Bungie has created is just incerdible. From the different races, to mythical heroes and weapon's story. It has a bit of everthing, and there is a lot of it to enjoy.
In that regard i'd say it is similar to an Elder Scrolls or a 40k game, you have to earch for every little bit of info if you want to fully understand. Progress has made lore more obvious since the Beyond Light dlc when I write this tho.

For the gameplay there is nothing in particular to say, everything is at it's place compared to others looter shooters. It is the balance of the weapons that can be underwhelming at times. Devs listen to the comunity even if they don't change thing instantly.

It is a game better enjoyed with friends. It can be played solo and it is a good experience but you'll miss a good 10 to 20 percent of the game not doing raids and endgame content with people you know and laugh with. If you can, stay away from LFG, saltiest recruting on planet earth.

I absolutely love this game but I tried to be honest with it.
See you at the tower, Guardian.\t\t\t\t\t
