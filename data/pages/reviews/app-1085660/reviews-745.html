\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you find yourself the type of neurotic person to collect stuff in other games, and enjoy the feel of how guns shoot in a video game, this is for you. There's plenty of collecting and content off the bat, amid a sea of so many choices you will feel overwhelmed. The game's lore is great but hidden in items like a dark souls game and in journal entries found in menus. The mechanics of the game have varying degrees of difficulty and synergy for weapon perk/class ability and team play. The endgame content is where the game really shines for how much effort you put in, but at the cost of forsaking some actual interaction/response to aforementioned mechanics. Bungie puts a lot of love into the aesthetics of the game and if you like that, it's a blast.

The trade off that any true Destiny player will tell you (I bought the game at launch deluxe on BattleNet, and have paid/played every DLC/season since) is that the game devs often take 2 steps forward on making the game better, and then 1 step back on what they think is what the playerbase and themselves as devs want/need out of the game. That being said, the season for summer 2021 was one of the best in 2 years, and the devs have paved the way to adjust or fix a lot of the f*** ups they've made in the past 4 years. I'm excited for the season that launches 24/8/21 as it will be a lead up season to the next DLC in spring 2022. The game is free to try for a lot of very easy "pick up and play" style content that is given now (that a lot of people paid for in the past) to try and get you to pay for the most recent content, and while not my favorite method of game dev, it's done a fine job so far.

If you've managed to grind out all the free content up to the DLC and season pass, then the Beyond Light deluxe is definitely worth it on sale or get the regular version of BL if you can't find it on sale. If you read this and enjoyed the game, feel free to add me as I love helping others in this game and have made plenty of memories with strangers turned friends.

I'd say download it and try it out, find a group of friendly people and keep them close, it makes the content way more enjoyable. If you find yourself enjoying what's happening then you can decide to buy a season pass or a DLC. If you haven't bought any DLC I would recommend waiting to buy Forsaken DLC as it may become free in the next 6 months.
