\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: Great online shooter game that can get fun and addictive but is in need of massive changes. There is a lot more I want to write about it along with positives but... The game currently is a bloated DLC collection with the addition of dungeon passes that paywalls dungeons now. Still heavily relying on FOMO while somehow pushing low-quality content that's been drip-fed for every season for the last 24 months. Ever degrading performance on PC. Old UI/Interface that is just insulting for PC use, it's bearable but please it's almost 2023. Lack or slow balance changes. Lack of actual love for the PvP-side of the game. Melee still whiffs.

---

Destiny 2 is a great game to get into with great gunplay thanks to Bungie's magical hands with a mix and twist of sci-fi and space magic on the side. Probably even the biggest game in its own genre due to how old the game is and how long it's been out in the market. This may even help by the fact that the game isn't that heavily demanding in terms of what specs you'd need to play the game but we do have those instances that performance on PC has been a hit or miss as it's been degrading patch after patch...

There's a lot of variety that a player can play around with; there are the general PvE Activities, Which range from Strikes, Dungeons, and Raids, and then we have PvP, which is composed of the crucible (6v6's and 3v3's), a weekend try-hard competitive mode, Trials of Osiris, Some rotating game modes, and also a competitive mode which is another reskin of what we had previously... And of course, the loved and hated PvEvP Mode, Gambit. We also do have seasonal activities but this is mostly locked behind the season pass, which also further enhances and adds more to the story's progression every 3-4 months.

The game is currently Free-to-Play as what they want to tell or sell you, but in truth, it's more so-like a Demo or a free trial with limits to what you can participate in or do, due to the fact that there are a ton of DLCs that you'd need to buy just to stay relevant because the game will lock you out from almost 70-80% of the content. But that doesn't mean you'd have to buy them all at the beginning since it's more than often that the current expansion or season is enough for you to start with unless you are chasing items and other activities from previous DLCs.

But with how the game has been doing lately albeit with its popularity and success, It's difficult to even suggest the game to newcomers or those who are looking to start out.

The game as of this moment is in a bloated state of continual drip-fed content whether it's in terms of seasonal content or balance changes. The same old seasonal formula/framework that has plagued the game for almost 24 months even when the new expansion comes out has been stated by the game director in their tweet it won't be changing until two seasons into the current expansion. The devs not really showing any love to the PvP side of the game with a lack or slow responses/updates to balance changes, and a lack of new maps for PvP with how long the game has been up and running. Not forgetting how old the UI has been in the game that we still can't track all the buffs we have on the screen as it's still limited to a few numbers, Still stuck with the old interface that doesn't let you stack items to the amount you want to get/buy from a vendor that you have to manually press over and over just to get acquire it manually.

Along with systems that gatekeep you from most content aside from being DLC or Season pass locked, Light/Power level grinding has heavily bloated the game itself with newly added mechanics and systems, on top of this which makes the game overwhelming and a slogfest at best, limiting you already from what modes or grinds you want to focus on and this without even adding RNG that factors for your loot. We still don't have any drop/dupe protection or focusing for pinnacle or powerful rewards.

The Introduction of Dungeon key/pass (with a price of 20$), which wasn't a thing only until this year has made me wary of what other monetization practices would Bungie do in the future when we didn't have these in place for dungeons before the WQ expansion which was part of the expansion price already before. On top of the other prices from older expansions or season passes, etc. makes the game expensive and overwhelming already.

The game is still great but the game is in need of massive changes.\t\t\t\t\t
