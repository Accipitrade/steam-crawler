\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI play Destiny for a several months scattered throughout each year. I usually play from new content drops, will occasionally hit 100+ for that season's pass. I enjoy raiding & pvping but am not stellar in either. I imagine most D2 players are like me or are similar. I love D2 and recommend it to all of my friends because it feels satisfying to play, it can be challenging, and the story/lore is actually really good if you pay attention.

As a shooter 'mmorpg' I think D2 does well for the space it fits. There is plenty of content to play through and gear to grind.
Did they archive some old content? Yes.
Do I miss it? Not really as most of it wasn't applicable, except maybe the raids.

Is there an in-game shop? Yes.
Is the game play to win? No, all of it is cosmetics (as far as I know).

Can you get currency by quests in game? Yes.
Why are people complaining? Entitlement I guess.

Does gear get shelfed and item level locked to make way for newer content? Yes.
Why are people complaining? I guess they've never played another mmorpg.

Is Bungie perfect? No. They make mistakes but usually do a decent/good job of communicating workings of different items throughout an expansion/season. Could it be better? Yes. But I have 750+ hours and I've kept coming back since the game came out periodically every year and enjoyed every release.

Ultimately:
If you want to play the game nonstop, day-in & day-out, then you might find a better game.
If you're looking to play consistently throughout the year, but play other game too, you'll enjoy this one.
If you're a casual gamer who enjoys playing with friends and can sink some time into one of the few games you play, you'll enjoy this one.
If you just like to dabble in games that you don't have to actively play to play new content or want to put a few hours in here and there, you'll enjoy this game.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is in a better place than it's been since Forsaken or Season of Arrivals, but it's still not without it's flaws. PvP matchmaking still leaves much to be desired, and there's painfully little FTP content. If you can afford the "pay to play" seasonal model it uses, I would consider it worth it purely for the visuals, music, and incredibly rich lore.

The high-end PvE content, particularly the dungeons and raids, are some of (if not the) the best features the game provides, and while the gunplay is consistently interesting (to me at least), it can sometimes get stale, so it's important to remember that it's not *just* about the guns: using your space magic abilities to complement the rifle is intrinsic to Destiny's identity as an FPS RPG.

TL,DR: If you can afford it, it's worth it. Otherwise, FTP has *some* content, but not enough to keep players consistently engaged. Way to gatekeep your own game, Bungie.


EDIT: I have to clarify, I am a long-time Destiny player. I started playing D1 in Year 2 with the release of The Taken King, played all the way up to Destiny 2's release of the Black Armory, took a two-year break until about half a year before Beyond Light, and have been playing regularly up to present. What I'm saying is: I have a long history with the Destiny franchise, and the very thorough understanding of the game and it's mechanics that comes with it. However, the current Destiny 2 model is excruciatingly biased towards players like me.

If you are a new player with absolutely no history with the Destiny franchise, then I'm sorry to say that ever since Beyond Light dropped (nearly 2 years ago now), the Destiny 2 New Player Experience has not just been unfriendly, but downright hostile. It is horrific: the game drops you into the game world, gives you a half-assed tutorial on the mechanics, throws you into the main social space, then slaps you on the ass and says "good luck."

Before Beyond Light (Pre-Vaulting), there were three full storylines you could play that all worked in sequential concert to familiarise you with the combat system, the loot system, the leveling system, and the overarching storyline. Now (Post-Vaulting), all of that is gone. The basic tutorial teaches you the bare F'ing minimum about the combat system, almost nothing about the loot or leveling system, and absolutely nothing about the overarching story. I've seen Reddit post after Reddit post asking for story clarifications because there is no longer a *crumb* of context for new players to discover. How is a new player supposed to get invested in a story they are completely unequipped to comprehend, with gameplay mechanics they barely understand?

And don't even get me started on the combat style mod system (which fortunately I've heard rumors that the mod system will be getting the much-needed rework we've been asking for), I would triple the length of this review just talking about *that.* I'll just say it's easily the worst part of this game (now that skill-based matchmaking is back in PvP, making it slightly less of a cesspool than it was) and leave it at that.

If you are a long-time fan of the Destiny franchise and have money to spend, I can gladly recommend Destiny 2 for you. You're probably the type who already has a solid understanding of both the mechanics and the story, and if you're late to the party you're probably already invested enough in the story to suss out the vaulted details for yourself.

If you are a new player looking for a fantasty FPS MMO looter-shooter, like the vast majority of people who look at reviews, I advise you to look elsewhere. This game is not made for you. It was once, and I long to bring my friends into this game, but I simply can't recommend it in good faith.

EDIT TLDR: my original point stands: "if you can afford it (and have some background in the Destiny franchise), it's worth it purely for the visuals, music, and incredibly rich lore, and the high-end PvE content are some of the best features the game provides. Otherwise, FTP has *some* content, but not nearly enough to keep players consistently engaged."\t\t\t\t\t
