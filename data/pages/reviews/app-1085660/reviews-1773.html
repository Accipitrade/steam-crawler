\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI bought this game on Xbox on release day, and while I enjoyed it, it did not give me the same level of magic and awe that Destiny 1 did for me. I beat the campaign and quickly put it down. Now, after picking it up again after a couple years of hiatus, there is much more content in the game and I am enjoying it much more. If you have played D1, then you will likely enjoy this game as much of the mechanics and controls are the same. Making the base game free to play was a very good move on Bungie's part, making the game accessible to everyone. i'm not a fan of the microtransaction stuff, but that's just the way things are now.

Not a huge complaint, but my biggest gripe even since the Xbox release was the fact that Icebreaker and Gjallarhorn were no longer in the game. Destiny 1 players will feel me on that one :P However, many more exotics have been added to take their place. This leads me to an "actual" complaint: exotics in Destiny 1 were truly exotic, at least in their rarity. Finding one and equipping it made you feel like a god. In Destiny 2, there's a bajillion exotic quests. This makes these items more accesible to the more casual players, but takes away from the exclusivity they had in Destiny 1.\t\t\t\t\t
