\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor context, I've played since just before the release of the first mini-expansion, Curse of Osiris, all on PC. So in other words, I've played the game from its lowest low to its highest high, and this is it chief. Over hundreds of hours in game, I've completed 4/7 raids, gotten most exotics, etcetera; and i can say that Shadowkeep makes it all worth it. The changes to armour make it much easier to get a good build, while increasing the difficulty due to high costs for the best upgrades to gear. The changes to damage scaling mean that PVE content is now truly challenging, since you can't melt nearly as many bosses. Gunplay is supremely satisfying both in PVE and PVP, and there are so many skill-based mechanics to master for every class. The only downsides are the following:

1. Gambit is the worst mode in any game I've ever played. It's slow, you're unable to do much to affect the outcome, and you are barely rewarded for completing it. Gambit prime just makes this worse. Compounding that are the numerous balance issues (invaders op) that continue to plague the mode. Overall not a good time.

2. Crucible(PVP) balancing is in a rough state. Since each exotic provides a unique playstyle, they also provide a unique way to break PVP. And since the devs aren't particularly speedy(at all) at balancing PVP, issues get left for months.

However, these two gripes can be avoided for the most part. Overall it comes down to this: you can play a very large part of the game for free, and the two expansions are more than worth their combined price. Heck, $25 for Forsaken is still a steal, since it was arguably even better than Shadowkeep. So give it a try, for your own sake.


Beyond Light update:

Alright: Gambit still sucks, but it's better. Crucible balancing is even worse, somehow. But, more mobility exists in the game, and therefore it feels much better. On top of this, the new raid Deep Stone Crypt is an incredible experience. Even if you don't want to spend money, just watch it.

The loot is an issue. For the money, you get much less in terms of actual loot, and the fact that so many guns were sunset means that many of the offerings are somewhat lackluster at this point. However, this has already been somewhat rectified with the raid and seasonal content. Here's hoping more is added soon.

Optimization is an issue. Getting 60 fps is spotty a lot of the time for computers with GTX 1060 quality processing, and that's really unfortunate. But, if you have a better CPU and an SSD to run it on, you'll likely go far.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have previously recommended this game but the more I play it the more flaws I seem to run into. The PvE aspect of this game is great, the gunplay is great. The stats are great, there is grind but not an unreasonable amount (to my knowledge).

The issues? Netcode.

Bungie has a habit of screwing the networking of this game very very hard.

Previously in PvP occasionally your shotgun wouldn't do anything, it didn't happen often but it was annoying, rubber banding was rare. Ping is regularly above 100 ms for no apparent reason, just enough to screw with your aim. Beaver errors galore.

Now in PvP, rubber banding is very common and very visible, shotguns literally do nothing 50% of the time, ping is the same. They also added a weapon in the previous update that most people can get immediately with the season pass that breaks any semblence of game balance, you'll know what it is because it stays on the ground and damages you for a very long time.

I cannot stress enough how frustrating it is to unload two shotgun shells into someone point blank and have both do absolutely nothing.

For reference I regularly play other FPS games and neither the ping nor the netcode are a problem in those.\t\t\t\t\t
