\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs a Guardian who left 5 years ago and is returning now.

I left after "Forsaken" ended due to some upsetting circumstances with my Raiding clan. But I'm now trying to return to the game.

I have to say about the state of the game. Both Positive and Negative. But the Negative is why everyone is reading, so let's start there.
The fact that they have entirely removed several whole expansions worth of content, along with the original base game, is still wholly unacceptable to me.

It is all content that we paid good money for, and should still be able to access. Their explanation is that the install size of the game game was getting too large and they wanted to cut it back. Personally, I wouldn't care if the game was 300GBs if | was still getting everything I paid for. If their concern really was about game size, they would've done it the correct way and made installing data for specific expansions optional and variable. All the content they removed wholesale could've been done at the will of the user instead of ripped away against their will.

Any arguments that it was part of the story are wholly irrelevant too, since I fully believe they wrote that effect into the story after making the decision to nix the content, just to have some sort of explanation for it.
There might not be anything happening on Mercury, Titan, lo, or Mars, but I should still be able to go there if | want to.

Moving on from that, the new player experience is a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ travesty. | started a new character to see how it is, as well as invited some friends to join me. In short, every new player completely quit the game. How the hell do you write a story where the new player is expected to simultaneously feel like they're new, and like they've been there all along at the same time, while also having story critical content completely missing from context? You're spinning multiple webs of narrative at the same time and it's almost impossible to make sense of as a new player without doing outside research. Not to mention there's a bit of new player content they have to complete that is buggy, just downright not working, or is implemented so poorly that it's incredibly jarring to see.

The current state of the game for a new player is Horrendous.

Adding back the base game story and removed expansions stories would completely fix this issue. They wouldn't need to spend so much more time and resources on trying to accommodate and band-aid a broken, inferior setup.

TLIDR: If you're new to the game, don't expect to understand anything that's happening without doing your own research on your own time.

Moving on to the existing player experience.

It's actually not bad. Yeah, the game expects you to grind a bit to get the best gear, but it's always been that way.
The gun play is just as fantastic as it's always been, and with more options for build customization to boot.
"Witch Queen"'s story seems to be great enough to content with the best moments in Destiny to date, "The Taken King" and "Forsaken", and the seasonal story additions are nice too, so there's not so much story drought between expansions.
But it would be far better if they stopped removing the seasonal story additions when the season ends. How do you expect people
who weren't present to keep track of anything?
I think a better solution would be to convert these seasonal story missions to pickup quest lines so you can do them later and have it so you still have to purchase that season, but at a discounted price to account for the lack of live event activities. This would make it so players can catch up on story content on their own, AND Bungie can still earn money for it after their Live events are over.
As you can probably tell from everything else so far, They have a horrendous track record of removing content from the game for what looks like no reason at all, other then to incite Fear Of Missing Out in the players. They do make you pay for access to each season's worth of content, but | can accept that since they are also adding an acceptable amount of content for that season as well. JUST STOP REMOVING IT!

I don't know how to end this. For now, I'l keep gritting my teeth and playing the game. For what it's worth, I do still have fun playing this game. But the decisions they have made have been so confusing. I dare say moronic.
If there's one thing | need to impress, it's to stop taking away good ♥♥♥♥ we paid for.\t\t\t\t\t
