\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game can offer something to almost anyone who likes FPS games.

Do you like PvP activities where you can flex your abilities to tap heads or like to occasionally trash on them with abilities like in Overwatch or Valorant? Play Crucible.

Do you like PvE activities? Well, yaay, this game can offer you easy Strike playlists, harder Nighfalls or pinnacle Grandmaster Nightfalls (but you´d better gear up for them). There are also mechanicly harder Dungeons and Raids, Dungeons for raid-like experience with 3 players, Raids for true test of cooperation, because of challanging mechanics and stronger enemies with bosses. In terms of community: I cant think of any other community in game, that would be more welcoming than D2 community (unless you get to meet some d*ckhead with ego up to the roof lol). Community Discord servers are full of people who are happy to help New Lights or returning players and others who can help you with playing.

Give it a try. Even tho this game has some bad aspects, such as long time between patches, sometimes buggy, and it is grindy af, it is worth a try.

And in terms of it being "expensive". Yes, Deluxe edition of a new expansion is 80€, but it is a year worth of content! Maybe do not buy the Deluxe until you know you like the game, but if you do, it´s definitly worth.

9/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame is good to me. Look at my playtime...
On a serious note, I've been playing this game since D1 Alpha. This game has had so many ups and downs that for all these new lights, I'm not sure why there aren't many negative reviews. The New Light experience, based on going through the early missions, seems so lackluster. I personally feel like if you're going to enjoy the game you need to be aware of the community. This is a social game, while you can play solo, it is definitely not going to be as immersive solo with all the harder content locked behind instances that require 3-6 people to achieve, unless you have gotten to that point where you can take on the solo or less than suggested amount of people challenge. I know I have been negative so far but this is where that ends.

If this game satisfies an itch that you have been wanting from a game for so long, you're in for a long ride. Destiny 2 is one of the few FPS games to completely satisfy that itch for a game that I want to put my time into. The lore, the pve, the community, and the pvp for the most part is enjoyable, especially once you get a good group of players together in your fireteam. The diversity in weapons and armor lets most players have a good time picking their playstyle. The late game content offered such as Raids, Dungeons, Trials and Grandmaster Nightfalls provides content for any player interested in testing their knowledge and ability of the game. If you're good in the pvp of the game you have Iron banner and Trials of Osiris to test your might and acheive the armor and weapons provided by the respective modes. The pve modes Raids with a group of 6 or the dungeon with a group of 3, as well as nightfalls with a group of 3. All provide their own set of mode exclusive armour or weapons that you can feel accomplished to obtain but the random rolls make you want to go back and do it again for that "god roll" you're seeking. If you're a "gamer" playing with less than the suggested amount of players and still achieving the W is challenging but even more self rewarding once mastered.

tldr;
The game is good, but you need to put effort into it and keep up with the community with watching a youtuber or being apart of the reddit group. It will be hard to absorb everything happening to you without someone explaining it to you.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny is an awesome game if you're willing to sink a lot of time and money into it. The worst part about the game is how incredibly difficult it is for new players to get into. Sure, the base game itself is technically free-to-play, but that doesn't mean jack friggin' squat. If you want to experience all of the content that the game has to offer, you have to be willing to fork over a fair chunk of change in order to do so. Most of if not all of the relevant content in game is tied to the three main expansions, those being Forsaken, Shadowkeep, and Beyond Light respectively. Simply put, if you don't have these three expansions, the game is basically unplayable. As of the writing of this review, purchasing all three of these expansions would cost you about $75. The fourth expansion, Witch Queen, is going to be here in a few weeks as well, and that's going to be another $30. So really, the game actually costs about $105 without tax, and is also chock full of in-game purchases for cosmetics and things like that. It's like paying for a monthly subscription for an MMO without actually paying a monthly subscription.

And then, as if the price alone wasn't enough of a deterrent, the game makes basically no effort at all to properly educate or inform new players about its various systems or mechanics. Crucial things like gear modding, bounties, triumphs, and endgame content are explained very poorly, or aren't explained at all. It's also never made apparent which campaign players are supposed to do first, or how all of these things tie in with the game's grind towards increasing your characters' power level.

If you can somehow manage to look past all of that, the game itself is actually really fun and super addictive. But, even as a D1 console vet, I honestly can't recommend the game with the way it is right now.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2... where do I begin. What a journey this game's history is. Not just talking lore either.
When this game first released, it was... rough to say the least. A grindy mess with a weak story catering to a small audience who played Destiny 1, myself included, but left every new player wondering what the heck was going on. Then the game released a few decent, but still unimpressive DLCs. Then came Forsaken. A high point in the Destiny Community. Just before Forsaken was released, I stopped playing. I was tired. The game drained me of time, money, and even fun.

I came back a year or two later, bought Forsaken and had a good time with it, but never really got back into the game. I put it aside for a while during the... Sunsetting phase of the game. Dropped it like a ton of bricks.
Never before had I been more disgusted with a game I paid for. I figured Destiny was dying because I stopped hearing about it. Until Beyond Light. I heard plenty of controversy as the old bits of the game no one really played or cared about got cut and people complained because they paid for it. I get that anger. I was angry too.

However, the updates made to the game have been fantastic. QoL is much better, grinding has been cut in half if not quartered. I can play the game for 30 minutes and STILL feel like I made progress (Very important as I have 3 children and work full time.) The story went from mediocre to intriguing and deep, and the entire gameplay experience was enhanced by making the players faster and stronger. Gone are the times of The Red War. Destiny 2 feels like a completely different game from its initial release, and I mean that in a very good way. The biggest downside I see is that the game continues to grow expensive and one has to come to terms with the fact that some parts of the game you paid for will be cut to make room for newer (and hopefully better) content.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe core mechanics of this game are fantastic, and the lore of this game is great even though sometimes the cutscenes can ruin the atmosphere the game creates with corny acting.

The grind isn't so bad as long as you have time to do so, and the free to play element at least in PVE seems viable without having to sink a lot of money into expansions unless you want to.

The issue I have with the game is the higher end PVE, and just about all aspects of PVP, most of the criticisms about the community of this game itself

In a lot of high end PVE, you will find that the community is selfish, they only want loot, they do not care about teamwork, or reviving you even if it causes a wipe, and if it's a grandmaster you will have a bad time if you LFG.

In PVP the same 3 or 4 broken weapons tend to get used, and cheaters still run rampant even after the anti-cheat was put into place. Shotguns are about all you will see in crucible now, and the community is so toxic that you should expect to get teabagged every match.

Doing raids is a pain, I have been doing them for years, and they can take 10 to 14 hours with people who refuse to help or listen..

TLDR: Playing with randos in this game with result in a bad time, the community is as toxic as they come.
If you plan on getting it, try out the free to play aspect for a week to see if it clicks before spending any money on the expansions.

Play it with friends if possible. This community is not out to help you, and this game has just become a loot lottery, the gameplay aspect is too shallow especially in PVE to keep things going right now, so just be mindful of this.\t\t\t\t\t
