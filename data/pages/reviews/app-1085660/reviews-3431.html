\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOne of the worst (if not THE WORST) new player experience in the game.

This game transformed into a whole new game since they went Free.

When it was launched, Destiny 2 had a main story campaign which is mandatory if you are going to unlock everything. Now? Everything is available from the get go.

You can buy the game, play 5 minutes of small tutorial and then join the veteran.

and THIS IS WRONG.

I am not affected with this at all, because I had 500 HOURS experience.

But for new player, you will get overwhelmed and confused.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------
Now, why I am recommending this game then?

This is one of the best COOP FPS game in the current gen.

The shooting mechanic is awesome, a lot of different builds and weapons to choose, and even with on going meta build, you are going to find many MANY different class build in the community,

The community itself are not toxic, they are willing to teach you how to play, and not expecting everyone to Raid Ready in the first couple of hours.

This game DOES NOT, and I cannot stress this enough, DOES NOT FORCE YOU to play PVP.

There are 3 main gameplay:

- PVE : Story, dungeon, raid
- PVEVP : Gambit, you are still 90% PVE, but with a touch of 10% PVP
- PVP : Crucible, pure PVP gameplay

There isn't any single moment in game that block you to progress your gameplay if you are not playing PVP.

You can be GOOD without even touching the PVP.

---------------------

How about the vaulted content? Missing content each year?

----------------
Well, I am not saying i am agree with this system, but I never touch the missing content anymore. Like, I am not gonna miss anything from it.
----------------

How about microtransaction?
----------------
100% Cosmetic. Not a single dollar can change your stats.

-------------------------------------------

In the end, it is a great game if you are willing to INVEST a lot of time and learn the basic mechanic. Does not require big brain, but a little bit of patience and friend to play.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWithin just a few months bungie has pulled this game around big time. I've been playing destiny since the taken king first came out and this is the most fun I've had with destiny in a long time. I play mainly on my ps5 so that's why I don't have a ton of hours.

Sunsetting really through me off of the game, suddenly they were going really hard into the WoW gear mentality. Which is making your gear useless every patch, or rather every year after the season where your gear comes from in destiny. This not only made my time feel wasted, but it felt like a slap to the face by bungie. At first I was optimistic about sunsetting, but after season of the arrivals I realized sunsetting isn't about reducing power creep, it was about reissuing guns to us as "new content". It wasn't about making powerful new pinnacle weapons akin to the recluse, or mountaintop. My motivation to play was at an all time low in beyond light. 50 dollars for beyond light and season of the hunt. 50 dollars they charged me. For mostly lackluster content and 40 GUNS!!! 40 GUNS in a looter!!!!! Never have I seen such a low for this game since curse of osiris.

Season of the chosen came out a few months later, it was solid. Returning strikes and new fun activities like battlegrounds and presage, all with a TON of enemies to fight. Now we really get to show off our power. But I didn't care, I didn't care how fun it was. Because I knew all the gear I grinded was going to be for nothing in a year. But then bungie shortly after announced that sunsetting was being removed from the game. Sadly all the guns before season of the worthy were still going to be sunsetted, but I don't care. My motivation to play was quickly restored and I started grinding the game again. Season of the chosen was amazing, it felt like bungie finally was getting better with their seasonal model.

Season of the splicer has been out for a bit. And yeah, it's not as good as chosen. And the new 6 man activity is kinda lame compared to battlegrounds. But THE LOOT THO. My god, if they didn't bring back vault of glass this season would be c tier for me. But vog coming back made it S. VOG is amazing man, they did such a good job making it feel just like d1 but modernizing it. The amount of content in splicer for 10 dollars is a steal. And the bright dust system has been completely fixed, ever since chosen you get a ton of dust from the season pass and weekly challenges.

Destiny is looking bright, the bungie team has expanded massively and you can tell by the amount of content and solid loot we get now in comparison to year 2 and 3. And even the beginning of year 4. I can't wait for witch queen and what lies ahead of this game. Yes, there is still a lot of work to be done. Tons of exotics suck, and the transmog system is the worst I've ever seen in a video game. But looking past the flaws, brings me the best looter game out on the market. And one of the best mmos out there as well. Not quite as good as guild wars 2 or elder scrolls online, but still good.

8/10 IGN\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny is one of my favorite franchises of all time. I've been playing since 2015 and was ecstatic when the game came to PC and even more excited when it came to Steam. I have over 1,000 hours in the game and would like to give a little insight to those getting New Light and the F2P version of the game.

For new players, the base game of Destiny and it's two expansions are A LOT of content for free. You get one full campaign with two expansion campaigns, three raids and a lot of exotics to chase. With the introduction of Armor 2.0 and things like randomly rolled armor and weapons, you have hundreds of hours to complete for free along with being able to roam other destinations.

If you've never played Destiny the New Light offering is a great way to see if you enjoy the game and want to continue playing a potentially getting the Forsaken and Shadowkeep DLCs. Forsaken was some of the best content to be released in Destiny 2 and The Last Wish raid is one of my favorite pieces of content in Destiny's history, right up there with the King's Fall raid from Destiny 1.

All in all, Destiny is a great game with a great dev team. It will have its ups and downs. It will frustrate you. You might sit the game down for a month, but I have made some of my best friendships through this game and can't wait to help out some new Guardians!

EDIT TO THE ORIGINAL:
Bungie has vaulted some content that was originally in the game so the F2P experience isn't as good as it was, but I still love Destiny. It has it's flaws, but I love the game. The endgame is very fun and it's a great game to play with friends. The community is great and there is a ton of support through Discord servers if you're looking for groups.\t\t\t\t\t
