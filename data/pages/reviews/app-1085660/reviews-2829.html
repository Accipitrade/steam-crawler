Destiny 2 is a pretty decent game, it is a looter shooter online game where you do activities in order to earn better gear and to level up. More emphasis is put on the activity themselves than on the story, which is fairly short and not that impressive.

The game puts a lot of emphasis on doing activities together as a team or in a clan, most high end activities will require you to have friends to tag along with. Though, matchmaking provides your opportunities to queue up in them alone if it hopefully pairs you up with someone. The raids are quite specific in what you need to do and not only do you need a 6 man team that will cooperate properly, you need everyone to work in tandem and in a proper manner since they are set in a way that just 1 person failing might lead to a wipe.

The game has a pretty demoralising micro-transaction scheme, since new content seems to be highly biased towards the Eververse store. Dust earning is quite tedious and slow, most items require you to pay for with actual money and the item rotation is both unpredictable and pricey. Previously, we were able to earn for items fairly well and the dust system wasn't as bad, though now it just seems like they are reusing old content( which they always do) and most newer items are locked behind silver. They may be purely cosmetic but that doesn't deter from the fact that this is a looter game and having cosmetic loot just locked behind of paywall is quite a damper on the mood.

Forsaken was quite an amazing turn of events when it launched, giving better content, story and activities. Though Shadowkeep doesn't seem to feature the same enthusiasm as it, the seasonal pass seems even worse and quite counterproductive in the sense that it'll be content that will later be wiped, not that the first season was amazing by any means. A lot of the content themselves are lost to time, locked behind paywall or time limited for the moment, meaning that you are missing out on a lot of content that just vanished if you did not get them or weren't here when they were. This would also contribute to download size accumulating for things you'll never be able to acquire.

The PvP aspect is quite unfairly balanced as well, titan's being the most dominate in terms of ease of use, with warlock and hunter trailing behind. Warlock also has an easier time while Hunters having the steepest skill curve and people pointing them to be dominate due to their "mobility" even though a person with good aim can shoot down a hunter jumping around, specially since jumping interrupts shooting. With how the OEM nerf was handled and for how long it remained up, it clearly shows that balancing is not really a huge concern. The game requiring PvP balancing this far long it's life does concern a lot of people.

The game is good, I have fun racking up my playtime in it and will keep doing so, However, the game's future seems a bit bleak and it seems like Bungie is more concerned in investing time into Eververse instead of focusing on content for the players. Previously, they stated that the monetising and micro-transaction system was cause of Activision, after the split they seemed to go full steam ahead towards this path, as such their words no longer hold any weight. Having a neutral button would've helped a lot here.

The Red War(base game), Warmind and Curse of Osiris is free though, plus you have access to all the planets. As such, it might be worth a try if this seems like your cup of tea. Forsaken is also worth it on a discount due to the amount of content it gives. As for Shadowkeep and the Seasons, only time will tell.

Update:

The game peaked during the Forsaken era, plenty of content, plenty of activities, a lot more additions and things that looked like they took effort to add in. Not to mention even as a free/base game player you could enjoy the abundance of fair bright engrams drops that would drop current season drops at a good rate. Best of all, you weren't tied down to any time gated, FOMO or seasonal pass rewards for things you paid for.

Most of those previous stuff has been wiped and now it's kind of been pretty lackluster seasonal activities and having to be committed to playing the game every season if you purchase the seasonal content as the activities will disappear later and you need to grab all the seasonal rewards before they too are gone.

The grind is pretty atrocious as well, specially the armour grind which for some stupid reason include the exotics as well. You'll spend a lot of time going for the perfect weapon perk roll or armour stat roll. Just be ready repeat activities tediously again and again.

As a free player, you feel like you'd have even less activities, places to explore, things to do and things to gain than before. Forget about the fair eververse store of yesterdays, a lot of focus is given on the cosmetics side of eververse but a lot of focus is also given on making it as frustrating for the player as much as possible without causing a full blown riot.

Even as a paid player, since most of the content can and probably will be vaulted, you too will start feeling barren. Game is basically a subscription based model game now so get used to it. If you don't wanna be tied down to a game just to be able to experience content you paid for, don't bother with this game.

Gunplay is still fun though.\t\t\t\t\t
