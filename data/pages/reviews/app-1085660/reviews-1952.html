\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit: Everything below still stands. However, Bungie is so money hungry that I cannot recommend this game. They erase expansions after three years to sell you more, sell you seasons, cosmetics and capitalize on FOMO to keep you playing. Halo Infinite is free and has its problems, but has a much better pvp if that's what you're looking for. Sorry Bungie, my wallet can't keep up with this game anymore. Bye Frosaken and Cayde, you'll be missed. I may be back, toxic relationships are hard to break.

Where to start with Destiny 2. I've been playing Destiny since 2014 and have a complicated relationship with this game. If you like: Outstanding shooting mechanics, awesome loot, superb art direction, playing with friends, feeling like a total badass space wizard/tank/stealthy boy, and GRINDING, this game is for you.
If the idea of a game that you can grind hundreds of hours in chasing that dank new piece of loot or weapon sounds appealing, then give this a try. If you're in it for the story, go play Halo, the story is waaaaay better.

The free tier of this game is now barebones.. You get one mission and access to just enough to incentive for you to buy this game. It's a ton of fun, but super expensive. $30-40 yearly expansion pass with a $10 season pass every three months and now $30 for one dungeon and rocket launcher. These passes add A LOT to the game, so if you like it, it's a win however, the season end after 3 months and you lose access to some of the events after that. I have easily spent over $200 on this franchise since it's release and don't regret a dime of it nor do I regret the hundreds of hours I have across the Xbox, Playstation and Steam. HOWEVER: $80 of the content I've paid for has disappeared from the game and I have to pay another $40 to get something to replace it just for that to disappear in 3 years. Bungie is weaponizing FOMO and you deserve better than a game that strong arms you into playing it and spending money on it.

This game can be played solo and is a blast, but when you team up with friends or randoms is where it shines. Destiny has some of the highest highs and lowest lows for me in the last 6 years of gaming, here's to hopefully another great stretch.

Word of warning for if there is ever a Destiny 3: Destiny 1 launched with barely any content and was not great. They fixed it with numerous expansions and patches and made it amazing. Bungie then did the SAME THING in Destiny 2. Charged $60 out of the gate for an unfinished product. Luckily, it looks like Destiny 2 is around for the long haul since Bungie is talking year 4,5, and 6. But if you're reading this before the launch of a possible Destiny 3, be warned.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a fun free to play multiplayer FPS (with the option of a season pass and some expansion packs to purchase should you wish too) it has easy to learn combat that is fast and fluid and a lot of fun when you start to master it.
There are loads of different types of play modes (raids/strikes/PVP etc) to help while away the hours, It has a good story with a long line of quests to do, has great graphics and some really cool looking weapons and armour to mess around with as well as hover bikes to get around the large world maps.
Then you have the exotic weapons and armour (and other fun stuff like shaders for armour and emotes) to find and play around with some having unexpected and often hilarious results.
It does have it's issues and because of those it's not a perfect 10 game, things like random (and often raid failing or PVP match losing) disconnects, having quite a few hackers in PVP and a few other minor ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ stop it from getting the top marks.
But at the end of the day all I care about is having a great time messing around in a game with my friends and having fun and with Destiny 2 I get that in spades, Thanks Bungie.

9/10\t\t\t\t\t
