\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBungie got off to a rocky start; partly because Acitivision pushed for a game with heavy microtransactions. Though, to be fair, I don't think Bungie EXECs really complained about the prospect of more money.

Having split and released their first expansion (post-split). It seems Bungie 'gets it'.

The armor rework makes it easier to keep the look you love. - Though, I really wish they'd adopt Blizzard method of it: Transmog / re-skinning equipped gear.

Game continues to run pretty smooth after the transition to Steam. Though, as of this posting, they did have a bit of trouble with the servers; seems to be mostly ironed out.

...and omg. The lore. I won't say no more, because I'll end up getting lost...again....in the grimoire.

Weapon-play feels pretty solid the added 'abilities' give you an extra dimension beyond the 'point and shoot' for a typical FPS game. - Supers are hella-fun.

The MMO-esque feel of the game really works, too. Couple that with the 'quick gearing' of an FPS, and you got yourself a game you can play with your friends when you feel like it. No need to worry "oh no, I'm gonna fall behind I HAVE to login and play".

My one major complaint is that it looks like Destiny 2 will end up being retired and Destiny 3 will take it's place. Not necessarily a bad thing, but going back to that MMO 'feel'. You might not like that your character gets wiped (or at least "power reset"); basically strip off all your sweet gear and go grab some vendor 'common-grade' gear...ya. That sucks.

I'd rather they just continue Destiny 2, maybe rename it since 'Destiny' is already associated with the first game. Then just release expansions and technical improvements over the years; a Destiny [true-]MMO!\t\t\t\t\t
