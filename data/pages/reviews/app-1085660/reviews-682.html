\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE (March 22, 2022): Absolutely do not get this game if you're still stuck using Windows 7. While they claim to support Windows 7, it appears that this support has essentially been dropped recently (Starting with Season of the Lost). The game was unplayable for the first 3 weeks of Season of the Lost and hasn't been playable for the entirety of Season of the Risen (currently one month in). They have made no statements regarding the outage and given their response to last season, paying players will not be reimbursed. Keep in mind that non-repeatable events have been missed due to these outages meaning the associated loot/rewards cannot be obtained.

If someone has the drive storage space and are just looking for a brief FPS romp through some visually appealing terrain, I would recommend the free version of Destiny 2, sure. The gun-play is enjoyable and the control is generally tight and the voice acting and score is generally very good (some fantastic songs, some very grating/irritating songs - based on personal taste). If they are looking for an MMO to sink some time into, I definitely would not recommend paying for it. It is also a game that much of the content can be enjoyed by players more interested in running solo.
I should point out that I started as a free player (New Light), but bought both the Forsaken and Shadowkeep expansions shortly after starting (on sale).
From a game-play perspective the number of activities are relatively limited and can get boring/tedious quickly despite some of the beautiful environments. There is, or was, essentially no tutorial so there will be a very steep learning curve, though you can just go out and start shooting things once you figure out how to travel. There really isn't any in-game chat and no in-game LFG system so you won't find much help in-game. Also, there is no way to play through the original story content (or the Forsaken and Shadowkeep content for that matter, which I purchased...) so you won't know what's going on without spending a lot of time reading or watching gameplay online. In fact, the bulk of the current story (where ever it's at when you jump into the game) is not replayable.
Initially, you will spend a lot of time levelling and the levelling process can be confusing if you're not familiar with the game. Levelling is essentially based on random drops as your power level is based on the power level of you're equipment. This will require you to continually farm for higher level drops. There are 3 different classes available. Despite each class having access to the same weapons, their abilities differ enough that I think they are all worth using. Each class will need to be levelled separately. Initially, this will likely be enjoyable as you'll be able to explore much of the game content right away. Ultimately, though, you'll be running those 3 classes, assuming you level all of them, through the same limited activities, lost sectors, bounties and strikes over and over and over again. In part due to the rewards for these activities being quite poor. This grind is also be tied to farming items having stats determined randomly when they drop. It can get extremely grindy...
End-game content is limited to PvP, high level strikes and a handful of dungeons and raids, though you can continue running the other activities, lost sectors, bounties and strikes and farming for improved gear. Some of these activities have options for higher difficulty levels...though the rewards seem far from compensatory for the highest levels. You will need a separate application to find groups for dungeons and raids and will also almost certainly want to use it for high level strikes.
The 'Seasons' that you pay for (regularly released DLC), periodically add content (Several hours worth of new story-rated gameplay, a handful of weapons/items, etc.). Some of this content is available to New Light (free) players, though they will not have access to any class upgrades or similar features which may limit their performance in high-level content or possibly PvP.
As to PvP, I can't provide much insight having spent probably less than 10-15 hours playing the 2 modes. I found the PvP in Destiny 2 to be a clausterphobic unbalanced mess (Gambit being a little 'roomier' than Crucible). There didn't seem to be much player balance when I tried, so be prepared to be matched with highly skilled players when you're first starting out. My guess is that this is to reduce wait times... I can only image how annoying PvP is with the freezing and &%$*(#^ ice walls due to the recently introduced Stasis aspects.
ONE IMPORTANT WARNING: in the past, the developer has seen fit to remove large swathes of equipment from the game. This includes items players may have spend MANY hours farming for, or even paying cash to obtain. They have also removed story/expansion content from the game that players have paid for. For example, they have removed entire planets/maps from the game (this includes all the related activities, lost sectors, bounties, strikes, dungeons and raids). In view of this, I wouldn't recommend spending a lot of time farming for the best gear or spending money on in-game purchases for fear of the items acquired may be removed in the future.
During a recent update (Season of the Lost), the developer broke the game for a portion of the player-base (unable to play the game for ~3 weeks). Paying players lost 3 weeks of time-gated rewards and there was no compensation.
In summary, I definitely wouldn't recommend this game as an MMO to sink a lot of time and money into...and you can sink a lot of time and money into it. I think it would be fine, though, for those that just want a free FPS 'quick fix' given that much of the free content is immediately available and can be enjoyed as a single player.\t\t\t\t\t
