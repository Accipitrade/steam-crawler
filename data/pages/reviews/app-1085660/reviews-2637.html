SH11T, however, has really been going downhill since late Shadowkeep-Beyond Light and ever since the game started lazily regurgitating very specific weapons that they defiantly retired against all human reasoning, I am darn tooting mad.
And now that Bungo's MaGnUm OpUs DeStInUm has fumbled miserably in meeting my low and humble expectations, I refuse to let this outdated review suffer its excruciatingly irrelevant existence any longer.
8.618 hours (on Steam, mind you) seem enough; let that be the end of this.
I wish I had enrolled in a language course or two 8,6K hours ago--then by now I could have been many an egregiously nostalgia-exploiting recycled hand cannon farming away from being able to semi-fluently flirt with at least a couple more nationalities of charming ladies and gents.

I almost never write reviews but Destiny 2 compelled me to put some words down.If you have an addictive personality, Destiny 2 will consume your life. The worst part of that is, you won't even care it does. What's really the best about this game is the variety of content that will appeal to most types of gamers who look for specific kind of gameplay. This is the third year since release, and Destiny 2 has evolved so much to satisfy so many different types of cravings.

If you are the more competitive type, D2 has PvP modes so you can show your prowess against the most vicious and cruel opponents you will face in this entire game: other players. If you are all about hunting down alien monsters to reclaim Earth and beyond for humanity, PvE indeed is the most supported and active mode with tons of contents including strikes and raids. Co-op is strong with this game but those with social anxiety who struggle to play with strangers on the internet may want to know that solo play is entirely enjoyable once you obtain some adequate gears and get a bit proficient at the game.

For those who can't decide which you like better, D2 has this heavenly mode called Gambit, very strategic, intense, fast-paced, run by a mysteriously charming host who offers some pretty random "food" for thought before chucking you onto the battlefield to fight both the usual monsters and opposing guardians at the same time. (Yeah yeah Gambit is quite a polarizing mode in Destiny 2 but I personally found this extremely enjoyable especially if you meet teammates who really understand the mechanics and actually play to win.)

It's very easy to find people to play with, even in modes that don't offer matchmaking, through active online communities that offer fireteam searches as well as the official companion app. I've found the Destiny 2 player community mature and supportive for the most part (with the exception of hackers) whether you are in a clan or not. It's not hard to find a clan that suits you, and if you do join one, you will not only receive in game perks and items but you will have other experienced players to guide you through the difficult endgame activities such as raids, and other like-minded players to enjoy specific activities with.

D2 also have several mechanics that I found so addictive and very very rewarding. You and I and all other gamers whose middle name is Grinding, this game is literally made for the likes of us. There are so many weapons and gears with randomly rolled stats and perks (of which there are so many) that you will have no problem finding yourself grinding dozens of hours just to get a god-rolled weapon/armor that you like. This game will also help you embrace the inner farmer inside you and you will find that there are tons of consumables, materials, and in-game currency to farm and harvest to infinity.

If you are an achievement hunter like me, you can say goodbye to your life because although Destiny 2 has a relatively small number of Steam achievements to complete, the in-game Triumph system is massive and a good chunk of them are actually very difficult to achieve, meaning you will easily be conditioned to grind and farm for just one specific triumph, just to experience that 3 seconds of overwhelming sweet sense of achievement. In case I sound sarcastic, that is not my intention. The triumphs and gears that you grind for are extremely rewarding if you are the hunting type.

The amount of items that Destiny 2 challenges you to collect is just mind boggling. Just to add, it's not just the gears (weapons, armors, related mods...) you can hunt down; you've got interstellar ships and land transportation vehicles to farm, in addition to 'shells' for your in-game companion Ghost and SHADERS! So many shaders. Any fashionista will have no problem finding the color combo that they deserve. I personally love to collect useless stuff, so naturally I love farming for shaders. It's nice to look good while you kill stuff.

Another allure of Destiny 2 (actually the entire Destiny universe for that matter) is the LORE. Oh my. If you a lore type of person, you will not be disappointed. The Destiny universe has a fascinating stories to share with you, ranging from feats of glorious interstellar conquests, chronicles of bitter vengeance that span centuries, and nostalgic reminiscence of broken hearts... I can personally sit and read these pieces of Destiny's lore for hours. And YES you have to hunt pieces of stories too in game!! (Alternatively you could go to a wiki or YouTube but where's the fun in that.) What makes them even better is that the lore is often enigmatic and open-ended, leaving it to the community's speculations, imagination, and creative interpretations. The Destiny lore community boasts many great historians who attempt to decrypt the stories of the universe and they are such pleasure to read and listen to.

In addition to the lore, the Destiny universe is visually stunning as well. The game's scenic atmosphere is actually a major part of what kept me coming back when I first played it. It's true that you would eventually get used to it and it becomes less impressive as you become more focused on getting stuff done. Still, even hundreds of hours later, I sometimes randomly stop in the middle of the open world, look around, and just marvel. So visually pleasing (some maps/destinations more so than others).

I can go on rambling, just enumerating nice things about Destiny 2, but every moment I spend writing this review is every moment less I get to spent playing it. So I will now go away with a few very obvious drawbacks of the current state Destiny 2 is in.

First is the rampant disorder caused by shameless cheaters and hackers. This is a heavily PvP related issue, but it's not hard to find egregious cheaters in Gambit and even in strikes as well. I mostly play on the Asian server and here this is such a glarinig issue that Bungie doesn't seem to handle very well. (It's not that other regions/servers are spared from hackers; I just don't know enough about them to evaluate.) Hacks such as instant respawn, unlimited ammo and super charges are an instant match breaker in PvP. I tend to stay far from PvP unless necessary just because of the cheaters. Maybe it's because Destiny 2 tend to put less focus on PvP than on PvE that this problem has not been addressed more adequately... which brings me to the next point. PvP receives significantly less content than PvE, especially when considering that, according to user statistics, a fair chunk of users at any given moment is engaging in a PvP activity. People seem to enjoy PvP a lot and yet they seem always left hungry for more. I do think PvE should be the main focus of Bungie's limited resources, and frankly there are alternatives to Destiny 2 if your main attraction to it is PvP. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is surprise of the yer for me. It shines both as a single player experience doing quests, side quests, exploring instanced lost sectors and running strikes with random people or as end game raiding fun with friends - and raids here are really intense. There is also a piece of cake for PvP players, who can enjoy either pure dethmatches or mixed experience, where you play both agaist environment and other team.
Game core is looter shooter with amazing gunnplay, where you, as a gun wielding technomage stanfd against alien hordes to save last remains of humanity, taking shelter in small zones on earth and in outposts throughout our solar system. And if I say amazing gunplay it is not an understatement, you can feel distinc weight and impact of each gun. Most of the guns you can modify to your play style and guns have also unique characteristics called traits, so there is always something interesing waiting to be looted.
With 120hrs currently on record I still just barely scratching the surface of the huge amount of content. There is about a dozen of locations / planets and each one is filled with quests and activities.
Talking about quests, it is not the usual chore - meet the NPC, kill some monsters and bring me some worthless item. Quests here are narrated stories taking you both into a familiar and new amazing location and each contributing to general lore about the game story or location history.
It's also rpg, you have character stats, you have weapons and armour, you are levelling your character. And all this comes with a grind.
