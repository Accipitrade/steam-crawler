\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Its a great game with a beautiful universe marred with questionable development choices and a highly repetitive gameplay loop. It's my favorite game and I hate it.

Original: Destiny 2 is highly representative of what Bungie itself has become. A shell of its former self. Since launch, D2 has been packed with bugs and questionable development choices that make it a challenge to play even when there ISN'T a game breaking bug currently in the game. The universe of Destiny is slam packed with lore and (formerly) interesting and memorable characters, both of which Bungie loves to ignore, rewrite, undermine, or kill off to fit whatever sort of theme or mood they are currently trying to set in the game. The worst part of this is that even after killing off Cayde 6 years ago, they have yet to remove his voice lines from several activities which only serves as a stark reminder of how good the game could have been. The Forsaken DLC was the pinnacle of D2 and now with the upcoming launch of The Witch Queen DLC, they will be removing all previous content from previous DLC's from before the Beyond Light DLC while providing no incentive or reparations to those who have purchased those DLC's that are being removed. In other words, you will no longer be able to access or play that content that you purchased in any capacity. Bungie continues to take actions that leave the community baffled and upset and yet the long time players like myself just can't cut off the bad fruit. We keep coming back and Bungie in turn believes this means they have a "loyal" fan base. The truth of the matter is that most of us just see the potential in the universe and pray that one day Bungie will manage to make something amazing from it. In the mean time its quite a lot like watching a train wreck in slow motion; terrifying, sad, epic, costly, and we can't look away.

EDIT 1: It's worth noting that I continue to play this game in spite of the fact that my opinion remains the same. The game play loop is the same, the rewards are the same, the game is very much unchanging. The new "ability 3.0" system is nice until you realize there is a set meta that dictates what you must use for any given end game activity. It is the illusion of choice. I played considerably less during the Witch Queen expac than I have in the past simply because the game bores me. That being said I will continue to ride the D2 train to the end. I will continue to watch the story unfold with baited breath, waiting for the day the game finally realizes its potential. Here's hoping for great things in Lightfall.

Edit 2: At the time of this edit it is three days until the launch of Lightfall, I will be revisiting this review once again after I play a bit of that DLC but for now I wanted to add some to my previous comments. I realize that some of my comments were fueled by emotion previously but I won't delete them, I stand by what I said and my opinion of the game, at large, remains unchanged. That being said, I still love Destiny. It has remained one of my go to games for years and although the gameplay loop is unchanged there is comfort in knowing I can always come back to a beautiful universe full of space magic, guns and hordes of enemies to slaughter. When competitive shooters have me raging, coming to Destiny to slaughter a Nightfall Strike is the perfect balance to the sweaty competition of other shooters. Maybe I'm just feeling a bit nostalgic after the final scene of Season of the Seraph but I'm excited for this game in a way I haven't been in years.\t\t\t\t\t
