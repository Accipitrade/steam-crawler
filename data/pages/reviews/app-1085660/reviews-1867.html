\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTbh, I would've reccomended this game to others had Bungie actually gone in a different way, I think this game could've been improved. I began playing during the start of Season of the Worthy, or the Bounty Simulator. And, I had recently built my pc, with 8GB of memory on my graphics card, and new to pc gaming in general, I wanted to play and test my computer out. But, I then spent about $15 on the Indian market for Season of the Worthy, and I spent it to it's fullest. I played once everyday, trying to get the season pass used to it's fullest. I finished in about 60 days into the season, so I stopped and began playing Mordhau and whatnot. But, with the new season, and their refusal to offer the deluxe edition, I feel as though I shouldn't spend more money on this game. If you love "shloot" games, like Borderlands, this is your go to game. But if you don't like wasting your time on hours to farm for random odds, fight against hackers, play a game with no anticheat. I don't want to play this game anymore because Bungie, all they did was just add more bounty sources to farm. There's like 10-15 people that give bounties, just for XP and their faction stuff. The reason why I loved D1 was because there was a ring/charm to it where I wanted to try and play it to it's fullest. Quest for exotics that were unknown. But I can't in D2. I know what all the exotics are, and they're a misery to obtain. If you wan't to do something and drown yourself in repetitive actions or farming, that is your own sorrows, this is your go to game. It's irritating, so I don't think the casual person would like this game.\t\t\t\t\t

It's not a game... It's a lifestyle.

Updated 2/2023

I have been playing Destiny on PS3 since the first Destiny in the Beta.

Destiny has seen its good days, and its bad days.

From patrolling the cosmodrome in D1 to taking on the vault of glass all the way to stopping Eramis from destroying the Traveler using Rasputin.

2019-2020 has been a terrible year for Destiny 2. With limited-time events, missable story bits, FOMO-based grinds and broken PVP Metas. As well as boring bounty fests with very little rewards. Thankfully this is... kind of a thing of the past. My gripes with this year (2021) is instead of removing the seasonal content at the end of each season, they keep it till the next expansion (The Witch Queen) comes out. Which, fair for the seasonal model, is unfair for the player who loves lore and story. The activities themselves are rather copy-paste, but the storylines we got this year have been top tier for the destiny universe. All 4 seasons have expanded the Destiny universe immensely. To see all this go away from being playable is pretty heartbreaking. I hear the argument "Just watch it on YouTube" get tossed around by the rabid fanboys but its just not the same as playing it and experiencing it yourself.

(2/2023) The Witch Queen has been the BEST expansion for Destiny 2 so far! The storytelling is on point, the loot is great, and the seasonal stories have been amazing, although I still think Beyond Light's seasonal stories where better. Season 19's finale was the perfect send-off for Year 5. Weapon crafting is a flawed system that's thankfully being re-worked in Lightfall. But it's a fantastic way of creating your own weapon with enhanced stats and perks! The new dungeons where phenomenal! The downsides of The Witch Queen though, all the seasonal content will be vaulted on Lightfall's launch, but the exotic missions will cycle on a weekly basis. Overall, The Witch Queen shows Destiny in its best state, though seasonally not as great as Beyond Light, it was still a very good year.

Destiny and Destiny 2 have had controversy after controversy, rabid, white knighting fanboys and absolute haters.Yeah, i'll be buying every expansion for this game and playing it. This game is *my* World of Warcraft, FF14, Runescape, etc. I'm gonna declare bullshayza where its needed.

Case in point, the DCV (Destiny Content Vault)
Removing content on a yearly basis is generally not a good thing. Newcomers to the franchise won't get to experience the content that was here before. This creates a "had to be there" attitude and that's not great for long term storytelling. Imagine watching Breaking Bad, but after season 2, season 3 is removed, but season 4 is there. And you're watching season 4 without knowing the events of season 3. Was that the wrong thing to do? You're ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ right it is.

THINGS YOU SHOULD KNOW BEFORE HOPPING IN AS A FREE PLAYER (A New Lighter):

This is not "free to play" it is "Free to start". You get to sample all the stuff in the game, the locations and whatnot. Although you will miss out on Raids, Dungeons, Campaigns, certain loot, and Lore. You essentially get the PvP mode, which is dominated by people who own the expansions and season passes. Gambit, which is the worst mode in all of Destiny and i will die on that hill. And the Vanguard Strikes playlist. 3 player missions running point A to B killing things along the way up to the boss. Basic content for the low low price of free. Although, if you enjoy the game, check out keyseller sites and watch for steam sales for the expansions currently out.

I've rambled on long enough, so heres my final verdict

As a day 1, server opening launch in 2014, it's both hard to see what Destiny has become and great to see it expanding. If you truly want to experience Destiny 2, download the free to start version, play around a bit. and if you like it, consider buying it. If you try it out and dont like it, it didnt hurt your wallet. this game is not for everyone.

Thanks for reading my rambles.\t\t\t\t\t
