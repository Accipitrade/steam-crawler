\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game, itself, is great. The scenery is beautiful, the world building is great, you can get very attached to some characters, and the gameplay is usually fun. Weapons are mostly balanced, with some very fun and useful ones being added into the mix, and enemies scale with your ability so that you can't return to a previous area and just demolish everything. If you're into lore, this game does have quite a bit of it, although it's not really hidden; just complete triumphs and you've got stuff to read.
But there's a few huge issues that I've had (emphasis on "I") that makes it a little difficult to recommend.

First off, the matchmaking in PvP games doesn't really work. Rather than pitting you against people of the same ability, it feels like Bungie just throws people together and calls it a day. This means that I could be against people who are Flawless (people who get streaks on the hardest PvP challenge out), leading to me getting shot and shot and shot again. If I've got just 20 hours on this game, I shouldn't be put against those who've been playing for years. (Even while playing it more, I still struggle a lot of PvP)

Then, the handling of multiplayer is a bit odd. With some research, I've found that this is apparently the worst time to actually join the game, with how dodgy the intro sequence is. I'm not sure how true this is, since I haven't seen the others, but I can say this: The game is odd in the way it handles multiplayer during the introduction.
My first mission was to get to a jumpship and escape the somewhere to the Tower. Here, you have to fight Fallen and then defeat a boss, suddenly being introduced to some others, which is fine, essentially a mini-strike. From then, unless you're doing Vanguard / Gambit / Crucible, you can pretty much forget there's others around you. There's a couple of activities where you work as a team, but that's it.
That is, until, you get to the Power Level "soft cap". After this, you're immediately reminded that this is a multiplayer game, and you're basically forced into playing AGAINST others. And for me, this jump was very jarring. I don't typically play games that are PvP, but the game suddenly requests you do so in such way, that I felt bad for not trying. Thankfully I'm not as anxious about doing PvP, but I'm still awful. Especially with my previous problem.

So TL;DR: Beautiful game, but multiplayer matchmaking has to be worked on.\t\t\t\t\t
