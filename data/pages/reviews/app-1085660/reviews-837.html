It's a great game to play with friends (especially if you have a group big enough to raid with) but I mostly enjoy playing it solo. It has beautiful environments to explore, great-feeling gunplay, and a gameplay loop that can go for as long as you want it to.

Low-key, if you like getting high and playing single player games, give Destiny a try.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t-Sound design is absolutely stellar
-Soundtrack is on par with Halo
-Lore is vast, interesting, and has many youtubers who read lore on their channel, makes for good background noise
-The feel of the game, jumping, shooting, doing literally anything feels buttery smooth and well made
-Cheaters run rampant in Competitive PVP (as of June 3 2021), however there are rumors of implementing a stronger anti cheat.
-updates are slow to roll out, mostly because of the dated game engine bungie uses, and not because the devs don't listen. Because of this, the game frequently breaks every few weeks or months, but not in a terrible game breaking way, more like a "that's annoying" kind of way. A little community joke here is always Blaming the bugs on the Exotic Telesto because that gun commonly breaks the game because of how it functions.
-Every 3 ish months, a new "season" starts. New Gear is available, and some of the old gear either goes away or is unobtainable, but recently, all seasonal content is supposed to stay until the next big Autumn expansion
-Along with the season, a massive "sandbox" update happens, which means the devs make certain guns/abilities good or bad. almost every season fixes the last seasons issues, but then presents its own issues that plague the game until the next season. overall, the game breaking issues are fixed rather quick.

The 3 month cycle of 4 seasons is absolutely infuriating, as a common cycle is: Trash Season with bad sandbox -> Good season with ok sandbox -> Ok season with Good Sandbox -> Big expansion with garbage sandbox, large power grind, new destination/raid, maybe dungeon, overall good season with new stuff to do but your guns are horribly imbalanced for either 3 to 6 months.

I absolutely hate this cycle, BUT, in the long run it turns out to be a 2 steps forward, 1 step back type deal, until they leap 10 steps forward and stumble on their ass backwards 3 steps on the landing. Over 7 years, they really do listen, and the game is very well made, but the slow speed all boils down to the very dated engine Destiny uses, which literally takes 6 hours to start and shut down.

You WILL have a love/hate relationship with this game and it is incredibly addicting.
11/10 I hate it\t\t\t\t\t
