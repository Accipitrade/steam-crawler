\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a weird game. It's a looter shooter with great gunfeel and some really interesting gun ideas coupled with amazingly fun space-wizard nonsense.

It's also a game that seems to be perpetually at war with itself. The last big sandbox change added a subclass that appears to have been designed with only PvE in mind (because it destroyed PvP). That subclass was then tuned with PvP in mind at the expense of PvE. Entire planets and storylines and activities that must have taken tens of thousands of person-hours to create are just gone, with really very little to replace them. The concept of putting expiration dates on your old loot was introduced because it would make space for new exciting loot. Except that that loot never arrived, so now your old loot is in the loot pool again so you can grind for the stuff you already had with a new, later expiration date on it.

I've spent a few hundred hours in D2, and I /have/ have had a lot of fun in that time. I have enough friends who have played that I have done Crucible and Gambit and Strikes and Raids and Menagerie. We still jump in occasionally to look at new things, but only briefly, as though we were visiting the zoo to look at exotic birds. It's not that the game is unfun, it's that it is an absolute mess right now. To play D2 is to be utterly bewildered trying to work out what the developers are trying to accomplish.

It might be the case that D2 is in some transitional state where it will become great in the future, but if you're not already playing with a group, I can't recommend jumping in now.\t\t\t\t\t
