If you want to play, do not spend a single dollar. You'll regret it later. If you can get like, 3-4 DLCs for $10, it's maybe worth it, probably better to just buy yourself a Chipotle burrito so you can enjoy something.

The game is NOT free to play as it seems. It's "free to try." The amount of content you get for free admittedly seems like a lot, but when you realize how much you're missing, that's when it really sucks. The game is arguably pay-to-win now as well, with the best weapons being locked behind content that you need to purchase.

I have a few thousand hours in the game, I REALLY want to enjoy the game, but everything they are doing is ruining it for me. They want to "bring the challenge back to Destiny", which I totally agree would be great, but they decide to make all the simple content that you can (and sometimes have to) run hundreds of times irritating now. If you don't have a fireteam of semi-skilled players, it'll be painful and long. If you are doing it with two random people, chances are the activity will take over twice as long as normal, and again to reiterate, you have to do these activities... Hundreds. Of. Times.

I cannot, in good conscience, recommend this game to anyone. If you want to play a real looter-shooter, you would be much better of buying Borderlands when it's on sale for $10 and playing that with some friends.\t\t\t\t\t

I was near getting a bunch of exotics, almost done with wendigo, didn't realize a bunch of that would go away - but besides the weeks of lost progress, I've been trying to remain positive, working through shadowkeep (and realizing that two totally different quests in a row took us to the exact same location with the exact same pattern, boss in the same place just a 'different' boss), then getting to europa super psyched and...

glimmer extraction

i don't know, wasn't glimmer extraction like.. the first public event on destiny?? i honestly felt kind of insulted and sad, i had worked a long time for all those grenade kills, lose my progress, lose entire planets I paid for and.. for what? to play the exact same public event I've played for years... in snow? and coming from a moon planet to a snow planet, it's kinda.. gray?

outside of technical bug reports i try not to whine about stuff too much, i have worked in tech and know how hard things are, but i feel like destiny has entirely embraced being 'free to play' and i feel like we spent SO MUCH money, literally 150 dollars and we're kind of bored(cause we lost our progress) a couple days in. Besides this I've been getting my old consoles out of storage and collecting retro games

do you know how much retro gaming 150 bucks can get me? i just.. idk. it actually emotionally upset me lol, I feel hurt. I've been through a lot of buggy launches but I haven't really ever felt betrayed before. I know it's the 'start' of the season but like.. why wouldn't you at LEAST want to make the new planet feel.. new?

recently we also bought Borderlands 3. The gunplay isn't as tight, but it's goofy and fun and there feels like there's endless content, we played through two times without even a thought about it and we've never repeated a game together before. it just kind of rubs it in.. I would love to come back and change this review later (I'm going to try and get my money's worth, maybe get better at pvp) but right now I felt I had to let people know that it's just not a good use of their money until they add more.

And even then, they need to do more than reskinning enemies and reusing locations, they should make progression and exploration feel rewarding, not empty.\t\t\t\t\t
