\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhen Bungie decides to address the rampant cheating in PvP and especially trials, I'll change my recommendation and reinstall the game. Until such time, your time will be better spent going outside.

Edit: I have subsequently reinstalled the game since Bungie made independent verification required to play comp or trials. I mean, know what you're getting into; a game with hundreds of weapons with random rolls, each with a unique set of perks, and a massive system of armor stats to modify those perks also with random stat rolls and plenty of one-hit-kill special weapons that feel cheap when you're on the receiving end and a shocking amount of aim assist means PvP will never feel as balanced as we all want it to be but that does not make it any less Got. Dam. Infuriating. when you get one-shot by some camping plebe across the map playing on controller who need merely move a thumbstick in your general direction and the game is like "Oh, did you mean to place the reticle RIGHT HERE, ON YOUR OPPONENT'S STUPID FACE? SAY NO MORE FAM".

This is why I main Jotunn. If I suck ass at aiming to the point where a game with brutal aim assist and the hand cannon with the highest AA in the game + two targeting mods on my helmet means I average 50% precision accuracy, then I'm very well going to pick the gun that aims for me instead. Imagine if you're halfway good at aiming, what chance to the rest of us have? Oh and stop putting your TTV in your name ffs. Your streaming career is not going to take off.

Once every three or four months or so I get so tilted in PvP that I rage-uninstall the game and do something shockingly productive with the extra free time like remodel my kitchen (true story, ask me for pictures, it's gorgeous), to remind myself that I could be a valuable and productive member of society if it weren't for this monumental timesuck which has so accurately localized the mesocortolimbic pathway of my brain that I find myself utterly incapable of pulling away lest I risk losing that daily dopamine hit.

A final thought: I would like to point out that whenever I am not playing Destiny I usually just alt-tab out of the game and leave it running in the background so I can easily boot back into it when I do come back to it. However Steam probably counts this as "played hours" while it's still running. I add this in the hopes that anybody reading this, while readily acknowledging that I am indeed a loser, also acknowledges that I am perhaps not as shocking of a loser as the "hours played" might imply seeing as "130 hours in the last two weeks" suggests that I spent like 50% of all available spacetime, day/night/sleep/wake/work or otherwise, playing Destiny. This is only a partial truth and I want the record to reflect it.

0/10 would not recommend, it's my favorite game.\t\t\t\t\t
