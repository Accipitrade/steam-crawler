\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like running around and shooting aliens this is one of the better games for it. Great visuals and sound design, satisfying weapons and abilities, and smooth gameplay with responsive controls. There's a whole lot of content available for free at this point and the expansions are a good value. (I bought the game full price at launch and bought each expansion as it came out; I feel like I got a good value for my money at each purchase.)

Be aware that this game has an MMO-style gear grind at its heart. There are lots of events and adventures to play through and the gameplay is very satisfying from moment to moment, but ultimately there is a lot of repetition to get random drops as you slowly hone your arsenal one perk or stat point at a time. I enjoy the grind for what it is but it will definitely get tedious if you're not into it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGoing onto almost 9 years since playing Destiny from the Open Beta days on my PS3, its sad to see the game that essentially turned my life around after depression back in high school, going to the state of the game of how it is. This used to be the game that made the sad times go away, it created friendships and bonds that wont ever be broken again, had become a shell of itself. The gun-play is like no other game out there that tries to mimic its play style, may have attempted, nearly all have failed, some are still around today. The State of the Game posted on August 3rd, 2023, has confirmed to me that the game I once knew and loved, that saved my life, is no more. I have no further inclination to play this game for the loot or the sheer amount of fun I used to have, as the passion I once felt behind the development team is no more, has become and obligation to them to maintain, instead of the drive to make and play a game that you know people will love.

This game is just not worth playing anymore. New Lights have to spend around $180 to get the full experience of what this game has to offer, while games like Final Fantasy XIV are starting to have previous expansions be free to play forever as new ones come out, with previously seasonal content no longer being there once the latest expansion releases. Destiny 2 has become a game you are obligated to play, not for fun, or to catch up and play with some friends you met in-game, but as a job, to make sure you keep up with a lackluster story that has pay offs 1 week before the new expansion comes out that takes an entire year to get to its climax. Do yourself a favor, and move on. They no longer care about this game, and why should you if they don't anymore.\t\t\t\t\t
