\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere is so much to say about this game.
It is a lot of fun. It has a ton of content and awesome gun play.
I first bought Destiny 2 Deluxe edition when it first came out on PC via the Battle.Net launcher.
Content was rather limited at the time and I forgot about the game for a while, until it came to Steam but did not play for too long.
Finally, I returned this year (2020) and have been enjoying it the most since launch. Note that I did finally purchase the expansions.
I am enjoying this game even more now as I play it with my 9 yo.
We are having a hell of a good time with this game, although of course it's not without frustrations sometimes.
It has a lot of issues too but overall if you like shooters and RPG/looting elements this could give you great satisfaction.

Recommendations to new players:
- Definitely join a clan and community that has a Discord. Here is a link to the clan I joined recently, which is full of wonderfully chill people and welcoming to new players: Clan's Discord
- PvP can feel brutal, between cheaters and pro players. Matchmaking can feel very random. I still find it fun, you just have to adjust your expectations accordingly :)
- There are tons of really nice people happy to help new players in PvE
- The Destiny 2 mobile app is also a nice and easy way to find players to join you to help with challenging stuff or simply share the fun.
- Take a moment to study the weapon perks system.
- Don't be ashamed of looking up stuff online: some of the quests are simply obscure and there is a good chance developers had to leak the how-tos themselves.
- If you are subject to FOMO, this game might not be for you, unless you have a lot of free time to dedicate to it.

Now a few suggestions to the developers (just insert the word "Please" before each point):
- Remove the limit on how many kinds of shaders you can "carry". Color options shouldn't be linked to items that take up inventory space.
- Overhaul the low-level loot. I find myself consuming 100% of the rare items (blue), as probably 99% of players do. Why not either give us less or simply give us the corresponding resources rather than forcing us to manually consume low-level loot for resources? Not only that but when playing PvP the blues fill my inventory to the point that purples end up being sent straight to the postmaster. Maybe give us an option to auto-consume blues?
- Introduce an Armory/Loadout system: right now it is a pain to change configurations. You have to carry a bunch of armor and weapons to address various in-game situations.
