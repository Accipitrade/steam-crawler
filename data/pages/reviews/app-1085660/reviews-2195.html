\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's the age-old story of a potentially great game in the hands of a suboptimal developer, or at least one who makes a lot of bad decisions.
The core of Destiny 2 is really fun and I do generally enjoy my time with it, but there are so many infuriating problems that seem to have existed for a long time now without ever having been adressed as far as I can tell that I can't really in good conscience recommend it, at least not without several caveats.
First of all, the game HATES explaining anything at all. I can live with that with regards to the story and lore. It's unfortunate, but there's websites and videos for that (although there shouldn't have to be).
It stops being quirky when it comes to mechanics and systems not being explained anywhere. You know how most games bore you with lengthy tutorials regarding stuff you already know? Well, Destiny 2's very few tutorials bore you by not being very helpful and then stop short of explaining the stuff that would need explaining. My first round of Gambit, the PvE/PvP Hybrid mode, was an absolute mess because the game wouldn't really tell me what was going on before or during the game. Same goes for most reward systems, although less bad. The pinnacle of this was probably when I finally learned after 40ish hours that "Main Quest" was NOT actually the game's main story, but just their way of dragging new F2P players through their game modes. The ACTUAL main story has been given to a random NPC with no hint or explanation and loses any dramatic effect since the game makes sure you know how everything turns out in the end before you can actually play it. I had to look up a lot of mechanics, how certain events or quests worked or just terms the game uses and even then always learned new things, which sounds good unless you learn absolute basics after already having played for 50 hours because they aren't mentioned anywhere, like certain control options.

Then there's the mess that is Strikes (basically short dungeons). Specifically, Strike bounties, which are daily missions that contribute heavily to your season pass progress. They usually require that you kill enemies a certain way. This might not sound bad, but the problem is this: Only the last hit counts. So in a co-op mode, you are encouraged to try and snag every kill for yourself. This leads to most players trying to abandon their team in order to get these as you will have to share kills otherwise and your bounties could take a bit longer that way. This of course leads to a split group in a mode where you usually shouldn't split up and you either get left behind and try to catch up or everyone dies. You don't have much fun either way. It baffles me how a fundamentally cooperative mode has been hit with such competitive design and this complaint exists for quite a while now.

PvP doesn't seem very smart either as most people appear to run around with weapons that I have no idea how to get and usually would take a while to obtain. You aren't useless without them, but it still stinks. Also, snipers seem to be the dominant weapon of choice which I happen to dislike so tough luck to me I guess.

Furthermore, this game throws a LOT of quests at you. You can lose your overview rather quickly. Most of them aren't interesting, just a lot of very specific grinding, which I don't mind but would like it to be listed better, like with filters for example. Instead, we got a shared limit on how many quests and daily/weekly bounties you can have at any given time. I seriously can't figure out the benefit of this as you still always have the option to abandon and later retake quests, so if you like the limit, just stick to it yourself. Why on earth would you enforce it for everyone?

Lastly, the fundamental gameplay complaint I have is that I often take damage or just immediately die and am not sure why/from which source that came. There isn't always an obvious indicator either of what exactly killed you or of what you should have done about it. I don't know if this is just down to me having to "git gud" or understanding what the game is trying to tell me better, but if that's the case, the game sure is a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ communicator. I also often don't care for the platforming. Movement is usually fun, but most of the platforming is very uneccesary and just costs time without being much of a challenge.

All in all, very fun core, but so many baffling design choices that annoy me every session and make this quite a rollercoaster between addicting grind and hovering over the uninstall button. Definitely not a bad game, but the flaws annoy me too much to give it a thumbs up, even though I'd really like to.\t\t\t\t\t
