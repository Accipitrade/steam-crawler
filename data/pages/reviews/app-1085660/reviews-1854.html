\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnother one of those many games that is great for the first 50 or so hours until you get to the endgame content where they go out of your way to make the experience as misreable as possible. The whole level tied to armor things seemed like an ok idea until crossing into the "pre-endgame" grind (1000->1050) where the pace slows dramatically. Everything is still "theoretically" possible to accomplish and do, but the devs go out of their way to suck as much fun out of it as possible, and everything is far more time consuming, punishing, grueling and frustrating.

==========TLDR============

If you're the type of person who dedicates themselves to only one game, likes casinos, and thinks Clash of Clans is a well made game as far as the user experience goes, this is for you.

If you aren't, you'll mostly end up feeling deceived, cheated and sucked of your joy and wasting your life away with little to show for it all for arbitrary purposes.

==========Cont.==============

It was bearable up until recently, when these seasonal content drops that come with some regularity, were dropping with some higher levels on occasion through some small resource investment. Seemed to me like it was intentional, like the devs wanted to get people focusing on the seasonal stuff over advancing through old content, which I can be cool with.

Unfortunately this was a bug, and since fixing it, I've managed to find myself stuck around the mid 1020's, with 4-6 hours going by at a time where I'm getting nowhere and just frustrated by the experience. I am also locked out of a lot of otherwise enjoyable content thanks to not being geared properly, which is hard to do, when the *only* way to advance any further is to swap out old gear with new higher level stuff the moment I get it. It's frustrating having to feel like I need to dump lots of resources into temporary items, or items I will have to regular spend more resources on, to keep up with new powerlevel items, resources I will run out of very quickly if I do this with any significant regularity for even just one or two items, let alone all my gear.

Was hoping to get to the max level and go through a lot of old content and collect items that are about to be removed in September, but the low chances for advancement and the time needed to just have that chance, means its a very real possibility that through just some poor luck, I can get stonewalled long enough to make it unrealistic. The more things I do at a lower level, the more items I have that will be worthless to me when I need to advance past them again, or I have to get a ♥♥♥♥ ton of these upgrade modules to max them out later.

=====Further Thoughts======

I've noticed a pattern in these MMO's where they seem to have figured out like, where they need to put the brick wall of grind at to best get people to purchase stuff first. I had also picked up a thing on like a super cheap sale before really getting into the game recently, and it was just making me think how I bet they do that to get people investing *some* money into the game like psychologically, it opens us up to the idea of spending money later on. Like I bought a season pass after enjoying the game for a while, even being hesitant about the endgame, and like clockwork was hit with the "bug fix" and a string of frustrating rage inducing sessions since. I bet thats one of those things like the pavolovian effects of opening lootboxes and ♥♥♥♥ like that.

Either way, I'm cutting my loses and my motivation to hold out for better has rapidly diminished. It's clear to me that regardless if it'll be fun again later, this is another game developed by a team of people desperately intent on using manipulation of phsychological tendencies and coercion of the human mind for their profit at the gamer's expense.

I miss when video games cared about profit from people enjoying the game first. It stings a little extra coming from the name that was behind Myth and Halo. Those days are long gone. Like the only reason I would want to pick up the game is all FOMO about seasonal stuff or my pass or the September content cut. Not because I had fun, enjoyed my last several gaming sessions, or am excited about the next play session. It's the same with all these other games like Destiny, its all just a negative and misreable experience propped up by a significant enough group of people who are willing to tolerate it all.\t\t\t\t\t
