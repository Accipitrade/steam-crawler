\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOver 1000h on PS4, and more than 3600h on PC, Destiny 2 is the one game that I've dedicated the most of my time as a gamer. Glancing back to this experience as whole, I really have a complicated feeling toards this when re-editing this comment.

Let me jump to the conclusion first:
DON'T PLAY IT
If you just happen to watch some Twitch stream about this game and it looks fun or new to the game as a free-to-play player, fine. But if there is any chance that you are deciding whether to purchase all the dlcs ( you do need them all if you want the full game contents), or looking for a mmo that you could settle and waste a lot of time on, my advice to both would be an absolute NO.
Run if you can before it's too late.

No doubt that this game has all its positive specs, epic music, fantastic graphics, rich story lores, etc, I will you that. Still, I would highlight the cons above all. The game play is highly repetitive once you figure all out, the first 300ish hour woluld be absolutely enjoyable based on the enormous accumulation form Year 1 (It's now the end of Year 4) even thought lots of old contents been deleted. but after that, it's the endless grind basically for everything. And if you happen to be a item/achievement collector like myself, bruh that would be even more miserable. Not only will you be glued up to this game on a daily basis in case missing out any time-limited or seasonal exclusive loot, but also drain a lot of money on the in-game store purchases.
I used to decide to quit this game permanently, but when some friends dragged me back, the strong feeling of remorse of missing out what I could've got haunts me. And the the major reason why I am still playing is pretty much for the same reason. (I hate my obsessional personality)

Just go away before Destiny 2 and Bungie, which has been trolling as always, give you nothing but void and emptiness.\t\t\t\t\t
