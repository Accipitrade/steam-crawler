\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI would advise against buying before the next big expansion. Based on what they've done before, they'll probably have a much cheaper "buy the base game and the big update" bundle than if you were to buy it now and the update early next year. Also, that may save you time and money if the update sucks, kek.

Since my last negative review I've played a ton more, with some friends. With friends, this game can be a lot of fun. The raids are fantastic, unique experiences in gaming. Mixing mmo-style boss mechanics with more interactive FPS gameplay is really engaging and fun. PvP can also be fun. Playing dumb meme builds/guns against really sweaty players and still doing ok is pretty entertaining.

Solo, however, I'm not sure the game offers the same kind of enjoyable experience. You could play it just for the story and characters, but the story is hit-or-miss, but the lore is fantastic. The The lore isn't SUPER accessible in-game, but a lot of it is there if you look for it.

And it's not without other issues. Balance in pvp seems to be chosen by random number generator sometimes. PvE balance shifts from season to season, which actually isn't terrible design. The meta shifts with a a guiding, albeit heavy-handed nudge from a mix of numeric adjustments and the seasonal mods available for different weapon types. Not an issue itself, but sometimes it feels incredibly limiting to be forced away from your favourite exotic weapon or weapon types. The upshot is that it gives a little refresher when updates come, because you're likely to switch up the types of guns you use.

In-game store can make your eyes roll, and getting the individual items for free can be grindy. Not awful if you're playing a LOT, but it would be an issue for any casual player. Speaking of which, grind. Grinding can be a welcome distraction. It can be comfy. But it can also be mind-numbing and awful. I think there's some of both in Destiny, because the moment-to-moment gameplay is usually fantastic. Guns feel and sound awesome, with the more unique options among them feeling powerful and sounding memorable. The big problem here is that sometimes a previous grind will be totally invalidated. I'd say the "sunsetting" is just MMO influence, but losing an old tier set in wow doesn't completely change the BASE abilities of your character, so it's not really comparable to losing a unique gun for competitive play.

FOMO is rampant and awful.

It's hard to outright recommend this game because of the immense frustrations that I and a lot of players have felt with it. For new players, I have no idea how the experience might look. But I do love the game, in the end.\t\t\t\t\t
