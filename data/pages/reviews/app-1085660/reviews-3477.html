\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 has gone through a lot of changes since its release. I've played Destiny off and on since Y1 of Destiny, and have seen changes for both better and worse.

Good things:
-Fun, smooth gunplay. Bungie knows how to make a fun FPS.
-Lots of content. You will never feel like you have nothing to do.
-Deep lore. Not all of it is easily accessible, but there's a lot there, and always more to discover.
-Weekly updates from developers, trying to keep in communication with playerbase.

Not so good things:
-Lots of content. This can be good or bad for some players, but as someone who doesn't really like to no-life one particular game, there are times I feel like I can't keep up without heavy grind.
-New seasonal content rotates. The new approach to a "changing world" means that aside from the main campaign and following big DLC campaigns, a lot of content will phase out and disappear to avoid bloating the game too much. Can cause FOMO, but also keeps things from getting out of hand i suppose.
-PvP content needs more love and updates. It's hard to keep the PvP of any game with RPG aspects and loot well balanced. More competitive PvP players still feel like they don't quite have a home.
-A couple cringey characters and voice-acting. I'm sorry, but Failsafe and Asher are both the worst.

While the not-so-good list seems bigger than the good list, a lot of it is preference and I still recommend at least trying out the game. The free content with New Light is plenty to let you know whether you will enjoy the game or not.\t\t\t\t\t
