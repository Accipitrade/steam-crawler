\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been playing Destiny since just before the Dark Below expansion was released. This is the first shooter game that I have enjoyed and stuck with. When Destiny 2 was first released, I was one of the fan boys that kept defending that game, but after a couple of months, I could not longer keep my blinders on to the bad practices in the game. I wanted to love the game again, and rejoined when the Curse of Osiris was released, but after the constant waves of controversies that came flooding out of the Bungie/Activition HQ, I felt I could no longer forgive the game studio that made one of my favorite games.

However, I still wanted to game to do good and decided to give them another chance when the Warmind was released. Once again I felt betrayed, and this time I had had enough. I deleted Destiny off of all of my systems, and moved on.

I had a friend tell me the news when Bungie split from Activition, and my only response then was, "Alot of the mistakes made were the fault of Bungie as well." I did not care to throw any more time to the game that had become like an abusive relationship.

Skip to the start of Shadowkeep. A friend that I had played Destiny together with, for almost 3 years, tried to tell me that things had changed. I did not want to listen to him, so he ended up buying me the game to force me to try it with him again. In all honesty, I am glad he did.

Destiny 2 has made one of the largest comebacks I have seen in the gaming industry, (not quite as much as No Man's Sky or Final Fantasy 14) and I find that I am once again enjoying the game. I hope that Bungie has learned from the past two painful years, and that the game will continue to grow and flourish. I once again find myself in love with this universe. Per audacia ad astra Guardians.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSupportive:
The colors, environments, and characters are on point. It has something for just about anyone/everyone. Coop, Coop PVP, Solo PVP, Solo PVE, Coop PVE, Teams, Matchmaking, etc. etc. etc.
The gear is extremely deep in it's variety, and the color customization is really solid.

Critical:
This game needs someone fully dedicated to fixing menus, and screen/item interactions. I call these people "Quality of Life Guardians". There is a SIGNIFICANT increase from Day 1, but that really only goes to show how pathetic and terrible their UI design is. There could be an *ENTIRE* expansion dedicated to nothing but these QoL improvements, for $20, and I would still strongly consider buying it. I migrated over from the Blizzard app where I might have logged close to 1000 hours of time on this so it's not enough to make you NOT want to play, but it is enough to keep me from giving it my full throat endorsement.\t\t\t\t\t
