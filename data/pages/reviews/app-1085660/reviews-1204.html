\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a hard game to recommend. After putting in over 1000 hours into this series (note: I played a lot of Destiny 1, and had several hundred hours on Battle.net before the switch to Steam), I still find myself conflicted on it at times.

I love it for having the best feeling gunplay of any game series I've ever played. I also love it for the incredible worlds and atmosphere it provides. The world of Destiny is really something special.

However, the game begins to falter when you get into its MMO aspects. There's some really odd game design choices that range from mildly infuriating to outright incompetent. Destiny has never been and never will be a perfect game, that I accepted long ago.

Despite all this, I still love Destiny. It holds a soft spot in my heart for all the memories and inspiration it has given me. The music, lore, and worlds of Destiny have helped me through some of my toughest times and forged some of my greatest friendships. My journey as a Guardian has been nothing short of epic and I am excited for it to continue.

Try the game out if you want. Play a couple missions of the free to play version. If you enjoy the gunplay and think you'll enjoy it for several hundreds of hours then this is the game for you. If the story, world, and lore interests you as well that's another added bonus.

I know Destiny isn't for everyone, but it certainly is for me. Thank you Bungie. Regardless of the countless missteps you've made throughout the years (and the ones you're still going to make let's be honest), I'm proud to call myself a Guardian.\t\t\t\t\t
