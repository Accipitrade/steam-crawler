\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo begin first I will be brutally honest about this game, from when Bungie was still owned by Activision Blizzard to the state of the game now, and where it is going and how the experience has changed over the years.

I will start with the positives of Destiny as a whole and how the game has changed from Destiny 1 to Destiny 2, and how most of the changes to the game are good, and how some are bad, that will be focused on later down the line in the negatives.

Positives:
- The PvE experience is peak story telling, with a wonderful world that has been shaped over nearly 8 years, it's on par with some of the best written stories you could ever experience within any media. You'll be attached to the characters with compelling stories and real pain and experiences.
- Gun play is key in a looter shooter, as well as the gameplay loop and loot itself, and the gun play does not disappoint, it's fluid with things that make using a certain weapon type or that perfect crafted weapon so much more wonderful to continue to experience and use. With jumping into the air firing a rocket into a group of Hive and watching them all fall apart, or taking your Trinity Ghoul and firing the chaining lightning through a hoard of Fallen Pirates, it's spectacular.
- The loot, the most important part of any looter shooter is the gear, weapons, and other things you can earn to flesh out that vault of yours to feel like that god killing guardian you are.
- The lore, from the factions you face to the allies you gain each faction, other than one, is fleshed out and established feeling as though each has it's own purpose and place within the world of Destiny.
- The power, all good looter shooters, or class based shooters you need a sense of that your are that god killer that Guardians has earned as a reputation. The power fantasies you can experience are that of the flowing fluid monk of an Arc Hunter, to the wall all can stand behind with the Void Titan, to the fire in the sky, raining hell onto everyone below with Solar hunter, every fantasy of power you want to feel, you can with build crafting being so much more simple with the rework to all of the classes now.

Having spoken of the positives, this game like all is not without it's flaws, and Destiny 2 has quite a few to this day even with the changes that have happened over the years.

Negatives:
- The PvP experience is extremely poor, to nigh unplayable sometimes. With the changes to the matchmaking is the public playlist adding skill based matchmaking, as well as leaver penalties in something that has no competitive gain is ridiculous, not only that if you do not use the meta you will NEVER will a gun fight, as someone who hates to be a meta slave this is a ridiculous notion.
- Seasonal Story Telling, Live service. I have personally never been a fan of live service games, in the past, Destiny would release small expansion packs during the down time between large expansion, this did not work for Destiny, the stories were not good and the content that was added was not fun. IE The Dark Below from Destiny 1, Curse of Osiris from Destiny 2, year 1 many of these small expansion were horrendous, the problem with the game being live service is that the content you pay for yearly, around 10 dollars per season will be gone by the next major expansion, even though these new pieces of content they add is fun, exciting with amazing story telling, it'll be gone within the year.
- The Monetization Model of Destiny. Speaking of spending money from the previous point, if you though this was a F2P Looter shooter, it wasn't always, I was one of the original D2 players that payed for the base game, Curse of Osiris, Warmind, Forsaken, all of Forsaken's seasons, Shadowkeep, all of it's seasons, Beyond Light, all of it's season, Witchqueen, all of it's season and now Lightfall, and all of it's seasons. This equals without even including the money I spent of Destiny 1 or the micro-transations within the Eververse store roughly 500 to 600 dollars so far. Not only that, nearly half of the content I have mention is no longer in the game, meaning around only 200 dollars worth of what I've spent is still within the game.
- Sunsetting, now as I write this all content that is in the game now, other than seasonal stories and events will be staying in the game for good, the problem lies in the fact that the base Destiny 2 campaign, it's original raids and all of that content is gone, for what feels like good, instead of bring back that content from the game they already removed that continue to add content from Destiny 1 that you could go play right now.

Having stated my peace of the positives and negatives I still believe you should pick the game up, make your own opinions and choices, and to allow yourself to believe if this is right for you, if you want a similar Destiny experience there really isn't one, Destiny has always been a one of a kind gem. If you hate Destiny try Warframe, I hope you enjoyed this review and thank you for your time and for reading.\t\t\t\t\t
