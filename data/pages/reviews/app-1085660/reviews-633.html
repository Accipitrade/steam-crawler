\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's becoming more and more apparent that the motive behind vaulting content is to essentially force current, new, and returning players to buy expansions in order to experience any content to feel relevant. The opposite of what Final Fantasy 14 does. You are given a lot to do but you don't feel bad for not spending more money.

I have so many good memories on this game but this free to play game practically forgot the reason it became so. How can you switch to a free to play model while gutting 80% of the content that was supposed to be free to play. And then ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ over returning players that did dump a lot of money into the game by stripping away their content to.

It's just frustrating and annoying, which is not a good formula of keeping players interested and around. Why would players start, continue, or return if they know that the money they spend now will just be burned at some point in the future? Find another way. Players are content with games taking up so much storage, and even if not, giving players the choice of what content they want and don't want on their storage would be a decent alternative. Instead, they force the current story down a route that explains why content is no longer available, so people literally have no chance to explore the original content they paid for.

I don't know what I'm talking about anymore. I just know that this path they are taking is not a good one and they're not stopping any time soon. If you have money and time to burn, play the game, but right now, despite my grand memories, can't in good faith recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
