\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile Destiny 2 is a beautiful game with amazing graphics and magnificent gameplay, it is ruined by a few major drawbacks.

First of are the absolutely ridiculous amount of hackers and cheaters in the PvP section of the game called Crucible. Especially in the ranked or prestige modes the amount of aimbotters and wallhackers has gotten very out of hand. The first few days after an update it is doable because presumably the hackers need to update their hacks as well. As you near the end of a season the PvP mode is simply unplayable.

The second drawback is the inventory management. The sheer number and variety of items you can get with all kinds of random rolls is amazing, but also very difficult to deal with. Your inventory and vault will be full in no time, with no idea what you can delete. Then to top it off, you can invest most of your in-game fortune to upgrade an item to it's maximum stats only to have it become obsolete with the next season. There are many third-party application that do a really good job of helping you sort through your items and give recommendation of what to keep, but the fact that these apps exist clearly implies the in-game solutions are insufficient.

The third major drawback is the lack of content after a big hype. Don't get me wrong here, the content that is provided is beautiful, nice to play with friends, rewards cool weapons, and adds to the ambiguous storyline. However, the amount of content is often disappointing. Especially after a hype over the new expansion, and the price of said expansion. Additionally there are very few quality of life improvements made to the game even after many groups of users asking for simple features that everyone would benefit from for years.

To finish off i would like again to put emphasis on the amount of hackers and cheaters in PvP. Bungie has never done anything publicly about it and nobody knows if they do anything about it behind the scenes. There are many hackers that have been aimbotting for years without getting caught, which is concerning. Finally, since Destiny has moved to Steam the number of hackers has increased several fold.\t\t\t\t\t

Having joined the journey with the PS+ offering I have had a bit of a an up and down with Destiny 2. The model of selling expansions, a battle pass and in Game cosmetics is way to many ways to gate content behind a paywall... Not buying the battle pass in particular locks you out of so much progression/content for that particular season. That artificial creation of FOMO is not welcome at all.

Given the above, with beyond light (which I got the deluxe edition) I have decided to take a more chill pace and not try rush to power cap asap or grind out all the weeklies/dailies because that does definitely creates burn out, and so far i am enjoying destiny much better than when i was gripping about the season passes.

The recent implementation of drip feeding content with Beyond Light is welcome by me.\t\t\t\t\t
