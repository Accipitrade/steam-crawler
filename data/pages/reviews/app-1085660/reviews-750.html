\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first started playing Destiny during the Destiny 1 Beta on Xbox 360. I played on 360 until Taken King came out. At the time you could buy TTK on Xbox One and get the first 2 DLC included. I played up until Age of Triumph. I'm about as veteran as you can get these days, so trust I've seen some highs and lows. And nothing has since compared to the lows in Destiny 2.

THE GOOD:
- Lore
- The Universe
- Tons of potential
- Gunplay is great
- Free to play
- Has a fairly sizeable community
- Has enough to keep you busy for months
- It's not required to do endgame stuff and lot of challenges are up to player choice
- Seasonal model that adds some interesting stuff month by month.
- Textures are nice

THE BAD:
- Competition in this game is a joke (it's either slay out in braindead ways to win or get farmed while your team does nothing. It's either be over-powered AF or lose. And it's even worse in Iron Banner.)
- Trials has a bad loot structure and isn't rewarding
- Community has a toxic section that contains some Trials sweats who preach Trials stats are the ONLY stats that matter
- Gambit is underwhelming, uninspired, and the Gambit loot is a nightmare to farm for
- Adept weapons invalidate pretty much everything else
- Game can be a lagging mess
- Fireteam chat bugs make LFG annoying
- Sunsetting and every incarnation of it
- Content vault
- Stasis and how long it took to actually FIX it
- Every PvE mode wants to be Menagerie but Bungie sunset Menagerie despite everyone loving Menagerie
- Re-Issuing D1 content and making no new D2 content
- Re-Issuing sunset gear with inferior perks
- Removing perks from weapons so previous god-rolls can't be acquired (this is terrible for balancing)
- Takes way too many materials to change armor element
- Level progression sucks for power levels, season pass, vendors.
- Great story potential but awful implementation. Everything's either done in fetch-quest vendor dialog or while monotonously grinding the same mission you've done 100 times already.
- No actual game mode variety. Every PvP match plays the same way: nobody plays objective and everyone just wants to farm kills. Every PvE match is about speed-running because people want to farm loot. So it's either go so fast and competitive you barely enjoy the game or get obliterated and still have a bad time.
- Guns use the same gun sounds...
- Guns steal weapon models from other guns...
- Grinding for loot (you can't re-roll legendaries, stats for armor, or farm specific loot like you could in Menagerie)
- Eververse has cooler stuff than you can grind for. Better get those wallets out because cool-looking FOMO!
- Seasonal content FOMO
- Class customization barely exists - everyone plays the most busted stuff because loot and stat pandering and anything that isn't pretty much S-tier has a tendency to get left behind. Bungie even considered some sub-classes not "IP defining" and suggested removing them.
- Game manages to half-bake things that couldn't be any-less half-baked.
- Content drought is still a thing.
- Content drip still sucks.

Bungie is still doing that thing where they "resell" old sunset weapons back: Loaded Question > Null Composure, Redrix > Messenger, Beloved > Adored. And if it's not that it's re-issuing weapons on the Moon and Dreaming City but making them terrible or bringing back D1 content and hyping it up. So far Nightfalls don't have a single original D2 weapon in their rotation: Hung Jury, Uzume, Plug ONE, Palindrome, Shadow Price, The SWARM. And then there's power creep. Like how bout the fact every new pellet shotgun was made to powercreep the last?

Nothing but the same boring stuff year after year, honestly. Little actual innovation these days. Everything in PvE wants to be Menagerie or Sundial but never actually does it. Everything wants class diversity but then you have 1/3 of all Trials players being Revenants and then there's the super-spam from Chaos Reach, which is still annoying. Stasis is at least better but it took nearly a legit YEAR to do anything about it.\t\t\t\t\t
