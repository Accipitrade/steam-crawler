In it's current state, destiny is becoming less and less of an engaging activity and more of a mindless chore every update the game releases.

While I love the core game, the lore, and the characters of the Destiny franchise, I truly feel that the game is being taken down a very bleak path by the lead directors at Bungie. With the introduction of Sunsetting and the Impending removal of a large chunk of the game's content, it truly feels that the game is being shifted more towards the idea of a mindless "make the number go higher by doing x, y, and z" MMO that expects the player to be constantly moving towards a goal that is constantly moving away. Weapons and content are becoming obsolete and outright inaccessible, meaning that newer players of the game won't receive half the experience that someone who played the game since day 1 has gotten.
Along with this decreasing flow of content, the raised price of DLC in relation to game content has significantly decreased.

Look at Forsaken: $40 at launch, and It included a new campaign, 2 new planets, a new raid, a new dungeon, a new gamemode, 22 exotics, 73 new weapons, new armor sets, 9 new supers, and several new maps for crucible and strikes.

Compare this to Beyond Light: Also $40, it includes a new campaign, 1 new planet, one planet taken from destiny 1, a new raid, NO new dungeon, NO new gamemode, 11 exotics, (a currently unknown number rumored to be) <50 weapons, new armor sets, 3 new supers, and 5 new maps for crucible and strikes.

I can't honestly recommend Destiny 2 to my friends like I used to. I've noticed myself playing the game less and less ever since Shadowkeep, and I truly believe that Beyond Light might be the stop where I finally get off.

That being said,

some of my fondest memories have been in this franchise and this game. Spending 8 hours with a group of my friends trying to beat our first raid, playing private matches with everyone as we wait for our LFG to fill, the time I got a real exotic engram in the area where the fake exotic engrams spawn. I've put over 1000 hours into this game, from battlenet to steam, and I'll always remember my experiences that I had with this game. However, there were always moments of frustration mixed in with the happy moments, painful grinds for weapons, annoying mechanics for weapons, that one ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ taken phalanx. Every good thing the game did came with 2 other bad things you had to go through to get to it.

In the end, I guess I love to hate this game. It's free to play the base game (though a lot of it's going to be removed in about a month) so take it for a spin and see if you like it. The most I can tell you is that the experience you, as a new player, will have, just won't be the Destiny 2 a lot of other people experienced.\t\t\t\t\t
