\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor a Free-to-Play game, there sure is a lot of content. I get this used to be a $60 game once. But getting the full game all those DLCs for free is a pretty sweet deal. Even if you don't buy Forsaken or ShadowKeep, there's still tons of content in here.

The graphics still hold up, like, come on, it's only a 3 year old game. The gunplay and combat is awesome! Though the tutorial could have been a bit clearer in teaching the game mechanics.

Remember how I said there was a lot of content, like, two paragraphs ago? There's literally so much ♥♥♥♥ to do, you don't know where to start and where to go next. I'm 13 hours in as of writing this review and I still haven't started any of the main missions. And looking at the quests in my roster, it seems unlikely I'll be getting to that anytime soon.

Honestly, I haven't found too many flaws yet. The framerates are stable, the connectivity is solid, the graphics are nice, the missions are fun, if a bit repetitive and the voice actors are A grade as well. I believe there's Lance Reddick, Gideon, and other top rated voice actors on here. And it's made by Bungie. The guys who made Halo. You can't go wrong.

In the end, it's a $60 game gone free including free DLCs. What's not to like?\t\t\t\t\t
