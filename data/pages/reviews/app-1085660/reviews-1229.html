\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really want to like Destiny 2 because Bungie have put a lot of love into many aspects of the game, but Destiny 2 is a MMOrpg and it makes the cardinal sin of MMOrpg's by disrespecting my time. If you just want to shoot stuff look elsewhere.

I'm talking about the Beyond Light version of Destiny 2 here which removes four planet locations, but adds a new planet and brings back the cosmodrome from Destiny 1. As you would expect from a Bungie game the sound and art is really good, the gun-play is solid and there is a decent amount of lore to back up the story. Make no mistake though, Destiny 2 has been steadily changing from a shooter to a nanny state MMOrpg for the last couple of years now, so expect 'kill 30 mobs here' MMOrpg style missions that got old a decade ago. Expect many of them. Then you've just finished a mission here so you've fast travelled to where you are told miles away to only to be told to go back where you were... and there is no fast travel there... 'YOU WILL ENJOY THE SCENERY' again and again and again.

There is also a MMOrpg style combat loop which is in almost every game-mode and it goes like this:

Thing you want to shoot is 'immune' from damage because 'space magic,' so you need to shoot a different thing until it drops a ball which you need to pick up and either throw it onto a thing or deposit the ball into a thing, then you can have your fun shooting stuff.

To be honest that paragraph sums up Destiny 2 in a nutshell, because it is pretty much all you will do.

This is my take on the lore:

'It's like the year 3000 or something and our system has been infected by space magic and you - as space messiah - are going to save it single-handedly* armed with revolvers, bows, shot guns and other assorted guns from the year 1914 because some bloke called Joe banned guns before handily giving the world away to a communist state. There was no development at all for the next 1000 years because most people were selling out their neighbours and fawning over their communist overlords.
Then a moon-like thing turned up and started bringing folk back to life - even the ones sold out by their neighbours - with 'Light space magic' because as always the communists were following 'the wrong kind of communism.' And it spake "bring me offerings of dusklight shards and like baryon boughs maaaan, and I'll like trade you for stuff, and make you immortal after I've brainwashed you... erm - I mean - cleansed your memories with light" and humanity thought that was a pretty good deal. Thus was formed the vanguard, humanities last defense.
Now the moon-like thing they called it 'the traveller' because it's actually an alpha male that goes from system to system stripping all resources it can 'spreading it's light for communism' - never paying it's dues mind - and as such it had angered a few folk.
...And all of them were capitalists. EVIL dark capitalists. There were many species of capitalists who the traveller owed a lot of money - red ones, purple ones, ones the traveller had left anorexic (without body shaming them) and they were all very dark and very capitalist: and then there are ones of a colour so dark it cannot be mentioned anymore, and they are the most evil. Some of them were gods even who taught capitalism to their followers through a doctrine called 'sword logic....' because it's the future and swords are still a thing.
Then the lore writers had a bit of a rethink because this light and dark thing we've got going on might be seen as a bit dodgy in current year.... and so they went to starbucks and hatched a plan to make 'the light' a darker hue than white.
So now there is yet another evil - much more evil than the really really dark ones whose colour we cannot say anymore. And it's resemblance is on all of the bank notes that matter *insert X files music here* the dollar bill!
.... SPACE ILLUMINATI!
.... and now you're kind of dirty, wielding light as well as darkness and Bungie are safe from being call an -ist or a -phobe etc.

Destiny 2 wants you to play only three game modes only until you hit a certain level (hard cap.)
- Crucible which is team or solo PvP and full of cheating weasels and I've no interest in this mode, but you have to play it if you want to progress in Destiny 2 even if you loathe it. In my experience it is common to find folk walking into walls for the whole match because they must complete a certain number of games but have no interest in playing it (so they do that not to get kicked.)
- Gambit which is a team mob grinder with PvP aspects, and can be fun but I find it incredibly stressful and exhausting.
- Strikes which is straight PvE in a team of three which can be fun but once you've replayed the same thing five times it starts getting old. Folk fly through strikes as fast as possible to the boss room, I assume because they would rather do something else.

Playing those three game modes for the next fifty light levels doesn't appeal to me and they aren't really any other options.

One for strikes, one for Gambit, one for crucible, one for raiding that auto-load whenever participating in said game-mode.

I could go on and on, but Bungie have really dropped the ball here. I'm getting deja vu with the last version of Firefall before that started punishing players for having fun - actually, I'm thankful isn't that bad. I'm also thankful I'm not that invested in Destiny 2 as a lot of people whom I can only assume are masochists for putting up with this. I thought last season wasn't bad - plenty to do and I felt rewarded regardless of what I was doing. This season is just a slap in the face.
