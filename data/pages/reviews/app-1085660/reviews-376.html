\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm somehow both ashamed and proud of my total playtime on this game. Destiny 2 really has two different games: PvE and PvP. I find both parts of the game fun, but they are very different. I wouldn't be surprised if a player found one of the two completely unplayable for any number of reasons such as difficulty (whether too easy or hard), lack of friends to play with, or not enough free time. These are a few of my criticisms of the game/warnings for new players. Destiny 2 is an FPS-MMORPG, although that I would argue that the you aren't really role playing considering you get zero choices to change the story other than the order sometimes in which you do things. The story is still very interesting and it's fun to express your personality/character through your appearance, play style, weapon choices, which statistics you choose to prioritize (otherwise known as your 'build'), etc.

The Problem for anyone who has never or even barely played Destiny 2:
Destiny 2 has been out for years and continuously releases new content, and that content is shown to you from the jump, but of course, you haven't unlocked it yet and have no idea how, if at all, to get it. You can see almost every possible triumph, exotic, armor piece, weapon, emblem, shader, you get the point. I've been playing Destiny 2 on and off for usually about 2-3 months at a time and even when I boot the game back out all the new content is so daunting its hard for me to know where to start. Then I look back at all the content I missed and am jealous that I don't have it and am disappointed to find out so much of the content is inaccessible. Therefore, I can only imagine the exaggerated feeling that any new player will have when presented with everything the game has to offer. That isn't a bad thing, I'd hate to knock a game for a fairly constant stream of content, but I empathize with players that are scared off from devoting a 1000 hours into it.

A little about that content:
I own all the expansions for Destiny 2 except the 30th Anniversary Pack because I didn't think $25 for a rocket launcher was worth my money, but the other expansions for the most part are. An extremely tough pill for me to swallow is the content being entirely removed or at least rendered forcefully obsolete after a few years in the game. Guns and armor that you spend dozens of hours grinding to get are no longer usable. I understand, the file size of this game is very large already, and it probably be about double if content wasn't removed, but I can't come to terms with the fact that content that I, and everyone else, paid for is now literally pointless. It's akin to a subscription without being advertised as a subscription. I can't help but feel robbed. I believe that after the Tangled Shore is removed, this is going to stop. This could be a sign that Bungie hadn't figured out the identity of Destiny 2 from launch and now they have, but I really don't like it. I just want new players to understand that the content you are paying full Triple A price for, may be removed in just a couple of years, even if you are still playing Destiny 2.

Have Friends to Play With:
Of course, I find Destiny 2 fun, but if I didn't have a group of 10 or so different people I can play with, I think the quality of my experience would be a lot worse. If you don't have a bunch of friends with $100+ to spend on video games and a bunch of free time, there are tons of ways to find random people to play with such as LFG discords or subreddits, and a lot of times that can be almost as fun as your real friends, at least in my experience (there are always horror stories but in general its pretty good). As a rule of thumb, 'early game' content can be done without a group, but the harder the content gets, the more necessary a group becomes. On the PvE side of the game: Raids are designed for 6 people, Dungeons for 3 people, Nightfalls for 3 people, etc. The PvP side of the game only has a competitive game mode and Trials for 3 people, but there is also Iron Banner every few weeks for 1-6 people although it's not nearly as competitive as the first two I listed. Nothing I listed requires a full group to play, but I would say the experience is easier and more fun with a group that has communication and at least some familiarity with each other.

Free Time Necessary:
You'll find out quickly that almost everything in Destiny 2 has a 'timegate' meaning that you either have to wait a certain number of days or weeks to get something whether that's a gun, triumph, seal, etc. This is great because it prevents you from doing all of the game's content in a few short days or weeks, but this can really ruin your day when you realize you just missed the week that you needed to finish what you've been working towards, and now you have to wait an extra 1-2 months in some instances. Therefore, you have to be able to consistently play every week if you want to achieve something. You may also need to play every week if you want all the weapons from a raid, the Nightfalls, or Trials for example. As a follow up point, most weapons in this game have 'random rolls' meaning that the perks and traits of a weapon are taken at random from a pool of possibilities. Therefore, you may spend an almost an hour to complete a nightfall to get a weapon, and then its perks have zero synergy, and you feel like you wasted your time.

While Destiny 2 is technically free to play, it'll feel like a trial version to the full game because so much of the content is locked behind expansions. Much of the content from the base game that used to be free to play has been removed, including several iconic Raids that were great for new players.

So should you play Destiny 2?:
It's free to play, so yes. I would recommend you at least boot up the game and play the content that you can. Try out the unranked PvP, explore the different planets, do any of the missions that you can. I know for me, looking at all the items and triumps/seals you can get is what draws me in to playing more. Go into your inventory and look at those seals, exotics, collections, etc. and if that is enticing to you, maybe watch some YouTube videos on the raids, campaign, items, but I'd understand if you don't want the spoilers.

Remember that subjectivity is implied. I tried to sprinkle in reminders throughout that this is just my opinion, but when you speak so much about a game and all the different facets, it can start to sound objective. Although I stated many facts about the game, I think it's worth forming your own opinions through your own game play experiences.\t\t\t\t\t
