\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a game that you very much have to be the kind of person to actually enjoy the game to like it.
Sounds confusing? Right?
Destiny 2 is very much a grindy game, not to a massive extent mind you, but still enough where it can turn off some people.
The pvp in this game is also very different from others of its kind, focusing on a more arcade like feel rather than an ultra-competitive one.
Some of the design decisions made for the game can be questionable as well. Such as the seasonal content model or having gear be on a yearly rotation.
At its core Destiny 2 is a fun game, and considering the game is free I would give it a try to see if the game is right for you. If you have friends that play the game, or just want a new experience for the time being, pick it up. If you like it you can buy one of the dlcs if you want (I recommend always picking up the newest one seeing as most of its content will still be relevant). Again, its not a game for everyone, but those who do enjoy it are very dedicated to the game, and with content like Raids, it has enough to keep those players coming back.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy friends got me into Destiny 2 a little over a year ago now, on the Xbox I have sunk over 800 hours of my life into the game since then. The game itself is usually a blast, especially if you can get on with some friends. The game has become quite different from when I started playing and the grindy nature of it can be a big detractor if you can see past that the game is almost always a good time.
EDIT
I can no longer recommend this game as much as I enjoy it, the game has too many problems. The new player experience is abysmal it lacks depth and anything engaging or worth while, it is in dire need of a facelift. Currently the artifact system leaves a lot to be desired as well. While I understand the champion mods are being used to get interest in the new weapons that come with a season and keep the meta from getting stagnant like it was a few years back, it ultimately serves to hurt the the game more than help it, for higher level activities it forces you into an extremely limited loadout with very few options, something that is only hurt more by the recent sunsetting of a lot of weapons that could have been used. Mods like Oppressive Darkness, Breach and Clear, and Particle Deconstruction are another example of the forced meta, and while I have had fun with the damage you can pump out with these sorts of mods it is just boring and empty to have a mod who's only purpose is to make a weapon hit harder, or inflict a debuff. Weapons and abilities should have their own identities with how they interact with the sandbox and that's what should define the loadout you choose to run, not a gear check imposed by a champion mod. In a game where personal playstyle is supposed to be an important factor, limiting what people can use is just not a good move.\t\t\t\t\t
