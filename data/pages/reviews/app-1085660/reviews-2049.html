\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUPDATE //
The complaint about the amount of "you had to be there" content still rings true, and is even a bit worse at this point. The quality of seasonal and expansion content has significantly improved, and is way better than ever before. I genuinely am enjoying the new content and lore Destiny 2 offers recently.

Unfortunately, after vaulting stuff from previous seasons, Bungie has created a drought of free to play content. Stuff that is available at the current moment is locked behind DLC or the current season. I do think the opportunity Bungie gives you to play the first few missions of the new expansions is pretty awesome, especially for those who don't know if they want to invest in the new released content.


OLD REVIEW //
Regardless of the downsides to the games, Destiny is by far my most favorite game series. I love the story development, environments, raids, and lost sectors. I love that there's bits of lore strewn about every nook and cranny of the game, even on weapons and armor and random items in the open worlds. Easter eggs and secret golden chests are everywhere. I love all the NPCs you meet along the way in the story and even the less important people mentioned in lore, or found in the Tower and the Farm.
I definitely recommend playing the first Destiny game if you've got a console, as it makes a lot of references make more sense and gives a deeper backstory into the various characters in the game, and makes certain moments a lot more emotional (looking at the Forsaken DLC).

The downside to Destiny is the massive amount of "you had to be there" content, mostly in the season pass content and limited time/holiday events. It's really disappointing having a new or returning player ask you, "where did you get that gear?" and explaining to them that it was a limited time event and you can't get the gear anymore through any means, unless Bungie throws it back into world or engram drops. I understand the model, but it sucks that it's consistently used, mostly since New Light (F2P) has been released.
I never understood the hate for Eververse, the microtransaction storefront featured in the game. The only thing Bungie sells there is purely just cosmetics, season pass, and the 2 new DLCs for the game. You're never *required* to buy anything from there, and Bungie never really bugs you to buy anything, unless you count the "store" tab in the Director which was included for ease of access. A lot of the items you can earn from playing the game or levelling the season pass, if you have it. There is a TON of armor and other cosmetics you can earn purely just from playing the game.\t\t\t\t\t
