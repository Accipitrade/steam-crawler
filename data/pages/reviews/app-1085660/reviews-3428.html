\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPlayed the game for over 100+ hours when it was initially made free on Battle.net. But the pricing was atrocious so didn't bother with playing Forsaken. Now that it's on Steam and the pricing has been fixed, I can finally play in peace. And also review.

Graphics - Mind you, this game isn't exactly new. There's none of that fancy DLSS or Ray Tracing stuff here. Even the engine isn't exactly the newest. But jump into the world and you would be forgiven for thinking this game has all the graphics bells and whistles of the latest AAA offerings. The graphics are detailed (excessively so since it is a very fast paced game), the environs are breathtaking for their size and variety and the endless addition of new areas and content means that the game never feels very old. Even if you are heading into the same areas over and over again (and you will do this more and more as you move from the basic story missions and into the endgame stuff), chances are you will continue to admire the beauty of the walls, the excellent reflections, the details of the enemies and even of your own clothing. In the distance, the forests of the EDZ or the emptiness of some of the alien planets make this game worthy of more than a few screenshots!

Rating - 5/5

Plot - It's a multiplayer game at its core, so expecting a complex branching storyline probably will lead to disappointment. But what story there is is enjoyable. The primary one (which you start off with) revolves around protecting something called the Traveler from the Red Legion and their desire to harness it for their own ends. Later missions involve finding the truth behind the death of Cayde-6, etc. Some of the locations are repetitive, and the story generally involves fighting enemies in a specific number of locations before facing off in a boss-fight or two to end the mission. Ditto for the adventures, patrols and other similar ones. There are tougher missions called raids and strikes but IMHO, they don't really add much to the story and are completely optional. All that said, a new entrant would find the story engaging enough to be hooked into the game itself.

Rating - 3.5/5

Gameplay - That's the meat of the matter. As mentioned above ,gameplay is fast and flashy. The basic premise is that as a guardian, you have access to three weapons plus a category-specific set of superpowers that you unleash when a meter fills up. Think of it as a special move. In practice, the game involves shooting fast enemies quickly, while running around so as to dodge their attacks. There's plenty of jumping, which helps make the level designs meaningful and allow you to feel like a superhero as you prance around your enemies.

Enemies themselves are hardly ever too difficult to take down individually. If they are, chances are you may have ended up in an area above your level. The real challenge comes from the sheer number of enemies. Without going into enemy types, let's just say that a huge flock of Hive or Vex isn't good for your health. Enemy attack types are reasonably varied, ranging from melee attacks to laser beams (Servitors) and various guns. Bosses have some special attacks, but in most cases these are variants of the weaker enemies' attacks.

Multiplayer is of course, the major draw for many players. You have co-op missions such as strikes, which allow you to bring your friends or join in with random people. There are also random public events in which you can cooperate with as many people as possible. Equally enjoyable are the competitive modes, which include the standard deathmatches. What really sets this game apart though, is the Gambit. A combination of cooperation and competition, Gambit is the right blend of PvP and PvE. Mostly PvE but just the right amount of PvP to ensure that you are always looking behind your back (or making others do so by invading theri side). The maps are decently large, but not so much as to allow someone to hide in a corner forever. Regardless of the mode though, both competitive and cooperative multiplayer is simple in essence and thus enjoyable even without chats, Discord, etc. (I'm glaring at you, Division 2 Raid).

Lastly, it should be mentioned that the game has a rather immersive set of RPG elements. Leveling up isn't super hard, as long as you are taking on a variety of activities. Gear have scores and special attributes. The only one that really is of any concern is the second weapon's attack type (fire, energy, etc.). Each season has a certain gear level cap, which currently starts at 750 and ends at 950+. Initially, this will matter a lot since you may find some areas extremely easy or terribly hard depending on the gear score you have. But in endgame, most areas (except the strikes) will spawn enemies that your guns can cut through like a hot knife through butter!

Honourable mention here to the engram system, which awards you with various gear and other loot when you tick specific boxes. They are also awarded for leveling up in certain cases. If vanity is a weakness, you can also go in for bright engrams, which give you shades with which to colour your gear, plus emotes with which to impress that random bloke who just wants you to get on with the game.

Rating - 5/5

Characters - As with the plot, the characters are really of secondary importance. You might develop a liking for Sloane, or Cayde-6, or Devrim, or someone else. But none of the figures are too engaging, mostly because they serve only to give missions and engage in a little banter along the way. Sadly, there are no branching dialogue options and most characters will go silent after offering a fixed set of lines.

Rating - 3/5

Conclusion - It may appear that I am not terribly impressed by the plot or the characters. Truth is, one doesn't have to be. What keeps the game engaging is the gameplay, which is crisp, fast and varied enough to keep you interested. Combined with good graphics and a season system where you have something new to do every few months, this gameplay makes Destiny 2 a must play even for those who aren't into multiplayer or online games.

Overall rating - 4.5/5\t\t\t\t\t
