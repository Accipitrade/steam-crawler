Bungie's Destiny : 2
It's been a full year and a few months since Bungie went on their own, The game hasn't gotten any better from it there is nothing to be done about it, I give up on Bungie there isn't any glimmer of hope anymore.
And now my reasoning.

This game , will not respect your time. It won't respect you as a person.

Chapter one : Sunsetting

I really had hope.. sunsetting could've been something great, it really could've.
It was a way to stop powercreep by retiring old weapons and issuing new ones to replace them
It sounded fairly nice on paper, we'd be getting rid of a stale meta and we'd get new fancy toys.

So how did it fail?
Well.. they made several mistakes in releasing it.
First obviously is the range at which they sunset was too wide, they removed every weapon in the game except for the last 3 seasons, that was more than 90% of the weapons just.. gone.
Most of these weapons were part of the Shadowkeep and Forsaken expansions meaning that buying those would now virutally be worthless, considering most of the loot you'd get would just be useless.

Second mistake, They didn't issue enough new weapons, 31 in total as well as some Reissues from past expansions. in these 31 ones 6 of those were exotics and most of those weapons weren't even that unique.
The reissues are even worse considering most of them didn't even change the perk pool meaning that you'd effectively had to grind for the same weapon and lose your kill trackers for regrinding.

Third mistake, which is mostly a personal opinion armor was also sunset. That causes a myriad of problems
the main one being having to regrind for full sets of armor and having to upgrade them back to max. only for them to be sunset again in a years time.

And so the sun sets down as it is a cycle, it is here to stay.

Chapter 2 : Power leveling

Power leveling is a mechanic that's been with destiny 2 since forever however, with the introduction of the seasonal format it's been taken to a whole new level.

Each expansion adds 200 light level, each of the 3 following seasons add 50 each. at the start of each expansion the cap is also moved up by quite a bit. So where do i take issue you ponder?
well let me tell you about how a simple leveling mechanic will turn your game into a chore.

The way leveling work

The light level has 3 caps
Soft cap which is current max minus 60
Powerful cap which is current max minus 10
And Pinnacle cap which is current max.
So if the max light was 1060
Soft cap would be 1000
Powerful cap would be 1050
and Pinnacle cap would be 1060

How do these caps work :
- Soft cap : is the easiest one to level it's a passive grind pretty much every drop you will get while under it will be an upgrade.
- Powerful cap : is the second easiest only certain activities will grant powerful rewards and those can only be completed once per week. except for the competitive playlist in PvP but that's an edge case.
- Pinnacle cap : this one is the worst one, the sources for pinnacle rewards are low and limited to once per week the upgrade will always be a +1 to an slot that means that it takes SEVERAL weeks of chores to get to pinnacle cap.

Time consuming leveling

But you say in the back : you don't "need" to reach pinnacle cap and you're right you don't most activites you'll do just fine with the powerful cap which is easily reachable in a few days of work, however
remember how they sunset armor?
To get good armor you need 3 things
First a lucky roll on a piece you need
Secondly you need to upgrade it to power
and Thirdly you need to upgrade your armor slots to be able to slot in the mods you want.
that require upgrade mats, upgrade mats you need to acquire from a mode called nightfalls
the most efficient way to get those are to run them in master mode which requires;
an organized squad, and high light to be precise PINNACLE CAP, so basically to grind
materials you need to grind for power level for which you need to grind armor for which you need to grind power level- And it goes on forever

What i mean to say is that if you want to have your gear customized the way you want , you'll need to spend WEEKS doing the same 3 activities over and over. it's maddening

Chapter 3 : Eververse
Good lord eververse.
What have we done to deserve this... abomination..
Now i understand a game needs funding especially live service games like destiny
However i do believe that with the constant growing playerbase the game does not need the eververse
not when bungie already sells yearly expansions for 50€ along with season passes which are 10€ each
you get the first free with the expansion or at lest you used to so that's "only" 80€ a year to play the game with all the content unlocked
Here is where i take issue with the system.
Two things that are shady about the in game Eververse store;
One it does that thing where you have to go through another currency to hide the real price that's a manipulation tactic a lot of other companies use instead of letting you just pay upfront for a produce.
Two they exploit something called "Fear of missing out" By rotating items in and out of the store and not bringing them back until way later, they also do that in a way with seasons. that forces playtime and careless spending. it's predatory.

Conclusion
Destiny.. oh you... bloody.. game.
The game in itself is a good game , the art direction is great the music is great
the lore is very nice
the gunplay feels AMAZING
but the company behind it just wants to milk every second and penny you have, i do not recommend playing
or even touching it.. just look at it from afar you'll get the full experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat to say about destiny 2, is this game great in a lot of ways? Yes. Is is a giant flaming turd? Also yes...

Overall the gameplay is actually really good and there is a lot of amazing mechanics from the character classes and their abilities, Special mission specific game mechanics, the way you get to level up and progress through looting, and the movement in this game is fairly top notch. The story of the destiny universe is also very well built and laid out, there are plenty of awesome characters to meet along the journey. There are plenty of bugs and annoying mechanics as well, certain enemies that break physics and server issues for every occasion. The PVE aspect of it is fairly grindy and repetitive but it is a looter shooter so that's pretty normal, and recent years have added a bit more to do. The PVP aspect of it is fun but a bit sweaty and depending on the day you will most likely encounter a ton of players who don't take damage or teleport 5 feet away due to de-sync and connection issues. Cosmetics are pretty fun now with the new ornament system so that's cool.

I used to Love this game, then I hated it, now I love it again mostly. There are a ton of issues with this game and that's probably never going to change but I would say that there is plenty of good to outweigh the bad. So overall I would say its a good buy with the new content at least, Especially if you can get it on sale.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who has been with the destiny franchise from the beginning, i can honestly say that Bungie has managed to ruin something with so much potential. I am a huge fan of the original destiny, having bought the game at launch in 2014 and sinking 2000+ hours into it on Xbox. The original destiny was something special, always having some sort of loot to chase or enjoyable activities to complete was what kept me hooked. Destiny 2 on the other-hand i find to be a mind-numbing experience compared to the first game. nothing but copy-pasted content and weapons from the first game, lacking the charm and excitement that made them special in the first place. Simplified customisation options, specifically the subclass trees, stripping a large amount of choice from players. And a terrible monetization system featuring items that should just be earnable from completing in game activities. Maybe its just nostalgia, but this game is a shell of its former self. Nothing in it feels like destiny anymore, even the raids feel more like a chore than the fun and compelling activity that you and your friends would spend hours trying to complete on a friday night. ill admit that the game is in a better state than it was at launch and some of the more recent content has been somewhat enjoyable but this game doesn't have the same place in my heart that it used to have.

Extra gripe: The PVP is now ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥, do not play PVP if u want to have any sort of fun playing this game.\t\t\t\t\t
