Have over 1.5k hours between D1 Vanilla, dark below, house of wolves, taken king, rise of iron and D2 Vanilla, curse of osiris in PS4. Have about 300 hours of D2 Forsaken in battle net plus the 1.3k I have on steam, so I'd say all in all I have about 3k hours in the Destiny universe and I'd say I'm a "veteran" of sorts, so if these "credentials" serve any purpose, go ahead and read my pros and cons. This is all subjective, so try it (since it's f2p) if my words influence in any way your opínion.

TL;DR, pros and cons below:
this is one of my favorite games of all time, but I understand the difficulty of picking it up without a guide or a friend. Nonetheless in my opinion, the whole gameplay experience is unlike any other game and it stills feels good to start the game and play a couple hours a day. I highly recommend this game while also understanding that it might be a tough journey to understand anything if you're a new player.

Pros:
- One of the best gunplays in a shooter
- Beautiful graphics and scenery
- Good class customization and build variety for a shooter/RPG
- Content can be challenging or easy, depending on how you approach the activities and the difficulty you choose
- Weapon variety and perks makes chasing loot not boring and not feel too grindy.
- Lore is fantastic (albeit not told in a good way ingame, but lots of lore books to read for lore freaks)
- Can be played mostly solo (except for Raids and comp for pvp) or with friends/people and still have a pleasant gaming experience.

Cons:
- Not much content for the F2P people, treat this as a demo of sorts before buying the expansions and season pass
- A bit of a shortage of loot after Bungie vaulting everything pre Forsaken and (almost) everything Shadowkeep and prior is sunset by level cap, which was adressed by Bungie hiring more people for the rewards/loot team and they'll be adding more weapons (6 for a total of 2 for each activity and 3 more for endgame Strikes) which is a step in the right direction, but still a ways to go. Addressed this in the last two expansion, heaps of loot added (20+ in the prior season, 30+ the current one)
- Not easy to pickup by new players and a bit difficult for returning ones. Explaining all the changes and things to do and what rewards and so on and so forth is a tad cumbersome unless you have a guiding hand.
- Activities without matchmaking (raids, high difficulty content, comp pvp) can be frustrating to play due to toxicity with LFG and elitism (aka, you need a 2.5 kd ratio, need 1000 raid clears so on and so forth).
- No ingame LFG
- Social space isn't that social, but it's a nitpick.
- Traders selling sunset gear and asking for vaulted currency to purchase certain items. Still selling sunset gear, but no longer asking vaulted currency.
- Sadly, there are instances of FOMO with certain weapons and gear that isn't tied to events like halloween or Christmas.
- The game being season based means the story is progressive and after vaulted content it's a bit of a bummer not having the full campaigns and previous season story tidbits to be explored and replayed to understand how the story progressed (this was talked about with the DCV - Destiny Vault Content, which was created to lower the size of the game by vaulting (to revamp and re-release in the future) content that was hardly played (one expansion had ~1% of people playing it at its peak last year). I'm on the fence if this was a good idea or not, but for the new/returning people, the current status of the story can be a bit of a mistery.
- Old expansions (Forsaken, Shadowkeep) are available to play, but reward sunset gear so there's really no incentive to play any of that content besides completing them to unlock exotic gear quests. Adressed in the past and current season, old activities from the mentioned expansion have reissued gear with new rolls.\t\t\t\t\t
