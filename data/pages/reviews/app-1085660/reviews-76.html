Felt the same way, as the first 500 hrs in Borderlands 2.

(•_•)
( •_•)>⌐■-■
(⌐■_■)

It's a wonderfully balanced game, with all the charm of the old Halo titles, as it especially samples the gun-play and movement, while still improving them.
The weapon system, was the first thing, that had drawn me in - as a Borderlands-Lover - and gives of a very familiar feeling, as grinding for the perfectly rolled legendary in BL 2.

The story keeps oneself heavily engaged. As a casual, you'll find the story exceedingly overwhelming, until you've understood the basis of it all.
While on the deeper end; You'll find yourself conflicted, as it draws no boundaries - even giving room for your imagination to break the 4th wall.
Seeing as weapons and armour sometimes have their own lore tab, it gives off vibes of incredible depths in a "go digging and loose yourself" atmosphere.

As a mostly solo Player; So much time invested, Yet I've never done a Raid. So I can't comment on those.

The smaller versions of the raids - dungeons - I, however, have all completed and can wholeheartedly recommend. As solo player, they offer an optional challenge to face, once experienced enough. At the same time, they are great fun with friends.
While the older dungeons have gotten quite easy in comparison, the newer once are the exact tipping point from beginner level content to a confidence boost for the harder content.

Now getting into a bit more "controversial" topic; The games monetisation.
It makes use of;
An annual "subscription", in form of the yearly DLCs (~50 €/$ on release date), whereby even the old once are required for a 100% playing experience. Keeping in mind, that some of a given DLCs content can (and has been) partially or completely removed from the game, leaving only exotics from those eras behind.
A quarterly subscription (~10 €/$), via a season pass, that locks quiet a large chuck of the seasonal content behind itself.
As well as micro transactions, in the form of cosmetics (from 2 - 20 €/$), emotes and other little see-for-yourselfs.

Conclusion; 8.5 / 10
I'd recommend it. At the very least, as the free2play version, that it generally is.
Try it. View it as a type of demo, instead of expecting a free full-blown Triple-A title, and make any purchase decision, after having made your own experiences with D2.

PS: I've created and played through a large part of the free2play experience on a second account, to give a less biased review. Mind you, that I can't just forget preexisting knowledge, to view Everything from a new player understanding.
