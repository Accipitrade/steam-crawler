TL;DR Don't expect this to be like Halo or Borderlands because it is neither. Get this game if you want extremely satisfying gunplay while running around clicking heads and experiencing some semblance of a story. Friends enhance the experience.

Pros:
- It is now Free to Play with a decent amount of content available right out of the gate to get your feet wet
- Set pieces are amazing, the environments are incredibly well designed
- The gunplay is incredible in this game. If nothing else, Bungie knows how to perfectly craft some unique experiences while clicking heads
- 90% of the content in this game is able to be completed solo and it does nicely fall on a spectrum of brainless mob killing to some fairly difficult content requiring strategy.
- Group play for PVE is also great and the endgame raids allow for some great team based gameplay
- If you're a lore/collectibles fiend, there is a TON of both to discover (doubly so with the DLCs)
- The Forsaken DLC is very much worth the money on its own, if its on sale definitely worth grabbing

Cons:
- This installation is massive
- Bungie is trying to go for this hybrid looter shooter/traditional MMO style game and that might be a bit jarring if you're mostly familiar with one or the other. Warframe is probably the closest similar experience, but even that is a bit of stretch
- Bugs/stability/performance issues are sadly somewhat common, although Bungie is pretty good at fixing truly game breaking bugs quickly
- The "non-ranked" PVP playlist does not have skill based match making which can result in some....interesting rounds
- There are a lot of things in the game that are not immediately clear, mainly around progression and what certain items are for
- True "end game" content is sadly a bit limited
- While a lot of content in this game can be done solo, most of the end game content requires a group and there is no in game LFG tool or match making for it
- The universe Bungie built is incredible, but not enough of it is actually provided in the game via cut scenes and such so the in game story experience is often times a bit "light"
