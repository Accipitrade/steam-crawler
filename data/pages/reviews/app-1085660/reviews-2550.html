Destiny 2 - Greatness with flaws

As someone who never played Destiny, I started playing Destiny 2 right on the release. I had a blast in the 400+ hours I put into the game in its first year, including the "Curse of Osiris" expansion. After that, I dropped the game for the time being until the free-to-play release on Steam in 2019.

I was skeptical at first, thinking about the reasons I dropped Destiny 2 back then: content drought - I would say today. All in all, I went for a fresh start, deleted all my characters, and started over. Gone was the Red War campaign; here to stay the seasonal model of D2. Against my first feelings when returning to D2, I felt it again: the excitement for loot, the love for the gunplay, relaxing in strikes, and yeah ... Gambit. (To be honest, I like it.)

Destiny 2 is still awesome when it hooks you in the loot-and-shoot loop and still frustrating when the disconnects, lags, etc. got you. I put 1.000+ hours into the game after deleting my characters with about 400+ hours of playtime. I would say this game is truly great ... with flaws.

The flaws
  • Introduction to the game: it's a mess! Even as someone playing this game for a long time I don't know how to introduce new players properly to the game. So many systems to explain, so many times you can't answer questions to in-game-descriptions like "Does more damage against bosses." (Boss Spec) Why isn't there a simple "+7.77% DMG against bosses" description? Why do I need to ask Reddit or a Wiki for this? And why is the introductory questline forcing you to play certain modes/activities you're not interested in at all?
  • Currencies & buzzwords: God bless the number of currencies added, removed, or reworked in the game. Noone that decided to play this game has a clue what he/she is supposed to do with them. A friend that played D2 in the Season of the Splicer with me phrased it pretty well: "They could also write: Collect X Bibbly and Y Bubbly to defeat Wobbly the bad guy to help Dumblenumble to defeat the darkness."
  • Bountyland: Yes ... bounties. They are here, they give you XP. That's it. Thanks - next! Really, no one needs them except you're in power-grind mode and want to take part in the world's first race of a new raid. I mean I do them - from time to time. But even without any bounties, I have no problem reaching level 100 of the season pass in 3 months.
  • Pay to play: I know Bungie needs to make money to keep the lights on. But, and I really think this isn't asked too much: Locking complete ability classes, and activities behind paywalls that are the main attraction of a season without any kind of "friend-pass" I think turns away a lot of potential players when it comes to "giving Destiny 2 a shot".
  • Eververse: Just because everyone complains about it. I don't. It's customizations only. Enjoy your stay.

The greatness
  • Soundtrack: Yes I start with this. It's awesome! Keep up the good work Bungo. ;)
  • Gunplay: One of the best, if not the best gunplay I had in years. Each gun feels distinct from another and the random perks add up to the personality of each gun you find. (I'm curious what Bungie is going for with the weapon crafting that comes to D2 with the next major expansion.)
  • Enemy design: It's on point and I would say the enemy AI is one of the better ones in shooters like that. The only downside are these ... dumb immunity phases on certain bosses.
  • Story & lore: The world-building of D2 is and always was good, but the last three seasons (Chosen, Splicer and Lost) just showed how good Bungie is in creating believable and great characters in its world. Most "secret" lore is still in lore pages and stuff like that, but it adds just enough more depth to the universe for those who are eager to just get more into the story.
  • Gameplay: It's rock solid and entertaining. The power fantasy is there! Enjoyable strikes, Gambit matches are fine too (after the thousandth rework), Crucible is fine for one or twenty rounds (yes, including Trials).
    Nothing to say about raids, because I'm a lonely Guardian and playing cards with my Ghost in the evening.

TL;DR
Destiny 2 is a great looter shooter for those who love good gunplay or want to enjoy a power fantasy. There is a lot to do and weapons to collect as long as you can look over the content vaulting and are willing to pay about ten bucks for three-month-long seasons. When you look at the content vaulting keep in mind that D2 is going for a rotational reintroduction and removal of certain game aspects, systems, and planets. It's ... always changing, but like the memes: "If you close your eyes, It seems like nothing changed at all." This is a good, and a bad thing.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I had some strong reservations about pulling the trigger on the Destiny 2 PC port through Steam. After a year of waiting for the original Destiny port, which Bungie said was coming multiple times, before they decided to drop it altogether, I decided to jump in and give it a shot. The game is a decent mmo with some interesting mechanics. I liked being able to just wander around to see what was what. This game doesn't necessarily force you into the same old point A to B to A questing. The quests are there, if you want to purchase them with a currency called glimmer, but you can also just walk into a public event, engage in strikes, or enjoy some pvp. The graphics are decent, but some of those jump ships seem like they're right out of No Man's Sky. Regardless, the game is really fun overall. So much loot, and the weapons are many.\t\t\t\t\t
