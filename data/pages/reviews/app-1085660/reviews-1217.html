It's the same old story, short campaign with continually reused assets and mission. The same old busywork is present and hasn't been innovated on, Bungie putting in least amount of work they can get away with and charging more than any other expansion. Europa is a fantastic zone with barely anything in it, only two drop points to fly in on (Get your running shoes on and make sure your sparrow has a full tank of... Ether?). Bungie still requires you to go thru the campaign on ALL characters (Cannot skip it) and gates the new subclass behind it which is fine on the first character but simply annoying on the rest. Fully unlocking (at least what is currently available.) the subclass requires A LOT of busywork that simply isn't fun after the first hour or two and it becomes a mindless trek (Not skippable either if you want to play another class, gotta go thru it all over again.).

New exotics are fun, however for everything that's been removed in the sunsetting revamp the game feels hollow. There's only a handful of new legendary weapons that are available which is disappointing. Bungie could have added the transmogrification system in this expansion to help you customize the look of your character which would have been fantastic but they didn't. The new armor looks fine but there's nothing special about it. With the way stats work in the game you're better off using the higher stat roll gear you've already previously obtained.

It's a shame.. Since Bungie split from Activision-Blizzard I had hoped the game would get better but it hasn't, it's become stagnant. Putting out sparce amounts of content and stretching it so thin that when you initially enjoyed it, it becomes increasingly more disappointing. AND still no improvements with quality of life updates to the game! (No improvements to the vault, no in game group finder, no improved postmaster, etc.)

Beyond Light has also been riddled with the same bugs and exploits that have been present previously in Destiny 2 that continue to plague the players experience...

Beyond Light players will get the least amount of content and effort put into Desitny 2 while also paying the most it has ever charged for an "expansion". Previously for $39.99 you'd get the expansion + the first season pass, not this time, you have to shell out an extra $10 for that privilege bringing the comparative cost to $49.99.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell, they undid sunsetting. However, the game is so full of bugs, glitches, and other similar stuff, I really still can't recommend it.

Pros:
1. Lots of neat guns and neat perks. Most of those perks are RNG as well, leaving some room for some grinding fun to get the perfect roll for yourself. Can be a bit tedious, but I'll come back to that.
2. Good gunplay. The guns generally feel nice to use, and work well enough on enemies.

Cons(It's a long list...):
1. Events are just FOMO(Fear Of Missing Out)-laden grindfests for the most part. Don't expect anything beyond them holding out a bit of loot and going "Oh, if you don't get it now, you won't ever get it!" and making a point of being vague on if the event will ever come back just so they can do it again next year with the same amount of FOMO. They're best enjoyed by getting the one thing you want and not touching the game again for a minimum of several weeks.
2. The physics engine is total . You can get killed by stuff that can't even hurt you with their guns because of it. All they have to do is melee you near a wall, cover, or where there's a slight bump in the terrain behind your heel, and you hit it for a bajillion physics damage thanks to the knockback, resulting in literally EVERYTHING being able to instakill you with a melee attack. I'm stunned by the fact they thought that was a good idea to leave uncapped.
3. The game loop is literally just grind for the sake of grind. That's coming from me, a prior WARFRAME player. You know, the game literally known for crazy amounts of grind?
4. Owned by Bungle, a completely untrustable company who is known for being scum at this point. Everyone collectively cringes when updates are released now because each one progressively makes the game worse. Between them releasing Sunsetting(Basically wiping your save file every three months), the new money-grab of a Transmog system for armor(You can customize armor, but only if you grind hundreds of hours per armor piece. Or just pay ten bucks to do your whole set at once), and a million other things, you have to play this game prepared to never have them value your time or money.
5. Totally ignores the community. To get them to remove sunsetting, a mechanic that basically wipes your save file every three months, it took them losing upwards of 80% of their community. Even then, they still didn't restore any of the affected weapons or armors despite the thousands of hours many individuals have put into getting them, simply saying 'Yeah, we won't do it again' like that's some kind of reassurance. Even though they did the same thing in Destiny 1 and said the same thing then. Sure, whatever you say, Bungle.
6. PvP. Destiny 2 is primarily a *PvE* game. However, it suffers from a hilarious case of identity crisis. It *thinks* it's a hardcore PvP game. They use the same sandbox for balancing, so the result is that PvP is full of people who simply camp corners with shotguns thanks to their tight spread and ability to instakill at 20ft away thanks to the small maps while scout rifles are left useless in PvE because they got nerfed for PvP reasons. End result? PvP is basically like Mario Kart turned FPS. No skill is involved. The winning side is just whoever has better shotguns and can camp harder. Meanwhile, the PvE has a large number of now non-viable weapons that were fine until they were nerfed to fix PvP. Hilariously, this even applies to abilities. Nova Warp is a great example, as it's a Warlock **SUPER** ability that is now borderline useless thanks to PvP "balancing".
7. The lore. This would be a pro as the lore is super neat and cool as well as very deep(Think almost like the Star Wars Expanded Universe), but given that they literally completely ignore it all over the place in their own game, it ends up acting more as a way to end up misinformed when they retcon the next thing so they don't have to know their own stories.
8. Rampant cheating. You're guaranteed to find aimbots in at least every other match, and paid carries are everywhere. I'll cover why in a moment, but it's pretty damn bad. Bungie even knows about the problem and, instead of actually make an anti-cheat that's more effective than placing a crude drawing saying 'plz no rob' on your car and calling it an anti-theft system, opted to simply try to sue the companies making the cheats. Literally as predicted, they just went away for a few weeks before some new ones resurfaced with completely different names and... the exact same tools. It's almost like all they did was make these less than legal companies do was change their names. I can not roll my eyes hard enough.
9. The difficulty curve for ALL of their content. The way they make things harder is they just make the enemies do way more damage, have way more health, and then add a million modifiers to the activity. For example, your average Legend(second hardest difficulty) Nightfall activity is literally just the lower version with more health, more one-shot attacks from enemies(most of which are borderline undodgeable), and modifiers such as Match Game(you do f*** all damage to shields unless you're running the same energy type to match it), Empath(All melee attacks will literally oneshot you), and so on. Not fun. We're here to fight the enemies, not the modifiers, but that's all you'll spend your time doing. Cheese strats shouldn't be a requirement to accomplish ANY of the activities, let alone the vast majority. Yet here we are.

-Grinding a perfect roll on a weapon can be incredibly aggravating:
--If the weapon is just a random world drop, there's no way to even guarantee you get the right weapon, meaning if you want, say, a Eternal Blazon rifle, you could go through hundreds of drops before even getting one, let alone trying to get a hundred plus drops of the gun itself for the right roll. Lots of RNG, but no way to direct a lot of it.
--If it's something like a Raid weapon, you can only get a limited number per week of them, and only if you're lucky enough to have them be what drops from the raid. You want a god roll of the Heritage shotgun from the DSC raid? Some weeks you might not even get one on your run, and you won't be able to get one after that.

-The Raids:
--Many of them are overly complex and they also don't explain anything about how to do them, requiring you either have someone teach you during what will likely be a(not joking or exaggerating) 2-5 hour run, or watch literal hours of Youtube videos just to learn how to do them.
--They only give you the rewards once a week so they can force you to get on once a week through FOMO instead of letting you grind them at your leisure. When did video games turn into literal weekly and daily chores?!
--Most mechanics simply kill your entire team if anyone screws up. Anyone in a six person party. The vast majority of the methods you are killed are either 'just cuz' or wildly lore unfriendly. You either drop dead for literally no apparent reason or for some arbitrary reason that runs literally counter to how physics work in their own lore. This is wildly unfun and against a ton of basic game design principles.

They have started to, in order to compensate for story telling in Lightfall's DLC, hide important plot points in seasonal content where they'll stop being accessible after the year is up(f*** new players, right?), and have capped your power level for most activities, making what little was actually fun and enjoyable now just a painful grindfest where you have to play peekaboo from behind cover for half an hour rather than enjoying your space wizard with guns fantasy. Just go play Warframe. Very similar game with literally none of these problems.\t\t\t\t\t
