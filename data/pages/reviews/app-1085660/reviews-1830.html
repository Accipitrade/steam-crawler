\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn the years leading up the free to play release of Destiny 2 on Steam, I heavily questioned if this game was one I would enjoy. After several plays of Destiny 1 on a friend’s account, I decided that something drastic would need to happen to get me into this game. Little did I know that only a few years later I would be diving headfirst into the sequel.

Once I started playing, there was no stopping. Day after day, I would hop into the game and time would fly by. I have never put 50 hours into a game in a week before Destiny 2. Even out of the game, I was doing research on skills and weapons to take my experience to the next level. There is a learning curve to each class, but it is not too intense (which is enjoyable because it allows for the mastery of several classes).

The one downfall that this game has is the bounty system. At the time of writing this review, Bungie (the developer) has addressed the community’s frustration with this system. I am excited to see what changes are implemented in the coming seasons.

Overall Destiny 2 has held my attention span for well over 6 months now and the constantly updated content keeps me interested. I highly recommend this game to anyone. 10/10

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing Destiny since November of 2014 (I literally had to dig up when I started playing cause it's been that long) and I can easily say this is on my Top 5 Favorite Game Series. I can't tell you how many hours of my life I gave away to this game, I spent days playing nothing else and I did it all by myself. D1 was a joy to go through and doing it solo gave it the difficulty and pace I needed to play it.

When Destiny 2 was announced, I was so excited for it that I even pre-ordered the collectable edition of the game and I played it since it dropped for quite a bit of time. I won't lie though, I took a long break for awhile cause I had to move and bunch of other crap. But each time I went back to playing more D2, I was never bored. I always had something to do, bounties and activities, season passes, etc. I never had a dull moment, I wasn't even upset when they decided to go free. I thought it was a good way for more people to experience the game and find the activities they enjoy most to do.

Personally I bought everything up to before Forsaken was released on my Xbox before I ended switching to PC. It was rough for me to switch from controller to mouse/keyboard but it just feels more comfortable now and I have an easier time playing. I enjoyed it so much that I bought the available expansions for PC and I've just been playing on and off for awhile until it started getting closer to the release of Beyond Light. I even pre-ordered the deluxe edition. I do not play when it comes to this game.

When the Beyond Light dropped, I had gotten up early and made sure everything was patched and ready and waited in the queue to play. The BL DLC is so beautifully done, the atmosphere is beautiful, the game play, the graphics, the interwoven quests to do for the DLC is a great idea, I even noticed the updated graphics when it comes to the Guardians. Facial features are more sculpted, they have more expression, the hair is more textured and looks better than the hair that seemed to just sit on top of the Guardians heads.
Not only that but the way Quests pop up and how its organized makes it easier to get through, the UI for the Triumphs and Collections feels easier to get through with better organization, the lore tab, the exotic ornaments tab, well thought out and eased in. I also really like how the Stasis tab in the Super diamonds(?) is much more customizable compared to the previous Arc, Void, and Solar diamonds.

What I'm trying to say is that I love pretty much everything about this game. Even if I'm not a fan of the game mode Gambit, it is still well done and interesting for sure. I may not be good at the Crucible but I'll queue up for it and play some rounds because I enjoy myself there. Yeah, some guns/armor/supers are OP at the moments but it doesn't mean it'll be like that forever. I just live in the moment and enjoy the games that I like to play. And honestly, I don't care if they vault content away to make room for more, it's vaulted away not thrown away. I'll keep spending my money on this game because I genuinely enjoy it.

IDK man, this game is good and you just gotta have some patience, not every good thing about a game will be dropped right away otherwise it gets boring.\t\t\t\t\t
