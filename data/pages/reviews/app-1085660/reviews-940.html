\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my second game review ever which is made for my second biggest online-shooter game after Warframe. So please bare with me if I'm not that good at writing a review.

Overview

Alright, so Destiny 2 is a very action-oriented multiplayer game, which revolves around you doing many activities in many different areas. Some are like chunks of open-world where you can hang around with other passerby (like the Tower which is a hub area full of NPCs and EDZ which is an open field littered with enemies and occasional public activities), and some are missions that requires you to gather up as a team or just go alone and kill hostiles in unique areas. There are also PvP-style activities and typical high-leveled Raids that may interest you. From those missions and activities, you'll usually get a resource that you can exchange for new-and-better equipments. Sometimes you'll get equipments as direct rewards.

The good sides

I like many things from this game. The guns look sleek, futuristic, and diverse. The environments look beautiful. Indeed there are power levels that you must increase to do some higher-tier activities, but when you get down to it, your skill will have more impact than the gun you're holding. You have to know when to shoot, deploy abilities, and take cover. So I think the game still has some sense of appreciating the player's efforts. Also, there are enough configuration options to make your own ultimate character, which the min-maxers may be interested in. The loot reward systems are good enough. I don't feel underpowered just because I don't have the highest-tier arsenal (legendary or exotic). And the game is updated frequently, which shows that the devs care about the player experience as much as the money in their bank accounts. Say what you will about the concept of a game-as-a-service, but so far they've been providing good service. EXCEPT for some things:

The bad sides

Yes, the sky and landscape looks beautiful. BUT YOU CAN'T INTERACT WITH THEM. You just walk pass them and probably forget after the enemies start coming. You can't climb, you can't throw slabs of stones at bosses, you can't overload a reactor to make Michael Bay Explosions, all in all very minimal environmental actions; except if it's already scripted. It's an only-look-but-no-touching kinda relationship. I find the movement inertia kind of annoying, at least when playing as a warlock. I died once just because I can't change directions mid-air. It's unacceptable for a space-faring-elemental-controlling-reality-warping spec ops. It's silly, and unnecessary. Other than that, I need to criticize the dev's decision to put the old content to a vault and replace it with a new, more lackluster one (Beyond Light). I want to defend them for the great game they've made, but I can't. We paid for the content. Literally. And now it's being cut outright, with some features converted into a mere shop interface. When it comes to real money, it's becoming a real-life problem, not just virtual. There are no excuses.

Bottom Line

So in the current state, the game is doing great. Lots of things to do, lots of equipments to grind, lots of configurations to get nerdy at. You should try it, especially with friends. It's fun, and for many people fun is enough. But the future state is where I'm worried at. Will we have to pay again when they finally put the old contents (The Red War, Menagerie, Black Armory, etc) back into the game? I can't tell. For now, just download the main game and try it since it's free and no harm will be done. Get Beyond Light if you want, but I can't give you any heads up since I don't own it. I'm just telling you to play and have fun, and only pay if you feel like it's worth it. I give the game 8/10 .\t\t\t\t\t
