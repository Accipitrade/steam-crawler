\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI started D2 during the first lock down in the UK in 2020, I started a free account and had a fantastic time. Beautiful Sci-Fi worlds with fun and challenging opponents, I could not believe the creators of D2 (Bungie) let F2P users have such access.
4 months later and about 200hrs into the free account I still was loving it and thought that it was time to buy the game plus a few expansions. It opened large parts of the game I had never seen, more surprises and interesting content. I have been a fan ever since.

There are some downsides to D2 as with all things in life, one of the big things being grinding, once you have run most of the strikes, dungeons, raids and worlds a few times you are essentially waiting for the new content to drop and when it does there are normally a lovely little story line that leads to a hill of repetitive grinding for a few new weapons. Then you are left in the content hole you have done before, normally after the first few day of a content drop the player base spikes and dissipates just as quickly as that is the only thing experienced players play for. The online community is fairly friendly and I have had no negative experiences, but unless you are in an active clan, you essentially play by yourself as there's almost no world chat and hardly anyone ever types/chats in the game.

Also, there's no matchmaking for raids, dungeons or higher level strikes, most mmo's nowadays have this feature, but D2 only has it for low level strikes and that's all, so unless you want to work your way into a clan/guild and then get a team together that 100% of the time require discord then that content is essentially locked off. A shame.

But these gripes to one side, I still enjoy D2 and will keep playing well into 2022. It is one of the best FPS games to slam on some fun music and jump into some strikes and tear up the map with 2 buddies.
I wish they would bring back sparrow racing from D1 ^_^\t\t\t\t\t
