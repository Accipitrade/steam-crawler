\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am giving this game a thumbs up, but I am a former player who got tired of the same old story and struggled with giving this a thumbs up or down, so I'm coming it from that angle. I was a die hard Destiny 1 player and logged in over 800 hours. Then Destiny 2 came out and I logged in a few hundred more on console before switching to the PC. So I have my share of time invested in Destiny.

From a franchise point of view the story is great, art direction is wonderful, and it's a great IP to get lost in. A lot of story arcs are never really given enough attention, but I completely understand it's hard to do that in this type of game. Gun play is wonderful and I can honestly say I haven't played any other FPS that has such tight controls. You can feel the weight of the weapons if that makes any sense.

My issues with Destiny comes from the unnecessary grind that the developers have put in. Often times the grind to get a weapon is so over the top that you spend hours and hours, with absolutely nothing to show for it other than shaders. In a game where there is no crafting, you end up wasting a lot of time chasing something down and you feel like the time was wasted. And I stopped playing a long time ago...I still follow Destiny and have seen that the grind has not gotten any better.

I also am not the biggest fan with how Bungie is drip feeding Destiny 2 players the same content/gear from Destiny 1 and putting that behind expansion pay walls (how many times do I need to pay for the same content?) I just think that is a little lazy and am really curious why other die hard fans go crazy and fawn over this.

Final gripe is the push to PvP game modes even for those who are more PvE focused (I'm old, my reflexes can't compete with die hards). Yes, you don't have to do PvP, but there are some quests that require you to play PvP to get a specific weapon, and what's worse, grind some pretty crazy requirements to progress (i.e. X head shots with a hand cannon to progress). I think Bungie is really trying to push PvP too much, even with all the problems plaguing it.

So in a nutshell, great game, lots of fun playing if you have a clan that you can play with consistently, but for a solo player who wants a fun PvE experience, just know that you will be locked out of a few activities.\t\t\t\t\t
