\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo, I've started playing Destiny 2, by Bungie, roughly Novemember/December 2019, seeking for great graphical experience to compensate with my new laptop. The game met my expectations right in the tutorial, with the impressive landscapes and lighting, and smooth gameplay.

Now, what mostly stands out in Destiny 2 is the PvE content. Firstly there are the campaigns, thrilling stories accompanied by plot twists, a decent difficulty, and unique characters which involves you into the D2 universe. The campaign is an essential activity for any player, and it may be needed to unlock certain features of the game, so that's negative if you don't like story modes. Anyways, then there are the cooperative strikes, raids, quests, and gambit, which are all other nice PvE activities you can play. You don't actually need 'friends' to actually play the game, I played alone for multiple hours without a problem, however you will be missing out on raids and dungeons, and some PvP modes.

Surprisingly, even if you finish all of the main content in the game, like the main quests, there is still good endgame content, such as raid challenges, higher difficulty strikes (nightfalls), and quests for special rarity items (exotics). So, about the 'grinding' that many people talk about: Like, there's a bit, honestly I wouldn't say it's too much though, bounties are mostly optional, and even if they are necessary for a quest, they don't take more than 10 minutes, and you can do multiple at once.

Destiny 2 then has its own PvP content too, with a few different game modes. It is fun, and although people say there are cheaters, I haven't quite seen any before. In more competitive modes like trials though, there may be more cheaters. If you want D2 "only" for PvP, I wouldn't quite recommend it, since you need to do the PvE activities and quests in order to get good weapons.

For DLC, I recommend nearly 100% both of them if you like PvE content and campaigns, they are just like a full new game, with a lot of new different exotic weapons, dungeons, activities, raids, and new strikes too. Shadowkeep DLC will require you to finish the campaign in order to get access to additional content, and its campaign is a bit weaker, so if you don't like story mode I wouldn't quite recommend Shadowkeep as much as Forsaken. Lastly, season pass isn't bad at all if you like PvE content. Compared to previous seasons, (I have only been there for 2 seasons), It has a decently larger amount of content, with legendary lost sectors, bunker arenas, fairly easy bounties, unique rewards (kind of), with way more challenging activities.

So, unlike many other games, it's free for a great amount of content, so even if you aren't convinced, it's worth giving it a try (that's if you're up to the 100GBs of game files).\t\t\t\t\t
