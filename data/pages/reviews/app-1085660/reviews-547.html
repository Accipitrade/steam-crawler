\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'm a destiny one vet, and I gotta say...
This game has more lows than highs, honestly I'm conflicted on how to feel about this game.

I keep hoping that one day D2 will be a game that I can confidently recommend to strangers or even friends who don't play it, but with core content to the experience being locked behind pay walls that of themselves are always one announcement away from being vaulted. (i.e stasis, raids, dungeons, middle tree light subclasses, etc.)
It's a hard sell.

I don't regret the money and time I've spent on this game, but I fear my enjoyment of this experience is only because I was willing to thoughtlessly hand over money in hopes that "This time it's gonna be worth it!"

I like the game I think it's fun, but it's the kind of fun that hits hard and fades quickly.

As of this review if you aren't already into destiny 2 I'd check back in a couple of months or a year to see if the dust has settled.
Destiny 2 is in a rough place right now , and I cant, with good faith, recommend a game that asks you to pay money for content that might not even be in the game a year from now.

Conclusion:
Destiny 2 is a unique experience that many have tried to emulate but to little success.
For this reason I'd actually recommend playing the free version of the game and getting a feel for it, see if it's your kind of game.
If you do become addicted I strongly urge you to spend your money wisely, really think about what you're buying and be aware of the massive time sink that awaits you.

I wish you luck out there, Guardian.\t\t\t\t\t
