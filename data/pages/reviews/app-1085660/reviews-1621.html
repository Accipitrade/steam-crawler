\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEDIT: I can no longer recommend new players to start this game. I still think it looks beautiful, has fun shooter gameplay, and one of the most interesting sci-fantasy settings ever. But after taking some time away, I've realized just how badly Bungie mismanages it. They always have their focus on the wrong things, the endgame MMORPG mechanics have always been awful, it is inhibitive to low-investment players, and I realize now how little freedom I had to choose what I wanted to do in the game. It was all about doing what the game said to do as you continue the grind. It's a shame, since the occasional bits of traditional campaign gameplay are excellent, but that's just such a small focus (and there aren't good ways to replay story missions).

Anyways, my original review follows. The only thing I'd change is that the content is not longer there; Bungie removes major story content and entire world regions to "make room" for new content, half of which rotates in and out on a seasonal basis. There is no longer a main action RPG story to play through, only the endgame grind looter addiction machine. If Bungie ever pulled together all the story missions and sold that without the looter shooter grind, I'd recommend that to everyone. But that's not what Destiny is at all.

---------- Original Review ----------

I play way too much of this game. I know the Destiny community has a reputation for complaining and trashing the game, but it's still one of the best shooters ever made in one of the coolest game universes ever.

- Possibly the best feeling shooting mechanics ever.
- Very beautiful, mostly due to phenomenal world art (though the graphics good technically as well)
- Plenty of content in the expansions: even if the community complains about not having enough to do, there is plenty of content, we've just put 1000 hours in already and done most everything multiple times. There's more than enough to keep a new player occupied for a good long while.
- The world of Destiny is great, I'm not going to spoil any of it, go see for yourself.
- Performance is great, it ran perfectly smooth when I had only an i5-3570K and GTX 660 Ti\t\t\t\t\t
