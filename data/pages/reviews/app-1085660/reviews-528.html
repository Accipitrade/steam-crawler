\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first started playing D2 right before Beyond Light and have 927.7 hours in the game. I quite enjoy the game as it comes 2nd only to Minecraft in hours played. But anyways, something that is present in all games (even Minecraft) is pros and cons. Here are some of the ones I notice in Destiny 2:
+The community isn’t extremely toxic. Though this may seem like a low bar to cross, there are games out there where the community hinders new players from joining/keeping players engaged in the game because of their use of language, their reactions to losing, the jokes they find acceptable, etc. I’m not saying that the Destiny community is amazing. There are definitely toxic parts to it, but it is a community where you are less likely to hear racial slurs and, I feel, is more inclusive to women and people of the LGBTQ+ community. Though then again, maybe the people I have encountered in the community are the odd ones out, who knows.
+ Lots of new content. I feel that Bungie has really done a good job giving us new content to play through at a decent pace. There are new seasons a few months apart, each with new activities (if you pay for them) and even if you don’t want to pay for anything there is still a good amount of new content. Though as far as free content goes, Bungie is still tinkering with what works best. Also, when you have finished the seasonal content there is a weekly rotation of activities to partake in such as, Trials of Osiris, the Iron Banner, and loot boosts to already mainstream activities like strikes and gambit.
+LOTS OF GOOD STORY. I love stories and I also hate and adore unanswered questions. I’ll be honest, I have fallen down the Destiny lore rabbit hole and I now live there. The story is well thought out and extremely interesting and captivating. I also love how each season weaves the story into the gameplay and builds on the last season. I mean seriously, whoever writes these knows what they are doing!
+ The graphics are amazing. If I asked one of my friends what Destiny 2 was, they’d probably say “that fps game with the high detail and cool ornaments that you are obsessed with”. This game is beautiful, not only in its environments but also in the armor you can wear. The ornaments, as they are called, for guns are mediocre at best, but the armor is fire.
+ You can purchase exclusive skins/emotes with in game currency. In Destiny 2 there is bright dust which is a currency you can get in the game. It is not easy to get, and the process of getting it is extremely grindy, but at least you can get it. It is used to buy things that normally can only be bought with real world cash. It should be noted that not all things can be bought with bright dust.
+ The base game is free. Even if you are not 100% sure if you want to invest your time into D2, you can at least try it out before investing any money into it.
+ The game is fun. A lot of people play D2 because they like it, me included, but not everyone likes the same games. Personally, speaking I would recommend this game to friends (as I already have) just because I enjoy playing it so much. I do have to say though, this game is much more fun with friends than done solo.
+ A lot of weapon variety. There are so many weapons in Destiny it is unreal. This allows players to choose the exact weapon they wany to use or allow them to use specific weapons that they prefer for different activities.
+ Builds are technically a thing. There are mods for armor and perks on weapons that allow you to make builds for certain activities. I’ll be honest bungie kind of underestimates the D2 community’s ability to create broken builds but hey, it’s fun to be able to throw 10 grenades that that boss in a few seconds.
+ Destiny 2 offers both pve and pvp content. Sure, the pvp isn’t csgo level amazing, but it doesn’t suck. If you are really into the pvp side of things and you want a really nice fps that stars in pvp, then maybe get something else. But if you just transferred from controller (like I did), haven’t done that much pvp, or are more of a pve player, then this should work out just fine.
-THE GRINDS OMG. This game is a looter shooter at its core, that’s what it is and that’s what it will always be. Unfortunately, in Destiny 2 this means a freak ton of grinding. I have heard so many stories of people doing the hardest activities for months on end, hours upon hours of constant grinding… only to still not get what they were looking for. I have experienced it myself even though I just started harder activities. You have to do the same strikes, the same seasonal content, the same raids, the same pvp games over and over and it is never a guarantee you will get that god roll grenade launcher with blinding naides, slideshot, and vorpal. Honestly this only really happens after season content has gone dry for the casual player, but honestly the best way to get through this stage is to just make some friends and chat while doing some strikes.
-Limited time. In Destiny, you only have so much time to unlock things. For example let’s say you buy the seasons pass, which includes new activities and new storylines. You will get a few months to play through the story and collect some loot. But if rnjesus doesn’t bless you that season and you don’t get that god roll, you can never grind for it again. This can lead to a feeling of not wanting to miss out on the chance to get a god roll weapon/piece of armor, and then extreme disappointment and anger when you get killed by an equally skilled player in pvp because they just so happen to have the god roll. This paired with grinding makes getting good roles on good weapons hard.
-New players are at a disadvantage. Everyone was once a new player, for me that was right before beyond light, and in a game like Destiny with a constantly changing storyline, I think Bungie did a decent job but not a good one. I get that introducing new players into a story that is so complex is hard, but it goes beyond that. Right after the tutorial a lot of my D2 friends are like “ok, where do I go now?” They get lost easily and without the support of someone who has played before their experience would not be great. Not only in the introduction hard, but because of the limited time mentioned above they don’t have access to weapons brought out before they choose to play. Since I am not a D2 vet I understand the pain of wanting a gun someone else had… only to accept that I will never be able to use it. There are ornaments that look SO cool that I will never get, and content that was said to be so fun, that I will never be able to play, all because I heard about Destiny 2 later than others.
-Weapons and Armor is confusing. The armor in D2 is easier to understand than the weapons by a long shot. After one simple explanation I understood it and have never had a problem since… until I found out where you could apply mods to do fancy things. Mods aren’t as bad as the weapon system we currently have, but then again, I still don’t fully understand them even after having people explain. The weapons on their own are fine. When you are first starting out you aren’t going to pay attention to what perks your gun has because they are not important. But as soon as you get to the harder activities, you will start needing specific perks on your guns to get into fireteams (groups of people) or raid teams. This is even more obvious in pvp where the difference in gun perks can often win a fight. Unfortunately, just like armor mods, gun perks are really confusing. With different perks working better for the different types of guns, different combinations you would want for pve versus pvp, the specific perks themselves being tweaked, and the fact that putting them all together into a good build is confusing is the reason why a lot of my friends and I fill out vaults up to the brim with weapons that may be a god roll.
All in all, I fell like destiny is a good game that needs some specific things changed.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game seems fun at first due to an overwhelming amount of things to do. Once caught up to the max light level, the game settles in to a pretty standard endgame. Raid, grind for better rolled items, and pvp. The key issues that killed my enjoyment for the game at this point are:
1) The primary group size is 3 and 6 making it awkward to play with friends at times.
2) The pvp is some of the worst I've ever encountered in a game. There is no skill-based matchmaking, meaning there isn't much of a platform to improve yourself. The core competitive mode wants you to win 7 games in a row, (or 7/8 matches w/ a mercy card). With no matchmaking it is incredibly frustrating, and for most people is only obtainable once a special pool is activated on Sunday.
3) The game is p2p... in 2021. Lagging is advantageous to the lagger, there are frequent desyncs, and on a daily basis you will die when you are around the corner on your screen. This combined with point 2) pretty much kills any sort of viable pvp.\t\t\t\t\t
