\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can only barely recommended this game...

Came from PS4. Many hours played. Too many when you count Destiny 1. If you like pew pew, this is one of the best game to date to get your pew pew on. Keyboard/Mouse or GamePad on the PC is very much a top notch experience. My pew pew friends would say repeatedly "Bungie has figured out the secret sauce to Aim Assist". The guns feel and behave like guns.

However. I gradually reduced my play time over the years. Grew tired of giving them money only to do more grinding.

Then they started taking away whole areas/sections of the game to play. Let's call this for what it is: You give them more money... they take away your stuff. Your gear is useless for the new DLC, while also removing areas where that gear was viable. Why would they do this? This game, being mirrored on the aging PS4, only has a limited amount of hard drive space. Each new DLC which brings new areas, means they have to sacrifice old areas to make it fit. An area you paid for. When you remove whole sections of the game like this, this makes replaying the story impossible (hint to new player: the story will make no sense, as you won't be able to play it chronologically from the beginning).

Bungie figured out the average player remains loyal for about 2 years, so let's make the game cater to this new paradigm. I can't fault them for wanting to make money! But when did Bungie stop caring about making a great game? The Destiny franchise was supposed to be a 10 year plan. Not a revolving door of two.

I get it though. This is Bungie's only way of bringing in new players, and keeping the new user experience on par with the veterans. Their plan now includes bringing back old gear, with better stats to make said gear viable in new DLC. But you have to grind for it again ;) Ouch. Believe me, this is a kick to the crouch when you already spent many hours obtaining this gear, and it's sitting in your vault. Ya know the slogan they use: "Become Legend". Well, we did. With that gear. So can we please use it? "No. Go grind for it again like a new player must."

This game is a rock solid shooter with weird/lacking longevity, or story potential. It's why it is free, no? If that doesn't bother you, and you just want to blow off the day's steam by killing aliens and not play their meta-game collecting loot drama... then damn. There are few better games. So, if you meet the above credentials, I do recommend Destiny 2. Perhaps even the revolving door DLC keeping it fresh (if you can remain calm when they remove your stuff).

Why am I salty? I suppose it's because I was sold on the "tremendous potential for what this game could be over 10 years time" bandwagon, and it's obvious Bungie has instead settled on "20 years in incoherent pieces". Money money money mooooney.\t\t\t\t\t
