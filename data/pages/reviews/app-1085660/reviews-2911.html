\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI loved Destiny 1. I played it way too much. Destiny 2 at launch was...less impressive. I sort of assumed it was because Activision was throttling Bungie, forcing them to cut effort to push micro-transactions. Now that Bungie escaped Activision's clutches, everything is great, right?

Not really.

For starters, Bungie doubled-down on microtransactions and the cash shop. Most of the development effort goes into real-money items. Shadowkeep and the Season of the Undying is a good example; the expansion itself is literally just a pile of reused assets with a story behind it. "You have to fight the ghosts of all these old bosses again! Spooky!" The season is just reskinned garbage and extremely depressing "evolving content". Meanwhile the Eververse (real money store) is constantly flooded with new items.

Next up, constant server issues. Say what you want about Activision-Blizzard, they know how to handle traffic. For whatever reason, pretty much every play session now that we're on Steam involves getting punted off entirely, or semi-constant "we have lost connection to Steam servers, please log out and back in again" issues.

As for content, it's generally just lackluster. The basic gameplay is fun, because Halo was fun. And Destiny is just "Halo: the Looter-Shooter", so it's hard to mess up the bones, but the meat is pretty poor. Forsaken was good, but everything else has been short, underwhelming, and overly-expensive, and I don't honestly see that changing.

It's F2P now so you can't really go wrong. Just don't believe any promises or hype Bungie puts out, and don't buy anything unless you can get it on sale. Wait a few weeks, see how whatever they're selling pans out, then decide to buy or pass.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing Destiny since the Alpha back when it was console only, I've put countless hours into this franchise. When vanilla Destiny 2 launched, I was very disappointed, and stepped away from the game for two years. When it was announced that Bungie was moving D2 over to Steam after their split with Activision, I figured I'd give it another shot.

First, it's Destiny. The experience has been pretty much the same since 2014. It's a grindy looter-shooter, pseudo MMORPG. If you don't like spending hours grinding for a certain item (weapon/armor piece) that you want, then this isn't the game for you. That being said, Destiny 2 has definitely come a long way. I'm constantly reminded why I fell in love with this game every time I log in.

I won't get too much into the gameplay, but you can choose three classes, Warlock, Titan, or Hunter, and each class has three subclasses with three separate skill trees. You have a decent deal of customization. There's obviously an established "meta" that hardcore players expect everyone to follow, but the player has a great deal of personalization in how they play.

I like PvE much better than PvP (Crucible), mostly because the Crucible has always gone through interesting cycles. It can be frustrating, but also rewarding when you find a way to make your play style functional, even though you will find people using the cheesiest load outs possible. Raids, dungeons, Nightfalls, and other end-game PvE activities provide a good level of challenge, with rewards that feel, well worth it.

At the end of the day, none of that is what keeps me coming back to Destiny. Truly it's the community. This is said so much that it sounds cliche, but there is no community I've ever seen or experienced that's like the Destiny community. There are people who take this game very seriously, but there's just as many people willing to help you learn the ropes. Bungie has their own "Fireteam Search" on their website which will help you find other Guardians to attempt higher-level activities with. Don't be shy, step out of your comfort zone, you might find a new friend on LFG sites.

Either way, New Light is free. New players can get a majority of this game FREE TO PLAY. The only DLCs which require a purchase are Foresaken (Year 2) and Shadowkeep (Year 3). All content from launch up to Foresaken, you can try without it denting your wallet. Sink a couple of hours into New Light if you're skeptical, if you don't like the game, no harm done. If you like it and wish to keep playing, I'd recommended that you at least buy Shadowkeep, so you'll be able to participate in all upcoming content planned for Year 3.

Like I said, it's Destiny. You can feel it out for free, and decide to purchase later, but at the very least try it out.\t\t\t\t\t
