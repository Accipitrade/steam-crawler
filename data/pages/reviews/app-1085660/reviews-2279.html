\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has an abundant amount of content to explore for new players without buying the 2 other expansions. But I find that my friends who have played this game were not as interested mostly due to repetitive grinds not really going anywhere and the fact that this game is mainly for lore seekers and maybe pvp players. If you are really into game lores, youll be interested in D2, but if youre new and never played D1 its all fine because the story now catches the player up to what has happened so far in a very smooth way and gradual. Cheaters in pvp, those need some attention as well. Anyways back to friends, my biggest concern is how D2 encourages players to play together and my main problem is that none of my friends are interested in the game at all to even play with anyone. So all the Raids, exotic missions requiring a fireteam I cannot access which is super frustrating. I am really interested in collecting all the exotics (weapons and class armour) and i cant collect all if I need a fireteam (which I just said i cant get) to get 1000 voices, whisper of the worm, rat king, outbreak and those others.

Reflection (2 years later): The game offers quite the insufficient amount of content to say the least for new players. As a D2 veteran, its sad to see older campaigns go like the Red War and now Forsaken is going to be vaulted, Bungie just likes to disappoint players sometimes. The lore is what got me hooked into this game and the subject to habit of playing this game overtime is what got me to stay, that and the fun builds you can do and the cool lore which is getting spicier and better. The community that Ive observed( even tho I dont really participate in is actually quite welcoming, tons of nice players and not many kids as far as I can see. Take that as you will. Weapon, armor, and ornament collecting is pretty fun, just dont get sucked up and buy all the unnecessary items you dont really even want because they get quite pricey. However, there are some armor pieces or weapons that require raid completions and such to even acquire which half or so of the player base cant even get. Other than those, I give this game an 9/10 on visuals, 8/10 on gameplay, 8/10 on community and 6/10 for a game being a bang for your buck.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSimply put, Destiny is the best social game ever crafted. From its rocky beginning where the only thing to do was Vault of Glass, to the greatest PVP mode of all-time in Trials of Osiris, and now the sprawling MMO-lite it has become with Destiny 2 three years in.

I'm not a social gamer by any means, and found Destiny's original beta to be quite boring, but, the simple grind and newness of needing to rely on people to finish events in a video game hooked me, and kept me coming back for more every day. Destiny has provided me with some of my most memorable gaming moments like my first raid clear with the previously mentioned Vault of Glass, or clutch 1v3 comebacks in Trials. I've met and played with countless people, providing numerous memories.

The game has gone through a lot of ups and downs over the last six years, but that gameplay loop has managed to always keep me somewhat interested. Destiny 2 got off to a very poor start, but Forsaken was the start of improvement, and Shadowkeep has built upon that foundation, and hopefully the return of Trials will see the game return to its former streaming popularity. I hope going forward into Year 4 of Destiny 2, Bungie will cement it as one of the top FULL-FLEDGED MMOs in gaming.\t\t\t\t\t
