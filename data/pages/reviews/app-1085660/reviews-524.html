\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 and I have a complicated history. I was addicted to Destiny 1 up until the Taken King where I got kind of jaded and just stopped enjoying the game. I came back near the end of Destiny 1 and enjoyed it a ton and played through all the endgame content.

Coming into Destiny 2 I was not the hardcore player I was in D1, but I still was expecting to play it a fair amount. At launch D2 was nowhere near perfect, but where it is today is much better than where it was at launch. That being said, unless you're willing to buy a bunch of seasonal content (which in all honesty is pretty repetitive in nature and not very "transformative") you probably will get bored of Destiny 2 pretty quickly. It is free to play now so I definitely would give it a shot, and I have definitely gotten my moneys worth out of Destiny 2. Whenever new DLC's come out with new stories, I always try and play them, but don't buy the yearly pass unless you fully expect to be happy doing the same grindy missions 5 months from release.

PVE is what I enjoyed the most, and raiding and doing the difficult nightfall strikes were what I would always get most enjoyment out of. The great thing about Destiny 2 is you can do almost any activity with any class, some hardcore players will tell you "you HAVE to have this subclass to do this activity" or "this weapon is needed" but in reality Bungie is pretty good about letting you play the way you want to. Try not to min/max everything and I think you'll enjoy the game a lot more than if you're always stressing to play the "right" way.

PVP is fun but not for me, if you enjoy ability shooters I think you'll love the PVP, and outside of certain playlists you don't have to get the "right gear" to excel. Overall a fun but flawed game. If you love the grind, you'll enjoy Destiny 2.\t\t\t\t\t
