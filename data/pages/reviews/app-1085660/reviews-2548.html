\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a masterpiece as it's free. For the full $60 it was, I enjoyed the main campaign. The game runs fine, about 50 FPS solid on my old 1050 Ti, yet on my new PC, it's constantly running around 160. (Though I keep it locked to 144 HZ.) The game is enjoyable just to explore and the art direction and music production are simply stunning. Nothing compares. The gunplay is smooth and fluid, which makes not only the PvE but the Crucible (PvP) extremely fun to get into and play. The only major issue plaguing the PvP is the (supposedly) overpowered Titan class which is virtually built for success in PvP, but nothing against that as you can get along with any class you decide to main. (Me, being a Warlock, I get around just fine.)

However, this doesn't even hold a candle to the fact that not being a Titan means nothing. You can make multiple characters of multiple classes, and with the newest update of the game becoming free-to-play, Destiny 2 now unlocks all of the abilities for the classes right off of the bat. You can still acquire the orignal three campaigns, the main Red War and the two DLC packs, now free, though acquiring them is confusing and a new player would probably never be able to find them. However, re-playing them as a Destiny 2 pre-order veteran for Xbox One, it was a magical sight to go through it all with friends once more. Oh, plus the introduction of Cross-Save (not cross-play, unfortunately) is such a lovely QOL (quality of life) improvement that really hits hard how Bungie considers their love for the community. While things have hit a low point, if you truly enjoy the game and get the DLC packs, there is more fun to come.

As many of my friends have mostly played the Xbox One edition, I haven't had as much time to pick up the PC version. However, this will definitely be changing in the near future as much as I have been enjoying the game. I would 95% recommend this game for anyone who wants a good shooter, and for anyone who desires a good adventure. Especially for the price (free) of the original three campaigns, I would say it is definitely worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
