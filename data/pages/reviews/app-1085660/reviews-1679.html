\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is not without fault - year 3 was "difficult" to say the least - and if I'm being honest, going free-to-play was one of the worst possible decisions Bungie could have possibly made for the short and probably long-term health of the game, with respect to PvP. The PvE is good, albeit a bit monotonous at times, and I question the developer's vocabulary when they use statements such as "meaningful choices", and "make your Guardian feel more powerful", because for the most part, the in-game economy has declined to the point where enemies grant a fraction of the currency they once did, armor and weapons cost more to upgrade, there are more sources (of more upgrade materials) to maintain an inventory of, character & weapon shaders are consumable items, that are rarely refunded if the item is dismantled. The list goes on.

There's also the problem with the power system, where your character power level seems to mean absolutely nothing, so one wonders why it's actually there. If I enter an activity, even on "Heroic" level, with a recommended power of 750, and my character is say, 1065, one would think that activity might be fairly easy. Not in Destiny 2. No, you get slapped around as if you met the bare minimum to launch into that mission.

The sandbox team also doesn't seem to understand what "balance" means, as their concept, by their own confession, entails gathering player usage data, analyzing which weapons the players use most, making them almost completely unusable, and boosting another weapon type until it is obscenely, obnoxiously powerful. It used to be funny, but now it's one of the things that makes PvP almost unplayable.

There's also the new concept of "sunsetting" weapons & armor players spent hours, weeks (and sometimes months) playing to earn. I realize sunsetting isn't new to many games, and it's happened in some form in Destiny before, but the problem here (and I knew this would happen) is they merely reissued the same, mostly bland, weapons with new expiration dates, while phasing out the ones myself and other players worked for, and have memories attached to.

The value of Season Pass content is debatable as well. The armor ornaments have 2 out of 4 seasons been hideous, and the materials granted from the pass were just a small bonus to what one might find in world (although, as I mentioned earlier, with how sparse everything is, it's a bit of a blessing). There is the aspect of the minor XP boosts and such contained in the Season Pass, which I just realized might make it considered pay-to-win, if there was actually any "winning" in Destiny. There isn't. It's just endless grinding, bounties, and the same missions.

Seasonal content...laughable, but that's been a problem for this franchise dating all the way back to the first iteration of Destiny on console (with the notable exception of The Taken King expansion, which is probably the only reason this game is still around). There are maybe 30 minutes total actual story content per season, with what I would generously estimate to be about 2 minutes of cinematic cutscenes (this has actually gotten slightly better with the seasonal content model).

Speaking of seasonal content, we mustn't forget Destiny's seasonal events such as The Dawning, Festival of the Lost (aka, "Festival of the Cost"), Guardian Games (or as I liked to call them, "Bounty Olympics 2.0"), and of course, Iron Banner, the power-level-enabled PvP event. In Destiny 1, these events were fun, although still somewhat labor intensive, it was fun to do. Now? Not even. You spend hours upon hours grinding bounties - bounties with an excessive in-game currency cost, *and* an expiration date - for obscure tasks like, "defeat 25 solar shielded shanks with an arc energy grenade launcher". Rather than taking the time to explain (ha, I didn't mean to do that, in-game joke), you can only find enemies like that in maybe two places, one of which is a raid (a 6-person mission), there are 0 arc energy grenade launchers in the game that don't suck, and then you have to kill 25? It's like that. OR the instructions are so poorly and cryptically written you have to spend hours (if not days) experimenting and researching the proper way to complete it. I'm not even kidding, google "Destiny 2 Warlock Void Feast Bounty". It's just one example.

Now, I recognize my review may seem rather scathing (and I didn't even bring up how absolutely broken PvP is). I'm simply being honest. Destiny isn't just a game, and the people in its community aren't just players. I still play - a lot - but I am critically aware of the game's (and the developer's) shortcomings. Anyone considering investing their time in it should be aware as well.

Edit: 3/18/2021 - In yet another example of demonstrating how the script leadership gives their community managers isn't the same, has decided to undo "sunsetting" - the process of making weapons and armor have a maximum power cap, and therefore irrelevant after a certain point. While some argued this would be good for the game, I personally was not in that camp; I spent too many hours working for some of those ridiculous ritual and pinnacle weapons, which are now and will forever be useless in anything other than base Crucible (PvP, which is an absolute disaster, that I avoid at all costs).

Speaking of PvP activities, Trials of Osiris, the endgame PvP activity has been cancelled more weeks than it has been active so far in 2021, or at least in Season of the Chosen. Speaking of Season of the Chosen, while some are gushing about what they call "storytelling", I can only assume they've never read anything with an actual plot. I'm sorry, this ain't it, chief. It lurches along, week-to-week, as the game hands out tiny little morsels to keep players engaged just enough to embark upon these ridiculous quest lines requiring a great deal of drudgery through old content just to get to something new.

Speaking of old content, in possibly one of the best decisions Bungie has made (and I realize this may sound contradictory), they've seen fit to bring back several missions and weapons from Destiny 1. I see this as a good move, because unlike Destiny 2, D1 was nearly perfect in every way, and I'm not sure why they decided to change anything. That's not nostalgia or romanticism. Objectively, it was just more fun to play, so I'm at least pleased with that.

My original assertion still stands. I'm not certain this game is worth playing, unless you have friends you can constantly complete activities with. It's not the playability, or the content that makes it worthwhile, but the players you meet. If Destiny 2 were a solo game, it would have failed long ago.\t\t\t\t\t
