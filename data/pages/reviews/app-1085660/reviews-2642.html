This game is one massive illusion, set up in such a way that in makes you repeatedly grind the exact same content over and over again, thousands of times. That is how this entire game works now. This game would legit be better without PvP and until this game launched on steam it had the absolute best PvP I had ever played. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tIts a pretty fun game, a bit different from the original but in a good way.

***I should start off by mentioning that at the time of writing this review, I've only played one character, and I haven't even gotten close to finishing all of the content for that "file". So if you're looking for something you can sink time into, this is a pretty safe bet.***

-Classes : You have three character slots, and there are three character classes; seems pretty obvious on what to do there. The classes are Titan, Warlock, and Hunter.
~Titans like to get close and personal, and a lot of them have melee skills that give shields, heal themselves, or just let them do more damage with punches while their armor is generally more themed around being tough, and packing on more resilience. These are your meat-shields, meant to protect their allies.
~Warlocks like to destroy from afar, though that's not to say they aren't lethal up close. Their abilities range quite a bit, from throwing void nukes or turning into Emperor Palpatine all the way to assuming the visage of a burning phoenix. Their armor focuses on increasing their abilities, and all of the relevant stats for them.
~Hunters kind of do their own stuff, and that's not a bad thing since it allows them to be anywhere and everywhere. Be it sniping from long range, joining a firefight at mid range, or attacking enemies in melee range, they're pretty much good at it all. They have low health like Warlocks, which keeps them on the move.

-Basics : Your characters stats are determined by the armor you wear, which will in turn update your "light level"; the higher this is, the more damage you can both take and dish out. You'll often want equipment that focuses on improving your favorite stats, and planets in the solar system each have their own sets you can work towards. From there you'll go down to planets either alone or in a group of up to 3 called a "Fireteam". There you can do patrols, adventures, and bounties, which are good ways to get new equipment early on.
-Strikes are essentially dungeon runs with a boss at the end, and will (usually) match you up with 2 other players to complete them. These Strikes have different levels of difficulty, and can be a good way to nab some high tier equipment earlier on in the game than you're supposed to--(Be brave, have fun!)--which will in turn raise your light level even higher.
-Raids are an even higher form of dungeon, grouping up to 6 players together to complete it. These always have modifiers to keep it interesting, meaning that you'll need to plan ahead for the dangers you'll face within.
-The Crucible and Gambit are pvp modes, pitting teams against each other to complete objectives. You can earn reputation and even exclusive armor and weapons by doing this, so even if its normally not your cup of tea, its worth it to at least try it out.
-Story missions can be picked up at the ship hangar of the tower, and I recommend that you do these first; they provide a lot of background information and will also provide a semi-linear route you can follow while you figure out the game. I personally wish that it automatically started you off in the base story--(There are technically 5 stories as of this reviews creation, so keep that in mind)--since the missions that they give you to introduce all of the game's functions tend to quickly overwhelm people; in the previous game, you were weaned into it in a much more manageable process, but I can understand that returning players who already know all of the functions don't necessarily want to sit through it all again, so oh well. Aside from being a bit cheesy, the missions will get you involved in the world, and will make all of the NPC's passing comments make a lot more sense.

To end this, I'll just mention that I really love the game so far, its been a blast to play and I really love the improvements they made from the previous game. If you were a fan of the original destiny, give this one a shot. If you were ever interested in the original but didn't think you'd like it, then just try it out, you never know if its something you'll love.\t\t\t\t\t
