\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPros: From a gameplay perspective, Destiny 2 runs very well with excellent sound and graphical design, especially when dealing with weapons. Lighting, graphics, and performance exceed exceeded all of my expectations, even at the Battle.net launch. With recent patches, and the latest expansion, Shadowkeep, I have not experience any drop in quality. Furthermore, in my opinion, the guns (and other weapons) are some of the most unique and satisfying things I have experienced in any other AAA game.

Cons: Some complaints I have heard deal with reusing weapon designs, primarily for year one and/or year two weapons, grinding a weaker story than Destiny 1, and increased monetization Addressing these concerns, for me the weapons are one of the best parts of the game, with minimal overlap in general. I have heard complains about Bungie reusing D1 weapons and armor (esp. exotics) in D2. For the most part, I feel that they have been reintroduced well, and thus I would consider this to be a minor complain. For dealing with grinding, it is important to remember that Destiny is not just a FPS, it is also a looter shooter that will require some investment of time to acquire weapons and armor of a high quality. Lastly, I would say I have enjoyed Destiny 2's story, though I will say that if you are interested in lore or story, it will be worthwhile to check out lore books or various Destiny content creators. Lastly, with regards to monetization, Destiny is at the end of the day, a free game, with optional ornaments and expansions, and with a few exceptions (ghosts and new weapons), monetization offers no benefit other than customization.

Rating: 10/10
Overall, I think Destiny 2 is an amazing game with minor issues. Though it is a rather large download, given that it is now free, I would recommend Destiny 2 to almost anyone.

P.S. Moon's haunted.
