well its a simple review, the good, the bad, and the relative.

The Good!
  • An extremely EASY game for anyone (New to gaming on not) and have a fun enough time with collecting cool looking
    armor and weapons and unloading loads into bullet sponges for hours!
  • Has a wide range of play styles and cool abilities
  • Huge open worlds to explore, find fun little secrets everywhere, able to waste hours on the game just doing nothing
  • A decent campaign with alright and totally NOT forgettable characters with witty dialogue

The Bad!
  • PvP
  • The STRIKES you play in order to achieve the best loots are so repetitive, its the same stuff you'll be playing for HOURS for the better weapons and armor
  • A lot of the game is META based, so trying to get the best is annoying especially if you sign your soul away and play PvP
  • The game will almost always feel the same, only thing you can really change is HOW you kill the enemies

    The Relative!
    • For the PEAK experience of the game, you will need to fine tune EVERY piece of armor for best performance depending on how you make your class
    • It forces you to play the campaign in order to do certain things in games
    • in order to get the strongest weapons you need to grind extremely hard and long quest/activities and HOPE to god it drops, otherwise you need to wait another week to get another chance for it to drop, then there is also the perks and you PRAY to god you get the groll (god roll)

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t1K HOURS LATER (on pc, several more 1000 hours on Playstation)

Honestly it's just a great game. Gets bad reputation because of its conflicting audience.

Sure it's FPS. But at its heart its an MMO RPG. Hours of grinding and multiplayer activities.

Perfecting your loadouts and strategies. Grandmasters and Raids requiring high levels.

New seasonal story every 3 months. Story progresses every week for the first month or so of each season. Leading up to the yearly expansion that brings huge updates to the game and systems.

But it's not perfect.

Leveling system needs some help. Although Seasonal objectives pretty much negate the old bounty xp grinding, its still an xp grind. Meaning easily exploited, while being a slog for a normal player. Feels like an arbitrary number. Sink "x" amount of time into the game and gain access to this activity.

Then, the problem with rewards from these Endgame sources. Power Creep is a real thing that plagues every live service game. Not to mention the inclusion and dedication Bungie has to pvp. Meaning these endgame rewards are often only slightly more powerful than a regular weapon you can get by doing anything. "Adept" weapons game a +2 stat increase to your stats. But the stats go from 0-100, so this stat boost is next to meaningless. The mods on these adept weapons provide some benefit, but ultimately are only useful in pvp. Nothing can be too strong or its causes power creep and pvp balance issues.

Finally, the HOT TOPIC among those who don't play the game.

The dreaded CONTENT VAULTING.

Yes, Bungie has "Vaulted"(their term for it) certain pieces of content. Usually the content is around 3 years old and they remove the stuff that provides no meaningful rewards. Content like the base game story missions, which were irrelevant to the current story and ultimately wasted space.

Don't forget, the game is 5+ years old. Although the graphics hold up tremendously, the engine itself is outdated. Semi-regular Engine Upgrades have staved off most issues, but it looms in the distance. Reminding us that we can't go forever. At some point Destiny 2 will see the end of its lifespan and have to start over.

Basically, this game has some outdated systems and it only grows older. Engine limitations requires removal of older content. Bungie makes no money from Vaulting content. They do it for the health of the game. One thing Bungie excels at is communicating with the community. They have emphasized many times that the game cannot continue without this content vaulting.

Last time they created a sequel, it did not go well. Destiny 2's start was rocky and no MMO player wants to completely restart while losing all their hard-earned gear. A necessary evil if you will.

That said, it still sucks. At this point in time, everything up until the Beyond Light expansion is in danger of getting vaulted. Right now, that pretty much only means Shadowkeep, easily the most underwhelming expansion.

But the future may be hopeful. The term, "Vault" means that several things may return. Several of the raids have been vaulted (already confirmed to never happen again) but I have hope that this term means that they may return at some point.

Basically, if the content vaulting bothers you, its certain that the this recent expansion and the next two are staying. At least until a new game.

If your a returning player outraged that content you bought was vaulted, then stay mad. No seriously, its clear that you value that 3-4 year old content. If your seriously that angry about it, then you should probably not continue until another sequel.

But if you are returning, I assure you that the game has significantly improved. The vaulting allows for faster patch times and quicker bug fixes. Even balance patches come out much faster. The story is so much better and in-game lore isn't needed to understand everything. But the lore hints at things to come and tells smaller stories, making the world feel alive. It tackles much more than just "fight big space alien", but actual moral choices. Uldren is now a guardian and, having lost his memory, is confronting his past life.

Weapons are cool and raids are better than ever. Deep Stone Crypt and Vow of The Disciple are easily the best raids they've created in a long time. Longer than the old raid lairs and plenty of cool loot.

Microtransactions aren't really a problem. Play enough and you can eventually earn pretty much everything. Weekly store refreshes introduce new stuff in the Bright Dust (in-game free currency) store. Bright dust is also plentiful, as you obtain large amounts from Seasonal Challenges. New Challenges every week except for the last few week, giving players a chance to complete them all.

Basically, this is the best Destiny 2 has ever been for new players. Give it a shot. It has it's problems like any other game. But Bungie genuinely cares for the game and doesn't abandon the game like many "live" games that have been attempted by other high profile companies.

If you do give it a shot, I'd recommend starting for free and do the first two new campaign missions released with the Witch Queen. If you enjoy the New Light mission set and the campaign, then the next step is trying out some other activities like PVP (plays very differently from other FPS games, mobility is key). If you really enjoy all these things, buy The Witch Queen DLC. Then maybe later the Season pass for this year. At some point I'd buy the Beyond Light DLC, but only if your certain you enjoy the game loop.

I hate Destiny 2. It's my favorite game...\t\t\t\t\t
