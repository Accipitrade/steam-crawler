\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played Destiny 1 at launch until Destiny 2 came out. Had a ton of fun playing with friends, exploring the beautiful and interesting worlds, and growing my characters. It wasn't the best game I'd ever played by any means, but it was cool and my character was very customizable. When Destiny 2 came out my friends and I played it for about the first month before we lost interest. I came back several times, mostly for each DLC. They were fun I guess, but it's been so long since I played them that I couldn't tell you anything about their plots or missions, and that says something about the game. I found each time I'd come back that I'd get frustrated with something and quit. I just started playing this again and probably have just as many hours on console, if that matters. I haven't bought Beyond Light and don't plan on it unless the price lowers to around $10, as the amount of content they ripped out of the game is astronomical. It's supposed to be similar to an MMO and ever expanding, yet they removed almost half the game. Not cool, especially since that was content I paid for. This lowers the value of the game significantly. I can understand the seasons system, but it doesn't make me happy.

Balance is an issue, especially in PvP. Have fun getting killed by the same three types of weapons and the frost abilities, especially Winter's Wrath. Everything else just isn't viable there. The top scoring members of each team are almost guaranteed to be using these weapons and abilities. Additionally, no one ever goes for objectives in Control, arguably the most played mode, and instead just kill each other. I've lost countless games because no one pushes objectives. PvE and Gambit are much more forgiving and thus much more fun, but there are still issues. Speedrunners gun through every strike and cause you to miss half the level since they rush to the boss and make you teleport, which obviously ruins the fun. They're only really in it for loot and to finish bounties. If you join a level in progress it always spawns you a million miles away and even blocks your path depending on the level. Strike missions ending sometimes will send you to what appears to be a new strike mission, but it's actually just patrol. With Gambit, a PvE and PvP mix-up mode, the only issue I have is its PvP aspect, as usually you'll get screwed over by anyone running PvP meta abilities and gear once the main boss spawns and they effortlessly kill you to heal it.

The game itself plays and looks amazing. The worlds are stunning and full of stuff to discover, but there isn't any incentive to. In D1 you had reason to do patrols and thus explore, but now they just start you off with gear near the highest level and you don't even have to play the story beyond a few small intro missions. I couldn't tell you anything really about the plot- 99% of it is written logs you have to navigate the menus for, while most of the actual game's story isn't even accessible unless you go to this tiny hidden vault at the Tower. I vaguely remember the Cabal destroying the Tower and killing the Speaker from D1, but couldn't tell you much else. There was something about every guardian losing their light? I don't know anymore, it didn't draw me in or feel important and there was no sense of urgency/danger to me. The music wasn't memorable either except the main menu theme since you have to listen to it every time you boot up the game. It sucks, because Bungie did story and especially music right in Halo, but it seems they've lost their way. I think it's a case of having a big vision without the means to see that vision through.

Overall Destiny 2 is the most frustrating kind of game. It has every opportunity to prove itself as good, but it falls short due to a huge variety of small reasons that build up. A lot needs to change. Right now I'm in this cycle of booting up, getting bounties, completing bounties, and then quitting as there isn't any real endgame that you can do without friends.

Personal List of Issues I'd Want Fixed:

-Make Hard Light(rifle) stay on the energy setting you set it to until you change it, not resetting things every time you die
-Nerf frost abilities to the ground and remove that stupid ice wall that players can throw down. It's always getting in the way during PvE
-Make more weapons viably competitive- Automatic weapon time to kill needs to go way down and grenades need to be more powerful
-Incentivize playing the story so that players don't miss content
-Make Warlock jumps actually jumps and bring Blink back to the Hunter
-Get rid of subclasses and let players pick and choose abilities like D1
-Bring back the old content. Also, these planets are supposed to be missing... Why are there still maps in PvP for them?
-Better enemy/boss variety, not just re-skins
-Maybe add side objectives to missions to help cut down on speedrunning
-Add matchmaking for raids. Unacceptable that there isn't
-More heavy weapon drops in PvP

That's all I can immediately think of. I really wish I could recommend this game. It is fun and I do enjoy it most of the time. There needs to be a neutral review option.\t\t\t\t\t
