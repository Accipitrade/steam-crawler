Positives are that they game actually has a story now and a lot of the deep lore is available in game, plenty of replayable missions and public events / strikes / gambit maps to allow you to "play together alone".

Negatives,
The quests for weapons (at least the really good ones) are ring fenced away from the sole player unless you either find a group to do the required missions or (most likely) pay for someone to do the group tasks for you.

The biggest negative will always be the end game reliance on the raid, very few will ever do it and even fewer will repeatedly ever go back but this end game content takes up a lot of resources which could have been deployed on more strikes / story missions / maps.

Overall a great game if you dont mind the grind, hope Destiny 3 will finally be able to give us the actual Destiny universe vision that Bungie want us to experience (even if the raid and Bungie's weird obsession with forcing the player base to experience certain parts of the game in a co-operative manner which is the biggest obstruction to many players experiencing all the game has to offer)\t\t\t\t\t
