\r\n\t\t\t\t\t---TLDR is at bottom of page---

Absolute waste of time. Ive easily put 1,500+ hours on this game between xbox, blizzard, and steam and I can definitively say that it is going downhill fast. The game is advertised as a "grinding" game where you spend hours trying to get the best weapons and gear while leveling up to the maximum light level, but quickly turns into a monotonous chore fest where you log on, do all the weekly stuff, gain maybe 10-20 light per character each week, then wait till next reset because you exhausted all of your weekly drops.

The most glaring issue with this game is the lack of competency from Bungie. There are glitches and bugs that have existed anywhere from a whole season to over a year. An example of this is the Izanagi's Burden sniper rifle, which the dlc for this gun came out a whole year ago (Dec 4, 2018), and the quest to get the gun was broken for many people. Bungie saw this issue, fixed it, and in the process of fixing it, broke it again. Issues like this plague the game left and right with Bungie hardly even batting an eye at them.

Another major (but more subjective) issue is the direction the game is headed towards. I don't mind the game being free to play and actually enjoy it being FTP since I can play with friends who previously did not own the game. However because of this, Bungie is strapped for cash and is entirely reliant on season pass sales and Eververse (paid cosmetics store). This is causing them to change the dlc releases from massive expansions with many new guns, exotics, armor sets, raids, strikes, quest lines, etc. to dlc releases with minimal content that is locked behind time gates throughout the season. This new way that Bungie is doing their content turns each new dlc drop into: 1. do new seasonal event, 2. do raid (if there is one), 3. hit max light, 4. wait till new content is released. Rinse and repeat season after season, year after year. Its sad to see since after seeing and experiencing Destiny 1's massive dlc drops like Taken King and Rise of Iron, and even Destiny 2's Forsaken dlc (which was a bit lack luster, but was heading in a good direction) you can easily see how great this game franchise can be, but Bungie is taking the game in a direction that is making millions of players turn off the game and never return.

---TLDR---

Honestly wish I could say great things about this game since ive been a die-hard player since Destiny 1's initial release, but in its current state there are few positive things I can say other than nice graphics and gun-play mechanics. If your looking for a looter shooter with hours of content just play Borderlands 2 or 3, and if your looking for an immersive MMO with leveling, grinding, and a deep story try Final Fantasy 14. Id rather pay for a good game than stay in this soon to be train wreck.\t\t\t\t\t
