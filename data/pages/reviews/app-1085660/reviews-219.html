\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 Is a genuinely fun game, with a lot to do and plenty of content throughout the year. But despite my review being positive, let me point out some very big negatives that may impact how you approach or like this game if one ever plays it.

The Biggest issue for any new player is the disconnect from the games story, and the large amount of stuff there is with little explanation or direction. For that I'd genuinely recommend waiting until next year as Bungie is Adding a lot of stuff that will greatly help the new player experience. Being a guideline that will help point on what to do next and an In game LFG for end game content.

The next issue is that the Price to play for a year is around 100$. What I mean is the Yearly DLC plus any seasons will add up to around 100$. Despite the game being Free to Play, there is a lot that is locked behind DLC. Personally I definitely get my moneys worth from the time I play but this may differ person to person.

Those are two problems that is the biggest for new players. If you still want to play and enjoy the game today (At the time of this review anyways) The community is very welcoming and there are plenty of guides online that will help. I highly recommend looking up Datto, Aztecross or even Houndish as a few youtubers that have videos that can help you get into the world of destiny.

Destiny is a special game to me and I am without a doubt a massive fanboy of the series. So I am completely bias on my outlook on the game.\t\t\t\t\t

The roasted cashew you call a "brain" will not align to the understanding of the developers or of the god of Bungie, Luke Smith , hallowed be thy name. If you play this game and think you're not a blueberry, it's too late for you. Perhaps you'll even need a third part time job to continue to feed that crack cocaine addiction, as that does help in the game's case.

Or go to reddit and complain about the game, whichever is more fun.

Summary
Destiny 2 is the best looter shooter out there. With engaging endgame content for solo and group players alike backed up by superb gun play you'd expect from the OG developer of Halo. It's main issues stem from design choices based around the free to play monetization model. But[/but] the reality of the game is, it is not very Free to play friendly, as a lot of content is gated behind yearly paid expansion packs. Ultimately, this game won't be for everyone and will really depend on if you can look pack the monetization model to enjoy the solid game that lies underneath.

Gameplay
The game play is superb, Bungie has been a master at FPS games and you can only wonder what Halo would be like in 2022 if they still had the reigns. The abilities and class builds are varied, and the guns are designed well, with them each managing to feel unique. Which is a challenge given the 100s of options available. Plus, they all feel very satisfying to shoot.

For PvE Raids, and Dungeons are great group content to enjoy, with scaling difficulty options. You'll need to use the app to find a group though (in game group find arrives next year!). For some reason only certain modes have matchmaking. But even for a solo player there is plenty to do, whether by yourself or matchmaking in silent with people for one of the other, less intense pieces of group content.

PvP is there too, however I find it secondary to the rest of the game. IMO I would prefer a mode that normalized character stats and weapons. There is too much variety for my liking for it to feel like an enjoyable PvP FPS experience. It's a pain to know what people are using, and some weapon/perk combo's are clearly superior to others. Where in PvE this matters much less, it can be very noticeable between the have and have-not's in PvP.

Monetization
For me, all of the issues Destiny 2 has can be traced to design decisions that are meant to keep your playing, and logging in, to get your more invested and more likely to finally give in a swipe the CC to the cash shop. Now this is not a new design model. It's common with many Free to play games, and Destiny techinically is F2P.

In reality, Destiny 2 sits in the camp of more a permanent Free Trial experience, with a lot of content being gated behind yearly paid expansions ($50). Then, each year there are 4 addition seasons of content that add new content for everyone, but to get the most out of them requires the purchase of a battle season pass, at $10. While the seasons add new lore content, its usually played out through audio logs and some cutscenes. The game play aspect of each season boils down to very similar mechanics, and they all require regular logins to really keep on it. And after each season certain gameplay items reset, putting you to the beginning of the treadmill to do again for the next season.

Where anyone familiar with any type of MMOish game that has vertical progression is familiar with the gear and stat treadmill that expansions have, destiny makes use of this too frequently. ~every year a new paid content drop occurs, resetting a treadmill, and then there are ~ 4 more mini resets during the year with the Seasons. so 5 times a year, something happens that requires you to re-farm to the top. This is all designed this way to keep people in the game, and more likely to spend money to keep relevant.

Final point on monetization, they need to address. As the game ages, and more content becomes gated behind expansions they really need to address the cost of the game to unlock it all. Bundling of some kind for all previous expansions besides the most recent 1 needs to happen or monetization will become even more egregious.

Conclusion

There is a good game here, all of the actual gameplay is fun. Im kind of desensitized to the pervasive "swiping" mechanics that exist in modern gaming, so I'm able to look past all of the predatory models to enjoy the game there is. But I know not everyone will. Luckily you can try it for free, see how it's set up and make the decision for yourself.\t\t\t\t\t
