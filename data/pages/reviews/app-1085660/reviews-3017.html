\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played both this game and the prequel somewhat extensively. Like the prequel, this game has plenty of activities to do, but gives most of the base content for free (PVP, PVE, all of the raids that came before forsaken, and more). for a free to play player, you don't have endless things to do, but its enough to keep you occupied for a while. However, if you want to do all of the endgame content to reach max power, you will need to cough up at least $40 CAD for the newest expansion, and another $40 CAD for the last major expansion to get the full experience. Despite this, the devs are working hard to make the game better for everyone with constant hotfixes, tweaks, and more. Also, what make s this game great for the casual player like me who will not necessarily have the time to do everything, is all of the things you can accomplish over time through normal play. On top of that, for the players that have more time than others, there are plenty of activities and quests to keep you and your friends occupied for hours on end. However, I have a few gripes with this game. firstly, PVP is oriented in two directions: skill, and meta. The skilled players actually end up often using meta weapons to boost themselves even further, but some still like to play with hard to use weapons. The meta also doesn't shift very often; it only changes somewhat when the new seasons come out. Secondly, there also seem to be game breaking glitches almost all the time. Just about a week ago, using the exotic fusion rifle, telesto, the sticky fusion beams (I don't know what to call them, If you are confused google the gun) dealt damage to you when shooting them at the floor or wall. If you shot five or six bursts at the floor and stood on top of them, the resulting player damage would boost your super to 100% from 0. But otherwise, this is a great game to have in your library for free, and also a great game to have full access to.\t\t\t\t\t
