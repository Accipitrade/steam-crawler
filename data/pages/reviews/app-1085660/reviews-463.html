\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have 250+ hours on the steam version of this game. Overall, I have about 1200+ hours between console and PC, and that isn't even considered hardcore. That being said, here's my take.

The game is really best put as an addiction, really. The game's lore is something that I've enjoyed for a long, long time. Its the main reason why I even still play, honestly. If you love lore-rich games, Destiny 2 is a great example. However, I cant recommend this game for people who aren't gonna open up their wallet, and it's completely understandable if you don't want to spend money.

FAIR WARNING: if you aren't willing to sacrifice money to play this game, its an extremely lackluster experience, that being almost everything is behind a pay gate. That turns a lot of people away from the game, and that's understandable. The pricing for new content is genuinely absurd and needs to be looked at. Pro tip: if you do want to spend money, wait a few months. The price for the expansion will go down gradually, and the only thing you'd be missing out on is the day 1 experience.

The overall feeling of the game is also important. The game can feel really fun, and it has a lot of things going for it. However, the main issue that I have with this game, along with a few others, is that it feels like more of a chore than fun after a few weeks, mainly because of weekly recycling of activities. I know this is meant to keep the player base interested in the game for a longer time, but the game feels dry after about a month or two into the new expansion or season.

Speaking of activities, the endgame content and such is really the main reason why people are invested in the game in the first place. Bungie's take on the raid system that's prominent in a ton of MMOs is the pinnacle of their mechanics and gunplay. Playing raids is easily the most fun I've had on the game. Playing with strangers just trying to get the best loot that the game offers is a fun experience most of the time.

As for the community, I find it that PVE players are some of the nicest people that you can find, and I've met a lot of them. I really only remember the times people have been nice to me in the game, rather than people being rude. That being said, you'll always have a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ or two when you're playing the game no matter what. Raid teams tend to be really nice and chill, and all they tend to ask from you is that you're also chill and have some common sense.

The lore of the game is something that I find the most interesting aspect. The lore is so deep and rich that its so interesting to see what every expansion or season has to offer. It's one of their main selling points for a lot of people, and it's really something that i encourage people to look into if they love story games.

In conclusion, I cant recommend this game to people who dont want to spend money. That being said, if you like MMOs and you like grinding endlessly as well as wanting to spend money on the game (because you're gonna have to), Destiny 2 isn't a terrible game. Again, I'm not the most hardcore player there is. I've been playing the game for over 4 years, and I've shared a ton of memories with complete strangers who also have shared some.
