\r\n\t\t\t\t\tConditional Thumbs down.

Having reached end game content in D2, i can offer a complete overview of the game.

D2 is a looter shooter with a ton of content which is heaped into a huge disorganized mess.

Example: interact with an NPC or random object and you might get a quest that promises you a mysterious bow or strange shotgun You do the grind in the quest and the quest tells you to go see another NPC. You do that and get another quest with another grind, etc, etc, etc. If you research the quest ( which is often necessary), you'll find that you need to do 12 quest steps and hours of grinding, sometimes in game modes you don't enjoy, to get the reward. At this point, i usually say 'screw it' and drop the whole hot mess.

sometimes after doing an activity, you might get a item dumped in your quest list that simply says 'a strange object that smells of mint' and you have no idea what it does or what it's for. Again, you have to research that object to see what the heck is going on. the game literally makes no attempt to tell you what's going on.

It's as if the game dev have produced tons of content over the years and have simply dumped that content into a game with no organization, rhyme or reason. The exception is the store where you buy cosmetics for real money, which is well organized and updated regularly.

If you don't mind spending time on researching quests, weapons, mods, gear, then you'll be in heaven. new players are often completely lost.

also, blatantly stupid game design decisions are very common. In one of the DLC quests, the quest tells you to kill a mini boss... ok, no worries. But the mini boss is immune to damage and the flipping game doesn't give you a clue how to damage the immune boss. So after 20 minutes of looking around, i ended up dying and researching on google how to damage the mini boss while i was dead. Very annoying.

Perhaps 1/3 of the PvE activities in D2 are more annoying than fun, mostly because of blatantly stupid design, which i've never run into on other popular FPS games.

i enjoy probably 2/3 of the PvE and none of the PvP in D2. PvP is known by players to be in bad shape and seems to be generally ignored by devs, except to bring back old PvP game modes now and then.

Most of the long multiple quests to get the best weapons in the game are too long and annoying for me to bother with.

Additionally, Devs regularly nerf weapons that players spend 10s to 100s of hours grinding. They blatantly nerf popular weapons because they're popular. stupid.

So if you don't mind sorting through a huge pile of PvE maps, caves, missions, game modes, gear, mods, quests, secrets and hidden locations, and if your google research skills are sharply honed, you may enjoy D2. There are some fun PvE activities

Also, if you enjoy PvP, i'd suggest you look elsewhere.\t\t\t\t\t
