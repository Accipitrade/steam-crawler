\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been a long time player of Destiny. From the beta of Destiny 1 on XBox 360, right through to playing Shadowkeep (the latest DLC for Destiny 2) on XBox One & Steam today.

There have only been a few games in my 30 years as a gamer which so totally involved me that I would recommend them to any gamer as "essential"; Wizard of Wor, Gauntlet, Champions of Krynn, Ultima Underworld, UFO: Enemy Unknown, Ultima 8, Tie Fighter, System Shock 2, Deus Ex, Guild Wars 1, STALKER, Elder Scrolls, BioShock, Path of Exile and now Destiny (1 and now, of course, 2).

Destiny 2 has perfected the combination of Shooter (FPS) mechanics and Looter gameplay, in addition to now sporting a HUGE array of "Triumphs" for players to pursue. It really is frighteningly slick and engaging. :-D

From well-paced single player storyline, to super-tight competitive 3v3 PvP to huge, collaborative, 6-player raids, it really does have something for everyone. They ahve even managed to mash these styles of play together to give us Gambit (PvE + PvP) and a whole range of "mini-raids" for 3 players to play collaboratively...

I cannot recommend it highly enough.

I will however highlight it's few failings;
1. It requires you to complete the single player story line with each character class, which is both unnecessary and very frustrating.
2. It offers far more complex loot than you can easily store. Storage management is an unintended mini-game (& annoying time sink).
3. It has times when Bungie are transitioning between old and new mechanics which can be disruptive and a little chaotic.

Anyway, considering the scale of the game and how amazingly well it plays across all it's different modes I am absolutely unequivocal in recommending you at least try it's free version (New Light) today.

Should you bother?

Destiny 2 the F2P, FPS and MMO-lite manages to both sustain itself as a shooter and an MMO. By utilizing the best elements of its genres Bungie has managed to produce an engaging solo experience for your average FPS enthusiast. Furthermore, the back-end gearing system as seen in traditional MMORPGs alongside the fast-paced FPS HUD provides a uniqueness to the D2 IP that triumphs over competitors.

However, the peer-to-peer connection makes fast pace and otherwise well engineered gameplay mechanics easily exploitable; bosses can be cheesed and manipulated with ease and PVP can be further exploited by simple client-side hacks. The lack of a dedicated server is therefore a real set back to this otherwise enjoyable game.

On the bright side, I would argue that the business model for Destiny 2 is effective and fair. And I would advise other developers to take after Bungie, and take inspiration from their live service model. But with every business model there are of course some flaws.

The last few seasons have been riddled with retextures, and have ultimately offered very little in terms of new content to the game. I would further argue that this content deficit is due to the sheer time required to create maps and environments as innovative and expansive as seen in previous Destiny 2 updates. This problem being a very common issue with ambitious MMO games, so this was to be expected- it can be hard to sustain MMOs that demand higher quality content on such a frequent basis. And unfortunately for Destiny 2, with its constant retextures and return to old content, this demand issue is starting to show.

Overall, I would suggest giving this game a try and judging it for yourself; again It’s not everyone's cup of tea. But the game has some interesting features to offer. I personally think it’s a decent game just not worth too much investment.
