\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLook, this game is good enough. It's fun for a while, and I'll continue to play it due to the sunken cost fallacy. The issue?

It's so incredibly, ridiculously, boring. I feel as if I'm stepping closer to ego death with every time I grind for a single point of a gear score, made to artificially elongate gameplay (while also having staggered releases: If you purchased the newest DLC, you don't even have half of the activities it promises when it launches).

I'm sure some of you are thinking "Oh the gear score is artificial elongation huh? Then so are all looter shooters.". Which looter shooters make you get the same item, over and over, with it being FUNCTIONALLY IDENTICAL except for a number that makes it higher level. Each weapon can roll from a certain selection of mods, leading to ~10 different iterations. They don't have any major differences or stat rolls, so you will often be forced to use objectively worse weapons simply because they have a higher level.

Does the level actually do anything? Yes, enemies in ranges above and below you have damage modifiers at certain thresholds. However, what this actually means is the "end game activities" are locked behind grinding for these higher level weapons and armor, even if they are worse than your current gear.

And as people reading this may know, you can only even get the chance to obtain these pinnacle weapons on a weekly basis, or by making multiple characters and grinding the same stuff over and over. Let me give a quick example of how this works based on the current power limits:

You start at 1060, and the soft cap is 1200. Gear is spread over 8 pieces of gear, and your level is the average of them. Each time you get a new piece of gear, it'll either be from slightly lower or a couple of levels higher than what you currently have equipped (so in this example something like 1058-1062). So, to go up one level, you need to gain at least 8 levels over your entire loadout, increasing the minimum and maximum level range for dropped items by 1, rinse and repeat.

This is the easy part though, since these drops can be obtained practically anywhere and without much effort. The issue is what follows.

From 1200 you will no longer get increased levels from random drops, you now need to use "Powerful" and "Prime" engrams to increase to the new soft cap of 1250. "Powerful" gear can only be obtained by certain weekly events, meaning you cannot grind for them in one sitting. "Prime" gear, however, is now a rare random drop: you can still obtain these from normal activities, albeit rarely. For example, over the last 5 days of practically nonstop playing, I've only got 3.

Finally, you have the hard cap: from 1250 to 1260, you require "Pinnacle" drops. You can only obtain 5 of these at most a week.

This is the levelling system. The entire time you'll be using the same items, juggling them for which has the highest level regardless of benefit. In case you're curious: This is NOT fun. It's fun until maybe 1210, but then it gets increasingly tiresome.

And this isn't even mentioning the very poorly implemented sunsetting...

Enjoy an activity? They've been removed from the game. For example, there were originally 6 raids. Now there are 2.

Like a certain weapon? It's been rendered useless. Since weapons now have an absolute level cap factoring in future raising of the cap, the vast majority of weapons are now totally useless. Seriously, of the two raids left, only one of them can even drop gear strong enough to use.

Like a planet? As you probably know, half of the planets have been removed. Like unique activities, like menagarie? Removed for the foreseeable future.

From somebody who has played this game from launch on the blizzard launcher, it has been incredibly disheartening to see bungie fumble this game so constantly. I'll continue to play in hope that it gets better because I've already wasted so much time on it, but for other people?

Do NOT play this game unless you have absolutely nothing better to do. The best review I can give is that it genuinely makes me tired and sad.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYou know, considering this series' now 6 years of age... across 2 separate game launches, 2 entire season passes, the 9 total expansions, and the now 13 or so battlepass-esq "seasons", I have spent more time in this series total then I have probably spent consuming any other form of media, period. And so it brings me no joy whatsoever to admit that this game has officially lost just about every ounce of enjoyment I found in it all those years ago.

I have always been, and will always be the type of person who immerses themselves in the games they play. With the intent of getting lost in the universe and getting connected to my character and environments I'm given to explore. And frankly at this series' peak, which I consider to be the era of the first game's largest expansion, the Taken King, it was able to provide that kind of universe for me. But this game has gone so far off that path it laid out for itself back then, all in an attempt to sell itself as some faux-MMO, that I have officially lost all connection to both its universe, and its gameplay loop.

Back in the days of Destiny 1, particularly before the introduction of the Silver currency, if you saw someone wearing a set of armor with a matching weapon set, all with the stats to take down literal raid bosses, it had the potential to blow the minds of all the players around. You and the players around would stare in wonder at how well a player may have put together their appearance and more then ANYTHING ELSE get an insane sense of determination to want to go out into the universe and find that set for yourself. Figure out what kind of identity you could create for your own Guardian.

However as an example of how far the series has strayed from that vision;
Beyond Light launched a few days ago. An expansion ($40 mind you) and general change in direction that I as a previously disappointed Destiny fan, was hoping could re-spark my love for the series. I launched up the game the day of the expansion only to after about 2 hours in, see a Warlock wearing hands down my favorite looking Warlock armor set to ever be added to this series. Simply to say seeing that player utterly BLEW my mind. It was a theme for an outfit I had wanted the Warlock to have for a long long time. And it was finally added.....
And then... the utter disappointment I was overwhelmed with when I looked into the armor set... only to find it can't be unlocked through normal gameplay under ANY MEANS, and is instead locked behind a SEPARATE paywall. The battlepass-but-not-a-battlepass, poorly masqueraded $10 subscription service: "Seasonal Pass".
And the cherry on top? After this "Season" is over, that armor set is potentially never acquirable again.

I'm done. I've spent well over $200+ on this series across the years. I'm absolutely done being asked to spend more and more money to facilitate my enjoyment for the game. ♥♥♥♥ FOMO. ♥♥♥♥ the audacity it takes to ask the player to continue to spend that kind of money. ♥♥♥♥ the sheer AMOUNT of avenues this game has JUST to generate revenue.\t\t\t\t\t
