\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI got the game free with the purchase of the GTX 1080ti I got back in 2019. The game I got back then is gone, only a shell remains. I purchased the first season pass that later became free with the base game, and then cut. I even bought Shadowkeep before I learned my lesson, hoping I would get back into it. Regret.

There are good things about the game, the visuals are amazing and really appealing in a sci-fi fantasy semi-realistic manner. Some of the best gunplay I have ever seen in a game, almost every gun looks and feels powerful and weighted, as opposed to the tinny clattery popcap guns you can see in other games.
Mobility with both your character and their speeder feels natural and intense.
Customization is amazing aside from the primitive locked color pallet method, but having an amazing array of armor pieces and cool looking guns can make up for it.

Shame that once you find something that looks cool, its obsolete and useless to you in a setting of difficulty.
They tried a method of being able to retain the appearance of old gear but it died (to me) in a blaze of hellfire thanks to greedy pay-to-progress microstransaction methods to bypass grind.

They deleted the game I payed for and left me with the slap in the face that is PvP which isnt even fun unless your down with the meta and/or some kind of hardcore MLG pro mega chad who pours milk into the bowl before the cereal with an obsession with the word 'bruh'.
This **** aint fortnight, give me an engaging story and a multitude of environments that are fun to be in that i dont have to worry about being Thanos'ed in a year's time.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're looking for a review on this game you've come to the wrong place, but at least I can give you strawberry cheese cake recipe.

ingredients:

250g digestive biscuits
100g butter, melted
1 vanilla pod
600g full fat soft cheese
100g icing sugar
284ml pot of double cream
For the topping
400g punnet of strawberries, halved
25g icing sugar

STEP 1
To make the base, butter and line a 23cm loose-bottomed tin with baking parchment. Put the digestive biscuits in a plastic food bag and crush to crumbs using a rolling pin. Transfer the crumbs to a bowl, then pour over the melted butter. Mix thoroughly until the crumbs are completely coated. Tip them into the prepared tin and press firmly down into the base to create an even layer. Chill in the fridge for 1 hr to set firmly.

STEP 2
Slice the vanilla pod in half lengthways, leaving the tip intact, so that the two halves are still joined. Holding onto the tip of the pod, scrape out the seeds using the back of a kitchen knife.

STEP 3
Pour the double cream into a bowl and whisk with an electric mixer until it’s just starting to thicken to soft peaks. Place the soft cheese, icing sugar and the vanilla seeds in a separate bowl, then beat for 2 mins with an electric mixer until smooth and starting to thicken, it will get thin and then start to thicken again. Tip in the double cream and fold it into the soft cheese mix. You’re looking for it to be thickened enough to hold its shape when you tip a spoon of it upside down. If it’s not thick enough, continue to whisk. Spoon onto the biscuit base, starting from the edges and working inwards, making sure that there are no air bubbles. Smooth the top of the cheesecake down with the back of a dessert spoon or spatula. Leave to set in the fridge overnight.

STEP 4
Bring the cheesecake to room temperature about 30 mins before serving. To remove it from the tin, place the base on top of a can, then gradually pull the sides of the tin down. Slip the cake onto a serving plate, removing the lining paper and base. Purée half the strawberries in a blender or food processor with the icing sugar and 1 tsp water, then sieve. Pile the remaining strawberries onto the cake, and pour the purée over the top.

voilà!
