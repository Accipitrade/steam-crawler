\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf I were to give this game a review with my hours played, it's a 4/10. The main issue with the game is that there's not a good model to sustain the year by year content. So unless you like grinding the same content over and over again in PVE, that's what happens seasonally. PVP by far is worse. For me, it feels like the best pvp out there with the movement options and multiple weapons and builds but the main issue and primary reason for the low score is that Bungie punishes the players who have invested the most time in the game with player versus player.

How do they do this? The longer you play, by Bungie's internal metric, they will match you with players far better stacked on the opposing team. They don't have a good metric for players teamed up or great individual players together, so if you want to play solo you will be inordinately punished by the matchmaking system. The next point you're punished on is the game modes. In the classic playlist, which is supposed to be a mix of 3 rotating game types, in actuality you will be playing half of one game type - control - while playing the other two game types -clash and supremacy - the other half. Why this is an issue is because there is already a control playlist that exclusively plays this gametype. And every few weeks, Iron banner is a special playlist which is also a variation of Control (which is controlling three points which makes kills multiplicative based on the number of points controlled). Thus, when you play control, most people will not play the objectives (control 2 points, and get power ammo). What this leads to is that because people don't play the objective, they'll quit which puts your team at a disadvantage. What is infuriating is that the Bungie PVP team refuses to store the participants of the match so if you're losing, they incentivise you to quit at no cost. You can't report people who quit making this a common practice. This turns into another form of PVP punishment where as a solo player, you will be thrown into a losing match where there is no chance of winning.

Another issue with PVP, is that the team will not adjust the sandbox with any frequency, allowing for overtuned weapons to reign free and allow low skill weapons, to be meta for months at a time. There's no transparency here, and so the playerbase has to figure out the mechanics of the game on their own. Also, PvP is P2P, meaning that even if you defeat someone with a weapon first, as long as they have a worse connection, they'll be credited with a kill of yourself if they do it in the general time period. So you'll be actively punished for having a good connection.

TLDR; I'm a masochist who plays D2 pvp because it feels the best even though Bungie punishes its most passionate players through opaque communications, lack of updates and new maps, and systems that punish you the more you play.

UPDATE as of FEB 2021: Bungie still doesn't care about PVP and there's still no transparency nor substantial content updates with the new releases. Yes PVE, PVP still neglected.\t\t\t\t\t
