EDIT FOR BEYOND LIGHT:

Im a pretty big Destiny fan. My review on the source game is purposely facetious so as to joke about the amount of hours I've put into it, but after the release of this DLC, I simply can't envision myself pouring the same amount of time into the game going forward if this is the standard to be set for future releases.

If you're still reading, you may consider it interesting to note that I'm a New Light player, and when I started there with no prior Destiny experience, I constantly praised the game for it's plethora of content. The epic storytelling of Halo was from a Bungie long since past, but at the very least they could provide a solid looter shooter with a respectable level of content to keep you engaged and make it a game capable of pouring 1000+ hours into. The tight gunplay and power fantasy elements are things I hadn't found in any other game since Halo, and this meant I had an online experience to look forward to every time I logged on.

This is no longer the case in Beyond Light. I would consider myself more of a PvP main than a PvE one, which might earn me a wary look from lots of Destiny faithfuls, but Ive done my share of high level PvE content. A few master night falls, all the campaigns, most seasonal activities since NL, I have Divinity, etc. Where is our compelling replacement to the Menagerie? I don't think we are going to get one, but even if we did, WHY would we play it?

The lack of content in this expansion is baffling. I never played Destiny 1, and yet I see countless players saying that they would be satisfied with reprised Blues from D1&D2 turning into legendaries with an extra perk. Many believe this would be an excellent way to aid the art team and design team while not requiring the same monetary effort of entirely new models. The deletion of what's seems to be 80%+ of the games content has left a void, with what is statistically the most content deficient sandbox the game has ever seen. RIGHT after a FORTY dollar expansion? This isn't only baffling, it's downright insulting.

Sunsetting was a business decision masqueraded as a gameplay decision, and the effects this has had on the game could be irreparable if this is the course they continue to follow. The tuning of mountaintop and its current lack of effectiveness in the crucible PROVES that if Bungie dedicated enough time to it, they could balance the previous pinnacle weapons to exist in current sandbox without completely removing essentially what equates to entire previous generations of the game.

Think about this: if someone buys forsaken, they won't be able to use ANY OF THE GUNS (besides the raid weapons the majority of players will never get) from that expansion in current high level activities. None of them. And yet, there is no explanation in the store that would warn a new player to purchase this DLC instead of the previous ones. Consider that fact before you consider supporting Bungie going forward.\t\t\t\t\t
