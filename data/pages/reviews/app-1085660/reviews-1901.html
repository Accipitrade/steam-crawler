\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo start: I wish I could be more than Yes/No black & white on my recommendation to play this game. A bit of my history and experience with this series to give my review a bit more context. I played Destiny 1 on launch. It was a disaster. A week before Destiny 2 launched a buddy talked me into playing through the whole campaign start to finish and all it's expansions to prepare for Destiny 2. I actually really enjoyed myself and felt they did a great job turning the game around. It was really easy to tell the new content from the old. When Destiny 2 launched I loved the campaign. Once I got to the end-game and the first raid though I didn't care for it and bounced off. Better start than Destiny 1, but it still had massive problems that needed to be remedied. After I jumped off I kept up as Bungie tried tons of things out. Different pricing models. Annoying yearly pass shenanigans. It all just turned me off.

Now expansions later that same buddy that talked me into playing Destiny 1 has talked me into playing Destiny 2 again (all current expansions up to Shadowkeep). I decided to start a new character and start from scratch. The moment I started I knew something was different. There was no campaign. Where was it? After a brief tutorial I was just dropped into the tower at light level 750 with next to no direction on where to go. This "New Light" system is confusing and frankly jarring. I had to look up how to even start the original campaign! Progressing on the original campaign felt disconnected and jarring as I had to look up where different quests started from. It didn't just push you forward through content in a clear way anymore. If I hadn't played this game before I imagine it would be even more so as someone brand new. Eventually I just jumped on an old character that I successfully ported from the PlayStation 4. Trying to do the original campaign via this new system was not intuitive, fun, or seamless. On my old character I was able to jump right into the Forsaken content (after purchasing that and Shadowkeep on sale as a bundle) and play through it in a presentation much like the original campaign was at launch.

The game is fantastic when it's good, but it's held back. It's held back by it's UI being a mess. By content being confusing to access at times or (in the near future) outright removed. I really want to like this game. It's lore is fascinating (even if told in sometimes awkward ways). The way guns and movement work is fun and can be very exciting. Major campaign story quests are pretty great even if they get a little repetitive. It's just the little things that keep me pulled out instead of being immersed.

Overall, the reason why I wish it was more than a black & white yes/no for recommendation is because it's not for everyone. I want to recommend the game based on some of its merits. The negatives, however, make me not want to recommend it. Give it a try, but just keep in mind if you are new: You are jumping into a game that has gone from having an accessible campaign that anyone could jump into to a game that is morphing into a games-as-a-service style constantly advancing narrative where you can't access old content anymore. In the future they plan to remove old content (they call it putting it into the "Vault") and recycling old strikes and planets whenever they want and it fits the current narrative they are trying to tell.\t\t\t\t\t
