\r\n\t\t\t\t\tthis game is completely ass for new players, especially those who do not want a lot of time commitment. If you're here for the grind though, this game will let you meet tons of people, play with your friends, have some laughs, relax, and grind for the gun of your dreams.

Here are my problems as someone who has played for almost 250 hours.

1. One tutorial.
At the beginning of the game. It tells you how to move and shoot and all of the stuff you'd figure out in like 10 seconds.... good luck after that.
Google is your best friend, not bungie. Bungie tells you ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ when it comes to how to play this game. It makes the whole thing very frustrating at first.
The quest system is almost moronic. It's confusing and unintuitive, especially coming from someone who plays mmorpgs. You can never keep track of what you need to do where. Even if you track a quest, it literally goes away after you finish the step and you have to track it again... why?
Just use destiny item manager (google), join an lfg server/use bungie lfg, and google how to do all of your quests.


2. Campaign.
Forget the campaign. The story sucks, seriously. You'll be more confused than anything else.
Also, you have to find the campaign. The one they give you isn't really the first campaign. You can play the campaigns, but they don't really do anything other than pass the time and give you some context for the world (there are some exceptions)

3. Early leveling system.
Pretty much 95% of the game isn't playable until power level 1000 (or (max power level for gear - 60), for those who read it after beyond light), which could take dozens of hours. All of the loot sucks and means almost nothing. Don't worry, it gets 10x better once you get exotics.

4. Matchmaking
PVP matchmaking is complete dog crap. You'll match against guys popping 30-40 kill games while you struggle to reach 10, every single game. Don't expect a low rank to save you, it does not. However, matchmaking times aren't the worst I've ever seen.

Basically, destiny for a new player without google is a confusing mess that people would drop a couple hours in.


Once you get past all of that, destiny really starts rolling.

1. Raids
Remember the LFG thing I told you about? People will literally teach you how to do the raid (which requires 6 players and coordination), completely free, over discord, and all you have to do is look for an opening... then join. That's it. It's not rare or limited either, you'll find someone. I've never found someone toxic, and everyone just seems really cool. I could be wrong of course, but my experience with other people has never been negative.

2. Guns
The loot system in this game is pretty well done. It's meant for grinding, although not as frustrating as it could be. Your weapons and armor can roll random perks/stats, meaning that you can continually improve what you're wearing all the time. At the same time, however, you won't find that bad rolls instantly mean useless, just less useful. Although your random rolls impact your game, it isn't so much that bad rolls automatically lead to a loss. It's more about your choice of weapon and armors that impact your game.
In terms of actually shooting them, lets be honest this is destiny. It's solid stuff.

3. Tons of things to do - for now
I'm almost 250 hours in, still haven't come close to being finished with things to do. There is less, sure, but I am not done. It's pretty hard to run out of things to do in destiny until the very end. Things refresh every day and week, but it doesn't feel restrictive like mobile games who impose time limits. Grind to your hearts content.
I will note here though, that there is less and less to do at the high power levels. For now though, this point will stand.


Overall, whether I recommend it depends on the type of player you are. If you want the grind, destiny is for you. The first dozen hours might be rough, but eventually things will settle and you'll get into the groove. You'll meet people, become powerful, and see everything that destiny has to offer.
If grind is not your style, skip this game entirely. It's a confusing, frustrating, yawn infested first 20-50 hours of gameplay.\t\t\t\t\t
