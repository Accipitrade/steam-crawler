\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's good, honestly.

Destiny 2 is a fantastic game that has been marred with questionable choices. Every time Bungie does something right, it seems they feel the need to do something wrong, though this isn't always the case.
The base game is fine, a decent main campaign and a good sandbox to play in.
The first expansion, Osiris, is extremely bare bones. It shipped with one of the smallest patrol zones in franchise history and every single story mission and adventure is a trip into the Infinite Forest, which if you've been through it once you've been through the same section a thousand times.
Warmind was definitely a sign of good things in the works. The base campaign for it is memorable, comes with a great patrol zone and a world activity that still sees dozens of players today.
Forsaken though, now that is a good expansion. Shipping with not one, but TWO new patrol areas, and a raid so good I'd put it up there with Vault of Glass from Destiny 1.
Shadowkeep is a return to a familiar area from D1 and harkens back to a lot of good nostalgia. The story missions are fun, albeit feel a bit short, but it makes up in other ways.

Now for what I said about the bad. After Forsaken, Bungie decided to launch the Annual Pass. It came with 3 seasons of content to get players through the drought until the next major update. The first expansion launched in a bad way. Players couldn't play the new game mode due to being low light levels, which the update didn't raise to a point that they could play, so it took most players a week of grinding to even get to the new stuff. the next two updates launched in much better states and, in my opinion, are far better. With the release of Shadowkeep, players were treated to a new season, which for most was an extreme let down. While I got a good deal of enjoyment from it, I agree it could've been a lot better.

While it's had some missteps, I HIGHLY reccomend this game, especially if you can get some friends together.\t\t\t\t\t
