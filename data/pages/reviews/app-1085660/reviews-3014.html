\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh how the mighty have fallen.


I love Destiny. I have played Destiny since the first game, played through the whole game in my younger years, all DLCs completed and I loved the experience. I eventually got a PC and in 2019 I bought D2 on Battle.net and got myself all the DLCs (up to forsaken). My god. What a wonderful time that was. The experience was arguably better then D1 in some aspects, and all the quests and DLC content was interesting. Even the raids were fun, which as a PVP player myself, really attests to how well the game played. I know the game was rough as crap around the time before Forsakens release, but it was absolutely amazing during Forsaken.

Then, in 2020, Shadowkeep came out. I bought it like a sucker for the full asking price of 60 bucks. I didnt really like it, I got consumed in school matters, had a really tough few years, and only in March 2023 did I come back to Destiny 2, expecting to complete whatever remaining content from the DLCs I had completed, finishing off Shadowkeep, and progressing to the new DLCs.

Except I never will be able to play all those dlcs or even the **base game content**.

They removed Red War, the flagship story campaign. They removed all the DLCs that people paid good money for. Then they mutilated the Forsaken DLC into being the pack it is now.

They made the game free to play, but its not free to play. Its free to start. The new player tutorial in 2023 is confusing beyond belief, unpolished, and annoying for anyone to start.

Bungie's inept decision making has essentially locked Destiny 2 into being a game that only people that have been playing the game since 2021 or before can actually play and understand what is going on. No way in hell someone who just is new to Destiny can understand what is going on. Thankfully, they will no longer content vault stuff. But the fact that it even happened is egregious. I have never heard of a game developer making a decision to lock out the new players playerbase, considering thats where most of the money will come from. Maybe thats why they needed to charge players 60 dollars for every unpolished half-assed DLC they have released since Forsaken (cough cough Lightfall).

I understand their alleged reasons for why the content was vaulted (low player activity + took up way too much space). I understand how that last point can be important for a game dev. No one will want to play your game if its 200gb. But there are solutions for this. Modern COD titles since MW2019 have allowed you to download individual gamemodes (campaign, multiplayer, battleroyale). Thats what D2 needs. Allow us to utilize old content, and uninstall when we dont need it or dont want it.

TLDR: If you are a new player, dont get into D2, you will not understand it and not have fun. If you are an old player thats jumping back into the game, you can jump back in, but you will see a game that has been dismembered. If you are a consistent player thats been playing this game, then why are you reading this review. You have eyes and understand whats going on.

I hope for the eventual day that maybe they finally wake up in Bungie HQ and implement my suggestion, or any other method of bringing old content back. But we will most likely not ever see this occur. Guess we gotta wait for Destiny 3 to ♥♥♥♥ up even harder then this game.\t\t\t\t\t
