\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 has come a long way since its release. I came back to the game after it moved to steam, since I wasn't particularly happy with the content during release and the anemic DLC content that came along in the first few months. It's still not perfect, but Bungie separating from Activision was probably the best move for this game and the company as a whole. The newer zones feel much more active and devs have increased the amount of interaction the enemy factions have with one another. These interactions really help and sell the idea that the solar system is at war over the Traveler. The universe itself just feels much more active since the original release of the game, which felt very empty. The environments were beautiful to look at, but it was like a painting, there wasn't anything going on. It felt very static. This pretty, but inactive environment seems to have largely been solved and gets better with each update and DLC that's released. Destiny's still not as robust as similar experiences, however, if you like games like Warframe than this may be worth a shot, especially if you're trying it out as an F2P. The weapons, armor and enemy models look really good and the game is relatively balanced.

If you want a shooter that's a little further away from the norm that gives you a decent amount of depth and control over your character, it's a good game for that, especially if you've got friends that are already playing. That's where this game really tends to shine, either in PvP or PvE. The story is engaging, though it's pacing does feel off at time and there is quite the liberal use of cliff hangers from point-to-point, but it does an effective job at keeping people interested in the universe, with lore that seems to be expanding at a decent rate now.

If you like Space Wizards, you'll probably enjoy Destiny 2. If you weren't fan of the first Destiny, this game may not be for you, as much of the systems are the same. There have been improvements, but whether that's drastic enough to change your mind is really up to the players. I do wish that they would put more time into the PvE portions of the game, but right now there is so much new content that I haven't seen, that I really can't complain about all the great, new missions and raids that have been added.\t\t\t\t\t
