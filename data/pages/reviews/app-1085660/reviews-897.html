\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis review is entirely my opinion from a career player standpoint. I recommend reading alot of reviews to get alot of opinions. I've spent 7 years of my life grinding the Destiny Series. Between the 2 games over multiple consoles and now PC, I have 5000 hours played. This love/hate relationship I have for Destiny is due to quite a few reasons. Here are my Pros and Cons of Destiny 2.

The Pros
- The Gun-play is pretty darn good. Bungie has always been able to create unique weapons and play-styles in there games. Destiny is no different.
- The three classes are fairly diverse, with many different play-styles and a some unique builds for different characters.
- The story and lore of the Destiny Games has always been a strong point. Bungie knows how to tell a story.
- This is the best point in time to play Destiny 2 so far. Let me explain. Destiny 2 has had it's ups and downs since release. Content droughts have plagued the series in the past. However they have adopted a content trickling system which has helped with Bungie's inability to shovel out new content in a timely manner. I would much rather have something new with a 2-3 month seasonal system, over a single yearly expansion that almost always got delayed.
- The PvE Endgame is pretty good. I could explain for hours why Raiding in Destiny 2 hasn't been as good as the original game. However, and that's a big however, Bungie has removed 4 of the 7 raids in the game with an aim to replace the stale ones with new(ish) raids. The raids that left were honestly some of the shortest and worst raids in Destiny history and I'm glad they will be replaced with future content. The current three raids as of this review are the better more challenging ones in my opinion. A 4th raid is dropping in a month or two. The raids are a great way to spend your late game grinding for cool armor and weapon's and the raid exotics are fun to go out and get. If you have 5 other people to go and complete raids weekly, It's a lot of fun. If you don't have people to play with yet, you can choose between the not so great guided games system or going on Reddit and finding a group there. That's how I found my ride or die squad that I have had for 7 years.
- If you are a new player, there is currently hundreds of hours of content for you to do and new content drops every 3 months.

The Cons
- There is no Anti-cheat system in place and there are no plans to ever put one in the game. PvP in Destiny is fun, until you encounter a blatant cheater who is able to see through walls. I'd say if you are looking at Destiny for PvP only, look for a different shooter.
- Bungie's communication is lackluster at best. If you ever talk to a career Destiny player about Bungie you will hear the term, "We hear you". This was always Bungie's statement to the community for years about issues and complaints for various systems in the game.
- *Balancing and changes mid-season. I'm putting an asterisk on this one because they have gotten better. Just know that Bungie will always nerf or fix something that breaks the game in the player's favor immediately, but take 3 months to balance something that is making it worse for the player.
- Gambit is a lost and forgotten PvP meets PvE game mode. Gambit needs an update.
- Recycled content. I'm all for bringing stuff back from Destiny 1 to appeal to old players. I'm not OK with it when it takes the place of new and exciting content. The cold hard truth is that Bungie started bringing back more and more old content recently in order to keep up with player demands for "new" content. I'd rather them just port the majority of Destiny 1 stuff to the current game and get it over with so I can get only new content in the future. Now if you are a new player this doesn't effect you because this is ALL new content for you.
- Eververse Store. This is the paid for cosmetic content. I wont say anything more.

Final Verdict
I will keep this short and sweet. You are buying what you are getting based on where you stand as a new or returning player and if you are a PvE or PvP player. If you are a new player, I highly recommend this game. There is plenty of content to grind through and everything you see come out will be brand new and shiny to you. Bungie caters this game to you. If you are a returning player, I say take it with a grain of salt. You will enjoy the game as it is, but you wont have as much fun as someone completely new to the series. If you are looking for a new PvP FPS game.... go elsewhere. If you are looking for a game for PvE, with a rich story and alot of lore, with cool looking enemies and powerful player abilities, I'd give this a try.

New players - 7.5/10
Returning Players - 7/10
After 1000+ hours - 6/10\t\t\t\t\t
