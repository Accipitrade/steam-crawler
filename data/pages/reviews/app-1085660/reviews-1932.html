\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst of all, the game's anti-cheat system doesn't allow Linux-systems at all. I've not dared to try it with Proton because of this claim. Especially since I did buy an expansion to the game. While I played the game on a Windows machine, I still couldn't recommend the game.

The game's server load balancing seems to be a mess. Populated areas took ages to load. This was an issue since only place you could retrieve most valuable quests, were only in one place. And you usually had to retrieve quests several times in one play-session. I talked with my clan, and the issue seems to have been with the game for several expansions. I cannot tell if developers didn't care, or this was technical limitation of the game-engine. The issue being there for so long time, gave off feelings of mistrust.
There is no genuine progression. You are meant to start at the beginning of the end-game. All of your items start at quite high level, and most of item rarities mean nothing from the start. All of your skills are available from beginning. This did however mean that you're not locked out of most of the content because of new player being too low level. The game didn't feel rewarding to me.

Payment model of the game is strange. You can play older parts of the game free of charge, so I do encourage anyone to try it if the game sounds interesting. Any moment in time you can either buy the latest expansion, or optionally buy the season pass. Even if you DO buy the expansion, there are some events which are only for players with season pass. At the time of writing the "VIP"-events were some public events' rewards, and some bounties (bounties are daily/weekly quests), and I saw no worth in purchasing those.

One thing which other games should really implement too, is that during loading screens, you could still interact with your inventory, skills, view skills and so on. I do believe that this was a must, since there is quite a lot of loading screens in the game. Still, I would like to see such feature at somewhere else.

As a new player, or being part of "new light", I felt I was cut out of a lot of story. It is the biggest downer for me: characters and cutscenes are cut off from the game from previous expansions. The game did hint that I should do the older quests, but I was all the time asking "why am I here", or "Why should I care of this character". Old areas and quests are still there, but I felt that they were meaningless without context given by story and cut-scenes. This led badly to the expansion: I still felt that I don't know what is going on.

Since older expansions are free, I believe it is worth a try. In its current state I cannot recommend to spend any money in to the game.\t\t\t\t\t
