\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you get the base game, there's plenty of content to run through. Grinding light levels up to the seasonal cap without doing difficult, pinnacle gear-rewarding tasks takes maybe 5-6 hours of game-play. Doing the harder missions is always a fun challenge, and there are plenty of people who will join you for a quest or two on Bungie's 'Fireteam Search' website. New players are automatically met with a paywall though, if you want to play specific raids, like Last Wish, you have to pay for that specific DLC. Considering their age though, most of the current DLC's should be cheaper or free within a year or so. If you don't have the money, just do the main quests and you'll have a great time battling enemies in (my opinion) one of the greatest feeling 1st person shooters in the gaming universe. If you do buy the season pass, you gain access to seasonal challenges, missions, story line, and lots of materials and weapons that without it would be objectively difficult to obtain. A new exotic weapon is released to season pass holders every season, and there's a whole load of seasonal gear that will boost your light level with progression. If you pay 70$ for the beyond light deluxe bundle, you'll get access to the next three season passes and the stasis subclass for all of your characters. In other words, it's really your personal preference whether or not you want to buy the DLC's and season pass, so I recommend playing the base game for a little while, and play around with what you have access to, and discover what you don't. If you really want to do something you can't, just spend the money and get access to a load of fun. If you're not a PVP person, don't play crucible when you start up... it's an immediate turn-off considering the amount of sweaty people with shotguns; play strikes and nightfalls for more fun :). So uh, have fun I guess!\t\t\t\t\t
