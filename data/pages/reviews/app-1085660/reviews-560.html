\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I can't recommend Destiny 2's base content by iteself, I'd say that you should gauge if you like the the gunplay (as well as the gameplay in general) before buying any expansion. As well, I need to warn that this game can be quite grindy and you can get burnt out on and fall behind on the season pass easily if you're not careful, so pace yourself if you're a newcomer. If you do like how the game plays, get the "Beyond Light" expansion as it unlocks Stasis (the most recent and most powerful subclass), as well as the nine subclasses (3 per 3 classes) that are locked in the base game, most of them which are quite useful.

As for it's content, Destiny 2 has a wide variety of content but (sadly), about two thirds of the content has some aspect of required PvP which is weighted more towards your weapons' traits then your skill as a player imo, even if two players are evenly matched in terms of the latter. The most of the Raids are fun and somewhat newcomer friendly, but I would wait until you're more experienced before even looking at the Last Wish and Garden of Salvation raids.

Content access in this game is based on what expansions you own so it can be frustrating at times, plus Expansion purchases do NOT transfer between Xbox, Playstation, and PC so if you play on Multiple platforms, be prepared to buy the same expansion multiple times. That said, content is being cycled in and (eventually/hopefully) out of the "Destiny Content Vault" so be sure to prioritize the older content over the newer if you want the full experience.

The monetization is horrendous, but ignorable so be prepared to pay the big bucks for good ornaments in the Eververse.

If you like how the gameplay feels, you should enjoy the rest of it, but if you don't then you can safely move onto another game.\t\t\t\t\t
