\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is the only game where I can sit down for 15 hours in one session and still want to keep going even if I'm tilted AF. I started just before it moved to steam and with 1k hours I've got a few things to say.
First is how wildly beautiful this game is, it is ridiculous. Not super graphically demanding, that's kinda nice but still requires not a potato. Lots of destinations to go and see (even after the vaulting) and lots to do from raiding to basic patrols to vanguard strikes. PvE is done so well and you can see that most of Bungie's team goes towards that. However, PvP content is hugely lacking. It has been 600+ days as of this review since Bungie came out with a new PvP map, last one came out at the beginning of Shadowkeep if I'm correct. As well as PvP is kind of a hell hole right now, Stasis as fun as it is, is not fun to fight against. As well as if you go into Trials, cheaters are in just about every 2-3 matches I've found; PvP fans beware. Though if there's just guns in PvP, its not too bad. Just watch out for Felwinter's Lie.
Overall Destiny 2's story is ever growing with new seasons and new stuff coming out roughly every 3+ months and a new expansion every year. Needs a bit of fixing but if you're looking for a FPS MMO, this is it, among the best out there. Total score is 8/10 PvE. 4.5/10 PvP. 7/10 Story. 10/10 Destinations.

Moon's Haunted y'all\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA gamification time sink that will deprive you of having a real life. It's your choice.

There is an art to the story telling, but it is not enough glue for the endlessly repetitive and pointless grind: unless cool skins for your guns is a thing for you.

What is sad is they have the talent and backstory to make kick-butt single player content, but you're paying for a tease of that possibility in cursory set pieces to add some context to quests that simply no longer feel consequential.

Sadly, newcomers will never experience the wit and charm of Cayde and FailSafe.

On the plus side, the game is casual as you want, so it is easy to jump in and get out.

After 600 hours, I guess a "thumbsup" is obligatory but it is mostly "meh" these days. Alas, everyone is playing catch up to League of Legends and Fortnite since that is where the money is: fair enough.

Another plus is the game does evolve: it is only a matter of time anyone will feel the pang of something lost, but I am sure something is gained as well albeit perhaps for a different crowd.

It still has many redeeming qualities for sure. It would be nonsense to troll after all the amazing production value and effort Bungie has put into their love child. Change is good, but it will have ups and downs.

You can roll with this one and still have a good time.\t\t\t\t\t
