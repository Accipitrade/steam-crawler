\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been wanting to write this for a while (and by god there is a LOT of stuff to go over) and now I have found the means as well as time to do this. Here we go:

The short and skinny TL;DR for those interested: Destiny 2 is by far the most conflicted game I have ever played. For a wide variety of reasons I like and dislike it at the same time. If I could give sideways thumbs on Steam I absolutely would for D2 because, while it is fun, it has a severe amount of downfalls that plague this game. I am definitely not recommending this game to people, but I will advise this for those that do want to try Destiny 2: Enter at your own risk, set low expectations, and partake in it at the base level before you consider going deeper.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

And now for the big, the bad, and the ugly part...

Where do I even begin with this? Best I can gauge that is with the negative parts first. The things that actually detriment this game and prevent it from being that much more that it has potential for. The biggest negative I can think of for this game: DCV (abbreviated Destiny Content Vault). DCV is basically Bungie's way of shelving content in this worldspace and, A) forgetting about it entirely, or B) use it for recycling later. DCV is not only ridiculous but it is borderline unethical. Controversy galore given that Destiny 2 is the only game in the industry that literally removes paid for content. Yeah you read that right: Paid. Content.

Big issue with this is: Why would people wanna spend money on something that is going to get removed from the game entirely? And this leads to yet another glaring issue with Destiny 2: FOMO (Fear of Missing Out). That thing you potentially paid for has time limits. You are hardly able to enjoy the game at your own pace in fear that you, quite literally, will miss out. For many people this leads us to Sunken Cost Fallacy. Where individuals are left feeling the need to play a game solely because it is an active investment. You can argue sure: It's a F2P where you do not need everything in the game to enjoy said game HOWEVER note that this applies to the actual STORY aspects of the game. You paying for yearly DLC drops, season passes, and as of lately whatever dungeon activities Bungie decides to put behind yet another layer of paywall has the big risk of just simply disappearing. Those people that bought this game at launch know EXACTLY how this goes: Red War campaign, Warmind, and Curse of Osiris are the first to have entered the DCV. Forsaken's story part of the expansion is following suit here in Feb. of next year when Witch Queen releases. All because "we wanna reduce game space" which you could also translate to as "pay us money and put play time into our game more so we can show our investors exactly how many people are trapped in this never-ending cycle."

DCV, the amount of FOMO this game pushes, and the further marketing decisions regarding things like Eververse, features in the game, or simply the lack of real F2P substance this game somewhat had pre-Beyond Light. Now the New Light experience suffers dramatically, and you are essentially forced to invest into this game if you are willing to get the most out of it. Fine if you are one of those people that does not mind putting a little something in to get stuff back, however for those that are F2P and/or see this from the outside these decisions that Bungie has made are nothing short of predatory. It's revolting. Even in a F2P environment there is not a single AAA game that completely eliminates paid for content and expects its fans to be totally a-okay with it. Kudos to Bungie though as they have successfully built themselves a fanbase that is willing to basically put up with any ♥♥♥♥ that is shoveled their way gleefully and with mouths agape. These issues listed, and many that hundreds and thousands of others have listed in other reviews, are why I would never recommend this game to anyone reasonably without any semblance of caution. Loads of overwhelming issues that plague this game either because its build is archaic or its functions are flawed. These things turn what could be a masterpiece into a mediocrity.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Enough of that though - Time for some positives:

This game is a game I view more as a social experience, and in this case this game does well for it. Recent things like the introduction of crossplay and other accessibility updates being added to the game help bridge the gap for those of us that do enjoy larger-scale/end-game PvE coop activities. Things like raids bring this game life, and it is very unfortunate that only one of FOUR of these raids are F2P accessible. These activities can be done from a F2P aspect however, the fact that this game is plagued by layers of paywalls for things that are near-mandatory in order to be optimal, it is a tough task. This comes to little to no surprise though as, again, we mentioned this is F2P. Have we mentioned this is F2P?

Because it isn't free-to-play, more accurately, it is freemium.

Can still be enjoyed free, however your enjoyment is limited. Because as far as anything of story-related substance is concerned that is virtually nonexistent within the bounds of this game. Thankfully YouTube exists! That is if you can handle going through hours of lorereading.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

As far as my personal experience goes: Where I have my gripes concerning Destiny 2 I am also aware that in some ways I give it credit where credit is due. Thanks to this game I have expanded my social circle and met some pretty nice people. Built a clan, did some raids, do some challenging content. There are some wonderful design choices aesthetically-speaking that I absolutely adore. Nobody makes skyboxes like Bungie makes skyboxes, and the soundtrack is something that is tough to talk down. Again from my standpoint: A game that has the potential to be a masterpiece is flawed mostly by its marketing and hence is labeled as a mediocrity. Fun game: Just not a good game.

And as for the thumbs like I mentioned earlier seeing as Steam doesn't allow neutral reviews: I'm going to go with an overall thumbs down. Maybe more things will change upon the release of Witch Queen and the future events after that however with the current state of this game: I cannot recommend this to first-timers.\t\t\t\t\t
