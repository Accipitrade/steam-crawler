\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI can genuinely say that Destiny is my *favourite* game, but as much as it hurts to say I just can't in good conscience recommend it to new players...

It's "free to play" but the content you actually have access to without any dlc is pretty limited and doesn't *nearly* do the game justice, the new light ""campaign"" is just... really really bad, leaving so many things completely unexplained and leaving it to the player to look up way too much about the game. A lot of people don't want to have to do homework to get into playing a game and destiny's in a spot right now where that's exactly what you need to do because you're left so out of the loop on the incredible lore, many game mechanics are glossed over, the enemy types are barely explained, and worst of all when you do decide that you want to buy some dlc and *actually* get into the game it can be SUPER confusing trying to figure out what to get and what it comes with.
The game needs a hook, it needs something to capture new players and pique their interest. The Red War campaign did that PERFECTLY back when d2 first went f2p and I picked it up, but that's been removed and the New Light campaign just doesn't do it...


If you do decide you want to take the plunge, here's some useful info:

- First dlc you should grab is the Witch Queen, it has the best campaign by far. get the legacy collection instead if that's on sale (which it is at the time of writing this) as that's a really good deal for what you get.
- Seasons are sold separately from dlcs and will have the more live-service side of the game, this is where a LOT of the constantly building story ends up.
- A lot of people complain about "the grind". Ignoring that it's a very expected portion of any mmo, it really isn't bad if you're just playing somewhat casually, as a lot of the core activities don't have any level requirement, and anything with no requirement will basically disable levels and place everyone on the same playing field only separated by skill and the quality of gear. Just play the game how you like and mess with new things as you happen upon them
- Pretty much all of your Glimmer as you are first starting should go into unlocking all of your subclass stuff, as the biggest power boost (and best part of the game imo) is in buildcrafting, between your sublass and armor mods you can make some really fun and wacky builds, don't be afraid to consult youtube for ideas there's no shame in using youtube builds that pique your interest, just make sure to experiment too.
- The Light Level system will feel very confusing but it's fairly simple in practice; your light level is determined by the average level of all the gear you have equipped, + some bonus level from the seasonal artifact that you'll just gain passively as you play. Every new annual dlc the level floor is raised above the previous ceiling to put everyone at an effective zero, this year's being 1600. The first 150 levels gained from there will go quickly just about via any source of loot, the next 50 will be a bit slower and you'll only get higher level gear from rewards that say either "powerful" or "pinnacle", these are in limited supply each week so once you've done all of those you gotta wait for the next weekly refresh on tuesday. The last 10 levels will be gained slowly and only from "pinnacle" rewards.
A lot of the game's activities do not use the light-level system like, at all, so don't stress about it too much until you start getting a grasp on the game and want to try the harder stuff.
- And lastly, look up MynameisByf on youtube, he has incredible lore videos that you can use to get caught up in the universe since the game does such a poor job of giving you access to that in-game, I wholly reccommend the "The Complete Story of Destiny! From origins to Shadowkeep", it's 4 hours long but well timestamped so you can take it at your own speed as you wish, Byf (also known as lore daddy) does an incredible job compiling and presenting the game's lore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a complete and utter joke of a game now, and has been ever since Beyond Light. While the game was definitely worthy of criticism back in the first few years leading up to Forsaken, the game steadily went downhill after the Sunsetting and Vaulting. Playable content being locked away, weapons people enjoyed using and armor we had good stats on already were being axed, new content released now being far more expensive and locked behind separate DLC, and Bungie being a worthless company when it comes to actually fixing the game in issues that actually hamper the experience

CONTENT VAULT
Yes, is helped with server stability since the game engine was not made to handle that much content all at once, but now that it was "upgraded", the game has not had the first 4 years of content reinstated or revamped, forever locked away seemingly

MICROTRANSACTIONS
For the newest DLC, Lightfall, it will either cost you 100 USD to buy the ultimate edition with everything included for the year, or 50 for the DLC, 20 for Dungeon Access, and 55 for all 4 season passes (The first season of the year was still normal price, but this current one and the next 2 are now 15 dollars instead of 10). I'll talk about the price of everything for a new player in the next segment

NEW PLAYER EXPERIENCE
There is no New Player Experience. The content we had, gotten for free, or was actually worth buying is now gone. The vanilla campaign and first 3 DLCs are gone, and unless you buy a story DLC, you have very little content to play outside of the standard playlists. If you want all the game's major content, no cosmetic or silver included bundles, here's the price of everything. Forsaken (20 dollars), 30th Anniversary (25 dollars), Shadowkeep (25 dollars), Beyond Light (30 dollars), Witch Queen (30 dollars), Lightfall (50 dollars). It will cost you 180 dollars to buy content for a now Free 2 Play game, 140 if you buy the DLC bundles. Keep in mind, Forsaken was one of the DLCs they vaulted, selling only the Raid, 2 strikes, and around 20 Exotics to farm for. It should be free, and don't get me started on the Dungeon Keys and Season Passes. You'll spend over 300 dollars getting everything

SERVER STABILITY
It's ass... I got nothing to say, it's just ass. We are on our 9th server maintenance this season. SEASON! We have 2 months left! We aren't even being compensated for all of this, and clearly none of the money they launder from us is going back into the game

CONCLUSION
I miss when Activision was the Publisher. While the first few years of Destiny 2 were understandably bad most of the time, we at least had playable content and thankfully minor amounts of microtransactions (They were still bad, but not as bad as current times). Players are completely fed up with Bungie's way of handling the game, having the gall to focus more on another title and leaving us with a broken, festering mess of a game they expect to live on after the next DLC. I'm calling it, Final Shape will be the final DLC before Bungie pulls the cord on Destiny once and for all, breaking their biggest promise of keeping the game updated and alive after the Light and Dark saga\t\t\t\t\t
