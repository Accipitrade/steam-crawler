\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an in-depth review from a player of four years, nearing 3300 hours as of writing.

TL;DR - Bungie, the Developer and Publisher of Destiny 2, has been slacking in terms of balancing, is spending ressources and time on features and changes no one wants or needs while neglecting performance issues and PvE/PvP balancing, and is slowly introducing more and more systems for the sake of monetization, not for gameplay enrichment. What would have been a great game to jump into 1 or 2 years ago, is slowly becoming an abusively monetized hellhole of a game. And it hurts, because underneath all that there is a lot to love about the game.

WHAT IS DESTINY 2?

Destiny 2 is an FPS Loot-Shooter with MMORPG Elements. It takes place in a Sci-Fi Postapocalyptic setting, though the story is more so fluff for the gameplay, which consists of cool guns and flashy abilities. You'll jump into the role of "The guardian", one of many resurrected protectors of humanity, which has been brought near extinction - all of this is just set-dressing, which basically translates into "Go and kill evil space insects and turtle-rhinos and ♥♥♥♥"-Gameplay.

WHY IS DESTINY GOOD?

I could (and did) write paragraphs that about that, which Steam would never allow me to release because they're too long. To make it as short as it can be, Destiny 2s greatest strength is the gunplay and buildcrafting. With the plethora of weapons, armor mods, subclasses, abilities, and exotic weapons/armor pieces at your disposal, you can create hundreds, if not thousands of builds - and though they'll all be primarily for DPS and Add-Clear, and only tangentially for Tanking or Support, these aspects can still be fulfilled.

Add on top of that a Story that is (or used to be) good enough to get involved if you want to, and discover an outrageously deep complex of lore underneath, but also subtle enough to just jump in, turn your head off and shoot at space rhinos, and the beautiful aesthetic of Destiny, which consists both of a more grounded, Sci-Fi-Militaristic look, and a less grounded, more fantastical Sci-Fi-Fantasy look, and you'll be able to feel right at home shooting Aliens, slinging grenades, and being one of the last protectors of what's left of humanity.

WHY IS DESTINY BAD?

Though all coins have two sides. For every gun that feels awesome and good to use, that feels like a powerful addition to your arsenal, there are 2 that have been forgotten about and swept away in the power creep, and 3 that have been sunsetted to never return, that you'll never obtain and never be able to get a use from. For every interesting and streong Exotic Armor or Weapon, there's 2 that have been forgotten about because their effects are just no longer relevant.

Bungie is getting very greedy. There is an in-game shop that is fueled by premium currency, though every week there is a new assortment of items that can be bought for in-game currency. However, these purchases are still abusively prices so that you're always buying either too much, or too little premium currency. The seasonal Events, which used to be free-to-play for everyone, are now ebing additionally monetized by outright locking cosmetic loot behind an 'Event Pass' for 10$, and they increased the season pass price now to 15$ - bold, considering that Season Pass Content is removed annually with the release of a new expansion. Expansions, that are full-price may I add, at 50$ or alternatively, for 100$, which also includes all the four season passes until the new expansion releases. Both of which is a price hike from the prior Expansions.

And all that for not a shred of content more. In fact, a lot of seasoned players are going through major phases of inactivity and burnout at this point, because Bungie has not been adding a significant enough amount of new content. A lot is revamped, and not actually new. Old content does not receive updates once it's released, so once you've comleted it and received the loot you want, it essentially becomes worthless - which Bungie 'fixed' by adding major levels of RNG and luck so you have to repeat the content over and over again to get what you want. On top of that content is being stretched out by adding needless grind inbetween, adding currency cost (Glimmer) just so you always have to keep a little eye on that and, if you actually want to do a lot of stuff at once, have to go and grind that currency because you can't save it up, and they will fix any sort of trick to get just a little more than intended within the day to keep you grinding so you have to do more without getting any content.

Meanwhile bugs can go rampant for years. As of writing this, many exotic weapons still do not gain Anti-Barrier capabilities against non-champion enemies (A bug, that at this point is more than 2 years old). Guardian Ranks are still being wrongfully displayed on name plates and the War Table still blinks over the upgrade currency Bungie gave us too much, performance issues prevail and persist. And all of these take apparently time to fix, whereas an exploit to get just a little bit more of the currency that only exists to block you from doing too much at once is fixed by the day, and a weapon that behaves just a little bit too good, to the point where it would make the grind easier and allow players to have a little bit more fun while grinding, is immediately stomped down by Bungie and fixed or disabled, whereas gamebreaking PvP-Metas take weeks to even be acknowledged and even longer to be fixed.

And there's so much more to be said, but I don't want to... It makes me sad to say all these negative things about the game that kept me busy throughout some tough times, and which honestly can be a lot of fun, with great gunplay and awesome abilities, if only the developers set their priorities straight, and if only the Execs stopped being so greedy. If only, If only, If only. But If is an If, not an is, and so, for now, my dear reader, I wouldn't recommend this to you.\t\t\t\t\t
