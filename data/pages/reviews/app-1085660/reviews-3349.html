Yes the game has a lot of moving parts, it's more of a multiplayer rpg than an fps at sometimes and that can be a lot to take in especially as a new player with no real good introduction to all of these parts. Just jump in and if you want PvE go to Amanda Holiday in the tower and do The Red War Campaign and if you want PvP get some weapons you like and jump in the Crucible. Once you find what you like and get in the rythme of the chase for higher power level and better weapons and armor the game really opens up and becomes very fun. As a fairly new player (Only around 40 hours before the steam move) there is so much to grind for and so many activities and quest to do I won't run out of content even on the f2p side for a few hundred hours. If you want to spend some money forsaken, a great campaign with great quests is definitely a good value. And keeping up on all the new content is only $10 a season which is 3 months which for the amount of content between the Season Ranks (Battle Pass), new seasonal activities, and quest for new weapons. I definitely think if a looter shooter game like Borderlands interests you but you want an ongoing game as a service experience, give it a try.\t\t\t\t\t
