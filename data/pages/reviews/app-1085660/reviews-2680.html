- If youre not much of a PvP player, then of course there's PvE, and boy is it a lot. Raids, Activities, Strikes, NightFalls, youn name it they've got it. And if on just such an occasion you're feeling both PvP and PvE. look no further to Gambit, a hybrid of Pve And PvP.

---The Bad---

-Cheating has become far more prevelant in recent times. because of the switch to Steam and its availability as a free to play title, hacking (Especially in High Elo Competitive) is extremely common. many times i've had my fireteam face an enemy who teleport's, is invincible, or will insta-kill with a Prometheus Lense.

-Steam does little to counter this issue. as much as i hate to say it, prior to the switch to Steam I had hardly ever seen cheating as much as i am now. countless profiles are dedicated to Crucible Boosting and are still active. one such profile came to mind: the comments section of his profile was littered with complaints of hacking and network abuse; the activity had been over the span of months, and it seemed close to 5-6 pages of comments were speaking of his abuse. Additionally, this profile openly advertised their services on multiple games on this platform, and joked about how superior of a played they were. To my knowledge, this profile is active and at large.

---The Conclusion---

Overall, i love this game; I always have. Albeit the competitive scene is somewhat littered with hackers and network abusers, 90% of the game remains untouched by them, and boy do i love it. I think, in all honesty, this game will fit comfortably in any casual or competitive players library. However you chose to enjoy it is entirely up to you, and there are numerous different avenues to explore your character and customize them in whatever fashion you see fit.

Eyes up Guardian, your adventure awaits.\t\t\t\t\t
