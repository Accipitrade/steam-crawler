\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have been playing Destiny since the Rise of Iron Days (Didn't get the chance to really play before that sadly). When Destiny 2 came out, I found the game amazing enough to share with friends, and while I remember they were a little sceptical at first, all it took was a single session and they were hooked on the game. It has been quite the journey since then and the game is a LOT better to play when teamed up with them.

Just avoid LFG... well, not completely, but be cautious.
LFG for Destiny can be relatively Toxic; from people only wanting the most Elite of the Elite to Trolls who will boot you before you can earn your activity awards. My personal experience has been with people who are EXTREMELY aggressive and treat the game like it's a Life or Death situation for not only them, but their entire family, their friends, and their friends families. There are good people in the community and have made good friends with people met through LFG, but have met a good share of bad ones and have seen countless stories and clips of negative LFG experiences.

I hold a lot of personal... unpopular opinions as well from my time playing Destiny 1 where I felt aspects in D1 were better than in D2, everything from Matchmaking (Where I found in D1, Mercy Ruling was very rare because the SBMM felt balanced, whereas in D2 Mercy Ruling isn't Uncommon and occurs quite frequently due to Matchmaking being all over the place) to particular Exotics having a ridiculous amount of power that don't make sense. I dare say no more because the bracketed matchmaking comment will get me flamed hard - hence why I say "Unpopular Opinions".

Anyway....
- Game is great; especially with friends.
- The Community in comparison to other big gaming communities is honestly one of the better ones. It does have a very nasty side which isn't hard to find, but the community as a whole is genuinely a good place.
- A lot of balancing is needed in the game; from class/subclass balancing, weapons and gear, and the in depth stuff like Matchmaking and Region locking. The game has been out for some time, yes, and with how much stuff is in the game, it makes finding that sweet spot very difficult where once one item finds it's right place, another twenty items are now out of place. I can't judge Bungie rightly on how difficult it might be to keep this balance, but things like Matchmaking and Region Locking (Especially right now in PvP and PvE) need working on.
- Coming from Destiny 1 to Destiny 2; or going back and playing Destiny 1, you'll find that the first game (Depending on Opinion) will have done quite a fair few things better than Destiny 2 (E.g. I loved the layout of the Subclass Ability Trees; still do even after the 3.0 updates for Subclasses), but the improvements made to Destiny 2 from the first game are evident and goes to show the dedication to make this not just a game; but something far greater.

- Also.... GIMME MY ARCHONS FORGE! I MISS IT! xD\t\t\t\t\t
