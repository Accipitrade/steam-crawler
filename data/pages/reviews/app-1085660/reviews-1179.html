\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, I would say that Destiny 2 is a great game...when the game director Luke Smith is not actively taking measures to ruin it.

Destiny 2 (and its predecessor) has an excellent story in the futuristic science-fiction realm, and the lore books can often tell a better story than the game itself. Really worth reading them online. As you complete quests, pieces of the lore also drop in-game for you to view.

You start out as a Guardian of the Light, protecting humanity against its various enemies with the help of the Traveler, a paracausal entity. However, there is an opposing paracausal entity...the Darkness and the Pyramids. Last season, the Pyramids (the Darkness) have finally arrived in the solar system, and with the most recent expansion, it is the Guardian's first taste of the powers that the Darkness offers. All really fascinating.

Destiny 2 features PvE and PvP elements. I am mostly a PvE player. There is an activity in the game, called Gambit, where you fight enemies of humanity while an opposing team is in their own arena doing the same. Members can invade the other side and attempt to disrupt the progress of the opposing team. I find it interesting to play. The better the team, the better the experience.

Edit: Bungie has announced the removal of sunsetting! I will leave the rest of the portion of this review for historical reasons.

As I mentioned at the beginning, this game really is great when Luke Smith is not doing his best to destroy it. With the recent introduction of *entirely arbitrary* sunsetting of weapons and armor, over 75% of the weapons are no longer usable. Sunsetting armor was entirely unnecessary, and now any legendary item you get in the game expires within a year or less...it is a constant loot grind and it is entirely unnecessary. Hopefully, sometime in the future, Bungie will get some sense in their head and get rid of Luke Smith. The game always feels just shy of its peak potential, and he's always having to apologize for his previous mistakes.

And yes, the community has been complaining about this for a very long time. Luke Smith is hard-headed.[/url]\t\t\t\t\t
