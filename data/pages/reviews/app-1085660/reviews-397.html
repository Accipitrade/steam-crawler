\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL:DR - If you haven't constantly played D2 since forever, I would only recommend to hop back in if you want some semi fun pve until you hit the insane endgame grind. DO NOT, I REPEAT, DO NOT BUY THIS GAME FOR THINKING YOU WILL HAVE SOME FUN PVP. This is, easily, by far, the worst fps pvp experience I've ever had. The newest battlefield was LIGHTYEARS better. Read the last part of my review which covers that if you are interested as to why.

Recently hopped back in after years of not playing. Fun little progression to the game until you get to higher light levels, then it becomes a real slog of dailies and weekly grinding to get one more light point. I can't even imagine how boring it is for the people that play until max light level then farm and grind even more for perfectly rolled gear, which you have to roll god stats and perks on each item. The strikes are fun until you have done them a million times, then it is just autopilot farming. The Witch Queen campaign was a pretty good, although there are spots, like most of the other parts of the game, that heavily rely on gimmicks and bs. As for raids, I've read that about 12% of the playerbase has actually raided. I tried twice but was instantly kicked because I didn't know the raids by heart. This includes the new raids for The Witch Queen. If you want a fun raiding experience, I doubt this is it. Overall, not a bad experience, but I'm not sure it was worth $60. Well... definitely not worth $60, but if you are bored and got the cash who am I to judge.

Now, about pvp. Again, unless you have played destiny 2 and only destiny 2 for years and have a full set of bros to play with, do not get into this thinking you will have a fun pvp experience. This is, by far, the worst fps pvp I've ever seen. Firstly, their network is P2P based (they claim it is a mix of dedicated servers and P2P, but I'm 100% sure that pvp is all P2P.) Remember 2003 when host advantage existed? Yeah, it's 100% still here in 2022. I've had games where I was clearly the host and could actually play, and others where the obvious host was at a 15.0 kda. Secondly, they say the pvp is balanced because you can pick a mode where "light levels" don't matter... but god roll stats still do. I've unloaded full clips of my best exotics into a guy to barely get rid of his shield to just have him one shot me with the same weapon. Unless you have the most optimal pvp rolled gear ever, you will be facing people with a distinct advantage every time. When you run into guys maxed the f out, you will lose to them in every single 1v1. This brings me to the third and last nail in the pvp coffin. When you fight that guy with maxed out pvp gear because he plays nothing but destiny 2 pvp for 12 hours a day every day, he has 5 other friends with him just like him. Even if you are solo queued. There is no MMR or skill matchranking either, so you can literally join your first crucible solo and face off against the hardest of hardcore premade with every single piece of meta gear and builds that will literally mercy kill your team going 100 points to 0 with you never getting a shot off as you are instakilled the second you move. This was the case in about 90% of the matches I played. The only game mode that prevents 6 man fireteams from joining, so real solo q, is a mode where light level DOES matter, so again, you are if you do or don't. I also have the distinct feeling that aimbots are freely used, but I can't say for certain. What i can say, and I cannot overstate this enough... DO NOT DOWNLOAD THIS GAME FOR PVP. Once again, this is worse than the newest battlefield.\t\t\t\t\t
