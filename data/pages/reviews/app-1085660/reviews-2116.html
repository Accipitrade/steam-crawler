\r\n\t\t\t\t\tProbably Destiny 2 is the best FPS games I ever played maybe. It is not just a FPS game, but MMORPG as well. I thought Destiny 2 is a single player game but I was wrong. So, let me summarize positive side and negative side of this game.

What do I like:
- Some of campaigns stories are very lengthy and interesting, especially Red War and Forsaken. Also, I do enjoy to watch cinematic cutscene a lot, it really looks like a movie.
- There has been so many activities and quests to do (raid, strike, nightfall strike, exotic quests, dungeon, nightmare hunt, public event, escalation and much more). Some of them are lengthy and challenging. For example, legend and heroic nightfall strike is really tough but still I enjoy it a lot. However, you must plan carefully about the equipment you used before start to play nightfall.
- Playing D2 together with your friends is really fun. If you are being stuck with some quests, just find your friends to overcome it together.
- If you want to have a lot of fun, then just buy all DLCs.
- The campaign story is replayable, it is available in Vanguard.

What do I dislike?
- Always encounter some error codes like beaver, bee, weasels etc when playing crucible and gambit. I really frustrate about such networking issue but dont know what to do with it.
- If I can remember well. There is one of Shadowkeep strikes still currently unplayable. No enemies are around at the beginning and I even can't access to next checkpoint. Sometime I also have encountered some bug when playing Vanguard strike. For example, when playing Scarlet Keep strike, I was spawned randomly at somewhere which is far away from outside of Hive fortress and can't go inside it. It is ridiculous.

On the other hand, using some 3rd party softwares or tools like FRAPS, MSI Afterburner, Reshade etc to SLIGHTLY modify the game will get yourself banned even if they aren't cheating tools. Don't even try it if you attempt to do so. If doing so, you will be issued account ban or device ban. Bungie is SUPER HARDCORE on these stuffs. Some players have been banned because of it.

Nevertheless, I just pray Bungie will release PC version of Destiny 1 in one day. I never play it though before. Lastly, I would recommend you to spend your money on this game, 9/10.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's good fun to play PvE, especially with friends.
The PvP "system" however can ♥♥♥♥ right off.

Allow me to elaborate, what makes a good pvp game?
-Obvious distinction between friend and foe.
-Looking at an enemy should give you all the info you need to fight them.
-A balanced system, be it weapons or beyond.
-The ability to have a fair fight (looking especially at you, stasis)

Destiny 2 lacks just about all of these, sure you can equip the meta weapons but what if you don't like playing with those types? well you're gonna get destroyed.
I would make an argument for the first point, but then I remember how the lighting systems makes it completely impossible to see into bright areas from a dark room and vice versa.
Realistic? Sure. Fun? Yeah ofc it's fun to get killed by someone I couldn't even see or react to!
See the problem there?

I doubt I have to address the "balanced system" especially now that Bungie had the bright idea to allow stun mechanics in PvP, games that know how to do PvP either got rid of them or never had them, Bungie. There is a reason for that, Bungie. Being unable to fight back even for a couple seconds is broken, Bungie. And no mashing V to break free does not fix that, Bungie.

And then the last point I want to make, being able to assess your enemy and how to fight them quickly. Thanks to underlying armour stats, class abilities being enabled in PvP, and some modes even having your light level be a factor it is completely impossible to accurately judge your opponent's strenghts and weaknesses in a reasonable way.

So in short do yourself a favour, enjoy PvE to the fullest, it's great fun.
And - unless you're a masochist like me - stay away from the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ that is PvP.\t\t\t\t\t
