\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is without a doubt one of the best shooters on the market, but at times it will push your patience right to its limit.

Lets start with the gun play. There is nothing better currently available in my opinion, it feels almost perfect. The weapon variety is wide enough to suit almost everyone's style of play, recoil is nice and predictable on most if not all weapons and the ADS mechanic is spot on. Even the transition to 3rd person view when wielding a sword is seamless and works extremely well.

The world (or worlds) you have before you to explore are also vast and varied. From the derelict buildings of Earth to the strange caves of alien planets, everything is beautiful to look it with an exceptional attention to detail almost everywhere. The engine renders everything well and plays silky smooth, the optimisation is exceptional. I achieve a constant 144fps at 1080p on my slightly aged gtx1070. This is no walking simulator either, with the ability to spawn a vehicle almost anywhere whenever its required, you can traverse these beautiful and vast landscapes with speed and efficiency.

There is a wide variety of enemy's to aim your cross hairs at too, all sporting different abilities and challenges. From shield wielding to health regenerating and snipers to kamikaze bombers Destiny 2 had every base covered. There are six different races (or factions) of enemy each requiring slightly different tactics and strategies to defeat them efficiently. But if that's not your thing, with most of the rank and file enemies just point and shoot and it will usually do the trick quite well.

The bulk of your time playing D2 will be spent grinding activities and completing a multitude of steps on each different quest line to reach the end goal. Sometimes that's a rare (exotic) weapon, sometimes its to progress the narrative in the story. Either way if you prefer to reach your end goals quickly then D2 probably isn't for you, some quests can have upwards of 10 steps that are needed to be completed in order to achieve the goal.

Where D2 falls down for me quite significantly is the steps that can be required to complete a lot of these quests. I play solo, no clan or community to support my adventures. Some quests are just impossible to complete solo. As a result this makes sometimes quite large portions of the game inaccessible to me. Quest lines have to be abandoned over halfway through as the next part requires more than a solo effort, this can result is hours of grinding wasted and your progress up to that point pointless. There is of course lots of other quest lines to complete instead so you can always just move onto something else, i just wish there was an option to filter all content that cannot be completed solo so you don't end up wasting vast amounts of time on quests that you will never be able to complete. Some of the activities in the world(s) do have matchmaking and for the most when its available it works well, but not all.

Endgame content called 'raids' also suffers with the same issue. I have never participated in one as it requires you to have a team of 6 players (no matchmaking available) before you can play it. So unless you are lucky enough to have a clan or community with a nice amount of active D2 players, or you go looking for a group of strangers on discord this endgame content will be blocked for you. A shame that this wasn't made simpler so that it could be completed with matchmaking as a lot of chances at end game high tier equipment is lost to the people that cannot participate.

The MMO elements have some fundamental flaws also, the main one being communication. Jump into any other MMO out there and the chat box is constantly scrolling with people chatting about all sorts of things from the large boss around the corner, to next doors cat called Figaro. In D2 though don't expect much of this, the chat box almost never moves. No idea really why this is really, but you will almost never get a reply to a question or even see a question asked, it is literally just a waste of GUI space. I can't see that it's all D2 players just being boring and rude, it is much more likely that it is a flaw in the game somewhere as chatting seems to be actively discouraged.

There is PVP elements to the game, 4v4 co-op and 6v6 or 3/3 death match or domination style modes. Personally though i don't participate in these much. Some quest lines will force you into them but apart from this I tend to stay away. If your looking for a PVP online shooter there are much better options out there.

Overall though D2 is a majestic game that you can easily get lost in for hours. Just be prepared for a little frustration down the line if the majority of your playtime will be solo. More matchmaking options and the ability for all quests to be completed solo if desired and Destiny 2 would almost be the perfect game.....almost :)

In its current state 8/10!\t\t\t\t\t
