\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Note: I did not play Destiny 1, and started Destiny 2 with New Light/Shadowkeep

Destiny 2 is a game built on a great foundation of mechanics, but broken down when it comes to avenues to try to actually enjoy the game. The world and lore behind it are interesting and provide enough of a base to go out and fight each of the different faction. The shooting mechanics are very good, with every weapon type feeling useful while allowing for different playstyles to feel useful. Bullet magnetism, where shooting within a certain radius of a target will "magnetize" a bullet and pull it to a critical spot and thus guaranteeing a hit, was a new concept to me, but it definitely does work with the combination of looter shooter RPG mechanics and generally variable fast paced gameplay. There is plenty of stuff to do as well, as there are a variety of worlds available for exploration along with all of their hidden secrets, strikes, raids, exotic weapon quests, and more. There is also PvP available that is widely encouraged and seemingly fun, though I have not played much of it as I am more intrigued by the single player side.

As for the bad stuff, one thing sticks out like a sore thumb: this is an MMO, predicated on social interaction in order to do the highest level of tasks such as raids, and yet it's borderline impossible to make social contact with anybody outside of a third party system. Chat is disabled by default, and the most players in a single game is 12. This isn't a problem for most of the content, as PvP and the 3 player strike modes have matchmaking for you to team up with random players, but high level strikes, exotic quests, and raids do not have matchmaking. Clans are a great way to do team activities, but the only way in game to join one is to hope you randomly end up in the same instance as a member and ask to join. Realistically, the only way to join is to go online and find one that is recruiting and hope they will take you. It's just a really obtuse way to go about interacting with such a significant part of the game and is a grievous oversight.

Aside from social interaction, the New Light experience is a crapshoot. Very little is explained, such as how to access the actual campaigns that might explain anything about what's going on. You're taught how to do the basics, such as movement and weapon mechanics, and your melee, grenade, and ultimate abilities. After that, you're mostly left to your own devices. There is plenty to do, but it seems that a lot of things were changed up with the base light level being bumped from 1 to 750. It is easier to access the late game, as much of the grind was eliminated, but simple things such as how to change your class from solar to void and select different sub abilities aren't explained at all. Exploration and discovery are parts of a great game, but when I'm having to google basic game mechanics just to know how to use my character, there's a problem.

Lastly, progression is a bit of problem. Once you reach the soft light level cap of 900, you can only get better gear by doing certain types of activities, which more or less involves doing group activities and precludes most single player free roam exploration. Running around in the EDZ to find all of the lost sectors is still plenty of fun, but when I have no chance to acquire anything better than my current loadout, there's a pressure to go play the less exciting modes. The weekly challenges combined with this make even the potentially progression parts a chore, and when a game dictates how and when and where I'm allowed to have fun, there's no purpose in playing anymore.

There is a great game buried somewhere in Destiny 2, and if you have a group of friends willing to learn and play together, it can definitely be found. Destiny 2 requires so much commitment just to learn, and so many hoops to jump through in order to enjoy the finer points of the game, that I cannot recommend getting into it at this stage.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\t++Looks good
+++Sounds great (Music-wise, mostly)
++Plays well
++Challenging endgame that definitely requires teamwork and proper gear

~Lots of reading and lore for you bookworms out there. In fact, the brunt of the game's lore is in text entries that only a minority bother to read, because reading books is for Warlocks.

-Writing and story are ehhh...
-Many bosses are just bigger versions of regular enemies, but with new attacks. Lazy.
-Many seemingly PvE quests require you to do both PvE AND PvP, ensuring lots of frustration.
--Monetization consists of yearly expansions, a cosmetics store, AND battle passes. Yeesh.
--Updates and bugfixes are scarce due to the developers seemingly wanting to update only on specific intervals... Expect minor but consistently annoying bugs to be squashed only when they feel like it.
---Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)-based content updates may retain some players, but basing your game's new updates on "You had to be there!" and then removing most of the content afterwards just leaves a bad taste in my mouth.
---Vaulting content isn't a new thing (Warframe vaults Prime stuff constantly), but the way Bungie did it is actively detrimental to the game. Because the game is becoming supposedly bloated in file size, they've decided to outright remove a bunch of old content (open worlds and the like) whenever they add new content. 1 step forward, 3 steps back.
---Their plan to "sunset" seasonal weapons by preventing you from increasing their power level past a certain limit is just the cherry on top in this FOMO-ridden, constantly content-shaved trainwreck of a game.

I used to love this game, but I do not like the direction it is heading... I sound like a jaded veteran, but I am not. I'm just another newbie they managed to catch at one point, but they lost me eventually.

Avoid.
5/10\t\t\t\t\t

Almost all the Content available in Game is fun.
Season of the Forge was a let down and Strikes get really boring once you figure out how everything works. But imo the most fun PvE content is Raiding which Destiny has 7 of.
Gambit, its PvEvP Content, is disliked by most but with a coordinated Team, it turns into amazing content too.
PvP gets a new Sandbox update, shifting meta every 90 Days and every Season we get new Gear to mix and match for new Loadouts and Playstyles for all Content.
I really like this intricate World, Lore and matching Music to underline this.
Even when I took a break for 6 Months I kept thinking of how amazing this game is.
Apparently it has quite a bad intro to it and it's very complicated to start but it's worth it just watch a few vids on YT or something.
I'd highly recommend everyone to try it out and see for themselves. Especially if you like to immersive yourself in a RPG-like Experience while blasting Aliens.\t\t\t\t\t
