\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't think I've ever played a fun, exciting game like this that actively suffers from timed content in such a prolific way. There are so many builds that rely on certain weapons that you can't obtain anymore. I'm a returning player who skipped all of Beyond Light Launch and onward until Lightfall, and god it is just so painful to see a cool build or a build that is *required* for some sub-classes to even function well in any meaningful content... And have the weapon that makes the build work require a catalyst that is now a random drop, or you can't even get anymore if you tried. Seasonal weapons have done a great job as the game's life as gone on to address pain points of boring loot and weak rolls, but now a player who has missed even just one season can be behind forever... Now there *are* ways for these weapons to 'come back'. With set rolls on vendors, at random completely indiscernible times for a week at most. I waited for a Calus Mini Tool SMG with demolition and/or Incandescent to be available all the first two seasons of Lightfall for my solar builds (builds that are now much weaker due to season of the deep's mods)... And it just hasn't reappeared. I had to settle for a much less effective weapon and weapon type I could craft just to function on my solar builds. This pattern is repeated with Arc builds this season, where my Thunderlord hasn't gotten a catalyst to drop and I have no good primary/special weapons for arc to use.

Destiny is super fun, really! It's extremely exciting, the effects are all top-notch, enemy design is varied and there is alot of challenging raids and content... But, back when I was playing the game years ago, we didn't have to buy dungeons. We paid less for expansions and seasons, and retained the same amount of content. Now, not only has these been brought up in price, and dungeons now being sold separately from the normal game... Bungie are making more money every year then last. They are making two more brand new games, one of which was just announced. They are not doing this, nickle and diming every last piece of content, to make the game. They are doing it to make a profit. So, as such, these problems amount to alot of frustration. For now, unless you've been playing this game since it started or years prior to this review, avoid at all costs. It's not worth it, period.\t\t\t\t\t
