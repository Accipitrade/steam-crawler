\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTl;dr: Still all about the grind, rewards even worse, impossible to get armour exotics (game changing gear) for casuals.

The last time I played was the end of Forsaken and start of w/e the DLC was after that. I left soon after launch as the grind was tiring, the rewards poor and the story was rubbish.

The story is still bad and for me peaked at Forsaken, the game is still a grind, and the rewards are still sh1te. What’s best about Destiny 2 is its gameplay, and the new ability Strand is fun.


What is grinding my gears now is, coming back basically from the end of Forsaken, I have none of the exotics from then until now, which is a massive problem with missing exotic armour. If you need an essential piece for a build, you have to run these mini-dungeons with maxed out gear, solo. Looking up builds, to get the gear to help me run the dungeon solo, I need the gear which I don’t have that has a chance of dropping from the dungeon. Can you see a problem here?


Added to this, to put the grind and levels in perspective, as a semi-casual player I have played about 130 hours since I bought the latest DLC. You start at level 1600, and in that time I am now at level 1800. You need to be at level 1830 to run the dungeons. The rate at getting from 1800-1830 is actually much slower than 1600 – 1800 as 1 level is now only given by specific limited quests each week. So as a casual, I am realistically never going to hit the level I need before they go up next season to even think about running the dungeons to get a CHANCE at a drop for an exotic I am missing to make a build to run the dungeon I can’t even run anyway because my level isn’t and will never be high enough.


I still maintain Destiny 2 was run better under ActivisionBlizzard.

I am having fun, just a lot of the game and its builds, which I paid for, are locked-out to me because I am not playing the game every waking moment.

As such, I can't really recommend this game to any of my mates.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMoon Cake

Ingredients

CRUST:
1 cup water
1/2 cup butter
1 cup all-purpose flour
4 large eggs
FILLING:
2 packages (3.4 ounces each) instant vanilla pudding mix
3 cups cold whole milk
1 package (8 ounces) cream cheese, softened
TOPPING:
1 carton (8 ounces) frozen whipped topping, thawed
Chocolate sauce
Chopped nuts

Directions

In a saucepan, bring water and butter to a boil. Add flour all at once and stir until mixture forms a ball. Remove from heat and cool slightly. Add eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition.
Spread on a greased 15x10x1-in. jelly roll pan. Bake at 400° for 30 minutes. Cool but do not prick, leaving surface with its "moon-like" appearance.
Meanwhile, for filling, beat pudding and milk until thick. Add cream cheese; blend well. Spread on crust; refrigerate 20 minutes. Top with whipped topping. Drizzle chocolate sauce over top and sprinkle with nuts.

Variation: Substitute a different flavor of pudding and garnish with chocolate "curls" instead of chocolate sauce.
Nutrition Facts
1 piece: 253 calories, 17g fat (11g saturated fat), 96mg cholesterol, 238mg sodium, 19g carbohydrate (9g sugars, 0 fiber), 5g protein.\t\t\t\t\t
