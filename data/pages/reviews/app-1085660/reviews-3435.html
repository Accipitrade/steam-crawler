\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSome of the best shooting mechanics out there, with some of the most baffling, least helpful, most hindering design and interface decisions wrapped around it.
Very convoluted UI, bad usability (though pretty decent accesibility options!)
At least the lore/fluff text is IN the game now, unlike destiny 1 where it was behind a damn app/website.
Destiny has never had good story telling. The world is fantastic and the background fluff is EXTREMELY good, as well as it consistently having amazing art direction, but the games have never been good at telling stories. Forsaken does it kinda decently, but still not the best.
Also, it is a shame that the base campaign was kinda bad, and the Osiris expansion very dull and boring. Warmind and Forsaken are very good, but getting to them is... Well, it is a chore.

Still, it plays ridiculously well, the presentation is stellar (ui and menus are ridiculously stylish, which makes them kinda hard to navigate and use to be honest). Very worth playing, but there are so many silly little things that can make it hard to recommend. Still, it is very good at what it does well.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is great. I played it for around 300+ hours on battle.net before it went on steam.
Both the PvP and PvE are really well designed, and most importantly, fun. It's great for anyone, from casual players to more "hardcore" players, with end-game activities that are genuinely hard. The raids are incredible, too.
It's hard to think of downsides, as I enjoy the entirety of the game. The PvP competitive mode meta can be annoying sometimes, I guess. Some endgame PvE stuff can be frustrating too, if players don't make any effort to understand what they have to do (might be due to the language barrier, shyness or just the pressure of having to do something for the group to go further)
Overall, I really wish for this game to get the success it deserves. Its devs made some bold moves by rejecting the awful company that is Activision and I want them to be an example for the gaming industry.

Edit: JESUS I almost forgot to talk about this: the LORE is insanely good. Everything surrounding the Destiny universe is an absolute treasure, that you can dive into for hours upon hours. This franchise offers some Lord of the Rings-grade lore. Also the music's are incredible. No kidding.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith three-thousand hours in the first game and 1200 hours in this game, I want to give whoever may be looking at playing this a fair in-depth review. To start, Crucible.
The PVP aspect of this game is chaotic. If you are looking for a hot competitive scene, go somewhere else. Tournaments are hosted every once in a while, but good look getting into any of them unless you are from renown clans like BSK or iAM. Otherwise, competitive is full of opponents stacking with their friends who milk the META and will pub stomp you unless you utilize the meta and stack with your friends as well.
With the transfer to PC, I expected an influx of players, but to my surprise, I returned from a 3-month break to the same toxicity that I left because of. Quickplay is still fine, it's casual and if I want to relax and drop 30-40 kills a game I will play QP. But if I want to be challenged I play competitively. The first match on launch I logged onto, I was greeted by a 3-stack of One-eyed mask using, bottom-tree striker pub-stomping titans, who all were unbroken title guardians. Now, THIS is matchmaking at it's finest. It goes without saying I lost.
Quickplay is fine though. In my opinion. You will meet the occasional laggy storm trance warlock or a streamer who screws the whole lobby. but for the most part, it is enjoyable. You can use mostly whatever you choose, and will still do generally well.
The gunplay is unique and unrivaled. It is smooth and there are so many weapon choices for whatever style of play you choose. Whether it be a hand cannon, a sniper rifle, and hand-cannon-sniper-rifle, auto-rifles, scout-rifles, you name it. Though keep in mind there IS a very prominent meta in this game that unless conformed to is ruthless. Especially in PVP.
The social aspect of this game can be pretty barren unless you meet people through destiny LFG. Ingame you will encounter toxic people in PVP, those random blueberries who message you in the tower, or those people looking to raid in some random PVE activity. It's a very.... unique community.
The lore for this game goes deep, look into it, you won't be disappointed. But don't EVER start an argument with lore junky, you WILL regret it. They will dunk on you with their overwhelming knowledge of ahamkara's and some bs about Darth Plagueis the wise.
The game is BEAUTIFUL. If you want content that will look good in videos and edits, then this is the game for you.
PVE is an entirely different realm from PVP. The communities are two different entities. PVE in this game is always recommended to do with a friend, you gain benefits such as revives in restricted areas, and having a friend to watch your back always helps. In general, it is a lot more fun to play with a friend rather than solo.
Raids will require you to play with a team of six and some insane coordination. But the rewards make the effort all worth it. On your grind to maximum power, these activities will be paramount.
Some additional advice:
*As a titan use one-eyed mask, bottom-tree striker, the last word hand-cannon, and mind-benders ambition shotgun.
*As warlock use dawn blade with an exotic of choice, a shotgun, and a hand cannon.
*As hunter use spectral blades with either stomp-ee5, or gwisin vest. Also a hand cannon and a shotgun.
In conclusion, give it a shot. Though it's not for everyone.\t\t\t\t\t
