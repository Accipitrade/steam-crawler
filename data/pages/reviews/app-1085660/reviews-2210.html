\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's the classic: "I have thousands of hours on this game, do not recommend". Thing is the game was fun while it lasted and Bungie cared, and I had a lot of run with the amazing gameplay and PvE content that simply does not exist in any other game like raids and dungeons.

The community in general is also great. You have people who dedicate themselves to teach raids and dungeons out of the kindness of their heart to complete strangers, no need to get into clans and have your own group of friends which for me was a first. Not that I am anti social, but as real life gets busier, it's hard to log at certain hours to do X and Y, and Destiny is perfect for this.

However Bungie has become lazy and greedy. After a lot of good will coming from a great release with Witch Queen and its subsequent seasons they ♥♥♥♥ the bed in Lightfall which was nothing more than a cash grab. The seasons became more expensive and now Bungie resorts to mobile gaming strategies to get you to buy silver (premium currency): Seasons cost 1200 silver, but for 10 dollars/euros you can only buy 1100.

The content has gotten a decrease in quality. Latest raid is easy as it gets, where most of the team just deals with adds. PvP is abandoned, they say they do not have the resources to make new stuff. Bungie is a AAA studio backed by Sony. To say that they do not have the resource is baffling. They got bought by billions of dollars. It's more like the resources were allocated elsewhere and Bungie just doesn't give a crap about this game, even though it's the only thing keeping them afloat.

The sunsetting of content, meaning their complete removal from game, has hurt the new player experience immensively. As a result the story is super confusing when you start and the paywall is huge because Bungie does not bundle their content properly as new expansions come out. I fail to see how this is sustainable on the long term to keep new players coming in.

Bungie claimed at the GDC that their aim is to deliver content fast, regardless of quality, because they found out that this works and sells anyway. This just reeks of complacency and provides sense to what this game has become.

All and all I am surprised I even tried this game. With 2 terrible launches with Destiny 1 and 2, Destiny had a bad rep in my book for years. When they split from Activision and went f2p I decided to try and fell in love. But like most Destiny players I leave dissapointed how Bungie continues to fail to bring this game to its true potential.\t\t\t\t\t
