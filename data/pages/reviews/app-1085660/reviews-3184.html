\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've got a lot of hours in Destiny 2 between Battle.net and Steam and will be continuing to put even more in as new "Seasons" come out.

Let me start by saying that the gunplay is the best in the business - what I mean by that is the satisfaction you get from slaying enemies throughout the solar system...Especially in regards to precision shots. Nothing beats the sound a Fallen makes when you pop them in the head and the ether vacates their body. If you're looking for a good PVE shooter, look no further.

As for the Crucible (PVP), it's done a much better job of being a space shooter than Call of Duty could ever aspire to be. Sure, there are balance issues between the classes and subclasses, as well as the weapon archetypes but the meta gets shuffled every season. Bungie does a decent enough job at listening to player feedback in regards to what feels terrible and what performs too well.

Endgame PVE is different than what it used to be in the original Destiny. While Nightfalls and Dungeons (3-man endgame) are fun and engaging but not too mechanically complex, Raids (6-man) are the opposite. I loved the raids in Destiny 1 but the raids here are far too complex to be entertaining unless your idea of entertainment is completing hard content and not being able to screw around with a larger group of people while clapping alien cheeks. The raids suffer from the same direction that WoW's raiding takes as the devs continually have to cook up more challenging content to satisfy the playerbase (although they do a much better job, creativity-wise, as opposed to Blizzard).

There is not really a cohesive story. Other than what hits you directly in the face through missions (which isn't a lot and not very clear), you have to use third-party resources to really understand the lore/story. So if a space RPG with good story is what you're looking for, stick to games like Mass Effect.

tl;dr: 8/10\t\t\t\t\t
