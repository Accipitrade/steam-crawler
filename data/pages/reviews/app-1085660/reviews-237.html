\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh boy, there is a LOT that can be said about this game.

The major con at this point is the new player experience, due to the size of the game there is a lot of content from the first 2 years that is no longer included, and the story seems a little disjointed to start as a result. Additionally, there are some wildly deep systems that without having had the slow rollout of that content over the last 5 years it is like drinking from the firehose to figure out. Even with someone fairly helpful and knowledgeable, it's frankly just A LOT to deal with.

The pros are, well, the entire game. The PvP community does have some fair complaints as far as what it has been and what it is now, the amount of content they get each year and the various issues of it is less than great, but otherwise the gunplay, the variety, the story, the VERY PRETTY worlds, some pretty major swaths of the community are quite great. And let me reiterate how utterly good the story is, just wild depth, fantastic long tail experience if you are willing to dig into the lore.

If you have a friend who has been urging you to get into this and is willing to help teach you Armor 3.0, how to get exotics, and help you through some higher level content until you get on your feet, there is a phenomenally good experience. Also, next content year (Lightfall) they are looking at improved teaching experience for really how to get new players up to date, so if you're on the fence watch how people react to this around March 2023\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAs someone who has dedicated over 1k+ hours to this game, I can safely say that this game IS NOT worth it for new players. The moment you start up, you are introduced to a tutorial that bombards you with like 20 different things at once. Hell, once you start up, you get a brief introduction to events that happened in the first game, as well as what happened for the first 2-3 years of this game. Speaking of the content, you need to have been there at the start. about 60% of this game or over has been removed: weapons from that time are no longer used in the endgame as they're weak to the current meta; expansions and seasonal story lines + activities gone; characters that are dead are still used in select "legacy" missions as dialogue. Destiny lives up to its name as a single evolving world with so many interconnected story lines. I love the lore of this game, but if you don't know what's happening, merely reading the lore won't help unless you play from the beginning. This game is primarily, if not only, catered to the veterans who have given $100+. That doesn't seem welcoming for new players. I love and hate this game, but It is Hell for new players. I wouldn't recommend it to them. The only thing I can say is that if you have a PS4 or Xbox One, buy and play the first game, that'll help you understand the story a little bit, but Destiny 2 moves faster. Great game, but I will subject new players to it, just not worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
