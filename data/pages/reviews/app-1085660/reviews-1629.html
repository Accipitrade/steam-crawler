\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a very fun game to play. There are many different aspects to the game that are fun to engage in and play. That being said it is very hard to feel like you are getting a lot out of the game, most of the end-game grinding comes down to getting either such specific gun rolls that you wouldn't even notice or getting a perfect stat amount on a set of armor. The game starts to feel slow after a certain amount of time, you will start to notice the game taking the same pattern after an elongated amount of time. Although I will say when you are starting out getting used to the tower, planets, and different gamemodes it has to offer, the game is very enjoyable. I would recommend this game to people who enjoy grinding out long and difficult content. There isn't much in terms of "loot" towards the endgame mostly just completion bragging. This game is best played with a group of friends where you don't have to focus so much on preforming just enjoying yourself. Although I will say this game requires you to log in and have at least an hour of playtime a day to keep up with the new content coming out. If you can't handle that then Destiny 2 is not for you. 6.5/10\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I recommend this game, I cannot recommend it to someone who is expecting a lot from something that seems so large. While at first overwhelming it becomes very apparent that the game is a mile wide but one inch deep. You find yourself doing repetitive objectives and trying to figure out how to access the story. As far as the solo experience goes it can be enjoyed by many but for anyone who wants to do anything worth while it requires a group or friends to be able to achieve anything truly worth while. There is a lot to do but little do that seems engaging. The game devs like to say "blame me" for x problem or y issue that wasn't addressed and hurt the game but they never actually own up or say it is going to be fixed.

The positives for sure are that if you have a group of friends willing to play the game you will have quite the enjoyable experience. You will have something to do every time you get on. You will feel a lot of accomplishment. This is all at the cost of securing a group of 5 other people so the game for sure encourages friendship. The gun gameplay is some of the most fun I have had in any game, and the pool of guns to choose from, despite there being a meta, is actually refreshing.

Come into the game expecting a shooter, but nothing more and form your own experience.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll try to keep this review short.

I'm a veteran of Destiny. I've been playing since year one of the first game, and only fell off of playing it around year two of the second game. I recently came back to it, and so much has changed.

The battle-pass system they have in place is satisfying to complete, and I think that it is very generous (even on the free side). The end game is one of the best I've experienced in a game, and it's honestly what has kept me playing. Bungie has done a good job realizing this and helping to speed along newer players to this point.

The gunplay feels extremely good, and it's so much fun testing out new weapons to see which ones feel good to you. I wish the armor customization was a little bit better, but they have an interesting upgrade system in place that makes it worthwhile.

While I can understand this game may not be for everyone, I would recommend at least giving it a chance up until the endgame. Most people get bored and fall of in the early- to mid-game portion, which is admittedly the dullest part of the game. Just hang in there until you can actually experience everything, and then give it your judgement.

All-in-all, I have been greatly enjoying my return to Destiny 2, and look forward to seeing how the story and gameplay continues to develop.\t\t\t\t\t
