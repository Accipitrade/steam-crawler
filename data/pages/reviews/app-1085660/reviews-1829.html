\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter recently passing the 1000 hour mark I decided to finally update my review for this game this probably wont matter much since nobody will see this but I want to update it anyways. Destiny 2 is one of my favorite games, I can complain about certain systems all I want but the fact remains that I find this game fun even with its numerous flaws.

Things I really like about the game:
Buildcrafting: recently Destiny 2 has become more focused on making builds which I like, not much else to say about this it just fun to make unique builds
Raids and Dungeons: Playing Raids and Dungeons casually with friends is some of the most fun I've ever had in videogames and I hope Bungie continues to make these Raids and Dungeons with the focus of it being both challenging and fun (it is a game after all)
The lore: When I first started playing and for a long time after that I didn't really care about the story very much but now I actually read the lore tabs for new weapons and such. I don't know what to say about this I just find the story pretty interesting.
The Gameplay: A game is nothing without gameplay and Destiny 2's gameplay is what keeps me playing. Because of buildcrafting becoming more and more relevant the gameplay has changed up even more and gotten more interesting, the movement, guns, and abilities feel unique from other games and its whats kept me playing for so long.

Things I really don't like:
inconveniences: There are a lot of quality of life features missing from the game for no real reason, for instance you cant save loadouts in game but you can through a third party app and the developers are completely fine with you doing this through an app so why can't we do it in game? We're also unable to access our vault in game at anytime and have to go to the tower to access it but with the same third party apps were able to access it from anywhere, so why isn't there just a tab next to our inventory for our vault?

Weekly resets:
One of the things that annoys me the most about this game is when I do a raid on all 3 characters and then I dont get to have anymore loot until next week. The only reason this is a feature is so that more players will log on weekly, Bungie doesn't like it when you take a break from their game, this system may seem like something that will make players take more breaks but in the end it keeps them playing for longer. If you complete a raid 6 times in a week then you will receive much less loot than someone who completed a raid 3 times in one week and 3 times the next week. You both still put in the same amount of effort and completed the raid the same amount of times but because you didn't wait an entire week you get less rewards. Why are players ok with this I dont get it Its one of problems I dont see many people talk about why have we just accepted that we have to have a weekly reset for rewards. I want to be able to play a raid as many times as I want in a week without feeling like Im wasting my time. Why am I not allowed to progress without waiting an entire week?

The monetization:
This game is heavily monetized which makes me feel gross whenever I do buy something. This becomes apparent when you see at the launch of a new season how many items they add to the cosmetic shop. And then you realize that theirs barely any cosmetic rewards for getting triumphs completed. I feel like that's something that would make the triumph system much more interesting. The main rewards we get from it are these pointless triumph points and then a ghost shell and then of course the titles but I want some cool looking armor that shows I completed a really hard challenge the closest thing we have to that are titles and emblems. Its just unbelievable how many cosmetics the game has and how little you can actually earn.

The Destiny Content Vault:
After the Beyond light expansion was released Bungie introduced something called the Destiny Content Vault (DCV for short). After awhile some old content would be put into the DCV and become unplayable. Its sad to see things leave from the game but the point of this system is to reduce the size of the game and make it easier for the programming devs to work on the game. But I still find it unfair that they would just take this stuff away, this stuff I paid for. It makes the dev's work easier sure but its still content they made money from I feel like they could afford to spend more effort on the game because they owe it to the players who spent money on those things. I'm not a programmer I don't know how much harder it would be to program the game if the DCV wasn't a thing but it still feels unfair.

The New player experience:
The New player experience is just awful, it teaches you some basic things and then youre left wondering what you do from there. Youre sent into the middle of this story that where youre just supposed to somehow know whats going, I don't really have much to say other than that because of this I don't feel like I would be able to ask my friends to try this game out because I know this would turn them away.

At the end of the day Destiny 2 is a game I love but it has a lot of problems that exist from Bungie's. greed I'm sure that a lot of devs at Bungie care about this game too but Bungie itself is way too greedy.\t\t\t\t\t
