\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI'll say it up front- this game can be annoying.

The grind on Destiny 2 is long and arduous. The game can break randomly for no reason because one gun has a mind of its own. (Looking at you, Telesto.) It's expensive- the release cycle is pay-gated in a way that is frustrating, so you definitely do have to think about it as though it's an MMO that you're paying a monthly membership fee for. The content vault sucks. PVP netcode could be improved upon quite a bit. There are things- I'm not saying there aren't things.

HOWEVER.

I have 700 hours in this game at review time, and that's just on steam. My gameplay numbers on other platforms are probably just as high. You don't play a game for 700 hours unless there's something that keeps you coming back. Destiny 2, for all its frustrations and annoying quirks, might be my favorite game, and there's a very compelling reason why- it just feels good. Really good. It's just so damn fun I don't even really care that it's sort of annoying sometimes.

The MMO aspects of the game are cool, and I love how it encourages you to play with others in a variety of settings, but the way your character moves, the way abilities feel, and the way guns operate is what got me, right from the moment I booted up Destiny 1 for the first time on Xbox. Pop your hammer of sol or arc staff for the first time and tell me you don't crack a smile. I dare you. Blast a very unfortunate Acolyte into pure energy with a fusion rifle and tell me you don't cackle a little bit. I never feel more powerful in a video game than I do in Destiny, except maybe when I'm carving bandits to ribbons as Geralt of Rivia.

People forget that Destiny 2 is, at its heart, an action FPS. In being an exciting, interesting, and genuinely fun shooter experience, it succeeds with extreme conviction. It is a vessel by which you can explore a fantasy of being a super-powered immortal battlemage in space. Grab the free content, treat it like a demo, and I think you'll see what I mean.\t\t\t\t\t
