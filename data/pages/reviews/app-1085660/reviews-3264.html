\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt’s a good/fun game that's having an identity crisis of what to become.

Pro
  1. Visuals - great atmosphere, audio, and level design (Raids, Dungeons, Strike/Nightfall, Secret Missions)
  2. Gunplay - loadouts, feel, aim-assist, bullet magnetism, impact of weapon vs enemy. *However slowing becoming a Con i.e. Bows, hand-cannons, flinches, recoil, damage falloff, not registering, less dmg, etc.

Cons
  1. Repeatable enemies/content - reused weapons and gear from vanilla Destiny 2, and even from D1 (maps, weapons, locations), with little incentive (bad RNG, no worthy materials, etc)
  2. RNG - there is so much Random gear/weapons in this game it feels very defeating. 80% of what you get is literal trash due to bad rolls, bad stats on gear, low stat gear, etc.
  3. Forced Sunsetting - gear and weapons from older seasons/DLCs which inherently affect Theorycrafting, multi-builds, masterworking.
  4. a. Undecisive - Bungie sunsetting older gear like Scourge of the Past and then creating an event where the gear has Season Stamp i.e. new expiration timestamp. Which begs the question of why are we sunsetting?
  5. b. DCV (Content Vault) - supposedly the vaulting of content is due to massive game and keep patching with irrelevant and un-played content, even thou they just showed us that old raids can be made relevant by making the gear have a new time stamp. If we vault "old" content and bring out "older" content like VoG is that a free service? Do I have to re-pay for already payed for content like Leviathan in a future DLC or Season Pass?
  6. PvP - just anything in Destiny related to PvP is a pretty bad experience from majority of players on PC. You may encounter hackers, net limiters, aimbotters, recovs, and overpowered weapons (shotguns, snipers), and just general destiny issues/crashes.
  7. Pay-to-Play/Win - a lot of, if not all, of the content is put behind DLC which is then put behind Season Pass. Basically if you do not own DLC/Season Pass you do not get to experience all activities, story missions, exotics, or unique styles (Weapon Ornaments, Gear Ornaments, Emotes) and even more unique-ness from Eververse. In most cases your best stat gear comes from Season Pass (65+ base) in the favor of the character you pulled from i.e. High Recovery for Warlocks which almost makes running any high-end content likes Raids or Dungeons meaningless.
  8. Flavor-of-the-Month - It seems like Bungie has this intentional design to keep "changing the meta" where in one season a Sniper can be overpowered and in the next its Assault Rifles or Side-Arms. So maybe spending weeks or even months getting a weapon that’s considered god-tier could be utter garbage in the next season. Remember Izanagi in beginning of Shadowkeep being practically mandatory for endgame Raids and where is it now in Season of Arrivals?
  9. MMO - What exactly makes this game an mmo? Is it because its online? Feels like Bungie found an MMO checklist and just made sure they met the bare minimal of that list. Online players - ☑ dungeon/raid - ☑ , gear - ☑ , weapons - ☑ , store - ☑ , npc vendors - ☑ , side-quests - ☑

It's one of those games thats hard to turn away but at the same time you start to question; "what are you doing?" "why am i playing?"\t\t\t\t\t
