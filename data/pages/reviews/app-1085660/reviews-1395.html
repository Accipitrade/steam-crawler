Want to spend too much time playing a game you don't understand why you love it so much? Me too! Destiny 2 has some oddly gravitating quality to it that just keeps me coming back. Yes the "content" gets stale at points. Yes the game can get grindy sometimes. Yes I am not touching Trials again for a hot minute, but the payoff when the game hits is second to none. The feeling of playing destiny is something I have never before experienced in an FPS shooter, and the thoughtfulness put into the game is blindingly obvious.

The destiny community is intensely dedicated, and as annoying as the constant complaining can feel sometimes, I have never experienced a better relationship between a die-hard community and the devs of a game. In my experience, Bungie handles community criticism very quickly and is not afraid to admit when they made a mistake. They are also not afraid to push the game in directions that in the long-term will benefit the game, even if they do receive complaints in the moment.

Overall, my experience with Destiny has been incredible. I cannot directly pinpoint a part of the game I enjoy so much, and this is more of a testament to the quality of the game as a whole rather than the game feeling lackluster at points. Have patience, enjoy yourself in the moment and Destiny 2's experience will pay you back 10 fold.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAll in all, I think this is quite a good game. Warframe meets Doom meets Borderlands. Kind of. It plays well, it looks nice, it's got a lot of good voice acting and a surprising amount of lore that I haven't really gotten around to reading yet. Three classes, each with three subclasses, and each subclass sporting several "modes" to play them in. Plenty of different weapons and armor, and most of them drop with rolled stats or perks, so you can spend some time anticipating that perfect roll.

I would not recommend playing this game by yourself. For one, it's a pretty steep learning curve. I was lucky enough to have seasoned friends who could show me the ropes, but I think just having someone with whom you could talk things through would help a lot. Also, I find the gameplay itself a lot more rewarding when you're properly coordinating with a friend or two.

The game itself is free, but they're not at all shy about suggesting you pay up for the DLCs. Quest lines etc will be visibly unavailable, and there's a few other mechanics that implicitly sneer at you for being such a pleb. This is most noticeable at the start, and very much dies off after a bit, so either the game gets the message or you simply stop noticing its . I did end up buying the DLCs, for whatever that's worth. 100+ hours of entertainment would be a lot more expensive at the cinema.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's difficult to review a constantly evolving service game such as this, but I'm trying to anyways because, if my 250 hours logged at the time of this review's publishing doesn't give it away, Destiny is my all-time favorite game.

Destiny, which is the blanket term that I'm using to refer to my experiences in both the first and second titles in the franchise, is a complete experience. It frames a silky smooth shooter experience within the context of a larger-than-life cosmic storyline, told in various media from cinematic cutscenes to readable lore entries. The environments are beautiful, the progression system is unique, and the collectible loot is unforgettable. Part of what makes the game special is its accommodation of varying play group sizes. Want to play by yourself? There's plenty of story missions and gear pursuits that are totally achievable solo. Want to play with two to three people? There's Crucible, Gambit, and Dungeons, depending on your group's preference. Have an even larger group? Six-player Raids have been some of the most fun I've ever had in a videogame. To make things even better, Destiny 2 is a free-to-play experience, with additional upgrades available depending on desired investment.

This isn't to say that there aren't things to criticize. Destiny has gone through significant growing pains to become a rewarding experience, and it will likely continue to evolve until the servers are shut down. In its current form, Trials of Osiris is an experience broken by rampant cheating, the game's menus are weighed down by defunct progression systems, and its new player experience is marred by a lack of onboarding. I've been playing Destiny since launch, and the game's capacity to create problems hasn't stopped. However, Bungie hasn't stopped working to improve the experience, either. Despite the criticism they constantly receive online and elsewhere, the developers at Bungie manage to consistently deliver enjoyable experiences and quality of life improvements to the game.

For me, Destiny has literally been a game of all seasons, and besides being one of the more polished game experiences in recent memory, it has provided me with friendships and memories that I can't quantify. I highly recommend this title.\t\t\t\t\t
