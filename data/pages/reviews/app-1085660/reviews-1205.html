\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPurchased at launch was Destiny 1 all over again so I shelved it.

Came back for Forsaken (DLC) and thought the game was improving but that was short lived. Shelved once more.

Came back again a little before Beyond Light (DLC), and played a lot of new interesting content that had slowly came out in my absence. I'm talking guns, gear, actual story narration that didn't feel like an after thought, and fun repeatable activities. Then Bungie Announced that it would all soon be removed with Beyond Lights launch (Content vaulting).

Beyond Light came and the giant hole that was made from removing the previously mentioned content was NOT filled in the slightest. Handful of new things, many bugs (is to be expected but they handle these things poorly), plenty of time gated content, lots of obsolete "legacy" content, and a grind that wears the player down. The story is there but feels a lot like "hand holding" with your ghost (player companion) telling you things as if you are new to the universe at all times.

In many ways the game feels like a fan project rather than a AAA title.

Edit 1:

Four Seasons into Beyond Light and the game feels like a full experience again, albeit still less to do than before Beyond Light.

Its been a long time coming (~1 year), and I know whats coming next. The Witch Queen (DLC) will launch and all the activities added over the seasons will be removed along with some older content and the game will have a slow drip feed of content for the next year again.

Its not that I can't recommend the game, but it has a cycle:

Low content > High content > Low again.\t\t\t\t\t
