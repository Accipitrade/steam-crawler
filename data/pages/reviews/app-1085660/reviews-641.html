I don't have much to say but that Bungie is a great dev who cares for their community! And if you're looking for a unique MMO, love FPS, and some creators who actually care for their consumers, go ahead and try Destiny 2 out and invite some friends while you are at it! Try something new, don't ever look down on any game like I do.... a lot. You never know that later down the line, you'll regret not trying it out sooner and will enjoy it! :D

===ALL THIS TEXT HERE===

So, fun story about this all. A long time ago, I was provided a copy of the game by Blizzard, back when it use to be on there before it became free and moved onto Steam. At the time, I disregarded the game because I never really played FPS. As someone who played and spend about a year each on, World of Warcraft, Maplestory 2, Riders of Icarus, and a few months on Tera, being shown a "FPS Shooter MMO" was quite bizzare.

Many years later once Maplestory 2 closed down... Here I am playing Destiny 2, after I ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ at the game for not being my style of gameplay while playing on a Minecraft server 2 years prior with some friends who played Destiny 2. LOL

===The Developers===

First off, let's talk about the developers. Bungie is a great developer, while I have never played any of Bungie's Halo games up until after I got into Destiny 2 (since Halo Masterchief Collection was gifted to me), seeing a group of devs work so hard to try to make sure that content is readily available to all and in working order, is amazing.
- When there is a game-breaking exploit (Telesto), they look quickly to disable it to make sure it doesn't ruin the game.
- When there was a game-breaking exploit involving 12-manning activities, now the fact that they let us enjoy that for a few weeks before Grandmasters, now that's pretty amazing.
- FOMO, now many games always make you think about this, you are fearing that you'll miss something. While, yes you do miss out on things involving the DCV (Destiny Content Vault), you still are provided time to get things done before it's vaulted. Plus they let you know months in advance. Worried about not finishing a season? Don't worry, there's time up until the next DLC releases! Always worried playing one character means you're missing out on Bright Dust? Ok, Bungie decided to place in season challenges, so you aren't force to be overwhelmed on playing classes you absolutely hate.
- They try their best to balance out the meta in PVP and PVE. Now, I don't play PVP a lot since I played War Thunder for years...and with that, grown salty at players, but I know they are always trying to balance out weapons to get certain metas to calm down so many guns are usable. While yes, some changes hurt, but in the end, that revolves around your own play style.... At the same time, I'll still hate PVP, and Bungie will never change my mind. XD As for PVE, I like the idea of how the meta of each season changes. An example would be Anarchy. Anarchy use to be top tier DPS for many bosses, it got a nerf because well... other guns kinda can't compete with it in the heavy slot. Upsetting, but very understandable of why a change was needed. There's got to be other ways then just using a single weapon combo to do everything in the game after all. Plus the grenade launcher is still usable. I did talk about seasonal changes, and with that I also refer to your artifact mods for the season. One season could be a Fusion Rifle meta, another season would be grenade launchers. Then another, swords. There's never a bore ending to it all.
- Size of the game, Bungie is one of the only developers I know who actually cares to not be like CoD and become a 100+ GB game. They understand us people have limited space, and have other games to play and work to do. Sucks we have to deal with the DCV, but what can you do. Maybe they got plans to bring some of it back in a rotation one day!
- Time consumption is something Bungie also cares about. They understand once more, we got jobs, lives, we got other things we want to do. Bungie isn't out there being like, "hey play my game for hours!" instead they want everyone to have an easy life, not binging and being an addict. The biggest change bungie did was the level cap that use to raise 30 per season. Because they saw how time consuming and stressful it was to gear a character 30 levels, they changed it to 10. So much less stress! Much more time to just enjoy other matters!

===Content===

Content can sure get repetitive at times, but you can't blame them. Everything is repetitive depending on how much you do it, and how you do it. Although, Bungie at least gives us a few options to work with, so that gameplay is not toooo boring.
- Dungeons exists, and can be done solo or solo flawless. You are rewarded for pushing your limits to try to do something on your own. While the only flawless I've personally done was Harbinger, it's super fun to just challenge yourself and your abilities to try to beat something that's a 3-man activity. Plus, sometimes the loot is worth it, depends if you need the weapons, or looking to get a better set of weaponry or armory!
- Raids are pretty fun with a group if 6 people, assuming you want to LFG or just hang out with friends. While yes, Raids are more difficult and you really need to work together, that's what I find enjoyable. Raiding on Maplestory 2 and World of Warcraft, neither game provided that idea of that "everyone plays a role" and that you just can't carry some newb through it all. Everyone's hard at work doing something and each encounter, you are rewarded with some pretty cool loot.
- Playlist Activities, strikes, crucible, and gambit. While this game offers PVE, PVP, and PVEVP, it provides everyone something that you'd like to enjoy. You wanna focus more on PVE? Sure, you want PVP? Sure! I mean I know I didn't mention Trials or Survival... but again I'm no PVP player. There's so many activities you can do to just do with your friends or on your own. Sure it gets boring, but I mean, you get rewarded upgrades, weapon rolls and bright dust for your hard work! Go on, sweat in PVE or PVP, or both at the same time. But I must admit, we need Gambit Prime back to make PVEVP fun again...aaaaaa.

===MISC. Comments===

Bright Dust and Eververse Engrams are crack. No game have I known provides so much ways to just get anything for free. While yes, off-season armor ornaments and finishers, along with a few other packages are locked behind silver, which is the real world currency stuff... do you just want to spend 100000000000000 bright dust that you earn to just get everything for free? Saves quite a lot of money, not gonna lie. Honestly one of my favorite things to do every season is to spy on what's available on the bright dust store on Eververse Weekly. Then every Tuesday reset, I'm excited to still see what's available, even if I already know most of the items! During every season, the released armor of that season in the silver store would be available for bright dust, meaning, you save 15 bucks and much more! You just got to buy all the ornaments with bright dust throughout the season! Eververse Engrams are rewarded as you level through the season pass, even after level 100. The fact you can get weapon ornaments, emotes, exotic armor ornaments, ghost shells, ships, sparrows, projects, and whatever else they provide (and rarely some bright dust too!), is so worth spending the time and effort to gamble away what you'd get. Yeah, sure you get a bunch of purples repeated ghost shells, but something about spending time with your friends to gamble on what item may come out of those engrams, makes the time worth wild, even if it's super disappointing.

Overall money. You also aren't asked to spend a load of money. Yes you have a hefty amount of DLC cost and then you have the seasons as well but in reality, if you play frequently, the free bright dust and engrams that I stated earlier, it'll be worth it.
