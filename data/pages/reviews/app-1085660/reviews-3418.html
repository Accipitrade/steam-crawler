\r\n\t\t\t\t\t- This game is all about growth your own character, you fight alien, loot stuff, upgrade, modding and equip stuff so that you can fight stronger alien to loot better stuff and repeat. Raid is final challenge which test your skill and your character power to extreme, it also drop best gears as reward.

- This is looter shooter game, it all about the loot so expected alot of grind and loot if you have played Diablo you can know the feel that after week/month grinding the loot you want has dropped, the loot game is all about that feeling, that moment that all hard working you put in is rewarded in the end.

- There is is a RPG feel where you can build toward a certain playstyle or your own via gears and mod system, you want build a glass nuker Warlord you can build for it, you want to be a heavy armor Titan you can build for it, you want to be a Hunter archer, just build for it.

- Gunplay in this game is very good as expected from Bungie, the impact and recoil of the gun feel so right, it feel very good to shoot thing in this game there is lot of gun and each one have unique feel to them. The movement is not fast Warframe neither slow, the amount is just right enough for a sci-fi shooter game (trust me those guys know how to nailed this)

- The story for me is okay, not good nor bad, it's enough to get you to introducing you about the world, but Destiny is not just about story it's the lore. The lore is rich and depth even i haven't explore it yet but seeing a 4 hours video about the story of the world i known this would be a great one for me.

- The artstyle beautifully, i don't know how to put it, just play it yourself you may enjoy it.

- The world building is great. You can feel the different unique alien cultures on each Planet and history behind them.

- Music is fitting the world and sound great.

- There won't be much hand holding stuff, the game drop you in at power 750 that viable to do almost every content in the game and let you do what ever you wanna do that it. There is some soft guide to tell you where to go what to do by introduction quest but it can easily be missed. I've seen lot of negative review cause the game is confusing and not clear. I think that actually the way that devs want you to play the game, you been thrown into a big strange mysterious world to exploring it not guiding through it, if you don't know something just look it up on google or do your own test-trial to find out what is it or just simply do another thing, sometimes you may end up knowing what is it later, you will eventually learning stuff as you play.

To clarify i'm actually a new Destiny players and a fan of MMORPG and looter game also like to researching, learning stuff so i'm enjoying the game and actually did some research before and during playing the game and i enjoy doing it, for me it's more fun when you learning, overcome challenge, solve puzzle by yourself. If someone hand holding you every step you take then you're just followed them not

I'm not say that hand holding, linear game is bad, it's just another experience, different approach what i'm trying to say is Destiny is the kind of game that give you sense of exploration, role-playing in a sci-fi fantasy world with deep lore building in the background, the game rewarding as how hard you willing to work, it's kind of game want you to take seriously not just 10 hours of entertainment.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI find Destiny 2 a hard game to rate. I'm giving it a thumbs up because it gets most of the important aspects of a FPS looter shooter correct. It does have some flaws but the core of the game is rock solid. The visuals, sound and shooting/combat is all very polished as you'd expect from Bungie. It's currently the best game of its class out there (only other one is Anthem or maybe Borderlands 3). The games biggest problem is the games lack of information. I've never played Destiny or Destiny 2 while it was owned by Activision so I had no idea what was going on with the story. The new Destiny 2 seems to be geared for veterans who are already aware of all that stuff. Worse still is that there is no info on any of the game systems and almost no tutorial. If you want to play this game be prepared to go out and research all this stuff by yourself. It took me two hours to figure out how to get the story mode and how to start it. When you start right away the game just directs you to go directly into the first open area and start shooting stuff. I've been playing for over 12 hours and I'm still confused on certain systems.

TLDR: A solid FPS looter shooter with really crappy documentation. If your new to the series prepare to be confused.\t\t\t\t\t

· Overall Thoughts ·
The game is a blast to play, I've clocked in thousands of hours on the original Destiny game, it certainly continues it's light with the second iteration and new content available to us players. The graphics, music, lore, story and game-play still hold up to todays standards, I always commend them on their level design and attention to detail while playing. Despite having only played The New Light for 20 hours, I've sort of been a veteran player, having participated in the beta testing, pre-release and playing the campaign from The Red War to Curse of Osiris to Warmind, having stopped after this point due to work. I've can safely say this game is worth the time and even the money if you enjoy playing these three campaigns, support the developers, they've done an outstanding job and making it free to play for everyone.

Although I did give this game a positive review, don't get too happy, there's flaws in the game, like every other game, it doesn't necessarily mean it's a bad game, because it's far from that. However, they need to understand that not everyone knows how to play, what to do and all that. I used to be a veteran player, but I know next to nothing about the game since it's pretty much new to me now. They did an awful job at getting me started, like why don't give us an option to view the tutorial, explain information and quests better, half the objectives are impossible to understand since I have no clue what it's about. Having a skip option is fine for those who have completed Forsaken but for those who have played and forgotten how to play, should be given the option to redo a tutorial or a portion of the campaign to reboot your memory. I'm glad I had a friend show me around and explain the lore, what to do, how to do quests and play through or I would have genuinely left the game because it was far too overwhelming for me to get started. It just automatically assumed I knew everything, which I didn't.

If you're a player returning and you haven't kept up to date, you will most certainly have trouble, watch some videos or even ask a friend to help you out because you will have an awful experience for the first 5 - 10 hours, other than that, Destiny 2 is fantastic, I hope it continues to do well in the future!\t\t\t\t\t
