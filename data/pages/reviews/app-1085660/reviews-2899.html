\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI never played this game before the split from Activision, but I can say this game is amazing now. You can see where the split happened by the creative jump in content.
Overall the classes are well balanced between each other, and the sub classes are fairly well balanced but with a noticeable strength towards the DLC subclasses. HOWEVER Bungie is constantly tweaking the subclasses and is at the time of writing this is about to release a tweak for all solar (Fire) subclasses.
The economy is well balanced as well with the most common currency, glimmer, being used as a way to balance weapon and gear upgrades through associated costs.
I will say that it can be quite overwhelming to start off because you have access to everything right off the bat meaning your quest tracker fills very fast (Not that there is a limit to the number of quests you can hold).
But this does tie into what progression there is.
The only real progression is gaining access to more and more difficult raids to gain more light level to gain access to more difficult raids etc...
I will say that the exotics are very interesting and fun to use, you can only equip one piece of exotic armor and one exotic weapon at a time however.
One large downside is there is no matchmaking at all for the top tier raids, which makes sense, you don't want a troll griefing your amazing run for the lulz but it does mean that solo players are required to either find a clan or continually get groups together in the tower (Main social hub).
PVP is fun, I am not usually a PVP player but I do enjoy it in this game and I never feel railroaded into playing it.
There are MTXs but aside from the very expansive campaign DLCs they are limited to cosmetics only and the majority of those can be aquired through normal gameplay.

Overall a great game, but for me as a solo player the endgame gets really wearing because you need premade groups to play the endgame raids.\t\t\t\t\t
