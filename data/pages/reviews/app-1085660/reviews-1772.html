CONS:
Bungie was too ambitious and chose to go for an unhealthy business model, that is to say, MMOFPS (at least to a certain extent, if not a full-fledged MMO). Not surprisingly, all of the major cons come from the game being the genre itself.

Even before getting to play Destiny 2's end contents, playing this game soon feels like an endless chore than enjoyment, and I increasingly feel being exploited just like a horse chasing a carrot on a stick. My sense of accomplishment after gaining some rewards is soon robbed away with their next contents riddled with full of FOMO tactics and yet another tightly stacked time-limited events, quests and bounties. And recently such sentiments have been exacerbated with their announcement of "vaulting" the content, that is, removal of old contents to make a room for new ones and nobody knows if they will ever come back and for how long they will remain vaulted. Even if they come back, they will be modified to a certain extent, and thus it is safe to assume that the same experience is completely gone. As a casual New Light player, I still haven't finished playing the contents that are soon to be removed. My unfinished progressions will be removed as well and thus I feel forced to finish them ASAP.

It is easy for someone to just come and say something along the lines of; "if you don't like, don't play". However, gamers are easily addicted to games and they can't easily quit them especially when they already have invested so much time and money. That is why this particular game greatly utilizes (exploits) gamers who commit sunk cost fallacy. Some people may still hate the game deep inside or at least to some extent but only pretend to really like everything about the game in order to justify their investments. Some others may indeed like every aspect of the game until today, but I highly doubt their existence. Here is one thing I would like to tell you: There are many gamers who experience a love-hate relationship with games they play and such a relationship is the very manifestation of human psychology I just stated above.
