\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 has been one of the most interesting games I've have played in the last decade that somehow always makes me play all the time despite the array of problems that has happened in that same time frame. As a shooter, it is top notch. The gun-play is phenomenal, some of the best that only few shooters can say rival it. With new weapons introduced every season, you'll definitely find want your looking for and even farm for the same weapons, just to get a better roll to suit your builds. The classes and subclasses give you such a range of options to make your build, along with your gear, mods and exotic gear and weapons, you can make whatever build you want to suit your playstyle. One negative however, is the access to older weapons and gear that are either, not obtainable or sunsetted where it is useless in the most up to date content. In terms of PVE, i cannot go wrong with Destiny 2. From going on patrol, doing strikes, to doing raids and even some cool secret missions, there is alot to do in this game. The one issue that kind of kills it sometimes is the fact that, as of Beyond Light, a lot of strikes and raids were "Vaulted" meaning if you are just starting out now, you missed out in a lot of content in the past three years, no to mention the original campaign is also gone. As for PVP, this is where Destiny 2 falls short hard. The PVP meta changes drastically every season, which to say at this point if you played long enough, it is the norm. The biggest issue I have personally is, just like the Vaulted Strikes and Raids, a ton of PVP maps were also vaulted, so variety is lacking. Another issue is the lack of modes for PVP, that does not take advantage of the different ways players use their classes and weapons that make it stand out from other shooters that do not have these luxuries. You have standard deathmatch, team deathmatch, control (domination), Elimination and some variations of these same modes that barely change the rules. I would love to see capture the flag (Rift is Destiny 1), Assault, where you capture control points on a map in a consecutive order acting like a tug of war mode and even Attack and Defend type modes. Not to mention, in the first destiny, it had sparrow racing, Sparrows being your main vehicle in Destiny, where there was not shooting at all, just racing, which many in the destiny community miss as it was something very different. Overall, PVP is okay, but it could be a lot better and much more balanced if Bungie gives it more focus. Overall, Destiny 2 has been one of the most divisive games for people to either hate or love. Me personally, I love it as it was a game that saved me from a deep depression long ago, right from the first beta on PS4. Thankfully, this game has cross-save where your progress is shared between PC and Console with crossplay coming soon. That alone makes the game very accessible too a lot of people. If you are an old school guardian when this all started, it has been one hell of a ride that I will continue to support despite some of the problems that could of killed this game a long time ago. If your new, don't worry about missing out on a lot of the stuff that is gone for now. Hopefully Bungie will bring back all the old content for people to enjoy, but have fun with it and at least try every single mode and activity that the game has to offer and enjoy this ever-changing world that Bungie has built.\t\t\t\t\t
