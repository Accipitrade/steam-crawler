\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI originally wrote this review at 79.5 hours, and since then I've gotten more into the game. Even at that time, I hadn't experienced everything. I still love the game, maybe not in the same way as I did back then, but even after 1500+ hours this game is still fun for me.

Calling this free-to-play isn't exactly accurate. To get to the real meat of the game, you'll have to buy the expansions. If the price seems a bit much, don't worry as they go on sale quite a bit. At the very least, seasons cost $10 and include some extra gameplay bits and access to the seasonal pass.

With that said, this definitely is a game where I accept the bad with the good as MOST of the time the good outweighs the bad. While this game's core is fun and most of the repeatable content is genuinely fun, there's still a problem with the direction the company seems to be taking the game in. A good example is the introduction of weapon crafting. It was originally hallowed as a way to cut down on grinding as you were able to get the exact gun you wanted and for a while this was definitely the case. However, it was pretty apparent the very next season that crafting weapons was just another grind, and an arguably worse one.

Bungie has good intentions but it can very quickly turn against the player. However, I can say that season 18 was the only season where I felt my time wasn't being respected and Bungie walked back on quite a few of their decisions. I've been playing for 10 season and only 1 has been a truly negative experience.

I don't want to make it seem like the game hasn't been good to me because that simply isn't the case. I know this is a game where a lot of people say "I hate this game, I play it every day." and sometimes that may be true but there does always feel like there is some reason to play the game as much as a lot of people do. There are definitely plenty of pursuits a player can chase. For me, I like chasing titles and craftable weapons. For my friends, some love to do the hardest content the game has to offer while others enjoy the PvP.

The guns still feel great, the endgame content is absolutely phenomenal, and the story is finally being told in the game in a meaningful way.

Original review:
The thing with games in this genre is that I've never liked them. Warframe and Borderlands never got my attention, so I thought looter shooters weren't my thing. Destiny 2 completely blew me away. The game is great. The gunplay is amazing and borrows some of the best elements from DOOM 2016 like glory kills. Every weapon has been super fun and the bounties encourage you to change up your play style, which makes the grinding more bearable.
Overall, I recommend this game completely.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2, Controversial and polarized to hell when questioned about if it's good or bad. Many players I meet on my LFG runs would say "I play this game everyday, but I hate it" followed by a sad chuckle. It would be an accurate summary of what this game is to its core playerbase.

In my case, I don't agree with that statement, I think it's a good game. But it got soured by the people that play it as a chore or the people that finally realized that this game is riddled with micro transactions and greedy formats to take money out of your wallet. And yes, these are there. In full view even, bungie doesn't hide it with any shame whatsoever.

So why should I like the game? Am I jaded and only see the stellar gameplay, Beautiful vista's and the rich lore? No, on the contrary, I see the season passes, the dungeon keys and the amount of cosmetics stacking up in the eververse store just as much as the things that make this game good. But the key difference is that I know what sort of company this is and what sort of game they're pushing. Which is exactly why i'm not mad when bungie ♥♥♥♥♥ up.

People hold bungie in way to high regard, as if they're the golden child that made a beautiful game that's somehow magically 'tainted' by the suits and marketing teams that want to gouge this prize pig for everything it has. It's time for players to realize that this is a live service game that's out for your money and they'll drip feed you very good content that will keep you busy for hours after a day of work. It's the perfect timesink and grindfest you want it to be and it slaps on a large amount of cool world building and narrative to keep you going in for more exotics, more godrolls and more weapon patterns.

The sad part is that players who play the game often imagine how much better the game could be rather then playing the game for what it is. It's only natural to see the pain points of a game if you clock in 4 to 5 hours a day playing it. But this drives this wish of players that think that this game will become so much more then it already is if they just listened to their players. Which is misplaced hope that only leads to players being let down and disappointed, worst of all they create this disappointment themselves rather then Bungie doing so by holding this game in such high regard. While, in essence it's just very fun and very grindy, over monetized game. Which the Developers are fully aware of.

Which is why I shamelessly admit I like it. I like buying a cosmetic every once in a while from the eververse, I like the infinite grind and steep learning curve that keeps new players away. I like the FOMO and want to show all my peers online that i'm the sweat that goes for solo flawless dungeons. And I love that while i'm doing all these things i'm falling in love with a beautiful world rich with lore, npc's that I adore.

So yes, I highly recommend this game. But don't be deceived by what it offers. It's a pit you shovel money into for cosmetics and endgame content that's so well made that it gets you hooked for years on end.
