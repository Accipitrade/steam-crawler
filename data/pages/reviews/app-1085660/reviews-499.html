\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is an engaging, compelling game that keeps my attention over long periods of time. The story gets better and better with each new season. They have done a lot of work to improve their company culture and it shows in the game; they have some of the only on screen queer characters in gaming at this moment. They have characters of color in positions of authority, and a lot of truly lovely things to say about trauma, healing, and redemption. It goes beyond a simplistic light vs. dark conflict that I, at least, am heartily sick of by now.

The gameplay is fast paced and electric. I have experienced some very fun chaos in trying to accomplish the feast of triumphs, quests, strikes and more. The gunplay is satisfactory and there are many builds that will work with a variety of playstyles.

My quibbles are mostly quality of life and accessibility. Quality of life things that really should be included: being able to turn your character around in the character creator. More than three character slots. The ability to change your character's appearance in-game, and other things of that nature. When it comes to accessibility, it relies far too often on disorienting visual effects. As a legally blind person, it can make things outright unplayable. The lighting also needs work. For example, when entering a dark cave your character automatically turns on a flashlight. This is harsh and actually inhibits vision instead of aiding it.

Overall, I highly recommend this game.\t\t\t\t\t
