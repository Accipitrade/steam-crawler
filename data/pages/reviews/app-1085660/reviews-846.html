\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is hard to recommend to others because of the complexity behind its continued development. Despite the strides they've made to make it more friendly to new players, it's difficult to get into unless you really enjoy grinding. As someone who has played since its initial release, I can follow the story and really soak in the lore, but a new player wouldn't have been able to play the sunset material or really understand the game.
Despite that, the gameplay is top tier, and despite a few issues with balancing and PVP content, the game offers a lot for everyone. There is mult-leveled activities for all players, and endgame content that will punish and reward those who wish to try it.
As a free to play game, I would not recommend it to anyone. I see no reason to play this game unless you want to experience everything. If you have OCD, this game will abuse it constantly, with all the badges, triumphs, exotics and seals to collect.
Now for the story; it is one of the greatest, most extensive universes I have ever played in, and the lore is top tier. There are so many stories to get lost in, and the game's story can be enjoyed even when you're not playing the game.
Youtubers such as My Name Is Byf, have made lore videos that help you dive into the world and understand the past content that is no longer playable.
Overall, I highly recommend the game to people who want something to sink a chunk of their life into, but this game is not a casual one.

Edit: This game is not something I can recommend anymore. After 6 years, the gameplay loop of destiny is no longer rewarding in a good way. Every system they implement has a drawback that makes it a terrible addition, such as weapon crafting and transmog. The devs are showing their wishy-washy behavior by failing to establish a proper plan. About the only thing planned out properly here is the story, and I'm pretty sure the game will dwindle before it can come to a conclusion. Bungie continue to get more greedy and go back on their word consistently. They preach diversity and dip their hands into every bit of controversy they can to be "supportive", but honestly it's a slap in the face to feel represented by such an underhanded group of individuals.
The talent and story behind this game is insane, but it's constantly undone by cutting content, adding redundant systems, and putting stale content into parts of the game that should be fresh and new.
This new season started out great, with a good over-arching storyline, but has quickly become stale and grindy.
The need to put champions in every activity has already reached it's expiration date, and having to tailor my build around a terrible seasonal mod system is mind numbing and tedious.
Myself and all of my friends are quickly losing patience with this game, and if Bungie doesn't implement a solid plan moving forward, then I will be discontinuing my time with this game.
I do not advise you pick this up unless you are fond of dismissing your mental health.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOverall, Destiny 2 is a great Loot n Shooter. Fresh content is regularly released and you can complete most things solo because of matchmaking. That said, a few improvements could be made. First, not having crossplay is a real pain if you play on a platform like Google Stadia. Sometimes it takes a while to get matches for PVP or Gambit. I know Bungie is releasing Crossplay later this year so this shouldn't be an issue much longer. Also, and probably my biggest complaint: Stasis inside of PVP. For the last 7 months or so it has been a completely overpowered class to the point that PVP is almost unplayable if you main a Light subclass. This is Bungie nerfed Stasis somewhat and I feel that it's a good start, but the community at large agrees that it needs more work.

All in all, I can recommend the game. It does suck that you have to buy DLC on each platform you play on, but I hope that's no longer the case once we get crossplay.\t\t\t\t\t
