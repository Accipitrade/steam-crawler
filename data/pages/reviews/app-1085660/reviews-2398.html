\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI first started playing Destiny 2 a while after it became F2P and thought I was enjoying it up until I realized that all I had left to do as a solo player were objectives like "kill 300 enemies in PvP with this weapon that's bad in PvP," "participate in 400 matches," and other poorly designed quests that boil down to "hold this quest in your quest list (where you can only hold a small finite number of quests) until you accidentally stumble onto the right thing to do with zero guidance whatsoever and then go do a raid or dungeon, which is a co-op only mode which you need friends to complete."

I stopped playing once I realized it wasn't fun anymore. Years later, I have a few friends from work who play the game as if it's their second job and I figure "I'll give it a try since I now have people to play it with" only to find that it's way worse than it was when I left off. In addition to removing content I paid for and not fixing any of the issues I had with the game, I've found that there's only two difficulty settings, almost exclusively restricted to open world activities and the exotic quests/raids/dungeons my friends are doing, respectively: baby ez mode and grind your testicles between two coarse stones hard mode. Supposedly, the testicle grinding mode gets easier and more fun if you grind for perfect weapon rolls and get your power level up to a high enough level through months worth of weekly activity, but that's a few hundred hours I'm not interested in investing just to play "the good content."

You might have fun with it for 50 hours, then delude yourself into thinking you're having fun for another 200. You may even make it to 1000, but at that point I don't think there's any saving you. That being said, I'm pretty sure there's LESS content available for solo players now than there was when it first became F2P, what with all of the removed content and lack of new solo content to replace it. Probably just don't get invested in the first place, because everything Bungie does now is for the hardcore player and at the expense of the casual player.\t\t\t\t\t
