Do not let the green Metascore of 84 fool you. The game is not worth an "84", the content is not worth the "84".

This is the worst $60 dollars I spent in the last.. probably 3 years or so.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has fun Gunplay. Graphics and sounds are world class. Weapons and gear look pretty good. Classes are interesting and different. Community is pretty good, Aand... yeah, that's pretty much it.

The biggest problems with this game:
- Free-to-Play game, which isn't really a free to play: There is crazy amount of microtransactions with PAID DLC and PAID season passes. You can't get access to endgame bosses, maps, weapons, armor and so on if you want to play this as F2P. Even story missions are locked behind the paywall. So the ''free-to-play'' element is there just to fool new players to buy DLC and season passes, and microtransactions. So basically this so called F2P thing is just a trial.
- Enemy variety: You will kill the same enemies over and over and over and over again all the time. At first it's not that big of a deal, until you starts to understand how bad the enemy variety this game has. Even the end game has the same enemies, only with different names and light level. Not fun.
- The story is confusing and honestly, bad: I have played Destiny 1 and Destiny 2 and every time I start to think what this thing is all about, it gets really confusing and cliche. Not going to spoil anything but it's just another dark VS light thing with forgettable characters, enemies, and events. Nothing interesting is really happening in my mind and I just gave up with the story. You aren't missing that much by not following this whole story.
- Destiny 2 app: Honestly, who thought that this was a good idea to design this mobile app this way. To find a fireteam (basically a party) in a certain strikes, nightfalls, raids and so on, you must find players by yourself, usually through this app... Why just not integrate this thing to the actual game! It's basically just a way to give players a bad yet needed reason to download their app. Also this mobile app is pretty badly designed and also the clan finder is the worst I have ever seen. So hard to find actually a clan you might want to join.
- This might be the most silent ''MMO'' I have ever played: Many people don't use mics and raids can be a pain to the as* because you need good coordination and teamplay. This can be fixed if you have friends which are pretty much the same light level as you or actually own this game. Same thing goes with that stupid ''fireteam'' finder on mobile app.
- This isn't really an MMO: By this I mean that this isn't a game where you might have hundreds or even thousands people on the same area. In Destiny 2 you have this hub area, which has the most people on the same location compared to other areas, mostly. Yet I can see only like 20-30 players simultaneously on this hub area... Yeah, that's not a good sign on a MMO. This game feels like it tries to be MMO Halo/COD but it fails to be either. It's not a great MMO or even a Halo/COD clone/game. I mention this because I saw multiple ads where Bungie clearly states that this is a MMO, or even MMORPG, which it isn't.
- Grind which isn't fun: I haven't reached endgame, but by look of it I honestly don't care (it's pretty much the same as the rest of this game but with raids which don't look that great). You are going to grind you a** off if you want to get exotic missions done and some missions are clearly designed the way that you spend the maximum amount of time, or to buy your way out with real money (for example to get glimmer and other materials).
- Some strikes, nightfalls and raids are okay, but mostly it's just there to give players a reason to play this game. They aren't great, not even the ''best'' ones are that fun. Cliches everywhere with the same enemies and Dark VS light plot.
- Microtransactions

So there you have it. It's just another looter with multiplayer elements which doesn't do anything new or interesting to keep me playing. 4/10. Not worth grinding.\t\t\t\t\t
