\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPretty good game overall, I've played since year one, release day even, and I can say the game has definitely gotten better with Bungie cutting ties with Activision, the original stats came back which I loved, and I really like the randomized stats on gear, the game in year one, up until Forsaken came out was pretty bad, guaranteed rolls meant, which in turn meant no replay value, but Forsaken was the first step of making the game good, it gave weapons random rolls, which meant you could get weapons, and like them but you could get better, and the main thing in Destiny is to grind, now if the grind is boring and is guaranteed to Give you a good roll, there is no excitement, but getting that good drop after grinding for a long time when you could have gotten a bad roll makes you feel so good. Adding randomized stats on gear added even more fun into the game, as well as the updated stat system, it gave you more customization and made you feel really attached to your gear.
Getting a full raid team of friends is also one of, if not the most fun thing to do in Destiny, especially if you go in with no idea. It forces you to think, and when you finally find out whats going on and beat the encounter, you feel a sincere sense of accomplishment.
The game also has many game-modes to play in which makes you never really get bored playing it, my personal favorite is Rumble (infinite stealth for hunters is hilarious).
The game has had ups and downs, talking about you year one 10 minute walk at the beginning of red war, but it's definitely at a high point right now, with the release of the new Stasis subclasses coming out soon I think the games only going to be more fun, and interesting to see how it works, though I'm pretty sure I'm going to get mad in crucible when I get frozen.

I know this was long, but I really love this game, and thank you for reading my long love letter about how much I love it.\t\t\t\t\t

The combat feels really good for the most part except for some places where it recommends a certain light level (example: exotic quest strikes where it recommends 750 light level and it does not matter how high you are you will get decimated by the enemies, which just feels bad and forces you to find other players to help.)
There is some matchmaking in the game for the activities it offer but mostly you will just use the discord destiny 2 lfg for most of the content, which while nice still makes it difficult to find people who are interested in helping you. For me the discord lfg was mixed results i had good groups for raids but trying to find people to do exotics quest was and is a chore and could take between a few hours to a few days to get anyone to message you.
Onto the crucible, gotta say its pretty fun with decent modes to choose from. Story wise as someone who jumped back into destiny i have no idea whats going on as the introduction does not really explain anything. So basically your gonna have to youtube the destiny 2 story if your new and want to figure out what is currently going on. Would i recommend the game? if your like to grind and grind hard for very little sense of accomplishment then yes this game is for you! If you are a casual player then no i would not recommend for you.\t\t\t\t\t
