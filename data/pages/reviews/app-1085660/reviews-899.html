\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny, when looked at in a vacuum is in a pretty decent state. However, when taking in the full picture, the game is decrepit. An over three year long wait for a single new pvp map ended not too long ago. Issue is the map is only good for a single gamemode that isn't even permanent. Strikes and seasonal playlists are made boring thanks to them being braindead easy and the comical amount of power creep that players have recieved over the years, which has been amplified further by the recent subclass reworks. I no longer feel like a badass super-soldier going on risky ops, I feel like I just clocked into a 9 to 5.

There is challenging stuff to do, but that content also has their own slew of issues; rock paper scissors enemy design, negative modifier spam, and lack of incentives (looking at you master raids). Also, this harder content is locked behind power level which is an extremely uninteresting grind. Increasing power level, unlike the name suggests makes you less weak not more powerful. Another issue with power level is that every season the power level of previous activities increase, forcing you to have to regrind power to gain access to something you've unlocked in the past (probably multiple times).

Many more issues plague Destiny 2, such as balancing, the dreaded Gambit gamemode, and much more. Bungie have been announcing many improvements that I am looking forward to. They haven't addressed all of the concerns like power level and core activities, but, maybe they will; I'm not getting my hopes up though. Unfortunately, many of these improvements are coming in next years expansion, Lightfall, so there isn't much reason to jump in right now unless Bungie's FOMO tactics work on you.

It isn't all doom and gloom though, the Witch Queen Expansion is pretty alright. The campaign for it is excellent especially when played on its harder difficulty. The rest of the content added ranges from okay to boring. The gear, both legendary and exotic are pretty good overall, although the methods to get said gear is debatable in the fun department. Weapon crafting is a good idea on paper, but is very rough in its current implementation. The seasons so far have been the same old schlock that I and many others have grown tired of, weekly story and a new braindead seasonal activity. The Witch Queen feels like a rough diamond in a rusty crown.

As of right now, I do not recommend Destiny 2. It's been in worst spots, but right now it's definitely at a low point. Maybe when Lightfall drops next year the game will be in a good spot again. Don't get your hopes up though.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2:
A game with incredible hype and a once amazing studio behind it. Given every oppourtunity to make something great. Even after a somewhat bumpy ride we handed them a clean slate after their split with Activision. The core game feel and artwork is top-tier, no doubt about it. But the direction the game has been heading down and only seems to go further down is greedy, poorly executed and obviously milking its core playerbase of every penny they can get away with.
The game used to have real promise. Used to even be my go to game, but consecutively bad design choices has lead me to despise it. I will always have the memories, but in its current state, I cannot recommend this for more than a casual free-to-play no-brain looter-shooter.
I still love the franchise, the vision and the universe it exists in, so pray to Bungie they can reinvigorate some of that old ambition and love for game design that they once had.\t\t\t\t\t
