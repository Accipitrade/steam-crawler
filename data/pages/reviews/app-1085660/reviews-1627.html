\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGood points:
- It's a solid game; many enjoyable dungeons, several planets to explore, beautiful scenery and great dialogue.
- 3 classes, each class has 3 ability sets, each ability set has 3 variations (even though some of them are clearly broken/far more powerful then the rest.)

Bad points:
- The story isn't captivating at all.
- Gun variations/modifications are extremely limited. If you're looking for a unique weapon, chances are you'll spend months just to find one.
- The game becomes grindy pretty quickly, to the boring point. I'm not saying other games aren't grindy, but that's all you really do with games that offer season rewards.
- PvP has many modes, but essentially gear-based most of the time rather than skill-based.

Final Verdict:
It's an ok-ish game, but don't get too hooked up in it because it's rather 'empty' in terms of content. In spite of the previous sentence, you better not play this game for its PvP content. Warframe is still superior by many levels despite being completely free (meaning you can get everything in the game without paying a single dollar.) I can't help but compare between the two because I spent a lot of time playing Warframe and I actually enjoy it. I started playing D2 just because my friends pulled me in it. D2 is a good distraction for me when I burn myself out in Warframe.\t\t\t\t\t
