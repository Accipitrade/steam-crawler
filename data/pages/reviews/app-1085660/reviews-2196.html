\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMeh. As a player since the D1 beta I have probably given Bungie too much rope expecting them not to hang themselves with it by giving them so much leniency. But alas they found a way. The only place I can give unassailable praise is that the visual design is incredibly well done and interesting, it makes me want to like the setting even though there is so little of actual substance to like.

The gunplay is mediocre, it isn't breaking new ground or really doing anything interesting, most of it's weapons are incredibly mundane even the exotic ones. It doesn't have visual flair the violence so it feels like GI Joe when the almost entirely intact (exception being for the the blatant robots) bodies of your enemies just flop onto the ground. The Enemies themselves are as bright as a shattered light bulb, many of them struggling to hit you accurately without the help of AOE attacks, pausing momentarily to stare into space before resuming their attack. The danger they mostly represent is a growing of swarm of paper in an air tunnel threatening to grow to an amount that will eventually paper cut you to death by sheer numbers. The only way the game knows to increase difficulty is by adding more enemies and giving them more health and more damage, though they have added some that bring a little bit more to the table they still function as HP boosts along with the additional damage that their higher level gives them. Ultimately the gameplay loop is just sweeping out all the trash before getting to work on the harder stains.

The RPG mechanics are pretty barebones, the classes ultimately all operate as a form of Space Marine just with some slightly different space magic between them that they use for jumping, slapping things and blowing stuff up. The main difference is the supers, ultimate abilities activated with a button push that gives you damage protection and lets you use a high damage attack, except for the 3 utility ones that ultimately just provide a route for everyone to do more damage and not just the person that used the super. In PvP it operates the same as a passively generated kill streak, so that everyone can have a chance to OHK another player. Otherwise the RPG elements operate entirely around the armor system, with perks being set by what you slot into your armor, limited by an energy amount on that piece with each mod costing a certain amount of energy. For the most part mods are fairly bland, with a few seasonal ones shaking things up as of late but if you missed that season you are SOL. Otherwise armor just helps determine your power level and gives stat points to your 6 attributes, 4 of which are basically related to cooldown timers for your health regen, melee ability, grenade, super, and Class ability (survivability utility) that is tied to your class' main stat. Like I said, for the most part you all play as a space marine you shoot stuff with guns and occasionally you use an ability to kill them instead.

Speaking of guns those are the main draw of the game, or the main hook for keeping you around. Guns are your primary source of interacting with the world, because most of your interaction is clearing spaces out of enemies. The weapons like I said are all rather bland, with only the fusion rifle, and their trace and linear cousins, as the only non-contemporary weapon types. Exotics are the only ones with any real flavor, and they are somewhat watered down in order to keep them from becoming a problem in the game's PvP modes. The way you get these weapons is by severe repetition of the same tasks over and over again. Some of those drops are gated off to be once on that character per week and some can be done multiple times. This is the main driving force of the game, weapons are the most important part and thus getting ones that you like using the most serves as the main drive. The problems is that in the past and now once again, Bungie intends to 'retire' older weapons by making it so they can't be enhanced to future levels of power after a set amount of time, because people like what they have and Bungie isn't happy when people don't like what they want them to like. Besides from rendering all that time wasted as all of the weapons you worked to earn are made worthless as it can no longer be useful in any future content it also makes the old content completely pointless as it only rewards things that are no longer useful, so why bother taking part in those activities? This is a massive problem because the majority of this game's permanent content is the old content that will be affected by this change, and the newer activities don't hold a candle to what came before and only operate as transient time fillers for the season.

The reason I have so much time in this game is because of setting personal goals and striving to meet them, complete the raid X times, complete my exotic armor and weapon collection, getting X weapon with Y perks. You can't work towards cosmetics because they are all locked up in the cash shop, the weapons and armor are pretty much it. And through meeting those goals I have wasted a lot of time, but I cannot think of a single moment where I was glad to be playing the game, where the game had a unique sense of enjoyment that I couldn't find elsewhere. It was just filler. And hey sometimes that ain't too bad but with Bungie making it clear that their intent is just to waste my time and prod me into doing it? Yeah no thanks, I'm done.

I feel sorry for all the creative people who made such an interesting world so full of potential only to watch a be completely squandered by small minded executives more interested in play time and microtransactions.\t\t\t\t\t
