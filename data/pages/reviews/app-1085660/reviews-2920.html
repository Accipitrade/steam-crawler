I previously played this game on Battle.net for not that long and quit when Osiris came out, i felt there was a lack of content at the time, jumped back in when Shadowkeep dropped and ended up buying Forsaken as well, there is currently quite a bit more to do in the game compared to before, the gameplay as a whole is good from the shooting to the movement of characters, i don't have any complaints in that regard.


Performance - Game runs well, haven't had any issues playing at 4k with a 4790k and SLI 1080ti, average about 80 fps or higher

Campaign - It's generally good, pretty fun and it does tell a somewhat interesting story with cool characters but is not very long.

Strikes - This time around i found myself not playing strikes as much, it has gotten a bit tedious to me and the fact that they do not let you matchmake for anything 950 light and above is dumb, essentially you'll need to LFG through their mobile app or discord groups to find players to play with which for some reason Bungie insists on to create a "community", it's a huge annoyance since it's endgame content and not everyone has people to play with or wants to go through such methods of having to seek out people online, just let us match with people in the game itself. The way the seasonal artifact abilities are worked into the strikes is also not the best, they essentially force you to use abilities like overload, anti barrier, unstoppable, but those types of mods cannot be applied to all weapons, they pick and choose which weapons can be slotted with them so sometimes you wont be able to add them to your favorite weapons or exotics.

Raids - Much like higher level strikes there's not really any matchmaking and there are no instructions on what to do in the raid, you will have to go online and watch some fairly long videos to even know what to do since much of the raids consist of jumping puzzles or odd mechanics you otherwise may not be able to figure out if you go in blind, the raids support up to 6 players.

PVP (crucible) - This is big point of contention for many players, the pvp is very imbalanced and Bungie only compounded the problem with the addition of their seasonal artifact which gives buffed damage and effect to certain class elemental abilities making them much stronger while leaving some subclasses with nothing, so naturally a lot of the player base shifts to the more OP/cheese builds, Bungie seems to encourage this type of behavior. They do this on purpose to dictate the "meta" for that season. When i do jump into crucible I primarily get on quick play to do bounties but have also had to play competitive for certain weapon rewards which can only be earned there. For the most part quick play was ok for me but can be a toss up, competitive on the other hand is typically filled with sweaty try hards especially at higher levels, this is a pretty well known fact among the player base, a good majority of the time you'll be killed by shotgun apes, long range snipers, Titan shoulder charges, warlock charge grenades(all i've mentioned are pretty much always one shot kills) and generally really meta scumbags, you also can't forget that teams will just hide to run out the clock if they are up on lives in comp. All in all I'm a pretty average pvp player but didn't really have any issues climbing past fabled(2100) in comp, just keep at it, take a break when you get tilted and you should be ok. As i mentioned before a lot of balancing is needed in pvp to get this game where it needs to be.

Gambit/Gambit Prime - This mode is is half pve and half pvp with 2 teams, you kill waves of enemies and throw the mote drops in the bank to summon enemies to attack the other team, at certain points of the game a portal will open and one player on your team can invade the other teams field to kill them as they will be trying to do the same to you, the aim of this mode is to bank motes as quickly as you can to summon the final boss and defeat it before the other team, i don't play this mode a lot but it's ok for a change of pace from doing other things in the game and earning some rewards.

Weapons/armor/mission format - I'm a big fan of the variety of things available to use and wear on the guardians, there's pretty much weapons and armor to suit everyones tastes, it ranges from really normal guns(hand cannons, auto rifles) to sci-fi themed launchers which shoots bees or a laser gun that bounces off walls, only downside i've found was that many weapons are stuck behind crazy grinds across all modes, strikes, gambit and crucible all have their own respective weapon unlocks which individually can take upwards of 20-30 hours per weapon depending on how efficient you are, this is exceptionally bad if it is a mode you don't like or play at all, some of the most powerful weapons in the game require you to do these. Some weapon quests are unlocked through the story or other various events but in many cases don't actually tell you straight up what you need to do, this is a common theme in destiny 2, much like raids Bungie expects you to go online and find out from the community what to do which could be watching youtube videos or reading walkithroughs, in general i found myself sometimes needing to do about 20 minutes of research before attempting to do what i was intending, what makes matters worse is they don't have any waypoints or allow you to set your own, all they say is "the item you are looking for is on X planet", the mission system in some cases is fundamentally flawed. At the end of such quests(izanagi/whisper) you may have to do things similar to strikes/mini raids which again they do not let you matchmake so again you'll have to LFG somewhere unless you are confident in your ability to solo, you see the trend right?

When you start as a new player you will be 750 and the soft cap is 950, getting from 750 to 950 is relatively simple but once you hit 950 the only thing that will up your light level will be pinnacle drops from either the 100k nightfall ordeal, raid or nightmare hunts(which i have not played yet), i may be missing something tho, all of which don't have matchmaking so again you'll have to LFG online or find friends who play unless you are powerful enough to solo, this in itself may not be the issue for some players, the problem is that there aren't enough sources of weekly pinnacle event drops outside of iron banner which is only once a month, Bungie intentionally does this to really slow down progression and it's something i can't really get behind.


I've had more fun with the game than i expected since getting back in at Shadowkeep but the game has a lot of things that just annoy the hell out of me, from the no matchmaking on much of the end game activities, to the cheese that goes on in the crucible and lastly no clear direction on many missions without some type of guide, these things make me not want to play sometimes but your milege may vary, tho I did enjoy the game I will likely be taking a break next season due to the annoyances since i doubt anything will be fixed or changed.\t\t\t\t\t
