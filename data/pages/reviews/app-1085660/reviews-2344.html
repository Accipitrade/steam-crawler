\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is an incredible game like very few others on the market, with a community hardier and more resilient than almost any other. It's gunplay is unrivaled. It's atmosphere, visual design, and sound design is spectacular. The gameplay loop is addictive, suitably rewarding, and throws in enough variation to not get old. The endgame is respectable, if sparse when compared against the MMOs it emulates.
It is not worth your money, or more importantly, your time. The game's microtransactions are insidious and unfun, it's seasonal model requires time investment akin to a part-time job, it's pricetag is higher than most full-priced MMOs even though it's gone F2P, and it's story outside of the fantastic Forsaken expansion is a vague and meandering mess only veterans of the first game will have the perspective to fully grasp. It's lore is fantastically written, but is not a substitute for a proper narrative for the player to directly interface with.
Destiny 2 is an incredible game unlike almost any other, but it asks of you far more than it returns. I do not recommend that you purchase it at this time.\t\t\t\t\t
