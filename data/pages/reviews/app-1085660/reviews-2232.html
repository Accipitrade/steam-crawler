\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have over 3000 active hours in this game since Curse of Osiris. I started at this games lowest point, and I feel less excited about this game on a base level than I did back then.

to open with the important bit. I would not recommend getting into D2 as a new player at all tight now. as much as I love seeing kinderguardians about, I'm not okay with them thinking that this is the game. this season was better than Lightfall sure, and the story is still interesting, but I don't feel any drive to just play the game.

I'm writing this shortly after state of the game. maybe it'll look better with coming twids, and with next season. but the game is a weekly chore sim. you login at reset, knock out playlists, and seasonal story. then ♥♥♥♥ off.

Gambit is in shambles, and all they're doing for it is recycling an old, not that good map. PVP is getting a new map, and a couple gamemodes which is less than it deserves considering the amount of the playerbase that applies to. and strikes are being mixed up by recycling old seasonal activities that got stale 2 weeks into their respective seasons.
worse yet is that they're switching the weekly challenges to be a joined 9 rather than 3 each, so you're just gonna AFK 9 rumble matches, or speedrun a few strikes.

the game is currently seeing a drought for reasons to play the game baseline, and Bungie is dropping reasons instead of giving them.

Bungie puts so much work into this game, but so much of that work goes into little quality of life things that everyone forgets they added a week after, for instance I bet you forgot you could turn those rainmakers in your inventory into glimmer.

I'm no marketing expert, or gamedev, but I feel that Bungie could make a lot of steps in the right direction if they took a season off of the major content for a core gameplay overhaul since we're dropping players like flies, and season launches will only work for so long.

hell I feel like if Bungie doesn't find a good way to heal the core gameplay of D2 they may inadvertently sabotage the launch of Marathon too, since who's gonna want to play a game from a dev known for neglecting the biggest problems with their previous game.

I genuinely have enjoyed my time with destiny up until recent, and I really want to see Bungie succeed as a company, but not at the cost of the game that made me fall in love with them in the first place. I genuinely try to see the light in everything Bungie does, but damn is it dim recently.

I hope I'm given a reason to rewrite this review in the future, but for now I'm not too sure about where the game is going.

With genuine love for this game, I sadly am leaving this negative review as I've seen what this game can be.\t\t\t\t\t
