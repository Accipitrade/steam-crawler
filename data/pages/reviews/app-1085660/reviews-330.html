\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI hate that I keep coming back to this game, the monetization is honestly insane. It still feels better than pretty much every other shooter. It looks and runs incredible, the sound design is top tier, the music is fantastic, the power fantasy is completely unmatched. But the pricing model almost ruins all of that, and for a lot of people it will/did. The fact that people bought this game for over 100 dollars for the super big ♥♥♥♥ edition and now basically none of that content is playable should be criminal.

The content vault is a travesty and really makes no sense, content you pay money for now, will inevitably be locked away and become inaccessible forever. And yes, that will probably only happen about a year after you buy it, giving you (probably) plenty of time to run through it, but that's still insane. The expansions are not cheap...because if you want to play ALL of the content each expansion comes with, you have to buy the seasons/pass as well. This is not immediately apparent and I could see people buying the base expansion, only to be locked out of some of the content and have to go buy the season at a higher price than they would have got it at if they bought the season pass with the expansion, which they are also now locked of buying. Think it's ridiculous yet?

Even just buying the current expansions isn't enough because SOME of the content from previous expansions is still playable and used in the weekly nightfall/dungeon challenge rotations. So say a year down the road, you hop on and get really into the game again but the weekly dungeon challenge or nightfall raid just happens to be from a vaulted expansion that you didn't buy four years ago... Now if you want to take part of that content you have to go buy the whole expansion, which will only give you access to those specific pieces of un-vaulted content. And you never know what will be vaulted... weapons, armor, missions, strikes, raids, entire planets (RIP Mars)... nothing is off limits.

I don't think it would be nearly as bad if players could request a refund for the content they never got to play through. Like if someone buys an expansion but falls off of the game before completing the campaign, then they should be able to get some amount back for the missions/strikes/whatever gets content vaulted. Even if it ends up being like 5 bucks back from not doing the raid, I think that would be a much easier pill for people to swallow. Of course that would be a pretty convoluted solution, but this is the one thing preventing me from calling this one of the greatest shooters ever made.
