Summary
In short, it's not perfect, but Destiny 2 is my favorite game of all time. No other game has managed to capture my interest for years like the Destiny franchise. As a Destiny 1 veteran, I can say Bungie doesn't always get it right, but they remain committed to releasing great content, updating the game, and balancing the hundreds of weapons, armor, and abilities. I'm excited to see where they take this franchise in the coming years. Overall, I would absolutely recommend this game to anyone who loves shooters, sci-fi, and RPGs.

Pros
Cons
  • An impressive amount of high quality content and gameplay for a F2P game
  • Industry-leading gunplay, every weapon feels smooth and distinct
  • Abilities are tons of fun, buildcrafting is accessible and impactful
  • Triumps, challenges, and Guardian Rank objectives means there's always something to do
  • Vast amount of lore for the lore nerds
  • Fantastic voice acting, beautiful environments and orchestral soundtrack
  • Live service game, so consistent updates, fixes, and content drops
  • Great community engagement and involvement
  • Steep learning curve for newcomers
  • External third-party applications are essential for getting the most out of the experience
  • Many activities require a non-matchmade fireteam, and the Guided Games feature is insufficient for this purpose
  • Expansions are numerous and increasingly expensive
  • Microtransactions are abundant, but only cosmetic
  • Unnecessary political opinions or commentary are occasionally present
  • Server issues and bugs
  • Some dialog and characters are poorly written or executed

Keep reading for my thoughts on the game's story, gameplay, activities, quality of life, and community.



Story
The story of Destiny 2 has seen its share of ups (The Witch Queen), downs (Curse of Osiris), and everything in between, but considering where the game was at launch, it has drastically improved. While Lightfall was a miss in terms of its campaign and story, the overall narrative continues to be engaging. New Seasons and lore have expanded on the story since the expansion released, which makes the confusion around Lightfall's lack of explanation of core elements easier to bear. Overall, though, the story of Destiny is one that has captivated me since I delved into it, and I would definitely recommend the game to anyone interested in a well-developed science fiction narrative.

Gameplay
Gunplay
Bungie has been in the first-person shooter space for decades, and their experience in the genre shows. The members of Destiny's community disagree on nearly every front, but they share a nearly unanimous opinion that the weapons feel great. Every one of the game's hundreds of weapons look, feel, sound, and play distinctly. If you love first-person shooters, that is reason enough to give this game a try.

Abilities
The abilities of Destiny 2 are what the game's core identity is in, however, and these feel equally as great as the weapons. Each of the three classes has a wide range of options to create builds that shine in various situations or activities, while also looking and sounding impressive, making you feel like the space magic-wielding hero you're supposed to be.

Exotics
The Exotic weapons and armor take Destiny 2's combat experience up a notch, changing the way you play the game and providing an anchor point to create builds around. The great thing about Bungie's commitment to Destiny as a live service game means no ability, weapon, subclass, or Exotic over- or underperforms for too long.

Activities
Destiny 2 has a wide variety of modes and activities, both PVP and PVE, with new ones being rotated in regularly with the release of each expansion and Season. The core playlist activities (strikes, Nightfalls, Crucible, Gambit) are fun in and of themselves, but don't receive updates often enough and can begin to feel stale quickly. Seasonal activities range from being a little boring to being a blast, but they require purchasing the current Season. Other one-off activities, like Lost Sectors, are short and fun enough that I have no problems with them.

Endgame
Where the game really shines is in its endgame PVE activities, such as Raids, Dungeons, and Grandmaster Nightfalls. Here, Bungie flexes their muscles with taking their combat experience, difficulty, and creativity to the next level, barring a few lackluster exceptions. These activities are more difficult, and require more time and coordination than most others in the game, but completing them is a feeling few other games have come close to replicating. Unique weapons, armor, and Exotics make these activities even more rewarding. Furthermore, additional challenges exist for these activities (like solo flawless Dungeons, Raid challenges, or GM's with specific subclasses) to keep them relevant long after their release.

Lack of matchmaking
The only major unfortunate side effect of these intense, team-based activities is the lack of matchmaking. Because these activities rely on solid teamwork and communication, matchmaking is an ineffective strategy for creating teams, so it's understandable that Bungie hasn't implemented this feature (despite years of requests from community members). Unfortunately, then, more casual or solo players are often left out of some of the game's best content, forcing them to either accept that they won't be able to play this content or shoulder the burden of matchmaking for themselves with external LFG sites. Bungie's Guided Games feature that released when the game launched was intended to alleviate some of this burden by matching solo players with experienced Clans, but it never seemed to actually perform its function, and has received no significant updates since its inception.

Quality of Life
Destiny 2 is constantly receiving QoL updates, changes, and improvements that have made playing the game much more intuitive and accessible. From the new loadout system with Lightfall (one of my personal favorite features), to UI updates, accessibility features, mod system revamps, and better descriptions, the game looks, feels, and plays better than it ever has.

Community
While this is a review of Destiny 2, not its community, considering the requirement to be involved in it (see Lack of matchmaking above), I believe it's important to note what kind of people you can expect to interact with. As with any game community (or any group of people, for that matter), the Destiny 2 community has its share of bad apples. You'll undoubtedly come across some distasteful behavior in LFG groups, PVP matches, and especially if you're on the Destiny side of Twitter. D2 players are some of the most committed I've seen, and they are very passionate about the game we play, so they'll complain about every feature and every other player at every opportunity. However, once you filter out these negative voices, and particularly if you find a supportive, committed Clan to play with, you'll realize what an awesome community Destiny 2 really has. Destiny 2 is an fantastic experience as it is, but it becomes much better once you play with other people. Don't let the negativity deter you, find some great friends and clanmates, and you'll see what I mean.\t\t\t\t\t
