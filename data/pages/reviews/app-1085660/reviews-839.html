\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have to say, there's something magical about the world of Destiny. I don't just mean magical in the sense that there's quite literally a "magical" aspect to the game with super powers and abilities, but rather there's something alluring about the game to draw you in. I started playing Destiny back when the "Taken King" expansion dropped in 2015, and been a fan since. Whether you're here for some engaging gun play, to work with friends to fight off hordes of enemies, to exploring a rich world and story, Destiny honestly offers a little something for everyone. Let me be clear -- This game isn't perfect. It suffers from a handful of issues that have tested the mettle of the developers and community alike. But it's never been enough to pull me away from the game. Steam says I have over 1,000 hours logged into Destiny 2, and that's not including the time I put in before the transition from Battle.net to Steam or time spent with friends in the Xbox days of D1.

When it comes to PvP (especially Gambit, the PvP/PvE game mode), there's plenty to be upset about. From the looming presence of cheaters to frustrating server connections to a clear "you either have this weapon and are gonna do fine, or you don't and good luck getting kills" meta, there's plenty that DOES frustrate players on the daily. I don't sink a lot of time into the PvP area because that's not really my scene, but certain aspects (such as pinnacle weapons or weapon skins or bounty progression) of the game require you to at least dip your toes into the waters of PvP. I know also that it's a huge issue for people, not just because most of the big D2 creators on YouTube also are frustrated by it, but because of conversations I've had with many people online. Even people in my clan are tired of PvP and just feel exasperated at the thought of having to play PvP again. This is probably by far my biggest complaint with the game, and I know Bungie said they're constantly evaluating and working to make PvP an enjoyable experience for all, I just hope to see them deliver on this and soon.

I know it's easy to sit and dwell on a games shortcomings, but lets talk about what Destiny gets right. The game is visually beautiful. Destiny is an MMO shooter game that offers an arsenal of weapons and armor to wield. I love variety, especially when it comes to aesthetic and weapon performances. Destiny does this thing that every now and then, you pick up a weapon that you just cannot stop using. It feels so finely tuned, like it was made just for you, and you use it to hold your ground against the horde. And as time goes on, you discover another weapon that feels even better than the current weapon you have. Suddenly, you find yourself packing a vault full of sentimental, enjoyable weapons, ready to break one out for whatever situation you need. You find an armor piece with high stat rolls, or perhaps you just like the look of it. You find yourself very quickly overburdened with armor as well. Exotic weapons and armor, powerful custom class tuned equipment to further enhance your play style, or introduce you to new play styles. Finally have the armor look and weapons you like? Take a pick from the wide expansion of color shaders to apply to your character to truly flesh out your creation to your liking. There is a large variety of equipment given to players to work with to make their experience feel personal and enjoyable.

And finally, what I consider the best and most engaging part of Destiny, is the story. To say the universe of Destiny is rich and dense would be an understatement. This game has so much lore, you could spend days reading stories or watching cut scenes and feel like you're only just scratching the surface. You play as a guardian of humanity, one who wields great power, given from the traveler, and you must protect humanity from any threats, no matter where they might emerge from. There will be leaders, kings, gods even, all with an army of creatures from the darkness. It is up to you (and sometimes your friends as well) to stop evil wherever it arises.

All in all, I'd give the game a 8/10. A great game with addicting game play that needs a few tweaks here and there. Eyes up, Guardians.\t\t\t\t\t
