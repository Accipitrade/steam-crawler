\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPVP

I hate destiny pvp and its probably my most played game mode.

I originally made the original one liner review for this game as more of a joke review but after my too many hours of playing this game what I can say is in Destiny's current state I personally dislike the pvp.

Let me break this down

It's a combination of both the current meta of weapons as well as and most likely more importantly the lack of pvp maps.

Before Beyond Light there were more than enough maps for pvp, after that update we lost a countless amount of maps and left with what feels like 5 maps (theres more maps I know but thats what it feels like).

With both of these combined its pushed myself personally away from pvp, for other people this could be the most enjoyable meta and good on them or you reading this.

Thats honestly it, 2 small things that have shaken me off pvp. I will admit though that throughout my years upon years of destiny I have played countless hours of PVE as well but in comparison nothing compared to my hours in pvp.

PVE

Honestly let me tell you, this season is probably my favorite season for PVE, breach and clear exists, crown splitter is absolutely broken with energy acc on titan, at the end of the day its a combination of enjoyable and just overall fun.

I've had more fun doing GM Nightfalls (Grandmaster) with absolute randoms than nearly anything else, the adept weapons feel good and are fun to use (specifically talking about nightfall adept weapons)

VOG (Vault of Glass is a more than enjoyable raid thats honestly super easy to learn for beginners to raiding as well as having more than solid loot.

Conclusion

If you took the time to read this overly long review, thank you. Overall I still do LOVE destiny, its a combination of my favorite and least favorite game for a lot of different reasons that I cant cover in a single review, but if you're on the fence honestly I'd say to give the game a try, there's a lot in the world of destiny and its great, the gun play alone is fantastic as well as the lore being actually interesting and probably the only game where I actually care about the lore.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've been playing Destiny 2 since Year 1, starting on PS4 and eventually making my way to PC in the later years. Throughout it all I've lost several accounts to my own mistakes, bought several copies of the disc (before free-2-play), bought several DLCs/Expansions multiple times and STILL find myself coming back. You thought CoD was unbalanced? You haven't played Gambit. You thought Dark Souls didn't want you to have fun? You haven't played a GM Nightfall yet. My point being: the game has some downs, but it also has some serious replay value everywhere you go and as long as you have a basic understanding of the game there is hardly ever a dull moment. Playing solo, or with a 6-stack, it doesn't matter. It can be fun. Destiny 2 mashes a ton of mechanics together and it makes for a very tuneable experience. You can be an open world explorer who just does bounties and basic quest. Or you can be the hardcore Ironlord crushing teams with Stasis and Fellwinter's lie. You can hunt down humanity's nightmares in the Deep Stone Crypt. Or you can run basic level strikes. It all has potential. I'd highly recommend this game to anybody who doesn't have a large library of games and likes to focus on 2-3 games at a time. It can very demanding to get to higher levels and be able to do high-end content but it's very worth it and very satisfying.\t\t\t\t\t
