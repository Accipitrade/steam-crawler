\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've spent a LOT of time playing this franchise over the last six years. Multiple breaks in between - that is fine. In short, I believe the Raids and Dungeons are some of the best Co-Op PVE content in any game ever. The problem is, there is just so much busy work to get there and a community that continually becomes more and more 'meta' as time goes on.

If you are caught up enough to do the new raid the weekend or within a few weeks of it coming out and can do it blind - do it! It is amazing fun with a good group and the puzzle solving is incredible. If you have a group of friends to guide you through the beginning of the game and get you raid ready, do it!

Just know that the highs of this game are really, really, good. They are infrequent though. The lows make up the core of the game and are busy work. You may have heard of "bounty simulator - the game" memes about Destiny 2. Those are completely correct. Every quest has at least a few steps that boil down to "kill this enemy 100 times" "get 100 precision kills", etc. Painful drudgery.

The story is worth following season to season and is pretty entertaining, but if you don't play each season you basically have to look up youtube videos. This is supposedly being corrected starting with the Beyond Light expansion. That said, the game is mostly in a great state right now - plenty of raids and end game content to explore. Most of that is going away with Beyond Light though and I am worried about the future of the game. This review may radically change.

Performance wise, I'm able to 4k/60 with a Ryzen 5 3600 and GTX 1080 Ti with all settings Ultra. Minor dips here and there, dropping into the mid 40s, but nothing too bad. Very impressed with the performance. On my laptop, an i7-7700hq and GTX 1060 mobile 6gb, I lock 1440p/60 ultra and it runs flawlessly with no drops.

Download Destiny Item Manager for Android or Edge/Chrome and enable farming mode. Makes it so your inventory won't annoying be filled with the nonsense blue quality drops that plague the endgame.

This is easily the best MMOesque shooter out there. The gunplay is unmatched. Now, if we could get a Halo quality campaign to go along with it, it would be an incredible package. As it stands now, it is a good social game and a disaster alone.\t\t\t\t\t
