\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is an MMO-lite looter-shooter from the masters of FPS games, Bungie. And of course, since they perfected the genre, the gunplay in this game is unparalleled and no matter how repetitive the content may sometimes seem/get, the minute-to-minute gameplay carries you through the grind with ease.

Each class feels unique and the customisation both visually and build-wise is surprisingly deep for such an expansive game. The best part from the 'looter-shooter' aspect is easily how they handle the gear. Every weapon is its own entity, rather than the Borderlands approach of guns being built from parts and each manufacturer being somewhat limiting to how the gun can feel. Exotics are even more unique and have separate mechanics on top of pulling from the pool of traits a weapon can have. Armour is less noticeable/impactful outside of visuals but the ability to tweak each piece with mods is nice.

Another aspect this game really shines in is the mix of PvE, PvP and PvEvP. You would think that being split between three components would lower the overall quality but it is very easy to 'main' just one area and not feel like you are missing out (other than the area-exclusive loot). The Crucible (PvP) is a beautiful successor to Halo and the constant game mode rotation makes sure it never gets dry. Momentum Control, a time-limited mode which is already gone, better come into the regular rotation because it was a Halo SWAT replacement.

I will say that the game going F2P is potentially damaging to the game's future. I am a New Light player but understand that a lot of cosmetics that were previously obtainable from in-game activities have been put behind a micro-transaction paywall. And it is hardly 'micro'-transactions when it costs £10 for one single item. Also, the season pass itself is well-done and feels rewarding but the content that comes with the season is lacking in comparison to previous expansions. It is something to keep an eye on because the 'buy-in' to these seasons (if you go the Shadowkeep Digital Deluxe route) is expensive and you obviously want to get value from it. However, the F2P version is probably the most generous AAA model I've seen in a while and there's hundreds of hours in the base game alone.\t\t\t\t\t
