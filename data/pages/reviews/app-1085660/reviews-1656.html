\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a lot like fast food. You know it's bad for you, but you can't get enough of it.

I have a love/hate relationship with Destiny 2.

I love it because it's just fun. In an era of gaming where fun seems to take the back seat to make room for genre developments, graphic/visual advancement, and "compelling" storytelling, Destiny 2 provides players with a sense of enjoyment--albeit more in a "mindless" or "senseless" way--that just isn't found in other games. The gunplay in this game is phenomenal, and it's very enjoyable to mow through the trash mobs and bosses D2 throws at you. You'll quickly find what type(s) of gun(s) you like to use, and then enjoy defeating hundreds of enemies with them. The three different classes are all fairly unique and interesting to use, and the weaponry is great (note my prior comment on the gunplay). Honestly, there isn't much depth to Destiny 2, but it's just fun. Especially now that it is free-to-play, you should definitely try it for yourself.

I simultaneously hate it because it has MAJOR glaring issues that can either hinder or enhance your enjoyment depending on what type of a player you are. As you've probably already heard, the grind is very real in D2. The most in-your-face grindy aspect of this game is indisputably the power level grind. Hitting the light level cap is no small feat, and it will likely take you weeks of time to get there with a single class. Then, you must repeat the process two more times for the other two classes. THEN, you need to make sure to get the right rolls on all your armor and weapons to maximize your build. It's a grueling process that laughs at you as you play the same content over and over again trying to reach the peak. Furthermore, some of the absolute best weapons in the game are a total grindfest to obtain. I won't go into too much detail, but just look up the requirements to unlock Redrix's Claymore if you're interested. Another inexcusable sin committed by this game is its severe lack of content. Once you've beaten the uninspired and dull story, you'll find that you basically have but a few varied options for what content you'd like to repeatedly complete. You can pick a) multiplayer, b) strikes, c) raids, d) gambit (pvp and pve mashed together), or e) seasonal events. All of these will get stale quick, and even when something new is added to the game via new DLC, it still just feels like a re-skinned version of the existing content. Not to mention, although you get all the new strikes, multiplayer maps, etc. for free when new DLC drops, you cannot obtain the new exotics (arguably the most interesting part of this game) unless you purchase the DLC, which is typically a ludicrous amount considering what you are receiving in exchange for the hefty investment you make.

If you've read this much of this review, thank you for your time. I could go on and on about the negative aspects of D2 as someone who legitimately enjoys the game, but I feel that I've ranted enough. The bottom line is that I think you should try this game out for yourself, but do be aware of all the negative pieces of the game before you spend your hard-earned money on every DLC available.

TL;DR - D2 is very fun, but contains a lot of bs. Try it out since it's free, but buyer beware if purchasing DLC.\t\t\t\t\t
