\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLittle background on me for this review; I've played through all of the first game, including it's DLC's and put in double the hours you see here on Steam on the PS4 version of this game before I transferred my characters to PC.

This game has been through a lot since it's initial release. I got it on the PS4 back then, beat the main story, then left it to sit in the dust for years until Shadowkeep Expansion. Just like how the Taken King DLC from the first game fixed a lot of the in-game problems and brought back a lot of original players, Shadowkeep seems to have had the same effect on the sequel.

Apparently Bungie broke their ties with EA on Destiny 2, and it shows! The game's core mechanics are still the same, but it does feel like less of a chore to grind for materials than it did back on release. The in-game microtransaction store, Eververse, doesn't shove itself into your face anymore either. Nightfall Strikes are much easier to handle now, but can proof to be quite difficult when moving up the difficulty even one notch!

I'd highly suggest giving this game another shot if you've ever played Destiny 1 or played this on release years ago.\t\t\t\t\t
