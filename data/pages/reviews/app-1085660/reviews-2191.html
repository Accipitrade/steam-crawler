\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI absolutely love the gameplay of Destiny. I've been a Bungie fan since the original Halo ... but this game has actually gotten WORSE since they left Activision, which I didn't think was even possible. Their implementation of "seasons" has been terrible. They last several months each, cost $10 and there is absolutely NO content. Each season has it's repeatable activity and you're expected to grind that activity ad nauseam until the next season, rinse repeat. They slap on one or two cut scenes and ... that's it. That's a season. ALL of Bungie's efforts and energy have been put into the Eververse Store (micro transactions). Eververse continues to get all of the best cosmetics, ghosts, items, etc. while the base game continues to get the barest minimum, which usually means old, recycled guns/armor from Year 1 Destiny 2 or Destiny 1. When people complain, Bungie continuously whines about how "hard it is" to put out a good product as if they were a small, indy studio.

I'd suggest only playing the F2P version of the game (New Light), but even that is hot trash. I've never seen a game as confusing, unhelpful, un-intuitive and flat-out anti-new player as New Light. You get one mission, then you're dropped off in the Tower. No explanation of what the Tower is, what's happening or what you're supposed to do. You get a mission to run around talking to people (vendors), but even then you can't get missions/bounties from them. You have to run that specific activity (which it doesn't even really tell you to do), THEN you get a mission to talk to them, then you get a mission to PLAY the activity (again), THEN you get "intro" bounties (which you get by going back to talk to them a 3rd time). You HAVE to do these intro bounties to fully unlock each vendor. You can't pick up shaders, emblems, pinnacle/ritual weapon missions, etc. until you've fully unlocked that vendor.

The best part is that NONE of this has ANYTHING to do w/ any of the campaign or story missions. To start those, you have to talk to a random vendor in a far off part of the Tower, i.e. NOT in the center of the Tower, where a vendor like that SHOULD be. You don't HAVE to do any of the campaign(s) ... but ALL of the planets are locked and they only slowly unlock as you play the various matched gamemodes: Crucible (pvp), Vanguard Strikes (dungeons) or Gambit (a mixed pve/pvp mode). Of course, there's nothing really explaining what any of these things are ... they just expect you to figure that out all on your own.

This is EASILY one of the worst New Player experiences I have ever seen.\t\t\t\t\t
