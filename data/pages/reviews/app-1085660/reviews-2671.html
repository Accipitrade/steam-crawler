\r\n\t\t\t\t\t New expansion Shadowkeep is underwhelming in terms of pure content, but the gameplay is as solid as ever with more armor customizations and builds than ever before in Destiny 2. Going f2p breathes a lot of new life into the game, and the game is a grind-fest but lots of fun with friends as you guys work together to do exotic quests for awesome guns, high level nightfalls and raids for pinnacle gears, or just ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ around in jump puzzles or the open world. The gunplay feels great, and the three classes do feel distinct, with a few further distinct differences via their subclass. The gunplay feels great as expected of Bungie, but the game is clearly a "console-first" game with poorly though out UI and lots of loading screens. You are limited in the amount of quests and bounties you can have, up to 63, and some quest items are in your inventory while some quest items are in your quest log, making juggling quest space a real problem. Having the mobile or pc app to change gears is the best way to play the game if you don't want to travel back and forth loading screens just to deposit weapons and trade weapons between toons. Another problem is the small fireteam sizes for most activities.
Inevitable comparisons to Warframe will occur, with which I can only say that this game is much more casual and simplified, which is a good thing in my case as a busy working adult. There are daily bounties and what not, and weekly rewards for activities, none of which are overly important and are pretty optional save for pinnacle gears for the endgame grind. In terms of movement, the game has a few "bugs" that are almost similar to Warframe's crazy movement, such as sword launching with Warlock or sword flying with Titan. Overall, Warframe is the Dota 2 to Destiny 2's League of Legends in terms of complexity, and while Destiny 2's gunplay feels amazing, Warframe has the crazier and better feeling movement.\t\t\t\t\t
