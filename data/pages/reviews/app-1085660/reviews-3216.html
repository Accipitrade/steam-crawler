\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere's A LOT of people who seem to not fully understand what an MMO is, and how grinding incorporates to an MMO. NO, this is NOT an MMO, but it has traits FROM it anyway. Grinding will be a pain, and if you're not used to it, then I'm sorry, but you're SOL. It's been a part of the game since Destiny 1 on consoles, and it's still around today.

HOWEVER, they have recently released transmog 2.0, which literally allows you to customize your legendary (purple rarity) armor to look HOWEVER you want within a certain limit. This means that now, you can grind for whatever stat you'd like, and make it look however you want it to look.

This game has a lot of content for a casual, or even hardcore player. There may be times when content is lackluster or weak, but it's still content to play. Especially as a new user.. You have a lot to do, my friend. Players who spend too much time will run out of things to do. Quickly. Again, it's something that Destiny has had for a while, but it's still a trait from MMO games in general. Not a whole lot we can do as users here, but I feel like Bungie has been giving things a good flow.

Crucible (PVP) is very fun, and not too much to complain about unless you're one of those players who nags over meta builds/weapons/what have you. There are weapons that are better than average, but there's so many different combinations, that I'm sure you'll eventually get your hands on a few. The meta is always changing, as far as specific guns go, so don't sweat it, just have FUN. People forget this in Destiny.

Competitive is hit or miss. There are (were) a lot of hackers in Trials of Osiris, which is just skill-based matchmaking for some really nice rewards. The skill-gap can be VERY high. I used to think I was above average until I came into these matches. To each their own here, but it's something I've seen complaints about.

TL;DR - Yes. IMO there's very few negatives unless you're a competitive player, and a lot of fun stuff to do.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI want to love this game for its visuals, story line & lore, amazing quality sounds and everything else that makes this game an amazing time sync MMO, BUT this game has so many flaws and so many of those flaws are centered in the PVP side of the game, which is my favourite part of almost any game.

Invulnerability frames for some of the classes (all, except Hunter) one hit kill mechanics, which sometimes take no skill. Melee attacks which are auto-directed to the closest player or any other target. Lack of rewards for the PVP players and lack of content for PVP in general.

PVE-wise and event-wise this game is great, especially if you have people to play with.

All in all, this is a great f2p game, but the flaws in PVP and sometimes lack of content for the players-who-dont-have-5-other-friends makes this a non-recommended game for me.

I will still play it and still enjoy parts of it, but as for the big picture - I would not recommend this as a game for anyone else to spend major amount of time in.\t\t\t\t\t
