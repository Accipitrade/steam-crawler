\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt has a high learning curve (think souls games) for new players in both pve and pvp but the game is very rewarding for players who can get past initial learning curve. I wouldn't recommend it for the pvp experience because you will likely be stomped by vets easily with better gear and stats since the game doesn't have any Skill based matchmaking (horrible, I know).

People often ask me what the end game is in Destiny 2... with nearly 3k hours, I think the true end game of destiny 2 is collecting god rolled guns (perfectly rolled guns) and just having fun in various activities. To me, it's a game about collecting stuff. The Raids and end game pve content are all relatively short and can be completed within an hour with a competent group. This game has a huge, highly active LFG Channel in Discord that I use often to find groups.

The PVE community for the most part are very friendly and helpful, the most toxic players I've ever ran into in this game are from pvp or guild drama related man-childs. I've had many good pve memories playing this game, some in pvp as well but much less so.

Even though I would compare this game to mmorpg like WoW, it's more a mix of MMO/Looter-Shooter/ARPG. It's definitely one of a kind since it's first person for the most part.

Although, it's labeled as a F2P game, it is definitely not. It's more like Pay yearly for the expansion with a seasonal pass system. F2P is very limited and is more like an extended demo. If you enjoy the game, you will be missing out alot if you don't buy all the expansions and seasonal passes.

The worst part about this game is the terrible new player experience (game doesn't explain anything to you, basically everything you learn is from experience or mentors), no skill based matchmaking in pvp and vaulting. Vaulting was introduced in Beyond Light expansion where old content you paid for goes away for a undetermined period of time. I personally hate it because old exotic missions that were awesome are just suddenly vaulted, same goes for the old raids.

I'd still recommend the game after all that if you enjoy committing to games. Destiny 2 is the opposite of quick satisfaction games like Fortnite or PUBG.\t\t\t\t\t
