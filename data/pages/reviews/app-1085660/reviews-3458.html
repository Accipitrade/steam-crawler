\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI previously recommended Destiny 2 because it was free-to-play and provided free access to a lot of historical content that used to be locked behind really annoying and confusing paywalls. When the free-to-play release occurred, things were a lot clearer. Free players could explore every destination and play the first three campaigns from 2017-2018 and get all of their associated loot.

None of that is true anymore. Half of those destinations were now removed from the game, even for those who paid over $100 for them. No one can access them at all. The first three campaigns are just gone now. The annoying and confusing paywalls are back, just in a different way.

Bungie's claim is that this is because of the game losing scope and being way too large to download. Warframe, its biggest competitor in this space, manages triple the content at half the file size of Destiny 2. I don't know what's going on at Bungie, but clearly they don't know what they're doing anymore.

I enjoyed certain aspects of Destiny 2, but I can't recommend this game given how they screw over their playerbase.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhenever you hear the word "Destiny" you think of the most ambitious science-fictional game that Bungie has ever attempted; a universe so vast with such a unique mythos and story that it will even given Mass Effect, Halo, and even Star Wars and Star Trek a run for it's money. Sadly that wasn't the case; instead we got something different that took years upon years of improvement for better or for worse.

'Destiny' has had a long and strange ride from first conception to now. For this review i will split it between the "PvE" and "PvP" portion just to keep things organized...

PvE

You are Guardian. A peacekeeper of the Light basically. And you go around protecting the universe against those who are against the Light. And that's pretty much it; the writing and character whether it be Activision's fault or Bungie's fault isn't all that great. The lore might be, but boy the story and character is much to be desire. However, if you look pass the horrendous story and character. This is a fun loot-shooter as you play with other players or with friends as you beat baddies with awesome powers and looking cool while doing it.

There are a ton of PvE stuff for you to and quite frankly the way the game is structured NOW. In an odd strange way, it's less tedious and boring, rather more engaging and fun! While of course you can eye-roll at the characters, writing, etc. Overall one of the core strengths of "Destiny: New Light" is the gunplay which is most excellent here.

PvP

The PvP portion is a bit tricky, this isn't COD or a Battlefield multiplayer, not even a Halo one or Gears of War; the best way to describe it is the better gear you have gotten over the course of your PvE experience, the better chances you have of winning or at least doing good. Obviously team-work is key; but so is the gear and your abilities, etc. So in other words; there should be no room for complaining. Just grind through PvE stuff to get better stuff for Crucible.

Gambit is a combination of PvP & PvE elements as you fight enemies and other players with a Dark Souls style invasion system. This mode at first glance can be fun but unless you got a good team that knows how to specifically play to Gambit's style of gameplay you will find yourself on the losing side a lot.

Outro

Despite the roller-coasting writing and characters. The only other weakness is the technical issues from gameplay to just outright server issues. If you can look pass the server issues and the jaded writing, etc. Overall, to me. Bungie has done a lot of wonders since first conception and i personally can't wait to see what they do next.
