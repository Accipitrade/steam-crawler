\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGrind to get abilities so that you can grind to gain rep so that you can grind to get weapons so that you can grind for exotic drops so that you can realize you don't have five friends who play it to do raids with. Absolutely baffling monetization and purchase schemes will lead you to very special confusion about how to access any of the five million game activities. The game will never, ever bother to explain itself or those activity mechanics to you. The transmog system is weirdly complex and stingy, and definitely designed to extract more cash from you. Bungie "vaulted" aka "removed entirely from the game" its best storyline, featuring a character voiced by Nathan Fillion. Absolutely confusing remnants of this plotline exist in "strikes", which are match-made dungeons you'll spend hopelessly trying to keep up with the bored people speed-running an experience they've endured maybe a hundred times already. To manage your inventory, you will need to run at least one of about a dozen external applications built by players using Bungie's often bogged-down API.

And yet, it's shiny, and the moment by moment play loops are extremely fun. There's lots of loot to collect and categorize, so if that's your jam this game has it at Diablo levels. The writing is at times excellent if you like space opera. I understand why I've spent this many hours in it, even though at another level I will never understand it.\t\t\t\t\t
