\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you like looter shooters (with the emphasis on the "shooter" part), Bungie is still one of the best at making a satisfying and crisp feeling shooter from top to bottom... guns feel great to use, and since the change back to (mostly) random rolls, the quest for the "perfect roll" goes on. Gameplay is tight and well-balanced considering the variety of types and rolls of weapons available. Movement and animations are top-notch. Crucible (pvp) has come a loooong way since launch but balance can still get a little wacky at times, some loadouts and strategies may seem "meta" and overused, but don't let that keep you from experimenting with "unorthodox" setups in the PvP sections of the game. There are certain challenging activities like raids that may require specific loadouts, but don't let anyone tell you how to play. I have lots more to say about PvP since I'm mostly a PvP player but I'll try to keep this short. I hadn't played a competitive pvp shooter since the original modern warfare days so I sucked at first, but I kept playing. Got better and better a little bit at a time and I'm closing in on 1.5 K/D overall which is a big accomplishment for me playing mostly solo.

As far as PvE content goes (admittedly less time spent on this)... Exotic and unique weapon quests can get repetitive at times with continuous grinding of old content, but this game is a space-magic shooter where loot is king, so grab a group of friends (irl preferably) or on LFG and go have some fun. Most things are perfectly attainable yourself but I just prefer to do PvE with friends. I haven't even mentioned Gambit which is kind of a hybrid mode between crucible and PvE. Not my cup of tea. It's fun, but you need a semi-coordinated team for the best results (kinda like in a Raid)

Get out there Guardian. Games been out for going on 3 years now, there's lots to do. No excuses.\t\t\t\t\t
