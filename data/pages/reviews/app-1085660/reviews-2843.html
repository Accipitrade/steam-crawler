\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving spent many hours while this was a Blizzard game I can say without a doubt this is a fun game.
The steam version is just as good (mostly because it’s exactly the same game)
In terms of gameplay it feels smooth, guns have reasonable recoil, enemies aren’t too hard to kill (with the exception of some bosses), level progression feels reasonable and weapons scale appropriately with enemies.
Though this game can become really grindy at a few points. (Mostly when you’re trying to complete quests for legendary/exotic weapons) and PvP is pretty BS until you can match the power levels of the players in it. (Usually in the 900s) But starting from 750 you can pretty easily make your way there by completing the story and some side missions.
Overall I’d say it’s worth playing (mostly because it’s free and a lot of content is included.)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI really really wanna give this game a YES recommendation, because I really do find a not-insignificant portion of it quite enjoyable, and the base gameplay is as solid as everyone says it is... but the FOMO in Destiny 2 is possibly the worst I've ever experienced, and if you're like me and instinctively treat dailies and weeklies as obligations of sorts regardless of how much you actually need them, this game will chew you up and spit you out. Bounties, rotating cosmetic inventories, timed events everywhere, and now a vastly increased amount of seasonal exclusives in general... This game is teaching me how to prioritize, and how to say "enough is enough, I do NOT need to complete all of these things every single day/week/whatever". It's a magical experience getting used to the world of D2, but after a solid year of addiction I'm burning out faster than I ever did with Warframe.

And then I should probably bring up the horrible new player experience that Bungie introduced this season- if you don't have friends with you (and this is definitely one of those games where everything becomes immensely more enjoyable if you've got a couple buddies helping you around), you'll more likely than not wind up floundering around with no idea where to go, because the main questline is a barebones introduction to the game's more basic activities and all the old campaigns have been relegated to poor Amanda Holiday in her lonely hidey-hole in the Tower hangar. If you can find friends to come with you, check out the game. If you can't... I hate to say this, but you might be better off waiting until Bungie figures out what it wants to do with this game. It's taken over half a decade now; when will it end?\t\t\t\t\t
