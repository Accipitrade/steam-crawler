\r\n\t\t\t\t\tLemme pre-face by saying I stuck with Destiny since the Destiny 1 Beta on PS3 (later switched to the PS4 version) and I loved the concept from day 1. I had a ♥♥♥♥ job at the time and the game helped me cope, then helped me through a phase of unemployment, settling into a new job, losing that job too after 2 years and finally ending up with the job I'm currently at. So I had a huge investment into Destiny 1, both time and emotionally wise. Few games hold my attention that long, and The Taken King was the absolute highlight, a high point of story and new gameplay elements. But the last expansion (Rise of Iron) didn't do much for me and I moved on after beating it, not engaging into post-game.

When Destiny 2 was announced I was hyped, I pre-ordered the game for PS4 and I enjoyed it...for a while. Once I reached Base-game post game however I saw how shallow the game became gameplay wise, how much more grindy it became. The Red War storyline was pretty good as far as Destiny Story Expansions go, and I didn't dislike the subtle changes to gameplay (gun feeling, movement, new defensive moves), but the post game was barren, and the first expansion (Curse of Osiris) hinted at how hamstrung the game was, and I dropped the game once again, having a lot of disdain for what Destiny became.

Whether it was all Activision-Blizzards fault for wanting the game cut up, a focus on microtransactions and sticking to an unsustainable 10 year timetable, or the Team at Bungie not being able to deliver on their high promises and ideas is for debate. I think both are at fault.

Meanwhile, I finally got myelf a good gaming pc in 2019, and later that year got back into Destiny 2, giving it one last chance after hearing about their cut ties with Activision-Blizzard and hearing alternating praise and critique of the game.

Aaaaand...thats really where we are. Praise and Critique. Destiny 2 is a mixed bag of good and bad, moreso than other games. Sometimes it has pretty good story and storytelling, other times it becomes shallow and paper thin. Just like with Destiny 1, it has a few weak expansions (Curse of Osiris, Warmind) and some pretty good ones (Forsaken, Shadowkeep). The gameplay overall pretty solid (movement, gunplay, class abilities, different game modes) but a lot of the game is very grindy (especially exotic quests) and often quite buggy. Not to mention when you can SEE the negative space where they probably did want to add a feature or quest or whatever but didn't, for whatever reason (Chosen of the Hunt's measly 4 targets you can hunt, or how the "Dark Vanguard" is featured front and center in promo material and trailers...but doesn't get involved in the storyline of the expansion, past one cutscene and some pep talk). And the dev's vision for this game seems muddy at times, unclear and undecided. Especially seasonal events suffer from this. Not to mention the bane of Sunsetting content and equipment (though it seems they learned their lesson there...somewhat).

Like...Destiny 2 is THE most buggy game I currently play and I honestly cannot even tell WHY in most cases (as in, why the bugs happen, not why they are there...we'll come to that). Sometimes the game doesn't want me to snoop around in a certain area and will crash, giving me an error code. Sometimes it will crash and not give me a code. Sometimes it will freeze upon trying to enter social space, but still play sounds and won't let itself be closed unless you brute force it via task master. I tried connecting once to a group of friends, but the connection took AGES to establish and once I was...the game crashed, saying my connection is bad. Had that one happen a few times, and WHEN such a connection error appears, trying to reconnect is impossible. I get booted back to the title screen, press a button, it connects, says its signing in...then says my connection to character server was cut. Closing the game and restarting usually fixes that, but I had cases where it didn't, and I couldnt play the game for an entire week.

While I am generally happy Bungie got off Activision-Blizzards leash...I feel that the lack of money, manpower, technical support or server structure they MAY have given Bungie to work with is pretty easy to feel. When I started playing Destiny 2 on PS4, I never had technical problems, never had connection problems (and I played the game back then on a wireless connection with WORSE internet), the game ran super optimized. Hell, when I started playing on PC, the game ran better...and then gradually became worse, more buggy, more unstable. I used to run it on highest, costum settings, now I have to play on High or else it crashes constantly...but the graphics didn't improve drastically or anything.

So, why do I still give a thumbs up for this game if I just tore it and its creators down.

For one, because, as sad as it sounds, this is THE Looter Shooter if you enjoy this kinda genre. The Borderlands series, Planetside 2 and Warframe both came first, but all three have aged worse than Destiny 1/2. Defiance and Firefall tried to compete but both are dead now. Division 1/2 have their fans but it seems they arent getting any support or content expansions anymore. And Anthem...yeah we all know where that went. Outriders may be worth checking out but from what I heard, that game is also having bad server and bug problems right now. But yeah, despite its often messy execution, Destiny 2 IS the game to play if you crave this style of Shooter.

As I also mentioned, when the game IS working, its generally a blast to play. The 3 classes do feel different, and their subclasses offer enough variety even if you only play on one character. The gunplay is pretty excellent, guns generally sound, look and feel pretty good. Hell, the game in general sounds good. The music is excellent, the sound effects nice and crisp and punchy, voice-acting is high quality with good actors. The graphics are, even on low, appealing, with a distinct visual design style to everything. And if you are into games with lore, this game has a lot of it. Mileage may vary, I know lots of people hate the idea of reading through lore in in-game (or out-of-game in Destiny 1's case) codexes and databases or on various equipment, but what is here is generally well written and sometimes goes pretty deep and meta, with cross references to older Bungie games (Halo, Myth, Marathon). There is a reason there is an entirely fan-made site that collects all Lore entries from Destiny 1 and 2, and dozen of youtube creators making videos entirely based on its lore.

Lastly, the game IS free. Yes you need to buy a season pass/expansion to play a good chunk of the content, but if you just want to know if the gameplay itself is anything that could interest you, download the game, play through the beginners campaign, maybe some PvP, and then decide whether you feel investing more time and money into it is worth it.

Regardless, I would recommend not playing the game too much. I did, and slaved myself to the seasonal events (which generally last 2-3 months) to max out the season "battle" pass. I started burning out quickly. Now I only play 1-2 times a week, preferrably with my brother, and we approach the game a bit more casual. I guess that much holds true for most "life-style" games and MMOs.

tl;dr review: Game is flawed but still enjoyable, try out for free, don't become addicted.\t\t\t\t\t
