\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTo say these games were among my favorite games ever would be an understatement. I have close to 2000 in game hours, and 3000 total hours spread across both games. Most of it is on Xbox (look up GT: Spartan6056 on Destiny Tracker for proof). I have made some awesome memories playing these games. However, with the direction this franchise has been taking, it hurts me to say I can no longer recommend this game. This will be a long one, so strap in.

The seasonal content system has absolutely destroyed this game. If I had to describe this game now, it would be FOMO. FOMO stands for "fear of missing out". It is a system in which people only continue playing the game because they don't want to, well, miss out on the content that is only there temporarily. Every new season brings out a small set of new weapons, abilities, and activities, but at the end of each season it is all whisked away, and the content is made completely unobtainable until an unspecified later date. That is if they even decide to bring the content back at all. The season pass itself is also poorly executed. It is almost impossible to reach max rank on the pass playing normally. Normal activities give so little xp they are not worth doing. Instead, most players have to spend hours grinding out simple bounties like "kill x enemies with y element" and so on.

What this all comes together to create is a game that demands to be treated as a part time job. If you want to get the most out of the game, you have to log on each week, run through the weekly chore list checklist, and grind out some bounties so you don't run out of time by the end of the season. Multiply all that by 3 if you have multiple characters.

If you choose to play the game casually, you are punished. You will not be able to get all of the new weapons. You won't be able to unlock the new abilities, and you won't get to play the end game content because they keep raising the power level every season so you are forced to log on every week to once again run through the chore list checklist to upgrade your gear. If you decide to take a break from the game, you will almost assuredly miss out on all the content from the current season.

Eventually, this brought me to a sense of I don't want to grind this game hardcore this season, so I guess I'll skip out on it entirely because I'd miss out on the content either way. This feeling snowballs each season as I miss more and more content until I don't want to play the game anymore because I've already missed out on so much. The thing about FOMO is it only works until the player gets burned out and hits a breaking point. After that point, the player will drop the game entirely and never look back.

This problem would not be as bad if the game didn't rely solely on the seasonal content, but the base game is so devoid of any content aside from raids that seasonal content is the only thing keeping this game alive. Even most exotics and their catalysts are locked behind seasonal quests and activities now. Long gone are the days of new exotics being able to drop randomly for any activity. So now you can't even get new exotics if you miss out on the season. Almost all previous DLC content, such as past raids or dungeons, are irrelevant as they do not offer powerful gear, so they just go to waste.

The fall DLCs have been pitiful since Shadowkeep, and they seem to get smaller each year, and the little content they do have is usually received poorly. Bungie seems to have recognized this, so now they have focused on bringing back old Destiny 1 content rather than trying to create better original content. Over this last year, they have brought back D1 strikes, armor, weapons, map locations, and even the Vault of Glass is coming next season. Bringing a little bit of content back from D1 for nostalgia is fine, but it shouldn't be the selling point of each season. Bungie might as well just abandon D2 and start creating more content for D1 at this point.

There are a lot of other problems with this game such as sunsetting (which I didn't mention because it's getting removed), lack of strike loot or a vendor refresh, and the strange obsession with making lost sectors end game content, but those could be easily fixed in a season or a DLC. I still believe the main thing killing the game is the seasonal model that relies on using shallow, temporary content to force the players to continue playing in fear of missing out rather than providing a steady stream of good content that builds on itself each season. However, I am smart enough to realize that the seasonal model is never going away, which is why I believe this game (and franchise) is a lost cause. At least D1 is still locked in time and I can revisit it whenever I want.\t\t\t\t\t
