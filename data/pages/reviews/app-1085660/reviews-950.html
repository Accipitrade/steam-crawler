\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, just a soul-sucking freemium game at this point. It's a game to play because you have other friends that play Destiny. It's amazing that they took a game with such fun gun-play and abilities and made the core gameplay grinding the same 3 activities you've been grinding since the first game in 2014.

Story is nonsensical at best, every season introducing the new big bad that you manage to kill with a blue smg you found the mission before. The strikes are worse since the game wants you to play these dozens of times a week to get all of your challenges and bounties and quests done. Nothing worse than launching into a new season with a new DLC and having to run a strike that you've played, literally, a hundred times already.

PVP is a mess. The matchmaking system is a joke, full stop. If you lookup stats, there are people with 90-100% win rates. This isn't a testament to their ability, this is a testament to the matchmaking system not working. As a solo player, you will frequently be matched up against a full team of clanmates and get mercy-ruled.

What's most frustrating about Destiny 2 is that this game is great when it wants to be. Raids are fantastic... when you have 5 friends that want to play/haven't already played that week. Strikes can be a lot of fun... the first few times you do them before they turn into a monotonous chore. The gun-play is fantastic... until they nerf the guns you like, even essentially deleting some. Each class feels unique and powerful... until they nerf them until they are unplayable.

I could write a book on the nerfs in this game. Bungie's balancing philosophy has always been to nerf, and nerf fully, what is popular in PVP. I'm not a professional game designer, but a popular recommendation is to balance guns differently in PVP and PVE. Instead, if a gun is too popular in PVP, it is nerfed into the ground, frequently making it useless in both PVP and PVE. Same goes for different classes, including the newest subclass that released in the newest DLC. It's very frustrating to be enjoying a gun or ability in PVE, but have it ruined because it's overused or too strong in PVP, especially if you don't frequently play PVP. I'm a strong believer in balancing gameplay to make different play styles viable, but the full-scope nerfs that are so common in this game are too much.


I want to like this game; I've tried very hard to like this game. But I don't and I can't recommend it.\t\t\t\t\t
