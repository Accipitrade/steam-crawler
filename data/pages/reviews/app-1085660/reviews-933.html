\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBungie employs immensely talented programmers, artists, designers, engineers, etc. They've made an amazing game in a great engine. It's fast-paced, snappy, challenging, and above all, a dream to play. So why the negative review? Because upper management and lead designers have consistently taken all of the hard work from these employees and ruined it by implementing the worst decisions you could possibly make in a game like this. It's so fun to level up and amass great power to destroy the enemies with, but then you're gimped here and detoured there, delayed and gated all over the place, making you feel like you're always chasing the carrot on the stick. They implement "challenge" and "difficulty" mostly by ramping up damage taken and nerfing damage dealt, and you rarely get to enjoy the hero fantasy of being an all-powerful god-slayer, despite that being central to the plot of the game. They micromanage the player experience, forcing you to play how they want via methods like grinding and using mandated weapon types/loadouts, which flies in the face of the depth and breadth of the available powers, weapons, and play styles on offer. The drastic switch in tone between D1 and D2 was also jarring and disappointing. We went from sci-fi/horror/space opera to saturday morning cartoons. The writing went from being aimed at teens and adults to children and teens. The hardest part to deal with for me is the choice to gate so many of the activities from solo players. I'm 43 and I've had my fill of playing games with randos via LFG, after over a decade of doing so. There's nothing inherently wrong with using LFG sites, but with no in-game matchmaking, and the difficulty in finding a consistent, reliable clan, they're essentially forcing you to find strangers on third-party platforms in order to access the most compelling content. It's a brute-force method of trying to build and maintain player engagement and nothing else. I keep coming back because there are so few contemporary FPS games that feel this good to play, and then I'm always driven away again in frustration and disappointment. So much wasted potential.\t\t\t\t\t
