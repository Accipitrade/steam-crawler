\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI used to adore this game, with thousands of hours played across console, Battlenet and Steam. Destiny was an excellent experience with amazing gunplay. From stories to bring you into the universe you could then move on to repeatable experiences such as strikes, pvp crucible, gambit a hybrid of PvE and PvP of sorts, raids, dungeons, special missions. Connection you had with the world was deepened through secrets, special events and an excellent community. With the arrival of Shadowkeep expansion all of the content prior to Forsaken was made free and it was one of the best onboardings a player could get in gaming period. FOR FREE. All systems played into each other seamlessly and it felt like a cohesive unit. At its core is you. Your Guardian. I never felt connected to a character in a game as I did to my Warlock. It was a special experience that I would bore my friends with to death. And the seasons were getting good, if a bit repetitive, but the stories were better and better, smaller in scale, but fun, with cool weapons, characters etc.

All of that changed with the release of Witch Queen when a few things became apparent, and further releases of the year that have completely broken the structure of Destiny.

1) The seasonal model is the only thing that matters in Destiny currently. Recently during his GDC talk, Justin Truman, I believe talked about their approach to Destiny 2. To them Destiny 2 is a train station, where your goal is to keep players there for as long as possible with super quick delivery of content. Speed is more important than good or anything else really. And as they implemented this strategy something different happened to the game (in my opinion to be fair). Instead of a train station Destiny became a railroad, where you have one set path: seasonal activity and the season pass. The rest of the game doesn't matter. Where before strikes, crucible, gambit and the rest of the activities mattered, now they don't. You... really don't need to play anything else in the game. Matters aren't helped by the fact that the seasonal blueprint has remained the same to the point where players will pinpoint with baffling accuracy when and what will drop during the season. From exotics on the season pass, number of story missions, seasonal activity structure, reward structure, ALL of it is predictable on the rail experience.
2) This broke the Destiny magic for me, which is my second point. Due to the amount of resources that go into making of a season, the rest of the game suffered. Each expansion for example, will introduce a new location. The problem? That location is relevant during the campaign and then forgotten along with the rest of them. They sit there, with no reason to explore or roam them. They can be fun viewes into Destiny, but now they are just empty spaces used for nothing. Why do they exist really? Strikes as well are a poor shadow of what they meant before: guardians have became more powerful and capable as the time progressed, but strikes themselves have not gotten any significant upgrades in years. There are new strikes introduced with more enemies, but that's about it. One or two per year. There are harder modes that do test players, but with little incentive to actually do them. Rewards are often old weapons that we get for... no reason at all really. PvP is in a sad state as well. I don't think I have played it for 4-5 months? In an effort to ground players and add depth Bungie introduced Airborne Effectivness a stat that punishes your... aim? when you are in air. The problem was that it is too punishing and it killed gunplay in the game. It is not as noticable in PvE but in PvP... Wow. Additional issues range from poor structure, poor rewards and honestly... lack of maps. One map per year is the best thing that Bungie can bring to PvP as a significant update. There has been implementation of a competitive playlist but, I'm not sure ppl should bother because well there's a lack of rewards, aside from an old handcanon everyone gets as a participation. And Gambit hasn't seen a significant update since Beyond Light where it LOST 4 maps.
Why do I mention all of these? Because they are CORE of Destiny. These are the things we play when we burn through an expansion or a seasonal activity. It's a fun pass time that further engages you with the game or the world, and it's gone.
Another way this has been felt is with the lack of secrets and missions aside from the paid track ones. Cool missions people discovered and played with their friends such as Whisper and Zero Hour, community puzzles are things that have stuck with me for years, and I still remember when I got my Whisper and helped someone else get it. We don't get that anymore. Puzzles that unite the community no longer exist. These are the things that made us feel that connection and it's not there. Players and the community have been cut off from the world of Destiny.
3) The payer character is another point of separation. Granted this might be the point most of the community will disagree with, but it's something I feel strongly about. This is the year Bungie revamped the 3 classes, and I for the 99% of the time play one of them: Warlock. Warlocks have always had an excellent kit for PvE, with other classes lagging behind. This year Hunters and Titans were brought up, rightfully so, but for this to be done some aspects of Warlock's kit has been spread out amongst the classes. But aside from some shuffling about and cuts Warlocks didn't get much in terms of flavor or something truly new and unique that would separate them from the other 2, leaving the class feeling bland. Class identity has been shattered for me, and that has cut me off from my character. New system was suposed to add more customization to the game, but ironically the set up really gives Warlocks one thing to do: drop a Well. I'm not sure about other classes in the game to be honest, but the system left me feeling bad. Detached from something I felt so connected to. Character wasn't mine anymore, and that character isn't part of the world in turn. My feelings on the situation weren't helped by responce given by Joe Blackburn, game director, when he stated that this sentiment was part of an internet echo chamber and things will change when loadout system is introduced. I don't think he wanted to tell ppl to eff off, as there was a lot of work put into this, but it still felt sad to read that.
4) Weapon crafting, or to be more precise the grind for it. In Destiny grind for the ultimate weapon is the essence of the game. All players want to strive for the best of the best equipment and to gather most of them they can. Weapon crafting introduced a way for all of us to get those perfect weapons with BETTER effects and perks than ever before. The problem is the system behind it. To get one you need to unlock it through Weapon Patterns which are special versions of the weapon that you collect, use and then throw away to unlock its craftable version. Once you DO unlock it you then have to further grind that weapon to bring it up to 16th lvl or so to unlock all of its perks to create its ultimate form. And to do that you need to grind for materials, but to get those ultimate perks you have to use special material that is in limited supply. That's for one weapon. Days if not weeks of play to get ONE of them just as more come in. While you don't have to do this, the entire system and set up prays on mentality of an average Destiny player, but also works against it as more and more is introduced. I don't think that Bungie intended things to be this way, but it kind of broke our relationship with weapons accidentally. What used to be a moment of joy now is just another thing to be throw away in the eternal grind for the perfect roll of the season... that will be ingnored as new season drops.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave been a hardcore Destiny player since the release of first title. I have had some of my best gaming memories and moments completing raids, strikes, etc. With over 2k hours between all platforms, I can say with confidence this is one of the best PvE looter-shooters out there. Albeit the game has its quirks (particularly with PvP balancing and overall staleness of Crucible atm) and can definitely cause you to burn out, this is especially true once you have played through all the story content. Destiny is a great game with interesting storytelling, amazing visuals, and very good gunplay mechanics. If you are willing to shell out the money to buy the expansions, and with sunsetting of DLC in the past (according to recent Bungie statements) there is an unlimited amount of content to play through. With the base game being free, why not try it out and see for yourself?\t\t\t\t\t
