Short update to my review. Although they seem to have been supporting this game with finally introducing Cross Play/Cross Save, instead of buying the DLC and it being accessible across your account, you have to buy the same DLC on any platform you want to play on. So if I primarily play on steam, but sometimes want to go play on the big screen in my recliner on PS5, I would have to buy the DLC on both to be able to actually play on both. Again Money Grab which really sucks.

From Bungie website: "DLC - including expansions, the Annual Pass and seasons - will be locked to the platform you purchased it with. To play that content on other platforms, you have to purchase it again on those platforms. This includes expansions such as Forsaken, Shadowkeep, and Beyond Light, and any Annual Passes"

I'd like to start my review by stating that I used to be a devoted destiny player. I beta tested the original destiny and even platinumed it on the PlayStation 4. I played this game every day, and loved the community . I met many new friends playing this game online, and even though I was disappointed by the ending of the original, I knew that there would be more to come. I also put in over 40 hrs into Destiny 2 on PS4 (This is before I got back into PC gaming). This is where the disappointment started.
You see when Bungie stated that destiny would be a 10 year game, I thought that they would be consistently releasing new content and DLC's to continue the story. I was wrong. What they ended up doing was trying to turn this into a cash cow. Instead of releasing DLC's, they decided to make sequels . Now yes they did actually release DLC’s, but in the first game, the majority of the DLC's were crap. Even though I was disappointed that they were creating sequels instead of just releasing DLC's to continue the story , I was excited to play more destiny. As I started to play destiny 2 I realize how disappointed I was that they removed so much of that hard earned content from destiny one that I had earned. I had emblems , etc that were very rare, yet for some reason I could no longer use any of that in destiny 2. Certain weapons like the gallahorn were no longer available, even though you had to work sometimes months ( I had a friend with the worst luck and got the gallahorn about three months before destiny two launched ) to get this weapon. What a disappointment. It just went to show me that no matter what I earn in destiny 2, I would probably lose it in their next iteration of the game. Then Bungie decided to leave activation which I was a excited for. I felt destiny 2 would finally become the game that I always wanted. Bungie would finally be able to give us more content that we were requesting, and Bungie announcing that this game was going free to play was amazing. I figured they've copied so much from Warframe, why not the free to play? I thought by going free to play they would definitely release more content , weapons, gear, etc to make up for the money lost in sales. How wrong was I?
They released the original content of destiny 2 for free and started selling dlc’s and expansions at full “new game” retail price. Now why would I go and pay full price for an expansion, when it should be priced at expansion prices? Especially since when destiny 3 comes out I'm going to lose everything I've earned in this game. How far is that from free to play? The thing that is really pissed me off more than anything is that if you are a loyal destiny player, and you support Bungie, they reward you with crap. There are no bonuses or thank you. The only benefits they give are to new players by offering them everything all wrapped up in one package for the same price you pay just to get the expansion. This review can go on and on because there's so much wrong with such a great concept. Don't get me wrong, this is an amazing game. There is so much potential here, but greed has interfered with all of that. Everything is pretty much blocked behind a paywall, because even if you don't want the DLC's, there is So much is blocked by pay walls . You can't do any of the new content, or even get up to the new light levels without purchasing the game and the expansions. You are limited a lot, unless you pay into their DLC's and expansions. I would suggest waiting a couple years from this games launch to buy the all-in-one. Yes, destiny 3 will probably be around the corner, but it's not like anything you earn in this game will be carried over anyways. With that said I have to regrettably say that this game is not recommended at least until it's been out for a couple years.\t\t\t\t\t
