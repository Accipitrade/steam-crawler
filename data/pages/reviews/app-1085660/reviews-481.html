I would love to find another first-person social shooter as good. But there aren't any. (Defiance was excellent in its day.)

You can try the game for free and there is a ton of content. But once you commit, you will need to buy the annual story, and also four different seasons a year. get this as one annual pack for about â‚¬100 and enjoy the next year of gaming without being nickeled-and-dimed. Everything in the in-game store is cosmetic and unnecessary.

In 2022-23 there have been many quality of life improvements, including costume customisation and loadout saving. There are now five different elements for each of three classes, any one of which can be built/played many different ways. No-one will ever run out of options here!

The number of unique guns and armor pieces is in the hundreds and, unlike other games, these are all truly unique, with mechanics, sounds, skins, etc. you haven't seen before. Most are easy enough to get... it only takes time.

The Witch Queen story was amazing. Lightfall was extremely poor. But though the writing is all over the shop, the game-play remains the best in the industry.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAfter 1600 hours, I think it's time I give my two cents on this game.
Over the last 4-5 years, Destiny 2 has gone through many different changes, from the way "power" is calculated to randomized loot rewards and dungeons that give powerful exotics. As a person who played from day one on the original game, its fair to say I've seen this game evolve in ways most players haven't. Here are a few things that I think set Destiny apart from most FPS games, let alone looters that occupy the same space.

The gun play: Best in class for me. Movement is fluid and with different assists the game gives you, makes you feel like a killing machine. Each type of weapon has its own inherent characteristics which separate themselves even within its own gun type. There are also a myriad of choices when it comes to weapons of war. I would rate it a 9/10 for variety and identity within each class of weapons.

The world: Destiny gives you a massive sandbox spread across several planets to explore and engage with. Some planets feel as if they didn't receive the same level of attention, but for the most part are well fleshed out. From public events to take part in at the over world level, to lost sectors that function as tiny dungeons that reward loot at the end of them (almost always trash unless at end game difficulities), there are a number of different ways to team up and slay out. I must say here though that the world building team at bungie do a great job with the actual worlds themselves. They are beautiful and deserve more praise than they're given. 10/10 for the environment team, really hats off!

The Loot: This is the part of my review that turns a bit negative. If I didn't know that the loot system in this game was changing 1 week from the time I'm writing this review, I would be more wary in my recommendation of this game. Loot can be very disappointing in Destiny 2. From the way they gate the player in getting "chances" at exotics, to the inability to share loot (still not changing btw), its incredibly frustrating trying to get anything serviceable at much higher difficulties. At this moment, there are no reliable ways to "target farm" the items that you're searching for, In a round about way, yes, but it isn't very approachable for most gamers. so in this regard, destiny 2 gets a 3/10 for loot. Much of this will change with the Witch Queen DLC, but for now, its pretty ass.

All in all, aside from minor issues, Destiny 2 is a big, expansive universe to explore with your friends, or make new ones as you tackle raids together in a 6 man format. There are gripes yes, but overall there is a great game here for people who haven't jumped in yet. Just know it is a massive time investment if you really want the most out of this game.
