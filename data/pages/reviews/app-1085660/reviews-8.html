\r\n\t\t\t\t\tImagine a game series that has the potential to become the best in its genre but continuously never reaches close to that at all. That is Destiny.

This game has these moments where you just feel great, combat and movement just combine and its so fun. But the moments are far and few. Harder content boils down to "do x mechanic to unlock boss for damage".
The game very rarely does anything outside of "sit around and unload damage" and just lacks any meaningful mechanics.

The story is ok. The narrative bounces between incredible to painfully bad. But the world building is incredibly solid and will always be Destiny's strongest point.

Visually the game is great but it has grown stagnant. The seasonal content is entirely made of rehashed content and mash ups from existing places. This extends to weapons, with almost all new weapons being either reskins of the same weapons from Vanilla D2 or weapons brought back from the content removal of Beyond Light. It makes the game stagnant and underwhelming. They seems to only have one 3D modeller capable of producing armour as there is so little added each time, it kinda sucks.


PvP has rotted away from a lack of updates. Nothing is added with the only changes are small balance adjustments if something ends up overpowered. Its crap, PvP is what keeps a lot of people playing in between content drops

Strikes have also been abandoned. They still haven't brought them to the level that Destiny 1 left the system at and each expansion adds 1 or 2, so I do hope you haven't already played the same strikes for years on end!

Gambit exists, but Bungie will pretend it doesn't. its stupid and Bungie should be receiving a lot more crap for leaving it alone.


Seasonal content has become entirely bland and boring and its a really big issue of the game that has recently really started to pile up. The seasonal content of itself is ok. But the content is very grindy and the time gating forces player time in a really engaging way. The content bounces heavily in quality between seasons, but the format makes it feel like chore rather than active content to play. However all of that is shadowed by what doing content like this is. By having all content made in 4 months, only to last 4 months of player time and then thrown away once all 4 have finished has lead to: The narrative being unapproachable, playable content being uninspired and the base game receiving no content or updates. Its incredibly problematic but it makes Bungie too much money for them to ever improve, do more or change

Next, the monetisation. Its really really bad. The base game is a demo of the game since anything of importance requires "expansions". Shadowkeep and Beyond Light are laughable, they plainly suck to play last barely any time and add very little. There is no reason these should be more than £5 and should be base game by this point. The other 2 expansions (Witch Queen and Lightfall) do justify paying more for, they do have a proper campaign to play through and Witch Queen's campaign is Peak Destiny. So to get the full game you have to buy 4 expansions, then there are 2 dungeons (3 player raid-likes) in a dungeon key, then there are the current seasons (either bought separate for £15 each or with the current expac's Annual pass edition) then the current Expac's Dungeon key. Then ontop of that there is a constantly stocked in game store that is overpriced as heck and each event is just a money spending festival. To be entirely frank, they make incredible ammounts of money and not much of that goes into the game, clearly.

Overall, I want to like this game. But god, its such a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ now. I didnt really talk about the current state of the game, cus its liable to change. Destiny just isn't what it used to be, greed and laziness has taken hold up top and the game is just rotting away.\t\t\t\t\t

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Look, I'm not gonna sit here and say "this game sucks" with nearly 900 hours on Steam and God knows how many thousands of hours on console, but my recommendation definitely comes with a few caveats:

1. This game really is micro-transaction hell. If you are particularly susceptible to succumbing to bankrupting yourself over digital glitter - STAY AWAY. Destiny 2 is damn near 'mobile game' levels of monetized.

2. Don't come here for deep story and immersive campaigns. The original lore of this game was great and yes, Destiny could have been so much more, but it isn't and it never will be. Comparing Destiny 2 to its original lore is like comparing a terrible book-to-movie adaptation. Just take pleasure in the moment-to-moment gun-play.

3. Don't come here for the social experience. If you're looking to make friends or run raids with people, etc., Destiny 2 ain't the place to do it. Sure, you can go online and try to find people to run stuff with, but despite the game's "social spaces" and group content, unless you already know people playing the game - expect to be alone. Expect to talk to nobody. Expect to be locked out of most of the content, unless you REALLY go out of your way to find people to run it with (Reddit, DestinyLFG, etc.).

Finally, 4. PvP - I hold the unpopular opinion that PvP is fine. It's mostly completely ignored by the dev team, but I think that might be a good thing (seeing that they released, ya know, Lightfall and all). That said, if you don't like PvP, there are little quests for weapons and stuff that force you to play it if you want to unlock certain things. So, bear that in mind.\t\t\t\t\t
