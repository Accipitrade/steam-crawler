\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo let's say I have a mild addiction towards this game. Most MMO games bore me pretty fast, but for some reason Destiny 2 not only managed to somehow not bore me to death with grindy exotic quests, but it made me sink 212 hours as of today.

What's nice about this game:
- The gameplay is really good. Like, the shooting mechanics are IMO some of the best. It just feels good to shoot and easy to hit.
- There is plenty of content to grind, if you like grinding for content. Fortunately, you don't feel forced to grind anything and you don't need to grind something if you don't want it.
- The graphics don't disappoint. They are not cutting edge, next generation graphics, but the game looks good and it is well optimized.
- The DLC's are discounted pretty often. I bought both of them at a discounted price (15 Euros for Forsaken, 20 for Shadowkeep)

What's absolutely disgustingly bad about this game:
- PVP weapon balance. Weapons always get nerfed and the meta is always changing. But somehow, there's always going to be a really overpowered weapon, which requires absolutely 0 skill to use, and probably even a brain dead person could use them.
- Tryhards in Crucible and Gambit Prime using those broken ass weapons. Like man, if you invade me in Gambit, at least have the balls to use something else than a machine gun or The Mountaintop. Or even a sniper.
- Team balancing. The matchmaking can be really wonky sometimes. One time, I had the highest level in my team, which was 963. Their lowest leveled team member was 967 and their highest was over 975. Most were around 970. And yes, item level does not directly reflect the player's skill level, but being over 960 usually says something about how many hours you played the game, because it's pretty hard to increase your level after the hard cap (950), and it's even harder to increase it after the pinnacle gear cap (960).

In conclusion, I have a tough love-hate relationship with this game. I am addicted to it though. And it's really nice to play when you have friends you can play it with. Especially if your friends are competent.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIn October of 2019 this game made a rather risky jump from a full price title to a free to play game with optional DLC / Addons. And it actually landed on it's feet gracefully. I would guess the fact that everyone on Battle.net could've already gotten the base game for free in November 2018 helped make it not feel that big.

Overall Destiny 2 is a First-Person-Shooter MMO with slight RPG elements. It features 9 different Planets / Worlds with changing world content, Singleplayer campaigns, PvE Content for automatically built groups as well as externally built, larger teams, PvP modes with changing rulesets and rotating types and finally a unique PvE and PvP combination mode called Gambit. Overall, there is more than enough content for all types of players, and you will not be punished for opting out of those you do not want to participate in.
Covering all of the mentioned modes exist a large amount of Quests and pursuits to chase after over longer times as well as Bounties, which provide Daily / Weeky resetting objectives, which definitely hits the spot for players looking for daily tasks to check off.

Destiny 2 features a total of 4 DLC, 2 of which are now free for all players. Each provide a new Planet / World (which you have access to previously, but now have more content for,) a campaign storyline, new gameplay mechanics and special activities to do and generally, lots of new content.

Last but not least there is now the new Season Pass. Seasonal content is nothing new for Destiny 2, yet this Season Pass requires either a seperate purchase for 1000 silver (10€) or is included in the purchase of the newest Addon Shadowkeep. It features nothing too significant, or rather necessary, but does provide a few additional rewards along your way.

Overall, you can easily try the game for free and if you enjoy it, any purchase, be it Addon or Season Pass, will enhance your experience.\t\t\t\t\t
