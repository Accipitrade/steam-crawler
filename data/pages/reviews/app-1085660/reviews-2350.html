\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhat can I say that’s nice about Destiny 2: it looks stunning at times, the sound design for the most part is great and the levels seem fairly well done when it’s not just a bunch of platforming. Good voice acting from the supporting characters, not so much your player character. Visible weapon variety is pretty big and continues to grow, how much of it is actually viable is honestly a very small percentage.

Far too many PVE story quests that require you to suffer through the awful and horribly imbalanced multiplayer aspect of this trash title. Bungie yet again proves that they simply do not know how to do balanced multiplayer and ruin a half decent game by flat out forcing players to play it. Major quests that you gain from beating story missions or DLC quests end up giving you other bounties or quests that are multiplayer centric 90% of the time. Bungie out right forces you to play its horrible Halo trash multiplayer that was dead back in 2004. By the way you pay for the majority of said quests only to be blocked from any progress or getting any "god tier" weapons due to the multiplayer aspect.

Let's talk about the Free to Play aspect of this "game", it could not be further from the truth, this is a fee to play system. More then half of the entire game is locked behind paid DLC, you heard me right, more half. More then half of the exotics/armor/base world weapons are also locked behind the paid DLC. So far the season passes have been a joke, culminating into the single worst one so far "the season of the worthy" which was renamed by the players to "Season of the Bounties" because that's all it was, bounties farming. Then they made it worse by adding in this "community event" called the Guardian Games which again had NOTHING but bounties. Bungie as a company is completely devoid of ideas at this point, as if this wasn't evident by the fact that D1 and D2 are basically the same games and the same basic design gameplay wise as Halo Combat Evolved.

On the subject of multiplayer, which isn't saying much, it is overrun with cheaters, hackers and players who abuse their network connection. If it's not the cheaters then it's the try hard Youtubers or lobbies filled with stacked teams looking to pad their stats and look "good" for the camera. Mostly however it's cheaters and exploiters who play the multiplayer, it's a "free" game after all so why not right? Bungie sure hasn't done a single thing to fix the problem so why shouldn't the cheaters exploit this for "fun"? Weapons in the game are 100x less balanced then any game that I have ever played in my entire life. If you're up against some 20,000 hour no lifer you're going to lose every single time. Not because he's better but because his gear and weapons are stronger by design, there is basically zero skill in this "game" and that is a fact. Any losses that happen flat out punish the loser and only the loser, doesn't matter, Bungie is for the rich period so that means anyone who loses also gets a negative against their match. Winning in a row nets you even more points, thus it's a circle of rewards for the cheaters, that's all that this game's multiplayer teaches you, if you aren't cheating to win you are punished 2 times as much. As with Bungie’s only formula at this point, the combat is slow but with more jumping and verticality then the previous Halo games which isn’t saying much. Classes that can not use the most verticality or jumping/maneuverability get punished, period. There is basically only a single class that you can realistically use in multiplayer, which is the Hunter, no need to believe just me, look at Bungie’s own player stays, the Hunter class is by far the single most used in the entire game, not just multiplayer. So balance wise there simply is none to speak of. If this game was more like Halo it may have been good, however since Bungie tried to do the whole space wizards thing they added class abilities which further mess up the really poor balance of Bungie’s combat type. If you cast your “super/alt” then you win, automatically unless of course the player that you’re about to kill has an overpowered exotic or extreme meta weapon then there’s a chance that you won’t kill them but the chances are fairly slim. The best phrase that I can use to describe the multiplayer is "the rich get richer" other then a horribly tacked on game mode that is painful to play.

On the PVE side of this “game” you have 50% of the enemy types that have some kind of ground pound ability, this knocks you into orbit because I guess the rejects of Bungie thought that it was funny. Every single boss type enemy in the “game” has this mechanic, making more then 65-75% of all of the weapon classes and 80% of all class abilities across all 3 classes an extreme liability and rendering them pointless and far more of a risk then a reward. The loot game is really not all that great to begin with, sure starting out you’ll see fun stuff consistently but as you get past your first few hundred hours you’ll start to see how shallow the whole experience is. Sure the levels are nice to look at and seem to be well made but the core gameplay itself is just a bad Halo:CE with lame powers.

Network connectivity, any other game is perfectly stable online, Bungie's abortion? Not even remotely, you'll hit things square in the eyeball and nothing will happen. If you want to have a fun multiplayer game, this is not it and it never will be so long as Bungie is behind the wheel. This is what happens when you have a developer who has spent the past 15 years making console games, they’ve forgotten even basic tenants for making a proper game. Want a lobby? Nope 100% piss poor XBL style parties and P2P systems. Want to kick someone who is obviously cheating? Nope can’t. The UI in ever respect is just a simple console port UI as well, the entire game, from the user interface to the small icon and map functionality is all a really poor console port, simply put. Also enjoy “Error code: Beaver” because that’s what you’ll be seeing a lot of, weather you were simply in the middle of joining a match or just organizing your inventory, the network is that bad.

The technical end of the spectrum I'd say that this "game" is very unstable. In the "short" amount of time that I have been playing it it has crashed and hard locked my system more times then I care to count. Coming from a developer name like Bungie I would have thought that the gods that come from that company would be able to make a stable platform for their money making scheme but alas it seems like the majority of the real Bungie left a long time ago. This game is so unstable and bad that it makes titles like Warframe look good by comparison.\t\t\t\t\t
