\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is an awesome game.
While it is a bit confusing at times, the PVP isn't super balanced and the story isn't the greatest or most entertaining, this game is awesome, and I will tell you why.

For new players:
All I can say is yes, this game is super confusing to jump into, and yes, it is not simple for new players to grasp. But whats funny is that you have all of these people complaining that it is too confusing. For one, there is this thing called YouTube, and while I agree that you shouldn't have to go to YouTube for a simple quest, it's still there, and it's still a resource that's available (and I think that I speak for the majority of the community when I say that there are times that us veteran players have to use YouTube as well). If you don't want to use YouTube, then just go to the director (hold tab) and go to the quests tab. If you are ever lost or looking for something to do, I would just immediately just go there and then to YouTube (keep in mind that in order to start the main story, you have to go talk to Amanda Holiday, the Shipwright. She's in the basement with all of the ships in the tower). While at the beginning, it seems like a hassle, or that its too confusing, believe me when I say that it's worth the couple hours to figure out how to do everything.

One of the things I love about this game and Destiny 1, is how confusing they were at times. It made me think, and sometimes made me a better player. It's a fun game, with a lot to do, and amazing lore, if you're into that.

For Destiny veterans thinking about jumping in:
I played a lot of Destiny 1. I was thinking about jumping in when Destiny 2 came out, but was never impressed or happy with the game until New Light. New Light really has changed the game. There's a lot of new content, the DLC is not a mandatory buy, and it finally feels like Destiny again. I recommend it to those who are on the fence, because I feel that the game is finally something that veterans can appreciate, and something that is new, interesting, and engaging, but yet also familiar. It feels like Destiny 1, but is very different, and so far, any complaints I have are far outweighed by the enjoyment I've have.

One of the things I love about this game is that it feels so familiar, but yet offers so much new content for me to enjoy. It feels like a similar game without being the same game.

PVP:
PVP is somewhat of an unbalanced mess right now. I'll say it. as much as I want to defend the Crucible, that's just the current truth. But you want to know what's amazing? No matter how many times I get fisted or tackled by some stupid titan, shotgunned, killed by recluse (an SMG), or blown to bits by The Mountaintop (a Grenade Launcher), PVP remains... fun. I actually have fun. Even when I'm not the top of the board, or getting massacred buy all the meta abusers, I enjoy it. And when I do good? Oh man does it feel amazing to be no 1 on my team, dropping 40 or 50 k, just straight slaying. I love it. Both the highs and the lows of the Crucible are enjoyable, and while it needs to be balanced, it's still fun. I can count on one hand the multiplayer games I've played that are enjoyable during PVP meta abuse stages, and I'd need 10 hands to count the games that aren't.

I enjoy that the Crucible remains fun. Even when I feel burnt out or in a tough spot in terms of k/d, I still find that I enjoy it, and because the game has a bit of a meta issue right now, I feel that I have become a better player. No longer am I just looking for the god roll weapons so that I can just slay with those, but now I look for ways that I can out-smart my opponents, and I am a better player for it.

Story:
If you are here for the story, then I have a tiny bit of bad news. Don't get me wrong. The stories are interesting, and have engaging themes and present fascinating ideas, but the bulk of the story and lore are hidden in in-game eBooks. It's weird for sure, and not the way that I would like it to be done, but after doing a little bit of research on your own time and checking out the lorebooks and YouTube lore videos, the stories change and become so much more interesting and enjoyable because you get to a point where you kinda understand what is going on. I love Destiny lore, and while I don't love the way that it is presented, I do find it interesting.

Like I said, I LOVE the lore in this game. Bungie has really created a universe with Ideas unheard of, and a Universe that feels alive. Even the backstories to the enemies, their religions, beliefs and cultures are interesting to learn about. Heck, the lore behind a lot of the guns are fun to read. It all feels really thought out and the themes and ideas presented throughout the lore and game are so interesting.

One thing I love are the enemies and the stories behind them. There is one main enemy in the Destiny universe, sure. But that one main enemy has allies, then there are the enemies that are in a grey area, then there are the allies that seem like enemies and so on. I don't want to explain it all, but the system that is going on here is so much more interesting than the black and white, good and evil methods of conflict we see in so much media today.

PVE:
(PVE stands for Player vs Everything). This is the place where the story will develop and where you will be fighting AI monsters. It's fun. I enjoy it. There's not much to say about it, other than it can be really rewarding and engaging, but also at the same time monotonous and sometimes unrewarding. It's a ton of fun to hop into raids and strikes with friends, and also fun to just do it yourself. It does sometimes feel like you are chasing levels, I will say that, and because of the way that the game is designed, all of the monsters kinda level up with you which doesn't help the feeling that you aren't getting anywhere, but I usually take this feeling of monotony as a sign that I need a break and come back later.

I love that even though sometimes PVE feels monotonous and can feel boring, when I am enjoying it, it feels like I really am getting places and striving for something. Setting goals can help, as stupid as that seems.

All in all, I love this game. It's a ton of fun and just has so much to do. While I realize that not everybody will love it, I do recommend it and if you are iffy, just download it and give it a shot. Don't spend any money. The game literally never asks for it, just play the game and see how you like it.\t\t\t\t\t
