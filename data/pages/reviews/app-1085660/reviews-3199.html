\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI feel like I gotta revise my review.

So right now, D2's sandbox is in a mostly good spot. The new abilities all feel fantastic and each class/subclass combo has all been real good. The introduction of character builds has been really healthy for adding variety to playstyles and tackling encounters. Witch Queen is a fantastic expansion and is a refreshing return to form, easily one of the most fun campaigns in the franchise.

However most of the issues of this game have been a core of this game since Shadowkeep unfortunately. Most of the content that is being added on a seasonal basis are all very simple gimmick encounters that are streeeeetched across three months so you are expected to play these quite a bit across this small period of time and it does not respect your time and burnout is such a common issue across the playerbase. FOMO is lessened but it is still very much an issue with this game's battle pass and given this game's past- it's very much uncertain that most of that content will be removed from the game come future expansions.

Despite that, I'm still just waiting for the slow reintroduction of the removed Forsaken weapons available with the new crafting system (seriously, Black Armory reprisal when?). But despite how good this new system is, it's held back with even more tedium and grind. Gotta grind more currencies to actually get the weapon that are all tied from random drop weapons that need to be levelled then retrieved, then gut your loadout because you are using a sub-optimal weapon that doesn't get good until level 18 on the actual gun itself.

Game right now feels like it is winding down before the next major Bungie project, and right now it's hard to recommend to new players because how much you have to go through and how much reading you have to do to understand the story of the game. Much of the newer content is just more of the same, but with more enemies painted all over the screen to offset the new overpowered abilities. The core gameplay despite all of the new ability additions: is the same ♥♥♥♥, and that is the most disappointing part of this game. They're wearing this loop thin and need to come up with something more interesting this upcoming expansion.

But at the same time, I just keep coming back because there's just nothing out there that plays like this. Another example of Steam needing a middling recommendation.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game lacks substance, I only really play this with friends and even then it just sets in that there's not a lot to do. There's so many Quality of Life things this game is missing that's borderline unacceptable for a developer with this much money at the helm and with how hard they're pushing it. There's so many little things that would make this game a lot better, a North indicator on the mini-map so I don't have to open the terrible interface to re-orient myself, matchmaking for raids and dungeons with a "cleared" and "practice" queue so I don't have to use an app to MAYBE get into a fireteam. All the rewards being stripped from previous expansions (Both the free ones AND Forsaken) is a huge flaw as well, Bungie are giving new players nothing for their time. On previous expansions, paying for Forsaken is lame, especially since said rewards are stripped it should just be included with every new expansion. On top of all of this the sense of progression on both a GEAR and NARRATIVE level is completely destroyed, I haven't felt immersed in this game at all and it's quite jarring that in the short time I've played. The PvP is sort of balanced with a few exceptions, the gunplay is fair and sometimes fun. But it's just a very inaccessible game with a lot of tiny flaws that make it look like a moth eating curtain put on display.\t\t\t\t\t
