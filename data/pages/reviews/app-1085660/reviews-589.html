\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've put in a lot more hours into this game, and this franchise, than my hours on Steam will reflect... so please keep that in mind. I've played the series since the first open beta, which was before the first game came out. I say this because I want to mention that I've been with the franchise since the very beginning. I can still remember my excitement over the first game's surprise E3 reveal, seeing advertisements for it in Game Informer articles back in the day, I've been around for a bit. I didn't just play the first game, but plenty of this too, on Xbox One and on BattleNet before it was on Steam, and almost 300 hours on here as of writing this. Now, for the meat and potatoes of the review. Would I recommend Destiny 2? No, lol, absolutely not. I wouldn't recommend this to anyone. Bungie turned this game into a source of malicious, downright predatory monetization. Destiny 2 launched as a $60 game with a full campaign, and was eventually given several expansions/DLC. The game, evidently, went for a free to play model eventually after they parted with Activision some years ago, and the game has only gotten worse since. Bungie has mishandled just about everything they could possibly ♥♥♥♥ up, and have done so in what could only be described as the Bungie way. They started cutting content and more aggressively monetizing the game with the release of Beyond Light, the game's most recent expansion, and plans to really double down on things with their upcoming entry to the debacle that Destiny 2 has become, The Witch Queen. The developers continue removing content that was previously free under the guise of needing to save space and stop the game from bloating, which is a fair point to make, however it's also a lie. The game's file size is huge, I mean, it's pretty damn big, yet somehow there's virtually no content left in the game anymore. In order to justify being free to play, and in the name of shrinking the download size, they have removed the base game's content, they have removed the first 2 expansions in their entirety to the best of my knowledge, and plan to continue with this trend by removing Forsaken (which was one of the most highly regarded entries to this shallow sham of a game)... in case you are worried about missing it, don't worry, it's still on sale for ***full price*** despite going away in a couple of months! Then, in February I believe it was meant to be, another area will be erased along with it's activities, but the campaign will still be purchasable for those who would like to spend money on what little remains of the expansion. They have a celebratory event coming up soon, Bungie's 30th anniversary, where they will be literally selling access to what was arguably the best loot in the first game, as well as an activity that will only be temporary. Yeah, they're gonna charge like $30 USD for access to a temporary event and loot, and then they're gonna pack it up. I should also mention that around that same time, anybody who does not own Forsaken or the Forsaken Pack, which is what will be sold when they disembowel that expansion, will lose access to 3 skill trees, which would be the middle skill tree in each light subclass. Beyond this, there is also of course the cash shop which has only been expanded upon after going f2p, and existed back when this had a full triple A release price tag. There's a lot to unpack, I could talk about the endless amount of content locking, horrible mismanagement over pre-existing things players were given, as well as the lack of half decent story telling in the game as of right now, paired with how confused new players to the series would be, and just how frankly unwilling the developers are to consider anything more than trying to cut content and then stretch it out, adding things to make both gameplay and grind more artificially difficult. Oh, and battlepasses that you can pay extra money to get, and then pay extra money to get a 2nd time after they go away at the end of the season. Speaking of which, you can't access seasonal content without one, meaning the few and far between additions the game gets outside of overpriced DLC, you have to pay to access. I'm going to play the game because I like the gameplay loop, but as someone who can see the game as what it is, I would not recommend anyone get involved in it as of writing this, lest you be taken into the sunk cost fallacy that keeps many players here. Please respect yourself, your time, and your wallet... and go elsewhere. Wait for the devs to fix this horrid mess of a release, it's not worth supporting unless you really wanna play or already know you liked the game before it became this way. Thank you for reading.\t\t\t\t\t
