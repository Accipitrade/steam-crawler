\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe good and bad...Destiny 2 is not quite sure what to make of itself. Is it an MMO? Is it Competitive PVP, Is it Compelling Storyline? Yes and no to all three at the same time. The MMO aspect is there in just that you have lots of folks on line at the same time, but unless your doing the very few events that are match-made then no. You can solo a lot in this game outside of Raids and Strikes. Competitive PVP...they try so hard but have fallen flat each time they try to balance things out. Most of the time, if something gets fixed for PVE and will be changed in PVP that way also so there becomes issues with that. It also doesn't help that that PVP anti-cheat feels that it even isn't there and is based off of everyone just reporting other players. Storyline is very hit or miss...Forsaken is a wonderful story to play through but Warmind, Curse of Osiris, and Beyond Light were just meh to blah. The real meat of the story telling is very Elder Scrolls in that you have to complete journal entries and read to get the full story, and at the start of this season Bungie killed off a charater that we dealt with through Curse of Osiris in a story that was published online.

All that said, it is still a fun shooter that is slowly finding its footing again and should be at least watched to see what happens. Some of the story elements feel like they are setting up a much longer endgame for Destiny 2 before moving onto a new release for Destiny 3 in the next couple of years. With everything that is going on with sunsetting and content being shelved for later use, it would be worth it to at least play through the current Storylines before those too go away\t\t\t\t\t
