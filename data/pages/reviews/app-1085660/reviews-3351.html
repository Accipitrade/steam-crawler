\r\n\t\t\t\t\tVery good first person MMO-lite, tons of activities to do, decent story, although most of the stories left me wanting more, but in general, very polished, I'd say well optimized, and a joy to play.

Some of the activities it allows you to do are your basic PVP playlist, being Crucible, where your builds and such do still carry over.

Gambit: PVE, and PVP. Kill enemies to get Motes, deposit motes to send blockers to enemies side, deposit enough, a portal will open to allow a player from your team to cross over, to attempt to make other players lose all their motes. (Motes drop on death.)

Strikes: If you've ever played an MMO before, it's similar to their dungeons, in this, a full party for a strike is 3 players, work together to clear the dungeon. Higher difficulty = Higher reward. But the higher end strikes, are for sure a challenge.

Raids: 6 player fireteams, take on a much larger "dungeon" with many more mechanics in each boss fight. I haven't done them all, even being a day one player of Destiny 2, back on blizzards client, (I quit a few times) but, i came back each time gladly, the only raid I've attempted (and completed) back when the game was new, was the first raid, the Calus raid. Me and my friends had a LOT of fun with it, the mechanics and such in this game rival that of top MMOs. Such as WoW, or FFXIV.

There is some other stuff to do, but, not all of it is super interesting, like farming materials to hand in to vendors on each planet, or going to planets to do simple bounties.. Although good for you to do, can sometimes leave you feeling like it's a chore.

Exotics: Exotic weapons are guns that behave overly different then other guns in the game, be it changing elements, or a mini gun, or even a trace rifle, to even a grenade launcher that shoots grenades... that walk at people? Exotics are very interesting to create different and unique builds for your class, but more so, when we get into talking about Exotic Armours. The Armours very clearly normally change something with your characters skillset, for example Hunters can get a Helmet that allows them to recover health and shields on using their class ability (dodge). Or Warlocks can empower their void tree, using a Helmet, where it allows them to get faster cooldowns on all their abilities as long as they're dealing Void damage. And Titans can make a melee build where punching enemies is sometimes your first choice instead of shooting!

This game does have some balance issues, In pvp, Bottom tree Striker Titans, are quite literally unbearable to face, they're super tanky, heal on each kill, and if they use their super move, each kill they get with it prolongs it, which feels very unfair to face most times. They aren't impossible to beat, but they don't quite feel on an equal power level either, which can lead to it feeling quite unfair.

In PvE all classes feel good, but some shine a little brighter then others, Blade Barrage Hunter, Chaos Reach Warlock, Melt bosses faster then anything else (That I've seen) which i could be wrong. But, it does feel like if i don't pick those sometimes I won't have a high impact like them.

But that's just me! You might be different. Overall doing what YOU want to do, is what will give you the most enjoyment. Find your build and just have fun with it. Doing stupid little builds and changing mods around might give you a build that you enjoy much more then anything that might be considered good, or top tier. And at the end of the day that's what it's all about.

Overall, this games definitely worth checking out, and now that it's free to play, I can't see a reason not to, Give it a shot, it's gunplay and class system is rather polished, and there is tons to do, and have fun with, bring friends for an optimal experience, but spare a few people afking through activities, the majority of the community seems rather nice, and it isn't hard to queue up and find people to play with!

GL&HF Guardian.
