\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt plays like Destiny 1. It looks and feels a little bit different because of the different game engine(thanks activision...) but mostly it holds true to it's roots. It is so much better on PC than on console. I have always felt like D2 was made more for PC than for XB1. I play it on both but it is so much better on PC. More frames, more details, faster and more accurate aiming! And that is the important part. There are some quick moving enemies in here.

Drawbacks are that it is a bit of a learning curve and a grind. PVP is not balanced right now. There is a meta, and you have to grind to be in it otherwise you just get destroyed. Unlike the last two, the first two story lines are pretty much crap.

Positive is PVE. It is a lot of fun for me. Tons of content and areas to explore if you have all the DLC. One thing I have always liked about Destiny is exploring and shooting enemies. The worlds are beautiful and fun to climb and jet pack through. Leveling is not bad until you hit 950. That is where the elite pve players start to really differentiate themselves from the casuals. I like that. The grind is pleasant with friends. There are discord groups to always find someone to play with.

Pick your main character wisely. Maybe do some youtube investigating the classes before you choose. The class can really make or break your experience.

It's ok. Not a game to solo. If you don't have any friends or a mic and discord to make new ones, you will not like this game. Those are my thoughts anyway. Coming from someone that played the crap out of Destiny 1.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's been a long way since Vanilla Destiny 2. It has grown to be what a lot of people wanted : a near full-time hobby.

The free-to-play version is a no brainer : you'll have lots to do before feeling the need to put some money for the next DLCs.

There will be a lot, and I mean a lot, more content if you get Forsaken, it's almost crazy. Though if I had to put my finger on something that doesn't please me about Destiny 2, it's their "Quest" that translate by "Do X a few hundred times" then "Go here, do that" and so on so forth. It could be just tedium, but there's also when random element halt your way and you're just sitting here waiting for something to not happen.

Apart from that, the game is probably the only of its kind to deliver a decent mix of PVP, PVE, and even PVEVP. If you have a decent connection for the 50Go, try it, It is a great game for people who are FPS fan with a side of RPG. It is not a great game for RPG oriented people though.

The customisation/loadout options just not that thrilling : you pick a skill tree and that's it. You don't get to chose which passive or which part of the tree you don't want etc...It is what makes pvp even remotely possible I guess, but I need to point that out I guess.

Shadowkeep DLC is in my opinion, not worth it at the moment. The main story is short and bloated by fillers. The moon is absolutely gorgeous and the area have a very dark vibe that makes you want to know more and explore, and the activity I've done were ok, but it's still a weak expansion in my opinion. Either Shadowkeep is weak, or Forsaken was fluke and we're now expecting too much.

Either way, the game is great, got a lot of replayability, and I could probably play it every single night until the end of 2020.

TL;DR
Enjoy The Free. Buy Forsaken If Enjoyed the Free. Honestly Don't Buy ShadowKeep unless sales > 70%\t\t\t\t\t
