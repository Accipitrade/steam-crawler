\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 lacks the polish that made so many of us love the first game. The poor polish and bugs essentially destroy several major aspects of the game. The amazing gunplay and great atmosphere is seemingly always ruined by a prolific amount of teleporting enemies. Any hope towards Bungie's talk of a cohesive solar system, where players are supposed to come together in the open worlds seamlessly can immediately be expected to be ripped away by the game's horrendous servers. You will not get through a session in Destiny 2 without seeing at least 1 server disconnect error message. The list goes on and on.

For example, Destiny 2's lack of polish really came to a head for me when doing the current active dungeon in the game. Dungeons are basically mini 3 person raids with puzzle mechanics, mazes and a boss which require a strategy carried out by a communicating team. After finally reaching the required level to access the dungeon, my friends and I descended into the depths of the moon, eager to see what the dungeon had to offer. What we found was an amazing castle like structure carved into the walls of a massive cave system. Peering further down into the abyss below, we could just make out the glowing silhouette of an even more ominous structure than what lay directly in front of us. Before we embarked on the journey ahead, we made note of the 3 glowing runes scrawled onto the wall as they were surely important for some kind of puzzle that was waiting for us. This part of Destiny 2 was great. At its best, this moment reminded me of the many sunken cities throughout the Dark Souls series, where the path through sprawling areas can be seen waiting for you to traverse. Sounds fun right?

This is where things started to almost immediately fall apart. To make a long story short, this dungeon area felt like a complete alpha build of this part of the game. Enemies teleported in and out of existence every where we went. Collision mapping across the ground was so poor I was constantly falling into the world. Even the elevators necessary to traverse this area would bug your movement and often instantly kill me as I rode them, leaving me in midair to wait the 30 seconds to respawn forcing my friend to wait for me since there was no way to reach my corpse and revive me. The dungeon never got better. This piece of content that I looked forward to my whole time leveling almost immediately became an example of the worst this game has to offer.

Overall, I believe the best way to describe Destiny 2 is that it's a Ferrari held together on the inside with chewing gum and scotch tape. It's amazing to look at and admire from afar, but when you get in and start driving it you'll find it falls apart. Destiny 2 is a game with tons of promising mechanics and ideas that crash and burn due to the developer's incompetence.\t\t\t\t\t
