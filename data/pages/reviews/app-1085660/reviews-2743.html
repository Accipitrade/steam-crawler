\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first, I was skeptical getting into another Destiny game after the constant grind and numerous problems with the original Destiny game on the Xbox One. The constant grind that got old too quickly because of doing the same raid, the same nightfall, the same thing, over and over again. I found myself buying the Iron Lord DLC and I quit 1-2 weeks later because life got busy and the game felt more like a chore than fun.

I tried Destiny 2 (D2) after it went free to play (If you have considered playing the game and have not yet played it or the original Destiny game, give it a try. It's free to play base game is worth a little time to decide if the game is for your or not.) I was instantly hooked, to some point due to nostalgia and the game feeling fresh again, but also because so many needed changed had been updated in D2. There is a fairly large amount of content to the free to play platform, but I decided to upgrade and get the Forsaken DLC and I got the Shadowkeep a day or two after launch. Well worth the money (I try to stick with a $1 per hour of gameplay in a game that I do go out of my way to buy). Everything can be done to level up your character to to the current soft cap of 950 light without needing to party up with other players (though it is more fun to have friends to play with).

Once you obtain that endgame standing, (Which I am only somewhat at on only one character after almost 250 hours of gameplay; I have not finished all of the raids, I have not finished the Shadowkeep campaign, etc.) I would highly recommend joining the Destiny 2 LFG discord to learn and complete raids/end game activities or have a solid crew of friends to join you in the chaos that is a D2 Raid. The D2 raids are more complexed than that of the original game, but after a friendly, helping hand from the community you will be able to complete them.

This game just has so much to do when you first get into it if you are getting into the game as late as I did. Tasks, quests, bounties, events, all piled on; you have a lot to do and a lot to work toward. This was a little overwhelming at first until I was able to get a few of the major things out of the way (mainly the campaign). My biggest recommendation in regard to this, play the campaigns in order FIRST. This not only allows you to casually level your light up, but it also catches you up with the story and sequence of events quite nicely.

Pros:
A lot to do, so much so this game keeps having me come back time and time again. 10/10
Visual graphics have been greatly improved over the first game. There are some minor things like the ability to get out of the map in some areas. This is more of an oversight/exploit that has no impact on the gameplay. 9.5/10
Sound quality is high quality, but if you are playing with other people and chatting with them on Discord, you will need to turn the overall volume down. 8.5/10
The community: there is always someone else looking to do what you or working on, or willing to help out. The Destiny 2 LFG is your best friend. 9.5/10

Cons:
The occasional crash or error code. Only a minor inconvenience, usually a restart will fix it.
Servers can have problems, it happens, but it is rare and the game doesn't stay down for long.
Previous campaign specific items are a rare thing (armor, weapons, etc). The most recent campaign items drop most if not all the time. So, if you want to get that amazing looking armor that was part of another DLC, you won't be able to. A bit sad about that to be completely honest.

Neither Pro nor Con:
Micro transactions for cosmetic items. Thankfully these items do not have a pay to win aspect (which is why the are not in the Cons area). Some look pretty cool, but I would rather see them as a reward to completing other tasks during gameplay.
There will always be a meta in a game like this; a particular gun (or a few) people use for certain things, armor type, stat usage, etc that will exist. Which makes it all the better when you get that item/build or until it gets nerfed/patched in an update.
Season Pass: When I purchased the DLC, I was given a "free" season pass for this season. Play, earn xp, level up, and get rewards. These rewards vary, but the one that I love the most is the Bright Engrams dropped during this season, giving me the chance to unlock previous season rewards.
You will do a lot of web surfing to guide you through some quests to obtain exotic weapons and armor. Your best friends here are the following: Destiny 2 LFG Discord, Breytech.org, and just Google searching the quest you are trying to complete. These will give you the 411 on how to complete the tasks set before you.

Overall: 9.0/10
This game has been a huge improvement over the first and continues to grow. I would highly recommend giving the free to play a try if you are a fan of the first game or simply new to the series. If you like it, consider picking up the DLC during a sale, but the game/DLC is worth it at full price in my opinion.\t\t\t\t\t
