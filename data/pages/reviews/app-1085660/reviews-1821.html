\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHonestly, I dont even know if I enjoy this game anymore. However, I think that it s a fantastic game, being able to hold me for 372 hours. I would highly recommend buying the expansions, not for the campaign necissarily, but for the end game content.(campaigns are fun, and enticing, but foresaken just seems like a bunch of strikes and shadowkeep is just a little short). The free-to-play experience includes the first 2 dlc, each of which take about an hour for an average player, and when I started playing on my f2p account again I hadnt completed those campaigns and dlc, and just didnt feel like I needed to play them, because you dont really get anything from it. once you beat the red war campaign you enter what bungie considers "the end game", but I would only consider that phase in the game about half way before you are ready for end game content (such as raids). I have carried multiple noobies through the leviathan, and its possible, and an easy raid, but for people who dont have access to the exotics and legendaries that people who have played more than just the red war campaign it is very difficult. I think that if you are to play this game, get through the red war campaign, which is a good baseline for what to expect and how to play the game. after that, I recommend going through some crucible and gambit to get some better weapons which will make your life easier. Then I would say find some friends to take you to the leviathan, and get a raid experience. If you enjoy playing, buy the dlc. you get access to much more. and I think that shadowkeep, while not worth $35, at its current $20 is worth it, so try to get it on sale. the game does get kind of boring after a few months of playing when you have done alot of whats in the game and all thats left is grindy stuff. overall, I would recommend that you play this game, it is a good time investment if you like looter-shooters that you can sink hundreds of hours into or just play a little bit, because your stuff will never be deleted.\t\t\t\t\t
