\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a first-person shooter set in a sci-fi universe. The game features a variety of different weapons including a shotgun with a rocket launcher attached, as well as the ability to throw grenades or fire the player's own projectiles. The game features three game modes: PvP, PvE, and a secret game variant. The game also features a classic tower defense mode, Tower Defense, where players take control of a tower and enact defense. The game uses a progression system, in which player characters gain new abilities and items, such as new armor, attachments, perks, weapons, and perks and weapons. Otherwise, for customizability, there are a variety of different classes within the game, including the Hunter, Ranger, Soldier, Summoner, Warrior, and Titan Master which include several different subclasses for each character.

The game features multiple difficulty levels including casual, expert, and master and a secret difficulty variant. The missions in the game take place in a plethora of locations including Europe and the solar system. The game also features several secret mission variants.

Overall this game has a lot to offer from the first-person perspective, multiplayer, and towers defense. Destiny 2 features some excellent graphics, smooth gameplay, and a lot of fun content which makes a solid first-person shooter game. If you are a fan of the first-person experience, then this game is a must-buy.

- Excellent graphics

- Fun

- Good variety between game modes

- Good weapons

- Variety of weapon

- Great progression system


Cons:

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith over 1100 hours logged, I wish I could recommend this game. I would have recommended it several months ago during the Season of Dawn, despite its flaws then. The aspects of this game that made me play it for so long were the fulfilling challenges, being able to "play my way," and going on adventures with friends. All three of those things eventually faded away due to the direction Bungie took the game. I thought I'd play Destiny 2 for at least a thousand more hours in the years to come, but one-by-one, my IRL friends (that got me into the game) stopped playing due to the same reasons I eventually lost interest. I could write several paragraphs detailing the reasons why I stopped playing the game, but I'll just list my four biggest reasons:

  • Cheaters in PVP - This is rampant, especially in competitive play. The types of cheaters you'll run into most are aimbotters. They're even all over the place in Quickplay (non-competitive PVP). There's no EasyAntiCheat.

  • Implementation of weapon and armor "sunsetting" - Weapons and armor you worked so hard for can no longer be increased in power level if they come from a few seasons back. It's honestly a slap in the face of players that just got done grinding for that gear, or were almost done grinding for them. Instead of creating new, interesting gear that we want to grind for, they decided to sunset older ones so we would be forced to use "new" ones ("new" is in quotes because they just reuse gear and re-release them, or use existing models and name them something different).

  • Boring, repetitive, and pressured grind (FOMO) - In order to keep up in the power level chase, you feel pressured to log in whenever you can and do bounties over and over again since they are the "activities" that grant you the most experience points. I would knock out 5 bounties that would net me triple the experience points that I could get from playing a few hours of PVP (Crucible).

  • Network issues - not much to say here other than that this is the only online game I've played where I would lose connection in the middle of whatever I was doing and receive a random network error on a regular basis (and I'm not alone).

I still think this is a good game if you want to occasionally jump in and experience some awesome gun-play and fluid mechanics. It has some of the best gameplay mechanics I've ever experienced, but that can only carry a game for so long. If you're looking for something to pull you in and make your time feel valued and money not wasted, this is not it. There are too many cheaters, the grind is too mindless and boring, the power limit on gear makes you feel like you're on a timer, and connection issues happen too regularly. Bungie seems to care more about pulling in new players and not retaining them in the long term, because they've done little-to-nothing about these issues while continuing to churn out new paid content.\t\t\t\t\t
