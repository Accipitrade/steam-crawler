\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDon't let my steam hours fool you I played this on battlenet and console and have hundreds of hours in Destiny 1.

This is a game I would love to recommend, the moment to moment gameplay is fun, boss fights have been historically fun, raids can be amazing (though I am not sure what tops the Destiny 1 raids in D2). If you never played Destiny 2 give it a try its a good bit of fun.

Now here is why I don't recommend this game overall and its not just a Destiny problem, its general bad game design that has plagued the industry but Destiny 2 takes it to another level.

- Daily and Weekly Chores if you want certain exotics, certain items or optimal progression get ready to have to do boring missions like kill 50 enemies with a specific gun type or grenade element. You have to do this daily and it forces you to play and change your gear to the terms of the quest.

- The Repetitive Grind revolves around doing your daily quests, season quests which are like daily quests with a longer time limit, the same strikes over and over and the same exploration missions. (Unlike Diablo there is no randomly generated enemies that can really push your build either)

- Progression is boring; get to a certain gear level and then your progress is tied to doing raids on each of your characters and for optimal progression you will want to have multiple max level characters and do daily quests with those alternate characters.. 10/10 :(.

- They have basically thrown out old content and your legendaries are only good for about a year and a half before they are retired and new seasonal gear comes out that you will want to get. (This means content is thrown out and you will have to wait for it to come back if ever to play it again).

- There was some good ideas such as in D1 Forsaken King DLC where you had secret area's and missions based on hidden conditions, exploration was more of a focus but I have not seen that with D2 instead its rather kill the same enemies, the same boss no randomization to keep things fresh and a lack of variety of things to do besides run strikes, raids and missions, weekly & dailies.

Tons of Potential but bad game design choices hold this game back like the majority of other online games these days.

Take a hint developers; Daily Quests, Weekly Quests ARE NOT FUN they ruin the experience, if your designing progression to be tied to those mechanics you are failing your players. I don't play games for generic static chore lists that don't even let me play the game the way I want to.
