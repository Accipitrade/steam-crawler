\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've put far to many hours into D1 and D2 on the Xbox and have just moved over to PC.
For newcomers welcome to the game and some quick bits of advice:
1. The community is mostly friendly and if your ever stuck try asking others for help (you'll be surprised how may people will).
2. Take quests and bounties when you can. Even if your not working towards them you may complete them while your out in the wild.
3. Do the story missions. While I'll admit the story (up till year 2) is a bit washy. It'll provided good experience and you'll learn basic raid mechanics.

Shadowkeep:
I've only played for 10 hours so far. But so far the story has been intriguing (especially if your into the lore). Armor 2.0 is a great improvement and really allows you yo customize how you play. Weapons feel punchy and there is a satisfying progression to the higher light levels.
One negative or positive depending on how you look at it is the new moon map is almost identical to D1. For me I'm happy with this as its great to go back and see how things have changed but I can understand why people may be disappointed if they wanted a "new location".

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played Bungie games since I was a kid and if Destiny would remind me of anything accurately from those games, it'd be Halo, particularly the first few games. Destiny feels comfortable in terms of a shooter gameplay, there is a pleasant repetitive aspect to its core gameplay element, the social/multiplayer aspect everyone craves for and a -story- to spice everything up in a nice stew.
If I were to play Steam reviewer, the pros of this game are very subjective, depending on each player's preferences and playstyle, yet here's some of them:
*Repetition
*Quick paced arena shooting PVP (Specially for Quake fans, this is the deal besides Q Champs)
*The overall aesthetic of just about everything science fiction
*Graphics and how easy most engines can handle such pretty space a e s t h e t i c s
*Tons of items to hunt and tons of items to upgrade, plenty of builds and playstyles. You're never forced to leave some items that you like behind. (Just upgrade them!)
*It feels like Borderlands and Halo made a kid which made both parents proud.

Now, since everyone craves for cons too, here's what I've observed based on my own needs:

*No proper system to build up your own personal char looks, no transmog, whatever you want to call it. The gear you wear is the gear you dye (Which is the only way to look somewhat fancy.) I wish they'd implement some manner of gear looks replacement.
*Dyes run out. While it helps some currency flow left and right, it'd be cool if they'd make a dye permanent and just require some low-tier currency to re-apply since some dyes are rare. :)
*Myself, for even whining at this game.

Play it, it's great.\t\t\t\t\t
