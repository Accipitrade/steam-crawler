\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIt's hard to say what there is to love about Destiny, without commenting on what not to love about it in tandem, and I don't want to tear this game down at all. It is by far, one of if not my favorite games of all time. There are problems with it like any other game. Yes content is missing that was there last year. Yes the meta in PvP is stale. Yes the core playlist, especially for free to play players, is basically void of anything worth doing. But my god, there is just something about this game that, for the right person, really pulls you in. And I don't mean in an addictive way. I play it maybe 5 hours a week max these days. But those 5 hours consistently are some of the most fun I have in any game.

I love the classes, the mechanics, the movement, the storyline (yes it used to be a joke but these days the lore and the story are legitimately some of the best written content Bungie has ever produced). On top of all that, the music is some of the greatest music in any game I've ever played. The game is beautiful, it is fun, and, especially with friends, it's just such a great game to pass time with bonding with others. I love this game. I am biased, I understand that, I recognize that, so take it for what you will with that context in mind, but really if you and your friends are willing and able to put up with some of the issues the game has (mind you ALL GAMES HAVE PROBLEMS) you really will have a great time with Destiny. I can't recommend this game enough, and with how the direction of the game is looking, it's shaping up to only get even better from here.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIf you're picking up this game, don't get trapped in the cycle of grinding stuff to easier grind for better stuff, it's hard to avoid this since it's core to Destiny's design, but very often you have the choice to make it work (with what you have), or working till you have no choice but to use the best stuff, which, after a few hours in the game, let me tell you, it isn't too fun grinding for good weapon 'roll' 'til your eyes bleed.

Personally, I mostly play the pvp, so sometimes I do have to grind out the pve to stay on top of meta equipment, however, Destiny 2 is an incredibly vast game with a lot of choices in terms of loadouts/synergies you can build and use, while still maintaining a competitive edge because of their lack of use.

Final Verdict:
Is fun, grindy if you want it, max your funness in the game, too many sweatlords for it to be anything else.\t\t\t\t\t
