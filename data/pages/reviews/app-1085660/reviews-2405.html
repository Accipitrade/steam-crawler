\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBought all the dlcs and played through endgame contents like raids, dungeons and hunts, is it so bad that I want a refund? No, but if there's a thumb down following by a smaller thumb up, that would be my score.

What does it do right? Gunplay and some of the level design, combat requires awareness of your surroundings and sometimes intense collaboration with your teammates. On top of that you get tons of toys to play around, if you just want a decent looter shooter, sure it does a pretty competent job.

What does it do wrong?
1. Stories and characters are so bad that I wanna hire two giant donkeys until they kick both my ears deaf so I don't ever have to listen to any of em talk. True, you don't have to like the story to enjoy a looter shooter, but those suckers will talk nonstop. If you tell me the writers deliberately do it so over the top to make it a satire against bad writing, then I would rather believe you. Just look up any character in this game, listen to them talk for a minute or two, how do you feel about that? Now imagine you have to listen to that over and over throughout the grind of a looter shooter.

2. Extreme requirements in endgame content but piss poor support structure built around it. It asks you to take a Bungie Jump sucking your right toe while holding up your left ring finger the whole time, but hey your safety rope might or might not work. First of all, connection issues. If they have to codename every animal existed for each connection issue, you know how bad it is. It could get frustrating in endgames contents like raids. Then the matchmaking, it's extremely dated and the community built a decent structure around it, but still, it doesn't excuse Bungie with their piss poor support structure for extreme requirements in endgame content. Lastly the anti learning curve. You either become the absolute liability with the other competent five, or you will unlikely to get through even halfway with five greens. How do you learn? Youtube videos, which leads to my next point.

3. I think this is how part of Bungie's marketing strategy work: Design overly difficult mechanic driven endgame contents, gives slim chance for you to figure it out yourself in the game. Players will go on internet forums to talk about it or resort to content creators to figure it out. Either way it significantly bump up the traffic of the game. Raids, exotic quests you name it.
Now this alone wouldn't even be that much of a negative. Community thrives, human connection made, and corporate profits gained. But here's where my first two points come. Stories and characters are nut crunchingly bad that the one thing truly shines content-wise is the endgame contents and the exotic quests. And they didn't build a competent structure to support it. Now look at the anti learning curve and their marketing strategy in this context, it leaves a bad taste in my mouth.

Would I recommend destiny 2 to anyone I know? No. Would I stop someone as if it is a plague. No.
Here's the sad part, it looks so good from the outside if you haven't played it yet, it seems to have the potential to be a game that transcends beyond others in the same genre when you starts to play it. But in the end, it's a big budget studio project that fail to deliver its ambition, at least it is the case to me.\t\t\t\t\t
