\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAlright, I've held off long enough. Yes, I would, in fact, recommend this game to new people.

I love this game. Call me a simp or whatever, but I have bought all the expansions and I've been playing this game on and off since it came out. For every dollar I have spent on this game, I got at least an hour of play time, usually several. Bungie consistently updates the game, adding new content and removing things they tried that didn't work very well.

When this game first launched, I did not purchase it because I wasn't into Sci-Fi and that's what I (mistakenly) thought Destiny was. When the game became free, I tried it and absolutely hated it. I said, "I hate this game and I didn't even pay for it. If I payed money for this trash heap I would be furious." But, Dude, They listened and changed the game. And when the old content got where it couldn't be salvaged, they reworked the game from the ground up and improved it.

Destiny 2 is now solidly in my top ten favourite games. Cause, I get to play a Space-Wizard with a six-gun that shoot lasers, Man. This game is Space-Fantasy, not Science-Fiction, and I absolutely Adore it. It's not to everyone's taste, but it's so much fun! At least for me.

Now, While I love this game, it does have some things about it that are mixed.
1: Due to the game being live with seasons, it is like the game is alive. The scenery changes and the events change the world, which is great if you've been playing since the beginning.
2:As a result of point 1, the game has Fear of Missing Out pretty bad. If you don't play during a season you miss that content.
3:Thus, you cannot really play the game over from the beginning. You can absolutely redo quests and things on a new character, but you can't change your worldstate back to the old days. New guardians will wake up without some context and be a bit confused.
4:Which pretty well emulates the way the game works. You wake up from Ghost choosing you to be his Guardian and are thrust into a galaxy wide conflict.
5:Except, the game treats your character as if they've been around the *whole* time, which can break immersion.
6:EXOs Lore Spoiler EXOs have a number beside their name, which is how many time's they have had their memory reset. you could totally just play an EXO with a number beside their name and have a Lore reason for why your character doesn't remember slaying gods. Not that the game let's you actually make dialogue choices though.

These are not positive or negative, they simply are, but I put them here as they may rebuff some people.

All in all, Destiny 2 is a really fun game to play with friends or alone. The weapons are cool, the story is fun, and I get to play a Space-Wizard, what's not to love?\t\t\t\t\t

And personally I feel this game isn't a free to play, it's a free to try game which isn't all that fun all while getting shoved with adds to buy the expansions and game.
Also game would be much more enjoyable with the servers were actually functional. Constantly getting kicked out of game due to errors in the servers.
Paying the big bucks every season and expansion only to get re-skinned or rehashed content is a slap to my face. I'm glad the game is almost over so I can be done and un-install the game never to look back on it again. I've only gotten the expansions and season passes because I've played this long and I want to see the end just so I can say I was there for that roller coaster ride.

Pros:
Fun: Can be fun with friends (if you have the money, willing to deal with crashing servers)
Gameplay: As far as point and shoot/dodge/grenades is fun
Lore: AMAZING LORE though you probably would be better looking it up.

Cons:
Not fun: Game is frustrating and sad with crashing servers, glitches and bugs happening all the time, cheaters.
Expensive: Garbage company charging crazy high prices for everything, in the in game store a skin is $20 for one character.
Resold content: Company had us pay for expansions and season passes only to "vault/sunset" all the content that we grinded for and not offer a refund.
PvP: No new maps for in years, PvP community has been abandoned by the company, infested with cheaters.
Pay to win: Game is slowly over the years entering pay to win territory.


Final thoughts, new players I would suggest not investing in this game. Your money and time will probably be better spent elsewhere.
