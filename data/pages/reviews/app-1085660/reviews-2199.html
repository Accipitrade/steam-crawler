\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny is a peculiar game. It's the best game I'll never recommend to anyone. I have almost 1400 hours in on PC alone, and probably 2000-3000 on Destiny 1.

Let me put it bluntly: New players will be VERY confused, since the beginning and middle of the storylines are sunset( removed from the game to save server space for future expansions). In the Destinations menu, there's a recap of what's happened so far in the top right, but I would recommend watching a playthrough to catch up since the ingame index is the cliffnotes. There's a massive story that new players are unable to access otherwise, and the cutscenes are phenomenal. While it may be confusing, it's pretty straightforward. If you click on a quest, it highlights the location of it, and you can press TAB to use your Ghost to guide you. There's a lot for FTP players, but you DO need to buy DLCs for Season Passes, Raids, and a good chunk of other content. I would recommend considering the preorders, since you DO save a few bucks and get an entire year of season passes, but don't do so unless you're ready. Make sure you want what's there before you spend a hundred bucks. If you want to do raids, there's groups called Sherpas you can find. Sherpas guide players through raids, and these raids are tedious and take HOURS. Hating the game is a part of the process of playing it. You WILL hate this game, but so do all Destiny veterans. We hate it with a burning passion, but we always come back.


Overall, Destiny is a fantastic game, and one of the best ever made. This game is absolutely brilliant since Bungie got ActiVision's greedy little hands off of it. The season passes , unlike some other games, are absolutely worth the price. I love the content, there are so many lovable characters, the designs of gear are a labor of love, and the seasons last three months. I was in love with the previous game, but I absolutely adore this one.\t\t\t\t\t
