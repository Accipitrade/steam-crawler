Action FPS + MMORPG.

Excellent gameplay/feel (typical Bungie-level satisfying), really good story and characters, playable solo or with friends.
The game has an extensive, very rich and interesting lore for those interested (as well as an amazing soundtrack). The overall atmosphere of this fantasy-scifi game is quite unique and will be compelling for a lot of people.

However, this has sadly become one of the absolute worst FOMO experiences in any game : the devs keep deleting paid content to ""make room for"" new content (this is a lie)... This is one of the worst things I have ever seen in a live-service game, let alone in an MMO (LOL), and I don't even know if this is legal or not. Oh well, the community doesn't seem to have any problem with it, and you get harassed if you dare point it out...
And because of that + the seasons (Battle Passes), Destiny 2 has one of the worst, most frustrating FOMO situations in any video game ever (and don't get me started on the micro-transaction store and the Pay-to-Win mechanics lmao).

Oh and this is actually not a "Free-to-Play" game, it's just a "Free-to-Try" game ; and if you weren't there from the start, don't bother trying to understand anything when it comes to the story, as, like I said, every campaign (+ its story, loot and locations) eventually vanished after 2-3 years, even when you paid 100 bucks for it.

In conclusion, Destiny as a franchise is just disappointing and disheartening, and I can say that I long for the day when this amazing universe wil finally be rebooted from the ground up, in a new actual (PC ?) MMO-RPG (why not open world while we're at it) with no FOMO and no narrative deletion. But I do not kid myself, I know Bungie is no longer independant, so this dream is becoming less and less realistic.\t\t\t\t\t
