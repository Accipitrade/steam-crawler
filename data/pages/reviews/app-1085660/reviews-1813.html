\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have a love/hate relationship with Destiny 2. Let me explain.

What I love about Destiny 2:
-Gameplay. The gameplay of destiny 2 is amazing. Im not talking about the content or the story or anything like that. Im talking about the general feel in game while playing. No other game has the smoothness and fluidity of movement that Destiny 2 has. There are a multitude of options for how to build and customize your character and create a playstyle that suits you best. There are no limits to how you can play this game. Movement and general gameplay is what keeps drawing me back into Destiny 2. As a PvP player, the movement and skill ceiling that is present within the crucible is unrivaled among other games in the FPS class.
-Graphics. This game is gorgeous. Back in 2014 with destiny 1 i was always in awe of the beautiful worlds that bungie created and this is continued into Destiny 2. I cant think of another game that is as beautiful to me as this game. Graphics are phenominal.
-Endless possibilities. As i mentioned above, you can play this game any way that you want. With time and effort, you can craft a build that plays to your strengths as a player and allows you to have a great gameplay experience.

Things i dont love:
-Loot/Grind. I understand that Destiny 2 is a looter shooter but recent changes to the game are making this less accessible for the average player. With the introduction of armor retirement, players are forced to keep regrinding weapons and armor. For the average player who just wants to play casually, it is difficult to keep up.
- Balancing. Every season and patch brings about a new host of broken and overpowered weapons into the mix. In PvP, it is very difficult to compete and keep up with the crowd unless you are using a weapon from the small pool of what is meta that season. In a game with so much loot, there should be better balancing of the weapon classes to make them more viable to the playerbase and create more variety within the crucible. Bungie also tends to over buff or over nerf weapons. Buffed weapons are usually busted for a long time and when they finally do get nerfed they become unusable. More time playtesting on the developer end would create a more balanced environment for all players.
- Cheaters/Recovs/Carries: I am lumping these all together because they all degrade the quality of the pvp experience for the average player. Most notably in Competative and Trials, the presence and cheaters and account recoveries makes high level crucible unaccessible for many players and as such these gamemodes suffer from an extremely concentrated playerbase that makes it even more difficult.


Overall Destiny 2 is a great gameplay experience and definetly worth trying out. If you are into FPS games, give D2 a shot and maybe youll fall in love with it like i did.\t\t\t\t\t
