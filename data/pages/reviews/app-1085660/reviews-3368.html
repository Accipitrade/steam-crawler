\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2. Still not quite as good as Destiny 1. However, it's improving. With the departure of Activision, Bungie are now thinking more about the fans. Take consumables, they're basically free now. If you've had a shader once then you can reclaim as many as you like for a small fee of a couple of legendary shards per. The new battle-pass come with the new DLC, no extra £15 just for the battle-pass, and it gives you 100 items.

Destiny 2 is fun; it runs like a knife though butter; it's sound design is almost flawless and it looks amazing. It brings an interesting and enjoyable story that is still growing with every weekly reset. You can just get lost in the world all around you. Meanwhile, on the other end of the spetruem competative and PvP into the mix. I personally had a lot of fun climbing as high as 4950mmr. The beauty of Destiny is that both PvE and PvP can be mixed. It brings them both into one neat package. You have a certain weapon you like in PvE? Why not try it in PvP?

The Power Level system, or Light Level as most people will refer to it as, is unique and works really well. You can choose what difficulty to tackle and activity at. Want a challenge? Go in at a lower level. Finding it too hard? Level up some more and try again. It allows for each player top tailor there experience to them.

However, Destiny 2 has it's shortcoming. It's grossly unbalanced at times. Now as someone who likes PvP this effects me a lot. However, PvE players are not exempt or immune. Take a weapon like The Recluse. It's an pretty standard SMG. However get 1 kill and it becomes a power house. All it's perks are completely gear'd to making this weapon a demon after the first kill. Master of arms is a perk that is exclusive to The Recluse. It activated when you get the final blow on an enemy. It takes the weapon to a 0.4 second ttk in PvP. It's the only weapon you'll see in PvE because Mater of Arms is so easy to pull off too. It's easy to get and you'll get sick of it being the only viable weapon at end game. If you're not using it then people look at you funny.

Another example of this PvP unbalancing is Titans. There wall allows them to completely take control of heavy in a comp game. 'Whoever gets the heavy wins the round.' Bottom Tree Striker Titan is the thing that goes bump in the night. And if they're running the One Eyed Mask exotic just give up right there. To put it bluntly, it basically gives an overshield after every kill.

With all these issues you'd expect Bungie to step in with the Nerf Stick and wack away. Nope. It'll take Bungi up to a few months to a couple of years to address and fix these issues. I will say they've been better with the launch of Shadowkeep and with any luck they'll continue to improve.

All this said I would still recommend Destiny 2. It's free for a start. I don't see why you can't try it. Just go for it. I'd rate this game at a 7/10.\t\t\t\t\t
