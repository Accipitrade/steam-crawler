\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOk, So I played the original Destiny and Destiny 2 on battlenet and loved both.

Pros:

- The game being free for newer players has gotten 3 of my friends alone to start playing and to buy the expansion(s)/season pass.
- There is so much content, a lot of exotic hunting, and so many triumphs (achievements) to complete for people who like being perfectionists
- You can raid with 5 friends, do dungeons with 2 friends, patrol with 2 friends, and do pvp with 2-5 friends. A lot to do with friends.
- The graphics are STILL beautiful and the game is optimized with me running it at 1440p and 144+fps... smooth as butter.
- Some of if not the best gunplay for a FPS currently out. Every gun is unique and helps diversify how you can/want to play the game. One of the biggest pros of the game.

Cons:

- A lot of disconnect errors (beaver,cockatiel,anteater,etc). This happens with my whole group of friends from 50/50Mbps to my gigabit connection and ALL of us have the same problems.
- Cheating in pvp. It is a shame because I remember going flawless in Trials of the nine on D1 and D2 when it came out. I would go flawless on all 3 of my characters every week. After coming back to this game it seems next to impossible for me to go flawless. My skill didn't magically deteriorate (i maintain k/d 1.7+) and can outshoot just about anyone I come into contact with. Just about every other game I have a cheater in my Trials matches with it being blatant (with TTV names or a different name) carrying 2 guys who have at best a .5 k/d with his 8-10+ k/d in my matches. People are paying to be carried by cheaters and Bungie is doing nothing to stop them.


TLDR: Amazing amount of PVE content, great story, great graphics, great optimization, ♥♥♥♥♥♥ disconnects, cheaters in pvp.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tMy recommendation is to PLAY THE RED LEGION CAMPAIGN FIRST!

"The Red Legion" is the name of the first campaign in Destiny 2. A few years ago it was a mandatory campaign (at least the first mission), but later on they threw this an two more campaigns into a "Legacy" mode and now you don't have to play them, you even won't find them or want them if you don't know they even existed.

The problem with that is The Red Legion campaign explains THE WHOLE THING to you, the world, the races, what are guardians and ghosts and how they relate to the light and the traveler, who are the main NPCs that give you missions and why is there a freaking giant ball falling apart in front of the city.
If you don't play that one campaign you don't know what the hell are you doing, and you're missing out on the main thing a lot of us love about Destiny 2 and games in general: The context!

For real, now D2 starts on the 3rd campaign, everything is just going on and the characters are already developed. You just fall in the city and the main characters of the story are just NPCs that give you missions, Going around making nonsensical quests feels like a generic mmo, but after getting introduced to the whole thing, each quest feels more real and like you're really making a difference and that each character you get to know is important.

Pros:
- Fantastic story line, though you can get really lost if you don't actively decide to play the campaigns in order.
- PVE is delicious, the satisfaction is high for killing trash mobs as well as big hard bosses, be it with melee swords, sniper bows or to the face shotguns and SMG.
- It's visually stunning still in 2020.
- Weapon and equip play is fantastic with lots of perks, mods and leveling, each weapon feels unique, even if it's just the same with another perk roll.
- Gambit, the PVE+PVP mode is super fun and it's a good place for a lot of team play.
- Clans have cool multiplayer functionality.

Be aware:
- The story missions are all mixed up and you need to actively look for the missions in order and try not to spoil the whole thing or get confused by playing campaigns in the wrong order.

Cons:
- PVP (Crucible) is completely broken. As the matchmaking is Connection Based, what matters to the matchmaker is that players are close and have a stable connection. Which means there's no elo or any other means of measuring each player skill level. High level long trained players go around the map killing people as if they were trash mobs and you as a new player can't do anything about it. The GIT GUD feeling is too overwhelming it doesn't even makes sense.
