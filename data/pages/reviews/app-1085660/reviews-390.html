\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNot many games, especially online games, have kept my attention for as long as Destiny 2 has. With over 1000+ hours across PS4 and PC, I’ve lived through some of its highs, and undoubtedly its lows. Though there must be something about it that keeps me coming back, keeps me looting another lost sector, keeps me checking off another bounty and so on. There probably is one thing that gives me the urge to play another mission, to jump right back into the action, to live through a multi-year long story arc, but I couldn’t tell you what it was.

Destiny 2 and I have always had a love hate relationship. There are times when I will praise it like it is the best game ever made, while other times I’d rather it burn in the very temperate flames of the Sun. Destiny 2 is a game that has changed from its launch so many years ago, and while the comparisons can be made to show a lot of positive change, it’s not all sunshine and rainbows (or Hive Tomb Ships and Exploding Thrall).

The biggest negative I have always given Destiny 2, is its lack of Solo Player focused endgame activities. The best we have now would probably be the Preservation mission, that came alongside the Witch Queen, but even then that required a Group activity to be completed first before it was unlocked. Heroic Nightfalls and Heroic Campaign missions come to mind also, but they don’t deliver the same bountiful exhilaration of completing a Raid or Master Nightfall, not to mention the rewards. As a Solo Player through and through, there is only so much I can play before it feels meaningless.

Though I have always felt somewhat ignored as a Solo Player across my tenured playtime, Destiny 2 has kept me coming back, always made me buy the next expansion. Part of it is probably to do with my love for the first game, and Destiny 2 has just further expanded the universe I fell in love with. A part of it is also to do with the bountiful amount of loot to hoard, I mean it wouldn’t live up to its looter shooter title if it didn’t. From cult classic exotics like Last Word or Ace of Spades, through to newer entries like Osteo Striga (I love that gun), and on to the bountiful armours and Legendary gear, every drop could be another notch on the toolbelt, besides those pesky blues of course.

However, the biggest factor for me is the story. One thing I love in a game above anything, is a good story, one that can make you feel invested, one that makes you feel involved. Destiny 2 has a story that from the days of the original Destiny and the Black Garden, through to current day Destiny 2 and the Witch Queen arc, not to mention everything in between, has had me hooked from the get go.

There have been characters I loved, like the one and only Cayde-6, through to characters I love to hate, like Uldren Sov. There have been moments where we’ve been against the blade of battle, against the numerous species (the Hive being my personal favourite), and even against those we once thought of as allies. The one thing the story has done very well, is give us many answers, but so many more questions. It is the act of mystery that keeps you guessing, keeps you wondering, what is the endgame, when does all this fighting mean something, and are we fighting on the right side?

In conclusion then, I like this game for my own reasons. The story, the rich universe and mysteries untold. There is so much to this game that words will never do it justice, from the subclasses, PvP content, the freedom of character progression, and much more. The base game is free, so if you’ve been on the edge wondering about playing it, there is no harm in giving it a try, feeling for what the game can offer.

So until you’re bashing heads with Cabal, or disrupting rituals of the Hive. Eyes up Guardian.\t\t\t\t\t
