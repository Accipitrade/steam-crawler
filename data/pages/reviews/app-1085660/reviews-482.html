\r\n\t\t\t\t\tYes I have 963 hours, but I cannot recommend this game. The main reason why is because of Bungie wanting to 'vault' content, many of it paid content. When Beyond Light released, Bungie vaulted the base game, Curse of Osiris, and Warmind including campaigns and raids. Many destinations like IO and Mercury were also vaulted along with their strikes. I played through Beyond Light, but the game just feels too empty now, and Bungie still feels the need to vault content which is why I'm dropping the game. With Witch Queen releasing, Bungie will be vaulting the Forsaken campaign as well along with other content. Now imagine this: What if you bought a video game, like God of War, and then three years later, Sony just locks you out of the game with no set time for when the content will supposedly return? This is the problem. Regardless if the content becomes free later, there is no excuse to remove content and promise to return it at a later date.

Some argue that it doesn't matter since 'no one plays that content anymore.' This is a very flawed argument. Raids were still being played regularly based on LFG activity. Challenge runs for raids, whether the game implemented them or players put the challenges on themselves, are now impossible to do since those raids are gone. Strikes were randomized as well to keep the playlist fresh and engaging since you would have a larger pool of strikes to play through. Now that most are gone, the playlist is incredibly stale. Crucible has had no new maps in over a year, which is absolutely ridiculous. Gambit as well got no new maps, and I think a map may have been vaulted as well. All this content is actively played. Campaigns can be reasoned that no one played it, but that's because the process of replaying a campaign was to remake a whole new character, which is ridiculous. Also, with that much content just gone, new players are going to be even more confused than ever. Even if they played Destiny then came to Destiny 2, there is going to be a huge chunk of content just gone. The fact that there is no date or estimate on when this content will return is just sad.

New content as well feels bland, boring, and lazy. Bungie really brought back a raid from Destiny, made a few changes, and called it a new raid. There was also only one new strike in Beyond Light, and no new dungeon. The raid Deep Stone Crypt is also incredibly easy and requires very little thought or communication.

Beyond this, the game does have some high points. The gunplay is satisfying, the lore is interesting, and the gameplay can be fun; but none of this excuses the removal of content. Raids were the highest point of content in the game. Six man activities like this are very fun. Dungeons were also a high point being 3 man mini-raids. The main stories are usually not very interesting, but a lot of the background lore is interesting. It has an enjoyable loop of earning weapons and armor to see those pretty number grow higher and higher. If you really, really love MMO games with lots of loot, then sure, go for it, just know that probably 3 years down the line your content will likely be 'vaulted' for an indefinite amount of time.

Judging on gameplay, I give it a 7/10. With the vaulting ♥♥♥♥, I give it a 3/10, and I'm still gonna play the game cause I'm too damn addicted to it.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game is a joy to play. The movement is fluid, the gunplay is satisfying and responsive, and the experience the game offers as a whole is unique. It's the only FPS I've played with such a large focus on teamwork and build customization, with every weapon or armor mod offering something to help you make incredibly synergistic builds. Individual builds can also be tailored to be compatible with other players', allowing for team-wide absurdity that almost feels like exploitation of the game mechanics. It's too bad the developers disrespect their entire customer base by bleeding their money away with live service expansions and locking (one specific weapon) behind a paywall, which is why I have chosen to stop playing.

Destiny 2's strongest suit by far is its cooperative activities. There is no experience quite like teaming up with a group of friends or strangers and overcoming raids and Nightfall strikes, some of the game's greatest challenges. Making strategies and practicing to overcome those challenges and get rewards is not only enjoyable, but also incredibly memorable. For example, I haven't really played this game for about 4 months at the time of writing this review, but to this day I could guide a raid team through the entirety of the Deep Stone Crypt like the instructions were written on the back of my hand. Even the PvP aspect has a decent chance of being fun if you have friends to team up with.

The main grievance I have with this game is its current business model, particularly with the way the developers chose to implement live-service content. Yes, this content is technically "free," but if you don't want to dedicate your life to the grind, Bungie offers you the alternative of (you guessed it) paying Real Money. Buying the season pass for $10 gives the player 99 extra rewards, including experience boosts, early access to the season's exclusive exotic weapon, and increased reward drops from the season's featured event. Toward the end of a season, you also have the option of paying even more Real Money to instantly rank up your season pass. I didn't mind when Bungie monetized their cosmetics, but even having the option to monetize the gameplay loop is disrespectful to the playerbase. This doesn't even mention the 30th Anniversary pack, which is *one* dungeon and *one* weapon behind a higher paywall than a typical season pass, iirc. Disgusting.

Destiny 2 once reigned supreme as the gold standard of cooperative shooters. With the state that this game is in now, I wouldn't recommend it to anyone anymore.\t\t\t\t\t
