\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI didn't want to make a bad review but after getting back into the game, I can't stop thinking about how the game has changed in bad ways for a player like me.

First off I like Destiny, the story stuff and so on, even after they bailed on the first game so quickly.

D2 started out ok at first and got better then just turned into a giant grind simulator with players only caring about stats and how quickly they can get through missions to get loot.

As a causal gamer, I don't have the time that this game has turned into to complete "events" and such so I get to miss out of a lot of things and storys. I still do not know how to activate the missions or raids of that Big enemy guy with all the Gold or even the story of all that. I shouldn't have to wiki a game.

Strikes suck since you have to do them with random people that just run through the things so quickly I miss out on what's going on. This is a game people, if you're that focused on just stats and loot then why are you playing a "game."

Now they want $40 and more for a new DLC, but is it a DLC or are we paying for the whole game again and it comes with a new story? Paying real money for in game currency? I know that silver currency has been here for a while now but come on. When a game wants customers to give real money for digital stuff that'll just be worthless when the next game comes out, it stop's being a game.

Now the developer wants D2 to be like Fortnite with active events and crap? I player shouldn't have to be shackled to a game to enjoy the content.


I just can not recommend this for a causal gamer or someone that plays, then stops for awhile and gets back into it. You'll just miss out on a lot and be confused on what's going on.

If you like grinding for loot and numbers and not actually play a game, then this is for you.

Bungie has done a terrific job of ruining what was once a game and perveting it to a cash grab tool.\t\t\t\t\t
