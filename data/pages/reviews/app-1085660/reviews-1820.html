\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFun Spider Facts for Kids!

• Spiders are arachnids, not insects.

• Other members of the arachnid family include scorpions, mites, ticks and harvestmen.

• Spiders have 8 legs while insects have 6.

• Spiders don’t have antennae while insects do.

• Spiders are found on every continent of the world except Antarctica.

• There are around 40000 different species of spider.

• Most spiders make silk which they use to create spider webs and capture prey.

• Abandoned spider webs are called cobwebs.

• Most spiders are harmless to humans but a few spider species, such as the black widow, can bite humans and inject venom. Deaths from spider bites are rare however.

• An abnormal fear of spiders is called ‘arachnophobia’.

• Tarantulas are large and often hairy spiders, the biggest species have been known to kill mice, lizards and birds.

• Most tarantula species pose no threat to humans.

• The largest specie of tarantula is the Goliath Birdeater.

• Giant Huntsman spiders have leg-spans of around 30cm (12 in).

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHaving started my journey with Destiny on PS4 then playing Destiny 2 on Xbox One I was glad to come over to PC and finally Steam. My hours recorded here is a fraction of my total play time in this game's universe.

Let me get out two points that I think everyone reading reviews for a PC game looks for. Just so if you are looking for quick thoughts you can move on:

First, this game is a great PC port. Other than few older areas in the game world. It runs fantastically. I added a 50 dollar graphics card to a several year old work mini-desktop. It runs better than the console version on that. At home I have a Ryzen build with a 5700XT. I can get to 120-144 FPS at 2K at a pretty stable rate.

Second. Is this game worth your time and energy? Yes. What you get with the free to play version is enough to leave anyone happy. I think both expansions are worth it. And they always go on sale. Season passes in my opinion have been worth the ten bucks too, but I would recommend getting into those once you have spent some time in the game.

If you care. The rest of this review is why I play this game. Honestly give this game a try. You have probably heard so much about this game. Good and bad. Jump in and see what you think.

So why do I play this game?

The games that formulated what I want out of a game were Halo/Halo 2, Star Wars Galaxies and then a lil later on a number of RPGs including TES: Oblivion, KOTOR, Mass Effect, Dragon Age: Origins, and Demon's Souls.

I love great gameplay, beautiful worlds to explore, an active and passive sense of community, lore that adds context to everything and a player character that slowly over the course of time has their own story written by the player, other players and these systems I mentioned.

I wouldn't feel that Destiny was a game created just for me until I did my first raid in Destiny 1. The Vault of Glass. 6 years ago I joined up with 5 random people and we went into the Vault of Glass. We spent hours upon hours in this raid. It was the freshest experience in gaming at the time. A challenging 6 player environment with bosses, phases, strategy, puzzles and platforming. All in a FPS game.

I play this game because my character has been shaped by so many similar moments. Moments that are unique to this game. This game rewards players who stick with the game, but don't think you have to play 2-5 hours a day. You don't even have to play every week. It's not just raids. Community events bring players together to solve HUGE puzzles or complete challenges. Resulting in new content, new PVP modes, story cutscenes, lore books, new weapons and so much more.

Bungie is striving for two main things currently: to deepen the game and make it a truly evolving world. They have had their hits and misses since leaving their publisher, but I believe we will see more and more hits. It makes me excited because I think Destiny is one of the most unique games on the planet. Destiny is gaming's best known secret.
