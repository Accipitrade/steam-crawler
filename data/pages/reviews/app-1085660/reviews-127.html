\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny is made by the legendary Bungie who crafted the original Halo and redefined the shooter genre. Their work is on full display in Destiny 2 with epic weapons and skills to use in well-crafted missions and of course the endless chase for ever more powerful gear and seasonal builds.

I think this game is fantastic but I can't recommend it for casuals. In Destiny they are removing powerful weapons every year to be replaced with a new batch but often the most powerful options are from the past. Completing the most difficut content requires masterwork weapons and gear which are often out of reach for casuals. Destiny requires specific knowledge of how to play optimally in order to get the most out of each season and if you aren't doing endgame activities you are missing a large portion of what the game has to offer and many players won't see those endgame activities not due to skill but more impart to a lack of time to commit to playing and lack of knowledge on how to optimize play-time.

Is Destiny 2 hella fun, most definitely but its not a game for everyone. Unless you're going to play 10+ hrs a week its probably not worth jumping into. However if you really enjoy the gunplay then I'd say it's worth it to persevere and build your guardian up until your buildcrafting your way to victory like the OG's

Destiny 2 is full of secrets to discover, minor secrets like weapon interactions with exotics to major secrets like hidden missions with awesome rewards and that sense of wonder and excitement is what keeps me coming back after more than 1000+ hours. I take long breaks but I always come back to Destiny because its got the perfect combination of gameplay and community. I've played other games a lot more but most of my steam friends are from D2 because the community is pretty good.

Gameplay: A+
Story: B-
Sound: A
Visuals: B+
Control: A\t\t\t\t\t
