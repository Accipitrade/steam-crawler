\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played a lot of Destiny back in the day but Destiny 2 just isn't grabbing me the way the original game did. I feel like everything is just about season pass this and season event that. I tried to get back into D2 with Lightfall and as soon as I finished the Lightfall campaign and Witchfall campaigns I just was left with the grind, not a bad thing but the formula hasn't really changed much to me. If you have no intention of raiding or pvp then you're left with strikes, lost sectors and dungeons with the occaisional reason to go on patrol. I really wish Bungie would bring "arena" like activities back such as reckoning from Gambit or the Prison of elders. I feel like I've already seen everything Destiny 2 has to offer because the series for me hasn't really peaked since "The Taken King" back in Destiny 1. The 'new' mechanics from the original game just feel oppressive such as barrier champions. Instead of making more intelligent and engaging mechanics like they did in Forsaken briefly, the rest of Destiny 2 has felt lacking to me because instead of encuraging players to communicate and work around mechanics they just expect that each player has X weapon to help with X mechanic. It forces you to bring gear instead of letting players learn mechanics for each dungeon or fight, Remember in Rise of Iron when we had to translate a simple binary code for part of the raid? Remember Taken king when we had to engage with the rules set by the Dreadnought and learn the new ways to fight "the Taken" which was just a spin on how prior enemies worked?

Well D2 FINALLY introduced an interesting enemy with Lightfall, the Tormentor but outside of that I just get bored playing . I hope on a sparrow in a patrol with some music for an hour and all my motivation goes away when I look at how many days are left in a season. I remember that all the work I put into my gear to be able to "qualify" for raid's means nothing because in a few months its just reset and you have to grind light level again anyway.

I WANT to love Destiny, but it still feels so stagnant for someone like me who grew up and poured thousands of hours into the first game. Nothing will bring back the tears I had past midnight with my friends on Xbox live. Nothing will beat getting the Touch of Malice day one raid with my friends. Every experience I've had so far on Destiny 2 has just been a shell of what I enjoyed. It all feels shallow to me and I think I'm the problem. I think that because I had such a great time with people who're no longer with me or have moved on to other parts in life that I just haven't been able to recapture the lightning in the bottle. I'm chasing embers of a once guilded time. I haven't been able to enjoy Destiny 2 and I think it's because I'm always alone. I've tried the forums, I've tried raid finder and I just haven't met people I like to play with when it comes to Destiny 2.

I think if you have friends or if you never played Destiny 1, this is a must buy game and will probably be something you love. But it's not for me. I miss my midnight raiders. RIP Southsplash, Monygan.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWell I am done with this game at last. Bungie decided that we had all had it too easy and decided that it was time to up the overall difficulty of the game, forgetting that not everyone has 16 hours a day to devote to the grind. The big alterations to the Champion feature seemed like it would be an interesting way to help buildcrafting, however all it did was force a *different* build type if you wanted to be able to deal with both Champion types in an activity - which if you are running with randoms - you'll need to, because lord knows no-one else will bring anything that can deal with them.

Then there's the further revised "always under-level" gameplay of the power deltas in all of the mid and high level content. I appreciate that some people want an additional challenge, but there are players (including myself) that do not need it, and all it does is lock us out of gameplay. I've made peace with the fact that I don't get to enjoy Raids or Dungeons, but being locked out of the Seasonal activity and Nightfalls is something else. If I hadn't already put too much time into this year's expansion, I'd be looking to refund it.

Unless you have a large friend group that you will always get to play with, just don't bother. Communication is now paramount, and there is no casual play scene.

Fairwell Destiny, you've eaten your last few hours of my life.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game and it's one of me favorite games ever.

I've been playing since Forsaken and I have to say that Destiny 2 is a very fun and enjoyable game, more so than what some people say about it. Sure, it's not everything always good, but all games have their ups and downs. Destiny 2 is one of the few or even the only very good live service game out there, it has great story, interesting characters, awesame visuals, beautifull music and great loot. Don't get me wrong, there are negatives, the new light experience should be made better and seasonal content shouldn't leave, in my opinion that is.
But, if you are to join this game, be careful, the absolute worst thing about this game is the community. In all of my years playing games, no community was as wierd and bipolar as the Destiny one. This community is winning all the time (this is more common for Destiny 1 fans for some reason) and even on things that they asked, even if those things are delivered they way they wanted. Some of it is actual critisism, with some very good points and you can see that these people care about the game, but most of the "critisism" is constant winning (the Destiny 1 fans I mentioned before fall in this category most of the time). For example, they complain about the game being easy, but when Bungie increases the difficulty in a very pleasant way they complain abou the game being "too difficult". Although, some aspects of the community are nice and you can find some very helpful people.

Edit; I forgot to mention that in my opinion they should reduse the prises of the DLCs.

If there are some errors in my grammar please forgive me, english is my second language.
Thank you for your time and thank you for reading my Destiny 2 community rant.
Have I nice day.\t\t\t\t\t
