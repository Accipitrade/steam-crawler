\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Good:

Amazing, mechanically. Tons of variety for activities, and all sorts of builds to try. Want to be a teleporting space wizard that casually summons black holes? That's the Void Warlock. Want to be a wild-west style gunslinger empowered by the sun to throw explosive knives everywhere? That's the Hunter. Want to be the Lord of Lightning and bring the thunder? Thundercrash Titan has you covered. The story telling has been getting better to, but be prepared to watch a few lore videos or risk being at a total loss for what's going on. The iceberg runs deep. Oh, and did I mention the Loot? D2 has some pretty fantastic loot variety to keep you busy as a newcomer. Most weapons within a category will feel similar overall, but the particulars will change between weapons, and between the randomised perk rolls of the same weapon.
TLDR good: Activity variety, build variety, weapon variety, gunplay, story is on the rise (especially with the final expansion in this saga coming out in a couple months).

The Bad:

By Thor's sweaty, electrified marbles, the quality of life in this game is ♥♥♥♥♥. Worthless gear fills your inventory and requires constant management, or you risk losing the good stuff. You have fiv-... six hundred spaces in your shared vault between three characters, which is often insufficient to store the cornucopia of things you want to keep. resources are capped for seemingly no reason whatsoever, meaning you have to use up that precious vault space to store it (if you even can, it's by no means consistent). PvP severs are atrociously laggy, especially when the time-to-kill is less than a second. Problematic glitches don't get fixed for months at a time, but if they benefit the player in some way (especially regarding resource collection), often get patched in a couple of days. And last, but certainly not least, if you want to do the highest-end content you WILL be railroaded into specific builds and weapons due to a combination of the strength needed (not level, but build strength), and Champion enemy rotation. Oh, and the game can be really grindy chasing the perfect rolls on gear. If you aren't aiming for perfection though, the grind is surprisingly light most of the time.

What this game is not:

Free-2-play. Oh it says it on the tin, but you're dipping your feet in the kiddie pool of content, when the olympic swimming competition is right next door. It's not some monstrosity we often associate with the f2p model though. The seasonal content is pretty well priced, and if you're into the game, the yearly expansions are worth the full-game price tag. The only truely pricy thing in this game is the cosmetics shop, but that only applies to exotic skins (ornaments in destiny lingo), and even then sometimes you can get them for free.

A solo game. I play this game solo, enjoy it solo, and they're honestly getting better about it, but this is a highly multiplayer-focused game. If you don't have friends who want to play it with you, get a Discord looking-for-group server, because there's a lot of content you can't do solo. Luckily the majority of what you'll be doing is matchmade.

An RPG or MMO. This in an FPS first or action-rpg-fps hybrid with social elements. There are no decisions to be made here beyond your loadout or choice of activity. The story beats are; lore, shoot bad thing, lore, shoot bigger bad thing, wait for Ghost to hack ancient computer for five minutes, lore, use temporary godhood to kill The Big Bad (tm), get cool loot. You are never going to make a choice of who to save, or what side character to romance. You are going to kill drive your enemies before you and turn them to dust, whether they be God or Dreg, you are inevitable. Mostly because you don't stay dead.\t\t\t\t\t
