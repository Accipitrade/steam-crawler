\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR: I don't see any thought, care, or attention being put into this game. Even if you have a veteran to team up with and walk you through the many hurdles, I recommend to just play a game that actually wants you there. If you do play, either stay free, get your 10-20 hours out of it, and uninstall; or, calculate the season pass into the price of the DLC, otherwise half the game is still locked out from you and you might as well play free. Completely disagree with their monetization model and have completely given up on this game. Now to find out if I can refund DLC.


1st Day

Returning player experience is trash. Can't imagine the new player experience has been kept up with, either. Visited the Tower first, as usual. Cleared all the flashing NPCs on my map. Noticed half the stuff at vendors is locked behind season pass, even after you buy the latest DLC. What exactly did I buy? Many items in my inventory said they were no longer useful and could be dismantled. There is no way to dismantle these items. The term they meant to use, and that the UI uses, is Discard. Not a major issue, just a bit confusing; however, it is a good indicator that the quality of the game has dropped when they don't even know their own terminology.

The DLC is not introduced to you, you simply have to know that a new location is on the map and start it yourself. Before I got to that, I checked out a quest the game gave me (when I logged in?). It was a seasonal quest that takes me to a new (to me) location HELM. And, it appears to be bugged. I search the net to find out I have to do a quest on the Moon that the game makes no mention of. Abhorrent.

The Moon quest starts with a long, boring walk following some apparition I'm apparently supposed to know, followed by an ambush with a "Survive" objective. I haven't played in 2 years. How you build a character has changed drastically. All my gear levels have been boosted without me gaining any game experience to go along with it. Numerous weapon/balance changes have occurred. And you want me to survive an ambush as my first encounter? What a terrible welcome back.

It's 4:30 in the morning, probably my fault. Start fresh tomorrow.


2nd Day

Get through that and the stupid "Run" objective that follows it, including waiting 5+ seconds for the game to realize I was at my ship and to end the mission, and I'm back at HELM. Interact with the crown (which is just a spot on the floor in front of a poorly lit statue/figure/thing) and I get a huge wall of text combined with narration. I ignored the narration and begin to read the text, and about halfway through realized the narration was not following the text. Why would you do this? This is terrible flow.

Cool. My first artifact. Let's check it out! Awesome, I get to select a mod. Oh. The mod isn't activated on the artifact, it's simply added to my inventory and I have to put it on a piece of gear myself. This isn't a bonus!? This is just a convoluted way of giving me slightly better mods. WTF? This is pathetic design. Let's hope this engram I need to upgrade at the War Table actually feels good to get.

Go to the War Table and find the exchange on the 2nd page. Only Worldly Armor is available without a season pass, no weapon loot box (specific items can also be bought with additional currency that hasn't been introduced to me and the season pass). I don't really want armor, since I spent quite a bit of play time yesterday rejiggering it all and don't want to do it again until I get higher level gear and need to (oh yeah, this convoluted engram isn't even going to end up being of a higher level than my existing gear). I notice my quest isn't tracked, open it up, notice that the War Table isn't blinking on the map, but the crown place is...the quest text clearly says "at the War Table". Sloppy, again. Aggravating.

The crown place has both Worldly Armor and Worldly Weapons loot boxes. So I exchange for a weapon (I still loathe having to hold the interaction button down for several seconds to do anything in this game and you still can't delete stacks at a time - such a waste of time, I absolutely hate devs that waste my time). Hoping for a kinetic, but get an energy pulse rifle. Not better than my current legendary scout rifle. So, yay! I've now done absolutely nothing beneficial to my character in any way. Hours wasted and I still haven't even started the damned DLC!

While finishing the artifact stuff at the Tower, I am reminded that world currencies exist and pick up some cores - nice that we can now trade for X/week of various upgrade mats (I never bothered to learn how to get them before, I just "randomly" got them). I go around to worlds to clear out inventory items and maybe get some new weapons from reputation. EDZ is same as always, quick in and out, 2 levels (popped a masterwork, not immediately useful but may be fun to try later).

Dreaming City is unrecognizable. I start trading Baryon's for some tier 2 charges of light, since there is nothing else of interest. After 5, I can't buy any more. No reason why, I just can't buy any more. Go to inventory (for which there is still no hotkey for - "I" opens the character screen, because that makes sense) and see there is a cap of 5. Why isn't this shown when I am purchasing? Isn't my current quantity and the cap important information to know before/when buying something? Isn't a blocked UI element worthy of hover text or some kind of notice as to why it is now blocked? Terrible UX.

Track the Blind Well on the map and after 30s realize the game is taking me in the opposite direction. I turn around and find the location marked on the map: it's an empty cave tunnel... WTF is this game?!? Turns out, the activity was active, but the map doesn't care to show you the location of the current objective (this one jumps around the map) - it only shows that when you start tracking and go off in the "wrong" direction. Seriously, who makes these idiotic design decisions?

Everything in this game is under explained, overly convoluted, and generally unrewarding. And, they still make you leave the game to find people to play the game with. Absurd decision making and I regret that I have spent any money on this, in the past and recently. Such a disappointment.\t\t\t\t\t
