\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis game has the essential problem of being an MMO-like FPS that does NOT value its players time. If you can get over that, it can be a good time. If you can't, or you don't have a lot of time, avoid it.

You CAN play it primarily as a PVP shooter, and some people may find that satisfying. I do not. I am a story focused gamer, and the lore in this game has some exquisite writing. I still remember the Book of Sorrows from D1 and love it, and there are some comparable stories here. If, however, you ignore that and just look at the story as its presented in the game itself, its pretty bog-standard. It boils down to "kill those guys cause they are not us and they are evil cause I say so." You have no lasting antagonist except a vague "The Darkness" which desperately needs some in-game characterization. I don't think a single antagonist has lasted beyond the campaign its first introduced in, and the protagonist has only recently become more than a voiceless shell of a character. Maybe things change for the better, maybe they don't.

But those are personal preferences and I can forgive that, what I cannot forgive is the Bright Dust grind. You can pay for cosmetics, or grind through 8 bounties (objectives that are as uninteresting as "kill 25 things with submachine guns, or solar damage, or hedge clippers) to get 120 bright dust per game mode (Crucible PVP, Strikes PVE or Gambit an admittedly fun fusion of the two) per week for a grand total of 1080 per week.

For context: a skin for a gun or a single piece of armor is 1200 bright dust, and the things you can buy with bright dust rotate weekly. How do you make up the difference? Repeatable bounties that cost 3000 glimmer to buy, and again, are boring kill 25 things with a spatula or a sidearm or whatever. Each bounty, of which you can only carry 5 per game mode at a time, gives you 10 bright dust. You want a ghost shell? 2000. Some "expensive" emotes? 3000. If you want two things on a given week and don't have leftover bright dust? Have fun grinding 200-300 bounties! There isn't a thing in the Eververse store that costs less than 250.

But those are optional, so they don't matter right? Well its kind of the only thing to do once you run through the season's content. Raids are cool, but once you run it once, you've seen it all. You can grind triumphs, if you feel up to repeating the same content 50-1000 times. After that? Grinding for the best fashion is pretty much all that's left, if you aren't a PVP addict or for some reason feel like running through the same PVE mission for the hundredth time.

All that said, its a unique experience, there's a beauty to the aesthetic and it has a unique take on the post-apocalyptic setting, with more of a scifi-fantasy style of things. Gunplay is exceptional, even if some enemies are more bullet sponge than interesting encounter. I just find the business model distasteful, and wish it would go a little more interesting directions with the story. Season of the Splicer is shaping up to do that, but we'll have to wait and see.\t\t\t\t\t
