\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUnder the pretext and promise of "you have just got to get there" I have spent, at the time of writing, 129 hours googling what buffs do, how to get the next "powerful gear" and pretty much every other mechanic in this game.
The "satisfying gunplay" that reviewers keep chattering about when reviewing Destiny is overshadowed by the terrible muzzle effects, tracers and nonsensical design of the vast majority of guns. Within the gunplay are the age old RPG mechanics of damage, range and talents indicating their effect with a bar and vague descriptions. If you want to know what these stats actually are, you have to find datamined info on some 3rd party website.
Bungie must have looked at their game spanning levels and light power and thought it too complex to play through that they threw away your bonding with the character and instead put you in at light power 750 and no pretext - "go nuts".
Reality is you have no clue what anything does, where to start, what the NPC's even matter and 100 hours in I learned that, whatever the name of the strike bounty NPC is, he is supposedly the leader of all Titans. Because this game is not about story or fun mechanics - it's about saving a diverse arsenal of weapons so you can complete bounties and weekly challenges to get your XP and throw upgrade modules at your gear so you one day can find a group of six to do the raids.
The raids boil down to a cycle of DPS check > button hidden under something > 65iq puzzle with any variation of buttons or tasks equals 6 > another DPS check.
Any grindy game and activity are fun in good company with a healthy amount of bugs and random weirdness. It's simply a fidget cube or some other gadget with button combinations to pass the time while you chat.
In conclusion, the game tells you nothing about what is going on or what anything means, the difficulty comes from being either too low light power or too high light power and if you googled your way to progression or not. PvP and balance is an absolute mess, though a fun mess. And any kind of build diversity boils down to one of 3 archetypes of damage and if you got overshield or not.

An absolute waste of time I recommend anyone to skip. If I were to give this game a punchline it would be; "The content warframe should have had, but without the game."\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is overall is a fun, loot-filled, exciting RPG.

This game like the first has its issues. It's a major grind and the story is sub-par but it has may redeeming qualities. The gameplay is enjoyable and content packed, the graphics are quite an upgrade on PC from console (I run a solid 70-110 FPS on a GTX 1070), game mechanics and smooth and make the experience easy for controller or keyboard and mouse.

You don't get quite the awe and amazement of the first game, but the key aspects of Destiny are there, great PVE content filled with long quests and challenging missions, exciting PVP, weapons that are exciting and fun to use, the grind of leveling and waiting for that one exotic gun to drop after hours and hours. Raids are complicated and rewarding.

It is difficult to hop into for new or returning players because of the sheer amount of content with little to no explanation of how to play the game. That said once you get past the confusing and annoying learning curve it is quite enjoyable.

The community is great, you can play solo which is not bad but its even better when you can find people to play with, who know you might even make some new friends.

Destiny 2 isn't for everyone but it's a solid RPG shooter with lots of content, a must play in this genre and certainly if you played Destiny 1.\t\t\t\t\t
