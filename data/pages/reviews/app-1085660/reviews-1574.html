\r\n\t\t\t\t\tNo need to go into what Destiny is at this point, if you don't know and are considering the game then either you've been living under a rock or have intentionally ignored it. There are far better sources out there than myself to break the game down.

I'm glad they finally decided to offer it as Free to Play, though that just means I got screwed over for purchasing it at full price for the Xbox 1 at release. I quit playing it shortly thereafter when all of it's shortcomings were laid out for the world to see. I did not return to the game until after Shadowkeep had released. The game is finally in the state that it should have been in at release, but we'll not go into that. Disappointing to see that there is nothing offered to those that return to the game who paid full price to compensate for the bungled launch and then giving the game for free to everyone else. The least they could have done was give us $60 worth of Season subscriptions free. The fact that nothing at all is offered as an apology drastically decreases my rating of the game.

As far as gun and game play goes, it's Destiny, it cannot be denied that the mechanics are top notch and probably the best in the genre. Anyone who has played an FPS game in the last few years will immediately feel at home with the controls. The learning curve is very shallow and easily picked up by any gamer of just about any skill level.

The New Player experience is FAR improved over the original release model. I agree with the method used for allowing players to pick up main story arc's as mission/quest's from Amanda. Though it must be pointed out that the glaring mark against this is the character creation options. How is it that the Destiny franchise is this old and they are STILL using the SAME anemic character creation options that the ORIGINAL game shipped with?! Seriously?!?! That is just straight up disgraceful.

Weapons and Armor, I don't know, I guess I'm not going to complain too much on this category, I DO love the Bows. The upgrade options I find lacking and in need of a rework. They're fine if you're someone who's been with the game for years and have a ginormous stockpile of legendary shards, but FAR more prohibitive towards new and/or casual players.

The idea of the Seasons is alright - a bit MEH in my view, I'd much rather see them as supplementary to more densely packed, traditional linear story arcs. These just feel like the same three-month long mission objectives tacked onto a different narrative each time. It's good they are advancing the story, but I get tired of having to spend hours grinding away at public events just to satisfy an arbitrary completion % just to have to rinse and repeat the same thing on a different planet/location the next week, etc. etc. And everything is far too pricey for what you get. The emotes were played out in my estimation looooooong ago, yet they just keep cranking them out into the cash shop.

The game looks great at high-refresh rates. I run at a buttery smooth 165 fps and it looks great - though when using a controller I sometimes get these massive, extended drops in frame rate almost to the point of it being unplayable until I switch back to the mouse and keyboard, then the game instantly smooths out back to buttery smooth 165. I cannot find anything to explain it, there is absolutely nothing running in the background that would cause it - my CPU and GPU are never anywhere near even a quarter of what they are capable of, I have more than enough memory, and the game is running on Samsung Evo NVMe drive - there isn't a single hardware bottleneck anywhere in my system, it just doesn't like using Bluetooth connected Xbox controllers. Other than that, the only other graphical issue I've encountered has to do with HDR which seems to work OK for about 15 minutes when enabled and then reverts to a god-awful broken color mess until I disable HDR. I think this is more an issue with Microsoft's implementation of HDR in Windows than an issue with the game itself though, so I don't attribute this as a fault against the game.

All in all, a fun enough game to play every now and then when I get bored. A far better way to play the game than either PS or Xbox until the next gen systems release and we can finally enjoy high refresh rates on console, at which time I will probably switch back to playing on Xbox where all my in-game friends still play.

Would I recommend others to play it? Yes. The price is right now. Don't get suckered into paying for the seasons until you know you are going to dedicate the time to playing the game for the duration of the season. And IMPORTANT: Wait for a new season to start before you buy into one - the subscription WILL NOT roll over into the next, regardless of how close to the end of the season you purchased it! This is a serious bone of contention for me - if someone pays for a season then they should be allotted the full length of time for a season and it should roll over into the next! They do not clearly explain this anywhere and the first time I bought access it was 6 days to the end of that season. I got 6 days to play and then I had to pay again to start the next season.
