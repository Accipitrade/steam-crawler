\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 (like its predecessor) is a game that varies so wildly in quality from expansion-to-expansion and season-to-season that it's difficult to relay to newer players how their experience of the game will vary so wildly depending on when they decide to start playing. But as a hardcore player, my only regret with the game is enjoying it so much that I rarely ever play anything else. If a newer player can brave their way through a lackluster introduction to the world, characters, and core activities, they will find the game's peerless moment-to-moment balance between pristine gunplay, satisfying abilities, and bottomless loot grind. Destiny, for better and worse, is best in its endgame- its raids specifically housing not merely the franchise's best moments, but some of the best in gaming history. The central barrier between these experiences and newer players isn't a skill issue, or even a matter of having the best available gear- it's understanding how to get into these activities, how their mechanics operate- and most importantly- overcoming the anxiety of joining a team of strangers for the first time.

In short: the game is sometimes amazing, sometimes confusing, buggy, exploitative, "how-was-this-greenlit," and just generally insurmountable for the uninitiated. I recommend the game only for its best qualities- namely the raids and dungeons, which a majority of players will never experience due to the reasons listed above. Without the game's best content, its negative aspects outweigh the positive. Play the game to play the endgame, or you will probably feel like you're wasting your time.\t\t\t\t\t
