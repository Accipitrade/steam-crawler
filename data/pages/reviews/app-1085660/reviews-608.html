\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI am adamant of writing a review but i see a lot of "re re crycry" reviews lately and people just wanting for the game to die or drought or drop to "push Bungie to respond".

Anyway, an honest review is the following: "It is a good game if you are willing to commit to it."
Think of Destiny 2, at this point, as FFXIV. The game in a F2P context is very lacking, with a loose story, "meh" PVE content playlist and PVP is perhaps its saving grace with a non-competitive diverse, fast, smooth and exciting playlist where gunplay and skill is showcased more and more, with easy to pick-up mechanics that are hard to master.

I'll give the point that RAIDs and F2P dungeons offer mechanics and dynamism to PVE that makes it exciting and fun, keeping you on your toes and offering a challenge- besides the challenge of dealing with a group of "strangers". Nothing anyone who plays MMOs (particularly RPGs) isn't used to.

However, the game shines on the B2P (Buy to play) aspect as the DLCs and season pass are essentially the life and core now. Why? "Because of bad game design", yeah yeah you can hate the game all you want for not including new players into the destiny 2 world with ease (or at all if you dont care about lore). However, the reasons are:
1. Destiny 2, sequel to Destiny 1, has so much story and world building at this point that there is an AGENDA of a story to be told.
2. each season, each DLC brings not only a fresh input of new content, but of story, of world changes, of weapon meta changes and activities.

That said, once you commit to this seasonpass/DLC hybrid structure, Bungie does offer 1 main thing (beyond lore). High-end / End-Game content. You see, the more you play the game, the more of a challenge you seek. This means mastering the game and only by accessing the highest difficulty of PVE, or the competitive of PVP can you do this. Honestly, it makes a world of a difference. It opens the world and the game as you learn more, you crunch numbers, you analyze, the game goes beyond mind-numbing shoot-kill to actually strategizing and understanding your actions and fine-tuning. IT IS NOT A CASUAL PLAYER GAME AT THIS POINT.

If you are, you wont enjoy or actually do most paid content. You wont fight for "world's first" or "day-1" runs for Raids/Dungeons. You wont seek being a conqueror, dredgen or flawless guilded player (emblem/title). You wont follow the week-by-week stories, nor read the lore, nor seek secrets. So, don't spend on it.

The game isn't perfect, we constantly have bugs. Connectivity, fps and latency issues now that XBOX, PS and STEAM have merged into true crossplay. Still errant cheaters occasionally. "Content droughts" as bungie has a schedule of 90-100 days content (wont speak of the current "drought" as i am a researcher and COVID impacted many things). The "meta" is forced upon us instead of us "choosing" or "finding" the meta at times. PVP can be frustrating and demanding. PVE can be extremely frustrating by elitism, players un-willing to communicate and/or bad-mannered people. Weapons are mostly reskins and there is an actual GRIND for god-rolls, it is horribly time-consuming to hunt fo rthe armor/weapon you need/want. And, LOOT awarded can SOMETIMES feel NOT-WORTH IT because of RNGesus (yes, loot in D2 is RNG-based and it can be cruel at times).

Be a lone player, a new player or an old player, D2 has a space if you understand that this is an "old" Looter-Shooter MMO RPG game with a WoW-type structure of DLCs + Seasonal passes that all must be bought (each new DLC offers a promotion of 1 time buy all seasons+dlc). It requires patience and time, but the community- however loud, bitter and negative it can seem- is only the 10% and you WILL find clans and nice people who WILL help you.

Eyes up guardian, you are one of us now.\t\t\t\t\t
