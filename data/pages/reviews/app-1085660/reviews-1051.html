\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is (obviously) a sequel to Destiny from 2014. Compared to it's predecessor, there are lots of quality of life changes and updates to the gameplay. For example, each class (Hunter, Warlock, Titan) has a class ability (Dodge, Rift, Barrier respectively). The gunplay is buttery smooth and one of the best feeling that I've played with so far. The story in-game is honestly alright. The game's lore is where everything really shines. Destiny is extremely rich in lore, and goes in-depth about many topics, such as the golden age of humanity, the Traveler, specific characters, etc.

Year 4:
Destiny 2's status after Beyond Light's release is interesting; The majority of the game's content (Vanilla/Red War to Warmind) has been "removed" from the live game and placed into the Destiny Content Vault (DCV), where it would stay until further notice. This specifically includes the destinations (Titan, Io, Mercury, Mars) and their respective content. This is mainly to make things more streamlined, as according to Bungie, it's becoming more problematic to keep everything updated and stable each patch.

Sunsetting is a major point in Year 4 of Destiny 2. Most of the weapons and armor from previous expansions have a hard cap on them, meaning after a certain point, they cannot be upgraded to the same level as weapons from this expansion and season. As of Beyond Light, the base light level is 1050. Most gear (excluding exotics and certain weapon pools) from previous expansions have a light level cap of 1060. For comparison, the current hard cap is 1260. It's been very upsetting for many players, including myself, as a lot of us spent time to grind that gear, only to learn that it's pretty much useless now. Sure you can still use your old gear, but the low light level will be a detriment to activities that have a recommended light level that is higher.

This is where my issue with the game's current status. You still have to pay for Forsaken and Shadowkeep to get the rest of their content, but at the same time, most of the gear from those DLCs are pretty much useless save for the exotics. Even some activities like Shadowkeep's Nightmare Hunts are worthless, as the rewards only reach light level 1060. Unfortunately, Bungie's execution of sunsetting was terrible. In the first few days of Beyond Light, some bounties required you to use a Heavy Machine Gun to complete. Issue is, the current weapon pool has none of that. New players couldn't complete those. This lead to Bungie bringing back weapon pools from a previous season (Season of The Worthy).

My overall opinion: I mean, you can give it a shot, it's free to play. Unless you're buying the DLC right off the bat, you're not really losing anything. However, it can become a massive grindfest and start to feel like a chore after a certain point. It has a lot of ups and downs. I don't hate the game; otherwise I wouldn't have this much time invested into it. However, after Beyond Light's release, I cannot recommend the game to people.\t\t\t\t\t
