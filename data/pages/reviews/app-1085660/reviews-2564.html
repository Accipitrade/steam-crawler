\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a nice first person shooter MMO with good production values. Sci-fi, futuristic setting. RPG elements. Fashion. Drop-in multiplayer. Raiding. PVP. Daily/weekly tasks. Seasonal optional content for a 10 USD pass. There's a lot to do in this game.

pros:
- good amount of content for free
- weapon/armor design is cool and consistent
- weapon perk variations add variety, having different perks on the same weapon can change your experience drastically
- exotic weapons (super rare) items add a lot of unique gameplay options
- for the most part, daily/weekly tasks feel relatively doable
- combat/gunplay is polished and feels satisfying
- variety of playstyles due to different weapon/armor/class ability combos
- story/npc quality is consistent and above average
- f2p and relatively transparent gameplay loop; you will have an easy time telling if this game is for you, and it's free to try
- the lore is deceptively deep and is interesting to read about; it's almost as deep as Lord of the Rings.

cons:
- the game does an incomplete job of guiding new players; it's very easy to get lost and confused. find some guides.
- seasonal content contains some items that are inaccessible to free players. even if you buy all the expansions there will be some items you simply cannot get.
- certain small features seem to be missing and forgotten, such as the ability to rotate your character/items in certain viewscreens, or the ability to reorganize your inventory/storage without the use of a separate application.
- some of the quest tasks are mind-numbingly dull and require many repetitions despite having a 24-hour time limit.
- despite the production value, the story feels quite shallow, and it's difficult to get invested in any of the characters

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSigh. Where to begin. Destiny 2 is an online MMO FPS RPG Looter Shooter that has too many buzzwords, and fails at being enjoyable. Let me get some facts straight about me as a D2 player. I'll leave a TL;DR for those who want it below.
1. I am a solo/2 person player. On a good day, I can play dungeons.
2. I have never played a single raid. As much as I want to, I am introverted as hell and can't bring myself to talk in a vc with complete strangers.
3. I have been playing destiny since the D1 beta. I've played the story of every campaign of every destiny game, from dark below to lightfall.
4. I am a purely PvE player. I have played crucible and gambit, but none of them interest me.

So. Let me go into pros and cons about this game. No dlc or seasonal content shall be mentioned.
Pros:
+ The weapon system is incredibly fun. Most of the guns are really enjoyable to aim and shoot.
+ Some of the subclasses are really enjoyable to use. Arc titan, void warlock, and solar hunter are my favorite subclasses of each class.
+ The music is genuinely so good. I will sit in areas and idle just so I can listen to it.
And that's about it for me. The things that I enjoy about this game are DLC content, which is...sad...when you think about it. Anyways, cons.
- The planet selection is both limited and terrible. Nessus is a PRIME example. It looks terrible and has no reason to go there.
- Some really interesting exotics require pvp quests. I do not think that in a "looter shooter" pvp should be a necessity for an exotic.
- The transmog system is just terrible. Never before have I been required to do a whole quest just to get ONE piece of armor as a vanity piece.
- The base campaign is just terrible. It's incredibly tiny and barely gets people into the "flow" of destiny. No mention of raids, dungeons, or even the essentials of build crafting.
- The power system is just...off. As a looter shooter, it never gives you the impression that you're powerful. The dreg you fought in the cosmodrome allll the way when you were just starting out will take the same amount of damage and dish out the same amount, as it did then, now.
- The exotics feel so, so, SO much weaker than they make it out to be. There is usually 2 or 3 exotics per class that are really good, with everything else being used only for vanity.
- Speaking of exotics, the limit they put on them is absurd. With how pitiful they are in reality, there is no reason you should be able to equip only one exotic armor and weapon. ESPECIALLY in PvE encounters. I can understand it for pvp, but not for pve.
- The nerfs. Almost every month on average something gets nerfed. And it's almost always for PvP reasons. Exotic weapons and armor SUFFER in PvE for this one reason alone.

All in all, this game has had its highlights. But each and everyone of them is related to a DLC. Witch Queen. Lightfall's strand subclass. Stasis in beyond light. All cool things. But if coming into this game as a F2P, you're gonna have the worst time possible by being extremely limited. You shouldn't have to buy DLC to enjoy a subpar experience.

TL;DR:
Destiny 2 has many flaws, such as nerfing exotics to the ground, limiting free players, and explaining next to nothing. I've given this time more than enough tries to live up to the expectations it's setting up for itself, and it's failed almost every single time.

Final verdict
2/10. Don't give this game the light of day.\t\t\t\t\t
