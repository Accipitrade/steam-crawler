\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI admit, I was not a huge fan of the original Destiny game on PS4. I played it mostly because my wife enjoyed it, and calling it a mildly entertaining co-op experience is the best praise I could muster. Overall I felt there wasn't a lot of content to explore, and I wasn't into PvP at the time killed the Crucible's appeal. We ran through the main story missions and put it down shortly after that.

Last year I tried Destiny 2 during a free weekend while it was still on Battle.net. I found that the UI was still pretty hilarious, but it seemed like many of my old gripes had been addressed. Still, it failed to capture my attention and I didn't purchase the game at the end of the free period.

Then we were locked down, and I noticed that D2 was now F2P. "Why not," I thought, "My wife will probably get a kick out of it, and I didn't hate it as much as the first one."

All I can say is: WOW. I love, love LOVE how much they've improved this game over the original. The combat is fun, the game looks gorgeous, the NPCs are fun and memorable. The environments make me actually want to explore, and I love how varied they are.

That's the good, now for the bad: both the tutorialization and the discoverability of the UI are comedy levels of terrible, but if you're not afraid to use a search engine it's not that big of a deal. My other gripe is the mandatory matchmaking for Strikes. My wife and I are pretty low-level, and it's been extremely difficult finishing Bounties when we get matched with folks who can basically solo the Strike while we struggle to catch up. I understand why Bungie has made matchmaking mandatory (in short: it's more detrimental to people who need help than it is for people who don't) but it's pretty frustrating.

Overall, I think the game is very fun and definitely worth a try. It's hard to beat at the low low price of free-ninety-nine!\t\t\t\t\t
