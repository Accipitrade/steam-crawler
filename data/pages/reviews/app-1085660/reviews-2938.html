At a combined total for 3K hours across multiple platforms (Destiny on PS4, Destiny 2 on PS4/PS5, & PC) - Destiny as a franchise is my second most played game ever.

Seven years on & off - it's definitely a love/hate thing.

At it's Best...
Destiny is an exceptional multiplayer/co-op FPS experience with peak & pinnacle activities like Raids & Dungeons - filled with exploration, challenging platforming, intricate jump-puzzles, combat mechanics, unique Boss encounters & mechanics - they can be difficult, brutal, punishing, but so rewarding & so damn fun (King's Fall Raid (2015), Whisper of the Worm Dungeon (2018), & Zero Hour Dungeon (2018) - are the most memorable co-op experiences I've ever had in a video-game). All wrapped in a triple A production with combat & gunplay that is polished, tight, & responsive. There's a variety of enemies & enemy types - with impressive AI making the combat more engaging. The visuals & the environments are stunning, the sound-design & the level-design are incredible - and the score is absolutely phenomenal Â· â™ª Â· â™« While the original vanilla Destiny launched with a vague, mysterious, & esoteric narrative of light and dark - it has since expanded far more with rich & deep lore (D1 Exotic quotes, Exotic Hand-Cannon 'The Last Word' lore, The Book of Sorrows with the origins of the Hive and Aurash, Xi Ro, & Sathona...*chef's kiss*). Oh, and Bungie has done a phenomenal job with cross-progression & now cross-play - giving players the option to play where they want (PC, PlayStation or Xbox) with whoever they want (PC, PlayStation, or Xbox). I wish more multiplayer games would implement those features!

At it's Worst...
Destiny can be an extremely monotonous, repetitive, & soul-sucking time-consuming grind. Some of the best activities are locked behind an artificial power grind to reach a higher number for your level. You're not rewarded for your skill, but for the amount of time you're willing to sink. Admittedly, with recent seasons the power grind has been diminishing - but still at it's worst it feels archaic & unncessary. I can't think of anything less enjoyable or engaging then launching into the Tower grabbing X/Y/Z bounty from X/Y/Z vendor to kill X/Y/Z enemy with X/Y/Z weapon on X/Y/Z planet - for your daily & weekly bounties - just to slightly level up your character per day/week. As a live-service game Destiny has also suffered from some severe content droughts, although I've been playing since 2014 - I've gone long periods of time - months if not years in & out of Destiny. Nowadays, I only comeback for the big, major expansions to hop in & play for a couple of months before checking back out. Also, while the Destiny Content Vault has been causing a major stir for long-time players - I find myself a bit more mixed on that. While on the surface level, it sucks to have any content you paid for removed from the game - my main question - was the content being removed actually being utilized to it's full potential? And was the content actually being played by a large enough players? Destiny 2 had become overly large & overly bloated with so many destinations - that were not being utilized to their max potential. So if vaulting some of that content paves the way for newer & more interesting content I'm all for it. But this segues to ONBOARDING - it's something that I feel like Destiny has always struggled with, especially now going into it's 7th year. Now with the Destiny Content Vault removing the original Destiny 2 main campaign - new players coming are dropped into a far more confusing, & esoteric start than ever before. A friend of mine recenty tried to start Destiny but was so confused at the story & what was going on - there simply isn't a real great way to hop in & make sense of the story without doing some additional research. For the most egregious part of Destiny is the microtransactions. Despite putting a lot of the blame & onus on Activision for the overly aggressive monetization & microtransactions in the game - since their split Bungie themselves have done some pretty bad practices. Most recent comes to mind was last summer with the addition of Transmog, a feature players have wanted since vanilla D1. You could either grind an additional 40 hours a week or pay X amount in the Store - that was insane. Destiny 2 has a lot to offer in their Store with exotic weapon skins, armor sets, emotes, ships, shaders, etc. - if they utilized their in-game currency more to make the content more readily earnable - that'd be ideal. Lastly, disappointing to hear about no support for Destiny on the Steam Deck - was looking forward to that! ðŸ˜¡

Overall
While the Destiny experience has been a bit of a mixed bag for me - it's undeniable the core co-op PvE it's offered me has been the most memorable, fun, & rewarding co-op experiences I've ever had in a game. And for all it's major struggles year in & year out, Bungie always manages to deliver amazing content especially in the form of it's major expansions - The Taken King (2015), Rise of Iron (2016), Forsaken (2018), Shadowkeep (2019), Beyond Light (2020). I remember hearing about all the Destiny-killers out there, games such as Tom Clancy's The Division 1 & 2, Anthem, Marvel's Avengers, Outriders, etc. (all dead games). Destiny 2 consistently sits inside Steam's Top 10 Daily Most-Played Games, or just outside of it. It's wild to think about all of Bungie's comebacks with Destiny 1 when it struggled, with Destiny 2 vanilla when it struggled hard, and now to be nearing their 8th year - so close to finishing their 10 year vision - that's beyond impressive. While I don't know how hooked or engaged I'll be in between the next set of major expansions, I'm far too close to the finish line to ever give up on Destiny entirely - and too invested in the story. With Lightfall (2023) around the corner, and the big finale The Final Shape (2024) - I definitely want to see how the saga ends.
