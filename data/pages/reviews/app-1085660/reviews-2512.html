\r\n\t\t\t\t\t6 year veteran of Destiny. A great game that is expanding all the time, has a loot and mod system that encourages experimentation and discovery, and provides the most interesting environments to explore and aliens to kill. Destiny is best played with friends. It was designed from the ground up to allow people with the same or different objectives to play together and everyone get rewarded - no fighting over loot drops here! Plus they have attuned the progression and rewards in the y3 seasonal models to be even better the more people you have on your fireteam.

The PC version is arguably the best-looking, best performing version of Destiny, if your computer can keep up with it. I have a decent system and had to frame-lock it at 30 FPS to get consistent performance, but it feels just like the PC with an Xbox One controller attached.

The Cross-Save functionality (share progress on PS4, Xbox One, and PC) is the make-or-break feature for this game. If I had to start all over and manage two accounts (6 characters!) and re-earn all the weapons and gear, it would be a non-starter on PC, no matter how much "better" it is. But the fact that I can bring my exact, current progress over from Xbox where my clan is, make some progress on PC, and then load back up on Xbox without losing anything, is invaluable for time-limited events while on vacation and managing inventory in-bed while the wife watches a movie.

Note that this is cross-save, not cross-play, so I still have to jump onto Xbox to play with clan members. (I can still chat with them cross-platform through the xbox pc or mobile app).

Destiny can be as deep or shallow as you want to play, but if you get sucked in, it might become your "main" or even "only" game!\t\t\t\t\t
