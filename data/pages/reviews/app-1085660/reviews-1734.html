Yes for the PvE- The PvE while yes a repetitive, Destiny is a looter-shooter, so repetitiveness is bound to happen. The raids are fun, especially with friends. This game in general, is better with friends. But then we go to PvP

No- The PvP in this game is more unbalanced than Dark Souls 1. I don't know what goes through Bungie's head, but they keep bringing back OP weapons (example of this is Felwinter's Lie). Now making good weapons that you earn from completing hard achievements is good. BUT NOT EVERY SEASON. Example of this would be the Recluse smg, which was easy to get. As a result of that everyone used the gun, and it made the game heavily unbalanced. My opinions on shotguns are 50/50. Most shotguns are fine as they need a close range to be effective. But then we guns like Felwinter's Lie, Mindbenders, and Astral Horizion which are snipesr disguised as a shotgun exist that when the game starts to get annoying. Seriously, a shotgun shouldn't one-shot me from 10 meters away. If it was 5 meters then yes it would be fine. Bungie claims they care about the meta in PvP, but if that was true shotguns would've been nerfed as they are always in the current meta. Snipers rifles, and scout rifles are underused, as the flinch by hand cannons, AR's and pulse rifles is horrible. Sniping wouldn't be as much as an issue if it weren't for the maps. The majority of maps in D2 contains long narrow hallways which are good for shotguns as there are no place to take cover from shotguns. If there more maps with a plenty of open space for sniping, sniping would be more enjoyable. Speaking about the size of the maps, they aren't meant for 6v6 as they are too small for them. The spawns in Rumble is a joke, as you most likely always spawn next to an enemy which usually uses Felwinter's Lie. I always get respawned near the enemy that killed me. The only thing you have when respawned is an overshield which is useless when a person spawn-camps you with a sniper rifle and kills you with a headshot, or a shotgunner who was near you. Bungie can't also properly balance guns as they either make a weapon archetypes too powerful or too useless, and nowhere close in between. Trials of Osiris is a joke with hackers on a rampage. PvP in this game is pure garbage and would not recommend, but the PvE is nice.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFirst the Pros

This game is fun and always something to do for everyone. Gun play is very well done and balanced. Abilities are fun to use for the most part. Enemies are challenging for the most part and overly powerful in some instances which I will discuss in the con section.

Now for the Con's

1) Why is it that almost every exotic or quest is forcing you into a pvp to finish it. If I remember correctly they stated that we did not have to pvp if we didn't want to, but we are somehow forced to do it if we want to complete quests or exotics. Yes there are options for some, very few, to do the newer pve challenges, however they ether do not drop nearly enough or way to challenging for those who are not in the upper 1000 on there light.

2) Enemies can be over challenging for those of lower light levels. At the writing of this I am above light level 1000; however for those of lower light levels we should not be running into high level enemies in the field trying to get to our objectives. Why? Well if it was done properly and if I remember what it was before the enemies were adjusted to the level of the person engaging the enemies. So a light level 750 and light level 1080 could engage the same enemy and it's resistance would adjust based on who was hitting it.

3) The Gambit needs to be adjusted so that just like the Crucible people can engage on equal grounds. Yes there are parts of the Crucible where PVP level advantage is enable, but that should be on the Prime not on the normal. If you are going to force people in PVP style combat as described in comment 1 you need make adjustments for those who do not want PVP or is not very good at it or you will frustrate the player.

So overall I will recommend the game, but I think there needs to be some adjustments to make the game more balanced and fun for everyone.
