\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI see a lot of people complaining about the state of the game. Overall, I've dumped 1100+ on PC and 900+ on PS4 so I have a decent idea of how the game runs, and is supposed to run. Here's my 2 cents.

To start off you're thrown right into the mixture of what Bungie calls "New Light Experience" which basically consists of sending new players to go off into the various worlds to complete easy quests to earn an exotic quest (for an exotic weapon) at the end. Immediately after, you're shown the world of experienced players through ; Aimbots in Crucible and KWTD Raid teams you've seen hang around the social area known as "The Tower" and the power leveling system in the open worlds requiring you to complete monotonous tasks to earn a meagre bump in level so small you might dismantle your new item by accident. All on board?

Seriously though, this game HAS been a great example of a looter shooter with guns randomly rolling perks to find what you like the best and what works, armour with 6 different stats to work for each playstyle (yes, I can hear you saying "Recov is the only stat that matters" from the back) and all the fashion and personalization you can ask for. Your personal assistant can wear a sun hat this season for example.

A game isn't without it's flaws. Recently we've had 1. A lame season with nothing to grind for but an exotic shotgun that's immediately thrown into storage, 2. a public event they purposefully made harder so people couldn't get an endgame reward for prowess, 3. the return of Trials of Osiris which they said they didn't, and I quote, "want it to come out half baked", which it exactly did with zero update to anti-cheat, network errors running amok and no recourse for new players entering the format to protect them from the tried and true vets smothering them for loot and 4. currently an event called "Sunsetting" used to remove older gear that the majority of the playerbase had to work hard to earn, loves and is upset to have forcefully taken from them for endgame content.

This game is a grind, plain and simple. And the Devs aren't perfect. If you want a game to find friends, hang out with long term doing a single task for hours and develop a position in a community or your personal clan, this is the game for you. I especially recommend this to anyone who wants to develop good team working skills with smaller groups, such as trials, dungeons or even raids or anyone who has found a lot of free time on their hands recently.\t\t\t\t\t
