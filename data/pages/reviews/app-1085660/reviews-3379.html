\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGameplay is fun, but has several exotic quests that pretty much require you to run with pre-made groups. Can't get a group? Oh well sucks be you. Guess you don't get to enjoy the content because Bungie made several missions that will not matchmake. Some exotic quests do matchmake, which makes the exclusion of some exotic quests even more baffling. It sure isn't fun to sit in a player hub for an indefinite amount of time in local chat and pray that 1-2 other people want to help you or also need to run the mission.

I'm not a fan of PvP, especially since any weapon/class changes made for PvP sake also affect PvE (also PvP is unbalanced as all get-out). Although I find it annoying that a large amount of quests for gear make you play PvP, at least you get matchmade into a game and can always find a match quickly. For exotic quests that do not matchmake you have to either go in with a pre-made fireteam, or be insanely good. I'll admit I'm a casual player and can't do some of the crazy solo stuff I see other people do on Youtube. That's why it would be nice to have missions for weapons like Wish-Ender or Outbreak Perfected matchmake.

It's not so much the difficulty as it is these missions being designed for 3-player teams and they not matchmake.

I enjoy that they made Nightfalls matchmaking, simply opting for the higher tiers of difficulty making you go in with pre-made groups. I wish they would do something similar with all exotic missions/strikes. You typically have to run them again on Heroic difficulty anyways if you want the catalyst, so why can't the normal mission for the weapon itself be matchmaking and the Heroic catalyst mission be pre-made?

The game has also been heavily redesigned for returning/existing players. New players just get dropped into the game and get thrown a gun and told to just go do stuff. A personal gripe, but this game has been out for a couple years now.

I will say that the game runs just fine. Usually my laptop hates playing most games and always gets rather hot, but I have yet to run into that issue with Destiny 2.

In the long-run, there's still a lot to do even if you don't own the Forsaken and Shadowkeep expansions, but most of the player base will be playing that content, meaning if you wanna play with everyone else you'll probably end up having to buy those expansions.

If Bungie does make a Destiny 3, I'll be interested to see how they handle the game now that they've separated from Activision, but for now I think I'm gonna stay away from the game. Not much fun when your content is locked behind hours and hours of grind and lack of matchmaking. If you got some friends to run content with, then go for it.

(Although it says I've only played about 30+ hours on Steam, I have played many more on PS4 before they enabled cross-save.)\t\t\t\t\t
