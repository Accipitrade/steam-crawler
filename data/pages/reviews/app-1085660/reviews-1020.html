\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: minimum viable product to get our money

This game is good, and that's the worst part. I have more than 1k hours in it. It is really fun, but filled with most predatory monetization, and most toxic decisions you've ever seen.

They REPLACE content you own, with content you don't own. The content they produce since they introduced seasons, is some of the most boring AND expensive content i've ever seen in gaming.

They emptied this game from fun diverse content it had in the beginning, to replace it with boring, repetitive seasons.

This game is really fun, but it is run by worst scum in the industry. And has some of the most toxic apologist communities too.

Bungie now, rather than the Bungie that was lorded over by Activision, is absolutely one of the vilest, most predatory developers in the industry - comparable to worst case Ubisoft or EA modern myths. Everything about the game now is built to grind you down and screw you over, with the only saving grace being the controller-specific gunplay experience, some of the VA work, (Morla, Lance, etc,) and the lore.

After doing reserch for a while and before writing this review, the issues actually started back in Destiny 1 since I've spoken to a load of veteran players, read posts and checked archive pages.

Eververse was only supposed to be a live-team funding system. When it started eating into the core reward system, the loudest voices claimed it was Activision pushing for more monetization. Many of us shrugged, because it was just cosmetics, and it was going to stay that way - right?

But then came Destiny 2 and the temporary seasonal content system, and its endless repetition for a couple of voice lines a week of fresh lore, followed finally by the DCV - but post split with Activision, note.

The vaulting of paid-for digital content set an abysmal industry precedent, and we're extremely lucky that few developers and publishers have followed suit, in their wake. It was sold to the playbase on false pretences, and remains completely unnecessary to this day - with the only players shilling for it being those with the worst cases of Stockholm syndrome, who simply can't see license theft for what it is.

If you absolutely must have a Destiny 2-esque shooter-looter experience in your life, and happen to own a console, just go buy Destiny 1. It's fair superior to this current mess in every single way, and lacks only a few QoL changes and the higher fov/framerate. Both have negligible impact after a few hours of playing it.\t\t\t\t\t
