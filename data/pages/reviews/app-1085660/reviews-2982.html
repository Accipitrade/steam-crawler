I have played Destiny. I have played a lot of Destiny. Destiny, Destiny 2, doesnt matter. Too many hours. But despite the amount of time I have put into it, at the launch of Destiny 2 I played a lot. Until I didn't. This game on launch was god awful, Everyone remembers. Not much build diversity. Not much meat and bones for the game if you were not a crucible hound. There was the main story and that is it. No depth. I guarantee everyone that tried it has one or two complaints. And some stayed in spite of it. But many of us left.

Forsaken came along and everything changed. The Honor Bound mission to avenge our dear friend and colleague Cayde-6 burnt a desire into my heart that could not be treated without the blood of Cayde's killers flowing like a wave of water in an otherwise dry place. With Forsaken came the return of Destiny. Gone were the days of waiting in queue for 10 minutes to find a match. The community had a rush of new players. All around, Forsaken was a big win for Destiny. With Forsaken came the annual pass. And for all the people saying, "But why do I have to spend 25-40 bucks to get the annual pass?". You must not have ever done much programming or 3D modeling. These people work painstakingly for a game that they are very much a part of the community for. You don't have to look far to find an act of awesomeness by Bungie. Just cut your Vbuck budget and fork out some dough. Its 25-40 bucks for hours and hours and hours of content. You would spend the same money at a meal that could cost way less or a movie that is overpriced and you pay anyway.

ANYWAY... where it stands right now, Destiny 2 is only just getting started with the overhaul. Things are evolving organically and the community is at it's peak of participation. The grindiness of it has been severely reduced (humph...lucky nerds) so you can be more capable of progressing and becoming a true Dad Gamer.

Rating for Gameplay: 8.5

Rating for Soundtrack: 5

Rating for Community: 10

Broken Weapons: Plead the Fifth.
