\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is an interesting game. I've never played another game like it. An MMO RPG FPS, you won't find much like it on the market. I've put in 1000 hours on steam, 500+ hours on Battle.net, and have done almost 200 raids. I can confidently say that Destiny 2 is worth your time. With the constant updates to content, to the overwhelmingly grand raids and dungeons, this game is fantastic. There is a huge amount of lore and story, alongside interesting characters and sometimes catastrophic events that help define the 'ever evolving world' of Destiny 2. If you like MMOs and FPS', you should give D2 a shot; it might end up your favorite game.

2k hours edit: still great, but drip feed hurts

3k hours edit: even better. Bungie, I assume at the hand of Joe Blackburn, has almost made a 180 from their year 3 ways. The game is headed in a good way, much better than before. If this trend continues, year 5 could be the best Destiny has ever been.

4k hours edit: still pretty good, time and repetition are starting to show their downsides in spite of the continued improvements to the game. The new seasonal model continues to be refined but doesn't offer the same pull and allure it once did. Hopefully the game picks up story wise in the second half of WQ. Lightfall seems promising but new activities continue to be underdeveloped and too small in scope; boring if you will. I hope that Bungie changes things up and starts to add more content each season instead of rehashing old content and moves to make activities more challenging and rewarding instead of accessible to all but useful to none.
