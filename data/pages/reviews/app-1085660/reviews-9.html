\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny is a series that I have played since day 1 of the first game. My family and I played it together on the same console, with our own characters, and activities we would pass the controller around to take turns in. It's outlasted Call of Duty, Battlefield, Overwatch (lol), all these different shooters; Destiny maintained it's longevity with our evening's together and free time. With it's compelling story, rich world, fun characters, and top-notch first-person combat, we were hooked through every expansion the game ever released, even when times in game were tough. These reasons and nostalgia alone were enough to be hype about Destiny 2, and when I got my own PC for the first time I started playing with my partner and friends to much delight. The game was amazing, and on this fresh account alone, and 1,300 hours later, I can truly say the game has died, a shallow, tedious, money-hungry shadow of itself. It was a slow, painful death, sputtering to a halt at a time when, narrative wise, I should probably be more invested than ever. No matter who they bring back, no matter what different content they choose to recycle, the soul is no longer present in this world. In so many ways, it feels like the Darkness has already won, and the only losers here, are the fans. Destiny 2 is a game I will mourn for. From obsession, elation, and some of the greatest gaming years of my life, to watching it choke on it's own blood as the life fades from it's eyes before mine. As it currently stands, I cannot recommend this game to you. I hope one day that can be different. Eyes up, Guardians.\t\t\t\t\t
