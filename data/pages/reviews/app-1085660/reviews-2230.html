\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I would like to keep this short, I do not believe that it is possible to do so.

For those thinking about picking up this game / trying it out: don't. It is both a waste of money and time that perpetually guilt trips you into playing due to FOMO (fear of missing out).

Here's why:
- On release this game had a great campaign (for the most part). Effort was clearly put in. This campaign is now gone. In its stead there is now a totally confusing mini campaign that explains very little, shoves prompts in your face, and ends abruptly.
- Huge portions of the story have been removed, and so very little makes sense (for example Cayde's death).
- Can you imagine pouring hundreds of dollars into DLCs, not all of which are remotely worth the money, only to have them (and their associated content) sunset (made virtually unusable)? Money wasted. However, funnily enough, Bungie think it's appropriate to re-release the exact same content they took away from you... for money, again!
- The game's sandbox is less stable than I am, with constant nonsensical fluctuations in what is "balanced" vs what is "fun". It took the dev team an entire season to nerf several absurdly overpowered items (Loreley Splendor, Renewal Grasps).
- PvE content is filled with false difficulty, bullet sponges, and immunity phases, although it is far more polished than this game's PvP scene.
- With regards to PvP, there is an absurd quantity of potentially that is quite frankly completely and utterly squandered. A movement shooter, with fun abilities, unique gunplay, endlessly possibilities in terms of builds and playstyles, and they nerf the ever loving crap out of mobility options.

It genuinely feels as though Bungie are trying to cater to new players, get them hooked enough to buy the DLCs, and then leave.

For those thinking about returning to this game: don't. It is likely that your previously loved gear is sunset and at the very least you will have to pay for and earn it again.

For those coming to this game from Destiny 1: save yourself.

It breaks my heart seeing the state of this game. I've made many wonderful friends and had bounties of incredible moments playing it, however this abused corpse of a game needs effort. There is clearly lots of talent at Bungie, with content like The Witch Queen being great- however for every superb addition to the game there comes a fistful of backsteps.

I myself have quit this game 4 times and each time I come back I find myself regretting it.

One last thing: this current season (Season of the Haunted)... is a disaster. Bungie announced that they would be overhauling PvP aerial gunplay. How did they achieve this? By making weapons feel clunky, inconsistent, and untrustworthy while in the air. For those that do not play crucible (PvP) constantly like myself, it may not feel too different. However, for someone who has built up a level of confidence and comfort in how things play, these unneeded changes have impacted my enjoyment tremendously. Weapons that were once considered "old faithful" are now unfun and difficult to use (in a very bad way). The seasonal activity (basically just escalation protocol, if you know what that is) is bland. There are countless bugs and exploits allowing players to reach max level and max legendary shards + glimmer. It is not difficult for a player to start up the game and find something wrong. They took weapons from the old Menagerie activity and reinserted them into the game. Hooray, they were awesome so I'm glad they're back! Oh, wait, an insane grind lies ahead of you if you want them ... and also they didn't break back the actual menagerie activity, so you're stuck running around opening chests for tens of hours. It is also worth noting that the "new" IronBanner changes which have been hyped for months are incredibly underwhelming.

I feel vicious typing this, but something has to be said. Sure, there are many great aspects to this game, but they are simply not worth it.\t\t\t\t\t
