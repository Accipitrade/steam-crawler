The state of the game is the worst it's ever been, especially for dedicated Crucible players. They've recently announced some pretty serious upcoming overhauls to address this issue, and astoundingly, seem to have found a way to make it even worse.

The pvp team clearly doesn't understand the wants of the dedicated Crucible player. They're comically far off the mark. From introducing a ranked ladder (that also has SBMM for some reason?) with no tangible rewards for your efforts, to removing virtually all mid to close range maps in favor of very long range maps that everyone hates, to allowing ridiculously degenerate playstyles like perma-stealthed sidearm Hunters to go unaddressed, to punishing players for aerial movement (the true skill cap in competitive pvp), to insisting on the continuation of a poorly thought out leaver penalty for casual modes, they've proven an ongoing, fundamental misunderstanding of what players want here, and they're leaving in droves because of it.

But hey, maybe you don't care about Crucible and only want to pve. You can look forward to the same rehashed seasonal content and paltry updates that have plagued the game for years.

There's a great game at the underlying all this garbage. It's an incredibly well polished shooter with a fun core gameplay loop. Bungie needs to start paying closer attention player feedback and making a concerted effort to understand and make design choices based on that feedback. You can make the argument that it's their game and they can do whatever they want with it, but the proof is in the pudding. Player count is lower than ever. If you want them to come back, you need to start listening and give them what they want.\t\t\t\t\t
