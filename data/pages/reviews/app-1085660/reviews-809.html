\r\n\t\t\t\t\tIs the game good for a new player?
No, new "light" players experience is known to be horrendously bad. You will either need to deep-dive into YouTube guides explaining base setups / information, or have a veteran player guide you through all of it. Either google every term that confuses you, or ask a player if they can explain it to you.

Is the game good as an F2P experience?
No also, the game is marketed as F2P, and that model was only viable for the content pre-DCV (Destiny Content Vault, Bungie's way of cutting out content due to technical limitations) ; a current new player experience / F2P is extremely limited, and requires to go break the piggy bank for all DLCs.

How's the overall gameplay? PvE? PvP?

PvE + PvP :

Classes?
The game possesses 3 classes ; Hunter, Warlock, Titan, each with affinities for specific play style such as Titans being walking tanks, hunters being sneaky and DPS spammer, and warlocks able to burst heal but also do decent damage with great movement.

The community overall has its usual case of class elitism bantering on each class, and saying theirs is better than the rest.

The overall verdict being ; hunter has the closest movement kit to FPS / Platformers with a triple jump setup and in-land dodges, the one most players are accustomed to it, Titan and warlocks behave on different move sets unique to D2 with gliding and in-air dashes that can be confusing / irritating for new players.

If you want to feel like a space wizard, throwing nova bombs out of their hands and being able to fly / glide and in-air dodges and go extremely fast? Warlock is for you.

If you want to feel like a walking tank, that can transform itself into flying missiles, or punch enemies to death with mini hammers on fire? Titan is for you.

Gun play?
Extremely nice and enjoyable with a decent variety of guns / armors to craft builds for, from machine guns shooting thunders after precision kills, to fusion rifles shooting sticky bolts that explode on impact. A lot of choice and build crafting is doable.

Movement?
Used to be amazing, got severely nerfed due to some issues of unified sandbox (explained later in the post).

Content?
Gets repetitive extremely fast if you are a regular player of the game, aside from challenges and farming for weapons, most PvE mechanics are pretty easy, and PvP... Well you know how to shoot and kill.
The new season refresh usually brings out some seasonal activities that can be grinded in a few days worth of game play and then left to dust if the seal is acquired, most content comes from BIG DLCs (such as Lightfall, Witch Queen, etc.)

Grind?
Except if you are someone with very precise objectives in mind (quad 100s armor builds, 0 wasted stats, perfect weapon god rolls, etc.) the game is not grindy compared to other "MMOs" such as warframe. A solid 5/10 in term of "grindiness" if you need to invest time for perfecting builds. That grind will of course be on the long-term for activities capped on a weekly basis such as raids or nightfalls.

PvE alone :
Most seasons will have obnoxious seasonal mods rendering us overpowered and a slow powercreep has been stacking up for the past year. The only way to feel "difficulty" in endgame PvE is to force yourself to challenge yourself with harder version of raids (low-mans) or go for deathless/flawless completions, as an example.

PvP alone :
Meta is defined 99% of the time by the obnoxious seasonal mods also abused in PVE, Bungie's old design philosophy had one singular sandbox, and they slowly realized the limitation of such design and are slowly separating them, which ended up generating a lots of nerfs both movement wise and gunplay wise.
Meta is reigned by braindead combos of weapons both in low level lobbies or the "sweaty" PvP modes such as Trials of Osiris and/or Iron Banner.

Overall, both communities are pretty secluded, hardcore PvE players will consider PvP as a chore they have to do for loot, and vice-versa.


How about the community?

The D2 community, both PvE and PvP are extremely active, the in-game Looking for group are dead and were outshined by the numerous massive LFG discords and/or D2 mobile app LFG.

You can meet a plethora of players there, ranking from :
- The general meme, the destiny 2 dad, only can play 15 mins per week and wants his loot, but the legit way, no cheesing !
- Your average player, does his raids and activities diligently, and knows the mechanics and willing to learn.
- Your PvP edgelord, that will require a full trials of osiris report, and your K/D in the last 5 seasons.
- Your PvE edgelord, aka raid farmers, that passes most of his time chaining up raids and fast completions of activities.

Each sub-categories has its extreme on the spectrum, going from Positive Mental Attitude God, to overly racist player, you're warned.

How does D2 compare to other game of the same style, such as warframe?
Similar in term of fast-paced movement, different regarding in-game mechanics, where Warframe will be a "one man army" kind of setup for clears event at high end PvE ; Destiny 2 still requires you to be a team player in some activities.

So, is the game worth investing time and money?
D2 is not a game that can please a lot of players, but if you like the very limited experience you can get from the F2P? You can become addicted pretty often, just skimming the reviews you'll see the average time of most reviews is bordering the 1000h+ ; showing you that the game can be extremely addicting.\t\t\t\t\t
