\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is my opinion which is subjective ‘cause I like this game.
It has changed a lot over the years since Destiny 1, I've spent over 1700 hours in this game and I love it, I like the lore, play style, art style, etc. I feel like this game is more enjoyable when it's played with friends, there are some activities that you won't be able to complete solo anyways, I'm talking about Raids or challenging activities like the Nightfalls in highest difficulties.
PvP is enjoyable, kind of broken sometimes but since this is not an eSport focused game, I think that is fine. PvPvE (Gambit) is ok when playing with friends IMO. The main content is PvE, you can just spend some time for free roaming in different destinations killing adds, completing public events, lost sectors and so on, completing strikes, nightfalls or Raids. There are different quests and missions as well: 3 story driven campaigns (Forsaken, Shadowkeep, Beyond light) which are part of the paid content, the red war and the first 2 DLC's were removed from the game when the last DLC was released. There are tons of equipment, armor sets and weapons that you can obtain from random drops, completing specific activities or after completing a quest. I'm not sure how the game is with the free content alone, but that's a good start to figure it out if you like it or not and then proceed to purchase the additional DLC's and season passes. I would recommend you buy the Deluxe edition since it has all 4 seasons passes for this year along with the last DLC. (Forsaken and Shadowkeep are not included but they can be bundled for better deal).
The game has its flaws (PvP sometimes broken, bugs here and there, but bungie usually addresses those issues to keep the game enjoyable). Some people complain about the "lack" of content. But If you are reading this, then you are probably new and haven't played the game yet, in that case I'd say that the game has a lot to offer, start with the free version and if you like the core game, then you'll enjoy the rest. One last advice; find a clan or look for Destiny servers on discord.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAt first, a lot of people gave Bungie the excuse of being under Activision for all of the "Pay to Win" schemes that were ruining a lot of promise that Destiny 1 had; Trials, Iron Banner, Crucible all became limited by a very finite meta that was limited to 2-3 guns within a single gun-type. It had it's ups and downs; eventually making it possible to compete without grinding ToOr which helped other players I would help during their run. Now after a long time I tried Destiny 2 with the F2P concept helping me ease back into it. A lot of content felt familiar and a little overused but fine nonetheless. Beyond Light is the single worst "Pay to Win" concept that Bungie, without evil ol'Activision to blame, has implemented that not only ruined PvP but gutted the PvE most players enjoy. No Time to Explain is legitimately broken and to make it worse you have to buy the right thing to get or best of luck getting past someone who dropped another 10-30 USD for it. Ace of Spades is just as bad and while I understand there will always be a meta to play by there needs to be an entry point for players with the skill to win to come into; but besides the broken guns atm the Stasis ability is the SINGLE WORST thing to ever happen to PvP. Broken, unfair doesn't come close to describing this clearly overpowered subclass. I do not understand how a company like Bungie can honestly say they play tested it and thought 'this works'. They have "patched" it but it doesn't come close to fixing it and sadly because players, myself included, paid for this subclass I don't see Bungie fixing it until they feel like players got their money's worth.

I will not recommend this game to anyone unless Bungie openly admits they screwed up by gutting this much content, making you pay FAR too much for it and promoting the most clear cut "Pay to Win" system for the entirety of Destiny (1 and 2). It's embarrassing that Bungie is following in the same steps as a company like Activision; and I hope they change.\t\t\t\t\t
