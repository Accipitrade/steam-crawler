\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny is a difficult game to play. Not in the way a typical shooter is, with aiming, reaction timing and ability configuration. I mean in following along. Unlike MMO's where you can go through past content and get a full picture of the game and its story at your own pace, if you were not here when the content was ongoing, you've missed out, and there is no way to get that information in-game.

Destiny has decided that rather than giving a story that you can follow along with and play as you go, as they did with the launch of D2 and it's first 2 expansions, Bungie began to "Block" or "Segment" each story into mini episodes for each "season". And the main Expansions HEAVILY run on information from these mini episodes. Yes, you can sort of block off each expansion as their own story, but in-between the expansions are the seasons that provide details and give more context to what in the world is going on in the world. These are also time-gated, so you can't play through it all at once, and have to wait week by week to slowly unravel the story. If, like me, you can't always spend an hour or two a week playing the game, (and per the limits of the game that isn't enough), you will not be able to get the details you need to follow along in the story.

As an example, you have a chicken at the start of the month. It is a chicken that you want to raise, and you need to feed it once a week. Unfortunately, you must go on a trip to another city, and will be gone for 3 weeks. You cannot feed the chicken, and must rely on a friendly neighbor to take care of it. Upon returning from your trip, you find that the chicken is gone, and in it's place is a pig. You try to ask your neighbor what happened, but your neighbor is missing. You have no idea what in the world to do with this pig, and you don't know where your chicken is. Unbeknownst to you, the chicken was chased out of your yard by a wolf, who was chased by your neighbor, only to find the chicken already eaten by the wolf. Your neighbor instead puts a pig in it's place, hoping you won't notice, and moves to another city in order to hide. How did you find this information? By talking to another 3rd party who happened to have been around during the interaction and saw it.

Congratulations, you now understand how to get information and follow the story of Destiny; going to Youtube or someone else who can tell you what the ♥♥♥♥ happened.

The hardest thing about this game is following along with what's going on. If you can't actively play, you're out of luck. And as you've seen in other comments this game is VERY repetitive, so it's understandable that you dip out for months at a time, only to come back to a story that you cannot find ANY context for. There is a "History of Destiny" button on the grand System Map, but it gives such a tiny synopsis on what happened that it's practically useless. You don't know who Crow is, you don't know who Calus or Osiris are, and guess what? Good luck finding that information in-game.

I understand this is a "you had to be there to have seen it!" kind of approach, but for new players who have no idea what's going on, how in the world are they supposed to follow along if you don't tell them what in the world you are doing? Or what you're doing it for? Give me a freaking glossary where I can go over the history, getting key details, or something! I would not have been able to get back into destiny if it wasn't for Youtubers like "MyNameIsByf", and it's painful that Destiny doesn't have some form of backlogging or "cliff notes" for me to at least get an idea of what's going on.\t\t\t\t\t
