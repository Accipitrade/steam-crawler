\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've wanted to write a review for Destiny 2 for a long time, but haven't really figured out what to say until now. I have so many feelings about this game, and I'll preface this by saying that almost all of these feelings are very positive. Destiny 1 was one of the first games I played on my Xbox, and put so many hours into the game my family started to worry about my health (somehow despite this I never did over half the content in the game, but whatever). However, when Destiny 2 launched and was met with very critical review, I was swayed despite how excited I was to play more Destiny content. Because of this, I can't attest for any of the community experience prior to Shadowkeep, but I will say that I know however it was I wish I had been there for it. I have never found a game that quite fits my preferences like Destiny does. I love the feeling of progressing in this world as your own character, and becoming more and more powerful in this fantasy/science fiction setting. The way they set up your character in the world in my opinion is so well done. It is extremely easy to do anything from making an intricately woven backstory for your Guardian, to caring nothing about your character. They allow for your own creativity to fit into the universe, and I feel like that's an often seldom-captured aspect of the RPG game genre, particularly in the FPS environment.

On a similar beat, I have never found myself so engrossed in a fictional universe as I have been and am still with that of Destiny. Although some complaints of dialogue can be justified (this is most likely due to their relatively tame audience appeal) the overall grand story of the universe is so profound and so detailed that it becomes so easy to become obsessed. I have now reached the point where my girlfriend has to beg me to stop talking about Destiny lore that I spent that morning looking into. One thing I look for in a game's story is a relatively simple nature that you can understand as you go through the game, but if you choose to look further you can find a much more detailed world. Destiny accomplishes this like nothing else I can think of. Some of my best experiences with the game are getting a new exotic and reading the lore and seeing that it references a character that was mentioned in another lore piece, connecting the universe in ways that I would normally never know about or need to know about. I feel so rewarded for being invested in the story by feeling like events in the constantly progressing story are so much more important.

This ties into another point, which is the narrative tools that the game uses. Ignoring gameplay entirely, the storytelling of this game is so grand and powerful, and they enable this through their story mediums. There are the obvious storytelling components such as the campaigns, but there is so much more than that to those who want to see it. Every questline, every strike, and all PvP activities have a narrative purpose, and although it doesn't matter in the gameplay loop, it makes the game all the more immersive to those who want to care about this story.

Focusing more on the gameplay, this is where the game becomes more mixed. This is deeply polarizing aspect of the game that makes some people (including me) incredibly addicted to the game, while others can't play more than 50 hours. There is no denying that there is a massive amount of content within the game. At this time there are 15 strikes across 7 planets, with 3 raids, 3 dungeons, and more lost sectors than I would care to count. Not to mention the PvP Crucible and the PvPvE Gambit game modes that add much more alternative content to the game. All of this, with the addition of new quests being constantly added make for great activities in which you pursue a certain weapon you've been seeking out or a high-stat armor piece to complete a loadout. The only issue is that the developers have designed the game in a way where the actual gameplay loop is far too large for the content they provide. There are too many weapons in the game that all have unique combinations of perks and stats for a game with fixed content. I don't mean to imply that the game is bloated with weapons, in fact I would say that the game could use more unique weapons. The issue is that the meta of the game changes constantly, and that requires players to then have to grind out new weapons almost every few months in order to stay competitive in both the PvE and PvP meta. There will come a point as a rigorous player that you will grow tired of the same 15 strikes, the same 7 planets, the same 3 raids, etc. There are many who don't, and can find fun in grinding out weapons. There is merit to this, as finally having the perfect Auto Rifle in PvP can be a blast and feel like a wonderful payoff. In the past this issue with the gameplay was a "just deal with it" sort of thing, but as time goes on Bungie is becoming increasingly good at adding content that can be replayed over and over. They give you weapons that you want to grind for, and activities that you find fun on your 30th completion (As of writing this review, I still have not gotten Eyes of Tomorrow after nearly 30 completions of the Deep Stone Crypt raid, and I still love it to death).

TL;DR: The moral of the story here is that Destiny is game that rewards you for being invested. This is true for the gameplay as well as the story. The more time you put into the game and making your character more and more powerful, in addition to understanding the story and why the things that happen do happen will make you so invested in this game that you might just find yourself unable to escape.

The real TL;DR: This game is infuriating, but I love it to death.\t\t\t\t\t
