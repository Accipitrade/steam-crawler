\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBefore anything else I have to say Destiny 2 as a free to play title is shining example of what this pay model can bring to the table. As a new player the experience is enjoyable to get into. Yes there's a clear depth to the mechanics and as an MMO player trying to know it all will take time. But the core game play is so much just do whatever you want and you'll progress, that there's not a feeling of lost efficiency from the new player start up. This also means that the free player experience doesn't feel all that limited.

That said, as hard as I dove into Destiny 2 I also dropped it just as quickly. The combat was fun, the game looks good, and there's some fun weapon variety involved. But the low toll of death (yes that can be a negative), the irritating million step obscure objective quests, enemy warp in at 360 of your position, and frankly I didn't really like the cosmetic system all that much, eventually didn't keep me vested. The plot for me was also not really a draw. Stalwart heroes rattling off the most generic dialogue with no self awareness, and when it is the comic relief characters they're not really all that clever. Neither of the two options for writing meet the mark. Also, and this is probably a point in the game's favor, I just don't like space marines all that much in any iteration. So as much as it was able to draw me in, getting vested in a world where I don't like my player avatar isn't meeting that MMO mark for me.

So I guess the takeaway is that if you like looter shooter's Destiny is a very good game within that genre. Since the cost of entry is zero dollars if you are interested I would highly recommend giving it a shot.\t\t\t\t\t

It's a 40€ expansion (2/3 of a full-price game), and all they did was recycle a map from the first Destiny 1. The story they added, was just a mash of nonsense that made me scratch my head and continue to play, thinking it would all make sense in the end.

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHas it truly been... 253 hours already? ... My god.

As a new light player, this game will consume you.
Being a 'New Light' means you are a fresh start account.

Planning on buying all the expansion packs? Do it.
The content you get is mind boggling for its value.

There is so much to do and see that even with 253 hours in I feel I am just getting started, yet EVERY play session I have feels rewarding. Whether it's a quick 7-15 minute Nightfall (one of the tougher PVE 'Strikes' you can do where you get matched up with 2 others and together have to defeat bosses to get to the end) or just roaming around admiring the beautiful landscapes and vistas... it's always worth it.

Don't even get me started on the raids! Have yet to do a full run with a guild, but it's on the list.

Then there's the Crucible, the PVP part of the game. Meant as a training ground for yourself and other guardians for the battles to come against the many enemies present in the world of Destiny.
Tactics are bountiful, load-outs feels unique and interesting to play with, it's never dull.

Sure, some people are 'meta' - they run really strong (overpowered?) builds - but do not let that discourage you from trying out new things. Most people are quite chill and they play what they like, not what is best.

So yes, I do recommend this game. I'll check back in after I put in another 250 hours of my life...
Have fun in the world of Destiny, and see you in the Crucible... Guardian! ;)

Time to finish up on these bounties.
