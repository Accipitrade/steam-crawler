\r\n\t\t\t\t\tJumping in for the first time at the start of New Light and I absolutely fell in love. For new players the game doesn't hold your hand which is both good and bad. It's good in the sense that it throws you directly into the endless game play loop; however, I think many would prefer to be started in (or at least have clear direction with how to start) the campaign. It should be noted, players can play through the campaigns after they've completed the initial tutorial, but you will need to go pick up the campaign quest-lines from Amanda Holliday in the Tower. That was how I started my personal foray into Destiny. After rushing through the very short Red War campaign I immediately had to play all of the others until I was caught up through Shadowkeep. After making my way through the campaigns I was introduced to the endless barrage of content known to be Destiny 2. From the PvE content - Raids, Strikes, Vex Offensive, etc. to the PvP content - Control, Survival, Gambit, etc. (I understand you may not understand what alll of these terms, but know there's lots) believe me when I say there is truly something for everyone due to the overwhelming scope of the game. Destiny 2 is the epitome of a great-games-as-a-service game in 2019. This game is lightning in a bottle, and since the base game is free-to-play you have no reason not to check it out.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWith the New Light update, Destiny 2 became free to play. Having had a brief experience with the original some almost five years ago, I figured that I would give this one a try.

I became hooked instantly. The core gameplay in Destiny 2 is sharp, very sharp, and accomodates a massive variety of playstyles with nine subclasses each capable of wielding a vast arsenal of weapons in three armament slots. I personally play a Titan, which suits my interest in a tankier playstyle. For this, I like playing around with pulse rifles (memories of the Battle Rifle in Halo 2-3 comes rushing back, God!) and fusion rifles, with the heavy bow Leviathan's Breath rapidly becoming a favourite of mine.

There is something for everyone with regards to gamemodes - really. There are three core gamemodes - Strikes, the main PvE portion of the game, offering something akin to dungeons which each take ~15 minutes to complete in teams of three, with Raids being the bigger counterpart, taking up to five hours to complete for a team of six, from what I hear (this is yet something I have to reach!). The PvP portion of the game, Crucible, is fantastically enjoyable, with some VERY rapid gameplay. There is little space for downtime within the arenas, all of them being relatively small and with such an amount of routes that a player always has opportunities for assaulting their opponents from any direction. The last component of the game, Gambit, merges PvE and PvP components, pitting players in 4v4 matches where the goal is to slay mobs faster than the other team in separate arenas, with players from each team having the option to invade the other's arena. This mode is very frantic and at times becomes totally chaotic, what with having to keep oneself oriented on several fronts at once. In general, all of the gamemodes are very enjoyable, and doing the bounties related to them rarely feels like a chore.

To address bounties quickly; loot is primarily earned through completing daily/weekly quests known as bounties, which provide XP and tokens for gaining gear through vendors. Better loot can be acquired through weekly challenges, raids, and different missions, usually through "perform X repetitions of action Y". Sometimes, more flavorful missions are offered, but in general, the gamemodes themself are fun enough that they need no justification to be played.

As for the areas and mobs in the game, I fail to become tired of the enemy types. There are four main opposing factions in the game - Vex, Cabal, Fallen, and Hive, with an additional fifth faction, the Taken, which aggregates corrupted versions of the four previous factions. They all have unique identities through the visual styles in their units and areas and offer different challenges in dealing with them.

Continuing off of visual styles, Destiny 2 is GORGEOUS. All of the areas shock me in how pretty and detailed they are! Nessus particularly wows me every time I go to it, with the strange, hyperfuturistic cubes of the Vex littering the planet. The visuals of The Reckoning is amazing as well, with its weirdly clean, geometric shapes, appearing as some sort of modern art installation with the Taken roaming around.

To top it off, I'll say that the team-feeling is Destiny 2 is lovely! The classes compliment each other well, and I always feel like my fireteam has my back, and I theirs. For people seeking a more social FPS experience, this is it.\t\t\t\t\t
