\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhere to start. I am a long-time gamer, I started playing video games when they first hit computers. Yes, that means I am in my 50's and as such you may want to read my review with that in mind.

I should start with the positive, and for the record I think there are some very positive points to this game.
•\tThe storyline is engaging or it was once I found it. The art and graphics are amazing.
•\tTechnically speaking the game plays well on my PC and its no juggernaut. I think that there are a number of other recent titles that could learn a lot from how Bungie optimized this game.
•\tThe voice acting was also top notch no complaints there at all.

The negative and I have to be honest the negative has given me significant pause, so much so that I am considering moving on to other games without these issues.

•\tNearly zero introduction is provided to the player. If you are new you are quite literally thrown into a hostile environment with no understanding of what you are fighting or what you are supposed to doing.

•\tThe list of tools at your disposal is large and yet you have no understanding of how they are supposed to be used. I understand that for returning players with previous experience this is not as big of an issue but for a net new player this is bewildering to say the least.

•\tThe interface is absolutely atrocious. In fairness this could be fixed with a few simple typographical and interface changes. By way of example I will focus on the quest system which as to be the worst in any game I have played in recent years.
o\tPick up a quest and you are faced with a one-line blurb that suggests you go kill so and so person or being. Down below that is a reward. There is, however, nothing to indicate that you will have 6800 other steps to complete before you actually get said reward.
o\tFixing this is as simple as inserting a step count, i.e. step 1 of 4 or however many steps there may be. Someone will suggest that this is immersion breaking but I would argue that the quest telling me that doing X gets me Y and then adding a step over and over again is far more damaging than any minor immersion issues.

•\tThe storyline has been enjoyable but it would have been far more enjoyable if I had been guided to it right off the bat. There were some minor comments about talking to folks but nothing specific.
o\tThis also could be fixed with a simple game toggle during the initial moments of the game. Offer players an option to toggle story mode. This would then ONLY display quests and activities directly related to the original story. Players could toggle this as they choose. I am no programmer but toggling content has been done in other games and doing that in New Light would be a big help for brand new players.

Let me summarize before this gets to long in the tooth. Bungie has made a great game but the developers appear to have forgotten, or don’t care about how the game looks and feels from a new players perspective.

UPDATE: Having now put alot more hours into the game I will update my review with the following. The points I made earlier are still, for me, true. having said that I can also say that if you are ok with having to go to the web to get more information on almost all non campaign related quests then the game can still be enjoyable. I have enjoyed the campaign far more than I expected and the campaign quests are well laid out and fun to do. I also enjoy the public events as well as the listed adventures (these are separate from the campaign but still well written and enjoyable) so with all that said I can say once again, if you don't mind that fact that the non campaign quests are terribly written then the game is certainly worth playing.
