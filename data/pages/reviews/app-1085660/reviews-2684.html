\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played alot of Destiny since it came out in 2014 for the first entry. To say I have put a bit of my life into it (roughly 1,200 hours between 1 and 2) is putting it lightly. Just because you put alot of time into something doesn't necessarily mean it's amazing. There can be alot of pushback, complications, slow downs and issues that also take part that can change your perception easily. The best analogy I have for Destiny is being in a marriage/relationship with someone. While some can see this as a good thing, after all being close helps the heart grow fonder, it also has its downs too. Funnily enough I will attempt to completely review this game in this manner. Please enjoy.

Destiny is a beautiful looking, wonderfully sounding S.O. that can't tell a good story to save their lives because they try to be too vague and inclusive. They certainly can write a good deal of the world and events down no problem and finding those notes and pages around the house at times is at least thoughtful. Their choice of music is excellent and when they want to just play that in the house I would not mind (seriously though Michael Salvatori is amazing in these games). When they want to be cooperative and work together in not "striking" out in baseball, they do just fine. When "raiding" a paintball course however it's more messy and the commitment to doing it is too high for my liking. When it comes to competitive, I cannot get along with them but really this is me being more helpful and kind. The competitive nature of them is alright I suppose: I don't like the "crucibles", "gambits" and gauntlets they like to throw at me with our little games but if it makes them happy than I can't be too upset. Just not my cup of tea.

Their biggest problem is satisfying them over the course of time. There is so much to them to learn and understand, yet the "end game" can be quite difficult to enjoy with them because it's solely what we already do in terms of activities. Unless you like those activities and can shut your brain off, I cannot help but they they are kind of boring me just so we can have sex or something. and feel a high afterwards. It feels cheap and a bit dirty of me to think that but they are sort of like that anyways and seem to accept that for years. I was there when we went to gardens, cavern pits, prisons, a king's palace, Russia, took back our house from an infestation of mole rats, Egypt, looked at Mars and the Moon through telescope, Grand Canyon, and New Zealand. Lots of memories that I liked and disliked. Even had a falling out at points (the infestation of mole rats was probably the worse one following that trip to Egypt and looking up at Mars), times I just said "no I'm sorry, this isn't working out. We need to see other people. It is YOU and I. We just don't have good chemistry."

I go on a break, I find some amazing things in my life and upon maturing I realized that sometimes it is okay to just be friends instead. Even despite the possible awkwardness after sex and stuff, but really they are down to earth and don't mind it. They don't change and there is lots of flaws to them upon further inspection that I was blind or ignorant too, They even "freed" themselves from their ♥♥♥♥♥♥ dad to open up more to people. Their recent experience was "keeping in the shadows" for a nostalgic moment of our past and it was not that great. Cute to try to make me feel good remembering when we first looked up to the moon but it's not the same. The grind of maintaining our bond is huge and almost seems pointless if we really step back from it all. Yet something still holds me with them.

Perhaps it's because they are still with their mother (a renowned "bungie" expert when jumping off bridges) and I always respected their mother for what they did long ago and divorcing the husband was ultimately the right choice to make. Doesn't make it easy for her or my S.O. and the first year from that shake up is "not something I think anyone can truly get upset for" if they make some mistakes. They are human beings though and make plenty of flaws and such all the same. What we should always do is see if they are needing improvement and hope that mother can remind them of their place so they don't go down a hole they can never get out of. "I would recommend" people like this in my life because I now look at them as friend rather than lovers and seeing so many amazing people from our breaks is a win-win situation. They are flawed to a point absolutely, but when you initially saw them as this perfect being you secretly hope you still get that moment. This year in particular was a shake up. Next year they really have to step up their game,

P.S.- Their shopping habits are terrible and while I understand they do it for their mom (since she just left her husband and lost the house), I think they go a bit to far on how to make their money. It's borderline selling their soul.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI played Destiny 2 in Beta for PC way back and was extremely pleased with the over all feel/control and fluid combat system. The system keeps you fragile while making you feel powerful, exactly as human guardians are intended.

The initial release left MUCH to be desired and I was truthfully fairly angry about having spent the amount of money I did on the preorder edition + the First Osiris expansion.

However, after taking a long/angry hiatus, I returned to the game when it was ported to steam.
Without purchasing the next remaining expansions I set out to see if the game could at least provide the content hours I initially paid for.
To my surprise there was an overwhelming amount to catch up on. It seem the game at some point started adding weekly content patches to the existing game, and I was looking at a new and exciting learning curve.
The weapon mod system, that had once been pretty complicated, had been nicely and neatly cleaned up any they had also removed the initial currency involved in upgrading them by providing account wide infinites.

I then purchased both expansions when they appeared on sale and have been fan-boying all over the game with the same feeling of promise the game provided in Beta.

If you had previously played on Battle.net and were at all hesitant about returning to the game, or even jsut intimidated by the new DLC price, I highly recommend giving the game another go.

Overall, the game offers a unique-feel combat system, an insane surplus of weapons that are all a unique experience to equip and use. The planets/planetoids/moons you explore are expansive open worlds with unique and detailed landscapes at a high res. And the multiplayer experience for both pve and pvp is both exciting and immersive.

Don't miss the experience.\t\t\t\t\t
