\r\n\t\t\t\t\t*Note if you dont want to read the book long review just skip to the bottom for basically short answers. Also look at this review has more of a NEUTRAL review. Not a postive or negative one.*

I was looking through the reviews and saw a lot of biased reviews either they are favoring destiny just because or they are hating it because. I figured I'd give an opinion thats a bit more neutral. I've been playing Destiny 2 since day one on battlenet app. (had around 2,5k or so hours before steam release. I have around 560 hours after steam release). I've seen the game go through waves where it changing for the better then the shift in it changing for the worse. Right now destiny 2 is for a specfic kind of gamer. Which makes it hard to give a flat out yes or no if it's worth it. I'll break up the sections so you can figure out if this game is for you or not.

Are you planning on playing destiny has a free to play player or a paid to play player? <-- that is the most important question that determines if this game is worth it for you or not.

If you just planned on trying it out and playing it as a f2p then its absolutely not worth it. The way they changed the format to where it just throws you in the game not in a campaign at all with no direction on what to do and limited activties really cause for a confusing expierence. There isn't a path that a new player can see to take to get hte hang of the game so they just get thrusted into the game not knowing anything. New players have all access to year 1 activities which are strikes (coop short missions), basic nightfalls, "open world events" on planets (except moon, tangled shore, and dreaming city), and 3 short campaigns (Red war [base d2 campaign], Osiris campaign and the mars campaign). Free to play players have a limit on their power level, are EXTREMELY limited on exotics and legendary equipment which make a huge difference in PvP and end game content. They also are missing out on some of the most viable sub class trees (tied to foresaken). Most of the "fun" actvities are tied to forsaken or shadowkeep. Oh and theres 1 raid (the worst raid of them all that f2p players can play). So in conclusion to this point. F2p players can't really get a feel for the game since the hand they are dealt with is just half assed.

Now if you are looking to get the full destiny expierence you need at least foresaken. That alone adds on so much more end game content and even items to collect (viable items). This is where destiny shines. It shines in the content that was brought in forsaken and continues to shine in the content that was brought in shadowkeep ( a bit less though ill talk about that later). When someone has 1 or both the new expansions they have so much content that a new player will or a vet that hasnt kept up with the game will find a good amount of hours worth of gameplay before finally parting ways with it. Which is good... to a limit. Once youve done all the content and got the rolls for weapons that you want the game turns old real fast and the charm dies down.

Destiny has a problem (usually only people who have played hte game long enough see). Bungie hasn't figured out how to correctly push out content that feels fresh and worth grinding for. Yes, they did offer a battlepass like most games these days but its purely cosmetics and extras that are on the paid side. The mosti mportant things f2p players can get. With that being said if you are a person that enjoys pvp. You will be extremely frustrated with destiny. Everytime they "Fix" something a new problem rises thats 2x more annoying then the previous one. (A big reason why ppl who have done all the content and been around for a bit just decide to part ways until the next update).
