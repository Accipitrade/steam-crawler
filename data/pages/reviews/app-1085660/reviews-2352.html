\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game is quite pretty and somewhat immersive, the voice acting is good and the story stuff is interesting enough (although not spectacular).

The classes are great with a good mixture of skills and things to play around with. The armour selection is very limited and boring, really. The armour and weapon mods are flat and not too interesting. The main thing that took my interest is the ‘exotic’ gear, which you can only equip one armour and one weapon from. The leveling system is dull and caps out quickly enough with no real way to progress.

Most of the ‘end-game’ content is locked behind raids, which have no lobby system or way of meeting people in game to partake in. This makes that part of the game inaccessible and a downer. Also consider that these types of activities have no in game clues or guides, you gotta play with a wiki open to get things done (yawn).

Last note, the real-money shop is a yawn fest and has taken most of the staff’s energy it feels, since Bungie parted company from Activision. There just isn’t enough content to keep me interested. What is there are loads of tedious errands tied together into quests to unlock unique equipment. Most of which is uninspired and not worth it anyway.

By the way, I’ve made it just past 300 hours with buying all the DLC stuff for real money. So without that I would have been bored and done by 50 hours probably (the main campaign and some PVP stuff). I just can’t really recommend it to anyone, but feel free to play it since the base game costs nothing.

An average fare over-all with a distinct lack of varied content and end-game content.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is currently a pretty great game. To call it free to play would be a little disingenuous since each expansion after the archived ones needs to be paid for, but at the same time, they are great expansions and well worth the dollars, especially since the base game is free and if you get them on steam sale (they are usually 25 per, but I've seen them as low as 35 for 2)... even so, it's pretty close to free, and most free to play games aren't all that "Free" because you need to invest in certain things in the game to make it functionally playable, but destiny 2 doesn't really do that so much, they just give you a game that feels fun and rewarding and is pretty entertaining. I would classify it as about as free to play as warframe and path of exile, though less buggy than both. There are cosmetics you can buy, but to me they aren't all that appealing and are pretty overpriced and you can get everything in the game just by playing short of cosmetics.

Overall It's a solid game; the voice acting in the old acts (the archived ones) is a bit hokey at times, but makes up for it with better writing and voice acting in later expansions.

Fun game, plays well, few bugs, excellent pvp system and pve gameplay loop. Would strongly recommend if you like the idea of FPS with RPG elements and abilities/spells thrown in for good measure.

Only major criticisms I have are that they don't explain raid mechanics very well in the game so you'll have to research those online first, which isn't a big gripe since that's pretty much all games with raids, and also that the load times can be pretty crappy and disrupt the flow a bit, but even then it kind of gives you a nice breather when you move between areas to stretch and get some water or whatever, but just keep that in mind and take care of everything you have to in one place before moving on to the next for the day as much as possible or the load times will significantly affect your progress. The UI could be a bit better, but it's honestly not bad, I can just see places where it could/should be improved.

There are also nice 3rd party sites that give you some extra quality of life inventory management, but I'd hardly say that's necessary to enjoy the game, but it is nice that they support that. They also kind of use some FOMO tactics for their marketing of cosmetics (limited time stuff) but since I honestly don't really enjoy the cosmetics they have and consider them overpriced for what they are, I am not affect by this at all.

D2 is the kind of game you'll probably play a ton of at first and then complete each expansion when it comes out, and maybe jump on for an event if you feel up for it, but it also doesn't punish you desperately for missing a single day and thus falling behind forever. Granted you can get more resources and progress and rewards by playing more frequently, but they aren't gamplay critical or power enabling so much, more just making things easier on yourself later, so if you do enjoy grinding your face off, you can do that too and still feel rewarded.

I will say that while there is some RNG elements to rewards in this game, they really aren't that bad, and some stuff is a little grindy, but nothing is even remotely egregiously grindy. All in all I appreciate everything bungie is doing at this time.\t\t\t\t\t
