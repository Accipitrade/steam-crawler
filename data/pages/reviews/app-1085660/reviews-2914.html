\r\n\t\t\t\t\tBeen playing since Destiny 1, and when I made the jump to PC for Destiny 2, I was blown away by how much more smooth and fast gameplay felt. If you're thinking of hopping over from console, try it out!

If you're new to Destiny in general, be warned that this game comes with pros and cons. Gameplay and mechanics is great. One of the best feeling shooters I've had the pleasure of playing, ever. And when you're in a raid and your team has the mechanics down, it's an extremely satisfying feeling. Some weapons are also really cool. Not Borderlands level OP, but aesthetically pleasing and fun to use and handle.

However, Destiny 2 is best played with friends or a clan or whatever you prefer. As long as you have someone that you can play with, you will absolutely have a great experience. Solo... It's doable, but definitely not as much fun in my opinion. My best memories and moments are from goofing off in PvP with friends, or just chatting while grinding PvE content.

And now, we get to the cons. And these are all simply my opinion. Story is very meh. Without lore masters like Byf, it's very hard to get into the lore. You find yourself shooting things for no particular reason. Probably just for loot. And speaking of loot, RNG is very real in this game. Some really great weapons that are almost required for certain loadouts are locked behind RNG. I'm looking at you, Monte Carlo. Because of the heavy emphasis on RNG, it's made me look at the game as less of a min/max RPG, and more of a fun MMO that I play with friends. It's hard for me to take this game seriously anymore because of the many mechanics that don't really respect my time.

All the cons aside, I still play this game a lot, and sink a lot of hours into it, and that's because it still is very much a fun game that I like to play with friends. And I strongly stand by my recommendation to play this game. Even if you don't get into it, it's worth trying it out, because it might just be a place to make great new memories and friends.\t\t\t\t\t
