\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe game continues to evolve, and the developer is not afraid to say when they feel that they have made mistakes and take steps to rectify those mistakes. Since the games intial (and troubled) launch they constantly moved to make the game better and more enjoyable.

Two points of critisim for the developer, however. A lot of issues stem from one exotic weapon or another unbalancing the pvp or pve meta results in nerfs that can wreak approaches to the game or ruin a weapon. The sniper rifle Whisper of the Worm is a great example, its perks and damage were so good that it became a default pve weapon for boss damage phases. In its current form its ok, but not a great exotic, and the mission to get it is both challanging and interesting. It started out as a great weapon, but rather than making it less effective on bosses to get damage in line with other exotics the nerfs to it push other weapons into the fore. These will also eventually be nerfed, because Bungjie has issues with any weapon or power that becomes so effective that a raid team considers it essential. The constantly moving bar can cheapen the effort and time invested in these weapons. Some exotics are also very weak to use, and as a result it can be very disappointing to get them when you do get an exotic drop. The exotics as a whole need to be examine and reviewed, random drop weapons should be less effective than quest exotics, and the harder the mission the better the weapon should be. Outbreak Perfected should be an amazing weapon and should stay an amazing weapon, better than a random drop Huckleberry. Armor exotics should be revamped, their intrinsic perks should be reworked to ensure that they are a quality item and highly specialized in their application. I got a Stag helmet and sighed deeply, for its effectively useless for its perk, and I will almost always wear my Pheonix Protocal, Crown of Tempests, or Geomag Stabelizers due to its all around usefulness. If they want to keep these types of armor they should be linked to special missions like the exotic weapons, making these (objectively) better armor pieces a reward for time and effort and not getting lucky with a random vex henchman.

The second point of critisism is end game rewards, specifically the post 950 grind. Power boosts from the artifact are great, but needing to fill out every slot and the things needed to push those slots being limited to such extreme activities as raiding and master level hunting and such can be very exclusionary to solo players. It would be better to have this system in place where all powerful rewards contribute to advancement and these "pinnicle" activities reward a better light bump or have protections in place so you get something you need every time and not your third set of gloves in three weeks. This would make that part more satisifying.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tHave a lot more hours on battle.net, and have enjoyed every single one. This game isn't an mmo or a looter shooter, but rather an interesting fusion of the two, and it just works so damn well. The movement, gunplay, and general mechanics of the game just feel so good, and have been refined a ton over the 5 years the franchise has been around. Every expansion brings new, refreshing content to play, and everything Bungie puts out whether it be weapons, maps, etc feels extremely refined.

This game can support any kind of player, whether it is a casual player that doesn't want to grind to the top power possible and would prefer to just play for an hour or two a day, the pvp god that just wants to destroy players in the crucible, or the person looking for a challenge by struggling through difficult raids requiring coordination between you and your allies. This game really is something special, and covering every single aspect of why I love it would be near impossible.

Seriously, if you are on the fence about this game just try it, there is a ridiculous amount of content they give you for free, and there is no reason to not give it a shot.\t\t\t\t\t
