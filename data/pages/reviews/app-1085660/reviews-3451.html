\r\n\t\t\t\t\tUpdate: Jan 2023

I took a break from this game since the first season of shadowkeep and decided to pick it back up and play though beyond light and witch queen. Both expansions have their ups and downs but witch queen I'd say has been better. I still cannot figure out how a new player and in this case a returning player can keep the stories straight between the two expansions when bought at the same time. Not only that but you have seasonal story content that just drops randomly throughout the season. So you could be grinding out beyond light story and the game forces you into a seasonal story mission next time you launch the game. They really need to take the time and get their content setup in a way where someone can play through the story without jarring jumps of 'who are you and why are you talking about this' going on all the time as well as 'are you part of the current story i'm working on or am i getting something i'm not ready for yet' I hope with lightfall they make the ability to display all the story missions in order in the director and give you the ability to replay any of them.

Its also crazy to me that one can buy Forsaken and yet not have the ability to even play it since the tangled shore is not currently on the map. I understand they are unvaulting everything with lightfall but its got to suck for someone who buys forsaken without knowing they can't even really play it.

The good news is the game still feels and plays as good as I remember it. And its been fun playing through what I can of what I missed, just wished I had access to everything so I can keep the story straight on what I missed.

Original Review:
The game is pretty good overall. They made everyone start at the same level with the recent expansion they released if you have even played it before which was wonky. I get the idea of wanting to play with friends right off the bat who have been playing for a while yet at the same time it would of been better if they gave you an item right off the bat that would do it and you could choose to use it whenever you wanted.

Other then that I do recommend trying it out and reach out to the various communities the game has for help and assistance. Lots of helpful people out there love helping new players.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI've played around 500 hours of D2 on console, an additional 150 during it being hosted on the Battlenet platform via Activision Blizzard, and here I am, just getting started on continuing the journey on steam.

The cross-save functionality is a MASSIVE QOL improvement and steps like these are pushing the rest of the industry to keep pace. You're now able to hop over to console or steam with your account after doing a bit of back-end account/character linking via the bungie website. Destiny has always been a game that is expotentnially more enjoyable with a friend or two / a clan -- so being able to break down some of the isolating factors that fracture the playerbase is a great step.

An additional positive move is this F2P model being introduced. While there is a cash shop via the Eververse store - it is cosmetically based. You're able to grind and get items, ships, vehicles, etc through playing the game and grinding PVP/PVE/ etc.

This game is not pay to win from what I've personally encountered.

The soundtrack is exceptional -- each expansion continues to push the composers to get better. Some very memorable tracks and the music compliments a lot of the missions / environment / crazy battle scenes quite well, just as it did in D1, getting the pulse up and making everything feel very edge of your seat epic.

While I'm only just getting started with the new expansion, I'm pleased with some of the changes I've seen, excited to try to get back with my old console pals while also making progress on the PC side - and I hope the steps taken will both bring in new Guardians, get old veterans re-invested, and keep this francise growing positively.

Bungie has noted they hope to continue to make this game more MMO feeling / story based. I think that coupled with the astoundingly good gun play, continued new content / raids / challenges -- while keeping the game challenging and removing the old faceroll through everything issues which have plagued the game in the past - they could very well have a bright future.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tGame plays just like before, I haven't noticed any huge changes yet, so that's good enough I suppose.
The game has 3 minor strikes with me so far:

1. They finally implement a transmog system, but they decide to tie it to the cash store rather than exotics and collection system which saves every item you ever picked up. Pretty idiotic decision.

2. Timesponge: Once you start the shadowkeep storyline, you get abruptly dragged out of it because they decided that "Here's an item, charge it by doing stuff." was a great idea after two missions. Sadly this means you'll be farming the same three public events for a while. If unlucky like me, the zone will be empty and this will take a while.

3. Activision might be gone, but their spirit is still here. Everest store now has more content than ever, and sells silver in even numbers. They are wise enough to know that by making prices odd numbered, they can cash whales easier. That item you want is 700? Buy 1000k. But there's nothing at 300, so remember to refill later. Least they learned something sleazy from activision. This probably ties in with Transmog system, they are grabbing cash big time on microtransactions.

The game loop until you get to endgame is weak. Do public events for blues, get em heroic for legendary and chance at exotic. Just remember that most players don't bother finding out how to upgrade an event or use chat.\t\t\t\t\t
