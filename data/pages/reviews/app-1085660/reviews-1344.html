I was never a fan of Bungie. But, on the recommendation of a few friends, I picked up Destiny 2. I actually had fun with it and spent quite a few hours in it, so I decided to pick up the expansion and spend a little coin on the ingame currency and such.

A few days ago, I had Vortex (nexusmods.com) open, messing with Fallout 4 and then Stardew Valley. Little did I know, that is one of the apparently vast collection of unrelated applications that, if Bungie's unnecessarily invasive DRM / Anti-Cheat detects it, results in an instant, permanent ban.

No, I was not cheating. I had no interest in cheating in Destiny 2, as I was having a lot of fun just screwing around in low-intensity PvE and collecting stuff. Who the hell cheats to do THAT?

I went to Bungie's "ban" site to appeal it, but the button to appeal doesn't even work.

They essentially took my money and ran.
