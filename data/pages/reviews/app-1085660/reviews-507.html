\r\n\t\t\t\t\tPersonally I love this game, been playing on and off since D1 launched in 2014 and they've done a good job adding to and improving the game. Definitely need a certain type of attention span to continuously play this solo (hence my multiple year-long breaks). Group activities such as raids are an incredible experience bringing out the best of this game in every category- game play, cooperation, environments, connection to story, and rewards. Very interesting lore, if you like Marvel and that kind of sci-fi you will love Destiny lore, only thing is you need to go out of your way to understand all of it, such as reading the in game lore tabs or watching videos. Unfortunately story missions have never been good in my opinion, even when they address really cool lore, they almost always follow a "go here and shoot bad guys" format. PvP is good, lot better than what is has been, competitive playlists are sweaty as heck. Used to be a cheating problem but it seems Bungie has fixed it for the most part. Tons of ways to customize literally everything in the game, all kinds of loadouts, mods, and ways to trick you gear out. Overall this is a great game especially if you play it in moderation, lots to do but if you do it all at once or play too consistently you may find yourself burnt out (unless you have that special kind of attention span where the only thing you need to keep you going is the game play itself, which, in my opinion, is very beautiful and satisfying). There is no one way to beat Destiny, and there is almost always something to do (assuming you are playing in moderation). I would recommend to people who enjoy MMOs and looter-shooters. If you do decide to try Destiny, I highly recommend finding people to play with. As with any game, playing with friends is always better, and with Destiny, having friends to play with allows you to access and beat the best endgame content it has to offer. If you don't know anyone, ask around. I was surprised at how many of my school and work friends play. It's a big community.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tDestiny 2 is a fantastic and wonderful experience! An amazing FPS and looter mixed with the awesome atmosphere of space, interstellar conflict, strange beings, mind-bending powers, and lore to rival some of the most in-depth Sci-Fi literature out there!

Despite the few minor design annoyances and outright silly decisions (i.e., pointer proximity to the screen border autoscrolls the map when trying to select a spawn point at a destination forcing you to move your mouse to keep up on PC), Destiny is easy to lose yourself in and comes with a community of quite friendly folks at-large looking to have fun and cast some space magic!

A minor note would be that it is difficult getting started as a New Light not knowing where to begin, so expect a little bit of experimentation and research on your part to figure out how you'd like to get going.

Alongside the business model of monetizing extra fancy character customization with 0% pay-to-win and DLC largely packed with new content on a regular basis, you'll find yourself spending $60 easily on those aspects and feeling just fine about it.

Bungie has also been making effort into removing negative elements and bettering itself and its work environment for its many amazing artists and creators and is often very conscious of its community's value and the power of good will - which is pretty refreshing considering other game publishing establishments today.

For all of these reasons, Destiny 2 is an experience to jump into and especially if you remotely enjoy even the smallest things about outer space and find yourself looking up every now and then. Highly recommended.
