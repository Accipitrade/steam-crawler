\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI held off from playing this for a long time because I always figured: "Why start this as a pathetic noob when 90% of the other players have sunk like 5 years and $10000 into this already, are all PL 999+ and will constantly crap on and troll me for simply showing up to a match? And what's 'Gambit?' I thought that was an X-man."

Weirdly enough, I have Anthem to thank for getting me into D2. I got Anthem for free because I'd bought a video card, and that game got me back into the spirit of the grand old space mecha opera, but alas, it quickly became unplayable because of server-side issues. So I sought out the closest-looking thing and it was D2. It helped that D2 was being offered for free on Steam and I didn't have to get Battlenet for it. I did shell out an extra 20 bucks because Shadowkeep's box art with Eris Morn in profile was really cool-looking. Too bad Eris herself actually needs to see a therapist.

Anyway, I ended up liking it a lot. The gameplay is fast and fun, and while you can't fly like in Anthem, the gunplay is rock solid. The fact that is is at its heart PVE is a real help. It changes the mindset of the player pool to want to help each other, rather than destroy everyone else's enjoyment of the game. Frankly, that's what drove me away from PUBG. Even in PVP, which isn't as well-developed or realized as a true PVP game (ie Battlefield V, PUBG, CoD, etc) I actually managed to have some fun and not lose all the time. I also don't feel like I'm getting destroyed from nowhere 99% of the time. I can actually see my enemy and make a credible effort to fight back (and sometimes win), which I never feel is the case in the aforementioned titles.

Anyway, I would recommend it highly. Especially for a 30 year old Boomer who has lot the fast twitch reflexes he had in the early days of Counterstrike.\t\t\t\t\t
