\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA great game for a niche audience that loves FPS games mixed in with a dash of RPG elements. Story and World building is great but quickly falls short while time goes on.

With this game being a looter shooter...we should talk about the "loot" aspect of the game. Bungie has almost perfected the feeling of gun gameplay in Destiny 2 and is almost addicting from the smooth animations to customizing your perks to the play style that you enjoy. Each gun that you aquire on your journey to max power level will have unique intrinsic perks that caters to specific playstyles. The one issue that has plagued the game for a long time has been that every gun in that specific class of weapons feel completely the same with some exceptions depending on your perk choice. With Armor, the game has a very low-profile look compared to Destiny 1. With some exceptions to a couple raids...You will no longer get the crazy and wild effects of lights and glows coming off your armor like you just got back from a rave in miami(There are some exceptions with regards to specific raids and events) Most exotics in the game do "feel" exotic and again, will cater to a playstyle of your choosing.

The first 200 hours are probably worth buying the latest DLC itself with stunning pre-rendered cutscenes and amazing worlds like Europa, Earth, Nessus, and especially the Dreaming City. The endgame mostly consists of raiding with 3 raids (a fourth one coming next season), competitive PvP as a game mode called Trials of Osiris, Dungeons, and Grandmaster strikes which reward max power level gear and materials.

The problems:
With the couple years that I have played Destiny 2, a couple problems have really stood out with regards to the average player after hitting endgame.

1. Raids become a joke after reaching the required power level with no difficulty spike at all other than learning the mechanics.(This is the same with regards to dungeons as well).

2. Competitive PvP is not supported enough to go anywhere higher with your skill other than streaming the game on a service like Twitch or Youtube.

3. After hitting endgame, it becomes almost impossible to go higher in terms of character building and skills...this game focuses on the FPS a little more than RPG.

4. For PC...Cheaters have recently become a major problem in PvP with myself running into cheaters a lot more than usual...it has cleaned up a little with the last expansion though.

Finally, this game is a steal for a free-to-play game and is worth your time for the experience and story...just make sure to have other games on the side for specific content droughts which happen near the end of almost every season.

Have fun and happy hunting for those god rolls guardians!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have seen your future and I have learned.

Now that the older school Bungie boys are paying attention: this game has been a roller coaster of chaos, more often beautiful than not, since I have begun playing it following my interest in its predecessor. It is a service model game with the Bungie feel from the glory days of Halo in my humble opinion. Activision tried like hell to monetize it, but it has held firm and even now as each new season arrives is still slowly, but pleasantly repairing the damage that drove so many away halfway through it's life (including myself and friends).

If you are familiar with anything Halo or FPS, you will catch on fairly quick in basic play. Getting used to the armor and weapon systems to learn how to best optimize for your playstyle can take a bit longer. And the Light skills are wonderfully crafted to make you feel godlike.

There is a bit of something for everyone here: Cooperative strikes and competitive Crucible, 6 player Raids that are the epic HALO: LASO runner's wet dream with some puzzle twists. Seasonal and monthly shake ups to almost all of it....it can get nuts.

Take a shot at Destiny 2 and see what you think if it's your type of thing.\t\t\t\t\t
