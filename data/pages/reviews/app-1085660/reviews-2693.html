While I haven't gotten to sit down and play as much as I'd like since their move, what I have seen done I've enjoyed. Bungie always delivers on the great action packed fps fights and the Graphics are hella fine too. All in all, the game's great but it does suffer from a few 'issues' that I've found. The largest one being that unless you've got others to play with, it gets stale relatively quick. The other issue, for me at least, is that 'end game' content in terms of PvE stuff, feels pretty much the same. Sure, scenes change, but it's always: Charge through as fast as you can, ignore as many mobs as you can, down the boss and gtfo of dodge and requeue with strangers who do the exact same thing over and over again and then get mad that blasting through an area gets them killed as opposed to paying attention.

I like the game, it's a great despite the two issues I stated above and sure, there's other issues I'm sure. But if you're a filthy casual like me, those are probably going to be the two largest issues you face.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tFor the ungodly amount of hours I've spent in this game, I would still recommend the game to new lights who join the Destiny community. However as much as i love this game it really has felt like its given up on caring about the core fan-base, the people who've played for years and years. As much as i welcome change, having the same stale formula for seasons with very unimaginative linear weekly seasonal quests that give you the same activity to complete once a week for 8 weeks and then leave us dry of content until the next season is a pain. especially from seasons like Lost and Arrivals that are extended due to delays with the next DLC. The other quam i have with the game is moving releases onto a weekday, as in the past things drop on the weekend meaning more people can play the new content the move was logical, however, the ♥♥♥♥ show of the release of Spire of the Watcher has really made me lose faith in this entire release on weekdays. The idea behind releasing on weekdays was so they can fix issues from launch and not work on the weekends to give the devs a break and what not but with the release of spire they ran into a massive issue and just removed all of the accessibility until after the weekend. Having progressively worse loading times in the tower, worse menu loading times, constant bugs it really breaks my heart to see the game in this state. With the lack of support from the Destiny Development team, with no way to reach out for assistance except the bungie help forums where there are no bungie developers actually on them to help the community, having issues such as what i've been experiencing for the past 3 months (Code: Nightingale) where i can't even log on and have to go through a load cycle loop for 30min / 1hr just to get onto the game and then having the chance to be weasled back to title screen to redo that entire load cycle is extremely painful and through all these bugs, errors and general bad game design i still support them. through the rising prices of their DLCs dropping them at a all time high with lightfall i still support them but i feel like i'm just going to be burnt out for good soon. I wish they would pay attention to the people who've supported them since 2014, atleast give us avenues to seek support other then getting ignored on the bungie help forums after posting 5 + times with no replies over months of needing assistance. I wish they would add new content but still patch the old. There is a huge list of issues with the game i can provide however it would just sound like i'm being too picky but i dont know, being error coded the other day like it was day 1 VotD and having the bungie team there to fix it and just made the game bearly playable really just put one of many nails in the coffin for me.\t\t\t\t\t
