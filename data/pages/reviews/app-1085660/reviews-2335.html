\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTL;DR version: The game is pretty, but not rewarding enough.

I simply love this game. i like it very much, which is why i've played it for over a year, since before the steam move. It is very fun, yes, cool action and everything. But it is very addictive. and not very rewarding. you do a strike you do an activity and generally its not so bad until you see how its all about a hard grind and rng to get items that need to compete with other people that play better than you. it is stressful, as even the shiniest chest or the shiniest engram or how interesting the quest, no matter how hard it is to obtain, or how cool the animations are for them, the truth is the rewards are very little. like they couldnt add some legendary shards with the decryption of an engram or easier ways to get ascendant shards rather than finding someone to play 980 master nightfall with and go through some frustrating and awkward moments.

Why i dont like it:

Some items are So good, that people Require you to have them in order to play high difficulty levels with them that you couldnt do yourself. But some of these items and content is locked behind a gigantic quest or grind which you will have to painstakingly dig into, sometimes having to only play one gamemode which eventually becomes extremely stale and unpleasant. For example, in order to get 21% Dellirium, you have to do an infamy rank reset, which is built through gambit matches. Now gambit is the absolute worst thing i have ever played in a video game. If you win you get minimal progress and slowly, and if you dont win you get almost nothing. This gambit gamemode is full of moments when you lose your progress Very easily and it is extremely frustrating because you try your hardest, try your best to be a good member of the team and win, but then you get killed and Lose points and this can happen over and over and over again. Gambit is highly frustrating and i dont know who is the insane freak that came up with this idea.
The worst thing about this is that you are Obligated to complete HUNDREDS of matches of this gamemode in order to get a very good gun ( 21% Dellirium ) that you cannot get in any other way.

The worst:

This goes for other weapons or content in the game, unfortunately some weapons are NERFED, So the issue is that players who have them absolutely wreck newer players, so then when new players decide to get their own gun, they grind, they play for hours and hours to get it then Bungie decides to NERF that weapon or item, and it isnt nearly as good as before. that feeling is very disappointing. im so frustrated because of that, when i got Whisper of the worm it didnt have infinite magazine anymore, or when i got Recluse it didnt have critical damage in bodyshot anymore, which makes it ineffective for enemies with different energy shields than void, and before it did critical damage even on shields of different energy types which is SO COOL!, but no, nerfed, now i got it and its not as exciting, and not as great as before. i couldnt get to enjoy its perks or whisper's perks at all. i'm so frustrated, and i've worked SO HARD for them, on my own.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tWhile I don't fully agree to some consumer practices of this game, a purchasable season within a purchasable dlc, having some weapons locked behind paywalls, etc.
This is the first ever evolving MMO I truly played, and by that I mean, storywise.
You know when you're playing an MMO and the quest is like "kill 50 wolves", and when you do the quest giver goes like "yeah, you helped, but nothing changes and the problem still continues, but thanks for your help"? Well, nothing of the sorts here.
Destiny is full of unique events, community events, puzzles, etc. Just Google the story of the Outbreak, Outbreak Perfected, Corridors of Time, 15th wish or Labs to understand what I'm talking about.
The online community (youtubers, streamers and their viewers) are truly engaged on these events and its amazing.
Bungie really challenges us with these events, and although I dont agree with season passes, they are a way to evolve stories and the timeline 3 or 4 times before each DLC. So you have this really neat impression that the story is going on at a really good pace.
The game have a substantial grind, although you can live without them fairly well. Plus, contrary to most MMOs, you don't need to grind Best in Slots BEFORE running raids. You just need 5 friends and you're set.
Wraping up, this game have some issues, sure. But I've been playing for a couple of years now, and it's money well spent so far.\t\t\t\t\t
