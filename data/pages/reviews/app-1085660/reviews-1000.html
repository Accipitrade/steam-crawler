\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI love this game. I would not, however, recommend it to new players, as the developers have removed essential content from the game in the form of legacy campaigns and the beginner experience has worsened dramatically. I believe one of the most compelling things about the game is it's story and lore, but there is little to no way to learn the intricacies of the story and universe from the game itself. Without a friend to explain the interesting details of the history of the Destiny universe, or without watching videos explaining said details on YouTube, there is little chance a new player will become invested enough in the story to warrant more than a few hours of playtime.

If the story isn't what you're after, you may find that the gameplay, while consisting of high quality graphics, animations, and gunplay, lacks diversity in the department of playable activities. There is something to be said for open world discovery and the ability to team up with other players to complete missions, however...the core process of ranking up consists of completing mundane assignment after mundane assignment, only for the goal of maximum level to be tauntingly pulled just out of reach every 3 months. Quite un-lovingly referred to as a "Bounty Simulator" by the community, this game never disappoints in the repetitive nature of the activities within. This, coupled with the practical removal of well over half of the game's weapons and armor pieces by making them effectively useless in end-game content, makes Destiny 2 the game which I will play for countless hours, while not retaining any sense of accomplishment or completion.

If you are a new or returning player, welcome! At least from those of us guardians who aren't part of the toxicity that is the Crucible. However, if you are new to Destiny, and you want to play a game that you will genuinely enjoy and be able to invest yourself in, steer clear. A year ago I might've said differently, but at this point in time, I know that if I were a new light guardian, I would hate picking up this game for the first time. I played Destiny 1 on PlayStation, and played Destiny 2 from launch for about 9 months, until I dropped it for about a year and a half. When I returned, the game had taken great leaps and bounds toward being a great game, but I think, at least with the decisions Bungie has made in recent months, is on a downward trajectory.

But, as most any Destiny player would tell you, part of being a guardian is remaining optimistic and hopeful. Probably too hopeful at times. Regardless, I am optimistic about where Destiny 2 will go for my (relatively) seasoned playership. I'm just not sure I can say the same for players new to the franchise.

Thanks for reading! If you are new, and decide to play it, feel free to prove me wrong. I certainly hope you do.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tSo where do we start guardians.
There is a laundry list of issues that Bungie still haven't done anything about, which continues to grow, hence why this is so long and took so long ("That’s what she said"). This list is by no means exhaustive, but also potentially the rantings of a madman.
The Crucible, Shaxx’s arena to test a Guardian’s might or their connection to the host. – The matchmaking is atrocious, if you aren’t matchmaking people from other continents with far superior connections or connections plugged into a bucket of water, you are with a serious mismatch of skill levels.
If that doesn’t make you want to rage quit, the sheer number of FTP hackers will. Why can’t we have anti cheat? Don’t know. Why does matchmaking take so long? Don’t know. Why can’t we have dedicated servers? Don’t know. Why do matches start with a 2 v 3 and you are expected to play it out or be penalised for leaving yourself? Don’t know. If you are 5 v 5 and losing why do they give the winning team the extra player? Don’t know.
They actually had match making pretty good a few seasons ago (about S9-10) but we can’t have a good thing for too long. Why are the rank up banners still so big that they take up your bloody screen? Don’t know. Also, why does it keep kicking me out of the menu when flying in to an activity? Don't know, Maybe just have one kick at the start of the match.
Why are we still prevented from leaving matches as soon as they finish and have to go through the animations of garbage for post-match? Why can’t we skip these menus like other AAA FPS titles? Don’t know. At least they fixed all the black screens they introduced between matches. Bravo Bungie.
Now off to PVE where the season pass model is in its 3rd successful year. When I say successful, I mean successful for making Bungie the maximum profits with the players getting the least content. We used to get 2+ raids a year and those were good times, now we are lucky with one and a quarterly hoard mode activity which outwears its welcome in a matter of days.
Constantly introducing Nerf’s to weapons and abilities and anything that brings any kind of small joy to this title instantly, but leave crucible issues for 12+ months outstanding. Like how hackers can ruin countless Flawless tickets on the last win. But nah, it’ll be fine, we must nerf swords again, our Ads are getting killed.
We didn’t know how good we had it in Forsaken, 3 great raids, new interesting stories and guns, lots of guns. Those guns carried us for a long time, until Bungie thought it great to introduce Sun setting. No not the lovely kind that you sit down the beach watching with the love of your life. The kind where the darkness is encroaching and all your loved ones (guns) leave for no reason at all other than Bungie wants you to play how they want you too.
Seriously, it is frustrating to see Bungie 180 on their decisions and commence nerfing everything and anything that gives players a slight edge in PVE. Almost like every raid boss must be a fight over several phases to extend playtimes, rather making unique new challenging bosses.
Also, what is with the hard limit on materials? Why is my vault still only 500 spaces? Why is Luke Smith still in charge of this title? Don’t know.
If it isn't the weekly lip service of Luke Smith and the other devs, completely ignoring the issues at any present moment and making things worse and worse to the point that they then bring stuff back to original or a previous version. Not long before nerfing it again... bright dust economy, cough cough.
But there is the obvious comment most will mention, just play something else. Well, at this point, I’ve sunk far too many hours into this game since D1 that I am invested and I genuinely want this game to be good, the blueprint is right there for Bungie to enact, but they consistently make changes in the opposite direction. Like introducing game breaking bugs every update.
Seriously Bungie, who is testing your builds? Is it a monkey in the basement? Someone call PETA. Bring on the next Battlefield title.

-\tA Mad man driven to the depths of insanity, expecting Bungie to make sensible decisions.
