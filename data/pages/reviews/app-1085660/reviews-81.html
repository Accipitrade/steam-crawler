Released in 2017 for Xbox One, PlayStation 4, and PC, Destiny 2 is set in a fictional universe where players assume the role of a Guardian, protectors of Earth's last city, as they combat alien races and other threats. In this review, we will discuss the new player experience and how it could be improved.

New Player Experience

For a new player, Destiny 2 can be overwhelming at first. The game's deep and complex lore can be difficult to follow, and the tutorial is not comprehensive enough to cover all of the game's mechanics. As a result, new players can find themselves stuck and unsure of what to do next in the game.

To improve the new player experience, Bungie should implement a better tutorial that not only covers the basics of gameplay but also provides guidance on where to go and what to do next. The tutorial should introduce players to the game world, its factions, and its characters, and give them a sense of the game's story and lore.

Additionally, the game should provide clear direction on how to progress through the game. New players should be given a clear set of objectives and goals to achieve in order to progress through the game's campaign. The game should also provide guidance on how to find and access different activities and game modes, such as strikes, patrols, and crucible matches.

Gameplay

Despite the lack of guidance for new players, Destiny 2's gameplay is fast-paced and action-packed. The game's gunplay is satisfying, and each weapon feels unique. The game also has a wide variety of abilities that players can use, such as grenades, melee attacks, and supers. The game's movement mechanics are also impressive, allowing players to jump, glide, and dodge in ways that are not possible in other first-person shooters.

The game's open-world areas are also impressive. The game world is vast and detailed, with a lot of hidden areas and secrets to discover. The game's public events are also a fun way to engage with other players and earn rewards.

Graphics and Sound

Destiny 2's graphics are stunning. The game's environments are detailed and colorful, and the character models are well-designed. The game also has a great soundtrack, which adds to the game's immersive experience.

Conclusion

Destiny 2 is an excellent game that offers a lot of content for both new and veteran players. However, the lack of guidance for new players can make the game feel overwhelming and confusing at first. By implementing a better tutorial and providing clear direction on how to progress through the game, Bungie can improve the new player experience and make the game more accessible to a wider audience. As someone who has put a lot of time into Destiny 2, it has been worth it for me, but i am very disappointed by the new player retention and attitude that Bungie has decided to follow. \r\n\t\t\t\t\tThis is a review from a Destiny 1 veteran, written just a few days after the Lightfall expansion has dropped.

Destiny 2 is a first-person shooter with abilities, co-op, pvp, and huge and constantly expanding world. The good parts of this is the best feeling gunplay in the industry, and this is coming from a person who grew up and COD, Halo and tried many, many new FPS shooters to come out in the last ten years. the abilities and builds are constantly changing and powerful, and definitely add to the flavour of the game and truly make Destiny, Destiny. the player versus player is extremely fun, with insane amounts of guns and builds to choose from. Even if Bungie does occasionally screw the balance horribly, but that adds to the experience to an extent. If you want amazing gunplay, awesome and cool abilities, outstanding co-op AND good pvp (although not as big of a focus as the co-op content), then Destiny is definitely for you.

but where are the downsides? I'll tell you. I would say Destiny 2 is an "easy to learn, hard to master" type of game. Bungie has put lots of effort and resources into improving the new player experience, and in Lightfall, I think they have finally made a "good" new player introduction system. D2 is intricate with many layers of lore, gameplay and buildcrafting, and quests that can overwhelm new players with all the content, with no where to really know where to start. Lightfall's changes only partially solve these problems. Other problems include PVP content draught, FOMO, and no in game LFG system (coming later this year), although Bungie has partially alleviated these problems with Lightfall and previous changes, they are still definite problems the community wants fixed.

Destiny 2 is a great, incredibly fun game especially with friends and I'd recommend the game to anyone who wants THE BEST fps mmo game. impeccable co-op (amazing end game, not like anthem), great pvp, and awesome gameplay. even with the problems new players might have without a friend to guide them, and the other problems I mentioned before, D2 is a fun game with near limitless potential to grow and become better.

So, Lightfall.

You may have heard very negative feedback so far about this expansion. The critiques the community has for the DLC are completely valid and I overwhelmingly agree. The campaign, while fun, was a narrative letdown. However, the quality of life features, the new subclass strand, the difficulty overhaul and seasonal model changes are definitely positive. If people ignore the campaign, I'd say lightfall so far is a very good DLC. as someone who played shadowkeep and beyond light, Lightfall is better. On top of that, beyond light didn't stop evolving and adding content in the first week. After the raid was beaten new content and exotics were released, so I assure you we have definitely not seen all of Lightfall just yet.

This is my first review I have ever put effort into, so I truly hope you enjoyed and found this informative.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tOh Destiny 2, the game I love and hate to recommend. Yes, it is a game and sure you can play it, but should you? I don't know. It's a looter shooter and you will be doing a lot of looting and shooting. If you like looting and shooting, buy it, play it, and have fun. If you do not like to loot and shoot, don't buy it, don't play it.

Ok so seriously:
-Many of the game modes are very stale. Strikes, Gambit, and Crucible have not seen any real innovation maybe ever. Nightfalls (harder strikes) are a bit more fun, based on the challenge alone but do not add any more mechanical complexity to the game mode outside of Champions.
-Raids are atmospherically brilliant and typically fairly challenging. Mechanical difficulty here is great and very engaging. As a raid should be, it requires a fair amount of team communication and is just fun.
-Dungeons are a lot like strikes but with added mechanics and are very fun as well. There is a shortage of these in the game though and the last one that was added (with the base purchase of the game) was years ago, Prophecy, and its the best one in my opinion.
-Exotic weapons typically feel exotic and generally the weapons in D2 just feel good. A lot of them go unused and to be fair I think that's to be expected in this kind of game and Bungie does put effort into reviving old weapons by adding catalysts or simply buffing.
-Exotic quest lines can be fairly decent as well though typically start out with a ton of grinding and then end in a Mission. These mission can be really good and worth the grind alone. Presage is one of my favorites of all time and is some of the best story telling in the game- in my opinion.
-The monetization in this game is becoming a bit too much for a boomer like me. There are seasonal passes (I think these are like $10? and they are roughly every 3 months), a dungeon key of $20, and an anniversary pack of $25. This does not count the base cost of the game at $60. There is also a cosmetic store where you can buy skins for your guns and armor with "Silver". Inside the season pass you can also use the games real money token system, Silver, to buy seasonal ranks out rite. A lot of this doesn't bother me all that much but being able to buy seasonal ranks and hiding content behind a paywall (anniversary and dungeon keys) for a game that you already paid for is a bit scummy.
-PVP is ass and I am ass at PVP. I do have fun with PVP though and it can be fun for what it is. It needs some new maps though. This game needs further development period.
-Story has never been all that good in my opinion and often times it is carried by youtube personalities trying to make sense of it. I dont personally know anyone that even really knows what the story even is as it really isn't told to you an any memorable way though gameplay. This reminds me of how World of Warcraft continued to recon their story until even the lore nerds didnt know wtf was going on.
***Lightfall***
BAD. LIGHTFALL IS BAD. there is only so much good will that you can cash in for money and bungie is running out of good will with me. This games campaign is way too short and from what I can tell, does not answer any questions with the story. Sense the end of Shadowkeep, D2 has run as far away from the from the original narrative it created with the arrival of the darkness and sent us on sidequest after sidequest and for some reason all these years later when we get back on track we are then sent to Neptune to do ♥♥♥♥ all with the silver surfer and find some veil we know nothing about, but apparently everyone else does. The City is empty and for that mater, the game is empty. I swear that Bungie has made space feel so small and empty that its a crime. Where are all the people we are protecting Bungie? HUH? Whats the point of all this nonsense?
-The end game loop of collecting and completing bounties and weekly tasks is also very stale and boring but I can't say that I have a better idea so it is hard to criticize.

Anyway...
Yea, I played this game for 620 hours on PC and probably triple that sense the game came out on console way back when. I've gotten my moneys worth for sure and if you can two.\t\t\t\t\t
