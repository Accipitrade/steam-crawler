\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThere are too many issues with this game to list but here are the major ones. Their server are garbage, pre and post Corona, with the constant disconnections and failures to queue for content like Crucible makes the waiting screen the most screen time the game gets. I understand a portion of the issue could possibly be on the player's side but this is a widely reported issue amongst the community.

The game is plauged with bugs breaking the game and/or weapons and armour. When the community reports such breaks and bugs the fixes are slated for months into the future or forgotten all together. This I can kind of understand as there's plenty of things on these people's plate but how do you acknowledge an issue, forget about it, and re-acknowledge it's existence later on?

Their content is regurgitated from seasons past, baked with no QA, taken out half way done and topped with bounties. Some of the content is just a daunting task to even get the amount of people together to do it. this pertains mainly to planet events where a Fireteam has a maximum size is 3 where often you need 6 or 9 people to complete. Not having matchmaking for content like EP, raids, and [insert expac/seasonal planetary activity] is mind boggling stupid.

Bungies ability to tell a story has been better in recent history, more specifically speaking, Season of Dawn where we finally got an ending to Curse of Osiris, 3 years after it's release. They've left holes in plenty of other storylines making each new story appealing till you get to the end. Hopefully Season of the Worth can fill those left by Warmind, 2 years after it's release.

The game is massive in filesize, +100GBs, and I dont see it getting smaller in the near future with Bungie scrapping D3 and porting it's potential content to D2. Some people suggest 'The Darkness' nuke the smaller planets like Titan to reduce filesize.

Apart from the terrible servers, the bugs in some weapons and armour and the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ storytelling the game has it's cool moments but the game is displeasing, I hope it gets better but I'll watch from the sidelines. As someone who paid for all the games content I cant recommend the game as I look back at it's history and it's constant disappointments. To someone who wants to shoot ♥♥♥♥ and looking for a F2P game I'd recommend it but strongly suggest not getting too attached.\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tso, free to play is largely where this game belongs. Currently destiny 2 has a tonne of content, (a grand 189GB worth when decompressed) it looks beautiful, plays awesomely, has some really cool fun hidden quests, and a surprisingly cool community.
A lot of the weapons are unique and fun to work with, and this is the first time I've actually felt *engaged* by a looter shooter. Perhaps it's because bungie understands how to make good gunplay, maybe it's all the awesome build options, maybe it's something else.
There is however, some glaring flaws: Time gated content, the season pass referring to "seasonal content" rather than the DLC (of which forsaken trumps shadowkeep by... a lot imho) and some quests being grinds to complete rather than a genuine challenge to complete. Something taking time to complete due to it being hard is far more fun than it being a grind to complete by... a long shot.
also bounties... bounties suck. They feel like meaningless busywork which distracts from the quests in your quest log that you should actually be trying to complete. Overall, I love destiny. But it's also horrible. But I also love it.\t\t\t\t\t
