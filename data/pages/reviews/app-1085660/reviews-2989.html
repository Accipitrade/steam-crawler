\r\n\t\t\t\t\tEdit #3: (Season of Seraph)
Witch queen had me wanting to actually recommend this game again. But second season of this year had been mediocre at best. Third season was crap. People had been saying this last season would be make or break, but Bungie has once again proven that they will never disappoint when it comes to disappointing old players. They are trying to get all of these wacky weapons into the game through crafting, to get attention from players. The most common thing I've heard is 'well this season has good weapons'. Yes that's true, but would it be fun to spend hundreds of hours on these weapons only to use them on reprised old content?
Bungie has dug themselves into a hole, and I've lost all hope.

Edit #2: (Before Edit #2 this review was a recommended. Now it's not.)
Never thought I'd want to update a review for a game like D2 a second time. I've already written a lot, and a lot still holds true. But there is simply one thing I would like to add.

Bungie has never disappointed in disappointing their most loyal players.

I do miss my time in the sundial. Though that season was not perfect either, but at least I had some fun.
Also, Bungie, can you ever imagine how sad it was for me seeing Ada-1 lock her doors? Black Armory was my favorite thing in the game, period.

-----EDIT #1 FOLLOWS-----

So here I am, updating this review after hundreds of hours sunk into this game. This is easily the best game ive ever played in years. Bungie sure as hell knows what they are doing with a looter shooter. Now, I know, there are many criticism surrounding the game's current seasonal model and various other things that Bungie has done in the past year. But hear me out. The Destiny community trashes Bungie daily, and its a good thing. It keeps Bungie on the ground.
Yes the seasonal contents are very boring and after Season of Dawn, everyone knows the gig, its no longer fun. But Bungie did pay a hefty price when they parted with the evil lord Activision. They lost two studios' worth of development power and creativity. Keep in mind that along side developing the seasons, they are still working on a big fall DLC. Which is not a easy thing to do.

A big problem in this game would be content reprisal. For us players who started playing since the move to steam, this is not a huge issue. But for those who were around since the start of D1, its certainly nice to have the Vault of Glass raid come back, but its not new after all. And now gear sunsetting is coming in, its definitely another way for Bungie to bring back more reprisal content without having people angry.

Didn't know where i was going with this review, but here it is. Thanks for reading my brainfart.

The original review follows.

9/10 Great Game.
When I first bought this game I didn't have any expectations. I knew the game, it came to steam, I heard it was good, and I bought the season pass pack. It's well worth the money.
I'm a long time Warfame player and so many people told me I would love this game also. They weren't wrong. Destiny has superb gun mechanics, there's a lot of diversity within the weapons. Which is something I think Warframe lacks. Sure it still is a very grindy game, but at least the missions are pretty replayable. With the addition of weekly challenges and a whole lot of other modifiers to the missions, you won't get bored.
However the game isn't very self-explanatory, a lot of the core mechanics of the game are not explained well. Often these things are glossed over in the introduction missions, and it's the player's job to rediscover these.
The social system in the game is basically non-existent. Yes yes you get local chat channels and stuff, but there isn't a way for the players to communicate with strangers in game. So clan recruitment is very difficult. All of the clan recruitment stuff needs to be done through the website or the mobile app. This wouldn't be a huge problem if all of the coop missions have matchmaking though. Some of the more important missions (like the Leviathan Raid) does not have matchmaking. It's up to the player to form their own teams and complete the mission. Which is pretty hard for new players, or those who are only playing this game casually and do not have a clan or friends to play with.
It's still a great game though, probably one of my favorites in 2019.
EDIT: I do realize now if Bungie does not give you matchmaking for an activity, randomly matched teams probably cant beat said activity anyways. Just find some friends and help them to get to the end game.\t\t\t\t\t
