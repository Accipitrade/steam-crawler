\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI have played almost 9k hours (and counting, oh god) in this game, and here's my revised review for the third time:

Please, for the love of God, don't get addicted to this game. You will regret it.

Story/Visual:
This is the main reason I still play this game. It is absolutely beautiful. You could never stop exploring, and the story itself has so much depth it kept us asking: "what's about to happen next?" and really question our existence as a Guardian. If you're here for that, the DLCs are worth a buy.

The gameplay though, it really needs some help. It's a 5-year-old game after all.

PvE:
It's ok, but it needs a revamp on the storytelling and interaction with the gameplay. Some strikes have been either from Destiny 1 or just really old, like 4-5 years old. While there are 1-2 new strikes as of Witch Queen and its new Legendary Campaign, it does not encourage replayability. Endgame PvE, like Grandmaster Nightfalls, does need a revamp with Champions being outdated and one-dimensional. With all the builds present in this game, they're too easy to kill. On the bright side though, Bungie did listen to comments and allow gamers to gild Conqueror the moment it comes out; props to them!

PvP:
Ever since Void 3.0 came it was horrendous. Everything mentioned about gunplay (2021, 30th Anniversary Patch) has seemingly vanished; now infected with ability spams. I do not like that idea at all. Rift in Iron Banner is a skill to learn, but I am not optimistic about its longevity and really needs to have a new and inspired game mode to make it new, or better; even.

Trials is ok, but a new map called Dysjunction is a complete disaster. It is way too big for 6v6. Cheaters are back (apparently), but I am optimistic that Bungie will take action accordingly. Still, a Flawless pool exists, despite better incentives if you play in it. Again, no one wants to sweat their ass off in Flawless pool and some are still avoiding it so they would farm stats. This has to change. Instead of a Flawless pool, implement Freelance Sundays, so the prestige of reaching the Lighthouse would still stay, and also remains as an "Endgame Content" in PvP; the equivalent of Grandmaster Nightfalls, Legend, and Master Raids in PvE.

Gambit (PvE/PvP):
While Gambit has revamped its rewards system (engram focusing) it is still not incentivizing. The main reason: the gameplay experience itself. It is more tedious to have more invasion portals toward the Primeval phase and prolong the game itself. It is not fun at all.

Raids:
The devs did say that they would bring old raids back, which is ideal; so players can relive the old times, or even experience what it's like playing its old counterpart. However, the most recent raid, Vow of the Disciple, has mechanics that are too one-dimensional. Unlike Last Wish, with different mechanics that test your skills instead of just memory. Hope this changes when Bungie has more resources in the gameplay department.

VERDICT:
Don't play it. The New Light Experience didn't provide the way Bungie intended, and some mechanics were sunsetted due to updates of the new DLCs and such. Don't even get me to talk about PvP for newer players. If you're not committed to the game, do not play it. Don't even buy the DLCs, would not be worth the money or the time at all. That's all I guess.\t\t\t\t\t
