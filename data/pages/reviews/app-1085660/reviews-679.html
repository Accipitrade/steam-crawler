\r\n\t\t\t\t\tThe Destiny series peaked in the months leading up to Destiny 2's release in the Age of Triumph -- so many activities available, both PvP and PvE, as well as plenty of loot to get. It's sad to see how far this game has fallen since then. PvP is absolutely no fun now that almost every super in the game is a roaming super. And the PvE is a step down from what it used to be, mostly due to the decline in the strikes. In the modern age of Destiny 2, Bungee seems to rely on FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) and big DLC pushes to keep players interested. FOMO keeps players engaged in between content releases for fear of missing gear that expires at the end of each season, and the new DLCs bring new raids which are admittedly usually great.

I regret spending as much money as i did on this game the past 7 years or so, and if you're considering it, I would recommend playing something else. The gunplay and movement feel great in this game, but its not worth the predatory sales practices and borderline abusive relationship they have with their fanbase (in 2 years time -- "It will all be better in Destiny 3! I promise!!!! forget about what happened in Destiny 1 and Destiny 2!!!" )

When I think about what this game could've been back when Destiny 1 released in 2014 versus where it is now, I get real sad.\t\t\t\t\t
