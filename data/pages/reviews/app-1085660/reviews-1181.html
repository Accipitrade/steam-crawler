\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI think a couple months ago, I would've recommended Destiny 2 to anyone who even had a small interest in the game since there's was an massive amount of content for anyone to play that didn't even need any dlcs to access! It was wild, you had like 4 raids, black armory stuff, a ton of exotic quests and a lotta planets, which aren't the best things on their own but a change of scenery is always nice if you wanna do something. Some of the cooler activities you had to grind a bit to either unlock or be at a high enough power level to be able to do without getting instantly killed, but to me it never felt like the grind was too bad until i was focusing down the last of my exotic quests that were leaving with the content vault.

Now I don't know, at least for free to play players, I think Destiny 2 at the moment probably feels closer to a demo than a fully free to play game, which is made worse by the fact that (to my knowledge), the other two dlcs have no real reason to be played (unless you want to play the story but as a new player you will be pretty confused at least for shadowkeep since some of it relies on you playing the original story) since the weapons and armor (aside from the exotics) aren't scaling to the seasonal power limit anymore, so even if you wanted to look fly in some moon or dreaming city armor, all it is is to get it for that transmog system that's coming at some point. Granted, I don't know if the raid/dungeon armors and weapons are completely out of the picture but the armor set i had grinded from the Moon's dungeon all had been slapped to not have the seasonal limit.

The game is still fun and really beautiful honestly, Europa is really awesome and i like a lot of the new stuff that they added (though I have my own gripes with the dlc as well). I think if you still want to try Destiny, you should still try it, it's a fun game on it's own and what you can do for free is still fun to play on and off and you might toss in a couple double digit hours or even more, but at its current state, free to play destiny feels more like a demo, a slice of most of the game you can try before you decide you want to play the game or get the dlcs and season pass just because of how much content was slashed that was available without having to pay a single cent. Take all this with a grain of salt though, since i have played a ton of this game so not having a lot that's new isn't as fun as exploring it for the first time
(also it says i recommend the game but i'm more neutral about it)\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tI genuinely cannot recommend a game less than I would Destiny 2.

Lets start with the new player "experience": The game is not free to play, it's essentially a demo, and the DLC will be repeatedly shoved down your throat whenever you even think of doing something that requires it.

The DLC costs 175 CAD (approx. 140 USD) assuming you buy the bundles instead of individually, not to mention the two dungeon keys which aren't actually included with any expansion, instead requiring you to buy an additional 2000 Silver, the in-game currency, with 2000 costing 25 CAD (or 20 USD).

The game has gone down for maintenance 9 times within the past 2 months, as well as server instability. They have had an armor mod disabled for two weeks now, but when a bug was affecting the in-game store it was patched within less than 24 hours.

The only non-balance related update that crucible (your standard PvP modes) has gotten in a long time was a "new" game mode, which was actually just returning from Destiny 1. Gambit (4v4 PvPvE) has barely been touched aside from the removal of certain maps.

This game is terrible and not worth your time or money, if you don't already play it, do not start. The only way bungie will even consider fixing the current state of the game would be if a majority of the player base stops playing, and I don't see that happening.\t\t\t\t\t
