After that was Halo 1 on PC, and now here on PC again for Destiny 2. As the years have gone by many things have changed in FPS gaming, but one thing that has remained consistent between all of these Bungie titles is the immersive world-building. The feeling of dread I experienced the first time I loaded into "The Rose" which was an intensely challenging level on Marathon, complete with a horror-inducing soundtrack... The simultaneously enigmatic and beautiful ambiance of patrolling Nessus and spelunking it's many cyber-organic cave systems... the LORE behind each and every chapter of each of these games is just astoundingly rich (if you're willing to dig around different places to find all of it, not always in-game). I love Destiny 2 because it really scratches that itch for escapism and adventure - the gunplay is top-notch, the sound-design for the weaponry is crunchy and satisfying and the environments are colorful and enveloping - all of these things when combined with the rich and deep lore/worldbuilding behind them truly come together to present something memorable; something worth revisiting time and again. Thank you Bungie for continuing to provide this platform we can use to live out our Cyber-Wizard/Gunslinger/SpaceMarine
FPS fantasies!\t\t\t\t\t

\r\n\t\t\t\t\tA very fun and interesting game - if you so happen to be into this kind of game. When I first started Destiny 2, I just wanted to grab a dumb looter shooter with no interest in the story, the characters and whatever else. The reason being that I just don't like the kind of humor and personalities this game presents me. I was interested in the guns, the classes you can play and the aspect of PvE

Fast forward and here we are today. At this state I cannot recommend this game. The game play boils down to "Go here and shoot this" and the story is even more simple: everything hates humanity, so you kill everything.

PvP and PvE are very fun, even if you play alone. The problem is how fast you hit a dead-end in the game's design: Eventually you NEED a bunch of people to play with. So of course, I tried reaching out to different people, some I knew and others I added, and absolutely nobody wants to play or even reply. There was only one person I did a mission with and afterwards, different time schedules got in the way. So if you are planning to play this game, start immediately with a few pals. Otherwise you will get bored really fast.

Of course the game is F2P, so you can expect a paywall to hit you sooner or later if you wanna have access to certain missions, weapons, skills and more. As far as I know, there is no real "Pay to win" mechanic in this game, so at least I am grateful for that. My biggest problem, however, is the grind in this game. If you want a perfect weapon, you have to sink hundreds of hours into this game. If you are not a lucky person, then I have to inform you that you will do the same kind of missions over and over again just to get the right weapon.

Bugs are problematic. Bungie often takes forever to fix some bugs and if you are expecting new content, it more often then not is broken. Of course, new content is even more grinding, so value your time and pick up a different hobby.



In conclusion I don't recommend this game as it is. The community, it seems, is more often then not just interested in the grinding part of this game. If a mission or even content is just short, people will demand something more time-consuming.

Play with friends of stay away, and don't look into "God Rolls" for any weapon you happen to pick up and enjoy. It will ruin you. I'd give this game a 3/10. I will still play it, but with a huge disinterest in whatever they add to it.\t\t\t\t\t
