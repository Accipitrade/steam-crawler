\r\n\t\t\t\t\tTLDR: Unless you are very bored and desperately need an FPS "MMO" to play, or you enjoy getting pegged, I would not recommend this game.

Bungie is the company that created the Halo series and lost all momentum when they created Destiny. Regrettably, I have played Destiny since the very first beta (for both games), as well as playing both games on release. Destiny 2 has some great lore and story, though you will barely be able to get all that from the game itself and have to rely on outside information like Byf (Youtube). In Destiny 2, there is mainly 4 factions: Taken, Vex, Cabal, and the Fallen, this has not been changed since the very first game back in 2014 (taken from 2015). The enemies are distinguishable from eachother which is great considering other games have a hard time with that. A downside to the mobs though is how bad their AI can be from their pathing, non-stop shooting, teleporting around, etc. Destiny 2 used to have a main story called the 'Red War' campaign, you may not know this information though since it (along with the lesser DLC campaigns) isnt required upon playing the game. The main story is about how the Cabal started a massive invasion of the Tower (guardian hub) trying to take the travelers power for themselves while stripping your abilities in the process, this lasts for about 30 minutes though so its not a big deal. Halo was mainly known for its arena style FPS PvP but when you look at this games PvP you are left wondering if this is the same studio or some startup indie company with the way they balance things. Issues with PvP include: bad balancing (and it takes months to fix one thing), some garbage peer-to-peer hybrid connection causing disadvantages and disconnections leaving you as the one getting a timed game suspension, and cheaters not getting punished due to the game being Free to play. All this and more has not only made the PvP aspect a joke, but also made the eSport for the game nonexistant. The tournament prize pool smaller than this game is probably a Nintendo funded smash tournament. This has led the game to mainly rely on the PVE aspect which is far more enjoyable. The gunplay is great and has a ton of quests and exotic weapons/armor to be obtained. The content such as Strikes, Raids, Dungeons, etc are all very fun (unless you have played the game for over a year and get tired of running the same 2 strikes and 1 raid every season (obviously the strike count is exaggerated) to get to power cap. Bungie, ever since the first game have loved doing some really stupid ♥♥♥♥ and then changing it after to farm love from the community. Their seasons are all basically the same involving drip fed content and running the same horde killing activity for a weapon. I do not think the game is growing in playerbase due to all the people with functioning braincells leaving early on, but instead just maintaining the players it has. The new player experience is terrible and can be very overwhelming for many who try and get into the game. All in all, you are better off just making your bad investment when they overhype and release their next game in 2025.\t\t\t\t\t
