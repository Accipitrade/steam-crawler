\r\n\t\t\t\t\tAnd here I thought it was Activision that was pushing the predatory monetization. This game is unique in that it makes me wish there was a middle-thumb recommendation setting because I'd place this as a "maybe." For reference, this review is coming from the point of view of a long-time casual player who's been coming back off and on since season 1.

The gameplay is fun, it's Halo movement and shooting with some small caveats, so at its' core it works and it's a fun experience. At the same time, drops for equipment you might actually like can be infrequent unless you either have a set group to play with, or don't mind chancing frustration with randos. The PvP has always been a problem with incredibly rigid meta that punishes not using the "best" gear, class, subclass, etc. I enjoyed Halo's multiplayer, and I've played arena shooters and the like since I was a wee one so this isn't just my recent inclination towards avoiding PvP, it's genuinely a slog and certain missions in the game will just go un-done because I don't want to deal with the hassle.

Recent changes have been a mixed bag. On one hand, the U.I. has improved over time, it's easy to use once you familiarize yourself with it and queuing into most activities you want to do at the time is nice and easy. On the other hand, while applying shaders is now free once you've acquired them, and you can apply the appearance of (almost) any armor you wish to any other non-exotic piece, the grind for acquiring the required resource to create a cosmetic piece that way is a pain, and that almost seems intentional to further incentivise paying real money for ingame currency to buy them. I haven't spent much further than just purchasing the expansions, and I have a good collection of things for my chosen class.

And that's another point, if you focus on one class then you'll be very proficient with that class, but unless you're willing (and able) to invest a significant amount of time into diversifying your kit across multiple classes, doing so will be a pain for all but the most highly skilled players. This is coming from a largely casual player, and I do not consider myself to be particularly skilled. I can hold my own in strikes, raids and the like as a sentinel Titan main, but I will fully admit that Sentinel gives you an absolute ton of crutches and safety nets.

And that's another-another point! Not all classes are created equally. Titans have had it very, very good for a long time. Hunters and Warlocks have largely gotten the sh!t-end of the stick. Unless they constantly use their abilities, their staying power is abysmal compared to the class that can fully restore its' health, and double it with an overshield just for melee-killing the nearest minor mob. Having a massively diverse arsenal of *stuff* is difficult to balance, but a lot more care could be put into that. Personally I wish they'd bring back each weapon/armor having a fixed set of traits so they have a niche and stick to it instead of the random rolls they have now.

And that's all I can think of at the moment. For the TL;DR section I know we all want, it's generally a good time for shooter fans, particularly if you enjoy interesting mechanics, but be ready to get forced into doing stuff you don't want to do for your shiny new toys. At least spending money doesn't buy you said toys (directly) any more for the most part. As it stands, while it's a good time I can't fully recommend it because of the flaws it does have.\t\t\t\t\t
